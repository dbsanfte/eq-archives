Some inside scoop on Anarchy Online from one of our members!! Read below!

What is AO about?
AO is a sci fi mmorpg that takes place 30,000 years in the future on a planet called rubi-ka. The main story line in AO is supposed to be a conflict between 2 sides. First you have Omni-tek, that is a big organization that came to rubi-ka for business reasons and to mine the planet for its recourses. Then you have the rebel clans that are opposed to the things omni-tek wants to do. So when you start the game you have to pick which side you want to join. There are supposed to be an advantage to each side. I think weapons and armor is supposed to be more available to omni tek players and skills and such are supposed to be more available to rebel players. You can also choose to be neutral, and you can switch sides at times during your characters career. since there is a conflict between the 2 main sides there is PVP. How they have it set up is different zones have different levels of pvp. When you are in a main city you can attack anything, once outside you can only attack wildlife ( all the different mobs) then they have political zones where you can attack wildlife and players of the other side, then the mayhem areas that are free for all pvp. But to get to the pvp areas you have to do some traveling, so you don't have to worry about random PK if you don't want to. They want to make it so that all pvp will be story based.

There are 4 races and 12 classes- there's some info on them on the Dragon's Bane website (http://www.dragonbane.net).

The game world is huge, it took me about 3 hours to run from one side to the other just following the roads and I hit a invisible wall where it was the end of what was in beta at that time. But there are other ways to get around that are faster. They have teleports in most of the main areas, vehicles, and the grid system which is like teleports but you can go more places and there's your average run buffs.

AO has zones but not as frequent as eq. You don't have to zone each time you get to a new area. It's sort of like Asheron's Call, where you could run through different zones without "Zoning", you would just get a message that you are now in whatever zone. most of the time you could run around 4-6 zones before you have to "zone". For example in Everquest, running from Quellious to Freeport would be all the same zone. but the cities and mission dungeons are always a separate zone.

The one thing that is different about AO than what's been in the current games is the mission generator. Throughout the game you will find mission booths that you can get missions (quests) from. You can adjust different things in the mission like the difficulty, if you are looking for experience or money, and a few other things and it will generate several missions that you can pick from.

Each mission will have a description on what you have to do - which could be to assassinate someone or find an item, destroy something or something like that and it will have your reward and experience listed for completing the mission. Once you accept a mission you will get a key to the mission dungeon ( which is randomly generated) and it will tell you the location of the dungeon and it will point you in the direction using your compass. that dungeon will only be accessible to you so you don't have to worry about anyone camping or anything. you can get people to help you by buying a mission key duplicator and make copies of the key. so you can do missions as a group or by yourself. You can also create missions to give to other players.

There are a lot of different weapons and armor in the game, so much that I have never really held onto anything I got that I didn't plan to use, just sold them to vendors.

Oh yea, another interesting thing they have are implants. implants are items that you have installed on your character that can buff stats, add spells, increase armor etc. in your inventory you have your average weapons screen and clothing screen but you also have slots for implants. there are a slot for each part of your body that you can install implants. Implants can be found or bought but also can be made using trade skills.

Also I haven't seen it done, but you are supposed to be able to take any weapon apart and see what it is made of and create your own weapons and armor (this is a trade skill also).

What is combat like in ao and the apartments like?
The combat is sort of like Asheron's Call and Everquest mixed (ever play AC?) there are different skills like EQ but a lot more, there's martial arts (hand to hand), 1hb, 2hb, 1hs, 2hs, pistol, rifle, shotgun, smg, bow, energy melee and energy ranged. Each skill is available to every player, but to raise it you have to put points in it like how it is done in ac. Using it doesn't raise the skill, only when you put points in it. You have a aggression slider (like ac) that goes from defense to offense. Everyone mostly slide it all the way to offense, it makes you attack more. When you fight a mob its just like EQ. You click on it and hit attack and the damage you do and the speed at which you attack is according to the weapon and your skills. Every thing else is like EQ as far as fighting.

There's a few differences in melee weapons and the guns. The range at which you can attack something. melee weapons are close ranged like in EQ so you have to be in close distance to fight, where the guns have different ranges depending on which gun you have. Some you can be
way off from the mob and fight it some you have to be closer but if you are in a group for instance, you can stand back and fight if you are using a gun and not get hit. One thing that is a problem right now in beta is all the root spells are unbreakable so you could get a group of gun
toters together and root something 50 levels above you and blast it away without getting hit.

But the guns and swords and stuff are all basically the same if you don't think gun. each weapons stat would look like this. damage: 10-50(25) speed: 1.50. the only difference is the range on a sword will only be like 10 meters while a gun might be 40 meters. The second difference in guns and melee weapons is guns have special attacks ( which right now makes them not balanced against melee weapons). guns could have 1 or 2 of these attacks: burst, full auto, flingshot or sneak attack. these are extra attacks that work basically like bash did in EQ. You use it and have to wait till it times out. Also they are all skills so the damage you do with those attacks are dependant on how high your skill is. There is a special attack that everyone gets called brawl. it's like a 1, 2 punch that works the same way. But if you are using a melee weapon you only have brawl while a gun toter might have brawl, full auto and burst. the martial arts class does however have its own line of special attacks. So other than the graphics and distant there's no difference in fighting with a gun or melee weapon than it is in EQ.

Oh if you use a gun you have to buy bullets and you have to reload the gun when its out. It reloads automatically but its slow. You can reload manually and it is quicker. Also you can make shortcuts to your weapons on the hot bar- that way if you have 3 different guns that all have different special attacks you can have them all hot keyed and switch from gun to gun in a fight so that you can use all the different specials.

As for apts you start out with one, it has 4 rooms and I think you can upgrade it later. You can store stuff in your apt like a bank and you can invite people to your apt. this is new but when you start the game, to cut out on lag what they do is create a newbie level that only holds so many
people. it will have things to fight up to level 4. You can only leave that newbie area once you get to level 4 or have 1000 credits(money), getting to level 4 doesn't take long at all.

The way they handle death is different than EQ also.

Anarchy online
Races and Class info
Here is a little bit of info on the breeds (races) and professions (classes) of anarchy online and some of the things they can do. There are a lot of nano formulas that aren't known yet and are not in the current beta yet but this will give you a good idea of what each class is about and may help you to choose your breed/profession if you decide to play ao.

There are a few things to keep in mind when looking at this:

1) there are levels in this game ( 200) but your abilities are skill based not level based, at the beginning of each level you get ip points to put towards the skills of your choice. you are limited on how many points you can raise a skill per level but you don't loose those potential points if you don't max it each level.  for instance, if you can raise pistol  5 points per level and you decide not to raise it at all for 2 levels, the 3rd time you can raise it 15 points not just the 5.

2) there are about 30 different  skills. each skill has its own color coded rating depending on which breed/profession you are. skill colors are light green, green, dark green, light blue, blue, dark blue, what that means is depending on the color it takes more or less ip points to raise that skill 1 point. for example if you have a pistol skill color of light green as a level 1 it may only cost you 10 ip points to raise it 1 point but if its dark blue it may cost you 60 ip points to raise it 1 point. you start out with 1500 ip points and then you get 5000 each level and at level 15 I think it raises then again at other various levels although I don't know which and how much. also as your level in a particular skill gets higher the amount of ip it takes to raise it also gets higher.

3) you can choose to raise any skill you want, so you are only limited to using different weapons and equipment and some spells that aren't class specific buy your skill. for instance you can be a martial artist (similar to eq's monk) and use a rifle if you are willing to pay the higher ip cost to raise the rifle skill.

4) the spells that are general spells that anyone can use are mostly buffs and debuffs of almost every skill, a few healing spells and some other various spells but they do not copy most of the profession specific spells. they are good if you don't have a high enough skill to use something and you can use a general spell to buff that skill so that you can use it.

5) and this is important to know. EVERYTHING has a skill requirement. this will cut down on twinking. when I say everything I mean every weapon, piece of armor, implants, spells, firstaid/treatmeant kits, stims (one time buffs that you can buy), the use of maps etc. everything. also there are skills that allow you to "hack" items with a hack kit that will reduce or remove some or all of the skill requirements of items.

now on the the breeds and professions

Breeds ( races)

I will put links so that you know what they look like. keep in mind that there are about 25 different faces for each breed ( all separate from breed to breed) and during char creation you can alter his size /build to be tall/medium/short in height  and fat/medium/skinny in build. All breeds are basically a form of human so there are no dwarves or elves or anything.

This is your average human it is supposed to be a well rounded class that is able to do most things moderately.  you can play almost any profession with this breed and do pretty well.

Opifex     http://216.74.158.92/game/breeds/opifex.html

this breed has bonuses in a lot of the rogue-like skills and trades. they are good with certain weapon types (mostly ranged) and not as strong as a solutus so the hp us generally a little lower and it takes a little more skill work to wear the best armor. professions that work well with this breed are fixers, agents, adventurers, traders.

Nano  ( similar to maybe a high elf or erudite)  http://216.74.158.92/game/breeds/nano.html

This breed is the best at anything that has to do with Nano ( magic/spells/etc)   it has bonuses in all the nano based skills and the skills that determines how much nana ( mana) you have. most of the combat skills are dark blue except I think pistol ,and it is the weakest as far as str and stamina so it takes moderate skill work to wear the best armor  and the hp is the lowest in this breed. professions that are good with this class are doctor, nano- tech, engineer, bureaucrat, meta-physicist.

The background of this breed is that it was a breed genetically created to be the work horse of the corporation ( either side can pick this breed though). when it was created it has the male form but it actually doesn't have a gender  ( there's no choice of gender for this class only male but it is considered to not be either). like your average eq barbarian , it is the strongest of all breeds and has the most hp, probably the worst at anything to do with nano ( magic). professions that are good with this breed are. martial artist, enforcer, soldier.
