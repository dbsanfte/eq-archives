March 26, 2000
Four Guild Springtide Festival
Our Springtide Festival involving Legendary Heroes, Peace Conclave, Bootleggers and us was a huge success! Not going to write much on it, as Epic from NotCrazy Network was there covering our event. Here's what he said:

"Wow! What a Great Time! (Story)
Sunday, March 26 7:26:32 PST -- epic

Last night I attended the greatest EQ event I had ever seen in my life!

The Quellious (server) Springtide Festival was the most organized, most fun, most rewarding event I have ever been to.

I decided to attend this event after posting a bit of news about it on Notcrazy so I created a new monk (Ashrum) Just for the event. I traveled to the East Commons tunnel at around 3:00 EST. Members of the Legendary Heros, Dragons's Bane, Peace Conclave, and the Bootleggers were all handing out free drinks and other great items such as oracle robes, fine steel weapons (I received 2), and black iron crowns (I got one!). The tall high elf man that handed me my crown was none other than the leader of Dragon's Bane Cmitchel. He introduced me to Oriz, the leader of Legendary Heros who I found out later was going to renew his vows that night. The guild leaders proceeded to show me around and I was amazed. There was corpse hunting (which I took part in but couldn't win because of my human eyes), trivia in which a lot of people won great hard to get items, a lottery, a slave auction, give-aways, a bazaar, and free binds, sows, buffs, and especially drinks (my alcohol tolerance went up 25 points =)

Oh and of course the beauty contest! As soon as I heard about this I promptly entered hoping that all the females would be swoon my my great bare-chested monk physique. Boy was I right! I came in second behind a gnome named Lorazon.

Oriz offered to give me a ride to the Karanas which was where he would exchange his vows. We teleported there and I met a great ranger by the name of Tamas. Tamas guided my frail level 4 body through all the wolves, lions, and aviaks to reach our destination: the aviak village in South Karana.

Ah the wedding! Great time had by all. As many as 40+ high level characters came from all across Norrath to see this great ceremony. Oriz and Kirastar exchanged vows and everyone cheered. I wished Oriz good luck and said goodbye to all my new friends. This was truly my finest day playing everquest!

I would like to thanks Tamas, Oriz, and Cmitchel for guiding me around the event and showing me a great time. Hopefully I will get some pictures up of the event but if I don't you can always go to the Quellious Quarters for a different write-up at: http://www.davalkyries.com/quellious/

I hope this was worth the lack of news yesterday and I hope Quellious has many more events like this!

Epic AKA. "Ashrum"
Quellious"
March 19, 2000
Planes Report - Day 3
"Well, we did it. We killed Cazic Thule. To the best of my knowledge, we were the first to even attempt him since the patch where he got beefed up.

With 41 people, including members of DB, LH, PC, SoS, Vis Maior, and Emerald Templars we took CT down easily. There were perhaps a few other guilds represented that have slipped my mind.

Anyhow, Cazic cried out for his little minions to help him, but none were to be found, for we cleared the entire zone. Cleared it with just minutes to go before the zone started respawning, as a matter of fact.

During the course of our raid DB won most of the loots (for once). Karre has a list of what all dropped, and I'd assume a list of who got what, also, if you're interested.

It's our intent to (ahem) farm the zone for a few days, without the interference of CT and his death touches, nor that pesky Dracoliche.So our guild's rangers and paladins will soon be seen sporting more of their spiffy plane armor! We felled Dracoliche yesterday in Day 2.

So at last in EQ, our guildname has been lived up to. We not only led an expedition that slew a dragon, we also slew a god.

I would say this is a major milestone on the road of acceptance as a powerhouse in the world. :)" - Tamas

March 17, 2000
Planes Report - Day 1
"A definite thanks go out to Kilrtom and Karre for their hard work last night in organizing the Plane of Fear Raid which is continuing Friday and Saturday.

With so many of us being "new" to planes raids/and or fear plane, it was great that they knew the locs, obstacles, "ins and outs" that we had to overcome.

There were members from our friends present also, Peace Conclave and Legendary Heroes as well as members from other guilds that came to lend their support.

For those of you that weren't there, what happened yesterday was we logged on as soon as the server came up and a few of us (about 7 or 8) decided to go through the portal (located in the back of the Spec cave) so we could camp in the plane and start from there with everyone else. We thought that the plane would be clear, but it wasn't lol! We got swarmed as soon as we zoned in, and several died :( We didn't do any body recovery at this point because it was too hard with our few numbers.

At 6:00 we met on chat, so we could be told what to do, what groups we were in, etc.

Then we went in en masse on the server and into the plane. At this time, due to the server being down again, there were no mobs and we could make it to the "safe spot" before being attacked.

We had approximately 30 people at the height of the raid all organized into groups, pull group, 1st group, second group, etc. We killed a lot of "weird" looking things and during the night a lot of shaman, monk and rogue armour was dropped (a lot of it no drop), a druid belt, cleric belt, valorium breasplate, TV legs and a ranger axe. (I know more was dropped I just forget what it was.) Fatalities were at a minimum until one point when we downsized in numbers and got several mobs at once. At that point, most died and we did that a few times in a row. After that though, again it went pretty smoothly.

The looting rules were decided in advance of the raid, so there were no arguments about loot, etc.

All it all, it was very well organized and went extremely well. The raid ended at 6:30 am est.

The raid is continuing tonight again at 6pm est. So any of you that couldn't make it last night, I hope you can tonight or later in the weekend. Even if you can't make it at the specified time, give a tell to Kilrtom or Karre when you do log on and I'm sure they'll work out how to add you to the groups." - Pantera

March 5, 2000
EQ Gathering II
"Well, the EQ Gathering II which was held in St. Louis on Sat. March 4, in now over. It was a complete success and was thoroughly enjoyed by all attending.

I arrived, registered and went to find a seat to watch the other EQ addicts arrive. Three people arrived in the full costume of their chars, including one fellow in an FBR. The four people showed up with sticks to which they had attached clear plastic sheets and painted their names in either blue or red. These sticks were then stuck in the back of their shirts giving the appearance of our chars names on the screen.

Kevin Scott opened the Gathering at 9:30 AM and told us to mingle and get to know one another. After looking around at name tags and feeling that I was the only representative of Quellious, I headed up to check out the artwork of the designer of the EQ Gathering t-shirts, posters, and mousepads. A few minutes later I was hailed by Kumaddara with "Quellious!" We stood and talked for a few minutes and then along came, Fenick, Sanguril, Swifter, Darion, Gudor, Woneid, Altrax, and Feadorn from Quell. We found a table, camped it and began talking about everything EQ and Quell related.

During this time, the famous Brad McQuaid arrived along with Bill Trost (Lead Game Designer), Mike Hutchins (Asst. Producer), Jeff Butler (Customer Service Mgr.), Geoff Zatkin (Game Designer) and Matt Yaney (Game Designer). Needless to say they were swamped as soon as they walked in and were introduced. Everlore was also there with their computers and broadcasting live on their site pictures of the EQ Gathering attendees. There were also a number of GM's attending (and no, Ozzmar, Mandybeth, and Cosil were not there).

After lunch was served, a Real Life Quest was started in which we grouped by server. At this point I had the chance to meet Ariann. We had 2 Quellious groups since we were limited to 8 people in a group and there were 14 of us from Quell. We were handed bags and a list of about 90 items from EQ (Soil of Underfoot, Fairie Princess Dust, etc.) to find in about an hour and a half. We set off on our scavenger hunt. The imagination of some of the groups was unbelievable. One group actually went down to the Mississippi River and brought back a real fish tail for the quest! Yuck!

During the time our bags were being checked, Brad and the gang were demonstrating the expansion, Ruins of Karnac. We did find out the following on the expansion:

1. The levels max out at 60.
2. The new continent will apparently be huge with 25 zones, the smallest of which is the size of WK. There are 4 city/newbie zones for levels 1-25 and the other 21 zones are designed for levels 25+. One of these areas is guarded by 3 wyverns that are level 53.
3. There will be one teleport pad each for wizs and druids and they will be located in the same zone.

After the the bags were checked and graded we again assembled but by class this time for a Trivia Contest. We were given 15 minutes to answer 3 pages of simple questions such as "Name all the zones on Norrath", "List all the
Level 1 spells for all casting classes", and "Name all the Gods of Norrath". Doh! Needless to say, no one completed them all.

While these were checked, dinner was served and the Quell group spent more time talking and getting to know one another. After dinner, the prizes were awarded to the Real Life Quest team winners and the Trivia Contest winners.
The prizes were, of course, a copy of the expansion for each group member! Before you drive all of us from Quell crazy, NO none of us won a copy and the copies are being mailed to the winners! Copies were also awarded to the 3 players who attended in full costume and those imaginative people with the names above their heads. Verant then handed out posters and pics of the RofK to all of the attendees to finish off the evening.

The Quell group said farewell to each other and promised to stay in touch on game (which some of us already have done) and we headed off all fired up and ready to get back on EQ.

EQ Gathering III is being planned now and if you have an interest in attending check www.everlore.com. They are supposed to setting up an area for your vote on the next site for the convention."
Sharac
