IN GAMING TODAY.....
2002.09.12| Shadowbane, Will it be everything that EverQuest, Dark Age of Camelot and Asheron's Call were or more? Prepare for the fantasy ...
By Tom Ham | Sept. 8, 2002
Gamespy.com
Original Article can be seen here.

Although the genre is relatively new, it's arguable that massively multiplayer
RPGs are already getting a little stale. After all, how many more times can you go to some dungeon and look for orcs before it gets boring, or search for the rare "Get-A-Life Sword" that only certain warriors can wield?

On a recent trip to Ubi Soft in Montreal, Canada, I got to check out what could be one of the most innovative MMORPGs to date, one that takes all the cool stuff you liked about EverQuest and Asheron's Call and combines them into a slick package that adds real-time strategy into the mix. Developed by Wolfpack Studios and published by Ubi Soft, the game in question is
Shadowbane.

A New Mix
What looks to set Shadowbane apart from the competition is that it combines role-playing with strategic elements. Not only are you leveling up your character and learning new spells and abilities, every decision you make will affect the history, politics, and landscape of the game.

"Shadowbane is a little of both types of games," explains Sean Dahlberg, spokesperson for Wolfpack Studios. "We've taken elements from both genres and mixed them together to make something a little different. Players have the option to build and siege cities, use tactical information and own territory like in any RTS, but players can still quest, adventure, collect treasure, and act as a diplomat with any RPG. Basically, we've expanded the concept of what a
MMORPG is.

Rather than focus solely on the story arc of a single character, we've extended the experience by creating a backdrop of political intrigue. The best part is that each individual player chooses what aspect of the game he or she wants to involve him or herself in. If someone doesn't want to help build a city, they don't have to. If they don't wish to join in on epic quests, they don't have to. There are many options and roles for players to participate in and the choice is up to the player … as it should be."

As one would expect from a game of this scale, the backstory is quite involving. The story of Shadowbane covers over five-thousand-years of recorded history, filled with legends, epics, and tragedies. "Shadowbane's deep history is one of the things that sets the game apart from other MMO games," continues Dahlberg. "The depth and sheer amount of backstory serves as the backbone for Shadowbane's strong role-playing elements. Wolfpack's head writer, Sam "Meridian" Johnson, created some 5,000 years of recorded history and sculpted an in-depth society for all the different races and factions within the game. The Shadowbane story will unravel throughout the next five years, and players' actions in the game will directly affect how the story unfolds."

The Shadowbane world was once whole. During the War of Tears, fought mostly between the elves and humans, the High King of the Humans, Cambruin, was betrayed and slain by his own weapon, a sword named "Shadowbane." This event ignited "The Turning," in which the doors of Heaven and Hell closed fast, ended the Age of Kings, and began the Age of Strife. Players are brought into this world of strife and chaos, roughly a century after "The Turning." The world is still torn asunder and most fragments are rife with chaos.

Players begin their Shadowbane experience in the newbie city, a kill-free zone where they are able to build up their character, meet other players, and acclimate themselves. Once players leave the newbie city and enter the Shadowbane world, the options are endless. Whether the path is to quest alone, build a city, join a guild, rule an empire, or siege enemy kingdoms,
players have the ability to become anyone and do anything they desire.

Shade
It's also important to mention that Shadowbane is a PvP game, allowing players to kill other players, which may be a welcome feature to many gamers (including myself). "Shadowbane is like an RTS on hardcore steroids," explains Michael Steele, director of operations. "It's more visceral than games like Age of Empires. This is brutal gang warfare. While you're playing
Shadowbane it feels like rival sports teams rather than friendly, social circles like in other games.

Everything you do in the world has a consequence. Players have to work together as a cohesive unit in order to dominate in this game. It's really quite interesting to see how the players on the beta program have sort of banded together in their separate groups and how they work through problems and such. I can't imagine how many different types of guilds are going to spring up
when the game is launched."
