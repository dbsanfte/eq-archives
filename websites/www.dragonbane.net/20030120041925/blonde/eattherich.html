Eat the Rich

Money, there never seems to be enough but that’s a given. One thing I have always pondered on is why some professions make the big bucks and other professions, which seem much more important don’t. Teachers, cops, firemen and so on make so little in comparison with sports figures, actors, and the like. Why is that? Is entertainment so much more important than our futures or our safety and lives? You would think that if you have a job that has a high risk of death/injury or is particularly unpleasant (garbage men come to mind) that there would be some compensation in the form of wages but there isn’t.

Doctors make good money and I figure they deserve it due to the importance of the job but when you ask your doctor why they picked that profession do you really want to hear “ Because I wanted to make the big bucks”. Personally I try to look for the doctor who decided to be one to help sick people. Guess what? I’m still looking, pretty sad. I’m spoiled though. My grandfather was a doctor in a small southern town. He actually cared about and for his patients and even made house calls. Unbelievable as it might sound nowadays there really used to be doctors like that. Shouldn’t our standards be higher considering the amount of money we are paying for such an important service?

We pay large amounts to maintain and repair our vehicles. Auto part stores/suppliers have four prices. “Jobber”, which is what the storeowner pays. “Dealer”, which garages pay and “retail”, what the guy off the street pays. The fourth price is “list” which is the price you pay for the part to the garage that fixes your vehicle. Now most auto repair places wouldn’t dream of, nor allow, a customer to bring in their own parts. It’s not enough that a place can charge whatever they want in the way of labor, they like to get you on the price of the part as well. In most cases the “list” price is double what the “dealer” cost is.  What can you do about it? Absolutely nothing short of boycotting your own car and riding a bike or becoming a mechanic. A car is something most Americans wouldn’t want to be without so you pay the money. I always laugh when a garage owner or dealer repair shop do the “I’m doing you a favor at my own expense”act. I worked in that sector and know how the game is played. Being female just adds to the mirth J

It’s almost laughable (if it wasn’t so pitiful) that one of the few times the American Congress and Senate (Dems and Reps alike) agree on something it is to give their paychecks a nice healthy boost. The big boys from Enron did all right too. I wonder why no one thought of freezing their bank accounts until that whole mess got sorted out? The proceeds from some of those Aspen properties probably could have helped those employees who lost everything and have no funds to retire on. Unfortunately any profits from those liquidations are well hidden now. Score another one for the fat cats.

A few weeks ago (way before the “under god” controversy) someone berated me with the saying printed American currency, “In god we trust”. I, surprisingly enough and after some thought, think it’s a very appropriate saying considering we worship the coins and paper it’s printed on as if it was it’s own religion. The holy house is the bank, the altar a cash machine, and the sacrifice is our faith in the system.

Stay safe and stay sane J
