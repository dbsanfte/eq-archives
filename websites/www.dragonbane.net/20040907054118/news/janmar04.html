Knight Online|by Menntal/Xannder
Hi all. Last night I tried the beta for a new MMORPG called Knight Online. It is currently in open beta, and I found it really fun! I would recommend you check it out.
You can get the download instructions here:
http://www.knight-online.com.my/client.asp

Their boards are here:
http://forum.knight-online.com.my/

Here is a copy of the "first impressions" I posted on their boards:

"Hi all. I downloaded the open beta client last night and played for a few hours. I wanted to share my first impressions of Knight Online.

First, a little about my background. My first MMORPG was (of course) EQ. I have played AO, DAoC, Neocron, and FFXI. I have beta'ed many games, including SWG, AC1, AC2, Earth and Beyond, The Sims Online, Rubies of Eventide, Planetside, and Horizons. I would say (in games) my personality type is mainly of an adventurer / achiever. Okay, enough about me.

1. Graphics (World): Very good job! The graphics look great, and I was able to run them on my main PC (AMD 2500+, 768 MB RAM, ATI Radeon 9700 PRO AIW) with no choppiness. I think they rate right up there with some of the best looking MMORPGs on the market today.

2. Graphics (Character Models): I would have to say these are good, but some of the animations could use some work. I particulary disliked the sitting / standing animation. Other than that, good job on these as well.

3. Music: I really like the music. The only minor complaint I have is that sometimes the music seems a bit too dramatic for the task. I found it strange for dramatic, epic music to be piping through my speakers as I killed worms

3. MOBs: I like that, at least in the newbie areas, these are plentiful. I found the fights to be fast-paced and enjoyable.

4. Loot: Great job on this so far! I like that you can get usable items and coin off of MOBs at a very low level. Too many games today go the "no loot" route in my opinion. Even if the loot ends-up not being great, just having it is a big plus in my book.

5. Lag: I had a few spots of lag, but, overall, this was fine. I was able to play virtually lag-free for most of the time.

6. Population: Please please keep a relatively few number of servers when impressed that you have scaled the # of MOBs (in the newbie areas at least) to accomodate the # of players!

7. Control: Great idea on this! I love being able to control my toon by either the standard WSAD (or arrow keys) or point-and-click. I usually prefer the WSAD method, but I found your point-and-click interface to be very intuitive!

Overall, I am very impressed with this game. I actually look forward to playing it when I get home. That has not happened, at least at this level, with a MMORPG (esp. one in beta) in a while. Good job! I will also be recommending that my guild try this game. So hopefully that will bring more load testers in!"

Anyway, in my opinion, its worth a look. I am playing as Xannder on the "1" server (there are 2 servers, a something1 and a something2 (I can't remember the exact names :) ).

Lineage 2 Open Beta|by Valkarian
Hi all, for anyone out there interested in taking a look at Lineage 2, Japan Open Beta is running now, and Gelrog and I have been messing around with it for fun. Anyone can get an account, and the Beta is very playable, even with a lot of the quest/merchant speak in Japanese. So far I think the graphics are outstanding, and the content is average. Supposedly much like DAOC the fun begins at the higher levels with siege warfare. Haven't gotten there yet but interested in seeing how it works out. I don't think I would leave DAOC for this game but it is a fun alternative to get on for a few hours to play around. Anyway purpose of this email is to give you a guide to get a Japan Open Beta account if you are interested. Here is how:

1. Go to the following link and click the Bright Red Box on the left side of the screen(Says in Japanese "Sign up for Open Beta" I guess) http://www.lineage2.jp/

2. (Page 1) Click the left side bullet and then click the button (This is the license agreement)

3. (Page 2) Enter your email address in the next box. Need to use a real one since it will then send you a registration code. Click the button once you have entered it.

4. (Page 3) Go to your email box (in another window) and cut and paste the Alpha-Numeric code they emailed you (it will be the one in english letters) into the box. Push the button.

5. (Page 4) Fill out the next page according to this graphic. http://www.un1337.org/l2-j.jpg

6. (Page 5) Next page is a confirmation of your information. Click the left hand button to confirm.

7. You are now signed up. Download the Client here : http://download.bbx.lineage2.jp/L2_040211_J2.exe Highly recommend using Internet Download Manager (http://www.pcworld.com/downloads/file_download/0,fid,22363,fileidx,1,00.asp) It very nicely breaks the file down and doesnt lose it if you crash. File is a hefty 1.18GB so it will take a while.

8. Run the client and enter your account/password info. Go to the options page (all of this is in Japanese now so you will have to guess a bit) and select English. Now all of the important content will be displayed in English.

9. Gelrog and I are on server 3. Names so far are Valkwynne, Gemmalin, and Valkwyn. Great resource for help on quests, items, etc is www.l2orphus.com

Hope to see ya in there :) If you get stuck feel free to send me an email and maybe I can walk you through it with teamspeak.

If you're taking a break, how about a Beta|by Hytstuph
Check out www.ryzom.com. This is the game that has characters that age and die, at which point you get to keep physical wealth and reputation, but have to level a new toon. This may solve the power-gamer versus casual gamer dilema most MMORPGs face. Also, guilds level, so your groups abilities grow through the ages despite members passing on. It's skill based not level based (like SWG) so non fighting professions don't fight to level. It's in final Beta, but is scheduled for release on March 1st. The race abilities seem more distinct (ala Shadowbane). All non-starter zone land will be controlled by NPC tribes when the game goes live (with very sophisticated AI) and once conquered you have to keep them either subjugated or appeased.

I think the game is PnC, and I'm not sure how the PvP is implemented, so it could go the way of Shadowgank... er, Shadowbane.

The reviews are mostly positive so I may give it a shot.
