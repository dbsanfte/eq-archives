Who am I?

*long pause as the cursor blinks on relentlessly*

I am a 34 year old haus-frau who lives in a suburb of Chicago named Wheaton. I am very far from being a so called “soccer mom”. My tattoos and leather jacket scare most of the soccer mom types away and being on the PTA was never my ambition anyway. I am a student of life and I take my education very seriously. I wouldn’t have made it this far if I hadn’t the ability to learn from my many, many mistakes : )

I have been exposed to computers for most of my life but avoided them like the plague. My father, a physicist, always tried to get me interested but in my younger days doing what my parents wanted would have been too non-rebellious and we couldn’t have any of that. Blissfully ignorant I stayed until …..

Three years ago I got the urge to explore the Internet so I purchased a Web TV and one month later ditched it for a computer of my very own. I have been online in one form or another ever since. Naïve and childlike, I have blundered through the courting process with my machine and have arrived here, scarred, but not too worse for wear.

Everquest has been my first gaming experience. I was never one for video games of any kind so I was extremely surprised at myself when I devoured the online game and became an instant addict. Interest lost for the moment I avidly await the new games coming out this year. To be able to share the experience of entering new realms with my friends in Dragon Bane is going to make it all the better.

This column is not meant to be anything other than a place to read my mindless babble and run on sentences that I seem to have a penchant for. Enjoy, critique, explore. I am always looking for ideas for topics from the silly to the sage so drop me a line if you get a chance : )

The Written Word

Way back when in the dark ages a letter could be written with thought and without spell check could be proof read for not only errors but appropriateness as well. Even after slipping it in the envelope, it would need to be addressed, stamped and walked to a mailing point providing more thought on whether this should be sent. Rashness and rudeness while still readily available was not so easy to commit as it is in the day of the electronic word. You had time for second thoughts in the good old days.

Instant messaging, chat and email have given us the gift of quick communication but have we lost something as well? I am constantly deleting things I have written out of intense emotion because I don’t want to hurt someone’s feelings or have what was written taken in the wrong way, or simply put, I don’t want to look like a moron. The lack of the face-to-face and the almost nonexistent accountability seems to have increased the honesty in the way of seeing others immediate emotions. In most cases no one will ever be confronted for some rude remark written in cyberspace thus the freedom to let your evil side show thrives. Regrets in hindsight (if there are any) are rarely able to repair the damage done by broadcasted reflex reactions.

Anger, or defensive knee jerk reactions pop up everyday in response to a perceived tone or insult where none was intended. Are we becoming more volatile with all this new found courage of the faceless many that populate the Internet? Sarcasm is a favorite brand of cynical humor for me outside of the virtual world, is one I have to leave at the door unless I want to use an emote. Sarcastic humor doesn’t seem to work as well unless you can see my classic eye roll and my flamboyant arm movements to know I am not being mean or rude.

With no view of the person you are talking to every decision they will make about you is reflected in what you are able to convey via words on a screen. If you have numerous misspellings they might think you are under educated. If you use shortcut abbreviations or “k3wl d00d” speak they might consider you to be a youngster. In turn, anonymous users can make others believe they are something they are not by the same practice. Case point : the classic pretty wood elf nymphet played by a 45 year old beer guzzling truck driver.

I see more miscommunication than I see quick communication, daily. Whether it is on purpose or accident our lives are changed by it. For those who have regretted a lost friendship or a rude word passed on to an anonymous stranger just remember to give yourself a moment to collect your thoughts, reflect on the ripples your words might cause, and take a deep breath before hitting that reply, submit or send button. It might cut down on your quick response time but to me it’s worth it.
