For those interested in joining Dragon's Bane,
below are our recruitment guidelines every recruit must meet:

Before submitting your application

  • You need a sponsor. A sponsor is a member of Dragon's Bane that will agree to be your sponsor, who will vouch for you personally.

  • You must have read the "About Us" section of our website and our Charter. It also helps to have read as much of the website as possible.

  • You need to group with more Dragon's Bane members then just your sponsor. Meet as many members as possible.

Filling in your application

  • You must complete the application form in its entirety. The form will be sent back to you for completion if you leave it incomplete.

  • Note that any false or misleading information on this application form can lead to termination of membership at the discretion of Dragon's Bane.

After submitting your application

  • You will be notifed by email that your application has been received and is "in process."

  • Your application must receive unanimous "yes" votes from the team membership and final approval is by the Guild Leader and/or Team Leader.

  • You need to demonstrate "effort" on your part that you are trying to fit in and be a part of things. If you offer to help Dragon's Bane members you don't know, that goes a long way in member's minds.

  • You need to show up. Period. DB needs to see your face around. If you can only be online whenthe majority won't see you, and that you will rarely if ever have the chance to group with other Dragon's Bane members, there is not much point in  joining since you don't meet the above recruitment criteria.

  • Cmitchel, as Guild and Group Leader has got to know who you are, and better, if you have talked to him, sent him a /tell and been at an event. He's the Guild Leader. The Guild Leader has final approval on all applications. Approval won't be there if the guild leader has never met you or talked to you. In cases of teams where the Guild Leader is represented by a Team Leader, the same goes with the Team Leader.

  • We're not asking recruits to turn their lives upside down for Dragon's Bane. If a recruit has a busy real life schedule and can't juggle the demands of our guild at the present time, then they should not be recruited because there are some time demands for recruits.

  • Casual gamers are fine but even casual gamers must meet our recruitment demands of being online at least three days/nights per week, grouped with DB members for the first few months.

  • It is up to recruits and new members (in probationary status), that if they can't be online for a few days to let the Guild Leader or Team Leader know they won't be online and why. Otherwise they are MIA. It's just common courtesy.
