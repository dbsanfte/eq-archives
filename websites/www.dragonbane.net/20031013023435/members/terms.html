The following are Terms and Conditions and a general outline of
what to expect for new members of Dragon's Bane.

Cmitchel founded and created the Dragon's Bane Dark Age of Camelot Team. It is part of the Dragon's Bane online gaming group at http://www.dragonbane.net. He has had extensive experience in running online gaming clans, tournaments, etc.

As a member of DB, you would have access to other DB online gaming teams, and upcoming new DB teams.

Please ensure you have read the Dragon's Bane Charter at http://www.dragonbane.net/dbogg/oggcharter.html and About us at http://www.dragonbane.net/dbogg/oggabout.html

Also, if you do not " really know" other members in the Guild, take the time to do a tell and say "hello" to the other members when online. Get to know and group up with each other. We've got the best group of players in Dragon's Bane (my humble opinion) and we're losing out by not taking the opportunity to get to know each other.

Make sure you check the dragonbane site regularly.

Guild Email Lists
As well, you will receive a lot of email in future, so regularly check your email. We have also established a DB General Email List. This list is used for general Team updates, newsletters, upcoming new game information, anything that is not specific to daoc or shadowbane. (dbane@yahoogroups.com) Should you wish to join the other DB mail lists, go to http://www.dragonbane.net/dbogg/oggmain.html

Please note regarding the email lists -
-Keep size of email reasonable, no huge attachments, link to them instead. We have a lot of people using free email services that don't take kindly to these.
-Sign your email with your character name(s) so people know who you are.
-Under no circumstances add player email addresses to any non-db mail list, without their approval.

Guild Member Responsibility
It is the members' responsibility to keep up-to-date on what is happening within the guild. This includes reading your email on a daily basis and checking the DB website on a regular basis. All guild policies are posted on the site as well as emailed to the members in advance of any change. Should a member disagree with a change in policy or a new policy, it is the member's responsibility to contact the guild leader in advance of the changes being implemented and not to argue after the fact.

New Member Responsibility
All new members must be active on the Team for at least the first 2 months. Active means you must be online at least 3 times a week. Should you not be able to be online at least 3 times per week for the first 2 months, you must let cmitchel know. Otherwise, your membership in Dragon's Bane shall be forfeited.

As well, Hammerthought keeps track of all alternate characters of members. Please keep Hammerthought informed, when you create a new alternate character.

Probationary Period
All new members are on a probationary period for one month. During this time, you have to mix well with other members of Dragon's Bane. This is something that cannot be determined through a yes or no question or filling out an application, so we ask that you hang out in guild chat and take part in our raids and events so that we can get to know you better. Please understand that this is pretty much the most important criteria for joining Dragon's Bane. This takes time and we ask anyone who is truly interested in joining Dragon's Bane to exercise patience. You can ask our members... it's worth the wait to join our team!

Sponsoring a New Member
Any member of Dragon's Bane can sponsor a new member. However, new members cannot sponsor recruits until they have been a member of Dragon's Bane for minimum of one month.

The following are Dragon's Bane's goals with regard to the guild: we want to keep Dragon's Bane a close group. We want all members to know each other well. We want to be a membership that mutually supports each other.

To sponsor a new member, the follow steps must be taken:

1. Each new applicant must have 1 sponsor. When a sponsor is sponsoring a new applicant, the sponsor will have ensured that:

- they have grouped several times with this new person and knows this person extremely well.
- the new applicant has been to the DB website and is familiar with DB goals and aims and general guild workings.
- by sponsoring a new member, you are vouching for that recruit.

2. All members can vote for new applicants from their first day on as a Dragon's Bane member.

3. The applicant must fill out the application form and send it to cmitchel before they are tagged as a recruit.

4. After I send out the application for opinions/votes, I must only positive response emails within a week and no negative vote in order for that person to be accepted. However, all applications are subject to GM approval. Please note that an "I don't like that person" is not acceptable alone; a reasonable reason must also be included to turn down an application.

Disputes
Should you have a problem with a fellow member or a dispute, you are to inform me by email to formally complain. I will mediate and deal with the situation. Disputes and arguments shall not be held over the guild chat channel or alliance channel, but referred to the Team Leader for action.

DAOC Team Specific Information

Bots
Dragon's Bane maintains several bots and vaults to help our members. They are:

a) Vault Bots. These bots contain extra jewellry, weapons and armour a member may be able to use and a member can access them as needed. Please do not give out the login or password to anyone else, as this bot was created solely to help Dragon's Bane members. Please contact Hammerthought/Arclight in game who will give you the login and password information for this bot.
b) Crafter Bots. This bot is maintained by Hammerthought/Arclight and is intended to help members with their trades. Should you be trying to get your tradeskills up but need a donation to help, please contact Hammerthought/Arclight in game who is in charge of this bot.
c) Housing Bot. With housing, there is a need to have a bot to house rental monies, and we're always looking to members for donations to this bot. Jockoo/Uncut and Barab/Iace are in charge of housing, so should you wish to make a donation, please give it to either of them.

Alternate Characters
All alternate characters must be in Dragon's Bane as well as your main.

Trades and Power Levelling
Dragon's Bane has master crafters for every trade possible and all members are strongly encouraged to buy off DB crafters to help support their trade.

Regarding power levelling, Dragon's Bane does not have a specific policy on it; however begging for power levelling will not be tolerated. Some members offer to power level other members which is fine, so long as it is voluntary.

Guild Chat Channel
Being a member of Dragon's Bane gives access to all members to our guild chat channel. This channel is to be respected by all. Swearing, name calling, spamming, talking badly about other members, harrassment, begging for items/power levelling will not be tolerated. You also have access to our Alliance channel, as we are in the As One alliance. The same rules apply to this channel.
