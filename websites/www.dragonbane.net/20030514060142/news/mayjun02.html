Neverwinter Nights Tips| by Strokke and Locutus
This is for those of you who have just gotten the game, or are new to multiplayer. I have no experience in single player, I just jumped straight into Multi :)

1. Avoid the "action" games at all costs. (Servers are divided into categories.) To get the full experience of NWN, join a role-playing server designed for low levels. (Would have a level range of 1-5 for example.)
Bad things about action games: DMs have no idea what they are doing. DMs give out too many rewards, which ends up twinking your character. All of the stupid people play on these types of servers, so you will people running around at level 1 with glowing swords, or people by the name of Tough Biyatch2859.
2. Learn about the class of your character before you create it.
3. we are all daocers so we know the rest :)

I am fairly experienced in multi so hopefully I have somewhat of a clue of what I'm talking about. If anyone wants to ask me any questions about NWN, email me, Strokke at Strokke@cox.net

****************
"I'm having problems with lag when I play NWN - any ideas?"
Hehe depends on your system. I run a Tbird 1.2G @ 1.4, 512 ram, Geforce 4, Soundblaster 512, and a speedy HD.

At 1000x700 (not exact resolution but I can never remember the EXACT numbers :P) I don’t experience any lag at first. But after playing awhile, I notice it starts to get a little quirky. My personal belief is there might be a memory leak somewhere and I think we just need to be a little patient as Bioware
tracks it down.

I had the same problem with Morrowind and it has better graphics. It used to get really laggy (IF I could even keep it running!). But they said there was a memory leak somewhere and when the patch came out I was able to hit 1200x1000 and now I don’t experience any probs.

NWN doesn't’t have the detail that that Morrowind has yet I tried 1200x100 and things did get laggy. So in my opinion, there has to be a memory leak in the game somewhere,

Some things to try:

There is a option in the configuration to force 16 bit color mode. I don’t mean the 16,32,64 meg texture packs, but 16 bit COLOR. Its under advanced when u start the game and choose Play, toolset, dm client, etc. Goto config, then advanced. 32 bit color does make a system work really hard.

Turn off all background programs.

Change your virtual memory from a dynamically changing one to a permanent size.

Change your “Role of the computer” into a “Network server” instead of a “Personal computer”. This will increase the Cache reserve.

And finally the most important yet often neglected thing u can do is KEEP YOUR DRIVERS CURRENT!!!. Video card, sound card, Via drivers (IF u have a Via board), mouse, keyboard (for those with fancy ones), network card, and modem. Latest Direct X (non-beta) helps too. New drivers are usually coded to be quicker and more reliable. Even a new mouse driver might free up like .01 seconds of I/O time which in computer time can be an eternity. New sound and video drivers usually are made to be more compatible with games and new ones come out.

Also, be on the look out for good buys. Pricewatch has some good deals (www.pricewatch.com). Watch the list carefully. The first item is not always the best buy. For example. Cost $120. Shipping $15. 6’th item down might cost $123, but shipping might only be $10 hence u save $2. And although everyone always wants the best, upgrading a Voodoo 3 2000 to even a G2 Ti is a hell of a difference, Course a G4 IS way better, but G2 Ti are cheaper. Also be aware Voodoo went out of business 2 years ago so new OFFICIAL drivers are not available anymore.

Anyway, we’ll wait for the next patch and see what happens! If you have any questions, email me, Locutus at jedicandie@qwest.net
