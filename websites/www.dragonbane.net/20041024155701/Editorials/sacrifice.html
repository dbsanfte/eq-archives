Sacrifice


I want to discuss helping others this time. Last night while playing EQ, I was the beneficiary of some amazing sacrifices on the parts of my friends (and even a couple of strangers). Every one of them died so that I might gain something.

To my own shame, I was so numbed by my own losses involved in this, that I improperly displayed my gratitude. Well, to all involved, let me pubicly express my gratitude. Come to think of it, pretty much everyone I grouped with last night, even briefly, went above and beyond the call. Not everything went well, but after reflecting upon it, that makes things all
that much better in the end.

So, I want to say, to Akurei, Zasc, Magool, Euclidian, Tantricone, Iceberg, Cmitchel, Kalene, Flinte, Aoramek, Mortag, Garadel, Pantera, and Kaylan - Thanks! You all helped me on a difficult night. You may not have realized it, but you did. Many of you were having difficult nights of your own, and I can only hope that somehow I helped make some of the misfortunes we endured a little bit better. You were all terrific, thank you.

In some ways I can't help but feel unworthy of such great sacrifice. But that doesn't make me any less grateful!

So it got me to thinking. We all know people who assist us as we pass through Norrath. They assist us without any gain to themselves aside from just knowing that they did something NICE.

So please, take the time to say thanks for someone's sacrifice. They may not have died for you, they may not have camped something for hours for little or no exp so that you could gain. But then again, they might have.

Thank the druid or wizard who is kind enough to gate you around so that you don't have to take the boat, or cross Kithicor at night. Thank the stranger who heals you when a fight is looking bad, or intercedes so that you can escape.

During the past few days I had been so stupid, eyes so glumly focused upon my exp bar that I had closed my eyes to the hundreds of wonderful people who make this game so damned addicting.

Norrath is populated with a myriad of heroes large and small. Too often we focus on the villains and bastards, we see petty insults thrown, accusations of kill stealing, and other acts of selfishness, greed and betrayal. So please, I urge you, open your eyes. If you really take the time to observe what's going on as you pass through the world, you will also see that all
the little acts of good, the minor sacrifices, truly do outweigh the blatant acts of pettiness that seem to normally get focused upon.

Take a moment to help a stranger. Throw a heal or two on that warrior you see sitting under a tree healing up. Bind that poor person who's desperately shouting for one. I could go on and on about minor acts of selfless kindness we can perform, but you'll see the opportunities if you look.

And to those of lofty level who look down their noses at the struggles of newbies, be a hero equivalent to your experience! Answer a question, step in and help that poor level 3 monk with three fire beetles on him. Make someone's day, leave them thinking "Wow, I can't wait until I can do that!" Because odds are, if YOU do that now, they'll do it too, when they can.

Sacrifice a moment to help someone. Your minute sacrifice can truly help make this game more enjoyable for everyone in the long run.

And that, in the words of Forrest Gump, is all i have to say about that.

Tamas
