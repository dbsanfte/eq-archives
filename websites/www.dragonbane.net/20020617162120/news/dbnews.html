Ok..... I have a team set up on Teamplay.net. All RA3 players will have to go and add themselves to the roster there. Here is how you do it.

First, go to the website: Teamplay.Net Register yourself clicking on "Join Now" link, and following the step, using your [DB]Name

Next log in by using the link in the upper right corner, and using your email address, not your user name.
After you are logged in, go to www.Teamplay.Net and click the link labeled "Team Central" on the left menu bar. In "Team Central" find the Dragons Bane team in the alphabetical list or just search for it in the search box. When you find the team, click the "Join" link next to the team name. You may then supply the password join the team and you are set. Then you should be in.

I also have to make sure that everyone is on the dragonsbane clan-mail list, not just the dbane yahoogroup mailing list. Since the yahoogroup email was primarily set up for interaction between divisions and friends of Dragon's Bane, and xfighter's clan-mail list was set up for intraclan mail, then I will begin sending all RA3 info via xfighter's clan-mail only............ so make sure you are receiving it properly. If not then go to the website and sign up for it again, or contact xfighter.

Rangers Ode to Mythic - (why I have 60 lvls worth of alts)| by Carthaigh

Blow Me!

... <calms down> ...
... <tries again> ...

A celt of pride I came unto
this fabled land of Hib
A bow unslung
a cloak unfurled was all I had to give

Like others of my archer kind
I hid amongst the trees
the sun shone bright upon my face
and blessed my bow with glee

But dark and roiling clouds assailed
the courses gentle greens
and Rangers from across the land
glanced upward from their tees

A midnight wave of undoc'd nerf
descended from the sky
it hid the sun blocked out the stars
shaded victory from my eyes

The prayers I prayed
the words I spoke
now rang hollow to me ears

My strength decreased
my quickness waned
spite the vestment of my years

Still confidant within my class
I strode upon the field
times and tactics these may change said I
but Rangers never yield

Neath sheltering boughs
I stood en'stealthed
and gazed at what I beheld

My foes like,
rabbits joined
and seeded Emain with their yield

Their skin now pulsed with
eerie light 'gainst which
my shots rebound
the wounded revived with wicked speed
and instas all abound

With caution I crept around
now searching for my nads
I preyed upon the old and weak
the kobolds and the adds

A mighty voice then spoke to me
and trembling I received
the word from high
like rivers waves come babbling to the seas

Group up young lad
find your team,
and lend your eager bow
A solo stealthers an evil thing
as every caster knows

Your glory days are over kid,
we've heard the Albs' lament
and grant their wish to
nerf your hide and make your melee shit

Whine not to us
the Makers said
we've had enough of this

Ye once were gods
and now are men
so please get used to it

But lord I cried
my skills lend not
to groupings crafty ways

Still no new spells
nor styles came
to ease my remaining days

My father
his sire too
did sprint without a limp
but here their heir
in shadows hides
a lone and unloved gimp

My prayers buff not
nor melee add
to what others can not bring

When around a group
you see me sit
'tis the song of leech I sing

Yet leech is not
a beloved state
when to groupage does not lead

So once again
despite the jeers
tis a solo life for me

I don't much think nor speak
when XP'ing I seek to be

have you need or should you call
I'll be alone ...
chopping trees

Was Checking out Newsgroups and Lo and Behold...| by Locutus
This reply was made to someone who is starting to find DAOC boring. I
got this from the newsgroups:

(Be sure to read entire part ESPECIALLY the middle)

Before I say anything, I know most suggestions will be "Well, quit then.."
Well just quit then....
sorry :)

Having said that, this game is becoming less and less enjoyable, and I'm find it exceedingly boring.

My opinions ...

Unfortunately you really need a constant and enjoyable group of people to make the game fun for a long period of time. Think of it like a card game. It's the same old game, but the socializing keeps it fun. I team up with a semi-local group of players and we have a pretty good time every night. We use roger wilco (voice) to talk, I can't imagine a text only experience (yuk) and I think this helps a great deal.

http://daoc.warcry.com/content/columns/deldon/

Check out the May 28th interview with Dragon's Bane. This is a family oriented guild and something unique to the community I think. Might not be your bag, but it gives some thought to how important community is to a MMORPG.

A single player game might have more content than DAOC, but it ENDS, and that's very important. DAOC never really ends, so you'll have to find other means to entertain yourself. This is one reason I like the additions of new content, no matter how small, to keep things interesting.
