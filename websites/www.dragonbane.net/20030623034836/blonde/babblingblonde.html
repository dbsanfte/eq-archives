The Schoolyard

What is it about the online gaming world that regresses some of us back into the “terrible twos” or the clique-ishness of the schoolyard? I am constantly amazed at the violent outbursts and desperate measures some players will go to over an item that will poof the second the power goes out. I often wonder if there was some horrible war or global disaster that made computer games a legend of the past in an apocalyptic world, how many gamers would endlessly wander the wastelands bitching on and on about how much virtual loot and items they lost in a game that exists only in their memories?

Some people might choose this moment to chime in and complain about how many hours of work it took to gain that ultimate level or acquire that coveted item and I say playing a game should never be considered work. It’s easy to fool yourself into that little trap but in the end if your not getting something accomplished in the really real world it is probably what it says on the back of the box, a game.

I haven’t had much of a desire to play anything just because of this problem. I play a game whether it is board, PS2, computer, or just plain ol’ cards to have fun. I can get my fill of backstabbing politics, strife, misery, and downright meanness by just flipping on the news. The gamers that get angry because you made the wrong move, or cast the wrong spell, or simply cut you up behind your back have no place in my gaming utopia. Like I said before I can get that anytime in the real world.

I think the greed I have seen displayed is the worst side effect of the all-powerful game. The extent that some players will go to get that bright shiny bauble is astounding. Makes you wonder do they apply this behavior in their lives outside the game and if so how come someone hasn’t given them a swift kick you know where?

Dragon Bane has been a wonderful experience for me even with all my complaints about the gaming world J Yes, bad apples pop up in all guilds and clans but in DB they eventually get kicked out of the basket so I always have hope when one rears it’s ugly head. I know it is only a matter of time until all is right with my gaming utopia J

Stay safe and stay sane (most of the time anyway)

Cynder
