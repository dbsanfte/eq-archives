Thank the Gods for Cable & Videos!

Whether it is the dancing colons, the singing toilets, or the cheezy local car dealerships, commercials drive me crazy. Not regular irritated crazy, I mean boycotting products for the pure stupidity of the ads to sell them crazy. On the rare occasion I happen to be caught in the headlights of one of these wonders, I can’t help but try to imagine what was going through the minds that thought up the ideas we are berated with while watching TV.

My current dumbfoundedness occurred only a few moments ago as I actually listened to the pharmaceutical advertisement cheerfully dancing in front of my eyes. Happy people running through flowery fields, riding bikes wearing big funny hats, and playing with adorable kittens, puppies and ponies, were no longer cursed by allergies because of this wonder drug that would cure virtually all symptoms of their allergies. It was quite beautiful until they came to the last few seconds where the drug company listed all the possible (they stressed the word, not I) side effects : drymouth, sleeplessness, nausea, diarrhea, and headaches to name a few. So I can either deal with an allergy or get relief and suffer from stomach flu type symptoms, unless I am pregnant, breast feeding or have a heart condition….I think I’ll stick with the allergies. 

On occasion I will be duped by a pretty picture if it is a sundry product. I have a weakness for magical bubble baths and shampoos. Some women buy shoes, well I buy body wash & hair care products like there is no tomorrow. My most recent drama of being sucked in was when I saw a glorious commercial which involved a beautiful red haired maiden being breathed on by a magnificent firey dragon. After my initial disappointment of discovering it was not to spread word of some awesome new medieval comp game, I was curious about the shampoo and conditioner for sale. It didn’t matter that my hair didn’t need heat protection, I wanted to be that beautiful maiden and live in the land of dragons…lol. Well after being saturated in that particular commercial I broke down and bought the product. I didn’t like it (it made my hair feel crispy, go figure) and the now nearly full bottle sits in my bathroom cabinet, gathering dust. Score another point for the advertising industry.

Being an insomniac I also have the (watch out here comes the sarcasm again) pleasure of trying to avoid the most dreaded of commercials, THE INFOMERCIAL. Whole blocks of time scheduled under the guise of paid programming by the program guide. These gems sell everything (using out of work actors and over the hill celebs) from fantastic super cleaners to fool proof magic money schemes to the most illogical automatic nail loading hammers. That a consumer would actually sit and watch this for 30 minutes astounds me, let alone buy the garbage. These companies must be making money though because informercials keep on comin’.

One last form of commercial I try to avoid is the email advertisements. I got smart early on in my relationship with the Internet and procured a hotmail account for all those times you really don’t want to give out that all too precious main email addy. I check this account every three days or so and usually find 300+ emails out of which, maybe, 3 have some importance to me. Junk emails are easy to spot but are similar to cockroaches. You block one and ten more breed in its place. I laugh sometimes at the subject lines of these all so obvious hooks just waiting for that sucker to bite.

“You and Your Family are at Risk !!!”

“A Secret That Could Change Your Life!”

“THE BEST XXX SITE ON THE NET GUARANTEED !!”

“Do You Still Like Me?”

“How To Get Women To Ask You Out!”

The ones that fooled me at first were the “Re:” mails. You know when it’s looks like they are responding to something you wrote them first, which is not true of course. Just another scam….

“Re : Your message”

“Re : Your inquiry”

“Re : Order # 068492”

“Re : Got It !”

“Re : Send me Information”

I’m not even going to venture into the subject of pop-up ads, or billboards of the net. Although I do wonder why I haven’t seen tobacco or liquor ads via the Internet. You would think they would be one of the mildest forms of pop-ups in comparison with what else has slapped me in the face while innocently surfing. Ok, maybe not so innocent, but I   like to chose what porn I see before I am exposed to something that could kill the mood, if ya’ know what I mean…lol.

In the end the world wonders why ordinary everyday people snap for no apparent reason…….pssst, it’s the commercials……
