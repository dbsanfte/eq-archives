IN GAMING TODAY.....
01.04.04| Tired of Quake and/orEQ? |by Sharac
For those of DB who are tired of Quake and/or EQ -

Taken from Computer Games magazine;

Kingdom of Drakkar - Available Now
This is a top-down view role-playing game from 1992. Most areas of the game are free to play now. You can get a free account at www.iecentral.com/drakkar.

10six - Available Now
Action game with a dash of real-time strategy building tossed in. Claim a patch of land and defend it with a variety of weapons and structures you build, but you can also drop into first-person mode and attack other players.

Mankind- Available Now
3D real-time stratefy game that combines space battles, political affairs, trading, quest and power. You control a fleet of ships, colonize planets and build an empire. Other players will try to steal you empire, grab your planets and destroy your fleet. You can download a demo that limits the number of units you can control or you can pay $49 for a yearly subscription.

Allegiance - Available Now
Is an on-line game that combines the real-time strategy genre with the space sim genre. Up to 32 players battle it out in a game that combines harvesting, research, and space battles.

Star Peace - Available Now
Massively multi-player game of economic building. It's a lot like SimCity but instead of controlling a city, you are playing alongside other players. There are over 300 different structurews to research and so on.

Neverwinter Nights - 2001
It is a bit of a hybrid online game. It won't support more than 64 in a game and can also be played offline in solo mode. It will ship with an editor that Bioware is promising will be full-featured and easy to use, so we may see a lot of mod activity spring up around this game. Supposedly, you can creat games where a live Dungeon Master will control the action, or where the AI will handle those tasks. You will be able to create a character that will advance in levels and Bioware has plans for secure servers that will allow players to "check-out" their characters for play and then check them back in, with the idea being that this will prevent hacking.

Anarchy Online - In Beta Now
Should be the first science-fiction roleplaying on the market. IT has a four year story arc already planned that covers the conflict between the rebels and the Omni-Tek corporation. Players will also be able to build their own quests and will have randomly spawned dungeons that will be generated in-game for groups of players looking to receive a mission.

Lineage: The Bloodpledge - In Beta Now
Is a Korean game that is extremely popular there. It's a fantasy game of orcs and elves that resembles "Ultima Online" and features battles for control over towns and castles, a magic system with over 50 spells, gambling events, dogs (yes, you can have a pet) and a rather elaborate item creation system that has you following formulas to create items like bracers.

RedMoon - In Beta Now
Another Korean game. It's set in an anime-styled science-fiction world. IT offers players both pre- and post-cataclysmic settings. There are nine types that players base their characters on. Players will battle spies and cyborgs, cast spells, and so on with no limit on the levels the players can attain. The players will be able to e-mail items to one another in the game and JoyCity promises constantly updated quests.

Dark Age of Camelot - In Beta Now
This is an Arthurian game with three factions: Vikings, Celts, and Brits. Each faction will have its own adventure areas in which player versus player conflict within the faction isn't allowed. The ultimate goal that players will build for will be to adventure in the rival faction's areas and engage in player vs player combat against enemy players.

The Sims Online - 2001
You can expect to play a version of "The Sims" in a massively-multiplayer world with the focus being on social interaction. Character advancement may be similar to the original game - get a job, improve your dwelling, etc. It looks like players may compete by getting other players to visit their homes or businesses they set up.

Shadowbane - In Limited Beta Now
Fantasy-themed "Shadowbanes's" big strength will be its complex player versus player conflict. In fact, they are building the game around this controversial feature. As to the style of play, it wil be level-based and Wolfpack has said that they want the gameplay style to be similar to "Diablo", though the game uses a 3-D engine. There will be guilds that can control territories, the ability to form alliances, and so on. Player races include elves, humans, aelfborn, dwarves, half-giants, shades, and irekei. There are four basic character classes: fighter, healer, mage and the rogue, but they can specialize in one 18 advanced character classes, such as druid, crusader, assassin and more.

Motor City Online - 2001
Race for ponk slips. Pull into the burger joint and show off your ride. This is a "persistent-state hot rodding community" for car lovers. You'll be able to buy, sell and customize hotrods, including classics, stocks, muscle cars and customs. You can even sell custom paint jobs to other players. There will be racing too allowing players to race on back streets or in a sanctioned racing circuit. AND you can listen to songs on your car "radio"!

Jumpgate - Q2, 2001
It's a persistent sci-fi universe that's basically "Privateer" or "Elite" in a massively multiplayer universe. Players engage in ship combat, trading, smuggling, research, and more. You will use money to upgrade your shie and hire new pilots to command a fleet.

Neocron - 2001-2002
This will be the first massively multi-player first-person shooter It's set in the 27th century and the Earth is in trouble so we have colonized other planets including Neocron. The city is patrolled by powerful non-player characters (police and robots) but the surrounding outlying areas will be where the first-person shooter action will take place.

Planetside - 2001-2002
Another game by Verant, it's a faction-based shooter with money and implants taking the place of gaining levels. Any player will be4 able to kill another player.

World War II Online - 2001-2002
This will be the first virtual battlefield. Something that wargame fans have dreamed of for some time. You can be an individual soldier in the trenches or in comand of a tank or you can also battle in the air or on the sea. Players who have advanced in rank can even take command and issue orders to other players. They have eliminated the Nazi insignia.

Atriarch - 2001-2002
Another Sci-fi fame in the works. There aren't any humans in this world but there are a few humanoids. It allows players to build not just homes but entire cities. As such, you can expect a detailed economic system as well to
go along with the adventuring. You will be able to form an army of NPC's and wage war. The developers have promised that players will be able to have an impace on the game world and change it.

BigWorld - 2002
It's a former penal colony and will include dynamicallygenerated missions, player vs player conflicts, factions, and more. It utilizes a third-person view and will start players off knowing nothing of their past. They will discover it as they play the game. There are three playable races: Human, a cio-mechanical Cybrid, or a tribal alien Beziel.

Sovereign - 2001-2002
This is a massively mutli-player real-time strategy game that promises to have much more depth than the typical games in its genre with diplomacy, spying, alliances, trade routes, and many other features that almost make it sound like a turn-based "Civilization"-style game. The game will begin and end with a winner. The persisitent element is the player's character, the "ruler", and will be used in each new game, accumulating experience and
goodies.

Star Wars Galaxies - 2002
A Sony Online game that is headed up by ex-"Ultima Online" developers that will have it's emphasis on skills instead of levels. They aren't saying much about the game other than you will not be able to play Luke Skywalker or Darth Vader but may see them ingame.

Everquest 2 - Unknown
All that is known about this game at this time is that it will use a much more powerful graphics engine than currently at use in EQ.

Project Entropia - Unknown
Another sci-fi game with a couple of differences. It will be free to play but to buy items in the game, you will be charged real money.

Caeron3000 - Unknown
Another sci-fi setting instead of fantasy that is set one million light years away from us. You will start on a planet with 5 different races that have been mutated from years of radiation. You will be able to travel from planet to planet via spaceship and death is permanent unless you've cloned yourself. There will be planetside and space battles, bounty hunters, and ingame newspapers.

Horizons - Unknown
You can be a wide variety of characters in this game and will have governments. There will be trade routes, trade skills, faction system, and the combat will focus on player versus player. There will be built-in penalties for killing within your own factions.

Middle Earth Online - Unknown
Sierra is currently in court with the Tolkien estate over the rights to do Middle Earth computer games. So the future of this is unknown at this time.
