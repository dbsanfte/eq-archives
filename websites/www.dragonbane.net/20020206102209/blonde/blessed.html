Blessed with Gracelessness

When I was just a tot one of my first memories was of falling down a flight of stairs only to stand up unhurt and step on a thumbtack. This should have given fair warning of the years to come. If there is an accident waiting to happen and I am in a 3 mile radius it is waiting for me and no other.

As a child my parents just thought I was accident prone. Now that I am nearly 35 it’s considered terminal gracelessness.

I can pretty much count on at least one incident a day. Whether I am leaving a room too quickly and my hair catches (it’s very long) in the shutting door (yanking me off my feet) or misjudging where the coffee table is and falling over it backwards because I was busy yapping at someone (no names mentioned) it’s a daily occurrence.

Considering I’m a “glass half full” type of chick I have been thankful that most of my performances have been given in the privacy of my own home. There have been some occasions that I wasn’t as lucky. There was the time I went out to a fancy dinner all dressed up and while getting out of the car in front of the restaurant my high heel caught on my purse strap causing me to spill out on to the pavement. Of course the dining area is visible from the entrance and 40 faces are staring at me as I lay on the sidewalk As I looked up the poor valet was standing there with his hand outstretched with this dumbfounded look on his face. I couldn’t help but giggle.

Or there was the time I thought I was looking all sexy and cute in a pair of short shorts and a tube top and ran into a local convenience store to get some sodas for a car load of friends. On my way out (with my arms full) I promptly fall flat on my face, pop cans went flying, and my tube top did a disappearing act. Not as many witnesses as the restaurant deal but a bigger finish J

Stairs are my greatest enemy but I have tumbled down them enough to believe I have actually mastered them. Not in the sense of eliminating the fall entirely but being able to curl myself into a bouncey ball to avoid the worst injuries. It is commonplace in my household to hear my body thump down the flight and here my giggling in the aftermath. I am truly a goof.

Some might suggest that I try to be more careful. Larry (DBEQ: Synsear) says it at least once a day. It just doesn’t matter. I am blessed and there is no getting around it. Trying to be less akward would only intensify my clumsiness. So I endure each mishap as it comes and hope another trip to the emergency room can be avoided.

Exasperating as such a gift is, it has given me lots of giggles J
