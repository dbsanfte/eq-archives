9-11

I am afraid. I am angry. I am in shock.

I see the same in every other face.

This ever present lump in my throat and slow unpredictable tear ups are the norm now.

My parents both moved to the east coast a couple years ago. My son just left for boarding school on the east coast a week ago. It was his first plane ride alone. I was already missing him but now I am not with him to look in his eyes and hold him safe. There are some who will never again be able to hold their lost and missing. They are in my every thought, waking and sleeping.

It is not as quiet in Chicago as it was Tuesday or Wednesday. Being so close to O’Hare airport I never really noticed how much air traffic there was until it was gone. It is Thursday, planes are flying and it looks like rain here. Finally appropriate in a simple sad way. The beautiful relentless sunshine of the last two days has been a mockery of sorts to the common sorrow in the area.

The worldwide outcry has been comforting. To know that not everyone hates gives hope. To see the tireless labors and efforts of strangers and loved ones alike gives hope. I don’t think the human spirit has been broken but I believe its’ heart has. As Americans we felt safe. No outside source has ever hit our mainland like this. That ideal is gone. Sort of like emerging from a happy unrealizing childs’ world into the naked and brutal vulgarities of adulthood. Now we, on our own soil, truly realize how much another person and their following can hate someone elses way of life. This was not an act of war. War is striking out at soldiers, military bases, and weapons. This was an attempt to start genocide. The willful killing of a whole race or nation. Do you honestly think this is the end? Not a chance.

Against my beliefs that taking another persons life is wrong the person responsible for this horror has to be stopped. The only way this can happen is for this person to cease to live. I never thought I would ever wish for someone else’s  death but I do. Not for vengeance (that would be a slow torturous death, tempting but no, I haven’t changed that much I guess) but for the elementary desire to continue our life, liberty and happiness. This person, if left to live, will continue his assault on humanity.

The persons responsible are really only a percent of my emotions. A small part (which is growing steadily) is the fear of my fellow Americans. Last night (Wednesday) and tonight (Thursday) there have been “Patriotic Rallies” held in a suburb of Chicago (Bridgeview). These rallies start out peacefully enough but last nights’ gathering got out of hand when the tempers flared and the desire to march to a Mosque and an Arab part of town overtook the crowd. Police from many neighboring communities are there right now to keep the peace. Please, please, please use common sense and use caution before turning on  fellow Americans for deeds done by someone who is simply a very bad crazy man. There have been tons of them of every race, creed, and religion throughout history. You don’t have to fit a special mold to be a monster. I could name names but we know them all. Do not assume that because someone has a certain religion, ancestors, or last name that they are not an American first. No matter what, I have to believe that most people are human with goodness inside.

Before my eyes unwillingly blink shut, deep in my heart, I try not to think of the families who are glued to their radios or TVs who live in that far away land and know as little about me as I know about them. I know they love their kids. They have to.

Take care all.

Cynder
