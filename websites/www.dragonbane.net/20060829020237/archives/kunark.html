KUNARK

Just some thoughts about Kunark this week. For the most part I've got to say I like Kunark. I've not been to every zone, not even more than 1/3 of them. It took a little adjustment, but I'm liking the place very much. (To my readers of lower level, please forgive my high level slant)

One thing I'm really liking about the place is that it seems to be bringing people together. I've seen groups forming and been in groups of people who probably wouldn't have come together in Lower Guk or gotten together for Planes raiding. This strikes me as very cool. Personally, it's given me, at least, a chance to re-evaluate some people I'd long been suspicious of and to realize that perhaps I'd been wrong in avoiding some of them all this time. I can only hope that perhaps others are seeing people through new eyes in Kunark as well.

Another aspect of this is that I've seen some really generous attitudes toward some of the neat new items. So far, people seem to be doing a fairly decent job of seeing items going to those classes that can use them, and that makes me smile. I've heard about a ridiculous trade or two that might make people feel taken once the item they traded so much for becomes more common, but hopefully those are isolated cases!

I do have to wonder about something, though. A lot of old near-forgotten names have been cropping up, people who made 50 long ago and got bored, either not playing much, or playing twinks. What baffles me is that a lot of these people who were 50 so long, and bored, are now back and determinedly driving for 60. Kunark's been out a week, and there are a few level 53
people already! (I've heard some other servers had level 54 players before this past weekend. Egads.) C'mon folks, stop and smell the roses! Explore a bit. Hell, you might even find a spot where you can make BETTER exp...

As for Kunark itself, it still feels vast, like being outdoors. I know the sense of hugeness will fade as we become more familiar with the zones, but it's still very impressive. The rest of Norrath looks bland by comparison, like a cartoon background. I was in West Commonlands the other day and I
kept expecting to see Elmer Fudd chase Bugs Bunny across my screen. (/shout Wussup Doc, Elmer Fudd to Kithicor zone!)

I'm not entirely certain I like Firiona Vie having a seperate faction of it's own. Supposedly, being a recently-formed outpost, one would think that your existing faction with elves and dwarves, etc, would be the same there. But by virtue of travelling across an ocean and slapping up a few  buildings (Side note: Verant graphics designers, and apparently high elves too, have a LOT to learn about masonry!) these NPCs have suddenly forgotten the esteem they previously held us in. Doesn't make a lot of sense to me, but, it's just a game, I suppose. There could have been some high level monsters that one can kill to gain faction there, however. It's as if Verant expects level
45+ folks to go slay level 20 goblins for hours just to build up some faction! (I laugh, but I do plan to go do that soon.)

I would assume that this faction situation applies to evil races and their outpost as well, but can't say for sure, since I don't play an evil character. Oh yeah, I suppose the new faction is necessary for all those who've slain endless legions of dwarven guards, so they can have a fresh start, too...

Anyhow, the scenery is impressive, the wildlife and denizens are unusual, and it's just a lot of fun finally seeing new stuff! They really do need to get some sound effects made though, it's bizarre that so many of the mobs use the default human sounds; I keep thinking one of my companions has been slain! If you've not gotten your CD yet, for whatever reason, you're in for a treat. Oh...and you DON'T have to be level 49+ to go there, as a lot of people seem to think. Kunark is for everyone, even if some of the low-mid level spots are challenging to get to. (In fact, some of the low-mid level mobs drop items that make many currently prized items look like trinkets from a gumball machine.)

I do thank Verant for putting armor and weapons in Kunark that make tanks effective for more than just taunting mobs off of spellcasters! There are some items that are so nasty that if equipped with one, a tank could...*gasp*...solo!

This is my only spoiler tidbit. Don't get a gate to Kunark, take the boat to Firiona Vie! Not only is it a dangerous journey to town, but you're missing one helluva neat-looking ship!

I hope to see you there soon.
