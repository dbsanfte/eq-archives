Ask First

We've all been here...on one side or the other.

So you've just killed a mob and the rush of the fight is fading. You're talking a bit with your groupmates, perhaps you're trying to remember who's turn it is to loot, perhaps the looter is A.F.K. (Away From Keyboard). Total side note. Hey Verant, isn't the purpose of an acronym to abbreviate something and make it shorter? So why do you spell it out AND use the acronym? Excuse me, I had to step A.F.K. (Away From Keyboard) because the UPS (United Parcel Service) guy was here with a part for my PC (personal computer) that I ordered from IBM (International Business Machines). Now where was I?

Oh yeah. So that mob has sat there unlooted for a moment at the feet of your group. Along comes some grabby Gus and loots it! Right out from under your nose. The sheer gall. I'm terribly sorry jerkoff, but you could ASK (no, that's not an acronym). It's a verb, meaning to request permission, to seek authorization, to obtain consent or denial. To be polite.

Yeah, we were just standing here, that mob just fell from the sky, really. No, you earned the plat coins and FS weapon just because you walked by. Not like we killed it or anything, or deserve to loot it.

Do you walk by someone's table at Mc Donald's and snag their Big Mac because they don't happen to be chewing on it at that moment? Maybe not, but I bet you'd snatch a handful of fries, dumbass.

It might surprise some people, but even high level folks need money. Just because they don't loot something within a nanosecond of killing it doesn't mean they don't want it. And even if they don't want it, it doesn't mean they want you to have it. Sure, sometimes people happen to be around an
unlooted mob that they didn't kill. But I guarantee you 99.999% of the time, if you see a mob at someone's feet, they killed it and just haven't looted it yet. Just because the priviledged loot time has expired doesn't make it right for you to walk up and grab someone else's earned loot. Ask permission first.

I'm going to continue to beat a dead horse here (before looting it). This instance of loot snatching is mostly prevalent in newbie zones where people hunt guards. Low level characters seem to have this idea that high level players don't need loot, and are obligated to help them out. (check my
archives for the Intro to Begging column)

>From my experience, ogre players are particularly prone to this ill-mannered habit because often many bouncer corpses lie around due to the fact that they have a lore item on them that doesn't get looted. One particular lootsnatcher I can think of, used to beg and beg for a weapon or two.
Several days running. Like we didn't remember that a party member felt pity on you yesterday and let you have one? Oh and then, this takes the cake; on another day Mr Green Con Ogre On Anonymous, I happen to see you in another zone, not naked, not on anonymous, and find out you're 30th level! How freakin cheapskate is a person who walks out of town naked and anonymous pretending to be a newbie and begging? Holy cow, have some pride, good God
Almighty.

Anyhow, to recap. Other people aren't playing to make YOU rich. Even if they happen to not immediately loot something, that doesn't make it right for you to grab it out from under them. Ask permission first.
