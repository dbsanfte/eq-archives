Last night, DB played Acme Clan in an OGL LMCTF match.

I just like to take the time to comment on our OGL LMCTF match against the ACME clan. I guess I'll start off by saying that these guys have to be the biggest bunch of sexually repressed idiots in the history of the human race. First we agreed to a 4 on 4 match on C's server. Well one ACME guy shows up and leaves, reconnects leaves, etc. His ping was about the same as mine was so he didn't timeout. Then Taz[ACME] gets on the server and obviously this idiot has seen one too many episodes of Judge Judy, yelling at us to get on their server "NOW" or we forfeit in two minutes. So as his little stop watch is ticking down, me and C begin to think...hmmm. Well they challenged us, they picked the map, and now they want to pick the server. I'm not really familiar with the OGL rules, I know reading 5 pages of rules about a computer game league gets some people off, but I guess I'm just weird. So we join the server and there are about 7 or 8 Acme guys arguing with Hitman about things they had already agreed to days before hand. Well with Hitman being the nice guy he is, he gives in and we are gonna play right? Wrong. We sit there for a good 10 -15 minutes while these guys decide who's going to play for them. They have no clue how many players the match was set for, but can remember the agreement on the server, please. We wait patiently, and guess what they end up with 3 guys with pings of 100 or less and one guy with a 250-300 I believe. Now C and Hitman's pings are up into the high 300's and they are on cable. I've got about 290-340 and some really bad fps and MacDixon had the same fps problem. So finally we start and well as expected they killed us. Now losing I can handle, but these guys felt the need to rub it in even though they had every advantage and were breaking the rules. It was 24 caps to none I think. Now I don't know what the problem was with these guys. Maybe they didn't get lucky at the prom, or get picked on in school, or are pissed off cause mommy and daddy forgot to tuck them in the night before. It was a total disregard for sportsmanship, you don't kick someone when they are down, its a basic rule of humanity. I play this game for one reason, that's fun. I have fun when I win or lose, but these guys have a hard on to win so bad that they completely ruin the appeal of the game.

Now to some of them, I apologize for this, there was one guy (forgotten his name) that stepped out when C disconnected, a few times. And they didn't talk any shit although they really didn't have to, sometimes actions speak louder than words. And when these guys play a good clan I hope they get the same thing, and I promise you they won't show the class that we did by not complaining.

Now I'm a fairly laid back guy, and don't take games all that seriously, however this match touched a nerve. I went out had a smoke and was over it, but thought I'd throw in my 2 cents on the match. Guess you have to deal with idiots in life, but you don't have to like it :0

Practice went really well on Monday night. All the DB teams were enthused and it was a learning experience for all! A lot of work and energy was spent by all members as they competed against the other teams with their strategies. Below, are some recaps for the practice:

My strategy was position based, with each position controlling a certain area and therefore a certain weapon. The positives of the strategy, in theory and in gameplay, is that the enemy's weapon choice will be greatly limited. Also, traffic throughout the level would be severely hindered by constant attacks. However, there were many flaws that I did not foresee. One, I counted on perfect attendance of all the team members. That was not good, one of them couldn't show, he had more important things to do. I tried to patch the place, but the positions were spread too thin. Second, although each position can help 1 or more, a lot of times people would be outnumbered because of team damage... often a person risks killing or hurting his own teammate. I think that this strategy would be great if thoroughly practiced and explained, but it would require usually more than 4
people, depending on the level though.
