Simple Pleasures

Most things that we use for comfort we take for granted because they are usually in our lives everyday. The way Larry can make me laugh hard enough to sound like a braying donkey. The 3 feather mattresses and 8 pillows I insist are absolutely necessary. The siamese cats that ensure I will never have a boring moment whether I like it or not. My parents whom I genuinely like (of course I love them but how many can claim to truly like as well) and enjoy talking to about whatever/whenever. My son who can melt me with a mere hug or kiss. I am lucky. These are my everyday comforts that make life bearable when it seems to be in one of those intermittent downward spirals. You know, those days when it rains it pours.

There are some pleasures though that because of their infrequency makes them special no matter how simple they seem. For me this happens to be “Little Cokes”. There’s a history here so let me explain:

Little cokes are Coca~Cola in the small 8 ounce glass bottles sold primarily in the south. When I was little going down to visit my grandparents (both sets) in Lancaster Kentucky was always a high point in itself. I was spoiled horribly (as my Mom will attest to and still does regularly) and being the only grandchild for a decade, rightly so. Little cokes were always in the frig and I associate those good times with them to this day. Christmas, summer vacations, the large family feeling. Funny how all these memories fit into one small bottle.

Once a year little cokes are sold as a Christmas promotion in the Chicago area. The other night Larry told me to close my eyes as he led me into the kitchen. When I opened my eyes before me sat a case of little cokes. I guess I’m pretty silly but the rush of good feeling and memories of good times the sight always brings me is hard to put into words. That I have someone who knows what it means to me makes me feel like the luckiest person on earth.

So here I sit, typing away next to my half empty little coke. I think of my uncles who use to tease me terribly by calling me “frog” (I have no idea why they did or why it bothered me so…lol). The huge paper Santa cut-out that was always welcoming us on the front door or the endless laughter caused by my great grandmothers choice of gifts she gave. In the summer, drives in my uncles current sportscar or walking up to visit my great aunt and uncle at the printing (newspaper) office or my other great aunt teaching me how to play backgammon at the Hotel Walker. Being prettied up by my beautician aunt at her salon, playing airplane in the livingroom with my other aunt or just fishing in my grandparents pond with by one of my grandmothers dobermans for company. It all comes back in a massive rush with just one sip of that magical little coke. A can, full sized glass bottle or two liter isn’t the same. Hell, I rarely drink soda of any kind, iced tea is my drink of choice except for when it’s little coke season.

My simple pleasure is best served ice cold with the endearing warmth of some of the best memories I have.

Stay safe and stay sane J

Cynder
