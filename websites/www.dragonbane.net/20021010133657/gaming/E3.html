IN GAMING TODAY.....
2001.06.07| E3's Best PC Games |by Sharac

The lineup of new pc games was one of the best in the convention's seven-year history! Below are the most impressive PC titles from among the several hundred on display on the show floor or behind closed doors. They are divided by genre and, unless specified, should be on the shelves within 18 months.

Action Games: Electronic Arts' Medal of Honor: Allied Assault was clearly a "Best of Show" contender for its nail biting WWII action. This demo had the crowd speechless as soldiers landed into the choppy waters off Omaha Beach.

Max Payne, from Gathering of Developers, is best described as The Matrix meets film nooir. In this gritty thriller, due to launch in just a few months, players assume the role of a fugitive undercover cop framed for a murder he didn't commit. Boatloads of cool moves, weapons and sticky situations to get out of.

Lastly, Activision's Return to Castle Wolfenstein is another WWII based 3D shooter but with lots of fiction added into the mix (seasoned PC gamers may remember the classic 1992 action game Wolfenstein 3D). Once again, players wear the combat boots of "B.J." Blazkowicz who is out to stop the super-villain Himmler, his supernatural creations and of course, Nazis.

Role Playing Games: LucasArts and Verant's Star Wars: Galaxies was shown behind closed doors and, while it's at least one year away from release, it's shaping up to be a jaw-dropping online-only, massively-multiplayer role-playing game. Imagine the game Everquest but with photo-realistic graphics and set in the massive Star Wars universe. Absolutely outstanding and another "Best of Show" for E3 2001.

Earth and Beyond is Electronic Arts' online-only space based RPG where players must explore, trade and conquer in a persistent world with thousands of other players simultaneously ("persistent" means all the events in the game continue to happen even after you've logged off.) In this intergalactic adventure, players can board their own spacecraft to engage in combat, commerce or both.

Dungeon Siege, an action/RPG hybrid from Microsoft, is shaping up to be the true Diablo-killer with its easy-to-get-into-gameplay, deep storyline and beautiful indoor and outdoor environments. Players can customize their party to include up to 8 unique characters, plus a free editor will ship with the game so players can create their own worlds.

Other promising role-playing games are LucasArts' Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic, BioWare's Neverwinter Nights and Bethesda's Morrowind.

Strategy/Simulation: Empire Earth. This astoundingly deep strategy game challenges players to control a civilization through 500,000 years of human history, from the discovery of fire to laser blasts and beyond.

Microsoft's Sigma: The Adventures of Rex Chance has an interesting premise: players use "bubble-gum biology" to mix and match parts of animals to create an army of mutants on an island. For instance, a beast with the arms of a gorilla and the backside of a skunk make for one strong animal that can climb trees. then send a deadly stench toward his enemies!

Other noteworthies include Microsoft's Age of Mythology, Electronic Art's Simsville, Blizzard's WarCraft III and Sid Meier's Civilization III.

(excerpted from Marc Saltzman, "At E3", Toronto Star, May 31, 2001)
