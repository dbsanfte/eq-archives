Dragon's Bane was officially formed on April 19th, 1998. It is a virtual community of gamers, known as the Dragon's Bane Online Gaming Group. Members of Dragon's Bane find "like minded" gamers within our community.

What do we Offer?
Our unifying theme is that we offer the "lone gamer" a community to belong to. While in-game clans and guilds generally exist around six months or so, Dragon's Bane has existed since 1998 and plans to be around for a long time in future.

Typically, our members will play a number of online games and often we move as a group into the latest online world.

We concentrate on those personal relationships as well as the games themselves. We have several members that do not actively play games. With the use of ICQ, Email, MIRC, and Message Boards we provide several ways to continue personal relationships outside the gaming atmosphere. When a game does come along that is good we generally get a good amount of participation.

So what games do we play?
We have organized leagues at this time in Quake 3, Dark Age of Camelot and Neverwinter Nights, however, some members meet up together to play other online games as well. In past, we have had Starcraft, Baulder's Gate, Quake 2, Everquest and Forsaken Leagues.

What do we offer the Gamer?
A) Stability. We are old and stable offering a secure world for a player to add value to and not have to worry that if they invest time it will vaporise when the guild/clan folds in a month.

B) Support: many members with good communications mechanisms (we use IRC, ICQ, email) means you can always find answers to questions, people to play games with and help with problems.

C) Events: We do a lot of events.

D) Security/Maturity: We offer a secure gaming environment away from the anti-social gamers that plague some areas/worlds. We attract a high quality of player, mostly adults, with a similar gaming outlook

E) Friendship: We aren’t about numbers or a specific game but about being a community so we offer friendship above and beyond the game. This has even led to some real life relationships and friendships.

Should I Join Dragon's Bane?
Due to the nature of our type of group, membership is not the casual adventure that an in-game guild is. There is a higher level of commitment involved in joining an OGG and even the joining procedures themselves are neither immediate nor simple form-filling.

We represents a section of the gaming community that wants to achieve the most out of its gaming, however, those who would join just to be part of a "bigger" group, or who think then can gain some immediate gaming advantage from this, are not suited to Dragon's Bane.
