What is your all-time favorite meal including dessert?
Starting with a Shrimp spring roll appetizer, with a good Chardonnay,.the dipping sauces would be a lightly spiced sweetened fishsauce or lightly spiced sweetened nut-shrimpsauce (both the sauces have Asian names but , currently elude me). A large fresh garden salad with a variety of leaf lettuce (everything from roman, iceberg, to spinach leaves) cut carrots, tomatoes, radishes, diced celery, and sprinkled with fresh Cilantro (of course).  Salad dressing would be a good Herb vinaigrette, or house Italian depending on the mood.  On the side would be a variety of Sushi and a plate of Kimchi.   The first part of the meal would have a dish of rice noodles set in front of you,..in the center table is a small oil hibachi where several plates of  seasoned meats everything from beef and pork to fresh walleye and squid is laid out to choose from.  Using your chopstick (or fork, depending on your manual dexterity) flash fry your selected meat dip in your selected sauce and eat with noodles. Plates of cut  mangoes, pineapple, beansprouts, fresh basil leaves, cilantro, endives, and lettuce are strewn around the table to add to your plate as desired (these are condiments much like relish is added to a hotdog). The main course is plate of shrimp and lobster stirfry with only certain vegetables (No baby corn or broccoli,... since when were these indigenous to Asia?,..hehe) cooked in a light chili garlic fish sauce.  This is all laid on bed of white rice. Water and a good Pinot is usually good to clean the palette.  I enjoy a Banana cream pie with a fresh strawberry sauce and topped with whip cream or ala mode.  Then relax with 30yr Single malt scotch, (Glen Morin, or a Walker blue label is good,... don't care too much for the coastal blends)
