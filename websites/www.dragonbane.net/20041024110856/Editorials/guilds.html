Guilds

FOREWORD: Any guildnames I may mention in this column are purely fictional. Any resemblance to guilds of Quellious, or any server or other game is purely coincidental.

There are a helluva lot of guilds in EQ, but what are they REALLY? What does your guild mean to you? How do you perceive other guilds? Think on these concepts a moment before continuing.

Some guilds have fancy codes of conduct and lofty ideals. Some guilds are just a handful of friends who want to share a chat channel. Some guilds are collections of people striving to make a whole that's more than the sum of its parts. And, some guilds are basically just a bunch of misfits banding together because no one else can stand them.

Is a guild a family? Is it a social club? Is it like a political party? Is it a mark of honor, of pride, of disposition? Is it some, all, or none of these things?

Let's proceed on the concept that a guild is like a family. Guildmates (especially in larger guilds) might not always personally like one another. Heck, in real-life families, not all people like each other. But even though you don't like Cousin Joe, are you proud because he got into a prestigious
college? Even if you think Aunt Jane is a total bitch, aren't you outraged that she got mugged on the street? Family goes beyond personal likes and dislikes; a guild should, too. Even if you don't particularly care for Joe Necro in your guild, you should cheer his accomplishments, and stand up for him if you can. A guild loses strength when members let their individual differences come between them. After all, if you let a guildmate down, aren't they likely to let you down next time? And if a guild is continually letting each other down, what good is it? Treat your guildmates like family; without mutual obligation and support, a guildtag is just words behind your name.

Now let's look at guilds from the outside. When you see a stranger sporting a guildtag you've never seen, what thoughts cross your mind? Do you feel positively toward them if the guildname is something noble or poetic? Do you look on them negatively if the guildname is something punkish, or dastardly? Speaking personally, I'm going to be more inclined to trust a member of the
Legion of Valhalla, than a member of the Throatslitters. Sure, it's just a guildtag, but that guildname means SOMETHING. It didn't just appear there by chance.

Now, if you're dealing with a stranger, but of a known guild (I'm not going to even address the concept of twinks of known players, that's a headache waiting to happen), how do you react? Do you look on them positively if it's a guild you have friends in or have had good experiences with in the past? Do you look on them negatively if it's a guild you've had negative dealings or bad experiences with in the past? I bet you do.

Is it wrong to look at a person and think "I know 10 members of that guild, and they're all jerks, he's probably a jerk too"? Perhaps. But again, I bet you do. I'll admit it, I do. A person is known by the company he (or she, sorry ladies) keeps. Guilt by association, you could call it. Granted, one
shouldn't judge a book by its cover, but first impressions ARE important.

Do you scold your guildmates when they've publicly acted like asses, or cheated people, or ninja looted, thus lessening your guild's reputation? If a person sends you a tell and says "Soandso your guildmate just screwed me over" do you ignore it, or look into it and attempt to see the right thing is done? Do you even care? You should. Again, people judge you by the company you keep. If you have a guildmate who's a bad seed and makes the rest of you look bad, everyone suffers.

I could go on posing different questions all day, but I'll stop here. I urge you all to examine the actions of yourself and your guildmates. The point has been made over and over that reputation is key at the higher levels in EQ. But the concept of reputation goes beyond you personally, and extends to your guild, too. If guildmates come down on you about something, don't just say "what the hell is your problem?" Because if you've done something bad, it doesn't only reflect on you, it reflects on them. By the same token, compliment your guildmates when they do something great. Too often, thanks are taken care of privately, and disputes handled publicly. If someone in your guild does something nice for you, thank them in guildchat, let everyone know! It seems silly, but you'll all be the stronger for it. And, isn't that something we join guilds for in the first place, to overcome our limits as individuals?

Enough babbling for one session. Now go forth and be excellent to each other. (Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventure is on cable, lol)
