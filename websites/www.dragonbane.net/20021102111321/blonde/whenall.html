When All Else Fails

I own a townhome, a house that is attached to 3 other units, and is governed by “the association” (a management company who’s job it is to care for the outside upkeep). My townhome is located on a short court thus making all the neighbors very, shall we say, nosey enabled. I am rarely seen by these neighbors so this doesn’t really bother me. I keep to myself, am respectful of the others who are in such close proximity and basically live and let live……unless you piss me off.

My immediate next door neighbors and I share a common wall. They play music (truly awful stuff) so loud I have to turn up my TV to be able to hear what I am watching. They have hammered nails in the walls at the most ungodly hours and have a penchant for leaving their fast food garbage outside so that it blows away usually over to my front and back steps. Needless to say, I don’t particularly care for them but I am a quiet shy chickie who would rather avoid confrontations. They are renters. They have been here for a couple years already, so they can’t be here forever, right?

Last week I literally snapped. Let me explain once again that I am quiet and shy. When I get angry I will stuff it down deep inside and it’s usually a long time and over much abuse that I actually lose it. When I do erupt it has been described as VERY SCARY due to my normal nature. I guess there is a major surprise factor in there.

Back to the story, my neighbors decided to jam out once again as I was watching a pay per view movie with my family. As the blaring sounds of “Beat It” drowned out the sounds of the movie a white hot throbbing started at the base of my neck and quickly spread to my brain. Could it be possible in all my non-confrontational behavior that they didn’t realize how thin the walls actually were here? Well I decided to show them just how bloody obnoxious living next to a crazed metal-head could be and how cool I had been since they had moved in and so graciously blessed me with themselves.

As my family watched dumbfounded (yes, my temper tantrums still shock them and they live with me) I proceeded to turn my stereo speakers to the wall. I then instructed Larry to go downstairs and do the same with his stereo. We then blasted them with the joyous combined sounds of  Macabre (a speed metal band who only writes songs about serial killers) and NON (a particularly annoying metal band that even I can’t listen to at length, you have to hear it to truly understand, just take my word for it). This lasted for 5 minutes, enough to give them a massive overdose of their own medicine. After shutting both stereos off you could hear a pin drop in the silence.

Yesterday my son ran in from playing outside to give me the blessed news that the neighbors were moving, go figure. I don’t flatter myself with the idea that I am the cause, maybe just one of the factors. I guess they want to go live where the neighbors were more respectful of others..……J
