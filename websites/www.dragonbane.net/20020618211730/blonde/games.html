Let the Games Begin

My first interest in the Olympics was way back in 1976. Being only 9 years old at the time I didn’t care about nationality or politics (I guess I still don’t come to think of it). I held my breath and watched a girl a little older than myself make world records. Nadia Comaneci was a wonder to watch and I can still remember it like it was yesterday. I wanted to be like her so badly but considering my accident prone-ness, it just wasn’t going to happen J

A year later I was lucky enough to be able to travel overseas and live in Munich Germany. I stayed at the Olympic Village and site of the 1972 Olympics. I swam in the very same pool that Mark Spitz did and got yelled at by a lifeguard (in German of course) when my bathing cap came off after a jump off the high dive. I didn’t know at the time about what actually happened in 1972 at the very place I was staying. I was a child. Terrorism and murder were the last things on my mind. It was only later after I had returned home that I saw a documentary, recognized the buildings and realized what horror actually happened there. I assume the adults didn’t mention it due to my age.

Watching the games now is very different. CNN reported that the opening night had the largest amount of viewers ever recorded. I wonder how many watched just to see if there would be another terrorist attack? (Sorry but my faith in my fellow man is in a downward spiral) I just wish the games could be one time when nothing else mattered but watching the best athletes our world has to offer. No controversies, no bad sportsmanship, no stupid politics, no our tragedy is worse than your tragedy, and best of all no threats or violence. Couldn’t this be the one time when we all get together and forget the BS of our differences and current events? They are called games after all. Doesn’t that mean fun or at the least some goodwill should be involved?

Enough of my ranting. I guess my favorite event so far has been the Snowboarding. The thought of doing it myself scares me when I think of long hospital stays that would be required in my case which is why it’s so exciting to me. The rush those boarders must feel has got to be intoxicating. Hats off to all of ‘em J

The event I have never been able to follow is Curling. I keep watching it and just when I think I have got the idea of what’s going on I realize I haven’t a clue. Someday I am going to have to do some reading up then maybe I will get it. I know if I tried it I would probably be in the same shape as if I tried to snowboard. I have a hard enough time on solid footing. Too slippery for a klutz like me.

In the end I really don’t care who wins. I enjoy watching talented people do wondrous things. I am happy for those who do get a medal. I am bummed for those who don’t, not because it matters to me, because I know it probably matters to them. I just like to watch the excellence all the athletes bring to the games. Hell these guys made it to the Olympics; they are all winners in my eyes J 

Stay safe and stay sane J

Cynder
