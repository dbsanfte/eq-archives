Spellcrafting Costs| by Hammerthought
I have received a few contacts from guildies asking about the cost of Spellcrafting an item. From the way the questions are being asked, it sounds like everyone is concerned that it will take hundreds of gold to Spellcraft each piece of armour or weapon or shield.

Here are the facts:

For a lvl 51 99% item, the maximum Supply cost one can incur is 60 gold**. However, you could spend as little as 3.3 gold and use up all 28 imbue points.

For a lvl 51 MP item, the maximum is 66 gold. Again, it could be as little as 3.6 gold.

A lvl 40 99%, max is 40 gold, min is 1.3 gold.

A lvl 40 MP, max is 52 gold, min is 2.3 gold.

A lvl 30 99%, max is 20 gold, min is 70 silver.

A lvl 30 MP, max is 30 gold, min is 1 gold.

A lvl 20 99%, max is 9 gold, min is 40 silver.

A lvl 20 MP, max is 13.5 gold, min is 52 silver.

So, you see. Spellcrafting is really quite cheap when looked at from a cost benefit standpoint. It appears that about 350 gold will completely Spellcraft an entire set of armour and weapons for a lvl 50 character.
The cost of the armour and weapon are MUCH more than Spellcrafting.

**NOTE: The prices given are for Supply cost only. They do not include Spellcrafter margin, high-con failure and losses, time constraints, quality specifications for overcharging, general irritability of the Spellcrafter or indecisiveness of the customer.
