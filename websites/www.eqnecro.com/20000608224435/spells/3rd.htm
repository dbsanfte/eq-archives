Dark Empathy

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 20

Speed : 4.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Heals other and damage self

Comments:

Skyshade - The only time you would want to use this spell is when someone is about to die. If you are in a party when someone is about to die, chances are you will be next in line. The last thing you want to do is loose precious HPs. But there are some situations where this spell can be useful. After a battle, a member of your party is poisoned and dangerously loosing life, you can prevent his death until the poison runs its course with Dark Empathy. You can also speed your or your party's healing process (but don't try to heal your tank...) 
Lifebane - I used this spell quite frequently before. Great way to aid in downtime with warriors in group. Note: Damage healed continues to go up with level indefinitely it seems. I heal around 45ish at level 50 per casting so this might be a percentage of your MAX HP that your are giving away... never have figured that out though. 
 Maiya Samsara - This is actually a very useful spell when you don't have a healer in a group. One person is down to 20% health, bandage them up and then give them your health until you and they are at the same level. Meditate and bingo, full health for everyone. In all caster groups you'll find yourself using this a lot.

Deadeye

Skill : Divination 

Mana : 35

Speed : 3.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Gives the caster the ability to see invisible and infravision

Comments:

Skyshade - Except for a fellow invisible player, you won't be seeing that many invisible creatures in Norrath. But the infravision aspect makes this a very useful spell in case your race does not already possess this ability. As you should know, there are places in Norrath where you can see very little at night even with a good light source. Thus, infravision will make your life a lot easier
Lifebane - Well can't forget the ever popular invisible kill stealers. I always have some form of see invisible up when I camp for an item. Never who who is sneaking around nearby. Oh yes, if you finish the Harvester quest, you will have this effect on constantly while you are carrying the item in your hand.

Impart Strength

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 15

Speed : 4.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Give 10 points of your own strength to other

Comments:

Skyshade - What strength are we talking about ? Most necromancers are fairly weak and can't really afford to give their strength to anyone. At least not without getting encumbered... 
Lifebane - limited use as a small strength buff for your pet. But make sure you are not encumbered when you do this or you may find yourself not able to get away if you have to run from a fight.

Playge - Just a short notice on the spell Impart Strength, you can remove the effects on yourself by simply clicking on the icon, you will immediately regain the strength that you lost and the person that you have cast it on gets away with being able to carry that extra 10.  Woohoo! No more waiting on your tanks to go sell items just because they are 3lbs encumbered. Since the spell costs so little mana its a great buff for everyone in your group.

Shadow Step

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 10

Speed : 3.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Mini random teleport to a different place within the zone you currently are
Comments:

Skyshade - To tell you the truth I used this spell very few times. And whenever I did cast it, nothing good came from it. Popular cast at a duel. 
Lifebane - This is your basic escape spell. Quick to cast and a bit random in its effects, this spell is still very useful. What it does is when you cast it, you teleport randomly away from where you were standing. Note: you are still in the same zone and your enemy will track you down and beat you once they catch up. The main use of this spell is that it gives you more time! Perhaps the precious seconds you need to get off the killing attack spell, the slower casting gate spell, or even get you a bit closer to the Zone. I have found myself in the middle of an ocean while running from a Sand Giant.. a bit disorienting sometimes. Too unpredictable for some, it is still a much better alternative to just standing there and becoming dead.  NOTE: Do not use in areas with long drops or lava *grin*.



Vampiric Embrace

Skill : Alteration 

Mana : 30

Speed : 3.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Randomly Drains life each time you deal melee damage to a creature

Comments:

Skyshade - It was pretty bad when I tested it, but might have improved a little bit since then
Lifebane - Great spell at lower levels. The random lifetap effect does increasing damage as you gain in levels with no apparent upper limit. And you can use this barehanded or with any weapon, effect will work regardless. Note: Dexterity is rumored to aid in getting the effect to happen more often.
 Maiya Samsara - Here's an example of a spell that, if castable on others, would really make Necromancers more tasty to groups. At level 20, Vampiric Embrace would fire for 35 points once or twice a battle. At 30 mana, one fire is mana efficient.

Mark - I usually tank more than I should, just to keep the pet alive...I have found

that Dark Pact with Vampiric Embrace is very useful....increases my mana

while I tank, then can step back and cast.

Barazar - I am a level 20 Necro. This spell for me works WONDERS with Dragoon Dirk. I usually get it to proc 3-5 times in a battle (pending on how many hp's the enemy has) and averaged about 35 pts per shot. Couple this with pet, Banshee aura (8hp every time he hits you), and the average damage of 12 from the dirk.... your enemy is going down fast.  Although I don't recommend you doing this vs. greater level NPCs than you. You are better off Dot'ing them and let the pet do the bashing.



Ward Undead

Skill : Evocation 

Mana : 30

Speed : 2.1

Damage : Maximum of 45 points of damage to an undead creature

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Undead only direct damage spell that only works against undead creatures

Comments:

Skyshade - Until reaching level 8, you are still relying on your pet to deal most of the damage while you do some occasional melee damage while casting Lifespike. Ward Undead changes that as it deals up to 45 points of damage to an undead creature. That is major damage for a necromancer and allows you to take some tough undead that until now seemed impossible. The only drawback with this spell is that it is the first to require the Evocation skill. Thus, if you do not spend practice points to improve this skill, Ward Undead will fizzle very, very often.
Lifebane - Woohoo! XP time for the young Necromancer! Go fight undead now and pay them back for all the beatings they have given you.  Skeletons and zombies are not too scary anymore.
