Cordelia Minster

18 - 22

Wizard Spires near Stone

Spells Used

Darkness, Blood, Fear, Heart Flutter, Dark Pact/Allure of Death, Drain Life spell

Strategy

Be careful at level 18 to 20 because Cordelia is harder than she cons.  She is a Bard, but although she does not use songs on you she hits like a monk.  But if you are careful she can be beaten at those levels.  If you do not want to risk death then wait until level 20 because from 20 to 24 she is pretty easy.

 

Basically you use the standard DoT-Pet-Fear-Nuke strategy. First hit her with Darkness and then run a little ways to get her away from the Spires and Tower.  If you do not do this then there is a chance that the Patrolling Guard Fredrick will come along and he will help Cordelia if he sees you fighting her.  If you want to be even more cautious pull her all the way to the West Karana zone border.  It is not too far and will provide a good escape route, but you will need to practice a few times to learn exactly where the border is.  After you pull her to your spot send in the pet and start applying the DoTs.  I used Heat Blood, Heart Flutter, Darkness and Poison.  By now she is on top of you and very angry.  Cast fear until it sticks then nuke her with you best Lifesuck spell.  I did not try conserve mana here, I just wanted to kill her before fear wore off because she hits pretty hard.

 

Up until level 20 I had to use Shieldskin and my other buffs every time and even then it was close.  You may also need to feign death sometimes to get her off of you.  But from 20 on none of that is needed.  I also use Dark Pact (or Allure of Death if you have it) right before every fight to get mana back during the fight. You are Lifedraw/Siphoning anyway and once when the fight was close the extra mana let me cast one more Lifedraw and that saved my life.

 

The loot she drops varies a lot but on average is pretty good.  She drops a lot of Raw-Hide armor which is worth around 7gp to the gypsies in the zone.  She also drops Words and Pearl Shards but not very often.  The money loot ranges from 12cp to 2.5pp. Not bad for her level.

 

But the best thing about her is the XP.  I would guess that you get about 1 bubble of exp for killing her 6 times.  And since she spawns about every 5 minutes you can level pretty fast here.  If you run out and get a Scythe Beetle in between pulls the XP is really fast.  I camped her off and on for a few days and got very good XP and in all that time I never had to share her with anyone.  I guess only necros are willing to take the faction hit for killing her. (-Antonican Bards, -Qeynos Guards, -Knights of Truth, +Ring of Scale, +Mayong Mistmoore).

 

One more thing, she spawns right next to the Wizard Spire and stands there for a few minutes.  But if you do not attack her fast enough she takes a tour of the zone.  Basically she follows the path around the zone and finally stops just outside of the building with the 2 bartenders in the Northwest corner of the zone.  So if you miss her at the spires just head due north until you see the building on the path and wait.  She will soon walk up and stand next to the tree that is standing there.

Additional Comments
Manren (Prexus) Her level varies widely.  At 19 she conned blue to red.  Her loot varies widely from almost nothing to over 2pp in coin.  Very seldom did she drop anything other than coin.  Bears and highland lions will jump into the fight if they get close enough.

Cordella hits very hard so if a wandering mob jumps in, you'll have to be very quick to fear it or your pet will die quickly.  The last time I hunted her here's what happened.  She conned red to me but I had a good pet so let's rock. ED her, resist.  Run to pet and send it in.  ED her, sticks. Another DOT while she's tangling with pet.  She comes for me, fear her, sticks. Fear lasts just about 5 seconds, she's back on me.  Fear her again, sticks, Heart Flutter.  Pet getting beat up badly and fear isn't sticking for long.  Couple of lifetaps and OOM. Med up, fear gives out and pet dies.  She's on top of me, FD...

Now the wild part is that she stands where she was when you FD for a long time.  Get up, sneak away, invis and med up.  Cast pet, get to 100% and go back for her.

Round 2.  ED, dot and fear are working on her. Good, mind you she's the same level as before, pretty deep red.  Fear wears off, hit it again, the fight is taking quite a bit of time so the dot's are wearing off.  Fear her again and her and the pet take off.  OOM so I sit to med.  Few seconds later I go up over the hill to see how the fight is going and there's Cordella putting the spank to my pet while a grizzly gnaws on his leg.  Rut Roh!!!  Then the sound of bones falling on the floor and it's FD time since Cordella is still well over 25%.  So at this point I've got a problem.  I've used my best strategies on her and my best pets to no avail.  And since she isn't being hunted by anyone else she's gonna be around, and still deep red, until I or someone else kills her. I thought about asking someone higher level to smoke her for me but I didn't think they'd want the faction hit. With this info combined with my 2 failed attempts and the hour I spent trying... well... EK here I come for those last 2 bubbles to 20.  Safe, guaranteed XP was the way to go at that point.

So, I completely agree with the HG that she's very killable at 18 and 19 but her level varies so much she's also very dangerous too.  At 20 with the new pet and improved spells I'd imagine she'll be quite a bit easier and I'll let you know how that goes for me.
Sylphe I found that sticking a rusty dagger and a shield on my pet really helped at level 19.  I died once at this level because I forgot to memorize Fear.  Whoops.

Levels 20-22 are a walk in the park with this mob, open up with Darkness, if it sticks lay on a few more DOTs while heading back to the pet.  Once she comes in range, send in the pet, toss on fear, and Lifetap away.

Level 22 is a different story. The XP isn't all that great, but you can solo her without a pet.  Pull with Darkness, hit her with Fear, then layer in all the DOTs.  If she breaks Fear, recast it until it sticks, then pound away with the Lifetaps.  Even a blue con can be tough without a pet, especially if she resists anything.  One blue con resisted Darkness, Fear and Heat Blood.  I just feigned and waited for her to wander off.  On even pulls, just light up Shieldskin, and Allure of Death and cook away.
