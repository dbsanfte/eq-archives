Strategy

Crocodiles are non-aggro, so you can kill one, and their brothers and sister will not join in.  The only aggro mob near the crocs, are the Dry Bone Skeletons (18-20) which wander in from the sand dunes, pumas which come and claw you while you med, and occasional Hill Giants that comes to the coast and tries to kill everyone if nobody is standing guard.  Also, there is a Spectre island which is home to 4 Spectres, which you can see from the coast.  They will not come on their own, but when people pull them to kill them, they might not be able to finish one off, and it will make its way up the coast wreaking havoc on all, so be careful.

 

A real fun place to hunt, and is pretty safe if you are careful. A good place to rest, is on the side of one of the mountains in the water (rock 1 and 2). Rarely will you ever get killed, meding on the rock. I usually use this as a bind point.

Additional Comments
Kaalan Elgluth The guide is essentially correct although the XP level is a bit low. The XP here is great - the time is generally boring and there is no loot except for the occasional word or rune. The deepwater crocs, however, can go all the way to level 21.  I am 21 and still see the occasional white Deepwater Crocodile.  At 21 the deepwater crocs and deepwater caimans are all blue to me.

I use a slightly different approach when dealing with the amphibians.  I draw with Darkness, sic pet on them, and then hit the critter with heart flutter.  It reduces attack and AC (and makes your pet happy).  Then I immediately cast Fear on the mob.  It runs around getting bashed by the pet. You can either jump in and bash yourself, cast Lifedraw (or Siphon Life), or keep fear ready in case it turns again.  The good thing about fear, is that it seems the the mob never jumps in the water.

The only problem with this area is that it is generally very heavily camped.  I typically camp down near pillar 2.  The area is a little larger and even though it seems very camped, many times the big groups are waiting for Deepwater Goblin Pets.
