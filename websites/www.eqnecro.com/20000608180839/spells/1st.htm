Cavorting Bones

Skill : Conjuration

Mana : 15

Speed : 5.0

Damage : 

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : 

Effect : Summons a level 1 skeleton that becomes your pet

Comments:

Skyshade - What would a necro character be without a pet? An easy prey that is.  This is essentially the first spell a necromancer should buy as soon as he has enough money for it.  Unfortunately, it is not among the spells you start with

 Reionik - Buy this spell first. You should never hunt without a pet at lower levels.  You want to make sure you watch his life while you hunt.  Once he gets to about 50% health, rush the monster and take some damage.  Once you are hurt back up cast your life drain and get back into the fray.  This is the necromancer's biggest plus at lower levels.  We are the only ones that get pets at first level, so use it.

Lifebane - Kill, steal, rob, etc.... do what you must but get this spell quickly!

Coldlight

Skill : Conjuration

Mana : 15

Speed : - 2.0

Damage : -

Research or purchase : Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Creates a lightstone that when held in one of your inventory slots will make it create a dim light that makes it a little easier to see at night

Comments :

Skyshade - The light created by Coldlight is very poor, but it might help you find your corpse if you die and have no light source.  Do not rely on Coldlight as your light source though as lanterns and lightstones are a lot better.

Tarnaris - A great use for this spell is to continuously cast over and over again... preferably once you are level 4 and can meditate.  This is a great way to keep Conjuration maxed since most people rely on Drain spells more than disease attacks.

 Reionik - The best to max out Conjuration.  I have really found no other use for it (I play a Dark Elf so I have Ultravision :)  .

Lifebane - Never really used this as a Dark Elf. But it would be useful in finding your dimly glowing corpse in a dark forest *grin*. 

Disease Cloud

Skill : Conjuration

Mana : 10

Speed : - 1.5

Damage : 5 DD

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Makes an opponent diseased if successfully cast, dealing an initial 5 points of damage to your target

Comments:

Skyshade - Disease Cloud has a secondary effect that prevents the diseased creature from healing.  Even though at first it might not seem like much, even 1HP can make the difference once you are near death and petless.  Useful in battles that last longer

Lifebane - This Level 1 spell is quite useful for the first to fourth level caster.. then of course many people move on to bigger and better things.  I for one used it up through level 27. The purpose you ask? Ahhh.. what is the main effect of Disease Cloud.. 5 points of damage? No, no, no. The Disease Cloud prevents your target from healing (think of mummy rot)! When you fight a strong opponent, their healing ability can gain them back 50 or more hp depending on the creature. I for one have killed many a creature with barely a hair of life health on myself. Those 50 points that the creature did not heal due to disease made a difference. If the creature had 50 more points I would have been face down in the dirt... very dead. And for the mana cost of a 5 to cast.. the results are well worth the cost.

Reionik - Helps once you start fighting bigger monsters.  At lower levels its pretty worthless since most monsters die in a few hits =) .  Once you start fighting those bigger monsters cast it, because it  makes it so they don't regenerate hit points back.  It will allow you and your pet to tackle harder monsters than most at your level.

 

 

Invisibility versus Undead

Skill: Divination

Mana: 40

Speed: - 4.0

Damage: -

Research or Purchase: Purchase

Research Material: -

Effect: Makes you invisible to undead creatures

Comments:

Skyshade - You will not use this spell very often, but it might prove very useful if you are in a zone heavily populated by undead creatures such as Kithicor.  It is also imperative that you have it if you are an Erudite or a Gnome and want to get to Neriak's necro guild.  The ghouls guarding the area do not like Erudites and Gnomes and will attack you on sight.

Lifebane - It is a limited invisibility which works against only the eyes of undead. A few notes on this though. First, higher level undead can see past this without any problems, so before passing a powerful undead.... con it... if it is anything but indifferent, it can definitely see you! Second, your pet is undead... if you go invisible to undead, your pet will kill itself *mumbles about making this mistake a couple times*. Many times undead are accompanied by Necromancers... these are not undead so will not be fooled by your tricks... beware! This is however an excellent spell to assist in exploring undead type dungeons and the all too common corpse recovery in places like Befallen, Unrest, and Najena. It is one of the few Necromancer spells we can cast on others. 

 Reionik - If you ever plan to make it through Kithicor you will need this spell =).  This also comes in handy in undead dungeons like Befallen and Unrest.  When I hunt either of those dungeons I don't even zone in without it being cast on me.  Its the best way to avoid those pesky undead trains.  Remember though, that if you are hit you will become visible to everything.  So watch out for those non undead roaming the zone.



Lifetap

Skill : Alteration

Mana : 9

Speed : - 1.5

Damage : 3 to 5 HP

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Drains a max of 5 HP from target and adds to self

Comments:

Skyshade - The second pillar of necromancers spells, Lifetap is the first spell from the Drain Life series.  Extremely useful, it will keep you alive longer in battles as you can take some hits, back off while your pet fights the creature and replenish your HP.  Then go back into melee combat and repeat the same process.  A must have and you will as it is one of the two spells a necromancer starts with

Reionik - Dear Lifetap how I love thee.  This little spell saved my little dark Elf arse many a day.  This will cause damage to a monster while healing you.  This is the second spell you must buy.  Rob, steal, and beg you way to getting this spell.... This is a must have.

Lifebane - Get it and get used to using it. Will be an important part of keeping your Necromancer alive throughout your necromancer career. 


 Locate Corpse

Skill : Divination

Mana : 5

Speed : - 1.5

Damage : -

Research or Purchase: Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Turns your body towards the nearest corpse from target player (either you or anyone else)

Comments:

Skyshade - Very useful in large areas as you can safely go back and find the place where that nasty rat just killed you.  To make it work, select the player you want to locate the corpse (F1 to target self) and cast the spell.  Then just keep going in the direction you have been turned until you get to your corpse.  It is also a mean to raise money as some necros charge others to locate their corpses

 Reionik - This is a must have at early levels.  This is how I made my living at my newbie levels.  It also comes in handy when you are in a large unfamiliar zone.

Lifebane - Great spell! very useful in corpse recovery in large areas like West Karanas or the Feerott! And make a few coins with it, after all, you are performing a service for them *grin*.

Speed: - 2.5

Damage: -

 Reclaim Energy

Skill : Conjuration

Mana : 5

Speed : - 2.5

Damage : -

Research or Purchase: Purchase

Research material : -

Effect : Unsummons your pet for 1/4 of the mana used to cast it back

Comments :

Skyshade - The only good thing about Reclaim Energy is a rather important one at times.  Sometimes you cast a pet that does not have the level you are looking for.  Specially at level 12 when some pets have magical attack abilities and some don't, Reclaim Energy is pretty useful to dispose of a bad pet.  Thus, always con your pet and see what level is he.  If you are not satisfied, reclaim him and cast again. You will loose the bone chips though...

Lifebane - If not too worried about the mana, and of course you are a dark elf, just hide. Pet will die, recast it and hope for better pet this time. 
Smart? maybe not. Lazy? Yeppers! Don't have to sit and forget a spell then memorize reclaim energy then cast and forget reclaim energy and
re-memorize summon pet spell... bleh! 


 Sense the Dead

Skill : Divination

Mana : 5

Speed : - 2.0

Damage : -

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Turns your body in the direction of the nearest undead

Comments :

Skyshade - Useful for times when you are looking for something to kill or simply trying to locate skeletons to replenish your bone chip inventory.

 Siphon Strength

Skill : Alteration

Mana : 5

Speed : - 1.5

Damage : -

Research or Purchase : Purchase

Research Material : -

Effect : Steals strength (5 points initially) from target and adds to self  for the next five minutes

Comments :

Skyshade - This spell can also be used as a pull spell, reducing the strength of a creature before you engage in combat.  Once you are past level 4, this spell becomes rather useless.

Lifebane - A minor spell... Learned by all apprentice necromancers while in their first rank of studies. When you cast this spell, you draw some of the strength of your enemy and give it to yourself. The amount increases as your levels increase up to a maximum of 10 points. First, the weaker your enemy, the lighter they hit. Second, we are weak compared to the warriors of the land, as such we frequently become encumbered. Now, cast this upon any enemy and steal their strength.... you now have the additional strength to carry a but more weight without tiring. And as a note, this spell has never been resisted that I have seen, so it is useful for many levels.
