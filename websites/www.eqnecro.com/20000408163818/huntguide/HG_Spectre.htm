Strategy

Before I explain how you solo a Spectre, it is important to understand this critter and the surrounding area you will be fighting.  Spectres are mean creatures and hit hard and fast.  They also stun very often and the few seconds you remain under his scythe may be deadly.  Thus, you have to avoid close combat with it as much as possible.  The best way to take a Spectre is to pull with Darkness and follow with a pet attack command.  If Darkness is resisted, back away and cast it again until it stays.  As soon as it does, cast Invoke fear.  If Fear is resisted, back away and cast it again until you get it off.  Once you got both spells in, go for quick damage.  Venom of the Snake, Expel Undead, and an occasional Drain Spirit in case you were hurt during the battle.  Reapply Invoke Fear when its effect wears off.

 

A few comments.  Don’t forget to cast a pet buffing spell before battle.  Don’t forget to keep Harmshield and Feign Death memorized. Feign Death will often full them and is a lifesaver in certain situations.  Keep Root memorized.  You may need it before running.  Don’t attack Roror, the named Lizard that often shows up near the Spectre spawn.  He will kill a level 50 and won’t even be scratched.  And finally, the spot where this Spectre spawns is just in front of the cave that leads to the Plane of Fear.  Inside the cave you can find a bunch of other Spectres.  One thing that commonly happens is having the Spectre head to the cave entrance after a Fear spell.  Don’t let him go that way.  Most of the times, you can make him turn back by dealing any type of damage.  So make sure you send your pet and cast as quickly as possible.  If he doesn’t turn back, run to Rathe Mountains zone at loc +550 +3000.

 

XP is very good but loot could be better.  Spectres drop a few plat every now and then and Scythes most of the times.  Rusty Scythes are crap, Bronze Scythes are worth about 2 plat and Scythes about 10 plat.  Spectres are a good target for those looking for level 34 Words.
