Ocean of Tears Zone Guide

 


This expanse of sea known as the Ocean of Tears is full of many creatures to tackle and quests to fulfill. Most players simply know it as the zone between Freeport and Butcherblock Mountains, but the Ocean of Tears is far from just a boat ride.  I have broken the Ocean down into the known islands.

 

Zachariah Reigh Island (Loc -2000, 7500)

This is the boats first stop when heading to Freeport from Butcherblock.  Nothing much to this island except for three houses and a few NPCs that will not talk to a necromancer but don't KOS either.  Inside the western most house, you will find a vendor that sells food, water and buys anything you have to sell.  The only other site on the island is an Orc Spire like those found in other zones though no Orcs reside here.

 

Twin Tines (Loc -1200, +2000)

Four Aqua Goblins and two Sirens reside here, all around level 10.

 

Allizewsaur Island (Loc -3300, -500)

A few Island Goblins of very low level inhabit this island.  It is also the home of Allizewsaur, the large Dinosaur-like creature that kills everyone on sight.  Allizawsaur spawns and can be commonly found here, but it also often travels around the Ocean of Tears.

 

Grey Isle (Loc -800, -6700)

Ever wonder what very high level PC's are doing in this zone?  Well, more likely than not they are at the Grey Isle as a trio of Spectres reside here.  They stand or float next to a spire on top of the island and are in the upper 30s.  The island's other inhabitants are gargoyles ranging in level from 19-22.  Good hunting on the gargoyles, but be sure to keep some distance from the Spectres.

 

Oracle of K'arnon (Loc -600, -8200)

This island is overrun with Island Goblins ranging in levels from the Shamans (16) to the Warriors (low to mid 20s).  The Oracle Island isn't that good for solo play, but a good group would do well.  Other occupants of note are the Oracle of K'Arnon (level 35) and his protector (level 40).  They do not KOS necromancers and will talk to you freely.  They will actually give you a quest, but it seems to be broken.  The main tactic employed against them is to levitate the whole party and then cast from off shore where the guard cannot reach you.  This tactic has been successfully used by much lower level characters then the Oracle.  Another common tactic is to have a tank pull the Paladin (the Oracle's protector) and run away SoW'd.  That leaves the rest of the party with the Oracle, an easy battle.  Once the Oracle is dead, your tank can either zone or bring the Paladin back to the island.

 

Pirate Isle (Loc +1600, -6200)
This island holds the pirate clan on the northeast point of the island.  Beware that even if considered green, all will come if one is attacked.  They range in level from 16 to low 20s.  The Captain sometimes carries the Bracer of Erollisi.  Gornit occupies the shore near the pirate camp and Seafury Cyclops roam the hills.

 

Mudtoe Island (Loc +1300, -7900)

The easiest way to get to this island is to get off the boat at its first stop after leaving Freeport to Butcherblock.  Then head to your left and swim to Mudtoe Island.  Once you get to Mudtoe, you will see a small ruined keep.  This tower is the dwelling place of a High Elven vendor who will not speak to necromancers.  She is a popular stop for necromancers though since she sells the level 29 pet (among other spells useable by other classes). How do you buy the spell if you can't talk to her?  Either ask a friend to buy it for you or create a new character for this task.  The Mudtoe Brothers also reside on this island and are part of a dwarven quest.  Finally, a Cyclops roams the island.  Be careful!

 

Sisterhood Isle (Loc +1200, -9400)

This is the first boat stop when leaving Freeport to Butcherblock.  It is a very nice hunting spot for a necromancer. I headed here upon gaining level 12 and bound on the western edge of the island.  Avoid the docks and houses here as we are KOS.  On the southern edge of the island, you will find a tent and a Dwarven vendor, he won't attack if you don't talk to him, but will if you do or click on him.  The eastern part of the island is the safest to hunt.  The sisters of Erollisi roam the hills.  Pull them down to the shore and keep your pet guarding one spot so he doesn't wander off.  You can gain experience and loot her from level 12 to 18.  Once you reach mid 20s, you can start hunting the other NPCs that spawn on the island.  Two excellent targets are located close to the docks.  The first is on the docks itself and the second is next to a tree, just behind the docks and in front of the hut with 2 NPCs and the vendor.  Both are around level 26 and can be pulled alone.  There are quite a few other NPCs you could hunt.  The hut with the vendor has 2 NPCs that can be pulled if you are high level enough (around 33) or hunting with a partner.  The other hut close to the docks, the smaller one has two more NPCs.  Same case here, but the battle should be a little easier.  The prizes are Purity Belts, which sell to NPCs for 1.5 platinum piece.  In addition, a few NPCs drop a long sword that can be sold for about 8 plat to NPCs.  Great hunting area for necros and hardly anyone here as there are negative faction hits with the Champions of Faydark.

 

Aviak Island (Loc +1100, 3300)

Another good hunting spot for a necromancer. The Aviaks here are indifferent to you and don't KOS.  In addition, there is no faction adjustment for killing them.  Similar tactics here, pull to the shore and have your pet guarding one spot so he doesn't wander.  The Aviaks range in level from low teens to low 20s.  I was easily able to take whites, yellows and some red ones here, with up to 3 platinum in loot.  A named Aviak, Gull Skytalon, spawns infrequently and is level 25.  He drops Fine Steel weapons.

 

The Ocean of Tears is a great place to hunt and loot for a necromancer in the low teens all the way to level 50 and should not be overlooked. Besides that, it still is a fairly unpopulated zone in most servers.

Recommended Levels: 12 through 50

 

Creatures: Aviaks, Aqua Goblins, Sirens, Spectres, Cyclops, the Oracle

 

Common Loot: Common weapons, gems, Scythes, Words

 

Train Likelihood: Low to medium

 

Merchants: Several merchants located in the different islands

 

Special Items: Robe of the Oracle, Ring of the Ancients

 

Linked Zones: Freeport and Butcherblock Mountains

 

Things you should know: If you fall from a boat, you will not be able to get back in.  You will have to swim to the nearest island and wait for the next boat to show up.  Which might take some time since each boat trip takes about 20 minutes.  Also, you should not attempt to zone by swimming since that is not possible.  Finally, to move from one island to the other, check for little boats you can operate. That might avoid a surprise Shark attack since many live close to island docks.
