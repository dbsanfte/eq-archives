Strategy

Deputy Felp is located on top of the wall that divides Misty Thicket in two.  Once you zone from Rivervale (and you must be invisible at all times to make your first pull), follow the road until you get to a passageway guarded by two Deputies, Deputy Asler and Deputy Keld.  Leave them alone.  Do not cross the passageway but rather turn right and climb the wall in the first possibility you have. 

 

Once you are on top of the wall, look for Deputy Felp.  He should be close by.  If not, he will be running around killing creatures, Runnyeye’s side.  Once you find him, get down and move to safe enough distance to cast a pet and buffs.  The best place to med and heal is inside the tunnel where the zone to Runnyeye is located.  Every now and then you will get "trained" by some green trash, which in the end is pretty good.  You help out the newbies in the zone and can ask for their help later on.  Will go back to this further ahead.  When ready, target Felp and cast Darkness.  Send your pet and repeat Darkness if necessary.  Follow with Blood, and then you have a choice of Shock of Poison or a Drain Life spell.  Decide depending on amount of damage and resist rates.  Do not use Fear unless you are far enough from the other Deputies.

 

This strategy is a little different once you are past level 30 or so.  At this level, you can boost your XP by hunting without a pet.  Pull with Darkness and start running towards Runnyeye’s tunnel.  Stop occasionally to cast a DoT or a DD spell.  Keep going till you get close to the tunnel.  Then stop and cast a Drain Life spell or Shock of Poison.  You can also cast Fear to avoid the running around.  He will die quickly and in the end you will be close to Runnyeye’s tunnel.

 

Finally, at level 34 you can use a third strategy.  Instead of pulling with Darkness, Root Felp and start casting DoTs.  He will eventually get rid of Root.  As soon as you see that happening, either reapply Root or start running to Runnyeye's tunnel.  Sometimes you can run and even though he will be on your tail, he won't be hitting you, will be taking DoT damage, and you are moving to a safer location.

 

Other Deputies can be pulled as well.  Check Deputy Asler/Keld Hunting Guide.

 

A few comments.  A different Deputy roams around Misty Thicket.  He is around level 27 and will attack you if he sees you.  As you might imagine, dealing with two Deputies at the same can be very tricky and you will have to run.  Also, a fourth Deputy is found along the wall.  Deputy Budo is around level 40 and will kill you very very quick if he sees you close by.  He spawns on the opposite side to Felp's spawning location.  Don't go there.

 

XP is not that good anymore.  Deputies spawned every 4 minutes or so, but after the newbie guard buff patch, their spawn time was increased to 20 minutes! Anyway, they always drop a Spear (8 to 9 plat), a Shield (1 plat), two worthless rings, and up to three platinum pieces.  That means at least 10 plat per kill, pretty good loot.  And I said I would return to the newbie issue.  Well, Spears and Shields are very heavy items.  You won't be able to carry many of them without going above your weight limit.  So, make some newbie friends and pay them a few plat for a short trip to a vendor.  They will be very happy with it and you will be free of a heavy load.

Additional Comments

Nergal Teir'Draconis (Tunare)

In the mid to upper 20s (25+) Felp can easily be taken if you pair up with a fellow Necro. Here's the 411. The Setup: Felp spawns near the second tower (the one with the Bixies in it) from the gate on the north side. I've never been able to target Felp on the other side of the wall, so we came up with an alternative method. First  the two of you summon up pets and park them near the big stump between the wall and orc camp. For safety sake I suggest parking on the side away from the wall. Blixtin (level 25 halfling thief NPC) has been known to aggro through the wall. The Pull: Cast Intensify Death on the pet if you wish. Now, go down to the tower Felp spawns at. Have one necro sit up hill from the tower, while the other stand down by the wall. Felp will aggro through the wall (I suggest having Leatherskin up for this since he hits hard, but its not really necessary). As soon as Felp aggros, start running in a big circle. The necro parked on the hill then hits Felp with Screaming Terror until it sticks (I've rarely seen him resist it). Both of you then run back towards the pets to a safe distance and cast darkness (he seems to resist it quite a bit, if you both cast you have a better chance of it sticking), then run to the pets and stick them on Felp. and run back to a safe distance to nuke. Don't bother with DoTs, he'll drop to fast. The Fight: Felp will either stick on the pets, or be sufficiently mad at one of you to run after you. If he does stick on the pets...he'll come after you as soon as you SoP him. Now is where you're glad Darkness stuck. Work out a code with your partner to tell them when Felp comes after you (I hotkey '/g ME'). Whoever Felp decides to chase needs to run in a wide circle while the other necro nukes. When the nuking necro has done enough damage (probably 1 SoP), Felp will turn on them. The nuking necro gives the signal and starts running in a wide circle, the previously running Necro stops and starts nuking. Rinse and repeat. All the while the pets are chasing him around beating on him. You don't even need to turn off pet taunt. If you're down HPs from the pull, substitute a few Lifetaps for SoPs. If you're really lucky you can get Felp in aggro-pong, where he keeps running between the two of you but never gets to anyone because he's switching targets every time a nuke hits :  The Loot: Personally, I give the first shield I loot to my pet, keep the second (in case I lose my pet) and destroy everything else except the spears. Selling the loot is a pain though. If there are 2 of you, one can make a mule to do the selling, or you can just bribe a newbie. Every now and then you will get a super-Felp that likes to resist spells. It shouldn't cause to much trouble, but I'd suggest keeping Feign Death mem'd just in case. The gate guards can be taken using the same approach, the difficulty is in getting them separated.
