And the Winner is
C.H.A.M.P.S.



The Second Annual Gateway Cowboy Action Shootists Society's was a huge success but the true winner was C.H.A.M.P.S. All proceeds from the match went to this not-for-profit organization dedicated to serving children and adults with physical disabilities in the St. Louis area through the placement of highly skilled service dogs. Over $5,200 was raised which will be applied towards the costs of raising, training and placing special service dogs wtih children and adults with physicial disabilties.

These special canines provide companionship and offer physical assistance with activies of daily living, thus helping their human partners lead a life of greater independence. The emotional bond that develops between the two is life affirming!

 Scores

Over 90 shootists, men, women and children, participated in the 3 day shoot. Side matches were held on Friday with a chili supper that followed. Six stages were shot on Saturday and four on Sunday. The banquest was packed, with singing entertainment provided by our own Bob Sieck (AKA Bounty Seeker) accompanied on guitar by Craig Hirsch. Martin Bergin provided the group with tales of the old west. Awards for Best Dressed were given out that evening as well as numerous raffle prices and door prices. Sunday wrapped up with the last four stages and the awards for the best shooters!

This site exists for the use of the members of the Arnold Rifle & Pistol Club (ARPC). The purpose of this site is to distribute information about the CAS events and program activities at ARPC to its members. Participation in CAS matches at ARPC is limited to club members. Matches are not open to the public. Members of ARPC may bring guests to the range facilities for the purpose of determining if they want to become members. Contact a member of the ARPC if you desire to see the facilities and an Cowboy Action Shooting match.
