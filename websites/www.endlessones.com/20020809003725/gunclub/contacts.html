About Us

Gateway Shootist Society (GASS)is a group of members of the Arnold Rifle and Pistol Club (ARPC) who plan and pariticipate in the Cowboy Action Shooting (CAS) mathces and activities there. The reason for GASS's existance is to organize, plan and execute CAS activities for ARPC members.

One must be a member of ARPC to associate with GASS. GASS is an organization in name only. There are no rules, regulations, bylaws, articles of incorporation, charter, etc. The only requirement to associate with GASS is to be a member of ARPC and to abide by its rules, regulations and bylaws.

There are no charges, dues, fees, etc. to belong to GASS. Association with GASS bestowes no privileges, rights, or other status. Association with GASS is recognized by presentation of a cowboy style "badge".

Leadership of GASS consists of the "Cowboy Match Directors" appointed by the ARPC,and any ARPC members who what to assist or participate in the CAS activities there. We urge all our fellow ARPC members to join us in the CAS activities at our club.

Directions

Matches are held at the Arnold Rifle & Pistol Club, located along the I-55 service road, between the M/Barnhart exit and the Pevely exit.

From the Arch in Downtown St. Louis, take I-55 South to the M/Barnhart exit. At the stoplight, go staight (which turns into the service road, which is called Metropolitian Blvd) and go approximately 2.5 miles to the enterance to the range.

From the West, take I-270 to I-55 south, to the M/Barnhart exit.

From the south, exit I-55 at the Pevely exit. Turn left and go under I-55 and then under the railroad tressel, turn right on Metropolitian Blvd (about 100 feet past the railroad tressel). Go approximately 1.5 miles to the range enterance.

This site exists for the use of the members of the Arnold Rifle & Pistol Club (ARPC). The purpose of this site is to distribute information about the CAS events and program activities at ARPC to its members. Participation in CAS matches at ARPC is limited to club members. Matches are not open to the public. Members of ARPC may bring guests to the range facilities for the purpose of determining if they want to become members. Contact a member of the ARPC if you desire to see the facilities and an Cowboy Action Shooting match.
