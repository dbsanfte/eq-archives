Charity Shoot
Friday-Sunday, Sept 19-21, 2003
It's that time of year again! Everyone is invited to visit the Arnold Rifle & Pistol Club for a fun weekend and the opportunity to donate to C.H.A.M.P. Assistance Dogs, Inc. These special canines provide companionship and offer physical assistance with activies of daily living, thus helping their human partners lead a life of greater independence. The emotional bond that develops between the two is life affirming! Members and non-members are welcome to participate.

What's in store for the weekend??


10 Main Stages (6 on Sat, 4 on Sun)
Side Matches on Friday
Lunch available each day
Chili Supper on Friday
Attendance & Door Prizes
Vendors Galore
Banquet Saturday Evening
Match Awards on Sunday


Don't miss the chance to come out for some fun, good food, and the chance to watch some fine shooting! 		All banquet tickets must be purchased in advance. You do NOT have to participate in teh match to attend the banquet. We will be limited to 150 Shooters - so register in advance. There is a $25.00 late fee after Sept 1, so don't wait. For more information, call 636-296-8800(days) or 314-776-6885, 618-233-3592, or 314-434-7533 in the evening.

Need Directions?


We are located 17 miles South of St. Louis on I-55. From the North, East and West: Take I-270 or I-255 to I-55 Southbound. Exit at the M/Barnheart Exit. Go straight through the stop light and continue for approximately 2.5 miles. The White Sign and Red gate into the ARPC is on the right. From the South - take I-55 to Peverly exit, go under I-55, past McDonald's, then under the railroad bridge. Make the first right turn (north on the service road). Go approximately 1.5 miles. Sign and gate is on the left. CYA there!

This site exists for the use of the members of the Arnold Rifle & Pistol Club (ARPC). The purpose of this site is to distribute information about the CAS events and program activities at ARPC to its members. Participation in CAS matches at ARPC is limited to club members. Matches are not open to the public. Members of ARPC may bring guests to the range facilities for the purpose of determining if they want to become members. Contact a member of the ARPC if you desire to see the facilities and an Cowboy Action Shooting match.
