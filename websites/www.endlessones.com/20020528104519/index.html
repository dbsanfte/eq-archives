We came, We saw,
we learned a valuable lesson
Heya! Just a few words about this weekends Chardok run... First, many thanks to EVERYONE who turned out. We had a solid showing from not only EO, but Legion of Kings, Order of the Fallen Ones, and Twilight Avengers, who showed up with a full group.

We entered with 36 people, 6 full groups, every group had a healer and evaccer this week, and halfway down the tunnel, Nic asks if we have a rogue... DOH!!!

So I start hunting for a rogue while we continue on. By the time we got past the Bridgekeeper, we had a rogue, (THANKS JORNA!) and continued on.

Pretty routine stuff outside the palace ... pull/kill/pull/kill/oops/rezz... But every week is a learning experience, and this was no different...we tried clearing guards en masse with AoE's, which is a valid tactic for this spot, but for whatever reason, it didn't go off as planned. Big CR time at the palace entrance.

Which brings me to this week's lesson learned. We're at (actually past) the point where as much preparation needs to go into disaster-recovery as goes into the main raid plan. All the corpses were recovered soon enough for XP rezzes except Hodag's original.

SO...having survived this... Next time we camp a rezz crew before doing anything that may result in a wipeout... (AoE'ing can probably clear this area with 2 groups, provided they're familiar enough to coordinate actions).

I'd like to thank everyone for their patience through this one, 30+ people could have easily made life a lot harder for me yesterday, especially with clocks on rezz timers ticking, and RL calling.

Ready to go again?
