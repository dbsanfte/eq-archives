Hi, just my experience on how to level fast between lvl 9 and 14 as a barbarian shaman:

  1) The Bloodforge hammer quest
Getting the head of Trumpy Irontoe. As soon as you can, get a good friend with you and go to Queynos. He should be either a warrior of lvl 15 up or a caster who can root. Go to the (fishale?) tavern near the docks in South Qeynos and look for Trumpy Irontoe. If he isn't there, go outside and wait there. From my experience there is a no spawn radius inside the tavern (at least he always waited to spawn till I was outside). As soon as you find him, hail him. He will ask for a stout, which you should give him. It needs 4-5 of those until he tells you some names. Ask him about those names. After the second name he will walk outside. Follow him and when he is far away from the inn and any corrupt guard attack and kill him quickly.

  Warning: Do not attack him inside the bar. The other guests and Bruno Barstomper, who stays at the entrance, will help him and they are way to high level! When you attack him outside, make sure no corrupt guards are near, since they will help him too. Normal guards dont matter if you kill Trumpy. When you attack him is where your friend comes handy. Trumpy is only about  lvl 6 or 7, so killing him should not be that hard. But he will run and that will almost for sure get some guards or Bruno on you. So either  finish him quickly or root him so he cant run! Trumpys head is the thing  you have done this all for. It is lore and no drop, so make sure you get  it. Since a patch, you can store it away in bank till you need it.

 Traveling to Kaladim
As soon as you get lvl 9 and have SOW, go to Kaladim. That journey isn't  easy, but there are three possibilities to do it with minimal risk:

 a) let you teleport by a friendly druid or wizard. If you know one or have

 the possibility to do so, you can even go before lvl 9.

 b) find one or more higher lvl players, best a caster, to travel with you.

 They often like the idea, because your SOW makes the trip faster and more  secure. If you have someone to cast bind and/or invisibility, the trip  will be even more secure for all of you. In that case you can take the  shorter route over Highhold Pass (Check the maps at Everlore for details).  You should do fine. Just make sure to bind at Highhold pass entrance and  before entering Kithicor forest, since those are the most dangerous places  on your trip. Travel through Kithicor at daytime, there are way less  undead.

   c) if you are alone or have no one who can bind, take to route over Grobb  and Misty thicket. Its longer but a lot safer since you don't have to go  through Highhold. I cant say anything over this route from own experience,  since I used variant a) and b) so far.

   Just to give you an estimate on the risk: I did the trip at lvl 9 with a  lvl 17 wizard and a lvl 20 paladin. We did use SOW, INVIS all the time and  got bound as described.  I got killed once. Ran into a hill giant in West Karana (They can see  invis and are just almost as fast as you are with SOW).  The paladin got killed twice in Kithicor forest by undead (They can see  invisible too), that's how we learned to travel at daytime.  The Wizard made it without dying.

   When you reach the Butcherblock zone by ship from Freeport, go to Kaladim.  First to do is con Byzar Bloodforge in the warriors guild (downstairs, left wing), any the guards will do also because the always conned alike to me. If they don't can amiably, and they wont do when you arrive first, there is no way to get the bloodforge hammer at this time.

 

 Raise faction with Byzar Bloodforge
 You can raise faction by killing Orcs or Goblins. From my experience, Orcs are much better (provided that you are at least lvl 9). Go to Crushbone and group. Go for Orc Centurions and probably Orc Slavers (depends on group) on the left side after you enter CB. This will raise your faction fast. To even be faster you can do two quest (giving good exp with minimal effort too):

a) try to get the orc belts from the Centurions (and any legionaire shoulder pads you should find on Orc Legionaire bodys). Give the to the dwarf in the Kaladim warriors guild (downstairs, right wing, opposite side of Byzar Bloodforge) two at a time.

b) if you kill an Orc Slaver and there is a key on him, give it to the appropriate slave. To find out, which it is (the keys have numbers on them), just give a copper to a slave. He will say something like "I dont need that, give me key number...". Give it to him and you get exp as well as the needed faction, if it is a dwarf slave.

  Getting the bloodforge hammer
When the guards con amiably, go to Byzar Bloodforge, con him too to make sure he is amiably too, hail him (i dont know if its necessary, but you don't want to risk anything) and give him the head. In exchange, he will give you the Bloodforge hammer. That one is a decend weapon, which ,after reaching lvl 10, I prefer over the RTS.

   2) What to do in Kaladim between lvl 9 and 14
After you have done the Bloodforge hammer quest, you can  decide on traveling back to Freeport or Halas or staying in Kaladim. Since the journey is a risk, I propose you stay there until getting a teleport (best way) or reaching lvl 14 and having bind (by the way, you can get the heal spells, bind and root in the cleric guild in Kaladim, so no reason to go back at 14 without having those spells in your spell book).

   Hunting grounds for lvl 9 to 14:
 Crushbone:
CB ist good at lvl 9 and 10 in a group to kill Orc Centurions and Orc Slavers. At lvl 10 you can solo the Centurions too. CB gives good loot and experience at these lvl. Of course you can stay here at lvl 11+ too, but I found that the Centurions only give minimal exp at these lvl. So if you can find a good group (4-5) to go for Legionaires. Else leave for Steamfont. Do not hunt at the entrance or, without a good group, at the right side. From my experience there are trains way to often and the risk of dying is high. You can come back here at lvl 14 to hunt Legionaires in a 2 payer group (or even solo, but i dont think its wise).

  Steamfont:  
At lvl 10, at least at lvl 11 I would go to Steamfont. Let the others hunt those Minos, drakes and elems. You go for Skeletons! If you go past the windmills, past the minotaur mines you will reach a place at the end of the valley, where parts of a huge broken machinery lie around. At this end there is a ring of 5 Skeletons (Skeletons, Dwarf Skeletons, Decaying Skeletons) which ,in spite of being named differently, con all high blue or white to a lvl 10. The good thing about these 5 Skeletons is that they are all far away from each other so you can attack each one without the others joining the fight. IMO leave whites where they are, since guards are far away if you have to run (ok, you will reach them, but it takes much time better be used to kill the others). You can lvl fast here up to lvl 12 and even to lvl 13 or 14 at a slower pace. It can be boring sometimes, but you can do it with minimal risk and time, so i think its worth it. Loot isn't that good, between 4 sp and 3 gp. Probably you have the opportunity to sell the bones to a high lvl Necro to lazy to hunt low greens for himself. They sell at about 1pp for 10 if you are lucky. If you are not grouped or there are many players online, i would suggest going to Steamfont. Even if another player is hunting the Skeletons, they should spawn fast enough to keep both of you happy up to at least lvl 11. If more are hunting there, go somewhere else (the drakes and elems in front of the mines are  just a few seconds away).

  Greater Faydark:  
An alternative at lvl 12 or 13 is GF. At the south end of the zone, near an Elf Ranger camp, there are the Nybright sisters. They con high blue or  even white at lvl 12. You will have to group, or at least have someone to  break the spawn for you to hunt here, since they are 3 and gang up. Loot is decent (bronze weapons etc.), but you have to store it and go to Kaladim or Lesser Faydark to sell it, since you don't fit into the elven shacks of the Ranger camp. Orc camps are in GF too. The Centurions are high green to blue, but gang up with 3 to 4 so you need a group, which lowers experience significantly.

 Butcherblock:
You can also hunt at Butcherblock: Goblin camps at lvl 9 or 10, depending on what goblins spawn. The Orc camp at lvl 12+. There are only Centurions here, but there are 3 or 4 and gang up, and they are at least 2 lvl higher than their CB or GF counterparts, so be careful. The chess board is good at lvl 10 and 11, but overcrowded most of the time.

 Making money
A side note on money making. In spite of having hunted in Steamfont with its poor lot almost all the time, I had no problem getting together the money for a full suit of banded (thanks go to Kaolas for the good work :) ) and all my lvl 14 spells before entering lvl 13. How? Giving SOW! There are almost no Shamen and the Druids dont get SOW up to lvl 14. Most of them dont seem to care about the need of SOW from other players. This is where you come in. I generally charge for SOW (outside a party that is). The charge depends on the lvl of the player and for what he needs it (hunting = high charge; corpse recovery = no charge). There are many high lvl players hunting guards or other NPC in that area. Loot

 on those is good (15-20 pp sometimes), so I feel free to ask for 5pp for  the SOW. Its change for them! And I generally even gate or run back to  them over two or three zones, if they need another one. They are happy to have a SOW when they need it and I am happy to have a regular income. You can sometimes even make more money by selling their loot for them (because of reputation and race). Its a matter of trust but its reduces their downtime and gives you another source of income. Of course everyone,  who is roleplaying, has to decide wether to do it or not. But its generally a good thing for moneymaking.

 Going back  
Another sidenote: A SOW is a very good reason for a high lvl wizard to teleport you back to North Karanas! If he does, go for the island at Eruds Crossing and hunt wisps. At lvl 12 you should easily be able deal with one, probably two at the same time, and as a group you are unstopable! Good experience and loot!

Good hunting and may you have fun with this guide!
