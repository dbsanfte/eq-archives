Yamed’s Beginning Guide to Pottery
6/28/00

After trying to find a comprehensive guide to Pottery and having no luck at all, I decided to write this guide for all of you who might be trying to get into pottery.

Why Pottery

Pottery is not going to be a big money maker. Go into blacksmithing or jewelry making if you’re looking to make money. Pottery is generally used to make items required by other trade skills, and it’s really the only way to make any money at it. Some people just like the idea of making something new – jars, bowls, pie tins, cookie cutters etc.

Regardless of your motives, you will find out that pottery is one of the easiest skills to master. With very little investment, you’ll have your skill up to 150 in a very short time. In no other skill will you get eight skill increases in a row!

Where to Start

This guide is based on the assumption that you are using Halas as your home base. Halas is a very small town, doesn’t require zoning back and forth to get required items and everything you need is located in three places, all conveniently located near each other (in Halas, everything is conveniently located next to each other). You could use other cities to practice in, but the locations will differ (though the techniques will be the same).

You’ll need about 20 – 30 PP to get your skill to around 150, depending on your charisma and intelligence. The higher you are in either category, the better it will be for you.

Beginning Pottery

Pottery consists of putting a predetermined amount of clay, a sketch and a flask of water into a pottery wheel to make an unfinished product. Then, the unfinished product is put into a kiln with a finishing sheet to finish the product and make it ready to sell. You cannot sell unfinished products to merchants. Doesn’t really matter though. You’re not going to fire anything nor sell back anything to merchants by following this guide! Destroy everything you make. It costs more money to fire an unfinished product and you’ll end up losing money on it if you sell to merchants.

I put 5 practice points into pottery at my guild, but that was a wasted use of skill points! Just put 1 point into it (maybe none at all, I’m not exactly sure if it is a self-starting skill). On the dock area of Halas, looking out over the lake, there will be two female barbarian merchants on your left (the Bound Mermaid will be on your right). The one of the farthest left is the merchant who sells most of the sketches. Sketches are stackable, so they don’t take up much room. 

Dok’s Cigars sells the clay. Walk in the door and turn left. You’ll see another female barbarian merchant, who sells the clay. You can also purchase flasks of water from the merchant directly across from the clay woman, a male barbarian merchant (Dok, I believe).

The Golden Torc has the pottery wheel. It’s on the floor, to the right when you walk inside the door.

The amount of items you purchase will depend on how much inventory room you have. I purchased 8 backpacks and put all my items in the bank, so I had the maximum amount of room possible with basic backpacks (64 slots). You’ll need a lot of water, so purchase 60 flasks or so.

The first 21 Levels

Purchase 42 small blocks of clay and head to the pottery wheel. Left click on the pottery wheel, put 3 small blocks of clay and a flask of water into the wheel and click combine. Five things can happen:

You’ll make a block of clay – if this happens, put it in a backpack and hold on to it. Be sure not mistake it for a small block of clay!

You’ll make a block of clay and gain a practice point – same as above except you increase your skill!

You’ll get a message saying you lack the skill to make the items – just try again.

You’ll get a message saying you lack the skill to make the items and you’ll gain a practice point – same as above. Keep going!

Keep doing this until you reach a skill level of 21. Hopefully, you have made some blocks of clay. These will come in handy for the next 100 or so levels J

22 – 41

Go buy 30 or so large clay jar sketches. Purchase enough bars of clay (not small) so added together with the bars of clay you made earlier total the amount of sketches you have. Place sketch, flask and bar of clay into wheel and combine. Results will be the same as above with one exception. If you make a jar, destroy it and keep going. Yes, I know it seems wrong, but it’s the cheapest solution. Keep doing this until your skill gets to 41.

Should take all of about 10 minutes or real-time play.

42 – 122

This is where it starts getting old and a little monotonous.  Purchase a bunch of medium bowl sketches and a bunch of bars of clay. You know what to do. 1 bar of clay, 1 sketch and 1 flask of water. Combine, destroy if anything is made and repeat until your skill is up to 122.

123 – 148

Things will start to slow down a bit here. Really, the only things you can make at this point to increase your skill are poison vials.  Problem is, poison vials require a skin of some sort. Skins can be any of the following:

  •       High Quality Bear Pelt

  •       High Quality Puma/Cat Pelt

  •       High Quality Wolf Pelt

  •       Low Quality Bear Pelt

  •       Low Quality Puma/Cat Pelt

  •       Low Quality Wolf Pelt

  •       Medium Quality Bear Pelt

  •       Medium Quality Puma/Cat Pelt

  •       Medium Quality Wolf Pelt

  •       Black Wolf Skin

  •       Mist Wolf Skin

  •       Polar Bear Skin

  •       White Wolf Pelt

  •       Blackburrow Gnoll Skin

  •       Puma Skin

  •       Zombie Skin (stackable & light)

As you can see, the zombie skin is your best choice. It’s stackable and very light. Whatever you use is up to you, but it always seems harder to find skins when you’re looking for them. Ruined pelts cannot be used. Other skins might be used as well, though I cannot guarantee it.

As a benefit however, you’ll be firing unfinished vials and holding on to them. They can be sold for 2pp or so (I’ve seen them go for 5pp before) to rogues making poison, or enchanters making viscous mana or tailors making Wu’s Fighting Gloves. After you get your skill to 148, you’ll have enough vials to meet the demand you’ll probably get (which really isn’t a whole lot). I keep mine in the bank.

The same merchant who sells the blocks of clay sells the poison vial sketches. They are called vial sketches (or something close to that). There are also lined vial sketches and sealed vial sketches. Don’t concern yourself with these for now.

Get the vial sketches, small blocks of clay (not regular), and animal skins, then head to the wheel. Combine 1 skin, 1 flask of water, 1 small block of clay, 1 sketch and hit combine. Repeat until your skill gets to 148. Keep any successful vials. The bank is right down the path from the Golden Torc. I put my unfinished vials in there until I was to level 148.

One of the three merchants you are used to dealing with sell the firing sheets. I’m sorry, but right now I can’t remember who it is. Purchase enough high quality firing sheets to cover the amount of unfinished vials you have. Put a HQ firing sheet and the unfinished vial into the kiln. This will result in an finished vial, which again you should keep and sell to players for around 2pp each.

148 - 168

Lined poison vials are the only thing left to make at this point, as sealed poison vials are broken at the moment, probably never to be fixed. Same as regular poison vials except they require a sealed vial sketch.

Profit

Really, the only things that you can make a profit off of is:

Vials – used by rogues for poison and enchanters to viscous mana.

Pie Tins – used by gnomes in tinkering

Ceramic Lining – Used by smiths for pie tins

Clay Jars – can be used to put in the bank to hold 8 items. If you can sell them less than a backpack costs, then you could make a profit. Never tried this one actually.

You’re not going to make a killing off pottery no matter what you do. Your best bet is to find an enchanter and / or rogue and be their only supplier. Offer volume discounts to people.

Conclusion

You’ll be amazed at how quickly your pottery skill will rise. It’s really an easy skill to master. Have fun with it. If you get bored repeating these steps, take a step back and go swimming in the near by lake. It’s a great way to release stress, and sometimes the two female barbarians standing on the docks will join you! That is until their boss sees them goofing off and orders them back to work!
