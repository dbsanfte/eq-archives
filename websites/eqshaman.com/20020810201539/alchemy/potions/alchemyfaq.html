Do the recipes for "Old Alchemy" still work?

Yes...with a few exceptions. Most Old Alchemy recipes were removed in the long ago alchemy revamp, when vendor herbs changed completely. Those original herbs are now again available in Luclin and many of those recipes have been tested and found working. A few, however, seem to be buggy.

See our Recipes and Effects page, our Herb Vendors page, and our Herb Prices page for more information.

*****

What are the best potions to make in order to gain and/or improve my alchemy skill?

Try to find a balance between "potion difficulty" (ie, trivial level) and "cost to make" as per our Trivial Chart, taking into consideration those potions that sell best to players (Blood of Wolf, Invisibility to Undead and Shrink in the pre-100 skill area . . . Greater Aura potions to boost the appropriate resists for dragon raids, Stingwort and Gate potions in the post-100 skill area).

Keep in mind that while Titan potions have a high trivial, they cost only half as much per attempt. It has not yet been determined for sure whether or not improvements in skill come faster if you work on something with a lower trivial. So, if you are finding it very hard to fund your alchemy but want to continue trying, consider working on Growth potions for a while. You will succeed less often, but as most potions sell back to a vendor for only a tiny faction of the cost of the components anyway, you may still come out ahead in the long run.

Also, see our guide on Beginning Alchemy for more information.

Where do I find the herbs to combine with various "Giant" Toes?

Balm Leaf, Dragonwart, Elf Leaf and LarkSpur can be found at gypsy camps noted near the bottom of our Herb Prices page or our Herb Vendors page.

*****

Where do I find the various "Giant" Toes used for certain alchemy potions?

First and formost, from killing the giants and looting their toes. Some vendors (especially those near giant spawn points) may carry giant toes from time to time as a result of other players selling their looted giant toes to that vendor. There is no known vendor that commonly stocks giant toes in their regular inventory. See the bottom of our Herb Prices page for more details.
