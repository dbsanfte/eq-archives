Glacier Bear guide
by Oumgrok


I have successfully taken the glacier bear several times. The hardest
part is waiting for the first spawn. There is an 8 hour delay between
spawns.  Two ice bone skeletons serve as place holders for the bear.
I think the spawn is about 1 out of every three or four spawns is the
bear.  I have seen two bears spawn on more than one occasion.

Step 1)  Go to the glacier bear cave, and look for the wandering ice
bone skeletons and/or the bear (sense animals will find him if he's
there). If either of them are there, you are in luck. If it's the bear
great, if it is the wandering ice bone men (there are two of them)
that's still pretty good.

Step 2)  If you find the wandering ise bone skeletons, kill them both
(they are very, easy, easy green at lvl 35), and proceed to Step 4.

Step 3)  If you don't find the bear or the wandering ice bone men,
take a seat in the hallway half way between Sulon McMoor the evil
wizard (high 40's don't aggro him) and the little house where the ice
bone skeletons stand in place. Bring a deck of cards because you may
have a long wait (up to 8 hours). Once they spawn, they will walk out
of the room guarded by Sulon McMoor and come right past you. If you
are lucky, the bear will spawn instead of the ice bones.

Waiting there can be BORING.  There's no experience to be had and
nothing to watch or do in the cave.  On the other hand, if you go off
to play elsewhere, you may lose the spawn to someone else who is
camping it, and you'll never know if you did.

As an alternative to waiting, try making a pet, tell him to guard
here, and tell him to taunt.  Bind yourself to the area.  When you get
the message that your pet is taunting, gate back.  (I wish I had
thought of this when I was camping the bear.  Kudo's to the shaman who
came up with this idea if it works).

If you are low-mid 30's a good thing to camp while waiting are the icy
orcs and redwind who spawn at one of the ruined temples in Everfrost.
It is usually camped, which is good, because you won't be able to take
six orcs alone.  You can usually join a group there, or they will
agree to rotate orcs.  You should be able to solo an icy orc if
someone has broken the spawn.

Step 4)   After killing the ice bone spawn, camp, bind, go play a
twink, whatever.   Just write down the exact time and come back
exactly 7 hours and 50 minutes later (best to be a little early).
Sometimes the spawn can be late by as much as 20 minutes, don't
despair. If the ice bones spawn, kill them, and come back again 7
hours 50 minutes later. Whatever you do, don't miss a spawn -- you've
already spent/wasted up to 8 hours getting it  timed. If you go to
sleep, set your alarm and make sure you are back in time (obviously,
this is best done over a weekend). Do this three or four times in a
row and you will absolutely get the bear this weekend.

I took out two bears together at lvl 34 with my pet (we were both
buffed to the max). Direct Damage does not work (as others have
indicated). Hit him  with disease and poisen spells and debuffs to
slow him down.

A druid who can wolf form you is good to have.  Sulon McMoor will not
aggro you if you are in wolf form and stay to the far side of the hall
from him.  The bear actually spawns in the cave Sulon is guarding, but
as he walks out on his own as soon as he spawns, I don't recommend
waiting there.

The bear also sometimes drop an enchanted ingot which is part of a
quest for a fairly awesome, cleric only, shield.  The ingot is no
drop.  If you have a good cleric friend, and you have the bear timed
right, you might want to bring him or her to get it.  You can also
hold the ingot, and go with your cleric friend to the Solusek Ro
temple to complete the quest.  After the cleric gives the NPC all but
one of the items, you give the bar to the NPC and then the cleric
gives the last item.  I have done this, and it does work.
