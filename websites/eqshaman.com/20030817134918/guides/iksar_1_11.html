Iksar Shaman Guide (Lvl. 1 to 14)


Ok, let me try and pass over some Information for the starting Iksar Shaman. This guide is written as an newbie guide, but I think it contains some useful information for others too:

Getting started (Lvl 1 to 4):

Level 1:
First to do is outside EQ: Visit EQ-Atlas and print out their maps of East Cabilies, Field of Bone (FOB) and maybe Lake of Ill Omen. These maps are a wonderful help when starting out.

Then scribe your 2 spells in your spellbook, memorise them and use Inner Fire at all times. It is the best HP buff you can get mana wise and helps to get the Skill up.

Then visit the “Haggle Baron”. Tell the Klok there “I will perform the small service”. He will give you an 8-slot backpack and want you to fill it with cured wolf pelts. Perform this quest as often as you can because every turned in backpack will give you some coin (up to 1.5 gp), good exp. and a random piece of curscale armor, which will be vital for you for a long time.

Head out to FOB. Follow the path a little bit over the hill. There is your newbie area. Stay at the city side of it.
At lvl 1 hunt scaled wolf pubs, but be careful, because they aggro on you and will gang up. So attack one and step back some way towards the city entrance. This is called “pulling”. After you have finished it, heal up and pull the next one.

Stay away from beetles and scorpions at lvl 1.

As soon as you have filled your backpack with the pelts, combine it and run back to the Haggle Baron to turn it in.

Actually I found lvl 1 harder than in other newbie zones because of the wolfs tendency to gang up on me. But as soon as you reach lvl 2 FOB really begins to shine.

Level 2 to 4:
Continue to hunt scaled wolf pubs and clubs, but also try out decaying skeletons, beetles and scorpions. Just make sure to con them, so you dont attack something other than intended. While Decaying Skeletons are agressive, beetles and scorpions are not until attacked. Beetles will gang up when you fight one, scorpions wont.

Be sure to watch the incoming messages at all times, because there will inevitably be “trains”, meaning a poor soul running to the guards with some mobs on his tail. Since most of these come out of the “pit”, they are way higher than the ones in the newbie area and will kill you with one or two hits.

At lvl. 4 make sure to go to your guild master and train one point in channeling. It will max out fast

Dont spend money for weapon or armor at these lvls! You will need it to buy your spells at lvl. 5. But there are some lvl. 1 spells you might want to buy: Strengthen, Dexterous Aura, Burst of Flame, Flash of Light

Some may disagree on flash of light. Despite its somewhat limited usefulness in combat, it may help to get that spell through which otherwise would have been interrupted. Plus it helps to get the Evocation skill up, which will greatly help when you get Invisible at higher lvl.

On a notice: If you want, you can turn in the scorpion pincers you get from scorpions at the Warlord (1. Floor of the building right of city entrance when coming out of the city). He will give you less money than the Klok, but faction and some exp. for it.

The Pit (Level 5 to 11)

Level 5:
First run to your guild and buy as much of your lvl 5 spells as you can. Spells worth buying are: Feet like Cat, Sicken, Drowsy, Frost Rift, Summon Foot, Gate. If you can afford others, ok.

The Drowsy spell can be found as follows:
Use the teleporter in the guild hall, which will teleport you on the roof. Climb all ladders down and turn left. There is a Klok selling the spell. To get out again without Gate, climb up again and jump down at the buildings front. You will receive some damage from falling, but dont wander around in the basement trying to find a exit. There is a nasty hole in the ground, where you probably will die if you fall into it. And when not dying, it will be hard to get out there. I had to call a guide at lvl 5 to teleport me out. His comment was: “There is a way out, but it took me 3 hours to figure it out”. So be careful!

Next thing you can do at lvl 5 is making the first Shaman Cudgel Quest:
Dont bother around with the mortar quest they will give you! It can take hours to forage that mud crabs at Lake of Ill Omen.

Jump into the water at the guild and swim through the bridge, turn left behind bridge and search the side until you discover an underwater passage. Swim into it and surface in the last hole of the passage (if someone is already hunting there, the other holes are ok too, just take a little longer). To get out of the water, look straight up and jump! Hunt all the mobs here, which should be green to you now. From time to time an Iksar Outcast spawns. Kill him. He will drop a named skull. You will need 3 different skulls. For me he was dropping them one after the other, could be just luck though. Time needed: 30 – 45 minutes.

Turn in the skulls and your cudgel (mace) you got from the guild when turning in your newbie note to the trainer on left side of the guild leader (when looking at him from entrance). This will get you your new cudgel of the petitioner.

Now at latest get a piercing weapon and use it and the cudgel to get both skills up. A note on 2 handed blunt weapons: while most other races Shamen use them, I haven’t found any cheap 2hb to buy in Cabilies or as loot.

Now you can go into the “pit”:
The pit is a dried reservoir, having many ramps to get into. One is at the end of the street through the newbie area. There are two zones here: if you enter from the newbie zone, you are in the low lvl part (about lvl. 5 to 9), left you see a skeletal Dragon, behind it is the high lvl part (lvl. 10 to 15). But mobs from high lvl part tend to wander in low lvl part sometimes, so be careful.
From my viewpoint grouping is vital here and much more fun, so find a warrior or monk or maybe a second Shaman of your levelrange.

Just a notice on behaviour: Some people tend to use the ramps to rest. This isn’t a very good idea, esp. for the two ramps towards the city. These will be used by all people training mobs towards the city guards. It is almost impossible to train a mob past someone sitting and meding or healing without “loosing” it. Since they are low on hitpoints anyway, it often results in them getting killed. So dont rest /med on the ramps.
On the other hand: try to avoid at all cost to train a mob past someone meding/resting at the side of the pit. Its highly unfair saving your butt on the cost of others. I know it sometimes accidentally happens and want to apologise to the ones having suffered from me!

Mobs to hunt: Lesser Scalebones, Large scorpions, Carrion Shredder, Bonecrawler, Scaled Wolfs and Iksar footpads.

A notice on quests, money and experience:

  • Whenever you go back to town, stop in newbie area and do the curscale armor quest.

  • Even if you are no tailor, buy a small sewing kit. You can combine 2 spider silk (not spiderling silk) in it to a silk       swatch. Those stack (despite the silk) and can be sold for 1pp to players.

  • giant scorpion pincers cant be sold. But they can be turned in at the warriors guild with the Taskmaster in the          first floor. He will not give you the quest, but tell him “I need a geozite tool” and he will give you a satchel. In it   you can combine 2 giant pincers and give it back to him. He will give you very good exp, gp and a note to       deliver to Warlords at the gates. Turning them in with the appropriate warlord will even give more exp. Dont       do the quests the Warlords give to you after getting the note, since they will bring no exp.

  • You can get a cheap 4 AC helmet by doing the following:
          Turn in 3 loose scales and 5 gp in East Cabilies to the left guard at the left tunnel to FOB. He will give you an unfinished coif. Using a forge with the coif and 3 further loose scales will give you the final coif. If you fail, the       loose scales will be lost, but the unfinished coif remains. So just bring some more scales with you. 10 should       do even with no smiting skill.


At lvl 8 you get meditate at last. Only put one point in it, it will max out fast.

At lvl 9 by your next spells (if you can afford them):
Spirit of Wolf, Tainted Breath, Light Healing, Summon Food

You can turn over to the middle of the pit with hunting Greater Scalebone, Scaled Wolf Hunter, Giant Scorpions or Iksar bandits too.

An additional note on our DOT-Spells: Tainted Breath and Sicken stack, meaning you can use both at the same target without cancelling each other.

You should by now know what to hunt and how and are well received in every group with your healing and sow-capabilities.

Now its time to upgrade your weapon and armour:
1HB:         Fine Steel morning Star (8/38) from Kurns tower, selling from 5pp to 8pp
                Forged Morning Star (8/38) from your local smith for about 8pp
                Use your Guild Cudgel to maximise skill after levelling, because
                its that fast

Piercing:     Fine Steel Spear 8 – 15pp
                Bronze Main Gauche (3/23) from Kurns Tower to maximise
                 skill (sorry, got it as loot, so no price)

2HB:          If you are lucky, you can get a Runed Totem Staff (RTS)
                 for 10 – 15pp. Its nice, because it can hit magic creatures too

Armor:      Reinforced Rhino armour, about 0.5 - 0.75pp per AC from Kurns
                Banded 1 – 2pp per AC from Smiths

Kurns Tower (Level 11 to 14)

From lvl 11 on you can start hunting in Kurns at the entrance hall. Always group here! A group with a warrior or with one or two monks about a lvl higher than you is best for exp. If you go for cash or just want to get your weapon skills up, get one or two warriors about 3 lvls higher than you and play their healer. Use your weapon to raise all the time not meding up. Additionally you will get about 0.5 to 1 bubble of exp. per hour this way.

Kurns is ok for money, if you can get a group (3-4!) to go to basement at 13th or 14th lvl loot gets good too.

I hope I could help some young Iksar Shamen this way! Have fun and stay safe!
