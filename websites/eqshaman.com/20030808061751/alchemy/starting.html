Getting Started in Alchemy

So you wanna make potions for fun and profit? Well, maybe just for fun anyway. Here's the step by step way to get your new shaman skill started.

The Initial Step:

Alchemy is a Shaman-only skill which Shamans of level 25 or higher can learn, gaining the unique ability to make potions. Unfortunately, unless you like throwing away money, the skill cap on alchemy is so low at level 25 that you'll likely fail on most attempts to make potions and lose your platinum very fast.

What is the skill cap on Alchemy? Put simply . . .

(Your character level minus 24) times 5

Hence, at level 25, the maximum skill in alchemy you can achieve is 5 or [(25-24)*5] . . ., at level 26 the maximum skill is 10 or [(26-24)*5] . . ., and so on.

Since most of the Lesser Stat potions don't become trivial until 18 to 22 alchemy skill, here is our recommendation:

"Get your shaman to level 29 before attempting to train in Alchemy and be sure you've saved or accumulated 21 guild practice points when you hit level 29."

The trainer at your shaman guild in Halas, Grobb, Ogguk or Cabilis will allow you to put 21 initial practice points into alchemy skill, but no more. Further increases in skill must be obtained "in the field", which means to get further skill increases above 21 you will need to attempt to make potions at a slightly higher skill than you currently possess.

A Word about Wisdom vs Intelligence:

From the Ruins of Kunark Players Guide:

Intelligence: Affects how fast you learn many skills. For a Necromancer, Wizard, Magician, Enchanter, Shadow Knight or Bard, it also affects maximum mana at each level.

Wisdom: Affects how fast you learn specific skills. For a Ranger, Paladin, Cleric, Shaman or Druid, it also affects maximum mana at each level.

Despite what these definitions indicate, there are currently two schools of thought regarding skill increases. Much of this is due to conflicting reports from player guides, posts on message boards, news excerpts for Abashi (Gordon) and/or the general rumor mill:

First School of Thought: Intelligence makes skills go up faster, Wisdom helps success rates in making a product.

Second School of Thought: Whichever attribute is higher is the determining factor in both skill increases and success rates.

There seems to be some recent posts from Brad "Aradune" McQuaid, indicating the Second School of Thought is the correct one:

Quote from Brad McQuaid:

"WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

- Brad"

And another post that further supports the Second School of Thought:

Quote from Yakatizma, Verant employee on EQ Boards:

Without revealing the details of the equations the relationship is as follows.
The higher of either Int. or Wis. effects both the chances of success and the rate at which skill points are gained. The forthcoming various stat bonuses for various skills, such as the strength bonus for blacksmithing will provide a success bonus that will stack with the Int/Wis bonus.

Neither of these quotes contradict the first description under Wisdom above indicating that it affects "how fast you learn specific skills". Therefore, inasmuch as Shamans have inherently higher Wisdom in the first place, we recommend:

"Get your Wisdom as high as you possibly can before attempting Alchemy skill increases or potion making"

To Make your First "In the Field" Potion:

1) Locate and go to the Herb Vendor of your choice in either Halas, Grobb or Firiona Vie. (Check our Herb Vendor list for vendor names and locations).

2) Choose one of the Lesser Stat Potions that requires an alchemy skill of 22 to make (Lesser Power, Lesser Vigor, Lesser Cohesion, etc. ... see Trivial Chart).

3) Check the recipe for the potion you've selected on our Known Recipes page and purchase 5 or 6 of each of the two required herbs.

4) Purchase a Medicine Bag from the same vendor.

5) With the Medicine Bag in one of your 8 general inventory slots, right click on it to "open" it.

6) Place one of each of the two herbs you purchased in step 3 into the Medicine Bag and hit "Combine". Note: For this level of potions, there should only be two herbs (for example, one Lucern herb and one Birthwart herb if you're attempting to make one Lesser Vigor potion) in your Medicine Bag when you hit "Combine". Attempting to make more than one potion at a time guarantees failure.

7) After hitting Combine, you will receive one of five possible messages and success/failure outcome combinations in making a potion as follows:

a) You will fail and receive a message indicating you do not have the skill to make that potion. (bad, try again)

b) You will fail and receive a message indicating your skill in alchemy has increased. (good, keep going)

c) You will succeed in making the potion and receive a message indicating you have created something new, but receive no indication of whether the potion is trivial, which means it is not trivial yet. (good, keep going)

d) You will succeed in making the potion and receive a message indicating your skill in alchemy has increased. (very good, keep going)

e) You will succeed in making the potion and receive a message indicating that you can no longer advance in skill from making this item, which means the potion is now trivial for you to make. (excellent, time to move on to the next harder potion if you have not reached your skill cap)

Inasmuch as the skill cap for a level 29 character is 25 alchemy skill, you may want to stop attempting to increase your alchemy skill once you reach alchemy skill 22 (waiting until your shaman hits character level 30 before attempting the "trivial at 27" potions on the Trivial Chart), or you may wish to attempt to reach your skill cap of 25. The choice is yours to make.

Optional "Blood of Wolf" Method:

It has been suggested by some shamans that making only the Blood of Wolf potions from skill 21 through 37 (trivial level for Blood of Wolf) would provide the young shaman with a more viable product for sale to the public, thereby decreasing their learning costs as they can recoup their platinum. This is a valid method and you may want to choose this method of increasing your alchemy skill. What generally happens when using this method is your inventory becomes overloaded with BoW potions, forcing the shaman to sell quickly to clear up enough space to continue making more potions and gain more skill increases. Naturally, this tends to deflate the price charged for BoW in zones with the herb vendors for BoW. I've often seen shamans selling BoW in Firiona Vie for 8 platinum per potion which, when you consider failure rates, is very close to actual cost of making the BoW potions. It's all about supply and demand. BoW is in high demand which tends to push the price up, but by overloading supply in the BoW "herb vendor" zones, the price is pushed down, at least in those zones. Either method of increasing skill is valid, but as much as I hate to see one of our best potions get undersold, I too would recommend the Blood of Wolf method to increase skill from 21 to 37 to save the shaman some money in the learning process. Naturally, if you're the only shaman in the zone selling BoW at that time, you can still charge a fair market rate for the potions and will likely recover a greater amount of platinum for furthering your skill, although it might take a little longer to sell out your inventory.

Also, you can discuss pricing with another shaman if they are also in zone selling BoW. This is commonly known as "Price Fixing", which is illegal on the Earthen Plane, but then again, there is no Federal Trade Commission in Norrath to prevent us from doing such.

Underselling any potion only hurts all shamans in the long run. If all shamans stuck to a standard pricing guide , of course, this would be no problem at all.

What Do I Do With the Potions I Made?:

You have three basic choices:

1) Sell them to other players (see Pricing Guide)

2) Sell them back to the vendor, but they won't give you much for them

3) Drink them

Furthering Your Skill In Alchemy:

Your skill in understanding alchemy has increased!

Utilizing the reference pages on this website as a guide to trivial levels, recipes and herbs, and as you level your shaman up and raise your skill cap, pick the next harder potion to make, taking into account herb price differences in similar-level potions, and go for it!

Obtaining Herbs from KOS locations:

Eventually, you will want to obtain herbs from a location where your character is "Kill On Sight". To get around this, you'll need two things:

1) An "Herb Mule" character in the KOS city

2) A friend

Create an "Herb Mule" character in the city where you want to buy herbs but can't bring your shaman into. For my Barbarian Shaman, I created an Erudite Shadow Knight in Paineel with the highest charisma I could give him on the Character Creation Screen. Then . . .

1) Ask your friend to accompany your Shaman to a point near the city where your Herb Mule resides. A wizard with teleport to Toxulia Forest is especially helpful

2) Give your friend money to transfer to your Herb Mule

3) Camp your Shaman and log in your Herb Mule character

4) Have your friend give your Herb Mule the money to buy the herbs with

5) Take your mule into the city and buy the herbs, return and give the herbs to your friend

6) Camp your mule and log in your Shaman

7) Get the Herbs from your friend

8) Repeat process as needed

Note: Be very careful who your friend is and that they are truly a friend. You'll be giving them hundreds or even thousands of platinum to transfer to your mule so be absolutely sure they can be trusted.
