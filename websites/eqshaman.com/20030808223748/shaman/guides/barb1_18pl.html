Beginning Power-Leveling Guide to the Barbarian Shaman
By Yamed 18th season Shaman
Torvonnilous server

Everyone has their own theory on the best way to play a given race / class combination. I make no claims other than this is how I played my character. I am currently a level 18 Barbarian Shaman with 450PP in the bank. I have 2 magical weapons, full banded armor and every one of my spells. This has been achieved in just over 5 days of playing time. Is this good? I really don’t know. I’m telling you this as an indicator of where my character is at currently. It’s up to you to decide if it’s good or not and if this is the path for you.

Character Creation:

The only thing to consider when creating your character is how to distribute your 30 skill points. I put 10 into charisma for a total of 75 and the other 20 into wisdom for a total of 100. Your wisdom has a direct impact on how much mana you have, so many people will tell you to put all of your skill points into wisdom. I chose to put 10 points into my charisma for two reasons: cost of items and impact on future charm-type spells.

Items that you are buying will cost less, and items that you are selling will sell for a greater value the higher charisma you have. Will 10 points make a huge difference? Not really (though it does help a little), but when you reach level 14 and get your first charisma spell, you’ll really notice the difference.

Your befriend animal and charm spells are also directly related to your charisma. The higher charisma, the longer the spells will hold. While I have yet to achieve the level needed for these spells, I’m sure I’ll be glad I put those 10 points into charisma.

First Things First:

Before even starting your character, learn how the /LOC command works. Forget sense heading as the /LOC command is much more powerful. Besides, the clouds in every zone in Everquest move west, so you can tell which way you’re heading anytime.

Here’s a quick description of the LOC command - Basically, when you get a LOC (by typing /loc) you’re presented with a set of three numbers. They will looks something like this – 1682.25 678.12 37.89.  You are only concerned with the first two numbers before the decimal, 1682 and 678.

The first number, 1682 is your north / south location. The second number, 678 is your east / west location. When you go north, the first number goes up and when you go south, the first number goes down. Same with the second number, except it goes east / west. (the third number is your altitude, I think, and was only used for testing, I think, and was supposed to be removed but never was, I think)

Now, go to one of the many Everquest sites on the internet and check their maps section. Find a map of Halas, Everfrost, Blackburrow, Qeynos Hills, North Qeynos, South Qeynos and West Karana with loc numbers and print them out. This will help you from getting lost and make learning areas much easier. Everfrost in particular is very easy to get lost in.

Another important thing to do is to program one of your hotbuttons for the /LOC command. Anytime you think you might get killed, hit that button. Scroll up and look at your /LOC when you die and you will be able to find your body easily. There is nothing more annoying than someone /shouting every minute “can someone help me find my corpse?” They should have learned how to use /LOC.

 

Another important thing to do is in your display settings. Click options and display. Change the “same resolution” setting to yes and change your resolution to the highest setting your 3D card can handle (800X600 for Voodoo2 and 1024X768 for TNT2 – the only cards I have used). Also, set your clipping plane all the way to the right. If the game runs really slow at this point, reduce the clipping plane until it gets back to normal. My 366 has no problems at all.

Now, hit the F10 key and you will go into full screen mode. This enhances the game greatly, not only because you can play the game full screen, but also because it allows you to see more of your surroundings. Important for those MOBs sneaking up on you.

You’ll want to drag the following buttons to your hot buttons: persona, spell book, sit, melee 1, bash skill button and anything else you think you might need. Also, don’t forget that /LOC button.

Levels 1-5:

You will start in the city of Halas. Find your guild (by using the maps you printed out). Follow the hallway all the way to the back room. The woman in the middle is your guild master and that’s who you give your guild letter to. Memorize your inner fire and minor healing spells. Next, find the person who sells level one spells. See how much burst of flame is going to cost you and remember that amount. Put one training point into one hand blunt and head toward Everfrost.

As you leave Halas, you’ll have to cross a body of water. You can either swim or take the raft. My advice to you is to swim as it’s an easy way to increase your swimming skill. You’ll get your swimming skill into the 30’s or 40’s just by doing swimming back and forth across this lake.

When you zone into Everfrost, you’ll come out in an area that has two tents with merchants. Left of these tents is the pathway into Everfrost Peaks. You’ll notice two guards half way down the path protecting the fine citizens of Halas from outside invasion (or something like that). These guards will be your best friend for the next 5 levels.

As you follow the pathway, it will eventually come out into Everfrost Peaks. Walk out into the open area where the local MOBs are walking back and forth, right in front of the pathway. You will want to focus on polar bear cubs, ice goblin whelps, wooly spiders, decaying skeletons and gnoll pups. Don’t kill the wolves, as the reward is not worth the risk. Also, don’t leave this area. Keep inner fire on you at all times as well.

Kill any MOB listed above walking alone. Don’t attack if there are other like-creatures close by as they will gang up on you. Wait until you get one alone, attack it and run up the pathway a little bit to get out of the traffic. Kill it and repeat. If you get in trouble, and at this level I consider trouble down to a bubble of health, run to the guards and they will kill it for you. Also, be sure to kill anything that carries a staff. Cracked staves sell for over 1PP to vendors.

Keep the following – skeleton bones, polar bear pelts (not ruined, the white polar belt pelts) and wooly fungus. Sell everything else.

The first 2hB weapon you get from a kill, keep it and alternate using it with your club. This will keep your 1hb and 2hb skill levels up as you level. There’s a sever penalty if you try to learn a new weapon skill at a higher level, so keep this up.

As soon as you get enough money for burst of flame, head back to Halas and buy it. We will now change your hunting technique a little. Stand just inside the pathway leading to the guards. You are far enough away from the traffic where they won’t bother with you. Find a MOB by itself and cast burst of flame on it. It will singe for 5 points of damage and come running to you. This is called pulling and is a much safer way to hunt. Kill it, pull another one and keep doing this over and over.

Buy all of your spells as soon as you get the money. I’ll leave it up to you as to how you use those spells, but you will find a use for all of them. So make sure you get them all.

Polar Bear pelts should be given (given, not sold to) to Cindl in Mac’s Kilts. She will give you money, EXP and a piece of patchwork armor. This is a great lower level quest, and a great way to get your armor. If you don’t have that piece of armor, wear it. If you already have it, sell it back to her.

As you stand outside your guild, there will be a guy (can’t remember his name) standing in the corner outside the guild to your left. Talk with him and you will get the wooly fungus quest. He will give you as jar to collect 8 of these. Combine 8 in the jar and return it to him for money, EXP and a spell. This is another great quest. Get another jar when you turn one in, and sell any spells you get duplicates of.

Buy a backpack as soon as you can afford one, and keep your bones in it. Halas has a lot of decaying skeletons and you’ll soon have quite a collection of bones. I’ll explain why later.

This is what you will do for the first three levels, and they will go by pretty fast. When you get level 4, you can afford to head out a into the peaks a little more, but don’t get too far as it’s easy to get lost. Also, at level 4, be sure to put a skill point into channeling at the guild. This skill will go up by itself and helps in keeping your concentration while casting spells.

At level 5, you get gate, so go ahead and roam all you want. If you get lost, just gate back. Just be sure to get a /LOC before you get killed!

Levels 5 – 8:

Your poison / disease spells will have to be bought in the plains of Everfrost, which is a dangerous place for such a young Shaman. Basically, head into the plains and stay close to the left edge until you run into a ruined temple. The merchant is located on the opposite side of the platform. Get your spells then gate back. No sense in taking any chances!

At level 5 you can head to the Blackburrow entrance and kill the stronger gnolls that guard the entrance. Don’t get too cocky however; be sure to con them all first before attacking. Their levels can vary and you don’t want to run into one two levels higher than you. Continue to kill everything and gate back to sell when you get full.

By this time, you probably have a full set of patchwork armor from the polar pelt quest. You can slowly upgrade that to leather if you have the money. Just be sure that you have all of your spells first.

When you get to level 7, you’ll want to go into Blackburrow, run through it as fast as you can, and zone into Qeynos Hills.  Blackburrow isn’t actually that difficult, but the chance of getting killed by a train is very high at your level and the loot is better in QH anyway. You’ll hunt in Blackburrow a little later.

In Qeynos Hills (QH), hunt anything that cons even or lower to you. Most everything will still give you XP at this point, except for the very low-level creatures. You’ll still use the loot they give however.

By this time, you should have saved up a lot of those bone chips. You have two choices here. First off, find a Necromancer in the zone, the higher the better. You can either sell him a stack of chips (20) for 1 or 2 PP (you’ll have an easier time selling them for 1pp), or do what I did. Save a backpack full for a total of 160 chips, and offer them directly to any Necromancer higher than 10th level. I generally charge 12PP per backpack of chips, but I have also gotten 15PP per pack on occasion. This is a great moneymaker and bone chips are easy to come by, especially in Everfrost. I still do this into my 18th level. Kill skeletons, save bones, and sell a pack full for 12PP.

Get to know QH well. Walk around and con everything. Kill everything you can. Go to the ruins and kill regular skeletons when you can, just be careful of the Putrid Skeletons and Dread Corpses. Kill them too when you can, but that will be a few levels yet.

Keep a particular eye out for rabid wolves. Kill those and keep their putrid pelts. You’ll also notice rabid grizzlies running around. You can’t kill them yet, but when you can, you’ll be spending a lot of time doing so (see levels 9 – 14). In the mean time, take those putrid wolf pelts into North Qeynos (NQ). In the Temple of Life, you’ll find a ramp leading up to a teleporter. Run into the teleporter and find Priestess Clauria (or something close to that). Give her those putrid wolf pelts for some coin and XP.

AT level 8, you’ll need to go back to Halas and put 1 skill point into meditation. Now you can use that meditate button when looking at your spell book, speeding up the time required to scribe spells and lessening the time to regain lost mana.

Just keep doing this and you’ll be able to make levels rather quickly. Be sure to use your sicken spell when you’re battling those harder creatures. It will greatly speed up their deaths.

Levels 9 – 14:

Level 9. This is it. You get SOW and your life changes forever. Gate back to Halas and buy all your spells. Remember the poison / disease spells are in the plains at the ruins. Sense animals can be bought in Surefall Glade. Spirit sight can be bought in he mage guild in South Qeynos, though you’ll have to get someone to buy it for you.

Keep yourself SOWd at all times. Don’t go into a battle without it. Also, you can offer it as an auction for donation. Some people will pay 5PP for a SOW. You’ll often see people asking for a SOW and willing to donate. Never pass that chance up. Immediately offer your services and run to where they are to claim the donation.

The next important spell is tainted breath. Again, this will help kill a creature much quicker.

Now you will start to make quick XP and money. This is the best quest in the game in my opinion and I am still doing it on occasion at level 18! The rabid grizzly quest.

As you out from the Blackburrow entrance, you’ll notice a small rise on your left. Over that rise is a valley of sorts that runs all the way to the guard tower. This is the main spawning grounds for the rabid grizzly. Two other spawning points are across the road on the other side of QH, between the river and newbie area in the hills.

Con any rabid grizzly you see and if it’s even, attack it. Use your sicken and tainted breath spells (they stack) and melee it. Keep the skin, heal, meditate, and find another one to kill. Their spawn times will change from day to day, so if there are none around, kill rabid wolves and anything else you can. Remember to keep killing at all times.

Take those rabid bear hides to the same Priestess in the Temple of Life you gave the putrid wolf hides to. At level 9, I was getting almost a full bubble of XP, 1 – 2PP and a spell scroll per hide! This is by far the quickest way to level! I’ve turned in 7 pelts at a time (sometimes you get lucky), made 12PP and made the next level! I went from level 9 to 14 quicker than I went from 8 – 9. This just cannot be beat! Never stop hunting rabid grizzlies.

The server I am on has very little people so the hunting was never crowded. You may have a harder time if your server is crowded and everyone is trying to hunt the rabid griz.

I went from a couple PP to over a hundred during this time. As soon as you can, upgrade your club to a better weapon. I bought a morning star for 13PP from the monk guild in North Qeynos. Just always try to have the best weapon you can afford. Also, now is the time to start buying banded armor when you can afford it to replace what you have. Banded usually sells for 1PP per AC, with volume discounts given if bought in a set.

You can also head into the Qeynos aqueduct system anytime and kill the injured rat for the +5hp earrings. There’s some other nice items to be had down there, and guides are available on the internet on how to hunt in the aqueducts.

If you get a group, you can head over to West Karana and hunt bigger game, but this is in no way is as beneficial as the rabid grizzly.

This should quickly get you to level 14.

Levels 14-18:

Level 14 you get your next spells. Buy all of them.  Invisibility to Animals can be bought in Surefall Glade.  Poison / disease spells are in the plains of Everfrost.

Continue to kill rabid grizzlies. In the down time, head to West Karana and kill bigger game. You can also head into Blackburrow now feeling relatively safe, though you still have to be careful.

Blackburrow has two levels of gnolls. Regular gnolls and hard gnolls. Regular gnolls, like the burly, patrolling and guardsman gnolls will leave you alone at this level. The elite guardsman, commanders and brewers are what you are after. Kill them carefully for XP and good loot. Don’t be afraid to run if you get in trouble. SOW will save you and prevent you from dying by getting you to the zone.

Another good way to make money is by killing bandits in WK in a group. Bandits drop bronze weapons sometimes, worth 1 – 3 PP each, and human bandits drop bandit sashes. Bandit sashes can be turned into Chesgard at the Paladin guild for XP, coin and a weapon, sometimes a bronze. Sell the bronze for money to a merchant.

So, during these levels, kill higher level gnolls in Blackburrow, keep on the rabid grizzly and do big game and bandits in WK. This will make you a lot of money and get you good, quick XP.

Another thing you should do is get yourself a magical weapon. Around this area, it’s easy to get a Bloodforge hammer for 20 – 20 PP. As soon as you get one, start also killing willowisps (they require a magical weapon to hit).

Willowisps drop one of three things. Burned out lightstone, lightstone or greater lightstone. Destroy and burned out lightstone you get. Sell and lightstone you get. Keep any greater lightstone (GLS) you get.

I saved up two backpacks full of GLSs. In North Karana, there is a gypsy camp with a merchant named Myrissa (or something close to that). Give her the GLS one at a time and she will give you XP, coin and a book. Sell the book back to her for about 8PP each. Another great moneymaker. That’s over 128PP for two backpacks full of GLSs!

You’ll hear people talk about willowisp island (Erud’s Crossing) and how great it is to hunt willowisps there. Forget it. I spent a few days there but had much better luck in QH and WK. Don’t waste your time.

Phew! That’s a lot to read. This is what I have done thus far and it seems to have done well for me. In no way do I make any claims that this is the best way to play your character in EQ. It worked great for me, and should work great for you also, but you may find your path goes a different direction.
