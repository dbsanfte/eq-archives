I played UO after I left EQ and I enjoyed it very much.
I loved the skill based system of character creation.
(start a fighter/crafter, later change to a mage/crafter. then change again to a mage/tamer. ALL with the same character).
I also really liked the crafting and housing in UO.
I would spend all day making stuff and decorationg my Small Tower with the stuff I made.

The problems I had with it were-
1) Really didnt like the graphics. 2d was too cartoony (reminded me of Link for Nintendo) and 3d was just ugly.
2) Isometric view would make me dizzy sometimes.
3) performance was horrid. While I ran AO, dao.0c and even EQ (then and now) well on my system, UO would lag and glitch, rubberband and hitch far too much.
4) Ease of use- Game was just too difficult to operate efficiently. Macros helped, but to make it more efficient you HAD to buy 3rd party software (UOAssist).
5) AI- PC movement was bad enough, but MOB AI was just a joke. I could run away from anything and heal up while the MOB "2 stepped" its way to me. No- Minimal challenge in PvE fighting.
6) AoS- changing the game to be more item based than skill based was not the best way to go IMO. I had to carry 3 different outfits, and change depending on what I was fighting.

What I'm WAITING for is a game that blends the "soul" of UO (skill, crafting, etc) with the "body" of EQ ( look, feel, content, view perspective, etc) and NOT screw it up all to bloody hell (Ala SWG)

I only tried UO once a loooong ago and I know one night's play isn't giving any MMO a fair try but I just don't understand why UO is held up as the standard all others should try to emulate. That game has changed a ton yet still not nearly as much as EQ has in a shorter amount of time, I won't even pretend to know how UO is now though.

But even if you overlook things that were present originally in UO like free-for-all PvP world wide, looting the entire corpse of dead players even to the point of mutilating their corpse and the generally repeative gameplay of clicking combat on and repeatedly hitting 1 spell just like how the crafting worked you still have a game with almost nothing I found actually fun to spend my time playing. UO was terrific if what you were looking for was a "world simulator" where you can go up to any tree hack at it with axe to get logs, chip at those logs with a knife to make widgits 1-20 and combine said widgits to make product 1-40, ad nauesum.

The AI was braindead, the game engine was horridly bug ridden, servers were laggy- the world had vast stretches of emptiness interrupted only by player built urban sprawl of the same random houses and castles. The skill system while unique for the ability to create any kind of character you wanted, allowed you to basically "max" your character within a couple days and the general lack of interesting places to adventure rapidly lost my interest.
