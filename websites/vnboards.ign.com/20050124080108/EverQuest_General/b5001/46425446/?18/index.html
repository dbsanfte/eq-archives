Date Posted: 3/9/03 12:16pm Subject: RE: Do You Hope EQ2 Has PoP-Type Insta-Travel? - Date Edited: 3/9/03 12:28pm (1 edits total) Edited By: Riktor_before_IGN
There may be a handful on them, something similar to the Luclin model or the original wizard/druid teleport spells, but from what I read in one magazine article about EQ2 having to travel on foot/horse to reach most areas is going to be at least as prominent as it was in the original EQ world. They explained one of the "classes" by design is a trader who would hire a coach or wagon and have to defend it as you take it from town to town and sell off products.

Do I want to have instant ports available to everyone at any time in EQ2? Well, I agree it does make for a much smaller world, but they also greatly promote folks spreading out, before PoP and especially before Luclin, players largely all congregated in the handful of xp-chain zones, Oasis>LOIO>OT>Dreadlands/Karnor. This chain for the most part has been broken up now or at least expanded to include some choices. I gotta say though, by the time you are working on a 4th or 5th alternate character, traveling for 20min to an hour over ground you've covered over and over with previous characters is just wasted time really. If you do add world portals, perhaps add a minimum character level to access them or some other yardstick, that enforces the large world feeling early in the game and speeds play later on.

Ideally, I'd like to see a world where folks are not encouraged to all clump up in the most predicatable "easy" outdoor areas like the above mentioned for no other reason then you can't find a group anywhere else without a guild. I don't mind dying in the depths of some dungeon, at least we are having an exciting time exploring and surviving down there and hopefully are making good progress for the risk involved, just don't bore me with the routine and effortless combat of the outdoor areas.

So you need a world small enough so that the player population can fill most of it evenly, but deep enough to present challenges and rewards suitable for the difficulty.
