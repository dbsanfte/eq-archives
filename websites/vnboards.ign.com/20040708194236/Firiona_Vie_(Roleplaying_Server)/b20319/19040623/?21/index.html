If you're shouting in Los Angeles, I can't hear you in New York. I can't even hear you in San Francisco. I probably can't even hear you from a block away.

It is certainly an inconvenience for locating group replacements, but it does make sense (at least to me) from a roleplaying point of view.

Very simple: the rules are designed to do two things:
- Be more realistic
- Serve as a discouragement to play on the server

Why the second one? Because there has to be a reason you want to play here OTHER than just ease of leveling/grouping/etc. And that reason is Roleplaying. It's a trade-off: you give up the familiar/convenient rules of normal servers in exchange for the knowledge that there are a greater number of Roleplayers on this server.

If that trade-off isn't good enough for someone, then the new ruleset is working fine in my opinion.
