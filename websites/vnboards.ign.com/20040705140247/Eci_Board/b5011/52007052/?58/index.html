"FD does break aggro, i managed to ditch aggro more than once....you just have to stay down long enough."

Umm, no. Medz you might want to stop by Shadowknight.org to get some clarification on how FD works.

When you feign successfully there is a check to see if you lose aggro, mobs under level 35 will always lose aggro, mobs over 35 have a small chance to do so. If the mobs are roamers then that is the only check they will make. If they are static spawns with a defined spawn point then they will make another check when they reach that spawn point. This check has a much higher success rate than the first check when you actually feign.

It's possible to feign off all aggro with one feign but it has nothing to do with pure duration. You have to stay down until the mobs return to their spawn points and even then you can still retain aggro even if you stay down for 3 hours.

The problem with multiple roams is the 'strength in numbers'. When you FD, you have a chance of loseing agro w/ each one, not all or none, but a chance w/ each one individually. Thus, if you stand up a few mins later, and if just 1 didn't lose agro, it will start running at you again. What's this do? Add agro to every mob in assist range.

Most roamers do have spawn points, but they don't always go there. Especially after they chased you off their normal path. Ever see mobs camping zone ins?

That's why you'll see monks flop over and over, hopefully standing up and feigning again before the mob actually turns to run, and getting another chance to wipe agro before the still agro'ed mob calls for assist from his cronies.

Not trying to get into who did what and if on purpose, just going into the effects of FD.
