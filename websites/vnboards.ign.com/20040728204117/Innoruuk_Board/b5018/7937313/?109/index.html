well, as much as I think it probably *should* be sufficient force type of agreement, theres inherent problems with that.

First, some guilds *do* have the capability, but often have to wait for people to log on to reach the numbers required, whereas other guild have 3 to 4 times the capability, and can assemble forces much quicker. So if waiting is the only option for some guilds, why begrudge rhem the chance to take down VS?

Also, who decides 'sufficient force'? That is a highly subjective term. 18 people? Ok what level of 18 people? What if you have 18 people and no clerics...is THAT sufficient force?

Maybe we should outline it better as 'good showing'. ie the guild has 2 strong groups there, and are waiting for others. Maybe even 10-12 people. This allows people not only a chance if they have to wait for the majority of their high level players, but also prevents too much of a burden on guilds going down to check (because they don't need to take 18 people to 'reserve' the chance).

Shitty situation. The sentiment behind these encounters is good, but the mechanics of them leave MUCH to be desired. And thats game design. Of course, the best solution might be to just actually JOIN FORCES with people down there waiting, and come to some mutaully beneficial loot agreement before hand. Kind of a mutual respect type thing. But that has probelms too, because its hard to work with people you don't know well.

Mutual respect worked for the dragons for awhile, but it eventually deteriorated and GM's had to be called in. Its their ruling that we have followed ever since. Same thing will probably happen with this.
