Is it just that the games becoming simpler, or are we also becoming more jaded? Will any game ever recapture the depth and spirit of the first MMORPG we ever played? Before we were familiar with the genre; before we knew words like "pulling" and "main tank"?

There's an exciting sense of the unknown when you play a genre for the first time. That feeling was even stronger in the early days of EQ and UO, when there were thousands of people together all experiencing it for the first time. Everyone was learning their way together, and was an adventure!

But new games can't produce that same excitement, when we can say "I know what this is and I know how it's going to work" before we ever launch the icon. It's like playing chess with a fancy set of pieces. It may look different, but it's still the same game.

To recapture this feeling, we either need to put ourselves back in that frame of mind - focus on the story, on our friends, on enjoying the content without getting bogged down in the mechanics. Or, we need a new genre of game. Something beyond MMORPG. Something that breaks the mold again.

And if I knew what that something was, I'd be out writing it instead of trolling these boards. 8)
