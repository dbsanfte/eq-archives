I love these memory threads.. It almost puts the old zest for EQ I use to have back into me.

There was no need for trying to powerlevel a character, you just simply logged on into your ghetto character (Because everyone was pretty ghetto when a dragoon dirk was "uber") and started killing folks.

Good old times... Item loot times, no doubt.. Well, in all honesty, the best times were even before that.. They were when you could still fear/charm PCs.

That was the era of the might of the rusty weapon and patchwork armor! Yes, when weapon slots were lootable and people actually had a fear of dien.

That was the era when an enchanter was one of the most feared player in the fight. The enchanter could by himself, take out a group. Fear the healer, charm the tank and send it against the casters. All the while, no one ever really figuren who or where he was.. Yes, this was when the illusions would hold up under pvp combat.

This was the era of item ganking. Zone line camping to find those weak and hurt to get a quick, one shot kill in the hopes of looting that "UBER SBS!" or maybe even, heaven help you, a flowing black robe!!

This was the era of the newbie land raids; where you'd see massive "dark armies" forming up in the commons to attack the helpless humans in Freeport! It did not matter that this has been done 80 times already today, it was still on the agenda! Ahh, the might of 20 level 5 dark newbies armed with the state of the art patchwork armor, apprentice robes (SR. Apprentice robes, if they were UBER NEWBIES!), rusty weapons (OMG! If someone had a Fine Steel weapon, they were the lord of the battle field!).

This was the era of the commonlands being an area where a newbie feared to tred. He feared it because he knew that there was certain death waiten for him at the hands of some opposing force. Yes, it was not uncommon to see a gang (or gaggle, whichever) of shorties spreading terror and hate throughout the common lands! The elves would show up for a bit, but they'd not last long. The entrance to Nek Forest was ALWAYS guarded and it was a task just to get in there! But once in there, it was a grand time killing the snake murderes!

Yes, this was the era where things like root and snare spelt death; where someone was fearful of seeing that lone druid; where bards and rogues were laughed at, and the Cleric was useless in pvp.

Those were the good times.

So now that I'm a bit more sober...

The most fun night I had in EQ was when I was in Dark Storm, 6 of us went into Sebilis to camp the fungi tunic. At the time, Sebilis was pretty much up for grabs, the Prophets had been known to show their faces there every once in awhile to try and "reclaim" it, but they were just a mere shadow compared to 12p.

It had been one of those nights, met some new people, played with some old friends, generally just having a blast. The party we had finally ended up with was myself, Valent, level 60 warrior, Taelon, level 60 enchanter, Enfer, level 60 cleric, Ixtha, level 57 wizard, Murgos (I think?) level 56 shaman, and Minileed, level 52 (or 51?) necromancer. For some reason when we got to the fungi king, our group just started clicking...we killed shit faster and better than I had practically ever done outside of a raid...we were just feelin the vibe. Then the Prophets show up, 12 of them, looking for some blood. Naturally, they ask where we are, we tell them the crypts, disco, whole bunch of places, and then I told my group that we needed to move down to the juggs...they'd never get us there. The whole group was pretty nervous, besides myself and Taelon, they had never been down that low without 40 people, and even then they almost all died to Trakanon. But they did recognize that we needed to go down, and down we went.

It was surprisingly easy enough to clear the juggs, almost too easy, and by the time we got to the door to the reets, the Prophets had dropped off the ledge and were heading down to the king. So naturally I tell the group that we need to go to the reet cubby, because they SURE as hell wouldn't get us there, and with renewed confidence we rocked on into the reet cubby.

What the group didn't know at this point was that I had planned on taking a group to go kill Prot and Tola that night, and all I needed was some sort of catalyst to start heading down that way. So instead of killing the juggs and reets in the hallways, I head up to Trak's lair and clear out all the juggs but 2 Tola guards and 2 Prot guards. The Prophets, at the fungi king at this point, are clearly frustrated that they haven't found us. They had searched the place up and down and in and out and we were no where to be found, so the first bad pull they had at the king, they all gated out.

But we were still in the cubby kicking ass and taking names, and Prot and Tola had just been given the green light to kill. So up we went into Trak's lair, respawn happening in about 5 minutes, and we snuck along the wall to get behind Prot. We buffed up a little, medded a little, and I went out and pulled the Protector and I got my ass severely beat down, almost died even. Taelon was slingin mezzes around like nobodies business, he was rooting, he was dishing out clarity, I swear to you the man had to have like 6 arms to do all this. Enfer was pounding that CH key, throwing out some remedies. The shaman was slowing shit like mad, I couldn't believe how fast he slowed Prot and not manage to get aggro from it. Ixtha and Minileed were puttin some hurtin down on Prot, and his life was slowly, but surely, moving. How we managed to live through that is quite frankly beyond me, but we did it. Prot died and gave up a Hardened Clay Bracelet.

But if that wasn't enough, we still had Tola to kill. Tola proved to be quite the slippery bastard. Taelon at this point had stopped using mez altogether, and was solely relying on root to keep our asses alive. It was pretty funny tanking Tola and watching Taelon run back and forth across the screen, jumping, and then see 5 juggs running after him. But we dropped Tola who gave up a robe, and with 3 juggs still left and me trying to hold aggro on all three while Enfer somehow manage to heal me just in time, Ixtha ported us out.

Minileed was the reciever of the Tola robe, and was quite happy about it. Ask him sometime.
