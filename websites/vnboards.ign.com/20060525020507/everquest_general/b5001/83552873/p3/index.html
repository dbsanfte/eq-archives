Now you've done it. Thanks for making me remember why EQ of 5 years ago was still better than anything since...

Rewind the clock back to this:
- bronze items are useful
- rubicite is godly
- breaking PoF or PoH takes hours if not a day
- manastone still drops from the EE, and isn't nerfed to uselessness
- twinkage didn't exist, and people died in every zone and at every level w/o skill and communication
- lguk, solb were hotbeds
- trains from the basement in Unrest, Castle Mistmoore and dead-side lguk were legendary
- Lum the Mad was the first URL I would head to after work every day
- most importantly: everyone talked to each other and made you feel a part of a huge experience

Hell I would even return for that maybe, and I haven't played EQ since LDoN - and even then I had just come back to try it after initially quitting during Kunark as the wonderment was fading.

There was something about the whole world back then - the music, hearing that theme as you logged in, the sense of everyone exploring together, danger at literally every corner, trains that would alternate making you laugh or cry depending on what happened - it was rough and not easy at all, and everyone LOVED it.

I've played EQ, CoH, DAoC and WoW - and each time I get tired and quit is due mainly to one reason: they stop being fun since people become selfish asses, don't talk, don't group and stop making it feel like a community.

This thread brings back alot of memories for me. I played on Cazic-Thule when EQ came out for close to a year. I quit about the time Kunark came out.

I remember early EQ so fondly. It was the first MMORPG I played, and I remember being wowed by the concept. Having played a dark elf, I remember, and love most, Neriak and Nektulos forest. Like previously stated, I remember the auctions in the tunnel between North Ro and East Commons, endlessly camping lizards for rubicite armor, journeyman boots (jboots!), perpetually seeking a Spirit of the Wolf (SoW) buff, how vast the Karanas were the first time I happened upon them, and God, who can forget the epic trains? Trains were virtually a part of the game world, and they added so much drama to the experience. It's funny because, even to this day, as I presently play WoW, I'm still not use to a mob only pursuing me for a short bit, as I was so accustomed to getting chased relentlessly across the entire zone.

My greatest upset with EverQuest II (which I never bought) was, that they didn't capitalize on the nostalgic aspect like they should have. Everyone wanted to revisit the old world again, but things were so drastically changed that the familiarity was lost. What a shame. Poor planning on Sony's part.

Does anyone know if there are any old-school, server-specific, discussion forums? I'd like to chat with Cazic-Thule vets like myself again.

As for the old-school server idea, I think it is a great one.
