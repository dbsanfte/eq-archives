Don't buy a tank off ebay, you can get a tank for a few dollars above wholesale at PetCo.

If you were able to keep a salt water tank up you should have no problems with pirhana's. Keep them well fed or they will all attack the weakest one and eat it until you have none left(best bet is always have a small school of guppies in with them). Do more water changes then you normally would since they are really messy eaters and foul the water up pretty quickly. Other then that they are pretty easy to take care of as babies but once they get older you will have to get a much bigger tank to accomidate them. Somewhere along the lines of 15 gallons per fish. LoL this is funny, I just wanna make a couple comments. Im somewhat of a expert in aquariums and fish, both fresh and saltwater. First thing i wanna say is pirahna (true) only gets the size of your hand. Generally 5-6 in is max. They are illegal in most southern states. In my state texas you must get a license then when it dies cut its head off and sent it to austin. There's a wild population of pirahna in atleast one texas lake. Generally finding pirahna is basically impossible considering the ban in southern states. Many stores sell cousins of the pirahna such as pacu , silver dollars, tetras (yup even little neons are cousins to the pirahnas). Pirahna is nothing like what you have seen on movies. I got a book with a guy in shorts fishing them out of the amazon ... Nope hes never been bit. They get there reputation by swimming up tributaries that dry up and isolate them in small ponds. They eat everything in the pond , and then begin to starve. If a cow or just about anything else wanders into that pond of starving fish with huge teeth , they will eat them. The teeth are also somewhat overblown. A majority of the plaqured pirahna people have seen have there lips cut away to show there teeth and make them look more vicious. Personally I think there are alot of fish that make decent pets, but I wouldnt qualify pirahna as one of them. Ive been working with fresh and saltwater fish for over 20 yrs in a retail enviroment.

Transport - get 5 gal bucket , battery operated pump , and you should be good to go. You can bring them home in bags (standard) but be sure the store has pure oxygen. Fish generally spend 24-48 hrs in bags with pure oxygen on there flight from wholesalers to me.

Frogs, like pirahna, aren't cuddly creatures like a cat or dog you want to be able to play with and cuddle.

I've been bitten once b y my frog but he was too young t ohave developed his teeth entirely so I wasn't wounded, but you do need to be careful when handling them. Depending on what sex your frog is it will chirp, or get fat. i think mine is a female because she's fat adn have only heard the frog hiss when it tried to eat one of my cats from behind its cage.


Their tank isn't a hassle, just make sure htey have clean bedding, water, and mist them daily. Room temperature is ok enough for them, if you want to keep a light on a timer its fine just make sure it doesn't cook your frog.

There are times when creatures like these as well as some reptiles have been known to stop eating for a period but it is usually only temporary.

Just remember to always keep your frog moist because they don't drink, they absorb moisture through their skin.
