No offence Bailan but if you want to buy a game now then suggesting Vanguard that is unlikley to be out for <several months> or DDO that people will probably finish in 3-4 weeks doesn't seem to be the way to go at all.

I am going to assume that TheArabianKnight has played or considered games like WoW, CoH/CoV, GW - or maybe wants to consider EQ1 as one of the possibilities ... well:

The graphics were revamped to DX9. There is an awful lot of content. It is indeed relatively cheap. The bugs are long gone (new ones aside). Instances are well done, EQ1 being the first game to introduce them. There has been a lot written about it.

Against: there is an awful lot of content. Yes this is an advantage but the sheer size may put you off. In some ways it is dated. There are people in game but many are very powerful and eventually you do need a guild. EQ1 is very much two games; the first game is the grind to become powerful, the second game was built around raids into very powerful places, lots of planning and good leaders needed - even today. That side of things though is a long time down the road.

If you haven't played WoW then you can do a lot worse than give EQ1.
