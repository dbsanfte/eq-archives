Well for those neighsayers who are saying DaOC is close to eq, so I'm not going to play! Of course it's going to be close to eq, they want you to have some familiarity to the eq so they can grab some of us who want it to be like eq. As for no raiding, the raiding is done through the questing which over time becomes more engaging and hellacious in difficulty. The game is centered around 4 things; Skills(armoring, seiging, fletching), Questing, RVR, NPC fighting. I've been on the beta for over a month now and I actually feel like I accomplish something in DaOC in a nights work. Most of your lvl can be done through questing. And the RVR I have been engaged in, for now, was not too exciting. Though I can imagine it gets extremely exciting when you start seiging and driving deep into a realm after you have breeched it's defences. Just imagine the newbies looks when a force of humans knights, archers and casters come flying deep inside there territory blowing the hell out of stuff.

Class balance: the class balance in albion is right in the pocket. The scout is very powerful, but then they should be powerful. Mintrels are powerful too, extremely powerful. So are Palidans, and battle clerics. Put all your points in smite and rejuvenation, get a 2hb weapon and you got a cleric that can kick the shit out of most tanks. See the game isn't designed to be balanced the way were used to in eq, you balance your own character how you want it. If you want to have ressurection early before other clerics put all your points into rejuvenation and you get spells earlier. If you want to be a sick ass 2hs tank, put all your points into slashing and specialize in 2hs. It's all in how you play the character and how you use your skill points. Why was that scout so powerful? because they put the points into bowmanship and use the right arrows, some scouts will put all their points into tracking. Does that make the one who can track more powerful than the one that can pull a mob and down it with 3 bow shots. Maybe, tracking in the game can be extremely valuable to the right party.

Well my 2 cents, but it's not a horrible game, dont' let people tell you that. They obviously haven't played the beta, cause if they really had they would be as hooked as I am. And I love EQ even with it's shortcomings, but DaOC's shortcomings are negligable and the dev team does listen when there is a problem. My only gripe is the lag when you get 100 people attacking each other, well I'm sure they will figure that one out somehow.
