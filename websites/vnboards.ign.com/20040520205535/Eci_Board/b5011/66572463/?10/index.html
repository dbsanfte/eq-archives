As far as the bars go, the better they taste the more they cost.

As for the shakes, it can be cheaper if you get powder and mix it with low carb milk. I have a protein powder that has no carbs that I mix with carb-counter milk and drink after working out. It's a bit more work to mix it yourself but I get 16 servings for $10.99 as opposed to $2+ bucks per serving for Atkins brand drinks. They are usually in the health/excercise/vitamin section of the store, just make sure you check the label and get one that does not have sugar in it.

If your concern is just getting proper nutrients and stuff, you can order vitamins online that are significantly cheaper than what you'll find in the store.

Some more notes on the Atkins diet.

Don't get stuck on the idea, "I can only eat this or that.."

I have come up with several good meals. A lot of stores sell low-carb tortilla's. These are great for a lot of things. I take some chicken pieces, bacon, a little lettuce and olives and just a dab of mayo or ranch dressing and make a killer sub-thingy out of it. There are lots of other usese as well.

Low-carb bread that has 7 net carbs per slice is good as well. If you gotta have your bread, this is the answer. When I cook a hamburger at home, I take ONE piece of this bread and toast it for my burger.

Steak steak steak steak. Did I mention you can have steak? A small steak and some salad or green beans makes a wonderful supper.

Eggs. Some people dont like them, but a nice ham and cheese omelett is a good change now and then.

One thing that helped me a lot was buying a small George Foreman grill. This is great for cooking a small one-person meal rather quickly. I can come home from work, pull a frozen burger, steak, pork chop or really any kind of meat out of the freezer, and let George cook it for me in less than 20 minutes.

There are lots of snacks out nowadays too. Candy bars, low-carb ice cream (Fudgecicles!!!!) and even chips.

The key for the whole diet for me tho is my "Cheat day". Once every two weeks, I set one day aside (Saturday or Sunday) that I can eat anything I want. Funny thing is, I usually wind up not cheating that much, its just the knowing that I CAN cheat that helps me through the rough times.

Right now I feel better than I have felt in years. I weigh less than I have since Reagan was President, and my original goal of losing 35 pounds has been replaced with 50. I have replaced 3 pairs of "fat-boy" jeans and have gone up 3 knotches on my belt.

With this diet I can actually see myself approaching the magic 200 pound mark eventually, even tho its still 40 pounds away. The light side of 200 is something I havent seen since high school.

And through it all, I have never been low on energy, (in fact I have more because of the protein) my blood pressure has gone down enough that the doctor has reduced me to only 2 pills instead of 3, and then there is the immeasurable effect on my own self esteem.
Who knows? Maybe someday I may even start dating again... maybe.
