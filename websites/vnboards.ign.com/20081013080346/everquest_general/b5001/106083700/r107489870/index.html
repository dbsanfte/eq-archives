Date Posted: 1/24 2:22pm Subject: Why not package standalone original EQ for old timers?
When EQ debuted in the late 90s, it was incredibly fun to me. But by the Luclin expansion, I was burned out. Too much of a time sink. I remember fondly those early days of EQ, even before Kunark, before the planes of Fear and Hate, heck, even before the Temple of Solusek Ro quests....anyone even remember those?

If Sony were to somehow release a version of the orignal Everquest, before any expansions, using the old game engine, I'd certainly purchase it and play it. Instead of large servers to host it, maybe allow players to multilink privately for grouping or raids. I have read somewhere that a Sony rep has stated that the old game engine is gone, and will never be back..in other words, there is no magical backup copy of the game as it began. I think that's unfortunate. I think marketing classic EQ would bring back a lot of veterans, and put some more $$$ in their pockets.

I'm sure many will cite the everquest emulator. But I've tried that, and it sucks. It's not the classic eq experience, in no way shape or form. I guess what I'm dreaming of is Old Freeport and Neriak....having to use BOATS to cross continents...whining over whether bronze armor should go to the warrior or the pally....it was just a lot more intimate back in those days.....smaller number values on the gear stats...STRATEGY to win encounters....I really miss that.

Does anyone think it would be a BAD idea? If so, how? For a game that's approacing its ten year anniversary, I can't believe they haven't already done it. But maybe I'm missing something. Or maybe I'm just too old fashioned.......old fashioned and online gaming....who would have guessed? :P

Date Posted: 3/8 7:44pm Subject: RE: Why not package standalone original EQ for old timers?
They had a few servers that "worked" their way through the expansions as if from day 1 (other than the graphics were still updated models).

They opened 2 servers but people mass raided all the mobs to open the next expansion and within a month half the expansions had been opened for play. Within a year I think all of them are open now and they are nothing but normal servers.

The sheer number of exploits, not to mention bots and other fast leveling strats, that came to light on The Sleeper and The Combine boggles the mind. Plus, as you said, folks just leveled as fast as they could and banged out the requirements to open the next expansion. Relatively few people just "enjoyed the journey".

I think an Original EQ type of setup would work..........for 6 months or so. Then people would be clamoring for expansions to be added. You have to remember that the sentimental folks who still exist, largely no longer play the game (this isn't the same game as the one that existed in '99, after all). And while everyone seems to remember fondly all of the good things they experienced, few people make note of any of the BAD things. Like trying to get into a group camping desirable named in Guk or CT. Anyone remember "the list" to get into an AoF group? How about losing all of your treasured items because you consented a group mate to DRAG your corpse (remember, drag was the same as loot for permission purposes)?

I'd like this concept but I think it would be extremely difficult to implement simply due to all of the advancements that have occurred that the playerbase takes for granted now. Most newer folks don't go to zones outside of the current and maybe previous two expansions....unless they're forced to do so (for quests). I know of level 80 characters created in the TSS era who don't know how to get to Lavastorm or Skyfire or Chardok or The Hole or what PoP zones require flags (not levels) to enter. So they'd have to include some "new" features that didn't exist back then. The XMI based, customizable UI for example. I too miss the days of way back when I started (Thanksgiving Day of '99, after putzing around on various lowbies created on my best friend's account). But as many bad things about the game that I hate today (most of them revolving around sparse server populations), I don't know if I could/would go back to those halcyon days if given the choice. Plus, consider how SOE would see it. The progression servers' populations tailed off sharply after the first month or so, to the point where the two servers had to be merged. That doesn't bode well for a possible retry.

From webpage

About EQClassic
EQClassic is a project currently being developed with an intent to recreate EverQuest as it existed before the release of the Shadows of Luclin. Based on an original spawn database, this server aims to be the most accurate recreation of the EverQuest servers ever created. Using a client that predates many of the expansions, the EQClassic server will utilize its own custom loginserver and will be capable of hosting thousands of players eager to play EverQuest again at its prime. The server will feature among other things, the original user interface, character models, zones, spells, sounds, textures, music and NPC spawns. The server will be a great experience for past players of EverQuest as well as new players who never had the opportunity to see Norrath at its prime. Although this server is largely based on a classic version of EverQuest, we feel that community input is essential in running a successful server and we would love to hear your thoughts, concerns and ideas.
