 		Date Posted: 1/26/02 2:01pm Subject: RE: Evils and Goods - grouping troubles?
I am not so sure it is a bug, and therefore would also not be an exploit. It is in fact the ONLY way anyone good can EVER get their corpse summoned which is an absolutely vital capability to have access to at some point. I do not see them changing this ever, or at least not until they add some reasonable-to-attain item for goods that allows them to summon corpse.

As to how to RP it, I can think of lots of ways. The good person (and probably evil person) decides that while they may be helping their opoosite alignment some, they are helping their OWN alignment even more, so the balance is tipped slightly the way theu wish it to be tipped. Or, they are simply using the other person for their own ends. (And of course the other person may be thinking the same exact thing.) Or their alignment is secret, and they are simply biding their time to let fall a surprising strike on the other person. Or, they know the other person is their opposite alignment, but they know he's a peon and they wish that person to trust them until they can find out who the REAL bosses are. Or...whatever. Lots of RP possibilities here.

Incidentally, the same trick works for guilds as it does with alignments. And while I think it was originally not planned to work this way, I grow more convinced every day that rather than be annoyed that it's possible, that they are pleased by it and glad it does work this way, because it allows interesting RP scenarious, solves the summon corpse problem without any additional coding, and overall simply makes things more interesing. It's a PAIN to keep these groups. A LD of an evil or good means you have to reform the group again. Someone leaves, may have to reform. Wanna add someone new, definitely must reform. To make these groups work, and to a lesser extent the guilds with both alignments, you really have to work at it. Which means that the common goal is worth the extra hassle. And you have to keep track of who can buff/heal/rez whom and so on. It really does make for a more complex and interesting game.
