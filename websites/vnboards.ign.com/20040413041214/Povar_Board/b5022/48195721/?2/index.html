Well, it all depends on what you want. If you just want internet access for multiple machines, a hub is fine. Usually you will need to have your ISP provide multiple IPs (static or dynamic, depending on availability/price) Not much by way of security with a hub, but firewall software is easy to come by.

Personally, I prefer routers. Most routers have build in (hardware) firewall capabilities. You only need one IP addy. Most routers are as low as 70 dollars.

Anywho, my preference is either
linksys or netgear, but for the most part, you learn which vendors to trust by experience. If you are purchasing one from a store, seriously look into their return/exchange policy.

A hub and a router are fairly different devices. For a client/server network you can use a router to share an internet connection, but if you are using a peer-to-peer network you must use a router.

Most home networks are peer-to-peer (meaning you have multiple computers connected to the internet and each other without the use of a server, they go to the router directly and from there to the internet). A client/server network means you use 1 computer that has an internet connection and the other computers on the network are connected to it through a hub and share the internet that way.

If you have no network set up at this time, I recommend a peer-to-peer network for ease of setup and less hardware required.

I personally recommend the Linksys Network Anywhere Cable/DSL Router. Runs about $59.99 US and is pretty much bombproof. We all know how well Microsoft(tm) stuff works.
