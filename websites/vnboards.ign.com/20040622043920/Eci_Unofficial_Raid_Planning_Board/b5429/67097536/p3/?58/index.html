I personally use the DKP system for Ancient Guardian raids I host...They are guild and invited guests only.

I like the system as it takes some of the onus of the reward system and claims of favoritism and I personally HATE the random system...it also encourages people to show up to more raids even thought they might not have any other reason to be there.

Everyone keeps complianing that Newbies get screwed out of any chance at loot. I somewhat agree. But on the same note I have had a guest loot some (of what were to them) decent items. I personally think someone who has raided with us 10 times and has 70 points vs someone that has raided with us once and has the 5 points on credit should if he wants an item, get it.

While I know it's not a perfect system, I feel that it's amoung the fairest systems that I have heard and it rewards those that show up frequently for raids, and encourages others to show up more often.

I used to use a modified random situation.

When I lead the first Public Raids into Skyshrine, I set up a system which allowed those with more armor to finish suits faster...Basically a seniority system.

If you had no pieces, you rolled on a given number.

If you had 1, 2, 3, ect to LP you rolled on a given number.

You had to attend so many raids before eligible for any LP rights.

Granted, it was a static raiding scenario, and I really didnt care about the loot aspect, but it worked out pretty well, as it was easily verified and tracked.

Loots cool, but Ive never cared enough about it to get too worked up and worry about the intricacies (tks spellar errer) of any given loot system.
