 		Date Posted: 4/20 2:52pm Subject: Proposed Elder House points change: bonus points if nothing needed
It bothers me that more and more people (as a percentage) seem to only raid targets they NEED. Thus, I can rarely get a full raid force together.

This is, plainly, a case of greed before need. One must work with the reality of life, so I have a proposed changed to Elder House points to try to make it work better:


The bonus points for end-of-raid are only earned for those who do NOT get a flag or epic loot.


Here are some implications of this simple rule:

1) If you already have the flag, you have the incentive to come anyway to earn points over your fellow raiders, to get loot that drops.

2) If the raid does not succeed, everyone ends up getting bonus points anyway.

3) If someone does not get rezzed in time to get their flag, at least they get bonus points instead.

If experienced people start coming to raids, it is more likely for the raid to succeed. If points is what it takes to get people to come to raids, perhaps they need points.

Please tell me what you think of this proposal.

As an immediate thread hijack, here is another proposal, since people ask me about combining Elder House points with Zeek DKP:

What if I were to take all the points listed on the Zeek DKP site and double them, to add them to existing Elder House points? This would be a one-time conversion, and Zeek DKP would then continue on as it is. Elder House points should accumulate much faster than Zeek DKP, and this is intended to sort of even it out. Then again, who wants to see me get another 78 points instantly?

I thought of this because it might be a deterrent to public raiders from going on Elder House raids that they have no points with Elder House.

One problem with this conversion is Zeek DKP seem to be allocated somewhat randomly. I checked the site to see how many points I have, and see people got 15 points for "almost" killing Agnarr. I am unsure as to whether people got 15 points for actually killing him on 4/14/04. Do we want to introduce this kind of inconsistency into Elder House points?

It was never intended to be a one-leader organization, but a pool of raid leaders leading a pool of raiders without regard to guild affiliation. Perhaps we can collect a bunch of raid leaders together and start a new system, converting points systems to it on a specific date in specific ways.

Based on the responses so far, I intend to leave the Zeek DKP conversion alone. There is NO WAY I will be able to track Zeek raiders to see if their points have become vested. My current system is cumbersome enough, thank you.

Here are some examples if I run a 1 1/2 hour MB raid with no bonus awarded for those who get flagged, assuming MB dies, and with the announcement counting as a signup:

Player A hears the announcement, announces class and level, is placed in a group, but does not appear. -4 points as a no-show.

Player B hears the announcement, announces class and level, is placed in a group, but does not appear when I look at the /raid listing. He does show up at a later point, and needs the MB flag. Total points: 2 1/2, 1 point for "signing up" and 1 1/2 points for attendance.

Player C hears the announcement, announces class and level, is placed in a group, and is present when I look at the /raid listing. He needs the MB flag, and gets it when we kill MB. Total points: 5 1/2, 1 for "signing up," 1 for being on-time, 1 1/2 for attendance, and 2 for staying until the end.

Player D hears the announcement, announces class and level, is placed in a group, is present when I look at the /raid listing, and does NOT need the flag. Total points: 10 1/2, same as Player C but with the 5 point end of raid bonus. This is the maximum points possible on this MB raid.

Player E arrives at the raid after the announced start time, and is then placed in a group, but does not need the flag. Total points: 6 1/2, 5 for end of raid bonus and 1 1/2 for attendance.

Player F hears of the raid while we are killing spiders, and successfully comes to the raid without training. He needs the MB flag, which is why he risked coming. He gets 0 points, since he will not be here for a half hour, and besides it is a considerable nuisance at a bad time for me to put him in a group.


Suppose MB does NOT die instead. The players would have different points:

Player A would still have -4 points.

Player B would get 7 1/2 points, since he did not get his flag.

Player C would get 10 1/2 points, since he did not get his flag.

Player D would still get 10 1/2 points. The only difference between a win and a loss for someone who does not need the flag is the opportunity to SPEND points.

Player E would still get 6 1/2 points.

Player F would still get 0 points. I shall have to put in a provision for people present less than a half hour, to prevent point farming.


People after the flag are not supposed to care about loot anyway, though we all know this really is not the case for many people. Those that really do care about the loot are those that will not attend the raid after they find out about it.
