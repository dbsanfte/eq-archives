We have what others consider to be a large guild (although I think there are larger on Veeshan now). In our early days we had large numbers of people at raids. On occasion in Kunark and Velious we also had a large turnout for various mobs. What some people fail to understand is that CT was and still is more of a social guild and we did not turn away guild members from raids, regardless of the numbers.

People seem to construe that because we used large numbers that we were to gimpy to kill the mobs with fewer numbers. That's a really lame opinion.

Over the past month or so we have done quite well with low numbers on mobs, some examples:

VP with 30ish (cleared in under 2 hours)
Klandicar with 28 (with three cragwurms being trained on us during the fight)
Moonman with 32 (10 who went LD leaving 22 to fight while the 10 came back on in mid fight, half the clerics went LD)
NToV 'the approved way' with 34-38 people
Grieg with around 28
THO with 30ish

We also had attempts on Worm pit with 30ish, and during one of the waves we got our asses whipped, but we put up a good fight during this wave. I'm sure we will come up with another group make-up to try this again.

Learning the mob with low numbers can be useful, but you can also go with larger numbers and try to determine what numbers and classes can be used to win the encounter. You can learn from an encounter in either instance.

Just my two cents.
