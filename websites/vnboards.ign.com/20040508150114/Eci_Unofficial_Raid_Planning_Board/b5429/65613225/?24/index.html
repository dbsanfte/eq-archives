Perhaps it is time to abandon CoF the list?

I speak from the perspective of someone who will never get a CoF from Nagafen, since I am above level 52.

The lists began as a way to award EPIC drops to those that consistently supported the raids. A first-time raider would not likely walk away with a highly sought-after epic piece.

I understand the list was instituted as a way to encourage clerics to come to the raids, since clerics (and most other spellcasters) have little to nothing to use that Nagafen drops. The list is now having the effect of discouraging melee people from coming, since they have no chance of winning the CoF.

Here is a middle ground. Abandon the list, and allow those you see fit to roll on the CoF when it drops. If you wish to include clerics to encourage them to come, fine. If you wish to allow level 53+ people who help with the giants and/or buffing to roll (or not), fine. If everyone gets to roll on it regardless of need, fine. Just announce the loot rules beforehand.

Once upon a time I led Vox raids. I prefered Vox for several reasons, one of which was she drops something nice for everyone. I maintained a list for the Frosted Book until no more deserving paladins were on it. Same with the Scales for bards, though that took less time. At no time did I start a list for anything else, nor did I ever intend to. I just kept lists to be sure no one would ever be awarded the same item a 2nd time from my raids.

It is also worth noting that after 5 months of leading Vox raids, attendance gradually came up. We went from struggling to get enough people at the beginning to actually turning people away to go with no more than 6 groups. Everything by that time was being ROLLED on, NBG for every item (Karvuul's Mystic Pouch only to monks; monks could trade in a Tinker's bag for open roll to get one).

Experience shows attendance goes up the more is randomed. Then again, those attending only if loot is randomed are by definition greedy. Do you want to fill the raid with greedy people? What if the dragon cannot die without them?
