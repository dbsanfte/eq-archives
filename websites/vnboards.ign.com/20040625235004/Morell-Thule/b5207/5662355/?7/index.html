Ok, here's mine. I had been playing for a couple of months, but was a pretty casual player and split my time between a few characters, so the highest level character I had was a Ranger of about 7th level, I think. I was also mostly a solo player, just because I hadn't had many opportunities to really hang out with and get to know anyone else. I had read all sorts of things about GM events, and the ones that had gone well had sounded great. Being that they all seemed to involve mostly high level players, though, I figured it would be quite a while before I got a chance to do anything like that.

Then, one evening, I was coming out of Blackburrow, when I saw a message about someone chanting strange words to the Father of Hate, and calling the dead to rise. I ran out into the Qeynos Hills, and there was a strange ringed planet in the sky, it was snowing I believe, and the hills were swarming with undead. People were in chaos, running around, shouting, trying to drive them off. There were powerful figures of light and dark confronting each other...it was a madhouse. When the sun rose, the undead mostly vanished, but there was a huge moon hanging in the sky, through the daytime, and we all were pretty sure the dead would come again at nightfall.

No one seemed to be doing much to organize things, and as darkness began to fall, it looked like another night of chaos and slaughter. I didn't know if I would be able to do anything, but I figured someone had to give it a shot, so I started shouting for anyone who wanted to organize a defense of our home to meet me at the guard tower near the W. Karana zone. At first not much seemed to be happening, but slowly people started trickling in. As more people gathered, I heard someone say, over shout "I'm with Hound's group, at the guard tower." I was Hound, btw. The thrill of this being called "my group" was incredible. I started having people tell me their classes, so we could organize people into well-balanced groups. I sent out a call for Rangers to come, so that one could be assigned to each group, to track the undead. I'll never forget when one young Ranger ran up to me, snapped a salute, and said "Reporting for duty, Sir."

One of the best moments, though, came when I announced that I planned to stay near the tower and coordinate the efforts of the groups, through /tells with the different group leaders, and I said that I would need at least one warrior to be my strong right arm--to stand with me and defend me while I tried to coordinate things. A huge barbarian warrior stepped forward--I greatly regret that I cannot now recall his name--and said "I shall guard you with my very life."

Was I a great field commander? I don't know. We only had about three groups, and we pretty much just ran around killing the undead, and helping solos who got in over their heads. We kept things coordinated so a group could get where it needed to be as quickly as possible. I don't know that it was much of a war, but it was a great roleplaying experience. I took an initiative, and people just threw in and ran with me. It was wonderful.

~~~~Random (aka the Hound of Karana)
