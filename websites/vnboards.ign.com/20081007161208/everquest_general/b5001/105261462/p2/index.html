Drizzit appoached Maetron Baenre in a fast and agressive advance. Leave me alone forever! he said, his fist was clenched so hard that drops of blood hit the dark and dusty cavern floor. No! You will suffer same fate as Catti-brie if you dont join us! Baenre sounded as wicked as ever ,her hand made a slit throat gesture at Gwuenwyvar who snarled back but there was fear in it and Drizzit detected it. He knew now it was time to choose between these wicked as spider drow, his own brethen or the surface dwellers like Wulfgar, the stern barbarian who above all else had the bravest of hearts.

No! The caverns echoed and birdlike creatures rose though the tunnels making screeching noises. Drizzit narrowed his eyes and looked straight into Baenres and thus inspired courage in Gwuenwyvar who walked in front of Drizzit to protect him.

You cannot deny our nature ,we will rule wherever we want or see fit. For how long has the Harpers poisoned your heart? Your love for the weak will be your greatest mistake, Do´Urden have but one last chance to return to its former pride and strengths. You dont have a choice , havent I made my self clear! Baenre screamed in anger.

Suddenly out of the shadow a cloaked figure appeared rushing towards Drizzit. A glimpse of steel blinked,Twack! Drizzit had drown his left scimitar so fast and following the slip and slide that avoided the deadly drow throwing knife, he hurled him self through the air at his opponent he so well knew. For it was Artemis Entreri his arch enemy. Entreri had gained time to hurl one more drow throwing knife, ching! It repelled Drizzit mithril chain armor, had it been just one inch closer to his shoulder joint he would eventually been hit by the poisoned knife. Drizzit looked quickly for Gwuenwyvar ,to his surprise see him rolled on his back, letting out a groan. One poisoned knife wore deep in his chest.

Entreri had pleasure shown in his evil eyes filled with excitement for all he knew was by killing Drizzit he would rise into the higher society of the drow and without doubt killing him would be his greatest accomplishment and satisfaction of all time.

The clash of steel and and hissing from them both echoed in the tunnel in front of Baenres mansion.

Guards! Baenre cried. Gwuenwyvar tried to rise but stumpled about.

A swift stroke avoided by Drizzit would have landed deep in his chest. Quickly the ranger drow knocked the hilt of his right scimitar in Artemis forehead so hard that he fell three yards behind and landed in the dirt uncounscious.

The strife would never end, he would forever be wanted dead by the drows.

Footsteps becoming louder and louder, by the sound of it 20 or so guards . He pointed his left scimitar at Baenre but had nothing to say.

Gwuenwyvar was dead. He had nothing to do but escape now.

I was having ale with Ligolás and Gindalf, then suddenly a great figure stood in the doorway. It was an orc from Orgrimmar he startet dancing and got a groove going in the whole tavern called The weary dwarf. We danced in our own styles, jive,jittabog,hip-hap and Gindalf was stage diving from the bar.

We started singing all together in common. Oh, this was a great party indeed. Then suddenly we noticed an undead who had started a stunlock on Gindalf. Gimlei the short dwarf who was a warrior started to charge the undead from his battlestance. The rogued vanished quickly and didnt come back. The orc had something in his travellers backpack. Tassalhofe the Kendar had already looked. It was gold and spakling diamonds. The orc explained he had got them from grinding but Gindalf didnt believe him and pertitioned a gm. Much to his surprise this orc had surely got all this treasure from grinding in 3 years. The fun loving orc started to toss around diamonds and gold every where in the tavern. And everyone was happy!

The next morning the fun loving orc was gone. We looked for him and asked around, he was last seen at the fp. Many had great headaches from drinking and sore feet from dancing so I healed them and buffed them before I continued my journey. Ligolás was crying because he missed him so soon. Gindalf had told me I had to get level 70 soon and he was having some issues and concerns he was investigating in. He told me he would need my help. I saddled up on my cat mount and rode towards the forest edge. Just as I entered the woods, right behind from a tree a figure stepped out. It was that undead rogue from last night.

He laughed a deep dirty laughter. He wanted my gold and diamonds I got from the tavern. He was quite clearly a lvl 70. I knew he would kill me. He was already stealthed so I popped up my shield and went running.Suddenly I went chack! He was right behind me hitting me, I screamed but soon he was just back hitting me again.I stopped to heal but he kicked me, so I could not concentrate on it.

It would have been just one more hit from him comming right at my throat and I would be real done for. Right behind the undead was the biggest red gorilla pounding him in the back of his head. Some marking arrow was pointing down at the rogues head. Twack! Twack! Incomming arrows from Ligolás hit the rogue in every limb of his foul flesh. Kersplat! The rogue staggered about , the gorilla finished him off right on top on his head. Gonk!

The elf from Loorieèn explained he had a far sight and had watched for me the whole time. We departed and promised we would take care. He gave me some lembas bisquets and ale. I gave him a broche I had found on the streets in Telsamar.

As I rode deeper into the wood I wondered if I ever would meet the orc again and if Gindalf was getting himself in trouble.

Days had passed since I left the woods, the weather had ben fine. Now the clouds where getting dark it was only noon. I was at a farmstead, it began to rain. I took shelter in a shed and waited for a time for it to clear.

Now suddenly I heard someone talking. It was gutterspeak, a bloodelf and a undead talking. From what I could understand the bloodelf was a paladin he talked about the undead owing him a big favor. Just then I realised the undead was the rogue from the tavern who almost got me killed in the wood. He had been resurected by the bloodelf. They started to laugh their hysterical laughter, talking about killing a lonely druid in Ashenvale.

I sat dead still, they moved on. And soon they where gone.

I mounted and rode toward the next village I had on my map. After awhile I noticed pillars of black smoke in the distance. I entered a village that had been attacked and set a fire. All the quest givers where gone and I heard some sound of a battle going on in a tavern. I ran in and saw some alliance, a night elf warrior and a dwarf priest. The dwarf was healing the warrior who was in a tough battle against a bloodelf paladin. The warrior was being hit real hard as he was around my level too. I began to heal him aswell. Chack! Char-Char! The undead rogue was attacking the dwarf!

I healed them both as well as i could and put up some shadow word:pain on the hordes, but knew it would end in misery. I had little mana now, the dwarf moaned in pain and the night elf began shouting for heals! I had to choose who to save. I cast my last heal on the warrior and looked after the dwarf. He was gone instead there was a big white angel casting heals. As I feared the rogue went for me. I popped my shield up but he tore it apart. He kept on hitting me and the angel was running out of time.

A figure now stood in the doorway. Clack,clack,clack! Totems all over the place! I could see the hordes bound to the earth.I ran for it! Swoosh! The rogue was frostschocked and walked around not knowing who he should hit. I looked past my shoulder and saw the paladin surrounded by his divine shield.

It was a draenei shaman who had come by. He smacked around the bloodelf and undead so bad because they where so low already. Whatever tricks they had left was not enough to beat that good shaman.

Later that evening we celebrated around a campfire, and had ale and lembas. They told great stories of their journies and the shaman had resurected the dwarf. We began to sing an old song we all knew from Stormwind.
The night sky was clear and the stars were bright. We ran out of ale and had enough of lembas. I fell asleep and slept hard that night, knowing I was in good company.

I woke up and stared in to the embers of the camp fire. The dwarven priest and I went to a building to pray for salvation. We heard the sound of the shaman calling the natures spirits. The warrior had his ritual of combat practice and polishing his armor.

As we exited the building the shaman sat on his elekk, waving goodbyes. We told him he had the bravest of hearts and wished him safe journeys. The remaining two had been good friends for a decade, they invited me to come along. They were going to fight for justice in Arathi Basin. Almost as I agreed I remembered Gindalf telling me to hit level 70. Somehow I just knew something was happening of greater importance.

I gave them some of the diamonds and some gold and wished them a happy life. The dwarf smiled and pulled a handfull of blue vials from his robe and gave them to me. He told me they could help me save others when things got hot. The nightelf who was the leader of a guild called Temperance offered me an invitation and said I was more than welcome to visit him in Stormwind where he lived. I told him I would think about it but I would certainly visit him and his guild the next time I came by Stormwind.

I looked behind my shoulder and they waved to me their goodbyes.

Later that day I stopped at an inn. I wanted something else than lembas and was thisty for a pint of ale. I entered and it was rather crowdy. I found an empty seat at a table where a hooded figure sat. He was smoking pipeweed and the embers glowed in his mysterious eyes. As I sat down he said something I didnt understand, some old noble language by the sound of it comming from the far north.

I could not detect if he where hostile. Quickly I ordered two pints of the finest ale. I presented it to him. He drank it all in just a few seconds. I then tried in all languages of Azeroth to get a conversation going. He nodded a couple of times and laughed abit. He walked away from our table and threw a coin right in front of me.

I stared at the coin it was an unknown currency. He must have been from the far north.

That night I could not get any sleep. I had two many thoughts about the mysterious man.

Now these things got me all nervous and I wished me back to the day in The weary dwarf tavern. I wished the fun loving orc was there and we had a great party all night again.

Through hours we had some serious conversations with, golden dragons, elves from Looreèn and secret orders. We saw their compassion in the Pallenthir and called for their aid. As we ran outside we saw the open field, an army marching ,singing and shouting in a horrifying melody. They where as far as our keen elven eyes could see. They had burning torches and banners wore high in the air. Great siege machines came rolling with great noise. They where screaming DEATH!

A white figure came riding on a light grey horse in a great speed and haste. It was Gindalf on his favourite mount. He carried with him a banner of light that illuminated the snowy field with glory and bravery.

With his other hand he pointed his staff towards the heavens and tore open a rift between all worlds. He was an Istareee, one of five beings created by the old gods who had the ability to do great magic things of unbelievable power!

Golden and silver dragons came circeling from the heavens carrying noble paladins with long lances. Elves from Looreén and dwarves who had ale and secret orders came charging forward on an incredible rainbow.

We too rushed in battle not carying if we would die or not.

Dragons spew fire, lances pierced bodies and evil spells hurled back. The battle lasted til the sun rose high on the sky. Thousands of dead bodies laid scattered everywhere..

We had not seen Gindalf for a long time. Sad that he might have fallen and didnt know what to do we gathered the dead bodies and honored them in our minds and hearts.

A few hours later me and Dreeezt and Thoar and a couple of new friends from other worlds sat and played a tournament of Wow trading card game. All of us who knew gindalf, didnt felt joy. And there had been no sight of him nor Arthas dead on the field. I looked at my wand from Ligolás that killed many last night and started to wonder what the trinket of Gimleis was good for.

Suddenly Tassalhofe came in and laughted aloud. Following him came Gindalf and he told us of his victory against Arthas. He also announced that since he almost died he henceforth should be know as Gondalf!

Dreeezt rewarded me as he promised, 3 booster packs for wow trading card game. The dragons where kind to carry us all the way home to the old town. We entered The weary dwarf tavern and ordered free ale for everyone! We bid farewell to the dragons who had to go home to their own wolrd again.

We laughed and was in great relief. Then suddenly a figure stood in the doorway, he had big heavy bags and he laughed aloud. It was the fun loving orc and Ligolás started to cry out in happiness. Thoar suddenly kneeled in front of me and proposed!
