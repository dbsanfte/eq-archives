Hello EQ players.
I have been reading this board for a while and I see many people asking where are the Roleplaying guilds? And where are the people who enjoy the roleplaying spirit.
I have seen this as a oxy-moron to the servers you play on.
1:) How can anyone play on a blue server and feel as though they are roleplaying? You cannot do this period! If you are a light race and you are to fear the dark races. What do you do? Um...I wish to dual you.. Would this be ok with you?.. C'mon give me a freaking break!

2:) When you slay your creature (NPC) you loot them on any server. This should be applied to any person you slay. this can only be done on VZ/RZ. I suggest that if you wish to ROLEPLAY then get on a server that allows you this opportunity.

3:) I played on Tunare since the beta version. Then the Racewar server came up. I tried it out, leaving my very high level woodelf druid, to take a backseat for a bit. I have never gone back. There are the most roleplayers here where I am now. I have never had so much fun. Until you have spent time on a true to life server, you cannot experience the true meaning of roleplay.

4:) I found standing in line, waiting for a spawn on a blue server, was a joke. And to try to be happy to Darkelves, while I hoped for my turn was a joke. To just jump in and slaughter them, loot there stuff. And take the spot was true roleplaying at its finest!

5:) I never play naked. I have items that are always lootable. And I play fair. Most players I have found also offer this. I have been killed many times and have had a blast. All my items I have either taken from others or purchased. And I have never thought twice about stepping into battle -vs- my foes.


Just some insight regarding what I believe to be Roleplaying.

I started to think about it. One of the biggest arguments in EQ is that it isn't dynamic. Nothing changes and there are no challenges because you know exactly how a situation works if you've done it once or twice.

And then I realized WE are the dynamic.

I watched the Necro Death Squad slaughtering guards in Felwithe the other day. It bothered me, but, hey, they were just doing their thing, right? But what if they had been doing it in Erudin? My hometown. I would have felt obligated to do...something. Lay hands on a weakened guard. Toss around a few heals. But on a blue server, there would be no real penalty for that. Maybe they petition, and I say, hey, I am just protecting my people and it just becomes an ugly stew of upset people trying to get even with one another. Rather, them stalking me and returning the heal-your-opponent favor. Maybe something uglier. But if I'm red and so are they, the penalty for me is a swift and painful Necro Death Squad reaming. They leave me for dead and go on with their hunt. Or maybe my heals make the difference and the guards romp and stomp on them. Or maybe a few other locals join in and the Necros go down hard.

It stops being static. Within the context of the game, we make each other have to consider what we do, because there is the unknown of other players. Somehow that just seems right to me. I understand that maybe some Ogre gets his jollies blowing my weak ass out of the trees, but what's to stop other players from coming to my aid and giving him the same treatment? Friends and allies looking out for one another on both sides.

I went and had a chat with the Priest a bit ago. He gave me a copy of the Book. I turned it in.

Maybe, if all my babbling made sense to someone else, other people will start making Tarew Marr a place where the players are responsible for defending the right and smashing the light-wielding oppressors. I'm looking forward to it.

Definition of Roleplaying -Roleplaying is the activity of using one's imagination and a set of agreed upon guidelines to interact with other roleplayers (rper's) to act out the life and personality of a created character or persona.

*************

I have to make some sort of arguement here. First of all, I have never been on a PvP Server, so I will admit that my commentary is from an incomplete perspective. However, I am certainly one who believes in "Quality over Quantity". I feel that entering a PvP Server would be detrimental to MY enjoyment of the game; which is not primarily to fight... or level... or get items.

I started playing EQ to "see" the gaming that I enjoyed so much through table topping. It has been a long and droll battle, roleplaying constantly on a blue server (often roleplayign alone in a group of manniquins who had no clue what I was doing).. but progress has been made and there are people who have recognized what I am trying to do through example. And now.. I have people to roleplay with!

I play a DE.. a rather nasty one who does not hold back on her insults, prejudices, or hate. I refuse to group with "Pales" because she would NEVER help a Pale. I threaten. I insult. I use descriptive emotes to indimidate. And through all of that .. I find REAL Roleplayers. I have never backed down to a duel challenge and I don't even con the other players before I challenge them... I am THAT in character (IC).

I honestly believe that people who know how to roleplay and what it really is, do not need to have the safety bars On or Off.. It is inconsequential as to whether the "system" allows them to attack each other or not. Roleplayers react to roleplaying.. not "system allowances". I don't think I would be happy trying to roleplay on a PvP. I think all the PKers would ruin my high.... Drag me out of character. In order for there to be "fair" roleplaying, everyone needs to be their own GM and Player at the same time. I don't trust the Video Gamers to respect the Roleplayers on a PvP.

So, you may think I hide behind a safety net by roleplaying on a blue server, but I know I am not hiding at all... just trying to stay IC .. because that is the ONLY reason I even play EQ.

My point is a little unclear... hmm.. Maybe I can sum it up:

No matter what server you are on, if you are a roleplayer, just keep doing it.. eventually people will react and recognize it.

Hell.. I even roleplay with the NPC's when buying, selling or just plain banking. People must think I am crazy, roleplaying by myself at points... but hey.. its my story, and I am living it.
