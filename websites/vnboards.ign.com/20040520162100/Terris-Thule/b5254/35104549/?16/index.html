Well here I go:

I played EQ at launch and quit just before Kunark launched (so I played for 14 months or so, 3+ years ago). I quit because I felt it was damaging my life. I didn't touch mmorpgs until AO (ugh) and then DAOC, which I've played since launch (ie. for 12 months). Just recently I installed EQ for the first time in years.

Let me tell you, as a DAoC player who is basically rediscovering EQ, EQ is a breathe of fresh air. It's SO complex and deep.

EQs world is obviously about 6 times larger (That is probably not an exaggeration either - I don't believe Albion is even as large as Kunark). But more than physical size, the real issue is that EQ's world is far more complex than DAoCs. Everything from quests to lore to dungeons to loot, etc. etc. I've seen everything there is to see in DAoC 3 times over. I didn't feel that way after my 14 months of EQ (that's before ANY expansions, mind you. Now it's simply enormous.)

EQ has a much more interesting crafting system. To whoever said DAoC's was superior - I'm curious why you think so. DAoCs crafting system is strictly linear. There is nothing fun to craft. No unique or secret recipes. No armor or items special in any way. No experimenting. Nothing. Soon Spellcrafting will be added and that will improve things, but it still has nowhere near the depth of the EQ crafting system.

RvR in DAoC really is fun. It's an aspect I miss in EQ. However it is also fairly broken. It's not as terrible as many Vault board posters would like you to believe, but it does have many imbalances, bugs, and design flaws. Don't read that wrong though, RvR is a great thing and it's probably DAoC's biggest selling point.

If EQ fails to DAoC in any one area it's that it is SLOW paced. By comparison DAoC flies. Levels fly, combat flies... everything but run speed. DAoC is paced much better, imho. And that's no small thing - pacing can make or break a game.

However besides that, EQ seems superior in every way.

Overall DAoC is a great game. Hell, I played it for a year and I keep my account active. When Shrouded Isles launches this December I'll be there. However EQ is simply better. It is more immersive, more creative, more interesting and far, far deeper. On top of it all, the EQ community is WAY freindlier than the DAoC community. And really, what more is there to an online game?
