Ah, I see. No, the Damage Bonus is something that melees see on a weapon when they click on it after level 28. For one handed weapons, it goes up by one every three levels and affects the modal number. For two handed weapons, there is no formula that I am aware of, you have to use a lookup table. You can find a current version at http://lucy.allakhazam.com/dmgbonus.html . Damage bonus applies to the primary hand only, btw.

What you are describing is a totally different thing. All characters start out with the ability to hit for up to base damage times 2, so a level 1 character with your 10/100 weapon should be hitting for up to 20. After level 20, the main damage cap is removed and all characters, melee or caster, can hit for more than base x 2 with the maximum hit number determined by STR/ATK.

In other words, it is perfectly normal for a level 20+ Wizard to be hitting for 20+ with a 10/100 weapon, although the skill cap is too low for it to happen frequently.

Edit: Forgot to mention that the pre-20 damage cap is twofold: you can't hit for more than base damage times 2 AND you can't hit for more than 20. So a 30/45 weapon is effectively a 10/45 weapon between 1 and 19. Which is why, when I am leveling new characters, I give them a 10/20 weapon if they can wield it
