"Higher product costs can be misinterpreted as inflation...."

Higher product costs IS inflation. There is no magic "fair" price of a product. The price of a product is the price at which a seller is willing to part with it and a buyer is willing to pay. Since gasoline is price inelastic, people will pay what they must until they cannot afford it anymore. Unless they have a viable substitute.

No new refineries have been built in the US in the last 25 years or so. And in California, home of 20% of the country's gas users, the number of refineries has actually decreased, from 40 to 12, in the last 25 years. Couple that with double or so people and a corresponding increase in driving, it seems as if the price of crude may be only a small part of the problem.

In California it is predicted that due to the demand vs. supply problems AT THE REFINERY level, gas prices will easily reach $3 before summer is over.

So the quickest way to lower prices for us and our children may be to lobby the anti-refinery legislators to loosen the belt a little and let someone build. The answer is not to legislate price controls, but to de-legislate asphixiating regulations on refinery construction.

KFC mitigates the rising cost of poultry by working its ass off to cook more birds to sell and open more stores to sell them. Apply that model to the petroleum industry.
