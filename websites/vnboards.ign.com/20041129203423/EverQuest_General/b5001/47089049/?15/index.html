March 20, 2003 - First of all, welcome to EQVault version 4.0. It's a new look and feel for the site for those who have been frequenting the site over its long history.

Exciting things are in store, and the new tools and features currently being worked on are something to behold.

Right now, however, we're continuing to work the bugs out of the site. A brief rundown of where we're at:
  • Some links are randomly "exploding." We hope to have this fixed today.


  • Content transferred to the new system has gone missing. We're working to either restore it or re-migrate it over.


  • The Guidebook is offline as it is still under major reconstruction. The end result, as we've already hinted at, will be more than worth the wait however.


  • New sections for other titles in the EQ franchise are currently being finalized.


  • Right hand navigation will be enhanced with new features including a better way to navigate the boards.

    We'll have some more announcements in the coming days. Until then, we appreciate your patience and as always thank you for visiting the EQVault.
    In the meantime, we'd like to hear any comments, suggestions and ideas you have for how to improve the site, what you'd like to see and other things the community might find enjoyable.

    Thanks!

    I have been visiting EQVault for about four and a half years now. It has been an invaluable source of EQ-related information that I would visit on a daily basis to see what was going on with the game and the EverQuest community -- that is, until this year.

    First we go for weeks without a single update (even though there were MANY newsworthy events occuring during that time, such as the announcement of a new expansion (LoY) and race (Frogloks), along with a couple of important patches).

    Now we get a "new" format with all the important information squeezed into a 278-pixel-wide column on the left side of the screen with the majority of the rest of the site being either ads or BLANK. What's with the fixed-width main column (that leaves a gaping hole on the right side of the screen)? That's SO 1992! Even with the WindowsXP minumum desktop resolution of 800x600, the page isn't full width, and I doubt many EQ players use an 800x600 desktop.

    Oh, and DON'T EVEN get me started on the ad we have to get past BEFORE WE EVEN GET TO the bleedin' site! I WILL NOT BE FORCE FED ADS! I click on links that interest me, but recently, there have been so precious few of those that they've been wasted bandwidth on me. Again, I WILL NOT BE FORCE FED ADS! I will delete the bookmark to a site before I sit through being force fed ads, and so for the first time in four and a half years, I am VERY SERIOUSLY considering deleting my EQVault bookmark. It has already been moved off my main list of bookmarks (the ones I check daily), now relegated to my tertiary list (those I *may* check once a week). Is forcefeeding us ads REALLY worth the loss of ad impressions and/or page hits? I think you are slitting your own throats by force feeding us ads. I cannot emphasize that enough: I WILL NOT BE FORCE FED ADS!

    In short, I cannot stand the new format. With the forced ads, the amateur-looking page layout, the lousy presentation of the main subject content and all of the non-EQ-oriented IGN junk in which most of us have no interest, this site has become a dim shadow of its former self. It's too much IGN and not enough EQ. I certainly hope this isn't an IGN-imposed format to force this site to tow the party line, because it's not just a step, but a running and flying leap backwards.

    Please return it to something resembling the old format.
