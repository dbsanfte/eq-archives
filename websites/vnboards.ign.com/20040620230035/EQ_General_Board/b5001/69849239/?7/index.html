The druid nukes are a very poor return for mana used. Your friend is snare once you snare a mob you can take your time in killing it. Cast a DoT (Damage Over Time) spell or two. as a druid you get two main lines of Dot spells, a fire based one where you need to have a fire beetle eye in your inventory and a magic based one. These dot's wear away at the mobs health and if you have snared and rooted (root 'n' rot method) the mob you get a chance to med while the mob dies.
The next method is to 'kite' a mob, this is more mana intensive but can return greater rewards, best for later levels, but snare the mob, dot the mob and run away, nuke if you feel the need.
An alternate method is to fear kite, for a druid this only works for animals, you snare a mob, cast fear on it and then you can follow it about hitting it or cast a dot and med up till fear wears off and you need to recast fear.
A variation of the kite method is to quad kite(Definately for later levels). This involves finding and snare 4 mobs, running about till they are all in a tight group and then casting AE (Area Effect) spells at them, these spells will hit all four creatures. This is mana intensive but four dead mobs instead of one is always better exp.

At level 14 you get sow (Spirit of the Wolf) this is a run buff and will enable you to outrun mobs even if snare fails to land. Hope this helps.

One thing to practice is mana management.

My druid is now 51 and I tend to hover around 20-40 percent mana when I'm killing things.

My friend's druid is now 35 and she tends to empty her mana pool rather quickly even at 219 wisdom (roughly).

We both tend to root rot although I used to dot melee quite a lot when I was lower level.

Right clicks are wonderful once you are high enough to use them.

Currently I root, right click snare, dot, right click dot, dot, dot sit and med.

Look for items with focus effects also. I think it's the serpentine bracer that makes your dots do more damage. Other items can help etend the duration or range of a spell or even lower the mana cost.

Shop around and have fun!

Druids are one of the "awkward" classes till they get meditate. You're not a fighter/tank and you can't get your mana back very fast without meditate.

Stick it out till level 9 and get meditate at least, then you get a glimmer of what you can do. You'll still be meding alot after the kills if you try to kill with magic. (The alternative as listed is to get a little bit of cash and go buy some decent AC and a weapon at the bazaar and just melee with thorns till it becomes inefficient to do so).

Once you can meditate, you won't spend so long in downtime meding.

I used to play a druid a long time ago and found it to be extreamly powerful. I could solo easily but it was never as good exp as getting a tank partner or a group..but still I could do something alone if I chose.

My favorite form of solo was to load up on AC gear, as much as i could get, then double DoT on the pull and melee the mob. Usually cost me three to four spells a pull, 2 dots and 1 to 2 heals afterwards. I took White and Blue con mobs this way up to level 47 (depending on the mob..you need to research and find mobs that are either low damage or low hitpoints to take this type of beating efficiently)

My two cents

Well, you have to realize that as your level goes up, so does your mana pool. Root rot is not a fast way to level but it tends to be safe since the mobs don't whack on you.

It takes patience. I have yet to quad kite but I've multiple root rotted though.

At 51 (not recalling all spell names as I'm half asleep) I root, mana free snare, drifting death, mana free drones of doom, immolate, creeping crud, sit and med. Root tends to break at about 50% health, lower roots break early I've noticed. Dots wear off about 35% and require drift death and drones to finish them off. This is against snow griffons and tundra mammoths in Eastern Wastes. Nets me about 1/5 a blue per kill at about 20% mana use.

I recently started another druid to play with friends on E Marr.

At 26 I solo Broon in East Karana (and will probably start on griffs now that I'm 28) with root, snare, dot dot dot. I usually require two more casts of each dot before he drops. Lots of running and medding. I don't recall the exp amount but it was roughly half a blue and I used about 50% mana.

Both of these were without any mind candy. Overall thanks to LDoN I can make 1 to 2/12 yellow with my 28 druid per adventure, it's just that the cash stinks.

It's slow as oppossed to quading but it does get easier as you get higher. It just takes patience to let the dots do their work.

Here's another possibility for you, but it really depends on getting some good AC gear.

Pull with snare, swarm dot and fire dot. then melee the mob till your down to around 40% life or snare is about to expire. Then pull away, re-snare, pull further away and heal up, and either melee it out if it's low enough or re-apply dot's and melee.

As long as snare is on and you don't have a caster mob, it's pretty much as safe as root/nuke. But this way takes a lot better gear than my druid usually ran with. I sacrificed Wisdom and other stats to get the best possible AC and HP gear I could. And this worked well for me at least at 45.

I stopped doing it though because i found snare/dot's to be much more mana efficient in eastern wastes. I double dot and snare an ulthork then run far ahead of him and sit down and meditate while he approaches. I re-apply the dots when they run out and when he reaches me I run back the way i came and lead him back. Meditating and kiting this way usually made up half the mana i spent in dot's.

Also, like a previous poster listed...when you meet the level requirements look into geting the armor pieces that allow you free casts of some of your spells..especially the Dot's Works wonders and well worth the plat if you are a soloist.
