Hello, there has recently been several threads made asking question(s) about Everquest, both from people coming from other MMORPG's(massively multiplayer online role playing games) and from people deciding to get into the wave of MMORPG's. I have deicided to make this thread to help new players get their questions answered without having to look all over and ask. I have been playing Everquest since shortly after the Kunark expansion was realeased.

Here goes...

First I'll discuss the classes available in Everquest and a brief discription of the class in my own words.

Bard - Bards have the ability to play songs, either holding an instrument or not. Their songs are just like spells in more ways than not, and Bards get a new song, only 1, every level. Bards are very usful in groups as their songs, once played, are beneficial to everyone within range that's in the group, and can generally find groups fairly easily. Bard songs that are beneficial to the group are heals, mez, mana regeneration songs and several buff songs and many others..not to mention a few self-only songs such as haste, to increase your attack speed(and others of course).

Cleric - The healers of Everquest, greatly accepted in groups and is one of the most widely needed classes in Everquest, at later on in levels the Cleric gets the ability to cast a spell that regains some of the experience lost in a death, and play the role as the healer in groups,so dont expect much slash and hack action.

Druid - Druids are a versatile class, and can easily solo or find a group fairly easy, depending upon which zone they are in, at level 14 the druid is given one of the most wanted spells that every class needs at one time or another, Spirit of Wolf (commonly known as sow). Besides sow druids also get the ability to transport (or port) to various locations with spells, druid get their first self port only spells at level 19, and get later on in level, including group ports, which can come in handy when your group isnt doing so well and is in trouble. Druids get pets at level 55 and I can't forget to mention the Track skill at level 20, which comes in handy no matter what level you may be.

Enchanter - Commonly known as "chanters", they are highly needed in most groups as they have the ability to Mezmirize, or mez, which means if several monsters (mobs i will call them) are pulled, and are unable to be handled all at once, chanters cast mez on them and they stop fighting, until the mez spell wears off. Chanters also have the ability to cast "Breeze" (with an upgrade at higher levels, Clarity and KEI, i forgot what KEI stands for). Breeze and the higher upgrades preserve your mana to allow more spells to be casted, and this spell regenerates your mana faster. Chanters also get pets at lvl 4 and most of their new spell levels. A nice guide to learn about enchanters can be found at Enchanter guide

Magician - The summoners of Everquest, this class eventually at levels gets the ability to summon many items including beer and fireworks, and gets a companion (known as a pet) at level 4 and gets a new pet at almost every spell level.

Monk - The swift face to face fighters of Everquest. Monks are a very nice class to have in groups as well, as they have the ability to Feign Death at level 17 (they play dead) and if your Feign Death Skill is good enough, the monster will believe you're dead and leave you alone, very good for pulling single mobs (monsters). ne down side for monks, is to be able to keep their swift moving abilities, the monks weight allowance beeds to stay below 17 stones (pounds) and this limit raises at certain levels which i cannot remember at this time, monks can carry more, but it will lower their AC (armor class) which means that their chances of getting hit in any certain battle are more likely.

Necromancer - The evil, dark and gloomy characters of Everquest. But dont be so fast to judge Necromancers, they're very powerful indeed and can solo very well and have a very useful line of spells to help them, playing a necromancer,dont expect too much face to face combat beyond level 4, as then you may begin to fear kite, which involves casting fear in your mob then sending in your pet to kill them, along with you casting spell on it, a very useful tactic when playing a necromancer. Necro's get new pets at most new spell levels.

Paladin - The knight in shining armor type class of everquest. From level 1 the paladins have an ability of "Lay Hands" which is most of the time a full heal ability, usually used when you are about to die, but can be used on other people when you are feeling generous. Paladins are usful in groups, but you may have more trouble finding a group than say a cleric, Paladins also get spells at level 9 and have the ability to heal theirselves and others, but not as well as clerics, so in some cases you could say Paladins are more of a fighting cleric, but with some spell disadvantages compared to a cleric.

Ranger - The forest guarder of Everquest. Rangers are very nature like characters and have spells somewhat like the druid, but get them at a later level, Rangers get the ability to cast sow at level 30, and can handle face to face combat fairly well. Rangers have the ability to track players and monsters through a skill recieved at level 1, called nothing other than, Track.

Rogue - The thieves of Everquest. Although they have the ability to pick pocket, being a rogue dont expect to be able to go up to anyone and take what you please.. sorry. Rogues are very usful in groups and have the ability to backstab, when standing behind a monster, which does a very substantial amount of damage, and Rogues can take damage well.

Shadow Knight - Sort of an evil version of the paladin, but also revieving a different line of spells, the Shadow Knight (commonly reffered to as SK) are a fun class to play, and are good at face to face melee combat, SK also get pet spells at higher levels, and have the ability to Lifetap, which takes health from the monster and gives the health taken to whomever used the Lifetap. Generally,SK are accepted in groups, and should have no more peoblems finding a group than say a Paladin.

Shaman - The buffers of Everquest. Shaman get a large line of spells to help you gain stats (ie strength, hit points, stamina etc etc). Shamans also recieve sow at level 9, earlier than any other class that recieves sow, Shamans are greatly accepted in groups as they are very beneficial to have. Shaman also get pet spells.

Warrior - The get down and dirty heavy duty face to face fighters of Everquest. Warriors get no spells, except for maybe the spell effects on the armor they wear, but warriors make up for this in their ability to deal and take damage (warriors are also known as tanks) Warriors are a vital part of a group and can find groups, depending upon where you look, fairly easy.

Wizard - Spell Casters and porters of Everquest. Wizards get some very powerful damage spells, especially at higher levels. They have the ability to port as the druid does, but not only port, but translocate (commonly referred to as TL) which only ports the person the Wizard has currently targeted when the spell is casted.

Beastlord - I do not know much about this class as they are fairly new, I believe they get sow at level 30and eventually get pets as well, and I hear they can melee along with their pets fairly well.


PLEASE NOTE: The classes above were briefly discussed and all go into much further detail than what I have stated.

The availble races available in everquest are:

Barbarian - A hardier variety of the Human race that comes from the cold and rugged northlands. Because of the harsh environment, and their warlike culture, they are better suited to being Warriors than Humans, and are generally unwashed and rugged – possessing very few social graces.

Dark Elf -The evil brethren of the Woodland and High Elves. Their skin is blueish-black, their hair white, and they share the pointed ears and sharp features of their Elven brethren. Due to their life under the earth, and dedication to evil gods, they have the ability to see perfectly in the dark.

Dwarf - Dwarves begin inside the city of Kaladim. They are small and usually funny :-p

Erudite - The final variant of the Human race. Being the exact opposite of the Barbarians, the High Man prides himself on his intellectual abilities and social graces, to the almost total exclusion of the physical disciplines.

Gnome They aren't the strongest or cutest race, and they aren't the smartest, but they are the shortest! The first thing you'll find when starting out as a gnome is that Ak'anon, the gnome city, is absolutely stunning. The Ink Sea is inside the city. It's a huge lake with many buildings in it, and a massive waterfall near the city's guarded entrance. Ak'anon is worth exploring, and I mean that. Explore everywhere, there is a lot to Ak'anon. There's the zoo, the bank, the mines, the mechnamagica guild halls, the abbey of deep musing, the forge of defense, and Gemchopper Hall.

Half Elf - Half-Elves, being a blend between humans and Woodland Elves, share some of the strengths and weaknesses of both races. They most closely resemble humans, except for their slightly pointed ears and sharper facial features.

Halfling - Introduction Starting your character as a halfling in Rivervale can be a very fruitful experience, not only for the starting areas, about which lie great hunting spots, for anyone up to about 20th level. Halflings have a number of great attributes to start with and you will always be satisfied with what you come out of character creation with.

High Elf The High Elves are much more intellectual than their Woodland kin, and are the "royalty" of the Elven races (with the exception of their Dark brethren). They share the appearance of their Woodland kin, but are thinner, more beautiful, and more pale in skin

Human Normal humans. The standard race by which all others are judged.

Iskar (Lizardmen) - The Iksar are a Lizard type race that reside in Cabilis, which is the capitol of the Iksar Empire. The city lies deep within the Ruins of Kunark, a lost continent in the lands of Norrath. This race will start with 150 swimming skill, as they are natural born swimmers.

Ogre - Ogres are incredibly massive beings, with their average height being about 10', and weigh well over four hundred pounds, which makes them a truly immovable object. Due to their size, they are the strongest of all the races and are tied with the Trolls in pure Stamina. Conversely, they are the stupidest race, whose lives mostly consist of smashing things and eating them

Troll Trolls are foul looking, boisterous and somewhat dimwitted. In short, they make perfect warriors. They have the innate ability to regenerate damage (4 HPs roughly every 7 seconds), in addition to infravision (which can be a tremendous aid in the dark swamp, rest assured.) Trolls are second only to Ogres in starting stamina and strength. What makes the troll and ideal choice as opposed to the ogre is in the lengthy period needed to recover health between battles. Trolls also have the racial ability to "Slam", an unimproving skill which will do minor damage to and stun an unlucky victim. top Starting out with a Troll Warrior

Wood Elf - The Elves appear somewhat human at first glance, but their very sharp features and pointed ears set them apart. Elves are weaker than humans, but more than make up for this with their amazing agility and superior dexterity. They equal the High Man in their intelligence and wisdom, but are much more fair and charismatic

Vah Shir(Cat-men) - The Vah Shir are the neutral traders of the eq world. They are like dwarfs in the fact that they can pretty much go anywhere. They can be warriors, bards, rogues, and I think shaman and beastlords. Vah Shir are fast and can usually avoid a few hits, they have a natural 100 safefall, and they start on the moon.

(Race info. courtesy of Everlore.com)

Each race has the ability to be only certain classes, you can see which classes can be which races and vise versa on the create character screen.

Q: Where Can I get Everquest?

A: You can but Everquest at any computer software store, Everquest Trilogy (Incliding original everquest, Kunark and Velious Expansions) generally costs roughly $30, and the Luclin (moon of norrath) for the new redefined graphics and the Vah Shir race generally costs about $30 as well.

Q: How long will it take me to learn how to play Everquest?

A: That all depends on the person, but a tutorial can be downloaded here at : Tutorial

IMPORTANT NOTE: The EverQuest Tutorial is not compatible with current EverQuest files, be sure NOT to install the EverQuest Tutorial files into your EverQuest folder as this will render the EverQuest game or the Tutorial unplayable.

Q: Whats the easiest way to make money to buy equipment in Everquest?

A: Fight and loot (right click) the bodies of your kills and take the stuff on the body and sell them to a local merchant for money, and save your copper, as it will come in handy when wanting to buy that first new spell or piece of armor.

Q: Does everquest have a monthly fee?

A: Yes, $12.95 a month, or depending what state you live in there can be other taxes as well. Everquest can be payed with through an active credit card or Everquest activation cards which can be purchased most likely where you buy the game.

Here is my summary of classes from a high end/raiding standpoint for those who wish to know what a clase will be like in the later game(most change at least somewhat):

Bard-Bard's are probably the most versatile class of EQ. Caple of both soloing and serving in a group/raid situation very very well. The bard gets a Song version of almost every spell in the game(excluding teleport type spells). However, being a good bard requires a fast typing hand to "twist" songs(otherwise you wont be very good), and is generally far more "work"(that is, typing) then any other class.

Cleric-The primary cleric functions are to heal and buff. Do not play a cleric if you want to be very combat effective(unless youre fighting undead). Clerics have almost no soloing capability against living type MOBs in thier level range(unless youre decked out in NToV and Luclin raid-class gear). However, they are widely desired for grouping situations. And no serious raid can proceed without at least 1 cleric(for anything of real value youll need more like 10+ and the more the better). Clerics also get ressurection spells, which bring people back to thier corpse with experiance regained. Clerics are, infact, the best at ressurection.

Druid-Druid is one of the more solo-type classes in EverQuest. VI does a pretty good job of balancing out caracters. So since druids are more solo-capable, theyre not a critical class for groups. In fact, ive never known a group that couldnt get along without one. And theyre not too raid desired. However, Druids are one of 2 classes that can teleport. So they still maintain thier place in raids, as raids cant start unless everyone is there. Druids also get the track ability(see rangers), but its not nearly as good as a ranger. And Druids also serve as excellent backup healers for clerics. If you enjoy soloing AND grouping/raiding, this is probably the class for you.

Enchanter-The enchanter is probably the most group/raid-based class in EQ. Ive never known a chanter to be able to solo anything effectivly. However, as I said, classes lacking on one side of the spectrum in EQ are stronger in the other. Enchanters are highly prized in both groups and raids. A superb class choice if these are your prefered situations.

Magicians-Magicians are a very nice class indeed. Superb soloers(in some situations, the best), but with the advent of thier spell that lets them summmon mana-making items, coupled with the second best nuking(DD) spells in the game, and the best pets in the game, Magicians are very prized indeed. Be warned however, the Magician epic quest is the most deficult, but very nice.

Monk-Monks are a very nice class indeed. A monk can solo(in limited areas), though unfortunatly, not very effectivly in most places(unless you have good gear). Monks are also very well accepted in groups(though not critical in most places). You also get the benefit of being a hand-to-hand class. Monks are one class who could actually do decently in EQ without any gear(though it wouldnt be wise to do this on a regular basis). Monks are also the best pullers(as a clase). However, I have known SK's and even Necros that could pull more effectivly then the average monk.

Necromancer-My personal favorite. We are the most solo-driven of classes. However, that makes us, unfortunatly, not very desired in raids. One or 2 necros to a raid is a neccesity, however, anymore is usually just waisted space. Infidels! Ive played the only healer in groups as a necromancer! Granted, our heals suck. But a good necro can balance it out and make it work. Necros are also one of only 2(and the other class sucks at it by comparison, always have a Necro do this whenever possible) classes that can summon corpses. This is a very very important thing. Especially on raids when your entire raidforce is naked at the zoneline and all thier corpses are under the Boss MOB of the zone. Also, Necromancer's can ressurect for 93% experiance regain, however, its the only rez spell that requires a component. And the component is hard and usually very expensive to get.

Paladin-Capable of soloing undead pretty well, but other then that find a group. Paladins can be an awesome class to have, or just ok, depending on the makup of your group. However, they do have the benefit of being the only class besides clerics that can rez(well, unless you count the necro rez).

Ranger-This class can solo for awhile, but then it becomes almost impossible until you reach the high-end and get some good gear on you. Rangers can deal damage out probably the best of all the other melee or hybrid classes(excluding the new beastlord, or disciplines/special skills and the like). However, they can "dish it out but cant take it" if ya know what I mean. Rangers are also probably the weakest(in terms of tanking, that is taking damage) of the melee/hybrid classes. Rangers are also one of only 2 classes that recieves the tracking ability, and they get the most powerfull version. This ability allows them to track monsters, NPCs, and even PCs to a limited range. A powerfull ranger can track some entire zones.

Rogue-The rogue is a melee-type with a built in invisibility skill(sneak/hide). The rogue is probably the most effective damage-dealer of all non-caster classes, and most caster classes too. Highly valued in both raids and groups for this fact, however, they cannot solo until they get some good gear, and its usually not very effective then. Also, was mentioned originally that "and Rogues can take damage well." I must say this is false. Rogues have HP equal to that of most casters(excluding gear, since rogue gear is more melee-focused). Compared to a melee thier HP is horribly low. And even some casters with gear outsripe the average rogue with gear. Although I have used rogues for tanks personally, in some partnership situations, that is because I could heal them(and they had gear to heal themselves), and is not the norm.

Shadow Knight-SKs are probably smack dab in the middle of the spectrum. They are good soloers, AND can be very desired in both groups and raids. By class alone they are the second best pullers behind monks. However, I know many SKs that blow the average monk out of the water in terms of pulling. Also, SKs are probably the second best tanks(damage takers) behind Warriors. SKs also are one of only 2 classes that can summon corpses to them. However, they get these spells much later then a Necro would, and they never get the final of this line of spells. You will always want your corpse summoned by a necro if possible(cheaper and easier) however, if none are available, SK are still there.

Shaman-An interesting class indeed. The only class which can practice the Alchemy tradeskill(potion making). Shamans are excellent soloers the mid and high levels. But are also the best buffers(in most situations) and debuffers. And also have heals. Shamans are extremly valued on raids and in groups too. Infact, some raids cannot even succeed without the help of shamans. Shamans also get a weak pet in the midlevels.

Warrior-Warriors are a pretty simple and basic class. Best tanks, but not so good damage. Difficult to solo as a warrior, but you are highly desired in groups and raids.

Wizards-Wizards are the best nukers of EverQuest and the best pure damage dealers of spellcasters(and some rogues, depending on what spells they have acquired). Wizards are also one of 2 classes that can teleport. However, they can teleport to more places and in more ways then a druid can.

Beastlord-Many people have yet to see the BLs potential for raid-type situations, because at this point, since they are new, there are very few 50+ beastlords out there. I have realized this potential for a long time now. First, Beastlords make excellent soloers, some have said even better then necros. They get pets, weak heals, weak buffs(and perhaps debuffs, not sure) and can melee. Beastlords are a Monk/Shaman hybrid. However, BLs are generally even less desired in groups and Raids then Necros. Everyone has thier place in a raid though, and I have discovered why BLs would be such a valuable tool. BLs are the best class at pushing MOBs around Ive ever seen. This causes spells to be interupted(fail). So, a Beastlord could be very valuable against a casting type Boss-MOB.

Question number one: Put your fishing pole in your Primary melee slot and press the fishing button. If you don't have a fishing button, set it up by right clicking on one of the empty slots on your abilities panel (tab through the panels where "sit" and "camp" are located until you find it) and selecting "fishing." Hope that wasn't too basic.

Question number two: Most classes can solo at low levels. The best I've seen at low levels are any melee, Mages, Necros and Druids. At mid-high levels Melees drop off and Wizards, Shaman and Enchanters pick up. I don't know anything about Beastlords at any level.
