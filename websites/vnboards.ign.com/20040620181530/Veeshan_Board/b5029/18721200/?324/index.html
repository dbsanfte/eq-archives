Ninan,

I think your idea is nice in theory but...

a) it faces problems in terms of who decides who is invited to your discussions? What mobs/areas/encounters will be addressed by this agreement?

b) there is a lot of mutual distrust/disrepect baggage floating around here. Just some examples: CT and FOH loathe each other. OG, Legion, KOE and others don't like CT. Other people don't like FOH. Yet others don't like PD.

c) how do you ensure people abide by any agreement? you can't other than to come here and flame them, which guilds have in various conflicts, leaves nothing but a bitter taste in your mouth

d) another complication for Veeshan is the "wild card" of the Japanese guilds. Players from the U.K., Sweden, Denmark, Australia and other countries may frequent these boards, but I have never seen a member of a Japanese post here (aside from "Guru" and "eqsuxors" ). How would any agreement be influenced by their ability to take mobs covered by it?

e) Veeshan has always run on a "free enterprise" mindset(or "survival of the fittest" if you prefer): he who sees an opportunity and capitalizes first is right. The very idea of schedules (which is what some of the thoughts in this thread suggest) is anathema. Many of us don't WANT to wait 3 weeks for mob X because it is guild 5's turn this week. If the mob is up and no one is on it, we want it. Call it greed, call it ambition, whatever: most of us suffer from it.

Seriously? I think if anyone ever wanted to bother with drafting up such a proposal, the biggest problem would be issue B (tied in with E): many of the guilds on Veeshan just don't respect each other. A lot of people will insist on pointing fingers on this, but not nearly as many want to take the first step and show some respect to others themselves.
