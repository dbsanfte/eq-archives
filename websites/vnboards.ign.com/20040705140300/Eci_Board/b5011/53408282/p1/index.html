Registered: Jun 01
 		Date Posted: 7/14/03 9:01pm Subject: RE: What movie makes you cry???
There are lots of scenes in movies that stung my eyes a bit. "AI" definitely had its moments of tearjerking, but it was kinda blatant and manipulative though.

One odd scene worth remembering that brought tears to my eyes was the Hal9000 shutdown scene in "2001: A Space Oddessey." The point where Hal says "I'm frightened" just sort of hit me in the gut. For all of his sinister creepiness, Hal was just a scared little kid for a second there.

The ending of "The Miracle Mile" was so romantic/tragic that it made me weep a bit. The premise is, a young man meets a girl and falls in love right away, but he oversleeps and misses their first date. While he's at a diner feeling miserable, the payphone rings, it's a wrong number, and the guy at the other end is a frightened young SAC officer trying to warn his father that they have launched all their missiles at the Soviet Union. Harry has 90 minutes to find the girl he loves and escape Los Angeles! I won't spoil the end for you, but the last two minutes are so romantic/tragic that I felt weepy.

I shed a few tears when Lou Gosset, Jr.'s charatcer died in "Enemy Mine."

The final scene in the film "Casablanca," where Rick tells Ilsa to go with Victor Lazlo, because she might not regret staying with him then, but she would soon, and for the rest of her life, was a real tear-jerker. The sight of a man sending his One True Love away to live with another man because he loves her more than he values his own happiness is guaranteed to make anyone with any emotions at all cry like a baby.

But the absolute tear-popping winner is the Red Coat scene from "Schindler's List." That film was all in black&white, except for one scene in which one object, a small girl's overcoat, is colored bright red. Again, I won't spoil that scene for people who haven't seen the film, but it ranks highest on the weepometer.
