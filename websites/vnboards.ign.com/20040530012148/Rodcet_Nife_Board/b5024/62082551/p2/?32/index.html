Let's talk about the Drama
Let's put some things in perspective. I was told months ago that clerics needed a leader. I thought about that and then it went on the back burner, as many things often do because when RL calls I always take that first, just like any other person in this guild would and should. We all know, without RL, EQ is really meaningless.

Unfortunately, over time the fact that the clerics needed a heal channel leader festered into something that was far out of control. Disrespect built to an all-time high among some of them. Now granted, I personally felt bad about this. So in turn, I started putting whatever free time I could into it. The officers had discussed it as well and it turns out we didn't come up with any workable solution either.

I think in the mean time, at least a few officers were scrambling to try to help remedy the situation, including myself. Zerxes was putting time into trying to explain the situation, but somewhere along the way the message was missed by me and probably the officers as well.

…

Finally as a last ditch effort, I decided talking to the people who were at odds on the phone would help me understand what people were feeling. Let me just say, many hours on the phone (I'm talking 20+ hours on this issue in the last 3 weeks), and really no resolution. It was a deadlock per say and in fact I feel I made things much worse. Not only that, but I feel badly that it turns out people's feelings were hurt intensely, especially Sippi and Chiakai

…

People may come out and say I shirked them, but unfortunately I have to disagree, because I gain nothing at all by doing so. People wanted me to make a decision which was best for the guild, but they wanted me to make their decision. Unfortunately, since I know some people personally and understand them, I did not make the decision they wanted me to make, because I just didn't feel confident it was the right decision. So in turn, no decision was made on who would lead the clerics and we searched for compromises, which as you know from above did not pan out.

So the end result is myself and the officers failed to resolve personal issues between people. No surprise here, we are not trained in this and so life goes on. People will continue to dislike each other and that's that.

I personally failed to recognize that leadership was essential to the success of the healers, and that can be blamed on me and no one else. I can't say my own lack of decision didn't cause harm to the guild. Because it did, and I take responsibility for my part.

Let's talk about Zerxes
Please, everyone, don't get me wrong here, Zerxes is a great guy and he's always had UoP at heart. I have never had a ill feeling towards him and quite frankly I am saddened he has taken this route, because I can honestly say I have no idea why. If I said or did something that offended him, I did not hear it directly from him.

I feel like he was misunderstood in his communication about this issue of the Cleric heal channel. And clearly him and I had a big communication gap. However, I was unaware of this and he was aware. Things like this can be resolved without much trouble by making a simple phone call or an e-mail, which he did neither. Once I realized I may have been missing something, I called him, twice, left him messages and no return phone call. I care about what happened here and I am fully willing to discuss it. But it has to be a two-way street. It pains me to see things go this way, because it could have been worked out, and in the end we all pay for simple miscue of communication.
