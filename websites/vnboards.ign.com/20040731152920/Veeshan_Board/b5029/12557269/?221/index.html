From EQClerics

Tuesday, May 8
The Ragefire Camp
Posted by Rayne at 20:38 PM


For months now Clerics have been beseeching Verant to modify the Ragefire camp. Verant has been silent on the issue until today, when Absor responded. So, as with many intolerable situations in EQ we had the choice of dealing with reality as it exists in the game (don't analyze that too closely =P), or getting it modified. Verant declines to modify, so we must then deal (refusing to do the quest or camp is one way of dealing).

A large part of the problem exists due to the relative ease of the quest. Because of this many players are at the final stage in a state of frustration due to their inability to complete the quest quickly, if at all. In hindsight Verant admits that the quest is too easy. However, they refuse to ease the bottleneck so they are content with the status quo. Accept it.

On to the camp. I've read many threads concerning conflict over the Ragefire camp. While I sympathize with those who feel that their "camp rights" were denied, I also feel that it is time to re-define what it means to camp Ragefire.

First, let me list a few things that are not camping Ragefire:

Having a Monk or Necro'd feigned in the lair. Also, having any other character sitting around watching for the spawn.

Having a solo Cleric (yourself) sitting in the lair waiting for the spawn.

Keeping the Fire Giants cleared with a small group.

Any amount of players waiting on the spawn that are not sufficient of themselves to kill the spawned Ragefire.

All the above examples are merely an early warning system to allow you to get a head start on gathering the force necessary to kill Ragefire.

What is camping Ragefire:

A force sufficient to kill Ragefire immediately when he spawns.

See 1. above.

When Ragefire spawns, turn in your Shimmering Pearl and kill him. Don't ask for time to buff, for forces to get there, etc. If you can't kill him when he spawns you are not ready. Period. Call in the next group. Ok, I'd probably allow five minutes or so (heck of a time to hit the head), but otherwise you must be ready. If you aren't ready you aren't camping, your scouting.

Is the above harsh? /em shrugs It's reality. Verant gave us an easy quest. They made the reward for that quest incredibly desirable. That guarantees fierce competition for the reward. With the post from Verant I don't see how one can reasonably treat this camp any other way. The first group at the camp with the force necessary to kill Ragefire gets the spawn (don't dither turning in that pearl). And you know what? It's fair.

Rayne
