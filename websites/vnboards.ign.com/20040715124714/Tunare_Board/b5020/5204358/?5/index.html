1) You swear allegiance to a patron, who may have a patron of his own. Also, you may become a patron yourself, having vassals swear allegiance to you. Basically, patrons get xp when their vassals get xp. Not taken from vassal xp, but in addition to. So why become a vassal? Depends. Patrons often give clues, items, money, etc. It depends on what's promised. So anyway, you end up with these big, Amway-like pyramids of allegiance. Like a guild kinda, but more structured like feudal society. The cool thing about it is, it encourages people to be more polite to each other, because everyone is a potential patron or vassal.

2) I haven't played much, didn't have a chance to group, but I've heard the same.

3) I believe so. It's gotten a little harder recently, due to a 'nerf'. Yes, they happen sometimes in AC, too. Not as often, it seems.

4) around $40, and $10/month

I've quit EQ for a couple months, and I'm switching to AC, mostly because I don't have enough time to group, and soloing IS easier in AC. AC isn't as diverse and colorful as EQ, though, regarding races and geography. Well, Good Journey, wherever you may go.
