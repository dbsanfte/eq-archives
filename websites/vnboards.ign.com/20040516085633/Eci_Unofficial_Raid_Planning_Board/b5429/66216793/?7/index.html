This has nothing to do with DoA or Magget. Do not misunderstand me. I highly respect Magget and will follow his leadership just about anywhere (friendly to pacific standard time, of course..hehe). There seems to be a trend that I worry about. Now, I may be wrong, and if so, please correct me. (Correct me nicely though. You know how frail my paladin ego is.)

If a guild is hosting a "public" raid, since this is THE public raiding board, albeit unofficial, pertinent information should be disclosed here and sign-ups here should be honored. This is an OPEN forum.

If a guild is having a guild raid and opening it up to the public to attend, then that guild should just annouce that they have an open raid. Anyone interested should sign-up at the respective guild website since only sign-ups there will be honored. This is a CLOSED forum.

What seems to worry me is that open guild raids (guild raids that are open to the public) are now being referred to and generally accepted as "public" raids. Seems like the trend is backwards and not forward. If this continues and more guilds join the bandwagon on this, then it is no different than invitation-only guild raids.

The trend looks kind of discouraging.

Anyway, just my coppers. Be gentle.

Khuji,

I will respond to you about this for hopefully the final time and you will understand why the venue Magget chooses to use is ezboard.

VNboards simply do not provide all the functionality that is available via ezboard.

On ezboard topics can be:

--Editted with no time restriction
--Moderated to keep discussion on topic
--Threads added and deleted to keep the section free of outdated material.
--Access restricted to discourage anon posters (ie the burittos of life)

None of those things can be done on the vnboards. Also not to mention why would non-magget raiders want to read raid recaps and dkp postings etc.

Everything is done on the upside. All dkp is clearly posted (albeit sometimes a bit slow), all topics are discussed and freely visible to anyone that goes to the site, even those that are not raiders with Magget.

Now as far as your concern regarding ownership of the raid. The raids are kept on the DoA site simply because it can be viewed "ad" free since my guild pays for the ezboard space.

Have no doubt that these raids are truly public and to my knowledge no person has been denied access to attending for any reason other than they didn't meet the most simple of level requirements.

Had these been DoA "public" raids I can assure you a large number of people that have attended from rival guilds would have been told what tree to go F*ck but since they are public guild politics has not come into it.

Hopefully you understand the difference now so that I don't have to keep going over this.
