This is what I would do for Pallies and Bards if I was making game changes at Sony:

1. Remove the White Scale, Red Scale, and Green Scales from the loot tables. Make them quest returns from an NPC in an Antonican zone.

2. Give all raiders who kill Vox/Naggy/Sev/Gore/OtherKunarkDragons a flag, one for each color. Make the flag progressive, meaning that the first time you are there at a Vox/Gore kill, you get WhiteFlagOne. If you have WhiteFlagOne and you are in a raid that kills Vox, you get WhiteFlagTwo.

3. If a Bard has WhiteFlagFour (or whatever level seems best suited to the Sony Timesink Mentality, maybe WhiteFlagTen), and he talks to the quest NPC I mentioned in #1, that quest NPC will give the Bard the White Scale. If a Paladin has RedFlagFour, he would get a Burnt Book from the NPC.

Doing this would make it a requirement that to receive an Epic quest item from killing these dragons would require killing a dragon of that color at least 4 times (more if folks think it's necessary to make it a 5th level or 10th level flag to keep the game challenging).

There are Paladins who have killed Naggy a dozen or more times, and they still didn't get a Burnt Book, and there are Pallies who have killed Naggy or Tal ONCE and gotten a Burnt Book. Sometimes the dragon drops 2, sometimes none at all. If your Bard had to kill Vox ten times to get a White Scale, but you were sure that after the tenth kill you'd get your White Scale, you'd be a lot less inclined to mess with lists and schedules, and a lot more likely to enjoy the Epic Quest process rather than view it as a hell on Norrath. Also, Pallies and Bards would be less inclined to stay under the level cap for a year trying to quest their epics like Daethdar did.

Having to be in on 20-30 dragon kills would be a good time sink, which Sony likes, and would foster cooperation over competition. Pallies and Bards would enjoy the game a lot more I think, and I just don't see a down side to this. The epic pieces are dropping at an average rate of one per every two kills, this would up the "drop rate" some but it would require those that DID get the epic pieces to attend more dragon kills on average than the old method did, where a lucky player with a good guild could epic them up in a few days by racing to Kunark dragons. I think this method would be far less contentious in the long run, and would result in a happier player base, especially Pallies, Bards, and those of us who have to play with them.

If Sony can tinker with the Cleric epic last summer, I see no reason why they can't remove the contentiousness and unpleasantness from the acquisition of these particular quest components. Kill the dragons, not each other!
