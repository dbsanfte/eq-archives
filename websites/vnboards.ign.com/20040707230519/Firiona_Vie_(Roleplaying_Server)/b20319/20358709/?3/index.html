Gneen,

If you are seeking to travel, try to get a bind in the cities you are visiting. This is a spell that wizard and preist types get that will change your respawn point to where ever you are standing in the city at the time the spell is cast. So no more running back to FP from Akanon.

Your correct about the languages... it is tough. Make every effort to learn languages as you adventure. Hopefully by level 16 you will be "very good" at another language common to other folks. Enough to make communication fairly reasonable anyways.

Do try to group. It is possible to solo at your level, but I tink you will find finding groups can make the EQ experience alot more fun. Heck, if you let everyone know in the group that you are kinda new and looking for tips I bet you can discover a wealth of information too. Assuming you can talk to them =).

Good luck and have fun.

The first thing to remember is that the Firiona Vie (FV) server is more difficult to play on than the standard ruleset servers. If you get frustrated, you may want to try one of the other servers for a while to get a better idea of how the game works. But if you do, please come back! We need more good players on FV.

This server seems to have two lingua francas, namely Elvish and Human. You will find life to be much easier if you invest some time and/or practice points into geting one of these language skills up into the fifties (skill ranking of "average" or better). There are some other threads where the techniques for learning languages quickly are discussed, so I won't go into all the gory details in this post.

Note, this is in terms of playing a "good/neutral" race like I imagine gnomes to be. If you plan to spend most of your life hobnobbing with trolls and ogres, then I expect that Dark Speech would be more useful.

Considering that you are still Bound to Ak'Anon, I advise you try to learn Elvish.

As for the issue of Binds, the melee (warrior, rogue, monk) and hybrid (paladin, shadow knight, ranger) classes have a tougher time of it, because they cannot ever cast the Bind Affinity spell on themselves. Nonetheless, once you can overcome the language barrier, it isn't usually that hard to find people willing to set your Bind point for you.

Try to do some quests that will give you faction and cash. There's a dwarf named Gunlock Jure in the paladins' guild of Kaladim who wants bone chips. There's also a high elf in the temple of Felwithe who wants them. It's worth your while to whack every skeleton you see to collect the bone chips. You could also sell them to PC necromancers, if that's not against your gnome's ethics.

I think there are some quests involving items looted off slain orcs in Crushbone: shoulder pads and belts. You might want to look into those.
