With EQ at this stage, there are few 'unknowns', nothing 'new' around the corner. Because of that, players like yourself who have done it once (50+) will more than likely not be any happier here than on any other EQ server (except possibly trying your hand on PvP server). If exploration and doing something 'new' intrigues you, it isn't any better here on T.P. than back on R.N.

Most players here are veterans(like yourself), knowing exactly where to hunt at what level and what equipment to get, the crowds to level up in the better places are large, and often 'closed' to accepting additional members as groups and even guilds moved en masse here.

There are many players who can level quickly (already some 40+ here after 3 weeks) and rush to be the 'elite' of this server. The possibility of some of these forming the dreaded 'uberguild of zone control' who claim every God/Dragon/Plane as they do on some other servers is a real possibility. (there have been some nasty stories already) For some, striving to obtain this goal is what drives them, to be the servers 'firsts' and make a 'name' for themselves. Even on R.N., where for the most part the high level guilds co-operated, some signs of the overpopulation at 50+ are starting to show. The competition for the few God/Dragon/Plane drops is becoming tense.

Myself, I am still playing (my 4th mid-level character) and having fun with friends, both here and on Rodcet Nife still. But as new games come close, with opportunities to explore the uncharted, I find the sands in my EQ hourglass are nearly gone. Shadowbane(full PvP), Anarchy-Online(Futuristic), Atriarch(Sci-Fi), Horizons(full PvP), Dark Age of Camelot(teams PvP) all show a lot of promise, and the reports from early Beta of DAoC are very positive so far.

So if you really miss EQ, this server will not be the same as 'the early days' of 1999 when exploring unknown areas brought a sense of adventure to the game. But as a game, there is still some fun to be had in many ways. (I 'retired' and tried numerous other games[DiabloII, AoEII) in the meantime, but they seem to pale to the rush I got from EQ)

Anyhow, those are my ramblings from a boring day at work.
