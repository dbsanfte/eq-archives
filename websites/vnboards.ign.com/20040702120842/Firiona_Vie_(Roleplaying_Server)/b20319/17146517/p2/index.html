I see a fairly interesting discussion forming here, but we seem to have at least one misconception coming from one of the 'sides' that are forming.

There seems to be some sort of assumption by some that pvp is generally desired, rather than a minority of the EQ population.

Towards the end of Beta, there was a long discussion on whether Redeye (which eventually became Verant) would bring up even one pvp server. Everquest originally was a PvE game only and it was decided quite close to final to bring up a single PvP server. The PvP fans began posting to all message boards available that one server could not possibly be enough, and that they would never play on a PvP server that would be as crowded as the Everquest one would be, from all the people who wanted PvP.

Everquest final came, and went and the PvP server was virtually always in the bottom three for population. After some silence from the PvP fans, they began posting again, saying that the PvP server was not popular because it was too much of a free for all, since that was not REAL PvP. Verant brought up the first Team server, and again, the PvP fans began posting to every message board that one could not be enough, as everybody obviously wanted PvP, and indeed, when Tallon Zek came up, five thousand people regularly played it, often crashing the server, and as a result, Vallon Zek was brought online to handle the load, but within three weeks, all three of the PvP servers dropped into the bottom five for population.

PvP just wasn't that popular.

A lot of time passed, and the populations of many servers climbed to the point where servers had to be split. New servers were opened, almost immediately gaining far more population than even the biggest PvP server.

And more time passed, and it was declared by PvP fans that it was the four and eight level limit that prevented TRUE PvP, since after all, it was a free for all that players wanted, not restrictions (note reasons for previous PvP server), and Sullon Zek was born and indeed it was popular for close to a month, often rising nearly to the middle of the pack in server population, but after that month, it drifted down to a place with the other three PvP server, close to the bottom of the server population list.

You see, PvP just isn't that desirable.

And so we see a precedent here. A roleplaying server has been requested by players for months, and one has finally been offered to the players. It is an experiment and it may not be that popular, since nobody truly knows how many player are willing to take the restrictions of the server in order to have a 'better' roleplaying environment. One thing is clear though, if PvP is involved, the server population is almost guaranteed to be small, since the players have voted in the most democratic way possible, by their presence or lack of presence, that they do not in any way want another PvP server.

Okay, for what it's worth, here are my thoughts on this.

I've played almost exclusively on Rallos Zek off and on since EQ began -- at first for roleplay reasons (and indeed, the golden days of RZ saw quite a lot of very good RP before the griefers and gankers drove most of it out), then later simply because I had developed friendships there and felt too awkward trying to start anew on blue servers (I tried, but it didn't work well for me).

I'm not a griefer (or in RZ terminology, I'm not a "pk"). Nevertheless, over time, I've grown quite skilled out of necessity at the admittedly Quake-style tactics that are necessary to survive combat in RZ. More to the point, I've also learned a great deal about PvP environments and how they really work, at least as they apply to EQ.

So, to start with, I'd like to dispel a popular myth -- to wit, the belief that PvP with looting options prevents twinking.

This is patently false, as anyone who plays in RZ should know. The fact is that the more successful "pks" twink out their pking characters to the hilt with resist gear, HP gear, and fantastic weapons and armor far, far above their own levels that make them nigh unstoppable to anyone else in their range. Take, for example, the dwarven rogue known as Jooke. He can easily take on and defeat three people of his level at once. A caster at his level with full mana could blow her entire mana pool on him and not come close to killing him. Twinking doesn't just happen in RZ, it's one of the most powerful weapons pkers have at their disposal. And, of course, looting in turn gives them still more money for twinking.

There's also a fairly popular belief that if you take away looting, people won't have a motivation for engaging in PvP. This is also clearly false, as is made evident by the simple fact that people here are arguing for the existence of a roleplay-oriented PvP environment. Obviously, looting isn't necessary for such an environment to exist. However, it IS necessary to eliminate (or at least drastically reduce) the random, non-roleplay oriented attacks that dominate servers like RZ.

So how do you do that?

In my opinion, the key lies in determining what different factors DO motivate individuals to engage in PvP. After that it's simply a matter of removing those factors that encourage random attacks.

I feel that five primary reasons exist for people to engage in PvP (but if you can think of others, please do tell!):

1. They want to loot others.
2. They want to cause others grief.
3. They have an out of character grudge to settle (often a character who wasn't involved in the fight is used to settle this: "I'll get my wizzie and kick ur a**")
4. They're trying to help someone else in a fight (though this can be truly in character often as not).
5. They're actually roleplaying.

The first one is so easy to eliminate, it's sickening. And it's one of the very worst ones and even a direct cause of some of the others. Simply. Remove. Looting! ALL looting. Don't just make it coin-only -- take it ALL out! If not being able to loot makes you not want to engage in PvP, guess what? You're not interested in RP anyway. Go to RZ and join the stupid Flowers of Happiness; you'll fit in great.

The second one is trickier. People who kill you just to annoy and get a rise out of you will always be there, regardless of whether they can actually loot you or not. I don't think it's really possible to completely remove that element in a PvP-enabled environment. But most of them will probably get bored since they can't loot you and cause you any REAL grief, so that should decrease significantly as well. The few griefers who stuck around in such an environment would probably find themselves outnumbered and consequently outgunned most of the time, thus reducing them to little more than minor nuisances.

The third problem is another one of those things that never really goes away. Immaturity abounds. But with RZ still in place, it's doubtful that the more petty types of people would remain interested in this hypothetical new server for very long. Where's the fun if they can't loot and cause people lots of grief? And I submit that the people who are into that kind of thing are the same people who get into ooc shouting matches about who "owns" whom.

The fourth reason for engaging in PvP will remain, but that's not really a bad thing, as it can certainly be expected that Good Guy A and Good Guy B will generally join forces against Bad Guy C if the situation arises. And then finally there's the fifth, and that's the one we want most of all anyway!

So in conclusion, I believe that simply removing all looting from the PvP environment (and PvP faction hits as well, since not all light elves, for example, are really "good guys") would solve 99% of the problem. I've SEEN roleplay exist in the PvP environment. It IS possible. It's just that the rampant pillaging and looting of random pkers drives most of the roleplayers away, and even those who remain tend to abandon most of their roleplaying efforts and instead adopt a more paranoid, practical, abrupt manner ... just to survive. Like I did.
