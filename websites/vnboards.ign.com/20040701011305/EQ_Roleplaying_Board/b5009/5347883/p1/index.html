/ooc Thanks for starting a new thread, these things fill up pretty fast!

The orc lift itself was almost overrun by a surge of orcs. They were clawing up over the lift and draging the defenders over the edge to a quick death at the bottom from the Orcs if not the fall itself.

But a more sinister sight greeted my eyes upon the hill infront of the lift. A huge Ghoul with eyes of ice stood there. An ominous finger pointed up at the lift. I stood there, my eyes locked with his until a hand nudged my shoulder.

"Come young one, the battle is not over and today is not the time for thought." Said an old Bard, one I had never seen before in an armor of the deepest gold. "Let us join the fight and save our Kelethin."

With that he charged into battle with words upon his lips that seemed to terrify the orcs. I charged after him and we drove the orcs from the lift. With the Orcs temporarily driven from the lift the old Bard grabbed my arm and led me to the nearby Inn. Inside we found the Innkeeper. A young elf who had baricaded the door and stood at the window of her Inn with a sack of muffins in hand.

"What are you doing at the window with muffins? Do you plan to eat the orcs out of Kelethin?" I ask.

"No young one, I have been defending my lift from the foul orcs below and now I see why I have stayed" The young elf turned and bowed to the Bard in gold. "Welcome to my Inn good sir. I have awaited your coming throughout the years. Another has come to run the Inn and I am ready to leave this life for another"

Pointing to me the Bard commanded me to take the sack and take up post at the window.
"Throw with strength and accurcy young one for Kelethin now depends upon you"

I was confused yet stood my place as instructed. The Bard and the Innkeeper left the Inn and jumped from the lift floating down gracefully to the ground. All Orcs fleeing from their path.

Soon they stood there before the Ghoul, one glinting of gold in the fires and the other hidden behind the dark dye of a old suit of leather armor. A great battle commenced and they combatants were soon lost in the trees and the darkness of another wave of Orcs.

The Orcs began climbing again and I stood there with the muffins in hand not knowing what to do. Soon they reached the lift again and the few remaining defenders were almost overwhelmed. I saw an orc sneaking up behind an Elf locked in combat and in a act of fear threw the muffin in my hand. The muffin struck the Orc much as a large rock might strike a small child: with a sickening thud and a spray of blood. The muffins were as hard as any substance I had ever seen and I quickly began emptying the sack on the attackers.

Many days later, as reported by those who found me, I woke up with the smell of smoke in my nostrils and a beam of sunshine upon my face. I was the only survivor of those defending the orc lift that night. None believed my tale of the Bard in gold and the Ghoul that had so captivated my vision.

I was hailed as a hero and the muffins that saved the day have become part of the travelling pack of any who rember that fearfull night. For if left alone they harden to the strength of diamonds yet with a small dab of water they soften and taste like the finest muffin ever tasted.

Many years later I learned a curious poem from a traveling bard in Qeynos. I always end my tale of Kelethin as such:

The Demon Lord shall come one day,
The tired and weary shall fall his prey,
The strong and brave folly shall slay;
For the Demon Lord has come this day.

Sunlight and Darkness again shall roam,
To delay the Demon and send him home,
With their coming one shall see;
The Icy Demon choped to three.

Artifacts long lost will again appear,
To announce the slaying of one all fear,
Seven shall step forward to stop all woes;
To slay the Demon and destroy all foes.
