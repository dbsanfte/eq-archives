Being's post has made me think of a new idea... We should post our most traumatic/hilarious expirience ever in EQ. Should make for entertaining reading... Come on folks, I know you all have had odd expiriences.

I'll start, being a lil new to the game it wont involve mighty dragons of course... but I found it entertaining.

To anyone who has ever entered a fight, you know the first rule of combat. Look around to see if your enemy has help. I of course forgot this rule, I took a lazy glance around in the ButcherBlock Mts. and saw only 2 goblin whelps. Thinking I saw an easy kill, I sent my pet into combat and followed through with a spell. First thing I saw in the goblin camp was the 2 whelps, the gob wiz on the hill nearby, and another 2 goblins come running straight towards me and my pet. This didn't take long for me to realize it was a loosing situation... so after a quick goodbye to my pet and the /shout of "TRAIN" upon my lips, I did what any good mage at my level does. RAN LIKE HELL. To my good fortune someone picked off the wizzie and I was able to run all the way back to the cross-roads and the safety of the guards. A short while after the elmination of the goblins, while I was resting I got a message that I had gained expirience and that my faction had changed. Not 30 seconds later my pet comes over the hill at 10% life. The brave thing had apparently stuck out the fight with one of em and won! Not to mention traveled half the length of the BB to get back to me. Needless to say, I've been in love with mages since that day.

Probably not that great a story, but in my personal expirience it was one of my favorite...

Well, none of my characters have reached level 8 yet so my embarrassments won't be so stellar.

I got my Dwarf Paladin up to level 6 and decided to enter my first group. So I find and level 6 Enchanter and a level 5 Magician and we go around having a good ol' time. We killed spiders, scarabs, orcs or goblins and even an emerald drake or two. All the lowland basilisks are conning red to us. Finally one conned yellow to the enchanter and I, so we decide to give it a shot. Now, the magicians I'm with know they're going to burn quite a bit of mana in this fight, so they elect me to go pull it. It's walking away from us, so I run off as fast as I can after it. Over a hill I go, and finally catch up with my would-be prize.

Auto-attack.
*You bash a lowland basilisk for 5 points damage*
Good, at least I didn't miss. Time to run...
*A lowland basilisk hits YOU for 9 points damage*
*You are stunned*
Uh-oh.

No problem, I think. At least I have a lot of hit points. I calmly tell my group that I'm stunned and need help.

*You are no longer stunned*

Great! I start run back to the group killing spot, but when I get over the hill, no one is there. Still running, I reach the pulling spot and get a group message, "where are you?" They'd forgotten which direction I went! Needless to say, I fired off a /loc and did my first nighttime coprse retrieval.

-----------------

Of course, now I need to throw out a good story.

Same level 6 Dwarf Paladin. Same area (I think it's ButcherBlock). This time I'm grouped with a level 6 Gnome Warrior, who was nicely twinked I might add. We decide to take down a skeleton or two. So we engage in battle and he's taking all the damage, being a warrior. Usually finished a battle with 2 bubbles of health left, so that's quite a bit of downtime. So I get the idea to solo the skeletons until I'm beat up a little bit then have him jump in and finish it off. We set up an agreement that he'll rush into battle ONLY after I've lost a bubble of health. Works like a charm. We're talking just seconds of downtime between battles. Then he gets it into his head to take on another skeleton camp (the golden skeleton tower) because our camp took too long to respawn. There's only 3 skeletons here, but you have to fight 2 of them at once. We take down the one who solos us quite easily. Then plan for the 2 on 2 battle. I'll be the one to engage battle with both skeletons. My little Gnome friend will wait until I have lost one bubble of health, then attack the one I'm working on and draw his attention. We'll work on that one skeleton while the other whacks away on me, then attack the remaining skeleton. Again, this works great and at the end of the battle, we had both reached level 7 together! Back to Kaladim and call it a night!

That's long, I'm sure.
