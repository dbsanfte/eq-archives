I recently returned to EQ a few months ago and started from scratch on an established server. I've had little problems finding groups and/or friends.

A few things you can do to maximize your rise to 65 if you don't want to do it alone are:

1. Pick a class that lends itself well to grouping. I elected to play a shaman because they have a wide variety of abilities, they can solo or fit well in any size group, and they are highly sought after for full groups and LDoN. If you pick a class that's both fun to play and highly desired for groups you won't have to wait long to be in groups.

(Side note: on an established server there is much less low level grouping...and there really is no need for any class to group before 10th or even later depending on class...but even on an established server you can get groups.)

2. Develop a good reputation as a person and as a player of your chosen class. Hopefully this is obvious...but some basics are, play your class well so people want YOU instead of someone else that plays your class. Be nice to people and respectful (where respect is due). Avoid being rude. Avoid spamming for whatever reason (last thing you want to develop is a BAD rep due to spamming/begging). Etc.

3. This isn't critical...but it helps to acquire all your spells and decent gear. This might involve a bit of farming and it's very easy to raise cash even at the lowest levels. But having all your spells helps your reputation for playing your class (people won't want to group with you if you're missing a critical spell) and having decent gear makes you more durable and shows you're serious. In a game that features lots of twinks in ridiculously high level gear it's tougher to stand out unless you attempt to compete (you won't be equal...but if you have decent gear and play your class well few will be able to tell the difference).

4. It wouldn't hurt to join a guild - as it provides you with more possible companions and adventure options. This is a tricky issue because you ideally want to get to know folks in a guild before joining, and the chances of any guild worth joining (besides a friendly/family/theme guild - which can be very good or can just be another chat channel) recruiting low level folks is slim.

5. Avoid begging. Ask for advice, yes - you will often get great advice from your fellow players (in addition to smart arse remarks from the ever-present morons) but don't ask to be PLed, don't ask for money, and don't ask for gear. Also don't ask for buffs unless someone is offering and those buffs don't require reagents (and as a side note it is very rude to send /tells to buffers asking them to buff you when there are other people of the same class offering to buff via public channels - but I digress). It's way, way too easy to accumulate plats in today's EQ and there's no reason for even the most clueless noob to beg. All begging does is show that you're lazy, unmotivated, and unwilling to learn even the basics (of spiderling silk farming *grin*) and beggars generally get flamed to hell.
