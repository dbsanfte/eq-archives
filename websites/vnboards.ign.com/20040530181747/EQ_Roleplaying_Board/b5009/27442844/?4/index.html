I have a level 29 mage and a level 20 pally on the Rathe, but I'm also a grad student writing an essay about Everquest. I have a few questions for folks who roleplay on non-speciality servers -- i.e. non-PvP, non-roleplay, non-legends. If you happen to be from the U.S. or Western Europe, I'm especially interested in your responses. But, of course, I'll read anything anyone from anywhere has to say . No need to answer all of these, of course. If anything prompts a response, I'm happy to hear it. Thanks in advance for any thoughts you may have.

1. As a roleplayer, how does the "official" Everquest narrative (how Norrath was created, for example, or the new Luclin narrtive) impact the way you play your character?

2. Beyond the built-in in game factions, how does the way territory is divided within Everquest impact how you interact with other players in the game?

3. What do you consider the major "theme" in your character's life? i.e. why is she a loner, an orphan, lost royalty, the daughter of a dwarven miner?

Heh, I'm from the US, just so you know.

1) The "official" narrative helps me get into the mood of the world. I try to make a character fit into the world that surrounds hin or her; in a MMOG, shaping the world around you is all but impossible. I know most of the history of Norrath and Luclin and use it when I RP.

2) I'm not sure I understand the question... but as a wanderer (and someone who builds up faction for fun) it's not really an issue; I interact with folks regardless of where I am, and I go most places.

3) Cyst is a refugee from the hate- and fear-filled lives of his trollish kin. He wasn't raised that way. He strives to gain the acceptance of the Tribunal, helping others and gaining the contempt of his racial peers as he does so. *shrug*

Good questions ... i'll try to help you out.

1. I look at the lore of EQ or Norrath for example as a guide of what my character has a basic knowledge of. She is a rogue by trade and could care less about modern marvels so the impact of Luclin for example didn't bother her or change the way she perceives the virtual world.

2. She is half-elven so she has no family per say. Her mother was a high elf while her father was human. She actually has two half sisters ... one human and one high elf who are of the opposite profession. Paladin and Cleric compared to my rogue. She doesn't consider anyone as evil for she trusts only her family (guild) and looks at everyone as a decoy in case things go bad.

3. Her main personality is care free. She gives only the loyalty to her family and like i said above she will group with others without detest but she will leave them if need be.

1.) I use the original narrative to as a sort of tool in the way I play. For example, I ,based on what character I am playing, don't interact with races that my race are enemies with.
2.) I hardly ever venture into territory that belongs to a race my race is enemies with unless I am planning to assult them. Since my current character is a dwarf, I try to stay away from ogers and trolls and do not talk with them wherever I am. I know it seems sort of rude but I want to try and play my character truthfully.
3.) My character,Dalamur,is a drawf that is a descendant of Glight Snowchipper,one of the original group of dwarves who settled in Velious. Glight was a hero to the people and saved them from the frost giants. He was killed protecting the other dwarves. Dandurr's mother,Haldaafa Frostreaver,relative of future king Dain Frostreaver IV,and father,Colin Snowchipper,were killed when Dalamur was a baby by a group of frost giants who raided their home city of Thurgadin and killed many dwraves before they escaped. So Colin's friend Hogunk Ventille took Dalamur to Kaladim on Faydwer and raised him as a son. Now Dalamur is preparing to venture to Thurgadin and avenge his parents death.
