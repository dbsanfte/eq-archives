You can get a great education in the USA if you remember one important fact: State Certification is not equivalent to Education. Just because George W. Bush has a piece of paper from Yale that says he is edumacated does not mean that he is either smarter or more informed than the average high school dropout.

I used to run into this a lot when I was in college: An example problem in a math course leads to a possibly fruitful avenue of exploration that would connect two or more of the basic principles we've learned this semester into a coherent whole. We have a real chance to learn some of the fundamentals we need to proceed to higher studies by further exploring the example and applying a few more solutions to it. But, predictably, when one of the three or four actual students in the class poses a question leading to this avenue of further exploration of the subject's fundamentals, an audible groan is heard from those "students" who are not there to LEARN, but are merely there to obtain credit for the course so they can get a piece of PAPER that SAYS they know the subject. They don't have any interest in the subject itself, just in the rewards for owning a piece of State-approved paper that CLAIMS knowledge of the subject. These "pseudents" will Cliff's Notes and crib-sheet their way to their C- grades and do their best to forget what little they did learn so that they can bear that sheepskin that says they know the subject they so assiduously avoided learning.

And that is the crux of the difference between the concept of State Certification and the concept of Education. Our colleges are currently churning out lots of people who couldn't have passed a series of high school final exams from 1940, and is equipping them with State-approved documents that claim that they are Baccelaureates, Masters, and Doctors of Philosophy. But the two main "philosophies" being taught in our colleges currently are neo-dadaism and cultural marxism, both of which are antithetical to education.
