An air of calmness returns to the inn as the patrons gather around the new come Feir`Dal. All eyes off him, Lladerek silently moves to Olath Velve, still embedded in the scarred oak paneling. He pulls the blade from the floor sending a few small wood chips to scatter across the ground. The soft ring of steel echoes as the ebon sword is sheathed once more, clicking into place. Lladerek whispers to himself so quietly that none can hear more then the hushed sound of his breath. “Fools… they will never understand. They are not worth the effort.” He brushes a small, ring-sized bulge in his dark adamantite mail with his slight indigo fingers. His words form steam in the cold night air… intruding upon the common room through the ceiling’s rent. “Lend me your strength.”

Lladerek’s black cloak displaces the dust coating the floor as he unfastens his brooch. All eyes turn to him as he tosses the sable cloak into the air. With an arcane chant a roaring blaze jumps from his fingertips to the cloth, igniting it. The tongues of flame light up the room with an orange glow as they continue forward rekindling the dying embers of the hearth. The now roaring fire gleams in Lladerek’s eyes, an unearthly battle between sapphire and gold.

His cloak gone, Lladerek stands before the inn’s patrons, fully observable for the first time. His adamantite armor does not gleam in the fire’s stare. But for its slight gradient in shading, one might think it devoured all light that struck it. He wears chain mail except for his chest, arms, and legs, which are garbed in delicately crafted plate. His chest piece is bejeweled with scenes of great Teir`Dal victories and in its center the likeness of Innoruuk stretches metal arms over the brutal battlefields. His other plate pieces are of similar make, scrolling images all drawing attention to the central figure of The Prince of Hate. Upon his shoulders two adamantite Teir`Dal faces stare skyward, mouths open in tormented scream. The battle scenes continue on his back, centered by the crossed hands of house X’Lottl. More Teir`Dal encircle his collar, famous heroes of his house. On his forearms the flag of Neriak is surrounded by beasts of Teir`Dal mythology and less notable lineage. His shin pieces show two Teir`Dal figures reaching up to his knees, their hands forming spikes protruding from the pieces. They are surrounded by more figures and still more scenes of Teir`Dal violence.

Lladerek’s left hand comes to rest on the sapphire pommel of Olath Velve, a single finger tapping against the blade’s leather hilt. “You talk of tales of adventure and of shared drinks, while that” he points a finger at the vampire’s bloodied carcass still sprawled out across the inn floor “fouls the very air we breath.” He walks deliberately to the corpse, placing a dark hand on the beast’s pale, grotesque chest. Muttering a slow incantation he traces Teir`Dal runes and geometric shapes upon the vampire’s body… they glow briefly before fading. As he takes a step back and raises his arms calling forth the spirits of the damned, wisps of shadow reach out from hidden niches of the cadaver. They wrap around the body, digging into cold flesh and begin to encase the vampire in a blanket of darkness. Lladerek turns his back to the condemned corpse as it turns to dust and then, dancing on the breeze, turns to nothingness.

“Seeing as how you cowards have apparently chosen to yield, instead of defending your beliefs like elves, I too shall make an attempt at peace.” Lladerek walks to the empty corner table to retrieve his bottle of wine. As he turns the bottle up to take a swill of the sweet elven wine, firelight pierces the emerald bottle casting a verdant glow across his indigo features. He walks over to the more crowded end of the inn taking a seat at a small round table. Tilting the chair back he crosses his legs and rests them on the wine stained tabletop. He takes one more swallow of wine before resting the bottle on the oaken table.

Trying to hide his ego, but doing a poor job of it Lladerek looks Dunidain in the eye and speaks up. “You want me to tell a tale do you… in so hospitable an environment how could I refuse.” He looks down to a scene on his armor, right below Innoruuk. “I know many tales… that is The Battle of Bloody Kithicor and that,” he motions to another image, “is The Befallen City.” He touches another scene on his right breastplate. “That is The Hunt for the Crimson Teir and that is The Ninth Battle of Freeport.” He touches two figures on his collar. “The First of Innoruuk’s Children… you Feir`Dal might like that one” he says cynically. He looks to a particular monster on his left forearm. “The Black Dragon of Neriak and that one is The Pact of the Lying Gods” he nods to four figures on his right shoulderplate. “So which do you want to hear now… later I may not be feeling so sociable.”

Lladerek sits back in his chair silently watching as the patrons talk amongst themselves. He spins his sword idly… its tip rests upon a knot on the wooden floor panel nearest his foot. The blade whirls rhythmically, a turn and a half to the right, then a turn and a half to the left, and then to the right again, the sound of splintering wood echoing repeatedly. The shadow from his left leg ascends most of the sleek ebon blade. Where it ends the glare of the firelight turns the mirror-like steel the hue of autumn leaves. As Olath Velve spins the fiery reflection dances up and down the razor edge.

Lladerek lowers his head and ivory hair falls down his smooth indigo face, casting slight shadows across his elven features. He raises a hand to brush the fallen locks away. His sad blue eyes look to those around him, twin viewing glasses into a soul that has seen too much of the world for it to remain any type of adventure.

Lladerek speaks up in a choked voice that clears as he continues to talk. “I remember Kithicor, I remember it too well. I’m sure none of you can understand… you all know of what happened at Kithicor I’m sure, but I remember what happened. It was a cold morning, January I think, you could see the steam on your breath. We broke camp at sunset and were marching from the ransacked city of Rivervale before the night was even at its zenith. Throughout the march the armies of the lying gods picked away at our flank but we marched on ignoring the death cries of a few of our brothers so that the Teir`Dal nation could be victorious.”

“I was in command of the elite guard for our blessed savior Lanys T`Vyl, and my lover Shaleor served as my subordinate. We were so sure we would win, The Ebon Army was nearly twice the size of our enemy’s standing troops. We neared High Pass, yet blocking our way was the main force of our enemy, led by Firiona Vie.”

“Fighting along side my love to defend Lanys I bore witness to all that unfolded. The Ebon Army had the upper hand, our enemies fought with the desperation of a wounded animal. There is not a force on Norrath that can stand head to toe with the whole of Hate’s Children and the battle of Kithicor would have been ours along with High Pass had not things turned for the worse at the heart of the battle.”

“However heated the battle was as a whole, two combats burned a thousand times hotter. One between two seasoned warriors who had seen much war and who were the pinnacles of might in Teir`Dal and Koada`Dal form, Laarthik K'Shin and Galeth Veredeth. They fought like twofold serpents in the elegant dance of death that can only be seen through watching true warriors. The second battle, if less beautiful carried tenfold the weight of the first. Our blessed savior Lanys T`Vyl was locked in mortal combat with the chosen of Tunare, Firiona Vie. Like a cowardly child Firiona ran and hid from Lanys, striking from the shadow only to make yet another hastened retreat. Lanys cornered Firiona and the two squared off in single combat. From the onset it was clear who was the mightier, every strike let loose by The Child of Hate drove Firiona back two paces and the Koada`Dal never once gained time enough to swing her blade.”

“Then on the eave of our victory Tunare, The False Mother cast her meddling eyes down upon us. Swarms of insects descended from the sky and clouded the battlefield. Through the biting mass I could see the combat of our blessed Lanys only slightly. Firiona had grown in stature, a golden aura pulsed around her, and her ochroid hair danced with a wind that blew only for her. Brambles grew about Lanys’s feet, enveloping her, impeding her. The wretched interference of Tunare turned the tide of the Battle. It was her intervention that brought about what happened in Kithicor all those years ago, Lanys had to be saved.”

“Laarthik, having felled Galeth, turned to see the combat of his pupil and only then came to realize what had transpired. In the urgency of the moment he fell to his knees holding up an azure gem the size of a man’s fist. At the time I had not yet learned of the dark ways, but even I could feel the evil of that stone pulling at my soul, and I embraced it. That may be what saved me… what happened next I saw through the eyes of another, like some warped dream, and that other was myself.”

“Laarthik spoke in a strangled tone that would have normally produce no more then a whisper, but what came forth from the stone was a booming cry “Innoruuk! In the name of Lanys T`Vyl, I beseech you! Attend her!” His words are burned into the back of my mind and will echo there for an eternity. Time and space burst open, tearing asunder the bonds that had secured Innoruuk in His Decrepit Plain for centuries. A pillar of flame cascaded up into the heavens and carved deeply into the earth, incinerating Laarthik where he knelt. Pure hate poured over the battlefield from the depths of the Plane of Hate, hatred the likes of which Norrath had never seen. The mightiest dragon half way around the world would still have felt fear and cowered before The Power of The Prince of Hate. The screams of thousands who died instantly when the astral barrier shattered reverberated against my soul. Those damned souls writhed in agony as they were drawn into the spiraling vortex.”

“Tendrils of pure energy, of pure hatred reached down to cradle Lanys T`Vyl and lift her limp form into the astral gateway. The portal collapsed with a thunderous flash and the only sounds that remained were the death moans of both armies’ fallen, neither side was the victor. From that day until today nothing living has called Kithicor home. It is one of only two places on Norrath’s surface where The Father has touched our world and made it whole… it is only a shame that so many needed to die for the change to occur, including my love Shaleor.”

Lladerek grips the circle shaped bulge in his chain mail, fondling it nostalgically, and closes his azure eyes. His other hand still spins Olath Velve… back and forth. “I’ve seen so much death” he whispers to himself so that no one can hear. As Olath Velve spins the fiery reflection from the hearth dances up and down the razor edge.
