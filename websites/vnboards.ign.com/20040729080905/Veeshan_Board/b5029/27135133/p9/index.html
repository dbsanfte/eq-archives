In my opinion, the most important statistic to prove the marijuana leads to crime arguement would be what percentage of marijuana users commit crimes. There is no way to obtain this statistic b/c marijuana is illegal, and you have no idea who is doing it and who is not. You have no idea what percentage of the population uses marijuana and how often. It might very well be the case that a smaller percentage of marijuana users commit crimes then those who do not use marijuana. But there is NO WAY to find out.

I'm a bartender and a few weeks ago I had a couple in their 50's ask me where they could get some X. Would this couple admit they use drugs recreationally to a statistician? Maybe. A statistician that works for the government? Doubtful. The point is you don't know, nor will you ever have an idea unless you legalize it. Even then your statistics aren't going to paint a real portrait, only a vague image. But as it stands that image is extremely distorted. Those that rely simply on this image are naive and fooling themselves.

Again, what is important to me personally on the marijuana issue is whether or not the outlawing of it infringes on my rights and my freedom. I believe it does.

Marijuana has never made me want to commit a crime. The only crimes I commit are smoking pot and doing other drugs recreationally, and minor traffic violations which I get caught for about once a year. I have never harmed or will harm anyone intentionally, and if I did, pot would have nothing to do with it.

Most of the debate in this thread has to do with statistics and sociology. I mistrust the former and dislike the latter. I prefer to use MY OWN BRAIN to tell me whether something is good or bad. You can use all the pages of all the studies done on the subject, but I will still rely on my own experiences that I have had in my own life. It is that simple.

My experiences tell me:

weed > alcohol
weed > cigarettes
weed > other illegal drugs
weed > most other legal drugs
weed < sex

Based on my experiences, I can't figure out for the life of me why weed is illegal and other more harmful substances (such as addictive sleepaids and "herbal" speed pills) are legal. I can go to a liquor store and buy a shit-ton of alcohol which will inhibit my ability to walk, much less make intelligible decisions, but I can't buy weed, which calms me down when I am stressed, enhances my entertainment, and has a much less adverse effect on me then alcohol ever will. I never smoke so much weed that I black out and can't remember wtf it was that I did the night before (as I see so many alcoholics do on a regular basis).
