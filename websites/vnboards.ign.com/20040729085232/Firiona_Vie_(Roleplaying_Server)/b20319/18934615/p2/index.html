<"Twinking" clearly distorts the economies on the servers. Actually... no it doesn't. I'll explain what "clearly" happens....

What causes the "distortion" you are talking about, where people are auctioning many high end items, etc, is the server AGE. Not twinking.


Over time, as people level up and adventure in the dungeons and other places, more loot is introduced into the gaming world. As more loot is introduced, and the players get higher in level, eventually the *quality* of the available loot on the server goes up.

This is simple, and clear.

In the beginning of a server's life people are only able to loot, or craft, the simple items. They lack the levels to explore the higher end dungeons where the good loot drops, and they lack the skills to make the good crafted items (like Fine Steel Plate)

As the players gain levels and skills, this changes. More items from dungeons begin to make their way into the economy, the overall quality of the loot goes up.


This has absolutely NOTHING to do with twinking at this point in the server life!

Please forgive my forcefulness here, but this is so simple it really burns me when people can't see it.


Twinking is the act of putting items on a character that that character could not craft, or loot, because of their level. Putting a Darkforge BP on a shadowknight of level 1 is twinking because that character could never acquire that item by themselves, or with a group of characters his/her same level.


Now, that's what twinking *is*, but if you pay attention that is *not* what "distorts" the economy. It's the fact that that item is available because someone looted it. And that item is only available to put on a twink for one of two reasons: Because the market, over time, becomes saturated with the item, or because someone "farmed" the item in order to perform the twink.



Server age is the sole reason the economy changes and the items that people auction become greater in value. Even on this server. In a year, you pay attention to the auctions: the average value of the item being auctioned will rise. Patchwork will no longer be auctioned. Instead, higher level people will begin auctioning Blackened Iron and Bronze armor with more frequency. As they raid those dungeons, they will pick up more and more of that armor. As more of that armor makes its way into the economy, the price will drop. Eventually it will be so low that a new player on the server will be better off saving their money for bronze than purchasing banded. A new person will get patchwork for free as people begin to give it away. This is a product of server AGE, not twinking.

Twinking affects the server economy in only one way: It causes lower level people to desire gear their character cannot get. And as the server gets older, and more of that gear begins to saturate the market, those new players will be able to get that gear for very low prices.

The people who play on that server from the beginning will be the ones who pay the price. They will pay 600pp for a Dark Reaver at 10 levels before they can actually adventure in Guk, and then a year later they will only be able to sell it for 200pp. Those people, the first people on the server, will lose the most money because of the market saturation of goods.



Twinking has only one effect: it causes envy.


Now, if you want to argue about Farming affecting the economy, go ahead. Farming is the prequil to Twinking. Farming is how many items make their way into the economy, because someone farms to get an item of worth, and then auction it, so they can get money and buy themselves something they need, or buy something for their twink.

I would guess that upwards of 75% of farming is done to twink (as an end result), and the other 25% of farming is done to make money, or trade, for items that a primary character needs.

On this server, with the TLC, farming shouldn't be an issue as much, but we'll have to wait and see.


Anyway, sorry if my post seems harsh, inflammatory, or condecending. It's only meant to educate. The economy's biggest threat on any server is it's age. Over time, more items will become available, and that will change the market.



-Skelex

I dislike twinks. I don't think I am a "crusader" against them, but I REALLY don't like the practice, for several reasons:

1. Simple jealousy. I'll admit it - I like to think that I am capable of playing my character as well as anyone else out there, and it can be galling to see someone use a right-clickable effect on their boots, ask them where they got the item, and hear "oh, it's from uber_mob_045, my 60th level necro actually won it on a roll.

2. Play balance. It may be true that very few people are going to actually discuss whether or not to take the tank in the XX breastplate instead of the tank with YY breastplate. However, with melee twinking, it is VERY possible for a tank in gear twenty levels better than he ought to have to do the job of three other players.

In such a case, the group will not really feel it is necessary to even entertain adding more members to the group. Why should they? The tank is never getting damaged, the hurt he is dishing out with the Sword of Uberdom combined with the Belt of Unstoppable Haste is amazing, so just let those other guys LFG try to find some other group.

3. The economy. We'll just have to see what happens, but given the choice between someone farming an item in order to sell it on the open market in the bazaar and farming an item in order to give it to his twink, I'd take the former any day - at least I have the opportunity to bid.

This might mean that there is a rarity of items. It might mean that there is eventually a flood of items. The lessened occasion of twinking WILL have an effect on the economy, for good or ill, we can't say yet. I say "bring it"

4. Finally, and this is close to having very little to do with anything else, the lack of twinking gives players a better idea what sort of gear their character should have at a given level. Sure, that will get confused again once the server ages and the upper gear comes flowing down, but it's nice to feel that having your Ro quest armor at 35 is not a bad thing.
