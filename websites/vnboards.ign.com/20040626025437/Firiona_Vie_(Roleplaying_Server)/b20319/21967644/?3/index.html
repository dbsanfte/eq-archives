--Rules--

Dark Elf Roulette: Interested players will first pay (gamble) a 5pp fee (or more, if they feel lucky ). They will then gather in a circle in the Freeport Arena. Each will then roll a die matching the total number of players; the person with the highest roll... loses! All players will gang up on the unfortunate high-roller and beat them to a pulp. In the event of a tie, the tied players may team up to help one another. This process will continue, until only one player is left--the winner! The winner will then walk away with the total of the entry fees.


Craps:

Playing: All the players gather up in a circle. One player begins the game with the dice, "the shooter". The shooter gets to keep playing (and betting) as long as he doesn't lose. Once he loses, he passes the dice to the person on his left.

The first time a shooter rolls is the "come out" roll. He will roll
/ran 1 6
/ran 1 6
and add up the total. On a 7 or 11, he automatically wins. On a 2, 3, or 12, he automatically loses (also known as "rolling craps"). If he gets a 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10, he has established the "point", and will keep rolling the dice until he rolls the "point" a second time, at which point he wins. The catch is, if while trying to roll the point that second time, and the shooter rolls a 7 (the most common combination to roll with two 6-sided dice), he loses (he "sevens out").

Betting: Before rolling, the shooter may bet as much as he wants that he will win. The rest of the players cover the bet starting with the player to the shooter's right, each covering as much of the bet as they want. If the shooter wins, he doubles his money. If the shooter loses, each of the other players doubles the amount they covered. For example:

Gray is the shooter. He bets 12pp. Genashai, Rhael, and Kazanti are the other players, in order from Gray's right. Genashai covers with 6pp, Rhael 2pp, and Kazanti has to cover the remaining 4pp (12pp total). Gray rolls a 7 on the come out roll and wins! He collects 6, 2, and 4pp from the other players, and so has doubled his money. Now he feels bold. He bets all of his 24pp. Genashai next covers with 8pp, and Rhael decides to cover with 16pp--Kazanti is left out this time, as the bet is covered. Gray rolls.... and gets a 2. Snake eyes! Gray pays Genashai 8pp, and Rhael 16pp, before passing the dice to Kazanti. Net result: Gray is 12pp in the hole, Genashai is up 2pp, Rhael is up 14pp, and Kazanti is down 4pp.
