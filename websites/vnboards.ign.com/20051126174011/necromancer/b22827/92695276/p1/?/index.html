Right now, I have Shadow of Fear, Enfeebling Blood, Parasitic Bond and Mark of Pain on my hot bar, along with Death minion skills. Seems to me that AoE huge reduction in enemy damage, cheap degenerate/heal and great AoE damage dealer is really nice, so I'm curious why I haven't seen more Curse builds. Also, later, later on, Feast of Corruption would stack quite nicely.

Do teams find Curses desirable in later missions when they become difficult?

Curses are fantastic, but like Mesmer hexes they aren't obvious so people don't notice. They also tend to be highly situational, except for Shadow of Fear and Parasitic Bond which are useful everywhere.

I can comment on the ones I've tried, mostly for PvE. I finished the game with a necro primary, mainly specced for blood/curses/domination. I haven't found many good curses for PvP but that's because I have mostly done pickup groups. Certain curses work best when coordinated with other chars' skills (like spinal shivers).

Shadow of Fear is my favorite all-round curse, I almost always bring it for PvE and PvP. It cuts enemy attacks in half for 30 seconds or so, and you can usually get a couple targets with it, sometimes 4 or more depending on how tightly they group. Faintheardedness is the non-AoE version which adds a small health degen, but I prefer the AoE version.

Enfeeble and Enfeebling Blood (the AoE version) are great for Underworld as they badly nerf the Aatxe attacks. This can be a real party saver since Aatxe can kill monks (and you) in 2 hits, and the shadow mobs (forget the name) go through armor so they can even carve up warriors. You can save many party members' lives with these curses and they will never realize it, instead they thank the monk. :P

Plague Sending is excellent in certain situations. If you get bleeding or blinded you can transfer it to a group of enemies. If an ele sets you on fire you can light up his whole team (you have to be quick, and yeah it rarely works and not for long but it's fun anyway).

For soloing, I use Dark Aura then combo Barbed Signet and Plague Sending, for AoE shadow damage (twice) and AoE bleeding. I find plague touch more useful than plague sending though, since it's cheap and instant (but no AoE).

I also have a warrior/necro, and Parasitic Bond is my standby for him. It does a good job keeping him alive. I cast it first and get a health boost after 30 seconds or so, and I cast it on a dying enemy to get an instant health boost.

Mark of Pain is amazing in certain situations. For mobs that group in real close (minotaurs, trolls, scarabs) my warrior/necro can outdamage an elementalist. Since it's shadow damage, it ignores armor. Often my axe isn't doing diddly to my target mob but MoP is slaying everything else, so the only thing left after 15 seconds is my target. I hit a mob with mark of pain then use flurry and cyclone axe to maximize the AoE damage.

Of course MoP is useless against single mobs or mobs that spread out. I have used it in PvP and it can work really well, sometimes -- I cast it on a monk and start hitting him, then when a warrior comes to help the monk he starts getting slaughtered and he doesn't know why...

Rend Enchantments is a must for PvP, to shut down the monks who are runed and enchanted to the eyeballs and impossible to kill. At least one person on your team needs to have RE. Lingering Curse looks even better for this purpose but I haven't tried it (and it's elite).

The other curses I haven't tried yet but some look really good, for certain situations. There is a killer build for PvP using several necros with parastic bond and Feast of Corruption but I haven't tried it yet. You can find it on the web somewhere.

Curses are basically support spells. I suppose it's possible to build around curses only, if your team realizes that's what you are doing. However they will probably think you are standing around doing nothing. With blood nukes / wells or minions the effects are more obvious.

A necro/mes doing nothing but hexing and cursing could be a real asset, but no one would know.

A couple other things... Mark of Pain is only invoked by PHYSICAL damage. All staves and wands do elemental damage, so MoP does NOTHING for casters. And if the party tank uses a fiery sword or the ranger uses fire arrows, MoP does NOTHING for them. I screwed myself by putting an icy wrap on my best axe then discovered my favorite solo curse was now useless.

This makes MoP very hard to use in parties. You have to tell your tanks and rangers to only use physical damage weapons and only hit your target... and you can imagine how well they listen. Plus you have to tell the elementalists not to nuke your target because if it dies too soon MoP will be wasted.

With my necro/mes I tried MoP a few times in parties and gave up. So now I only use MoP with my warrior/necro.

If you are a necro primary, you probably won't bother with parasitic bond. The health degen is too small to matter much -- it's the health boost you get that is really the point of it, and if you are specced in blood you would use life siphon instead. Even a few points in blood with life siphon would be better than parasitic bond IMO. However, if you have a max death/curses necro, then maybe parasitic bond would be better (for PvP anyway, for PvE necrotic traversal and touch of death seem better choices to me).
