The day dawned warm, and promised to be beautiful. Swiftly moving clouds passed overhead, and in the distance, birds were heard singing. Lenore wandered the Great Library, browsing the many tomes and periodicals housed there. She passed a table where a Erudite and Teir'Dal verbally sparred quietly over the mechanisims on the whatchamagizmos, while the sleeping drake of the Erudite snuggled against the flaming energy of the Teir'Dal.
Continuing her perusals, a hand rested lightly on her shoulder. She turned and smiled as the face of her beloved stood behind her.
"Come, love. I have found the way to the place of our Father Karana's most hated foe."
Smiling, the couple left. Lenore noticed a new spring in her husband's steps. He was eager to vanquish the evil of Bertoxxulous, and stop it before it reached the lands of Norrath.

A brief trip through the domain of Quellious, the alter Plane of Tranquility, they found a guarded portal marking the passage to the Plane of Disease. Lenore looked at the foul smelling water bubbling from what looked like a giant spout, and wrinkled her nose in disgust. Chunks of unknown origin floated in the pool before draining into a large sewer. Closing her eyes, and holding her breath, Choco and Lenore clasped hands and jumped into the foul water.

The plane was horrid. It reeked of decay and disease. The sky was red, with a ever-present black cloud of Karana knows what, and was very grateful she still wore her Amulet, a gift from the people of Katta Castellum, to aid her in not breathing. While one could breathe in this plane, she was certain she didn't want to!
The ground was moist, and soft, almost fleshy. Large mounds of rocks jutted out of the ground, and oozed a green substance, almost like a sore or a boil upon the skin.
Various creatures moved about, and reminded Lenore of the carrion feeders on dead bodies. Flies, larvae, and spiders swarmed in abundance. A green pool shimmered and bubbled, as a stench of filth wafted out of its depths.
And when it rained!
Lenore was certain she would be washing for weeks! The rain was thick, and smelled of sickness. It was a light green hue, reminding her of a festering sore.
She began to wonder if this place wasn't the actual body of Bertoxxulous himself they stood upon. And an odd mountain range heightened her suspicions. It reminded her of a spine. An ever present moaning echoed across the surface, a moan of pain and dying.

After spending a few days in this place, Lenore and Choco decided to part. Knowing their battle against Bertoxxulous in his domain was going to be a long campaign, but a worthy one, made it worthwhile to return again another day.
That night, Lenore scrubbed and washed in a cold stream til she was sure she had scraped her skin to the bone.

The pile of metal lay on the ground, squeeking and creaking as its metal joints wobbled back and forth. The metal being had come from no where, and was very aggressive against them. Lenore noted that this place was a gnome's dream come true. She knew the gnomes loved to tinker, and make things from spare parts. The city of Ak'Anon was a mechanical wonder. All the bolts and gears strewn around would make a gnome tinkerer die happy.
Lenore felt more at ease in this plane, as the smell of grease was preferable over the stench of death. When it rained here, it had an oily feel, like, well, oil. Like the oil the gnomes used to keep their machines from rusting. Wasn't pleasant, but at least would be more easy to wash off later.


The sky was black, and dark pink clouds moved swiftly against the inky sky. The sounds of tortured souls, as if in the grip of a nightmare, echoed off the hills. The denizens of this plane, the home of Terris Thule, the Goddes of Nightmares, were indeed, the stuff of nightmares. Spiders lived here too, and seemed to be in abundance wherever she went, be it in the Plane of Disease, or the flatlands of Freeport. The treants, however, disturbed her. While it pained her to cause harm to such a majestic creature, it gave her some relief knowing she was ending their tortured existence in this realm of horror.

As her and Choco gladly left this place, she wondered where they would venture to next, and anticipated the journey.
