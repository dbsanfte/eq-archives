This is copied from another board, and I have brought it here as I think it is well thought out. Any comments?

Re: What would YOU want in an RP guild?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Okay you asked...

Use of nobility titles in the leadership structure is an example of something I wouldn't necessarily want to see. Much would depend on the backstory of the guild itself, however in a roleplaying guild, I imagine a structure that is set up in such a way that the leadership of the guild is primarily there for benefit of the guild as a whole.

Here are some possible examples:

(hmm.. as an aside, your original note didn't indicate whether or not you meant good or evil aligned guild...)

- The Council of Elders

This is a simple setup in which a static number of "elders" maintain the guidance of the guild and act as embassadors to the leadership of other guilds. The elders themselves are either selected by the general populace, or win their positions by performing some act that earns them the right. (If the "lead by performing an act" idea is taken however, care would have to be employed so that the action does not promote powergaming.) The number of elders could possibly be based upon representation by race or class, so you'd have one elder for each race, or one elder for each class. (Or just pick some specific number.) This structure would more than likely best serve only one alignment, so all members would have to be good, or evil, but not likely both.

- Guardians of Light and Dark

This structure exists so that a single guild could conceivably support more than one alignment. In this example, a polarized organization exists, with leadership split evenly between good and evil. Each arm would separately determine how their organizational structure is obtained, but for fairness sake, the number of leadership positions between each arm should be equal. An interesting twist would be to add a third body to this guild; Guardians of Law. (Order? Shadow? Something neutral.) This "arm" serves to seek balance and maintains a neutral stance in all things. This way, if disputes were to arise between Light and Dark, the neutral body could act as a deciding factor.

I can imagine an interesting roleplayed guild meeting:

<Scene: Small group of guild members at a planning meeting for future events.>

Praetor (good): "I see that my counterpart has brought his dogs with him? I thought this was to be a meeting of the Triune only."

Justicar (neutral): "Peace, Praetor. Obdurator Fallon has brought these members to relate their side of the dispute."

Obdurator (evil): [sneering] "Indeed, Praetor. But if you are shaking in your boots and soiling your armor over it, I will send them away."

Praetor: "Let's get it over with then before I become annoyed enough to fry your brain. What is their account?"

<and so forth>

- College of Lore

In this example, we have a setup revolving around a learning institution. Guild leaders take the role of Mentors and Teachers, sponsoring other guild members as students. This also presents an interesting idea for membership in that a petitioning applicant would have to be sponsored by an existing guild members for a specified period of time prior to an invitation to join. Such invitations would be voted upon by existing guild membership so that everyone has some sort of input into who is added to the ranks. (That does sound a bit elitist, but if you're attempting to keep the whiners/powergamers out of the guild, then this procedure may be important.)

Hmm that's all I have at the moment. I'll see if I can think of other ideas.
