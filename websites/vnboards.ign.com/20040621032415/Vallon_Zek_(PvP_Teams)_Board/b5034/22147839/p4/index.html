If there is two things I can say about the original Prophets (as a guild) it's that I both had a healthy respect for them and a dislike for how they operated.

While I agree that Prophets gave up Sebilis voluntarily, I also believe Defiant played a role in forming that decision. This is a zone that remains one of the best wholesale exp and money-raising spots. I seriously doubt any guild would voluntarily give up all rights to exp in such a zone unless their hand was forced.

Before I reached 57ish or so, I hated getting involved in group PvP with Prophets because it usually meant I would be CRing for an hour or two. Right around the time we made it a goal to fight for Sebilis, Defiant was nearly caught up in levels to most of the Prophets and we began to fare much better in PvP (from my perspective). There were quite a few people looking forward to finally being able to compete and score some victories. Personally, I felt kind of robbed and it strikes me nowadays that the 'we left because of Velious and people quitting' excuse, while it sounds credible, is a bit too convenient for my sense of how things were proceeding at the time.

I will agree that there was a goodly number of skilled PvE/PvP people in Prophets. That was one of the things I respected about the guild. Nanak, Kaltare, Morcrist, Deamondred and a select few others I'd never seen or heard anything bad about them. It was mainly during the large-scale battles that problems arose.

All that having been said, I strongly believe to this day that Defiant would have eventually broken the grip of power Prophets had on the server had they not broken up themselves. Most of the Sebilis wars I took part in involved me ressing dead Prophets afterwards..I never had the sense that it was hopeless to compete. But I guess we'll never know, will we? It's a dead-end argument.

Comments from egotists like Blizzy to the extent of 'well we had 24 ST keys before Defiant even had 1' and 'rest of the server will always stay skilless gimps' fail to impress me much. Instead it sounds like the rantings of a bitter, close-minded individual clinging to his glory days.

Here's yet another misconception you seem to have, Blizzy: Defiant is lead by a group of people, heavily influenced by the leader of the guild, Cuchulainn. We make all the decisions. None of us actively search the web for exact strategies to killing a particular mob. Most of the information we had about a new 'uber mob' came from reading forums and hearsay and we generally refined our strategies based on how our first try went. I can tell you firsthand that we did not use spoilers to accomplish the things we did. I know you'd like to think we just copied everything you did, but the FACT is we didn't. Sorry if the world doesn't revolve around you bro.

Defiant did generally progress at a slower pace than the original Prophets did, but not for the reasons the egotists in that later guild seem to think (what else could it be other than you're just so uber, right Blizzy?)). We've always had a more relaxed PvE attitude, more individual members to consider (takes longer to equip/level up more people, especially if they aren't all powergamers) and different goals. When Prophets were getting ST keys, Defiant had other goals. So it's not surprising that they got them first.

I sincerely wish Prophets had survived to compete with us today, but they didn't. Some people need to realize that arguments people present comparing a dead guild to a current guild are only opinions, NOT fact.

Nice post, Morcrist..it made for some good reading. I came along late in the Defiant/Prophet war and it's always nice to get a little back history.

-Xerxus
