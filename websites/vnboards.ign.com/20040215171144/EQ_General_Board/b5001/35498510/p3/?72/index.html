Some comments in addition to the good info already posted.

Bard - kind of a jack of all trades since they can sing just about any spell effect in the game. This can be good and bad. A well played bard is a phenominal group member - but due to the fact that bards have so many options it's easy to be a poor bard. Bards need to learn how to maximize the use of their wide variety of songs. Bards can be excellent pullers with lament.

Cleric - Probably the most sought after class in the game for groups for their heals (and usually buffs too). Clerics can be vital to group success with their lull/sooth/calm/pac line too, not just healing. I've grouped with some clerics that can pull better than many tanks I've grouped with.

Druid - Very versatile, often found soloing, and often not the best in groups due to the fact that they solo too much! A well played druid is an asset in any situation though.

Enchanter - Crowd control specialist, mana regen buffs, many other handy buffs, fun illusions spells, decent slower - an all around fun class to play and highly sought after for almost all group situations.

Magician - Very powerful class that can summon all kinds of fun and usually useful stuff, and has a very powerful pet. Able to solo decently and a good asset for a group.

Monk - I'm not happy with the evolution of monks - I would've prefered that they never started wielding weapons! But, I guess it gives em two more slots to get +stats from. :-/ Classically monks were the ultimate pulling class and in many cases still are. In my experience FD pulling is often slow compared to using pacify or mezzing adds. Monks have lots of cool looking special attacks over time and are a great damage dealing class - a good compliment to a tank that can hold aggro and take damage.

Necromancer - Versatile class with a wide variety of fun spells and a decent pet. A strong solo class. Exceptionally strong vs undead but effective vs any critters. (see closing notes on strong solo clases!)

Paladin - A holy warrior that can wear plate, often wields two handed swords/axes and/or nice paladin only weapons, gets some decent cleric-oriented spells, and is generally highly sought after for groups, particularly LDoN. Paladins get the pacify line of spells which allows you to pull singles all day long and IMO are often the best pullers in the game (pac, plate armor, easily hold aggro). If you can consistently pull singles and keep aggro on one tank your group will mow critters down.

Ranger - Can become decent archers, usually later in levels. Tracking is sweet. High damage output class that compliments stronger damage taking tanks very well. Nice assortment of druid-oriented spells at 9+. Some people consider rangers the best farmers in the game because they have the best tracking - and often farming ability is determined by ability to detect and then locate specific critters.

Rogue - Super damage dealers but rely on being behind a critter for maximum damage output. Not the best at taking hits. A very nice compliment to someone who can maintain aggro (to keep the critter's back to the rogue). With LDoN rogues are sought after to open chest and more importantly, for high DPS.

Shadow Knight - Somewhat of an anti-paladin. Gets necro-like spells starting at 9th. A nice medium tank that can hold aggro well. Often wields two handed swords/axes and/or nice SK only weapons. Gets a special ability called harm touch that can do a big chunk of damage (sort of opposite of lay on hands of paladins). Like necros, they are considered an 'evil' class and occasionally have faction issues.

Shaman - Shaman are also the "kings of slowers" - although other classes can slow and at the highest levels some have nearly as strong as slows. Shaman can solo with their pet. Shaman are generally highly desired for groups as they are group enablers - their buffs, slows, and other abilities enhance group effectiveness and efficiency.

Warrior - straight forward slug it out class that is best with groups - because they have no significant way to heal themselves and sitting and regenning health take forever. Warriors get the cool melee skills like dual wield, double attack, riposte, etc earlier than any other class.

Wizard - A high damage output class that is easily squashed (like most casters).

Beastlord - A very powerful class that's good solo or in groups. They get many shaman spells including slow over time...and also get a series of mana/hp regen spells that are very nice. They have nice pet buffs and heals for their pets. They can tank a bit with their pet but wear light armor. The add serious firepower to any group.

In closing a comment on solo classes. My feeling is that any class that can solo well should be a good asset to a group. If they are strong enough to kill critters solo, they should add to any group, yes? Druids are sort of the exception here, as druid typically solo by killing their prey over time - rather than killing things fast like groups want to do. Necros can use simlar tactics but do have more frontloaded damage options. Beastlord pets are ridiculously powerful and BLs have some instant offense besides their pets.

ok in answer to your two questions, to go into third person view press 'F9' this will cycle you through various camera angles. Find one that suits you and have fun.

In regards to handing the note in. 1st make sure you have the right npc targeted to hand the note too. If it is the wrong one you will at best get the note back, occasionally the will say thanks and you and keep the item. to pick up the item left click on it it will appear on your cursor and be removed from the slot. click and holding the button down will pick up the whole slot,(for use for hot keys) now click on the intended NPC. the item will dissapear from your mouse button and appear in a trade box on the left side of the screen. upto 4 items/stacks can be given to a npc. click on the give button when you are ready to hand over the item.

have fun! thats an order

Ringo,

Welcome to the maze that is EQ vault As to your question, here goes. EQ is quite an old game so a lot of the low level players will be alternate characters of long time players. I have noticed a number of new players still appearing on servers and asking questions The Stromm server is the newest server and should have the largest population of lower level characters.
Twinking is the action of giving items to a character that can not possibly have recieved the items themselves. While you will not be able to twink at first there are good starting quest for armor, weapons and spells for most races/classes. There are now many good items that are purchaseable in the bazaar (player vendors) for relatively low cost.
EQ Evolutions appears to be the best buy out there at the moment. I have a lesser spec'd PC than you and have very few problems.
Froglocks are ok nothing really makes me want to play one other than the way they look, some ppl will dissagree with me I am sure, they can swim better to start and can see in the infra range. Froglocks are also a 'good' race, this means that necromancers and shadow knights are not available to this race.
Culteral armor is armor that can only be crafted by the race that is intended to use it. There are only a few exceptions to this. This is in most cases a high level/skill peice of armor requiring a pretty high amount of time and 'plat' being invested in raising the skill to the required level.
A couple of good sites for general information is eqcasters realm and allakazams(sp?) just do a google search on everquest.

Hope this helps
