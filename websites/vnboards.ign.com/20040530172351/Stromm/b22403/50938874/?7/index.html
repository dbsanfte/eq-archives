Very important for when you die after level 8 when you need to retrieve your corpse to get the gear off of it. You no longer die and reappear with gear from this point forward. /loc is the command. Welcome to EQ and Stromm, Stein

I agree with everything above, except you really shouldn't be in GFay right now and do not rebind there yet.

Until you reach level 10, you get no experience loss free trips to your bind point with all your items via death to a monster (mob). Use this to your advantage while you can.

Return to Kaladim and seriously go after the newbie armor. I may be a bard now, but I was a 65 Dwarven Warrior on my old server and this armor is the best you're going to get for the next 20 levels at least, possibly longer due to the long ramp up time and expensive prices on mid-range items right now. Get your weapon quest completed as well. You'll need it. This also lets you save up more cash for your later needs.

I remember seeing you last week in Butcherblock at the X-Roads (crossroads - the small guard hut where the path from Kaladim splits into the path to Dagnor's Cauldron and Greater Faydark (GFay).

Additional Quick Tips - Collect all the spiderling and spider silks you can and hold onto them in the bank (it's in Kaladim on the way to the Paladin and Cleric guild). Also collect and hold onto all the snake eggs you find. Players will pay you for these or you can use them yourself later for tradeskills to make yourself things.

Also, while hunting around the X-roads area keep an eye out for the Goblins. They drop blood and heads that sell to the vendor outside Kaladim for as much as 4pp (based on typical Dwarven CHA values at that level). This is huge money for a starting zone.

Also, look into a quest for the Fine Cut, Diamond Inlaid mask in Plane of Knowledge. This is an excellent item for you to get. With a little luck and planning you can get it without a painful corpse retrieval if you do it before level 10 via the no experience loss free death with all your items intact rule. The details are on Allakhazam and you can buy the Sarnak Blood in the bazaar for a few PP (platinum).

Good luck, and enjoy the game. There is a lot more too it now than just hunting for XP and camping monsters for items. Try to experience all the facets of the game. This will help you decide if this is something that you want to keep doing.

Yeah - most people aren't coming out to the messageboard yet. Then again, they insist on having 2 marketplaces as well so who knows. There is another messageboard already that someone put up during the first week, but I can't remember the link for it.

PS - never refer to yourself as a "Dorf". Dwarves hate to be called this, and anyone calling you this owes you a pint of beer or a pint of their blood (the choice is yours <evil grin>)

Welcome to Stromm!
To clarify about the train thing;
Yes a "train" is when you are being chased by multiple mobs and you are running to a safe point, like a zone line or a guard. The warning is to inform other people that these mobs are coming because when you zone out to safety the mobs stick around zone line (and typically start to beat an other players).

The first few days are always a little tough, getting to know the mechanics of the game and such while competing for the precious few mobs level 1 characters can kill, trust me when I say it gets better, and slightly addictive :-)
