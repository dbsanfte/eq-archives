I too would like to see another PvP server. I like the diety format as in SZ. I've played EQ off and on since beta. My first character was on Rathe. I got to lvl27 and was hunting in CT. Once day, I just got tired of the endless camping and didn't log on again. I let my account lapse.

At the end of summer that same year, my family and I moved to a new job in Raleigh, NC. The company put us up in a luxury apartment till we could find a house. While there, we decided to pass the time and see what was happening in EQ. We renewed our accounts and logged on hours after the new server Tribubal came up.

At level 20 I signed on with a guild called "Elitists". Though they had a less than stellar reputation, I always conducted myself with dignity. Eventually, mounting pressure forced the guild to clean up its image.

During my time with "Elitists", I attemted to stay ahead of the leveling curve. For a while, I was the third highest level paladin on the server and the highest level human paladin. Also during this time was when the paladin revolt against Verant took place. After 2 years, Verant finally decided to balance the ailing class.

I finally reached lvl 50 when that was the highest level. I was becoming more and more disheartened by the bickering from the meager drops and the inter guild fighting over them. I play MMORPGs to have fun, not argure with people and scrap over every drop.

I logged back in from time to time, though not guilded anymore, and fought in Karnors till level 52. Once again, the endless cycle of camp, pull, kill, took its toll. When DAOC came out, I started 3 accounts. My kids and I had wonderful fun and were members of the best guild that I have ever seen or heard of, "Knights Valorous". Lack of content and unbalanced PVP only made it more difficult to accept the experience grind.

I decided to check out Luclin and logged on to my accounts. I found out about the diety PvP server. My son, his best friend and I decided to give it a shot. The server had much of the flavor of the early UO. Though I am pushing through the exp grind again, there is a purpose. I love PvP. We have been playing 4 weeks now. At level 33, things are becoming difficult. Hopefully when I get to sufficient level to join one of the major guilds, I can resume one of the parts of any of the games that I have played that I enjoy tremendously. That is, questing. An added bonus is the fun I have in ganking other players.

Though not my first choice, I play a dark elf warrior. My son and his friend has this thing about DEs. He pays an SK and his friend a cleric.

Well, the evil team starts out with a natural geographic advantage because of the proximity of evil towns to most low-mid level dungeons and leveling spots. Casters can bind anywhere but melees can only bind in towns of course so that influences where they will level at. Look at the closest bindpoint to say Guk that is a non-evil town. Freeport or Rivervale. It's debatable which is closest what isn't debatable is they are a long ways away.

Now look at the number of dungeons that are one or two zones away from evil towns: Upper and Lower Guk, Najena, Sol A and B, Cazic (I know it's high level now), Befallen...

Now where many of the best dungeons are, in Kunark, the evil team has the ONLY city there. Granted, most of their team can't get in there without a lot of faction work but NONE of the other teams' players are likely to ever get faction there to gain entrance. Now there are two outposts in Kunark. The OT and FV. While evils have Club Med leveling, shopping and banking in the OT, Goods and Neuts share the outpost in FV and frequently clash.

So you can see geography plays heavily in favor of the evil team. Because it gives them a leg up in controlling most dungeons, they also tend to be more wealthy at an earlier level with more items. At higher levels they tend to have really good gear while many goods don't have a complete set of good gear until they are in the 50s.

Do I blame the people that started in the beginning of SZ on the evil team? Not really. I do point fingers at the people that come here new knowing the existing imbalance and still go evil and the people that reroll off the good and neut teams to go evil. Those people are only exasperating an already bad situation and are not acting in the best interest of the server.
