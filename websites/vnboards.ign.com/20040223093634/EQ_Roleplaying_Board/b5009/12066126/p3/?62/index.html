Before it would seem possible, Sethix begins to stir beside the warmth of the fire, under the Godly healing of a Paladin.

With a groan that sounds from beneath the faceplate of his great helm, the Warrior twitches, his fingures wrapping instinctively about the hilt of his thick blade.

He quickly tries to sit up, only to wince and slowly lower himself back to the straw and dust of the inn's floor.

With mesured movements, the Warrior blindly unbuckles his helm and slips it from his head. Beneath the steel was concealed a head of blondish hair and a rugged face. Over one eye rests a black patch with gold trimming, peaking from the top and bottom is a long-healed scar, jagged as if from the weapon of a primative, unkept blade.

His good eye blinks, trying to focus on the light and faces about him. With a frown of concentration, he looks about, his gaze pausing momentarily upon the seated Troll. Realizing he is staring, he quickly moves his gaze from face to face.

He can feel the healing given, can feel the vitality returning to his muscles and soul. He nods slightly, to no one in particular, "My thanks for the rescue." he says, his voice coming from a dry throat, formed by a tongue that feels as velvet.

"I owe you much."

Amris blinked in surprise when Lothannon spoke to her, then blushed at his praise. Then she smiled, the unwittingly charming smile that her friends knew her for. Her typically sad face was transformed by the sparkling eyes, and quirky smile. Her naturally pleasant and genuine nature asserted itself once more, gone was the shyness. The tempo of the healing song changed, as she got out her mandolin, and began to sing along with the music, weaving the healing power of song into the notes, so that they danced and sparkled in the air all around them.

As she sang, the others felt the tension begin to lessen, the magic of her song and her presence seemed to keep the evil at bay, just outside the room. It was there; yet here, in the warmth of the inn, there was light, and music, and food, and a hint of peace.

Amris watched the knight at work behind the counter for a moment, then turned to the troll as he spoke. "I forgot to tell yoo... I met Fir'yona Vee, princess to da pale elfies, da udder day. Wuz sumtin to see, dat's fer sure."

Ah, her bardic instints perced up! A tale! Such a thing, no bard can resist. Another song to write, another tale to tell, is there a happier thing in life? Well, okay, ASIDE from the obvious. She looked around the room, and considered each person in turn. Ah, it appeared there were many tales to be told here.

She spoke into the silence, her voice keyed to sooth, to calm others. "May I introduce myself? I am Amris. I have achieved the honor of my 32nd song." Standing, she bowed to each in turn. "I sense that each of you have tales you need to tell. Mine is simple, so I will not bother you with it, but I think perhaps, a time of telling would do well? And a moment to introduce ourselves, so that names are known, and perhaps some understanding may be achieved, as to why we have all met under such strange circumstances."

She turned then to Cyst. "Perhaps you would be kind enough to begin? After all, you have already piqued our interest." She smiled again, the smile that won many who were reluctant, or shy.

She turned to the taciturn warrior, and invited him to join her for the healing that she could weave. She sang the song quietly then, melting seamlessly into the background, to relinquish the attention back to the troll.

He clears his throat and begins speaking again in a low rumble. "I wuz outside of Felwit' da udder day, healin' yung elfies an' learnin' da way round da Faydarks, when dese purty horns rang t'rough da air... I turned round to look at da gates, an' dere wuz Fir'yona Vee, in a reely purty white dress. I knew it wuz her cuz I 'membered her frum Kit'icor Forest, when dis troll wuz yunger... but dat's anudder storee.

"She smiled at her subjecks an' sed she wuz lookin' for an eskort into da lesser Faydark cuz she needed to find a friend. I is no follower of Tunare, but I helped da ladee befor, an' so I agreed to come. Me an' a buncha elfies, gnomies an' halflins gathered round her an' we walked to da lesser Faydark.

"At da pass, she told us we wuz goin' to meet Quest-rel, da purty horned horsey, an' dat we shud stay back an' be quiet so we wud no scare her. Den we set off. Wuz a long jurney t'rough da woods, an' I used da spirits to hide dis troll's form frum pryin' eyes cuz last time I wuz t'rough dat forest I got bashed.

"Fine-lee we found Quest-rel. She wuz shakin' wit' feer, an' I knew she cud see me, so I stayed back an' listened as Fir'yona talked wit her. She wuz talkin' bout a rityool dat Fir'yona wuz gonna perform, an' menshunned dat da evil races cud no help in dis ting. I asked Fir'yona if I shud leeve, cuz I is a troll, an' she sed it wud be hokay if I jus' watched. Den da two of em started walkin' to da rityool site, an' we all followed."

He pauses a moment, staring at the woodgrain of the table. "We 'rived at a cleerin' by da faerie place. Wuz dere dat Quest-rel left us, an' we formed a circle round Fir'yona. Frum da crowd she picked four peoples, t'ree elves an' a barbarian, to help wit' da rityool. She started sayin' tings in long words bout da el'ments an' tings, an' wun by wun da helpers yoozed da stones dey wuz given. As each wun did, da forest grew more livin', more bright... wuz sumtin to see. An' den, wunce da big words wuz dun, Tunare, da Mudda of All herself, appeared in da circle!

"Fir'yona bowed befor da Mudda an' sed she wuz sorry for whut happened recently.. sumtin about a staff, I tink. Da goddess sed it wuz hokay but warned Fir'yona dat da corrupshun of dis staff, da taint of Cazic-Thool, cud be affectin' her powers. Dey talked bout da Ring of Scale an' a few udder tings... I is sorry, but I wuz a li'l distrackted at dat point.

"I reely started focusin' again, t'ough, when da forest started to wilt all round us. Tunare told us to run to safetee, but no wun did. Screams came frum da camps in da forest. Da animals stampeded away. Waves of fear started hittin' me, an' frum whut I cud see I wuz no da onlee wun affeckted... an' den Cazic-Thool himself appeared."

Cyst sighs and clenches his fists a moment before continuing. "Dis was a hard moment for me. Cazic-Thool wuz da god I wuz taught to worship when I wuz a li'l troll, an' da fear is still in me.. but I stopped followin' da Faceless long ago. Seein' him dere, t'ough, onlee feet away frum me, towerin' twenty feet tall... neerly made me give in again. I did no, in da end. I proteckted Fir'yona wunce an' I wud do it again. I used anger to block da fear I wuz feelin', an' shouted at da god as loud as I cud.

"Da lord of Fear jus' laughed an' t'anked Fir'yona for helpin' him. He banished da avatar of Tunare wit' a wave of his hand... wuz den dat Quest-rel came back, willin' to stand by da ladee's side. Cazic looked at Quest-rel an' sed dat he wuz gonna leeve... but not befor wun last gift. Wit' dat, he took hold of da horse's horn. Quest-rel wuz makin' all sorts of pained sounds, wuz awful. Fir'yona raised her sword in da air an' told dose gaddered dere to attack, an' I did, even t'ough da spirits wuz fightin' me for control. Den, wit' a blink, da Faceless disappeared.

"We wuz left stunned, not knowin' whut to do, 'til da pained sounds of Quest-rel came to our ears. She wuz pawin' at her horn. Fir'yona asked whut wuz wrong an' whut she cud do, an' da horse replied dat Cazic had corrupted her, an' da onlee way to save her wuz by removin' her horn. At first, da ladee wud no do it, but den she reelized it wuz da onlee way, an' cut off da horn wit' wun sweep of her sword.

"Quest-rel ran off afore we cud do anytin', an' Fir'yona wuz left holdin' da horn in her hand. While she wuz distrackted, a troll frum da back of da crowd leaped forward, roarin', an' tried to kill da ladee. He did no succeed, an' wuz repelled by Fir'yona. As he wuz leevin', he glared at me an' sed "traitor" jus' so I cud hear. I did no respond. I is used to it.

"Fir'yona told us to meet her at da big spires in da greater Faydark. She sed my name in a list of peoples she wanted to speek wit', an' dat had me worried. Still, I wuz curious so I went where she told us to. When she appeared, wuz my name she called out first."

He clears his throat again, clearly unused to speaking so long. "I pushed t'rough da crowd an' stood befor da ladee. She tanked me for helpin' her an' held out a purty coin dat sed "Coin of Fait'" on it. She told me to take it to my homeland. I stared at da coin a bit, not knowin' whut to say, but fine-lee I told her I cud no take it, dat it wud hurt dis troll's quest in life. She nodded an' tanked me again for helpin' her, puttin' da coin away.

"I left shortly after dat, but it gave me a lot to tink about." He smiles again, but there's a hint of sadness to it. "An' dere's da storee."

/ic

Sethix watches the Troll as he speaks, listening intently to the story as the Troll unfolds it before everyone.

Through it all, the Warrior's steel gaze never leaves the Troll, never wavering from the large creature. Though he is not outwardly hostile, Sethix is obviously quite dubious of the Troll and all that his Race represents. All the pain of the Warrior's younger days, all the hatered that he feels from days long past, days partially clouded by time.

As the Troll's tale comes to an end, the Warrior sits up straighter on the floor, the plates of his armour whine slightly, but he finally manages to right himself. With reverence, he places his skull-shaped helm under his elbow and leans his chin in his palm, brow furrowed in concentration.

"Your deeds are at odds with your heritage, Troll." Sethix manages to speak the words with relative control over his distaste.

"But, in my eyes, you shall never be free of your...kin." The Warrior's striking grey eye bores into the Troll's face, no yeild in it's steely gaze. "Many have perished under the fell blade of your kind, many have been tortured, robbed, raped..." He trails off, fists clenched in rage, his teeth locked together.

He speaks through those clenched teeth, "Families destroyed." He turns his glower upon Cyst, his brow lowered in a scowl. "It was creatures like yourself that destroyed my childhood, that took my eye." He looks about, seeing nothing in this realm of time. "Rape, murder, torture, you know not the horrors your race has placed upon my family." He gently brushes the patch over his eye, his voice is lowered to a whisper, "you know not what I have lost to your hands."

He turns back to Cyst, shaking his head, "Because of those like you, I was orphaned, left for dead with my eye removed by the swipe of a cruel blade. Tied to the earth like an animal for days, while your kin would ate mutton before my starving eyes."

"Your...people -" he spits the word as a curse, "nearly killed me, just a boy! They laughed, jabbing me with sharpened sticks, throwing rocks at me."

He makes no outwardly threatening moves, but the tension he emits is nearly palpable. "Were it not for the band of Elves passing through, I'd have died alongside my raped and murdered sister and mother, or had my skin stripped from my body, such as was done to my father." His gauntleted hand grips the sword at his side.

"Yet, here I sit. Breathing the same air as you, listening to your broken speech, listening to you speak of Lady Vie as you would someone of the light..."

He looks away, forcing the next words through tight lips, "...and I am confused..."

His eye shines with the memories brought forth by Cyst's pressance.

/ooc
Sethix is embroiled in an inner battle between heart and mind at this point. His head is telling him that the Troll is not evil, yet his heart, his true being, is telling him otherwise as simply the sound of Cyst's broken speach, accent and physical being remind him of the horrors of days as a prisoner, a play thing for the followers of Cazic.
