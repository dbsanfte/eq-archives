To be honest I am not judging. Maybe a little age, sweat and work would do wonders.

http://www.usarec.army.mil/3rdbde/5dbn/Advertising%20and%20Public%20Affairs%20Site/downloads/woft.pdf

Caution high band width….its a PDF file. You need adobe acrobat reader.

You can join the Army as a pilot and not have a degree. When I was recruiting I put in 3 Flight Warrant Officers during my 3 years. To be honest most Army recruiters don’t like doing the packets because it takes A LOT of work, the applicant has to qualify and most are not familiar with how to process the applicant. It is a time consuming process and a learning curve for the recruiter first doing one.

If I found someone who was interested I would ask basic qualifications questions and then I would sit down with them and pull out the regulation that goes over the step by step procedure. I would show them it would not be easy. If they wouldn’t commit I wouldn’t bother them. Its too much trouble for someone to back out or be flaky about the whole thing.

Here are the basics.

Good grades in school.
Nothing more than minor traffic tickets and any traffic violation more than $100 is a moral waiver.
You have to score over 110 on your GT score which equates to somewhere between a 62-75 AFQT on the ASVAB.
Score a 90 or better on the FAST test. You can order a study guide from any book store. If you have any common sense the test is easy.
No drug usage EVAR.
Pass a Class I flight physical and eye exam. 1 in 4 males are color blind and that gigs em a lot.

More info: http://www.usarec.army.mil/hq/warrant/
