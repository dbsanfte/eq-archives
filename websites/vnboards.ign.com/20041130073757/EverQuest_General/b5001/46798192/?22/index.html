Date Posted: 3/15/03 6:22am Subject: RE: Did pop make eq less .. epic feeling?
Use of the Wizard Portals to travel to one location on each continent came out with SoL. The same debate happened then - making the world smaller - since boats became mostly unnecessary with that change (except to visit OOT and similar "out to sea" zones).

The books did the same things for "cities" as SoL did for continents, with a book for every city - almost...

There is more than one book on Luclin - one in the nexus and one in Shadeweavers ourside Shar Val. Despite being cities, there are no books for Katta or for Sanctus Seru to my knowledge. Sanctus is not far from Nexus but Katta still seems "remote" to me with a dangerous run through Tenebreous needed (which is the way I felt about all travel in the old days).

I think the analogy given above is very true - the original EQ felt like a world in the sense that the old UO felt (or they tried desperately hard to make it feel) like a real world. Somewhere along the line, the "feeling" of the game changed to a series of "shooting galleries" connected by zone boundaries. Unfortunate.... I am hoping that the prime directive they are following with EQ2 is to make it again feel like a huge dangerous (and challenging) world to those living there....
