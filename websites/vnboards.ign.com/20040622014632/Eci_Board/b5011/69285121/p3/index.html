Here's an interesting point of view:

Creation and Evolution are NOT mutually exclusive. It is possible to believe in both, simultaneously.

For example, the bible states the world was created in 6 days. Certain theories estimate the age of the planet earth to be 4.5 billion years. On the surface, this would seem like a direct contradiction. However, that is not necessarily the case. Please allow me to explain.

The Bible does not stipulate that the first day of creation was 24 hours long. The first day of creation could have been 23 hours long. Conversely, the first day could have been 25 hours in length... or 26 hours, or 27 hours... or 2 billion years.

If you read the Bible as locked, concrete fact, then it will continue to contradict and conflict with scientific discovery. This conflict will only serve to obscure the teachings and truth within the Bible.

On the otherhand, you read the Bible as literature which uses symbolism to outline its teachings and truths, you will see that it is still pertinant and applicable today.


It is difficult for a rational Christian mind to refute much of the scientific research on the field of evolution. Frequently, these types of discussions will revert to, "Well the Bible says this" and "the Bible says that." There are examples of this type of reasoning in this thread as well. It is important to realize that using the Bible as evidence, does not convince those who do not believe in the Bible to begin with.

Furthermore, these arguements are not effective, when presented with observations of 20 different finches in the Galapagos islands whose beaks have adapted to gather food more efficiently from different parts of the same tree. Animals do adapt over time, just as the human species has adapted significantly in the past 4000 years.

However, this does not discredit the Creationist's point of view. It is possible to be a creationist AND subscribe to evolution. If you continue to follow the scientific explanation of the history of life on earth, or the cosmos, eventually you will come to a singular instigating event, which science can not adequately explain.... The Big Bang.

What came before it? Why does it exist? Science can not answer these questions, because science is bound by the rules of THIS universe. We don't have the tools to measure events before, or outside This Universe.

The creationist can find divinity here. It is perfectly reasonable to believe that a God created the big bang, and established the sublime clockwork rules which govern this universe... be it Newtonian, Quantum, Darwinian, or what have you.

It is possible to be a creationist AND subscribe to evolution.

That's because you have FAITH in the non-existence of God and the primacy of man. I prefer to place my faith in God and to keep a jaundiced eye on man.

As for Creation vs Evolution, as has been pointed out, the two world-views are not entirely incompatible. There are biological mechanisms in place in Earth life that perform various functions which curb the deleterious effect of some point mutations. Genetic repair can and does occur inside cell nuclei. So DNA is not invulnerable to disruption by, say, radiation or chemical action. In other words, mutation is a given possibility in all species, and point mutation indeed does occur with clockwork regularity. While most mutations are likely to be either neutral (in regards to the success of that creature's ability to reproduce itself) or detrimental, there are distinct probabilities that a given point mutation will prove beneficial to some degree, or that two or more "neutral" mutations might, combined, provide some edge in reproduction capability (which requires a creature to survive and feed until reproduction occurs).

So, given that these mechanisms are in place, and that we can directly observe mutations occurring, and can directly examine both living and dead creatures and analyze our findings, I think it is safe to say that it is highly probable that creatures indeed mutate, and at least a few of those mutations are bound to provide survival/reproductive advantages, then the next question is, Does natural Selection occur?

I think that, once again, direct observation of creatures both living and dead has supplied us with the most probable answer, that yes, Natural Selection is definitely a factor affecting the growth or loss of populations of various creatures and plants.

Hell

Definition: The word “hell” is found in many Bible translations. In the same verses other translations read “the grave,” “the world of the dead,” and so forth. Other Bibles simply transliterate the original-language words that are sometimes rendered “hell”; that is, they express them with the letters of our alphabet but leave the words untranslated. What are those words? The Hebrew she’ohl´ and its Greek equivalent hai´des, which refer, not to an individual burial place, but to the common grave of dead mankind; also the Greek ge´en·na, which is used as a symbol of eternal destruction. However, both in Christendom and in many non-Christian religions it is taught that hell is a place inhabited by demons and where the wicked, after death, are punished (and some believe that this is with torment).

It usually means ‘life,’ or ‘vitality,’ or, at times, ‘the self.’”—The Encyclopedia Americana (1977), Vol. 25, p. 236.

Why is there confusion as to what the Bible says about hell?

“Much confusion and misunderstanding has been caused through the early translators of the Bible persistently rendering the Hebrew Sheol and the Greek Hades and Gehenna by the word hell. The simple transliteration of these words by the translators of the revised editions of the Bible has not sufficed to appreciably clear up this confusion and misconception.”—The Encyclopedia Americana (1942), Vol. XIV, p. 81.

Translators have allowed their personal beliefs to color their work instead of being consistent in their rendering of the original-language words. For example: (1) The King James Version rendered she’ohl´ as “hell,” “the grave,” and “the pit”; hai´des is therein rendered both “hell” and “grave”; ge´en·na is also translated “hell.” (2) Today’s English Version transliterates hai´des as “Hades” and also renders it as “hell” and “the world of the dead.” But besides rendering “hell” from hai´des it uses that same translation for ge´en·na. (3) The Jerusalem Bible transliterates hai´des six times, but in other passages it translates it as “hell” and as “the underworld.” It also translates ge´en·na as “hell,” as it does hai´des in two instances. Thus the exact meanings of the original-language words have been obscured.

What is the ‘fiery Gehenna’ to which Jesus referred?

Reference to Gehenna appears 12 times in the Christian Greek Scriptures. Five times it is directly associated with fire. Translators have rendered the Greek expression ge´en·nan tou py·ros´ as “hell fire” (KJ, Dy), “fires of hell” (NE), “fiery pit” (AT), and “fires of Gehenna” (NAB).

Historical background: The Valley of Hinnom (Gehenna) was outside the walls of Jerusalem. For a time it was the site of idolatrous worship, including child sacrifice. In the first century Gehenna was being used as the incinerator for the filth of Jerusalem. Bodies of dead animals were thrown into the valley to be consumed in the fires, to which sulfur, or brimstone, was added to assist the burning. Also bodies of executed criminals, who were considered undeserving of burial in a memorial tomb, were thrown into Gehenna. Thus, at Matthew 5:29, 30, Jesus spoke of the casting of one’s “whole body” into Gehenna. If the body fell into the constantly burning fire it was consumed, but if it landed on a ledge of the deep ravine its putrefying flesh became infested with the ever-present worms, or maggots. (Mark 9:47, 48) Living humans were not pitched into Gehenna; so it was not a place of conscious torment.

At Matthew 10:28, Jesus warned his hearers to “be in fear of him that can destroy both soul and body in Gehenna.” What does it mean? Notice that there is no mention here of torment in the fires of Gehenna; rather, he says to ‘fear him that can destroy in Gehenna.’ By referring to the “soul” separately, Jesus here emphasizes that God can destroy all of a person’s life prospects; thus there is no hope of resurrection for him. So, the references to the ‘fiery Gehenna’ have the same meaning as ‘the lake of fire’ of Revelation 21:8, namely, destruction, “second death.”
