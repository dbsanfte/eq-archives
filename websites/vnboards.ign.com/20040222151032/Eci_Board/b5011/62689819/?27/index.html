This is simple: The Federal government is authorized by the Constitution (Article I, Section 8, Clause 17) "...to exercise exclusive Legislation in all Cases whatsoever, over such District (not exceeding ten Miles square) as may, byCession of particular States, and the Acceptance of Congress, become the Seat of the Government of the United States, and to exercise like Authority over all Places purchased by the Consent of the Legislature of the State in which the Same shall be, for the Erection of Forts, Magazines, Arsenals, dock-Yards, and other needful Buildings..."

In other words, the Federal government is authorized to own land, and a ton of land it owns, too! The Federal Government claims direct ownership of something on the order of 87% of the land in Nevada, for example.

So why doesn't the Federal Government pay off its debts by sellig off some of its unneeded assets? I don't think there is any practical reason for the Federal Government to own so much land, and land is a valuable commodity, and when any private firm is in heavy debt and reduced income streams, one of the options open to it is to sell off its unneeded assets. Even sold cheaply, the Federal Government owns enough unneeded land in all 50 States to completely pay off the national debt without collecting another penny in taxes. The price of land is kept artificially high by dint of Washington DC keeping the supply artificially low, too, so if they sold it then more Americans could enjoy the fruits of land ownership (the quintessential American Dream is to own your own home).

EDIT: Oh, and currently the Federal Government does not pay property taxes to the States in which it owns property, so if it sold as much land as possible to private purchasers, those states where that land was sold would generate a lot of additional property tax revenue from that land. Those property taxes would pay for services that the States provide for their citizens (and illegal immigrants) in the many unfunded federal mandates imposed upon them. No child would be left behind ever again! SELL THE LAND!

>>"Do you know what a corperation that wants to make money would do with a national park? They would buy it all divy it up and sell it so people could build condos."<<

You have a shaky grasp of business concepts I see. You forget that it is government that zones land and issues building permits. And perhaps you didn't know this, but a deed can revert to the previous owner if any of the conditions of the contract transferring that land are not met. You can sell your land on the condition that the new owner not build anything on it larger than a restroom, and if he builds on that land it reverts to you. That would be the kind of deed parkland would be sold under, and under no circumstances would a local government zone parkland for development or issue building permits for non-recreation structures on that parkland.

But the owners could charge admission, set up campgrounds, install facilities like water fountains and hiking trails, and would have a vested interest in maintaining that parkland in good enough condition to attract visitors and keep the land turning a profit. Right now we are paying parking fees and trail access fees and camping fees to get on six-month waiting lists to get into the few parks that have camping facilities.

Public ownership keeps these recreational areas barely running if at all, and the facilities seem to grow more threadbare every year. And the Park Service no longer does brush clearing, so lots of devastating wildfires have threatened the surrounding developed areas lately. The situation is only getting worse, yet there is a huge demand for these recreational spaces that is not being met because of the bureacratic carrying capacity of the Park Service. So farm out all that responsibility to private firms, allow them to maintain those lands and clear the brush and install the trails and the restrooms and campgrounds, and watch the price go down and the availability go up.

Private enterprise is far superior to socialism. Why is it that you trust corporations to farm, process, and distribute your food, but you don't trust private enterprise to maintain a park? And why should your next door neighbors pay (via compulsory taxation) for your recreational facilities? Pay for your own good times! Like everything else the government does, private enterprise can do it better and cheaper.
