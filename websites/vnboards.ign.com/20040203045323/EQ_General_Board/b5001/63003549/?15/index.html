werock, The risk is what makes EQ more interesting IMO. I have played many of the other on-line games and found them boring. With no risk there is no real need to form a strategy. In EQ there is the nasty experience penality and the sometimes dangerous corpse retrevial. Which require you to think out your strategy, work with others and be prepaired to run away. This sort of gameplay appeals to me more than zerg fests, or walking up to a creature, click attack then go for a cup of coffee, come back and find if you have either won or lost. For the first 10 levels there is no experience loss or corpse retrevials at all.

The Experience losses can be mitigated by a cleric, necro or paladin, not a 100% but, a decent amount.

The nasty corpse retrevials have been drastically reduced with the new expansions, with grave yards and corpses appearing outside the dungeon. Also necromancers and shadow knights can summon your corpse and there are spells to locate your corpse.

I may leave EQ for a while to try those other games, but I will probably end up returning. Nothing out at the moment really holds my attention.
