It really doesn't matter since a pure Necro build is probably the strongest.

Ranger can heal minions quite well with Fertile Season and Healing Spring.

With Mesmer you can use Epidemic and Curses, etc to spread conditions, but you can use Plague Sending/Signet/Touch to do the same thing.

If you have a warrior or ranger secondary you can really amplify your damage using, Order of Pain/Mark of Pain.

With Ne/Monk however there are trick builds that can solo high level baddies.

If you wanted to go healer route you could use Well of Blood to heal more, well of Power and Offering of Blood to get energy etc. to heal a bit, but Monks tend to heal better because of Divine Favor esp Divine Boon.

I have the standard blood/curses necro with mesmer secondary. So far health has not been a problem. I lead with Life Siphon and keep one of those running at all times so I can keep my health topped up while I alternate Dark Pact and Vampire Gaze and curses like Shadow of Fear. As soon as a corpse drops I use Well of Blood so I can spam Dark Pacts to my heart's content. The party monk never has to worry about me. Plus as a necro you are usually the lowest priority target so you don't get beat on much. People don't realize how destructive we really are (shhh).

I don't use minions unless I'm soloing. They are a pain in parties plus the corpses are better used for Well of Blood. That is the necro's best party spell IMO. I have done several missions with a monk-less party where I became the primary healer. Often people thank me for using it... this after they say they don't need a necro in the party. I can't wait to get Well of Power.

For the mes side I use Domination for Chaos Storm and Cry of Frustration or something like that for interrupts, or maybe Backfire. Or the one that stops people hexing you is nice too, Hex Breaker I think.

I like Chaos Storm for AoE damage in PVE. In fact it's my only AoE damage spell so I need to keep it around. I can usually get 3 mobs at once for 10 damage per second for 10 seconds... that's 900 damage total, and it drains their energy too. It's mostly useless in PVP unfortunately because as soon as people notice they run out of it. Sometimes you can catch them napping though. Or you can drop it on the monk in the middle of a big battle and no one notices until it's too late.

Combine Chaos Storm with Life Siphon and blood nukes and you can dish out a lot of damage in a short time.

Any leftover points put in Soul Reaping. I like to keep it around 5. It's a real boost when you need it. I miss it terribly when I play my elementalist. Soul Reaping is why necros are still casting spells when eles and mesmers are standing around waiting for recharge.

This build has worked for PVE and PVP so far but I just ascended a couple of days ago and I haven't tried the L20 arenas yet. For PVP you probably want Illusion over Domination for the hexes, or maybe Inspiration for Channeling. That's what I hear, anyway.
