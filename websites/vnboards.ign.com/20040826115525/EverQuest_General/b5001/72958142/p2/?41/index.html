Quests are always a good idea because they are fun.

But what real game-mechanics-need is there for non-raid-style players to get raid-style equipment? There is no need. Non-raiders can easily get gear from LDoN and bazaar that makes all the non-raid XP zones easy to farm without totally breaking/trivializing the zones.

If the contention is "there should be more gear of the same level/style, but with different avenues of aquisition" then that makes fine sense. If the contention is "there should be better gear for those that don't play the raid-style game" then I cannot see how that is valid. As I mentioned before, raid-style gear is overpowered for the game that non-raid-style folks play, to the point of being broken. Broken to the point that raiders might should stay out of these broken zones like BOT.

Now, if mechanics allowed for players to somehow not be allowed to super-farm AAXP zones once the equipment level reached a "level of breakage-ness" for the zone, then a number of new itemization possibilities are more doable, but that is something that won't ever happen.

Oh, and most raiders will still tell you that the thrill of success beats the loot every time. A good raider likely has to kill 20-40 raid-type mobs for each little scrap of loot that he gets.

I am not saying that any of this is fair to either raid-style or non-raid-style players. Just trying to introduce some sensibility to these silly emotional contentions that raiders get "too much" or "non raiders need more stuff" The problem, if we agree there is one, is bigger than just item availability.
