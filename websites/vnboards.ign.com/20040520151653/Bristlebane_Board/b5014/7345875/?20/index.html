Ok, so I better clarify myself, because obviously if someone has no life at all, they couldn't possibly move a mouse or click its buttons. No, I'm not talking about dead people. I'm talking about people who might as well be dead.

Am I talking about you? No.

I'm talking about the OTHER guys out there with level 40+ characters.
The OTHER guys who spent $118.68 (12 x $9.89 <snicker>) and 30+ days of the last year collecting better electrons than everyone else.
The OTHER guys who haven't only wasted a substantial chunk of their lives, but are PROUD of it. Proud of wearing the Emperor's new clothes. Polished Ethereal breastplates and Shiny Ethereal vambraces. Whatever-the-hell the fanciest new equipment some acne-ridden game designer has dreamt up so that one Sneech can distinguish itself from another.

Now these "players" can tell you anything they want about "knowing the game better" or being "better at playing their character," but I guarantee you that if they have a level 40+ character, then they've spent 30+ days of their lives on Everquest. Maybe not on that character, but when you add up all the numbers they're afraid to add--the first, second, third failed character, the hours of surfing the web looking for hints, and the time spent reading inane post after inane post, they've spent a lot more than 30 days. And they have NO life. Nothing more important to do with 3 or 4 hours everyday than play Everquest. Nothing besides eating, shitting, and possibly holding a deadend job between the other 8 hours they spend comatose per day.

For those who don't know it yet, let me tell you how all of this plays out:

First, you have the normal people who spend a few hours once or twice a week playing. They can do without Everquest, and once Everquest dries up and blows away, they'll find something else to do.

Next, you have your powergamer wannabes. They REALLY want to play a high-level character, and see what's behind that next door, but they'll never have the time to spend. In the long run, they have two choices: buying a character, or progressively withering away from lack of exercise and eventually getting fired from the local McDonald's because they can't manage to come in on time, with a clean uniform, and without bloodshot, sleep-deprived eyes.

Lastly you have the "no-lifers." In Everquest, they have the highest level characters, the best equipment, the most platinum pieces, and the most friends. They are envied by everyone on "the server" and think quite highly of themselves for their "accomplishments." All of this stands in direct contrast to who they are in real life (or RL as they abbrieviate it--since to them REAL LIFE is not even important enough to spell out.) They were never very good at social interaction, and long months behind a computer screen have exacerbated their condition. It's difficult to understand where they find such devotion to a CG (computer game), and it's very likely that they will suffer severe psychological damage when Everquest finally ends.

I won't kid you. I don't know how Everquest will end. How many "levels" will Verant design for? How much slower the progression from one to the next until even the powergamers throw up their hands in disgust? No one knows. I can tell you one thing though: Verant has you by the balls. They've got you paying your $9.89 per month, and you'll pay it every month just so long as you have a character that you've spent 400+ hours of your life on. Their job is really pretty simple. They know that it takes 1000 or so hours to get a character to 60th level, and because of that they know there will be very few people who complain about not having much to do while sitting at the level cap. They don't have to roll out new expansions very often, and they're pulling in a cool $1.5 million a month to throw players the scant few bones they do. When you think of it, isn't it amazing that a person came up with a concept/idea/game that thousands of people will spend exponentially greater chunks of time on in order to gain arbitrary bits of status? It reminds me of that wonderful children's book by Dr. Seuss: the Sneeches. <<http://puffin.ptialaska.net/youngs/drseuss.htm>>

So why did I write this? Because of the 100,000 or so people who are spending 10 or more hours of their lives per week on Everquest, someone might have accomplished something productive, creative, or beneficial to the world or to themselves had they not wasted those MILLION hours last week transfixed in front of a computer screen.

Which is my rant for to-day.

Tune in tomorrow for my rant on camping.

Sinistro
Dark Elven Wizard, 36th Circle (retired)
