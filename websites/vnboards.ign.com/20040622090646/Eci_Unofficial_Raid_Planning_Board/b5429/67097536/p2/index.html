 		Date Posted: 4/15 11:48am Subject: RE: Poll: Would you be willing to attend a non-DKP public raid?
"A friend of mine who is a mathematician pointed out to me that the fairest method of loot distribution is /random. The way he put it was, with /random if you raid 30 times more than another player, you get 30 times the rolls he does too. DKP is actually less fair than /random because it prevents people from having even a chance at loot based on when they entered the system. If you aren't one of the original "Old Guard" players from when the DKP system was first implemented, you will never be able to fully catch up to players of comparable attendance levels. "

However, the mathematician doesn't take into account that not every raid, is the same raid. Some raids don't have wizard loot or cleric loot etc. If you went on 25 raids and someone else of your class went on 5 raids, but only 5 of those raids had wizard loot, your chances once again are equal to the person that doesn't raid as much, even though you would deserve the item more.

Also, random is exactly that, *random.* Just because you've had more chances to roll doesn't mean you'll get it. Some items can be used by all caster and priest classes, on those kinds of raid you'll have at least 30 people rolling if it's a full raid of 72. So despite the fact that you've had 30 chances to roll, your chance of getting it each time is still 1 in 30, whereas if it's a DKP system and you've been to 100% of the raids and the others haven't your chance is 100%.

Pulseless, you wanted to raid velious mobs, well, the problem with a /random loot system against velious mobs is that velious was an expansion with a itemization on raid mobs geared very much towards melee. Ask any wizard or mage from that time if they felt they were very useful before all the resist changes. If you raid Velious mobs you will have a hard time getting many essential classes. Fighting ST key mobs is great but besides the ST gear, their loot isn't. With no dkp system, how many of those people that get the ST key are going to show up for ST raids? Do you even plan on making ST raids? Mobs like Vindi, SoRZ and Tormax, speaking as a cleric, dropped absolutely nothing I wanted. What incentive would I have to help some melee get some loot, who has no obligation to bother helping me on a raid that doesn't drop melee loot?

DKP also lets you pick what you want to loot, whereas random doesn't. I've been on raids where the same person won 2 rolls, but they wouldn't let him loot the second item because he had already won something. Few people will want to pass on 1 item they can use in hopes of winning the 2nd roll for an item they'd prefer more. In DKP system you can say "That's a great shield but I already have a nice shield, I'm going to save points to buy that really nice helmet that also dropped and replace my cloth one." Or if you really need to replace that cloth helmet you can go on a lot of raids that have no helmet drops just to make sure you get that helmet when it does drop.

Even if random does distribute it fair for a lot of people, you will have some good dedicated raiders who will get discouraged when they lose a roll for the 10th time or more to people who rarely raid with the group and won't be seen again. Your core force will grow very slowly and your advancement will be poor. This is unavoidable no matter how you look at it.

Gearing your raid system to casual raiders is going to fail.
