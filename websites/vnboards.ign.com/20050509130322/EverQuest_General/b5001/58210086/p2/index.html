Date Posted: 10/27/03 7:57am Subject: RE: The only MMORPGS see in the NEAR future to give EQ a run for it's money is.... - Date Edited: 10/27/03 8:01am (2 edits total) Edited By: Seffrid
SuperSiabra, now I understand your point, which would appear to be based on a complete misconception. You do in AC2 exactly what you say you want to do in such a game, you don't switch modes in the way you refer to it.

In AC2 all characters have a basic weapon skill which will be based on their class (melee/missile/magic)or if appropriate their speciality ( Tumerok missile for example might be base/hive keeper/claw bearer). A tumerok base missile character will have a spear, for example, or if he specialises as a Hive Keeper he will have a nest. A human ranger will have a bow, and so on.

Your weapon will do base hits, that is to say once you hit the "attack" button the weapon will automatically attack based on its damage and delay stats - the same as in EQ therefore. In addition, however, you have special skills or spells which you drag onto your taskbar. It's up to you whether you launch a fight with one of those, perhaps to slow a mob for example, or whether you pull the mob with your basic weapon skill (which doesn't use vigor unlike your special skills).

I usually pull with a special skill and then hit the "attack" button to add in my weapon, whereas a ranger friend will do a lot of damage with his base weapon skill at no vigor cost before the mob has even got close, then he adds in some special skill attacks.

You can also put the weapons on the taskbar so you can switch between them if, for example, you have structured your character as a hybrid and want perhaps to pull with a damage over time magic spell (for which you must have your magic weapon equipped) but then switch to a sword for close combat when the mob gets to you. Some of the base magic spells like healing and restore vigor ones can be cast with a melee or missile weapon equipped.

You only ever get to hit the "peace" button if you want to stop the fight part-way through, otherwise once the mob is dead combat mode is left automatically. I think I have done it about once in developing 4 characters to between levels 16 and 27.

I hope that clarifies the point, it certainly isn't a cumbersome system, it's really intuitive and in a sense is exactly like EQ (cast a nuke to pull, hit "attack" when the mob is close followed by "bash" - taking a dwarf cleric as an example) but it does offer a lot of flexibility not least in permitting you to mix and match between classes so my missile character has the base magic skills that enable him to heal himself and restore his vigor, for example, and you also have maximum flexibility as to the tactics you want to use which depend very much on your role if grouped and the kind of mob you are fighting whether grouped or solo.
