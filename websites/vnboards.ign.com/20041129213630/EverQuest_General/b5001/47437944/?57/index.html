Date Posted: 3/26/03 11:10pm Subject: The good ole days of EQ are gone............
As I sat meditating in Loio today, I could not help thinking of the good ole days.

Remember when Loio used to be packed at all hours?
Now we're lucky to find 25 maybe 30 players in one of the game's largest zones.

Remember what it was like learn this game? Its lingo, its UI, how to play your class etc.
Now 98% of young players are simply alt twinks, gang banging mobs for one reason, exp. Not even loot, just exp.

Remember when clarity and breeze were the coolest things ever? Now we have KEI, which imo has almost ruined casters. Yes, it makes leveling much more caster friendly, but hey, noone played EQ because it was easy.

Remember when you actually had to know where you were going? When the sense heading skill served a purpose? When corpse runs could take hours upon hours? When porting was a vable means of making good loot?
Now you simply click on a book, click on another book and bang you're in the zone.

Remember greater Fay Mart and EC? Yeah, the bazaar has made shopping much easier, but far less interesting.

Remember when level 60 meant something?

As die hard of an EQ fanboi as I am, I can't deny the obvious, EQ is dying. Slowly, very slowly but quite certainly. I don't doubt that there are 200,000+ subscribers, I just doubt that more than maybe 50% play on a regular basis. I'll be stomping around the lands of norrath until the world ends, but sometimes I can't help but get nostalgic about the days when EQ was just that.
