The term does not refer to the intent, but to the method used. "Homicide bomber" could refer to anyone who commits homicide with an explosive device. "Suicide bomber" refers specifically to those persons who die with their victims. This is not a subtle distinction.

Our language has specific terms used to refer to specific things. Suicide bombers are a subset of the wider category of homicide bombers. When considering the phenomenon of suicide bombers, one is reminded of a quote from W.B. Yeats in his poem "Second Coming": "The best lacked all conviction, while the
worst are full of passionate intensity."
