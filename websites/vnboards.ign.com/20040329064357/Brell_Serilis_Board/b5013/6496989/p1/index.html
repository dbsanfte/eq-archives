Well, I picked it up a couple days ago as did my usual DM. Needless to say I made my first character last night and began a new campaign using the new rules. I must say they have greatly improved the game over 1st, 2nd, and the crap of Skills and Powers. They managed to streamline a lot of hte game and make a lot of suttle changes that make for a more enjoyable session. If anyone else has picked up the book I'd like to hear what you think. RE: WEBRPG

I've never actually used it (which explains why I'm promoting it, eh?), but a few friends of mine have. They've really liked the experience. Of course, this was before NWN, EQ, even UO (ancient history, I know).

I HAVE played D&D over e-mail with these friends; they say WebRPG is basically a cross between that and in-person gaming: you get the immediacy of the table-top (or the living room floor in our case, as our wives don't trust us on the good furniture!), but no visuals. Quite a bit depends on the DM's descriptive skills, but that's not so much different from face-to-face.

If anybody wants to set up a WebRPG D&D3 game, I'd love to give it a shot; our DM's out of country and therest of us are too slack to pick up the reins.
