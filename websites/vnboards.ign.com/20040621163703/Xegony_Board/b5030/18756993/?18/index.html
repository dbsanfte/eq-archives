A common trend I'm seeing here from people that bash DAoC, in particular, is that none of you reached true RvR (realm vs. realm, it's not called PvP). At best you experienced the various battle ground fields, which are filled with morons, idiots, and jackholes. RvR is EXTREMELY fun, once you get a 45+ character that can go out into the frontier and fight with the competent folk. For those of you bashing the level climb, I can't see how. It's very easy, ESPECIALLY with the Shrouded Isles expansion, to get from 1-50 in the time it takes an EQ player to go from 1-40. Another cool part about DAoC is that no 2 characters are going to be exactly the same. There are 3 different types of pretty much every class, and all are viable and effective in their own rights. I also really like the way equipment is handled, you can't really twink a character in DAoC. Items have lvls, and you can only viably wear things within 8 or so levels of yourself. Otherwise, they wear out and break very quickly. Tradeskills are much more fun than EQs, they're more useful, more rewarding, and better executed. Play made is nearly the best it gets. Most folks don't see maybe more than 1 piece of their final inventory be better (epic dungeon drops). There is no such thing as a "drop only" spell. If you are the lvl for a spell, have spent your skill points approprietly, you get your spell. So a caster in a small guild is just as good as one in a big guild at the same level, normally. With the advent of combat styles, DAoC is also much more fun from the melee perspective. Each Realm is different, so you have 30+ classes to play with, I think it's actually more like 42. I'm probably off there. The only complaint I've ever had about DAoC is yes, the world is substantially smaller. During prime time, all the dungeons are going to be full, and it's not nearly as viable to xp outside of a dungeon. Let me say this, however, as big as Everquest is, let's face it, there is a LOT of wasted space. Which brings me to another nice thing about DAoC, the world isn't overly huge. The game has no need for a teleporting system. If something is far away, you can spend 5 silver and take a horse and be AT or very close to your destination. There are a lot of other really nice things about DAoC, and all of them really just make your life easier. No corpse runs, XP loss on a death REALLY isn't big if you can get back to your grave and pray. If you get rezzed, then it's no issue at all post-Shrouded Isles. But the one big winner for me, is Mythic REALLY takes care of it's players. If you need a GM/CSR for something high priority, you get one. Each patch is more than a collection of bug fixes and typo corrections. With most every patch, Mythic enhances a class or two, makes dungeons or the world in general larger, creates entirely new tradeskills, awesome new features. For example, the next BIG patch, Player Housing. Estimated to go live in about 5 more months. Between now and then there will be several smaller patches, and tons of OPEN TESTING on the Pendragon server of the new features. The game has grown to nearly 10 times it's original size, NOT counting the expansion. The expansion doubled what had already been accomplished just in patching.

Basically, the only reason I'm playing EQ again is because I have been everywhere in DAoC, done everything. I have a lvl 50 character in all 3 realms. For story and environment, Albion will always be my favorite. But for just fun to play and sheer power, Midgard is hella fun. Hibernia is just a nice change with some really fascinating classes.
