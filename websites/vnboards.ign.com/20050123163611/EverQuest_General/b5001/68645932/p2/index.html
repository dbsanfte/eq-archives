Again, this is so laughably untrue as to make me think the extent of your DAoC experience consists of a single month of play after the game's release.

There are certain armors and mobs in DAoC that are weak not only to a weapon type (slash, thrust, crush), but spell types as well (heat, cold, spirit, energy, etc.). These don't limit which types of mobs (or players) you're allowed to hunt; all it does is provide certain bonuses or penalties based on what weapon you're using or spell your casting. A slash weapon against a tree-type mob may give a bonus to slash weapons, but a penalty against thrust type weapons. That same tree-mob may be weak against fire spells, but strong against spirit spells, so you'll do more damage using the former rather than the later. Contrast this to EQ which has none of the weapon-type bonuses and only a rudimentary version of the spell type bonuses (i.e., some mobs may be more resistant to a certain spell type).

More proof you're woefully misinformed (or deliberately spreading falsehood). In contrast to being "locked" into a certain spec, you're free to choose. You can't play another realm on the same server, but this doesn't mean you can't play another realm on a DIFFERENT server. As for the styles, again, not sure where you pull your figures (well, I can guess...), but in my experience most melee characters use up to 90% of all their styles. How many styles does an EQ melee character have or even use?

The mentality of EQ, on the other hand, is just one huge timesink treadmill. You PvE to gain more levels or AAs, which unlock higher raid content, giving you access to better loot, which leads to more raid content, new or easier PvE content, etcetera ad nauseum. Many players find this sort of deep character development and exploration rewarding and challenging (if not time-consuming). Acquiring a new Breastplate of Uberness that only a few others have after months of play is extremely gratifying. I know I was quite happy when my ranger got both his epic swords (Swiftwind AND Earthcaller). But when it comes down to it, EQ's basic treadmill formula doesn't change.
