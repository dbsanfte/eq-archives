It's easy to find both sides if you sit down and pick your numbers from the records. We can take 120 people to a flag raid, sure. Laggy as hell, healers in fear equipment keep dying or going oom, tanks with 6k HP die in three rounds to yard trash, people keep breaking mezzes, a druid accidentally evacs the MT and half the CHC, people don't know they have a KB weapon (or even what KB is) and the mob gets pushed around so much the poor tank has to run to keep up, and a third of the clerics suddenly have to leave for a guild raid... And somehow we win anyway.
Tunare only knows how Ash keeps some of our messes moving along. I'm pretty sure I couldn't do it. He's poured his heart and soul into training his regular raiders to be responsive, smart raiders, who can quickly adapt to whatever is thrown at us. And there's alot of veterans who are still willing to sit down and show a new raider the ropes. The KB disappears, the new offtanks start picking mobs up right on the pull, mezzes hold, a free healer steps in to keep an underarmoured tank alive, and by the end of the raid we almost look professional.

We can raid in small numbers... If it's mostly veteran raiders, and the right class mix is present. We're usually short of healers, especially clerics (quite a few former public clerics are arete or recon now...) Just like the massive overkills where we flagged 50+ people who had never been in the zone before - the record is there for everyone to see. Actually, our lower numbered kills are often cleaner faster raids. Look at our failures, and chances are that you'll find that, outside of the first few times we attempt a new target, we are more likely to fail with huge numbers than we are with a smaller force.

I *challenge* you to lead a raid - at the elemental level - with so many players with mixed experience and equipment levels. I seriously doubt you could pull it off. Ever.. Even after 100 attempts, if you could actually get people from 10 different guilds or unguilded to follow you through more than a few wipes the first time you tried a new mob. Try Tantisala Jaggedtooth when half the raid doesn't resist the AOE. Try Grummus with 30 people who haven't raided since a public Vox they went on 6 months ago (that was me a year ago, btw).

Try it. Chances are you'll fail miserably. Not repeatedly because after your first few attempts, nobody will be dumb enough to join you. Meanwhile, with Ash's leadership, we've enjoyed repeated success, and people come back to try for a first time kill after several failed attempts because they believe we can do it.

The reason so many are on our raids is not because we need them to win, but because Ashorty *HATES* to tell people "No, you can't join this raid". He'll do it sometimes, because we have to - lag was so bad on our last FRo run we had to ask a bunch of people to leave. But that was simply necessity - heals were not landing, recast times were all screwed up - it was a serious problem, so we had no choice but to turn people away. People come and they want to raid - you've got to respect them for it, and it's hard to say no.

There's 1828 toons listed on our dkp site. That's at least 1000 people who have taken advantage of an opportunity they would not have otherwise. More than 300 have over 200 dkp accumulated - people I would consider regular or semi-regular raiders. We have new faces every day, and former public raiders move on to Recon, Vane, Arete, NS, Alliance guilds, or other closed raid forces on a weekly basis. Between the huge number of people who join us, low average attendance, and high turnover, it's a *HUGE* task to equip our raiders with the gear and experience needed to forge ahead.

Face it... Those who bash our public raids as a "Zerg" force don't have the cohones to try it themselves. They'd rather sit in their nice cozy little closed raid force selectively filled with well equipped, experienced raiders without sitting down to invite, teach, and equip five newbies every week. Newbies who just might find a new love of the game and develop into skilled, dedicated raiders.
Chances are the people bashing us aren't even up to taking a *single* kind, promising new player (whom they don't know in RL), teaching them the skills they need, obtaining all of the flags and keys, and providing them with the basic tools to participate in and contribute to their private raids. These "Old Sk00lers" are so utterly clueless and hold less experienced players in so much contempt that they fail to understand why few people want to join their private raid force and wonder why our public raids have a larger, more active, and loyal raid force...

By all means, please keep standing around whining about how public has so many people ready to raid all the time while you never actually do anything to cultivate new high end raiders. Call everyone who just dinged 65 or isn't in one of the top five guilds a clueless hopeless n00b. We'll keep laughing and invite the "n00b" to help us clear two zones while you wait for enough of your "ubah elite strike force" to log in and realize there's nothing up anymore. If you don't like it, well.. There's only 60 or 80 of you, so it would be easy to move to another server. A public raid force could never manage a server switch, since we're so large and have so many diverse members. However, since you're such a tight, cohesive group with strong loyalties to each other you could move to a less competitive environment easily. Right? Oh...

Meanwhile, our raiders are heavily recruited by the "Top 5" guilds who value them as experienced players who come decently equipped and with most of the flags and keys... That is, with everything they don't want to sit down and take care of for people who might want to join them. And then turn around to leapfrog us even as I'm writing this post.
