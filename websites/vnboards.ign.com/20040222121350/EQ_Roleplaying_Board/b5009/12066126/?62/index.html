The young Knight stirs and his eyes slowly open to take in the darkened common room. Noting the lack of other presence he slowly stands and stretches the sleep from his cramped limbs.

"Those chairs may be comfortable enough to sit in, though it doth appear far too cramped to rest suitably in for one such as I. Well one cannot help where one falls asleep I suppose, and surely I hath slept on far worse gound afore now. "

Moving slowly to the fire he adds more fuel from the pile waiting and coaxes it back to life. He then returns to his chair and takes up his long spear-like blade and begins to slowly hone the edges. The rasp of the whetsone echoes loud in the dark of the common room as the Knight sees to his equipment with an air of relaxation and peace.

"I do wonder what hath befallen the others, still The Mother will watch over them and keep them from harm Im sure."

The Knight falls silent once again, sitting near the fireplace absorbed in reflection and the need to care for his arms.

In the rafters, the flickering flames are reflected in small bright eyes. The diminutive figure licks her lips nervously. The knight's awakening was not unprompted, as he thought, but was a product of slack fingerwork. Rigi was lucky that she had not been seen, his elven eyes slipping over the spot where she had crouched a moment before.

Rigi's fingers were one of her many worries right now. She could just imagine the Knight's cold stare as he took his justice, severing her hand with that vicious looking blade. Or maybe her head...

As if to punctutate her macabre thoughts he began to sharpen his executioner's blade. Each loving stroke shivered through Rigi like an everfrost wind. Then he stopped, leaving a terrible, leaden silence. She could feel a scream building in her, catching like a frog in her throat.

'Breathe deeply, and slowly' she thought, calming her rapid thoughts before they drove her mad. Self control as always, the young Gnome's worst enemy.

He moved over to the fire, the floorboards that she had earlier passed over like nothing but a draft, creaking under the heavy burden of his armour-laden step.

'Yes' she thought, 'stare into the fire. Let the darkness hug me tighter.' Trying not to repeat his unwitting mistake she examined the rest of the room for exits to the commonlands beyond.

She was perched about two thirds of the way along the simple wooden construction. At the far end there was a dovecot. Barely audable cooing eminated from within. No: she was small, but not that small. At the near end moonlight slipped through a small window. She began to creep towards it.

Sucking absently on a strand of her unkept aubern hair, deep in concentration, she took a tentative step.

Nothing. The paladin's face, faced not her, but the red warmth of the fire. The measured rise and fall of his chest, however suggested she had not aroused his attention. 'So far, so not dismembered' she thought grimly.

Her next step was more confident, but once made, the frighteningly close shriek of a roaming Griffin almost knocked her from her podium. The knight suffered also from that unhuman wail, his shoulders tensing. Was the fear for himself, or for his missing companions, lost in the dark? Rigi suspected the latter. Being the self-interested person that she was she hoped that it was busy making a dinner of them, maybe by the time she escaped this cage she had trapped herself in, it wouldn't have an appetite for her.

Her third stealthy step doomed her, the bulging pouch of her newly acquired coin brushed against one of the spider's web of cross beams. A platinum piece squeezed out, and started its slow inexoriable fall. Dropping to her belly to lie face down upon the beam she swept her hand in a desperate arc.

Fortuous circumstance indeed was the druid's entrance for the desperate figure in the eaves. Hoping to have drawn her captor's attention away, she leapt feet first towards the window, bursting open the shutters like a bellowing gust of wind, disappearing into the long grass by the side of the building.

The shutters of the open window banged back and forth in an unusual gust of wind; a leaf swept in, swirling up into the recently vacated rafters until it lost momentum, then spiralled down to land at Lenore's feet. Luclin, in her fourth quarter, gazed down from afar, casting a long beam of pale, mournful light into the formally cosy room. Then she was eclipsed by a massive, bloodshot eye.

Rigi had landed hard in the grass. Swiftness, not elegance best characterising her gravitational descent. Wincing, she popped her shoulder back into place, then waited for the dizzyness to pass. From the voices that drifted out of the window, she ascertained that pursuit was unlikely.

'Well' she thought, 'over already' Those Dwarves in Freeport had had a higher regard for their coin: the hour-long pursuit across the flat roofs, the crossbow bolts glancing off sandstone walls on every side. That seemingly never ending nightmare was not to be repeated tonight.

She had a decision to make: she could attempt the long, arduous night time journey across unfamiliar territory to the Misty Thicket, where cool jugs of ale and tales of mischief awaited her with open arms. Alternatively she could take the short safe sandy path back to Freeport which she had treaded with great rapidity just a few hours before. There were not many passing through the port city that fitted her description; most likely she would be spotted, sentenced without trail and hung up as an example to all. A harsh justice maybe, but one that certainly garnered her respect!

The beating of wings brought her out of her reverie. Instinct, as always, saved her. She rolled to the side as a great yellow beak furrowed the ground where she had stood just a moment before. The griffin towered above her, mighty and primal. Rigi, knowing where her only hope lay, screamed and darted between the trunk-like legs of the gargantuan beast, sprinting towards the door to the inn.

The clawed paw struck her like a tidal wave. The breath was expelled from her lungs. Like the bag of rags she so closely resembled, she hit the wall with a damp thud and embraced unconsciousness.

Across a black featureless plain Rigi trudges, dragging limp slothful feet. The horizon is a distant hazy line but she never looks up, intent on the mud and pepples that flow past and below her, like the inexoriable primaeval flow of those murky waters, a continent and a lifetime away. A soft breeze brings the sound of clanking machinery: only for this unnatural sound does she stop and look up.

And she is there again, amoungst the clockworks and merchants that fill the shores of Lake Tidmark. Looking down she gasps. Gone are the torn patchwork of armour and cloth. Gone is the black ragged cloak that hides her from pity-filled eyes. Instead a bright cyan blue allocyte's robe, snug, warm and ridiculously oversized hangs from her shoulders. Her hands rest deep inside cavernous baggy pockets, reaching all the way to her belt. Her feet are down there somewhere too, hidden beneath the robe's folds. 'I love this cloak' she remembers. A bell chimes, somewhere in the distance.

'Where does it come from?' she wonders idly, but then she catches sight of her reflection in the water by her side.

Gone are the scars, the vile disfigurations. Instead a young maiden, the young maiden of a barely remembered past, looks joyfully back. The bell chims again. 'Where does it come from?' she wonders idly as she walks along the waters edge

The merchants nod and smile to her as she wanders past and she starts to skip, emitting a strange gutteral sound as she does. She does not recognise it at first, but then remembers. Along the banks of the Tidmark Rigi skips and laughs, her heavy robe dragging behind her...

Back in the tavern Rigi smiles, a visitor to the land of dreams.

The chances of an evil race ever having as many 'good' individuals is SUPER low. But in EQ, it would seem that most people read the descriptions wrong and chose an evil race, even though they wished to play someone who follows the Light.

Dunno, I just hate RPing with the 'good' Troll and the honorable 'Ogre'. Too many Drizzt Do'Urden type people running around. No evil people. Gets tired. Heh. So I walk Seth away from such things.

No offence, but Trolls are Evil. That is just the way it is. The only two races that are truely neutral are Gnomes and Humans. They have an even balance of Evil and Good amongst their kin (though even Gnomes tend towards the side of Light).

Evil races are 99.99% evil, good races are 99.99% good. Humans are...well...pot luck. Gnomes are probably 70-80% good. Such is the design of the gods, and so should be that of the player, though too often those 'rules' are broken, which makes RPing it quite boring.

Such is my opinion and I do not expect anyone to share it. I don't mean to offend anyone with this, so I'm just going to bow out of this.

OOC: You're certainly entitled to your opinions, and I agree that there are entirely too many evil-turned-good characters in Norrath. I also play a dark elf that's as evil as they come, and a lower level dwarf that is insane and *thinks* he's a dark elf.

I enjoy playing different characters and personalities. *shrug*

Since the world of Norrath is largely religion-run, I play Cyst as one who has rejected the gods available to him and has not gained the trust or acceptance of any other deity. When another shaman is in the room, it's nearly impossible for him to cast anything, because they have the backing of a god whereas he does not. He tries to serve the Tribunal, but he's not all that good at it, frankly.

I don't believe any dark elf from Norrath should be good at all -- since they were created by the Prince of Hate, hatred is a natural and instinctive part of every single one of them. With trolls, they were created by the Lord of Fear, and fear is what they must overcome if they want to leave the darkness into which they were born. Cyst's still struggling with that.

As I said, you're entitled to your opinions. It's a shame that you're not staying -- I was impressed with your RPing, and would have enjoyed playing Cyst dealing with Seth's impression of trolls. I find it rather silly to leave because of one character, but I'm not criticizing; have fun, wherever you go.
