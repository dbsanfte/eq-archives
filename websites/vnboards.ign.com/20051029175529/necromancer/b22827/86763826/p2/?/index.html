What annoys me most, is the complete dominance of blood magic damage wise. Its not even close. Shadow strike does 41 damage, and 41 damage life drain if target is above 50%. Death Magic's deathly chill does 28 damage, and 28 more damage if target is above 50%.

You'd think a spell that offers only damage would do more than one that offers damage + lifetap survivability. I'd like to see Death Magic's direct damage do more, while Blood magic keeps its abilities to sacrifice life to damage and drain life to offer more survival options.

My necromancer has 16 death magic atm, hes level 17. My deathly swarm does a nice chunk of damage. My Touch of Agony does decent damage. Untill things actually start dying, those're the only two things I can do from my main profession. Once bodies hit the floor I can start summoning pets, but bone fiends are huuuuge energy sinks that I have to keep my mana high enough to summon before someone else randomly exploits the corpse.

I'm not saying that Death Magic is bad, hell I prefer death magic to blood magic in pve because of the bigger choices of options once mobs start dying. But in PvP, I get hit with distracting shot once while casting Deathly Swarm and for 20 seconds I'm just a half assed monk with no Divine Favor. I'd love to see more options for direct damage with Death Magic. Deathly Chill and Touch Of Agony could both use a slight upgrade to help us contribute a bit before bodies start hitting the floor. In my opinion of course.

The reason Death spells are not that useful in PvP is the best spells (most damaging) are too situational. Putrid Explosion is a perfect example. If someone dies and an enemy is standing right next to him, putrid explosion can be devastating. The problem is, if the enemy steps even 4 or 5 steps away from the corpse, putrid explosion does nothing. The blast radius on the explosion is simply too small for it to be used reliably. It works really great in PvE where corpses are very commonplace, but not so much in PvP.

I like Death magic a lot more than Blood, but the truth is in PvP you need to heavily spec in some other line to use until a body hits the floor, otherwise you're going to just sit there twiddling your thumbs while you're waiting.
