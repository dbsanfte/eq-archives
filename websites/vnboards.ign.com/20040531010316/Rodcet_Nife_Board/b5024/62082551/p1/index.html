I'm not one to create drama, but I'm also not going to leave misinformation as flagrant as this up here unchallenged. Our reputation is important to us, and we'd never take part in anything that would jeopardize that. We somehow have managed to get along well enough with all of the other elemental guilds in the past couple months of farming to escape without any real arguments. We've had our races to targets, everyone does. Some we've won, some we've lost. We've developed no ill relations with any of the other guilds out there. That speaks for itself. I also think that playing on our server for five years and maintaining a good rep makes me a viable enough first-hand source.

Simply put, we zoned in first, we killed first, we moved first. RoS had greater numbers by far than we did. That will always be the case and won't deter us from obtainable targets. So a race was started with Smoke as the target. We got two spiders, two nilborians, a mephit, a stormrider and a phoenix -- all roamers -- on us within the lifespan of the first mob in camp, which of course wiped our smallish force. It's bad luck. It's also because we had to rush. It happens. So we pretty much conceded Smoke to RoS. Arch Mage happened to be up deep in the zone, so we took DKP bids on it, and sent a single group up along with the winner to kill it. We had the remainder of our force zone out as they were not needed. The RoS guildleader followed us, and after we engaged Arch Mage, GM Exitsan appeared and reset the mob and ordered us out of the zone. The RoS force was nowhere close at the time. The GM said RoS had chosen Arch Mage as their target and we had leapfrogged them. When we politely offered to leave the Arch Mage to RoS and move towards Smoke as an alternate target, he ignored our questions, banished us from the zone and would not let us return. He had already said that two guilds could be killing alternate targets in the zone at the same time.

Yes, there was foul play here, and no, I don't fight these battles on this forum. However, I will defend my guild wherever I need to. The lack of drama only shows that other guilds know us and that our reputation speaks for itself.
