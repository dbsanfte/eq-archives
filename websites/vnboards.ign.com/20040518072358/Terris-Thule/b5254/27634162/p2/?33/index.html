I am the exact opposite of the poster. I believe that if you are high enough level to get twinkage then by all means twink yer alts. Why should a person that has reached a certain level have to go through hell levelling an alt without twinkage? And in turn, the last thing I want is a newbie EQ player twinked out because no one wants to twink alts.
It doesn't make any sense that you would want the high levels to twink the heck out of newbies. Who would want a bunch of uber-twinked newbies levelling like mad having no clue what they are doing? That's just asking for an over-ego'd geek training Cazel all over Oasis.
My 54 shammie was utter hell to level, as it's the first char I created on her server. My mage is twinked to high heaven simply because after reaching level 54 I should not have to go through the newbie process again. Granted I should not powerlevel up to 45+ because then I wouldn't know much about playing a mage, but at least with twinkage I can get her to level 15 or so pretty quickly. You really don't learn much about grouping before 20+ anyway.
In essence, the arguments against twinking has no validity if it is the higher levels own characters. The only drawback to twinking is the need to twink. It produces farming jerks playing a level 60 farming LGuk not allowing exp groups to hunt anything worthwhile. That, however, is another thread altogether.
