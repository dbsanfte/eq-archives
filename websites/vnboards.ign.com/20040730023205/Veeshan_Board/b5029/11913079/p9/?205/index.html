Varia,

You are unwittingly operating under assumption, and not fact, in that you -assume- Kylere was calling Krurk's quotations themselves into dispute, and not the facts (yes, the events were fact...I was there) of the situation contained within Tinket's alleged words. I am also working under assumption here (something I never claimed not to be doing), in that I interpreted Kylere's post to be disputing the events of the matter themselves. Case in point: He claimed that Zairus did not in fact do what Tinket (according to Krurk) said he did. Thus, he is disputing the events contained within Tinket's analysis of the situation, and he is therefore implying that Tinket is a liar. Simply put -- since Tinket remembers Zairus as having been ejected for disruption, and Kylere claims Zairus was not ejected as such, Kylere is therefore calling Tinket's recollection into question. His claim is against Tinket, regardless of the presence of Krurk, who merely acts as the intermediary of Tinket in the case of this specific issue.

You're right in that both viewpoints on the matter could be considered valid, as we have no way short of asking Tinket herself whether she actually said what Krurk quoted. Nevertheless, I can say with fair certainty that Kylere was attacking what he believed Tinket to have said. Were his quarrel only with Krurk, he would simply have said that Krurk's Tinket quote itself, and not simply the EVENTS described in the quote, was a forgery.

I hate to pick nits over matters this trivial, but what the heck. Your lengthy post about the meta-debate behind this debate required a response in kind.
