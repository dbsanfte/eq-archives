I've been a wizard to level 3 couldn't stand going any farther. Wizards are raw damage dealers as stumpe has indicated. They are the highest in the damage business and just like the mage they are doomed to weaker armour and elsser hps. For this they suck. I've not yet met a blue con that could be killed in 1 to 2 shots by anybody. Which means in combat a wizard casts his damage spell and then play proceeds as:
*goblin hits you for 5*
*your casting is interrupted*
*goblin hits you for 6*
*your casting is interrupted*
*goblin hits you for 1*
*your casting is interrupted*
So a wizard is powerless without someone to stand there and take the beating for him. Which means preparation time thrown into finding groups. Not to mention you have to split what you find with your groupmates

Now mage's on the other hand. We get pets. The pet is our so-called warrior who stands there and keeps the opponent distracted while we hit it with multiple spells. No interruptions here. Not to mention at the end of the fight, you get *ALL* the loot. So I say that they prep time we loose in summoning our food/water for the day is balance by not having to find a group.

Onto spell selection, wizards teleport, kill, root. Mage's summon useful items. Yes there is the complaint of having to waste time and mana on pets/weapons/food/water/bandages the moment we log on. Personally I summon a pet, a weapon for the pet, 10 food, 10 water, and 20 bandages the moment I log on. I also cast burnout on my pet and shield upon myself. This takes about 3/4 of my total mana. Which takes approx. 5 min to regen. 1 food and 1 water (given you aren't in a desert) will last you 20 RL min. So my 10 food/water should last me 3 hrs 20 min in RL. Which is conviently about how long I play the game for. Now if I couldn't summon food/water I'd have to run to town or a vendor and purchase it at 1 sil 5 co each. That's a cost of 5-6 gold for the same amount of time, but you have to add in the time for that trip to town. Which depending on your zone could take longer than 5 min

Bottom line:
If you want to see something fall over dead and then stare at your spellbook for awhile. Spend most of your time in groups or finding groups. Then become a wizard. People will love to have you around and you'll have fun later in life when you learn to teleport

If you'd rather be independant. And I *DO* mean independant go mage. We don't HAVE to come back to town, ever. We go for bank runs, and leveling. We can summon food/drink/groups, we can solo red cons and when you talk about travelling we can go invis/bind so we are anywhere we wish to be. We have no NEED for a group and cast the 2nd highest DD in the game. Which is why mages are not high up on peoples 'To-group with' list. Cause they don't get many opportunities to group with mages, the reason for this? Mages are not looking for groups. So as a mage, you are an independant. Whether you want to be or not, finding groups can be difficult compared to other classes, but you have the ability to go anywhere and come back at your leisure.

So it comes down to what you want to be.

Personally I'm a mage and damn proud of it.

Well, here are some of my thoughts...

I've played a few Wizards, up to level 5.
I've played a few Magicians, up to level 5.

I never got beyond 5th level with any secondary character because I do want to get places with the characters I consider my "main" or "favourite" characters... Shendare and Toromin. Even on my old server Bristlebane, these two stuck out as my favourites, and all the others I'd messed around with were nudged back into the musty, cobwebbed corners of the character select screen.

From the few levels I've played with both Wizards and Magicians and the research I've done into the kinds of spells they get throughout their lives, I have to say that Magicians are much more fun early on and then get a little old, while a Wizard is monotonous early on and then picks up speed after the early 20's.

A Wizard can summon a light source, root an enemy, blast it until the mana goes out, and AoE early on, that's about all they'll ever be good for until they get teleportation after weeks or months of dedicated playing.

A Magician can summon from thin air almost all the bare necessities of EQ life (food, water, weapons, bandages, and fighting partners), and can hold their own in magical damage with a few fire-based DD spells.

I must say I had a lot of fun summoning things and commanding my pet when I played my first Magician... A typical conversation (which was MUCH more fun with a few players medding nearby as an audience) would go like this...

(I'd log on.)
Me: Now, let's see... where in the world did I leave my dagger when I went to bed last night? Oh! That's right... the sock drawer.
(Summon Dagger)
Me: There we are. I feel badder already. Whoop! I packed my lunch and then left it on the table, didn't I? Well, no sense going hungry...
(Summon Food. Summon Drink. Repeat 2x)
Me: There we are. Hmm, it's dark out tonight. I don't suppose Kroner would mine if I borrowed his Wispstone...
(Summon Wispstone)
Me: I'll just return it before he gets home. Okay, I'm all ready to go and I'm already bbored. Hey, Konarkn, get one of your cousins up. It's time to go fight some things.
(Summon Pet)
Me: Hey, [Pet-Name]. Glad to have you along. Watch out for those orc centurions this time. I think it was your magic that drew their attention last time and got you sent back home too early. I need you here to help me fight, alright?
(Here I start giving the pet some strategic commands in order to prompt some appropriate responses to my endless babbling -- a fun trait for my Magician, since I can get the pets to talk back somewhat).
Pet: As you wish, O Great One.
Me: Good lad. Alright, we're all set. What do you feel like doing first?
Pet: Guarding you, Master.
Me: Aww, thanks, bud. You do know how to boost a guy's ego. Alright, besides that? Should we go hunt down some orc pawns?
Pet: As you wish, Master.
Me: Yeah, yeah... yuppy. We People always have to be the leaders in these little escapades. Alright, let's go.
(I walk away, while my previous command told my pet to stay.)
Me: Well, come on! What are you waiting for?
(Command pet to follow)
Pet: Following, Master.

Hee. I have tons of fun with a Magician, and had one person rolling on the floor one night listening to me and my pet.

I like wizards, too. I like every class in the game, to certain degrees. I've had more time to develop a strong taste for the Enchanter and Ranger, though, so they're my current major favourites.

In the Wizard vs. Magician debate, it's like this: Magicians are more fun pre-20's, and Wizards are more fun post-20's. In the 20's the Magicians start getting a little old, because their magic stays the same as ever, summoning food, water, bandages, weapons, and (later some very nifty) items. In the 20's the Wizards start getting interesting, because they start being able to Teleport themselves and others all over the world, and even outside it into alternate Planes of reality (dimensions, I guess), in addition to their raw damage abilities.

Has this post gotten too long to read? Many of my posts end up being longer than I intended. Ah well, I hope there was some helpful feedback and opining in it. *g*

~ Shendare, Koada'Dal Enchanter (8th)
~ Toromin, Feir'Dal Ranger (8th)
(And a few others that I never have time to play.)
