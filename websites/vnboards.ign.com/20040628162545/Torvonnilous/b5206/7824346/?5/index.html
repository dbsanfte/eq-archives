So you're sitting around playing EQ, baldurs gate 2, or some gibfest first person shooter...Happy in your little (or big) warm house, drinking your mountain dew, possibly beer, and quite possibly eating frozen french bread pizza, or maybe taco bell...all secure in your life, knowing you have a decent job with a fair amount of job security, maybe a girlfriend, boyfriend, or a husband, wife, or fiance...


You have enough money to buy leisurely items, the next pc game you like, that new sports utility car, or have enough extra cash to go down to the porn stor and rent porn or buy whatever sex toy you want...

Not to mention most if not all the food you want...I used to vollunteer quite a bit when i was younger, for various causes. Mostly for children (i was introduced to the neato world of helping others when i was caught blowing up/burning my 5th dumpster...thank god they didnt know about the other 20 heheh...shrug...i was destructive...it was public service to atone for my crimes against the department of sanitation)...

But then i forgot how much a small amount of my extra time can GREATLY help others in need. And i forgot about vollunteering, and then floundered into the "yeah everyone is as well off as me" mode. Then close to xmas i was sitting around thinking what to buy all the close people in my life for xmas, and on the news came a story about how the local homeless feeding service (used to be known as a soup kitchen) were MASSIVELY understaffed, and in a HUGE need for food to server the areas homeless for xmas lunch/dinner. Well i decided what i wanted to do. Instead of going through buttloads of money for everyone to buy everyone else gifts, and since the typical upper middle class american truely doesnt NEED more stuff, i thought it would be great for us to stick to a few small gifts, and for our LARGE gift/gifts to each other, would be to go down there and vollunteer our time in their kitchen to feed the homeless.

We all agreed, bought each other some smaller, more needed gifts...and went down there to help out anyway we could. Oh yeah, we also used some of our money we had left over to buy food for the dinner. The people down there were very thankful for us to show up, got us outfitted in our kitchen gear (apron, gloves, etc) and deligated our responsibilities. The setup was similar to a cafeteria line, as in there was a person serving the mashed potatoes, another turkey, another dressing, etc...

ALOT of needy people, and sometimes whole families came through to get a meal, sometimes seconds or thirds...some of these people/families hadnt eaten in quite some time and were famished. One time a little girl in tattered clothes came through the line for seconds on my mashed potatoes, asked me politely for some more and i said "sure honey, you can have as much as you want", and she started to cry, and said god bless you. Several instances of people being overwhelmed with emotions by knowing i truely wanted to help them occured that evening, the one that had the most emotional impact on me was that little girl.

So many times while i was serving food and doing a small part to help out, i had received so many thank you's, god bless you's, and over all sincere emotionally strong appreciation for serving them, I all of a sudden felt like the richest guy on earth. No not with money, but something priceless...Knowing that i made peoples lives alot better, even for just one day...Im sure if you have done something really nice for someone in real life, you have experienced this feeling...Im agnostic, but i hope that if heaven does exist, its a 24 hour warm feeling state like what i received that day. All of my family said they felt the same way afterward, and it was the best gift they could have ever gotten.

I want to do something similar this year, but i also must stress the holidays arent the only time when vollunteering to help those more unfortunate than ourselves is needed. Its needed year round, its just that on the holidays, these main vollunteers REALLY feel the pinch the most.

My point to you is this. Please if you can, stop grinding exp, camping an item, or generally doing something that is self serving for one-two days, and try to help others out. No matter if you are disabled, short, fat, tall, skinny, there are many ways you can help others, especially at this most needed time. I guarantee you it will give you a feeling comparable to nothing else in life...
