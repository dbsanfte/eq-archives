Once you get to lvl 55 the cash just pours in, heck, it's hard NOT to make money at that level."

If you say so. That's not my experience at all (having recently returned to EQ starting from scratch). Most people will make 100's of plats via adventuring - and that's weak when gear upgrades and spells start to cost in the 10's of thousands. Not to mention that most classes kinda need spells to be effective - and it never hurts to have somewhat decent gear - and there's no reason to go without when you can make lots of easy plats and still level like crazy.

You can make big plats at later levels, usually by farming gear drops that sell well, but not all classes can farm well, not all items are just waiting to be plucked, farming can be tedious, and there really are no gear drop farming options for noobies.

The best way to make cash as a noobie, as mentioned above, is to farm tradeskill drops that drop from super low level noobie critters. A great example is spiderling silks, which drop from spiderlings, which are about level 2-4. One spiderling silk sells for 5-10p or thereabouts, depending on your server. So a stack of em is gonna net you 100-200p or so. You can collect several stacks of spiderling silks while leveling if you're in a zone that has them, or you can farm the heck out of them at later levels just to make cash. And spiderlings are just one of many critters a low level person or even noobie can kill to get profitable drops from.

Shadeweaver Thicket is a great noobie zone for getting rich quick. Many good drops there that sell from 1p-50p each (always depends on your server's market). Some examples:

- Shadeling Silk drops from the level 1 worms - it usually sells for 5g- 3p (I'd say 3p is average price on my server right now) and stacks in 20's.
- All rockhopper variants in the zone can drop low/med/high quality rockhopper pelts - even the level 1 buggers (although they drop em less than the bigger variants, which are still very low level). LQ/MQ hopper hides sell for various amounts - on my server they've averaged as high as 20p ea - but it's down to more like 10p ea now. HQ rockhopper hides can be used to make handcrafted backpacks, so their value is always higher (probably 20-40p range each). Rockhoppers in general drop hides WAY more often than wolves, bears, or cats.
- There are all kinds of skeleton variants around - bone chips don't sell as well as most, but they do sell quickly for 5g-1p usually - which is a lot more than they sell for to NPCs. Since you're gonna be in the area and killing them anyways you might as well make even more easy plats.
- Lesser Shades - drop lots of shadeling silk (mentioned above) and less commonly drop 'swirling shadows'. The swirlies usually sell for 35-50p each. If you can dominate the lesser shade heavy area (near the tunnel that leads to Paludal) you can make a few thousand plats worth of bazaarables in a couple of hours - awesome money for a noobie/lowbie. I used to hunt this area with my ranger when he was 12th (tracking is nice for farming) and make tons of noobie plats (in 2-3 hours I could rack up about 2-3k worth of bazaarables - which at the time was huge plats for me). In fact, you can make so many plats at this area that you can often find level 50+ people slaughtering the area - either to make plats or to save themselves plats collecting the stuff for tradeskilling.

Lots of good advice.

Tradeskill stuff is always a safe bet. No guarantee though.

You do not need high level gear to hunt - it helps but finding the right zone/area for your group is the key.

At higher level the money does flow in - hill giant farming is still popular I found on my recent return.

Yes concentrate on leveling up - especially if it is your first character (when life is harder) but I would modify the advice about 'not worrying about your gear' to 'not worrying to much about your gear - which is what I think vn_jurojin means as the reference is to uber gear.

Look in the bazaar. There is some extremely good items that are plentiful and as a result very cheap - and I do mean cheap. 1pp or less in some cases. Count the stats work out what it gives you. do not forget AC and HP. You will also find that nearly identical items which are just +1 better cost thousands. There is a soft spot for most items - not to hard to find either.

As for Runnyeye. I would stay out of it as well. Its always been a vicious zone. I can remember when the Beholder used to come from the bottom to zone into the gorge. run we shouted to this sitting troll as our group legged it. 'But I'm safe by the zone ....'
