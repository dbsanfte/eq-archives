Well, I've been everything from a "warrior of the 12th season" to a "Stormguard of the 12th Axe." Either way, people get the point. From a general rp standpoint, I always felt that a level was simply an indication that a character had gained enough experience in the skills of his chosen profession to become noticeably better at them. Thus, I used my sword alot, so I gained some expertise with it, perhaps was even taught a new move or two by a more experienced warrior, even a guildmaster. Some things I would probably not learn on my own, so the teacher aspect can be important. As far as EQ goes, I prefer to stay in character, and I communicate my level as a clear measure of my gained experience. If I'm looking for a group, I will typically send out a shout, stating something like, "Greetings! Are there any who would hunt with a Stormguard warrior of the Twelfth Axe?" Failing a response to that, I assume no roleplayers are around, and I drop into the "ooc 12th level warrior lfg" mode. Heck, I want to play! As for accepting the limits of EQ as a roleplaying environment, I find that, as long as I roleplay, I enjoy myself. I use ooc and tell sparingly, but as they are intended. I often join in ooc discussions, particularly when game related topics arise, but especially when I'm waiting for a boat and someone starts talking about Monty Python or Tolkein or Eddings or whatever. I use tell to talk to persons I would otherwise not be able to communicate with, or when talking to them in those other ways (ooc, shout) would be annoying to others.

On a final note, as I try to stay in character, I tend to take everything someone else says as "in character." Certainly, in the heroic and busy environs of the game, there are many different cultures, languages, and ways of life and speech. Accept the way others speak as being in character, offer the "kewl doods" a little rp understanding, and everyone can have fun. I tend to enjoy treating doods as though they are from a different, younger generation than I am, sort of the way every generation looks at their grandkids generation eventually. Those newfangled youngsters! "SoW??? Oh, you mean a Spirit of the Wolf?"

Good travels.

Now, me, I consider it along these lines. At least, for most of the races

Circle for the 'mage' casters- That is, spell circle.

Benediction for 'cleric' casters- one per spell circle, of course

As for the melee classes or hybrids..well, that's where it gets tricky. For my Rallos Zek-worshipping barby warrior, it's "Ravaging"..That is, a Warrior of the 34th Ravaging.

For my Iksar, it's even worse. His backstory includes a tendency to skin the corpses of worthy adversaries and taken the skins as trophies, so he uses 'Flaying', as well as his 'Guild' rank..That's what makes Iksar fun.
