This isn't an attack on Recon or any other guild. But a general observation. I don't care what system people come up with for the "most fair(tm)" approach to raids and not stepping on other guilds' raids.

Whatever it is, if it's followed for the most part, then it's at least fair on some high level because everyone follows it (or at least most of them).

But the real problem I have with large guilds on this server and others is that the "rules(tm again)" that everyone comes up with or that are generally accepted get disregarded by the same guilds that push for them the split second that those rules don't help them in their quest to kill everything.

I understand the desire for loot and whatever, but at some point you (or I at least) would think that cooler heads would prevail. It's much easier to just call off a raid if another group has made it to some threshhold that's been agreed apon than to try and charge past them or around them.

They have to know that it's more headache than it's worth. They'll probably have to explain to a GM sooner or later (later these days, never sooner) and anyone that's had to talk to a GM will probably tell you it's about as much fun as ... well, I can't come up with anything here.


So my question all along has been how can people put so much value on loot that they can't extend a bit of common courtesy to a raid already in progress and just cancel their own raid? Or in the case of raids for places that are generally scheduled, how can they justify not respecting that schedule in a system that they themselves have things reserved on?

It's always confused me.
