The Language Bug helped to quash a lot of Roleplay, once people got tired of Roleplaying "I can't understand what you are saying."

The Language system now seems to be working as well or better than intended, so once people break out of bad habits, we may see some more.

I would not hold a Guild at fault for OOC in Guildsay. Some people don't like the idea of Roleplaying between two PCs where one is in Toxx and one is in Faydwer.. :-) Also, Guildsay is the ideal channel for use in coordination, figuring out where people are, what they want to do, etc.

Now, 'total immersion' may be someone's ideal of Roleplay, but I for one have NEVER played a Tabletop RPG where nobody spoke a single Out of Character word. Wandering West Karanas looking for a Guildmate but refusing to speak to him OOC to find out where he is and spending an hour doing that and maybe never finding him is neither fun nor roleplaying.

Without a 'GM' character actively moderating events, the Players are required to interract on an OOC level as well in order to have a Roleplay scene. For example, Joe and Charles are playing together. In Joe's background his father was lost in a Gnoll incursion, and he is Roleplaying his search for his father and Charles has agreed to help. The is NOTHING in EQ itself that can support this plotline so they pair will have to collaborate OOC on where they go, what they find out etc.

If excessive chatter in Guildsay is distracting you, there is nothing wrong with turning it off! Just say 'I'm shutting off Guildsay for awhile, send me a /tell if you need to speak with me.'
