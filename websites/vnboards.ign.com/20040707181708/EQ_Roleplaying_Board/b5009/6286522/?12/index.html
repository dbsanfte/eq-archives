An Official RP server is needed in EQ. Making an official server in no way deminishes the RPing and hard work that others have done on other servers but it does give us an Official home as opposed to us hopping from one server to the next and trying to claim it for RP. It also allows new players to the game to know exactly where they can go in order to find other RPers.

When creating the next unofficial RP server the word never gets to all the RPers and as a result we've ended up with several unofficial RP servers now. The RP community ends up being spread out into pockets on all these servers with powergamers and d00ds always being the majority.

We're not advocating anything but a server designated for role-playing by Verant. Obtaining this can mean a lot of positive things for RPing and for EQ. The server can be used as a base which helps to support other RP communities on other servers. (ie helping out with events and such)

Please read the threads and lend your support. With a truly united RP community Verant will need to listen and for once we will not be ignored or brushed aside.

Sorry I'm not very eloquent but I strongly believe this is something that's needed.

Although I personally would be all for an RP server if it were feasible, there are a lot of problems that would arise that are difficult to deal with.

You need to be able to define role playing. Many people have tried, but they either describe it biased to how they themselves role play or they make it so general and vague it can be interpreted a million different ways. If you cannot define something in a way agreeable to everyone, how can you enforce it? Someone walking around talking in horrible Elizabethan.. "Thee shouldeth head towards ye north" and such.. well, that's absolutely terrible, but they are role playing.. there's no "stupid filter" for this game, nor will there ever be.

Even if one were to somehow be able to define role playing and set rules against it, you would still have to deal with the d00ds coming to the server just to annoy the "l@m3r RP r33tz". I've known some d00ds that could pass for good role players in a pinch.. who is to say they wouldn't do that when a Customer Service Rep was watching, and then go back to their ways afterward?

Next, there would have to be a special Guide/GM staff to enforce these special rules and perhaps run more dynamic quests. That means money... which goes back to the Premium server discussion, really. Would you be willing to pay more for a dedicated RP server should they find a way to make it happen?

Game mechanics wise, there are a few things that would severely hamper role play. For example.. in game, my troll shaman worships the Tribunal, and my dwarven warrior is a loyal Innoruuk follower. Of course, to the actual game, they worship Cazic-Thule and no one, respectively. Verant has stated repeatedly that religions cannot be changed.. this would present a large problem for people like myself, who play the characters off the beaten path.

Would it be straight PvP? Teams? Could you switch teams? Would it be Good vs. Evil, and would you be strictly bound by your race, or would there be quests you could do? PvP, when done correctly, can open up avenues of role playing one could never hope to achieve on normal servers.. but PvP also presents a whole slew of new issues.

If I thought it were doable at this point, I would be behind you all the way.. however, at this point in EQ's development, I don't believe there would be a way to create a strict RP server.

OOC:
I am now slightly disheartened, I have ordered a copy of EQ because I was seeking an online RPG where I could indulge myself in roleplaying. After all that's what the game declares itself to be a rolplaying game, just like the many others out on the market most of which support the roleplayer in a miniscule way, yet who always end up buckling under the pressure of the masses and feed the needs and selfish demands of the majority that are the powergamers and D00dZ.

One of the main reasons why powergamers and D00dZ have a claim to ignorance of roleplaying, is because for years now the Video gaming industry at large has brought products onto the market titled 'role-playing game'.

They took the tabletop/pen & paper rolplaying games and used them to make money. Guess what? Playing solo on a pc or game console in a fantasy/scifi/horror setting by yourself is not roleplaying... its just plain gaming.

It must be acknowledged that Roleplayers represent a minority in most graphical online gaming unless your playing one of the few text based MUD's which have endured solely because of roleplayers.

I would love to see for once in a graphical online game, an RP only server with the rules and guidelines set out at the start-up screen, and then enforced in game. If this could be done anywhere I would imagine (at this time) Everquest to be the only place. After all where is the meaning in 'questing' if all participants(players) are intent on beating the game or acquiring some coveted 'shiny' virtual items?

If no one seizes the opportunity and accepts the challenge of petitioning for an RP only server, if there are no votes, polls or signatures taken, if we are left to moan, complain or discuss on forums such as these.. what will happen to online roleplaying, how long will we have to endure the constant dissolving of our group/solo fantasy experience, by others who dont respect our gaming style/preferences...? The blame for what is just another computer game will rest upon the shoulders of the game developers and the roleplaying community.

Can we ever keep the faith and keep the spirit of roleplaying alive, can we stop the flow of the Tsunami of gamers that are wholly against roleplaying in online gaming...?

Will we roleplayers ever find a small corner to call home..?
