HAIL A HILL GIANT

It tells of my first meeting of a hill giant.

--------------------------------------------------



Hey, look over there!
Wow, what a shock!
An ogre that's bigger'n
than mountain-rocks.

He's taller'n mountains!
He's taller'n trees!
I gotta get closer
so he I can see!

Wow! I've never seen
something that strong.
I think I'll tell him
That wouldn't be wrong.

Hello there, sir.
I just wanted you to see
You're very strong.
More than I'll ever be.

Eh? What's that?
What's that Half-elf yelling?
Hill Giant? What's that?
Is it to me he's telling?

Yeow! That hurt!
That ogre... what a clout!
Could he be that hill giant
that guy was talking 'bout?

Another hit... I...
I'm beginning to fall
I think.... I...
don't like Hill Giants After all.


~ End

The wind blew in damp and cold as the waves caressed the shore.

Together as we walked the shore, the Princess and her Knight.

Behind again
gee, you're slow

Calling for help?

Paladin of light?

Here they come

just look, past my trap
which should break their necks

Warning 'em now?

seems the first member
has stepped in the trap

4 out of 5
not a bad kill count

will make it out.

Oops! There I go!

but didn't you just
have hundreds of gold?

No more? Suprise!

mised me again.

oops! I forgot!

I guess that's enough

Missed me, sir.

And now our love grows more and more with every passing day,

Winersheen

A crimson dwan must now be falling,
the western hills are bathed in light;
for me, a new grey darkness, paling,
as the dawn wars with the night.

Like sentinels of dark forboding,
guardians of forgotten dreams
of night, the pines are softly turning,

Whitewashed, blades of green are foundered,
the icy torrents smash them down.
And yet, Nature is not abhorrent,
to the flakes that from the gown,

Of Lady Winter, sparkling, flittering,
like the laughs of passing mirths,

drift until they kiss the earth.
