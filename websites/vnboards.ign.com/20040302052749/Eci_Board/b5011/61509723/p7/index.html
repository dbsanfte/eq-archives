I often two box my mage and my GF's cleric for LDoN groups. Typically, it's out of necessity, because clerics can be difficult to find. I always inform the group that I'm 2 boxing, and they typically don't mind.

Personally, I think this combo of classes may be the easiest to two box. For the mage, all you need is three hot buttons:assist, pet attack, and nuke. For the cleric, healing is equally easy. If a someone needs a heal, F3, heal. One additional hotbutton to make make each toon auto follow the other, and you're set.

In the event of a bad, 4 mob pull, sure, two different people on two different toons are more efficient than I am. But under normal pull situations, the group does not notice a difference with me 2 boxing.

That said, I find it odd, when people want to pentabox, or triple box an adventure all by themselves. In my point of view, it's the interaction between people that makes this game so entertaining. Granted, sometimes those other people can do stupid stuff to jeopardize the adventure, but for the most part, I enjoy interacting with the people I play with.

Skill two-boxing varries from person to person, and also can depend on what classes they play. I've two-boxed another Recon wizard in a number of sitations. Being so extremely familiar with wizards and our timing, playing a second has virtually ZERO effect on my efficiency of playing a wizard.

I've played a wiz and enc in exp situations before, since I'm using EQW on the same machine it can hit into my efficiency of playing my wizard in areas here and there when multiple mobs are pulled, but I can still maintain my overall DPS. Played a wiz and cleric in a number of raids, I can keep my own group healed as needed and play my wizard just fine, but being in a healing chain can have an effect.

I wouldn't discount people simply because they play two characters at once, I know people that can play both better than many can play one. Though there definitely are people out there that don't realize their limits and just plain suck at it.
