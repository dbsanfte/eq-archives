Benanan..there are several flaws in your statements, although some of the actual tactics are sound (the reasonign beind them that you give is not, tho). Do you play a melee character on Vallon? Doesn't sound like it.....

Turning attack on and off does NOT negate delay. You STILL have teh delay. Otherwise you could swing any weapon faster than teh delay on it.

Example....Fine steel dagger. A very fast weapon...19 delay. This means it swings every 1.9 seconds. And it swings ONCE. When it does swing, however, you get rolls for double attack and dual weild. All of that is instantaneous, and you can get 4 attacks as easily dual weilding and jousting as you can 2 attacks with a 2hander. But yes, you will tend to do less damage with each pass.

If turning off attack negated delay, tehn you could swing about 3 times per second, which would be around 3 delay....and more if you used some turbo pad to toggle attack.

You can turn very rapidly using mouselook...so yes you can turn fast enough to keep up, although it is hardware reliant to a certain degree.

The strategy behind circle strafing and jousting is to hit your enemy when they cannot hit you, and to reduce the effectiveness of things like riposte where you must be facing the enemy. Also, it is to negate the OTHER persons weapons delay if they wield faster weapons than you. Maybe that is what you intended to say.....

But dancing with a troll is quite counterproductive, too...as they regen. Buliwyn gave a very good example of that. You need to go in toe-to-toe at some point against someone that heals as time passes.
