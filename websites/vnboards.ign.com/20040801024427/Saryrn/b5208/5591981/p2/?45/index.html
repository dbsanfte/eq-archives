Boy am I glad I stayed on Bristlebane... No one is saying having guild only events will lead to turning Saryn into another Veeshan. What they are saying is that running dragons and planes first come first server will lead to another Veeshan server, and it will if there's one guild with a lot of people who either don't have to work, or live in Europe. The small guilds aren't asking if they can go on your raids. They're asking if they'll even get a chance to run their own raids. Sure, right now dragons may be up a while since you guys don't have a lot of people on your server and everyone's Kunark crazy, but what about when they do their planned future splits? You Veeshan people may not like the idea of having to sign up for something, but you know it sure the heck beats having 2 or 3 groups show up in Fear with a "force" racing to Cazic. Calendars may not be "fun" or "spontaneous", but they're about as fair as you can get when you have 500 people all wanting to do the same things. Give it a try, you may decide you like having things planned out, and not having to deal with pissing matches over who can do the plane or dragon.

On a side note, BB doesn't do any guild only raids for the most part because for one, no one guild has the sheer numbers to do it without prior planning to make sure they all can be on. Plus, most of us don't want to do it with just the guild. BB has strong inter-guild relationships. We have friends we know and trust our lives with that we'd want to have with us on big hunts. Once our ranks are filled with people we know, we bring in complete strangers to give them a chance. Sure, every once in a while you get an idiot that causes a mess, but it's rare, and you better believe they don't get invited anymore. We maintain lists to replace people who leave. We give people chances. In short, BB doesn't have uberguilds. That's why we developed the calendar system we have, to prevent conflict. People left BB for Saryn not because some uberguild was dominating planes and dragons, but because there were too many people on the server. It became extremely difficult to find a place to hunt at in the 40s where 30 other people weren't doing the same thing. Kunark, however, has solved a lot of that for now. A calendar can be very successful, but it requires the cooperation of all the guilds on the server to make it work. It only takes one group to say this is bunk, and crash people's raids to ruin it.
