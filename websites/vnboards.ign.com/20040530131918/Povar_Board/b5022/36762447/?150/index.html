Midgetto Vidgetto here, started back in Dec' 99. The game hurt my RL a lil, hehe. I ceased being me and became Midgetto Vidgetto Halfman Bastard 02(56rogue), but I loved every minute of it. Any old-school Povar guys still around? Anyone from Nightmares Asylum/Forces Unknown, Harmonium, Triton, RA, Guardian Brotherhood, or any of them other folks still around?

I cherrish those days, as my first MMORPG I was completely blown away. Starting as a tiny Halfling Rogue(with the italian black facial hair). I grew up in a small guild named, "Krypteia," until 47. I learned a lot being in a small guild, we stayed one close family for what seems like years. Being small, we weren't too wealthy or concerned much with lewt, but opted to enjoy the company of each other and have fun with the game.

On one of those days(lv 38 or so), I was sitting in Northfreeport when these two halfing fellas I was watching infront of me dropped a bag. Before someone else had the chance to run off with it, I snagged it up and gave it to him, said, "Ya dropped dis." And sat back down. He was pretty blown away that I returned the bag(was full of 45+ gear), that he gave me a belt as reward.

That's when I met Bedrenken Pigsticker, Halfman Bastard 00 of Nightmares Asylum. From then on we talked about being badass lil hobbit ninja's with the same face and all. He got me interested in raiding. Hehe, I remember sending him a tell and not knowing where Sol B is, with him replying, "shhh, one sec dude." Trying to keep organized on a Naggy raid.

Continued to level and talk with Bedrenken and other folks of NA. Then went on my first plane raid, to Plane of Sky with NA. I was doing the raid to meet and work with the guild..and to go to Sky hehe. Running around up there with full Ravenscale cept a Crested helm on looking ded sexah. Was one of the funnest experiences, had to go up to the next island to find a good place for the guild to set up at. We all seemed to get along well so I got enough votes and joined the guild. Was great the whole time.

I love'd being a part of Nightmare Asylums and taking down big mobs. And being in a guild who's members gave so freely to each other that when big items dropped, people who deserved it the most, got it...even people who might have wanted it more were more glad to see the other get it.

Was more happy to see a Mylokars BP go to a brother rogue, Tello(Halfman Bastard 01), than I would of been had wore it away from Trakanon.

I miss racing around Norrath solving the Ragebringer quest. We did the general in Kithcor 3 times in one night when me Tello and Bedrenken had our quest to that point. Was never so scared to loose so much work, but we killed him perfectly 3 times in a row. Bedrenken and Tello finished their's at the same time, and me a few hours later as 5th on the server I think.

I remember when we tried to kill Gomar's(Barb rogue) general with the help of several guilds and friends. Then seeing us all fight bravely loosing the battle. I died about 7 times trying to prevent the unthinkable. Some died 11 times...was the most amazing thing, complete selflessness from so many people. Felt horrible about it till he got his Ragebringer later.

Sorta miss the wars between guilds over mobs. And the server split that sorta helped that.

I miss about 1000 friends

I miss the game a lot.

Too many folks to name, but if ya played on Povar through Dec99-Fall01 then I prolly said, aloo! to yah at one point. But I'm missin ya all.

Lv 56 Halfling Rogue
Midgetto Vidgetto Jedi Knight

Hope that was enough rememboring of times for yah. Forgive grammar etc.

Oh, and I miss Alexfu Sheng, and being MONKSTYLE. Meaning that you play for the game and the people, not worrying about dying or loot. Just going in and whoopin ass all up n down. Was a lotta fun.

I mostly miss ruling all ass in the Arena and in duels. Muahah, /bow. cept time to time when a Shadowblade DTs me, or the occasional, really evasive/lucky pet kiting mage.

We had a giant arena battle once, it was FFA, but everyone sorta got into a group to live longer. But when 5 rogue buddies and I grouped, it wasn't so much for survival as it was for our plan to be the last 6 guys badasses left standing...then to kill each other. For the first 15 seconds we stayed grouped and hidden behind a pillar so that everyone else fighting would have their targets picked and be in process of killing them. Then we sweeped around the arena, taking out groups at a time.

One of us died early, and 5 rogues and one wizard were still standing. At this point, the rogues' danger became each other causing a collision of 5 skilled assassins. Sounds of parries and reposte echoed as pairs of rogues combat in a way not seen often. Jineren went for his pappy sorta, who at the moment was preparing to explode a halfling(the guy I was fighting). As he died the other two rogues' fight was near end with each at a 4th of health. I pulled away to let them finish their fight, and to regain health after a close fight down to a fourth before my combatent exploded. Gomar(barb) won his fight but was soon killed by Jineren, before he went to back to find his pappy.

The last 3 people alive were, me, Jineren(sp) and his RL father Wizard guy. I was still hiding along the wall trying to regain health. Jineren chased Pa for a few moments while the Wiz tried to unsuccessfully cast root. Jineren caught up to him in the center square.

From the distance, I could see the wizard's power lighting the area and brief glimpses of the Dark Elf as he weaved toward his pops, poisoned daggers ready. Wizdad managed to get off a spell before Jineren reached him, but it was largely absorbed by his resistance gear. Jineren leaped toward him quickly spinning around to his father's back. Trails of the wizard's spell followed the rogue's feet...smearing light in an arc around him, only granting the wizard enough time to realize what will already happen, and then to frown.

The battle didnt last long, as is expected when a rogue/wizard fight happens. Heh.. someone's either gonna get blown up, or cut in half before 20 seconds passes. And since Jineren jumped through his Oldman's torso daggers first, the wizard was only able to buy me about 15 seconds of healing time before falling. Leaving me at about half health and Jineren(2 lvs up) at 4/5ths.

After spotting me in the distance, which was soon after he heard me cussin mah healthbar. Knowing my time for prepping is near over, I do a quick equipment check, load on my poisons and brace for impact. Wasn't any point in hiding, as my opponent's skill and technique paralell my own. Meaning that trickery is prolly not an option and he's got see-hidden ready. We headed toward each other, evaluating our situation a final time. With 5/8ths health, fighting a stronger, full-strength brother Ninja, my chances of survival were looking slim... But being MONKSTYLE, the thrill of a brawl with the only remaining foe from a battle of 50+ was too much for me to resist. And being the underdog in a battle watched by those 50+ cheering friends only helped increase the rush felt by this kind of fight.

We both jumped to action at the same time, closing the gap between us quickly. Our sudden start and charging at each other seemed to lift the energy of everyone there bringing it to climax as we contacted. A nice display of skill was shown as we agressively fought for position. Each of us knew the other would only need one shot to control the rest of the fight. Because of this we each worked our circling strategies doing our best to compensate for a circling target. We each landed a few pokes and defended several.

The fast action pace and defensive skill made it difficult to judge who was winning. But as the fight progressed, the crowd quieted and became more involved in the action. We continued our chess game until our movement/strafing paths overlapped granting me a split second opportunity. With luck setting up the shot, I was quick to capitalize my chance with a backstab. Tearing his health down below 2/5ths, leaving me slighting ahead. But as lewt of a rogue as he was, the dagger lodged in his back didnt break his concentration or stop his movement. About 1 second after I landed my backstab, our movement paths overlapped again, only this time with luck leaning toward him.

It wasn't bad at all going down like that. Felt nice to put on a good show for everyone, but I doubt they had the chance to pick up on the details. Prolly didnt even notice the one second period that I came close to victory, or the actions in that small time by Jineren that kept him in position. I don't think anyone missed the moment when the blur of dark armor froze, showing the champion....standing there with his arm stuck in the hole made by his dagger thru my torso.

Winning is nice, but loosing a well fought battle is sometimes more rewarding.

Was hard to remember all the details, but I think I explained the situation pretty accurately. Just wish I could remember that damn wizard's name.

Guess I got a bit carried away, I didnt plan on writing for 4 hours when I started. As usual, please forgive my inability to write...at all. Im a math person.

But I wouldnt be suprised if none of this made sense, wrote it in parts over the past few hours.

Hope ya enjoyed it.

hey Jasmine, I can't believe you forgot to mention Ashoc.

I started out on Povar in May 99 right after I came back to the US from Bosnia. My best friend got me into it, he started the day it came out. He was a little gnome wizard named Ianaka. He told me all about this cool guild he was in called Ashoc. So I decided to make a half-elf ranger. I happened to be watching a South Park episode when creating my character, hence the name Roshambo.

I worked at leveling up my little ranger, but I couldnt group with my friend or his new guildmates because they were all around level 20. So my friend made a new character so he could play with me. Thats when Ozmodius the necromancer was born. About that time another of our friends started to play, Kadthadius the wizard. The 3 of us were all in Ashoc and we pretty much levelled and played together everyday.

Now back then, Ashoc was actually becoming a pretty large guild. It was no where near as powerful as SA or Harmonium, but with the addition of a few more warriors (ashoc had alot of rangers) we could have made a bigger dent on the history of Povar. Ashoc was the rent-a-cleric guild for a while. We had lots of clerics and rangers, many went on to bigger and better things with bigger guilds, some forming there own. I'm sure if I mention the dreaded "Raid on Mistmoore", anyone who was in Ashoc at the time will have a chuckle.

Here is a link I found to an old Ashoc web page that is still up. http://www.geocities.com/TimesSquare/Metro/2782/order3.html
Take a look at the guild roster. Some of those people are still playing on Povar. Some, like Jasmine, Medik, Ryche, Anaxagoras, Ator moved on to Xev.

In my opinion, those were some of the best times I have had in EQ. And even though Ashoc, the guild, has been gone for well over 2 years now, I'm pretty confident in saying that anyone who was in the guild still feels some kinship with their former guildmates.
