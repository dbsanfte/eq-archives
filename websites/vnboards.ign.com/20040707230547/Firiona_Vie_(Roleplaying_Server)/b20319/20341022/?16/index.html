In other threads, Mheddik's been predicting that our server's economy will dwindle and eventually bottom out altogether.

Personally, I think it may be too early to make this kind of prediction. It's predicated on the premise that there isn't going to be a relatively constant influx of new players, who invariably need low-level items they can afford.

It's also predicated on the notion that people won't sell items to Merchants when they no longer need them, if they can't find a player to buy the equipment. Selling items to merchants effectively takes items out of the economy.

Granted, I've noticed some problems. Sunday afternoon, I was trying to sell Raw Silk in Kelethin, and got dribbles and drabbles for buyers--but I did get buyers. In part, I used fairly aggressive tactics--/inspecting casters as they came by the Bank and saying, "It looks like you could use Raw Silk sleeves" or something of the sort, in addition to /auctioning. I suspect that, in part, the reason stuff doesn't sell quickly is that a lot of the low-level characters who should be buying tradeskill armor/jewellry/etc. aren't aware of what they could use. On more than one occassion, I had to tell a caster why she wanted Raw Silk.

Speaking as a relative 'newbie' to the game myself, I know it's not enough just to /auction "Uber Short Sword 20/25 proc: Power of Uberdom!" If you want to find a buyer, you have to tell them why they want what you're selling. (And if your response is "They should know why they should want it" and you wonder why you can't sell it, you're not a good salesman.)

Also, in my experiences on both Tunare server and Sullon Zek server, prices are very relative. You could fetch 1 pp per Crushbone Belt on Tunare, and barely 5 gold per belt on Sullon Zek. That it's 3 gold per Belt on Firiona Vie is almost a crime--you can nearly get 2/3 that much turning them in and selling the patchwork you get. I presume powerleveling played a part in the inflation of the price on Tunare.

Lastly, I don't think Mheddik has taken into account the effects the trivial loot code is going to have on the economy. If there are items that are relatively common to 15th level characters but impossible for 30th level characters to acquire on their own, the price on these items will become inflated, especially if they're items necessary for quests.

There appear to me to be a myriad of ways for any given item to leave the server's economy (or simply not enter it in the first place): players leaving the server with their items and destroying items, as Mheddik has noted, but dropping items will also remove them from the economy, as will the trivial loot code and selling them to merchants. We're not necessarily talking about an economy of ever-increasing supply and dwindling demand.

Any members of the Traders Union have any thoughts on this? You'd probably have more direct experience with this than I do.

Oh yeah, and...

/derail



Anyway, back to the topic of Economics. Mheddikis right about the economy of this server. It will bottom out faster than other servers. The one-character-per-server rule ensures this.

On a normal server, people keep the economy going with twinks and secondaries. This happens because high level player A acquires an item they can't use. They elect to sell it or trade it for something they can use, or something a secondary of theirs can use.

Player B has 6 characters on his account. He sits in EC, listening to auctions, and finds the item from Player A. Said item will be great for Player B's level 26 secondary character, so they barter, and he buys it.

This is commonplace on a normal server, and it's a reason many items stay in demand for a longer period of time.

Twinking, and secondary characters (non twinked) are a driving force behind the economy of a server. Here, you have no secondary characters, and no twinks.

So, player A now has an item he can't sell, because after a while, nobody is going to need it, or want it.

Think about it: the reason some mid and low level gear still gets sold on normal servers is because there are mid and low level characters that will buy those items (or high level characters that will buy those items for their mid and low level characters). This happens because people have *8* character slots per account.

Do the math. It takes a long time to level a character to 60. Most people play multiple characters on their account. Ask most high level players about their accounts, and this is what you get: A character 50+ and then 4-7 characters between level 1-50. I have 5 characters between levels of 20-35, and they all need, want, and buy gear.


Assume for a second that the average number of active characters per account on a normal server is 5. I think that's a low estimate myself, but let's roll with it.

Assume 2500 people per server, active accounts.

That's 12,500 characters fighting, looting, buying and selling goods. That's a lot of people wanting and needing gear, and that's why items maintain price for a long time.

Now look at FV. Assume 2500 people on the server (which there aren't, just look at the rankings of the server).

Now you get 1 character per account.

That's... Drum Roll Please.....


2500 active characters.

One-fifth of a normal server. One-fifth the number of people wanting and needing gear.

If you're a level 50 melee and just looted a caster item, instead of having as many casters as a regular server has, you now have one-fifth the number of casters to sell to...


Now, before you say I don't know jack about economics, I know: I'm leaving out supply (which will also be cut down one-fifth by the number of people fighting on the server, and thus looting items), and I'm not even factoring in the loss of items through destruction or sale to vendors (which I don't think really matters, because people don't destroy good items, and they don't sell them to vendors unless by accident, or the item no longer has value and can't be sold).

Any economy is driven by supply and demend. Here, the demand is going to be significantly reduced. The supply, well, it won't have the same fast flow of a normal server for sure. But the key here is, when does supply outgrow demand? On a normal server, the supply of some items still has no exceeded the demand. I think that's because, simply, of the number of secondary characters on the server.

Here, I would be willing to bet that the supply for many items will outgrow the demand for them MUCH faster than on a normal server.

The market for many items will bottom out faster on this server than any other server.

In my opinion, the economy of the server will involve a 'moving average' that coincides with the ever-increasing 'average level' of the server's primary (and only) characters. What I mean is that at some point, FV will reach 'mature' age, and the majority of characters will be level 45-50+. At this stage, all of the items obtained will fall into this 45-60 range, and hence there will be a thriving high level economy as people will want to upgrade their set of gear.

For the 1-40s, new equipment will be available in one of two forms:
1) they will get to adventure and obtain the midlevel item for themselves.
2) they will be able to purchase the "dregs" (but still very nice by a level 1-40 standard) of the high level economy. I.e. for each inventory slot, there may be a "chain" of item progression representing upgrades. The bottoms of these chains will eventually not find a high level buyer, and hence will become available to the 1-40s.

I don't see anything wrong with either of these two options. Obviously once the glut of the server matures, there will be much fewer characters that fall into the 1-40 range. (i.e. say a year from now).

I think that everyone is in violent agreement with everyone else, but I personally don't think that the rare item market will be as impacted as one might think.

Certainly stuff like bronze armor is going to bottom out more quickly on FV - it drops from mid-high level creatures in open areas (Dreadlands, for instance), and it will be collected casually by players who are just grinding experience. The lowbie market for bronze will be shrinking even as the numbers of players in those areas increase. Much supply, little demand.

There are two other areas where this will get screwy, however:

First, the "under con" items. These are the items that drop from lower-powered mobs but should be from more challenging creatures. The Charred Guardian Shield might fall into this range, as (on this server) would the components necessary to create the Testament of Vanear. As the TLC kicks in, the supply of these items will decrease, but the demand will remain strong.

More important than the above are the items that could be farmed on other servers (creating steady supplies) but will require a hefty group on FV. Pulling an example out of the air - the Fighting Batons from the Lumpy Goo in Dalnir. On other servers you can usually find the goo camped by an individual (Iksar necros on my old server), but on FV you'll never see that, it'll take a decent group to clear the room.

And then there is the population of the server to take into account. I may have fallen behind the level curve (meaning I am not in zones that the "glut" is in), but it seems like it'll be tough to find a full group at upper levels. I suspect that the rate at which high-level items (from LGuk, Seblis, etc.) flow into the game will be slower here than elsewhere. That will not prevent "The Mheddik Effect," but it will delay it.

Anyway, I'm a cleric, the poorest of all classes until 39, so I will not be contributing to the economy for some time anyhow.
