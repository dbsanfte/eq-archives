your "Backspace" key should open the map when you are in Norrath. eqtoolbox.com (not positive on address) has a section where you can download detailed maps of every zone mapping is allowed in.

EQ is a totally different beast than it was at and before velious although the premise (kill beast get loot, kill tougher beast get loot, rinse repeat) has not changed at all. The game is much easier to progress in now and therefore you find less intelligent players at end game frequently.

Unless you were wearing the very best of ToV armor, it is unlikely that your gear is sufficient. You would be well advised to do some shopping in the bazaar.

Edit: Found this in another post ...

Hot Spots
-----------------------
Try out the new Hot Spot zones which give a boost to Experience while adventuring in them, in a similar manner to Paludal Caverns. The current Hot Spots are:

- The Castle of Mistmoore (20th to 30th level)
- The Lair of the Splitpaw (35th to 45th level)
- Dulak's Harbor (40th to 45th level)
- The Tower of Frozen Shadow (30th to 35th level)
- The Gulf of Gunthak (35th to 40th level)
- Hate's Fury (55th to 60th level)
- The Crypt of Nadox (50th to 55th level)
- The Torgiran Mines (45th to 50th level)

"Are there any updated leveling guides for any classes that include the newer expansions?"

See my hot spot info above.

LDoN adventures are based upon the level of your group so you can do any dungeon there after a certain level .. someone? I was 65 when LDoN came out but I believe its somewhere in the 20's-30's range on up to 65.

Planes of Power are all level 46+ to zone in. Once you zone in the progression goes (roughly):

46 - 55 Justice, Innovation (best in factory), Disease, Nightmare (hobgoblins are good)

55 - 62 Storms and Valor

62 - 65 Bastion of Thunder and Halls of Honor

There are many other PoP zones but they require key/questing. The ones I listed only require the lowest level noted to zone in.
