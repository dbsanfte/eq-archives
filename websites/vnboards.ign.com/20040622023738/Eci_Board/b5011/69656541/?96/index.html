Well - a couple weeks ago - I posted on this topic. The issue is people leaving the game. Th fact is that LOTS have left.

Some groups have recovered faster than others.

I would not rush to blame anyone for the problem - I really don't think this is a leadership issue. I think it is a game content issue. Yes - the time of year is a factor - BUT - so is the game design and direction. Check out Smedley's letter - and you get a feel that there is a big problem someplace. I predict an expansion will be delayed again - until after the first of the year perhaps - or at least closer to Christmas.

In every team-oriented game you tend to build up a core group that provides the innate strength. And in EVERY game the test of the team comes when the core strength retires, dies, or changes teams.

The high end guilds are largely made up of extremely focused - committed gamers. They are hand picked - they share a common drive. They are a team of "Michael Jordans". In the past - that is what made them great. They all played a lot - knew their class - were dependable - listened - etc etc etc. There is one-downside to this type of player. They are very demanding. They insist on challenging content. If they dont get enough - they go play another game that fills the need. Its almost like Barry Sanders getting tired of the game and just quitting football one day - what a shock to his team. Some teams recover - some don't.

Don't be too quick to blame leadership just yet. Give the folks still playing some time to sort things out. This game makes it very easy to quit - you just dont sign on anymore. It also makes it pretty easy to comeback - you sign on.

One thing I have concluded that has changed in a major way. The notion of instanced zones is allowing several groups to advance in parallel. That concept is causing more competition - and is probably a good thing.
