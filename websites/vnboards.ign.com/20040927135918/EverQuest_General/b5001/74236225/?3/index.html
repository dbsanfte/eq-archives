I was once a hardcore AC player (4+ yrs from the retail launch) and tried EQ about 3 yrs ago.

At that time it didn't really get me hooked, I played just the free month, and never got higher than lvl 2-3ish. Since I now have tried other games, and only one (CoH) has finally gotten me back into an MMORPG, I thought I might try out EQ again, just to see how things are, besides, I've been craving a Fantasy setting agian, and WoW is pretty far from release.

I am not a very picky gamer, but here are my interests:

Heavy, Heavy Content. If there is nothing to do in the game, then you won't keep my interest for very long.

Easy enough interface to learn and not get frustrated with.

3rd person view, sorry, I reserve 1st person for FPS games.

Easy enough to get started, but becoming increasingly challenging as I move up in levels.

I'm ok with grouping, but I do need some solo-ability, otherwise if I group I hope it's easy enough to get one. I never have a problem getting one in CoH, doesn't always happen within 15-30 minutes, but waiting for a group for more than an hour seems like a waste to me.

No forced roleplay, I Like roleplaying, and would very much like to do a LOT of it for once (since I rarely do), but if I slip and say something non-roleplaying, that I don't get a bunch of 12 yr olds calling me on it. If I say something out of the norm for a roleplay, It's because I am still firmly attached to reality, and this is ***just a game***.
Accidents happen ya know?

I think that sums up my fears about trying the game again. If anyone cares to comment, please do, I'm being exceptionally open-minded at this time, so please, let me know how it is.

1)Heavy, Heavy Content. If there is nothing to do in the game, then you won't keep my interest for very long.

-----------
Tons of things to do in EQ, and lots of things to do leveling wise as well. Once you hit max level (65 until next month, Omens raises it to 70) you still have Alternate Advancement abilties you can obtain.
---------


2)Easy enough interface to learn and not get frustrated with.

--------------------
Hrm.. some people complain about this, but if you've played other MMORPGs before, it shouldn't be too bad to get used to. You can also heavily modify it to suit your tastes, or download a pre-packaged skin ( www.eqinterface.com )
---------------------

3)3rd person view, sorry, I reserve 1st person for FPS games.

---------------------
Default is first person, but there are multiple third person views, and I believe you can just roll the mouse back to get a DAoC/CoH/AC sort of third person view. (I disabled that ability, so my memory of exactly what the camera is like is a little fuzzy)
---------------------

4)Easy enough to get started, but becoming increasingly challenging as I move up in levels.

-------------------
Yep, and the new tutorial should make it even easier to get used to.
-------------------

5)I'm ok with grouping, but I do need some solo-ability, otherwise if I group I hope it's easy enough to get one. I never have a problem getting one in CoH, doesn't always happen within 15-30 minutes, but waiting for a group for more than an hour seems like a waste to me.

--------------------
Hrm. Well, depends on your class here for both group desirability and soloing ability. Druid, Bard, Shaman, Necro, Beastlord, Enchanter and maybe Magician are top soloers offhand. I'd rule out Bard, Enchanter right away for a newbie really. Magician, Beastlord, Shaman might be a little more difficult, but workable. Necro and Druid are both quite easy to level up solo as a newbie, and offer value to a group, I'd lean more towards a druid having an easier time finding a group of the two.

Shamans, Bards and Enchanters are quite welcome in groups. Beastlords are decently welcome, and magicians I'm not too sure about, I think they're good in a group, but I don't often group with them.
---------------------

6)No forced roleplay, I Like roleplaying, and would very much like to do a LOT of it for once (since I rarely do), but if I slip and say something non-roleplaying, that I don't get a bunch of 12 yr olds calling me on it. If I say something out of the norm for a roleplay, It's because I am still firmly attached to reality, and this is ***just a game***.
Accidents happen ya know?

-----------------
Well most people don't roleplay on the majority of servers, some rare people do. Fironia Vie (spelling...) is the roleplaying server, and it's got some server side changes that make it more "roleplaying friendly", and it's more of a roleplaying enforced server. All the rest are in no one enforced, or forced.
---------------

To sum it up, I think your biggest problem MAY be the view, but it shouldn't be too bad. Secondary could be the soloing thing, depending on how you like those classes. Other classes can solo, just not nearly as efficiently, though with Omens you might be able to level up with the Task System (on release it will be designed for level 50 and lower, with after release possibly adding higher level tasks). Not sure how that will work exactly though.

The UI is WONDERFUL, the customization is great, it may take some getting used to, but out of like every MMORPG release so far, I'd say it has the best.
