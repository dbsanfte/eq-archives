as with any addiction denial is the first sign that there is something there

I freely admit I was addicted to EQ pretty badly up until two years ago, I just wanted to log in as much as I could.

Now part of it was undoubtadly down to other aspects of my life, but EQ, and its mechanics were a large factor, no other piece of computer software has ever made me so magnetically glued to my PC as EQ did, and none have since.

Quitting playing EQ was the single best thing I have ever done to improve my life in general.

Whilst everything is ok in moderation, if you start to find you would rather be in Norrath then in RL the majority of the time you are not at work / school then I'd recommend you re-evaluate your outlook on things, I know I did!

the game is designed to make you spend massive amounts on-line, don't let it dictate your free time, liek everything else strike a balance, maybe you can do what i couldn't and find a way to play EQ in moderation, but I found that it was all or nothing....

Date Posted: 2/17/03 1:20am Subject: RE: What's EverQuest done for your real life?
This thread inspired me to sign up and share my love of everquest.

I came across Eq when it first went live. I was married for 3years. Had decent paying job as a data processor. Played a few games off and on but nothing major. Then I heard a few people talking about everquest. So i decided to go out and purchase it to see what the big deal was. After I purchased Eq my whole life was forever changed.

I remember starting my first character in the new exciting world of Norrath. The first time I played nonstop throughout the weekend. Boy was I tired at work the next week. While at work I found myself only thinking about Eq, and what I could do to improver my status. Next I found myself calling in sick, and making up excuses why I couldn't come in. Heh, I probably should have put a little more thought into it....now that I look back how many realitives can one really have die in a month. I could no longer sleep at home because I knew the world that awaited me. When I did go to work I would lock my door and sleep on my floor behind my desk. Anyways I ended up getting fired. However when I was fired, I wasn't the bit depressed. I raced home back to my new found world, and began my journey once again.
Eventually my wife found out about it and gave me a choice. Either I quit the game and get a new job or she was going to leave me and get a divorce. Well needless to say I couldn't leave Eq. I tried explaining this to her, but it fell on deaf ears. She just didn't understand, and I didn't want to even be around someone who couldn't appreciate what had given me new life. Some people are born again christians....I liked to consider myself a born again eq'r.

Well life was great. I would literally go weeks without sleep. Wearing the same clothes for days and days. Eating and sleeping was a burden. I lost 15 lbs due to very little eating in one whole week. Eventually though I was evicted from my apartment becuase I didn't have the money to pay for my rent anymore. The money I did have I spent on two new computers to run two more accounts. I could have my own in game group. Since the eviction I asked my parents if I could move back in with them. They had no problem with this. They were concerned with me, and wanted to spend more time with me. Some people may feel uncomfortable being a 34yr old male moving back in with their parents, but to me this was a blessing. Now I had no payments what so ever. The only thing I had to deal with was my mothers occassional whine of when I was going to find a new job and get married again.

This is why I love eq so much. One day I told my mother I was engaged to be married. She was so excited for me. She asked who it was and when she was going to meet the family. I told her sure np come into my room. When she did I told her that she could see her right on my screen. She stared with a bedazzled look and said, "the only thing I see is a big fat green looking thing". I laughed so hard at her and said, "You better not let sarah hear you call her fat". I explained the situation to her. That sarah(a female ogre) I had meet in the game. We were due to get married in East Freeport that weekend. She said, "what the hell are you talking about". I told her it's an in game marriage, and perfectly legal. I told her that I had known Sarah for 4 months now, and that we both agreed that we were perfect for each other. Granted our relationship had it's up and down like any other, but we both had the desire to make it work.

I invited my mother and father to the wedding that Saturday. They both sat on my bed when that little druid gnome married us. Man that was one of the happiest days of my life. We had a huge turnout at East FreePort. Sarah and myself both had prepared our own separate vows...then the moment of truth "I do". Then we /kissed. Makes me feel all warm inside thinking about it right now. A few years have passed along with many expansions. Planes of Power just takes my breath away. I have lvld every class to atleast 50, and have explored every zone.

I wish people wouldn't knock eq so much. It has changed my life for the better. I have made so many virtual friends including my wife! My parents think I have lost my mind haha :=) They just don't understand. I hope someday they will try eq out, and I know if they will just try it they will see the "real" world.

Some may think, and many already have thought that my life is unproductive or I have wasted these years of my life. To all of you nay sayers out there I leave you with this "If you are willing to die for the new world then I will accomidate you....forever in Norrath!
