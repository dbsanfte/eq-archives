A large barbarian swaggered in.

"Damme...this place is a mess!"

Yet it was much as he remembered it from years of old, when laughter pealed and arguments raged and the ale flowed freely. Now the tables stood empty, dust-covered, a few broken and lying on their sides; cobwebs hung from the rafters, still blackened from years of pipe-smoke; ancient steps led upstairs where once he'd taken a woman or two.

Memories filled the barbarian with warmth as he stood and regarded his old haunt. Almost, he could see the shapes of patrons once so familiar: Gutbludpoopoo, Jaunzuria, Lisianth Starfall, L'vanon, Ulvanth...he grinned for no good reason.

There was someone there, a youth who seemed to be staring around curiously. "Aye, lad, 'tis a sad state this place is in!" he rumbled, his boots loud on the dirty floor as he picked his way around the ancient chairs and fallen tables. "But not always was it so...dunno about you, but I need a drink. Maybe that cursed old barkeep left something back here, eh?"

He rummaged under the counter of the bar and pulled out an old bottle filled with some unappetizing-looking swill. "Ah!" he announced, well pleased. "There's still some Trollish ale around!"

He regarded the youth. "I go by th'name of Tellwyn Teed. Will ye drink wi' an old soldier, lad?"

For a few seconds a strange, chilly wind seems to course through the room. Curiously enough, it seems to carry along the sound of lost voices, of laughter and threats, romance and old hates, all entwined into a murmur of times long gone by.

On a shadowy corner, a dusty pile of rotting clothes shifts around. "Probably the wind", you think. Still, for an instant you thought you could see a figure lurking there, white hair surrounding a gaunt face, pale eyes that seemed as dead as the deeper pits of Befallen, and a bony hand that pointed towards you.

Then the wind is gone, the dust settles, and time continues its slow march. And somewhere, very far in space and time, someone, or rather something, laughs.
