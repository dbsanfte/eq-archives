As many have said, Complete Heal should have never been introduced. The reason that at high levels druids and shaman felt so useless for healing was due to the way the game was designed as a direct result of the existance of Complete Heal.

What should have occurred for clerics long ago, was instead of CH, they should have gotten a 1000 point heal at 39, then along the lines of a 1600 point heal at 49, then in the 50's they should have gotten 2 to 3 heals, each getting better, with the last one at level 60 being like 3500 points. Druids and shaman should have gotten superior heal about when they did, 51/52, and then the 1000 point heal 56/57.

If that had been the case, pre-Kunark, not much would have changed. Kunark would have been different, Velious would surely have been different, and Luclin would be something alltogether different. Complete Heal was a factor in all expansion design. I'd lay money that during the design of each expansion, at least once, some developer said, "Wow! This is the greatest encounter I've ever built!" Then the guy in the cube next to him leaned over and said, "Pretty nice, but 1 group with a warrior and a cleric in it can kill that."

The way the game exists, excluding mana from the equation, if a warrior can maintain agro, and the monster cannot kill him in 10 seconds, it only takes 2 people to kill it.

Now, the CH change isn't going to affect the game that we know right now. If the warrior on your raids has 7000 hp, then your complete heal will work just like it always did. If your warrior has 8000 hp on the other hand, then your pauses between CHs on the rotation will have to get smaller.

Hopefully, this change will affect the game enough that we will see less mobs that hit for 700 or more while quadding, flurrying, and rampaging, as the raid whittles down its 100k to 1mil HP, and will also keep Verant from having to include "The Plane of things with 2 million hit points that quad for 2k per hit and death touch zones they aren't even in" in the new expansion.
