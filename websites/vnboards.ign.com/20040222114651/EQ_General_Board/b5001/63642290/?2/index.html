I played Asheron's Call for three years and enjoyed my time but as the friends who made it worthwhile faded and left so did I. What makes an MMORPG entertaining is the people you play with and the only real difference between one MMORPG and another is how they let you play together. Now I'm enjoying my time in EverQuest, it's nothing like Asheron's Call at all. There are many different races and classes and your access to spells, skills, armor, weapons, and other items is dictated by your them. Unlike Asheron's Call, you are inherently specialized and must rely upon others to attain your full potential. You can solo and solo quite well, however, unlike Asheron's Call you can not be 100% self-sufficient and still have all the greatest buffs and abilities.

In my opinion EverQuest is a lot larger and has a lot more depth. It is definately more conducive to player interaction, Asheron's Call had a very poor player driven economy and Asheron's Call 2 attempted to rectify that and failed miserably, EverQuest does the job very nicely. EQ is not like AC or AC2, if you really want the spiffy graphics you're looking in the wrong place because EQ's are even more dated than AC's. We must remember, however, that there is going to be an overhaul of that in the next few months, but of course we don't know what the improved version will look like.

My advice, buy EQ. It's fun, a lot of fun, it did right a lot of the things AC and AC2 did wrong and it is a very rich experience. What makes MMORPGs fun is people, and EQ does a fantastic job of bringing people together.
