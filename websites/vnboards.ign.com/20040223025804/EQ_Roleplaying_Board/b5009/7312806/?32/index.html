Now, many non-roleplayers say "Ding! I'm level 33!" Which usually gets most Roleplayers to think "Geez, there is no such thing as a level!"

What most roleplayers (Including myself) think is that: There is no strange figure standing outside the world, lookign at a monitor, and seeing "level 32" on a screen. They're right... from a roleplaying perspective.

But now let's look at this from a roleplaying perspective... RandomCharacter has been killing many rats and snakes. Unknown to him, his guildleader has been watching him. Upon returning to his guildleader, the guildleader says:
"Congratulations, RandomCharacter! I think you have proved that you have enough skill to represent our guild further. I now promote you to rank SomeLevel, and present you with the skill of RandomSkill due to your gaining this rank. Congratulations again!"

Doesn't that make some sense?

Any replies would be appreciated.

OOC - Glad to see this topic is under discussion, I have my little bit to add to it myself. Well I figured I'd try and either screw people up or make them think by doing this, I did not intentionally copy anyone's idea and I thought of this myself, but its not unlikely that someone may have done the same.
First I will start off by saying I consider my Halfling Cleric, his rank is...
3rd Order of Acolytes, 2nd Rank
Someone would look at this and EIther think I mean level 5, or, level 32 right? Well...
I consider that each 'Order' in my opinion is the level in which you get your new spells
and every level thereafter until the next spell level is a rank
So if at Level 9 I get Center and my other level 9 spells I am 3rd Order, and since I am level 11, thats two levels past level 9, I'd have a rank of 2.
So the highest place you could get in the 3rd Order is actually 4th Rank, because the next level you make after that will promote you to the 4th Order! Interesting? Or stupid? You choose.

I notice some people tend to assume that their level indicates their age. I don't see it as such. Druids get spells at level 1, but I was not 1 year old and killing snakes. Now for non casters, the same can apply. Certain levels for certain skills. This can also apply to the hybrid classes like rangers, paladins and shadow knights that seems to gain spells like every 9 levels.

Examples:

Well if you mean my abilities compared to a foe, I'd say if it were any more than a Gnoll Watcher, I'd be wishing I had the spirit of the wolf upon me.

Ah, I wouldn't trust my abilities to Gnoll Watchers. They usually get the better half of me, so I stick to the younger Gnoll Pups for an easy killing.
