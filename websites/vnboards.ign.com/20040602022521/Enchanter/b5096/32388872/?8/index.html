What I have done (LVL 44 enchanter) is move to the very back of the book, all spells I will never use again (like the lower lvl dots that dont stack with their higher lvl counterparts, etc). Then, page 1 and 2 are my DD spells, 3,4,5 are my buffs, waht I referrer to as "tools" or frequentle used spells are on 7-8 (like gate, root, mez, bind, etc). Having things clumped together into types of spells seems to help me a lot. After a while, I am able to jump to just about any spell group really fast ("/book 7" having its own hot key) and load up what I need. All of my illusions follow my "tools" page and are ordered pretty much in the order that I got them. I tried putting things in order of "spell level" but find reorganizing evertytime I bought spells to be a real pain, and I would forget where stuff was. Having clumps of like spells together has worked well for me.

Pages 1-2 should be filled with your most used spells, such as Tash, Mez, DD, Debuff, Clarity, Gate, Root, Rune, and one or two illusions (Human is a good one to put here, it is perfect for most general needs).

After that, I group spells by type. DDs get a section, Illusions get a section, Buffs/Debuffs get one or two sections, and so on.

The idea is that in most occasions that you will want to change spells quickly, the spell you want will be on pages 1-2.

I figured out how to change around the location of spells in my spell book. This is what I did to organize.

I took the 8 spells that I always have up, and put them, in the order I like them, on page 1. The other spells that I use, but not as frequently (like invisibility), I put on page 2. The buff spells, and my latest pet spell went on page 3. I took spells that I was no longer using, like suffocating sphere (since I now have choke) and moved them to the back. I also put the lower level pet spells here. When I get an update to a spell, I take the new one, and put it in the place of the old, less powerful one, and then move the old one to the back.

The biggest benefit that I can see, is that when I get killed, I don't have to spend 10 minutes searching through the spell book, trying to figure out what spells I had up, and where.

No matter which character I'm playing, I always have Page 1 with the spells that are currently on my spell bar in the correct order that they appear there. The prime advantage of that is that when (not if ) I die I don't have to rack my brain to remember which spells I was using and then search through the book to locate them prior to memorising them. That is quite handy with 8 characters as it's easy to forget who had what memorised!

I then have my current buffs and essential utility spells on Page 2. With buffs I tend to have one slot on the spell bar that I rotate between a commonly used spell (root for example) and then forget it when I need to buff and switch each buff into it in turn. I find it best to use the bottom slot for that as it's easily remembered and if possible I put as a regular spell there something that's comparitively low level so that it takes less time to memorise it each time I wipe it to refresh my buffs.

According to the class I play I then bunch any major sets of spells together, for example with my enchanter all my illusion spells, with my druid all my self-port spells and separately all my group port spells. Disused spells tend to gravitate to the back of the active part of the book, but I suppose I should move them right to the back or even delete them as that is now an option.

We all use different methods, my only main piece of advice (apart from the way I use Page 1 which I certainly commend) is that you adopt similar layouts for all your characters if you have more than one. That way you're less likely to mess up in a crisis. Crisis? What crisis ?
