Registered: Jun 01
 		Date Posted: 10/6/01 5:09am Subject: RE: Is there anything GOOD about a cleric?
Okay, gonna make it short and sweet.

Do you want to.....

1.Deal Damage?
If so, then DON'T be a cleric. Clerics jobs ivolve buffing and healing, and the occasional stunning and rare nuking if things get out of hand. As a cleric you will not ever be a puller or a tank.

2.Always have Money?
Then Don't be a Cleric. Above lev 49 money comes in a lot easier due to your res spells, but below that, you will always be strapped for cash. The reasons for this are simple. Many of your good spells require expensive components to cast, and the spells that u must purchase will be expensive. On top of that, you need armor.

3.Be primarily a solo player?
The don't be a cleric. Beyond level 12 soloing a cleric becomes pointless. You just simply can't muster the damage output necessary. Necro's seem to be a common choice of soloing individuals.

4.Be the most vital individual in your group?
Then BE a cleric. You have been granted with the divine abilities to restore health, protect your party from damage, and in extreme situations (at higher levels) to reach into the afterlife and resurrect your fallen comrades. A high level group without a cleric is not usually a good idea. At times, you will have to decide who in your group lives, and who dies. For example, say your group pulled two mobs. One attacks one of your two main tanks, and the other goes for the enchanter, after a while, you have enough mana for one more big heal, and both your tank and your ench are near death, who gets the heal? Well, (imo, and others will disagree) I heal the enchanter. Why? Crowd control. If I heal the ench and someone else can either stun or root his attacker, he can try to mes it. This would especially hold true in adungeon, where aggro is likely. This is also assuming that the mobs we pulled are killable.
Now, the same situation, but in an outside low aggro area, and we don't have a nuke in the group. Warrior gets the heal. In this situation its all about dealing damage, and well the ench just isn't going to be able to come up with it. These are just SOME of the things one must think about when playing a cleric. I never advise ppl to start out with a cleric, because at high levels its hard to know whats expected of you, when you are still learning the game. And nothing angers a group more than when their most critical member doesn't know what he/she is doing.

I don't want to tell anyone else how to play a cleric but I alway thought of my role in a group as being the guy that tries to make sure noone dies and needs to make the tough calls about who dies when the group is about to wipe.

Sure I throw a few nukes when I am FOM and I melee when I am between 60 and 80 mana but my primary job is to keep everyone alive.

I have almost never pulled mobs in my career cuz I feel that is better left to someone who doesn't have to regen mana or can feign death or snare.

The best things about being a cleric:
1) never need to look for a rezz.
2) very east to get a group.
3) if you are pulling in a very safe environment then you can add DPS with your melee (just for kicks really until you get to level 56).
4) if you are pulling in a group that is testing its limits, the healing job can be very exciting.
5) You have one of the easiest and most useful epics in the game (not easy but easier than mos tothers).

The worst things about being a cleric:
1) you NEED a group, you can't solo worth a dime (it can be done but you would be sooo much better off in a group its ridiculous) so you need to take up a hobby to fill in those LFG times (the new LFG function has really cut down on LFG time).
2) if people die, its your fault.
3) it can get boring sometimes.
4) you get pestered for rezzes.
5) your heal spells never get resisted =).
