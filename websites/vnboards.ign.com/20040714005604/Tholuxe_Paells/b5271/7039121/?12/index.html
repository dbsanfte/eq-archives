Stonez...the life of a cleric at high levels is one of the most boring lives out there. The excitement in the average night of gaming is mostly nonexistant unless you are doing something fun and/or dangerous and you get off some heals that would make or break the night. Most nights however, if yer just sitting someone safe getting exp, it's just damned boring. Sit, med, stand, heal, sit, med, stand, heal. Sure, every once in a while you could stun the casters from healing themselves (very useful stun line), and maybe lob a nuke now and then if your mana isn't tapped out, but for the most part, it's just a med/heal job.

However, that said, it's also been one of the more rewarding classes I've played (which is also why it's the highest level character I've ever played). Hitting level 49 was one of the most awesome events, especially the way it happened. We had a small problem happen deep in bugs/bats in solb, and the group had to evac, but we still lost a couple people. I zoned into SolB myself, only level 48, and while waiting on some of the other folks to get back, I decided to solo a green greater kob. Killed it, dinged to 49, and was able to give a much better res than I would have been able to had I not decided to solo that mob.

Soloing with a cleric is not an easy thing, but it can be done at most levels, however it's damnably boring. At low levels you can melee stuff to death, and use your mana for healing and maybe helping nuke just a bit. Stuns are great for the runners, although they don't last that long. Unrest is a great place in your early to mid teens, especially with the undead nukes at your disposal. And again, at 44, when you get both retribution (500dmg) and Banish undead (585dmg to undead), the world opens up even more and you can start soloing things again. soloing of course is damned boring, but I imagine in the right spots, a cleric could solo all the way to 51 if not higher.

Well, that was longer than I expected, but whatever.
