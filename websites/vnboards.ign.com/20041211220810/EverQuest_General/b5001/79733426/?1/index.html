My guild collapsed quite some time ago, not because of these two new games.

As for the rest, yes, naturally when hardcore gamer types try something else, they will attempt to be hardcore at it. So therefore they will hit that new game hard, see if it will pan out, feed their addiction like EQ might have at one time or was/will/whatever.

Since WoW and EQII came at relatively the same time, this flux in the player base is harder than any of the other ones I have seen. This could be as cataclysmic as what EQ did to UO.

Much of the adoo is about something, the folks who cannot afford to keep EQII or WoW going AND EQ, might side with what they know and stay with that. Could be a double edge sword too, I personally just think folks are over there for a free ride...and will come back to EQ, but they could decide to stay with new fresh online hopes/dreams.. just could be naive of me to think that folks will come back to EQ, but oh well.

I figure there are three types of gamers out there, and reason I mention that is WoW and/or EQII has to either appease them or shut them out. I do not think you will find people who 'love to hate' like in EQ. Even I fall into this category where I hate grinding, but I am doing it, I hate quests but I do them, I hate pick up groups, but I do them. They will just come back and cry like they normally did with something they know and got use to paying for, right along with me.

Anyways, that list;

Gamer type 1. Hardcore, the person who quits RL altogether to become the master of the game. 28+hrs/week. All they do is live and breath strats, and you see them incessantly talking about how much better than anyone else that they are, as if they would put anything about EQ on a RL resume, because of how obsessed they have become and would want something tangible out of it. Like an occupation.

Gamer type 2. Normal, not too terribly addicted to the game but has a grip on reality and knows when to say no. 18-27hrs/week. Could be a recovering EQ-holic or an up and coming casual player headed for the end of his life.

Gamer type 3. Casual, more than likely not in a high end raiding guild, prolly does quests, tradeskills, whatever, just logging in to chat with old chums.
-17hrs/week.

I myself, was on the thin line between 2. and 1. for a long time and now I am happy to say I am in 3. Where the hobby is healthy *).

Date Posted: 12/6 3:14pm Subject: RE: Topic of the Week 12/3/04 - Has your guild had to adapt to changing times lately?
It's been interesting watching the politics on the Stromm server. I am in an EP guild that used to share the EP's with several other guilds. Before the new games came out there were three guilds in PoTime and also doing GoD content. There were at least 4 other guilds besides mine that were in competition for the EP’s. All of these guilds were among the oldest on the server. Some since the beginning of Stromm’s day. Once the new games came out it changed the server of Stromm and devastated many guilds. One of the PoTime guilds went under. One of the EP guilds had just gotten flagged for PoTime a week before EQII came out and once the new game came out they too went under. The other EP guilds also went under. Now there are only two guilds left in PoTime and my guild left in the EP’s. My guild was much younger then the others and so far have remained strong. Yes we’ve lost a lot of hardcore members but now we seem to be getting a lot of new recruits that used to be in the guilds that went under. I have a good feeling we’ll survive. Not only that but with having the EP’s totally to ourselves we’ve been able to gear up our members extremely quick.

So in conclusion the new games have been good and bad for our guild. The bad part is having to backflag new members for the EP’s so we’ll have enough to raid there more. The good part is the fast gearing up process. Before the new games came out we were working on killing the EP gods. We had succeeded in killing Fennin Ro at that time. Now we have to backflag for a bit before we’ll have enough to try again. We’ll make it. It’s just going to take some time to build up again.
