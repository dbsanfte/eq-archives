Uhoh, getting sucked into the mire.

I play both of these classes, ranger and mage. The mage is handy solo, and is good for CoH. The ranger is unreal in groups and raids.

Just a couple quick facts: if your raid doesn't have a ranger, every melee on the raid is doing approx 7 percent less melee damage than they could be if they had ranger ATK buffs, which no one else can give. Rangers are second only to enchanters in increasing the melee damage of the whole raid. As for the tanking aspect: that's not what rangers do. Rangers do lots of damage (more than anyone in the game but rogues and monks), and they manage their aggro so as not to get hit (like rogues and monks). Did I mention that the ranger epic often sticks a slow proc on mobs that even the shamans are having a tough time with? We have 5 level 60 rangers in the front lines in my guild using epics; mobs don't stay unslowed long. Rangers used to need some work; they got it, and now they rock.

You can't get to a lot of places without a non KoS-55+ mage, so mages are vital. They CoH everyone in and then stay grouped with the pullers to yank them out of sticky situations. In terms of damage, well, it's true, they suck at that on raids. No pet, poor nukes. But it doesn't really matter anyway; if you actually played the mage in places like ToV, you'd trash your faction and no longer be useful, so you're better off logging on another character anyway. Mages need some work; they haven't gotten it yet. One can hope they will. EQ is a game of raiding; Sebilis is all well and good for getting some levels until you can do the big stuff, but a lot of mages get to 60, see what their future is, and then roll up a twink or try to trade the mage as this guy is.
