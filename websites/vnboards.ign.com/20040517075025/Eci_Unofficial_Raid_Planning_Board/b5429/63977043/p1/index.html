I make the following analogy not to claim the two situations are comparable, but to point out the difference between what's allowed and what's right.

I am sitting beside a pool, say, or a stream, reading a book, drinking some water, enjoying the day. A strange child in the pool starts to drown. Now, I can save this child simply by putting my book and water down, reaching a hand into the pool, grabbing the child and lifting them out. No effort at all really -- except the book is really, really good, and I don't want to be distracted right now. So I finish the book, and the child drowns.

With the exception of the very few states in this country with "Good Samaritan" laws, it is entirely legal for me to make that decision to finish the book. However, to the vast majority of us it certainly would not be the 'right' decision.

The actions of the Arete set on this occasion were legal, but not right.
