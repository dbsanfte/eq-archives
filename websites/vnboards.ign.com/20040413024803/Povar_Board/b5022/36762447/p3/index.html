I also think of Splos whenever I think back on my times on Povar. He was so unique and made it to the highest level going against the grain. He was the best Monk I ever saw, and I saw and/or worked with all of the elite ones at one time or another. It amazes me to think of how many Harm, Triton, GC, and NA people would say his name first when they were talking about such stuff. Main reason was because he did stuff for all of them better or just as good as their own elite Monks.

I don't think anyone can say someone is the *best* simply because there are too many variables and people only know who they work with all the time anyway. But it says a lot when over half the server's highest level player base puts him at or near the top in every ability. That means he could do it all and everyone knew it and respected it.

He taught so many people how to not only play his class, but how to actually be a person of total class period. He's the only friend I made who never joined a guild ever. He and Zaar are the only 2 high level people I ever knew to only play 1 single character and never even make an alt. He soloed stuff all the time that no other Monk even attempted (which to me is what truly made his abilities stand out) but he never got big headed or anything.

He liked and got along with everybody, and I can't think of one person who didn't like him. He was as nice and kind as he was skilled, and the entire server was made the better for it. Even the RA people who came to Xev all spoke of him with the utmost respect, and that is really the only guild I know of that he didn't help out or do things with. But if you ask Aeen or Aendayar or some other original RA crew they would tell you that Splos rocked and was the coolest.

There are so many other great people I remember from povar, and great times too, but if your going to write about a history of the server, nobody even comes close to having the impact Splos did. He interacted with and impacted more people than anyone else. He was just as happy helping 10th level people as he was pulling Trak for us at only Level 52. Nobody else got as far in the game as he got being solo. So, there must have been some reason so many other people liked him and accepted him for pretty much anything.

I guess this is basically paying homage to one guy and may seem silly, but it really is what I think of the most when I look back on my time on Povar. My roomie was a guide on povar and she says even the entire guide staff loved the guy and he was their favorite player to talk to. It doesn't surprise me in the least to know that he treated them the same way he treated everyone else all the time. He treated us all with courtesy, selflessness, and showcased great skill from the highest levels to the newbie ones. I'm glad I got to work with him and know him a little, and I doubt there will ever be the likes of him again.

Before SA was formed, there were only 2 groups in Cazic-thule. I still remember when Kylael's group killed avatar and Aleena came out with a ruby bp. That was really cool. They were like mid-30's too. Impressive consider Kylael was wearing only flowing-black robe which I think even twinks nowadays would despise to wear.

When the game just started, there were this halfing made himself famous by crafting banded armor. Yes, banded armors. People were actually chasing him down to buy them. Mac bought some pieces off him too.

Darn Xeffo was the first troll I saw on Oasis. I thought he was a monster and started to cast spell on him. He just sat there like a big meatball.

Sneaking in the ogre-city to buy my pet spell was not fun. I died many times trying. Eventually I got smart and made an ogre to buy it for me. big-doh~

Eventually, the top-tier groups merged into SA. I remember I wanted to be the first non-SA to hit 50. So I didn't join them until I made it to 50. Aeen was really cool to invite me to PoF. On my first trip there, I got a cool-skull-staff thing off the dracolich. After a couple more trips with SA. I decided to join them because they ain't arrogant people which at the time rumored to be. How I jumped when I got my final pet spell. It was kicking-ass by the way.

One thing about this game, I hate the part when you have to camp for an item. I never really liked the idea of camping for items. For those of you who knew me, I didn't care about items. Heheh, I made 50 with a black robe on. First vox killing, I didn't even stay to roll for the loot. I was surprised when Eogun(sp?) handed me the belt at Freeport. Oh that was the first vox-belt too~

It was a blast. Saw the EQ2 preview and I will probably be there again. Not as power-player though, but as an entertainment for a newly married-man. :P
