Appologies if this has been posted before. Was not able to find it in a search, but I'm curious to see if others are experiencing the same phenomenon.

Ever since this DX 9 patch, I see a very strange graphic phenomenon. This only seems to occur when my character is in a crowded area, (like 20+ other players in my immediate view).

On about 3 or 4 of the character models I see in front of me, one corner of one polygon, on the secondary hand seems to stretch and terminate in the middle of my screen. For all of the characters who are displayed incorrectly, all of the "deformed" polygons seem to terminate in exactly the same point on my screen.

If I turn, or rotate my camera view, the deformed polygons will continue to stretch and deform, so that the one point is always terminating at the same spot on my monitor. The end result is I see 3 or 4 very long triangles that continue to stretch and deform, as I walk around PoK, Baz, or during a raid.

Here are my cpu specs,
Geforce 4 Ti4200
Pent 4, 2.4Ghz
1Gb Ram
Windows XP, Pro
Latest DX9 installed
Latest Nvidia drivers installed

What makes it particularily odd, is that this phenomenon does not occur on my girlfriend's machine. Her cpu is a little bit faster than mine (2.8Ghz) but the other specs match exactly (including the video card).

Has anyone else noticed this phenomenon? Does anyone know of a work around or a fix? Many thanks in advance.

Try renaming your eqclient.ini file to eqclient.ini.bak. Then start EQ up and go through the setup options and log into your character. If the problem persists, then it's not a problem with your client settings. Go ahead and delete the new eqclient.ini file and rename your old one back to its original name.

If it's not a problem with your client settings, then delete these files/folders from your everquest directory:
uifiles folder, userdata folder, eqmain.dll, eqgame.exe, eqgfx_dx8.dll, EQGraphicsDX9.dll, all files named after your characters, and any other character name files that start with UI_[NAME of character]_[server name].ini

For your character .ini files I recommend copying them to a backup folder just in case there is a setting you wish to copy over later.

Now run the patcher and patch those files you just deleted back in, then run EQ and see if the problem has abated.

All this and lots more bright ideas can be found at this Sony Tech Support page:
http://eqforums.station.sony.com/eq/board/message?board.id=tech&message.id=8976
