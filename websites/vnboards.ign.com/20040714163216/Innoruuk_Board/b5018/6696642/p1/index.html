I'd think level would make a more important difference than disciplines, with disciplines only useful for moderately close matches.

Total AC was not all it was cracked up to be, however. Regen on those tunics is huge, but the AC suffers a bit compared to indi (and moreso cobalt), yet it still made a damn nice difference in combat over the AC gear.

Reasoning for this is simple: you're not recieving nearly as much damage from your opponent as you would from a MOB, heightening regen effectiveness, coupled with the defensive discipline the fungi tunic makes up for a whole mess of lost AC. With Quake style play, regen plays an even larger factor since you get hit a lot less.

In a straight up battle, we'll take a reasonable example of a warrior who does 35 dmg/sec, the tunic regens 17 hp/tick which is 5.67 per second.

Now you're doing under 30 dmg per second, for a total loss in effectivness of 17%.

So, how much AC is that worth? Now we enter into some theoretical ground. I'm going to say, for the purpose of argument, that AC scales linearly.

I know Anne has about 1100 AC, so that means that with the tunic she's pushing a theoretical AC of 1287 AC against a 35 dmg/sec MOB (opponent in this case).

Naturally, the regen effect matters less on things that do more damage to you, putting theoretical AC closer to actual AC.

The only reason I can come up with is the random factor involved in EQ. Every time you "attack" there is a random factor involved both whether or not you will hit, whether or not your opponent will trigger a defensive skill, and whether or not your weapon will proc.
The randomness of EQ is the reason why some days it seems you just can't land any spells on the mobs you have fought 500 times before. It is the reason you suddenly find yourself wiffing away at a mob, and it is the source of such rumors as "charisma affects your spell resists".

Please don't interpret this like I am saying "Syd lost because of bad luck". What I am saying is I think the randomness of EQ is the most important factor in a fight like this - especially with the procs on weapons.

IMO, this tournament proved that, without a doubt, Defensive is the discpline of choice.

In a long dancing contest (what was it, 15, 20 minutes?) like that of Bgal and Buliwyn, none of the short duration disciplines (Mightystrike, Fellstrike, Furious) are going to make much difference because they simply wont last long enough to make an impact (9-12 seconds of a 15 minute fight, with many of those 9-12 seconds spent circling or looking around for your opponent). Defensive is the only discipline that will last long enough to make much difference in a dancing contest. Incidentally, in a fight like this Disciplines mean much less altogether.

In a short, toe-to-toe fight like that of Syd and Bgal, disciplines mean much more because you will get the full benefit of the discipline because you are in a constant state of combat with your opponent. In theory, Syd's choice of Mighty Strike was not a bad one because, due to the straight-up nature of the fight, the discipline would have it's full effect. What we learned (or at least, I did) is that even if all of one's Mighty Strike hits land, Defensive STILL makes a much larger impact on the fight. It seems there's just no substitute for the long (3 minute) duration advantage it provides.
