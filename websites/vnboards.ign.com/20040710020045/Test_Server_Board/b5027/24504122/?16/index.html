on the nights i can't sleep (and there are many)
i work very hard at not lying in bed
and waiting to manage to sleep
because in the quiet of an empty room
and an empty bed
there's room for a small, still voice
from perhaps not so deep within me
to make itself heard

anyone who has ever maybe
not liked themself so much
knows this voice
knows it in the ways it
seeps into your ears
in the silences in the dark
knows it in the ways it
slides from the night to the day

there are times when i can't
hear it at all
and there is a colorful silence
and i can smile
for what seems to be
no reason at all
and i can hope
for myself

but those times don't last
i'm not built in a way
that can hold that
mood for long
there may be people who can
i'm not one
there are lots of things
i can't do

i can't bring myself
to like myself
i have a hard time
liking anything about me
my face or my mind
or my heart
the things i do for people
or the hopes i hold for them

i feel so hollow
i don't even deserve
to be called a shell
just a flat line
around the seeming of a person
as if someone
made a cookie cutter
shaped like me

there are so many things
about myself
i can't stand to see
i shake at night
in my empty bed
with rage that
i still exist
and i try very hard to not think about that

have you ever
felt a self hate
so cold it burned
so dark
the idea itself of light
was pale
and weak
and dying

and in that dark
the little voice comes
and in whispers
not in my ear
but in my self
it tells me
that i should
die

and i don't know
how to argue with it
i don't know
that i ought
i know how to ignore it
but like everything i do
not very well.

on the nights i can't sleep (and there are many)
i work very hard at not lying in bed
and waiting to manage to sleep
because in the quiet of an empty room
and an empty bed
there's room for a small, still voice
from perhaps not so deep within me
to make itself heard

back by popular indifference, midnight ennui.

actually, i struck enough of a chord with some people to justify continuing to write, even if only to make myself feel a little better.

(what kind of awful excuse is that? ha.)

i'm cheating this time; this was an email to a friend who'll probably be too tired to bother getting upset at me even if she sees this . i'm going to write more, i guess, but i have some email to do first, and i'll see if i'm in the mood after.


***

i get to the point in therapy where i know the
therapist well enough to be uncomfortable telling them
things about myself that wouldn't be in their image of
me so far. so i don't, and it just becomes silly.

i don't much have internal lectures, but i've got
plenty of scorn. the little inside voice that goes
'you SUCK!' every so often. i don't know how to like
myself, which is bad enough, but every so often,
there's the actively destructive part too.

i think most people would be better off if they could
just talk about the things that affect their
relationships with the related. er, person in the
relationship. but emotional history turns that into a
gigantic mess, usually, so that even considering it
will make people tense up. *shrugs* .

> you're very vague about what's bothering you.

i'm tired of the cycle of up and down that my moods
make. i'm USED to it, but it gets in the way of
everything i try to be.

i've been medicated; it didn't stop it, really. i
don't know how to describe it... if you were running
back and forth along a 10 yard line, and then someone
fenced you into a 5 yard line, and you kept trying to
run the 10 yard line... i couldn't reach the edges of
where i had been mood swinging to, which was kind of
frustrating in itself, but the _pressure_ was still
there. which sucked for both the down AND up swings,
and i couldn't take it.

it takes a lot of energy to keep other people from
having to see me like i am, much of the time. so much
so that i'm only really hyper/in a 'good' mood when i'm not directly
interacting with people, and i tend to cry only when
i've gotten rid of people who are paying attention.

i don't think either extreme is particularly healthy,
but i think it would be less unhealthy if i just felt
okay _being_ them. i can _function_ in either
situation, if only because i've got so much practice
at it, but i'm not comfortable with having those moods
when other people would have to deal with me.

i don't know what i need to fix things. not wholly. a
relationship with someone who knew these things about
me would help, sure, but i don't want to turn someone
into the mother i never had. that would be a minor
hell, isn't it? to wake up one day and find you hate
someone for what you made them?

right now i'm so painfully desperate to have someone
to simply hold on to, i'm afraid of even trying. that,
and i listen to other people hard enough to usually
find out what their problems are before they find out
mine, and then i feel guilty about trying to burden
them with my own problems, and so the cycle goes.
sigh.

i'm tired of people assuming things about how i must
behave because i'm male, but that's kind of a
different subject. i'm not particularly masculine in
anything but anatomy (that i can tell, at least), but
to have most women see me as a threat and most men see
me as an adversary just grates on me. i HATE petty
male ego sparring, it's just goddamn stupid. it
saddens me that every woman i talk to for any length
of time online assumes that if i find out they're
female, i'll either hit on them or talk dirty, because
i know that's usually not just made up. it's from how
other damned men have behaved. i can't even make an
oblique reference to myself as a 'man' without
flinching a little. i did it just now. can't say 'boy'
because it's not particularly accurate, and 'male'
ends up being my usual descriptor. mostly i want to
say 'jerk', but that's more self-hate showing.

i suppose i'm tired of lots of things; of hurting for
no reason, of the ways i deal with that, too. i used
to cut myself, i still bite myself some, because for
all the power of the loathing i can sometimes feel for
myself, none of it stands up to a basic bodily hurt.
sometimes, if i have the opportunity, i can take a nap
and wake up feeling a whole hell of a lot better. but
the circumstances under which i can use THAT way out
are fairly uncommon. if i'm feeling down or up enough,
i can't sleep anyway. and it's hard to arrange to take
a nap in the middle of the day anyway.

i agree with the concept of having someone to talk to
about what's bothering you, but a paid therapist just
isn't the way. i guess that's the stand-in for when
you don't have anyone to talk to in your life. you
track someone down who makes themselves available at
an hourly rate.

i enjoy the ACT of talking to people. i like finding
out things about them, and things about who they are
external to their responses themselves. you've gotten
the joy of putting up with my million and one
questions, and don't make that face, woman! =p

using writing to communicate is helpful in that it
insulates me from knowing too much about people, i
don't have to learn their tensings and their tones and
their EVERYTHINGs. not that i'm obligated to, but i
always look. they're a part of communication, i can't
NOT watch for them. but writing is painfully
frustrating at times because i can't use any of those
things to listen, too, and i can't use them to speak
with either.

go go paradox.

am i capable of being happy? i think 'happy' comes in
different shapes, and for me 'happy' is the whole
category. there's the little joys that you run into,
like the way an oatmeal cookie is chewy, or how it
feels to lick ice cream (food is an easy example, it's
always sensual) . the contentment of being in a
warm room near a cold rainy window, just getting to
FEEL the difference in the air. lying on sunny grass,
over moist dirt.

i don't think they're all physical, but those are the
easy ones to talk about.

while i was living with my mother and sister, and my
mother was on some 'let's be an utter twit' rampage
with my sister about something, and my sister goes to
her room to cry, and i go in to just give her a hug.
to be able to fight the things that have made me what
i am, by being able to be there for her. is that
happiness? satisfaction? i can't quantify that like i
can say it feels neat to be able to keep a cat's
attention when everyone says they're 'distant', or
like i can say how it feels to have a woman trust me
enough to put my hand on her breastbone (while the
small of the back is way up there in terms of intimate
gestures, i think the space between the breasts is the
most intimate and least directly sexual place you can touch a
woman; at least, as much as anything intimate can be
_not_ sexual). actually, i can't say _how_ it
feels to do that, i can just say what is done, and let
the image itself do all the work.

there are lots of little happinesses that you can't
describe the feeling of but it doesn't mean they don't
exist.


i know i didn't ask many questions, but i'd appreciate
thoughts. this isn't everything that's bothering me,
but problems are specific things: easy to address in
depth and hard to present in width.

no_one_important: I have an idea, if you'd like to hear it.

Often I find that in helping others, things become more clear, more happy and more dear.

I don't know what kind of community service options are available in your community, but you might check them out. Churches are good too. Even if you're agnostic or athiest, it doesn't matter. Often the R.C. churches of cities will sponsor food drives, community development, counselling programs, etc. You might find helping them with these to be refreshing. The priests and sisters are often non-judgemental, comforting people to work with. Just a thought, but with your talent for words, and with your need to cling to something/someone, volunteering/working for a church might be a start to brighter days for you. Alternatively, volunteering/working in a soup kitchen/shelter/food bank/community outreach program/whatnot could be a good catalyst too. Whether this activity be full time, or an hour or two a week, it will probably bring some new directions for you. I'm sure you've heard a billion pieces of advice; you're the one who must ultimately decide what to try. But if you do try this one and it doesn't work out, at least you'll have contributed something to someone who needs you.
