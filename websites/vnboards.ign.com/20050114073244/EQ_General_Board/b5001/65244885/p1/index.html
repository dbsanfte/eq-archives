Date Posted: 3/3/04 4:36pm Subject: RE: Why is corpse retreival stlil in EQ?
Agreed. You feel less sense of danger and less sense of accomplishment if there is no danger. Death is meaningless if it's not somewhat punishing.

You know you will have CR if you die. So, you plan accordingly. If you don't, you're an idiot. This means you choose your battles carefully, choose your hunting locations carefully, and attempt to minimize or trivialize your CRs. Yeah, it sucks to die, but if you're selective the majority of your CRs will simply involve running back to a safe spot to loot.

If you screw up there are many ways to get help. EQ is a social game filled with a lot of jackasses and idiots - but even in today's greed filled EQ there there are helpful people - and who knows, you might even get some help without having to pay plats for it. Unlikely, but it *could* happen.

Also, in today's EQ CR is WAY less troublesome than it was in the early days of EQ. Now you don't even have CR until level 10+. Now many high level zones, like in the planes, have 'grave yards' where your corpse pops to after a short amount of time eliminating CR entirely in those zones (which is silly to me since they're high level zones - although in some of those zones there are triggered access areas and without GYs you'd never get your corpse back).

So far in my 2nd round of EQ I've only had one troublesome CR - me and a bro were duoing deep in Sebilis and ate it and in Sebilis it's very difficult to go deep naked since invis isn't an option. So, we had to recruit a necro who was seb keyed to summon our corpses. Not a big deal.

If you're a noob and die and have rough CRs a lot then you are not being intelligent and selective about how and where you xp. Adjust your game.
