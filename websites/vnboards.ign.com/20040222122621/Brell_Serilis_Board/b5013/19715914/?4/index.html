Prologue:

Heedless of the danger, Ketha R’Thule continued writing. The quill never wavered in her hand as death cries rang through the halls of the House Royal. Finally, she laid the feather down. She slipped a ring from her finger and rolled it within the note she had just penned. “You must get these to Thryn. Use the back stair and exit through the tunnels. Make haste. Some must be saved.”

Marcaset’s eyes widened in horror as realization seeped into his mind. “M’lady, no! You must come with me. There is still time.” Panic choked his words as his eyes welled with tears.

A soft smile graced the tall woman’s lips. Even facing her death, her grace was not compromised. “Marc.” She reached a slim hand out and brushed the man’s cheek. “It is me they seek. They will not rest until I have been removed. I cannot run. They will rip through our city and towns until every person loyal to the house has been slain if I live.”

“M'lady, your people would rather die with you than have another ruler. Your family has ruled longest of any in the history of Tralean.” His eyes burned with anger.

“My people will survive. Jhelya has won. He will sit the throne. But they will never forget and those who survive will keep the truth alive until one day a royal returns to the throne. That day will come, Marc. Never forget that. Now hurry. I must insist.” She secured a silver band around the missive and placed it in the armsman’s hand.

“I will do as you ask, m’lady.” Drawing her hand up, he gently brushed it with his lips.

A crash sounded just outside the oaken door to her chamber. “Go now!” She hissed in a harsh whisper. Marcaset D’Larn slipped behind a large tapestry, woven with an image of a giant red dragon seated atop the castle from which he now fled.

The door buckled, splinters dropping to the floor as Jhelya’s men pounded against it with their weapons. With a final groan, the door gave way and men flooded into the royal chamber. Much to their surprise, they found a tall graceful woman seated calmly at a desk. Her raven-colored hair hung around her like a silken mist. Amber colored eyes met their war-fired stares. There was no panic-stricken raving woman, screaming and crying as they expected. The men looked to their captain who stood staring at the woman. He knew his orders yet still he hesitated.

“Kill her.” The words came harsh and low from behind the captain. “Kill her now.” Before the captain could respond, a knife shot past his ear and struck the woman directly upon her chest. Her eyes opened wide and then drew closed as a dark red stain grew on her golden dress. A young man with dark chestnut hair moved from the crowd of men. He stepped up to the woman’s limp form and ripped the knife from her breast, a wicked grin on his face.

The captain stood motionless, watching in a trace-like state. His eyes never left the raven-haired beauty. As the men filtered out to inform Jhelya that the Lady R’Thule was dead, the captain remained. Slowly he moved forward until he was directly in front of her. He dropped to his knees. His head lowered until it rested on the dead woman’s knees and a mournful whisper escaped his lips. “Forgive me, Mother.”
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Marcaset arrived safely at the home of Thryn S’Vlyn. There was a quick exchange as Marcaset told Thryn of Jhelya’s taking of the keep. Thryn unwrapped the missive from the Lady R’Thule. Tears filled his eyes as he read, absently rubbing the ring between his thumb and forefinger. His wife had emerged from the bedchamber and stood silently by the door. His eyes met hers.

“Thryn?” She whispered. “What is it?”

“We must leave. Now.” Standing, he crossed the room, taking his wife in his arms and holding her. He whispered, “I’m sorry, love.”

Gathering what they could into manageable packs, the couple woke their eldest daughter. The younger one was hefted into Marcaset’s arms for the journey. In dark silence, the five rode out from the town. In the distance they could see the glow of dying flames that illuminated the shattered city of Riahel.
