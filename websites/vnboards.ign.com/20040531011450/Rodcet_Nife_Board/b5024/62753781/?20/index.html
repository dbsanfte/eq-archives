I don't think retro raid type things are ever a good idea. It sounds too easily abused. Not allowing negative points will help some, but you still have to watch it.

Say what you will about it not being a problem because you only want guild members who aren't selfish. You'll get some who aren't very selfish, some who pretend not to be selfish but actually are, some who even think they aren't selfish even though they are, and some who just really obviously are. I think that covers it.

In a guild or any other group for that matter, a few people may truly focus more on doing what's in everyone's best interest, but you can't count on everyone doing that.

You CAN count on everyone being selfish. It only takes a few greedy people willing to exploit a system to royally screw it up and turn the less people into doormats. So the best thing to do when you're setting up a loot system is to start with the assumption that everyone is a greedy b***tard. Design a system that will work in that situation and you'll be set.

This lesson in practical objectivism brought to you by workplace boredom.
