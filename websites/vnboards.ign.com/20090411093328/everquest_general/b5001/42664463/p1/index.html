I have many fond memories of EQ, most of them from the earlier days... For me, EverQuest died when Shadows of Luclin came out... There's so much from the pre-luclin era that the new players didn't get to experience...


Rangers sucking

Erudite/DE Necros everywhere and later, Monk twinks with cured silk and fighting batons

/ooc anyone casting bind

/shout has anyone seen my corpse?

taking the boats, getting teleported by a player, not a book

PoD raids

Lum the Mad

EverCrest comic

knowing how many players were online

Abashi, Aradune, and The Vision.. Verant Interactive

Whineplay, Grollerdabard, Snoota

the intro movie

SSoY and planes armor

"AC/AO/DAoC is coming out next month, EQ will be so dead then"

actual GM events

people hunting in dungeons


The game and community have changed, some would say for the better, however I feel that EQ has lost its uniqueness. Sony whores out the EQ title every which way now. I think if I were to come upon EverQuest for the first time now I would pass it over. The unimaginativeness of the later developments, graphical, geographical, and gameplay-wise, leaves me wondering whether anyone at former VI still sees EQ as a game and not a cash cow.

I'm sure some of you can recall things from before release - I didn't have the computer to play EQ back then but I was following it... When I finish writing this I'll think of dozens more things to add but it's late so I'll finish up: EverQuest isn't the imaginative, innovative game it used to be.

The only thing I truly miss is newbies actually playing the game. Every "newbie" I come across these days is a twinked character. I was leveling up my monk from the start (without twinking) and everytime I grouped, it was with someone decked out in something akin to epic armor with a weapon that kills everything it hits the first time it connects. If not that, then the group I'm grouped with has another character just sitting around the area we're camping buffing us with 60+ buffs.

The days of actually playing this game so that it is new and difficult as you level up are completely over. I miss that most of all.

I wish they would start up a batch of new servers on US territory (not European), and not just one so that all of the uber guilds end up going to just that one like they always do and cornering the market on everything. Open up about five new servers at one time in the US, and there's the chance of actually creating an atmosphere that might be fun again. Sure, you're going to have powerlevelers, but they're going to be limited to one of five rather than all of them rushing in onto one server and dominating it like they always do.

An example of this was the Kane Bayle server. My old guild from DAOC (an uber guild if there ever was one) had an entire guild of 60th level characters on that new server while some people were still trying to get out of the newbie zones. Then they, and the other uber guilds on the server, took over major newbie spawns of known drops and made it so that the newbies couldn't even camp these spots at the level where they would have gotten something out of doing it.

That's just sad. But that's the way the game has gone.

I think we (the players) have only ourselves to blame. Wasn't it the players who bitched and moaned until EQ was made into what it is today? I agree the game has changed a lot from the original design. I think the makers had one idea (The Vision) and that was pretty much tossed away eventually. I guess that's why Brad left.

I'm not saying that players don't have the right to speak out and many of the changes made were for the good, but too many cooks spoil the soup. Many of the things that made the game a pain in the ass, like the boats, added to the atmosphere. I loved running a first level gnome from Ak-Anon to Qeynos. >>> - 2 extra free weeks at initial release of EQ since the servers were so unstable

The servers were actually rock solid, it was the ISP bandwidth and the login server that had to handle it that were hooped. Sony never expected the raw demand they got at the start and were caught with their pants down when everyone and their dog tried to log in. If you could get in, it was fine.

Two weeks later they added a new ISP (and, as they mentioned at the time, significantly increased the total bandwidth available to San Diego as a whole) and everything was fine. EQ is still the most lag forgiving of all the mmorpgs.


My own memory would be of the time where 98% the people were under L50 so it actually meant something.
