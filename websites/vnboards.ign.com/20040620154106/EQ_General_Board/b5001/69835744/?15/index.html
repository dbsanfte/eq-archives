Correct. Every loot in EQ is exactly the same each time it drops (sans it being upgraded or something), it's stats are not random each time it drops. So a Hammer of Hours isn't going to be 200 hp one time, and then 150 hp the next time, it always be 165 hp. Where as in AC there are 6 different levels of loot. And each mob in the game is tied to one of those levels for it's loot table. And the stats are randomly generated within the values for that level.

In EQ, you have random drops, such as X item can come from any mob/mob type in the zone. Or you have drops from named mobs, which have (usually) a common, uncommon, and rare item on their loot table. The pool of items that mob can drop, will be the same every single time the mob spawns.

Piejack, you can try EQ for free now with an "invite a friend" deal that's going on.. If you are interested let me know =)

Loot: it's the same as far as I can see, everything you kill has something on it, and there are ways of making money to buy good stuff in the bazaar. I am pro-lot and I have had zero complaints in this regard. The bazaar is like the marketplace only 500 times better

edit: Ok after re-reading the posts above, I see what you guys mean. But I remember doing newb armor quests and not always getting the drop. Sometimes the bandits would drop crap, sometimes they would drop the sashes I needed.. but never a consistent drop every time.

This is a more group oriented game. You will have good solo experiences in your first levels, while you are getting to know the game. I leveled all of my characters up either soloing, or playing duo. I love being a newb though, so I didn't mind it one bit. When you get into your teens, you will find that grouping is a much better way to gain experience, and I also found that it's not that bad of a thing. There are good zones for teens - twenties that are always full of people and it's pretty easy to find a group.

AC and EQ are very different. AC has 3 races, all human, and any template you feel like making. EQ has a crapload of races, and classes.. the difference is the class/race you choose is *sort of* the last decisions you really have to make. Unless you are a caster, you will be deciding which skill to specialize in when it's time, but usually there is a recommendation as to which one. Like my necromancer specialized in "alteration" because it gets used the most by a necro naturally.

There are too patches in EQ! At least once a month they patch the game.. they are not major monthly updates like in AC, but there are patches. New zones and stuff like that, like really MAJOR updates, are done via expansion but they are usually a few times a year give or take.


Anyhow, you never know until you try it. I am one of those people who really enjoys both games for the differences they have

If you are thinking of challenge factor, EQ takes the cake, hands down. AC is sooo not that challenging compared to this game

That's why it's called a loot table =-p

Bandit Loot Table (random example)
0.5% = <quest item>
1.5% = bronze weapon (50% slash, 25% blunt, 25% pierce)
5.0% = bronze armor (5% chance for any specific part)
25.0% = silver coin (random(1d10)
50.0% = copper coin (random(1d30)

And then the remaining 19.5% of the time, it drops nothing at all. And of course, there might be three rolls on this table, or seperated into multiples table. But, in a rough way, this is the way EQs loot works. Where an AC table looks something like.

Level 6 Loot table
0.5% = missile weapon (33.3333% chance of atlal, xbow, bow)
1.5% = melee weapon (20% chance for each of dagger/mace/sword/axe/spear)
5% = pea (5% silver 10% gold 85% copper)
etc etc

Now let's say it rolls a missle weapon and then bow, it moves to a seperate table

Level 6 Bow table
20% = 130%
30% = 127%
50% = 125%

20% = 15m
30% = 12m
50% = 9m

etc etc etc
