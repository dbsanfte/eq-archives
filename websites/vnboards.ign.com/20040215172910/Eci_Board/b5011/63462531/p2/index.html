I have been on both sides of the fence with this. I grew up in a very poor family. You see the cliche of people living off corndogs and fries, well in my case it was true. until I was 14 my family could not really afford much more than that as my Mother was a single mom raising 2 kids with little post highschool education. She grew up on a farm in rural Utah then married a GI who abandonded us for drugs. She did her best and though we were poor we were happy.

After highschool I worked in a Magnesium plant making a lot of money for my age while awaiting to leave for colledge. I was buying everything I could and would never have less than 1k in my pocket at any given time as I never know what it was like to have a seeming endless supply of money. I would help out my family and friends, splurging on everything. I was injured on the job and then spent a year in therapy, I missed my scholarship start date so it was awarded to another potential student. I could no longer work at my job and I could not afford to go to medical school on my own. Once again I was poor.

Several years ago a few friends and I were lucky enough to make some fast cash doing something we loved. At first we were excited as it was more than any of us had ever seen but not really "rich" per se. We were getting paid to play before thousands of people instead of for our friends. It was great at first but the more we made the more we wanted. At that point it turned into more of a job and we began fighting amongst ourselves over royalty and merchandise dispersement. Ultimately we all split and though some of us still went on in music none of us have really been as happy playing as we were when we were together. Money destroyed our friendship and after the Label was done with us for breaking our contract many of us were worse off than we started.

Now I work in an IT job for a large software firm, I have a wife and 2 small children. I make enough to be Upper Middle Class but I have seen the Yo Yo effect life has on people first hand so I no longer live in the moment and take money for granted.

Coming from a poor family I appreciate things more than my very rich counterparts and am thankful for anything I have or receive. Money did not buy me happiness, it destroyed what I once thought were life long friendships.

And yes it is true that it is not your fault that anyones parents can not afford what yours may provide for you. If they come into money they will have a greater appreciation for it than you ever will though as it appears you have just been handed most things in life.

I believe someone said it was because they worked harder than someone else. This is total BS. Lets take this from a music perspective and compair two bands - Britany Spears vs Simple Plan.

Britany has never written any or her own music and only has limited input to the lyrics she sings. She is a manufactured artist, more puppet, controlled by a record label to become a hit machine. Nothing she sings about reflects anything she has ever done in life. Her label is large enough to pay corporate raido stations to guarentee that her songs will be played a minimum number of times throughout a day during primetime listening hours.

Simple Plan has been on tour for 4 straight years with a single album out. They write all of their songs on their own and actually play instruments (I know it is a novelty these days). They are only now getting radio play and making some money off record sales. Radio play has only come through the dilligence of the street team contacting radio stations and making non stop requests in hopes the song will make it on the air.

Britany makes more money than Simple Plan but who is doing more work?
