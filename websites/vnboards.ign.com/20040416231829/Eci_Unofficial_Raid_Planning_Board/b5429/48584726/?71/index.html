Hi Guys/Gals:

I wanted to put this idea out on the table for discussion and possibly improve the overall raiding situation for the three zones I mentioned in the subject line.

Currently on our server Fear, Hate and PoG generally operate on a schedule, more or less. On this schedule we have guilds that hold days and there are also public raids.

As our server grows older and the population continues to expand, a few things are happening. The average level of players is increasing. The average quality of gear is increasing. The number of guilds (albeit small ones) is increasing.

In any given month, there are between 28-31 days. Generally speaking about 5 of these days are almost universally agreed upon as "public" days. For those that don't know, the public days are the days that people get together regardless of guild affiliation and hunt in a given zone under established and accepted loot rules. This leaves 23-26 other days in any given month. These other days have all been claimed by various guilds.

I would like to put a proposition to these guilds that are currently "holding" days to relinquish control of them and leave the days as FFA (free for all) days.

There are a few reasons that I think this would be the right thing to do. Many guilds claim days and then either never relinquish them or hold them for such an unreasonably long time that even the general public no longer acknowledges their claim. Another reason is because (in my perception) some of the guilds hold days but as a guild have moved beyond the zone. So for example if most of your guild is ToV outfitted and or is ToV capable, it is not reasonable to "hold" a day in one of these lower tier zones simply to get armor for your few low level players or twinks. The final reason that I believe guilds "hold" days is for the epic/named spawns. This particular reason is the single largest cause of grief on our server. Guild X just "ganked" Guild Y for mob Z. It is no longer reasonable to expect mobs to be left untouched simply because a guild has "claimed" a zone.

What's even worse is that many times epic mobs go wasted (meaning not killed) simply because the guild that held the day either didn't have the interest/manpower/need for the spawn and the rest of the server trying to be civil doesn't make an attempt for it since guild "X" has that day on "hold".

Putting the zones in question as FFA days allows the zones to be most efficiently used by the server. Mobs will be taken down as needed and every person guilded or not will hae an opportunity. Another benefit of putting these zones as FFA is that it will teach your guilds how to deal with racing for spawns.

In higher end content nobody claims a day/spawn zone. Whoever is the most mobile and capable goes for a mob/ringevent/etc.

Allow me to address a few common objections preemptively.

"Oh the ubers will take all our named mobs" Not true. The ubers have their hands full racing each other to Plane of Time. Most of them haven't stepped foot into ToV in 6 months let alone to these lower tier planes.

"Oh you are saying this so you can claim a days for your guild in these zones."
Ummm NOPE. The only things we want from these zones are epic spawns and quite frankly if a mob is up we are going for it. The armor from these zones means nothing to us.

"Oh but I'll never get my epic this way!" If you are in a guild that holds 1 day a month what are your chances that a mob with a 1 week timer will spawn....not very good. But if the days are FFA, whichever guild wants the mob/item the most will get it, and as FFA the hard feelings are minimized.

"Oh but to get to Tunare you have to clear an entire zone...all that armor is wasted" Wasted? Says who? If you want the armor that drops while guild X is in a zone clearing it to get to a god, more times than not those guild can't GIVE the armor away. I can't tell you how many times I've seen armor ROT because nobody in the zone wanted it. Should a guild not raid simply because you MIGHT want to go into a zone?

Well these have been my thoughts on it. I would very much like to see an intelligent discussion on this, preferably from guild leadership of the various guilds that "hold" days.

Change is a good thing people...you can only grow and advance if you are willing to change. One of the biggest reasons guilds fail or lose members is because they are unable/unwilling to make changes. There is plenty of room in EQ for everyone to be a winner. You don't have to fail for me to win. I firmly believe that this will go a long way to helping improve everyones game play experience.

Thanks for reading this.

The situation you described is a "leap-frog" which is frowned upon by the server and GM's have been known to take the side of the group that was leapfrogged.

If a group is hunting in a zone and is making progress to a particular mob and has sufficent force to make a reasonable attempt at it, has rights to that mob.

In the example you provided, the first guild would have rights to that mob as they were clearing path to that mob to engage.

Regarding the question you posed about waiting or respawn. The beauty of FFA is that because the zones are 8hr timer, essentially every mob is up 3 times per day (exluding named) Realistically in any given play "day" for us there would be two spawn cycles for the zone (since the 3rd is usually on Asian/European time slots) Now with 2 spawn cycles per day, we've effectively DOUBLED the amounts of chances per month that guild have a chance to raid a zone.

Also, keep in mind that FFA does not mean you own the entire zone. Especially with outdoor zones, it can easily support 2 raids. Hate albeit is a tad harder because of the dungeon environment.

Hope this helps further clarify.

I'm not a guildleader, but I am officer, and a long time EQ player and just wanted to comment.

The idea of FFA bothers me, mostly because I've seen what it can do to a server. It often comes from good intentions, but rarely stays that way. It works for higher level guilds, and or mobs, but I've rarely seen it work for lower level planes such as fear and hate. My biggest concern, is the armor farming as was mentioned before.

SoE, I believe anyways, did not intend for the armor turn ins to result in armor farming. I believe it was there way of giving higher level players something worth while in exchange for upgrading their armor and or giving them something to do with it, other then destroying it.

I know your plan involves keeping public days public, and I admire that. But what is to keep the smaller, first time guilds from being stuck trying to find left overs?

Many people still go to these "lower tier" planes for the joy of going. Some go for their armor, some go for the named, some go just for the chance to have a good time. If it becomes FFA, I fear those who NEED are the ones who shall suffer the most. They will ultimately be forced to work around, those who want to farm or kill gods, basically being forced to take, or having to settle for the left overs other guilds leave behind.
And I know you said there would basically be two spawns of mobs to kill, that would only be the case if the killing of mobs was timed. The majority of raiding is done in the early evenings, except on weekends, so that usually only gives one spawn per day available.

The lower tier planes, such as Hate and Fear, and even PoG are often the first steps for people new to the raiding aspect of EQ. Making it FFA in my opinion, would make it harder for the new guilds to learn raiding skills if they are often finding no mobs to kill, or other raids already there. I think it would also be harder for them to schedule or plan a raid for their guild, because they will never know for sure if they will find anything up, or another guild there without having to go check.

Yes I know higher level raiding is based on who finds what up and can get to it first, I respect that. But at higher levels, guilds are more capable of being able to set up on the fly to kill a mob.. first time planar guilds are often NOT capable of doing so, and would again suffer in the end because they do not have the numbers to gather at the drop of a hat.

I'm not trying to be disrepectful mind you. Just I'm a firm believer in trying to be fair. FFA would not be fair to the "little guys" out there, who do not have the capablity to set up a raid force on a minutes notice. Yes it would be good practice, but I see few guilds who actually NEED to raid fear, or hate being capable of doing that on a regular basis. I fear they would often find the zone already in use, or empty, leaving them disappointed and eventually even angered at their lack to attain the content they don't just desire, but need. It then can lead to bad relations amongst guilds, which is harmful, not only for the guilds involved, but everyone as angers flare and resentments towards others are established and often become all out fueds. This I have seen from experience, and that is why I left and came to E'Ci. When the community as a whole is so hateful over raid zones, it really does affect the atmosphere of the server.

You said change is good, yes it is. But it needs to be for the good of everyone to be effective. If it could be worked out so that everyone is happy, then I, like Vorgus would support the majority, albiet reluctantly, and hope it really works as intended.

Some very good discussion going on. As of now, it appears to me that the majority of the sentiment is to keep the status quo.

So let me pose this question to the gallery and see what solutions are offered. I will use my guild as an "example" to not offend other guilds.

DoA does not have Fear or Hate days, nor do we want any. Quite frankly my guild is beyond the armor drops of those zones. We do need however some epic spawns. As the system stands now, here are the options that I see we have.

01) Do nothing, ask for a day and then wait anywhere from 1month to 1year to get a day and then hope that an epic spawn occurs in one of our 12 chances per year.

02) Option 2, have the person needed the spawn sign up for public raids, attend earn points...etc...etc...eventually moving up the list. (assuming that week that he/she finally has enough points the public raid is even able to kill the epic mob)

03) Option 3, Ask the guilds that currently hold days and hope that they don't have a player that needs that particular epic item.

04) Option 4, stick a tracker in the zone, track it, when it pops gather guildmates, go to zone (assuming not already being raided) clear a minimum amount of mobs to get to ours. Kill ours loot it, gate out.

I don't see options other than those listed. If you can think of one please post it.

Obviously #4 would be the easiest one for us. We are more than capable of doing it, but as I've said before....Might doesn't make it right. But then again....should guilds/players be penalized for being achievers? Does everyone have to move at the pace of the masses?

I'm not saying #4 is what we will do, but it is the very position that many guilds are put into by the current system. That is why this discussion is so important.

Thanks for reading this.
