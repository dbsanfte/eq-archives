I agree with Meyhem but disagree with the way he said it. What's wrong with being civil?

EQ is a RPG, but that doesn't mean that you can't RP begging either. I don't ever "beg" items or money.
I HAVE asked politely for buffs, and don't get upset when I'm turned down.
I have freely given items to newbies (who didn't ask, or just almost got there butt whupped by a monster and could obviously use a leg up) just because, even though I could have sold said item(s) to other newbies/merchants for more $. I always need $ for spells, but I know how tough those first few levels can be when all you've got is a few copper pieces to rub together. I figure what goes around comes around. (BTW, it did. Someone gave me 2 pieces of Split-Paw armor for my druid <unasked for> just the other day.) I still pretty much consider myself a newbie, but at least I'm not broke. (right now anyway)

At the same time, I have ALWAYS refused requests for $, and generally refuse beggars I don't know.
I have freely given away loot to strangers nearby that needed it more than me. (For example, a monk needing a putrid rib bone for the headband quest that I really don't need) I have frequently asked permission to loot a corpse when someone was camping NPC's for exp. I usually get a yes answer. I don't consider this begging. If they say no, that's their perogative. I'm not upset in the slightest. They earned the kill after all, not me.

Politeness goes a long way. Even so, I don't think it is right for someone to hang around the bank or city gates and beg /politely/ until they are loaded up with stuff before they kill their first large rat!
