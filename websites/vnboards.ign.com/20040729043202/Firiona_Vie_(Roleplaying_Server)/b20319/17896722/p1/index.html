Sabra

Registered: Nov 99
 		Date Posted: 10/15/01 2:22am Subject: Abbreviations: How much is too much?
Some people are blessed with dextrous hands and the ability to type quickly. Other people suffer from disabilities and have a difficult time typing at all. Most people fall into a middle ground between these two, and more than half of the players that play EQ will type in abbreviations regularly.

What is tolerated on Firiona Vie? I personally was hoping for an environment in which I would never have to hear things like "how r u 2day" ever again. This hasn't happened, though, and while I find the practice annoying, it certainly doesn't discourage me.

Typing with U and R abbreviations to replace "you" and "are" is not necessarily the mark of a d00d. Some people have a legitimate problem when it comes to typing. Unfortunately, there's no way for everyone else in the community to know the story behind the typist's methods, so we can't very well determine whether these abbreviations are influenced by need, or laziness.

With this in mind, how forgiving are you when it comes to dealing with people that abbreviate in this manner? Think about these situations:

(1) When someone asks for Clarity and you give it to them, do you become a little irritated when they respond with a simple "ty"? Or are you content with the fact that this person did express gratitude at all? Do you find yourself thinking that they could have gone through slightly greater lengths to express their gratitude than typing two letters, or do you immediately forget about the encounter and begin meditating to replace the mana you just spent on the person to whom you just gave Clarity?

(2) Let's say you've just achieved a rank sufficient to cast Spirit of Wolf. How much detail would you require of the question before you agree and cast the spell?
a) sow?
b) sow me
c) plz sow me
d) will u plz sow me?
e) will you sow me plz?
f) will you sow me please?
g) Will you cast sow on me please?
h) Will you cast Spirit of Wolf on me, please?
i) Begging your pardon, but if it is within your means, will you please bestow upon me the lupine aura that renders a traveler fast and tireless?

Certain abbreviations are not only accepted, but encouraged. For example, the warrior tanking for a group in a higher level dungeon would certainly rather hear "OOM" from the cleric than die because the cleric was busy typing, "Thou haveth mine apologies, for I have been completely reft of magical energy, and can no longer heal thee."

But where is the line drawn between what is acceptable and what is considered d00d? Perhaps as long as the abbreviations are kept in /tell or /group, especially when expedience is a necessity, it is acceptable. I'm curious about the point of view that other people might have regarding abbreviations and how they're used in EQ today.

For myself, it is all about timing.

"Heal PLZ" is fine during a pitched battle - you are getting hammered on, and writing an essay on why you wish to experience the ecstasy that is the healing power of the gods is worse than futile. Actually, I'd be impressed they managed to add the "Plz".

"OOM" also falls into that category, but brings with it another factor: recognition. I played a high level cleric on another server, and once had a hot-key that wrote out "I'm spent lads, try not to bleed on me as I meditate!"

Utter and complete mistake. In the flood of information during a battle, the letters "OOM" are very recognizable. No one had time to read my clever note - much less decipher it - and several people died needlessly.

The other side of the coin is non-battle language.

"Could u Rez me, plz?"

For Brell's sake, you are asking me to call upon my god to resurrect you from the dead! More importantly, you are asking another player to drop what he was doing and spend time and mana on you. Take an extra moment to type out "you" and "please", especially since (as a dead man) you are certainly under no pressing time constraints.

[As a side note, using "Rez" instead of "resurrect" doesn't bother me - again, it's part of the EQ culture]

Asking for a teleport is in the same category: you've got the time and you are asking for a favor. Using gratuitous abbreviations implies that THEIR time is not important but yours is so precious that you cannot be bothered to type out "please".

More importantly, you'll be less likely to be turned down if you write something in roleplay. "It galls me to ask for help from an elf, but me old legs get tired easily nowadays. Could I trouble you to take me to the West Commonlands?"

Spirit of the Wolf is an odd one though. Almost all druids and rangers have it memorized, and often you have to catch them quickly before they run off.

In general, I'd say you should stick to the above. You are asking for a favor, show them that you know it. Still, I could think of several instances (especially in groups) where using the abbreviation SoW would actually be appreciated by all.

Abbreviating place names has never really annoyed me, and for two reasons.

First, the abbreviation often becomes the accepted term. No one on the East Coast of the USA goes around talking about “The District of Columbia”, it’s always “DC”. Similarly, when someone refers to “LA” in context of a place-name, everyone knows what you are talking about... almost to the point where calling it “Los Angeles” might get you an odd look as they work it out.

Some places in EQ have reached similar status, I think. “WC”, “Misty”, “NK”, “FV”, “Kith”, etc.

The second reason is typing. Waiting for long periods while a hunter-pecker (and terrible speller) like myself laboriously types and spell-checks “Western Commonlands” can break the flow of a conversation:

“Hail good dwarf! What business do you have?”

[ten-second pause]

“I seek transportation, pointy-ears.”

“Oh? Where might you be heading and what fare do you offer?”

[ten-second pause]

“Qest Commonkands”

“What?”

[ten-second pause]

“West Commonkands”

“Is that like West Commonlands?”

[thirty-second pause]

“Just take me to WC, you over-priced, pointy-eared, glorified ferry-boat!”

“50 plat.”
