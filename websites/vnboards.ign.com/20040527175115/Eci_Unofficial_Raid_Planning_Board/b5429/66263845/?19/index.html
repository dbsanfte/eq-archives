Demimetah, one of the reasons behind the idea of a generally accepted PoF schedule is as has been said, not to waste time going up there to find everything already dead (remember - 9 hour repop timer for everything except named, they are even longer). Another reason is to minimise conflict. If you want to go to Fear and you want to be respectful of other guilds play times, use the schedule and *negotiate* with a guild that is listed as having a day you are interested in going to PoF. This is not to say the schedule reserves a day in PoF, no one has the right to do that, it is merely a tool to try and prevent conflict and make trips to PoF as fun as possible for all. Please take the time to read the preamble to the PoF schedule thread. It tries to make this clear.

If HoD wishes to abide by the schedule, and your guild wants a generally accepted day in PoF, post a reply to the schedule thread and pick one of the open days. This way those guilds that honor the schedule will honor your day. Other guilds that for one reason or another do not recognize the schedule are of course free to do as they wish, at the risk of the rest of the server regarding their honor in this matter as nill. The majority of guilds on the server recognize the schedule and will at least make an attempt to contact a guild officer of a guild having a day on the schedule before entering PoF. Just as a courtesy notification.

One other thing, many more powerful guilds go to Fear and take out Cazic-Thule if he is up. He will call the whole zone to his aid. You have to clear the entire zone before engaging him. Some guilds which do not have (nor want) a day on the schedule, but do want to "race" to get a Cazic-Thule kill, have still tried to abide by the schedule by negotiating with, or notifying the guild that has that day. Ususally by dong a "joint" raid and giving all yard trash drops to the guild whose day it is on the schedule. This is considered the more "honorable" way to go to Fear when you want to kill named and still try and respect the schedule.
