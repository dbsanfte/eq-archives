DaoC is a fun change and when I first played it I thoguht I was done with EQ forever. After getting to high levels though, I changed my mind. Here are the plusses and minuses of DAoC.

+ Looting isn't a problem as it auto splits everything equally (items and money)

+ All armor can be dyed any color you like

+ There is no loading between zones (only between cities and dungeons)

+ RvR is incredible fun. A sense of fear goes through you when you invade other countries. Massive wars are so much fun. It's like it's out of braveheart

+ Clerics, friars and paladins all get rez. Clerics and friars get it at level 10.

+ When you die you don't have to go loot your body. All that happens is thaty ou lose a little experience and a gravestone is errected where you were. If you pray at the stone you get a lot of the exp back.

+ Getting places isn't an issue as horses are readily affordable and quick for everyone.

+ You get new skills and spells quite often. Every level you get points that you can customize your character with. Every class has multiple routes you can take to tailor yourself.

- Dungeons are vastly overcrowded.

- Some dungeons aren't finished yet (looting tables arent done).

- Higher levels pretty much force RvR. There is a lack of high level zones and they did this on purpose for RvR.

- This would be ok but you don't get any experience for killing in RvR. You only get realm points.

- Which sucks again because right now the realm points do nothing.

- All the armor looks the same. A level 10 armsman's platemail is going to look the same when he's level 50. There are very very very very very few unique looking items.

- Some of the animation isn't done quite right (jumping for instance).


SUMMARY: The early game I think far surpasses Everquest. But the high level game in my opinion right now does not. Hence I am back in EQ and doubt I will be heading back to Camelot. I do recommend you try it out though. You probably will enjoy it and may end up staying.
