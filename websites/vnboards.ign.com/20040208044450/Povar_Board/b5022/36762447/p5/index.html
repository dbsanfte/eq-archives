Those are some good nostalgia-yanking memories, Gigantuous. They ALMOST make me want to go out and buy EQ again. But I know those innocent times will never be again.

The first 6 months of EQ were definitely the highlight of my gaming life. Every day was a new experience filled with anxiety and fear and wonder. Gigantuous, your post sums it up nice. I remember each of those events fondly. The fears of Runnyeye, getting lost in Highhold pass only to discover that there was a castle in there, or running through WC and seeing in Red, "You have been slashed by Dragoon Zytl" again and again and again until you were dead.

Or even further back to the "beginning":

Trying to find your corpse in the dark forests outside of Erudin. It sometimes took me an hour to find my corpse. "Can someone help me find my corpse!?"

Seeing your first "camp site", whether it be a bandit camp in West Karana or a gnoll camp in the forests of Erudin, was a thrill because you knew that if you got enough people together, you could raid that camp. Then when that first raid actually worked and all the mobs were dead, it was a great sense of satisfaction, even though it was only 2 gnolls.

The first time you hear the cackle of a skeleton as he wacks at you from behind implants in your memory the fear response for months to come. Every time you hear a skeleton cackle, the hairs on your neck rise and you begin to turn around or run away in auto-response.

Going into a dungeon for the first time (for me, it was Blackburrow), seeing all the people gathered at the inside near the zone in and wondering, "What is everyone doing here?". Then trying to walk further in and seeing, "Train!" pop up in red text. Following that comes a dance of watching death as you first watch 3 or 4 people try to stand and fight against the train near the exit, and then watch one fall, then another, then watch the remaining fighters zone out of the area just before you watch yourself die and say to yourself, "That's why everyone was standing around the zone out."

Learning the hard way not to stand around and gawk at the new monsters you see. Whether it be a griffon, a sand giant, a skeleton, or a simple rat … standing and watching with mouth open the wonders of the world of everquest will more often than not get you killed by the wonder at which you stare.

Those were the beauty of the first 6 months of Everquest.

I would have saved myself much heartache if I had chosen a different class and race. I heard all kinds of opinions on which classes and races were the most difficult to level. The Erudite Wizard was never among the top of people's lists. But I always thought an Erudite of any class was very difficult to level when EQ first came out because we sucked so bad at early levels and the relative ignorance of EQ gamers (beta testers not included) meant that us Erudites would continue to try and get experience in that terrible Tox Forest. It wasn't until level 6 (or so) that I tried to go across the boat to Qeynos and find my way around the main continent.

I remember fondly when us Erudites tried that. It was me, Disputatious, Oxide and Mummeroth. Mumm was a Necro and he told us that he had to be very careful. I didn't understand at the time that NPCs would try to kill him if they saw him. IIRC our entire party got seperated and I got lost in Qeynos. It wasn't until a few months later that I met up with Disputatious and Mummeroth again (Oxide and I stayed in contact throughout) and we all joined up in a guild of mainly casters (and later joined together into the infamous Nightmares Asylum).

I do remember the name Zehaim among the realm of fuzzy names I remember from my Erudite youth. Thanks for the reminder of Runngupp! I completely forgot about him and his blindness spell. He was the first truly difficult kill for us Erudites. My first kill of him required me and 3 others. We tried all kinds of tricks to kill him: from standing on the bridge and shooting down at him to fighting blind with our weak staves.

I also remember the NPC necro badass who would tend to his skeletons in the cave (that eventually became the entrance to The Hole) in Tox. What a pleasure it was to go back many levels later and destroy him with one spell. At one time I thought going back and destroying the Kerran (Queen? I forgot her title) would also be a pleasure. But I didn't do it until I was questing for that damn gold ring! After a while, I hated killing that bitch. And she never did give me that damn ring!


Damn, how I miss those early days of EQ. If I thought for one second that I could return to those early days of exploration and wonder, I would beg my wife to let me get an account again for 2 months. But, alas, I seriously doubt any game will provide me with those moments of enjoyment again.

Thanks for the memories!
