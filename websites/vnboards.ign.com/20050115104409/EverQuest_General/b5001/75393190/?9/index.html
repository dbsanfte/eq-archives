I'll try to give a useful answer:

I have quit and returned many times myself. Everytime a new game comes out, I end up on an EQ hiatus. I'm back now and having a blast with my Paladin. The game is very high end intensive, especially on the established servers. It can be very difficult to level up from about 45 to 60 since there are a lot of 65's doing the raiding of the high end content.

Be prepared for some folks who think their pixels in the game make them some sort of gods irl. Sadly, there are people in game that are this way. However, there are lots who are very nice.

If you want to play EQ now, I'd suggest starting on one of the newer servers. These are at the top of the server list when you log in. Find a guild and get to know some people. If you can find the guild and people that match your play style, this can be a very fun game.

The high end of the game takes a lot of time and dedication. If you do not have this time, then you will be frustrated as you approach level 50. In this case, you might be better off waiting on EQ2, which should be more of a casual/smaller raid friendly game.

The economy of most servers is whacked. There are people who farm items/plat to sell on ebay. This results in a glut of plat in some folks' hands who are willing to spend real life $ for game items/cash. Think about paying 100,000 plat for one ornate mold for a piece of armor. That should give you an idea how the economy is in the game now.

I enjoy the game, but I've been playing off and on since 3/1999. I'm social. I enjoy grouping and helping people. This usually results in people helping you. If you are a social recluse, you can still play but the game won't be nearly as fun imho.
