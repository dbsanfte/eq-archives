Or perhaps they could use the tribute system to better effect... sell items that have been pulled out of the existing economy (but were earned to a greater or lesser degree by "the server").

In fact, Sony could probably make a good bit of money as the middle man for lots of those transactions-- easing the flow of items from people who play long hours to the people who play short hours--without totally corrupting the economy (since it's already "corrupted" by the sale of online plat).

I've poked my head back into EQ because a couple of friends who still play a lot convinced me to. However I'm well behind them in the places I can go (flags) and the missions I can do (gear/AAs/levels/MORE flags).

The PoP flagging system was difficult because friendships don't develop strictly from people with the same play schedule. They tend to, but when someone's schedule changes it's a shame to lose that friendship. They've realized that, and are looking for solutions.

Any solution you'd still want to balance against all the concerns of the "rich" being able to "own" the game because they can afford all the best stuff, obviously. So there's a fine line to it.

But being able to come back to the game, buy some platinum from a website in order to get some useful items from the bazaar was extremely helpful (in fact-- I wouldn't have done it otherwise-- I would have just played someone else's character).

It enabled me to be more than a tag-along character with some old friends. I could actually play and be fairly useful. I think it was good that I couldn't buy top of the line equipment-- but getting some of the things which are considered "basics" (focus items, some mana regen, etc) made me feel a bit less sub-par for having been out of the game for a year and a half.

Obviously, buying a fully geared (and flagged-- gah those friggin flags) would have been even easier, and I might have considered it if I was actually going to be playing with the same amount of time as before.

But this way I could jump back in, have people say "hey Aktar, long time no see"... and kill a couple of bad guys occasionally.

In that respect-- coming up with a solution to these kind of problems is actually building some retention for Sony as well.

Bottom line for me: I was wary of online plat purchase, but it ended up working out alright for me... within what I wanted and what I expected. Now if only I could buy some PoP flags and AAs... that'd be even better!

Actually, Eeyon-- I would have had a reason to buy plat from an online site regardless of whether or not a plat duping bug over-inflated prices.

The scale may have been different, but the fundamental issue wouldn't have been: being out of the game for an extended period of time leaves you with a character which can either (sort of) pick up right where you left off (with a different group of people) or a character which needs a series of basic things to play with the friends who have continued to play during your absence.

The arguments about online money interfering with the purity of the game have been done to death-- and frankly, I agree with them.

The problem is they haven't found a way to get around the "you are paying for the time I spent acquiring this [item, cash, x y z, etc]"... and until they do, the reality of online auction sites will continue.

And the reality that some (me included) don't have enough time to play this game in a certain way leaves three possibilities for Sony (or more specifically-- for any given account):

1) don't play the game and cancel account
2) be happy playing the game another way
3) purchase equipment/plat/characters to let you play the game the way you want to.

Obviously Sony would prefer choices #2 or #3... because that means they don't lose revenue.

Up until now, they have discouraged #3.

But the truth is, it's a lot easier for them to address #3 than it is for them to address #2 (City of Heroes addresses #2 an entirely different way with the "side-kick" system... EQ doesn't have that possibility).

I'm sure other games may come up with a solution to the "differing play-time for friends" issue, but EQ never did. And player auction sites seem to have done the best of a bad set up.
