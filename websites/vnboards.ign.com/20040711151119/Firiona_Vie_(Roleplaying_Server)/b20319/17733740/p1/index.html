If I understand the history of Norrath correctly, the dark elves were created through a corruption of light elves. If this is the case, then dark elves should hate other elves above all others. Other fantasy literature from which VIs dark elves are based (TSR) has similar story lines that dark elves are distant relations of "surface" elves, and the two hate each other without exception.

Also, If I take a dark elf SK, and bring him near *any* wood or high elf NPCs (druid or otherwise) I am very KOS. This is also consistent with the animosity between the elven races. It is clear to me that DE & other elves would 99.9999% of the time want to have nothing to do with one another.

Now, several ppl have said that we should just let player RP decide if/how/when unlikely companions will work together. The problem with that is that if players could be relied on to uphold logical RP choices when grouping/guilding, there would really be no need for the FV server. This is obvious by the fact that on the 30 odd other servers hardly anyone observes these RP guildelines.

So what is the point of FV then? The point IMO is that there should be some logical guidlines to enforce RP behavior of players, so that convenience will not overule any semblance of an RP atmosphere. The problem is that, again IMO, these rules have not been well enough thought out. It is a good start, but I don't think they are complete enough. As a result, I foresee several guilds forming mixing dark elves, wood elves, druids, necromancers, etc without regards to any consideration for RP. Once those guilds become predominant, FV will not look all that different than any other server, which was exactly what I was hoping to escape.
