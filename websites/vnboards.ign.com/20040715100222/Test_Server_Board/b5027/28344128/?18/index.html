I for one would consider an EQ2 only if it re-invented itself.

Top of the list would be adding something to the level grinding; any ( or all ) of the following would help.

- classes balanced arithmetically ( no more judgement calls )
- ability to alter the world in some fashion
- politics ( become mayor, or high priest, King, or even a God)
- tradeskills that produced better items than could be found on monsters.
- DM your own dungeon in the game.

EQ really burnt me out on online RPG's. Tried the other new ones and just couldn't go through the repetition again.

VI did take the easy way out every time though. If a bug came up that slowed down levelling, it was never fixed, but if it was declared an “exploit” ( i.e. it sped up levelling ) it was instantly patched, often at the expense of nerfing the entire spell/class as needed.

At this point I'd rather play an hour or 2 of Diablo or Dungeon Siege. No downtime, no medding, find lots of stuff, be really powerful and waste everything in sight....

I don't hate or love VI. If they put a game together that has something new I'll consider it. Or anyone else’s game. Shadowbane has quite a few elements from my list.
-----------
When I started EQ my vision(tm) was to get to L50 and reign supreme, be able to kill anything in the game, and be all powerful. Exact my revenge against all the monsters that squicked me when I was a lowbie.

When I reached L50 I discovered that I was weaker then I was L1 (comparatively). At L1 I could solo a white or even a yellow if I was lucky, I didn't lose XP on death, and I only had to med a minute to get back to full strength.

At L50 I was lucky to be able to solo a blue, lots of the greens could kill me, and I still huddled at the zone lines so I could run away (like a little girl) if needed. Never ever did feel really powerful. A L1 Bixie could (and often did) still resist my 50+ spells

That’s the interesting paradox about EQ. The higher level you get, the weaker you are in comparison to an equivalent level monster.
