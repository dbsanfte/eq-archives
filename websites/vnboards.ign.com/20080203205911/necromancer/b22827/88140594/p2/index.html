Kreuachee - I agree that it is utterly bizarre the Soulreaping has no associated skills. I'm not really sure what to make of that. However, I would suggest that Necro spells are generally designed on the assumption that you have Soulreaping, given how expensive they all are (almost all are 10 or 15 Energy, which is about the amount you can expect to have immediately after something dies with a decent Soulreaping score), and if you don't have Soulreaping, and intend to use Necro skills, well, I'd be very surprised if you genuinely rarely run low on Eenrgy, unless you have Claude or a player necro slavishly following you around with Blood Ritual or Blood is Power.

Could Soulreaping stand improvement, specifically some good skills based on it? It certainly could! However, is it worthless, as you suggest with "no point"? Not remotely.

The situations in which it is useful are not rare when I play. Maybe me and the people I play with a playing wrong, but I don't think so. I often come across encounters where large numbers of deaths occur (usually on the opposing side). I often successfully fight 5+ mobs at a time (with henchmen), and more often than I'd like, other mobs add during an encounter. I've prevented total wipes before with Putrid Explosion, as the party dies they provide me with the energy to detonate all their bodies, killing off the creatures which were attacking us, and leaving me and at least a couple of others standing.

Also, you say it's rare that you run out of Energy. Well, either you're a liar, or you don't cast spells, or most likely, what you mean is that you don't often hit 0 Energy. I can believe that, but that's far from the same thing as having abundant Energy. Now, if you're cleanly engaging 2-4 mobs with full recharges between, sure, Energy Storage is great, as I said. It's just that I don't see that situation that often.

It's a surprisingly powerful skill. Try it out and see. It may not seem like much when it's at 3 or 4, but by the time it gets to 6 to 8, you will REALLY be noticing it. With that and Offering of Blood, I literally have not had to stop casting spells due to lack of energy (barring brief interruption by hugely energy draining Mesmers) for the last week.

There are a lot of issues you have touched on in regards to Necromancer as the primary class.

Most notably, of course, is the lack of runes and headgear.

I've tried many things with the Necromancer primary... but I keep comming back to using it as a secondary.

My absolute favorite character so far in PvP is a Necromancer secondary and Ranger primary.

In an effort to explore the possibilities of using a Necromancer, I stumbled across a partial solution -- that is to say, one of our oft-used spells: Vampiric Touch.

And then it hit me. Vampiric Touch was not a Spell. It is a Skill. Then I looked around further still and discovered something very interesting...

Necromancers have four "Skill" type skills: Vampiric Touch, Touch of Agony, Vile Touch, and the wonderful Plague Touch. And, being Skills, they fall under Ranger's Expertise reduction. With a fast recycle time and drastically lowered costs... it's quite powerful, and I suggest you who have become dissatisfied with Necromancer primary to try variations of it.

Soul Reaping may come in handy. as may Death Magic spells... but the problem is, someone has to die. If you rely too heavily on needing corpses, you might well find that the only one you have to use is yours.

Therein lies one of the largest problems with the Necromancer class. You must bide your time until someone dies if you focus so much on Death Magic and Soul Reaping, and thus your team is weaker initially for it at the least.

Blood Magic is very good at surviving, but energy constraints and recycle times can pose problems. Then there's Curses... powerful, yes, but very tactical and wasteful if your teammates don't cooperate / don't know you.

Mesmers are billed as the most difficult class at times, yet they have nothing on Necromancers.

-Greven
