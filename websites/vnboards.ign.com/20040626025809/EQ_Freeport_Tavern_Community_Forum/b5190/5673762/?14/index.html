It's always best to group with someone your level, but not always possible. If I plan to get anything done, I'll group with someone by a formula I use. You take the digit in the "tens place" of you level and add 1 to it. That's your group range.

1-9 = +-1 level
10-19 = +-2 levels
20-29 - +-3 levels
And so on.

You can get experience outside of this range, obviously. But this will make it worthwhile to group.

usually you cant pick and choose your group, but if you can then outer circle is right, try to stay within three lvls of yourself. but the way exp works is that each person takes a certain amount from each kill.

a lvl 27 troll shadow knight would lvl just as fast as a lvl 27 human warrior. basically in a group you get the same exp as others your lvl and people that are higher take a little bit more and so on.

so if you a lvl 25 warrior and are grouped with a lvl 30 cleric your not going to notice nearly as big of a difference in exp as if you were grouped with a lvl 30 ranger or pally. so if you really care about exp more than having fun with friends then never group with hybrids unless you have to. humans of course are the best and trolls suck too, a troll sk is the slowest lvling thing in the game.

as far as the max cap works its pretty large. at lvl 23 i was grouped with a lvl 31 and still got exp. i play to have fun not to lvl really fast but if you cared that is how exp works. there is a huge thread on it somewhere in the everquest message boards, someone used show eq and figured it out. its hard to explain and i hope i didnt mess it up too bad.

another thing is that if you are in a group with all melee classes and one cleric you probably wont lvl as fast because your group cannot fight continuosly because you have to wait for the healer to med. a balanced group will always lvl faster
