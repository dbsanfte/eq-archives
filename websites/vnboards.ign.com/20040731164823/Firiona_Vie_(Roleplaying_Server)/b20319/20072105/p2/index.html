Let's look at all the points in turn.

"[Twinking] is actually fun..." I guess for some it must hold an appeal - I've met others who actually level up a "soloist" character like a Necro or Druid for the sole purpose of farming loot for another, more group-friendly primary character. You thus get the odd situation in which the player's higher-level character is not the primary.

Personally, I've got a 50+ on another server, but I never did more than consider twinking my alts - for me, the game is fun for the challenge and twinking takes away much of the challenge at lower levels.

"Jealousy..." Don't discount jealousy in the MMOG world. Even though EQ is a PvE environment and is generally group-oriented, you are still "competing" with the other players to a greater or lesser extent. Jealousy is a driving factor in much of what makes EQ what it is - you want to get to the same level as that ranger that rampaged through the gnolls that just killed you. You want that breastplate, you want that spell.

Twinking can skew the jealousy towards anger. You have a chance to catch up with the warrior the same level as you who already has a couple pieces of his crafted; you have no chance to catch up to the twink with full Cobalt.

"You stupid or something?" Tough call. The truly stupid are not smart enough to realize they are stupid and often say dumb things, especially on message boards.

"They EARNED their equipment..". On THIS server that is a good argument - any twinked gear will actually have been "earned" by someone. The TLC ensures that someone likely fought long and hard to get the BIBS or the BBC that the twink is carrying. On other servers, that argument still holds water, but it is brown and murky water that smells funny. Yeah, you worked hard to get the character up to uberness, but how hard was it for the 60th Necro to get that BIBS for the warrior twink?
