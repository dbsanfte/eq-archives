Lenore listened to both tales, horror clearly showing on her lovely face. She had heard of the battles and could not believe the once serene forest was now a living graveyard. It was when her husband had wanted to investigate was when she truly saw the devastation. The moans of the tortured and the damned sent chills along her spine, and seeing the tormented souls, their flesh scorched to their bones and still wearing their charred armor and carrying their ruined weapons froze her in terror. She and her husband had to flee for their lives. The sight of bodies of less fortunate travelers seared itself into her brain.
She felt Lladerek's pain at the loss of his lover, for if she were to lose her beloved husband she would surely die of a broken heart. She kept her sympathies to herself, for she was sure he would not want to hear her condolences. She turned misted violet eyes to him and he saw the offer, and nodded in such a way only she saw it. She sent a silent prayer to her God for the dead and for Lladerek to find some semblence of peace in his heart. She knew he was a hardened warrior, but even the most seasoned had feelings, be they of love and hate, and she could tell he had loved his lost love.
She quickly turned her gaze away from him lest he see her thoughts.
She looked down and twirled her wedding ring around her finger. Yes, she knew she would cease to live should her husband be taken from her. She lowered her face and raised her fingers to her lips and kissed the ring.
