Hmm.. well, for myself, there have been two methods I've used.

First, I "hire" younger guildmates to obtain items (such as microservos) -- when they accomplish the task, I reward them with some piece of gear that I've stashed in the bank (weapons, armor, whatever). That seems to work well --- it gives them a task or mission (excellent RP oppurtunity) gets them some piece of gear that they cannot normally get at their level (usually something 3-5 levels beyond their current) without just "twinking" them --- they've earned it.

The second thing I've done is as you did --- spend my time with them, healing/buffing/derailing (in case of trains in areas like Crushbone) -- giving up my time to help them. We both obtain what is needed that way --- in my case, the (for instance) microservers, and in their case, valuable experience that would normally take them longer to obtain.

I've never (yet) had to "pay" for something with coin... so I cannot answer market value questions along that line. I do think that 3-5 plat seemed a fair price in this case. Whomever it was asking for "Much more" was out of line --- the Trueshot might sell for 100 plat --- but there is much more to it than the micros (IMO).

Glad you got some of what you need --- good luck in the crafting <g>.
