Trolls are slightly more intelligent than ogres but still rather stupid. To show this, a lot of RPers adopt the "trollspeak" method of communicating.

The Cazic-Thule troll is a troll that worships the Lord of Fear, the Faceless. You are a shaman of your people. It is your duty, therefore, to inspire fear in others, to spread your Lord's influence. Trolls are indeed cruel, but not in clever, sadistic ways... they're more brutal.

Too, as a shaman, remember that you command spirits. Manifest that however you choose. Your personal belief could be that the spirits you command are the dead trolls of the past assisting the trolls of today in their work.. or perhaps the spirits of the light races, forced into servitude after death as was their fate. Nature spirits, too, if you want to go that path. Whatever you please.

Good luck in your portrayal.

Trollspeak won't be necessary. Think about it. You're proud, educated (By trollish standards) and charismatic (When compared to, say, a Froglok). Now, most of my now Iksar Cazicites have a motif of 'faceless'-usually shown in a featureless mask- in their description. Now, I can't show it in equipment (*grumblemutter*) but I consider that it adds to the effect. Think of how scary a troll is when you can -see- their face. Now think how scary they'd be when it's left to imagination.

Keep in mind that Cazic's goal is to spread fear, but this is open to individual interpretation. For example, instead of going on a spree of murder, wound them horribly and leave them alive...show them how close you can take them to death. -That- is infinitely more terrifying than simple oblivion.

And..I'd never even considered racial interpretations of the spirits before! God, my face is red. I'll have to think about this.

Cyst doesn't use trollspeak when he speaks his own language; naturally he would be fluent in that. No, the 'troll speak' only comes into play when speaking Common and such.

Speaking OOCly, I've often found trollspeak is a wonderful way to get your fellow troll and ogre players into character. Don't knock it til you've tried it. Everyone has their own flavor of trollspeak, and it leads to a wealth of fairly unique trollish characters.
