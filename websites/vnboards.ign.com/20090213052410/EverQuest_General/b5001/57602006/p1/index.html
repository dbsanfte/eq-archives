The basic problem I have with ATI cards are the bugginess and incompatability issues with alot of games outside true 3d games. My Radeon made games like Neverwinter Nights and some strategy games unplayable, while a much older Geforce was silky smooth. Even in EQ, with latest drivers, I get several bugs with Radeon if I enable higher or lower anisotropic filtering and anti-aliasing or disable either entirely. Bizarre stuff like reversing and disabling keyboard functions within the game in addition to the expected graphic rendering glitches. Now it does run like a champ but only in standard settings. Never saw these issues with the Geforce, it either worked perfectly or not at all depending on the driver.

ATI are slower to release driver upgrades and when they do they tend to create more issues with certain games or features.

On the speed issue, I can see the very slight edge Radeon has with the 9700-9800 cards over the latest from Nvidia, but tend to take all the published framerate numbers with alot of salt. Both companies have insisted PC or game magazines and websites evaluate and test their cards using settings and software that is pre-formated specifically for their card to get the very best results. ATI is documented as being the worst for this practice, making common 3rd party software to test framerates incompatible or non-functioning on their cards under higher stress settings like full AA at very high resolutions.
