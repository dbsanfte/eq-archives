Falamil writes:

"Donal:

"My rights end where yours begin."


Then you will understand my support of people not wandering a public street in any mind-altered state, be it alcohol or drug-related. Correct?"

Wandering the street, per se, should not cause any disruption of other rights. There are two keys here, for legitimately legislating. The first is to not excuse behavior, like we often do with alchohol, merely because the person was effect by a drug they voluntarily consumed. The second, is a reasonable degree of prophelactic. That is, drive while drunk does not per se conflict with the rights of others, but there is sufficient statistical basis for a strong likelihood of serious negative consequences to others from doing so to justify a prophelactic rule prohibiting driving while intoxicated.

My feelings are that use of Marajuana is unlikely to generate a significant risk of serious negative consequences to others sufficient to justify any prophelactic rule against its use. Using alchohol and tobacco as the legal standard, I think that is pretty clearly correct. Of course, using alchohol as the legal standard, a number of other illegal drugs would easily meet that threshhold. Whether the problem is with the illegality of those drugs or the legality of alchohol, I'll leave to you. I don't think a rational arguement can be made for the legalization of alchohol as compared to the illegalization of many drugs, marajuana amoung them.
