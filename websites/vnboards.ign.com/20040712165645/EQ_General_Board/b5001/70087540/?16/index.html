Most creatures are on a timer, in some cases the timer is a random time, but my experiene almost all the spawns are on fixed timers. Some of the older Planes were on 8 hour timers, this caused some major griefing metween certain guilds in the past. now a large poortion of teh timers are 15 to 20 mins.

In some zones the spawn locations are randomised so you cannot camp a certain spawn point for a rare spawn (Quillmane to those magi out there, or the hunter/forager spawns for seb key), in other areas the spawns are completely static in their location.

The exception to the timer rules are triggered spawns, whether through traps, quests or GM madness, or LDON instanced zones where there is no respawn of a defeated creature.

One of the issues with zones like Everfrost and other wide open zones is that there is only a certain percentage chance of getting the same type of creature to spawn as was killed. This means that when you kill a mommoth in everfrost there may only be a 50% chance that it will respawn as a mommoth, a 10% chance of a mommoth calf, 10% chance of an orc.
Unfortunately what this means is that as less and less people visit a zone and hunt accross the whole spectrum of chreatures certain types of creatures become rare, as they are being hunted to extintion

One way to counter this is as Okami suggested get a GM to re-pop the zone, another way is to go out there and kill all roaming mobs that you encounter, this is slower but a lot more likely to occur.

This is what I believe happens, as in North Karana, unless I kill snakes, bloodhounds, etc I run out cragspiders to kill for their silks.
