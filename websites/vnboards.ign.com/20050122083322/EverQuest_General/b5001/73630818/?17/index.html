I tend to agree that a lot of people moving to WoW from EQ will be disappointed.

WoW is very fun and fast and pretty, but it's also an extremely casual-oriented MMO. A great majority of people I know that play EQ play it exactly because it's such a hardcore MMO. Even casual gamers find EQ's scope and difficulty appealing - because the truth is most people actually don't want to hit end-game in a month (even if they ~think~ they do).

I expect a large exodus from EQ to WoW, followed by a similarly large recoil after a month or two. FFXI will see the same thing.

This is not because WoW will be bad (on the contrary I think the final is going to be an amazingly fun MMORPG) but because it's just not deep or complex enough to engage a player base so obsessed with complexity. Like many people I plan to buy, play and enjoy it, but there's simply not enough there to make it my "main" MMO.

I think the more fringe games - DAoC, AC2, AO, even CoH - will feel WoW's impact the hardest. EQ and FFXI and their established player bases will probably encounter more of a "low tide" than a "drought."
