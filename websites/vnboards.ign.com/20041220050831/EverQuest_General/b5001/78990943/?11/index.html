EQ will still be around 5 years from now just like UO is still around and both will have a stable population. It's sad how everytime a new online game is released there's several posts about how EQ will be dead in a year, when even AC (yes, I can't believe it either) is still hanging around.

I definately think it's worth trying out, especially since you can get that EQ Platinum box for around $24, that's all the expansions up except the last 2 which are strictly middle/upper- high level content. Look at it this way, for about half the price of a new consule game that usually only lasts a week, you will have a month to try out EQ before any monthly fee starts.

If you are still playing on an older, maxed, unupgradeable system like me, or have no definate interest in the Alliance v Horde PvP aspect of WoW (which, my opinion, will be 95% of the high end game there for quite some time) I would definately consider trying EQ1 out and seeing what you think.

On how good or bad EQ2 is I can't comment, a friend actually gave me the collector's DVD version of that as an early Christmas gift here and my older comp doesn't even have a DVD player to install it from.

Ah, the art book and the soundtrack CD are nice, yeah.

From what I have heard EQ2 begins feeling very "fantasy generic RPG" and it's not until you are in the mid teen levels and starting to see more of the world and using those team heroic special attacks it starts to really feel unique. On the other hand I heard it compared to a MMO version of Morrowind, which I think is the best RPG to come out the last 10 years.

Heya! I and several of my friends are the elusive "casual" (solo) players that everyone is looking for and apparently trying to appease (we are the irregular regulars of the gaming world).

Because of real life situations, we pretty much dropped on the idea of "team" interaction and had to go the solo route while leveling in EQ 1 and thought that the devs (game developers) would continue to promote a gaming atmosphere for both team and casual players. However, that did not happen and the devs "thumbed" the casual players and left us out to dry.

Since there are so many guild players just trying to "level out" on the game, the "casual" players got kicked to the gutter and we had to drop when the going got tough in later quests and game expansions were served up to "team" and "guild" players only.

It took me a year (a fun year mind you) just to get to a level 40 Iksar Necro (both solo and team play intermingled). However team play was harder to find after level 35.

My other friends averaged anywhere from around 35 to 50 levels over that same year. But,....IT WAS FUN until we got swept to the curb when solo stats were taken from us and quests were made unattainable for casual gamers without guilds or regular playing partners on line.

Making a long note short...if it is true that EQ II has gone the way of team play only then NO it is not worth playing for the single, casual gamer. The guilds will drop you like a 3 day putrified fish if you cannot spend the time THEY want you to spend with them. Until the game developers can satisfy the need for both the casual "irregular regulars" and the guilds, then we casuals will continue to move on to the next company's game hoping to find the Shangri La for casual players.

WoW requires more of your computer than even the latest expansion of EQ (OoW). Not much more, something along the lines of 4 gigs of hard drive space.

Upgrading your computer is always the gamers bane to the next generation of games that come out every week..

Thats moot. What is something I did see is that WoW costs more a month, unless you buy the 6 month blocks, which puts it right at where it is for EQ for 1 month.

I am sure there are new people starting on servers daily, however a huge majority of low levels (1-60) are going to be alternates, bazaar mules, tradeskill mules, etc. I have various low level alternates and I do remember grouping with an entire group of real new EQ folks in an LDON adventure.

What will help is if you start with a group of RL friends. That is my advice to anyone who wonders.. Ask yourself, what are my friends going to do, is that something I want to do? If you do have friends and all is totally new to these types of games (MMORPG), folks can go their direction and then each other can check out the game at each other's house.

As for guild's, I know that all three have these systems, WoW and EQII to me seem confusing, (lack of knowledge about them) from what I can gather from my old guildmates and friends who have moved on. However on Rodcet Nife there are low-to-relatively high level guilds you can grow up with and enjoy the game with. There is no red tape as far as what guild you join, no matter your class/race/diety/starting city combo. Unlike the other 2 games, they have those stipulations. Which seems like it could force some folks not to naturally go for something they want.

New sometimes seems better, greener, more lush but I think that was only applied to the era of when EQ was released.. call me biased now!
