Man, you guys all need to come to Cabilis or something. Sure, FoB is busy with the usual newbie traffic, but I've run into several RPers who'll stop to chat, and lots and lots of others who'll at least make in-character (IC) comments and thanks when you help them out in a fight. All of you folks looking for RP, as someone else mentioned above, have you actually tried instigating it? Or are you just standing there watching and waiting for someone to walk up and start talking to you? My coolest RP scene yet got started by me standing outside the FoB gate (where there's naturally lots of traffic to hear me) b****ing out one of the NPC guards for not helping us kill a Burynai. That drew the interest of a few other people who started talking too. One of whom (after we had it out in a duel in the middle of the courtyard, with all the shocked onlookers gathered 'round) has become an in-game friend, and we still RP back and forth as fellow members of the Legion.

Act IC. Salute your elders, even if they're NPCs. Make comments to them. Other people passing by will hear them, and even if they're not tempted to say something to you, it lends a lot to the atmosphere. And just /ignore the idiots using /shout and /auc for stupid OOC stuff. They'll always be there but their relative numbers will decrease, I think, as the server ages a bit.

My thoughts on /dueling... (kinda long, please try not to take offense, this is just something to think about).

In an RP environment (and speaking as a MUSH administrator, which granted isn't nearly on the scale of EQ, but it's sort of the same kind of thing), consensual combat is a GOOD thing. It's not perfect, and you'll still run into goobers who'll abuse it by acting like nits and then refusing to fight you, but you can always just write them off as cowards IC and mock them if you want. It's certainly a darn sight better than open PvP. Go on, try to RP on the Zeks some time. The hell you catch from griefers FAR outweighs any instance of actual quality RP that requires PvP. (Such was my experience, at least - both there and on Darktide.)

Open PvP attracts griefers, it will ALWAYS attract griefers, and unless you can add enough GMs to police the griefers out (impossible, at this scale), it's just not going to be a viable RP environment until you're level 60 and have little to fear from the goobers. It's one thing to be afraid of someone IC. To emote gulping nervously and stepping aside, or making yourself scarce, or foolishly stepping up and getting in the guy (or gal)'s face. It's quite another to have to fear other players OOC because they're just out to ruin YOUR (the player's) day, not just your character's. To start to emote gulping nervously, only to be attacked without so much as an IC word from the other person. Consensual combat protects you from that kind of garbage. And yeah, it shields the goobers from consequences too, but really, I'm willing to trade one goober I have to ignore for 6 griefers who'd like to kill and corpse-camp me.

Personally, if someone challenges my character to a duel with a good reason, I can't see him turning it down. Basically what I'm saying is, if the other person is a mature roleplayer and there really is an in-character reason to fight, they'll probably accept it. And if they don't, well, big deal, just ignore them, they're not really hurting you. Either they don't possess the maturity to RP a serious scene (in which case, why bother), or maybe they just have a pressing OOC reason not to want to let the scene lead to combat. They just ran 8 zones and aren't bound yet. They have the flu and just aren't in the mood. As a (hopefully) mature roleplayer yourself, you'll be considerate of their OOC feelings. Lack of such consideration is called griefing; if that's what you're into go to Sullon Zek where that behaviour is accepted and expected. Or go to DAoC if that's what floats your boat. (Hey, open combat can be fun sometimes when everyone understands the rules and understands that they WILL be attacked without notice. It just doesn't make it an easy roleplaying environment.)

Getting back to the original topic... I think FV has a lot of potential if we don't all give up on it in the first week. Keep roleplaying. We're out here. Look me up in-game if you want; Vos isn't the MOST jovial guy to hang around, but if you can appreciate a Klingon-warrior-type mindset and a bit of sardonic humor, maybe you can tolerate him. I usually play evenings EST, when I'm not playing with my other group of friends (who also RP!) on BB.

Voskor Oth, Proud Militiaman of Cabilis

My first impressions were positive although as I said before it'll be necessary to wait awhile before we know if it really works.

I created my Iksar monk. Unfortunately, what with having to go to bed early and taking time out for the President's news conference I was only able to get to level 3. Since I only stayed in the Iksar area (didn't even SEE a non-Iksar) I really couldn't see how the new rules will affect things first hand (something Shadoe should have thought about before writing his review).

Despite that I did notice a few interesting things.

1. There were virtually no shouts, /oocs, or /auctions. In fact I don't think I saw a single /auction. Mostly this was good although there were a few times when people had to run to the guards where I would have appreciated a quick "train to zone" shout or somesuch. On one of those occasions I discovered first hand that a bandit can one shot kill a level 1 Iksar monk However, since I was level 1 it wasn't a big deal. I just looted my corpse and got on with it.

2. There were a LOT of people. Cabilis and Field of Bone were crawling with lizzies. I have never saw so many Iksar in one place. Cabilis (the side next to Field of Bone, I can never remember if that's East or West) usually had 30 or so people which is unheard of on my old server (Xegony). When I logged off and looked at the servers sorted by population Firiona Vie was the most populated (the new PvP server was the least populated by the way). I suppose that's to be expected on a server which is trying something new but if the population doesn't come down I hope Verant opens a second server with the same rules because there may be demand for it.

3. People were unusually polite and helpful. If two people jumped the same critter there was often a "you take it"/"no you take it" kind of response. Very refreshing to see. Verant probably helped this attitude a lot in that the spawn rate in the newbie areas seemed to be high enough that everyone was able to kill lots of stuff. When I asked where to find some newbie things (it's been a LONG time since I was a newbie) people were always happy to give directions. I also didn't see anyone begging money, food, or items off of other players. Now admittedly a level 3 Iksar isn't going to attract beggers like a level 46 Iksar, but I didn't see people begging off anyone else either. I was also wondering if some of this politeness was because of the simple fact that most Iksar are only going to be able to group with other Iksar for a long long time (for language reasons if no other). You don't want to make enemies with the only people you can group with.

4. People were very focussed on killing stuff. Again this is not surprising. Most likely almost everyone there was leaving high level characters on other servers and probably wanted to get a few levels under their belts.

All in all I think things are getting off to a great start. I presume that the population will go down in a couple weeks but if Firiona Vie is still one of the most populated servers after that then I think Verant should open another Roleplaying Preferred server soon.

-----------------------------

I've also been giving serious thought to what the new rules mean to Iksar players in particular. Playing an Iksar will definately be even more of a challange down the road. I actually like that because the extra challange is one of the reasons I've always preferred Iksar to other races.

Was I right in my observation that Iksar start out only knowing Lizardman? If so it looks like Iksar who want to do something with non-Iksar are going to have to pay attention to their language skills.

Likewise, I don't know who Iksar can group with. Can they only group with evils or can they also group with neutrals? Also, how far can they change their alignment? While I personally think things should be fairly restrictive (otherwise the server may eventually start to look like every other server if everyone changes their alignment to neutral for example) it does make the game more challanging.

I think this server has a lot to offer and I intend to play on it for awhile. It definately won't be for most people though. People who just want to see how much experience and loot they can get probably won't see much reason to play on this server. None of the rules will make it easier on them and several will make it harder. However, people who just want to immerse themselves in Norrath and don't mind a little harder play may love it.
