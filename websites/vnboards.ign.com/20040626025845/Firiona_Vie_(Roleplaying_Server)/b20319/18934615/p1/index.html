Playing this past week, and reading these posts, I've come to the following conclusions...


(1) Roleplaying is something people either do, or don't. In the game, the only way to convey roleplaying is through emotes and speech, both of which are bugged. Thus, roleplaying is only going to happen with people of the exact same race. This greatly diminishes the intent of the server, imo.

(2) Language barriers and emotes not working are greatly hampering the roleplaying atmosphere. Last night, a druid laughed at me and my partner. I had no idea if it was a playful laugh, or a sinister one. She mumbled some garbled crap at me, how do I know what the context was. The only thing I could do was a /rude and walk off. There's no roleplaying there.

(3) /roleplay is killing the grouping mechanism of Everquest. This game, more than any other, is dependant on grouping. Without being able to check the levels of people in a zone with a /who, and without being able to communicate because of the language bug, grouping is severely restricted. This is sucking the fun right out of this server.

(4) I agree somewhat with the auction thing... Without it, it's just a pain. Auction can be turned OFF. Why remove it? To force people into city zones? I play a lot in East Commons on this server and my old one, when my characters are low level. On old servers the spam from auctions can be so great that I can't understand what my group members are saying because the text flies by so fast. What do I do? I turn auction off. Everything is perfect then.

Other times I just want to sell or buy. East Commons, and other auction-friendly zones, are great places to just sit down and listen to the auction channel.



Bottom line: Roleplaying cannot be enforced with a bunch of rules. People are either going to roleplay or not. Right now, I would say that the server rules are prohibiting roleplaying more than they are helping roleplaying. Those same rules are also prohibiting grouping more than they are helping.

I think a change is necessary. The language barrier either needs to be fixed, so people can skill up normally, or return to a common tongue. The /roleplay tag needs to be removable so people can do a /who and see the levels of the people in a zone. This would help grouping. And, I think auction should return. People can turn it off when they don't want to hear it.

-Skelex

Although I agree with the general sentiment, I thought it might be good to present some good points to our current language predicament:

1. Instead of doing a /who, you can shout for group members since only those who understand you are likely to interested (and benefit in xp terms from compatible alignments) from grouping with you. Level may be an issue but you can /con that when you meet up.

2. I actually quite enjoy grouping with races I can't understand (trolls notably and a lizard once). Those garbled sets of charaters actually add to the air of 'foreigness' and difference about them. It is not in-depth character differentiation but I find the mystique of such a unusual race (and who knows what they're thinking?)quite atmospheric. It makes tactics harder but I don't recruit trolls for clever tactics and can understand if they do their own thing, even if it is not in the best interests of the group. Sometimes mistakes are more interesting than successes.

3. I get to ignore 'lesser' races with a plausible excuse so that the player doesn't feel too aggrieved. This only happened once in a disputed camp where the other group refused to alternate spawns. I could drill out my insults and abuse and he could drill out his safely in the knowledge that neither of us could understand a word of it so I could just let it wash over me. He could (and did) shout out to the world but did I care or even understand it - nope.

4. You invite someone into your group because they show an interest (i.e. hang around the camp). I think this is conducive to friendliness and means that you don't get the pre-judgements common on other servers (no, we only need a healer or a chanter)or mean that people have to go through a pre-invite interview.

On the whole, I do think the language issue does need to be sorted out, especially for trading, but until then (and until I put in the time to learn more languages after it is fixed) I can take solace in these features.
