With all the (admittedly this is an opinion) advanced mmorpg's available to the sophisticated gamer, does anyone actually enjoy everquest for the game itself? Maybe it is community of 'friends' you have met? The death grip on a toon you've spent 5 years developing? (that's a borderline psychosis btw) Phat l3wt? Broken - ass old PC? I'm an ex EQ'r who, at a friends behest, started up again for a couple of months last year and was amazed at how quickly I levelled (60 in a month with the help of a guild, and I'm a part time player). Only to find a guild group on Plane of Valor (I think) sitting on a freakin hillside pulling 'generic mob #8'. The exact same drudgery that made me quit the game the year before.

My experience with returning to EQ in a nutshell:
1. Below level 60, joke.
2. Varied content, zero.
3. Playability for the part - timer, forget it.

Opinions? Flames?


Curious.

P.S. This is of personal interest, so constructive replies would be helpful. I love gaming, but my friend is lost inside of EQ and I am trying to get a feel for why. He is ignoring friends, family and work and I would like to try and understand. These 'online addict' sites seem to be flaky and extreme. I see no problem with chosing not to be productive in the community, particulary if you saw my community, but 16+ hours continuously on a game has me a little worried.
