<Beginning>
The woman screamed. She tried to blank her mind, blocking the horror around her. It did not work, for she felt the Tier'Dal brutally invade her body. He hit her with his mailed fist, trying to silence her screams of terror and pain. She finally succumbed to the blurring vision and let her mind protect her. The elf took her as her body slumped, feeling the pain he produced, and reveling in it. He crowed with passion as he released into her unconscious body. When the feeling subsided, the elf stood, fixed his armor, and left. The woman lay there, near death.

The village was burned to the ground, and most of the inhabitants were slaughtered. Some were taken as slaves to be given as prizes to the Ogre warriors who followed the Children of innorouk in their war. The woman went unnoticed in the heap near the edge of the forest. While the Ogres drank and their shaman sent prayers to their gods, the Tier'Dal legion left, to find a more suitable camp. The hidden figure watched it all. And when he felt the danger was less, he slipped silently into the smoldering village, and carried the woman to safety.

<Middle>
The boy scurried between the patrons of Seafarer's Roost, dodging bodies, fists and splashing ale. "You!" shouted some hulking warrior in the corner. "Clean this table!" The boy ran over with his rag to wipe the spilt drinks and half-eaten food to the floor. "Dloc!" shouted Gregor Nasin. "I need ya to move barrels in the storage." "Yes sir" sighed the boy, as he finished wiping down the table. "Here for yer troubles." said the warrior, flipping a gold piece at the boy. "Thank you, sire." exclaimed Dloc. The warrior just shook his head, mumbling something about half-breeds and bastards.

Dloc ran done to the basement of the tavern. He needed to hurry, so that he could finish sweeping the rooms upstairs, since a large guild had ordered the entire floor. As he leapt the railing down to the basement, a figure jumped from the shadows. "What are you doing!" whispered the figure, as a blade glinted in front of the boy's face. "J-j-just m-m-moving barrels, Mistress TGaza." stuttered the scared boy. "Whelp, I could gut you with my blade." said the dark skinned elf. "I am thinking about it, bastard-boy." "Please Mistress I am just doing chores for Master Nasin." exclaimed Dloc. "Fine, finish and leave." said Nestral TGaza.

Dloc worked feverishly, knowing the beautiful yet deadly female stood nearby, watching his every move. "I can see the blood in you." the rogue purred, "Only Pure-Breeds can see it, but it is there." "I...what ....uh...what do you mean, mistress?" asked the boy. "You are a half-breed, part Tier'Dal. Your skin is darker than even a tree-climber's." "Uh...." mumbled Dloc. The rogue smiled, catlike. "The hatchling tis nearly ready to fly the Roost. Boy, you may be sought by Mistress Nasin. She always needs messengers and such."

<End....and beginning>
Dloc moved silently across the street, keeping to the shadows. The figure ahead of him tripped, then continued stumbling towards the inn. 'Keep the target no more than twenty steps ahead of you, and no less than fifteen'. The advice Beur pounded into the half-elf's head kept Dloc's attention. As the man lurched, the thief made his move. He slid his blade below the purse string and cut it free. Before it fell a hands length, he had grabbed it, and placed it in his backpack. The young man quickly turned, and sprinted away from his victim.

Nestral watched from the rooftop. She turned to Elisi Nasin, and spoke. "He moves well for a half-breed. And his speed is quite good." "Yes, but he has not learned awareness of his surroundings. There is a guard nearby who could have caught him in the act." replied the powerful rogue in Freeport. "Come daughter, let us celebrate arrival of the newest member to our family."

<OOC> BTW I mean "daughter" as a term of endearment, not meaning actual mother-daughter relationship.
