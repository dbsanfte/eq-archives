And so it is said upon the 4011th page of the ninth book of the Prophets of Tunare:

An age ago, past time remembered, there was in the wood of the Faydark a rise that stood clear of trees and was covered in a down of snowy flowers and green moss. And, lo, upon the hillock stood an elf who was fair to look upon with skin like fresh cream and eyes that glinted as grey as coming storm clouds.

And it was he alone who sensed the coming darkness and sought out the wisdom of Tunare to turn that darkness aside. And She came to him in his meditations upon the hillock of green and white and whispered into his thoughts the hope for the future.

“To the elves will be born one great of spirit within whom lies the power to be the salvation of the elven nations, or the downfall. The One will be born pure as daylight, but fall as does the sun into darkness. Still will the One will serve the elven nations, fighting always the darkness growing within.

“This child of the elves must be taught strength through many trials of body and mind. For should the One fall to the darkness and serve that which currutps, all hope will be lost. The strength of the One can overt the coming darkness, or bring it down upon us.

“Let the elven nations watch always for this child of hope and may the One have the strength to save you all.”

“But,” asked he, “How will we know this child from any other?” and his question recieved no answer.

So, then, it has passed down all the years and the Koada’Dal and the Fier’Dal watch with unending vigilence for this chosen child of hope. And they pray they have not already watched the unnamed child grow and leave the Elvenhome.

For they know that this One is their only hope... and deadliest foe.
