LFG does seem harder in the last several months. At level 65, I used to get 4 invitations for groups within 5 minutes of putting up LFG. (One night after I clicked on LFG, I was browsing the LFG page, and by the time I finished and closed the window, I found 4 tells waiting for me.) Lately it's taken anywhere from 1 to 4 hours even when I know a lot of people.

The difficulty has been in trying to find a healer. And here is a bit of history:

Right after GoD expansion came out, many tanks went on a hiatus because they were depressed over how quickly they died in GoD zones where mobs hit for insane damage. All their work in building up their armor seemed to be in vain. Some went to City of Heroes to have fun. For about 2 months, I had 4 great clerics sending me tells each night, but I could only group with one of them at a time. It was sad to watch them LFG for hours when they were very good clerics. Then one by one, they quit. Some went to other games, others rerolled. Then the tanks returned after GoD was re-tuned, but too late. Now we have the reverse situation. Most nights there are many tanks LFG, but no healers. The one night I went LFG for 4 hours, I watched many rogues, rangers, etc. all LFG. Then finally one healer showed up. I grabbed her, and made a group right away. Those rangers, tanks, etc that I could not fit in my group went on LFG. So, in a nutshell, healers get to call the shots these days. They come in and are instantly grabbed. Whoever has a healer can make a group.

I hope the recent change on Test server removing peridot costs from all cleric HP buffs will help increase the healer population. Also, Omens of War expansion may bring more people back. I don't know how things will be for you at level 55. I'm talking about level 65. But good luck to you,... things may improve soon.

I came back about 3 weeks ago as a 56 paladin. At first, getting groups was nearly impossible. I used the lfg tool but I was a bit timid. It did help that I had a friend or 2 still around.

Don't take it personally that folks don't talk much in spatial. It's just the stage the game is in at its current maturity level in the life cycle.
