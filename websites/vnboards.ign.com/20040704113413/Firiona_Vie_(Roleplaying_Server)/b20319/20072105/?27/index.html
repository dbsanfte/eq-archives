I've noticed that some peoples' definitions of "twinking", here and elsewhere, seems to me to be somewhat overbroad.

I think anything acquired through what one might call "normal market forces" (barter, trade, outright purchasing), guild or not, is fair game and not "twinking" at all. By the same token, I think outright gifts on the parts of other players aren't twinking, either (but, please note the "OTHER" in this sentence). Both are, essentially, manifestations of a relatively normal economy, either a normal market one or a "gift economy".

It becomes "twinking", (and, I think, a problem) when a person is giving herself or himself something. Technically speaking, this isn't even a gift--a gift implies altruism, and twinking is the exact opposite: greed.

I came to Firiona Vie to get away from the twinks who have inundated all the other servers. (And anyone who admits she or he has multiple accounts goes immediately onto my /ignore list.) But I don't have a problem with people who get their stuff through Guilds, as gifts from total strangers, or by purchasing items.

First of all, I don't twink on FV. I have one account; it isn't even possible. But I have twinked on other servers. I've not been powerleveled, because frankly, I'm too nice to subject another person to that drudgery, and it's boring as hell for both parties, but worse for the higher level powerleveling person.

I never had much problem with people thinking less of me. Perhaps that's because I'm generally friendly, I don't run around saying 'my stuff is better than your stuff, nyah' or anything similarly childish. I just do my job, usually with a sense of humor, and I do it well. The twinking may make the group's performance a bit better, it may make the downtime a bit shorter, it may somewhat lessen the risk of anyone dying, and it generally means more loot for the other people, because if I don't need something, I don't want it. None of that seems so bad to me.

As to those who twink playing poorly - I find that to be the complete opposite. Your average twink may have played one, two, or half a dozen characters into the 50s or beyond, and you *do* learn things that way. I personally have seven characters in the 50s on another server, none powerleveled, some twinked when they began, some not. But I know all those classes quite intimately, simply from playing them into the 50s (enchanter, druid, necro, rogue, warrior, ranger, cleric).

Having played healing classes, caster classes, pure melee classes, hybrids, good, evil, pretty much everything, gives you empathy for those in your group playing those classes. Whatever class I play now, I know that clerics don't like (or need, unless they suck) to be told when to heal. Regardless of what class I play now, I know that enchanters NEED people to assist. Whatever class I play now, I understand agro management, and its escalating importance. I know how much damage most classes can dispense, and at what levels. I know what buffs most classes have, and at what levels. I know the cleric AC buff at 49 is not worth casting on everyone if you also have a 49 enchanter, since the enchanter AC buff is only slightly worse at that level but costs 200 mana less PER PERSON. I know a lot about a variety of monsters in a number of areas, their weaknesses, their pathing, often their loot, paths to get to them, pull methods, and so on. Knowledge is the biggest twink of all, and it's invisible, and in that respect I am certainly knowledge-twinked on this server; nothing I can do about that; one learns what one learns, and by playing so many classes to relatively high levels I have accrued knowledge.

In any case, twinking exists. Personally, I don't care whether others do it or they don't. I *do* care if they know how to play well. I *do* care if they're friendly and have a sense of humor. I *do* care if they're idiots or d00ds. I *don't* care what they're wearing, and the only reason I ever inspect anyone is to see if I have something that might be of use to them.

My overall philosophy is: 'What goes around comes around' so I try to be kind to as many people as I possibly can. And ya know, that generally works out great, what goes around *does* come around, often in totally unexpected ways. My philosophy has never been different, whether I was playing a twinked character or not, whether I was L1 or L55.

Incidentally, there are significant arguments I could make in regards to twinking helping the economy. It certainly helps some tradeskills; I know from experience. It has some detrimental economic effects too, but the positive effects are impossible to ignore.

But my number 1 reason of all time to twink is: conservation of time. If I acquire, say, an FBSS with necro (who can solo the frenzy room), then why should I go to town, spend the time to sell it to someone, then go to the frenzy room AGAIN and camp it with/for my melee? Simply transferring it optimizes the expenditure of effort: it is camped once that way by me instead of twice, and I don't have to spend time selling it at all.

You can argue that the game is more enjoyable if you go out and earn everything for every character every time (and that might even be true, for you), but if you do so you limit yourself to playing just a few classes because you simply would not have time to play more. And I enjoy playing lots of classes. Every one I have played is fun in its own way, with different strengths and weaknesses. Not twinking ever would have seriously constrained my ability to have as much fun as I have had playing as many classes as I have, simply because time is finite and twinking saved me a great deal of time.

In any case, while I am not a twink on Firiona Vie, I have nothing particular against it as long as the people who do twink aren't arrogant pricks. But if they're arrogant pricks I wouldn't like them anyway, twinked or not.

Ya know, twinking isn't exactly non-roleplaying. Consider the case of Elric of Melnibone. He began his adventuring career with fairly potent wizardly powers, one of the most powerful melee weapons in fantasy literature history, and piles of money; just one of his rings was enough to purchase, well, pretty much anything.

The best way to attempt to roleplay the most 'Elric-like' character possible in EQ would be to twink an Erudite Shadowknight (Erudin being the closest EQ approximation of the Melnibonean Empire and SKs being the closest approximation to the caster/melee type that Elric was). Give him some good armor (fine plate at least, or even better, Steelskin, for its ornate look), along with the biggest baddest two-handed SK sword around, and a few stacks of rubies, sapphires and diamonds. And then let the adventure begin.

Some people are simply too narrow in their definition of what roleplaying is: that it MUST be the poor guy who bootstraps himself into fame and glory through hard work, blah blah. While that is certainly one possible storyline, thinking of that as the ONLY possible storyline means ignoring the possibility of playing characters like Elric, who were born to royalty, inheriting vast sums of wealth and powerful items. Personally, my imagination ranges broader than the 'poor guy makes good' story, which gets rather boring after you've played five or six poor guys who've made good. Why not the rich guy with the nice stuff who is cursed? Or the thief who finds his way into the weapon vault of a powerful warrior and steals his good stuff to start his career? Or whatever; you get my point.

The 'poor guy makes good' storyline isn't inherently 'right' or 'better' than any other. It just happens to be the storyline that ANYONE can follow, because anyone can start out poor.

I have never particularly cared for twinking, but there are very valid reasons to do it, most of which Mheddik outlined fairly well.

The best, of course, is just the desire to get a new character out of the relatively boring "solo levels" and up to a point where the actually gets interesting. For some people, this level is 12, and for others it's 44. Perfectly understandable. Still (for me) very annoying.

I think I already posted my reasons for disliking twinks, but hey, the servers are down, and I can't be bothered to find the damned thread. I'll post 'em again,and who knows? Maybe they've changed.

First: Jealousy. This is a very valid reason, if not a particularly noble one. It's never fun to be sitting in your rawhide armor fighting with your bronze longsword alongside some guy in full crafted with some ludicrous pig-sticker that is reaping through the orcs with ease. Sure, he may be helping the group, but one of the reasons that you are in the game is to feel like the hero, right? Everyone wants to be one of the Super Friends - no one wants to be one of the Wonder Twins, much less that stupid monkey (am I dating myself or what?).

Second: Appearances. Chat is an inexact way of communicating context. In another thread, someone was talking about a twink in HH commenting on the SBH: "I've got better". The poster took that as a boorish comment on the rest of the party's gear; it may have been a simple declaration that he didn't want to roll on it. Regardless of his intent, the already-jealous group-mates went away with a bad impression of the twink and thus twinks as a whole.

Similarly, on other servers I've had a number of twinks inspect me and make a comment on how poor my gear was, usually followed by a suggestion on what I should upgrade to.

"You only have a lizardscale mantle? Not very good - you really should have adamantine epaulettes; they have +7 wisdom." Poorly-executed but well-intentioned advice to a character that has no realistic way of acquring the gear, or snide insults from some guy who has never really been in the non-twinked character's shoes? Tough to tell, but the latter is usually the impression that such encounters make.

Third: An effect on grouping. Few people are going to be lining up a bunch of warriors, picking the one with the cool gear and sending the others off to play elsewhere, but if you have twinked tank (and really, twinking is most evident in melee classes) who is cleaning up as fast as you can pull, you may be less inclined to allow anyone else into your group, since you are "doing just fine". No malice in the decision, and normally you wouldn't even realize that it is because you have a twink in the party; still, it puts someone out in the cold, and on this server, that's a big deal.

Above, Mheddik made a good argument about camping the FBSS twice and how twinking opens the camp up to those who have never been there before rather than requiring the same person needlessly take up a group slot for the second time. Excellent point, but on FV, you WANT people to be helping to camp it. With the TLC, you need a full group to hold that area and pull out the ghouls. Counter-intuitive, but this server breaks all the rules.

Fourth: The economy. Twinks take many of the best items out of the open market. Again, this is not malicious - a high-level necromancer gets the FBSS and would rather give this very valuable item to his own character rather than sell it. Understandable, but it means that the FBSS is not really going to someone who truly needs it - a mid/high level melee (sure, the twink will eventually be at the appropriate level).

Benares suggested going with level-specific gear, something that Verant has avoided. I think their decision was wise - after all, if you are handed a fine steel longsword, it would be silly to see a message "your character does not have the willpower to use this weapon" and have to go back to the rusty. And of course in many cases, they HAVE done this - procs won't go off until a certain level, and many right-clickable items are not usable until a certain point.

I would have like to have seen a more game-play oriented approach to this: My idea is that if a character wears or uses an item that is too high for them, there is a percentage chance that they will get no benefit from it.

You'd assign a minimum level to each item. For the vast majority it would be "1". For the others, you'd make that level some significant distance below what you should be able to acquire the item at. If a character below the minimum level attempts to use it, there would be a chance that they "miss-use" it. Thus a creature attacks the level 5 in full crafted, and the breastplate fails to protect him 50% of the time. A level 10 wielding the wurmslayer has a 30% chance that he will hit but do 0 damage.

Thus, twinking would be possible, but a risky proposition. Elric could have his sword, but at lower levels it would be more of a hindrance than a help (sounds familiar).

Anyway, enough blathering. I have yet to see a twink on FV. They are out there, sure, but I don't think that they are particularly prevalent. As the server ages, we'll see more and more of them, but by that time I'll be of sufficient level that I won't care quite as much.

Actually, item value deflation is something that twinking counteracts, a least temporarily, in that twinking creates an additional amount of demand. This is one of the beneficial aspects of twinking on the economy, this additional demand (from multiple characters per account).

For example, let us say that, somehow, twinking was no longer possible. Then the 48th level wizard would have no incentive to purchase nice weapons for his warrior, who does not have the finances available to purchase nice weapons directly. Less demand. Less demand + ever-increasing supply = item value deflation. But because said wizard CAN purchase the nice weapons and get them onto his warrior (and ranger, and rogue, and paladin, and shadowknight) there is suddenly greatly increased demand.

This extra demand most benefits some of the tradeskills, particularly (in my experience, anyway) jewelry, but probably high-end tailoring and smithing to some degree as well.

Market saturation is something that we will see happen vastly quicker on FV than on other servers because we lack that same amount of demand with only one character per account, which will result in rapid item devaluation on the market. In fact, for some items this is already visibly happening; I have seen charred guardian shields sold for 100 pp already on FV, and expect the price to continue to fall. On my former server I have *never* seen a CGS sold for so little, simply because there are still many druids, shamans and clerics in the world (as one or more characters on someone's account) who still lack anything that good, thus propping up the price for a much longer period of time. (Eventually saturation will still happen, however; but it may take as much as eight times as long to happen.)

And don't think things are being sold for so little because people are desperate for money; there is a lot of money floating around right now. Things are being sold cheaply either because market saturation is hitting now, or because people are expecting it to hit soon (and one or the other is surely true).
