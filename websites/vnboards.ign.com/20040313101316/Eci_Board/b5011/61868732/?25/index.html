This may not be the best place to say this, but seeing as how there is extremely very little chance any of you will ever get to meet me in real life and vice versa, I will say what I need to say and pray for the best.

I was about 6 or 7 years old when my father decided we had to escape Laos. At that time, we were hiding out in the forest around the northeastern ridges of the Plain of Jars. We were literally just a few miles away from the North Vietnamese border. This meant we had to trek around the Plane of Jars to the south and across central Laos to the Mekong River and cross over to Thailand.

My father was in charge of a regiment that searched, rescued and sheltered downed American and Allied pilots. Should my father or any one in my family be captured, the Communisits would have a field day. The Americans had officially left Southeast Asia so we had to travel in the dark and hide in the day since there were no more "safe" spots we can find shelter in. Those were scary days where no laughter could be heard and no smiles could be found. We could not cook. We had to eat what we found edible in the forest. They are not as scary as the events that have been branded in my memory of our crossing the Mekong River.

When we made it to the river, my father had secured a boat to take us across the river in the middle of the night. Everything seemed innocent enough. My father paid the driver and we sneaked onto the boat and laid down to hide. It was not until some time later that my father realized we were not going across the river, the boat was going against the flow of the water in the river. That meant the driver was taking us closer to Vientiane, the Lao capital. I heard my dad talk to the man and then they started shouting and then more people started shouting. I heard gunshots. I felt blood drip down my face. I heard my dad shout at me and my brothers and cousins to jump in the water. I felt my grandmother grab me and pull me on her shoulders. I saw lights flash and I saw people die.

The last thing my grandmother said to me before she died was... "Tuav kuv ruaj ruaj es txhob poob os. Tuav koj txoj sia ruaj mog." (translation: Hold on to me and do not fall. Hold on to Life and live.)

The majority of us made it across the Mekong River and found safety with the Thai patrol officers. And here I am. Khuji Ugani, Paladin of 65 seasons on Norrath.

Death is not to be taken lightly. If a close friend or family member of yours is thinking of suicide or is showing signs of being suicidal, tell him/her what my Grandmother told me, "Hold on to me and do not fall. Hold on to Life and live."
