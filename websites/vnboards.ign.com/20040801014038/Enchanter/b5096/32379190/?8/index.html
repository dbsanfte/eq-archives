With my limited experience, I can tell you that yes, it's a hard class to play.. Or more hard than pressing "A" as a Warrior to attack, anyway The problem is crowd control. Especially at low-level, your vision on groups (if you've played other characters before) will drastically change for the worse when you find out that people don't listen (to you). You're responsible for crowd control, you're responsible for those charm spells to land (and you need lower mana-resist spells like Tashan(i) to have better success). Although it's controversial, a higher CHArisma will help you with your charm spells as well. Oh and btw, people don't like chanters until level 16, because before level 16 "You don't have breeze, and I don't know what you add to our group" - one of the lovely comments you're most likely to get. So be prepared to be b*tched at til then, it gets better later on. Note that you will be a group creature, so you have to like groups. That's just my 2 cp's

HI
Zerem - 44 Enchanter here.
Never played anything else but concure with the others that lvl 1 to 16 was a pain. Even after 16 it was hard to find groups that knew how to use an enchanter properly but groups are the only way for the enchanter. I learned more about croud control in the Sarnak Fort in LOIO. I reall started to feel useful when I was able to group up with peeps there. Chanting is actually easy if you stick to some basic rules. 1. Dont solo. 2. Dont do direct damage spells (unless you have a lot of mana to waste). You server you group much better spending mana on mez, breeze, and haste spells. Keep up your reserves for that one extra mob that unexpectantly joins in when you least expect it. 3. The only say to survive is to not be hit. Dont try to fight with daggers, staffs, etc. Your strength is controling the croud. And look out for charm. Its iffy sometimes and can get everyone killed pretty easilly.
I have had a LOT of fun being a chanter. Cant imagin being anything else.

gnomes have 2nd best starting chanter stats after erudite

gnome good caster gear is very nice and steamfont is good place to lvl to 12 fast (you get to kill skellies and chicks too, so you can always run to kaladim for a quick xp boost by turning stuff in at the temple) and small plague rat trains are everywhere (almost 2 plats/tail at NPC vendor

And pet soloing is easy - at least up to lvl 16 - you just have to do a run to high hold keep to buy the pet spells!

when low-level pet soloing you dont need mez and I've found resists are so rare that tashan is hardly needed.. just pull a dark blue with DOT, engage in melee, take maybe 20% of your life worth of hits and step back and sit down - add a nuke if needed..you can deal with an add with mez or root easy enough (root is faster and you can nuke/dot through it) - as a get out if things are going wrong its often best to opt out with invis/mem blur or train back to guard rather than burn all your mana on one fight...one thing you have is lots of time to see things going wrong - even 2 on 1 your pet will last a few seconds and if your standing back you have plenty of time to react

if things get tight for time you can't beat flux + root (root is faster than mez) or just flux + run...

oh and stay away from meleeing large plague rats - their damage takes forever to recover from!

after 16 pet-soloing gets hard - but very talented people can charm solo at much higher levels so I hear..


anyway best advice I can give is:

read xorn's enchanters guide !!!


good luck..
