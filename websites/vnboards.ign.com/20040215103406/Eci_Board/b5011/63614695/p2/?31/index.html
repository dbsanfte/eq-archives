I had a pair of Piranha back when I was in college. Over a three year period they grew from roughly an inch to four and a half to maybe five inches. Personally I don’t know that getting more than two is all that wise. No one I knew had any luck with more than two in a tank for an extended time. I started with three; they killed the third within a month.

I started with real small guppies then used the inch long feeder goldfish at the end. I would buy them by the dozen. The two piranhas would normally finish them off within 24 hours. Although, they almost NEVER attacked with any lights on in the room or on the tank. I would dump the feeders in the tank, watch for an hour to no avail, go out for the night, sleep whatever. Then the next morning half would be gone. They rarely performed while anyone was watching. Piranhas seem to prefer to take out the tail of the fish first to make for an easier chase. They then like to attack the underbelly, least from what I observed. I never fed mine anything other than fish.

With my pair I would only feed them a dozen every two to three weeks, sometimes I would go a month between gorging’s. They are a very hearty fish; a guy that lived in the house with me had a pair and left them in his room for three months while he went home for summer break. Not once feeding them (did feed them a couple dozen prior to leaving). When we returned for fall quarter the fish were still alive although there was only about five inches of water left in the tank. He cleaned the tank, refilled it and they were fine.

I had my pair in a 35 gallon tank if memory serves me right. Remember though in addition to feeding, the number of fish you have in the tank will have a bearing on how fast they grow and to what size they grow so I was told.

By the way, anyone who says the teeth are not visible is wrong. True they don’t protrude from the mouth like a cartoon but they are visible, especially once they get larger in size. Also, be leary of anyone who says you can put any other fish in with piranhas. An aquarium guy told me this ‘dragon fish’ would be fine and maybe even kill the piranhas. I took him home and put him in the tank. The next morning half of his tail was gone and he wouldn’t leave the refuge of the castle that the piranha couldn’t get into because of their body size/shape. Im not saying there isnt a fresh water fish that can tangle with the piranha but just make sure you get a return policy on the fish ;P

Have fun...

I have a few tanks running at my house, a 125 gallon occupied by a few 8-10 inch sized cichlids, a 55 gallon live plant/tetra tank, and a frilly "wife pleaser" guppy tank. Personally, I wouldn't go for piranha's. The only time they are the least bit exciting is during feeding times and generally people like to starve them for around a week before feeding to create that frenzy of feeding everyone loves so much. Unfortunatly, that leaves the majority of the days where they are boring as all hell. Not very interesting fish to look at either, dull silver with a hint of red on their bellies. Oh that reminds me, there are differant types of piranha, the ones most likely everyone has seen are called red belly piranha. Piranha are a one trick pony as far as fish go.

Somebody posted earlier about cichlids, that would be my recomendation too. I've seen a mated pair of Dovii destroy adult piranha within 1 min of dropping the piranha in the tank.

American cichlids(as opposed to african) have TONS of personality, can be super aggresive, are colorful, can recognize their owner and treat them differantly than strangers poking their head into the tank, protect their territories against other fish with lots of bluffing, jaw wrestling, fin biting, etc. New World cichlids can get large though, so if you are interested in them, you should really go for a large 75gallon+ size tank.

Good luck with your choice though, Fish on!

I would also recommend cichlids. I have a pair of convicts and a pair of firemouths. If you get a male/female pair they are much more interesting than pirahnas. Each species claims one side of the tank, and you can see some interesting warfare as they try to protect their nest from the opposing species. You'll need to pickup some rocks from a stream or maybe off the side of the road to give them some good hiding territory, otherwise they will kill each other within a matter of weeks. Also give them a few inches of gravel substrate so they can dig their nesting beds. My fish like to rearrange the tank each time they breed (usually twice a month). A 55 gallon tank would be a minimum if you wanted to mix these species.

Oh, and they will destroy any kind of feeder fish you put in the tank. Believe me, buying feeders gets old after a while. These fish will also eat any kind of formulated fish food you want to give them for when the thrill wears off.

Fish can be cool pets if you have the time to take care of them. If you don't have the time, they'll just become a thorn in your side and you'll end up letting them die in their own waste. If you're in school and working part-time I would suggest that you think about it before just jumping into taking care of fish. You'll have to do a water change like twice a month if you get messy fish like pirahnas/cichlids. Maybe less if you get a good canister filter (Fluval 404 is good for a 55 gal tank with 4 fish). bigalsonline.com is probably the best online place to buy your fish supplies. You can get a fluval filter there for $90 (like half the price they charge at the local fish store in my area).

Good luck.
