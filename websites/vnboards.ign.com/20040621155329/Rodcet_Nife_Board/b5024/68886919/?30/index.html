It’s a wonder, that, after about a month of its demise, no one was commented on the events / goings on of one of Rodset Nif’s major guilds.

I remember, back in the day, when Forsaken was first starting. When I first met Rogo and company back in late 2001, my small guild and I was struggling to get our members epics. It was my last mob, dragon Faye, for my epic.. and our poor noob guild wiped four times prior trying … then here comes forsaken … fresh and new to lend a hand .. And I land my epic.

Then, just as they enter ToV our guild disolves, I decide to join their guild. I had been a faithful forsaken follower for almost shy of 3 years. I remember, we didn’t leave ToV till all were geared. We made that commitment to one another … then came VT with the same commitments and the start of empty promises.

I guess it was greedy eyes looking for that better loot – who knows. But I remember, “We will kill Aten enough times to insure everyone gets 2 pieces.” Everyone chooses their 2 pieces…. And what happens…. Top 1/3 of the guild gets theirs and they start moving on … more POP .. gotta get flagged … gear is better anyway.

And, considering, the time involvement in getting about 75 peeps 2 items from Aten, it made since .. so, we push on…. And push on … “We wont move into time till we get everyone gear…” … Yet, almost 3 years of service, and I am entering time in almost all (save 3 pieces – scaps that the twinks didn’t get) TOV gear. PUSH PUSH PUSH .. get ours and move on … let the people who work for a living and cant play 8 hours a day get the scraps we leave behind.

That is how forsaken ended. It doesn’t surprise me that it came to a close so abruptly. The Ranga apping to accent got his – and wants to move on…. Leaving the rest of the comrades that got him his unber loot with arrows up the rear…

And then the rest … what a huge coffer of a guild fund. Boy, did someone ever walk away with the PP cash cow? Oh the spells on the mules that are now going to flood the bazzar .. and the massive amount of PP or maybe RL money that these toons are now going to bring.

To the many people who got the shaft, GL to you and your future indevours. May you find a new guild worthy or your time and talents ... To the rest of the bums who got thiers and ran ... just hope the rest of the server sees you for who you are before having any dealings with you ... Hey, even Suncaster (if I remember his name right ...) finally realized what a bad rep can bring... may it fall on you 3 fold.

Dimazar
65 Druid

Eq has become a game, that requires full time dedication to advance.

Out of many systems tried, the general dkp system is the most fair way to distribute items amongst players. The only problem lies in that even if you can make 50% of raids it is unlikely you will keep 50% of the dkp of those who can make 100% of raids, especially when moving into new content and dkp prices are inflated on new items.

As far as moving through content too soon. It's been my experiance that guilds who do not consume content quickly then move on, stagnate and lose players. It sucks for those of us who hold full time jobs but we are not the key players in a guild. Key players make 80% or greater of raids and there fore not only are the best equipped but the most important to equip. Even though your dedication to a guild may be unsurpassed and your skill might be top notch, if you cannot be on most every single raid, your not producing for the guild and there fore the guild cannot produce for you.

It might seem unfair but there really is no more fair way to play EQ.

EQ isn't a game for the casual player anymore. Especially if you want to play the "uber" game. I don't know what happened with forsaken but I doubt very much you were targeted or anyone else to be cheated out of items and or fun.
