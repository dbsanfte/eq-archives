Zurin,

The article is, indeed, at least generally accurate, as the story of Bush sending the records to the presidential library is well known. You can find corraborating stories numerous places with minimal searching.

The "resume" itself is, indeed, accurate, as Bush did, in fact, initially seal his records. That they are currently available is a result of cases brought under the Texas Open Records act (name may be wrong). A previous poster was, however, incorrecting in asserting the records were still sealed.

Regarding death sentences in Texas, I may very well be incorrect, but here is my understanding of the governor's role:

1. All such cases are required to undergo a review by the governor. Technically, I believe what he sees are petitions for clemency. These are generally put together and submitted by his legal counsel.

2. The governor reviews the petitions with whatever degree of thoroughness he deems fit. He may choose to discuss this with others or he may not.

3. After his consideration, the governor may either choose to grant the petition or deny it. This is solely the governor's decision, and he need not give any supporting rationale for his decision.

4. If the petition is granted, the sentence is commuted. If the petition is not granted, the sentence is carried out.

You are correct the governor has no say in the initial decision to apply the death sentence. However, he ALWAYS has a say, and in fact the he generally has the FINAL say in whether or not the sentence is carried out. (I say "generally" as I believe it is hypothetically possible the governor could deny clemency, but before the sentence is carried out either the state or federal Supreme Court could issue a stay).

To my mind that does make him responsible for the decision to execute. Not solely responsible, but certainly he has a role.

To look at it from another angle, if the governor granted clemency he would be responsible for NOT executing the prisoner. He can't be "responsible" for one side of the decision, but "not reponsible" for the flip side of the matter.
