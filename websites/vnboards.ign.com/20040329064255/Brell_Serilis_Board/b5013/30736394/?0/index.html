The unforgiving demanding beast of truth exacts a heavy toll upon the spirit. Unfortunately her essence would not allow her to turn away or ignore its invitation to ultimate demise. The reality of chaos and meaninglessness that was the world. She was repulsed by the desperate seeking of the many weak to forge illusions of justice to hold together thier frail paper souls and minds.


How weak were they? How weak indeed must she be? Some meaning had to be attributed to it all or crippling insanity would result. Would it not? Dare she test her limits? She already walked a fine line bordering loss of control and annihilation. She embraced the deep rooted traditions of death in her culture. What maintained her stability and focus were the ancestors, as well as the high art of Necromancy. Even though chaos was ultimate truth, she recognised meaning, however false must be created, to an extent.

The urge to indulge and destroy, these dangerous forces came to form and fruitition through the art of death and the veneration of her ancestors. The heavy ornate iron door to the ancestral tomb groaned as she opened it. Soft whispering of her elegant dark robe as it moved over the steps, rustling with her descent. She passed between rooms of urns set upon carved pedestals. Approaching the final doors she carefully pulled them open.

Within were the ancestral ashpits. The most ancient of her family rested in individual pits with small kneelers and exquisitely carved shrines before them. Here the wise and holy founders could be given their proper due, in special circumstances even consulted for guidance from beyond the veil.

In the rear of the great vault upon a raised dias were the pits of the First. They recieved duty and veneration first. Her ebon consuming eyes drifted up to the House banner upon the rear wall as she approached. The Unholy Mother, The One, was adored. She was the servant of the true ruler of Neriak and death, Cristanos Thex. Alastrinel lit the candles,the brazier, and poured small amounts of scented oil into a chalice upon the shrine.
Her mind drifted to the teaching of the Great Mother.

In many ways the teachings mirrored the words of Vorshar the Despised. Vorshar was risen and annoited by Innoruuk as a Chosen One. He was principal in opposing the joining of the Teir'Dal Empire into the disgusting Combine the Humans tried to forge with the other lesser races. Many Teir'dal, even back then were, weak, corrupt, against the will of the Prince of Hate. It was Vorshar who knew the mind and will of the Dark Father. Fortunately the great kingdom was not corrupted by the Rein'dal imbeciles. This was the heart of the first Matron. Purity of Hate, Purity of House. The Outlanders were not dealt with directly, only through Rein'dal or lessers, preferably not at all. Eye contact was never made with these tainted ones. They were never addressed by name, and never welcomed into House grounds or holdings.

Alastrinel moved over to the shrine of the First Father next and repeated her duty, delicately smoothing the ashes of his pit with a runed spreader when through. This was done daily. The first six complete houses rested in the great crypt and had to be attended in this manner. Those that rested without, in urns, were collectively venerated by room.

It was fortunate for Alastrinel that her very nature caused her to serve Hate well. She knew the rituals were a form of weakness, comfort created through order, but she did her duties. She understood her position in the House, what she could ignore and could not. Such a thing as the Ancestors could not be overlooked.

The rituals clamed her mind, focused her Hate, for the time when she would lead her Great House. House V'drin maintained few ties with other Houses, as most had become more like the corrupted that wanted to move with the force of the Combine in the past. It was unfortunate. Innoruuk's will would prevail, time had demonstrated this.

She lovingly checked the line of bones that were set in the wall. They extended around the length of the entire crypt. They were known as the ghost fence. They were enchanted bones, usually from the fourth finger, that led to the heart, that formed this barrier. They kept out corrupting spirits, foreign magics and scrying. The Ancestors that sacrificed part of themselves to form this line always suffered in some manner by being continually bound to the world and never completely at rest. They were highly honored by the family.

She passed through the center door checking the last finger bone mounted there for protection. As the door clicked shut she could hear the sibilant moans of farewell from deep within the tomb echo up unto her. The chill of their spectral essence often reached within her. Her flaxen white silver hair brushed her regal cheeks as she bowed her head resting her hands against the ornate wooden door before the iron gate. She then quietly departed.
