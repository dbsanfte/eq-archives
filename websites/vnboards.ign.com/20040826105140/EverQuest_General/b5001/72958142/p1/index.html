Q&A with Brenlo - 7/25/04

1) Any eta on adding new elemental level LDoN armor? I am trying to decide whether to save points or continue purchasing upgrades.

Hehe this is a nasty rumor going around. We do want to look at adding more items to the adventure merchants, but if/when we do it will not be elemental armor.

2) Any more information on when we will see GoD legs/chests looked at for possible retuning?

I would imagine this could happen after Omens

3) Any plans to implement a guild alliance tool in order to improve comunication and coordination between allied guilds?

Something I will pass along to the team.

4) Will Research tradeskill ever see a trophy?

Probably will not happen as it is a skill you can use training points to max the skill out. The team would like to have some reward though and will evaluate what that may be.

5) Will Resarch ever be updated to research current spells?

Completely depends on the spells, and if we see a need for them to be more common. This probably won't apply to no-drop and ancient spells. (Per Maddoc)

6) Will we receive new pillar spells in Omens? (They have been on our wish list since Kunark - you can find our old wish list from then on caster's Realm asking for some)

Spell lists are not final for Omens so I really cannot say just yet.

7) Why does AA work on right clicks for shaman, necros ( ie thier dot pants can crit each tick), and every buff class in the game (ie their right click buffs, maelins methodical mind, maelins meditation, form of endurance and the like from time) lasting nearly twice as long. Yet when wizards right click a robe, we can not crit.

I am not sure why this would be. I will pass it along to QA as it may be a bug.

Would like to know the possibility of having the buff timer on pet buffs. Would like to know the possibility of the ability to click off buffs at will (for pets).

Buff timer is a possibility and I will forward it over to our coders. It might be something tied to leadership or an AA though (that is just me supposing) Removing pet buffs, I do not see happening soon.

9) Any chance of having voice graft added to our familiar?

As it would require some code changes (familiars are treated differently in code than standard pets) it will not be happening anytime soon.

10) What other zones are in the pipe for revamping (not in appearance, but for content), and will the majority be done similar to VP (raising the level of the zone) or simply changing the content around a bit?

For now we are turning our focus to Omens of War. I would look for revamp announcemens after it ships. I think you will see a varied approach, some levels will be beefed up while others are changed to be more appealing.

11) Any plans to instance any old content? The emp in Sraa is quite annoying for smaller guilds trying to get into VT. His loots are decent enough larger quilds kill him alot for loot and smaller guilds have a difficult time getting people the rifts they need for VT.

No plans at this time. It would be a massive undertaking though.

12) Any plans to reduce spawn time on older content?

Not currently.

13) Any plans to change wizard resists in GoD? (Our only big nuke is fire but all the major bosses have high FR with low CR/MR)

Not currently but it is something the team can re-evaluate after Omens.

14) Can they add a ceiling to zones they felt were exploited by Grav Flux and un-nerf it? (Grav Flux no longer fluxes you - it was nerfed to stop people jumping over walls in PoTime)

That would require re-building the zones. We may do this when the zones are eventually updated to the new graphics engine, but it's not going to happen anytime soon.

15) Is it possible to add a raid option to the LFG tool, so that if you are waiting for a raid invite you can put /lfg on, and click the raid button in the LFG tool, This would allow you to keep the /lfg tag above your head but would not include you in the LFG list when other players use the LFG tool?

Interesting idea. I will see what the guys here think.

17) What is the status of single player LDoN or similar type adventures

None coming anytime soon. I think the task system in Omens may fill this role though.

1 Where does the Plane of Sky stand on your "list" of zones in need of a revamp?

We have not plotted out revamps just yet so please stay tuned.

19) can you reveal any information about the Omens Task system?

Not yet, but I think you will be pleasantly surprised.

20) Would it be possible to add a tradeskill path to the solvents?

This is something under discussion.

21) Does Firiona Vie have any regrets over her breast reduction surgery? =D

She had surgery?

23) How does the team (i.e. Rytan) feel about some form of Group Harvest?

No idea, but I will ask him.

24) What is the possibility of seeing Portals added to some GoD zones - particularly Barindu and Qvic (Reminder that Nexus was the only port at beginning of luclin and then more were added)

It is always a possibility =)

25) Lately there has been a lot of threads about solo-content. What sort of plans are there in the next 4-5 months for some sort of alternative for progression than grouping?


The Task system! The system will be a fun alternative to grouping for players with short play times. The task system initially will be for players of 1st to 50th level, but wil be expanded to all levels after launch.

Thanks again Brenlo!

If your wondering why this seems slanted towards wizards, its because it is. Graffe's is the wizard class board and Gemdiver is their correspondent. Therefore he asked some wizard specific questions in his interview.

Raiding has been an important aspect of EQ since Kunark, but it was not the only way to travel up the power curve, just one of the ways. You could single-group in places like Sebilis and get gear about as good as any in the game. Velious definitely added more raid-level material and strengthened raiding as a mainstay of end-game play, but still there was enough one-groupable content all the way to the Western Wastes to keep players very happy. Luclin, ditto. But then PoP came out and it diverged from this pattern of supporting both group-level casual playstyles and large-scale raiding playstyles to heavily favor raiding over ordinary grouping. At release, there were only three hunting zones in PoP. To gain access to any other zones where mobs could be killed, one needed to pass a one-group Trials, which unlocked exactly two zones. Once into those two zones, one-groupable methods of entering Tier3 zones were introduced, such as the Diaku Raiders in Storms, but that was it. To gain access to anything beyond Tier2, one absolutely HAS to raid, and not the old-style raids like ToV or the old-world Planes, but heavy-duty flagging and backflagging and more backflagging, just to get to one-groupable content at the higher power levels. The one-group trail just ENDED after the Second Tier, and raiding was the only way to unlock any further zones, though the Elemental Planes has lots of one-groupable content they could enjoy.

It's elitism that favors members of large raiding guilds. Thankfully, I am IN a large raiding guild, but a casual player in a family guild is going to be old and gray before seeing the Elementals.

TS,

I agree that raid-style players get better gear than non-raid-style players. I totally agree that there is resentment. And there is too much hubris attached to equipment.

But it is also true that the non-raid-style player becomes more powerful in his encounters that he was ever meant to be if he has raid-style gear, but doesn't ever raid. Hell, raiders are more powerful than necessary for XP too. More powerful nearly to the point of "broken."

Any Time+ equipped player can go to PoN or BoT and solo the XP mobs. A group of Time+ players can XP in BoT or even Earth/Fire with zero (ZERO!) fear of death. No risk at all anymore. They are way overpowered for the XP grind. Hence the bazillion AA that many Time+ raiders have.

I am going to venture a guess that this is the reason the XP mobs in GoD are so much beefier for the level. Time+ players can currently go anyplace and farm AA with no risk at all. The GoD zones are build to give a little more risk to these players.

Now, making raid-style gear easier to get when it is obvious that a proliferation of raid-style gear breaks/trivializes all the XP zones is not a solution either. Look what happened to Guk when playing there became trivial. Its PL-zone now.

No answers here, just sensible observations for your perusal. It might be not that non-raiders don't deserve raid-gear, but instead that raid-gear would trivialize the game for them.
