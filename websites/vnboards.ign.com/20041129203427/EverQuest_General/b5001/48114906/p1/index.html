With the variety of items with casting/clicky abilities and charged items almost all those effects you listed can be generated by a warrior with the right gear.

Cobalt BP has a clicky 100 hp heal called invigor, there's at least 3 different items I can think with 10 or more snare charges that are all/all, jboots = sow for the non-sowing non-sow potion buying folks, there's about 6 different items at least warriors can use for invis, 3 aren't even personal only, go by Dulak harbor in Ykesha there's a shoulder item that drops every 10 minutes with 5 gather shadow charges that is all/all. Ikky ceremonial bp, fungi, various items with aura of health all help regen hp per tick. Eyepatch of plunder is clickable haste, there's even several items that give clickable or procing damage shielding.

If you want my thoughts on what warriors could use here you go:

After 30 seconds of continuous sitting and not on any mob's aggro list in the zone your hp per tick rate adds 1, for every additional 10 seconds 1 more hp is added to regen per tick. Any movement or being added to a mob's hate list immediately ends this effect and you must again sit and wait for 30 sec. This isn't for just warriors, its for everyone.

Going beserk makes warriors immune to all mind effects such as mez, fear, blind, stun, charm and cancels any such effect currently on them (yes, mostly useful in PvP) they gain personal haste equal to half their level that stacks with all current haste effects but not exceeding the cap in addition they gain half their level in non-magic damage absorption- ie. a level 50 warrior while beserk would ignore the first 25 points of melee and ranged damage.

For every 5 levels a warrior gains 1 point of non-magic damage immunity, ie. a level 10 warrior is hit for 2 points of damage and instead of taking any damage sees, "An orc centurian hits YOU but you shrug off the feeble blow!" If he had been hit for 3 points he would only see 1 damage taken. If nothing else this would make any arguement that warriors are not the best tanking class in EQ no longer possible.

Increase the duration of /shield by about a minutes and lower the reuse time to at least 10 minutes.

At level 45 the warrior gains the skill whirlwind- While using a 2 handed weapon clicking this skill will do normal combat damage to everything in melee range of the warrior, basically a free point blank are effect attack, it runs off the same timer as bash/slam and kick. Yep, something you really wouldn't want to use in groups with any crowd control but its a way for a warrior to generate mass hate in addition to their area taunt skill, something warriors have always lacked.

So most folks here seem to think all warriors need is a mega taunt. You do know shadow knights and especially enchanters can enhance the hate you are generating through buffs? it really does make a difference, especially for warriors.

If just by clicking the taunt key repeatedly a warrior could outaggro every class in the game you've just made the warrior a necessity to every serious group in the game because there's no reason now not to chain nuke or bother with things like memory blurs or hate reducing effects. Everyone is free to just go all out and if they get aggro blame the warrior for slacking.

The basic dynamic in most encounters in EQ high or low is letting your "tank" be it a warrior, a monk, or a pet generate enough hate to hold aggro, it essentially removes any difficulty or though to the basic gameplay to make the top tank class the top aggro class. It's part of the original "vision" that warriors the best suited class in the game for tanking have probably the least ability in the game to generate hate rapidly.

My point of view is that with the exception of situations where the /disc defensive is a necessity, warriors are overlooked not because they cannot hold onto aggro as well as a hybrid or even a monk, but because the difference in their tanking ability at the very high end is unnoticeable. If a mob is hitting for 150-300 pts consistently, do you really care or notice if he misses a warrior once or twice more then he might a paladin or sk or hits for on average maybe 10 points less on the warrior then a monk tanking for you?

As to the gear question, no, not everyone is an uber twink, I still see plenty of warriors wearing suits of crusty armor at 50+, gear will come with time and mostly by getting to know folks, grouping regularly and then getting invited into a guild that hunts together regularly and will help you get those drops you need. Once you start hunting regularly in places designed for 45-60+ groups believe me you will make money and alot of that gear you see in the bazaar will either seem like a bargain or pointless because you will be completing quests for better items or looting no drop stuff that is also far better.

As one example, the eyepatch of plunder can be done solo, yes solo, even by a warrior with sufficent levels (and with a heck of alot of waiting). I assume the step you are hung on is Lodizal, he spawns on a regular timer now, be there when he pops and the usual guild drops in 20-30 min later to whack him and request looting rights for the map piece, offering to pay or trade, half the time noone needs it and its left rotting, seriously, I've seen 4 like that myself out of the 7 times I've shown up for some turtle soup action.
