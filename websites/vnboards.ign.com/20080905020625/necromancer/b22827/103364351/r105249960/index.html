Okay well curses + melee is your best bet.... but just doing necro alone would be much better. My buddy was an elementalist and he was having similar issues.
Try combining your sword swinging with Flurry (warrior) and Barbs (necromancer).
That will increase the number of attacks you perform, but increase your damage output.
Also weaken armor will make your foes more succeptable to your melee attacks.

Try a build with some or all of these curses and axe skills...
Parasitic Bond, Barbs, Weaken Armor, Flurry, Whirling Axe, Enfeebling Touch,

Also

Being a curses necro/warrior although providing a good dmg output will leave you very vulnerable to taking damage... Warrior as a secondary profession is difficult to use in many cases, and as said before you might jsut be better off ditching the axe and changing your secondary profession when you get a chance.

Hi, I played Guildwars when it first came out (I don't play anymore) but my first toon was a N/W because the devs claimed you could basically make whatever you wanted. Anyway! You can still be highly successful in casual play with a N/W but they are a very unorthodox combo unfortunately...

I'd recommend with regular leveling and whatnot you want to go with blood and swords. You want to play this character as 80% caster and 20% meleer, it just doesn't really pay to get into melee range on the really tough encounters! Once you get used to this unorthodox playstyle it's actually pretty fun and can be successful in the right situations.

I'd say for solo farming, curses/swords work well. For random arena blood/sword with the elite life drain or soul drain, as well as team arena the twisting of the 2 damage add spells plus the IWAY power *was* pretty sweet. Good luck though, you won't get any sympathy from the Necro community if it doesn't work out hehe
