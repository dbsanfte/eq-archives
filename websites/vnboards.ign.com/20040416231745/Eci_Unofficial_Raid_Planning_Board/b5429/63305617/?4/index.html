Vox and Nagafen are traditionally killed by the public, for the public access to scales and books. This is generally accepted, but not set in stone. If the public raid leaders want a reasonable claim on them, they must organize raids to kill them as soon as they can when they are up. Failure to do so forfeits the right to kill the dragon, in my opinion, after 2 days of the dragon being up.

I have no idea how long Vox has been up, for I stopped keeping track. But if the recent ugliness noted in the 1/18 raid thread is a good indicator, Vox had been up for some time and no raid was yet posted to take her down.

No raid leader owns Vox or Nagafen. Dragons are too fickle to have as pets, and eat WAY too much, especially on a failed raid.

Perhaps this post should be on the main board, but things like this get discussed on this board.

I would think that being a public raid leader you would need to expect some private or even other public raid leaders to head up raids on your targets as well from time to time.

Although many people cry foul at some of these tactics, people need to remember that Ec'i is very different from other servers as far as the public raiding community . The fact that we actually HAVE a public raiding community and schedule raids both public and private AS A SERVER sets us apart from most servers.

I would ask that all raid leaders try and be courteous and state their intent here if at all possible, but sometimes these things can be overlooked, or forgotten. Stuff happens. Many people are not even aware of these boards.

Sony's rules are very implicit about these types of things, and we need to keep that in mind as well. No raid leader owns a spawn, even though many people do believe that they do. My thought is this: If you want that spawn, get your raid together and take it down. If it sits open for too long then its anyone's game.

I know public Naggy and Vox leaders are going to throw a fit bout this sentiment and start spouting off about the "tradition" of Naggy and Vox raids on Ec'i, but my response to them would be to get a raid posted and take the dragon down. Times change folks, better get prepared for it.

I couldn't agree more with the both of you.

There are those out there who stop levelling completely just to get their epic or a piece of dragon loot, but in the big picture if you keep on levelling there is much better treasure to be found and quested for out there..

As we all know how real life can affect our playing habits as well as time, I think that as long as there are people out there willing to raid and kill things that have been up for days, than they should have a right to. The public is the public- not a guild, just a giant mass of people willing to kill something. As long as there is a leader who is competent in doing so, than there is no reason why they shouldn't be able to kill the animal.

Especially if a raid isn't posted here.

IMHO it is almost the "usual raid leader's" (eg people who usually run weekly raids etc) job to post here when the mob is up. If he or she is tied up by aforementioned time constraints, so long as the typically public raids remain public, there should be no problem.


What WOULD help, however would be an ongoing track record for those who seek dragons and suck for epic mobs. If only 1 person from a public raid going on, during the time that a usual raid leader isn't present, can come here and check the most recent raids for epic points ... Then it would be a nice curtosey to do so for all parties involved. In fact, I think that is one of the reasons the public tries to have such a grasp on mobs like these, because epic pieces are still so hard to obtain by a guild, or solo.. you need a solid raiding force that respects everyone's wishes and organizes a way to help others get their epics.


I guess this is just food for thought, but I can see where both parties can get their panties in a bunch. just remember what Arrk said about owning a dragon. They're high maintainance, and boy, you better hope that you have a window open after they eat.

Perhaps if E'ci is changing enough, players will change with it and there will be a painless solution to all this
