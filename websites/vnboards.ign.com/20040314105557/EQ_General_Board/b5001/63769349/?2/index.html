Even with LDoN's ready-made content approach, it can be difficult to get a group at any level. I spend almost as much of my time trying to help organize groups as I do actually doing adventures.

Unfortunately, if you get in a lousy group, you will probably take a loss. That's happened to me a few times. You know the group type... 2 warriors, 1 shaman, 1 rogue, 1 enchanter and my necro. Warrior pulls 3, chanter mezzes 2, then each warrior wakes up a mob and proceeds to get the beat down. The shaman runs low on mana even though I'm using my ghetto mez, transferring mana, slowing the undead, etc. Eventually, half the group is dead. This was in part because the shaman gated out mid-fight "by accident".

With about an hour left, most of the group has disbanded. I leave the adv and take the loss because I can't solo the place in time to win.

Then there's the groups where everything works well as if everyone's been playing together for years.

Wins/Losses - it's hard to take them seriously when you have to "go out on a limb" to get in a pickup group and hope that the people know what to do.

Getting in a group for LDON is basically as hard or easy as you make it for yourself. Just like everywhere else only it takes a little more effort in organization ahead of time then your average pickup group.

Using the LFG flag and beginning your own group is usually the way to go and from log in to entering the adv with 5 others usually takes me about 10 to 20 minutes. I suspect it's probably harder at 20-30s because fewer people that level range seem to do LDON. Most of the real good stuff comes out of LDON only through amassing a ton of points which only works at 50+ levels but I see plenty of those groups still shouting and forming all the time. This used to not be the case right afer LDON was released: xp in adventures at that time rivaled 1st and 2nd tier Planes of power xp modifiers, now that's definately no longer true.

On the other hand you will have freaking wizards, necros, rangers, and rogues who will refuse to join any adv group unless it already has 5 other level 65 players (none-under 65 allowed) and spend 3 hours at a time just standing around waiting for an invite and complaining it's hard to find a group. Meanwhile I've completed the 2 advs I have time for in 1 play session only to still see those people still standing with LFG up waiting for the "perfect" group.
