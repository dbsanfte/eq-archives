Anyone playing this? Now I know alot of people will say it's cheesy, and it is. But hey, so is running around as a one eyed dorf for 4 years poking crap in the butt.

Anyway I just bought it last night out of boredome and it, so far, is pretty fun. You can get really creative or goofy with your costume and name which is cool. There are instanced areas you can go for quests and such, seems like most stuff is doable solo if you like.

the graphics are obviously leaps and bound better than eq, and although I'm not sure how long it's been out, it already runs much smoother.

Instead of looting mobs when you kill them, you sometimes get augments that inhance your abilities or buffs you can save to use whenever.

When you die you end up at the hospital, I havent noticed any exp loss yet.

Most of the fighting has just been gang members and zombie dudes, which all look pretty cool.

For whatever reason you can't have a cape, which seems pretty lame considering your a super hero and all. Also getting your super butt kicked by lowly gang bangers makes you feel just a tad shy of super.

The combat system seems to be turn based, you click a icon for the super power you want to use and it does it, if you have enough stamina. Then you have to wait for another combat move to refresh to use it. It's pretty basic.

There are no atributes to put points in on character creation, your just given the propper hp for class.

I don't know if it will catch on like eq or uo did, I doubt it since there are no unique and flashy weapons or armor to show your level of uberdome, but it's a pretty fun diversion from the same ol same ol.

CoH is great. Missions and instancing are all accomplished the way Sony wished they were. Dynamic content within dungeons that changes in difficulty as you add/subtract from your party. Freaking smooth.

You can solo with patience, you can build a hardcore party based character if you want as well. Graphics are shiny, skip free.

Death does have a slight penalty. You incur an experience debt that you need to make up eventually, however any experience that you earn get split between debt and your current total so you can keep levelling, even with a debt.

Combat's neat, only problem is it is a little more twitch than EQ. No real stats to worry yourself with, or resists. It's level/class based.

Custom titles are neat for status sake. You can be The Valiant Extraordinary Something.

Heya Diggins Been playing CoH myself. Some classes are more geared towards soloing, but i've noticed that it's been easiest with my blaster. The melee classes are just too exposed once you get past a certain level. A character with a ranged attack and hover has been easiest to manage... after all, kiting's pretty easy when you can just go straight up and out of range.

Currently am playing Colonel Angus on Virtue, (elec/fire blaster) but I tend to keep making and deleting new characters, just because the character creation process is so fun.

The best XP is absolutely in groups. One of the funnest things I've been doing is to gather a group of people, have them all go get as many missions as they can, and then meet back at a designated spot in 10 minutes. Then we go from mission point to mission point, fighting uncontested mobs, and then fighting some sort of cool unique boss at the end.

Don't get me wrong, going to Perez Park and just slaughtering stuff is fun, too, but I like having the structure of a mission, so you get a feeling like you all accomplished something.

I've heard the game is murder on a 56k connection, but I've also heard it runs "okay" from at least one person. One thing they do to reduce lag is to instance major zones, so it'd be like when you zone, you can go to East Commons, or East Commons 2, or East Commons 3. It's kinda lame because the same players don't exist on all different instances of a zone, but it does cut down on lag. Wish they had something where you can automatically switch to a different version of the same zone while you're in it, instead of having to zone out and back in.

All in all though, the game is just *fun* to play. No stats, no real inventory or items to speak of, just beatin' up bad guys. It's a nice change of pace.
