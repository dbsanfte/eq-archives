I recently started a claustrophibic dwarf on FV, who I'm roleplaying as a lover of the outdoors and animals, and thunderstorms in particular, but who is deathly afraid of tunnels and caves, and will not go back into Kaladim under any circumstances. You see, he was caught in a mine cave-in for several days, and that was enough for him! Not sure how his personality will develop yet. He thought he would live out his days mining and carving gems and jewelry in Kaladim, but now his fear has led him to a rather skittish existence outside. He really doesn't know where to go or what to do, and is not really embracing the outdoor life just yet. In game terms, he's a Paladin, but will roleplay as more of a bard/ranger combo...he loves to sing, after all.

I was just wondering if there were any other folks out there roleplaying a unique character? I heard about the DE necromancer who dedicated her life to Tunare, and I met a barbarian warrior who was ripped into this world when Nagafen ripped his mother's guts out.

Any other interesting characters out there? It seems like there are alot of great RP ideas out there, but not many of you speak dwarven just yet.

I think my Dwarf may qualify as "unique," or close to it.

First of all, she's beardless, and very self-conscious about her "deformity." She goes ballistic when people ask her if she's a Gnome or Halfling.

Secondly, she likes to learn languages, but hates to speak Dwarvish around non-Dwarves, or teach Dwarvish to non-Dwarves. This is my take on Dwarves in general, not just this one character. I basically stole this idea from Tolkien. In "The Hobbit" and "Lord of the Rings," the Dwarves hardly ever spoke any Dwarvish. I don't even remember any Dwarvish being spoken at all, except for Gimli's battle cry. And outside the Gates of Moria, when Gandalf is trying to open the password-protected door, he mentions how reluctant Dwarves are to share their language.

Thirdly, she's a lot smarter than most people play Dwarves. Yes, her natural Intelligence is only 65, but her natural Wisdom is 113. I don't really know how to role-play high Wisdom combined with low Intelligence, so I role-play her as if she had a high Intelligence.

Fourthly, I envision Dwarves as Russo-Germanic, which lead me to pick the name Brunhild, after the legendary chief of the Valkyries in the Nibelungenlied (see http://www.encyclopedia.com/articlesnew/01940.html ). And no, she is NOT named after anybody's sword. I expect she'll get a Russian or Polish surname. When she speaks Human Tongue, she uses a kind of Germanic syntax without articles to reflect that. I think the famous Russian stoicism is a good starting point for constructing a Dwarven personality.
