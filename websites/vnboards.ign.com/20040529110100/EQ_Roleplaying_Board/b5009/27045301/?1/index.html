You have to leave it to those gnomes. They provide the world with amazing feats of tinkering; BUT something went horribly wrong in Steamfront this evening.

A cry for help from the watchmen of Ak'Anon was heard during a peaceful meeting of our beloved Storm's New Outriders. With business being complete in the Greater Faydark, our very own Alistra created a portal magically transporting our motley group to the Steamfront Mountains.

Upon arrival in Steamfront, I must admit that I saw nothing out of the ordinary. Although the shouts of other travelers could be heard, a young gnome distracted my attention, asking if I could bestow the Spirit of Wolf upon her. Seeing the Rogue for what she was, I decided to oblige her so that she could be away from me quickly. I had no desire to let her stand there until my pockets started to look inviting. With the little thief safely away, I returned my attention to the situation at hand only to discover that I was all alone at the portal ring. Scanning the grass for a sign, I could clearly see Gustyn Farrunner's tracks heading straight for the windmill. Under the guise of a wolf, I was off at a run.

Then things got interesting, confusing, and downright bizarre.

"Wow", someone shouted. "He didn't stand a chance" Everyone was in a panic. There dead as a stone on the grass lay our noble leader Gustyn himself. Not far away Duece was becoming worm fodder. Alistra was running in circles looking for the evil. Xyran had called up an elemental, and had it chasing after something. As for steady reliable Nuldias, I was looking for him when I heard a grinding mechanical voice in a language not completely comprehensible. The voice said something to this effect: "Self destruct mode initiated? Or something like that. ... 3...2...1"

Concussion, a light as bright as 10 suns. Pain, as sharp as a knife-edge, yet light as a feather. ... Darkness. ... Nothing.

Then rain, blessed beautiful rain! Karana's. Tears washing all sorrow and pain from my soul. I opened my eyes and, for a moment, I swear I stood with Karana, warmed, cherished, and happy.

Have you ever stepped off a cliff on a pitch-black night on the way to High Keep? Neither have I, but the feeling I got next must be what it felt like. It lasted only an instant and after it passed, to my surprise, there I stood on a blighted piece of Norrath in the Steamfront Mountains. I could hear Gustyn talking, and Duece! I must have tripped and hit my head and dreamed the whole thing I was thinking to myself. Why, this old dead dog is what I tripped on! Upon closer inspection I realized that the dead dog was a dead wolf. Upon even closer inspection I could discern a glamour about the corpse. It was ME! I was Naked! Quickly I got my clothes and goods off my corpse. And, to my surprise, after removing the last ring from my paw, the corpse vanished.

Someone or something had really made an error. Perhaps some non-gnome had tried their hand at tinkering. Perhaps it was an assault launched on Ak'Anon by some of their own. I do not know. But obviously the Gods had a hand in the clean-up. One thing I do know, the next time I hear a shout for help from the watchmen of Ak'Anon, I am heading in the opposite direction. I may have a place at Karana's side when I die; but I am in no hurry to get there now that I can smell the Jum Jum pie cooking at the Goobers.
