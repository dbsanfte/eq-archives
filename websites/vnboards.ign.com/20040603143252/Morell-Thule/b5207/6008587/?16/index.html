This post is rated LR. It is long and ranty. Viewer discretion is advised.

I really like MT and I hope that the Diablo II thing helps out here.

Three points based on what has been mentioned so far:

#1)One thing about twinking that bugs me: Its not the twinking but the accusations of it that are false!

My experience on another server (The Seventh Hammer Server):

I played a rogue, and to get my leather armour I ran mail between Felwith and Kelethin. I did this while fighting orcs, and practiing tailoring with any wolf hides I could collect. I ran the mail route quite often. So much so that I roleplayed a post-office employee.

I got enough money together to buy Leather armour and a 2 platinum ring all by level 5 (Because I spent my time making money, being a rogue and all, and not levelling.)

What did I get for this? Endless, Endless, ENDLESS accusations of twinking. Every time I would defend myself people would just accuse me of lying.

Fortunately on Morelle Thule I do not have this problem!!!
On Morrelle - Thulle when I tell people I make money rather than level, they believe me!!

Anyhow, enjoy your stay here. If you are seeking to accomodate the players on Morelle-Thule who care about their server, please, leave out-of-character exclusionary cliquing and cyber-prison-gang mentality behind you, unless you can do it in the spirit of good role-playing. That is my wish. If you do this for me, I will be more than happy to accomodate your world by filling it with an interesting, roleplayed character.

1 voice from Morrele-Thule, the Server with Civilization!

#2)My last point. Some new folk of the people on the server don't understand roleplaying and have to get used to it!

Example #1)
Yesterday a dark elf stood outside the gates of Freeport and shouted how much he hated the evil humans and their evil city. It was great roleplaying, but many people didn't get it. I had to explain to them that it is part of the game, and it wasn't personal. I wish we had an easier way for people to get a hang of the difference between role-playing and intended injury to the people on the other side of the internet connection.

Example #2)
A mage was giving 3 gold to any one who would help him fight an orc apprentice. I showed up as a level 2 half-elf rogue. A level 10 wood elf rogue also showed up. He called me a half-breed and bossed me around in great role-playing style. I played along. Unfortunately, the mage didn't seem to get this. The mage wasn't into roleplaying, and never thought to think that the wood-elf was filling in my character with our interaction. I didn't bother explaining to the mage this time, but in the future I will. The mage kept "/tell"ing me "What a jerk!". He just didn't get it. (Unless he was roleplaying his feeling towards the other rogue, but I doubt it.)

Example #3) A friend of mind is an enchanter. He decided it would be fun to set up as a trickster comical character. He enchanted himself as a camp fire in the druidic ruins near Kelethin. He then claimed to be a Bonfire of Damnation and demanded that people feed him rusty weapons (to practice his blacksmithing skill) It was roleplaying. He new perfectly well that the other players knew he was an illusion. He was playing the man behind the curtain in the Wizard of Oz. Would he have taken the weapons without rewarding the newbies? Ofcourse not, he was setting up a spontaneous player driven quest. Did the players get it? Nope, ofcourse not. They petitioned his 'abuse' and challenged him to a duel for being a 'jerk' and so forth. Maybe one or two of them roleplayed responses that made it clear that they caught on.

People who come to Morrelle-Thule need to understand that many of us will roleplay, and do so non-stop. When we are in combat we do not always roleplay as well. But many times we only use \tell and \ooc for game mechanics questions. Even in the depths of combat.

#3) I respect non-roleplayers on our server, becuase even that population (yes I am biased) is nicer than on other servers. I say this publicly because I don't mind if our server fills up with nice folk. In fact, it would be making a strong statement to Verant if it did. Communities come together, and so far Morell-Thule is having limitted success in this regard.
