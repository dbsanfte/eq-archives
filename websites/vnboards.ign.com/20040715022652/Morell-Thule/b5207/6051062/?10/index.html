Even though there are a lot of people who like roleplaying, some of your rules limit teh activities of a player.

"- Aaricia is at the bank. Another Teir'Dal woman is there, which offers to give her 200 sp through the trade screen: she refuses. I mean, would YOU in real life accept money from a perfect stranger you meet in the street? I surely would not..."

I would. Who would turn down money? Then again, I am a necromancer...

"I am also looking for an inventive use of the duel command here. I think members of the group should NOT be allowed to refuse a duel, and I would like some sort of booty to be given by the loser of a duel (maybe only rings, jewellery, weapons and money, with ONE of those chosen by the victor)."

This basically says that if you want something that someone else has, simply duel him and take it. This is a case of the strong picking on the weak, and the lvl 11 guy who just got his bronze gloves has no chance against the lvl 25 who wants them to complete his set.

"So your characters should always try to obtain experience mainly through quests, not by 'camping' at monsters spawn sites. You should also never buy or trade for quest items (except within the group maybe), stop doing a quest after you have succeeded, and
so on...
The quest should also be the mainreason adventurers in the group join for a limited amount of time."

That would be nice, but unfortunately the game is not designed to support that. This role playing game is designed with the belief that doing quests for experience is exploitive.

"- Niphredil is a wood-elf scout. She can't stand Dark Elves or their necromancer allies. So, when she bashes a skeleton, instead of selling the bones to anyone, she simply destroys them so that they can't be used by any necromancers."

Well, there are quests for bone chips . Better than throwing away money and experience.

"- your druid really can't stand the erudite necromancer in the group. What better way to avenge yourself from his sarcasms than, just when enemies start taking him as a target after dispatching his pet, using that brand new gate spell of yours ;0)"

This is completely cowardly. If anyone in your guild does that, they will end up with a bad reputation with everyone. Not good for a loving druid in a roleplaying sense.

But overall, your idea is good. I'd like to see more roleplaying on our server, and maybe even get a chance to kill some of your wood elf members myself. Maybe we can have a guild war with each other where our good aligned fight each other but the evil ones help each other when possible and kill the good guys.

Hail fellow Norrathians,

I am one of the people behing the Two Chalices Guild idea, and just wanted to reply to some comments I saw above.

- First of all, the charter is there as a general background only, not a law book. Every character is free to interpret it depending on his personality, to add or remove to it, etc.
I never intended the charter to be a militation to free will, only a source of ideas...

- Second, about the 'lack of trust' and duel problems, these ideas are meant to be used only with other guild members, not imposed on other players. The basic idea of the guild is to have its members go questing together, so there should often be times when these ideas might apply...

- And finally, some of you simply mention they would not join such a guild, and that is fine with me: I have no intention of recruiting 5,000 members, as it would make the roleplaying and guild management part a nightmare. I only want people who share most of my views on EQ roleplaying to apply, and it is only logical that most people will not do so...

As a last word, thanks for your comments, and if you are interested in knowing more about us, visit our fresh (and still fledgling) website at http://users.skynet.be/2chalices
