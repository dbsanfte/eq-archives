Anon24.25.158.x
 Date Posted: 11/24/99 8:35pm Subject: sigh
I feel the same about the popularity contest issue. Guildmembers or other friends can unbalance a lotto by a lot, and usually do. Seems unfair to me, that your equipment turns out to be based upon who you know, not what you do. It would make more sense, to me, if people who contribute the most, or at least, just people who can use the item go in the lotto for it.
The mistwalker thing that happened at the vox hunt last night...screams lame. Honestly, what good is that sword to a level 50 druid? I never would have thought druids were a melee class. Yay, now we can have druids in a group, instead of using their spells, heals, dots, dd's, thorns, etc...can now concentrate on their insane melee capabilties. Ya, step up and swing at the lord a few times, if you're lucky, your low dex (compared to ranger) will proc your pet, and it will swing a couple times and die. Meanwhile, the tank of your group is getting himself flatened because no one is healing him. I really hope he doesnt die, cause that pet which your sword proced isnt much of a damage taker.
Now, I can't possibly be the only one who sees something wrong with a druid having a weapon like this. It must be every ranger's goal to obtain one of these weapons. It is a great item, and im sure it would enhance their melee ability by a lot. You must ask yourself, how often does a druid melee?
Anyways, that isn't the point. In situations like this(dragons), the loot is so good, and it is hard to obtain. People who can actually use and benefit from each item, should be the ones who go in for the lotto. Popularity contest is lame. After all, what is more fun? Going head to head with a dragon, or walking around with a piece of loot after? Funny...i think the problem is that too many people would pick the second part.

-Someone who is level 50 and still enjoying the game, with crappy equipment

Anon206.47.244.x
 Date Posted: 11/25/99 4:56pm Subject: I did find it kinda weird
It was my first time at a Dragon's raid. I was fine with the rule that only people who can use a weapon can enter a lotto. I can imagine the frustration of a Ranger seeing the sword go to a Wizard. At least if you don't win you know another ranger will put it to go use.

As for a druid winning a sword. Well...he can use it so he should be able to. How is that a waste ? A waste is a wizard winning the sword only to see it up for grabs the next day on E-Bay. I personally know of a druid, who is a good friend of mine, whose ultimate dream it is to obtain that fabled sword. He can use it. He wants it. So why not ? Why should anyone be allowed to say he's never to get the sword he dreams of because someone decided it was a waste. Well it's not a waste to him...who is to say otherwise.

Anyone who can use an item can participate in the lotto. It's a simple rule. It would be foolish to start diluting with exceptions based on perceptions of its relative usefullness to a class. That would only open things up to arguments and hard feelings.

As for the giving your vote to other people....well i find that strange myself. I am not sure how it evolved but I do agree it's disturbing how it turns a lotto into a popularity contest.

It should just be....if you can use it you can win it. Period. No exceptions, no value judgements...just a simple clear cut rules. That is always what works best
