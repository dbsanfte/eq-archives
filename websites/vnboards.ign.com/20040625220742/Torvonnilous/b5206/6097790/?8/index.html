"So this is the place, few dare speak of in the vale." Muttered Rynelphe under his breath, as he sloshed through steaming puddles that began to evaporate almost as soon as the hard rain formed them. He looked with wonder at the huge open doors, one of wich had fallen possibly hundreds of years ago and now hung precariously in front forming a rough ramp up to the doorway. "Don't forget were you are" Rynelphe thought to himself, as he gave a quick look around to make sure nothing was near or saw him that would take aggresion. He had seen countles magical beings made of fire wandering this land that was filled with pools of lava. He knew what they were, he had been told about them, they were fire demons, or elementals depending on who spoke of them. He knew he had strong magic to use against them, but he didn't want to run into one in surprise, after all they still frightened him. As he stepped onto the huge fallen door to make his way through the Archway he was careful. Suddenly, out ran a Dark Elf, holding a scythe and wearing a cloak so black it was as if it was a dimensional hole. Rynelphe trembled, up until now no one had been able to see him, he had been able to make himself invisible to all around him, with his druidic magic. But this elf clearly had seen him. Rynelphe watched as the Dark Elf ran out of view. "Why did I leave the Vale!" Wondered Rynelphe who's heart began to beat furiously. Then he remembered the resolve with which he journeyed through Kithicore. It had been a long journey with many perils, and all of it had only sought to strengthen his curiosity on what else was out there. Slowly he continued and peered his head into the doorway.......

Sitting along the walls were a mismatch of people. There were mages of various circles, Dark elf, Humans, even a High elf. They sat apart, some wounded and resting, but all sitting peacably among each other. It was such an awkward situation that Rynelphe found himself muttering out a greeting, "Hello all...." A few raised their heads and looked around. Rynelphe never thought he would see people who worshiped Innurok sitting peaceably next to those who worshiped Karana. It only made him more curious. Then Rynelphe saw the puzzled looks on their faces and realized that most still could not see him. He let go of the magic that held him invisible to all. Suddenly Rynelphe saw someone had seen before, months ago in Mysty Thicket where he had begun his Druidic Training. "Leareth!" Spoke Rynelphe as his eyes made a smile, reflecting his relief to have something he recognized in this new place. Leareth walked up and bent over to shake Rynelphes Hand. "Ahh, Hello Ryn. Long time no see." "Glad to see you here Lear!" Spoke Rynelphe. Leareth cast a spell that immediatly seemed to set Rynelphe at peace and ease his mind, he could feel the tiredness of weilding magic decrease and feel as if he was regenerating his power faster. "Thanks" In return Rynelphe moved his hands to call upon the Spirit of the Great Wolf, even here in this place so far from the vale he found it heard him, and immediatly Leareth began to move at a faster pace. "If you need help let me know" Spoke Leareth in a serious tone. "Thanks, I will." Responded Rynelphe. Rynelphe moved to the back of the hall and sat down. He knew of the place that existed further in these walls, the resting place of the Dark Sorceresss Najena. He knew he wasn't ready to go exploring there yet, he would come back one day. Now he rested to recover his magic so he could go about freeing this hurt place. The earth was cracked and split open, spilling it's blood as fire. And the evil imps and elemtals that roamed only added to the earth's torture here. Rynelphe stood, he would now go out and face his fears, he would slay one of these creatures...

Rynelphe did not walk far out of the halls of the temple to find his first target. There was a small imp, that sat by a pool of fire. A drake flew by it, making a horrible screeching sound. It was like metal grating on metal. Rynelphe cringed, as he went through his pouches making sure everything was prepared. He closed his eyes and felt the sourroundings readying his mind for what was ahead. Slowly he began to move his hands and a small blue light enveloped the imp. This light was a solidified mass of air, that would slow his target down. The imp turned and looked, being a thing of magic, it began to cast it's own spell, a surge of fire struck Rynelphe who was already falling back to the entrance of the temple. "Errgh.." Rynelphe shook as the fire struck him... Now he made motions for another spell. He new this imp wasnt natural to this world and was born of magic. So he began a powerful spell to send it back to nothing. As the strong natural light struck the imp, it staggered, and screamed. Then it ran towards Rynelphe as fast as it could, but for it, it was if it was wading through water, and Rynelphe was moving with the Spirit of the great wolf. Rynelphe tore at the imp again with another strong blast of his magic. Then suddenly, a strong force hit Rynelphe at his right, and nearly struck him to the ground. Rynelphe turned and beat his right arm to put out the flame that had ignited his sleeve. He saw an elemental, and it was fast running towards him again. Rynelphe moved fast up a hill and made ready another spell, and soon the ele was covered in the same thick air that enveloped the imp. Rynelphe looked at both foes, now unsure of what to do. Rynelphe ran down the hill and flew past the ele as it swung at nothing. He made a leap over the imp's head and kept running out into an open plane where he could see nothing around him. Then he began blasting the imp with his magic. By the fifth blast the imp fell, and Rynelphe nearly as well. He was nearly spent from using such magic, he was not used to these situations. He ran up to the imp and took the gems it carried from out of a pouch around it's waist. "These may pay for a new spell if I get, enough" Spoke Rynelphe, just as he fell on hs face, from a blast to the back of him. He turned around on the ground, another ele learred over him. His back felt as if it was numb, but slowly pain was seeping through the shock. As he stood to his feet the ele struck him hard accross his face. Rynelphe screamed in agaony as the eles fist seared his flesh as it struck. Rynelphe staggered up, and started running. He ached, everything hurt, and worst yet he came to realize, he was lost. There was nothing but a sea of grey rock before him in all directions....

Rynelphe ran in a straight line hoping to find the temple to hide in, or the way back into the dark Nektulos forest where he could easily dissapear and rest. His vision was blurry, and he felt as if everything around him was slowing down. He started to scream, or did he. Things were becoming surreal, and he knew his conscienceness was slipping. He was far away from his foes now he thought, so he slumped to the ground. He started fumbling through his leaflets of parchment trying to find a spell his masters taught him called gate. As he followed the instructions roughly and tried to cast he realized he had not the energy left. Looking around he saw a circle of pillars. "The druid circle!" Screamed Rynelphe. I knew there was one here my masters told me about, I may be saved yet. He began to run towards the stone pillars. As he neared he saw a small group of adventurers sitting near it. There was one playing a song while the others rested, and most of them seemed quite at ease in a place as dangerous as this. Rynelphe ran closer, until he collapsed at a pillar. He slowly raised his head, there was nothing, all was calm. Is that an ele in the distance, no, it's my sister. My sister Shralen. She's come to see me..... Soon everything was black.

Rynelphe awoke in the arms of a young human woman. "I see you are waking, are you all right? There was some people who saved you, and they left you in my care." Spoke the woman in a calm relaxing tone. "Th-thank you..." muttered Rynelphe. "Where are they?" He asked. "By now they are deep in the earth, fighting the fire goblin clan. They are a very brave group. They call themselves 'Gift of Thought', they have come here on more than one occasion to fight the goblin clan." Rynelphe stared in wondermeant at those words. He had a desire to not be such a small person, or play such a small role in the world. But he could never dream of having so much power. "You are the druid of the circle, aren't you?" Asked Rynelphe in the form of a statement, "Help me, help me to be stronger... Can you train me?", "I will help you." Spoke the druid. "But you are not ready for my training yet. There is a place you must go. You must go back through the forest, and head to the west commons. There you will find another ring, and there she will train you. I will send her word. Rest now, you can go in the morning. I will watch over you. Sleep."

In Rynelphe stirred a hidden desire, that would not befit a druid. He wanted to grow stronger, and he wanted to fight alongside those as 'Gift of Thought'. He wanted to be able to change things, and he would. Tomorrow things would change....
