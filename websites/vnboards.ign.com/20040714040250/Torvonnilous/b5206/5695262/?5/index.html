Its a hard life for a rogue. Little to no respect from all around me, and many weaknesses that are hard to overcome. Well, I chose my path aware of these obstacles. I am who I am.
I remember the old days, when myself and two good friends, the Bard Jinnu and Druid Adun, found ourselves as outcasts in our home town of Kelethin. Our hearts were filled with darkness, and our minds full of grand aspirations. We set out to find our place in the world, and to spread our message of darkness. Truly, we did so beyond my wildest anticipations. We made many enemies across the land, and even some friends, who wished to join us in our campaign against hypocrisy. We suffered in exile from Kelethin together, and prospered as one. These memories I will cherish, but they cannot continue to be.
I have faced many evils in my time, but never really understood them. I took the side of darkness out of opposition to light. I was ignorant to the fact that there exists a middle ground in life. Today as I faced the evils of Unrest, and the hatred brewing within my guild, I knew that I could no longer claim to possess a heart of darkness, when I find such reward in destroying the beasts of darkness.
It all seemed to come to me in a moment, an epiphany of sorts. I realized that my loathing of hypocritical 'goodys' did not necessarily confine me to a life of evil. There are truly hypocrits on both ends of the scale, and I hate them both equally. With this in mind, I then realized that Dark Malus was no longer pushing the message of evil that it set out to do. It was a truly disconcerting realization.
I made the decision then and there to leave Dark Malus, and did so immediately. My many friends within the guild will surely hate me for abandoning them so close to the hour of war with Pious Sodality, a war which I have promulgated at every turn. I know not the words I can express to them to atone for this abandonment. I only hope that they might forgive me.
I look around me and see so much potential joy in this world. I have been focusing on the sadness, when I should have been relishing my every moment of life. I shall not continue to live my life in this mistake. I must turn my attention to bettering myself, and making those around me happy, along with myself. I know that my life shall ultimately be committed to destroying the hypocrits, but I cannot do this yet. I have not the strength. nor the proper motivation. First I must truly discover myself, and it is to this goal that I now commit myself. My word holds true though, all you hypocrits, know that the day will come when I shall lay my daggers into you and cleanse your vile souls. This is my pledge.
Journeying back to Kelethin, after years in exile, my heart swells in anticipation. Will my hometown forgive me for my wrongs? Will I be able to live in peace? I approach softly, trying to remain hidden from the guards, but they hear my movements in the brush. There is a moment of anticipation as I step within view, and the guard turns his head towards me. A warm smile creeps upon his face, and he speaks gently..
"Welcome back, Bazzer. I trust your journeys were profittable."
In that moment I know that there can be redemption, even for the darkest of souls, and for the most mischevious of rogues. I reply with my own smile...
"All is well, Guard, for I am home."

Bazzer
Rogue of Kelethin
