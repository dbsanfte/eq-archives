I was one of those melees who never saw more than 1kpp at a time... being a Shadowknight who doesn't like to sell/bargain with other players makes you poor real fast. I hate selling stuff in EC, so I'd always either give it away, sell it dirt cheap, or put it on a twink.

Fortunately for me there are camps like FG. Great exp, and after a while you get a lucky roll =)


What I find interesting about this server is the saturation level.

On a normal server everyone has 8 character per account. Suppose there are 4000 accounts on that server, and at any given time 2500 of those accounts are online. Most people have multiple characters, and a lot of people have a full 8 (and thus have 2nd and 3rd accounts). So, let's assume, for guessing average, that a person has 5 characters on their account.

4000 x 5 = 20,000 characters

Suppose half of those characters are melees that would use an FBSS

10,000 FBSS

That's why the FG is still camped 24-7 on any blue server. It's an item of high value that hasn't saturated the market yet.


But on our server it's different. One character per account. We obviously have fewer people playing. So, let's do some more math. Let's assume 3000 accounts in FV. Let's assume the same ratio of melees who can wear the FBSS: half.

That's 1,500 players who can wear that item.

Factor in other haste items as the server gets older, and the fact that casters are going to roll on this item for selling, and you can see how the FBSS camp could potentially end up slowing down in FV.


I'm not saying that's going to happen, because this is total guesswork in the estimations, and we're not even factoring in new accounts to FV, but still, looking at what I think are pretty reasonable numbers, it's obvious that IF people camp certain items on this server like they do on a blue server, the market should become saturated with those items sooner.

If that happens, what you should see on this server is the average price of items will drop faster than on a normal server.





For instance, when I first started playing, before Kunark came out, one of the major levelling zones for anyone level 20-30 was South Karana. I met a fellow there one time who showed me Darkmail Gauntlets, and said how I had to have a pair. When I asked him what he paid for them, his reply was 600pp. A fortune at the time.


Today those gauntlets sell for about 100pp, or in some cases people like me just give them away.


That kind of dramatic price decrease, which has taken 2 years, due to market saturation, will happen sooner on FV because of fewer characters.

Or, maybe it won't. Maybe the low population and general slower levelling of the people on this server will cause items like the DM Gaunts to not make their way into the economy as *fast* as on a blue server, and so maybe the decline of prices on this server will be the same as a blue server, or even slower.


I dunno. I'm not an economist, but it sure is fun to think about this stuff =)

Plus, it passes time on a slow Friday at work.


-Skelex

The Seraph on Twinks:

From a RP standpoint, Twinking is sometimes accurate. Think of it this way.

Warrior A: The son of a farmer
Warrior B: Son of a wealthy merchant
Warrior C: Son of a nobleman

Who do you think will be better equipped? This could, in some ways, justify anything up to, let's say a FS weapon and assortment of strong but not magical armor on the nobleman's son, Maybe a normal weapon and up to ring mail or banded for the merchant's son..and maybe a prized and trusted tarnished and some leather for the farmer's boy.

Now, on the other hand, let's look beyond the mundane aspect. What about magic items? Now, what is Warrior D is the grandson of a legendary warrior? Could you not then justify him inheriting, say, a magic weapon or piece of armor (not the whole kit and kaboodle) as a family treasure?

But, now let's look at what I don't find acceptable. Anything you have to quest for seems wrong. I mean, all the money in a nobleman's coffers couldn't just -buy- the crafted of an aged warrior who owes his life to it. And what adventurer would part with his FBSS- earned in a hard-fought battle- for mere coinage? (Let's not get into how players do this.). It wouldn't make much sense. And, besides, the items i've described for all situations give a respectably advantage.

I may be spiteful, but after getting kicked out of a group for not being able to do the same or better damage than the other warrior (Twinked by her Ench 54 fiance), I think I've got something of a right.
