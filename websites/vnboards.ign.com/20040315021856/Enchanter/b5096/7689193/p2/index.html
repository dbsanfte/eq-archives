I think some people here are missing the point.

Jewelcrafting, just like any skill, is sellable. If you brought a chunk of gold and a diamond to a custom jeweler in New York and asked them to make you a ring, they would. They would also charge you a hefty sum for services rendered. And If they liked what they made for you they would probably take a picture of it for their portfolio to sell the same design to others without having to do the design work and reasearch again.

When you make a piece of jewelery you are performing a service for someone that they can not do themselves. Charging for this seems logical to me. You are not just trying to cover costs, you are charging for time and ability.

Charge whatever you want for the things you create. You will find out quickly what people are willing to pay for. As in real life, the market should drive the prices up and down with availibilty and desire for the item.
