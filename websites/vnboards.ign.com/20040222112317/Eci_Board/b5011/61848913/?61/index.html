The other day at school I walked back to my lunch table, sat down, and was offered a miniature Butterfinger. I said, "No thanks, Nestle sucks." My assistant principal was standing next to us and said, "EXCUSE ME?! What did you just say?" "'Nestle Sucks'". "That word *offends* me." I was thinking to myself, 'wow he must REALLY hate Nestle too'. I didn't think he would be that stupid to be offended by the word "sucks".

"I want you to apologize for that." So I apologized for using my awful language. Now, Mr. Mauro is our new assistant principal FRESH out of the Army Reserves. He walks around like he is ready for an ambush, never moving his head, but his eyes. He also claims to be anti-violent. Wonder how someone would be willing to kill, but yet be anti-violent. Also made me wonder why such a paranoid muscle man would be offended by "sucks".

Now, lets think for a minute. If a group of terrorists said the word "Jihad" in front of Cpt. America, do you think Cpt. America would get an apology out of the group or his ass killed? People, we can't be offended by the small things in life because that's what causes so much hate. Life isn't about pecan pie and reeses pieces. You need to realize that being offended is part of life and will get you nowhere. Next time someone offends you, don't ask for an apology, kick their ass. If he would've kicked my ass, I would've never said "sucks" in front of him again. That way noone will complain. Then we can hold hands, skipping through a field of daisy's, under blue skies, with birds singing, and us singing Kumbaya (sp?). Wouldn't that be great? Hell no...

Next time maybe I'll say "sukes" or "suxzor".
