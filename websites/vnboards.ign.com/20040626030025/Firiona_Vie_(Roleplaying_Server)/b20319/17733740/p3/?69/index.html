I'm currently involved in a similar situation. In this case, it is a DE necromancer that has forsaken Innoruk. She met up with (first) a Neutral Shaman of my guild --- the two travelled together for quite some time (killing gnolls in BB, and gaing acceptance in Queynos, where she can now walk more or less freely). Somewhere along the line, the two met up with me WE (NG) Ranger. Now.. my ranger had much reason to distrust her, despite hearing tales from the Shaman about her being "good".

I've spent time in talks (RP) with her, followed along and watched her at work, and discovered that, indeed, she has forsaken Innoruk. After months of RP (nearly a year game time), she petitioned to become an Initiate of Tunare -- and I granted her request.

Now, Verant dictates that she, as a DE Necro, must be evil. Her "official" EQ alignment is NE... however, she is not evil. She is RPed as a NG character.

There are obvious problems --- grouping, healing/buffing. However, my Ranger is proud to name this one friend, and will do what he can to help out. No, I will not change my alignment to be able to group --- that is not the character. However, we will continue to RP as we can, accepting the limitations VI has placed on us.

People are calling for stricter coding on the alignments --- I don't see the need for that --- doing so will prevent some wonderful roleplay opportunities.
