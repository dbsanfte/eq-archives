Eliezer is right; the server you want is Sullon Zek, Eladari. There's only one character per account, it's PvP (based on deity alignment), and no trivial loot code.

As I've posted elsewhere, I played there for a while. The big problem, I felt, was the sense of being entrapped in one's beginning zone, at least if you're Good. Also, there's a lot of random 'grief killing' of lower-level characters (like 6th) by higher level ones (like 51st). Repeatedly, and over and over. Certain character class/race combinations are unheard of simply because they can't survive it: humans (in general) and, in particular, human paladins.

There are lots of other problems that go along with this PvP environment. Roleplaying goes out the window, for example, because the only defense against 'grief killing' is leveling, magic resistance, and armor class. (Or a well-coordinated defense, which I've yet to see implemented.) Roleplaying also goes out the window because much of the discussion within a group is tactical. Scanning /who for names you don't recognize is a way of life; /shouting warnings about enemies in the zone is a way of life.

Training in the trade skills is very difficult for many because the resources are invariably in some enemy's zone. Want to be an elven blacksmith? Have to go to Kaladim, and you might get killed by some random dwarf 40 levels your senior along the way.

If you want PvP on Firiona Vie, use /duel and /guildwar, or turn in the Book of Order and Discord to a Priest of Discord. Personally, I'm glad to shed the paranoia of Sullon Zek and to play my character the way he should be played.

My comment was short and too the point, but not intended as a 'love it or leave it' message.

Sullon Zek and Firiona Vie have different, but similar Rulesets. FV has a more strict one in terms of Language and TLC, but a looser one in terms of 'Teams.' Good and Evil cannot group/buff but other than that anything goes. SZ has the 'standard' Ruleset, but hardcoded teams based on Deity selection. It also has rescinded PnP allowing for 'hardcore' PvP activity.

While Roleplay is possible on both, I think (and have posted several times concerning) that for truly viable Roleplay, consent is essential. But others have their own ideas. But as one of these servers is already PvP, I think leaving FV as Blue is the better choice as it allows those who do not like PvP combat to have a choice in who and what they fight.

Also, to make FV PvP would require either Free For All style a la Rallos Zek, or reconfiguring the Alignments into teams, which they are NOT at present. They are merely an enforced RP Guide to prevent Good and Evil from cooperating.

The problem you're going to have with ANY "Roleplaying" server is realism vs. playability. Without the proper rules and game engine, you're going to have to forgo one for the other. It's like a balance: the more realism you add, the less roleplaying that's going to happen.


The core problem with creating a more realistic game is simple: players who will spend all of their time causing havoc for other players in a PvP environment. Griefers I guess they are called. I just call 'em jerks.


The only reason this happens is because permanent death is not realized in the game engine. People, given no restraints, will do anything they please. So, you end up with corpse camping and "griefing". It's stupid, but it's a result of the game engine and immature people. Put the two things together and that's what you're going to get.


If you want a REAL roleplaying experience, with true depth and realism, the current EQ engine is never going to provide that. And you can't put real death in EQ because the way the game is made it is far too easy to die. A couple green mobs in the wrong place and BAM, you're done. How many people die multiple times in one day? And forget uber encounters... they would never happen in EQ with perm death.


So, if you're talking about using the EQ engine to create a roleplaying atmosphere, where people can develop characters and relationships and conquer grand foes, then you have to remove the PvP side of the equation. If you don't, this server ends up just like Sullon Zek, which is then pointless, because we already have a Sullon Zek.

That's what people mean when they make that comment: play on Sullon. There is already a server for that type of behavior. Changing the rules on this server only duplicates the role of something that already exists.

And believe me, if you try to put this ruleset on a PvP server, you'll end up with NO roleplaying. It's difficult enough now with the language barriers, TLC, etc. You'll just end up with a bunch of people killing each other. It will blow chunks.



Someday a game is going to come up with a way to do permanent death correctly. Or at the very least ressurections won't simply be a button click away. When that happens we'll have taken one more step forward toward a real roleplaying atmosphere.


I mean, think about it. What do you need to make a "real" roleplaying game (game-mechanics-wise). Think about it:




(1) You need one character per account with permanent death. That would change everything. You'll never stop assholes, because even those exist in real life. What you will stop them going unpunished. Jerks will be more apt to be jerks at lower levels, because at higher levels they're not going to want to lose their uber-loot to a bunch of lynch mobs.

(2) You need total PvP. No "safe" zones. Everyone accountable for their actions, totally. If you're level 50 and kill a level 5 person, you're going to be hunted down and killed, and all your uberness will be gone. Period. Accountability.

(3) You need a completely dynamic world. No multiple spawns of Nagefan. Just one. One Nagefan. When he's gone, he's gone. Some other dragon might eventually take over his dungeon, or maybe some other creature. But you can't have named Mob X respawning every 30 minutes. Just doesn't work that way in a realistic environment. Permanent death needs to work both ways, for you and the mob. This would keep the world moving, evolving. Norrath is stagnant. The history of Norrath hasn't really changed. Imagine a game where it did! Where YOU might be a part of that history. Where monuments might be erected in YOUR name because you and your friends slayed the dragon that terrorized your town... Imagine.

(4) You need unique items, and limited items. You need one-of-a-kind items. Everything droppable. Imagine if you ran across the corpses of some high level adventurers who died in the deep end of a dungeon, and found Innoruuk's Curse on one of them! And meanwhile the person who actually played that character is now level 3 and trying frantically to level up because they know where they were killed, and where that stuff is, but it's gonna be a long time before they can get back into that dungeon at that high level... So you loot it, and now it is yours. Imagine the stories that could be told from items changing hands in a variety of different ways.

(5) You need small server populations. Population caps. Period. Not enough servers? Get more. Every server should have a cap on it, and that number should be displayed. If Server X has 3 spots open that should be displayed so people can decided if they want to make a character on that server. Sure, only 60% of the population might be on the server at one time. Calculate those things in, but put a cap on it and keep it low. 80 people in a zone is rediculous.

If you did these things in a game, and made a truly dynamic world where GM events were't just death-slaughter-zone-wide killings, but real one-of-a-kind events that changed the history of the game, then you'd see a really good RP environment.


Currently, you can't do that in EQ, and the game isn't designed for it. So, a choice is made on FV: more realism (PvP) or less (TLC, no PvP). They choose less realism, but for the benefit of the server, for the benefit of roleplaying. I'm happy with that choice. I understand the contraints of this game, the bondaries of this engine. It's a fair trade.


-Skelex
