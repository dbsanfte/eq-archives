Soon enough, he's behind me with his lights on, so I pull over into the nearby churchs parking lot. He comes up to me and says "License, registration, insurance". I accidently gave him my expired registration/insurance (had it in my center console, just got new proof of insurance/registration today). He says that is expired, I give him the new ones.

He says "You don't have a front licenseplate on your car, why?" I say "It's a brand new bumper and I'm waiting for the mounting kit to get here." He says "Why are you driving it today then?" I tell him it's because I had to go to school. Please note throughout this whole thing I'm smiling and being nice, I was just in a really great mood today, up until now.

He walks back to his bike, assumedly radioing in my information to make sure I didn't have any outstanding warrants. He comes back to my window 3 or so minutes later and says "You are also missing the red reflectors in your tail lights." This is true. Then he says "And I assume this registration is to your father; It's not signed". I'm just thinking this whole time he is telling me what I'm going to need to fix.

He walks back to his bike and starts writing on a pad. I figured he was going to give me a fix it ticket: you know, the kind where you take it to court, prove that you fixed it, and the fine is waved. But no, he hands me a ticket for 202 dollars. I said "Is it a fix-it-ticket?" and he says "No, it's a ticket-ticket." It's at this point I realize what a complete douchebag this guy was.

As soon as he walks away, Kelly says "Wow, what the f**k? He just kept adding stuff on".

I drop her off at her house and go home, because obviously at this point I'm pretty pissed off. Luckily, I get home, tell my dad, and it turns out that both of my parents are friends with the chief of that towns police. I believe this will be getting fixed tonight.

Though, in a sense, I hope that I get to go to court because after I beat the ticket (using a lawyer), I want to tell this cop I hope he gets shot.
