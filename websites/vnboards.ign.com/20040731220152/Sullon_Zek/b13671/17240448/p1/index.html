The skill in PvP comes before people start throwing spells and swinging swords. It's about who your friends are, what your preplanned methods of escaping enemies are, how skillfully you locate and destroy enemies with minimal chance of failure...it's like real war (or gang fighting might be more appropriate). Real war is not about fighting people on even terms.

Besides, duelling is inherently unfair. No Wizard (at my level anyway, level 24) is ever going to beat a Paladin/Cleric/Druid/Shaman in a duel, unless the other player is dumb, because the other guy can heal everything the Wizard throws at him. At best in the right terrain the Wizard can flee indefinitely. Same with a Shadow Knight, except then the SK will kill the Wizard more easily. Rangers are shafted in many duels too, as are Clerics. Your level 6 Druid would get obliterated by a level 6 Mage who gave his earth pet a rusty scythe, and there wouldn't be a damn thing you could do about it. Is that more fair to you?

Duels remove the factors of locating ability (Track), working in groups, the ability to survive and escape assaults by superior forces...PvP is less unfair (though it still is unfair) since anyone can have friends, and Clerics can use heals to full advantage, Rangers can use Track to full advantage, and the disadvantage of long refresh times on Lay on Hands and Harm touch are made significant.

You just don't understand PvP. You think it's a bunch of losers killing you, and you are not to blame for dying, and there is no skill involved. To some extent that's true...but then again, why don't you find places where there is less griefing? Why don't you start saving your pennies for magic resist equipment (though it takes a while to get a suit of this stuff...by level 20 untwinked you should be able to start resisting Roots and Snares fairly often). Why don't you earn your way into a good guild that will give you the allies you need?

And...just suck up the griefing. It happens. Level 6 is not the time to do the PvP thing, any more than it's the time to do guild raids. It's really the time to level up and learn to survive while you are weak. Knowing how to survive while you are weak and how to level up to where you aren't quite weak is important. Everyone has to do the PvE thing to get stronger, even level 60's, since there are alternate advancement skills, and because gear matters a lot, and because your friends might need your help levelling, and the strength of your friends is your strength.

To appreciate PvP you need a perspective adjustment. Like I said, the fight is not when two even levels come together and duke it out. There's much more to it than that.
