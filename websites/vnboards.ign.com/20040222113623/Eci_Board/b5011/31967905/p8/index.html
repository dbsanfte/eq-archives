Snacko, my Agnostic take on your question is:

It is impossible to tell.

It really is. I was raised Catholic, and my parents were raised Catholic, and so on and so forth, so I could certainly see how Christianity (or at least Catholicism) may have had an impact on my moral upbringing.

However, I do not know the state of mind of my parents when they made their decisions on how to raise me. I do not know if my parents fully rejected, fully accepted, or partly accepted/rejected their prior moral upbringing and decided to act on their own recognizance.

For instance, I disagree with some Christian moral implications, and I agree with others. I agree that murder is wrong. I disagree that sex outside of marriage is wrong. Just two examples.

You may very well be right that the moral foundation of Christianity has "suffused" everything. That is a perfect word to describe your way of thinking, and it is something I have tussled with and admittedly have said in my own discussions with people.

I would say that your opinion is probably more valid than the opposing viewpoint, but it is impossible - completely impossible - to detect which side is correct. Either the moral foundations of Christianity has suffused into everything or it hasn't. I don't see how anyone can be sure of either side.

However, I maintain that without the Christian moral foundation, Humanity would still have either the same, or roughly the same, concept of morality. Other religions tend to preach the same basic moral code, even if they don't appear to practice it all the time. In other words, almost anyone of any religion will state "Murder is wrong." When you get to vaguer issues, there might be some incompatibilities, but religious differences are usually not moral ones - they are differences in tradition, differences in historical belief, and differences in theistic belief. There are, of course, philosophical differences between religions, and philosophy goes a long way in determining a person's moral code, but at the CORE of most issues, I suggest that most religions agree.

To me, this proves that morality comes from man, not religion. But that is because I am not religious. Perhaps there IS a "God," and every religion shares this same "God," but they are all interpreting "Him" differently. This could very well be the case, and Morality very well may be based on "God." The religious members of The United States, being subjected to Christianity more than most other religions, would obviously collectively believe that Morality was a direct result of Christianity.

My point here is that if you removed Christianity completely...if you blanked it completely out of existence - even going into the past - America would be just as Moral as it is now - in possibly the same exact way.

I believe the mentality of our forefathers is what shaped the collective moral code of this nation. I believe that Morality may be a part of oneself, perhaps even something genetic, and that it is shaped by society.

I guess the bottom line is: do I believe that Christianity has shaped American society? Yes I do. And I agree that it may have affected my moral upbringing. But it is impossible to tell.
