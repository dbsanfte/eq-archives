Yep, after hearing more whining from warrior friends here's another idea I had trying to think of way to make them more interesting but still balanced.

Beginning at level 40, warriors can train 5 new skills mimicking the caster/healer specialization skills. You can train all 5 skills and progress to 50 skill in all, but only one can exceed 50 and continue on to the top cap of 200 skill. So you could only have 4 skills at 50 skill cap and 1 at 200. There should also be a mini-quest to reset these specialized skills similiar to the caster one in Temple of Sol Ro. The chance of skill improvement should be inherently very low, as obviously you get a heck of alot more swings in one combat then a caster will get of castings and to prevent it being too easy switching specialization.

1) Specialization: Slashing
After training this skill, everytime the warrior swings a 1 or 2 handed slashing weapon in combat there is a chance of skill improvement just like casting specialization. A successful skill check on the swing results in +15 attack bonus and the weapon treated as though it was 1 damage rating higher then normal (a 10/20 sword treated as if it was 11/20) with +2% additional chance of getting a critical hit.

2) Specialization: Blunt
same as above with 1 or 2 handed blunt weapons

3) Specialization: Piercing
pretty obvious I think

4) Combat mastery
this works just like the defensive skills dodge,parry,riposte- a successful skill check negates the hit, ie. an orc attempts to hit YOU but you evade the blow! Perhaps unlike the above defensive skills this could even work when being attacked from behind.

5) Combat tactics
On a successful skill check with any swing by the warrior for the next 5 seconds anyone including that warrior will have an additional 5% chance of scoring a critical hit on that target with a melee hit. Even those classes with no inherent chance of getting critical hits receive this bonus, ie. a cleric swings and sees, "Bobthewarrior has created an opening! You score a critical hit (142)!"
This skill should also generate some minor value of hate for the warrior, the hate addition will stack with successive successful skill checks, but no additional 5% bonus to critical hits is applied, even if another warrior targeting that mob makes the skill check.

I think this is one way to satisfy alot of the complaints warriors have in a balanced method. They just have to choose what direction to go in and later down the road can change for example like to combat mastery if they expect to be tanking more raid encounters or whatnot.
