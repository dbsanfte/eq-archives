You can save them as JPG images. Open StartMenu>Programs>Accessories>Paint.exe, within that Paint prtogram use File>Open to open the pictures from your camera (I am assuming they are in BMP format), and then use File>SaveAs and under "Save As Type" select JPEG. I think JPG image compression is the most efficient way to store graphic images.

If you have Photoshop, open the picture in question, and then go to file > save for web. You get a preview of how compressed the picture is, and can see how big the file will be when it's saved. MSPaint does an awful job compressing imo, so if you have access to Photoshop, this is the way to go.

I'm not sure about Paint Shop Pro or any other fancy program, but I'm willing to bet they have that option also.

edit - As a side note, jpeg is the best way to compress colorful pictures photographs, while gif is better suited for pictures with few colors, like commercial logos.
