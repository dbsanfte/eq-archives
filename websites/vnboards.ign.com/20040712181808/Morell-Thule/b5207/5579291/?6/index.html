I often see the argument that you can't really roleplay on a "carebear" server, because if a High Elf saw a Dark Elf, he would attack it on sight...or similar situations. Where is it written that every single member of a certain race always attacks on sight every single member of a certain other race, at all times, under all circumstances? Yes, certain races are "KOS" in certain places, but that only means that a certain subset of that society (i.e. NPC guards and the like) will kill any member of said race. That does not mean that every player who plays a citizen of such a place MUST also kill on sight ever member of the KOS race.

I can reolplay just fine on a "carebear" server, since I tend not to roleplay characters who attack and try to kill on sight every member of any given race. For example, my Paladin, Greyjoy. Because of his background, there is only one thing he automatically kills on sight, and that is undead...as long as he thinks there is a reasonable chance of his winning. He is not a fool, and knows he can do no good for his God by dying needlessly. But if he sees undead that he believes he has the strength to destroy, he will attempt to do so. He will certainly kill other things, but not automatically any member of a race or species on sight. If he were to find himself face to face with a Bloodsabre, for example (the Shadowknight arch-foes of his order) who was within the realm of my level, *and I, the player, knew this other player to be a strong, committed roleplayer* I would attempt to destroy him in a duel, hoping that he would respond or not based on his roleplaying choices.

Roleplayers can really only roleplay with other roleplayers, and cannot expect to force others to interact with them in the same way. There are methods in place for roleplayers to try to attack and kill other roleplayers. Yes, they require the player's consent, but who says that is wrong? To claim that you can only really rolplay if you have the capability to attack and kill another character without their player's consent is ludicrous. Just to be clear, the *character* need not give consent, but the *player* does. For example, a Bloodsabre sees me, and recognizes that I am a member of the Knights of Thunder, his arch-foes. I do not know he is a Bloodsabre. The players tells me privately "I am a Bloodsabre, and would like to RP attacking you by surprise, will you accept a duel challenge so we can fight?" I will most likely agree, unless there is some mitigating factor. I, the player, knowingly accept the other player's duel challenge. But we RP that I was attacked by surprise and am desperately fighting to stay alive.

The systems in place on the blue servers require agreement between players that RP fights between them are acceptable to all parties involved. That is perfectly fair. No one should be forced to RP a situation they do not consent to. To kill someone who doesn't know it's going to happen, who has no interest in RP and in no way wanted to be a part of the scene you wanted to act out is nothing more than using that other person as a prop in your little drama. They should be fellow actors, not props. And that requires their consent. Do not misunderstand me. I do believe that that consent is there on the PvP servers as well. By chosing to play on one in the first place, you give implied consent. You know going in that you can be attacked and killed at any time, for RP reasons. You also know that you can be attacked and killed at any time for k3wl d00d PK psycho reasons as well. I don't want to be a part of that latter portion. Therefore I stick to the blue servers. You can do the same sort of RPing there that you can on a PvP server, it just requires active consent by all parties, rather than passive consent.

/applaud Random

That is the clearest and most concise statement I've ever seen of my own position on roleplaying. My hat's off to you, sir.

I am truly baffled by people who think roleplaying characters who attack everybody they disagree with is the only way to roleplay, or even a very good way. All you have to do is play a character who doesn't think that way, and IMHO those are much more likely to be a complex and interesting RP persona. No doubt these bloody minded folk have some point, but its an alien mindset to me, and probably something I can never understand.

I've played and GMed paper-and-pencil RPGs, D&D and many others for over 20 years, and I consider myself to be a "serious" roleplayer. And even there I found PKing to be unacceptable. It always caused so much discord between players as to break up the group. The best groups I played in never allowed it at all, and the worst groups, the ones I wish I could forget, often did.

I realize EQ is a far different environment, but I think the same principle still applies. The psycho-killer PK k3wl d00dz will invariably outnumber roleplayers on "red" servers, as I observed for myself on Rallos Zek, and I want no part of them. Real roleplayers who've advanced their RP skills beyond childhood "cops and robbers" can find other ways to RP than naked aggression.
