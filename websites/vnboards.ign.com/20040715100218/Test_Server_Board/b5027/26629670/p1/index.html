anyway so far i have:

general:
-run speed 3/3 (run faster!)
-innate regen 3/3 (+1 HP per tick per level)

archtype:
-mental clarity 3/3 (+1 mana per tick per level)

class:
-exodus 1/1 (mana free group intrazone evac, 0.5 sec cast time, 72 mins re-use time)
-innate camo (puts invis on a hotkey, so you can go invis at will without needing to mem a spell. instant cast time, 7 second re-use time)

working on:
-dire charm (9 points ouch )
-healing adept (improves amount spells heal)
-spell casting fury (critical nukes)
-healing gift (critical heals)
-mass group buff (nice on raids, esp down the road with more group buff spells probably going in for druids)

my thoughts on druid AAs:
-exodus: i'd strongly advise any druid working on class AAs to drop what they're working on and get this now-) basically anytime you're in a jam and want out, just hit a hotkey and you're zoning to the succor point in the zone. basically there's no excuse to die once you get this (except on raids where you can't evac) unless you're just not paying attention. no more dying = more AAs-) you can also use this to split spawns if you're a fast zoner. pull a train near the succor point, use exodus, and pick off the mob you want when his friends go home. poor mans FD!

-innate camo: also very useful. works just like a circlet of shadows does for a SK/necro (you can cast it on the run). nice for pulling because you can tag a mob in the back of the zone, and hit camo so you don't pick up other aggro trash on your way back to the group. saves a spell slot in a lot of zones too, and you can move improved superior camo to page 50-) just not having to mem improved camo ever again makes it worthwhile for me

can't really comment on the other druid AAs since i haven't gotten them yet. i do know dire charm rocks though from seeing my enchanter friends use it.
