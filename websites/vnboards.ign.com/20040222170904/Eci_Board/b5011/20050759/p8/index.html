In my apartment building, we had a big problem with smoke traveling between people's apartments through shared airwells connecting the bathrooms. A number of people wanted to make the whole complex non-smoking. (It's a co-op, so we have that kind of power over our living situation.) Others objected to this idea, because they felt it was unfairly discriminating against smokers to deny them housing. The compromise solution was a simple rule:

"It is the responsibility of the smoker to ensure that their smoke does not bother anyone."

Normally, the condition can be satisfied by closing windows and keeping the bathroom door closed, or by finding an outdoor spot that is not close to any open windows if your housemates won't let you smoke inside. The rule is enforced by appealing to the manager, and ultimately by fines. I found it interesting that what is effectively the same law was proposed by a real legal body, even if it was shot down.
