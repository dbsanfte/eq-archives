Life in the mines.. Not for this Paladin of Brell. Adventure, riches and glory for Brell were all that mattered to him now.

Orphaned as a child, Durox grew up deep in the mines, laboring to pay his way. As a child, Durox often played nearby the Knights fortress. Wielding picks and a furnace door his imagination ran wild of fighting evil wizards and immense dragons.

Trying to impress the Paladin Guild Master, Durox donned a fresh shirt emblazed with the Brell crest and started down the long tunnel. The wind blew hard into the mouth of the Dwarven lair; a chill crept into his bones. Onward he marched as the sun rose on Norrath. Vast green fields flooded his vision for the first time as Durox emerged from the mines.

Here starts the journey of Durox, the Dwarven Paladin from Kaladim

Life on the outside was different then he expected, animals of many shapes and sizes roamed the across the plains. Durox had read many a story of the vile beasts that lived above the mines, but these paled in comparison. Bats were everywhere, flapping their wings in distrust of anyone who wandered too close.

Slaying bats seemed fruitless at first, but once Durox learned how to strip the wings off of their slain corpses, he was able to start collecting them. Durox had heard stories of magic casters using these items as spell components, and perhaps the local vendor could part with some copper for them as well.

Sitting down in a clearing to take a short rest, another Dwarf happened upon Durox. A short conversation came in passing, mostly regarding the weather in Butcherblock as of late. To his surprise, the other Dwarf handed Durox a rusty Mace. Thanks was given all around and Durox was off to the hunt again.

There, across the way, a dwarven skeleton loomed. Raising his newly acquired mace, Durox called on the power of Brell and rushed the monster. A vicious battle ensued and when the dust cleared, there stood Durox, the proud owner of an old rusty long sword.

Not long after, Durox happened upon a young Cleric practicing his arts and beating on some skeletons with a club. Seeing an opportunity to fulfill his vows, Durox struck up a conversation with the Cleric. After a bit, Durox handed off the Mace to the cleric, who would surely get more use out of it then he. And a friendship was born. The two traveled through the plains together for a while, clearing a righteous path through the mountain range.

A day or so passed and Durox began to miss home. Needing to restock his supplies and sell off his bag of loot, Durox started out for Kaladim. Upon reaching North Kaladim, Durox happened upon some older Dwarves and a Gnome dressed in robes. Being to young to take up a trade skill, Durox asked around if anyone could assist him in sharpening his rusty sword.

To his surprise, the magician summoned a mystical shield, handed it over and told Durox to follow him into town. When the small group arrived at the bank, the magician set about giving some various armor and weapons to the gathered group of veteran adventurers. Shocked, Durox was handed a fabled Minotaur Axe and a Scarab Breastplate from the magician and from one of the other battle gnarled veterans.

Cheers and praises to Brell were heard and the group was back off to the mountains. Using his new axe, Durox cleaved through his enemies and was quickly advancing his weapon techniques and growing in experience by the hour.

As the sun was setting on yet another day in Norrath, Durox made camp, bedded down and drifted off to sleep.
