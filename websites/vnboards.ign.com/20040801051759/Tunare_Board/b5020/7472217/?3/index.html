I've had a few people ask me for stuff, I think it's the natural reaction of most folks when they see somebody in all black or an advisor robe running by. "Hey! (S)He looks pretty cool, maybe (s)he has some old useless things (s)he wouldn't mind getting rid of..."

That sort of thing I don't mind. I've even randomly given out pp, some times an item or two (even made an almost full suit of silk) to some of these folks. There's nothing wrong with helping out, or with this sort of reaction to us folks newbies may percieve as "higher level". Even though I readily admit I'm still a little guy! lol

But, on the other hand, this other sort of begging for the simple sake of getting free stuff and taking advantage of people's generosity, is bothersome. =/

I have to say though that I haven't experienced people getting angry because I wouldn't/couldn't give them something. Usually, I think it's just the way you word your response. I mean, think about it, you're wearing all that cool gear - certainly have *something* extra ... right? I've always responded with, "The only things I have right now are the things I'm wearing, and of course I need those. Sorry ", and I've always got an "Okay, thx" in response. If I have something to sell, I always mention it along with the general price I'm looking for.

Sometimes they'll ask maybe for a spare coin or two, but I handle it the same way.

All in all, I've only had a few people at most who's taken advtange of my willingness to help out "smaller" folks. And the obvious beggers I always brush away with a quick "Nope, sorry." Kind of message.

Basically what I'm getting at, is, try not to approach the situation with the frame of mind that they're just moochers trying to get free stuff. Usually, a kind word or way to handle it takes care of the situation with alot less bad feelings, and much quicker, then assuming they're some rotten moocher.

Though I'm not saying YOU do, of course. I've seen it, though, and I just have to think.. What happend to this being a *game*?
