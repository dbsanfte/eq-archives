Don't worry about your rusty weapon or lack of armor. I had a halfling druid running around with a cracked staff until lvl 30, when it got embarrassing and was replaced.

As a druid, your best weapons are in your spellbook. Flame Lick (useful into the 20's) and your latest, greatest fire-based nuke will see you just fine until you get a damage-over-time (DOT) spell at level 14. You should also use your Wind spell just to get your Conjuration moving so you can cast that lvl 14 DOT. Get into the habit of using Snare, and always keep your buffs up if only to keep your casting skills improving. Use Camouflage a lot to get your Div up, too.

My druids have generally begun purchasing 'off the rack' leather armor around lvl 7. It's expensive, so it takes a while. You can always look for cloth armor and patchwork pieces at the merchants in town.

The implementation of the newbie armor quests in Misty included the introduction of little diseased rats that drop tails worth over a plat apiece - i think they're level 2 (the ones in Steamfont are). In fact, all the rats in the zone drop these tails (I think), so making money isn't as hard as it used to be. You should be able to have a basic set of leather armor by lvl 10 at the latest. This will give you all the AC you need to get to lvl 20, when your druid's meleeing days are over anyway.

I personally think the newbie armor quests are very good (gnome casters make out like bandits). The halfling set is obtainable, but expect it to take a long time to get all the required items. To get started, talk to the guy that stands next to the shady halfling near the druid guild in Rivervale. When I did the quests, a lot of other people were doing them too, and would shout when something dropped that they already had, so you won't necessarily have to kill to get every last item. The halfling armor gives you a nice little boost in Wis and other stats, is fairly light, and has decent AC (1 better than patchwork). And it's free.

But don't get too hung up on equipment. You'll have to fight toe-to-toe at early levels (use your Thistle Shield when you get it), but you should still learn how to hunt like a druid. After lvl 20 or so, the essence of being a druid is not getting hit. Learn what your spells do, what you can do with them, and how to use them efficiently, and you'll be fine.

Ok, if you are still level 1, and have only 4 silver, and no items or armor, then i can put my finger right on your problem:

You ain't going out of town and killing stuff for XP and loot. Seriously, you can get to level 2 by killing 4 or 5 level-1 bats. What you need to do is get off your lazy duff, run out of town past those guard fellows, right-click some mobs until you find one that cons either white or blue, and attacking and killing it with your free newbie weapon. Then you right-click its corpse and you can loot it. Some stuff you can get at level 1 is worth some good money for that level when sold to a merchant in town.

Once you have 5 or 6 gold pieces saved up, buy yourself a backpack, then you can hold more loot before selling it. I would recommend spending your first 2 or 3 platinum pieces on backpacks, then save up for a Sewing Kit. They sell Sewing Kits in every town. Some of them are Small (2 slots), some of them are Large (8 slots). The only difference for you at that level is the price, the 8-slot costs several times what the 2-slot one does. Then, kill Wolves, and save up the Ruined pelts. If you don't want to kill Wolves because you are Nature's Friend, then you can buy them from other less-scrupulous players for a few silver each. They'll be glad to sell them to you if you /auction for them, because they can only get 9 coppers for them at a merchant.

When you have a few Ruined Pelts, go back to the guy who sold you the Sewing Machine, and purchase some Sewing Patterns. They cost a few silver pieces each. Combine one Ruined Pelt with a Sewing Pattern in your Sewing Kit, and you will either fail to sew properly (losing the Pattern and Pelt) or you will successfully combine them and will have a piece of tattered leather armor that you can wear.

As far as weapons are concerned, I would not worry about that until I was level 8 or so (just use the Splintered Club that came with your character), and then I would simply save up 5 platinum pieces or so and go to the Bazaar on Luclin and purchase a Velium Scimitar or a Velium Mace, so I could hit Willowisps and Ghouls (they can only be hit by Magic weapons). You won't need a better weapon until you get into your teens at least. At that time, the loot will be better, and you will be wearing full Rawhide armor at least. By then you will know enough about being a lowbie Druid in EQ that you will know just what to shop for with the money you have, so I will leave your subsequent weapon/armor choices up to you.
