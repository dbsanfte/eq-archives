Date Posted: 12/15/04 10:54am Subject: RE: Most of the people who play EQ 2 are people who quit EQ 2-3 years ago - Date Edited: 12/15/04 10:55am (1 edits total) Edited By: elduderino1971
I tried EQ2 and WoW, both of which I had been quite looking forward to. I've loved playing EQ for the past year and a half and it was the only game that grabbed me and held onto me. I've played DAoC (3 months), AO (2 months), CoH (1 week), SWG (1 week), & EVE Online (1 week) but none of those really grabbed and held on. DAoC almost did and so did AO, but not quite.

EQ2 could be a decent game but for a few issues. 1) The system requirements will preclude a good gaming experience for many out there unless they choose to upgrade their machines. Under the minimum requirements the game is too laggy to enjoy. 2) The shared death xp penalty sucks really hard and made me not want to group. 3) The xp is so meager that I could play for 2-3 hours and only gain like 15% of 1 level at just level 12. That sucks. I don't want leveling to be too easy but if it's too slow the grind just kills all. 4) The con system is laughable. I don't want to know if a mob is okay to solo or if a group is needed. That dumbs the game down IMO. 4) Sitting in my room afk all night to sell my wares. I can't believe that dumarse system even made it into the game. 5) Only 4 char slots suck. 6) KSing of nodes is rampant and horrible. 7) To buff folks you have to be grouped with them. That means no random healing of noobs or other friendly types. I miss that.

As for WoW...that game rocks. I haven't had that much fun in a mmorpg in a long time. More than any other mmorpg I've found myself not even paying attention to the xp bar. Several times I'd look up in wonderment as I had leveled and barely noticed lol. It rocks when you can be so immersed that you aren't noticing leveling or any kind of grind. Things that aren't so great about WoW are that grouping is rare and often pointless IMO. Also, the average maturity level is probably lower than any other game I can think of. My ignore list is so long it's pathetic. There ARE mature gamers playing but the folks who often stand out in general chat are the obnoxious ones and they are everywhere in WoW.

EQ is still the king IMO. I still play EQ in addition to WoW, but dumped EQ2. I'd rather stick needles in my eyes than play another 5 minutes of EQ2. WoW is great if you have limited time to play. You can hop on and do a quest in like 1/2 hour and then log off and do your thang. You can't do that in EQ, but EQ is still awesome if you have 2+ hours to play.

Anyways, my $.02.
