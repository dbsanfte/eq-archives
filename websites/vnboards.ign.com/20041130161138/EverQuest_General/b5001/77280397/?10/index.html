Date Posted: 10/21 9:09pm Subject: RE: Are ytou going to switch to EQ2 when it goes live?
EQ2 is a freaking great game. I have one character at level 21 and a few more around 10ish.

With that said, I have not decided if i'll be playing retail or not. Since NDA is dropped now I could go on about stuff I like and dislike, but it's pointless because everything has been said elsewhere.

Basically, if you like EQ1 stick with it - you may not like EQ2. EQ2 feels a lot like what Turbine wanted AC2 to be - tons of quests, small groups, the ability to solo, etc.

EQ1 on the other hand is more for those that like to socialize, raid, and group.

While crossover is present they are designed for two different types of gamers. EQ1 will continue to be supported, and you can be sure SOE is gonna keep cranking out expansions.

One other thing to note - system requirements for EQ2 are extremely harsh. Even at minimum settings if you want it to run like EQ1 runs now (which is not saying much...) you'll want at least a 2.5ghz processer, 1 gig of ram, and a full DX9 card (Radeon 9600 or up basically). I've tested EQ2 out on machines with 512 megs, 1 gig, and 2 gigs of ram - and in my experience anything under 1 gig is not an option for EQ2. This is one of the major reasons why I am considering not messing with EQ2 for another year or so until computers get more powerful and I can play perfectly smooth.

Last but not least if you're one of the unlucky people still stuck on dialup, be ready for some 24 hour patches frequently if SOE is adds content. Voice acting really bloats the patch sizes it seems.
