She was a child, laughing in a carefree way that children are allowed, frolicking around the protective grove of trees known as Suredall Glade. Getting underfoot as usual.
Her favorite uncle, Hagar Sureshot lifts her high into the air and catches her as she squeals with delight.
"Ah, Lenore, child. What a breath of fresh air you are." He gives her a hug and sets her down. He sends her scurrying away and laughing, with a gentle swat to her behind.

"M'Lady, please come quickly."


She is thirteen now.
"Mistress, why have I no parents?"
Te'Anara looked at her blossoming student with love. Of all her apprentices, she favored this one most. She reminded her of her own grown daughter at that age.
"Lenore, sweetheart, tis time you knew."
Lenore sat at the Elder's feet. Lenore knew her parents were gone, but she hoped they would return to get her soon.
Te'Anara looked into wide violet eyes, eyes filled with questions. Yes, twas time she knew the truth.
"Your parents are dead, dearest. Your mother came to us when she was pregnant with you and told us what had happened. Your father was killed by a cyclops named Froon and she herself had barely managed to escape with her step-son, your half brother. She returned the boy to his mother in Qeynos and came here. She greived her loss bitterly, growing weaker each day. After she gave birth to you, she named you and held you until she died two hours later. I will show you where she is buried. Your mother loved you so much." She then motioned to one of the other apprentices and he came forward.
"She had this with her. I assume it to be her family crest." It was a gold medallion, etched with a woodland spirit, arms outstretched as if in a dance. "Her family name was Freespirit." Lenore looked at the medallion and turned it over and over in her hands. It was beautiful. Te'Anara then took the medallion and handed it back to the apprentice, and he left, returning shortly with a belt in his hands.
"We had this crafted for you, so you may carry it always with you." She handed the belt to Lenore and she placed it around her waist. The medallion sat in the center of the belt.
"Freespirit." She said softly. She would assume her name when the time was right. Right now, she needed to find her brother.

Blackness....

"Hmmm, maybe it is a little more serious after all." The pair looked at the fevered woman, thrashing and mumbling.
"We need to find the bottle from which she drank and destroy the contents so no one else will get sick. I hope she did not drink too much of it, or share it with anyone."

Gnolls everywhere.
She is 15 now, barely into the Second Ring of Karana, sent out into the world, given the task to aid in the elimination of the gnolls around her home.
In the heat of one battle, a voice asks if she needs any help. As the gnoll falls dead at her feet, she turns to face the source of the voice.
And loses her heart.
She recognizes him from her dreams.
"My name is Choco." He says to her. He is handsome. Golden hair, golden beard and moustache, and playful green eyes, even though one is covered by a patch she finds appealing.
"My name is Lenore. Care to hunt with me?"
As the couple hunted, she learned he was a Paladin of Karana. As the two grew closer daily, he confided to her he had dreamt of her.
"Destiny." They said in unison and looked at each other.
The couple became inseperable, and one was very seldom seen without the other. And a deep, everlasting love was forged.

She proudly stood beside him when he earned the right to assume his family name, and he was beside her when she earned hers. When the couple married in her home of Surefall Glade, surrounded by their friends, and her family of the glade, she took his name as her own.

Mists swirl...

She is in Qeynos.
And she is holding a child. A son.
Through the mists, the child grows and follows the path of his father and becomes a Paladin, and she is proud.
Now, in Freeport, a daughter is placed into her arms. Time passes, and the girl has become a young woman, a fine warrior. As time passes yet again, the woman is soulbound to an ogre, and Lenore is pleased.

Blackness...

Choco and Peacebyrd listen to the delirious mumblings of the pale woman in bed. All he could do was hold her hands, trying to give her comfort, and will her back to him.
At the entrance of the troll, Choco addresses him.
"Seems she has been poisoned. Seems some wine may be tainted, perhaps a bad vintage, not properly brewed. I do not know how much of it she has had, but we need to find the bottle and destroy it."
The troll nodded. He seemed to remember when she sat at his table she had one glass of wine, and after half the glass was gone, she set it aside, as if in disgust, and turned to water as her choice of drink.
Lenore moaned and thrashed. Peacebyrd stands beside her.
"She is still fevered." Worry etched her in delicate face. She starts to ramble again.
So hot.....

So hot..

A battle ensues in the dusty, barren wastes known as The Dreadlands. A large canine creature begins to turn the tides and she stumbles to the ground, weak. Unable to call upon the powers of nature to heal, unable to even flee for her life, the creature silences her. As she floats above her body, she sees her blood seeping into the dry ground, and the man beside her cries out in rage.
Then all is quiet. She floats through a serene field, greeting people, searching.
"Lenore," a gentle voice calls. She smiles and follows the sound. A beautiful Feir'dal greets her and motions her to follow.
"Mother." The two women embrace.
"Come, meet your father." She takes her hand and leads her to a tall tree. Beneath the tree is a tall human. Handsome, with dark hair and blue eyes and a neatly trimmed moustache.
"Ah, Lenore, you have grown so lovely. We have been watching you, guiding you, and Karana himself smiles favorably upon you and the man you call Husband." He looked at his daughter and smiled.
"You have her beautiful violet eyes." he said softly. Lenore blushes.
"You cannot stay, dearest. It is not your time. As we speak, someone attempts to bring your soul back to you. You have made us proud. May Karana bless you, dearest."

Then she is back in the Dreadlands, weak and dizzy from the ressurection. A serene face peers into hers and nods her head.
"She is fine now M'lord."
"How can I ever thank you?" The graceful elf rose and said nothing as she walked away.

Peacebyrd listened to the ramblings of Lenore and wondered of the life of this woman. Gathering from the latest hallucination, she met her deceased parents recently. Not many can say they have journied to the nether realms and returned.
She placed a hand against Lenore's head and noticed the fever was gone. Ahh, a good sign.

In her dream state, Lenore sees her parents once more.
"Am I dead again?"
The wood-elf smiles and shakes her head.
"No, dearest. Just very ill. I am only here for a short moment." She kisses Lenore on the forehead and then bids her farewell.
"Mother.." "I love you.."
"Father.." "Safe journies.."
"Choco.." "I am here."
"Ah, the fever breaks. She will return to us shortly. And I thought I told you to get some nourishment." She placed her hands on her hips, scolding him like a parent.
Choco looked sheepish.
"I am sorry. I cannot leave her side just yet. Besides, I have functioned on even less than this." He turned his face towards hers and took her hands to his lips.
"Please come back to me soon." he whispered.
She sighed and fell into a deep, restful slumber.
"Has anyone below found the bottle from which she drank? I don't want anyone else to fall ill should they drink from it."

Her eyes adjusting to the darkness, wincing painfully. Her body ached and her head hurt. She felt the familiar weight of her husband beside her in the bed, but was not sure how she got here. Last thing she remembered was walking up the stairs, then nothing after that, and that was yesterday. She moved slowly and snuggled beside her husband for some warmth and went back to sleep.

The shined brightly through the window when Lenore awakened again. She slowly sat up, and propped herself with pillows against the back of the bed. She closed her eyes, listening to the sounds of nature. This is how Choco and Peacebyrd found her.
"Ah, my patient awakens." The lovely elf said smiling. A puzzled look crossed Lenore's face.
"Patient?"
"Yes, my love. You have been very ill for the last 2 days." Two days?!?
"Two days?" But how? from what? she wondered.
"Yes, two days. Seems the wine you drank was tainted and made you very sick. It is very fortunate you had half a glass, for a full glass might have taken your life." Peacebyrd watched as shock registered on her face. Choco took Lenore's hands in his.
Oh, my love, if I had lost you..." he whispered. He gathered her into his arms and held her closely. Peacebyrd smiled and averted her eyes from the tender scene.
"I will go below and inform everyone you are well. We will see your smiling face soon enough. I will send up food as well, since the both of you look like you will need it."
Peacebyrd bowed gracefully and left the room.
Lenore looked at her husband, and he did look like he had not eaten in awhile, and he looked tired.
"You did not eat?" she scowled at him.
"How could I? Fear at losing you made me lose my appetite. But now you are back with me and all I need is rest, as should you."
She yawned. She did feel tired.
"I will rest." she said softly. This surprised Choco, as she loved to argue with him. She lay back down and closed her eyes. He then lay back down beside her, gathering her close, and closed his eyes as well.
