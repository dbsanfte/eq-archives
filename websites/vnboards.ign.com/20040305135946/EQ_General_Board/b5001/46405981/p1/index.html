 		Date Posted: 3/25/03 11:44am Subject: RE: EQ VS. DAOC anyone play both ?
I played 2 years of DAOC and 3 years of EQ and I'm basically done with both games. Here are my thoughts regarding them.

Both are good games and have their strengths and weaknesses. Despite what people say, both require a decent time investment to get anywhere. Neither are 'part time' endeavors.

Everquest is more of a social game, and really revolves around being a 'team' game at the end. Everquest's endgame is built around awesome epic encounters. The lack of PVP in EQ is a problem, but also cool in that the endgame revolves around quests and other things that are quite fun and not too repetitive. Also, there is no class balance issues, really. There are racewar and PVP servers on EQ, but they are not the norm.

Everquest is a time sink, and it takes a looong time to get things accomplished. Also, EQ suffers from 'camp' spots with important items being taken and other static spawns being tough to get to due to overcrowding.

DAOC is really a PVP game, with some moderate PvE that is half baked and 'loot/cash' related. If you want to understand how 'not' to make PVE, then go read the DAOC sites and do a search on Galloria. DAOC players only get access to 1/3 of the PVE content due to DAOC's structure. DAOC's world is small in comparison to Everquest, also.

The PVP in DAOC is in a designated area. This is great in preventing ganking. The prototype of fighting revolves around small 'gank' squads or large 'zerg' squads. PVP is built around gaining 'Realm Points' which enable you to 'buy' abilities, and to look at your stats on the Camelot Herald. The classes are decently balanced and the PVP is fun, if at times repetitive.

The Magic system in DAOC is VERY limited. Magic, in particular is not comparable to EQ. There are few spells and the classes basically get a small group of useable ones. In EQ you get hundreds of spells. In DAOC, basically you get about 15 per caster class, many of which are given to other classes.

The stealth system in DAOC is quite controversial, and really is a whole subdebate. Basically, stealth is total invisibility and many players have problems with how it is implemented. I'd do some research on the 'rogue' classes before deciding to play DAOC.

From a social aspect in DAOC you are limited to interacting/trading/chatting with 1/3 of the server which bothers me. Basically in a 1500 person server you can interact, meet, and talk with 500 of them only.

Basically, EQ and DAOC are similar in that the endgame is farming/fighting for similar things.

In EQ you gain new uber equipment which enables your character to become stronger and stronger. You fight tougher mobs, do quests for massive epic zones. Go to alternate planes of existence, etc, etc.

In DAOC you farm other players over and over in Emain Macha in a counterstrike type of situation getting realm points to buy abilitites that progress your character similar to EQ's equipment.

Everquest is more of a 'Dungeons & Dragons' environment. If you are looking for a psuedo Dungeons and Dragons game, then you will be MUCH happier in EQ.

DAOC really is more of a generic 'Medieval' environment set around a Viking/Celt/Brit War.
If you are looking for a simpler game with a pure medieval 'historical' feel then DAOC is for you.
