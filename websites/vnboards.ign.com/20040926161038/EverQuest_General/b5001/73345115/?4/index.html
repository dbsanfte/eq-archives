Imagine that you are rolling dice to determine what your damage is on each swing. The number of sides the die has is determined by your maximum damage per swing, so whenever you roll it could be anywhere from no damage to maximum damage. Now imagine that the dice rolling is "weighted," that is, rolling under a certain number gives you a different die to roll, with half the sides blank and the other sides with numbers up to that threshold number.

That would be something like what a combat skill roll is like in EQ. If you roll badly, you either do a very small amount of damage, or, more likely, you "miss the swing." If you roll well, you do either full or nearly full damage. If you roll medium, you do whatever medium damage you rolled. The numbers are further weighted by your weapon skill, meaning you will miss most swings if you have a skill of 1, and you will hit most swings on a creature of equal or lesser level than you if you have a weapon skill of 200.

It gets complicated figuring out what affects your melee damage output, but all you really need to know is that your damage per swing can be anywhere from zero (a miss) to whatever your maximum possible damage with that weapon is, taking into consideration your level, your target's level, your offensive skill, your target's defensive skill, your weapon skill with that weapon type, the weapon's damage, the weapon's delay, and any factors that mitigate or increase damage such as haste (essentially shortens weapon delays) and +Attack equipment such as Vengeance items. The randomness is due to a random number generator "rolling a die" and then applying the result to a formula utilizing your statistics/equipment and your target's statistics/equipment.

By the way, ways you can raise your damage output:
Raise your Offensive skill
Raise your weapon skill with the weapon type you use
Equip a weapon with a higher damage or a lower delay or both
Have Haste spell buffs cast on you, and/or wear items that raise your attack rating through worn Haste, Vengeance, and similar effects
Fight monsters that have lower defenses against melee attacks
Level up and get some Alternate Abilities that raise your damage output

Other things that affect damage output include melee Disciplines, and weapons that process damage spells on the target.
