Hello everyone.

I just have a few questions for the masses here:

1. Is the environment newbie friendly?

What I mean is, my girlfriend and I would probably be starting from scratch. I want to know if being a newbie is still the nightmare it was a year or two ago. I mean, have there been any improvements, exp raises, early loot changes, sidekick-like systems with higher level players, etc that have been implemented.

2. Player Auctions?

6. Economy?

What's the world like now, pricewise? Does everyone have an abundance of the same loot? Is obtaining an item far more of a pain in the ass than buying it for 5% the effort? I would like to know if purchasing loot offline is a bad idea given the game state.

7. Low level population?

Is it worth starting over at level 1. I mean, is the low level game so void of population that I'll find myself begging to find larger groups?

That's all I can think of at the moment, thank you for your time.

1. Yes and no. Exp has been raised in certain "Hot zones". Lowlevel grouping is harder in the 'normal' world, but easier in LDoN. Loot = same. Sidekick-like = no. Highlevel buffs (Like KEI and Virtue) have actually been nerfed so they cant land on <L45, but there are plenty of buffsellers for lower versions of these.

2. Depends what kind of player you are. Constant rerolling and end-game-raiding don't go well hand in hand. I'd suggest figuring out what you want from the game: Casual fun, or end-game-raiding. If you are looking for the first, don't buy a player. If you want the second, go ahead.

3. EQ as a game is worth it, but if you know you are going to switch anyway...

4. I think you are quite wrong on this one actually. It used to be that the high-end game was highly saturated, but not so any more. The sheer amount of raid content has increased alot, and along with instancing I would not call raiding saturated. Waiting lists/rotations are more or less a thing of the past (and/or Stormhammer).

5. No.

6. Never bought anything offline so no real clue on it. Prices are a bit inflated but not completely out of control. The new tribute system and the casino has helped sink alot of the old items and PP out of the system.

7. See 1. At level 1, you should be soloing. Low-mid levels are probably the hardest to find groups. But if you choose the right combo, 2 ppl can easily make it to at least 60 (and probably 65) by themselves. I'm guessing that the 2 newest servers: Stromm and Morden Rasp are the best for starting fresh.

My wife and I recently re-started everquest on Bristlebane, and did not chose a combo that works well together (SK and WIZ) We hit 50 in three months of decent play. We did get one PLing session from a friend's Druid that raised us from 24 to 29.

Other than that it was LDoNs and soloing almost exclusivly. I think I had one group in Maru Seru one night... My wife grouped a lot in Dulak with some friends she made.

I would have to say, play on an established server as you will have access to better twink gear. Start up a merchant almost immediatly, and sell everything that is flagged as usable in tradeskills. Use that money to buy twink gear. (And do the newbie quest armor, it's actually pretty decent for most classes).

If you have been gone for a long time, FOCUS ITEMS ARE YOUR FRIEND!!!
