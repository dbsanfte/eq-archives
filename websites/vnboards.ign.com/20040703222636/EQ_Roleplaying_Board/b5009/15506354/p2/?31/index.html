 		Date Posted: 9/16/01 11:19pm Subject: *Dragging steps are heard leading to the door of the Inn*
Heavy footsteps can be heard making their way wearily up the roadway towards the Inn. Those with acute Elven hearing, or magical aid, might detect the weariness in the tread.

The footfalls step slowly up the few wooden steps that lead to the entry to the Inn and finally reach the doorway.

A slight pause and the door swings gently open and a figure enters. Stooped with fatigue and showing the tell tale signs of hard fought, and won, struggles the figure enters and glances slowly around the common room.

The eyes scan those present without hostility but with deep weariness, showing some faint resonance of the shadow through which he has passed.

The figure is clad in plate armour, shining brightly even in the gloom of the common room. Though obviously having seen much service the armour is well repaired and kept, no scratches mar its surface, no rust digs its cold talons into half forgotten blemishes. It glows a gentle blue in the silence, not the electric blue of the Bard, but a softer, more stately blue.

A measure of the haunted aspect fades from the figures eyes as his gaze falls upon the shadows where Gale sits and a wearied grin crosses his face unon seeing his old friend Cyst.

"Ah my friends, my very old friends, it is so good to return once again. Long it has been since I sat in these halls and spoke of the world with thee. For those that know me not I am Sir Lothannon Spiritlight, Knight of the Mother. I bid thee welcome to this haven in the wilderness, or perchance I overstep mine place, an I have not been here in long, perchance 'tis thou whom ought to welcome me."

The knight makes a gently dismissive gesture at the notion, disregarding any fault or protests.

"But tis of no matter. I must rest for the road and the trials to return here hath been long and wearying indeed. Tis fair beyond words to see thee again friend Gale, friend Cyst. And an honour to see new faces in the inn.

The knight bows to the silent figure of the Enchanter, Zimmerathe and to the Lady Dellirya.

"Honoured to meet thee Lady, and Brother. I shall return once the dust and travails of the journey hath faded a little. Till then, old friends and new."

The Knight bows once again and steps, a little less wearily, to the stairs and proceeds up to his old room.

The night suddenly fades into the more natural Infravision she had been born with and a mild curse tumbles from her Elven lips. With a slow shake of the head, she pauses in her walk to begin casting. Pinpoints of golden light drift about her form, gathering before her before slowly fading. Once again, her eyes perceive the darkness with the acuracy of the Drow.

Despite the nature of the vision spell (having come, originally, from Neriak -or so it was beleived), the High Elf prefered to travel under it's power. Though the Commonlands were quite safe for her, there was always the risk of running into the vile and corrupted Sargeant Slate. A night-time duel with (though his human vision was no match for even her natural Infravision) him would be a frightfull event, no doubt leaving her broken and bleeding upon the plain.

It was widely whispered by those that chose to fight the evil of the Freeport Militia that Slate had dealings with evil gods, giving him the ability to resist even a Wizard's spells. While Silariel had never gone toe to toe with the infamous Slate, her closest friend, the Champion of Marr Sethix Bladesong, had done so...To devistating ends.

Silariel began moving again, the faint glow of the Tavern's windows visible to her in the distance. It had been quite a time since her last visit to the Tavern. A great many battles, a great many sites seen, many spells learned and practiced. Who would have believed that Luclin would be discovered and that, she, a lowly Wizard of Tunare would find the chance to travel there. What sights she had seen that day! What spells she had found. And the beauty of the cat-like people, those who named themselves the Vah Shir...

It had been an eventfull few months, a time of growth and learning. It would be good to relax once more at the tables, among the kind-hearted people of the Commonlands. Not to worry about enemies lurking at every turn, prepared to pounce and devour the unwary traveler...She didn't even have her Invisibility spell memorized, for nothing could touch her in this, the Commonlands...
