I left DAoC for the reasons a lot of other people left: 1) to be effective, most people have to follow a "cookie cutter" skill advancement pattern with their characters; 2) PvP battles are not determined by strategy, but rather by levels - 4 level 10s can't take a level 20 REGARDLESS of what strategy they use as long as the level 20 is remotely smart; 3) Sieges are a big focus of the game, and in most cases, are extremely boring - walking a few steps forward, having 60+ people attack ONE NPC,seeing most of them die, finally killing the NPC, taking a few more steps forward, and then repeating FOR HOURS is just dumb; 4) the PvE aspect is almost non-existant except at low levels; 5) Many quests are messed up and broken (or they were when I played); 6)the DAoC developers (like most others) made promises of new skills/commands to be added within a few weeks of release - most of those commands and skills never appeared, including the trade skills.

DAoC has some good points, and I know of people that have made the switch permanently. I didn't. I definately recommend trying it - I found the early levels to be a lot of fun. The later game, however, just wasn't to my liking.

Thanks alot for all the answers--I'll definitely give it a shot, thinking about playing a druid; hope there aren't a thousand of'em running around =) I know the graphics of EQ will look a little dated after DAoC, but then again I've been playing a text-based MUSH for seven years now...I'll be fine as long as the community's strong =)

Dunnermun:
As for your question, I'll beg to differ with the previous poster and advise you yo wait for another 3 months or so before you try it. Almost all of my friends have left the game, some rerolled but most quit for sure. The game is simply awesome for the first month but then it turns so sour it's not even funny---and I was playing an archer class in the "perceived" powerhouse realm, Midgard.

1. RvR is either a small scale level-based Quake-style shootout or a largescale, buggy, laggy encounters that leaves you in tears out of frustration. That RvR was the centerpiece of this game is utter marketing BS, it was obviously just an afterthought---3 months after the release, -everything- about it is buggy, noone knows if the relics work, realmpoint awards donot work, and even if they do they just give bonus skillpoints, hardly an incentive to do RvR.

2. PvE is the most repetitive, boring XP threadmill you'll see anywhere; there are a handful of mob skins that behave the same and which you'll kill from level 1 to level 50 over and over again. 1-40 goes relatively fast (if you're not a causual gamer possible in less than 2 months--if you know what you're doing) after that it's maddeningly slow.

3. Game requires absloutely no strategy/skill to play. Level system is so hard-coded that a level 40 guy can kill sixty or more level 20 crowd without a scratch.

4. The game world is tiny and I can't express this enough. Within two-three weeks you see all there is to see. Midgard is OK, Muspelheim is interesting etc--Albion less so in my opinion, and I feel for the Hibernians as the whole tiny landmass is one green field. The dungeons are smaller than you can imagine, randomly generated mostly, often packed with people, and in some cases copy/pasted across the realms!

5. One of my pet peeves: everybody talks in /group or /sends and what you get is a realm of mute telepaths, no real sense of community.

6. Horrible class/realm balance. Certain classes are just useless and unwanted in both PvE and RvR. Hundreds of documented bugs regarding "working" classes' skills. Just to give an example, certain Champion debuffs (a "working" class) actually buff the mobs. This has been proven time and again and through the Champ TL Mythic made it known that they can't fix that problem anytime soon :P It's that bad...


So do yourself a favor and don't buy the game now. Take a look at the Vault dev boards every now and then--if you still see hundreds of people begging Mythic to do something about any of the issues I've raised above, not to mention class/realm balances, just stay away from DAoC for some more

I can guarantee you that you'd love the game for the first month or so, but the subsequent frustration it will cause is just not worth it.
