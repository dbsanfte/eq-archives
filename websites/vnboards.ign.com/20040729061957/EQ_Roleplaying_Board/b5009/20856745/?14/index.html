Sethix pushes the well used Tavern door open and stops, a bit confused. He looks about, seeing naught but empty tables and a cold hearth. With a frown (hidden by the silvery steel of his Skull Shaped helm), he moves fully into the common room. Heavy Cobalt boots thump loudly upon the worn planks of the old Tavern.

With a slight shake of his head, he pulls the blackest of black plate gauntlets from his hands, revaling a web of battle scars adorning both calused hands. The plated gloves thump upon the nearest table, followed by a pair of beautifully crafed blades, both bearing the ancient symbol of Sebilis. One with a hooked and barbed blade of some form of crystalline substance, the other a longer blade with a flare at the end and a spiked cross guard.

The plates of his armor squeek slightly as he seats himself wearily. His helm is placed beside his gauntlets and blades, stark white hair falls about his shoulders. His face no longer bears the black and gold eye patch, the legacy of his childhood. It is now unbroken by scar, his eyes seem to have shifted to a cold blue, their once gray fluidity replaced by a glacial blue.

Those blue orbs scan the room, sadness touching them as his hopes of tales and song dashed by the abandoned Tavern. The cold hearth sends depression flowing into his heart.

"Ah, but what was once full of joy and peace, is now as empty as the frozen plains of far off Velious." he whispers, remembering his last visit to this place. The encounter with the kind-hearted troll, his knee-jerk reaction to Cyst and the hatred that had filled his voice when speaking to him. Again regret filled his soul, so much had changed. So much...

Sethix looks up in surprise at the sound of foot falls and the heavy thump of a large being seating himself. He turns about quickly, squinting in the gloom.

Warrior instincts flare immediately and his hands dart to the blades resting upon the planks of the table. He grunts slightly at the sound of Cyst's calm voice. He looks closer and recognizes the Troll for who he is, from days long passed.

"Hail, Cyst-", he is about to say more when a voice calls out from the door. Once again, the Warrior instinct takes over and he whirls about to face the door, feeling as if he is a ball being thrown back and forth.

"Hail, unseen!" He calls towards the door in a flat voice. "You are safe here, traveler. As well you do not cause trouble!"

Sethix lowers his blades, looking from one silouette to the other.

Cyst's puzzled look nearly sent Jolene into a fit of the giggles. While Cyst was not overly ugly, but not many trolls could be considered good looking either, his look of puzzlement looked more like a sneer.
Sethix looked equally puzzled.
"Treeish," she began, "is what a lot of druids do when they either become so old and instead of dying like normal folk, they become trees, become one with nature, change as the seasons change, and slowly fade away that way. Or, like in my mother's case, to recharge." she said sadly. Jolene lowered her eyes and sighed. It pained her that her beautiful mother had to go treeish to begin with. She had always been faithful to her God, Karana, and tearfully told Jolene she had to become one with nature again.
"My mother told me she seemed to have lost her powers, as they did not seem nearly as strong as previous. And the only way to restore those powers was to commune with nature in the form of a tree. I never knew that, and now, when I see a treant, it makes me wonder. I wonder if that treant is a newly treeish druid, communing with nature, or slowly fading away, becoming lost from everything physical, forgetting all they used to be, and living like a tree the remainder of their lives. And we all know that trees can live far longer than us mere mortal types." she sighed again.
"And now, when I see people slaughtering those same treants, it makes me angry that they don't realize that they might be murdering someone they might have known. Or possibly, a loved one." she lowered her face once more, and peered into the contents of a mug of ale she had grabbed on her way back to her table.
"My mother also told me, that when a treant dies from violent causes, a pain fills her body, and all druids feel that pain as well. She said it is very excruciating. Like a massive rip in the balance of nature, and not only druids feel this. Their sisters and brothers, the Rangers, do too. Makes me think twice when I see a treant now. I never gave them much thought. Never had the notion to hunt them like common vermin."
Her eyes met her companions, and she could see they never had given this much thought either. And whether either of these two had hunted treants in the past, she didn't want to know, or at least admit to her they did. It wouldn't matter now anyway. What they did in the past was their own business.
"She told me that when the time was right, she would come back to the world of mortals."
