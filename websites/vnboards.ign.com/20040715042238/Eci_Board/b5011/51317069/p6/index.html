If someone puts an item up for sale for 50K, and nobody buys it, IT ISN'T WORTH 50K. If someone buys it at 50K, IT IS WORTH 50K. Simply because *you* won't buy it at 50K doesn't mean it isn't worth that. It simply means it is not worth that *TO YOU*.

The fact is, many of the 'important' high end spells frequently went for 50-80K, because the demand for those spells were high. To me, they were worth every penny. To me, they helped us push forward faster/easier/better than we would have had we not had them. To those who didn't buy them, they were not worth that much.

It really is very simple. In a free market, supply and demand dictate price. If demand is high (people want the spell), supply will go down and price will go up. If demand is low, supply will go up and price will go down.

You can say people are 'stupid', 'crazy', whatever for buying spells at whatever price, or can call them 'idiots' for pricing them so high, but the market dictates the price. You can throw your own bitter opinion into the mix, but the fact is, it means absolutely nothing.
