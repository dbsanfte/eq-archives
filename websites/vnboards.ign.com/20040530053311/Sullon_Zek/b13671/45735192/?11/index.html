I quit EQ's Sullon Zek for DAOC back in October 2001. Now I play both depending on my mood.

EQ is ALL about gear. The gameplay itself is all about collecting gear. You gotta have the best and you gotta commit an awful lot of time to get the best.

DAOC isn't like that at all. The best possible gear you can get is player crafted. You can get good stuff off of mobs, but it's never as good as player made. The game also supports pvp more and is very team oriented.

PvP is also 100% totally different. DAOC is designed with PvP teams in mind. It has castles to take and defend, and relics to to capture and defend (kinda like CTF), and siege engines like rams and catapults. It's also almost never 1on1 pvp; it's usually large teams clashing from 1group vs 1group to 100 on each side (very laggy).

EQ is often 1on1 or sometimes group on group. But there's no goals and the game really doesn't support pvp. Like I said it's too centered on gear.

EQ is a game of collect the phat lewts so you can go collect more phat lewts.

The two games are in no way similar. If you like big varied dungeons with lots of phat lewts and wonderfully varied and imaginative zones don't play DAOC! If you like being able to compete in pvp with a good team and not have to worry about uber gear and an enormous time commitment then don't play EQ!

EQ PvP is very different than DAoC. On SZ, you are immune to PvP until lvl 6. After that, you are fair game anywhere at any time by anyone not on your "team". Check out http://eq.crgaming.com/ and click on the "Sullon Zek Teams" link under the "Information" header for SZ team info.

EQ PvP is more about zone control and bragging rights. There are no contested targets designed specifically for PvP or designated PvP areas as there are in DAoC (with the exception of Mordred). With the exception of a couple of non-pvp zones, PvP can happen anywhere at anytime for any reason (or no reason at all) in EQ.

At higher lvls in EQ there are zones with high end content that are often contested for the right to raid the zone. Certain quest mobs and other random/rare spawns that drop pHAt LeWTz are also often fought over. This isn't controlled in EQ like it is in darkness falls, so battles can rage on for long periods of time and "griefing" can be a problem (such as one or 2 ppl try to ruin a raid for a large guild by training everyone while engaged with a boss). Corpse camping and XP loss discourage griefing, however.

DAoC PvP is a bit more of an organized event, where as EQ it's more random and dynamic. Some say there is no "point" to EQ PvP, but I disagree. Fighting over a fort with nothing in it and only gaining a small bonus or access to one mediocre dungeon from acquiring it isn't, to me, as much of a reason to PvP as fighting over a quest mob or a huge bad ass EQ dungeon.

Another thing I like about EQ PvP is the smaller encounters. In DAoC, you have to play certain class types to find one on one encounters, and group on group encounters can be sparse at times. The usual DAoC PvP encounter is usually a large-scale siege of some sort with lot of toons involved. These can be interesting, but the number of ppl usually makes for poor computer performance for all but those with the best systems, and having so many ppl involved at once makes things chaotic.

Small scale encounters are the norm in EQ, with large scale encounters a bit more rare until the highest lvls. I find smaller encounters a lot more enjoyable. Lag is usually manageable, and you can focus on what class your target is and develop strategies for those specific classes.

Also, it is a lot easier to kill someone in DAoC than it is in EQ (at higher lvls). This can be frustrating at times in EQ, but overall it is more challenging and rewarding, and the fights generally last a lot longer in EQ than in DAoC (more so at higher lvls). DAoC fights are over too fast to be fun for anyone except the stealth capable DAoC PvP classes, as they benefit from usually having the element of surprise, which usually dictates the victor.

EQ is also has far more depth and content than DAoC. There are a lot more PvP strategies and techniques, and no hand-holding features like /face and /stick. You have to learn to manuever and target in EQ, and in addition to gear and lvls, the ability to target and manuever quickly and effeciently is usually what separates the good PvPer from the bad.

In summary, I feel that EQ is a more difficult PvP game than DAoC, but is more rewarding as a result. EQ is a FAR more intersting PvE game than DAoC, so IMO they don't really compare. Pre SZ DAoC may have been the better PvP game, but SZ is, IMO, probably the best PvP MMORPG available.

On SZ, the "good" team is lower pop and generally the more challenging team. Team "nuetral" is a bit stronger, while team "evil" is probably the strongest team. Most would strongly urge new comers to start on the good or nuet teams for balance reasons.
