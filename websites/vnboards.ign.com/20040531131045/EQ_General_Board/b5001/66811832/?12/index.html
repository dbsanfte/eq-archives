That's the basics, but there are actually several ways to kite and everyone has their own favorite strategy.

You'll find that kiting one mob around and nuking it is pretty inefficient. Actually kiting one mob around using any spell is pretty inefficient since you aren't medding while it's dying. Until lvl 34, when you get your first targetable ae nuke that isn't a rain spell, it's best to root/rot or group I'd say.

Anyway, the two (three if you want to be exact) major forms of kiting used by druids are quad kiting and charm kiting (two main ways to do this).

Quad kiting - Find a good wide open area with several of the same type of mob. You'll find that if you try with different types of mobs they run at different speeds at times and that ruins the quads. Anyway, Spirit of wolf yourself, then snare 4 mobs and group them up. Group them by running around them in circles until their pathing is all the same and bunched up together. Then, using the previously mentioned targetable ae nuke, nuke them silly. When you first start doing this you'll probably have to med kite a bit until you get a bigger mana pool.

Charm kiting

First - The way I usually do it is to pick an area with plenty of animal type mobs and snare one and pull back to where you plan on doing your fighting. Charm it (before it gets to you obviously) and order it to guard here and run out and snare another mob. I prefer to fight other animal type mobs for this since a LOT can go wrong while charming. Then, after pulling the mob back to the charmed pet I root it and send in pet. The charmed pet invariably wins the first few fights with no help (if charm sticks that long) due to our pet haste/buff. Always remember to keep your pet snared, it just makes your life easier.

Second - Agro charm kiting. Follow the same procedure basically as the first type of charm kiting, but don't snare your pet. Order your pet not to taunt and then go snare another mob, on the way back also cast flame lick on it to really tick it off. Sick your charmed pet on it then run around while the mob chases you and your charmed pet tears up what you are fighting from behind. Cast flame lick and dots as needed to keep the mob chasing you.

As long as you're new, I'll share some hard-earned tips with ya...

1) Get (and use) the lvl 1 spell Flame Lick. It requires that you carry a Firebeetle Eye. Np if your Druid is a Halfling or Qeynos human, but Wood Elves can get them by PoKing to Rivervale (Misty Thicket) and whacking a few bugs. It's great for pulling, has a slight AC debuff, and is much more mana-efficient than your puny little nuke until lvl 9.

2) At 9, get (and use) Shield of Thistles. You cast it on yourself, and anything that hits you takes a few points of damage. My druids usually hang out around Crushbone until level 17. SoT is great for killing orcs, inflicts a lot of damage on the mobs hitting you, and is more mana-efficient than nuking. At lvl 14, Stinging Swarm only makes the legionnaires go down that much quicker. Put on your SoT, pull an orc, and melee away as it hits you. This works until level 18 or so, when your meleeing days will be pretty much over.

3) Don't neglect spells that are NOT of the same skill-type you depend on. Most of your nukes are Evocation, most DoTs are Conjuration. It's frustrating to get a new super spell and burn up half your mana trying to get it off. So... From lvl whatever to lvl 14, use Whirling Wind in your "combat rotation" - I cast it once on each mob. And after you get Stinging Swarm, throw a Combust on each mob to keep your Evocation moving a bit. It also doesn't hurt to overbuff to keep your Abjuration going, and to Camo yourself now and again for Divination.

I hope this helps. Druids are pretty easy to play when you get the hang of things, and they level fast. I recently had a druid make level 50 with 18 days played, compared to my first guy who is (still) 56 with 80 days played - but he started when I knew absolutely nothing about the game at all.

Good luck, and happy hunting.

Another way is SoW yourself, snare and dot the mob. Can't do this till you get your first serious dot, stinging swarm. Your mana bumps up once every tick (every 6 seconds). You want to be sitting when this happens. So, run ahead and sit. Watch the mana bar for the increase, then get up and run ahead again, but don't get too far ahead or your mob will take off. When you figure out the timing and get the rythm it's pretty easy, but you gotta pay attention. It takes awhile but you can solo some fairly high level mobs this way. Snare and dots are hard to resist.

One method i have used is the root dot method, not quite kiting but very effective. This can be used easily for 2 mobs at the same time.

root mob1
cast snare
cast stinging swarm dot line
cast fire based dot line

root mob2
case snare
cast ststinging swarm dot line
cast fire based dot line

root mob1
cast stinging swarm dot line
cast fire based dot line
cast creeping crud

root mob2
cast stinging swarm dot line
cast fire based dot line
cast creeping crud

done right you will be refreshing each spell just before it fails. If you get a fizzle, interrupt or resist go onto the next spell. The root spell is the only spell you have to recast if it is resisted and then miss out the next spell in the sequence.

There are various items out there that can help, if you worship tunare there is a quest for a neclace that casts root. At later levels you can use spiritist armor and cast stining swarm and snare (Only two I managed to get) With those three items I was using about a bub of mana per mob. so not a bad return on energy.

Fire dots are not worth the resist rate, imho.
It is more better to root-n-rot now that the root does not kill the snare.

1)Snare mob
2)Root mob (my 51 druid still used the first one...45 secs I believe, but tiny mana use)
3)Dot with magic dot.
4)Move away and Sit to get mana back. Refresh root if it breaks (mob is snared so it moves slow).
5)ReDoT when it wears off.
6)After two minutes refresh snare...this wearing off can be bad with big hitters.
7)repeat til mob is dead.

The sitting while mob is rooted gets you mana back so you can go right to the next one.
