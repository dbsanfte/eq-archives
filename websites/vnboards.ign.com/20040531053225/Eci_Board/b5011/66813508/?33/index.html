BRILLIANT~

I Firmly believe in this. Although, my tipping % may start higher than most others (up to 50% in restaurants for excellent service), I spent years in the restaurants industry and I KNOW what they are supposed to do. So everytime a waitress misses those minimum requirements, a few of the potential dollars get to stay in my wallet.

I NEVER stiff a tip. That may be misconstrued as you are a cheapskate or forgot. If the service is bad enough that I don't feel I should tip at all, I leave a penny face down on the table .. No, I didn't forget b*tch, you weren't worth it. If the server is rude/offensive, or hinders my ability to enjoy a meal (like not taking my order for half an hour etc) I will end up chatting with the manager.

I may seem like an extremist but if the service is excellent you bank on my table. A 50% tip on $150 meal is not uncommon from me if the server is on the ball, very friendly, and knowledgeable/helpful.

Other than full service restaurants, tipping varies. There are tipping guides out there but here are a few of my guarenteed amounts w/ basic service:

Full Service Restaurant - Minimum 20% for ok service
Buffet Type Restaurant - 10%
Delivery - 20-25% minimum
Bellhop - $5-$20 depending on how many bags he has to carry =P
Cab driver - 15-20% depending on how quickly they get me where I need to go and how clean the car is
Masseuse - 20% +
Hairstylist - 25% +
Manicurist - 15% +


I firmly believe if you can't afford to tip for good service you can't afford the service/restaurant at all and should do it yourself instead of cheating someone who is busting their @$$.
