Another thing to consider is asking people in the game for help. EQ is a social game, afterall. You'll find a lot of idiots that won't be much help but you'll also find some really helpful people. Nobody appreciates beggars (and not saying you are) but most EQers will offer help to a genuine new person.

Also keep in mind that MMORPGs are generally complex as far as games go - there's a lot to learn about playing the game and even more to know about the content. You have to learn a little at a time. I wouldn't expect you to learn the basics of combat, crafting, player economy, etc all at the same time. You probaby want to get the hang of combat and the interface first.

I pretty much always recommend that people make a pure melee type (warrior or monk in EQ) when first playing a MMORPG - as they're usually the simpler classes - this lets you get a feel for how combat works, how the interface works, and get a taste of the environment. If you have a lot of time to experiment you might then try a caster/healer for a while to get used to the magic system. Once you've adjusted and experimented a bit you'll be better prepared to get going with the class you truly want to fully develop.

A druid would also be a good class for a total new person, as they're a little more durable than your average caster/healer at low levels, can melee a little at low levels, and get a wide variety of spells even at low levels which would introduce you to the differenty types of and uses of magic.
