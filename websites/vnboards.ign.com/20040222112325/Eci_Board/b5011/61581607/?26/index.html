Pet agro is indeed transferred to the pet's owner under certain circumstances. If the mob is a caster and you move out of range it will HAVE to follow you when you're off pulling. Ever notice how caster NPC's sometimes melee with one character and cast on another?

Also, some mobs Rampage. And Rampage is an entirely separate agro table. Generally the first person to agro the mob is at the top of the Rampage list, and you don't get bumped down the list by other characters with more agro-- once you're at the top you stay there until you or the mob dies. Even zoning or camping out won't clear you from a mob's Rampage list.

Oh, and do you DoT the mob at all? Does anything you cast have a DoT-like effect? That could be bringing up your agro a bit. Even self-buffing will generate a little agro during a fight.

Warrior Taunt now uses Endurance. Our warrior was running out of Endurance the other night from taunting. I think they need to reduce the amount of Endurance used by Taunt because the spells Knight classes use to maintain agro don't use up that much mana so they still have a significant advantage there.

sylas

 		Date Posted: 12/26/03 9:53am Subject: RE: Holding aggro, new rules?
pets have always behaved that way.

your pet is a mob, mobs have aggro lists. your pet adds mobs to his aggro list by either you telling it to attack something, or by something attacking it or you. mobs work there way up your pets aggro list in the same manner that players work their way up a mobs aggro list. If you send your pet against multiple targets, you will notice that he sometimes switches between the two due to one generating more aggro than the other. generally, mobs of the same class/lvl do about the same amount of damage so your pet won't switch often, but they do switch. things like low health, or "frenzy" aggro also affect your pet just like it does mobs.


Now in the situation you just gave, you sic'd your pet against a mob while another mob began attacking you. The enchanter mez'd the one on you and your pet gave the "I am unable to attack %t, master" message. that only means that that mob was removed from your pets aggro list. Your pet does not need to be fighting a mez'd mob in order for you to receive that message, you receive that message every single time a mob on your pets aggro list is mezzed, whether your pet is fighting it or not.

Also in case you don't know, /pet back off wipes your pets aggro list, its a pretty helpful tool to use even when your pet is currently in combat. using it wisely will allow you to set the order of which your pet attacks adds.

Ark: "If all PCs go out of melee range and a pet in melee range has more aggro, the monster stays on the pet. "

This is not always true since "The Great Charm Nerf". If there are more than 3 on the NPC's hatelist, it will never choose to attack a charmed pet. It will run to which ever of the PC's who have highest aggro. This was done to prevent charm pet tanking for uber and semi-uber groups/raids.

Secondly (also since TGCN), your charmed pet *does* add significant aggro to the charmer. It is popularly believed to transfer around 50%, but it is just an estimate. In a normal group, a situation like you described should almost never happen, but with a bit of bad luck and a badly equiped warrior, it definately could.

Yes, I'm talking about charm pet aggro only. This was one of the changes during the mentioned "Great Charm Nerf". From what I know and my own experience with normal pet classes, I agree that non-charmed pets tranfer 0 or very little aggro to the owner.

Charmed pets work quite differently though, in many ways. Before TGCN, you could stick a hasted charmed pet on a mob, have the warrior taunt 1 sec later and if successfull, the warrior would basically have aggro-locked the mob. Example (numbers made up):
- Inc
- Aggrolist after 1 sec:
Charmed pet - 100 pt
Player1 (Warrior) - 30
Player2 (something) - 25
Player3 (Charmer) - 1 (assuming he did nothing other than send in the pet)
- Warrior presses successful taunt:
Player1 (Warrior) - 101
Charmed pet - 100 pt
Player2 (something) - 25
Player3 (Charmer) - 1
- Viola aggrolock. And since the charmed pet never gets aggro as long as a player is in melee range, there is no initial tanking problems. If you played with a warrior who knew this and how taunt works (specifically that the charmed pet is included when determining what aggrolevel a successful taunt puts you on), you never had problems with warriors holding aggro. I grouped with some very happy wizards when they found this out.

Now (post TGCN), the charmer gets some of the charmed pet's aggro, so while the above still works, it is not nearly as effective as it used to be. But come to think of it, the 50% transfer must be a bit too high estimated. Either it is a lower %, or a constant/sec. Otherwise the charmer would always steal aggro with powerful pets.

If you charmed alot (like I did), TGCN was very noticable in the aggro part. I got aggro quite often until I learned to deal with it. Until then, it had never happened.
