Big Mistake my Friend.

In the higher levels, the ONLY way for you to really survive successfully and be a valuable team member is to use 2hs. At least in my opinion that is. YES, there are some nice one hand slashers out there, but for pure damage, and to make the most out of your defensive tanking abilities, you need a 2hs to your arsenal. I have been going exclusively 2hs since level 21. BUT DO NOT FRET - the skill is easy to build up at that level. If your 2hs is still bad at level 19 or so, go to OASIS of MARR and hunt CROCS with a partner.

I went to Oasis at level 19 with a 2hs of 31. I solo'ed, and worked with a single partner. I dinged level 20 (only three or four yellow bubbles) and my 2hs was up to 90.

My 2hs is now 178 at level 34 AND I am doing some warrior-like damage with it. On average, I am doing 65 points a hit, and just today had a double attack of 79 and 67 points of damage.

Be patient and work your skills early. I recently had a SUPER find of the Messenger of the Queen for 100 Plat (a two-hand piercer) that hits for 20dam, delay 30 something. Very fast, and I built my PIERCE from NOTHING to 90 in about a week.

Now at 34, my two-hand slash is 178, one hand slash (ghoulbane) 140, Piercing 90 and one hand bash around 85.

Until I get WURMSLAYER, I probably won't use one-hand slash again.

In the high-levels, you CANNOT taunt a mob off your casters with 20 point hits of a one hand slasher.

Good luck and I wish you had not gotten rid of the Harpoon!

Yep, definately a big mistake there, 2hs is the staple of a good Paladin, which is obvious by the fact that both of our Epic Weapons (Fiery Defender and Nature's Defender) are 2hs weapons. It is absolutely neccessary to max out your 1hs, 2hs and bashing skills to properly play as a Paladin. Now its a little different for me because I play on a PvP server, and on PvP 2hs is absolutely king for melee vs melee encounters, while 1hs and shield is better for melee vs caster. The same can be true in PvE combat - when fighting casting mobs, especially at higher levels when they gate, etc, it is really important to have bash to interupt casting. 2hs, however, will always be a Paladin's best friend - we aren't very good as damage dealers, but we are good at our big 2hs hits, and of course Paladins are the best tanking class in the game. We technically don't have as many hitpoints as a warrior, but we have our own buffs (and at higher levels there are some awesome Paladin only buffs that put us way up there for HPs).. and with 3 different stuns, flash of light, root, a fire dd, bash and taunt, and the fact that we are the toughest class after Warriors for straight out unbuffed hitpoints, we are the ultimate class for effectively tanking mobs off of weaker classes. The big hits we get with our 2hs weapons are key to this as mobs will turn on those who do the most damage at one time more quickly than they will turn on someone who does a lot of damage in smaller chunks (such as monks and rogues, who outdamage Paladins by FAR but fold up like a lawn chair when they come under fire).

Heh.. just food for thought.. should have kept the harpoon. Go get yourself an Axe of the Iron back ..they're cheap as borsch and common as Honda Civics and are a really good newbie 2hs weapon.
