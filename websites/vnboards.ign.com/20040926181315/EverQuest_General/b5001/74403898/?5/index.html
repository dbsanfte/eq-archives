Well, I think your friend is right about a bard being a good choice for a duo. Bards wear plate giving good AC, have decent HP to tank, and their spells are very formidable! Along with their ability to mez for bad situations, you've got a lot of bases covered!

Adding a cleric to that would be optimal. Since the mobs both of you would duo would be non-slowed, the top notch healing class would be best. Certainly shamans and druids heal well nowadays, but not on par with a cleric. However, your friend is right about a cleric being occasionally boring. Med, heal, maybe a nuke... rinse repeat. I guess thay depends on whoever is playing, what their stamina or if easy duo support is what they need to have a fun time. I read at higher levels, clerics can get into the fight and contribute DPS decently with their summoned hammers. Couple that with yalup at that level, you'll regen mana as if you're sitting? It certainly seems that way when I play my 57 cleric...

For your possible third, I'd say a beastlord for more offense. You wouldn't need another healer with your cleric. Beastlords and their pets are serious DPS source. They have slows and mana regen to contribute greatly to the trio.

Some other suggestions would be shadowknight and shaman. SKs have serious HPs, AC, and offensive spells. With a shaman to heal, buff, pet attack, and max slow. You've got a good duo there as well. Toss a enchanter to that to add charm and mana regen, you've got a solid trio too.

So many choices. You can't go wrong.

But if your friend insists on playing a bard I belive that handycaps a little compared to other choices.

Bards are a great group support class and I mean great. They are also pretty good soloers. But in a duo situation they don't fit well. In reality once they get high enough to solo well. They are better off alone and the 2nd person will be abit useless. They need to kite and the 2nd person will get in the way more then help. Now thats in if your going to straight up tank mobs and go toe to toe with them.

Now, if you indeed do have a 3rd person. Then a bard works. You can go: Healer Type(Cleric, Shaman, Druid)> Bard > Melee Type Class (Some more ideal then others, but whatever is fun. Although a rogue would be tricky)

Now if your style is more of lets kite mobs and kill them with magic. A more solo approach. Then A bard with one or two other kiting classes could very well rock.

Say: Bard, Necro, Druid, Beastlord, Shaman, Mage even a Wizard. Any combination would be awesome. If you feel you need some healing then take Druid or Shaman as one.

If someone is going to play a bard then you really don't need an enchanter with either scenario
