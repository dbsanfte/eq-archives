Grouping starts as soon as you want it to. It's a bit much for most people to try to group at level 1, but you can do it. Most people tend to stay out of groups untill at least level 5, and some untill level 15 or so. About level 10 is a good time. If you are not on a PvP server there are numerous places to group for great experience under level 20. Dungeons and outdoor zones included.

If you want a game where you can totally solo all the way to the highest levels, don't play EQ. The game is designed to allow you play alone, but many things take groups, and at times entire raid parties, to accomplish. One of the great thing about modern games such as EQ, is the interaction with other players. Your fellow players can, at times, be as great a resource as any website (granted they can be as big a nuisance as your neighbor's barking dog at 3am as well).

If you realize this and think you can handle having a character that can solo most of the time consider:

Druid-they are the best kiters. They get SoW, snares, direct damage spells. They also get roots, which enables you to root/dot. Druids don't have the best Damage Over Time spells to be sure, but they do get several that can work at the same time on the same monster. They can also heal themselves just fine. If you are low on mana and hitpoints, you can use the Treeform spell line to help yourself out at low levels. At level 24 (unless they have changed it by now) you get a spell called Spirit of Cheetah. Although short duration (around 30-45 seconds), this spell can give you an advantage over other classes when you're really in a bind. The only thing that will outrun you with this on is a bard with some higher end gear. It seems silly, but this spell takes only 0.5 seconds to cast, making it very usefull as an escape tool. If you're lost, you use it to get yourself far enough away from the thing trying to kill you so that you can gate to safety. Very under rated and underused spell. Druids don't get invunerability spells like clerics, they get Spirit of Cheetah.

Necromancers-fear kiting seems to be a fairly common method. Necro's also get very potent Damage Over Time spells. Some of which even have a Snare type component. You cast your fear spell (makes the monster afraid of you and run away uncontrollably), you DoT with a snare componant, and cut your pet loose on it. At a melee type friend for extra damage. Also, a shaman or druid make a good partner with their added DoT's. Good solo class in my opinion, although be warned I have not played one. It should also be taken in that at a certain level (I have no idea which level) necro's get a feign death spell. Fast casting, max out the skill as soon as possible and it will save you many times over.

Magicians-They have no means of truely healing themselves. They can however summon bandages. The amount of bandages they summon goes up with level as well. They have their own pets that each have their own abilities. One roots, one has a stun spell, one has a water type direct damage spell, one hits real hard and casts a damage shield on itself. They also have direct damage spells that are of decent power. With your pet to taunt and hit the monsters, and your added damage when needed...the monsters don't stand a chance one at a time. Just remember not to get too crazy, as you can not heal yourself. You can heal your pets to a limited extent however. I have seen many magicians solo, but they are also good additions to many groups.

Shadow Knights-They can melee, have some of the necro spells (they are hybrid necro/warrior). They are capable of fear kiting like a necro. They get some DoT type spells, although I believe they are much later in level than the necro ones (as is common to all hybrid. I.E. Paladins get greater heal at a much later level than clerics). They make up for this with their own melee power. They also get a pet, although weak in comparison to the necro ones. The pet can serve you well. Think of it as an extra little DoT. It is also possible (as with any pet class) that your pet could save you. The monsters sometimes tend to turn on your pet when you are out of their range. This means your pet getting whomped gives you an extra second or two to escape. Sk's also get the feign death spell.

Beastlords-I have been playing one lately. Adept at soloing and in groups. They are a hybrid of the shaman and monk classes. They don't get double attack, but do get dual wield. They get some direct damage spells, DoT, lower level shaman buffs, and lower level heals. Chooseing your style of play will determine your strength. For some, their pet is their strength, for others they are their own. Beastlords are somewhat unique, in that, although they are a hybrid class...their pets are strong. The pets have a decent amount of hitpoints (considering they are a hybrid's pet) and not only can you buff them with all the same spells you put on yourself, they have a Direct Damage proc buff. From my personal experience playing both a magician and beastlord at lower levels (under level 35), the beastlord apparently can heal their pets more efficiently. Between your pet and yourself, you can deal some very serious melee damage. Beastlords also get a lower level shaman slow spell (drowsy). This has been invaluable to my play style when solo. My main problem is that I tend to want to get up and melee when my pet and I are both low on health. I keep forgetting that I can heal my pet with greater mana efficiency, and for more hit points at one time by far, than myself.

Any class is capable of solo to an extent, but off the top of my head...those would be my favorites if forced to choose. Paladins can heal themselves, but their only real life saver is Lay of Hands. Although extremely usefull, it takes a long time to recover. Still they are capable of soloing with success between their lower cleric level buffs and heals, with somewhat reasonable down time. As a warrior, forget about soloing for experience after level 15-20. You hit points take far too long to regenerate unaided, and you need them all. I have seen rangers solo, but it was my understanding that they can't heal even as well as paladins. Again I have seen rangers soloing on more than one occassion, just wouldn't be my first choice. Monks are ok, they have feign death and mend. Hard to solo past level 20 or so if you are not twinked.

Everything I have said is based on personal experience, or what I have heard from people that play the particular classes. I personally have a druid, magician, warrior, monk, beastlord that I play. In my statements I assume that the particular class in question is not twinked, or buffed from high level characters. If you are not sure what twinking is...if you could not have gotten all of your armor on your own, or with a group or raid of level compairable to your own...you are a twink. That is not to say that none of my characters are or are not. Several of them are. Anyone can save cash and go to the bazaar and buy items. There is nothing wrong with that. I still retain respect for people that go out and do it themselves however.

Road-Warrior welcome to Everquest

Glad to see that you are enjoying your first look into the world of Norath.

I am not sure what you mean by abilities... do you mean alternate abilities (which you need to be level 51 before you can start using) or do you mean the options/setup windows?

Regardless of the answer the answer lies in the bar of icons accross the top of the screen (default) these icons open and close various windows, from your inventory and spell icon window through to game options window. holding the cursor above the icon will identify the window that is opened.

Hot buttons can also be set up, to do this use the window in the bottom right corner (default) at the top of this window are 4 tab icons. These enable you to set up buttons that allow you to attack, use ranged item to setting up a series of instructions/messages that you want to produce on a regular basis, play around with this, it can be a handy tool. Once you have created the button as you wish click and hold the left mouse button while the cursor is over the new button, this will enable you to place the new button in your hot key box in the bottom left corner of the screen(default)

I hope that this has answered some of the questions that you may have.

EQ does not allow for macroing in the same way that you could in AC1. You can set up a button to do 5 separate tasks, but this will not enable you to level your character while you are sleeping. Macro'ing is against Sony's rules and is a banable offense.

As to a guide there are many sites out there on how to plave various classes, eqlore, eqstratic, castersrealm. I can't remember the full path names but just do a search on Yahoo or google to get a list of sites.

The Zek servers are all PVP servers and are probably best to avoid when learning the game. The rest of the servers have both good and bad people on a new server has just been created so you could start on a level par with other people starting on the server, or you could go to a more populated server where what was considered uber gear a couple of years ago can be purchased cheaply from the bazaar.

Just try a few characters to get the feel of the game, once you find something you like have have fun.

Cad,

I am not sure if there is any glossary of EQ terms, if in doubt ask. I still ask and I have been playing about 4 years.

As to your connection speed, the graphics are stored on your system so that you should not have problems playing the game in most zones. Problems may occur when lots of people are in the zone or there are lots of items/mobs/PC's/NPC's in a small area. Zones that are highly affected are the Bazaar and some of the Luclin zones. There are ways to reduce this, reduce the clip plane, turn off spell effects and name tags.

The other area that may be frustraiting for you is the download times. With the new LoY expansion some people were complaining of a 9 hour down load time. Until about a year ago i used to play on a 56k modem with worse download rates than you and I had no real problems.
