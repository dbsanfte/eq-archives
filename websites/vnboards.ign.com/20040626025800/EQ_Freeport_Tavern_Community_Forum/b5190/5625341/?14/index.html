Chapter Two
Except for possibly her father, she couldn't have chosen a better pair of hands in which to put her life. Velathir kept a keen watch on Seani. They began their trip in the Karanas, a beautiful plain, full of trees and covered by a wonderfully blue sky. Seani was glad the trip was long for she got to witness the beautiful spectrum of colors in a Karanas' sunset.
They ran along the water encountering little but vast space. Deeper into the plains, the magician's skills proved more useful. Creatures like she had never seen were everywhere! Hill giants, larger than the largest trees, rumbled periously close. Griffawns, Griffenes, and Griffens, half eagle and half other animals, flapped their wings lazily, unaware of the travelers' presence. Finally they made their way into a high pass, walking a sheer ledge and into a city in the mountains. It was there Seani saw her first Orc.
A small problem held the pair in the high pass for a bit. Seani had become visible right next to an orc, and Velathir had gallently taken the Orc's attention while Seani retrieved the wary guards. They waited there while Velathir's wounds healed.
Seani was amazed at this city that had been built in amongst the cliffs and quite near the frightful orcs. Why had these people not run the orcs away? The situation reminded Seani of the gnolls near Qeynos. As she thought of that, she understood. There were too many, they were too powerful, and they would co-exist with them as long as they could.
She also thought of her father. Velathir seemed to be asleep, and Seani took this moment to remove the beautiful dagger from it's sheath. As she sat, lost in thought, stroking the fine ebony handle, she was startled by Velathir's voice.
"I thought Cleric's did not believe in cutting other living things?"
"We don't," she replied softly, "but we have to cut our food, fabric, other things."
"That doesn't really explain why you carry a magician's dagger, one forged for battle. Some may not recognize the weapon, but remember what I am, dear lady."
Seani looked into his clear, blue eyes. She noticed for the first time how pure white his hair was, brushed back from his face. He really did look like royalty to this common human girl from Qeynos. He had been hidden from sight most of the journey. The girl had to trust him the entire way thus far - following his voice, directions, and the sound of his footsteps. And the entire journey she had felt safe. This incident only proved to her again that Velathir could and would be a loyal friend.
She looked away and into the stream running near them.
"It was my father's. He was also a magician. He gave it to me recently."
"I should like to meet him one day. I am fairly new in my practice of magic, perhaps he could..." He trailed off as he noticed a single tear fall from the cleric's beautiful brown eyes.
She looked back to him. "He's dead."
Velathir moved closer to the girl. He put one arm around her shoulder as she wept until all her tears were gone.
