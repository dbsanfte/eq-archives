Since you are in the business of insurance, or at least studying it, Din, perhaps you could research the effect of "Other Insurance" clauses in the standard CGL or other risk policy.

Additionally, you could research the studies concerning premiums charged vs. underwriting risk. Then apply that study to a person who changes their "risk profile." At that point, there are 3 things an insurer can do: (1) nothing, (2) increase/reduce premium, or (3) refuse to assume the risk of loss.

Related point, in South Louisiana, State Farm was writing new Property and Casualty policies right up until Hurricane Andrew hit. I moved residences after the hurricane. I was notified that State Farm was not writing any new P&C policies in Louisiana. Such is the business of insurance.

Again, as those in the military know, they are not without benefits or coverage. It's just different benefits and coverage.

There are policies out there that do cover war deaths. You just have to do research.

In fact there are policies that do special things that you wouldn’t think about. For instance if you are a member of the Knights of Columbus they provide a small member ship insurance that includes if in the event you were to get killed in war your children get a free education at any catholic college. The policies that KoC offer do cover a war death which is the reason I have it along with my SGLI that the military offers.

I know that example is religious specific insurance but I am sure there are other programs out there like that. You just have to look.

The SGLI is a very good program while you are in the service. About 17$ (flat rate regardless of age) a month for $250,000. The military does allow you to retain that when you leave service but since it’s a term life insurance it becomes prohibitively expensive when you start to get older. That is why it is smart to get a whole life policy when you are young to cover you when you get older.

A service members family who gets killed receives $250,000, an additional $10k to cover funeral costs, a full time liaison to help handle paperwork, details and the shipment of your property (at gov’t expense), and a burial with military honors.

If in the event that I go to war and get killed I know that my funeral is taken care of, my child has an education to look forward to and my family will not have to worry financial.

After all that is what insurance is supposed to do.
