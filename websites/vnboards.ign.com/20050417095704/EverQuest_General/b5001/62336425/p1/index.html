The time my guild raided... Erudin.

This was around the time shortly before Kunark was released. There was about 3 npcs left on tracking in the whole zone when we got done. Sounds kind of stupid I know, but we had a hell of a time.

The only thing I really miss about old world EQ though would be how groups typically formed in dungeons there, places that are abandoned now, like Permafrost. I mostly remember original EQ involving ALOT of essentially sitting and waiting to do almost anything compared to the current pace now. Or spending 30 minutes to an hour of traveling to THEN sit and wait.

People complain SE has "ruined" EQ by making the whole game too easy, especially for new players, what I see was a ton of useless timesinks being removed or mitigated by new spells, equipment and features like the PoK book portals. Everyone can accomplish twice as much in EQ now in half the time compared to the original game.

What do I miss from the OG EQ?

I miss the "newness" of the genre. EQ was my first MMORPG (I like to leave the "RP" in). My first character was a barbarian warrior. Navigating through Everfrost was a true adventure, where I had to watch my back at every turn. Powerleveling and twinking were virtually unheard of.

After taking 2 years or so off and then returning to EQ, it has some of the newness. However, twinking and powerleveling are abundant and very apparent, even on Stromm. I'm still having fun, though.

Agreed, jfmgraphics.

When I first played EQ I was utterly blown away. Having been a long time PnP DnD player it was so glorious to somewhat be playing computerized DnD with so many other people. I had a somewhat similar feeling of 'wow' when I first started DAoC since it has very different (and in many ways superior) graphics - but it wasn't the same since I was familiar with MMORPG mechanics.

EQ was also so innocent and brutal when it first came out. People would work hard to have banded armor before 20th...reaching 20th was huge...every zone was useful and hunted.

In a lot of ways EQ has been corrupted over time by greed, powerlevels, twinking, and somewhat by all the expansions since it has spread people out so much with so many zones and so many possible player levels.

Twinking, powerleveling, etc are a part of every MMORGP I've played once they get going, and IMO the games aren't as fun once they reach this stage since people don't seem to play the same way once a game evolves this far.

But anyways...

Despite any flaws _I_ may see I'm still having a great time playing EQ and overall I really like the way the game has evolved.

I never did like excessive death penalties, ridiculous corpse runs, running for 20 minutes to get somewhere just to find nothing to do, etc. And at a point it got old having very few places to hunt and having them packed to the point of having every single individual spawn point claimed and camped perpetually. I sure as heck don't miss all the down time of old.
