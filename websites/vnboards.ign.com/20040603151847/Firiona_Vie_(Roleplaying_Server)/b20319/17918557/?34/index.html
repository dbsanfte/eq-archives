Of late I've noticed more frequent incursions of softskins to the hunting grounds just out of sight of Cabilis and the troopers who'd slay the beasts on sight. They can be amusing creatures, shouting and flapping about that they can't understand our demands that they remove themselves from our territory. Personally, I don't mind their presence so much, as long as they don't interfere with our business - and if they do, the troopers will dispatch them soon enough. I rather enjoy watching their attempts to fight the local creatures; certainly it's worth a chuckle, if one comes screaming by yelling its fool head off for help, to watch it die. Soft weak little things.

I saw one up in Kurn's Tower last week, accompanied by a bonecaster of my dim acquaintance; he said that the creature was his pet, and it seemed subservient enough, though it would speak only to its master and didn't respond to any of our prodding and poking and general amusment at its expense. He said that it too worshipped Cazic-Thule, and had some similar spellcasting powers as those taught in the necromantic guild in Cabilis; I had a hard time believing it until I saw it summon a skeleton with my own eyes. I suppose in that respect they could make useful slaves if they could be properly broken and taught how best to serve.

Two nights ago, however, I saw something of a much more disturbing nature. On our way back to Cabilis from a hunting expedition at the lake shore, we came upong an Iksar hunting with - WITH! - two great bloated ape-men. My companion asked one how it had gotten so fat, and it replied in its own tongue, as translated by its Iksar companion, (and I should have beaten it for daring to speak so rudely), that it had gotten that way by eating so many Iksar. Disgusted, I asked the fellow grouping with them how he could bear to work alongside such creatures, and attempted to make it clear how degrading it was, but he would only reply some nonsense about our party "Coming to steal his camp." We decided that he must have some kind of mental deficiency, babbling on with such inanities, not to mention his willingness to fraternize with those giant ape-creatures in the first place, and resumed our trek back to the city. I must make a note to keep an eye out for members of his guild (they all wore the same identifying marks on their armor) and find out if this is their common practice. I'll not aid any Iksar so willing to throw away his own proud heritage and mingle with beasts like that as equals.

what I've noticed as far as racial tensions is an over-exaggeration of racial hatred. I remember when I first created my Bard. I went to Toxxulia to hunt Kobolds for a warrior quest in Qeynos. I was verbally assaulted everywhere I turned by the 'good' erudites of Erudin. No matter how many times I helped someone (without taking a kill) or tried to be friendly, I just got hatred back. People are forgetting that, even though we might be of a different race, what the history behind our race / class stands for.
I could understand the hatred if I were a dark elf in their forest, as dark elves have a very negative history on Norrath. Half-elves really only have one negative point about them, they're half breeds. And bards have pretty much no negative sides, everyone loves a good show, song, story or poem. For the most part, people are just saying, "Hey you're not of my race, I hate you!" Nevermind there were Erudite Necromancers and Shadowknights around Erudin's gate... the Paladins treated these folk with more respect (actually, I never saw a negative thing said to one of them) than they did me!
And before I hear another Erudite accuse me of being one of the many coming to Toxxulia... may I direct your attention to the 100+ folk in North Qeynos, about 45% of whom are Erudites?

"However, through a long and occasionally strained relationship with the high and wood elves, and from continuous battle with the evil of the goblins, they will stray toward goodness rather than evil, if indeed they stray at all."

That is a direct quote on dwarves from Verant having to do with their alignments. While dwarves and elves might not always get along, they do have, or supposed to have, a very healthy respect for each other. But folks playing a dwarf or elf don't look at it this way, they just think they're not supposed to get along with each other since they're not the same race and don't speak the same language.
Are there racial tensions? Yes.
Should there be racial tensions? Depends on the races.
Think about this kind of stuff while you're adventuring and roleplaying. If you want to roleplay a racist then by all means do so, but I challenge you to NOT follow the huge crowd that is already doing just that.

I feel I must add to this discussion, though I am hesitant, as I doth not wish to offer unjust reproach to any of kind heart and good character.
Initially I shall state that the general consensus that we of elven heritage, whether nobly or base born, conduct ourselves with arrogant demeanor, is indeed true. Even we, of the noblest race, hath our failings.
I must make note, however, that there art tensions amongst our two kindreds as well. I feel many of these tensions art preconcieved, and thus, some few mistakenly express ill regard toward those we art as of yet unable to understand fully.
Our dwarven neighbours art stout and worthy warriors, able priests of their faith, and my brother knights hath been respectful in all my dealings with them. They hath some knaves amongst them, roguish fellows full of greed, whom thou hath best not allow approach thee too nearly.
Our further neighbours, the learned gnomes, I hath found to be pleasant company on occasion, though I am oft sadly unaware of their ability. For this reason alone I hath, on occasion, withheld invitation, though I hath never been hesitant to offer aid or healing when I make note of one in need.
My lesser cousins, I hath found, oft display a greater degree of displeasure with my own kind than any other race. This seems a habit of the male half of the population.
As to humanity, I hath found many to be honourable and kind, able in their chosen vocation, and pleasant company. For each of these, however, another eventually comes about whom is disrespectful and in dire need of a bath.
I hath a healthy respect for all those of mingled heritage, for the trials they must surely endure due to their unfortunate circumstance. I hath witnessed my fellows react disparagingly toward these, and scolded them for such unbecoming behaviour.
As to the other races which inhabit our world; the ogres, trolls, and iksar...I hath seen few and become aquainted with none. Therefore, I must withhold judgement. I am told some of these folk may not worship their racial deities, and seek the path of light. I shall aid any such, and slay any other whom proves evil in nature.
And of course, there art my evil cousins. There is only one for whom I hath any regard other than wrath, for she proved herself honourable in her way, despite her black heart. I look forward to the day our blades may cross again. The rest art all cowards whom hide behind weak insults when I offer them the redemption of death by my hand.
Tamini, thou hath aided me in the past, yet asked payment for such. This I find disgusting. Thou hath put a blight upon thy race, which many of thy kindred hath convinced me to ignore. Many art honourable, and just. Thou art not.
Eladari. I hath read thy heart wrenching tale of thy childhood companion. I hath noted thy discourse explaining various meetings with my brethren which hath gone poorly. I understand thy ill favor, and respect thee for thy willingness to remain hopeful of a better understanding between our peoples.
Though for thyself, such understanding may prove too little when held against those ill moments thou hath endured, I offer thee my respect. We may never be friendly, Eladari, yet I will always aid thee in need, and gainsay any whom speak ill of thee.

These times art trying, and we shall all suffer to a degree. Such is the will of the gods.
May Tunare bless thee all, and thy own gods watch over thee. Fare thee well.
