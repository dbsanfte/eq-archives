Although i'm not 100% sure on this i'm almost certain that a mob calculates healing aggro in the same way as a nuke would be. 1000hp of healing = 1000dam from a wizard lets say. even if thats not the case healing at LEAST aggroes as much as an equivalent nuke maybe more. As for timing the aggro.. here is the PERFECT scenario one could hope for with a CH.
IE:

9:45:32 Melee_01 engages mob.
9:45:50 By this time the melee has generated enough aggro to very safely be healed. AKA 3 taunts..damage output.. and the occasional Disarm to generate max aggro.
9:45:51 Cleric_01 Begins to cast Complete Heal.
9:46:01 Melee_01 is completely healed with 5-10% Hp left.
9:46:09 Mob_01 dies
then at this point tank should have lost only minor % hp and a celestial heal or Divine light would suffice for the next pull.

My 2cp as I use this method for Chardok, Seb, Howling Stones for max mana efficiency as i do not group with chanters often.

For lower level encounters similar methods can be applied, wait a while before healing and if the mobs are doing too much damage to the tank of the group make sure that your tank (if he/she) is pulling is topped off on health before each pull.

Healing level of the caster vs that of the mob makes a diffrence. Lower level players will get more agro.

As for tanks that need a bit of healing at the begining of a fight I would use a HoT.
Perferabally before the tank pulls.
Also, keep up with heals by topping off HPs.

When in most grind groups the norm to start a CH is at 40%.

As for the amount of agro. It said in a patch that they were basing it on the amount of HP that you heal.
The actual numbers would make little diffrance cas VI doesn't tell you all the other values like how much hate taunt does.

It's a matter of trial and error. Try something if it works do it. If it doesn't try something else.

As for getting agro from mezzed mobs.
Brown Card that...

You might of not agroed the mobs, but they'll hate the chanter/bard when the mez ends. So keep a fast heal up.

Have DA/DB and don't be afraid to use them.
