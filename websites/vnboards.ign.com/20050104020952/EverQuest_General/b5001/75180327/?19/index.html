Date Posted: 9/17/04 12:14pm Subject: RE: Topic of the Week 9/08 - What do you want to see from the next EQ Expansion?
Everquest: The Ruined Worlds
A multi-faceted Gateway that leads to four Regions (very large zones) sits in the center of a ruined city that is only now being repopulated by Norrathian conquerors. Depending on which facet of the Gate you enter, you can teleport to:
The Region of Order, which is a flat endless plane of white tile covered with a recilinear array of various Constructions, ranging from basic geometric shapes all the way up to filligreed cathedrals with flying buttresses. The sky is a checkerboard of white and black squares.
The Region of Chaos, which has a swirling kaleidoscope of a sky, and where the ground is really a series of floating chunks of rock connected by crude swaying rope bridges. Each chunk or rock has the aspect of a different type of worldly territory, such as tundra, jungle, or desert, and below this crowded sky rests a vast and turbulent sea with no bottom and no land.
The Region of Good, which is a medieval fairyland full of fairies and sprites and furry creatures and airy castles flying bright pennants under a clear blue sky.
The Region of Evil, a foreboding gloomy cavernous region full of twisted spawns of dark origins, dark ugly fortresses, and mainly lit by burning buildings and funeral pyres.

Chaos and Order would be raid-and-group zones, and would have various smaller zones connecting through them. One zone off of Chaos would be an instanced raid zone called "The Immateria" which would be unpopulated on zone-in except for a single female NPC who, once the raid members had all zoned in, would be the trigger mob. Ask her to make the immaterial material, and she will populate the zone with creatures that are randomly selected from a very large list. You would never know exactly what you would be fighting before you started fighting in the Immateria. But the rewards would be substantial. This might be the only way to get certain raid-oriented spells for instance.

A zone off of the Region of Order would be the "Machine World." Not a random junkyard inside a labyrinth of canyons like the Plane of Innovation, the Machine World would be essentially like being inside of a working factory with an assembly line. Non-humanoid automatons would abound, from small toaster-sized repair mechanisms all the way up to gigantic construction-crane-like machines.

The Regions of Good & Evil would be solo and group-level quest areas. Nothing that can be directly equipped or used drops in them or the zones that branch off them, but many No Drop quest items drop, and the NPC's in one region send you to the opposing region to kill for the drops.
