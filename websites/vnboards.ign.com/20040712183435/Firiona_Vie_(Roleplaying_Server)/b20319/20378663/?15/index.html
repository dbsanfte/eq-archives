There is not enough data at present to say whether or not the graphics engine is "good" or not.

One thing to keep in mind is that graphics engines are optimized for the uses that they will be put to. I have heard a few people up on the main boards saying things like "if it is so good then why is my year-old Quake3 game so much better looking" and whatnot.

Quake and Unreal are optimized for "indoor" environments. That is, the amount of polygons that the engine has to render and any given time is severely constrained by walls, relatively small distances and an assurance that you will only have X different non-static things to render. Additionally, the amount of textures and models that must be tracked by the engine are likely small, comparatively.

EQ engine must be capable of rendering both tight indoor environments and massive outdoor realms (often quite full or trees and whatnot). It must be capable of displaying dozens, if not scores of creature-models, all changing position at the whim of an AI or player and thus not re-renderable via a simple transform algorithm. Finally, the amount of models and textures that the EQ engine stuffs into your system and video RAM is staggering, especially after this update.

So anyway, the new engine may in fact be a gnoll-booger, but a comparison in performance between EQ and some other "high end" game is not really applicable.

I'd be interested in hearing how the new EQ engine stacks up against AO's engine, which is supposed to be quite good.
