Congratulations on your business endeavor. I sure it has been fanstically exciting to dream about it, plan it, then execute it. I wish you the best of luck and sincerely hope for your sucess. Please keep us updated with your progress.

My girlfriend's cousin operates a LAN center in Fort Lee New Jersey. His business has struggled for a variety of reasons, however, your business model has several keen advantages over his.

For example, that part of New Jersey is over saturated with computer gaming centers. He's got local zoning ordinances which prohibit him from staying open past 10:00 pm. He's got enormous overhead liabilities, including rent and T1 connectivity. And lastly, his hardware was purchased with an initial outlay of capital.

So in light of these issues, you're miles ahead of him from the outset.

Out of curiosity, how do you plan to handle audio from the PC games. Having a dozen different games blaring uncoordinated music / sound effects would become a jarble. If you are planning to have headphones attached to each PC, how do you plan to deal with the hygene issue? Think about those nasty, greasy headphones at a CD sampling station at tower records.

This last question is really about your business plan... Perhaps it's a bit too sensitive, so feel free not to answer it... but... in hours rented, what is your break even point per PC? Or restated, how many hours would you need to rent out each PC to cover your associated costs for hardware lease, connectivity, software, etc.

Anyways, good luck! Hoping for the best!
