On the topic of raid rants... Towards the end of Spring in '03, I took myself out the public raiding cycle. The game itself was going in the direction I, personally, was not ready to take my gaming.

I went off and leveled and took it easy to help people who asked for help.

I return to public raiding and it has changed a lot from when I left it.

Here are just a couple of what I have observed:

1. There was some form of common sense respect for the raid leader(s). Now, everyone and their pet seems to think they know better than the raid leader and feel (for some reason) the raid leader should have done this or that their way instead.
2. There was raiding protocol that was followed to some degree. If someone in group wanted to know something about the raid, they ask the group leader and the group leader asks another group leader or the raid leader if he/she does not know the answer. Now, people ignore the role of group leader and send tells directly to the raid leader... and when a group person asks the group leader in group, the group leader answers, "this ain't my raid, find someone in your class and ask".
3. There was some degree of raid discipline and follow-through. Assist keys seem to be broken these days as well as listening abilities and unless there is a pressing RL issue, a lot of people are disbanding and leaving the raid as soon as it's over and does not remain to make sure everyone, included raid leader, has made it to safety. I'm not saying this never happened before, I'm just point it out that it's happening more often.

Khuji as a response to your concerns:

The decline of the quality of public raiding is a 2 way street. Yes it's true that there is an issue with the quality of raiders lately. However at the same time it is true that there is a severe lack of quality in raid leadership.

Where you used to have a relatively small group of people that led public raids. Aark, Bryko, Magget, and about a dozen others that led raids week in and week out and developed not only their own raid leadership skills but also a reputation as a solid raid leader that when developed has a good ability to attract people to raids led by them.

They go into these encounters with overwhelming numbers and simply put smother the event and they quickly get their flag never to return.

That honor of public raiding you refer to has devolved into a "mob mentality" approach of handling an encounter.

Not only do raid leaders (the real ones) need to look out for the loot/flag whores but so do true public raiders that enjoy the public raiding need to beware of the piece of crap players that call themselves raid leaders only to advance their own flag count.
