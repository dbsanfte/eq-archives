 		Date Posted: 12/8/03 1:12pm Subject: "Plat for Sale" Evil exploitation or Valid Business
Before I begin let me make the 2 following statements.

01) This is not an offer to BUY/SELL plat.
02) I have never and will never BUY/SELL plat.

============================================

Ok those disclaimers said, I've read a lot of the threads floating around about the alleged exploits some myth some true about increasing plat.

So my curiosity took me to a known website that people buy and sell plat (among other things). Of course it seems there are a large number of "sales" going on by a few individuals.

So I ask myself what do I as an individual care about whether people buy or sell plat or for that matter how plat was aquired. To really look at this we have to first decide what is the "true" source of plat.

01) Killing Mobs and looting plat directly.
02) Performing quests and getting plat as a reward.
03) Killing mobs and selling the various drops to vendors.

To my knowledge those are the only 3 sources of plat entering an economy. (Unlike real life where a govt can print/mint all the money they want, or people attempt to counterfeit it, or "debt" ie credit.)

Ok that being said now we need to look at what is plat used for?

While there are a million intermediate uses for plat the final "end" use boil down to 2 things.
01) Give to a npc vendor for purchases
02) Give to a player to purchase a looted/tradeskilled item.

Ok so now that we have that established one needs to ask how much plat can a person really need?

Ok I don't buy armor/weapons for myself because I have access to better stuff that is nodrop. As far as making purchases from vendors... even as a guild leader of an actively raiding guild, peridots, coffins, EE's, etc. We barely go through 10k plat per month thanks to MGB etc. Heck I could take my guild into CoD and tear the zone apart for a few hours on a slow sunday and easily come out with nearly 10k in various "diamonds". So that being said my need for plat on a personal level is even smaller.

Ok so now that brings us to why would I care about mudflation, inflation etc etc. Ok so a BoC sold for 200k last week and now it sells for 500k cuz more plat on the market. So? If you aren't in the market for that who cares. If playerX has 100 plat in the bank or 1 billion plat in the bank it makes no impact on my purchasing needs since the price of peridots is always about 12.5plat.

As far as buying plat online, Ok I can see why Sony would get mad about players buying/selling characters because that does take away from their own sales based. (if you assume that a noob player is a larger potential revenue stream than an existing player). As far at the plat goes though, the redistribution of wealth within a virtual economy shouldn't matter to Sony's bottom line. Now I suppose it could be debated that this redistribution of wealth unbalances the economy of a server if one believes that the "bazaar" is a crucial part of the game. I'm not convinced that it is to be honest.

Ok my final thought on this is, given all of this why shouldn't I buy plat. Hmm it takes me solo about 30 min or so to generate 1k plat. While looking at the prices on the previously mentioned website, you can buy 1000k plat for about 625 dollars. That's about 63cents for 1k. So I ask myself, is 30 minutes of my time worth 63 cents. Heck for 625 dollars I would litterally never need to hunt for plat again. It would take me 500hrs of EQ time to farm 1000k plat but only 10hrs of RL time to make enough money at work to pay for 1000K plat. 500hrs v. 10hrs. Kinda a forgone conclusion what the logical choice would be.

Ok let me open this up to some discussion, hopefully it will stay civil longer than some other of my threads tend to do. /grin.
