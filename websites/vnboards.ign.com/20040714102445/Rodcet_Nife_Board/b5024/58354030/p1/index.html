"Are you seriously telling us that three 300 post threads is less of an burden on the system than a single 900 post thread?"

Absolutely.

If you know how a database stores a thread, it's through a 'chained' list of posts. Each post has a 'parent' that it points up to and a 'child' that it points down to.

So, when you click on a thread, the Database has to pull up the list of posts in the thread, and assemble them in order.

So, it's not a matter of how much storage a post takes up - 900 posts is 900 posts, if the database never does anything with them.

It's a matter of what happens when the thread is clicked.

If 100 people click on a 300 post thread, it's only 1/3 the load on the database as 100 people clicking on a 900 post thread.

If 100 people click on a 10 post thread, well, it's a LOT less burden, hehe.

Also, as threads get bigger, the component posts get older, and slower to actually retrieve. That's why you see problems pulling up the first pages of a very long/old post. The database can time out before it has time to go get all of the posts.

And keep in mind that everyone is sharing CPU cycles here, so long posts don't just affect the person retrieving it. If the system is busy pulling up a lot of long posts, it appears slower even for people who only want to pull up a single post thread, because they are waiting in the queue longer.

Of course, one 1000 post thread on one board isn't going to bring the system to it's knees, but we have hundreds of boards here, and there were dozens of long posts on each board, so it all adds up.

As for locking old posts, that helps when you re-index and defrag the database (which we do on a regular basis). Once the information for an old post becomes static, through a lock, it can be consolidated both in the database and on the disk, resulting in much quicker retrieval even for older posts.
