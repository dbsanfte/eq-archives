I played in beta. Some people have called it "EQ in space" but I'm not so sure. It doesn't have nearly as much of a camp/raid/loot feel to it. I would consider it closer to "DAoC in Space". The newbie missions are simply amazing. You get useful items and skills by doing them, and they help to break you into the game more easily. Getting to around level 30 isn't any rougher than getting to, say, level 15 in EQ, but there are 150 levels in E&B. That seems like a lot, but it's really not that many, since the levels are a combo of 3 skill levels (50 max in each).

Graphics are pretty darn good. I don't think they'll have anything on SWG or possibly Eve if it comes out, but they're good regardless. You can solo or group, and both are viable, but grouping is much faster. For instance, a full group of 6 yields a 200% exp bonus, and a cumulative ship-specific bonus for each ship in your group. Also unlike EQ, you don't need 3 hours to do anything worthwhile. 1 hour will see you getting something meaningful accomplished. All in all, its a great game for casual players, but I think powergamer types would become bored after they maxed out their levels quickly. There are plans to look into a PvP structure, but no garauntees.

Also played beta.

I had fun but since the landscape is space it doesn't have the compeling feel of most mmorpgs. Lot's of open space in every direction.

If you are the type that likes to build upon your character, in this case your ship, in a steady mannor, and well developed quests will hold your attention then this game could be for you.

The drawbacks are that thought the quests run in an awsome well oiled format they typically are in the same flavor. Kill X mob bring Y drop to Z person, or Run this message over to A and he will give you something to give to B... so on so forth.

In the contexts of the missions it is nice to see your ship getting better and better in time with the new up grades but ultimatly I found it a wee bit flat geographicly to invest too much into.

I will say, they have a nice modle for experiance gathering. You get it 3 ways, exploring, fighting and tradeing so you can level up just by running around and checking out the universe.

Quests still have timed spawns that you have to compete for, I just couldn't stand to camp for content again.

Combat is overly simplistic & really seems to lack any depth in strategy and teamwork.

They've made a few good strides to having noncombat oriented classes, their craft system is decent.

They do a pretty good job of story telling during your early quests, you get a fairly decent feel for what's going on in the universe.

Ship customization, while interesting, is pretty useless - you really dont see others that well when flying around with them. You're not going to recognize people by their ships.

Short-burst playing is possible, you can log in, go do something for 30-60 minutes & make progress.

Travel times get to be pretty long, I often found myself browsing on my other machine, or going to do other things while waiting to fly from one point to another.

Insta death can occur from just exploring -- you'll warp into a point & it'll be surrounded with mobs that are vastly more powerful than you - no prior warning, no real escape possibility, makes for no real fun.
