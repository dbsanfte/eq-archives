If MB pretty much has no use in the "big game" portion of EQ, so be it. But there is still no way you can use the arguement of "the thrill of the hunt" right after the MB wizzies have already said how easy it is. Pull, nuke at same time, dead in 3.5 seconds, no real risk.

Back in the "old days" (yeah, I'm sorry for bringing that up), the thrill of the hunt was to see how few people you could take to kill certain mobs or clear certain zones. But there is a big difference between that, and what is being discussed here. Back then the "thrill" was trying to see if you could actually survive with so few people, knowing all the while there was a very good chance you'd die and have to try again.

This MB hunt against dragons is more like me getting together 5 other clerics, all with our best 925 damage nuke, finding a mob that is highly susceptible to magic spells, making sure the mob has something less than 5k hps (leave the other 550 out for the possible partial resist) and then go nuke it. It's removed all risk and tactics, all skill, and just gone out and killed a mob.

So since there really is no skill involved, no real risk, and no real worry of failing/dying...what's left? Loot? If it's not the loot, then I just don't really see this as any different than a lvl 60 wizzie wandering though Oasis and killing an SG for the hell of it...except an SG doesn't take days/weeks to respawn, nor is it still sought after by people in the game that still need and will use the items dropped.

But again...that's just me. Guess people get their thrills in varying ways. Just seems some understand the difference between "play nice policy" and actually playing nice.
