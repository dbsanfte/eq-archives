As Most of you know, I can't reply to a thread. So
I am starting another to reply to Sildyars post. Sorry
for the waste of space.

I agree with your points Sildyar, but would add some
of my views to it. Thanks to Verant, this game is
equipment based. Anyone can say all they want about
role-playing or not caring at all about their gear...
But good luck as a caster with a Kunark mob beating on
you if you are wearing all cloth armor...Or as a melee
wearing quest armor or worse tanking a Kunark mob.
The simple fact is you do need the best gear you can get
to be effective in this game. Sure some casters might
be able to solo a low blue almost naked, but it certainly
wouldn't be safe or efficient. I think most of us know
by now we need the best gear we can get. The difference is
Those who join uberguilds (most of them) Are all about
getting loot NOW. Those of us who choose to remain in
small guilds have much less of a shot at the uberloot, but
that's the price we pay to not be just another cookie
cutter uberguild. I think our guild has much more of a
'personality' than an uberguild. As some of you know
I was recruited by a few large guilds a while ago.
I'd be lying if I said I didn't think about it. I stayed
because I believe in what the guild stands for, and I
value friendship over loot any day.

Does us not being an uberguild mean we will never have the
gear we need? NO, it will just take longer to get their.
I think it will be that much more satisfying acheiving our
goals without compromising our beliefs. If we all just
keep slowly plugging away at levels and raise the
average level in the guild we will be able to start
doing things together as a guild. I want to keep doing
smaller raids when I get back. I believe we still need
more work together before we are ready for the 'big' stuff.


One thing I never want this guild to do: Exclude others
from a zone raid of any kind. I don't want us to ever
tell someone: 'Sorry, Guild ONLY'. If we have a friend
or two who want to come along, by all means let them. I think most of us at higher levels know what
it feels like to be excluded from a raid because we have
the wrong tag over our heads.

Once we start raids, We have people that will help us.
The Order of Djinn and Acid Cabal both have expressed interest
in raiding with us in the future. Possibly a few members of
black Tower as well. I look forward to the day when
15 or more members of OotHH will be in Fear or Hate with
me.

I agree with Sam wholeheartedly, weighted lottos are
not for us. There are possible exceptions though. What
would we say if we had broken fear with our friends and
were almost done when a druid zones in and asks to be
put in lottos right away with all the other druids that
have been there breaking and dying for 5+ hours? Situations like that are tricky, should be decided
by the class itself. I know if I died 6 times breaking
fear I wouldn't be happy if an SK showed up 7 hours later
as we were pulling temple and demanded in lottos. There are exceptions to the exceptions of course.
A friend zones in halfway through the raid, they only need
one more piece of their planes armor to complete their
set what would you do? I'd default that piece if it dropped...Depending on how good the friend is
would possibly even default the bplate.

I think we all have good friends in an uberguild. But as
Sam said, when they are all together they act different.
I could never imagine this guild saying we own a zone
GUILD ONLY. I don't care how big we get, this guild isn't
about exclusion.

So keep plugging away guildmates, I shall return one
day expecting you all to be a few levels higher. We will do some smaller raids again to keep the
guild cohesion going. I strongly encourage you all to attend
any events planned for the guild. You can camp EXP or camp
that uber item another night. Believe me, I know how you
feel...I fell into that exp/camp trap before and I still
have brief failings. But I find time to do other things
now besides exp/item camp all the time. ( AT least I did
before I lost my ISP)

Sorry for the long-winded post, I look forward to seeing
you all soon in-game. Good hunting.
