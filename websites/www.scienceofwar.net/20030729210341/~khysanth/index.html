High Priest of Ssraeshza Strategy

  • Event Summary: The fight is broken into two sections - a crowd-control (CC) team and the rest of the raid. The CC team is responsible for locking down and controlling the mobs that are in the High Priest's room while the rest of the raid force takes down the HP.

  • Spawn Tracking: Make a level 1 ranger, race doesn't matter. Put all of his starting points into stamina, buy about four or five invis potions, pay for a Spirit of Eagle from a friendly druid, and make your way to the Ssraeshza Temple. Once there, head down to the basement (nothing should see invis on the way) and make your way to point 3 on the map below. Avoid that commander as he paths along the red line. From there, with a tracking skill of about 10, you can check on the High Priest's spawn status. He is a 6 - 7 day spawn.

  • Duration of Raid: This raid typically takes Science of War just under an hour to complete from the time we enter the zone until the AE TL.

  • Getting There: First, you'll need a key. This key is dropped from a commander on the second floor named Commander Zherozsh and is aptly named 'Zherozsh's Key'. Zherozsh's key allows entrance to the High Priest and Xerkizh the Creator area. The commander can spawn in the place of any shissar on the second floor (take a right from the zone-in and go up the tube) and is fairly uncommon, although he drops the key every time. He also drops the 'Zherozsh's Ring' which is needed for the Ring of the Shissar quest to enter Emperor Ssraeshza's chambers. Once you are sure that someone in your raid force has the key, you'll proceed to the 3rd floor. www.eqatlas.com has a great set of maps for this zone if you're having a hard time getting there. From the zone-in, your guild will go through the door on the floor level against the right side of the room. Proceed forward and swim up the tube. Exit the tube and go through the door and you'll hit a 3-way dead end. Move your raid down the LEFT corridor until they reach its end, and then move them right into the next room. On the wall opposite you is a big white sarcophagus. Have a rogue pick this, if they cannot, try the key. Move through the hole in the wall that is revealed behind the overgrown casket, and swim up the final tube to the 3rd floor. From there, the CC room and the HP room are straight through the doorways that you see (the other hallway to the left leading to Xerkizh the Creator).

  • Buff Strategy: There is a trap in the room just prior to HP's room (the CC room) that will cast Curse of Rhag'Zadune on anyone in the vicinity. Its very annoying, as it overwrites the KEI line of spells and will slowly wear down your raid force. To avoid this, stay in the tube room prior to the CC room and have a monk pull that room clear and send a rogue in to keep the trap disarmed until the raid is in position (sorry, rogues - its boring but somebody has to do it!) The trap is located in the center of the room. Once the trap is under control, and the CC room is clear of all golems and chapel guards, move the raid to the far side of the room (next to HP's door) and keep them there. If anyone gets too close to the center of the room, they will activate the trap and waste more of the raid's time. Buff up here using the standard Symbol, Focus, and Blessing of the Nine routine and prepare for the most tedious part of this encounter - setting up the CC team.

  • Melee: High Priest's melee is pretty pitiful. He quads for about 600, but he is unslowable and he will rampage. He does, however, have quite a few hitpoints - in the 400k range.

  • Spells:
    Ssraeshza's Command: The High Priest will cast this AE spell every 27 seconds. It will inflict 100 damage to anyone that is hit and stun them for 9 seconds. It is more of an annoyance than a threat. It is only resistible with 200+ MR, but if you do things right, it is 100% avoidable by most of the raid force. The HP is located underneath a little gazebo-type construction and it has a few pillars holding it up. If your casters, rangers, and other ranged damage people can position themselves between the pillars and the HP, there are several "sweet spots" where they can maintain Line-of-Sight with the HP and the AE will not hit them at all.
    Sermon of Ssraeshza: This is the High Priest's single target DD. It should only be cast on your MT the entire fight, as he casts it often and it will inflict 300 damage, reduce AC by 150, and stun the target for 10 seconds. It is resistible with 150+ MR, but the MT should be grouped with a bard to increase his MR as much as possible so he doesn't get hit at all.

  • Crowd-Control Team: Easily the hardest part of this encounter is controlling the mobs in the HP's room. There are 12 mobs that spawn in his room on an extremely fast timer. As such, you will kill as few as possible and try and lock them down instead. Your CC team will consist of 2 - 4 enchanters, 4 offtanks, your necromancers, a shaman or two, and several healers. As you may have guessed, the 12 mobs are broken down as such: 4 mezzable, 4 slowable non-mezzable, and 4 undead banishable. When your raid is ready and against the HP's wall in the trap room, you'll begin to have your CC team pre-target there mobs by /assisting SoS rogues or people with Eye of Zomm. Your enchanters will need to target Zel'Zoszh, Zel'Zachum, Zel'Rekushoz and Zel'Krioz (referred to as "zels"). Your offtanks will need to target Ssraeshzian Terror Priest, Ssraeshzian Plague Priest, Ssraeshzian Death Priest and Ssraeshzian Blood Priest (referred to as "priests"). The priests will proc a single-target DD called Touch of Zebuxoruk which will decrease your offtank's max hitpoints by 400, decrease their AC by 60, and give them 9 curse counters. It is easily cured by Remove Curse, Greater Remove Curse, and the AA ability Radiant Cure, although it is not necessary until after the battle is over. Your necromancers will need to target Third Disciple of Rhag, Fourth Disciple of Rhag, Fifth Disciple of Rhag and Sixth Disciple of Rhag (referred to as "disciples"). Necros will be using their level 60 spell Banishment of Shadows on the disciples the moment they are pulled and each time they respawn during the fight, so be sure to have a good supply of star rubies, the spell's reagent, available. Now that all of the mobs are pre-targeted, pull them all into the CC room. Have your raid debuff them all immediately (tash, malos, malosinia and slow etc.) and get them into position. Bards can help enchanters mez the zels into the right corner of the room, and the offtanks need to move their mobs off to the left. While this is happening, the disciples should be being banished. Once the zels are locked down by the chanters, and the offtanks are set up with healers and a slower to keep the priests slowed, your raid is ready to move into the HP chamber and the hardest part of the encounter is now over.

  • High Priest Team: When the raid moves into the high priest's room, he may already begin casting his AE, but don't worry - he's rooted. Necros should follow the HP team in and stay in that room until the fight is over, banishing the disciple respawns during the fight. The Main Tank and Rampage Tank can shoot a few arrows at him to set rampage, while the rest of the raid moves into position (see maps below). Melees will move around behind the HP with clerics and other healers behind them. Casters and ranged damage will use the pillars to hide from the AE. Once everyone is in position, start the CH rotation and engage the HP as soon as possible. Go offensive when he is at about 70%, and burn him down. Be sure that your RT is getting adequate heals as well. Once he is dead, the entire raid needs to go back to the CC room (except for the necros) while loot is dealt with. Once looting is done, you can kill all of the CC mobs, or AE TL and be done with it.

  • Healing Strat: As mentioned above, you'll want one designated healer per offtank. A druid or cleric is the best choice, because the shaman will be busy slowing all of the mobs on incoming and reslowing them thereafter, and won't have time to heal at all. You'll also want a single cleric or druid watching over your enchanters, just in case.
