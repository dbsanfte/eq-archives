20020515
Recruiting Update

Thanks to some tremendously successful raids against Umbral Plains, the City of Mist, and Kael Arena, potential applicants have started to take notice of our "less is more" strategy for doing difficult encounters with tight-knit, well-controlled groups. As a result applications to be in the guild are coming in at a nice clip. The maximum number of people we'll take in any given month is twelve, however, so guildies should encourage top applicants to apply sooner rather than later. Applicants who are certain that they want to be a part of this guild should get their applications in ASAP. Next month, we'll be taking twelve more.

Phinigel Downed

Actus led the guild in a raid against Phinigel Autropos. The hardest thing about Phinigel is fighting him without his damned complete-healing guardians participating. Plan A was to pull him to zone via aggro+CoH, but that plan failed when CoH seemed to successfully clear all of Phinigel's aggro, returning him to his home point. Plan B was to have Rixalin, sexy Iksar she-monk, single pull him. We ended up with perhaps four complete-healing mobs on this single-pull because it is a very tricky monk pull. For Plan C we drew on the experience of Sopita, a level 60 wizard who had done Phinny many times in the past. Alas, hers was not a single-pull either, but we still managed to down him despite having two complete-healing seahorses in the mix as well. All guild members (whether you were there or not) should check the "post mortems" forum and give feedback on the raid so that the next time we do him our first plan will be our most successful one. Yaki (a raid guest) was awarded the Staff of Elemental Mastery: Water for his epic quest, Leatherneck was awarded the Fusible Ore for the Fusibility Research Quest and Vova (being the only Shaman on the take-down) was awarded a Rod of Malisement, but she first had to spam a Sailfish with the remaining seven charges on the Rod already in inventory.

20020425
Don't Trust the Spdat!
Numbers-oriented folks love to consume information from behind the Everquest user interface. They parse logs, they unpack maps, they search through files like spdat.eff to find out what spells really do. Thanks to a sharp-eyed Shaman, we've noticed that the spdat.eff files isn't as reliable as we thought it was, or at least that's what Verant's spell dude would have us believe. FUD or for real?

Here's the data from a recent addition to spdat.eff for a spell called "Ultra Death Spell."

Oh come on, you know that there is no such thing as an ultra death spell in this game. This is EQ - we balance things as well as we can. No tank-mages. No uber class. Etc. etc. Thanks for asking though. BTW - what are you doing reading this file? - Geoffrey Z.

Just a FYI - I do practice poor mans encryption. This means that I intentionally fill in some unused fields with bad data. We also cap things in code and then display different data here. In other words, don't trust stuff you read in here. Why? Read UDS III.

Why give bad data? Because: (a) players shouldn't be reading this, (b) we don't want to spoil the mystery - and hard numbers do that.

Therefore, expect to continue to see numbers change in spell.dat - this doesn't mean the real spell changes - just the info here.
