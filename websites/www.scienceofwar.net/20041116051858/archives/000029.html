Anatomy of a Brag Post / Agnarr Punked

Why do guilds always include screenshots of dead mobs in their home pages? For a few reasons, I think. First, they need to prove to all of the doubters that they actually killed what they say they killed. I'm sure that at one point people were saying, "You can't kill Naggy. No fuckin' way. He's not supposed to die!" Another reason for these posts is pride. People don't take pride in telling others that they vanquished an easy foe. But killing a difficult one is something that we naturally want our peers and competitors to know about. And finally, it's about nostalgia. It's nice to have a memento from the big event. It's like passing around vacation photos at work when you get back.

However, it is interesting what these posts almost never show. They're almost completely devoid of emotion. No smiling faces, no flag babes to kiss, nada. It's strangely sterile, especially considering how absolutely jazzed up you feel after winning a victory that takes such miracles of coordination and concentrated effort. So here I am asking for some webcam people to set up a cam and screenshot what REALLY matters next time we punk a mob. Capture some emotion and let me put it up on the page. Maybe we can show some victory posts with heart.

Oh yeah... we killed Agnarr. The teamwork was incredible. Fewer than 50 guild members were present for the kill. Every member counted. And last week we punked the second Ancient (new name for a Warder in the redesigned ST) with 24 folks. The third and fourth ancients will fall soon.
