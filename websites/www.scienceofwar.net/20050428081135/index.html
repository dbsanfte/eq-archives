Guild News - March 31, 2005
Guild News - March 31, 2005

"The characteristic of heroism is its persistency. All men have wandering impulses, fits, and starts of generosity. But when you have chosen your part, abide by it, and do not weakly try to reconcile yourself with the world. The heroic cannot be the common, nor the common the heroic."
- Ralph Waldo Emerson

A lot has happened since the last guild news update. We have a new site banner (visible in the forums...the front page hasn't been updated yet), a new recruitment process, several new officers, and a big game patch. Intrepid professors of the SoW School of Advanced Tactical Studies have ventured deep into Dire Maul and have emerged with bags of loot and stories aplenty. Meanwhile, the remainder of the guild has been leveling up rapidly. It looks like a large portion of the guild will reach 60 by the early half of April, way ahead of schedule. Larger guild raids aren't far away.

New Members

The guild has adopted changes to the application review process, based on our experience with handling apps so far. Applicants are now given a membership interview by guild officers after two weeks of initiate status. Based on the results of the interview, the feedback of the guild membership, and the involvement of the initiate we then decide to boot the player, give them another two weeks, or promote them to full member. This has helped us move through applications at a faster pace.

Since the last news update, we have promoted four five players to full member status:
  • Razorr - Hunter
  • Najira - Priest
  • Figleaf - Druid
  • Junckey - Rogue
  • Ireg - Warrior
These new members have all demonstrated adaptability, intelligence, and skill. Congratulations and welcome to the ranks!

New Officers

Science of War is proud to announce the promotion of two members to the officership:
  • Phobeus - Recruitment Officer
  • Softwinna - Recruitment Officer
Professors Phobeus and Softwinna have been assigned the task of recruiting new applicants, receiving applications, and monitoring initiates. So far their results have been fantastic and there are a lot of new names in our applicant channel.

Being an officer in SoW is a bit different from your standard guild. We don't grant officer status as a reward for long service or to favorite members. Each officer fills a specific role defined by a job description and a set of monthly requirements. Any member can volunteer for an office, but they are responsible for meeting certain goals and quality standards to retain the position. Officers are then "paid" for their duties (usually through a periodic DKP bonus). This system forms an officer corps that shares the work involved in building and running a top tier guild.

Membership Requirements

We are now under phase 2 of the Guild Level Calendar. Applications will be accepted for players of any level, but initiates must be at least level 35 in order to qualify for full membership. There's still plenty of time for applicants from other servers to create a character on Azjol-Nerub and play with us!

Guild Level Calendar
Science of War is a nascent raiding guild with aspirations to participate in high-end content. In order to do that, we're going to need everyone in the guild to be level 60 by September 01, 2005.

Here's the schedule for getting there:


Date.......... Required Level
2005 Mar 01... 20
2005 Apr 01... 35
2005 May 01... 45
2005 Jun 01... 50
2005 Jul 01... 55
2005 Aug 01... 58
2005 Sep 01... 60


APPLICANTS: You will need to keep up with the level calendar to earn a guild invite. If you are behind the calendar we cannot invite you but will instead allow you to remain an "initiate" until you catch up with us.

GUILD MEMBERS: If you are below the level calendar at any given date, you will not be able to participate in guild events. For instance, if you are below level 45 on or after May 01, you won't be invited to any guild raids that might happen after that date. This means that you will not earn DKP either.

Recruiting Has Begun
Now that the website is set up (mostly), we're beginning to kick recruiting into overdrive. Here's an overview of the SoW recruiting process.

How a Recruit Becomes a Member

  1. The recruit should read as much as possible About Science of War. Recruits need to understand who we are and what we're about. We're definitely not the right guild for everyone! We tend to attract focused, mature players who want more out of their gaming experience.


  2. If you're still interested, stop by the forums and read the application instructions.


  3. Fill out an application!


  4. After you have filled out an application, get in touch with SoW in the game. We'll invite you to the guild as an "Initiate" immediately. As an initiate, you will have no access to guild chat, but you will have a Science of War tag. Your evaluation period begins once you become an initiate. (For more information about how the guild is run, see "Who's running the show around here?".


  5. We then observe your behavior and try to get to know you to see if you'd be a good fit. You will be invited to guild events and in general should make every effort to be there.


  6. If we like you and you like us, you will be invited to the guild as a full member. This is where the fun starts.
