--LOOTING
   Every Guild pretty much has some sort of Policy implemented for whatever specific reason.  I don't ask too much from my Guild, and so the only REAL policy there is here is that of "looting" during raids or just plain 'ol XP hunting.  Now that about half of us are getting to the point where we can actually hold a camp, and receive items that are of worth, I've taken the liberty to come up with a loot policy.  I simply ask that you exercise good judgement when you loot from a monster felled during a combined effort on the behalf of you and fellow members. It is good practice to give whatever you can to help your fellow brethren, this is a great sign of compassion and charity, and is highly valued in our Circle. During Dungeon Campaigns, designated 'looters' will be assigned. You MUST abide by these rules. You are *not* to loot during this time. When you all return safely to an area of safety, the entire horde of treasure *will be* shared by all those who attending the mission. Excess equipment *should be* donated to the Ring of Merchants, or to a less fortunate member of our Circle. This guild is rich in resources, and what makes it rich is the members within.  The key to our success is sharing and making sure that everyone is taken care of...so help your fellow brother or sister.

--CODE OF CONDUCT
  Common Sense.  Fire and Brimstone has an awesome reputation, and I to this day have had no problems with complaints from OTHER guilds.  I would like to keep it that way.  Always use Common Sense when adventuring, and give people a reason to trust you.  Members of Fire and Brimstone are loyal, dedicated, honorable, responsible, professional, caring, brave, honest, giving, (and the list goes on and on), and this is attributed to our success.  Remember a few of these characteristics, and you shall be honored by others.

--POWER LEVELING
   Something that's needed to be addressed.  Just because you are IN a guild, does not mean that higher ups are under obligation to "Power Level" you.  Do not mistake someone's (for lack of better words) Giving-ness, for duty.  IT IS NOT OUR JOB!  We do it out of the kindness of our hearts, so do not abuse it.

--HAVING FUN...
   Generally, a small guild does not remain small because it is incapable of attracting members, but because it wishes to remain small. Therefore, it is extremely selective in its choice of members, if it seeks new members at all.  This holds true to Fire and Brimstone.  The day Fire and Brimstone becomes a massive guild, is the day we forget our friends.  Being in a small guild means that I know every member personally. The members of a small guild become close companions and good friends, in a way that a larger guild is unlikely to enjoy. In a small guild, one can come to know every member so well that one may trust each of them absolutely, with life and with honor.  Being in a small guild means that we play Everquest to be with one another...not to farm items, not to hold camps for days, not to conquer. We play because we enjoy one another's company.  Conquest or wealth may come, but it is incidental to the enjoyment we gather on the way.  One day we just might be an "UBER" guild, with massive numbers and members reaching from Norrath to Luclin, but if you ask me, we're already "UBER".  Being in a small guild means that we must each prize our reputation, and our reputation says it all...
