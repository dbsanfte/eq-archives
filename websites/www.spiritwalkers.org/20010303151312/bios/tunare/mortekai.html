Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Mortekai Spiritwalker
Race: Barbarian Northman
Profession: Shaman of Justice. Judge, counsel, jury, and executioner.
Likes: Helping others to find the spirit world. Bringing justice. Elven women, new faces, and the joy of battle.
Dislikes: Giants of all kinds, kEwl d00dz
Quirks: Given to deep reflection, and bursts of generosity.

I am Mortekai Spiritwalker, born to a family which has know both triumph and tragedy. Gather 'round ye who would listen, for I tell a tale now of History and Destiny.

Grown am I now to be a man of my middle years, respected by my community, loved by my family, and feared by the unjust. Yet it was not always so in my youth. .

I was a third child, born many a year after my sister Edain, and only two short years past the still-birth of my father's only other son. Edain was the star of my parent's heart, for I was not the strapping warrior my father had hoped to follow him. Instead, I was a small lad, given to mischief and sloth, sneaking away from my lessons with the sword and shield, and finding for myself many forms of trouble.

Since I was the last of my parent's children, I was left in the care of Edain when they passed from this realm to the next, safely guided and rewarded by The Tribunal. Edain's power with the spirits grew and grew, as did her love of a mighty warrior, Culbarth.They were married, and I continued to live in their shadow, having no claim of my own to glory or honor.

Edain and Culbarth had three children, Joril, Calamon, and Sanu. And when disaster struck, taking from me my sister and her husband, I was left, a young man, to raise three small children. This was to be the seminal event in my life.

Responsibility, listener, will change your very soul. Where before there had been no calls upon me for anything, I now had to provide for mouths and bellies that were never full. I learned the value of labor, the need to set a good example and more, the wealth that is the love of a child. So much was I changed that I grew in respect in my home of Halas. And with the respect of others came a sense of self-worth, pride and well being. The conditions were right for me to finally hear the spirits, and slowly, I became aware of them, and the powers they would eventually provide me.

But my story does not end here, for this is only the beginning of my tale. At the prompting of the spirits, I traveled to far Qeynos, lending my meager healing powers to fight an awful plague, unleashed by the followers of Bertoxxulos. Many died, and the death was a lingering foulness that ate the life out of its victims.

Relatives, one's of which I had been unaware, died there. They left a daughter, Morrighan, and none of her other family would take her in.

"Touched, she is," they said, "filled with the darkness!"

"Help her, for she is to you as shadow is to light, the darkness with defines the day, " whispered the spirits.

I was moved. I knew I could not leave this child alone in the world, and so I adopted her, and raised the child with the other three.

Now my children are grown, and I am a man of my middle years, tempered by labor, love and seasoned with wisdom. The spirits are my guides, my power, and my life. I travel the land, I help those in need. And perhaps, just perhaps, I show some the truth. Those that see it may someday join me as a Spiritwalker.
