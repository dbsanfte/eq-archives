Spiritwalkers Guild Member Bio

Name: Jilliany MacGrove
Race: Barbarian Northman
Profession: Shaman

Dislikes:
Quirks:

It is difficult for me to talk about my past even though my life has changed dramatically in the past ten years. However, I think the members of the Spiritwalkers that I have asked to trust and befriend me should know all that entails.

Somewhere in the deep frozen mountains of Everfrost veiled by magic from all human eyes lies a tower and fortress. Inside that cold fixture of marble resides a cruel and evil woman. Her name is Jaide, and to call her mother would be a sacrilege, but she is the woman who gave me life. Although I have never witnessed it I have been told that once upon a time, Jaide was someone I would have liked. Unfortunately I find this hard to believe.

Jaide was a shaman and very skilled at the craft but she was consumed by the need for power. She did not seek monetary power but knowledge, and control over all off things living and dead. In her travels and adventures, Jaide became acquainted with a rogue tribe of Dark Elf necromancers. As she studied with them her power became twisted and so did her mind. She abandoned all the teachings of the tribunal and the forces she had once used for good were now turned for her evil purposes. By the time I was two, Jaide had been banished by the tribunal never to step foot in the lands of the barbarians again by punishment of death.

The next eight years of my life are a blur, rejected by a mother who hated the very sight of me. I was isolated from anyone but the other necromancers and dark elves who studied with her. Residing in a home where even the servants were perverted undead attendants, I have tried not to remember any of it.

My one comfort in life was a locket that played a soft lullaby and contained a picture of my grandfather and my uncle. As a child I knew nothing of the locket except that it helped me maintain my sanity. Jaide had told me that the pictures were of my grandfather and uncle, Jared and Jac MacGrove.

Jaide despised me mostly because she saw me as a nuisance but also because I refused to be her apprentice. This was not a stand that made her happy. I refused to help her in her experiments and was often flogged for my brazen way of offending her guests. Regardless of what Jaide sanctioned even then I knew what she was doing, her magic, even her very soul was evil and sinister.

I would often confront and beseech her to send me to my father if my presence disturbed her so much. I n fact I would often beg her just to tell me who my father was and where he was. As a child I even imagined that he might be Cazic-Thule or worse yet Bertoxxulous. One evening when I was ten, I could not stand Jaide's ranting any longer. I opened my locket in an attempt to silence her droning with its sweet lullaby. She screamed at me and tried to snatch the locket from my neck. To my amazement and hers the locket released a bolt of energy than threw her across the room.

"Hand me that locket," Jaide demanded.

"No, I will not," I said shaking my head and clutching the necklace with both hands to protect it from her grasp.

"Then, you will die!" Jaide screamed

"That is hardly a threat for death would be preferable to remaining here a moment longer," I screamed back.

"So be it." I felt my life force ebb from me as I blacked out.

Days later I awoke in a bed made of bear skins and I heard the voice of a very old man say, "sip the broth slowly, deary."

"Where am I and who are you? Is this the hereafter?" I queried.

"No my dear" he chuckled, "I am Greywolfe a very old friend of your grandfather and uncle. I created that locket for you even before you were born. Your grandfather knew your mother would eventually loose her mind, and knew that if you opposed her evil she would try to destroy you. In that case the locket was to bring you back here. Unfortunately your grandfather has passed on but I have sent for your Uncle. He and his wife will be here in a few months to collect you, until then you can enjoy my hospitality and that of my village."

"What of Jaide?" I asked.

"She believes you dead, felt your life force leave you herself she did, or thinks she did," he added with a wink of his eye. "Then she saw you teleport away, convinced that her magic was stronger than your grandfather's. And she would be right about that but it is not more powerful than mine is. Sadly I do not think she will look for you for she is evil and cares for nothing but herself. I know that must cause you much pain little one."

"No sir," I stated with great resolve. "It, it causes me only relief and joy for you have shown me more tenderness in the last 10 minutes that Jaide did in the last 10 years." With that statement, I cried myself back to sleep as he held me in his arms.

During the next four months as I awaited my Uncle Jac and his new wife Rosin, I found that I was in the village of the Tribal barbarians of the far north tundra. There I learned what it was like to be a child, with no cares and responsibilities. I still swear that the first time I ever heard laughter was when I saw a boy of 12, wrestling in the snow with his pet wolf. I soon found out that he was Greywolfe's grandson Wolfegar. For the time I remained in his village Wolfegar showed me everything and taught me that life could be a joy not a torture, as only another child could do.

In the ten years since my rescue, for that is how I see it anyway, my life has been radically different. My uncle and aunt, are the salt of the earth and as they themselves are childless, have made everyday of my life with them one of joy and pleasure. Their love for me abounds and my love for them, I think of them as my parents for I have known no others in that manner.

I also found as I grew that my grandfather Jared MacGrove was as revered by the Shamans of Six Hammers as Jaide was despised by them. I also discovered that the magic in my blood is as great as the force that possessed my grandfather and Jaide. At first I feared this power and was convinced that it would consume me as it did Jaide. But my uncle and the Shamans of the Council have shown me that this logic is flawed. It appears that the locket was in itself a test of my purity of heart. When my mother first began her tainted rituals she was pregnant with me. My grandfather and all the elders were not sure if her dark ceremonies would corrupt only herself or me, her unborn child as well. They believed that if I remained steadfast and good of heart the locket would protect me and activate when it became necessary to remove me from Jaide's terrorizing. My very presence in Halas is proof of my worthiness to follow in my grandfather's footsteps.

My uncle has done two acts that have made me confident in my control of my gifts. First, he bestowed to me my grandfather's armor and weapon to use as I adventure. By doing this I believe he as given me a link to my grandfather's goodness and power. Second, he introduced me to Duncaan Macleod, one of my grandfather's last pupils. I have accepted Duncaan as my mentor and through his teaching and wise council I have learned much of my grandfather and his ways.

When Wolfegar's clan was destroyed it was natural for him to come to Halas and seek out my family. Secretly, I believe it was Jaide and her evil companions who destroyed the village and killed his people. I fear Greywolfe was wrong about Jaide's power. I believe that she knows I am alive and since she destroyed Greywolfe in the raid, now fears only me.

Yes, fears me, for I am sure that she believes that I will be the only person who can ever destroy her. This is why she tried for so many years to corrupt my spirit as she had done her own. She knows, she failed and knows that I still live. She feels my presence and goodness haunting her just as her vile evil soul haunts me. I believe the only way I will ever truly have peace is for her to die. I am convinced she feels the same way about me.

I loathe undead and the art of necromancy. I will go out of my way to destroy their evil. I also will never trust a dark elf for the memories of those that tortured me are too vivid.

I still do not know who my father was. I do not even know if he is alive but I continue to search for someone who can answer these questions for me. Unfortunately I think this secret died with my grandfather. I do know that he was an honorable man, who turned from Jaide when she first corrupted her soul and never knew of my existence. I often wonder what he would think of me if we were to meet today.
