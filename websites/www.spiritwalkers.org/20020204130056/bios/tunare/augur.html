  Augur walked into the darkened tavern, the smell of cooking meat melding imperfectly with the crude makings of the locally-brewed ale setting his already battle-tempered nerves on edge.  He was in a mood for violence, and hungry graves begged for tenants.

  Even from the threshold doorway he could see them staring at him; his imposing form filling the entrance, his mighty axe "Warbringer" held ridged in his hand in defiance of the town ordinance against openly bared weapons.  All eyes were set upon him; this Angel of Death standing at the entrance to this foul den.  He returned the glare from the tavern common room, a glare set upon him by the masses of drunkard humans who stood or lay before him in lewd positions.  Under different circumstances he would have been just another adventurer looking to wash the trail dust from his throat with a tankard of honey mead, but tonight was different; tonight he was in the den of evil, the tavern known as "The Black Lodge", home of the vermin followers of the Plague Lord.

  He stood at the doorway leading into the main room of the tavern, smiling slightly as he thought about Brother Trintle, the mouth of the darkling demigod, who gave his knowledge of this foul place after being "coaxed" by Warbringer.  Augur remembered the harsh blood-frothed words issuing from Trintle’s crushed throat, Warbringer imbedded deep within the monk’s chest.  To the victor comes the spoils of war, and information flowed easily;the lair, the guild-master, the trade in slave flesh, The Black Lodge as the center of it all in a fair city.

  His gaze swept across the room for what seemed to be an endless time.  He stared at each member of the cursed Order of Disease, allowing his sleepy-eyed look to pause at each of them and continue on.  Outside, a storm howled, as if in anticipation of the carnage to follow; shutters barely holding against the violence of the storm.

  "It is time" he thought to himself.

With a muttering of evil laden words, the crowd rushed him.  First to die was the bartender wielding a cask-opening pick.  The man slashed pitifully at Augur, who spun his axe a full circle and removed the bartender’s weapon and hands.  Continuing the motion, Augur swept the axe blade across the man’s throat, spraying crimson blood, impaling him with the terminal spike on Warbringer’s top.

  Augur hefted the lifeless corpse over his back, freeing Warbringer to slice down on the next opponent, slashing him from shoulder blade to groin, splitting him in twain.  Warbringer danced through the air singing it's sweet song of whistling death. With a backhand motion from its wielder, Warbringer slashed two opponents through the midsection, hewing cleanly through them, only to return to a parry position against a whistling rapier.

  Remembering his training with the Wolves of the North, Augur allowed the rapier to come close to his chest, then spun Warbringer once to free this delicate rouge’s weapon from its masters hand to spin across the room.  Warbringer came down on the now defenseless man’s head and buried itself deep within his chest.

  Augur's heavy crafted boot struck against the dead man’s midsection; tossing it several feet into the advancing crowd, freeing Warbringer.  In a semi-circular flourish Augur changed his hand-hold on the axe, steppingg back into battle position for the next tide of cultists who rushed him still.

  He felt the unholy might of the Dark Lord’s presence slam against his barbarian fortitude; a priest of the Plague God chanting foul words of contempt, invoking a might filled enchantment against him.  Augur reeled in the pain as magical energies coalesced, witch-lighting his hidden tattoos of faith in Rallos Zek beneath his blued Centaur-crafted amour. Llight issued from beneath his breastplate, illuminating his face in the death grimace of the reaper.  Augur screamed a challenge to the dark god in his native tongue as he laid Warbringer into the priest.

  Sparks flowed like the death-scream of a thousand will-o-wisps from the vile plague bringer’s chest as Warbringer struck deeply.  Augur allowed Warbringer to rest momentarily in the dead man, and drew an ancient dagger from his shoulder harness, only to bury it in the throat of another pitiful follower of Disease.  Spinning around on his heel, Augur frees his mighty war-axe and dagger, swinging both in a wide arc to meet his next opponent.

  In waves, in single combat, in teams of two and three they came. The floor of the tavern became slick with the lifeblood of the Rat Prince's minions. One after another Augur slew them, with precision and speed. 

  Filled with the rage of mighty Rallos, Augur battled on. Though sorely wounded, none were so grievous as to stop him and his mission. He battled on, reveling in the blood and carnage, alternately singing or screaming vows to Rallos Zek making an opera of death, the crowd obliging in morbid participation.

  None, save one, survived that eve.

  The thick oak door of the tavern opened, the cruel wind entering the tavern battering the tallow candles into submission.  Standing in the center of the carnage was Augur, facing the city guard of Qeynos, the servants of Antonious Bayle; their helms pulled low in a futile effort to protect them from the stench of death permeating the room.  Augur stared at them from within his blood-drenched massive helm, chest heaving from the long combat.

  For a moment the guards stared blankly at the crimson soaked barbarian standing in front of them.  Then, with a smile that a groom might give his bride on their wedding night, they respectfully bowed, backed out of the tavern, and closed the door behind them.

  Augur raised Warbringer in salute to Rallos Zek and prayed thankfully.  Dropping to one knee he offered the fallen to Zek, offered himself as the engine of death and retribution, offered all that he owned and was.

  With a swift motion, the mighty barbarian kicked a remaining cask into the fireplace, setting the tavern ablaze, sanctifying it with cleansing fire, destroying the foul, misguided occupants.

  From afar, heartbeats later, Augur sat on horseback and watched the tavern burn to the ground, and smiled.
