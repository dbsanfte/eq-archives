And the race is on!

This is a short look into one of the races that may study the grand art of Enchantments.

today's race Dark Elf (Tier'dal):
The underground dark elf city of Neriak is as large as beautiful, with a moderate amount of crowding the three zones are relatively easy to navigate once you get used to it. Neriak heralds as the only city designed for other races in mind as well as the Tier'dal (Dark elf), thier city boasts a foreign quarters with areas for trolls, ogres and even evil humans to purchase needed items. Everywhere you go there are protective runes scribed on walls or simply floating in the air.

Dark Elves begin their existence with a good Intelligence and therefore are very well suited for a life as a spell caster and of a tradesman they also posses the agility to defend themselves in hand to hand combat as seen in the stats below. Also Dark Elves have infravision for seeing at night. One of the largest bonuses for the Dark elf is that the only place to purchase the Enchant Metals spells are in thier cities library proving that the Dark elves founded as much or more of the arcane arts as the Erudites. As far as Enchanter races go the dark elf have the best all around benifits.

Spell of the week

Pendril's Animation

Level: (1)one
Where Found: found in your guild or in Highhold Keep
Description: this is the Enchanter's first pet spell
Required ingreadents: one tiny dagger(bought in your guild)

Damage: this pet strikes as a short sword for damage and has an average fighting ability

Special: though this is a pet spell it will not react to the pet commands and will only defend you.

Notes:
This is the beginning Pet spell of the Enchanter class. This will be your personal tank for the fisrt 3 levels or so. I suggest getting this spell as soon as possible your combat value will rise. Imagine you have your own personal fighter type with you at all times. For lower creatures allow them to take a swipe at you then back off as your pet ties them up, now shallow breath the monster and watch his HPs fall.

Combat Strategies

We recived this from Lady Delnara, hope it helps

We being Enchanters, we have the most control and selection of "What do we
want to do next." :)

An adventure I had back in my 12-13th year, if you play your cards right,
you can accomplish almost anything.

Here is a story of how I defeated 3 Lv 14-15 Orcs by myself.

One day while venturing through the vast area of Butcher Block Mountians, I
found myself straying off the path to a beautiful pinnicle off in the distance.
Upon my arrival, it appeared that the beauty was infact a Lure to what can be
certian death. I saw Orcs standing guard. Turns out that this place is one of their shrines.

Hmm... I thought for a moment and after considering the odds, I decided to
gear up for battle. Eventhough I love my pet, I knew that my faithful friend might not
be a good Idea at this time, for there were 3 powerful Orcs and one of them was a
caster.

I dispelled my pet by making it think I left him(casted INV on myself)
*sigh* I could not bear to tell him he was usless for this battle. If you think of the
odds, 3 Orcs vs you and your pet, you better be good. I looked at it as 2 Orcs vs me
and my soon to be friend. Thus making the odds 2 vs 2 instead of 3 on 2.

Cracking my fingers and beaming a smile at their Orcish Oracle, I started
to cast. Dancing and Smiling, saying Elvin words of Rejoyce, I was able to "Charm"
their Oracle. Quickly as my new friend started to make his way to join me, the
other 2 Orcs attacked him. My heart started to race as I watched them fight, I wasn't
sure how long my friends moral was going to stay(the Charm), so I started Dancing
again, recasting the same Elvin words of Rejoyce just to make sure his moral would
hold. Alas he was losing, so I decided to give him a hand.

Clapping my hands and with a few thoughs, I Envoked the power to smolder
the enemy's mind.(Chaotic Feedback) The Orc went insane as it started to walk away
with its brains smoldering, just to find my friend on it's back. I cheered on my Friend as
he slew the one and started fighting the other.

My friend was about to die, so quickly I told the Enemy to Dance! (casting
Whirl till you Hurl) and the Orc started to spin :) It is funny to see such large
clumsy Creatures trying to dance. I backed off and watched. It was a close fight, but alas
my friend died, and this severly weakened Orc started making way to me! I tried to
Envoke powers to aid me, but alas I fizzled and became drained. (OOM) I closed my Eyes
and took a swing with my Staff... Thawk! I hit um right up side the head! He turned
and started to run. I gave chase and started beating him over the back of his head. I
was able to slay him.

After cheering victoriously, I started to make my way home after I
collected my winnings.

Farewell and Gods Speed!
Lady Delnara

Spells used: Charm, Whirl till u hurl, and Chaotic feedback
Editor's note: I personally would not have attempted chaos feedback on him as he closed with me i would have attempted Color Flux as he got near, stunned creatures die so nicely.
Great combat stategy though, Cudos.

My idea for fixing the resist problems with enchanters.
Enchanters can be very powerful chars, some say perhaps the most powerful,
but the main limit is that they cannot effect RED creatures at all.
This becomes a major problem for a few reasons.
1. Most groups hunt RED's for decent exp.
2. The role of the enchanter is to make a diffucult to impossible kill,
possible.
3. As a dedicated casting class, it should be a stronger caster than say a
druid, or Shaman.

My solution to this is simple.
Make it so that the Enchanters "de-buff" spells effect the same range of
creatures as the Wizard's attack spells.
As it is the Enchanter is only useful to a group for Buffing, and that
usefullness is marginal, as a Druid, or Shaman would do better, as they
could fight better, and also heal.

Bottom line, the Enchanter would not be another Combat spell master, instead
he would lay in the backround controlling things like a master pupeteer. As
it is now, this can only marginally be done with White or Blues, oddly
Greens resist at a very high rate.

Jewelry:
Needs major vamping, as it is now, its a loose loose, situation, that only
pays when you can use gold, and pump as many points as you can into the
skill. Other than that it is way to exspensive, and to useless, let me
explain.
I did a test with a level 34 that dedicated over 200plat to figuring out
this skill.
Our first tests were with the cheapest gems and silver, well after using
about 50 plat, and not getting any magical items, I asked around and found
that you had to use the Pearl and up.
Basically limiting me to make +5 mana rings.
Oddly I was not able to sell even one ring to a "PC" it just didnt have the
value, also the SV disease, and poison were equally useless, as there are
magical items that serve other purposes, that do the same.
Solution:
It should be scale based.
That is, a Hematite Magic Silver ring should produce a +2 stat in increase,
this would be about as valuable as a Cuttthroat ring, while not profitable,
you could atleast break even.
Electrum +5

Plat +15
The scale of diffulculty should also be harder.
Thus at higher levels, you would really be a usefull and profitable on the
open market, and you could afford the climb.

Also the Enchant Ore I have never seen, nor seen any reference, to it, that
would be very desirable, atleast if the Enchanter is useless in group combat
atleast they would have a skill value.

Tides of Magic

PRELUDE


The sun danced on the horizon making the water sparkle as if imbued with magic, Toorac could make out the docks of Qeynos if he squinted his eyes almost hard enough to hurt. He did not look much different than the other passengers upon the Seaking other than his bright yellow robes that marked him as an Enchanter and his slightly larger forehead. As the mighty vessel drifted in to make contact with the dock many of the sailors where shouting in what Toorac knew to be common tongue, but what they said was so filled with slang that he knew not a single word.
"ALL ASHORE WHATS GOING ASHORE" shouted a large burly sailor.
With that the passengers moved toward the ramp which lead to the dock, some more quickly than others and some holding their stomachs displaying a unique shade of green. Toorac moved without urgency toward the ramp keeping an impassive face to hide his disgust of being in proximity to so many low humans. Normally he would not refrain from his haughty Erudite attitude but his current mission called for him to blend into the crowd, even if the crowd was filled with low humans.
Toorac allowed the crowd to guide him through the busy streets of Qeynos, past all the vendors shouting their wares and the common people haggling for their next meal. The cobbled streets were small, unclean and the drab buildings were crowded together, he noticed, amused that it was as if the streets and buildings where a parity of their creators within the crowd.
Keeping careful watch on the street names Toorac slowly shifted his path, almost imperceivably manipulating his position within the crowd. Anyone watching him would have seen a lost erudite caught up in a sea of drably dressed humans. But one set of eyes followed his every move as they drifted through the oblivious crowd not far from their target. The stranger seemed to read Toorac's mind shifting with him as he navigated the crowds. Finding the street he wanted Toorac slipped out of the crowd with the grace of a cat and found himself alone on the side street. For the first time since the Seaking docked Toorac noticed that the sun had crested and the torches lining the streets where causing the shadows to dance merrily upon the building walls. Toorac felt a shiver pass his spine and instantly scanned the deserted street, Nothing. After gathering his composure Toorac continued down the dimly lit street and listening to the timed scrape of his shoes on the cobbles quickly lost himself in thought, never noticing the quiet shadow moving and dancing within the others upon the wall.
Before long Toorac found himself standing before a large home, bright blue banners hung from the third level portraying a family crest of a unicorn overlapped by a jewel encrusted sword. Toorac smiled, this was the emblem he was looking for. Casually shifting his position into the shadows he noticed the two guards standing watch within the recessed foyer.
"Now for the wait" whispered Toorac moving deeper into the solid shadows and adjusting his stance so as to lean against one of the cold brick walls of the sleeping buildings. Again Toorac missed the slight motion of his predator shifting himself into a more comfortable position as the night closed over both of them.

It had felt to Toorac as if he had been waiting for hours and that the sun would surely rise any moment ,but in truth it had been no more than an hour and a half he had been waiting. He was almost to his breaking point when he heard someone walking in his direction whistling an extremely annoying tune.
"Finally" Toorac moved his lips and the sound barely escaped.
The figure moved at a gingerly pace down the dim street, but even at this distance it was easy for Toorac to recognize the figure in the dark red robes as a wizard. Toorac studied him committing every detail of the old man to memory, he would need this for the next step in his plan.
"Greetings Ashlak, blast any hill giants lately old man." the first guard said breaking into a grin and nudging the second guard in the ribs.
"Why yes I did Guard Rilek, and I believe he said he was your cousin just before he died" the wizard beamed back with a great smile. The first guard's faced dropped in surprise though the second guard was now leaning upon his spear hilt in attempt not to fall over laughing.
The first guard regained some of his wits, "Quickly now old man, the master of the house will not wait forever" he retorted casting a sideways glare at the second guard who even at that moment was losing his battle with gravity and sliding down the wall laughing.

Toorac took no pleasure in the laughter but did use the noise to mask his movement as he slipped further into the dark side street. The enchanter found a Pitch black nook within an unknown shops wall, he carefully slid into the protective darkness and settled in for his next task.
Toorac began to mutter Strange words just barley above his breath, yet if anyone where present they would swear the words echoed with a unearthly tone causing them to scream into their mind. He waved his graceful hands and a dim light followed their path creating glowing runes that seemed to float in the air before him. The dim light set his features into a sinister glow as he opened his eyes to survey the runes. Then with one quick motion he pushed the palm of his hands outwards smashing rune into glowing rune, the very fabric of reality seemed touched by his spell as the area he stood seemed to ripple as a pond would if a child through a pebble into it. Nodding in satisfaction Toorac began concentrating, remembering as his hands began to weave another rune within the ripples and once again he smashed the new rune with the palm of his hands. The ripples began to gain momentum growing to waves crashing upon realities shore itself, each wave held a slight change to Toorac's form. Wave after wave the second form seemed to overlay the first and each ripple held a different face, first that of an enchanter from a far off land the next an old human with a jovial look. Finally the waves calmed but where once stood a young Erudite in a flowing yellow robe now stood an old man in a dark red one.

Toorac, satisfied with his new visage moved back toward his previous hiding spot closer to the street. As he arrived he found the two usual guards still at their posts, the first guard seemed sullen and was not looking at the second who still chuckled to himself ever once in a while. Toorac was raising his hands to begin his next spell when he got the shiver again, a moment of panic gripped him, had someone seen his casting? Able to calm himself Toorac thought "No, no one is here but myself and the two guards" and with his fragile confidence rebuilt he returned to his duties. Once again he scribed runes but these runes were very much darker casting almost no light at all and were very much quicker to scribe. Once the runes were done Toorac's ancient brow creased as he searched first for the minds of the guards then finding them he sought a single memory in a sea of thoughts, satisfied he had found the memory he sought Toorac waved his wrinkled hand and scaterred the runes before him.
Both guards stood stunned for a second, eyes blank as if they were staring into a viod that noone else could see. Toorac knew that his que as an actor in this play was at hand, gathering his robes into one hand he scurried across the street toward the stunned guards. Slowing his pace and casting a sideways glance he positioned himself a few meters infront of the guards.

The guards seemd to snap back to reality with no recollection of the moment of time they were frozen.
"Greetings Ashlak, blast any hill giants lately old man." the first guard said breaking into a grin and nudging the second guard in the ribs.
Toorac took this as a good sign his memory blur had taken now if it would only hold. This close to the guards he found his source had not lied each guard had a monicule of sight over one eye, his invisability spell would have been useless against them. Toorac instatly filed the rest of the information given to him the magical traps and alarms that would make entry and possibly rapid exit much harder.
"Why yes I did Guard Rilek, and I belive he said he was your cousin just before he died" Toorac knew to make the spell hold perminant he must overlay this moment exactly upon the other. The first guard's faced dropped once again in surprise and the second guard began his struggle not to break out laughing.
The first guard once again recoverd, "Quickly now old man, the master of the house will not wait forever" he retorted casting a sideways glare at the second gaurd who was again sliding down the wall laughing. But Toorac noticed a strange look pass on the first guards face, he recognized the sideeffect of the spell, "De'ja vu", the thought that one had been here or done this before. Toorac grinned allowing the guards to think it was because he had won the discussion when in fact he was pleased the spells held so solid.
