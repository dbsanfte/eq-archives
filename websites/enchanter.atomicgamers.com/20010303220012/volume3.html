Enchanter's Announcement

And the race is on!

This is a short look into one of the races that may study the grand art of Enchantments.

today's race Human (freeport):
The human city of Freeport is made up of a rebel faction of humans and is built on the edge of the desert of Ro. though I have spent little time here I find this city less crowded and less confusing (though I still get lost quite often) than that of its sister city Qeynos.

humans begin with stats that are generally well balanced with no one stat showing a great improvement over the others. though this seems bad it does allow the greatest amount of flexibility for bonus points to be placed also humans are blessed with access to most of the cities of norrath depending upon the deity of their choice.

Spell of the week

Lull

Level: (1)one
Where Found: in your local guild
Description: this spell will lower the aggravation and reaction range of a MOB, if you lull one blue MOB
Required ingredients: none

Damage: none

Special: Lowers MOBs reaction range

Notes:This is a highly underrated spell, when you are soloing with an enchanter this spell is a godsend. Have you even seen that camp with 3 mobs all of which are blue well here's your chance cast lull on two of them and then hit the third with your beginning combo spell and you'll be amazed at how the two you lulled will stay put as the lone blue comes to you and your pet to be killed.

IMHO!

Name: Nalgar Everdie
Server: Tunare

Notes on Enchanter problems and the new modifications:
Some call us an Enchanter while now we are little more than an
Illusionist. Our spells center around controling the minds of others
not in Enchanting. We have a limited ability to Enchant items needed to
make magical jewelry but thie jewelry is not only non cost efective it
is also seems to have been mastered by non enchanters who have coned our
brotheren into linning their pockets with our blood and sweat. Our Most
usefull spells also seem to be the hardest to come by. Our Enchantment
after level 4 are hidden deep within High Hold Keep, A very dangerouse
area for thouse under 15 unless grouped. Our Enchant metal spells are
deep in the Dark Elf city and out illusion of Dark Elf spell has to be
recast every time you zone. Our Illusions also do not discise our
religion so some trying to sneak into cities will need to use
invisibility as well to get past the guards.
Reading the discription of the Enchanter in the EQ manual you will
note that we should be able to enchant weapons. This is no longer so.
While it seems that wizards can summon the weapons their fiends need we
can not make existing weapons magical even for a brief period of time.
Our spells get resised much more than any other class even with our
Tashan spell(which must always be used and tends to lower the number of
spells we can cast before the taget comes after us as well as increasing
mana cost) The resistance also adds to the number of castings needed to
be succesful and even resists count to the target as damage and even
high level warriors doing masive damage and using taunt are no match for
3 resisited spells. Our one saving grace was Memmory blur, a seemingly
unresistable spell. this spell is now just as resistable as the others
and no longer seems to make the monster forget as even a succesful
casting does not return a docile creature to a docile state(Camiens will
still attack me even when the spell works and no one is engaged with
them)
After level 16 you should rely on buffing spells like quickness and
RUNE. these spells seem to work great and I have not seen any other
class that can use them. Quickness also does not last long so you will
feel like you are doing something when your spells do not seem to be
effective. Also PC's never resist(don't tell Verdent or that may change
too)

PRELUDE part 2


Once inside the house Toorac drew on a map that he had committed to memory before leaving erudin, and remembered he found it almost child's play to memorize next to the complicated spells stored in that same mind. Toorac stood in the greeting hall, once again the family crest resounded through out the room on banners, shields and even carved into the marble walls itself. The ceiling vaulted at least 40 feet into citiadellike arches, and the floor was covered in a fine woven carpet depicting larger than life the master of the house engaged in a battle with a griffin. Before him was a great oaken fireplace and upon its majestic mantle sat a shiny brass shield with the family crest emblazed upon it. Hanging on the wall just above the shield was a jewel encrusted bastard sword like the one in the woven picture. To his right two sets of stairs lead to a small balcony overlooking the hall, this Toorac knew was where the lady of the house would sit and watch over the master of the house as he greeted his guests and discussed his business, a tradition that had gained favor among the low humans over the last few years. Below that balcony sat twin oaken doors braced by rune covered iron strips. Remembering the map he knew this would lead to the house proper, the living quarters of the master and lady of the house. But they where not why he was here, to the left of the fireplace sat a undecorated wooden door. Toorac smiled, the entrance to the house major where all others residing within these walls lived.
Toorac moved at a swift but nonchalant pace toward the wooden door, this was the most dangerous part of his mission if he passed someone who had seen the old wizard pass moments before they may cast enough doubt to see through his illusion spell. His sources did tell him that after a evening visiting his guild the hired house wizard would normally head straight to his laboratory to work on spells he had learnt that night. He passed through the door and entered a long narrow hallway decorated with the usual family portraits, each depicted a member alone or with others. Toorac stopped at one such portrait, upon it in vivid colors, obviously painted by a master, was a fair-haired girl in a flowing gown and at her feet a young boy dressed in fine black silk.
"Innocence must die for the good of all" Toorac mumbled almost as if in a trance
"EH, Aslak that you... what did you say... you know I cant hear so well" Toorac jumped at the voice, cursing himself for letting someone get this close without him noticing.
"AH.... Yes... Irudam it is me, I was lost in thought. How are you tonight you old goat." Toorac held his breath and prayed his source had given him the proper name for this wizen old man.
"HA. You call me old you decrepit old gnoll, you beat me in years by many." The old man gave a toothless smile, and Toorac wondered if without the illusion spell the half blind old man could have told Toorac from Aslak, or even a Orc centurion for that fact.
"You have me there Irudam, but alas I keep fit in the hills clearing them of the vile creatures that stir there. And you old man sit and watch the fire burn down." Toorac smiled despite himself he felt he like the old goat.
"Hrump... More like keeping fit running from those creatures." Irudam retorted with a sour face. "But what brings you into this end of the house at such a late hour"
Toorac thought quickly his next words could jeopardize his mission "I have business with a chicken in the pantry if you must know." Toorac made a serious face in an attempt to add humor to his comment.
"EH.. Oh.. ha, just as I thought an assassin," The words made Toorac's blood freeze "best not let Cook Elsa catch you or you will find yourself on a hook with a skinned bear as your neighbor."
Toorac relaxed as best he could and even managed to push a chuckle out, "Well Irudam, Good eve to you I should not keep my victim waiting"
