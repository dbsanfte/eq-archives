Abashi on our Tash spell line Posted Friday, April 7, 2000 by draconal
Currently it seems that this debuff does not lower the MR accurately ( 65 is the rumor, hard to tell in PvP because of the % being reduced ).

Debuff spells aren't subject to the % reduction in effectiveness. Only damage dealing spells are.

Gordon, could you state your 'view' on the MR of high creatures ( those 49+ ? ) or could we possibly setup some type of poll to see how many enchanters feel they are an effective part of the party when dealing with planes/giants/dragons and what their feelings on MR are?

Certain special encounters (dragons, gods) have high resistances (to the point of being immune) to all but one type of magic. Normal monsters typically don't have the incredibly high resists across the board.

We realize that this sometimes limits the damage-dealing potential of some classes, however those classes typically have either other ways of dealing damage (e.g. pets) or have other uses besides dealing damage (e.g. buffs).

- Gordon

Abashi on EXP from trade-skills Posted Wednesday, April 5, 2000 by draconal
I have always been a supporter of the idea that trade skills should give EXP to the character.
The way I envision it: Each time you get the "Your skill has increased in Tailoring (22)" You should get experience. As your trade skill gets higher, the EXP should incrementally increase - Logic: It is easy to progress through the early stages of each skill. But as you progress at the later levels, you have a significant amount of time and money invested. Therefore, early skill increases give a small amount of exp and later ones give larger (this would also combat abuses by people training in all trade skills to a low level.)

I am currently working on Jewelcraft. I have spent hours upon hours enchanting bars of metal, combining precious gems into jewelry. My character has become more adept as his skill increases. He has increased his knowledge as he invests this time. If I were out adventuring, I would be a level or 2 higher than I am now, but atleast I am a master jeweler. I feel that each trade skill increase should also be awarded with a slight EXP bonus (such as a quest is.)

I really believe that would give people more reason and purpose to become trademasters. I just reached Master Jeweller status and have invested literally thousands of platinum. I have nothing to show for it from an EXP standpoint. But I am sure my character has learned so much in that time. He went from an uneducated apprentice to a master tradesman. That is TRUE EXPERIENCE, and is as valuable, if not more, than learning to parry and riposte an opponent.

There are some problems in the trade economy. I am not talking about the monetary aspects of the trade economy. I think they have been created to stop abuses/minimize loopholes and I understand the logic.

Please rethink the idea of EXP for trade skill increases. This would increase the enjoyment for some of us businessmen of Norrath

Though a good idea, the current system of EQ wouldn't support this well as we would run into the problem mentioned previously (buying experience with Plat). I am aware that this problem exists a few places in game as it, and it isn’t very pretty. This is something that we can keep in mind for the future though.
- Gordon

Patch message March 29th Posted Wednesday, March 29, 2000 by draconal
This morning's update consisted primarily of server-side changes to improve performance of the game engine in general. No items, spawns, spells (with the exceptions below), etc., were changed as a result of this patch.

Area Effect "Fear" spells will no longer work in PvP. This affects a bard song, as well as the Cleric "Wave of Fear" spell.
Corrected a bug with some of the Resurrection spells that caused them to return too little experience.
We plan on publishing data and item changes next week if all goes well on the Test server. We apologize to those who are inconvenienced by having to wait for the expected changes.

- The EverQuest Team

An interesting post from Aradune(Brad) -LONG_ Posted Monday, March 27, 2000 by draconal
From the "Game FAQs" on everquest.station.sony.com/faq_frameset.html
QUOTE (any text in ALL CAPS is so emphasized by me) --
3.7 Will there be some other ways to travel than walking? Horses, boats or teleport spell?

Ships and teleport spells, yes. Horses will not be available at ship (meaning, possibly after ship, as an update or expansion - this has yet to be determined). There will also be a few OTHER MORE EXOTIC WAYS to traverse the lands of Norrath.


4.30 Do races have different languages and if there are can we have spells to translate ?

There will be languages, and there ARE spells that allow one to understand them.

6.8 Can I enchant weapons?

The Enchanter class CAN enchant WEAPONS.

8.6 Can we set up a "safe" house or rent a "safe" room? Or maybe safety deposit boxes in banks in major cities? Just a place to store items and treasures? Note: this implies that you go adventuring in order to afford rent.

A house will be as "safe" as you make it. Some may be vulnerable, some may not be, and some may be in-between.

8.6.1 What will be done to curtail the "urban sprawl" of houses?

Houses are rented and already exist in cities -- you do not build a 'new' house.

8.6.2 Will certain races have different player-buildings than other races?

Races definitely have different styles of architecture, which would extend to houses built for players.

8.6.3 Will houses have locks that require a key to open them, and the key is in turn stealable, or will there be another system, like a combination?

In that rentables (houses) will not be activated until after launch, we will not be releasing this information as of yet.

8.6.4 Will we be able to have player bought houses underground?

In that houses are rented rooms inside cities, and that there are cities underground, yes.

8.14 Will we be able to build and destroy buildings and boats?

No.

8.15 Is there an upkeep associated with owning a house?

Yes. Not only ARE houses extremely expensive, but there will be a periodic fee that guilds and players must pay, lest they lose ownership of their houses and halls.

-- END QUOTE
