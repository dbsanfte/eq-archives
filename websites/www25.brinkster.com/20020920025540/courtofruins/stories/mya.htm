She had just finished her training with Master Hekzn. As usual, Myanaa was anxious to try out her newly learned skills, and she was quite thirsty. Fortunately, she knew just the place to solve both dilemmas, The Maiden's Fancy.

She was never surprised to note that she was the only female patron, this was a dance hall after all; and then there was Ratraz. For some reason, most of the women in Neriak didn't like to be in the same room with him. That had never been a problem for Mya.

She exchanged a silent greeting with Molon and then glanced around the room, noting several new faces. They looked young, younger even than she. They must have been this year's new recruits. Time to start the dice games again.

Cecile suggested the fish wine, as she did every time Mya was here and Mya turned it down, ordering the brandy instead. She'd heard that there was a bet going between Master Hekzn and the K'jartans on just when Mya would get tired of saying "no thanks." Mya wanted to make sure Master Hekzn won.

Settling quietly into her favorite shadowy corner, she sipped her brandy and watched the patrons. There was the group of mages, who for reasons incomprehensible to her, chose to study languages in this noisy place. Some of the off duty guards were here, getting quite drunk as they had three days before their next tour. Of the new faces, she saw mostly shadow knights, a bit odd, as most of last year's recruits had been SK's. Perhaps the war had taken a greater toll than Her Majesty made known.

Losing interest in the customers and having finished her brandy, Mya wrapped herself in her great black cloak and slipped downstairs. She was please to see Belyea behind the bar; the night would be profitable.

Long ago, when Mya first began practicing her art, she targeted the Maiden as an "easy mark." The patrons were generally three sheets to the wind, it was dark, and the bartenders always seemed to be too busy to see everyone. She couldn't have been more wrong.

Before she managed to lift her first coin, Belyea had her in one of the back rooms asking her "What in the name of Innoruuk do you think you are doing!" Shocked, and more than a little scared, she just stared up at him for a moment. Finally finding her voice, she said the first thing that came into her head.

"I'll make you a deal"

Now it was Belyea's turn to be shocked.

He opened his mouth to speak, abruptly closed it, and sat down on a crate. He looked at her a moment, as if judging whether she was serious.

"Ok, let's hear it."

Now what? She had to come up with something fast.

"How about five percent?"

"Of everything you take while you're here?"

Mya nodded.

Belyea cupped his chin in his hand and thought for a moment.

"Make it ten and a ten person per night limit."

" And you don't target my employees or my cash box."

Mya stuck out her hand and said, "Deal." After all, ninety percent of something was better than nothing and being turned over to the Dreadguard.

Mya waved to Dirza and Mare and took up a seat at the end of the bar. The easiest place not to be seen was right out in the open. She ordered ale and passed a few words with Belyea. He always had a tip on who her first "customer" should be. Tonight it was the card game across the room.

She never cheated at cards. She didn't have to when the other players were either blind drunk or focusing on the fact that she was a woman. She poured on the charm and the plat came rolling in. Mya passed several hours at the table, picking up on several of the players' signals. She would ask a question here and there, about the game, or their work, sometimes about their families. It never hurt to know what you might be up against should you get caught.

The conversation had turned to the subject of work. The boys at the table were complaining about the extra long shifts of late. They said there was one detail in particular, which seemed to be getting the short end of the stick. Mya ask what they meant and they all passed glances before one of them leaned across the table and quietly whispered, "No time off for at least two months."

Mya sounded horrified as she replied, "How awful!" Her mind was calculating the plat in their collective pockets while her voice and body was expressing disbelief that such a thing could occur in Neriak.

"Two months… why, that just can't be right."

She looked around the table, noting that the expression on each mans' face bore out the truth of the statement. She was delighted. There would be the regular wages plus a bonus for extended duty and if that applied to the whole detachment… She almost couldn't keep the smile from her face.

Mya glanced around the room and noted the late hour by the lack of customers. She was just about to call it a night when the lights in the hallway faded a bit and several recently off duty guards walked in. The man next to her leaned in close to tell her these were some of the very same guards they had just been speaking of.

There were only four of them and she had already made a tidy profit for the evening. Then there was the fact that she knew these four had families and she still had to settle with Belyea. As she collected her things, she listened to the conversation.

Nothing new here, complaints about the job and the pay, asking after family and friends…

"Kzhaddul's not with you tonight?"

"No. As soon as we were dismissed he headed for the forest. Said something about meeting some friends for a hunt."

So this motley crew had a new addition. She'd have to be sure to check him out for targetability. Mya ordered one last drink and settled with Belyea. She asked him to keep an ear out for this Kzhaddul and then she slipped out into the night.
