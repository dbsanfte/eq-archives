The seeds of the Court of Ruins were planted many eons ago in the souls of three highborn in a Nameless corner of Norrath. These seeds found fertile ground but were unable to blossom and mature. When the time came that these three left the world, the seeds remained with their souls.

The souls and their kernels lay dormant until the universe declared they should be reborn. Some odd twist of fate determined that these souls be reborn as practitioners of the dark arts and in the forms of the dark races in an alternate Norrath known to its inhabitants as Luclin. Though they were placed far apart, these three were drawn together and the seeds were once again stimulated to growth.

The Court of Ruins was founded on the ideal that those of the dark races, practitioners of the dark arts, those following the dark gods and those following their own path should bring ruin to the existing establishment of Norrath. They would restore order to the world as they felt it should be. After all, the Dark Elves were once the ruling class in the world and should be again.

Guildhalls were commissioned in Neriak and the Court began to gather its forces. Dark Elves and Ogres came together with Necromancers and Shadow Knights of the "lesser" races. Plans were made and set in motion. The Court encountered many stumbling blocks along the way. Each was addressed and conquered until the strength of the Court was sufficient to being spreading to the world outside of Nektulos and Innothule.

The Council met to discuss the issues at hand. Much heated discussion occurred in those nights. Some were of the opinion that the Court would thrive and conquer as it was. No outside help was needed. The Court would stand on it's own and reclaim the world! Others expressed the need to take slaves from other corners of Norrath. After all, every empire needed slaves. Still others claimed the need for agents in other cities- spies to report the activities of the Court's enemies and lay the groundwork for the Court's invasions. A minority preached tolerance. If the Court was to rule, it must rule all fairly and justly or it too would be overthrown. These last voices were drowned out quite quickly.

Finally it was decided a few slaves would be taken to serve the Dark Masters. Gnomes and Humans found their way into the halls of the Court. The experiment seemed to work well enough. The Council met again and determined that some races such as dwarves and halflings, and certain classes such as the holier than thou paladins, simply could not be bent to the will of the Court and therefore had to be destroyed.

The Council met once more. With much discussion, it was finally decreed that those willing to commit to the ways of the Court and its ideals would be admitted but only after a test of their devotion. Followers of the fickle gods must be willing to become hated in their own temples. They must become outlaws in the cities of their birth. Only then would they be welcomed to the Court as equals. Amazingly, or perhaps not so, there were those willing to do just this. All the while, the ideal of tolerance was growing among the members of the Court.

The Council again met to take a look at the progress and goals of the Court. The general population had grown and changed, as had the structure and make up of the Council. Much had been accomplished since the Court's birth and many trials had been weathered. Of its peers, the Court was one of the few still standing. Over time, the Court's slaves had become spies. These spies were planted in many cities across the face of Norrath and had accomplished brilliant work in the furthering of the Court's goals. There were still hurtles that simply could not be overcome, no matter how they were approached. These puzzles could not be solved with the fear-induced work of slaves, or with the sometimes-grudging cooperation of spies. Something else had to be done.

A long forgotten idea and a revision of the Court's goals were presented- tolerance. Let us build a new world on the ruins of the old; not create ruins in the wake of our advance. If the Court was to restore the glory of Norrath, as it was before the great wars, acceptance and not dominance must be the rule of the day. There were places the Court simply could not go and goals it could not accomplish. This idea was met with grumbling from some of the older Councilors.

"Why should we be tolerant? Why change the status quo now?"

"Look at those guilds, founded at the time we were, who have devoted themselves purely to the dark ways. They are small and ineffective, if they are still in existence. They change little in the grand scheme and often find themselves drunk in their own halls or continuously drifting from one guild to another. They will never be face to face with their gods. The mighty dragons will never cower in fear of them. These guilds are not legends but footnotes in history. Those of our number who have become outlaws in their birthplace cannot advance in skill or understanding beyond what they had when they joined us. They have no one to teach them, no arena to train in. This is decidedly not the path to greatness!"

Those opposed to the idea of tolerance did acknowledge that there was a certain amount of wisdom in this argument. They expressed their continued belief that the Court should hold to its original intentions, though they did vote to ratify a new charter, one of mutual respect and cooperation. The Court would still seek to rule but rather than rule by destruction and domination, they would rule by production and cooperation.
