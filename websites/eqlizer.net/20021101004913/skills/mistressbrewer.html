How to Become a Mistress Brewer

By:  Sinuuyanea Nocturne of Quellious

Brewing is the fine art of creating beverages that are potent enough to grow hair on your chest, or burn it off.  From alcoholic beverages, to fruit drinks, to the specialized tempers needed for cultural armor, brewing covers many aspects of Norrath.  But, how does one master brewing?  With these words, I will guide you along the path of brewing, and you too can become a vending machine!

Before You Begin

If you are a forager, save any vegetables and fruits (not berries, mind you)  you find, as they are used in more difficult recipies.  Also, visit your local tradeskill site ( www.eqtrades.com) and find out what materials your smithy requires for their tempers, and stockpile those.

0-21  You Actually Drink THAT?!

For your first few points, you will have a few options.  Bog Juice is one of the simpler drinks, made from combining a snake scales, bottle, and flask of water in a Brew Barrel (brew barrels are found in most bars).  On a success, you will reciece a bottle of bog juice, on a failure, the bottle is returned.  If you happen to be a forager, you can also make juices: combine a berry or fruit and a flask of water in the brew barrel and hit combine.  On a success, you will receive 5 berry (trivial at 21) or fruit juices (trivial at 24), on a fail, you lose both items.  Foragers have yet another option, the production of flasks of water.  This is done by combining 4 pods of water and a bottle in the brew barrel.  The final option for anyone is to simply train in the guild until they wont train you anymore.

22-82  How Dry I Am!  *hiccup*

Your next step, as well as the remainder of the steps featured here, require components that are stackable (thank you for the ability to purchase full stacks), merchant sold, and cheap (woo-hoo!).  For now, you will be making Ale.  Ale is made by combining 1 barley, malt, flask of water, and a cask (the only item that’s not stackable, but is usually sold nearby) in a brew barrel.  A success yields 3 ales, a failure returns the cask, to be used again on your next attempt.  Ale is trivial at 68.  If you are not a forager, skip to the next step, please.  If you are a forager, you can produce White Wine by combining grapes (purchased), wine yeast, fruit, and a bottle in the cask.  Success yields 1 wine, failure returns the bottle.  White Wine is trivial at 82.

69-135 Temper, Temper!

This step is somewhat tedious, but it’s cheaper and easier than most other options available at this point in your career.  From 68 (or 82 if you are a forager) to 135, you will be making Ol’ Tujim’s Fierce Brew.  Combine in the brew barrel 1 of each barley, hops, yeast, malt, and a cask.  Success yields 2 kegs of Ol’ Tujim’s, failure returns the cask.  This will take you to 135, the point where tempers are trivial.

136-200+  Sinuu’s Tavern, Sinuu Speaking… (All right, so I’m not funny :P )

This step is mainly for those that want to make Tempers with a lower failure rate, without specializing in brewing.  To get to 168, the easiest path is to create Ginesh.  Ginesh requires foraged vegatables, flask of water, short beer (purchased off most merchants) and a shotglass, all in the cask.  Success yeilds 2 ginesh (which almost looks like an empty shotglass), failure returns the glass.  After that, you will have to make the one beverage that trivializes past 200:  Minotaur Hero’s Brew.  To make this, combine 3 malts, 2 flasks of water, and 2 short beers, along with a cask in the brew barrel.  Success returns 1 Mino Hero’s Brew, failure returns the cask.

Why Become a Grand Master Brewer? 

Below are a few of the products that are typically sought after by others that brewers can manufacture:

Heady Koila (trivial at 46):  Used by Tailors to produce Cured Silk and Wu’s Fighting Gauntlets, these are made by combining 2 packets of koila saps, a flask of water, and a bottle in a cask.

Tumpy Tonic (trivial at 135):  Used in quests, it takes only a koila nut and a flask of water.  Unfortunately, the Koila Nuts don’t stack, and are only sold in the Ocean of Tears.

Tempers (trivial at 135):  Used in racial smithing, and requires varying components.  Check www.eqtraders.com for the ingredient details, but you would make a lot of smithys happy if you had these in  stock
