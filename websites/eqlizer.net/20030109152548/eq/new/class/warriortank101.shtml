Warrior Tank Training 101

This guide was user submitted. It was submitted by Lady Solitica Destiny.

So ya think ya got what it takes ta be a warrior, huh? Got the guts ta stand toe-to-toe with a critter that’ll try ta make ya it’s meal? Think ya can stand the long sword fights with bandits? The strenuous activity of running around in plate mail? The sweat and grim of a hard earned battle? Think ya can stand the fact of being the first ta battle, and the last ta run? If ya said no to any of these questions, ya should try another class, cuz warriors are the very basis of the terminology "tanks." If ya said yes to all, then you already possess the warrior’s mentality. Let us begin with the trainin!

All right, lets start at the very beginning. Race? What ta be, hmmm? Well, the choices are all up ta you, but donna listen ta anyone saying that a warrior *should* be a Ogre, Troll, or a Barbarian. Ya donna have ta be big and strong ta be a lethal killing machine. I’m a wood elf, and any other guide ‘ell tell ya they are the worst warriors. Bah, they just are power hungry. They donna see the beauty of a wood elf, not ta mention all our racial benefits. But, once more, you be who and what ya wanna be. I’ll just be here ta train ya. Not reprimand ya.

By the way, High Elves and Edruties donna believe in the warrior ways. They cannot, and will not, choose the mind set of a true warrior, therefore they cannot be warriors.

Barbarian - Strong, big, yet a lil on the dumb side. Their charisma doesn’t do ‘em much good either, but they are a strong race, if ya can deal without infravision. There are a few weapons that require you to be a strong or big race, and barbarians are definitely big and strong. These guys have a bonus for combat, they can Slam. This donna do much damage, and most likely will be replaced with Shield Bash, but they got it so use it while ya young. Barbarians also have a blessin on their resistance towards cold.

Human - Average in stats, which is a blessin and a curse. They accelerate at one thing though: experience. All the races suffer a experience point penalty save for humans. They learn quicker. They also have the least bit of racial tension from other races. Like barbarians, humans donna have infravision.

Dwarf - Strong, enduring, and small. Dwarves are a sturdy race that donna seem to think working hard and overtime is a bad thing. The heavier, louder, and stronger a piece of armor is, they love it more. Ya donna have ta tell me twice that dwarves are great warriors, although I’ll tell ya something or two about BO with these guys. <phew!> Nonetheless, dwarves possess the nerves and mentality to be a true warrior. They possess infravison, a nifty bonus, high stamina, and high strength, although they lack in dexterity and agility. They might be able ta dish it out and take a few more impacts than I, but I prefer ta not be hit than ta be able ta withstand a few more hits. Just my view on life.

Halflings - Their not as strong as the other races, but they are mighty agile and dexterous. They might not hit hard, but they can hit more often and they can evade hits better than any I know. They have infravision, which is a great thing. Their only flaw is their strength. Without any magical assistance, I donna know how a halfling will ever get around to wearing plate mail…

Gnomes - Uh oh! Someone said a four letter word! (I can count, gnomes is a six letter word. =P ) Gnomes (oops!) are a sturdy race like dwarves, yet they are far more intelligent, well they presume to be. They aren’t as strong as dwarves nor as wise, but their intelligence definitely shows. They can learn quicker than any other warrior; skill wise that is, not experience points wise. They have infravision and they have a unique racial ability, tinkering. I donna know if that’s a good thing. Gnomes usually come up with weird stuff, but half the time it’s actually useful. Note that: half the time. =)

Dark Elves - The dark children of Norrath, but donna thing just cuz their shun from the world they canna be warriors. They are far more vicious than others would presume. Their not strong, but strong enough to get by. Their strength lies in their dexterity, agility and intelligence. They possess some truly remarkable racial abilities: Ultravision, the ability to see perfectly at night; and the hiding skill. They can vanish into thin air, sorta like invisibility, but at will. It only works 50% of the time, but it’s better than nothing in my opinion. They just suffer a big racial tension around the world, although their tension isn’t as bad as with ogres and trolls.

Trolls - Bigger than barbarians, but smaller than ogres. These guys are stronger, and more sturdier than dwarves, although their lights aren’t always on upstairs. Um, they lack intelligence and wisdom that is. True, a warrior donna have to be smart in order ta swing a sword or kick a critter where it counts. Trolls have infravision like and they have an admirable trait, which causes jealousy ta other warriors. Troll warriors possess regeneration, therefore they have a much shorter down time than anyone else. Their weakness is they have low tolerance to fire and their racial tension with the world is something to consider. Cuz they ain’t welcomed at my home town. Like Barbarians, they can Slam.

Ogres - Biggest of all, strongest of all, UGLIEST of all! BO, Brushin their teeth, BATHING all come ta mind when I see, no *think* of these guys. But, if ya can stand that, let me tell ya, a swing from an ogre might not hit often at first when their young, but when their older, a hit might be the difference between ya life and death. These guys are walking muscle. They can start off with a strength of 150, which has inspired lots of warriors. But once again, donna think strength is everything. Ogres have infravision but they have the worst racial tension with the world. Their KOS, killed on sight, with NPC’s, non-player characters. Ta be honest, their strength has allured my attention (forget their looks!), but the fact that they canna travel the world as easily as the good races, makes ‘em dwindle in numbers around the world. Ogres can Slam like trolls and barbarians.

Half-Elves - Handsome, agile, strong, yet a lil on the frail side. Half-elves are a very similar ta humans save for a humans have slightly better strength and stamina. Half-elves possess infravision and occasionally laugh at their human counterparts when they go bump in the night. =)

Wood Elves - Ahh, my race and choice in life. We wood elves are quick and agile, although halflings are more agile I will admit. We can be strong, getting a strength of 100. While weaker than the big races, we can boast a high agility with that 100 strength, we can have a 100 agility as well. So we can hit hard in battle, and avoid blows with ease. We also have a few nifty racial abilities. We can hide like dark elves, and we can forage for food like rangers, druids, and bards can. We can hide and forage 50% of the time, but it’s better than having ta make food runs or if ya low in health and danger is near, ya can always hide while ya recover. =)

Well, I’ve said all there is about the races. Ya choose whatcha wanna be and have fun, that’s all that really matters. Donna look at the numbers, stat wise, look at the fact that magical items can always boost ‘em and ya can do a lot more with a different race than ya can do with a strong race. Ogres and trolls have ultravision. Barbarians and humans don’t. But Ogres and trolls canna travel all over Norrath without doing a lot of good deeds. Barbarians and humans are accepted. Elves are quick and agile, but lack in raw power. So? Just takes me another swing or two ta take down my opponent…gives me such a rush! =)

Warriors in Depth

First off, we warriors can wear almost any armor. Can wield almost any weapon. Lastly we have the most hit points per level there is. At levels 20, 30, 40, and 50, we obtain more hit points. Therefore be prepared ta see some astronomical numbers for health. When ya get hit for 94 points, ya’ll just say it was a scratch and see ya health bar barely move. Warriors get the ability to go berserk if they drop down to 30% of their health, and the ability to cause critical strikes and crippling blows in combat. We donna get spells, but that just separates the strong from the reliant. Spell casters rely on their mana and usually spend half their adventuring time meditating…boring if ya ask me. Even the half breeds, paladins, rangers, and shadow knights rely on meditating. Their a shame to us fighting classes. But donna let the fact that they can heal themselves slip ya mind. It’s a nice ability that we lack. My advice to you is to train in bind wounds. It won’t help you get passed 50% of ya health, but it’s better than sitting down for half an hour or so. The higher ya level is, the more the down time is. Ya can either heal ya self to 50% or beg a cleric for healing. I’m not into beggin…

 

Armor Training

Decided yet? Good! Let’s get down to ya most important factor: Armor. Trust me, it’s a BIG difference when ya levels start climbing. Patchwork armor is good until level 5, afterwards I’d look for leather until 8. Beyond 8 I’d try for banded until 15. At 16 and up, I’d suggest trying to get bronze plate mail, or if ya can afford it, steel plate which offers the same AC as bronze plate it’s just steel is a bit lighter. If ya need to know why armor is more important that weapons, I’ll tell ya. When I was six, I had leather armor and was fairing well, until I met a wonderful person that bought me a full suit of bronze plate mail. I was in shock for about half an hour! That armor was terrific, although very heavy for me. I couldn’t carry much loot. But with that armor, I lived all the way to 14 without dying once. From 1-14 without any deaths is pretty good. But others will tell you that I was "twinked" because I had that armor earlier than most would’ve. Prolly, but it was given to me. =P

( Twinked, by the way, is a term used in EQ for people walking around with very high level stuff at a very low level. )

Donna worry about twinkers. Worry aboutcha self. Strive for breastplates first, cuz they give the best AC, then go for the rest of the armor. Now, there’s a choice that will come to you one day. Some armor have magical enchantments on ‘em but have a horrible AC compared ta other non-magical armor. Your choice again. I, like a lot of other warriors, firmly believe in STRENGTH enhancing armor pieces. But it’s up to you. AC or ATK. In the beginning I’d like ta think AC is more important due to most warriors will want ta solo. When ya get passed 20, forget ya soloin days and look for a group. ATK is more important now mainly cuz ya most likely will have healers with ya that will make up for ya loss in AC.

Weapon Training

The best part. Weapons. They come in all sizes and shapes. From fists ta huge axes. Warriors are the master of melee. We can wield almost any weapon in all of Norrath save for pure mages wimpy stuff. We donna need a water crystal staff that can choke our enemies, I’d rather slice ‘em down with a reliable axe and kick ‘em ‘til he screams like us women. =)

Note: To train in weapon skill faster, always strive ta wield a weapon in the field with a short delay time. Quicker strikes means the skill is checked for often and therefore the skill will rise quicker than a slower weapon in the field.

1H Slash - The common choice of all warriors. It’s not ta be looked down upon, mainly because there aren’t any special 1H blunts out there for high level warriors.

1H Blunt - As stated before, there aren’t any high end 1H blunt weapons for warriors only, but it donna mean you should over look this field. I personally am training in both, ya never know when one might pop up in the world. Weapon masters, such as myself, train in every weapon we are allowed.

2H Slash - The fun toys. These bad boys can really get a monster’s attention. Just give ‘em a tap or two with these weapons and watch them turn to you in fear.

2H Blunt - There are actually a few good 2H Blunts out there for warriors, but these weapons are extremely slow. The only offset they have is that they pack a mighty punch! A hit with these weapons takes out teeth from giants!

Piercing - These weapons aren’t favorable by warriors, mainly because their weak in power. Their perfect for rogues, especially for their backstabs, but for a warrior, it’s much preferred to use a sword or mace.

Hand-to-Hand - A decent training skill, but most warriors don’t train in it. Mainly because we excel at weapons, and not fists like Monks do. This field is best left to them, still if you ever want to avoid being disarmed, this is a fine choice. Ya will always have a backup weapon. =)

Archery - This is a great skill, or throwing if ya like that better, for getting monster’s attention. Since we donna have spells, and we don’t need spells, ta lure critters ta us instead of running up ta them and wackin at them. It’s also great when ya in a group and ya low in health, you can back up and fire away.

Throwing - Same as Archery, save for they have a shorter range than arrows. But ya can fire ‘em a lot faster!

Kicking - We may not be Monks with our feet, but we still know how ta kick ‘em where it counts. Aim low, below the belt. Even the shins will do finely. When fighting magical creatures, ya gonna need magical boots in order to effect ‘em injury.

Warrior Skill Training

This will cover our skills we’ll get as we progress throughout our years as warriors. Ya canna live without, these skills, and therefore train in ‘em often. Donna worry about putting skill points inta ‘em though, they rise quickly as you fight tougher and tougher critters. Save ya points for weapon skills that ya want to train but haven’t had the time ta. Note: ya need ta put at least one training point in the following skill in order ta learn it.

Berserk - This isn’t a skill really. All warriors begin with this ability. If ya fall beneath 30% of ya health, ya’ll flip ya lid and go insane. While berserk, ya strength will improve by 10, which increases our ATK.

Shield Bash - This obviously requires a shield in order ta be utilized in battle. Ya then use the flat end of the shield and SLAM the shield inta the critters face which can stun ‘em sometimes. The only thing is that kicking generally does more damage. But donna over look this skill, when ya up to ya neck against magic users, show ‘em how PO ya can get with a shield. It’ll disrupt their magic and ya might walk away with no actual harm. Remember, magic users need their spells in order ta be effective, show ‘em ya shield and they’ll run in fear of ya. =)

Dodge - A great skill I must say. Ya donna believe how many times I’ve dodged death with this skill. With our ability to wear plate armor, use shields, parry, reposite, and dodge, a warrior can be a difficult being to injure when their skills if properly trained.

Parry - I like this skill, ya use ya weapon ta stop incoming melee attacks and avoid damage. It’s a nice warriors touch.

Critical Strikes - This isn’t a normal skill that ya have to train in. It’s bestowed upon us warriors upon achieving ya 12th level. We then can teach critters not ta mess with us. A critical strike is a very rare event, so don’t get ya hopes up high.

When we go berserk, our critical strikes become crippling blows. Crippling blows do a hell of a lot more damage than a critical strike and even stun opponents. My record a this time is 59 points with a critical strike, and I did a crippling blow that did 95 points. See the difference?

Dual Wield - This skill is what a lot of warriors dream of. Wielding two powerful swords, or maces, against a critter in an amazing display of weapon talent. Just remember, put ya quickest weapon in ya right hand so this skill will raise fast. I’d practice this skill in a group, while soloing I’d just stick to a sword and shield or a two hander.

Double Attack - This is what we live for. Our ability to swing twice as fast in combat is a great thing, especially if ya mastered dual wield. Ya can sometimes get four ta five swings before someone wielding a two hander can swing once! Yeah, a two hander can swing for more damage per swing, but dual wielders with double attacks have better chances of getting critical strikes in combat. Still, double attack with a two handed weapon is still something to train in.

Reposite - This is an attack that’ll really tee-off ya opponents. When they swing at you, you get a counter-strike. Ya can bet this skill will be worth it except I don’t think it works as often as I would presume. It comes almost as rare as critical strikes do.

Disarm - I haven’t gotten this high yet, but I believe it’ll getcha some nifty weapons, and be useful when against critters wielding powerful weapons such as two handed swords. Most critters donna know how to use their fists effectively. Don’t over look this skill. Although ya have to be 35 in order ta obtain this skill. So it’s a long journey…donna rush. Ya’ll get there eventually.

Graduation

Okay, I have ta thank ya for readin my guide and havin the patience ta understand my accent. I hopes I helped ya on deciding whatcha wanna be and what ta train in. Warriors are a beautiful class. Donna let anyone tell ya that their the way of life is a boring and uninteresting one. Bah! They just got no patience. Warriors are masters of battle. A wizard may do 1000 points of damage ta one critter in the blink of an eye…but he needs room ta cast it, and while that beast will be battering him down, he’ll never get that spell off. In truth, it’s been said before, and I’ll repeat it. "Tanks need blasters and healers, while healers and blasters need tanks." I heard this via OOC chat and I give credit ta the guy. Atlas I didn’t record his name. =(

If ya decided this is what ya wanna be, welcome ta Norrath, I’ll be willin ta help a youngin anytime. Just don’t expect powerlevelin from me. Warriors are proud ta fight, and fighting everything on Norrath is part of my dream. So donna rush.

If ya decide this isn’t ya class, donna worry, whatever ya become, we warriors will protect ya, so treat us nice and ya’ll have a body guard prolly for life. =)

Lady Solitica Destiny,
