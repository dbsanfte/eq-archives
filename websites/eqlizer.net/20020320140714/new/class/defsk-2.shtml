Definitive Shadowknight Page 2


Level by level tactics

Level 1-5

The early levels can sometimes be frustrating for a first time shadowknight. This is especially true for smaller race shadowknights because they do not have the benefit of slam and can not cast any spells to make up for their melee deficeincies. Luckily the first five levels go by rather quickly.

Here are a few bits of general advice during these levels. 

First and foremost stay near your home town. Nothing slows your advancement down more than a long run for corpse recovery. In general I wouldn't recommend travelling more than 2 zones from your home city.

Be sure to save your harm touch for emergency situations. I would only use it if I were under one bar of health as a last resort. It is very effective at these levels. Having harm touch in your back pocket allows you to take some risks knowing that you can use it to bail you out if things go sour. Once it is gone you will have to be more conservative so hold on to it as long as you can.

Unless you are a twinked character or are being given bandages from others I would not recommend using the bind wound skill at this point. That money could better be spent buying weapons or armor.

Another common mistake by young shadowknights is using big and bulky weapons. Until level 10 the maximum damage that any player can do is 21. This means that any weapon with a damage factor or more than 10 is not being used to it's full potential.  Bear this in mind when calculating weapon efficiency.

Be sure to train one point into taunt when you first visit your trainer. The skill will not go up until you do. During combat constantly hit the taunt button after it refreshes even when not in a group to keep it maxed out. I would also recommend doing the same for sense heading until you get it up to a reasonable level.

Large race shadowknights may have a problem increasing their defense at early levels. This happens as a result of their high strength allowing them to tear through their opponents. As a result they get hit less. The best way to increase this is to pick a fight with two or three low blue mobs and allow them to hit on you for a while. Defense will increase much faster this way but be careful not to let them do too much damage. =)

Another important thing to note at early levels is that it is faster to advance via quest than via hack and slash. Concentrate on killing monsters that carry quest items if you can. This way you gain experience and have the potential of getting a piece of a quest which will not only reward you with an item or cash but also with a decent chunk of experience. Some quests which are good for this level range include:

Bone Hunt(Grobb)
Curscale Armor(Cabilis)
Deathfist Slashed Belts(Freeport)
Fire Beetle Eyes(Neriak)
Lizard Meat(Oggok)
SK Letter Delivery(Paineel)

Level 6

Bash

The addition of the bash skill into a shadowknight's arsenal is a very important step. Not only does this skill allow small sk's to gain a secondary attack when using a shield but it also allows large race sk's to slam(without a shield) for more than one damage.

Bash not only does additional damage via a secondary attack but it also has a chance of stunning your opponent. This is very important versus spellcasters and also can be used to momentarily stop runners.

Tips

Level 6-8 allow you a bit more freedom than the previous five. At these levels you can begin to explore some of the lower level dungeons such as Crushbone, Befallen, Black Burrow, Kurn's Tower, Upper Guk or Kerra Ridge.

There are also a couple of quests that you will probably want to complete including...

Lizard Tails(Oggok)
Black Shadow Tunic(Oggok)
Drosco(Qeynos)

Level 9

Level 9 is a huge step in a young shadowknight's life as it grants thiem the ability to cast spells. I will attempt to explain each spell in detail below but before I do so I want to give a brief bit of advice.

When you go to train your spell skills train one point into each school of casting with the exception of evocation. You do not receive an evocation spell until level 22 and you should wait to train until then. This way your evocation will start at 22 rather than 9.

Spells

Disease Cloud

This spell will most likely be used as a fetch spell. Use this spell to attack monsters from distance rather than running up on them and taking damage puling them to a safe location. It does up to 5 points of direct damage(DD) followed by a long lasting damage over time(DOT) effect which does one point of damage per tick(six seconds) and is disease based.

Disease cloud is useful at early levels because it khurts a monsters regeneration(due to the DOT). This helps with runners because if you miss they will not be healing(which could cause them to move faster).

It has also come to my attention(thanks to Leafweir) that this spell has an additional use at higher levels. When disease cloud is on a mob they will stay aggroed on the user even after they reset back to their home location. This is useful for feign death pulls. You can let all mobs reset then run a ways back and feign again(just to be sure). Hopefully this will allow you to pull singles a bit easier.

It is also useful for raising conjuration. It's easier to raise it this way than by summoning pets which use much more mana.

Invisibility vs. Undead

This spell allows you to render your target(group or self only) invisible to undead beings. This is a useful spell in dungeons such as Befallen which are primarily undead.

Leering Corpse

This spell summons a skeletal pet. It is the first in your line of pet summoning spells. This pet will be from level 3-5. It requires bone chips.

Some players will tell you that a shadowknight pet is useless. Ignore those people. A shadowknights pet may be low level in comparison to that shadowknight . This is true. A pet does damage though. It may not be a lot of damage but it is extra damage during the course of a battle. When you take into consideration the amount of damage that your pet does compared to the amount of mana it took to summon him then you will find it to be very efficient when compared to other spells.,

A pet can also be used to save you a few seconds when fleeing the battle scene. Those couple of seconds could save your life.

One thing that should be noted is that your pet will not make a very good tank. You should not allow him to absorb very much damge or he will die. One thing you should always do when summoning a pet is use the /pet notaunt command. This will prevent him from taunting and being killed.

Other useful pet commands include:

/pet follow me - This will inform your pet to follow you around(default)
/pet guard here - This is a very useful command when fetching or when pulling to a particular location. Your pet will assist you in any battles which take place near him. After the battle is over he will return to the spot that you told him to guard.
/pet attack - This command will order your pet to attack whoever or whatever you have targetted.
/pet report health - This command will cause your pet to tell you what percentage of his total hit points he currently has.
/pet sit down - This command will tell your pet to sit where he is currently located. Ihave not found this to increase healing time in any way so I would recommend using /pet guard here instead or else you will have to issue this command afer every battle.

Lifetap

This is the first in the line of lifetap spells. This spell will drain up to five hit points from your target and transfer them to you. This spell is useful to reduce downtime if you are injured but is not very useful in combat at this point because of the shadowkinight lifetap mana penalty. Until level 50(and life leech) all shadowknight lifetaps pay a mana penalty in comparison to their necromancer counterparts.

This spell is still useful for casting on a mob that someone else is fighting to heal yourself but I would not recommend using it during combat unless you are desperate for hit points and in a group. In a group you can then back up from the fight and lifetap until you are ready to resume the battle.

Locate Corpse

This spell can be used to help find a player's corpse. There are two ways to use this spell.

The first way is to deselect your target(hit escape) then blindly cast this spell. You will turn to face(and target) the nearest corpse.

The second and more common useage is to find a specific players corpse. If you target a player you can then cast the spell and it will point you to their nearest corpse.

Sense the Dead

This spell will point you to the nearest undead being. This can be useful for example in an outdoor zone where you might be seeking wandering skeletons(for bone chips).

Siphon Strength

Siphon is a very useful spell at this level. It ranks right up there with Leering Corpse as your most effective level 9 spell. This spell transfers up to ten points of strength from your target and gives them to you. This has the effect of raising your ATK while lowering your targets.

This spell is a very effective fetching spell at all levels because it only uses 5 mana. This allows you to fetch for longer periods of time without stopping to regain mana. Even high level players often keep this spell memorized for fetching purposes.

It can also be useful in a duel. Some characters weight allowances are not much different from their current weight. This is especially common among low-mid level characters who wear bronze armor. This spell might push them over the weight limit which will reduce their armor class and movement speed.

Level 10

Dodge

The dodge skill allows you to avoid damage that you might have previously taken. There appears to be a seperate roll that checks for dodge aside from the normal armor class check that occurs. Dodge is not that effective when first gained but will gain in usefulness as it raises in later levels.

Tips

Levels 10-25 can be an exciting time in a young shadowknight's life. During these levels you will be able to defeat some of the bosses of the lower level dungeons and venture into the mid level dungeons such as Cazic Thule, Najena, Permafrost and the Estate of Unrest.

It shoudl also be noted that at level 10 the damage cap is raised a bit. You will now be able to do into the 30s damage wise. This will allow you to use weapons with larger damage factors(normally on two handed weapons).

It is during these levels that you will likely begin to accrue a number of magical items and will be able to complete some interesting quests. Below is a list of useful items and quests that are obtainable in this level range.

Level 12

Meditate

Meditate is as an important skill for shadowknights as it is to all spellcasters. This skill allows you to regain mana at a quicker rate than when normally sitting.

From level 12-34 you will need to click on the meditate button from the spellbook menu to meditate. While meditating at these levels you will be unable to see what is going on in the outside world.

At level 35 you gain the ability to meditate while sitting. You can then view the action of the outside world. Meditation will automatically occur as soon as you sit at these levels.

Level 15

Spells

Bone Walk

This spell is an upgrade to the leering corpse spell. It will summon a pet that is approximately level 7-9 to act as your servent. The same rules apply as with your earlier pets.

Clinging Darkness

Clinging darkness is the first in your line of darkness spells. These spells slow your opponents running speed and also do damage over time(DOT). This particular spell does 30 points of damage in six ticks(36 seconds).

While this spell does not slow nearly as much as a snare spell it is very useful for slowing down runners. When soloing you will find this invaluable. It can also be used for fear kiting which will be explained below.

Endure Cold

This spell adds 20 points of cold resistance to the targetted player. It will prove to be useful when going up against cold based attacks later in your lifetime when battling wizards or Lady Vox for example. When you first obtain the spell it's primary use however is simply to practice your abjuration.

Fear

This spell will cause your target to run in fear if succesfful. It is primarily of use in outdoor zones. In a dungeon it will likely do nothing more than buy you a few seconds before it causes a train.

Fear has two typical uses.

The first use will be readily available for a level 15 caster. This is to buy some time while you are fighting a mob to either run or to allow other party members to meditate long enough to get enough mana for the heal or the direct damage spell that will finish a mob.

The second useage is for reverse kiting or fear kiting. This tactic has become very popular among shadowknights and necromancers. At level 15 you do not really have enough mana to be an effective fear kiter solo. In a group with a necromancer, druid, or ranger however you can still take advantage of this tactic.

Fear kiting involves two steps. The first is a snare style spell. Clinging darkness can be used to substitute for this but it does not slow your opponent near as much as a true snare spell. Later darkness spells such as engulfing and dooming darkness will do the trick much better.

The second step is to cast a fear spell. The mob will now be fleeing from you but will be slowed because of the snare spell. Melee types and pets then follow behind the feared mob and gain free attacks on it while it can not fight back.

Fear kiting has become immensely popular because it allows players to conquer foes that they would not be able to under normal means.

Lifespike

Lifespike is the upgrade to the lifetap spell. It drains 11 hit points from your opponent and grants it to you. This spell is effective than teh level 9 variant but is still a drain on mana and is primarily useful for reducing downtime.

Numb the Dead

This is the first in your line of undead calm spells. This spell lowers an undead beings aggressiveness if it is successful. This can be useful for example in Befallen if you see two or three undead next to one another. You can use the numb the dead spell on all but one mob and then fetch it with a spell. If succesful it can be a great way to pull singles.

Shadow Step

Shadow step is a mini random teleport. This spell will teleport you a few seconds away in a random directino. This spell is not very effective indoors but can be used in outdoor zones to give yourself a couple seconds of seperation from a mob. Unforuntately it often is disorienting and by the time you gain your bearings the mob has reacquired you.

This spell is very effective in duels with other melee classes. This can disorient them and give you time to cast your dot's or lifetaps on them.

Level 17

Parry

Parry is a defensive skill that is similar to dodge. It allows you to avoid damage that you would not avoid otherwise.

You may be wondering why you should need parry if you already have dodge. The reason is because it gives you a second chance roll to avoid damage. The two of them together allow you to avoid more damage than either one would alone(because you get a second chance if one roll is not succesful).

Level 20

Level 20 is a huge step in a shadowknights life for several reasons. The most obvious reason is the double attack skill which will be discussed below. However there are a couple of not so obvious reasons that we will discuss now.

The first is that your regeneration will be increased. From level 20-50 you will regain 3 hit points per tick when sitting. Trolls and iksars will gain 3 hit points per tick while standing and 6 hit points per tick while sitting. This will be very beneficial for reducing downtime.

The second reason is that the damage caps are lifted. At level 19 a dark reaver(for example) might only do 33 points damage maximum. Once you hit level 20 you will be hitting in the 40s or higher(depending on your strength) with that same weapon. 

Double Attack

For the last several levels you probably will have noticed your melee damage output slipping behind that of a warrior, ranger or rogue. This is because they had gained dual wield and double attack skills which allowed them to do quite a bit more damage than a single attacking shadowknight or paladin.

After gaining the double attack skill much of this will change. In this level range dual wield is not effective enough to outdamage a two handed weapon in most cases. You will find yourself doing the same melee damage output as a warrior of the same level with the same quality weapon.

Double attack works by having an addition roll. It is either succesful or not succesful. If it is succesful you gain a second attack during that melee round. It will dramatically increase your damage output. 

Level 22

Spells

Convoke Shadow

Convoke shadow is the third in your line of pet spells. It summons a skeletal pet that is roughly level 9-11. The same rules apply as with earlier pets.

Dark Empathy

Dark empathy allows you to transfer a portion of your hit points to another player. This spell can be useful for reducing downtime if there is no other healer present or if your healer is low on mana. 

The major drawback of this spell is a long recast delay. Still, it is quite useful when you first obtain it. It is especially useful for a troll or an iksar because of their regeneration.

Deadeye

This self only spell grants the caster both infravision(useful for humans or erudites) and the ability to see invisible beings.

Engulfing Darkness

Engulfing darkness is an upgrade to the level 15 clinging darkness spell. It slows your opponent more than the previous incarnation but it is still not as good as a snare spell for slowing down your opponent. It does make you more effective as a fear kiter however.

It also does more damage than the original. In this case it does 110 points of damage in 10 ticks(one minute). 

One major hindrance is that the snare spell overwrites engulfing darkness as do higher level versions of the spell. Thus if you are in a group with a ranger, druid or necromancer you may find this spell of limited use.

Spook the Dead

This is an undead only fear spell. It is much more mana efficient than your level 15 fear spell when fighting undead beings. Using spook the dead in conjunction with engulfing darkness you will find yourself pretty effective in fear kiting giant skeletons in rathe mountains. At later levels you will find it useful for fear kiting spectres and other undead beings.

Vampiric Embrace

Vampiric embrace is the most mana efficient lifetap that you will ever receive. The problem is that you can not decide when it will tap. 

The spell works like this. You cast it and for the next few minutes your hand will begin to glow(use your imagination here). During that duration every time that you swing a weapon you have a chance to proc vampiric embrace. The higher your dexterity the greater your chance to proc. 

When the proc does go off you will lifetap from your opponent. The amount of damage you will tap varies by level. The formula is (level/2)+1. Examples:

At level 22 you will proc for 12 points of damage.
At level 41 you will proc for 21 points of damage.
At level 50 you will proc for 26 points of damage.
At level 60 you will proc for 31 points of damage.

One problem with this spell is that even though it is a proc it can be interupted. This commonly happens when you are moving during combat. So if you use this spell while moving around or while fear kiting it may be wise to move in spurts and then set your feet.

Ward Undead

Ward undead is the first in your line of anti undead spells. It is also the first spell that you gain that uses the evocation skill. Hopefully you did not train your evocation until level 22 as we suggested earlier in this document. If you did then you will have a few more points to raise.

This spell will do direct damage(up to 41 points) against undead beings. When you first obtain the spell it is semi useful for doing direct damage but it is borderline. Later versions of this line of spells will be more useful but for a shadowknight level 22 is a bit too late. 

There are a few cases when it is of use however. Some prefer to use it as a fetch spell. I would rather use engulfing darkness as it is more mana efficient. However engulfing darkness can be overwritten by snare. So depending on party make up this may not be a bad idea.

Others prefer to use it to finish off runners. This can be useful for example if a mob has distance on you and there are other mobs in teh way. 

The largest problem you will face with this spell is raising your evocation. Even if you waited until level 22 to train evocation, you will fizzle a lot at first. The best way to raise your evocation is to summon a skeletal pet and practice the spell on him. Use all your mana. Sit and meditate. Then do it all over again. It works best if you can find an enchanter to cast clarity on you or if you have a bard play the mana song.
