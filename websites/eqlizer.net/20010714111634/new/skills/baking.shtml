Baking Guide

This guide was submitted by: Akaran of the Dead Men Walking.

G'd day ol' chap. If you're reading these mindless scribblings I'll assume that you wanted to learn the art of Baking. Or as I call it, "Having your friends for dinner." This guide will take you up in your skill quickly I'll be boastfull enough to say. This is a speed guide for baking, and you might find that you can get to even my skill - 128 - within a matter of hours, and at a very low cost of platnium. If your INT is low (below 100) it might take you a bit longer, but you'll soon find that this is one of the easiest and cheapest skills to learn.

The first thing you want to do is to move to Freeport. Freeport has a few zones that are very important to you as a fledgling cook, and even if you're KoS in that dusty town you'll find that you can go next door to Neriak and buy your supplies there aswell. You need one item before you acn do much else, and this will save you many trips too and from either Neriak or Freeport. The item is a Cooking Spit. They are sold in East Freeport and can be made by a skilled Blacksmith if you find yourself interested. The spit acts just like an oven, and you can carry it with you as you travel.

There is one vendor that is Very important to you right now. At the last group of huts in the Eastern Commons (closest to the Western Commons) there is a cute lil' Erudite that sells Batwings. And lots of Batwings at that. Infact, it seems that her supply is endless, and this is quite a good thing. Speaking of the Eastern Commons, there is a vendor at the First Inn (Inn closest to the Freeport zone) that sells Jugs of Sauce and Frosting. Don't dispare, there is also a vendor in Neriak Commons that sells the Sauce and Frosting too. Last but not least, you will need quite alot of Fish. Don't dispare, you won't have to sit for hours on end trying to get a large enough stockpile for the two meals you'll be making that require them. There are three vendors that sell lotsa fish: by the Fountain in Neriak Commons, at the Freeport Port Authority, and at the Barbarian Fishing camp in the Northern Desert of Ro.

Now if you have that cooking spit it's time to get to work. You can take the wimpy way out, if you have the training points to do it, by putting in 20 points into Baking at your local Guild Trainer. It helps, but isn't necessary. You will be making Batwing Crunchies. Ingredents list is as follows:

This is really easy. Buy up many stacks of Wings from that Erudite I told you about earlier (or you acn buy from the players, but she sells 'em cheaper) and then buy up an equal number of Frostings from Neriak or the vendor in EComs. Take 1 Batwing, plus 1 Frosting and then hit "Combine" in your Spit and you have made (hopefully) Batwing Crunchies. The skill rate advancement on this seemed to be pretty nice, but as always, expect many failures as you start out.

Now, once you have that completed, it's time to start the Fish recipies. Once you hit 46 either continue making the crunchies until you're out of wings and frosting, or just sell the components back to the vendors. What you now need are Fish and Jugs of Sauce.

I liked doing this part in Neriak myself. The vendor that sold the Jugs of Sauce was about a minute walk from the one that sold Fish. Again, take 1 Fish, 1 Jug of Sauce, and combine them in your cooking spit. It produces 2 Fish Fillets, which sell back for a decent ammount of coin. This part will take you all the way up to skill level 82 - and it's that simple. Failures will occur often, but the skill will raise respectably fast.

Now it's time to make yourself a Master Chef. Wherever you bought your fish from, go buy ALOT more. I think I carted around 200 fish back to the batwing vendor in EC and I still didn't have enough to take me as high as I could have gone. (And that's with a 201 INT)

Once you have as many fish as you can carry without moving at a snail's pace, go back to your Batwing vendor and buy up a bunch o' them aswell. This next step is pure simplicity and a fast advancement for your skills.

Even better, by teh time you are a master Baker you have gone well past the trivial levels for almost all of the new *stat enhancing foods*. Those foods and where to find them are in the "Advanced Baking" guide, also written by me. At the most, this guide should have taken you 4 to 5 hours at the far end maximum to master this skill and be able to cook yourself a mighty fine meal.
