Everquest To Celebrate Year Number 3
 11:56 EST - Hermana

Recently at the Developer's Corner, there was some talk of improving Everquest to celebrate their 3rd year live.

Given the sudden arrival of so many new adventurers into the world of Norrath, which greatly exceeded our expectations, we've taken time to enrich the EverQuest experience at lower levels.

At the same time, we're in the process of making a number of changes aimed at improving the game for players of all levels. Stay tuned to this page for a more detailed report.

Some of the changes we are making are substantial and sweeping, others are more cosmetic and fun. At their core they all have the same goal: To allow more entertaining and less frustrating access to the rich and rewarding world for which EverQuest is famous.

Pets
 12:59 EST - Hermana

Hello.
I have an update, rather a correction, on one of the answers to the questions that the Mage Compendium folks sent in.

I was just informed by Scott Hartsman that I made a mistake about the pet experience thing. Pets only take experience from a group if they outdamage the entire group. If they do, then they gain a share as if they were a group member. If they do not, they take no experience at all.

This is how it's been working the whole time, and it makes a lot more sense. I'm sorry for the mistake.

Alan

Would you really rather see EQ'Lizer completely offline, or contribute less than the cost of a single cup of fancy coffee to help us stay around?

In addition, once you sign up for Flashlink here at EQ'Lizer, you also get full access to both EQ Maps and EQ Prices. That's complete access to all THREE of the top EverQuest sites on the internet with one membership. Between our three databases we have over 20,000 items listed, over 10,000 NPC's, over 3,000 different spells, and thousands of quests! This is more information than ANY other EQ site can offer (including the one which is charging up to 25% more for their "premium membership")

We are asking for your help here to allow us to stay online, and doing our best to give you something of great value in return. Our site helps you elevate your game to the next level, and with how inexpensive a membership is, you won't even notice the difference in your wallet!

Your help by signing up is greatly appreciated, and will allow this site to remain online with the most up to date EverQuest information for many years to come.

We thank you for your support and help,

Wednesday, March 6, 2002

Items and Updates
 16:08 EST - Hermana

Maybe you all don't know but usually most emails end up in my box, as well as general comments people make about items. This is done so I can make sure anything that might be listed incorrectly or is missing information about a zone or NPC's can be updated right away.

I recently got a nice letter for a woman who said I should further separate items on the updates. She suggested I have a "Legends" section, a "Over 50" section and a "Newbie Item" section. She felt most players don't really want to know the fine details of a 4ac mantle or a 12ac breastplate.

I can understand where she is coming from. I've been playing EQ actively for about three years and was a long time level 50 before the expansion and was quickly to 60 after. But this still does not mean I do not want to know what items are being added and improved throughout the game that my level 10 or 22 might discover on their travels. I thought I would ask the opinion of you out there, am I wrong in assuming that most people would still like to know what items are being added even if their level 60 character might not use them?

There is one thing I have been doing since I arrived here, and that is not including every tiny item in the updates. I simply assume something like a Shissar Scale, a Essence of Rathe or even... oh what was it I saw on another site's update the other day..amber or emeralds are things you do not want to see spammed under Equipment Updates.

I assume those reading our page have some base knowledge about Everquest and do not need to be spoon-feed 35 "updates" a day that really are not updates but a class modification where the Vah Shir might be added to the list of who can use a rusty dagger. Do you really want to see a list of items that were just added a week ago because they are now dropped by a Xin Thall Centien in Maiden's Eye rather than just a Xin Thall?

Of course doing this would make EQ'Lizer look like it's filled with lots of updates and new items, surely more than any other site out there...but in fact it would not be. It just looks pretty on the outside but once you start to follow all those update links you realize that it's still the same old Icepaw Bangle but this time it drops from not only A Icepaw Kobold but A Icepaw Champion! Is this really worthy of relisting? I thought no, so you see I feel I already filter out minor items for our viewers and would hate to further filter by labeling something "Newbie" or for the over "50" player.

I feel such a update separation is not really something I would care to do. But if I have enough feedback to suggest folks would prefer to have things separated, I will then give it a go. I also am looking at adding a section under "New Items" that will indeed give you a list of recently updated ones, but in a manner that provides useful information instead of adding it to a list because it is now "tiny" sized instead of "small".

Thank you in advance for any feedback!

Tuesday, March 5, 2002

Ring of Vulak
 22:07 EST - Hermana

Posted on Casters Realm today:

I spoke with Alan VanCouvering regarding the Vulak encounter in the Temple of Veeshan.
It was confirmed that the encounter is working as intended. It will be receiving some minor tweaks in this weeks patch, but it is possible to spawn and slay Vulak at this time.

He was also able to get this information from the designer as to why there is no loot from the NPC's killed while trying to spawn Vulak:

'The NPCs in the event are not giving rewards at this time, and this is intentional. If they did, people would simply farm this event for the loot, and that is not the intent.
At some point, the later bosses may give minor rewards, but dont expect anything on par with actual dragon loot. Your reward for winning this is getting a shot at Vulak. Yes, there I spoiled it, but let people know, Vulak does indeed still exist within the Temple of Veeshan.'

Items and Updates
 16:08 EST - Hermana

Maybe you all don't know but usually most emails end up in my box, as well as general comments people make about items. This is done so I can make sure anything that might be listed incorrectly or is missing information about a zone or NPC's can be updated right away.

I recently got a nice letter for a woman who said I should further separate items on the updates. She suggested I have a "Legends" section, a "Over 50" section and a "Newbie Item" section. She felt most players don't really want to know the fine details of a 4ac mantle or a 12ac breastplate.

I can understand where she is coming from. I've been playing EQ actively for about three years and was a long time level 50 before the expansion and was quickly to 60 after. But this still does not mean I do not want to know what items are being added and improved throughout the game that my level 10 or 22 might discover on their travels. I thought I would ask the opinion of you out there, am I wrong in assuming that most people would still like to know what items are being added even if their level 60 character might not use them?

There is one thing I have been doing since I arrived here, and that is not including every tiny item in the updates. I simply assume something like a Shissar Scale, a Essence of Rathe or even... oh what was it I saw on another site's update the other day..amber or emeralds are things you do not want to see spammed under Equipment Updates.

I assume those reading our page have some base knowledge about Everquest and do not need to be spoon-feed 35 "updates" a day that really are not updates but a class modification where the Vah Shir might be added to the list of who can use a rusty dagger. Do you really want to see a list of items that were just added a week ago because they are now dropped by a Xin Thall Centien in Maiden's Eye rather than just a Xin Thall?

Of course doing this would make EQ'Lizer look like it's filled with lots of updates and new items, surely more than any other site out there...but in fact it would not be. It just looks pretty on the outside but once you start to follow all those update links you realize that it's still the same old Icepaw Bangle but this time it drops from not only A Icepaw Kobold but A Icepaw Champion! Is this really worthy of relisting? I thought no, so you see I feel I already filter out minor items for our viewers and would hate to further filter by labeling something "Newbie" or for the over "50" player.

I feel such a update separation is not really something I would care to do. But if I have enough feedback to suggest folks would prefer to have things separated, I will then give it a go. I also am looking at adding a section under "New Items" that will indeed give you a list of recently updated ones, but in a manner that provides useful information instead of adding it to a list because it is now "tiny" sized instead of "small".

Thank you in advance for any feedback!

Wednesday, March 6, 2002

Items and Updates
 16:08 EST - Hermana

Maybe you all don't know but usually most emails end up in my box, as well as general comments people make about items. This is done so I can make sure anything that might be listed incorrectly or is missing information about a zone or NPC's can be updated right away.

I recently got a nice letter for a woman who said I should further separate items on the updates. She suggested I have a "Legends" section, a "Over 50" section and a "Newbie Item" section. She felt most players don't really want to know the fine details of a 4ac mantle or a 12ac breastplate.

I can understand where she is coming from. I've been playing EQ actively for about three years and was a long time level 50 before the expansion and was quickly to 60 after. But this still does not mean I do not want to know what items are being added and improved throughout the game that my level 10 or 22 might discover on their travels. I thought I would ask the opinion of you out there, am I wrong in assuming that most people would still like to know what items are being added even if their level 60 character might not use them?

There is one thing I have been doing since I arrived here, and that is not including every tiny item in the updates. I simply assume something like a Shissar Scale, a Essence of Rathe or even... oh what was it I saw on another site's update the other day..amber or emeralds are things you do not want to see spammed under Equipment Updates.

I assume those reading our page have some base knowledge about Everquest and do not need to be spoon-feed 35 "updates" a day that really are not updates but a class modification where the Vah Shir might be added to the list of who can use a rusty dagger. Do you really want to see a list of items that were just added a week ago because they are now dropped by a Xin Thall Centien in Maiden's Eye rather than just a Xin Thall?

Of course doing this would make EQ'Lizer look like it's filled with lots of updates and new items, surely more than any other site out there...but in fact it would not be. It just looks pretty on the outside but once you start to follow all those update links you realize that it's still the same old Icepaw Bangle but this time it drops from not only A Icepaw Kobold but A Icepaw Champion! Is this really worthy of relisting? I thought no, so you see I feel I already filter out minor items for our viewers and would hate to further filter by labeling something "Newbie" or for the over "50" player.

I feel such a update separation is not really something I would care to do. But if I have enough feedback to suggest folks would prefer to have things separated, I will then give it a go. I also am looking at adding a section under "New Items" that will indeed give you a list of recently updated ones, but in a manner that provides useful information instead of adding it to a list because it is now "tiny" sized instead of "small".

Thank you in advance for any feedback!
