The Hole

Click Here For a Map

This guide contains information collected by our staff and submitted by various parties including Baaldok, Darkbane, Umacat and Bulwei. This advice is not neccessarily the advice of our staff but it is listed as a reference guide.

The Hole is a level 40-55 dungeon that is located underneath Paineel. It is the highest level non-expansion dungeon in the game and is available via the optional patch.

The dungeon has yet to be explored fully but here is what is known at this time. It is full of high level elements and golems. This makes it a perfect hunting ground for Magicians, Druids, Clerics or Rangers who have been itching for a place to use their ward summoned line of spells.

Unfortunately getting in is the hard part. Do not fall into the large hole in the middle of Paineel. Many players did this originally and found themselves falling for 10k damage. GM's will not res you if you do this so be warned.

The entrance to the hole is actually down the previously unused area past the newbie area of Paineel. Down at the bottom where it dead ends is a small pond. One of these stones down here is actually the locked entrance to Paineel.

You will need a key unless you are a small race(small races can squeeze throught he entrance). The keys can be obtained from a vendor in the building across from the entrance of the bank. The key is easy to miss as it looks like a bag. These keys will cost you over 100pp and are NO DROP.

Rogues and bards can also pick the lock. I'm not sure what skill is needed but I do not believe it is set very high.

The entrance is guarded by six elemental warriors. These mobs are level 40-42. They are spaced far enough apart that you can pull them one at a time if you are not careless. 

After you clear the warriors you will see a hallway which leads down. Beware because an elemental crusader(level 43-45) patrols this hallway and can make things sticky if he catches you off guard.

As soon as the hallway ends there are two elementals on the opposite edge. They are hidden from view however so beware. The one on the right is always an elemental crusader. The one on the left can be either a Crusader or a warrior. 

After disposing of these foes you will notice the hall begins to go downwards. Move to where it begins to slope down and turn right. This is a good place to meditate or buff. The only mob that patrols this area is the Crusader which you should have just killed.

After you are ready to continue your journey you will notice a fortress straight ahead guarded by a rock golem(level 47-53) and an elemental crusader. You can pull these each seperately if you target the golem first. 

The fortress is locked and noone has found the key to this point. We have likewise been unable to pick the lock. You can hit the mobs with spells to aggro them if you stand on the top of the bridge however this will bring many mobs at one time and is not recommended.

Instead I recommend you follow the path which leads down into the pit. This will be on your left. Be forewarned that once you drop down you will not be able to get back up without gating. There is no way up that I know except through the locked fortress door(which is the route that the mobs take). 

If you manage to lose some corpses down here there are two possible scenarios to get them. The first involves the obvious summoning of their corpses. The second involves an enchanter casting illusion: earth element on himself and then a party member casting invisibility on him. This will allow him to sneak by most of the mobs up to the pit and will avoid the earth elements seeing him(which can see invisibility). 

I recommend that you fight the first few battles while up on the ledge still so you do not have to fight too many mobs at once. 

The pit is filled with elemental curators(level 45-50), elemental wizards(level 45-50), flighting fiends(level 45-47) and rock golems. It will take a while to clear them out until it is safe to move but once it is clear you should move directly down until you are facing the entrance into the fortress below. This is probably the safest pull location you are going to find down here. 

Inside of the fortress you will find many of the same mobs that you have already encountered as well as ratmen warriors(level 48-50). This is as far as we have gone in. I do not know of any deeper point which is safe to pull to. I would suggest you send in your monk or shadowknight and have him pull to the entrance here. Even with a feigner however it is hard to pull singles here as they do not like to reset. So an enchanter is a definate plus unless you have a large group. 

Beyond the first castle is a large Tower - the tower has a throne room with many golems and elementals, no named spawn has been spotted in this room yet but I suspect the NPC on the throne is a Placeholder. 

Beyond the Tower is a fallen city. There are many shops with withering signs and lots of fallen in walls and cobwebs. This part of the zone is patroled by a couple of Revenents and Wanderers, but the elementals and golems are the main mobs and there are Alot of them. At the back of this part of the zone there is a small pond with a dock, an overlook into the depths of The Hole is here, but it is fatal to fall from this point.

Moving onward to the rear of the fallen city an opening in a large wall leads to a courtyard and another large Library like area, I saw a bejeweled elemental here but have yet to see one fall to know what treasure it keeps. 

To the left of the Library courtyard there is a walkway which heads to the Main Castle. The main throne room had 6 golems spawned and one Stonesoul the Unmoving - a named elemental. The throne had a plain rock golem on it which I suspect to be a placeholder. The real surprise was in the upper back corridor of this Castle, two bedrooms, one contained a common elemental but the other contained a Fiend Duo - Schnozz the Flighty and Kejar the Mighty.

To the right of the Library courtyard another opening leads to a long tunnel down - a very long tunnel down. There are a couple of wandering revenants and wandering ratmen as well as elementals and golems.

At the end of this tunnel a large grotto opens up with a large city of undead at one end, an overlook to The Hole on the right, and another tunnel down on the left. The city is filled with Undead Erudite Revenants, Wanderers, Fallen Erudite skeletons and one Niltoth the Unholy - The apparent Boss Mob of the zone.

The tunnel down leads to the very bottom of The Hole and the future doorway to what I beleive will be The Plane of Underfoot, the door is locked but small races can fit through the gaps on the sides.

The difficulty of this zone I think lies in the Sheer number of mobs that live here, the average room has 4-6 mobs of lvl 50-55, plus 2-3 wandering mobs in proximity.

Items in the hole appear to be random drop for the most part. There are a few known rares but most of the good stuff can be found on normal mobs. Crusaders drop Loam Encrusted armor at a decent rate but I have also heard that Rock Soul and Golems drop it as well. Ratmen have dropped random weapons.

Some items can be found in the Hole include:

Weapons: Bow of the Underfoot, Earthen Blade, Smoldering Brand, Mudman Enforcer.
Loam Encrusted Armor(Wiz, Enc, Mag, Nec): Loam Encrusted Pantaloons, Loam Encrusted Sleeves, Loam Encrusted Cloak, Loam Encrusted Veil, Loam Encrusted Lined Shoes, Loam Encrusted Cap, Loam Encrusted Bracelet.
Withered Leather Armor(Cler, Dru, Shm): Withered Leather Sleeves, Withered Leather Boots, Withered Leather Wristband.
Other Pieces: Imbued Granite Pauldrons.
