Old Textures?
 14:23 EST - jcsmith

Absor gave an answer to the question of why some NPCs in old world zones still have the old textures. Peep this...

That's not too hard to explain.

If you look carefully, you'll probably notice that the NPCs you see that have the old models are 'special' NPCs. For example, the Qeynos guards. This has to do with the way the old models work. Those NPCs are completely separate textures. They are not standard character models with the nifty Qeynos guard armor placed over it, they are complete and separate textures. In Firiona Vie I imagine that the old models you are seeing are the High Elf guards, in Rivervale it's probably them lil' guys with the funny hats. The same thing goes for them.

Because they are not standard PC models, they didn't get replaced by the new models. In a way they aren't any different than Orcs or fire beetles. So they didn't get a make over with Luclin.

Sony Online Entertainment Inc., today announced that EverQuest®: The Shadows of Luclin(TM) sold more than 120,000 units in the first day at retail, making this one of the largest PC game debuts in history. Luclin, the newest expansion pack to EverQuest, the world's most successful massively multiplayer online role-playing game, was made available nationwide on December 4, 2001. ``With the holidays just around the corner, Luclin couldn't have come at a better time,'' said Bob McKenzie, director of merchandising, GameStop Inc. ``With so many current EverQuest subscribers, Luclin is on the must-have list for a lot of gamers this year and first day sales were proof of that.''

AMD/Shadowhaven Crash Fix
 14:15 EST - jcsmith

This was posted at the EQ Developer's Corner this morning...

We apologize for the problems some of you have experienced getting into EverQuest after the launch of Luclin. As of just a bit ago, we patched a new graphics DLL that fixes the problems some of our users (AMD users in particular) were experinceing crashing to desktop. This does not mean that all crashes to desktop have been fixed, but this code had the potential for a lot of side effects, and fixing it is a big step forward.

In the coming week you will see us concentrating on fixing many of the problems that remain. We are also hard at work on optimizations for both framerate and loading time. Load time is a particularly big target for us to work on, and we have some promising results in development. Our goal is to have that code out to you as soon as possible, but it needs to really be tested properly before we will feel comfortable doing that.

In addition, we're also working on server side stability in a few zones, most notably Hollowshade Moor. These should be taken care of very rapidly as well.

Thanks very much for your patience.

Luclin Update 2
 15:20 EST - jcsmith

Absor posted the following Luclin update at the EQ Developer's Corner...

We're still working on some issues and we've fixed others. Keep in mind that we have a bunch of people working on things, so most of these items are on the top of someone's list.
While some of these issues only affect a tiny portion of our customers, we want to give a full and complete rundown of issues we are aware of. We will be keeping you updated as things change. Also, check the patch messages for more information.

Fixed since release (in no particular order, by the way):

- Bard songs and group buffs (these were related problems)
- Familiars no longer get added to hate lists and don't cause you to crash if you die
- Wolf form factions with the new models are now in line with intent
- Several face/hair issues, such as not being allowed to choose bald and some facial hair.
- /follow bug that caused folks to crash
- Transparence issues with i810 display adapters
- Crash bug related to root and gate
- Various item and content fixes
- Crash problems in certain zones


Stuff in the works, some of these will be fixed with the patch on December 8th (again, in no particular order, most are priority one for the person working on them):

- K6 Processors: We're working to fix, or provide a work-around for problems AMD K6 users are experiencing when entering some zones. Shadowhaven is one of those zones.
- Reducing loading and zoning times
- Crashes occurring for some people when logging in
- Some players are crashing in Hollowshade Moor
- Some characters being 'stuck' if game crashed while playing that character
- Slow framerate on some fast computers
- Large fonts and text that extends outside their boxes is affecting a few users.
- Choppy, buggy, faint or flat sounds for some sound configurations.
- A few people that don’t have Shadows of Luclin installed are seeing "Entering Luclin" when logging in and then crashing
- Some systems have mouse pointers which are being lost or duplicated
- Characters changing to wolf form look tiny
- Some people are seeing Vah Shir as male humans
- Some spells (mostly 'bolt' spells) not displaying effects
- A bug exists that causes random crashes when exiting game to server select, and then going back into game.
- Working on getting facial hair tinting for the half-elves, dark elves, high elves, and dwarf females working
- Faces that sometimes don't remain as selected
- A bug that was giving classes that had the old experience penalties more alternate advancement experience than they should have been getting.

Note: Those without Velious should be able to access the Alternate Advancement interface by hitting the 'v' key.

Also note: The inability to adjust gamma setting with some cards is an issue with cards that just don't support that ability. It's not really something we can fix in code. We are investigating to see if there are any possible workarounds out there for these older cards that are no longer supported by the manufacturer.

Thank you all for your patience. We are working hard to get these issues straightened out as soon as possible.

Patch Notes
 13:55 EST - jcsmith

Here's the scoop on this morning's patch...

*Fixed Bugs*

- Fixed a bug that was preventing characters from being bald (those that were allowed to).
- Halved the speed of the new Skeleton walk and run animations because they were playing about twice as fast as they should have been.
- Fixed a bug that was causing all of the facial features (hair, beard, etc...) to switch back to default in certain cases when you switched from one face to another (faces with special eyes, like the faces with eye patches and white eye).
- Fixed a crash bug with /follow.
- Found and Fixed the crash bug with soldungb and everfrost and other places.
- Fixed transparency code with i810 display adapters
- Fixed a problem introduced into the sound engine that causes framerate problems and sound stutter in some places.
- Fixed a crash bug that had to do with dying from your familiar effect.
- Research the group buff bug and fixed it.
- Fixed a crash bug with root and gate.
- Various item and content fixes.

*Hot Items* (The items we're considering top priority)

- Bard issues: We're working to correct the problems bards have been experiencing with their songs.
- K6 Processors: We're working to fix, or provide a work-around for problems AMD K6 users are experiencing when entering some zones.
- Armor layer tinting (e.g. tinting your armor without tinting your skin)

We are reviewing the issues sent to us as they are sent in and will be doing daily patches as necessary to get everything working to your and our satisfaction as quickly as possible. Please remember that EverQuest users need to have DirectX 8.1 and the appropriate drivers for their video card. Visit our tech support center first if you are having issues: http://everquest.station.sony.com/support/luclin/index.jsp

Luclin First Impressions
 13:52 EST - jcsmith

Gamespot gave their first impressions of Shadows of Luclin today. Here's a clip...

Though we only got a brief glimpse of it, the game's new character class--the beastlord--and the new character race--the Vah Shir--look interesting enough. The tiger-like Vah Shir inhabit a city known as Shar Vahl, a town that resembles a storybook Arabian palace, complete with gold-leaf trim and potted palms. Vah Shir may become bards, rogues, shaman, warriors, and the game's new character class, the beastlord--a hybrid fighter/spell-caster. Though we didn't get to spend much time with the new class, the beastlord seems to most closely resemble that of the EverQuest ranger--a class that complements its fighting ability with useful spells. At higher levels, beastlords will gain new abilities, and past level 50, all characters will actually be able to pursue different paths of advancement to help differentiate their characters from others.
