Howling Stones

Click Here for a Map

Howling Stones is also known as Charisis. It is a high level dungeon which can only be entered via a key which you must obtain through a quest.

The entrance to the zone can be found in the canyon area of Overthere. You will see a temple like entrance inside of the canyon. At the very bottom will be the entrance. Simply place the Howling Stones key onto the button and click the left mouse button.

Be warned that you may well be attacked as soon as entering the dungeon. Both living and undead beings patrol right near the entrance. The entrance is also not the exit so you will be stranded upon entering the zone if you get into trouble. It may be wise to get two sets of keys as a result although this is a long process(much more tedious than obtaining a key to Sebilis).

There are two standard tactics to gain a foothold into Howling Stones. The first is to have a character cast invisibility or invisibility to undead on all characters. This will at least reduce your risk to half way. You can then stand at the entrance until all the living beings move far enough away and pray that nothing walks through the entrance way.

The first method involved the cleric going in first with divine aura. Tanks go in immediately after. This way they can taunt off of the cleric and hopefully stave off the immediate onslaught.

The second method also involves going in invisible and invis to undead but it requires a bit more luck. The opening room has four doors(one to each side) which are each guarded by golems. However on the sides of the walkway you will notice a pit that you can fall into. This leads to a room with a gazebo and two more doors out. There is a very small ledge in this room. If invisibility does not wear off of anyone you can levitate to the ledge and then meditate/pull to there. It is a safe spot(to my knowledge so long as everyone stays cramped up in the one tiny area). The problem here is the pathing and 3d AI bugs which can lead to trains.

Some items which can be found here include the Ebonwood Cudgel and the Scorpikus Stinger Shank.
