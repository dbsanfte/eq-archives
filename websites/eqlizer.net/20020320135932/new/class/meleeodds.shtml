Melee Attack Odds


This chart was created by Sam Schlansky(Stusser of Seekers of Norrath) ok 3D News.Net and he has graciously allowed us to post it here:

These tables are derived from information given on the Ranger's mailing list. The original post can be found archived at The Ranger's Glade site. Thanks go out to all of these resources and especially the Everquest designer who actually (gasp!) shared some nitty-gritty data with us.

As a short synopsis... the formula for Double Attack is (SKILL+LEVEL)/500 with the result converted to a percentage value. The formula to derive Dual Wield is (SKILL+LEVEL)/600, also with the result converted to a percentage value. All classes cap at 200 for both Dual Wield and Double Attack with the exception of Rogues and Monks; Rogues cap at 210 for Dual Wield only and Monks at 250 for both.

My earlier chart had monks using the same (LEVEL+1)*5 skill cap progression as all other classes for dual wield; many emails have convinced me that is wrong... for dual wield only. I've seen no evidence that double attack uses a different skill cap progression from any other class. The question is... which formula does it use? (LEVEL+1)*7 or (LEVEL*7)+5? It seems more logical to be the former rather than the latter, so I've redone a whole monk chart using it.

I have had some tenuous confirmation from Rogues that they use (Level+1)*6 for dual wield only; so I've made a table specifically for Rogues. Interestingly, I got quite a few people telling me that the skill progression was (LEVEL*6)+5 and one or two even saying it was (LEVEL*5)+25 but it just seems more logical that the formula is (LEVEL+1)*multiplier to me. I was just amazed (and impressed) that enough people were still playing Rogues to send the emails!

Using the charts below, you can tell that Rogues have a miniscule advantage over other melee classes while Monks have a huge one! They cap off dual wield at level 35! The designer justified the Monk's advantage by saying that the speed of their hand-to-hand attacks never gets faster than 30, and they don't have any advantages that magic weapons with special effects can give us. Still, it's a fairly major advantage; at level 50 Monks are 8% more likely to get four attacks than any other class.

The four attacks column is left at 0.00% until level 29 for normal classes since (again, according to the designer) it's impossible to get four attacks until your DW skill reaches 150. Monks achieve 150 skill at level 21, eight ahead of everybody else.

The formulae to figure out three attacks and four attacks are considerably more complicated. To get your odds of getting three attacks, you have to take 2*((100-DA)*DA*DW). I'm not going to get into why, simply because it confuses the hell out of me too. I hate probability math. The four attacks numbers are considerably easier, simply take (DA2*DW). You can probably figure it out on your own, but DA = Double Attack and DW = Dual Wield.

I assume that the skill cap progression is standard for most classes at (LEVEL+1)*5. Rogues use (LEVEL+1)*6 and Monks use (LEVEL+1)*7 for dual wield.

I've gotten several requests to get this information in its original Excel format. So be it. If you have any more data on this stuff (particularly hard information on rogue and warrior skill caps) feel free to mail me.

I hope you find this information useful. Good hunting!
