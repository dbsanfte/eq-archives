GM Events.
Ah the gm event. Upon hearing this word most people search for a teleport to the location, as if it was life or death. The most recent major GM event was held on Halloween, when they held a ceremony of types to the date and the opening of the plane of hate. Unfortunately the event turned bad. As players were being killed by the dozens, lag started flying, and corpses were being duped, people who didn't even want to be in the event were dying, being butchered by the hundreds as they tried to get to their corpses and leave. Bugs were not absent, the zone line to commons was being a little tricky and throwing players back to high hold pass. People were crashing non stop, and many just shut down their cpu.

This type of event is perfect in showing how a gm event shouldn't be. It was fun for many, but the lag and the corpse issues were getting out of hand. Most events I have seen allow the player to choose if they wish to carry on or not. I personally enjoyed the event, but I find that many players didn't. The weapons being droped by the mobs was A Grade, but in the situation, was VERY hard to get. If you are fighting something, but have 75% pl, it gets hard. Many lag spikes were going on in all the zones, not only effecting the woods. GM events are a welcome addition any time they happen, but they are rare, and usually end in alot of people angry and confused.

On the Halloween event, I know a few people who lost 2 levels when they didn't even want to be in the event. Myself, with many others feel that GM events should be much more planned out, and have a more sense of true role-playing, such as the ButcherBlock hammer saga. The turn of events in that event have been unmatched as of this date, and I doubt they will be. Only a few people were in the quest for the hammer, but I found the entire server enjoyed it. It was THE mass event. It had a beginning, a middle, a turn of events, and an end. Many people think that there are not enough events, I agree with them, but, GMs should not just start doing the standard event. That would be creating 3 high level, and insanely powerful mobs, and attacking players, then at the end they drop a few very nice items.

People want to see more events like the Hammer quest, they want to see a saga of events unfold infront of them, they want to see a quest in the true sense of role-playing, with a greater reward at the end, after a long time of adventuring and putting your life on the line for the great sense of everything. The hammer quest only rewarded one person, but I believe many think that they had the best reward of all, being able to be part of the best GM event of all time.
