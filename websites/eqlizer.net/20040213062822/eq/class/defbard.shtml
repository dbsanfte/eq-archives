Nolar’s Definitive Guide to Being a Bard, Version 1, 12/21/03

About this Guide

            I have searched far and wide, and found no really good guides for bards, so I decided to make one myself.  This will probably be a continuing piece of work if I do not leave Everquest.  Sections about AAs, and high-end abilities and raiding to be added later when I have more experience with them.  I write this guide with intention of creating the most complete, detailed, thorough, and useful guide ever for bards.  It is very long, because I am going to discuss everything I can think about, including things that might be obvious.  Lots of things that are obvious to me I have never seen other bards replicate, so I feel the length is justified.    Everything I know about the bard class will be revealed in this guide, no secrets held back. 

Background Info

            Hello folks, my name is Nolar.  I am a level 53 Bard on the Maelin Starpyre server.  Before I go into details and discuss the Bard class, I’d like to give you folks a little background information about myself.  First of all, I do not claim to be uber, but I do consider myself a skilled player.   I have never reached level 65, being a casual player, but plan to get there somewhere in the future.  I decided to write this guide because I noticed an utter lack of bards and enchanters on my server.  The bards that I have met I find are also playing the class in a manner not even close its potential.  Also, I find that there is a general lack of understanding of how bards work and what they are capable of.  I hope that bards and non-bards will benefit from this guide.

            I first started playing Everquest when the game first came out years ago.  I stopped playing just before Kunark came out.  I recently returned to the game in July of 2003, to find that things have gotten 10 times easier than when I first played, and that is not an overstatement.  Back in the day, there was no bazaar.  Platinum was extremely hard to come by, weapons and armor were expensive and absolutely crap compared to what is common nowadays.  To give you an idea, Silvery War Axe (6/22 1 Handed Slash) were the norm when it came to weapons being used, and the Short Sword of the Ykesha was probably the best in the game (8/24 1 Handed Slash, Proc: Ykesha).  It would also not be uncommon for people to be fighting in places like Sol B and Lower Guk in their suits of Bronze armor, since they did not have time to camp for Rubicite.  There was a time when Solusek Ro armor (Crafted, Lambent, etc), did not exist.  There were no focus effect, or flowing thought, or KEI to help casters out, who had to stare at the damn spellbook while medding.  The experience attained per kill was MUCH less, and you lost MUCH more experience when you died.  There were no clerics around to rez you, and paladins didn’t have rez either.  When you died, you died HARD.  When you consider that every member in a 6 man group was significantly weaker than what most newbies are wearing today, the total effect made for fighting mobs and surviving much more difficult.  So, why am I giving everyone all this info about the old Everquest?  I find that in terms of skill and knowing how to play their classes, players nowadays are generally lacking compared to those of old.  This is to be expected considering the game is that much easier.  If you made it to level 40, you had to know what you were doing, and had to group effectively.  With mass experience lost, hell levels, and no rezzing, you learn to be damn good real fast.  Nowadays, it is no longer a battle for survival really.  However, if the players nowadays were raised to the caliber that was required back in the day, you would see much more ownage at any level, and much more effectiveness, and not just from level 65 uber guilds.  When thing were that rough, in my opinion, there were three classes that were essential for a good group.  The first is a cleric obviously.  The second and third are the enchanter and bard, for crowd control.  Coming in fourth I would have to say is the shaman class, with their backup healing and slowing abilities, which become crucial at higher levels.  My objective for writing this guide is to show how effective bards can be solo, and in group situations, where they shine the most.  Coming back after years of absence, I expected Everquest to be just as hard, so I was prepared for the worst, and optimized my Bard in preparation for dying a lot.  Because I came back with that mentality, and focused on being effective, I found that there was almost no group that I got into which I could not lead, and make function, since the game is that much easier.  With that said, let me start talking about the bard class.

Background Info About the Bard Class and Playing a Bard

            Playing a bard is not easy.  Your hitpoints and AC are low.  You cannot tank, cannot heal, cannot nuke for massive damage, and generally cannot compare to fighters or pet classes for damage unless you quad-DoT (which I almost never do, and will discuss why later).  Playing a bard effectively also requires lots of manual dexterity (see subsequent sections on juggling songs).  What is good about the bard however, is their ability to do a little bit of everything.  If you want to see big numbers while doing damage, or be able to heal large amounts, or be able to tank, the Bard class is not for you.  They are the jack of all trades, and masters of none (with two exceptions: running, and resistances).  Bards have the absolute fastest run speed in the game, and can buff all resists to insane numbers.  They sit right in the middle: they tank better than casters who crumple in one hit, but cannot compare to rangers and plate wearing tanks.  Bards only take two hits to die =D.  They also contribute significant damage.  Another huge advantage is being able to cast while running.  That’s right.  You can snare mobs, mez, charm, and dot while running around like a headless chicken away from mobs. 

To play the bard class requires lots of manual dexterity, changing instrument with weapons and juggling and weaving songs, all at once.  A good sense of timing is also needed.  You will be very busy playing a bard.  If you want to sit and turn on autoattack, don’t play one.

What bards are really good at: running and buffing resists which I mentioned before.  The other key abilities of bards: Mezzing, charming, solo kiting, fear kiting, and pulling.  I believe bards can be better pullers than paladins, clerics, and monks in most cases.  Bards are also the gods of LDoN (will explain later). 

If you want to solo, the combination of being able to run really fast and juggle 3 dots (4 with sow or level 53 Selo’s song) cannot be beat.  The mobs can’t catch you.  Or, you can use your snare song and then dot away.  Another option is to use a snare/fear combo and beating the crap out of the mob while it runs away.  If you get adds while kiting, you can just charm them and make them help you out!  If you start out fighting by charming a mob and sending it at another , they will beat the crap out of each other.  When charm breaks and both are lower on health, it makes it that much faster to dot them to death.  Everyone knows that mobs outdamage players.

In group situations, there is no equal to the bard for pulling quickly and effectively.  Our lull song takes 0 mana, and only 4 seconds to cast.  Clerics have to stand up to pacify, which means they are not medding for mana for heals.  Clerics and Paladins I believe cannot use songs to see through the eyes of a mob, so that you can target mobs hiding behind corners.  In addition, you do not have to be able to see the mob to cast lull on it.  This combination enables bards to pull singles most of the time quickly.  If 2 mobs come on a pull, you can simply mez it.  This leads me to the most valuable group ability of a bard: MEZZING.  With adequate charisma, there is nothing a bard cannot mez.  Mezzing up to 4 mobs at a time is very doable with some skill.  Turn an impossible 5 mobs vs your 6 group members fight into 5 single fights where your group only has to take on one mob at a time. Mez takes 0 mana, and with the ability to mez up to 4 things at once, I believe it is one of the greatest equalizers in the unfair world of mobs vs. players, and on the level of cleric Complete Heal, and Shaman/Enchanter Slow.  All of this is just an overview.  Everything I discussed above will be discussed in detail, with an explanation of how to do it, in subsequent sections. 

Starting Off As a Bard

Stats

  There are 7 stats and I list them here in order of importance: charisma, strength, dex/sta, agility, wisdom/intelligence.  Wisdom and intelligence are almost useless.  Your skills will go up if you place nothing in them.  Agility I have not found a use for yet (several points gives you 1 AC big whoop).  Stamina helps with hitpoints, and it can’t hurt spending a few points in this if you plan to wield heavy weapons.  I wouldn’t recommend spending any points in stamina, since you have a stamina song (Level 3 Jaxon’s Jig of Vigor) if you ever need stamina.  Dexterity is helpful if you want your weapons to proc, which can be quite useful.  I’ll talk more about dexterity later.  Strength is important because it adds to ATK, which enables you to hit mobs frequently, which adds significantly to your DPS (damage per second) over the long run.  Charisma is important because it is the key stat which enables you to mez, charm, and lull mobs successfully.  Throughout the guide, there will be examples of why charisma is important.

            Vah Shir: has the lowest base charisma, but significantly higher strength than the other 3 races.  The high stamina means more hitpoints, which never hurts.  The lowest dexterity of all 4 races might make proccing a little problematic.  Recommended: put starting points primarily into charisma and some in dexterity if you want to proc.  I do not know much about the Vah Shir race, and judging by the stats, I would not pick this race. 

            Human: Humans have no infravision.  This means you are rather blind at night, at least until you get your Mask of Deception (I will talk more about this later).  They have good strength, good stamina, decent dexterity, and charisma equal to that of the wood elf and half elf.  Recommended: put points into charisma, and possibly a few points into dex and/or str.  I personally would not pick this race, since I can’t stand not having infravision.

            Wood Elf:  They have infravision, but are lacking in strength, and stamina.  They have decent dexterity, and charisma equal to that of the human and half elf.  Recommended: put points into charisma, strength and/or stamina. 

            Half Elf: They have infravision, and are somewhat lacking in strength and stamina.  They do have the best dexterity and charisma.  Recommended: put points into charisma, strength and/or stamina. 

            I myself am a wood elf.  Looking back, I would have picked a half elf for their higher starting strength and stamina, and dexterity.  However, in the grand scheme of things, which race you pick really won’t matter much.  As a small wood elf, being able to jump over trolls (Elfmuncher, you have a big ass) and packs of mobs in dungeons and tight spaces has saved me and my groups many times when I needed to get to mobs to mez them. 

Singing

            At level 1, you get a song called Chant of Battle.  It affects your whole group, and buffs strength.  To sing it, simply memorize the song, and click on it.  If you miss a note, click on it again.  The spell icon should become darkened a little, meaning you are singing it.  It takes a little time to start up, but once it’s activated, it will keep on singing automatically without you having to do anything.  You can get bashed out of singing, so you have to start up the song again.  Even if you stop singing, the effect stays for the length of its duration (around 17 seconds).  To stop singing, just click on the song again, or another other song.  A better way to sing is to use the ALT key with the number keys to sing and stop singing songs.  An even better way is to hotkey your songs, and sing and stop singing by pressing the corresponding number once.  That is all there is to singing.  Once you have mastered singing, you will need to master juggling (singing multiple songs at once), which I will discuss later. 

Weapon Skills

            Bards can use three types of one handed weapons: piercing, blunt, and slash.  I highly, highly recommend getting one of each as soon as possible, and keeping all skills capped, and letting none lag.  Each category has a great weapons which are useful to a bard.

I will discuss each skill in order.  I will not discuss any tradeskills, which I have no experience with whatsoever.

a) alcohol tolerance: when you have enough money to spend on alcohol, and can afford to buy it, and have time to recover from being drunk, train in it as much as you can.  When your skill gets high enough, drinking alcohol will actually increase your strength by a small amount.  Priority: LOW

b) bind wound: almost useless considering we have multiple songs which add to regen.  When you are bandaging, you can’t juggle songs.  I’m not sure, but I don’t think you can use an instrument with the bandage while binding.  Priority: VERY LOW. 

            c)  defense and offense: these skills will go up by themselves.

d)  hand to hand: in the long run, the damage you can do with this skill is utterly insignificant.  I wouldn’t bother trying to get this skill up at all.  Priority: VERY LOW.

e)  swimming: useful in places like kedge keep, and plane of water (never been there).  Swimming is super easy to raise up.  It can’t hurt to have it.  I’d wait till level 40 and then build it.  It takes several hours to get the skill to 200.  Go to a place with water, and a wall.  I use the lake under the caster’s guild in west Freeport.  There is a gate there in the lake.  If you swim parallel to it, hit numlock to autorun and automatically swim up against the wall.  Go to sleep, come back the next morning, and you should still be swimming, but at a much faster rate.  IF you have an enduring breath item, just find any lake in a town, and swim to the bottom of it and press numlock.  You cannot die by drowning, and your skill will be raised just the same. 

f)  throwing: absolutely useless skill.  Why throw knives or whatnot at mobs when you can pull using songs such as dispel (Syvelian’s Anti Magic Aura, level 40). 

g)  percussion and string instruments: these are the two instrument types which you will be using the most.  Percussion for when you run with Selo’s Accelerando, and stringed when you sing Hymn of Restoration (level 6).  They will get up there by themselves.

h)  brass:  When you first get this skill at level 11, train once in it.  Then wait until level 18 when you get the song Brusco’s Boastful Bellow before you build it up.  Bellow is an area attack song, which will get you killed if you sing it near mobs and inside a town.  The safest place to sing this is in a spot where there are no mobs which wander by, if you know of such a place.  If not, I can think of one in the Oasis of Marr.  Coming from North Ro, run against the shoreline all the way south.  Head way way way south until you hit the wall at the very end, past the orc camp in the southeast corner of the zone.  Turn left, and swim out to sea a little bit, while hugging the wall to your right.  You should be able to enter a little niche, where no mobs ever enter, and can feel free to Bellow away.  Priority:  KEEP INSTRUMENT SKILLS UP =)

i)  wind:  To build this skill up, wait until level 16 when you get Tarew’s Aquatic Ayre.  Sing this song with a wind instrument before you go to sleep or when you are in the bazaar, and it will build itself.  Priority: See brass instruments above.

j)  meditate: train in this skill once, and that’s it.  It allows you to memorize songs faster, and cannot go above a skill of 1.

k)  forage: don’t bother.  The skill is capped too low for you to find anything of use.  If you need food, buy it, or ask a caster to summon some for you.

l)  dual wield: this skill will cap itself after you train in it once.  Bards should always be using two weapons after they get this skill.  Although they can use shields, I don’t believe I’ve ever seen a bard using one after they’ve gotten dual wield.

m)  hide/sneak:  I would build these two skills up at the same time.  They cap at a rather low number, and are easy enough to build up by activating occasionally.  Hide, when it works, can be used to break charm.  Priority: LOW

n)  sense traps/disarm traps:  Very useful for LDoN.  There are lots of traps in LDoN, and building these skills up cannot hurt.  Priority: MEDIUM.

o) safe fall:  This skill is extremely useful.  Bards run super fast.  Because they wear heavy plate armor, this means that they can take lots of damage when running up a hill then falling down.  It builds itself.  If you are in a hurry to get it up, go to a hilly place like great divide, butcherblock mountains, or oasis of marr, and run up and down a hill while singing heal song.  This will build it up fast enough.  Priority: HIGH

p) instill doubt: I have never ever used this skill.  I cannot think of a situation where it can be used in.  If it works, the monster runs away, feared.  This will get you killed in any zone you are fighting in.  In addition, we have a fear song (Angstlich’s Appalling Screech, level 26).  Priority: SUPER LOW.

q)  track:  Track is a SUPER useful skill.  It enables you to track to see if named mobs are up in LDoN, or just about any other zone.  When you get this skill, use it as much as possible to get it capped.  If you are in a rush, I’d even put points into this skill.  Priority: HIGH.

r)  pick lock:  I honestly have no idea how this skill works, and whether or not you need lockpicks or whatever.

s)  parry:  Useful, since it enables you to avoid more damage, just like dodge.  By the time you get it at level 53, to get it capped, you will need to get beat on by mobs a lot.  At this level, that is certainly not what you want.  Because of the difficulty of raising this skill given the level you get it at, some points spent might be warranted.

Levelling, and Juggling Songs

Levels 1 - 2

            At level 1, you get the song Chant of Battle, which increases strength.  Keep this song going at all times.  You need to build up your singing skill so you don’t miss notes as much.  For the first 5 levels, you don’t really need any equipment except the cloth armor you will probably get from the mobs you kill around your newbie area.  Keep all the stuff on their corpses, and sell them for money (if you need the money).

            At level 2, you get your second song, Chords of Dissonance.  It is an area song, which damages everything surrounding you.  Be careful not to have this song up when you are wandering around town.  The guys there will KEEL you.  Since you get your second song here, this is where you should learn how to start juggling multiple songs.

Juggling up to 3 songs

            Juggling songs is not hard.  It just takes a little practice, and some timing.  Every time you start up a song, you will see a little meter appear at the top of your screen, which shows you how close you are to finish singing a song.  IT takes about 4 seconds to start up a song.  Most song will last for 3 ticks, or about 6 seconds a tick, for a total of 18 seconds.    This means you have 18 seconds to cast 2 songs!  This is how you do it.  While fighting a mob, sing Chant of Battle.  After 4 seconds, the song should be activated.  Stop singing it, and start to sing Chords of Dissonance.  If you do not miss, You should have both Chant of Battle and Chords of Dissonance going, in about 8 seconds.  If you do happen to miss a note, simply start singing it again, and repeat.  It does not get any harder than that for juggling 2 songs.  You have another 8 seconds before Chant of Battle wears off.  So, after Chords gets activated, stop singing it and start singing Chant of Battle again.  You can keep both effects up forever like this.  Hotkeys will make this easier.  For example, if you have Chant on 1, and Chords on 2, this is how it will look like: Press 1 (to start singing Chant), wait 4 seconds, press 1 or 2 (to stop singing), press 2 immediately again (to start singing Chords), wait 4 seconds, press 1 (to stop singing), and repeat. 

            Juggling 3 songs is also quite simple.  Just add another song to the method I described above after you sing Chords of Dissonance.  Keep in mind you have to be level 3 to juggle 3 songs, since you get your third song at level 3 =D.

            Back in the day, there was no song timer to indicate how long songs took.  If you try not to look at the timer, and count 4 seconds in your head, you will learn the timing and eventually be able to juggle songs perfectly as they are activated, without relying on it.  At my stage, I can juggle 4 songs, while switching 4 different instruments, all while walking around, strafing, changing camera views, and attacking mobs perfectly without dropping a single song effect.  I’m not boasting, simply stating that situations do get complex enough to require that kind of skill later on in the game.

Levels 3-5

            At level 3, learn to juggle 3 songs.  You should be fine getting up to level 5 just hunting in the newbie grounds by your home town.  Try to collect enough money to buy at least cloth armor, or get a fine steel or velium weapon from a higher level character, at minimum. 

Juggling 4 songs

            At level 4, you can start to juggle 4 songs.  Juggling 4 is slightly more complicated than juggling three.  You better be able to keep 3 songs going at once before you even attempt 4.  It is also hard to do at low levels because of your low singing skill, which makes you miss lots of notes.  I would aim to get this skill down around level 20, where you won’t miss as much. 

            Let me explain how it is done.   If you rely on the timer on your screen to cast songs, you must learn to cancel the song a FRACTION of a second before the bar reaches 0, and IMMEDIATELY start singing the next song.  You basically stop singing a song and start singing the next one just before the bar hits 0 and disappears.  This might be a bit confusing.  This is because the song actually activates BEFORE the bar hits 0 and disappears.  Try it with just two songs first.  Sing 1 song and RIGHT BEFORE the meter indicates it is done, cancel it, and sing the next one.  You will notice something like this if you are doing it right.  Take the songs Chords of Dissonance and any other song. 

(Start singing Chords)….

Your song ends.

A bat winces

When you start singing the second song, it will end singing Chords BEFORE the actual effect kicks in where the bat winces.  Again, to do this right, you must time it perfectly if you want to keep all 4 songs going.  If you have 4 songs hotkeyed 1-4, this is how it would look:

            Press 1, wait until just before the bar reaches 0, press 2 twice very quickly (cancels and starts singing next song), wait until just before the bar reaches 0, press 3 twice very quickly, wait until just before the bar reaches 0, press 4 twice very quickly, wait until just before the bar reaches 0, and repeat by pressing 1 twice very quickly to start the chain again. 

            Done perfectly, your screen will have the “your song ends” message before the actual effect from the song kicks in.  If you don’t use the timer, you can develop a feel of exactly when to stop and start singing a song.  It gets even harder when you have to switch instruments, and weapons, and fight at the same time later on. 

Juggling 5 songs

            Do not bother, because it cannot be done.  Song effects last around 17 seconds.  Since each song takes 4 seconds to cast, if you do a perfect chain, it takes a whole 16 seconds, not enough to sing another song without dropping the effect of another one.  The objective of a chain is to keep 4 effects up at once.  A miss or two will be enough to break a 4 song chain, although just for a brief instant.  IF anyone can possibly think of a manner to juggle 5 songs by SINGING, and not dropping effects, I would be interested in knowing how because from what I understand, it is impossible.  If you do not need to juggle 4 songs, just juggle 3.  Even with misses, you have ample time to recover and keep the chain going forever. 

D’OH!  Where do I go from here?

            Because I want to write the most complete and thorough bard guide ever written, I want to discuss everything and not miss any aspects which are important.  This leads to the problem of how to present all the information in a logical, easy to follow format.  Because bards are so versatile, after the baby levels (1-5), there are many paths which the bard can take.  For people like myself who are 100% untwinked, careful planning and money saving for equipment and instruments will be needed.  Depending on what group you are in, the songs you will be singing will change, which adds to the problem of how I discuss everything.  Until I find a way to do it better, the following sections will be somewhat scattered.  From here on, I will talk about more advanced strategies.  I’m also going to assume that you can juggle 3 songs perfectly, and 4 songs reasonably well.

Songs

            For a list of songs and their effects, and where to purchase the songs, go to one of the following sites: everquest.allakhazam.com (under the spells section), or dotnetdiva.com (under the songs section).  Almost every song in our arsenal has a use, which might not be obvious simply by reading the description of it.  I’ll give a basic description of songs which I think are important, and their uses.  Note: I know nothing about LDoN songs. 

Level 1: Chant of Battle: Group strength, dexterity, and AC.  Effect can be enhanced by percussion instrument.  Can be very useful.  Strength for attacking, and dex for proccing.  It does not stack with the level 10 song Anthem de Arms, which has group haste.  So unless you have an enchanter or shaman hasting the group, it is better to sing Anthem.

Level 2: Chords of Dissonance: Area attack song DoT song.  Effect can be enhanced by stringed instrument for more damage.  Although it initially does very little damage, at higher levels, with a good stringed instrument, it can be used to destroy hoards of green mobs when combined with level 18 song Denon’s Disruptive Discord and a brass instrument.  I generally do not use this song, as area effects get you killed fast.

Level 4: Lyssa’s Locating Lyric: Useful for finding your own corpse if you die in some place like the Karanas.  Also good for helping others find their corpses.  In LDoN, some of the whirlwind mob thingeys are hard to target after they’ve died, which means you can’t loot the items you need.  This song will enable you to target them, and will make looting easier.

Level 5: Selo’s Accelerando: This song rocks.  With a good percussion instrument, you will run increasingly faster and progress toward a ridiculous speed as you get higher in levels.  Great for traveling, and is always memmed in my book if I am outdoors.

Level 6: Hymn of Restoration: Super useful song.  Group regeneration of HP.  Almost always sung at downtime with a stringed instrument for group regen.  At higher levels, it can actually be worth it at times to not swing weapons or DoT, and sing this song, with another healing song such as Niv’s Melody of Preservation (Level 47).  For example, in the plane of innovation things hit hard, and take long to kill.  Even with a shaman slowing, things hurt, and your healer can be hard pressed to keep everyone in good health.  Combined with druid or shaman regeneration spells, the net effect of the health regeneration is VERY good.  The healer will almost never have to heal anyone except the main tank, and fights can keep going and going. 

Level 7: Jonathan’s Whistling Warsong: Use only if you are soloing.  Grants yourself haste and strength.

Level 8: Kelin’s Lugbrious Lament: One of the most useful songs in our entire arsenal.  It is a single target lull.  What this does is makes monsters less aggressive.  If a monster is lulled, chances are you can pull his buddies without him adding and making your life difficult.  You do not need to see the mob to cast it.  In LDoN for example, you can sneak toward a doorway until you see just a little bit of the mob you want to lull.  Once it is targeted, you can lull them with this song.  The aggro radius reduction is enormous from what I have found.  I have been able to lull 5 mobs, and pull without a problem.  If your charisma is lull or if mobs have high resists, you will occasionally fail to lull.  This might cause the mob to think you just attacked them, and come rushing at you, with all his unlulled buddies.  This can get you killed in LDoN.  A high charisma will prevent mobs rushing at you when you fail a Lull.  Because bards generally have high charisma, I believe they can lull better than paladins and clerics.  It takes 4 seconds per lull, and takes 0 mana as well.  Lull is also useful in a situation where your group is fighting, and there is a mob wandering toward your group but has not aggroed yet.  When I fight, I am constantly changing camera angles using F9, and moving the camera around using ALT + direction keys.  This enables me to scan around and know when mobs are approaching.  When I see one, I can zap it with lull.  It will walk right by your group and the mob you are killing without adding.  Useful, huh? 

Level 9:  Elemental Rhythms: Increases fire, cold and magic resistances.  Can be amplified with percussion instrument.  Very useful for fighting casters, especially at higher levels.  At level 53, with a walrus skin drum, this song adds almost 100 to all three resistances.  I laugh at wizards with this song up.  I was also able to resist Naggy’s area fire attack quite effectively.

Level 10: Anthem de Arms:  Group haste (makes folks swing faster).  See level 1 song, Chant of Battle.

Level 13: Similar to Elemental Rhythms, except for poison and disease casting necros.  I do no like necros.  I do not like them here, I do not like them there, I do not like them anywhere.  This song is useful against them, especially with percussion instrument. 

Level 15: Kelin’s Lucid Lullaby: This song, is the first in your mesmerize lineup.  This will be the turning point of your career.  It becomes more useful the higher the charisma you have.  Despite what some outdated song lists might say, this is NOT an area mesmerize.  This was changed to a single target lull like all your other mez songs, which is a benefit in my opinion.  This song works very simply.  Target the mob, and sing this song.  If it works, they will stop dead in their tracks, and not do anything for a while.  You can leave the song on, and it will continuously re-mez the mob for you. Damage breaks mez on mobs.  Be sure to be mezzing mobs that are undotted.  With good charisma, it is easy to keep 3 things mezzed at once.  Mez one, and then move on to the ones that are unmezzed.  Once all three are mezzed, go back to the first and repeat.  They should stay there happily and not move at all.  I can’t remember how long this lasts, although I believe I have been able to keep 4 mezzed at once.  Mez is the greatest equalizer in the game, on par with complete heal and slow.  Turn an impossible 5 mob fight into five 1 mob fights =D.  Anyone who has played Everquest will agree that fighting 2 mobs is significantly more difficult than fighting one.  Each additional mob you fight makes it exponentially more difficult to survive, since mobs are much stronger than players in general.  Mez changes that.  In addition, mez can also be a CHAIN STUN.  That’s right.  When fighting multiple mobs, I instruct my group to kill the non casters first.  When fighting casters, just keep on singing this song nonstop.  Start it up again as soon as it lands.  Every time it works, it will break a caster’s concentration 100% guaranteed.  Because things like complete heal and wizard nukes take much longer than 4 seconds to cast, my mez almost always works before they can get their spells off.  Consequently, bards can rape casters.  Casters cannot bash you out of singing.  They don’t do much damage so you can afford the aggro from chain mezzing.  Watch as casters stand there and try to get spells off while your group beats on them and takes almost no damage at all.  To top it all off, mez has much lower aggro compared to back in the day.  Tanks can easily taunt off you no matter how many times you’ve mezzed a mob.  Talk about a beautiful song. 

IMPORTANT NOTE:  For mezzing multiples, it is important to take one thing into account.  Let’s say you have 2 mobs in front of you.  You target one, and mez it.  To mez the second one, you must stop singing, TARGET THE SECOND MOB, THEN MEZ IT.  If you mez the first, then stop singing, and start singing mez then target the second mob, the first mob will be mezzed, not the second.  This also applies for charm songs.  This is very useful.  Let’s say my group is beating on a mob # 1, and I need to juggle songs, and keep mob # 2 mezzed.  I mez #2, turn around, and sing some songs, and bash on # 1 with my group.  I then stop singing, target # 2, and start singing mez.  While the song is loading up, I can target mob # 1 and start beating on it, and mob # 2 will be mezzed. 

Level 17: Guardian Rhythms: increases magic resist.  Enhanced with a percussion instrument.  Combined with Elemental Rhythms, your group will have huge magic resists.  You may now proceed to laugh at enchanters, which cast only magic based spells muwhahahahaha.

Level 18: Denon’s Disruptive Discord: See level 2 song Chords of Dissonance.

Level 19: Shauri’s Sonorous Clouding: Group invis, see invisible.  Useful for when you don’t want to be seen.  Undead see through this song.  It also has a tendency to drop when you don’t want it to, so be careful when using it.

Level 20: Cassindra’s Chant of Clarity: The first of your mana increasing line of songs.  Casters always salivate over clarity.  Juggle this with hymn of restoration during downtime if you are under level 32, and don’t have the second clarity song.

Level 22: Alenia’s Disenchanting Melody: This is group cancel magic.  I haven’t found a use for this song yet, but I’m sure there is one.

Level 23: Selo’s Consonant Chain: One of the best songs you will get for a long while.  This song snares the mob (slowing movement, preventing them from running), and decreases their attack speed (slow).  The usefulness should be obvious.  Use in conjunction with level 26 Angstlich’s Appalling Screech for fear/snare kiting.  I always keep this song up in case snare or root fails, and mobs run.  I can run after the mob and sing this to snare it, preventing it from getting its buddies to train my group.  This does not stack with shaman or enchanter slow.  If this song is on a mob, it also overrides ranger/druid snare, and shadowknight/necro engulfing darkness spells.  Make sure they get those spells to land before you start using this on the mobs.

Level 26: Angstlich’s Appalling Screech: This is an area fear song.  Using it will get you killed in a dungeon.  It can be useful in an emergency situation however.  Also makes getting out of Sebilis much easier.  Just fear the mobs and make a break for the zone.  IF you get jumped by golems, singing this will increase your chance of making it out.

Level 27: This song charms a mob, making it your pet for a short amount of time.  The aggro generated after landing this spell on a mob is ENORMOUS, so inform your tanks to taunt madly, and your healers to keep you alive if you use this in a group.  For a group, this song is useful for peeling mobs off casters or healers who have generated too much aggro.  Mez will not do the trick because if your healer is low on health, you mez it, and a group member hits it while trying to taunt off, the mob will immediately engage the caster again.  When this happens, just charm the mob you are fighting.  This will make them super pissed at you, saving the caster, in addition to giving your group valuable seconds to recover.  If you have good equipment and AC, you should be able to use this song fairly regularly to add to the DPS of your group.  I personally like to play it safe and keep mobs mezzed.  In solo situations, use this song to make mobs fight against each other.  In your 30’s, you should have no problem charming hill giants and making them beat the crap out of each other.  Since it takes no mana to sing, you can keep repeating. Once both mobs are close to death, stop charming and dot one to death, then the other.  It’s like taking candy from a baby.  

Level 28: Crisson’s Pixie Strike: This is your second mesmerize song.  It works the same as the first one, except it pushes the mob back in the direction you are facing.  Be careful using this song, as it will push mobs around.  Careful not to push them into pits, which causes trains.  When I was leveling in Sol B, I used this song to push kobolds to my friend Paulcleric, because he loves kobolds so much LOL!

Level 30: Amplification: This is another biggie song.  It amplifies the effectiveness of  your non-instrument based songs (songs that use the singing skill) All the Psalms songs should be used with amplification, which buff resists and add a damage shield.  Both the resists and the damage done by the damage shield will be increased.  In addition, This song lasts for something around 25 seconds once sung.  This means you can sing it once, and sing about 8 or 9 songs before having to sing it again.  It works great in combination with psalms songs, and Level 32 Cassindra’s Chorus of Clarity for extra mana regen.  Amplified clarity is comparable to KEI.  Yes, it’s that good.  Also useful against mobs with high magic resists.  For example, giants in Kael are resistant to snare.  Amplification will increase the likelihood of you landing snare on them.  There are probably many other uses for this song, but I don’t know them.  I’ve been too busy mezzing my way through my bard career to really find out how useful this is.  I’ve also read about how bards can contribute lots via amplified damage shields from psalms songs, but I can’t figure out how.  If damage shields are doing lots of damage, that means the mob you are fighting is unslowed, and hitting your tank a lot.  That is not good. 

Level 30: Fufil’s Curtailing Chant: This is the first in your line of single target DoT spells.  This is magic based, and does damage, in addition to debuffing the magic resistance of the mob.  Once this spell lands, it is much easier to get it to land (since resist is debuffed).  Should be used with a percussion instrument for max effectiveness.  Also useful in places with high magic resists (plane of innovation), to make slowing mobs easier for the slowers.

Level 31: Agilment’s Aria of Eagles: Group levitate.  Very useful for crossing zones with lots of hills, such as great divide.  You can actually fall to your doom there.  Make sure your group members stick close to you.  If they are too far away and this song breaks, a 2000 damage fall is not pleasant.

Level 32: Cassindra’s Chorus of Clarity: Group mana song.  Can be amplified.  Every mana using class loves this song.  Always sing this during downtime.  When I am grouped with an enchanter in the group taking care of the slowing and mezzing, I almost always sing this song amplified.  Combined with enchanter KEI, mana regens at a very fast rate.

Level 34: Lyssa’s Solidarity of Vision: Casting this on a mob enables you to see through its eyes.  Very useful in LDoN when you have mobs hiding behind doorways.  Target a mob that you can see, and use it to target the mobs you can’t see, and lull them before pulling.  This song can also be used to explore zones which you are new to.  There is an unlimited range to this song.  Target one mob then another, then another, and you can see the whole zone.  I can see most of Karnor’s castle using this method, with some patience.  I’ve also used it while on a Naggy raid led by myself to assist the pulling monk, and telling him when to pull and what the position of wandering mobs are. 

Level 36: Vilia’s Verses of Celerity: Group haste (20%), agility, and AC.  This song I used a lot until I got the level 42 McVaxius’ Berserker Cresendo.  When grouped with a shaman or enchanter who has a better haste, I go back to the level 1 Chant of Battle. 

Level 38: Tuyen’s Chant of Flame: This is the second in your line of single target DoTs.  With it, you should be able to kite much more effectively, especially with a percussion instrument.  Just fire it up while running away from your mob along with Fufil’s Curtailing Chant, and repeat until mobs is dead.

Level 39: Katta’s Song of Dancing:  Song which adds a lot of dexterity.  With a percussion instrument, you get even more dexterity.  At level 53 with walrus drum, I get something close to +100 dexterity for everyone in my group.  This means LOTS of proccing.  The increased damage from extra proccing can be quite insane.  With people dual wielding things like Sceptre of Destruction and Delightful Orb, etc…. mobs drop like flies if several people in the group have proccing weapons.

Level 39: Solon’s Bewitching Bravura: This is the second in your charm line of spells.  It takes 60 mana to cast, but lasts significantly longer.  I will discuss multiple charm kiting with this song in a later section.  Because it takes mana, you can run out rather quickly.  Bards do not get any benefit from their own mana songs or KEI, and have a meditate skill of 1.  It can take 2 hours to get back all your mana from empty.  Because of this, I have never bothered to use this song much, except on hill giants, and fire giants in Sol B.  I remember reading about getting flowing thought items to help with mana regen.  I do not know much about this area.

Level 40: Sionachie’s Dreams: The third in your line of mez spells.

Level 40: Syvelian’s Anti-Magic Aura: Single target dispel song.  I almost always use this song to pull mobs.  It dispels rune on casters, and with another 1 or 2 casts, it will dispel most damage shields on mobs.  It also has long range and generates little aggro, enable tanks to taunt off you easily.  Also useful when mobs cast effects on you, such as in Skyfire mountains.  Simply dispel yourself, and remove the effect (I think it’s heat blood). 

Level 41:  Psalm of Mystic Shielding: Increases magic resist, can be amplified.  I put this song up before I attempt to pull casters, especially clerics.  They tend to cast root, which is easily resisted with good magic resists.  Also clerics only cast magic based nukes, which you can laugh at with this song amplified.

Level 42: McVaxius’ Berserker Cresendo:  Adds haste, and strength.  Upgrade to Level 36 Vilia’s Verses of Celerity.

Level 42: Tuwen’s Chant of Disease:  Now you get your third single target DoT spell.  At this point, you can kite hill giants effectively, and can raise money that you need.  Selos plus your other 3 dots, and you’ll own giants.  Throw in a charm and mix things up, and you will drop giants like flies.  Nothing like EGW.  EXTREME GIANT WRESTLING (concept conceived by Elfmuncher and I.  Do not use without permission =P)

Level 46: Tuyen’s Chant of Frost: Fourth single target DoT song.  After you get this song, you can turn yourself into a damage machine with Thunderous Drum of Karana, or Battle Drummer’s Main Gauche (more will be discussed later).  Paired with a snare or root casting class in hill giants, you will absolutely destroy them.  I can rake in over 1K an hour with any hunting partner in Rathe Mountains, assuming there’s no competition. 

Level 47: Increase HP regen, absorb 10 damage of magic attack, and increase strength by 10.  I normally use this song during downtime with a stringed instrument for more HP regen, for a Hymn of Restoration, Niv’s, and Amplified Clarity chain.  This song is especially useful for the shaman class.  More to be discussed in the Shaman section.

Level 49: Selo’s Accelerating Chorus: This song is like the level 5 speed song, except it lasts for something like 2 minutes.  This song makes kiting much easier.  Sing this once every 2 minutes, and you are free to juggle 4 songs or 4 dots, without having to refresh the song.

Level 49: Shield of Songs: This song is a rune which prevents both magic and physical damage.  With a stringed instrument, this song reduces something around 50 damage when you first get it.  This is extremely useful.  When faced with enraging mobs, which can drop tanks really fast, chain singing rune has saved my group’s tank in numerous situations when I didn’t have a slower, and in the time it took the cleric to cast complete heal, the tank could die.  Also very useful for the Shaman class, and will be discussed more in the Shaman section.

Level 50: Versus of Victory: group 30% haste, strength, AC, and agility.  I almost always sing this song, with amplification of course.  Even with a haste casting class in the group, amplified strength which adds something like 70 strength to everyone in the group, and 30 extra AC is great. 

Level 51: Largo’s Absonant Binding: This is an upgrade to your level 23 Selo’s Chain song.  I always have it memmed, in case a mob runs.

Level 51: Selo’s Song of Travel: Group invisibility, levitate, see invisible, and super speed.  This works only in outdoor zones.  The effect will drop if you try to sing another song.  Very useful for getting out of places such as City of Mist in a hurry.

Level 52: Battlecry of the Vah Shir: Group 15% haste.  This song stacks with our other haste songs, and also stacks with enchanter/shaman haste spells.  Paired with an enchanter’s haste, the results become ridiculous.  The enchanter’s level 49 spell Swift Like Wind spell is a 60% haste.  Most fighters at this point have at least a 20% haste item.  Combined with this song, that is a 95% haste.  Impressive, huh?

Level 53: Song of Twilight: This is your fourth mesmerize song.  It is a bit tricky to use however.  It knocks the mob in the direction OPPOSITE the one you are facing.  For example, if you are facing the mob, mezzing it with this song will pull it TOWARDS you.  If your back is facing it, it will knock the mob AWAY from you.  Be careful not to knock mobs into holes and such.  This song is useful when grouped with rogues, and you have mobs glued to the walls.  Pull them toward you and away from the walls with this song.  Chain stunning becomes a bit more interesting, because you have to do some maneuvering around, and can’t simply push the mob toward the nearest wall.

Bard Roles

            The first and most important thing for a bard to be doing is crowd control.  This requires good charisma.  Crowd control makes your group take less damage, and significantly lessens the chance of any one member or the entire group dying.  This makes for much more efficient killing, and less downtime.  Because bards won’t outdamage tanks or casters, the next most important thing for them to be doing is slowing mobs using Selo’s Consonant Chain (Level 23), followed by hasting the group so that everyone else swings faster.  Last but not least is the role of a bard as a semi-decent damage dealer.  If there is a shaman in the group, that means everyone else is hasted and the mobs are slowed.  This means the bard should focus on crowd control, and doing damage.  If there is an enchanter in the group, this means mobs are slowed, and the group is hasted, and crowd control is covered.  When this happens, the bard should be buffing stats (amplified songs), and doing damage.  For crowd control purposes, I personally believe that a bard should take on the role of mezzer over the enchanter.  With a bard’s pulling ability, there should never be pulls bad enough to require an enchanter’s mass mesmerization spell.  A bard can easily easily mez one or two mobs.  This frees up the enchanter to NUKE.  In addition, bards do not die if they miss a mez.  In places like Dreadlands and Innovation, I have personally witnessed enchanters with a fresh rune up miss a mez, and die so fast I didn’t even see their HP bar drop, just the purple one after they die.  This is with me paying attention, watching to see if they needed backup.  Bards can survive significantly longer than chanters, so if there are 2 mobs, and one gets mezzed and the second starts bashing on the mezzer, the bard will stand a much better chance of surviving than the chanter.  I also find that bard mez has significantly less aggro than chanter mez.  Not having to memblur is very helpful. 

            To be good at crowd control, all you need is charisma, which means charisma equipment.  You don’t need any equipment to slow mobs and haste the group.  Doing damage can come in several forms, and will require equipment.  Now I will proceed to discuss the equipment necessary to fill the important roles of the bard.

Charisma

            As of level 53, my unbuffed charisma with +10 charisma food is 188.  I find that to be enough.  If I need more, I can switch in a Symphonic Saber and a Opalline Earring for +15 more charisma.  Lots of charisma items are available for cheap.  This is because lots of classes have absolutely 0 use for it.  Search on the bazaar for some.  Here is a small list of droppable items for you to look up, which I have used in my career: Cloak of Greater Pernicity, Sarnak Bracer of Victory, Crown of King Tranix, Bracelet of Beauty, Mask of Deception, Peerless Dragonskin Mask, Breastplate of Twilight, Sarnak Bracer of Honor, Architect’s Bejeweled Breastplate, Earring of the Architect, Opalline Earring, Sludged Girdle, Split Paw Tooth Necklace, Lodizal Shell Shield, Saprophyte Eye, Gantru Charm.  Weapons: Battle Drummer’s Main Gauche, Crystalline Short Sword, Di’zok Wristsnapper, Symphonic Saber, Breath of Harmony.  Also look up the food Lucky Cabbage and Lotus Pie. 

Stat Foods

            NEVER EVER EAT YOUR STAT FOOD AND DRINK!  I see people right clicking and eating their Misty Thicket Picnics all the time!  This makes me want to cry.  You can get the benefits of stat foods without EVER eating ANY of it.  Here is how.  As long as the items are in your first 2 slots of the inventory (top left and the spot below that), you will get the stat bonuses.  To prevent from eating them, simply right click and eat some other cheaper food/drink, like iron rations and milk!  Make sure you keep yourself full like this, and you will never have to eat your expensive stat foods and drinks. 

Equipment in General

            Do not go and make a bard and get all the charisma equipment you can.  You will suck that way.  You will wind up having horrible stats, no AC, no HP, and no resists at all.  For the average EQ player which cannot afford to spend lavishly on really expensive items like the Lodi Shell Shield, and uber instruments and such, a balancing act must be done to optimize the character.  First I would push charisma up high as possible, but not over 200, because that is overkill.  Dark blue mobs with normal resistances should give you no trouble with charisma approaching 200.  Mobs with high resistances like in Innovation will resist your mezzes a lot even if you have a 300 charisma.  Also, you do not need a 200 charisma at level 20, that is also overkill.  Just try to gradually push your charisma up.  In addition to Charisma, there are several other stats which I feel should be focused on: AC (not getting hit, taking less when getting hit, surviving longer in case you do get aggro, etc), Strength (increases ATK so you hit the mobs, can carry more stuff), HP (the more the merrier, I am a big fan of not crumpling like a paper bag caster), Magic resist (resisting charm, mez, root, snare, fear, malaise, all cleric nukes, and all chanter nukes is sweet), Dexterity (for proccing), Fire and Cold resist (you do not want to die getting hit by a sneak attack ice comet and taking 1000 damage).  Here’s the list: AC, Strength, HP, magic resist, dexterity, fire and cold resist.  For myself, I concentrate on survival.  If I do no damage, and do not proc at all, it’s okay because I have my group doing damage.  I am no use if I am dead.  I personally rank the stats in the following order of importance: AC, HP, magic resist, strength, fire and cold resist, dexterity.  You will decide what’s important for yourself.  It is easy to go to level 50 without facing many casters at all.  I chose to level in places such as Sol A, Mistmoore, Sol B, Lower Guk, and Sebilis, which all have lots of casters, which is why I think resists are very important.
