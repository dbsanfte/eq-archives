Searching For Sullon Zek
 17:14 EST - jcsmith

Absor tackled another list of PVP related questions at the Developer's Corner last night. The most popular of the lot would have to be...

Ok, we're not going announce the exact time and date for the arrival of Sullon Zek.

The reasons are pretty simple, really. We are expecting a huge influx of people on this server. We'd like it to stay up for a while. Hopefully not announcing that it's up will help to stagger the volume of players logging in at any given time.

Interupting Spells
 14:11 EST - jcsmith

Absor cleared this question up on the Developer's Corner today...

Q: Under 'Magic changes in Test server' I read once you duck your spell gets interrupted immediately. Is this true? Can this be true? please say yes!!!! many of my friends have died after I accidentally clicked on the complete heal spell... Say this is true and will go live please!!!

A: This is true on Test at the moment. Whether or not is will remain that way until we patch next is uncertain. Right now, the plan is to leave it this way, but that plan could change so don't get too excited.

The main reason for this change is to fix an 'exploit'. We are fully aware of the beneficial side of this change, and as of our last meeting we are inclined to keep that beneficial side as it is.

What Makes an Online Game Succesful?
 12:45 EST - jcsmith

I came across this Gamespot article while carousing through Allakhazam's this morning. It takes a look at "What Makes an Online Game Succesful." Here's a text bite...

I think most online games now in development will fail because their ideas aren't interesting enough on their own. A lot of these games are being designed to improve upon the ideas of highly successful online games like EverQuest--but it'll take a lot more than some simple refinement to pull EverQuest players away from their favorite game. Some online games currently in production wisely recognize that most people don't have huge amounts of spare time to devote to gaming every day. Still, these and all the rest must also be fully functional as advertised once they're released. That may be easier said than done as far as the developers are concerned, but it's the least that anyone should expect. Later this month, another major online game--Funcom's massively multiplayer RPG, Anarchy Online--will be released. I sincerely hope the launch of this promising game will go more smoothly than that of World War II Online.

Online Gaming, Second Fiddle?
 13:40 EST - jcsmith

While loitering at Massive Multiplayer.org I came across this GameSlice article. It takes a look at online gaming from a view that we don't see often here and as a result I figured I'd link to it. Here's what to expect...

Of course I know online gaming is 24/7, but my gaming comes in spurts – a half-hour before I get the kids up for school, an hour after I put them to bed. It takes 15-20 minutes to hook up to The Zone, enter a room, and pick up a game of Close Combat V --let alone play the dang thing. Conversely, I can resume my Close Combat saved campaign in a couple of minutes and play till the instant before reality calls me back to parenthood.

Monday, June 18, 2001

Zone Reference Guides
 19:30 EST - jcsmith

I wanted to take the time to announce a useful new feature at EQ'Lizer. Underneath the Spoiler Lists menu you will notice a new option entitled Zone Reference. Our zone reference guides are designed for players to easily access all of our information on any given zone.

Things you will find in each zone reference guide includes what NPC's spawn there, what items are dropped and by whom, what quests are related to the zone, and links to our zone guides and maps. Not only are the new reference guides more convenient than using the databases, but they are also much faster. This first update includes the first five zones(alphabetically) and I will be going through one by one and adding the guides for each zone in the game.

Unfortunately the zone guides will not display optimally in resolutions of 800x600 or less. You can still view all of the information but ot may require some scrolling. I apologize for this but there was nothing I could do to prevent it.

New Zone References: Ak'Anon, Befallen, Beholder's Maze, Black Burrow, Burning Woods.
