Shadowknight Class Guide: Levels 25-45

Level by level tactics

Level 25

Level 25-35 are a transitional period in your characters life. During these levels you will find yourself defeating the boss mobs of the mid level dungeons. You will also begin to scratch the surface of the pre-expansion "high" level dungeons such as the Crypt of Dalnir, Kaesora, Kedge Keep, Lower Guk, and Solusek B and the Temple of Droga. You will spend most of your time at Solusek A and Castle Mistmoore during these levels. 

You should also attempt to complete your Temple of Solusek Ro Quests during this level range. Below is a list of useful items and quests for a level 25-35 shadowknight.

Level 30

Riposte

Riposte is the last of yoru defensive melee skills. Much like parry and dodge riposte gives you an extra roll to avoid an attack. Unlike the previous incarnations however riposte, when succesful, also gives you an extra attack. 

Example: Your opponent swings at you and you riposte. You will now get an extra swing at your opponent. This does not count against your normal swing time or ability to double attack.

Spells

Endure Disease

Casting this spell on another player or pet will add 20 points to their disease resistance. 

Feign Death

Feign death is perhaps the best spell that you will ever receive. This spell allows you to play dead much like a monk. If succesful monsters will stop attacking you. If you are the only person who they have aggroed(attacked) then they will eventually return to their spawn locations or patrol paths.

There are a couple of things that need to be noted when using this spell. 

First off you need to always take off auto attack before casting this spell. If you auto attack is on this spell will fail every time.  

Secondly is that this spell is not always succesful. If the spell is unsuccesful you will receive the message "Soandso has fallen to the ground." If you see this message you will need to get up and run or try it again. Because of the lengthy recast delay one failure could mean death to you.

Last but not least spellcasters are your worst enemy when using this spell. Let's say for example that you have succesfully feigned. The mobs that were meleeing you have stopped meleeing and are standing in front of you. If a caster had begun casting an offensive spell on you before you feigned and does not finish casting the spell until after you have feigned then you are in trouble. As soon as their spell hits you while feigned other mobs will re-aggro. 

So when using feign death while being attacked by spellcasters you have to be very careful. Try to cast it right after their spell hits you. Usually they will not recast another spell for a few seconds and you should have time to feign before they do so. When fighting multiple spellcasters however this can be very tricky.

There are two primary usese for this spell: Escape and pulling. Escape is self explanatory. You get in trouble and you don't want to die. So you feign death. You lay down and wait to be rescued. Or you get up and run for it when the monsters have gotten far enough away from you. 

Pulling is a bit more complex. Much as a monk can use feign death to pull single monsters where a normal character would pull several so can an SK. It's a bit more difficult for us because we fail more often and because the spell takes time to cast where a monks is instant. They can also mend wounds to escape trouble where we can not. However if a shadowknight is careful and is willing to bite the bullet and die himself rather than training his party then they can be just as effective as a monk at feign death pulling. The real key is not caring about death. If you choose to feign death pull you will die. You may die quite often. In doing so however you will save party members lives.

Now that is out of the way let's cover how to FD(Feign Death) pull. You should use a fetching spell to hit a group of monsters. Some prefer to use a darkness spell because it will have one mob lagging behind the others. Others prefer to use siphon strength because of it's low mana useage. Conserving mana is essential when pulling if you want to keep pulling nonstop. As a result I primarily use siphon strength when I am the puller. There are certain times when darkness is useful however.

After pulling the mobs you should run to a spot between where the mobs were originally and where your party is located. Try to make this a safe spot where you are not likely to run in to a patroller. Also make sure that you are far enough away from your group so that they will not be aggroed by the mobs. Last make sure that you have sufficient distance from where you pulled the mobs so that they do not re-aggro once you stand up. Next turn and face the mobs so you can see them coming and feign death. If there is a caster in the lot you may have to stand there and take a few hits until they cast a spell at you and then feign death.

Hopefully your feign was succesful. If it was not you need to stand up and try it again. If it is not succesful and you are going to die then try to find a safe place where it will be easy for your party to pull your corpse back to them and ressurect you. Do not train your party with these mobs. If your party could take this many at once you wouldn't need to FD pull them in the first place. Bite the bullet and take one for the team. 

If your feign was succesful you should just lay there and wait. Do not allow your party to cast any spells on you at this time. Doing so will likely make the mobs rush after whoever cast the spell and will defeat the purpose of the FD pull. Just sit and wait for the mobs to slowly trickle back to their spawn point. They will usually do so one by one. Sometimes however they will all go at once and you will have to get up and refeign and wait all over again.

What you want to do is wait until only one or two(if your party can handle two) are still standing over your corpse. If these mobs are not patrollers you need to wait for them to go back to their original spawn point where they will then reset. Then you can stand up and drag the remaining one or two mobs to your party to slay them.

Sometimes you will become bugged. As soon as you stand up every time(even if the mobs have reset) they will begin running directly toward you. If this happens you will need to /q and wait for five minutes. After five minutes you can relog back in and this should be fixed.

FD pulling takes a bit of practice. Those who are skilled FD pullers however are invaluable to a group. It elminates the need for an enchanter or bard for crowd control(although they are still useful just in case) and decreases the risk for all other party members.

Gather Shadows

Gather shadows is a self only invisibility spell. It does not stack with invisibilty versus undead(it is impossible in Everquest for any player to be both invisible to undead and living via spell). This spell works both indoors and outdoors and you will find it very useful for the rest of your Everquest lifetime. 

It should be noted that undead beings, beings over level 50 and certain other beings can all see through invisibility.

Heat Blood

Heat blood is the first in a line of the heat blood->boil blood line of dots. These spells do decent damage over time. They do more damage per tick than the darkness line of spells and are heat based rather than magic based. This makes them less resistable by many mobs but very hard to cast on mobs with high fire resistance.

Heat blood does 17 points of damage per tick for 10 ticks(one minute). The importance of this spell is that it stacks with both the darkness line of spells and with disease cloud(if you are still using that). This means that you can now stack engulfing darkness and heat blood on a mob to do a total of 280 points of damage over time. This is useful if you plan on kiting any mobs or for extra damage in melee combat.

Life Draw

Life draw is an upgrade to the lifetap line of spells. It lifetaps for 45 points of damage. This is the first lifetap spell which is semi useful for healing yourself during combat.  It is still very mana inefficient but in an emergency you will be happy that you have it.

If you need to heal yourself in an emergency I recommend that you step back out of combat for a second. Hopefully the mob will begin(if it is not already) attacking another player rather than interupting you. Then lifetap away until you are oom or until you are ready to re-engage.

Restless Bones

Restless bones is your level 30 pet spell. It summons a level 16 necromancer pet. Same rules apply to this pet as with the previous.

Wave of Enfeeblement

This spell is an area effect weaken spell. It will drain 15 points of strength to every enemy within the area of effect. It is one of those spells you will likely never use except to show off a new toy.

Hide

Hide is an underused but useful skill. It allows you to hide in one place(you can not move). If it is successful then you will be invisible to both living and undead beings. This makes it a great skill to use if you need to go AFK(away from keyboard) for a few minutes or if you need to go invisible in a hurry and do not have the spell memorized.

There is an easy way to check to see if hide is successful providing you are within sight of another NPC/Monster who is not indifferent to you. Right click(or use /con) the mob and see how they react to you. If they are not indifferent then hide has failed. If they are indifferent(and are not normally so) then it is succesful.

Level 39

Spells

Animate Dead

This is an ugrade to your line of pet spells. It summons a level 20 necromancer pet. Same rule applies as with all other previous pets.

Cancel Magic

This spell will cancel a magical effect(a random one) from a party member or an NPC. Depending on the level of the caster of the original spell and the level of the person casting the spell it may take several times for this to be successful. 

You can atttempt to cancel a specific effect from yourself by clicking on that spells icon on your screen and then casting this spell. 

This spell can be used to cancel dot's cast upon you by your enemies. It can also be used to attempt to remove damage shields or other beneficial spells from your enemies.

Expulse Undead

This is the second in your line of anti undead spells. It does up to 94 points of direct damage against undead beings. 

Similar to the original(ward undead) spell the damage output is somewhat outdated by the time you receive the spell. It is still useful in the same scenarios as the original however. It is also good to keep up your evocation skill for later levels when these spells will actually become useful.

Heart Flutter

Heart flutter is a new line of DOT spells. You will receive an upgrade to this spell at level 60(asystole). This line of spells not only does damage over time but also acts as a strength and armor class debuff. This makes it a very useful spell. It is also stackable with both the darkness and the heat blood line of spells.

This spell decreases(debuffs) your opponents strength by 20 points and their armor class by approximately 15 points. It also does 12 points of damage per tick over 12 ticks(1 minute and 12 seconds). Thus by stacking this spell with engulfing darkness and heat blood you will now be able to do 424 points of damage over time in just over a minute as well as debuffing your opponent. 

Resist Cold

This is an upgrade to the endure cold line of spells. They do not stack with one another. It adds 40 points to your targets cold resistance. This spell will prove to be very useful in raids versus Lady Vox. Your buff will be the same as the priests types which will allow them to save time and mana while you buff cold resistance. 

Shadow Vortex

Shadow vortex is an armor class siphon. It siphons 12 points of armor class from your opponent and gives it to you. This spell is extremely useful and is likely to hold a spell slot for the rest of your career. 

Not only is it useful because of the dual buff and debuff but it also is an excellent taunt. If you need to get your opponent off an ally but don't want to use harm touch a shadow vortex followed by a taunt is a good bet to do the trick.

Shieldskin

Shieldskin is a self only shielding. This spell requires cat's eye agate as a reagent. It will absorb approximately 50 points of melee damage that would otherwise occur on you. After absorbing that damage one time the spell will no longer be in affect. This means that it does not actually buff your hit points as a cleric/druid/shaman spell does. It is a one time protection that only works against melee damage(does not work against spells). It also does not provide protection against being stunned. 

I personally never found much use for this spell. I would use it occasionally before a big battle because every small bit of hit points can help. The fact that it uses a reagent and that it is a one time useage prevented it from holding a spell slot most of the time.

Some shadowknights however truly enjoy this spell. A tactic which they often employ is to wait until they get a succesfull bash/slam to stun their opponent and then immediately cast the spell during combat which gives them a shield. This can eat up reagents rather quickly but thankfully cat's eye agate is relatively cheap. 

Level 40

Disarm

Disarm is the final of your melee skills and unfortunately it is all but useless. Do not get me wrong. This skill can be of some use(especially in PVP) to a warrior, rogue or monk. A shadowknights disarm caps so early however that I have never once had a succesful disarm with it. Not even while in the arena dueling with level 1's could I ever succesfully disarm. 

The purpose of this skill is to disarm your opponents weapon. The flaw of it is that with the delay changes this only really became useful for dual wielders. The problem is that even if you disarm their weapon they still dual wield. All in all I think this skill is of VERY limited use for any class and for a shadowknight it is of no use whatsoever.
