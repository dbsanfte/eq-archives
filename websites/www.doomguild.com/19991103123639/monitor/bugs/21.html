3 . "Logically speaking, we should get less XP for undead"
 Posted by Malbusia on Aug-12-99 at 01:09 PM (EST)
Since we are the ones creating all these undead all over the lands (in theory, anyway) we should be interested in bolstering them and making them more plentiful. In that light, increased XP for killing undead is not a good idea...

There are still remnants of this philosophy around -- the Gnome necromancer guild faction hit of decaying newbie skeletons in Steamfont, for example. A cleric might very well get an XP bonus (logically, but I have no idea if they actually do) for killing undead; we should take a hit of the same magnitude. (And magicians should take a hit for killing elementals in the same way.)

I wouldn't hold out hope that there is any XP difference at all, though. That requires extra database work, and it's brutally obvious that the database design was done long before the full needs of the game were thought out.

It's amazing how different the game is when you don't mess up your factions needlessly. The second time I played a necromancer (both Gnomes) I only killed skeletons, pumas, and minotaurs in my level 7-12 range. In other words, only things that attacked me. Plague rats, ebon drakes, harpies, and clockworks all left me alone, and I, they. In-character, I had done nothing to them, and they nothing to me. (I like to play my character very intellectually stuck up, sort of like a female Dr. Doom, using this or that thing to my own ends...) Actually, that they had done "nothing to me" was not a moral justification of reciprocity in this sense. Rather, it was a precursor for their remaining alive. A necessary condition, but not a sufficient one always, since I did...liberate a few from this thing called life, purely out of curiosity.
