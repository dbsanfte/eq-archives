ADDITIONAL GUILD COMMANDS

 SUBMISSION FROM:Anduril Valar
EMAIL:elves@eqhq.com
WEB:http://elven.eqhq.com

Okay, now you have heard them all! This isn't about classes, this isn't about DOTing and Kiting, this is about a VERY USEFUL tool for the roleplayers who form together as a guild. Actually, even the non-roleplaying guilds could use this to =)

Anyways, this is my petition:

1. Make a command named: /guildwhoall This command will list ALL members of the guild, whether they are ingame or not. Now, I would like it to also list their race, class, and level of training even if they have /anon on but I could live with just knowing who all is in my guild.

2. Make a command named: /guildwho This command will list all members of the guild that are currently online. As with /guildwhoall, I would like it to also list their race, class, and level of training even if they have /anon on but I could live with just knowing who in my guild is online right now (especially if you have a guild with more than 20 members: Friend List). A few people have suggested using the command (that is already implemented) /who all "guildname" (i.e.: /who all "Elven Guard") but this rarely works. I have numerous people from my guild on my /friend list and I see them when I do /who friends but when I try the /who all "Elven Guard" maybe one or two show up! When you do a /who "insertguildnamehere" all, only members that are NOT anonymous and do NOT have /roleplay on are listed. Anyone with either of these two switches on will not be listed with the /who command. So the command is pretty much useless, since almost everyone has either anonymous or roleplay turned on.

3. Make [Rank] actually useful! I think just using [rank] to make someone an officer is absurd. In fact, [o] should be altogether another thing. In [Rank] you should be able to supply your own rank (and only officers or the GM could do this). For example, I run the guild Elven Guard. I have members ranked from Watch all the way up to General. Wouldn't it be nice if you go to the person's info box if it said [Sergeant] or things of that sort instead of just officer?

Last night, a few good comments and misconceptions about the Guild Petition were brought up that I would like to clear up. One comment made was:

"I would dread having every sword and spell slinger knowing EXACTLY what level I was at all times, imagine the abuse that would arise from the PK guilds!"

Now, after discussing this on the board, I cleared up that the /guildwhoall and /guildwho functions are NOT /who functions, they are /guild functions. With that said, only members IN that particular guild could use them, just like /guildsay. Example: Anduril is in the Elven Guard. When he does /guildsay, only those members in the Elven Guard can hear him talk. When he does /guildwhoall ONLY the information from those in the Elven Guard are posted (not say Black Tear who the Elven Guard is at war with). When he does /guildwho it only shows those members IN the Elven Guard that are presently online. So, your information is safe from those outside the guild and if you can't trust your guild brothers and sisters, well, perhaps you shouldn't be in that guild.

Concerning Guild Ranks, the following comment was made: "Also, assigning rank titles is nice - but could be exploitable. Example Homes D'Dog Norrath [Gangsters](rank)[Supreme Ghetto Pimpdaddy]"

Now, this is true but should work the same way as player names. If you are wandering the Faydark and come across a person named Iscrewdurmama then you notify a GM and they will take action on it. Now, if you happen to look at someone's rank and see it is I would suggest you do the same thing. Furthermore, this would be easier for GMs to handle because if one member in a guild has a rank like that, I would bet all my platinum on it that many more members would have said type or ranks and a GM could take the appropriate action needed. But, unless I am mistaken, only guildmembers can see your rank. If you don't like your guild's rank structure, you can always join another one.

Well, that is my petition. And I'm not really sure if it even counts as a petition. But I think doing these few and simple things would make guilds run MUCH smoother.
