CHAPTER 5 - INSEPARABLE

Classes finally started. Nic, Luísa and Ana Paula could meet each other every night at school, besides the time during the day they spent together. Nic and Luísa helped their mother with her work, but they still could find free time to spend with hanging out and talking.

With the beginning of the school year, Valéria started showing interest and sympathy for them. In the first days, Ana Paula tried to approach the girl, who seemed very friendly that day she went to school to enroll. As the two of them were studying in the same classroom, it was easier for them to become friends, and soon Valéria was friends with Nic and Luísa too.

"My parents are getting divorced", she said, while they were waiting for the next class to start, some days later. "Things between them got worse after we moved here."

"It must be terrible for you", Luísa said. "Can't you go back there and things can get good again?"

"No. Well, we can, but my dad doesn't want to go back, and neither do I. I’ve liked this city since the very first time I've been here. And I believe that a little thing would be enough for them to fight, and this moving was this little thing. They would get divorced anyway, sooner or later."

"I can't even think of my parents fighting, getting a divorce..."

"My parents actually don't fight... not like this. They don't yell at each other or anything. It's a silence war, if you get what I mean. I think that's why I’m not afraid or worried about it. I don't think they'll stop talking to each other at all."

"Man... your calmness scares me!"

"Anyone would be calm if they saw that the bad atmosphere in their own home is about to end... you know? Even if it happens this way. I like both my parents a lot, and I don't mind seeing them apart if that's what's needed to improve our relationship."

"And what's going to happen when it's all done? I mean, when one of them is about to leave home..."

"My mom's going back to Porto Alegre. My brother and I will stay here with my dad."

No one said anything. Valéria didn't care to talk about this subject, but the others were too embarrassed to ask her about it.

* * *

And although Valéria didn't live near the other guys and couldn't see them everyday, she became a frequent person among them. Besides regular school, she studied English in the same languages school as Luísa and Ana Paula, not in the same class, but at the same time.

* * *

"What about your brother, Valéria? I remember you once said he was starting some college course..."

"Administration. It's near here."

"And doesn't he hang out with you?"

"Well... he always had his own friends, but he's not going out a lot for the time being. Yes, we do hang out together sometimes. We became really close after this whole problem between my parents started."

"You have to introduce him to us. We've known you for over a month, and never saw your brother!", Ana Paula laughed. "If he's afraid he'll become a babysitter, tell him Nic is almost the same age as him."

"I guess you're going to like him. I do. And you'll meet them as soon as possible!"

* * *

And so it was done. The next Saturday, a sunny day, Nic, Luísa and Ana Paula went to Solar FastFood, a restaurant near their house. They waited for Valéria and her brother there, and it took them a half hour to arrive. They sat at the same table, in a corner, three in one side, two in the other.

After being properly introduced, they ordered some food, and ate while talking.

"It's a nice city. We used to come here a lot when my grandfather was still alive, Valéria and I, to spend our vacation here, and stuff like that. I like coming here to live definitely, but I miss my old life."

"Don't worry, Guilherme", Luísa smiled. "You'll enjoy your new life here. I think we'll run into each other a lot from now on."

"That would be good!", he laughed. "People at college are not that friendly, everyone either works all day, or has their own date to go out with... But it's been cool, so far."

"I have some friends in São Lucas that play basketball together, they practice on the weekends", Nic said. "They're nice guys, and sometimes I play with them. I can take you there with me, if you want. Do you play basketball?"

"Rarely... I don't have many opportunities to play it. I like basketball, I actually practiced it for some weeks once, but decided to quit. The guys were not nice. It's been, like, two years maybe?"

"So exercise your bones before going there", Valéria said. "Nic's friends play seriously!"

They talked for the rest of the day, and went to Nic's house when it was already dark. And during the following days, they were together as often as they could. In fact, they became inseparable.
