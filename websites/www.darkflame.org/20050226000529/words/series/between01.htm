CHAPTER 1 - VACATION, AT LAST

“Hi mom! I have just arrived here! It’s a wonderful place, with lots of people...”

“Paula, come on! Look at the size of the line!”

“Take it easy, Luísa! I'm almost done... Mom, I have to go, there's someone else who wants to use the phone. Kisses. Okay, Luísa, you can make your call now... ”

It was paradise. I had just arrived in a campground to spend one month of summer vacation, from the end of November until nearly Christmas, and everything was just amazing. I had never been in a campground before, particularly without my parents. I went with Nic, my boyfriend; Luísa, his sister; and my neighbors-- Guilherme, her boyfriend; and Valéria, Guilherme's sister. It’s amazing that my parents let me travel with only my friends and, especially, with my boyfriend... There were dozens of people there, whom I had never seen before, but that wouldn't be a problem. The chalets weren't big, and I would share mine with Luísa and Valéria and two other girls, Adriana and Alice. Nic and Guilherme would share another chalet with two other guys, João and Cláudio, at the male area.

While putting our things in order in the chalet, we started talking to Adriana and Alice:

“So where are you from?”, said Alice, the most cheerful one.

“Sorocaba”, Luísa answered, picking up the mood.

“We've been there once, in a gym competition. ”

“Are you gymnasts? ”

“Yes. I mean, I still am...”, said Adriana, joining the conversation.

“I had a problem with my ankle and had to quit the gym... ”, Alice explained. “It was a shame, I loved it more than anything. But I play volleyball now from time to time! ”

“Gymnast... aren't you a little "old" for that? ”

“Yes, but I'm still in shape. I can still pull it off! ”

“And where are you two from? ”

“Rio de Janeiro. ”

“You came here from Rio to spend your vacation in the middle of nowhere?” , Valéria laughed. “If I lived anywhere on the coast, I would never leave the beach! ”

“Oh, you get tired of something if it's too much... Don’t even mention the beach to me!”, said Adriana, on her way to the bathroom to put on her bikini.

“The guys in my brother's chalet... do you know them?”, Valéria asked.

“Yes, they're from Rio too. Cláudio is Adriana's neighbor, and João is his friend. I don't know how we ended up together, though.”

“Did you like them, Valéria?”, Luísa instigated, while arranging some clothes in the suitcase. “You can't live alone forever, you know!”

“I loved them both, Luísa, you can't imagine how much!”, Valéria answered sarcastically, since she didn't like the insinuation. Luísa laughed.

* * *

The pool was immense. With two platforms in the middle, which water fell from, and a waterfall in a corner that leaned on a wall of stones. On the other side, two water slides. Four changing rooms, a snack bar near the pool and a restaurant across it, along with the mini dancing club. It didn't look like a campground, actually... But that was ok. The entire place was really huge, with lakes, woodlands full of trails, and some of those gorgeous places that no one knows exists.

Well, the guys were already in the pool waiting for us. We introduced each other, and conversation began cheerfully, showing that the vacation would probably be really enjoyable. Nic challenged Guilherme, Cláudio, and João to a swimming race. Trying to show off more than the other one, they jumped in the pool. It's incredible how guys like to compete for everything, to show off for no apparent reason! Well, Nic wouldn't challenge anyone if he weren't sure he'd win. And that's just what happened: Nic, winner and very proud of himself, sat beside me and a couple minutes later led me... to the slides.

Nic knew that I was very afraid of those high, fast and – from the look of them - dangerous things, and I think that was just why he wanted me to go. When we were far enough from the others, I told him there was no way I would go.

“Nic, you know I don't want to go...”

“Why, Paulinha? Look at all those people who are going, it’s really fun...”

“But I don't want to go, I don't like it!”

Nic is not the kind of person who, in this case, would call me a wimp and grab my arm and lead me to where he wanted. But he was stubborn, and wouldn't give up:

“Paulinha, it's just a slide... And do you remember all those things we've been through six months ago? You risked yourself to save Luísa from those kidnappers and you did it just fine. Why would you be afraid of a slide now?”

I just couldn't find an answer to a plea like that. Six months ago, Luísa had been kidnapped because of a mistake, after having her mother killed also by mistake, and we saved her by ourselves. And I did the worst part, the most difficult one, and everything went just fine. But I envied Luísa-- she was right there, happy, telling everyone about that episode like it was something normal, or at least a bit exciting. I was afraid of a simple slide and always felt ridiculous because of it. Nic tried everything he could think of to turn me into a more "courageous" girl. At his side, I thought, I could at least look courageous...

The line was enormous, and it took forever for us to climb up all the stairs. Nic had the brilliant idea of taking me to the higher one. I did not want to go, but he made me go. The wait was killing me, because I had to do my best not to look down. I had never been afraid of heights, but knowing that I would throw myself down there on a fast, curvy slide was terrible. Well, some decades later, we reached the platform. There, while waiting for the others to go down, I accidentally looked to the sides and saw the pool, the little pool, down there. I was shaking. Nic noticed it, and said "calm down..." holding me by my waist. Before I knew it, I was already sliding. Nic was coming right after me and I had this bad feeling that he was faster than me and would run me over.

It was actually really fast coming down, and when I opened my eyes, I was already in the pool. I almost hit a little girl. Nic was laughing, happy, and suddenly hugged me and called me to swim with him. At that point I was already laughing too. It was impossible to get nervous around Nic. After a couple minutes, we went back with the others. Luísa asked me if the slide was fun, if it was worth the time spent in the line. I said it was, and noticed that Nic was holding a smile. If it were anybody else, he would be making jokes, and the funny thing is that no one ever felt offended by his jokes. But maybe he knew I'd get upset and remained quiet.

Luísa grabbed Guilherme's hand, and they ran to the slides. Luísa grabbed Guilherme, not the other way around... I wish I was just like her!

Cláudio had just arrived with some juices, and we stayed in the pool drinking and talking until Luísa and Guilherme showed up again, it looked like they took longer to slide. Since they started dating, after we rescued Luísa, the two of them were inseparable. They adored each other, maybe more than Nic and me. People even asked me sometimes if they ever got tired of each other's faces, even for a couple minutes...
