CHAPTER 2 - NIC

A couple days later, everything seemed to be back to normal. No one said anything about the school issue. Luísa, on the other hand, spent her days helping her mother, who made and sold breakfast baskets by ordering, and in her free time – which was often – she was always with Ana Paula.

* * *

"Hello? Paula, this is Luísa here!"

"Luísa, where are you? I’ve been looking for you since I woke up!"

"I'm here, in São Paulo. I haven't been able to call you before. We left in a hurry, I just managed to call you now!"

"What are you doing there?"

"I'm at the airport!", talking in a public phone, Luísa was yelling, so her friend could hear her voice among all the noise.

"What are you doing at the airport? Christ, tell me!"

"Nic, Paula! Nic arrives today! Man, you forgot? I told you so many times!"

"Nic... It's true! He'll be home today, that's so good! And how is he, speaking good English*, Luísa? Have you talked to him?"

* (This story is originally written in Portuguese. The characters live in Brazil and speak Portuguese as their first language)

"I talked to him by phone a few days ago. He says he's good at English, but didn't speak a single word of it to me."

"And you're coming home right after he arrives, or no?"

"I think we are. Paula, I've got to go, my dad is calling me!"

"Okay, I'm waiting for you, bye!"

Ana Paula hung up the phone with a smile on her face.

"Who were you talking to, so happy?"

"It was Luísa, Mom. She's at the airport, waiting for Nicolas."

"So he's arriving today? Luísa must be really happy, it’s been five months since she last saw her brother."

"Happy? Much more than that! You know how she loves her brother... Besides, he promised to bring her some stuff..."

"And what did he do with his studying, so he could be away all this time?"

"This guy is crazy. When he was informed that he had won this English course in some contest, he talked to his teachers and started studying like crazy so he wouldn't fail the year. Extra classes and things like that. He finished his sophomore year months before the others, and went to England. He said he needed to learn English so badly. It's exaggeration, we all know that. All he wanted was to have the chance to go on a trip."

"And did he learn English after all?"

"He says so. He's so smart, he would be able to learn anything."

"I bet he is."

"He is. I saw his school exercise books, his tests from eight grade in São Lucas, the ones which I had totally failed... I envy him..."

"Why did you want to see his tests? To know exactly what you would have to study?"

"No, I saw them one day when I went to Luísa's and he was explaining some Math things to me... back in the first semester, when he was still here."

"So you have a certain free teacher?"

"No, that was just a favor from him to me."

"With a brother like that, tell me, how did Luísa fail the school year?"

"They are different, Mom. And I don't think Luísa was interested in asking him for help."

"Okay, so, let's stop talking, your father is coming home for lunch and I haven’t made lunch yet. By the way, Carol called and told me to tell you to go pick up your book at her house."

"Thanks, Mom. I'll be right back."

* * *

"Luísa, come here! You never stop talking on that phone!"

"I was talking to Paula, Dad. What's the matter?"

"Your brother's plane has just arrived."

"It did? Are you sure?"

"Yes. Look at the number of the flight."

"And how long it will take him to get off of the plane?"

"Not much time, I guess. I'm hungry, I'm going to the snack bar with your mom. Are you coming with us, or do you want us to get something for you?"

"I'm staying here. I don't want anything."

Luísa's parents went away, she was looking everywhere, she didn't know what gate her brother would come from. Suddenly, she heard a loud voice behind her:

"Luísa!"

She turned back and saw her brother, with luggage all around him. She ran forward to him, and they gave each other a big hug.

"I missed you so much, Nic!"

"I missed you too, Lu! How are you doing?"

"I'm fine, Nic! Man, you look different, your hair!", she messed up his hair, which fell, glossy, just above his eyes.

"Dad and Mom, where are they?"

"In the snack bar. Let's go."

Nic walked silently behind them, and scared them. They hugged each other, and Nic complained that he was hungry.

"I ate something at the airport, before we took off. The plane's food was terrible", at the table, he was talking about his trip, while eating a sandwich. "Actually, the trip back was totally awful. Bad seats, heat... But I'm here!"

He grabbed a french fry, and continued talking.

"Look, I want to go home and ask you something, Mom... Make some food for me! Make me rice, beans, that good food of yours! I missed it so much out there!"

They laughed.

"Don't worry, son", Joana said. "But now, tell us how it was out there, how was the family who hosted you, how was the school you attended, you can talk for however long you want because we don't have a phone bill to pay now! Did you enjoy the English course?"

"Of course", Nic said, drinking some soda. "It was worth it."

Augusto had got up to go take the snacks. Joana utilized that time to tell Nic:

"You see, son, things are so different... You were studying English abroad, and your sister just failed the school year here."

He was surprised.

"What? Luzinha, did you fail?"

"I did, Nic... And don't call me Luzinha, okay?"

"Of course... but what are you going to do now?"

"Dad's taking me to another school. Against my wishes, of course", she made a face.

"Why?"

"Because São Lucas is so expensive, and I'm not worth the sacrifice."

"Hey, stay calm... I have an idea, Luísa. Why don't you go take the night classes? At São Lucas, just like I did. It's cheaper, and isn't Paula going to take night classes too? You can still see your friends there. And I know that you didn't study this year, but you're going to do it right next year, aren't you?", he said, like he was forcing her to agree.

"Yes... It's a good idea... Will Dad agree, Mom?"

"I don't know, we have to talk to him. I hadn't thought about it either..."

Augusto came back, calling everyone:

"Come on, family, let's go. Do you want to go straight home, or want to stay here some more, Nic?"

"Home. I can't wait to get back home and meet my friends again!"

"You're so cheerful, ain't you Nic? What did you do back in England?"

"Nothing so special... I think the trip itself was a good thing to me..."

* * *

At home, Nic went to his room and, before he laid down on the bed, he complained because while he was absent, his room had been used as some kind of depository of food and other things. Joana promised she would take the things out of there as soon as she found some other place to put them. Nic left his luggage on the floor, untouched, and fell on the bed, with his face on the pillow.

"Nic?", Luísa showed up, stretching out her neck inside her brother's room.

"Hi, Luzinha. Come in."

"Nic, I told you, please, don't call me like that! I don't like it!"

"Okay, sorry..."

"Did you bring me what I asked you to?"

"What? All those pencils, pens, diaries, CDs, T-shirts, and this camera right here? Sorry, I forgot...", he pointed to an opened suitcase that was lying on the floor, waiting to be unpacked.

"Nic! You... you bought this camera for me?"

"Yes. Do you like it?"

"Do I like it? You're crazy, of course I like it! It's a digital camera, I wanted one like that so bad! Thanks, Nic!", happily, she hugged her brother, who smiled, and then she looked at the other things he had brought for her.

"This is a cool T-shirt!"

"It is, isn't it? But, sorry, it's mine. So, Luísa, how are things going in your life? What about Ana Paula, and your boyfriend... Douglas, right?"

"Um, Ana Paula is fine, as always... I talk to her everyday... And Douglas, well, it's been about two weeks since I broke up with him."

"You broke up? Why?"

"Because he's such an idiot, a liar, a pig, a jerk. He cheated on me with Juliana!"

"That ridiculous friend of yours? Keep in mind that's just my opinion, by the way."

"Ex-friend, you mean. You had to be there... I argued with her in front of the whole school..."

"I believe you did. I know you well!", Nic laughed. "So, you can defend yourself without me now? I think me not being here did you good."

"First of all, my dear", she looked him in the eyes, talking in a funny tone of voice. "I always knew how to defend myself without you. Lastly, my dear, you have no idea how I missed you, all the bullshit you used to say, everything!", they laughed.

"I’m only a bullshitter to you, huh?"

"Well Nic, I'm going to leave you alone now. Unpack, sleep, do whatever you want. I have to..."

At that moment, the bell rang. The two of them heard Joana opening the door and saying hello to Ana Paula, who soon showed up at Nic's room.

"... as I said, I was going to leave you alone, unpacking, sleeping..."

"Hello, you guys", Ana Paula said hello, timidly, and looked at Nic like she was looking at something different from what she expected. He got up and hugged her.

"It's been so long, little girl!", he said. "How are you doing?"

"Things are alright with me. How was your trip? What about England? Did you do a lot of different things there?"

"Sure. Before I came back, I could see the snow. There is a lot of different things you can do with the snow that you can't do here, without it", he said. "I took lots of pictures, my parents are seeing them, I'll go there soon get them to show you. All the hard work I had to do before traveling was definitely worth it."

"And look at all the stuff he brought me", Luísa showed a lot of things among some clothes that would soon have a place with the other piles of things she already had.

"Whoa, Luísa... Particularly, I think this is exploration, I knew you had asked Nic for some gifts, but I thought that it would be something symbolic..." she laughed.

"Speaking of gifts, I brought something for you too", Nic said, searching in the suitcase. "I'm sorry if this is a little more symbolic than Luísa's gifts, but I hope you like it!" he gave her two T-shirts, a diary and a colored pen.

"Nicolas... man, you shouldn't have... Thank you!" Ana Paula felt embarrassed, and spoke while she was looking at the T-shirts. "They're so beautiful! Thanks!"

"You're welcome. I couldn't forget you, you're like my sister too! You're always with my sister..."

She smiled a weak smile. 'Like his sister...', he said...

While the three of them talked in Nic's room, Augusto and Joana were talking in the kitchen, while seeing Nic's pictures. Joana had tried every way to make her husband change his mind about their daughter's school, with no progress. She would now try to convince him to let Luísa study at São Lucas, night classes, since the girl seemed happy when she heard about this idea.

"At night?", Augusto said.

"Yes... Maybe it’ll be better, she could help me with the baskets during the day. I have had more orders than usual, and I'm thinking about making invitation cards too. It would be nice if Luísa could stay here with me, and it would be easier if Nic and her studied at the same place, at the same time."

"Okay, I promise I'll think about it. But I don't know if she'll stay in São Lucas. That school is too expensive, and increasing every year."

"It's cheaper at night."

"Okay, okay. I'll think about it."

Joana gave Nic the pictures and he started showing them to the girls. He had taken a lot of pictures while he was living in England, but Luísa made it clear that he could have taken even more. Nic was telling them the story behind every single picture, where it had been taken, who the people were that were in it, and they talked for a long time. When they were done with the pictures, Nic said he had to go see some friends, changed his clothes and left. Ana Paula said, when she was alone with Luísa:

"Your brother didn't speak a single word in English, did you notice?"

"But it's impossible that he didn't learn it in five months... I know he did! Because there was no other way to communicate there!”
