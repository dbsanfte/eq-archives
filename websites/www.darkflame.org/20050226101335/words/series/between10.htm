CHAPTER 10 - A NICE BATH

“Paula, please listen to me... Nic cares about you so much, can’t you see that that happened in a moment of weakness, it could’ve happened to anyone!”

“Guilherme, I'm sorry, but I'm really not interested in any of this. There’s nothing Nic can say to change what I’ve seen.”

“Give him another chance. Everything was so confused, Nic couldn’t get out of that room, he didn’t know where you were, what you were thinking, I... If I were him, I would have probably done something irrational too...”

“I did irrational things, Valéria! I slept outside beside the pool, I fought with Nic for no reason. But if he did this, it was because he didn’t really trust me, he didn’t think I'd come back to apologize!”

“Paula, come on!”, Luísa was upset. “Can’t you stop talking crazy and think about what’s really happening? Put yourself in Nic’s place! You could leave this chalet right now and kiss the first guy you saw, and that would certainly not change what you feel about Nic!”

“I'm disappointed in Nic!”

“Hey, let’s stop with all this”, Guilherme got up from the chair he was sitting on and looked at me, then glanced at Luísa and continued walking through the room.

“No more fights here. Let’s at least try to have a decent conversation. Look, Paula, if you don’t want to hear it, we understand. Think a little about what you’re doing and come to a conclusion by yourself. But there is one thing: the nurse said that tomorrow Nic may leave the ward.”

“What?”

“He’s fine, won’t be staying there anymore. So you better get ready because he will do anything and everything to have you back with him.”

That could only be a dream. Looking at Nic was the last thing I wanted. I wasn’t prepared for the amount of trouble this vacation brought to me. And if we were home, nothing would have happened.

We stayed at the chalet for a long time, maybe hours. Guilherme, Luísa and Valéria talked, but nothing relating to Nic or me.

Guilherme was looking at a perfume bottle that was laying on the table, Luísa and Valéria were looking at me, when someone knocked on the door.

“Hey, let us come in!”, Alice’s voice.

“Open the door!”, Adriana’s voice.

Hysterically, Luísa went near the door and yelled:

“Get out of there, Adriana, if I ever see your face I will hit you like you never thought you could be hit!”

“Hey, take it easy, it’s dark, it’s late, it’s going to rain soon, where are we going to sleep?"

Luísa hesitated for a moment, and opened the door, letting only Alice in.

“What’s this? Let me in!”

A thunder preceded Alice’s words:

“Are you gonna leave Adriana outside?”

“You guys inside”, Adriana yelled, becoming desperate with the idea of spending the night outside in the rain. “Don’t forget my things are in there, and I need some place to sleep!”

“Shut up, Adriana! You are not coming in, fuck your things, fuck where you’re going to sleep!”

“Hey, Luísa, you can’t do this! She’s my friend and it’s our chalet too!”

“Do you know what she did?”

Alice hesitated. As if she had not heard Luísa, she continued:

“If she’s going to be outside, I'm going too”, Alice opened the door and left the chalet. “And if we decide to talk about it with the campground’s directors, your vacation will end right now!” she shut the door. Valéria ran after Alice.

“Valéria, don’t go!” Luísa was watching from the door.

“You two” Adriana and Alice turned back. “If anyone ever gets to know what happened here, you’ll regret it. Don’t you dare tell this to anyone.”

“You’re afraid now, huh? Listen, Adriana likes Nic and she’s fighting for him, you don’t have anything to say about it!”

“I wouldn’t call it fighting, just being manipulative. There’s never been any cheating between us, until now! So it will be very difficult to forget what you did, Adriana... It figures it had to be someone from outside to ruin our relationships!”

“Oh, so you’re so perfect, and anyone who does not have blue blood breaks the harmony!”

“Anyone who doesn’t have any sense of shame, you mean!”

The first drops of water began to fall. Valéria didn’t say anything else, and went back to the chalet.

“Just forget about that help we promised you about Cláudio, Valéria, until the day you come to say you’re sorry for everything you just told us!”

Valéria stopped at the door, without turning around. Without a word, she entered.

Guilherme and I looked at each other, and then we looked at Valéria and Luísa.

“I'm sorry, Valéria”, I said. She was looking at the ground and didn’t say anything.

After that, we fell asleep.

* * *

“They must have slept in our chalet, with Cláudio and João”, said Guilherme, who had slept with us. Everyone was up already.
“And the confusion must be calming down by now...”

“Valéria... forgive me, please... I know you liked Cláudio, but it couldn’t just stay like that...”

“It’s not your fault. Adriana screwed everything up”.

“We still have almost two entire weeks here”, Guilherme spoke, with a scratchy voice. “I don’t think we’re enjoying it this way. So, if you want, we can go back home tomorrow...” Luísa looked at him, getting his point. To fix it all, put a finger right on my bruise: the guilt.

“I don’t want to end your vacation!”

“Luísa and I started it all off. Our vacation is gone, too”.

“I told you that you have nothing to do with it, not you or anyone else here, oh shit! If Adriana really wanted to do what she did, she’d do it, with a sick Nic, with a healthy Nic, with an upset me, with a happy me! SHE screwed up our vacation!”

“I think that if you talk to Nic, we can still have some good moments without them here” Luísa looked at me. I didn’t answer. We were all quiet.

“Paula! Open this door!”

We got scared. It was Nic.

“I know you’re there, Paula, open the door!”

“I'm opening it”, Luísa got up and opened the door. Before she did that, I had already ran and locked myself in the bathroom.

“Nic, you’ll need to use all your skills if you want to have Paula back...”, Guilherme looked at him out of the corner of his eyes.

“Where is she?”

“In the bathroom. She won’t leave any time soon.”

“We’ll let you guys alone”, Valéria left the chalet, the others went with her.

“Stay around, in case I need someone to help me take the knife out of her hand”, Nic said, motionless.

‘Nic, how can you say jokes at a time like this?’, I thought.

“Paula, come out, I want to talk to you”, he knocked on the door, softly. I didn’t answer. I was crying. “Paula... all right, you can stay there, but you’re going to hear me anyway. I don’t have much to say, as you must be thinking, but... please, stop crucifying me!”

‘Stop, Nic, for God’s sake!’, I was with my hands on my ears, but his voice kept going through my fingers.

“I'm so sorry, Paula, I didn’t know what I was...” I suddenly opened the door, interrupting him. I slipped through the space between Nic and the door, trying not to touch him. He followed me, at a certain distance.

“Paula, wait!”

“Hun, things are getting worse”, Luísa, Guilherme and Valéria were watching us. “I think it’s better we go after them.”

“I think it’s better we don’t. Maybe alone, Nic might manage to talk to her”, and the three of them went to the snack bar.

I walked, and walked, until I reached the lake in front of the forest and stopped, turned back to face Nic. He stopped too, but didn’t approach me.

“Paula, I know you’re upset with me, but you have no idea how I am feeling myself. I'm so bad, I'm making an enormous effort to talk to you, because the truth is I don’t know where to hide...” he was gesturing, somewhat between confused and upset.

I fell on the ground, in tears.

Nic approached me, but stayed up, waiting for me to get up.

“I'm sorry, once more.”

I got up when he said that. I got up and ran to the woods. Nic didn’t say a word. He didn’t follow me either.

* * *

The others talked at the snack bar.

“Look... I’ll talk to Paula, I’ll try making her think differently, but I can’t believe Nic cheated on her myself.”

“Luísa, that happens...”

“I know, but... Nic? Nic is not like that... He’s one of the few guys I know who take things so seriously, who is so sincere...”

“Really? Who are the other ones?”

“You’re one of them, of course...”

Luísa hugged Guilherme. He looked at Valéria, who smiled, and said:

“She loves me, look at that! If I climbed up on a stage, she’d make the noise of an entire crowd crazy for me!”

“You’re spoiling my brother, Luísa! He thinks he’s just the best, the king of the world!”

“At least I'm the king of this little heart that’s beating inside Luísa, carrying her blood and food to her cells, and giving her the energy needed to hug me!”

“He has a different concept than other lovers”, Luísa explains, laughing. “The heart is not the home of love, but the place where energy comes from to make the brain able to love! I mean, totally important for the whole process. If Guilherme wrote a poem, he’d win the Nobel for Medicine!”

“So Paula’s heart is out of order?”

“No!”, everybody laughs. “She’s crazy, that’s it. My God, she may not know why Nic did that, but how can she not realize he’s sorry for it?”

“She built a wall in her mind, subconsciously, which prevents her from realizing anything, and all she can think about is the bad things, the worse things, the terrible things...”

“Holy Christ, stop with this bullshit!”

The three of them continued talking randomly for a couple minutes, when Valéria looked behind Luísa and Guilherme and totally changed the expression on her face. From happy, she went to sad, upset, disappointed.

Guilherme and Luísa turned back right in time to see Alice, João and Cláudio. In time to see Cláudio turning his back to them. Or more specifically, to Valéria. She got up and ran to the bathroom.

“Valéria!”, Luísa ran after her, leaving Guilherme alone. “Valéria, please, don’t do this...”

In an isolated place, Valéria cries.

“Valéria... don’t cry... Look... If Cláudio is doing this to you, it’s because he doesn’t want anything with you, I mean...”

“I know what’s happening! They told him what I wanted and now he’s ignoring me! And it’s not because he doesn’t want anything with me, like you said... He’s doing it for the same reason we are! Because of friendship! To them, we are the ones at fault...”

“Valéria, do you think I'm happy for having fought with them? It could last only one month, it could definitely end when we leave this place, but we were still friends! But we can’t, not like this. They are supporting Adriana, we are not, and I don’t want to talk to one of them and pretend everything is okay. Don’t think I'm against you too, but it’s better that you lose the opportunity to give Cláudio some kisses, than doing it and messing with Paula and Nic’s relationship forever.”

“I know. How do you think I'm feeling? I pissed off Paula and...”

“For the last time, stop blaming yourself! Are you obsessed or what?”, Luísa yelled, and then spoke softly. “This all began after your fight, and, even if it didn’t, the trouble is there and now the ones who need to fix it are Paula and Nic, only the two of them. Do you get it?”

“Okay, okay...”

“Great. Let’s go back to the table now. Look right in the eyes of whoever you need to, show everyone that much more is needed than a ‘no’ from a guy to upset you!” Guilherme was still at the table. He was holding his head with one hand, and the other was playing with a piece of paper.

“Where are they?”

“Might have gone to the pool.”

Valéria looked at the part of the pool we could see from the snack bar. It was full of people, it’d be hard finding someone specific there.

“Hey guys...”, Guilherme stopped playing with the paper and raised his head, confused. “Adriana was not with them!”

They looked at each other, puzzled. All of a sudden, at the same time, they got up in a hurry and ran to the woods.

* * *

I guess it was actually better that I ran from Nic. I mean, I wouldn’t have been able to listen to him there. Maybe it was better to run than keep looking at him, hidden, listening to him.

Right after I entered the forest, I leaned on a tree and cried. I had never cried like that in my whole life, but the idea of losing Nic was just killing me. This, added to the fact that I didn’t have any courage to admit that I loved him and to forgive him, like Nic did, made my lachrymal glands work like never before. The one in this whole story who I should blame for that was myself, I knew Nic wouldn’t cheat on me anymore if we got back, but I just couldn’t do it. In fact, I don’t know what I was afraid of. While thinking about all this, I started going back to Nic’s place, slowly, ready to forget everything and make things back to normal again. ‘Within a few days, no one will remember anything’, I thought.

When I was almost at the edge of the forest, I stopped and observed. Nic couldn’t see me, he was looking at the lake, sometimes he would throw a little stone on the water. I took a step in his direction, but receded. Someone was coming and I knew exactly who it was, to my despair.

“Niiiic... are you there?”

‘Bitch’, I thought. I hid myself behind a big tree. Nic didn’t move.

“I finally found you!”, a sweet, malicious voice. “I thought you had disappeared, evaporated... You are a good hider, you know?”

“What do you want, Adriana? Wasn’t it enough for you, ruining my relationship, my vacation, what’s next?”

“You know what’s next...”, she held him from his back, caressing his chest. If there weren’t so many bugs in that tree, I would have pulled a piece of it with my teeth, I was feeling so angry.

“Get out of my life, Adriana!”, Nic got up abruptly, throwing her to the ground.

“Hey, take it easy! I won’t bite you... And keep in mind there’s only two weeks left, you wouldn’t wanna waste them, would you? I can give you what Paula never did...”

I wanted to kill her right then. In the worst way I could think of.

“You’re such a whore, Adriana”, Nic grabbed her shoulders, and pushed her to the lake. In this very moment, Luísa, Valéria and Guilherme arrived.

“Nic! What have you done?”, Luísa smiled. Adriana looked at them, angry and embarrassed, from the lake, totally wet. Seeing her, they started laughing. Including Nic. Including me.

I left my hideout and ran to him. I hugged him as tight as I could.

“Nic, I'm sorry!”

“I love you, Paula!”

That’s it. It was over. Just a little gesture, a simple gesture, gave me back my happiness. Nic was mine again.
