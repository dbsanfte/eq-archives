FREEDOM

Our choices give directions to the destiny.

It walks with its own legs.
If we don't stop it, it doesn't stop.
If we don't care for it, it doesn't heal by itself.
If we don't hold it, it falls.

And when it does...

The first falling may be light.
Just a hole dig by a dog.

The second one hurts more.
Not only dogs dig holes.

The third one is mortal.
It may be from a cliff. From a tower.
From a plane.

It doesn't help crying. Cliffs are cruel.
Regret doesn't kill, but also doesn't give life back.

. . .

I didn't cry.

I let it go. So simple.

I stopped it. I cared for it.
But I didn't hold it.

After so many fallings, it was too much hurt.
No magic could heal it.
Why would I suffer?

We approached the cliff.
And I let it fall.

. . .

I'm free.
And it was a big relief, so big.

I did my part.
I stopped, I cared.
But all the time they held out a leg, and the falling took place.
So I let go, when there wasn't any more medication to that trouble.

. . .

Relief. Lightness. Relief. Freedom. Relief.

And the lesson is left.
The story.
To tell
- not to everyone.

Did I lose something?
No... I didn't.
What was there to be lost?

But I learned:

. . .

Never again.
Down there in the cliff, it will remain.
