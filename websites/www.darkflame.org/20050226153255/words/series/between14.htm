CHAPTER 14 - HOLY NIGHT

“Wake up, Sleeping Beauties!”

“Nic?! Ah, I'm sleepy...”, Luísa rubbed her eyes. Nic was opening the curtains, and a big light filled the room. I tried to smile, still not moving.

“Where were you? We were waiting for you...”

“I'm sorry... We met some people, walked around...”

“Where’s Guilherme?”

“He went home...”

“Poor Valéria slept in our guestroom...”

“Well, I told him we’d meet him at Solar this afternoon.”

“We are?! Cool... Thanks for not asking us about it!”

“Sorry... But you’re going, aren’t you?”

“Sure.”

Luísa got up and went to change clothes. My clothes were all messed up too, so I went home.

* * *

“Hello?”

“Nic, it’s me. Are you going to Solar?”

“I don’t know, Valéria called and said Guilherme’s still sleeping...”

“Hehe, cool. But I can’t go anyway, I'm going out with my mother for some Christmas shopping.”

“Hmm, that’s right, Christmas is tomorrow. What are you getting for me?”

“A piranha for your aquarium, is that okay?”

“Okay, just let me go get some insurance first...”

“Okay, well I'm not telling you anyway. Actually I still don’t know what I'm getting for you... I’ll look for it today.”

“Wow, I'm really impressed! Well, Luísa and my father went to buy the remaining gifts, but don’t worry, I’ve bought yours already.”

“Cool. I'm not worried. Well, I’ve got to go now. Goodbye!”

“Kisses...”

Nic hung up and thought.

“How many 18 years old guys I know are in love like I am? Guilherme doesn’t count, because he’s 19 already...”

* * *

“Mom, we’ve been walking for decades with all these bags... Are you sure something’s missing?”

“Your aunt Lúcia, from Fortaleza, is spending Christmas day with us this year, and she has ‘a couple’ kids...”

“I know, ‘a couple’... Zé, Mu, Rafa, Pat, Jé and Kel...”

“And Juninho, who was born on the 4th of last month.”

“God, what a zoo! Well, okay... Um, Luísa invited me to go to her family’s house on Christmas and New Year. Even if she hadn’t invited me, Nic would’ve...

“Okay, but go home the next day to say hello to everybody!”

“Is everyone staying in our house?!”

“Yes... We’re going to borrow some mattresses, and we’ll put them where we can.”

“Man, that’s going to look like a beehive, with people falling out of the windows!"

And we kept walking, talking...

“Mom! Mom! Mom! Stop!”, I pulled her inside a store. “Look at this jacket, it’s perfect for Nic...”

“Which one?”

“The black one, with the ‘Bad Boy’ symbol on the back...”

“Can I help you?”, a saleman approached.

“How much does this jacket cost?”

I could feel my mother’s wallet calling for help.

But we bought the jacket, after some negotiation. After all, Nic deserved it...

* * *

“Hellooo...”, I said, still sleepy, I had just been woken up by the phone ringing.

“Paula, it’s me, Luísa. Are you coming with us today?”

“Yes, I'm coming... I mean, going!” the laugh on the other side of the line woke me up a little.

“So be here tonight, we’re leaving at eight o’clock, we’ll go to my aunt’s house.”

“Okay... what about Valéria and Guilherme?”

“They’re coming too. So will Álvaro, because the only relatives they have in this city are out on some trip.”

“I understand... You can wait for me, I’ll be there.”

* * *

“Whoa, gifts for me!”, Nic wanted to get the gifts I had with me.

“Don’t even think about it, only at midnight!”

“Let’s go, we’re late!”

Everyone got in the car. Álvaro, Valéria and Guilherme followed us, in their own car. In a couple minutes we reached the block, which had the most cars I’d ever seen.

“I guess that’s the house we’re going to”, I pointed to a big, noisy house, which was full of people.

“You guess well. Well, I don’t think we’ll have any problem if we park in the middle of the street.”, Augusto was driving, leaving enough space for a car to pass, if needed.

We got out of the car. I had done this before, because I already knew some of Luísa’s relatives from her mother’s birthday – her last one, by the way. Guilherme was calm around Luísa, but Valéria seemed a little nervous, maybe she was thinking we would leave her alone with those people she didn’t know.

“Hi, everybody!”, Luísa was the first one to enter, saying hello to the people as she was finding them, and introducing Guilherme and Valéria. And me too, obviously, many of her relatives still didn’t know me. Augusto followed us, with the bags full of gifts.

“May I help you?”, the host offered. It was almost nine o’clock.

We were all in the living room, us and Luísa’s cousins who were about our age. Over the next hour, more people arrived.

“Hello, hello... Um, I can see there are new people in here...”

“Oh”, Luísa got up and hugged her cousin, who did the same. “This is Guilherme, my boyfriend, Valéria and Ana Paula. This is my favorite cousin, Jefferson, known throughout our family as Magic Jé...”

“Magic Jé? You play basketball, I bet...”

“Exactly. Next year I’ll be moving to the capital to play professionally.”

“How old are you?”, I was astonished.

“Twenty-two. Okay, okay, you don’t need to say I look seventeen, but that means that I’ll look twenty-eight when I'm really forty...”

“Man, you really are from Nic’s family.”

“What can we do? The Porter family kids are perfect!”, Nic didn’t even open his mouth to speak. He got hit by some people, and that was all.

“But why is Jefferson your favorite cousin?”

“Because he made a three-pointer for me, and a two-pointer for Nic”, Luísa explained.

I realized that putting Nic, Luísa and ‘Magic Jé’ together could result in only one thing: an explicit demonstration of self-pride. It was funny. We were talking and soon it seemed like Valéria, Guilherme and I were talking to old friends.

“Guys, come on have supper!”, someone called to us.

“It’s midnight already?”

“No, it’s eleven-thirty.”

“Oh, huge difference...”

The food was all on a big table, people would serve themselves and go eat on the little tables on the backyard. It was likely it would rain, but everything was under a roof.

The clocks said midnight, everyone wished Merry Christmas to each other. A little boy wanted to see the gifts, and the dinner was interrupted.

There were tons of gifts there. I’d never seen anything like it, everyone gave presents to everyone else. It looked like a Christmas movie, just that the snow was missing.

Valéria, Guilherme and I only brought gifts for each other, but I bet no one else would care about it. So, after the rush ended, the five of us traded gifts peacefully. Nic wanted us to exchange gifts alone.

“It’s nothing spectacular, but I thought we could make this moment special...”

“You just did...”

Nic opened the gift I gave him first. I think he loved the jacket, because he wore it immediately, and it wasn’t cold at all. He couldn’t stop saying good things about it.

“As soon as I put my eyes on it, I knew it was made for you.”

“Ah, I didn’t know I looked like a Bad Boy”, he turned his back to me, showing the image. I smiled. “Look, this is my gift for you...”

Nic gave me the little box he had left on the table. I opened it. There were two gold necklaces, very beautiful.

“Nic... they’re so wonderful...”, I looked at them, feeling every little ring it had.

“I thought about a pair of rings, but I liked these necklaces so much”, he explained, while he put one of them on my neck. “This one is yours.”

I looked at the pendant. It was a heart crossed by an arrow, with Nic’s name written on the back.

“And this one”, he put the other on his own neck, and held the pendant, it was just like mine. “is mine. It has your name on it. Be sure, that for the time I'm wearing it, it’s because I love you.”

Nic never looked so sincere to me. If he proposed at that moment, I'd say yes immediately, certain that our happines would be eternal.

“So do I”, that was all I could say. We hugged each other, in a long, deep kiss.
