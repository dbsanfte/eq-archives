CHAPTER 7 - SOMETHING IS WRONG

During the following week, Luísa missed school two days and came back there only after Nic insisted a lot. Joana’s burial, on Saturday, was the most difficult moment for the two of them. Augusto didn’t know what to do to comfort his children, but slowly – really slowly – everything came back to normal. Augusto was working, Nic and Luísa studying. Luísa would go to the cemetery every weekend. Sometimes, Guilherme picked up his sister and her friends at the English school by car, and everyone went to the cemetery with Luísa. Guilherme was now closer to the group, much more than he was before.

Almost three months had passed, and vacation was approaching. During one afternoon at the end of June, Luísa found her father looking for something in Joana’s purse, which had been kept with all her things, in a specific room they’d set aside.

“Dad, what’s the problem?”

“I’m looking for something... I can’t find it... The silver necklace I gave to your mother on our 10th anniversary.”

“Can’t you find it? Isn’t it with her other jewelry?”

“No... but, that’s okay”, Augusto gave up, upset. “I’d like to keep it with me... But that’s okay, I’ll look for it later.”

He left. ‘The silver necklace...’, Luísa thought. ‘I wonder if it’s the one she was wearing that day? Hmm... no, I don’t think so, I think she wasn’t wearing anything that day.’ Luísa continued studying. The finals were finally approaching.

* * *

Two days later, on Saturday, Luísa called Valéria and Álvaro her father answered the phone:

"Hello?"

"Hi, is Valéria there?"

"Who is it?"

"It's Luísa."

Álvaro was quiet. Luísa asked again:

"Please, can you call Valéria...?"

"I... Well...", his voice was trembling. "I'll go check if she's here... I mean, if she's busy."

After a moment, he was back:

"Valéria is busy with... the English homework."

"But... Well, we don't have any English homework, English classes are over."

"I... ahn...", now, he seemed embarrassed, plus he was trembling. "That's what she told me..."

"I don't know... But, please, could you call her anyway? I'll be quick, it's just a moment!"

"I'm sorry, but she asked not to be... bothered."

"Does she have a problem?"

"I'm sorry, Luísa, but if she's not busy, I don't know why she doesn't want to talk."

"But..."

"I'm sorry again. Bye."

"Hello? Hello? Damn! What's going on with Valéria?"

Luísa called Ana Paula:

"I tried to talk to Valéria, but she didn't want to talk to me. Her father was acting weird, and when I started insisting, he hung up on me. Do you know if something happened to her?"

"No... Not that I know of..."

"Call her yourself, maybe the problem is me and I don't know it..."

"You? No way!"

"Well, anyway, could you do this favor for me? Try talking to her. It's about the party for the first semester ending, she’s supposed to give me the details..."

"Okay, I'll do it."

Ana Paula hung up and called Valéria. Álvaro answered again:

"Hello?"

"I want to talk to Valéria, please."

"She's not home."

"Not home? But Luísa just called you and she didn't want to talk, she thought Valéria was angry or something, and asked me to call..."

"She doesn't want to talk. Sorry."

"But... Hello? Álvaro? Hello?"

Ana Paula called Luísa again:

"Luísa, she doesn't want to talk. I mean, first, her father said she wasn’t home, and then he said she didn't want to talk..."

"That's weird..."

"Álvaro is even weirder! He sounded nervous, like he suddenly developed a stutter! He sounded scared..."

Luísa thought for a moment.

"Come here, Paula, we can talk about it..."

"Well, I don't know what you want to talk about, Valéria must have had a fight with Álvaro, and now she doesn't want to talk to anyone..."

"Is it that serious, that Álvaro would act so strangely? Hey Paula, I don't think so! Come here, we'll find out what's happening."

A couple minutes later, Ana Paula was at Luísa's.

"Let's try again", Luísa said, holding the phone.

She dialed. No one answered. She tried again. Nothing happened.

"Could they leave home that quick?"

On the third try, Valéria answered.

"He... Hello? Who is it?"

"Valéria, it's Luísa. What happened? I tried to talk to you, but your father said that..."

Luísa was interrupted by a rough voice, which was speaking to Valéria. It wasn't loud, but Luísa shut up, told Ana Paula to do the same, and she could hear the conversation:

"Hey girl, who is it?"

"It's a friend of mine, she doesn't want nothing special, let me..."

"Hang up!"

"Please, I just..."

"HANG UP! Come on, kid, do what I tell you to do, or else you know what's going to happen! Docka doesn't want..."

Luísa couldn't hear more because Valéria started crying. The phone was violently hung up, probably taken off Valéria's hand. Luísa still held her phone for a moment, like she was trying to understand what had just happened. Then, Ana Paula spoke to her:

"Hey, Luísa! What's wrong?"

"I don't know..."

"You don't know? What did Valéria say?"

"It wasn't her..."

"No? Didn't she answer the call?"

"Yes, but..."

"Come on, tell me!"

"Well... Valéria answered, and while I was asking her why she didn't want to talk to me, a rough voice came from the back and asked her who was on the phone, and told her to hang up... Before she did, she started crying and the voice told her to obey, or else she knew what would happen. Then he said 'Docka doesn't want' and that was all, I didn't hear anything else."

"That was all, you say? You think it's not enough?", Ana Paula asked.

"What do you think?"

"It's weird, of course... I'll call her again to check it out."

"Are you crazy? What if that guy does something to them? Maybe he has caller ID, and..."

"Luísa, don't be a chicken! Do you want to know what's going on out there or not?"

"Of course I do!"

"So, let me call her."

Reluctant, Luísa gave up. Ana Paula dialed, and after some minutes, someone answered. This time, it was Guilherme.

"Hello?"

"Guilherme? This is Paula. May I talk to you for a second?"

"Well, I... Hold on just a minute, please."

Ana Paula could hear the voices through the phone. They were distant, like Guilherme had put his hand on the receiver. She heard the ‘rough voice' arguing with him, and Valéria interrupted:

"Who is it, boy?"

"A friend."

"You have too many friends calling. Hang up now."

"I need to talk to her."

"Guilherme, please, do what he wants..." Valéria said, with a scared voice.

"Stay out of this, girl! And you, kid, hang up now! Go!"

"Don't call me a kid. Let me talk to her, then I'll hang up, and the problem is solved! No one will ever call us!"

"Do what I say... or you know what will happen!"

"Just a minute!"

"NO!" and Ana Paula heard a shot, and Valéria screamed. She screamed too, scaring Luísa. Silence. The phone on the other end fell down, but the line was still open. Ana Paula was terrified, and put her hand on the receiver when she heard that voice grab it and ask, insistently, who wanted to talk to Guilherme. Since he didn't get any answer, he hung up the phone violently, but before he did that, Ana Paula could hear him saying 'enough phone calls now'. Only after that, Ana Paula hung up too, and Luísa asked what she had heard.

"Screams..."

"Screams?"

"A shot..."

"What?"

"That voice wanted to talk to me..."

But Luísa didn't seem to be worried either about voices or about screams.

"Paula, tell me exactly what you heard. Did you hear a shot?"

Ana Paula told her everything she had heard. Then, they heard Augusto's voice:

"Luísa, could you do me a favor? I need..." he entered the room, but when he saw the girls' scared faces, he switched his question. "What happened?"

Nic showed up too. Luísa had just said:

"I don't know..."

Ana Paula explained everything. Nic was looking around, suspicious, Augusto was paying attention to her. Finally she finished, and Nic said:

"Could that ‘voice’ be some friend or relative of theirs?"

"I don't think so... What kind of friend or relative would do something like that?" Augusto said, thinking.

"Call them again and ask!", said Nic, sounding like he thought it was obvious.

"No", Augusto said. "Didn't you say that the man complained about the calls? Who knows what's going on there? They might be being robbed or something..."

"He threatened everyone..."

"Call the police, Dad!", said Nic.

"No."

"Oh God, why not?"

"Luísa, it's better that we don't mess with them. If the police suddenly arrive there, who knows what might happen. We don't know either who or how many of them are there!"

"But they're our friends..."

"This is a tough situation, honey. But let things go on, as soon as those people get what they want, they'll leave everyone in peace. Whatever it is that they want... I don't think Álvaro, Valéria or Guilherme would react to them."
"Yes, okay, alright... But what if that 'voice' is a murderer, a kidnapper, or..."

"Of course he's a bandit. And if I call the police, God only knows what he could do."

Everyone was silent, exchanging glances, thinking. If anyone had payed a little more attention to Luísa, they would’ve seen fear on her face. And she was really worried: 'I buried my mother. I don't want to do the same to my friend's family'. Luísa was thinking about her mother. About her mother's stupid death. 'I so wish to see a scamp like that getting what he deserves!' she thought. Then she got an idea, and before thinking more carefully about the bullshit she was about to say, she said it:

"Why don't we go to their house? No one would suspect us, a group of teenagers on a Saturday night, right? That man would never imagine we were the ones who called!"

"No way!" Augusto yelled. "Have you gone nuts? You don't know what kind of people are there! What if it's a murderer, who has a furious attack at the same time you're there, then he goes out and kills everyone?"

"Dad!", Nic said. "Don't be so pessimistic! Don't worry, we won't do anything. But, they're our friends, and they might be in serious trouble."

"I wonder if they did something wrong to someone?"

"I don't think so. Maybe that guy just wants to rob the house in peace."

Suddenly, a horrifying thought came to Ana Paula's mind:

"After that shot, Guilherme's voice disappeared, the phone fell down. I wonder if Guilherme... if that guy... killed him?"

"Don't even think about it!", Luísa yelled. "No one is going to die!"

"Wait, I just thought that..."

"I told you, shut up! Something bad may really happen, because of your curse!"

"Okay! I didn't say anything. That's it."

"I wanted to know what's going on there...", Luísa groaned.

"Well", Augusto said. "You did, but you won't do anything. I won't admit that you got into this, okay? Don't even think about playing detective, all right?"

No one answered. Augusto left the room. Luísa sat on the floor, held her legs and, with her forehead on her knees, she said, whispering, weeping:

"No one can die there... No way!"

"Calm down, Luísa", Ana Paula tried to comfort her friend.

"Paula, I've already lost my mother..."

"I know, I know..."

"If something happens to... to some of them..."

"To Guilherme... I understand you, Luísa..."

Ana Paula and Nic looked at each other. They felt pity for Luísa.

"I'll find out what's happening, and it'll be tomorrow, as soon as I wake up!", Luísa said.

"Don't even think about playing detective..." Ana Paula said, remembering Augusto's words.

"But we've already thought about it, right?", Nic said, hugging his sister.
