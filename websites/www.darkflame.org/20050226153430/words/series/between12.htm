CHAPTER 12 - FAREWELL

It was around ten at night. I was sitting at the door to the chalet, the weather was hot. Valéria had already gone to the dancing club, and Luísa had also “gone out”.

I looked at the other chalets. Open windows, music, people outside... I joined the group who didn’t care about the night bugs – the difference between me and them was that I was alone.

“Beautiful night, don’t you think?”

“Nic?!”

Nic arrived so quietly, I didn’t even notice it. He was wearing shorts, hands in his pockets, a very cute face. He sat beside me.

“I had to find something to do, since my chalet is being used... I imagined that maybe you would be abandoned too.”

“Did I have any choice?”, I smiled.

A nearby disturbance drew my attention. I took a couple steps ahead, and saw one of the campground monitors coming, after stopping in one of the chalets to eat something.

“Nic, go inside, hurry! The woman is coming, she cannot see you here!”

“Hey, don’t worry! So many people break the rules...”

“Your sister, for example...”, I closed the door and locked it. “But I don’t want to take the risk.”

“Well, anyway, I'm already here, I can’t go away... don’t you think it’s better to close the windows too?”, before I answered, he had already closed them.

“Hey, it’s hot in here... You could stay right here, no one would see you.”

Nic was up, beside the window.

“Paula, I was thinking...”, he paused.

“...about?”, something in his voice made me stay where I was, distant from him. I sat on a chair, but we didn’t look into each other’s eyes. ‘This is becoming dangerous...’, I thought.

“About... About us, about how we’re so happy together, this vacation brought good things for everyone, especially us, we’re going home tomorrow and I thought... why not...”

“Nic... please...”

He looked at me. I didn’t look back at him. I wouldn’t resist, but I wasn’t ready.

“Look, if you are worried about condoms, don’t be afraid, I have one here...”

“No, Nic, it’s not that, I mean... I... I am not ready for that yet...”, I felt like I was so stupid saying that. ‘Being not ready’ sounded so... idiotic...

After a short pause, Nic smiled, kind of disappointed:

“Well, if that’s what you want...”

“I'm sorry... I ruined your expectations!”, I smiled back.

“No, you didn’t”, he sat on the bed. “The thing is, I never thought it’d be so hard to tell you all this.”

‘It wasn’t easy for me either’, I thought. I sat beside him.

“I must have done something spectacular in my past life to deserve somone like you”, and we kissed each other. He hugged me, tight; we didn’t think about anything else, we just wished that moment could last forever, so we would be able to get to know each other more and more...

* * *

“Hey! Open the door, Paula!”

Valéria and the others arrived, around four in the morning.

“Paula, we’re all sleepy, the other chalet is locked!”

“Give us some time, Val!”, Nic yelled.

“Oh... yeah... I think I get it.”

If I knew Valéria well enough, I could be sure that she was smiling. But she must have gone away, because of the silence outside. I looked at Nic and laughed.

* * *

“João, what time is it?”, Valéria asked, yawning, with her head laying on Cláudio’s shoulders. They were all still outside.

“Five o’clock”, he was sleepy too.

“What are you doing here?”

“Luísa, we thought you’d spend the whole night with Guilherme!”

She hit Valéria, she didn’t appreciate the joke.

“Why don’t you get inside? Where’s Nic?”

“Aaaaah!” Alice yawned. “He’s in there...”

“With Paula?”

“Bingo...”

“Man, what... Paula?!”, she got more excited – or awake – than the others, and tried to see something through the closed window.

“Luísa, leave them alone!”

“Okay...”

* * *

“So, what do you think?”

“Perfect! Shall we let them come in now?”

Nic looked at me and smiled.

* * *

“What time is it?”

“Five-thirty...”

“Man, this wait is killing me...”, Luísa was now almost sleeping, like the others. “Did they forget about us here?”

“What about Guilherme? He’s alone in his chalet!”

“You can go there if you want...”

“Hehe, not now, we’re going to wait!”

“Hey... they opened the door!”

“Hallelujah!”, they all got up, Luísa and Valéria walked in front of the others. The door was only partially opened, they pushed it almost at the same time, and then...

“You son of a...”

“Nic!”, the two of them screamed, they could have woken up the whole campground. A bucket full of water that was above the door fell all over them.

Still outside, the others laughed. Nic and I, laying on the bed, laughed even more.

“Why ‘Nic’? Why me? BatNic here wouldn’t do anything without the help from the WonderPaula!

“Nothing’s like a cold bath after a ‘hot’ night!”, I was almost choking from laughing. I had never done anything like that!

“I'm gonna kill you both!”, Luísa jumped over us, laughing, and we got a little wet too. I was having a laughing attack, I couldn’t even move. Luísa gave up, because Nic was stronger than her.

All the excitement caused by Nic and me finally ended, and the guys went to the other chalet. Adriana fell asleep, Alice and Valéria were almost sleeping and Luísa, after changing her clothes, sat beside me on the bed.

“Okay, Paula, now tell me. It didn’t take you and Nic the whole night to set up that bucket. What did you do the rest of the time?”, Luísa’s odd tone demanded an answer. I laughed.

“It was really fast to set up the bucket.”

“And what else did you do, tell me!”

“Well, we did lots of things...”

“Did you have sex?” her expectation was enormous.

“Noooo!”, I laughed, laughed and laughed. I couldn’t stop laughing.

“I can’t believe it! Tell me the truth!”

“It’s true! We can go to a doctor if you want me to prove it!”

“Oh, Paula, why not?”

“Valéria is asleep...”, I looked around, as if I hadn’t heard Luísa’s question. At that time, I felt some regret, and suddenly stopped laughing. “Nic... he did try it...”

“What did he do?”

“He didn’t do anything bad, if that’s what you’re thinking!”, I smiled. “He just spoke to me, two meters away from me... Luísa... I did want it, but at the same time, I didn’t, you know?”

“Ah!”

“He’s so sweet... I feel so bad...”

“He’s really sweet... sometimes I just forget we’re brother and sister to each other!”

* * *

“Aaaaahhhhhhh!!!!”

A scream. What a good way of waking up... I looked at the clock. Almost ten in the morning. I intended to sleep at least until two in the afternoon, and then would wake up just in time to pack and go home, but Alice woke me up before that, and I realized I was the only one who was still sleeping. I smiled, no, I laughed to myself.

“Nic had to have just been here now...”

“What happened?” Adriana left the room in a towel, Valéria and Luísa came running from outside. “Is anybody hurt?”

“There’s... there’s a frog in my suitcase!!!” she put her hand over her mouth.

“Are you nauseous, or just afraid of it?”, I joined them, slowly, with a sleepy smile on my face.

They looked at me.

“Paula, I can’t believe it!”, Alice ran after me, I started laughing while I escaped from her. We got out of the chalet, I was still in my pajamas, people started staring at us. “You and Nic put that disgusting animal in my suitcase!”

I didn’t answer, I couldn’t stop laughing.

“Take it out of there now!”, Alice couldn’t help laughing too, like the others.

I went inside and soon came back out with the frog in my hand, I was holding it with a paper, because, after all, I didn’t know it very well.

“Paula, I can’t believe you did that! You never do things like that!”

“I'm just having fun... We’re on vacation, I'm far from home, I can do whatever I want without having to explain it to anyone!”

Luísa and Valéria laughed. Adriana and Alice didn’t understand, but that was okay.

“Hehe, okay... What else did you do?”

“I don’t knooow...”

“Nic’s really changing you! He looks serious, but he sure loves a little trouble...”

“I must agree that he can have fun without looking vulgar, like most 18-year-old guys!”

“Irk, what’s that?”

We looked to the chalet. Adriana was in there. We went inside.

“Paula, did you put a balloon in the freezer?”, she was holding the ‘balloon’.

“Balloon?”, Luísa laughed. “Can’t you see that this is a condom that’s filled with air? It’s Nic’s, I bet!”

“Exactly!”

“Oh my God, this is just disgusting!”

“You fool, it’s not used, can’t you see?”, Luísa and I were laughing to death. Valéria was laughing ‘a little’ less than us.

“I can’t believe you, I just can’t!”

“I can see it takes people more than just one month to get used to us. Especially with Nicolas Porter!”, a familiar voice said.

“Nic, it’s early, you’re already here?”

“I came to see how things are.”

“Everything’s just fine.”

“Do you have any more gifts for us?”

“I don’t know, how many did you get so far?”

“Two of them”, I answered.

“I can’t remember, maybe there is, maybe there isn’t”, Nic didn’t care much about that, and we laughed again. Then he grabbed my hand: “Come on, let’s go for a walk.”

“Won’t you stay to see what happens next?”

“Don’t worry, I know how it’s gonna be. Some screams, some cursing, and that’s it.”

We left. I don’t know if there’s any need to say so, but we were laughing.

* * *

“Nic, how did you find this place?”

“I heard someone talking about it and came to check it out.”

“It’s so beautiful! Wonderful! But what if someone sees us here?”

“If that happens, we just run. But it won’t happen.”

Nic had taken me to an enormous field, very beautiful, just like a field out of a dream. It took us fifteen minutes to get there, it was outside the campground, a private farm, I guess, but we were near the campground yet and very few people went there. We were sitting, hugging each other, near a little lake, full of flowers around it. It was a high place, so we could see a big part of the farm, the plantations, the cattle, the house, a big house, some not-so-big trees and the grass, which was a little taller than normal.

“I wanted to spend our last few hours here with you...”

Nic kissed me. I felt like in those movies when someone dies and goes to heaven, to a wonderful, quiet and peaceful place...

“Nic, about last night... I'm sorry...”

“Forget about it. It was nice, anyway.”

I smiled, and laid my head down on his shoulders. He threw some stones in the water, and I looked at the little waves that were formed.

Our silence made that moment magical. The birds’ singing, the light breeze, all the shapes of the white clouds, the movement of the employees in the farm, so small, far from us, just like little toys, an angry voice right behind us...

“What do you think you’re doing here?”

We got up and looked behind, scared. A man with an old smock and a hat was looking at us, upset:

“Don’t you know this is private property?”

Nic and I looked at each other. The man was probably an employee on the farm.

“Run!”

Nic grabbed my hand suddenly, and we ran off there. We passed through the campground fence at the same point we did to go to the farm, a broken point. We didn’t even look behind to see if the man had followed us, but we ran, ran, until we reached the back of the campground forest. We stopped at the clearing, the same one from the day Nic got sick. I was exhausted. We were laughing.

“Nic, you’re so crazy! You told me no one would be there!”

“I’ve only been there once! And just for a few minutes!”

“I never thought I could run like I did... among the trees!”

Nic let himself drop to the ground.

“Come on, sit here”, and then “so, did you like the place?”

“I loved it. That nice farm man could have left us there a little more... we didn’t mess with the flowers, didn’t steal the cattle, didn’t do anything wrong!”

“Forget it, Paula! It was fun!”

“Yes, it was... Nic, did you bring your watch?”

“Uhn... No...”

“We left at noon, stayed there for a long time, and the buses leave at six! I don’t even have one T-shirt in my suitcase yet!”

“Let’s go back, then.”

I knew the way back pretty well. When we reached the lake, at the end of the forest, I discretely smiled.

Nic asked a boy for the time. Two-thirty in the afternoon.

“My God, look at what you do! Over two hours passed and I didn’t even feel it!”

“I did that, Nic? I don’t think so. If it wasn’t for that ugly man, we’d have to catch the next bus, next year!”

“You’re right... Let’s go back to say thanks.”

“Oh, Nic... Sometimes you are so silly... so cute...”

We arrived at the chalet. Everyone was there.

“Finished packing?”, I asked.

“Yes, only your things are waiting for you, ma’am...”

“Man, what’s the problem? What have I done?”

“Nothing, you just put bugs and animals inside our suitcases, put hair gel into the jam pot...

“Anyone eat it?”

“Almost. Right, Valéria?”

“...you hid the toilet paper, put toothpaste inside the cabinet’s handle, changed the way the freezer door opened...”

“Poor Adriana was having a fit, trying to open it, when she found the condom! Then she realized it might be one of your jokes...”, Valéria interrupted Luísa, they both smiled, like they had done all the jokes themselves.

“...and some other things. What’s the point, that’s so childish!”

“We wanted to do it, that’s it! And I can say from your tone of voice that you did something to me too...”, I entered the chalet, suspicious.

“We’re going to the snack bar!”, they yelled.

An hour later, I joined them.

“Hey, what happened? We thought you were dead in that chalet!”

“I was just packing”, I answered, in a perfectly normal voice. “I'm going to get something to eat, I’ll be back soon.”

“What did they do?”

“Nic, you scared me! Are you some killer, coming from behind? Well”, I answered his question, getting the sandwich I had ordered. “They turned all my clothes inside out, and locked my suitcase zipper, but I put everything in order, pretend nothing was wrong, okay?”

“They’re evil... why do so much work? Well, are you planning to do something by pretending it’s alright, or are you just embarrassed?”

“Neither. I'm just tired of it. I'm really starting to think we’re acting like kids.”

“Oh no, it’s just having fun. My friends and I always do those things on our last day somewhere.”

“What an interesting tradition...”

* * *

“Goodbye, guys...”

“Bye! We’re gonna miss you all!”

“We’re going to write!”

Our last moments together. Everyone saying goodbye, getting on the buses, which started to leave, through every possible way.

“I wish I had my car now...”

“Oh, Guilherme, don’t be selfish... Buses are a lot more fun!”

Adriana and Alice made a sign to Luísa, asking about me. She answered with ‘I don’t know what happened’ with a gesture.

“Anything wrong with your luggage, Paula?”

“No, everything’s alright... why, should anything be wrong?”

“Forget about it...”

“Well, our bus is leaving right now”, Guilherme said, starting the last goodbye sayings.

Valéria kissed Cláudio quickly, we all hugged and got on the bus. I only waved goobye to Adriana, nothing more, just like Nic did.

The bus left. It crossed a small dirt road, until it reached the main road. Another bus followed us.

In a few hours, we’d be home again.
