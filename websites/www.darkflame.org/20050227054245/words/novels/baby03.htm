PART 1 - MEMORIES
CHAPTER 3 - MEETING THE FRIENDS AGAIN... THE FRIENDS?

"Hey, wait, what are you doing? Let me go!"

"One... two... three... now!"

Michael had just arrived at his father's house and was promptly thrown at the pool by his brothers, still in his clothes and everything. The "party" started, and they actually did some dangerous things, but they were all used to it, including Randy. He had the opinion that it was just a teen way to have fun. Clara would worry if she was there, watching them.

"Okay, boys, now let's eat and..." Randy had barely put the snacks on the plastic table near the pool. Before he could end the phrase, Stuart got pushed and hit his father, who hit the table and all the snacks went to the water.

"I'm sorry, dad!"

"Stu, are you a cockeyed, can't you see things in front of you?", Lucas threw a piece of bread at Stuart, who threw a hamburger back at him. Some minutes after the food war started, the pool was mucky.

"Man, look at what we have done", they looked at the water around them and laughed. Randy had watched everything, sitting on a chair far from them.

"Let's turn the filter on!"

"Are you crazy?", Randy got up. "All this stuff will obstruct the filter. Clean all this with your own hands, and later you can filter the water."

"Okay... Have you already noticed how disgusting wet bread can be?", the three of them started to clean the water.
When they were done, Lucas and Michael went to their room to sleep, and Stuart went to the club near Randy's house, the place he knew he could find his american friends at that time of the day.

"Stu!", from inside a small cement field, a guy yelled in the middle of a soccer game.

"Rich!"

The first friend met in Boston was Richard, an eighteen years old boy who was always training at the club. He interrupted the game to say hello to Stuart, speaking always in english:

"It's been a long time! Man, look, they changed all the club, it's bigger, with a bigger pool..."

"I need to stay in Brazil during the school time, right?"

"Of course, absolutely... Well, you already know John and Brian, this is Kevin, this is Josh, and you can see Mary and Angela at the bleachers."

Stuart waved. When the girls saw him, they ran to meet him and gave him hugs.

"Stu, it's been so long!"

"We missed you..."

"I missed you too."

"We miss you and your brothers a lot when you're not here. Speaking about them... where are they?"

"They're home, sleeping with the angels…"

"So, tell us the news!"

The eight of them walked to the big snack bar, beside the cloakrooms, where the boys entered to clean themselves after the game. They left Stuart, Mary and Angela waiting outside for some moments.

"So how long will you still live in Brazil?"

"I don't know. I have no plans to move here with my father, Lucas is already in college and won't quit because of a moving thing. But I'm seriously thinking about attending college here, to change things a little, you know?"

* * *

"Stu! I didn't know about your college plans!"

"No? I thought I had told you…"

"No, you hadn't… are you serious, do you really want to study out there? And leave us?"

"Don't worry, Mike… We'll always be brothers!"

"That's not what I meant… Forget it, you're on a trip…"

"No, unfortunately, I'm not!", they half-smiled, without really enjoying the comment.

* * *

They were all together again, and the conversation was cheerful. Some hamburgers and sodas later, Stuart said he wanted to see the new things on the club. That was, definitely, one of the three brothers' favourite places, where there were most of their friends from Boston.

"There are some basketball fields in there, and now there's also a baseball field", Richard pointed, while speaking. "Those closed places over there are still under construction, it seems they're constructing a playgroung and some other stuff for the kids. The baby nursery will be transferred to there, and I guess that will be an only-children place. That'd be nice. There is a running runway over there" and he pointed the other side of the club, "a salon that's rented for parties, and on the back of the club there is a convenience store, you can access it from the street too."

That place was really huge. You couldn't see its end. It was an incredible place, especially when seen through aerial pictures. It was a big club, well cared, and attended by people of all ages.

"What's that?", Stuart pointed to a crowd, from where some music came.

"There is a little stage there, and some rock bands play here from time to time. This one is a cover of some old rock band."

"Let's see, they sound good."

"I'm sorry to disappoint you, but John, Brian and I can't give you the honor of our presences… We have to go."

"Okay, Kev… Don't worry about us…"

Richard, Josh, Stuart and the girls went to watch the band. It was a simple stage, without lights and any other special effects, it was low and not very big, and it had the club's logo in it. But people seemed to like that sound, made by six young musicians.

"Hum, there it is. Skill, it's the name of the band."

"Cool… They are good."

"Hey, Stu, don't you want to go to that side, it's less crowded…", Angela asked, with am uncommon sweet voice.
Stuart looked at his friends. Nobody said anything. It was better not to disappoint the girl.

* * *

"Hello, guys, I'm back…"

"Come in, Lucas. Stu is almost there…"

"Cool! He looks silly in that part!"

"You're a silly, you gay!"

"Shut up, Lucas… he's sensitive today."

"Look, stupid, can you just close your big mouth? If you don't want to listen, at least pretend you're hearing!"

"I am, loggerhead…"

"Okay, we laughed, we said what we really think of each other, now let's continue, Stu."

* * *

"Angela, isn't it better to stay here? We'll lose the others!"
After walking for some moments among the crowd, Stuart decided to stop.
"Okay. It's better in here."
"Why did you bring me here?", he tried to look ingenuous.
"To talk to you, just that, it's been so long since… Stu… What are you looking at?"
"I… I think I saw someone I know…"
"Who?"
He kept looking, the eyes trying to find that face again.
"Angela, can you give me a second?", without deviating his eyes from a certain place, Stuart left Angela and went to the direction where he had seen that face. He saw his friends, at the same time he saw the person he was looking for, some meters from him:

"Stu, what happened? Where's Angela?"

"She's still there… Does any of you know that girl?"

"What girl?"

"That blonde one, wearing a bikini, talking to the short girl…"

"Okay, okay, we saw her. I think I've never seen her… Who is she?"

"I think I know who she is. I just wanted to be sure…"

"Go ask her."

Stuart hesitated. He looked at her again. Yes, it was her.

"If you want to talk to her, go ahead, because Angela is coming. I don't know if you noticed, but she has a crush on you. She've had it for months."

"Hein?!"

That sentence brought Stuart back to Earth. Back to that crowd. He wasn't expecting for that. Angela?! Now, what would he do to tell her she was nothing but a good friend to him?

'If I don't go talk to her now, I can never see her again. But if I do, and Angela sees it, she'll be so upset…"

But Stuart knew that he had other good friends, who distracted Angela while he talked to that girl.

"Hey, excuse me…"

She turned to her back, looked at him for a second and recognized him:

"I have already seen you somewhere…"

Stuart smiled, inside and outside. That friendly voice made him feel comfortable, forget Angela and all the rest.

"Enny!"
