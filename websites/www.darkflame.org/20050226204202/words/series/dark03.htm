CHAPTER 3 - END OF YEAR

It was the 25th of December. Joana's big family would celebrate Christmas with a barbecue, at the house of one of her brothers.

At seven in the morning everyone was up already. Joana was defrosting some meat, Luísa was helping Nic to wrap some gifts and Augusto was trying to figure out, among the 400 Christmas tree lights, which was the one that was not working. At nine o'clock, just like they had previously agreed, Augusto went to his brother-in-law's house with his kids, and waited there for Joana, who still had some things to do before leaving.

"I don't know why she didn't do it before...", Luísa complained.

"Take it easy. She is probably taking care of the food. It’s worth the wait."

Joana soon arrived, with the food that they were waiting for. At three in the afternoon everyone had had lunch. The men were talking in the living room, the women were talking somewhere else, the kids were playing everywhere with their Christmas gifts, and Nic and Luísa went to the streets to take a walk with the rest of the older cousins. They weren’t used to hanging out together, except on special occasions, but they were all good friends anyway.

* * *

They talked, in a square, for hours. The sky started to cloud up, and they decided to go home. When they arrived, it started raining. Some meat was being baked again and people were eating, without waiting for them. The get-together lasted until the night, when most of the people went home, including Luísa and her family.

* * *

After the first week of January went by, Luísa's father went back to work and she felt obligated to ask him about her future school, because he hadn't mentioned it yet. But she didn't even have to ask. One night, during dinner, with all the family at the table, he announced:

"I have decided where Luísa is going to study this year. She'll attend eight grade again, at night, in São Lucas."

"Really, Dad? Thanks! Thanks, Mom!"

"Congratulations, Luzinha, you lost the battle, but won the war!", Nic said, hitting her on her back.

"Don't call me Luzinha! Am I speaking Greek or something?"

"Okay. I give up. For now."

"We'll see."

After the dinner, Augusto went to the living room to watch TV, Joana left for the kitchen. Nic knelt down in front of Luísa and, holding her hand, he spoke, looking at the astonished face she had:

"I am so sorry for having offended you, milady. Because of that, but also because milady has achieved a great victory in the war, I want to have the honor of taking milady to the movies this night. Milady Luísa Porter is Sir Nicolas Porter's special guest."

She laughed.

"Um, that's right. Let's do it. I’m broke, so I will take your invitation."

"You selfish...", he got up and turned back to her, forgetting about the staging.

"You invited me!", she laughed.

"Okay", he looked at her again, standing up, in an polite position. "My carriage will catch milady in your room at eight o'clock."

Joana, who was coming back from the kitchen, heard Nic's last words, and laughed, just like Luísa. Nic ended up laughing too.

"My son is an actor!", Joana said.

"So why are you laughing instead of applauding?", he asked.

"Ok, superstar!", Luísa clapped once or twice.

* * *

A candidate to acting, but so unpunctual. Nic and Luísa arrived at the theater over ten minutes late. Luísa didn't say anything, because she was just a guest. But the part of the movie they missed didn't make any difference in the end. They left walking slowly, talking and laughing, when it ended. Outside, in the street, Nic said to Luísa:

"Hey, isn't that guy...?"

Luísa looked where Nic was pointing, and saw Douglas. More than that, she saw a scene that made her feel like laughing. Juliana was sitting on the sidewalk, crying, throwing all the mean names she knew at Douglas face.

"It's just a pity that that idiot always ends up with a good deal..."

And she was right, because Douglas left with another girl, leaving Juliana alone.

Nic and his sister walked through her. Luísa gave her an evil smile.

"What's your problem? Why are you looking at me like that?", she spoke, with a red and wet face, and got up. "What's the matter now?"

"I think this is not a good time to talk to me like that, you fool..."

"Yes", Nic said. "Look, girl, I never liked you before, and I think Douglas did so well on leaving you to be with that girl, because she's prettier than you. I believe that's the only reason, though, because she must be as superficial as you are. Don't come threatening Luísa."

"What's that? What do you have to do with my life? What are you going to do if I threaten your little sister, Nicolas? Are you going to hit me?"

"If you really want to know, go ahead", he held Juliana's collar, and then let her go. She looked at them, angry, and left.

"Would you really hit her?", Luísa asked, when Juliana was far enough not to hear them talking.

"Man, of course I wouldn't! I'd never hit a girl. Even if it's someone like her. But there is something really true here: she's such a chicken, I can't believe it!"

Luísa laughed, agreeing, and thanked her brother with a handshake, and they went back home.
