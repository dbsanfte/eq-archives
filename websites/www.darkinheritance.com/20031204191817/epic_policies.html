Epic Quest Policies

Epic Tracker Link

Dark Inheritance is a small guild. Epic quests are not the reason for our existence. However, we want to assist our members earning their epics when the desire is there.

Upon reaching level 50, any full (non probationary) member of Dark Inheritance may submit their name to me for inclusion here. If you do not, then we cannot track your epic or help you complete it. Nor will we try to. We cannot do epics FOR you, but we can help you do yours. Epics require that YOU, the person questing the epic, do most of the work, not your guild.

Do your part! Do the solo camps and single group portions of your epic on your own and with close friends, as much as possible. As a guild, our nights where we can field full strength are limited.

Dark Inheritance makes no promises that you will obtain your epic. Nor do we feel we need to help anyone acheive their epic that does not wish to return the favor when others need help. Epics are a team effort.

Members must also realise that they may need to join open raids or join raids of other guilds, or solicit outside help in order to complete certain portions of their epic. Many epic targets are simply beyond the combined strength of our guild to overcome at this time.

We hope that all members that are truly determined to get their epic, continue to level. If you are not leveling up past 50 you will not be effective help in obtaining your own epic or that of others. All epics require a minimal amount of level 60s to complete. Having said that, a small group of level 60s can accomplish ALOT. The reality of a small guild is that if we work on epics together, we shall have to level to overcome the lack of numbers. This is the simple math of Epics.

We encourage all members to be patient. If you play part time it can take over a year to do some of the more difficult epics. Being impatient, or sullen about this fact will not change it. As I do, I encourage you all to do the portions you can, as you are able to and when the opportunity arrives to do them. You never know what the future may hold for both our guild and you individually.

To prepare for doing your epic I suggest the following:

  • Continue to level - do not ding 50 then stop leveling because you are epic level. At level 50 you are not sufficiently high enough in level to be effective help in completing your own quest, let alone that of others. Continue to invest time into your character. Epics are a HUGE time commitment and leveling is part of that. Especially in a small guild
  • Collect Resist Gear - Do not rush out and buy a tonne of resist gear. But collect a set, little by little. You should be able to keep your magic resistance above 100 and any other additional resist above 100. For example a raid may call for cold and magic resist gear, or fire and magic resist. It is almost always magic plus one other resist. Resist gear makes an enormous difference against certain epic mobs
  • Get an Enduring Breath Item - Farm a fishbone earring, buy a gnomish tinkered rebreather, etc.... an Enduring breath item is something you will use over and over agian in the high end game
  • Do all the portions of your epic that you can do alone or with a few friends. Most of your epic work should be done by you and not your guild
  • Be ready to help others doing smaller portions of their epics. Your guild mates are prepared to invest time and likely some experience losing deaths in seeing you get your epic. Be sure you are willing to do the same for them before you start your own quest.
  • Be prepared to be patient. Be ready for some raids to fail. Be ready for nights when your item just does not drop. Epics are VERY HARD. They are meant to be hard. Accept that some parts of your Epic will take multiple attempts and be prepared for it.

REMEMBER:

Keeping the information up to date on the epic tracker is your responsibility. If you do not keep your information up to date we cannot help you do your epic.
