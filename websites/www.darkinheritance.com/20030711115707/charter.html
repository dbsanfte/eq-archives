Charter Of Dark Inheritance

Section 1 - Out of Character rules

Note: Each of the three Houses may have additional rules but none will supercede these general rules

 

Not knowing these rules is not an excuse, all members MUST read and know the guild charter

 

Mutual Respect - Members must, at all times, treat each other, and other players on the server, with maturity and respect. You must be a legal adult in real life to join this guild and you will be expected to act like an adult at all times.


Tasteful Roleplay Character Names - While we understand that Verant has loosened its naming policy, Dark Inheritance will not allow any character to join the guild who's name is out of sync with a roleplay environment. Most names that form sentences, sound like titles, or contain words like Pimp, Shiz, etc., will assuredly cause your guild application for those characters to be refused. In short, your name should sound like a name and not a moniker, if you wish to seriously join and roleplay with our guild. Some exceptions may be made but are at our sole discretion and all 3 House Leaders must agree to allow the exception to occur, with the Guild Leader retaining the right to veto. This also applies to any combination of first name and surname that violates the guild name policy.


Respect the Guild Tag - Don't initiate or participate in behavior such as camp stealing, or kill stealing, ninja looting, etc., that degrades the enjoyment of the game for others.


No Profanity - Do not embarrass our guild by using profanity in any public channel, this includes shouts, OOC and especially, guild chat. We don't want it.


Respect the Roleplayers - This guild is not roleplay mandatory BUT it is to always remain roleplay friendly and roleplay encouraged. Members who disrespect members for roleplaying their characters will be tossed out.


Talk to the Character not the Person - If someone is roleplaying treat them as though they are the character they are roleplaying and not Joe Smith from New York. Just as we don't force you to roleplay, do not force those that are roleplaying to break character. If you need to speak with them OOC simply send them a tell saying OOC.

Designate your OOC text in Guild Chat - As of June 1, 2003, all Out of Character discussion in the guild channel must be designated as such. Acceptable methods are to prephase the chat with the term OOC such as "OOC - Work sucked today but then again work sucks everyday" or to place the OOC text in brackets such as "((Work sucked today))" Other commonly used methods to identify OOC chatter are also acceptable.

Section 2 - Guild Hunts and Raids

Competence - In order to attend raids we expect you to be competent as a player of your chosen class. We expect enchanters to be good at mob control, warriors to be good at main assist roles, etc., know your job on a raid. We also expect you to follow all instructions, assist correctly, and be a team player.

Official Raid Strategy - Know the official guild raid strategy, and know it well. Attending a raid and not knowing the strategy will get you noticed, and not in a good way. Learn our guild raid strategy and you will find things run like clockwork and the entire raid experience is a lot more fun. And by the way, we don't care if you have pulled in North TOV a million times or run 200,000 vox raids, you have to know and abide by our strategy on our raids.

Loot Distribution - Know the official loot rules of the guild. Not knowing will not be an excuse. Ninja looters both in exp groups and in raids, will be kicked out of our guild.

Raid Chat Channel will be OOC - The raid chat channel will be in OOC, the raid leaders and pullers and main assists simply cannot stay in character during some busy events. So, all raid channel communications will be OOC.

Section 3 - Roleplaying Guidelines

While we realize everyone roleplays differently we have some guidelines that all members should strive to follow so that all members can roleplay on the same piece of solid ground.


The Guild Plot is Immutable - You may not in any roleplay, or writing, alter the information in the official guild plot. It will not ever change to suit your own story, your story will have to work around it. The guild plot is deliberately left sparse so there is alot of room for personal and House creativity.


Remember there is a Person behind the Personae! - Never forget that there is a person behind the persona! If you have had a particularly heated roleplay discussion with someone please send them an OOC tell to make sure you have not hurt their feelings. Its not a sign of weak roleplay to do so, but a sign of mature roleplay.


Remember we are all friends OOC - We may roleplay as rivals and enemies but the people behind the characters remain friends and are willing to help each other as needed. We are all united by a guild tag and are all roleplayers on a server that is not very roleplay conducive at the best of times. When doing raids, or exp groups, make room for your roleplay adversaries for they provide you, and you them, with a richer game experience.

Section 4 - This Document Supercedes all House Charters

Binding Document - While Houses may create Charters that reflect their roleplay ideal for membership, no House may pass rules that contradict, or supercede the rules in this Guild Charter.


Section 5 - Evolving Document

This charter is in its earliest form and may change frequently for the next while as needed by Council. Please keep up to date with any changes.
