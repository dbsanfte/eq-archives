Loot Distribution Rules of Dark Inheritance

NOT KNOWING THE RULES IS NOT AN EXCUSE


Removal of Loot Rights:

If any raid participant continually fails to follow raid leader instructions, the raid leader will remove them from the loot lists, and/or the raid. Know the Raid Bible, learn it, live it. It may not be the word of God, but its a close second on our raids.

Other Systems And Why We Don't Use Them:

I have given this much thought. After over 2 years in guild admin I have been involved in many loot systems.

“Can and Will Use” is a very well intentioned system that is always abused by someone. In every single raid I have been in there is always someone who seems to think this means “Can Use, its my divine right to roll on it even if I’m going to sell it”

“Can and Will Use” requires judgement calls, many of them, by the Master Looter. And can result in grumbling by raid attendees who do not agree with the decisions.

“Points Systems” are very good in that they reward, in the long run, those who put in the most effort by ensuring they get the most drops. The drawback is that part time members feel slighted, and the points are a management nightmare to track.

To me, the ideal loot system requires no judgement calls by the guild management, period. Instead it relies on peer pressure in a very real way, to ensure members take the morale high ground when they loot or roll on loot.

Reserving Drops:

Dark Inheritance reserves the right to reserve a drop per raid for its members. Some raids, where multiple epic efforts are being run, D.I. may reserve more drops.

Dark Inheritance will only reserve drops when the purpose of a given raid is to obtain those drops for a given member who is trying to complete a quest.

Distribution of No-drop loot:

No-drop loot must be distributed in a fair and rapid manner as a long discussion can result in the corpse rotting and the loot lost.

No-drop loot will be distributed in the following manner by the designated raid Master Looter.

  • Master Looter will hotkey the following /RSAY or /Channel-name “A NODROP item has dropped – I will now post a link, PLEASE, ONLY roll for this item if you it is something you will use for a long time before replacing, or need for a quest you are currently working on. DO NOT ROLL YET”
  • Master looter will spam this message again after a few seconds
  • Master Looter will post a link of the item
  • Master Looter will spam the raid 3x with “/Random 100 NOW if you are rolling on this item”
  • Master Looter will determine winner by highest roll and confirm that the random was not cheated by the use of emotes.
  • Master Looter will tell the winner to loot the item using the raid channel.
  • DO NOT LOOT UNTIL TOLD TO BY THE MASTER LOOTER – This is important!

Droppable or Tradable Loot Distribution

All droppable loot will be collected by the Master Looter for distribution at the end of the raid.

There is no requirement to include anyone in loot distribution who does not stay until the end of the raid, regardless of circumstance.

There is NO opting out of loot rolls for tradable loot under this system.

The Raid Leader will use the raid window and do a /random (totat raiders) for each item on the raid that was looted

The Raid Leader will use the result of the roll and count down the raid window list to determine the winner of that item. If a zero was rolled, the random will occur again to get a valid result.

So you win the roll, now what?

    • If you win the roll and you can and will use that item, on the character you attended with the raid with, then use it in good health with the blessing of your guild mates =)
    • If you win an item you will not be using on the character you attended the raid with, then you have decision to make.
    • You can give the item to someone who attended the raid with you that can actually use the item. You can repay those who have helped you in the past by remembering them now. Who gets the item is up to you. This is definitely the morale high ground.
    • You can keep the item to sell or twink an alt that did not attend the raid. It is your item, and this is your right. And if you want to be greedy and look out only for yourself then that too is your right. Just don’t be surprised when someone wins something you really need, and they award it to someone else.
    • If nobody on the raid really needs the item then sell it or twink with it with a clear mind =)
    • If someone tries you award you an item and you really don’t need it, then refuse it, give it to someone else on the raid, or tell the person who won it in the first place to keep it for sale. If someone awards you an item they are going to expect you to use it and expect to see you using it!!!! If you accept it to sell you will be remembered. If Serrik or another officer catches you accepting items then selling them, they will likely boot you from the guild. This is greedy behaviour and we do not want greedy people in our guild. You have been warned.

This system works on peer pressure being applied to people to be generous and kind to their fellow guild mates. Guilds only work if people look out for each other. So look out for each other.

This system not only serves the purpose of ensuring the loot goes to those who will need it, but gives people a reason to keep their reputation intact as your peers will be deciding if you get loot. It also serves to reveal the greedy people. And that is always a good thing.

Role-playing your Loot Decisions is NOT Allowed:

We are a roleplay-based guild. Role-playing your character has nothing to do with your loot decisions, period. And it is never an excuse for greed.

Your loot decisions ALWAYS reflect the person at the keyboard and not the character you are role-playing.

All Dark Inheritance raids are run out of character so that all three houses can take part. Make your decisions as a player, not a character, in regards to looting.
