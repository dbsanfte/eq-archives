The Dark Inheritance Guide to Raiding and Dungeon Crawling.

What we like to call our Raid Bible

Dark Inheritance is primarily a roleplaying guild. We are not about making Everquest a 2nd job, instead we find the journey to level 65 as rewarding as being 65. And, although we are not out to become all powerful, organized raids are a must.

An organized raid means the following things:

    • Raids that start on time - less boredom
    • Raids that have a much higher chance of success - more enjoyment
    • Less time spent recovering from wipe outs - less frustration
    • Achievement of objective more likely - sense of personal accomplishment

An organized raiding force:

    • Attracts quality people to future raids and to your guild
    • Learns to work together to handle surprises
    • Becomes a tighter group of friends

There is no part of this guide that is not important. Etiquette, etc... is all equally important to strategy in a raid environment.

What this guide entails

This is a plan for multi-group raids, and dungeon crawls. It allows many diverse groups to work through all threats in an organized manner and maximizes their chances of success.

The goal of this plan is nothing short of maximizing the destructive potential of a raid force by focusing all its damage dealing capabilities upon one solitary mob (critter we are hunting) while the crowd control teams keep the other mobs at bay.

Raid Etiquette (Or getting invited back)

I know it may seem like common sense, but raid etiquette is extremely important. This is especially true in a smaller guild like ours where you will want to attend open raids held by other guilds in the high level game.

People wont invite you back if you were a pain in the butt. Even open raids should be viewed as an 'invite only' raid. Many 'open' raids will not assign you a group if you have earned a reputation for greed, incompetence, or immaturity.

These are all suggestions on how to conduct yourself on a raid. Following them will pay off.

Attending a Raid - Your Guild or Another

    • Sign up for the raid if required (Let them know to expect you)
    • Learn and Read any strategy posted by the raid leader
    • Ask any questions about the raid in advance of the raid, do not ask them as you arrive at the raid the raid leaders are too busy
    • Arrive at the correct meeting place 15 minutes early if at all possible, avoid arriving late at all costs. (I do not wait for late comers on my raids I start them on time.)
    • Bring the right gear - IE resist gear to dragon raids, etc..
    • At raid start do the follow command line options "/showspelleffects OFF" "/assist OFF" "/serverfilter ON" - doing so will reduce your lag, zone lag, and reduce your chances of accidently breaking a mez and getting you yelled at
    • Find out who the main assist (MA) and second assist (SA) is and make a hotkey to "/assist (name)" for both

During a Raid

    • Never send the raid leader tells, instead wait for instructions in the raid channel or over shout, if you have questions ask your group leader
    • Send tells to the Raid Leaders and Master Looter ONLY when instructed to do so
    • Watch carefully for instructions in the raid channel. Missing an instruction to pull back, assist, etc... can wipe out your raid. Missing an instruction, even during combat, to submit yourself for a loot roll will cost you loot and that's just tough beans.
    • Keep the raid channel open for the leadership. Keep your socializing to tells and say and group or guild chat.
    • No matter how important an issue you have, if you spam it you will piss everyone off
    • Never, ever, ever, never wander from the designated pull spot, always stay with the raid force. Only pullers and scouts should EVER leave the main raid group.
    • Remember that monsters can actually *see* you from farther away when you sit then when you stand, and choose your medding spots with care.
    • Always watch for the shout to RE-ASSIST!!! The number one cause of wipe outs on raids is someone fails to re-assist and the enchanters can then not do their job.
    • Ignore the 2nd assist if the main assist is still alive and fighting. Sometimes you get a lousy 2nd assist who starts shouting for assists. 2nd assists should only be assisted IF the main assist is dead or link dead, or cannot reach the camp.
    • If you feel loot is not being distributed fairly, keep quiet and simply do not attend the next raid hosted by those people. No matter how justified you are complaining about loot will tag you as a greedy player. Its sad, but its true. Just move on and don't join them again
    • Be prepared to earn No EXP at all, or don't go
    • Be prepared to leave a raid with less exp then you started d with, or don't go
    • Be prepared to win zero loot on a raid, or don't go
    • Be willing to wait patiently on a rez if you die, see above about sending raid leader tells. If you die, sit, wait, the call will come "Anyone not rezzed?" and thats when you speak up. Your group leader will know you are dead and will make sure you get a rez most of the time.
    • Never, ever publicly criticize a raid leader on his raid, or after it. You make yourself look bad. Running a raid is an enormous amount of work and responsibility, especially for dragon or planar raids. If you feel the raid was run poorly, let the raid end on a happy note and speak to the raid leader a few days later. Approaching them with a simple "I'm curious as to why this was handled a certain way" will make you seem less criticizing and open a discussion. Telling them they sucked will get you a reputation you wont outlive. Even if they did suck.

People who always leave a raid early will get a reputation for it. If you are willing to earn loot you should be willing to stay to the end and help with any wipe outs, etc.....

    • Do not tell other people how to play their class, even if they are really lousy. If someone is endangering a raid with their incompetence, send your group leader a tell, and then forget about it. Good raid leaders will correct improper conduct. Just be sure you are doing your job and let the Leaders worry about everyone else.
    • THANK the raid every time you win an item!!!!!! EVERY TIME Yes and put a smile on that! Congratulate anyone who wins an item especially if you were in on the roll! Be a sport.

Raid Progression

Generally you will see the raid progress through the following steps from start to finish. Some steps are left out or included depending upon the destination.

    • Raid information posted on a message board, people sign up
    • Gather at designated time and place
    • Formation into break-in groups - you get assigned a group and a group number, make sure you know it
    • Buffing circle (everyone gets buffed to high heaven - mostly dragon raids for this)
    • Camp to chat (once buffed you camp to a designated chat room to save them until needed - mostly dragon raids for this)
    • Zoning into raid zone - often called break in or portal break, many deaths can occur on difficult portal breaks
    • Moving to a safe or pull spot
    • Puller seeks mobs and attempts to bring them to raid one by one or in small groups
    • Main assist targets one mob and shouts for the assist
    • You press your /assist (name) button and begin attacking the target, you do not EVER target with your mouse on a raid
    • You ignore all other mobs while the enchanters get busy mezzing them (Putting them to sleep)
    • The current target mob dies, main assist targets next mob, taunts it a few times and then shouts assist. DO NOT attack until you see the shout ordering you to assist
    • The above 2 steps repeat until all mobs are dead, then the puller goes out for the next mobs
    • When there are no more mobs close the raid is moved as a whole to the next area. If you get aggro while moving, call it out and bring it to the next camp. Do not engage it while traveling, and if you die, you die, and they will rez you after the next camp is secured.
    • This repeats all night until the zone is cleared or the designated target is reached.
    • Raid target obliterated

Raid ends

Its sad but its so true, independent thought gets you killed on a raid and you will probably take several others with you!

Be a robot, follow orders, don't show initiative, don't try something new, don't don't don't do anything but what you are told to.

Sit where you are told to sit, attack only the one mob you are told to attack. Move where you are told to move. The raid leader is the brain, and you must be the body. Failing to do so will get you removed from the raid.

If you have a better way to do a particular raid, then run your own raid. When on another person's raid they are your Parents, your God, and your Grade School Principal all in one being!

Things that can happen during combat on a raid

You could be ordered to do the following

  • Re-Assist to a new target Sometimes a mob cannot be mezzed by the enchanters and when that happens the main assist will target that mob and call for a RE-ASSIST. All members of the raid must focus on this new mob and the enchanters will then put the mob you were fighting before to sleep. If just one person does not make the switch, the enchanter cannot mez the mob. The mob will then kill that person, then the enchanter, and then random players with a free hand until the unmezzable mob is dead and the main assist can target that mob again.
  • Pull back Once again if one person does not do this, its pointless. Often times you see raid leaders having to spam PULL BACK over and over and over and over and still people are not doing it!!!!!! When you are told to pull back ABSOLUTELY everyone must return to camp and not attack the mob til its back in melee range. If one person sits way out and didn't pull back, they will be preventing that mob from returning to the raid force. They will also die cause they are soloing a mob that can kill them in 5 hits. And they will also drag agro from all other critters roaming past who will then train at the raid once that player is dead. SO PULL BACK AT ONCE when you see the shout to.
  • LOOT!!!!! There is a shout that such-n-such an item has dropped and to send a tell to so-and-so to get in on the loot roll. If you miss the instructions and don't get loot, its your fault not the raid leaders.
  • AND REMEMBER - NEVER TARGET WITH YOUR MOUSE, ALWAYS USE ASSIST TO TARGET I just cannot stress that enough. The whole system fails if people don't assist.

Pets

Never summon a pet without first being told to do so

Always watch where your pet is at. Even though pets are not non agro, they can still be agro if they have a mob on their hate list and are roaming.

Be ready to kill your pets if the shout is given. I don't care what happened in the patch or what Verant and Sony tell you.

Pets sometimes STILL BREAK mez it just happens LESS. When you have 3 pets on a mob, and they are casting, one of them will be in the middle of a cast as mez lands. Its pure math. Kill your pet when you are told to.

If a raid is wiped because of lousy pet control the response is normally quite vocal. So remember, be a responsible pet owner. Keep Sparky on a tight leash.

Evacs and Gates

If you gate out on a raid you will make enemies I guarantee it. Never leave a raid without telling your group leader you need to go. Never leave a raid without permission. Avoid leaving raids early but if you must get permission first. Otherwise you will be assumed to have abandoned the raid and that is a very very very bad reputation to earn.

Never evac without the raid leader calling it! If you evac your group out on your own accord you are going to hear about it. From your group and your Raid Leader. Often if you evac there is no way to get you all back to the raid. Sometimes its preferable to let a raid wipe out rather then evac, so a camped cleric can rez you back.

If you hear the command to evac, and you can gate, gate!! The raid leader is trying to avoid a lengthy corpse run and the few bodies the better so if EVAC is called, tell your group EVAC Called, I am gating!! Unless you are the evacer of course then you had better take your group with ya

After the Raid

    • Let the raid end on a happy note and save any complaints or criticisms for a few days before you act on them. And if you do, do so with a calm head.
    • Stay until every single raid member is safely out or rezzed.
    • If you can port, offer to give people a lift after the raid.
    • If the raid leaders did a good job tell them so!
    • Post a thank you on the host guild's message board, especially if you won loot.
