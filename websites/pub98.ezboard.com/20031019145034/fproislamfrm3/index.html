Once again, the United States has come across race relations as the national discussion. Amazingly, this time there was no Rodney King or O.J. Simpson to catalyze this national discourse; instead, a Republican politician made the foolish mistake of insinuating that had an ex-segregationist been elected, America would have been the better off for it. After a few weeks, when the dust had cleared, the Bush Administration decided to really let black America how the Republican Party felt about minorities by needlessly weighing in on the University of Michigan’s admission criterion. The result? A national debate, once again, about race.

You would think that after 45 years of civil rights, black Americans would finally get a clue. After all, white America gave them the right to vote, the right to an education, the right to work for equal pay, and the right to buy liquor on any street corner in their community. White America publicly desegregated schools using the National Guard, while making sure that blacks would always remain in low-income inner cities, sentencing the property taxes and therefore the school operating capital to penury. White America gave equal representation to blacks in all walks of American life; now we get to watch black actors on television approximately 22% of the time, where they make us laugh, since all of them are comedians. And now we come to the last bastion of the racial divide. We look at the University of Michigan, and we wonder why we allow any institution the right to use race as a contributing factor in the admissions process. Surely this is wrong; it is the vestiges of a policy defined by an overzealous Left during a time that the Right was nowhere to be seen. There is something fundamentally wrong with that, at least according to the neoconservatives.

What makes this particular conversation so disgusting is the backdrop of the War on Terror. While neoconservatives tell us that using race as a determinant is fundamentally wrong and racist in principle, they continue their fundamentally racist policies of racial profiling and targeted deportations. They claim that the University of Michigan is insulting poor blacks by offering them some additional points in the weighed factors that determine admission eligibility, despite the fact that similar factors exist for poor whites living in rural Michigan. They do not let you know that the basis for these factors is the fact that educational institutions within poor and black communities do not produce the same type of candidates that their white counterparts do, due to types of teachers that accept jobs in inner-city schools, local property taxes, and incomes levels of local parents. Rather, neoconservatives suggest that they are the result of the real racists – the far left who crafted minority programs like affirmative action and racial representation. Never mind that George W. Bush got into Yale and Harvard based on similar factors, except these factors were who his father was and how much money they had.

For a moment, let us accept this totally flawed reasoning as truth. This will allow us to look at the pathological adherence to party philosophies that the neoconservatives abide by, even if the policies fly in the face of reason. If using race as a determinant for anything is racist in nature, then how is it that racial profiling is morally admissible as policy? If blacks that receive the lowest standard of education in our country cannot receive only slightly more preferential treatment during the admissions process, what makes it right for the government of the United States to use race as a determinant with respect to who will be targeted by the INS, the FBI, the CIA, or anyone else in the Justice Department? What gives authorities the right to racially profile people driving a car, flying on an airplane, or applying for a visa? Are we to believe that there some type of difference in these two types of racial discrimination?
