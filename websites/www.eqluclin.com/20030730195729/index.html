Magelo Update - Wednesday July 30, 2003
Posted by: Bicrius
For those who've been having troubles with Magelo lately, I found a detailed update on their front page. You can read the full thing at Magelo.com. But to save those who are lazy some effort, the important two snippets are below:

"...the web server and the mail server are up.The website is kinda uber slow at the moment because it is rebuilding its entire profile cache, once it's done it should perform very well."

"Again, sorry for this pretty long downtime, hopefully everything is fixed now and the service should be stable and responsive in a day or 2."

Thus, it sounds like they've got a little more work to do, and the system will be up and down a bit for another few days. I'm sure we'll be profile surfing again in no time though. ;)

The new EqLuclin forums are up!
Posted by: Bicrius
Our EZboard home only had 3 days of Gold Status left on it, and it appeared to have disappeared completely (it's back now apparently, maybe it was just coincidence). But advertising thier "FREE GOLD!" offer by having one of their admins posted about in our PRIVATE and PASSWORDED admin forum didn't fly over well with our mods. Also, after paying for gold status as long as we have been, we weren't going to ask the community to shell out another $400+ dollars to keep the gold status there, especially when EZboard's sdemello(sysop) user had been interfering in posts that didn't even appear to break EZboard's TOS. Being moved to different pub (server) recently that appeared to all to be MUCH slower was basically the final straw.

Without further delay, for those who are surfing around trying to find the new boards, you can find them here.

Everyone be sure to give Tymmon and Ealdayen a huge round of applause and thanks on this one. Ealdayen believes he can continue hosting the forums for free (just as he's been doing with EqLuclin.com all along), and Tymmon has been a machine getting the new forums ready so that we can get away from EZboards, having to pay for them and put up with various issues regarding them. A mass e-mail to all 900+ registered EqLuclin users is incoming in regards to all of this.

And thus we continue to adapt to adversity at the speed of light... Another chapter in the history of the Luclin community begins...
