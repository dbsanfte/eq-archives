A Request to All Shamans

Fellow Shamans,

Our new, revamped alchemy has been around for about six months now, and due to the hard, diligent work of many shamans, we are finally getting some good numbers on trivial levels put together. Yet, we know that some of these numbers may be off a few digits or more.

As each of us increases our skill, we attempt to discover the exact point where a potion goes trivial, but we cannot always be successful. Sometimes we have only been able to establish a range of "greater than" or "less than" numbers. Other times a report from a shaman has been relied upon, but not necessarily confirmed, creating the possibility of incorrect trivial numbers.

For this reason, and in order to get our alchemy information as accurate as other trade skills, we would like to ask all shamans, especially the younger ones who are just starting out, to fill in missing information, or correct mistakes, on the trivial chart as they advance their skill.

To help us out, print the latest trivial chart (the printable version might be easier) and as you practive your craft for skill increases, attempt to confirm or disaffirm the trivial numbers listed. When you are absolutely positive you have discovered a mistake or confirmed a correct trivial number, mark your trivial chart accordingly.

Note that in order to actually confirm the accuracy of a trivial number, you must:

1) make at least one of a given potion when it wasn't trivial,

2) attempt to make one more of the same potion (success or failure) that raised your skill by one,

3) and finally, make one more of the same potion that gives you the message "You can no longer advance in skill from making this item",

thereby indicated that the given potion did, in fact, become trivial at that number.

Then, post your finding(s) on The Cauldron, our message board under the Alchemy Updates forum.

Our thanks go out to each and every shaman who endeavors to complete the information about our unique craft.

- Belgar
