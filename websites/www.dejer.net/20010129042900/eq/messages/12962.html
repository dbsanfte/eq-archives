When you die, you lose a 'percentage' or rationed share of your current level, based on exp charts.

As our ranger chart will still reflect the 40% penalty, and we will be gaining exp with a 40% bonus (compared to today's numbers) to effectively remove this penalty this is the scenario.

You have 1,000 exp points.

When you die you lose 1% total exp.

before patch:

You lose 10 exp points. You can gain them back at the same old rate you did before with the 40% exp penalty.

Lets say it takes 10 kills to get it back.

after patch:

You lost 10 * 1.4 = 14 experience points, as you gain the exp back without the 40% exp penalty.

Takes the same 10 kills to get it back.

This is a no-gain situation for the death penalty vs. gaining it back. Which is the point.

We are gaining on advancement, but they did not want us to get an extra benefit of losing 10 exp upon death, and gaining 14 back as a result of the new removal of the penalty, essentially, wanting to keep the 1% exp death penalty intact.
