Introduction:

If you play Sony's very successfull game EverQuest, and you would like to see more information on your characters than the inventory screen provides, YALP may be just what your looking for. YALP will summarize the information contained in the log files that EverQuest generates as you play, showing you very detailed information on what it finds. There are many EverQuest log parsers available on the web, but YALP's rare mix of speed, detail, and features sets it apart from the competition.

Haven't you ever wondered about the amount of damage that somebody you were grouped with did, or who has the best critical hits? With YALP, you can answer questions like this because YALP provides a full account of damage from the perspective of any party involved in combat, not just from the perspective of your character. YALP also provides a complete list of all the fights you had, with detailed information for each fight.

YALP is still in development, and is by no means a totally mature product. I've got lots of ideas for new things to put into YALP, and I will post updates every so often to this page. In the meantime, download the latest version of YALP, and dont hesitate to send email to YALP@shaw.ca with any suggestions or comments you may have. Thank you for using YALP, and happy EQ'ing!

Features:

Here is a short list of some of YALP's many features.

  • It really is speedy.
  • It can handle HUGE log files.
  • It displays the parsed log file in colour.
  • It has an interactive search cabability.
  • It provides damage detail down to the per-hit level.
  • It shows a fully detailed list of each fight you had.
  • It will never change or delete your log files.

News:

May 25th 2002

Today I started a new EZBoard message board for people to ask questions and have some discussions about YALP. There is only the one forum as of yet, but I hope to make more very soon.

May 18th 2002

I did a quick change to take care of a problem introduced by a recent patch. It involved pricing code, and could cause YALP to hang. I think this will be a common problem, so I advise everybody to download YALP again and get this fix.

May 1st 2002

I managed to track down and squash a big bug that caused some people to experience a large number of "access violation" errors when parsing some files. Many thanks to Mirko Thöle in Germany for helping me fix this one.

Other than the major bug fix, and a few other tweaks, the only visible change is the addition of a breakdown of your offensive damage per second by attack type on the fight detail page. Currently, I'm spending most of my YALP time re-designing the fight list. There will be a big change to the fight list comming, as well as some new features - just no promises on how soon.
