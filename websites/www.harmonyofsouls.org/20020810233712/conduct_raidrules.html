Raid Guidelines

his guide to raid rules is intended for all HoS raids. Certain guild members may choose to use different rules. If so, they will state them in their initial post. If you plan to attend an HoS raid, you are expected to know and understand these rules ahead of time.

HoS operates on a 100% merit based system. If loot is going to be an issue at any time during the event, bring it up early with the Loot Master. The Raid Leaders have absolute say on loot distribution.

Key People on the Raid
It is imperative to know who the following people are, what their functions are, and how to interact with them appropriately:

Raid Leader (RL)
The RL is the responsible for the raid and has ultimate authority over the raid. It is the raid attendees' responsibility to listen to the RL and follow the RL's instructions. The RL has final say in all matters concerning the raid. The RL is the only person with the authority to request a cleric to camp.

Loot Master (LM)
The LM is responsible for distribution of raid loot. HoS issues loot by merit. The LM may or may not be the same person as the RL. All questions about loot and requests for item awards should be directed to the LM in the form of a direct tell.

Main Assist (MA)
The MA is the main tank. Unless directed otherwise by the RL, all raid members should assist the MA using an assist hot key with auto attack turned off. (The obvious exception pertains to individuals providing mezzes and other forms of crowd control.)

Secondary Assist (SA)
The SA is the secondary tank. Depending upon the tactics dictated by the RL, the SA may be the backup MA (in the case of the MA's death, LD, AFK, etc.) or the SA may be required to tank "add" mobs. All raid members should assist the MA unless directed otherwise by the RL.

Tertiary Assist (TA)
In some raids a TA is identified. The TA is tertiary tank, which is a logical extension of the SA above.

Group Leaders (GL)
The GL is responsible for his/her group. The raid leader should ensure that his/her group understands and follows the RL's directions. The GL is responsible for announcing group member deaths, LDs, and relevant AFKs in the raid channel. The GL is responsible for responding to the RL's requests for group status in the raid channel. The GL is responsible for looting his/her group's kills and reporting loot to the LM. The GL will only loot droppable loot.

Buffers
Buffers are identified in the raid channel. Individual buff request should be made by a direct tell to the buffer. Group buffs are made by the GL in to form of a direct tell to the buffer. Requesting a non-essential buff during a pull is considered bad form.

Puller(s)
The puller runs out and brings back the mobs. Do not follow the puller.

Channels and Raid Communication
With the introduction of raid channels, raid communication has become much easier. However, different channels are configured for different purposes. HoS uses the channels listed below. An explanation of channel usage accompanies each channel listing.

Raid Channel
The RL will establish a Raid Channel. All raid attendees must join this channel. The raid leader will use this channel to issue raid instructions. It is crucial for raid attendees to pay attention to the directions delivered in this channel. Chatter on the Raid Channel must be minimized. One more time, chatter on the raid channel must be minimized. Assists, pulls, mezzes, debuffs, snares, etc., should be called in the Raid Channel. Important raid related announcement should be made in the Raid Channel.

Specialized Channels
Depending on the raid tactics, specialized channels (e.g., a Healer Channel) may be configured at the discretion of the RL.

Group Channel
The Group Channel is used for group coordination. Mana and other status should be reported to the Group Leader using this channel. Raid attendees that need to go AFK should inform the GL using this channel. Heal messages should be issued in the Group Channel unless it is a cross-heal. Any questions concerning the raid should be asked in this channel first. There is a very good chance that a group member will know the answer.

Guild Channel
Chat that is non-essential to the raid may be conducted in the Guild Channel. Guild-specific issues or concerns should be directed to the Guild Channel.

Direct Tells
Buffers expect direct tells. The LM expects direct tells. A very urgent issue or emergency may be handled by a direct tell to the RL.

Other Channels
Raids should be fun. A couple of clever emotes can lift spirits after a nasty CR.

Engagement
A raid is assembled to take down mobs. That's why there's a raid, after all. Each raid employ's its own strategy, but common tactics are listed here.

Assist
Unless directed otherwise by the RL, assist the MA by using an assist hot key with auto attack turned off. Do not engage the mob before the MA calls for the assist. One more time, do not engage the mob before the MA calls for the assist. Yet once more, do not engage the mob before the MA calls for the assist. (Again, the obvious exception pertains to individuals providing mezzes and other forms of crowd control.)

Nukes
Do not nuke early. Nuking at 50% health is a reasonable rule of thumb unless otherwise directed by the RL. Be very care when using DoTs.

Pets
At the discretion of the RL, pets may or may not be permitted during a raid. No pet may be summoned unless authorized by the RL (with the obvious exception of wizard familiars). Any player using a pet must maintain control of the pet at all times. If a pet owner goes AFK, the pet must be destroyed.

Positioning
In general, unless directed otherwise by the RL, stand behind the MA while waiting for a pull; the only individuals that should be in front of the MA are the pullers and, possibly, individuals engaged in crowd control. When fighting, be aware of what is happening to the mob and how your actions may affect the mob's position. The MA or the RL may call for re-positioning the mob (pushing or centering the mob).

Stand up
At this level of play, stand up upon the pull call.

Looting
Only the GL may loot his/her group's kills. The GL may only loot droppable items.

Lag Mitigation
It is important to minimize lag. Lag causes fights to be lost. Lag causes wipes. Whereas computer configurations vary widely from individual to individual, the following are suggestions to reduce lag:

  • SoL models off
  • Showspelleffect off
  • Unnecessary channels off (auc, ooc, shout, etc.)
  • Severfilter on
  • 3d sky off
  • PC names off
  • Clip plane down

Common Courtesy
The following are common sense items that make a raid pleasurable for everyone.

Fun
Have fun. Enjoy the raid. Crack jokes in the appropriate channels during downtime. Make new friends. See new zones. Take down that big bad boss mob. Gratz on that uber loot!

Language
HoS does not tolerate profanity, racial slurs, or raid attendees engaging in personal attacks. Of course, gnome-bashing is fully encouraged. Bragging or complaining about experience gained during a raid is in very poor taste.

Promptness
Raids are listed with a specific time. This is the time that attendees are expected to be at the raid, not en route to the raid. The raid start time is not a good time to be asking in the Guild Channel for a port. However, due to real life responsibilities and time zone differences, it is understood that members may wish to join a raid in progress. This is permissible upon approval by the RL.

Patience
The Key People listed above usually receive a zillion tells. An immediate response to a question or buff request is sometime not possible. Be assured, it is nothing personal. Polite requests usually are answered expeditiously, but occasionally tells pile up or an emergency arises. During a Corpse Recover (CR), let the RL and people actively engaged in the CR perform their tasks with minimal interruption.

Listen
Listen to directions in the Raid Channel and follow them immediately. If something is not clear, it is much better to ask a question than to do something that wipes the raid.

Understanding
All raid attendees are human beings including the RL and LM. Not everything goes perfectly and mistakes do indeed occur. This is all par for the course. If all encounters were easy, EQ would be quite dull.
