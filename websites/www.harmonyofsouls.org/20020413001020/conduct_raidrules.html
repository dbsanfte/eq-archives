Raid Guidelines

his guide to raid rules is intended for most raids. Certain guild members may choose to use different rules. If so, they will state them in their initial post.

Koikeeper RiverRhymes

Attendees
There is a sign up sheet for every event on our board. If you want to be included in the event, you MUST sign up. You may send me an e-mail, or a tell in-game. But I may forget to add you. It is the attendees responsiblity to make sure they are on the list, not mine. Feel free to remind me- some people do, but the only way to guarantee a slot is to sign up on the board.

That being said, I will add people at the event itself. I give preference to Guild, then allies, then friends, then friends of friends, then strangers- in that order. I only do this when it is apparent people are not showing up. If enough add-ons are there and on-time, i may create another group.

If you did NOT sign up, AND you are late, it is very unlikely that you will get into a group. I'm sorry, but it is confusing enough organizing and moving 20+ people without worrying about fitting someone in. On the other hand, after 2 hours or so, people start to leave. You may find it easier to get into a group at that point.

 

Starting Time
We have a scheduled starting time. Please try to stick to it. If you are going to be late, send me a tell, ask what group you are in and make arrangements with that group leader. After that, it is up to the group leader to coordinate with you. Do not come into a dungeon late unless you can do so safely by yourself.

 

Looting Policy
Only the group leaders are to loot an item. Cash items may be looted by any person, but any item must be reported to the group leader. Group leaders are to turn these items in to the raid leader. NOONE is to loot a no drop item unless told to do by your group leader.

 

Loot Distribution
All loot is merited with the raid leader’s discretion. Loot disputes/arguments will not be tolerated.

 

Leadership Roles
Every raid has one raid leader. Each group has a group leader. There is a specific chain of command. ONLY group leaders are to speak with the raid leader. If you have an issue, give it to your group leader.

 

Assisting
At the beginning of the raid, the protocol for assistance is called. There are two types of assist; main assist and party assist. Either the raid leader or puller will OOC what type of combat is to be used at each battle. The raid leader will designate a main assist, and the group leader will designate a group assist. You are expected to use assist keys and NOT attack keys.

Example:
Koikeeper says out of character: "Incoming target, assist the Cosin!"
Koikeeper says out of character: "Multiple targets, assist your group's primary"

 

Experience
Do not come to the raid expecting experience. The raid leader will make an effort to set up a raid that is appropriate for your level, but once the raid begins, the raid leader will not be involved in experience issues. The group leaders are expected to coordinate kills and spawns to maximize the experience for all memembers.

 

Ending the Raid
The raid leader will announce the end of a raid. If it is a deep raid with significant dangers, all parties will be asked to leave (i.e. kedge, dragons, kaesora, etc).

If it is a low risk raid that has camps typical for the levels attending, you may stay- as long as it does not put any other person at risk. If you decide to stay, you understand that the resources of the raid may no longer be available once we leave (rezzes, ports, invis, etc.).

It is the raid leaders preorgative to use the resources of the raid. This may include asking druids and wizards to port out the tanks.

 

Do Your Jobs
Well This is where we shine. I get many tells and e-mails after a raid commenting on our organization, skills and understanding of our class. So when you come to a raid, you are in a group, but more than that you are a footman in the army. Monks pull, clerics rez, bards feed manna, tanks take beatings, druids buff and damage shield, etc....

You will probably be asked to do your class job more often during a raid than any other time. Be prepared to do it.

 

Having Fun
This is why we are here. This is why i lead these events. You may not get exp, you may not get loot, you may die and not get rezzed. Those are the risks you take when you attend. Come ready to deal with that and don't be upset when it happens. Chances are you will get exp, loot and rezzed. But if you forget about those issues, you may have a lot more fun.

 

*These guidelines may be modified by the raid leader at his/her Prerogative.
