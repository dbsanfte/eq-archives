Mistmoore Castle

 

What better place than here to go and slay dark elves to your hearts content? Maybe Neriak, but Mistmoore Castle offers better loot and not quite the risk involved in the dark elf homeland. Hunting is available for those of between the levels of 18-50. I would venture to guess this is one of the three zones I has spent the most time in; Split Paw (both before and after the take over) and Kithicor Forest being the others.

There are as many as 12 hunting spots that can be identified within Mistmoore. It is an outside area so all druid spells are at their peak here - especially the wind series and Harmony, which you will use if you have half a brain. I also personally believe there is an experience bonus here and most of my friends will agree. Not sure if it's a bonus due to the zone, due to the fact that you are typically slaying casters, or a perceived bonus because most nights we had a never ending flow of kills. Another nice feature of Mistmoore, for clerics and necros at least, is many of Mayong's minions spawn as undead during the night. So pull out those undead spells for those freaks. The downside of this is if you are trying to invis or camo through certain areas you may get by all but one, then you are in deep trouble as everyone will see you after the undead one blows your cover.

The problems with Mistmoore that I typically see are twofold. The zone huggers or runners, cowards, as I like to call them, make life rough for many people. The second issue is the upper level soloing crowd that is there to farm items from the zone and will train rare spawns out to the zone or get in over their head often and train the zone rather than die themselves. People like Neaoi on Fennin built great reputations for themselves for this sort of behavior, constantly training or kill stealing for his own personal gain. I have never understood what sort of idiot does this when it is much easier to gate out while in trouble rather than let your foolish mistakes kill others.

For the Mistmoore beginners there is the lake area. Right inside the zone and down the hill you have an abundance of bad guys available, dark elves and a few gargoyles. These are the same gargoyles you find in the Ocean of Tears. The trick to this area is to use Harmony and grab one of the corners to the left of the entrance and pull there. You do need to learn the pathing here, as with many places in Norrath it's as buggy as it was before release and this causes deaths almost as often as trains from the cowards. Right past the lake area is a tunnel that runs back to the graveyard area and depending on how many people are around you may be able to pull from there also. Make sure you have someone on snare duty at ALL times, this goes for anywhere in Mistmoore.

Past the tunnel area is the graveyard and secret room area. The graveyard has plenty of spawns to keep one good group busy all night, possible two depending on size, age, and makeup of groups. Within the graveyard is a shadowman. He is one of the good guys as far as shadows go. Killing him hurts Temple of Ro faction, as he is part of a necromancer quest there. You may also raise Temple of Ro faction by giving him the flower that spawns in the first crypt of the graveyard's mausoleum. Facing the mausoleum, the back left wall of the graveyard is a false wall. This is the passage to the secret room area. It consists of 3 rooms, all closed. To open the 3 rooms you must use the door that is straight ahead when you enter this area. It will open all 3 rooms at once and it may get ugly. The cleric ingot drops in here, or appears, for one of their quest armor pieces. Also in this room is the passage to the tower area you saw when you first entered Mistmoore and strolled down to the lake.

The first set of stairs is fairly empty, most days. The avenging caitliff walks this path and has some nice shoulders for the melee types as well as being the quest person for the maid and butler fangs to receive Electrum Bladed Wakizashi. Further up the stairs you will run into a few more of those vile smurfy type elves as well as some gargoyles. When you reach the top of the stairs you may continue right or left. Lots of rooms with different bad guys and this is also one of the paths to Lasna Cheeron, who sometimes has the diamondine earring.

Back down to the graveyard area and you can exit through the gate and go straight ahead into a passage that requires an immediate right hand turn and then a descent. This is the courtyard area and typically has 3-6 guards, sometimes with pets. This is a good spot for a group to hold, and have a little fun. To the left is a winding small trail that leads up to the castle. However, directly behind them you will see a passage up that leads to a few more elves and then some gargoyles that guard Enynti at the very back area. She is part of an enchanter quest for the mask from the Temple of Ro, however I have found that she doesn't drop the rune near like she used to in the good old days. ;-)

Making your way up the trail to the castle doors you will be greeted with a full entourage of Mayong's women. Typically they are not happy about visitors. The best tactic I was ever shown here was to camo past all of them and cast Harmony from the back portion of this area and then pull the front one down the trail to your group that has cleared the courtyard. Personal experience tells me that you need to cast Harmony on the ones at the walkway into the castle for best results first, then the ones on the wall, then go pull. However - WORD OF WARNING - there is a roamer that walks along the walkway in and out of the castle. If he sees you and is alerted he runs into the castle first and brings LOTS out after you. It is typically a lower level pledge or initiate and if he spots you its best to gate, run, or die. Do not cast on this guy. Let him walk away. You have been warned. Keep this in mind if you are starting the Harmony cast and see him walk out of the castle gate, interrupt your self, back up and pray.

Ok now inside. I do not suggest anyone pull from or fight in the first hallway. Take the right or left turns and go around to the main entrance area where you will see stairs to go up or an archway forward guarded by gargoyles. Against this wall, facing the archway, with your backs 100% against the wall is pretty safe for reinvis or camo if anyone needs it. This is also a halfway decent place to clear this front area. It can get a little rough at times, but an absolute blast. Also remember to snare. It's almost crucial to have an enchanter with you. I am pretty biased in the fact that I believe it's crucial to take Cailette with me anywhere so who knows how the rest of the world manages anyway, certainly not me. Too many casters around for a root type crowd control to be very effective.

Past the archway you have an opened music room area full of the poor gypsies Mayong has enthralled. These are not your average gypsies as they are also werewolves at night. Very, very rarely they have the claws and pelts Karg in Everfrost is looking for to make the gloves and cloaks, but they do have the talon that the South Karana centaurs would like for the crafted breastplate. Miss Davissi also makes her appearance in this room from time to time. Bards are very familiar with her and what she carries for them, the Mistmoore drum. She is an enchanter and a pretty good one. She has actually charmed me, taken me through the entire castle and tower and made me break up 3 groups before charm ever broke. It was the longest charm ride I have ever been on and the most destructive. So beware of that issue if you do take her on. Back at the front of this area you had alcoves both right and left again. The left one is the conference room; the right one is the piano room. I will come back to the conference room later.

The piano room has a few of those cute elves again as well as a door to the outside garden upstairs. This garden is filled with more of the same, but if you enter garden and turn right you can run over a bit and find the fountain. This is the home of the Damphrye, where the hooded black cloak comes from. Pretty much always camped and sometimes they are keeping the garden clean out of boredom.

Back into the castle and through the gypsy music room area is a door. This door opens into a small hallway, which widens right up ahead. As the passage widens there are 4 gargoyle guards in each corner. This area also has a lower level roamer, like the front gate area. Leave them alone if they don't see you. He or she has a habit of screaming and running into the conference room and bringing all his higher level friends. Inside this area is a door to the left that opens up into the conference room area. This room has 3-6 visitors, some with pets, and is where the Gypsy Ambassador is typically found. If you have the gargoyles cleared it is possible to camo, open door, target someone near door, back up, open door and cast harmony from outside this room. With practice you will figure out where is the safest spot to do this, but be prepared for a few failures. They are called learning experiences or some call them deaths. <snicker>

Continuing back down the hallway where the gargoyles you will round a corner and come to some doors on the right wall. This is the kitchen. The cooks here are TOUGH. Much tougher than many of the others in the castle. The kitchen has 3 possible spawns, 2 of them being cooks, and the other quite often having a pet. You are getting closer to one of the most heavily camped spots here so this area may be cleaned out, but don't count on it. Past the kitchen is another room on your right and a passage. The room is the home of the butler; the right passage is the path to the maid's quarters. They are rare spawns and this camp can get tedious, however it can be fun if you have a good puller. On slow nights our group was able to pull the maid/butler, kitchen, gargoyle hallway, conference room, and the gypsy's music room. Our largest pull here is probably about 12 at once between pulls and respawns, which doesn't begin to compare with some of our Kithicor nights, but was a lot of fun nonetheless. It was actually started by a doofus we added to our regular team who kept making trains because he kept casting camo while our bard was running mana song. So he continued to walk out of the room truly uncamod and ignored the fact we kept telling him that and he kept getting spotted. What a moron that guy was, but it was rather comical to see him say "brb" and within 30 seconds /shout Train to zone! One of those times I went grabbed his train and brought it back to alleviate our boredom. A good tight group can rock the castle, but it takes a puller with a clue, an enchanter that understand their class, and a team with balls of steel. I have been fortunate that I have had all of that for sometime. We typically did this sort of mass pull and long fights with Cailette (enchanter), Spliner (cleric), Wyrd (ranger), and myself. The other 2 slots were filled off and on by other friends of ours that knew what craziness we caused up here. I would never do this with a pickup group, ever. We never trained the zone and we used my Succor spells very rarely.

Ok, that covers the first floor of the castle. Now back to the main entranceway where the stairs when up to the second floor. Go up, through door and left and you will come to a couple of alcoves with various critters. I suggest leaving them alone if you can get by them. The next main room is the Princess and throne room. This is another fun place to be. Total in the room is about 10. If you are absolutely lucky you can harmony the back half and only fight the front, but don't count on luck. Your best bet is to bring lots of firepower, say your prayers, or call us. Cai, Spliner, and I did this as a threesome one-day. My pulse still races thinking about that one. Not only was it a nice experience ruin for 3 people, but it was a time of fear and that makes it all worthwhile. In the times we have killed the Princess she never had anything of worth. She used to drop a very nice mace that was pulled a long time ago or is so rare everyone believes its been pulled. Back behind the throne is another door and this will lead you to some bedrooms, hot tub, the Troll SK that has the hilt for Soulfire and more fun stuff. I spent a little time back there, but most of our time was spent in all the other places. One note of caution though, in the bedroom that is connected to the hot tub I was never able to separate the bedroom spawns from the hot tub spawns. Only 2 attempts so it is quite possible I just hadn't figured it out. We did not have any paladin friends we needed to do this for so I never did go back and try again.

If I missed anything you think should be disclosed please drop me a line. Yes, I know I left out some. That was intentional. I wanted give a decent guide to my favorite Castle, but I didn't want to ruin it. Hopefully, I didn't give too much away.
