Cai's Crafting

 

As you can likely guess, as soon as I passed the 8th test of knowledge in my craft, and had learned the beginnings of transmuting metals, I quickly apprenticed myself to a well-known jeweler. I would like to thank Liriela, the first Teir'Dal resident of Rivervale (Ligge took me home to meet the folks) for her kindness in talking the Teir'Dal scribes into letting her borrow the scroll for me to study. I stayed with my Master listening to his teachings and watching his techniques until I had passed the 21 tests he set for me. It was both a joyous day and a melancholy day when my Master told me that I now had all the knowledge he could impart to me. I was now on my own to learn the secrets held by each gem and each metal combination.

As, Ligge and Kjella can tell you, during those early days after leaving my Master, I scraped and saved every coin I came across. I spent lots of time in the taverns talking with travelers to see what sort of jewelry they would find of greatest use. It was my habit to gather up my coin until I had enough to pay the scribe keepers to let me study the next rank of spells I would need to continue study in my craft. Once I had gathered their fees, I would rush off to purchase silver bars. I would then proceed to the nearest lapidary. I went slowly learning the properties of each gem, which would take what sort of a setting, which stones had which innate magical qualities. I had to start with the least expensive stone until I had learned its secrets.

Once I had passed the 12th test of knowledge in my craft, a strange comet, or at least the gnomes in the Lesser Faydark told me it was a comet, passed over Norrath. It swept across the face of the lands, near to the surface. In it's passing, it changed the magical properties innate in all the gems found in the land. After hearing that the comet had changed the nature of the stones, I took all of my remaining funds and quickly tried crafting some items with the stones whose nature I had already mastered. Finally, many of the pieces of jewelry the tavern patrons had mused of having were possible!!!! And the birth of a merchant jeweler had happened. However, an unfortunate event happened at this point in my crafting career. While running through RunnyEye with a bag full of newly crafted jewelry, a goblin must have snuck behind me and emptied my pack. I loudly proclaimed the wares I had crafted for any who were interested in them in the Plains of the Karanas and found to my embarrassment that all my wares were gone. Flat broke =(

Well, after crying over my losses in the fine brew that Ligge gave me, I decided to focus on my studies to learn more of the process of transmuting metals. This decision turned out to be a good one, since it allowed me to mass up a small pile of coin to spend learning the secrets of the new metal, electrum, that I could transmute once I had passed the 16th test of knowledge. It took all of my small pile of coin to learn even the basics of the metal electrum. I must confess though that I did have an eye towards items that the tavern patrons had mentioned wistfully in my earlier days. There had been many a druid who sighed about bracelets that would capture an animal friend's attention long enough that they could cast a magical charm over them. Of course those of my own craft also sighed wistfully over such bracelets as well, but we also coveted bracelets rumored to add both our life essence and magic energy, while adding a small protective shield. And well, jade is just such a pretty stone, and we enchanters can be a rather vain crowd. Others who practiced the magical arts mentioned an interest in wearing jade as well. Various priestly types had heard rumor of magical earrings which would whisper words of wisdom in their ears. Monks had sighed over rings that would make them move with such agility that their foes would not know where they truly stood. So I spent every spare moment that we were not out adventuring and learning the lands working on my skills at forming the transmuted metals and gems into new creations. Then I would find a corner of a local tavern and display the goods I had crafted.

I was beginning to build a name for myself as a merchant jeweler by this time. I always kept a pack full of jewelry on me as I ran about the world adventuring. I would call out my wares for any nearby who had an interest to inquire. I found that I generally earned enough to keep crafting and increasing my skill by offering my wares for purchase. Now, the knowledge of transmuting gold was in sight. That would be my next goal, since there were even more baubles that travelers had mentioned on their wish list available for me.

As soon as possible I set myself to achieving the 24th test of knowledge in my craft. I memorized the scrolls explaining how gold could be transmuted, then ran to buy bars of gold and gems. Again I started with the least expensive stone that crafted a product that I'd heard others dreaming about in the taverns. Again, I carried a variety of pieces on me at all time. By this time I had definitely built myself a name as a jeweler. I was very fortunate in having a partner, who was able to travel easily, which gave me more exposure in more places as we went from place to place seeking adventure. By this time I was making a slight profit at my crafting, so I started saving for the rumored expense of gaining the skill to craft platinum jewelry. I'd heard tales of fortunes of 10,000 platinum pieces burned in not time crafting platinum.

I almost gave up crafting as I started closing in on the skill level needed to craft platinum. Those skill level to get into the range to try crafting platinum are long, expensive and time consuming. They burned through much of my savings. But I did finally get near the point I could start attempting crafting platinum pieces. I had long since passed my 34th test of knowledge. Platinum is an unusual metal. Although it may seem to be transmuted correctly when casting the spell on it, often times when combined with the various gems the metal and gem break into bits and are lost beyond repair. Even after gaining the ultimate in knowledge of crafting, these pieces often fail. I will discuss pricing, haggling and twinkie jewelers in another article.

In retrospect, in giving advice to an aspiring jeweler, I would suggest waiting a bit to begin crafting. If I had not been partnered with Ligge, many of my expenses would have been higher for many different things. Ligge kept us both fed with his skill in foraging nature's bounty. He was generous with me when we gather loot from our vanquished foes, always let me have items with magical properties we came across in our travels first. He also saved any and all gems he gathered a booty for me to practice with. So in terms of pure start up costs, I was fortunate in my youth. I would suggest any aspiring jeweler wait until they have earned their surname before starting to craft. The other thing I have noticed is that as more and more people pursue jewel crafting as a trade, fewer and fewer people have need of items. I again was fortunate in being able to turn my profits back into my craft. I am not certain that a new jeweler would have the same success I did, when training my skills.

A few more words of advice for any aspiring jewelers out there:

  • Your reputation is your trade.
  • Be fair, but firm.
  • Price where you are making a profit so that you may stay in business, but not so that you are taking advantage of your fellows. My prices currently reflect the average cost of making the item with 10 to 30 platinum profit margin built into it. I currently trade mostly in platinum jewelry, with special orders done for special friends.
  • Leave the lower end markets to those learning the trade. You struggled through it too.
  • When you are on a streak where nothing you try shapes itself as you would like, stop. Being stubborn can cost you the ability to stay in business as a jeweler.
  • Most importantly, become a jeweler because you love to see the beautiful pieces you can create. Jeweling is time consuming, a labor of love. It is not a path to quick riches. Only a select few will get rich jeweling, and there is little market for pure cash transactions. However there is some satisfaction in being able to "treat" your group to a trip to the Plane of Sky….. I keep dreaming of it.
  • Use any secret mojo you think helps you to successfully craft the pieces you are working on…. You never know, there might be something to it <wink>
