8/24 12:00pm - Well it looks like the accidental nerfing of Selo's Consonant Chain is now permanent:
Author Topic:   Abashi please confirm - did you really mean THIS about Bard Chains?
Abashi
Station Member		 posted 08-23-2000 12:50 PM           

>>>Prior to your patch of the level 54 AE Snare song, the lower level Chains song (level 23), for high level Bards, was able to stop a SINGLE damaged MOB dead in its tracks at 50% health. In order to do this, considering the high resist rate and the early break problem, Bards had to devote ALL of their casting to doing this one thing and this one thing alone.
>>>We know that green MOBs that run run at 50% health, and that blue MOBs that run run at 25%. After the change, the song does not stop MOBs dead in their tracks until 20%. This means that, prior to the level 54 patch, Bards could stop ALL runners. Post the level 54 patch, Bards can stop NO runners. We CAN'T STOP TRAINS now.

The change that was implemented about two months ago placed a cap on the maximum amount that a creature can be slowed prior to the addition of damage. At the time, the people making that change didn't think that it would affect any other spells or songs than the 54th level song. After it did, and due to the complaints here, we tested the song in depth, and determined that the way it was working before was far too powerful. The current functionality that you are describing is the correct functionality for the spell.

-Gordon

Another scam has been found. Someone has put up a web page offering a utility called the "EverQuest Connection Enhancer". 8/18 12:30pm - In the patch yesterday they changed how haste and slow spells stack.  It seems one side-effect is that if you are slowed you lose your haste buff and no new haste buff will take hold till the slow is gone:
Author Topic:   Monk neft in last patch (unintended side effect?)
Kobe
Station Member		 posted 08-18-2000 12:32 PM           

As of the last patch, monks using the Robe of the Whistling Fist can no longer activate the robe when a slowdown spell has been cast on them. This was probably a result of the slowdown spell-overwrite fix.

The net result is, not only are we slowed by the spell, but we are additionally slowed by not being able to supply our additional haste that we were using before. It is sort of a double slowdown. Not to mention that we also lose the STR and AC component of the song.

-Kobex Bloodfang
Kobe
Station Member		 posted 08-18-2000 12:34 PM           

I should clarify that using the robe with a slowdown now results in the "that spell did not take hold" type of message. This never used to happen before.
Abashi
Station Member		 posted 08-18-2000 12:35 PM           

It wasn't a monk nerf, but you are correct in that the fix to the attack speed debuff problem is what caused it. Sometimes fixes have both positive and negative consequences. Attack speed debuffs will always overwrite haste spells now.

-Gordon
