A Scam?
posted 4/12/99

We received this scam alert from Grimfold today:

I just want to alert people of a situation that has developed on the Innoruuk server yesterday.

Two people claiming to be paladins going by the names of Leoric and Heimdall were offering to sell rare items, such as Mino axes in the area of Greater Feydark. They would initiate a trade with a player and then switch the item after the purchasing player had clicked trade.

In my case, I checked that the item he was offering was indeed a Mino axe, but then after I had clicked on the trade button, Leoric switched the Mino axe for a rusty axe.

This can be avoided by making sure that the person selling the item has locked in the trade before you lock in the trade, however it still means someone has got to initially put some trust into the transaction.

Of course, when I realized what had happened (which was immediately after the transaction) and confronted Leoric on it, he laughed and stated that he had checked with a GM and that such duplicities were not a violation of the rules. I don't know if this is true, as I never received an answer to my petition.

Some may say that I should have known better, that it is my own fault. Maybe they are correct. However, that is not my point here. My point is to warn others.

Although there may be no repercussions against such dishonest actions in the rules, there is nothing stopping me from spreading the word of these people's dishonesty.

As I told Leoric and Heimdall, "I can always get my money back. You can NEVER get your reputation back."

Thanks for the info about a switch scam! Players can always change names, but we can be on the lookout for these tactics.

Update
posted 4/6/99

April 5th, 9:00pm
-----------------

We will be opening up a new section on the EverQuest website solely dedicated to the Test Server. Once the Test Server section is available, changes and bug fixes made to the Test Server will be documented, so that you may help us test the changes.

As for server updates, from this day forward, we will be updating the regular EverQuest servers every Tuesday morning at 3:00am Pacific Standard Time.

We will be performing a patch and server update at 3:00am Pacific Standard Time on Tuesday, April 6th. The patch contains the following additions and fixes;

1. Crashing while giving newbie items to NPCs.

2. Going link-dead would cause the character to regenerate extremely quickly.

3. Various sound related crashes were fixed.

4. Created items disappearing from characters after zoning.

5. Characters able to use skills, which are not allowed for their specific class.

6. A limit to the successful hit and spell messages has been implemented that should reduce the number of unneeded messages sent to the players.

7. After slaying a creature, the player will receive a proper faction hit.

8. Occasionally, after being disconnected, users would crash upon re-logging into the server.

9. The DLS sound system is now enabled.

10. A new text command, "/reply " has been implemented. This will allow players to reply to the last character that sent a private tell message.

11. Many adjustments to the faction system were made.

12. Multiple quest-related bugs were fixed.

13. A slew quests were added.

14. A number of new music tracks were added to various zones.

15. Occasionally, sound settings would reset to default, while graphically, the slider bars appeared to be correct.

16. Various safe zones were eliminated to prevent exploitation.

17. Some equipped weapons and items would draw into the user interface overlays, which would result in the client crashing.

18. It was possible to strafe at normal speed while rooted or sneaking.

19. All hybrid classes now receive meditate at level 12.

20. Experience over level 30 will now be reflected properly on the user's experience bar.

- The EverQuest Team

Client Freeze with Riva
posted 4/1/99 by Jaella

With a Riva 128zx card my client would freeze up about once an hour. I had to do a hard reset each time. I could not ctrl-alt-del, I could not move my mouse, anything. Each incident occurred in different places under different conditions. Time playing did not matter, it happened if there were 300 or 1000 people on server. Fortunately it never happened in combat.

Thinking it was a memory or hard drive space issue, I increased from 64 to 96 RAM, and bought a 8 gig hard drive. I still encountered the problem. We updated to the latest DirectX drivers. It still froze.

This did not happen to me in beta. When the commercial was released I changed from a Voodoo to a Riva. The Riva had more texture memory, and was thought to be "better". Given that we'd tried everything else, we changed back to the Voodoo.

I am happy to say I have not had the freezing up problem since. It may be some type of incompatibility issue with my Riva 128zx. I do not know if the same thing would occur with a Riva TNT, but I wanted people to be aware this may be a problem. I have filed this at the RPG Bug Base.

Three Times a Charm
posted 4/1/99 an altered joke

The Qeynos Madam opened the brothel door to see an elderly wood elf. His tunic was disheveled and he looked like he foraged for his food. "Can I help you?" the madam asked.

"I want Verjica," the old elf replied.

"Sir, Verjica is one of our most expensive ladies, perhaps someone else..."

"No, I must see Verjica."

Just then Verjica appeared and announced to the old elf that she charges 100 plat per visit. The man never blinked and reached into his backpack and handed her a heavy coin purse. The two went up to a room for an hour whereupon the elf calmly left.

The next night he appeared again demanding Verjica. Verjica explained that no one had ever come back two nights in a row and that there were no discounts... it was still 100 plat . Again the old elf took out the money, the two went up to the room and he calmly left an hour later.

When he showed up the third consecutive night, no one could believe it. Again he handed Verjica the money and up to the room they went. At the end of the hour Verjica questioned the old guy: "No one has ever used my services three nights in a row... where are you from?"

The old elf replied, "I am from Kelethin."

"Really?" replied Verjica, "I have a sister who lives there."

"Yes; I know," said the old elf. "She gave me 300 plat to give to you."
