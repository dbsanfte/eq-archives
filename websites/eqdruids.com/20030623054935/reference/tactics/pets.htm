Charmed Pet Tactics

Beginning at level 14, druids get a line of spells that allows us to charm animals (and plants) to assist us in our hunting. The 'charm' time is random and whatever you charm WILL come after you when its done. In general, the lower the CON (risky blue, etc) the longer it will stay charmed.

* Note: With the advent of the Shadows of Luclin expansion, there is now a level cap to the following marked spells.

At lower levels, an easy way to get used to any of your charm animal spells is to head to North Karana.

Find a blue Griffawn (not "on", or "enne") and charm it. Immediately send your new pet after another creature, like a scythe beetle.

Let your pet soak up the damage from the beetle, while you do damage from afar, or melee. Sooner or later, your pet will turn, and you can either recharm, or kill the weakened griffawn.

If all works out best, you’ll end up with a dead beetle, and a pet that is already weakened and much easier to slay. This will take time and practice. For the first few tries, while you get familiar with the strategy, make sure you have a zone handy!

In addition to the charm line of spells, druids also get two pet buffs, Feral Spirit (14) and Savage Spirit (44), which makes your pet more effective in battle. While it makes your pet a killing machine, especially with a thorn shield, it will turn on you eventually so be ready!

Many players who have mastered the Charming technique say it gives better experience than normal kiting.

Basic Pet Commands:

There is a very good list of all the in-game pet commands at the Mage's Compendium. You can find it here.

I'll only list some of the more frequently used ones here:

/pet attack (or /pet kill) - Tells the pet to attack the current targeted mob.
/pet guard me - Tells the pet to attack anyone that attacks it's master, or that it's master attacks.
/pet guard here - Tells the pet to guard its current location.
/pet follow me - Tells the pet to follow you, but ends when you cross a zone line.
/pet back off - Tells the pet to stop its attack and return to its /pet guard here point.
/pet as you were - Returns the pet to neutral, belaying all prior orders.
/pet sit down - Tells pet to sit down.
/pet stand up - Tells pet to stand up.
/pet report health - Displays the pets health as a percentage.

Charm Tactics:
 adapted from original guide written by Nyrril

Utilizing charm can easily add as much exp or even more than normal kiting can.  The trick to effectively using this spell line is not to get ahead of yourself and attack something you normally wouldn't be able to handle. It's important to note that charming stronger creatures seems to affect the charm time, thus (at lower levels) charming a white creature will not last near as long as charming a blue creature. At higher levels (44+), much of what you would be able to charm will con green to you.

Here are the tactics I've used to go from 14th to 17th in relatively short time:

I charm a blue creature and then I immediately command it to attack another blue creature. I would then use a DoT, direct damage, and some melee to assist the pet and keep my pet from taking too much damage.

After this kill, immediately target another blue creature, command your pet to attack it, then DoT it.  Come in for a little bit of melee, but mainly use direct damage to help finish it off.  It is important to make sure that your pet (and you) survives, but takes the majority of the damage.  It is also important to reserve enough mana for a couple more damage-dealing spells.

After this battle (perhaps before its even through) your charm will wear off, and your pet should be ready to turn on you. You should have saved enough mana in anticipation of this. Immediately begin attacking the former pet and you should be able to take it easily (its low on hitpoints from fighting). If it's hitpoints are low enough, it will begin to run and you can simply melee it. If the charm wore off prior to the end of the second encounter, don't bother re-charming your former pet. Simply concentrate on finishing off both mobs.

Using these tactics you can take down at least two to three blue creatures within a few minutes. Depending on your level, this can yield more exp than one yellow and gives as much as some red creatures.  Compared to normal kiting, you will get almost as much exp without spending nearly as much time.   Overall, your exp gain over time will be much greater.

Unfortunately, these tactics don't always work well.  You'll sometimes have your initial charm resisted (I usually try at least twice before giving up and blasting the creature). You can also have your pet turn on you too early in the second fight, and find yourself having to take on two relatively healthy blues (it's normally best to zone in this case).  Luckily, you will always have SoW up, and you should not have much of a problem getting to a zone.

It's important to make the decision to Fight or Flee immediately and stick with it -- If you aren't going to win the fight, don't waste the mana and hitpoints seeing if you can kill one of the creatures before fleeing. Its much better to just zone, have a quick rest break, and get back to it.
