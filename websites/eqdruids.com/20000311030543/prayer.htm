The prayer sent to the gods today....
by Brennos  

posted 10/4/99 11:30:38 AM

I Brennos Oakkeepp,
*raises head to the sky, stretches his arms up to the heavens*
Leader of the Keepers of Oak and The Rathe Druid Council send out this prayer.
I call today on the Strength of Karanas and the compassion of Tunare.
As Druids we are blessed with many powers and we thank thee for making us
so capable as the keepers of the balance of nature. There are some issues
I have been assigned with to pray to you for. It is believed my powers
as a leader will gain greater favor and thus hopefully we shall see some
improvements. I am but a humble woodsman, but others don't see me that
way and so I pray to you know to please hear our requests and know that
they may not be my requests, but the druid majority felt they were issues
needing divine attention. As your devote follower and a capable, yet humble
woodsman, I lend myself to help thee in anyway to resolve these problems.

We have several spells which are supposed to be improvements on others,
yet they show know ability to be better in anyway. These would be the upgrades
to snare, befriend animal and grasping root line of powers. Because of this
imbalance we have spells many wise druids refuse to purchase and inscribe. It
is felt that the gods are so busy with other issues, that these are being
overlooked. It might be better to remove them from the world than to taunt
us with the promise of improvement.

At this time I shall pray about our strength as a class. I know some have
sinned greatly and taken the lives of the humanoid guards, thus making us
all incur your wrath and lose our strength in our Damage over Time spells.
However, as a keeper of balance and a spokesman for all responsible Druids,
I must lodge a request to reinstate this power. Of the hundreds of Druids
that attend the Druid Council, the majority still feel, even after emotions
have calmed, that this power should not have been tampered with, and the
punishment too severe across the whole class just to slow down those that
choose the path of the Unkempt Druids (power gamers). We are certain that
with all thy powers, there must be a better solution to slowing the
progress of the few, without crippling the many.

And finally I would like to state that I see great wisdom in not giving us
a pet spell as we are the keepers of balance and this would hurt that
balance. However, we pray to you now to allow those animals that we
befriend to journey on their way when our spell runs out, and not attack us.
Mayhaps the animal would make the decision to leave or attack based on how
much of it's health it had left. If a Druid had healed and buffed it, or
nay used it as a battle partner at all, it would go on it's way, not
realizing anything had been done, however, if it took damage and had not
been healed, then it's anger and pain would be turned on the Druid. Although
I have explained to my brethren that part of our job is to help curb the
overpopulation of predator animals and we shouldn't feel badly about culling
the herds a bit, it still leaves us empty to kill one we have befriended. I
know of only a few druids who use our befriend line of spells, and even then
as a novelty, not a legitimate battle tactic. It has been suggested that
mayhaps taming animals be a trainable skill for Druids as opposed to a spell.
This also might be a solution and a way to be sure our "pets" are not too
powerful as we would only be able to tame certain leveled creatures.

The Druid Council tries desperately to form some sense of community to
Norrath. The economy is in desperate need of attention, which although
would be unpopular, would be fixed by making items repairable, and breakable.
Suddenly people would have a need for each other and items that had been
camped and handed out across Norrath, and thus lost their worth, such as
the Runed Totem Staff, would again be worth something, as would the skills
of tailors and blacksmiths.

There are, of course other issues which we shall leave for now, but our main
one is the feeling that we are not the favored children of Tunare or Karanas,
but instead are the siblings of misbehaving children, being punished along
with them. I am sure the wisdom of Tunare sees through this, as does the
strength of Karanas and know that the majority of Druids are responsible
and love and care for our home of Norrath.

As your humble servants we pray to you now, to hear our words and know our love
and respect and please guide us to find our path, balanced once more, by your hand,
and your skills, we shall bring Norrath to a pinnacle of splendor in the universe.
*lowers his head, drops his arms, and kneels with his hands on the moist earth
of Surefall Glade*
I, you humble servant Brennos, beseech thee to answer our prayers and help us.
By the strength of Heaven,
Light of Sun,
Radiance of Moon,
Splendor of Fire,
Speed of Lightning,
Swiftness of Wind,
Depth of Sea,
Stability of Earth,
and
Firmness of Stone...
Amen
