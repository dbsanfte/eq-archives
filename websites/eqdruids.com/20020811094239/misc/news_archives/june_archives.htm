Lots of Screenshots of New Interface from Test Server 6/25/02

Ok, I jumped on test server to grab some screenshots of the new interface.  Here are some thoughts and pictures!

First off, the new character selection screen.  The entire screen rotates now, instead of just a window.  Its really hard to look at!  But I am sure we'll get used to it.

Now, for the first shot of the new interface!  You'll notice all the different sections including the window selector.  You can use the window selector to open/close different windows on your screen.  Not bad, but I didn't find a way to get rid of it once I was done with the selecting of windows.

Click on picture for larger screenshot

I went ahead and opened all the windows I could to see what was what.  I like the new options menu a lot.  Easy to change settings on the fly as you are playing.  The friends window was a nice addition, and the pets window is very nice.

Click on picture for larger screenshot

Below is the inventory window.  Basically the same window that was available before in the high resolution choice.  With the autoequip feature in the middle.

 

 

 

Click on picture for larger screenshot

The new spell book took a little getting used too.  Mainly because as you can see they have changed the spell icons.  But again, shouldn't take long to get used too.

Click on picture for larger screenshot

One of the nice features I have to say, is the "hover help".  If you put your cursor over any of the features and leave it there for a second, it will pop up with a little text tag of what the button or feature is.

Click on picture for larger screenshot

The new chat setup is VERY nice.  Right click on the chat menu, and you will have all your settings right there.  Including filters, channels, what language to speak in etc.  No more missing what is going on, while you make changes.

Click on picture for larger screenshot

One of the nicest features for chat, is the ability to make multiple chat channel areas, and assign different chats to them.  Example below, one chat box is set to guild chat, another to auctions, and the main is ooc/shout.

Click on picture for larger screenshot

The alternate Advancement area has been cleaned up, nothing too spectacular there, but does look nice.

Click on picture for larger screenshot

The training window changes are nice.  I like the ability to see multiple skills with their rank, what value I am at in each, and how much each would cost to train. 

Click on picture for larger screenshot

Overall very nice changes.  The only thing that takes some getting used to is this.  When you don't have your mouse on a particular window, the window fades (becomes almost transparent).  When you put the mouse on a window, it fades back into focus.  So it allows you to have a window on top of what you are looking at, and still be able to see through the window.  This takes a little getting used too, but again I think it is a nice improvement.  It opens up the screen a little more.

New Interface is on test! 6/25/02

From the Developers Corner:

Here's the patch message from Test.

(By the way, don't forget this is the Test server, and that entails).

---

** Monday, June 24 2002.

Welcome to the open beta phase of testing for EverQuest's new interface!

It still has a few rough edges and some pieces that aren't complete, but after using it in-house for a couple weeks, we'd like to see how well it does in front of a larger audience.

By now, you've downloaded all of the graphics and layout files. If you choose to run the new interface, please edit your EQCLIENT.INI file and, under the defaults section, add the line:

NewUI=TRUE

...and feel free to have a look around!

As always, your feedback is welcomed through the Developer's Corner.


* Random new toys worth checking out in the new interface:

- Item Inspection: Alt-Leftclick now pops up the inspect box and keeps it there.

- Quantity Selection: You can now type numbers when referring to stackable items and coins.

- Key Mapper: Every top level window still has a hotkey you can use to open and close it, and all of the keys can still be remapped through the Options screeen. (The hex nut icon in the window selector, or Alt-O by default). To turn the window selector itself on and off, the default key is Alt-W.

- Mousewheel: ...should automatically work in every UI screen that has a scroll bar. (including Chat, Merchants, Looting, etc.)


* For those interested in developing custom skins for the interface:

You'll notice that there is now a directory called UIFiles beneath your EverQuest install directory.

The default skin (aka "The New UI") is stored in the directory called, predictably "default," and we've included a document called "AboutSIDL.doc" in the UIFiles directory.

If you make yourself a new skin, please create a separate directory for it in the UIFiles directory. Once the skin is in there, you can switch to it by typing "/loadskin <directory_name>"

Working with the skins at this point is not an activity for the faint of heart, but if you're inclined to hack around in those files, AboutSIDL will hopefully help out a bit.

This one we definitely can't just sign "- The EverQuest Development Team."

Many, many people have contributed to this effort, be it artwork, technology, or suggestions.

So, for now, we'll sign it this way:

- The EverQuest Team
- The Sovereign Team
- The SOE Platform Art Team
- The SOE Testing Department
- The SOE Game Operations Team
- The SOE Customer Service Department
- The SOE Platform Coding Team

Everquest Adventures for PS/2 review! 6/23/02

The July 2002 issue of the Official U.S. Playstation Magazine has a review of the upcoming Everquest Adventures for the PS/2.

It didn't have a huge amount of information, but some of the highlights were:

  • There will be over 1200 quests in the game
  • Dying will take you to your last save point, not like in EQ for the pc
  • You will have the ability to use a keyboard addon to type to people if you want, but it won't be necessary to play the game.
  • There will be a new melee interface that is being built to use the strengths of the PS/2's controls
  • There will be no loading of zones, it will all be on one disk.  So you will not have to pause in your play.
  • There will be pets, but not sure which classes will have them available (didn't say)
  • There will be extra installments written for the game, made as mission packs

Those are the biggies.  For more information on Everquest Adventures, you can visit the official website at:  http://everquestonlineadventures.station.sony.com/

Report back from Everquest Invasion Tour 6/22/02

Ok, I made the trip to Long Beach to see the Everquest Invasion Tour setup.  This is my report!

If you are an established Everquest player, you will probably not have much to do at this tour.  This tour is basically to spread the word about Everquest especially to those that haven't experienced it before.  Also important to know.  They do have a server running Everquest for people to try out the game, but it is NOT a normal server.  You can't logon to your character and play here.  It is a localized setup.

Now, if you haven't played Everquest before or have a friend or someone you want to show this all too, this is a good place to go.  They do have a demo setup where you can try out the game, and they will show you a little about everything Everquest.

Another plus is that they are giving out Everquest Invasion Tour cd's which are the basic Everquest game with a month free service.  So again, if you are looking for a second account, or want to get a friend playing the game, definitely head down to the event.

While I was there, I was able to talk to Brian from Sony, and I wanted to pass on a few impressions that he left me with.  Everquest 2 is going to be AMAZING.  He said he has never seen anything like it, and that it will just blow folks away!

Also Planes of Power.  There are going to be some VERY nice areas in Planes of Power.  This will be one of those upgrades that I think everyone will really enjoy. 

I'll include some pics I took of the Everquest Invasion Tour setup.  I'll thumbnail them, so if you want more detail click on them.
