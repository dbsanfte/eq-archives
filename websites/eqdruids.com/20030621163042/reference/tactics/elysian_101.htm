Elysian Hunting 101

submitted by Khirsanth Lifespike, Drinal Server

 

So you want to solo (or at least hunt) disturbed Elysian remains in Umbral Plains?  Well, let's see if I can quell some of the many questions that are posted here repeatedly.

 

  1. Worth of the Armor:  This armor is well worth the time needed to invest in receiving it.  I guess you could call Elysian armor the "uber armor of the non-uber druid."  You do not need a large guild to raid for this armor.  More often than not if you have some decent gear, some wits, skill, and KEI, you can solo these MOBs.

 

  1. A second commonly asked question I've seen is can you solo the elysians at 53 with _____ or without ______.  Well, the answer is yes.  At 53, as long as you have decent mana (probably 2200 or 2300 at the least) and some KEI, you can solo here - but you MUST be careful.  Last night, I myself just dinged 54, and I would imagine 3/4s of my experience towards that level came form Elysians, grouping and soloing.  You do NOT, I repeat, do NOT need the Warden's Symbol of Tunare or your epic … however they would help a lot.  I personally have neither, and had no problems whatsoever.  You should know that conventional druid root is very very unreliable.  You will need at least Clarity 2 or KEI to do this solo at 53.  Root (if you do not have the Warden's Symbol) will eat a good portion of your mana, especially the lower the Elysian's HP become.  Lastly, there is one item you most definitely need.  A manaless DoT item such as the Elder Spiritists Vambraces or Rowyl's Bracer of Nature is a must.  Even with the epic, every little bit of damage helps speed up the process.  More kills = more drops.  At 53 it took 11 minutes to kill ONE Elysian solo.  That was with 10 casts of Winged Death, 10 casts of Drones of Doom (from my Elder Spiritists Vambraces), and one cast of Drifting Death.  Add all that up with anywhere from 5 to 25 casts of Engulfing Roots (160 damage a pop as well) and you have little time to meditate and lot of spells to cast to deal around 20,000 HPs of damage.  Fun, eh?

 

  1. So you want to know how often the armor drops?  In my experiences, the armor drop rate is about one in every four kills.  Keep in mind, however, that the same Elysian can (and WILL) often drops multiple items.  Some will disagree, I am sure, but the rarest item (this is my own opinion from experience and experience received from other druids) is the Elysian Crown.  I've killed over a hundred or more Elysians altogether, and have yet to see the crown.  The tunic and armplates also seem to be rarer than the rest.  As for the spell drops - Foliage Shield and Spirit of the Eagle are the only two druid spells I have seen (FS twice and SoE once) drop from Elysians.  Some say they replace armor drops as loot, but that is not the case.  I saw armor (legs and SoE, gauntlets with FS) two of those three times.  Gems are on the spawns about half the time.

 

Now for those finding out about this armor for the first time, here are some (vague) directions to get to Umbral Plains and the safe spot/camp.  (Someone please retrieve these /locs as I am typing this on the go.)

 

1.                  First, you need to gate to Dawnshroud Peaks, invis yourself at the gate in point and head south for a few feet (past the run-down house thing) and then go southeast.  You will see a tunnel ahead and slightly to the left.  Go there and zone, you have entered Maiden's Eye.

 

2.                  Refresh invis and head along the right wall (yes I know there are faster ways, but this is the route I prefer), continue until you see an off white octagonal structure in the distance.  AVOID THAT STRUCTURE!!  Creatures around that structure Harm Touch for 750 HP a pop - - OUCH!  Cut across the field past the structure and rejoin the right wall.  Continue on it until you come to what looks like to be a Horn either sticking out of the air or out of the ground (sometimes it appears to be one to me, sometimes it appears to be another) and a tunnel.  Stay invis and run past the Mobs here and you have entered Umbral Plains.

 

3.                  Once in Umbral, levitate and re-invis and stay high on the right wall.  You will come to a temple, go around it and get back on the right wall.  You have now entered the safe valley.  Personally I bound high up on the wall in the middle for a few reasons.  One - if I die I am near my corpse.  Two - when I have KEI or have to get KEI, I can always gate right back in and be ready to kill.  Thirdly, and most important - you can Egress or Succor if things go south, then just gate back to your camp.

 

The place you pull from is just ahead on the right wall, stay high on that wall and look down into the valley there, catch the wanders, and ONLY the wanderers.  Avoid anything that isn't Elysian, because there's a good chance it summons faster than you can Egress.  Snare the Mobs and pull them SLOWLY back to camp - safest bet would be to only pull yellows or under - but most that I killed at 53 were reds.  The pathing near the corner of the camp/safe area is HORRIBLE near one certain tree (you will see what I mean), so you may need to take a hit or two (keep Chloroplst/Regrowth ON) to get it to cross that line.  That's about all for pulling.

 

My personal opinion is, if you are going to group, go ALL druid.  Don't be selfish.  The loot will come quicker grouped than solo, promise ya that.  Another nice group I was in was Enchanter, Cleric, Druid (me), Monk, Ranger, and Warrior.  DoT the MOB, thorn the tanks, heal the enchanter and monk.  Also have been in a Druid (me), Necro, Warrior, Cleric, Enchanter group.  Was also very nice.

 

All in all there are many ways to get your Elysian, from waiting for /oocs of rotting armor to grouping, to soloing.  There are only three key rules:

 

1. Be safe - if things turn sour, don't be afraid to boogy on out of there.

 

2. Don't be greedy; your druid friends won't want to help if you win all the loot.  SHARE THE WEALTH!!

 

Have fun, the EXP solo is great, and grouped isn't half bad.  The loot is awesome and it's a challenging fight.  What more can ya ask for?
