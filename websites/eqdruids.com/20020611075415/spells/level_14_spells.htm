Spirit of the Wolf:  

Effect:  Increase Movement between 34% (L9) to 55% (L50)

Skill: Alteration
Casting Area Restriction: Outdoor
Allowable Targets: All

Range to Target: 100 feet

Resistance Check: None

Mana Required: 40
Spell Duration: 360 ticks (36.0 minutes)
Duration Formula: 3
Casting Time: 4.50 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.25 seconds
Recast Delay: 3.50 seconds

Spell cast on you: You feel the spirit of wolf enter you.
Spell cast on someone: <Target> is surrounded by a brief lupine aura.
Spell fades: The spirit of wolf leaves you.

Many players will flat out say that this is the best spell in the game. I will not disagree. It adds SIGNIFICANTLY to your overall speed. No longer can monsters run you down! You will run circles around them. Better yet, groups which were pretty indifferent to adding a Druid to their ranks now get considerably more excited once they have been exposed to the benefits of SoW. This spell probably adds more to your character's survivability than any other spell.  This spell has an extremely long duration, longer than any other buff spell we have yet. It’s a good thing to because if you want to cast it on yourself and several party members it takes the majority of your mana. However it is an outdoor casting spell only. 

This spell is one of your main spells.  Keep it handy at all times!

Purchase at:  Druid Guild

Cascade of Hail:  

Effect:  Decrease Hit Points (HP) by 27 (L14)

Skill: Evocation
Allowable Targets: Target Based AoE

Range to Target: 150 feet
Area Effect Range: 20 feet

Resistance Check: Cold + 25

Mana Required: 62
Spell Duration: Three Waves
Casting Time: 2.75 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.50 seconds
Recast Delay: 12.0 seconds

Spell cast on you: You are pelted by hailstones.
Spell cast on someone: <Target> is pelted by hailstones.

An Area of Effect RAIN spell. Does about 60 damage over a span of 10 or 15 seconds. Like Invoke Lightning the area of effect is very small and you need to make sure the monsters are "shoulder-to-shoulder". Oddly enough the graphic for the spell looks more like a rain of fire than it does a cascade of hail. It costs 62 mana to cast, so it is more mana efficient that Invoke Lightning, but it takes much longer to recover from casting. However, unlike lightning it is not an outdoor-only spell.

Purchase at:  Druid Guild

Stinging Swarm:  

Effect:  DoT for 117 Hit Points (HP) in 9 ticks

Skill: Conjuration
Allowable Targets: All

Range to Target: 250 feet

Resistance Check: Magic + 25

Mana Required: 65
Spell Duration: 9 ticks (54 seconds)
Duration Formula: 1
Casting Time: 2.45 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.50 seconds

Spell cast on you: You feel the pain of a thousand stings.
Spell cast on someone: <Target> is engulfed by a swarm.
Spell fades: The swarm departs.

A Damage Over Time (DOT) spell. This is an awesome spell. This spell does about 117 pts of damage (if you have your Conjuration skill fully trained) over about a 54 second's worth of time when cast on myself. It only costs 65 mana to cast, so the mana:damage ratio is very good. 

Combined with Spirit of Wolf this spell is wonderfully effective. You can cast the spell on a monster and lead it on a merry chase, stopping every 30 seconds or so to recast it. It is absolutely essential that you max out your Conjuration skill before you get serious about using this spell often however. At 80 Conjuration skill it still fizzles 5-10% of the time. 

Note some sites list this spell as outdoors-only, but that is NOT true. It can be cast indoors as well. 

Purchase at:  Druid Guild

Bind Affinity:

Effect:  Bind Affinity

Skill: Alteration
Allowable Targets: All

Range to Target: 100 feet

Resistance Check: None

Mana Required: 100
Spell Duration: Instant
Casting Time: 6.0 seconds
Spell Recovery: 2.25 seconds
Recast Delay: 12.0 seconds

When you cast: You cast Bind Affinity.
When someone casts: <Target> casts Bind Affinity.
Spell cast on you: You feel yourself bind to the area.
Spell cast on someone: <Target> is bound to the area.

Finally you have the companion spell, to Gate.  You can now cast this on yourself and set your bind point.  Self casting, you can bind almost any area.  Casting on another person in your party (must be in party) you can bind them in towns only.

You will use this spell a lot.  But don't memorize it, until you need it.  Make sure when binding that you bind in a safe area also.  Nothing worse than being killed, and zoning in on a Sand Giant.

Purchase at:  Druid Guild
