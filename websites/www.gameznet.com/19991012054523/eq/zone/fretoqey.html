Freeport to Qeynos
This guide was submitted by Akaran of Veeshan.

Ok, this is a generally easy, but long, task. Most of the problems you'll face are on the Qyenos side of the continent. And if you run real fast through HighHold, you won't have much problems there either.

By the way, it makes sense to stop and get bound in Rivervale when you get there, but that's a little ahead of things.

Starting at the West Gate, make sure you have some food, water, and some type of light source (Lightstone or Greater Lightstone; even better is Serpent Sight). You don't need Speed of Wolf (SoW) but it might be helpful if you get caught in the middle of a train. Don't forget to be fully armed and armored because chances are you WLL meet up with somthing big and ugly.

By the way, if you're a Paladin or Shadow Knight, you might want to wait on your trip uintil your "Lay Hands" or "Harm Touch" is able to be cast again. I'm not kidding about these nasties, and it's always good to be fully prepared for any lengthy trip.

Ok, from the West Gate, head West into the Eastern Commons. Simply follow the path, don't go off on any splits in the path, just keep walking until you get to the West Commons. In the EComms, just keep your eyes open for Young Kodiacs, Black Bears, Black/Shadow Wolves, Lions and Lionesses. Oh, and there's a rather large Griffen that spawns in there too.

Once in the WCommons, you can either follow the right edge of the map or the path. I generally follow the right edge, but be careful becaue you'll have a chance of running into some Orc/Derv Camps and a Mummy/Skel spawning ground. Keep folowing the edge until you hit the entrance to the Kithcor Woods. Watch out for Kodiacs, Orcs, Zombies, ect.

In Kithcor, it's probably dark. It's ALWAYS dark in Kithcor. Keep your eyes open and your weapon ready. Usually I'm through this zone so fast I don't bother to stop and look at the locals, just keep going until you hit the fork in the path. If you are feeling daring, go ahead and follow the fork on the right which goes to HighHold Pass. If you're feeling intelligent, go on the left hand path and head into Rivervale.

Once in Rivervale, scream until you get bound. If you can't find anyone to bind you, go into Misty Thicket and ask there, donate if necessary. (East and West Commons aren't fun to go through naked) Stock up on provisions if necessary, and try and refrain from making any short jokes. Also sit and heal, meditate to recover any mana spent. This is very important, because you're going on the path through HighHold, which is, arguably, the NPC training ground for the Fear Plane.

Leaving Rivervale, follow the path back to the fork. Go on the path on your right, head to HighHold Pass.

Once you hit HH, start running and don't stop. Don't stop to read this guide, don't stop to grab a drink. Don't stop to help or heal, if you do you'll probably be dead. (Ignore this if you're above level 15 or so.. if you're that level go in and have fun) At the entrance you'll be in the middle of an Orc Spawning ground. Run. Go straight. You'll cross two bridges in sucession, then have a chance to turn twice, don't. After the second brach on the path, you come to a third turn on your left. Take this turn. And pray. This is Gnoll spawning ground, so go through it very quickly, if you get hit keep running because the zone isn't far ahead of you. Soon as you hit East Karana sit and take a breather.

Well now, that was fun, wasn't it?

The trip through East Karana is a vacation. Don't look down, however. Take the right hand ledge and walk through this zone. I saw no monsters here at all. Just keep walking and stay close to the mountainside. (It's a long way down) Eventually the edge will lower down to a open field, get on it and kiss the ground. From there, start walking and cross the wooden bridge into the nest Zone, North Karana.

Ok, durring the trip through most of the Karana's, I didn't see much hostile. Much, that is. I hear stories of Griffens, Hill Giants and other biggies. And I saw a number of Silvermist Wolves, a varied assortment of Bears, and a Bandit. Through north Karana, don't deviate from the path, don't take either of the two left turns you'll see.

West Karana is HUGE. Bears, bugs and other biggies spawn here, so be careful and back away from anything that cons higher than blue to you. Also, avoid the big barns near the farms. You'll see what I mean. Bandits, both caster and non-caster hide in there, and will attack upon sight. Just stay on the main path, don't take any of the branches on the track. Near the end of your journey, you'll see a human on the track (usually). He's a bandit, and a hard one to kill. (Yellow to me when I passed through the area at level 9) Leave the track long enough to put alot of space between you and him, then once you've safely put a large ammount of space between the two of you, get back on the path and zone into Qyenos Hills.

From the Qyenos hills, you're set. Just follow the path down into Qyenos, and ask for a bind.

That wasn't overly difficult, but if you want to have a higher survival rate (or lower, depending) go with a small group. This means you can have more people covering your back, but it also means that you'll have a greater chance of attracting somthing nasty.
