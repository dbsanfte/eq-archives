Brad On Ruins of Kunark Posted Friday, March 3, 2000 by jcsmith
Gamespot has posted up an interview with Everquest producer Brad McQuaid. The interview covers Ruins of Kunark. Here's a highlight...

GameSpot: How [if at all] will Ruins of Kunark specifically address issues like camping static spawns with good loot and specific zone overcrowding?

McQuaid: It doesn't - it adds a new playable race and a new continent to explore, increasing the size of Norrath by over 30 percent. Improving the camping situation is a continual process of moving treasure around and making sure that highly wanted items are not class specific and the product of quests. Specific zone overcrowding is generally solved by retuning zones, often making less frequented zones

One Year Down Posted Monday, February 28, 2000 by jcsmith
Today marks the one year anniversary of EQ'Lizer. Hard to believe it's been a year already. My how time flies when your having fun.

A year ago we were the only site which gave an extensive equipment and quest sections, we were the only site which had spells above level 20 posted for every class. Times have changed. Now you can browse through the net and find any number of sites which offer quests, daily equipment updates, zone guides, spells as well as news and our staff consisted of one person(myself).

At this time last year the game was on the closing days of beta testing. It had already gone gold and was readying a mid-march release. It has grown and matured much since then. In the initial months we were quite hard on Verant for in my opinion rushing the release. There are still certain moves that I might disagree with but all in all the game is vastly improved from a year ago.

I just wanted to take the time to thank all of the editors and readers who have been through these gates in the past year. All the editors have done a great job from Iuons to Fyndhorn to most recently Buck Satan and a good number of other editors(such as T and Welor) who are too many to mention. Thanks for making EQ'Lizer the site that it is today and here's to another year in operations.
