The Ultimate Starting-Out Guide: ROGUE

Overview

 The Rogue is the thief and assassin of Everquest.  He is able to do extraordinary amounts of damage in a quick amount of time, because of his powerful backstabbing skill, which makes him handy to have around.  When you add in other skills he possesses such as poisoning weapons, and picking doors in dungeons, the once-broken rogue class has become a favourite of many Everquest players. 

Pros and Cons

(+) With backstab, rogues can do extraordinary amounts of damage, making them the most damaging class in the game, for the most part.

(+) The ability to sneak and hide allows the rogue to move invisible to almost every monster, alive or undead, at the same time – a unique ability of the rogue

(+) Pick pocketing ensures that you will never be low on platinum pieces.

(+) Your ability to open locked doors makes you required in some groups, which are attacking dungeons with this obstacle.  Some other classes can pick easy locks, but none can master the skill like the rogue can.

(+) Your previously useless sense and disarm traps skills are now useful in Velious where traps can kill an unprepared party.

(+) Can wear many types of Armour and are able to equip most daggers and 1H Slash weapons.

(+) Get many useful skills, which help keep you alive, such as safe fall, dodge, etc.

(+) Rogues are the only class who can tell if they have successfully activated their hide or sneak skill.

(-) Because of their large damage output, a rogue can get waxed pretty quickly if he isn’t careful

(-) They are a melee class, but because they need a large range of motion they cannot wear most plate armor

(-) In order to use backstab, a rogue must have a dagger as his primary weapon

(-) They aren’t the most… well liked class in Norrath.

(-) Soloing = Suicide in most instances.

(-) Your large damage output from backstabbing requires a group to take the heat off of you.

Examination of Races

Like the warrior, the rogue can do well as almost any race that is able to specialize in this class.  They have many talents, and a player can suit his playing style to the race he has chosen.  Despite this, there still are still some races which are better as rogues than others for various reasons, and if you are interested in starting to play a Rogue I would suggest one of the following three races:

Halfling Rogue – This is a very good, balanced rogue.  Many people argue that they are the best rogues due to their excellent rogue stats.  The strength isn’t too bad, and the agility and dexterity are quite a bit above average.  Combined with their inherent mischievous nature, halflings seem rather suited to being rogues.  Their experience bonus doesn’t hurt either!

Wood Elf Rogue – Many people choose this combination because of role-playing reasons.  Wood Elf rogues are close to halflings in the stat department (although noticeably lower in strength and stamina, this can be easily compensated for) yet seem to act as more of a “scout” than the “thief” role a halfling is suited for.  Combined with the fact that wood elves (especially the ladies) are a lot better looking than halflings and better overall faction, many people choose to take the slight statistical blow and go Wood Elf. On the other hand, if looks are that important to someone, I think that half-elf might deserve some consideration (they are stunning in plate).

Barbarian Rogue – Although I would never play this race as a rogue simply for role-playing reasons (it is very difficult for a 6 foot muscular human to sneak around) the Barbarians have the highest strength available to a rogue race.  This is important, because the damage formula for the all-important backstab skill has a strength term, meaning that the higher the strength, the harder the backstab.
