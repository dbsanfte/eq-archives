Everquest Review

I will never forget the first time I played a pen and paper RPG. I was 10 years old and my older cousin handed me the AD&D Players Handbook. He promised me that if I read the book and learned the rules he would allow me to play in his next session. For the next 5-6 years I played pen and paper RPG's at least twice a month and I knew the rules by heart.

By the time I was 12 computer RPG's were beginning to flood the market. Games such as Bard's Tale, Ultima, Wizardry, and Might & Magic will forever be engraved in many players minds. In the years to come we would see the overhead tile based games turn into tile based three dimensional games. Years later games such as Ultima World and The Elder Scrolls: Daggerfall would move our genre to the world of free scrolling 3d.

However it was not until the launch of Ultima Online and Meridian 57 that we began to realize the potential of large online role playing games. There is something about actually being a part of a persistant world. In old CRPG's we would hear of the legends from the past. In an online RPG we were given a chance to be those legends. Not only could we participate in a persistant and changing world but each time we would log in we would notice the same players crossing out paths. Friendships were sparked and teamwork could greatly enhance your productiveness and your survival rate.

These early online RPG's were not without their problems however. Lag was horrible, Ultima Online was using cartoonish graphics from a third person perspective. Player killers(PKers) ruined the game for many by preying on new players and stealing their items. The artificial intelligence was anything but and it these online worlds were reduced to a neverending game of Quake leaving many players asking for more. There really was little to do in these early titles. You could learn trade skills to make money that you really didn't need or you could hack and slash monsters with little to gain. In the longrun your characters skills were maxed, the dungeons were repetitive and boring and the only danger was from PKers outnumbering you and stealing all of your goods.

Well this all stops now. Sony Interactive's Everquest is a breath of fresh air for the online role playing community. Rather than go through a long review that will wind up exactly like every other review on the market I have decided to list all of the features which makes this game superior to games such as Ultima Online.

A Large 3D World

Well the most obvious advantage are the graphics and the immersion factor. Everquest's graphics are fully three dimensional. They are 3d accelerated and allow you to view they game from several programmable camera views. This is a big step up from the cartoonish look of Ultima and Meridian.

The engine is top notch and handles large outdoor scenes very well using a heightfield based engine. Indoor scenes are of acceptable quality though they can not match the indoor scenes from popular 3d action titles such as Unreal and Quake 2. The textures are the only part of the graphics which I do not feel is top notch. Some textures are very well done but there are some places in the game(such as the basement of Unrest) which have a somewhat cartoonish look. Believe me though when you are running through those hallways to escape the hordes of undead beasts who are breathing down your neck you will not pay much attention to the textures.

Graphics aside the world is large enough to keep you exploring for quite some time. There are three continents currently in the game, several islands and four alternate planes of existance. The first couple of times travelling throughout Norrath you will be in awe of the graphics and the well thought out maps. When you feel up for some indoor excitement you can travel to one of the many dungeons in the game. There are 18 different dungeon zones in the game for characters of all levels.

Speaking of zones the game is split into many different zones. Each zone is in essence it's own seperate server though you can contact players from other zones and keep track of them. However monsters can not chase you from one zone to the next. This is similar to the way the dungeons in Ultima Online were handled except it is a bit more apparent in Everquest.

A Lag Free Playing Experience

The one thing that worried me about Everquest was lag. I played Ultima Online in the early days when you would take 3 steps and then wait for about 15 seconds to be able to move any further. I was wondering how Verant would be able to handle a large 3d world with a large number of players in a single zone. Anyone who has played Quake or Unreal can attest to how badly a server can bog down with more than 32 players.

I am happy to tell you that Everquest is virtually lag free. Of course there will always be lag and if you have a poor ISP you might notice some lag spiked and packet loss. However for the most part lag does not affect gameplay. I believe the biggest reason for this is the high quality prediction engine in Everquest. The way prediction works is by sending updates at so and so intervals and then morphing position a to positon b on the fly. It is a very effective technique but it also causes a few anomolies that you will notice from time to time in the game.

Sometimes you will be following a party member and lag will hit. He will appear to run around in circles if he was previously turning which might cause you to get lost. Thankfully several classes can track and usually you will not be too far away by the time you realize what happened.

Another situation that you will notice is that other players and monsters health bars will not always be updated immediately. Sometimes you will notice a party members health drop rapidly due to the intervals between the last update. After a while you will begin to anticipate about how much damage is actually being dished and this will not be a problem however you should know of it's existance.

Class Based Gameplay > Skills Based

One of the largest problems in Ultima Online was the skills based gameplay system. Traditionally in pen and paper role playing games you choose a class from a group of templates. These classes could be a warrior, cleric, wizard etc. In recent games such as Daggerfall and Ultima Online the trend was to allow players to customize their own players. In Ultima Online if you practiced your spells your magery went up, if you practiced with a bow your archery improved. While this is perhaps more realistic it hurts game playability.

First and foremost by not having set classes you reduce the need of players to depend on one another and grouping becomes unneccessary. In Ultima you had players who were grandmaster mages yet they wore full platemail and could wield any weapon which they desired. In Everquest you will NOT see this type of inbalance.

The classes in Everquest are extremely well balanced and will need to depend on one another for survival. Higher level monsters will require a well balanced party to overcome them. Grouping is really not an option in Everquest it is a neccessity if you wish to conquer the games most powerful beasts and win the ultimate prizes.

There are 14 different classes in Everquest: Warriors, Paladins, Shadowknights, Rangers, Monks, Rogues, Bards, Magicians, Necromancers, Enchanters, Wizards, Clerics, Druids and Shamans. Each class has it's own unique advantages and carries with it it's own flaws which will be undesirable. At early levels you can get away with solo playing however at higher levels it will become a neccessity to group if you wish to remain competitive. This is a major plus because group play is much funner than solo play. By grouping you will meet new friends and you will need to depend on them for survival.

In addition to having 14 different classes to choose from you can choose from 12 different races. Each race has it's strengths and weaknesses. Depending on the race you choose you will only be able to choose from certain classes. Some races also have special racial abilities such as the ability to hide or tinker.

Another throwback in Everquest is the return of levels. In Ultima there were no levels and there was no need for experience. In Ultima if you wanted to become a grandmaster warrior you could grab another player go into the forest and slash at one another for a period of time and become one. In Everquest you will need experience to reach new levels. Each level grants new mana(if applicable), hit points and skill points. Some levels will grant additional skills or spells. Experience can be gained in one of two ways: by killing comparably matched monsters(easy monsters will not grant any experience) and by completing quests.

Group Play Is Simply Funner

In the above paragraph I mentioned that group play was much funner than going solo. In this portion of the review we are going to explain why group play is funner and what Everquest how Everquest game play is different than other games.

One of the things that made teaming up in games such as Ultima Online was the notoriety system was geared towards whoever got the last hit. The last hit got the entire notoriety boost from a monster. The largest weakness of UO's system however was the looting system. Anyone could run up to a monster and grab all that they wanted. People who weren't even part of the fight would often run and try to grab the loot off monsters that you worked to kill.

Everquest solves these problems and more. First and foremost if you wish to team up with some other players you can invite them to join a group. There can be up to six players in each group. Every competitively leveled monster that your group fights will give experience. This experience will be divided among every member of the group. Anyone in the group can then loot a monster that the group kills. If everyone has the autosplit option turned on the monsters coins will be split among party members, though items will not. Another great feature in Everquest is the fact that only the person or the group which killed a monster will be able to loot it for a certain period of time(a few minutes).

There are a few limitations to grouping however. Group members must be within comparable level ranges. Anyone who is blue to the highest group member will be able to join the group and get experience for kills. This ensures that level 1 characters will not be able to group with level 50 players and get free experience for doing nothing. You can still join a higher leveled players group and you will still be able to loot monsters that the group kills and collect your share of the loot, but you will not gain any experience.

To PK Or Not PK

In games like Ultima Online and Diablo player killing ruined the fun for many players. It was discouraging for many players to be killed by much more powerful characters or by large groups of others and lose all of their items. Other's felt that Player Versus Player combat was the most challenging and fun part of online gaming. In Everquest the system is aimed to please all players not just one group or the other.

When your character is created you will be given a PK Note. If you turn this note into the priest of discord you will receive a small amount of experience and you will become +PVP. What this means is that you will be able to kill or be killed by other +PVP players in your level range. The level range limitations insure that high level characters don't run around and pick on low level players thus ruining their gameplay experience. +PVP characters can be distinguished by the red text above their names. +PVP characters can be looted by other players. All your cash can be taken but a looting player can only grab one item off of your corpse. There is no experience granted in Everquest for PVP combat which is keeps it from becoming exploitable.

Players who elect not to turn in their PK Note will be immune to this aspect of the game. There is no way that a +PVP player can do anything harmful to a -PVP player under most circumstances.

There are certain areas in the game however where anyone can be killed. These areas are the arenas which are located in the cities and also an entire arena zone which is hidden away in Lake Rathetear. In these locations everyone is free game however you can not be looted and no experience will be lost.

Sometimes even -PVP players will want to settle their differences or spar with others. This can be accomplished by dueling. You can challenge other players to a duel and if they accept you can fight until the death. If you do not wish to fight until one player is dead then the duel will be ended when one of the players runs to the nearest zone. The winners and losers of each duel will be broadcast to the entire world. Those fleeing through zones will be labeled in the broadcast as cowardly dogs!

One disadvantage of the game is that +PVP players can't have helpful spells cast on them from -PVP players. This caused many players who might otherwise go +PVP to remain -PVP and simply fight against the environment. For this very reason Sony has created a special PVP server where everyone is marked +PVP. If you do not wish to participate in player versus player combat it is recommended you choose one of the games other servers.

Artificial "Intelligence"

One of the largest shortcomings in other games was artificial intelligence or the lack thereof. In Ultima one could hit a monster run a few steps away and the monster would get stuck on an obstruction and simply stand there while you used your bow or spells to destroy him. Easy money and easy notoriety boosts. If you ran into trouble in a fight you could simply outrun any monster in the game. If you had to do a corpse recovery you could simply do an "Eric Dickerson" ducking and dodging monsters easily and getting them stuck onto obstacles.

You won't see much of this in Everquest. First off monsters often run as fast as you and some can actually outrun you. They hit hard when they hit you from behind and have a greater chance of stunning you(preventing you from moving). Monsters will rarely get stuck on obstacles. They will run when in danger and get help if need be. The artificial intelligence in Everquest is by far the best I can remember from any computer role playing game past or present.

That having been said the AI is far from perfect. Some NPC spellcasters will follow patterns of casting spells which can be used to your advantage. For example some shamans will insist on casting root and drowsy on every group member before casting anything else. If your group has high resistance that can make these easy prey. However these problems are being stamped out every day and they may not even exist by the time you read this review.

Spells Galore

One of the most impressive aspects of Everquest is it's large variety of spells. There are over 700 spells in the game and many spells are class specific. There is far more variety spell wise in this game than any of it's predecessors.

You can levitate, become invisible, root monsters in place, damage monsters with a variety of offensive spells(some which effect a wide area), cast a variety of healing and buffing spells, not to mention summoning pets or creating illusions.

The spell effects are very well done and quite varied throughout the game. There are several circles of magic some are more important to a class than others. For example a magician will specialize in conjuring and summoning beasts, weapons and equipment. To a magician conjuration will be an extremely important skill however divination will not be used very much.

Travel will be a bit more difficult in Everquest than other games. In Ultima you could mark a recall rune anywhere in the game and instantly teleport yourself or party members to that location. You would often have many different recall runes stored in the bank. In Everquest all pure casters can bind a spot(drop an anchor) and then gate back to it. However any other class will need to rely on a caster to bind them and they will only be allowed to be bound inside of a city. Once you are bound to a spot you will return there any time you die or gate and you can only have one bind spot at any given time.

There are only two different classes in the game who can teleport to other locations. These are druids and wizards. These classes will be able to teleport to specificed areas throughout the game and will be able to teleport their party members with them at higher levels. Wizards will also be able to teleport into alternate planes of existance. However you will never be able to mark spots all over the world and deep inside dungeons to instantly teleport back to that spot on a whim.

Another important spell is the ressurect spells. Clerics are able to raise players from the dead and restore some of that characters lost experience. Only high level clerics are able to accomplish this feat.

There are two ways to raise your spell skills. One is to practice by using the spells from that particular cirlce. The other way is by traning at your local guildmaster. Guildmasters allot you 5 training points per level and they can add up over time. You will want to save these until you need them. You never know if you will get that prized weapon or spell and if it will be of a type that you are not adept in.

The Price Of Death

One problem in online rpg's in the past was the price of death. In a game like Ultima if you died you lost no experience or skills. This was important because if you did pker's could quickly drop your skills in a night. The cost of death in Ultima was usually a loss of all items however. In UO this was not a huge problem as a full suit of platemail could be bought with the cash from an hour of adventure. In Everquest money is hard to come by and it would take you a month or so to buy a suit of plate. Let's not even get into the amount of time you would need to invest to get a nice collection of magic items.

In Everquest death will cause you to lose experience. If you just gained a level you are likely to lose that level as a form of punishment for dying. However your corpse will remain where you died until you are ressurected, until someone else loots your corpse for you(which requires you to give that player permission to loot your corpse), until you return and loot all the items off of it or for eight hours after which it will dissapear. This means that in the most cases you will not be forced to lose all the items which you were carrying due to death. The experience penalty and the travel time to get back to where you died however will keep you from taking unreasonable chances.

Notoriety And Reputation

Ultima online had one of the simplest and most exploitable notoriety systems that any game could have. If you killed evil stuff you became more good, if you killed good stuff you became more evil. Depending on how good or how evil you were you received titles. If you looted, robbed or attacked a "good" person it would make you more evil. There were many problems with this. Players would run through your fire walls and flag you as grey then attack you and be able to PK you. If your friends hit them it made them evil. Fact of the matter is half of the "good" players in the game were in actuality evil or pkers.

In Everquest there are literally hundreds of different factions. Depending on the race and class that you start as your factions will be different. For example ogres and trolls will be killed on sight by most other races. Necromancers and rogues are often not killed by their own races(or the allies of their race) but they will often not be sold or dealt with. With hard work and a lot of patience you can raise your factions with any other group by killing their enemies or doing quests for them. Keep in mind however raising your faction with one group often entails lowering it with another.

This is one of the more interesting aspects of the game. You must choose your friends and associates wisely. There will always be enemies lurking in the distance. For example if you are a noble paladin of freeport your mission will be to seek out and kill the corrupt freeport militiamen. These militiamen are actually the guards of the city. In killing them you are going to make things rough on yourself as the guards will kill you on sight. Even by doing quests for the paladins guild in freeport you will slowly start losing favor with the freeport militia.

New Mountains To Climb

By far the largest shortcoming of Ultima Online was the lack of purpose. After a while you were reduced to either running around and killing innocents or running around and killing those who killed innocents. It simply became boring to kill the same mindless monsters with little danger and little reward. The treasures were always similar and the magic items were nothing special. You never wanted to become too attached to any item because it would likely be lost on your next death. More importantly there was no real option to killing(except at early stages when you practiced your trade). There were very few quests and they all involved take npc x to place y or something similar to this.

Everquest is a long way from Brittania. There are over 650 quests in the game at press time. Many of these quests are small tasks such as take item x to npc y similar to Ultima and have little reward so it seems. However the reward is often what is going on behind the scenes. As you do these quests npc's will change the way they look at you and you will raise factions(or lower them) by completing various quests. They will also open the doors on more powerful quests in addition to giving small cash or item rewards. The more difficult quests will grant powerful magic items or spells which make the quest worth the effort.

Questing isn't your thing? Have no fear. There are monsters in dungeons which carry nice magical items that you are going to want to claim as your own. The large variety of different magic items, many of which have unique and useful effects, will keep you busy even after level 50 trying to add the next piece to your collection. The main gripe with the magic items placed on "named npcs" is that we know which NPC's carry these items and can keep killing them over the course of days until they carry it. This causes what is known as item camping and is one of the weak spots of the game.

Fear not my friends for many of the most powerful and rare magic items are actually found on rare npc's which will spawn from time to time in the countryside. You will probably want to bring a ranger, bard or a druid along to track for these NPC's as they only spawn once a day or so and there will only be one at a time usually even in very large zones. The items on these NPC's are often worth the effort of killing them if you can find them however and because of how rarely they pop you will find very few other players who will have the same item as yourself.

Perhaps the thing that will most keep your attention is the wide level of difficulty of the monsters. In Ultima by the time you reached grandmaster status you could more or less kill any monster in the game and usually make it out alive. In Everquest you will find that each level you gain will simply bring more monsters that you can fight for experience. I remember back in mid september when I seen a level 17 character looting his corpse asking him, "You still die at level 17?". His reply was, "Yea I fought tougher monsters." Well even at level 50 you will still find the game a challenge because there are many monsters in the game who rank higher than level 50. These monsters will require a well balanced group of characters of the highest levels to conquer and will still be quite a risk indeed. However the reward on these monsters goes beyond bragging as they will carry the ultimate prizes in terms of items.

Around level 30 you will find it a neccesity to find groups if you want to make the most out of the game. The monsters at these levels are very tough to solo and it's usually better to have a group to mow through them. This is what promotes group play and sparks friendships in the game. After all this is a team game...

No Game Is Perfect

All that having been said no game is perfect. Everquest is not without it's share of problems and we will attempt to describe them here.

As we stated earlier some of the textures are a bit cartoonish and indoor zones suffer from low polygon counts. What we haven't mentioned is that music is only available for sound font compatible sound cards. These leaves a whole slew of users without music. Hopefully Sony will remedy this problem sometime in the near future.

I am a big fan of group play. I have spent a lot of my time in the game solo and find that I have the most fun in a group. However there will be some times when you will not be able to find a group or will be doing something where you are better off solo. The main gripe here is that after level 30 or so there are very few classes that are viable solo. Wizards remain viable when fighting one target at a time because of their ability to dish a lot of damage in a hurry, but they will have to rest after the fight. Clerics and necromancers will be able to solo some undead beasts due to their strength versus undead beings. Most other classes will find it very difficult to solo at all past level 30. It is still possible but it will be much less efficient than playing in a group. Melee classes will be able to solo some things but will then be forced to rest for obscene amounts of time while they heal.

Another problem is what leads some players to call the game(tongue in cheek) EverRest. After each battle you will need to regain your hit points and mana before you continue. In pen and paper and single player rpg's you could simply rest and time would instantly go by. In Everquest you will need to sit and rest as a warrior(or get healed by a priest type) and sit and meditate as a spellcaster. It takes much longer for a warrior to restore his hit points which makes them totally unviable at higher levels. It will take a level 40+ warrior over 30 minutes to regain himself back to full hit points. It will only take a spellcaster 5-10 minutes even at high levels to regain back to full mana but you will be unable to see any of the action during this time as you will have a spellbook in your face.

The last major problem is with camping. After a while most players will know where the good magic items are in dungeons. Players will begin camping these items waiting for them to appear on an NPC. The NPC's that carry them don't always spawn and when they do they don't always have the item. The problem is that if another party is already there camping for it you might have walked 20-30 minutes only to find that your out of luck. Even worse there is anti-camping code implemented into the game. If you are resting too close to a monsters spawn point the desired monster will not appear and a lower level monster(without magic items) will appear in it's place. This is a problem as you can't control where other players go and they won't always be happy if you kindly ask them not to go near a monsters spawn location.

The Verdict

Regardless of the aforementioned problems Everquest is the funnest online game I have ever played. I have been addicted to online games in the past beginning with Quake. I was utterly addicted to Ultima Online for about 3 months until I realized that it was a shallow game and I quickly became bored of the title. However I have been playing Everquest for six and a half months and I must say that I have never become bored with the game. There is always another mountain to climb and beast to conquer.

The only thing I must warn you off about is the addiction. Everquest fans are like crack addicts. In the four days since the beta ended and this review was written I can't stop thinking about the game so I wind up writing about it in the meantime. I have dreamt about Everquest in my sleep and I have thought strategies over in my mind. In the beta when the server would go down for upgrades the chat rooms would be filled with people simply waiting and chatting. It was not uncommon for people to sit in the chat room for hours waiting for the server to go back online so they could get another fix. Players often will play into the wee hours of the morning when they have to be at work in a couple of hours. The only warning I can give about this game is not to buy it if you don't have a lot of spare time on your hands.
