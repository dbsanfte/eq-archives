Becoming A Jeweler

So you have decided that you want to become a jeweler, eh? Well, with the additition of the new expasions to the game, such as Karnors and Velious, jewel crafting is a little more simple than it used to be, as far as ease of finding resources.

Other Jeweling Text Resources:
Foramen's Jewelery Chart
Foramen's Jewelery Trivial Chart

Below is a list of cities that you can buy all of the items involved in jewel crafting.

Evils: (Troll, Ogre, Dark Elf)
Neriak

Goods: (High Elves, Dwarves, Half Elves, Wood Elves, Gnomes, Humans, Halflings, Erudites)
Akanon, Fironia Vie, Qeynos and Kelethin

Lizards:
Cabilis

Once you have chosen the city to start in, all you need to do is print out the trivial chart, buy a jewel crafting bag and go to one of these cities and find the jeweler's shop. Some of the cities don't have all the jewelery resources in the same shop, so you may have to travel a bit.

Now, to raise your skill, just combine the items listed on the trivial chart starting with silver malachite. Just grab a silver bar and a malachite gem and put them both in the jeweler's kit and hit the combine button, viola! If your skill is good enough, you will make the item. If not, you will fail and get nothing for your efforts. Just start working down the list. Normally, the closer the trivial level of the jewelery is to your current skill, the more times you will successfully make the item, thus making your overhead less, since you can sell the items back to vendors for almost what you paid for them, assuming you have great faction and good charisma.

I would suggest having a very high INT for any tradeskill. Having a high INT will cause you to gain skill faster.

I wouldn't bother with enchanting the metal, you can get skill gains no matter if the metal is enchanted or not and if you don't enchant them, you can just sell the items back to the vendor and make 99% of your money back, but if you insist on enchanting the metal to make a profit, here is the location of where you can purchase the enchant spells.

Enchant Silver: Neriak - in the Library
Enchant Electrum: Neriak - in the Library
Enchant Gold: Neriak - in the Library
Enchant Platinum: Neriak - in the Library
Enchant Velium: Thurdagin, in the Rogue's Guild

For the best cost effect, once you make it to Silver Opal, I would suggest skipping up to Electrum Malachite. You will make the item less often and you will be working with those two pieces for a lot longer, however, it will be much easier to buy malachite from the vendor, then it would be to track down the more rare gems and each failure would cost you much less since the average rare gems starts at around 60 plat and can go up to around 1500 plat!

With 200 INT, I was able to make 3500 plat into a 200 jewel crafting skill. Just remember, trade skills are not limited by levels, I know for a fact that you can be level 1 and have a 200 in jewel crafting. As with all other skills, you have to have the kunark expasion to be able to make it to 250 jewelcrafting and I am not sure if 201-250 skill is limited by the normal level of 50. I am currently at 200 crafting and level 22 and I have yet to get a skill gain in jewel crafting, but I also haven't spent a lot of time trying either. I will update this guide as soon as I find out what the truth is.

I hope that this guide is easy to follow and helps those that are attempting the life of a jeweler! It's an expensive trade, but well worth it in my opinion!
