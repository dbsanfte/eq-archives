Iksar Necro Skullcaps
This quest was submitted by: Ralph.
Additional information by: Basiliskus

These quests begin in Cabilis. They are similar to the monk headband and sash quests of Qeynos and Freeport in that you progress one skullcap at a tmie.

To start the necro quests, visit the level just below the harbinger on the ramps.  (Harbinger is who you take your newbie note to in the Tower of Death)

The master's name is Xydoz, he's right next to the 3 floating brain jars.

When you speak with him he tells you of the first rank apprentice cap if you complete his quest.   His quest is to gather 4 sarnak hatchling brains, a frequent drop from the Sarnak hatchlings, easily do-able for a lvl 4 necro.  Gather the four brains, run to him and hand them all over at once.   You get exp, faction and the First rank apprentice skullcap.   It is NEC/IKSAR  with 2 AC.  (no other bonuses)   It looks just like the initate's helms outside the temple.

You must now ask him ab out the second rank skullcap,  he doesnt volunteer the info when you get the first.    He now asks for a "faded tapestry" and you must tell him "I am adept in tailoring" in order to get the next part.....the Sarnak hatchlings have this item, and you must get both halves and sew them back together.

You must kill Sarnak Hatchlings (the same who drop brains) and fetch the "torn" and "ripped" tapestries, put them in a small sewing kit and combine.   I had used 15 practices in tailoring and did this just fine.

Return to Master Xydoz and hand him the tapestry,  you get some cash, a bit of exp (at lvl 4 it filled me half up to next lvl!) and the second rank skullcap.   (3ac, otherwise same as 1st rank.)

Downstairs Master Rixiz has the third Rank skullcap.

Thanks to Shilarva of Fennin Ro for submitting information on the third skullcap:

To complete the quest you need a sarnak crypt raider brain, which is a lvl 10-11 spawn that come up along the lake of ill omen, and embalming fluid.

The kicker is:  a) you can not DIE while holding the brain.  (if you get any brains, and die, you lose it,unless its in bank.  Its not NO RENT, it doesnt say it on there, but I've lost a brain every time I died.)  b) embalming fluid had never been made before, and there were two recipes on how to make the stuff needed for the fluid.

To make embalming fluid you need:

1 rubbing alcohol. 1 bone granite powder and 2 bog juices.  a amauter brewer should be able to make it.

You ask the mortician how to make bone granite powder he says:  as it sounds, 2 of one, a pile of the other, hammered in heat until it is one.

Translation: Combine 2 Bones (seperately), the Granite Pebble
and a Forge Hammer (bought at the warrior guild merchant next to the forge) and combine in a Forge.

If you ask the alchemist NPC how to make bone granite powder. (see other quest on bone granite powder) he says:  using you skill of alchemy, combined three piles of bones from decaying skeletons, and 1 pile of granite pebbles.

Now...no one has made the bone granite powder....but I finally got it done.  The hard part is to get the granite pebbles.  You need two froglok escapee shackles.  With a skill of 20 in smithing, I made the powder on my first try.  It said it was trivial, but when i messed up and lost the powder it took me three more tries to make it.  The bone granite powder *IS* NO DROP, NO RENT, LORE.

Because the bone granite powder is no rent we must recommend that you have your blacksmithy and brewing to 20 or more before creating it as you do not wish to lose all the work you have put into it.

Anyway, combining JUST the rubbing alcohol, 2 bog juices and the powder, it gives you a sack of embalming fluid.   The good thing is if you fail, you dont lose the bone granite powder. (in case you were wondering) but you lose everything else.

Combining the fluid and a sarnak crypt raider brain in the canopic jar, it formed the preserved sarnak brain I am sending you....handing over the 2nd rank cap and the jar gave me the third rank cap I am sending also.   The third picture is how the iksar look wearing the cap.   As a side note: you can hand in JUST the jar, and then the cap, or vice versa and still get it, or both at once.
