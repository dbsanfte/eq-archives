Warrior's STINK.

Okay I've sat down and written this little thingie over the past few weeks, in places it repeats itself, you may even find it
incoherant in places. And hell I'm to lazy to load it up in a real word processor to run a spell check (go notepad!). But
take it as it is, I think it still has something important to say:

I play a level 46 troll warrior in EverQuest. In most every game I play I'm always a warrior and fairly happy with the role
and abilities warriors are given in most games, wether it be an online or offline game. Now when I was making my troll
warrior I did a little research and heard things like this:

- Warriors get more hitpoints than any other class.
- Warriors at high levels do more damage than any other melee class.
- Warriors have the best selection of magic and mundane weapons and armor.
- Warriors are the most skilled at weapon-to-weapon combat.

Now some of these are things people take for granted about the sterotypical role of a warrior others are conclusions that can
be made after reading comments made by Verant about warriors (wish I'd cliped the quotes to put here).

So for the first few levels I was pretty happy with the warrior, with a trolls regeneration you can quickly get out of the
lower levels. My first observation of an imbalance was that all other melee classes were advancing just as fast in weapon
skills, with 5 additional points of offense and weapon skill each level. So that a level 10 Shadow Knight and a level 10
Warrior would both have the same weapon and offense skills (assuming they're using the same type of weapon). So I'm saying
to myself what's the point of a warrior? Our melee skills are not outshining anyone.

So life goes on, now I'm around level 20 or so and still I haven't found any equipment that puts warriors above others, our
skills are still on par with anyone else. So what about the duel wield, double attack issue? Well by this level rangers get
them both, monks get them both (with a higher skill level than any class), thieves get them both. And so well warriors get
them both. So again I begin to question the point of warriors.

So now I start comparing hitpoints. Well rangers don't have as many hitpoints.. but they can buff themselves and heal
themselves.. monks don't have as many hitpoints but they can mend themselves (and don't tell me, oh but if it fails they get
hurt bad, cuz I group regularly with a monk since level 37 and I have yet to see him fail a mend)... ShadowKnights don't have
as many hitpoints but they have HP drains... Paladin's don't have as many hitpoints but they can buff, heal and lay hands..
So in the end I actually end up having LESS hitpoints than most any other melee class because after buffs and considering
healing and life drains the other melee classes (except rogues) have more HP than me. But being a troll with a high stanima
I get a few extra hitpoints here and there which add up to around 150 more HP over other melee classes (at level 46 mind
you). At level 46 this accounts for maybe two additional hits from a monster worth XP. So a warrior's benefit is not in
it's hitpoints.

So what about armor, they can wear most anything right, so they should have extra high AC? Well not quiet, even tho rangers
can't wear plate, they can wear most magical versions of it, and they have AC buffs. Paladins, ShadowKnights, Bards can all
wear plate also (man the acoustics of playing an instrument in full platemail must be odd). And of course these classes ALSO
have AC buffs! Then there are monks, well they can't wear much armor.. but the lighter they are the more AC they get! So
again I'm left out saying, well what's a warrior good for?

So someone tells me, well most melee classes skills cap at level 30 and warriors keep on going. Well level 30 rolls around,
and other melee classes keep right up with me. So maybe it's level 35 they cap, nope. Well maybe they cap at level 40 and
warriors can goto above 200. Well level 40 rolls around and yes other melee classes do cap at 200, well warriors do ALSO!
UGH. I've held out so long waiting for some benefit of warriors to shine through, kept getting told at higher levels you
really shine! Well I'm at the higher levels and if anything WARRIORS are the WORST melee class beyond level 40 (gauging my
experience with grouping with high level Rangers, Monks and ShadowKnights). In fact Monks don't cap at 40! They can go
above 200! But my Parry, Riposte, Pierce, 1h Slash, 2h slash, taunt, bash, ALL of them maxed at 200!
