The Ultimate Starting-Out Guide: CLASS TYPES
Pure Melee

 There are three pure melee classes in the game: warrior, monk and rogue.  They are what many people consider the “bread and butter” of the game because they are pretty much a necessity for any serious task a group wants to accomplish in the game.  They all have a fairly unique set of skills, which they can use in various situations.  On top of that they all have their various strengths and weaknesses making the choice between them a difficult one.  They are classes that do a lot of damage up-close and personal whether it be in hand to hand combat, slashing with swords or piercing with daggers.

 Priest

 The Priests in the game consist of three rather distinct classes:  Shaman, Druid and Cleric.  The speciality of each of these classes in the game is healing, and this is their main function.  However, clerics surpass the other two classes as the best healers in the game making them essential.  Druids and Shamans make up for this with other areas of strength, and both tend to be more aggressive classes than the cleric.  All of the priest classes are also excellent buffers and between them they can buff almost any aspect of a character from running speed, to hit points to attack speed.  These classes are popular in the lands of Norrath as they are excellent additions to make a group much stronger, as well as to give the group superior durability.  The mana pool for these classes is determined by the wisdom stat, making it very important to them.

 Caster

 These guys are the classes that rely almost entirely on their magical abilities to keep themselves alive and well.  These classes are the Enchanters, Magicians, Necromancers and Wizards of Norrath.  However they all use their magic in very different ways ranging from powerful attacks, which do extremely high amounts of damage, to spells that “freeze” the opponent removing them from battle.   These casters have their mana pool based on intelligence making that their most important statistic in general.  However their magical strength is hindered by their extreme physical weakness.  They can hardly wear any armour and wield even fewer weapons.  In fact most casters after level twenty or so do not even use a weapon until they find one that gives superior stats to a held item.

 Hybrid

 As the name of this category suggests, the hybrids consist of several different classes mixed together.  They are the Paladin, the Shadowknight, the Ranger and the Bard.  The Paladin is a mix between a cleric and a warrior, a Shadowknight is the mix between a necromancer and a warrior, and the ranger is a mixture between a druid and a warrior.  Bards are a special case.  They have many parts that make up their class and have a unique “magic system” of songs.  They would be made up of part rogue, part druid, and part enchanter if you had to combine classes.  The hybrid classes also all have their own various abilities, which few or no other classes possess.  In general, hybrids are approximately 75% melee and 25% caster or priest.  Bards could be considered 50% caster, 50% melee but that depends on the playing style of the bard.
