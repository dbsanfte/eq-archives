Who Controls the Story? Posted Wednesday, January 31, 2001 by jcsmith
Gamespy has put up an article by the name of Personal Space: Who Controls the Story. This article takes a look at the MMORPG asks the question, "Who should control the story?" Should it be the players or the game's creators? Geoffrey Zatkin(Everquest) and Matthew Ford(Asheron's Call) were among those spoken to. Here's GZ paraphrased on the MMORPG...

In those games, the "player is not the center of the universe, merely a participant in it and everyone has to be a member of the universe with about equal priority." This is challenging, as you are "not designing the game around the actions or what you think will happen with one person," unlike a single-player game, which tends to have a main character. Thus, the purpose of the designer of a MMORPG is to create the framework--basically a "huge, chat room world"--although Zatkin believes that the type of "world is basically irrelevant."

I also wanted to clear up any confusion that you might have had regarding the Advanced Search update that Foramen has underway. There are two different databases types that we support in addition to our normal(classic) equipment listings.

The first type is the Compendium. It comes in three flavors(Armor, Miscellaneous, Weapons). These listings include information on the zone and the NPC that drops the item and crosslink to our classic display listings which many people prefer. The weapon and miscellaneous versions of these databases are completely up to date. The armor version of the compendium is about a week behind currently but we are working on catching it up.

The advanced search is an alternate database that we supported until some time in early Kunark. It allowed for more detailed search filtering than the compendium but the layout was viewed by some as beingn more cryptic. At that time I began to fall so far behind in keeping the site up to date(with a massive supply of emails) that I decided to cease updating the Advanced Search database. It had remained in this form but online until recently. Foramen has taken it upon himself to catch these databases up to date and they should soon be another viable option here at EQ'Lizer.
