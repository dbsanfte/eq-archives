Intelligence Guide
Intelligence is the primary skill of pure casting types(magicians, wizards, necromancers, and enchanters). It is also an important skill for Shadowknights(for their spell side of things) and can be beneficial for other classes as well. What makes this so?

Well first off for the pure casters intelligence directly affects the amount of mana you have. More mana equals more spells before you have to sit and med and that extra bit of mana could save your life more than once. Often when players create casters they pour points into stamina (for hit points) or agility (for armor class) figuring their intelligence is already high enough. In my opinion this is a big mistake. Although these statistics are important at lower levels(when you will actually be meeleeing with your opponents more times than not) they are obsolete after level 12 or so. At higher levels you aren't going to want to let the monsters get anywhere near you and if they do that extra hit point per level isn't going to last long when you are getting smacked for 96 a pop. Pour it all into mana and when it maxes out use the leftover 5 points to beef up your agility or stamina or whatever you suits your preference.

Intelligence can also be beneficial(though not overly important) for other classes. Intelligence directly affects how quickly you learn your skills.  This is important especially at higher levels where skills become harder to raise. Characters with high intelligence will often have their skills maxed out for their level early on where characters with low intelligence may not max at all.
