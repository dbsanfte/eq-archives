Twinkle Twinkle Little Twink
This editorial was submitted by: Trinist Shadowisper

Twink - low level player equipped with high level items.

We've all seen them - the lvl 5 Paladin with the Ghoulbane, MM shield and Full Plate;   the lvl 10 Wizard with a SMR;  the lvl 17 Ranger with dual Ebony Blades and full Ivy Etched armor - it's how we view the Twinks of Norrath which I am addressing.

There are several basic viewpoints on twinks and twinking which I can
summarize into three basic reactions I've seen.  Admiration.  Jealousy.   Digust.

The first is becomming more common, as the players of EQ universally level up, and more and more people create Twinks of their own.  The admiration comes from the knowledge of what it must have taken that person in camp time, trades, and platinum to give their secondary character such nice items.  A good twink takes alot of effort, and when well done is quite impressive to see.

The second is generally held by those who simply don't have a higher level character but wish that they did.  It's not uncommon to see people with this attitude begging for items or coin.  I've found the best way to deal with this is to remind them that the begging skill is for
NPC's.  A complaint that often accompanies this view is "Damn power leveling twinking @#$#@#$, I'm not a geek like you, I can't play 24-7".  Verant's handy Ignore command works well here, as the Have Nots will always complain about those who have more.

The digust attitude is a bit more understandable - the basis here is that
Twinks are a form of cheating.  Giving the high level weapons and armor to a low level character can be seen as bypassing the natural progression of leveling that should occur.  EQ is most closely based on the old text base pen and paper RPG.  When you create a new character in D&D, you don't get to start them out with the best weapons - cheating is self defeating and robs you of the fun of the game!  The whole point of the old role playing games was adventure -  discovering the weapons and loot as you went along, not making
an invincible diety that can easily defeat all foes.  No risk =  No excitement!

However, given the limitations of the graphics based RPG world that EQ is, Verant has not been able to keep up with player progress.  Lvl 50 was reached much more   quickly than anyone could have anticipated.  And what do you do you do now, if you have reached level 50, killed the dragons mulitiple times, killed the gods multiple times, acquired
all of your class specific armor, done every quest you could find?  Well,
the obvious answer is to create a new character!  And having put so much time in to EQ - the above mentioned would be nothing short of months and months of work - what truly   valid reason can be put forth that you should NOT give your new character nice items.  If anything,
you deserve to have nice items for your second, third, fourth ect. characters.   Besides, it's just plain fun!

Verant has taken certain measures to make sure this doesn't go too far in extremes.   No drop armor on the planes won't be gotten until you are 46+.  Weapons don't proc until your level is high enough.  You need faction with races and guilds before you can start some quests.

But where does twinking go too extreme - where does the role playing really end, and plain twink mania come in?  How about taking your level 1 char all the way to 20 without killing a single mob, by repeatedly doing some quest like Tumpy's Tonics?  It's really just a form of buying experience, which to me really defeats the purpose of the game, which is adventuring.  But it could be argued that having already made lvl 50 there is nothing new about leveling the standard way, and it is simply a faster way to get your new char to a level where you can group with your friends.

Whatever view you take on twinks, they are always going to exist, and are going to increase in number.   I do suggest making one before you go too harsh the criticism.  If you keep playing EQ, it's rather unavoidable that you will reach lvl 50, and you may find yourself wanting to make a new character, and wouldn't it be nice if they had a mithril plated harmonica, or a flux bladed toe nail clipper...
