EQ'Lizer Chat
October 1st --

A lot of people seem to have caught the news that the Temple of Solusek Ro has been added for download on the test server and would be coming to our live servers in the somewhat near future. I've received quite a few mails about this zone so I decided to explore it on the test server and go ahead and release a bit of information.

First off I would like to say that this zone is a fresh new idea for Verant and i'd like to congradulate them on making something new and interesting for the game. It's a zone different than any of the others in the game and that is a good thing.

Next I wanted to let everyone know what the zone is not. This zone is not really a zone targetted for adventuring though I suppose players could come here and destroy everything. I wouldn't recommend it though. Especially not until you have had a chance to explore the place and complete the quests available for your particular class.

Now to what this zone is. This is a zone chock full of quest giving NPC's. The entire zone is non aggressive. There appear to be NPC's here who give class specific quests for a variety of classes. I could not find NPC's for every class. Not sure if there are only certain classes who get the quest treatment here, if the quests for certain classes were not in yet, or I simply wasn't deft enough to find the NPC's for every class(a very real possibility). =)

For example you might find a certain NPC gives quest items for magicians. He might have three or four quests which he will grant. Most of these quests require you to travel far and wide for the items. For example one quest might require an item from Solusek A, another from Permafrost, and yet another from Steamfont. Most of these quests appear to be low to high level quests, though a few of these are quests that could be done by low level characters.

I think this is a very interesting zone. It is a zone that will give players a reason and a purpose. This is important considering the faction problems of many of the pre-existing quests(for example a troll shadowknight is not going to be able to do quests for any other shadowknight guild in the game without signifigant work, and in some cases not even then).

What it is not is a cure for crowding problems. We will have to wait for the addition/fixing of other zones to help alleviate crowding. This zone is definately a step in the right direction however.

I posted two screenshots of the zone. They aren't great screenshots but will give you an idea of what to expect from the visuals here.
-J.C.
