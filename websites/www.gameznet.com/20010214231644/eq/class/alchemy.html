**The trivial chart is a bit out of whack and most of it is word of mouth.  Even so, it will give you a good idea of what potions to 
make next to raise your skill.

First of all, Alchemy is a Shaman only skill gained at Level 25.  For the beginning Alchemist who may want to experiment (an expensive
addiction to say the least), first pump all the training points into it you can.  This will save big cash in the long run.  Buy a medicine
bag and watch for these four messages when trying to combine herbs in it:

1)  You combine the ingredients to make something new.  (This is a good thing...)

2)  Those ingredients don't combine in that quantity to make anything (Not so good.  You just wasted your hard earned plat!  Check
your potion ingredients.)

3)  You haven't the skill...  (You might though, try it a few times and see if you get a success.)

4)  This potion is trivial to make.  (Make something harder to continue raising your skill!)

If you look at the above chart carefully, you will see that certain herbs effect specific stats.  For instance, Lucerne boosts Strength
while Sage Leaf boosts Stamina.  Some herbs have a catalyst effect.  That is, they increase the strength of a potion itself.  For
instance, Blue Vervain Bulb will change some Lesser Potions like Lesser Accuracy into a stronger one (accuracy).   While Valerian Root
will make it stronger still (Greater Potion of Accuracy.)

A quick word about intelligence and wisdom.   The higher your intelligence is, the faster you will raise your skill.  I've heard it
recommended that you shouldn't try to train in a skill without a minimum of 100 Int.   For us Shamen, that's not always easy to
acquire.  So, an investment in int raising items will pay off in the long run.

Wisdom controls the success in making an item.   When you are trying to make potions to sell and you want the most bang for your
plat, get your wisdom as high as possible first.  That way, hopefully, you will fail less.

Int for skill raising, Wisdom for success.   Simple, right?  Most of the lower level potions aren't worth much and you will end up either
wasting them or selling them to a vendor at a fraction of what it cost you to make.   So, int can't be stressed enough at the lower
levels.

And that's all the tips and tricks I've learned so far.  Hopefully you will be as excited as I am to finally get our skill working.   Right
now it's not profitable, but you can bet once our appetites for Alchemy are whetted, Verant will have to bring the skill more into
balance and make it profitable.  For now, SoW, Dmg Shields, Shrink, Invis to Undead and Levitate (which still requires bat wings to
use!) seem to be my biggest sellers.  At first don't try to make a profit at it.   Instead focus more on cutting your losses and sell the
potions a bit cheaper than they cost to make.  For instance, most people aren't going to pay 40PP for a SoW potion when they can
usually get one for free.  So, forget the three failures you had and focus on selling them for around 15-20PP.  Hey, at least you get
SOME money back while you raise your skill.
