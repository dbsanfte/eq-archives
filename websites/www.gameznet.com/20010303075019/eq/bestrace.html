The Ultimate Starting-Out Guide: RACE

There are thirteen distinct races in the lands of Everquest.  They all have strengths and weaknesses as well as different relations with the other races in the game.

HUMANS - The Human race is the race that all others are measured by.  They start in either Freeport or Qeynos, which are two of the biggest cities in the game.  The available classes for them are Bard, Cleric, Druid, Enchanter, Magician, Monk, Paladin, Necromancer, Ranger, Rogue, Shadowknight, Warrior and Wizard.  You will note that this is every class in the game except for the Shaman.  The main advantage of the human race is that it essentially has no weaknesses.  They can be almost any class, and do fairly well as any class.  There are no gaping holes in their starting statistics, making them ideal for the first time player who does not have a great deal of insight into where to specialize.  They are also one of only two races in the game that can be the monk class, and they are generally considered the best monks because of their harder hitting hands and superior balance.  This race has no experience penalty.  Faction wise, most humans can enter most cities with the exceptions of Grobb (Troll), Ogukk (Ogre), Cabalis (Iksar), Paineel (Evil Erudite) and Neriak (Dark Elf).  This is not true under some conditions – generally if your character is considered evil due to her class or deity.  It should also be noted that humans do not have any form of night vision.

 BARBARIANS – The Barbarians are the rugged humans of the north, who have developed their bodies while sacrificing their mental abilities.  They start in Halas, which is perhaps the most remote city in Everquest proper (the world of Everquest  before the expansions).  The available classes to the barbarians are limited to the Shaman, Warrior and Rogue.  You will note that they are one of the four races that can be shamans and are the only “good” race that is able to be one (as opposed to evil).  They are the strongest of the good classes as well and therefore are naturally suited to be warriors.  They can also be excellent rogues because of their high strength (the harder you stab somebody’s back the more it hurts evidently).  Their weakness is in their mental and social skills.  They start with inferior intelligence and charisma, but this is balanced by their superior strength and stamina.  Stat-wise they are the most neutral of the “big” races.  The race has no experience penalty and has similar faction restrictions to their human cousins.   They also share their optical deficiencies with their cousins, having no night vision whatsoever.

 ERUDITES – Erudites are simply the smartest race on the face of Norrath.  They left their inferior cousins, the humans, to start a new life on the island of Odus, and founded their home city of Erudin.  Evil Erudites begin in the dark city of Paineel, which is hidden somewhere on the island, and was founded by the dark sorcerer Miragul.  The classes available to them are Cleric, Enchanter, Magician, Necromancer, Paladin, Shadowknight and Wizard.  They are best suited to be one of the four mage-classes, as those classes are intellect based (the stat in which Erudites excel), however they loose out due to their very low physical skills.  Most noticeably lacking are strength and stamina.  The race has no racial experience penalty.  Faction for Erudites is split into the good and evil ones.  Those who live in Erudin are generally accepted in most cities in the game following the same basic rules as their human cousins.  Those of Paineel are only allowed into the dark cities of Neriak and sometimes Grobb and Ogukk.   Cabalis is hostile to even them – the Iksars are not a particularly friendly bunch of lizards.  Once again, they are blind at night like their cousins.

 HALF ELVES – This is the crossbreed between humans and elves – they struggle to belong in a world where neither society fully accepts them, yet neither rejects them completely either.  They can start in a great number of cities depending on other choices they make.  Felwithe, Kelethin, Freeport and Qeynos can all be what a half elf might call home.  The classes available to them are the Bard, Druid, Ranger, Rogue and Warrior.  Half Elves have a lot of strengths because of their mixed blood.  Perhaps their most important strength (especially if you are a bard) is that they always look mighty good.  They are without a doubt the coolest-looking class while wearing plate armour.  Those seeking to look good sometimes refer them to as the “masters of plate”.  They also have fairly balanced stats, with above average agility.  This is balanced, however, by their less-than-desirable wisdom deficiency, which makes wisdom-based classes more difficult to play as a half-elf.   They also get infravision that helps them see in the dark.  This is especially helpful at night in the depths of the Feydark.  Their faction is very similar to that of their distant Human relatives.  The major difference is that they are greeted with open arms nowhere; however they are more neutral with some of the other races than humans are.  They have no racial experience penalty.

 WOOD ELVES – This is your standard run-of-the-mill elf.  They live with nature and have the innate ability to forage up food if they need it.  They are the commoner of the elves and start in the tree-city of Kelethin.  This is perhaps one of the most confusing and dangerous cities in the game.  There are many different platforms connected by bridges and planks, with no guardrails.  One wrong move and you may find yourself falling to your death.  Because of this innate danger, Kelethin is the home to many-a drunk racing competition.  The classes available are the Bard, Druid, Ranger, Rogue and Warrior.  They have exceedingly high agility and share the best wisdom for a druid-possible class with halflings, however their stamina, strength and intelligence are below par.  They have infravision and have no racial penalty.  They are welcome in most cities in the game except for the aforementioned evil ones, allowing them to travel and see the world with relative ease.

 HIGH ELVES - These are the nobility of the elven kingdom, and are considered the most egotistical race.  They view themselves as superior to pretty much every race other than the high men (Erudites).  They also wallow in their own beauty, having graceful bodies and pale complexions.  Their starting city is Felwithe, which is perhaps the most easy-to-navigate city in the game.  It is also one of the most aesthetically pleasing, with marble pillars and such.  Their strengths are in their higher than average intellect, their extremely high wisdom and their charming personalities (high charisma).  They also possess higher than average agility, and adequate dexterity.  This all makes them ideally suited as a caster-class, and they are among the best choices for Clerics and Enchanters.  However, all classes they can decide to be are very playable.  The only possible exception is the high elven paladins, which are challenging because of the race’s inherently weak strength and low stamina.  The classes available are Cleric, Enchanter, Magician, Paladin and Wizard.  They are welcomed in all good cities in the game, and even have a “second city” in Kunark, Firiona Vie.  On the down side, they are particularly hated in evil areas of the game, most of all by the dark elves, who are what you might consider their evil twins.  They, of course, have infravision, which helps them navigate the forests where they spend much of their time.

 DARK ELVES – Born from Innurok’s tortured souls of the kidnapped high elven king and queen, the Tier`Dal are the race to be for those who just love to be pure, unadulterated evil.  They worship Innurok as their father, and almost all Dark Elves have him as their deity.  They start in the eerie city of Neriak, which can be confusing until you get to know it.   They are an extremely intelligent class, being among the top three in this area, and also share the high agility and dexterity that their distant wood elf relatives enjoy.  However this is balanced out by their below-average strength and stamina, as well as their less than charming personalities.  They can be a fair number of classes:  Cleric, Enchanter, Magician, Necromancer, Rogue, Shadowknight and Wizard.  They do well in almost all of these classes, yet dark elf Shadowknights share the same problem that high elven paladins suffer from.  Their evil ways have caused them to be hated throughout the lands of Norrath, and they are safe only in their own evil realms.  At first, the only cities that will accept them away from home are Ogukk and Grobb.  They also have an outpost, much like the high elves, on Kunark.  Those Dark Elves who decide not to worship Innurok can gain partial trust in the nearby human city of Freeport.  However, they are in grave danger when on Feydwer, as they are the blood enemy of the light elves, the Feir`Dal.  Their night vision is superior to all other races – they possess ultravision, which grants them perfect night vision, albeit with a slightly purple tinge.

 GNOMES – The Gnomes are a fantastic race, which has mastered the art of mechanical creation.  They live on Feydwer, in their home of Ak’anon.  This marvellous city is a testament to their mechanical expertise, as they have robots called clockworks that help run the city for them.  There are guides who will show a traveler around the city and merchants who sell goods.  They also have a king who is inexplicably a very short human.  They are second to only the Erudites in intellect, and have melee skills that are just below par.  However they have terrible charisma as well as lower-than average wisdom, making them more suited to be a caster than a healer.  They are extremely short as well, making them interesting to play.  It is rather amusing to watch a level 60 gnomish warrior destroy an Ogre or Troll of a similar level in a duel.  Class-wise the Gnomes are able to be Clerics, Enchanters, Magicians, Necromancers, Rogues, Warriors and Wizards.  They are accepted in most cities in the game, and can even build up enough faction with the Dark Elves to be allowed in Neriak.  They possess infravision that helps them see in the dark.

 DWARVES – Strong, stout and sturdy little people, the Dwarves have a unique society, which hails honour, drinking games and facial hair.  Almost all dwarves indeed do have facial hair, including most women.  Only those who have been “corrupted” by the ways of the humans shave their hair.  As you might be able to guess, Charisma is one of the weaker points to a dwarf.  They live in the city of Kaladim, located in the Butcherblock Mountains on Feydwer, near the forests of the elves.  The city has many facilities for Dwarves including numerous pubs.  To their dismay, however, they still have fewer than Kelethin.  They have high starting strength, stamina and wisdom, and decent dexterity.  However they suffer with their extremely low charisma, and below average intellect and agility.  They can choose to be few classes: Cleric, Paladin, Rogue and warrior are the only disciplines that they can grasp.  They start with a sense heading ability of about forty, which allows them to navigate the world better than a typical newbie, and they also receive infravision like so many other races.  They are accepted in most towns throughout the world, and follow the same general “rules” as the humans, faction wise.  They are just more liked in Kaladim, and less in human towns. 

 HALFLING – Halflings are the hobbits of Norrath.  They live in their home city of Riverdale, which is crunched between the extremely dangerous Kithicor Forest and what is considered the best newbie zone in the game, Misty Thicket.  They are small, devious creatures who are very well suited to be rogues, with their above average strength, dexterity, wisdom and agility.  They, however, suffer from lower intellect and charisma.  They are allowed to be Clerics, Druids, Rogues and Warriors.  They are among the best Druids and Rogues in the game.  Faction-wise they are most hated by the dark elves.  They have set up camps of their weaker comrades near Neriak and are regularly slaughtered there.  They are allowed in most light cities in the game, however they are rarely trusted in them because of their rather mischievous nature.  They do have some innate racial abilities that help make up for this though – they are naturals at foraging and hiding, and they have infravision.

 TROLLS – Trolls can be best described with three simple words – big, ugly and green.  They are a simple society that has a natural speech impediment that many players are compelled to have fun with.  If you hear somebody takin lic dis den dey be eder troll or ogre.  They live in Grobb, which is a simple town.  Anything more complicated and the trolls would have a very difficult time navigating.  They are big and ugly, which inherently means that they are most likely strong, an assumption that is correct in this case.  They have the second highest strength and stamina in the game, which is balanced by their absolutely horrendous intellect, wisdom and charisma.  They are hated everywhere in the game except in the evil cities listed above.  They also have the advantage of faster-than normal regeneration, which makes them ideal for somebody who wants to solo, and be a melee class.  Class wise, only Shaman, Shadowknight and Warriors are open to them.  They are also blessed with infravision and the ability to “slam”.  They, however, have a racial experience penalty because of their regeneration.

 OGRES – Ogres are… big and ugly.  Although they aren’t green, they make up for this by being even bigger than trolls.  It is impossible to compare the degree of ugliness between these two races, as they are both disgustingly ugly in their own unique way.  The Ogres reside in the simple town of Ogukk.  Again, the town is simple because if it weren’t, the residents would get lost.  The main strength of the Ogre is just that:  Strength.  It is absurdly high, which makes them (arguably) the best warriors in the game.  They also have very high stamina (also the best in the game), which makes them able to tank better than other races.  This is balanced by their absurdly low intelligence and charisma, as well as their awful wisdom.  They do not have a natural regeneration, like the trolls do, which is what balances these two massive races out.  Their racial skills are a natural immunity to being stunned from the front, the ability to “slam,” as well as infravision.   Because of these other abilities they also have an experience penalty.  They are evil and thus hated everywhere except for the evil cities (once again, the “evil cities” exclude Cabalis where everyone that isn’t a lizard is hated).   They can be Shamans, Shadowknights and Warriors, and are good at all of these, especially warrior.

 IKSARS – Iksars are the lizard-race who make their home on the rediscovered island of Kunark.  They have kidnapped the high elven princess (otherwise known as the EQ-cover girl), which caused the other races to finally sail a little farther south and find Kunark.  Their home city is Cabalis and it has several exits that lead to different parts of Kunark.  This is the one city in the game that does not accept any visitors.  Everyone is marked kill-on-sight unless they are an Iksar.  It is possible to raise your faction high enough with these lizards to earn their trust and let you into their city, however the effort is simply not worth it as there are two other cities on Kunark where one can bind, shop, etc.  They can be Monks, Necromancers, Shadowknights, Shamans, and Warriors making them have a fairly diverse class set.  This is presumably because they have no allies so they have to be able to do many things on their own.  Their stats are fairly average, with advantages in agility, dexterity and wisdom.  Surprisingly this makes them suited to be very balanced Shadowknights, and arguably the best shamans in the game (they have the best starting wisdom of any shaman race).  They start with the advantages of a superior starting swimming skill and regeneration much like the Trolls.  However they cannot wear conventional plate armor for some odd reason (it hinders their scales or something like that) and they are hated everywhere they go.  They have an experience penalty and infravision.
