Darkforge Armor
This quest was submitted by: Zekris Shadowblaze

The following is a post I placed on Everlore's message boards regarding the Darkforge Armor quest, it's components, and advice regarding how to get each. Remember the that quest begins and ends at the Undead Knights at the Temple of Solusek Ro, and you will be spending a LOT of time in Cazic Thule.

I hunted in Cazic Thule for 12 levels (26 onward) in my persistant search for Darkforge. Here is some general advice regarding hunting Crusaders in CT and attaining the various other items required for the quests:

-When hunting in CT, your best bet for Crusaders are by the Ziggurat/Pyramid/Temple (same location, often called any of those) either on the ground outside of it, or camping the Archon and pulling from the lower levels of the temple. Crusaders will spawn where the other high lvl lizards spawn(Justicars, Zealots, Judicators). The top of the pyramid can be camped by a full group of low 30s with little difficulty (especially if you have an enchanter, as is often the case). The other place where high level Lizards spawn often is in the sewers (not the gator pools) which can be distinguished by the plank walkways over crocodile infested waters.

-As soon as you join the group, ALWAYS tell them that you are looking for Crusaders so you can loot pieces of decayed for quest armor. Remind them that it is No Drop. It is also often best to remind them nearing the end of a battle with a Crusader, in case someone decides to loot the corpse and grabs some decayed because "it looked like banded". Countless times i have had pieces of decayed that I needed looted off of Crusaders, only to have the person claim "I didn't know you were looking for it" or "I didn't see the message". Granted, sometimes this is actually the case, but more often then not they are getting the decayed to do the quest themselves (SKs are desperate for Darkforge, and everyone knows it). Of course, treachery is not prevalent in all groups, but if you're hunting in CT for an extended period of time, you should meet some of the same people and figure out who you can trust.

As a reminder, here are the components needed for each piece of Darkforge:

Darkforge Breastplate: Decayed Mail, Decayed Breastplate, 2 bars of enchanted platinum (not stacked)

Darkforge Helm: Decayed Helm, Decayed Visor, 2 Freeport Militia Helms (I believe they changed the item name on the helm, but if it was dropped by a dead FP guard, it is the right helm)

Darkforge Vambraces: Decayed Left Vambrace, Decayed Right Vambrace, Qeynos Kite Shield

Darkforge Bracer: Decayed Left Bracer, Decayed Right Bracer, Broken Minotaur Lord Horn (note, this makes one bracer, if you want 2 you need to do the quest once, turn in the items, then go back and do it again as all items required are NO DROP, LORE)

Darkforge Boots: Decayed Left Boot, Decayed Right Boot, 2 plies of Faerie Dust (unstacked, looks white, not yellow like pixie dust)

Darkforge Greaves: Decayed Left Legplate, Decayed Right Legplate, 2 bricks of Melatite

Darkforge Gloves: Decayed Left Glove, Decayed Right Glove, 1 bar of enchanted platinum

In regards to the various non-decayed pieces for the quests,here are a few small tips:

-When getting Faerie Dust (for DF Boots), it is best to hang around near Felwithe in Gfay and kill pixies and other small game until you see a Faerie spawn. They are usually a Faerie Royal Guard, Maiden, Princess, etc. Dust drops usually on 1 out of every 3 or 4 of them, so it shouldn't be too difficult. Remember, Faerie Dust is white, Pixie Dust is yellow, be wary if people try to sell it to you. I would also suggest resisting the urge to go to Lesser Faydark and camp the Pixie building. True, Faeries have a better potential to spawn here, but you risk running into a wandering Brownie, NPC druids who are TOUGH for their level (30-45). Your best bet is to be patient in Greater Fay.

-The Broken Minotaur Lord Horn (for DF bracers) is always dropped off of the Mino Lords in Steamfont. They are reasonably difficult, but if you are a good soloer you can take them alone at 33+, otherwise get another person around your level to help and they should be no trouble. One thing to watch for is that deep in the Mino caves (if you follow the left path) Mino Lords spawn and wander, dangerously close to lvl 35 gnome necro, Meldrath. Anyone who has ever fought NPC necros around their level before knows that unnecessarily engaging one is generally not the best idea. Remember that the Minotaur Hero is different from the Lord and a much higher level mob (mid to high 40s i believe).

-Freeport Militia Helms (for DF helm) can be gotten rather easily by sneaking into East Freeport and waiting for the guard that walks by the dock (provided no one is camping the guards by the gates). He is pretty low, and is far enough away from other guards that, as long as you have him darknessed and kill fairly quickly, he won't aggro any others.

-Qeynos Kite Shields (for DF vambraces)can either be attained by killing Qeynos guards (hard way) or by giving a low level 10pp to go inside the city and buy one off a vendor for you (sell for 5-8pp last time I checked). The shield you require is indeed a Qeynos Kite Shield (not a wooden kite shield, or any other form).

-Melatite (for DF Greaves) is found on the clockworks in Sol A. People often are selling melatite in the zone for 50pp per brick or so, but if you want to camp them it drops fairly often (1 out of 2 or 3 has it).

-Finally, Enchanted Platinum Bars are pretty easy to come by. Your best bet is to buy the bars yourself and then just give it to an enchanter you trust to enchant for you. Enchanters sometimes charge an excessive royalty for fetching and enchanting the bars themselves (enough to make up for whatever discount you may think they'd get for having such high CHA). The cheapest place i've found to get the platinum bars is in the Foreign Quarter in Neriak on the Ogre/Troll side at a store called Shinie Things. She usually sells the bars for 108 (with a cha of 55 or so) which is generally much cheaper than other jewelry stores from my experience.

And thusly I end my diatribe. Best of luck, young Knights of Shadow, A full suit of Darkforge (best earned, not bought) is truly an honor amongst our class. Wear it well.
