Twinkle Twinkle Little Twink Response
This editorial was submitted by: Tatter D’Malion

My background first. Yes, I have a level 48 wizard. I have however switched my prime character to a bard. I still run the wizard sometimes, but not to finance my bard.

The author lists three attitudes about twinking.

First, admiration. Yes, it’s becoming more common, but only because there are more twinks. It’s becoming a clique. Of course they admire each other.

Second, envy? He labels them due to the fact they don’t have a high level character to twink them. My bard has been approached for spare coin and gotten griped at when I decline to help. It’s not a simple case of haves and have nots.

Finally, disgust. Some folks think the character should earn her/his items. Not be handed to them.

Yes, I know twinking is going to exist and cannot be stopped. Level limits just make you wait to use items. But there is a problem being caused by the twinking. It compounds the camping problem already in place. If a high level is camping a mob for a item for his/her twink, it prevents a character "whose is currently playing a character who can USE the item" from camping the item. There have been times when I got a group together to camp a mob for bard armor quest items. We get to the spawn point and find a high level, solo camping the spawn. We ask why do you need the item and get "It’s for my bard I’m going to start." Curses. Can’t out damage him, and he still gets experience from the kill. Can’t ask him to yield either.

I believe this to be wrong. I could have used the item and not have to pass it down. However, I can not get the item because a high level wants to pass it to a new character still to be created. I’ve seen, "Wow, I got a such-and-such. I’ll think I’ll start a warrior to play with it." It makes me ill.

This also happens in groups. I was fighting in Mistmoore with a group one day and we got the Mistmoore Drums. I was looking forward to getting one someday and thought I did. But no, we random roll and an enchanter wins. Curses. But wait, what luck. We get a secound drum later. Random roll, a cleric wins. I ask politely if I could have it since I can use it and I helped kill it too. One says he is starting a bard later and the other offers to sell it. Being that I’m not rich, I decline and moan, in character, the rest of the day.

Yes, I was greedy assuming I should get one of the drums. Yes, I could have tried to buy it. However, I view things a little different from the greed in EQ. I think that on rare items, the characters "who can use" the item should be the only ones to roll for them. The group members come first, not their twinks. But alas, I am a rare breed. And yes, I have passed items to folks who can use them personally.

So, in the future, when you’re camping that spawn to twink your alt, and a group appears with a member who can personally use the item, consider giving the spawn to the group. Yes, camping with a lower level character is painful. But if you have the view, "Been there, done that," why are you still playing? Give us second class citizens a break. Please…

Tatter D’Malion
Brell
