Melee vs. Hybrids: 56-60
Level 56

Monk

Inner Flame

This discipline adds 4x your base damage to every blow landed for 12 seconds. It is important to note that this only adds to your base damage not to your total damage. It has a base reuse time of 30 minutes.

Let's take a high end example and assume a monk is using Ton Po's Bo Stick of Understanding(dmg 44 delay 40). This will add 176 points of damage to each and every landed blow during that 12 minute period. The numbers will be a bit lower depending on the damage of the weapon(or your fists).

Warrior

Furious

This skill will cause you to riposte all attacks against you for 9 seconds.  It has a base reuse time of 60 minutes.

Rogue

Rogues receive no new disciplines at level 56.

Shadowknight

Spirit Tap

Spirit tap is an upgrade to your lifetap line of spells. It is a 150 hit poit lifetap for 144 mana. This is the exact same ratio as your level 51 spell with a slightly longer casting time but it allows you to tap more hit points at a time.

Life leech is still a life saver but spirit tap will make you more effective at self healing if need be.

Steelskin

Steelskin is an upgrade to the level 39 shieldskin. It can absorb a much greater amount of damage however. Somewhere in the neighborhood of 250 hit points. 

It has the same pitfalls as the original however in that it is a one time damage absorber that only works for melee damage. It also requires Jasper as a reagent which can run a bit expensive to use in every battle.

Paladin

Counteract Disease

This spell is an upgrade to the cure disease line of spells. It is more effective against higher level diseases that would normally take several casts using the older cure disease spell. 

Yaulp III

This is of course an upgrade to the Yaulp line of spells. It is a self only spell which grants +30 strength and +12 armor class for the 18 second duration. 

Ranger

Chill Sight

This is a self only ultravision spell. Ultravision is of most use to human rangers but can also be useful to elves as well as ultravision gives slightly better night sight than infravision.

Greater Wolf Form

Greater wolf form is an extremely powerful spell for rangers. Not only does it grant an increase to running speed and changes to factions but it also increases ATK by 40 points. 

The ATK is perhaps the most useful aspect of the spell as it can dramatically increase your damage output over the course of a battle.

The weakness of this spell is that it can only be cast in outdoor zones and will cancel itself upon zoning.

Level 57

Monk

100 Fists

This discipline will grant the monk haste for 15 seconds. During this period all of your attacks will have their speed increased. The base reuse delay is 30 minutes.

Warrior

Precision

This discipline will give you a greater chance of hitting your opponent but has the side effect of also allowing you to be hit more often. It is sort of like a reverse evasive. It lasts for 3 minutes.

It has a base reuse time of 30 minutes.

Rogue

Kinesthetics

The discipline of kinesthetics will grant you instant double attack and dual wield success for 18 seconds. This means all four of your attacks will land every time you swing. In addition this can also allow you to double backstab.

The base reuse delay is 30 minutes.

Shadowknight

Vampiric Curse

Vampiric curse is a DOT lifetap. It heals you for roughly 200 hit points of damage over time. Even if a monster dies you will still gain the hit points. It is by far the most efficient lifetap you will ever receive.

That having been said it also is more of a taunt than your normal lifetaps. Because the hit poits take a while to be transfered to the caster it is probably not your best bet for healing during combat especially when considering the taunt factor.

I would recommend using this spell for downtime healing.

Paladin

Superior Healing

This is the last in the line of paladin healing spells. It heals approximately 600 points of damage for 250 mana. This is also the last in the line of shaman/druid healing spells which makes paladins quite effective filling in as a healer.

Ranger

Greater Healing

This is the long awaited upgrade to the heal line of spells. It is the final heal that rangers will ever receive. It will heal 300 hit points for 150 mana. For perhaps the first time in a ranger career you will actually be somewhat useful(although borderline by this level)  for healing during combat. 

Level 58

Monk

Monks receive no disciplines at level 58.

Warrior

Fell Strike

This skill will add four times the base damage of your weapon to your damage for 12 seconds. For example if you are wielding a Bladestorm(dmg 30 delay 45) you will get 120 points of damage added on to each succesful attack.

It has a base reuse delay of 30 minutes.

Rogue

Blinding Speed

This discipline will haste you for 15 seconds. This means that you will attack much faster than normal.

The base reuse is 30 minutes.

Shadowknight

This is your final pet spell. It summons a level 44 necromancer pet. This pet can hit for up to 47 points of damage and can also lifetap. It is a great damage dealer and can take a decent amount of damage before dying.

Nullify Magic

Nullify magic is an upgrade to the cancel magic spell. By this level cancel magic will fail to cancel most spells without multiple casts. Nullify magic does a much better job.

Paladin

Nullify Magic

Nullify magic is an upgrade to the cancel magic spell. By this level cancel magic will fail to cancel most spells without multiple casts. Nullify magic does a much better job.

Symbol of Naltron

This is an upgrade to the symbol line of spells. It adds 525 hit points to the selected player but requires an expensive reagent(Peridot).

Ranger

Nullify Magic

Nullify magic is an upgrade to the cancel magic spell. By this level cancel magic will fail to cancel most spells without multiple casts. Nullify magic does a much better job.

Shield of Spikes

This is an upgrade to the rangers line of damage shield spells. It can be cast on other players or on yourself and will grant a 17 point damage shield. Any damage done to a player with shield of spikes will cause the attacker to take 17 points of damage in exchange. 

This may not sound like a heck of a lot of damage when you are taking 120+ damage per attack but it adds up. It is especially useful when cast on the parties primary tank.

Level 59

Monk

At level 59 monks receive not one but two new disciplines. I find this somewhat surprising since they receive no disciplines at level 60.

Silent Fist

This discipline will allow your next succesful dragon punch/tail rake(for iksar) to do an additional amount of damage. It also has a chance of stunning your opponent. The weakness of this skill is that many monks have probably not used this attack in some time opting to go for the flying kick instead.

It has a base reuse delay of 7 minutes.

Ashen Hand

Using this discipline will cauase the next succesful eagle strike to do an obscene amount of damage(appears to do around 8-10x base damage). It has been reported to also have a chance of instantly killing your opponent. I have not been able to confirm this in my testing but it may have certain level limitations set in place.

The base reuse delay is 72 minutes.

Warrior

Fortitude

This skill grants a warrior invulnerability to melee attacks for 8 seconds. This is greatfor tanking ultra mobs or for saving yourself in a tough situatino. It does not affect magical attacks.

It has a base reuse of 60 minutes.

Rogue

Duelist

This discipline adds 4x the weapons base damage to the amount of damage you do with each attack for 12 seconds. This means if you are using a bloodpoint for example(dmg 10 delay 21) that you will receive 40 points of damage bonus for each attack.

It has a 30 minute reuse delay.

Shadowknight

Cascading Darkness

Cascading darkness is the last in your line of darkness spells. It will stack with all DOTs other than previous darkness spells. 

This spell can do around 1150 points of damage over a long duration. It also slows as well as a snare spell. 

The downside to this spell is an extremely long casting time. This means that if you plan on using it for fear kiting you need to use it to open your fetch and you better hope it is not resisted. 

Paladin

Ressurection

This spell allows a paladin to not only ressurect a player(thus teleporting them to their corpse) but also restores the majority of experience lost(around 90%). 

Ranger

Calefaction

This fire based spell will do up to 450 points of direct damage to your opponent. It casts quickly(only a 2.5 second cast time) but has a ridiculously long recast delay of 45 seconds. 

If you need to dish damage in a hurry then this spell can be a good last resort. The long recast seriously limits its usefulness however.

Level 60

Monk

Monks receive no disciplines at level 60.

Warrior

Aggressive

This discipline will cause you to do more damage on your attacks for 3 minutes. The downfall is that it also causes you to take more damage.

It has a base reuse delay of 30 minutes.

Rogue

Rogues receive no new disciplines at level 60.

Shadowknight

Asystole

Asystole is an upgrade to the level 39 spell heart flutter. It will stack with all DOTs other than heart flutter.

This spell does 69 points per tick over 10 ticks(one minute) for a total of 690 damage. It also debuffs(lowers) your targets strength by 40 points and its armor class by approximately 20 points. 

When used in conjunction with boil blood and cascading darkness a level 60 shadowknight can DOT for around 2300 points of damage in addition to their other abilities. 

Drain Spirit

Drain spirit is the last in your line of lifetap spells. It lifetaps for 226 points of damage for 189 points of mana. This is a 0.836 ratio compared to the 0.973 ratio of previous lifetaps and the 1.125 ratio fo life leech. 

The weakness of the spell is it's long casting time.

Paladin

Resolution

This is the last in the line of a paladins courage->daring line of spells. It adds 250 hit points and 15 armor class to the targetted player.

Shield of Words

This is the last in the line of a paladins armor line of spells. It adds approximately 32 armor class to your target.

Ranger

Enveloping Roots

This is an upgrade to the grasping roots line of spells. It roots your target in place for a longer duration of time and also does up to 110 points of direct damage when cast. 

One can argue over the merits of root vs. snare for hours. The bottom line is that snare is the spell of choice in most cases. Root however can be quite useful for crowd control or if in a crowded area. 

The crowd control useage involves the ranger dragging a mob away from the party and then rooting it. The ranger can then return to the battle and hopefully root will buy the party enough time to kill the other mobs or at least enough to do a decent amount of damage. 

Thorncoat

Thorncoat is the last in the line of the bramblecoat line of self only buffs/damage shields. It adds 32 points to your armor class and also acts as a long lasting 5 point damage shield. 

In Conclusion

In my opinion the majority of hybrid complaints are coming simply from grass is greener syndrome. There are a few areas however where hybrids have legitimate gripes such as the lack of quality spells on hybrid armor in comparison to melee armor. I think that this is an area that perhaps deserves some looking in to.

While I do not play a ranger very often I must agree that from the outside looking in it appears that the ranger class is the one most shaken up in expansion. I am not saying that I believe that rangers are underpowered. I still think it is a fine class. However there are a couple of areas that might merit looking into.  These would include the defensive skill caps set upon rangers and the recast delay on calefaction. 

It might be wise to replace calefaction altogether with a new ranger only spell. Not only is the recast delay limiting but the spell is less mana efficient than the level 52 firestrike. I think that a level 59 ranger deserves a bit more than that. 

I have no problem with rangers receiving less defensive skill points per level than shadowknights or paladins. Shadowknights and paladins currently receive 3 per level post 50. I think that 2 points per level or even only 1 per level would be a benefit to the ranger class. 1 per level would cap their defense at 210 which would help a bit. Let's not forget that they also have quite a bit less armor class on their tolan's darkwood or thorny vine armor. The two of those combined make rangers very poor tanks.

There are also certain scenarios where a melee class is much more valuable than a hybrid. I think these cases are few and far between however. I also feel that hybrids need to accept their role as a hybrid and not expect to be as good as their parent class in what their parent class does. Hybrid advantage lies in their flexibility. I still feel despite the problems that are alluded to by many that hybrids are very fun to play and very powerful post-expansion.
