Warriors Guide From 1-60

First I suppose I should introduce myself, Im Nukh Hungry 59 trollwarrior on Fennin Ro.

Im from Sweden so if theres any spelling errors blame it on the fact that Im a troll.

First of all if you are going to play a warrior there are three races to select from: Barbarian, Troll and Ogre.

Choose whatever you feel benefits your playingstyle best but any other race is kind of a waste of time and you will regret it on higher lvls.

There are two stats you need to put points into, these are Str and Sta.

Dont listen to others talk bout agi and dex, agi gives good ac up till 100agi BUT you will get that from items wether you want it or not. Dex they say makes you proc more often BUT real melee output does more dmg so str is better up to 255 str.

Why sta then, well at high lvl you gain about 6hp for every point of sta and face it you are a tank and you will get beat alot, high hp saves mana when clerics use complete heal.

As of your religion I couldnt care less, sure Rallos Zek is good if you plan on being giantfriend later but that is bad in my opinion, why i will explain later.

By the way, be sure to keep all weaponskills maxed, nothings more sad then seeing a 55 warrior getting a good weapon and his skills are so poor he get to spend hours of annoying people trying to get it up.

Ok now for fast levels:

1 to 5: around your hometown, cant say how good Halas area is because I refuse to play any class wearing a skirt J

6 to 15: I went to South Ro, made very good exp from orcs etc, not a crowded area but many high lvl persons camping Anicent cyclops to buff you.

16 to 20: At this lvl I went to Oasis, lots of crocs and orcs to kill, easy to find groups.

I recomend getting druid and shaman friends here for future use.

21 to 25: I went to Mistmoore, dont know how many plays in this zone nowdays but with a good group its damn good exp.

26 to 35: Time to head closer to home, I went to Cazic, great dungeon at this lvl and you get to know how to play with an enchanter in the group.

I also went to Guk a few times, mostly to learn the area.

36 to 45: Overthere here I come, nice to see The dark breathren has put a mark on Lizard territory, also great exp and you will find alot of your shaman and druid friends from old oasis days here.

Place is great if you have regen and sow on.

46 to 51: Dreadlands all the way. Must say my normal group was druid, bard me or druid, rogue me. Great exp, made about a lvl a day. Avoid ravishing mobs, they got lots of hp and give very little extra exp. Be sure to dispell spiders dmg shield hurts worse then their actuall dmg output.

52 to 55: Hope you got some resistgear and maybe some type of regeneration item or good bandage skills because we are going to Cynderella.

This necromancer by fearportal has been farmed for exp by casters forever, what melee people dont know is that after she has been killed once she is EASY.

Sit close to her spawnspot after you killed her the first time and she will attack once she spawns.

No buffing, no summoning pet, she will dive after your throat asap.

She fears though so use some resistgear, also worth mentioning is that she is a 6 min spawn.

She drops lots of gems etc for cash so you get rich too.

Once you hit 55 she wont give exp anymore and you will have to start going to dungeons.

55 to 60: What can I say, if you are evil race you will want to have a Worker sledgemallet to be able to gate to Overthere at all times so Howling stones and Karnor is out.

Whats left is Old Sebilis, Sol B and Dragon Necropolice, etc.

Why I mention these three is because they are my favorites there are several other good hunting grounds.

In solB you got imps etc (efreeticamp) and Stonespider room.

In Old Sebilis you get exp from everything except in later lvls where a few camps might be slow.

In Dragon Necropolice you will get exp from everything, and good weapons and questgems.

Ok now to the part in Velious where you want to choose if your going to go with Dragons or Giants.

Now in my mind and on the raids i attend to people need resistgear against cold fire and magic ALOT, that forces them to sacrifice ac and hp items, what if that resistgear could be your everyday items…

Another thing is the Bracer of Benovelence and Vindicator Bp that I personally use over myrmidon because of the great resists, these items cant be obtained on giantfaction.

I might be out in the blue but resists has saved me a ton of times where no Ac in the world could have, thats why I suggest Myrmidon armor.

Another good thing is that you can stay dwarffriend when you are on giantfaction and get ring of Dain etc.

Ok ill admit going Giantfriend might be easier for the smaller guild due to that Tormax and gang are harder to kill then Yelinak and most dragons in Western wastes but in my head its the equippment posebilitys that rules.

Some other things worth mentioning about warriors are that you dont need to put more then 1 point into skills like dodge, riposte etc they raise fast anyway.

I must say ive put points into the disarm and hand to hand and twohand weapon skills instead.

Im not trying to say hand to hand is usefull, but its fun dueling hand to hand when bored J

The disarm skill on the other hand is very usefull since it works as an extra taunt button, nothing piss a mob off as much as when you try to steal his weapon, which is great on mobs you cant disarm.

If you wonder why you should keep twohand weapon skills up just look at the Primal weapons from sleepers tomb, you never know if you get one some day.

Hope someone finds this warriorsguide to be of some use, and to all you halfling warriors: Nukh is Hungry
