The Ultimate Starting-Out Guide: WARRIOR

Overview

The Warrior is, in a sense, the melee to which all else is measured.  In general they have the best weapon caps of any class, the most armour available, the most weapons available, most of the combat skills (dodge, dual wield, double attack, etc).  Not to mention, they get them first and have the most hit points of any class.  This makes it so that other class’s melee and tanking ability is measured against that of the warrior.

Pros and Cons

(+) These guys can take damage like none other.  They have the highest AC and the highest HP, which makes them the ideal tank.  Also, complete heal is therefore the most efficient on this class.

(+) They have the widest variety of equipment available to them.  They can use practically every weapon and every piece of armour they find.  The only exception to this general rule is when dealing with class-specific armour, or with things made specifically for casters.  For example, they cannot wear caster robes or equip staves.

(+) They are always in the battle, up close and personal, and because they are the best tanks they will always be wanted in groups.

(+) They get the combat skills before most classes.  Among these are duel wield and double attack at level 15, which is before any other weapons-based class (this excludes monks)

(+) They get high caps on their combat skills – highest caps in the game for the majority of them

(+) They get an experience bonus, with a 0.9 rating, meaning that nothing levels like a halfling warrior.

(-) After duel wield and double attack, there aren’t many things to look forward to at higher levels other than the disciplines

(-) Because of their power, warriors don’t do much more than sit and take damage.  This can get feverously boring, especially for a player who likes using his quick fingers.

(-) Only get kick and bash (slam) as a secondary attack.  Most warriors never use shields, so only large races can slam to stun.  Also, kick and slam cannot both be used – only one secondary attack can be chosen, although after level 55, kick does stun.

(-) Very hard to solo at high levels because of large number of hit points.  After a tough fight, it could take anywhere up to 45 minutes to fully heal without the aid of magic!

(-) Very group dependent – your life is in the hands of the cleric and it is tough to develop a trust like this.

Examination of Races

The warrior is a class, which can be played by many different races.  Keep in mind that all warriors can be very good tanks and damage dealers, despite their race.  I would recommend one of four races for the Warrior class, as follows:

Halfling Warrior – This would be my choice for the newbie Everquest player.  To put it simply, because halflings are the only race with an experience bonus, no race / class combination in the game levels at the rate a halfling warrior does.  They also have fairly balanced warrior stats (strength, stamina, agility and dexterity) although nothing is remarkable.

Ogre Warrior – This would be a choice because they are the biggest, strongest, meanest and arguably ugliest race in Norrath.  Because of their incredible strength and stamina they will have more hit points and hit slightly harder than other warriors at “endgame.”  On the downside, they are hated throughout the lands, which makes them a bit tougher to play, especially for a newbie.  

Troll Warrior – I would recommend this combination because of two factors.  First of all, trolls are a massive race, like ogres and share much of that high strength and stamina.  On top of this they have regeneration which makes them a good choice for someone who doesn’t have a lot of time to play and might have to solo a little bit more than they would like to.

Iksar Warrior – The Iksars, in my always-humble opinion, make the best warriors once they have hit the highest levels.  This is because of the natural Iksar armour-class bonus.  This means that at “endgame” in the best armour in the game (right now, Bezerker armour from Plane of Growth) a fully clad Iksar would have more AC than any other warrior race.  This means that they can tank the best of any warrior race – after all, armour-class is the warrior’s most important stat.  However the downside is that it would be extremely difficult to achieve this state of being, which nobody has been able to do thus so far.
