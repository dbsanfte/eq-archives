Aegirson's animations tips and tricks:

A complete walkthrough.

 

1. An idea:

What works for me best, is to do an animation of a character that best portrays the character's abilities. A spell, a practice move, or even an emotion animation. Something to keep in mind, is that the more complicated the movements, the more screenshots you will have to make and therefore will have to put them back together again. You do not need the full idea to get started - the way I usually work is to first take screenshot of the character performing a movement.

 

2. The screenshots:

You will not escape this, you must take screenshots of the character in game. Your target and you must not rotate, move forward, change illusion, duck or jump during the shoot. I strongly recommend shooting indoors where lighting is stable. I also recommend shooting in an area where there aren't many people and especially mobs that could disrupt the shooting session.

I take as many screenshots as I deem necessary. Ideally, I need a screenshot of every key frame of an animation - so that the movement does not appear too jerky when I reassemble the frames later. Typically I take around 50 screenshots. 

 

3. Crop all your screenshots:

If you don't crop all your screenshots at exactly the same coordinates, your animation may appear as if there is an earthquake, or that you may have moved during the shooting; here is a trick to crop all your pictures perfectly:

This is a normal screen shot

In Photoshop, first I created a new layer.
I selected the area that I wanted to crop - then inverse-selected it. Filled the area around with black.
Now with the magic wand tool, I selected the center part again, and selected the crop feature from the image menu.

I did this for all my screenshots.

 

4. I Rearranged my frames in my GIF program.

Why do this now? Simply to cut down my editing work by at least half. I took many screenshots in order to make sure I captured all the frames. In that process, I always have screenshots of some same frames.  

I imported all the frames - I had about 50 of them. Since I didn't take them in the perfect order that they appear - I use my GIF program to rearrange them and figure which are the unnecessary frames. This task may be overwhelming at first - it is very similar to trying to untie a cluster of knots - or to do a huge puzzle; I try to match several clusters of frames together which I then further rearrange with the other clusters. Here is a first draft: 

Once I have figured which frames I want to keep and the order in which I want them, I then export each picture (copy/paste) into Photoshop and save them in sequence - with a filename that reflects the number of the frame. I like to have 15 to 20 frames.

 

5. Removing the background of each frame

This is tedious and I spend a lot of time on this. Polygonal lasso here I come! I usually select the entire character and inverse-select it to delete the background. I do this for each picture and here is the result:
At this point, I am pretty happy with myself since all the tedious - boring part is done, now comes the creative / fun part. There are 16 frames in this animation.

 

6. Coming up with a background:

The easy path would be to find a real picture from the web or Photodisc or such, but I really like to work with Bryce 3D and create artificial environments. I also particularly like the moving skies.

 

Here is a wire frame view of the background. I found the castle model and the crypt model on the web at http://www.3dcafe.com and imported them in Bryce 3D.

I move the sky object from left to right within a 3 seconds time span and export the animation as an AVI.
I imported the AVI in my gif program and exported 16 consecutive frames to match the character frames.

7. Merging the character animation and background animation:

I simply pasted each background frame with the matching character's frame and came up with this result:

 

Now I am not 100% happy yet, I look a little too artificial - I like to add touches. For each frame, I added an artificial shadow of the character on the ground. I further added lens flare effect to simulate a far away bright star rising from the Castle. I further added a 50% translucent layer that contains Fitz's name on all the frames.

Now we are getting somewhere!!! I exported the animation from the GIF program as an AVI.

Ok ok maybe I'm pushing it a little too far now - but I really want this animation to look good, so I decide to go with Fitz's dissolving "mission statement" with Fitz's animation itself - which then transitions with KEG's logo to finally transition to the mission statement again.
