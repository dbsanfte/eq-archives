Monk Disciplines


Introduction to Disciplines

Disciplines are not gained every level. They are spread out over different levels(almost every level). They can not be used over and over again. Each discipline has a base reuse time. These vary depending on the discipline. After using a discipline you will not be able to use any other discipline until the reuse time has expired. This means if the reuse time is 45 minutes you better be careful of when you pick to use this discipline. The reuse time decreases depending on your level. It will decrease one minute per level. The monks Stone Stance discipline for example has a 12 minute reuse time at level 51 but decreases to a mere 3 minutes at level 60.

Level 51

Stone Stance

This discipline will absorb about 75% of the damage that you would normally take during combat. It only lasts for 12 seconds and has a reuse delay of 12 minutes initially. This reuse will fall to a mere 3 minutes at level 60.

Level 52

Thunder Kick

This discipline will cause your next succesful flying kick to do more than the maximum damage. I'm not sure how much more it will do number wise. It seems to add somewhere near about 25-50% damage for one attack.

The base reuse delay is 7 minutes.

Level 53

Whirlwind

At level 53 a monk will gain the discipline of whirlwind. This discipline will cause every attack against you to be riposted for 9 seconds. This wil allow you to do a bit of extra damage in combat. The problem is that the short duration ensures that it will not do that much extra damage.

It is seldom used however because of the 60 minute reuse delay.

Level 54

Voidance

This spell will allow you to avoid attacks against you for 8 seconds. It only works versus melee attacks. A common usefor this discipline is to buy time if a feign death fails and death seems certain.

You must pick and choose your useage of it very wisely however as it has a 60 minute reuse delay which will reduce by one minute per level to a minimum of 54 minutes.

Level 55

Monks gain no disciplines at level 55.

Level 56

Inner Flame

This discipline adds 4x your base damage to every blow landed for 12 seconds. It is important to note that this only adds to your base damage not to your total damage. It has a base reuse time of 30 minutes.

Let's take a high end example and assume a monk is using Ton Po's Bo Stick of Understanding(dmg 44 delay 40). This will add 176 points of damage to each and every landed blow during that 12 second period. The numbers will be a bit lower depending on the damage of the weapon(or your fists).

Level 57

100 Fists

This discipline will grant the monk haste for 15 seconds. During this period all of your attacks will have their speed increased. The base reuse delay is 30 minutes.

Level 58

Monks receive no disciplines at level 58.

Level 59

Monk

At level 59 monks receive not one but two new disciplines. I find this somewhat surprising since they receive no disciplines at level 60.

Silent Fist

This discipline will allow your next succesful dragon punch/tail rake(for iksar) to do an additional amount of damage. It also has a chance of stunning your opponent. The weakness of this skill is that many monks have probably not used this attack in some time opting to go for the flying kick instead.

It has a base reuse delay of 7 minutes.

Ashen Hand

Using this discipline will cauase the next succesful eagle strike to do an obscene amount of damage(appears to do around 8-10x base damage). It has been reported to also have a chance of instantly killing your opponent. I have not been able to confirm this in my testing but it may have certain level limitations set in place.

The base reuse delay is 72 minutes.

Level 60

Monks receive no disciplines at level 60.
