Specialisation Guide


Here is the link to the Spell Database! Please review your class spells before making a decision.

Specialisation will increases a casters mana efficiency in a given area of magic.  Once an Intelligence based caster reaches Level 20, or a wis based caster reaches level 30, they obtain the ability to specialise in the fields of magic.   Only one field can exceed a skill of 50, though you can specalize in all abilities of magic.  Whichever skill first exceeds 50 becomes the primary specialisation of the caster from that time on.  If you choose the wrong specialty, you can alter it by completing a quest in the Temple of Solusek Ro.

What does Specialisation help?

You have a chance of making your specialisation check each time you cast a spell.  The higher you leve in that skill the greater your chance of making your specialisation check and reducing the amount of mana that spell uses.  This higher specialisation does TWO things, reduces mana used by that spell when casting and being successful on your skill and reducing the chance of fizzling on that spell.  If you cast the spell and you do not make your specalisation skill trigger (it's like rolling dice sometimes it checks and sometimes it does not), you simply will cast that spell as normal.  That means you will cast it at the usual mana cost and chances of fizzle.

Specialisation does NOT increase your chance of recovering after an interruption or make your spell cast faster.

Make sure when you reach your appropriate level where you can trigger a specialisation, you train in the skill you want to raise.  You can also cast a lower level spell in the skill you desire until you reach 51.  The max skill limit on specialisation is 5 + (5 x Level), with a maximum of 200.

When trying to decide what to  specialise in, take a glance at your spell book and look up your future spells. Not all classes want the same specialisation.  For example if you were an Enchanter thinking of specialising in Evocation, you would notice that only 5 of the 200+ spells use this skill.   

Also, not all people in one class may choose to specalise in the same thing.  Shamans, for instance, might want to specialise in damage spells, dot spells or buffing spells...still other Shamans might want to specialise in a skill that improves their healing spells.  It varies from person to person and how you play the game. 

Here are some suggestions:

Enchanter - Alteration and Conjuration.  

Alteration -  80% of enchanter spells are of this nature.  It includes roots, hastes, slows, charms, and most buffs/debuffs. 

Conjuration - includes all mesmerisation spells.  This is the major use of crowd control in the game. 

Magician -  Evocation and Conjuration.  

Evocation - Used for battle spells and what Magicians do best.

Conjuration - Most spells are conjuration based. But this does not include most of the damage lines of spell and instead summoning spells and spike/blade type spells.

Wizard - Evocation and Alteration

Evocation - Only way to go, Wizards are the kings of direct damage spells and this is what you want!

Necromancer - Alteration, Conjuration and Evocation. 

Alteration - This covers most of the undead charms, Lifetap's and Damage Over Time spells.

Evocation - If you plan on soloing much this might be an option since most direct damage spells are of this skill.

Conjuration- This includes the pet summonings, darkness and disease lines of spells. 

Cleric - Alteration and Evocation.

Alteration - This is what most choose because it covers heals, cures, roots, and resurection spells. 

Evocation - If you plan to solo much then this might be an option. Clerics get nice direct damage spells and most are Evocation,.

Druids - Conjuration, Alteration and Evocation.

Conjuration - Druids are great soloing!  And if you plan on playing that role this might be an option because your Damage Over Times spells fall into this skill.

Evocation - You also get excellent Direct Damage spells and this skill helps you with that. 

Alteration - If you plan on buffing, healing, and teleporting much, this is a great option. 

Shamans - Alteration and Conjuration.

Conjuration - If you solo or kite much, this can be a great choice.   This skill includes your most power damage spells, the DoT's or Damage Over Time line.

Alteration - Shaman's buffs are some of the best in the game and this skill follows that line of spells.  This includes heals, regenerations and strength/dex/stamina buffs.
