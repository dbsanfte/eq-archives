Welcome to the Dark Side.  The purpose of this site is to share information and advice with those who come here, and who knows, unite to kill a dragon or two.

 

The Dark Side is open for players of all races and classes, but is focused to necromancer characters.  Necromancers can find answers to the most commonly asked questions such as guild locations, spell list, and how to improve your faction standing.

 

Please enjoy this site and be aware that you might not want to see some of the information contained here.  One of the great things about EverQuest is the thrill of discovery.  But if you are like me, a little help every once in a while cannot hurt.

 

Comments, suggestions and contributions are greatly appreciated and I will make sure that you get credit for it.  I am willing to make this the best reference site for necromancers and I can do it with your help.  Thank you for stopping by and may Norrath treat you well.  Walk in darkness my friend.

A quick marketing lesson.  In the real world, one of the most important attributes of a product is its brand.  As an example, about 50 years ago, Santa Claus was dressed in his usual clothes, but they were all white.  Coca-Cola, one of the worlds most powerful brands, started putting his image on its can.  But on their cans, Santa Claus was using a red jacket.  Several years later, I doubt anyone can even imagine Santa Claus without the red jacket.  One more example involving Coca-Cola.  In a blind test conducted by Pepsi-Cola, the majority of people that tried Coke and Pepsi blindfolded preferred Pepsi.  Nonetheless, Coke is still the undisputable leader in the Cola wars.  As you can see, brand plays a major role in the customers purchasing behavior.  But what does this have to do with EQ?  I am only trying to prove a point that not only because something looks nicer, costs a lot of money, or is the most sought after item in the game means you should also join the frenzy.  In EQ, brand would translate to something like popularity.  My advice is to simply buy good items, not popular ones.  In my humble opinion, why would you pay big bucks to the Gossimer Robe for example?  It does look nice, but that is all it does...
