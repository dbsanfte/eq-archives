Feista Online Wedding System Preview

Posted By amandasaurus, 2008-06-20 16:53:47 for Fiesta Online
The folks over at Outspark have given us a sneak peek at their new upcoming Feista Online wedding system, slated for release in the first week of July. The new wedding feature will take place in the Blessed Garden. The marriage process works like this; the players will buy a pair of rings, and a proposal takes place. A marriage license will then need to be purchased from the cash shop. The purchase includes the license itself, wedding outfits for the bride and groom, and 15 invitations for guests. When the marriage is ready to take place, the happy couple simply visits an NPC in Elderine. The license is used to transport the couple to the ceremony, and the guests use their invitations to get there.

Marriage comes with a few neat features. The bride and groom will each receive mini-pets (seen in the picture) to advertise their matrimony. Also, the wedding rings can be used once per house to teleport the couple to each other. The price has not yet been set for the weddings, but is expected to be quite reasonable, and there are no special requirements for marriage eligibility.

Champions Online: Ask Cryptic

Posted By amandasaurus, 2008-06-19 23:16:39 for Champions Online
The folks over at Cryptic have taken the time to answer your community questions about Champions Online.

Our Ask Cryptic feature returns with the answers to community questions about combat, updates, villains and character creation. Thanks to Jackalope, poz, Arkayne, Heretic and Absinthe for their answers!

There's been a little talk about some of the "dynamic new combat systems" being used, such as in Age of Conan. But what about those of us who don't have the refined reflexes of a belligerent teenager playing the latest FPS gankfest? Will there be a means by which those of us with mediocre hand-eye coordination & typing skills can still participate and feel that we're pulling our own weight, or will we be relegated to observer status? (Starhammer)

Jackalope (Chief Creative Officer): Rest assured, Starhammer. I confess that I have little, if any, hand-eye coordination (though my typing isn't so bad). We're not trying to create systems as complicated as say, a fighting game, where every twitch of the hand creates a new baffling maneuver. We're trying to create a system with more depth than "click A and pray," but not so much that old-school gamers can't keep up. One of our principles for combos, for instance, is never do anything that takes more than 3 button presses to execute.
