Changes

Posted by Raaz Hevenlaenn on January 18, 1999:

As soon as he stepped through the dark portal, the gaping maw closed behind him, leaving him in his magical home. He knew his face would start decaying if it was not already starting. His body would soon be nothing but bones and he couldn't afford to show those in the Conclave that he was a member of the undead, not yet. Mumbling a single word, he created a black broad-rimmed hat to cover his head. Pondering about what to cover his face with, he dipped his hand into the shadows of his longcoat and withdrew a black scarf. After putting on the scarf and the hat, only his eyes could be seen. Knowing his eyes would eventually turn into red points, he decided to take off his spectacles. This way, the others would know his transformation was finally complete and his foes would think twice before attacking him. Grinning slightly under the scarf, he walked about into his room, waiting for Megera to tell him about the current events within the Conclave.

Posted by Raaz Hevenlaenn on January 31, 1999:

Raaz sat in the darkness, waiting. Waiting for his transformation to take effect, waiting to see what he must live as. Most of his body had already changed for he could no longer feel the pain which was surging within his flesh a few days before. He began to record the events which he had gone through in the book which he had acquired what seemed like lifetimes ago. It was a thick tome covered with human skin in order to keep its magical power. He recorded what had happened since he had found the book, he recorded very little about himself, but more about his powers and what he could do with it. He recorded it to remind himself that he wasn't always an undead creature, but that he was once flesh and blood.

As he wrote the last part of his past, his vision faded into red. He no longer had to use his magical spell in order to give him vision, but now his transformation was complete. Raaz Hevenlaenn was no longer a creature that was limited by mortality, he was now what most mortals would call a "Death Knight."
