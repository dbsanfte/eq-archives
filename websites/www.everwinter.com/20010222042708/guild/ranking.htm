House (Guild) Master:
(Rank Five)

The guild master’s primary duty is to forward the mission of the guild by providing organization, resources, and representation. The maintenance of a roster (organization), events and news (organization), web site (resource), message board (resource), and guidance for other officers (representation) are typical tasks assigned to the guild master, or delegated to assistants. Ultimately, the guild master is accountable for the welfare the guild, notably member satisfaction.

Assistant House (Guild) Master(s):
(Rank Four)

The Assistant House Master is the designated assistant for the guild master, acting in all ways as the House Master when he or she is not present. Special tasks may be assigned to the Assistant House Master.  If more than one Assistant House Master exists, one will be designated Senior, for purposes of succession.

Clan Lord/Lady:
(Rank Three)

The Clan Lords or Ladies are responsible for mentoring, distributing guild wealth (equipment or coin), collecting spare wealth (equipment or coin), monitoring the progress of their clan, and sponsoring an event once per month. Currently, there are four clans: Blackmoon, Justice, Tempest, and Warclaw.  Others may be added in the future.  Official welcomes to new members should be issued by these officers, as well.

House Elders:
(Rank Two)

A non-specific role, these guild members are granted "officer status" as a reward for their long and exemplary service with the House. They often act as mentors, guiding other members by example and interaction. In some cases, they may be appointed representatives of the character originally awarded the role.

House Fellows:
(Rank One)

House fellows are expected to adhere to House Law and keep the goodwill of the House in mind.  We have no formal policies on grouping, assistance, and such, but expect harmony among our membership.  All Fellows must adhere to the activity requirements found in the House Law.  All members of Fellow rank and higher may sponsor a Squire.

House Squires:

Squires are sponsored by House members, but generally have severe time-limitations.  They are *NOT* required to adhere to the activity requirements in House Law.
