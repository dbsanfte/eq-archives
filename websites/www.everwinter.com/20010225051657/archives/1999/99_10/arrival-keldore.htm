Arrival and Arrest

Posted by ag'Keldore on October 11, 1999:

Though slight, the alteration magic used in ag'Keldore's spell interfered with that in use during the evacuation of Cynnabaris. Briefly, Keldore, Bowen, and Drakor touched the ground before the Dark Spire. The mixing of the two similar magics flung the three to a random point. The power of the Spire's magic was sufficient, however, to ensure their arrival on the world of Solon.

Linked by luck, the three dazed individuals ended up together in the heart of the powerful human nation, Pembroke City. Tall hedges and the aroma of flowers surrounded them. They sat for a long while, not moving or speaking, gathering their wits and trying to discern where exactly they were.

Bowen was the first to speak, "Have we made it to Solon?" He stood, brushed off his pearl colored robes, and gazed upon the magnificant gardens that lay around him.

Keldore was the next to rise to his feet, likewise brushing off his robes. "I'm not sure. See anyone around?" He, too, began noticing the flora around them.

Drakor stood, still wary of the two with him, and there was an immediate shout from across the gardens.

"Halt in the name of the law!" Three armed and armored figures were approaching. The three were too stunned to prepare a defense, nessecary or not. They just stood, awaiting the imminent questioning of the guards.

Posted by ag'Keldore on November 6, 1999:

The guards did not bother asking questions just then. They demanded immediate surrender instead. Keldore and Bowen complied, Keldore raising his hands, Bowen setting his sword upon the ground. Drakor was hesitant, however. It was clear that he was prepared to fight his way through this mess and land the three of them into a larger one.

"There is no use fighting...cooperate and we will be freed," Bowen whispered to the dark elf, not taking his eyes off the guards. They seemed noble enough, but one could never tell by simple appearances. City guards were not known for being gentle with prisoners who didn't do what they said. Finally, with just a bit more goading from Bowen, Drakor complied.

One of the three guards stepped forward with manticles, approaching Drakor. The dark elf seemed likely to strike out at the guard, so Keldore intercepted the guard, placing himself between him and Drakor. "There is no need to bound us, good sir. We will cooperate with the law. I am sure you will find us to be without transgression." The guard muttered something to the others and they nodded, grunting somewhat.

"Come with us. No funny business either," the guard with the irons barked as he backed away from the high elf. The guards led them into a small gatehouse at the entrance to the gardens. Two of the guards flanked them, while one brought up the rear. Keldore was still wondering what he was going to tell the guards once they started to ask questions.
