The day of rebirth arrived, and Weab was scarcely able to contain his joy - while the rebirth signifies a great road of pain for the future, with it comes promise of grand adventures and great fame.

Admittedly, Weab is not your ordinary Iksar - while most Iksar have contempt for the so-called 'soft skins,' Weab has some strange fascination with them. Their culture intrigues him greatly, and he longs to one day convince the other races that he is not like the ordinary Iksar, that he might be able to walk among them to better study them and understand their way of life.

Not only the way of life of the softskins, but also their fighting techniques, the nature of their magicks, and the values and intricacies of their trade skills.  The other 'evil' races especially interested him - he wanted to know why the trolls and ogres were unaccepted like him. Another special interest of Weab's was the fighting styles of the Iksar monks' human counterparts, the monks of Qeynos and Freeport.

It was then that Weab fully understood what the rebirth meant to him and what the Shackle signified. The Shackle stood for determination, and while most Iksar thought of determination to continue in a Monk's training, in Weab's mind it was a reminder that someday, no matter how far in the future, he would be strong enough to do deeds the 'soft skins' would accept him for.

With newfound courage and energy, Weab moved to the Field of Bone. He saw before him limitless opportunities.

After experiencing the basics of combat hunting scaled wolf pups and other small game, Weab felt prepared to move to the pit. All was well, when he encountered a strange softskin, one half his size. He spent a good deal of time studying the small creature, trying to decide how and why it could possibly be here. With such small arms and (apparently) weak muscles, there was no way it could face even a skeleton, let alone one of the feared scaled wolf hunters.

Yet Weab was soon to be given an important lesson. The creature ran into the battlefield, and provoked about six deadly creatures into attacking him all at once. Being an abnormally sensitive Iksar, Weab shielded his eyes from what was sure to be a slaughter. After a few seconds, however, Weab's curiosity got the better of him. He peaked through his scaley fingers to see the softskin not only alive, but decimating the creatures before him. Four were already dead, and the other two were severely wounded.

Later he learned the creature was called a 'halfling,' and that although they are naturally small, they can become as powerful as some of the most legendary Iksar.

This knowledge only strengthened Weab's interest in pursuing his goal - becoming accepted on the distant continent of Norrath.

As he peered into the night sky, he knew a long path lay before him, one he was most eager to walk down. As dawn arrived, he walked to the Field of Bone once more, to resume his training and reach his goal.
