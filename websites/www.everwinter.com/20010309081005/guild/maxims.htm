EverQuest doesn’t provide a great deal of structure for some serious role-playing issues. The guild has agreed to some ideas to provide a bit of continuity and depth to our role-playing.

Maxim: A character’s true class, and even gender or race, may differ greatly from the game mechanics. A human might be "part" Iksar and exhibit traits such as claws, hissing, and the like. A druid might consider himself a conjurer, or even a ranger. Accept the role-playing and ignore the game mechanics.

Maxim: A character’s religion—if any—does not necessarily reflect their class and race. For example, a dark elf cleric or necromancer is confined to worshipping Innoruuk within the game, but the player may choose another power, or none at all, entirely. As a guild, we do not assume religion based on game mechanics. A dark elf cleric *could* be a priestess of Tunare, for our RP purposes.

Maxim: A character’s level doesn’t indicate their power or strength. Surname’s indicate an acknowledgment of the citizens of Norrath or the character’s guild. As a guild, we may role-play a fourth level necromancer as a powerful character (the equivalent of a 50th level character) or a 30th level enchanter, as a young and unworldly prodigy. This is left up to the player, though we encourage some realism.

Maxim: Not all characters are natives of Norrath. Characters from other worlds may well find themselves on Norrath. Some characters within the guild hail from other online games, such as Neverwinter Nights, Ultima Online, or the Realm. Such histories are encouraged and can be worked into the history of Norrath; said characters are plane-travelers; they may have a much weaker form in Norrath, perhaps taking time (experience) to master magic, or the laws of the world.

Maxim: Real life time limitations do not equate to a character being absent from Norrath. Characters can be assumed to have an existence beyond the time their player enacts them. If the player is absent for three weeks, perhaps the character has been at work learning a language, a trade skill, visiting a relative, spying on an enemy, or even on another plane. Character absence, particularly when a player has an extended leave, *can* be explained by the player, but it isn’t required.

Maxim: A character does not die when they loose a battle. Instead, we assume that a character that falls in battle is saved at the last moment by a "death pact" with Lady Vox; this Winterborne character is teleported away to a safe spot, sans equipment. Other characters outside of the guild might be saved by the magic of their guilds, their own contingencies, or their guardian power (god or goddess). A cleric resurrection spell allows character’s to be brought back to life, however, if the player of the deceased character so desires; otherwise, they are simply drawn from their bind spot.

Maxim: House Winterborne is a "noble" house of Norrath, specifically the city of Qeynos, on the western shore of Antonica. We role-play that the House has a guild hall within the walls of North Qeynos even though the game mechanics don’t permit player/guild-owned property.

Other Maxims will be added to this list if they are needed to further our pursuit of role-playing.
