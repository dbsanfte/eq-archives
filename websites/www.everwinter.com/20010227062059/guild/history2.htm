Excerpts from the Journals of dr’Shivrehel Despana
((Twenty and Four Years Before the Orb Winter--Neriak, Norrath))

There is a human saying: “Discretion is the better part of valor.” My kind would call that cowardice, but survival has always been of paramount importance to a creature with cards of defiance and spite. Who am I to argue my nature? I play the hand I’ve been dealt and, like any gambler, I prefer to win.

Much has changed since I’ve been able to take up quill and jot down my thoughts. I long for the sanctity of my Spire. Would my hand have been different, I would have chosen a simpler life--perhaps a fisher. The waters have been frosted with white-caps of late. Lord Infernius of Mire is at the center of the whirlpool. In his cantankerous pride, he attempted to thwart my efforts to unite the Black clans and a rather bloody battle between his Clan and those under my control ensued. The shores of Fireshore were red with their spent life. Black scales littered the sands like shells, and bones washed up like driftwood.

Beauty aside, the Council intervened, summoning me to the Aerie. My control of three clans and meddling in numerous others was matched with another charge. A rather vain bronze named Olam and a self-righteous brass called Rianna followed me to Solon and reported their findings.  Mykell, the stubborn Custodian and puppet of the status quo, issued a decree that carried the punishment of death in a most barbaric manner. They wanted to revive the custom of breaking wings, plucking talons and fangs, and exiling me to Forbidden Isle. As tempting as that vacation was, I chose to invoke a portal to an adjacent plane.

I stepped to Norrath into the form of a seasoned dark elf in the underground city of Neriak. Over the last few days, I’ve used my knowledge of the theory of magic--thaumaturgy--to become an acolyte of the Necromancers’ guild. The truth is, while I understand a great deal about magic, I do not know the incantations of this land, and remain as weak as a babe. Only my ability to open rifts between worlds remains. I am treading carefully. They seek proof that I am not who I say I am, merely a commoner embracing the will of Innoruuk. I intended to give them no such information, but--

Today, I may have cracked the ice. The Teir’dal--the dark elven name for their people--were kind enough to teach me an minor incantation of shielding, as well as the spell to draw life from opponents. Thus armed, they expect me to travel to the Nektulos Forest and prove myself upon the creatures there. Such is the will of Innoruuk. I am not suited for combat here and make no such pretenses of grandeur. I have no items of magic in my possession, only the stained gray robe they hung over my shoulders and a dull dagger. I have no intentions of facing a shadow wolf so ill-prepared. Martyrs may make a good tale for their clergy, but I fancy a longer game.

There were merchants offering more spells for gold I did not possess and I can’t stomach begging, so I turned to the library. If I could learn more about these people and their magic, how difficult would it be for me to create my own incantations? I have seen dark elves with less intelligence than I summon a skeleton to do their bidding. Surely having someone fight for you, whether they be dead or alive, is preferable to facing a black bear.

My research in the library of the necromancers of the Teir’dal has resulted in several interesting notes on archmagi, powerful men capable of mastery over all the schools of magic of Norrath. I know I should be concentrating on blending in, but the people here are so different! I’ve always found it natural to study all fields of magic, yet they divide their study by school. There are enchanters, mages, and wizards in the Common District of Neriak, but the necromancers call them the Scorned, misled. My interest in visiting them grew over the first few days and finally, I searched for tales of a necromancer that employed other magic.

I found such a man among humans--imagine the surprise--who lived in a place called Erudin. His name was Miragul. Even in these libraries, so controlled by the guild and so distant from his homeland, he made an impact. This power could allow me a comfortable life on Norrath, prevent assassins from the Isles from posing a danger, and perhaps my vision could be realized with such influence and ability. I have heard of few, if any, of my kind on this plane. Who could stop me from bringing about reason... from destroying the gods that feed off our faith?

When I asked the guild master about him, he treated my interest as heresy, and my reputation was blighted already. I intend to conduct any future research more covertly. The ghouls guarding my room have pronounced my situation grave--no pun intended.

((Twenty and Four Years Before the Orb Winter--Neriak and Surefall, Norrath))

Again, I owe ye an apology, diary of mine. Times are desperate and I’ve been watched carefully.  I have tested some minor magic and gathered some supplies should a quick egress be called for.  Unfortunately, the laws of Norrath are very different from the Isles. What I’ve been able to do was alter my seeming. Now, that may sound like an impressive feat, but it carries with it a high price, as I’ll relate.

I was trying to clothe myself in an illusion, as the enchanters often do. As my dark elven visage faded, I found myself in the body of a middle-aged human man, perhaps thirty winters old.  Humanity has never been one of my preferred races. They live quickly and die with alacrity that terrifies even the coldest of elves. I would be quick pickings for the Teir’dal, save the fact I was no where near Neriak.

I learned that the rural town I had manifested in was known as Surefall, and was home to an order of druids and rangers, the Jagged Pine Folk. I listened to the people speak of the will of Tunare and mention a rain Lord known as Karana. I couldn’t even pretend to follow this Tunare, a power of life. But Karana... the demands were sparse. I could feign devotion to such a being. Thus, I undertook a quick study as a druid--perhaps the first godless druid.

I had little time to spare, and in short order, I resumed my dark elven being, returning to Neriak. I had, surprisingly, not been missed. Perhaps the ritual that moves me from form to form also hides my absence. It is an alien magic to the Teir’dal. They understand illusions, but this was true form.  I could not invoke my shielding spell, nor the ability to tap life forces as a human. I did master two short prayers, however. One healed wounds and the other fortified my skin with the strength of wood.

Ye may find it odd, my pursuit of the druidic magic. Consider this. They tap the anima of life around them. They say it is faith in Tunare or Karana, but I assure ye I have no faith. Rather, I have will and understanding of their magic. Their prayers carry the same mystic words the incantations of necromancers do, though with an emphasis upon the living and natural instead of the undead. Most will never see the similarities, but I suspect the clergy and the mages of this land have more in common than they’ve guessed. They need a guide, as all save a few of the eldest race, have from time to time.

If time and safety permits, I intend to return to Surefall. The fishing is superb and it is a quiet, idyllic dell. A man could pass his days in peace in such a glade. That lure remains as tempting as a grub is to the trout, though I always as though I’m swimming; if I stop, I shall sink forever beneath the waves. Though, isn’t that peace, too? That’s a poor mindset for the potential savior of the young races, but I can’t help the urges within me. A rest would be so refreshing, and yet I know that will not happen. My sense of duty to these poor, misled sheep won’t allow it. They need me, even if they’d never admit it.

I plan on continuing my investigation. I have found no sign of pursuit from the Council. Few of my kind have the ability to travel. Fewer still could locate this world. And, of them, who could master the magic of this place quickly enough to pose a threat? I rather hope there is no answer to that question.
