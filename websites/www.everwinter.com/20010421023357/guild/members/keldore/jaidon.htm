A small silver bird, a hybrid created by his father, sat on the sill of one of four windows in his bedroom, chirping lightly to Jaidon. Jaidon smiled at his small friend and dotted the air around the bird in sparkles. "My friend, I must say, your songs become more enchanting everyday. I'm afraid, however, that this is the last time for a good while that I will hear them. I depart with my father this evening." The bird's song became slower and heavier, and it eventually took wing. It did not want to hear of his leaving again.

Jaidon then pushed himself from the sill and took a glance around the spacious room. He would miss the large villa in the secluded, wooden valley near the city of RhyDin. He would miss the view over the glittering lake from his window. He would miss the Red Dragon Inn and its sultry barmaids and shady patrons. Shaking his head of these thoughts, he ran his hands over the smooth oak door. It closed.

He had released himself of his material possessions in this world. He was told that he could not take anything to Norrath with him. Earlier in the week, he had bid his very few true friends fond farewells. He had one more task to accomplish before he could go. He made his way to the comfortable common room. His father sat there, beside his mother.

"Can't you two keep your hands off each other for a second?" Jaidon laughed as he jibbed his father. "Do you really want another one?" His mirth hid his regret.

"Just you wait, Jaidon. I'm going to feed you to an ogre the second we set foot in Norrath. She will not be able to help you there." The two grinned, Vahnna did not.

"Old man, if you do anything to harm our son while you two traipse that damned realm, I'll make it so you'll never have another one!" She pinched his arm, hard. "Get over here, you, and hug me before you slay me with worry."

Jaidon slipped onto the couch at her other side and wrapped his thin--yet toned--arms around his mother.

"Well, it's time for us to be going." The elder Silverwand stood, the younger following suit. Keldore kissed his wife passionately, as usual, and then released her to the couch where she sighed. She did not want either of them going off. Jaidon was about to offer another snide remark, when his father turned to him. "Your sure about this?"

His reply was a mere nod. Jaidon had been preparing himself to follow his father to the land of Norrath for a good many months. He had only asked this of his parents a week ago, and so he had plenty of time to consider the matter and decide. He was sure, and he thought he was ready.

Saying good-bye to Vahnna again, the two left the common room and went to Keldore's laboratory in the East wing of the villa. The room was dark, save for a tiny globe that illuminated a large mirror on the far wall. The mirror itself was unadorned, and seemed to be nothing but a common reflecting glass. It was the only artifact in the room that was used on any consistent basis. This was where the four feet carried the two Silverwands.

"Remember, Jaidon, Norrath's magic is unlike anywhere else. It is incompatible with my own for the most part. This mirror will get you there, but exactly where and in what condition is anyone's guess. Where ever you end up, seek out the nearest city and enroll in the enchanter's guild if you can. You seem to be inclined that that discipline, and at least that should be consistent between worlds. Then, seek myself or any one of House Winterborne out. We'll get you settled into Qeynos to begin your training."

Jaidon's reply again was merely a nod. His excitement would allow no words.

The last thing that Jaidon remembered in his former world was a sensation of falling into a white mist.
