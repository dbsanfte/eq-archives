Thinking of Joining House Winterborne?

Our House is rather selective about who we accept. Class, race, and level have little bearing, but our mission calls for energetic role-players who can function as mature team-players, keeping the interests of the guild in mind. If you don’t enjoy enacting your character (role-playing), you’d be best off finding another guild more to your liking.

Pre-Joining

  • Minimums ~ Your primary character is level ten (10) or higher.

  • Step One ~ Familiarize yourself with our brand of role-playing.  In short, role-playing is: "The activity of using one's imagination and a set of agreed upon guidelines to interact with other roleplayers (RPer's) to act out the life and personality of a created character or persona."

  • Step Two ~ Review the House Law. Our rules are fairly lenient, but we rarely grant exception to those who break them. They exist to promote goodwill among our members and fellow guilds and to further role-playing within the EverQuest game.

  • Step Three ~ Determine how your character fits within the House. Review the House History.  Keep that in mind as you read the following--a guide for making a Winterborne character OR for adapting your present character to our premise:

    • You are an “animation” created by the archmagi Winter. For more information on what an animation is, see below {HERE}. We prefer new members enact characters that are animations. It is the most direct link to our long-term story-lines and our guild premise.

    • You are a family member, close friend, or lover of an animation (see above). The animation in question can either be an NPC or an alternate character of your creation.

    • You are a mercenary or vassal of a character that belongs to one of the first two classes mentioned above. We prefer that new members avoid this option if at all possible.

    • You are a follower of Lady Vox, the dragoness from Permafrost. You have been assigned by your mistress to assist House Winterborne.

The Application Process

  • Step One ~ State your interest in applying to the guild by composing a character history. This history should be a minimum of 500 words, and should be related to the Clan Lady or Lord who most fits your character's outlooks on life.  Clan information can be found {HERE}.  If you don’t care to describe your character to us, you aren’t interested in being a member of our guild. You can post your history on our message boards {HERE}.  Please register to use our message boards first.

  • Step Two ~ Gain the sponsorship of three active members of the guild. You can do this by adventuring online with these members, contacting them via e-mail, or interaction on the message boards.  We also require that you attend three guild events or outings prior to becoming a member of the guild.

When we confirm three sponsors and review your history, we will either accept or deny your application to the guild. We try to keep up on applications, but it may take as long as a week.  Denial does not mean you cannot re-apply later, but we are not obligated to explain our decision making process and why you’ve been denied.

What is an "Animation"?

An animation is not to be confused with the enchanter creation that bears the same name. The term was applied to Winter’s second generation of artificial life. Following the completion of similaricrum, creatures fashioned in the shape of others by made from snow and ice, Winter managed to mirror life in all important ways.

Winter’s children, or animations, are not golems or automatons. They have free will, living and dying like any other mortal race. They tend to be superior members of the races they are fashioned after, though they possess no unnatural abilities. Their personalities are their own.  Beyond what the rest of the world might think of an animation, they are impossible to detect without magical devices fashioned in Winter’s time. Even these artifacts cannot unerringly detect the presence of one of Winter’s children.

Over the last few years, both generations of Winter’s creations have begun to fail. The cause is unknown. Lady Vox’s similaricrums (ice goblins and ice giants) suffer more frequently than the second generation, but all can be affected. Malfunctions manifest in a myriad of ways, ranging from simple pain mimicking mundane arthritis, to acute headaches, blindness, failing organs, paralysis, or death. Madness is not unknown.

House Winterborne works to discover the reason for these malfunctions and, when they achieve that, a cure.
