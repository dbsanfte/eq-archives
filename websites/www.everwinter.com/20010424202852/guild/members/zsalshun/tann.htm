The little gnome bounced into the room. He always bounced, more or less, having about three times the energy that his tiny body could really hold. In fact, it was a dire day indeed when there wasn’t some sort of spring to the steps of the overactive deep gnome. Today was no exception.

"Hey Saph! Hey Tanner! How’s things? Just been to..well, I’m not quite sure where, but Meg and the clan have left Solon. Not sure why either, they were up in arms about er…fading or disintegrating or being spit out or something of that nature. Or was that their magic?" He paused in the narrative to scratch his bald pate. For one momentous moment the small deep gnome was lost in thought and quiet. However, the moment passed as briefly as it came and he punctuated his next sentence with a crinkled ear to ear smile. "I didn’t have any trouble…see? Still intact, even my magic, but I got sucked into the backwash of some spell or the other and BLAM I blew up a large portion of this new place. Well, that wasn’t my intention really but I’m glad cause otherwise I think I would have blown up the Arie again. Don’t tell Shiv OK?"

The genuine look of guilt and concern on the minute gray face caused Saphrael to burst out laughing. Tanner just shook his head, catching about one in eight words of what the infernal gnome was talking about. As usual, his graceful wife took the situation in hand and welcomed Tan back from his travels. The little gnome prattled on, perfectly oblivious to Tanner’s bemused expression as Saphrael listened intently to the narrative. The stately elven woman had worked more closely with Tan on Faerun and was able to follow the rapid speech with little loss of content.

"…AND I am more or less mostly a gnome now. At least I have been so on the last handful of lands I have visited." Again, the deep gnome grinned a wide toothy grin and accented it with a short bow to no one in particular. "Although, I wouldn’t mind finding myself in my true form every so often." The grin faded for a split second as his fingers absently fiddled with the odd dark speckled choker around his neck. The piece locked away his birthright and most of the powers associated with it and probably would even after the day the little gnome was laid to rest.

Tan shrugged as his face brightened once more. "Course, it’s much safer to be a gnome in most of these places eh? Well, if I want my wings and tail I suppose I could always step into Faerun and hope. Nuff of that, where’d everyone wind up?"
