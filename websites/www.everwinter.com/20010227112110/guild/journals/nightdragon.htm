How many ages have past since I first began running? I can no longer count the years, fled as I flee. The Night Dragon pursues me as death pursues the lesser races, infecting all I hold dear. I am the living refutation of his faith. My existence is an anathema to him, a role I chose over eternal slavery. Our race is beyond such toil, though the Elders of our kind would have ye believe otherwise. His worship rises among the clans I have nae influenced—Blackwater, Boghold, Nighthunt, and Nightshriek. Lurker wavers and Mire survives only because I drove off Inferius, already a servant of the Night Dragon, no doubt intent on the title I abandoned. Only Blackmoon remembers his treachery, understands the corruption of what he offers.

But that is far away, back in the Isles. In Neverwinter, his claws rend me in a differently, though more deeply. Vespre has succumbed to his foul rot, though she died believing it was the punishment for any Teir’Dal—the name for the dark elves in her land of Norrath—who went against the will of Innoruuk. I know the truth, heard the shout of his clergy. Call him the Despoiler, the Father of Hate and Undeath… whatever the case, Faluzure the Night Dragon and Innoruuk are the same. He stole my love, tortured her for supporting me. And for that must I find a way to elude him, defeat him.

On the wake of her death, I feel desperate. Who would follow? I shall brave his rotting lair and play my hand against his bloated, degenerate might. I have warned my brethren in this exiled Council, but many show their metallic natures—brave past the call of bravery. And I ask, who am I to wave them away from the battle? The Night Dragon is as wrong to their senses as it is to mine, though the matter is personal. How can they hope to distract him long enough?

And what of this young body, this op’Lokisun, brother of Eclipsed and exile from the land of the dark elves? Their love, though wrought malignant incest, is as true as any I’ve seen… but he would have perished were it not for my will. His life is mine to borrow, a life that bares part of my essence and would die without it. She will hate me more than any other is capable of, and yet if I should perish, what future would await her, Loki, or any of our kind? Surely death would be a release from the alternative I have lived… hopeless servitude in an order of oppressive decay.

And still, the doubt nags at me. Am I truly important or is my defiance merely a reflection of my nature. As a red must be the bully, am I defined by my spite? I have tried to enlighten my race, and yet I am heralded as doom-incarnate, a bad omen to scare vassals and hatchlings, a reviled villain among the grown. They call me the Dark Song, a lament of what even the most morbid of my scaled race can spawn. And so, I embrace this dirge that I have become. I hear it striving against the cacophony of the chaotic urges of the Night Dragon, ever hungry, ever eating. My power is born of will, nae the bondage of faith. If this makes me singer of the elegy that ends the Elders’ deception, than so be it. I will bare the weight.

Into the astral we shifted, my personality dominating what survived his soul-leeching in the Gray Wastes. I tracked the color pool that would take me to Toril, free at long last from Faluzure… and there she was.

Chronepsis, goddess of our time upon the planes, speechless since her rape by her brother… she who amid the screech of despair gave birth to the First Borne… my mother. She gazed upon Loki’s form, but into my eyes, the child that had claimed his Hour Glass before his time, the child who had stole his immortality. I cannot give words to the feeling, but my soul, whatever it’s fate, was torn from the body I inhabited, destroyed in a swell of purging fire that left Loki alive and free of me… and the skeletal form of me floating in the waves of the color pools, a hollow husk.

Then, ye ask, how is it my pen still moves, still labors to move thought from mind to parchment? I claim a miracle, by Io’s will. Beyond the Elder powers, the ancient among our race, even more ancient than me, there is a force… perhaps a God, though more likely the very will behind the universe… and that force has chosen me establish the Order that shall be the last buttress against the slavery of life. I claim I am the Dirge, the song that speaks of lament for resolution. I have be slain by the most powerful of those who claim to be divine… and I survive, arisen without soul to be sold to these mocking powers.

So this is the my self-birth, the emergence of a stronger dragon. Let the world dance to my song. Every land the slavery oppresses… I shall infiltrate, quietly at first, then building volume as the tempo increases. Against the chaos, I shall stand.

His quarters in the Aerie were dark, as befit his nature. He kept the candles burning, refusing to use glow gems, even those that flickered with the dullness of muted faerie fire. He preferred the simplicity of the candles, the erratic games between the flickering light and evasive shadow. His walls were a mural of woven silhouettes, a quilt work of shade and the yellowed offspring of fire and wax.

Dirge tugged on his slender finger, twisting a phantom ring. He looked down and frowned. Violet eyes regarded the bare finger with a coldness rivven would have found terrifying in its depth. He leaned back, tilting his head and closing those purpled irises. He sighed to himself. Exhalations were a delight to a wracked, aged seeming.

The dark elf thought back on the past few days, questioning himself, and following the dream-path to the inevitable conclusion, staring at a crude painting he brushed.

Turning from the lights of the tree, Rianna pulled her cloak about her form and motioned with her head for Dirge to follow. Striding quickly to the slightly open door of the hall she stepped out and stood for a moment in the light of the moon. Taking a deep breath to brace herself, she swept her hands open and allowed her true form to take it's proper place.

Dirge noticed the brass scales, the firm length of wing, and yet there was also a noticeable absence of the power that used to surround her. Her scales did not gleam with the shine that they used to, her golden eyes seemed almost sunken in her massive head. The movement of her wings was almost too fast to watch, and yet to his eyes, they were slow, lethargic. Saying nothing, keeping his thoughts to himself as he was prone to do, he took his own form beside her.

The wind that whipped through the hall at their leaving swirled the faerie lights about the tree and brought another twinkle of bells to those in the hall...

The night had dropped the temperature and Rose was beginning to shiver. The fire had died down a bit. Returning with more kindling wood, Mel got the fire roaring once again. Johan was in a fitful rest. Rose slept quietly. She knew she was strong enough today to fly Johan back to the castle, but she could not carry both and leaving her daughter for even a moment scared her to death. She had to wait.

She could feel Dirge and Rianna as they searched. There presence grew stronger and Mel reached for them. Linking minds she told them of her location. Before long she was hugging her brother and sister. Even the coolness of Dirge felt warm to her. "What can we do to help?" Rianna whispered.

"We can mend them better at Castle Graye. Johan is stable enough to carry now," Mel replied. Dirge bent over the sleeping Rose and softly cradled her into his arms. His white hair surrounding her slight form as he bent over her. "We shall not fly" he smiled and in a wave of magic and the rush of displaced air, he was gone.

"Show off." Mel looked at Rianna. "Sister I am too weak for such a use of magic. Can you take Johan? I will simply fly back."

Rianna hesitated. "We can fly together" "

"No, Johan can use the aid quickly and I will be fine from here."

Red knew there was no use. She nodded and gathered Johan in her arms. "But Mel, How did you get her in this shape?"

"I shall tell you at the castle" Red nodded and was gone.

With her family cared for, the worry slipped away from her mind as she shifted and soared above the tree line... to the castle she headed...

The dark elf set little Rose in her quarters. His cock-eyed smile was a contrast to his sharp features. Dirge winked at Rose, putting the tip of his black nailed finger into her little fist. She squeezed and sent him a thought-scene. **Your cold!**

He chuckled. His replied with a gentle thought. **And thou art sweet, little one. Thy mother and father are well. They wilst be with thee soon, Rose.**

**Daddy!**

Dirge grinned despite himself. He thought of the burly warrior known as Earl Graye being called "Daddy". It was… immeasurably… warm. **Thy daddy misses thee, too, little one, but he’ll be tickling thee soon enough.**

He pulled the homespun up, tucking the child in her crib of solid gold. Stepping away from the crib, he noticed a tiny insect perched on one bar. He smiled to himself. **Take good care of her, Calmire**

The dark elf stepped from the room and pulled the anima tightly around him, like he had the blanket around Rose. He stepped into the rift, reappearing hundreds of miles to the north, deep in the Moondark Mountains, in the Aerie. He strode through the draconian-sized halls, fragile among their towering pillars and the ancient throne.

His caverns were rather spartan compared to the lush lairs some of the wyrms maintained. They were simple and sized for a rivven, not a dragon. The oaken door opened inward…

Compared to his stable-adjacent quarters at Castle Graye, his suite in the Aerie was an improvement, but that was simply due to the grand scale of the home of the dragons. As in the Castle, a desk was the focus of the room, and like the Terran Greeks had kept their focus burning, at all costs, his desk was the altar devoted to a single, ever-burning candle. Two spirals of an unknown mineral floated about the candle, like Moebius-strip Ioun stones. In truth, the two stones were gifts from a three-millennium dead mage, Congenio Ioun. They were a reward to Dirge, who had served to aid the faern. He kept their powers hidden, never knowing when they might aid him.

He walled around the room, looking intently over the many cases of books. The titles were diverse and obscure: Nadul Daroni’s Sketches of High Horn. Briel’s Book of Shadows. The Pages of Pain. Alphon’s Eyes, Visions, and Arcane Sight. The Fiendomicon of Iggwilv. The Lost History of V’elddrinnshar. Libram of Twelve Seals.

Instead of choosing one of the tomes, he regarded the bottles stored above the shelves. His selections of wines were as vast and baffling as his books. Finally he paused, remembering the recent delivery made by a certain elf from the Sword Sheath Clan. He turned, and squinted at the note on the keg, grinning. "Ah, this will do quite nicely." His voice was a whisper, deep and as dark as the unnatural shadows in the room.

His door almost burst from the frame as burly Olam plunged into his quarters. "Dirge… you better come to the Grand Hall. This is… important." His face was tight with pain. The dark elf was accustomed to the Elder’s outbursts, but the great bronze was rarely so urgent. He dealt with problems in his own brash manner. He followed the armored man out, into the gargantuan great room. It took him a moment to take in the situation…

The dragons were assembled in their various seemings, a collective look of profound sadness on their faces. Rianna sat in her throne. The dark elf could see the length of her hair, the nape of her neck. He looked to Olam, but his friend was silent. Rianna stood, her voice softer than he could remember.

"My kin, I have kept much from ye these last few months, and ye are deserving of an answer..."

Sighing deeply, she whispered a word of magic and dropped the illusion she had maintained for the last few months. Her sunken eyes and brass scales were spotted with large black areas of decay… the true nature of her sickness.

No one could see the narrowing of Dirge’s eyes, the extent of his pain.

"I am dying. I have been dying. It has been a long and hard winter for me. I have led this Council for many months and now find myself weak with age, worry, and frustration, as well as afflicted with this illness. I have searched long and hard for a cure, and as yet I have not found one. I have hope that I will find one somewhere on Faerun, but it will require a very long journey for me. During that time I will be unable to maintain even the limited contact I have had with the Council.

"It is therefore in the best interest of this council that I step down from my position. I have great faith in the leader that shall take my place."

She motioned to Dirge. His expression was hidden under the mass of unruly white hair. Olam could see, though, though he had no need of looking.

"Dirge shall take over the mantle of leadership and all that it entails. It is my hope that this transition will be easy for all of you. Olam will also aid in this transition. It is my belief that the Council can move forward in the manner that we have wished it to, and with the advent of all the new dragons this shall open up more possibilities for everyone. I shall begin my journey as soon as the changes seem stable. May you all have great adventures and profitable travels. Perhaps someday when I have found a cure for that which is ailing me I will return."

The great bronze hugged his queen and kissed her cheek. He'd seen the things she'd hidden from the others and fought back the tears that swelled forward.

"My queen, I know this is Io's will and I hope he shall guide you in all you do. I hope you find your cure and your return is swift."

He looked to Dirge, and continued, but the pain was evident. "My loyalty is to the Council, you have my concurrence." Was it blame in his words? If it was Dirge could not hold that ill will against him. He had earned it.

The dark elf looked down, his eyes brimming with tears of blood. "Sister, it is with a grave heart I accept this post. I hold if for thee and long for thy cure. Ye know I will do all I can for the Council, whilst thou art gone. Not for my fellow dragons. Not for myself. Not for the Will of Io. For thee, thee, thee..." He turned, and walked back to his simple quarters in the Aerie, his skeletal shoulders swaying to the dirge that only he heard.

Nodding to each dragon as she passed, Rianna walked slowly through the halls and to her quarters, to begin the packing for the long travels that lay ahead.

A soft knock was heard upon the Queen's door. At the soft greeting, Johan and the pale figure of her mentor, he who was called White Leopard by the brass clans, entered the chamber of their Queen.

The Earl spoke, trying to console her. "Rianna, thou doth know Melody and I shalt sorely miss thee. Remember lass all things art possible in this land if thou doth have enough faith. Thou wilt be in me prayers." He kissed her brow lightly, looked to White, and left, closing the door behind him.

Leopard had never been a talkative one, but the tension brought words to his reticent lips. "My queen? I am saddened by what has happened, but I am even more saddened that you did not confide in me. We have been through much together in the past and so I ask a humble favor from you before you leave. Allow me to go with you as your guard. There are many out there who would take advantage of you present condition and I could not live with myself if I just watched you leave without a companion and friend whom to share your long and difficult journey. Will you grant me this one favor, my queen?"

She nodded, and they packed in silence.

Sighing deeply as she stood before the final gates to leave, Rianna addressed all of those who had come with their wishes. Many stood and fought the tears, some showing anger, and some shock. "My kin, it was with great sadness that I made the decision to leave. I have no wish to do so, and yet I cannot risk any getting the illness that I have. It is for the good of all dragons, the truth I have stood behind in these many years, that I do this. When I have gained the cure I shall return. I shall keep in touch as I am able. I will retain the link granted me by the will of Io and will truly miss you all."

She looked to White, standing to the side. "My brother, long have we fought together, I can still recall the day you found and brought me here. I will not deny thy request, for surely I shall need you in the days that come. As my illness brings me down I shall need thy strength. I humbly accept thy request, and know that we shall return together, strong once again, on some future day."

She motioned to all the new dragons. "There are many that will come forward in the days ahead to take up the mantles we are leaving behind. The council shall ever remain strong with the blood of these new ones. I wish each of you safe journeys...."

A final tear cut its way down her cheek and Rianna stepped next to Leopard, beginning her long journey. Olam clenched his fists and bit back a howl of anger against the sense of futility. In the shadows not so far from the archer, Dirge’s malevolent violet eyes were shut tightly, but he saw it all… and felt the heart slip from the Council.
