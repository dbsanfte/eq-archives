I. Thou shalt represent thy House well.

All guild members are required to enact their characters in an in-character, role-playing fashion when speaking aloud (shout or speaking). Out-of-character comments must be designated as OOC, either through “OOC:” or double parenthesis surrounding the comment.  Roleplaying is central to House Winterborne. We are willing to teach those with an interest, but we seek skilled role-players as recruits.

II.  Thou shalt nae create discord among thy fellows.

All guild members are expected to behave in a mature fashion. No harrassment, profanity, or vulgarity will be tolerated. No hacking will be accepted. Dynamic role-playing is acceptable, but consider the interests and welfare of your guild-mates. Arguments are good sources of character development, but petty bickering adds nothing positive to the gaming experience.

III.  Thou shalt reply the wishes of House officers promptly and to the best of thy abilities.

Electronic mail from guild officers (indicated on the roster) should be answered promptly and with as much detail as needed. Administration functions should be secondary to the role-playing; officers who donate their time to these tasks should be treated with respect. If a player is going on a leave of absence of more than two weeks, we request notification.

IV.  Thou shalt be permitted inheritance and assistance from the wealth of t’ House.

House Winterborne allows what is commonly referred to as “twinking”, giving items to other characters that are beyond their means. We prefer these cases of gifting aren’t exorbitant. Use your best judgement.

V.  Treasure ye gain shall be offered first t’ the benefit of thy House mates.

When items are found, before auctioning them through the general channels, make certain the guild members have a five (5) day period to purchase them for a fair price. Gifts are always welcome, but we understand characters earn their “living” on their finds.

VI.  Combat with other power-groups is permitted, as well as duels and Arena battles.

PvP is supported by the House within the boundaries of the Neverwinter Consortium servers.

NOTE:

Our guild is not “aligned” and not exclusive. This means characters of all moral and ethical stances can be found within the group. Likewise, players are free to have other characters in other guilds. Our guild exists to add to Neverwinter gaming experience, providing a dynamic story-line for role-playing, as well as traditional guild benefits. Maturity among our members is expected and exceptions are quickly dealt with.

Alternate/Secondary Characters:

Once a player is accepted into House Winterborne, they may add up to two alternate characters to the roster provided they have submitted a seperate character history. Exceptions to this rule may be granted by guild officers.



Neverwinter doesn’t provide a great deal of structure for some serious role-playing issues. The guild has agreed to some ideas to provide a bit of continuity and depth to our role-playing.

Maxim: A character’s true class, and even gender or race, may differ greatly from the game mechanics. A human might be "part" Tanar'ri and exhibit traits such as claws, hissing, and the like. A druid might consider himself a conjurer, or even a ranger. Accept the role-playing and ignore the game mechanics.

Maxim: A character’s level doesn’t indicate their power or strength. Surname’s indicate an acknowledgment of the citizens of Norrath or the character’s guild. As a guild, we may role-play a fourth level necromancer as a powerful character (the equivalent of a 20th level character) or a 18th level enchanter, as a young and unworldly prodigy. This is left up to the player, though we encourage some realism.

Maxim: Not all characters are natives of the Forgotten Realms (Toril). Characters from other worlds may well find themselves in Neverwinter. Some characters within the guild hail from other online games, such as Ultima Online, the Realm, Norrath (EQ), or the Dark Ages of Camelot setting. Such histories are encouraged and can be worked into the history of Neverwinter; said characters are plane-travelers; they may have a much weaker form in Neveriwnter, perhaps taking time (experience) to master magic, or the laws of the world.

Maxim: Real life time limitations do not equate to a character being absent from Neverwinter. Characters can be assumed to have an existence beyond the time their player enacts them. If the player is absent for three weeks, perhaps the character has been at work learning a language, a trade skill, visiting a relative, spying on an enemy, or even on another plane. Character absence, particularly when a player has an extended leave, *can* be explained by the player, but it isn’t required.

Maxim: A character does not die when they loose a battle. Instead, we assume that a character that falls in battle is saved at the last moment by a "death pact" within the House; this Winterborne character is teleported away to a safe spot. Other characters outside of the guild might be saved by the magic of their guilds, their own contingencies, or their guardian power (god or goddess). A cleric resurrection spell allows character’s to be brought back to life, however, if the player of the deceased character so desires; otherwise, they are simply drawn from their bind spot.

Other Maxims will be added to this list if they are needed to further our pursuit of role-playing.
