After over two years, I've finally made it back to Kaladim.

After exchanging pleasantries with the guards at the enterance to the city, I venture forth into the caves. Familiar faces of city guards and royal bodyguards greet me in Southern Kaladim. Ah, there it is...Kal's Pub...I remember spending many a late night in that place. Outside Kal's, I notice my friend Kilam, the bard, trying to talk some young dwarf warrior into delivering a package half way across Norrath for him. I listen with a smirk on my face, as I hear him deliver the same pitch he hooked me with many years ago. As the youngster carries the package off, I whisper, "Got another one on the list, heh Kilam?" We laugh about the private joke that the bards have been pulling on young adventurers for years, the joke that Kilam let slip one night after having an ale too many. The important letters that need to be delivered all around Norrath are simply a list of the names of all the people that the bards could sucker into carrying them!

Bards with a sense of humor, who knew!

Kilam offers to buy the first round at Kal's, and says he'd like to hear about my journeys. I know that I should really report in to Gunlok Jure about my travels, but it's been a long trip from the docks, my shield arm is acting up again and a Dwarven ale would go down real smooth right about now. I also recall the words of my good friend Brombeard Brewmeister, that an ale or two, purely for medicinal purposes, mind you, is the best tonic of all. And Kilam is buying...

Five hours, and many ales later... "Hustrun Vallahir, reporting in sir," I say after standing at attention and saluting Master Jure. I struggle to stay awake, let alone at attention, as Gunlok Jure takes his time acknowledging me, half-heartedly shuffling through the piles of paperwork on his desk. After what seems an eternity, during which I have repeatedly cursed Brombeard and his cure all tonics, Master Jure looks up at me and says, "At ease."

I reach down to my backpack and fumble through it, looking for the log of my adventures. "There's time enough for that, Hustrun. Follow me." Master Jure says, as he walks out of his office. I follow, expecting the enevitable...

Whenever I had erred in an assignment or misson in my youth, Master Jure would escourt me to the warriors arena in South Kaladim and "teach" me some advanced fighting techniques to improve my chances of surviving in the wilds of Norrath. I'd always end up bruised and battered, eating the arena dirt, after every one of those "lessons".

Expecting more of the same, I was surprised when he turned towards the temple of Brell, instead of towards the bank, and South Kaladim. He stopped at the door and simply ordered me, "Enter."

The two brass door opened before me. Two robed acolytes stood before me, looked at me as I walked through the doorway, then turned and slowly walked towards the main temple hall. As I followed, the smell of incense was overwhelmed my sences. That, coupled with the dim light radiating from the dozen or so candles in the hall and my earlier consumption of "Brell's finest" at Kal's, I battled to remain awake and on my feet. Before me, upon the dias, was the High Priestess of Brell, Ghalea.

She commanded me. "Kneel."

And upon my doing so, the priestess presented me with a silver goblet, saying,"Drink from this, the Chalace of Conquest, given to us by Brell's own hand. Know now what holy mission he demands of you!"

Slowly, with great reverance, I accepted the chalace and drank of it's contents. Ale!

It was difficult to surpress a smile. Never before had I drank it's like, and I probably never would again...at least not in this lifetime. As I savoured the holy drink, the High Priestess again spoke, softly this time, "Meditate now, young Paladin. And learn..."

I remained kneeling and closed my eyes.

Pictures of people flashed before my eyes...

Brombeard, Artes, Meer, Jerhan, Balkan, Zsalshun,...dozens of others that I have never met. These faces fade...

I see the sky...an overcast sky.

From behind a cloud appears a majestic creature...a dragon!With scales of ivory, circling overhead.

She suddenly disappears into the clouds from which she came.

The clouds then move, violently, through the sky...

A form takes shape from the very clouds themselves...

A Dwarf...

BRELL!!

"These are your allies, your friends and they shall protect and aid you, as you shall protect and aid them. Together you shall discover the means to rid this land of the evil that infests, corrupts and destroys. This is your quest. This is my will."

I feel the cold stone of the temple floor as I awake.

I look around me, there are no priests or acolytes, no candles lit, no incense burning...

I lift myself from the stone floor and walk towards the closed temple doors. Before them, I see my weapons, armor, backpack...I understand.

Walking through South Kaladim, I turn to see Kal's pub, and spy Kilam entering. "No, not today...," I say to myself aloud, continuing towards the city gates, "I have work to do."
