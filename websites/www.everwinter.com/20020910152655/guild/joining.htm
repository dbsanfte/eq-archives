Thinking of Joining House Winterborne?

Our House is rather selective about who we accept. Class, race, and level have little bearing, but our mission calls for energetic role-players who can function as mature team-players, keeping the interests of the guild in mind. If you don’t enjoy enacting your character (role-playing), you’d be best off finding another guild more to your liking.

Pre-Joining

  • Step One ~ Familiarize yourself with our brand of role-playing.  In short, role-playing is: "The activity of using one's imagination and a set of agreed upon guidelines to interact with other roleplayers (RPer's) to act out the life and personality of a created character or persona."

  • Step Two ~ Review the House Law. Our rules are fairly lenient, but we rarely grant exception to those who break them. They exist to promote goodwill among our members and fellow guilds and to further role-playing within the Neverwinter Nights Consortium community.

  • Step Three ~ Determine how your character fits within the House. Review the House History.

The Application Process

  • Step One ~ State your interest in applying to the guild by composing a character history. This history should be a minimum of 500 words, and should be related to the Clan Lady or Lord who most fits your character's outlooks on life.  Clan information can be found {HERE}.  If you don’t care to describe your character to us, you aren’t interested in being a member of our guild. You can post your history on our message boards {HERE}.  Please register to use our message boards first.

  • Step Two ~ Gain the sponsorship of two active members of the guild. You can do this by adventuring online with these members, contacting them via e-mail, or interaction on the message boards.

When we confirm three sponsors and review your history, we will either accept or deny your application to the guild. We try to keep up on applications, but it may take as long as a week.  Denial does not mean you cannot re-apply later, but we are not obligated to explain our decision making process and why you’ve been denied.
