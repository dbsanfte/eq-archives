Mingling With Shadows

Posted by Zsalshun D'Vox on July 25, 2000:

Zsalshun stretched and watched a group slaughter a treant. She smiled; she did not think she would ever tire of watching the menacing trees fall. She thought to tarry a bit longer but she had a long trip ahead of her. With the blessings of the wolf still fresh from Shar’s last casting, the priestess headed out to Lake Rathatier and the dark swamps beyond that lay between here and her homeland. Tonight she was going to meet a new menace; hopefully one that had more clues to the Winter ailment.

The priestess frowned at the thought. Again, she was returning to her roots but this time it was not to hide but to seek. She had come to terms with the fact that her research was quicker and easier within the confines of the guild but she still felt uneasy dealing with many of the members. Her thoughts were cut short by a perfunctory whisper in her mind **Get to the Druid Rings in Northern Karana, Priestess** Morphite’s dark tones of impatience cut through her musings and the Priestess turned back on her trail. The enchanter had arranged for a teleport and the Tier’ Dal could almost see him foaming with impatience. It did save her the unpleasantness of wading through miles of swamp life to get to the far side of the world.

After being dropped off in the midst of a coven of druids and their servant trees, Zsalshun ran invisible to the borders of her homeland. Here there were no giant trees hell bent on destroying her. A quick run through the forests of her child hood brought her to the land of lava and the new menace to conquer; Shadowed men.

Keldore, Amorsaylnn, Morphite, Trespare and Birik were already scouting out the shadowed men when the priestess arrived. She nodded to each and began casting the customary shielding spells. The group had cordoned off a section below the shadow’s camp and used the calming magics of Trespare to lure one at a time to their battlefield. It worked well and many of the invisible men fell to the blades and spells of the group. Nothing, save a few words, were found on the bodies.

Later that eve, a familiar “Hello” bellowed out of the night and the priestess smiled as she recognized Balkin’s stout visage. There were too many Winterborne to fight effectively so it was decided to split into two groups and step up the slaughter. With so many available, both groups made short work of the shadowed men and a call was made to take their camp directly.

The shadowed men were no match for the lightening blades of the stout dwarf and lithe ranger and soon, as at the base of the camp, the Winterborne frightened the enemy into being more cautious. Fewer shadows came to try to retake their land so that the dark priestess even got to help with melee and cast her limiting damage spell. All was going smoothly, albeit with little progress as to why the shadowed menace chose to take arms against the lady Vox, until Balkin excused himself. Amorsaylnn, Keldore and Zsalshun sat down to med and mend, leaving the few scouts to the other team.

Moments later, a cry of alarm rose up as the other team realized Trespare also had excused herself to attend to other matters quickly. Suddenly, the hill top was alive with Wizards meleeing and priests attempting to out damage the shadow scouts and all hoping vainly the warriors returned soon. The casters held for a brief time, then the shadows became bold in the face of those not trained to wield a blade and pushed hard. Morphite called a retreat and everyone narrowly escaped.

At the bottom of the hill Amorsaylnn and Keldore said their farewells and went about their business. Nothing was learned as to the reason for the shadow men’s aggression towards the Winter cursed and the remainder of the group faded away to continue on with their own musings. The dark Priestess took one last look at the hilltop camp and nodded to her adversaries. She would be back to discover what they knew, but now she had business to attend to in Nerriak.

Posted by Morphite on July 27, 2000:

The groups power, indeed had impressed Morphite. The enchanter had fallen not once, but twice, during each of their previous engagements. It seemed a ritual Vox had organized for him. He could almost hear her say to her minions, "follow Morphite, see to it that he perishes each day . . . no, twice each day!" 'Bitch,' he uttered. He hated her. He hated her mysterious way of making him learn everything in the most difficult way. It wasn't until she had allowed Shar, the barbarian to find a mysterious parchment of tactics for his kind, that everything started to make sense. He was indeed ready to set up that inn in South Karana, and retire from adventure. Perhaps Vox sensed that and decided to test Winterborne's cohesiveness by giving the knowledge to Shar and not him. No matter. The group passed the test and grew more powerful each day.

No, this day Morphite would not fall. It was amazing to him that this rag-tag group of Vox's gathering was indeed measuring up to what she wanted. What that was, who knew. But he was caught smiling and even exclaiming his joy once or twice, at the group's prowess.

And so it was, with that confidence and excitement, that he called to take the rift site of the shadow men. Indeed, why not? They had succeeded in keeping the site clean. Why not continue the fight to the very site of their entrance to Norrath?

Well, there are many reasons not to do this. It's difficult to coordinate the splitting of the beasts. As they were brought down in pairs, they tended to return in pairs. The distance that Trespare and Balkin had run before allowed them to split them up. Now, however, wizards and clerics were forced into melee until the melee-types were available to engage. No, that decision would not be made again. Indeed, even with the nasty terrain Lavastorm, they groups did well until the call to engage the rift directly. No, that call would not be made again.
