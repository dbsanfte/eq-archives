The dazzling crystal rapped on Windheavin’s door. Her clothes were torn and dirty. Her normally pale blue skin was taking on an odd copper hue and was rather weathered looking from the wind and sun. The trek from Lake Kiij to the new Aerie was a long and frightful one, but with Orgrum’s assistance, she knew it had been much easier going than it could have been.

"Fallon, ye’ve returned!" Maddie, the young selkie, opened the door and threw her arms around Fallon, squeezing her sparkling green eyes shut tight, fearing Fallon might dissipate before her eyes…again. Fallon hugged Maddie back and smoothed her gray-blonde hair. She smelled faintly of peppermint and gardenia. "Maddie, darling, I am returned. Have no fear of that, child." Fallon struggled to be attentive to the young girl, but so many things had changed. Normally, she’d fret over every little thing about the selkie, from whether she was eating enough to who was mending her clothes while she was away. This time though, she was too weary to dwell on anything beyond the girl’s safety. She’d made it safely to the Aerie and was under Windheavin’s care. That was enough for Fallon. She cradled Maddie’s face in her hands, "Is Windheavin in, child?"

"Yes, ma’am. Last I spoke with him, he was in his study, researching some new maps of Norrath he came across." Maddie saw Fallon look around and realized this was her first time at the new Aerie. "I can show ye to the study, m’lady." The young selkie’s eyes lit up. She held hands with Fallon and walked her through a series of adjoining receiving rooms and corridors until she finally reached an etched crystal door that almost reached the top of the vaulted ceiling. The designs on the door were beautiful, visions of Windheavin’s no doubt. Whether or not he was actually the one to fashion the door, Fallon was certain he’d had some input on the design. She smiled approvingly and followed Maddie into the study. A study wasn’t what Fallon would’ve called it though. On a much grander scale, this study was a full-fledged library. There were aisles and aisles of books, neatly organized. Fallon mused over who had the delightful task of organizing them. She walked down the main aisle, scanning the titles on the shelves and running a finger over the bindings. As she moved down the aisle, she noticed a halfling stooped precariously on a table looking over a pile of papers. She nodded to Maddie, who quietly returned the way they had come.

Fallon walked down the aisle to where the halfling was sitting. "Brother, it has been ages upon ages that I’ve seen ye take on thy seeming. Nae, would I say it doth not become thee. For it does. Tis just a bit surprising." Fallon’s eyes welled over with tears and she knelt over and hugged Windheavin.

"Fallon, aren’tcha a sight fer sore eyes! Heh, even in rags, you’re still the pinnacle of beauty. Welcome home, lass! Been keepin’ an eye on the search parties that’re out lookin’ fer ya and some other folks who missed the Spire. I knew ya’d make it back safe and sound though." Windheavin beamed. "Care for some tea?"

The dazzling crystal choked back the tears, "That would be divine, m’lord."

Over hot cups of tea, Windheavin, the Lord of Clan Starlight, shared the news of all that had happened within the Conclave while Fallon was away. Fallon’s normally cheerful visage was clouded with worry. She’d heard a few rumors upon her arrival at the Aerie, which was why she came to see Windheavin straight away.

"It’s been pretty peaceful here on Solon, things remaining quiet, as most of the rabble rousers of the Conclave have left to seek adventure in the land of Norrath." Windheavin chortled to himself and sipped his tea. "That’s the reason I came back here. I don’t get a moment’s peace in Norrath! Mind ye, I don’t mind, I rather like all the company. There’s never a dull moment with our guild brethren. But sometimes, sometimes, even I need a moment’s rest from the daily rigmarole of the guild."

"Aye, I was told ye were in charge of the guild there. I also heard thou art a fine leader there. Everyone has taken a shine to thee. Not to say there would be any doubt, m’lord. Ye’ve been with the Conclave since long before myself. Ye’ve come to know what it means to be a part of this family. It is pleasing to see that ye’ve found kindred spirits in the new realm of Norrath to take under our wing."

Windheavin sighed heavily then. Fallon noted earlier that the Crystal Lord seemed rather pensive. His sigh confirmed it. "What else is there, m’lord? I have heard things. Tis the real reason I’ve come. I fear ye carry a heavy burden, more so than mere leadership duties of our fine guild. Do tell, m’lord, that I may ease that burden in some way."

"Oh, Fallon, ya always were the doting one." Windheavin patted Fallon’s hand warmly and looked into her eyes. The sadness she found in his was overwhelming. "I got this note here from Dirge the other day." He pulled a wrinkled piece of parchment from his coat. "Seems him and a few others realized their calling and it doesn’t involve the Conclave. And after a lot of thought, they’ve decided to part with the Conclave and apply themselves to their new goals in Norrath. Ole Dirge has handed me the torch, if ya will. I gotta tell ya, Fallon, we were all quite surprised by this whole turn of events."

Fallon grasped absently at the pendant around her neck and stared at the scattered maps on the table, not seeing any of them. "As am I, m’lord…as am I." After a moment of silence, she lifted her head and frowned questioningly at Windheavin. "Did he name those that would be leaving with him?"

Fallon’s anxious look pained Windheavin. He knew how close she was to Shar. Would she also leave to be near her charge? He sighed again, "Aye, and Shar was counted among them, o’course. I am sure ya would be welcome to join their ranks if ya wish to be near her. I know how much she means to ya."

For a moment, Fallon sat in a stupor. My, how things could be turned inside out in such little time, she thought. Had she been gone so long? What brought this about? Who else was leaving? Were they now the Conclave’s ally or enemy? If their goals had changed so much, perhaps they would be enemies. There were so many strange things to this she simply could not reconcile.

Finally, Fallon smiled softly at Windheavin. A frown clouded her coppery blue face. "That is most gracious of thee, brother. I wish there was but another choice. I care not to leave ye, though I am torn…"

She turned her head then and watched the sunlight play upon the beautiful stained glass panels that surrounded the top of the walls of the library. Windheavin understood and sat in silence until she was ready to speak again. After a cleansing sigh, she looked at Windheavin, her beloved brother. "Windheavin…I…I am exhausted from my journey from Guntu, a tale I’ve yet to share with thee. But I must not tarry until I speak with Shar and the others. I really must know the reasons behind this faction."

The dazzling crystal bowed before the halfling lord, kissing the back of his hand lightly. Her silent tears did not go unnoticed. "I understand, dear Fallon. I understand."
