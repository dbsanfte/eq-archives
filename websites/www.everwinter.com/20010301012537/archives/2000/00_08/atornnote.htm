A Torn Note

Posted by Rackin Di'Githyen on August 21, 2000:

A torn excerpt of a journal written in Tier’Dal dated quit some time ago*

To all those of the House of Winterborne,

I Rackin Di’Githyen of past aquaitence of both Conclave of Wyrm and The House of Winterborne do henceforth renounce any and formal ties to The House of Winterborne. I will no longer wear this badge of honor for I can no longer wear its burden. From the start of the Winterborne I have shown my support and aided when was possible for me to do so. Most of you have seen me through some troubling times as of late; the reappearance of my daughter ((OOC: she never met any of you but was part of the ongoing story with Rack)), my dip into the madness which was pure hatred of all things alive, and even the loss of all memories and the recovery of what I believe to be my humanity. I have often been in the dark corners in far of lands where it is hard to reach me or for me to aid you in your journeys. Because of my lack of participation I renounce the name Winterborne. Because of my lack attempts to get to know you I renounce the name Winterborne. I do regret my lack of participation but I know that I will not regret my dispersal from this group.

To Peony Furfoot and her ever faithful husband Dolgo, I pray our paths will cross again and that you will not look “down” on me for my decision. To Trespare Winterclaw, also known as Rianna the Queen of the House. Thank you for leading such a fine guild and for showing strength when times where ill fated. To Erondis, brother, friend, the person I never quite figured out who or what he truly was. I thank you for teaching me the tongue of the Dragon and for seeing so many days go by in the dessert of Southern Ro with Peony and I. To Dirge my your studies take you places and my your road be easier then my own. Balkin warrior in arms, Train well your weaponry and see that you fight each battle with a personal code. If no code exists then what are you truly fighting for. Zsalshun D’Vox, you powers and understanding of the Tier’Dal are much greater then you understand. You have seen and experienced what most have died trying or even thinking of, may you always stay one step ahead of the “game”. To all others who I fail to call by name it is not because I do not deem you worthy but it is simply that my actions with you have either been limited or not all.

I have never been very well at much except fighting, but through my time here I have learned what it is to be a friend, I thank you for that experience. For it was somewhat “left out” of my training in my home city of Hate.

Rackin Di’Githyen
Warrior of the Tier’Dal
Friend of the Winterborne
