The Beginning Of Revelations

Posted by Olam Wyrmsoul on December 6, 1999:

Things had gone quite well for the ranger of Norrath. The start, albeit rocky, was no unlike the start others had made when they were placed upon this world. Why curse himself for stupid mistakes that no one told him not to make? He would always have regrets . . . let it go.

So he had, for some time. Those of Norrath he had fought with counted him a brave and noble friend. He'd already made numerous friends, though none but a few close. No matter. He wasn't here to win popularity, only increase his knowledge and power. Tunare had guided him, albeit poorly, but he was sure she was with him. She'd led him down painful roads, but he was sure she was there . . . she had to be.

So it had come, Olam had reached the 24th nock of Rangerhood. He'd felt alive, powerful. The time in the oceans had taught him some things. He'd kept a watchful eye upon the druid Kimber. Her powerful teleportation magiks and feral magic had done wonders to keep him alive . . . surely a gift from Tunare! His friend, Saidaar. Finally settling on the magics of summoning, stood next to him. All was well. The goblins bled, he bled, they bled. No matter, it was Tunare's will. So it was when they decided to head for the haunt of Unrest.

Saidaar, Kimber, and Olam boarded the boat. They chided Kimber for taking the boat with them. Much to their enjoyment, she continually asked, "Are we there yet?" The might druid of teleportation, taking a boat trip just to ease their trip, how amusing, how kind. The oceans were kind, and no storms washed them from deck. It was fortunate. Olam was about to crack from too much time in the oceans. Already Saidaar had showed signs of insanity. Without his stability, it wouldn't be long until Olam succumbed as well. Thus they'd voted to leave.

The trio entered Unrest quickly, unprepared for the trials that faced them. Why not? Tunare had protected them before, right? Thus, it was, within five minutes of entering the keep, that two of the members were cut down. Olam fought until the sound of Kimber's death cry cut through him. Saidaar and he sounded the retreat and began the run. Saidaar fell shortly thereafter, not even making the stairs. The ghouls had rooted him where he stood and Olam was no match for the mob. Olam was rooted next. It was a miracle then, that he survived. Oh yes, he survived! He ran with all his might. The root had removed the feral spirit from him, and he found he was no longer able to move with the quickness he once had. But Tunare was with him, right?

It was then, while resting that the voice entered his head. "I have saved you once more, Olam, ranger of Norrath." He replied, "Thank you, my goddess, Tunare." The chuckle was crisp and all too clear, "Ah, you have much to learn, Olam . . . much to learn. Tunare is no closer to you than you are to the gods." Olam was startled. He quickly stood up and looked around . . . nothing. No one was near, save a group of half dead warriors and their healers, making the cavern look like a triage unit. He thought about asking them "did you hear that?" but knew better. They were moaning and did not look like they were in the mood for his ravings. The voice came again, "Vox has saved you Olam, I know your wyrmsoul is true, do not ignore your true calling." Olam laughed aloud, breaking the silence of the cavern. His laughter was more manical then he intended and several looked at him out of the corner of their eyes. He talked within his mind, careful of any words whispered aloud. **"Vox, the lady of Winter? Please, Tunare would nae let you out of your cave, yet alone take credit for my safety, begone!**

Olam rushed out of the cavern and back to the haunt. He nae needed the help of a half-forgotton dragon of the North! Please!
