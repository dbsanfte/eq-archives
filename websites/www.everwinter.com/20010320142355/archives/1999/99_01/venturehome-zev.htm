The Venture Home

Posted by Zevinox on January 20, 1999:

Javhyri leapt from the window and landed into the trees down below. He swiftly climbed down to the ground and looked up into the window one last time to bid his companion a last farewell. He knew the young monk would triumph in his quest and would once again restore the pride and honor of this temple. He turned his back and once again began his journey.

He was about half a day from the temple before his thoughts focused elsewhere...he had trouble shaking the young monk out of his head. It wasnt common for one as Javhyri to get hung up on one thought, specially one as these...and as much as he would hate to admit, he missed the words spoken to him as they traveled down the paths, even though Javhyri never answered him, Jun always had something to say about something. Perhaps the noise poluted his mind...or perhaps it was something else alltogether, nevertheless he had a long journey ahead of him and these thoughts would only get in the way of his path.

He made a fist and turn his hand over and back the other way examining his new seeming. The young half-elven form was quick, cunning, and strong, it was perfect for him. The way the battle had gone was very much to Javhyri's liking, this surfaced a grin to his face. The skin that was tan had now became a dark greyish color looking more elven then human...in fact his entire body was elven, if it hadnt been for the shape of his eyes and the texture of his medium grey hair. He didnt look drow but was definitely a look of a dark elf. Reaching into his bag he pulled out a book covered with ancient runes, same as his swords and bow, the waved his hand over the top of the book and enchanted a few words of magic, he then opened the book and began to flip through the pages. Often when he traveled alone he would dive into his spellbook and study his spells as he walked. It killed time and allowed him to refresh his mind with the magical words.

He traveled three nights and three days before stopping for rest, many times he shifted directions getting a sense of pull from whatever direction he turned to. He slept a little then continued on...four days had past when he finally arrived at a small city maybe half a days travel from a mountain range. He decide it would be best to get a good sleep at an inn and perhaps a meal and some drink...it also wouldnt be a bad idea for him to get some more supplies for the road either...maybe see if some work existed in this city. Satisfied with his decision he traveled off into the city seeking shelter for the night.

Posted by Zevinox on February 9, 1999:

Two weeks the dark stranger had travelled two weeks till he had found the town his instinct led him to. It was a small city rather, and not too keen on criminal types. The town made him wince, if he wasnt on a much more indepth venture at the moment he would have shown the town perhaps a thing or two about real darkness. It wasnt long before word spread back to him that the Temple of IO to the north was under new rule and that humanoids were now able to join the Brotherhood in it's teachings. This brought a grin to the Elf's lips, he knew the young monk would grab hold of destiny and make use of his beliefs, though the thought of having such a care for any one being almost made him choke on his grin. It was a side of him that he would have to deal with..a flaw he'd say. Thinking back to the monk led him to the name given to him...many days of travel gave him time to think...oh no he wouldnt discard the name which was rightful given to him by his comrade..but more just build on it. In fact his name had already come to him in his new birth he just saw no need to use it while uncomplete as it was. It bothered him not...he could tell he was but a few steps away from his home and nothing would dare bother his gateway there.

He ducked into an alley way and headed to the back his body concealed in charcole black robe he picked up from a few scavengers along the way. At the end of the alley was a brick wall standing even with the tavern and weapon shop of it stood between...he paced his steps in a magical song he went over in his head, and just before running head first into the wall he reached forth his arms palms together and spread them open as if walking through a curtain opening, the wall split and a portal oval in shape and green in color opened up as he passed through, once through the portal closed again and disappeared as it was never there. He knew how it was there, why it was there, and how to open it, but in a way he didnt know, it was no matter his home was just ahead.

The scene was more then he ever remembered, beautiful and glorious, but something didnt feel quite right...something was wrong, something felt empty. He had only a short distance to travel before reaching the primeter of the Aerie and of course he would be kindly greeted by the local guards, he laughed, yes kindly greeted that's a way of putting it. No matter he was home those situations would have to be dealt with as they came.

As suspected at the entrance to the grand hall stood a few guards...usually, or he would assume, there would be more but something was amiss from all this something he couldnt place at the time....

The guards looked at the stranger then looked again before persuing..."Who wishes to enter the aerie grandhall, identify yourself for passage beyond these doors."

Zevinox grinned a half cocked grin as his answer passed through his lips like his breath..."I am Bistehln Javhyri de' Zevinox, decendant of the Zevinox. I am home send a message to Shivrehel Despana that I have arrived, he will sort this matter. In the meantime I shall have a look around unless you think one man is capable of bringing down the entire aerie by himself." he laughed...then sighed a moment.

The gaurds looked at one another in confusion thinking this man to be rather cocky...."We will send word to the lord, you may pass as a guest of the aerie."
