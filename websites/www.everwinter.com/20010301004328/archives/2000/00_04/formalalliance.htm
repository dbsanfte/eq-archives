A Formal Alliance

Posted by Trespare Winterclaw on April 29, 2000:

The runner knocked softly upon Rianna's door. He wasn't too sure of just what had occured, but this was his second trip from the Winterborne House to the Crystalline Dragon's caves. The creatures frightened him in thier seemings, but he dealt with it for the shiny platinum that seemed to pour freely from the new Queen's hands. Or was it the old Queen? He shook his head. Who knew?The strange and new voice came once to him through the door.  "Enter dear boy."

How did she do that? He knew she couldn't see through the door. Or could she? Sighing with dismay and a slight tinge of wonder, the young human opened the door slowly and entered the rooms that now belonged to the Queen. They had remained empty since Dhirge had built the House. Had he known she was returning? He thought so, since the rooms seemed to be built around this new presence, as if she belonged there. Fine silver and iron mirrors graced the room, reflecting the light from several wax candles set in sconces on the wall. Just opposite the door on , about 20 feet away sat a burnished oakwood desk, covered with etchings of leaves and forest creatures. He could almost swear he felt the life of the wood warm his blood as he drew closer. The leaves were so detailed and delicate, that he just knew some magic had plucked them off of a tree and then transformed them to wood. No human hand could have carved such a thing. A padded chair sat behind it, empty for now, but often holding the new Queen's wood elven form. Off to the right, a large arched opening led the way into a sitting area for guests. Scattered about in small groupings sat several cut stone chairs from Kaladim, burnished wood stools from Kelethin and Felwithe, and an assortment of the small 'couches' the humans preferred. The ones directly from Qeynos were done in soft greens and browns, reflecting the area's historical closeness to the druid and rangers that frequented the area. The others, shipped in from Freeport, were done in an almost garish assembly of red and yellow. There was even two extra large chairs suitable for a troll or ogre. Shaped like a Fungus creature, with the arms of it's body outstretched to serve as armrests, it appeared almost lifelike.

"Come small one, stop your looking. I have work to do." The sharp tone in her voice echoed off to his left from the arch that led into her bedchambers. A shimmery magic screen kept him from seeing what was inside, but he noti ced only the small slender form standing just in front. Her etched green tunic gleamed with small jewels set in strategic locations to hold the magic it needed to protect it's wearer from harm. The banded silver leggings of her armor molded itself to her shapely legs, and overlapped her soft leather boots. Her flowing auburn hair seemed to get more reddish by the day, but he felt sure it was just a trick of the light. Her almond shaped green eyes were narrowed in amusement. He handed her the scroll case, bowing repeatedly, and stammered an apology. REaching into the small belt pouch on her slim waist, she removed 5 platinum coins and pressed them into his palm. "Thank you boy, you have done well. Go to the kitchens and get yerself some bread and cheese from the cook. Tell her I sent ye. You can then await my summons in the messenger hall." She smiled slighty as she said this and the boy quickly bowed once more and hurridly left. Walking to her desk, Rianna sat lightly upon the padded chir and removed the parchment from the bone scroll case. It's plain case hid the importance of the documents within. Placing two small stone scroll holders at the top of the scroll, she unrolled the white parchment to read the contents.

Alliance (Full)

Purpose:

I - To bind House Winterborne and Crystalline Dragons in a bond of friendship, honor, trust and protection.

II - To declare that House Winterborne and Crystalline Dragons will defend one another the best of their
abilities.

Tenets:

I - House Winterborne and Crystalline Dragons are prepared to join forces should either come under
attacks of aggression from opposed guilds, clans, houses, or individuals in the lands of Norrath and
will defend one another to the best of their abilities.

II - House Winterborne and Crystalline Dragons pledge their members will come to the aid of the other, not
only as warriors, but as scouts and healers for as long as needed.

Terms and Conditions:

I - This alliance may be terminated at any time by either House Winterborne or Crystalline Dragons
when it is deemed that irreconcilable differences have arisen between them after the leaders of the two
guilds have made every effort possible to resolve those differences.

II - This alliance will remain intact as long as both House Winterborne and Crystalline Dragons are
active guilds in the land.

III - This alliance shall be re-negotiated in the event that any two of the signatories of either guild
are no longer the active representatives of their respective guilds.

OOC Notes:

EverQuest’s PvP toggle prevents many acts of aggression; this document is intended to promote
role-playing between House Winterborne and Crystalline Dragons through an in-character alliance
and spirit of cooperation. In day-to-day occurrences, this equates to offers of inter-guild grouping,
invitations to inter-guild events, and assured inter-guild trade, all with a sense of fairness and honesty.


Nodding her head with satisfaction, she placed her own emerald green seal on the bottom next to Lord Dracone's, sealing the agreement. Leaning back in her chair, she took a moment to rest before leaning over to summon another House page. This alliance had the potential to bring 2 powerfull houses together in common cause. Dhirge had spent much time paving the way for such an event, but had done little to formalize it. Now that it was complete it was time to announce it to the House, and send the document to Dhirge for his records and keeping. Blwing an errant strand of hair from her eyes, Rianna leaned forward once again and pushed away the dizziness that assaulted her. It was becoming a common occurence in these last few days, and she hoped that the trip to Unrest would not prove a mistake. She would hate to let down her guard there. Ringing the small bell on her des, Rianna handed the scroll and case to the young human girl that appeared at her door.

"Take it to Dhirge's office Leal, he will be expecting it I'm sure." Rianna then turned and began to pack up her weapons and supplies for the long trip ahead.
