A stern-looking silver of mature age instructed his sons and daughters:  "We of the Silver Celare are charged with safeguarding the lesser races from those who would seek their destruction, enslavement, or degradation.  Through Claw and Wing, Sword and Staff, Magic and Charms, we fight against those who would destroy that which Io laid down to us."  The young dragons, one little more than a hatchling, listened with rapt attention.

Then the end came. In a place where dragon's dwelt in relative peace, the magnificent creatures grew idle. Few things managed to hold their attentions long. A leader among those dragon's saw doom fast approaching.  Taking a riven guise, he went among the non-dragons and planted seeds of conflict. The first Dragon-Slayers began to emerge.  

A collective of these creatures, composed of humans and dwarves, assaulted the peak in the Treebear Mountains near the center of RhyDin's most-heavily populated continent. They wielded magical weapons, specifically  designed to slay Io's brood. They had no qualms about killing the metallic dragons. The service to the people that the silver's had performed was long forgotten.

The three eldest children of ag'Dangeant, ag'Melir, ag'Merjus, and ag'Kinir, had one idea in their minds, to ensure the safety of the youngest among them, myself, ag'Keldore. Melir, with her powerful evocations, hurled ice and lightning towards the attackers. Merjus, brandishing her claws, impaled many of the beings to the rocky ground. Kinir, taking an Elven seeming, and wielding an ancient holy sword, shielded his siblings from the magical onslaughts and cut down many foes. I, young but all ready adept in magic, did what I could to slow the slayers. We only delayed the inevitable.

House Silverwand was outnumbered seven to one. The slayers overwhelmed  our defenses. A hint of red scale in the sky alerted us to the presence of another enemy. The retreat and race for survival was on. Exhausted in our magic, we silver dragons took to the sky. Magical arrows were unleashed to the clouds, striking down a large number of us. Phos, the red who took an increasing interest in the battle, slew more than a few fleeing silvers himself. It was not long, however, before a well-aimed lance knocked him out of the sky as well.

There was little hope for any of House Silverwand. Dargeant cursed the human and dwarven races, until a black iron battle axe severed his larynx.  The rest of the battle was a blur to me. My sisters used their limited spells, waning reserves, and physical strengths to literally throw my brother and I away from the mountain. We awakened to find ourselves in a strange place, alone.

Our adventures were many, my brother's and mine. We traveled the width  and breadth of the continent, taking different seemings--never human or dwarf--and learning about the people. We learned as much as we could from a place, then moved on. I grew powerful in the magic of that world, and Kinir became a master swordsman. We were happy.

Suffice it to say, I was very close to my brother. I cannot relate the tale of his demise, for it would bring back a depression that I fought long and hard against. However, I will tell you, that like a true paladin, he died in honor.

At the age of four hundred eighty-seven, I recovered from my brother's death, I decided to settle in a place for a while. I happened upon a small kingdom called Cavalyn. The queen, Vitesse, and I grew to be friends, and I was appointed to construct a tower of magical learning and research. Mages came from all over the continent to study and leave tokens of alliance or ill-will to be given to the queen. I joined the Order of the Ebon Hand's House of Lore, and renewed my studies. Once again, I was happy.

It was during my time in Cavalyn that I met a human woman who captured my heart as nothing else had. I found it strange that I should be so enraptured by a non-dragon, let alone a human. I did not resist her charm, though, and after a brief courting period, I was mated to my sweet young Vahnna. We have two brilliant and strong children, Kirsha and Jaidon. You might see the latter adventuring here with me in Norrath.

It was also during my time in that peaceful land that I happened to meet a very spirited individual, an electrum dragon by the name of Starallon. It was he who renewed my interest in other worlds that held so many undiscovered wonders. I followed him to Elanthia, but after attempting to utilize that world's magic, and falling short, left. I then struck out to Krynn, where I found little more success in that war-torn land, and returned to RhyDin.  

Starallon then introduced me to the Conclave of the Wyrm on Io's Blood Isles. I had heard of this place before, but consigned it to myth. I never imagined that I would ever see it, let alone live there. I was disillusioned, however. The Conclave was in a state of turmoil, and I left.

A few years later, I returned, more mature and more advanced in magic, and opened myself to the Conclave more fully. I entered the silver city of Pinnacle and served under ag'Talisman. I fought in four major conflicts: the battle for Starlight Citadel, the Challenge of Claw and Wing, a sea battle against a pair of dragon slayers and their fire giant allies, and the Orb Winter. I was successful in the first three, but the last was disastrous.

The Orb Winter followed on the Challenge's heels. The circumstances and results of that battle can be read up on in our library, and I will not retell that story here today.

My stay on the continent of Solon was brief. Following the steps of other Conclave refugees, I came to the world of Norrath. It seemed an exciting new place, and I was attracted by its magic. I was disappointed when I was forced into a particular school, and limited in spell choice, but I am making due.

Dirge then formed the House Winterborne to work with Lady Vox to solve the problems afflicting Winter's creations. Soon after, I was summoned...

The elf on the docks of Qeynos groaned and stretched after rising. Blinking and licking his salted lips, Keldore vaguely remebered how he got here. It had something to do with the fishing pole lying on the ground beside him, and the empty mead bottles next to it. No use dwelling on the past, he decided. Gathering his things, headed for the bank. He didn't like carrying this pole around with him.

Depositing his things, Keldore began planning his day. While scarfing down a few nutritious but un-delicious rations, and gulping down an entire flask of water, he decided to prey on the gnolls of Blackburrow. They seemed to be launching many more frequent and powerful attacks as of late. It was probably due to all the so-called adventurers slaughtering them. They deserved it though.

One, two, three, four corpses lie at the entrance to the tunnel joining Blackburrow to the Qeynos Hills. A strange site it used to be, but becoming all too usual. One, two, three, four corpses pilled up around the wizard, felled by his moderately powerful spells and inadequate combat abilities. It was a bit exhausting, and Keldore decided to take a break and regather his energies, both physical and magical.

He found a nice corner in the gnoll home, and sat down. His inviting spellbook was opened in his lap and he began studying once again. The pages began to swirl, but he dismissed it as the lingering effects of last night's party. They began to flip wildly, before coming to rest upon those dealing with cold and ice. These rose from the pages, nipping at him with their chill. While this weak body felt pain, the silver dragon soul within reveled. The mind wondered just what was happening.

The answer came in a voice that was inviting, yet domineering. The rocky scape around him faded to a cavern of snow and ice. His feet danced to the voice, bringing him to his feet. "Welcome to my home."

Keldore found himself staring up at one of the most beautiful sites he had yet to see on Norrath. It nearly shattered his heart to see the great dragon that loomed over him, the pain of losing his draconic abilities and shape still fresh. At once he knew her name, from rumors across the land, from annals in the library of Erudin, and from the presence he had felt since coming to this world. "Thank you, My Lady Vox."

Studying ones spells requires the opening of the mind. Just about anyone with the inclination and power to do so could follow the streaming magical words, gestures, and patterns into Keldore's brain. He had already been studied and accepted, but like the others she had already contacted, he needed to be shown the vision and be Winterborne.

The carnage soon to be wrought in the world was like a hammer blow to the wizard, who thought the world was streaming along nicely. The vision was not doubted, however. Once the signs were pointed out for him, he could see the picture. He accepted the position offered to him by the Lady, happily once he learned that some of his friends had done so already.

Snapping shut the spellbook and rising, he once again began battling the gnolls, this time with a different purpose.

I continue to gain power in Norrath with the others who have joined the House. We are making thorough searches of all locales in the world for any information that could lead us to cure the malfunctions in Winter's creations. Aside from the problems we face, I am happy.
