Marton Sayor’s Letter to Winterborne
(Written Twenty and Two Years before the Orb Winter)

I wanted to write this, keep things straight in my mind. You deserve what little I know of your past and here it is.

I was out wandering, looking for gnolls that wandered a bit too far from their fellows. It was midwinter’s eve, and I had a hard time resting with the threats Lord Elgnub had made. That’s when I found my charge swaddled in the rich white furs of northern bears, nestled between the snow-covered hills north of Qeynos near the caverns that lead to the Black Burrow. The gnolls were watchful, but Tunare protected me and I eluded their malicious intent. The Mother knows that the Darkpaw Clan has threatened my family before. Suffice it to say, I’m no friend of the gnolls and wresting this bundle from their grasp was a small victory for me.

I knew I could not properly care for this parcel, and headed to the Jagged Pine Folks. Though we don’t always see eye to eye, they are a good people, kind and practical. Corum Finisc, one of their leaders, met me and inspected my find. He was delighted and named you—my find—Winterborne. It’s a fittin’ name, and you seemed down right comfortable, just sitting in the snow like that.

I don’t know how you got there, or what the future holds for you, but Corum said he’d train you in the ways of his people. No doubt I’ll see you more, as you grow and master Tunare’s magic. Maybe, when your power has come of age, you can help me seek justice against the gnolls and find out about your past.

Best wishes… Marton Sayor

Shivrehel Despana’s Review of House Membership:

It was odd to be approached by the young servant of Tunare. He said he had a dream of Vox, and was her child, though he was trained in the ways of a druid—much the same as this seeming of mine. He called himself Winterborne and was not referring to our house, but rather his given name. The coincidence was too much, so I called upon Aulura, whose service has been bequeathed to me from the Lady.

Aulura brought confirmation today. Vox’s agents had been researching a lost animation that once was entrusted to her Empire. The child had been lost during a skirmish between ice goblin representatives of the dragoness and the gnolls of the Black Burrow. Twenty two years ago, a merchant of the area—Marton Sayor—found a young boy outside the burrow, wrapped in furs. He was taken to Surefall, and had been raised as a druid.

Since the night Vox conversed with me in the dream-vision, he had been receiving lucid revelations of his past, his future. I can not say for certain what she intends for the young man, but he holds much promise and can only assist us in our mission. I have inducted him into the House and look forward to divining more of his origin and the fate that surrounds him.
