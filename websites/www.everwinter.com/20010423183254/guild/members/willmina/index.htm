With gentle urgings of Dirge, I've taken time from adventure to put down my beginnings on parchment for the guild's archive. Why he'd want to hear about my meager start still baffles me, but committed I am, so read on, dear reader, and try not to drool on the pages as ye nap, it will smear the ink!

My beginnings are unknown to me. For, you see, I was raised as an orphan in the City of Qeynos, daughter of none. Those kind folk who ran the place knew nothing of my past, except that I was dropped of in a basket of fine woven rush, swaddled in the skin of a bear cub. Some of my elders speculated that it was made in the northern city of Halas, but others think it came from the eastern forests of Felwithe. Back then in my early days I would spend many hours wondering of what my parents were like, and who they were, hoping they'd come back for me someday. Alas as the years ticked by my mind was distracted by other matters, hope turned to despair, despair to grief. I do not spend idle time thinking of such things any longer.

Early life for an orphan was hard, but it gave me insight into the ways of the world. It was a cruel place, and one rarely came across a kind soul in the bustling city. It was so full of corruption and deceit!  

Even now it remains on the verge of chaos. With hair of flame, and attitude to match, the elders decided upon a name to fit my nature, Willmina. "Will" for willfull of spirit, and "mina" meaning everyone (from some ancient tounge it still know not it name), since I was always trying to get those around me to listen to my dreams and desires!  During these early days, my propensity for controlling magic became quite apparent! Once while trying to explain my ideas to an elder, I grew angry and had slammed my fist upon his desk in spite. The wild magic and anger combined into a ball of fire that promptly made a pile of ashes of his poor desk! My belly went empty that night for punishment, not to mention sitting was quite painful as well. The very next day I found myself being dragged kicking and screaming all the way to the guild of mages and wizzards on the south side of town. I had been by there before, whilst running errands and had seen them casting their spells, and reading old musty books, but paid it no mind at the time.

The elders sat me in a room alone, and spoke with the casters quitely in the room next door. After a litlle while, one the most lovely ladies I've ever met came and spoke with me. Yestura was her name, and she asked if I'd like to stay with them a while. I was shy at first, leery of the new surroundings, but an insatiable curiosity of the magical power they wielded made me speak and give my affirmation. The thought of going back to the orphanage was unpleasant at best. Besides, I had shown some of this power myself, and I began to hope that I too could be like the Lady Yestura someday, and be a master of magic!

Thus began my days as a junior apprentice for the magician's guild.  Long days of memorizing spells, learning to channel my magic and begin to harness its power. I also took up cooking the daily meals for the mages, an a part of me today still enjoys the occasional bout of culinary dabbling. While still an apprentice, I was out hunting some rats outside of town for their whiskers for Lady Yestura, I happened across a wily monk fellow name Mott. He was jovial and kind, but fierce when the Vile Darkpaws would attack. Never before had I seen such punishment given without blade nor spell! I took a liking to him, and him to me, and we made fast friends. As our skills increased we began to hunt the hills of Qeynos, coming ever closer to the dreaded Black Burrow. He was part of an adventurous guild call the "Conclave of the Wyrm". He introduced me to several junior officers, and I was invited to join as well. Since Mott seemed happy with them, I decided to follow along and adventure with them as well.

My masters at the magician's guild were pleased with my progress, and were happy that I was doing well. By my 10th circle, I had begun to notice that Mott was becoming displeased more often for some reason.  When pressed for further details, he had finally given me the news of the leaders of guild we were in was having a separation of ideals, and that a change would be coming soon. I had noticed that the upper ranks of the guild were unavailable most of the time, and I had little interaction with them up till that point. When the news of the splinter faction reached me, I knew that I too would follow the new guild. How ironic that I would be travelling through Black Burrow to the icy halls of Everfrost, home of our new guild Winterborne, at the time of the split to see my good friend Talora. As I sat on an icy rock meditating, the snowy white landscape made me feel at peace. Our new group was alive, and full of energy. The words of our leader Dirge rang loud to my ears as they were the whispers of the Lady Vox herself.

Let me tell you kind reader, that finding out that one is a creation can be quite unnerving, especially one that is so easily detected by the device in Flutroses' care. Once I had come to peace with the idea, my whole existence had suddenly become clear. The orphaning, and the basket from Halas-- all pointed to the very fact that I was indeed destined to be part of Winterborne. The breath of the dragons live on in my hands of flame.

Now I enter into my 16th circle, and I have mastered yet another level of magic. I aspire to be like my friend and mentor Flutrose, a mage of extraordinary caliber that ranks high within our guild. She aides me when I have questions, and comforts me when I'm down. I look forward to her future teachings. Back home in Qeynos, the mages who raised me from a girl, are now less eager to sell me their scrolls, since they are busy with other young mages. So I must seek out and learn the source of their power on my own. From here on I travel across the land, researching magic from all over the world. I have traveled the width of Antonica. I have been to the Deserts of Ro, sailed to the Ocean of Tears, and tramped through the treaturous Kithicore Forrest. Even though it holds many dangers, I am eager to see the rest of Norrath, and continue my persuit to become a master magician!
