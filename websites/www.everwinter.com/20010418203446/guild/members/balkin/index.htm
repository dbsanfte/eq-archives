Balkin watched as the old sage tottered into the inn. Watched as the old man peered around the common room, and settled his gaze on the scared Dwarven warrior. Continued to watch as the man made his way to Balkin's table.

May ye sit? Aye, old one, sit down before ye fall down. That's quite a sheaf o' paper ye have there, what is it yer after? What's that ye say? Me story? What? Does not the map of scars all over me tell ye me story? I am a warrior! That is my story!

Research ye say? Animation? What be that? Me? Bah, what need I of research, I need a sharp blade, and some good armor, that's what I need.  Alright! Alright! Buy me an ale and be quiet. I'll tell the tale if ye'll leave me be afterward! I hope your old hands are firm enough to be writin' quick! I've nay much time before I must go and my armor still needs tending to! From the beginning? Very well, as I said, I've nay much time but here it is.

Me father says he had no idea where I came from. He was workin the forge, deep inside Kaladim, and suddenly a bitter cold wind blew through the smithy, extinguishing the forge! Can ye believe it!?! Hundreds of feet underground! In the aftermath, he says he found me on the floor by the anvil. Quiet I was, but not for long. Me mother says she's never seen a Dwarf ask so many questions. From the moment I could talk, she says she had nary a moments rest or quiet! He he he. The more things change the more things stay the same! I still talk to much, which is why I'm talkin ta you instead o' cleanin me armor. My parents? Nay, they are no my true parents, though I honor them as such.

What's this about a blade? Ye know the way's of the Dwarves I see. Yes I was given a weapon before I could walk. Me father said it were like this. He handed me a dagger ta see how I might handle it. Firm was my grip, says he. But the fun part o' this is that he says it took him ten minutes to get the blade away from me! Says I gave him several nasty cuts in the process! Is that normal? Nay, now that you mention it, it is not. Most will hold the blade, nay many will take a swipe with it. T'was then he says, that he knew I would fight. Then it was, that he knew I would become a warrior.

Did I excel at my training? Aye, the best of the lot I was. There's nothing like the whistle of a blade through the air, nor the feel of steel crunching into the flesh of your enemies. Are you all right old one? Barkeep! Water for the sage! He seems a might distraught! My circle? Aye, you do indeed know much old one. I have reached my 20th circle. An achievement you say? Maybe, but to me it is another step in a long journey, one that only true death can complete.

Now where was I. Ah, indeed, me father said I was not for the guards, I was for the world. Me first job was assigned by the Warriors guild master himself, The mountains surrounding Kaladim are infested with foul goblins, bats, and magic'ed skeletons. Twas my duty to clean them away from the main gate of Kaladim so that travelers might be safe. Did it go well? Aye, well indeed. I'd show ye the scars from those first days, but I can no find them amongst the others Ha ha ha. Ye want to see the whole map? Ha ha. Nay? Right, probably not a good idea. Barkeep! More water for the old one! Yes, I'll buy it!

From there I traveled the roads to the Elven lands, escorting travelers and helping all as I may. From the Elven lands, I found my way to Crushbone. Aey, ye've heard of the place I see. A haven of foul, evil Orcs it be. I met many worthy spell casters and warriors there, and together we did our best to kill them all! Alas, Orcs breed faster than rabbits it seems, they are unending! However, I earned my reputation there. The Orcs of Crushbone have declared me a Villain! Har! I can think o' no better compliment! Ha ha!

Then onward I traveled to the Steamfont Mountains. A journey that be, yes. The gnome have many strange and wonderous devices. I was mightily impressed, though they are a bit strange at times. Some o' those clockworks now.

How did I get here from there? By me feet and the boat! How else!? Freeport be the largest city known to all. Where else does a warrior go for work, besides to a city? I've traveled most of the known world. I've been both a mercinary, and a personal guard. What? Why do I not stay in any one place? Nay, I'm not angry at ye, tis a good question. I can no answer it. I've a drive to go. a drive to see. I seek something, yet I know not what. I know only that I will know what I seek when I find it.

What's that? Again with the animations. Aye, ye know much old one, but ye know not all. Dhirge say ye? I should speak with him?

Balkin downed his ale, then another while listening to the old one talk. Pictures, diagrams, and text rolled forth from the stack of paperwork the old sage possessed. Most of it ancient, much of it spoke of the Winterborne, and those who lead them. Dhirge was one of these, and had to be either as old as eternity, or a decendant from the first Dark Elf of that name, mentioned long ago.

Aye old one, it seems I might be going that way soon anyway, I've a job guarding a caravan that is headed that way. Mayhap I'll seek him out. How do you know all this old man? Ah, now ye keep secrets! Ye pry my life outta me then have no answers to my questions!? Har Har Har! This is to much. get ye gone old one, I've armor to tend to and provisions to buy, tis time for my journey to continue!

Aye, Aye I'll seek out this Dhirge. If for no reason than to keep you quiet! Go now and let me rest a few moments more. Armor be heavy ye know, even for a dwarf!

A short while later, clad in his armor, a sword on each hip and a pack on his back, Balkin clomped down into the inn commons. None would meet his eyes as he glared around. There was something about an armored warrior that made people stare at their cups instead of the one in armor. That was fine.  He would return here someday, remove his armor, and sit in the commons listening to and telling stories like all the others. He had no doubt those present would remember him. Scarred faces are often remembered, he knew that.
