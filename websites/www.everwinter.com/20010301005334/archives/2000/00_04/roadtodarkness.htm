The Road to Darkness

Posted by Trespare Winterclaw on April 3, 2000:

Stumbling down the pass from High Keep, Lady Trespare wept tears of bitter shame. The coming darkness mirrored her mood. "Why? By all the Lady's Grace, Why?" her voice cracked as she quickly limped onward to retrieve her belongings. The scenes kept running through her mind, blazing themselves deeper into her soul and blighting her memories. The thought that she could hunt with Lord Keldore was a welcome change. He was not often about when she was, and she truly wished to aid him and herself in learning the ways of magic and power. But from the first, thier pairing was a disasterous one. They set off to hunt the deadly crags of the plains, only to find themselves outnumbered by several other creatures while they faced the spider. Lord Keldore was able to summon his wizardly magic and disappear in a swirling silver cloud. She had yelled at him to go, and faced the beasts alone, laying down her sword and life so that he could have time to summon his magic and retreat. Upon reawakening with her Gods blessing, and finding Lord Keldore again, they set off for another try, only to be brought low again. Another run of the foul plains beasts set upon them, and this time her sword and magic was not enough. Blue-white magic flew from her hands and trailed about him, her axe and scimitar sung in the air they moved so quickly to strike the crag down, but it was nae enough. Shivering from poison and weakened by the effort, he gave a brief cry and fell into his God's arms. Pouring all her rage into her movements, she soon slew the spider that had felled him and dropped next to his body with tears running from her eyes." I am sorry Lord, so sorry! My honor is broken, my shame complete. May Tunare take me in payment for thy life!" In the midst of her weeping, a dark stalker, hearing prey near, set upon her. Holding her gaping wounds, she could only sigh in sorrow as the last life bled from her and she was whisked once again to the nether realms.

"May ye forgive me my Lord..."
