Posted by Shar Hel Despana on July 11, 2000:

“Note to self: Do not aggravate centaur chargers.” Fallon read the scrawled handwriting that was on a scrap piece of parchment. “What’s this? Ye were wrestling with the centaurs again Shar?”

“Aye, but do nae ruffle thy feathers. I was accompanied by Balkin, Zsalshun, Morphite, and even Daddy made an appearance.” Shar answered through mouthfuls of warm bread dipped in gravy. “We had a great night. Well, once I learned how to behave. I’m afraid I’m still not used to working with people.”

“Oh? Tell me what happened.” Fallon wiped her hands on her apron and took a seat across from the pale lady.

Shar took a long draw from a goblet and began, “If ye’ve ever been to the southern reaches of the plains, ye know there’s a centaur village there with a handful of stables. Well, we decided we’d claim one of the stables for our own, which meant keeping out all the centaurs. Aye, I know, twas a pretty arrogant notion, but we were intent on proving ourselves.

“Our first bout with the centaurs was disastrous. No sooner did we slay a few of them, when Daddy Charger came home. Unprepared to take the beast, we were forced to flee. However, we came right back. Gluttons we all are, ye see?” The pale lady laughed and wiped her mouth. “The rest of the eve went fairly well...once I slowed down and paid attention to Morphite’s directives.” The pale lady stopped then and looked Fallon over. “Ye should reconsider thy homely ways and strike out with us some time. Morphite opened my eyes yester eve to the potential an enchanter has in combat.

“Ah, but back to my note. I quickly grew impatient. With Zsalshun there, I was hard put to find my niche. The night before, I used my shamanic powers to keep Varlik alive while we fought the aviaks in their village. I knew my place, so to speak. But what happened there is another story. My point is, my sole purpose in tree town was to keep Varlik and Talamara healed and prepared for battle. In the stables, Zsalshun was more than able to keep everyone healed, at least I believe she was. Once everyone was prepped with my spells, I didn’t have much else to do. It took quite a few poundings by some grand daddy chargers to convince me that poisoning them was not the way to contribute. But by the end of the night, I began to relax and even enjoy our time bashing those hooved brutes!” Shar paused and took another swig of the Saerloonian Topaz.

Fallon forgot herself in Shar’s story. The anxious look on her face made Shar laugh. “Do tell, what did ye finally do then, if not poison them? And what of the rest of the party? How did they fare?” She didn’t say what Shar knew she was thinking. “What of Morphite. How did the enchanter make out?”

Shar let out a throaty chuckle and continued. “What did I do? Well, once I finally figured out that poisoning the larger centaurs aggravated them into a rage, I decided to stop that and join Balkin and Vaber in the fight.” The pale lady flexed her arms. “I am shaman, I am versatile!” Shar roared with laughter.

“I wonder, who is this Vaber fellow? Ye’ve nae mentioned him until now.”

“Ah, Shiver’s bag of bones! Vaber was the servant he summoned that eve.” Shar’s face became grave then and she leaned closer, whispering, “Ye know, he grows more powerful by the day. I don’t doubt he’ll be wrangling with the gods of Norrath soon.” The lanky barbarian leaned back in her stool then, stretching and yawning. “Well, m’dear, there’s more to this story, but I reckon I can’t stay awake a minute more. Perhaps one of the others will be along and they will share their version of our horse hunt with ye.”

Fallon rose, smiling. “Aye, ye go right on ahead and gets some rest, lass. Io knows ye could use it.” Fallon patted Shar on the back.

“Good night Fallon,” mumbled Shar as she headed to her quarters.

Nae lass, a bit o' whatever that is that smells so good, and I'll give ye what I saw."

Posted by Morphite on July 13, 2000:

The journey was long and hard, but the enchanter had finally acheived the experience he needed, to convince the guild leaders he was more than committed to the ways of alteration, and could, indeed, handle the responsibility that gold enchantment carrier. The rain was pouring down over the plains and the quicker he could return to Qeynos, the faster he'd be able to get to work practicing his new skills. Trading partial clarity of mind for spirit of wolf, he sped across the plains, chuckling about his new found skeleton illusion.

Arriving in Qeynos was as mundane as usual. The same guards held their post, the same drival passed this way and that. "No matter," he said half to himself, "I'm happy to have a good part of my journey behind me." He touted his horn to Balkin and Sharhel who offered contributions to speed his way on the gold craft he was about to partake. After receiving their gifts he was told by Shar to visit Fallon in the nearby inn. "The lass would like to know more of our night at the horsees!" she gaffawed, slapping the frail man on the back. The assault nearly dislodged his neck from his body as he tried to look unharmed and responded with a weak, "okay."

Quickly he ran about Qeynos trying to find the spell that would allow him to enchant gold. "Where is that blasted scroll!" The merchant laughed. 'You'll nae be finding that sort of enchantment here, Erudite. The Tier' dal have stolen all known copies of the scroll and placed it in their library. Morphite's cursing could be heard far across the plains. He'd have to work someone into the Nariak home to get that spell for him, it seemed that even Morphite's illusions and magiks could nae fool some of those guards.

He sighed heavily and entered the inn to wear the small enchanter sat cleaning away the last of the meal.

Fhallon's eyes sparked when she saw the black robed enchanter come through the door. She was aflit with all the energy her youth afforded her. "Morphite!" She squeeled, as she dropped the dishes she was holding. "Come, tell me the tales! Tell me the stories of our craft!"

A bit taken back by her zeal, it was welcomed. The erudite had become accustomed to stuffy converstations regarding tactics and life, and hadn't really thought about the abstract world of feeling liked! Softening his tone he looked about to be sure no one else heard him baby talk the young creature.

"Well, where would I begin? I'm sure you've heard tales from Shar? Of her roles and others?"

She nodded, "Yes! And you just missed Lord Balkin, of Warclaw. He was doing a fine job telling of the dreary nature of hand to hand combat, but then he started drifting away . . . you know that look people get when they start thinking of something else?" Morphite nodded. "He started tracing his scars, icky!" Morphite laughed.

"Well, then, what can I tell you more . . . let's see . . ." he scratched at an invisible beard and closed his eyes. Leaning back he recounted in his mind's eye, the events that unfolded.

"There was much to be learned that night. My life, the life you now pursue is one of great power and great peril. You can control the flow of battle with the wave of your hand, but just as quickly, can that same tide turn and engulf you. Only a bright warrior and clever cleric can save you from certain peril." The conversation was a bit too global for her tastes. He could she was looking as his garb and jewelry rather than listening to the conversation. He smiled.

"Very well child, let me sum up for you the issues you will face... but please do, pay attention for just a short time, will you?" She sheepishly smiled agreement.

"Quickly then, you have in your arsenal, or you will, the ability to bewilder monsters to the state that they will not attack and will just stand there."

"And then Lord Balkin and Lady Shar can bash them and poison em to death!" Fallon blurted out excitedly.

Morphite glared, "NO!" Fallon fell back in her chair in a chastisized slump. "THAT, my child, will get you killed!" "Don't you see? A bewildered creature, we'll call him enthralled, is not frozen or stunned. He's meerly convinced for a short time, that there are things to ponder, rather than fight. That said, ANYTHING that causes that creature pain will distract him from that thought path and thus, fighting becomes paramount once again. This includes slashes from the mighty Balkin or the sickening wrench of Shar's poison. These things are best saved until the group has picked their primary target. The others must be left alone to their thoughts, or even better, we do what we call debuff!"

Morphite had actually consumed himself in the discussion. Fallon, in the meantime hadn't wandered, but actually took out note and pen and scribed notes! He'd reached her, finally, an apt pupil! "Some ale, Fallon, I have more to speak!"

The ale flowed freely and Morphite continued.

"Now dear Fallon, I'm sure you've been told that I was a rather controlling man that night, yes?"

Fallon paused but then blurted, "Well, Shar said she was not going to poison, and said Zsalshun was bored and Balkin was .. . " Morphite hushed her with a wave of his hand.

"Please, Fallon. Slowly. Let me address that which you've noted already. Do you know what Zsalshun's main function is in a party?"

"To heal?" She half asked, half stated. Mophite nodded a smile in agreement.

"Correct. And what is Shar, the barbarian Shaman's role?"

Fallon looked stumped. "Well, she said she's a healer."

"Is that when she's with Zsalshun?" the enchanter queried.

"Well, not so much as you'd notice." Fallon giggled.

Morphite chuckled, "Aye, she's what'd they call backup healer. But that's not her main function anymore. Do you know what shaman's CAN do, Fallon?" He didn't wait for an answer, "They debuff! Do you remember that term? Shamans are trained in all sorts of ways to cause problems for their enemies. When coupled with an enchanter, they can ease us some of our burden and actually become a backup enchanter of sorts! That saves our nose from the book as often!"

Fallon continued to scribe half notes to herself. Most of it, he was sure she'd bounce off Shar for validity, which is what he wanted... intelligent conversation always befitted the family.

"You see," he continued, "her job in groups with clerics present and enchanter in full effect, should be to discover her less utilized spells that do not cause damage, but can prepare the enthralled for the beating to come. Let's take a small example to explain this to you." Morphite looked at the table and made up a mock battle scene for the excited enchanter.

"Okay, this salt shaker is you Fallon. This fork is an archer, this knife a charger, and this spoon another archer. These other condiments are your party. You survey the situation, and lucky for you, the creatures are not normally hostile." Fallon looked sad. He quickly answered, "We'll worry about why you attack them in another lecture." She nodded solomly. "So you know that your magiks are most likely to fail against the tougher beasts. Knowing this, you suggest to the group that they focus on the hardest beast WHEN You're ready. Get their agreement. Next thing you do is haste your melee members -- the more damage they can deal, the more the enemy hates them, the faster the enemies dispatchment, the less likely your enthrall spells (when cast) are to fail at an inopportune time."

He watched her scribbing subside and continued, "Next you go for the next weakest creature and enthrall him. Easy enough, right? Wrong. This is an act of agression, any monsters near that target just got very angry with you. Quickly your party should focus on the originally agreed upon primary target. As soon as that target is off you, enthrall that second target." Morphite arranged the utencils to trace to the battle. "Now there are two creatures enthralled and the last is busily fighting your meleers. Reduce the magic resistance of that creature and, now is where Shar should step in and slow him."

"But I can slow them!" Fallon said energetically. "

"But you don't have to do it all, my child," Morphite responded. "Shall I guess and say that Shar felt a bit out of place that night?" Fallon nodded. "Don't you see then? There was plenty to do and be a part of. She has an arsenal of debuffs and whatnot that do not cause damage and can considerably make enemy's lives harder. Following on the footsteps our magic debuff, you know, the one that makes them look nervous? She should have her drowsy magiks flowing!"

Fallon smiled. The light was brightening.

"Follow that with Shiver's strength drain for example, and you have a party full of debuffers, that don't have to cause damage and still leave the creatures enthralled! Now, one note, Fallon. Remember our tanks are fighting the hard creature, we've attempted slows and some debuffs on him , only to the point of too many resists. Don't waste too much time when you're resisted . . . move on. The cleric is there to watch out for him. Let me guess, Zsalshun was reportedly bored as well?" Fallon again nodded. "Don't you see? There is a world of work to be done, that when spread out, takes the load off of that one overbearing enchanter and makes the whole group integral!"

Fallon laughed. "It's a family affair!"

Morphite sat back with a puzzled look. Seeing the innocence in her eyes he merely agreed, "Sure. I suppose it is, I suppose it is!"

Morphite sighed and tipped back the last of his mug. "Well, child. I grow weary and must continue my craft. Please do watch yourself and your friends. And remember, each of us is important."

Posted by Zsalshun D'Vox on July 13, 2000:

Zsalshun frowned, trying to decide whether to go back to the Eastern plains to kill gorge hound or to lite out after the treants and see if the people there could use the skills of a healer. On one hand, the hounds were tedious with poor fur that sold for little while the Treants were much more satisfying to whittle down to size. Unfortunately, she was languishing in the midst of near uselessness. Anything she could do, a druid or a shaman could mirror and much brighter. She chafed to wake up one day with the knowledge of greater healing. Then, only then would she feel like she contributed something worth while to a party.

She sighed and stretched. She had a few things from the centaur raid to sell off and she thought, with dark ironic glee, to take them right back to the same village and sell them to the hoofed merchants there. It appealed to her dark humor. With the speed of Shar's spell she flew along the plain towards the stables.

The raid had gone well, after the initial confusion. The priestess was enjoying working with Morphite more and more and was again impressed with his battle savvy. The enchanter kept the party from being slaughtered more than once and kept her flow of mana from draining entirely. That the erudite called the shots bothered her none the least. Her job was to sit and watch for leaks as the archers, shelties and chargers sliced and ripped at the party. She was also able to add to the health and survivability of the group by blanketing them in the gifts of her god. She knew what to do and she was ever getting a better sense of knowing when and where to do it.

She frowned again as she checked her supply of agates. Shiver had given her a sizable amount which forestalled another trip back to Qeynos but several had been wasted when she wound up overwriting the same spell cast by Birik. The worshipper of light did not coordinate well and his disregard of mana efficiency constantly grated at the Tier 'Dal. His mana was his business, but foolishly jumping in front of an enraged charger and getting his limbs and organs rearranged soon became the dark priestess' problem. Even Shar did not take that long to learn what hurt and how not to make the hurt happen. Birik just kept leaping in with his glowing mace held aloft and limping out with the last shreds of his life hanging about him. Zsalshun supposed it might be the bright light given off by the weapon that addled his brain so.

Enough thought on that, he would either learn or perish. The centaur village lay quiet in the night but there were still lights on in the merchants stalls. They never asked questions about the obviously horse crafted yew and they even paid a fair price for their return. The money would help defray the cost of the spells she would need when Innoruuk decided she was ready to contribute positively to a group. The formidable healing and stronger protection magics were almost in her grasp yet so profoundly far away that she fairly screamed in frustration. Thus the dark god mocked his clergy and thus the ebony priestess would be on edge until she could prove her worth.
