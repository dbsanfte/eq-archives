Cancellation of a Wedding

Posted by Flutrose Dreamchaser on April 5, 2000:

Flutrose drummed her fingers on the table as she considered the message she was about to write. The candle flickered very slightly now and again as she would reach out and sip of her honey mead, then set it aside and once again stare at the blank piece of parchment before her.

She sighed and muttered an oath to herself at the perfidy of men. Placing quill to scroll, she hastily scribbled a message, sprinkled sand upon the ink to rapidly dry it, and then tinkled the bell which summoned a page.

"Boy, do ye know the location of the guildhouse of House Winterborne? Tis verra far from here." The lad eagerly bobbed his head up and down. "Good. Take this scroll, and deliver it unto the House Scribe ye shall find there. Tell him that Lady Flutrose wishes this message sent to all people of the House. This money is for thy efforts. Ye will receive the same amount again once I am told ye have completed delivery." She handed the boy five platinum pieces, and summoned food and drink for him that he not have an excuse to tarry along the way.

One day later......

The halfling bobbed from side to side, grinning at the Scribe. "You be da scribe fer Winterborne?" The scribe looked up impatiently from his sheaf of papers. "Aye lad, that I am. What business hae ye with me? Make it quick, I am a busy man."

The halfling cleared his throat and grinned to himself. He handed the Scribe a rather tattered scroll, and began to speak. "The Lady Flutrose wants ya to send this to all da peoples in Winterborne. She also wants ya ta give me ten plat, and she will pay ya back later." The scribe reached for a purse and handed the halfing ten platinum. Dismissing the boy, he broke the seal on the missive and began to read....

"I have been asked by Lady Trespare to inform all that, due to unforeseen circumstances, her proposed marriage to Lord Amabala has been cancelled."

The scribe then began hiring the necessary couriers and transcribing the message into the Records. His opinion of the message mattered not; he had a job to do.
