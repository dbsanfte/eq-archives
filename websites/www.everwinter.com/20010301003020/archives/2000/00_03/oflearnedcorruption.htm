Of Learned Corruption

Posted by Malozc on March 7, 2000:

Strangely, as Malozc tried to fit in, he only seemed to make new enemies. The time spent in Highhold in particular confused him. He helped fight the hoards of orcs and gnolls that punished the citizens and guards of this place, yet, something seemed unclear. The thoughts would continually come back that something was not right. IT was then, after a night of mastering brewing, and falling asleep on the Inn's dock that he found out why.

The morning was bright and the sun stung his sensitive eyes. As he rolled over to block the light, he fell off the dock and into the cold water of the pass.

Screaming aloud in the water's medium he was suddenly lost of which way was up, excitedly he swam for an opening that promised air. As he found that air he realized this was not the same view he'd left.

The walls rose and created echoes as the water dripped from his robes. He spied someone coming and tried to hail him but found himself stopping. The man had a dusty appearance, a sinister look that Malozc instantly took for aggressive. Quickly he summoned the shadows and hid himself. He looked down at the water pool beneath his feet and cursed silently. Slowly he crouched down and held his breath. The man came to the waters edge and reached to ground as if to listen. The man waited and listened. Just as if he might discover Malozc, another man, much like himself smacked him back, "What's the matter? Life of the smuggler gotcha jumpy!" His cohort laughed. "Nae, just a feeling." Quit worrying. Ye know we've got the guards in our pockets, now come back to camp, we've just gotten a pottery wheel in, a complete set of equipment so that we can master any skill we wish!"

'Aye, I suppose you're right. Let's go"

Malozc let go a painfully held breath. "Baaasssstards! I help you by fighting the scourge of orcs and gnolls and ye are simply a front! His voice carried more than he had intended and the echo brought foot steps from the pass. Quickly he dove into the pool and retired to the inn. The thoughts of being played for a fool were too much. There would be revenge. There would be restitution and oh yes . . . there would be death.

Posted by Shiver Hel Despana on March 16, 2000:

The hate welled up inside and sent a tremor of anger through the Teir’dal. "This is what it is to be a dark elf," he thought, then corrected himself; "This is what it is to be a dragon denied his true form, his birthright of power." And yet, it was more. The shaking in his hands, the rage that surged through his frail form… it came from a place so deep, the necromancer couldn’t begin to fathom the extent of the sensation. He crouched within the secret entrance to the aqueducts outside the gates of Qeynos, unseen and safe from the human guards, but his mind seethed with visions of vengeance. He had long forgotten the extent of the crimes committed against him. He was consumed with the urge to steal the life from the sheep that swarmed the lands around him, oblivious.

The citizens of Qeynos would not let him enter the room he had rented in his dark elven guise. He would be stripped of his valuables and thrown from the gates if caught within the walls of the city. Such was the gratitude he had earned? How many gnolls had been dispatched with a swift strike from his staff? How many bandits had been consumed in a wave of flame, a swarm of stinging insects? They didn’t understand the magic of form change, only illusions. Shivrehel Despana, Teir’dal Necromancer, was branded a Heretic in Neriak, and an outcast in the lands of light. He had to forge a new reputation, his accomplishments as the servant of Karana limited to his life as a human.

Amorsaylnn, at the request of her father, had offered to assist Shiver. She was capable of destroying entire camps of the bandits in the Karanas. She would weave an enchantment of invisibility over him and announce to the residents of Qeynos that the two Teir’dal were off to deal with the brigand menace. The guards scoffed, though not loudly. She had brought proof of forty-one dead bandits in one evening alone. They scoffed because Shivrehel was clearly new to the world, though his equipment and company spoke otherwise. How they could sense his weakness, Shiver did not know. He knew that it would be remedied in time… and they would come to fear his name.

"And this too, shall pass." The words bloomed in his mind, surfing the white-caps of his rancor. Revenge! He longed for the day Lady Vox would bow to him and Innoruuk… the Lord of Hate would bleed at Shiver’s feet, broken by the power he had given all Teir’dal.

He rose, clenching the Staff of Writhing tightly in gloves infused with the power of darkness. He knew his appearance impressed the young warriors venturing near the eastern walls of the Qeynos region. The brilliant white gossamer robe, dark cloak imbued with the power of necromancy, silver rings set with jade, and bracers forged in electrum and set with matching jade, a bat-fang headband, and the skull of a gnoll shaman held in his other hand… he was adorned in equipment far beyond his minimal training, gained over twenty years ago. They were gifts… from Erondis, Olam, Morphite, and his own work as a human. He would not begin his adventures with a training robe and dagger this time.

He heard gutteral shouts near his hiding spot, the foul tongue of a gnoll. Stepping forth, he turned to face the squat dog-like creature, focused the anima about him, and pulled the very life from the gnoll into his body. It raced towards him, the swirls of malevolent magic trailing. He swung the skull-topped staff—a gift from Olam—and the gnoll fell, silent at his feet.

"Citzens of Qeynos…" he whispered. "Soon ye will welcome dr’Shivrehel Despana into thy fair city. Soon ye will embrace a name that will change thy precious Norrath forever."

Posted by Olam on March 17, 2000:

Most of what occurred in the world occurred while he was away. The skies turned bright, the skies turned dark. It didn't matter if he was present or not . . . time continued pass and events occurred. Young warriors became skilled in the way of the land and surpassed his present prowess. How sad. The world didn't revolve around him. He chuckled. A half-hearted chuckle that only mildly disguised his distaste for the situation. Often he moved from one land to the next . . . surveying, watching, slaying. This was Olam Wyrmsoul, he'd wanted a reputation for power, but to get that power he needed to accept this world for what it was, not fight it . . . not hide from it . . . oh, he so wanted to hide from the deceit and foolishness that ran rampant. But he couldn't. It was present, and it was time to stand. "So be it," he mumbled and arose from his perch overlooking the lava soaked plains of the land known as Lavastorm.

A bird of fire flew close to his head but did not pay attention to him, just merely floated by on the waves of heat rising from the ground. Olam sipped from his nearly depleated reserves -- water had become scarce and his days of foraging in Kelethin had not taught him enough to find water in this environment. The caves of Solusek lay below him now. Their openings almost clouded by the steam whisps that arose from the lake of fire below. "Tempting . . ." Olam mused, as he imagined a martyr's leap into the pool, "but no." He made his way to the upper cavern's opening. "Here . . . this will do for now."

He leaned hard against the hot granite of the hill side. The heat made travel difficult and his chain itched, despite his cloth undershirt. His eyes rolled back into his head and he sighed. He focused on his life before . . . the times in the Aerie and the times that he used to enjoy with his vassals. It was then, in the reflection of his past, that he felt the power that was familiar to him. "Shiver?" he half-whispered aloud. Again, the wave went through him. He could feel it's power but it was faint . . . barely distinguishable from the myriad of cross-talk he'd received from this plane. Each time the familar feeling passed through him and he smiled a faint smile, "Ah then, old friend . . . you've arrived and are already using my gift. It is well!"

Olam arose to his feet. He looked at the cave's opening and smiled as he entered it's depths . . .

Posted by Malozc on April 7, 2000:

Days had gone by slowly for Malozc. The incestuous power of the smugglers he had underestimated. They were entrenched more than he imagined and it was too much for he and his incantation alone. Even the power Innoruuk provided through his pets were no match . . . indeed, something that Vox had told him he was being set up for . . . that is, the eventual realization that Innoruuk was not a God to be worshiped so much as used.

Thus, the dark caster had relegated himself to the plains of Karana to watch the peoples and learn their ways. He slew that which others slew, he fought and sold himself to their ways. All was good? "This disgusts me. I grow tired of the norm. I seek that which was bid me to do!" His words called out more loudly then he intended. The water that ran under the wooden bridge seemed to stops its incessant wailing at his call. Just then he heard a pebble roll by him. Quickly he swung his flail, the mighty Harvester, at the attacker's head . . . unfortunately it was only by mere balance that he didn't fall on his ass. The would-be melee'r missed the target . . . a small boy with parchment, by many feet. The boy stood there, pale, and sweating. He mumbled something akin to "I've GOT to get another line of work" as he offered Malozc the scroll he held. "Please sir, I mean no harm and I bring nothing more than a message from Dirge."

Malozc snickered. "Ye bring no harm, but yet you bring a missive from Dirge?" The boy looked confused. Malozc shook his head, "I nae expect you to understand that.

Malozc took the scroll and read it over. A brief look of despair crossed his face and he nodded. Quickly he scribed a letter in response.

Here," he hands the boy a scroll. "be a good lad and take this to the man." The boy smiled. Malozc looked at him and knelt down. Leaning on his scythe he whispered in the boy's ear, "Should you fail, your head will be a rotting nest for maggots, and your body my newest pet!" The boy's smile quickly faded as he backed away so quickly he fell. Scrambling up the hillside, the dust of his departure hiding his feet, the boy ran West towards Qeynos.
