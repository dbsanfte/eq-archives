In Human Form ~ Dhirge

What can I tell thee that ye’ll believe? I was born so long ago, time was meaningless if it even existed. My kind are long-lived and I am exceptional among my kind. As brazen as that sounds, I have ample support for my audacious claims. I was named dr’Shivreheldespaire, and ruled the Under Hel of the first world at the behest of Faluzure, the Night Dragon. Eventually, I tired of his divine posturing and his corruption. Dragons should nae be subject to the spectre of death or parodied in undeath. I fled, eluding the elder power, until I returned to the surface and became lord of Clan Darkmoon.

I quickly subjugated two other clans, Lurker and Mire, then set out to settle a score. My kind were always hunted by humanity and when my brother Magdelen fell, I swore to avenge his death. I seduced a young acolyte and brought down justice upon the Covenant of witches that had taken residence in his lair, but the Council of Wyrms did not permit interference in human culture. The library holds an adequate account of this mission in Solon (HERE). I was sentenced to death, but escaped to another, much younger world.


In Dark Elven Form ~ Shivrehel

In Norrath did I meet my wife, Vespre. She was born of the Teir’dal and recently widowed, a mother of two adopted children. Soon did I learn these children were nae what I expected, but rather magical creations wrought by an archmagi of the land. Faluzure found me through his persona as Inorruuk. He cursed Vespre to a horrible death, a rotting disease, and I fled to Toril, a cosmopolitan city known as Neverwinter. I have found several journal entries I made from this time. Ye are welcome t’ examine my diary (HERE).

In Neverwinter did I find the fate of fellow exiles, notably those who were so richly "rewarded" for their role in reporting my interference with humanity. They too were exiled from the Isles, from our home land. They banded together under the leadership of a gold calling himself the Great Wyrm, of all things. He was clearly a deluded hatchling. In a matter of weeks, he was dead by my hand and this new Council was well under way. Again, my journal has a more detailed account of the event (HERE).

I was called away to Terra, a land in which no magic functions, shortly thereafter. Vespre was suffering, the disease wrought upon her by Faluzure effective even in that land. Eventually, she succumbed to his dark will, though her children were safe. They followed me back to Neverwinter, guided by their long forgotten nanny, ry’Fallon. They sided against me, joining forces to end my existence, believing me to be the cause of their mother’s death. They did nae succeed.

Years passed, and I devised an elaborate ruse to keep Faluzure from my life. I saved a rogue topaz known as Lokisun, preparing his nearly vacant mind to hold my own personality. It took many months and during it, he was reunited with his lover and sister, Eclipse (here). Nonetheless, I completed my mission. I fought Faluzure, knowing I would fail, and transferred my mind into Lokisun’s body. Unfortunately, my comrades in the exiled Council felt obligated to assist. Several perished and my Queen Rianna suffered the same disease Vespre had, years earlier. While fleeing the battle ground, I was trapped by Chronepsis on the astral plane. She reared up, breathing over my new body… and suddenly I was a corpse, though aware. Lokisun proceeded to flee as my destroyed, true self, watched.

((The history behind Dirge's nature and the loss of Rianna can be found HERE.))

I learned much about myself then. I was truly a child of Io, a force greater than the powers. I would not perish. Perhaps I could not. I tapped the power of the eternal and branded myself Dirge, the dark song. I returned to Neverwinter, though few knew of my identity. In time, Rianna showed signs of the disease and stepped down. I ascended to the throne of the exiled Council.  We recruited a veritable horde of humanoids to assist us, notably the venerable Star Riders and a group known as the Sons of the Grey Wolf (Steppe Warriors). Together, we laid siege to the Aerie of the Council on the Isles. We were victorious, though the price was high. We called the new rule the Conclave of the Wyrm and I proceeded to send agents to other lands, to make their mark.

We traveled to Sosaria, a land known as Tanarus, a place called the Realm, and to Norrath. Over the years, I found myself suffering from a hollow sensation. Those I called friends were few and far between. The crown, so to speak, was heavy upon my head. Finally, a creature known as the Radiant united many of the Council-loyal clans and they were opposed to my rule, and that of my friend, Valator, who had foiled the Radiant long ago. Battles raged, Valator was lost, and finally, we settled on a compromise.

The Radiant was inducted into the leadership in Valator’s place. He used his time and my aversion to what the Conclave was becoming. He corrupted the artifacts given to my kind by Io himself, or so legend stated. The Dragon Orbs the clans communicated with were permeated with a powerful charm of servitude to the Radiant. I discovered the plot far too late, but we fought valiantly to save our race. Dragons could nae live in slavery. Death was a better fate. I destroyed the binding Orb, but the backlash of energy took a toll upon us.

Our dragon forms were stripped, our power greatly reduced. I have some accounts of the events of this Orb Winter, held in the library still (HERE). These accounts make me weep for lost friends and have displaced the very essence of what we are, or so I thought. Months passed, and I grew to loathe the Conclave. My friends were few, the price of leadership high. I no longer had my wings, nor much of the will that moved me. I journeyed to the land of Norrath again, intent on diversion. Shar, my adopted daughter, came with me… and I learned of her fate.

This is how I came to lead Winterborne... and as of a few fortnights past, I have come to step down and assume the role I was accustomed to in Neverwinter. Rianna, the Queen that I followed in that land--she who assisted me against the Night Dragon--has returned from her ghostly banishement (HERE). I feel a great weight has lifted from my shoulders, a debt I can now address. Again, I seek what I once did... influence, power, respect, even fear. But this time, I seek this for reasons benevolent... to cure my fellows of House Winterborne.
