The Aided

Posted by Morphite on July 5, 2000:

The day was hot and humid. The steam arising from the beautiful falls of East Karana made it hard to breathe deep. Kimber wiped the sweat from her smooth brow and looked at Morphite. "Are you ready for my help, wuss?" she coyly replied, brushing the dangle of red hair from her eye.

Morphite calmly closed his book and struggled to stand upon his frail legs. A slight smile as he pursed his lips in a short response, "Yes . . ." The response was half sigh, have spoken word. It was in Common, he didn't want to confuse the lass by using anything more complex he mused.

The pair ran quickly, blessed of the wolf spirit, and came upon the prey -- a stinky, half decaying reaver of the dead. The beast, no doubt raised from the dead by the Split Claw necromancer that had slain Morphite only a day earlier. The dark caster was the first morphite had seen in the area. Most beasts were of melee capability only. But this one . . . he slew Morphite with almost no trouble. In fact, Morphite's tale tells of a close battle, lost only due to the multitudes of other creatures who aided in his defeat . . . a lion, a spirit wolf, and of course, the dark one's pet. But that's his redention of it, narely a bit of truth to it, I'm sure.

The two raised arms and books and began weaving colors of lights from their hands. The shadows they played across the plains and trees was earie but beautiful. Morphite's animation,carrying shield and axe, was surrounded by thorns on his invisible body, and sped to a fervor. Morphite raised his mug and saluted the creature by tearing at his ability to resist magiks. The enraged beast stormed his way, arms flailing.

Calmly, the enchanter backed up and rooted the beast, it took! The next magiks slowed the monsters movements and then attempted to choke whatever air he may be breathing out of him. No doubt due to pure adrennelin, the beast broke free of the root and attacked Morphite. Instantly the animation was upon the reaver with a furor. Slash after slash tore into the beast, but it didn't care . . . no, at the moment it wanted the frail enchanter lying upon a pool of red on the plain.

The first blow drew blood from his chin and the second knocked Morphite back. Unable to cast against the onslaught he attempted a feeble block. Thankfully, slowed and weakened, Morphite was able to bewilder the creature with a colorful spray and he withdrew from the melee. Quickly he made the earth grab the creatures feet and while the animation slashed, he sat and read . . .

Morphite looked up from his book. The reaver was in sad shape. Unseen thorns ripped at his hands as he clawed at the animation. He broke the earth from his feet but didn't care about the enchanter any longer. The animation had his full attention. Rotten flesh and dirty blood dripped from the animations weapon and shield. Each piece of flesh hung on the invisible thorns that Kimber had summoned. And then . . . it was over.

The beast's howl echoed across the plains. Morphite turned to smile at Kimber, but she was gone. Off to find another victim no doubt . . . off to continue the hunt for Morphite's sole benefit.
