Posted by Eglantyne on March 31, 2000:

Eglantyne felt the cold steel of Sharhel's blade against her cheek, its tip a mere fraction from her eye. Inwardly she laughed. Such a typically female reaction! And so if she lost an eye, what did it matter? A male would have had the good sense to apply his blade to her throat, where it could do some real damage.

She searched within the Barbarian, and only rage was present. That did not surprise Eglantyne. She doubted the barbarian had the capacity to feel more than a single emotion at one time. Extending her search to the others in the room, she felt anger, hostility, curiosity, and even a little fear - but to the monk's credit, he hid it well.

"I already answered those questions, wench. I am Eglantyne. I serve no man, no god, no winged beasts. Only myself."

Eglantyne's gaze landed upon Vage - he who was cursed with losing his dragon seeming and forced to wander Norrath in guises that were more or less of humanoid form. Her mind opened, and she felt pain. Intense pain.

The cleric smiled to herself. She had wanted to draw the attention of the Winterborne, and it appeared that she had admirably succeeded.

She was not concerned with her present predicament. Her safety was already assured, though they knew it not.

Eglantyne spoke again to Sharhel, the armor of the wench digging into her back and slightly scratching her skin. Her voice rang loud and clear, with no inflections of fear contained within. "Sharhel, look upon your old esteemed Vage. He lays upon the floor near you, near dead from pain, and yet you waste your time concerning yourself with me."

Every pair of eyes in the room swung to the corner where Vage lay writhing in pain, obvlivious to his surroundings. A collective gasp arose and Eglantyne felt the barbarian's hold upon her loosen. Sharhel and her companions made one step towards Vage, and Eglantyne took advantage of the opportunity. She quickly slipped from her grasp and fled the room while everyone's attention was diverted elsewhere.

Speeding quickly to the snake and rat-infested land surrounding the city, she collapsed against the wall, laughing with joy and a little relief. Aye, her plans were progressing even better than she had hoped.

Although Tanner gave the impression of studied casualness, Eglantyne knew that he watched her tiniest movement like a griffon eyes its prey. He had no wish to harm her, else he would have already done so; of this she was certain.

Tanner lowered his claymore from his shoulder to his side, the muscles holding it in a relaxed fashion, yet ready to pounce in an instant. "Quit playin' games,lass. Why do ya pester everyone about how they're feelin'? Ya tryin' to start trouble?"

Eglantyne looked at him intently. "Because I sense what they feel. And it amuses me to see how many of them lie to me, even try to lie to themselves." The barbarian's eye gleamed. "Bah" he said. "Ye weren't born when half this crap they're fussin' over happened."


"That is quite true. But the bonds of time do not erase feelings. They erase the current events, the physical happenings, that is true, but the feelings remain. They may be deep, they may have many stone layers around them, but they still exist."

Tanner rubbed the stubble on his chin while he considered this answer. His voice became hard, insistent. "How do ya feel these things?" Eglantyne shrugged. "I do not know 'how.' You might as well ask me how I know my name or that I am female. It simply 'is.' And once I sense a feeling, often I can also 'sense' the incident or circumstance that caused the feeling. Not always, but sometimes. It just happens."

The barbarian snorted, perhaps in disbelief. "Why do ya toy with people?" Eglantyne shrugged again. "Because it amuses me to do so."

Eglantyne felt a miasma begin to interfere with her body and mind. Everything seemed to dip, to swirl, to become slightly out of focus. She tried to move, but was unable to force even her eyelids to do her bidding. A voice from behind her spoke.

"Eglantyne. Ye are hereby charged with the murder of Kenials, Cleric of Quellious. Ye will be taken before the High Council of Erudin and there stand judgement for your crime." A cloud of silver smoke swirled around her, and she disappeared.
