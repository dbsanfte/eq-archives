The Pale Lady's Pitch

Posted by Shar Hel Despana on August 18, 2000:

The midday sun beat down on the witch's coif, making her uncomfortable. She pulled it off and shook loose a thick mane of auburn hair. Turning to Balkin and the others, she called out, "it isn't much farther now." She eyed the motley entourage while running backwards. Some old friends, some new. Some not friends at all, but kindred nonetheless, in that they all had a common goal, unraveling the mysteries of Winter.

Balkin glanced up, as he ran along side the barbarian. She was awfully excited, and unless she was killing something, that was unusual. He had no idea what she was up to, but running across these damnable plains was annoying him. He sighed, as she declared "not much farther now" and kept on running.

Finally, they reached the crest of a hill and she paused. Before them stood the beginnings of a huge complex. The outer walls of the vast stronghold were complete, outlining an enormous parcel of the southwestern corner of South Karana. Unmanned parapets dotted the fortified walls.

Balkin immediately started judging the stone work. Even from a distance, his critical dwarven eye was drawn to several points along the wall and around the gate. He frowned suddenly… Human work, he thought to himself.

"What do ye think? Ye can't see it from here, but the area inside is parceled off. There is a market square, an arena, and a sizeable spot that's been set aside just for us. I was hoping we'd make this our home away from home. The guards in Qeynos are lazy. Here, we'll be amply protected. The guards will be paid well but beaten severely, should they slack off." The pale lady smirked as she watched Fallon's mouth gape open. "Well, they aren't the reliable constructs Daddy was capable of creating on Toril, but I think we can keep them in line. I'm kidding! I'm kidding! I have no intention of using untrained civilians. I'm hoping we can entice a few of the Brokenblades in joining us here."

"What do I think? I think ye hired humans to do the walls didn't ye? Bah, dwarves woulda been better. We've got the best stone mason's on Norrath. Still, I guess they be adequate."

The pale lady rumbled with laughter at the thought. "I think your eyes are tired, ye crotchety ole dwarf! That IS the work of your kin! Come, let's give ye a closer look. Have ye met m'brother yet, Balkin? Like ye, Tanner would ne'er let me do such a fool thing. Besides, I've been around long enough to know who knows what about building something that'll stand the test of time."

Balkin scowled and scrutinized the stonework. "Hmmph. Human guards I take it? Might better get yerself some elves, and dwarves as well. Ye'll need someone that can see through the dark. Better yet, if ye know some mercenary company we can trust, might be better to bring an established group in. Won't need to beat em often if they be established, and well paid besides. Ye understand, I'll be wantin' to test anyone hired to guard me new guild home yes?" Looking way up at Sharhel's face, he spoke again, cutting her off, "Don't even argue girl, ye heard me."

Shar smirked at Zsalshun, "I'm counting on it, Balkin. I think ye'll find your match among most of the BrokenBlades."

As his Spirit of the wolf faded, Balkin joined the group, walking through the gate. "Broken Blades? These be some o yer friends from yer past yes? I guess, if ye trust them, that would be acceptable. I'll be meeting with their captain before any final decision is made though. Warclaw's job be to protect the clans, and I'll nae shirk the responsibility."

"Indeed! And I believe ye may have already met their captain, Tanner. If he agrees to work with us on this, I think the two of ye would make an excellent team for devising and constructing the compound." The pale lady beamed enthusiastically. She didn't share the dwarf's sense of responsibility, but appreciated his zeal nonetheless. "And this here will be the market area. Those two connecting buildings there are mine, another reason I wanted to move out here. I doubt the folks in Qeynos would agree with the business. On the left will be the Tin Man, a gambling casino named as a tribute to Blood Eyes, a legend among wyrms. And next to that will be Auril's Delight, an ice cream parlor/brothel."

"Bah, I donna think I want to know what will be goin on in there either, just don't get yerself killed girl." Shaking his head, Balkin grinned. "And a casino as well, eh? Bah, games of chance always favor the house. But, since we be the house, it can't all be that bad. And ice cream. Tried that once in Qeynos. Too damnable sweet fer me. Might be fine fer ye tall folk, but not fer me". Glancing at Fhallon, he replied slyly, "a brothel though, now that might be a fine addition!"

With a smirk, Shar continued reassuringly, "Oh now, don't be too concerned. I know ye are all wondering how we'll make ice cream without milk. I've already chatted with vendors in Qeynos and they've agreed to sell a few of their most coveted milk cows. Well, they don't look like any cow I've ever seen, but that's what they call them."

Balkin removed his helm, and shook out his deep auburn hair. "Bah, Cows be fer eatin! Far more useful that way if ye ask me."

Shar gave Balkin a queer look and continued, "As for the brothel, I wouldn't fret over that too much either. I'm merely doing my civic duty to bring a much needed service to Norrath!" Shar laughed, ignoring Fallon's look of shocked disapproval.

Balkin roared his laughter, at the scandalized look Fhallon had on her face. She really needed to get out more.

"So ye see, there is plenty of room for other trades vendors. I plan on having a forge built and whatever else we need. Winterborne members will get first dibs on a store front, should ye be interested in selling your wares."

With a calculating look, Balkin watched Fhallon's face light up, at the thought of a store. Then again, maybe she shouldn't get out much. She was a gentle soul to her core, and though she had skills, in combat, she much preferred the home guard. He thought maybe she had found a home to guard indeed. And he was equally sure that she would sell anything he made for a small commission. Perhaps he needed to sit down with her, and give her a bit of help with her tailoring. She was coming along well, but still had a long way to go.

"We'll need a forge lass, that be true. And it should be one of us that mans it. Makaz be more than capable, and a master smith as well. Ye should approach him, and see if he wishes to take over permanent operations. He'll let us novices use it on occasion, perhaps even tutor us a bit, till we be masters as well. It can only be good for the Winterborne."

"Excellent idea, Balkin."

The pale lady led the group through what was to be the market square and to another open area. "And this, if ye wish it, will be the new, or alternate, base of operations for Winterborne. As ye see, I've nae begun the construction. I wanted to get your input first. If ye are nae interested, I have other uses in mind for it. If ye agree, I will send for Tanner. I'm interested in getting help from him on how to fortify the place. Hopefully, he'll find this location agreeable. If not, nothing is set in stone, pardon the pun. We could just as easily move to a different location. I chose this spot because it is out of they way, yet very near one of the main trade routes between northern and southern Norrath. It's also partially surrounded by those pretty daunting looking mountains there."

Balkin paced through the area that was soon to be Winterborne Keep. He walked over near her and the others, and leapt up onto one of the giant blocks of stone near by. He shaded his eyes from the setting sun, and scanned the whole area again. Finally, he looked at Sharhel. With in internal smile, Balkin crouched down, and motioned her to come to him. With the haft of his Executioners Axe over his knees, he spun the vicious double blade via the haft, and watched as her eyes were drawn to the spinning blade. She walked over nonchalantly, till there was less than 5 feet between them. Crouched as he was on the block of stone, they were now eye to eye. He continued spinning the double bladed axe, and with a hard glare, finally spoke.

"The inner keep be the seat o' the Winterborne. It also be our final defense, if the outer walls ever be breached. I will nae trust the lives o' the house on inferior work. If'n yer friend Tanner be an expert on fortification and defense, then send fer him. Him an me friend can work out the specifics, but I'll brook no nonsense from you on this. Ye have one chance; one evening to change me mind. If ye can't do it, I leave in the morning. If I come back, and construction has been started, me mates will take down what's up and start over. Understand me?"

Shar let the disgruntled dwarf vent and reasoned that she probably should've included him on her project sooner. "In for a copper, in for a platinum, aye, Balkin?" The pale lady laughed and raised her hands to the others. "What say the rest of ye?"

Posted by Zsalshun D'Vox on September 7, 2000:

The dark cleric smiled at the drifting clouds as she lay on the grassy hill. In the background was the low lazy buzz of cicadas punctuated by the frequent sounds of workers. The outer walls of the new fortress were all laid in and hordes of laborers bustled about to shore up their formidable heights. The inner walls and buildings were a little behind in completion compared to the immense first defense. Zsalshun had accompanied Shar and Balkin to the fortress a while back but had decided to keep out of the way. The barbarian woman’s skill in prodding workers to their best efforts made the Tier’Dal think of the merchants of her homeland. The white woman took a dark glee in reprimanding those who slacked in her presence. No further behind, although with entirely different methods for encouraging the workers, Balkin hovered over the dwarven laborers checking their every move. The priestess could offer nothing that the two weren’t already doing, so she opted to range the plains.

She had just come back from killing the treants that lived in the northwest boundary of the land. Her dark god had finally granted her the right to spells of the 24th level of accomplishment but the ones she hunted with were a waste for her new talents. They were competent enough to stay mostly out of harms way but they squibbled pettily amongst themselves and continually got into trouble that they expected her to bail them out of. The priestess used them to gain her spells then left them to their own devices. She cared not what became of them, their names already fading from her memory.

Now she found herself lying on the gentle slope of the hills overlooking the fortress in the distance. Even from here she could hear the cranky old dwarf yelling at the stone workers for whatever flaw he had found now. Zsalshun smiled and headed towards the source of the tirade. The workers were going to kill Balkin before long and the grumpy warrior was probably not even that upset with them. The priestess thought that both the workers and the warrior would benefit if Balkin could work some of his pent up energy out on something else.

Approaching from behind the dwarf she insolently leaned up against the smoothed surface of stone. “You want to spend the rest of the eve yelling at the workers, dwarf, or would you rather let them do what they do and practice doing what you do? It’s about time to head up to Runneye if you are still up to it.”

“BAH! We ain’t got enough people to assault the temple there!”

The warrior bristled as he critically eyed one of the masons. Zsalshun could see that his presence had been tolerated up to this point but even the most stoic of people can be pushed only so far, large 2 handed Battleaxe’s non-withstanding.

“So then, what shall we hunt? There has to be something here worth while.”

The dwarf paused, a crinkle of distaste washing over his face as he thought about hunting in the southern plains. “Aye. IF we could find some gnolls outside the entrance to Split Paw, that might work.”

Zsalshun nodded. “I need to get some things from the fort. Shall I meet you there then?” Without waiting she walked into the gap that would be the main gate.

Balkin Eyed her back as she walked away. She had a way o’ getting him riled up that wasn’t even explainable. It wasn’t dislike at all, but she sure could rub him the wrong way. Or maybe it was that she knew him to well eh? Shaking his head, he watched the dwarven stone workers placing another perfectly hewn block of stone in place. They didn’t need his help, that was certain, but he was so antsy to get this done, he had been riding them pretty hard. The outer walls of the Inner keep were coming along fine, and he was tired o’ the inactivity anyway. With a twirl of his axe, and a “Harumph” he spun on his heel and stalked off.

Grabbing his packs, he headed towards the gate. Another contingent of dwarves was working on finalizing that as well. The huge iron banded doors were complete, and would be hung in place in the next week. As he walked, he could feel Sharhel’s eyes on him. Looking up on the outer wall where she stood, hands on hips, he pointed his axe up at her, then back at the masons working on the inner keep. She knew what he wanted. No words were necessary. With a burst energy, he ran full tilt out the gate and didn’t look back.

Zsalshun jogged up to the place Balkin said he and some others were waiting. She had to laugh when she discovered he had found another warrior with similar prowess as his own, a ranger and a paladin not too far behind the two warriors. The dwarf was in a blood letting mood and had assembled a formidable team for just that purpose. Swords would flash and limbs would fly and Zsalshun was there to ensure that only gnoll limbs were the ones that remained unattached and leaking on the sun soaked ground.

The warriors waded in and easily dispatched gnoll after gnoll. The ground turned red from their victories and still they clamored for more. The ranger ran the width of the southern plains, bringing in all sorts of creatures for the group to wet their blades on. At one point in time Zsalshun looked up startled from her studies as the ground shook and a monstrous Cyclopes lumbered up to the bevy of warriors. With little help from her god the four whittled at the giant until it collapsed. Balkin let out a triumphant war whoop and urged the ranger to find another.

The night went on merrily as foe after foe fell to the blood lust of the fighters. Eventually the paladin declared that he had things to attend to in his temple and bid farewell to the group. Zsalshun sat down to reclaim the mana that greater heal depleted so quickly and was startled to hear the ranger talking to a wandering druid named Xantherax. The priestess looked up from her book at the fuzzy muzzle of a wolf.

“Would that be you, old friend?”

The dog grinned and lolled his tongue. Somewhere in her mind she heard the greeting thoughts of the druid she had not seen in months.

“You might ask Balkin to join if you want. A druid would be a boon.” The priestess smiled wickedly, “Besides, then you could take over half of the buffing spells.” Her new healing spell plus the standard buffs were straining her mana. The druid would ease the outward flow of the precious energy.

Balkin roared a greeting and invited the wayward druid into the foray.

Balkin’s grin could only be called vicious, as he landed another critical blow, Axe biting deeply into the side of his latest victim. The addition of Xantherax to their ranks merely heightened the intense rush of emotion, both pleasure and pain that he felt at the time. It had been many months since the druid had been seen in the lands. Feeling the healing magic of Zsalshun wash over him again, Balkin finished off his current victim, and looked around quickly for another. To late, the other Gnoll was falling to the blades of his current companions. Turning back to the body of the Gnoll at his feet, Balkin took what little coin it had, kicked it once to make sure it was dead, and left the body to rot. The damnable gnolls deserved nothing else.

Taking a moment to rest, no enemies being in sight, Balkin removed his armored mask and helm, used one hand to wipe the blood off them. Even with that protection, he could still feel where the blood had worked it’s way down under his armor, drying there, mixing with his sweat. Nothing like the smell of armored, sweaty, bloody dwarf. He replaced his armor, and prepared for the next round. The gnolls were nothing, if not persistent. Another 2 or threesome of scouts would be leaving the lair soon. Just in time to die.

He had to admit, as he spotted the next scouting party leaving the lair, that Zsalshun had been right. He needed this more than he needed to oversee those masons. THIS was what he lived for. THIS was who and what he was. Leaning his axe against his side, he pulled his bow off his shoulder and quickly knocked an arrow. Someone needed to get the attention of this group eh? Might as well be him. The arrow sank into the thigh of the lead gnoll, who turned, and charged the dwarf, and his companions. Slinging his bow, Balkin grabbed his axe, and backed up to the middle of the skirmish line. He would bear the brunt of the assault, but that was nothing new. Wish a vicious swipe of his axe, battle was joined again.

Zsalshun watched, as the mighty dwarf unleashing his full fury on the hapless gnolls. His growl turned into a battle roar, as he let loose his control, allowing the rage within to boil outward. These foul gnolls were already dead, they just didn’t know it yet.

He had been holding back, not letting himself go for some time, though he knew he had to. She would let him rage for a time, then heal him, and cast her soothing magics on him, bringing him back from the brink of rage and madness. A good fight was one thing, but he had to release his rage, or it would consume him. Though they had never talked about it, she understood that he placed his mind and sanity in her hands at times like this. Very few warriors were blessed, or cursed, depending on how you looked at it, with true battle rage. For better or worse, Balkin had it.

She watched, as the battle raged, almost continually. Timing her healing spells so that she would not anger the gnolls any more than necessary. The others stayed a step or two away from Balkin, even between battles. He was in his own world now, not even acknowledging her healing, or Xantherax’s buffing spells. Just stood there, growling and breathing hard, waiting for the next kill. Finally, after countless battles, it was enough. His body was growing weary and he might fall unconscious soon.

She stood from her meditations, and nodded to the other fighters nearby. They understood, backing away a few paces. Concentrating, she healed the bloody dwarf again, and immediately followed that spell with a mind lulling, gentle spell, trying to force a calm upon his raging heart. What happened next always unnerved the dark priestess and made her cringe back on the inside despite standing her ground. Balkin’s head whipped around, and he took several steps towards her, axe raised as if he would attack. What she could see of his eyes past the Grotesque Mask he wore seemed to drain of rage and pain. He halted, and lowered his axe, breathing like a bellows, and obviously near exhaustion. Zsalshun nodded slightly, whether to the dwarf, herself or her Dark god she wasn’t sure but once again the warrior had come back from his rage without impaling her upon his axe.

Balkin looked around, planting the blades of his axe on the ground, and leaning on the haft. He was so tired he could hardly focus on the events at hand. Unhinging the mask from his face with one hand, he stared hard at the dark elf, just a few steps away. She nodded, and glanced at the others behind him. With a sigh, he turned, and looked up at the others, at the same time, noting the bodies around him. The carnage was actually rather shocking. Xantherax was still there, so were the Ranger and Warrior he had started the night with. The paladin was gone, to his temple Zsalshun whispered into his mind. “I must go my friends” He removed his gauntlets, and grasped forearms with the ranger, and then with the tall human warrior, who nodded seriously, recognizing what had occurred this day and night. Then he turned to Xantherax. “It’s been some time my friend, next time, don’t be gone so long.” The druid nodded and smiled, as he to grasped hand and forearm with the dwarf. “It won’t be so long again my friend,” said the druid, as he blessed all with spirit of the wolf.

“Zsalshun, run with me?” asked the dwarf. She nodded, as the group split up, each heading their own way. Though he was far to weary to run, he would manage for a time, she knew. Perhaps he would even be stubborn enough to make it back to the keep. With a mental shrug, she ran along side his armored form. Glancing out of the corner of her eye, she saw his forehead beetle down into an intense frown. Yes, he would be stubborn enough. He would sleep when they got to the keep, probably through the next day and night as well, and she would watch over him until she knew he was out of danger.

The waif of battle stench drifted over to her as they ran and she briefly wondered how difficult it was going to be to coax the warrior to bathe before he slept into oblivion for the next couple of days. The priestess knew full well it would be a monumental task but one the workers, herself and anyone who passed within a stone’s throw of his quarters would greatly appreciate. Shaking her head, she settled into an easy lope, pacing the dwarf, and ran into the night.
