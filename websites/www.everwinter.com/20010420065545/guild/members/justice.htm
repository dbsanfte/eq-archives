History: Like the other clans of Winterborne, Justice hails back to the Dragon Isles, specifically a family of benevolent gold dragons making their home in the City of Law, on the great Exaurdon Isle. For many centuries, Justice was led by Magic Lawgiver. When she stepped down, Johan Graye, the platinum paladin, took control of Clan Justice. The Grayes disappeared in the wake of the Orb Winter and are believed to be lost, but House Winterborne continues their tradition by naming this clan in their memory. The Clan has also been strongly influenced by Clan Pinnacle and Talisman Silvermane. Though Talisman does not travel Norrath, his student Keldore Silverwand does, and has been a cornerstone of what the revived Clan Justice stands for.

Dogma: I am glad to see you are drawn to our Clan. We of Clan Justice welcome all good beings. Good? A vague word for many of the less educated, but a respect for life is our cardinal goal. It's that simple. Any lasting and just society must be founded upon this principle. To join our Clan is to fight darkness with light. We are the legend behind the knighthoods of many lands. Chivalry stems from our code. Many possess the literacy to read of our codex, but few possess the fortitude to practice it.

We stand alone when we must, but we do not seek to become a martyr. Such aspirations are the sign of unfounded pride and a desire for fame, not what is right.

Unless a burst of pure brightness is the only choice that will route the enveloping night, we stay with our Clan and charges. Our lives are more important than that of our base foes, but no life is more important than that of our charges. Without our word, accomplishments are simply self-serving. Rally in the righteousness. Those of the light can join together, yet we do not lose our individual hue. The more woe we best, the more weal will welcome the innocent.

Pride is not for us. Our clan metal—silver—is the metal of purity, a mirror ready to reflect any flaw back at those who gaze upon it. Those who are of the light will strive to overcome these shortcomings. Those who cannot resist the darkness will strike out against us, blaming the faults on the Mirror, unable to see those flaws in themselves. So our Clan earns the fury-born of envy from all who flock to the banner of evil. Pity them and, when necessary, eliminate them, but you cannot stop evil by destroying evil people alone.

So you wish to follow the virtues we espouse? Honor your Clan? It is a steep hill to climb. Sacrifice is frequent. Compassion is difficult to hold dear when those of evil mien exploit any sign of tenderness, but to be relentless, uncompromising… this does not serve our goals. We would be little more than the mercenary Warclaw or the ruthless Blackmoon.

We live in a world of great potential, but there are those that do not value life. To them, be you a good wife, a child, or a brave warrior matters naught. We can teach you how to wield your blade, to cast magic many have forgotten… to mend flesh and knit bone. In time, you will learn the cost of sacrifice… and the liberation the virtues bring. There is no freedom that can rival knowing your actions are just.

If you wish to be a member of the Clan Justice, your path will be fraught with peril and temptation. Follow the light even when the Stygian night obscures it. If you falter, you will fall. If you fall, strive to fly again. You have the look of nobility about you, a gleam in your eye. Perhaps you will become the next legend among our kind.

Code of Conduct:

  • Never betray anyone, not even your enemies. Converse with those of the darkness only when the greater good will be served, and even then be wary of the corruption and hate they foster.

  • Always tell the truth. The truth is always better than a lie.

  • Protect everyone. Those whom go against the light should be destroyed.

  • Law is a tool to preserve order and weal. The light flourishes in a lawful society. Follow the laws of the land when they are just, and shun the lands in which they are not. You are the role-model for change in such places. Practice temperance.

  • Take prisoners. There is always hope, but do not risk those who rely upon you needlessly. Those who live by the sword will die by it when they are blinded by wickedness.

  • Never strike an opponent in the back or attack a defenseless opponent. There is no honor in it.
