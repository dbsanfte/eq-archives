I am Amorsaylnn Frost and as I put this quill to parchment, I am a young magician having just reached by 21st season. My father is Makaz, a retired enchanter and animation, who has sponsored my membership with the Winterborne, to serve in his place in an effort to unravel the mysteries facing this group.

I was born and raised in Neriak, a beautiful if dangerous city especially for one such as I. While my father is of the appropriate heritage, my mother was not. Although most of the surface world mistakes me for a pure dark elf, my intense green eyes being my only obvious odd trait to the unfamiliar eye, to another dark elf it is often evident that my mother was human.

I spent my younger days within Neriak, having only recently seen the surface world with my own eyes. My father, a merchant and Enchanter named Makaz, provided the necessities of survival while he pursued his own studies and adventures, his priorities of profit and power occupying nearly all his waking moments. My younger days were spent under the tutelage of skilled sorcerers in Neriak; an honor that my father purchased with platinum as my heritage would normally not afford me such a privilege. Most of my days were spent in the silent camaraderie of various elementals, which fostered my interest in magic and summoning. Later I worked in The Blind Fish, my father's bar in Neriak, and learned many practical skills from watching the common fights and treachery that occurred routinely.

Several months ago, Shivrehel, a member of the Winterborne, approached my father with a fanciful story of ancient magic and animations. He explained that he sought out these animations, magical "golems" of such complexity and ability that they are almost indistinguishable from the living, except for a few select methods of detection. My father was not easily convinced, but in time both he and I came to believe Shivrehel's story and the fact the truth: that my father is an animation. In addition, Shivrehel found that I was something of a puzzle, having some but not of the animation traits: essentially a mixture of the magical and mundane, the offspring of a hybrid union of the magical and natural.

At the time of this revelation, Makaz suspended his business dealings for a time in order to work with Shivrehel in researching the mystery of the animations. I began to travel outside of Neriak for the first time, quickly gaining an appetite for greater knowledge of the outside world as well as increasing my own skills as a magician. I saw other races without the chains or branding indicating their status as slaves, and experienced the prejudices that are harbored by most against my kind. I began to travel with other Winterborne, fortunately discovering that not all of the surface folks share those traditional views of my people. I have also begun to learn the harsh lessons of my own limitations, including my own lack of control over my temper and emotions, perhaps a trait I carry from my father.

A few days ago, my father was forced to leave Neriak, banished as the end result of attacks against him by those enemies he had made over the years. The lurid details of his punishment are not worth putting to paper here, but suffice to say he is no longer welcome there. His body has begun to betray him, not from age but the accumulation of injuries and a difficult youth, but he has found welcome refugee on the west coast, in Qeynos. He and I have both adopted that city as our home, and Makaz has begun a search for my mother on my behalf, while I work with the Winterborne on his behalf. In the meantime, I look forward to further exploring the wonders of Norrath, as it is a diverse and wondrous land. I am trying to overcome my agoraphobia, my discomfort of the open sky that is a common aliment of those from Neriak who live on the surface. Most importantly, I am eager to meet with others of the Winterborne and work toward our common goals.

Amorsaylnn Frost
Dark Elf Magician 21st Level
