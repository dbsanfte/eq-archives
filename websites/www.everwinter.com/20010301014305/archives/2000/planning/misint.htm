Misinterpreted Dreams

Posted by Mica on December 5, 1999:

Mica woke up disoriented, cold, hungry and vaguely uneasy. He remembered running into Qeynos after thinking he had mastered the tiger strike SiFu LuSun had shown him several orders ago. He had jogged up the ramp to the guild house. Always on the move and always impatient, the young monk burst through the door and found SiHing Phin. The elder deemed Young Mica worthy to continue on and had given the monk a blocking drill to practice and use. Mica spent time going through the drill and memorizing each move until he could do it in his sleep. He wanted badly to run back to the planes and put this technique to actual application in hopes of mastering it and moving onto the next. Mica had always been first and best in his training.

Unfortunately there were errands he had to run in town and it made no sense to run back to the planes loaded down and heavy with coin and odd weapons he had picked up. He gritted his teeth at the delays and finally realized that it was too late to run back. He grudgingly walked back to his room at the Lion's Mane Inn and lay down on the bed. He would rest for just a moment to gather energy for the run back. Somehow he had fallen asleep.

Now he woke up somewhere strange in a land covered in snow and ice. The wind howled through his thin silk clothes and his feet crunched through the thin ice cap of the tundra snow. He shivered as the heat was ripped away from his body to be dispersed uncaringly on the long flat horizon. Mica picked a direction and started walking. Anywhere else was better than standing in the middle of nowhere.

After a time the featureless tundra became a blur in his mind. He was shaking so violently that his steps were starting to become erratic and his mind slipped over visions and conversations of his past in no particular order. He could no longer feel his feet as his frozen sandles picked themselves up endlessly one after another and moved him onward.

Something tugged at his mind and he became aware of the fact that he had stopped . Before him the snow began to ripple and the monk stared at it in fascinated stupification. The unease he had first felt grew and a part of his mind analyzed it. The rippling snow exploded in a draconian image and the unease intensified. He recognized it as dragon fear as his conditioning kicked in. The Ancient had taught him to control the human desire to cower from these magnificent beasts. The Ancient had also taught Mica to revere them above all else and the frozen knight reflexively bent to one knee to offer homage to the creature.

**This is what you have offer me?**

The voice that filled his mind echoed with scorn and disgust. He felt the repulsion of the creature who invaded his thoughts. It was looking for something and Mica cringed as the presence rifled through his experiences as if they were a stack of child's drawings and discarded them as inconsequential and useless. Mica was ashamed. He was nothing compared to this mighty creature.

**You disgust me, child of Azure. Your pathetic clinging and groveling at my feet sickens me. Mighty knight.** A derisive snort of laughter roared through his thoughts. **Your whole order is a waste of time. What do you possibly think you can do for the likes of me?** An expectant pause filled Mica's head. The creature was still in his mind but waiting.

Mica was confused. He had tried to follow the creature's thoughts as they plowed through his memories but all he wound up doing was trailing behind the beast as it tore through his head. Now there was a calm in the draconic storm and Mica pulled back into his own mind. The young monk sought the peace and quiet of his inner core in hopes of regaining his balance.

**There is nowhere you can hide.** The presence followed his thoughts, moving through Mica's weak mental shields as if they were shadows thrown across a pathway.

"This is not the way it works-" He threw the thought at the beast. Laughter bellowed in his mind as the dragon lowered it's head to Mica's level. The huge eyes glinted iridescently at the monk and arctic cold breath puffed his silk tunic flat against his chest.

**And who says so?**

The Ancient had taught Mica all there was to know about the order of Azure. Dragons were supposed to be noble and the sons of Azure were to be their protectors in their time of trouble. So it was envisioned, so it was meant to be.

Again scornful laughter interrupted his thoughts. **You misguided idiot. Do you really think my children can not handle something as trivial as a small setback in power? Do you even have any idea how long my children have been overcoming adversity? **

The visions though, the Ancient had taught him about-

**Azure was a fool prone to flights of fancy. Sadly you are a product of his delusions, that is all.**

That wasn't right. Azure was wise and chose to train the Sons for the day when his brethen would need their help.

**Your purpose has no foundation. A dragon needs no help from the likes of you to overcome such a trivial matter of a home destroyed and some magics lost.**

But they had trained for generations against-

**Against nothing. You never had a time, you never will.**

Mica blinked. He had been told as far back as he could remember about Azure and the visions. He couldn't shake the conviction that there was truth in the words.

**So like the infant races to cling to their uninformed ideals.** The beast narrowed it's eyes and lifted it's frost adorned head up off of the tundra. The snow under the massive jaws had been crushed flat in a wagon sized divot but not melted. The chill of the beast wrapped it's icy fingers around Mica's heart even as the last puff of breath whipped his wild hair about his head. He felt compelled to follow the beast's gaze as it drew back to it's full height and towered over the small human. Years of conditioning ignored the beasts words and comforted the knight against the thoughts the dragon provoked in his mind.

The beast did not know what it spoke of. It could not know because it was not a son of Azure. Mica stopped as the automatic response filled his head. It had become like blocking a blow with his shield, so often he had been told how he and the others were different. Now he no longer thought about the incoming attack he just moved his shield into place and the blow glanced off of it. It never occurred to him to look beyond and question what was thrown at him. Now he did.

The small human stood ankle deep in the biting snow with his head craned uncomfortably back while his eyes remained locked in the misty swirl of the giant beast's gaze. The empty tundra tugged at his body, pulling at the cured silk cape draped around his shoulders. The fabric offered him no protection as it snapped and danced to the soulless whine of the northern winds. They whispered to him a haunting tune that mocked his teachings and belittled the foundation of his being. He questioned his purpose.

His order, trained for generations in the spine of the mountains, believed in…a cause. It was said the noble Sapphire turned his back on his kin to one day save them. The brotherhood was his legacy, they were the tool of the winged one's salvation. Or so he had been taught. So had generations before been taught. So had generations before trained, taught and died believing. If the beast was right and the dragons did not need the brotherhood then what was there to fight for? What was his purpose? Mica heard the whisper of the Ancient in the bitter wind: Stand tall and remember the teachings.

"This is a test. You seek to undermine my faith and shake me from my cause-"

**SILENCE!**

Mica dropped to his knees and cradled his head as the shout exploded through his mind. The world went silent as he stared unseeing into the dazzling snow. Vaguely he was aware of moisture rolling down his face drop by drop as splashes of crimson added color to the tundra snow. He put a hand to his face.

**Faith is a tool for warping the weak of mind.**

Mica pulled his hand away. The palm was slick with blood. He could feel the warm trickle running down his neck as he sat back on his heels. He noticed that his thoughts were still now and wondered at the calm in his mind. He wondered if it had anything to do with the growing stain on the snow. He wondered if he were dying. He wondered if anyone would really notice the absence of one young knight. He wondered why the knight was a knight at all. He wondered what he believed in. He wondered if it was anything different than what he had been told he believed in.

He closed his eyes. Why did he exist at all? Why would something as powerful and noble as a dragon need a human such as himself? It was like training a mouse to be the salvation of his own race. He saw that now; the flaw in the brotherhood. They had gone on for generation after generation; so sure that the next one was the cumulation of all their efforts, going on another cycle when it wasn't. An entire community of trained mice waiting for their chance at a delusioned glory. He had never stopped to question why, but the question had always been there buried under years of training and lessons. It was a question who's answer left him as empty as the frozen waste he knelt on. Without the cause he had no meaning. He opened his eyes to the vast unending horizon and felt the emptiness wrap itself around his heart. It offered mocking comfort for his fallen messiah.

Mica breathed in a long silent breath and then puffed it out into the frozen air. He was a fighter. Stripped of his brotherhood, equipment and ideals he was still a fighter. It was the one constant that he could count on. It was the one thing he was good at, it was the one thing he would do. That would have to be his meaning and purpose now that the beast in front of him had yanked the truth from his mind and laid it bare for him to discover. He hated the creature for its cruelty.

Instantly he felt ashamed as his programmed reverence for the creatures of scale balked at his thoughts. He savagely pushed aside his shame and let the anger take seed. He glared up into the eyes of the beast. He would know this creature's name so that when the day came where he had grown strong enough, he could shout the creature's legacy mockingly to the winds as he stood atop it's severed head. The tundra winds would take his words and spread them over the planes to die in echoing whispers alongside the vestiges of his faith. It gave him a purpose and something to look forward to.

The creature looked back down at him and the young knight felt a shift in it's thoughts. It was like a mental nod as if something it had been waiting for had come to pass. Before he could even start to puzzle the shift out a myriad of images jumbled through his thoughts. He saw a smoke red horizon. The crumbling ruins of a city wall. The burnt out remains of a building. A battlefield strewn with mangled corpses. A low angry sun glaring across a beach of bones. The twisted corpse of a violet eyed drow. The lifeless face of his arctic consort. Faces of those he knew lying sightless as the sun sank into the earth. In the last dusky glow he noticed the first movement he had seen in all these visions. A solitary earth pet moved slowly back and forth over the body of it's master. Her clear brown eyes looked into his own for a moment before the spark of her being wavered and died. The pet rippled once and soundlessly disappeared. The moon never rose.

Mica reeled from the onslaught. He didn't understand. He tried to sort out the visions and isolate them into some sort of order but there were too many too fast.

**That was Azure's dream. What did you see?**

Mica frowned. He remembered vividly some of the vignettes while others were a blur. He shook his head. Death, destruction but most of all it was the overwhelming sense of futileness that griped the man's mind. He was disturbed by the lack of sense in all of it.

**This is the reason for your existence.**

A frown creased the young man's mind. These visions had lead to the creation of the brotherhood and the beguiling of generations of his people but no where did he see any clue as to how the brotherhood could even begin to prevent such a catastrophe. He wondered how Azure had come to the conclusion he had.

**My children have their flaws too. Azure recognized some of the bodies as his own brethren and concluded that it was a premonition to the downfall of his kind. Typical of my children he assumed the vision was meant for him and him alone. He decided to create a legion of fighters that were trained well enough to protect his race from this fate. He missed the point.**

Mica quirked his head to one side. If the point wasn't what Azure had thought then what was the point? He went through the visions he could remember detail by gruesome detail until he saw something he had missed. In the vision of the ruined wall was a corner of a shield. It was green and yellow with a city emblazoned on it. The standard of Qeynos.

**You are beginning to understand. These are the last moments of this land. Few believe what will come to pass. Fewer understand how it will happen. I alone believe it can be stopped. I call you to stand against the force that will see this world ruined.**

Mica furrowed his eyebrows. Wasn't this creature asking for him to place faith in what it said? He hesitated.

**Not faith. Conviction. Look around and tell me you can not already see the signs.**

Mica thought about the new land he had come to. There was a strange tension that one noticed at first but then quickly got used to. Races walked cat's feet about each other as they went on their business. Factions formed, fought and dispersed as the people of Norrath went through their lives, oblivious to the growing unease that crept across the land. The monk nodded. He understood in his heart that something was wrong. Not because this creature had told him, but because the sense of wrongness grated against his soul. He could feel it's black claws picking delicately at the fabric of the land as it unraveled it slowly bit by bit.

**Join me.**

Mica hesitated again. He was leery of placing his devotion in another cause so quickly after his first one had been stripped from him. He felt the presence in his mind do another one of those strange mental nods. The human felt that his hesitation was expected and even approved of. He wondered who this creature was.

**You have the answer to both questions. You will know what to do when the time comes.**

The dragon turned translucent and faded slowly away. Mica shivered as he was once again left alone on the open waste.

He sat bolt upright in bed, his heart pounding. The window showed the faint light of the setting moon and Mica realized he had slept longer than he had planned. He shook his head, the soft crackle of the merchant's distant fires a sharp report in his ears as the sounds of the night rushed into his room. After the silence of his dream the hushed murmur of the night's voice fairly screamed at him. He took a deep breath and swung his legs off of the bed.

"She comes to you in dreams!" it was whispered. The lady Vox, calling her chosen by the tendrils of dreaming had paid a visit in the ghosts of his sleeping. Mica sat a moment longer as a wry smile tugged at his mouth. He had the answer to his other question as well. He looked at the round moon as it dipped below the city walls. He would fight to keep that orb in the sky and this land from being torn asunder. He stood up and stretched, filling the basin with water to wash the last of the sleep from his eyes. He would need to practice what LuSun had taught him if he were to be any use to the lady. The monk splashed water onto his face and stopped as the bowl filled with a watery crimson. His skewing reflection stared back at him as the rivulets of dried blood mixed with the water. The arctic winds whispered in his mind.
