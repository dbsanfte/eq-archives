From the Journals of dr'Shivrehel Despana
(Twenty and Three Years before the Orb Winter)

It has been less than three days since I’ve returned to Toril. Aneste and Cade have my house in good order, despite my absence. They arranged a sizable purchase of Alurlyath from the nearby drow city of Rilauven, courting the favor of their Valsharess, Nedylene. I have a penchant for good wine, and the Temple is known for the best. They have likewise saw to my mundane needs… conversion of our coins, stocking of the pantries, a suitable wardrobe, and so forth. They’ve been diligent and that speaks highly of their devotion. It makes my exile so much more tolerable, though I miss my home… and I miss Vespre.

It is not right that I should be separated from my wife so soon after our wedding and yet how can I tolerate existence on Terra? It is a gray world, devoid of magic. I plan on splitting my time between Toril and my wife’s abode until I can solve our curse. Shar and Shard, so young, will not miss the ways of their home. Norrath is behind them, Terra ahead. When the time comes, they will welcome our estate on Toril and revel in the magic at our disposal, but I digress.

Since arriving, Aneste made known the appearance of others of my kind. It appears they were exiled here. I confess that I was surprised at the Council’s reaction. My sentence was rather barbaric, but predictable. I posed a serious threat to their way of life. On the other hand, cn’Olam and zu’Rianna were everything a member of the Council should be—heroic and self-sacrificing, if somewhat vain. Then again, there are few of my kind that aren’t arrogant. Given our gifts, can you blame us? That was rhetorical. Our splendor is obvious, even to the humans I am now surrounded by. Even those of the metallic bloodlines are more than a human could ever be…

And yet folly is not something we’ve evolved past.

White Leopard, the venerable mentor of Rianna, accompanied her. Others seem to have been exiled, though I know not why. ag’Saidar, companion to cn’Olam, is said to have voluntarily followed his friend. I have heard tell that cr’Melody has also traveled to this land. Was she, too, exiled? I had little to do with Clan Bloodtide, though they seemed like admirable allies. Who else might have come to Toril?

I shall make the time to meet my brethren and ascertain their intentions. Are they truly exiled or have they come seeking my head? If they have been exiled, what do they plan for themselves?

As I said, we have not matured beyond folly.

I have spoken to cn’Olam, the bronze from Clan Battlewing. I couldn’t help but note how frequently he punctuated his dislike of me by reaching to the pommel of his blade. I suppose he was banished because of me, though it was his own audacity to meddle in that which he did not understand—

Suffice it to say, our discussion was strained, though I learned much. It seems the Council was moving against another trend. A gold, very young and idealistic, believed he was touched by Io. He calls himself the "Great Wyrm", claiming to be an spokesman of Io’s Will. Naturally, Io’s will conflicted with that of the Council’s and he, along with several ardent followers, found themselves banished to Neverwinter. Olam and Rianna, at the request of White Leopard, have supported this mad drake’s labor to build another Aerie, another Council of Wyrms.

I know little of Toril, but I know dragons will not be welcomed with open arms. I have taken great cares to conceal my origin and nature, yet this youth would place me at risk by naming me a "fellow wyrm." Last night in the Red Mage’s Inn, I had to assure my drinking companions he was sadly mistaken, but it was embarrassing. He’ll be a blight on my reputation soon enough, to be sure… this mad man claiming to be related to the likes of me…

Still, I must gain the confidence of these other exiles. I need to know how many there are. The hatchling will be dealt with when I have assumed my rightful position as their leader. I can easily provide a vision more enticing than the bloated worm’s. I’ll have to think on this matter a bit. I’d like to preserve my reputation as a mercenary within the confines of Neverwinter, but having the assistance of fellow dragons could also be an asset.

Again, I am amazed. I began tracking membership among the new Council in the guild hall district. The organization is so poor I was easily able to slip into the position of scribe. Olam and Rianna, perhaps the only members who would readily recognize me, are often out, traveling the land. Wanderlust is a seductive mistress, but I’m resilient, defiant to such desires. I have too much to loose to succumb to the embrace of roving, reveling in my power among these lessers. My kind has a tendency to squander their gifts. Hoard treasure, spreading our wings, making men cower or bow in awe… this should not be our way. Power is our way and with this strength, the ability to enlighten the young races.

Power, however, can manifest in two distinct breeds. It can be the direct use of force, be it physical or magical, or the indirect use of knowledge, through the manipulation of events. There is a place and time for both forms. Now is the time for knowledge and subtle use of our gifts, not the bold declaration… "We are dragons; hear us roar!" Such can lead to incredulity, mocking, or enough fear or ego to elicit dragon-hunters. Too many of this young Council can not defend themselves.

The roaring has already attracted the attention of another House, known as ITB. Those "in the bond" are power-hungry, rash, and ruthless. They would have slave dragons, dragon heads in their dens, and the treasure my kind hoards. Despite their age, I must admit they are powerful adversaries. The chaotic Council has quickly showed their inability to deal with humans, their innate sense of greatness. Pride is fine, when justified. Here it has not been proven. The "great" wyrm runs to other guilds, crying for assistance against the predators of House ITB. I find myself embarrassed, disappointed, and… ready to defend the name dragon.

I see two courses of action must be taken. The first, fight and defeat a member of the House in single combat. I will not revel in the victory, but treat it as if I expect to win. I do, frankly. They are formidable, but so am I. Keep the weak of the Council sheltered and attack any exposed flank in the House with the strongest. Give them pause. A war becomes futile if the cost is too high, and the gain too little. They stand nothing to gain by our defeat. We are new-comers to this land, held in contempt or ignored by most. Our defeat would be expected and trivial. On the other hand, any success, however minor, against this House ITB would strain their reputation.

Second, I must eliminate the madness at the head of the Council. There are many within their ranks that have potential. Though the metallics have never been as strong as my family, they are still dragons and powerful in their way. Their moral center guides them into what can be weakness… but so to can they make allies among the young races, in a way a chromatic couldn’t or wouldn’t. Removing this "great" wyrm will leave room for a one with more rationale. I have seen several promising dragons… White Leopard is an obvious choice, though his student Rianna is charismatic and more passionate. Reklaive, a gold from Toril, could be a good choice as well.

I shall endeavor to face the humans of House ITB, at least one of them, and buy this Council a chance.

The fight was a silence-slavery match. The terms were simple. Should I win, ITB would leave the new quarters of the Council alone, ceasing their frequent harassment. Should I fail, I would declare my servitude to House ITB. I can tell you, I had no interest in being a servant. As fate would have it, I was victorious in the duel, though the battle was closer than I had hoped and my win could be called luck, not the clear-cut victory I had sought. I played the victory well, though. I won respect by not bragging. In return, the House honored the terms, and the Council has been quiet of late.

I have heard House ITB is constantly at war with self-declared demigods known as KAAOS. Perhaps my victory would prove ample target for the jeers of these demigods. I knew two, a woman known as Xylina, and a man known as War Knight. I had drank with them more than once, and Xylina and I had teamed up to hunt down a so-called ally of the young Council. Great Wyrm was quick to challenge my authority. After all, I broke an alliance. And yet, this was the excuse I had been waiting for. None of the Council members seemed sure of their leader, nor the alliance he had forged.

I met the Great Wyrm in the Arena of Triboar, after his many falls on the way through the Lost Hills. He was escorted by a formidable knight, the Earl of Daggerford, Johan Graye. He was courting cr’Melody of Bloodtide, to my amazement, but he was an honorable man. He encouraged the leader of the Council to fight honorable, to fight with heart and his steel. I could see the doubt in his eye. He could not help but see the disparity between the two of us.

Great Wyrm had followed the route of the paladin, serving the human deity known as Tyr, the Justice-Bringer, as well as Io. We honored the traditions of the Arena. Many priests of Tyr from the nearby temple were present to support their young champion. Faurual Blackhammer, Lord of Triboar, made an unexpected visit, as did my old companion Odinsun. I thought I could see Olam and Rianna in the crowd, though they remained neutral.

Next to the fluted plate and mail of the young lad, I seemed frail. As always, I was dressed in my royal purple cloak, breeches and doublet of black silk, platinum jewelry and Soul-Forge, my favored weapon, in hand. I ran my long blue fingers through my bone-white hair smiling. Dark elves were not uncommon, particularly in the area. My midnight blue skin was a bit odd, but the drow accepted it as an oddity.

I marveled at how young he was, how naïve next to me. Faluzure declares you should never underestimate an opponent. I did not, though any cause for planning was moot. The hero does not always win. In fact, it is rarely the case. My spells cut him down as he tried—pitiably--to fend off the kampfult of the Arena with an enchanted blade. In the end, he tried to poison me, drawing on a scroll beyond his power. I saw the Earl drop his face in disappointment and shame. Naturally, the spell rolled off of me. You can hide the scales, but they remain.

I summoned the power for a killing blow, touched him lightly on the his exposed hand as he struggled within the grasp of the tree-like creatures. I drew the last breath of life from his body. Priestly healing magic was powerful when reversed to harm. He learned, but too late. The clergy of Tyr tried to revive him, but the Gods were kind. They claimed the false dragon.

The Council returned to the Hall. There were decisions to be made in the next few days. I would steer them down the path of greatness.
