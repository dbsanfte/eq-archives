Hi Ho!

Posted by Biancha on December 28, 1999:

As usual, the swamp was alive, full of creatures bustling about their simple lives. Insects sang and bull frogs croaked, creating a cacophony that was soothing to Biancha. She was reminded of the alligator skin plaque that hung outside Ragnor’s shack, “Hom Swea Tom.” She’d trekked through the swamps and beyond a handful of times but this—this was home.

She waded through the tepid waters, her eyes deftly tracking a pair of fungusmen in the distance. She licked her colorless lips and decided their heads would make a tantalizing addition to the froglok stew Zimbittle was so famous for. Zimbittle was one of the best bashers in Grobb. Biancha admired her braun and delighted in sparring with her occasionally. She always paid dearly for it, bruised and battered the next day. But oh, it was great to hear Zimbittle’s words of praise when she survived a bout.

As she neared her prey, a runner ran to her with a message from Grobb. “Mama needs you quick. Go!” The troll messenger grunted at Biancha and shoved her.

Biancha shoved him back and grinned broadly. “Danks. I go now.”

The shack was dark inside. Biancha heard a whimper from behind the tattered rug that separated the sleeping area from the eating area. She found her mama, who happened to be one of the oldest witch doctors in Grobb, administering some putrid ointments to her da’s festering sores. If anyone could heal him, she could. He’d been suffering from a rare swamp malady for the past few months. But the last few days had been the worst. Biancha did her best to stay away. She couldn’t stand to see her father so vulnerable and in so much pain.

Her mother saw her standing in the doorway and grunted disapprovingly and waved her away. “Biancha wait out there.”

Biancha bowed her head and decided to wait outside the hut. The dank smells of the swamp were refreshing in comparison to the smell of death that hung in the air of the dingy shack. She sat down heavily on a worn stump and picked at the dirt with a stick while she waited for her mother. Finally, the wooden door creaked open and her mother hobbled out.

“It’s time fer Biancha to go. Spirits tell me. Listen up, girl. You make family proud. Spirits say some dragon in north has work fer you. You go. Meet these people and…” Biancha’s mother stopped speaking abruptly and whacked Biancha on the arm with her walking stick. “Is IMPORTANT! Listen up! You do good work, dis dragon promises cure fer your da. Git it? I packed grub already. You go now. Meet tiny man with swift feet at entrance to ogre land. He show you way.”

Biancha took one look at the dilapidated shack and grinned madly. “Yah, I go now!” She stood, kissed the wort on her mama’s chin, and grabbed the sack from her. The thought of leaving the swamp was scary, but definitely a better prospect than spending one more night in that shack.
