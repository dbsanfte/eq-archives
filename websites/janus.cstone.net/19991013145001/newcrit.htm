The Log Beast - by Rick

The Log Beast is a 60+ level invisible MOB who, like Queen Klicknik, only spawns in response to a certain event. This event varies, but it takes the form of a player telling his group "OK, just one more battle, then I'm logging off." Rather than attacking the party directly, the Log Beast increases the spawn rate for aggressive monsters in the immediate area, plus any aggressive monsters already in the zone have their paths changed so that they intersect the location of the party. If the party starts to gain the upper hand against the hordes, the Log Beast will then spawn a Griffon or Hill Giant to assure the party's demise.

At the same time, the Log Beast will cast Fear on all guards in the zone, and the chances of spells fizzling or being interrupted increases ten-fold. As the party approaches a zone, the chances of being stunned increases proportionally until it reaches 100% two steps from the zone border.

Unlike other aggressive monsters, the Log Beast does not "reset" after party death. Rather, it remains in the area, focused on the party for a period of no less than an hour, typically it is two hours or more. As the party attempts to loot their bodies, the monsters again descend and cause the party to now loot TWO bodies. This pattern continues until a party member either loses a level or is reduced to exhausted tears over the injustice of it all.

Rumor has it that if you manage to kill the Log Beast, you gain the Staff of Frustration, which casts a de-buff that causes attacks to miss, magic resistance to fail, and armor to be completely useless. The sound this spell makes is Brad McQuaid's maniacal laughter.

The Sadist

(Benares). Yes, that's right! Verant has pulled out all the stops with the new Sadist monster! Sadists are extremely high level and appear to be any class and race, usually powerful enough to take on Nagafen the Dragon one-on-one, so it is only natural that their favorite prey is anything at least 20 to 50 levels below them. They tend to spawn in areas where groups of players have been waiting for hours at to get that "special something", be it a weapon or piece of armor, usually off a named NPC. The Sadist monster is unique in that it attacks PCs not DIRECTLY, but rather casually meanders by and obliterates whatever NPC they were struggling to get to in a single shot, loots the corpse for the item that meant something to the PCs but means little more than a fancy door-stop to the Sadist, and then walks away. Players can tell of incoming Sadist monsters by the sounds of "Goddammit!", "Lousy KS bastard!", and "Wow, look at all the stuff this guy's got!". When not hunting named or important prey, Sadists can be found in front of Freeport casting Ice Comet on Deathfist Pawns or near the entrance to Qeynos using Firey Avenger on rats. Players are warned not to bother or talk to these fearsome beasts, as some can be so tempermental that they'll follow the PC around for hours after that, demonstrating the power of the latest lvl 49 earth elemental they can conjure up on anything the player might need for experience or sustenance, be it a skeleton, sand giant or merchant. It is rumored that Sadists are controlled by gods, large and smelly ones that haven't seen the light of day in weeks and are destined to never know the touch of a woman. Walk with trepedation, young adventurer!

The UO monsters

The UO monsters are the most annoying opponents a Norrathian could ever face. They claim to serve some sort of Dark Lord or demonic force which is commonly referred to as OSI. Scholars throughout Norrath have been baffled by these strange creatures. All UO monsters are humans, and they all seem to speak a strange dialect which involves the use of a multitude of abbreviations. Examples include BRB, AFK, LOL, ROTFL, FUK, and other things. Although information is limited, several of the most outstanding scholars and adventurers have been able to capture and interrogate these peculiar beings. They claim to have come from a land far across the sea, called UO, hence their name. Most of them seem to be proficient swordsmen, archers, and mages. Some believe that each of them has also mastered at least one trade skill. All of them carry around halberds, have bone armor (certain ties between them and the ogres of the Karana plains have been suggested,) and know strange magics. They have a universal battle cry they yell before attacking someone: "Crop Pore." What this means in their language is unclear. They seem to divide themselves into three main groups, or "Castes."

The first is known as the UO PK. They attack indiscriminately, usually attacking newbies. They always attack in large groups, and teleport away whenever the odds turn against them. The second group, which is sometimes referred to as a subgroup of the first, is known as the "UO Noto PK." These limit themselves to attacking people who may have less than perfect reputations, such as rogues, necromancers, dark elves, ogres, and trolls. Both of these groups rarely talk, and when they do is is usually some strange expression, such as "Kel Ort Port" or something similar.

The third group is sometimes considered the "royalty" of the UO monsters. These are known as the UO K3WL D3DZ. These, while lower in number than the other two groups, are the most dangerous. They seem to weild uncanny abilites, an otherworldly magic they call "Xploits" or "bugs." They seem to have a particular grudge against "roll players." What exaclty defines a roll player is unknown, but the K3WL D3DZ seem to be able to identify them. K3WL D3DZ always attack in extremely large hordes, backed by PKs and Noto PKs. All three groups prefer to hunt near the entrances of dungeons, particularly those which are visited by less skillful adventurers. They do have one weakness, however: they sometimes fight amongst themselves. Why this is few can tell, but apparently they are so bloodthirsty that they must keep killing to survive. A scholar who captured a UO PK and locked him in a controlled environment saw that without anything to kill, the pathetic being disappeared into thin air, cursing OSI! No one knows where these strange creatures came from, but we MUST take up arms against this new threat to Norrath, lest they overtake it like they overtook their homeland, UO!

The Lag Creatures - by Brendan G.

Lag Creatures:

Ranging from lvl 1 to fifty, just so newbies can attack hard ones by accident, these creatures will cause astounding lag to anyone inside their sight range. Here are the types:

Decaying Lag Skeleton: This monster uses AOL and often seems to follow you around, asking pointless questions about easy-to-figure-out things, keeping you near. Despite its great lag, it seems to not be affected, only causing you the pain. Its attacks are, of course, pitiful though, but you never get to see the fight happen.

Lag Zombie: A MOB running EQ on a slow computer at peak hours, no matter when you encounter it, causes greater lag then the skeleton, and is around lvl 4-10. It hits for around 10-20 dmg, and again, you don't get to see the battle happen, so no spells.

Lag Goblin: a little laggier then the zombies, theese are lvl 10-25 black goblins that can cast shaman and wizard spells, meaning that by the time you realize the lag is gone you'll either be rotted and dead, or see the goblin running. The new dungeon for these creatures is "The Cave Of RunnyToe, sister dungeon to RunnyEye" with a lvl 40 king which could cast shaman spells, named John Murthough (a friend of mine that lags like hell with AOL and a slow computer).

Orc Lagmen: Same power as the goblins, but on Faydwer, with a dungeon that replaces Lesser Faydark (hey, there wasn't anything interesting there anyways, lol)

Lag Giants: lvl 40-45 giants around the Karanas. and Commonlands/Ro, which are to replace hill/sand/ice giants, because the lag giants have a cooler graphic anyways. They hit for around 50-200 dmg, and you must melee them, because of the lag they cause, you can't cast spells.

Lag Daemons and Greater Lag Daemons: lvl 45/50 creatures that look cool, and cause great lag to anyone in the zone. They are in any place people under lvl 30 would want to hunt, because we at Verant feel that lower lvls need to learn how to survive outside. They hit for around 200 dmg, and can cast all wizard spells forever. They take a long time to kill, and kill ohers fast.  Like any creature for high lvls, there will be almost no rewards for killing these but a rusty weapon, if they're carrying them, and maybe daemon wings, which look like bat wings, but are part of a quest we're making "soon", but sell for nothing, and weight 50 stones each. Daemons can also fly.

Da big lag PiMp: a large black dragon, who came over from UO after being banned, lvl 70. Spawns in a random newbie zone every 5 minutes, until they're all filled. It will take multiple lvl 50 parties top kill them, so newbies will just have to watch out while hunting now. It will help them learn how to survive. This monster can also cross zone barriers, so don't expect to escape. Like the daemons, this monster causes lag to all people in the zone. it has claws that do 500+ melee dmg, and a AE fireball that does around 200 dmg.
