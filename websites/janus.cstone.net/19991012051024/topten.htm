Top ten spells that never made it past beta  - Benares of Povar

1.) Summon : Solusek Ro -- originally a cleric's spell, this gave the ability to summon the evil god Sulosek Ro to fight for you. While the evil races enjoyed use of this spell, seeing as how any good races attempting to use this spell were immediately stepped on and eaten by the angry god, Verant felt this put too much power in the hands of certain players (especially since the spell was available at lvl 16).

2.) The entire digestion line of spells (Constipation, Diarrhea, Abdominal Discomfort, etc...) -- shamans across Norrath threw a small fit when this spell line was removed and replaced with disease spells instead. While the disease spells such as Sicken had many of the same penalties upon the target as the original versions, they lacked the amusing effect of making the target stumble around in agony with their hands between their legs while biting their lower lip.

3.) Summon : Money -- this spell, available to the enchanter class, granted the caster the ability to summon platinum pieces. Although removed only a day after first being tested, those few who were able to get the spell from vendors are currently making a small fortune via Ebay.

4.) Illusion : GM -- casting this spell enabled the enchanter using it to have the text over their name change to an aqua-green color and added the tag "*Senior Guide*" to their name. Due to intense abuse of this ability, Verant removed it after a number of newbies were informed that Castle Mistmoore and Lower Guk were being renovated to accomodate levels 2-5.

5.) Cataclysm -- this spell was created in Everquest's infancy as the programmers used Ultima as a reference for spell development. In the original Ultima series, Cataclysm would kill everything on the planet that was native to it, which included everyone except the player and Lord British. Needless to say many beta-testers were furious when thefirst lvl 20 wizard found this spell and decided to try it out.

6.) Evacuate : Nagafen's Lair -- This was intended as a high-level escape spell, but due to the fact no one at the time was high enough level to effectively go to the red dragon's lair as a means of escaping from a greater threat this spell was tossed on the sidelines; many wonder if this will become available again after the expansion is released.

7.) Spirit of the Beggar -- casting this spell on someone gave them incredible begging powers, enabling them to stand around in a set spot for days on end holding out their hands and asking for coppers. This, as well its upgrade spell Spirit of the Madman (which resulted in many newbies casting this on themselves to be killed for their water), were scrapped due to lack of demand.

8.) Tank of Wrath -- this spell was eventually trimmed down to Hammer of Wrath after a group of paladins and clerics used this spell to slaughter all the inhabitants of Neriak.

9.) Bard's Song : Busta Rhymes -- while many people enjoyed this spell (which gave a combined effect of Haste and Charm), the requirement of this song was to type in rythmic lyrics really fast on the keyboard to keep the spell going. Unfortunately, this resulted in Verant spending a fortune in replacing player keyboards after many letter keys broke and simply burnt out from over-working and the spell was dropped.

10.) Alter Plane : New Jersey -- one of the wizard's many transportation spells, this teleported a group to a mystical dimension of unbreathable air and raw sewage. Aside from the fact that anyone with the language filter couldn't talk with the locals, many players, even seasoned beta-testers, were too intimdated to use this spell more than once. Toning down the horrors of the area, Verant restructured this dimension into two smaller, less threatening dimensions, which are now accessible via Alter Plane : Hate and Alter Plane : Fear.
