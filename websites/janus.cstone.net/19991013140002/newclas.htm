The Manager by Zadkiel Hopeseeker, Level 22 Monk, GM of <<The Doom Guild>, Xegony.

This class can be of any evil race, and his stats are all at 1, except Charisma which is at 300.

Skills: Beg (level 1), Avoid Work (level 1), Delegation (level 3), Smarm (level 5), Persuade (level 10), Charm (level 15)

The Manager gains experience by getting other people to do his work for him. If a Manager completes a quest or kills a monster himself, he actually LOSES experience, however every time he persuades another player to do the job for him, he gains double experience and all the positive faction adjustments, while the player who did the job for him receives all the negative ones.

If a Manager wants an item, he must send player after player to retrieve it for him, and the more that die in the process the better. The Manager must never show any remorse at the players deaths, or help
them retrieve their corpses in any way. Once the Mob holding the item is slain, the Manager is automatically teleported to its side, and is the only person able to loot the corpse.

If the Manager buys an item, as long as he gets a receipt for it, he is refunded the money when he takes the receipt to the 'expenses department', a monster filled dungeon in the 'Plain of Hate'

At level 20, the Manager undergoes a change of class, and becomes a 'Director'.

Skills: Sleep (level 20), False Emotion (level 25), Seduce Secretary (level 30), Interview (level 35), Intimidation (level 40)

The Director automatically receives 100pp a day in his bank account, and has to do nothing to earn it.

The Director is unable to move from his home (unless summoned by a CEO, see later), and must do all his work from there. He is only capable of speaking to Managers, who must immediately organise other players to do what the Director asked him. The same limitation on the Manager of not being able to do the job himself still applies, or he loses a level. If the Manager fails to get other players to do the task the Director set him, no matter how difficult it may be, then the Manager is automatically deleted.

There is no way for a Director to lose experience. Once he reaches this level, only greater gains are possible.

<bold>At level 50, the Director becomes a Chief Executive Officer, or CEO.

Skills: Sack (level 50), Create Board Meeting (level 50)

The CEO instantly turns invisible permanently, has every player on the server added to his 'ignore' list, and gets 1000pp a day in his bank account. A CEO has the added ability of being able to announce a 'Board Meeting' to which every Director is automatically summoned. They then talk in a strange language that no other class is able to understand for several hours, and then the Directors go home and issue particularly ridiculous things for all the Managers to do.

The CEO gains the 'Sack' Ability, with which he can instantly delete any player on the server.

 

The Postman   by Ativannus

This fighter/spellcaster hybrid class is available to all races.  High attribute scores in strength and endurance are essential, given the huge encumbrance factor this class faces.

A Postman's sacred duty is to deliver important items throughout the far-flung lands of Norrath.  Whether it is icy glacier, searing desert, or steamy jungle, the Postman always gets his package to the intended destination.  His task demands the full use of the following special skills:

Speed Walk:  Posties are able to travel great distances at an accelerated speed.

Dodge Canine:  They have an innate ability to dodge attacking wolves, coyotes, and other canine animals.  If dodging fails, then they can resort to....

Pepper Spray:  A jet of noxious liquid is conjured.  The target is overcome with burning eyes and choking.  If all else fails, then they can.....

Go Postal:  The character goes into a berzerker rage, with +30 bonuses to strength, agility, endurance, and dexterity.  Unfortunately, intelligence drops by 60 points.  Posties are liable to attack random targets, and seem to have a tendency to choose taverns and other public places to use this skill.

Riposte:  This skill is commonly referred to as Return to Sender.

The Beggar - by Nuramis

(original screenshot provided by Venin)

They are masters of disguise, able to pretend to be any of the other classes, and are not restricted to any one race. However, they do have 1 class trait that allows a knowledgable person to be able to spot them, regardless of diguise: Beggars can only speak in shouts, and can keep shouting for extended periods of time.

They are most often found prowling the gates to the more populated cities of Norrath. To stay clear of them, citizens are encouraged to move quickly and quietly, not making eye contact with anyone else in the vicinity of the city gates.

However, should one be forced to confront a beggar, deception is by far the best weapon: They are easily distracted by shiny objects. A common tactic is to throw a flat stone painted like money away from you. The beggar, believing the stone to be real money, will quickly run off after it, leaving you free to go your way.
