Ranger Strategies


Kite, Fight and Win

Requires:
  • Snare (Lvl 9)
  • Archery skill and, of course, a bow with some arrows. :)
  • Any Ranger DD/DoT Spell (Flame Lick at the very least)
Recommened:
  • Spirit of the Wolf (lvl 39.. Or get a buddy to cast it on you)
  • A Backup Plan :)

Hey, druids can do it, why can't we? In some senses the ranger is better equipped for trying out this technique out in the open areas of norrath. First of all we have some pretty decent DD and DoT spells to open up with. Of course, since we typically don't have extremely large pools of mana to mindlessly nuke mobs we're gonna have to rely on other methods of attack like archery or melee. This is how it works.

Pick a Mob, ideally yellow, black (white) or blue. If you select a red your gambling with the xp you've already earned. I personally like yellows for the sheer challenge.

Snare it. If it doesn't hold, engage in melee for awhile, and attempt to snare it just after each attack round.

**(You can choose to use the "Cold Feet" strategy if things aren't looking too favorable)

Once you have the monster snared you have two options:

  • Archery:

    Depending on your bow's range, you may find yourself having to stay pretty close to the monster while picking it off with arrows. Make sure you have your range attack hotkeyed! Run backwards and start shooting arrows at the mob. Ideally, you should give yourself enough space to resnare the monster. If not, get ready to engage it in melee for awhile while you attempt to resnare.

    This is also a very effective method of building up the archery skill. With practice and persistence you can begin to get a reliable amount of damage from your bow. Like most things, there is a downside. It can get expensive depending on the type of arrows you use. Personally, I like to use the cheap arrows since I have other things I need to buy. But if you have serious money to burn, you might want to kite using archery only. Hey, you never know :).

  • Magic:

    Another nice technique. You can choose to use DoTs or DD spells. DoTs were the favorite of druids long ago. But DoTs have been reprogrammed to do roughly two-thirds of the full damage amount if you flee from a monster (or kite it). Because of this, DD is usually more favorable because it does the damage right away at a better mana cost. There are wrong ways to DD Kite and right ways. Let me put it this way: is it worth to expend a full bar of mana to solo a blue? I don't think so. A good idea is to spend about half your mana on the DD spell of your choice and just finish him off with archery or melee.

Once the battle is over, heal yourself up. Rest and med, and then do it again if you wish. I don't recommend kiting as a main source of xp. I find alot of avid kiters need to be reminded that there are groups that need their skills :).

Submitted By: Baldemar

Cold Feet

Requires:
  • Snare
  • At least 40% Health
Recommended:
  • SoW (You may not have to read this after all :))

Let's face it, things will get ugly at some time or another. But the difference between a dead and alive player (Other than the OBVIOUS) is their strategies in getting away with monsters.

Snare is your friend!

The moment you think your in big trouble, cast snare on your foe. Make sure that you time the castings right. If you have SoW, run away for a little while, then use snare. You just bought yourself a great deal of time. There are two things that you can do.

If your lost in a really open area (or even a closed area that has alot of empty space to cover) then run for awhile, not too long though. I usually run for about a minute or so, maybe a little longer. After you've run your butt off. Take a break, turn around, sit down and CAMP. All it takes is thirty seconds worth of distance between you and the monster. If the monster does happen to catch up to you in time, you'll be able to see him coming. This is a good tactic when your fresh out of options.

If you know where the zone border is, go there! 'Nuff said.

The Ranger Super Taunt (RST)

Requires:
  • Taunt (Max it out!)
  • Snare
  • Flame Lick

The Ranger Super Taunt (RST) will work nearly every time, even for a finger waggler that chain-nukes. The simple, but effective formula is Snare+Flame Lick+Taunt = RST. With the Ranger Dual Wielding abilities and those 2 spells and a taunt, you will always have a monster that wants to get friendly with you.

Submitted by: Gadas Amroth, Feir'Dal Ranger of the 30th Acorn

Stick and Shoot

Requires:
  • Snare
  • Bow and Arrows
  • Rooting Spell
  • Melee Weapon

First off. Snare the sucker. If your plans fail, and you get down to low health, he still won't catch you. Then, Root it. I personally like Ensnaring Roots for that because it does a little damage too, but it's highly resisted, but hey, when on level 15, it's all you got. After it's snared/rooted, stand back and shoot it with your bow, and while your Range Attack button (got it hotkeyed I hope) is down, hit it with a DD spell. Arrows are best for this though, because they don't take the mana you need for rooting. If you don't get root off enough, go ahead and run backward and shoot it with your bow until you get bored of that, then just stand there and whack away!

This strategy works best against green-yellow that don't have good Root resistance.

You can also use this strategy without Root for you guys still down on level 9-14. Just Snare it at the farthest range you possibly can cast Snare at, then shoot it while it runs (more like crawls) in. When it gets to you, finish it with melee. This works exceptionally well against the Aqua Goblins in the Ocean of Tears for example, because they'll stand around and try to shoot you too.
