Shadowknight Strategies


Healing Time

Requires:
  • A spell in the Lifetap series
  • Lots of mana
  • An area with plenty of lowbie (preferrably green) monsters
Recommened:
  • A Pet
  • Shadowstep, if things get crazy

You've been fighting for awhile, and now you need to heal up before continuing on. This strategy might make you think twice about NOT playing a SK :).

Go to any lowbie area of your choice. Preferably with monsters that are so weak that you really don't have to worry about being killed. Just start casting Lifetap/spike/draw on your foe and start watching your hp begin to rise. If the monster is moderately hard, send in your pet to fight it. Then just keep casting away while your pet draws its attention. Repeat this for as many times as you see fit, then go off and kill a REAL monster :).

Submitted by: Baldemar

Me and Mr. Bones

Required:
  • A Pet
  • A Ranged Attack
  • Jumping :)
Recommended:
  • Fast long distance bow
  • Long distance arrows
  • DoTs and debuffs
  • Friends that wait with your pet
  • SoW

Enter an outdoor zone and summon a pet. Go to a relatively safe place, for example, in Lavastorm I go west from the Nektulos pass just a few feet to the first tree.

Give the pet command "/pet guard here".

Now go out and pull. Remember a bow is less likely to bring an entire group of monsters than a spell is. Once you've got the mob's attention, run and jump back. By jumping you gain more time. If you are SoWed you don't have to jump, but it still helps. Save at least one bubble of stamina for jumping away if things go bad.

Once back where your pet is, turn and fire off spells and arrows until it closes to melee distance. Your pet will not attack monster until it damages you. So if you make it back without a scratch, it will not run up to greet the mob. If you are hit, it will.

To save on bones and mana, give your pet the command "/pet no taunt". This keeps the mob on you and off your fragile friend Mr. Bones. On the other hand if the mob does go for the pet, you can get in a quick Life Tap.

Submitted by: Grishnakia

Nothing to Fear by Fear Itself

Required:
  • Fear
  • Clinging Darkness
Recommended:
  • Good bow and arrows
  • Pet

Do this outdoors. Hit a mob with Clinging Darkness. Repeat until it sticks. Hit the Mob with Fear. Attack from the rear as it slowly ambles away. Send in the pet and practice archery if you like. Or just melee from behind.

Submitted by: Grishnakia
