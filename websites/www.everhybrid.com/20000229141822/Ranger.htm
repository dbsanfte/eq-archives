The Ranger

An Overview:

Hearty travelers, master outdoorsmen, these folk enjoy great freedom in the open spaces of Norrath. They are versatile and useful in almost any situation. Being gifted as both a warrior and a druid, most rangers find themselves with a mix of good magic and physical offense. This alone makes them welcome in any group. But the rangers are gifted in tracking their foes from their first level in the game, and that is what makes this class *REALLY* shine...


Warrior Attributes:
("stick the pointy thing into the squishy thing!")

When it comes to being a Warrior in battle, only the Warrior does it better! But the ranger does it good too, mind you! Unlike other hybrids the ranger enjoys both dual wield *and* double attack. This alone makes for some insane melee damage in those up-close-and-personal battles; but the ranger also gets to utilize the kick skill for some added damage... Nice :)

Secondary to those key features the ranger also gets the standard dodge and parry. Alot of people don't appreciate these skills but they still help out in a battle. Taunt is also at the rangers disposal to get that darned mob off the spellcasters back. Whew..

Last in line, but WELL worth a mention are the skills riposte and disarm. Riposte gives the user a counterattack after every successful dodge and parry. While disarm allows you to smack your opponents weapon right out of his hand. Both are useful in their own rights.

Druid Abilities:
("mmm.. Careless lightning")

Available after lvl 9

I can remember back in the good ole days when druids were the "SUPERCLASS" and could 'kite' their enemies into extinction (if you don't know what kiting is, it's a super cheesy tactic, don't worry about it.). Anyhow even if these days are long gone, the druids wide spell capability still remains. So take heart young Ranger!

Top
Curative Abilities
("heal..meditate..heal..meditate..heal..meditate")

Nothin' cuts downtime after a battle like a good heal. Having these makes you a warriors envy ("hehe. Want a heal? CAN'T HAVE ANY! muhaha"). But after nine levels of not having these at you command, you'll be sympathetic to your warrior buds soon enough. However, you heal spells are good for JUST that... Downtime and not much else. Although if you were to be in a tight jam in a fight, you could heal yourself.. a bit..

Top
Damage Sheilds
("Take this!..*OW!*..Take that!..*OW!*")

Nothin' cuts fight time like a good damage shield. Notice a trend? Rangers are known for this: casting a damage sheild on themselves, engage the enemy and let HIM be the assistant in his own demise! If you are not familiar on how this works, it's easy: the mob attacks you, and the magic damage shield counter attacks for a set amount. Rangers enjoy these at later levels though. Power comes with patience my son...

Top
Damage over Time (DoT) Spells
("Hey, how come my health bar is dropping so FAST?!")

...And speaking of patience, if you want to cut your fight time even MORE, cast a DoT spell. The first significant DoT spell a ranger recieves is stinging swarm. A DoT spell works like this: it "eats" away at your opponents hp over time, kinda like what poison does to you. Anyhow, if you turn around and head for a guard while your DoT is in effect, it won't work. So use it wisely, lets say as an opening attack, and it will surely pay off.

Top
Direct Damage (DD) Spells
("Ka-BOOM!!")

Are you drooling yet? You should be. Although i'm not going to readily admit that this is the ranger's forte. I've got but two words for you: "Careless Lightning"... Or any DD spell for that matter! Combined with root or snare, you can surely get some good monster busting spells in. Considering the fact that you'll be completely out of harms way in the process makes it a good idea. The only downside is that you mana bar is going to drop like mad, so be ready for some good-ole-fashioned fisticuff's if you run out.

Top
Buff Spells
("Cower at my hugely pectoral muscles girly man...")

In the best interest of self betterment (Is that a word?). The ranger also boasts some pretty decent STR and AGI buffs aside from the usual AC boosting buffs. Although if your really looking for someone for a pick-me-up, your better off leaving it to a shaman or cleric.

Top
Utility Spells
("Need a SoW at gate.. will pay")

Ahhh the freedom. Breathe underwater, levitate onto an unsuspecting troll's head, render yourself invisible to all, and run circles around a sloth-like ogre. This is everything a ranger gets to enjoy and more. Who else (other than a druid of course) gets to change into a wolf just to run like the wind and howl at the moon? Very cool indeed.


Top
Neat 'Little' Extras:
("..and now for something totally different!")

Notice how I said 'little'? Well it isn't! Rangers boast critical hits with bows, so don't be surprised if you see some nice ranged attacks coming from that bow of yours. Other nice skills the ranger gets to play with is...
Foraging: the convenient way of getting food from your surroundings and into your backpack.
Hide: Now-you-see-me-now-you-don't kinda thing (Not to be confused with I-hit-you-and-hid-so-why-are-you-still-beating-on-my-head?!).
Track: You'll always be able to find something to kill easily.
Sneak: Another nifty method for avoiding monsters.


Top
The Downside to it All:
("We have to deal with THAT?!")

The Ranger has lots and lots and lots of stuff to draw from it seems; but nothing is quite as 'specialized' as you may like it. This makes for good independance, but lacking power for more urgent situations. You are also limited to banded armor... ew. This makes you pretty vulnerable to the higher leveled monster attacks. These two flaws are serious, but if you can look past them for a versatile magic melee class, then go forward young ranger, and become one with the forests!
