The Paladin

An Overview:

The Holy Knight is a powerful adversary and a welcomed friend in any conflict. The merge of Warrior and Cleric makes for a sturdy character balanced on offense and defense. Being able to wear a variety of armors including plate *AND* wield legendary holy swords is also a bonus. Asides from all these special traits there is one thing that really makes the paladin stand out: Lay Hands.


Warrior Attributes:
("slash...slash...*yawn*...slash...slash")

The Paladin is literally a damage SPONGE in combat. This hybrid has the second highest AC rating and HP amount in all of the EQ classes (Second to Warrior, tied with the Shadowknight) . His ability to use plate armor puts him in the 'tank' spotlight. Paladins are also able to use skills like disarm, bash, riposte and double attack. Bash is a pretty good skill in itself, while not as damaging as kick, it has the potential ability to stun the enemy in combat.. Not bad!

In the lines of attack avoidance, dodge and parry are at this hybrids disposal. All I can say is that it is better than nothing, and combined with an already superior AC makes for an air tight defense.

It should also be noted that, like warriors, paladins get a noticeable HP bonus later on in their career. This wonderful occurance happens at lvl 25. This allows them to function better as tanks than the less protected Ranger class.

Top

Cleric Abilities:
("Wound?! What Wound?!")

Clerics are awesome! They preserve life like no one else in the game. When someone wants to set up a successful group, often a cleric is sought as a necessity. They have the best heals, best AC/HP buffs and greatest spells to damage the undead. Plus they are the only casting class toting platemail armor. Paladins derive their spell list from the cleric, and upon first look you should be very pleased... Paladins get all the good spells. :)

Top
Curative Abilities
("Step aside and watch how it's really done!")

Paladins are efficient healers next to Clerics and Shamans. They get the first four spells in the Cleric healing line. Believe me, it's enough considering no other class can come close to the healing power in the long term leveling. You won't see a ranger or a druid besting the Paladins heals. Factor in poison cure, disease cure, and a ressurect spell and you have an all purpose first-aid kit for the nastiest of fights.

Top
Buffs
("It raises your hp HOW MUCH!?")

Cleric Buffs are great. The paladin takes full advantage of the Cleric buffs. A high level Paladin can increase his AC immensely, his HP astoundingly, and have enough mana to assist his group. The yaulp line of spells also temporarily increases STR and AC temporarily for little mana, and that is always a welcome addition.

Top
Direct Damage (DD) to Undead Spells
("Let me show you how to lay-to-rest something that is already dead")

Paladins are excellent against undead opponents, from scaring them to crippling them, only Clerics and Necros can best the Paladin. These spells are pretty mana efficient compared to other hybrid DD spells. In an area like Befallen, which is full of nasty skeletons, a paladin can really clean up. However, the DD spells won't scratch a non-undead creature, so the spells lose their usefulness in alternate situations.

Top
Utility Spells
("Now you can see me, now you don't)

Imagine dying to a ghoul (this is easy for some.). Now that isn't fun at all because he'll be waiting for you to come back. The paladin can avoid him though, being blessed with "Invis Vs. Undead" and "Spook The Dead" makes for some safe to downright humorous situations (if you ever scared a mummy that is clearly gonna beat you to a pulp, you'll know what i'm talking about). Not much else is at the paladin's command. But this is often enough for a Holy Knight.


Top
Nice Bonuses:
("...and you thought I was gonna die")

Lay Hands, Lay Hands and LAY HANDS. This ability rocks! It heals anyone for a set amount of HP (25 * Characters Level). What else renews you to full health in such little time? It is useable every 45 minutes real time or EQ day (approximately). So use it sparingly. But it will be used often, as it is the Paladins best tool for the rest of his career. Keep in mind that it can save lives other than your own, very moving to the person being healed. I also cannot stress enough the ability to use plate armor. Plate armor absorbs more damage than any pansy banded armor could hope to take (Sorry Rangers!). Finally , for the true and patient paladin is the true prize: The holy swords with amazing effects. But i'll leave that for you to discover...


Top
The Downside:
("heal...fight...rest... this is getting boring")

Your good at what you do, but not much else. In the ways of convience in travel and combat the paladin lacks alot. This is because the paladin is a preserver of life. Your a defensive tank; meaning you have little offensive magic (and what you have is undead specific). The lack of dual wield also is annoying, and most will find themselves using 2H swords, thus eliminating the 'Bash' advantage that requires a shield. But if your more interested in playing a class that is a defensive, play a paladin by all means.
