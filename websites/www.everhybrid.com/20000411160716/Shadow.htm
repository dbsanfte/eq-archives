The ShadowKnight

An Overview

The Shadowknight is a very unique class indeed. While every other hybrid class needs to dirty their hands in combat, the shadowknight has offensive spells and pets to help him out. The shadowknight also has good physical offensive and defensive capabilities (equivalent to a paladin). This hybrid, used correctly, can destroy his target and be ready for the next battle immediately. In addition to all of this, the shadowknight has another tool of destruction in his disposal: Harm Touch.


Warrior Attributes
("Thrust! Parry! Dodge! ...RUN!")

The Shadowknight is no pushover in combat, this dark warrior can wear plate and weild many weapons. Being able to bash, double attack, riposte and disarm puts the shadowknight alongside the paladin in terms of warrior abilities. The only initial difference the shadowknight has over the paladin is his one key ability: Harm Touch. Although this ability can be resisted, it has the potential to do alot of damage.

In the lines of defense, the shadowknight has dodge and parry. At best they cause the ocassional avoidance in attacks. When monsters begin to hit REALLY hard, you'll be thankful you have it.

Of course, like their Warrior counterparts, Shadowknights get a hitpoint bonus later on in their development. This bonus occurs at lvl 25 and allows the Shadowknight to function better as a tank then the less protected Ranger hybrid.

Necromancer Abilities
("Fear Me.")

Necromancer's are known for being powerful and independant. They drain the life out of their opponent, make their targets health ebb away, and even set powerful skeletons against their foes. It was only recently that their "Drain" line of spells were made resistable in the interests of class balance.

Top
Damage over Time (DoT) Spells
("5..4..3..2..1..0...Dead on Schedule!")

One of the necromancer's strengths, using great DoT spells, is at the shadowknights disposal. The other DoT weilding hybrid class, the Ranger, does not enjoy the extra effects that come with the Necro's DoTs. This set of spells is what makes the Shadowknight feared in combat (and the reason why alot of people love Cancel Magic so much).

Top
Skeletal Pets
("Who needs friends? I *make* mine!")

NO OTHER TANK GETS TO USE PETS! So feel free to exploit your advantage by all means. The pets, however, are grossly weak for the level you obtain them in. However, the pet can be used for opening attacks and as a distraction to your opponent while you cast spells. Nothing quite beats the feeling of fighting a weakend monster with full health.

Top
Utility Spells
("look at that Mummy run!")

Suprisingly the shadowknight enjoys some great utility spells. Shadowstep, a spell that transports you over small distances, is a great way to get a step ahead of a mob that is chasing you. There are other spells that let you breath underwater and make your opponent run for cover. You get everything you need to get the upperhand on your foe.

Top
Direct Damage (DD) to Undead Spells
("I can make you, I can break you")

Like the paladin, the shadowknight recieves spells that do damage to the undead. The paladin and shadowknight match each others equally in terms of power of undead spells. So keep in mind that you can create and destroy undead creatures, it'll come in handy if you are ever looking for an easy way to get experience.


Top
Draining Spells
("Here, let me ease your burden of HP...")

Probably the coolest thing about the shadowknight is his ability to use draining spells. These spells basically suck the life out of your opponent and restores your HP in the process. Combined with Pets and DoTs, these spells allow you to reduce downtime *and* maybe get some experience in the process!


Top
Nice Bonuses
("Hey! I didn't do anything! I only touched him.... *snicker*")

Harm Touch is a decent skill addition to the Shadowknight class. It deals a good amount of damage that goes up with every level, and it's a good "panic" button for when fights get a little hairy. There is a problem with this skill though; it's resistable. Although it is understandable why it was made this way (Do you want to see fourty lvl 1's taking out guards in your hometown? :)) i'm sure it becomes less understandable when a more reasonable target (IE. Mummy) resists the skill. Even if the spell is resisted, the platemail armor that a shadowknight gets to wear can absorb much of the damage any mob could dish out. The bottom line is the shadowknight is a strong offensive class while keeping some defensive abilities.


Top
The Downside
("Few buffs....Why am I playing this class")

If you played a shadowknight back when they were unable to use draining spells. Then you'd probably dismiss this paragraph. Solely because this class has improved greatly from the beginning. Anyways Shadowknights have very little buffs to assist themselves with. The supposed line of Unholy swords are also pretty much still being discovered as we speak. The fact your also going to be hunted down by many good aligned NPC's is also a discouragement to this class. But if you can look past those three weaknesses then your truly on your way to becoming a powerful Shadowknight!
