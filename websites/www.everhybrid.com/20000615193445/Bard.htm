The Bard

An Overview:

The Bard is the jack-of-all trades class of norrath. Deriving his skills and song from many other classes, the bard never finds himself at a loss in any situation. Being a support class, the bard is best suited for grouping purposes. If you wish to play a class that will often find himself in the company of others, then the bard class is an excellent choice.

Warrior Attributes:
("Ow..OWOWOWOW... RuN! ")

Eventually you'll notice as you progress through levels that the bard isn't best suited for combat individually. Being unable to use double attack, kick, and bash sets the bard apart from the other hybrid classes later in the game. However the bard gets dual wield, which allows the bard to unleash a decent amount of damage. The ability to wear many armors, including plate, also make the bard a decent tank when the going gets tough.

To evade his enemies the bard recieves instill doubt and dodge. Instill doubt is a skill that has the ability to make the scare target and make it run away from combat. Instill doubt is an ability no other hybrid has, so use it well.

Top

The Bard's Songs:
("Bellow and Selo!... What? You can't hit me? That's the point :)")

Bards have some amazing songs. They cover each and every aspect of the game from damage shields to stat buffing. Another great point about the bard songs is that you won't have to wait till lvl 9 to be able to use them. This allows for some instant gratification that other hybrids don't get to enjoy. Nice!

Top
Curative Songs
("Let me sing a song about a wound that is soon gone..")

The bard recieves a couple of songs that restores Hp. This might sound bad at first; but later on you'll notice the songs ability increases as you level. Also, eventually you'll acquire a song that regenerates Hp *and* Mana at the same time! The only downside about the song is that it doesn't heal right away, only over time. So it is best suited for after battles. However, if you must use it during battle you'll find it acts as a great taunting mechanism for monsters.

Top
Buff Songs
("...and of a tale of a buffed up knight who could prevail...")

Immediately you'll notice the great thing about bard buffs, they last as long as the song is being played! This makes for some long lasting buffs that won't disapeer in the middle of a battle. Another thing is that they buff the whole entire party immediately. If you are ever ambushed by a monster, the bard can have the party buffed as soon as possible.

Top
Direct Damage Songs
("I've got a Horn and I know how to use it!")

For the bard in a fix there are some instant damaging songs that do a good amount of damage. Although the drawback with these songs is that they take a good amount of time to recharge. The *other* DD songs, such as BBB (Bellow song), are somewhat of a "kick" for the bard. While these do not nearly do the same amount of damage as your "panic" songs, they are great for kiting... If your into that stuff :).

Top
Utility Songs
("What can't I do? Ressurect? Yup, thats about it.")

Lets go over what the bard can do shall we? He/she possesses songs that can increase speed, grant ultravision, grant water breathing, grant invisibilty, increase mana regen. rate, teleport group members, lull opponents, grant levitation, and cancel magic. Oh wait, thats not it, they can cause fear with their enemies, increase faction with NPC's, Locate corpses, and regenerate mana. Did I mention that they can stun and snare their opponents as well? It's enough versatility to make a shaman or druid whimper. The only drawback is that only one to three of these songs can take effect at any time. Even with that mentioned, it doesn't take a genius to realize where the bards true strength lies.


Top
Damage Shield Songs
("See? It did hurt you more than it hurt me!")

The bard also possesses some songs that act as damage shields. These, like other effects, last as long as the bard plays the required music. This spell is great for speeding up long battles. Since there is no time limit on how long the effects last, the longer the battle, the more useful the song. Bards truly have it all.


Top
Nice Bonuses:
("...I can do it all baby!")

The extent of extra abilities the bard has is overwhelming somewhat. The bard can Forage, Track, Pick Locks, Disarm Traps, Hide, Sneak, and Safe Fall. This truly shows the versatility of the bard class.


Top
The Downside:
("I can do it all; but i can't do it as well as *HE* can!")

The bard is like a class that samples the abilities and spell effects of other classes. While the bard has everything, it isn't necessarily as powerful as the other classes. Bard skills, for example, cap at lower figures than other classes. Although this might not be as important for skills like track; Abilities like safe fall need a high skill level to become reliable. All in all, the bard class makes a great group supporter, and has a song for every battle and situation.
