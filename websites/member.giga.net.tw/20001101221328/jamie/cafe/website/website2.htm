Ching Wu was a very famous painter but nobody ever saw him painting for anybody, He lived alone on a small island, but people knew about the unique gift which he was blessed.

The story of Ching's ability to paint the most beautiful pictures traveled throughout the country and finally reached Danzan Tze, the king of those times. He was a lover of art and beauty, so he sent a courier to invite Ching to paint for him. As his man returned, Dazen Tze learned that the painter was not interested in coming to paint for the king. Being a man of respect, the king traveled to see Ching Wu himself. When he saw Ching he knew that the painter had something special and humbly asked him to come and paint for his court.

Ching Wu saw the sincerity of the king and agreed to come on one condition: Dazan said, "Whatever you ask will be given to you, gold, jewels....name anything."

It is nothing that I require, "Ching replied." It's just that you have to promise not to ask when the painting will be ready, and you may not see it until it is finished."

The king agreed at once, and they traveled together to the court where Ching was given a room in one of the towers, where he would not be disturbed. Time went by, spring turned into summer, and summer into fall. Soon, a full year had passed. The king started to wonder but remembering his promise, never said anything when he met Ching on his walks.

Another year passed and the king became impatient. He met Ching early one morning in the park and was about to ask about the painting when Ching said " I know you are become restless but wait a few months longer."

So another month passed, and another, and finally Ching went upto the king to invite him to see the finished painting. Ching was looking radiant and Dazen became full of excitement. They made their way up to the tower and into Ching's room and there it was.

It was a beautiful landscape covering the entire wall. As the king saw the painting he was touched by its beauty, but felt disappointed, not feeling it to be something extraordinary. Just in that moment, Ching turned to the king, bowed down and bid him farewell. He bent down, picked up his bamboo staff and walked toward the painting. He stepped onto the path, walked down the valley, over a bridge and disappeared behind a mountain.
