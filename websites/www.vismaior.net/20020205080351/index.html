The box for Everquest claimed long lasting adventures and hours of fun, and it delivered that and much more.

Everquest was different, not your everyday game. There was endless content, adventures, quests, people to meet, things to see. But in the end, it is still a game. Ever game ends at one point, and I don't think this is a big surprise, but I wanted to make it official.

There was hours of fun, hours of frustration, hours of corpse recovery, planning, grinding experience, chatting; Everquest had it all. I met some awesome people, some I'll never forget, some I met in real life, some I talked to on the phone, and hopefully some of those friendships will last for a good deal of time. I spent countless hours on EQ, but I don't regret any of it.

So that's it. I'm a freshman Engineering Student at Cornell University (New York). Gunna focus a bit more on grades and school, and hopefully I'll make it outa this place in a few years. Everquest isn't something I'll soon forget. Best of luck to all my friends on everquest, try and keep in touch :). Keep kickin' ass and Takin' names.
