Round 1:
On {HAWK} server, pings very smooth and even. Nice! Pre-match ends and the scouts are off to the races. I think we had attackers in their base before {HAWK} had even cleared the bridge, but they had some Engineers quick with the wrench because our O was cut down pretty quickly. We finally started getting some movement on the flag and brought home a few caps. {HAWK}’s offense wasn’t far behind. They had some trouble getting a hand on our flag for the first half of the round but they eventually cracked the code and brought home several caps of their own. Our D held tight and held {HAWK} to a couple less caps than we got, including getting our flag to return a few times. Final for Round 1: 72-53.

Round 2:
Pings were a bit high on our server so we decided to play Round 2 back on the sweet {HAWK} server. {HAWK} needed a few extra minutes to start Round 2, I think because of Roger Wilco or Battlecom problems. Meanwhile, if our offense was able to get past the bridge, we were getting decent touches on their flag and managed to pull out a handful of caps to solidify our lead. {HAWK} kept pushing but just couldn’t break out. Round 2: 63-19.
