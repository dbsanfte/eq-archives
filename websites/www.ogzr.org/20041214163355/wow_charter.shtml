World of WarCraft Charter


Who are the Old Geezers?

The Old Geezers were founded on June 22nd, 1999 by SlimWit. The reason for starting the group was due to frustration caused by immature gamers. Word spread and many responded strongly that, in gaming, fun and maturity were not mutually exclusive. Today, the Old Geezers span the entire globe and its roster has almost 100 members. The Old Geezers have played and are playing a diverse range of games: MMORPGs, RTSs, FPSs, Simulations, and many other genres. If it exists, we've played it multiplayer.

The Old Geezers World of Warcraft (WoW) guild is also about fun and maturity. We approach our guild activities as an extension of the fellowship that we share with each other. We appreciate the fact that real-life is the most important thing we have and it takes priority over gaming. We don't get so wrapped up in gaming that it causes us to jeopardize our fellowship (or our real-life). We understand that communications in the virtual world are difficult: we are not purposefully insulting or otherwise disrespectful and we have thick enough skin to accept constructive criticism. We value getting together with virtual friends to game more than we value how good we are at gaming.

World of WarCraft Goals

Based on the Old Geezer philosophy, our first and primary goal is to have fun playing WoW with our guild mates. However, In MMORPG’s, part of having fun is leveling up, exploring new areas, and attaining new gear. Our ultimate goal is to take down the biggest, baddest boss monster in the game. We will work towards progressing through the content of the game, starting with low level content, and working our way up through higher level content as guild progress allows. And yes, although we are not on a PvP server, we will participate in Horde vs. Alliance battles when it becomes necessary. However, high level progression and power gaming will never become more important then the fun, maturity, and friendship that we share playing WoW together.
