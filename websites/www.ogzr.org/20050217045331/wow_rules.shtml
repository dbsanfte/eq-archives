World of WarCraft Rules


Playing expectations
To maintain an active guild status, you need to:

  • Send a greeting on the Guild channel when you enter the game

  • After you login to World of Warcraft (WoW), /join ogzr when to see if there are Old Geezer friends online

  • Group with other guild members as much as possible

  • Update your Allakhazam character profile at least once per week

  • Participate on the discussion forums

  • Be aware of other guild members' and share as much as you can

  • Attend and participate in mandatory guild meetings

Ranks
WoW has some inherent ranks in the game and we have some custom ranks to help us establish roles and responsibilities:

  • Guild Leader (GL)

  • Officer: Appointed by GL

  • Member: Full membership to guild after successful trial membership

  • Apprentice: Member seeking full membership

  • Retired: Accepted member that is not active

Promotion
To be promoted from Apprentice to a Member of the guild, you must:

  • Be registered and have posted a greeting in the discussion forums

  • Have completed an Allakhazam character profile and posted it in the discussion forums

  • Have completed 30 days of good conduct as an Apprentice

Apprentices that meet these requirements will be put to a vote of all existing members. Members can vote for or against full membership, or abstain. The Guild Leader has veto authority over the vote in all cases and is the final authority in membership decisions.

The Guild Leader and existing Officers are responsible for promoting full members to Officer. There will generally be one Officer per five guild members, excluding Retired members. Members promoted to Officer are not demoted except by the Guild Leader. Additionally, only the Guild Leader may move members to the Retired rank or remove members altogether.

Looting

The default loot policy will be "Need before Greed" (NBG). When you loot in the party, ensure you fully loot the corpse to allow fellow party members loot after you're done.

Due to the fact that NBG generally enables orderly looting, some of the guidelines below may not apply while you are grouped. However, it should portray how we expect to handle items in the guild. Our loot policy can be summarized as follows:

  • When we are grouping, and an item drops that can be used by several in the group, and it is an upgrade for them, those that can use it roll on it. The winner takes it.

  • If no one in the group can use it, but another member of the guild can who is not in the group, the item is saved for them. If the other guild member can use the item, and it is an upgrade, they get it, no need to roll for it.

  • If they don't want it, or it is not an upgrade, then the item is up for everyone in the original group to roll on for their alts. Winner of the roll gets the item.

  • If no one can use it, and nobody’s alts need it, it should be put up for sale, and the money split between the people who were in the group.

We realize that the NBG system works well with mature gamers, and when a guild is small. However, as guilds get bigger, NBG often becomes more difficult, because there could be several people that legitimately “Need” an item. We are aware of this issue and will monitor the size of the guild and make sure the loot is being distributed fairly. If it gets to a point where NBG is no longer viable, we will switch to a points based system. However, we will cross that bridge when we get there.
