The Old Guard and the Scarlet Monestary
March 12, 2005 - 2:39AM by SlimWit

The Old Guard made its first all-Geezer foray into the Scarlet Monestary, a World of Warcraft instance found deep in Tirisfal Glades. There was not much planning beforehand but it proved to be great fun nonetheless. The party was Valrain (lvl 45 Hunter), Eownmacmedb (lvl 45 Druid), SlimWit (lvl 45 Priest) Edeilirra (lvl 42 Warrior), and Chovak (lvl 38 Rogue). This was a group adventuring together in a significant scenario for the first time in this fivesome. The evening began with the necessary and long crossing of the Western territory, deep into Horde-controlled land.



Scarlet Monestary is a group of four instances in which we had quests for three. We started with the easiest of the three and steadily moved through the dungeons. As you would expect, there were numerous books to read in the Monestary; some so interesting that you sometimes forgot what you were doing and why you were there...then again, maybe that was only my problem.


We tried a variety of attacking strategies and found that Chovak's sneaky ways really allowed us to control the opening of each battle when we hit the groups of two or more. There were a couple of moments where this did not work, however. On at least one occassion, dispatching a key enemy caused a swarm of enemy to respond, overwhelming us to a humorous point at least once. Fortunately, Valrain was patient enough for the rest of us to return as she survived this swarming by feigning death.


The fact that it took three separate instances to complete the quests we had lead to a late night we old people, especially because each instance respawned many of the monsters. We had to fight our way in and out in two cases. In the end, The Old Guard was successful: we completed our quests and had fun doing it, not to mention the fact we learned quite a bit. Valrain gained level 46 and the loot was decent. After defeating the final enemy (one of them twice as he was resurrected), we gathered for a group picture and rode hearthstones home. Thanks to all who participated!


Uldaman venture
February 12, 2005 - 4:27PM by SlimWit

A bunch of us got together to make our first run at the Uldaman instance. The party was Kikash (lvl 47 Paladin), Kutlachs (lvl 44 Warrior), Valrain (lvl 44 Hunter), Eownmacmedb (lvl 38 Druid), and SlimWit (lvl 43 Priest). It had been a while since we took a beating like this, so we dusted ourselves off and proceeded deeper into the cave.

We did very well and entered a very large room where Kikash placed a staff into a small statue, which caused the staff to generate a bright beam of light, destroying a large, sealed door and freeing a massive monster that was a higher level than any of us had ever fought. It was a long battle, with Eownmacmedb bravely throwing himself in front of the monstrosity, followed by Valrain, and then SlimWit, before Kutlachs and Kikash, both very low on life, were able to take Ironaya down.

After reforming the party, we press deeper still, and encountered some huge battles against incredible numbers. We entered a large room with many elites dwarves and an Ancient Stone Keeper.

Well, we wiped, plain and simple. Uldaman is an instance that respawns, so we returned in, but it was clear to us that The Old Guard would not take the instance. Returning through the portal, we hunted some of the missing quest items and turned in three quests for a lot of experience and some decent rewards, but wondering when we would return to this extremely fun instance.

Blackfathom Deeps
January 17, 2005 - 2:32AM by SlimWit

The Old Guard has been very active in recent weeks, but last night a few of us met to take on some new "instance" content on the Western shores of Kalimdor in the Blackfathom Deeps. After putting a call out to Kutlachs (lvl 33 Warrior) and him dying a few times on the cross-ocean feature ("a feature"), Valrain (lvl 38 Hunter), Eownmacmedb (lvl 29 Druid), and SlimWit (lvl 38 Priest) picked up a non-Guild but equally worthy party member in Ruta (lvl 30 Druid) and ventured into the Deeps. Both Ruta and Eownmacmedb had been here before and were our seasoned guides, but neither had seen it to the end of the instance.

Like other instances, the path to the portal was harried but we eventually made it through. Our progress was steady through the instance with a lot of underwater battling.


Unfortunately, Ruta has some serious connection issues and was dropped and didn't reconnect until we were almost done. Eownmacmedb also had a connection, but was able to rejoin us and remain connected to completion.

I must say that the dungeon was very impressive and worth the time investment. We fought underwater and on the surface; casters and melee enemies; humanoid and summoned pets; all with one thing in common: triple the life as everything was "elite." We have one particular battle towards the end where the enemies kept coming and coming that was extremely fun. Then, soon after, we got a little cocky and did not heed our guide's advice of "don't touch the candles and stay away from the middle." Well, it seems that translated in our heads as "hey, this is interactive...I wonder what happens when I click it?" Instead of taking 20 or so enemies in 1/4 at a time, they all rushed us and we all paid for our lack of respect. No worries, though, as this instance is not a re-spawning one: we returned to our corpses, resurrected, were rejoined by Ruta, and proceeded through again, taking a turn or two that we missed before to close a quest for Kutlachs. Into the room where we died with a little more caution rewarded us with victory and access to the final boss, which Kutlachs pulled brilliantly and we made short work of.

While the adventure may not have gained us many experience points, it was great content, the drops were fine, the cash was decent, and the quest rewards were excellent. Well done, all!

Gnomeregan
December 28, 2004 - 7:05AM by SlimWit

The Old Geezers ventured into the fallen Gnome capital of Gnomeregan to avenge our brothers. Initially, Kikash, Valrain, and I assaulted the underground city and defeated the malfunctioning Techbot mech to return its memory core to its creator, Tinkmaster Overspark. After completing the quest, we moved into the instanced dungeon to explore further.

As we explored, we encountered a frenetic sidequest that had us battling multiple incoming enemy at a time, twice, before the breach was sealed through explosives.


After defending this incursion, we were joined by Edeilirra and, while Kikash was picking him up, we picked up a fifth member to the party: Carallion, a brave level 32 Warrior. These two additions to the party made us much stronger and, in retrospect, allowed us to adventure further. Without them, it is unlikely we would have been able to get much deeper.

We pressed on in search of irradiated enemy in order to extract radioactive fallout samples for the Gnome scientists to analyze in their search for figuring out how to take their city back. This quest was difficult as you had to take the sample from a living enemy, and trust me when I say that they were less than cooperative. After taking the sample, we stumbled upon a hidden enclave of Gnome refugees that allowed us to catch our breath before adventuring further.

As we progressed deeper, we encountered more and more mechanized enemy which allowed us to collect Robo-mechanical Guts for our third quest of the evening. These abominations provide some comic relief as, when destroyed, they ejected their pilots with hilarious flair.

We did learn a hard lesson while mech-hunting. There are alarm mechs that, while weak, call for reinforcing bots when allowed. Our first encounter with two of these alarm mechs resulted in an overwhelming amount of enemy. With Valrain feigning death, we eventually made it back to the ugly scene and avenged our deaths.

We then embarked on a quest requiring us to hit a series of computers to gain requisite cards, but missed one and had to backtrack, which resulted in another wipe. As the instance resets when a party-member re-enters, we decided to take our weary bones to bed as the time approached midnight. We headed to the surface for celebration and fresh air.

It was a very good evening, despite the deaths. We completed three quests in Gnomeregan and gained enough experience to level Valrain, Kikash, and SlimWit. Our first foray into Gnomeregan also gained us familiarity necessary to finish the few remaining quests. We also want to thank the brave Carallion for joining us.

Two Elite quests at the same time
December 2, 2004 - 6:04AM by SlimWit

I stumbled onto an unbelievable gaming session this morning. As some of you know, I awake at 5am to get a couple of hours of grinding and other administrative stuff done. I don't expect much out of this time; I was very wrong about that this morning...this morning was on of the best times I've had in-game, yet. It's always a pleasant surprise to group with people you don't know and actually have a fun time. World of Warcraft is a great game and my gaming in the early hours connects me with fellow serious and mature players.

I was headed out to the East side of Loch Modan to pick up a few quests. As I was waiting for the NPC to show up, I partied with a level 15 Dwarf Paladin so we could do the quest at the same time and not have to wait further. We escorted the NPC to Ironbad's Excavation Site and I picked up a quest that the Paladin said he had difficulty with earlier.

We ventured into the excavation site and started knocking off some mobs, and were joined by a level 18 Night Elf Warrior. He helped tremendously in pulling and holding aggro. We completed that quest for all three of us, feeling rather flush with our success, I must admit.

The Dark Elf asked if we wanted to try the two Elite quests in Loch Modan and we agreed after the ease of completing the former quest. Along the way we found a Gnome Mage and asked if he wanted to join us. He inspected us and surmised that we'd need a lot more horsepower to survive and departed. No hard feelings on our part, but we decided to focus on the lower of the two quests based on his feedback: Mercenaries.

We approached the ogre camp with some trepidation based on the Gnome's wisdom, and inched slowly against the ogres. The Warrior pulled one and I quickly went through my mana as we defeated it. Being out of mana as a Priest is not a good thing for the party, so I began to scale back my damage spells a bit. We made nice progress against the quest but only because we were engaging one or two at a time.

Along came a level 23 Dark Elf Hunter and we invited him to join us. That addition to the party really turned the tide against the ogres and we made quick work of the quest, pushed into the cave, and made the decision to go for Loch Modan's other known Elite quest: WANTED: Chok'sul Alliance

The pace was pretty frenetic. So much so that I don't have the normal screenshots to share with you unfortunately. We engaged more ogres in the cave (level 19 or 20) and made it to Chok'sul's chamber, where 2 casting ogres and Chok'sul were waiting. The fight was a long one, and I was largely in the role of healer as I should have been. The Priest's Power Word: Shield was invaluable to our survival. After eliminating his companions, we focused on Chok'sul and defeated him with tanks at 50% health and a dead pet for the Hunter who was a true hero in the engagement.

I'll spare you the details of returning to Loch Modan (I died once, we fought the Loch Ness Monster, etc.), but I do want to share the rewards. Twenty silver pieces for the Mercenaries quest, and for Chok'sul: Minor Channeling Ring and Kimbra Boots, as well as a new level for me, SlimWit.

World of Warcraft: My first raid
November 18, 2004 - 8:46AM by SlimWit

I feel a little background is necessary before you read this. I have successfully resisted massively-multiplayer online role playing games (MMORPG) since the beginning because I am completely cognizant of the fact that I am an inherently undisciplined and addictive person. There is a reason they call it “EverCrack” I think. That feeling, however, is in direct opposition of the cheap gaming b@st@rd in me that can’t pass up free multi-player gaming. So, when a friend at work mentioned that I should try the Open Beta of World of Warcraft (WoW), I said “sure.” Well, I liken the “Open Beta” to “high school heroin dealer” as they both put out free stuff to get you hooked and then you willingly pay later.


I was finally able to hook up with aspalmat last night. We’ve been talking a lot about the game but haven’t been able to meet-up in-person until last night. My friend at work (Valrain) and I traversed to the Dark Elf island and met aspalmat before he logged off. The time was about 11:30pm. A “raid” was starting from the Dark Elf land planned to go into the Horde (the other player side in the game). Valrain and I decided to join the raid to see how it went.

First step was to get from the Dark Elf island to the enemy. We took hippogriffs to the next land and then boats to the land after that. I recall that aspalmat mentioned in chat that the transportation is a lot quicker and more often in WoW than in the original EverQuest, FWIW. After getting off the boat, here is where the raid goes a little squirrelly.

Those of us that had been on the Eastern Kingdoms before were able to take an air taxi (Griffon) to the next rally point, which was Loch Modan. Those that didn’t had to take the long and dangerous land route which just happened to skirt the enemy land. At that fork in the road, a lot of the raid participants (40 in total) turned North to the Undead city instead of South to the rally point. They got slaughtered as their numbers were small and they were relatively unsupported. You see, there was only one priest out of the forty players in the raid...not a good thing.

While we were cooling our heels in Loch Modan waiting for numbers, the enemy Horde had attacked in significant numbers on Kalimdor which is the western big island and the first stop mentioned above. The raid purpose immediately went from offense to defense as we all land traversed back to the port town to hop on a boat and repel the assaulters of Auberdine.

The boat landed and we immediately traversed to the South of town and succeeded in repelling the attacking Horde. This was a massive PvP battle of Horde against Alliance. I have never seen as many corpses in all my time in this game. Unfortunately, our numbers were down to about 30 with others following. The raid then turned to offense as we made a loooong land traverse South to end up in The Barrens. Along the way engage some of the Horde remnants, but encountered a huge resistance in The Barrens at the Crossroads, where I quickly died to a huge monster.

The time was about 1:30am, so I decided to take my old and weary bones to bed. I teleported via my hearthstone back to Loch Modan, took a griffon to the Dwarven city of Ironforge, and went to bed.

Overall, the raid was good experience. I learned a lot about the Horde vs. Alliance aspect of the game and enjoyed that. I also learned a lot about how to conduct such a raid, but that diatribe would go on for a lot longer, and I've already tortured you enough.

aspalmat and I (SlimWit) are planning to lead an Old Geezer guild in WoW. There hasn’t been much existing Geezer interest (VRMan and rumored others) so we will likely be recruiting outside the existing roster. If you are interested, please hit the Join Us page and we’ll look you up in game. As Blizzard has not yet announced the servers names yet, I can’t tell you where will be, but I will announce in The Forum what we decide.
