Both rounds were played on the Mercenary Outpost server where pings were not far apart. Server settings were NOT STA, but we played anyway.

Round 1: Geezers as Ts, Mercs as CTs.
The round began with almost no warning and caught we Geezers off guard, but that did not stop us. We pulled to a quick lead. But it didn't last long. After a couple of their members dropped and rejoined (with higher pings) they started bringing the pain. Their amazing accuracy brought them back to a round win at 14 to 9.

Round 2: Geezers as CTs, Mercs as Ts:
This round started bad for the Geezers. While we couldn't hit a bottle of metemucil if it was right in front of us, their accuracy was good enough to puncture a colostomy bag at 100 yards. We lost each round big and bold as they always seemed knew where we were. After the first eight or so losses, we realized that we couldn't count on being able to get to the timer in time, so we just tried to kill them all. The round ended in a 13 to 3 loss for the Geezers.

This match showed an interesting result of our practices. Each and every one of us went to our positions and did what we were supposed to do. Damn fine job of that, but the other team was still able to kill us, no matter what we did. Just goes to show, a superior plan and a group of great players can still be beaten if the other team takes the right steps.
