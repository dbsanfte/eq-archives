Many memorable quotes have been spoken about The Shaggs. They appear in magazines and on their albums mostly -- some good commentary and some bad. Amongst this mix of opinions lie the opinions of some very important musicians, such as Frank Zappa, Jonathan Richman (of The Modern Lovers), and Bonnie Raitt. Keep in mind that none of these quotes necessarily represent the opinions of the person that created this web site. We maintain a neutral stance on the issue, but feel free to peruse what others have said in the past!


"The Shaggs. Better than the Beatles -- even today."
- Frank Zappa

"The Shaggs are like castaways on their own musical island."
- Bonnie Raitt

"...For me, one song about your real life is worth ten 'professional' songs. The Shaggs convince me that they're the real thing when they sing."
- Jonathan Richman

"They bring my mind to a complete halt."
- Carla Bley

"The Wiggin sisters simply weren't necessarily ahead of they're time; their simply on a different plane altogether."
- The Record

"May be the best worst rock album ever made..."
- New York Times

"Philosophy of the World is the sickest, most stunningly awful wonderful record I have heard in ages: the perfect mental purgative for doldrums of any kind."
- Debra Rae Cohen, Rolling Stone

"I thank Rounder Records and NRBQ for getting this one to see the light on CD."
- Rock & Roll Disc
(speaking about the 1988 CD release)
