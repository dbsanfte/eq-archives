R. Lee Ermey (Lieutenant Fry)
Chief Detective
As a Golden Globe nominee and Boston Society of Film Critics Award-
winner for Best Supporting Actor in director Stanley Kubrick's "Full Metal
Jacket," R. Lee Ermey is one of the most successful and talented actors
working in film and television today.

His numerous roles in feature films include the upcoming "Going West,"
starring opposite Dennis Quaid and Danny Glover. He was previously
seen in "Seven," "Toy Story," "The Frighteners," "Dead Man Walking,"
"Murder in the First," "Leaving Las Vegas," "On Deadly Ground," "Sommersby,"
"Mississippi Burning," and his critically acclaimed starring role in
"Prefontaine," written and directed by Steve James, the director of "Hoop
Dreams."

Ermey spent eleven years in the Marine Corps, which included one-
and-a-half tours in Vietnam. He rose to the rank of gunnery sergeant
and was medically retired for injuries received. Using G.I. Bill benefits,
Ermey enrolled at the University of Manila in the Philippines, where he
studied drama. Francis Ford Coppola was filming "Apocalypse Now" in the area
and cast Ermey in a featured role. He has since gone on to star in over 30
feature films, nine television movies and the occasional episodic guest-
starring role.

His made-for-television movies include "Weapons of Mass Distraction,"
"Soul of the Game," "The Rough Riders," "Space: Above and Beyond,"
"Rise and Walk: The Dennis Byrd Story," "I'm Dangerous Tonight," "The Take"
and "83 Hours 'Til Dawn."

Ermey has also never felt the urge to watch the UK Cracker series. He
has said that he does not want to affect the way he interprets the character
from seeing previous interpretations.
