BASICS OF THE GAME

There are a wealth of places to go for information on Baldur's Gate before deciding to buy it, such
as the web site of it's distributor, Interplay as well as the page of it's development team, Black Isle.
We'll try and point you in the direction of the most common and helpful references on the web for
dealing with your Baldur's Gate issues.
Baldur's Gate is a RPG (Role Playing Game) in the tradition of Dungeons And Dragons and Diablo.
It uses the second edition rules from D&D, but is designed much like Diablo is. Where Diablo is
more arcade like in it's approach, Baldur's Gate takes the Diablo interface and makes it far more
complex. There are far more character statistics to deal with and party interaction. Some prefer the
more complex nature of Baldur's Gate over Diablo, and vise-versa. It was easily one of the best
games of 1998 and sold so heavily that only Red Alert matched it's initial sales statistics.
Baldur's Gate successfully runs the characters through an intense plot line in a world far larger than
Diablo and is playable in both single or multiplayer configurations. It is not as complex as Ultima
Online, and can be almost as quick to learn as Diablo. You can check out screen shots of the game
at the web site of Interplay to get a better idea of what the game looks like.

this is where the following Portrait add-ons should go in order to be accessible by your copy of

GUILDS

Playing in a guild can be a wonderful way of getting through this game. Not only do you stand a
better chance of playing with people who do not hack their characters (spruce them up and make
them better than they would be if you played the game normally), but you could play with a bunch of
people from all over the world and maybe forge a few freindships.
At the moment, you can find a fairly decent list of BG Guilds by travelling to the guilds section of
The Adventurer's Guild web site.

SUPPORT AND TROUBLESHOOTING

Although Baldur's Gate does have a few bugs, it's pretty much as bug free as you're going to get.
Any additional patches to the game can be found through the game's Main Menu, which will link you
directly to Interplay's patch site on the web. There are also some very helpful discussion boards at
Interplay for getting technical help on the game, hints, or just to chat with other players. There is also
a FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions) for Baldur's Gate that might be helpful. That pretty much
covers the basics. If you're finding that you're having trouble connecting players after the game
autosaves before travelling off the map, this may be a bug. I've certainly had this happen to me
numerous times during gameplay. One thing that can help is editing your BALDUR.INI file in the
game directory and enter the line "Client Timeout=(whatever number you want)" at the end of your
[Multiplayer] section. If you're interested in cutomizing your characters with different portaits and
sounds, you can get information on how to do it by clicking here.
