BASICS OF THE GAME

There are a wealth of places to go for information on Starcraft before deciding to buy it, such
as the web site of it's distributor, Blizzard. We'll try and point you in the direction of the most
common and helpful references on the web for dealing with your Starcraft issues.
Starcraft is the sequel to the equally successful Warcraft 2, which helped set the original standards
for multiplayer real-time strategy games in the mid 90s. Where Warcraft is set in the past against a
fantasy backdrop, Starcraft is set in the far future where humans fight more than just Orcs. This time
they're up against two new races -- the Zerg and the Protoss. You can play it single player and
follow the basic storyline that surrounds Starcraft, or go online with Blizzard's own Battlenet to play
with and against others from around the globe in intense multiplayer combat. You can check out
screen shots (1, 2, and 3) of the game at the web site of Blizzard to get a better idea of what the
game looks like. There is also a FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions) sheet available from Battlenet
that gives a full run down on all of the features of this game.

STRATEGIES

One of the key things to being a good Starcraft player is knowing popular Starcraft strategies. It is,
after all, a real-time strategic war simulation. You can always ask players for advice at the Battlenet
Discussion Center For Starcraft. Blizzard also maintains an excellent list of up to date fan-based web
sites that handle popular strategies for Starcraft.

MAPS (aka. SCMs or "SCUMs")

If you're looking to really get the garbage beaten out of you, there are tons of maps out there you
can add-on to Starcraft to get yourself extra missions or multiplayer battlefields to play with. Blizzard
has an area of listing SCM Creation and Technical Defence Sites. You can also check out
www.starcraft.org, whose SCUMS section is one of the best on the planet for add-on levels.

LINKS

There are hundreds of Starcraft fan sites. Since we don't have the intention of making this a page a
big centre for Starcraft, it would be foolish for us to assume that any list of links that we would
concoct would be at all accurate. For this reason, we refer you to the excellent resources of
www.starcraft.org. They may be a fan-based site, but they are packed with extremely helpful
resources, technical help, etc. The Starcraft Compendium is also quite good. If all else fails, Blizzard
maintains an up to date list of fan-based sites. There's also Blizzard's homepages for Warcraft 2 and
the Expansion Pack add-on Brood Wars for Starcraft.
