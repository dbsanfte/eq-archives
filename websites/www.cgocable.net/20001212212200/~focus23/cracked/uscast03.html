Carolyn McCormick (Judith Fitzgerald)
Ex-Wife To Fitz
Carolyn McCormick first realized that she was interested in acting
during high school speech tournaments. That led to performing in plays
while attending Williams College, where she graduated with an honors
degree in theater. She went to graduate school at the American Conservatory
Theatre in San Francisco, California, where she earned her MFA degree.

Prior to accepting her role in "Cracker," McCormick was a series regular
on the series, "Law & Order" and "Spenser: For Hire." She also appeared in
the made-for-television movie, "Cries Unheard"; and guest-starred in the
series, "L.A. Law" and "Star Trek."

Her feature film credits include "A Simple Twist of Fate," "Rain Without
Thunder," "Enemy Mine" and "Burnzey's Last Call." In addition, she has
appeared in numerous theater productions, including "The Time of My Life,"
"The Way of the World," "Present Laughter," "Counselor at Law," "Saint
Joan," "Uncle Vanya," "Much Ado About Nothing" and "Philadelphia Story."

McCormick currently resides in Los Angeles with her husband and son.
In her spare time, she enjoys snow skiing, water skiing, mountain biking
and cooking.
