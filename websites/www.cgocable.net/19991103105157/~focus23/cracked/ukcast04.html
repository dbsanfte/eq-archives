Christopher Eccleston (DCI Bilborough)
Detective Chief Inspector and Fitz' First Boss
Christopher Eccleston is one of the most promising, up and coming new
actors to come out of England in the past few years. Best known by
us for his incredibly memorably portrayal of DCI Bilborough on Cracker,
he was first seen in films like "Let Him Have It" and "Anchoress" before
he was seen in Cracker. He must have caught a few eyes in the process
as he immediately went from good to better after leaving the series, when
he was asked to star in Danny Boyle's "Shallow Grave". Not only was
this one of the best roles Christopher Eccleston has done, but it
internationally launched the film career of Danny Boyle, who was known
in the UK for films like "Twin Town" but went on to create such notorious
films as "Trainspotting" and "A Life Less Ordinary".

Some of Eccleston's other credits include "Death and The Compass", the TV
mini-series "Out Friends In The North", "A Price Above Rubies", and
"Jude", in which he starred alongside the very talented and very beautiful
Kate Winslet.

Another credit that is very worthy of notice is "Hillsborough", which was
another fine production penned by Jimmy McGovern (who of course wrote
Cracker). "Hillsbourough" also starred Ricky Tomlinson, who played DCI
Wise on Cracker. Although not shown widely outside of the UK, it received
excellent reviews from Cracker/McGovern fans who managed to see it.
