Safe charming
by Nomalas (02/21 09:07 AM EST)



All you need for safe charming is one friend - a druid. Druid gives you sow, pulls mobs, and snares. You charm. When charm breaks, you run a bit away (easily done because of the speed difference between you and the mob) and reapply.

On those rare occasions when things get hairy (too may wanderers and such), you just zone your sow'ed self.

99% of the time, though, this is basically risk free xp, and good xp at that. And the best part is that there is almost no down time at all. You don't use much mana in the first place (since you're using the mobs to fight each other) and you can med during the fights. Although, medding during the fights is much safer once you hit level 35, where you no longer have to look at the spell book.

I've been doing this on occasion since my late 30's. Never charmed before then because I had heard too many horror stories. Now I'm kicking myself for not having done it sooner. Started with spectres, then moved to spirocs, and now doing it with forest giants in Burning Wood.

The key to charm safety is the druid. Evidently they are, in fact, good for something. Who knew? Hehe.
