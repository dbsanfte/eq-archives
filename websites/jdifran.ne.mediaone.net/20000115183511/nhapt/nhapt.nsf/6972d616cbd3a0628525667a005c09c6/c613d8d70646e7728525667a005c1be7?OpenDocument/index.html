Mission Statement and Working Philosophy
Mission Statement

The mission of NHAPT is to:
    1. Advance the knowledge, understanding, and ethical use of the MBTI among its membership.

    2. Foster opportunities for its members in training, networking, and fellowship.

    3. Provide a forum where participants can "talk type" with other interested practitioners.

    4. Increase the public understanding of type theory.

Working Philosophy


As NHAPT, we will:
    • find better ways to increase your personal training skills utilizing the MBTI
    • provide an active support network with colleagues that can help you with your efforts
    • provide a forum for dialog about our own preferences and how this affects our approach with others
    • provide an opportunity to learn about and discuss the latest advances in the field, topics on type theory, and other instruments using type theory
    • provide regular communications with members on local, regional, and national events
    • work to inform the public (businesses, schools, government, etc.) of qualified facilitators' availability
