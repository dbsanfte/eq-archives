WoA High Council Guide

Be a leader

High council members should be above all positive. They should have time to look at the bigger picture, and to deal with the possible conflicts between members of the guild. This may mean sacrificing a member for the good of the many, but this should not be taken lightly. This should be seen as an option only when all other diplomatic solutions fail.

Arbitrator

If anything gets escalated through the guild as a matter arrising, it should take precedance over your current game. You may be in a fantastic group gaining all the exp in the world, - but if you love your guild, isn't that worth fighting for over the exp? So, guild business should be a priority. You are expected to help the wheels keep turning, and making the guild members day to day lives as easy as possible.

Disciplinary Action

As a member of the high council, your role in this respect is the same as an officer, but you may be called upon to witness difficult situations where an officer may want your consent in a matter for instance, to remove a member from the guild who they are not the mentor of.

Event Hosting

High Council members are expected to host or assist on guild events. Members should send their event information to the webmaster. At the beginning of each month a schedule of events will be posted on the website. Information should be made available at least 3 days before the event is scheduled to occur (7 days is preferred). All Members should try to follow the rules set out in the Raid guide.

Recruiting Members

High Council members may recruit new people into the guild.. Anyone who is invited to join this guild should be friendly and have a positive attitude. Before an invitation is issued, a few questions should be asked to the candidate to get an idea about their personality. Some good questions are: How long have you played EQ? Is this your main EQ character? Have you ever been in a guild before? How did you hear about this guild? Who do you know in the guild, and how do you know them? How old are you in real-life?

The HC Member that recruits the new guild member is responsible for that member, and will act as a "mentor" for that guild member.

Only the "mentor" will be allowed to offer the "in-game" guild invitation to the new member. The new member will be "trial-member" for the first month of guild membership. HC Members should only Mentor 2 guild members at any given time.
