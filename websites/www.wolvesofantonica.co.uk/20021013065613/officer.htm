WoA Officer Guide

Below is what we think is a fair guide to the roles and responsibbilities of a WoA Officer.

Be a Positive Role-Model

Officers of this guild are required to present themselves in an honorable fashion at all times.

Officers should know and follow the guild rules, and act as examples for others to follow.

Arbitrator

Officers often act as an arbitrators between two people. As an officer, if you are put in this situation; do not make assumptions; listen to both sides of the story. After hearing both sides, decide how the matter can be resolved. If the dispute is between two guild members then listen to both sides, and inform each member of your decision.

It should be noted that an officers decision is final. If the dispute is between a guild member and a non-guild member, and the guild member is determined to have been in the wrong, then the officer will instruct the guild member to make specific reparations (i.e. give back the item/camp, pay 20pp, etc.).

If the dispute is between a guild member and a non-guild member, and the non-guild member is determined to have been in the wrong and does not agree to a resolution, then a GM will be contacted if necessary, or an officer in the offending persons guild will be notified.

Disciplinary Action

Officers of this guild are givin the authority to discipline and even remove fellow guild members. It is an obilgation of being an officer to ensure that fellow guild-mates know and follow the rules of this guild. If an officer feels that a guild member has violated a rule, and a simple warning is not sufficient, than that officer will issue an "Official Warning" to that member.

Official warnings will NEVER be done in chat, but with a direct tell. After the warning is issued, the officer will post that member's name and the offense on the "Officers Message Board". Members who's names get posted on the officer's message board are considered to be on probation, and will remain on probation until their names no longer appear on the message board (1 month). If a member who is on probation is caught violating the rules a second time, then that member shall be removed from the guild.

Event Hosting

Officers of this guild are encouraged to host a minimum of one guild event per month. Officers should send their event information to the webmaster. At the beginning of each month a schedule of events will be posted on the website. Information should be made available at least 3 days before the event is scheduled to occur (7 days is preferred). All officers should try to follow the rules set out in the Raid guide.

Recruiting Members

Officers of this guild are often required to recruit new members to join our guild. Anyone who is invited to join this guild should be friendly and have a positive attitude. Before an invitation is issued, a few questions should be asked to the candidate to get an idea about their personality. Some good questions are: How long have you played EQ? Is this your main EQ character? Have you ever been in a guild before? How did you hear about this guild? Who do you know in the guild, and how do you know them? How old are you in real-life?

The officer that recruits the new member is responsible for that member, and will act as a "mentor" for that member.

Only the "mentor" will be allowed to offer the "in-game" guild invitation to the new member. The new member will be "trial-member" for the first month of guild membership. Officers may only Mentor 2 members at any given time.
