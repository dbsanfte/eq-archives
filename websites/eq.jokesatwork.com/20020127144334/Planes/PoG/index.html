Plane of Growth.

Zone in: The portal is located in the wakening lands. The location is Pos1000 Neg500. I suggues getting an invis so you don’t have to deal with the creature in the zone while making your way to the PoG zone in. At the location I gave you you'll see a lake with a small island. A bridge crosses the water leading into a tower on the island. Fauns guard the tower. If you're not an evil race/Class you should not be KOS unless you have hunted them. Check your faction before strolling past them. If they "Scowl, Ready to attack" or if they are "Threateningly" you'll need someone to invis you. If your smart you'll already be invis'd from the run to the tower. Run into the tower and on your right there is a ramp. Runing up the ramp to the top you'll see the tower has a few levels but is primarily vacant. Once at the top of the tower, you'll see a big orb looking thing. You click on it to zone into PoG (But don't go in until your raid leader tells you to zone in). At the very top, the groups should buff up and get invis, and all zone in at once.

Break in: When you zone in you'll find yourself standing on a white pad. This looks a lot like a druid port circle but is white mable. There is a creature called a "Whorl of natural energy"  Once you appear youll be facing south. So turn right and head to the zone wall (west). The zone wall is not very far from the zone in spot. By the time everyone has made it to the west wall. Recently the Whorl has been given the ability to see invis. He will most likely agro on someone. Pull him to the zone wall to kill him. Everyone else should stay invis until they get to the wall so they dont agro other creatures on the way.You'll need to kill it quickly. The "Protectors of Growth" will be on thier way immediately. The "Protector of Growth" usually spawns in pairs and they see across the entire zone.  Ocasionally only one will be up when you zone in. They will head towards your raid as soon as someone who is KOS is visible. At this point drop invis (if you didnt have to kill the whorls) and get ready for the protectors of growth (pets go up, etc). They can't be mezzed. The most common practice is to have everyoen get on one Protector and have one or two Melee types (High Level) tank the other one. Kill one then move to the next. Appoint somone to keep track of the timing on the Protectors. Their spawn time is 28 Minutes. You will have to kill them several times and they drop nothing.

Scouting: You can have druids/rangers/paladins scout the place. DO NOT have druids/rangers/paladins fight in PoG. If they do they won't be able to do their epic and life will be miserable for them. If they accidently take a faction hit, they need to leave immediately. 2 kills will make you kos. If you do happen to take a faction hit, go to west freeport and give that guy in the inn tons and tons of muffins. This will repair most of the faction lost there. There are 4 factions you take. Faydark Champions, and 3 tunare related factions. I honestly have found little use for scouts. The puller will generally have a good idea on whats out there and how to pull it.

Pulls in PoG: After pogs die, you can pull mobs from the zone. The best class to have pull in PoG is a bard. Alot of the creatures run faster than sow and some even summon. Bards can handle both of these issues. The creatures that run faster than sow will still be slower than a bard so pulling them back to the groups wont be a problem. The creatures that summon will do so if they take any dmg. Bards avoid this by using thier songs to taunt the creature and pull it back to the groups. A monk is the second choice puller and will help is some of the pulls anyway. The only downside to having a monk (or any melee) pull is that the creatures faster than sow will beat on them the entire time they pull to the groups and sometimes they wont even make it back. The creatures that summon are not as much of an issue but without a NON Damage Ranges attack (like a bard song) the melee type pullers will have to get close enough to the creatures to agro them. This will put the puller in danger simply because of the DMG output these creatures have. On the west side of the river the creatures are not as tough, but there are lost of them. Another advantage a bard has is that he has track. A bard can pinpoint the locations of each creature and pick the best pulls. Once you move East of the river the creatures get tougher and harder to pull.  There isn't as much field of view for the puller as this area is heavily wooded and hilly.

Pull Locations: Here is a map of the zone.

The following is a list of the usual spot to pull from and the creatures you can expect to see in those locations. At any of these location you may see creatures that spawn zonewide. There are almost no static spawns in PoG. So many of the creatures will roam in and out of the area while pulling. At just about any of the pull locations you my or may not see named creatures such as

  1. 1st location Neg2400 Pos3400. (Break In)
    You will want to start the pulling just west of the zone in. On the west wall youll have plenty to pull for hours.  Here youll pull lots of     Gale Wolves, Fastride Unicorns, Rolling Plains Steeds, Spirit Flux Wolves, Glade Stalker, and even a few Sanguine Kodiaks. If your luck you'll get to see and kill Galiel Swifthoof or Sarik the Fang. When things  start to thin out youll want to move north.
     
  2. 2nd Location Neg0 Pos3400. (Intermediary) Move north along the west wall from the break in spot.
    This location can vary and usually will. The bard (or puller) will decide the best spot since he/she knows wwhen the remaining creatures will be. At this location you'll pull lots of     Spirit Flux Wolf, Sanguine Kodiaks, a few more Glade Stalkers and a few less Gale Wolves, and Rolling Plains Steeds. If your lucky youll get to see and kill Grahl Strongback or Sarik the Fang. This aread is a stepping stone to the waterfall. you will want to hunt here until you've cleared (for the most part) the zone in the North West Corner.
     
  3. 3rd location Pos3500 Pos1000 (Waterfall) Move north along the wall from the Intermediary spot.
    Here you will start pulling more from the east side of the river. The creatures get tougher and the puller will need to be more carefull. Here you will pull Sanguine Kodiaks and the remaining Glade Stalkerss and Spirit Flux Wolf (hopefully). You will start pulling Entolling Harvest, An Entoling Culler, An Entolling Essense Channeller, and Feral Amalgams. If your lucky you'll get to see and kill Grahl Strongback or Mumbling Totem Man.
     
  4. 4th Location Neg3500 Pos1500 (Castle) Move South on the west wall to the south wall then to the caslte (stay away from the castle walls).
    Here you will pull remaining Glade Stalkers, Gale Wolves, Rolling Plains Steeds, and even a few Spirit Flux Wolves you might have missed. If your lucky you'll get to see and kill Mumbling Totem Man. You probably wont stay long. This spot usually serves only one purpose. Clear the path to the Lake. Once the area is clear youll move to the lake and around to the other side of the lake.
     
  5. 5th Location Neg3500 Neg500 (Lake) Moce in front of the castle to the lake and then follow the shore to the cliffs.
    Here you will pull from the forrest area. The pulls here are very tough and a monk often used to help seperate pulls. Here you will pull     Serene Forest Spirits, Sylvan Protectors, Thifling Chanters, Thifling Focusers, Thifling Lords, Thifling Orators, Thifling Sprites, and a few remaining Feral Amalgams, and Entolling spawns. If your luck youll get to see and kill Ordro & Ordros Assistant.

Precautions: At all of the pull locations you will face creatures that can gate or wipe out the raid(when you pull too many). If the creature gate it will usually heal itself and start summoning people. if this happens you will want to camp out immediately wait a minutes or two then come right back. DO NOT /q or /exit. Sit camp, and wait the 30 seconds. If you /q or /exit the game will treat you as ingame and you could cause problems with agro. If you get summoned just get your location and die. The raid will get your corpse after everyone camps out and comes back. The best way to prevent creatures from gating is to have the nukers wait until the creature is about 40% life. Then CHAIN NUKE and STUN. The Tranquil Treants are the gaters you'll deal with ealry in the raid. When you start killing the Thiflings you'll deal with the other issue. These creatures will wipe out a raid if you get too many. Some raids I have been on got wiped out when we got just 3 of these. The trick is in the pulling. If you ever see a puller come back with more than 3 of these, don't even wait. sit and start camping. Unless your raid consists of many 55+ players you will not be able to handle that many of these. The Thiflings are not too bad when you get one or even two at a time.  you just have to be carefull. you will have to gauge the skill of the players in the raid. some raids will be able to handle many of them and some will recover corpse after pulling just 2. 

Corpse Recovery: This is prolly the most unpleasant parts of a raid. If you have a well organized raid and several high level players, you might not ever have to do a CR. Sometimes even good raids get wiped out though. Here are a few tips for the CR. There WIL be PoGs to deal with durring the proccess. What youll have to do is have the bard kite them while the rest of the raid does CR. Hopefully you were able to have a cleric camp out before the wipeout. If so then he can log in and res the clerics. Then rez the manna batteries.

Raid Finally: Gate/Translocate/Port. Nuff said. This is the only way out so make sure you have wizards and druids on the raid or your tanks will be stranded.
