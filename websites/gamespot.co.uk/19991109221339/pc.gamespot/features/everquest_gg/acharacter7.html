Faction Standing and Economy

Woe to the elf who tries to buy anything in Kaladim! The dwarves have little love for the fair folk, and their ridiculous prices surely reflect that. When you are first born in Norrath, the various races and creatures of the world have a certain opinion of you. That opinion is called "faction standing" and changes constantly - usually through killing a friend or foe of the respective opinion holder.

For example, there are those who kill the fairy folk on sight. So the fairies come to fear or even hate them, and they could be attacked on sight should a fairy powerful enough happen along. Fairies view me indifferently, however - I tread my own path and leave them to theirs. Orcs are another matter entirely, however. Orcs look upon me with hatred and disdain as many have fallen to my trusty hammer.

What does it matter what orcs think of you? It matters greatly. A very large and powerful orc, greater in power than you, might strike you down instead of your friends because he knows of your deeds. This is a dire situation if you're a mage; an orc may go so far as to ignore the warriors and beat you to death - of course, most beings hate mages anyway.

Generally, most inhabitants of Norrath don't care about you when you begin, though that is not always true. I once witnessed a guard in Freeport attack a gnomish necromancer because the guard could not stand the sight of the evil magic user. I have heard complaints from many a necromancer who could not buy even food and water because the merchants would not speak to him. Rogues often have the same problem.

TIP
You are best off if you deal with your own people - in your own guild if possible!

I always make an effort to buy and sell from the Wood-Elven merchants at the Druid's guild in Kelethin, as they are my very own kind and profession. There I get the cheapest goods, and sell my artifacts at the best prices.
