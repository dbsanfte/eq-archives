Safety in Numbers
Unless you are a level 50 warrior wearing full plate armor, you should always travel with other players outside city limits. This is especially true when exploring difficult zones or dungeons. With a group, there is the obvious benefit of having someone to watch your back; however, this is not the only advantage.

Consider what a single player would have to know to successfully explore a dungeon. He must know lock-picking skills to open trapped chests; he must know healing skills so he could heal himself after battle; and he would probably require some knowledge of magic. He must know all these abilities - in addition to the regular fighting skills. Is it possible for one player to fill all these roles well? Of course it is. When adventuring alone, you are forced to become a jack-of-all-trades and a master of none.

A prime example of traveling in groups is that different characters can have specialized functions. For example, a group might contain a rogue to help with any locked doors, chests, or traps; a cleric or healer; a bard to keep morale high; a wizard for offensive spells; and several able-bodied warriors.

Joining a group or creating your own is easy if you're a member of a player guild. Grouping with people you know is a primary advantage of joining a player guild. However, many players you meet in your travels will be glad to include another member in their parties. You should be wary of strangers, and they most likely will be wary of you, but if you're a new character, most players won't be especially leery of letting you to join. Don't be afraid to ask to join a group. The worst it can do is say no.
