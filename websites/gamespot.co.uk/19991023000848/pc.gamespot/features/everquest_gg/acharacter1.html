Trade Skills
Noncombat skills (or trade skills) are also quite valuable, especially as a means of making money. The old adage, "It takes money to make money" is alive and well in Norrath. I'll use tailoring as an example. To be a tailor, you must purchase patterns and use pelts collected from various beasts throughout Norrath. Wolves yield small cloth armor (for elves, gnomes, and dwarves), pumas and lions yield medium cloth armor for humans, and bears yield large cloth armor for trolls, ogres, and barbarians.

As you gain a level, you are awarded five skill points to spend as you wish. I recommend they be saved, initially. Later, you can use those skill points in a noncombat, professional skill. This way, you won't lose all your pelts and patterns because you're poorly skilled. It is unwise to use your skill points in other ways, such as in combat or magic, because those skills are used very frequently and increase quite quickly on their own.

TIP
Don't waste skill points on combat-related skills. Those rise quickly enough simply by adventuring.

The exception for this rule is learning new skills. For example, at level 8, druids gain the ability to meditate (a very valuable skill). It will cost one skill point to learn the initial skill, but no other points should be spent on it. The reason is that meditation (and most other such skills) is used so frequently, it raises quickly on its own. See Chapter 9 - Advanced Skills for more information on trade skills and how to use them.
