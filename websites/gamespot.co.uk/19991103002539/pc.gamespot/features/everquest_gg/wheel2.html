Camera View
Above your hotkey buttons, you see a button labeled View." By pressing the view button, you can toggle through a series of five camera views. These views are the perspective in which you view your character in EverQuest.

You start out with a first-person view, and if you click the view button once, you will see an overhead view. A third click, and you will see a view from over your shoulder, and the next two views show a view of your area from farther away and are a good way to watch and make sure no one is sneaking up on your back. Another way that you can toggle the views is by using the F9 function key. You can scroll through all five views with F9 as well. From an external view, you can use the right mouse button to move your viewpoint. The view button can be made into a hotkey.

Information Scroll
The scroll in the center of your screen contains your interface for communication and information. At the bottom of this scroll is the command line, where you see text as you type. This is how you speak to other players and NPCs. See Chapter 4 - Off to see the Wizard for more information about communication.

TIP
You can filter what information is displayed here under the options menu.

If you right click on the words in the view while in windowed mode, you will get your language screen. Here, you will see a box for Current Language and a series of default channels for text output. This will be what your character does when you hit Enter and begin to type. The default for this is Say, which will let you say things to the people around you. Another options here is Tell the group that you're grouped with, Tell a particular person (you would type in his name), Speak out of character, perform an action, or Speak to members of your guild. Right-click to get out of this box once you're finished here.
