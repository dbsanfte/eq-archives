Races

Race is a very important selection, as it will not only determine your appearance, but will also define the classes available to you. It should also be noted that some races are inherently evil (dark elves, trolls, and ogres). If you choose one of these races, do not expect to be treated very well in the human cities.

Certain races have advantages that are rather obvious when you go cycling through the various combinations of race and class. As you select a race, the portrait, classes available, and base statistics all change. One of the less apparent differences between the characters is the use of infravision or ultravision (the ability to see in the dark). Humans, erudites, and barbarians do not have this ability; all other races do in some form or other. If a character is considered evil, he will be slain on sight by guards in barbarian, dwarven, elven, erudite, gnomish, halfling, and human cities and towns.

The following information and tables are presented for each race to assist you in choosing the race and class most suitable for your character. There is no wrong selection here. EverQuest's character choices are extremely well balanced and provide players a wide range of choices. Numbers aren't the only aspect of a character that affects how your character will develop. Skills, magical abilities, NPC reactions, deities, faction standings, and more will influence the success of your character.
