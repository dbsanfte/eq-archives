Monk Skills

Hand to Hand
Hand-to-hand combat is the monk's hallmark skill. At very high levels, you'll get magical punches. But you can get magic punches much earlier by finding any pair of magical gloves and wearing them into battle. Some monks train up their blunt weapon skills as well, but your hand-to-hand skills will eventually far outshine any blunt weapon.

Kick
Monks have a series of special attacks, starting with kick (though other warrior types get this, too). All the special attacks have different damage potential and recycle rates, but generally, the more advanced skills are much better than the novice ones.

Mend
This skill is very frustrating to build up, but will become your favorite skill at higher levels. This single skill makes monks a viable solo class. A high-level warrior could sit and spend days healing, as you naturally heal 100 hit points every five minutes. But your mend skill grows with you, and you will always heal for around one third of your life, no matter how many hit points you accumulate. This will cut resting time drastically and often save your life or turn the tide of battle.

Bind Wound
This skill is really too expensive to afford at low levels, but later on, it can prove useful in getting yourself rapidly up to half health.

Dodge
Monks are the only class that starts with dodge. It's too bad it works so infrequently as to be almost useless. It will rise automatically in combat.

Throw
While it's very cool to toss shurikens and even somewhat useful at times to attract a monster's attention, don't rely on throw too much for hurting enemies.
