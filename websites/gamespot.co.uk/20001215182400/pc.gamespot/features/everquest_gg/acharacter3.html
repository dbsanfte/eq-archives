As long as you fight near your city of origin for the first few levels, you should do well enough. You'll find some levels more difficult to pass than others. As a druid, I found level 4 quite tedious, though it was worth it to attain level 5 and new spells.

Upon reaching level 5, it is wise to group up with people of a similar level. Be wise in choosing your fellow travelers, however. Some cowards flee, leaving you to die, while others take more than their fair share of the loot from fallen foes. Too many members means less experience for you, and if their levels are much greater than yours, that also means less experience. I stay within two levels of myself (as a general rule). I am loathe to group with those much weaker than myself, as it can mean death for the whole group.

On deciding whether or not I wish to group or journey alone, I use this simple rule. If I am seeking gold to line my purse, I travel alone and kill creatures I know are not too risky, but still give worthy loot. If I desire experience, I find myself a diverse group and journey with it, never worrying too much about loot I may have missed. You also fight much more difficult creatures in groups, going for nothing less than even fights. If the group is strong enough, it will attempt to attack creatures that are red. In time, you'll learn to discern whether one companion is good, or many are desired. It really depends on your level and the area in which you travel.
