Features



To group with another player, target him and hit the Invite button on the main menu of the windowed view. If the player accepts your invitation, you will see the character's name and his red hit-point bar in the party window on the right side of the screen. If another player invites you to join his group, the Invite button will change to say "Follow." Clicking Follow will add you to that group. To leave a group, hit the Disband button. If you are the leader of a group, you can selectively remove group members by clicking on the member's name in the party window screen and clicking on Disband. If you target yourself or have no target and click Disband, the entire group will disband.

While grouped, any experience gained from any member of the group killing a monster is divided up among the party members. If all the characters are the same level, the experience points are divided up evenly. If there is some disparity in the levels of the characters, the higher-level characters will get more experience than the lower-level ones. The important thing to remember is that higher-level characters get more experience points in a group than lower-level ones.

NOTE
To prevent "twinking," or higher-level players carrying lower-level players around and killing monsters for them to get experience, there is a limit on the maximum distance in levels between the highest-level player and the lowest-level player in a group. At the lower levels (below 10th), that maximum is around 3. So, if a 10th- and a 7th-level character are grouped, the 7th-level character will get some experience from monsters killed, but a lot less than the 10th-level character. However, if a 6th- and a 10th-level character are grouped, the 6th-level character will get little to no experience. As characters advance in levels, this maximum increases, so that a 38th- and a 30th-level character could adventure together, and the 30th-level character could still get experience.
