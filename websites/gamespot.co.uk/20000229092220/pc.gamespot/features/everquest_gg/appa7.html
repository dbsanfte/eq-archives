Felwithe
Felwithe, much like Erudin, is an architectural wonder. Wrapped lovingly in plants and flowers, this city is the hub of art and culture on Faydwer. The high elves who call Felwithe home have constructed this wondrous city deep in the Greater Faydark Forest. It may take you some time to find the city, but your journey will be well rewarded. Felwithe's solid limestone design and ivy covered walls serve as a constant reminder that the city of Felwithe, like the high elves, has been around a long, long time. Like Erudin, Felwithe contains teleporters for quick travel about the city.


Felwithe
Felwithe boasts several places that should not be missed. Therein the more scholarly of classes can find refuge in the Great Hall which balances precariously, nay impossibly, upon a small point. There magicians, wizards, and enchanters alike can find comrades and teachers so as to improve their arts. Standing proudly and with honor just over the bridge passed the main gates is the paladin's guild. There, new and veteran paladins can train and equip themselves as necessary. Because Felwithe is a much larger city than Kelethin many more wares can be found there - so that elusive plate or those hard to find boots can often be easily located within Felwithe's fabled walls.

Freeport
Freeport is largest city in all of Norrath. Just like it's name suggests, it is a port city built along the western edge of the Ocean of Tears. Constructed of sandstone blocks, it was founded as a gateway between Antonica and Faydwer. While Freeport is primarily known as a human city, the port brings in many of the races from Faydwer. Do not be surprised to see an assortment of dwarves or elves in town. Set in the rough desert environment, travelers are cautioned against venturing to far out without an ample supply of water. The Deathfist Clan of Orcs can be found in the Commonlands to the west and north, and their scouts can often be seen approaching the city walls in the early morning hours.


Freeport
Freeport, although devoid of abundant plant life, has many sights which are worth seeing. The Temple of Marr is a must see at night, as the glow emitted can be seen for miles. Other unique sights include the arena and the sprawling docks. If your journey through the Desert of Ro leaves you a bit parched, the Jade Tiger Inn has some of the best locally brewed ale to be found. Regular ferry service is offered between Freeport and port Faydwer.
