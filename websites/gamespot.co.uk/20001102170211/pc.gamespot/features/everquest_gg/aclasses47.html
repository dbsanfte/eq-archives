Getting Started
Your first quest will be to find your guild, which is not as easy as it may sound. Shadow Knights and necromancers do, after all, practice their skills in dark underground passages, as close to fresh corpses as is possible (and not-so-fresh corpses, too). Your letter of introduction was somewhat vague regarding the location of the guild. Look for it in the darker side of town and don't be surprised if the entrance is hidden and perhaps underground. Your guild master will greet you after you show him the letter and offer to train you in a variety of skills. These practice sessions are precious, however, and you should save them for a while.

Initially, practice sessions should only be used to acquire the fundamentals of a skill. After you know the basic skill, you can increase the skill by using it, rather than waste practice sessions at your guild. You will find that skills fall into two categories: those that advance easily when used (like combat skills) and those that are difficult or expensive to develop (like trades skills and several skills of the arcane nature). Choose a weapon skill that you will stick with for some time and work on getting the appropriate weapon.

It's a good idea to find the bank about now. After all, you will soon be making some deposits. Now that you see where your coins will be stored, it is time to take some from some hapless cleric or, better yet, a wood elf if you can find one.

So, draw you blade and venture forth into the hinterland.
