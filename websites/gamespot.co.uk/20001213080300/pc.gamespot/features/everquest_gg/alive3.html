Player vs. Player

Player vs. player (PvP) combat is the single most debated aspect of any online role-playing game. EverQuest has a PK switch to allow those who do not wish to fight other players a choice. By default, you start out invulnerable to attacks or theft from other players (also known as PvP-). Likewise, if you are PvP-, you cannot attack or steal from other players.

When you start the game, you will have in your inventory a PK book. If you want to fight other players, (also known as PvP+), read the book and follow the instructions. If you do not ever want to fight another player, stay PvP-, you can click on the note and move it to the Destroy button right above your inventory items. Doing this will destroy the PK note forever. Another option is to put the PK note in the bank for possible future use. In either case, the choice to be PvP+ or PvP- is entirely yours.

NOTE
Once you become PvP+, you cannot go back. Choose wisely!

If you choose to become PvP+ (more commonly known at PK), you can fight with any other character who has also chosen to become PvP+. However, to prevent uneven battles, some limitations are built into the PvP system that limit the level difference between combatants.

Unlike normal combat with NPCs, if you die as a result of a PvP battle, you will not lose any experience points. Likewise, if you are the victor in a PvP battle, you will gain no experience points. However, the victor in a PvP battle can loot the fallen foe and take a single item from his inventory as well as all his currency.

TIP
Never engage in a PvP battle in town. Guards will slay you both on sight!
