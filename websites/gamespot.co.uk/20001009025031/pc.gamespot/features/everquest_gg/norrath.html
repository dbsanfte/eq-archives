Norrath Overview

The world of EverQuest has a rich history that sets the stage for your role playing. The following introduction to the history, races, and geography of Norrath will serve as an initial guide to help you get started.

History of Norrath

The history of Norrath is well documented, both by scribes and storytellers alike. While the history described herein is a summary, it is by no means the complete historical telling of our land.

Long ago, the god Veeshan, a crystalline dragon, found the world of Norrath pleasing and decided to place her brood on the continent of Velious. With a swipe of her claw, she opened great wounds upon the surface of Norrath. As time passed, other gods noticed Veeshan's work and began to stake their claim to Norrath as well. Brell Serilis was first, and he created a portal to a cavern deep in the heart of Norrath, where he placed his dark creatures and let them flourish.

Over time, other gods came to Norrath. After some time, Brell Serilis convinced them to discuss the future of this world. Brell proposed an alliance with the other gods, without revealing the fact that he had already placed his mark on the world. The world was called Norrath and divided up between these beings of power for the purpose of keeping Veeshan in check. Each would create a race of beings to watch over Norrath and keep a vigilant eye on the schemes of dragonkind. Brell claimed the bowels of the planet and created the dwarves, stout and strong, deep beneath the mountains of Norrath. In the abysmal depths of the ocean, Prexus left his children, the kedge, hearty aquatic beings of great mental power and stamina. And on the surface of Norrath, Tunare create the elves, creatures of limitless grace and beauty. Rallos Zek created the giants, fierce and formidable beings, intent upon the defense of their lands.

The gods are always watchful of what their rivals are doing, and it did not take long for other gods to notice Norrath. It was the elves who attracted the unwanted attention of Innoruuk, Prince of Hate. Innoruuk was vengeful and angry that fellow gods did not involve him in the creation of Norrath. Thus, in an act of hate, he stole away the first elven king and queen. He tore them apart, both physically and mentally, over a 300-year span. He then reformed the remains of these elves and deposited them in the underfoot of Norrath. Innoruuk's revenge was thus completed, and so were born the dark elves.
