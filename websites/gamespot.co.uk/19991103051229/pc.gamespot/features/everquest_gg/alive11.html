Fighter Class Strategies
The following tips are aimed toward the fighters in the group. These are typically warriors, monks, bards, rogues, rangers, paladins, and shadow knights.

Fighter classes are relatively straightforward. They take damage, and they deal out damage. You're the muscle of the group, so it's your duty to annoy the monster so the others in the group can land blows. You must always watch your health and know when to back away and let another fighter take some of the damage. You must keep an eye on everyone's health and know when your group is running low on health. Sound the retreat if the fighters in the group start getting too low.

If you are in multiple creature combat, attack anything that decides to turn on your spell casters and taunt mobs that refuse to turn away from a wounded group member. If you're a good fighter, you'll have thick armor and relatively high agility. This way, you will take less damage than another member of the party.

TIP
Don't take all the damage yourself. Know when to spread it around.

As a fighter, you must know when to keep the monster on you and when to back away and let others take some damage. In a good group, no one leaves combat unscathed. It is more efficient to have everyone healing (especially if you have a bard in the group) than just one. Engage the monster and then rotate the target, saving weaker members as needed.
