Creating a Character
When you arrive in Norrath, all you will have is a dagger, some food and water, two spell scrolls (shock of ice and minor shielding), a Tome of Discord, and an introductory letter to your guild master. Shock of ice creates a blast of ice around your target, and minor shielding adds to your defensive abilities. The Tome of Discord, if read and brought to the Priest of Discord, shows you the way of chaos and lets you kill (and be killed by) other players. Even if you plan on following this path, bank the tome for now and get some experience first.

Getting Started
Initially, practice sessions should only be used to acquire the fundamentals of a skill. After you know the basic skill, you can increase the skill by using it, rather than wasting practice sessions on it at your guild. You will find that skills fall into two categories: those that advance easily when used (like combat and magic skills) and those that are difficult to develop through use (such as the trade skills). Due to their weak nature, piercing and two-hand blunt are the only viable wizard skills, as wizards cannot wield any other form of weapon.

It's a good idea to find the bank about now. After all, you will soon be making some deposits and even perhaps a few withdrawals. It may be on the opposite side of town as your guild. Then again, it may be right next to your guild. Now that you see where your coins will be stored, you are acutely aware that you have none.

So, unsheath your dagger and head for the gates. It's time to hone your combat skills and earn a few coppers in the process.

Beginning Adventuring Tips
The areas near the city gates are filled with lesser beasts. These are your ticket to fame and wealth at this point, so go get them. You'll kill bats, rats, lesser skeletons, snakes, and such. Gather their skins, eyes, legs, wings, meat, or anything else that you can scavenge because it's all worth money. Run for the protection of the guards if you are in trouble. If you are killed, no one can loot your corpse, unless you are PvP, so do not worry about your gear.

Remember that the skill cap is 5 percent plus 5 percent per level you have attained (3 percent plus 3 percent per level for melee skills). Your piercing, offense, and defense should all raise through combat. When you go up a level, check with your guild master (or the following list) to see if you are eligible for training in a new skill.

It is usually good to help others who are in trouble. They will greatly appreciate this, and it can earn you new and lasting friendships.
