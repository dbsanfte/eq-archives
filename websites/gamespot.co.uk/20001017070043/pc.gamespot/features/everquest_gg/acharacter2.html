The Great Hunt - Improving Skills

It is the natural order of things that you should hunt for food or other sustenance as the need arises. It is also in this way that you gains experience and improve your body and mind. Wise adventurers will choose creatures that are suitably challenging, though not deadly, to increase their skills.

In my experience, it is better to adventure alone for the first four levels of your life. Fear not! From levels one to four, you suffer no penalties for dying, all your experience is kept intact, and you begin anew with your original starting equipment. However, be cautioned - you do not start again with your guild note, so it is important to deliver that before you begin hunting.

TIP
Deliver your guild note before leaving town the first time you're in the game.

For the first couple of levels, you should remain close to the safety of the guards and the city gates. The guards will slay any creature that attempts to harm you if they happen to see it (and are close enough). So it is better to run to the guards for protection when hope seems lost - it saves you a trip to your body to recover your inventory.

It is important to Consider (by right clicking the creature) wisely all creatures you may fight. If the creature appears green to you (that is, the warning text is in green), you stand a very good chance of winning. If the creature is blue, it represents a minor challenge for you and the experience gained will be greater. If the creature is black or white, it is likely an even battle for you. (The words the Consider command give you will help guide your decision.) If the creature is yellow, the battle is risky, and your chances of winning are very slim indeed (though still possible). If the creature appears red and kindly asks what you wish on your tombstone, you have no chance at all. Remember that many creatures have different strengths. Just because you defeated that orc centurion with ease moments ago, the next one that comes along could be significantly more powerful, even though it is the same basic kind of creature.

TIP
Creatures of the same type can vary in power.
