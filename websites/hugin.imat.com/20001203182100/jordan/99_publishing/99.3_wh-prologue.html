99.3 What is the deal with the Prologue for Book 9?

Will I be able to read the Prologue of Book 9 on the Web, before the book goes on sale?

In the past, Tor has made the Prologue chapters of TWOT books available on their web page, prior to publication of the respective books, in order to promote the books. This will not be the case for Book 9.

Rather, Simon and Schuster have bought the electronic publishing rights to the Prologue of Winter's Heart, and will sell it under their Scribner imprint to desperate readers for $5 a pop, starting September 13, 2000. So, you will be able to read it if you want, but it'll cost you.

I know that, but I don't understand what it means. Explain?

Here is the official word from Teresa Nielsen Haydon, the Extremely Nice and Helpful Tor Books Editor:

This business with the Prologue developed very quickly. What happened was that Robert Jordan's agent sold *just the electronic rights* to *just the Prologue* of Winter's Heart for a substantial six-figure sum. They're planning to charge five dollars for it. Just to clarify one point: When our edition comes out, it'll have the Prologue in it. This deal only affects the electronic rights.

$5 seems kind of expensive. Are they trying to rip us off?

Well, Scribner/Simon and Schuster is probably trying to recoup the "substantial six-figure sum" they paid RJ for this treat. But just because they're suckers, doesn't mean you have to be. Let's assume that WH will feature similar print size to TPOD; that is about 400 words/page. According to Scribner, the WH Prologue is 20,000 words, which comes out to 50 pages. $5 for the prologue comes out to ten cents per page. The book itself is 625 pages, so by Scribner's pricing scheme, WH should cost $62.50. That is over twice the list price of $29.95. Talk about inflation! From a typical customer's point of view, this is not a good deal.

This is an outrage! Who can I complain to?

Firstly, do not send nasty-grams to Tor Books. They have always been very helpful to their readers, and they don't have anything to do with this project.

If you want to complain to somebody, or make some sort of protest against this scheme, here are some suggestions (copied from the rasfwrj FAQ, courtesy of John Novak:

  • Simply do not buy the prologue. You'll get it at the regular price when the print version is released. Remind others who might be inclined that this makes no economic sense.
  • Go to the Scribner web-site, and use that forum to politely but firmly express your dissatifaction. Let people considering buying this realize exactly how silly it is.
  • Write a physical letter to Simon and Schuster at:
    Simon & Schuster Online
    1230 Avenue of the Americas
    New York, NY 10020
    Let Simon and Schuster realize POLITELY but FIRMLY that they are not only unlikely to make a profit, but are garnering negative publicity in the process and perhaps losing customers.
  • Consider writing a printed text letter to Jordan himself, through normal channels, expressing those same sentiments POLITELY but FIRMLY. At the same time, impress upon Jordan that he ought to save all the letters which express dissatisfaction, and make his agent read those letters.

This plan is great! Where can I bend over, er, pay my $5?

Try the Simon and Schuster web site (http://www.simonsays.com/).

Will Tor provide any on-line sample of WH?

Teresa Neilsen Haydon, Tor Books Editor, says, "I don't know of any current plans to put sample text from Winter's Heart up on the Tor webpage. As usual, I'll let you know if anything changes."
