Journal Nine
                
 
The Dark Trine

Malgorm appeared on the shores of West Karana. Waiting for him was his dark rose, covered in black armor and carrying her praised scythe, Pestilence. Envy gave an evil smile, filled with desire for chaos, and Malgorm only returned it. Within a few words they had conjured a dark plot to display their power and kingdom to the weak mortals that made up Qeynos.

For hours they sat along the outside roads of the city, where Envy taunted the followers of light and life into a frightful rage, while Malgorm offered money for the help of those without a faith to harrass. Then they entered the city itself and sparked a riot of great proportion. The streets were filled with hateful screams, arguments of whose god was stronger. The guards were doing all they could do to contain the city. As the war made it's way to the great gates of the arena, the guards pushed in all that wanted to shed blood.

The chaos began. Holy men of different faiths wet their hands with blood from one another… and out of the shadows approached the three dark figures behind it all. A trine of great wickedness. The two men stood as twins; one in darkest armor and the other in black flowing robes, each appearing as a dark avatar of evil. Then rushing forward with a bloodlusted scream, the mistress of death carved her scythe Pestilence it's place inside a paladin. His blood flooded the earth as the crushing power of Lord Faileas' dark blade finished his life. But he didn't stop there.

As the battle raged, Lord Faileas changed from his usual cold calm fighting style to the vision of an uncontrollable plague. He attacked everyone he saw, as if he were fighting the light within himself, cutting down paladin after paladin. Envy looked shocked by his recklessness; Malgorm stood cloaked in the shadows of magick, observing. The chaos continued, with Lord Faileas fighting all that lived. He had caused wounds to so many, that in fear and realization they had all begun to turn on him. Alarmed, Envy took a protective stance, turning her offense to defend him. Malgorm felt the rage burning inside Faileas, one of the first displays of emotion from him in a long time.

The greatest Paladin of the match then turned to Faileas. He was a man of great power and reputation, but nameless to the demon. They all looked like gnolls to him. In acrimonious rage for life, Malgorm summoned a force that stopped the great light bearer in his tracks, and then went on to turn pure hell fire loose upon him. Without remorse, the wizard watched the man struggle and drop before he even had a chance to place his blade in defense.

By then Lord Faileas had taken many mortal wounds, only to keep fighting… with blood-slicked armor and sword he still sliced through any man foolish or unlucky enough to cross him. Finally exhausted, Faileas sank to the ground with all but one man dead or quickly getting there. As the lone paladin approached he was suddenly blocked by a menacing Teir'Dal with flashing eyes and a scythe bigger than herself. With passion, Envy dueled him in what could have been called suicidal intent. Malgorm knew the man was of greater skill than Envy, but she showed no fear. When the fight was over she stood alive, mocking the corpse of a great knight that dared battle the dark mistress of death.

The Dark Trine merely walked away from the scene, stepping casually over the bodies littering the arena and streets of the city. Malgorm smiled in delight with reflection; the three of them had fooled the light bearers into battling themselves, and their own egos had finished them off. Perhaps he would use this in the future… why waste all his energy and time killing an enemy, when he can have more fun getting his enemies to kill each other? The demon laughed.

Bertoxxulous' Love

In the dark woods surrounding the Lavastorm Mountains, Malgorm stood recovering from his teleport and and saw her there waiting for him. Her deep purple eyes were lit alive with with divine fire and freedom. The sight of Terina was all inspiring, a perfect beauty. She was the only light in the dark forest. What had brought her here was unknown to Malgorm, all he cared for was that she was here. The hunger and the plots and the evil were once again washed away in the bright purple flames that were her eyes.

He followed her without words as they marched up the mountains of lavastorm. They spent the night killing off the goblins that lived around the lava, as well as the newly formed men of the shadows… some kind of spirit. Malgorm killed them at her will with no thought of where they came from; only knowing that they swung their weapons of magick against her. Of course, any that dare stood to harm her were soon met with an all-cleansing release from life.

As the night grew darker they made camp in the black forest. Malgorm gazed at the lovely goddess of freedom. In him, something deeper than Azzothoth, something deeper then Bertoxxulous called for freedom. Malgorm was the opposite of Terina, he was the slave and the enslaver. She stared back into his eyes; or rather, the dark soulless pupils that were Malgom's eyes as if she knew what he felt.

"Do not fear. I will free you from Bertoxxulous." The words felt like daggers to his heart and an inner fire lit up.

"My lady……" he muttered. The words he wanted to say came into his mind, "I am a demon. You can not save me; I love you because Bertoxxulous wishes it. He wishes to use you for some reason. I do not know what for, but I knew you from lifetimes long ago… I have dreams of you from a different age."

At his thought, The Hunger screamed in outrage and servants of the undead arose from nowhere to attack the wizard. They were not sent to kill but served as warning. Terina looked shocked and she hacked them down with ease. After several moments he tried to speak again, and as the words rolled off his tongue more servants arose from the dead.

Terina screamed, "Leave him alone!!" Her words sounded liking breaking glass.

For the first time The Hunger attacked Malgorm. He watched her eyes go from bright purple to the dark violet of the robes of the Bloodsabers. Her skin reflected the skin of a dark elf and her smile became Envy's, taunting his weakness. Then he felt the madness creep inside him.. the voice laughing was his own voice..

"Weakling…" it said, mocking him the way he had said it to so many. The mental lashings of his own making fought against him. He struggled not to let a tear fall in front of Terina, swearing not to let her see that he was weak. For the fist time, the wizard shielded himself from her. All he could see was a dark elf staring at him; one that used him and laughed about it in front of her sisters. Bertoxxulous too laughed from inside, taunting and laughing as he slept. Had his enslaver taken her from him as well?

The Guilt and Greed

For many days the Wind Rider Silveril had been tortured by Malgorms mocking thoughts. Finally she had went to face him. She began her attack with the same flirting nature that followed her blade in most battles. She snapped angry questions about how he had two children with her, and yet now had a different lover, some "Terina". This outraged the demon, just with her knowing that angered him. Silverann must have told her all. Oh, how the rat was going to die. What fate the stupid child had brought upon himself.

Silveril's attacks were at first bold, but the abuse the demon had taken nights before only fueled his attacks upon her mind. The whipping was fierce, and the mighty warrior was falling to the pain that Malgorm and Faileas had caused her. Malgorm taunted her with this mercilessly… the dark rituals used to hold her dear lover had drained his spirit. The great teacher Stormangel was dead, drained of life and soul- and another now took his place. As for Silveril during that time, she had bore the unholy offspring of the dark ritual; the half-breed and the freaks she called children echoed pain in her mind and heart. Malgorm instilled in her mind that even she was a freak, and for that her friends would never trust her.

The pain was not enough. He sensed a deeper, darker guilt was behind her tones. What other pain was she carrying? What madness had the birth left her with? The mental raping of her soul ended with Malgorm feeling fear and remorse… Not for Silveril, but what if this could happen to Terina? The truth of the monster he was came in mind once again. Malgorm shook it off as a question of where Silveril and his guilt was coming from. He left the Estate of Unrest where Silveril stayed much of the time, fighting the endless legions of undead. Perhaps to hide the pain that she fought each time she swung that axe.

As Malgorm making his way out, a small halfling that he recognized crossed his path. A cleric of the great god of thieves, named Glendale. He had saved the halfling from the Divine Intervention, a holy guild that Glendale had stolen and escaped from. Malgorm had offered him the protection of the shadows long ago. The halfling only smiled with gleam of greed. The halfling, like most Striders had learn not to gain the attention of the demon. And the halfling was clever, he only bowed. Malgorm looked at the impish creature and handled him a letter.

"I promise this will be a profitable task." The wizard hinted. The halfling glowed in happiness at those words, and ran forward in excitement.
- Malgorm Azzothoth
