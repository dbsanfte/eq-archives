Journal One: Beginings
Malgorm's birth
 
The Plague is Born

Deep in the darkness of the sewers of Qeynos, a wizard stood staring into the water at his reflection. Gazing at a face that he did not know, a body he had never seen, with a name he did not understand. All he felt was an aching of rage and despair. A void that ate him inside- a hunger of unimaginable pain. Then he heard it: a "squeak" coming from some small rat hugging the damp wall. Malgorm turned in rage at life and the hunger shot forth from his finger tips. He watched as the rat's blood froze in pain and the slow death the animal experienced somehow gave him comfort from the hunger.

The timid natives of the sewers cringe as an unnatural scream fills the darkness, "I need more !!!!! Do you hear me?! More!!!!" Then Malgorm turns back to the watery refection of himself. Not for one moment did he consider where the power came from or how he had did it, only that he must use it again to feed his hunger for death. Looking up, Malgorm saw a man in deep purple robes with dark eyes and hair. A young man of wicked ambition. "My name is Terreon Callust. Follow me, Bertoxxulous calls you to his temple."

The first word Malgorm understood was Bertoxxulous, as that was the name of the hunger.

The Reflection of a Soul

The poor beggar ran to the wall. He could hear the monster so near; he had only wanted a copper to get some cheap ale, but that man was no man. The beggar turned the corner, and felt the cold steel of a blade cut into him from the side. Blood poured onto the sewer floor. The beggar watched in terror as the man in the purple robes spoke a word- "Bertoxxulous" -and fire burst forth. The beggar screamed in pain as his skin blistered. At the edge of death he looked up, whimpering "what are you and why…." The man smiled evilly and tossed a copper to the beggar. He turned to walk away. The beggar gagged in pain and relief; believing his life spared. That realization left as soon as it came, with a dark sword swiftly placed in his skull.

"Malgorm," said a voice from the shadows.

Malgorm turned to see a man in dark leather with the same face as his own. A stunning female dark elf was by the man's side. He spoke again, "My brother, I am Lord Faileas." Malgorm smirks in understanding as the knowledge floods his mind.

Faileas continued, "This is Envy. She is my apprentice, and will aid you while you grow to power. We have much to do." Envy only smiled at Malgorm, appreciating the display of the evil he had just shown. What terror they would bring….

The Soulless Beast "Sugan"

Hours had passed. Envy spoke many truths to Malgorm as they hunted the sewers for blood. She taught him there was more than the blind rage; she taught him the power to control others. A single lesson he would never forget- if a being wants something, then they could be controlled. And everyone wanted something. The words of her lessons echoed across his soul; Envy had given him his greatest gift. But it was time for him to leave the sewers. Envy was to show him secret ways in and out of the city to be safe from those who wanted to stomp the virus before it infected the world. Malgorm waited to meet Lord Faileas outside the city gates, so Envy left him there.

For the first time the sun touched Malgorm, beating down upon the being's skin and burning his eyes like the pain of birth. He was born from the sewers; it was his mother. Making his way to the gates of the city, Malgorm saw Faileas in the distance. To his shock a blade was swung at him. A group of well armed knights stood by as a female with red hair fought Lord Faileas in an epic battle. As he drew closer, a rage entered Malgorm. He knew he was here on this man's behalf to protect to advise him… and to kill that woman. Then the voice came. Malgorm heard her thoughts; the love she felt, the passion and life. She was a divine storm. A storm that gave Malgorm power. As he stood over the defeated Lord Faileas, he felt his pain and knew that would be the last time she would ever best him. In despair, Sugan felt Malgorm's thoughts and his will for the first time as well. Malgorm blasted her with all his will and magick but like a goddess she was immune, merely looking confused. Already in emotional turmoil for hurting her brother, she seemed stunned seeing Faileas in another man.

Malgorm fled to his mother the sewers, the place where they could not get to him. The power he had just gained- it all fit into place. In her rage and despair over her brother, Sugan let Malgorm in. It all fit; it was suppose to happen. Her victory here was her defeat and he knew it. The hunger told him so.
