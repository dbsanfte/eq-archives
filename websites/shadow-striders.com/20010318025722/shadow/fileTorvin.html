Torvin
Barbarian Rogue
 
History: Barbarian's they call us, and think us thick whited, such is the way of civilised lands and town folk.

They cower behind their locked doors in fear of the dark and the terrors it brings. Not so I. Make light of their jibes and their taunts, laugh as you asses their worth. When night falls so does the façade.

With morning comes horror and panic, the fat merchant grasping under his pillow for gold no longer there, the Lord runs from his bedroom in robes with sword in hand to fight a foe no longer there.

And I? I walk the street and listen to their woes... "Torvin, they came in the night and took all!"

"What evilness do you speak of my fine merchant friend?" I reply, fighting to keep the smile from my face "What cruel men could treat one of you stature so unjustly?" "You Barbarians are simple folk" they say "I wish such simple honesty was more common place in our fare city, though I could never get used to the animal skins" They laugh at me to cheer themselves and rush off to tell the watch. I walk away and laugh at such city naivety, civilisation how foolish.
