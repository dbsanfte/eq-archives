October 04/98

Just saw this on EQ Vault and thought I should share it with those that may not have seen it. The post is about experience and loot.

I have heard that the kill of a monster goes to the character inflicting the most damage upon it,
and that the loot goes to the one delivering the final blow. My question is what do you get for the
kill? Is it the guy who gets the kill that gets the exp.? Or is that only if the characters fighting the
monster is not in a group? Or does the guy with the kill simply get more exp. than his fellow
group members? Some answers to this would be greatly appreciated.

-New To EQ


Alrighty, here's how this stuff works.

*Player not in a group*

When an NPC is killed, the player that did the most damage to it receives the experience and is
able to loot the NPC. No one else can loot it. No one else gets the exp. Example, player A has
been beating on a gnoll for some time. The gnoll is almost dead. Player B comes along and
delivers the final blow. Player B receives neither the exp. nor the ability to loot. Player A receives
the exp. and may loot.

*Player in a group*

The difference here is that the group benefits from what a player within that group does. Thus, if
a player in a group does the most damage to an NPC and the NPC is killed, the experience is
split amongst the group. Also, anyone in that group may loot the corpse, not just the specific
player that did the most damage.

** Please note that these are player vs. NPC scenarios, not pvp.

-Brad
