Raid Rules

Raid Behavior Requirements:

1: BE ON TIME! 15 minutes early is even better.

1a. Have fun.

2. Transportation to the raid is your responsibility. Guild members might be available to help you get to the raid, but don't rely on that. Plan on printing your own maps and paying for your own ports to ensure you get to the raid.

3. Send a tell to the staffing lead upon your arrival. Always carry a stack of peridots on you. The RL may decide everyone has to donate 5 or 10. Be patient during prep...MA, SA and groups *will* be announced. If you have to leave before a raid ends, send a tell to the staffing lead, do not just poof.

4: Never use auto attack. Make an /assist hotkey and hit it only AFTER the MA (Main Assist) calls his target. NO ONE should be attacking, shooting arrows at, casting on, or taunting the mob before the MA calls for assist. The first 10 seconds of the fight are the most important, and the MA must be given time to build some agro.

5: If you're not willing to listen to the RL (Raid Leader) and pay attention, don't bother coming. The RL is always right. If you do not agree with something done on the raid, you are free to lead your own raid and do it your own way on your own raids. Be a good follower, or risk being asked to leave if you cannot.

6: Do not wander, not even a little bit. If you wander you will agro mobs and they will kill you then move on to the group. Boredom is not an excuse for running circles around the group during downtime, and makes buffing harder for some classes. If you get antsy, step away from the computer and do jumping jacks or something. Do not move from the camp.

7: Evac is called only by the RL, MA, or pullers. Don't ask questions - if the RL calls camp, do it. If the RL calls "cleric camp", everyone else fight to the death to make sure those clerics get camped. If they don't call it, you don't do it. This applies to gate too. If you gate out of a raid to avoid death, you may not be able to return to the raid. Simple logistics.

8: Don't Heal/Buff/Bandage the pullers unless asked. Usually they have agro'd other mobs and by healing/buffing them agro may be transferred to you, and the raid party..

9. If the chanters call an unmezzable mob, the SA should tank it (and call it) and keep it's attention until other mobs are being killed. If we're raiding a place with many unmezzable mobs, all tanks should be aware and prepared to tank mez as needed.

10. Feel free to chitchat and joke around in /say and have a good time, but please refrain from annoying spam like eating messages, assist messages, and fake roll messages, etc.

11. Keep your tells to the Raid leader at a minimum. Ask questions of your group leader or class mates but the RL has a lot of stuff on his mind. Don't bother him with minute details or smalltalk.

12. No pets until the RL calls for them. If we are in a place where you feel you can safely control your pet, feel free to ask if pets are allowed.

13. Expect to lose xp during a raid, not gain some. Control your agro to prevent summoning by mobs and to keep them from pingponging around from person to person. Do not overburn, taunt, snare/root etc in an effort to gain xp.

14. Be patient during CR after wipeouts. Be prepared to reimburse the cost of your coffin if we need to summon corpses out. (80pp or 180pp, depending who summons)

15. Always be aware of mob push. Get on another side of the mob and push the other way if need be, especially when you see a mob being pushed towards a cliff, ledge, wall, etc.

16. Familiarize yourself with the guild's Loot Guidelines. Keep the guild's best interests in mind when rolling.

17. All pulls should have an accompanying pull message to warn the raid of imcoming mobs.

18. If you are sitting during pulls and the last 3 mobs went straight for you when they got to camp, pssst...stand during pulls.

19. Remember - if you want people to show up to raids that drop your stuff, you have to show up to the raids that drop theirs.

20. Cannot be stressed enough:
Do only what you are told.
Take no action you are not told to take.

If you can follow these guidelines and pay attention, we will all benefit and have fun.

Raids require more discipline than a normal group situation. Raid leaders keep their raid members alive by knowing exactly what is going on at all times. If you are doing things that he/she does not expect, you are putting the raid in danger. If the RL says no pets, kill your pet IMMEDIATELY and don't ask why. It is things like that that will make raids the most profitable and fun.

Groobash
