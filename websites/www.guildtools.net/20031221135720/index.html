Guildtools Network - Newsletter - 11-14-2003
Hello Everyone!

I wanted to take this opportunity to go over some of the tools that are available to you through the website and also discuss some projects that have been sitting on the back burner for a bit. I would also like to request feedback from you on current tools and encourage you to request others that are not currently offered. Finally, I have a personal request that some or many of you may be able to help me fulfill.

As many of you know, the Guildtools site has been around for a few years now. I'm glad to say most of the tools that I offer have been received favorably by the majority, with minimal death threats and hate mail. =) As always, the site has been running free and without banners or pop-up ads since day one, something you won't find many sites doing out of their own generosity in this age of information overload.

EQ Crafters
This is my most recent (completed) project. It allows the players of Everquest to add their characters tradeskill abilities to a database that can then be searched sorted and sautéed (ok, well... you can't eat it, but 2 out of 3 isn't bad!) Some people add their characters for fame seeking out the elusive 1750 club, others add their character to increase their fortune. No matter the reason, I'm proud to announce we have over 750 very skilled crafters in the database now. I do hope we can break a thousand before the new year, so spread the word to your friends and guilds! http://eqcrafters.guildtools.net

RDB
Roster Database. This was the first tool the site ever had, and after many revisions it's finally gotten to the point where another version would be pointless. RDB is currently only available for hosting. But the 50 guilds and 2,800 members who use it don't seem to mind! =) Setting up the RDB is painless and instant, anyone can do it. http://rdb.guildtools.net

Forums
There is room for maybe 1 or 2 more large guild forums, or 5 or 6 small guild forums. So, if your guild is looking for a free forum for its members then sign up quickly. Forum requests are also created instantly, so it's fcfs. All forums must be for online gaming use only. No exceptions. Currently there are 14,500+ posts in 15 forums with 700+ members. http://forums.guildtools.net

Articles
One of the least used resources on the site is the Articles section. There are many good articles in this area that a guild could and should take advantage of. You are able to vote on articles, and those that drop below a 2 in rating will be removed form the site. If you would like to submit an article to the site please send it in an email to grim@guildtools.net. http://articles.guildtools.net

The Backburner
A few projects have been sitting in the wings for a while now. Any feedback (positive or negative) on these would really help me get these things out or trash them for good. DKP - Quite a few guilds in EQ use DKP to keep track of raids and member attendance at them. There is already one very good source for a DKP program http://www.eqdkp.com but that site does not host the database for you. My version would be hosted by the site but not available for download. IDB - Item Database. This would be similar to RDB, except it would be used for... items. This is one of those projects that I could easily finish on a rainy weekend, but I'm just not sure how many guilds would have a use for one of these. Signature Hosting - I really doubt I will bring this back, it's just a massive bandwidth hog. If I did, it would add a small hosted by tag to it much like strike9 does. Site Hosting - I've toyed around many times with the idea of making a portal type system for guilds much like guildportal and umm that other one do. Except, mine would be without all the banners and costs and it would actually look nice. (no offense) If you think this would be a good idea, give me a push.

Personal Request

I'm looking to work on projects ranging from a few hours, to several weeks, from simple html or banners to backend databases that house a large amount of information. ALL LABOR WILL BE FREE. Minus any hosting costs your company may incur. I'd be glad to speak with you on what I think a reliable host should provide.

Why would I do this? I'm looking to add a few things to my resume that will soon be floating around out there again. So, drop me a line and let's talk about what I can do for you. You will not be obligated to use the end product, and you will be consulted through all phases of development.

Until next time,

Guildtools Network - Oops! - 11-13-2003
Site was down for about a day and a half. Sorry about that.
It seems my newsletter tripped an internal failsafe on the host which caused it to suspend my account. Apparently you can't send out more than 1000 emails within a 30 minute period or you are a spammer =(
We got it all worked out now, and I can send out the other 1100 newsletter emails soon. =P
Very sorry about the downtime for those of you who do currently use the tools. I'm sure you know it is not a common scenario. My host has been pretty damn reliable for the past 2 years, and this was my mistake. (even though I have never seen and still can't find this stipulation in the Acceptable Use Policy anywhere)
Anyway, the site is back up and looks to be in working order. Thank all of you for the feedback on the Newsletter so far!

IDB - Item Database - 11-05-2003
Going to finish up the Item Database I started today while bored at work over the next few days. It will allow a person to add items and specify certain values and also make certain items private so only guildies can see them when they type in the guild password.
It will have very basic password protection and only 3 generic user levels. Admin - Full control. Guild Member - View all items. Guest - View all items that aren't private.
If you have any feedback on this when you see the demo, please hurry and send it while I still feel the urge to work on this.
