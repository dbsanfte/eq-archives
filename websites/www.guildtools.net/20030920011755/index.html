EQCrafters - Feedback - 06-28-2003
Thanks for all the feedback so far. I'll try to answer some of your questions and concerns here for all to see.

"For the research indicator to be useful, you'll need to include class, since, say, wizard cannot research chanter only spells."
Yea, that was a slight oversight on my part. I've added a class field now. If you are an Enc, Mag, Wiz, or Nec there will be a letter by your name in the research column so that viewers will not have to look at your profile to see what class you are.

"You might also include a check box, like the trophies one, for folks who have a skill-mod item since it would add +3-20% to their posted skill."
I've been asked to add this several times. Here's why it probably won't happen: This site is mainly to show off a crafters acheivements. I would rather not include items to your skill that weren't made or obtained with your own skill. For example, geerlocks and the skill modifier items form the boss mobs in luclin. If you wish to let people know you have these, that's what your notes section is for.

"If there could be some way to specify alt's skills and tie them to the main's char, that would be helpful."
This is more of a design 'feature' (read: flaw).
When I first started making this, I was certain no one in their right mind would want to slave away at tradeskills on more than one character! Boy, was I wrong. It's not a majority, but apparently some of you crazy folk have multiple crafters. I didn't consider the older crafters that wanted to go over 200 in many skills but didn't have the option of aa =(.
So, let's consider this a design 'feature'. I'm not going to have the time to modify the script to allow multiple characters per account. So what I propose you do is just make one character and add your skills to him/her, with a note stating that "No, this Ogre doesn't have a tinkering skill of 153. That would be my Gnome Snakfurphatty" or... something.

Please keep the feedback coming. It'll only make the site better. (hopefully)
