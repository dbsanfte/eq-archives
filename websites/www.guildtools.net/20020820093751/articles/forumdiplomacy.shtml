Forum Diplomacy

This seems like a very good time for a guide on how to make decent posts and how to avoid becoming a flamer or the target of a flamer.

Talk is silver, silence is golden
Possibly the most important thing to remember is that you will need to have a reason for making a post, if you don't know what you intend to say, what opinion you are looking to express then there is very little point in making a post in the first place. I have deleted countless posts before I hit the 'Submit Post' button myself and I assume that many others have done so too.

In the same vein is Silence is its own answer, bear in mind that just because someone has made a post addressed to you is not a requirement for you to answer it. Before you make a reply ask yourself if you really need to do so. Often, you want to get a certain point of view across to the potential readers of the post in question, but notice that it is very possible that anyone who might read the post to which you were considering making a reply might very well form the very same opinion of the original poster that you wanted to get across.

Also consider that if someone launches an offensive post at you, by replying to it you are making said poster aware that you take notice of his post and are touched by it. If you were to simply not reply to it then there is a great likelihood that it will simply fall into obscurity without being greatly noticed by the average reader. Realize the power of publicity and use it to your advantage, if you constantly make replies to an attack on you, you are in effect making sure that people notice that attack, which is not really what you want is it?

"Luke, many of the truths we cling to depend greatly on our own point of view"
When making a post of any sort you are in effect expressing your own personal opinion on the matter (unless your post is scientifically proven) and you would do well to keep in mind that just because you hold said opinion is no guarantee that everybody else will hold the same opinion. It then falls to your readers to respect the opinion that you put forward as being your point of view, and it likewise becomes your responsibility to regard any replies to your post as the personal opinions of others. So don't be surprised if people disagree with you.

The English language has a lot of words synonymous with hate Be well aware that what a person might intend when writing a post might not be the same as the impression another person gets when reading the post.

It is possible that you write a post in a particular tone or frame of mind, but it is almost certain that your reader will not read it in the same tone or state of mind. So in order not to needlessly cause animosity where it is not intended, make sure that when writing your post you do so in a way that leaves as little as possible to implicated or insinuated. This includes the various sorts of snide comments. It is very easy to include a snide comment or crack a joke at someone’s expense without realizing that the person that suffers the bunt of your joke can read the board just as well as you. So avoid the "You're a joke just like so-and-so" type of wisecracks, for they will return to haunt you. No matter how well you write, if you say something negative about someone then that person will take offense.

Many people have a tendency to write as they would speak, but the danger there is that very few people read the same way as they speak.

Lastly, if you read an offensive post directed against someone else, you might want to resist the urge to jump in and join the flaming against either person unless you have a good reason to do so.

-Elhimac-
