Article I
Amici Loci Antiqui formed to insure the ancient tradition of friendship survived within the chaotic world of Norrath. We are a community that supports one another and strives to promote diversity within Norrath. As a guild, quality and excellence are our primary goals. Amici Loci Antiqui is comprised of people dedicated to improving the guild, Norrath, and ourselves. Each of us contributes a unique and interesting aspect of ourselves to the guild.

Resolved, that the guild shall work for the betterment of its membership, in the areas of experience gain, equipment acquisition, quest completion, and most importantly, an enjoyable game-playing experience.

Article II
Membership

A. No character shall be inducted into the guild that has not reached the twentieth level of experience.

B. Guilding of alternate characters shall be limited to those that the player has a serious intent in persueing. At any one time, members may have one guilded alternate character that is below the level of twenty. Upon reaching level twenty, any alternate may be considered to be a full member of the guild. There shall be no limitation upon the number of alternates above the level of twenty, except as provided above.

C. New members may apply by posting a message on the Applications Board, including the information listed below, and shall be voted upon by two-thirds majority (2/3) of Council Members upon the completion of their probationary period.

1. The Recruitment Officer shall be responsible to insure that the inductee is familiar and competent with his/her character, that the inductee knows proper game-play, including, but not limited to:

a. Appropriate group participation.
Inductees should group as appropriate for their class. Caster classes should not act as tanks, tanks should defend weaker group members, healers should buff and heal.

b. Appropriate zone presence Inductees should investigate which zones are appropriate for their level of experience, and not attempt to play in excessively dangerous zones.

2. The Recruitment Officer shall instruct the inductee to visit the guild's website, register with the bulletin boards there, read the guild bylaws, and submit an application in the open board area containing the information listed below.. Failure to do this will be interpeted as a lack of interest on the inductee's part.
a. Information required for application:

  1. Character's First and Last Name, if applicable
  2. Character's Race, Class and Level.
  3. Mastered Trade Skills, if any.
  4. Listing of previous guild membership(s) and reasons for leaving, if applicable.
  5. Expections from membership in ALA.
  6. Time of day you usually play, in EST

3. The Recruitment Officer will post a new thread on the journeyman board. This thread will include the full name, level, class, and race of the character. The day the thread is posted will begin the thirty (or more) days of the trial period.

a. The Recruitment Officer is also responsible for finding an officer to give the guildtag to the inductee if he is not able to do so, so they may learn to function within the thirty day period.

D. Newly inducted members shall be probationary members for a period of not less than 30 days.

E. Inductees shall be unguilded at the time of recruitment. It is not the policy of this guild to "steal" guild members.

F. PVP - The guild does not accept player killers into its ranks. Dueling for fun or honor is not a problem. If one aimlessly challenges people to duel as a form of harassment; they will be dealt with.

G. Member Removal

1. We encourage people to settle differences amongst themselves. In some cases one may feel that they wish to express their grievances anonymously; this preference will be respected. In such cases the council will review each case individually, grievances can be submitted to the Guild Leader or Recruitment Officer in written form and sent by email.

2. Cases shall be investigated by members of the Council by questioning any involved parties and the results of which shall be reported to the Council. Given the nature of these investigations and discussions, any action shall take as much time as required and shall not be made in haste.

3. Removal of a member or recruit shall require a two-third (2/3) vote in favor of such an action. Any removal will be final.

4. The Guild Leader shall reserve the right to overrule in cases where it is felt the decision will injure or undermine the guild.

Article III
Raids

A. In the event of guild raids, players should make every effort to assure their charactors are at the staging area prior to the scheduled start of the raid.

B. In the event a teleportation is required, players are expected to be at a teleport location appropriate for their teleporter (i.e. Druid Ring for druids, Wizard Spire for wizards)

C. Players are expected to familarize themselves with the raid zone prior to the raid by visiting EQAtlas or an equivialent, viewing descriptions and printing maps of raid area, so that to lessen the chance of an ill-timed death.

D. Raids, staging areas, and posible group formation will be posted on the guild web site when scheduled.

Article IV
Prohibited
As we are all ambassadors for our guild, the following activities are prohibited:

A. Begging
1. Given the minimum required level for membership, guild members should be reasonably well equiped for their level. Begging, either guild members or others, for equipment or cash is prohibited. Groups and raids will be organized to further member's needs. Players are expected to participate in these groups and raids if they expect to profit from them.

B. Requesting Powerleveling

1. A higher level character may, at his or her discretion, powerlevel another character, however, requests for powerleveling are prohibited.

C. Kill Stealing

1. In no event shall non-accidental kill stealing be tolerated.

D. Camp Stealing

1. While Verant doesn't not recognize camping a location, players must be flexible when encountering camp conflicts. In any event, it shall be considered honorable on the member's part to respect other's camps.

2. Arguing over camp locations shall be prohibited, as Norrath is large enough to provide players with a reasonable play experience in multiple areas.

E. Verant Rules

1. All Verant rules apply within the guild. Each member is responsible for understanding Verant policies.

Article V
Council Members
A. Council Members shall:
1. Visit guild boards daily so as to keep abreast of current discussions.

2. Keep discussions on the Council board within the Council.

3. Attend Council meetings held via AOL Instant Messenger.

4. Fulfill the roles and duties appointed to your station.

5. Know the bylaws of the guild and advise members as needed.

6. Report to the Guild Leader or First Officer any problem or pertinent information.

B. Voting

1. All Council members shall cast their ballot via Council board thread when a vote is called and seconded.

2. Votes shall be called for all journeymen upon the completion of their probationary period.

a. A two-thirds (2/3) majority of favorable votes shall admit a journeyman as a full member.

b. Less than two-thirds (2/3) favorable shall indicate an extended probationary period at the discretion of the Guild Leader.

c. Less than one-third (1/3) favorable shall indicate a rejection of the journeyman.

3. In the event a vote is called on the removal of a guild member, a two-thirds (2/3) majority shall be required to effect such removal.

C. Recruitment/Induction Officers shall:

1. Review all applications.

2. Keep track of entrance dates and probationary periods.

3. Submit applicatants to the Council for approval.

D. Raid Officers shall:

1. Schedule activities.

2. Monitor requests for help with quests or activities.

3. Research areas pertaining to these activities.

4. Announce via boards and MOTD any major events at least one week in advance, noting date, time, place, and level requirements for the event.

5. In the event of joint raids, coordinate with designated raid leader of allied guild.
