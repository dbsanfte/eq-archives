A Run Through the Woods (back)
by Tigguhr
(A Short Story that took 2nd place in a Guild of Heros Monthly Contest. Ole Tig plays with stream of consciousness writing.)

Half running, they moved through the trees, dwarven lad and elven maiden. One gliding, another barreling, both apprehensive. The Woods. Dread. Fear. Knowing that there would be trouble. Reaching the edge of the woods, they plunged.

Sweet, sticky smells, ignored. Cricket songs distract. Ale and wine untouched. Nerves on edge. Full hazy moon their only light, brave companions, working hard to avoid a fight. Barreling, the dwarf rolls into a roadside camp of giants. No fear showing...but on they run. Fight and become weak. Weak and unprepared. Unprepared means death in the woods.

Dodging roots, dodging stones, dodging creatures of the night. Near, near, near! So near is the crossroads. Crossroads of doom? Dark denizens praying on the traveler? Maybe. Creeping close, listening. All quiet. Serene. "Hmmph?" the dwarf asks. No crickets. Too quiet! A furrowed brow from one. A raised brow from the other. Step towards the bridge. Step. Step, knowing it will come. The avalanche. Bodies, so large. Fantastic! Horrid faces near invisible, high up in the night sky. Monstrous moans to rake your spine. An elven magic blast! Flames! Light! Darkness please, begs the dwarf. Charging forward, there is no other way! Brawn bulling forward, dwarven axe swings, HARD! Bury the smoking beasts quickly. Hard swings! Split them in two. An elven whistle. A call to get down. The dwarf tastes dirt and more fire roars. Most are dead now. Only one lurches.

And now a new foe. Many images of the same black figure fly across the bridge. Ambush! A road raider! Which one to attack? Shimmering, grinning, casting, the vile mage comes. Quick tingles, yet elf and dwarf shrug. No foul one! Not this day! Another vile spell! The dwarf stops. Maiden sees and casts back. Volleys traded. The air itself burns and sizzles. Both stand fast. Dwarf unmoving, sees the lone giant. Near death, yet he comes for vengeance. Foul behemoth! Closer, closer as the light and dark casters sweat and toil, cursing each other.

Doomed dwarf. A frozen plaything soon. Giant bellows. He laughs though near death. "Eat you!" he slavers and reaches out. Dwarven axe, frozen in the air, drops slightly. Free, he holds until he feels the hot breath. "Death frum

Below!" The axe takes a leg. Giant tumbles, twitches, travels to the netherworld.

Wizards! Tired. Drained. Tossing mere sparklers. No victory here. The dwarf bellows. Black figure trys again. Dwarf shrugs. Black figure barks, yet retreats. Off into the night. Elven maiden slumps, but smiles. Dwarf winks. Brandishes mugs. A small keg! The maiden laughs then refuses. Laughs then accepts. Amazing ale! Axepeak ale. Feeling better. Ready to move.

Again, half running, they moved through the trees, dwarven lad and elven maiden. One gliding, another barreling, both apprehensive. Onward, through the woods. Run again, run again, stay alive. To make Berun. Safety. The Inn. Quiet, unassuming, yet safe. Rest now. The next road. The next road will be harder. Drink. Laugh. But remember. Death is close. A whisper, a breath away. And thank your god! Thank your god for your companion. Not of blood, but common purpose. And respect. A furrowed brow. A raised brow. One slight smile and one "Harumph!"
