Talking like a Dwarf (back)

Listening to dwarves talk amongst themselves has been described as listening to "a bunch of seagoing Scotsmen". This is at least partially accurate. If you attempt to mix Irish and Scottish accents and blend in the kind of accent you hear in a bad pirate movie you'll come pretty close to talking like a dwarf from Clan Axepeak.

There are some specific things to keep in mind when trying to talk like a dwarf which will make the process easier.

  • No & Not - Use "nae" instead of "no" or "not"
  • The & To - "The" becomes "tha". "To" becomes "ta".
  • (-ing verbs) : Drop the last 'g' and replace it with an apostrophe. Replace "is", "am", or "are" with "be". (Human - "I am going to the temple." Dwarf - "I be goin' ta tha temple.")
  • Don't, Didn't - Use "dinna" instead of "don't" or "didn't"
  • Can't, Couldn't - Use "kinna" instead of "can't" or "couldn't"
  • Should, Could & Would - Drop the "ld" off of these words and add an apostrophe. i.e. - "should" becomes "shou'"
  • Ken - The dwarven word "ken" can be used to mean either "can" or "know".
  • You - If used as an object in a sentence, use "ya". If used as the subject, use "ye". Examples : "I be goin' ta kill ya!" "Ye be sittin' on me helm!"
  • My - Dwarves never say "my". Use "me" instead.
  • Night - Use "noche" instead of "night".
  • Morning - Use "morn" instead of "morning".
  • Magic - Some dwarves use the word "magix" instead of "magic". This is an archaic form, and many dwarves have dropped it.
(NOTE:This speech guide evolved completely through player interaction, not through any kind of research.)

Keep in mind that if you prefer that your dwarf not speak this way it is entirely your choice. It is suggested that if you choose a different dialect for your dwarven character that you create some role playing basis for this, such as being adopted by non-dwarven parents or being born on a different continent.
