Personal Enemies (back)

Every dwarf in Clan Axepeak has the opportunity to declare a Personal Enemy. Once a personal enemy is declared, several changes must take place in the dwarf's behavior:

  1. When the personal enemy is encountered in battle, it will be attacked to the exclusion of all other enemies until utterly destroyed.
  2. The types of attacks against the personal enemy will be, in order of preference, hand to hand attacks, missile attacks, attacks through spells or other magic when no other attacks are possible.
Upon declaring a personal enemy a dwarf gains the title Hunter, with the name of the enemy preceding it. For example, a dwarf that declares Ogres as a personal enemy would be known as Ogre Hunter. Once the dwarf has completed certain requirements, he is then granted the title of Slayer and can resume normal behavior.

In order to gain the title of Slayer, a dwarf must personally slay twenty of his enemy. He may travel and fight with companions (this is in fact, recommended), but the twenty enemies must be dealt the killing blow by the dwarf personally. The twenty enemies need not be slain in a single battle.

The dwarf must keep a log of his battles while he strives for the title of Slayer. Every battle in which a dwarf slays one of his enemies must be recorded faithfully in the log. Each log entry should contain the date and exact location (area and location) of the battle, who the dwarf was travelling with at the time, and how many enemies were personally felled by the dwarf. The entry could be as simple as "6/12/99, Butcherblocks, crossroads, Traveled with Felnar, Emmrald, and Theldim. Killed Corflunk." The dwarf may choose to tell each log entry as a story, but this is not required.

Upon applying for the title of slayer, all relevant log entries must be submitted to the Clan Chief (Rorn Gor) and Clan Sub-Chief (Dun Gor), who will review the application.

For those enemies that have sub-categories (Giants, Dragons, etc.), a dwarf may choose only one type as a personal enemy, or may opt to choose all types. If all types are chosen, the dwarf must slay at least one of each type. For example, if a dwarf chooses all orcs as a personal enemy, a Pawn, Centurion, and legionnaire orc, (depending on level) etc. must be included in the twenty orcs killed by the dwarf.

If the dwarf dies during a battle, no enemies killed during that battle count toward gaining the title of Slayer.

Traditional dwarven enemies include orcs, ogres, goblins, trolls, giants, kobolds, duergar, and drow. Any form of undead can be added to this list as well, because they are just nae natural. A dwarf may only choose one personal enemy or one group of personal enemies at a time.

The enemy must meet the following guidelines:

  1. It must be a dangerous enemy with the potential of killing a small band of dwarves of more-or-less equal level. (Assume a band of 3 dwarves.)
  2. It must be able to be found somewhere within the game.
  3. A rationale must be provided for the enemy; i.e. - some reason that your dwarf wishes to exterminate all monsters of this variety.
