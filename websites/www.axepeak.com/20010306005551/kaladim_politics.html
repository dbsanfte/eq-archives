The Politics and Personalities of Kaladim (back)

This document is intended to help writers understand the political underpinnings of Kaladim, allowing everyone to write within a consistent framework about the dwarven city. It will discuss the various factions at work in the city, important personages, and the most pressing concerns that occupy the thoughts of the leaders of the city.

Important People of Kaladim

This rundown of important NPC's in Kaladim is by no means comprehensive, and will no doubt be added to as time passes. It currently includes the most prominent political figures in and around the city. Future additions will include key shopkeepers and other NPC's that can be found within Kaladim and The Butcherblock Mountains.

King Kazon Stormhammer

Kazon Stormhammer is the King of Kaladim. As such, he is by far the most important and powerful dwarf in the city. All clan chiefs and guildmasters bend their knee to Kazon.

Datur Nightseer

Datur Nightseer is the guildmaster of the Paladin's Guild. As is evidenced by the mighty fortress the paladins call their home, Underfoot Hall, the Paladin's Guild wields tremendous influence in Kaladim. They have a close working relationship with the clerics of Underfoot Cathedral.

Priestess Ghalea

The head cleric of Underfoot Cathedral, Priestess Ghalea's words are carefully considereed by all who hear them. Being Brell Serellis' most prominent representative in Kaladim gives her a unique standing in the city. Even Datur Nightseer and King Stormhammer pause before ignoring her council.

Furtog Ogrebane

Furtog is the guildmaster of The Stormguard, the warrior's guild of Kaladim. While not as influential in political matters as either Datur Nightseer or Priestess Ghalea, he none the less is taken very seriously as the leader of the most powerful fighting force in the region. Anyone who has seen the Kaladim Guards in action know they are a force to be reckoned with.

Mater

Mater is the guildmaster of the local Rogue's Guild. While few people actually like the Rogue's Guild, they recognize the nescessity of it. While Mater has no official political influence, he often makes his opinions and wishes known through "unofficial" channels. Since he controls the single most powerful intelligence network in Kaladim, his opinions are listened to carefully, if not openly. Surprisingly to some, Mater has little love for The Butcherblock Bandits. The bandits operate outside the blanket of The Rogue's Guild's approval, and in fact are considered by the guild to be an indirect threat. Since they attack travellers indiscriminately they are bad for business, and when business suffers the Rogue's Guild suffers. As Mater is fond of saying, "Don't pick your mark's pockets too dry, or he won't be able to refill them."

There are persistent rumors that The Miner's Guild is merely another front for The Rogue's Guild. While there are some indications that this may indeed be true, nothing has been confirmed thus far.

Nyzil Bloodforge

Leader of the reknowned mercenary band The Bloodforge Brigade, Nyzil Bloodforge may well be one of the most skilled warriors of Faydwer, and perhaps among the best in all of Norrath. While he has little, if any, political influence, his band of warriors is considered one of the finest in the region.

"Peg Leg"

The leader of the Butcherblock Bandits is known simply as "Peg Leg". He is a rogue of tremendous skill, and should not be taken lightly by any who face him. While the other bandits are not nearly as formidable as he, in groups they can be quite dangerous to the unwary. The Dunfires and the Smeltpots are two clans that seem to have thrown in their lot with the Butcherblock Bandits - any dwarves of these clans should be dealt with cautiously.

The Kaladim Guards

The Kaladim Guards can be found in many places in the Butcherblock Mountains and, of course, within Kaladim itself. Following are the names of a number of the guards that can be used in fiction to lend an air of consistency to any writing about Kaladim and Butcherblock:

Political Concerns of Kaladim

Few people will argue that there are two main threats to Kaladim at the current time : the goblins of Clan Runnyeye, and the undead.

The undead are by far the simplest, if not nescessarily the least dangerous, problem. They seem to spawn from the ground itself. Butcherblock is one of the few regions in which the undead can be found during the daylight hours, which has led many to speculate that some dark necromantic power is at work in the regions. More than one ruin has been said to be permanently inhabited by dwarven skeletons, something the paladins of Underfoot Hall have sworn to put an end to. Despite their best efforts, however, the undead problem persists.

Clan Runnyeye has been a threat for some time now, and as time passes the threat only grows. The goblins have actually managed to establish a camp disturbingly close to Kaladim, in spite of frequent attacks by the dwarves that seem to wipe it out on a regular basis. The difficulty, it seems, is that the goblins breed with a remarkable speed, and nobody has yet discovered where exactly it is that they're breeding. Fortunately, only the whelps have thus far been foolish enough to wander near the gates of Kaladim itself, and they are readily dispatched by passing dwarves and the guards themselves.

Recent reports tell of aqua goblins, the water dwelling cousinns of the goblins of Runnyeye, making ocassional appearances in Butcherblock. They are generally considered to be more dangerous than the land dwelling variety of goblin, and the fear is that Clan Runnyeye is forming some sort of alliance with them. Such an alliance would represent a fundamental balance of power in the region, something King Stormhammer is justifiably concerned about.

Adding to these difficulties is the recent incursion of the orcs of Clan Crushbone into Butcherblock. Previously the orcs had been considered a strictly elven problem, as Emperor Crush has thus far deployed all of his legions into the Greater and Lesser Faydarks. Lately, however, orc runners have been spotted along the road to Greater Faydark, presumably carrying messages of some sort. The incredible speed of these runners makes them difficult to intercept, so thus far little is known about their overall purpose. Travellers have also reported seeing orc oracles in the region, and they may have already established a camp of sorts in one of the more remote areas of Butcherblock. There are also rumours of dark elves seen coming and going from Crushbone Castle. Of all possible alliances, this one frightens King Stormhammer the most.

The Butcherblock Bandits, of course, remain a constant thorn in the side of The Stormguard. While they had, until know, been content to remain within their camps and conduct their "business" quietly, lately the bandits have been spotted on the roads attacking travellers. One bandit by the name of Qued has been particularly active, although the Dunfires have been seen around as well. Fortunately for all concerned, Peg Leg himself has not yet shown his face outside of the camps. In these dangerous times, very few dwarves consider the bandits to be "romantic". They are merely another threat to travel that needs to be dealt with. The Miner's Guild, in particular, wishes the bandits eliminated due to the threat they pose to local trade.

One final concern is the presence of two wandering ogres, often found in the vincinity of the crossroads. Of all the problems Kaladim faces, these two ogres are the least pressing. While they are indeed a danger, alert travellers are usually able to avoid them, or at least make it to the safety of the guards before they can attack. It seems unlikely these two represent any sort of intelligence gathering force from Oggok. Not only is Oggok on the other side of The Ocean of Tears, the concepts of ogres and intelligence gathering simply do not go together. They are, at worst, a nuisance that King Stormhammer is convinced will be dealt with in time.
