The History of Clan Axepeak (back)

by Nystramo

Introduction

Clan Axepeak, the dwarven guild in the game of Neverwinter Nights, was one of the most unique and unexpected organizations within that community. Most predicted that it would never enjoy a large membership, including its founders. Many, in fact, expected it to die out within a few months. In spite of this doomsaying, Clan Axepeak continues to not only exist, but thrive, and it is considered by many to be one of the finest sanctioned guilds in the game.

This history of Clan Axepeak will be presented in two halves. The first half will deal with the out of character (OOC) history, detailing how the guild came to be and the foundations of its philosophy. The second half will be the in character (IC) history, which will review the major events that took place on the message boards and in the game. Enjoy.

OUT OF CHARACTER HISTORY

A Dwarf Comes to Neverwinter

The very beginnings of Clan Axepeak can be traced to a single dwarf: Nystramo (later to adopt the full name of Nystramo Axepeak). That dwarf was, and is, me.

I first created the character Nystramo as something of a lark. I had been playing an evil cleric/Magic-User for some time (who still exists to this day), and had also played elves and half-elves. Deciding to try something entirely different, I thought a dwarf might be an interesting character.

The original Nystramo was a Fighter/Thief. I couldn't quite bring myself to abandon spell casting entirely. (Nystramo is now a pure Fighter, which I eventually decided was more in keeping with his character, and which was made more playable with the introduction of the potion shop, fine long bows, and the implementation of the dwarven constitution bonus.) After some time in the rolling room, I first stepped into the city of Neverwinter as a dwarf.

At this point two fairly pivotal decisions were made. First, I decided not to use the ubiquitous long sword as a weapon. It struck me as un-dwarvish, and if I was going to play a dwarf I was going to play it right! I decided on the battle axe eventually, though the hammer was also a serious consideration. The hammer finally lost, however, when it was discovered that fighters did not gain multiple melee attacks with them. It was then and there that the traditional dwarven distaste for "elfy" weapons was born.

The second decision was to speak with an accent. I honestly have no idea why I decided to do this, though I suspect that at the time I just thought it would be fun. The accent came out as vaguely Scottish with a bit of a pirate's edge to it. It was an instant hit with other players, and it helped me maintain Nystramo's character in the most trying of role playing circumstances. That accent remains the standard for dwarven speech to this day.

For quite a while Nystramo remained the only regularly played dwarven character in Neverwinter Nights. His generally grumpy demeanor, his taste for insulting elves and his occasionally biting sense of humor made him a popular companion with many players. He became moderately well known within a few months.

One particular activity served to increase his renown: PVP. I liked to throw Nystramo into the middle of PVP's in Port Llast (back when that was the PVP capital of NWN) just to see how people would react. He rarely survived, of course, but he did manage to cast a few successful Hold Monsters on the evil PVP'ers. His highly vocal participation, however, always guaranteed that he was noticed.

Enter Melkarris

After a time, another dwarf entered the game: Melkarris. Created by a player who was inspired by Nystramo's often eccentric behavior (eccentric by human standards at least), Melkarris was introduced as Nystramo's brother. Together they wandered the realms from end to end, refining what it meant to be a dwarf in Neverwinter.

It was during this period that two more aspects of the dwarven character were defined. The first was the dwarven love of ale. Melkarris and Nystramo would often relax at a local inn (often Mataga's, and it was at this time also that it became the traditional watering hole of dwarves) downing enormous quantities of ale while chatting with the other patrons.

The second was the dwarven distaste for elves. While Nystramo enjoyed belittling elves whenever he met them, Melkarris was positively cruel to them. It was he who truly established the enmity between the two races. While that enmity has mellowed in the ensuing years, many dwarves in the clan still refuse to treat elves with any degree of respect.

Melkarris' most significant contribution, however, was his suggestion that I form a dwarven guild. Although I was a bit reluctant due to the number of dwarven guilds that had started and failed in recent months (I can remember three, offhand) I eventually decided to try it anyway. A few days later, I opened The Society of the Stone folder with the dramatic description: "Yet another dwarven guild... hopefully longer lived."

And Patroc1us Makes Three

Perhaps a day or two after opening the folder, Melkarris and I ran into one of the truly legendary members of Clan Axepeak: Patroc1us. (For the record, his name is pronounced Patroclus, not Patrokeeus or Patroshus.) We met under Crossergate, I recall, and immediately laid into him for using a longsword like a "blasted elfy" and talking like a human. While he quickly picked up "proper" dwarven speech, the battle over his longsword was destined to be a lengthy one. (He eventually did trade it in for a proper dwarven short sword so he could keep his backstabs.)

Patroc1us' contributions to the Society of the Stone were many. He was the dwarf who first used manticore spines as weapons and who determined the best dwarven tactics for many of the more difficult battles in the game. As significant as both of these were, the most important contribution made by Patroc1us was the introduction of dwarven words to the clan vocabulary. Without Patroc1us such phrases as "sonn ultok", "findar torst", and "raugh ar dun!" may never have come to be.

The First Meeting

Shortly after the meeting with Patroc1us, it was decided that it was time to formalize what The Society of the Stone stood for. The three of us met in a private room and hammered out our charter, what we offered members, and what we expected of them.

What we offered was basically what any guild offered: camaraderie. We also offered hope for any player who thought that non-spell casters could not survive in the environment of NWN. Finally, we offered a solid role-playing environment, something which was sadly lacking in many of the current guilds.

What we expected was simple. We expected our members to role-play well and consistently. This meant more than simply remaining in character during a conversation. It meant accepting the limitations of dwarven culture, which in turn meant restrictions on weapon and armor selection. Long swords, two handed swords, and long bows were to be shunned, as well as using bracers and rings as primary means of defense. Our members would bear the arms and armor of dwarves: axes, hammers, crossbows, and true mail armor. While some (mostly Patroc1us) thought this too limiting at first, the final consensus was that we were not going to play dwarves halfway. Eventually, however, enough traditional dwarven weapons were identified to ensure both variety and utility in the selection.

Growing Pains

For quite some time it was only the trio of Nystramo, Melkarris and Patroc1usthat were regular dwarven players. Many dwarves came and went, but few stayed for any length of time. Membership was usually between 8 and 12 for most of that time and the prospects for any significant growth were few.

Slowly, a stable core of perhaps half a dozen members was established. These were all dedicated role players who were attracted by the Society's firm commitment in that area. Some were veteran players who had grown bored of the power wielded by Cl/MU's, while some were new players who simply liked the idea of playing a dwarf. One type of member was conspicuous in its absence, however: females.

The Society limped along in this fashion for many months. Most people expected it to die out in time, or at least to not grow anymore. It was during this period that the Clan Resource Files were created. They began as a simple file describing the various aspects of playing a dwarf in NWN, from both a practical and a role-playing standpoint. Over the years they have grown into roughly a dozen different files that cover dwarven language, culture, and religion, while also offering tactical advice, information on dwarven titles, and guidelines for posting on the Clan Axepeak message board. No new member of Clan Axepeak can ever say they lack information on the guild.

It was around the time that the Clan Resource Files were introduced that membership began to slowly pick up. Members were staying around longer, perhaps due to the solid grounding the files gave them in the clan. While membership never quite reached twenty, it was a more robust guild than it had been. We were finally getting noticed in the game, and dwarves were no longer quite as much an oddity as they had been.

The Missing Chief

Unfortunately, just as things were picking up, disaster struck. Due to personal reasons, I was no longer in the game, or even online. The clan was suddenly left without a chief, and it lost direction. During these months membership flagged, and clan activity almost stopped altogether. Things looked grim.

Fortunately, I returned before the Society's folder was eliminated, and the clan slowly recovered. In time it regained it's former strength, and there it remained for some time.

Homecoming

Eventually, we realized that, as a clan of dwarves, we needed a home, someplace in The Realms we could call our own. It was not unusual for guilds to claim ownership of a certain area in the game (such as the GOC laying claim to Longsaddle.) Earlier in Nystramo's career, I had hinted in a role-play with the Grey Company that the mountain found in the North Western Woods had been an ancient dwarven stronghold that had fallen during the war between the dwarves and ogres. So, in keeping with this information we decided to officially establish the clan's home in what was later dubbed "Lonely Mountain". Ritual quests were regularly held to cleanse the mount of the giant and drider infestations that seemed to continuously plague our mountain home. It was around this time, also, that the name of the guild was officially changed to Clan Axepeak, to more accurately depict the guild's clan structure and to achieve a more dwarven flavor. It was then that I officially added "Axepeak" as Nystramo's surname.

Ver 2.0

Finally, after years of player requests, the next upgrade of NWN was released. While this was a significant event for all players, of course, it held particular interest for the dwarves of Clan Axepeak. Rumors of enhancements such as dwarven clerics abounded. Unfortunately, most of the rumors proved untrue once players were allowed to beta test Ver 2.0.

What did not prove untrue was the increased difficulty of the game. In almost every area combats were made more difficult. In particular the Lost Hills had become particularly nasty, which made travel to Triboar a chancy proposition and Lonely Mountain itself had become significantly more deadly.

Many players predicted the demise of the dwarves at this point. Even humans were having difficulty with the new challenges the game presented, so few expected dwarves to survive at all. Unfortunately, it seemed this prediction would hold true as the dwarven presence in Neverwinter slowly dwindled.

Fortunately, the clan rebounded after a short time. Once we became acclimated to the new environment, we found new ways to travel. Fleeing battles became commonplace for dwarves, especially those traveling solo. New tactics were devised to defeat the new enemies that we faced. It was certainly not easy, but we learned to survive in a world that had been designed to bring the humans to their knees.

A New Chief and a New Area

Once again, it was time for me to vanish for a period. In my absence, Patroc1us took over the reigns of Clan Axepeak. This was the period that Clan Axepeak began its most impressive growth, and also encountered some of its most interesting controversies.

The growth was largely due to Patroc1us' dynamic leadership. He actively trained new members in areas such as tactics and the art of fleeing, helping them to survive without magic. Clan outings were regularly scheduled, allowing members to adventure with each other frequently. Many of the current clan policies involving membership, titles and elder positions were established while Patroc1us was chief of the clan.

Another factor that contributed to the clan's growth were the "fighter friendly" additions to ver 2.2. The potion shop in Westbridge, where one could buy potions of strength, speed and extra healing were particularly useful. Another help was the Lifegiver quest, which rewarded adventurers with Amulets of Healing, something no dwarf should be without. The new areas introduced were populated largely with NPC's that could not be easily eliminated with fireballs, a human's favorite weapon. For many of them, the best solution was a strong fighter with triple digit hit points. These areas were made to be laid low by a war party of dwarves.

The first controversy was brought about by version 2.2 of the game, which introduced, among other things, fine long bows to the game. Some dwarves immediately took to these weapons, since their value for a fighter was unquestionable. However, many dwarves (myself included) rebelled against them. We argued that bows were elven weapons, and no self respecting dwarf would use a weapon favored by elves. We stuck to the trusty manticore spine as our missile weapon of choice.

The debate went on for some time. The times were changing, though, and eventually the fine long bow was adopted as an acceptable dwarven weapon. Its usefulness simply could not be easily ignored. To resolve the discrepancy of dwarves using bows, I wrote a short story detailing how the fine long bow was actually a dwarven creation, not elven at all. Considering how this weapon emphasizes strength as well as agility, this is not an altogether unreasonable assumption.

The second debate revolved around the clan's sanctioning. Despite its high visibility both in the game and on the message boards, Clan Axepeak had not yet earned sanctioned status. This was due to the relatively low number of members in the guild when compared to other sanctioned guilds. While we were close to the number required, we always seemed to fall short by just a few members.

One of the solutions offered was to merge with the duergar. The duergar, for those who have not encountered them, are a small band of evil dwarves who live in the Underdark. While not true dwarves, they are very closely related. A few inspired players banded together to create some duergar characters, most notably Gimkur, their chief. For a short time they provided some of the best role played enemies the clan has ever seen.

Merging with the duergar was a controversial suggestion, at best. On the plus side was the help in gaining sanctioning, as well as the benefits of being able to work more closely with the duergar players in developing interesting plot lines. In addition, if Axepeak gained sanctioning and Crackskull (the duergar guild) did not, our folders would be seperated. This would discourage inter-guild role-play. If we were to share a folder, such interaction would be quite simple. On the negative side were the difficulties such a merger would present, such as who would be the GM of the guild, Patroc1us or Gimkur? Since there were so few duergar, would they be fairly represented? Finally, of course, there was the simple point of not wanting the two clans to become too "friendly", which was a risk if they were to merge. Finally, a vote was taken and the suggestion was overwhelmingly defeated. (For the record, I voted for the merger.) This was the first and only general guild vote ever held on any guild matter.

Shortly after this vote, however, the clan gained official sanctioning. This tremendous achievement is thanks almost entirely to Patroc1us, who worked relentlessly to expand our membership to the required size. The impact of sanctioning went much further than any had predicted. Almost immediately membership in the guild skyrocketed, and has been maintained at that level since. With sanctioning came one other benefit that none could have guessed: females.

Women With Beards

Women had been under-represented in Clan Axepeak since it's creation. The first female dwarf on record was Krishara, Nystramo's own fiancé. She was, unfortunately, not meant to last and after Nystramo's first disappearance she disappeared as well. She has returned twice since then, but did not remain for long either time.

There had been isolated female dwarves prior to sanctioning, but it was not until Axepeak became sanctioned that steady female dwarves came on the scene. While still largely under-represented, there are now a number of female dwarves who have become not only regular players, but are included among the clan elders and the chief's guard.
