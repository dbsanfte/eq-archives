Dwarven weddings are centered on the worship of Berronar. The two dwarves to be wed stand before an anvil, each with a ceremonial bronze hammer and two halves of a ring, one half of silver, the other of gold. Gathered are the dwarves' closest family and friends.

First the priest presiding over the ceremony asks if there are any challenges to this bonding. The male dwarf raises his hammer and looks to each of the other males present as if threatening them. The female does the same to the other females present. Assuming there are no challenges, the ceremony may continue. The couple then joins their silver half ring with their intended's golden half ring, forging two complete rings of silver and gold. The silver half is in honor of Berronar, the golden half symbolizes the future prosperity of the couple.

At this point the priest declares the joining complete, and every dwarf present consumes absurd quantities of ale. Absurd if you are not a dwarf, anyway.

DWARVEN WEDDING RITES

--Drums beat a muffled tatoo which echoes throughout the caverns of Kaladim-- --At the center of the great hall is a raised dais. An anvil rests upon the platform, and upon it rest two bronze hammers-- --Behind the anvil stands the Chieftains of Clan Axepeak, dressed in mithril armor-

--As the drums fade slowly into silence, the dwarven cleric begins to speak-- Kin, samman,... --Her dwarven words are quickly translated to common for the benefit of all guests-- Kin, friends, and honored guests, tonight we gather to perform one of the oldest and most sacred ceremonies of our culture. The forging of wedding rings represents an appeal by two dwarves to gain the blessing of the gods upon their union..... What two dwarves ask this blessing? Step forward and be known. BRIDE/GROOM: -steps forward- I do. --nods at bride and groom-- Do you both make this request freely and of your own will? BRIDE/GROOM Yes. --nods again--

Then take the ceremonial hammers. BRIDE/GROOM --takes hammer-- --looks up at audience-- Is there anyone here who dares to challenge this union? (brief pause) Very well. --places four half rings, two silver, two gold, on the anvil's surface-- The silver half-rings honor Berronar, dwarven goddess of love and the hearth. The golden half represents the future prosperity of the couple under the smiling face of Brell. If the gods approve of this union, the rings will join and the couple will be ensured love and prosperity throughout their lives. --looks back at bride and groom-- Kin, forge the rings of your union. BRIDE/GROOM-- Lass: "may the halves of our joining be equal and strong" -ting - Lad: "and may they never be separated" --ting-- (... forging sounds) --A blue glow emanates from the dissimilar metals as they are bonded into one ring-- --Holds up the newly forged rings, still bathed in a blue irridescence-- Let all be witness! The forging is successful! The gods in their wisdom have approved the union of bride and groom!

--Handing one ring to both bride and groom-- You may now exchange the rings and declare your love one for another. BRIDE/GROOM -vows- The hammer and the anvil are symbols of Moradin, creator of the dwarves known to Clan Axepeak. Tonight you have used his tools to forge something equally as wondrous and lasting. As the rings have been joined, so too have your souls been joined. The two are now one. The bond you have forged has been sealed by the gods, and so can never be broken in this life. -smiles- Kin, we dwarves are creatures of the stone. Let your love be a reflection of the constant and everlasting strength we derive from this stone in which we live, and let it always be living and growing, as the stone which lives and grows around us. I declare you now and evermore a couple, joined for as long as you shall both live. -turns to groom- You may kiss the bride. BRIDE/GROOM -kiss- The end.
