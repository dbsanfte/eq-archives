Dwarves and Ale (back)

There are those that say dwarves love ale more than life itself. While the dwarven love affair with ale may not be that strong, drinking ale is unquestionably one of the favorite dwarven pass times.

Clan Axepeak stores their ale in 30-gallon casks, which is probably enough to get a party of dwarves through a single night of adventure, assuming they aren't very thirsty. Most dwarves carry their own personal mugs for traveling, and a spare as well in case of disaster. These mugs are often very large and very ornate, and no dwarf would ever trifle with another dwarf's favorite mug.

There are several varieties of dwarven ale distributed by Clan Axepeak, detailed below.

Axepeak Regular - This is the standard ale produced by Clan Axepeak. The standard price for a keg is 5 PP when being sold to non-dwarves.

Axepeak Quality - This is high-grade dwarven ale produced by Clan Axepeak. It is used for gatherings of some note and for well prepared meals. The standard price for a keg is 15 PP when being sold to non-dwarves.

Axepeak Special - This is the top grade ale produced by Clan Axepeak. It is second to none in terms of quality. It is served at only the most exclusive gatherings. The standard price for a keg is 25 PP when being sold to non-dwarves, which is a rarity.

Axepeak Light - This is a slightly weaker variety of ale (still stronger than any human brew). It is mainly drunk during meetings and such, when a clear head is vital and the constant talking demands frequent lubrication of the vocal cords. Many dwarves prefer to travel with this ale when adventuring, while others consider this to be too weak for good fighting. The standard price for a keg is 3 PP when being sold to non-dwarves.

Note that the standard price for a 30-gallon keg of human ale is 1 PP. Even the weakest dwarven ale puts the best human ale to shame, and only they wealthy can afford to provide dwarven ales at their gatherings.

(These ales and prices are role-play devices at this time. However, Clan Axepeak does have quite a few members that are developing their brewing skills.)
