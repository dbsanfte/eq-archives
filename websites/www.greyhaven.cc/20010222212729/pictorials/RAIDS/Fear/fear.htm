Plane of Fear

"First off, I would like to start by thanking Cyriakus, Jenora, and all of Noble Blade for my first plane run. 
They are a great group of people and a lot of fun to fight with.  Thank you very much!"  - Thaldin

 

The Scenery

 
 

In a hidden cave, deep in the Feerott, lies a portal to the Plane
of Fear.  Protected by specters and a powerful necromancer, approach
with great caution!

 
 
 

 

Here we have the heroes preparing to invade the home of Cazic Thule.

 
 

Approaching the portal, then turning around, reassured by the group
with me that we will survive and prevail in this place of nightmares.

 
 
 

 

More have gathered to join us on this raid into the heart of fear itself.  Sitting
on the ground in front of me are Jenora and Cyriakus, the two who invited me.

 
 

We have made it in and fought our way to western portion of the plane.
After clearing the area, we take a small breather to regroup and ready
our forces for another round of onslaughts.

 
 
 

 

Ah, the fright finger house.  Horrible little things.  Hands, without anything else,
scurrying around, casting foul magics.

 
 

Hourglasses, like this one here, are spread around Fear.  An indicator to
remind us that our lives are limited? To remind us of our own mortality
in this place where in dreams walk death?

 
 
 

 

The night here has drawn to an end, it is time to leave.  Now, to find the portal
out of this realm, back to my friends.

 
 

What's this? The portal is in the temple, surrounded by a ring of fire.

 
 
 
  Ah, at last.  The portal out of Fear, to the Feerott.  It lies in the heart of the
temple dedicated to Cazic Thule.

 

 

 

 

The Inhabitants

 
 
The shiverback apes, big, ferocious, and generally not very friendly.
These large beasts were kind enough to provide me with a new pair
of gloves to use, however.		  
 
 
  The scarling, a nasty little imp with a wicked backstab.  They also have the
ability to translocate you, and seem to like to relocate you to their friends.
 
 
Chillspiders - well first off, who likes spiders in the first place?  Then
add an especially nasty venom and you have a bad combination.		  
 
 
  And you thought regular zombies were annoying? Wait until you meet their
larger brethren, the fetid fiends.
 
 
Turmoil Toads....and they live up to their name.  Not only can they
drain the strength from your legs, but they can get behind you and
backstab you for quite a bit.		  
 
 
  Frightfingers, casters of a not so nice nature.  Though they are nice enough
to drop some wizard gear.
 
 
Ah the Samhain.  I find these so highly amusing. You hit them and they
scream "OW!"		  
 
 
  Now these, the glare lords, are quite annoying.  I hope you do not
wish to keep any of the buffs and spells you have on you.  They mass
dispel, and they do it a lot.
 
 
Amygdalan Warriors, guardians to the temple of Cazic Thule.  
 
 
  Amygdalan Knights, those who have gone beyond just being a guardian.
They are shadow knights in the service of Cazic.
 
 
I bet your mom or dad told you the boogeyman did not exist like
mine did.  I TOLD YOU HE WAS UNDER THE BED!		  
 
 
  It is odd, any time I saw a gorgon, suddenly I heard druids yelling,
"STARFIRE TARGET!"  I wonder if this has anything to do with the
vermaculated armor drops?
 
 
Worry Wraiths, larger and less peaceful will-o-wisps.  No doubt, they have
been twisted by the realm surrounding them.		  
 
 
  Beautiful and sleek creatures, the nightmares possess quite the evil temperament
though.  Fierce in combat and a tendency to blind you, be cautious.
 
 
Phantasms bringing nightmares to promote fear across Norrath.  
 
 
  Dread, the stone golem.  The name says it all.
 
 
Fright, the mud golem.  And a fright he was, half our raiding party
disappeared when he showed up.		  
 
 
  Terror, made of steel, inspires his namesake to those who fight him.
