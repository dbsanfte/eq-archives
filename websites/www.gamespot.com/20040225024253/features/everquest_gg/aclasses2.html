Creating a Character
Bards are born of the races of the half elf, human, and wood elf. A bard must be strong, charismatic, and have high dexterity, so these are the traits you must boost when creating an effective bard.

Getting Started
You start out with a short sword, the standard food and water, guild note, and Tome of Discord.

Your first order of business is to officially join your class's guild. Read your guild note and then search around. You might want to type / to see if there are any bards around who can point you in the right direction. When you find your guild master, hand him the guild note and click Find to choose a few skills you wish to train.

Beginning Adventuring Tips
As you get training points by gaining levels, it's not necessary to waste them on combat abilities beyond learning the basics of a given skill. Your combat abilities will go up nicely during the course of adventuring; instead save your practices for the critical noncombat skills. As with any class, consider carefully any monster before you attack.

Bards are weavers of songs and do not use mana to cast spells. Instead, their singing is what heals you, makes you stronger, and moves you faster. In addition, the spells continue as long as you keep singing - so your spell could theoretically be never-ending. They are quite popular in parties because most of their songs affect everyone around them. Since these spells do not use mana, your party won't have to wait for you to regenerate, as you would with other spell-casting classes. Make certain to sing a song at all times, especially in combat.

Bards will never be as good a fighter as a warrior nor as good a mage as the cleric, but the combination of the two provides for a good character. While the bard is not as easy as other classes to play solo, he is indispensable in groups.

One choice you must make early on is whether your bard will be a mage-warrior or a warrior-mage. Since many bard songs are enhanced by a musical instrument, you must choose to wield either the instrument or your weapon. This is where your choice comes in. Do you stand back and wield your instrument, bolstering your music? Or do you wield your sword and use the base song to compliment your hacking. The choice is yours, and either is a good one.
