While a ranger belongs to a class that is a master of nothing, he is still proficient in many thing. A ranger is neither warrior nor mage, and his profession in Norrath is one that can take many paths. At higher levels, rangers gain the ability to cast some spells and can attack using the dual weapon skill. They live in the wilderness, and their abilities can work wonders in the woodlands.

Creating a Character
Rangers can be of three different races: human, wood elf, and half elf. During your initial character creation, you'll see four areas that are key to being a successful ranger. Each skill has its positive and negatives, so use your points wisely.

As with all characters, your first quest will be to find your guild. The only guild hall for human and half-elf rangers is in the city of Qeynos. However, when you start, you'll be in Surefall Glade. The wood elf begins the game in Kelethin.

Getting Started
Unfortunately, rangers, as with any class, don't have much in the way of armor, weapons, or skills. You start out with your trusty and rusty short sword, the standard food and water, guild note, and Tome of Discord. The PvP choice is up to you, but at the start, low-level rangers won't have much success against their higher-level PvP enemies. If you are interested in that aspect of EverQuest, you should bank the PvP note and wait until you are either accustomed to the gameplay of EverQuest, or you reach a level in which you can take care of yourself and have the means to replace your equipment in case you lose it.

The Qeynos Ranger Guild is located in Surefall Glade. Your guild master is Hagar Sureshot, and he's located to your left when you start. The Greater Faydark Ranger Guild is located in Everfrost; your guild Master is Maesyn Trueshot. If you're searching for the city of Qeynos, exit Surefall Glade and follow the trail, which is directly in front of you from the exit. Stay on the road and make the first right in the road you come to and you'll run smack dab into the city of Qeynos.
