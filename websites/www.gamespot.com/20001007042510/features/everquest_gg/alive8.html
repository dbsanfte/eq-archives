Successful Grouping

Grouping serves another purpose as well. It is an excellent way to forge friendships and let your honor show through. An honorable player attracts other honorable characters, so that if you watch the back of your brother, he will watch yours as well.

Not all groups are created equal, however, and there are many strategies to successful grouping. While you are in a group, keep in mind that a good group is a group that takes advantage of the skills each person in the group provides. Know your comrades and how to use all your combined skills to your advantage.

A successful group will designate a single leader of the group. To succeed, the leader must understand grouping and how to effectively manage the group resources. Leaders should be responsible for keeping people together, scouting the enemies, and knowing when to sound a retreat.

A poor leader can easily doom a group and detract from the gaming experience. Don't fret, however. By reading this section, you will be well on your way to becoming a good group leader. Hang tough, it takes some practice.

Group Communication
The number one destroyer of groups is not a monster, but poor communication. Even in parties with good communication, sometimes bad things happen. Mostly it's because someone either wasn't paying attention, or because someone forgot to mention some action he was taking that affected the other members of the party.

For example, someone in your group may have gone out to try and lure a monster back to the group for an ambush. Pretty soon, you hear the person yell, "Incoming." If your mages have not regenerated mana, and your fighters are still weakened from the last battle, you are in serious trouble. Let your leader go get the monsters or assign someone in the group to do it.

TIP
Obey your group leader. Vigilante members will ruin a group!

Most successful groups have several messages that are vitally important and also quickly and easily abbreviated. These messages should be understood by all, and everyone should know exactly what is to be done upon hearing of any of these messages.
