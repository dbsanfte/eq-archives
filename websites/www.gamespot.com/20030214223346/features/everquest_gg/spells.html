Chapter 10
Spells

Nearly everyone enjoys dabbling in the magic arts, from the casual spellcaster to the legendary Merlin himself. Norrath is not unacquainted with magic and the power it provides; in fact, ultimate survival in Norrath may come down to your skills in magery. The classes that can access the magical arts can command some of the greatest powers in EverQuest, while the alchemists can concoct powerful potions, especially those for healing and poisoning.

In this chapter, you'll learn the particulars of magery, which includes more than 600 spells available to the various classes. Each spell listed describes its effect, the classes and levels it is available to, and what skill it requires and any additional mana required to cast the spell. It should be noted that many spells exist merely in legend and not in practice. Information on many high-powered spells is severely limited, and the effects of many of these spells is still a mystery.

Bubble, Bubble, Toil and Trouble

The classes with magical abilities are quite powerful, and if you choose to follow this profession, you'll need plenty of practice in ingenuity. The effective management of available mana and spell use will either make or break a mage in Norrath. The mage who tries to gate to safety only to realize he doesn't have sufficient mana, will soon find himself in an early grave.

To cast spells, you must first have the spell scribed into your spellbook. When you start the game, you will most likely have one or two spells, which are written on scrolls. One of your first actions in EverQuest should be to scribe these spells into your spellbook. (you don't need any special skill to accomplish this).

NOTE
Spells that are scribed in your spellbook cannot be lost. When you die, your spellbook remains with you.

Once the spell is scribed in your spellbook, you must have a spell memorized to cast it. You can memorize up to eight spells at any one time. Once memorized, you simply cast the spell by clicking on the appropriate spell icon. Your success in casting spells is governed by the amount of mana you have and your skill level with that particular spell type. Bards are the only spellcasting class that does not use mana. Their spells are considered songs, and the magical effects are provided by the voice of the bard.
