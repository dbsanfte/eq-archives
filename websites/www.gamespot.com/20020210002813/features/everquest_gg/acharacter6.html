Dungeons
Dungeons come in many forms in Norrath. I admit to having precious little information on these dreadful places, but I'll tell you what I can.

One dungeon I have frequented is called Befallen. I am not certain as to its origins, but it is a dark and terrible place. It is run down - broken down in fact - as if it once carried some greater glory. Now, it is the refuge of shadow knights, necromancers, mummies, and all matter of cursed undead. Befallen runs three levels deep, and each level is guarded by a locked door (and usually a host of creatures as well). To reach the second level, you must find and slay a shadow knight. He carries the wooden key that will give you access to the second level. The creatures there are great indeed! Even at level 12, I have difficulty journeying there and never journey beyond the first level alone. This is certainly one of those places where grouping is not only enjoyable, but necessary to survive. It's best to have a cleric with you as well to heal your wounds and turn the undead back to their graves.

Befallen is suitable for characters level 9 (foolish are those that journey there that young, though I was one of them) to at least the early 20s. This is a truth for many dungeons. Often, creatures there offer a fair challenge for those of lower levels, but more powerful creatures are often peppered throughout the dungeon as well. It is for this reason that foolish pride often results in death - you must run when your foe is too great and do not get lost, either, lest you find the whole dungeon after you!

TIP
Start running when you reach two bubbles of health - any lower, and you will slow down.

Not far from Butcher Block Mountains sits the Estate of Unrest. This is another wicked place full of sleepless souls. A terrible hedge maze precedes it, and many worthy adventurers met their death there. Though I have not yet been brave (or perhaps foolish) enough to actually enter that dreadful place, I am told that those halls are suitable for those of levels 13 to 30. Fear that place my friends, it is not for the faint of heart.

From Lesser Faydark lies Castle Mistmoor. I fear I know precious little of this place as well. I was turned back by the Mistmoorian guards in a most unpleasant manner before I ever laid eyes upon its gates. The foul guards cast a wicked spell upon me that set my blood afire - boil it did, and I would have surely perished were it not for my swift feet and crude (but effective) healing spells. Strangely enough, my friends escaped unscathed - I suppose the guards of Mistmoor have a special hatred in their black hearts for wood elves. I am told that even the greatest among us tread lightly there, and I know it shall be many long years before I dare walk near that place again.
