To Thy Own Self Be True
Be true to your character, not to the human that drives it. Role playing is based on this principle. If your character is an ogre, your character is to stupid to understand the principles of virtue and shouldn't be seen giving an eloquent public address. Think this through when examining initial class and race considerations. If you want to play the game as yourself, create a character that is your race and class in real life. If you create an ogre and give him super intelligence, you have destroyed the role-playing environment. Race and class considerations are very important.

Think carefully when building a character. Here are some points to consider:

  • Give the character a history (biography)
  • Give your character a personality
  • Give your character a language
  • Give your character a face and physical appearance.
Its important to try and create a virtual person for others to see. I have a picture of King Arthur in my mind. Tales and my interpretations of him have created that picture. I think I know what he looks like. This visualization is important for your characters and helps others to visually imagine what you look like. This helps to create the environment for role-playing. Show everyone who your character is by actions and dress. Demonstrate your personality and your character flaws. Do this often! Go to a tavern and just sit at a table. Then, describe everything you do, from shifting in the chair to brushing the hair out of your eyes. The more you do it, the better people can build a picture of who you are in their minds.

A final word on this topic. It is perfectly acceptable to create a character that is very similar to yourself. If you feel comfortable and happy role-playing this way, do it. What is important is that you are true to the character and not yourself in role-playing. If you're true to the character, you are helping build the role-playing environment. If an ogre comes to you and speaks of virtue, you should laugh at the idiot and walk away. That person just ruined the role-playing experience. If you want to be intelligent and powerful, use the race and class combinations to create a character that fits your desire, not the other way around.
