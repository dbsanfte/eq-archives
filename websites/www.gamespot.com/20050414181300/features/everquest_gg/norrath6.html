Know Thy Environment
The first thing you should do in any role-playing game is become aware of your environment. The age and ambiance is very important. A 21st-century police officer is a ludicrous character to have in 14th century England. Determine the relative age of the environment. Is it similar to any age in our real history (that is, EverQuest is very similar to 14th-century England or the Dark Ages). Determining the age of the land will provide the ambiance for your role-playing. Your character can help create this ambiance or destroy it. The better your character responds in this age, the better the experience for everyone.

Know Thy History
Each world has its own unique history. It is this history that attracts people to role-play in that world, and much of the role-playing environment is based on the history that the game creators have set forth.

Norrath has an extensive history. The racial tensions that exist in Norrath complicate every character's existence. The use the races and classes provide plot and intrigue to your character and game experience.

Watch Thy Tongue
Each world has its own native language, and EverQuest has many languages. It is usually acceptable to "slang" this language with the use of normal English. In general, the language in EverQuest is Elizabethan in nature. However, it is acceptable to integrate normal English when speaking. This makes communication easier, since you needn't learn the Elizabethan language. Just throw in a few "thees" and "thous," and life is good. The better your character learns the language, the better the role-playing. It helps set the environment and ambiance for everyone.

Another component of language is your character. EverQuest has various languages and dialects. Let your character speak through his own race, not your human form. If you play a dwarf, use of "me do this" or "me thinks" is very appropriate. Likewise, ogres and trolls are not very intelligent by nature and tend to use short phrases like "ugh" or "dat hurt." When you develop your character, be aware of his native language vs. the language of the land. This includes any speech problems that your character may have, such as a lisp.
