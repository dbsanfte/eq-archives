Backstab
A rogue's position in group combat should be behind a monster. Backstab is the single most effective melee combat in the game. If you have another fighter who can taunt or a mage who can burn, you have a great way to distract the monster so you can carve up its backside.

Bows
If you can use a bow, you should have one in your inventory. A bow lets you back away from a monster and continue to deal damage. This is a good tactic to use while your health regenerates. While it can be expensive replacing the arrows, it is very useful in a dangerous fight. Once you are past about level 12, the expense of the arrows is well justified. The mobs begin to do much more damage and also begin to give more loot per mob. Soon spending a gold or two for arrows won't seem so bad.

Feign Death
Monks should become proficient with this skill. It lets you immediately drop out of combat, either to switch mob target or regenerate health. Feigning death makes the monk the single best sacrificial lamb the group has. If the fight is going poorly, sound the retreat and have everyone else take off running in one direction. Let the monk stay behind and lead the monster in the other direction. Before your health gets too low, feign death. You'll have given your party a good head start on fleeing.

Hymn of Restoration
Bards are the ultimate group character. Once they reach level 6, they can learn Hymn of Restoration, which when sung, heals all the members of a group. Since the bard continues singing without using mana, the spell can regenerate health during a fight. Group healing is an amazing addition because when people back out (which they should do at around 80 percent if you have a bard in the group), they can regenerate and return to the fray quickly.

Lay on Hands
This is the last resort for a group paladin. If your healers are out of mana, this is a one-time shot at healing. Don't use it unless you have to.

Multiple Attacks
While unable to use weapons, monks make up for it by the sheer number of attacks they have. At high levels, a monk can get off three to five attacks a round. This makes them great for taking out enemy spell casters. If a spell caster is constantly being hit, his concentration is broken, and he cannot get off a spell. Remember this advantage and use it to interrupt and pummel those casters that are about to unload on your group.

Taunt
Taunt is another skill you should master if you have it. Taunt makes a monster hate you as if you'd actually done damage to it. This lets you keep the monster's attention so it doesn't go beat on that spellcaster who just burned the monster for huge damage.

TIP
Do not taunt a monster too much. Doing so will put it into a frenzy, which could get you killed quickly.
