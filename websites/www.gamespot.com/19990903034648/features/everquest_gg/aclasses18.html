Basic Survival

Levels 1-5
You can reach level 5 without leaving the newbie zone if you prey on fire beetles as you approach level 5. However, you may find that level 4 goes too slowly, so venture out into Qeynos Hills or East Commons. But be warned, it's often very difficult to find a large supply of level 3 to 4 monsters to fight. Start saving your money now for your set of leather armor.

Levels 5-9
These levels can be a trial as you begin to fight more intelligent and more powerful foes. The fastest way to advance through these levels is to team up with a cleric or bard of the same level. Having a healer who can deal significant damage and has a reserve of mana will keep you from dying much and cut down the time you have to rest. A monk and a bard is the ultimate team. Don't group too much past two or three except in special situations. The best experience gain comes from a pair.

Level 10+
Starting at level 10, you'll begin to grow into your powers. You'll find that your armor class tends to be higher than all but the most well-equipped warriors. Mend starts working more often than not and becomes fairly dependable. Level 10 gets you tiger strike, a very quick recycling special attack. Level 12 earns you block that, unlike dodge, actually works quite often. And level 15 gives you the awesome double attack.

As you adventure with larger parties and enter more complicated battles, develop your leadership skills. Be aware of what's going on and capable of reorganizing your party during battle if something isn't working. As a monk, your general strategy is to target a monster and beat on it until it dies, which leaves you a lot of opportunity to take charge of the larger battle, since you don't have to worry about casting spells or healing.
