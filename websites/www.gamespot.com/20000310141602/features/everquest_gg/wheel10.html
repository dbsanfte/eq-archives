User Interface - Common Questions

How come I can only carry eight items?
Later on, you can buy containers, or sometimes you will get one from an NPC that you slay. These containers use up one of your eight slots; but with a right click, they open up to provide you with more space to put things in. Backpacks, bags, boxes, belt pouches, and wrist pouches are some examples of containers. They can each hold a certain number and size of items. Backpacks are especially handy because they can store up to eight items, and they can also accept large items that will not fit into bags or pouches.

Where is my paperdoll?
From an external view, right click on yourself. This will bring up your picture with a blank area below it. Clicking on this area will let you type a character history visible to anyone who looks at your equipment.

How do I loot something that I have slain?
Right click on the corpse of the being that you have slain. It will bring up an inventory on the left-hand side of your screen. This may be empty or contain items that you can take. Any money that was on the corpse is automatically taken when the loot inventory comes up. You can grab an item with a single click, drag the item to a free inventory slot, and single click to release it. You can save a lot of time if you just right click the item on the corpse. It will automatically be placed in an empty inventory slot. Make sure you have an empty slot when you do this or the item will disappear.

How do I make change for all this copper?
You can do so in a bank by dragging your coins into the place of a coin of another type. The banker will exchange the coins for coins of that type, as he is able.

How do I switch from my two-handed sword to my one-handed broadsword quickly?
Create two adjacent hotkeys. One is your primary melee weapon slot, and the other is the backpack slot where you store your extra weapons. You can now switch weapons easily between your hotkeys by simply dragging the spare weapon into your primary attack box.

My skill or spell creates an object in my hand, why must I open my inventory to put the object away?
You don't. Create a hotkey for your skill or spell alongside a hotkey for the inventory spot where you will place it. If your skill or spell creates a random item, keep a backpack in the inventory slot to hold the assorted items.

Why can't I loot some things?
The person who gets to loot is the one that did the most damage in the fight. If you come across a corpse that you wish to loot, you may have to wait a few minutes before you can, this gives an opportunity for the person who killed it to claim his loot. You cannot loot other players' corpses unless they give you permission. This is done by typing "/consent ". If you are getting a message that says "it is futile to consider the dead," you are simply to far away.
