Chapter 6
Advancing Your Character
Scribed by Ardanna Kelvar

I'm certain you can imagine my surprise when I was asked to share my knowledge on how to makes your way through Norrath, growing in physical and magical, power. How is it that a simple, woodland elf druid finds herself in such a position to have such knowledge?

Well, I won't bore you with the how of it for now, but rather dazzle you with the what of it!

Skill Advancement

I'll start at the beginning. Many of your skills, indeed virtually all of them, will be at the astounding level of awful. For the mathematicians out there, that would be a skill percentile of 1 to 10 percent. Fear not, these skills will rise fast enough!

There will be some skills (precious few, I might add) that will begin at a much higher level of proficiency. As a druid and an elf of the woodland folk, I began with the forage and hide skills, both at the below average ability (41 to 50 percent).

Your skill level will be quite high, excellent, in fact (though I am uncertain of the skill percentage), in your language proficiencies. The proficiencies you start with will vary with your class and race; you will start will excellent common (which everyone can speak) and the language of your race (in my case, elven).

Initially, your skill titles change with every ten percent increase in skill level. However, once you reach the average proficiency, the stretches between each new skill title becomes greater.

Combat Skills
You increase skills by attempting more and more difficult tasks as time progresses. However, I must caution you on taking up newer skills at higher levels - most notoriously weapon skills. When I reached the tenth level of proficiency, I decided to switch from using my trusty (albeit rusty) mace to a great staff that was generously donated to me. The skill set for my mace was one-handed blunt, while the skill set for my great staff was two handed blunt (which I was awful at).

At first I was dismayed - I thought I would progress quickly in this area, as it was a new skill for me, and I was quick to learn at lower levels. However, I later discovered that I must fight creatures of near or the same level as my own for it to increase! This was extremely difficult, as I had not the skill to hit the creatures under this new skill set. Though not impossible, it quickly became tedious, and I switched back to my old weapon type. The lesson I learned was to choose the weapons I wanted to use at the beginning of the game and stick with them!

TIP
To continue increasing combat skills, you must fight monsters that are near your skill level.
