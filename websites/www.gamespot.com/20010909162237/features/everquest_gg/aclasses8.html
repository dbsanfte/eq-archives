Creating a Character
Druids are born of the races of the half elf, halfling, human, and wood elf. A druid must be wise and has great stamina, so these are the traits you must boost when creating an effective druid.

Getting Started
You start out with a club, the standard food and water, guild note, two spell scrolls (Skin of Wood and Minor Healing), and Tome of Discord.

Your first order of business is to officially join your class's guild. Read your guild note and then search around. You might want to type / to see if there are any druids around who can point you in the right direction. When you find your guild master, hand him the guild note and click Find to choose a few skills you wish to train in. Don't train in battle skills unless you do not have the skill listed on the right. If that is the case, just spend a single point on the skill. Train in the spellcasting skills for your two starting skills (abjuration and alteration).

Beginning Adventuring Tips
As you get training points by gaining levels, it's not necessary to waste them on combat abilities beyond learning the basics of a given skill. Your combat abilities will go up nicely during the course of adventuring; instead save your practices for the critical noncombat skills. As with any class, consider carefully any monster before you attack.

Druids are the masters of nature and feel a bit odd when out of their environment. A druid in a dungeon has a difficult time casting his normal spells, since many of them simply will not work indoors. A druid that in a dungeon should work as a cleric for a party or even help protect the party's members.

Druids also are the masters of travel. Use this to your advantage to find the best hunting and best deals on equipment. In fact, a druid could start up quite a trade, bringing goods to areas where supply is scarce.
