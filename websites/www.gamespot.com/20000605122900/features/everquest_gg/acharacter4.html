Zone Difficulty

As you begin to grow in power, you'll find it necessary to explore further and further to gain experience. New adventures can be found across zones, and different zones bring vastly different experiences.

From about level 5 to level 9, I spent much of my time in Greater Faydark, which is the same zone that all wood elves and high elves start in. An orcish encampment near the cave leads to Crushbone. This area is excellent for people from levels 5 to 9. However, for those of you on the lower end of that scale I would strongly recommend grouping together for your own safety. Those at the higher end of the scale may start to journey in this initial orc encampment on their own.

At approximately level 10, you could be brave (or foolish) enough to venture into Crushbone. You'll do well there, but be cautious, it is a zone for people from about level 10 to about level 20. Some creatures provide excellent experience, and others mean instant death. (I myself fell to Emperor Crushbone and his royal guard more times than I care to admit.)

There are many choices out there for you. Instead of Crushbone, you could journey instead to Butcher Block Mountains. That area is ideal for those of you at about levels 6 to 10. Of course, stronger or weaker characters can do well there, that is just the average. When in doubt, stay near the guards; they will help you as they can.

A loosely put rule is: The further you get from a civilized town, the greater hold chaos gains on the land. Creatures become much more aggressive and powerful. The following table breaks down the zones in which you should venture (or not venture), depending on your level.
