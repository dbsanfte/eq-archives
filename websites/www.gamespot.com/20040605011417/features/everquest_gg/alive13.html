Offensive Mages
The following tips are aimed toward the offensive mages or those who can cast attack spells. Most spellcasters have some form of offensive spell. Directing damage to a monster is a good thing, but don't waste all your mana trying to fry the monster to death. Let your fighters use their skills. You're the brains in the group, not the brawn.

TIP
Know your role in the group. Am I fighter, healer, or distracter?

Offensive spells should be used for attracting an enemy's attention - either to start a fight or to distract it from another party member. Remember though, that if you try to distract the monster from another member, it will turn on you. Be ready!

Another good use of offensive spells is to finish off a fleeing monster or to deliver the final blow as opposed to waiting a few rounds of melee combat. It also is a good last resort. If the fight is going badly, the group will begin doing everything in its power to stay alive. This includes throwing every offensive spell you can muster at the monster.

TIP
Use offensive spells sparingly. Don't use all your mana at the beginning of the fight.

At this point, spellcasters often ask, "What am I supposed to be doing?" when the fighters are effectively landing blows. Well, use the time to work on your own melee skills. Let the fighters land two hits and then step in. Your dagger or staff will never catch up to the fighters' melee, but it will do a little damage. Every bit of practice helps, and it also lets you take your share of the damage from the monster. Following are some specific uses of offensive spells.

Pets Magicians and necromancers can summon pets to help in the fight. If they do, treat the pet as another fighter. Monitor its health and back it out of the fight as needed. In general, pets are much more valuable against multiple opponents than against one or two. Using pets serves as a way to distract the additional monsters until you dispatch a couple and have a chance to turn your attention to the rest.

Root Wizards, shamen, druids, clerics, and enchanters can root a target to the ground. If something is running, stop it. It is also very useful to stick a melee mob with no ranged attacks in place, letting your fighters arrow it to death. Root is also resisted quite often, so watch your mana.

Weaken the Mob Enchanters, Shamen, and necromancers can cast spells that weaken the target. If you can get such a spell off at the beginning of combat, it can dramatically speed up the result. It is one of the most subtle of all the spell types, but if you compare weakened mobs to mobs at full strength, the difference is amazing.
