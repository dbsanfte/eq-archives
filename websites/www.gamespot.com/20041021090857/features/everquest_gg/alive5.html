Preventive Measures

Now that you know a few of the ways you can die, you may want to know what steps you can take to avoid following in the footsteps of those less wise. Needless to say, if you are a level 1 cleric with only a dagger, you should probably stay very close to town. Should you decide to be adventurous and travel away from the safety of the towns, be sure you are prepared for what you may face.

Run Fast!
Combat with a mere rat can prove deadly for low-level characters. It is wise, therefore, to stick to the newbie zones close to towns. These areas were designed to accommodate new characters, giving them a chance to survive and advance in level.

Even in such areas, you will undoubtedly face a foe who is getting the best of you. Perhaps the best tip for survival early on is to turn tail and run toward town as fast as your legs can take you. Towns, guard towers, or anywhere guards patrol should be considered a safe haven for the times you get in over your head during combat. As you run away from the monster, it will chase you until one of you lies dead. The trick is leading the monster to the guards. As the monster is drawn into the range of the guards, they will command it to halt. Upon failing to heed their command, the guards will dispatch the threat. You can sit and catch your breath and perhaps even buy the guards an ale in gratitude for saving your neck.

TIP
If you have a long run to the guards, do not stop to look behind you to see if the monster is still chasing you. It will be! The creatures of Norrath are relentless in their pursuit. Continue running until you are safely standing next to the guard, then you can turn to look behind you.

If you are not near any guarded areas, you can also run to another zone. By moving to a new zone, you effectively lose the creature chasing you, as they cannot cross into the new zone. Upon entering this safe zone, you can simply turn around and reenter the zone you just left. Upon reentering the zone you were adventuring in, the creature chasing you should be nowhere in sight.
