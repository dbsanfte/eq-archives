Creating a Character
Paladins come from the races of the dark elf, dwarf, erudite, gnome, halfling, high elf, and human. A cleric is strong, has great stamina, and is quite wise, so these are the traits you must boost when creating an effective cleric.

Getting Started
You start out with a club, the standard food and water, guild note, and Tome of Discord.

Your first order of business is to officially join your class's guild. Read your guild note and then search around. You might want to type / to see if there are any clerics around who can point you in the right direction. When you find your guild master, hand him the guild note and click on Find to choose a few skills you wish to train in. Don't train in battle skills unless you do not have the skill listed on the right. If that is the case, just spend a single point on the skill. Train once in each of the magical skills that corresponds to the starting spells (for courage and minor healing, you train once in both abjure and alter).

Beginning Adventuring Tips
Since clerics are the medics of a party, make certain that you use your healing skills often. You must become more proficient in healing spells and their accompanying skill, alter.

As you get training points by gaining levels, it's not necessary to waste them on combat abilities beyond learning the basics of a given skill. Your combat abilities will go up nicely during the course of adventuring; instead save your practices for the critical noncombat skills.
