Levels 7-9
You are becoming quite a good warrior in addition to developing your rogue skills. It's also time to learn pick pocket! Practice on humanoid monsters whenever you can. Orcs and goblins make good targets if they are distracted by other party members. You cannot use this skill while your auto attack is on, but you can turn it off for a split second and steal. Also, the beggars in town make good targets if the guards are not looking. Just be sure you can take them out in an alley or that you can run away if they catch you in their pockets. Picking pockets will either get you coins or (rarely until you are more skilled) or items of equipment. You get less and less from the same target until he runs out of money, or you are caught. This pilfering of coins does not, as of this writing, decrease the loot on the creature when he dies, so party members shouldn't get upset with you. If you get an equipment item though, like a water flask or a gemstone, you get to decide whether to tell everyone else what you got. If you run across another party fighting a humanoid, run over and pick pockets while everyone is distracted in combat. You should have a better weapon by now. A good spear is your best bet, though still quite expensive. You might want to take up archery or throwing. Both are good ways of getting a monsters' attention or polishing it off as it runs away.

Level 10
You have arrived. You gain the highly esteemed skill of back stab. Warriors will respect you, mages will envy you, and women will adore you. Or... err... something like that. Use up most or all your saved practice sessions on back stab. The better your skill level, the more often you hit and the more damage you do. Back stab is not an automatic skill. Set up a hotkey for it and try to get behind a monster, sneaking if possible. Target the monster and hit the hotkey. Your attack should do two to three times the amount of damage as a regular piercing attack. In combat, use your piercing auto attack as you normally would, plus hit the back stab hotkey whenever it lights up again. If, during combat, you use back stab while not behind your target, it is still an extra piercing attack. Back stab will almost always get the attention of the victim, however, so be prepared for his wrath. If you are with a party, try stepping back a couple steps after the monster turns toward you. If he turns back to fight your friends, bury your blade in his back again.
