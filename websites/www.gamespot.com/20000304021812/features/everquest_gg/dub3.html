Classes

The classes available vary with the race chosen. Not all classes are available to every race. Changing the class chosen changes the base attributes of the character. It is important that you look at race and class combinations prior to selecting a race or a class. If you have your heart set on being a bard, you are limited to what races you can choose from. The following section lists which classes are available for each race.

For more detailed information on classes, including starting equipment, skills, and spells, see Chapter 8 - Advanced Classes. Following is a brief description of each of the classes.

Bard
Bards are the jacks of all trades in Norrath. Bards have the unique ability to cast (sing) their spells during combat and fight at the same time. In addition, a bard's song does not use mana, so it is much easier to rattle off a series of spells without waiting for your mana to regenerate. That ability alone makes a bard a very versatile player, though he will never be as good a fighter as a warrior, nor will he be as proficient in spellcasting as some of the pure mage classes.

Cleric
Dedicated to the art of healing, the cleric only engages in combat when attacked or when battling infidels. Clerics use only blunt instruments of war, but their real strength is their ability to protect during battle and offer healing after. Clerics draw their magical spells from prayer and their faith, making adherence to a religious creed essential.

Druid
A Druid's connection to nature is the source of his inspiration and magic. It is through careful understanding of this connection that the druid becomes one with nature - and gains the benefit of nature's knowledge. Ever a friend to all manners of beasts and plants, none of Norrath's animals will ever lay tooth or claw upon the druid, unless it is first harmed.

Enchanter
Enchanters are seemingly unimpressed with the mundane creations of the races of Norrath. When an enchanter looks upon a sword - he is not concerned with the sturdiness of the blade or even its sharpness. What an enchanter sees when he looks upon such artifacts is a wealth of possibilities. Perhaps he could alter the metal to make it stronger. Perhaps he could take the flame of a torch and bind it forever to the sword. Enchanters take the ordinary things found in Norrath, and turn them into things of legend.

Magician
It is often joked amongst other practitioners of magic that magicians are perhaps the laziest of spellcasters. Although magicians are quite capable of holding their own in magic battles, they much prefer summoning other creatures to do it for them. No other practitioner of magic is as adept at summoning creatures as the magician. So, while this spellcaster stands safely at the sidelines, his minions do all the work in addition to taking all the risks.

Monk
The martial arts purists, monks are focused, mentally disciplined physical fitness enthusiasts who mainly use their own bodies as weapons. This requires enormous amounts of training and the strictest regimen and diet. Pugilism, karate, or just about any form of combat that involves the body and its potential is the forte of the monk.

Necromancer
Commander of the undead, usher to the underworld, gatherer of dark clouds, a necromancer is bound to the evil and the arcane magic of death. Powerful wielders of magic, necromancers are ever drawn to the graves of the fallen and reanimate these corpses as dark servants. With their allies the shadow knights, necromancers wreak a bleak path before them, turning light into dark and brave heroes into soulless minions of evil.
