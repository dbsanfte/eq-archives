When you find your guild master, hand him the guild note and click Find to choose a few skills you wish to train in. Do not train in battle skills unless you do not have the skill listed on the right. If that is the case, just spend a single point on the skill. I recommend using at least one or two points on tailoring for later use when you have accumulated enough wealth to purchase patterns and a kit. You can make your own armor, and tailoring does comes in handy. One skill, which is of the utmost importance, is tracking. This skill lets you track things you want to fight, helps you find lost friends, and assists you when trying to fulfill a quest. Practice this skill often because it is a very handy skill.

Beginning Adventuring Tips
The areas right outside Surefall Glade, Qeynos, and Greater Faydark, or in the town of Everfrost are filled with things to attack. Once you find something you wish to attack, do not forget about the /c command or right click on the monster to find its strength out. If your targeted victim is yellow or red, let it be. Normally red or yellow enemies will end up killing you unless you have a guard or cleric nearby who can help during the process. Remember, dying is no fun and takes up unnecessary time. On the other hand, when you die at such an early state in the game, the penalty is small. But later on, dying can cost you levels. The good thing is that if you die, you're in your home area and will be very close to your body. You can run back and loot to get your gear back. No one else can loot your corpse unless you're PvP.

Once you reach level 5, train in kicking, which gives you an extra attack and can cause an additional one to eight points in damage during the earlier levels.

As you get practice points from training, it's not necessary to waste them on combat abilities beyond learning the basics of a given skill. Your combat abilities will go up nicely during the course of adventuring; instead, save your practice points for the critical noncombat skills.
