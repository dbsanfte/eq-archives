Basic Survival

Levels 1-4
Stay close to the guarded areas. Once you get higher than level 3, start adventuring outside the city outskirts. As a wizard with few spells as yet, you will probably be somewhat of a loner at this stage. Sense heading is a good skill to practice. Buy a backpack when you can afford one - that way, you can haul away more loot! Rusty weapons from skeletons and other creatures make great things to sell and can easily get you extra spells. The first spells you will want to purchase are frost bolt (a great spell for attacking targets from a long distance) and numbing cold (extremely useful when many creatures attack you at the same time). Bind wound is the only healing skill you will ever know, so you probably want to learn it. Once you attain level 4, be sure to go to your guild and train in the skill of meditation. This will greatly reduce your down time when regenerating mana. In addition to meditation, head to your local wizard merchant and purchase some new spells. Among the first spells you buy should be shock of fire (similar to shock of ice, yet more powerful), gate (teleports you to where your soul is bound, which is where you begin when you die), and root (holds the target in place so it cannot move).

Levels 5-7
Here, you will begin to gain more power. You should now be exploring away from the areas just outside of the city and heading into the more dangerous areas. Although you may feel as if other mage-type classes are more powerful than you, your hard work will eventually pay off. At this time, you should attempt to purchase a giant snake fang from another player, as it is much more powerful than the dagger you are probably wielding. You should also begin trying to join groups of adventurers and tackle greater monsters. This makes for great experience and can be extremely rewarding as far as your bank account is concerned.
