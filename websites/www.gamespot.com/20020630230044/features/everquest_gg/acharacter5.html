Creature Havens
Creatures and monsters have their homes just as the civilized races do. There exist numerous goblin camps and orc outposts - even a small fortress in Crushbone stands where orc slavers drive the free races under their unforgiving whips.

You can be certain that any mark on the landscape that is remotely out of the ordinary harbors some sort of creature who doesn't want to be disturbed. I remember being fascinated by a giant chessboard I happened upon in Butcher Block Mountains. The chess pieces were massive, far larger than I. I moved in for a closer look, and much to my horror, the great skeletal pawns sprang to life and quickly cleaved me with their mighty (and giant) swords.

As terrible as that was, it was not as bad as my first experience with the brownies of Lesser Faydark (never go to the left from the pass leading out of the Greater Faydark and into the Lesser). I wandered about the countryside and saw quite a few tree stumps littered about a medium-sized stone structure. Dismayed at this terrible treatment of the trees, I ventured around the front to see who the offenders were. I am ashamed to say that in a matter of not even seconds, I was slain by the brownie guards who blasted me with burning magic from a distance. I did not even see the faces of my attackers before they felled me.

TIP
Be suspicious of anything out of the ordinary.

Also be careful when a spot is cleared of monsters. Their kin can quickly return, and it is not uncommon for them to be more powerful than their fallen brethren. I recall smiting (barely) a dwarven encampment full of bandits with a companion of mine. While we congratulated ourselves, new bandits appeared. We attacked them again and later found (to our ultimate doom) that these new bandits were stronger than the previous one. I forgot one of the most important rules of adventure: Consider all monsters! Ah well, another lesson learned - painfully.

These are strange and oft-terrible times. Even a delightful and beautiful ring of mushrooms can hide certain doom. Fairies are beautiful, but often deadly.
