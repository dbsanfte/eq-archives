Fall Back (FB)
This command can apply to the whole group or an individual member. If you hear this command, especially if the group leader mentions your name specifically, you best retreat from the combat. Sometimes scrolling messages can be missed by a group member or lag may slow the response down. The bottom line is that if the leader tells you to fall back, you best listen. A good leader will help monitor all members health and sound the warning when someone starts flirting with death.

Incoming (INC)
A very common method for group combat is by having the group remain in a relatively safe location and have either the group leader or a specified scout bring the monsters to the group. If you hear this command shouted by the scout, you better be prepared for an incoming monster. Position yourselves for combat and find out which direction the scout is coming from. If you're prepared by the time the monster arrives, you can get a good jump on the combat.

Mana Report (MR)
All spell casters should get into the habit of using this command. This lets the group leader keep track of mana resources within the group. If mana starts running low, the group leader must judge how combat is going and giving the group insight as whether to stick out the fight or sound the retreat.

Out of Mana (OOM)
This command is a must for all spell casters. Notify both your party and leader when you are low on mana as well as when you run completely out. You can shorten this command to LOM for "low on mana" or use OOM for "out of mana." If the party doesn't know its healer is out of heals or that the wizard has no more mana for root or burn, the group is in serious trouble.

Each group is different, and you may develop your own commands for use within your group. The examples here are ones we feel you should incorporate into your group. You'll thank us later.
