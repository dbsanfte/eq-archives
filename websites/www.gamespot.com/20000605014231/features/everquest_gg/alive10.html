Group Health
A group comprises several people with a lot of combined health. Why would you let one person get mangled? Trade hits! Determine the amount of health everyone has and try to spread the damage across the entire group. This way, you have two to six people regenerating hit points instead of one regenerating while the others remain at full health. If you start taking some serious abuse, back away from the monster. However, beware of frenzied monsters. They tend to concentrate on one opponent until it's dead.

TIP
Always back away from a monster before you get below 40 percent health (two bubbles). This gives the group time to distract the monster from you and onto another of your group.

If you realize you're in over your head in a fight, and you hear someone yell, "Fall back," don't waste any more time. Back away, but do it in a controlled fashion. In all likelihood, the person sounding the retreat is probably already well away from the fight. People with more remaining health should try to lure the monster to guards or to a zone line. After you regroup, discuss whether you just had some back luck or were you simply not smart about attacking such a tough opponent.

TIP
Know when to let one or two members die. If you are fighting a really tough opponent, the group will still benefit if you can eventually slay the monster. Retreating from it can often take longer to regroup from than waiting for a member or two to return to the battlefield.
