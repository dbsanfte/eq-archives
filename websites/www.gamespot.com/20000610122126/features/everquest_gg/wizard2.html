Notes
Most characters start with two notes, and spellcasters will usually have a scroll or two of their basic beginning spell or spells. One note is a "tattered note-rolled up." Right click on this to read it and find out the name of the NPC to give it to. This is your first quest: to join your profession's guild.

TIP
Be sure you complete this quest, as the reward you get from it may literally save your skin.

Once you've read this note, proceed to your guild hall, which may or may not be somewhere in the vicinity of your starting point. To give the note to the person mentioned in it, left click on the note, then release the button. You should now be holding the note in your cursor. Turn your character so that the NPC you want to give the note to is in view and click the note on the NPC. A trade window will appear along the left side of your windowed view, showing items that you are offering as well as any items the person you are trading with is offering. Click the button at the bottom of this window to complete the trade.

NOTE
If you give this note to anyone other than its intended recipient, the NPC will simply say "Thank you," and the note is irretrievable.

When you give the note to the correct NPC, you will receive a small amount of experience and a piece of clothing. Open your inventory and drag the item of clothing onto the image of your character. Releasing the mouse button will equip your character with the item.

Congratulations, you've just completed your first quest. Rest assured, most other quests are not this simple, but you will soon appreciate the meager experience and clothing you just received.

The second note in your starting inventory is a PK (player-killer) note. If you want to fight other players, read this note and follow the instructions in it. If you do not ever want to fight another player, click on the note and move it to the Destroy button right above your inventory items. Another option is to put it in the bank for possible future use. Read the player vs. player section in Chapter 5 - Staying Alive to find out more about PKs and player vs. player (PvP) combat.
