Basic Survival

Levels 1-3
Only take on easy prey at the lowest levels, as your combat and casting skills are weak. Learn how to time your spells so that your adversary's strikes don't interrupt your casting. I have found that as a spellcaster, you can stay solo until about level five. Then, you're forced to find a party because the targets are just too tough for you to withstand too many hits.

Levels 4-7
At this level, you should be adding many spells to your repertoire. You must kill monsters that give decent loot, so concentrate on combat with fire beetles, goblins, and so on. The first spell you should buy is haze, which you can cast on the fighters in your group to make them more difficult to hit. Group with one solid fighter type and maybe a cleric to take on tougher monsters that yield good loot. Your main purpose in the group should be to help the fighters and hinder the monsters. Even if you don't cast any direct damage spells, casting strengthen on the melee fighters in your party and weaken on the monsters will give your group a huge advantage. Fear is also a nice spell, especially if the monster you are fighting has the upper hand, and your group must abort the attack.

Levels 8+
This is the level where you get chaotic feedback, a powerful spell that causes damage to your target and stuns it. You also get root, which freezes a target in place, giving you time to finish it off, heal, or save a party member who is being chased. Lesser shielding, a more powerful version of your early protection spell, should begin to replace minor shielding.
