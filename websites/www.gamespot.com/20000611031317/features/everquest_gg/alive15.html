Know What You're Attacking

If you decide to go hunting by yourself, be wary. It's nice to get out by yourself at times and enjoy the scenery and the thrill of a kill all your own. Always keep an eye on your health bar and attack creatures you know you can defeat. If a klicnik drone takes half your life, it's a good bet you're not ready to take on the queen klicnik.

If you already know from experience that you can take a creature, go ahead and attack, but be wary of siblings or other creatures in the area. Even the most harmless of creatures can do mortal damage if it finds some pals and gangs up on you. If you find yourself in big trouble with multiple creatures attacking you, get moving and try to escape.

TIP
When in combat, hit F9 to switch to an overhead camera view. This view will let you view the entire combat field, so you can see if another creature is sneaking up behind you.

The Healing Process

It's important to make sure you're capable of healing yourself if you become wounded. Your hit points will regenerate over time, provided you have food and water. That may be fine and dandy, but you can't exactly stop fighting a monster to wait for your health to regenerate. For those instances, you need the capability to heal yourself or bring along someone who can.

Healing comes in several forms, like binding your wounds, healing potions, and healing spells. Wounds may be bound with bandages to provide a minor aide to the wounds of battle. In addition, healing potions and spells can restore lost health in varying degrees.
