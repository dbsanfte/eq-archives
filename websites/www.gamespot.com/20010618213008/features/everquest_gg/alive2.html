Watch Where You Swing That

While every effort has been made by the game designers to ensure safety while in town, there are a few things you should keep in mind. A careless mistake can often lead to a swift death at the hands of a strong guard.

One surefire way to find yourself on the wrong end of a weapon is to mistakenly target and attack an NPC - especially one you are simply trying to buy goods from or converse with. Crucival T'almud's tale is an example of learning the hard way.

ANECDOTE
Having finally stepped through the black moongate onto the dirt packed west gate of a city called Freeport, I was initially overwhelmed by the new sights and sounds around me. Eventually, I overcame my wonder and decided to be human instead of a goggling plant rooted to the ground. The first people I see, of course, are those nicely dressed, ever present, and protective guards that watch over us at the gates. Being the friendly sort, I decide to ingratiate myself with these fine fellows who I am sure will more than earn their pay saving my butt from nasty orcs I already see chasing other hapless citizens like myself. So, I walk up to one of them and target him to see his name: Guard Reskin.

"Hail Guard Reskin," I try to say. But before I could utter even those words, I see myself lying on the ground. Dead.

Being unfamiliar with the layout of the controls, I had failed to enter into chat mode before talking. Indeed, I did say hello to the friendly guard, the H key automatically did that for me. But then that darn A key immediately following it did me in, since it activated my auto-attack functions.

It was not a pretty sight. Please don't make the same mistake I did!

The moral of the story is, "Be careful of your auto-attack key. It is very easy to hit it prior to hitting the enter key to speak." Many players reprogram their auto-attack key to a noncharacter key to avoid this deadly mistake.
