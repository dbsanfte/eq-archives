Basic Combat

Combat is initiated simply by targeting your opponent and attacking it. Targeting is set immediately when a foe attacks you. If you are fighting several opponents, you must target a second opponent yourself after felling the first one.

You can target an opponent by clicking on it or pressing the target key for nearest player or NPC (default F7 and F8). One of the most important things to remember about combat is the Consider command. After targeting, press C or type /con, and you will be told your odds of success. If the results are in green, this opponent is so easy to kill that you won't get experience for killing it. Blue means the odds are in your favor, and you will get experience points. White means the outcome could go either way; attack alone with a good deal of risk. Yellow means that you are outclassed, but can probably take it with a few friends. Red means you're outclassed, and if you attack this creature, expect casualties.

Attacking is accomplished by casting an offensive spell, firing a ranged weapon, or pressing your attack key and staying next to the opponent for one weapon cycle of the weapon your holding. Chasing flying bats can be a challenge for new players. Try to stay just behind them 'til you get a swing off, and they will stop to fight you. Spells and missile attacks will cause the creature to run to you in most cases. Your character will continue to swing until the combat is resolved. During combat, you can use other skills, such as special attacks and spells. If you have secondary attacks like kick, make sure you have a hotkey made for use while you're in combat.
