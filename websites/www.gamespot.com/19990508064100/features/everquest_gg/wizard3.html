Spell Scrolls
If you are a spellcaster, you will begin with a basic spell or two, which you must scribe in your spellbook. Spells on scrolls are basically useless during gameplay. To effectively use spells, you must scribe the spell into your spellbook and then memorize it.

Other spells are also available to buy in or around your guild hall. See section spells to find out more about preparing your spells for casting.

Skill Training
When you find your guild hall and complete your first quest, do not be so quick to leave. As a first-level character, you have five skill practice points you can use to build some skills prior to braving the world. Double clicking on the guildmaster or a trainer will bring up a training menu along the left side of the screen.

This training menu will list all the skills you can learn and use. Scroll down the list and note which skills are listed as "no charge" and which skills have a level designation by them. The skills designated as no charge indicate you can use those skills at your level and that training for that skill is free. Skills that have a level number beneath them indicate you cannot use or train in that skill until you have reached that level.

TIP
Don't waste your precious skill points on weapons or magic skills. Those skills are easiest to increase through adventuring. Use or save your point for harder skills to advance in, such as any of the craft skills.

You can come back to train at any time, spending practice points you have earned by gaining levels. To spend your points, highlight the skill desired on the left by clicking on it and click on the Practice button at the bottom left of the skills list. You are limited to five skill points per level gained, so choose wisely.

Your first few practices in your skills are free, but more advanced training will eventually require payment to the trainer. To view all your skills and their rankings, click the animated icon or Persona button on the left of the screen and then the Skills button at the top left. As you move about the world and fight, the skills you use will advance on their own, without training, as long as the basics of the skill are known. The maximum a skill will rise on its own is dependent on your level. As you advance in level, this ceiling will rise as well. There is no cap or maximum total number of skills a character can have. Each skill has its own ceiling, which is based on the character's race, class, and level.
