IDEAS - Guild Leadership

For the Roleplaying Guild Startup

EverQuest Shadow Knights: This article pertains to EverQuest Shadowknights (your traditional anti-paladins) and their brethren, the undead-summoning Necromancers.

1) Race support. Chances are, if you're a guild of Shadowknights, your guild will consist of trolls, ogres and dark elves, all of which are inherently evil. Thus, by guiding, helping, and protecting your own evil race, your guild will gain a sort of "good notoriety". You will get support from your own people and they will look up to the evil deeds you do and perhaps receive Brimstone's "WOW factor", wanting to join. Support is always useful when...

2) Raids. A favorite past-time of evil guilds (especially shadowknights) is the raiding of human and other good race's cities. This will certainly draw the "WOW factor" from your race and a bit of respect from the raided race if you do it maturely. Advice: don't loot the corpses of your enemies, this will really make them like you.

3) Blocks. Due to ogres and troll's large sizes (the two most popular shadowknight races) you can block bridges, tunnels, dungeon entrances, etc. and demand a toll or item for crossing. You can also ambush...

4) Necromancer's invisibility. Great for ambushes.

5) Recover the Dark Reaver, Fleshripper or other very high level Shadowknight only swords and display them in your guild hall for all newbies to see. Major "wow factor".

Submitted by: Antigrob, 11th level Shadowknight, Rallos Zek server, EverQuest

Clerical Guild: Your members select an imaginary god or relic, and that becomes the focus of your members. You can hold rituals, sacrifices, events, etc... all using that symbol to promote fun roleplay. Your can either be a good aligned version of this, and heal fellow players, escort them to safety, offer free things, etc. Or you can play the evil version of this and harm people, steal them away (If they play along) and perform dark rituals on them, etc. The "Clerical" guild has SO MUCH to offer any RPG.

Knights Guild: Suits of plate mail, shiny weapons, honor and codes of virtue, all play into this theme. Uniforms should be strictly adhered to, roleplaying is essential, helping innocent players and providing them with safety will be a great focus for the good version of this guild. Of course, on the flip side, is the evil version. The "Anti Paladin" or "Evil Knight". Endless possibilities on this one...

Barbarian Guild: Rugged, mean-spirited, broken english, savage warriors. The barbarian guild, in my opinion, should consist primarily of "Fighter-Types", with maybe an occasion "Shaman" (aka Healer). Barbarians HATE magic, and therefore, this should be a common element in the "Barbarian Themed" guilds. Be creative when playing this guild type, there are SO many fun things you can do as a mentally-challenged giant.

Sage Guild: Ok, Sages are scholars, people who take knowledge and wisdom VERY serious, it is simply the most important aspect of their lives. If you seek to play a sage guild, consider a Single-Theme for your Sages, or perhaps allow your Sages the flexibility to be a master of any skill they wish. Sages 'rarely' fight with weapons of any kind, save the occasional 'bash on the head' from a staff or wand. The "Sage" would much rather battle wits, or debate a given situation, as opposed to drawing blood and risking death. You can of course have "Good" or "Evil" themed guilds based around this type.

Druidic Guild: Ok, my favorite 'theme' is the Druid. I love the idea of someone who is a master of the natural elements - Earth, Water, Fire, Air, etc. The "Keepers of the Forest", has many fun possibilities for players. You could play your guild many ways with this theme. Perhaps attacking players who litter, or assisting players who are responsible in the wilderness. Granting escort to players who seem lost in your woods, and have respected their surroundings. Imagine a guild of 15 druids, all hidden (Invisible) in a certain spot of the woods. Some 'lone' player enters the area, unsuspecting... then at once you all emerge and bestow some honor (or threat) upon him/her. You think that he/she would be impressed? You can BET on it. Have fun..

Secretive Guild: Tons of themes can fit into this guild-type. Assassin guild, "Sage" guild, Spy Guild, etc. Find your edge, and stick to it. Perhaps SELL your secretive data for in-game gold. Make mules to spy or blend in with various guild communities, record all you hear, save logs, make them available on web pages, etc. I think that THIS type of guild would be a TON of fun... if done right. Use your imagination!

Thieves Guild: First of all, bank thieves that abuse fellow players and the game engine are pathetic. Using exploits to steal from another player is NOT cool, but more along the lines of ignorant, asinine, pathetic and of course, DewDish. I would much rather be a 'mark' to someone who sweet talks me, perhaps bumps into me and steals away with my things, etc. Anything creative in an attempt to rob me is fine... so long as it is honest (you know what I mean), mature, etc. I personally think that you should NEVER exploit someone and leave them high and dry. If you are going to steal, fine, enjoy it and be cool about it. I think that if you wanted to be a 'respected' thief, one with style, you would be quite responsible about it. Perhaps make a web page with your latest 'marks' and details on it. Perhaps offer them a way to get the stuff back, perhaps in exchange for some dark and devious deed, or for gold payment, etc. One last thing: Don't be the kind of guild that uses cheats to break into peoples secure homes... that is 'dewdish' and really pathetic. Instead, stick to more 'social and creative' ways to steal and earn a living.

For the "Player Killing" Guild Startup

Ideas: For the most part, your traditional "PK" doesn't really get to involved in "roleplaying". So to say, "Do one of the above 'themes'", just wouldn't do. Therefor, I have a suggestion (Based upon various feedback I received after writing the "Kinder PK" article):

Select a "Purpose" and "Trademark", then kill  your 'marks' based upon this criteria. One person told me that they will only kill players who wear green or red clothing. They then only steal items from the player that are "Green" or "Red". As you can see, without saying much of anything, the feel of "Purpose" and "Reason", as well as indirect "Roleplaying" sets in. You don't even HAVE to roleplay...

Another person said they only kill players who wear platemail and act or are perceived to be "Knights", good aligned knights.

Others stated that they attack players who talk "Dewd Speak"... we can use more of this kind of PK.. ;)

Others selected victims based upon gender... I know, sick..

Others pretended that they were werewolves, and after killing victims.. they would rez them, then cut up their corpse and pretend to eat it. They returned just about ALL of the 'marks' belongings, and really only wanted to keep the head. THIS was extremely cool, in my opinion.

As you can see, if you give yourself a theme and stick to it... you will soon earn fame and respect, as well as recognition. Players will soon like your play-style, and praise you for your intelligent gameplay.

If you are a player killer who kills without style, if you gang up on players and leave them high and dry, or insult them after they are down... you are simply being an antisocial punk who smells like a typical 13 year old BOY. You may not grasp this concept yet, but it really is a pathetic way to play any online GAME.

-Brimstone
