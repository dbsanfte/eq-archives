Kicking It Up A Notch

I first wanted to begin this article by thanking Gasper and the UO Vault Staff for allowing me to express my ideas, opinions and advice regarding Ultima Online, but more importantly, the UO Community at large. Together with MoOk, I feel that we have much to offer everyone. With that all said, I shall continue with my article.

It never ceases to amaze me. So many guilds popping up each day, all with a dream to become the greatest, the "most feared", the largest, the best "roleplayers" and so on. Some fail, while others seem to do it effortlessly. What is the key to success? I am sure that this question is asked quite often, and not many people know the answer. Some guilds have strict rules that make their members stay in character at all times while in game. Other guilds demand that their members have the highest stats and ultra-precision PVP tactics. Extreme? Perhaps, but at least they know what they want and they are taking steps to achieve 'greatness'.

In this article, I wanted to mention some equally important aspects of guild life. Some 'ideas' to help spice up your membership, increase your friendships and bonds, and perhaps help you to break the current 'mold' that may be keeping you from expanding into other games, or simply keeping you from doing more with your up and coming guild.

Move Over, Tommy Hilfiger

A few years ago, I created a design for a t-shirt that would feature our guild on it, along with the names of all of our members. The task seemed huge, and I had no idea where to start. Picking up the phone book, I quickly found a place who did silk screens and within minutes, I knew what was needed from me to begin. Two months later, our guild sported shirts (about fifty of them) and thus began our REAL WORLD advertising. It was amazing. We were all so proud, and so eager to wear the stylish shirts. So far, we have had two designs, one each year... and a total of about 140 'miniature ads' are walking around the world each day. Not bad, is it?

I'll Buy THAT For a Dollar!

Several months ago, I was thinking about how much I wanted to recruit new members who were located closer to my area (Pennsylvania). With all the "real life" gatherings we have, it would be great to have a ton of people from my area attending. While on my way home one day, I stopped in a fast food restaurant and noticed they were selling "Shamrocks" for a charity. For a dollar, you could write your name on it and they store would post this 'Shamrock' on their wall. Well, I paid my buck and wrote, "www.lostorder.com - Online Gaming Guild" on it. I never expected it to work, but I felt good about it nonetheless. Two weeks later, an application for membership came in from someone who, "Saw the shamrock ad at the Subway shop". Amazing. So simple, and it only cost a buck.

Real Life Meetings and Furthering the Guild Unity

Has your guild ever held a "Real Life Gathering" yet? If not, you are definately missing out. They take a little time to organize, but if handled properly, they usually come off without a hitch. You should begin with a survey to your guild, asking for the locations (city/state/country) of everyone who is a member. Next, select a region that is a good midpoint for everyone involved, if possible. At this time, it is really up in the air and you can have any 'theme' you want for this gathering. To name a few: Paintball, Bowling, Sky diving, Sight Seeing, hitting an arcade and grabbing some dinner, etc. Stepping away from the virtual world with your 'virtual friends' is truly remarkable. If you think that you are good friends with someone now, and you only have known them from "UO", I can speak truthfully when I say that meeting people in real life usually increases the closeness you have. If you plan a RL Gathering and need some help, just send me a message and maybe I can offer you some pointers.

Kindness and Giving During The Holidays

I will end my first article with one last idea that will again tip the scales of "Guild Unity" and "Pride". One of my biggest inspirations, Lord Stubby of Akalabeth, suggested that our guild donate REAL money to a charity. He organized the entire thing, providing the name of the Charity, the address to mail the cash/checks to, as well as taking pictures afterwards to show us the happiness of the children who will benefit from our offerings this Christmas. Three years ago, I never would have imagined a guild existance that exceeded all of my expectations. A group of people who didn't only play games and worry about the virtual world, but an "Organization" with so much more potential, that they could affect the REAL world as well. If you stop and think about it, it is truly a great force you all have at your beck and call. Guilds. Groups of people from all over the world, united 'virtually' for a common goal. Perhaps after you read this article, you will all step back and think about your next 'big hurdle'. After this article, what creative things will you do to KICK IT UP A NOTCH for your guild members?

I hope that this inspires some of you... I truly do. If you do anything different with your organization as a result of this letter, please, let me know. In the future, I would LOVE to feature your guilds and organizations in follow up articles about the things you did to enhance your virtual bonds, or even your real life bonds.

Humbly I remain,

Lord Brimstone
