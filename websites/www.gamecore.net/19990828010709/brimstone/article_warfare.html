ARTICLE: Organized Guild Warfare - UO

Posted: 5-14-99
"Organized Guild Warfare for Ultima Online"
Written by Brimstone, LOA

Overview: Organized Guild Warfare System
One of the greatest things that two guilds can achieve with each other is TRUST. EVERYTHING that you do in guild warfare, requires trust. With some guilds, the estimate of participants can number anywhere from 15 to 200 people, and for the most part, guilds often have those one or two members who are 'trouble'. It happens, sometimes, someone will break rules and threaten all the hard work you have put into the relationship with another guild. This chapter will discuss ways for you to approach a guild for warfare, strengthen trust and friendships, and organize some very excellent guild wars. This chapter will focus on Ultima Online, however, it can apply to many other games that are being released soon.

Your Guild's Image: Mature, Respected and Professional
If you want to be taken seriously, and if you wish to earn the trust of another guild, you simply MUST have members who will act maturely and respect the fellow players. Just one person can spoil the fun to be had, so you want to make sure that you have the right people in your guild, before you go arranging 'Organized Guild Warfare' (OGW).

If you recruit the right people, and help them grow within your guild, you should be fine. Try to stress the importance of your policies, your relations with other guilds/players etc. Explain that breaking those rules can and will result in their loss of membership. For the most part, guilds who are actively looking to enhance the fun of the 'game' and 'guild' experience, will have members who will NOT want to be 'banned' and removed from that fun. Being on the outside of something so great is NOT fun, and they will hopefully shape UP before being shipped out.

Making Your Approach, Finding the Right Enemy!
In earlier chapters, I mentioned the importance of reading guild advertisements on the various message boards. There was a reason for that, and I will now go into a little more detail. Your guild has a mission, a theme, a reason for being an organization in whatever game you play. Everyone has enemies, and therefore, it is imperative that you find a good 'match' for your guild. If you are the good guys, and perhaps you are a 'Paladin' guild of holy heroes, well, you may want to find a nice undead, evil-doing group of damned people. That way, right off the bat, you all have something to write about, and something to increase the 'RPG element' of your upcoming relationship and battles.

Once you believe that you have found a match, you now must get to know their membership rules, their personalities, their 'goals', etc. If they are KewlDoodz, and if their vocabulary consists of 5 words, AVOID THEM LIKE THE PLAGUE. Odds are, they aren't interested in being fair, or at the very least, they will not help you achieve the high levels of role-playing you are looking for. This is an unfortunate stereotype, but one that is quite valid with online communities.

Before you dive into wars, name calling and story telling, first focus on the friendship portion of this relationship. Have IRC meetings with them, and discuss your backgrounds. Perhaps introduce your real life persona, then introduce your "in game" persona. Talk about your guild's goals, morals, purpose, etc. Even discuss some combined RPG elements that your guilds will share in common. For instance, the good guys may have an artifact of power that grants its owner unlimited knowledge of the mystic arts. The opposing guild may be on a quest to remove that artifact from  your possession, thus making a reason for your war. You have a gajillion reasons that your guilds may be at war, so use your imaginations and have fun with this. I promise that by the end of your meetings with this enemy guild, you will all truly admire each other and look forward to learning more about each other.

With the seeds of friendship blooming, you can finally discuss bigger and brighter things. War. A fun thing that I encourage our members to do is to visit the enemies forums (i.e. message boards, chat rooms, etc.) and initiate role-playing confrontations. Keep it in game, and don't ever come across as if you are insulting the 'player', and not the 'character'. Keep it fun. Write stories, perhaps telling tales of your guild winning some minor skirmishes, or one character overcoming another. An important thing to consider would be to write stories that not only GLAMORIZE your own guild, but also write a few that makes your enemy look good as well. This is a good show of faith, and will help promote similar actions from your new friends. This is RPG, you win some and you lose some, share the fun with everyone. After bombarding the forums of each side with 'War Teasers', it is time to organize the event that you have all been waiting for - the BATTLE!!

Keeping the Sides Even and Some Rules
As you know, some guilds are larger than others. It is important that you allow the smaller guild to set the # of participants for each side. You may be able to put 30 men/women into combat, whereas your enemy may only have 10. Keep this in mind, and make the best of it. Later, I will discuss how you can get ALL your members involved in the war, even if they weren't selected to fight against the enemy. After you set the # of participants for each side, it is now time to set the rules.

I believe that looting sucks, and when having a war for fun, there is absolutely NO reason to loot and steal from your friends. As I said earlier, we are focusing on UO for this chapter, therefore, the rules will cater to UO rules:

Rule One:   No Looting, ever. Unless it is to protect your friend/foes things from decaying or being thieved from a third party player. Then looting is by all means necessary.

Rule Two:   No Deathrobe Killing. Once you foe is killed, allow them to resurrect and have faith that they will remain out of the battle until the war is over. Deathrobed players have an important part to play, they will now become "Observers", and be assigned to resurrect other players, as well as to guard against 3rd Party Troublemakers.

Rule Three:   No Town Killing. Unless your guilds enjoy town killing, and having a swarm of 3rd Party idiots stealing/looting your dead, I would simply avoid town wars altogether. It leads to unnecessary chaos.

Rule Four:   No insults or rudeness to your enemy. Hey, this is meant to be a fun event, but still things can get out of hand. If you are upset, do not take it out on your enemy. Wait until the war is over, and discuss your differences, maturely, with your guild and your enemy forces. Keep a cool head, you will be thankful you did later.

Rule Five:   Respect and Adhere to the Rules!! Don't break rules, bottom line. If you enjoy the wars you have setup, don't go screwing it up! Respect each other and respect the rules, always.

The Observer Position
I told you I had a role for ALL your members to fill during the wars, and this is it. The Observer Role is a key for every battle. If you don't have enough Observers, I would avoid warring until a later time. The Observers must dress in a bright color, so as to stand out against the actual war participants. Each Observer is in charge of guarding the fallen bodies and loot, resurrecting and healing the dead, and engaging 3rd party idiots who are jealous and seek to ruin your events. Trust me, Observers are VERY needed, and this role can be quite fun!

Observers should NEVER take ANY part in the actual battle. Healing participants who are fighting is a no-no. You simply are their to watch, protect and if you can, grab a few screenshots!

Once a player has been killed in war, he must get his stuff and then keep his/her deathrobe ON. Do not remove it - this robe will set you apart from the other war participants. Every person who dies instantly become an Observer once resurrected.

The "Point System" - Upgrading your Forces!
This is all new, and I created it weeks ago after a recent battle between the LOA and the OES. We had a hard time keeping the sides fair, while still allowing each side to have SOME element of surprise. To remedy this, I decided to take rules from other games and apply them to Ultima Online. Thus, the point system. When each guild sets a war, you can set the # of human participants, then assign a # of points for each guild to use as they see fit. This chart can be modified however you see fit, it is entirely up to your guilds.

 

Upgrades and Point Cost
White Wyrm 300 pts
Dragon 250 pts
Nightmare 200 pts
Drake 150 pts
Hellhounds 100 pts
Energy Vortex * 150 pts
Daemon * 100 pts
Fire, Water, Air Elementals * 50 pts.
Earth Elemental * 25 pts
Unlimited Use of GH and GE Poisons 200 pts
Pets (i.e. horse, bear, gorilla, ostard, etc) 25 pts per pet
Additional Guild Member 100 pts
* Summoned Creatures must not be dispelled by the opposing guild. This is due to how easy and cheap it is to dispel anything summoned. To keep things fair/fun, I recommend straying from the dispel spell.

Feel free to modify the points according to how you feel they would best represent your guilds. We originally had 'Deadly Poison' on this chart, since it can seriously impact the war. After some discussion, we decided to remove this cost from the system.

Armed with this table, you can now challenge a guild quite easily by setting the maximum points for the war, the time and place, and the number of participants for each side.

After the War
Once the war is over, it is a good time to hold a mini-festival to celebrate the event that just took place. Good and evil, together, can put aside their differences and intoxicate their characters into oblivion. This further adds to the friendships that you seek to form. The winner of each war SHOULD have bragging rights, that is only right. They should be expected to write stories of their victory, posting them all over the known forums of man. In addition, it wouldn't hurt to have stories written by the losing side as well, as a 'gift' to your enemy, admitting defeat.

Your stories, screenshots and success with these battles can literally become your KEY advertising methods. When you post to message boards about these wars, and display images of grand battles... don't you think that you will attract others who will be looking for some action? You can bet your arse that you will get applications. Successfully implementing guild warfare is a sign of a mature, dedicated, professional guild/leadership and members. If you've got it, FLAUNT IT!

The Award for Victory!
The Lost Order of Akalabeth and Order of Ebon Skull haven't set up an award yet for out battles, but I think that we may do so soon. It would be nice to have a pot of gold (sitting in the bank) reserved for the winning side. Perhaps something like a Sword of Vanquishing, or perhaps a pot of 10,000gp, or maybe 2 or 3 full spellbooks, etc. That way, when you win, you know that SOMETHING is coming your way. You can set this up with the war notice, and simply say that the losing side must have something worth 'x' value to give to the winners. It should then be up to the losing side to come up with whatever they choose, so long as it all is worth the agreed upon value. It can be cotton, ale, boots, gold, etc. - perhaps a funny ending to a great battle!
