ARTICLE: Finding a Guild to Join

Posted: 5-20-99
"Finding a Guild to Join"
Written by Brimstone, LOA

Overview: Finding a Guild for You!

You are at the point in your online career that you wish to seek a guild, a group of players who will hopefully enrich your online gaming experiences and perhaps lead to many new friendships. Guilds come in many flavors, such as roleplaying, PVP (or powergaming), or a mixture of both RP and PVP. In addition to those flavors is the age factor of guilds, some are a younger crowd while others are older and a bit more reserved. Top this off with the fact that there are literally thousands to choose from, and you have yourself a heck of a task ahead of you.

In this guide, I hope to answer some questions, provide some helpful tips and hopefully point you in the right direction in your quest for finding a home for you and your online character.

Giving All You've Got!

If you are guildless and looking, you are worth your weight in gold. There is a guild out there right now looking for you, a guild that has hopes and dreams of becoming large, famous, and prosperous. That is a great feeling for you, the guildless. You have some power in your hands, if used properly. You should take time and do your homework, search around, interview guild members in game or in IRC/Message Boards, ask around, etc. Don't JUMP into anything, it isn't worth it. Once you find the right guild and you believe in this guild, you truly should dedicate yourself to making THAT guild the very best. You shouldn't expect a hand out, you shouldn't expect people to pour all their time into you, and you shouldn't expect to have your way with their hard-earned possessions in game.

I stated that you were needed and wanted in a guild out there, and this is true. You deserve respect from your guild of choice, you deserve some 'breaking in' time and some hands-on training in the guild rules and goals. You do not however, deserve hand outs. Once you join a guild, you become a part of that force and must contribute equally to earn any forms of payment, rank, awards or wealth.

As a guild leader, I have always focused on the PERSON behind the character, not the character. I focus on what the person is like, morally. Does he/she worry about their share of loot? Or do they first worry that everyone else is taken care of? If the person applying for our guild is a truly nice person, giving and respectful, loyal and hard working, they are THEN, and only then, worth their weight in gold. I have witnessed players who sought to join a guild for one purpose, mooching and leeching from our hard earned success, and often not wanting to offer anything in return. Players like this jump from one guild to the next, often unable to survive and persist in ANY guild for more than a month or two.

DON'T be that kind of guild member...

What Do You Really Want?

You need to really think hard about what you want in a guild. Who are you and what is your character going to be like when you play? Are you evil? Are you good? Do you like the ocean? Are you aspiring to tame dragons and use them to serve Justice? Do you see yourself sacrificing innocents and praying to the dark gods of Sosaria/Norrath? If you are joining a 'theme' guild, you will have to define your character before applying or searching for a guild. Also, if your heart lies in the roleplaying environment, please think hard before joining a PVP oriented guild. This is a stereotype, but for the most part, PVP guilds aren't too fond of roleplayers, and roleplayers don't mix well with PVP oriented people/guilds. It is a fact, I have seen this first hand. If you can find a guild that truly loves both and has worked to mesh the two styles, then jump on that application form and snatch it up fast!

The Roleplaying Guild

Just because a guild says they roleplay, doesn't mean they REALLY roleplay well. I have seen guilds claim to be RP oriented, yet they were involved in major PVP, their RP skills, if used, were horrible and crude, and they basically had a bad reputation. If a guild is active and truly roleplays, you will see this quite clearly on their public pages and forums. Their news will be written in a roleplay manner (sometimes), their site will most likely have stories written in RP, their message boards will usually have posts written in RP, and their chat rooms will readily accept RPing within, and not look at you like an idiot. Of course, if you meet this guild 'in game' and they roleplay well, that is a huge bonus.

If you wish to make a GREAT impression on a guild of this nature, prepare a biography for your character and tell the guild a story about your persona. Make them WANT to have you in their guild. Also, when you fill out the application form, praise this guild and tell them WHY you selected them to be your new home. Guilds love hearing this, it helps pump up the motivation and also tells them indirectly that they are doing SOMETHING right.

Roleplaying guilds and players are often stereotyped by PVP guilds as being goofy, stupid, queer, too caught up in the game, etc. Depending on how deep your guild-choice is involved in roleplaying, expect SOME negative comments from PVP guilds who fall under the "Immature" category.

The PVP Guild

To join a PVP guild, you pretty much need to know your stuff. You should be well versed in the various skills in that game that are KEY to fighting prowess. If archery is the new skill on the block, then learn it fast and master it. Most PVP players also find comfort in the use of 3rd party programs, this isn't a bad thing, it is simply a necessity. Your enemies have these tools and if you plan to survive and help a guild get the 'edge' over their enemies, you need to have the proper tools of the trade. Period.

I have noticed that a lot of PVP players feel the need to brag a lot. This isn't a blanket statement, and it certainly doesn't apply to every player who PVPs, however, the majority that I have met does fall into this category. I risk much by saying this, but I strongly believe it to be true. If you can PVP well, that is a great thing and you need to let the guild know. More importantly, you need to log in and PROVE it. Get your hands dirty, kick some ass and take names. Even if you die, at least you can make some form of impression on the guild you wish to join. I think that if you can PVP well, and NOT brag and become arrogant about it, you will be the very BEST PVP-type player in game.

I have noticed that there are two types of PVP guilds: Immature and Mature. You will find that many (not all) younger players fall into the Immature area, and hey, that is normal. They are young, impressionable, and they readily fit in to that crowd. I am not making fun of them here, simply making an observation. Also, this younger crowd of PVPrs aren't too shabby, they are effective at fighting styles. One of the major downfalls of this crowd is their use of bad language, game exploiting, hacks, cheats, abusive actions against other players, and last but not least, the use of "KeWlDuDe'ish" typing. This form of player seeks to not only hurt the characters in game, but seeks to hurt the player behind the keyboard on an emotional level. As you can see, this is my opinion and my guide, so take this with a grain of salt and make of it what you will. I am sure I will get lots of flak for this. To counter this statement, I can say that I *have* met young players (13-14yr) who were every bit as mature as the others in our guild.

As for the Mature PVP guild, they are quite simply the best to join. They are respectful of the roleplaying guilds, and give them room to breath. They are perfectionists at the art of PVPing, they often use the 3rd party programs to further excel at PVP, and their members refrain *usually* from the use of bad language, exploits, abusive behavior, etc. Simply put, they love PVP and enjoy a good fight. They aren't looking to hurt the PLAYER behind the character. If you to join a good PVP guild that is worth anything and will make you a better person, join the Mature PVP guild. They are out there. Notice: The use of most 3rd party applications is strictly prohibited by OSI. Using these programs can and will get you banned from playing Ultima Online. Make sure that the 3rd party application you are using has been approved by OSI. Also, make sure that when you download ANY 3rd party program, the source is reliable. THOUSANDS of players have been hacked/infected with various viruses from downloading applications that are bogus. Be cautious! - Brimstone

The Mixed Guild of RP and PVP

It exists, I swear. I happen to love this form of guild, and I should, this is what I created long ago with the other LOA founding fathers. It is a volatile mixture, that is for sure, and it takes time to nurture and build. To this day, we still have some issues creep up on us, and sometimes, it leads to revolts, members quitting, hurt feelings and a drop in guild moral. A large price to pay for a diversified guild. At any rate, if you can find a guild like this, in the shard/server of your choice, strongly consider it. It gives you access to creative playing styles, up to date information on in-game plots and stories, as well as access to very competant PVP players. If you join a guild like this, make sure you take your time to get acquainted first, before jumping right in. Get to know the members, the guild history, the mentality behind what is right and wrong for that guild, etc.

I think that revolts and guild upsets are frequent in this form of group because you have to very different 'goals' and 'mentality'. The roleplayers aren't big on Anti- or Pking. The RPrs often feel that 3rd party programs are 'cheating', they feel that the PVPrs perhaps earn all the fame and often considered the 'heroes' when they answer a call for help, or lead a successful raid. There is also the "Circle" effect that diverse guilds face. When you have two or three elements in a guild, such as RP and PVP, you will see very defined 'circles' of friendships taking place. The RP members bond together, and the PVP members bond together, and then some members find friendships in BOTH groups. So long as the member to member respect is healthy and enforced, this situation will not blow up on you. If a highly successful or famous player on the RP or the PVP circle comes under 'percieved' attack by the other members of the guild, or the Council of the guild, expect the boat to rock.

As a player in a guild like this, expect moral issues to come into play, it happens. If you still strongly believe in the leadership and the ideals of the guild, be loyal and faithful to the GUILD and the leadership, not the members who have personal issues with the guild and leadership. I have seen people quit, thinking they were making the right decision, only to ask for readmittance days or weeks later. They got caught up in someone else's problems, and left without thinking about how much happiness they truly found in that guild. Luckily, our guild believes that members in good standing should readily be allowed back into our fold, for they made a mistake and learned from it. Other guilds aren't so forgiving, so make sure you hold your tongue until you are 100% sure you wish to go...

Donations, Time and Involvment for the Guild!

I just wanted to stress how important it is that you donate all that you can to your guild. Donate gold, items, your Real Life skills (html, cgi, graphics, etc.), etc. in an effort to make your guild stand out above the rest. "It takes a village.", and everyone needs to work together in the success of the guild.

Please realize that the guild leadership cannot do it all, they rely heavily upon the time and efforts of all members in making the guild all that it can be. As a member, your in game actions can lead to good press (i.e. saving someone from a PK, being a ruthless killer, donating gold to newbies, guiding someone to a shop they are looking for, etc.), and you out-of-game actions ALSO can lead to good press (i.e. Intelligent posts to public message boards, game resource pages for players, leading a cause for the game you play, donating graphics to other players, etc.) - all of these things inevitably lead to great advertising for your guild. So do it, and let others know why YOUR guild is the best.

Reporting Your Actions

In closing, here is a tip for ALL guild members, no matter WHAT form of guild you are in. If you want to progress, get awards, get promotions, become famous, earn respect, etc. - you need to make your deeds and actions KNOWN to everyone in your guild. The best way to do this is via a report. Simply write your report of your deed/s via your guild mailing list, message board, icq, etc. and send it to everyone who is in your guild. Do this, and be proud. :)

Actually, the LOA has found that the stories submitted (often in RP style) were excellent sources for advertising our guild to the RP community. In addition, getting screenshots of PVP battles and writing stories in OOC also lead to great advertising to the PVP players out there. Again, the members of the guild made a huge impact on our success.

In Closing

I hope that this helps all of you seeking to become a member of a guild, or seeking to get noticed more in the guild you are in. There is no reason why you should simply "BE" in a guild and not contribute as much as possible. As a member of a guild, you can make a humungous impact and lead your friends to great success and fame. Don't hold back, be creative, be inventive, be involved and be thankful.

Also, if you feel that the leadership or the guild overall is no longer as good as it used to be, make sure you mention this in meetings, using tact, of course. As a member, you deserve the chance to share your concerns and keep the leadership of that guild on track. Without good leaders, a guild will most likely fail. If your leaders cannot handle your 'tactful' comments, perhaps it is time to begin your quest and search for another guild.

The very last thing I want to mention is... you are playing a game, your guild is comprised of many different people, different ages, different nationalities, and different moral codes. Don't get too personally involved in anything that will make you an unhappy person. Have FUN with the games you play and the guild you are in. I have gone full circle now, and I know very well the 'personal' side of being in a guild. I have lost sleep, I have lost friends I met online, I have been depressed at times, etc. This is all part of being in a guild and associated with people on a day to day basis. If you know you cannot handle such things, avoid a guild altogether and keep things simple.

Good luck!

Brimstone,
