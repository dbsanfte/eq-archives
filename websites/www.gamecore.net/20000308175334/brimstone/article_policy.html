ARTICLE: Sample Policy for your Guild

Posted: 5-14-99
"Sample Policy for your Guild"
Written by Brimstone, LOA

Overview: Sample Policy
To avoid chaos and to limit the amount of "Political Mumbo Jumbo", you need to have a good policy and proceedures in place. If person 'x' does something against the policies, and if you have been looking for a reason to remove person 'x', you can always fall back on your rules. I always made it a rule of mine NOT to ever remove someone, just because I didn't like them. I had to have a reason, and it had to be a valid reason. Owning and running a guild should not be a "Popularity Contest", you shouldn't recruit 'cookie cutter' members, and throw out those people who perhaps are different than you. I believe that the best guild is a diverse guild, as each person adds a flavor and value to your community. Also, if you get in the habit of ruling without any form of order,  you will not only lose members, but you will never see any new ones either. Let me correct myself, you will get members, but they will be the antisocial types who didn't cut it in another guild that DID have good rules and enforced them.

To help get you on your way, I would like to cut and paste the policies that the LOA created so long ago. This was a work in progress for MONTHS, and it evolved over time. We removed things that didn't work, and added things that needed to be enforced. On our application form, we specifically asked if the person applying read the rules and policies. This is to cover our butts should we later choose to remove him/her from the guild based on their actions.

Before moving on, I wanted to point something out. Our application form is located at the BOTTOM of our policies page. This is so that people HAVE to at least see the page before finding the link to join. :)

The Sample Policy - Feel Free to Modify for Your Guild

The Lost Order of Akalabeth's Mission Statement:

The Lost Order of Akalabeth is a Guild devoted to role-playing and ensuring the maximum enjoyment of Ultima Online for as many of the people within the Ultima multiverse as possible. We strive to uphold all the Virtues within the game, and keep ourselves above reproach. We are a Guild firmly rooted in the concept of "Goodness" for all that the term implies. We do not condone mindless player killing, and as such shun all those who participate in player killing, yet neither are we a Guild of bounty hunters or revenge extractors and do not require our members to participate in such activities. We see the Lost Order of Akalabeth as a large family, and actively encourage all members to assist each other where ever possible, within the constraints of the game and our policies.

Village Policies:

The Lost Order of Akalabeth should maintain at least one series of buildings for the use of all members. The upkeep of these buildings will be the responsibility of the Guild Treasurer and the Guild Leader. A responsible member of the Platinum Enforcers unit will be designated to be in charge of the security of these buildings.

Guild Equipment:

All members of the Guild are encouraged to donate surplus equipment to the Guild. This surplus equipment will be catalogued and overseen by the Guild Quartermaster who is responsible for the distribution of this equipment to Guild members who require it. The Quartermaster will also be responsible for the furnishing of Guild owned buildings.

Policy on Player Killing and Murderers:

The unjustified killing of other players by members of the Guild is and will not be tolerated by the Guild, and conversely the Guild will not tolerate any other players who engage in this activity. While we believe that such crimes should not go unpunished, we as a Guild do not see ourselves as the Judge, Jury, nor Executioners in such matters. Because of this we do not enforce it upon our members to hunt player killers, and neither do we condemn those members who choose to do so. We see this matter as one of personal choice, and will do all within our power to see that the hunting of player killers remains so.

Policy on Looting (New):

We simply ask that you exercise good judgement when you loot from a monster felled during a combined effort on the behalf of you and fellow members of our Order. It is good practice to give whatever you can to help your fellow brethren, this is a great sign of compassion and charity, and is highly valued in our Order.

During Dungeon Campaigns, designated 'looters' will be assigned. If you join a Dungeon Crawl, you must abide by these rules. You are *not* to loot during this time. When you all return safely to our compound, the entire horde of treasure *will be* shared by all those who attending the mission. Excess equipment *should be* donated to the Ring of Merchants, or to a less fortunate member of our Order.

This Order is rich in resources, the key to our success is sharing and making sure that everyone is taken care of. Thank you for adhering to this policy.

Becoming an Initiate of the Order:

Before you become an Initiate of this Guild, many things must first take place. We need you to fill out the Application Form for our review. After that, we ask that you visit our IRC room, on DALnet (Instructions are including below). We want to meet you in that chat room, and see what your personality is like. Based on that, we will meet up with you IN game, and see how you behave/act when in different situations. This step can take days or weeks, that depends on the impression you make with our members.

We look for several characteristics in a person AND his online persona. To name just a few, we seek those who are loyal, dedicated, honorable, responsible, professional, caring, brave, honest, giving, and this list goes on and on. If you believe in these things, you are one step ahead of becoming a member to our Order.

The Pre-Initiation Phase takes as long as needed. It is really up to you. If you are IN game alot, and follow the policies, and strive to better the entire guild, you will find that the rewards are sweeter than you could have ever imagined. By giving your time and loyalty to our Order, you will find that our members will always be there for you, through thick and thin. If you do not put forth a strong effort to participate, we will drop you from the Initiate Phase, and say goodbye to you. The key to this stage is getting to know our members, and striving to show them that you are a team player. Also, make sure that you ask them to report your actions and deeds to the High Council - this helps us keep track of your good faith and participation.

The only way to become an Initiate, is when four (4) members of the Lost Order of Akalabeth finally vouch for you. People have been known to get vouched for in 3 days, others have taken 3 weeks. This method is one that we believe in, and it helps filter out people who are not responsible enough to wear the title of the Lost Order. Please, do not think that we are a conceited guild, I know that it may sound that way. Our members love the fact that they belong to a guild that takes pride in every aspect of the game and our following. When you enter our ranks, you will see that THIS guild is very different from the others.

During the Initiate Phase, you will be treated as an equal. You are considered a member of our Order, but still need to polish up on Guild Training and your activeness within our Fold. Simply attend meetings, missions and constantly display the characteristics expected of our members. Within days, you will be assigned the Level One rank, and be removed from Initiate Status.

I wish you good luck, friend! Maybe in time, you too will wear the title of the Lost Order. Until that day, I fare thee well.

Brimstone

After you are accepted:

Once you are accepted into our family, many things will take place. You will be granted higher security clearances in our Chat Room (Private Shrine on IRC), added to our mailing list, added to our Roster Pages, have the option for creating a character biography, and the private guild structures will be open to you.

If you are interested in learning more about the LOA rank structure and levels - check out our Ranks Page.

Guild Uniform:

Our guild uniform is flexible to meet the needs of many. Our primary colors are: Steel Blue and Burgundy. Maraxus Moor and several other members know the EXACT hue of the colors and will gladly help you get your uniform together.

Wearing a uniform is mandatory for all members and prospective members. If on your own time, away from the guild, you wish to wear your own colors, that is fine. Wearing the guild colors is thought to be an honor and should be cherished and worn whenever possible.

The Ring of Merchant Unit has a vendor stocked with uniforms. A complete uniform costs as little as 75gp. For details, contact ANY member of the ROM.

Participation is Key:

The most important aspect of our guild and our survival, is Participation. We have created several methods for you to participate such as: IRC, ICQ, Guild Mailing List, Guild Events, Recuiting, Advertising, etc. If you make an attempt to meet others, IN and OUT of game, and show the Guild that you truly care - you will do just fine.

We realize that each person has there own amount of time that can be given towards our guild. Some can play for one hour per week, others have given four hours per day! You simply give what you can, and we will respect you for this.

Out of Game Participation:

  • Advertising via Newsgroups, chat rooms, ultima pages, newspapers, etc.
  • Meetings in IRC.
  • ICQ messages to members and allies..
  • Mailing List Participation.
  • Stories, Poems, Songs about our Guild, Members and Adventures.

If you join the Lost Order of Akalabeth, you will be immersed in friendship, honor, dedication and motivation. We all have energy unmatched by any other guild, many say that it is the Shard of Light that casts it's warm, powerful glow on our hearts. I believe this to be true.

Using Internet Relay Chat (IRC):

Mandatory requirement for our members. When logged into Ultima Online, we ask that all members enter our Guild Room on DALnet so that we have instant access to each other at all times. IRC acts as our communication crystals, and we stress its importance to all members.

If you are unsure about using mIRC or registering your nickname under DALnet, simply contact one of our Shrine Guides found below, and definitely read over our IRC GUILD GUIDE.

We hold daily gatherings on DALnet in IRC. Our public chat room is #Akalabeth and is used to discuss all Ultima related issues, meet new friends, recuiting, declassified IN game communication, and more. We normally have about 5 to 25 members online, at any given time.

For our Wednesday night meetings, or emergency meetings, we have a private room setup named, #ModChannel. This room is moderated, and also requires a key to enter during meeting times only. While in this room, you must be granted permission to speak on a topic, this helps minimize channel traffic. ANY member can approach the podium and voice their thoughts, concerns, opinions, etc.

If you wish to talk privately, about the meeting DURING the meeting, you can enter our restricted access channel, #PvtChannel. This channel is for CLASSIFIED, IN game communication. Only Initiates and Members have access to this channel.

Guild Mailing List:

Announcements are sent to a mailing list that contains all the members of the LOA. We use this list to inform our members of meetings, new Initiates, promotions, missions, changes, etc. This mailing list is PRIVATE and cannot be joined without going through the List Owners.

When sending mail to this list, if it pertains to a certain unit only, make the subject line appear in this format: <Unit Abbreviation> : Topic

LOA-INIT Mailing List:

The LOA now has a mailing list for our Commoner and Initiate members of the Order. This list is for those candidates to use for anything they wish. To organize events, to share tips, to ask questions to the High Council, etc. It is a tool to help you understand the LOA and to learn our culture.

Using ICQ:

Most of our Guild members have ICQ. We ask that when you become a member, you add your Unit members to your contact list, as well as the High Council members. This way, we can all communicate with each other and have a better chance of knowing who is online from our Guild. ICQ can be found at http://www.mirabilis.com and is currently a free file and service.

We ask that as you add each member to your list, that you rename the entry to reflect the LOA suffix. (i.e. Brimstone should read: Brimstone_LOA). This process will help you to identify a guild member much faster in the case of an emergency.

Biography:

Biographies and Photos are sent to Brimstone. All members are allowed to have their very own biography page. This helps form your character history and identity with our guild and in UO. THis is not a mandatory feature. Thank you.

Meetings:

We have a meeting every Wednesday night at 8:00pm Eastern (GMT-5) which lasts up to one hour. If you cannot attend a meeting, try to notify your Unit Leader. You will receive a copy of the meeting log via the guild mailing list.

If you have topics or ideas that you would like to see discussed in our meetings, send them to Brimstone. Your topics will be included in our Meeting Notes and Reminder message mailed out every Monday or Tuesday Night.

Sometimes Freejack or Brimstone are not able to make it, when this happens, the senior member of each meeting will be required to HOST that meeting to the best of their ability.

If you attempt to enter our meeting and the channel kicks you out, ask a Shrine Guide to OP you for the #GL-UOGUILD room. Only the High Council members or the Shrine Guides are allowed to OP Initiates. If you are already OPPED and still cannot enter the room, you most likely forgot to IDENTIFY yourself with NickServ.

CMNR/INIT Meetings:

Every Monday morning at 11am EST, the LOA Commoners and Initiates, along with a few High Council members, will meet in IRC (#LOA-INIT) to discuss ideas, issues, concerns, etc.  If someone cannot attend, the log of each meeting will be mailed to the LOA-INIT mailing list afterwards, at which time, you can reply to the topics via the list for discussion. Be open with your questions, and use this resource to learn more about the LOA and your character. It is all about developing a bond with our guild, and strengthening your characters role in our Order.

Trust-Tax:

We believe that the only way our guild will be able to afford a village (i.e. citadel, castle, outhouse, etc.) is if we ALL participate in giving money to a trust fund - managed by Carnifex. I want to call this our "Trust Tax" because we will NOT set a percentage or a limitation. We ask that you GIVE as much as you can, knowing that it is for a good cause... Survival. If we notice that you NEVER give to our Trust Fund... eventually, this will creep up on you...

We will set up awards and trophies for those of you who donate large amounts of goods/money for the Village. We may even NAME a structure after the member or UNIT that came up with the most money. We can chat about this in a later meeting.

Benefits of Trust Tax:

  • Shelter (When Available)

Responsibilities of the Guild Treasurers:

  • Record all transactions.
  • Provide notes regarding purchases, loans, etc.

Choosing a Class:

We realize that Ultima Online has several classes to choose from, as well as the ability to have a MULTICLASS character. Well, I think that for our OUT OF GUILD roleplaying side of things, our character classes should be diverse. For instance, if you wish to be a FIGHTER/MAGE, wouldn't it be nice to call your class: WarMage. If you are a mage that really wants to focus on nature or druidic spell casting, instead of MAGE or DRUID, you could call yourself: Earth Mage or Elementalist. My point is this, just because Ultima Online provides a basic structure for classes, you can create a class other than those given. I think that it would be fun to give yourself a unique class - and roleplay this out within the game and in our meetings.

Leaving Our Guild:

If at any time you wish to be removed from our guild, simply email Brimstone or your Unit Leader. We will remove your info from all areas of our site. We will not give you a hard time about leaving - things come up and we will respect your wishes.

Negative Guild Actions:

  • Personally insulting fellow members of the LOA.
  • Disrespect of the Leaders and Founding Fathers of this Guild.
  • Give information to other Guilds regarding the LOA or its tactics and strategies.
  • Cause harm to guild members or diplomats within the game unless it is in self defense.
  • Grant anyone access to #GL-UOGUILD room unless instructed to do so from a High Council member.
  • Rude statements and/or foul language around people who do not appreciate it. Get to know people first, before you go running off at the mouth.
  • Disregard for Guild Policies.
  • Inactivity

Operational Behavior while in the Lost Order (During Missions Only!):

o In any operation there should be one or two leaders who will issue orders and speak for the group. This includes any unplanned impromptu situations that may arise, some individual MUST take command in a moment's notice and everyone must recognize that person as the leader. People should be aware at all times, even if only standing around outside the house, what the chain of command is. In any sort of emergency situation, that individual must be deferred to.

o Everyone should remain quiet and pay attention to the operation leaders unconditionally.

o If you MUST say something msg it to the leader in IRC. Do not be vocal in game it looks bad and confuses the situation.

o From now on, everyone should be in uniform at all times. Only the HC or your unit leader for a special op can dictate exceptions.

o All involved in the operation should be in the private channel (#GL-UOGuild) and will likely also be in operation specific channels. This means virtually NO communication in the public channel (#Akalabeth).

o If you have problems accessing the private IRC channels fix it yourself. It is your responsibility to know how to deal with IRC. If you cannot enter the private channel and will not be able to due to networking problems (frequent disconnection's, DALnet services not responding, etc.) you should separate yourself from the operation. Orders and intel will be conveyed through this channel and without access you would be without the information needed to participate. This isn't meant to alienate any individual, just a fact of life, you should respect the commanding officer if he/she asks you to detach from the operation. Additionally, if you have questions as to how to do an operation on DALnet, feel free to ask any ops questions, just don't do it during a "situation" or operation.

o Do not convey any operational intelligence to non-LOA unless it is an explicit joint operation, in which case a private channel will be arranged for all participants.

o There WILL BE respect for leaders. This includes any of the High Council, any Team Leaders, any Operational Leaders and anyone who assumes leadership position when no one else is of higher rank or able to assume leadership.

o Respect is demanded from ALL members, at all times, despite rank or current leadership position.

o A character's POWER does not make him/her a leader. Actions, respect and trust are what promotions and leadership positions are based on.

Interaction with Other Guilds:

Any interaction with another guild in regards to forming an alliance or a joint operation should first be referred to the LOA's diplomatic unit, or, if that is not feasible should be referred to all available members of the High Council. At no stage should a member of the Guild make any promises to assist another guild or form an alliance without approval from one of those 2 sources.

Rule and Policy Violations:

Any member found to be in violation of guild policy and/or rules will be first issued a warning. Upon the second violation the individual will stand in judgement before the High Council. The strongest penalty is banishment.

The Application Form:

After reviewing all this information, and if you agree with our ethics and practices - you may now submit the application form to the High Council Recruiting Advisor.

Thank you for your interest in our Order. May you walk with honor and spirit!
