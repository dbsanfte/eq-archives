ARTICLE: Voting System for your Guild

Posted: 5-14-99
"Voting System for your Guild"
Written by Brimstone, LOA

Overview: Voting Process
Once the applications start coming in, and trust me, they will, you will now be tasked with the fun job of monitoring their involvement, their behavior, their "VALUE ADDED" for your guild. You don't want to become the home for those players who are too 'kewl' for their own good and have been rejected from all the other guilds. This is your time to not only show off what your guild is all about, but it is also time for you to be selective and to hand pick who will move on to become full members of your guild.

Initiation Process and Some Ideas
When someone petitions you for membership into your guild, you really know nothing about them, unless of course they are friends or family of your current members. So you have a right to be a little paranoid. Rather than opening the doors wide and allowing people to just be, "IN", I have the following system to suggest to you. The LOA has been using this method for well over 6 months now, and it has taken us 2 years to develop, lots of trial and error.

This method can be called, the "Two Step Initiation Phase".

Step one: The Commoner or Total Guild Newbie Phase. Feel free to change the names of these ranks to whatever best suits your guild theme or 'edge'. "Commoner" happened to fit well in the LOA 'village' community atmosphere. You may select names such as: Pawn, Scribe, Apprentice, Novitiate, etc. Have fun with it.

During this phase, the outsider undergoes training and information gathering. In addition to learning your ways, your history, rituals, and all the personalities of your members, this will be YOUR time to really take notes and monitor this commoner's actions. Normally, thirty (30) days is a good amount of time to form opinions and know if this person is 'right' for your guild.

Once the vote is held, using the method mentioned above (i.e. +1, +, o, -, -1) - you can decide if the person deserves full membership, needs more time (another 30 days), or should be asked to leave altogether. To reward those who do a good job, yet aren't ready for full membership, we have created a rank above Commoner called, Initiate of the Order. Still not a full member, but above the other Commoners.

Step two: The Initiate of the Order phase. No longer a newbie to your guild, this person is now doing well within your framework. He/she has showed good activity, showed an interest in your 'theme' and has been learning your history and practices. I often consider the Initiates as "Senior Commoners", only because they are excellent sources for our newest commoners to turn to for help. This rank should have 'some' nice little perks as well. In Ultima Online, we allowed the Initiates to wear the uniform worn by Full members, whereas, Commoners had a totally different uniform. Do whatever you can to recognize their achievements, awards and recognition are VERY important.

Allowing all Full Members of your Guild the Chance to Vote

Many guilds have a leader, and oftentimes, that leader calls all the shots. I know that when LOA was born, I took on way too many roles when I should have delegated some work to others. I was solely in charge of accepting new members, and I did this without much interaction from my fellow guildmates. That didn't last long, and the reason for that is simple:   I have no right assuming that everyone in the guild is going to get along with whoever I bring in. I spend maybe one hour per day online, so if the person is an idiot, I normally won't see this. Therefor, allowing EVERYONE to vote and share their opinions, ensures that the candidate is truly GOOD for everyone, not just you.

It is most important to allow ALL your members to have a voice in your guild. Seek their ideas, their concerns, and make sure you concider them all when making decisions.
