ARTICLE: Leave Your Ego at the Door

Posted: 01-18-00
Written by Brimstone, LOA

LOA Public Message Board - EQ Faction
LOA Public Message Board - AC Faction

"Leave Your Ego At the Door"

Inspired by Aleph Aeirs, Founder of the Heralds of the Awakening, Atlantic Shard

Just a few days ago, I called someone who I met online through UO. He is a dedicated, creative, inspirational 'rolemodel' for the Atlantic players. I called him to discuss various topics about our shard, including his new organization (HOA). During this conversation, the topic of "Very famous/visible" people came up, and we were soon discussing how some people handle fame within the UO (or really 'any' community). This brief discussion is what inspired me to write this column.

As the years have gone by, I have gained some 'fame' (for what that is worth) in the online community. I can't say that I set out for that recognition, but it did come after a long time of building my guild, sharing our 'ideas and methods', as well as helping other guilds with web pages, graphics and more. We (the LOA) learned early on that sharing ideas and HELPING others really does pay in the long run. By making a small logo for a guild (15 minutes of my time), for free (or for a minimal cost), that guild would no doubt remember my name (and the name of the guild I lead), and they would be quite responsive to our needs in the future. This is a lesson that many guilds and players will hopefully learn, eventually, as more and more 'virtual world-like' games are released. You get what you give, no more and no less.

As more and more people learned about my 'alias', and as the fame of our guild continued to grow, we tried very hard to keep things in perspective. We didn't want to become conceited, 'too good to help others', 'too busy with ourselves to spend time with the community', and so on. In fact, to this day, we receive letters from players and guilds for all sorts of games, including Ultima Online, EverQuest, Asheron's Call and many others - all asking for tips/advice on how to 'run a guild effectively', 'how to advertise and become noticed', 'how to recruit quality people and not get caught up in quantity only'. We seldom turn aside requests for help/knowledge - and that has been such a great thing for our Order. I have enjoyed the last 3 years very much, so much in fact that helping build communities is one of my proudest achievements in life. That statement may have some of you laughing or shaking your heads, however, it goes further than that... the skills and lessons learned from the virtual communities has translated QUITE well into my real world job and duties. As we approach the year 2000 and beyond, our workplaces, social gathering places, family lives, etc. will become more and more about physical communites and virtual communities that share a common theme. I know, this is altogether another 'topic' for another 'article'.

So, how does this tie into the column 'Title', "Leaving Your Egos at the Door"?

As you prosper in the community - be it UO, EQ, AC or even in the real life... don't get so caught up in yourself that you no longer have time to help those who truly need it. As you become famous, you also become a leader, indirectly. People see you and your accomplishments, and they want to either be a part of your magic, or learn from your lessons. Either way, you should do your best to either help them out, or nicely explain that you cannot assist them due to limited time.

It is also important to know that despite how much fame and recognition you have earned to this date, it can very quickly come crashing down around you in a puff of smoke! If you carry your ego around with you, and if you talk down to others, or consider yourself above reproach, and think that what you say is 'gospel', and others should 'bow down before you', you will become a "has been", and your name will turn to dust. (What a long sentence)

My time is limited, moreso than ever... and I have had to step away from playing UO, leading the LOA (at a pace that I enjoyed), reading/sending email, reading/sending ICQ messages, and basically being a presence IN "Sosaria". Real life has put the "Smack Down" on my virtual life, and there is nothing (at this time) that I can do about it. In the 1 to 3 hours I have, per week, to be 'online' - I still manage to answer those who come to me for advice, guidance, ideas or simply support. It may take a week or two (or three), but eventually... I will respond. I hope that there are many other people out there who freely give their time and wisdom to others who take the time to ask for help. As a matter of fact, if you know of such a person, I would like to know who they are, as well as a few words (from you) about their 'character', and how they have impressed you over time with their deeds/actions. Perhaps in a future column, I will simply highlight those people and their deeds, maybe inspiring yet others to do the same.

Take care, friends...

Brimstone
