RESOURCES - Guild Leadership

Resources that all Guild Leaders should Know About
(Idea for Resource Page, submitted by Malachi)

Special Thanks: First of all, I wanted to thank Malachi for the idea to add this new page to the Guild Leadership Community. I will now go around and organize all the little hints, tips and tricks from the web that can help you and your guild get organized and running more efficiently.

Ratings: On a scale of 1 to 10, 10 being the best, the following resources will be given a score. This score doesn't reflect the design of the tool, or how well it was written... it simply scores the resource as it relates to "Guild Leadership" and making your guild the best it can be. - Brimstone

Extreme Tracking: If you want to track all aspects of the visitors who come to your guild site, you will NEED to setup "Extreme Tracking". As a guild leader, this "Free" resource was extremely useful to me. It allowed me to track what ads were working for our guild. If I posted something catchy, I noticed that our daily hits were jumping up to 200+. Using this resource, I also noticed how many people use the 800x600 resolution, # of colors, browser type, county they are from, etc. With this data, I was able to ensure that the MOST amount of people could view my site without any problems whatsoever. (Rating: 10)

Guild Boss: The absolute greatest Guild Organizer in the world is the, "Guild Boss" application. This program has it all, and more... ;) - You can manage members (and all aspects of members), guild statistics, donations, fees, ranks, allies, enemies, etc. You can also share guild data files with your friends or allies, so that everyone involved has detailed information about each other. Other features include: QuickMail: send email to one or more recipients directly from Roster Pane grid, QuickPage: send ICQ "EmailExpress" pages to one or more recipients directly from Roster Pane grid, Create and save pre-written boilerplate messages and send them off with a click, Dynamic web-page publishing: Generate custom web reports on-the-fly: rosters, inventory, bounties, allies, etc.., Pre-defined custom HTML formatting preferences via the Web Report Setup window, Insert reusable custom blocks of HTML directly into your reports. A free demo is available that is quite useable, minus some key features you get when you register. (Rating: 10)

SitePowerup.com: If you need a message board and don't have a "CGI" guy to help you create one, then configure it to your needs... SitePowerUp is for you. They provide FREE (Unlimited) message boards that are easy to apply for, and configure. The only downside to this is that there are 1.) Add banners on all free message boards, 2.) Limited # of special 'codes' for forum admins and 3.) sometimes the speed of the boards isn't very good, but not usually. Other than those few gripes, "Free is Free". I believe that SitePowerUp will soon be offering free "Forms" and "Polls" for its members. With this resource, no guild should be without a message board. (Rating: 10)

Mind's Eye Graphics & Services: A shameless plug, but I hope you will agree that it belongs on this page. I created a service for creating graphics, html, cgi, creative writing and more. The great part is that we accept either real money (USA) or in-game gold (UO and/or EQ). Prices and conversion rates are provided on our site, so take time to view what your best options are. The Mind's Eye have been providing services to guilds and players for well over 2 years now, and we have never had an unsatisfied customer, ever. (Rating: 10)

(Yahoo) Free Counters: If Extreme Tracking isn't what you are looking for in a counter (ET's counter buried and found on the "Summary" page), you can head over to Yahoo: Free Counters and search for a solution that works for you. Some are java based, cgi, simple HTML script, etc. You simply have to look around and find something that is easy enough for you to setup, and trust me, they are out there! My personal Counter Page of Choice is: Counter Guide. (Rating: N/A)

OneList (Mailing List): Every guild MUST have a mailing list, period. This type of service opens up the lines of communication and makes your community much more effective and "In touch" with the issues. Gone are the days of having to CC: a million members each time you wish to send mail. If you don't have access to a "Real" listserver or majordomo, OneList is for you! It is "FREE" and simple to use. Again, all guilds (organizations) should have a mailing list. The LOA uses 5 different mailing lists, all focussed on a given guild concept (i.e. Council, Ultima, EverQuest, Initiates, Main, etc.) (Rating: 10)

Free Graphics from Brimstone: Once in awhile, I make graphics that are called, "Templates". They are designs that lack guild-specific text, thus being a good canvas for you and/or your artists to customize to your guilds liking. I have included menu images, guild banners and more. I try to add new images each week, but that isn't always the case. Anyway, again, free is great! (Rating: 7)

UO Journal Converter: This resource is for UO only. Being in a guild, you will definately be faced with policitics in one way or another. I always believe in CYA, otherwise known as, "Cover your ass." Keeping this in mind, as a guild leader, you should make it known to your members that recording a log and/or snapping a screenshot of 'difficult situations' could be in everyones best interests. The UO Journal Converter is a great tool that actually organizes and manages your Journal. It breaks the journal up into days, and allows you to customize all sorts of things. If you have the drive space available to save your logs, or at least portions of your logs, I would HIGHLY recommend this tool. (Rating: 8)

UO Screenshot: This link leads to a "Direct Download". As with all downloaded files, please make sure your anti-virus software is up to date. The UO Screenshot file is not hosted on an "Official Site", so be cautious. With that said, UO Screenshot is simply an amazing tool that EVERYONE should have. So often, great things in game are lost forever because nobody knew how to snap a picture of it. Well, those days are gone! UO Screenshot allows you to snap images in a flash! They also save to your hard-drive in .jpg format, as opposed to .bmp. The old method was using Alt+PrintScreen. This method stored the data into memory, and you would then have to use a Graphics Program to take that data and put it into a file format, then save it, etc. (Rating: 10)

mIRC Chat Client: Another must have for guilds! This program is a client that allows you to Chat on the internet, in real time, with everyone in your channel. You can have HUNDREDS of people in a chat room, and still be able to chat with little (or no) lag at all. I know that many of you use ICQ, and you love it... however, ICQ is more unstable when it comes to security, and ICQ takes up WAY MORE system resources. UO (or any game) will lag more if you have ICQ running, as opposed to running IRC. mIRC (and Internet Chat) takes some time to learn, but will lead to great guild communitation in REAL TIME, as opposed to being restricted to email and/or in game meetings. (Rating: 10)
