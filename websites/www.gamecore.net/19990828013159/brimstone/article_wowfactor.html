ARTICLE: The WOW Factor

Posted: 6-01-99
"The WOW Factor"
Written by Brimstone, LOA

Overview: The WOW Factor

If you belong to a guild that has gone unnoticed for weeks, months or even years... it is truly a shame. Unless of course you want to remain low-key, in which case, you will not find much use in this particular article. What I will 'touch' upon in this article is the use of "Style" that inevitably leads to great events, creativity, 'shock' value, and the infamous, "Wow Factor"! For years, I have worked hard (with my colleagues) to perfect this 'concept', and I believe that we are quite good at it now. It has helped us become somewhat famous, and now, I wish to share some of these "Trade" secrets with other guilds. Perhaps you too can earn some fame and recognition using these ideas and methods.

What is the "WOW" Factor?

Simply put, the "WOW" factor is anything that you (or your guild) does in game (or out) that will make other players stop, take notice, feed their curiosities and perhaps make them wonder who you are, what you are doing, why, etc. The WOW factor should also inspire players, breed excitement and wonder, and hopefully lead to players asking more and more questions about the guild and/or organization responsible for the "WOW" event.

Initiating a WOW Event

Once you understand the concept of this article, you can start working on your "WOW" event. Make sure your guild reads this as well, so that EVERYONE is on the same page, and eager to join in on the action. As a team, you can do amazing things and earn some fame for your guild in the process.

Step one: Define an event that is on topic with your guild's theme or purpose. If you are a good-aligned guild of healers, consider a 'wow' event based on that. If you are evil murderers, then make sure your event has that feel to it. No matter what your theme is, you need to do this with STYLE above all else. Style, imagination and mature gameplay is key... always.

Step two: Once your event is discussed and agreed upon, make sure that everyone participating is motivated and serious about the event. To make this believable, to capture your audience and make people rave about what it is you are doing, you must stick to the gameplan. If you have people involved who aren't seriously about it... they are best off left at home. Also, try to gear the event at a time/date that is best for the majority of your members, although I am sure I didn't have to say that...

Step three: Advertising. If your event is something for the masses, make sure you write up the announcement in a creative way. Good grammar, spelling, etc. Make sure you send this notice to all the big 'news' sites for that game, as they can make/break your big event. Examples of the "Big" event would be: Grand Opening of a new Player-Run Shop, War between two guilds, races, clans, etc., The beginning of a player-run quest, joining of forces to conquer some VERY difficult monster, etc. If your event is low-key, then skip the advertising part. Sometimes, you can repeat certain things, day to day, that are extremely 'WOW' oriented and lead to excellent fame and notoriety among the other players. Examples: You could perhaps guard the miners everyday at 4pm EST, and help thwart the murderers and looters. You could perhaps escort fallen victims from a certain dungeon, everyday, like clockwork. You could donate new weapons and armor to players who were killed by local murdering guilds. If you set something up that is repeated, and something creative and/or respected by the community, expect some fame to come of this. I could literally name thousands of things, simple things, that could put you and your guild on the map, forever. More examples: You could make daily visits to a given shrine, where free heals/resurrections are given to players in need. You could guard 'known murdering zones' and protect players from harm, if possible. If you are an evil player, you could do the opposite of all these things: Haunt various graveyards, every day, making threats and being creative about how you approach new victims. You could hide in a dungeon room or corridor, with SEVERAL guild members, and perhaps swarm onto an unsuspecting victim - perhaps "Forcing" them to engage in some ritual, before you "set them free" (Remember, you don't have to 'kill' your victims to come across as being evil, feared and loathed). You could charge fees at a certain crossroad, demanding payment from anyone who enters that area, failure to pay could lead to harm and/or death.

As I have said before, you MUST keep 'style' in mind for everything you do. The "WOW" factor will not work if you aren't stylish in your presentation. I also wanted to say that anyone can accomplish this 'concept', that includes roleplayers, player killers and everyone in-between.

Closing Statement about the WOW Factor

Armed with this information, I hope that you will work together with your guildmates and come up with unique ideas that will 'wow' your audience, and perhaps earn you some fame, some notoriety and hopefully, new members and alliances. You don't have to make everything you do a "WOW" oriented event, but your guild SHOULD try to do things once in awhile that are indeed focussed on 'WOW'ing' the players around you.

The larger the guild, the easier it will be to implement your ideas and events. With more players involved, it is easier to impress other players. This poses a huge challenge to smaller guilds who have less than 5 members. For the smaller guilds out there, I have some STRONG advice for you. 1.) Be organized. 2.) If playing Ultima Online, or any game like UO, wear the same uniform. This makes a great impression and builds awareness of your guild & colors, 3.) Stick to a simple, repeated "WOW" factor event until you grow in size. For example, make your guild synonymous with perhaps a certain dungeon, or town, or shrine, etc. Either help players, OFTEN... or become a known threat (with style, always use style) to players in a given area/zone. Eventually, you WILL become known and player will either join your cause, or rally up against you. (If you have used style and respected your victims, they won't "hate" you during their attacks against you, it will be more of a mutually respect they have for you and thus, wish to engage in PVP with you on a fair and honest level).

If you are looking for some ideas or concepts, I don't mind helping you and your guild. I cannot spend large amounts of time chatting with anyone about the concepts in this article (or the others), so I hope you will understand if I must cut our discussion short. I am hoping that from this article, you were inspired to create an event, or series of events, that will be implemented with style and 'fun' gameplay in mind for all parties involved.

Sincerely,

Brimstone
Lost Order of Akalabeth
