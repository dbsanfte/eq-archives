Interview with Cindy Archuleta Submited on Thursday, November 15, 2001  posted by  aidden

Okay, first of all, a short description of Cindy's job for those that don't know her:


Quote:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
My job is to plan, organize and oversee the EverQuest Fan Faires. In addition, I am sometimes called upon to work on other events that involve players (such as local game signing parties). I also try to keep in touch with the major EQ fan operated news sites, getting them important announcements concerning the EQ community and being available to them when they have questions.

I look forward to the opportunity to get to answer questions about the Fan Faires. These projects are near and dear to my heart and I always enjoy sharing with people how much fun they are!

Cindy Archuleta
Community Relations Manager
Sony Online Entertainment
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Now for the questions:
Bold text are the questions that you asked Cindy.
Italics indicated her answers.


------------------------------------------------------------------
-Are there any plans for a Fan Faire in Alaska?
-Are there plans for Fan Fair for Northeast?
-Are there plans for a Fan faire somewhere in NYC or New England?
-Are there any plans for a Fan Faire in Toronto, or Buffalo?

Wow… this looks like my email inbox! Everyone wants the Fan Faire to come to their city! And that’s wonderful! I can tell you that we have and are discussing all of these areas, with the exception of Alaska. I am not sure we would be able to get enough people in Alaska to support a Fan Faire.

-OR, better yet - can you provide a rundown of *where* the next fan Faires are likely to be?

Well… I can’t be specific about cities yet… but I can tell you that over the next year, we will have a Fan Faire on each coast and in a central state as well. If you have kept track of Fan faire locations, you might have noticed that we visit each section of the country in turn. Coast… coast… middle. So we should eventually make it to an area where most everyone can reach at some point.

-How do you go about selecting cities for Fan Faires?

There are several factors in this decision. First is the number of requests I get in email and on message boards. I keep track of these and do note which cities and/or areas are being mentioned the most.
The next thing to be considered is if the location will draw more than just local people. There needs to be an airport nearby and plenty of things for people to do when they are not at the Fan Faire.
And finally, it comes down to finding a suitable hotel- one that has the room for all of us and is affordable.

-Do you hire local actors do play the parts of your NPCs?

Nope. All of the NPCs in the Real Life Quest are either employees of SOE or guides. And by the way… they all take the initiative to make their own costumes. If you got to see some of these in Orlando, you know that these people take great pride in the parts they play! They were incredible!!

-Since the European fan faire was unfortunately canceled, are there plans to have another one -- but in a more populated environment (i.e. a city, so parties afterward are easy to find in the hotel)

Initially, yes, that was the plan. It is still something I would very much like to do. But we will probably have to wait until things settle down somewhat in the world.

-Do you play EverQuest still? If so, what? +10 for you if you say gnome.

Shoot…. No +10 for me!
I play a Human Druid. I do not get to play as much as I use to… but I am still a player

-Whats the best thing about working at Verant?

First, the opportunity to do something I enjoy so much for a living. I get to plan parties for EQ players! I mean, how fun is that??
I also have very much enjoyed getting to know the people that have made this fame what it is. This is a fun group and I am happy I have had the chance to work with everyone here.

-How do you choose who at Verant comes to the Fan Faires? Draw straws?

First, I have to determine how many people can go, depending on our budget. Then, it is really a matter of who is available to go, who has something to contribute and who the players would like to see. The bottom line, though, is if a their supervisor decides they can be spared.

This is actually one of the parts of my job I don’t like. I would love to bring everyone! I hate that not everyone is able to attend.

-What type of planning goes into securing the location for a meeting? Is it a lot of work?

This part is actually kind of fun now. Hotels around the country have started hearing that we are a good group and now they are asking us to book with them! I am in the middle of negotiations for the next Fan Faire and there have been several hotels bidding for us!

The main factors in choosing a hotel are cost, size, location and the facilities available for us. Once we have a contract, then I begin working on everything from choosing the menu to arranging for the audio-visual needs. There are a hundred details, it seems! It takes me 2-3 months to plan and organize each Fan Faire.

-Do you have any problems with stereotypes of the types of people that will be coming? (or maybe the stereotype is that we're much more complacent, well, I think the safehouse parties have proved that wrong, err I'm getting off topic.)

One stereotype that always comes up is that they assume that it will be all younger people. They are always surprised when I tell them that 50-60% of our attendees are between the ages of 25-35.

I also usually have to tell them that even though we are all addicted to EQ and seldom leave our computers behind, we are at this event to have fun getting to know each other! They sometimes think we are all going to be there playing on laptops the whole time. And yes… I do warn them that there will be parties hosted by groups like The Safehouse.

-Do EQ Fan Fairs make money?

They usually do make some money… enough to keep us going to put on the next one. But they are not a real profit-making venture. They are held so that we can provide a way to make our EQ community stronger.

-Is the FV chick worried about being type cast?

Well, since she is not an actress, I don’t think she is too concerned. I do know that she enjoys her role at Fan Faires and has a good time each time she joins us!

-How do you go about picking which hotel to have them at?
-Do you have some kind of deal worked out with a particular chain?

There are a couple of chains that we do try to use if possible. But again, it comes down to who has enough space, the best location and the best price.

-Are there any plans for a more grand scale Fan Faire (similar to what you had planned for Europe) but in the States?

This is something we have actually been discussing for some time. All I can tell you right now is… Stay Tuned… J

-Hiya! Jus' wondering: Not that Verant might admit to such, but, do they see DAoC as a major threat to business? If so, will there be any distinct effort to combat the competition, beyond releasing Luclin?

Hey! That isn’t a Fan Faire related question!!!
Seriously though… my job focuses totally on the Fan Faires and I am usually not privy to discussions concerning development. Sometimes, I hear news about EQ the same time most of you do!
I just plan the parties… that’s all
