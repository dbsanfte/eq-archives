Rogues Guide to Poison

Compiled by Zato Thornblade

This is a listing of all the information about poisons I could gather. The credits from all the Rogues who helped are too lengthy to list. But I must mention Sethric who posted the Poison book info and Vaslin for the poison effects. There IS Honor amongst Thieves!

Introduction
First off, what does poison do? Well, there are several different effects depending on the type of poison. Once your weapon is poisoned you then have the chance of poisoning your target on the first hit. If the poison effect is attempted on your foe (it CAN be resisted) the weapon is NO LONGER poisoned until another successful application is made. Also if you zone before the poison effect goes off, the weapon loses it's poison and must be re-applied. Keep in mind that the poison is considered by the game to be a spell. Thus if you are moving or are hit when the effect goes off, it will say your spell has been interrupted and the weapon is no longer poisoned! Poisons can be fully or partially resisted so resulting effects will vary. Keep in mind that in reality, the poison is applied not to your weapon, but to your primary melee slot. Because of this, you cannot poison a weapon and stow it for later use if need be. After applying the poison successfully, your next hit (even with BARE HANDS) will attempt to poison the target. The strength of the poison effects is directly related to your level. So a level 50 Rogue can make a more potent Spider poison than a level 25 Rogue.

You will need to have a mortar and pestle to combine the components in. For EACH vial of poison you will need:
A suspension fluid (about 6g).
Various poison components.
A vial made by potters (about 5g)

Poison Vial recipe (Must have 100+ Pottery Skill to have reasonable chance of success)
--------------------
1 small block of clay
1 vial sketch (Substitite Lined or Sealed Poison Vial Sketch)
1 flask of water
1 animal skin (NOT ruined)

This makes the unfinished poison vial, then you must fire it using:
1 unfinished poison vial
1 high quality firing sheet

The poison vials ARE stackable!
Empty Vials Found in Qeynos and Halas herb shops WILL NOT WORK.
Normal vials become trivial at a pottery skill of 148.
Lined vials become trivial at a pottery skill of 168.

APPLYING POISON
Simply sit and click a piercing weapon on a poison to apply it. There will be a 10 second delay while you attempt to apply the poison.
