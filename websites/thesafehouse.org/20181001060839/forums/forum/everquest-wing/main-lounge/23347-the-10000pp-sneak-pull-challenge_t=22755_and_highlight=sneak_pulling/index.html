              Re: The 10000pp sneak pull challenge.

              There are many places you can sneak pull. The easyest place you could test this out would be RS. The reason I say RS is cause even if your a absolute Noob to Sneak pulling you can pull things over each other if they are not the same race. Basically go to RS find a mob that isnt a Pyrilen for example sake, and find a good location that when you unhide and unsneak that you wont have agro from something else. Use a bow or a Throwing item and hit the mob, (then hit Esc on your keyboard, do it fast, and then hit sneak and hide) and if the animal has not hit you or something else has not agro'd you and is a different race in RS for example you can run him all threw the Pyrilen's and they wont agro you as long as they dont see SOS.

              Granted sneak pulling takes some kind of skill and ability, but it is still done, and does still work in several other places. RS is by far the easyest place to do it that I have seen. I guess people have the misconception that you can tag a mob that is sitting in the middle of 1000 of his buddys, and because you hit sneak and hide the others wont come? It doesnt work that way but if your are good enough, you can still sneak pull in several places.

                          Re: The 10000pp sneak pull challenge.

                          yeah our rep knew what i was refering too. Its an oldschool thing about sneak having an agro radius reduction effect.

                          Take this guy from this thread
                          http://www.thesafehouse.org/forums/s...=sneak+pulling

                          People can claim all the hypothetical reasons they have to explain away sneak pulling as a fluke, coincidence or anything else they like, but the simple fact is, from vast experience with it, it works and it affects the assist aggro of mobs in most situations. (Disclaimer: All of my experience only relates through the Vellious expansion - I quit playing after that so have no idea if anything is different in later expansions.)

                          It's very simple. Forget about all your pulses, aggro delay and other silly nonsense. Find a spot where you can stand out of aggro range in front of a pair of mobs or more. It has to be a spot where normally, if you fire an arrow at one mob, the others will come - if they won't, due to your level or pathing or whatever, then obviously the parameters of the test are faulty. Now, without changing anything else, put sneak on and fire an arrow. A single comes. I could repeat this successfully in countless areas throughout the Old World, Kunark and Vellious. Changing *nothing* else, but the fact that Sneak was now engaged, the pull that previously brought multiples , would bring a single. Not a one time fluke, not a happenstance of good luck with the game timer and pulses, but *consistently*, it would result in a single mob instead of multiples. I repeated it countless times, with the same results every time.
                          That is the incarnation of the old school sneak pulling mythology right there! That guy is living breathing old school sneak is power rogue. Thats exactly the myth of sneaking that im talking about. find a test that validates that opinion of that post is what i was offering 10k for.

                          The invis tricks are nice. and many of the known sneak pulls might have been invis tricks in desguise. (ie the pull was single however the invis like effect of being behind the mob created the same effect as if you were invis)

                          There are some easy to find background threads:
                          http://www.thesafehouse.org/forums/s...=sneak+pulling

                          That one actually has 3 suggestions to get sneak pulling to work. Ill test em.
                          2 camps + a sit trick. The sit trick is most interesting because its the one trick that doesnt involve an invis effect. But does the sit trick only work while sneaking is the question?

                          There was an interesting discussion here that seems to indicate sneak never had an agro radius reduction effect as rumored. Derrict offers an easy to recreate experiment to debunk the rumor that sneak ever had an agro range reducing effect (or did SoE just ninja nerf the agro range reducing effect sometime after the release hmmm.. scandal scandal)

                          http://www.thesafehouse.org/forums/s...=sneak+pulling

                          However even in the midst of the thread you get things like:
                          However, I have managed to single pull the entire Frenzy room in LG, including the large room just before it at level 49 with no splitting. Feel free to think that sneak pulling is a myth, the rest of us will just keep amazing our groups.
                          Which is an ehem. Just when you think you have seen the sneak effect beyond invis quashed someone says he can pull something you cant neener neener~
                          But then someone refutes!
                          I remember my monk friend distinctly pulling singles in frenzy, without using sneak OR FD...he just flipped a dagger from far enough way. Probably you did the same thing and all that "sneak pulling" was gratuitous.

                          BTW the fire things are the leading candidates as people keep bringing up their names however derrict offered:
                          Mobs do assist but they have Nurga/Droga-type assist range, which is basically standing next to it. The reason AMY or Reparm can be singled is because the pathing path doesn't take them through mobs most of the way. Trying to pull a mob from the fields won't yield singles like they do in the castle area.
                          Which is just invis not sneak. The mechanic is that mobs have two radiuses. 1) assist
                          2) agro on you

                          The reason you cant do it noninvis is because you the agro range on you is decent. However the assist range is tiny.

                          So far the most interesting mechanic lesson offered on this thread was the effect of invis while pulling through mobs of a different faction in RS. Thats assuming that isnt just the plane of fire (tiny assist range) in desguise. Which can be tested by running the different faction mob right over the top of some other mob which is something this theory of fire cant do. Which is why i call it more interesting.
