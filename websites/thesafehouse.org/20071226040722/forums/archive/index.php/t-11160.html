I'll say the same thing I've always have. Anime is no different than any other media. It has it's good shows, and it's bad shows. As previously mentioned, it is a different way to present the story.

What makes it different than road runner, looney tunes? Well, several things. The art style is very different, it may not be different to an ordinary person, but someone who sees enough of it starts catching the differences. It's kinda like being able to tell the difference between uh, Van Gogh (Italian Painter?)'s earlier works and later works. I couldn't tell the difference, but if I was involved in it, I probably could.

It typically is aimed at a much more mature audience (by American standards, anyway) so the topics discussed are often "adult" topics, for lack of better explainton. What makes this different than anything America has? Well, probably the only thing is the way the story is presented.

There are things in Anime that make me enjoy it more, namely it's overexaggeration of, well, just about everything. There are a lot of realistic anime, too, of course. Another thing is, that Anime is able to show things that can only be shown through art. Over the top fight scenes, magic, destruction, etc. Could anyone really make a live action counterpart of Naruto, and One Piece? Is an Anime like PlanetES doable live-action without looking stupid and corny? Last Exile? Full Moon wo Sagashite? I could name hundreds.

Perhaps so, but past experience says no. Does this have anything to do with it being Japanese? Not much. A lot of anime do not take place in Japan, and therefore do not include Japanese culture (see Monster, for example). It's always interesting learning new things about other cultures, and Japan is a really interesting place. Bunch of weirdoes over there. (I'm joking, kinda).

Often people talk about Anime as if it was the god of all entertainment. It's not. These people often look through rose colored glasses. It's like someone from another country watching Band of Brothers, From the Earth to the Moon, Simpsons, and Schindlers List and calling us the god of entertainment. That's foolish. We have reality shows. Need I say more? Most (read: not all) anime fans have only seen the popular shows, and talk as if it's everything anime is.

I guess that about sums it up.
