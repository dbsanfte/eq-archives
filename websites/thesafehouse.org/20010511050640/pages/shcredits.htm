Zato Thornblade a.k.a Aaron Tarvin


As luck would have it, Zato found his way to a new haven for Rogues, known as "The Safehouse" just days (maybe hours) after its incarnation.. but that seems so long ago.

Not long after he established this new community, Remmi the omnipotent, soon realized he needed help. Thus Zato offered his services to handle the guides and whatnot of the new Safehouse Library. As membership swelled, so did the need for more help.. bringing the likes of Kezzek and others into the fold. Zato was thrust into a humbling role with the Safehouse when Remmi decided to pass from the world of Norrath, and bestowed the ownership of this fine community to him. This was many many moons ago, but seems like just yesterday. Still, to this day Zato will tell you "I'm just a simple peanut."

The "real life" Zato is not too much different from his online persona. A programming consultant in Dallas, Texas, he enjoys moving to new clients to tackle new challenges.

Enamored with multi-player games, he has had the great opportunity to beta test and play many of the massively multiplayer games out there. He played a text-based mud (Darkwind) for years, being the guild master for the Necromancer class. Hard to believe given the Necro in EQ, but the Necro on this mud was very much like the early Rogue in EQ - Underpowered, underutilized, and underappreciated. Playing in the alpha, beta and final for Ultima Online, his guild the Brotherhood of Immortals (Atlantic shard), took their place in history as the first guild to place a player owned castle on any server. He moved to the world of Everquest in phase 2 Beta and never looked back. Though he played a Cleric through most of beta, he made a rogue in phase 4 and became obsessed with the class. He loves being something different, not one of the masses... and being one of only 3 Dwarven rogues on the server was quite appealing. Too stubborn to shelve his rogue during the rough times for the class, he grew to be more and more like the bitter stubborn dwarf he played. Still bitter, but with the pride of what he has accomplished despite the challenges, he doesn't see an end to his life in Norrath anytime soon.

Kezzek Bladefiend a.k.a J.S. Omundson


Abandoned in the wild at the age of 3 and raised by spider monkeys, Kezzek became a part of the Safehouse in the few short weeks after it opened and quickly rocketed to superstar Mod (and later, Godlike Admin) status despite a penchant for flinging his own excrement when upset. Coping with his ever-swelling ego, Kezzek changed his name for a short period of time to the unpronouncable symbol "@#$%" and demanded that forum visitors address him as "The Vanilla Thrilla". The forums were forever changed when in a fit of existential angst he declared, "I wanna be a swashbuckling chocolate bunny, dammit!"

The Kezzek character is inhabited by one Jason Steven Omundson. With far too many gaming years under his belt, he broke into the online computer gaming world via text-based MUDs and later ran amok in the graphical splendors of Subspace, Meridian 59 and Ultima Online. Hooked in the Beta 4, Everquest has been his stomping grounds for the last 2+ years.

Real life finds Jason posing a simple Civil Engineer by day and a spandex-clad, crime-fighting astronomical superhero by night. Cleverly hidden by a "parts-his-hair-on-the-other-side" disguise, this superhero has been protecting the citizens of the Midwest from bad lighting, alien mind-rays, and mad-cow disease ridden sheep. Much to the heartbreaking dismay of astronomy groupies worldwide, Jason is to be married in a quiet ceremony of 36,767 this October.

Vaslin Slickfingers a.k.a. Phillip Davis

Vaslin has always had a thirst for knowledge. His ultimate goal is to accomplish every quest in Norrath, and to uncover those last few remaining unsolved enigmas in the land of Norrath.

A Scout of Tunare to the very end, he requires acceptance into the enemies camp. Faction is very important to him, and the mundane work of killing skeletons or the purchase of enough red wine to fill The Hole itself, he uses their acceptance to scout out their weaknesses for the invading army. Every quest is fair game to Vaslin, whether the final result be a wizard rod or a Iksar monk shackle.

With all the gold, diamonds and jewelry in the world, nothing means friendship like a stack of bone chips.

Phillip is an avid programmer who has been in the field of computers for over 22 years. His current position is in the field of biometrics and fingerprint identification. His systems are installed in over 120 sites across the world in police and national security departments. (www.afix.net)

At night, he also works for a communication analysis company in Missouri where they do online survey and business profiling. (www.theforteinstitute.com)

He is the creator of the very popular Everquest tool EqCaster. A feature rich spell analysis program that has solved many of the queries about the spells and effects of the arcane. This is also the site of 'Vaslin's Everquest Compendium', a several hundred hour project dedicated to the chronicling of the history of Norrath. Also included is EqOption, one of the first and still popular text-color editor. His Everquest website is at www.eqcaster.com

In his spare time, he enjoys smashing termites with large objects.

I would like to thank my dear wife of thirteen years who has to put up with my daily adventures into Norrath and away from reality. She is my best friend and I would not be who I am without her.

Aidden Bloodmoon a.k.a. Tom Hassell


Aidden arrived on the front door of "The Safehouse" about a year ago. Lost, frightened, and close to death, Kezzek took him in and provided food, shelter, and much needed medical attention. Kezzek took great care of Aidden, and was able to bring him back to 100% health. He then immediatly put Aidden to work.

Sweeping floors, taking out garbage, cleaning up Sunday morning after a rousing night in the tavern all became his payment for the "kindness" Kezzek bestowed upon him. Often he finds himself daydreaming of being an adventerous rogue, out in the "real" world slaying beasts and dazzling the ladies, only to be awoken from his fantasy to the shouts of Kezzek "Damnit Aidden, quit your dawdeling, and get me my Vanilla Beans"

Tom is a Network Engineer and has been in the technology industry for over 9 years. He currently is working for a large Web Hosting company, managing a group of engineers responsible for the daily support of customer servers. His online gaming experience goes back about 8 years and started with a mostly unknown team based shooter called Cyberstrike. The World of MMORPG was thrust upon him with Ultima Online during the last 3 weeks of Beta. He joined Everquest about 4 months after its initial release and hasnt left yet.

Tom currently considers Web design his hobby and hopes to soon make it into a profession. He provides hosting and design work through his own company called Bloodmoon Interactive network, and has had some good succeses lately.
