Rogue Redemption Quest
Burning Rapiert
Compiled by Many Safehouse Rogues

1. Goto Lon the Redeemed (536, -.83) in the temple of Solusek Ro. He wants you to quest to find two gems (stamina and rightiousness) which you are to give to Ortallius in Sro. Talk to him, and he sends you with a note (NO DROP) to his friend the poisoner.

2. Go find Conium Darkblade in Dagnor's Cauldron(poison merchant). Give him the note, and he gives you an axe (NO DROP) called "never stops chopping" and a clue about the "top of the world".

3. *optional* Talk to Falyn in the back room of "top of the world" in Highpass, and he sends you to Bryan.

4. Goto Bryan on the back deck at the Lumbermill Inn in Highpass. Give him the axe and he gives you a box (NO DROP) to fill with 10 different beverages from around the world. NOTE: You must have an empty MAIN slot to hold a new container or you will receive nothing and lose the axe. The drinks to fill it are:

Underfoot Triple Boch - Kaladim - Tumpy Irontoe
Tunare's Finest - Felwithe - Wela Muselender
Vasty Deep Ale - Sister Island in OOT - Or in Erudin
Lendel's Grand Lauger - Rivervale - Mac Deeppockets
Gator Gulp Ale - E. Commons Tunnel -
Bubar Frozen Toe Rum - Halas - Scon McDaniel
Oblong Bottle (LORE/NO DROP) - OOT @ location neg 1611, pos 2924
Blood Spirits (LORE/NO DROP) - "a hemo enologist", Mistmoore, garden, ~lvl 40 mob and drops more than one every time.
Clockwork Oil Stout - Clockwork CX, Solusek A near Gabbie Mardoddle - (Pickable)
Blackburrow Swig -
Need a Blackburrow Cask. Found on Gnoll Brewers/Master Brewers in Blackburrow. A common spawn is at loc 32,32 on level 3. Placeholder is a Gnoll Guardsman. Brewer is rare spawn, and Master Brewer ultra rare.

Combine in the Blackburrow Cask: (no brewing skill needed to combine - DO NOT STACK components)
4 BlackBurrow Gnoll Skins(NOT PELTS), rare drop of elite gnolls or commanders
2 gnoll pup scalps
2 casks of Ol' Tum Jumm's fierce brew (made using high brewing trade skill)

The Blackburrow Cask disappears after you correctly combine the ingredients to make the Swig. WARNING: Put the cask in a VERY safe place - many disheartened Rogues have accidentally drank this quest item on accident! Also the swig looks exactly like the cask of Ol' Tun Jumm's fierce brew - so be warned.

Once you have all 10 drinks in the box, hit combine to create the box of spirits.

5. Return the box of spirits to Bryan, and he gives you the Gem of Stamnia (NO DROP).

6. *optional* Goto the Freeport Sewers and find Toxdil. Ask him about the gems, and then about the Gem Case. He tells you that he has the key, but will only give it to you for the gem inside the case. He says he last sold it to a jewler.

7. *optional* Talk to Noresa Sparkle in West Commons. She asks you to save her from Kiz Gix. She also tells you of a "Gem Case", which you would need two keys to open, and would have to return both box and keys to the "original boxcrafter" to find what is inside.

8. *optional* Kiz Gix roams around befallen side of WC. Upon killing him you recieve a note writen in Goblin, telling you of a Dark Elf "Outcast" who stole a sparkling purse.

9. Find the Outcast (Lynada the Exiled) in Sol A in the High Shaman room. Killing the outcast gets you the Sparkle Purse (NO DROP). Lynada is a ~35th level caster with a pet and can be nasty if you have to fight goblins nearby as well. She is a 4hour 15min spawn with no placeholder.

10. Return the Sparkle Purse to Noresa Sparkle and she gives you one of the keys (NO DROP) needed to open the "Gem Case".

11. Find the Gem Case (NO DROP). It is hidden behind a lock door in the basement of Hallard's Resales in E freeport (loc -100, -500) that you must pick. (Approx 122+ lockpick skill) It used to be guarded by a dog named Scrapps. Another will spawn immediately in its place.

12. Go back to Noresa and purchase off her a "Sparkling Saphire" (this is the Gem Toxdil wanted) (Cost Approx 500-550pp, is NO DROP). I would recommend asking for a charisma buff (the 49th Level Enchanter one is GREAT) to help save quite a bit of plat.

13. Return this Saphire to Toxdil, and he gives you the other key (NO DROP) necessary to open the Gem Case. Toxdil is in the Sewers under W FP. The entrance is at the bend in the north side of the wall outside of the gate. Just look around for him, but he is not always spawned. He has place holders of a sewer rat, orc or a guard. He is not always in the same place, but often can be found in one of the dead ends in the sewer or across from the stairs.

14. Give the Gem Case and both keys to Bubar in the East Commons Tunnel to receive the Gem of Righteousness (NO DROP). The Gem of Righteousness looks just like the sparkling gem (when you right-click it) bought from Noressa, but if identified it is indeed the real gem.

15. Return both Gems to Ortallius (Orc NPC in desert camp on W wall of SRO), and reap the rewards.

Rogue's Guide to Upper Kedge Keep (Gloomwater Harpoon)
 Submitted by Wolvinsbane Latrocinor
Edited by Zato

This guide is written to be as helpful as possible with ANY group makeup. The group I go to Kedge with is made of of the following. Although it's high, you do not need this type of firepower to control the upper level (except for some exceptions, mentioned later). 50th Cleric 46th Druid 41st Warrior 37th Druid 35th Enchanter 32nd Rogue (dats me!)

First off, before you go to Kedge Keep, go to the Inn that is in East Commons RIGHT NEXT TO the zone to West Commons and buy a TON of Fish Scales for your druid/enchanter so that he can cast Enduring Breath. Bards can also come in handy with their EB song. You could also get a Rebreather or Aqualung from an Tinkerer and support their trade. They can be expensive though. Magicians can also summon Waterstones for underwater breathing and are great for those times when your Enduring Breath (EB) is fading during combat (which it shouldn't, more on that later) and you need to keep alive till your EBer can recast it on you. You can also purchase Potions of Water Breathing in Halas I believe. They go for 12pp. Our group got 6 of them and have them hotkeyed just in case of emergencies. On the subject of underwater combat. Few things you should be aware of.

Melee Classes - When hit underwater by these fish, you get pushed back. At first it's tough to fight underwater, but you'll get used to it fast. Generally, you want to find something to prop yourself against so you don't get pushed out of attack range if the fish swings around and hits you back. It's not nessecery, but it is helpful. Just past the Pull Zone (on the map below) right before it opens up into the room is a ledge (you'll see it when you get there and understand) that you can prop up against when your puller drags it into the hallway there. You cannot swim backwards normally. If you duck (D key) you can swim backwards though! Also if you have SOW cast on you before you go in you can swim backwards. You will also run low on stamina alot because of swimming and fighting. Enchanters should have augmentation on everyone to help with this, and other classes have stamina regeration spells if need be.

Casters - Movement back / forth WILL interupt your casting, up down (without forward or backward movement) will NOT interupt your casting. Be aware that if a fish hits you, you WILL float backwards and thus, interupt your casting. It is HIGHLY suggested that casters stay in the corners, that way if they're hit, they don't float back and at least have the chance to roll for a continued casting.

On Enduring Breath - It is the ENTIRE group's responsibility to be aware of the time remaining on EB. If someone dies due to drowning, it's EVERYONE's fault for not keeping on top of it. Enduring Breath is a 25 minute spell. Have someone in your group with a stopwatch start timing when the first EB is cast and let the group know when 20 minutes have elapsed. Recast when he says the 20 mins are up. Don't wait the full 25 mins, no need to cut it close. Everyone in the group should be aware of time remaining. Don't get angry with folks asking "time on EB" ... they just want to be informed. Casters under level 35 have the problem of not even being able to tell when it runs out until they are gulping for air, which is often times too late to save them.

On Rezzing in Kedge - If you have a death in Kedge, and you have a Cleric who can Rez, have your Druid or Enchanter ready to cast Enduring Breath on the newly rezzed player. Also have the Cleric ready on the heal. Rez sickness wipes any and all buffs from the player, you'll have to be ready with the buffs. Okay, on to the actual fighting...

Map of Kedge Keep's FIRST FLOOR

 

Creatures on the First Floor

30-31 - Lancer Swordfish (blue to me at 32) 33 and up - Piercer Swordfish (yellow and red to me at 32) 42ish - Impaler Swordfish (RED to me) 43ish - Fierce Impaler Swordfish (RED to me)

Lancers - Not too many HPS. They hit hard (60s) and fast (double, triple hits). Great XP for a mid 30s group. Lancers are 98% of what you will see on the upper level. They are placeholders for the ONE Impaler/Fierce Impaler and few Piercers that spawn on this floor.

Piercers - Can be nasty. They tended to be more RED then Yellow to me at 32. I'd say they average in the 34 to 37 levels. They are pretty much the same as Lancers 'cept they hit for more in the 70s and have a good amount more HPS.

Impalers - 42 or so creatures. They are VERY nasty. They hit hard (101 was a common theme) and fast (double hit for 101 is fast enough for me). They also have a LOT of HPS. Do NOT let your casters get these aggro on them, you'll be minus a caster before you can do much. Seemed to spawn every half hour instead of one of the Lancer spawns on the far end of the room from the pull area. Those spawns are near the ceiling.

Fierce Impaler - Take an Impaler, give it more HPS and let it hit more in the 110-20 range. This creature drops the Gloomwater Harpoon that we all are after. It is a RARE spawn that takes the place of one of the Impaler spawns. Rare for even a rare spawn. I suggest that a mid 30 group be VERY careful with the Impaler and Fierce Impaler. You best have a VERY cohesive group. I'd venture to say that a upper 30s group could take the Impaler and Fierce Impaler spawns. The roaming path marked on the map is where the two flanking lancer spawns will roam. They just go back and forth.

Upper Level Tactics - Tactics used in this area were pretty basic. Basically, be aware that you're prolly going to be pulling multiple fishies. We had an Enchanter who could Mez or Charm the extras. Druids can also Charm the fish. This can be vital during fights down here as these fish are very nasty critters. I highly suggest and Enchanter in the group. If not, have your Druid Charm an extra fish if you get in trouble. Beware that when Charm breaks, the Charmie is going to be VERY angry at your Druid, so beware. If you have an Enchanter or Shamen, use a SLOWNESS spell. It's a GREAT spell down there. Make sure to have your casters wait till the meleers have taken the creature down a bit before nuking it. Fish and casters do NOT go well together.

INFO for HIGHER level groups camping the Gloomwater Harpoon - If the upper room is cleared and you want to pull something, there are some Fanged Tooth Piranna right when you go down on the second floor. BEWARE of them. They were BLUE to the level 50 cleric in our group. They hit hard (80s, double attack) and have A CRAPLOAD of HPS. I'd venture to say they're considerably nastier than Seafury Cyclopses. Our group (the one outlined at the begining of this post) could manage 3 of them at a time, but this was ONLY because of our Enchanter and Druids charming and Mezing, even then, we found ourselves medding during the fight and, well, it was prolly the nastiest fight I've witness in my time in EQ, and I've seen some nasty ones. You WILL pull more than one from the below level. Be warned, they are VERY nasty.

Other Facts - Most creatures can NOT see invis

Conclusions - This is prolly my favorite zone in all of Norrath. It's great fun to play down there. The XP is simply GREAT for a mind 30s group camping the upper level. Do NOT go down there for loot, it sucks (only got cash of the Shark on the second level). All in all, it's a GREAT time. I hope this guide has enlighted those of you wanting to go down there and get your Gloomwaters and helps you have a good time. Good luck fellow Rogues!

Book of Knowledge: Getting a Mask of Deception
Written by Ralluwen Shadowstar

Contents of the post:

What you should know before starting -

For a good map and guide check out the Relentless Monks website at monks.volitionx.com, the lower guk guide is specifically at http://monks.volitionx.com/lowerguk.htm. I suggest you get these maps, print them out and follow the guide through with them, marking your path so you can easily find your way. The maps look pretty good even when printed in Black and White (Greyscale). The spawn you are looking for is 'a ghoul assassin' that spawns in the Supply Room, also called the ass/supp spawn, on the maps the area is marked as '19'. The mask you are looking for is his common drop, the Serrated Bone Dirk (8dmg/27dly Pierce, Effect:Engulfing Darkness(Snare/DOT), Rog Only) is his rare. He has place holders, so it can take a while to spawn. Before going you should maybe put all your stuff in the bank, if you die down here you may not get your body back. At anywhere less then 25 be very careful as if you are trained you have very little chance of making it out alive. If you have any questions don't be afraid to ask.

PART I - Getting to Lower Guk Dead Side

You should enter lower guk by the dead side entrance, this can be found if you follow the right wall all the way from the innothule zone to a lower guk zone. This is generally right as there are a few spots you can skip and will want to. The only thing on the way you should run into that can see through your hide/sneak is the large and giant heart spiders. These are rare spawns and while going thru the spider rooms (you will know them when you see them) you can stick to the left wall to aviod them (even thou these spawns are likely camped or dead) Normal heart spiders can be snuck upon. After the spider room stick back to the right wall you will enter the scryre room, just stay on the lower level and take an exit behind the left water pillar, head down the ramp here and you will enter a room with stone walls and pillars, head to straight away from the ramp to the second section and along it to the left (you can continue to follow the right wall but you will hit a secret wall, just follow this secret wall as thou it were solid) Continue allong the right wall, you will pass a turn off to the left, keep going straight, you will slide down a slippery section, and at the end it will open into high bluffs over a watery area, carefully go over this area following the right edge. Continue following the right wall until you turn into a hall off from the main one you were just following, there should be a stone just before the turn and a flaming pot in the hall, this flaming pot is just opposite the dead side zone to lower guk.

Preparations in Lower Guk -When you get into lower guk ask immediately if the bedroom is camped as well as the assassin spawn, if the bedroom isn't you might have to go out and get a SoW, or get an invis to undead before proceeding. The reason is that you will run into some water and unless you swim fast you won't make it under the water sneaking (I am unsure what swimming skill you need to do it with sneak on and without it on). Ok, now be careful, invis to undead(itu) and sneak/hide do not stack and there are both undead and living things near the zone. More often then not they will be cleared out.

Getting to the Bed Room (BR) -Now proceed down the dead side ramp and when you come to the end turn to your right, proceed down another ramp (if you go left it will be a slippery ramp and you will want to go back) You will turn a gentle corner and come to a river, swim under it and head to the right, you will agian turn a gentle corner then a sharp corner (this will be a 180 degree turn) before you come up, to the left will be the area called the Bed Room(BR), if it isn't camped or cleared there will be ghouls here, so don't jump out of the water unless you have a way by them (itu or sneak/hide).

Going through the BR, Spider Room, and getting by the GIBs - Once in the room there will be two halls leading over the underwater passage you just came though. It is best to hide here but if you try while ghouls are in the room and only have itu you will become visible. Once you are hidden (or if you wanna try going with itu) go thru the left hall, that is the one nearest the water. At the end of this hall is the spider/bat room. The spiders and bats can't see thru my invis but it is unknown to me if it is level based, if you hug the left wall and move quick thou they won't see you. Now you are in another hall, head down it aways and once you start turning hug the right wall. To your left you will see a room full of about 5 greater ice boned skeletons. Hugging the right wall they won't see you but they don't see my sneak/hide. Just move quick.

The Bridge and Dead Tower -Now you will come to a left turn that will mount some stairs and open into a bridge with a large tower in front of you, to your right are gargoyles, they see everything but magic invis. On the other side of the chasm to the left is a ledge where the ghoul ritualist spawns, head straight across this area (careful not to fall, i would not suggest drinking before starting this endevor) and into that tower you see (this should not be confused with the live tower and it is more commonly refered to as the bridge area or dead tower) Once you enter the tower head through it into a room behind it and turn left, follow a hall way straight to a ladder, this ladder leads into the Store Room (SR, somtimes called the ass(assin), supp(lier) or ass/supp)

Inside the Store Room (SR,ass/supp) -This is where the ghoul assassin spawns along with the ghoul supplier. Now be very careful, if there isn't a group at the camp because the assassin can see thru rogue hide (although sneak will work on him as long as you are behind him of course). Now ask the group there if you can loot the next Mask of Deception, If there is already someone in the group or one that got there before you waiting then graciously allow them the first loot and they will allow you a subsequent mask loot. At this point you have two options, you can wait until you mask drops or you can ask a member of the group there if they will tell your alternate when one drops and you can go play him until the call, with the latter way thou you may get in trouble if the group evac's, leaves or you take to long and someone else gets the mask. It can take several hours for the assassin to spawn and drop a mask, so patience is a virtue here.

Getting Out - Getting out can be just as dangerous as getting in, if someone in the group at the ass spawn is nice they might evac you to SRo or NRo/WC but don't think they have to. If not you will have to reverse the aformentioned process and go back the same way you came. If you are leaving via the dead side you can take the water exit, but if your swimming is bad it will be a long and hard swim, at least you will get practice. I am not going to detail this way as it is longer and generally striaght forward. If you look at the maps you should figure it out.

PART II - Getting to Lower Guk Live Side

This part is only really effective if you are non-KoS to frogs, If you are KoS I suggest you do the dead side method instead as this is basically just a little safer and slightly faster to duplicate. Follow the same directions as in Part I, Step 2 to get to the lower guk dead side zone, but instead of taking the side hall to dead zone continue straight down the hall Into a room, take the other exit out of this room and take your second right, you will turn into a resevior area, take the wood bridge and then take the next plank directly right of you. Follow the right wall until you come to a large open area. Now follow the shore line to the wall and follow the wall to another plank room, take the double plank across and then take your left this time. This will lead to the live side zone.

Getting to the Patriarch Spawn - Note that if you are KoS by living frogloks then STOP right now and go back to the dead side zone and go that method, this ways is only safe of you are non-KoS. I can't stress this enough. The patriarch is one of the mobs in live side that sees invisible, and if you are taking this route you won't need to sneak, just start running on in, the only thing that would be trained that might attack you is the evil eye, anything else would go to the dead side zone or the king's back door zone. Now head straight down the ramp, straight down the hall, across a bridge and follow the hall straight (stick to the right side, you will hit a false wall but it has a solid wall right behind it). Now you will pass one turnoff to the left, then will make a sharp left turn into room with three exits, This is where the minotuar patriarch spawns, he casts see invis and wanders.

Getting to the Elder Spawn - Continue to follow the right wall out the right exit of the three way exit room. You very shortly come to another intersenction, one path goes left, one right and another does a U-turn back on the other side of a wall. Take the left. Once you have taken the left follow the right wall again past two left turns and follow it through another for exit room, you will take a dip under upper cave that you can see a bit of through a crack and after that take your first left. Once you take the left take a right and follow the hall past a right turn and into a room, this room has a pit over on the other side of it where the minotuar elder spawns, it also cast's see invis.

Getting to the Bridge - Follow the right wall though this room and stop before going down the hall, now read this first until i tell you to continue. There is a pit trap down this hall, if you are to young to have safe fall it might do you some nasty damage as well as annoy you. If you are KoS and haven't yet been seen and you fall down this you might die here, you won't be able to make it up the ladder with sneak on, it has to be removed. Now to see this pit trap look for a red mark on the left wall, this mark indicates where the hole is, take a run at it and jump, you should make it easily, if not climb up the pit into the room behind you and try, try again. Ok continue down the hall, through a small room and to the left up some ramps to a ladder, climb the ladder into a small round room. Now just outside this room is where it gets deadly, there are gargoyles out there and they won't like you regardless of faction, race, or religion. They are living and see invis to undead and they also see hide/sneak, I think thou that regular invis will get you by them. Otherwise you will have to call on someone to pull the gargoyles (many like to since they aren't to tough and drop eyes that sell for 9pp).

The Bridge and Dead Tower - Once they are pulled put on sneak/hide and head out, go to the right and you will come to the bridge/undead tower, From here on out you can follow the dead side directions from the same number, just be aware you are entering the tower from a different way and will have to turn to your right on the bridge. It should be easy to understand when you get there.

Good Luck getting your mask
