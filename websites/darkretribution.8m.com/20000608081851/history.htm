History of Dark Retribution
It was bright sunny day across the lands of Norrath, a day where the Dark Elves prefer to stay in the shadows waiting for the calmness and stealth of night. This day was the perfect day for the human invading party. The sun hit high noon and the raiders from Qeynos attacked the guards. The Dragoon Guard put up a valiant fight even though the numbers of humans in the siege quickly overwhelmed them. The human invaders swept through Nektulos Forest and into the main city of Neriak, killing many of the residents and merchants of Neriak, and kidnapping carefully selected members of the Neriak Elder Council.
Wliab, an apprentice Cleric, was hunting around Neriak with three of his long time friends when the attack took place. Dmizzar, an apprentice Necromancer, Xyanthon, an apprentice Shadowknight, and Shimmerdark, an apprentice Wizard. During their hunting trips, Wliab was often outspoken about his ideas on the teachings of their God, told his friends about his philosophy. He told them of his disagreement with the Elder Council, a source of agitation between Wliab and his father, and his hatred of the "false-guilds" currently controlling that Council. He felt that these false guilds were watering down the pure race that Innoruuk called for and their acceptance of non-evil members were leading to the downfall of the Dark-Elven race. Although his hunting partners were sympathetic to Wliab's ideas, they were somewhat skeptical. This philosophy had made him an outcast in Neriak and most viewed him as a radical, or fanatic.

Wliab's beliefs were finally put to the test when the hunting party returned to Neriak to see the carnage that remained from the human raiding party

Although all lost family members, Wliab was the most devastated. His father, Tlaan, a Cleric of much power, and standing member of the Elder Council, and his baby daughter, Morania, were kidnapped and taken to a village outside of Qeynos. To compound his misery he found the body of his wife, beaten and broken by the humans, left with an ominous but clear message on her body on the street outside of his house. The humans would be back.

The four hunters quickly met to plan their attack on the small village outside Qeynos, but the remaining Elders got wind of the plan and paid a visit to Wliab and his team. The acting Lead Elder explained to the group that the Elders were waiting for a sign from Innoruuk, as to whether or not any action should be taken. He went on to forbid any action against Qeynos or humankind until such a time as a sign from Innoruuk was given to them.

Wliab was stunned and incredulous. He pleaded to his hunting partners to help him get his father and baby daughter back from the village. He spoke to them of the dark retribution that they must carry out. He told them that the Council would never proceed and time was of the essence. The time for retribution was now; it was time for the humans to feel the darkness of Innoruuk's wrath.

Finally, they all agreed and set out on their mission. Under the cover of darkness, the hunters approached the village and attacked the humans. All went well with the attack they were successful in finding Tlaan, Morania, and the others kidnapped by the humans. They were able to kill everyone in the village. Wliab had made it clear before the attack that all must meet the wrath of Innoruuk, but Xyanthon, searching for any survivors, came across a newborn infant and did not have the heart to kill him.

Wliab was furious, and took his evilly blessed Bloodforge Hammer out, ready to sacrifice the squalling and obviously terrified child on the spot. Tlaan quietly walked over and stayed his hand, having his own form of retribution in mind. He named the baby Sarzek and decided that the baby would become a Shadowknight. Tlaan was determined to turn this human into an enemy of humans, raising the pale baby as a Dark Elf would be raised, to hate all humankind. He felt that he would then have his own brand of dark retribution.

Upon their return to Neriak, the Elder Council admonished the four hunters for their disregard of the laws of the land. Tlaan stepped in and was able to persuade the Council to pardon them. From that point on Wliab knew that he must do what he could to change the path of the Council and Guilds in Neriak. Tlaan was able to convince Wliab to hold his tongue and wait until the time was right.

Years passed, everyone went their own ways and continued to hone their skills. Tlaan raised Sarzek to hate everything that humanity stood for. Morania became a rogue against Wliab's wishes. He had wanted her to stay within the family's chosen vocation and be a Cleric, as had been many generations of D'sai before. However, as all young people do, her stage of rebellion and bitterness about her mother led her to the path she ended up following. Wliab eventually came to see that Morania, instead of being the healer her father was, had become a deadly weapon in the fight against the pale races.

In the interim, it was discovered that the High Elves had been the ones to provide locations and strategic information to the Qeynos City Council, precipitating the attack, and in Wliab and those who were with him, a resounding hatred of those pale cousins grew enormous.

Sarzek grew tall and large, and fell in with a somewhat questionable crowd, which eventually formed a Guild. Morania, seeing the problems that could come from her childhood friend being in this organization, pleaded with her father and grandfather to help convince him to leave this false Guild. After much consideration, Wliab decided that it was time to form the clan he had been born to lead.

It is time for the darkness to prevail, to feel the wrath of darkness. The night of retribution is now at hand;

Dark Retribution shall prevail.

-Wliab D'sai Guildmaster: Dark Retribution
