Abashi clarifies again02-Aug-00 11:25 AM

Submitted by: Xilith T'Vyn

I think what is confusing us is that we are unsure about this
question:

Do any hybrid spells have a casting cost that is based on a
percentage of our total mana? (In other words, if you have two
characters of the same class and level and one has an int/wis of
150 and the other has an int/wis of 65, will the 150 int/wis
character be able to cast more spells?)

Please clarify this for us.

Though I answered yesterday as you mentioned, probably the
best way to describe spell-costs for hybrids is:

Spells cast by hybrids cost their listed cost, plus a percentage
of the listed cost that is inversely correlated to the level of the
caster.

They do not, however, cost a percentage of total mana, nor is
the cost based upon total mana at the time.

- Gordon

Abashi answers SK mana questions (at last)01-Aug-00 10:18 AM

Submitted by: Xilith T'Vyn

Do any SK spells cost a Percentage of the mana bar as opposed to a
flat rate of X mana?

All hybrid spells cost more than the stated amount of mana.
This difference in cost is greatest at lower levels, and
approaches the listed cost at higher levels, though it never
quite reaches it.

Is SK mana INT-based?

Yes, just as ranger and paladin mana is wis-based.

Does a small race have more mana than a large race, with equal (+50
INT) equipment? (Small races have a higher starting INT.)

No. The amount of mana you have per point of INT is not based
upon race.

In your sole opinion, is the INT bonus of the small races comparable
to the str/regen/nostun perks of the large races to make a more
viable character?

My opinion would be 'no'. Of course, large races make better
SKs, Warriors, etc, as designed. They do pay an additional
experience requirement for their race-specific skills and
abilities.

- Gordon
