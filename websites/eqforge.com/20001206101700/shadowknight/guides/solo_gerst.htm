Shadow Knights Solo Tactics

Gerst D'Harbazshan

I can honestly tell you i dont recommend going solo, but if you dont have a group or you chose to mess around alone, i have some tatics presented below. First of all, make a pet. Sure pets are pretty much worthless in groups, but every little bit helps when soloing. Additionally, pets give you a good five to ten second head start if you have to run. For the most part, you tank. Not the pet. Pet is there to give you that extra 12dmg or so a round. Though sometimes i like to send out the pet to engage the enemy and then cast whatever DoTs i can at will. If you give your pet a weapon, give it a piercing weapon or nothing at all. Pets fight better with fast weapons. One tactic if have used is to cast fear on the mob and have the pet attack the mob as it is running. You can get a few DoTs in as well before the mob comes back. I have gotten the mob halfway dead or more before i actually engaged in melee. Use this tactic in open fields ( like the Karanas, aviaks are primo targets), not in dungeons or mob my bring back friends.

Spells for SKs are very helpful as you reach higher lvls. For soloing, the darkness line of spells is a must. Its a DoT and more importantly, slows the mob down. This is the spell you cast on a mob to engage. Darkness will slow the mob down enough so you have time to cast other DoTs or do various tatics. Mob will also not run away or attack you when it reaches the "run" part of its health ( point in mob's health when it trys to run.) Also very helpful spell in kiting or fear tactic mention above. Disease cloud is a good spell if you are low lvl. It prevents the mob from healing ( 2 tick per sec dmg negates 2 tick per sec heal of mob). Another important spell is siphon strength. To me this spell gives me a pernament 9 str bonus. Very quick casting time and low mana to cast. Always cast this spell as much as you can. Once you reach lvl 22, you can get vampiric embrace. This adds a lifetap effect (drains hpts as you hit) to your weapon and it helps that every little bit. Other offensive spells in your arsenal are heat blood and heart flutter. Heat blood is the strongest DoT SKs get and heart flutter lowers AC and Str in addition to DoT. Dont forget the various undead DD spells. Helpful in unrest, befallen, lguk and such. Besides the obvious offensive spells of the SKs, there are surprising number of complementary or defensive spells. Shieldskin gives you an extra two or three no hit protection. This spell is even better used when you are in a group (Will explain in my SK group tactics guide) Locate dead is essential if you are soloing, cuz you are gonna die alot solo and you probably will lose the location of your body. Shadowstep is an all around good spell in all situations. Gives you time to cast DoTs and such. Use this spell in open fields though. Feign death is a very good spell for SKs. Whether soloing or grouping, feign death is awesome. Soloing, you want to use this spell if you are getting low in hpts and you know you wont make the run to the zone. The mob will think you dead and leave you alone. Saved my life many times. Other useful spells in certain situations are endure cold and disease spells and cancel magic, shadow vortex, and lull undead.

Soloing pretty much limits you to certain zones to solo effectively. Big, open flat zones are your grounds when soloing. These are the karanas, ros, oasis, commons, etc. You probably want to sit yourself near zone line or a relatively safe place where mobs dont roam. If soloing in dungeon, most always park your butt at zone line. Best tatic is to distance yourself from mob spawns, cast your pet, tell your pet to guard that area ( use /pet guard here) and cast whatever buffs you have on you. Then look around the zone for mobs. Once mob is in sight, cast a spell to engage. Usually i cast darkness, so it slows considerably so i can cast other DoTs or give me prep time. When mob gives chase, run back to pet and either cast DoTs, wait for melee, or have pet engage ( using /pet attack) . Eventually though you will have to engage in melee and you will have to tank yourself. Hopefully all the DoTs and buffs you casted already will have the mob already half dead. Use your pet health to your advantage too. If you are getting whacked alot, back off and let the mob hit the pet for a bit. Then jump back in. Mob will almost always engage in you again since pet is relatively weak. If you are really getting whacked, run away and let the pet tank. Then while still in range of spells, gauge whether to cast DoTs again (since DoTs last like a min or so) or to run your butt to zone. If you kill the mob, congratulate yourself and sit down to med or heal. Its important to use tactics even while medding or healing. Keep your pet in the direction where most trouble looms. So if a big sand giant comes towards you from the front and you have the pet in front of you, the sg will attack the pet first.

Harm touch is your best backup plan when soloing. Nothing else turns the tides of battle quicker than a well timed harm touch. Very deadly due the instant casting time and big dmg. Harm touch is most effective in the lower lvls as most mobs around those lvls are hurt considerably by a harm touch. Again though, you have to use harm touches wisely. Cycle time is about 1hr and a half. Never use harm touch at beginning of battle unless you know you could never kill the mob without it or you just want it dead fast. Though best to use it later in battle in almost all situations. Try to gauge the point when the health of the mob, when ht is cast, will cause the mob to run. This can save you precious mana and health. It is important to use HT at a certain time in the battle. The mob should be weakened considerably so its resist is lowered. Also, HT is more effective if you use it behind the mob. This will help you get max dmg. It is important to /con the mob too White mobs resist half dmg alot and yellow and up commonly resist most dmg if not all. Of course, harm touch is most important if the battle is real close and you are almost dead. HT will surely turn you in favor of the battle.

I hope you future shadow knights in training heed this guide and learn wisely from it. I will update accordingly when given time. May Innoruuk be with you!
