    Can I Have What He's Drinking?
I can only conclude he is drunk or stoned. After reading the speech President Bush gave on Monday (see www.whitehouse.gov) one can see how Mr. Bush has no concept of how he fits into history.

Forget that such a speech is about six months too late, or that there was no details given as to how any of the proposed goals will be achieved. Let’s not even discuss the issues of the U.S.’s new plan to incorporate the very militias we have been fighting into the new Iraqi army and police force. These are all issue that bother just about everyone.

What baffles me is how Mr. Bush can continue to grasp at the straws of “weapons of mass destruction” (which really applies to N. Korea and various other states), deposing tyrannical rule, and the “war on terror” and even bring democracy as causes for this war. I am still not sure how our government was duped into going along with invading a foreign country in the first place. His speech spoke not at all about his actions, or his cabinet’s actions in this whole affair. Instead we are supposed to believe “this is the world as we find it” and that “we did not seek this war on terror.”

So we didn’t invade a country which had no known connection to terror since 1994? A country that attempted to comply with rather harsh investigations and sanctions and still attempt to keep some show of dignity? In the face of contrary evidence the Bush administration continues to maintain their reasons for going to war against a very poorly armed nation. And then “some of Saddam's elite guards shed their uniforms and melted into the civilian population.” What did Mr. Bush and Co. expect? What invasion ever did not face some sort of a guerilla insurgency? Is the president completely delusional? Does it make sense for use to cry foul when others don’t play by our rules?

“They hope to wear out the patience of Americans.” Forget them, how about you Mr. Bush? You are wearing out my patience pretty quickly.

“All along, some have questioned whether the Iraqi people are ready for self-government, or even want it.” Well I suspect they do. Is this something that needed reiterating? Did anyone actually have to ask if they wanted self rule? The best part is we are still not giving it to them. We have declared that we will turn over Iraq to its own devices with advisories of various governments but we have yet to even begin gathering these advisors. We certainly won’t actually go and ask for advice. And we are still forcing the Iraqi authorities to report to us. We are not even removing any troops. What kind of pull out is this?

It isn’t one. We are lying to everyone. And no one is fooled, unless you’re an American citizen. In that case you probably didn’t even see, hear or read the speech, because you were watching American Idol or even worse were absorbed by “The Swan” – a TV show about low self esteem women who get plastic surgery and compete in a beauty pageant after.

Maybe I should be happy no one saw this.

    Swatting flies...

Condoleezza Rice finally made it before the commission (you can read the transcript at www.washingtonpost.com). Her appearance alone was a victory for those calling for more information, as President Bush had been badgered into allowing her to speak publicly and on the record. In a excellent performance, Ms. Rice answered and argued her case with great calm and poise. With few exceptions the panel recognized her achievements and got down to business quickly, possibly to ensure they could stay with the ten minutes each had.

Unfortunately Ms. Rice may not have any real new information at all. In one statement she went so far as to say that the attack on the World Trade Center could not have been avoided. She cited the Millennium incident in which bomb making materials were “luckily” found on the Canadian border; cause an alert to go up. Ms. Rice did however make it clear that the government was not prepared for this and could not have been given the 233 days the Bush administration was in office and the nature of democratic societies. The key, according to Ms. Rice, was that the Bush administration inherited the terrorism situation as it stood and did not have the time to ramp up to a higher alertness.

While this might seem rather convincing, it has been argued that the Bush administration has decreased spending for homeland security as ti is now called. Combine this with the fact that the Department of Homeland Security is a complete shambles from which no form of intelligence could ever be gotten and a bleak picture emerges. Still unanswered is the Iraq connection. Why did we invade Iraq if after all this time the President has finally admitted that there was no connection between Iraq and Al Qaeda? In fact there is evidence that suggests the only Al Qaeda relationship with Iraq may have been via a northern camp which was working with Kurds. These are the same Kurds we abandoned after the first Iraqi war and then assisted and used in the Second Iraqi War.

We seem to forget that Osama Bin Laden was prepared to defend Saudi Arabia against possible invasion from the secular Iraq invasion after Iraq had moved on Kuwait. It is the sudden appearance of American troops in the Kingdom of the Two Mosques that caused such issues for the Taliban, many with Saudi Arabia and Mr. Bin Laden. Why would Al Qaeda work with Saddam Hussein, a person considered to be a great enemy to Islam by the Al Qaeda? The Al Qaeda camp in Northern Iraq was likely assisting insurgence.

Questions still unanswered:

  • What was contained in the August 5th 2001 directive that the President chose to ignore. Many have said that there is information in this still classified document regarding strange goings on at flight schools in Florida, involving various Saudi nationals.
  • Why did the President push for a connection between September 11th and Iraq? Why did he pursue Iraq as a terrorist nation when there was no evidence (and not for a lack of looking) to show that Iraq had any connection with Terrorism after 1994? What was to be gained by this?
  • How is it possible to have a tax cut, hundreds of billions spent on a war and not lose any effectiveness on the home front? It isn’t. Where is this money coming from and who si going to foot the obviously large bill when it comes due?

    And these are the obvious questions!

        Post this...

    Spring is in the air and things have begun to really heat up around here.

    The Washington Post has an interesting article about Edward Kennedy bashing President Bush. While I am inclined to agree with Mr. Kennedy's arguments, I always wonder if we could get a better poster boy?

    In a related article in the Washington Post, the Iraq war seems to be causing President Bush's approval rating to drop. Many are realizing that the June 30th deadline will not likely happen. The likelyhood of a civil war in Irag looms large and certainly that would make the entire Irag war effort seem a failure (especially if a side we don't like wins). And related to this, Americans are starting to realize they do not "feel" safer. The essential perception is what needs to be dealt with more than the reality.

    One possible motivator for recent activities could be related to recent events in Spain, which may have emboldened various factions world wide to play their hand in hopes of effecting the upcoming elections here. This possibility has not recieved much media attention after the Spanish elections, but I am certainly curious to see what develops as we get closer to November.

    On top of all this the President was essentially forced to allow Condoleeza Rice to testify in public and under oath. I am sure many wait with baited breath to hear what the "Warrior Princess" has to say. The commision has basically announced that 9/11 could have been prevented, and I cannot imagine how Ms. Rice can put a positive spin on this. The N.Y. Times even goes so far as to refer to her as "The Performer" in an article by the same name, and rightly acknowledges that a lot rests on her shoulders. Much to Ms. Rice's credit though, even with questions arising about her depth of experience, she is likely the most capable person in the entire administration today.

    Recently the Bush administration was crowing like roosters over 300,000 new jobs being created. What many don't realize is this is the first time this happened in years (I cannot find the details but it may be the first time during the Bush Administration). Even with another 300,000 new jobs a month, President Bush's term will end with a huge loss in jobs. As the Washingtin Post aricle notes, the only thing that seems to give the Bush Administration hope is that enough jobs will be generated before the elections.
