A: The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland encompasses the following nations; Scotland, Wales, England and Northern Ireland. The UK is located in Western Europe and is a member of the European Union.

Confusing? It's all to do with our history. England has existed as a unified entity since the 10th century. The union between England and Wales was enacted in 1284. In 1707, England and Scotland agreed to permanent union as Great Britain. The legislative union of Great Britain and Ireland was implemented in 1801. The Anglo-Irish treaty of 1921 formalised a partition of Ireland, with six northern Irish counties remaining part of the United Kingdom as Northern Ireland. The current name of the country, the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, was adopted in 1927.

2   Q: Can I submit my site to Regional/UK?
A: If a site about you, your company or your interests is relevant to the UK then your site is acceptable. Sites that do not emanate from the UK may be acceptable if the content is correct, relevant and of interest to the UK viewer. For example you don’t have to be from the UK to create a site about a popular British television programme. An American site about how to find the best mortgage would not be of use to the UK audience and would not be relevant. It can be difficult for editors to acertain if a commercial site emanates from the UK if you do not have contact information available, in these cases the content of the site will be examined and sites that appear not to have been produced for a UK audience will not be accepted. Sites offering general advice and information would be best submitted to the relevant category in the main Open Directory structure.
by ness at 1999-05-15 07:34:45
3   Q: My company sells to the UK, can I submit my company's site?
A: If your company sells products and services worldwide then its best that you submit the site to the relevant Business or Shopping category in the main Open Directory structure. Sites that do not specifically cater to the UK market will not be accepted. If your company has offices in the UK and there is an area of the site that is relevant, you may submit this. If your company is not based in the UK but specifically caters to the UK market your site may be relevant, please submit the appropriate area of the site if this is not apparent from the home page. Pages with little content are unlikely to be accepted.
by ness at 1999-05-15 07:36:57
4   Q: My site is in English, can I submit it here?
A: If the subject matter is relevant, yes.
Non-English sites about the UK should be submitted to the appropriate World category to that language (common languages are: Dutch, Esperanto, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Polish, Portuguese, Russian, Simplified Chinese, Spanish and Swedish.

5   Q: What category do I submit my site to?
A: Finding a relevant category to submit to allows editors with a knowledge of that category to review your site and hence the process of review is made more efficient.
Please do not submit to categories that are obviously not relevant for your site, if in doubt spend some time browsing and searching the UK categories to locate sites similar to yours. (A good hint is to first find the county your business/organisation is physcially located and then 'drill-down' from there).
Commercial sites should be submitted to the Business category unless there is a category that more accurately reflects your site, such as Health or Sports.
If you can't find a category for your site then submit to the main Regional/Europe/United_Kingdom category and editors will find an appropriate place for it - although this will take a longer amount of time.
by beebware at 2001-02-08 11:04:54
6   Q: Can I submit my site to more than one category?
A: If you feel that your site is relevant to more than one category then submit it where relevant. Choose the most relevant categories to submit to first.
Spamming the directory (submitting multiple entries to irrelevant categories) causes inconvenience for editors so please be thoughtful - your site is more likely to be listed if you carefully consider how relevant your site is to a category.
Bear in mind that sites with minimal content may not be accepted, if you have multiple areas in your site that may be relevant to multiple categories please wait until the pages contain content before submitting them.
by beebware at 2001-02-08 11:05:14
7   Q: My site relates to a specific area of the UK, where do I submit it?
A: If your site relates to a specific town or county then it's best to submit it to the relevant regional category. There are categories for cities, towns and counties in the England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland categories. The Channel Islands and the Isle of Man can be found in the Dependent Islands and Areas category.

8   Q: My site is under construction, can I submit it?
A: Let's face it, most web sites are continually evolving and no site is ever really finished. Sites that contain very little content are unlikely to be accepted so wait until your site is established before submitting.
by ness at 1999-05-15 07:44:47
9   Q: Can I make a suggestion for the UK FAQ?

Editors may suggest additions and changes the same way or via the regional forum for discussion or post to the FAQ direct.
