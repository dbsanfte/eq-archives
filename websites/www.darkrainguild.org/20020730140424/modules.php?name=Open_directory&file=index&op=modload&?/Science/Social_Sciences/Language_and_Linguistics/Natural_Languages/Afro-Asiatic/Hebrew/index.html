  • The Academy of the Hebrew Language - The official site of the Academy of the Hebrew Language, Hebrew version. The Academy prescribes standards for modern Hebrew grammar, orthography, transliteration, and punctuation.

  • Aleph-Bet on the Net - A set of interactive tutorials for students using the textbook "Encounters in Modern Hebrew" by Edna Amir Coffin. The tutorials cover the vocabulary lists for each of the ten lessons in the book and allow students to read and type Hebrew. The site does not require any special fonts or browsers.
  • Ancient Hebrew Research Center - Online Ancient Hebrew resources with history of the alphabet and different scripts, pronunciation, lexicon and bookstore.

  • Biblical & Modern Hebrew Grammar for Macintosh, Windows - 50-lesson Course designed for college students and available on diskette (online sale). Includes: exercises, vocabulary tests, verb parsing charts, vocabularies, and translation materials, formatted in Microsoft Word. One of the rare Hebrew for Mac products.
  • Biblical Hebrew - This UK based website offers some introductory features on classical Hebrew and information on courses. Links cover both online stores for study materials and interesting areas, like the Dead Sea Scrolls.
  • Biblical Hebrew lab - An academic website with an index of introductory features on Classical Hebrew, such as exercises, quizzes. Style of presentation is appropriate for students and individual learners, offering back-up for students in the Faculty.
  • DaaT - Web-based Hebrew programs by Danielle Sharon in reading skills, Modern and Biblical Hebrew language, offering instructor guidance and practice (by registration). Also proficiency assessment test and SAT tests.
  • Hebrew Alphabet - Illustrates the letters and vowel points of the Aleph-Bet, along with their names and numerical values. (From Judaism 101.)
  • Hebrew for Me by Zigzag Inc. - includes Instructional programs based on Java applets created with young learners in mind
  • Hebrew Language at the University of Texas - This site offers a useful collection of video clips and sound bites in Hebrew. You can play the files in your web browser to practise your listening comprehension and translation skills.
  • Hebrew Language Learning CD-ROM - A multimedia Hebrew CD-ROM, including Hebrew for Beginners and Everyday Hebrew. The CD contains over 6,000 words and phrases recorded by native speakers. The interactive Hebrew course claims to offer rich audio material, including a facility to compare one's pronunciation against a model recording.
  • Hebrew Made Easy - Sample pages and ordering information for workbooks, resources and s.
  • Hebrew on the Net - A page of s to resources for using Hebrew on the internet. It covers a wide range of topics, including Hebrew fonts, HTML documents, e-mail, etc.
  • Hebrew page of Multilingual Books Language Bookstore - Courses for sale online, with description; also reference books - dictionaries, syntax, grammar.
  • Hebrew Self-Study - Self-Study group using J. Weingreen's Practical Grammar for Classical Hebrew for beginners who want to be able to read from the Chumash and siddur. Participants work via the Internet on a scheduled course, with exercises; answers are posted on the website, but study material is not.
  • Hebrew Speaktionary - Hebrew speaking picture dictionary on CD-ROM.
