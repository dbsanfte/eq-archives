Nagena Part One

I am thankful for many things. Aye, the wine we drink, the poems we write: even the very blood in our viens. In fact, the thing I am most thankful for (at least today), is that I am able to write the second entry into this journal. Indeed, I have been blessed with many things, and my blessings continue to this day.

Although I haven't written about her, I met a druid named Lilthia recently. She is a wonderful person, and her attitude on life is like no other. Indeed, she breaths life, and justice.

It was for these two reasons that she wanted to travel to Nagena, and take care of the hags and witches there, for they, we heard, are destroyers of life, as opposed to proponents of it. I had no idea how underpowered we could be.

The journey began with ease, comming from Rivervale and traveling through the commons in the general direction of the feared(at least to the "light" races) forest of Neriak. We had no choice but to travel through these dangerous grounds to reach Nagena, far in the Lavastorm Mountains.

"We must cut through this forest, Ryndal." Lilthia said, studying a map she bought from a halfling merchant.

"Aye, milady. I'll get us through here as fast as I can...but no promises. The dark elves will not take kindly to our kind here." I was a bit disturbed by our having to travel so close to our evil cousins homeland.

Lilthia looked up from her map. "I believe caution would be advisable over speed here. I think they can see better in the dark, not to mention the fact that they know this forest better than we do."

I suppose I should have agreed with her, then sung Selo's Accelerando anyway, but I did not.

We began our journey through the forest with caution. I noticed that the forest was very dark, especially for so early in the afternoon. The tangle of branches was so thick that almost no light shone on the floor of the forest, and the air was stagnent. We could hear strange sounds, and it seemed to us that something was watching us.

All around, I could see shadows, and eyes. Aye. Eyes. Little pairs of eyes, seeming detached from their bodies. Looking at us. Gazing with curiosity, and wide with....fright? I doubt it.

I wanted to see if I was going insane and ask Lilthia about it, but it seemed as though speech might make them all attack us. Perhaps that was a wise thing to do.

We crept around the forest for hours, hoping against hope that we'd come to the entrance to the Lavastorm Mountains before dark.

"You see that path?" she wispered pointing just beyond a large sycamore tree.

"Aye" I said.

She pointed to her map. "That is the path we need to take."

Looking at the map myself for the first time, I noticed that there was a river in the way, and that a bridge spanned across it. "Hmmm." I said, " I hope I'm wrong, but I 'll wager that the bridge is guarded, to keep the light races out."

She crinkled her nose. "I hadn't thought of that. Well...not much we can do but go and see". She said this happily, as if she knew that death would not touch her. Indeed, she breathes life.

We crept along, just off the path, although we had it in plain view at all times. The eyes that were watching us began to thin out here, and I could hear the sound of a rushing stream.She motioned for me to stop, and she crept silently through the brush, as the life giving plants seemed to make way for her, and welcome her footsteps silently.

She trotted silently back, and wispered, "There are two guards. At least two I can see. I would assume that there are two more on the far side of the bridge. I saw them checking travelers' bags, and making sure who is entereing their lands. We must be close to the city."

I sighed, "Aye. It would seem we have found their check point."

"What are we going to do? We can't swim the river, and the branches are much too high to climb across." she said.

I am sure she didn't expect me to answer, but I actually did have a plan. In fact, my plan was to rush the bridge with Accelerando, and call it good. And, in the end, it is exactly what I did do. But, at that particular moment, we did not, because we heard:

"STOP! LIGHT ELF YEILD TO MY BLADE!"

I turned and saw a dark elf warrior, wearing blackend plate mail. His sword looked huge in his hand, and was most likely magical, albeit unholy.

Lilthia grabbed my hand and ran back into the forest, all while I began sining Selo's Accelerando. Soon, we were running fast enough for me to detach my hand drum from my belt, and we ran even faster. I shouted to her, " I think we should rush the bridge!"

"What?" she yelled, trying to hear herself over the wind in her ears.

"Rush the bridge!" I yelled again.

"What? Brush my, what?"

I sighed, although she couldn't hear it, and turned around. Her arm was almost wrenched from it's socket, but it got her attention. While we were stopped, I said, "Rush the bridge. You know, run across it, and hope these evil people don't hit us"

She looked at me for a moment before she started to answer, "Bu-"

"STOP!!"

I looked up an realized that we did not distance ourselves enough from the elf. I began singing and running again, but this time headed strait for Nagena.

I will never be able to explain the eyes of the first dark elf guard at the bridge when he saw us comming, suffice it to say it was a look of pure bewilderment. We were running faster than any animal that roams those parts, and given that dark elves usually stay close to their home, he may not have ever seen anything this fast...let alone two of them. Lilthia and I raced passed the two guards before they knew what happened, although it didn't take long for them to regroup, and come after us.

At this point, just to recap, we were running across a long bridge, with at least three dark elf guards hot in persuit. We had no where to go but forward. Our biggest concern, was that if one of the guards behind us could use magic...well, we would be dead.

After running a few more feet, I glanced to my left, and saw Lilthia happily laughing. I was astounded to say the least...I suppose I wasn't feeling very bardic at that moment, and besides-if I began laughing with her, I would not be able to sing, and we would slow down.

We were quickly approaching the end of the bridge, so I began scanning the mist for new guards. As I suspected, there were two more. They heard me singing, and saw the globes of light around me, so they tried to block the bridge. As luck would have it, (and perhaps some intervention by Bristlbane), we skirted the edge of the bridge and made it across.

But now there were five guards (at least) chasing us through the woods.

Remembering what Lilthia had said about the road leading to our destination, I followed the path. Each step getting us closer to reletive safety.

"STOP"

Yet another--no two more dark elf guards chasing us through Neriak. Great, I thought. Now there are seven.

"It seems that one of them was able to use magic," I thought, for it was not long before I could disern shapes in the mist in front of us. Six shapes, to be exact, and all of them assuradly guards. But, luckily, that was not correct. It was, in fact six dark elf adventurers fighting some beast or another. Imagine their surprise when they saw the dark elf guard train we caused.

Soon, we made it to a mountain pass, that led out of the forest, and into the safety of Lavastorm.

I am sleepy now, journal. I will write more about our encounter in Nagena later, although I might have to spice it up a bit, as it wasn't nearly as excruciating as our encounter with the dark elves. How can one race hate another so badly to want to kill them on site? What had we ever done to them?

I suppose some questions shall remain on the tonges of the unwilling, and some answers left in the breeze. Aye. I am tired.
