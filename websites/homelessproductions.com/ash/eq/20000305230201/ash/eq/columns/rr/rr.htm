Rant Submitted by Ryndal on 5/10/99

I have traveled the lands recently only to find a few things disturbing. The fact that so many people forget what good role-playing is all about is the most disturbing of all.

You know, I once met a man (I'll keep his name to myself) who ended every word with "th". I suppose he might have been trying to mimic some Shakespeare or somethingth, but, more than likely he was trying to make fun of the role-players.

Everyone is entitled to play the way that they wish. But for a game to have enough ambiance to literally suck you into it, it is unfortunate that the feeling is disturbed (not entirely destroyed), by a player who is talking about his car, wife, school, etc.

I believe the downfall (if any) to online RPG's is not going to be technology, camping, too many magic items, or any of those hotly debated issues. I believe the problem with these games are the people. Ironic, isn't it?

Sadly, there is nothing that can be done about it. You can't force people to role-play, and EQ, so far, has proven to be very condusive to role-playing. The problem is better solved by leaps and bounds over UO, but still leaves a bit to be desired.

For every bad incident I have had, however, I have had two good incidents. This gives me great hope, in that perhaps good role-playing will be taught to those too unfortunate to not know what it is, or how good an experience it can be.

I was waiting for the ship to take me from Freeport to Kelethin, and I asked a dwarf who was standing there if he knew when the boat was going to get there. He told me he wasn't sure, but if I didn't stop singing, I was going to scare the fish away! (Aye, I am a bard). This turned into a long conversation about BlackBurrow Gnolls, the cites, guilds, and several other in game topics. Soon the dock was full of players, in character, speaking of passed adventures. And even though this dwarf was high level, he escorted me to Kelethin, and later to Crushbone, all because we were in character. Because of role-playing, I made a good friend.

That was, perhaps, the most fun time I have had to date. We never even lifted a blade to strike an opponent, or sung a damaging song, but I was every bit in Norrath as I had ever been.

The next thing that bothers me is the fact that people are so defensive. I don't know where this phenomenon comes from, but it's evident everywhere. In game, newsgroups, where ever. What makes people flame?? Indeed, it's something that could be researched for years.

There was a guy who was obviously in a party in Northern Ro the other day. Everyone in his group was using the /group tag, except him. He was using auction. Usually I keep to myself, but I felt sorry for him. "Maybe he doesn't realize it," I foolishly thought. So I sent him a polite /tell asking him to use the group option instead of auction, as the rest of us didn't care to see "Who's Loot", and "Man that was close".

What he responded with shocked me. "Who the F*** are you to tell me to do anything?" Of course this was still on the auction channel, so now seventy other players are seeing this, and probably wondering what he's talking about, since they didn't see my message. I didn't immediately respond with anything. So for the next five minutes he harassed me with auction (generally making an ass out of himself in the process), then he said, "What if I don't want to use /group. Then what?"

I wasn't going to respond, but then I said, "Then nothing. Just common courtesy, that's all."

I'm not sure if he reflected on this and decided he was being a jerk, or if he had too many /tells after that to count, but he suddenly stopped. Both harassing me, and using the auction channel.

Now, I realize that you can just turn off all auctions, but that is rather inconvenient if you are shopping for armor, (as I was). It is far more convenient, and courteous to other players, to use the channels the way they are intended. Why broadcast messages intended for your group to the entire zone, especially when it is simple to put an "X" into the group box?? I fail to see any reason or excuse to misuse the channels.

But that goes back to what I was saying about role-playing. Verant has made it extremely easy to be courteous to other players, but no one cares. Very few characters actually give a damn about other's. Most only care what their skill is in 1h Slashing, and how many minotaur axes they can sell that day. Which is fine. I just wish there was enough courtesy in the game world to let people play, and associate with players of like mind, uninterrupted.

Thanks for the rant,

Ryndal
