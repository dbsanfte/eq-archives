Kithicor

It was cold that night, and rain came down upon my hair. Cold in Kithicor Forest? It never had been before, and perhaps it was an omen of what was to come.

"Guildmaster be damned! If I never kill another goblin, it would be too soon," I thought, as I exited the cave that leads from Rivervale to Kithicor. The guild had sent me to Rivervale to help the locals with their goblin infestation problem, but they were much more a nuisance than a threat. I began to think that they had just sent me away, as if I were some kind of problem.

As I jumped down the cliff face to the dark green grass, I shivered, and looked about me.

The Kithicor woods, named after a strange, silent wood elf named, ingeniously, Kithicor, was one of the most empty hunting spots I had seen in my travels. The locals concentrated all their efforts on the Thicket side, and, thus, left their backside open for attack. Hence the reason I was here, in the cold, wet, foggy mess that is Kithicor Forest.

I quickly ran to the shelter of some trees, and ran into, of all people, Aldarin.

"Hail, old friend!" Aldarin said, probably as surprised as I was.

"Hail, and well met!" I said. "What brings you to Rivervale?"

He looked at me with a smirk. "I went to Faydwer without you that morning, but something told me something was wrong. So I went back to Qeynos, spoke with Gatha, and she told me you came here on a guild mission to Rivervale."

"So," I said again, "What brings you to Rivervale?"

Aldarin chuckled, "I thought you might need some help...if you even found it in the first place. I am quite surprised to see you here in one piece."

"Heh. We will see who saves who next, my friend." I said, glad to see him again.

I quickly told him the situation in Rivervale, and my reasons for leaving the relative safety of the Misty Thicket to come to these woods. "Let us investigate, then, and see if anything is afoot."

As we traveled in Kithicor, we met a cleric of Karana named Gnorth, a humble little fellow, who claimed to be from Freeport. Since we were hunting Darkweed Snakes, among other poisonous things, we decided to let him travel with us.

"You two know that there are rumors of Ghouls in the area, eh?" Gnorth asked, with a suspicious tone.

Aldarin stopped in his tracks. "Ghouls? This close to a city? I do not believe it."

I paused, thinking of the adventures I had had with Levarne. "Aye, Aldarin. I do believe that Ghouls are around. That's part of the reason I wanted to come here."

"This is mad!" said Aldarin. "Do you think we can handle such beasts?"

Gnorth smiled, "We shall soon find out, friend."

We continued our travels, and looked for anything suspicious. After skinning perhaps eight or nine snakes, we all heard a tremendously odd moaning sound.

"Stay put," said Aldarin. "I believe we may have stumbled upon our Ghouls."

Gnorth and I stayed under a thick tree, which helped us avoid the rain. We were silent, a bit nervous, and very worried about our ranger friend.

He suddenly appeared over a ridge. "Seven zombies line up in front of some ruins just over the ridge."

I looked at Gnorth. "I think we can handle them, don't you?"

Gnorth smiled and lifted his mace. "Aye."

"I think its a bit risky," said Aldarin, but then, he too drew his sword. "But we can't let the undead camp this close to a major city."

At that, we began the most dangerous war of our lives. Seven zombies surrounded us, as we stood back to back, and took them out one by one. Aldarin claimed the unlife of three of them before Gnorth and I had taken out one (at least that's what Aldarin claimed after drinking some Dragon's Breath back in Rivervale later that night). I was freezing some of them, and burning others, as Gnorth smashed heads with his mace, even as they shrieked and slashed us with their claws.

Soon, a pile of seven dead bodies littered the clean forest floor, that seemed to disrupt nature itself. The bodies reeked of burned flesh, but there was no blood to be found.

"We have probably alerted the master" I said, in a somewhat concerned tone. "I wonder if he is working with the orcs and goblins of Runnyeye?"

My question was met with a grunt from Gnorth, and utter silence from Aldarin.

A quiet moan alerted me that something was indeed amiss. I stood and spun around, just in time to see a zombie slash at me. It hit my arm, and it went limp. The coldness of the creature seemed to freeze my blood, and by it's touch, I wanted to faint. I stabbed feebly with my dagger, and struck it several times, but still, it swung it's arms in a frenzy, trying to take the life from me.

A sharp crack sounded suddenly, and the zombie fell into a heap onto the forest floor. Behind it stood Gnorth, and he was patting his mace in his other hand. "Where are they coming from now?" he asked, ready to fight again.

Aldarin held up his hand, to keep us quiet, and then pointed to his left. From where he pointed, no less then five more zombies came over the crest, and the battle was on again.

Swords slashing, and maces crunching, shrieks and sobs, and cries of pain were heard through out the forest. I cast as many spells as my body could handle, and I even stabbed a few with my Giant Snake Fang, and soon, none were left. The severity of the problem of Undead and Rivervale was apparent.

We ended our travels back in Rivervale, after alerting the proper authorities. Of course, they said, "We'll look into it, and see if there is anything we can do."

I suppose that means that Aldarin and I will have to go back, and see if we can destroy their master.
