Gilgadet Is Born

Hail, and well met, stranger," said the old man, as wind blew across his thinning hair. "You have come to hear the story of Gilgadet, both Wizard, and Scolar. A man of worldly origins, but perhaps, godly power. I will spin the his tale for all to hear."

And so he did tell me.

It all began seventy years prior, when Shamak Westwater, Gilgadets grandfather, decided to move his merchant business to Qeynos, as he heard of better business there. After the long trek from Freeport, he and his wife settled down and enjoyed years of profit.

It was during these years that Gilgadets father was born. He was a small baby, but Evander grew into a healthy toddler, however, he was quite destructive. Even though he was young, he often flew into fits of rage that no one had ever seen from a boy so young. His mother would protect him, and tell people, "He is just a baby!" However, none of the other people saw it this way. They chastised Shamak, and spoke such atrocities as, "Kill the boy, or leave the city, that is your only choice, now!"

With little knowledge of weapons, or art, the couple removed themselves from Qeynos, and relocated to a small village in the Jaggedpine Forest. There, they took up farming for a living, and raised Evander in relative safety.

Throughout adolescence, Evander learned to better control his anger. Although the family had lost it's fortune by being thrown out of Qeynos, they began to live a happier life. The time came, though, for Evander to leave the family home, and begin a life of his own.

He moved from the small village back to Freeport, fully intending to regain his family fortune. He began a small merchant outfit, The Humble Backpack, and began selling his merchandise. After a year, he met a beautiful woman who went by the name of Rune. The married, and began a family.

Gilgadet was born, on time, and healthy. They lived in relative peace for five years, when Evander's rage rose once again. On a stormy winter night, he and Rune had an argument, and he slashed her with a knife several times. He drug her body into the night, fully intending to leave her for dead. But she did not die.

With her strong will, Rune returned to the family home, to find that Evander was beating his only son. She snuck behind him, and smashed him with an iron cooking pan. As his eyes rolled back into his head, she lifted Gilgadet in her arms and whispered, "We must leave."

She packed very little, and moved away from Freeport; as far as she could afford. And so, the family once again returned to Qeynos. Not wanting to alarm the people, for she had been told of her husbands follies there, she changed her name from Westwater, to Qual' Iian, which meant in the elvan tongue, Blessed Ones. It also explains how a full blooded human aquired an elvan name. People, although very curious, do not seem to force the issue.

For ten years she worked odd jobs, trying to find a niche in the Qeynos society, so that she could provide for Gilgadet, and she had almost forgotten upon what terms she came to the city. She returned home one evening, after running some errands, and a knock sounded loudly on their inn room door.

As she opened the door, she found a note on the floor, with no trace of who left it. The note read:

Don't think you got away this easily, woman, for I know your every step. And, mark my words, Gilgadet will be dead soon. I promise.

Tears welled up in her eyes as a myriad of memories enveloped her. She knew that Evander was serious, and that something had to be done to protect Gilgadet. So she went to the Hall of Sorcery to see what could be done about it.

The Guildmaster listened to her words carefully, and said, "Woman. The best defense is a good offense. No one could protect your son as good as he. Send him down for a test, and let us see if he has the skills to become a wizard, so that no man, including this heathen, Evander, can harm him."

The old man sat back in his chair, looking at us, and waiting for some reaction. He sighed and stood and said, "I will tell you more in good time. These old bones need to rest." He walked away, and we wondered if we would get to hear more.
