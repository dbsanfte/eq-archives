Well, once you have started EQ, you cant go back to the other monitor. What you CAN do, is use the keyboard to change the WinSHowEQ display. I scan the keyboard buffer for keys pressed, so while you are playing the game, you can press END for example, and it will scroll down to the end of the tracking mob list. But, since WinShowEQ is put in the background, it runs a lot slower ( thinking about upping its priority). Anyway... to get the key to register before EQ nabs it from the buffer you have to press and hold it down for a second or 2.


As for the graphics card problem.. some BIOS setup chips will allow you to scan from first to last and vice versa.... if yours has this option, you can change the order in which your OS finds it.

Ok, ran it for the first time last night. Annoying graphic flashes (I am on 1 computer, and just run WSEQ and give it the IP). I think minimizing it after running solved this problem, I forget, crashed too many times last night (machine locking up, heat issues).

After a good nights killing, I wanted to look over the spawns I saw. Ok, grabbed the source to SEQ, hacked showspawn a bit [ie. ::fopen(LOGDIR "\\spawn.db", "rb");], and am starting to parse the file.

I am 99% sure that there is a logging problem. The sizeof(dbSpawnStruct) is 192 (0x0C0). Inside of this structure, things are at a specific offset, always (duh, just spelling this out). The name of the spawn is always at offset 0x066 (inside the structure). The bsaic formula for the spawn name will be:

sizeof(dbSpawnStruct)*record_number + 0x066

[Pretty straight forward, just want to clearly state my point.]

Ok:

The first record (spawn) is read perfectly fine. The offset of the name of the spawn is at 0x066 in the first record.

The next record is read into the structure, but the offset of the name of the spawn is at 0x0127?! (0x0C0 + 0x066 = 0x0126, NOT 0x0127)

The above offsets are from the file, NOT the structure, so something is being recorded incorrectly.

Just an FYI, hopefully fixed next patch (god I hate hearing that from the QA guys here - heh).
