Concerning Verants patch announcement... :: 4.4.2000
If you haven't read the recent patch message, you can catch it here: 04.04.00 Patch

This really shouldn't be news, but I guess it is. Yes, using the programs you find here violates the
contract you agree to every time you play EverQuest. Yes, they are allowed to search your tasklist for
certain programs running [because you agree to it when you click 'Accept']. No, they will not be able to
detect Linux ShowEQ. No, they will not be able to detect wether you own hacking programs so long as it
is not running at the same time EverQuest is or if it is on a different computer and not interfering with
the EverQuest datastream. Assuming Smedley keeps to his word about their scanning. He claims they will
never scan your hard drive nor registry, but it does make me wonder why this wasn't mentioned in the
agreement...?

"It's disturbing that the amount of recent developments in the hacking community force us to do this"

It is equally disturbing to think that recent developments in the hacking community justify your decision
to search for specific running programs on your customers computers. Since Verant is able to ban any
account for any reason, this next step they have chosen to take scares me a little. I've always supported
Verants protection to hacking attempts, but I simply find this inexcusable. The programs I choose to run
is my business. I have never used any program to gain an unfair advantage in EverQuest so I am not afraid
of being banned, that is not why this bothers me. What bothers me is that internet-based companies usually
copy each other. What if companies decide to make this a standard for internet-based software and future
complications arise when a company finds you running their competitors software? If you agreed to it...
they have every right to deny you service. I am not saying I think Verant will do this. Simply the
possibility bothers me. They do not have to give you a reason as to why you have been denied service so
there would be no telling as to why you would no longer be able to continue using the software.

Again, I'll restate that they can not detect ShowEQ. I do not know about WyvernX's ShowEQ, but the Linux
ShowEQ we have available on this website can not be detected. Nothing has changed.

Oh and just so you know, my opinions are not necessarily the opinions of others here at HackersQuest. If
you're going to quote this, don't quote it in HackersQuests name.
