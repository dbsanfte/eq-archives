Safe Inside Incunabula
by Lumbering Oaf
24 March 2000

Materializing from a rather ugly shade of purple, Lumbering Oaf appeared underneath the spire of Eastham. He stood there for a minute, admiring his stylish green outfit and gloating over the phat xp received from his vassals. With a wistful sigh, he dreamt of being interviewed by the Cold Presence. For he knew the most important answer.

"Who amongst you little beings is the most powerful?"

He would simply reply, "I am. A formidable archmage, I wield magic in all forms at the highest levels of power. But most importantly..." At this point he would slowly stride to a ledge, then turn around. "I fear not death." With a small hop backward, he would plummet thousands of feet to his death. The Cold Presence would then snicker be taken aback in awe...

"Hey, are you going to join us in Incunabula Vault or what?" Zaketh's voice interrupted LO's daydream.

"Oh, um, yeah... on my way." Oaf then hurriedly portaled to the scenic (meaning usually deserted) town of Dryreach. With a quick buff or three, he hopped through the East Dires portal. Greeted by some very friendly gold wasps, he regretted the rather plodding pace at which he could run.

After a few well-practiced RLASG maneuvers, he managed to disentangle himself from the swarms of swamp gromnies and mire wasps long enough to reach the entrance to the Incunabula Vault. Lumbering Oaf then began the intricate and rather lengthy process known to others as simply 'buffing'. Zaketh, Jin Lee, and Fyre-sun popped to the surface scant moments later, detecting his presence. Smiles, greetings, and snide comments about buffing followed.

As he twirled around in portal space, he could already hear the screams of white dots being cruelly pierced by orange dots. Things would be different once he materialized. There would be another screaming white dot. In any case, the group of four descended upon the Olthoi like gnats on a bull. That is, they were mildly annoying. Nevertheless, with the help of a few other intrepid adventurers, the immediate area was soon cleared of the big fierce bugs with pointy things. They plunged deeper into the depths... at least four or five feet. Stiff resistance was encountered in the form of four more soldiers. These too were dispatched, after Zaketh had executed a rather skillful maneuver where he got two soldiers to gouge each other's eyes out. It was really rather amusing, especially when they threw cream pies at each other and started saying 'Nyuk, nyuk'.

A lone soldier had apparently gotten one of his appendages stuck in the wall. Being of insectoid intelligence, it was running into the wall instead of away from it. As one can imagine, it wasn't having much success in extracting its arm. Zaketh, being the humanitarian (insectarian?) type, moved to put the Olthoi out of its misery. The three mages lazily watched as they chanted their sad song of Puish Zharil. The lighting must have been bad or something, because Zaketh's first mighty chop severed the Olthoi's stuck appendage instead of its head. He looked a little flustered when the Olthoi turned to focus its baleful, red-eyed glare at him. A scurrying sound quickly ensued, and a living wall of Olthoi swarmed out of the surrounding tunnels. No less than ten soldiers engulfed the hapless swordsman. Lumbering Oaf thought he heard a faint trickling sound, but he couldn't be sure.

Jin Lee muttered to herself, "Not good." Lumbering Oaf simply blinked in surprise, never having seen so many Olthoi at once. Fyre-sun, eminently practical, muttered a half-hearted "Malar Zhapaj" and motioned in Zaketh's direction. He didn't really expect it to hit a living target. By some miracle, Zaketh managed to squirm his way between two soldiers. Speeding past the three mages, he squeaked, "Help."

Many close calls later, the four sat resting next to the exit portal, exhausted and heavy with phat lewt. Vitae had been rather scarce, with no deaths actually inside the dungeon. Zaketh had escaped a fierce Olthoi with the merest sliver of health by jumping through the exit, only to be burnt to a crisp by a drudge ravener when he reached the surface. Other than that, the night had been remarkably un-VR-like. Jin Lee and Fyre-sun left, carrying a lot more equipment and a lot less life than they had come in with. Lumbering Oaf, being the greedy type, and Zaketh, being the even-greedier type, decided to stay. And so the vitae did rise.

Zaketh learned the joys of a shield buffed and polished with LO brand ShieldWax, the Impenetrability / Acid-Piercing Bane VI variety. As Oaf was rebuffing, Zaketh was casually tanking two soldiers. He declined the offer of aid, mentioning that the soldiers were nicking him for 0 and 1 points of damage, thanks to the shield. "As long as I keep them in front of me, I'm fine," he remarked.

"What happens when they get behind you?" Oaf raised an eyebrow as a pink swirl formed behind the 3l33t tank. One snickering Olthoi later, Zaketh was lifted off the ground, experiencing a rather sharp, stabbing pain.

Zaketh tells you, "Oops."

LO continued to rebuff, patiently waiting for Zaketh to return. After fighting a few Olthoi, he began to wonder why Zaketh wasn't back yet. Moments later, his ears turned red as Zaketh's voice came to him, muttering an impressive string of curses. He had died on the way there, in 'a tough spot'.

After retrieving Zak's corpse inside the dungeon, the duo set off into the heart of the Direlands swamps in search of Zak's other corpse. As they slowed to a stop, Oaf's face took on a puzzled look. "Something must be wrong with my radar. It's all orange. Oh well." He shrugged.

Zaketh blushed a little, then pointed to his corpse, several meters distant. Standing on top of it was a shadow lieutenant. And another. And another. And two panumbris shadows. Who were apparently conversing with four raveners and three stalkers. Who had brought along their pet gold wasps.

"Dang." Oaf began his buffing process, starting with Zaketh, laying every available protection on the blademaster. LO quickly exhausted his available mana on buffing him. Oaf was in the process of regaining mana to buff himself when another hapless adventurer zipped through the veritable fog bank of orange dots. Zaketh, seeing his opening, sprinted to his corpse while a few of the monsters were distracted. Oaf frantically spat out healing spells with what mana he had left. Zaketh managed to grab everything and sprint away. Oaf, being a little slower, was targeted by a few shadow lieutenants and drudges when he saw two nice blue messages scroll by, kindly informing him that:

Armor Self VI has expired.
The spell Impenetrability VI on Gelidite Robe has expired.

A nice, spinning blue crystal filled his view shortly thereafter. Lumbering Oaf heard a Cold Presence laughing. He sighed. Out of the four deaths of the night, only one (Zak's) had actually been in the dungeon. He wished he was back with the Olthoi, safe inside Incunabula.

- Oaf
