Last Stand at Fort Alamo: views of a Tank
by Zaketh
11 November 1999

Once again it was time to answer the call of Vitae Rising. This time we met in Holtburg with vague plans of going and depopulating the countryside. Last week's record attendance was shattered with 3 new additions to this week's trek of mayhem and vitae. In addition to our reluctant leader Hearding (he tries to deny it), Bruised Lee, Chan Tatao (who is late due to work?), myself (Zaketh), Lumbering Oaf, Jin Lee, Kilk, Putty, and Han Fu we had the additions of Odyssey, Baru Serpentslayer, and Samson al-Khalif. Watch these new ones they are up and comers as far as rising vitae is concerned.

A great discussion was brewing on what to do and which direction our depopulation efforts would take when someone spoke up with a good idea. It seems there was a fort in the mountains to the north that guarded a pass into the Northern Aluvian lands. Jin had gotten word they were making a desperate stand against forces of Banderlings insistent on breaking through and raising havoc.

Well Jin Lee being a stunning mage surprises us all and opens a portal that’s all white that whisks us to the floor of the great crater to the north. Dang mages are so convenient. Upon reaching this destination we are still waiting for Chan…. Guess work sucks, whatever that is. We all go snoop…oops wandering around the area and use the local lifestone. While waiting Bruised Lee and me get bored and start looking for something to kill. Well we found it…or it found us in the form of a pair of ugly Tuskers. At which point our fellowship descended upon the poor Tuskers and wiped them out. When you got 12 people there isn’t much to worry about.

After that Chan shows and we follow Jin out of the crater. Upon exit some Banderlings and a few mites sprang a vile trap on us. They were probably sent as a delaying action.

I hate mites. They remind me of a four-legged creature that haunted my dreams as a child. This yapping beast had four legs and ball shaped areas of fur and while small was disturbing to say the least.

Well to make a long story short we beat the tar out of this ambush. Alas poor Samson had fallen in the fight. The stupid mite had made a beeline for the young mage and gave him an express visit to the lifestone.  While Samson caught up all manners of theories on mage attraction in mites were discussed with a final conclusion being summed up by the Axeman Kilk.

Once Samson returned we continued on to the Fort and just a couple of valleys over we once again were set upon by tuskers. I was like…Woohoo! More easy fodder for the kill! Dangit don’t you hate it when you are wrong. One minute we were fighting the next someone yells “Theres a Goldenback”. Well I am an expert fighter I searched out this Goldenback and set upon him to show him his error in attacking us.

Damnit!!…I hate irony. Howdy there Mr. Lifestone how's it going…yeah those Goldenbacks are a little much aren’t they. While I was getting my wits back Lumbering Oaf is given the same free ride to the Lifestone as me. Well we start heading back and when we get there everyone has safely broken off and we are able to recover our items off our bodies.

  Well I was hell bent on getting revenge. No one slaps me around like a Drudge and gets away with it when I have 11 people backing me up. After getting a plan together we hit the Goldenback first. Oh btw while I was conversing with Mr. LS a Female Crimson Tusker makes an appearance to help her mate with pesky adventurers.

HA HA!!! We showed them what we were made of, at least in large gangs that is. The Goldenback and Crimson fell before us and thanks to the Mages we were spared visits to Mr. LS.

After much celebrating, looting of corpses, and pats on the backs we stopped to reflect on the scene of this fight. Odyssey then made an observation that dampened the mood for about 5 seconds before more celebrating continued.

Time being of the essence and us still needing to get to the fort before it fell we had to continue our trek across the valleys and snow covered forests. Needless to say if you listened closely you may have heard some low whining. Man we were passing some good killing. Jeez not to mention loot. It was tearing my warrior’s soul apart to pass by worthy adversaries.

Along the way our line became ragged and stretched out so a break was called to get us all together again. After we regrouped we set off again, but I decided a little killing was in order and attacked a Snowy Mattekar. Come on you can’t blame me, can you? All run and no hack and slash makes Zaketh a grumpy tank.

Well you would think I had learned my lesson long ago. Needless to say before my stalwart companions could bail…. mean come to my aid I got to see as my last dying vision the Snowy Mattekar collapse under VR blades.

DAMN!.. Howdy LS…. yeah I know it’s only been a bit since my last trip…no I don’t do it on purpose it just happens. Well I was way behind now and had to run to catch up. I even missed the brilliantly executed Chicken with its Head Cutoff Maneuver. I miss everything. Well when I finally reach the Lifestone near the fort the Chicken with its Head Cutoff Maneuver was coming to an end and everyone was forming up with only a few stragglers.

There it stood on the hill to the northeast. While not much to look at it was a military mans dream…*sniff*…it brought a tear to my eye, either that or the stinging north wind, can’t tell when I get choked up like that.

Well after that moment passed we ran to the defense of the fort and proceeded to teach Banderlings how to die in short order. This was a glorious battle where many were heroes and death was in the air. It was so tense you could almost taste the vitae.

While the fighters fought in the trenches the mages took to the parapets to rain death on Banderlings and to cast a timely heal to help the defenders throughout the fight. Here and there individuals did their best to stem the grim Banderling horde from succeeding. Little did we know that a bad omen was to walk our way.

What? We asked. Bruised Lee explained it again in a lull in the fight. His woman said he had to go. Well when something like that happens what can you do? Nothing Dammit. Jin Lee whisked BL back to the crater so he could continue on to his woman. While there Jin went on a supplies run for the rest of us. Did I mention I love convenient mages?

Bruised Lee’s departure was a hit to morale. Doom and despair entered our ranks and the lines were threatened by this break. Then our reluctant leader stepped into the spotlight and gave a rousing speech that ended with a line being drawn and all who would cross it was to stand and kick Bander butt. Needless to say I was pushe…umm….first to cross the line and volunteer to stand and protect the fort. The others followed and we decided to stand.

While Jin was shopping we continued to repulse sporadic Bander attacks with our morale renewed.  Many small raids were made and thus we were forced to run around and protect many fronts. Little did we know this was part of their diabolical plan to take the fort. When Jin returned we distributed supplies and continued the defense.  At this time the massive wave attacks of the Banderlings were renewed. We met them and held thanks to our newfound morale and supplies.

However, while we fought their Tumerok masters had finished digging into the fort basement and set off their evil spell of LAG. Then the Tumeroks emerged from the basement and proceeded to throw us into chaos. Things got desperate for the defense. We were pushed to the limit and beyond. The main group of our defenders got separated and small groups fought on their own. There were Hearding and Chan Tatao guarding each other’s backs. Baru Serpentslayer, even though less experienced, threw his body repeatedly into the fight regardless of the danger and helped many to rest and repair armor.

There was Odyssey and Han Fu cutting a swath thru the Banderlings and fighting Tumeroks all the time taking damage and sometimes dying and having to return to the fight a little less powerful but more determined.

Myself, Kilk, and putty fought a valiant defense of the stairs to the mages perch. During the fight we noticed two mages missing with Jin Lee being the only one left. When questioned she did not know when they had left.

Several attempts were made to push the Tumeroks out of the basement into their tunnels. The only thing accomplished was to push them down the stairs and leaving behind some of our bodies. Even though Vitae Rising was a mighty group we were outnumbered.

Slowly but surely the battle was being lost. The Tumeroks occasionally were pushed back and there were lulls in the battle in which we had to survey the situation. Then it was decided that we could no longer stand so we were forced to abandon the fort to the Tumeroks and their evil spell of LAG.

We fought our way out and were regrouping near the lifestone when I saw him. There he sat on a brilliant white stallion wearing brown leather armor and with a fur cap with a tail and a mask. “You did the Alamo proud son”, the figure said. I turned to my companions and told them to look. However, by the time I turned around again he was gone and I heard a faint “Hi ho Bowie!” I explained what I saw to Chan Tatao and all he did was look at me a little weird and looked at my helmet and asked if I hit my head or there were any dents in it. I tried to convince my companions of this masked man but they only rolled their eyes at me. I know the truth.

I looked once more and uttered, “Who was that masked man?”

“Why that was the Lone Davy Crockett”, said a grizzled miner in a funny hat.

Again, I yelled for my companions to look and again he was gone with a voice in my head that told me, “Remember Fort Alamo”.

After much looking over my head and helmet Jin opened another portal to the crater and thus we left the field of battle and left Fort Alamo to the Banderlings with vague promises of return
