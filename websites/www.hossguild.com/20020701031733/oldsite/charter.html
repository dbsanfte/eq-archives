Guild Structure

Hoss has no guild leader. The leadership consists of 7 officers. Their role is to assist with membership issues, loot distribution, and to address any problems that arise in the guild.


Membership Policy

The policy for adding new members is outlined in the Prospective Members section. Any member can sponsor a prospective member. To sponsor someone, the member will make a post to the new members private forum. If no outright objections are voiced in a 2 day period, that person will become a trial member.

The length of the trial will be determined on a case by case basis. In general is the person is well knows to a majority of the guild the trial will be very short. In cases where the person is not well known, the trial will be longer. In general most trials will last 2-3 weeks. During the trial period the prospective member will be guilded, and remain guilded. They will be given access to the private guild message forum. They will not have access to the new member discussion forum. During the trial members are encouraged to post their thoughts regarding the prospective member to the new member forum. If a situation arises where it is clear the trial member would not be a good addition to Hoss, the trial will be ended prematurely.

At the end of the trial the officers will make a final decision as to whether to make the trial member a permanent member. If granted permanent member status, all threads pertaining to said person will be deleted from the new member forum and they will be given access to the forum so they can become involved in the discussion of future prospective members.

If a situation arises where a conflict between 2 or more members cannot be resolved by the parties in involved, the officers will intervene. Should the officers decide that the best thing for the guild is the deguilding of one of the parties involved, that will be done.


Loot

The guild policy on loot is divided by category. The categories are defined by how many people are required to obtain an item. So while a particular item may be extremely rare, if it only requires a single group to obtain, it is considered common loot.

Distribution of common loot (i.e. Hierophant cloak, bloodpoint, etc) will be entirely up to those people involved in hunt. Uncommon loot (i.e. most pieces of planar armor) will be distributed by a rotation. The individual classes will decide on the order of the rotation, and be responsible for maintaining the rotation. Exceptions, such as a person missing a single piece, etc, are up the class to decide about. Absence will not result in you getting extra pieces when you are present (i.e. you are third on list to receive an item. You miss a hunt and the 2 people above you each get 2 items. The next hunt you are there, you will be up for the first item, but do not get 3 items in a row to bring you up to parity).

If a problem arises in distribution of uncommon loot, the officers will make final decision. Distribution of rare loot (God/dragon/planar BPs/robes) will be done by the officers. This decision will take into account need, participation, usefulness, etc. In most cases the decision will be made beforehand (i.e. before a known dragon raid). While the officers will make all final decisions about all rare loot (even in cases where there is overwhelming agreement as to who should get item, in order to maintain consistency), this decision will not be made in a vacuum. Rare loot distribution can be discussed on the guild's private message board.

All rare loot is considered guild loot. This means that if a person gets an item they are expected to use the item. If a time comes when said person no longer needs/wants, or regularly uses the item, it will be redistributed just as if it were a new item (decided by the officers). This does not apply to items being used part time (i.e. shaman using Nagafen hammer sometimes, paw other times...or warrior switching between 1h and 2h weapons as situation dictates). It is also expected that valuable items obtained with a group (i.e. common or uncommon as defined above) not be traded away for personal benefit if other members of the group that helped obtain said item, still want/need it.

No lotto's will be used for uncommon or rare loot...ever.


Interguild Events

Any member can propose, arrange, coordinate an interguild event. In cases where a small number (or single) of non HOSS people partake in a hunt, they will be considered equally for all common/uncommon loot. For the most part they will not be considered for rare loot, except in cases where said person has grouped with the guild many times, showing a pattern of participation that justifies being considered. This means they will be added to the rotation for planar armor, etc. Where they go in the rotation is up to the individual classes to decide.

In cases where the event is more a multiguild event, the person coordinating the event will discuss loot distribution with the other guild. All rare loot that comes to HOSS will still be distributed by the officers (i.e. it is decided to zone lotto an CoF from Talendor, if a Hoss member wins lotto, it still goes to the person decided upon by the officers, regardless of who won the lotto).


Communication

All members are encouraged to check the private board regularly. When issues come up involving the guild, lack of response will be assumed to mean assent with the decision that is arrived at.

Other forms of guild communication outside the game include IRC and email. Members are encouraged to partake in as many of these as possible to stay up to date with news and events.


Code of Conduct

It is expected that members of the guild treat other members with respect at all times. Problems arise when people make off the cuff remarks in guildchat. There is often more to a situation than is readily apparent, and unless you are sure you know exactly what is going on, members should refrain from making derogatory comments about other members in guild chat. If a member has a problem with another member, it should be addressed with an officer.

'Bashing' other guilds in guildchat is highly discouraged. Regardless of what you think of another guild, their methods, tactics, etc, it never accomplishes anything to talk derogatively of an entire guild. This is not to say you can't have personal opinions, but it is expected that members keep those opinions out of guildchat.

Topics discussed on our private forums are generally not to be seen by or told to any non guild members. Compromising the security of the guild's website and/or private forums will lead to disciplinary action and may lead to deguilding if the intent was malicious in nature.

No posts should be made regarding Hoss' accomplishments to other message boards. We will accomplish what we accomplish, we can be proud of what we do without having to brag to others publicly.
