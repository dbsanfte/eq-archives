Eight new purty maps
Submitted by: NiteRaven on Monday, June 19th at 11:36am CST

As always, I seem to love this number, I have eight new maps from cartographers around the vast land of Norrath. First up is a set of maps of Dalnir (set 4) from Draelon Stormsong. I haven't heard from him in a long time but he is widely considered to be among the elite cartographers out there. He has a unique style of layering his dungeons that I have yet to see duplicated.

Next two maps are of Kurn's Tower, one of the upper floors (map 1) and the other of the basement level (map 2). Both of these come from newcomers in the trade but show great promise with the skill and detail taken to draw these maps.

The old veteran Maps of Veeshan comes out with another map, this time Karnor's Castle (map 2). I'm somewhat disappointed in this map because of the lack of detail and the new approach she has taken to drawing maps. It appears she has left her old style of color pencil sketches and is now moving towards computer aided design. Her hand drawings inspired many...

Another cartographer that has caught the eye of many in recent weeks is Zealott of Seekers of Lore, Veeshan. His maps have real nice old style to them that keeps you in the game. This week he brings us all two new maps of Temple of Droga (map 2) and Mines of Nurga (map 3).

Bringing in a new era of map design, Ratok Pathfinder has created a map of the Sarnak Fortress (map 3) in the Lake of Ill Omen using a program called EQmapper that I mentioned in last week's news post. Although this is only a small map, there is something nice about a map that is perfectly to scale and is has perfect precision.

Last but not least, another old veteran to cartography Kuan of Cazic Thule brings us back to the old school with a map of Rivervale (map 3). Aren't halflings so cute? :P


Nine new maps
Submitted by: NiteRaven on Thursday, June 8th at 01:41pm CST

Today's update includes some great maps made by three cartographers. First map is of Dalnir's Crypt (map 2) and comes from Karle Lichloathe, his first map but very well done! The next two are of Dalnir's Crypt (map 3) and Lower Guk (map 5) and come from Zealot of Seekers of Lore, Veeshan. I really like his Lower Guk map because of the painstaking detail put into it. I've added their guild website to the link section to share the rest of their impressive library of lore.

The remaining maps come from our old friend Kuan of The Taken, Cazic Thule. His new maps include Frontier Mountains (map 3), Lake of Ill Omen (map 2), Temple of Droga (map 1), Mines of Nurga (map 2), Firiona Vie (map 3), and Warliks Woods (map 2). He took a break for a while then recently started pumping out Kunark maps like crazy. As always his maps are of great quality and print out better then most.

One last thing I wanted to mention was a really cool program called EQmapper made by Jebo. It allows you to create very detailed maps by taking locations in the game while you have the log feature on. By taking many locations of and around landmarks or buildings in the game you are plotting their positions. Once you exit the game, the program will load the log file and connect all of those points and create a map. So far he has created only a few maps from the program because of the amount of work it takes to plot all points, but with help of others he hopes that he will have an entire library full of perfectly accurate maps. Please support him in this effort and send both of us a copy of the map so we can share it with the everyone.
