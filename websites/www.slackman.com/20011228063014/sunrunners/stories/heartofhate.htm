Episode 1: Girl bashes things in the swamp

     The froglok moved through the swamp, it powerful hind legs propelling it with short hops. It zigzagged through the dense vegetation in search of food. It stopped by an old tree, and started to dig. Its small webbed hands efficiently scooped out muck to uncover a dull, waxy root, which was quickly put into a netted bag. Layers of fungus, scraped from a tree went into another bag. It began to hop away in search of more food for its brethren, when something caught its eye. Paleness, white really, was standing out strongly against the browns and greens of the swamp. The pale form had the color of metal in the middle. Quietly, the froglok crept closer. What it saw was not a Troll and thus it relaxed a bit. Still, the paleness was too large to put in a bag. Losing interest, the froglok turned its bulbous eyes away from the pale figure, completely unaware that someone else was sharing those same eyes.

    "Yar!" Grammaw thought to herself, "Nudder stinkee whitey commin tu da swamp. Gud, meeze needin tu git sumfin frum dat fing." Dipping her hands into the algae-laden water, she soon had a squirming fish. In three quick bites there was nothing left of the fish but the head. Grammaw found it strange that so many Trolls actually cooked their food nowadays. She seemed to be the only one who remembered the old ways. Eating something fresh let you consume part of its essence, its spirit. Eating the fish made Grammaw a better swimmer. The body housed the soul. Fire cleansed things, took the power away. What had happened in Grobb while she has away? She remembered different faces, different ideas. But that was Before. Grammaw’s Other Self remembered the Before. There was Carver Gnask, who was much shorter than the Carver in Grobb today. Nobody else recalled Carver Gnask. The Other Self did, though.

    Shaking her thoughts aside, Grammaw called out to one of the spirits of Rage that hovered about. With a few sharp commands, she forced the spirit into her body, consuming it entirely. For a short time, before it was utterly consumed and absorbed, the spirit would add its strength to her own. She then took the fish head, and with well-practiced motions, chewed the gills off and spat them into her hand. Small animals had weak spirits, and parts of the body were needed for the magic. The body housed the soul. Pressing the gills to the sides of her neck, she called the spirit of the fish. The gills, still holding some of the essence of the fish, blended into her neck. Soon, Grammaw had gill slits in her neck. Sliding her bulk into the swamp, she opened her mouth and let the water wash over her new gills. Grammaw began to swim.

    Peering from beneath the water, Grammaw could see the pale elf sharpening his blade. Even in the middle of the swamp, the elf had the vanity to keep himself clean and shiny. With a push off the bottom of the swamp, Grammaw leapt onto shore. Her moss and algae covered form exploding from the swamp must have startled the High Elf. Grammaw called out to the trees next to him. The elf recovered from his surprise just as the nearby vegetation twined around his legs, holding him fast. Grammaw quickly found and harnessed a large snake-spirit, and sent it into the elf. The toxic spirit entered the elf’s nose and mouth, and released its venom into the organs of the elf. While she could simply wait and let the elf succumb to the toxins in his body, Grammaw needed to be close when he died. There were some things that required a more…personal touch. Unlimbering her Gatorsmash Maul, Grammaw charged the immobile elf. She brought the maul down onto the shoulder of the elf. The magic in the weapon caused a large chunk of the elf’s flesh to split apart. The Moment was coming. The elf turned away from his attacker and began to flee. She had seen it in his face though. The expression of doom. This was the expression that most beings had when they knew for certain that they were going to die. Grammaw again brought the Maul down upon the elf. He spun around and sank his blade deep into Grammaw’s left biceps. With a grunt, she dropped the Gatorsmash and trapped the elf’s blade in her elbow. Her right hand then shot out, and she grabbed the elf’s throat and began to squeeze. The elf frantically tried to pummel his attacker, his pale skin showing dark black and purple where the veins drew the poison to the skin.

    The Moment had come. The elf’s eyes started to widen in terror of his final seconds. It always was the same. The eyes always were wide open at the moment of death, as if the victims…saw something.

    "J’ariel li, n’qua?" Grammaw growled at him in his native speech. What do you see? She repeated it again, "J’ariel li, n’qua?" What do you see? She debated relaxing her grip so that the elf could speak. One last breath was too many, she decided. The elf stopped moving, all but for a leg. This was due to a small alligator that had fastened its jaw around the foot of the corpse and was tugging at it, trying to wrest it from Grammaw.

    "Puuk tak, bah bok!" This is my kill. When a Troll utters those words, there will soon be another death. The alligator was grabbed by the tail and smashed into a stout tree. Its spine was broken by the impact, and it lay in the muck, writhing, slowly dying.

    Having fended off all rivals to her kill, Grammaw skinned the elf, and turned the carcass into nice gobbets. These would feed her for a time. Then she took the carefully prepared scalp back into her cave in Grobb. She knew she had to work quickly, Juzu would wake soon.
