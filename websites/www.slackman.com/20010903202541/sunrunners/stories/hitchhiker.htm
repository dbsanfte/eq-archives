Hitchhiker's guide to Kunark....well, sort of....
 
    A beautiful half-elf Ranger named Lgashu Mannadrain decided one day to explore new challenges in the mystical land of Kunark.  Now, Lgashu, having been a Ranger of Tunare for only 23 seasons, she had to be wise in her adventuring choices.  Unfortunately in this case, her adventurous spirit almost got the best of her.
 
    Lgashu had ventured to Kunark a few years earlier, but quickly returned after being overpowered in battle numerous times.  She was still quite wary of this wondrous land.  Most of her discomfort comes from the necessity of finding a city to call home while adventuring.  Even though she is a Ranger, for some reason she can never seem to find an affinity with the great outdoors.
 
    The closest thing to a city other than the horrible evil land of Cabilis, is the main port of Kunark called Firona Vie.  Firona Vie is crowded with many hideous creatures that Lgashu can tell just by looking at them would have no greater pleasure then to feast on her remains on a moments notice.
 
    Lgashu however had heard rumors of a place called "Overthere", which contains a small stronghold called "The Outpost" that she might be able to find some comfort and short term residence in.  The stories of the "Overthere" spoke of fierce creatures that traveled alone and would be easy prey for Lgashu as she sought her 24th season adventuring in Norrath.
 
    After asking some fellow adventurers about the Overthere and it's Outpost, Lgashu learned about traveling to the area via an old, broken down raft that starts it's journey from the Oasis of Marr.  This Oasis was a familiar hunting ground for her and she was quite familiar with the raft, so Lgashu decided it was time to explore this new hunting ground.
 
    So just hours after realizing her 23rd Season, Lgashu journeyed to Oasis, eager to begin her new adventure.  She boarded the raft and soon found herself on a small island in the mysterious ocean called Timorous Deep.  She noticed the island was inhabited by a small band of Ogres who had set up a small merchant camp.  To her surprise, the Ogres scowled at Lgashu, ready to attack if she presented them with an opportunity.  This troubled Lgashu as Ogres had always been friendly and amiable in her dealings with them.  This should have been her first clue that she had exhibited bad judgment on beginning this journey.  However, her pioneering spirit persuaded her to continue.
 
    Lgashu decided not to provoke a confrontation with the obviously misled Ogres and used her powers of camouflage to hide from them as she boarded another raft for the balance of her journey to the Overthere.  Soon after departing this island, the raft entered an area of calm seas in Timourous Deep and just sat there, rocking silently.  Being eager to catch a glimpse of Kunark, Lgashu ventured to the starboard side of the raft and FELL OFF!  However, rather than panicking, she used her superior swimming skills to quickly make her way back to the raft and get back on board.  This should have been Lgashu's second clue.  But alas, she continued her journey.
 
    After sailing a bit further on the raft, the mysterious land of Kunark finally came in view and for the first time Lgashu saw the "Overthere" and marveled at it's wide open plains.  Lgashu knew full well she would enjoy this place.  Being eager to track down her first prey, Lgashu dove into the water and swam to shore.  Upon reaching the beach, she was about to seek out someone who could help her set up her camp in the Outpost when received a message from Brother Mindert, a fellow traveler loyal to the brotherhood of the Sunrnners, as is Lgashu.
 
    Brother Mindert inquired incredulously of Lgashu "The Outpost??  Followers of Tunare are killed on sight in that area!  What are you thinking?  Get out of there quickly and DON'T INTERACT WITH ANYONE!"  For the first time Lgashu realized the gravity of her situation.  She looked around and saw many creatures scowling at her, ready to attack at a moments notice.  She could tell by looking that she would be no match in battle for these fierce creatures.  For the first time in many seasons, Lgashu felt fear creep into her soul.
 
    Lgashu quickly found cover and made her shape again blend into the surroundings.  She then opened her pack and dug out the roughly scrawled maps of Kunark she had acquired from fellow adventurers.  Her heart raced and she trembled a bit as she studied the maps, looking for a way out of this dangerous place, all the while silently praying to Tunare that she allow her to survive this mental blunder.  She glanced up just in time to notice the raft she had arrived on disappearing over the horizon.  She had no way out but to travel across Kunark to find a safe haven.  After studying the maps for a few minutes, it was apparent that Lgashu's only safety lied in the dangerous port of Firona Vie. 
 
    After weighing her options and discovering they were a slim few, Lgashu decided to regain her wits about her and begin to make her way to Firona Vie.  Lgashu checked to make sure her camouflage was adequate and began to run north along the shoreline as the maps had indicated, looking for a small wooded enclave that would lead to an area called Warsilks Woods.
 
    Lgashu ran for what seemed like days till she finally noticed the tracks leading into the woods.  This must be the way to Warsilks Woods she thought, and she followed the tracks.  Sure enough, she soon found herself in an area that seemed more like a jungle than mere woods.  The area was overgrown with trees that swayed quietly in the wind.  That breeze was the only sound to be heard for miles, and it was disquieting.
 
    After running for a while, Lgashu came upon a bumpy dirt trail of sorts that resembled one of the trails scrawled on the map of Warsilks Woods that she had.  She began following the trail northeast and soon found herself in the middle of some sort of stronghold that was overrun with goblins.  As she peered at the goblins from a distance, she decided that they were no threat to her and continued her journey, but all the while making sure she adjusted her camouflage to match the surroundings.  After safely winding through the goblin fort, Lgashu soon began noticing the terrain beginning to change and she knew she was approaching an area that the native Iskars called the "Field of Bone".
 
    Field of Bone was a barren wasteland that resembled part beach, and part desert.  But the area was also littered with bones from creatures that had died.  This was obviously where the name came from.  Soon, the huge monolith known as Kurns tower came into view and Lgashu had a good idea of just where she was as this was an area she had fought in a few seasons ago.  She made her way around Kurns tower and continued east, all the while adjusting her camouflage so she would blend into the barren land.
 
    Lgashu soon noticed an area ahead that she knew to be a swamp known as "Swamp of no hope".  Lgashu made her way through the dismal, dark swamp while only encountering one creature along her path.  It was one of those strange frog like creatures, and although she didn't feel this as a threat to her, Lgashu still maintained her camouflage and quietly slipped past the frog.
 
    Lgashu's heart began to pound again as the sounds of Firona Vie began to eerily seep into the swamp.  Lgashu again adjusted her camouflage and began to sprint through the area, running as fast as she could in her efforts to reach the safety of the docks.
 
    All was going well when a pair of strange creatures came into view.  Travelers through Firona referred to these creatures as Drolvargs.  Lgashu froze in her tracks as she realized that she could be seen by these creatures, despite her efforts to blend into the surroundings.  As she examined them from afar, it was quite clear that she would be no match for one of these creatures, much less 2 of them.  Lgashu again began to fear for her life and thought about jumping off the steep sheer cliffs into the Timorous deep.  She pondered the possibility that she would survive the fall and actually be able to swim to the docks.  She quickly glanced over the edge of the cliffs and decided that the fall would probably kill her. 
 
    Just about the time that Lgashu decided to turn around and head back to the relative safety of the swamp, she noticed that the Drolvargs had disappeared and she slowly began making her way south towards the docks, keeping a wary eye for the various creatures of Firona Vie that would sure bring death with them.
 
    Soon the unmistakable shape of the docks shimmered into view and Lgashu sprinted for their safety.  She soon came up to the bridge that led to the docks and made her way across.  Once she had crossed the bridge, Lgashu stopped and breathed a deep sigh of relief!  Somehow, with her skill in camouflage, her keen sense of direction, and her wits always about her, Lgashu had made it all the way from the shores of the Overthere to the docks of Firona Vie without once having to raise her swords in anger!  
 
    Lgashu sat and prayed, thanking Tunare for choosing to spare her and forgive her for her err in judgment.  Lgashu then meditated on the fact that it just goes to show how important it was for her to continue to hone the many skills she had learned and to strive to learn new ones.
