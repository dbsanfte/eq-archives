Loot Rules


Golden Rule:

Please remember to thank the Raid leaders. They need to know we appreciate them for taking on the difficult and mostly thankless task of running a raid. They are always doing their very best to make sure the raid is run fairly and in a manner that provides the most enjoyment for all. Without them, all the cool loot would not be possible.

General Rules:


1. Every raid will have a designated Main Looter (ML) who will handle all Loot and Lottos for the raid. He is responsible for tracking loot drops and winners for each class on each day. Group leaders will pass any droppable cash items to him, as well as let him know of Class specific drops. Never loot any No Drop item unless you have received specific instruction from the ML to do so.

2. Gem lotto is run separately from Class specific Lotto. Gems are typically defined as: Diamonds (D) and Blue Diamonds (BD). Black Sapphires and Jacinth may be either lottoed or count as cash items, depending on the raid.

3. All cash items will be used to reimburse the costs of the raid. Peridots for clerics, Portstones for the planes, coffins should there be a need, and any other expenses incurred during the raid. Cash items include Rings, Necklaces, Earrings, but may also include Black Sapphires and Jacinth.

4. NEED before GREED! (i.e. A weapon that is all melee class but mediocre for all except Shaman, is not a NEED item for other than the Shaman class).

5. NEVER send a tell to the Pulling Group. Tells such as 'pull X MOB' or ''When do you think MY mobs will be pulled" are considered a serious breach of Raid conduct and will be reported to the raid leader and/or the appropriate guild leader. The pull group will get to the mobs that drop your loot when it is safest for them to do so.

6. If you have suggestions for the raid rules/loot/strategy make them before or after the Raid. NOT during. The Loot rules are published before the raid as a means of eliminating any difficulties during raid. If an unusual situation arises during the raid, or you disagree with the disposition of loot, please save your comments until AFTER the raid has concluded. In all matters, the Raid Leadership decision is final. However, after the raid, the leaders would be happy to discuss the decision and consider any possible improvements to raid procedure you may have.

7. All raid participants must turn off /anon and /role before the raid begins. Often the ML will use /who {class} to determine if everyone has rolled or if the Class Specific lotto needs to be reset. If you are /anon or /role there is the possibility of being skipped on a loot. If a party member is AFK or LD and eligible for the lotto, group leaders should definitely make the ML aware, but the loot may be given away to the current highest roller if it is No Drop. Under no circumstances will the loot be allowed to decay waiting on someone to come back from AFK or LD.

Specific Looting Rules:


1. Group Leader (or whoever the group leader nominates) of the experience-gaining group loots the MOB. No one else!

2. Looter of MOB calls out loot to Zone (even if there is nothing), makes the ML aware of the drop, as well as giving any droppable cash items to the ML. The looter MUST have "/lootnodrop always" set.

3. The ML will then call out the Loot, the classes eligible for lotto and the random numbers. After the roll, the ML will close the Lotto and announce a winner. Please hold any congrats until after the ML has announced a winner.

 

DKP Rules:

The fair and equitable distribution of loot acquired on raids to members of Death Misdealt will be done using a point system. Points are earned by attending official guild raids. Items are obtained by spending points. This means that guests will not be entitled to loot. Pledges and Probationary members will need to earn points before being able to participate in loot. Pledges do not earn points until they are voted in and become Probainary members. Probationary members earn points just like full members. Points are assigned to the player - NOT to the character. If a player brings 3 characters to a 3 point raid, they will earn 3 points only - not 9. Points may be spent for whichever character the player decides.


What is an official guild raid?

An official guild raid is one that is planned by an officer and is posted on the raid board. A raid requires a minimum of 2 groups. Points will be earned for official raids only. The only exception to this rule is a guild-run raid that is for killing a major mob that needs the guild to assemble on the spur of the moment (such as Trakanon or Severilous). All official raids will be assigned a certain amount of points based on the type of the raid being held. The points given to each raid will be stated when the raid is posted. But most raids will be easy to figure out the points awarded from the type of raid held.

Attendance and being on time will be stressed for raids. Showing up on time will give you 1 additional point as an incentive for being on time to the raid. Being on time will be defined as arriving no more than 15 minutes after the scheduled start time or when the raid begins, whichever is deemed appropriate by the raid leader. There will be an exception of a 30 minute allowance, if the raid requires a lot of time for buffing and prepping for the raid to be a success. Again, this will be part of the raid post. There will also be an additional 1 point awarded for the raid if you stay until the end, when the raid leader calls it or when too many have left the raid for a successful continuance.

Earning Points:

All faction raids will be worth 2 points if you stay for a majority of the raid, no start or finish points.

The minor raids that do not need a major showing on will be given a point reward of 2 points. Examples of this might be to go farming items in zones like Maiden's Eye or Umbral Planes. There will be a beginning point awarded, but no ending point since these kind of raids usually run until people just give up and go to sleep.


Raids that involve getting kills for epics are worth 3 points. No beginning or ending points will be awarded. The raid must be scheduled and require 2 or more groups. An exception situation will be where an officer may declare an epic raid on the spur of the moment when a rare mob spawns.

A raid for killing a single major mob such as Vindi or Trakanon are worth 2 points. A beginning point will be awarded, but an ending point will not be awarded unless the raid continues after the mob is killed, such as farming the lair for spells after killing Trakanon.

The major raids that we need a strong member showing on like ToV or Plane of Fear are worth 5 points. There will only be a beginning point awarded.

If a raid fits in multiple categories, the officer scheduling the raid will decide in which catagory to place the raid and announce it in the post.

Points will be awarded at the end of the raid and will be available for spending at the next raid.


Item Costs:

Common drops at a raid that are minor will require no points to purchase them.

Common items that no one wants except by one person will not be given a purchase point cost. This may also include some class specific items that may drop.

Good items that are major upgrades for many people (such as a high end weapon, ex. Cresent Blade of Luclin) will get a point cost of 16 points. This applies even if no one on the raid needs the item. The item will go to the guild bank so someone will be able to buy the item later on.

Epic items will cost 6 points.

Major items that are needed by people will be given a point value of 20-30 points. An item like this may be a high end haste item like a Cloak of Flames or a Breastplate from Trakanon.
Major uber items from uber mobs like Vindi on up will have a point cost of 35-60 points depending on the item, location, and difficulty of attaining that item.

Basic planar armor (including Thurgadin and Kael Drakkal) will be given a 6 point cost each. Basic armor drops in ToV will be given a 8 point cost each.

Planar legs, robes and bp drops will have a cost of 16 – 20 points. ToV legs robes, and BP drops will have a cost of 25 points.

All droppable items remain guild property. When that item is upgraded, it is to be returned to the guild. Turn in value is half the points used to buy the item. The rules for items in the bank still apply. All items that are received from raids or the guild bank are still guild property whether you bought them or not.

Starting Points:

Members with a main character under level of 60 (minimum level of 52) will begin with no points. For each level reached by a main character, 5 points will be awarded. An additional 5 points will be awarded for the super hell levels of 54 and 59 for a total of 10 points on these levels.

Level 60 members will start out with 15 points to spend since they can no longer level to get points.

Spending Points:

When an item drops, the raid leader will announce the item and it’s point value. All those with enough points to spend on this item will be allowed to roll. As always, the random system will still be used to award the item. The winner of the roll will be given the item and the appropriate amount of points will be subtracted from the person's point total. If only one person has sufficient points for an item, they win by default. If no one has sufficient points for an item, and the item is no-drop, then those wishing to roll may and the winner’s points will go negative. A person with negative points will not be allowed to roll on any other items until their points are in the positive numbers again. The exception here is for epic pieces. A raid for an epic item is not to be scheduled until the person needing the piece has the required number of points for the item. If no one wishes to spend points on the no-drop item, it will be left to rot. At no time will anyone be allowed to loot a no-drop item without spending the required points. An exception to this is when a mq'able no-drop item drops. An officer may loot the item to hold. All droppable items that are not bid on will go to the bank.

On some special items that drop that are exceptionally rare, they may be allowed to receive a buyout bid in points. What is meant by this is that an exceptionally rare item may drop and it might be an upgrade for someone that never uses his or her points ever because nothing drops that will upgrade that person. In this case, the people with enough points may be allowed to bid as many points as they want up to the maximum that they already have until only one person is left to get the item. This is necessary so that even the people that never need anything will have a chance to spend their points on something once in a while.
