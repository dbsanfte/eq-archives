Rules of Conduct

 

Above all else, treat others as you wish to be treated. This applies to your guildmates, as well as the other residents of Norrath. Rudeness and abusive behavior will not be tolerated.

Be sensitive to others feelings. Please be considerate when talking about things that could be considered offensive to others (racial remarks, sexual remarks, drugs, objectionable language, etc.) on guildchat, any public channel or on the message boards.

Kill stealing, training and ninja looting will not be tolerated.

Making derogatory comments about other players or guilds on either guildchat or public channels is not acceptable.

Arguing on public channels is not acceptable. If you have a disagreement with another player, take it to /tells.

Cheating and underhanded trade practices will not be tolerated. Death Misdealt has an enviable reputation on SolusekRo. Your job as a guild member is to uphold that reputation.

Druids and Wizards are NOT taxis and will never be forced to provide transportation. Their services may be offered but NEVER demanded. Asking if a gater is available once, is acceptable - constant requests / demands are not.

Clerics are never to be forced to resurrect you. Asking if a cleric is available once is acceptable - constant requests / demands are not.

Mages are never to be forced to provide Call of the Hero. Asking for it once is acceptable - constant requests / demands are not.

Guildchat is not the auction channel. Please keep price checks and selling to a minimum.

Request for powerleveling / equipping an alternate is never to be demanded.

Blatantly disobeying any rule will result in ONE warning from a guild officer. If the problem continues, immediate guild removal will result.

If an officer reprimands you at any time, you are expected to cease and desist immediately. An officer has the power and authority to forcibly remove any member who is insubordinate in the face of a reprimand, no exceptions. This applies to guildchat, open channels and the message boards. At a later time, you are allowed to settle your differences with an individual officer via /tell only. If in dire cases you feel you are being severely wronged and wish to bring your grievance up with a higher authority, you are allowed to write a formal e-mail, or send a /tell to the guildleader who will then bring up the issue with the full complement of officers. The decision of the officer council is final.
