Raid Rules

 

Please remember to thank the Raid leaders. They need to know we appreciate them for taking on the difficult and mostly thankless task of running a raid. They are always doing their very best to make sure the raid is run fairly and in a manner that provides the most enjoyment for all. Without them, all the cool loot would not be possible.

The Raid Leader is responsible for posting (on the Raid Board) the location, minimum level requirement (if any), and the gathering and starting times for the raid.

The Raid Leader has total authority for the raid. Groups will be formed to ensure the success of the raid. Arguing over the composition of the groups will not be tolerated.

Do not send tells to the Raid Leader unless you are requested to provide specific information. The coordination of a raid takes a lot of work and the constant interruptions only make it worse.

Do NOT send requests for the puller to bring specific mobs. They and the raid leader / pull group will determine what will be pulled and when.

Do NOT offer to have your friend(s) pull. The puller / pull group will determine what gets pulled and when.

Failure to follow the Raid Leader's instructions will result in your banishment from the raid. A second occurrence will result in exclusion from two future raids. A third offense will result in guildremoval.

Transportation will be provided until 15 minutes before the posted starting time. If you are unable to make the stated time, arrange to move to the gathering point the night before or arrange for your own transportation.

Call of the Hero will be provided when possible, but should not be expected.

Peridots and pearls will be supplied for raids. Contact the Treasurer if you need them.

Loot distribution will be done according to the posted guild loot rules. Any exception to the rules will be announced at the start of the raid and at intervals during the raid. Arguments over loot will not be tolerated.

The Puller / Pull Group are to be left alone during a raid. /Tells are disruptive and can lead to the failure of the raid. If you have a question, talk to your group leader. If the group leader cannot answer the question, they will contact an officer.

The Raid Leader will designate the Raid Looter. Each group leader will appoint one person in the group to loot. The looter MUST HAVE confirmation of looting no drop items turned on (/lootnodrop always). Droppable loot is to be given immediately to the Raid Looter. The description and stats of all no drop items is to be sent to the Raid Looter, who will announce the item. Refer to the loot rules for distribution information.
