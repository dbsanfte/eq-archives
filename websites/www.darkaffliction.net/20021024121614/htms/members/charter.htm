Our Charter

*Our guild is not a "Transition" guild to join til you can join a higher level guild. We have leveled together and become great friends and will stay together at high levels. Together, we will achieve great things and defeat nasty beings. We will continue to recruit members within our level guidelines and carry our evil throughout Norrath!* 

"We are Dark Affliction and our evil will spread through eternity..."

  Dark Affliction is an evil guild on the Firiona Vie server, even as such Dark Affliction expects its members to behave in a respectable manner.

While many aspire to the conquest of great beasts or the acquisition of vast wealth, Dark Affliction is devoted above ALL else to developing and encouraging good play and camaraderie in its members.

Dark Affliction guild members seek always to lead by example, and to demonstrate fairness and generosity at all times. Selfish and dishonorable conduct such as intentional training, kill stealing, harassment, ninja looting, or deceitful trading will not be tolerated.
Roleplaying is encouraged but not required.

I. CODE OF CONDUCT
Dark Affliction is a guild that strives to set a high standard of honorable behavior. Be aware that you are a representative of our guild and that your actions reflect upon us all. Guild members should seek to treat others with courtesy and respect at all times. Discordant alignment and honorable behavior are not in direct opposition to each other. We expect everyone to behave within the guidelines set below.

Dark Affliction deems the following as unacceptable conduct:
a) Use of profanity in a public chat mode. (This includes Guild Chat, Say, Shout, Auction, and emotes).

b) Harassment of another player, either verbally or in any other way attempting to continually disrupt his game play.

c) Begging for cash, items or "power leveling". (This includes Guild Chat, Say, Shout, Auction, and emotes.)

d) (Public) Harassment of other guilds. This includes Say, Shout, Auction, and emotes.

e) Kill Stealing. Intentionally trying to kill npc's that are already engaged in combat with another player unless that player asks for your assistance.

f) Intentional Training. Intentionally training mobs on other players or groups, even if they are of light races, is not acceptable.

g) "Ninja" Looting. Do not loot bodies of fallen monsters unless (1) you were ORIGINALLY awarded credit for the kill by the game (meaning, you were able to loot the body immediately), or (2) the kill has been CLEARLY abandoned. Also, know and abide by your group's loot arrangement (if you do not agree with it, LEAVE). This includes looting while fighting, loot order, and especially the handling of "named"/rare drops.

Note: Any guild member deemed guilty of an offense will receive a warning. Members are allowed two warnings. Additional offenses will be grounds for expulsion from the guild.

II. RECRUITING
We are an Evil Guild, as such we are only recruiting people of an evil alignment. Characters of good alignments will never be allowed in the guild.

All NEW members must meet the following minimal requirements:
a) Must be at least level 30.

b) Must be of evil alignment.

c) Must know Tier'dal at the level of excellent (90) or higher.

d) May not have alts in other guilds.

All new members must post on the message board "The Overthere" and announce their intentions to join the guild. This allows us as members to review the incoming membership prior to them being invited into the guild. All memberships applications will be posted at least one week prior to the new member being recruited, this should allow enough time for members to review the applications. Guild members will then post their comments in the "Applications" section of the board and after at least a weeks time, the officership will decide.

Note: Members who have alts and who wish them to join Dark Affliction will have the level limit and language limit temporarily waived, but they will be expected to meet the minimum requirements as soon as possible.

III. GUILD MEETINGS
Guild meeting will be held on the first Saturday of every other month (Febuary, April, June, August, October and December). Exact time and location will be announced two weeks prior to the date of the meeting.


IV. LOOT
The following rules are in place to assist in the distribution of named/rare drops on guild raids.

a) Item drops Item drops will go first and foremost to people who are present at the time of the raid/loot roll. Officers or the leader of raid will decide what classes should roll for said item just because its all/all doesn't mean everyone should get to roll.

b) Rolling - once the classes are decided, then who should roll? Anyone who hasn't been deemed have won their fair share (Almost always one item per raid unless they are the only bard and bard only armor drops) Until everyone else of their class has something before rolling again. Officers (or the leader of raid) will keep track of who won items and who didn't on raids to try to spread out as best as can with a random system as so everyone has a shot at nice stuff, not "this has to go to main tank" and the like.

c) Extra Items - Items that are Unwanted by the people on the raid will be collected by the officers for later distribution among the guild.

d) Epics - Epics are totally different everyone should know who gets what before raid begins regarding epic pieces. Whoever closer and 50+ of course gets.

e) Tracking - Keeping track of loot is a must after raid so other raiders will know who has gotten what items. All loots will be posted by the raid leader or the officer in charge as soon as possible.
