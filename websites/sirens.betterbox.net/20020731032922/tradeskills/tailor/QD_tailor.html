QUICK AND DIRTY TAILORING GUIDE TO 200

Note: This is not a full guide on tailoring, this is how I mastered the art. To get a full guide, see the many sites on the net. I performed this trade skill in Freeport. All items can be found in Freeport, though you have to go to monster areas for other items (i.e. commonlands for pelts, East Karana for silks). To learn tailoring, a couple of items need to be addressed:
  1. Charisma plays a big part in the buying/selling...you will make more profit with high charisma.
  2. The closer you are to the "trivial" level, the better chance of it leveling (and succeeding).
  3. Contrary to some posts, you can increase level even if you fail, though it is less likely.
  4. On all recipes below, only use one of each. Use the CTRL-left click to only pick up one of an item.
  5. You use a sewing kit to make the items, left click on it. Then place items into the sewing kit and hit Combine.
  6. You used to be able to trivial on the lowest item (mask), but that is not the case now. I don't know the exact trivial numbers for each level, but it usually goes up by two on each item till tunic will trivial. You can just do masks and skip to next level for a more expensive route if you like.
Preparation
I prepared by going to Freeport and putting everything in my bags in the bank (with the exception of the food/water). Because of weight, I used 6 large bags and the sewing kit and left a space free for the containers, though you can use backpacks (they are three times the weight though).

Mastering Tailoring

Note: Since you have to find some materials off of monsters, you may do different routes depending on the items you can find. Also, some pelts you find may be of higher quality that you need. Use a skinning knife to reduce its quality (or, if you can, save them till you need them later [much preferred]. Also, you may need other items from other skills (smithing, brewing, enchanting). Either improve the skill yourself (if you can) or get a friend to help (or buy the items from PCs).

Location of Ingredients

I am referencing Freeport, because everything was pretty close. Numbers in parentheses refer to eqatlas map (Thanks eqatlas!). Other places may have the items you need; this is where I found them.
Hint: If you can bind/gate, I placed bind in North Freeport so I could return when needed.

Taking Tailoring past Mastery
The next stage is looking at moving from the 100s to 199 (and possibly beyond). The reason I stopped at 199 was that I did not want to grand master in Tailoring (yet). Note that if you take a skill to 201, you can't take others past 200.
Note: I was looking for the fastest way to level without doing too many steps and the least number of different components.
