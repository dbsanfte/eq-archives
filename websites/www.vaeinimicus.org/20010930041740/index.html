09/14/2001
. . . . .

Norrath truly is a magical place when you stop to think about it. It's a land where we ignore race and religion for the most part, for the sake of the greater good. Sure, I'm a tree-hugging wood elf that worships the Mother of All, but I fight side by side every day with lovers of darkness that worship the Prince of Hate. (Hell, I've even been seen congregating with Halflings on rare occasions). We've overcome superficial differences to recognize that if there is an enemy out there, it is one we all share in common. Fighting amongst ourselves will prove only to lessen our comrades when the time comes to battle a true adversary. We don't agree on what forces govern the universe, and our skin is different colors and textures, but for the sake of survival and above all else, for the sake of progress, we overlook these things. Who would've ever thought the real world could take a lesson from a video game centered around slaughter?
I apologize for the lack of updates over the past few days. For most people, life goes on. I don't have the usual meticulous list of what's dropped since the last update, since my mind has been elsewhere. I also apologize to my guildmates, who haven't heard much more than a meep out of me in the last week or so. All we have is time, and every so often I need to stop and look at how I'm using mine. I don't intend to dwell on this any more than I have, since this is a place to forget about the real world and enjoy the land of make believe. So without further ado. . .

09/03/2001
Temple of Veeshan Cleared


We hit Temple of Veeshan North again this week, and three nights and very few deaths later, Vyemm and Vulak are dead. This is the first time we've killed them, and only the second time they've been killed on Tunare, so seeing their corpses damn near brought a tear to my eye. I was impressed enough when we killed Aaryonar flawlessly the day before, but I have to say that a flawless Vulak slaying with only 48 people made me nearly forget about Aaryonar. The entire fight (which lasted about 24 minutes) I sat there, waiting to see the corpses pile up. Instead, the only corpse I saw belonged to Vulak'Aerr. Vyemm with 47 (ok, a few deaths!) is something we're pretty damn proud of as well, especially on our first slaying. I've never been so happy to be a part of this group of players. I'd like to put Khrolan and Morrison's names in lights for the work they did these past few days, but since I don't have access to that sort of technology, they'll have to settle for this honorable mention! Seriously though, Vyemm and Vulak were the most exhilirating fights I've experienced in the last two and a half years of playing this game. I was afraid once they were dead, people would just say "what now?".... but instead, people seem to be saying "let's do it again!!!" (thanks to Morrison, Durn and Tolar for some of these images) :)
