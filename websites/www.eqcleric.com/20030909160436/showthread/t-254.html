Cerrick
Recently a post was made on the forums on the Druids Grove website. The intent of the post was to give some insight to the changes in the harmony spells. Inadvertantly he gave some insight to the line of Lull spells for clerics, bards, and chanters. Here is the quote reguarding these spells.

quote:
Harmony is the druid and ranger version of the lull line of spells. Several classes get these types of spells, with enchanters, bards, and clerics being the best at it overall. Lulls work by lowering the range at which monsters will attack you, allowing you to pull a single monster out of a group by lulling it's companions.

Traditionally, most lull spells haven't really worked very well. Lull had a high resist rate and wasn't very reliable. We've recently looked at lulls and improved the way they work to make them more useful to players. You should find that the lull line of spells works more often than it did, though it will still fail some of the time.


Basically, they are possibly makeing useful a line of spells that in my experience was never worth the risk of tryin to use.

With some play testing, I will have more to comment on this "change". Hoping that we are now able to help make safer pulls from areas where only FD and the original harmony spells worked before.

Keep in mind, this line of spells checks against your charisma for success checks.

-Cerrick

Well, Finally the spells work actually, though since I first posted this they have been continually messing with them. I bought a donals bracer (clicky: pacify) and that worked for a long time to calm down the maniac mobs in PoI so that I could single pull them. It worked on nearly everything (with Archaeic Models and a few random others being too high leveled). Then they changed the spells.. they went from 40 mins to like 7 in duration, but in the process they took a spell that worked fairly reliably and made all the creatures in PoI too powerful for it to land on. Then I try wake of tranquility.. same results (seems the potential effected mobs are the same this is just AE pacify).

Also, to amend my prior post, these spells do not check against your cha for success. Rather they stick or fail as per normal resist rules but, CHA plays a role upon a resist. A CHA check is made on a resist to see if a mob will aggro from the casting .. or simply ignore you and pretent you did nothing to it.

Unfortunately, I am not 65 yet and dont' have pacification so my experiment is off for now.
