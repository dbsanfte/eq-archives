Preface

The purpose of this article is to focus on the mechanics of EQ as they relate to clerics. It's intended as a more in-depth and more technical discussion than the more general cleric guides, and it's aimed at more experienced players.

Mana Regeneration

Your mana regeneration rate is calculated as follows:-

(1) Standing mana regen.

All clerics regain 1 point of mana per tick naturally. The only exception is level 65 clerics, who have an innate bonus of +2 mana per tick, so a level 65 cleric gets 3 mana per tick just standing up.

(2) Sitting bonus.

You get +1 mana per tick for sitting. If you are level 8 and over, then you get a further bonus from your Meditate skill.

(3) Meditate skill.

You regain 1/10 of your Meditate skill per tick from meditating, provided that you are sitting down or riding a horse. So if your Meditate skill is at 252, you get +25 mana per tick from meditating.

(4) Flowing thought/Mental Clarity

+1 mana per tick per point of Flowing Thought; +1 mana per tick per point of Mental Clarity. The largest amount of Flowing Thought you can have is 15, but Mental Clarity will enable you to exceed this cap, so a cleric with FT15 and MC3 would be on +18 mana per tick.

(5) Buffs.

There are four cleric buffs which increase your mana regeneration rate, and they are all self-only. They are divided into two lines. The first line is Blessed Armor of the Risen (level 58 spell, +6 mana per tick) and Armor of the Zealot (level 65 spell, +8 mana per tick), which do not stack with each other, and the second line is Yaulp V (level 56 spell, +10 mana per tick) and Yaulp VI (level 65 spell, +12 mana per tick). The Yaulp line is only useable in situations where you cannot Meditate.

Additionally, of course, there are enchanter, druid, and beastlord buffs, plus bard songs, all of which can increase your mana regeneration rate.

Mana Preservation

There are three ways of reducing the mana cost of your spells, which are as follows:-

(1) Specialize skill. As a cleric, you should specialize in Alteration. At Specialise: Alteration (200), the mana cost of all of your Alteration spells is reduced by 11%. At Specialize: Abjuration (50), the mana cost of all your Abjuration spells is reduced by 3%.

(2) Spellcasting Mastery. You should maximize this AA as soon as you can. At Spellcasting Mastery 3, the mana cost of all your spells is reduced by 10%.

(3) Mana Preservation items. These do vary, and the effect they have is random. If you have a Mana Preservation IV item (e.g. Ornate boots) then the mana cost of all your spells is reduced by a random 1% to 15%; the mean mana saving is 8% of each spell. There are other focus effects which reduce the mana cost of all spells (or sometimes particular categories of spells), and can have an even greater effect.

Mana preservation items do NOT stack with one another, and do NOT affect CH.

Armor Class

There are three factors which affect your AC. (Note: I do not believe that your dodge skill affects AC; as far as I am aware it is checked separately. Melee classes have a higher dodge skill cap and they also have parry - or block for monks - and riposte, so they take less damage than clerics from monster attacks as a result of their skills.)

(1) Your AGI. AGI has a very small effect on your AC (it's about 1ac per 9 points of AGI), but you take a serious AC penalty from having AGI below 75.

(2) Your Defense skill. You gain 2.5 points of AC per point of Defense, so when you get to Defense (200), you have a +500 bonus to your AC.

(3) Your gear. Equipment is subject to a 1.56 point AC multiplier, so a 50AC breastplate really adds (50*1.56=) 78ac to your character.

How AC works

AC has two effects. The first is that it can cause a monster to miss you entirely. The second is that it reduces the amount of damage which a monster can do.

Monster damage is calculated according to the following formula:

Damage = DB+ n*DI.

DB (damage bonus) varies from monster to monster, but there has been some excellent work on the warrior boards at http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forums to ascertain it. You can see a detailed discussion in this thread: http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/foru...?s=&threadid=13

"n" is an integer between 1 and 20 inclusive.

DI (damage interval) also varies from monster to monster and detailed discussions can be found at the links above.

The effect of AC is to reduce the value of "n" in the calculation above.

Please note that the value of AC over about 1200 for clerics has been called into question by a fair number of parses. According to the best information I have available, above this threshold a difference of +500ac reduces the total amount of damage taken by ~3%.

If you want to take less damage when under attack, you should definitely invest in the Combat Agility and Lightning Reflexes AA's.

Shadowfrost
The 1.56 multiplier isn't level-specific, no. A level 1 cleric equipping a 20AC breastplate would actually gain about 31ac; a level 65 cleric would gain the same amount of AC for equipping the same item.

If you had Defence (200), you would have 500 natural AC plus a small bonus for your Agility... call it roughly 520 AC. If you then equipped a full suit of armour with a total of 449 raw AC, then your displayed AC would go up by 700, to about 1220AC in total.

I wouldn't worry about getting to 1200 AC unbuffed, though; you're a cleric, so your AC will always be buffed. There's no need to aim so high in terms of raw AC - although if you raid, you'll probably get to considerably more than 1200 unbuffed AC entirely by accident.

Shadowfrost
Yaulp doesn't work if you're sitting on a horse or lizard. (It used to when Yaulp V was first implemented, but Sony stopped that happening as soon as they realized.)

Basically, you can only use Yaulp in a situation where you aren't medding. It's useful in indoor zones while you're casting - e.g. in a CH ring, or pacifying a room full of monsters - or while you're moving around, but medding is better.

Yes, it is very definitely "worth messing with." Mana regen is mana regen! Every time I stand up in an indoor zone, the first thing I do is to Yaulp.
