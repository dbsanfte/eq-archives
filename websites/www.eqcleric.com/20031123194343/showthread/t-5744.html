Cancel Magic removes a random buff. This might be the DS you want to get rid off, this might be the Slow or Tash or other buff or debuff the mob has.

With MoK you always remove just the DS, no matter how many buffs the mob has.

For me, it's cheaper to use MoK when I see that the mob has a DS rather than having Cancel Magic cast as the first spell on the mob, because if the spell fizzles, or is resisted, or does not remove the DS (the mob could have many buffs), you have to cast it again, in which case it could fizzle, resist or not remove the DS again.

Meanwhile you have the chanter and/or shammy waiting on you before they can tash/slow and everyone hitting the mob are taking damage and you end up having to heal a lot more than the mana "saved" by using Cancel Magic (30 mana) rather than MoK (75 mana).

Annul Magic costs 75 mana, the same as MoK, so you're not saving any mana using AM over MoK, and if AM does not remove the DS on first cast, you're actually wasting mana.
