Qyadar
It is my understanding that when a detrimental effect is cast on you it adds a set amount of counters to your character. Say if you were slowed by shammy mob with Turgor's insects, your attack speed is lowered by 75% and 18 disease counters are added. The slow has a set duration and you can wait it out or you can attempt to cure it. IF you cast counteract disease it will remove 8 disease counters per cast hence taking 2 casts to remove the slow.

Now lets say your group is hit with an AE dot that added 15 counters of disease and 9 counters of curse. If you had RC 1 it would only remove 9 of those disease and all of the curse counters but leaving the dot on you and your group but if you had RC 3 it would successfully remove all of the counters and also eliminating the dot from your party.

Now this is pure speculation but since some curses add a ridiculus amount of counters, I beleive RC has a chance to remove them all on a successful cast. This is represented by the detrimental counter. One such curse that comes to mind is Gravel Rain which is a snare that last for 30 minutes and adds a whopping 72 curse counters. Even RC 3 doesnt even come close to removing that many counters but it does remove 6 detrimentals and possibly having a chance of removing it in whole. If you look at the spell data for purify soul, it removes many counters including 54 detrimentals which would rid you of any ailment as the description says.

So imo it is justified to max RC out to give you a better chance of curing whatever afflicts your group atm. Although I think PS should be a group cast also like RC hence giving us that one up on the other priest classes to signify our ablity to heal and cure but what can you do. Once again this is my understanding of how cures and curses work and I could and often am wrong. If someone knows for sure I would be interested in knowing because knowledge is power :D
