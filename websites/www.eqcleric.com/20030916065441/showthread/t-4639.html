This is the kind of attitude from druids I just don't get. What does it mean to be "necessary" in a group? No class is. Tanks can be Warrior, Paladin, or SKs; healers can be cleric, druid, or shaman. Slowers can be Shaman, Beastlord or enchanter, Crowd Control can be done by Enchanter, Bard. and improvised by others, Damage Dealers come in all varities, Pulling can be done by several classes, Evacs can be done by wizards or druids, etc. In general, no class is necessary...just types are necessary. It's the way EQ should be. There needs to be interchangeability.

The basic truth is that in EQ only four real things are needed for a good group in a basic zone: tank, healer, slower/debuffer [and/or (depending on zones) crowd control], and damage dealing. Druids can fill at least two of these roles. Sure druids dont' heal as well as clerics, but they make a decent healer in exp groups and they contribute other things to the group such as a worthwhile DoT, DD, snare, ds, or charm. Alternatively, if a cleric is present they make fine back up healers who can do a fair amount of sustained dps while providing nifty things like damage shields and snare. Moreover, they are one of two classes with the evac line which is quite necessary in dungeons. No they don't nuke like a wizard, but then again the wizard can't throw in a heal if its needed. So, druids aren't the best at anything per se, but few classes can do as many things for a group as a druid. It is this utility, this being a good healer and a good nuker but not that best at either, that makes druids attractive to play and one of the few classes that can effectively solo. And maybe it does limit your attrativeness when folks want a "specialist," but it doesn't mean you don't bring anything important to the table. It just means that other players aren't aware of what that is.

It strikes me that the druids greatest problem is bad press. Bad druids tend to set the reptuation for the rest. Morover, the complaints of the druid community on Druid's Grove tend to get heard widely and I think they embitter a lot of folks who feel druids are much more balanced than many other classes. Moreover, the sheer quantity of druids out there is staggering at times. I think the market is flooded with them, causing some to continue to sit on the shelf when the want groups.

But most of us who have been around a while know a few druids and are greatful for the times they've saved our hide. I'd rather not count the number of times I've been saved by a timely evac, heal, or nuke from a druid.
