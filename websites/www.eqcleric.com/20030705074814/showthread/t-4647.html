It will be impossible to keep everyone topped off on health during quite a few levels unless you take breaks from pulling (which most groups never have to now adays.)

You can keep up also by using any heal over time spells you get they can keep up quite nicely.

Illi
Clerics don't really become the main healers until level 24. Up to that point, Druids have the same heals that Clerics have. At 24, Clerics get Greater Healing, which heals up to three times as much as a Druid can at that level. Then at 34, you get another good healing spell which can heal 1.5 times as much as a Druid can. Then at 39 comes THE Cleric healing spell -- Complete Heal. This is what separates a Cleric from the other priest classes (at least until THEY get their nearly-complete-heals at 58).

On the other hand, however, in PoP, it all becomes a different story. The creatures hit so hard and so fast that you often have to start healing the tank at around 75% (depending on how well the creature is slowed, if at all) in order to keep him alive. If the creature isn't slowed, there is a chance that the tank may die in the ten seconds it takes to cast the heal.

Additionally, on raids, when you're in a CH chain, there may be the opposite problem. Depending on how precise anf fast the chain timing is, you can sometimes spend 400 mana to heal the tank only 300 points because either the chain timing is too fast or the creature isn't hitting as hard or something. When this happens, the tank's health doesn't drop enough after the previoius Cleric's heal to make your cast mana efficient. (I had this happen to me last night -- it was irritating to wasste so much mana on so little healing, even though I was keeping at exactly the specified interval.)

So, cheer up. Although it looks like you're not doing enough healing now, things will change as you advance. Just keep at it and don't give up.

Bakanar
In Planes of Power in experience groups, your main heal is going to be Complete Heal, which you get at level 39. From 39 on, your main heal is always going to be Complete Heal because it is the most efficient way to heal someone. A complete heal is not really a complete heal if your tank has enough hp's, which wont happen unless your in one of the most top guilds on your server. A normal complete heal does about 7000-7500 healing. You can get AA abilities that can increase that number to 15000+. So, a complete heal really isn't a Complete Heal.

When experiencing in Plane of Power zones, you will also need fast heals to heal the casters in your group, such as the enchanter or magician because they are more frail than the tanking class. Your fast healing spells will cost almost as much or more than your complete heal spell, but still use the fast heals because that is what saves their lives, don't try to Complete Heal every person in the group, because it simply won't work.

At lower levels, the same old rule applies. Heal on three bubbles. That's how I've always done it, and how I've heard other people do it. I did this when 40% was less on a character, so should be an even better rule now. I've never had Celestial Renewal to work with though.

At 39, CH was not a spell I used. Consider how much you need to heal to make it useful. I forget the math, but I think you needed to heal at least 1800 HP to make a CH worthwhile. Ask your tank their HP, and if they have less than 3k, probably not in your best interest...start complete heals on 2 bubbles. If you are in the planes, start at half initially and adjust.
