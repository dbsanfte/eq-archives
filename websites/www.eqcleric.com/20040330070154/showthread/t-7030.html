Lucretia
I've only had one night in the new expansion, and only fought mobs in 4-5 zones (only one seemed unique of what I saw), but there are definite differences between these mobs and old mobs.

For one the mobs seem to hit with posion often. They also proc or cast (couldn't tell in our brief encounter with a few mobs) dots that hit for a lot of damage. So far I've been casting heals early to compensate for the dot damage and then casting Antidote on the group member. My spell graphics weren't working in the expansion although I could see particle effects on weapons, so I had to watch text very closely to get everyone who was getting hit with dots. Group cures are going to be very popular this expansion.

Another thing I noticed is that when a mob gets to about 20% they get an ability (I'm assuming) that says something about a "dark aura". When this goes off melee damage goes down to almost nothing (<20dmg) on both slashing and blunt (didn't try piercing). Almost all mobs have this ability it seems. Blasting them to death at 20% may overcome this. Spells seemed to hit fine.

Mobs hit harder for their con than in previous expansions. Sometimes the damage isn't always higher per each hit, but they all seem to either quad more or have more haste. Trash mobs in Ferubi hit for upwards to 1000hp per swing though and still cause poison dot damage.

We only usually killed mobs at the zone ins as we went from zone to zone. There weren't many different models (they were neat though even if duplicated), so I'm not sure how much variance there is. Anyone have other observations?

Knowing we were trying this guy, I cast Divine Intervention which went off, and then I blew Div Arb, and finally I lost him. He nearly got one rounded at about 8k hp. Despite that, he lived til the mob was at 25%, and ultimately we got him down to 10.

Wish we would have gotten a second try, but the real point in there was the chain-SL. I think Verant is trying real hard to kill complete healing. I simply did not have time to cast CH in that fight. There were fights with normal trash where I couldn't use it, at least not until the mob was slowed -- healing from 100% as the mob reached camp wasn't good enough. Even beyond that though, was the damage SPIKES. Our ranger was taking a pretty consistent beating, but every once in a while he'd take a really bad round; I don't know if this was a proc of just bad luck, but these mobs have the chance to do a lot very suddenly, and you need a spell that can react in under 10 seconds.

I suggest you all get yourselves a spell haste item and not forget about BoR like I used to. They may not affect SR or CH, but SR doesn't heal enough and CH takes too long.

The thing I have noticed most so far in concerns with mob DPS is how irregular it is. These mobs must have very low ATK compared to PoP mobs becasue ive seen a single round take anywhere from 2% to 30% off a tank. IMO its the irregularity that is makeing CH so hard to cast. As a cleric we have learned to time our CH to hit before the tank dies but after enough damage has been done. However with low ATK high max hit mobs DPS is almost impossible to guage.

I started this expansion CH around 80%, sometimes the CH would hit when the tank was 40-50% sometimes it would hit when they were at 75%. I've actually lowered my CH time down to around 60-50% now (much like elemental healing), but I try and start it right after a bad round (AKA the tank goes from 80-60 in one round). That way the tank has the HPs to take 1 more bad round and still live for the CH.

As for named killing, killed one with a 10K+ tank (Def /on) yesterday. Started CH as the mob was inc (grats SK death on pull), which gave me a 6 second CH instead of a 10. Hit that CH around 20%. Chain lighted a cpl times. Once slowed the thing was an absolute puss, musta been hasted or something casue after slow I could CH the tank at 50% and still get it off. However the thing that struck me the most about the named was consistent DPS. Seems the names have much higher ATK so once slowed they are an easier guage then the common mob.
