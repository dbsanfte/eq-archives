Hannorel
Take a break from the character or take a break from EQ for a little bit. I've done this several times before when I was getting burnt out or frustrated.

I'm in a family type guild, as well. Between us and our ally guilds, we have guild events ("raids") several times throughout the week. One thing everyone in the guild realizes and supports is that RL > EQ. I'm the second highest cleric in my guild at 52, and currently the highest active one. Do I feel pressured to be at every raid/hunt we do? Heck, no.

It's a shame your guild is so selfish as to lay a guilt trip on you for making or breaking their raids. Maybe some of them should level up a cleric to give you a break or ensure there's more around?

Another possibility is to keep your eyes open for a guild that will appreciate the time you can and will devote to the raids. If you're like me, leaving a guild is no small matter; it's hard to avoid feeling like you may have betrayed them by moving on. Ultimately, though, if they are reducing your enjoyment of the game, it may be time to move on.

Of these all, I would try taking a break from your cleric or even a short break from the game first. It will help you build interest in the game again, and perhaps finally make it sink into your guildies' heads that they are being unreasonable in their atttiudes and expectations towards you.

Dyschord
Being a cleric in any guild can be hard, and at times a stressful burden. The class itself has a rather high burn-out rate, and alot of guilds have a high cleric turnover.

Talk to your guildleader, let them know how you feel, and discuss the possibilty of having someone level up a cleric for raiding, or even recruiting more clerics. Don't let it fester, because you'll only end up resenting the dependancy that gets attached to our class.

Most importantly, just let your guild know that you need some "you" time. If they are a family/friends type guild, then they should understand, and be behind you on that ;)

I believe everyone's pretty much hit the nail on the head. Here are my suggestions.

1) Make an alt and tell your guild you're taking a mini EQ vacation and that you'll be back in 5 days. Being guildless and little is refreshing, esp. if you go to another server (I personally like Firiona Vie).
