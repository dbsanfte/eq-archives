Hi there. First I wanna say hello to everyone-this is my first post here. I too recently started a new cleric on a 2nd account, after hunting with my Ranger for 3+ years.
Just as you, I did NOT want to spend the next few months getting her high enough to group with my ranger. So this is the technique that has worked wonderfuly for me:

Get your cleric up to about lvl 12 with DS and regen. Should take all of 2 hours, tops.
The, find yourself an enchanter who is similar level to you (close enough to group with) and who is at least level 12 (which is when they get this wonderful spell called memory-blurr). Memory blurr is much like the cleric spell Atone - it erases the mob's damage history. So the friend who is PL'ing you (the druid, or shaman) beats the mob to near death, then roots mob and backs up. The enchanter (who is grouped with your cleric) then memory blurrs it (I generally cast it twice, because it doesn't always work the first time). Finally, you nuke or melee the last few HP to get full experience for the kill. Works phenominaly well.

So far, my leveling zones have been as follows:
1-6: Any noob zone
7-12: Bandit camps in North Ro
13-24: Paludal Caverns in Luclin
25-28: Giant fort in Frontier Mountains
29 - 31: Kaesora. First near the library (eastern part of zone) and then near the "church". This zone seems good enough to last you through 34. At 34, you get atone, and can now use this technique without having to solicit the help of an enchanter. From then on, go to your favorit 40+ dungeon and enjoy :)

I've made buddies with most of the young enchanters on my server. I just do a /w all enchanter level-range. Send a /tell with a brief explanation of what I'd like to do, and most of them agree to give it a try. Once they do it, they're hooked Lol.
The experience just flies, because you're getting red kills every minute or so. Faster with my ranger pulling and killing, but I expect it will be pretty good with your druid or shaman friend helping too. One important thing to remember: Don't lay on really heavy dots, because even after the mob's memory is blurred, the dot is still killing it, so you may not be able to do the majority of the remaining damage (which is what you have to do to get the kill) before it dies. To be honest, this technique works best with a ranger PL'ing for several reasons: A ranger has a tremendous range of utility spells to make getting into and out of areas easier. Also, the ranger puts out great melee damage, and can easily take hits from greenies. Finally, and prob most importantly, snare. Once mob is beaten sufficiently enough, and yo snare it, it'll turn and just stand there, waiting to die. Better than root beacuse you don't have to worry about it breaking unexpectedly, or gaining proximity aggro.

Oh, one other thing: With the above method, you will gain experience frighteninly fast (1 - 30 in about one day played so far). Obviously, it is very easy to leave your other skills far behind when doing this. Make sure to take some time to work up your casting and other skills.

Ok, hope all of the above made sense, and gl with your new cleric!

restandre
How did you play your druid? If you were a soloist and haven't done much group healing, I would not recommend PLing; you'll need that experience.

Different classes can PL well in different ways. Enchanter can C3 and haste you, and mem blur the mobs and is usually your best option. Monks can beat the mob to nothing and then FD. A druid can DS and HP buff you, and then heal you and finish the mob. Killing undead with a necro partner who can take the monk approach and also funnel you some mana, provided they're careful not to dot heavily.
If you want to PL a wizard, DEFINITELY get an enchanter. Have the enchanter pull anything and everything, and AE stun. Then use your PBAoEs and kill them all at once. You *could* do this with a cleric, but I wouldn't recommend it and you'd probably want to pull mobs 4 at a time.
