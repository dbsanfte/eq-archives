Don't let the people above ya scare you. Clerics are damn easy to play, or at least not any harder than all other clases in EQ are.

You buff, sit and heal, refresh buffs when they drop, heal, stun to interrupt casters. Some crowd control sometimes with root. This isn't rocket science.

The main things you should know about clerics are:
1) their life is easier than most since they're always wanted for groups and therefore have no trouble getting them
2) most people are nice to them because a) they will eventually want a rez and b) a group will ditch most anyone else before they ditch their cleric
3) they're rewarding to play in their mid levels, 34-54 or so
4) they're boring to play at high levels, 55-60

There's one cleric's opinion ;)

Filadan
Just because every group wants a cleric (which is not even true) does not mean that there will be a group for every cleric out there. Sorry, but melee have it much better off when grouping. You don't say "Sorry, I already have a melee in my group, I want something else". There is no reason to have multiple clerics in a group, since if you want additional healing, a cleric or shaman adds much more to the group than a second cleric.

As far as wanting to pull with magic, be able to melee, and heal, go for a paladin. Clerics are not a melee class, and their skills are to help them survive long enough to get help. Clerics were never meant to deal melee damage (Verant just pulled the hammers out of their asses because they no longer have any integrity).

Anyway, any class can melee up to a certain level, like 10-12 (or higher, depending on gear). After that, you abandon melee. Sure, you CAN get up and whack things if you want, but it is pretty much a waste of time, and groups tend not to appreciate it. If you want to melee as a means of contributing to the group, then you need to pick a melee character, not a priest or caster.
