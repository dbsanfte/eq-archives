Wow, this is the very attitude I'm afraid will break many clerics. It's hard enough getting groups as it is, and now they are diminishing the one aspect that has always ensured some exp and groups in favor of soloing capabilites in the upper part of the game.

It's been long understood that Verant is changing the game so that players can more easily reach the upper end of the game where the REAL game starts. We've all seen the changes sub-lvl 10, no hells levels, and soon no post-50 hell levels. But these changes send a message, and many clerics don't like it.

I for one hope they ditch the new druid and shammy heals. (Hey, all the players in both classes that I've talked to recent the changes coming, feeling they already do plenty in the game...) And I hope they let us keep the new soloing capabilities. Just makes it worthwhile reaching the 50's and having a chance at some experience when you're tired of bouncing between Seb and Velks LFG'ing. :)

Dramadon
Well, I certainly don't speak for everyone else, but I'll take a cleric over a druid or shaman for healing any day of the weak. Sure, they may be able to heal effectively, but having the insurance of a rez is very important to me. I tend to like to do risky things provided the group has the ability to do it but I also like having the assurance of a rez if something goes wrong.

That being said, if there is no cleric available then I will be happy to have a shaman or druid do it. Heck, that's no different than what I do now. Anyone who uses the reasoning of "well we can get this guy because he can nuke/slow/dot, whatever and the cleric can't" screw them. Let them die down in some dark whole then say it'll cost ya 100 pp for a revive when they come begging you for a rez.

Deian
Actually, I love the new priest cheal. Last night we farmed the kael arena for exp/armor with 4 people (me, 58 shaman, 58 warrior and a 50 monk) and the shaman 2k heal allowed us to do things we couldn't do before.

Anything that we could slow, we'd kill... we pulled the usual assortment of troopers & guards, but we also grabbed a bunch of nameds (mostly lvl 57-58, GREAT exp).

On the higher ones the shaman would usually bounce a few slows before he stuck one, and I'd cheal him and cheal the tank. He didn't have much mana for healing the tank on those high-dmg mobs AND slowing, but with the superior mana regen afforded by the new heal spell - it's a poor man's torpor, practically - he'd be able to slow and even throw in a CH to relieve my mana at times.

We couldn't have done the mobs we did otherwise, and I felt the change they got is a great & welcome one.
