Adaria
My primary is a cleric, and I play her almost exclusively, 'cos I just love the class. :) I'm only level 45 though, so the following advice is probably no good for the high-end game.

I have a few rules I follow religiously when I play, which not everyone agrees with, but which work for me well.

1) CHeal the tank at 50% if the hps are going down fast. Find out what their max hps are (buffed) and work out a reasonable point at which to heal them. Usually 20% is just fine, but depending on gear and buffness, you might need to adapt. (For your own peace of mind, don't wait until lower than 20 percent. It's just not worth the stress if a mob gets in a few good whacks, or you get lagged, or a sudden pop nukes your MA.)

2) Chuck a celestial heal on the nukers as soon as you see that spell go off. They -will- get thumped, and preventative measures go a long way.

2a) If you don't have the mana for a celestial, throw the base-level stun on the critter right after the nuker has hit it. It'll aggro onto you, and you can take the damage a damn sight better. (OK, this only applies if you're a battle cleric. For non-defensive clerics, stick with the heal option) The non-painful alternative is to chain-stun it until the MA gets aggro back. Works fine, and no-one gets hurt.

3) If any person does something stupid that puts them in danger, I'll heal them, and /tell them what went wrong and not to do it again. If they do it a second time, I'll /say the same, so that anyone in 'earshot' can hear me warn them. Third time, I let them die. Without rez. If you've died through your own stupidity after repeated warnings, DO NOT expect me to fix you up good again. If I'm feeling nice I'll do the zero-xp rez back to your corpse for you.

4) If any group I'm in has one of the No.3 people above in it, I'll hang there for the first 2 strikes, then advise them that I'm leaving. I don't bother sticking around for Strike 3. Its bad enough when idiots get themselves killed but when they risk a group, I'm outta there. If the rest of the group lack the pattern recognition skills to see what's coming, then they probably deserve it. (I'm talking specifically about post-30 groups here - people who've been playing long enough to know what constitutes good gameplay and what doesn't.)

I know that these rules might seem harsh, but even though I'm a healer class, I don't see my role as that of Protector of the Dangerously Stupid. My honest opinion is that it's better to let them die a bunch of times until they get their act together and work out where they're going wrong. Pandering to them, compensating for their idiocy, does no-one any favours in the long run.

Been reading the above comments for when to complete heal and I have seen ranges from Tank health from 20% to 50%. My opinion to clericing has 3 objectives. 1) Keep group alive 2) mana management 3) Avoid agro if possible. In regards to the first objective, especially in high level zones, to keep group alive you main concerns are your main tank, slowers mezzers and of course yourself. Others in group are expendable and if they are drawing agro and i have a choice of healing the shammy or the ranger guess who loses. 20% tank health is not an option when fighting tier 2 mobs in pop for healing your tank. Even slowed mobs get bursts of hits and i have seen 6000 + hp tanks drop from 40% to 0 in no time. The method I use in PoV is on arrival i cast supernal elixir on tank as mobs starts its first damage. I have yet to have drawn agro for doing this. This allows we some time that i know 2000-2500 hp are going to tank in the next 24 sec to have slowers target ready incase of his drawing argo and needing a quick remedy. This has also worked with muti mob pulls with chanter being targeted after tank gets his first shot of HOT. Clerics take a lot of flack for deaths that are not their fault. This is a group game no one class can save a group but one bad player in group can surely cause failure. shaman and echanters do misread screens and holler mob slowed. Other class like druids, wizs bards and all other support classes must know their main role is to keep agro on tank and not themselves. , RULE #1 for cleric always watch group HP bar. If you wanna watch TV while playing eq start a wizard. Mana management is also a key, dont start using nukes and wasting mana when you join a group. Learn your groups dynamics untill you see what abilities they have to handle the mobs in the zone you are fighting. If after that you see you have extra time and mana for nuking or even melee assist then do so. Casting nukes and then having to tell group to hold pulls is a waste of time. Remember you main class objective is to keep group alive. Watch the flow of HP losses on your tank this will help ya learn what spells to cast for the lowest mana use for a fight.
This is not foolproof but anytime something is foolproof life creates a better fool.
