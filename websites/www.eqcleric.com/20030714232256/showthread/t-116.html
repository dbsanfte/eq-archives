Cerrick
With the recent additon of Yaulp V, Hammer of Judgement, and Bash.. Clerics 56 on up have recently had to relearn how to be the greatest benefit to their groups. It is distinctly more challenging to add to the damage of the group through melee and spells (Mark of Retribution, Unswerving Hammer, Nukes, DoT's) and still be able to keep your head about you for well timed heals. While we still maintain the ability to play in the back row of the group and heal, few clerics do this... and in my opinion.. why would we.

With the Standing mana regen from yaulp.. your medding full time. May as well be mixin it up if things are controlled and damage to the party is minimal.

On the flip side of the coin, Clerics pre-56 have been sittin on their now flattened legplates since about level 30 (or earlier). Med, Heal, Nuke, Stun.. rebuff. Thats the game for those of us not yet 56. Yes, we can dive in and melee sometimes, and with bash we have and additional attack now.. but what about your mana. So, you have to sit through 6 mobs to med up .. then hop back in for one fight.. then back out.

Here is the point:

The Idea, as I see it, behind everquest is to have fun, level up, and find more exciting places to go with your friends and hunt.

What then is the point of basically forcing a cleric to play one way though the majority of his career (the sit and med cleric) only to mix it up when the villan's in the game are at their strongest, and the need for a healer is at its greatest.

Although I think I catch on pretty quick. I know (because my tank friend yells at me when I mess up melee'in) I am not well schooled in how to contribute to melee.. 2 years of sit and med never left me the time to learn to pound the nasties. Not to mention.. keepin healing first thing on your mind while chasing around a mob to make that crazy hammer proc.

After a month, I think I have things pretty well under control. A few mistakes have enabled me to know when to jump in there.. and at what point to back out of the fray.

Here is what I suggest:

With luclin the boys behing the desk at SOE added bane damage to the lower level summon hammer spells.

While that is nice. Its not enough.. They need revamped.

For example: 44 spell Hammer of Requital: Summon a 7/29 hammer +5 bane (undead ) damage.. No Drop ect.

Even with the bane.. I would never have used this hammer.. 12/29 is decent.. but with no proc.. it aint doin enough damage to bother standing up.

This spell should look like this: Hammer of Requital Summon hammer 9/24 +7 bane proc: bane of the gods (30-75 dam high frequency proc)

Now you have a weapon that is fast.. hits for Ok damage on its own.. but falls in line with our later hammers where the damage we output comes directly from the gods ( the proc in other words) and is more effective VS. undead 16/24.

They must add standing mana regen to the previous Yaulp spells.. if only for clerics. Not to shun the paladins in this case, but I am unaware of how this may balance out for that class. Scale it down perhaps.. instead of 10 per tick make it 6.. something though that allows you to remain in the fray.

If they would choose to do this.. starting at level 24. and Revamp (rather than add more) they would most certainly give younger clerics (24-55) the ability to play their class in the same way as lowbie clerics.. and those of the highest levels.

Just a thought,:p

-Cerrick

Galv
AD&D has influenced my perception of what a cleric is, and to be frank, EQ's version still doesn't hold up. In the AD&D, a cleric got into melee, smacked around the monster a little bit, healed, blessed, and through out the occasional offensive spell. More importantly, the Cleric manhandled undead. We had the ability to "turn undead," which depending on our level, either literally "turned them" (like a fear spell) or destroyed them utterly.

Do EQ clerics have a power that even comes close to comparing to this?

In addition, depending on what diety you worshipped, the cleric would get certain abilities. For example, let's say you worshipped a god that's primary function was healing and curing. Well, guess what? You get a bonus to your heals: maybe +1, or an extra d4, etc. Does EQ have this? No.

Well, what's my suggestion? Make us the bane to the undead for goodness' sake! Don't merely give us some token extra damage versus undead, give us something that is going to make an undead yelp! And, don't just give us a top notch weapon (relatively speaking) at the high end game. The lower level clerics need to weapons more so, as grouping during this time is MUCH more difficult. Remember the competition you had with druids for groups? The playing field needs to be a bit more level at the beginnings of this game.
