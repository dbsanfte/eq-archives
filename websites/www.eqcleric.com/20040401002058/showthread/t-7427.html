"The sky is falling!"...not.

At first glance, if this goes live, it won't be as bad a thing as you think. Most druids and shamen don't want to be main healers, and are most often tapped only if a cleric isn't available. This will simply allow a shaman to fill in (somewhat) for a cleric if they aren't available. Trust me, most people are still going to want a cleric as their main healer.

There's another thing we bring to a group that druids and shamen don't have - rezzes. If things "go South," then you will always have a cleric there to bring back some of that hard-earned XP. This was brought home when I saw someone 2-boxing a cleric and BST, PLing in Paludal. I asked him why he wasn't using a druid, since they have spells that are a little better suited to PLing (IMO). He said that this way if things went bad, he had a rez on-hand.

So, try and look at this in as objective a manner as you can.
