pet classes are great for farming silk and skins too. East Karana is good for silk but it is overfarmed on Brell. Many go there for skins too. I prefer to go to stonebrunt. It is usually empty on my server for some reason. (scratches his head and raises an eyebrow) The cats all drop one or more skin and almost every time it is low, med, or high quality. I have hardly ever seen a ruined pelt there.

If you are going to do your shawl quest I think the first skill you need is for shawl 3 and that is baking. If you want to do it really easily you can buy/make a fillet knife. It comes from smithing which if I remember correctly is trivial at 42 so you should be able to make it with little to no skill. Just go to your guild first and put a training point on it and if you have never done it before it will jump to your level. While there do the same for baking. Then make your knife. In a regular forge add one water flask, one scaler mold, and 3 metal bits. Hit combine and if succesful you will have your knife. Then head to shadowhaven. Entering shadow haven from the bazaar take the stair up to the city and then make a 180 degree turn. Keep heading in that direction and you will pass an oven. Keep going and there will be a halfling woman a bit further on your left side under a leanto. She sells bear, lion, wolf, and scorpion meat. Stick to the furry creature meats because they will give you better skill ups. Remember to pump up you wis or int to its max, which ever is higher. Your skillups depend on that. Buy 10 stacks of what ever meat you want, i think it cost me 1.7 plat per stack of bear meat with a 127 charisma. Now go back to the oven that you passed on the way. Put your knife in the oven and add one meat. Hit combine. With an int/wis of 227 I went through 20 stacks of meat before this was trivial to me and my skill was 142. I went from 0-142 in about an hour. Yes...I forgot to train in baking when I did this so I started at 0.

If you don't have plans on obtaining the prayer shawl in the future at the moment, you can always begin tradeskills that suit the personality of your character. To give you a little example, I first began EQ with a Barbarian Warrior and I could really picture him on a forge hammering heated steel in the cold northlands so I choose smithing for him. However I could not see that path at all for my cleric, with half the strength and not as battle intensive. I looked at the other tradeskills and thought pottery would suit him well as a priest and as a lot of higher end items have effects that would benefit a casting class. I thought that would relate to his personality the best.

If at a later time you'd like to work towards the prayer shawl, you'll have a start on skills and will be able to adapt to picking up and learning new trades easier. That's what happened to me anyways as I didn't plan on making a run for the prayer shawl at first.

One tip regarding tradeskilling that can save you a lot in the long run if you plan on doing a lot of the various tradeskills (for the shawl or your own satisfaction) is the following:

When doing skillups, bear in mind that many materials used in one tradeskill, are items created by another tradeskill.

For example, if you are doing brewing skillups, and plan on doing tailoring skillups eventually, a good thing to be making at a certain point in your brewing skillups might be heady kiolas which will then be used in tailoring to make Wu's fighting armor for skillups. If you are doing smithing skillups and using metal bits, studs, etc. to skillup, those studs can eventually be used in tailoring for skillups with studded leather armor. Later on in smithing you can farm chunks of condensed shadow, make shadow tipped arrowheads out of them, and use the arrowheads later on in fletching if you choose to eventually take up fletching.

These are just three examples of some of the 100's of sub-combines that when used to skillup in one tradeskill, can eventually be used to skillup in another tradeskill. Just something to bear in mind when choosing things to make for skillups, if you plan on doing skillups in another tradeskill further down the road.
