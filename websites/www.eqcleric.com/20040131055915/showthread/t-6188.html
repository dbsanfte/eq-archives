Rauri
I am an officer in a small to medium sized family guild, and I'm seeking some advice. We have recently seen a boost in people interested in joining our guild, which has always been a fairly small but close knit group of players over it's two year existance. Our guild will normally have 20 people on each night, we raid once a week (usually for epics), and the vast majority of our membership is 55+ with half a dozen 65's. Most of us are working adults with limited play time and we have always tried to cater to people who have to put Real Life first.

What rules do your guilds use to determine who gets a guild invite? Do you require potential members to apply at a website, to be sponsored by regular members or by officers, are they approved by vote, etc.? We've tried a few things but haven't found the right idea for our guild yet.

I've love to hear how your guild does this, and any advice is appreciated!

Hannorel
My guild's current recruitment policy is simple (in theory). "All" we require is that three people form the guild sponsor you as a potential member, and you're in. In practicality, it's not as easy as it sounds, as we have, for lack of a better word, attitude requirements. To understand what I mean, feel free to check our guild site.

The three sponsor requirement may be changing early next year. That's one thing we'll be discussing at our guild meeting in January.

Kan
What my guild does is require people to post on our recruitment section in the forums. Next they have to stay active with the guild via the forums and our recruit channel for two weeks. After the two weeks they need to be sponsored by an officer or one of our special delegates named "Super Seekers". Then they get an invite and are put on a probation period. The sponsors are responsible for the recruits behavior and such. We are also a family guild but are very large atm as alot of peeps are wanting to join weekly. This seems to work quite well, as it shows how much they really want to join, and lets us get to know them first to see if they are "incapatible" with the guild's friendly and family outlook on things.

fretlonhor
With our guild you have to post an application in the application section of our forum..
You have to meet requirements, which are pretty steep..
Clerics/enchanters lvl 65 and 20aa
Necros/SK's/Bards lvl 65 and 150aa
Everyone else lvl 65 and 100aa

Once you meet those requirements you app, then become a prospect.. The prospecting period can last anywhere from 2 weeks to 3 months depending on your play time, attendance, and ability..

During this period you are typically subjected to intense ridicule and are berated a lot (this doesn't change once guilded btw).. If you survive the prospecting without ebaying, quitting, or shooting yourself you get an invite..

Once invited you become a "Junior" member.. The period you are a junior member depends basically on when the officers decide to promote you to "senior" member status.. being a junior member basically means you can only bid on loot that no senior members bid on..

Lilcure
We require that you are level 65 and 50aa, and that there be a place open on our roster or your class. We try to keep a pretty close balance on the classes that we have as we are a primarily raiding guild. After that they are posted as a lead on our board and those of us that know them discuss them, and if the guild leader thinks they might fight our recruitment officer interviews them. After that they are invited as a candidate member for a period of a month where they join us in our raid channel and in groups and on raids. After that you get tagged as an initiate member for another month after which we vote on them again.

So all in all about 2 months to get to know them before they are a full member. This has always worked pretty well for us and we generally end up with the kind of people that we'd like to be guilded with :)
