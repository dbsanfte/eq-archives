Mojoejojo
After reading the thread titled What makes a bad cleric, I found myself bothered a bit. Seems so many times, the negative gets more attention. I want to start a thread detailing what people think makes a cleric exceptionally GOOD. I'm cutting and pasteing my reply from the bad thread, because I mostly detailed good cleric stuff.

While my cleric is only level 16, My shaman is 62, in a high end raiding guild. Let me pass some of the things I see that EXCEPTIONAL clerics do.

1. Hot keys are your friend.
A. Set up your hot key for your CH rotation to cast CH twice, with a (very) short pause between. In the event of a fizzle on your first cast, the second cast will catch it, and CH is on the way faster than you can respond to the fizzle message and retap the key, or click with the mouse.
B. You can also hotkey your heal call, so everything gets done at the same time. Wouldnt be a bad idea to include /stand (or is it /sit off) to ensure that you dont forget to stand before you cast. If a big raid, set up CH rotations and call your CH in channel, then call the next person to CH. (example, somthing like "CH for %t. Pause 40 {4 second pause}(more or less depending on how tough the mob is) Cleric x, heal now.) This does 3 things. Calls your CH, Casts your CH, and tells the next cleric to cast CH, all with one key stroke.
C. Have a second and even third hotkey set up to quickly switch to second and third tanks in the event that MT goes down, or to extend rotation pause once slow lands.

2. Use channels, and set up a healer channel. Use a noticable color, so you dont miss important messages. Have all the clerics in the raid (or even druids, pallis, shamans, etc if needed) in that channel. In a 72 person raid, the less message spam in main channels, the better. If the MT wants to hear the CH calls, have him join the healer channel.

3. From a suport healers standpoint Shaman primary), my main job is to keep #1 my cleric alive so he/she can heal the MT, #2 keep myself alive so I can heal the overnukeing wizzies or druids, or the resisted shamans and chanters, etc alive. This only takes effect AFTER my primary class functions are complete. Malo, slow etc. I dont want my cleric to be distracted from their primary job of keeping the MT alive by having to spot heal other people.

4. Clerics MUST be organized, flexible, and able to adjust with the battle. If the MT goes down, they have to instantly target and CH rotation on the secondary tank without missing a tic. If slow is resisted, they need quick CHs on the MT. If the mob is slowed, they need to increase the time between CH's so they dont go OOM before the mob is down.

5. Horsies can be great for mana recovery, but remember, if you just cast CH as the MT drops, you are stuck casting CH for the next 10 seconds. Dont hesitate to click off Trigger so you can duck and get back into the action.

6. Know your enemys ahead of time. Know what mobs you are going after, how much they hit for, and how fast. Its too late when you show up at the gunfight armed with a knife.

I'm sure there is more I'll think of later, but dont want to get too drawn out.

Give me some more ways to be a good cleric. :)

Dyschord
A Good Cleric ~

1. Is "rarely" AFK.

2. Is always aware of what's going on around them Whether it be down-time, mid-battle, mid-chain; a good cleric notices deaths, aggro switches, on-the-fly instructions.

3. Always has macros set up for any conceivable situation. That includes pre-made macros for fellow clerics for on the fly chain additions, MT /target macros, hybrid /target macros, Mob /assist macro (for those that are graphically challenged and cannot look up on all raids), an /assist or macro for sudden tank switching, and a permanent /assist key for fast spot-healing.

4. Always "spots themselves" in a ch chain. (You know, you fizzle, get interrupted, etc).

5. Doesn't make a raid "wait" for buffs.

6. Is always prepared for any situation wich may arise while traveling. That includes an invis item (GVD), possibly potions to be able to invis on the run (Invis is fickle!), as a back up. A Levitate item/potions, shrink item/potions. In general, be as self-sufficeint as possible. Use what is out there!

6a. Has DA/DB, Target DA, and even a DA item, if you can swing it! Just remember to be kind, and let your raid/groupmates know you are attempting to DA/DB so they don't sit there trying to heal you, when they could be healing others.

7. Is able to manage mana in any given situation, and communicates well with those around (People don't have ESP, and tanks can be dense!)

8. Doesn't sit around while CR is going on. Be active. If you aren't ressing, help heal, drag, rebuff.

9. Has the patience of a saint, and the thick skin to match. Deaths get blamed on us. Don't get emotional. If you know you did everything you could, then don't ever let other's anger ruin your day/week/raid!

10. Has the sense to look out for themselves monetarily, without being a greedy whore!

11. Looks out for group/guildmates. If you know you have people waiting for corpses to pop in a GY, especially at the end of the night/playing session, take a minute and wait with them(If you are in no real rush). Seems like a small thing, but people really do appreciate and remember it.

I'm sure I'm missing things, but those are some of my personal guidelines.

Fight the good fight!

Krystanie
I think what makes a good cleric depends a lot on the situation you're in. A good cleric in a grouping situation is one that can:
1. Manage thier mana (and be able to do so without brain buffs). I have found that by doing this on a regular basis in grouping situations you learn quickly to be very efficient, and are much better prepared for that suprise mob that dispells you.
2. When mana permits and everyone is healed, clerics have quite an array of nukes, stuns, roots, and other utility spells at thier disposal. Rather than sitting and waiting, use the mana for something useful when you have time to med it back.
3. Learn your buffs, know which to use on who and when to use them. You do not want everyone in your group to need a full set of buffs at the same time. That will quickly put you oom and cost time.
In raiding situations:
1. Communication, hotkeys and channels are all a part of this. Communicate with others and be very attentive.
2. Mana efficiency is less of a concern when raiding, in my opinion. Your main focus is to keep the tank(s) alive. There should be support, such as rods and mana dumps if needed. This is not to say CH every time the spell refreshes, but you shouldn't be as concerned about it.
3. Pay attention to where you are and what you're fighting. ALWAYS keep your tank in heal range, yet stay out of AE range.

My 2cp :)
