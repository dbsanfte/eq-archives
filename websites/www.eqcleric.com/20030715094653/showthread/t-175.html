Galv
Buffs eat your mana. They always have and they always will. Typically, it's not until you are about to ding that buffs don't seem as mana intensive. Thankfully, however, it gets a little bit more reasonable when group buffs become available (52), but that of course is a long way off.

Remember too, that because your mana pool is going to be naturally larger than that of a hybrid, it of course will take longer to regain it. For example, a Paladin of my level at FM might have only 25-50% of my total mana. Obviously the numbers for the mana aren't exact, but I think you get my point.

In terms of regaining mana more quickly, the best suggestions I can offer is to try to get some form of breeze/clarity. In later levels you'll be able to get some FT gear.

Filadan
There's really no way to know whether or not a mana-calc will be accurate. However, keep in mind that being able to MGB BoA with mana remaining does not mean you have 4k+ mana. To start casting the spell, it requires at least 2k mana (the cost of the spell). MGB takes up to double the spell cost (if you have that much mana remaining). Remember that you have specialization checks in there.

As far as having an endless supply of mana with BAoTR, that is hardly the case. 60 additional mana every minute is not THAT big of a difference, believe me.

Filadan
Also, for the original poster, I forgot to mention. You could have 255wis at 21, and I can tell you that you still would not be pleased with your mana pool. Realize that at lower levels, each point of wisdom does not give as much as it would if you were level 60.

Buffs are mana-intensive by nature, so you will need to get used to conserving your mana, and timing your buffs in a way that makes it easier on you.
