Laleldil
Although this may be too late for the original poster amybe it will help some one else, or at least lead to more discussion.

Basically healing over time vs immediate heals

At 58 I have tried both. Yes you will find most immediate heals a better ratio to mana at the moment of cast and the hp effects. However it seems most are forgetting the recovered mana either during the actual casting, like complete heals long cast, or the lesser aggro of healing over time and the ability to sit. Alot of times you will not be able to sit unmolested particularly if you over healed or the MOB has intelligence and takes out the healer first, like we do. Since healing over time was only 44 Celestial Healing when I was growing up, I do not know the cast times compared to the immediate heal cast times. But I do know KEI will almost replenish the mana used during the time of the heal.

So when I start a healing for a new area or mob, I often cast celestial healing at one bubble of health lost, then sit medding and watch the health bar. Using your heal over time will tell you several things of interest: will the over time heal keep up with the damage the mob is outputting and also how much damage they are doing. You might wonder why I use the heal to tell me the amount of damage being done rather than watch the hits window. I do not have the hits coming into a readable window. ALL melee damage save from spells are in a minimized window, thus stopping alot of battle spam and missed messages. Knowing the tank's HP and your caster's HP you will know which heal to use for immediate heals on them.

Another aspect of is this heal better than that can only be decided at the scene. If a monk's feign fails when pulling and he does live to successfully feign, you may be tempted to immediately and fully heal him. I do not recommend that, you may want the tank to get aggro and not you. you may instead wish to cast the healing over time to the slower (shaman/enchanter) then the monk, then prepare for complete healing of the tank Alot of slowers desire to do their part real soon, something you like and dislike immensely at the same time and they will probably get aggro real quick, so haveing a heal ticking can help a little bit. Then the monkey puller is healing while he is fighting and you are recovering mana for your version of complete heal.

So please think of your heals as situational and not this is my best method. Explore all your options. I was pleasantly surprised my 44 celestial healing was healing for as much as the spiders of VL were putting out, as long as aggro was maintained by a single tank. A couple of tanks claimed to appreciate the idea of being nearly full health all the time. Remember you can always use the immediate heals in a overpull. Again risking aggro, but your proven use of healing over time assures them that you know how much damage might be inflicted in those ten agonizing secs and the ten secs to get the aggro back off you so you can sit.

It is nerve wracking to see the tank go down just before the heal lands. Practice keeping them full hopefully gives you the opportunity to see the MOB's big hit. Believe me a 61 Pally taking a 900 hp hit is unnerving enough. Waiting until he is already 3/5 dead and finding that hit is possible is deadly. Yes that innocent little gnome in the factory can hit that hard. Had I not been trying to keep him FP, the first 900 might have killed us all, instead I knew complete heals were needed at 60 percent HP.
