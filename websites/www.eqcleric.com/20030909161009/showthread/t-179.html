Get a port to GD, then run (East?) to the Eastern Wastes zone. Get the loc of the Ry'gorr fort (invis and lev before hand is a must) and head over there. There is a SERIOUS pathing bug here, like the rest of Velious. You can pull any mob to here, and as long as you stay in the middle of the path past the broken bridge the mob will STOP IN ITS TRACKS, NOT MOVE, OR FIGHT BACK! With Wrath/Smite/wahtever you can hit the 4 Stalag Terrors without wasting a bit of health. Make sure all 4 are DEAD before you go to loot!

Vaanilla
Crystal Caverns, Solusek B and Overthere are fine, but much as others have said: if you spent your previous levels in Lake of Ill Omen...and even if you didn't, you'll get bored in Overthere in a hurry; as for SolB and CC, the people there become rather unbearable once their 15th train hits you. In other words, I wouldn't recommend those places unless you have a very strong group. The most reliable and fun place I have found to fight is in Dawnshroud Peaks.

Whether you're soloing or grouping, it's good exp and easy to get around. If you're grouping, head over to the tribals or the GE (Grieg's End) zone line. If you'd rather hunt zelniaks or lightcrawlers, though, stay near the Netherbian Lair zone. There are usually plenty of people to find for a group considering it seems to be a hot spot, but nobody knows of my secrets! ;)

For soloing, however...(listen closely now) the best thing to do that I have found from about lvl 30-39 or so is hunting the mushrooms spawns. Heading out from the NL zone, take a left and you should see three static mushroom spawns. If you use your Wrath spell to hit one from a distance, it will be a single pull. Meaning, when there's 3 or 4 you can take a single and root/blast/whatever you want. The mushrooms have low HP and root lasts an unusual amount of time upon them. If you grab a pair of Kobold-hide boots, as well, you can use Extended Affliction to your advantage. In any case, that is just one of the spawns. There are a few trap spawns scattered throughout the zone, but it shouldn't be that much of a problem if you have root memmed. If you follow the wall from that first spawn, you will find another group of three (and a fourth one if you get close enough ;) ). Taking a right out of NL zone, you will find two static spawns along the way to the mushroom palace...which is near the GE zone. The mushroom palace, of course, is the best place to find mushrooms to fight and kill. The same rule applies, if you hit one from far enough away, only that one will aggro on you. This is the most dangerous spot, however, since it has the most spawns. In any case, I have had the best experience soloing against the mushrooms of DS(P). Plus, for loot, don't mind the deadly, spotted, or what not mushrooms that drop since they cannot sell...unless you're doing a quest; but do loot the Fungus Covered Mushrooms. They are not stackable but can be sold for about 4pp depending on your charisma in the Bazaar. Well, that's my two cents. Enjoy!

Phixin
ToFS!!! WOOT... My favorite place.

There are several threads here covering Frozenshadow. That place simply rocks.

Lvl 29 - Murder everything on the first floor. Careful of the slick floor. There is a pit trap full of skellies. Lootz: Will get some FS, and some Shadowbone Armplates, and change. The Lesser shades ocassionally drop a sythe worth 11pp.

LvL 34 - Library. Ghosts... Watch out, they nuke, mez and charm. Yep, they are a blast to fight. Keep Magic Resist going. If you can, get a druid to go with you. Let them be the healer and keep you covered in a nice layer of thorns. Learn to tank...woot! Angry Librarian can be a bit tough. 2 decent drops off Angry, and some change. Dont think much else drops there.

LvL 39 - Library still gives EXP. If you have a group of 3 or 4, to up one floor. Kill the armored shadows (not undead). Work your way from entrance. Go right down hall, then left at corner, follow that down and you will see a door across from the next hallway intersection. Go in there, kill the butlers. From that door, to the left is another door. Kill the zombie's in there and make camp. That mirror next to you is your way out. Pull the undead cooks, and watch the exp fly. This floor will get you to lvl 44. I still get exp there at 51 some of the time. Loots there are not bad, with one decent ring that drops from the enraged cook. Lots of FS, Frozen shadow weapon and crystalized shadows, which sell for 1pp each to vender.

Hello, Just signed up and thought I would give a contribution to the soloing cleric (/gasp). I solo quite a bit and though at times I end up on the wrong end of the engagement, I do enjoy it.

I soloed Giant Skeletons in Rathe Mountains at level 24. My main Spells were Dismiss Undead, Celestial Remedy, Healing, Spook the dead, Undead DOT (name escapes me ATM), Sense the Dead, and Flash of Light. I normally would start at the Rathear Lake zone in where there is a hill with Cyclops and Giant Skeletons. Then once this area was clear I would move over to the Skeletons were at a deserted druid ring is. I had to insert “Calm” into my spell line up to help pull singles though I did get adds more then I would of liked. I also would venture over to where the Hill Giants spawned though I had to be on my toes at all times. I would kill any skeleton that conned yellow or below.

My technique would be to melee initially and then FOL the skeleton (or spook) and nuke it as it runs off and as it returns I would not root because FOL was less manna and somewhat more reliable (though sometimes resisted). These skeletons have very low HPs and do not hit very often. At times, I would pull with the Undead DOT and melee longer or for the entire fight. I have 187 wisdom and this method normally only took a bulb and a half to finish one fight.

At 34, I ventured into the Feerrott and camped the tree spec. Expel Undead rocks their world :c). I started my camp at the top of the hill directly facing the Spec (I wanted him to quiver in my presence) Cast the Undead DOT and run backwards until my spell gems would refresh then hit her with my best Undead Nuke. I am taking damage now and it is easy to lose confidence but I FOL or Spook the Spec and keep trying through all the resist. Once I have the Spec running I again start nuking and continue until dead. The spawn time is about 6 minutes and I needed every second to regen manna and health.

What ever you do at this camp you must never Spook or FOL the Spec at its spawn point. It will head towards the wall and you will have many more specs eating away at your HPs. Also even after you pull it as described above you must ensure you do not go to a wall this can bring more specs raining down on you. If you happen to get more then one Spec you may as well RLH to Rathe Mountains zone.

At 35, I ventured to Gunthak and battled undead on the beach. They do not have many HPs but they do hit hard and fast. While I did try to melee them, it just was not worth the effort. I would just nuke them to death either by channeling through the melee or rooting then nuking (Heals required as needed). I normally would set up my camp along the left wall near the Troll sailor. Pulling from out in front and the spawns to my left. I could safely AFK med near the Troll as long as I wasn't trained.

Using the above, I am maxed in Offensive and Defensive with 1HB and Bash very close to max. Best of all I had a ton of fun except for the few CR’s I had to do. I grouped at times just to keep my skills up while working with others and for a change of pace.

Hope this helps someone.
