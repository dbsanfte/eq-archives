Merrybrook
This is going to sound corny, but... You will know when to join the guild for you. You will find yourself grouping with the same set of people often, they may be from the same guild or from a couple different ones, but you will find yourself wanting to join a guild so that you can keep grouping with your friends, and joining them on guild activities.

The best advice I can give is, do not rush into joining a guild just to have a tag above your name. Especially as a Cleric. Join a guild because you have found a group of people that are fun, and you enjoy hanging out with them. :D

Rauri
A good guild is a comminuty...they do things together, help one another, celebrate their truimphs together and mourn their losses together. They are working for a common goal, which varies quite a bit between guilds. Some guilds exist to kill the toughest mobs in the game, some to roleplay, some to laugh and have a good time, etc. Think about why you play EQ and find a guild that shares that goal.

Take your time choosing. Group with several members of the guild before you agree to join and then when you do explain that you've never been guilded and you just want to see what it's like...no hard feelings if you decive to /guildremove. If they have a web site check it out, it will tell you a lot about them.

Note that in the higher end of the game being guilded is just about required to participate in some of the activities, like getting your epic. But this really isn't a good enough reason to join a guild in and of itself.

You'll be shocked at how much being in a guild that shares your goals and is a good community enhances your EQ experience!

restandre
After writing this big long post, which I deleted, I came to this conclusion: Your convictions toward playing EQ for progression in the game, and playing EQ for the social aspect of talking to people and grouping with friends, are the two factors that influence joining any guild.

My first guild I joined just to join. I left when it broke up.
My second guild I joined because some friends were starting a new guild. I left when most of the people had moved on for bigger and better things.
My third guild I joined because people I hadn't known started inviting me to groups, and eventually we came to be friends and do things with groups of more than six people. I left when the guild collectively agreed to go our separate ways after the leaders left.
My fourth guild was a raiding guild I joined because people from the third guild had become a part of it and encouraged me. I left when the pressure of being in a very small core of healers, and the only person who would ever take charge of them, gave me a lot of undue stress and I found myself logging on daily out of duty rather than because I enjoyed it.

I'm at a crossroads of deciding where to go now myself. There are other guilds on the server I'm completely not interested in, which leaves me with a choice between:
1) Applying to the top guild on the server, which while I know a few people there, would be more about working through the endgame and earning uber phat l3wt.
2) Join a guild that was "lower" than my previous guild that has a number of old friends in it, but would involve content over six months old to me.
3) Remaining guildless for a while and just enjoy EQ as I knew it when I started playing.

I've always gone where the winds have blown me. Now I must decide which is important to me -- progression, or social life? Answering this question should help guide you to the guild you seek, if any.
