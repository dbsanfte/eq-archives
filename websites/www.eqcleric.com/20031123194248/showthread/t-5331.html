Dorinkoff
As you gain a few more seasons you'll see MoK do a little more healing. It will eventually do 6 hp per hit on each person who hits the mob. Note that it does not heal on strikes from the offhand. So only hits made from the primary weapon will cause healing. In PoP and against raid type mobs with strong AE attacks the spell is not very usefull because the mobs simply hit too hard. You'll notice a difference though if you fight easier mobs and have a pretty tough tank. I used to farm Seb bugs for plat with a 62 paladin. She had pretty good damage mitigation to begin with so adding 6 hp each time she hit the mob really helped her. It is also awesome for power leveling especially coupled with MoR.

Kamiff
The reason MoK debuffs a mobs damage shield is because it is treated as a damage shield and overwrites it since a lot of damage shields do not stack. This is almost identical to a mob casting snare on you, and the snare will overwrite Spirit of the Wolf. And I agree completely with Merrybrook in that it does not really do a heck of a lot to help a main tank out, but I have noticed that it saves me from having to heal the support melee dps in many situations when they occasionally get hit. My BP has a clickie Mark of Karn on it and I use it a lot on long fights, when a ranger or rogue or other melee takes that occasional hit that eats a little hp off them, the MoK, along with regen buffs from shammie, druid, or ranger gets them back to full hp by the end of the fight, and I'm only needed to keep healing the MT.

Insurance
As several have stated, the best use for any of these spells is to overwrite a mob's damage shield. However, as Merrybrook and Kamiff stated, it doesn't do much to help a main tank out. If you rely on MoK to act as a healing component in your strategy you'll be using that clicky stick alot.

MoK is very useful on raids where a lot of people are going to be whacking on that helpless mob. You may see some benefits on the healing side here as other players besides the MA/MT/TM etc have a few more hp's left over than normal after the battle. However, it's the DS mitigation that really counts.

MoR is not very useful in a raid situation. Most yardtrash mobs that are being taken down are going to be slowed. In these instances, it won't be returning much damage at all. However, on those 'fast hitting' mobs it could have a bigger impact.. not much, but bigger. Just remember, on fast hitting mobs you'll probably be more concerned with pumping mana into heals than popping out a MoR on a nasty, unslowable, frenzy, rampage mob. :)

Factors to consider: How long will the fight last? Will the mob be slowed? Does the mob have a natural DS? Does the mob have special melee attacks? (Ramp/rage etc..) I believe MoR only returns dmg on the mob's 'primary' melee attack btw.. any ideas on that issue?

Short fight No DS: Don't bother with MoR, use MoK if you must use one.
Long Fight No DS: MoK a plus, MoR not beneficial if the mob is slowed.
Short fight w/ DS: MoK if necessary, otherwise nothing.
Long Fight w/DS: MoK a plus, MoR if mob is unslowable but watch your mana and pay attention to the tanks first.
Any Fight where the mob is slowed: MoR not worth it.. nuke baby nuke!
