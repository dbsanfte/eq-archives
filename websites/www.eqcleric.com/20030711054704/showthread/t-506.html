Dyschord
quote:
But the spell you are mentioning is in your tests is Divine Intervention, not Death Pact maybe they work differently?


Death Pact works in the same way. The differences come in the final effect, and regularity with which it will fire off. Both spells have a preset "chance" to work, so naturally Death Pact is set lower.

quote:
Can anyone answer why the target's charisma would be in the equation if CHA is indeed in the equation? The reason I ask is that surely the caster can control his CHA and wouldn't the CHA requirement on the target limit its usefulness.?

e.g. Enchanters can cast CHA on themselves to boost chances but often the MT has a full range of more important buffs and some armour lowers CHA making the spell nearly useless to an MT.


The reasoning here is based on game balance, and the need to make classes dependant on others.

Tanks generally do have a low charisma, hence the need for outside charisma help. The best scenario is group the MT(s) with a bard, and the bard can twist in their best charisma song. Pre-PoP, and pre-bardsongs window, tanks had to balance out their buffs a little better. Now it's fairly easy for them to get both a shaman charisma buff, and bardsong.

quote:
And if the CHA doesn't matter, then how come the rumour started?


The rumor came about back when Death Pact was new, and it seemed that the caster's charisma was the key factor. It was along the same lines as "Low charisma makes fear last longer".

Basically, very little was known about the spells function, in relation to stats. Assumptions were made, but changes to Death Pact have also been made stealth-like. The Death Pact of today, is not the Death Pact that was first released.

In fact, it wasn't until Divine Intervention, that people really began to test how the spells really worked.
