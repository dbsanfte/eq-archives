Do you like your guy? If you wish you'd made him another race, then now's the time to change. Second time around doesn't take nearly as long at lower levels as the first time.

I think the most you could have put into wisdom at start is 25? If you like your guy, then believe me at later points in the game it is easy to recoup 25 wisdom through items. It's much harder to get 25 stamina.

If I could reroll my starting stats (hah - not likely, I made this character about two months before Kunark) I would put 20 of my 30 starting points into stamina, and only 5 into wisdom. The other 5 would go into strength, as even at early levels plate is heavier than frail high elves can carry.

I put 25 into wisdom and 5 into strength when I made my cleric. Granted, I was a twink at the time, my original main (druid) camped the cleric Ro armor for me and even got me into Mistmoore at level 5 to loot the part for the quest mace from there. But by the time I was level 30 or so I had more wisdom from items than the cap allowed for. Wisdom items are plentiful. If you like who your character is, it's surely not worth junking him over 25 wisdom.

Illi
Just a note on Wisdom: it's easy to get at high levels. Gnomes start with the lowest of all Clerics (maybe even Froggies, since the LoY site shows Froggies start with 75 Wis -- Gnomes start with 72). Putting all my points into WIS on creation, I have 102 base Wisdom.

However, with equipment, I have an additional 171 Wisdom, which puts the total at 273 (capped at 255 at level 60). With AA points, I can boost the base another 10 points (pre-PoP AAs) to 112, which would make my total 283. With PoP AAs, I could raise it ANOTHER ten points to 122 (to a total of 293 with current equipment, which WILL be upgraded over time).

So, unless you absoltely hate being blind and smart (and wrongsized, but that's another matter), I wouldn't necessarily suggest restarting. HOWEVER, since you are wrongsized, it would be a blessing to be reborn rightsized. :D
