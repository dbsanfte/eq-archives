leucol
Hello everyone...I look forward to participating in this forum.

I came to EQ from a D&D background. I loved playing D&D clerics and, of course, my first EQ character was a cleric. I was so dissapointed that I almost quit the game.

However, while doing research trying to find a way to play a cleric the way I wanted to, I found a few very compelling things about rogues, and have been one ever since. I really enjoy playing the class.

I've had an EQ style clerric as an alt for quite some time, but I spend very little time playing him, as I find the role boring and effete. Just my opinion.

But when I heard about the proposed changes to clerics, I created an entirely new cleric. Dwarf Cleric, maxed out starting wisdom, put the rest into strength. Gave him 1K to get started and went to the Bazaar. Emphasis on STR and AC, got a nice fast Burning Brand to keep 1HB up. Have soloed him to 20th in an amazingly short period of time (Thanks for Paludal, Verant!).

With self-buffs only, he wades into battle with these stats (approximately...I'm not anal anough to memorize every single stat on my toons lol)...

STR 131
AGI 95
DEX 106
WIS 121

AC 550
HP 700 (Cast Celestial Remedy before wading into battle and effective HP jumps considerably)
ATK 400

Its very hard to keep 1HB maxed out, I plan on using training points toward this along with Bash.

So far he solos exceptionally well. I'm still developing tactics, but right now I can solo two dark blue mobs at the same time with relative ease (a Spell Haste focus item is essential for spell casting while meleeing). I can take on three dark blues, but its an iffy situation...if I can get one successful root off, then I'll own them. Other wise I DA, leap into the waters below and use the time the critters take to path to me to Gate out.

Single yellow mobs are a good fight. If I couldn't get a couple DD's off it would be a toss-up as to who would win. With successful DD's I end the fight at about 50 life and 40 mana.

Double yellows are an adrenaline rush. Root one, back off and melee/DD the other and kill it quick. Re-root the other, heal and DD until there's only enough mana for Gate, then wade in and finish it off melee-style. These fights (when succesful heheh) leave me at about 20 or less life and OOM except for the Gate reserve.

Conventional wisdom keeps insisting that my battle-cleric will run out of steam somewhere between 25 and 30th level, and have to revert to a rear echelon role. That may be, but I am prepared to bankrupt my main to provide this little guy with whatever gear will enable him to continue his career. I love playing my rogue, but if I can make a battle-cleric work, I will definitely demote the rogue to alt and make the cleric my main.
