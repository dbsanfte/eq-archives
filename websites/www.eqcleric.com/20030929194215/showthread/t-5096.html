Some nights, I shouldn't play EQ - Click HERE for Original Thread
Doohickey
Well, after a 10 minute session last night that convinced me to not play the Dynamic Duo for the night, I will now have a new Train hotkey.

The evening started well enough. Broke camp at the lighthouse in Gunthak, Shazzog buffed us both up, invised us, and off we went.

About halfway through the tunnels to Dulak, I got a /tell asking me if I could rez. Stopped at a fairly safe spot and said, "Yes, but only 50%". I continued on my way at that point, as the Dulak zone line is the most likely place they will need the rez, and I can always *Bamph* back if it's at the lighthouse...
No, it is at Dulak zoneline, as I expected. Shazzog and I zone in and I rez the poor Lizard, while Shazzog loads up his buffs and makes sure Mr. Lizard stands a better chance at survival next time.

Mr. Lizard presented me with a nice Emerald Ring for my troubles, so I am, all things considered, rather pleased with my night so far. Shazzog reapplies the invisibility after rememming his Combat Load and away we go to our favorite picnic spot in the SW corner.

Once there, I see Johny Fisherman is in his usual spot. He'll do as a warm up round for the night. I toss off my level 1 'nuke' to get his attention, and Shazzog tosses out the Sparklies of Slowness while siccing Old Yeller on Johny Fisherman. (Yes, his name is Johny. You'd know this if you spent as much time in that little picnic spot as we have. The tired looking Pirate off to the left? That's Ralph.)

In a well practiced manuever, I reach over and hit hotkey 4 for Yaulp II. Hmmm, Yaulp seems to be taking awful long to cast...
Ah fer the love of Inny...I forgot to remem Yaulp II. Gate is still there.

Hello there, SoulBinder. So nice to see you again. Sorry, can't chat, Shazzog is still back there at the Picnic spot and probably wondering where I ran off to.

Thank the Lords of Norrath for Cloudy Potions. Shazzog would have been really annoyed if he had to put Old Yeller to sleep so soon just to come do the White Paper trick with me again.

I make it back to Dulak with no problems to find Shazzog making up a batch of "Missing: Silly Dark Elf" posters. He is a bit of a worrier, that Troll. Not his fault, he still remembers the days when I would go wandering off and come high tailing back with a dozen Kobolds on my tail. Ah, those were fun days.

Once he stowed his crayons away, I went to play tag with Robert, the first Boat Scrubber down on that empty gangway. I head back for the relative safety of Shazzog and Old Yeller while pressing Shazzog's HotKey #3 (/assist Duhikae, /pet attack, /cast Sparklies of Slowness). Shazzog just sits there, looking at me. Old Yeller is cleaning his nether regions, unconcerned with the Fishy Smelling Doom coming towards him. Hit HK3 again. Nothing. Start jabbing HK3 repeatedly. Nada.

I glance over at Shazzog's monitor. It appears he had started talking to our Klan at some point, and forgot to hit the Enter key. Probably right after I *Bamph*d back to Gunthak. Probably asking if anyone had seen me.

Ah well, no time to backspace. The Klan will just have to puzzle over the estoric meaning of ‘333333333333333333333333’. Now, try it again. Ah yes, there are the sparklies. Nice to see you paying attention, Old Yeller. Mind getting Robert off of me, perchance?

Robert took a nap shortly thereafter.

Now that Robert is resting comfortably, it is time to move on to Fred, the Engineer. A quick check to make sure Shazzog isn’t trying to talk to the Klan again, and I am off.

Fred, being the predictable sort that he is, takes offense at my minor 20 damage nuke and chases me up the gangway.

This time, Shazzog’s hotkey works as planned. Sparklies sparkle, Old Yeller growls, and away we go.

I’ve often admired Fred’s ability to shrug off the attacks of small insects. He displays these talents once again, and resists the sparklies. He is, however, still offended by them, and begins to beat on Shazzog. Silly Fred.

Fred does manage to get some good shots in. Shazzog is down to about 40% before Fred realizes the End is Nigh, and turns to run. Shazzog isn’t going to let that happen, though, and casts his Rooting Sparklies.

Did I mention how very resistant Fred can be? Root doesn’t. Fred spots a wandering guard and heads straight for him.

Fred is a little slow, sometimes. Heck, I’ve killed Fred and his crew of scrubbers at the very feet of the guards on countless occasions, and they never seem to notice or care.

Hey, why is that guard running at me? Why is he hitting me? Did Fred finally pay his Pirate Dues and now they suddenly care?

Well, it’s been fun – time to go. Got a…hair appointment. Yeah, that’s right. Reservations, hard to get an appointment, can’t be late, you understand.

I run for the zoneline screaming ‘TRAIN!’ and hoping I don’t draw too much unwanted attention on the way. Apparently not, as I make it without incident. I think I ran so fast that the pirates I passed were still wondering what happened as I zoned.

Shazzog, in the meantime, wisely decided now was a good time to load up his own Gate spell and test it out. Can’t have it failing at inopportune times, after all. It worked, and he was able to wave goodbye at the returning guard just as Scotty pulled down on the metaphorical slider switch.

…

At this point, I decided that tonight (last night, now) was probably not a good night for me to be playing EQ. At least, not as the Dynamic Duo.

On the bright side, the episode did make me decide that it was time to create a .
