AanyaLorien
I remember when they lowered the spellbook requirement from 50 to 35.

I was level 50 (a druid at the time, hadn't made the cleric that turned into my main yet), sitting in the hot tub in Mistmoore with a couple friends camping Xicotl. We'd heard rumors that the spellbook was going to go away for those above level 35, but it had not been posted in a patch message yet. My friend, a cleric, stood up and started screaming OMG OMG OMG!!! She said she'd just been sitting, without her book open, very lom. When she stood up, the mana bar filled.

I tried it, and sure enough - my mana bar filled when I stood up. It was bugged at first - your bar didn't go up while sitting unless you were behind your spellbook, but your mana was regening as if you were medding even though it didn't show. I don't recall myself feeling cheated because people who were level 35 didn't have to go through 15 more levels of spellbooks than I did, perhaps I was just too thrilled that I'd never have to look at it again (well... then after that I made the cleric and was I ever eager to hit 35).

Yeah, people who start EQ these days have it a lot easier than we did, long ago when there were very few clerics high level enough to do a res, there was no clarity spell or assist feature, and the Guise of the Deceiver that was usable by all from inventory still dropped in Lower Guk. Does that ruin the game for me or for them? It doesn't for me, I guess time will tell if it does for them.

I do know that I'm sick to death of getting tells from some level 19 three zones away wanting me to run over to res them. My res rule now is I won't cross a zone line for someone who is anon. They show themselves or get someone else. If they're under level 30, I'm not going out of my way to res them unless they are bound far away from their corpse (and then they have to listen to my lecture on how they take things others can do for them too much for granted and they need to take some responsibility for their own game, not rely upon finding someone who will give up their own personal game time to benefit a total stranger).
