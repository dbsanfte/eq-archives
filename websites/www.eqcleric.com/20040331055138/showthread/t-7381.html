Usually I have two different CH hotbuttons, one for my group members and one for the raid MT.

Group:
/sit off
/cast 1
/g Complete Healing on %T in 10 seconds
/g If you move out of range I WILL NUKE YOU
/cast 1

Raid:
/cast 1
/3 Qunos will land CH on %T in 10 seconds
/cast 1
/pause 40 [or whatever]
/3 Meatwaggon cast NOW!!!
Sometimes line 5 is blank, depending on the boss.

If you are in charge of setting up a CH rotation, make sure everyone knows whether they are CHing or patching, and if CHing, what order in the rotation they are. Make sure clerics, druids, and necros have a heal channel separate from the main raid channel. Make sure all of them are actually in the channel :). If possible, make sure every necro has 1-3 healers assigned to mana feed. If it is a long fight or the boss mana drains, mana feeding should be the necros' only job during a boss encounter. Make sure you know, or acquire from the RL, info regarding how long the fight will likely be, what kind of damage the boss puts out, so that you can coordinate MGB EEs or what not, or if everyone should just spam heal the tank, etc.

If you are not in charge, make sure you do EXACTLY and EVERYTHING the RL says to do and not do. Make sure you do everything the guy in charge of healers says to do. Do not be an ass and put up the rebellious 'don't-tell-me-what-to-do' front, or you may wipe the raid. If you are that kind of person, it would be better for you not to go on raids at all. In other words: lead, follow, or get out of the raid.
