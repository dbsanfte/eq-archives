Shadowfrost
You refer mostly to melee soloing. At 62, one of your best options is probably root rotting; you've just got access to the Sermon of Penitence spell.

Root rotting technique

Pick a monster which is undead and dark blue. For the original poster, a good place to start would be Maiden's Eye, where the Vampyre Bats are dark blue to 62 but not tough enough to cause a serious problem. You may find you need Wake of Tranquillity to single pull them.

Root them with Paralyzing Earth (Petrifying Earth is better for a level 65 cleric) and then cast Epitaph of Life and Sermon of Penitence on them. Refresh each spell when it wears off. Repeat until you get xp.

When doing one at a time gets trivial for you, try root rotting two at once. ;)

Shadowfrost's guide to soloing for exp

Don't. If you don't have time or attention to devote to a group, then as a cleric, you will not receive a meaningful amount of exp solo.

Shadowfrost's guide to soloing for loot

Go to Chardok B. The hardest part about Chardok B is getting there. If you have decent faction with the sarnaks, then it's easy. If not, then here is the procedure:-

a) Bind in BW outside Chardok.
b) Get DA, DB, and BDA up.
c) Drink a sow potion (can skip this if you are familiar with the route).
d) Run to Chardok fort and DB. Call a train now.
e) Run through the fort to the first bridge. Jump off the left side of the bridge.
f) You'll land in a room containing a couple of slaves with one exit. Take the exit and turn immediately right.
g) Follow the left hand wall from here. It's quite a long way. DB will wear off; DA the moment it does.
h) Eventually you'll reach the door into the bank. Go through it and through the next door as well, still following the left hand wall. You'll emerge in an open area containing a lake and a waterfall. Keep left, keep going.
i) Swim through the waterfall. Just past the waterfall on the left is a small alcove. In the alcove is a lever. Click the lever and you're in Chardok B.

Once in Chardok B, you can pull monsters to the entrance room and solo them at your leisure. They'll produce a bit of +Chardok faction, some PP, and a tiny bit of exp. You can melee solo them or root rot them, either will work.

All of the static-spawn undead are PH's for the Rabid Chokidai. Rabid Chokidai cannot be pacified or rooted (and if you're duoing them, you also need to know that they can't be snared and mitigate slow). They summon, so you can't root rot them; melee soloing is about your only choice. With maxed defensive AA and halfway decent gear, they're a fairly easy melee solo.

Their common drop is a 6ac 40hp 40mana earring (junk). Their rare drop is a tradeable caster earring with 45hp, 60mana and permanent see invis, which is better but still not worth it. For decent loot, go further in.

Specifically, you need to invis yourself with improved ITU, go out of the entrance room, and turn right. Pretty soon you'll come to another junction where there's a bridge ahead... again, turn right.

Be careful here. The open area to your right contains live critters which can see you. Hug the left wall until you're near the doorway at the other side of the cavern.

At the doorway, there's a set of stairs ahead. Guarding the stairs are two golems. These are live critters and they can see you; the other critters in the room are undead. Cross the room smartly to the right hand wall. At the first sign of aggro, DA and run to zone; you'll have to zone back in and start again. With a bit of practise, you can cross this doorway safely.

From here, hug the right hand wall until you reach the next chamber. You'll get a message about the "cackling of undead in this haunted chamber". The haunted chamber is your destination, and you'll need to clear one monster to establish your camp spot; it's over against the right hand wall. It'll be undead, so kill it however you like.

There are several other static spawns and wanderers in this room. If you're over where you killed the first monster, you're out of wanderer aggro; they're subject to pacification so you don't need to kill them. Clear as many of the static spawns as you can.

If a Rabid Chokidai pops in this room, it will have different drops. Common drop here is a pair of 15sta 40hp boots (junk); rare drop is a tradeable necklace with FTII. That necklace is your jackpot - it sells for 150,000pp to 250,000pp on Xev.

Shadowfrost's guide to soloing for brag value

Go to the Halls of Honor. Drink an invis potion or use your Larrikan's Mask/GVD. Follow the left hand wall. Keep going until you reach a place where there are three wrulons. Run past the wrulons and you'll see six critters immediately in front of you which look like white suits of armour.

These critters are rootable, undead, and do not summon. Therefore, you can solo them. They're worth about 5%aa exp each.

Choose a corner of the building to solo in; corners are safe from wanderers and you can go AFK there. Select the nearest white suit and pacify the other suits near it. Pull it with Destroy Undead, and root it out of aggro range of its friends. Use Epitaph of Life and Sermon of Penitence to kill it, and Dawncall if you have a Hammer of the Sun (a Hammer of the Sun really, really helps here.) Expect root to break early and often, and expect the critter to resist stuns and roots sometimes.

Kill one of these, and you've killed a monster with something like 40,000hp who hits for over 500, hence the brag value. ;)

Restandre
Soloing up to 60 is possible. Been there, done that. Melee, nuking, dotting. Anything goes in the right spot.

Soloing post-60 is tougher. There are a couple spots with baby blue mobs you can kill, like Karnor's basement and Nadox. The only place I might recommend at all though is HoH, at the white spirits camp. (Southeast shrine)
Go to a front corner of the room. Pacify the side spawn. Pacify the near spawn in the front. Pacify the rear spawn on that side. Pull the side spawn, and root it in the corner. Dot it with whatever you've got, and stay on your horse as much as possible. Time root yourself so you can refresh it before it breaks, assuming it goes the full length. A Hammer of the Sun really helps for this, (right click Dawncall,) but can be done without. I'd advise using clarity unless you know you can do it without. It takes about 5 minutes to kill a mob with 3 dots, plus any med time you might have.
When you pull the front spawn, pacify the other front spawn and the rear. Then the rear, if you get to it, is just pacify the other rear spawn. Unless you're so vewy powa, you won't get more than 3 spawns per cycle. I've heard of people doing two at once, but you'll go oom after just as many spells either way; don't risk it.
