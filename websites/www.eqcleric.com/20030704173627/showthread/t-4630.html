Shadowfrost
What a horrifying idea.

When I'm in an exp group, my job is to keep people alive and I do that job to the best of my ability. I have never intentionally allowed someone to die in my group, and I tend to feel personally responsible for every death that happens, whether it was really my fault or not. I hate that feeling of guilt and I'll do everything I can to avoid it.

The idea of a cleric intentionally letting someone die disturbs me. It's like imagining a tank who won't taunt off his cleric because he doesn't like her... in situations like that, you don't have a group at all. What you have is a bunch of people who're too selfish to help each other, and it's going nowhere: time to leave.

The relationship between cleric and tank is so trusting that it's almost symbiotic. The tank stands there and taunts a critter that can kill him in under thirty seconds because of the trust he places in his cleric. The cleric stands there and heals that tank in the knowledge that it will irritate the critter because she trusts the tank to taunt it off her.

Betraying that trust is wrong.

If there's someone in the group I genuinely don't like, then I'll leave. Clerics are in demand enough that I can get another group pretty easily.

Let me put it another way:-

When someone dies in an exp group, that affects your reputation, particularly if it was preventable. Do it more than once or twice and your reputation will suffer, and it's a lot harder to fix faction with the playerbase than it is with monsters!

Your reputation matters, and people will remember. Do your job as well as you can - all the time.
