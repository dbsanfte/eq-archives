After you complete two adventures, you're supposed to talk to someone for a tidbit of story. There's no reason to do this every time though. When you get to 10, you can go to that person and get all 5 at once, and then your charm should be upgraded. The second set is usually elsewhere, via some NPC other than the camp head. It should also be noted that eventually the numbers become a little more erratic -- the charm upgrades don't fall strictly on the 10s, and your charm will sometimes upgrade by 3 instead of 2.

quote:
Oompaah said in post #5 :
The thing I'm wondering about is the charm you can buy at the wayfarer's camp that supposedly increases a certain stat when you ldon... that has never raised for me.


These charms (amulets, talismans, etc) do not to my knowledge increase in power as you adventure. What they do is add a set value (+1 to +5, depending on which you buy) to one stat. This is *only* valid when you are in the dungeon.

Example: my rogue has an Ebon Talisman. When she is actually doing an LDoN mission, that charm gives her +5 dex. When she is outside of the LDoN dungeon, it does nothing.

Note: Many people bind their Adventurer's Stones to these charms. Just in case someone has this question, the Adventurer's Stone *will* give you its benefits outside of LDoN. My charm shows '+4 hp +4 mana' when I'm in a normal zone, and '+4 hp +4 mana +5 dex' when I'm doing an adventure.
