Restandre
If I were to write a post like this, Wall of Polls would not be the place I would look for it to already exist. Lenne wants human, verbal responses. Not a pick from a multiple choice question that doesn't begin to scratch the surface of the question he's asking.

EQ has been a lot of different things to me. It started off being an amusement - my new pre-occupation that just replaced the last game I was playing. Then it became a pastime - something that would linger when the next thing came around. When I started to be overcome by depression, it was my salvation - there was literally nothing else, even going to the bathroom at times, that would get me out of bed. Now it is my curse and addiction, and I am trying to break free of it, while at the same time it is also my hobby and something I have put so much time into that I don't know how or why I would want to just walk away.

In the last 3 years, come Feb 2, I will have spent (as a crude estimate) 280 of those 1095 days doing nothing but playing Everquest. Needless to say, it means a great deal to me, and a good many things. Three years...reminds me of a quote from Our Town...something to the effect of "How can I ever leave this place? It's all I've been, it's all I've known." Quite honestly, I find it hard to identify much with my life anymore without it. Is this the sign of an addict, or someone who just truly loves it, or somewhere in-between? I don't know, but I'm not averaging 12 hours per day any more, and mostly just continuing for the people now.

So I suppose that's what EQ is to me now. A place where my friends are.

First of all...not a repost... Wall of Polls is a place for polls...This is a post asking for input...

I'm an avid gamer...always have been. Never did find a game that had serious longevity beyond completion and I kept wishing they'd come out with a game that never finished...

I was hesitant to the whole EverQuest concept at first. I did not like the idea of paying a monthly fee to play a game. When a friend showed me his character, bringing his CD's and showing the game off, I got hooked. It was so enticing and amazing.

Being an old chatter, and an old IRC user to me EQ is like a chatroom with a game around it. The social aspect in the game alone is what keeps me going. Some days I log in and just chat, not knowing what to do..other days I'm all hardcore gaming, hardly uttering a word.

Being Jarvis in-game is not always easy. It can take me hours to get anything done. I get a lot of tells and a lot of cross server tells, so I often slip into alter-egos, playing in the shadow of an unknown character.

I'm really looking forward to EverQuest 2, anticipating the changes coming, but I can also tell you that Sigil Games, holding the core of the orginial concept of EQ is getting my attention.

I guess what I'm trying to say is EQ is to me what a chatroom and a game combined would be. It's everything the TV can't be and it's everything I like in a game...Oh and I get to kill stuff..yeah...killing stuff is fun...
