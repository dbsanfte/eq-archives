Tatertot
I am sure most of you are quite aware fo this information. However, for others who may be neophytes at EQ, I want to share information that makes me a much better cleric. I receive it from my mentor and Guild Leader Faithkilz:

Word of restoration, the group heal, also cures disease and poison. So when you fight something with an ae poison or disease proc like Trak or Sev it could be very handy indeed. Word of Restoration heals everyone in the group for about the same number of HP as Divine Light. For it to be efficient 3 or more people need to be healed. Seems like it might be handy to have up if you can spare the slot. Not sure of it's general usefulness tho.

The store bought spell Bulwark of Faith and the drop spell Aegis are very similar. They are both targetable AC buffs and supercede the Shield of Words spell. However Bulwark gives AC37 and lasts 1:21 (a long time) whereas the drop spell Aegis only offers AC34 (still a lot) but lasts 2:24 (a REAL
long time). Nice to have both but the store bought one will serve and be preferable in most cases. The drop spell would be more for say buffing tanks when the group breaks up or otherwise when you won't be around anymore. Similar to HB (or Heroism) vs Fortitude. First is a little better, second is a lot longer.

While on the topic of AC buffs: when an enchanter is around, let them do the AC buffs unless requested otherwise. Theirs are not as good generally (ac23) but are MUCH cheaper (mana80). If the situation warrants the mana expense to maximize AC it will be apparent and someone will say so.
Your mana is better conserved for healing. If there is a sufficiently high druid, potg is better
than cleric your ac/hp buffs (HB/Fort), and enchanter’s straight ac buffs (Bulwark/Aegis) are more efficient than clerics.

But always remember to keep Armor of the Faithful on yourself. At 58 you get a very nice upgrade, which includes a clarity (6mana/tic) effect.

:) Hope this helps someone else.

Noubourne
Never used Aegis. Did not buy it. Nor the other low mana cost long term compromise buffs. With good mana management you can stay ready for rebuffs. I suggest a timer on your buffs. Here's a tip on how to do that. Start by giving 3 of your group Bulwark, then med back that mana and do it to the rest of the group. This way you will have them on a cycle. HB is cheap mana cost, great spell. I don't think there is any excuse to downgrade the potential of your groups abilities. Much less to save mana in the short run. Put quite simply, if your group wanted inferior cleric buffs, they would have grouped with a lower level cleric. In battle the extra HP and AC will translate into saved mana for you in the long run. Don't kid yourself, the best you have to offer is often not good enough. The only compromise toward buffing to be made would be on component spells, such as your symbols. These in a normal group I cast on tank and chanter. In a great exp group in a zone with good loots I would hand them out to all. I never had to ask ppl for extra loots to cover dots, they always offered. In any case, it is not difficult to politely mention your personal costs of running a group, just don't whine about it. Also, for buffing people will be more than happy, in my experience, to hold pulls a couple min while you give them the best buffs you have. They know as well as most players that 4 AC will translate into saved mana in the end, as they will be hit thousands of times with that buff on.

Of course everyone has their own way... but I didn't get to lvl 60 in 5 months being a crappy cleric. I have had many many guild invites, and most of the best guilds on my server have told me I am welcome should I desire to leave my current place. People often comment to me that I am one of the best clerics groupwise they have met. My motto is keep your group buffed to the nines, and the exp and loot will come to cover your efforts.

Filadan
This thread is old, but let me play devil's advocate. Are you really aware how MINIMAL the difference in damage mitigation is as a result of AC? Believe me, 3AC makes almost no difference, unless it so happens to fall at an increment of 100AC.

That being said, group heroism costs a small enough amount of mana that you can cast it whenever needed. Armor Class buffs are mana-intensive, and offer relatively little benefit, and thus a double-duration buff, with a minor AC loss is the best option.

And, as a bit of perspective, leveling to 60 in 5 months has nothing to do with personal skill... it has to do with how much time you spent playing the game in those 5 months, and what zones you did it in. Everyone gains experience at the same rate (unless you are a halfling or troll/iksar). There are no bonuses for "being a good cleric". It aggravates me to no end when people believe that by plugging through to level 60 in as little time as possible makes them "special". I'm betting that you did the ever popular LoIO --> FM --> OT --> DL --> KC --> Velks track to 60. In reality, those are the people that must of us choose to avoid, because they usually lack the experience/skill to handle more difficult areas/encounters.
