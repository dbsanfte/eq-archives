Swimming wasn't the problem, it was getting out over the edge.

Or the first time I zoned into North Karana, I saw this "Flying Lion" aka Griffon, I was so amazed at it's looks I ran to it to get a closer look and was pummeled quickly.

Or the first time I discovered the guards would save my ass. I had been playing around and getting killed trying to get some XP. I had no idea I could run to the guards, then I finally said, screw it, I'm gonna try to make it to the zone so I ran like a madman, and was astonished when I saw the Guards yelling and turned to see them stomp my pursuers to death.

Man the number of deaths back then before we knew anything about the game. It was hell but looking back it sure makes you appreciate what you have now!

I've got TONS of stories but will save them for later, let's here some of your early days stories!

Hannorel
My first days were spent in the Greater Faydark, as you might expect. I probably stayed there and in Clan Crushbone far too long; the mobs were greening out...but where else could I go, right? Sure, some folks had talked about heading to the Estate of Unrest. The way they described it, I wasn't sure if I was ready to travel all the way there! /eek

I remember with great awe my first boat ride; the fact that the world was big enough for a boat trip to take half a day (relatively) amazed me. I had just come off of playing Diablo 2 pretty religiously, and this was totally different in breadth and scope.

I remember nervously eyeing the chat window as I stuck my face in the book, medding my little elven butt off, to keep an eye out for anything bad that might be happening.

It was truly a wonderful time, all of the firsts; I don't mourn the loss of some of the game mechanics. The PoK books have been both a boon and a bane; they are a great convenience, but at the same time they do trivialize a lot of the faction issues for race.

I do miss those early days, sometimes, for their wonder and fear.
