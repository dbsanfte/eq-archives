stratys
There's definitely a difference between asking which is the toughest class to PLAY and which is the toughest to MASTER. With some classes, it's not hard to figure out the basics and do a halfway decent job, but it might be much harder to get to the point where you are really wringing that extra bit of potential out of your class, achieving things the vast majority of people don't even attempt.

Different environments can influence how hard it is to play a certain class. Some classes can be very complicated at times and very easy at others (I oftend find the enchanter to be one of the most difficult classes to master, but to some degree on high level raids it can become simple/boring.

One also has to consider what is meant by "toughest"... for instance, without a doubt I think bards are the most physically challenging to play well (I say this based on reading up on the class and on having played a bard up to the whopping level of 13 -- I make no claim whatsoever to have mastered even the basics). Twisting requires constantly toggling songs on and off, so you're constantly at work, and switching among songs and instruments and weapons and so on involves intricate patterns of keypressing with fairly precise timing... there is a physical skill to be leanred, like playing a piano.

The concepts involved in playing the cleric are pretty simple and it's not hard in most cases to do a halfway decent job (thus the reason you see so many cleric bots)... but in a group that pushes the envelope it takes real talent to come out shining and keep bodies from hitting the floor. In fast-paced play, the cleric can be one of "toughest" in terms of stress; spiky damage and mobs that can kill in a few swings means you have to be alert at all times, any lapses WILL be noticed. a fast connect is more of a factor than it is for many classes.

Hey all

New poster here :) As well as my 56 Cleric, I've got a 65 Bard who is my main.

I want to clarify what some have said about Bards: we are NOT the hardest class to play. Some Bards will say we are, but in my opinion, all it takes is manual dexterity. Now, what we are is a hard class to play properly. It takes skill to know what songs you need to sing in any situation, and for that you will need a good knowledge of your class.

A lot of players get daunted by this. But on the other hand, I'd be daunted playing a Shaman if I hadn't played one before! For one thing agro management is far easier with a Bard since we're specifically coded to not generate agro. An example: I can debuff, slow 52%, and snare a mob on inc. As soon as anyone else touches the mob, they will have agro 99pc of the time.

And twisting songs is not difficult either. Choose three, whack them onto your hotbox, and press the keys to start a song, press a key to stop it and then start the next song. If you can twist four songs it doesn't mean you're a good Bard - it means you've got a fast computer and a good, stable connection ;) "Twisting" more then four generally means you have an effect from a Bard clicky / Epic proc etc which lasts longer than the 18 second duration of a Bard song.

I hope this clarifies things :)
