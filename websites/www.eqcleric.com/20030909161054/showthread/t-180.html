Jawn Doh
If you've never been to Tower of Frozen Shadow, grab a partner (preferably a Ranger and a few more if you want, but definitely a Ranger if no one else...Necromancers are good to have, too).

First, purchase a Dawnfire, Morningstar of Light, if you can afford one.

Quick guide to getting keys (after, of course, printing a map from www.eqatlas.com):

First floor: anything spawns the undead gnoll (drops key to 2nd floor), so kill everything. Use fear...the mobs run straight to the mirror...easy to kill, just be careful not to click the mirror.

Second floor: again, kill everything. Don't fear, just heal Ranger, nuke some. Anything can spawn the Angry Librarian (drops key to 3rd floor).

Third floor: Again, kill anything. Spawn enraged gnoll (drops key to 4th floor).

Fourth floor: This floor can be a total PAIN. Just focus and keep going. Anything can spawn Carmilla Omican (not sure on name, but close enough...she spawns in a coffin next to the mirror that pops you to 5th floor; she drops key, a No Drop dagger, Embalmer's Skinning Knife, and Socerous Bowl).

Fifth floor: Easy solo here for a Cleric, but you'll want to move to 6th floor for easier solo. Kill anything and spawn enraged relative (drops Beer Stained Coldain Tunic, Velium Spiked helm, key to 6th floor). Also, some broken (or unsolved) quest items drop here, as well as some nice resist gear (off of Nosja, Damad, and Priest Majes Medory).

Sixth floor: Here you can solo. Load up Superior Healing (if below 44; if 44+, use Celestial Healing), Panic Undead, Wave of Fear (in case of adds; just use Wave, then use Panic the Dead after all mobs are feared), Yaulp (if you want), Sermon of the Righteous, Turning of the Unnatural (if you don't have this spell, it will eventually drop), Banish Undead (or your most recent undead nuke), and a stun or two. Follow the hallway and on your first left turn, you'll see a gnoll. Sometimes he aggroes, sometimes he doesn't. Eventually you will learn how to *not* aggro him before you can fear. Fear him, then follow him to the wall, and melee/nuke/DoT. Follow the same hallway and keep pulling the gnolls and spectres. Only grab the 2 gnolls and 2 spectres from this hallway, then move to the opposite hallway. With KEI, I was able to do the first 2 gnolls and spectres, and then 4 more gnolls before they started respawning.

After getting your keys, the trick will be getting to the spot by yourself.

First floor: use IVU.

Second floor: IVU.

Third floor: After getting to the mirror on second floor, cast a Cloudy Potion (sells for 11pp in Thurgadin) and run through the floor.

Fourth floor: There is *always* a mummy that can see invis, but everything else can't see through normal invis. Before popping to 4th floor, cast another invis potion and just pray you don't run into the mummy. If aggroed, cast DA quickly and keep running. The mobs won't follow you to the next floor, but camp if you want to be extra careful.

Fifth floor: use IVU.

After 10 levels here, I've only ran into trouble once with the 4th floor, and I leveled FAST, with or without KEI. I grouped and soloed through these levels, but if I had mostly soloed, I would have leveled faster. Exp is good to level 54, but slows down considerably after 50.

The whole TRICK to this spot is, sadly, the Dawnfire weapon. If you don't have one or can't get one (it's still a decent Cleric item, regardless), I wouldn't bother hunting in this spot.

Mid lvl exp zone info

This is part of one of my posts from my old guilds board. Keep in mind that when I posted this I was playing my Ranger as my main which was lvl 56 at the time and my cleric was 54. I leveld my rng from lvl 30 to 51 in this zone and I leveled my cleric to 34 - 51 in this zone (dual boxing with my rng). I also solo'd my necro from level 39 to 49 in the caves there. I made 60kpp+ with my necro solo and did the Shield of Bane warding quest 6 times with my rng and cleric boxing. Selling all shields for 20-25kpp each (only the skulls drop here for the quest). They go for about 10kpp - 15kpp now. I also made probably 20kpp from other drops and vendor crap with my rng and cleric. Ok on to the post.

On the topic of mid lvl exp areas I favor Tenebrous Mountains. One major reason is there are LESS PEEPS there. Two is there is great exp and great loot at the same time. I exp'd there from lvl 30ish to lvl 51.

There are two zonelines you can hunt from solo ( Katta entrance and Grimling Forest zone).

There are also some great quests items that drop there like Shield of Bane warding piece ( Tormented vah shir skulls is one piece ). Which is a 20ac 20wis 12svm shield. That camp is to the far left of the Katta wall and is the safest camp in the zone (no trains and no wanderers). You sit at the rock (safe spot from all spawns) with the tree ahead of you and to the left and watch for the spawns on the hill by the corner of the wall. The respawn is 3 mins and constantly spawns a lvl 35 to 40ish npc of a different variety (grol baku, darkwing bats, craig lagi etc.) as the PH (place holder) for the Tormented Vah Shir. The TVS is lvl 45ish i'd say. You can also pull from the valley below while waiting for the repop.

You can also hit the Coterie outposts after i'd say lvl 45. Which are spread around the zone along the zone barrier. They are a lot harder and lifetap like crazy.

Or you can take one of the two grimling caves (drop runes, acrylia, windstones and some gems). Which is between the Grimling zoneline and the Katta wall. This is where I made the all my pp while soloing my necro. Selling Acrylia and Windstones to other players in the bazaar and selling the rest of the stuff to vendors. 200 to 600pp a day of vendor crap depending on how long I was there and my necro got up to 3 levels a day there. Up to 4kpp a day in acrylia and windstones (long day. lol).

The loot in this zone is great and has great exp too. In one night off random mobs you could make 300pp+. The random mobs drop acrylia and windstones and essance of the wind(use for tradeskills or sell to other players in bazaar for great price), gems of many kinds (some sell as high as 16pp each to vendors) and some pp drops too.

Now for ultimate loot and some good exp you could hit the Coterie Castle (aka fort). You will need a good FULL group of lvl 47+ with a good healer and tank. You will also need a monk or necro to pull so you can split the mobs. I have hunted here quite a bit and the mobs can be harmonied so a rng could do the pulling too. Druids just don't have the hp's and ac for it I don't think. They hit hard there and lifetap hard and some DD for up to 1k-2k. The mobs also invis themselves so watch out. I've seen each member in the group get up to 2kpp worth of drops from a night's work (5+ hrs). Sometimes more. Some really good INT caster loot drops here (blood weave), A rare cloak and rare wis shield quest piece (not SoBW). Blue Diamonds and many other gems worth in the 100pp to 300pp range to vendors and some are used for quest gear and tradeskills, Coterie gear (general boots and general capes), and other misc drops that can be vendor sold for PP split to group. The castle is located to the right of the Grimling zoneline (as you enter TM) in the corner of the zone. You will pass a coterie outpost on the way there (stay far right to get by untouched). You'll know when you get there by the size of the place. Its about 20 times taller then you.

Hope this helps you all. I've spent a great deal of time in this zone.

Dulaks is a zone off Gunthak. You run through the tunnel that is on the beach in Gunthak. If you keep to the right side of the tunnel and follow it all the way, you will come to a small town, there are 2 zonelines in this town. Keep to the right still and circle the town and you will enter a zone, this is Dulaks.
There are a few good spots for hunting here. Dont stay at the zoneline too long unless your group is ready as there is at least one wanderer that comes close enough to aggro.
You can fight at the zoneline (I found this is generally one of the hardest places to camp). You can camp around the corner from the zone on the left wall (another hard spot). You can do the caves (better have a good group for this) or you can do the ships.
Run to the right from the zone in and you get to the harbour. There are 4 ships along here and they tend to go from easier to harder. The first ship, Storm is the best one to start on and is the easiest. 2 People can do this no problems (any 2 classes). Dont worry about aggro from mobs, as long as you stay on the boat you are pretty right, just sit near the ramp on the boat. Pulling aggro is pretty good but use Pacify just in case.

Gunthak beach is great. I havent been there in a few levels, but I would guess that levels 39 to 47 could solo here and get exp (great exp). The best place to camp is on the dead side (from the lighthouse, the left side is dead side, right is live side). From the Lighthouse follow the left wall to the beach, dodge around the few spawns near the wall..about halfway up the wall you will find a lone sailor. I cant remember his name,but he is a live mob.
