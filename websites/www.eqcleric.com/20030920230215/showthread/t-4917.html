Shadowfrost
Powerlevelling casters is harder than powerlevelling melees.

The rule is that a character will gain experience if they are 2/3 of the level of the highest character in the group. So, I recently agreed to help a friend level her 40 enchanter; the way I did it was to stick the enchanter in a group with my level 60 monk and clean out the spiders in Velketor's Laboratory.

The enchanter didn't do anything really except for hasting. Effectively, the monk soloed the spiders and the chanter leached some experience. She got 41 in under an hour.

From your point of view, your enchanter is 32, and will gain experience when grouped with someone at level 48 or less. My advice is to drop the enchanter in little experience groups around the Overthere or Dawnshroud or somewhere equally mindless and easy until you hit level 38ish, at which point you will be able to stick them in a group with the druid while the druid quadkites. That will gain you a lot more levels at a surprisingly fast rate.

An enchanter is the easiest character to p/l in my opinion. What you need to do is find the mobs that the enchanter has no problem with memblurring. one or more of his friends pull the mob to him and beat it down within an inch of its life. Have someone root it so it doesnt go into a death walk. The chanter memblurs after you back out of the fite. Then the chanter walks up and hits it once, it reciprocates and his pet jumps in and finishes it. Or if the mana is flowing he can dot it to watch it die as the group pulls another and works it down.

At 32 I would try splitpaw. Just go in a little deeper than the bedroom, maybe just past the bridge so the lower level toons can still experience hunt in the dungeon.

Mistmore castle could be interesting. Lots of mobs to pull not too far in. You would not want to use a pet there though. That enchanter pet could chain from one to another and pull a horrible mess back to the enchanter.

Nagafens Lair would be a little higher but if the charisma is good on the chanter it would be a great exp builder. Again the Kobolds run there when down to 20 so root is essential.

Often these old world dungeons are empty and can very useful to power level in.

This is probably not the best way but it worked for me to help a chanter friend that deleted his chanter out of frustration then later decided to start over. I used my druid to do it for a while then switched to my paladin for the taunting ability as the mobs got higher. Now my pally duos with that same enchanter when I play him.
