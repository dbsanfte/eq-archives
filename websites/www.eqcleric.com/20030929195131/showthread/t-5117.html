Dyschord
I have to disagree on that one.

I once worked as a glass ladler in a glass factory. For those unfamiliar with commercial production of stained glass, most glass is cooked at a temp averaging around 1400 degrees.

To ladle this glass, requires stamina, strength, and some coordination and timing. You use a ladle that can be anywhere from 2 inches circumference to 8 inches circumference. This ladle is attached to a steel bar that weighs about 60 lbs. After adding molten glass to said ladle, you can be chucking around 145 lbs at the most (For the larger ladles). You are doing this on a platform that has 4 furnaces all burning at 1400 degrees, so during the summer, this platform, with fans blowing fully, is about 130 degrees. (Furnaces are closed unless you are actively grabbing glass).

Now, I'm not a big girl, but to look at me you'd think I might be. I did this job for quite some time, despite the "boys" thinking I wouldn't last a week. I watched boys that boasted and puffed their chests out end up puking after 30 minutes in the heat. I watched some literally fall to their knees. Yet, I watched a 52 year old man do their job and never break a sweat. (He's who trained me to do it, in fact, so I knew his ancient chinese secret)

So to base stats on gender would be wrong, simply due to too many varibles.
