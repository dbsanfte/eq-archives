Q: When to use Complete Heal?

A: Every time you can.

Let me elaborate a bit on that.

There are five things that would make you choose one heal in preference to another:

(1) Efficiency, i.e. how many hp you heal per point of mana you spend.
(2) Speed, i.e. how many hp per second you are healing.
(3) Saving lives, i.e. will the heal land before your target dies?
(4) Range, i.e. is your target so far away from you that you have to pick a long range heal?
(5) Aggro, i.e. will you be killed if you cast this?

You're avoiding using CH because of (5) - but CH is the best spell for considerations (1) and (2).

At level 51, you can keep your ~4khp tanks standing with Celestial Healing. It'll be fairly tough on your mana supply, and you'll probably need KEI to do it in LDoN, but it can be done. Celestial Healing is the only other real option compared to Complete Healing, costing as it does 225 mana to heal 748hp.

Let me give you the numbers behind what you're doing, though. I'm going to assume you have the following skills: Specialise: Abjuration (50) and Specialise: Alteration (200).

On the above assumption, Celestial Healing really costs you ~219 mana to heal 748hp, giving it a real efficiency of 3.42 hp healed per point of mana you spend. The alternative, which is Complete Healing, would really cost you 356 mana to heal an average of perhaps 2,500hp, giving it a real efficiency of 7.02hp per point of mana you spend.

In other words, mathematically, you're using twice as much mana healing as you need to.

You see, Complete Healing as a spell heals more hp per point of mana, and more hp per second (i.e. factors 1 and 2 above) than any other spell in the game.*

Now, as you gain more levels and you group with tanks with more and more hp, Complete Healing becomes the only viable option. At level 51 now, you're reaching the point where none of the other heals are good enough to keep your tanks standing without causing unacceptable amounts of downtime.

Playing a successful cleric in the level 50+ game absolutely requires a mastery of CH. Between levels 50 and 60 it should be the spell which you cast most frequently in your gaming life, no questions asked.

Casting CH will sometimes cause you to take aggro, and my advice will sometimes get you killed. You need to have Divine Aura memmed at all times so you can shed aggro when you need to, and you need to have a Root spell memmed at all times so you can park the monster and run out of range.

Even with the best tanks in the game, some heal aggro from time to time is unavoidable. The best advice I can give you is, wear equipment with a lot of HP! :)

*Nitpickers will note that there are two exceptions to this rule of thumb: Word of Redemption - the level 60 group CH spell - can heal more hp per point of mana than CH if you're in a group with a lot of people with a lot of hp, all of whom are taking damage; and Supernal Light, in the hands of a level 63+ cleric with the right AA and focus effects, can sometimes heal more hp per second than CH. Both of these cases are far more applicable to raids than to exp groups.

quote:
One thing you mentioned Shadowfrost was to buy gear with lots of HP's on it. I take it that wisdom must still play a very important part in your gear?


Well. Must it?

Personally, right now, I don't even look at how much WIS is on a piece of equipment. I add up the hp, mana, and focus effects/FT. More hp, more mana, or a better focus effect means I want it, otherwise I don't. If I could, I would happily lose 50 WIS for an extra 100hp.

Let me take a moment to explain why.

What WIS does for you is to increase your total mana pool. That's all it does; it doesn't increase the rate at which you regain mana, so unless you're casting from FM to empty, WIS means precisely nothing.

You're level 51. That means that 1 point of WIS is worth about 10 points of mana to you. (That's true until you reach WIS 200. Above 200 WIS, 1 point of WIS is worth about 5 points of mana.)

So, if you had WIS 210 and you gained a piece of equipment with +10 WIS on it, you'd really be gaining about 50 mana. Put another way, you'd be gaining about a quarter of the amount of mana you need to cast a Celestial Healing spell.

Yes, it's easy for me to diss the value of WIS when I'm at the stage of the game where I'm not exactly hurting for it, but when I was level 51 I had about 150 WIS. I got killed a lot less than most clerics though, at least partly because I also had a lot more HP than other clerics did, which meant that when I was being hit, the tank had another chance to taunt and the enchanter had another chance to mezz.

WIS is almost meaningless compared to hp. You're a plate class - think like a tank!

Jabbur's post mirrors my own opinion here to some extent. However, I would like to stress that you can't always set up a textbook situation on 'when' or 'where' to use certain spells. Especially with one of the most generic and utilitarian classes of spells: the heal. Most certainly there are certain encounters in the game that require the use of specific spells, strategies or avoidance of certain spells. All in all though, the determination of 'what' to use, 'when' to use it is a matter of experience, expertise and play style.

Succinctly, an experienced cleric who is a good player will, more often than not, choose correctly the spells that they will use to enable the party to survive. In almost every situation, the 'healing' strategy will change over time. Any experienced cleric has changed their heal strategy several times over the course of an exp/camp/loot group session. Buffs drop, party members cycle in and out and sometimes you just try something different. Either you screw it up and the session is over, or you hit upon a 'winning' strategy and help to enable the party to survive and have a good time.

My advice is to learn to recognize the capabilities of the members in your party. Got a warrior who's MA'ing and doesn't have appropriate gear? Then you better land a hot before combat and be ready to start a CH around 50% (or sooner). Tank is a veritable walking warehouse of Monty-Hall gear: Relax and pop a HoT now and then. Tank can't keep aggro but the shammy doesn't care if he gets beat on: Pop a HoT (be sure not to overwrite his/her own HoT's) on the shammy before combat. In other words: Your choice of strategy will be chosen for you dependent upon the capabilities of the group and the mobs your are facing. Experience will guide you eventually into developing winning heal strategies for various situations that go along with your playstyle.

Be advised; everyone plays differently. However, our 'good cleric' profile has but one criteria: Enable the party to survive and do something they would have otherwise been unable to achieve without a good cleric.

p.s. In order to more directly answer the question "When to use a CH?" I will respond thus: In a regular exp session or camp, try to time your CH so that it lands when the target is below 40% hp. You will avoid a lot of additional aggro this way. Always keep DA or DB up (or both) and never attack a mob that is putting the party at risk by deciding to beat on you from heal aggro. Wait and let the MA get it off ya or root/DA/atone and step back from melee range when you're getting whacked. Aggro will not only kill you but, once you are gone, can condemn a party to certain doom and a long CR.
