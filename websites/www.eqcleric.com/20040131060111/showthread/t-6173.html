Varyx
There seem to be a lot of flawed arguments flying around here.


Argument: The sale of platinum is directly responsible for platinum cheats and hacks.

This is obviously false. As mentioned (ironically in a post against selling platinum) other games exist that have no economy and no translation to American dollars. In these games (for example, Half-Life) cheats are rampant. I would go so far as to say they are on some order of magnitude more abundant than in EverQuest.

We already know for a fact that people value EverQuest platinum as highly as they do the American dollar, at a certain ratio. Therefore, platinum must hold some intrinsic value to buyers (i.e. there would be no market if everyone was buying platinum with the future intention of converting it back to American dollars). People are willing to steal American dollars, it is therefore reasonable to conclude that people are willing to steal (hack, cheat) EverQuest platinum, regardless of relation to American dollars.

Getting rid of the sale of platinum to American dollars will not halt the flow of cheats and hacks into EverQuest. I doubt it will even slow it.


Argument: Buying and selling platinum hurts the EverQuest economy.

How? In the arguments against buying and selling platinum, this is somehow taken as a given rather than a point that needs to be proven. If this holds true, then gifts of platinum would also be detrimental to the economy. We would have to argue that giving 5k to a friend to help him buy a horse is wrong. I doubt anyone here would be willing to argue that.

In fact, the purchase of platinum ultimately reintroduces saved coin back into the economy. I know several high end players that are sitting on hundreds of thousands of platinum with no intention of doing anything with it. All their gear is nodrop, they own the highest end horse... what could they reasonably do with their platinum? Giving their platinum to a middleman who would distribute to players willing to spend that platinum would simply reintroduce coin into the economy that belongs in that economy. The fact that American dollars are exchanged and that the middleman is making real profit is irrelevant to the EverQuest economy. Certainly no one would fault a high end player going into the bazaar and giving away his money on a first come, first serve basis... why when American dollars are added to the equation is this suddenly an offense of morality?


Argument: Buying platinum leads to a less skilled playerbase.

Better platinum only means a player can buy better gear. This won't effect their relative skill level one way or another except in the natural consequence of faster leveling. To this end, the purchase of characters is much worse as the time to level is zero rather than just less. To illustrate this, how many of you, when meeting an extemely inept player that doesn't know the first thing about his class, assume that the person bought the character? How many of you assume that the person leveled the character from level one but with bought platinum which thus enabled him to level faster? The fact is, even without the problem of buying and selling platinum *or* characters, we will still need to deal with players who are twinked, powerleveled, or flat out don't understand the game.
