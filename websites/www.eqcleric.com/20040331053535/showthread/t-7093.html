Shadowfrost
Depends where you are in the game.

If you're level 20-40 or so, then adventures really are the way to go, but if you're 40+ then you can get faster xp elsewhere. The exception is if you're doing a Hard adventure; Hards really are stellar xp for most people.

Once you're past the mid-fifties it's worth going back to LDoN, not really for the xp but because the points you gain can be used to work towards some worthwhile gear.

Skywarrior
I think it varies a lot depending on your level and the dungeon itself. Tak is notorious as about the most difficult of the dungeons.

The trick with LDoN is getting in a group that can finish a given dungeon in about one hour and is willing to do 2-4 dungeons in a row. The xp at the end of that time will be better than just about anything you could get somewhere else, barring power levelin

If the group is finishing in significantly less than an hour you probably should consider doing hard setting. If you are finishing in significantly more than an hour (or not finishing on time) chances are good you have a wide spread of levels in your group with at least some (if not all) of your dps at the lower end of the group level spread. This is a more significant problem once you get into the 50s and up but is still a noticable factor even in the lower levels. At its most extreme, you might have a group where all of your support classes are at the upper level end with all of your dps at the lower end. This will create a situation where the mob level is high enough relative to your dps that the group will have a difficult time completing the dungeon quickly, if at all. The ideal group, of course, is one where every member of the group is the same level. After that, having a tank that is one or two levels above the rest is good.

The most difficult adventures I've ever been on were ones with a seven level spread and me (cleric) as the highest level.

SubRock
I almost always form my own groups in LDON. If you do this, you can fill your group with higher level players. Making yourself the lowest level ensures you of the best xp. Also, when you choose your group makeup you don't get into groups with 5 casters and a monk to pull and tank.

XP is not the only factor to consider in doing LDON adventures. There are some great items to be had with the adventure points (AP) that you earn in LDON. There are Flowing Thought II items and all manner of armor and spells. For the time invested, LDON is not a bad option.

LDoN is all about the clock and NOW NOW NOW! You can't stop, you can't wait, you certainly can't leave. If I have a phone call, I can muddle along in a normal exp group, just casually hitting heal macros, but I need to move in LDoN, and sometimes pull, which often gets the person on the phone angry at me when I'm not focusing on their conversation. If I have something that requires complete attention? Forget it, I'll wait until the adventure is over in an hour, even if it's coming down to the table for dinner.

And then once a group has you, and everyone else wants to do another adventure, but you want to leave, you look like the bad guy when you say "sorry, I got to go to bed. I live on the East coast and I have an 8 o'clock job." When you don't have a reasonable excuse and just don't feel like continuing, its often easy to have your arm twisted...and you can't look for a replacement, because the replacement can't join until you finish an adventure. So often they won't come when you say "we only have 8 more drops left."

For the people that find the timing works out well for them, great, but I simply do not find the gaps in my schedule that are the right size for adventures.
