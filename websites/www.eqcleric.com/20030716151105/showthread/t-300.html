No matter what people might say, Clerics aren't made to solo. Sure, you can root and nuke. Sure, you can twink your mighty cleric at the lower levels so he will have enough mana to do these things--and with C3, it should be more than possible for you to do this. But, the reality of the situation is, Clerics are made for grouping.

At lower levels, you should be able to find a group no problem, so long as you don't hang out in places where clerics initially are. For example, when I started a Barbarian Warrior, it was nearly impossible to find a cleric to help out--or a shaman for that matter. I guarantee that a cleric who was available was able to group with peeps very, very easily. Hell, it got so bad that I was grabbing rangers who saved their mana for heals.

Anyway, that's my bit, but I'll add more when I can.

I have solo'd. I'll tell you where. First of all, I will say its boring. However, there are situations where its appropriate. Imagine a Saturday, you pretty much have the day to yourself. Your doing laundry, talking on the phone, cleaning house...whatever. These are times its ok soloing a cleric. Two things you MUST know going in. 1) It won't be exciting. 2) I won't be quick easy XP. Below are places I solo'd, and felt as if I did pretty well. However, in all cases, I would have been better off grouping.

Level 1-5. Anywhere killing anything.

Level 7-10ish. You can do ok against skellies and such in the upper floors of befallen...if your server still has the old befallen, like CT does.

....Thus ended my soloing days for a while. Just not worth the effort.

26ish-30. Dalnir, in the entrance room, at the zone. Root nuke. I did this with no mind buffs for about 3 levels. It was one mob at a time, but at level 26 with the XP mod you will get almost a full blue per kill. Probably the most "economic" zone I soloed in. Just remember, hang at the zone and "cut bait" and run if you get wandering adds. You may can control an add, just will slow you down in the long run.

Insert many levels of little soloing.

40-46ish. You can do pretty well just soloing the "Tree Spec" in ferrott. Only thing to remember is don't let him wander toward the cave where the PoF portal is...you will get at least one add if he does. He is pretty much on a 5 minute timer and if you don't have two many nukes resisted you can pretty much med to full between each spawn. At worst, you'll get behind and have to stare at him a moment.

49-50 Spectral Keepers in TT. I solo'd these without too much problem. Generally you'll have enough mana to take two down, then will have to med. Only pull the keepers...don't mess with commanders and such. There is a whole area of the zone full of them near the NPC you turn in to for your Seb key.

I'm 54 now. None of the above was "fast" or overyly "fun". However, it all cases the XP was better than hoping for a group.

I recommend grouping at all times, however, if you have stuff to do, and you can't committ to a group, there are a few ideas.

I know that Clerics are all about grouping, and looking out for our fellow man (or troll, or ogre, or frog...)

But you certainly CAN solo, and solo well! The first thing to consider is that before you reach level 20 -- you're basically a Paladin, with better offensive and healing spells, with considerably better buffs. Levels 1-24 are a great time to work on your 1HB, offense, an defense skills. Once you get into the mid-twenties through mid-thirties, you start hunting around for undead, and beat them into submission with your hammer!

You have an awesome crowd/mob control spell in the Calm/Pacify line, can stun mobs - averting damage, with Stun and Holy Might, and get fairly efficient nukes in the Strike/Furor/Smite/Wrath line. If you're going after the undead baddies, you really step up to bat at level 24 and 29 when you're getting Dismiss Undead (very efficient) and your undead DOT. Keep in mind you also have invisibility vs. undead to stalk your prey and find a good space pulling spot while you're in undead-infested localities.


Truth be told, if you're already a middle-aged or senior cleric, it might be extremely difficult to work your offense, defense, and 1HB skills up to the point of being supremely effective as a melee offensive character. Partnering up with another Cleric of similar interest (or two) could allow you all to work up your melee/defense and stay alive at the same time!


I enjoy my Battle Cleric, and I hope through good mob selection to remain as a "mostly" solo character. There are times to group and heal our fellow man, but we can -choose- when to be the faithful healer, and when to be the undead ruling hellions we were designed to be!
