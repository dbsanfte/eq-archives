Kamiff
Now I do not recommend this for everyone, but...

If you have a good, well equipped grp in LDoN, since the missions are timed and cutting through mobs quickly is critical, our group heal spells work like a charm. Let me explain:

Over the weekend we did an LDoN adventure and I was grped with an all 65 grp of guildies. We are fairly well equipped (elemental access as of Sunday) and the group consisted of two beastlords (RL husband/wife team) a monk, a mage, a chanter, and myself. At the start of the mission while the bst's were buffing their pets I told the group, "Hey, here's an idea, the three melee all fight at one time and I use grp heals to keep everyone's health up" We tried it and it worked great. Monk and two beasts fighting three seperate mobs at once, chanter mezzing any adds (very rare) and me using grp heals. Chanter would slow the monks mob while the beasties slowed there own mobs, mage rotated his pet around the various mobs until dead, etc, etc. We finished a mission in less then an hour. It was the Guk zone we did so lots of undead and I was using undead DoT, and whacking mobs with my hammer as well. If you do this, VOQ is mandatory, and both beasts I was with have their AA's in Paragon which helps A TON, as well as eating mod rods like they are candy.

Like I said, not recommended for all, but something that can make for a crazy, fast paced adventure.

Two tactics Ive tried. Remembering that at level 52 I am not equipped in elemental gear and I dont have an endless mana pool.
First is to chain stun mobs. This works well as long as you direct the knockback. Very little healing needed on tanks and generally it saves mana. But this is pretty situational.
Generally I use HoT. Once per fight (depending on the health of the tank) ill HoT, the rest of the time Im adding damage by using nukes (not DoTs, mobs die too quick for DoTs). Pretty rare that I will use a CH in LDoN and my mana very rarely gets below 50%.

No, but it's a way to get through the adventure faster.

I've tried this technique, and the one thing that saves time is that the tanks in your group don't have to keep spending time pulling agro off of you, the cleric.

By using tickheals, which work very, very well during these encounters, you reduce the agro drawn on yourself compared to Complete Heals, or fast heals like Supernal Remedy/Light.

If you have Divine Arbitration, which any cleric should have when possible, you can easily use it to balance the health of your group, then cast tick heals to get their health to full.. You save mana, and you avoid agro.

In all the adventures I've been a part of using this particular approach we've always finished the goals with time to spare.

If you have a mage in your group make sure to annoucne the minute you use the last charge on your modrod so you can get a new one. Always nice to have should you suddenly need that little boost of mana.

Make sure you have KEI or better before going in. This is a must. There is no using mounts since these are dungeons of course. Sit as often as you can, but beware during incoming mobs.

This is another reason for using tickheals and trying to limit agro. You need to med and you need to med as much as you can.

Don't be afraid to use your Marks either. The help adding DPS as well as a heal bonus to the tanks. Try to throw in your hammer pet when you can for a little added DPS.

Try to keep a nuke/stun handy, and if you're going into undead areas it doesn't hurt to have your undead nukes/damage over time spells handy.

Meleeing is also an option if you're not hurting for mana, and the combination of Yaulp/Summoned Hammer adds a lot to a group, especially when you combine it with your marks and hammer pet.

Good Luck and Stay Safe Out There!
