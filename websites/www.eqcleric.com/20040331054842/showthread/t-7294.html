Meatwaggon
Perhaps your guild is complaining about you going oom all the time because you don't sit down. ever. lol.

Learn to sit. Even in LDONs you can sit most of the time. If you find that your mana pool is going down significantly after each pull instead of regenerating back to full or nearly full, either your puller is pulling too fast, or you are not being mana conscious.

I don't know if it is the case with you, but alot of clerics I have come across have an issue with sitting. "I DON'T sit. I am a BATTLE cleric" or "My FT is too uber to bother with something so newbie as sitting." Pride cometh before the fall. A cleric's main job is to heal his/her teammates, NOT to contribute measely DPS at the cost of mana regen. And don't fool yourself, standing mana regen with yaulpVI is not at all comparable to sitting your asscheeks on the dirt. If you are too uber to deal with the mundane task of healing, then it's time to pick another class.

That is not to say a cleric should never swing that hammer (or throw that nuke), of course. There are times and places. But not if you have to sacrifice your mana pool and having to call oom all the time.

That is also not to say that a cleric should always be sitting either. There are times and places where you should not be sitting, whether you are swinging or not. During some of these times, a mount is beneficial. Like during a multi-pull without effective CC or a boss that auto-aggroes anyone sitting.

BTW, the only benefits of having a mount is aggro-less mana regen and spell-casting mana regen. If you are fighting or moving while on a mount, you do not get the sitting regen rate (and you can't cast Yaulp V or VI while you're mounted either). If these two reasons are good enough for you to get a mount, then by all means. They were good enough for me.

The mana lost by Yaulping is noticable, even at higher FT.

With Yaulp VI I gain 10 a tick (subtracting off cost to cast) for 49.

Sitting/mount I gain 26 a tick instead for 65.

So I get about (49/65) = 75% the mana meleeing that I would sitting.

Obviously the more mana regen effects you have the less of a difference the 14-16 mana lost from meleeing matters, but every bit helps especially when you're fighting a tough boss or area.
