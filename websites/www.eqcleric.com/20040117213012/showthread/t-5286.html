Battering -

I think that a cleric has a spot in melee under certain situations. Obviously, if the mob is at 30% or lower, I dont see how the cleric could steal the aggro from the MA at this point. There are a lot of factors though. Do you need to train in 1H or 2H Blunt? Did you heal the MA (if needed) before you started melee? Did you ask first before going into melee? Some people are just uncomfortable with it, and after an incident, I do realize it.

I was grouping with a 59 Druid, 65 Monk, and a 65 Necromancer in Great Divide, killing the wurms in the tunnels, when I decided "Hey, I'm level 50, I have some great tanks with me here, why dont I just jump in and melee?" Well, somehow the necromancer ended up getting aggro'd, I'm still not entirely sure how that happened, perhaps I aggro'd some mobs over to us and they ended up hitting the necro somehow? However, the next thing I know, I looked up, there were 3 wurms, all 3 of us were basically tanking our own mobs, and everyone slowly but surely losing hit points, and I am unable to heal becuase the wurms are knocking the crap outta me, so we had no choice except to succor out. Luckily the druid was not in combat, and was able to do that. Needless to say, the necromancer was a bit disappointed that I entered melee, and asked me to stay out of it. Heh

After that incident, I was pretty shy about doing melee, but once I reached mid 50's, when you finally get the Hammer of Judgement spell, you get a weapon that actually does some good damage, and when combined with Yaulp V, you can attack pretty fast. So, I decided since my 1H blunt and Offense were basically at newbie levels (only around 60 each), I made sure to ask the group before I entered melee, and the MA was fine with it. So, I have come to the conclusion that if you see that your group can handle the mobs, make sure it's ok with the rest of the group if you "practice your 1H blunt, and offense", and generally that's an accepted reason for a cleric to melee. I was doing melee and pulling very little aggro in IC camp at Plane of Valor on the razorfiends. Cast unswerving hammer, sit back and heal until the mob gets down to 50% or lower, then jump in and melee some, making sure to do a CH or a HoT onto the MA before you begin, that way you can ensure the safety of the group, and everyone gets what they want. DPS, practiced skills, healed MA, dead mob :)

I still dont think that clerics are very well at melee, unless they've trained their skills high enough. (NOTE: be sure to melee as much as you can at earlier levels, because finding a group that will allow you to melee is less common at higher levels. Either the mobs hit too damn hard, and your mana and meditation is more necessary, or, the simple fact that clerics aren't the best soloers, so, just be careful, and considerate of the group)
