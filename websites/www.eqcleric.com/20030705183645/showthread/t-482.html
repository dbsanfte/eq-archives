Isig
Ok, for those younger ones out there (like me) who don't have complete heal, I'll pass on what I understand. It's basic, but hopefully it will help.

Don't heal too early. Many young clerics that I've seen start healing the moment the tank gets below 4 bulbs of health. This is too early. The tank hasn't built up sufficient aggro by then to keep the mob in his face. As a general rule, don't heal a tank before he's 50%. This goes for all lower level groups. I don't know about high-level groups, because I haven't got there yet.

Know the percent of HP healed on your tank--for all your party members for that matter--for each of your heals. By the time you have greater heal (level 24) a tank should have about 1000 to 1200 HP. Your GHs will heal roughly 25% of his/her HP. A regular Heal will restore about 1/2 a bulb. Keep this in mind when healing for a couple of reasons. Obviously mana conservation is one, the other is that it keeps aggro down. I for instance, do not try to restore a tank to full health during a fight. I usually hit him every time he gets to 50%. This will bring him back up to about 75%--a comfortable range in my opinion. It also keeps me from gaining aggro by popping him twice in a row. I have found that by doing it this way, I don't gain aggro by sitting down. I usually either get it right away or not at all. If you are worried, then by all means, let a tank hit the mob 2 or 3 times before sitting. Just don't forget to sit.

Top him off between rounds. As has been stated previously, top off the tank between pulls. If the tank is not on a mob, then there's no hate list for you to worry about.

Create a "help" macro. This sounds narcissistic and corny, but create a macro to let the group know you are being attacked. While this isn't a specific aggro reducing technique, I've never met a cleric who hasn't caught aggro. (If you are one that hasn't, please let me know how it's done).

In the heat of battle (especially with multiple mobs) sometimes you can be attacked without anyone noticing it. You may feel you're screaming like a little girl, but hey, if the cleric bites it, there goes heals and resurrections. Basically, there's a good chance the entire party wipes with you down, and you aren't even there to rez the group. This forces more down time and can even lead to a group break up as frustrations rise while everyone is attempting to get back to the group, etc. My macro is a simple "MOB ON ME!" I've toyed with using "%t ON the CLERIC" and other such examples, but found that the target more often than not is a party members (tanks) name. The thought was to alert the group as to WHICH mob is on me during a multi-mob fight, but to be honest, it's not the easiest to target a mob before hitting the macro. The extra hit or two you take may mean the difference between healing another day and meeting Brell in person.

If anyone finds fault with these general guidelines, please speak up. I am always looking for a better way to do something.

Shadowfrost
My god, don't listen to these people who don't keep root memmed. Root is an excellent tool for aggro control, since while the critter is rooted, we know precisely who it will attack. Once you're rooting, the main tank steps forward, everyone else steps back, and hey presto, one perfectly-controlled monster, ready to be slowed, debuffed, and then converted to xp.

If your melees do not know this, explain it to them, because as melees they do need to understand about root.

If you're having trouble with gaining unnecessary aggro, root the critter and step back. If you're having trouble channelling the root, then stun it before rooting.

Tip: If you want your enchanter to fall in love with you, root critters which are mezzed just before your tank breaks mezz.

Further tip: At higher levels, you have access to two root lines: the `immobilize` line and the `paralyzing earth` line. The `immobilize` line is fast-cast and low-resist; the `paralyzing earth` line holds for rather a lot longer. But there's a lot to be said for your old level 5 root spell... it casts amazingly fast and it's easy to channel.

At level 65 I regularly use root in experience groups, and I even have it memmed on many raids.
