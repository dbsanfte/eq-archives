With the Ldon zones all located in old world Manastones became again priceless for clerics IMHO... little calculation to illustrate:

5460hp (buffed) cleric manastoned down 5460/ 60 =91clicks * 20 mana = 1820 mana - 400 (for CH) = 1420mana gain

Time : manastone is instant click .. CH takes 10 sec

rinse and repeat *4 = FM cleric in little over 40 sec


Also, with the use of the Faithstone (right-click 30 second cast self gates to home temple) a soloing or even grouping cleric with manastone can :

Bind in an expansion zone near where they hunt if they die or get OOM
use the faithstone back to their temple (old world!) Manastone up to FM
Gate back to their bind point, and continue the hunt.

Restandre
Manastones used to drop off the Evil Eye in live side of Lower Guk. When Verant realized how ridiculously over-powering they were to clerics with Complete Heal, that could "cann' themselves to an inch of their life, and heal it all back instantly, they took it out of the game. Unless you are playing on one of the original servers, there are no mana stones where you play.

That said, if you do somehow come across one, they only work in EQ classic, in zones that were never altered. This does not include the Hole; this does not include CT. Basically it does not include anywhere that a leading-edge player might have been since Kunark was released.

What Nellal is saying, and makes it useful, is that while the manastone will not work in Takish-Hiz, it will work in Oasis of Marr. You can leave your Mistmoore adventure and pop out to Lfay real quick to "med" to full. I'm not sure how practical this is - most of my LDoN groups wouldn't wait for me to run back to the entrance, zone out, and come back a minute later - but it definitely is interesting.

First i am talking about hard ldon adv only .. not the normal setting once.

Having manastone does speed things up.
Instant click is not a pain at all.
Zoning out and back if you hapen to die on hard adv is well worth it (taking 30 sec to get to full mana instead of few minutes may be the diff between loose and win)
I dont run OOM often but it does happen occasionally on hard adv as it also depends on the group. I have been in groups (guild and with friends) where i never run below 80% with me maleeing.. but then i have been in those (pick up mostly) where i did run LOM. Sometimes you just have to work with the group you got.

But i want to adress something here that Lenne hinted:

Cleric running OOM is not necessarily a result of low mana pool, low mana reg, or bad mana management although that can be the case. It is not the case with me. A good cleric does not OFTEN run OOM but to say that a good cleric NEVER runs OOM is rediculous!!!
It all depends on the intensity with which you play .. if you take things easy.. take pulls slower .. on hard adv group with EP geared ppl (or highly twinked if you are a lower level cleric) and only play with friends and never with strangers than sure you may never run LOM because the situations you are presented with are controlled most of the time. Does this make you great? I think not.
Great cleric is the one that can act fast, does not panic, and can keep group alife when things get though (through crowd control and healing). If you put yourself in those situations often enough (in order to be challenged) you will run into those moments (not all the time but you will) when you will get LOM if not OOM. The adrenaline rush i get from those situations and the satisfaction that i can handle anything thrown at me ( not matter how bad the group is) is why i play this game. It is why i play a cleric.
