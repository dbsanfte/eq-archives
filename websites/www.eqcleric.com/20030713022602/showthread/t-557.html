Shadowfrost
2750hp for 600mana isn't going to get anyone overexcited about the efficiency of Supernal Light. In terms of hp healed per point of mana, SL is horrible.

But, CH heals 7,500hp in 10 seconds, or 750hp/sec. SL heals 2,750hp in 3.25 seconds, or 846hp/sec. In other words, running with just BoR up, SL heals more hp per second than CH - and it's the first of our spells that does that.

Plus there's the fact that CH only really heals the number of hp your tank has lost. Most times it doesn't land for full effect, while SL usually does, which moves SL further ahead of CH in terms of hp per second.

The real key to this spell, though, is that focus items work with it. Once you have planar healing adept, a healing focus item and a spell haste focus item - and there are tradeskill-made focus items for these things which will be readily buyable once more tradeskillers are in the elemental planes - it really comes into its own.

It's mainly useful for PoP boss encounters where the critters have massive and unpredictable burst damage. Massive burst damage requires massive burst healing, and that's what SL is all about.
