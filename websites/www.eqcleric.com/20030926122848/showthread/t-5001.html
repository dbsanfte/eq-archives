Voelfgar
You don't have to up your int. Tradeskills use the higher of your Wis or int. So in our case, it would be Wis. I would try to have your Cha as high as you can and buy and sell in your home city for best possible prices. Every plat counts.

Now I cannot confirm this, but I have read that the smithing books can affect your success ratio.

Start off with 21 pts in Smithing if possible,

make metal bits, then metal studs. This should get you to 35. You can sell the studs to tailors' for their studded armor

Then make metal rings but save them, you will use them later. I them made toolboxes and sold them to the newbie gnomes. This got me to around 52. At this point I made silber tipped arrowheads which is trivial at 72. you could also go the route of HQ rings or lanterns. With the rings of all qualities, these are needed later on in making chain jointing, which you can sell to other smithys' in making their cultural armor.

Now that you are at around 72, you can either go ahead and make the chain jointing or go for the banded bracer, which will get you to 95. The banded helm to 106 and the banded tunic to 117.

From here it is pretty much just skill grind

122 ornate chain bracelet
128 ornate chain gloves
135 ornate chain leggings
142 ornate chain Mail
148 ornate gold chain leggings
155 ornate gold chain mail
166 fine plate collar

At this point you can stay with the fine plate or you can start with some cultural armor, Dwarven chain followed by dwarven plate.

this will get you close to a skill level of 200.

Hope this helps
