restandre
If I didn't understand how this worked, I would be confused by now.

Once you have 6 AA points spent, you will become a baron or baroness.
Once you get 12 more points that you spend in the archetype tab, you will become "venerable". Points spent in general don't count.
Once you spend 24 points specifically in the class tab, you will become an exarch.

This title floats in front of your name and automatically shows up. You've probably all seen somebody with Baron Bill Smith or whatever. Other classes do not use the same titles, except for Baron/Baroness.

If you really wanted to know, other class titles are
Bard: Baron(ess), Veteran, Virtuoso/Muse
Beastlord: Baron, Venerable?, Elder
Cleric: Baron, Venerable, Exarch
Druid and shaman: Baron, Venerable, Elder
Enchanter, magician, and wizard: Baron, Master/Mistress, Sage
Monk: Baron, Brother/Sister, Sensei
Necromancer: Baron, Sage, Lich
Paladin and SK: Baron, Sir/Lady, Duke/Duchess
Ranger: Baron, Veteran, Hunter
Rogue: Baron, Veteran, Marauder
Warrior: Baron, Veteran, Marshall
