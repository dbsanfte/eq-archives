Shadowfrost
What originally drew me to playing a dark elven cleric? Definitely AD&D.

One of the best memories of playing AD&D that I have is from around twenty years ago. (Yes, I'm old, I know.) We were playing through the "Against the Giants" modules, and this was back in the days when they were sold as three separate little booklets. After many weeks of play, we'd finally made it into the depths of King Snurre's dungeons.

Bear in mind that nobody had ever even heard of the Drow at this point. To us, Elves were good and nice, Tolkeinesque figures; an evil elf was unheard-of.

It was then that the rogue in the party, scouting ahead, encountered the Drow Priestess, Eclavdra. Now, at the time, we had the best DM that I've ever met, and our DM managed to bring the character of Eclavdra totally to life in our minds.

And that's what Shadowfrost was when I rolled her. An old DM's interpretation of Eclavdra, re-interpreted by me.

What keeps me playing a cleric now, 175 days /played later? I like it.

I mean, the class suits me. I'm an intensely social person and I like to chat, a lot. Being a cleric gets me out into groups so I can meet people, and it's usually sufficiently undemanding that I can spend a lot of time talking. I've generally got several windows devoted to different chats that I'm participating in.

To me, that's why EQ has been such a success. The actual gameplay isn't all that great, really... but it's easy. Exp grouping, raiding, camping rare spawns, tradeskilling, none of these are hard, most of them are extremely repetitive, and most of the time they don't take up a lot of attention. What they do do is to give you something to look at and a few keys to press while you chat.

I suppose that to me, Norrath is basically just a huge internet chat room with fantasy-themed graphics.

To someone with that attitude, cleric is an ideal class to play, particularly compared to the classes which require a large number of keystrokes. I could never play a bard.

Braeaelin
/agree Keyera - That's one of the driving motives for me to play a cleric.

I began EQ last year as a Barbarian warrior. I fought in Everfrost, Qeynos Hills and West Karana. The cities I visited were Halas, Qeynos and once Surefall Glade. I didn't have any expansions at the time so I didn't see too many different races. The majority were Barbarians, Humans and sometimes I might see an Elf (this was before PoP mind you). I had different buffs generously cast on me at times and I always wondered what it was like to play a caster.

The day came when I upgraded to all expansions (last August). I had to travel to the bazaar to check that out so I made the l-o-n-g run to the spires in WK. When I arrived in the Nexus I was totally blown away by all the different races, levels and diversity! One of the most fascinating races to me was the Dark Elf.

Eventually the time came for me to make my first alt. It was going to be a Dark Elf but I wasn't sure what class to make although I wanted a caster. My cousin, who has been playing since the EQ release, suggested a cleric. I played the cleric for awhile but in the early stages didn't understand the role completely. In time and after reading many informative websites I learned what a cleric's part in EQ is (although I'm learning more and more each day thanks to you guys;)).

Around this time I picked up "alt-itis" and began many new characters. It wasn't until January when I was playing my shaman in Marus Seru when a friend of the monk in our group, who was a 65 cleric named Semirahge on Zeb, came and helped us out. She cast BoA on the group and stood by assisting us for around an hour. I was totally blown away by the power she wielded and since then I've been devoted to my cleric.

I just have the most fun keeping people going in the game. When I'm traveling and see someone sitting to heal, it always makes me feel good to throw a heal their way. And it's fun buffing low level players to help them out in their early lives. When there is a death, it makes me feel awful as well and it usually bugs me for quite a while after and I'm always thinking what I could have done to prevent it.

It took me a while to find my calling in EQ, but I finally realized I'm a cleric at heart.
