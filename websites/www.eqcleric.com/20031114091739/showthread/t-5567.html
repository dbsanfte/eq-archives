He loves the class, he loves playing the shaman class.

He runs low on mana constantly. Of course he can "canni" and he sits there and hits the button over and over and over....but even then its hard to catch up sometimes when he is casting Malo, Slow, dots...and then hes buffing, sta, agi, haste...I get in a group and the group wants HoV, no prob, 1/2 my mana, gone, ok, I med. He gets in the same group and gets tells and demands in group for everything in his spell book! I rarely get asked for anything else in a group other than heals and HoV.

I may be bias, but be nice to your shamans, yeah maybe this guy was a bit rude, but just because he can canni and gain mana back doesn't mean hes running FM all the time.

Think of it this way, your shaman buffs up everyone, canni's to get mana back, he drops in HPs. For shamans HP's = mana. The tank pulls, the cleric didn't heal the shaman, the shaman now needs to slow. Slow = aggro. A few hits and hes dead. A shaman can make or break a group if hes good at what he does. I have seen many a cleric die, and I have watched the shaman keep the entire group alive and the mobs controlled, even with several adds.

I heal whatever shaman is in my group. If he is using canni I wait until hes down low and then I hit him with a quick heal, even if it doesn't bring him to full HP's. Shamans like regen items, and they cast regen, he will probably heal in time in a couple minutes anyway if he just has a little health missing. They appreciate being healed, its one less thing they have to worry about. I wouldn't always assume because he has a heal spell as well that you shouldn't heal him. If hes getting hit, make sure your on him with heals, because hes probably pissed off that slow didn't land...or something...lol

Sorry for rambling its late, and I guess I am just bias to shamans and healing them and taking care of them. They do a lot for a group and a raid. I would rather go into a dungeon in LDoN without a chanter than without a shaman.
