Pinzarn
Few different terms being tossed around out there. While everyone might call it something different, here is how I view them...

Cheal rotation - A pre-determined number of clerics casting Cheal in a determined order with a pause between each for casting.

Spam Healing - Everyone casts a heal (not cheal) on the tank instead of setting up a cheal rotation. In *most* cases this occurs once the mob has become trivial (read as not worth doing the rotation), or the mob has some AE that makes the long cast time of cheal not practical.

Spot healing - The mob is hitting so hard and/or fast that there is a potential that the mob can kill the tank between cheal casts. Also used in groups to heal the tank (or others) while the cleric is casting cheal. The non-cheal caster will cast a heal to keep the tank alive for cheal.

On some mobs, it is necessary to use Cheal + spot or Cheal + spam.

90% of the boss mobs in EQ will have a cheal rotation. You'll find those random mobs that cast silence, stun, fear, shadowstep, etc. that makes the rotation ineffective, so switch to spam healing. Depending on the zone and era, sometimes you can "hide" the clerics from the AE.

Having someone on spot heal for the main tank is always a good idea. If it is the sole responsibility of that person on the raid, then even better.
