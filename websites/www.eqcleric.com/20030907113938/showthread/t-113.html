Sornae
Ah, chanters and clerics. Probably the most important relationship in the game. Having played both, and having read that post months ago when it was put up, I want to point out a few things.

First of all, it was a RANT. He/she got frustrated from dealing with that crap day in, day out, for many weeks I'm sure. I know it, because I went through it too. I've noticed that the problem with tanks calling "mez add" pretty much goes away sometime in the 40's. By then, they learn that most folks have either learned to to their jobs or have quit. Also - if you read the entire thread, the same person posted later that he/she doesn't explode like that in groups. There's only so much you can say or do in a frustrating group, and if people don't get the picture after one or two polite attempts, ya just leave. It's very simple.

As an enchanter, it is very seldom that I run into a cleric that will cast more than heroic bond on me - until I'm squished and need a rez. It only seems to be that AFTER this happens they're all, "oh, whoops. Guess I should buff you so it doesn't happen again!" Now, I'm not saying that I require a cleric to stop what they're doing and buff me, but if (most) clerics know that we get aggro a lot, and that we have very low hps/ac, it just seems to make sense to me that I could get a little help. At level 53, my best hp buff adds 150 hps, and my best ac buff is 25 ac. Whooptidoo! You guys have armour better than that. Secondly, not all clerics do this. I've met one or two that said to me, "You kidding? Enchanters should get buffs before everyone else." Honestly though, it's rare.

Now that you've been informed, please kindly consider your enchanters 900hps and 500ac next time you're in a group. Rune costs peridots, so they probably have some on them if you're worried about casting symbol. However, I think that knowing an extra hundred or so hps helped a chanter get a stun off to step away from a mob to mez it before getting squished is enough. It sure is for me!

As far as clerics being "lazy" by not having stuns up - that's a personal call on how you play your cleric. Rest assured that a good chanter keeps stuns up and will do their best. Yes, if there are 3 mobs beating on him/her your stun or root is much appreciated, but I don't think I mind when a cleric is more concerned with the group's health than they are crowd control. Help is nice but it's not your job, so don't stress it! I also happen to think that every caster that gets root should always keep it up. "Root and step" is one of the best lessons I've ever learned.
