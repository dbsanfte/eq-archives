** For the New Player **

- Find Feature added. This will create a mystical indication of the path to take to find certain people. This feature works in the Bazaar and in newbie zones and cities. In the Bazaar you just click on the merchant you seek in the Bazaar window and click the Find Trader button. For all other uses, just hit CTRL+F to open the window, select someone on the list and click the Find button. This will locate any Bazaar trader or any NPC with an alternate name, such as (Spell Vendor). The exception is that it will not locate NPCs in the Wayfarer's Camps.

- The auto-attack key now defaults to Q (not A).

- New characters start with their weapons and armor equipped.

- New characters start with their spells scribed and memorized.

- When a caster reaches the level required for one of the first two rings of new spells, they will get a message informing them that new spells are available.

- New characters will begin with a stack of bandages. These bandages cannot be sold or traded.

- When a character trains in a hotbutton-able skill that he did not previously have skill in, a hotbutton for that skill is created on the cursor.

- New characters will have a page of hotbuttons set for them based on the skills given to them by their class and race.

Maybe I'm a bit old fastioned, but I don't think it's all that bad of idea to have to figure some of this stuff out for yourself.

Was it really that big of a problem of playing the game that this stuff had to be done for you? The tutorial goes over this stuff pretty well, but people seem to skip this step.

I didn't even get to turn in my newbie note or scribe my new spells, I ended up at the bottom of the river feeding the pirahnas. (it was night and I stumbed into the lake) I learned a lot my first few levels. I didn't beg, I didn't whine. At level 25 I was still wearing some patchwork in some spots and banded in others and was damn proud to have earned it.

I think the newbie armor, weapon quests were a stroke of genius though, encourging new players to explore the areas around their own newbie zones in order to obtain them. For someone who would be completely new to EQ, these quests are definately a positive thing.

How many times have you been asked for money and equipment out of the blue from someone you've never seen before. Sure, every social demographic has a few that see no value in earning what you have, but it's starting to get to rediculous proportions.

Ok, I'm done rambling, thank you for listening if you've gotten this far.

upcomming changes for the newbies :

Help for new players - New characters will start with a bag. This will give them a little more storage space and the opportunity to learn how storage containers work earlier rather than later. We are also working on removing one of the more tedious "features" of the game, the need to practice Sense Heading. Most folks have that skill mapped to trigger along with one of their movement keys. Not an exciting skill to practice. So we plan to have all characters start with a 200 skill in Sense Heading. This will allow new players to navigate more easily, and will reduce a bit of tedium over the course of gameplay.
