AnsalonFV
Hello,

I've a general question about the cleric class. I play on Firiona Vie, where alot of "real" (ie. not rerolls) new players begin their EQ careers; and lately myself as well as other Paladins on the server (as well as paladins on other servers according to PoN) have come into a conflict with clerics using DA when they suffer damage.

Basically what seems to be the trend, Cleric gets to around 1 bubble of health and fires DA; unfortunately this happens as Lay Hands is attempted, so the LoH is lost; and the cleric is still low health and often dies due to blood aggro when DA wears off.

This is by no means an everyday occurence; but it's happened collectively to Paladins on FV and elsewhere to warrant an attempt at some sort of comproise.

What, from the Cleric perspective, would be the best remedy for this?
(besides the obvious dont let the cleric get hit, period)

Dulcinea

Qyadar
Since DA and DB are last minute save spells, this can and usually leaves us in the low hp aggro range. As clerics we know that we are gonna get chased by the mob as soon as it wears off. When this happens to me I get some distance from the mob, and start clicking my heal over and over until DA fades and then my heal lands. This usually takes me off the hate list and I can get back to healing.

IMO, you should save the LoH for while the Cleric is DAed incase the Tank needs a heal or possibly the chanter. If say the Cleric cant get enough distance from the mob then you and him/her can coordinate when to click off DA and to toss the LoH. DA is a reflex action for clerics and we do it when we get hit multiple times without thinking where as I found Paladins to be a more conservative with LoH saving it for the last few % points of health to use it. Therefore by the time the paly is about to LoH the cleric has been DA for a few seconds already.

The most important thing is when the cleric's DA has faded that the mobs are thoroughly pissed off at someone other then them so that they can bring the group back up to health.

Bottom line, ask the cleric in your group what they would liek to do for that XP session or raid.

Shadowfrost
Don't leave it until the last minute to LoH when your cleric's being thumped.

Unless you have LoH-boosting AA, you'll only heal 2500 with LoH in any case, which isn't all that much by the time you're in the planes. Either LoH your cleric at about half health, or (preferably) use that time to grab aggro off the cleric so she can heal herself.

But you need to plan for the fact that a cleric under two bubbles of health and still being hit, will be casting DA. It's a very safe assumption to make.

Since LoH is instant, 'we' as Paladins try to get the most bang for the buck from it in most cases. I've never played a cleric, and apparently despite the similarites between the Paladin and Cleric, there really doesn't seem to be much in common between the two except maybe the place where we may buy some of our spells.

As for grabbing aggro back - this is a big part of the LoH timer for us as well I think. During the time that the cleric catches aggro and the moment we'd normally use LoH - that's what we're attempting, and for the most part successful at until/unless the LoH/DA conflict arises.

Again it's not a frequent occurence to even lose aggro in the first place - generally in the rare instances where everything that can go wrong does go wrong, is the only time this even comes up - Unfortunately, those exact times are the worst possible times for it to happen.

That's the key! Communication communication communication!

I have a group of friends I regularly group with. Our tanks know they'll be getting a Cheal when they get to ~40%hps, sometimes lower, sometimes higher, depending on damage output. Pullers get Cel Heal to top them off before they pull if they're not full hps after a fight. Our Chanter knows he's going to be getting Cel Heal as soon as he starts getting hit, and he may get a sup heal on top if it, depending on how hard a time he's having mezzing the mobs (I have absolutely no qualms about over healing our chanter). I know if I'm getting beat on, I need to stand still and our chanter will mez what ever is beating on me, if the chanter is busy I can attempt a root and scoot until the chanter is free. If we get a bad pull, I watch my chat screen for the call from our chanter to zone or camp (He has "MERRY ZONE" and "MERRY CAMP" hotkeys, hehe), my group absolutely insists that I survive so I can rez quickly and get us back in the action. These are our tactics, they work for us, and we work very well together. Because we communicate!

I think your idea is a sound one, talk to your group before, during, and after fights to make sure that everyone is clear on what they are doing.
