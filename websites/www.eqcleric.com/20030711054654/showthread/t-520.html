Another spell question: Mark of Retribution? Lucy's says it is a Reverse damage shield. How does it work? Do you cast it on the mob or the tank? Also, how much reverse damage does it do?

AanyaLorien
You cast it on the mob. Every time the mob hits someone, it takes 15 (if I recall correctly) damage.

It upgrades to 34 damage with the 65 spell Mark of the Righteous.

I've always thought it fun to use. And every little bit of damage helps ;)

It was VI's way of cutting back on DS messages, as I recall.

Shadowfrost
With Mark of Karn, you need to understand that you're looking at a nerfed spell.

Back in Kunark, Karn used to heal useful quantities of hp. But then someone posted a log of a monk soloing something in Sebilis - memory tells me it was the Sebilite Protector - armed with a Mosscovered Twig and with the assistance of Mark of Karn.

Nowadays, of course, soloing the Sebilite Protector is nothing to write home about, but back in Kunark the Protector was a semi-raid monster with some pretty good loot.

Well, one of the results of that incident and the attendant whining was that the Mark of Karn spell was nerfed into near-uselessness and it has never been retuned since. (There were other results, including the nerfing of the Mosscovered Twig.)

Markof Karn is occasionally useful for stripping damage shields, although mostly it is cast as a breastplate clickie rather than memmed. Mark of Kings is, as far as I can tell, completely pointless, since it costs more mana without improving on Mark of Karn in any significant respect.

Incidentally, from a balance point of view, I'd suggest leaving Mark of Karn how it is and giving Mark of Kings some slightly more useful effect; personally I'd love it of Mark of Kings gave you some mana as well as some hp, which would be a nice boost to those clerics who like to melee.
