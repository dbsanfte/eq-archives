Lysisandra
ok so this may not be a revelation to anyone, but im new at this so ...
I found out that our stuns are pretty cool. Grouping with another higher lvl cleric in DL I had a lot of time and mana on my hands.
What I did was mem my 2 (or 3) stuns and then just chain stun the mob, I could usually keep it stunned at least half the fight lowering the heals needed on the tanks. This is in teh 30s stunning even yellow red mobs with about 60% success per cast. I think it really helped so I though i would just pass it along as a suggestion. maybe others can add info on stun
*NOTE: I cannot endorse this for soloing, and if you are the main healer I say save your mana for heals unless you are constantly FM*

Hannorel
I keep my 2 highest single target stuns (Force and Holy Might) memmed for most XPing situations. They are particularly useful against most caster mobs. Some mobs can't be stunned magically, as you may be aware, so keep an eye out for the warning message.

I don't ever keep my PB AE Stun memmed- that's just begging for more aggro than your HP can handle. I'd be curious to hear if any of the other clerics out there ever use it, and in what situations.

Penecillin
I find that if I don't keep the caster stunned all the time, the stun spells are often too slow to stop them once they start casting. Bash is also good to share with casting Mobs.

Braeaelin
Yep, if you're fighting a caster be sure to watch for it casting then let a stun off. I usually only have one stun up to slow a mob down if I haven't snared it before it runs but sometimes I'll have two stuns up. The recast time on them is fast enough to keep a chain stun going. Also, bashing with your shield is very useful as well.

I also use it when an enchanter is trying to mez a mob. This gives them a chance to get the mob under control without it chasing the enchanter all over the place.

Nexes
I keep PB AE stun loaded all the time when I am grouped. Especially in places where mulitiple pulls are common. One place it always worked out well for me was in Sebilis. I would use AE stun and it would help keep the tank and chanter going long enough for mez's and slow to make the fights trivial. Though sometimes I took a beating, more often than not it worked out well for me.

Dagis Tuckman
I have Stun, Holy Might, and of course Bash at my disposal in Highkeep (well, I did, I just rerolled) and I had trouble with the higher casting time on Holy to get it in on time. So I would use Holy to prevent anything, and use stun or bash when they started casting. Stun first, then Bash. My bash skill is(was) still low, so it wasn't 100% reliable, but really only would fail no more than 25% of the time. Like I said somewhere else, except for the occasional Malaise to get through, I could keep a caster from slinging spells pretty damned well.

I can't say I've ever used the AE stuns. I memmed them once in an AE group, but I never put them to good use.
