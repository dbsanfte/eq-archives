Divine suggested I should post this here, it was originally over at EQClerics (the original one with the "s" on the end). I hesitated because I was a little rough in what I said over there and those folks sort of know me and I can get away with it. So anyway, forgive the harshness.

If mana management in LDoN isn't an issue for you, then skip this posting. It doesn't apply. But... if you're holding up the group for med breaks, you might want to look this over.

I was a little disappointed with how I saw a lot of clerics performing in LDoN (my main is a rogue these days, but I still play my cleric of 4 years a lot). I think it's due to so many clerics leveling so quickly, not spending time in dungeons like in Kunark. I also think it's due to the insane mana regen from horse, VoQ, and whatever else, newer clerics don't really think about how to best utilize their mana. They just cast whatever spells they're used to casting until they're oom. I grew my cleric up in a time when we didn't have horses and the best thing we had was clarity, then later on C2. Not having tons of mana to throw around was typical.

Finally, I think a newly minted level 65 cleric with no archtype title, at least, is at a big disadvantage in a level 65 dungeon. The AA's like SCM and DvA are incredibly useful tools and can really help you stretch your mana out. I would urge you to slow down your progress and stick to AA's and level 60 dungeons until you've got SCM 3 and DvA.

I think that ideally a group in a level 65 dungeon should almost never have to wait because a cleric is low on mana. And I think with a little discipline, learning which of your spells are most mana efficient and won't get you killed with aggro, getting a few AA's, and acquiring your spells (especially the vendor ones) you should be able to avoid being the one slowing up the group. I can't help you with groups that are dysfunctional in other ways, but maybe these tips can help make sure you're not the one causing the problem.

I think the problem defined in one sentence is, there are some level 65 clerics out there with level 50 skills and spell books.

LDoN I think has really shown what poor habits many clerics have fallen into since there haven't really been any dungeon crawling since Kunark. So many sat in their 50-60's in PoP zones gaining a level every few days. Now they're crawling out of the woodwork and really showing a lack of skill I can't remember ever having seen from so many clerics.

Now granted, I'm an crochety old-timer and cut my teeth on dungeon crawling in HS, Sebilis, Droga, Nurga, old CT and a lot places. It was before KEI and before flowing thought and of course, horses. You had to consider all your spells and which ones were most effective for the mana and you WOULD go out-of-mana on big pulls and have to manage aggro and all kinds of things.

Now I'm grouping with random clerics that don't even have all their store bought spells, like Celestial Elixir and Etheral Elixir. Rather than using a 75 mana Mark of Karn/Kings they're letting meleers take 1000+ points of damage on damage shields then using 1100 mana Word of Restoration (or other WoR spell) to heal it up. They don't even have BAOTR or any spell costing more than a couple of plat in Bazaar. If there was one thing that was always a priority for my casters it was getting their spells. If that meant farming some plat, farming the spells myself, doing tradeskill combines and selling things like pre-made celestial essences, so be it!

Then after they've squandered their mana like that, they prounounce themselves oom. Well... no duh!

How about, rather than using WoR, use EE, at least it's a bit cheaper to cast. Or better yet, coordinate with the beastlord and/or druid or Pally and they can cast their group heal. Even better, mem MoK and use it to prevent all that damage shield damage. Don't use the one on your BP, it casts too slowly. MoK on incoming if there are lots of damage shields. Don't know if there are? Ask a meleer. They can see their hit points going down by 50 with every hit that is landing. I've heard stories of clerics yelling at meleers to not get aggro, when in reality the problem was a damage shield. Kind of ironic.

Here's some advice from Aunty Electrae

Buy SCM right away If you're leveling, stop leveling and put points into AA's. Go on the level 60 adventures rather than the level 65 adventures where you won't be as taxed.

Buy your spells! Time to take that 200-300 plat per adventure and put it into filling out your spell book. Make the trip to ShadowHaven, do the vendor quest and get your spells. Get your Kunark spells too.

Look up your spells (LUCY!) Learn how much mana each spell is and the casting time and see when it's best to use one over the other. Personally, I've almost never used the WoR line of spells, especially once I got Etheral Elixir. It's cheaper and doesn't have the aggro of casting 6 heals. But other clerics, ones with lots of mana and no mana/aggro management issues, might like to cast it.

Prevent Damage My rogue is sometimes taking 1000+ hit points of damage per fight from damage shields. Sometimes it seems like half the dungeon has a damage shield. Just use MoK and you can prevent most of the damage to non-tank meleers.

Use heal-over-times when the situation isn't dire. They're cheaper than chain casting divine/supernal light or chaining etheral remedy.

Nuke when you can if you're nearly FM contribute some damage and either nuke or melee (if you can manage it and not get too caught up into watching your procs versus watching your group).

Don't stealth AFK. Need I say more?

Respond when asked what your mana situation is.


A couple of other tips. Don't freak out about people's hit point bars not being 100%. If they're not the ones supposed to be getting hit, let them carry around a little damage until it makes sense to use a heal or a group heal (preferably etheral elixir). An exception to this is the enchanter, of course. Or if they're meleers and you're using MoK, eventually they'll heal up just fighting.

Anyway, that's sort of my lower-end clerics guide. Not really applicable to clerics with zillions of AA's or Elemental gear and FT 15. Hope it didn't offend anyone. :p

Rauri
An interesting post. As you say, most of the people that are here care enough to try to do things right and generally aren’t the problem. Nevertheless, your post has some solid suggestions for clerics approaching or recently achieving level 65.

Here are my thoughts (for whatever that’s worth):

1. It is my opinion that clerics have the greatest challenge with managing their mana properly; we have high cost spells and comparatively little mana regeneration (none at all until 56 and nothing very effective until 58 ). This is usually why you will see the cleric cry “OOM!” before other casters. As you say, this requires skill and is a key to being a successful cleric.

2. I’m a strong proponent of staying at level 60 or 62 to work on AAXP as you suggest IF you intend to use LDON. You are FAR better off being a strong level 60 than a weak level 65 where LDON is concerned.

3. Damage Shields: most of the LDON mobs’ damage shields are not overwritten by my Marks (Retribution and Karn for me). I believe (but don’t know for certain) that our Marks overwrite the Mage DS but not the Necro DS and almost all the mobs seem to use the latter. That’s why you should pull with Annul if a caster occupied the room (he’ll DS everyone around him).

Ok, those are the points where we agree. Now comes the part that might get argumentative: The fact is, if ANYONE in the group doesn’t know their job, is under-equipped or is generally a bonehead, the CLERIC will go OOM. A cleric out of mana doesn’t make him a bad cleric, but it probably means someone in that group is a weak player (maybe the cleric, maybe not).

Tanks with poor equipment, enchanters and bards that can’t crowd control, rogues that don’t know what the evade button does, necromancers that insist on pulling agro, shaman who refuse to ever slow, etc. are all cases where the group’s inadequacies force the cleric to expend extra mana to make up the difference. We have the power to make up for these shortcomings, but it costs A LOT of mana.

A good group monitors mana levels and adjusts their play as the adventure progresses. Is the cleric gradually losing mana? Help do something about it! Think about what you are doing that might be causing me to expend my mana too fast and correct it. In short, play YOUR class correctly and give the cleric a chance to play his with maximum efficiency too.

Actually Mark of Retribution does not overwrite damage shields, it adds one. It never has, but some folks get them confused. Mark of Karn/Kings does. And they work just fine in LDoN, outside of the occassionally resistant mob. As far as I know, it reverses ALL damage shields, but I'll pay a little closer attention next time to see if I'm wrong about that. I recommend MoK over annul because you don't necessarily want to dispell someone elses debuff or dot. Also, MoK is a wonderful pulling spell because it has so little aggro the warrior looking cross-eyed at the mob can pull it off you. Also, it heals up a bit of damage on those dual weilding meleers. However, if someone else is pulling with annul, that's fine too.

Like I said, I can't fix bad groups, but I can help fix bad clerics. Sometimes it's just a little tweaking of the spells and you'll find your mana goes a lot further than it did before.

Oh yeah, something I forgot to mention. Yaulp for when you can't sit. DvA on bad pulls so that you give the group more opportunity to get things under control before you start casting a bunch of heals and getting aggro.

stratys
/agree. I have never had Mark of Karn or Mark of Kings fail to land due because a mob had a certain type of DS, if it isn't resisted, it has always replaced the mob's original DS.

MoK is the best choice for removing a damage shield once the mob is in play, since there's no risk of dispelling snare or slow or other detrimental spells cast by your group. The suggestion, however, was to use an annul/cancel magic spell to pull with, and so there would be no such risk. Both MoK and cancel magic are pretty low-aggro spells. MoK has the advantage that it targets the DS specifically, but if there is no DS it will not debuff the mob (it will still aggro the mob); cancel/annul has the advantage that it can be used to take off haste/ hp/AC buffs in addition to damage shields, and you may experience less resists -- not sure on that end, but seems like they bumped up the resist rate on the MoK line awhile back.

These are all really good points. The truth is clerics have been spoiled with mana regen, horses, self buffs and Brell knows what....Thankfully I still remember the days when a Clarity or C2 was awesome and groveling for it was accepted. :)

There are 2 great aspects to LDoN that a cleric should live by; don't drag agro and don't run out of mana. Sit when you can, and gauge your heals as best as possible. DvA is a must here, it really is. So many players have excellent HP regen, so balancing their health is a good way to even the playing field a little. Then you can always toss a tick heal to whoever needs it.

I have never gone OOM in LDoN. I've been LoM from very ... interesting pulls, but never OOM. It's not rocket science but it might require you to take a good look at your playing style and perhaps rethink some of your approaches. This is especially true if older addons/dungeons are not familiar to you.

However, it's not all resting on the cleric. I've been in groups where 5 minutes into the adventure it was obvious to me we would not make it based on the other players. Maybe it's just me, but I find it more and more true that the players out there today, of the newer era, are lacking some of the skills and capabilities I remember so well back in the days.
