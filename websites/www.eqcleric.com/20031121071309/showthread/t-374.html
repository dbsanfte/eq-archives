FoL and mobs running is dependant on 2 things:
Do you have the most aggro?
Are you within it's melee range?

Speaking from my twink 60 beastlord's point of view I could successfully fear kite a mob provided I chain cast blind on the mob and my pet had an aggro lowering proc. If at any time my pet out aggroed me, the mob stopped running while blind.

Teaming up with a druid(or ranger) and snare did wonders. Druid chain snared (more aggro than FoL), sick my pet after a few snares were in, I would FoL and my beastlord, my pet and the druid would go to town on the mobs.

How this might apply to a cleric, I couldn't tell you other than possibly teaming up once again with a druid or ranger or using blind as a way to run to a safe place if whilst soloing you got into trouble.

Nhin Impious
Flash of Light is an awesome spell-- especially with the mana cost.

I've been soloing in Overthere off and on for 20 levels now with it (I'm 39); all you need to "blind kite" (comparing to fear kite) is a wide-open space and no mobs in a specific direction that will join agro. FoL is cheaper than Fear or just about anything else, so you can cast the following:

Stun spell (then back up to just within range)
Direct damage spell (mob starts incoming at this point)
Flash of light-- if the mob is on you at this point then STEP BACK
Direct damage spell
Wait...the mob's just leaving range. Even though all the cleric DD spells I've seen take a while to re-pop...here's the coincidence. The spell will re-pop to cast right before the mob comes back in range.
Direct damage spell
Flash of light and STEP BACK...lather, rinse, repeat.
Usually takes me three trips through this little cycle to nuke something down.

I also have a social set up for groups-- it casts Flash of light and says "STEP BACK and %T will RUN"... since most folks don't recognize the spell.
