quote:
Is the EQ Cleric's life still pretty much the same?


I feel like alot has changed.

The nukes have been slightly adjusted, so they're not horrid anymore. Its an option for you to nuke in a group.

Pacify line *works*. I may have been silly but I was always playing like it lasted the duration it said it did (42 seconds); so even with the recent "fix/nerf" I feel unaffected. I like this one the most because it created something valuable for a cleric to do in a 2 cleric or cleric/druid group. One of my LDoNs yesterday I was puller while the druid was main healer.

You have melee options, which you mentioned you were lightly familiar with. The hammer and yaulp lets you get up and bonk if you feel like it in a group (and can handle it). You can use it to farm tradeskill components. While you still probably won't solo for XP, you can spend AA and gear to augment this ability to pretty respectable levels, if thats your thing.

There are 2 buffs now that clerics have that no one else does. Vie line increases mitigation, and I find it very useful. Some raids might even try for a "Vie-Chain". There's a spell haste line, again useful.

Resurrections have more value with LDoN now. Groups with a rezzer recover from minor deaths faster, and the clock matters. Groups without a rezzer wait 30 minutes after the adventure to get their XP back.

quote:
Does that mean I won't really be able to hunt anywhere (in a group) unless I buy PoP, LoY and LDoN?


You won't be able to level to 65 without PoP; so thats going to be an issue. Also, the zones people do XP in are all PoP zones.

You don't need LoY. If you're going to skip one, this is the one to skip. Still, the expansion brings Maps and Bank Size.

LDoN is the best expansion ever released IMO. Anyone who wants to be nostalgic about Kunark or Velious is entitled to their opinion, but I haven't had as much fun in EQ ever as I do in LDoNs.

LDoN to me combines all the best times, like old style dungeon crawling, working together with what you have, sometimes some odd combinations. The timer forces players to be on the ball. LDoN ditches the worst aspects, too. There are no "grind camps" in LDoN. Adventures take 2 hours start to finish, MAX. Most can be done in about 1. If you've got to go, you can do so freely between adventures without grief.

Hope that helps :)
