Yes, to the first. No, to the second.

If you plan on taking up trades to make money, stop right now. You will not get rich with tradeskills. If you're trying to make money, your time will be better spent hunting the mobs that drop the basic components *used* in tradeskills, and setting up a bazaar vendor. Any level 1-6 toon can make a pretty nice chunk of change killing spiders and spiderlings, and selling their sillks.

My main has skill levels of 175+ in baking, tailoring, smithing, brewing and pottery. The only uses I've found for tradeskills are saving yourself some money when you're very young (which is only important if you're an untwinked main character), contributing to several good quests (which is really the only good reason to get into trades), and making high-end gear at the 250 skill level.

You actually can make money on the high-end items, and by selling your services to other players who aren't into trades, but the effort needed to gather the necessary components makes it not worth the trouble. And by the time you get your skill up that high, you'll be a high enough level that money will no longer be a critical problem (and, if it *is* still a problem once you reach the higher levels, you're still not going to solve that problem with a tradeskill).

Yes, pick a trade. No, don't expect to make any money at it. In fact, expect to *lose* money in the long run.

I spent maybe 20pp at the most to get to 188 brewing (but I spent a couple of hours doing a circle around Shadeweavers picking up stacks of paeala fruit).

Baking was a bit more expensive than that to get to 191, but still not bad. Less than 100pp, I think. Biggest nuisance was running to Jaggedpine to buy cheese for patty melts, but it was fairly inexpensive per combine, as I recall. If I recall correctly, base price of cheese is 1s, 5c each. I stuck the leftover cheese on my trader for 1g each and it sold overnight. I bet I could have charged 1pp each and it still would have sold.

Fletching was sheer misery since it was nerfed. My necro symbolically burned her fletching kit upon reaching 190 skill. Cost about 600pp or so, selling the arrows back to the merchant. You can possibly make back the cost selling arrows in the bazaar, but I honestly don't know if arrows sell well, so that's an IF.

Pottery, the same, sheer misery. My necro has used 24 stacks of zombie skins making poison vials and is only up to 172 (*wince*). Skillup rates were seriously nerfed. Hundreds of pp worth of poison vial sketches. If you know a rogue who's into poisonmaking or a chanter making mana vials, you might make back the costs, but that's an IF. Another of my toons is a grandmaster potter, and and in the 240's it was sometimes 160 to 180 combines per skillup, at a cost of 75pp or so per combine I think (star ruby steins, have a 50-60% failure rate at modified skill 255, and weren't selling well on Xev at the time). Real tear-your-hair-out misery. I will never do that again, I swear.

That said, she can make a modest income on pottery now, when I bother to log her in. *shrug*

Jewelcraft has been nerfed -- my grandmaster potter got to 183 before the nerf, with 255 wis and 180 cha, cost her about 1200pp just selling stuff back to the vendor, definitely gonna cost my necro more now (probably at least twice as much, I'm told, between the skillup nerf and the vendor buy-back nerf).

Smithing -- my necro is up to 132 for less than 100pp, but that was farming anything that could be farmed. Gonna cost more from here on out, but hopefully not too bad if I do all my own farming. Currently farming HQ ore and FS weaps to melt into HQ rings to try the next skillup path to 155 on ornate chain -- less costly than fine plate IF you do the farming to make the rings. She's also stocking LQ pelts for padding after ornate trivs and fine plate is the next skillup path. Gonna stop when fine plate is trivial.

Tailoring -- *shudder*. mana vials.... heady kiolas.... stack after stack after stack of silk swatches, competing with druids and rangers to farm the silks (tracking and SoW, GAH! heading for a spider only to watch a ranger streak in and whack whack dead spider *sigh*).... ahhhhhhhh run away!!!!! Necro is still only 115 in tailoring. Need to farm crystalline silks. Not looking forward to Wu's. *Really* not looking forward to acrylia.

All for a shawl with FT3 and Zephyr of Brell (extended buffs).... as soon as my necro has that shawl, she's going to slaughter Coldain in revenge for the pain of tradeskilling, I tell ya.

I'd work on it with my cleric (the shawl would be better done for her *sigh*), but the Coldain hate Binkin. Seems they're not very forgiving if you kill their Dain. *sniff*

In all seriousness -- tradeskilling *isn't* that bad IF you can tolerate the boredom of farming and the tedium of doing combines AND you're not worried about cost vs. skillup ratios. My necro is tradeskilling on a shoestring (Binkin is saving every copper for HoV someday -- over 100k on Xev last I looked, EEK!). So far, so good, but I don't recommend it.

Unless yer a tradeskill junkie. Then go for it. I know this bard who is seriously into questing and tradeskilling -- in the time it's taken him to get from about lvl 30 to 61, I got one cleric from 35 to 63, took a 6 month sabbatical, then started Binkin and got her from 1 to 54. But he's enjoying himself immensely, and that's the whole point of the game.

Bottom line: try it. If you like it, more power to ya. If you don't -- there are other ways to have fun and make plat at the same time.
