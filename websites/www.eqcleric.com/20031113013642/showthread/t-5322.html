Ariowen
Play your class as the need arises or the whim takes you.

I have pulled, and meleed, still keeping in mind that I am main healer and doing that job as well.

I have nuked, meleed and still kept my group alive.

I have had to do crowd control and root park and still kept my group alive.

I have had to do nothing but med and heal cause the action was fast, very fast and needed me to heal only.

Each class has it advantages, and can always be played to those advantages, but there are many more things that each and every class can do and even do well. Don't let yourself get into the mindset that your a healer only and that you have to only sit and med and pop up for a heal.

I have pulled the new zone, SolC, since I can pacify mobs and we didn't have a FD puller. Yaulp5, procing hammer, ranger atk buffs and you know what, pretty impressive dps from a cleric, mana regen keeping up and keeping group alive to boot.

You also need to know when to switch roles, go from a puller/meleer to the sit and med version to the crowd control version, ect., such as when you get adds, if no chanter, bard or necro to mez, you play root parker and do crowd control.

Play the game as you see fit, but do remember that since your a cleric, it is your primary job to keep folks alive, as long as that happens, play as you see fit.

I personally got into an argument with a RL friend about this...he felt I should always sit and med no matter what "just in case." Of course he had only ever played a paladin and never touched a caster class at all. I can say that the answer is "Depends." If mana is good and group is cruising...get in there and rough some beasties up. The mobs will fall quicker and thusly the xp will flow faster. So don't be afraid to get in there in that case. But if you are taxing your mana or are in a notoriously high add area (KC for example) or an area where your tank is getting pounded on I'd say sit and med...eventually the world is going to crash in and your going to have to bail out that fool puller.

Guess what I am saying is don't listen to people who say you should sit and med all the time. They have obviously never played a Cleric and realized how much of a waste of time it can be (especially when you are fm all the time).
