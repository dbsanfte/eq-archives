There seems to be some confusion between what is referred to as a "tort" or what is an infraction of the criminal code.

A tort is a break of contract law rather than criminal law. If you are an adult and you agree to a contract and then break the contract, you could be held liable for damages as a result of your consequences.

I really believe that most of us understand that we have formed a contract with SOE when we log on. The length of the agreement, the inconvenience of reading such agreement is not likely an arguement for defence that would hold up in any Western court of law especially Canada, Britain and the US. That is because our case law commons from a common source.

However, SOE would have to prove in court how they were harmed as a result of your actions and also put a dollar figure on the the damages resulting from that breech of contract.

When you look at the amount we pay on a (usually monthly) basis to SOE, the damages for a regular player would not be that much. Consequently, SOE will generally only bann the player from playing. The costs of pursuing court actions would far out strip any possible settlement.

But there are parrells to the down loading of music from the Internet. In certain select cases, the music industry has selected certain "victims" to pursue in court. Though the amounts per conviction are very small, the thought process is to put fear into everyone else and stop them from downloading music.

So, in theory they could very well select out certain players for "punishment" and sue them. And likely would be successful. Ignorance of the law is never a very strong defence arguement.
