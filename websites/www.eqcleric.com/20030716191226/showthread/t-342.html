I have had similar problems, as a result of two seperate issues.

1- I had a modified UI which, after recent patches, caused me to crash out of game as soon as I logged in only on the characters with those UI's loaded. So what I had to do is go to My Computer > C Drive > Program Files > Sony > Everquest Trilogy > uifiles > and copy all of the files in my default folder into my modified ui folder. Then I was able to log into the game without crashing out.

2- I tryed to enter Windowed Mode while Trading in the Bazaar and crashed out of the game, and then couldn't log back in. I had to go into eqclient.ini and reset windowedmode=FALSE before I could get back in.

Hope this helps :)

Thurian
See I work in the Tech support field for about 5 yrs now and I have a few options for you.

1. Nvidia has new video card drives as of december 2002. Download and use them.

2. Service Pack 1 is buggy as hell....made my system unusable ....have same video card and close to same specs.
Uninstall service pack 1 if you can. (unfortunately I had to do a complete wipeout/reinstall on my pc).

3. If active restore is on...go back to a date that you know it was working at. (save any files and so forth...read warnings).

4. Close browsers (or any apps) in background including, instant messenging software.

5. go to Download.com and download the lastest version of adaware. It willl remove any adsoftware that runs inthe backgournd. Since the Eq Ui is xml based...sometimes it will run in background and continually send data to whatever ad site sneakily installed them on you system.

6. Try logging in right before patches or right after them....sometimes the lag is so high in certain zones (pok, bazaar) you will lose connection right away...I know someone who could not enter bazaar on dial-up at all. Try getting techsupport to move character to less laggy zone.

7. Worst comes to worst...backup your files (makesure you backup your socials). And reformat, reinstall.

8. Change your ISP (internet provider)...this may incrrease connection. Sometimes ISP have bottlenecks on there end not yours (too many subscribers).
