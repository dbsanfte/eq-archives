Baldeer
First of all, I anulled magic like 10 times to get rid of most of his buffs (especially the damage shield)

He had his bear pet summoned, but I killed him first thing with banish summoned.

After that, I mostly just used our summoned (and really cool) hammer of judgement to beat him down. The proc really hurt him. I also used the hammer pet, mostly for a distraction and to keep him flustered. When he landed DoTs on me, I either anulled magic on myself, or cast celestial healing to keep myself alive.

When he ran out of mana and started trying to use his right clickable DoTs, I just bashed him. Worked every time. ;)

Most of all, it helped that we were in an indoor and VERY SMALL room. If a druid ever wants to duel you, make sure YOU get to pick the location. He couldn't use some of his biggest nukes and his speed advantage was nothing to worry about all because it was an indoor zone.

Again, thanks for the tips and the good lucks. It payed off.

Koboldt
When I was younger, maybe level 15 or so, I duelled my partners Monk. Now, most people would say "And he wiped the floor with you, laughing and making jokes about rezzing yourself huh?"

Actually... no. Yes, he did deliver some damage to me when I went head on with him (we were just doing it for fun). But, never under estimate the weakness of a blind enemy. I cast Flash of Light on him which left him blind, quickly moved away and cast root on him and that allowed me to run up behind him and swing at him or cast from a distance. When Flash of light wore off and if I needed to med or heal, I just Hide (being a DE). I seriously could have killed him but, I think he was getting annoyed that I was using my cunning to fight the fight instead of going head to head.

Needless to say he stopped beating my ass before he killed me but I knew inside that if I were to have played my character as I would for any other fight, my lil' Cleric would have mopped the floor with a Monk. WOOT!

Restandre
The thing that hurts clerics the most in a duel is that everything is based on the same resist. It's not hard to get a few MR items to make it exceptionally difficult to land any spells, which can leave you to not much more than meleeing and healing yourself as you go.

I wouldn't be afraid of fighting a druid in an outdoor *arena*, but don't fight him in a huge open area like a duel in an outdoor zone. "The Arena" is a rather large place to fight, and the bazaar arena isn't a whole lot better. The arena in WFP would be a nice compromise.
The worst thing to deal with is sow vs. snare. If you can contain him somewhat, the other outdoor-only spells shouldn't matter a great deal. Fury of Air may have an interrupt on it, but it shouldn't affect you much. I'd say you know exactly how to handle things, though.
