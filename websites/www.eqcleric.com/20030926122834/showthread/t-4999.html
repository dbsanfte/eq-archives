Shadowfrost
Epic camps. Bergergle, Gimblox, the Skyfire shards, and the wait for the human merchant. I keep reading posts from people who think those are time-consuming, and I just can't keep quiet.

The cleric epic is not hard any more.

I helped a friend of mine to get hers epic in three days flat. We killed Overking Bathezid with one group. Of alts. I think nobody who killed Bathezid even bothered to log in their main that day. After a quiet afternoon spent at the VP entrance camping shards, we did kill Rage with our mains, because there were only three of us around; druid, cleric, and shadowknight -v- Zordakalicus Ragefire, and I spent more mana nuking than healing.

Now, back in the day, the cleric epic used to be hard.

I took a thirty-person raid into Chardok for my note from Overking Bathezid. And we wiped between the palace gates and the library. Five times. If I hadn't been guildleader, I would never have gained the support to keep going.

Five successive weekends running, the guild paid for 30 coffins to get our raid out of Chardok.

A few weeks later, once I had my singed scroll, the "changes" to Ragefire were implemented. Nobody had figured out his timer; I just sat in Skyfire and camped him. When I got too tired, I had relatives and friends log me in and keep watch.

Over 170 hours later, he popped, and another guild beat me to the spawn.

Yes, that's 170 hours staring at the wall in Skyfire, for nothing.

So I camped him again. It took longer the second time. And when he popped - at 4am my time, I was woken by a telephone call from Australia - I called in every favour anyone owed me in the game in order to get four groups together so as to be certain of killing him.

And I had an easier time of it than some.

I killed over 60 PH's for Gimblox, but compared to the rest of my epic, Gimblox was just trivial. What's thirty hours solo camping a goblin? You'll do worse things for your VT key, that's for certain.

Personally I thought the storyline for the cleric epic was appropriate to the class and rather interesting. The camps weren't fun but the story was reasonably good.

It starts as a tale of contrition and atonement - killing Lord Bergergle was a Bad Thing To Do - and works its way up, through a conspiracy of the Plasmatic Priests working to summon a Big Baddie (Ixiblat Fer), to the final confrontation with the evil Ragefire and a reward offered by the Avatar of Water.
