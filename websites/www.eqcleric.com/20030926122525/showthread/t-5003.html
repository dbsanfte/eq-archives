Restandre
OK, EQ is a game in the same genre of D&D, which means a few things. Most notably you play a character, and you can step in and out of that character and into another or back into "real life" if you choose.

Someone once called me the world's worst role-player. I don't take it incredibly seriously and speak ooc most of the time; I wouldn't really call myself an RPer. But I do like to play EQ with a little consideration to that fact.

It irritates me to no end when somebody calls their character a "toon". A toon is a comic-drawn rabbit, not my high elf. My high elf is a person -- it is who I am when I play Everquest; my real life alter-ego has little place there and I get very irritated at people who assume I'm a guy and/or call me a mangina. (They may be right, but that's not the point.)

I also get annoyed at people who want to restart their character with different stats or a difference race and call it "rerolling", as though we actually toss some dice and sort those numbers around rather than just allocating a few points to customize pre-determined stats.

There are innumerable other ways people bother me in this way; these happened to be the first two that came to mind.

EQ is a game. You can play it how you like. There's a zillion different right ways to do it. But it is in essence a role-playing game. I don't care if you don't want to treat it as such; most of the time I don't either. But I wish that others would show the same tolerance for people who want to treat it that way at least a little.

At the least, please don't refer to my character as a toon.
