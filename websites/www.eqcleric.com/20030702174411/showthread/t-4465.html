Well, having just over 600 days /played, I've got more than my fair share of stories to tell, for sure. I will limit it , though, to just a few! ~

Some of my "greatest" EQ memories ~

The opening of Hate Plane!

I was on Karana Server, at the time, and I happened upon a DE in Nektulos, who had all these players surrounding him, trying to get him to talk. I don't really even remember what I had said, but back then I was very much into roleplay, so I RP'd with this fellow a bit. After a bit of conversation, he asks me if I'd like to help gather forces for the King. I said yes, of course, so he sent me on a journey to South Ro, to pick up a Troll, and bring this Troll back to Neriak.

Off I went, running to South Ro, and searching for a bit, until I see a Troll sitting off by one of the Derv camps. I go up to him, say what the gentleman in Nektulos told me to say, and the Troll responded. All is well, I'm thinking, so I ask this troll to follow me home. The troll, being controlled by a GM, was MOST misbehaved. everytime we passed players that were not of evil alignment, he killed them! It didn't matter if they were busy trying to get exp, he went up and beat them down! He also had to rest like...every ten minutes. This turned into the worst babysitting job ever, and also the longest run back to Nektulos I had ever had/uhoh

After finally getting this troll under control, we get to Neriak. It was pretty cool, because I ended up getting cash, a few bars of platinum, and later, after the event, Mennix gave me a custom title. The big bonus to this event, for me, however, was actually seeing Neriak's King/cool .

While I later found out the event went differently per server, this is probably one of the single-coolest GM events they did.It also solidified my belief that Mennix was the best GM ever! (As an aside, my custom title got nuked when I moved servers, but the memory of the event will always remain;) )


Open Fear Raid ~

Opening Day of Ayonae Ro saw what was probably the single longest and worst Fear raid ever. It was an open raid, lead by one of the server's oldest and well-known Erudite Clerics, Volan. There were no guilds formed yet, so everyone was still pretty happy with each other, though people from their former guilds on their old servers stuck together, until we could reform our guilds.

In any case, everyone knew of this open raid on Fear, but most of my guild at that time decided to not go, and spent time just hanging out. I get a tell from Volan, asking me if any of us could possibly help him out. Bored, I said "sure", and dragged my co-guildleader, and my boyfriend along. We get there, to the Fear portal, in Ferrott, to find that he needed our help with some nasty CR. Seems the open raid had become a slaughter. So, after talking with my boyfriend, and co-leader, we decide to plunge on in.

The three of us formed a plan, then went into Fear. What we found was simply the worst case scenario you could imagine. mobs were aggro and pathing all over, corpses were already starting to get close to decay, and Cazic himself was running rampant through the plane. Not to mention, there were paniced people zerging themselves back into Fear, to try to get their corpses/sigh .

I sent my boyfriend to work. He started out training mobs around for me, while my co-leader and I worked on getting resses started on key classes. We got a few done, when a nasty little monk came in, and tried training us/grr . Luckily, I was fast with the camp button, and saved myself from falling int the corpse-pile. This monk went on for a time trying to be an ass. We ended up getting GM help, to get this monk removed, but sadly, the GM wouldn't help us ress/no .

After a few near-death experiences, we managed to get about 95 percent of the corpses back to their owners, and most people safely ressed. Volan did great at keeping people calm during the time, and it took our crew of 3 about 3 hours total to get everyone.

The three of us didn't step foot inside of Fear again...for about 3 months after that night/dead

Those are just a couple, but I've been long-winded enough!

How does one define their greatest moment? Is it the time you narrowly squeezed by a mob or duelist in single combat, eked by a fight with their group under incredible circumstances, or snatched victory from the jaws of defeat on a raid? Is is the time you finished a certain quest you've been working toward, or finally acquired an item off a mob you've wanted so long? Is it the time you helped someone else do that? Is it the time you helped someone else in another way? Is it the time you did something so unusual and unique that it left you proud to no end?

There are so many ways you can call a time your greatest moment, or at least truly great. Obtaining your epic. Narrowly and miraculously camping out in time to save a raid force for the entire night. Helping with a rescue mission. Teaching a newbie how to play the game. There are entirely too many ways to interpret this challenge - describing your greatest moment.


I have to admit though, the times that thrill me the most are when you truly dance with the mobs -- keeping your group alive, avoiding death yourself, and assisting in or perhaps entirely handling crowd control. In my earlier days as a cleric, I was grouped in the warrior room in HK. We had a full group of people, who while partially competent, were not hardened veterans of the game. The rest of the group is all melee, for some reason at that time there were no casters in High Keep.

The monk brings a pull with an accidental add. I purposefully take aggro on the add, pull him to the corner and root him away - mind you this is not a large space to work with. As the first mob is almost half dead, we get a respawn. I root it and tell everyone else to move away. The warrior tanking isn't superb but I'm keeping him alive alright.

Your immoblize spell has worn off.
POP!

Now we've got 4 mobs and one is pissed at me. The first mob finally dies and the group grabs the root-breaker beating on me. I heal the rogue being hit by the newer pop and end up with aggro on the second heal. I pull him to the one remaining corner and park him there.

You have gained experince!
POP!

The tank moves on to one of the other corners, and suddenly two roots break. I barely get Divine Aura off, and tell someone to tank those mobs. A ranger moves over and takes one. I move to a free corner, drop DA, and heal myself, which bring aggro back to me from the free one. Stun. Root. Move away. Finally heal the warrior that is in sore need of it, and the rogue that keeps getting aggro. Root breaks. Ranger taunts it, I root it, we move. Unfortunately one of the mobs is rooted in the doorway corner, and suddenly a hallway mob comes running in.

I'm at about 40m and half health at this point, and with lots of people needing heals. As the third mob mercifully dies, we've still got 4 more in the room. There's nowhere to stand safely in this room. The monk starts tanking an add while the warrior works on one. Just as I'm about to get a heal off on the warrior, who is starting to need it, a root breaks. Your spell has been interrupted. I try to stun, interrupt.

Ranger runs over and taunts it, which fails. While I keep getting beat, the warrior is down to one bubble and backs off while a rogue tanks for a bit. I finally channel a spell and move clear. I heal the rogue who needs it, while the monk mends. Heal the warrior finally. He goes back to fighting. The ranger backs off and starts tossing baby heals. The first mob finally dies, the root on the fourth mob breaks. I replant it while the group moves on to the third and leaves the monk soloing.

I'm at negligible mana now. The ranger runs himself oom while I pause in the corner. I can't even cast a root on the monk's mob. (Call this mob #2.) One of the rogues takes aggro and tanks that third mob for half the fight. Just as the mob dies, the root on the fourth breaks.

The warrior and the other rogue go over to it, the rest of the group finally goes to help the monk. The monk finally feigns death before he dies. The warrior has to back off. One of the rogues is low and the other is tanking a mob. I'm not looking too good either.

The warrior re-engages, while the rogue still has aggro. The rogue is gonna die though. Try Greater Healing. Not enough mana. Try Healing. Not enough mana. Finally in desperation I manage to cast Daring. Warrior takes aggro, with mob still at half health. Other mob still has a good chunk left. I tell everyone to get on the monk's mob and finish it. Meanwhile the ranger somehow steals aggro. Monk dusts himself off to jump back in. Somehow that mob dies with everyone below two bubbles, most below one.

Last mob is beating on, I dunno, somebody, and I manage to get off another Daring just in time. The monk mends, but doesn't matter cause he can't get aggro. I'm desperate. I sit down to try to med. Oh look, now *I* have aggro. I see no other options left...

I turn auto attack on. The mob turns back to the one rogue, who is at half a bubble, when my uber velium hammer procs - I had just turned 25, first time it had done that. Probably coincedence, but just at that point the mob turns and finally starts running.

Group chases him down before he can run into range of adds. I blink dumbfoundedly at the screen a few times. Group is equally astounded. After a couple exchanges, I go afk and get a soda. I need the energy, and darnit, I'm thirsty.

A year and a half later, I go onto eqcleric.com and start writing about it, and when I'm about done, I look back, and say wow I wrote a heck of a lot, and apologize to the people that actually read the whole thing.

You need to buy spells and I can not let a fellow cleric run out into the Fay with all the evil little orcs out there just waiting to pounce on a person. This is the armor, shield and weapon that was given to me upon my entering into the Cleric Guild by a fellow Cleric and I wish to pass it on to you as it was passed on to me and the Cleric before me. We had been able to easily kill mobs such as Vindi, Velks, DS, RC, but suddenly we started to lose people. One day lost to Vindi because of lack of DPS -- the utter frustration of seeing a once trival mob slowly wipe us because we couldn't keep up with his natural regen is indescribable. We decided to merge and found a guild with similiar values in the same situation. Despite several months of "testing" out the idea of a merger and being excited at our future, the moment that I sat done in the stables of the bazaar with my closest chanter buddy and watched the guild tag disappear, then I hit F1 and typed /guildremove I was crying in RL.

With that background in mind and my most humbling moment in EQ being at the hands of Derek the Vindicator, the day that my guild, filled with old friends and new friends, killed all the boss mobs in Kael had to be the best. We started with King Tormax for breakfast and ended with killing the Avatar of War. It was truly a triumphant day.
