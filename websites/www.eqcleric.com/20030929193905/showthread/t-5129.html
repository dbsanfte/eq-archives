Now is good. You will do fine there at 42 (and finding a group should be easy enough). The Emblem is a fairly common drop (a hell of a lot more common than my Shaman's Empty Flask was, I'll tell ya)

Pulling just the SW corner mobs, I usually get 3 in a 4 hours session. It will probably take longer to catch the Female Spirit up for the final turn in than it will to get the item you need in Dulak.

Duhikae has had his spell banked since 31, but that is, admittedly, because I made him stand in the corner and watch till Shazzog killed something that dropped it.

Chances are, you won't even ding once while waiting for the drop...especially if you /OOC that you are looking for it. On Lanys, at least, people are fairly good about announcing drops they don't need there...and there are lots of 'em.

Oh, and remember, it's Dulak that drops the Emblem. Turn in is in Gunthak, but you need to go to Dulak for the drop. Bring Cloudy Potions or (better yet) find a druid or shammy to act as escort, and have fun running the tunnels. I have a seperate Train hotkey just for the tunnel run.

You saw one drop in Gunthak? Never seen one there. I wonder if it was at one of the villages?

They are bloody common in Dulak (points to previous post), so if you get tired of waiting for them it in Gunthak, head across.

BTW, camping the SW corner...

There are 6-7 mobs in my easy to reach rotation (exhausted pirate and mercenary to the left, Harbor fisherman right in front of me, 2 scrubbers and an engineer down the gangway in the SW corner, and a Lighthouse Keeper straight up the way). I can expand that to 11 or so with no real difficulty, but I am lazy (another mercenary, fisherman and scrubber just past the lighthouse keeper). That's without even going into a building.

There is a named Scrubber (Vulag, Vorag, something like that) that is a semi-rare spawn and can pop on any gangway (or in the place of any scrubber, really). He normally drops a cheesy belt, but also can drop a 'Worn Gold Tooth'. It's a nice little Haste item for any Melee types you want to make very happy. Got one last night, finally. Got the guy twice.

On Lanys, the SW corner is rarely camped and (when it is) it's usually a Shammy pulling Drunks for their drop. Plenty of room for two small groups if you are both reasonably friendly and considerate.

For the low to mid 40's types, StormWave and Lady D are both easy boats for a decent group (used to do it at 43 or so with my Shammy, a 45 Warrior and 1 random other person of any class). I can solo StormWave with just my Shammy it 51 (with KEI), and held it with no real trouble dual boxing it with Shazzog and Duhikae (was doing OK even without KEI dual boxing, but KEI made it easier). Lady D has a few more spawns, but I think I could probably solo it as well, and could certainly dual box it.

The other three boats get just a wee bit tougher. Like, 'wait till your 50's and\or have a good, full group' tougher. I started a really nasty train from Oceancrasher once. Need to go back there with Shazzog now that he's grown up a bit. Hmmm, time for the Klan ov da Grok to have a day at the docks, I think...

Other common areas to camp: Shark Bar, along the docks, between StormWave and Lady D. 6-8 mobs (never counted 'em) My Warrior partner single pulls 'em prtty easy, and there is a warehouse across the street if we get bored (dual box solo'd it once when the SW corner was actually camped. Not too rough, but the mobs in the bar hit hard and fast.

The Armory (which is where the Mine entrance is, btw): Harder to single pull without Harmony or similar. With Harmony it's not too bad. (Haven't been there with Duhikae, so I don't know how well our Pacification spells work there.)

The Warehouses with the Money Counters: Druids need this spot, as that's where their drops are. The Deepwater Emblem, however, can drop anywhere, so if you have a Druid with you, you can camp for both of your drops at once. I know we hate letting Druids play healer, but it would work well in this situation. Let the Druid pull and snare, while you tank and they assist\battle heal. As long as you stick to single pulls, it should be easy. If you have any kind of pet melee to bring along, or any kind of slower, it will be cake.

I've seen something called 'Wench Tavern' called as a camp, but I have no idea where it is. I suspect it's the Shark Bar, but I have seen them both called at once before, so I may be wrong.

There is also the Enraged Soul Stealer, of course, but that is a camp for the SoulStripper and not much else. Not good for camping your emblem.

There are also probably another 4 or so mini-camp areas where you can snipe wandering street mobs with relative impunity. Once you learn the guard's pathing and aggro ranges (changed recently, it seems) it's pretty easy to avoid adds.
