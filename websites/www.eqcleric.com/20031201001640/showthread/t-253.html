My race-of-choice is halfling, but I created a dwarf cleric just to explore the solo-melee possibilities (BTW this character is still soloing dark-blues at 34th level).

Dwarf clerics are awesome. Don't get too wrapped up in WIS, having a deep mana pool is not de rigeur...proper spell choice and timing is FAR more critical. A dwarf cleric seems far more versatile than either halfling or high-elf (I have both).

The RP possibilities with a dwarf cleric would seem to be a lot of fun. My guy is a very gruff, one-eyed veteran who took up evangelism because his battle injuries forced him out of the army. The church offered him a chance to keep bashin' heads (which he loves), so he became a cleric. His melee capabilities are not what they once were, due to losing an eye, aging, and a few too many blows to the head, but with tthe blessings of Brell to fortify him, he does quite well.

In high end raids you are a healing machine and manapool > all. For screwing around or soloing by that time you will have gear to switch out for haste and str if you need it. Having a high level cleric makes me cringe at the thought of having my melee alts grouped with a low-wis cleric. 2 more heals often is the difference between one group death and complete group wipe.

That's just my opinion of course. My aim was high end game. If you're just in it to RP and aren't considering having to cast MGB Aego 10 times a day, maybe wis isn't that big of a factor.
