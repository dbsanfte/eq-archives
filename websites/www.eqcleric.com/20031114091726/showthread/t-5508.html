Shadowfrost
Use Mark of Karn for stripping damage shields.

There was a time when Mark of Karn was a useful (or even overpowering) spell, but it was nerfed into near-uselessness and there is no prospect of it becoming useful again now.

Mark of Retribution is decent at level 54 when you get it - it's effectively a 15 point damage shield that stacks with druid/mage damage shields, and doesn't cost a lot of mana. It's a situational spell, and you're best off using it as a soloing tool. It also works well for pulling since it costs very little mana. In terms of damage output to mana spent, it's good at 54 but not so brilliant compared to Judgment or Condemnation, so you won't be casting it for damage when you're in your 60's.

stratys
First of all, both spells are not dependant on the mob hitting players -- Mark of Karn/Kings is supposed to grant a small amount of healing when players hit the mob (think of it like this: instead of covering the mob with thorns or flame like normal damage shields, you are covering the mob in Bactine or ointment or something). In theory, it SHOULD be great for monks or rangers or anyone who tends to have many fast, smaller attacks, since they would be healed more often. The point is pretty moot, however, as they've mucked it up to the point where it really doesn't heal for any significant amount anyway. The spell is very useful because it's a way to replace a mob's damage shield without using dispells and thus taking off any snare/slow/dot spells you've already put on the mob. Other than that, I don't bother unless it's going to be a very long fight.

Mark of Retribution/Mark of the Righteous -- IfI have the mana, I use this very often if fights aren't super short. it's a 15/34 point damage shield that stacks with all other damage shields because you cast it on the target instead of yourselves (think of it as giving the mob gloves with thorns inside.. every time he hits someone, he takes more damage). It may not be the most efficient damage possible in some situations, but it is very low aggro, so it lets you do a fair amount of damage, and you can cast it pretty much whenever its convenient without worrying about taking aggro. If you're tank mezzing, it's a great way to have the mob wear off even more of his health while the group is focusing its attention on another mob.

Due to its low aggro and being a negative spell which doesn't directly cause damage, this isn't a bad spell to pull with (with the advent of pacification, it's not unusual for the cleric to find himself in the puller role from time to time). A tank can grab the mob off you by shooting an arrow or a quick spell at the mob as it comes into camp.
