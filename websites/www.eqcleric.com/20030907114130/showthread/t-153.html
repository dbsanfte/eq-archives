For Luclin, the Burrower cycle in the Deep. That's an on-your-toes every second fight. Worms come in waves - first three levels of small worms (they small, they still hit hard) with a couple minutes in between waves. Velious... I couldn't not mention the Ring 10 War. That is such an incredible event - the script, the Coldain captains sitting around in their circle after you spawn them (and they piss off everyone experiencing in GD by despawning everything else in the zone). The ranger scout saying from what direction the current Kromrif wave is coming and the player forces running (well... It's an awesome piece of programming and anyone who hasn't seen it before should take the time to watch it or participate in it. Shadowfrost
"High End" is a slippery concept because it keeps changing. I remember my first ever "High End" raid... on the Efreeti Lord in Sol B.

We took three groups, and it was still pretty tough. In a way, that was my favourite raid, because it was really my first one and raiding was so new to me.

I remember wiping my entire guild five times trying to get to Overking Bathezid for the cleric epic. (We kill Bathezid with a few twinks now... not worth logging our mains on for him.) In a way, Bathezid was my favourite raid because of the buzz I felt when it was over.

I remember 300 hours spent camping Ragefire. I mean, we'd taken down Ragefire for other guild clerics just by logging in and finding him up, but my own camp was hell on earth... spawns taken by other guilds, the lot. In a way, Ragefire was my favourite raid, again because of that buzz when I finally got my Water Sprinkler.

In terms of being an enjoyable encounter, another of my favourites was Ventani the Warder in Sleepers Tomb, because we needed to use a whole new kind of strategy and it felt so different to kill him.

I don't enjoy the Luclin fights at all. In Luclin, it's safe to say that with all the critters, if you can get them to half HP, then you can definitely kill them; I think that the whole Luclin expansion would be more fun and better balanced if they halved the HP totals of every single critter, across the board.

I haven't found a PoP boss critter which I enjoyed as much as the Kunark/Velious ones; but some of the PoP exp groups have been great fun for me. For some reason, single-grouping the Rabid Wrulon and the Undead Inhabitant one after another were enormously enjoyable for me. ;)
