Restandre
At early levels, yeah pretty much. Later on, if you can start getting into groups that don't challenge your mana quite as much (which is partly a matter of the group and partly your mana regen,) you can either melee or nuke on the side. Occasionally you might even be asked to pull now that the lull line of spells works with some degree of reliability.

A battle cleric is a cleric that generally melees. Since the introduction of the proc hammers (summoned weapons starting at level 56 that frequently do an extra 75-150 per swing,) clerics that get in the fray are a lot more prevalent.

Millaria
There are times when a cleric can get herself in there to mellee, but make no mistake we are not tanks. I don't believe that there is really such a thing as a "battle cleric". No one in their right mind would ever confuse us with a war or even a ranger for dps. Our nukes (except against undead) are pitiful compared to a wizzie or even a mage.

That being said there are times when our dps can help out. So, it does make sense to work on our offensive and defensive skills. I found out that there were different stages in a cleric's life when it was more possible to melle than others, up to around 40 I did a lot of hammer swinging. From 40 - 45 (until I got KEI), I had to concentrate on meding to keep mana up. The MOBS were bigger and stronger and the tanks required more healing. After KEI it seemed a breeze again. On normal LDON and regular grinds I always seemed to have enough mana even when bashing away. Now that I am in the low 60s, and often in PoV or doing hard LDON, I have to med almost continously.

Hope this helps.

Sydian
Our main function is and always will be to keep the group alive.

We can also add some damage by meleeing (proc hammers at 56+) or nuking if it's safe to do so. This isn't common until upper levels when KEI and CH efficiency rising make mana less of a concern. We're probably still near or at the bottom for damage.

Of course it always depends how much damage the group is taking, if you push the group's limits then almost all of your mana will be going to heals. That's not to say there's no skill, CH adds a lot of timing at upper levels.

Ironically I found being a Cleric at lower levels harder. You don't have CH or KEI on your side, although many tanks and DPS classes are heavily twinked nowadays which speeds things up.
