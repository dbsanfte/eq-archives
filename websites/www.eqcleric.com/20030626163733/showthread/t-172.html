Sedrek
Your gear is coming along quite nicely for a 37 cleric. There are a few things to consider.

As you progress, you will eventually start getting much better armor, Hate / PoG / Kael etc, and eventaully ToV. In these cases you are always going to be covering your major / minor slots, so you funds will mostly cover your "acessories". Granted you need something to get you the next 10 lvls till you are planar capable.

As your skill increases, your AC / HP get much better and (or a big part of them) will be derived from your (and group) buffs. As you become better and better at playing your class, you will learn how to avoid getting aggro. Having 30 extra AC for the 10 times you get beat on in an hour isn't as affective as 30 wis that you would be casting on for that hour. For those three reasons, I would suggest you take WIS gear over AC (rearead second paragraph as well). Somthing to note, your first planar gear (hate) is miserable on WIS but decent on AC.

When you do get up in lvl (50's), you will find that maxing your WIS becomes MUCH easier. When you get to this area, you will start replacing WIS gear with +Mana gear (like your rings for instance).

Still can't read magelo... oh well. But for arms and legs, here are some suggestions.

Try to get the Krom legs from GD. It's a rather easy quest for which you'll need some higher level pcs to help. It'll give you about 15 ac plus big stats (relatively speaking) in a handful of other categories. Also, if you can maximize your wisdom in other areas, then pieces like Donal (which have 0 wisdom but high AC) can be used. Thus, I'd go with the +6 wisdom rings or diamond wedding rings (ToF) and make up for the ac with Donal arms (ac 21 or so) and casts Valor (whoopdee doo).

If you are a dwarf, take a look at some of the dwarven armor too. All of it runs under 2k each and gives you strong AC and other bonuses throughout. Currently I am wearing the wrist (just one), arms, and hands for the AC and relatively good wisdom.
