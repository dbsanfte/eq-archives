Dowel_Fearn
I dumped 5 points into Stamina for two reasons; the first being the extra Hitpoints that it gives and secondly for the added endurance. Now, I know that 5 points into stamina doesnt give alot for either reason in the beginning, but at later lvs, i am relieved that I did it.

Sycotica
I did the same route putting 5 into sta.

In the higher end game sta can really add up and the more hitpoints the better!

Dyschord
Having started newbie clerics on other servers, I have to agree with dumping points into Stamina.

When I created my main on Opening Day, not alot was really "known" about creation stats, so I ended up going off what I would have done with a D&D character. I went with Wis, Charisma (and I still don't regret charisma, btw!), and Str.

My newb clerics I have gone with Stamina, Wis, Charisma/Str, race depending.

Bukkiah
I put somewhere between 5 and 10 into stamina simply because it is the toughest stat to raise with items. Wisdom is ridiculously easy to get to 200+ with relatively little expense. Strenght items are very common as well but that depends on race a little more. Being a dwarf I think I only put a few points there. I went with stamina, charisma (like Dyschord I don't regret it one bit), strength, then wisdom.

Dragonkid239
I did STA and WIS just because as a Erudite, They lack HP like the High Elves do. ANd in the long run, with my extra HP items, It has really helped.

Galv
I dumped it into wisdom, as galv was my first character. However, I do not regret it at all. By having high wisdom, it gives me the flexibility to switch items around and still maintain max wisdom. Regardless, when all is said and done, I don't think it really makes much of a difference, as spells such as kei and focus will max your stats anyway.

restandre
Wisdom is most important to your mana pool.
Stamina is most important to your HP.
Dexterity is most important for your chances to successfully cast a spell.
Strength is important for carrying stuff.
Agi, Int, and Charisma are not terribly important, except when it comes to buying peridots.

I dump into wisdom and put the rest where needed; stamina is probably uour best best if you plan on being in it for the long haul. If not, whichever of those three is lowest.

Parklyn
While my cleric is not an opening day character (that was my druid) I still maxed out wis then did str. Not much was known about stats except wisdom for priest types/int for pure casters/etc.

Funny that this thread came up, I saw Casters Realm cleric creation recommendation and thought that is so not what people knew back in the day! Heck people did not even know stamina helped hp's!

Anyone on the Stromm server would be wise to follow either philosophy...wis/some sta or sta/whatever. Even though equipping yourself will be slow you know what is out there to expect and there are an abundance of wisdom items...not so with sta for a cleric.

I would go with sta all the way then a little wis on a dark elf or halfling cleric (In fact even though I have a cleric and several other alts I am thinking about making a halfling cleric anyway on Stromm.)

Braeaelin

Having the boosted charisma helped in the early stages but now I wish I had put those points in stamina as I could simply use a couple opal encrusted steins to boost charisma when needed. Oh well...
