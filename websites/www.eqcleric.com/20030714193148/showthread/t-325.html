Cerrick
Not sure but there is a grave yard in Katta Castellum that makes a nice solo spot for clerics. Not sure if 52 is too high though to get the maximum out of it.

This is a root and rot place.. stun/root/stack DoT's.. repeat .. finish with a nuke. But I can't for the life of me remember if its good at 50ish or 45 ish.

Anyway, there are areas.. some are hard to find. I did some soloing in City of Mists couple other places.

In Trakanon's Teeth there are several buildings that spawn some ghosts. Topped off alot of levels soloing those. Once you enter the zone from Emerald Jungle on your left are two ruinous buildings one has about one has 4 spawns one has 6 or 7. Finally there is a third to the right of the zone in with a few more. Keep in mind these are caster mobs (most are) and will nuke the tar outta yah. High Fire Magic and Cold resists are a must (buff those up). Occasionally they will annull you so you're likely to lose some buffs, but you are fairly close to a little used zone where you can zone out if you get into trouble (and not have to worry about training anyone). I used the same methods for soloing all undead back then.. root/stack DoT's .. but then run out of casting range for the mob. You will see the message when your DoT's wear off.. run back in and reroot and reapply DoT's, then nuke. I could get 2 or 3 mobs per mana bar. The key is to move in and out. You will get used to the mobs names.. and be able to tell which are the warriors. these are much easier as you can just stay in range and med between applications of DoT's.

SoW potions and KEI are nice.. but small risk they will be annulled.

Another problem its a pain in the arse to get there. Easy to get in.. harder to get back out. Maybe worth a shot though.

I've been soloing in Trak through 49 and 50, gaining about 2 blues per hour. The two buildings just east of the EJ zone are excellent camps, the wandering mobs so frequent throughout the other undead camps won't bother you at these buildings. The undead won't help each other, which is very nice! About one third of the mobs turned light blue at level 50, which makes be doubt this will be a viable camp at level 52.

Cerrick: I've never tried soloing mobs just with dot's, I'll have to try that technique out. I do land a dot as my second offensive spell, but just to add some extra damage throughout the fight. Thanks for the tip!

Suseanne: your best friend with the root 'n nuke techique is your stun spells. Your nukes will break root roughly 50% of the time, so you've got to expect this will happen and be prepared. The mob will charge you and beat on you, maybe even stun you, but don't dispair, and don't DA. As soon as you are able to cast again, cast Holy Might. This spell channels very easily and will stun the mob long enough for you to recast root and back away before he can take another swing. Hope this helps!

Hey, if a cleric can't rez an old thread, who can? /snicker

Seriously, the Katta Castellum graveyard was even or dark blue to me at 45. I was soloing like mad there to make level 46 and be planar enabled. They dropped a few plat, and if you were lucky, the folks killing the guards wouldn't loot the weapons. Those would sell for the equivalent of Fine Steel weapons.

I was able to solo Ice Giants (in Everfrost) at 50 and 51. Without any sort of mana regen buff, the IG would respawn before I was back to FM. A mount helps in that department. I tried two basic strategies: 1) root, nuke til root breaks, re-root, nuke, etc. 2) root, fear, nuke til root breaks, re-fear when it wears off, etc. Method two kept the IG from charging right at you after root broke, but used extra mana. It does, however, save you mana that you would otherwise be using to heal yourself.

In addition to decent XP (not great, but decent), they also will fill your bank pretty nicely between coin drops, FS weapons and gems. The problem with these spawns is that they're pretty much perma-camped, so you may have to wait in line for one to open up.
