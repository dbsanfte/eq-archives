Braeaelin
Last night I was in a great group in the Dreadlands (50 warrior, 45 warrior, 46 bard, 53 rogue, 46 shaman then cleric and myself). We were set up north of KC and was killing at a fast pace. The group started out with myself and the 50 warrior and we just added more as we fought. He wasn't looting at first, so before the corpse would rot I would loot them. I made sure to allow enough time for him to loot but he preferred to be pulling instead.

Later when the group existed of the lineup mentioned above, the 45 warrior was looting every kill while it was dropping. At first I didn't mind as it kept the corpses cleared. However I was noticing nobody else was getting a share of the loot (as you know, the splits are minimal compared to the loot that can't be divided from the mob which usually consists of a few pp) including gems and other drops so I sent the guy a tell asking him to ease off and give others a chance. He agreed and for the next few mobs everything was fine. Next thing I know he's back at it looting as mobs are still falling and when the rest of the group is engaged with another mob (that pissed me off seeing him looting while everyone else is getting beat on). So this time I let him know in the group channel and everyone agreed with me. Next thing I know, it was a controlled situation and everyone had a chance to loot and share the riches from this great hunt.

Ya this is beating the ol' horse, but it really annoys me to see this kind of behavior. Maybe this guy is used to being in groups where nobody says anything. He ended up getting razzed a bit later on by the rest of the group.

History Lesson:

Ninja looting originally meant sneaking up (like a ninja) and looting a person/groups bodies before they got to them. Its evolved into meaning any unfair looting. Just like KSing used to mean letting someone else soften up a mob and moving to get the kill and the exp with a nuke or a charm. Now it means taking a mob once someone else has gotten aggro.
