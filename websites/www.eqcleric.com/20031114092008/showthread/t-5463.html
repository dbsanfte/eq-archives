Pena
Tell your friend to remember that being a battle cleric still means he's a cleric - not a rogue, ranger, paladin, warrior, or any other class.

Personally, I hate meleeing, but some people really enjoy it. It's possible to do so easily in an experience group, but on a raid (which it sounds like is where you're having the main problem, since he wanted to be in the MA group) said cleric runs several risks.

A) That he'll be in the wrong place at the wrong time, and get flattened on a pull.
B) That he'll take an ugly riposte and get to low-HP aggro levels, and get flattened on a kill.
C) That he'll be so busy meleeing, he'll forget to keep an eye on the group, and one of them will die due to his lack of attention.
D) That he'll take an AE (depending on the raid) that he shouldn't, and be unable to heal - or so busy healing himself, he'll be unable to keep others alive - which, at the end of the day, is really his responsibility.

Clearly I don't need to lecture you, but some "battle clerics" seem to forget that our responsibility is to keep everyone alive. While that's not always feasible, I find that if I'm worried about positioning, and how often my hammer is proccing, and whether I need a haste buff or not - I'm far less concerned with everyone else's health.

On a raid, that simply won't do. It's selfish, and it's dangerous to a raid. Perhaps if he wants to mess around and not heal, he should remove himself from the raiding scene. Or better yet - go make a paladin or a rogue or a warrior - or any of the classes that are heavy on melee, low on attention span.

To those who listen to my lecture above and still think "oh, but I want to melee, and it won't hurt anything, and I, unlike every battle cleric she's ever run into, can pay attention on a raid, and assist heal, and do my job while still meleeing!", then my final advice is always the same. The biggest rule for battle clerics, from my perspective, is: "If you are meleeing, that had better mean that 1) you are full mana, and 2) everyone in your group is full health."

And for the record - rangers are wonderful cleric healers, in both xp groups and on raids. I can't begin to count the number of times I've been saved by a ranger who is on the ball and hitting me with chloroblast.
