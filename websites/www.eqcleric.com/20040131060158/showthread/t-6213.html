I'd say it's worth it if your guild's tanks have 10k+ HP for sure. If all your guild clerics but you have 9k CHs then I'm guessing you are in a high end guild.

I think something to consider is the investment to make HA3/AHA3 really worth it for you. Most of the archetype abilities do not begin to really have any effect until the second level. Basically, it all comes down to your play style. Just like the MGB discussion, if you're not a raid cleric, don't bother with it (the MGB skill). HA really shines if you do a lot of spam healing (supernal remedy/light). If you're a heal over time person, or CH fiend (non-raid), then HA really isn't going to do too much for you. If you're waiting for 20% hp on a tank with 9k HP to heal, well, that just isn't logical, and you're likely a raid cleric anyhow, so you'd want HA3/AHA3 anyhow ;) Otherwise the normal 7500 heal is sufficient for most people.

And no, Healing adept does not affect HoT spells, only instant heals (CH, SR, SL, words of restoration, etc.).

However, Spell casting reinforments DO lengthen HoT's (to include CR, extended enhancement focus items do not extend CR however). Nice to have longer lasting buffs, but I think longer lasting HoT spells is a much better reason to spend points in SCR.

On a side note, it was mentioned that focus items do not affect heal over time spells... This is mostly true of course, but I understand that the Extended Blessing aug (10%) from the North Ro adventurer merchants is an exception to this. If you look up Extended Blessing vs Extended Enhancement IV on Lucy for example, you'll see that the minimum durations are listed as 24 and 48 seconds respectively. In other words this aug would work for Supernal Elixir, Quiessence (SHM have done tests on this), and I'd assume Celestial Regeneration too. Of course, 10% isn't a lot in this case.
