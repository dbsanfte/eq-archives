I posted this on the CR boards today, and thought it would be neat to move the topic over here...

I'm rather new to the boards, and I noticed a lot of "Hi I'm new to the boards" posts, so I thought a round of introductions was in order. Here is my story... :)

quote:
I've always been a huge Dragonlance fan. I started reading the books in High School and was hooked immediately. I didn't know anyone who played AD&D and didn't know where to go to find people who did. If I had known I would have played. I wanted to play. I wanted to roleplay in the world of my heroes.

Years later a friend of mine (who is a huge Forgotten Realms fanatic) handed me a copy of the original Dark Elf trilogy. He was sick of hearing me try to compare Dragonlance heroes to Drizzt (we would often argue...) when I'd never read a thing about Drizzt. So, I read his books (which I have yet to return...) I was enchanted immediately, and before I even knew what happened I had read ALL of the Drizzt books.

And though I loved the Dark Elf books, they did not hold the same magic for me that Dragonlance has always held. So, he suggested I read this other trilogy...a triligy he was sure would impress me more than the Dark Elf books, and would replace Dragonlance as my favorite fantasy epic... So, I rushed off to the bookstore and picked up my very own copy of Lord of the Rings. I can't tell you how amazed I was at the world Tolkien had created...he was right, Lord of the Rings by far surpassed Dragonlance as my all time favorite Fantasy epic!

A short while later another friend of mine approached me and said she was thinking of taking up online RPG gaming. There were two games she was considering, one Ultima Online had been out for a while, but had a horrible reputation. The other had just come out and was apparently tearing up the reviews, it was called EverQuest. After reviewing the websites for both games we decided to go with EverQuest. She rushed off at lunch and got her copy, I went after work and got mine....

I'll never forget what the guy at the video game store told me as he handed my copy of EverQuest into my anxious hands... I remember his words exactly:

Him: "You know the rules about this game right?"
Me: "Rules?"
Him: "Yes the rules."
Me: "Oh, you mean I can't return it if I open it cause I have to register an account and it requires the CD key, and no one else will be able to make an account with this copy of the game after me?" (My friend had filled me in after she got her copy...)
Him: "Well, yes there is that rule, but there's another one as well."
Me: "What's that?"
Him: "If you buy this game you are agreeing to leave any semblance of a social life you may have had behind you..."

I laughed and walked out of the store.

That night my friend called and told me she was playing on the Bristlebane server, so I should join that one. I agreed and logged on...

I created my first character, a Wood Elf Rogue. I will never forget logging into the game and listening to the music of Kelethin, taking my first few steps in the Elven City in the trees. I was there, I had finally found a way into the world of Dragonlance, and Drizzt, and Tolkien. There were Elves (ELVES) running around with sparkly lights flickering around them magically, leaving a trail of magic on the floor behind them. I was hooked from the start...

And though the guy at the store was right, EQ did dominate my life for a number of years, I've learned more about the value of the real world because of my time in Norrath, and I think I'm a better person for it. I do have a social life, though I didn't at first... Hehe

It makes me sad sometimes that I'll never experience the newness of EQ again, and every time I have the opportunity to help a true newbie I take it simply because when I started there was no one around to help, my friend, me, and the friends we've made since then (yes, we still play together) had to learn on our own. And helping newbies brings a bit of the original magic of EQ back to me... I remember my friends turning Clerics away from grouping with us because "our Ranger could heal"... I remember my friend falling in the well in Befallen. I remember when we first attempted to run to Qeynos, because my friend wanted to go to Erudin to get one of those "robes with a hood" for her wizard... I remember seeing my first troll...

Yet, everytime I get frustrated with EQ, angry over class balance, or any other such thing, I remember the first time the loading screen dissolved into the Elven City in the Trees, and I smile. No matter how bad things get sometimes, I'm still happy I get to enter the world of my heroes...


-M
