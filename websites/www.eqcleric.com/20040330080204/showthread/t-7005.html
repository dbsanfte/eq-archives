stratys
First: in my opinion, if you see a guild shouting/oocing for people to join, run -- don't walk -- away as fast as you can. It is quite likely a guild that is so interested in adding warm bodies that they aren't very discriminating about what those people are like. They may get some good people, but they are likely to go through some rough times as they separate the good from the not-so-good, and they may not survive the process.

There are a number of boards that have lists of guilds on each server with links to websites, and you can actually just do your own search using a search engine like www.google.com -- search for "solusek ro" and "guild" and see what you get. You can add "Everquest" if you are getting a lot of off-topic links.

Here's a site with links to 25 seemingly active guilds on solusek ro. If you don't feel comfortable talking to people about their guild, this can be a way to quietly get a peek into what the guild is like. Most guild boards have public forums -- you won't see the innermost workings of the guild, but you will likely see their charter, which will give you some idea of what type of guild they are/want to be, a roster, and generally some messages by people who are not in the guild interacting with people who are. What may be equally valuable is you get a chance to see what type of forums their guild board has, post counts to let you know how actively their members use the board, etc, to let you know if it's a guild that actively communicates. often the boards will have short clips describing the guild's latest accomplishments, screen shots, etc, that really help to give you the flavor of the guild.

Look at a number of these boards to compare them against one another. keep an eye on guild tags of people you group with -- people that you like and people you don't. If you meet one or two people from the same guild and feel like they are people you would enjoy getting to know, see if you can locate their guild. If it's not on one of the lists you come across, do a web search specifically for that guild name.

Guild boards almost all include a description of their recruitment procedures, and a forum where you can post a short message proclaiming your interest in checking them out. My personal bias is to make sure that the guild is careful about who it tags -- that they require prospective recruits to group with them, attend a few guild raids or events, etc, as a way for both sides to figure out if it's a good fit. To me, this is a sign that the guild is interested in maintaining a sense of stability and camraderie, and is not going to tag anything that breathes simply to have more bodies to throw at the next dragon.

A few other tips: check for a calandar of events, or any indication of the kinds of activities that they are currently involved in -- are these things you are interested in doing and that you would be able to take part in at your level? Also, try to get a feel for the typical time slot when members are active. Sometimes the guild's web site will state what time zone(s) they base their raiding on (or, if you can see their calendar, check what time they schedule events for). If not, do some /who all "guild name" commands at various times, and see when they have the most people on, when they seem to be gathering, etc. It's definitely a good idea to do this anyway for any guild you feel like joining: If they never seem to have more than a couple people on during hours that you typically play, it's probably not going to be a good fit.

Good luck! Being in a guild that feels comfortable is important. Take your time and explore your options until you find one you like.

Milletoux
I was lucky in that I found my guild relatively early (my shadowknight was level 35; this toon didn't even exist). I found it because I knew a couple people who were in a guild called Company of Wandering Souls. I was JUST about to ask them about COWS when I noticed that one of them, Jhet, had a different guild tag. I asked him about it and he directed me to two of the nicest people I've ever met online -- the new guild leader of Crimson Snow and her husband. We talked, I liked them, their play style and ages matched mine. They expressed a desire to build a family, slowly and carefully over time, to pick more mature players (the average age of CSers is probably now about 30 -- I should do a poll to figure it out; I know we have a few older teens but we have members in their 50s as well), and to build a no-pressure guild where people could grow and learn at their own pace. It matched my idea of a guild, so I joined. Shortly afterward, I was asked to become an officer, and today there are over 300 toons in CS (probably 50-60 active members). We haven't strayed from our original vision either. We're not an uber guild by any means, but we are solid, have a nice record of accomplishments, and more importantly, stay true to our original belief system.

Compare this to what happened to my then-level 5 enchanter, Eliste (she's 40 now). I was running her around in EC hunting bugs or some such, and was sitting down to rest. A guild that shall remain nameless (it was young at the time; it's grown a bit since then, but I still have no respect for them) was shouting in EC about how they were on a "recruiting drive". Now, although I was in Crimson Snow, I hadn't had this particular toon tagged yet, so I was tagless. This guy runs up and suddenly I see the guild invite box on my screen.

Puzzled, I declined. Brief pause. It flashes up there again.

I declined again and sent a tell "Excuse me, there must be some mistake. I've never even talked to anyone in your guild before. You must have me confused with someone else."

He sent a tell back that said something like, "No mistake. We're on a recruiting drive."

THIS is their idea of a recruiting drive? Going around to untagged folks in newbie zones and inviting them without so much as a howdy-do? I've never gotten a good GROUP that way and I can imagine what kind of guild I'd get.

I told him I was already guilded but just wasn't tagged yet. He went on and I noticed that they DID manage to pick up about 10 new members this way at least.

Best way to find a guild is find people you like, whose play styles match your own, visit their guild website, talk to people about the reputation of the guild. You're wearing the "union label" after all, and that guild's baggage will be applied to you by default. There are guilds on my server where I will happily invite members to a group without reservation, and there are some guilds I will NOT invite to a group because my experiences with their guild members have been overwhelmingly negative.

Despite the fact that I have only ever had one guild, do realize that a guild membership is not permanent and if it isn't working out for one reason or another, you have the power to leave. OTOH, guilds do talk to each other, and people who get the reputation of "guild hopping" may not get into their preferred guilds.

...Mille
