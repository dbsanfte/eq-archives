Shadowfrost
The Umbral Planes critters I'd suggest are the Spirit of Radir and the Spirit of Tawro initially, and then possibly Zelnithak if those first two come off without any major problems. You also need to be thinking about Zlandicar; although he's a bit of a step up from the epic dragons, Zlandi is a wimp compared to the big Velious named, and the main problem with him is catching him while he's up.

You also need to be starting to raid Kael Drakkal, which will have two effects: First, it will result in some (frustratingly rare) armour drops, and second, it will build up your Claws of Veeshan faction in anticipation of your early ventures to the Halls of Testing.

What you're working towards with this is a chap called Derakor the Vindicator, and Derakor represents a sharp dividing line between the family/epic guilds and the guilds who're on the point of starting some serious raiding. He's level 70, respawns every eight hours, and his common drop is a breastplate which represents a decent item for your tanks, shamans, and bards. More importantly, Derakor is where you'll learn CH ring tactics and rampage management. When he's trivial for you, the Velious Royals (Dain, Tormax, Yelinak) are your new targets while you build up a set of keys for Sleeper's Tomb.

The Derakor the Vindicator encounter requires you to deal with a critter with over 100,000hp, who hits for 700+ and rampages. Unlike PoP critters which rarely hit for their maximum, Derakor will hit for 700 most of the time unless your tank is defensive. High end raiding guilds might kill him without a CH ring and sneer at him as a trivial encounter, but a younger guild shouldn't take him lightly.

Dulcinea
Velkator the Sorcer is a great target to learn. He needs a small cheal rotation but he's not as tough as Vindi. Since he's deep in Velk's, killing him is a great chance for your pullers to learn how to move a raid force and for your raid to practice listening and following directions. Velk can be pulled to the area outside of his room and pinned or you can rush his room as a raid force. Both tactics are great to learn early.
