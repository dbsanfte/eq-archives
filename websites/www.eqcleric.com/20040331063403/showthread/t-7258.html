I think at best it takes your character 30 seconds to disappear after /quit, so I can't envision a lot of cases where you'd want to use it instead of camping. Avoiding faction hits for boss mobs might be one reason, I don't think you take hits if you /quit first although I could be completely wrong on this. Maybe someone else knows.

BDA is a good way to get around this if it's a raid with other clerics, I think you'd have to time it exactly...then there's the issue of the other cleric surviving those same 27 seconds. That is unless BDA overwrites DA on other clerics, I haven't tried this either.

I wish BDA could be used while sitting, but that'd be ridiculously overpowering.

I don't think you can even take AE melee or spell damage under DA. I was thinking getting summoned might interrupt camping out though, maybe someone else knows whether or not it does as well.

Meatwaggon
I've done both, but I seem to die less often with /quit.

The only possible advantage of camping is that you get to be there for all 30 seconds and can theoretically do something to help yourself along. I have never found that to be the case, however. The only situation in which I have had to consider camping is during a raid, and more specifically when fighting a boss. I don't know about anybody else, but I have NEVER been able to root a boss, channel heals through a boss's attacks (what's the point anyway?), or in fact anything else to prevent a boss from killing me once it has decided to do so. Once it has decided to kill me, I die within a few seconds anyway, and therefore have little opportunity to do much of anything to stop it.

In my view, sitting and camping just makes the situation even worse. It is likely that you're already on the boss's hate list since you've been healing for a while, and if you sit down to camp, your position on that list goes way way up. In other words, the boss will turn to kill you much sooner than it would have otherwise done if you had NOT sat down. If you simply /quit, you don't have to sit down, and the hate list gets attended to by the boss as it would normally. You are in Norrath for approximately the same amount of time with either /quit or /camp, so DA would not cover the last 12 seconds either way. DAing up before quitting is still good, since it prevents you from casting or attacking the boss if it attacks you. Then again, it doesn't matter anyway, since once it's on you, you're going to die unless someone can steal aggro from you (a smart and selfless ranja guildie friend, for example).

Copperhead Fang
Let me consider only the situation of being on a raid that is wiping out.

1. Do you want to "camp out" at all? In other words, will you be able to reenter EQ at some later point and help your raid recover? You need to know the placing, spawn times, agro ranges, etc of the area you are in in order to really do this right. If your raid leader says "camp" though, it's probably a good bet to just do it right where you are and as soon as possible.

2. Are you likely to get hit with AE spells at the place where you are trying to exit the game? If yes, /quit is not an option since being hit with a harmful AE spell causes the NPC version of you to attack the AE caster.

3. How high on the agro list are you? In other words, will you draw agro within the next 30 seconds by sitting? If you are able to sit for that long, then /camp is preferable to /q because A) You remain in control the entire time B) If you do die, you know exactly where you died (with /q you are left as an NPC for a while which can move) and C) You are guaranteed to be out in 30 seconds (with /q, it could be longer). If you do not think that you'll be able to avoid agro while sitting for that long and there are no AEs in effect, then /q might be the better option.

During a pickup BoT towers raid a few days ago (the SW water AE mana/HP draining mini-boss), we were in the process of wiping and the RL called for me to camp (I was the only cleric, with 2 droods), and I decided to once again experiment with /camping.
