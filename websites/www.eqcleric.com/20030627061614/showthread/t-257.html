Shadowfrost
*grins*

One of the things about the EQ economy is that for most items, prices are in a state of continual free-fall, while the amount of platinum in the economy tends to increase.

(Yes, I know about fungi tunics and boots of flowing slime. I said, `for most items.`)

As an illustration of this, with Velious, I had enormous trouble getting hold of a copy of Celestial Healing. I was level 53 when I finally scribed it. The standard price for the spell at the time was 4,000pp and I could no more get hold of 4k than I could fly; I think the most I'd ever had was 1,300pp at the time.

Nowadays Celestial Healing is a few pp in the bazaar, and people sell the spell to vendors.

Well, the same is happening and will continue to happen to PoP spells. There are some people who have enough platinum to fill their spellbooks first, and they'll pay the prices. Once those people have filled their spellbooks, prices will come down until the next bunch of people are prepared to pay to fill their spellbooks, and then they'll come down again.

Eventually rune spells will sell for a few thousand pp, but in the meantime there are a lot of people hunting for the spells and competition forces the prices up.

As for guild leaders making RL cash... the allegation's been made, and in a few cases it's probably true, but I think it probably doesn't go on all that much. I've been leader of a raiding guild (Blood Cult on Xev) and I can tell you that guilds have a lot of things to spend their money on.

The weekly peridot bill used to be a fairly enormous item all by itself. ;)
