Braeaelin
First you take a screen shot of your assembled character then paste it into your favorite imaging software. For myself, I press the Print Screen key which copies the visible screen area to the Windows clipboard (alt+PrtScn copies only the active window) then open up Photoshop. I create a new canvas in PS and paste there. Then I crop the image to leave only my character with a bit of space around it. Then I index the colours and export it to a transparent gif, selecting the background colour provided by the website (either dark or light) as the transparency. If that background colour is represented in my character as well, I will first use the Magic Wand tool with a tolerance level of 0 to select the background colour manually, then delete it. Otherwise parts of my character will be transparent as well. I will then place a new background colour of one that is not represented in my character (usually green 0.255.0) then index and export it to a transparent gif.

If you don't have Photoshop, Paint Shop Pro or similar imaging software, there may be demos you can find of something useful. If you have a scanner, chances are it came with software that can do this.

What you're looking for mainly is the ability to crop and save as a .gif or .jpg. Transparency is nice, but is not necessary.
