Shadowfrost
Mashing a key 10,000 times isn't using your brain! :)

It was hard for Shadowfrost, when I made her. You didn't respawn with all your gear, so I lost my stuff at level 4. You didn't start with a lot of helpful tips. You couldn't make a lot of money easily; I made my first money selling bone chips for 5 gold a stack. When I'd saved enough money, I went and bought a copy of Enchant Silver, and took it out into the EC tunnel to sell it. After three hours' vigorous marketing, I went home with 5pp profit - an astounding fortune for me in those days. I spent it on a Banded Bracer, which meant a lot to me then.

Getting a group at level 10 was practically impossible. There were waiting lists for all the orc camps, and eventually a SK friend and I went out to Sister Island in the Ocean of Tears - a camp which we shared with three other groups - just to get some xp. Most newbie dark elves now wouldn't even know where Sister Island IS unless they were smiths.

And those were the days of the Vision (TM). That meant pointless BS; things like spiderling silks didn't stack so you couldn't sell them at a reasonable profit. Medding was just staring at your spellbook - you couldn't see what was going on around you, which made you a sitting target. And experience was nothing like as fast as it is nowadays - it took me several DAYS /played to get to level 14.

At level 14, I sat at the West Gate in Freeport, binding people for donations, in order to make enough money to buy the rest of my level 14 spells. The average donation was a few silver.

Yeah, new characters nowadays have it easy. But compared to those of us who started in Original EQ - or even Kunark era - most of you lot had it pretty easy too.

Nowadays, nobody camps the Temple of Solusek Ro armour. (Ask a really old cleric one day what they had to go through to get their Sol Ro bracer.) Likewise, you can all get your epics without having to camp Zordak Ragefire; I spent over 300 hours at that camp. Collecting shards might seem long to you, but I'm telling you, it's EASY.

If you don't know what it meant to own a PKT - if you don't know what drops the Abalone Gorget and you've never participated in a three-group raid on the Ghoul Lord - then don't complain that other people had it easy.

I'm here to tell you that removing pointless BS like having to mash the Sense Heading hotkey 10,000 times as you level up is a good change for the game, ladies and gentlemen.
