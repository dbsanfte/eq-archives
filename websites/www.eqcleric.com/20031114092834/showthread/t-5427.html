Restandre
The rampage list is essentially the order people get on the hate list. So if warrior_01 tags the mob, then warrior_02 actually tanks it, warrior_01 will be on rampage as long as he is an elgible target for the mob from where the mob is. If the RT is out of range, dead, FD, or whatever, rampage will pass to the next person. So the clerics end up with rampage after everyone is dead that generated hate prior to the first heal landing. Would-be rampage tanks ideally should be performing a nominal tag of some kind. (Not necessarily to finalize the pull.) A monk pulling could be the RT from simply not feigning off aggro.

The key thing to remember about the list of people on the rampage list is that it is permanent, so it has to be done right. Once you are on that list, nobody can move ahead of you, and the only way you won't get rampaged is if you are out of melee range. (Some mobs have an even larger rampage radius.) It is possible to pass it to the next person by hiding or putting some distance between you, but you cannot actually remove your position by anything. Not even dying or camping.

DA will not protect you from being rampaged, but it will shield you from the blows...paladins pulling with Divine Aura make great rampage tanks since they can walk the mob in for the MT without being hurt, and block the first few rampages until hopefully the mob is slowed and/or crippled. Monks often end up with it if they haven't successfully feigned off aggro before the first rampage. For mobs that don't move, or are leashed -- Rhag 1, i.e. -- debuff the mob and then mem blur it, which will reset the hate list before rampage has occured. Then have your tanks each fire an arrow.


Disclaimer: This is a technicality, but many people claim that the rampage list is based on the actual hate list at the moment the mob first rampages (and adds to the list with each rampage.) I don't necessarily believe this idea, but then again mem blurring the mob before fighting will keep you from being hated after buffing a monk that was pulling the adds around him (or someone else who had).

Shadowfrost
Things to understand about basic (i.e. not AE) rampage:

(1) Rampage is a proc. The frequency with which a critter rampages goes down noticeably once you can debuff its DEX.

(2) Rampage has a larger range than normal melee. Someone standing some distance from the critter can still be rampaged. A mob can rampage someone who's behind them with equal ease to someone who's in front.

(3) Your position on the rampage list, once established, is set in stone until the critter is dead or back at full hp. Even if the Rampage tank dies and a new one takes over, but you then rez the old Rampage tank, the old one will be back on Rampage.

(4) You establish your position on the Rampage list by the order in which you aggro the mob. Have your designated Rampage tank shoot the critter with an arrow as it comes in before anyone else creates aggro, and you're fine.

The definitive Rampage management encounter is probably Arch Lich Rhag`Zhadune (known informally as Arch Lich Damn-He-Rampages-A-Lot). You'll need two CH rings - one for the main tank, one for the RT.

Healing rampage

1. EVERYONE should have an I am on RAMPAGE hot key.
2. The person eating rampage is NEVER the person top on the aggro list, so assist healing will never target the rampage tank.
3. Designate a rampage healer, preferrably one that can type /tar tank, quickly and accurately.
4. For mobs like the AL, have a dedicated rampage healing team. A druid cheal rotation works well here.
