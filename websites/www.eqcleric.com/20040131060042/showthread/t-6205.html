Shadowfrost
That's not precisely true, Moonsorrow. There are a couple of critters out there which are really unique, and can be defeated easily using the correct strategy but can wipe out huge raid forces in seconds if the wrong technique is employed.

A large part of the fun of EQ is figuring these things out for yourself. Nevertheless, I'll make reference to a few of the more basic strategies or techniques which raid guilds use.

1. Corner tanking.

The tank stands in the corner and the melees push the monster into him. The effect of this is that all melees except the tank are behind the monster, and therefore don't get riposted - very important with big-hitting critters, and when your guild first takes on Vindi or the AoW, they feel like a very big hitters.

2. AE sheltering.

During the Velious and Luclin expansions, there are monsters in indoor dungeons. These monsters may have very large AE, but for the AE to work, you have to be in line of sight of the critter. You can heal from outside line of sight; this means that you can park your healers near the tank but around a corner out of the AE. Important for encounters like Gozzrem.

3. Single-tanking.

First time I used this was on Ventani the Warder, again during Velious. Ventani was a boring old dragon (same model as Lady Vox, if I remember correctly) with what was, for the time, a huge AE - but the real problem was that his AE had a silence component. If the AE hit you, you weren't casting any spells for several minutes - but (at the time) he needed a 3 second CH ring.

His weak point was, he didn't have that many HP.

The effective strategy we found was to park one tank in front of him, in range of the healers, and hide everyone else around a corner. Every time the AE hit, the tank would call "AE" and we'd know that we had a few seconds when we could run out, cast, and run back and hide again. Only our tank was standing in front of Ventani, so his position was tightly controlled.

I've intentionally concentrated on old content with widely-publicised strategies, by the way. More advanced stuff you need to figure out on your own.
