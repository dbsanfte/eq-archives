Isig
My very first question would be ... WHY? Having asked that, I'll attempt to answer so as not to be a troll.

If I've understood what I've read regarding attack rating it's based on several things--strength (either directly or indirectly), the offense skill, and weapon skill (piercing or 1-hand blunt in a chanter's case). Raising any of these three raises ATK. The two you can "skill up" are offensive skill and weapon skill, and you can only do this by swinging away. DEX is a good buff to get, but I don't know about any brain-buffs (WIS/INT?) helping at all. I know smithing is affected by STR, so offense/weap skill ups might come faster to someone with STR buffs.

One point to remember is that you'll have to skill up with EACH weapon type (piercing/1HB) that you want to use. Either will raise offensive skill, but each is independent of the other in terms of raising weapon skill (obviously). So, you may get good skills and therefore good ATK with piercing, but your 1HB ATK rating can still blow.

Coming from a rogue background, I have a strat I would suggest. If you are grouping (and Lord, I hope you are...) your intent should be to never gain aggro. Having said that, procs cause aggro, so do NOT use your illusion that procs a lifetap. What you are looking for is overall hits, therefore, speed/haste (as you surmise) is the best tactic here. I'd suggest a VERY low damage and low delay weapon. Low delay equals more hits, low damage means you aren't pulling as much aggro. You should aim to hit like a paperweight. Get behind the mob and keep tickling its backside and your skillups will come.

~Isig

Ok ... here's the "simple" deal on Combat skills etc.

1H Blunt - it's a weapon skill and you can get it up by either using a 1H blunt weapon, or using training points at the Guildmaster. it caps at 175.

Offense - This is a more general combat skill, which you can get up by using any weapon or hand to hand fighting. You can get this up by either going into combat and beat stuff up, using points at the guildmaster -or- let something beat on you while you have a Damage Shield up ! For some arcane reason, Damage Shields makes your offence skill go up. it caps at 200.

Bash - This is also a weapon skill, that we can train at level 25. You use the weapon type "shield" in your secondary hand and use the Bash skill which you can make a hotbutton for just like Autoattack. You can train this by putting 1 point into it at levle 25 at a guildmaster and then happily boink MOBs on the head with a shield in hand. Or just spend a lot of training points in it. It caps at skill 180 pre level 50 and at 200 post level 50.

The governing stat that helps raise all 3 skills is DEXTERITY.
-- Shamans can cast some very good DEX buffs.
-- Enchanters can cast Boon of the Garou, which turns you into a Werewolf, gives you more raw ATK and also raises your DEX by 100.
-- Clerics have the Yaulp V and Yaulp VI also adds DEX ... 75 DEX from Yaulp V , and 90 DEX from Yaulp VI.


So what is ATK ?
ATK is an amalgam made up of :
... Weapon Skill
... Offense Skill
... Class bonuses (Rangers gets some innate bonuses for sure)
... Strength
... +ATK items (such as Vengeance items)
... +ATK buffs such as Boon of the Garou, Yaulp IV, several high end Ranger buffs and Shaman's Avatar buff.

The Weapon Skill and Offence skill stand for the biggest part of your ATK rating though, which is how EverQuest balances Melee classes against each other.



Also, since you get skill ups by hitting things, hitting them as fast as possible is a good idea !
Items with +Haste is a good thing, there's a lot of different items out there ranging from 5% to 41%. And not to forget, buffs - Enchanters gets some awesome Haste buffs. Also, Yaulp V & VI have Haste in them which seems to stack with all other forms for haste.


Also, getting a weapon with a low delay helps.
Clerics get a nice line of summoning hammers at level 56+ that are actually very fast, they are known as the Hammer of Judgement / Divinity / Souls / Damnation which only have a Delay 18 and procs a lot. I currently use Hammer of Judgement which I love a lot.

However, a 1 handed weapon can never hit faster than a 10 Delay and a 2 handed never faster than 25. Also, there's a hardcap on how much haste you can have, which is at 110% as far as I know.


You can get skillups on both hits and misses, however, the MOB you are hitting must be within EXP range, so you get no skill ups from green stuff (or at least abmysally slow).


So .. there ... go beat stuff up. :D
