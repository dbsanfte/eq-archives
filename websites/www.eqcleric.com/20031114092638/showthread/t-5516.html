Tactics? Ive found all you really need to get these adventures done are high DPS and a cleric. Of course shammy and enchanter and the rest are useful as well. Get yourself 2 good tanks, a cleric and then fill in the rest as you can.
Dont muck about in the dungeon. Fully buff and med before entering. Go in and start immediately. Those that want pets can get them as soon as they enter and then begin fighting straight away, you can buff pets as you go.

Milletoux
My shadowknight main had a couple early bad runs but four successes in a row. I haven't been so lucky, but although the group can definitely fail because of the lack of a good cleric, the group cannot absolutely win because of one.

The keys seem to be:

- a fast, steady puller who keeps the mobs coming
- good DPS (I like a rogue as a secondary melee because of high DPS and the ability to disarm traps).
- alternative forms of mana management to keep the cleric and casters as full up as possible (necromancers, beastlords, mages for mod rods -- necros being my personal favorite) to avoid downtime
- a slower
- a good method for crowd control (mezzing or pacify splitting) -- adds beat down the melees and healing that much eats a lot of mana
- keeping your eye on the goal (don't get distracted with any mobs that won't aggro, leave chests and such alone til the end)
- MAPS (maps.eq-toolbox.com) in the LoY map system to direct you in the right direction so you aren't wasting a lot of time clearing side rooms. Not necessary -- you can get the key ratios while staying on a direct course. Wait to clear side rooms or kill named mobs until after the adventure is completed.

When I am acting as puller with my shadowknight, I don't waste time with mana checks. I tell and expect the casters to tell me if they are low on mana and need a break, but otherwise I assume they are managing their own mana.

It is going to take some time for pullers to learn how to pull a dungeon because it is different from pulling other areas. With other areas, pullers mostly have time to study the lines and make sure everyone is ready for a pull. With a dungeon, you don't have time to waste.

...Mille

Merrybrook
Here's a strategy that I use, that I've found works well for me. Keep in mind, I'm level 58 (not very high level), and am using all the spells available to me at this time. I imagine things will probably be very different as I get higher, but for now this is working well. :)

First off, everything everyone said about buffing before you zone in, and no one zoning in until everyone is ready is true.

But more from a strategy standpoint, during combat, this is how I do things. There's not a lot of med time in LDoN, at least not if you want to finish the mission in time, so you've got to find alternatives to medding. Make sure you have KEI, either from a chanter in the group, or from someone in PoK. Always make sure you have Blessed Armor of the Risen (or it's upgrade) on.

As soon as the puller comes in, let the MT take aggro then hit the puller with a Cel heal (provided your puller is not your tank, this actually works better if your puller is your tank), and then wade into combat, by the time the tank is around 80%hps you should be FoM, hit the tank with a cel heal. The mob should be dead, or nearly dead by the time the cel heal wears off leaving your tank at or nearly at 100% hps. While you're fighting, keep Yaulping, it's a nice mana regen while standing, and will quickly fill the little mana you lose from cel healing often. This allows you to keep fighting (adding dps) and not run low on mana. You'll be standing pretty much the whole time, so you can also avoid sit aggro.

I've been using the strategy with great success. Last night we did a rescue in MM, the group was me, Pally, Monk, Necro, Chanter, Beast (level range was 54 (monk) to 61 (paladin), we finished the mission with 20 minutes left, and I never dropped below 80m. It's great with lots of DPS, but I've found this strategy works well even if you don't have lots of dps. I was grouped with me, Warrior, Druid, and chanter and it worked just as well.

The thing to remember is to keep yaulping, keep your mana regen buffs up, and keep cel healing the tank at around 80% hps.

Of course this is all provided your tank is keeping aggro and other members of the group are not getting slammed. I've found though that most folks around this level know how to play their classes fairly well and aside from the occasional aggro draw from a slower/tasher, things are fine.

Give it a shot and let me know if it works as well for you as it has for me. :)
