Azeal
This is my first time posting here, and this may seem like a dumb question, but exactly how do you heal in PoP? I've had my cleric for years, but once SoL came out I made a beastlord and fell in love with him, but I am missing my cleric more and more and actually would like to start him up again. However, I've been going to the same spots to group that I was able to solo with my Beastlord (PoJ,PoD,PoI,PoN), and I simply can't figure out how to heal right.
Either:
A) My CH hit too early (70% life on MT), so I am wasting a ton of mana
B) My CH lands too late (I start casting CH at 60% life on MT), or
C) The slower grabs aggro, and I can't heal them fast enough (even with Remedy/Divine Light), so the mob doesn't get slowed, I have to chain heal the MT, catch aggro, and die.

As well, I don't have any Velious, SoL, PoP, or LoY spells for the cleric, so is there some spell or line of spells that I jsut need to grab up?

Shadowfrost
It's just experience, I'm sorry to say. You learn how meaty your tank is, and you learn how hard each critter hits, and you judge your CH'es accordingly.

At 54, Kunark spells are fine, although a complete spellbook is nice you don't actually need it. By the time you hit 58, you need Blessed Armor of the Risen (Luclin drop spell), and at 60 you'll need Aegolism (Velious drop spell) and Blessing of Aegolism (Luclin drop spell). After that you can get the upgrades you need from the Planes of Power.

Braeaelin
Quite right about how the situation depends on the tank. Before a MoB is slowed the situation is usually pretty hairy. You can either cast Celestial Healing right away on the tank then depending on the rapidity of the tank's health declining you then cast Complete Heal with the Celestial pumping HPs into the tank while you cast. This isn't exactly mana efficient, but helps. Otherwise if a MoB isn't slowed, I usually start cheal around 60% and when slowed in the 40% range. But it all depends on the situation and you'll learn in time. Whatever you do, don't try to save your cheal as long as possible but then again don't cast too early. Just keep in mind it's better to be safe than sorry.

Monitor the speed your tank and the target's health declines by hitting the /assist key to toggle between the two and you'll begin to establish proper timing. This is what I watch most of the time, aside from group chat which I only glance at. Timing is critical and if it helps, count the seconds from when the tank is at 100% to give you an idea of how much damage is done knowing a cheal takes ten seconds to cast.

Merrybrook
I agree with Brae, usually I will cast a cel heal while the tank is incomming, this gives me some breathing room until the mob is slowed. With a slowed mob, CHEAL at around 40 or 50% health works fine for most tanks, just depends on how badly your meat shield is getting beat up. If it looks like my CHEAL isn't going to land in time I usually will duck out of it, drop a remedy, then a divine light (if necessary). That should give you some time, and by then you should see how hard your tank is getting hit and be able to better time your CHEAL.

If your slower catches aggro, Remedy is your best friend (I've found this to be true with Chanters as well as Shaman). If you're not getting it off in time you may want to look into purchasing Blessing of Faith if you don't already have it. If you do have it, keep it on yourself at all times, Remedy is a fast cast, and even more so with BoF up.

It'll come to you with practice. :)
