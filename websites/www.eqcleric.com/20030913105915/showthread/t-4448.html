Jarvis
I can definitely agree with exp-grinding being tedious and boring. If you do nothing but work on experience you're going to burn out fast.

Being in a high level, very capable guild makes everything more fun and exciting. I love logging on to find we're doing one raid after the other, especially on newer encounters that haven't become routine yet.

Doing the flagging in PoP can get boring, especially since you have to keep back-flagging for your guild to have the necessary force to counter upper level mobs. However, now that we have access to the elemental planes things are more fun for sure.

A full group gets 2-3% AA per kill in Plane of Earth for instance, and there's plenty of mobs worth a raid-strike if only to try something new. (Getting your ass kicked does indeed happen...)

If you get tired of exp'ing, and your guild doesn't raid as much as you'd like, there's nothing wrong with working some tradeskills, or doing some quests. I hear there are plenty of quests in EverQuest ;)

Although tradeskilling is very repetitious, it's also very rewarding if you can master one of them. If you become a GM tradeskiller you have a definite source of income as well.

One thing I like to do when I get tired of the grind, and raids are not happening at the moment, is to explore. Believe it or not but there are still plenty of zones I have yet to see and explore well. It's fun to go back to zones that could pose a threat before and just check it out.

One example was ToFS (Tower of Frozen Shadows). I had never spent much time there 'growing up' and I decided to check it out with my brother who playes a mage. He was in his mid 50's at the time. We ended up duoing the entire tower including Tserrina, and it was a lot of fun. So if you have a buddy and you work well as a team, don't be afraid to attempt some mobs/zones you never did before. You might be surprised.

EverQuest is all about having fun. It's a very modular, free game, and as such it can take some imagination to keep the interest going. If you think about how big this game is, and how much you can do it's actually quite overwhelming. Try not to get stuck in the same old grind all the time and mix it up a bit. Even if you don't constantly progress your character your EQ experience will be a lot more rewarding and fulfilling.
