Cerrick
Personally, I do not think its possible for you to play this game and not roleplay to some extent. In my experience there are those who attempt to take on a classic roleplaying genre for thier class and have fun doing this. Others, choose to be themselves in as their character. There is an evolution though that I have seen. I for one am not entirely like Cerrick. Nor are my real life friends like their characters.
Also, seems like you can gerenalize groupings of classes and the people that play them. Rangers for instance seem to be quiet folks, not altogether engaguing in conversation. I think the play style of the diffent classes lends itself to how much a person might roleplay or moreso, how their character might appear to the rest of the online players. Clerics have a tendancy to talk alot (cause we sit around and med). Casters in general have less button pushing to do lending themselves to more outgoing people, or folks that would choose to say as much as they do.

EQ is a roleplaying game in as much as anything totally online is. Some choose to take this a step further, others leave it as that.. avatar's of thier own persona.

Point is to have fun, and not ruin someone elses gaming experience by trying to force your RP approach down other people's throat.. that tend to make them want to vomit.

Rauri
Each guild is different! Some are high powered raiding guilds that require your attendance as a condition of staying in their guild. Some are roleplaying guilds. Some are small groups that all know each other in RL. They really are quite different.

The point is, a guild is just a community of like-minded individuals who play for the same purpose. You shouldn't have any trouble finding a guild that shares your purpose for playing. Ask around, guilds really do enhance your gaming experience and make EQ much more fun!
