Shadowfrost
Raising your own tradeskills will not make you money unless and until you are a Grandmaster. Working up your tradeskills is a huge, direct investment of time and platinum. Even after you are a Grandmaster, Brewing, Baking, Fishing, or Fletching will not make you rich.

Pottery is a relatively easy and relatively cheap skill to raise, but Grandmaster potters are becoming quite common now. Jewelcraft is also easy to raise but requires more money.

If you would like to be a Grandmaster Tailor or Smith, you're embarking on an EQ career which will absorb hundreds of hours. Personally I'd advise you to start by creating a necromancer on a second account and powerlevelling that character to at least level 50 so they can farm the components for you... honestly, I think that's probably the easiest way. ;)

In pp per hour, you're much better off soloing HG's in the Rathe Mountains or Guard in Sebilis than raising your tradeskills.

However, there is a flip side to this bleak picture. Many people with surplus pp enjoy raising their tradeskills, and they may well be prepared to purchase tradeskill supplies from you.

A good way of raising money is to farm leather paddings. You'll need to be level 20+, and you do it like this:-

1. Buy a sewing kit and a skinning knife; the skinning knife is made by smiths.
2. Go to the Feerrott and head to the standing stones.
3. Kill all the spiderlings there, which are plentiful and respawn quickly, until you have a couple of stacks of spiderling silks.
4. Combine two spiderling silks in your sewing kit to make a silk thread. Repeat this until you have a stack of them.
5. Get a port to the Stonebrunt Mountains, or if you're a little higher level and a little more confident you can get there through the Warrens.
6. Kill the leopards and leopardesses. You want Low Quality pelts; but if you get Medium Quality or High Quality pelts, you can combine them with the skinning knife in your sewing kit to downgrade them to Low Quality. (Other kinds of pelt are not useful for tradeskills.) Repeat this until you have a stack of them.
7. Combine a Low Quality pelt with a Silk Thread to make a Leather Padding.
8. Sell the Leather Paddings in the Bazaar to the highest bidder.
