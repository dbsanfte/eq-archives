Shadowfrost
In answer to the original question:- It's okay for a cleric to melee if that's the most useful thing you could be doing. As a general rule, that only happens between levels 1 and 20, and after level 56; between 21 and 55, you should be medding full-time, and if you have any spare mana left over, nuke!

A lot of clerics in their 60's melee with our summoned hammer. But personally, I don't melee much any more.

The reason for that is that of everyone in the group, I do the least damage. That means that I'm the member of the group who can most easily be spared to go and pull while the rest of the group is finishing off the monster. If I can spare time and mana, I tend to nuke rather than meleeing.

So in LDoN, I'm not a tanking cleric, I'm a pulling wizzicleric.

Restandre
You should not melee under the following situations:
1) Your group is requiring your full medical attention. (The usual reason - that is why you're there.)
2) You're medding back from something that dropped you low. (Lots of buffs, nuked a little too much, crisis healing)
3) You're getting in the way. (Blocking rangers and rogues, you can't fit around 5 beastlords with their pets)
4) You'd rather be nuking. (Out of desire or usefulness)
5) You feel you would be more useful pulling.
6) Misc. other things occupying your attention. (Tells and RL distractions, playing ghetto CC)
7) You conclude that your meleeing is useless. (Don't let this get you down.)

Any other time you should melee if you are so inclined. If you are of the belief that you should nuke instead, then you REALLY should be meleeing unless you're feeling lazy. If you're nuking, you obviously have mana to burn and are focused on damage output, so why not stand next to the mob to do it and swing while waiting for your nuke to recycle? If I'm dealing with a mob that requires a spell interrupt, I'll always get near it, and sometimes melee while waiting for the "A Vann Geistlig begins to cast the gate spell."

Often I will get tired of meleeing after a while and stop, but I think it's fun for a change, especially during the times when you "aren't useful" as a melee class. I used to always be a butt-caster. For 60 levels I was. Now I melee when I have an excuse.

Heh, gotta love those idiots. He may very well have a cleric...and bought into that tripe.

We can melee just fine, and still heal the group (in many, not all, situations). The trick is learning when you can and when you can't.

This is not to say that our melee skills are our strong suit...obviously, a melee class can and should do it better.

But even without level 56 and it's nifty hammer, we can contribute just fine to DPS. My cleric is about equal to my shaman in melee damage...of course, the shaman has the pet and the slows\hastes\uber buffs, so overall he is better...

A non fighting cleric is a broken cleric (IMNSHO)...your defensive skills don't get raised and you will be in a world of pain if you need to fight to survive.

Healing comes first, yes. But you can still get in and swing away. Or sit back and nuke, if you prefer (though as someone already said, if you are so mana heavy you can nuke, you should also be standing and swinging between casts)

Remember, slowers are your best friends...they cut down how much mana you have to use for heals. That gives you more time to return the hurt'n favor to the mobs.

Of course, as Shadowfrost said...you may want to do pulling instead or as well.
