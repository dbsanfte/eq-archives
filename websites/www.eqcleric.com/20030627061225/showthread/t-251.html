I had been ready for the final fight of my epic for four months, in March of 2001. Nag's lair was constantly camped. The public channels in the zone were filled with foul language, people KS'd and petitioned and yelled. My monkish significant other spent days fd in the lair in hopes of getting the camp for me.

I was on the Rathe, a horribly crowded server - the only one of the original servers that had not yet been split. Finally the split was offered, we could choose to movelog to Zebuxoruk (and who could ever spell that one /rolleyes ).

Some friends and I moved. The first night on the new server, I got home from work (I live 4 hours behind the East coast) to find my friends with all their alts out, staging to kill Nagafen for me. We killed him - with no guildchat - what chaos!, and 26 hours later my Zordak spawned.

I'm still in awe that, on the first night in our new home my friends chose to do that rather than any number of MUCH more fun things that we could have done. It was a total surprise. Makes me tear up even now remembering how great it was.

Don't know about you guys - but if it wasn't for the friends I've made, and been on so many adventures with these past four years - well, four years in one game is an awful long time, isn't it? I think that while Verant may suck in some ways - what they've made is a pretty darn incredible game. Otherwise... surely people would not still be playing it four years after release date. That anniversary passed last week - March 15, 1999.

Galvatist, I camped my epic during that unhappy time. I was also an officer of a guild ( ) that camped several other cleric epics prior to the changes and was heavily involved with the spawn on Xev.

I personally discussed the matter with GM's on more than one occasion, and took part in the (abortive) serverwide campaign to get a Ragefire list set up. I also met and talked to Juliears, who was the cleric involved in what was perhaps the most controversial Rage kill on Xev, during her camp.

The incidents you referred to did indeed result in clerics having their epics stripped from them, and other clerics having Ragefire spawned for them by GM's.

However, they weren't unique to Xev. The screams of anguish from the playerbase across all servers eventually reached the attention of the BBC news network, who published an article on it. I feel that it was the BBC's article on the Ragefire spawn which finally made Verant (as it was then) wake up and smell the coffee. Xev was involved, but only peripherally really.

Personally, I found that my own Ragefire camp was what enabled me to give up smoking. Yes, seriously.

Every time I experienced a craving, I told myself, `I camped Ragefire. This can't be worse.` And that thought sustained me.

I can confirm that giving up a 20-a-day cigarette habit required considerably less willpower and force of character than completing Ragefire the hard way.
