EverQuest Melee Changes Q&A
Answered by Alan VanCouvering, Game Designer - EverQuest


Lepidus: For those who know nothing about what you've planned, can you outline your basic goals with the redesign of melee in EQ and the timetable over which this will occur?

Alan VanCouvering: The goal of the melee enhancement is to repair some balance issues between the melee classes and hybrid classes and to make playing a melee character more fun. To do this we are adding a set of skills that only melee characters can use. These skills can be used when an opponent makes a mistake or when the character makes an exceptional maneuver.

At the same time we are changing the way that disciplines work for all characters. The new skills and all disciplines will require an expenditure of endurance (a new calculated statistic). Moving disciplines to the new system allows us much more flexibility with them, and makes adjusting them much easier.

The enhancements are already on Test. We plan to move the changes to disciplines to the Live servers on December 18th. The melee skills will need more testing, and won't be moving to the Live servers until some time in January.


Lepidus: EverQuest is still the most popular North American MMORPG. Are you at all worried that despite the demand from the community, you could alienate many users with sweeping changes to such a core element as melee?

Alan VanCouvering: That's always a concern. However, I think you would find that our players would say that leaving it as is was would have been a bigger risk. :)

We always have to evaluate the potential risk of alienating folks. In this case we are not changing the way melee works at the core, we are adding an enhancement on top of that system with the hope that it will make playing a melee character more fun and address some of the concerns folks have. This certainly could be too much of a change for some players. But we feel that it makes EverQuest a much better game, so we have to take that risk.


Lepidus: The "opening system" has people's interest piqued, but how do you intend to ensure that it is fun and not just micromanagement?

Alan VanCouvering: Testing and feedback. Initial impressions from the Test server indicate that we have more tweaking to do to it than we had anticipated. So we're going to leave it there and get more feedback so we can make changes until we have it were we want it.


Lepidus: While these things may make combat more fun, fun does not always translate to balance. Will you be increasing the overall power of warrior-classes in general so that they are more competitive? If so, are you worried about creating dramatically shorter fights?

Alan VanCouvering: We worry about all of that. There have been concerns from some folks that we'd over-balance things. There is certainly a problem that needs to be addressed, we can't be afraid to fix it because of the possibility of alienating folks or of missing the mark when it comes to balance. We'll work on it until it's right on Test. I have no doubt that we'll get very close to the goal with the melee enhancement.

Lepidus: Downtime is always a bane of warrior classes when they try to advance. Are there any plans to further address this?


Alan VanCouvering: You mean when they try to advance by means of soloing, right? I've never seen a warrior in a group with a healer have an issue with downtime. These enhancements are not aimed toward improving soloing for warriors, but some of the improvements to disciplines may help out.


Lepidus: What kind of steps will be taken to ensure that fighting classes remain the 'tanks' of their group, which is to say retain the attention of monsters?

Alan VanCouvering: If you were asking about Knight classes (I'm unclear what you mean by "fighting" classes), some of the changes we are making are designed specifically to improve the warrior's ability to get "snap" aggro. We don't have any immediate plans to change the way knights gain aggro.


Lepidus: Can you explain how exactly people will go about finding "tomes"?

Alan VanCouvering: The current plan is to have them purchasable from vendors. In the future we may introduce quests or NPC drops to gain Skill and/or discipline tomes.


Lepidus: What exactly brought you to the stage where you decided that these changes had to be made?

Alan VanCouvering: As most of you know, there has been a lot of feedback from the community on this issue. That certainly was a factor. But, basically, we recognized the issue a while ago and we've been working on a functional and interesting solution since.


Lepidus: What has the feedback been on the test server to these changes so far?

Alan VanCouvering: The feedback has been mostly positive. Comments about the changes to disciplines have been very positive, but it's clear that we need more time with the melee skills on test to tune them. The feedback from folks on Test has been very helpful.


Lepidus: Will these be the last sweeping changes of this type, or do you have other areas in mind where redesign is necessary?

Alan VanCouvering: There are a few areas that we want to look at. Odds are this won't be the last time we make a change to the game. EverQuest will continue to grow and improve.
