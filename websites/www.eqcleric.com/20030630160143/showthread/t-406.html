Phixin
MT = Main Tank. The main tank is the meat shield that is doing all the taunting. He usually has the highest AC (armor Class) and the most HP (hit Points). He is suppose to keep all the agro so that your job as cleric is much easier. If the MT keeps agro, you dont have to worry so much about wasting mana healing that wizard that casted too many nukes in a row, etc...

Agro = attention. If you agro a mob, you have his attention, you are put on his HATE list. Usually you get his full, undivided attention if you hold the agro. A tank holds the agro by taunting, kicking and attacking, but whenever someone does any damage to that mob, they move up the hate list. When the tank taunts again, the last agro moves back down the list a little. If someone gets higher on the hate list than the MT, they gain agro, and you get to use extra mana healing them, if you have time. In high level encounters, you might not have time when the mobs are quad hitting for 400-600hp.

MA = Main Assist. In some groups, you will have someone doing the pulling that is not the main tank. Monks are a prime example of this. Monks are usually not the MT in a group if there is either a Warrior, Paladin, Shadow Knight, or Ranger in the group, but monks make excellent pullers. When they get to camp, everyone uses {/assist MTname} so that all are attacking the same mob. If there is an enchanter in the group, their job will be to "MEZ" or Mesmerize any ADDS or extra mobs that join the one that got pulled. If someone breaks Mez on the mob not being engaged by the group, it can really hurt the group, or get all of them killed. Especially the Chanter. Mez is a VERY BIG agro and puts the chanter at the top of the hate list.

Maanen did a good job putting out some nice macro's to use.

Hope this helps.

Phixin
Your MT can be the MA, but, normally the puller (when not the MT) is the MA. That way every one targets the same mob. If the puller come running in to camp with 3 mobs on his tail, it is much easier for the MT to /assist puller than to have to try to click thru the inc targets to get the one origionally pulled. On a raid, the MA might be the raid leader also (have seen that once in 12 raids). It is also the MA's responsibility to make sure everyone is taking down the mob's in the order needed.

There are many different ways to use this as well. The MT might have the puller set as the MA, while everyone else in the group assists of the MT.

Also, if you are in a really good exp group, your puller will be leaving when the mob is at 15% hp unless the healer calls med. In that case, assisting off the MT would be everyone except the puller's best option.

Disclaimer: This is my interpretation. It is not a hard and fast rule anywhere.

I too have started to use macros for casting. This is a smart way to play I think. It keeps everyone informed and also keeps me, as a healer, more alert. There are more than just heals that can be good information for the group to know. For instance, (ok, strange situation, but you group with who you can get...) I was grouped in DSP last night and a rogue was our tank (yep, a rogue. The group was me, a wizzy and a rogue). So I created a macro for my stun so that the rogue would know when a potential backstab opportunity was coming up.

I have seen people use /say, and I've seen people use /g. I prefer /g because it shows up in the "group" color as opposed to it being mistaken for some chatty lumpkin standing nearby. Also, I don't want to necessarily spam nearby groups in places where it's crowded. The only drawback is when a higher level toon is helping you, and is not grouped for XPing purposes. We had a higher lvl druid who was pulling for this group, and he dropped several heals on top of mine thinking I wasn't going to get it off in time ("Step off Mr. Drood! Healing is MY territory!"). Anyway, that's the only downside I know about.

Also, the Function keys are wonderful for targeting group members. Get to know them. They are your friend. You don't have to worry about crappy framerates making your cursor slow or targeting people in a crowd...heck, you don't even have to be facing the battle to target and heal your party member. I hear some exclusively play with their back turned to the action!

Anyway, too much information in my opinion is better than too little.
