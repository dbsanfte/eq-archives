chrisemau
The timing of heals is a very important skill to learn. I try to wait untill the last second to heal the MT to avoid being aggroed. The problem does not get better as you go up in levels, in fact, it gets much worse. The higher level MOB's can do some serious damage to even the most buffed Cleric and they mess up your attempts to heal the MT's and if you are not careful, get your self waxxed at the same time.

Here are some tips on your particular situation:

1) Pull one or two of the MOB's off the MT with your root spell. This is most effective in such a large MOB/MT situation. Work with teh canter in the group to solve that problem. CC is their department nut you can help too.

2) Maximize your mana by using the largest available Heal spell and wait untill the last allowable time to cast it without endangering your MT. Sometimes it is hard to judge this and you have to gauge it especially with that level tank.

3) Always announce your heals. Set up a hot button to announce that the heal is incoming and who it is for... that way the supporting crew can expect a MOB or two to go after you and keep an eye out for you in that siuation. It doesnt pay to have your healer get killed, especially if you are in bound far away.

These basic guidlines will help you in all of your groups!!!!!!

Shadowfrost
If you're fighting multiple pulls as a level 10 cleric, expect to do some tanking. I can't see a way around it.

The bad news is that your level 5 root and your level 5 stun are inadequate as crowd control tools. You DO have Divine Aura, but your low channelling skill may well prevent you from casting it while you're being pounded on.

The good news is, it gets easier as you level up and clerics are surprisingly effective crowd controllers in your own right. If you were a level 65 cleric facing a room full of blues, you could pick one of them out and pull him to your tank without any of the rest of them aggroing at all.

At lower levels, your best bet is to get some AC and HP gear and wear it!

You'll find that with experience you should be able to control your own aggro generation fairly easily as you progress up in levels. One thing that I didn't see mentioned is 'prox aggro'. This works seperately from the aggro you produce while casting. But it adds dramatically to the probability you are going to get mobbed. Basically, it depends on your distance from the mob. In lower levels, if you are close by and already have some aggro, you're probably going to get jumped unless the tank has good taunt abilities. Try standing farther away from your heal-target if you are getting swamped frequently by mobs he is actively attacking or should have aggro on. This could also help you get a well-timed root off if you happen to pull one off the tank with heal aggro.

Level aggro definitely comes into play here also so be careful if you are slightly under-level for the zone you are in.

Good luck and always carry a big shield. :)
