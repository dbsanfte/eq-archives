norvik
I am only 30 so my symbol uses bloodstones. I throw my symbol around like a french whore if I got the mana in the party to keep it refreshed. When we get to peridots the cost gets to be higher, I know.

My question is do you guys charge for it? I was in a group with my pally the other day. We were in KC and the warrior asks the cleric for a temp. He had put the other 2 buffs on both the warrior and myself but no symbol on either of us. We all knew he had it because while the group was forming outside he was hawking his wares in OOC across the zone. Anyways, he told the warrior he would temp him if he paid 20plat. Being a cleric of no great level I was a bit taken by that. I would assume he would make that back in the group over the course of time. I look at it as being the clerics job in the party. Buff and Heal are #1. I can understand charging in POK if that is how you want to spend your game time, but not in a group.

What is your take on this?

If I play a melee alt, though, I carry a few stacks of dots on my and offer one each time I ask for a buff.

Doohickey
I never charge for it, but I also never have a problem getting a fair split of the loot. Yes, it means I make less than the others in the end, but I don't care...I always group with Guildies and Alliance types, so I have no problems taking the hit for them.

Of course, I also have no trouble making money. If I was constantly broke, I'd probably ask to get dot costs covered. As it is, these are the people that made sure I had my Epic as quickly as we could get it, and who will cheerfully camp anything I need camped. Even if I wasn't getting any loot, they would get everything I had to offer them. (Any peris that drop do automatically get handed to me, though. I never asked for that, they just do it because the cleric and enchanter should always get the peris)

As it is, I think I have blown through three stacks since Saturday just doing drive by Tempings on the people in OT and M Seru, where I have been leveling my enchantress.

If I somehow found myself in a pick up group I'd be a lot more likey to request getting at least partial coverage if loot was going to suck. Then again, money really isn't an issue for me, so probably not.

If you are a broke cleric, though, I don't see a problem with getting costs covered in pick up groups. (Still not 20pp, though. You are getting XP, you shouldn't be looking to make a profit)

Gymp
I deal with Dot costs differently depending on who I am grouped with:

Guild & Pickup group regulars: Temp if they need it no worries about the dot. Most are usually pretty good about throwing something extra my way to cover the costs but I don't sweat it.

Unknown Pickup groups: I ask for dot donations or an extra vendor item to cover the costs up front. I still buff, whether I get cost/dots. Most folks recognize the extra costs involved and have no problem helping a cleric out.

Buffing: I buff for a minimum donation of 11p or a dot. If I didn't, I might as well stand on a corner in PoK and 11p to everyone that walks by. If folks feel like donating more, great, I appreciate the extra plat.

I disagree with the whole "cost of business" ideology. Why should a cleric be expected to eat 50p right off the bat when they group simply because they have buffs. I could see the point if the buffs only benifited the cleric but everyone gets an edge from the HP/AC buffs.
