Galv
On Saturday, Hawkestone woke the Sleeper on Xev. Needless to say, quite a few people were upset about this, as Xev was one of only a handful of servers (five I believe) to have the Sleeper still... well, sleeping.

Anyway, they claim they wanted to see new content, but after thinking about it for awhile and discussing it with others, I'm throwing a bullshit flag. On Xev, there was an unwritten rule of not killing all the warders--it really is that simple. The most "uber" guilds on our server have respected this unwritten rule for years (regardless if it was to farm ST) yet, some 30 or so people in Hawkestone opt to go against the wishes of the majority?

WTF?

My guild is by no means the most "uber" on Xev. But, we have been progressing very quickly over the past few months. And we figure to be in ST and NToV by the end of the summer. But now, the thought of ST isn't so appealing to me anymore. Yeah, obviously there is some very good gear there for everyone, although some incomprable pieces are now lost, so doing ST for us is anything but a waste... but, it's different now.

I've commented on this board and on other boards that EQ is just a game... as a whole, just pixels and a good time waster. Obviously, emotion is invested here, but in a limited fashion. But, this evening while pondering the actions of Hawkestone, I've come to a realization of how much EQ is a microcosm of real life. And how, no matter how big or small something is, someone out there will flip you the literal or figurative finger. Hawkestone, by there actions in game and on various message boards, illustrated to me just how inconsiderate and selfish people can be. In particular, one member consistently /shrugged at the grievances people brought forth.

I realize now that there were two main goals I had when I first started playing EverQuest. The first was to get my Epic, which I long ago accomplished. And the second, which seemingly was within my grasp, was to take a screenshot of the Sleeper still deeply in his slumber. Hawkestone took this second goal from me and countless others--forever.

EQ might well be a game, but how utterly disappointed I was at bravado and selfishness displayed by this guild on my server. With that said, grats Hawkestone for doing what the server as a whole wished not to be done. Grats for doing what numerous guilds could have done years ahead of you. Grats for taking an EQ experience from me what other guilds so kindly and considerately left for you. So it goes, I guess. People--even when playing "just a game"--still suck.

Apologies to Galv, but I think perhaps some of his answers need further clarification.

1) What is the sleeper exactly?

Kerafym, offspring of Nagafen and Vox in a mating prohibited by the dragons. The most powerful critter anywhere, ever, and quite literally unkillable.

Estimated to have 3.28 million hit points, Kerafym is the only known critter to be able to death touch anything in the whole zone. He does so every ten seconds, far faster than the other death touching critters.

He is unkillable because any force large enough to have a chance at killing him, would also be so large that it would crash the zone concerned.

2) How has this been dealt with on other servers?

He has been awakened by the first or second guild into Sleepers Tomb. Only on Xev did the leading guilds have the courtesy and respect to leave him alive for the people who followed.

There were a dozen or so guilds who progressed through Sleepers without waking Kerafym, and they did so over the course of several years.

I am very proud of the fact that when I did Sleepers Tomb, I was one of those who voted not to waken the Sleeper. Many guilds followed in our wake making the same decision, even after the Sleepers Tomb revamp.

3) Can it be put back to sleep?

A GM or programmer could presumably do it. There is no way within the game to do so, though; the script was intentionally writted to be a one-time-only, unrepeatable, irreversible server event.

4) Has anyone ever tried?

No, see the answer to 3.

5) What is the role of the warders or do they respawn?

The power of the four warders keeps Kerafym asleep. They used to respawn every five days, providing that at least one of them was left alive. When all four were killed at the same time, Kerafym was awoken, and the warders ceased to exist permanently.

Since the zone has been revamped, they will presumably be replaced with the new Ancients. This is actually a benefit to clerics, since the Ancients drop WIS caster loot; in Sleepers Tomb on Xev prior to 8/6/03, there was no WIS caster loot in the zone.

6) Can they help?

No, the warders must be dead for Kerafym to be awake.

7) I've heard that the sleeper eventually starts zone hopping, is this true?

Theoretically, Kerafym was supposed to move from Sleepers' Tomb to Skyshrine where he would kill his rival Lord Yelinak before vanishing. In practise, this did not happen because the script was bugged.

Actually there are two issues here. The first is the waking of the Sleeper, and the second is a patch (quite some time ago) that revamped the Sleepers' Tomb zone.

Before the patch, there were no "Ancients" and no "Ancient Prismatic." As I understand it, waking the Sleeper caused the Warders to stop spawning, to be replaced with... nothing.

Also before the patch, as I can personally confirm, Dire Charm used to work in Sleepers' Tomb, and some of the encounters worked differently. There's nothing like tanking the Master of the Guard with a 30khp Dire Charmed golem to trivialize an encounter.

After the patch, the Ancients came in and the zone became the Sleepers Tomb we have on most servers today.

However, on a few servers (notably Xev) the Sleeper was not awakened before the patch. So we had a sort of hybrid zone... the new-style MoTG and an end to Dire Charmed golems, but with Warders instead of Ancients and Kerafym still slumbering down there at the bottom.
