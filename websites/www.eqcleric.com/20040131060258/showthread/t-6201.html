Well its been a few weeks since I came back to EQ and started my cleric, and I have to say being on the support side of a group has been an eye-opening experience. Not to sound conceded, but when I was playing my warrior or monk, I always had a good tank :)

Since I started my cleric, I have gotten to experience tanks who dont wait for me or a chanter to pacify the room, they just pull all of them. Because thats how they did it in Crushbone and it worked fine.

I have found that in undead style dungeons having two clerics in a group is almost as much fun as having sex. Almost!

I have learned that almost no one gives a second thought to reagent costs for spells like symbol(at my level it isnt a major factor and I am pretty set for plat, but still. My other toons ALWAYS have a stack of dots on them).

I have learned that while I am very pro-twink(to each his own), Twinking really does erode at the basic group skills. Folks ignore the facet of crowd control in the 20's or 30's because folks are so twinked they can handle 2 or 3 mobs at a time.

Hearing a level 24 chanter say "So many mobs came into camp at once I didnt know what to do", is a lot more funny than hearing a level 60 chanter say the same thing. /Rude Ebaying

Lastly, I am learning why FT items cost so damn much. :)

You know, that's always been a peev of mine. My wiz main doesn't go *anywhere* without 2 stacks of dots and a bunch of jaspers (I don't even walk around without Steelskin or Manaskin up). Anytime I get into a group, as soon as the pre-fight buffs are done, (with no symbols cast for the tanks), I go to our cleric and just hand him/her enough dots to cover 2 Symbols for each of the melees. I usually get an immediate /tell saying "wow. thanks. What are those for?" /rollingeyes

"For Symbols for the tanks, you knuckleneck! When we're done here, I'm gonna be worried about my xp bar, not my bank account!"

Constantly seeing both sides of the argument, I'm not sure which pisses me off more...tanks that are inconsiderate enough to expect clerics to keep them buffed with reagent-expensive spells, or clerics who won't cast them, because they're too cheap to do it without payment. When I'm the doc, Symbols get cast, without question...with or without payment. Think about it...if you take the attitude that you won't cast Symbols because they're too expensive, isn't that kind of hypocritical, since you're saying the tanks are too cheap to pay for them? The Symbols on the tanks are for the group's benefit, not just the tank's. Cast the spell...reagents be damned.

On the other hand, when I'm playing a non-cleric, I also try to remind everybody else in the group before we disband that those spells cost in-game money, and anybody who can afford it should kick a little extra into the cleric's kitty, to reimburse him/her. Especially if the cleric gets left out (again) of the majority of the looting while he/she was medding furiously between heals.
