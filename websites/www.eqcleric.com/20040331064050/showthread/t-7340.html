stratys
whether or not you're being lazy depends on your motivation for pointing it out to him I suppose... but the fact remains that you are correct that his lack of aggro management skills can only lead to trouble when he moves into situations that are more challenging.

Learning where that edge of the aggro envelope is and riding it without going over it is a major part of the job of any DPS class; how much respect would you have for a wizard who chain cast nukes from the start of every fight and ganked aggro constantly?

Any DPS class has some control over their aggro: choosing when to attack, when to stop attacking, fighting from max melee distance, switching to slower weapons and/or ones without procs, etc. Monks have a few nice tools in addition to that, notably feign death, to help them ditch aggro at will.

If the monk doesn't want to FD or stop attacking for a sec to lose aggro because he wants to maximize the kill rate, he needs to learn the real effects of what he is doing. Is he attacking from the front of the mob? if so, then he's needlessly being parried and riposted etc and losing damage that way. If not, then every time he ganks aggro he disrupts the damage output for himself and the rest of the melees as the mob spins around and faces those who need to be behind him. If they adjust to compensate, then they are further disrupted when the MT regains aggro and spins the mob back around. Pets especially will be affected by facing the front of the mob and not being able to adjust. Needless to say, this annoyance turns deadly when a mob that enrages is flipped around at the wrong moment.

Any braindead moron can buy up the best gear around, hit autoattack and spam the special attack keys; showing me your uber DPS really doesn't impress me that much. Showing me how well you can ride the line of maximizing damage without ganking aggro and I'll perk up and take notice.

Shadowfrost
My monk alt is 63 with 52aa.

There is no excuse for a monk over level 17 to take aggro unnecessarily. None whatsoever.

Monks can FD, stand back up again, and start attacking immediately. FD works like evade: It lowers aggro even if the monk stands up and attacks again at once.

However, it IS efficient for the monk to use Mend every time it repops; it's a mana-free heal. Every six minutes the monk can regain 25% (or sometimes more, with AA) of his or her hp at the press of a button. So many clerics make it a rule to let the monk Mend rather than heal.

On the other hand, this particular situation is more complex. There's no taunt tank in the group - so the monk MIGHT take it that it's his job to be higher on the aggro list than the cleric, in case the berserker dies. In that case, the monk might well choose not to lower aggro through the use of FD, for good reasons.

It's a thing to sit down and discuss, in my view.
