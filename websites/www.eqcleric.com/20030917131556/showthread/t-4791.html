Well, actually the truth is that the druid community as a whole is doing no such thing.

It's important to distinguish between most druids - the perfectly ordinary druids who you you meet in-game - and the rabid psychotic cleric-hating wierdoes who inhabit one particular website. It's important to appreciate that that particular website does not speak for all druids, and in fact it does not even speak for most of them.

It speaks for an established group of a couple of dozen very old-school druids, plus a floating pool of their newer hangers-on and cronies, who would much rather write a fifteen-page dissertation on `reasons why I can't get a group` than actually log in and make friends with people. Most of these druids actively enjoy playing a game I might call `Everquest politics` and most of their posts are written with rhetoric in mind.

It's very easy, on glancing over that website, to believe that `The Druid Community as a Whole` believes that it is seriously broken, totally underpowered, and incapable of getting a group anywhere, ever, under any circumstances, and the only things that will `rescue` this doomed class are if abilities which properly attach to other classes are handed to them, immediately, in return for no effort whatsoever and with no compensatory downgrading of their other abilities.

In fact, Sony representatives have been fooled into believing that the website in question represents the druid community and speaks with a single voice before now.

Make no mistake:- That side is dominated by individuals who spend more time playing EQ politics than they do playing EQ, and they control what is said there very carefully in order to ensure that dissenting voices are squelched as fast as possible in order not to `derail` their demand threads. They do not welcome dissenting voices, and if you aren't prepared to toe the party line you're best advised not to post there.

Don't blame your druid friends in-game for that website, they aren't responsible for it!
