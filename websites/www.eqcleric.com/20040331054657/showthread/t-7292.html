Sydian
Hit 65 first. This will up your mana pool by a fair amount alone. For cash, I don't recommend LDoN any more since that was nerfed. If you need money get a friend (tank or pet class most likely) to help you farm rare drops in zones like CT, Droga, Veksar, Sebilis. Or find something to solo but this is usually a lot more boring IMO.

SCM3 should be your first goal after getting Baron, since it works on CH unlike MP IV.

EE IV is nice but with the price of Ceramic Shields, I'd just save the pp to get the 8th shawl (will run you 10-20k, depends how much you farm the components vs powerskilling). 8th shawl not only is 20% ext buffs as opposed to 15%, it has FT3 which is huge also.

SCR3/Mastery are nice, I'd get that after SCM3 and AA's like Divine Arb. The biggest use is to extend Supernal Elixir by 50%, making it a 3k heal. Good for Virtue, Yaulp, Spell Haste, Self Armor as well.

Single V on 6 people will take 6k mana, so there's not a lot you can do about that. If you come along a group where every single person needs it, they should expect to wait after you buff (you're not 65 yet, after all). HoV helps a ton as it is 2.5k mana, less than half the cost on both mana and dots. I'd just spell haste pure casters and main slower if it's expensive on mana. If other classes request it, grant it...otherwise they probably don't need it or care.

I don't have MP IV yet, but I imagine it only helps more. Ornate boots only drop in Tower of Sol Ro as far as I know, so they're difficult to hunt for and expensive as a result. With a decent FT count and SCM3 things are pretty easy though.

My advice is try to get to 4k mana by level 65, even if you can't try to get close. My gear was about the same as yours when I hit 65, ~1800 HP and ~3500 mana (which is low, but not too too bad for a new 65). I always fought with KEI at these levels just so it wouldn't take too long recovering. I do even now just so I can nuke and add dps to the group (less boring to me). Do LDoN to get up your FT with augs and try to camp 2 bracers at BoT Tables.

Ayreon
Ah, the everburning question.

I think it boils down to a few things.. besides what your focus is (getting to 65 or learning to use some neat new toys). First, it's where you primarily play, or play to, what type of groups you end up playing in (pickups, friends, guild) and buffs you end up having.

Yeah, I know that sounds stupid, but let me explain.

If you are an adventure nut, or plan to spend a lot of time in BoT, go for levels + gear. They both get you more mana, which is a must. No BoT group wants a cleric with 5 CH's worth of mana before he's OOM. For adventures, the time limit (well, they are pretty easy now) kinda forces you to be buffed with mana regen, some FT and a good-sized mana pool. If you spend a lot of time in other places, such as tier 1 planes, Luclin, etc.. then none of this is such a big deal and you can spend some time on AA. Especially if you are sub 60 and spend time in Droga or Veksar (55ish), the AA xp is really nice. At 65 AA goes slow in a lot of zones, the best probably being BoT or an all-65 adventure (this is provided you have no PoP flagging done).

I mention groups, because playing with friends all the time is a totally different ball game than playing with strangers. There are less expectations, they don't generally mind downtime here and there, and you can "afford" to work on AA if you prefer. I wouldn't go nuts on AA though, maybe get your 6 general and 12 archetype, then hit the road to 65 though. There's a few nice goodies to get if you have trouble getting gear (SCM helps, MC1 or 2, Celestial Regen, etc..) all those really help regardless of your mana pool/FT/gear. I think I finished off SCM3 before i hit 65 because to me, it felt like a more logical choice.

Raiding clerics : 65 > AA, there's no real dispute about that. A few early AA can't hurt, but mana pool, plus spells and level really help.

I mentioned buffs for a couple of reasons. If you can always count on having KEI/VoQ (I do, I play 99% with my wife who is a 65 chanter), and AoZ or you play with a beasty/bard a lot, then mana regen/pool isn't as big of a deal. You can get away with having a little less mana since you're getting it back a little faster.

This is just from personal experience.. I found that i was very worried about being in BoT and my healing powers, especially when pulling kriegs and the mini's before 64ish. You're also more likely to get into a group if you advertise "65 Cleric" ;)

BTW, that set of cultural armor is great for the cost. I still have a few pieces.. The AC is really nice, especially sub 60. You can crack 1100 easily by 65 with just a full set alone and normal accessories.

Good luck..
