Bonra
I am an AMD user, so here is my skinny on the two.

Performance wise they essentially run neck and neck, Intel often being able to do large number crunching better and AMD being a little better at crunching graphics.

The key to stabilty and performance actually lies in the chipset, i.e. Intel, VIA, nforce, etc. VIA makes a lot of AMD chipsets and are the least expensive but are proned to problems, Nvidia's nforce2 is much more stable and provides better performce. I'm not as well versed on Intel chipsets unfortunatly.

AMD Pro: Price. For equal performance an AMD cpu is far cheaper than Intel. Both companies have their economy chips too. The Duron by AMD is still stable just lacks the built in memory cash. The Celeron hasn't always performed well from my experience in selling computers. (as far as Parklyn's experience I have an extremely hard time believing this. Overclocking any CPU drastically hurts it's stability espcially at that rate and I know it's not technically possible to have a Windows 98 system stay up for days on end due to memory leaks, running no programs on one and just leaving it up and running for a week and you will Have to reboot to free up system resources)

AMD Cons: They run hot. All this really means is you need more than just a CPU fan on it, basically just a vase fan preferably with something to direct airflow away form the CPU.

You can disagree with me if you like but you'll be wrong ;)
