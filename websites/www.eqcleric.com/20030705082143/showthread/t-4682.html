Merrybrook
Hello! I was wondering if I might get some advice from you all... First let me set up the scenario...

Last night I was invited to join a group to go to Tower of Frozen Shadow. I found out later that the group was comprised of RL family members that usually group together. As you can imagine they are a tight group that has tons of experience together.

The group was comprised of 2 Druids, 2 Warriors, a Necromancer, and myself (the Cleric).

I could tell that the group was fairly used to having their Druids as their healers since they would rapidly start firing off heals every time the tanks would reach ~75% hps. I stated that it was ok to let the tanks drop to ~60%hps since I could sup heal them and it would get them back into the 90% range. Despite this, and me calling my heals, there was a lot of cross healing going on. I only got to use Cheal once, and that was because a tank got lost and I was able to heal her though a wall. (It was lucky here that the Druid was able to track the tank to a point where she was in range and got off a Gheal that gave me enough time to get my Cheal off, that was awesome!).

There were two deaths... One tank was pulling and on the way back got killed, I ran off to heal her, but by the time she was in range she was a corpse already. The other was the Necro, which had gained aggro from 2 Wizard mobs (a shadow) and was burned down before any of us could get a heal off.

Now for my questions (sorry this has taken so long)...

1) Have any of you been in a situation like that, where your healing powers were underestimated by those used to grouping with Druids/Shaman? How did you deal with it?

2) I know neither of the deaths were my fault, but I still felt quite bad. All-in-all I've had a pretty good run with Merry, I can count the number of people that have died in a group with me on one hand. Not to say none of those were my fault, I have made mistakes just like everyone else... But I still felt really bad for those deaths... How do you deal with feeling bad for those who die in your care?

I'm not complaining about the group, I just want to make myself a better Cleric, and I want to be able to learn as much as I can from the experience last night. :)

The trip to TOFS was VERY fun, the group was a great bunch of people that were very funny. TOFS is also one of my favorite dungeons in EQ, so it was fun to be able to go back. :) One of the tanks and the Necro got the roll on that dagger that summons bandages, so they were very happy.

I sent these questions to my guild's main Cleric, and he replied with some great advice. I wanted to open it up to you all to see if you had any words of wisdom to offer. :)

Thanks!

-M

what i would of done in your situation, would be to ask that they make a key anouncing their heals. if they continued to heal over me, i would of just ignored it.

i don't think they were underestimating you, i think maybe they were just used to playing without a cleric. so were not used to the power of complete heal.

deaths will happen. i still take them personal. but that is what rez is for ;-). don't let them get to you too much!!!

Zandilar
Heya,

It all comes down to what sort of level you're at, and what kind of pulling your doing. :) (and what buffs you have, KEI anyone? /smilewink)

As a cleric, your mana is best used to heal. Your nukes are pathetic when compared to a druid of the same level. By the same token, a druid's heals suck when compared to a cleric of the same level.

So... I would have explained, polietly, to the druids that I have enough mana to keep both tanks healed, especially if one is assisting the other and not taunting. (Aggro control is very important at the high end game, and it's best to start learning early.) If the secondary tank had to offtank, you could have asked the druids to spot heal, but honestly their mana is best used at what they do best... dotting and nuking (and strangely enough, many druids resent filling the healer role because their nukes are pretty darned good). In some ways, your job is easier if the druids are nuking, because that means the mobs are dead sooner and in the long run do less damage to the tanks. If you did get low on mana, then the druids could step in and help with the heals... In a single group one cleric is more than enough to keep the main meleers healed.

Hope that helps. :)
