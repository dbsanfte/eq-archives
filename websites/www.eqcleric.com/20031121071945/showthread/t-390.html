The spells I have lined up are:
Cheal
Remedy
Celestial

Remedy - the puller has taken a beating and all other heals are out of range and 'OMG heal him NOW' heal. Also known as the 'WTF why doesn't the MA's cleric cast CH's on time - bah I'll slip in a remedy to keep the guy alive whilst that lazy **** cleric thinks about doing his job' heal.

Amazingly fast cast time and range - frankly this has saved quite a few lives in my groups and raids I've been to already and if you haven't loaded this one then you should. You won't regret it.

The quicker cast time and longer range more than make up for the lesser number of HP healed compared to Superior Healing. I use Celestial as a general purpose heal.

I never use group heal unless we've all just zoned, are all very low on health and we're in a relatively safe area.

In a live group situation I tend to fill up slots with 2 or 3 stuns and would rather be able to chain stun a mob and prevent damage, than have a slot taken up by a heal that I might use rarely.

I am currently level 56. I didn't start really hunting in the planes until about 54 or 55. Until then, I really didn't see much use of Remedy except the occasional quick heal on the enchanter at raids.

However, all that has changed. PoP mobs are so unpredictable. They hit random amounts and quick. My spell line always is:

Complete Heal
Divine Light
Remedy
Celestial Heal

I sometimes also mem the group heal for encounters where a DS is involved ( HoT ) or it just looks like everyone is getting agro.

Divine light is a GREAT upgrade from Superior Heal. After getting this spell, I moved Sheal to the back of the book.

Remedy has saved my tanks, enchanters, myself NUMEROUS times in PoP. Very quick heal and with Blessing of Faith I can repeatedly cast it on tank until I've stabilized his health enough to Cheal.

If you're comfortable with your healing style and you keep everyone alive, use what ya want :)
