Study: Games Market $86B by 2006
05-31-2001 | poster: Sherlock | All Sections
According to a new study from London's Informa Media Group, the size of the games market could jump 71% over the next five years, making it an $86 billion industry by 2006. This massive jump in numbers is mostly due to the next wave of new console systems—the Microsoft Xbox, the Nintendo GameCube and the Sony PlayStation 2—as well as games spreading to set-top boxes and next-generation mobile phones.

"The big difference over the next few years will be a move away from the image of gamers as 'nerds,'" says Media report co-author Adam Thomas, "Games will move very much into the mainstream - something that will see 'ordinary' people playing games much more, using their mobile phones and TV sets."

Circuit City to Gamers "ID PLEASE"
05-25-2001 | poster: Sherlock | All Sections
Circuit City Stores Inc. this week joined a growing list of companies and businesses that have instituted the policy of stopping selling mature-rated videogames to customers under age 17. This new policy was confirmed Wednesday by Circuit City spokesman Jim Babb, and will be in effect soon.

"We just felt that this was the right thing to do," said Babb. "We still believe that parents are absolutely the primary authority on what their children should see or play. This policy is a reinforcement of that philosophy."

Starting sometime next month, workers at Circuit City's 627 stores will be told to ask for identification from younger people who attempt to buy M-rated video games without a parent along.

E3 - All Gone: For Now
05-22-2001 | poster: Sherlock | All Sections
The seventh annual Electronic Entertainment Expo concluded at the Los Angeles Convention Center on Saturday, having drawn more than 62,000 interactive entertainment professionals from more than 70 countries.

More than 400 exhibitors showcased thousands of computer games, videogames and next-generation technologies, including more than 750 products shown for the first time ever. Approximately 80 percent of the products displayed at the show are anticipated to hit retail stores by the coming holiday season.

Douglas Lowenstein, Interactive Digital Software Association (IDSA) president, said, "2001 is an extremely exciting year for the industry, with the introduction of new hardware and the debut of stunning new software. E3Expo is the launch pad for games that will shape the future of entertainment."

The 2002 show will also be held at the Los Angeles Convention Center, on May 23–25, 2002.

Video Games More Popular Than Politicians
05-21-2001 | poster: Sherlock | All Sections
A recent survey conducted by electrical retailer Dixons shows that Britain's first-time voters know more about computer game characters such as Lara Croft and Sonic the Hedgehog than they do about politicians. The data revealed that the 1,000 18-22 year-olds surveyed were able to name at least eight computer game characters but only four members of the Cabinet, and two members of the Shadow Cabinet. In perhaps a twist of irony, Dixons was unable to say have many computer game characters British senior politicians were able to name.
