Study ShowsGaming Industry Good for the Economy
05-02-2001 | poster: Sherlock | All Sections
According to a recent study, the computer and videogame industry has led to a better economy. As part of a report titled “Economic Impacts of the Demand for Computer and Video Games,” it was shown that the industry created 220,000 jobs and close to nine billion dollars in wages. In addition, the study found that the sales of games led to another $10.5 billion in economic activity.

"This new study clearly dramatizes the growing importance of the video game industry in America's high-tech sector," stated Doug Lowenstein, president of the Interactive Digital Software Association (IDSA)."With the introduction of new advanced game machines, surging demand for video game software, and an expanding base of dedicated and casual game players, it's clear that the industry will be a major force in the American economy for years to come."

3Q Offers “Clone” Sharing Service
04-18-2001 | poster: Sherlock | All Sections
3Q Inc. , the company that scans in a user’s face and turns them into 3-D models that can be used in various computer games, has announced the launch of its Web-based sharing site called the Trading Post. The site enables users who’ve already been “cloned” to store and swap their images with others over the Internet.
The company currently offers its scanning booth at three retail locations—Plano (Dallas), Texas; Tuwila (Seattle), Washington; and San Jose, Calilfornia. The user enters the booth, much like the photo booths at arcades and amusement parks, which scans their face. Software then takes the data and converts it into a detailed 3-D model, as well as inserting it into Half-Life, Quake and Unreal skins for use in those games, all of which is given to the consumer on a CD at a price of about $15. 3Q expects to expand the list of games in the future to include The Sims and others.

Indrema Dream Dies

Indrema president John Gildred, has told Video Business magazine that the Linux-based Indrema L600 console is no more. The system, powered by a 600 MHz CPU and GeForce graphics had tried to lure developers with the promise of open source.
