Many players choose their character based up the skill sets available to that race and class. Everyone has their own unique skills in which they excel making them an asset to the players around them. Some skills are activated automatically, while others are only useful if the player trains in them first. You can train a skill by finding your guildmaster and putting skill points into the desired catagory. Once you have learned the basics of a skill, your accuracy will increase the more attempt to use it. Practice makes perfect!

Abjuration - One of the several skills related to the art of magic, abjuration deals with defensive magic, for example a spell that enables the character to take less damage from extreme cold.

Alcohol Tolerance - The amount of alcohol one can consume with out felling the distorting, disorienting effects. With a high alchohol tolerance, drinking a lot will cause intelligence to be exchanged for strength and stamina, which is helpful for tanks.

Alteration - Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, alteration deals with changing things, whether it be illusion or an actual physical change. An example would be a spell that teleports something, or that enables a character to move faster, or forces him or her to move more slowly.

Apply Poison - A skill specific to the Rogue. If successful, the targeted weapon will have a chance to poison an enemy.

Archery - In addition to the skills dealing with melee combat, range weapons are also prevalent in the world. The skill of archery deals with a characters efficacy in using bows.

Backstabbing - The Rogue has in his repertoire many unique skills, including the ability to strike at his opponent from behind, often dealing a great deal of damage in one blow. However a Rogue can only backstab when using a piercing weapon in their primary hand and positioned directly behind their target.

Bash - Certain races and classes have the ability to knock an opponent down, thereby stunning them, inflicting a small amount of damage, and also interrupting the process of spellcasting. A shield must be equiped in order to use this skill.

Beg - Lets you beg for money or items from NPCs, such as merchants and guards. However each NPC has a different tolorance for beggars and will attack you when they become agitated.

Bind Wound - Lets you use bandages to heal yourself and others with less than 50% health points. Some classes have the ability to heal themselves to 75% hp once the skill ability is over 200.

Block - Negating an attack by deflecting the blow with one's body is a skill unique to the Monk, and blocking is one of several skills available to that class.

Brass Instruments - Certain Bard songs require specific instruments, and the better the minstrel is at playing Brass Instruments, the more effective he or she will be at playing those songs.

Channeling - One of the several skills related to the art of magic, channeling allows the caster to decrease the amount of time necessary to cast a spell, regardless of its nature.

Common Tongue - Ability to speak and understand the Common Tongue.

Conjuration - Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, conjuration brings something from elsewhere into the world, whether they be elemental creatures or powerful clouds of energy.

Dark Speech - Ability to speak and understand the special dark tongue of the Dark Elf, Troll, and Ogre kind.

Defense - The general ability to save one's own skin in melee combat.

Disarm - This skill allows you to dislodge a wielded weapon from your opponents hands, knocking it to into your possesion, and forcing him to fight hand-to-hand.

Disarm Traps - Allows a Rogue to make the rest of his party very happy by disarming potentially life-threatening traps on objects or in passageways.

Divination - Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, spells of divination allow the caster to both detect things previously concealed, as well as to conceal things previously visible.

Dodge - One of the better ways to negate damage is to completely avoid the blow itself, and the skill of dodging allows the combatant to do just that.

Double Attack - Allows the user a chance to double their weapon's attack speed, thereby attacking twice in the time that it would have taken them to attack once.

Dragon Punch - Special Monk attack cuasing more damage than a typical punch.

Dual Wield - Certain classes will be able to wield a weapon effectively in both hands, thereby increasing the number of attacks and thus the amount of damage dealt in a given amount of time.

Eagle Strike - Special Monk attack.

Elder Elvish - Ability to speak and understand Elder Elvish.

Evocation - Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, evocation deals with things that go boom. Fireballs, force spells, etc. all fall under the auspices of this offensive skill.

Feign Death - Allows the user a chance to convince NPCs that they are dead and to attack someone else. This is a Monk only skill but also a spell used by Necromancers and Shadow Knights.

Fishing - As your fishing skill increases, you have the ability to more often catch fish or other treasures from the water. You will need a fishing pole and bait.

Flying Kick - Special Monk attack cuasing more damage than a typical kick.

Foraging - Rangers, Druid, and Woodelves have the ability to forage food and water from local surroundings. Usually providing enough food to put off the next trip into town for supplies.

Hand-to-Hand - Although this skill can be mastered by only the Monks, most every other class can at least throw the occasional punch. Besides, one never knows when one might be relieved of one's weapon, both during combat and at other times.

Hide - Lets you hide to avoid being attacked or seen. You cannot move while hidden, or hide while moving.

Instill Doubt - Yet another skill unique to the Monk and Rogue classes, instill doubt allows a character to fear a target and make it walk away.

Kick - Being able to dish out a bit more damage during combat is attractive to many of the warrior classes in the game, and kicking one's opponent in unpleasant places in between sword swings does just that.

Meditate - Another of the several skills related to the art of magic, meditation allows the caster to memorize spells and regain mana more quickly.

Mend Wounds - A Monk ability to heal one's self. If not performed properly this skill can actually cause damage to the target.

Offense - The overall ability to inflict damage during combat.

Old Erudian - Ability to speak and understand Old Erudian.

One-Hand Blunt - Ability to use a blunt object that only requires one hand to wield. The better one is at these skills, the more effective they are with that type of weapon.

One-Hand Slashing - Ability to hit others with one-handed blades, such as small to medium swords and axes. The better one is at these skills, the more effective they are with that type of weapon.

Parry - Ability to deflect blows and avoid taking damage with one's weapon.

Percussion Instruments - Certain Bard songs require certain instruments, and the better the minstrel is at playing Purcussion Instruments, the more effective he or she will be at playing those songs.

Pick Lock - Oftentimes finding that precise key is just too time consuming or expensive, and the lock picking abilities of the Rogue class come in very handy.

Pick Pocket - Another art known only to the more shady of individuals, this Rogue-specific skill allows them to pilfer items and money from both players and NPCs. Their intended victim, however, will at times detect this activity, and will rarely appreciate the attempt.

Piercing - Ability to use piercing weapons, such as spears and daggers. The better one is at these skills, the more effective they are with that type of weapon. A favorite skill of rogues.

Riposte - Special attack where you deflect your opponents attack and inflict that amount of damage onto them.

Round Kick - Special Monk attack cuasing more damage than a typical kick.

Safe Fall - The ability for Rogues and Monks to fall great distances, yet still land on their feet, incurring minimal if any damage.

Sense Heading - Although compasses are known to exist, they are also typically rare and expensive. Certain races and classes, however, have the innate ability to determine their heading and direction.

Sense Traps - An ability of the Rogue to detect traps on objects or in passageways.

Singing - Bards (one can at least hope) have great singing voices. And the better they are at this art, the more effective their songs, which are themselves often magical in nature.

Slam - An ability peculiar to the the larger races (no matter the class) resulting in an extra melee attack during combat.

Sneak - Allows you to walk slowly and quietly so that enemies don't detect you. Sneak is often more effective then an invisibility spell but there are mystical creature that will see through this ability.

Specialization - When a magic user attains a high enough level this skill becomes available. It allows the user to "specialize" in the spellcasting skill of his or her choice. The added talent in the selected area allows the magic user to cast faster and with less mana usage.

Stringed Instruments - Certain Bard songs require certain instruments, and the better the minstrel is at playing Stringed Instruments, the more effective he or she will be at playing those songs.

Swimming - Many areas in the world of Norrath are covered by water, and one's ability to maneuver in that environment is directly related to one's skill level in swimming.

Taunt - Allows the user a chance to enrage the target to such a degree that it considers the user enemy number one and will try to kill them before killing everyone else. This is a good way to save a comrade's life when he is down to a sliver of health.

Tiger Claw - Special Monk attack.

Thieves Cant - Secret thieves' language performed by the use of body language. Also called "the bird".

Throwing - In addition to the skills dealing with melee combat, range weapons are also prevalent in the world. The skill of throwing deals with a characters efficacy in using throwing knives, daggers, boulders, and other similar devices.

Track - A great skill that lets you detect creatures around you is Tracking. It will point you in the right direction so you can locate mosters, merchants, or players within the same zone.

Two-Handed Blunt - Ability to use two-handed blunt weapons, such as Warhammers and Great Staves. The better one is at these skills, the more effective they are with that type of weapon.

Two-Handed Slashing - Ability to use two-handed slashing weapons, including the larger swords and axes. The better one is at these skills, the more effective they are with that type of weapon.

Wind Instruments - Certain Bard songs require certain instruments, and the better the minstrel is at playing Wind Instruments, the more effective he or she will be at playing those songs.
