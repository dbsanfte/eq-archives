December 30, 1999 - Veny

    This was sent to me from Bumguren today, and posted on the web, we are not sure if it is fact or fiction but its better that we all know about it in advance.... This is the original post by a player in Fennin Ro:

    Thought you might want to know this. A new dupe bug has been discovered in EQ and it's causing people to lose their accounts. It almost happened to me on Fennin Ro.
    Here's what I understand about it. Some hacks are looking for high level characters. When they see a character they want to try to dupe, they watch the character on the /who list. Apparently when you zone, you're character is temporarily unavailable to the name server. They quickly create a newbie character with the same name. When you log back on or zone back on, there are now two of you. You may not notice this, though. Now when you log off or zone, the other person with the dupe name is able to some how log on with that character name and they get the high level account instead. They then proceed to strip the account. Apparently if the legit person logs back on afterward, they get the low level character instead and have now lost all their stuff in their other account.
    I don't know all the specifics, and even if I did, I wouldn't post them, hehe. Fortunately, I avoided having my account duped although there were two of me on at the same time. I personally witnessed this happen when my friend told me to do a /who on Supa (another high level character on Fennin Ro) and sure enough there were two of them. One of them disappeared pretty quickly.
    The GMs are aware this is happening and are trying to respond to petitions very quickly when it happens. A GM told me to log out and then he deleted the duplicate name. I then had to change my password for extra security.
    Apparently one way to hinder these dupers is to play /anon so that it is harder for them to tell what level you are. It isn't worth it to them to dupe lower level characters. Of course, it doesn't help if they already know who some of the higher levels are.
    So, until this bug is fixed, I would advise playing in /anon or /roleplay.
     Please everyone be aware of this, and hopefully the bug will be fixed soon. See you in Norrath!
