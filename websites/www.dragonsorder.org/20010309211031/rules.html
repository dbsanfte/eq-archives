The Dragon's Order is a Xegony EverQuest guild of people bound together by a common goal. A noble guild, each person has been chosen to be in the guild due to having the mark of the Dragon. Each person of that has this mark exudes excellent character, and wishes to strive forth, united, in hopes of gaining knowledge of forgotten arts, power, long life, wealth, and wisdom. All quality's of the Dragon. Our guild was based on the idea that our fates are intertwined, no matter which race and class, and together we can break any chains holding us back.


  • Above all else have fun! This guild is based on friendship and having fun; never forget this rule!

  • Members will not cause other players deaths on purpose. The safety and sanctity of the Dragon's Order must come first in all actions.

  • Harassment of any sort towards the citizens of Norrath by any member will not be tolerated. *This does not include self-defense or justifiable retribution.

  • No member is permitted to beg, steal, or cheat from other members of The Order. Persistent begging will be treated as a violation of the charter.

  • Be good to your fellow members. In time the wheel will rotate and they will do well for you. This does not include any obligation to respond to begging.

  • Always remember: use the gifts of Norrath for the betterment of our guild. Any items that might be useful to the guild should be auctioned or offered for trade in guildsay before selling to a merchant or stranger.

  • Members will adhere to guild loot rules, and will defer to the Council's decisions when there is a loot controversy.

  • Members exhibiting repeated violations of the above guidelines, or who's behavior elicits repeated complaints against them will be considered for removal from the guild.

  • The Dragon Council will have final say in all decision-making matters within the guild. However, the Council will NOT make any decision without giving serious consideration to the guild charter, the guild Feedback message board, and consultation with guild members.


One offense results in immediate /guildremove

  • Dragons do NOT publicly insult, berate, or criticize the Order or its members (i.e. via /guild, /say, /ooc, /shout, or /emote). Other channels are available for complete freedom of expression (i.e., /tell, /gsay, and message boards), but you can't publicly disparage the Dragon's Order.

  • Dragons do NOT cheat or steal from other members of the Order, or anyone else for that matter. This includes ninja looting.

  • Dragons do NOT let themselves get bored while playing EQ. Extended play-time without ample entertainment value will not be tolerated. =)


    It must be understood that failure is certain if one attempts to please all of the guild members all of the time. When considering loot controversies, understand that you may well feel cheated in some way at some time, but that does not necessarily mean that the Order's policies or its members are corrupt in some way. There simply cannot be a pleasing solution to every problem every time. Conversely, by everyone adhering to the same rules, DO can establish fair and satisfying precedents for sharing loot which will result in a very gratifying gaming experience.

  • Lottery by /random will be the default means of deciding who acquires an item. However, this can be overridden by the group members there, if deemed necessary.

  • Loot lotteries will be need-based. If the item required is actually needed (i.e., will be equipped immediately and for the foreseeable future) by members actively involved in the hunt and killing of the npc, then they shall be the only ones in the lotto. If only one such player is in the group(s), they receive the item by default.

  • If an item obtained is not immediately needed by a member, it will be lotto'ed between all members actively involved in the hunt and killing of the npc.

  • In grouping with non-members (of Dragon's Order), we include non-members in lotto's as equals. However, if the group is going to loot according to D.O. guidelines, the non-members should be clearly informed of the procedure ahead of time.

* Items in the Planes: Loot lotteries will follow the same pattern as the above rules with this exception: Lotteries for class-specific items will be /randomed within that class. At the outset of a planes raid, someone from each class will be appointed to be class looter and will lotto these items within that class.

* Looting For Twink Items: If, following the above parameters, an item cannot be assigned to a particular member of the group(s), the item will be /randomed to all members actively involved in the hunt and killing of the npc. Weather one chooses to use the item for a twink, give it away, or sell it is irrelevant.

* It's Just Loot: Members will regularly do reality-checks for themselves. We will strive to remember that items don't really exist in the concrete sense of the word. They are just pixelated images.
