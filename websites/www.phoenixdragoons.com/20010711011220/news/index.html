2000-May-25
Driven by the codes of strength, virtue, and honor, our union with Clan Jax, Sovereign Guard and Sacred Havengoes goes by the name of TRINITY.

  • At long last a triumphant victory can be celebrated! What began as a dream in the Hall of Phoenix ages ago, now has finally developed into reality. United through virtue proven on the field of battle, and by honorable deeds across the land, four powerful nations have joined in an effort not yet witnessed on the world of Norrath. Not by a mere merging, but rather a more powerful bond that far surpasses any of the unified might have yet seen, this band, this alliance, this brotherhood of men, has proven that destiny is unstoppable. We welcome the members of Clan Jax, Sovereign Guard and Sacred Haven into this union. Driven by the codes of strength, virtue, and honor, our union goes by the name of TRINITY.
  • The first raid conducted under the Trinity banner was a raid on the City of Mist. Using the talents of all the members, the dark reavers fell easily before the advance. Deep within the confines of the evil temple and fortress, our forces were forced to withdraw as the alarm sounded. The defenders were able to split our forces, leaving a very brave few to withhold the onslaught allowing the rest of the members to hastily gate to safety. With this raid under our belt, much was learned. Let it be known that very few mortals have seen the depths of the citadel in the City of Mist... and we shall return.
  • Also Kudos go out to some of the members for very valorous deeds off the face of Norrath. Sir Omarin Stoneforge and Sir Sabatini Scratch have been diligently creating webspace and design for not only our second messageboard, but also the Trinity Alliance homepage.
  • Exploration of the new continent has revealed many new weapons, items and treasure. Commendations to all our porters who have made travel to and from the new continent much easier.

2000-Feb-14
Dragoons Messageboard v2.0 is up! A no frames version is also available.

  • I just posted v2.0 of our Messageboard.

    The major improvements include:

    • the ability to delete messages you wrote (if you set a password when you wrote the message)
    • links to websites and email addresses will be automatically made clickable when you post the message (example: if you enter 'siuan@phoenixdragoons.com made http://www.phoenixdragoons.com' the email address and website will become clickable when you post it)
    • the ability to quote the original message when posting a reply
    • a left message index frame (for those who prefer, a no frames version is available, too)
    • the ability to retain the exact formatting of your messages (like when you write poetry with many spaces)
    • displayed at the bottom of your new message: Optional Link URL and Title, Optional Image URL, Password (used if you wish to delete the message later)
    • a counter that shows how many times each message was read

    Others points of note:

    • Settings lets you choose to display only recent messages (in the past 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 999 months, etc.)
    • Settings also lets you view messages in a different order or style. There are 13 styles to choose from!
    • Search lets you find messages that have certain keywords or messages in a certain date range, or messages posted by a particular individual.
    • The previous message is now quoted by lines beginning with '>'
    • Single line breaks will no longer be merged into one line (before, you had to double-space to make a new paragraph, now you only need to single space). Still, double-spacing is preferred: easier to read.
    • After writing your message, check the "If you want your formatting (spaces, etc.) retained, check this box:" box to retain your exact spacing

    What doesn't work:

    • You can't edit your messages after they have been posted
    • There is a checkbox to email you when a reply is posted to your message, but this feature doesn't work yet (I don't know how to call the Email server under Windows NT -- can anyone help?)

    Unfortunately, work is still keeping me extraordinarily busy so you won't see Siuan in Everquest just yet (in fact, this messageboard was updated and used in another contract job -- that's why v2.0 exists!)

    siuan@phoenixdragoons.com

2000-Jan-02
Phoenix Dragoons and Sovereign Guard teamed up for a raid on Grobb!
(by Serax)

  • On December 30, 1999, in response to an aggressive campaign of destruction and pillaging in Southern Ro, the Phoenix Dragoons and Sovereign Guard temporarily joined forces for an assault on Grobb. The coordination of two powerful guilds for such an action was novel indeed. Over 30 players from both guilds attended the event.

    An eyewitness to the battle and freelance photographer Kimian said, "The trolls were dumbstruck with the lightning takeover of their town square. They never knew what hit them."

    Allied forces suffered the worst casualties after the warrior guild was alerted and rushed to meet the forces of good.

    Colonel Zenki, of the Guard, and the Phoenix Commander, Lord Vanion, later triumphantly posed over the corpse of the leader of the troll raiding forces that have been responsible for the latest troubles in Southern Ro.

  • In other news, Sevastian attended a world congress on determining whether the world is indeed flat, or perhaps as some theorize, round. We await his report.
