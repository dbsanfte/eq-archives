Rules of Conduct

1. Clan Brigatta is first and foremost a guild of friendship and family. We are together because we enjoy each other's company and adventuring together. We exist to help each other become stronger and wiser in the ways of Norrath. If you cannot accept this and are only interested in joining for loot, you've found the wrong guild.

2.Obviously, disagreements or differences of opinion will surface in guild chat. When these situations get out of hand, you will be asked to continue your discussion by a more appropriate means, i.e., tells, group chat, or the message board. The message board is a place for rational discussion and conversation. Name calling, flaming, excessive swearing with the intent to harass or hurt another guild member, and excessive, intentionally cruel sarcasm will not be tolerated.

Clan Brigatta strives to collaborate and interact with other guilds and players on Druzzil Ro in an amiable, honorable, and just manner. All members should attempt to work out problems with other guilds/players calmly and rationally. If you find yourself in a bad situation that tries your patience or temper, take the situation to a Circle member for an attempt at resolution. We recognize that many people who play EverQuest do not extend the same courtesies to us, as we try to extend to them. All we ask is that you do your best to represent Clan Brigatta in a positive light.

 3. Leadership is important and officers have been selected to lead the guild through taking on certain responsibilities that are vital to the efficient and smooth functioning of the family. Guild members are free to ask questions and make suggestions, even to volunteer to work with the officers. Please do not challenge officers in a negative fashion, understand that anyone that holds a higher position, be it the Guild Master or Officer, is doing so as a service to the entire guild and not to "lord" it over anyone in the guild. We ARE a group of friends, and people hold these positions because without leadership nothing will ever get done.

4. Clan Brigatta will be there for each other in times of need. We are there to help each other when things go bad. It is a fact that people will get frustrated, feel depressed or even isolated from time to time. Don't let this get you down, we will only be strong when we have the wisdom to work through the hard times together and when we do that nothing can stop us! If a family member is truly in need then it should be easy for someone close to give a hand.. Not helping a member that really needs it is inexcusable. Examples include corpse recovery, but not ports, rezzes, or such. Ports and resurrections are a benefit of your family and not to be abused. Everyone is here to enjoy the game, but if you really need help then we should be there to help you.

5. It is the responsibility of each family member to check the boards regularly. All meetings, quests, and events should be posted there (unless they develop on the spur of the moment and whim of the family) at least a week in advance.

6. Members are required to show their flag at all times. If you don't show the flag then you aren't proud to be a family member and will be reconsidered for family membership.

7. Members are required to be in attendance at all guild functions, i.e. meetings. If you are unable to attend and are online while the event is in progress, we will reevaluate whether you really want to be part of us. Contact an officer if you are unable to make an event.

8. Lastly, Clan Brigatta WILL NOT TOLERATE Kill Stealing, Power Looting or Cursing people or other guilds in /shouts, or /oocs. Show some restraint and do not allow yourself to be provoked. Your actions in these channels reflect upon every guild member. Reports of inappropriate use of these channels will be investigated and dealt with.

Membership & Recruitment Policy

I. Membership Policy

1. All membership requests will be posted in the New Member Discussion section of our boards.

a) This will ensure that everyone has a chance to see that we have a potential new member.

b) This will also ensure that everyone has a chance to voice their opinion on this person BEFORE the officers decide to join them.

c) The discussion on this board will be moved to Preserved Threads BEFORE this potential member is joined to the family.

d) The officer moving this discussion will post the final decision on the thread before moving it.

e) Names on this board will remain for at least ONE week before a decision is made. If the potential is under 20, they will remain till ONE week after the potential reaches 20. The potential will be voted on and approved or disapproved after ONE week.

2. A minimum level required to join is 20.

a) If a player under 20 wishes to join, they will be assigned a mentor who will guide them to their 20th season. The mentor will then make the recommendation to the guild on whether the player is suitable for family membership or not. If the player is then joined, they are subject to the sponsor system with the mentor being the sponsor.

3. All new family members will be assigned a sponsor by the Membership Officer.

a) The sponsor is responsible for getting new members acquainted with the family and familiar with rules.

b) If the potential new family member is over the age of 20, they will be joined after approval by the Circle and a sponsor will be assigned to them.

4. All new family members will be in a 30-day probationary period.

a) After the initial vote to join a new family member, there will be a second vote by the officers and the sponsor 30 days after they are joined to the family.

b) The initial vote for a new family member will be done by the Circle of Six after viewing all the discussion on the New Member Board. All family members are encouraged to get to know the potential listed on the board and then post their opinions on the board.

5. Real life family members and real life friends are automatically approved for family membership.

a) The real life relation of these new family members will act as the sponsor.

b) The 30 day probationary period is still in effect with these members.

II. Recruitment Policies

1. All players submitted to the Circle are treated equally, whether they be friends of the family, or new friends seeking a good natured playing environment.

2. Only officers may invite players to join the family.

a) Any non-officer may submit a players name along with his or her recommendation to the New Member Discussion Board.

3. No family member shall at any time actively seek users to join the guild that he/she does not know

a) Such practices as shouting for members to join in a zone are not positive ways in which to build our family.

Loot Policy

1. The following rules apply ONLY to guild sponsored events and no other camp, spawn, drop, loot, quest, or event, regardless of the number of guildmates in group. If there are 6 guildmates that make up the group, then for the sake of the following rules, the previous conditions have been met.

2. Race/Class specific equipment (i.e., non ALL/ALL) will go only to those members of the family that can use the equipment. When more than one person can effectively use the piece of equipment, then two conditions must be met in order for an individual to receive the item.

a) That item will go first to members participating in the family event.

b) If more than one member is participating, then a random roll between 1 and 100 will be made with the highest roller being the winner.

c) If noone in the event meets the requirements for the item, then the Loot Officer (or another officer at the event) will acquire it, pending further consideration on who would best be served by getting it.

3. ALL/ALL equipment will go to the race/class that it will be of the most benefit to. If more than one member is participating in the event that will benefit from the item, then the item will be given according to the conditions listed above. AC items will never go to pure casters unless the melee class declines the item.

4. No Drop items will be assessed in order to determine who will best benefit from them.

a) If it is no drop only because of supreme stats or other use, it will be determined according to the conditions outlined in #2.

b) If it is no drop because of a quest, the see #5.

c) Any no drop whose value can not be determined will go to an officer.

d) If more than one officer is participating in the event, then the item will go to the officer with seniority.

5. Quest items will be divided between the members of the event who can use them as outlined by the conditions in #2. If the quest item also has beneficial stats which will benefit a member of the event, then they will also be added to the random roll. If noone can use the quest item, then it will go to an officer for later distribution to the family.

6. Officer responsibility for any acquired item apart from their own winnings in random rolls will be as follows:

a) Non NO DROP items will be assessed and given to the player/alt determined who could best benefit.

b) Only 2 alts per person will be considered when distributing loot from a family event. These 2 alts will be the 2 listed in the Character Matrix of the boards.

c) NO DROP items will be assessed and any quest done which will yield non NO DROP items will be done if it is at all possible. The result will be given to the family member that is determined to make the most use of the item.

d) If a player can not be determined, then the item will go to the guild bank pending further consideration.

7. ALL items gained by family members will be returned to the guild banker when the member no longer has use for it, except for the NO DROP items acquired.

a) A family member will NOT sell such items, nor TRADE them for better items.

b) Family members who are found to have given away, traded, or sold items gained from a family event will be disciplined according to the rules stated in the Charter.
