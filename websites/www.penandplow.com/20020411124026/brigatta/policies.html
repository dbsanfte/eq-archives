Rules of Conduct

1. Clan Brigatta is first and foremost a guild of friendship and family. We are together because we enjoy each other's company and adventuring together. We exist to help each other become stronger and wiser in the ways of Norrath. If you cannot accept this and are only interested in joining for loot, you've found the wrong guild.

2.Obviously, disagreements or differences of opinion will surface in guild chat. When these situations get out of hand, you will be asked to continue your discussion by a more appropriate means, i.e., tells, group chat, or the message board. The message board is a place for rational discussion and conversation. Name calling, flaming, excessive swearing with the intent to harass or hurt another guild member, and excessive, intentionally cruel sarcasm will not be tolerated.

Clan Brigatta strives to collaborate and interact with other guilds and players on Druzzil Ro in an amiable, honorable, and just manner. All members should attempt to work out problems with other guilds and/or players calmly and rationally. If you find yourself in a bad situation that tries your patience or temper, take the situation to a Circle member for an attempt at resolution. We recognize that many people who play EverQuest do not extend the same courtesies to us, as we try to extend to them. All we ask is that you do your best to represent Clan Brigatta in a positive light.

 3. Leadership is important and officers have been selected to lead the guild through taking on certain responsibilities that are vital to the efficient and smooth functioning of the family. Guild members are free to ask questions and make suggestions, even to volunteer to work with the officers. Please do not challenge officers in a negative fashion, understand that anyone that holds a higher position, be it the Guild Master or Officer, is doing so as a service to the entire guild and not to "lord" it over anyone in the guild. We are a group of friends, and people hold these positions because without leadership nothing will ever get done.

4. Clan Brigatta will be there for each other in times of need. We are there to help each other when things go bad. It is a fact that people will get frustrated, feel depressed or even isolated from time to time. Don't let this get you down, we will only be strong when we have the wisdom to work through the hard times together and when we do that, nothing can stop us. If a family member is truly in need then it should be easy for someone close to give a hand.. Not helping a member that really needs it is inexcusable. Examples include corpse recovery, but not ports, resurrection, or such. Ports and resurrections are a benefit of your family and not to be abused. Everyone is here to enjoy the game, but if you really need help then we should be there to help you.

5. It is the responsibility of each family member to check the boards regularly. All meetings, quests, and events should be posted there (unless they develop on the spur of the moment and whim of the family) at least a week in advance.

6. Members are required to show their flag at all times. If you don't show the flag then you aren't proud to be a family member and will be reconsidered for family membership. Roleplay is suitable for hiding class & level, but anon is restricted from family members.

7. Members are required to be in attendance at all guild meetings.

A.) If you are unable to attend and are online while the event is in progress, we will reevaluate whether you really want to be part of us. Contact an officer if you are unable to make an event. Meetings and prescheduled events take precedence over spur of the moment raids.

B.) Members are expected to attend scheduled events, at least once a week, if online. Continual missing of family events will result in reevaluation of membership.

8. Lastly, Clan Brigatta WILL NOT TOLERATE kill stealing, power looting or cursing people or other guilds in /shout or /ooc. Show some restraint and do not allow yourself to be provoked. Your actions in these channels reflect upon every guild member. Reports of inappropriate use of these channels will be investigated and dealt with.

Membership & Recruitment Policy

I. Membership Policy

1. All membership requests will be posted in the New Member Discussion section of our boards.

a) This will ensure that everyone has a chance to see that we have a potential new member.

b) This will also ensure that everyone has a chance to voice their opinion on this person before the officers decide to invite them to the family.

c) The discussion on this board will be moved to Preserved Threads before this potential member is invited to the family.

d) The officer moving this discussion will post the final decision on the thread before moving it.

e) Names on this board will remain for at least ONE week before a decision is made. If the potential is under 46, they will remain till ONE week after the potential reaches 46. The potential will be voted on and approved or disapproved after ONE week.

2. A minimum level required to join is 46.

3. All new family members will be in a 30-day probationary period.

a) The initial vote for a new family member will be done by the Circle of Six after viewing all the discussion on the New Member Board (before they are invited). All family members are encouraged to get to know the potential listed on the board and then post their opinions on the board.

b) After the initial vote to join a new family member, there will be a second vote by the officers and the sponsor 30 days after they are invited to the family.

c) Upon receiving full family status (after the 30 days), the new family member will be given 50 points on the point system.

    1) This should help balance out the new member with existing family members. Give them an opportunity for drops and such.

4. Real life family members and real life friends are automatically approved for family membership.

a) The 30-day probationary period is still in effect with these members.

b) If a real life friend or family member is under the current base level of Clan Brigatta, they will be reminded of such and still joined to family.

II. Recruitment Policies

1. All players submitted to the Circle are treated equally, whether they be friends of the family, or new friends seeking a good natured playing environment.

2. Any non-officer may submit a players name along with his or her recommendation to the New Member Discussion Board.

3. No family member shall at any time actively seek players to join the guild that he/she does not know

a) Such practices as shouting for members to join in a zone are not positive ways in which to build our family.

b) No family member shall at any time actively recruit players that already have a guild.

Loot Policy

1. The following rules apply only to guild sponsored events and no other camp, spawn, drop, loot, quest, or event, regardless of the number of guild mates in group. For the sake of these rules, guild groups are specified by group leader or officer.

2. Race/Class specific equipment (i.e., non ALL/ALL) will go only to those members of the family at the event that can use the equipment. When more than one person can effectively use the piece of equipment, then the following will occur.

a) If more than one member is participating and can use the item, then the member with the most points earned will receive the item. 

1) If this person declines the item, then it will go to the next on the list. This will proceed down till all have declined or one has accepted the drop.

2) When the member accepts an item from the raid, it will lower his points by 50. This allows others to move up the list and receive items.

b) If none at the event have a desire for the item, there will be a group roll with the winner acquiring the item to do with as they wish.

d) If none want the item, it will go to the Guild Banker for distribution to guild mates.

3. ALL/ALL equipment will go to the race/class that it will be of the most benefit to.

a) If more than one member is participating in the event that will benefit from the item, then the item will be given according to the conditions listed above.

b) I.E., Stamina/Haste/AC items have the most benefit to the fighting class whereas Int/Wis have the most benefit to the casters & priests.

c) If there is a dispute as to who will benefit most, then the senior officer at the raid will decide. If no decision can be reached, the item will be set aside pending discussion by the Circle of Six.

4. No Drop items will be assessed in order to determine who will best benefit from them.

a) If it is no drop only because of supreme stats or other use, it will be determined according to the conditions outlined in #3.

b) No drop quest items will also be determined as outlined above.

c) Any no drop whose value can not be determined will go to an officer.

d) If more than one officer is participating in the event, then the item will go to the officer with seniority.

5. Quest items will be decided as outlined above.

6. ALL items gained by family members from the guild banker will be returned to the guild banker when the member no longer has use for it, except for the NO DROP items acquired.

a) A family member will NOT sell such items, nor TRADE them for better items.

b) Family members who are found to have given away, traded, or sold items gained from a family event will be disciplined according to the rules stated in the Charter.

7. God/Dragon/Boss Loot will be distributed by the Circle, based on Circle decision.

Point System

1. When you arrive on a raid, you will sign in with the "Raid Leader"

a) The "Raid Leader" is responsible for guild mates at the raid, in addition to posting the results of the raid on the "Attendance" section of our boards.

b) After the raid, an officer will update the points in our point system database. 

c) If something is acquired by one of our members or guests on a raid, the person who acquired the item will lose 50 points on the Point System Board.

1) This will allow others a chance at getting drops at future raids.

d) Guests will be afforded the same courtesy for earning and spending points as Clan Brigatta members.

2. There will be plenty of opportunities for all family members to earn points. Spur of the moment raids, surprise events, and other items will be cause for adding points to the Point System Board.

a) The Circle of Six will always accept input from members on something that they deem worthy of the extra points. Just submit a private message, on the boards, to an officer to get your idea posted to the officers board.

b) Sinya will be running a "mirror" of our evening activities in the later evening hours. She will be keeping track of the people who are late niters and making sure that they have the same chance as the rest of the family for gaining more points.

3) If a member is found to have been belligerent, or in another fashion disruptive on a raid, then there will be an officer vote (in private, on the boards) for that member to lose 10 points from the Point System Board.

a) The reason for this is because of the many things the "Raid Leader" and officers have to do on the raids.

b) Constantly reminding someone to "Get off the walls", or to "Back up behind a certain point", or to "Stop rushing the incoming NPC", requires time and effort that only causes more grief on a raid than is necessary.

4) The following is the list of points available:

a) Weekday Raids - 10 points
b) Weekend Raids - 20 points
c) Late for a raid, but planned ahead and ARE in zone - same points
d) Late for a raid, but didn't plan ahead (IE, not in zone) - half points
e) Special events and Surprise Points - at least 5 points
