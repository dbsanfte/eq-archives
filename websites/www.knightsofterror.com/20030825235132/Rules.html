Knights of Terror - New Recruit Primer

 

First Drafting, July 6, 2003 – Rikko Streako

 

1.0  Guild history

 

1.1  Early years

The Knights of Terror formed in the early 90’s in a text-only multiplayer RPG called MajorMUD. It was there that a large number of the core members came together and also the place that KoT began developing a name as a serious PK guild.

 

1.2  Dark Age of Camelot

Many of the current KoT members joined the guild in DAOC. We played in Albion on the Bedevere server, as well as on the Andred (now merged to Mordred) PvP servers. Again, we focused on RvR/PvP and made a name for ourselves as aggressive player killers. If you played on any of our servers, even if you didn’t know us personally, let us know!

 

1.3  Shadowbane

Shadowbane’s release led to a fragmentation of KoT. A number of KoT still play DAOC on the Bedevere/Mordred servers, some are playing Shadowbane on the Treachery server, and others have fallen off the map entirely. After several changes to the guild structure and policies, it is time for rapid expansion of the guild so we can resume our role as a respected PK guild.

 

 

2.0  Guild hierarchy

 

2.1  Guild leadership

The official leader of the Knights of Terror is Chance Nifield. His characters in Shadowbane are also named Draxon and Paris. Reality has forced him to take a back seat in guild affairs in both DAOC and Shadowbane for the time being.

 

2.2  Shadowbane leadership

The acting guildmaster of KoT on the Treachery server is Corrupted. His other characters include Doula, Child, and Hippocrates. In order to stay alive in Shadowbane, it has become necessary for the guild to alter its play style subtly to accommodate the game mechanics and politics inherent in a game of this nature.

Presently, Corrupted’s inner council is made up of Rikko/Rakko/Huk/Aethelia/Fryzor, Lithrien, and Stifler/Fruck. Corrupted is typically very busy handling diplomacy or city maintenance when logged in, so if you have an issue that does not require his personal involvement, please direct any problems to an inner council member.

 

2.3  Chain of command

KoT always has (and always will) followed a strict military hierarchy with respect to internal affairs. Please note the ranks of other guild members. You will defer authority to a member of higher rank if there is a problem. No KoT boss one another around, but rather if the destination of a group is in question or a tactical decision needs to be made, you will not do your own thing and order others to follow you. Disobeying a direct order is grounds for immediate banishment from the guild.

Please keep in mind that an officer cannot tell you to “go farm” or somesuch – what you do with your time online is your business, provided you are not risking the reputation of the guild or bringing possible ruin to us all by running off your mouth.

 

 

3.0  Internal affairs

 

3.1  Grouping

3.1.1        XP groups

In a group focused on leveling, every player should be actively helping. If you are of too low level to damage the mobs (read: you are getting powerlevelled), this does not entitle you to wander off and get a free ride. If you are able to track – do so. Often. Keep the group updated about new people in the area. If you are a healer, keep everybody buffed. If you are a mage, be ready to ID magic items. If you are a tank, you can offer to be the main looter (ML) and pick up all the items that drop so the fighters waste less time.

If your group comes under attack by other players, do your best to engage and kill the players. KoT does not run. KoT will not be cut down in the tracks, fleeing in random directions. Exceptions may be if a group of R3 players comes under attack by a full group of R6, or if someone is carrying especially valuable loot. Remember: you might get away, but you probably won’t. But if at least one of you survives, then they can’t loot you.

 

3.1.2        Farming groups

Farming groups are a sad, boring necessity when you have a city. Upkeep is in the millions every week, and a lot of cash flow is necessary to keep a city growing. If you want to come help us farm, we typically reserve all gold for the tree. Any items that drop can be distributed among group members based on need. Also, feel free to bring along a lower level alt if you’d like a few levels while we do our work.

 

3.1.3        PvP groups

A PvP group is typically under complete control of the group leader. The objectives may range from patrols to search-and-destroy. If your leader tells you to jump, don’t even waste time asking “how high?” – do what he says. In a game where a channeler can hit you for half of your hitpoints with a single bolt, there is no time for a strategic discussion.

You may have your own strategies for engaging other players – try to establish your method of attack before your group engages so others know where they fit in. For example, if a caster is planning to AE snare an enemy group on contact, this knowledge is especially useful to everyone else as it allows the enemy tanks to be ignored for a short time while the casters are taken out.

Most importantly, when you engage other players, turn OFF following. This is important for group leaders to do, but group members should have a hotkey available to return control of their movements to themselves in an ambush.

 

3.1.4        Siege groups

Siege groups are more or less PvP groups in a congested area. Try to stay together and keep each other alive. The key to holding a city is the control the tree – don’t give our enemies a chance to rest up and return.

As with PvP groups, listen to your group leader above all others. If you are the group leader, find out who the siege leader is and listen to their orders and relay them to your group. Explore targets of opportunity when provided, but don’t leave a siege to chase a single rogue.

 

3.2  Looting

3.2.1        Main looter

The main looter (ML) is usually selected ahead of time and is responsible for looting all items acquired by the group. Respect this rule and don’t even “peek” at mobs.

 

3.2.2        Splitting loot

Loot splits in groups can be done in a variety of ways. Establish the looting rule well in advance of a sought-after item drop to prevent petty squabbling within the guild. Whatever splitting method you choose to follow, please remember it is considered good form to announce any drops when you get them. If you turn over an item you have looted to someone who can ID it – that item still belongs to you. If an IDer decides to “liberate” an item they are IDing, attempt to settle the problem privately. Barring this, contact an officer and it will be dealt with.

Split methods:

Free-for-all: Gold split is turned on. Whatever player gets to a mob first picks up whatever he or she pleases.

Gold-to-guild/items-to-group: All gold collected in the group is given to an officer before the group disbands so that it can be placed into the tree. Items dropped can be divided among group members as they see fit.

Items-to-guild/gold-to-group: This less-common method can be used to help increase the bank accounts of the members while providing a good amount of magical items for the guild to place at the merchants. At the end of the group, all items picked up will be turned over to an officer who can deposit them with the proper merchants.

 

3.2.3        Selling/buying loot

If you need gear, you need it. Simple enough. Our city may not have what you need. Take a trip to one of the multiple merchant cities which are generally neutral,  and you might find what you need.

Selling, on the other hand, is something we’d like you to remember. Our merchants offer fair rates for items, and we really need the loot (both junk and halfway-decent magical) to maintain the city. Please do all NPC selling to merchants in our city and no others.

 

3.3  Sharing among guildmates

KoT do not sell or trade items with one another. Period. If you have an item that one of your guildmates really needs – give it to ‘em (or shut up about having it if you can’t bear to part with it). If you think you really need the item for another of your characters or are sitting on it until you can apply it later on – so be it, just don’t attempt to extort from your family.

If you are looking for something, ask in guildchat first. A lot of us have a great deal of crap saved in our banks in case it comes in handy some day.

 

4.0  External affairs

4.1  Initiating PvP combat

We are not an enormous guild – it does not pay to have a lot of people hating us. Do not assault guilds that we have good relations with, and do not assault our neighbours (unless they are hostile). If you are attacking a group that contains members from an enemy guild and others, focus your attack on the enemy guild until and unless the neutral group members engage you.

 

4.2  Defending against a PvP attack

If you come under attack by a player and cannot handle it, quickly ask in guildchat for an emergency summon. We will try to pull you out if we can. “/gu SUMMON ME NOW PK” is all it takes – then tap sprint and try to buy yourself some time while we get to you.

It goes without saying that if you are attacked you may defend yourself. Our relations with the attacking guild are a non-issue. They attacked you, you will destroy them for that.

 

4.3  TOL camping

Camping another guild’s tree is generally considered lame. If you make a sweep through a hostile town and have thoroughly pacified the area, don’t wait around their tree for someone to respawn. It’s a boring waste of time for both parties and a sign of poor sportsmanship.

 

4.4  “Ganking”

If you come across a group of an enemy guild, destroy them. It doesn’t matter what level they are or what they are trying to do. They have made enemies of KoT and this is the price they pay. If it is a friendly guild or lowbie/errant XP group, pop by and say hi for a minute, then continue about your business. If you have a thief in your group, you may obviously leave him or her behind for a few minutes to “secure” the camp.

Simply killing a random player 40 levels below you is idiotic and the bad press against KoT will outweigh the 1k gold and See Invisible ring he might have been carrying. Beyond this, you may have cost us a future recruit.

This said, we are a PK guild and will engage in combat when the opportunity arises. If someone has a problem with being killed, instruct them to have an officer of their guild contact a KoT officer and we can negotiate a relationship.

 

 

5.0  Diplomacy

5.1  Chat conduct

5.1.1        Private tells

Private tells are the closest you will get to an out-of-character conversation in Shadowbane. Use a PM to reach an understanding with another player about something. Don’t use them to harass other players. The game becomes significantly less fun when your account is suspended by a CCR for harassment.

 

5.1.2        Conduct with guildmates

5.1.2.1.1        Guild channel

The guild channel is normally used for communicating with anyone in the guild about any topic. Ask for groups, ask about items, ask about game mechanics here. KoT guildchat is notorious for raunchy shit talk.

 

5.1.2.1.2        Group channel

If you are grouped with KoT only, treat group chat exactly as you would guildchat.

If there are non-KoT in the group, take care with what you say. Don’t be especially vulgar and DO NOT DISCUSS ANY TACTICAL OR POLITICAL SUBJECTS! We will make our members aware of diplomatic issues in guildchat – this does not mean it can be advertised outside the guild.

 

5.1.2.1.3        City channel

Keep in mind that come guilds have sworn under us (and at times we have sworn under other guilds) and so many non-KoT could be bound to our tree. As a result, try to use the city channel only for coordinating groups or the like. Leave chatting for groups or the guild.

 

5.1.3        Conduct with other guilds

 

5.1.3.1  Nation members

Nation members are to be treated as KoT without the perks. If you have room in a group, invite them. Do not go out of your way to provide them with items when they can be of more use to KoT.

Mind your manners with nation guilds and remember that many of them may roleplay. While KoT is not a roleplaying guild, remember we are in the nation so that we form a stronger whole and childish behaviour will probably only weaken the alliance.

 

5.1.3.2  Neutral guilds

Neutral guilds could one day be our allies. They could also be our enemies. If you come across another guild that we have had no previous contact with, attempt to open a dialogue. If they give you attitude, kill them. If they are friendly, try to leave on good terms – a week later they could be subbed under us.

 

5.1.3.3  Enemy guilds

Kill enemy guild members on sight. Do not PM bragging or sore loser messages after combat. Ignore them unless they contact you. We’re not here to be their friends and we aren’t interested in a prick-waving contest.

5.2  Disputes

 

5.2.1        Internal

If a conflict between guildmates occurs, attempt to resolve it in private tells. This may involve a loot item, the leadership of a group, or anything in between. If you cannot resolve this, contact an officer and they will handle it. Listen to your officers. If you think the decision handed down is not just, feel free to discuss it with the officer. If the decision was just and you’re just a sore loser, you’re best to deguild now because the guards will probably be kicking your ass in a minute.

 

5.2.2        External

If a diplomatic problem comes up and you are in the spotlight, find an officer and describe the problem to them. Then, tell the non-KoT that they should speak to an officer and name the officer you have spoken with. We will deal with the problem. Don’t lie to us. If you did something stupid, at least tell us so we can deal with it. Lying will only create a bigger problem for the guild. We will not sell you out.
