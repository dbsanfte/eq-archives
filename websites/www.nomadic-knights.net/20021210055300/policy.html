Nomadic Knights

Guild Information and Policies

Our Guild Charter

The Nomadic Knights are a guild of friendship who aim to promote fair play and honor throughout the world of Norrath. We will accept any Walkers of Norrath or Luclin into our ranks regardless of their age or experience. We will pass our skills and knowledge onto the inexperienced and readily learn from those who are able to teach us new skills. When we fight we shall do so with our honor and integrity to the fore. By doing this we shall achieve our mutual goals, and benefit all within the guild and those who accompany us on our travels.

Our Guild Goals

The Nomadic Knights wish to be a family strong in both people and resources.  Our long-term goals are to become a guild on the Vazaelle server that is for helping others on the server, and holding public events for all to attend.  Our short-term goals currently are to build our guild through quality membership, particpation, and alliances, providing the best that we can offer to players of all levels on the Vazaelle server.

Our Guild Policies

Membership Entry

We welcome all walkers of Norrath and Luclin into our ranks regardless of level and experience. To apply for membership into Nomadic Knights, click here.  After the application is filled out, an officer will contact you for further instructions.  Please note invitations and re-invitations are dealt with on an individual case basis.  General invitations will usually include a 30-day probationary period, to observe that the new member is meeting the expectations of the guild.

Role of a General Member

As a general member, you will be expected to participate by the following:

  • Checking and posting on the message board a minimum of once a week.  It is highly recommended to check more often, as you may find something is going on that you would want to attend.

  • Provided there are sufficient members online, group with guild members first.  We understand that there are not always enough people online to group with, or you have friends that you would like to group with.  We encourage you to bring along your friends, but if you do not group within the guild you are missing out on gaining valuable knowledge of other guild members, such as fighting technique and habits.  These become important as your character progresses in Everquest and especially when you need to group with others for higher level drops.  If you cannot find enough members in the guild in your level range, consider checking with our allied guilds to see if you can find more.  For a list of current guild alliances, click here.

  • If there is a raid in your level group and you are online, you will be expected to either attend the raid or give reason as to why you cannot.  Again, this goes to knowing the other members of the guild and building teamwork within the guild. Raids will be arranged in accordance with policies set up by the raid officer. To view those policies, click here.  Guilds that we are allies with may also have raids that you want to attend.  Please check their forums on the message board to see what they have going on and don't forget to let them know if you plan on attending.

  • Be willing to assist guild members when possible.  All members will want to get an item of importance to them at some point in playing the game, and they will need help.  If you see a post on the message board or discussion in game about plans for this and you are in the level range, please consider helping them out.  At the very least, be willing to apologize if you cannot help and wish them good luck.  If you need to request the assistance of the guild with a drop or quest, we recommend a 2-week notice so that people have time to see it and hopefully get more to attend and help out. 

  • Recruiting:  As a Nomadic Knight, you may find people asking you about joining our guild.  In order to keep a good relationship with everyone, including those that would want to apply to our guild, simply refer them to our website, and let them know there are application instructions on our website.

Disciplinary Actions

 When a complaint is received to the officers about a member of the guild, it will be reviewed by them. Upon this review, the officers will issue a one-time warning to the player in question regarding the action that lead to the complaint. Upon receiving this warning, the player will be placed on probation for a set period of time depending on the specific offense. If another offense occurs during the probationary period, the member will be disbanded from the guild at that time. Certain offenses, such as theft, will result in no warnings, but upon verification of the claim, the member will be removed from the guild.  In addition, any offense within the probationary period following an invitation into Nomadic Knights will result in removal from the guild.

Behavior Unbecoming of a Knight

The following is an example of some of the things that Nomadic Knights will not tolerate. Exceptions on these behaviors are given on a case-by-case basis.  Please contact an officer in game or leave a post on the message board asking for exception authorization.

  • Whining

  • Begging (For money, items, ports, rezzes, etc.)

  • Excessive use of foul language in game or on the message board

  • Demanding donations for services rendered.  For more information on this topic, please check our Good Practices page.

  • Intentional bashing or flaming guild members in game or in any other way.

  • Intentional training of mobs onto other players

  • Ninja looting

  • Camp Stealing - Camp stealing can include traditional camps and also when groups claim multiple camps. The main type of group in the latter category is an AOE group. If you want to camp an area an AOE group is claiming, --DO NOT just walk in and take what you want, send a tell to the group and ask if you can camp x. This is more likely to achieve an amicable settlement.

  • Playing using the Anonymous mode.  We require the guild tag be shown, therefore Role playing is allowed and Anonymous is not.

  • Lack of participation in the guild.  If real life requires you to take extended time away from the game, please let us know so we don't just think you don't want to participate in the guild.  You may be questioned on things that are going on here on the website, and not knowing the information (raid rules, guild policies, etc.) may put your guild participation in question.

  • Powerleveling (Helping a character gain experience and levels faster than what would usually be the norm) or twinking (Giving a character better equipment than what they should have at that level).  As always, Nomadic Knights has never and will never dedicate full guild efforts to powerleveling or twinking.
