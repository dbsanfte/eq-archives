Raid Rules and Policies

General Info:
Raids will be set up for the purpose of an opportunity to group with other guildees and obtain experience and items that a single group may not be able to obtain by them selves. A few basic raid rules will be needed to ensure the safety and enjoyment of everyone.

 - There will be one Raid Leader assigned to each raid. He/she may ask others to assist but this is at their discretion. All instruction should be taken from the raid leader or any person he/she has asked to assist in running the raid.
· All raids will have a minimum level requirement. If you do not meet the requirement you will not be assigned to a group in the raid.
· The starting time, meeting place and length of the raid will be stated in the Raid announcement. Raid attendees/guildees will not be required to escort you in or out of the zone if you will be late or need to leave early. However, at the discretion of volunteer(s) someone may be able to pick you up. Please do not ask repeatedly for assistance while the raid is going on.
· If you have a balanced group which you typically worked with, please let the raid leader know in advance and that person will try to place you together unless you are needed to fill another group.

Getting started:
· Ensure that you have a map in hand of the area being raided.
· Arrive at least 30 minutes before the planned start time of the raid. It will take at least 30 minutes and maybe more to set up groups and coordinate hunting locations.
· When you arrive at the grouping location, immediately disband your self from any group that you were part of. Put up the Looking For Group tag by typing: /LFG ON (Ensure that you are not in anon or roleplay in order for the leader to see your class and level)
· Once assigned to a group, move to your group leader's location, remove the LFG tag, sit down and minimize chat until all instructions are stated.
· Prepare all of your hotkeys. For most characters, this will consist of a "/assist Main_Assist_Name" key.

During the Raid:
· Raid Leader will use a specified chat channel to communicate to everyone. This will include requests for status from group leaders as well as coordinating the looting of no-drop items.
· Do not wander away from the group, stick together or you may aggro additional MOBs. Raids are (usually) not the time to explore.
· Only the group leaders should be communicating with the raid leader or raid assistants unless there is an emergency. If you need to inform the raid leader of some information try to do so via the group leader.

Loot Rules:

General Loot:
· The group leader should designate a master looter within the group. If the group members change during the raid the master looter should distribute the items and coin as evenly as possible before disband.

 Special Items:
· When a special item drops, the group leader will notify the Raid Leader of the item and relevant stats on it, to include who can use it. Then the Raid Leader will announce in the chat channel that the item has been looted and ask for those who can use it to let their group leader know. Numbers will be assigned and called out. A /random 1 xx will be done (with xx being the last number assigned) and the winner announced.. The items should be distributed as to how they best support the guild members. First anyone who can use the item for their character at the raid to improve their stats can roll on the item. If no immediate person can use the item, the raid leader will determine some form of equitable distribution such as roll for alts, open roll, one for each group to sell and split proceeds, etc. depending on how many items there are.

 No-Drop Items:
· When a no-drop item drops from a MOB, time is of the essence. No-drop items are a special case and will be treated as such. In most cases these items are quest related and should go to whoever is closest to completing the quest. At this time the Raid Leader may ask in /chat channel who needs the item but it will be up to the Raid Leader to decide who gets to loot.

Have fun:
· Remember this is a game and it should be fun for everyone. Do not allow yourself to stress or cause others stress during the raid. Follow instructions of the raid leader and take occasional mishaps lightly. If the raid is not going well, post a constructive suggestion on the boards after you log to make it go more smoothly the next time.

Last but not least Rule #3 NO DYING, This is in Honor of our Great Friend Aarcanus. One of the great teachers of EQ.

 

Raid Hosting Information

 
Both officers and non officers will be allowed to host raids, however, the raid and raid leader must be approved by Mystykal or Aztor. In cases where someone’s raid leader experience is unknown, Aztor or Mystykal may assign someone to co-host the raid. To have a raid approved, email or post the details with the request to Aztor or Mystykal. The details will be reviewed and posted on the board if approved.

Details to include in post:
Date of Raid
Place of raid (attached map is preferable)
Meeting place
Meeting and start time in EST and GMT*
Details of transportation to and from (if available)
Minimum Level Requirements
Any other useful or pertinent information
----------------------------------------------------
*GMT is EST plus 5 (noon EST is 5pm GMT)

Qualifications for leading a raid:
-You should be familiar and prepared to enforce raid policy/rules.
-You should be familiar with the zone you intend to raid.
-You should be able to respond to multiple tells and give directions quickly and effectively

Raid leaders responsibilities:
-Arrange groups with appropriate mix and level where possible
-Ensure all attendees are appropriate level for the zone
-Ensure all attendees are aware of the raid rules and have a map of the zone
-Resolve any disputes and address any misconduct
-Ensure corpse recovery of all attendees prior to logging
-Ensure all have been offered the opportunity to be led to a safe area to camp prior to logging
-Because you are assuming all of the above, it may be good to (1) call the raid’s end 30 minutes prior to when you are prepared to log (2) pre-assign someone reliable to take over incase real life or technical issues force you to leave early.

Raid standards:
-Raid leaders organize raids for the benefit of all attendees and not for their own personal gain. Raid fees and loot preferences will generally not be acceptable. Any special circumstances for these should be discussed with Aztor and Mystykal in advance and clearly announced in the post.
-While it is not the main purpose of the raid to get XP, it is always good to distribute the opportunity for XP whenever possible and safe to do so. Single puller raids should have an “Inc for Group 1, 2 and so on” and unless the mob is too powerful for one group to kill, other groups should allow the announced group to have the kill. No one wants to spend several hours at a raid sitting around watching the power group kill everything when they are capable of killing most of the mobs in the area on their own.
-Whenever possible it is good to assist guildees in arranging transportation to and from the raid, but not required.
-In some cases it may be necessary to deviate from standard raid policies such as on Looting rights on Epic Quests, Non familiarity on Explorations, etc. These should also be approved in advance by Aztor or Mystykal and clearly defined in the raid announcements.

Last minute miniraids, powerleveling sessions and other mass guildee get togethers do not require approval in advance, however, the organizer is expected to follow raid leader standards and raid rules to the extent possible.
