Comments

  1. The key stats for comparison are AC, sta/hp, wis/mana, resists, and effects. There is nothing bad about having good numbers in other areas, but they are not where a shaman lives and dies.

  2. I have included ten breastplates that I judge to be in the “high midrange” of EQ shaman chest armors. There are items that are considerably stronger; there are items that are not as good but still quite decent. NK is solidly in the upper midrange: these are the armors we should be looking at.

  3. The three velious quest BPs are roughly equivalent. They all have the same clicky effect. They all have solid AC, good sta/hp, and a hefty amount of mana. But they all have an immense drawback: rarity. This ranges from severe (Thurg) to extreme (Skyshrine) to never (Kael). That is, given the faction of Nomadic Knights, no NK shaman will ever have the Spirit Caller BP. We spend a lot of time farming for the Thurgadin drops in the Kael arena, and the quest piece might drop next time we’re there: but it is difficult for any NK shaman to plan on obtaining a Rune Crafter BP. We will (someday) start to get drops for Wolf Caller armor, but this can only be a daydream for any NK shaman until we have advanced to farming in ToV.

  4. There are six buyable chest pieces listed. Although the Jaundiced Bone BP may once have been the finest armor a shaman could get, it nowadays it is harder to be excited about its relatively low AC and sta/hp, and its lack of resists. The Emerald Dragonscale Tunic is a good purchase for a shaman just moving into the high midrange, especially when seeking higher wisdom, but it clearly is the low end item among these ten BPs. I judge the Tae Ew Chain Tunic to be “best all around” of the ten. It has high AC, high sta/hp, decent wis/mana, and good resists. And it is quite affordable. I’ve noted Artificers Ringmail which is Barbarian only; the other races have parallel cultural armors with nearly identical stats. Cultural, Night Terror, and Hurricane are all quite decent pieces, but they also suffer from rarity: if you ever see them for sale you will be paying 4 or 5 times the price of a Tae Ew for stats that aren’t quite as good.

  5. I’ve selected the Chestplate of Vindication as the high end of the upper midrange. Some would argue that it is a melee item rather than a shaman item, but the game itself marks the item as shaman usable. When we succeed in killing Derakor the Vindicator on a regular basis, these will begin to become common in NK. Once our main tanks have better equipment than this, shamans should certainly expect to be on the list for these: very strong sta/hp, very strong resists, and strong AC will mean the shaman will live longer – and that’s a good thing – when a couple of resisted slows have gotten a mob upset.

  6. In terms of strategy, I recommend that any shaman who does not have a BP as good as one of these ten should aim for the Tae Ew Chain Tunic. If you have no other source of platinum whatsoever, you can solo Ice Giants at the gate to Permafrost from level 49 on. (The trick, of course, is getting one of the camps.) In a week or two of serious exp grinding there, you can expect to level up a few times, and you will have generated 8000 to 10000 pp in cash, gems, and FS weapons: enough to buy the Tae Ew with a little left over.

(Alas, the Ice Giant strategy isn’t very exciting. The first few times you attack them, it’s pretty scary, but soon you’ve done 20 of them, and then they’re all the same as you do hundreds more. Its only virtue is: success. Two weeks and you’ve got a Tae Ew tunic. Two weeks of raiding in the Kael arena might produce 1 Breastplate – which someone else is going to win the roll on.)
