A heroine finds the end of her quest.
Friday 12.03.2004
Yes, as I sit on top of the hill, over looking all of Norath, I realize how much I have done and how much time had gone by. As I sit there with my Firery Defender at my side I remember all of my life; a life that was not a grand as some, but a life which fulfilled my dreams.

I remember the early days of running for my life from the gnolls or adventuring in the darkest parts of Runeyeye. Yes, I was a paladin. I never once took money for buffs or rezzes, I just could not imagine doing tat to someone. It was my duty as a protector to help those in need. When I was a midlevel knight of only 17, I used to spend hours in Freeport or Qyenos helping the new adventurers. What a life I have lived.

I remember my very first guild. The Guardslayers of Norrath. It was funny, because I really did not know what a guild was, yet I joined. Of course the idea of slaying guards really was my forte so I left them when I met someone special by the name of Peji. I was in Dread Lands when I first joined her in a group. We stayed for hours slaying the evil creatures of the land. I later met Lync, her leader and that was the turning point of my life.

I remember all of the adventures I had with Lync and her small guild, The Sins of Wrath. We were small, but we had heart. We may have been beaten back my many of the creatures that roamed Norrath, but we never gave up. Whether it be the Hole or Griegs End, we were there to fight the good fight.

I know for some people who have traveled further then me, I may sound tiresome or inexperienced. You see, for me it was never about the loot or the magic. Not even the experience kept me here. You see, for me it was the good people that I met. They were my guild and they were my best friends.

As all tales have a climax, so does mine. For you see there was a Dark elf that I met in the Plane of Air. Her name was Blueberry and she led a guild that was not unlike Sins of Wrath. They were small and they had heart too. She helped me with my final part of my quest for a sword of power; for the Firey Avenger. I was ever so thankful for her and everyone else who helped me get there.

There were people that I truly held dear to me. Spoiledbrat, Diviyne, Bastinda, Ikyth, Errian. They were all officers with me in this guild. I know that they all have moved on and I wish them well wherever their travels take them.

As I sit there with my sword held high, I salute you Norrath. I salute all who will stay and continue the quest. For this paladin, this Lightbringer, the quest is over. As I bid my final salute to the world, I put away my Firery Defender, extinguishing the light forever and I cry.

******************************************

Yes, I am leaving Everquest. I know longer feel the desire to log on anymore and my time is less and lees then it used to be. To all of those that I knew. I will miss you. To those that I never met, perhaps had things been different I would have met you. Who knows. Some roads are never traveled, yet we cannot go back and try again.

I wish you all well. Who knows maybe one day, I will see you again. Berthall, you all have your ups ad downs and I have enjoyed the ride. I wish you all well as well. I may poke in here from time to time. I guess time will only tell.

If anyone wants my email, please PM me for it.

Lightbringer Shaylia Stormfaith
May your light never be extinguished.
