Angel's Fletching Guide & Recipes

Fletching Starting Information

I suggest you invest very few points if any at all in Fletching to get it started. Be warned that fletching is NOT fun. It is very tedious. You have to put 1 of each of the 4 arrow components in to the kit 1 at a time. If you put in a stack you will get back 1 arrow if successful. So, before you start get comfortable and be ready to exercise your wrist often. I recently worked Fletching on my newest Character, Crimsonrain, with the intent to just get her to Master in Fletching. It cost just over 100PP to get her to the skill of 102 using the chart below.

Bowering Info & Recipes

So, you are seeking wealth and fame young Fletcher? I would advise you leave now. Fletching is about the art of making arrows, and when accompanied by its brother Bowing, is quite remarkable.

I will pass the basic ideas of making an arrow and a bow, except I wish to impart this bit of knowledge before I go on with higher level fletching information.

  1. You will not make money
  2. This tends to be only for your own benefit, unless you are willing to work hard to make some of the more complicated bows. Then others may buy your bows.
  3. In my experience as a Master Fletcher, the main source of damage comes from your Bow, so just use the cheapest arrow that has the range needed for your bow.
  4. Max damage down by a bow shot is determined as followed.
    Damage of Bow + Damage of Arrow= Max potential damage
  5. range is determined by the shortest range of the bow or the arrow.
  6. The difficulty of the item, except in Dual Cam bows, is determined by the most expensive item involved, not by the sum of the parts. True for Arrows and Bows

Now that we have those important things addressed, on to more information.

Rough Wood Staffs

First you must decide on the strength of the bow you wish. Damage done buy the bow is dependant on the strength of wood and the string used. The more costly the wood the stronger it is, and subsequently the harder to craft. Starting from weakest the selections are: Hickory, Elm, Ash, Oak, Darkwood. You may also fashion a bow from Steel and Ceramic, these being stronger still than wood.

String

The strength of string also determines the damage done by the bow. The better quality of string the better the damage. There are 3 types of string, they are: Hemp, Linen, and Silk. Only the best bows are strung with silk.

Tools

Next we come to increasing the quality of the bow, which usually means decreasing the delay or rate at which the bow is fired. Unfortunately damage is also decreased by this process. There are 2 tools to date: Whittling Knife, and the Plane. Once again each is an increase in quality and price. Each again is more difficult to use.

Pulleys

To pack a wallop, adding pulleys will cause damage to increase dramatically. There is only one class of pulley but you may add one or two, they are unfortunately very expensive!

Fletching Kit

There is only one kit to buy for crafting both Bows and Arrows. To use it for bows, it is fairly simple. At beginning levels you need only buy the staff and string, place both in the kit and press combine. As with any craft you will wind up making a bow, or not. Using the tools is both expensive and risky as they are expended when added to the kit. A tool can be used with Elm or better wood, it is simply placed in the kit with the staff and string.

 

 

 

 

 

 

As you increase Damage, you increase Delay. If you decrease Delay, you decrease damage. This is true with all bows, except with Cams. By using the different strings, you can tune a bow to the user. With an elm bow, you can make the following:

Damage 13 Delay 51 Uses Hemp, no tool
Damage 12 Delay 47 Uses Linen, no tool
Damage 11 Delay 43 Uses Silk, no tool
Damage 10 Delay 39 Uses Silk, whittling Knife. Called a Carved Elm Recurve bow

This type of trend is common in bows. I have not made a Elm with the other two strings and a knife, so I do not have the stats for you.

  • Hickory bows - skill 35
  • Rough Elm bows - Skill varies with string of course, but around 75 or 80 to be trivial
  • Use of the Whittling knife becomes trivial around 90
  • According to others: Rough Ashwood, around 100.

String is required for all bows. Choices include Hemp, Linen, and Silk. Hemp is the simplest to work with, and yields highest damage but greatest delay. Linen and Silk are progressively more complex to work with, and provide progressively lower damages and delays.

Tools and cams are optional, and indeed only allowed on certain staff materials. The simpler tool choice is the Whittling Knife, which yields a Carved bow. The more advanced tool is the Plane, which yields a Shaped bow. The use of Cams yields a cammed or compound bow, and are usable on Oak (1 cam) and Darkwood (1 or 2 cams).

Re-stringing Bows

It is possible to change the string on your bow.  This allows the discriminating archer to change out bow strings and modify slightly his/her bow characteristics according to the situation at hand.

To change bow strings, simply place a completed bow into the fletching kit along with the new desired string, and hit the combine key. The new string will disappear, and the newly strung bow will appear on your cursor. The old string from the bow is not recovered. Changing strings appears to become trivial at skill 16, but appears to always succeed, offering an interesting alternative to gaining skill at least up to skill 16.

Arrow Components & Recipes

Arrows are constructed using four components:

  • Point
  • Shaft
  • Fletching
  • Nock

Creating a batch of 5 arrows is accomplished by placing 1 of each of the above component types into the same fletching kit used to make bows and pressing the "Combine" key.

The base arrow type has a damage of 1 and a range of 50. The final value of damage and range in effect for a particular bow/arrow combination is determined by adding the range and damage of the bow to the range and damage of the arrow.

The selection of the component types used for the four components affects the cost, complexity, and final damage and range values of the resulting set of 5 arrows, according to the following table:
