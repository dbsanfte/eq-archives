Norwolf Shadoweyes Ranger Guide

A HUGE Thanks goes out to Norwolf Shadoweyes (40th Season Half-Elf Ranger) of the Erollisi Marr Server for submitting this Ranger Strategy Guide!!

Hunting

Level 10-18

Bandits / Brigands in West Karana I have hunted the Bandits near the hill areas solo using harmony and when grouped in the bandit camps. They are great for BOTH experience and loot in that they occasionally drop bronze weapons [~2pp] and 1 out of 4 or so drops Bandit Sashes. You can turn in the Sashes to Chesgard Sydwen, in the house of thunder [Paladins Guild near the docks], he will give you exp, faction, some decent gold and an item (often bronze) if you bring him a bandit sash.

Level 20-27

I have hunted Solo near Kurns Tower hunting Burynai, Skeletons, Scaled Wolfs, Etc you can be BOUND near the ruins at the entrance to Kaesora and can zone to the Swamp, Jungle, Tower, or Castle if things go bad. If you have a group move into Kurns Tower if you like. The burynai drop Burynai hide armor and you can also try for the Jester inside for the bezerker club. You can typically sell some of your items and give buffs to lower level Lizards to have them go trade for you in the city.

Level 25-55 [I've only hunted here level mid 20's to low 30's]

Castle Mistmore is a great spot to hunt grouped or solo [if near the zone or in a area like the graveyard or someplace you are less likely to be trained and you can control what you hunt]. You can get some great equipment here, Ivy Etched Quest items as well as other quest items. The Hunting I have done was in the area near the lake in my mid 20's, moving to the graveyard in my high 20's [still able to get Experience at mid 30's solo there as well], when in my high 20's to mid low 30's with a great strong well rounded group we were able to play some inside the castle as well.

Levels 25-35

IF the Fort in LOIO is booked and the Fort in FM is as well you may consider hunting near the Lake zone on the FM side in a group. The Experience at lower levels here is great, the fighting is furious, and it will keep you on the edge of your seat pulling blues, yellows and reds. Other benefits in grouping here are the zone safety near and the ability to go to the fort if you want. Some words of wisdom if doing this try not to pull goblin or Bunari casters that are red to you, even the yellow ones will be tough for your group. If its red to your highest level member be prepared to retreat. Have a ranger pull, I like monks and druids, etc BUT sorting by range to find the mobs in the nooks and crannies makes good pulls much faster, and because of the terrain its easy to get turned around and you can use tracking to find your party again. BINDING, there is a spot near some ruins in FM... but in truth its closer to a death trap I would use the outpost in the OT just swimming around the wall and getting bound as you are more than likely KOS there as many not.... This is a excellent bind point.

Kernels of knowledge to Seed:

  • Buy and practice the dancing firefly spell.... It's the only way to keel you conjuration still up until you get stinging swarm that you will want to use at lower levels

  • Use foraging as you need this to complete your Epic quests at a minimum

  • Keep your sneak and hide up. Use hide while meddling [until level 35 you have to med with your book] and at other times. Hiding at these times is just one more protective factor from being jumped or useful if unable to cast Camo [with unreliable duration]. And you can use sneak for NPC's interaction if faction is a problem.

  • BOW skills - use them

  • Pulling with a bow is the best.... you don't have to waste time running up to them [esp if they are moving away from u] and hit them, you don't have to STOP and cast a spell to get their attention.

  • Get use to using a different view [F9] and running backwards while they are Snared and you are SoWed. When pulling this lets me get typically 2-4 additional arrows over my pulling arrow for 50 - 100 points more then if I were to just run back [cast in your DOT and hedge it a bit more if you like] and you can kite things in this way and make sure you do not run into anything that will cause a problem [cliff, mob, tree, water]

  • Something fun... 2 or 3 rangers tag teaming a hard blue or yellow mob kiting it... the one with agro using the bow and kiting, the other ranger swinging at it... if they change agro to the other just swap position [I have done this with giants in FM, and with corrupt guards in WK]

  • Fletching, its cheaper than buying or have if you can have a mage summon arrows for u.

  • Weapons / Armor / Agro - there is a balance there that one must be aware of and have an ability to control. I use 2 weapons [pierce main, 1HS second] and can control my agro by easily and quickly swap to a shield when I'm catching to much agro so this allows me to put out less damage [hence less agro], and give me additional AC. I do this by keeping my second slot and a innovatory slot in my hotkeys.

  • At level 22 use fear kite.

  • As a Ranger I pride myself in knowing the zone better [or strive to] than anyone in your group.

  • Know how to use your abilities.

You are not a meat shield nor a caster, you are a Ranger.
