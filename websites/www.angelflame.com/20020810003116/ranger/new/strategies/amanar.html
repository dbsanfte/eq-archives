Strategy Guide: Amanar, 50th Half-Elf Ranger of Karana on Vazaelle

A Huge THANK YOU to Amanar of Vazaelle for taking the time to write and submit this Ranger Guide to us!! /Huggles Amanar! (Amanar is now level 60, and will perhaps one day submit an update for this guide he wrote  =P)

Levels 1-7

Lets see... at level 1 Amanar was a real noob... he ran around Qeynos Hills being a half elf ranger and basically killed bats... to large bats... to large snakes to giant bats....all the way up to level 7.

Levels 7-15

Now I was finally feeling confident in my ability to play the game, so my friend told me I was ready to go to Blackburrow now (dungeon near of Qeynos Hills.... One of my personal favorite zones still) at level 7 I fought mainly at the top just killing normal Gnolls, but then once I hit level 8 I got a group of 3 or maybe just a partner to fight giant snakes on the snake ledge.... And boy is it UBER loot, each fang gets you 4 gold and each sack 4 gold... and half the snakes drop two! I partnered there till level 10 when I was able to solo them... but remember to snare them because they run off... but if a giant snake runs trust me let it run... it will be back.. if you chase it its going to run you into a lot of trouble. Level 12 I would find a group for either the green room or the clover room. These both have elite gnolls and commanders and even the clover has some refugee splitpaws. Get a good group of level 12s or so and your getting great experience! Besides they drop collars with 3 STR on them which I used till 45... and 2 AGI earrings and other good loot for the level. I hung around there till level 15 when I got my new spells.

Level 15

At level 15 I wondered around Oasis because I heard it was great experience... killed alligators with a friend. Wasn't bad but didn't have any loot... so once I leveled it was time to move on.

Levels 16-18

At level 16 I got my first group at Highhold Pass. One camping the Gnolls. Was GREAT experience and can you believe it I rolled a perfect 100 on a PGT! (yup still remember) which I sold for 100 plat... man was that a good day. At level 17 or so I also tried looking for Orc groups which give more experience... but things can get ugly its good to have some crowd control and a solid group. Good stuff drops their like the herbalist pack too.

Levels 18-30

At lvl 18 well my friend told me he had been hunting in this... Lake of Ill Omen. Thought it sounded weird but he said it was great experience so I took the trip to Kunark... almost died by Drachnids before getting bound (note: get a SoW and camo yourself before journeying up the stream...) There I found groups on the shore hunting Sabretooth tigers and such for a couple levels... till 20. Level 20 I was able to get a group outside the Sarnak fort... ouch we were fighting red Sarnak recruits... but we took them down without too much harm and man what great experience! Did this till level 28... at level 22 or so when you get new spells its good to head inside the fort pull in there. Level 28... well level 28 is when I began my career of leveling the same way I do now... soloing. Yes unlike Rainangel I soloed my later part of my career instead of my earlier. But man was this the best experience ever... and I mean literally haven't found better. Here's what you do. That panic animal spell you got at level 22...first it seems kinda worthless. But wait... go pull a Sabretooth Grimalkin to the shore... snare him... then cast panic animal. It costs 10 mana so keep refreshing it it won't waste too much, and while the animal is running, well just keep whacking! If it breaks step back some and refresh it. I did this all the way to level 30.

Levels 30-45

Iceclad. I went to Iceclad... visited the gnome pirates... don't worry that bunch won't hurt ya they are quite funny. Began there by soloing cougars. Use the same method as on Sabretooth Grimalkins.... And man will you be raking in the experience. But that's not all, it wasn't just great experience... but every 50 or so drop a Cougar claw earring.. 4 AC 3STR 2DEX. Worth about 250 plat on Vazaelle at the time... now worth more around 200. Well I was getting a level a day and 250 plat a day. I was RICH. Finally I could buy a lot of new gear I needed... Cougars lasted till 40. 40 was a hell level... trust me if you don't solo like me all the time... be good to yourself and SOLO hell levels. They go by much faster. So at 40 I went to the island with the dragon bridge and found a nice big ice patty to pull dire wolves to and fear kite them. Sure they didn't drop the nice loot but all I needed was experience... although green at 44, I managed to do them until 45 barely.

Levels 45-50

Stumped as to where to find more things to solo, I ventured into Eastern Wastes. First I figured well let me track some blue animals, and I'll pull them to near the bridge and kite them... Well let me tell ya I was pulling BIG mammoths with tons of HP... pathing was bad... it just wasn't the awesome kiting I had done. So then I found a 53 ranger who lead a life of soloing just like me... and he said he had a GREAT spot. So he told me to go left from the bridge until you track Icefang... track him until you reach his hut. Right around the hut there are never any mobs so it's a safe med spot. Icefang is great experience up until level 52 or so I hear. At 45 he will be semi hard but just keep working on refreshing fear and he's nice, and then you usually can pull a blue walrus in between Icefangs. Note there is a Coldain ghost named Korrigain here who will assist icefang... but you can still pull him by targeting him and running off towards the fire, then pulling with snare from there... Korrigain won't aggro ya. If you have bad Coldain faction though they will scowl... so be aware. Well I have done them up until now... and still fight them a good bit when its time to do the exp grind. Hope this guide helps some young rangers get there way up in the ranks fast and discover how good rangers truly are in the end... although the beginning may be rough.
