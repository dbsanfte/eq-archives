Ivy Etched Armor Quest Information & Guide

 

 Below you will find detailed information regarding all the parts to the Ranger Ivy Etched Armor Quest.
 
Ivy Etched Bracers
Starts in: Kithacor Forest
Who: Gandari

You will need:

  • The bottom of the Rune of Ivy (from Orc named Redwind in Everfrost)

  • A watery ring (from a tidal lord in Dagnor's Cauldron)

  • 1 Fire Emerald

Redwind in Everfrost

Redwind is considered a Shadow Knight. Because of this, he has harm touch. For those of you that don’t know, that means 99% of the time, he will open up with a direct magic based attack (resistible, but rarely). He also likes to use the darkness series of SK spells (slows (like snare) the target, and also DoT’s them) and will fear from time to time. Redwind always drops the Rune of Ivy needed for the bracer. Using harmony on the other Orcs is a very effective move, only Redwind will come if used correctly. He can be soloed in the mid 30s without much problems (this was before rangers got SoW at 30 mind you, probably even easier now), or a group of mid to high 20s could kill him.

 

Aqua Goblin Tidal Lord in Dagnor's Cauldron

 

If you are able to kill Redwind, the Tidal Lord should be no problem. Be
careful kiting this guy as the land here is rough and a sharp fall can kill you. There is a goblin
camp outside the “hallway” to Unrest. If things go bad, stay near there, so you have a zone to
run to for safety. Also, you can almost always find a nice soul to help you that has just been
trained out of Unrest.

 

 

Ivy Etched Helm
Starts in: Kithacor Forest
Who: Gandari

You will need:

  • Top of the Rune of Ivy (from a Deathly Usher in Mistmoore)

  • Cyclops Charm (from Tarskuk in Rathe Mountains)

  • 1 Sapphire

Tarskuk in Rathe Mountains

Tarskuk is a named Cyclops. As you might have learned, all the “bigger” races run as
fast as Sow. Tarskuk is suppose to run faster then Sow. From what I understand, he dropped the necklace 3/4 times. Tarskuk was quite easy to get to spawn.
Kill all of the lizards, Skellie's, and Cyclops around the gypsy camp near the Lake Rathe zone line.

A Deathly Usher in Mistmoore

   

The Usher was found most commonly in the Jail area. There are a lot of spawns
in this area sometimes, and it can get crazy. If you have trouble getting a group, there are 2 spawns in room number 14 that you can camouflage into (from the EqAtlas site posted above), if you can manage to harmony pull or root park one, you should be able to hold the room (watch for trains) until you get the Usher. Also, MM is one of the most often trained zones. If you stay camo’ed (undead can see though camouflage, beware this) at the zone line, trash is always trained to the zone, if you watch, Ushers will almost always come with the deep trains. If someone takes out the train, the rune could drop off them, and if you ask nicely, they might let you loot (is NO DROP by the way).


Ivy Etched Tunic
Starts in: Kithacor Forest
Who: Gandari

You will need:

  • Skull of Meldrath (from Steamfont)

  • Right side of the Rune of Ivy (from Brownie named Thistle Underbrush in Lesser Faydark

  • 2 Rubies

Meldrath The Malignant in Steamfont Mountains

 

Meldrath is one of the meanest little buggers of the Ivy Etched armor. He’s a 35 Necro,
and Necros are almost always underconned. Also, Meld is almost always with 2 mino guards,
and he also has a pet. A group of mid to high 30s should be able to take Meld,
make sure and have a healer, and an enchanter is recommended.

Thistle Underbrush in Lesser Faydark

 

Thistle is a named brownie, but quite easy compared to some of the mean brownies in
the area. Many people have different thoughts to get him to spawn. Just watch your tracking. Now that there is a scroll bar with Tracking, if he is up, you will know. You can just watch from a
zone line, be careful of the brownies, and if you are traveling, or anything else, stay
camo’ed, as the brownies can’t see though camo, and you will have the upper hand. Thistle is
only 25, but if you go up against Thistle without proper readiness, you might die.
When you see him pop, (druids will quickly take him down for the cords he drops) quickly, refresh SoW and Camo, and track him...when you see him, cancel magic him until his thorns drop. This will take off his AC, HPs, SoW, and damage shield buffs. After that, save your kicks for his healing, and watch for those insane DDs.

Ivy Etched Sleeves
Starts in: Kithacor Forest
Who: Gandari

You will need:

  • Heart of fire (from an inferno goblin wizard in Solusek's Eye)

  • Left Rune of Ivy (from griffon Grimfeather in N Karanas)

  • 2 star rubies

Grimfeather in North Karana

       

Grimfeather is one of the banes of North Karana. He is a named griffon, and flies faster then (or at least close) to sow. He always drops the Rune you will need from what I have heard. Be sure to use Snare on this guy as he is extremely fast.

 

 

Inferno Goblin Wizard in Solusek's Eye (aka Sol A)

   

These wizards (and you need the inferno, not the others, and you must get the wizard,
not the others) spawn all over the southern end of Sol A. Sol A is a playground for anyone 40+. As you learn how to get around in Sol A, and such, makes it much easier.

Ivy Etched Leggings
Starts in: Kithacor Forest
Who: Gandari

You will need:

  • Quiver of Kithacor from Lieutenant Leafstalker

  • Star of Odus, which can be purchased in Erudin

Wiltin Windwalker in Ocean of Tears (Seafury Isle)

   

Wiltin is a pain to many rangers. But if you know these simple things, he’s not that bad
at all. Wiltin’s place holder is - A Buccaneer -. Now, here is the fun part. Most monster’s
place holder needs to be killed to get your spawn. Do your best to protect this guy. Every EverQuest day, at 8AM game time, he will despawn, and at 10AM, he will spawn as either another
buccaneer, or Wiltin. Wiltin is suppose to despawn shortly after spawning. If the buccaneer is killed, it takes about 2 EQ days to get anything (buccaneer or Wiltin). So if he dies, expect a longer wait. There is a safe little inlet of land that you can sit on. If you look at the camp and put the boat path behind you, and Gornit to your right, you can safely sit there for real life hours. Nothing heads there, and you can watch the spawn from there without any problems. The quiver off Wiltin is a container, so make sure you have room in your inventory for a container, not an item.

A Brownie Outcast in Lesser Faydark

   

The way to get
these to spawn is to go to the pixie tower, and slaughter the pixies, just run around killing everything in the area around the tower, and keep track up. The SECOND they spawn, get to them fast (stay fully buffed, you don’t have time to buff when you see them) start cancel magic’ing them until they lose their thorn shield. If you catch them early, they will only have one to no buffs on, and you can spend more time hitting, and less time casting. Stay near the tower doing this, and stay camo’ed as you do, so you get the upper hand, and don’t get jumped by one. You need one of each of the arrows. They all have different names. Arrow of _______. After getting all 6, put them in the quiver, and hit combine.

Ivy Etched Boots
Starts in: Kithacor Forest
Who: Gandari

"This quest is one of the most annoying of all the Ivy quests."

You will need:

  • Ronium

  • Mistmoore Granite (from gargoyles in Mistmoore)

Rathmana Allin in South Ro

    

You have to deal with faction,
and lots of little chores. Rathmana is not for killing, and judging by his level, I wouldn’t
recommend trying it any ways. Rathmana is on the faction of the Temple of Sol Ro. For the
boots you need only 3 things. Ronium, MM granite, and cash. Sounds easy?...nope. If you are
willing to sit in EC auctioning for Ronium, and don’t mind buying it, do that. A lot faster, and in
the end, a lot safer. Also, if you get the Ronium yourself, you will lower faction to possible ‘kill
on site’ to the gnomes of Ak’anon. But as much work as it took, I don’t think I would part with
a piece of Ronium for less then 250+ platinum. Maybe it was because I did most of it solo,
maybe it’s just because I never seen Ronium for sale, but they are not that fun to obtain. Rathmana is the first of the people you can use to raise faction with. Give this guy 30 GOLD until he is amiable faction. This will mean that the Temple of Sol Ro will be amiable faction as well. Then
you can move on to the next part.

Lord Searfire in Mistmoore Castle

    

Once you are amiable faction with Rathmana you will be amiable faction with Lord
Searfire. Ask him, “What about Ronium” and he will give you a NO DROP cauldron. This is
what you will need for the Ronium. It is a 6 slot container, and the 6 items needed are;
-Two Bars of Enchanted Platinum (store bought platinum bars, enchanted by a 34+ level
enchanter.)
-Soil of the Underfoot (Quest given by Priestess Ghalea in Kaladim)
-Sands of Ro (Dropped by Sand Giants)
-A bar of Melatite (Dropped by clockworks in Sol A)
-Mistmoore Granite (Drops off gargoyles in MM)

Gunlok Jure in North Kaladim

This is another NPC like Rathmana who is not part of the quest other then used for
raising faction. Giving this dwarf 4 bone chips will raise faction with the North Kaladim residents
(including Priestess Ghalea who you need). I filled a back pack of bone chips (yes, that means
100+ bone chips) to give to this dwarf. It barley did it. I suggest /auctioning to buy them off the
newbies while you sit at the chess board killing the skellys for the chips. After you fill a pack,
turn them in, 4 at a time, to Gunlok. Keep con’ing him till he is amiable, then you can move on
to the next part.

Priestess Ghalea in South Kaladim

This is a sub-quest, in this sub quest, for the Ronium, for the boots (tired yet?). When
she will talk to you, she will give you a bag with 4 slots to fill. What you need is four ‘faerie
dusts’ from the faeries that fly around in Lesser and Greater Faydark. Here is the clincher. The
faeries are also for a quest for the “Enchanted Trueshot Longbow” (Aka the Raincaller). If you
kill the faeries, you will not be able to do the Raincaller quest. Now, if you don’t care, then
slaughter the faeries. If you do, then your options are limited. You can either have a friend kill them, and you track them, pay for the dust, or do something like I did. Either way, good luck.

A Faerie Royal Guard in Greater Faydark

These are the highest level faeries of Greater Faydark, and have the largest percentage
of a chance of dropping the dust you need. (Make note that you need faerie dust, and not pixie.
Looks white instead of yellow.) Get four of these, and combine it in the pouch given to you from
Priestess Ghalea. Combine the pouch, and give it to Ghalea, and she will give you the ‘Soil of
Underfoot’. Put it in your container from Lord Searfire.

A Sand Giant in South Ro

These are the Sand Giants you have seen your entire EQ life, stomping around the deserts or
Ro. They do not drop the sand every time, but it is not rare as far as I know. They are very
resistant to fire spells, and if you can kill a HG with a group, you should be able to take a Sand Giant with the same group.

CWG/CWS Models in Ak'anon

The Melatite is not always dropped off the gnomes, but it is not extremely rare. If you
check the vender that sells the tinkering bags, you can sometimes find some that has been sold to him. The higher the letters, the better chance of it dropping the Melatite. Also, Gabbie that drops a nice cloak and the EXG model that drops a nice + wis shield are in the same area. I suggest buying the Melatite, for killing the gnomes will make you ‘kos’ in Ak’anon. The only way they aggro is attacking one directly or being ‘KOS’. As you enter the gnome area in Sol A, you will see a spawn right next to a bridge. It is one of the mid level ones, and has a chance of dropping the Melatite, without many problems. The highest models have a knock back spell, so make sure not to put your back to any lava.

A Flouting Gargoyle in Mistmoore

The MM granite is needed twice in this quest, but it is *Lore*. So I suggest getting it last.
It drops off any of the higher gargoyles in MM, and when you get one, put it in your container and
now that it is full, hit combine, then get another piece of MM granite, and you are done. I got
mine from the room just to the right of room #14 on the above EqAtlas map.

Ivy Etched Gauntlets
Starts in: Kithacor Forest
Who: Gandari

You will need:

  • Morin's Bow of Kithacor Part A from Unkempt Druids in Rathe Mountains

  • Morin's Bow of Kithacor Part B in Lower Guk from Slaythe the Slayer

Maldyn the Unkempt in Rathe Mountains

    

This guy is on a spawn table with the Jboots gnome, the high level monk, and ‘a drake’.
If any of them is up, he won’t be. They all despawn after enough time, but waiting can take up
to 80 Real Life hours. If the Jboots gnome is up, and you can kill him, do so. You lose no faction, and have a chance of spawning him. He tends to roam in the HG area, and the Hill Giants don’t like him much. If a HG sees him before you do, he will be killed, you won’t have any chance of getting
him till next go around.

Slaythe the Slayer in Lower Guk

Slaythe is a low level frog. He is
higher then 13 as it says on Illia’s Bestiary because it took me about 20 seconds of slashing
away before he fell. Also, on EqAtlas, it seems a little confusing about where he is on the map.
He is in the hallway NORTH (as in up) from the number 7 on the map. The twisty hallway
above the number 7 is the one you want.

After all that work and time... That's it! Your done and a complete set of Ivey Etched Armor richer! Congratulations!
