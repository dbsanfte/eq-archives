Angel's Ranger Guide Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

 

I get a lot of emails, asking all kinds of questions and for advice of all sorts. Unfortunately, I am not always able to respond to all the emails I get due to different reasons and such. I hate not being able to answer everyone's email in a timely fashion! I do read every single email that is sent to me, and I really do appreciate you all taking the time to send them to me. To help answer some of the most common questions and requests for advice tips I receive in emails, I have decided to add a FAQ (Frequently Asked Question) to Angel’s. This will be an on-going and often updated page, so please come back often after the updates!

I will be quoting questions right from the emails I receive and will be listing the submitters name (if provided of course), so don’t be to surprised if one you sent in is listed here… Submitted questions are in BOLD, and my responses are in Italic.

 

I'm not real good at this ranger thing I guess, and I was wondering if you could help me level?  I'm level 19 right now and hunting in Oasis.  Anything you could offer would be great.

If you look on the Ranger Strategies section of Angel’s, there are a few player written guides listed there, including one written by me quite some time ago. They are a good starting point to give you some ideas where to go to get good exp and groups.

I am currently a level 15 ranger (third of a yellow to 16).  I've gotten allot out of your site and wanted to thank you.

I've worked Fletching up to level 120 and still have failed at making the Trueshot 11 times.  I play very little, so this has been a pretty serious % of my play time.  Am I doing something wrong?  Or is it just luck of the draw?

Unfortunately, as any Ranger can tell you, its just luck of the draw. I made my first Trueshot (first attempt as well) at skill of 120 in Fletching. I then failed the next 5 times. The higher your skill in fletching, the better your chances of making the bow. All I can suggest is to keep at it, keep working your skill up, and keep collecting those components!

Also, I don't gain skill levels when fighting light blue cons, only dark blue (Kind of risky but worth a shot).  I love to solo so taking on dark blues is a bit of a risk.  Can you offer some advice for soloing that will keep me alive and still gaining in skill?  My 1HS is low enough that I'm using 2HS until I get duel weld, then maybe I can survive long enough to move it up.

At level 15, I was in the dreaded Oasis fighting Crocs of all kinds. All blue cons to me, as well as a white here and there if I remember correctly. You should be able to take blue cons with out to many problems solo, just keep in mind down time while you med up will be time some. Also I would work 1 hand slash as much as you can, and keep it maxed, as well as all 1H Weapon skills. Once you get Duel Wield, you wont be using a 2 Hander to much any more, except maybe for for skill ups after gaining a level.

 

I was wondering if you can add poison to the arrows you make.  Haven't started experimenting yet but wondering if you had thanks.

Unfortunately, Ranger's are unable to add any type of poison to arrows. With the Luclin expansion, we do have new options though. I have yet to look into the new arrows we can create yet, and am planning on doing this more once I hit 60 and start working on AA exp, endless quiver and Archery Master. Keep an eye out for updates to the Fletching Guide as I learn more!

 

I would like to know how to do the quest for the Jboots and what is a good level to do this quest.

The Journeyman Boots Quest can be done in the 40's I believe. Unfortunately, the Ancient Cyclops is probably one of the most Kill stealed NPC in the game. I have not written up a detailed quest for JBoots, but if you do a search on the web, I am sure you can find the information you seek.

First off I'd just like to complement you on this fabulous site. It is so hard to find good, useful information for Rangers since everyone gives us such a hard time for not being "real tanks". So thanks for this great site! And congratulations on 59!
