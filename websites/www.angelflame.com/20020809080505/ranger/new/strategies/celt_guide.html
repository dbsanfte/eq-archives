The Ranger, your job post 50.. Your abilities... & why you are simply irreplaceable

I found this posted on the Ranger's Glade by Celt. I thought it was great information for other Rangers out there so I added it to my Guide. Note: I did not write this.

Sifting thru the posts today on my Sunday afternoon...I came across a post about "where rangers stand" and "why pick a ranger"...ranger bashing etc...everyone saying we deal less damage than most damage melees yadda yadda....yet on my server, I have been sought after (along with my ranger comrades) for everything since Verant paid a bit of attention to our needs. Although we could use a few more hit points to warrant the taunt button, overall, the Warder has a DEFINITIVE niche in both exp groups to help level his friends, and the REAL reason for myself and many other high level rangers to play... the raid. Going to break this down in a few ranks... the pre level 60 ranger for exp groups/small raids...and the ranger at the pinnacle of his/her abilities...at level 60.

The post 50 ranger...to level 59

Rangers are one of the most versatile exp grouping classes in the game... with amazing abilities if used in the correct hands. We all know that the ranger is meant as a supplementary damage dealer, a protector of the casters...a master of the outdoors zones for finding prey, and THE BEST animal class hunter in the game. THIS is what you can bring to a group during these awkward levels.

Solid puller in outdoor zones
No class has our tracking range... the ability to find an exp giving monster in the out of doors is unmatched... with the ability to cast harmony before the pull to separate a single from a crowd, to FIND that mob half a zone away...and bring it to the group on the PERFECT vector (with the aide of tracking) is simply an amazing catalyst for a groups exp. Find a mob, pull it...get it to the group...and if they can defeat the mob without you ...GO GET ANOTHER ONE...snare the beast if it's too close and the first one isn't defeated yet...and get it to the group just as the last mob dies. This will keep the exp flowing....and in outdoors zones where blue monsters might be sparse (or overpopulated zones), this constant exp attack method will level your friends and YOU extremely fast.

Solo strength versus animals
No matter how you try to explain it...fear kiting is a frighteningly efficient means to solo. Rangers do this better than ANY CLASS versus animals (which you can find blue all the way to 60..although very sparse after 55). The ability to just buff, snare, fear, and melee a mob until said fear drops... run a bit away, re fear rinse repeat... med for 2 minutes and go again... is just frightening. Panic animal is so strong I was worried about the dewd aspect of the new rangers coming in...but for the older rangers who are part timers or sparse playtimes... this ability to go to Eastern wastes, Iceland, and Wakening lands is downright deadly. Use your ability to fear kite and you won't NEED a group to get SOME yellow bar... although you will find that a well balanced group is not only more lucrative in the exp department... but fun as well.

Duo ability with an enchanter unrivaled out of doors...fast EASY exp to 60.
Find the blue mobs with tracking...get buffed by your enchanter comrade... and laugh as you duo mobs that are difficult for most warrior/cleric combos to do in such rapid succession with invoke fear and ensnare. Enchanters make rangers GODS everywhere but the HP department. The combination of clarity II, gift of whatever for mana regen, WR, VOG, AQ, Snake for haste, tepid deeds (or shiftless/forlorn on the mobs that hit for 200 ish :P), chloro, slnature/diamond, ensnare and invoke fear ...coupled with harmony pulling, gwolf form and you are SCREAMING with attack power destroying a hapless running mob from behind. Adds are mezzed, and dealt with later...bedlam/berserker spirit keeps you from getting chapped too badly...post combat gheals or just chloro...ranger enchanter duos are unmatched... and if you find one you can bond with and play with extensively... you can kill whatever you want for exp all the way to 60 outdoors.

EXCELLENT exp group buff selection
It has been said a million times on this board... how druids sometimes don't use their spells to the fullest of their abilities... while the ranger will make sure every last member has that famous level 15 spell... feet like cat for the paltry agi/ac bonus LOL. You have buffs! USE THEM. Many folks just don't realize the added bonus of a rangers buffs at these levels....and later at 60 they allow a smaller raid to do what would require at least 5 more tanks to accomplish. Rangers should use their abilities to the fullest....damage shield the warrior! (especially if relying on your Swarmcaller to slow the beasts... or that warrior's truncheon at 4 am with no shaman/enchanter around). At 53, rangers have a fantastic strength buff. Pass it around! Get your warrior to 255 STR and that rogue over 200 STR... to maximize those big hitters/backstabs/flying kicks. Couple Strength of Nature... the most underrated lower level buff in the game. Get your group members attack rating UP... with LIQUID NITROGEN attack (pure attack... not STR based) and they will hit much more difficult monsters more effectively AND those paladins/SKS in your group will be doing MUCH more damage because *bonk* they will actually hit things that their attack might be too low to touch (ATK compared to ac of target mob). Call of Earth is a WONDERFUL ac buff... with a damage shield to boot. It's ac/mana ratio is GODLY folks....25 ac for 50 mana AND a 4 pt dam shield? This is a buff that paladins, clerics, hell ANYONE can't touch for that type of mana. This spell tears even the higher level ac buff of enchanters apart...BUFF EVERYONE including those casters...make your warrior tank better...make that rogue shrug off attacks in time to evade...make the enchanter take 24 Dmg instead of 120. CHLOROPLAST is one of THE most mana efficient spells out there to keep hit points up and running. At level 55 (and beyond../weep half elf wisdom) this spell is mana intensive... but at the very least keep that warrior buffed if he doesn't have the advantage of regrowth from someone...and you WILL be able to do much more than without. It's also wonderful to keep on yourself or damage tanks because the cleric/druid/shaman wont have to splurge a heal if your controlling your agro.

Combat prowess
Some folks will argue that the warrior is better for pure melee damage equally equipped. This will ring true for a while to some extent... but as you attain your epic weapons, there is a HUGE rift in what you can do. I'll cover epic in the Warder section later... for now this is how you can out damage EVERY warrior you ever meet. Rangers have dual wield... and post 50 its a phenomenal use of our abilities...coupled with a swarmcaller for the all powerful group friendly 50 percent slow...what a deadly ability indeed. Although on the books...rangers have the same melee power as a warrior...when you are performing your role you will find that you will ALWAYS out damage that tanking warrior if you maneuver behind your opponent (avoiding riposte/parry/dodge) and dig into the back of your opponent. Couple this with jolt and you are *always* behind the mob, and unless your warrior is an ogre (immune to bash based stuns from the front) you will just destroy said tank in overall melee damage because he gets stunned 2-3 times per mob. I have done tests with *another program* with real time combat stats that prove this all through my career. Also, if you move to the absolute limit of your range (you are well out of sword range... but can still swing and hit...if you ever use the F9 pan views you know what I mean) you can minimize the chance of the mob turning on you. Equally equipped... no warrior will touch your damage potential... and versus monks without epics, you will find your damage to eclipse theirs (unless they are just getting REAMED for lack of feign tactics to control agro) from the ability to make sure you are TEARING into your opponent from the back.

Combat spell selection....why your more valuable in the exp group than the warrior in many instances
Rangers post 50 are akin to the enchanter in crowd control of MOVING, ACTIVE MOBS. Enchanters keep the "adds" from attacking...they put them to sleep while the party engages one mob at a time. The ranger however, can totally CONTROL the mob under attack. Period. Using our spells and good tactics...that mob is HOPELESSLY under our control until it dies (unless its immune to magic /rude Verant heheh) and if said mob happens to be immune to magic but not to fire, then we are still at half power in this respect at level 55 with cinder jolt. 3 mobs come in at the chef room in Sebilis...your level 55...party ranger warrior rogue enchanter cleric shaman. Warrior engages a Krup warrior....the Krup wizard and Krup shaman also engage him...you fire a snare at the /assist on the warrior and root him afterwards. That mob is GLUED to the warrior...and you watch the enchanter. The Krup wizard resists that first dazzle... and he's on the enchanter. The cleric counters with level 5 stun to try to preserve the following mez attempt (level 5 stun=0 Dmg... so if its late it wont break mez) while you snare him... to get yourself some agro. The enchanter is interrupted and fires a PBAE stun (hey that shaman and wizzy are pissed) but stunned...the enchanter fires a dazzle on the wizard again... resist...

at this point if you were a warrior the group might be in trouble...the shaman passed out turgurs at this point but you are losing valuable mana and slowing down the pulls unless the ranger takes matters into his own hands...

The bad ranger
continues to attack the said mob from the back and watches the enchanter drop to a bub...and keeps attacking the mobs trying to get the first mob dead....it dies... and moves to the next mob direct assist on warrior. That mob attacks the chanter continuously and either the chanter dies or sucks tons of mana

The good ranger
Fires off another snare on a mob on the enchanter...then targets the OTHER mob on the enchanter and fires an ensnaring roots quickly (careful here... it breaks mez) but then snares that mob after root holds and now YOU are its target even if root breaks... guaranteed. Announce rooted and that mob is out of the picture until the enchanter can get to it...attacking mobs down to ONE on the chanter...9x out of ten your cleric will level 5 stun, chanter pbae stun uninterrupted... and its mezzed. THEN make sure to snare it AGAIN (great aggro inducer) and fire a taunt in close..>>THEN assist the warrior. If your level 57 you can also fire a greater heal on the enchanter to keep him up AND gain aggro pts. When the first mob is dead, break mez with ROOT and I'm sorry there's no way he's on the enchanter....and your casters are MEDDING instead of pissing mana into hp bars...getting ready for the next pull... instead of saying OOM i need a break :P

No other melee class contributes to 'constant pull environment' like the ranger. Period. The combination of snare, flamelick, immolate, ROOTs, and our call spells/fast nukes will assure that no matter what the circumstance....that mob is going to be upset with YOU if you so desire, and you can deal some pretty hefty damage once the pull is in "stasis" or all mobs either asleep or on the tank.

Finally...rangers bring peace of mind in the event of having to run. If you do your job and everything is snared almost immediately... if you gotta jet you will not have to deal with the original pull at the very least. We are the glue for an exp group...we control the runners... we control the agro of the awake mobs. No other melee class can root, snare, dump aggro points with a click of a mouse like a ranger....Keep your group alive.

No damage melee can double running speed, raise attack by 33, hp by 75, STR by 35, hp regen by 10 per tic, ac by 25, (if no cleric/paladin/druid cast sldiamond :P), damage shield for 16 (and later at 58, 21) for EVERYONE, and still manage to snare runners, root park, jolt off aggro at a whim (feign/evade have reuse time 3x that of jolt, albeit manaless) showcasing a multitude of abilities in one HIGH DAMAGE melee machine. Period. Make every xp group a showcase of what you can do...don't let anyone forget that you have abilities far above any pure melee when the S*** hits the fan.

The Warder
When the ranger reaches level 60, if played correctly there is no other class that compares to its damage capabilities over the long haul versus high end monsters, as long as they are affected by either magic or fire. Couple this with the irreplaceable call of the predator on raids, the huge enhancement of our epic weapons... and our unreal quest armors in Velious...and you have an absolute BEAST of an asset to any exp group or raid.

Folks say that we barely out damage warriors at level 60 with triple attack chances etc...on a raid that might well be true if said warrior isn't tanking...yet with our epic weapons...we have something that warriors don't. Innate 40 percent haste, RAW 54 attack bonus on our Swiftwind...and pure attack buffs (STR of Nature, and Call of the Predator) to enhance the abilities of the other hybrids/melees... and make a DEFINITIVE impact on warriors taunt effectiveness. (higher pure Atk=higher hit ratio versus immense armor class opponents for EVERYONE) No other class has this ability to enhance EVERYONE'S pure attack. Period. Shaman can do ONE PERSON for a tiny amount of time... Avatar's duration is pathetic, and later it will proc on your weapons. Rangers can make Paladins and Shadow knights HURT UBER MoBs with melee... and make warriors HIT often and HARD...keeping the taunt alive with proper tactics. NOBODY does that but the ranger... period.

We stay ALIVE the LONGEST of any melee class if played properly on things like the Dain, Velketor, Yelinak, Vindicator, Statue of Rallos Zek (ESPECIALLY THIS ONE heheh) etc. If it's a big ugly, hard hitting mob....rangers have the ability to simply DESTROY them unmolested until they are heaps on the ground. Rogues and Monks DO have the ability to shrug aggro with Evade and feign respectively... but overall if jolt or cinderjolt hit the mob... rangers will simply destroy them in aggro management. Feign isn't cumulative aggro management... if said monk feigns off the mob... he has to wait till someone else climbs the hate list higher than he/she is...cutting damage output. Also feign has a reuse time that's fairly high. Rogues CAN pre-evade... and it works very well. However, that rogue CAN'T evade every 2 seconds...again the ability has a re-use... and unless they turn off their attack... they WILL be slaughtered ...and what if it fails? They have to wait MUCH longer...and with 1100 hitting mobs...rogues wont last that long unless everything is perfect (or they know when to back off). Rangers on the other hand...have cumulative anti-hate that can be recast every 2 seconds (normal spell delay) to push themselves SO LOW on the hate list they aren't touched once IF the mob is properly debuffed. Also they have the ability to deal damage from afar to begin with to allow TIME for the key debuffs to land on the UBER MoBs (Tash, Malo, Breath of Ro, Ro's fiery Sundering, Ice, Malo for example) with Trueshot.

Rangers can and DO Trueshot in my guild...dealing obscene damage from afar with albeit expensive but powerful arrows dealing VERY IMPRESSIVE damage (though not rogue or monk ish) with almost no recourse whatsoever. When the Trueshot fades...we have already dealt 5-9k versus an excruciatingly HIGH LEVEL opponent (thanks to our obscene attack ...yes higher than any rogue with full raid buffs unless that rogue plays with stacking issues or has a Firefist ring from draco). No rogue will ever have greater wolf form, predator, STR of nature, plus we match their pure attack on their epic AND their epic 40 percent haste...only we can apply that to our bows and slaughter mobs. THEN we can proceed to cinder jolt until it hits 6 times (the magic number versus most Velious ubers in my guild... depending on how hard your melees hit it may be more or less) and close for melee unadulterated ..staying behind the opponent, with cumulative anti hate equivalent to minus 6 epic warrior procs on our previous damage...and then throw in a cinder jolt every 6 seconds or so. Staying behind the mob we aren't reposted on, we dig in with our huge attack...and as we watch our damage tank comrades eventually add too much hate and die, we stand stalwart dealing damage over the course of the 15 minute fight until the mob is dead or we run out of mana and finally succumb to the hate list.

I cannot remember a raid that predator /STR of nature wasn't asked for by our Paladins /SKs, and at least predator on everyone else (divine STR is nice :P). I also can't remember the last time I died with mana on a Velious ubermob. Rangers are a difficult road, they have gotten much easier as of late with the additions to our class. We have the ability to augment a raid or exp group that is tank based so greatly...and just like discovering how strong VOG is over the snake on raids for its attack...predator and STR of nature is 87 net attack when its all said and done on the meter. If played correctly you can assure that no matter exp group or raid, small or large gathering...a ranger has its place. Play well and folks will regard rangers as one of the best classes in the game. Because we ARE.
