Strategy Guide: Rainangel Skyefyre

Rainangel started out her life in Kelethin as with any other Wood Elf. She slayed the bats and wasps in and around Greater Faydark and obtained a few levels. I would suggest to anyone creating and starting any type of Wood Elf, Be it Ranger or Druid, to head over to Rivervale and the great woods of Misty Thicket. I did not do this with Rainangel, but I did do this with my Paladin Skyeangel. In Misty Thicket you can easily obtain levels up to 10 quickly with the benefits of a town nearby. (I was able to get Skyeangel into her 10th level within 2 days inside of Misty Thicket)

Update - 7/25/2001 - I have gotten many requests from other players on how to get their new Wood-Elf Rangers to Misty Thicket. Its a long and Dangerous run, so I created a page explaining how to do it. Please click HERE for the detailed information...

Level 10 - 13

After reaching level 10, and getting her new spells at level 9, Rainangel hopped the Boat toward Freeport. It was her first (as well as my own first) experience on the Boat that was full of promise. Rain stayed in East Commonlands for the next 3 levels. As a Treehugger, Bears and wolves would not attack me on site, and they actually seemed to like my presence in their woods. I leveled quite quickly here, and always found Vendors more then willing to buy. Stay clear of any Griffins that tend to roam the area, and if you get into trouble, there is usually quite a few guards that wonder the area. Be sure to use your new spells, including Snare. Snare is a spell you will use for the rest of your Ranger's life, and if I had to choose only one spell out of all the spells we get in our early life, I would give them all up to keep Snare.

Level 13 - 17

After reaching the 13th Season of Rain's life, I then headed West into Kizdean Gix's Realm of West Commonlands. My favorite scout area in these lands was the large hill directly behind the Toll Both. Thanks to a real life friend of mine, in game name of Nuriel (Dark Elf Shadow Knight) I obtained my first magic weapon in the form of a Combine Long Sword. Willow Wisps here I come!! Attack and kill anything that Con's blue to you. Be careful when attacking any Bears, as they do come to each others aid and more then one at a time can be a bit to much to handle. Good place to Med up and sit and recover after a fight, is at the Toll Both. There are two guards here that will keep you safe from most anything that would wonder your way. As you sit at the Toll Both, take a look to your left a bit, behind the tree and you will see Kizdean Gix. A very nasty Dark Elf Shadow Night that will attack you in a heartbeat. He has killed Rain at least 10 times in her early life. He also has a friend by the name of Dragoon Zytl that is a bit tougher then he is. if you see either of these two near you or on tracking, stay clear! If you do get attacked by one, Run to the East Commonlands zone as fast as you can! Every time I was attacked by Gix, He killed me. But with Dragoon, I was able to out run him 3 times and get away. Again, stay clear of any Griffins and Hill Giants that tend to roam this area. At level 15, you find you Ranger has 8 new spells available to her... Its worth the trip back to your home town to acquire the spells.

Level 17 - 20

After I made it to the 17th level of Rainangel's life - I headed to Oasis of Mar. Lots of great things here to kill and gain experience! Caimans, Crocodiles, Deep Water Caimans and Crocodiles galore!! Attack any that are blue to you, and be on the look out for Lockjaw, as he is a tuff (and large) named Crocodile that will (unlike his weaker kin) attack you on site. I stayed near the waters edge the entire time I stayed in this zone. You think Hill Giants were bad enough in the Commonlands? In the Oasis, you now have Sand Giants, Specters and other nasty high level mobs. The experience here is great, and the dangers much greater. If you find your self in trouble here, there are no Guards, all you can do is run towards a zone line for safety.

Yea!! Level 20 !! Now what last name should I chose for my Ranger???

Level 20 - 25

This was the time I started to group, but mostly I grouped with my RL Boyfriend, Xthisu (Iksar Necromancer) and Friend, Nuriel (Dark Elf Shadow Knight). We headed to The Overthere in Kunark. I know allot of people at this time head to Lake of Ill Oman, but The Overthere was sooooo much less crowded and the experience was quite awesome! At these levels, you will want to stay near the water, and you can get bound on the other side of "The Wall". (Just swim around to the other side, and stay AWAY from the dock. you are KOS there. We killed Kunark Rhino's, Succulents, Saber Tooth Tigers & Tigresses, and of course Sarnaks!! The loot here was great, and there is an Iksar Vendor at the Mud Puddle that will buy your findings. Stay clear of the Sarnak Fort, as there are quite a few Tombstone Reds inside it. I kept Rain here for 5 levels. Once she reached 25 - It was Revenge/Pay Back time for her.

Remember how I told you how Kizdean Gix had killed Rain at least 10 times? Well it was pay back time for Gix! Gix was going down no matter what! Nuriel wouldn't have anything to do with it (he is after all, a Dark Elf) and went to Lake of Ill Omen while I got my revenge with the help of Xthisu. It didn't take long at all, and Gix con'd blue to me at 25. He did Harm Touch/Life Tap me for 192 points of damage, but with Xthisu there with his nasty DoTs and DD attack, Gix was a rather easy kill. Needless to say, we killed him I think 5 times that day   :)   Ahhh Sweat Revenge...

Level 25 - 29

After a bit of convincing from Nuriel, Xthisu and Rain headed over to Lake of Ill Omen... I took the shuttles from Butcher Block to the big boat that would take me to Firiona Vie. (Xthisu had to take the other route as Iksars are unwelcome in Firiona Vie) It was a long ride but I did finally make it there. I got a bind in front of the bank, Camo'ed myself and was on my way to LOIO. You have to run through some nasty spiders, so your best bet is to Camo yourself and run run run following the stream to the zone line. Once we met up in LOIO, we headed to the Wind Mill. Here we camped a hill just away from the Wind Mill. We killed anything that coned blue to us, Goblins, Iksars, Sarnaks and more Saber Tooth Kitties. We also camped the back entrance to the Goblin cave that connect to Warsliks Woods for 2 - 3 levels. Again the loot is good, but its a long run (and dangerous one at that) to Firiona Vie.

We also visited South Karana during these levels, at the Aviak Village. The Loot was great here as well, plus at this level I now had my Harmony Spell which made pulling single Aviak Advocates extremely easy. Great Experience here!! Worth a visit!

Level 29 - 31

We decided to try out the now infamous Giants in Frontier Mountains at this time. I had just hit my first of many "Hell Levels" and Xthisu was 29. We again met up with Nuriel and off we went. It is in this zone that I first met Reztips Theta of Lords of War. We grouped and camped a hill in front of the Giant Fortress. The action was great, the Experience was awesome and pretty good loot as well. (They drop Forest Loops, Weighted Axes, Leg Choppers, among others) After one good night of grouping, I was asked to join Lords of War, and I accepted. I managed to make it out of Hell Level in FM. I never did get lucky enough to win a roll on a Forest Loop, but I had a great time! At level 30, Rangers finally get that long awaited spell, Spirit of the Wolf! from this point on, you will never be slow again!

Level 31-34

For levels 31 - 34, I soloed again, in LOIO. Right behind the zone line to Frontier Mountains is a great place to both group and solo. Sarnaks, Goblin Warlords, Charbone & Warbone Skelies, Iksar Pariahs and not to mention the Kitties were great experience soloing. I loved this area, as it was never real crowded here, there was a zone line in case of trouble, and the loot was really nice as well. This area took Rain all the way up to level 34 easily. I also made my first life costing mistake in this area. I was fighting a Goblin Warlord and all was going as planned when another goblin Warlord joined the fight. I knew I couldn't take them both as I had 1 yellow bub of health left, so I targeted myself, casted a few heals on myself and started heading for the zone line. On the way there, I thought I could slow the second Goblin down by Snaring him (as he was hitting me really hard in the back and threatening to kill me before I reached the zone line and I had all ready snared the first one) so what did I do? I started casting Snare... As I casted it, I noticed I didn't have the Goblin targeted, I had myself targeted! Let me tell you how slow you move when you have less the a yellow bub of health and have been Snared!! Needless to say, I died and lost over a yellow bub of experience! *sigh* good thing I was bound in Firiona Vie!

Level 34 - 35

Xthisu and I headed back to FM and the Giant Fort. How could we resist the chance to get a Forest Loop while getting good exp? I still never did get one. This time though, we did not group with others. We were taking them down easily with my Snare and his Fear. I made it to my next hell Level of 35 here.

Level 35 - 37

After hearing about the new Scars of Velious, It was time for Xthisu and I to go check it out (we were over 30 after all). We took the Pirate's Skiff from North Ro and crossed the little Isle to catch the Ice Breaker Boat to Velious. It wasn't to long of a ride, and we found ourselves on a dock of a large island of Ice and Snow. Here we hunted Snow Cougars and rested at the "Igloo's" for the next 2 levels. Rangers can Solo here to a point, Rangers at level 22 get a new spell of Panic Animal that acts the same as "Fear" on animals only. I tried this tactic on the Snow Cougars with some success. The only times I had problems is when the Cougar would agro other Cougars onto me. There is no where to "Zone" and escape here and its a long run to the "Ice Bridge". I died 2 times attempting this tactic. I will say before I died the first time, I was gaining half a blue bub EXP per Cougar Kill. If you can locate an area to snare/fear Cougars without Aggroing others, Id say go for it! The Cougars also drop the Famous Cougar Claw Earrings (Great item for a Ranger). This is a nice place to group, the loot is just about nil, but great EXP.

Level 37 - 39

The Cougars were still giving good EXP, but we had become bored with no loot and the same thing every time. So Xthisu and I headed out to the Great Divide. (We needed a bank run anyway). I SoWed and camo'ed us and we were off. Its a very long run. Once we got there we headed to the bank and restocked on Food and Water as well as Arrows for myself (Bind!!). We then headed to the Tizmaks in Great Divide. These are great EXP, for a group and quite easy kills. They carry no money, but do drop Velium Weapons, as well as Tizmak Armor and Rare drops of Cured Tizmak Tunics and even more Rare is Cured Tizmak Gloves. The weapons sell for about 4 PP each in Thurgadin and the regular armor for about 2 PP each. The Cured Armor Id hold onto and sell to Players in East Commonlands. I sold a pair of the Cured Tizmak Gloves for 500 PP   :)

You also have a chance to kill Yakka Razerhoof here (A really large named Tizmak), and he drops the Velium Stone mace that is worth about 500 PP.

I am now level 39, and still quite a few of the Tizmaks con blue to me. I will continue to fight here in the Great Divide for the time being.

Anytime I pass through West Commonlands now, I have to stop and visit good 'ol Gix. Did you know if you Hail him first before attacking him, he will NOT cast Harm Touch/Life Tap on you? I think I have killed him (just cause I can now) at least 20 times   :)

Level 40-45 *Updated 8/14/2001*

For the past few levels, I have been spending my time mostly in Eastern Wastes at the Ry'gorr Fort. I have been able to gain quite a few levels, and quite a collection of nice Ry'gorr Armor. I have also been able to complete the Kromrif Military Leggings!

I have also been working on my faction as well as getting good experience by killing Giants and Drakkel Dire Wolves in the Great Divide. I recently obtained the 45th season!! I have started to work on my Epic Quest... and will continue to post more as I get further into it. The picture of Rain on the left is how she now looks in her Kromrif Military Leggings, and various pieces of Ry'gorr Armor.

Level 46-50 *Updated 9/6/2001*

For the last 4 levels, I have spent most of my time in Velious (Great Divide & Eastern Wastes) Xthisu & I group near the east wall in Great Divide (Right next to the zone line to EW) and fight Giant Scouts. They are good EXP, and drop OK loot... We can usually make about 200 PP an hour here killing Giants. Not to mention the Faction they give with the Coldain. *Smiles* Xthisu and I are now Ally with the Coldains and are continuing to work on our Claws of Veshan Faction.

Xthisu and I decided to take Amanar advice about Icefang in Eastern Wastes, and well he was correct about that place!! I can solo Icefang alone, and he gives me about half a blue bub EXP for each time I kill him!! Great Experience!! Not to mention all the blue Walruses in the area. This is a great place for a Ranger to solo through those hell levels of 45 & 50. While I'm grouped with Xthisu we pull anything and everything that cons blue to us. Xthisu is a Necro, and can fear any creature or animal. We kill Giants, Manticore, Walrus, Icefang, Snow Griffins (Got quite a few eggs now too!) Anything we can... Last night 9/5/2001 I dinged Level 50 and am planning to get to my first of many hell levels, 51 by the end of this Weekend...

Level 50-60 *Updated 5/10/2002*

It has been quite some time since I have updated my guide here. I am now level 60, and I continue to work my Alt Advancement when not raiding with Eternal Advocates.

During my lower 50's, I decided it was time to move into more end-game, and after much, hard and long thought, I deguilded from Epic Heroes.

I went Guildless for about a month or so, during which time I got to know Eternal Advocates more, as well as join in some raids with them. I later became a member of Eternal Advocates, and they have become my family and my friends during these past months. /Hugs EA HARD!!

During the lower 50's, I continued to Fear Kite Icefang, Walruses and any animal that came across my path there. I stayed at this spot until about level 53ish.

That was when Crescention and I started heading to places like the Gray in Luclin. SoL had come out, and we were still deep exploring the new lands that had opened. We stayed here in the Gray for a few levels, fighting the Shissar that pop in front of the Temple of Ssra, as well as the Golems. The Experience was good, the loot pretty nice and it was fun for us. Just keep in mind if you decide to visit the Gray, that is is considered a "Vacuum" and you will need some type of enduring breath item.

Wow level 54... Yes this was the worst Hell Level up to that point. It was slow and monotonous. We grinded 54 in the Gray, and in Scarlet desert. In Scarlet Desert, there is a plateau, and on top, are two nice camps. Sun Revenants and Aliens. Rain stayed with the Sun Revenants for many levels with her Cleric, Eliania at her side. Great experience, not so great loot, and it makes the time go by.

I ended up grinding the worst of all Hell Levels here at this camp, Eliania healing and Rain slashing away. And yes, 59 was the worst, most horridly slow and painful level I have endured. But in the end, it was worth it:

I dinged level 60 in Charasis, Howling Stones. I had just under 1 yellow to go, and Cres and I went to HS, and stayed until I dinged. It was non-stop exp grinding, and it took about 8 hours to do it.

HS is a great place to go to for exp and loot. My favorite wing is south, it just has so much to pull from.

~Rainangel Skyefyre, Warder of Eternal Advocates on Vazaelle
