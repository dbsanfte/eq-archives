EQ, A Bit of Fun
It is a strange community indeed, where complaining is the norm.  Cries of "I've been nerfed" and "suchandsuch's class is too powerful" echo endlessly through the message forums and the newsgroups.

Main Entry: 1game
Pronunciation: 'gAm
Function: noun
Etymology: Middle English, from Old English gamen; akin to Old High German gaman amusement
Date: before 12th century
1 a (1) : activity engaged in for diversion or amusement

I suppose it could be argued that complaining endlessly is in fact a diversion, but why oh why would anyone choose to do it?   Everquest is a game.   It is meant to be a form of amusement.    Why is it then, that a portion of those who are playing this game feel the need to complain endlessly about it?  Would it not make more sense to just stop playing and enjoy a different game or another passtime?

The malcontents respond to my questioning with the same drivel over and over;

1) We pay our hard-earned dollars and we demand that we get what we want.

Last I checked nobody was under duress.   Accounts can be cancelled.  Other games can be purchased.  $9.89 is a bit of joke for a month of entertainment.   A movie costs that much and only lasts for two hours.   I would hazard a guess that many of the complainers are spending 90 or more hours a month playing EverQuest.   They then spend at least 180 hours per month bitching about it in the newsgroups.  11 cents an hour for entertainment!  You can certainly demand a lot when you are paying top dollar like that.  Do these same people ask for their $12 back when they don't like a movie?   Ah, I didn't think so.

2) Verant is making big dollaros dewd so they owe me.

Umm, yes, and welcome to the free world.    They owe you nothing.  You vote for their product by paying the monthly fee.  If enough people stopped paying,  the game would change or disappear.    Apparently you enjoy the game enough to pay $9.89 a month.   If you don't, and you are still paying, then what does that say about you?

3) Brad McQuaid is just trying to screw me.

Yes he is.  I'm positive that he has singled you out and is making changes solely to increase your level of unhappiness.    You are not really paranoid if they are truly out to get you.

4) I spent a long time, invested it you could say, in building a character only to have the rules changed out from under me.

Possibly the most rare retort.  It is almost an endangered species.  In some cases it is a legitimate complaint.  In others, it is plain and simple rubbish.

Some changes have been a bit drastic, but they were almost all made for the same reason, people were exploiting the rules of the game.    Some classes could kill MOBs without risking their own lives.  Because of this exploitation of the rules 'kiting' was made more difficult.  The way in which it was 'fixed' is up for debate, but the reason is legitimate.   Another class had a skill that allowed it to pull MOBs one at a time without risk to itself or its group.   I cannot imagine that the game was intended to be played this way.   Verant made a change that deterred players from being able to do this and an entire class cried foul.

The latest snivelling and babbling revolves around the Guise of the Deceiver.   It was broken and any race/class could use it.    Now that only the proper races/classes can use it there is a big uproar.  

Thank goodness I don't have to deal with that kind of crap.   Brad and the other Verant PR folks should be given medals for patience.

The screams and sqawks of 'we can voice our opinion' are already reaching my ears.  

You know what?  They are right, and I just voiced mine.

Think I'm wrong?  Know I'm right?  Why not fire up a discussion in our message forums?
