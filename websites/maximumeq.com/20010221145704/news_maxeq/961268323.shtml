Additional spell changes
by Chris - 06/07/00 12:50 EST
Abashi posted these additional changes that didn't make it into the patch message:

This morning I was handed a list of spell changes that didn't make it into the patch message. I'll put up an addendum later today, but here's the list for review:
* Banshee Aura has had its duration increased.
* Scale of Wolf and Spirit of Scale will no longer be canceled by being the target of a melee attack. Ranged attacks and spells will still cancel the spell.
* Shaman can now cast Charm Animal
* Some enchanter and shaman spells have had their casting times/mana costs altered.
* Augment, Glamour of Kintaz, Rapture, and Clarity II have had their mana cost lowered.
* Root spells that cause damage (Druid roots, Hungry Earth) now work properly.

Locked door exploit/work around
by Chris - 06/07/00 12:40 EST
There is another way to open locked doors, by using AoE spells. However, as my guild found out, this can meet with dire consequences :).

There are currently three ways to open a locked door without lockpicking it:
1) Sense dead (for dead mob) or sense summoned (for a pet), then send a pet in. Pet will teleport through door without opening it, but when it aggros mob inside, the mob will run back and will open the door.

2) Eye of Zomm/Stalking Probes. If you summon an eye while standing directly next to door and facing it, the eye will appear on other side. Then you fly him in and aggro a mob who kindly opens a door for you.

3) Fear. Not always possible due to pathing, but in some cases, you can cast fear on a mob, and he will run into door and open it.

What is the position of GMs on it? Is it allowed? Or is it a bannable offense?

Gholam Stickyfingers
Level 51 Rogue, E'ci
Sacrament of Chaos
 It's not considered illegal at present, but more of a design consequence. In any case, I don't think that it is as reliable as having a rogue there to open the door for you.

We've been considering a few different options to eliminate this issue. One would be for all NPCs to walk through doors without opening them, like it was in beta. This looks bad though. We've got a couple other ideas that we are thinking about as well.

Does anyone feel that this is an issue that needs attention? In other words, do you feel that these are reliable enough options as to decrease the importance of rogues?

-Gordon
