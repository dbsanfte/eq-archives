Monk's hand damage
by Chris - 06/01/00 13:03 EST
A lot of people have been concerned that a bare-fisted monk is going to be worthless, with all the monk weapons showing up now.

>>>All we want to know is the damage and delay of our fists from 50-60 so that we know if we can even come close to doing our best damage with our bare fists- not an unreasonable request, I must say, considering the fact that all other melee types get to knw their damage and delay with any given weapon.
 Ok, I guess I can buy that. I think that it would be fair to say that assuming that a monk has maxed out all related skills, that an armed monk, wielding looted weapons appropriate for their level will generally be better off than an unarmed monk, but not to the extent that an unarmed monk is "useless". Human monks hands will cap at 14/28 at 60th level, with Iksar monks being slightly slower.

-Gordon

Monday, May 29th, 2000

Ask Milo updated
by Chris - 05/29/00 14:56 EST
Ask Milo was updated today.

We've updated the Ask Milo column here on Everlore. Just in case you're wondering who Milo is, he's the 3D Character artist at Verant. Most of those well designed characters we're brought into 3D by this man, and now he let's us bug him with questions which we post here on a weekly basis.

Anyway, this week Milo covers the latest barrage of questions ranging from which RPG's the EQ team used to play, to why Halas Barbarians run like they're constipated. Funny and informative as always, here's a snip from the page.

Just wondering if you or the other programmers were RPG players before you went to work for EQ? I have notice the Mobs look allot the RPG pictures given in monster manuals for almost all RPGs. Is this due to those visual conceptions of those original artist or your bosses lack of creativity? Or are you just constrained to the RPG players conceptions of the mobs in their minds? Do you get to free-lance any of the graphics for mobs? The armors and such have some great details and originality; so I assume you guys are the creative wizards for EQ.
