Customers Versus Verant
The following reader submitted letter was sent to me in response to the article posted here.  My comments are in italics.   I have not corrected spelling or grammar.

Graeme's Comments

I figured I'd add my $.02 to your email bag.  Take it for what it's worth... :-)

I've been playing EQ nearly since inception.  It represents, to date, the best attempt to implement the multi-player CRPG vision most of us share... a shared world we can adventure together in.  As a veteran consumer of CRPGs and offline RPGs, I have opinions as strong as the next guy regarding what will make a great game.  As someone who works in this industry and has delivered a LOT of games to market, I also understand Verant's side of the coin fairly well.

So what issues are up that are being to hotly debated?  I think they boil down to these:

1)      Overcrowding... both from a zone standpoint and a server standpoint (they are related obviously, but have different direct effects if a similar overall one)

2)      Poor roleplaying / quest support in general

3)      Class/race balance issues

4)      Hackers/cheaters/"bad people" of varying forms (from show EQ folks to the jerks that discover things like mesmerize kill stealing (now fixed))

5)      GM/Guide program and Verant's PR/Customer Service (or lack thereof to the minds of some)

6)      Verant's decision making on what to fix and when (ie., the current SB Live issue)

In your note, you chose to specifically deal with #1, leaving the rest to be debated later (though you touched on #2 and #4 as well).  I'll restrict my comments to #1 as well, and look forward to adding my $.02 again later for the others :-)

The Issue - Overcrowding

As I mentioned above and you clearly defined in your note, there are two issues here...

*       Number of people on a server contributes to lag, crashes, and other related problems. 

*       Number of people in a zone contributes to wasted time traveling to an area to hunt only to find that everything is dead or being killed... and to other anti-social behavior like kill-stealing and arguing over the ethics of camping.  This is affected both by poor breakdown of levels in a zone, and how many zones are available for each level group.

[Ed: Another point, each EverQuest world is made up of multiple servers.  Each server looks after a few zones.  For instance, a crash caused by over-crowding in Oasis can affect players in other zones.]

Let's discuss each in turn.

Number of people on a server

Once the number of people on a server reaches a certain point, server stability begins to become problematic.  I have seen this on several crowded servers (I have characters on Fennin Ro, The Rathe, and Cazic Thule) and the point of breakdown seems to be above 2100-2200 people, depending on the server and other intangibles (random chance, the weather, who knows...). The recent letter posted to MaximumEQ from a Fennin Ro wizard indicates their server was quite unstable above 2200. 

So how to we fix the number of people on a server problem?  Several options have already been discussed...

a)      Cap the maximum number on a server, disallowing logins past this point.

b)      Split the servers, allowing players to choose to go to the new server or remain on the old.

c)      Allow players to move their characters from server to server.

d)      Restrict new accounts from joining "veteran" servers.

e)      Add new servers in greater quantities.

A few more MAY exist but haven't really been discussed as much...

f)      Increase server capacity by redesigning the server architecture

g)      Try to increase bandwidth to help deal with the results of overcrowding

So, can any of the above work?  The first option (a) seems unreasonable... you only anger and alienate your audience more.  It cannot be considered a reasonable option. 

What about the server split (option (b))?  Will it help? Sure, but only temporarily.  It will bring down the server population quickly... but that will vanish in a flash.  How do we know this?  Witness the recent server addition.  Within hours it was over 2000 players (just think of how fun it would be trying to find a SINGLE large rat to kill).

[Ed: The fact that 2,000 people found it more enjoyable to do this, rather than to play on their primary server should tell us all something.]

What about moving characters from server to server (option (c))?  This doesn't remove overcrowding unless the server load is highly imbalanced. This is not the case, so this won't help.  It will, however, create a whole new logistical nightmare.  This option is out, at least for solving this problem. 

What about restricting new accounts from joining older servers (option (d))?  This would help eventually, as people leave the game (for whatever reason), eventually server populations would dwindle.  However, this is unlikely to give you a short term solution, and will definitely anger some elements of the player base as they can no longer get their friend to join their server. 

Finally, option (e)... adding new servers in greater quantities.  Let's think about it... when they added one server, in an hour there were 2000 there.  Were the other server loads lightened?  Yep, most definitely... by a small but notable margin.  If there were 30 more servers tomorrow, I virtually guarantee our problems would be solved for a long time.  Let's mark this as a solution.

[Ed: This would solve the problem, but at what cost?   Each server must have a trained staff of GMs and Guides.  The other downside to this suggestion is that eventually the game is going to die.  What do you do with 50 servers of 200 people each?  I guess you could combine them. =).  The addition of new servers is, I believe, the best solution available.  Unfortunately, I cannot guess at the cost.]

So what of the other items?  Can they redesign the server architecture?. Could they initiate a redesign and then, once ready, implement it?  You bet. Would it cost?  You bet.  This makes it an unlikely event, even it if could well work.  They simply didn't realize the scope of the game when they designed their current architecture... and now that they DO understand, the could likely do a much better job.  I'd mark this as a definite possibility, but a highly unlikely one.  As for increasing bandwidth, I know nothing of their current pipe.  However, I am willing to assume that they are connected as well as they can be... with as big a pipe as is reasonable.  They have so stated, so either we believe them or we don't.  I choose to believe them... so this is a non-fix.

So our two choices that have any promise at all are:

*       Add new servers in greater quantities (as soon as possible)

*       Examine a server architecture redesign for a later implementation of the game

I would add to this my own suggestion... an alternate form of server split What if there was another "plane" you could transfer to that had ONLY level 50+ stuff in it?  New cities, new zones, new everything... effectively a new game.  Instead of continuing the situation where you have 50s and 1s running around the same city (and twinking, and all that goes with it), what if instead you made the build path actually DESIGNED to transfer you from server to server?  When you reach "demi-god" status at level 50 (or 55, or whatever), you then have the option of transferring to "high level land". You can't go back... it is a world just for you.  Think of it as if the planes were separated from the world of Norrath... and the servers are split so that if you're a Plane guy, you're no longer a Norrath guy.  Is that so bad? 

[Ed: I personally do not mind this solution, but it would destroy guilds as we know them.  At some point, guilds would have players in both worlds.  I'm not guilded so it doesn't affect me, but it would affect many players.]

The only short-term solution is, in my opinion, new servers.  The split carries problems, but could be combined with the new server idea... but regardless, the issue really comes down to MANY servers FAST.  If Verant adds two new servers a month, they will not be able to keep up.  They need to bring a dozen new servers online IMMEDIATELY, and then add two a month from there.

[Ed: I agree.  I also do not expect it to happen.]

Number of People in a zone

This is the other half of the coin.  Even if you DO allow Fennin to split... even if you DO have only 1500 on a server... if they have a high percentage of level 40+, there is still an issue.  This comes from the breakdown of levels... of what areas are useful and what areas are not.  A quick analysis of the game shows why this is:

Levels 1-5 - Newbies... zones aplenty, no shortage of stuff to do... and you level through this so fast it isn't an issue.

Levels 6-15 - Still newbies, just now hunting in the zones just outside newbie zone.  Crushbone, Lfay, Steamfont, West karana, Feerott, Innothule, Kerra Ridge, and many more...

Levels 16-25 - The teen years.  Still many options... both zones (Karanas, R. Mtns, Cauldron, High Keep, etc) and dungeons (Unrest, Befallen, Permafrost, Upper Guk, etc.)

Levels 26-35 - The slow growth years.  Options are beginning to thin... Karanas still work... as do R. Mtns... Permafrost... Unrest... Guk... Solusek A... but you will begin to feel the pinch if the distribution of population is even.

Levels 36+ -- The big folk.  Your non-planar options?  Lower Guk.  Solusek B.  Kedge Keep.  Hunting Giants/Griffons.  Hunting Seafuries.  Hunting Spectres.  <pause for effect>  Not much else... which is why many turn to murdering guards or NPCs.

Now, if we assume that players are distributed pretty evenly by levels (ie., if 2000 players, you have roughly 40 of each level on the server), then you quickly realize you're in DEEP weeds with the above distribution.  Newbies can hunt easily, without any fear of not having enough to do.  High levels must sometimes search the globe for a place to hunt.  Could they be questing instead?  You bet... if the quests weren't largely useless (ie., delivering results not worth the effort).

The other issue is regards to the spawn rarity issue.  Verant's concept that you need to kill a basilisk to get a Hill Giant to spawn (rathe mountains) is dangerous.  It creates a situation where high levels will kill low level stuff (sometimes taking those foes away from low levels) in order to get what they need.  This is a poor design decision.  It also means that though there ARE high level foes in nearly every zone, most are "rare spawns", which means you must basically destroy the zone in order to get it to appear. 

Solutions?  First, retune the least-used zones and/or add new zones in order to restore balance to the overall distribution.  As you suggested in your letter, this would have a direct and significant impact on the game.  It should be noted that retuning does NOT necessarily mean changing the level, though it could.  Simple retuning of loot would be sufficient in many cases. Why does no one every go to Kerra Ridge?  Because in addition to the high danger level for any who do get experience here, the loot is awful.  This is true of many zones in the game, and if addressed would be a good step towards fixing this problem.  The second fix would be to repair the rare spawn problem.  Make placeholders LOGICAL and identifiable (even if you require work to figure them out).  Killing basilisks to make Hill Giants is not a good choice.  Why not Cyclopses instead?  At least connect the creature types.  In some cases this is done... in others, it is not.  Try to make the Ancient Cyclops spawn in South Ro sometime, you'll see what I mean. Finally, I would try to add more high-level foes (that either start out non-agro, or are localized to an area) within places they aren't currently. A camp of POWERFUL gnolls in High Keep (that don't wander).  A new type of super-beetle in North Karana.  Liches or Mummies in the center of Necro Isle on Kerra Ridge.  A wider distribution of foes in Lesser Feydark.  I'm just pulling items off the top of my head here, but the concept is the key.  Make most (not all) zones have something for nearly everyone.

 Summary

I realize this is a thorny problem... but it IS a soluble one.  It is my considered opinion that if they add a dozen servers RIGHT NOW, and then keep adding them in turn, they will largely solve this issue.  Polishing the zones and adding more for high levels to do will even the distribution and give a more balanced game.  I believe Verant will do much to improve their PR stance if they add a slew of servers immediately.  I know they have a high burn-rate (outlay of cash per month for overhead and development), but I also know that their sales and their constant cashflow of $2 million/month goes a long way.  They are HIGHLY profitable, and spending the money right now to improve their crowding problem will keep many players from jumping ship out of frustration.

[Ed: Again, I agree that more servers added *yesterday* are the answer.  If it were easy to do I assume they would have done it by now.  My opinion is that if at least eight servers are not added in the next 4 weeks the game will eventually be crushed under its own success.  The silver lining for Verant is that there are no real competitors to jump ship to.]
