New Skills for 50+
by Chris - 04/21/00 07:00 EST
Gordon posted a sampling of what the melee types can expect to see in the expansion by way of skills.

Also, if you want to take a peek at the new manual that is being shipped, it's available on the Official Everquest site (www.everquest.com).

Seeing as how I haven't had much time to spend on the boards today, I thought I'd stop by and let you know that I am indeed working on things that are important to the customers.
As we've mentioned before, the retail box for EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark will include a new printed manual. Though this will be nice for those just starting out, it doesn't do much for the existing customers.

You might be happy to hear that we will be placing the new manual on the EverQuest website, in PDF format, for you to download should you wish to do so. In addition, I'm currently writing a supplement to the manual that will have a few corrections, plus new information on some melee-specific abilities in the expansion. Though I'm not going to post everything here right now, I thought you might like a taste.

Here's a description of one of the many melee disciplines included in the post-50 ability-set for pure melee characters:

KINESTHETICS (Level 57): When using this discipline, all rolls for double attacks and dual wields will be successful, giving the rogue 4 attacks per round, guaranteed. This discipline lasts for 18 seconds, and has a base reuse time of 30 minutes. The reuse time for this discipline will decrease as the rogue gains additional levels.

Of course some of that data doesn't make sense out of context, but it might give you an idea as to what we've been working on.

In the spirit of the additional information released by GZ concerning spells, here's a taste of some of the things that melee types can look forward to:

Warriors: Kicks with integral stuns!
Rogues: Full speed (think RUN) sneaking!
Monks: Regen HPs as if you were sitting, but while feigned!

There's more, and to be honest those aren't the best things we've done, but we're not prepared to come clean on it all just yet.

Anyway, I'm back to writing. Everything is clicking around here and we're right on track for bringing the Kunark Zones online. Shipping is looking good, and we'll announce more information as we have it.


Thanks!

Gordon

Clarification on 2handed weapon changes
by Chris - 04/20/00 05:46 EST
This change has rangers up in arms over yet something else now. Abashi tries to explain why 2 handed weapons weren't working the way they should all along:

I was apprehensive to announce the change due to the complaints that might come from it. Just to be clear, damage done by 1H weapons have been correct all of this time. Damage done by 2H weapons was not. Here's a complete explanation of the problem:
Due to the problem, a 10/20 weapon was superior to a 20/40 weapon, even though they both have the same ratio. The reason for this is a damage scalar that is added to each contact at higher levels is added to the faster weapons more than it is added to a slower weapon, because they have the chance to contact more.

(note: These numbers are arbitrary)

For instance, suppose that the minimum damage that someone can do with the weapon in their primary hand is 11 points. This represents a 1 point hit, plus a 10 point scalar. That extra 10 points is added to every hit you make, regardless what weapon you use.

So, given two weapons have the same ratio, they should average the same amount of damage in a given time. However, since the faster weapon (based upon the stats above) swings twice as fast, it gets that extra damage more often.

-Gordon
