This is part of a series of questions that originated on the Gameplay forum on the Official EverQuest web site directed at Brad McQuaid on Sunday April 2, 2000. Brad offered to try and answer as many of them as he could.

What is the high-level view of the current state of the player run economy: specifically trade skills and their usefulness, and the affects of twinking on the economy?

**Trade skills, by their nature, need to be updated periodically in a deflationary player run economy (and it is our intent to do those updates). Twinking is also part of the economy and it accelerates the deflation, but can still be controlled by adding more to the game (better weapons, harder monsters, etc.). It can also be controlled to a point with no-drop items.

What are the current efforts to deal with bug reporting? I think a bug tracking system with a web front end would go a long way to easing some tension in the player community. Flaming on the message boards is out of hand because it is perceived that this is the best conduit for feedback. I would suggest a bug tracking system that deals with technical *and* gameplay bugs. (for an example of a similar system see:
http://bugreport.apple.com)

**The combination of the /bug feature as well as in-game player feedback and feedback from message boards has proved to be very effective in terms of us finding out about bugs. It's certainly more of a challenge to isolate and get them all fixed than it is often to identify them.

If you have been involved in the rogue tuning meetings, can you comment on poison being added as a one time proc per poison vial (or whatever form it is going to take) in DD or DoT form, or something completely different?

**I cannot yet announce the inner-workings of our poison upgrade because we're not done with it.

Why do paladins get a quality non planar 1hs weapon (ghoulbane) mid to low level, and sk's do not? Not enough people playing sk's to waste the time creating the item? I have heard that a 1hs is going to be put in for sk's that is of similar quality to gb any eta on that?

**I'm not going to address that specifically, but rather just say that all sorts of class specific weapons are on their way into the game, especially via the Expansion.

About EverQuest the game. If you had to do it all over again and start with a clean sheet of paper knowing what you know now, what would you do differently?

**Wow. No way I could answer that in just a few sentences, sorry. I'll say this - the game is certainly not without its flaws and we plan on making EQ better and better in the future, whether it be in the form of expansions or sequels.

I've heard a rumor about a dynamic quest team being implemented to only run dynamic quests.
Any truth and if so what type of time frame do you see them starting?

**We already have a team similar to what you describe, and we plan on adding more people to that team. We definitely want to see more dynamic quests and events and know our players do to.

Why are player concerns on Wizards dismissed out of hand? (too long to go into detail but there are posts 300+ messages long)

**They're not - we've posted several times in regards to those issues, and even announced that GZ would run some tests after he was done with the Expansion spell implementation and testing.

How do you expect Rogues to be a viable class in EQ when they come with such a horrible group unfriendly skills like PP?

**Loaded question. They are already a viable class in our opinion. As for PP, Rogues are Rogues… they're thieves, they're assassins… earning trust from other players as a Rogue is supposed to be more challenging.

Is a "second loot pool" feature for rogue's PP a viable idea in the future?

**No, no plans to do that.

Is it a bug or a feature that hybrids get less mana per wis/int from wis/int items than they do from their base wis/int?

**Don't know - I'll ask Abashi to look into it.

Also, why is it that hybrids get less mana than stated from mana items? ( i.e., 0-10 mana from a +25 mana item)

**See Above.

What do you think of the idea of allowing Shaman to forage for the herbs used in alchemy instead of purchasing them from a vendor? Would be a more realistic idea from a role-play standpoint as I don't think any self respecting Shaman ever bought their herbs at the local quickie mart.......

**Kind of a neat idea - we'll toss it around.

Are there any plans to make more "crafted" items from the trade skills needed to complete quests?
Much like banded is already required for totemic armor, could there be a quest out there that needs a forged weapon?

**Yes, but no short term plans.

Druid quests... can we get more? We kind of get tired of doing class armor quests for the other classes (two edged question there sorry)

**I think you'll see more and more class specific quests in general in the future.

Will hate and fear planes ever be changed to something more along the lines of air plane or killing the fire giants to allow high level groups to progress a certain amount based on the time they have available or will new areas in the expansion be structured in a similar fashion? examples: safe zone in points, removing roamers from hate so everything is on the same spawn to allow an even progression through the zone. After all, almost no-one ever really gets to see or enjoy the zone except for pullers or scouts.

**The planes are supposed to have a different feel and difficulty level relative to most zones. They are also supposed to often require more of a time investment from the players.

Why is it the stats of the guards throughout Norrath have not changed to either
a. more like Paineel guards to stop the High level farming of guard exp.
b. guards are made to be casters like clerics and shamans. I would think twice before I would attack something that could heal and cast blindness and/or a dot...

**We've slowly but surely been upgrading guards for the very reasons you state.

Why is that a lose of equipment due to a hardware malfunction... i.e. a Crash of the server cause people to wait for weeks to hope to get the item back and be required to /petition at least 3 times a week...

**It often takes time to verify item loss and to search the logs that we do have. That said, it shouldn't take as long as you describe and I hope we can increase the responsiveness of our customer service team in the future by adding more people.

Why is it after spending 1 week in hate (7 days) one piece of enchanter gear was dropped yet several pieces for the other classes, yes in the teens. And, how is this fair to even the loot being dropped per class.

**I'm pretty sure we've made some adjustments to Hate recently and that players on the Test server are reporting things are much better.

I know that every person wants "enhancements" for their primary class, and this is not regarding any particular class 8o), but why is it that certain spells do not work in the planes for certain classes?

**Planes are special places, and supposed to offer special challenges as well as some unique tactics to conquer. We reserve the right to have all sorts of spells either not work or do strange things in the alternate planes.

Have we all driven you mad yet with our inquiries?

**I'm getting there, thanks J

Can you speak on the possibility of Rallos Zek adopting the Vallon Zek or Tallon Zek PvP loot rules?

**No plans at this point to do that, no.

Why do hybrids get an xp penalty if they are balanced without it?

**Hybrids are generally more flexible classes, able to melee as well as cast spells. In order to maintain balance, we decided to compensate for their extra flexibility and often power by requiring them more experience points in order to level.

What are the general pros and cons you have seen with the implemented EverQuest class system?

**I have been and continue to be a huge believer in class systems for MMORPGs. They encourage interdependence, which leads to a more social environment - people are heavily encouraged to work together and often forge friendships and alliances over time. The only con that comes to mind is that some players appear to be eternally envious of the fact that they can do some things with their class, but not everything.

It seems like the good majority of complaints fed to Verant are related to player's beliefs of perceived class imbalances. Has it ever made you consider a different way to handle how characters are defined? Perhaps a more complex method that gives the players more control over their character while still maintaining game balance?

**Perhaps. More flexibility in general is a good thing, but you'll never play an EverQuest that doesn't have some sort of class based system.

Why are there so many broken quests in a game called EverQuest?

**Loaded question. I don't think there are that many. There are some, and we try to fix them. But we are also constantly adding new content and quests. With human error, that means we will inevitably add more broken quests as the game evolves.

Why not run 4 dynamic quests all at the same time in 4 remote locations. They would go a lot smoother =P

**Great idea. It just requires more manpower (here's hoping that, over time, given such a successful game, we'll get more).

Good Lord! -- Why are you working on a Sunday?!?

**Heh, good question. I think that even though I know that the vast majority of players love EQ and that the number of people who bitterly post on these boards is very small, that it still bugs me to hear false claims and allegations about Verant and our game. Some people say we don't care, or that we don't listen, or that we don't respect our customers. I've always been a person that is bothered by people believing things that aren't true about me (or my company, whom I represent). I also know that for every poster there are probably 10 or even 100 'lurkers' who just read boards, and I know that they appreciate when we post. Heck, whenever I do I see new names either post or email me, thanking me for the extra info. So I do what I can, and today it occurred to me to try to answer as many questions about the game as I could.

Whenever your inventory is full, and you have a stackable item in there, and you go to loot another stackable item of the same type, it says your inventory is full. So you have to manually pick it up and place it there. This is really quite annoying. Any chance of getting it fixed?

**No short term plans, no, but I'd love to see that addressed one day.

Why does it take 2 months to patch data? Why is the time interval in-between data patches (whatever that means) take over a month. Why is it when a response like "next patch it will be done" really means "next DATA patch" and not clearly stated "which will happen in over a month"

**It doesn't always, but it certainly does take some time. Patching data can often be the most dangerous type of patch, because it's so easy to miss bugs, exploits, and other similar problems. So whenever we add a bunch of data to the game, we try to test it for a longer period of time. Also, moving data over from our test environment to the live servers takes more time than just moving code. Sometimes, when we've had a decent amount of downtime, for whatever reason, we are hesitant to move data over and have delayed the process in the past.

Can you give wood elves the /lapdance emotes (yes with animation)?

**Hehe, no comment

A Rogue cannot evade when the only other thing attacking the creature is a pet. Is this intentional? Why isn't the creature considering the pet a valid target to attack if a Rogue evades?

**I'm not positive, but I think the skill was intended to work in a group situation, and that we assume there are other characters attacking the target.

Why aren't most items that have charged effects rechargeable?

**Because having items with charged effects allow us to place items into the game that would be too powerful were the charges infinite and/or rechargeable. That said, it does indeed make sense to have some charged effects items rechargeable and I think you'll see more in the future.

Are you ever going to reimplement fear and charm back into PVP? If not, why?

**I sincerely doubt it - the problems both of those spells caused became such a customer service nightmare that we took them out. And when we thought long and hard as to how we could re-implement them without creating additional problems, we couldn't think of a way.

When will we expect to see new improvements to Tinkering? Right now there are only a handful of marginally useful items to be made with it.

**Tinkering has a planned upgrade after we get the Poison upgrade in.

Will the new zones that you are creating post-ROK look like the new and improved graphics or will they have to be compatible with the old graphics engine.

**They will have the new and improved graphics.

Will the old areas ever be redrawn to take advantage of better graphics cards.

**No, we won't be doing this. We'd have to recreate the old zones from scratch in order to do this effectively, and we'd rather allocate those resources (game designers/world builders) to creating new content.

What is being done to stop showeq users and does Verant/Sony consider using it a violation of copyright laws or any other laws?

**We are occasionally introducing problems for showeq, breaking it with patches. This typically slows down the distribution of the program, but we've yet to devise a way to eliminate the program. Overall, it's not really that detrimental of an advantage to run the program.

Heheh... wonder if Brad is regretting this idea yet=)

**Getting a bit tired, yeah J

Religion... currently all its good for is getting oneself killed. Are there any plans to ever give people a benefit to having a religion? Say, quests limited to people only of a certain religion, for instance.

**Not quests, but we're thinking about making it such that certain items may only be wielded/used by characters who follow certain god(s).

Does EQ Dev team feel that Magician class is fairly well balanced in levels 50 - 60? What's magicians role in EQ?

**Yes, we feel the Magician is in pretty good shape. For a good description of the class, please see www.everquest.com

What is the underlying policy behind a nerf or enhancement to a class? Is it the "power" of the class? The desirability of the class (e.g. As expressed by player population)? The dependencies of the class?

**We nerf when we feel it's the best way to address something that isn't balanced and/or that's not working out the way we'd planned. We don't like doing nerfs, and realize it is upsetting to some of our players.

My main concern now is with dependencies. Is it Verant's intention to balance classes with respect to dependencies? It's good to have classes that each need each other, but if there are classes that need others that don't need them, would that be cause for tuning?

**In a sense, yes. Interdependence is key to EverQuest and has always been. Classes should need each other, and some classes should need other classes more than others (i.e. some race/class combos are better for soloing than others). This is all by design.

What use does minor illusion have on a non-pvp server where most mobs see right through it? (don't get me started on how sneaky and useful it is on a pvp server)

**This is probably something GZ should answer.

Why doesn't the tree illusion give the same regen effect that tree form gives to druids?

**Another GZ question.

Why is it that monks wearing magic gloves can hit magical creates but a spell caster wearing magical gloves gets the INVULNERABLE message(this may apply to all other classes...just don't know)?

**Monks, being reliant on their fists, are able to use the magic in the gloves to their advantage.

Why can't stealthy classes with high enough sneak ability, sneak anywhere? Don't we remember that historical Halfling that burgled a cup from a dragon?

**Actually, you could get pretty much anywhere in beta with sneaking/hiding/invis. We changed it because it was simply unbalanced - players could map out dangerous areas without risk. I also think the One Ring that allowed Bilbo to recon Smaug's Lair was probably an artifact level item J

Is there a possibility of Battle-Mage class or Ogre Mages(there is an ogre paladin in East Freeport)?

**No plans for additional classes, no.

Any chance that ALL ae spells can be given the NON-PARTY effect like bard songs?

**Doubt it, but that's probably one for GZ.

Is Verant trying to find a way to adjust the clipping plane for spells that are suppose to increase sight range? In their current state these spells are rarely used.

**Turns out we'd rather just let everyone see farther if their machines can handle it.

Is Verant considering making Wizards more group friendly. When I am making a group level 20 and above Wizards are my last pick.

**Loaded question. I think Wizards are group friendly.

Will Enchanters get an Illusion Iksar? I hope so.

**Can't say, sorry.

I know you are sick of this one. Any idea how the Iksar will fit in on Tallon or Vallon yet?

**On the Troll, Ogre, Dark Elf team, those evil bastards! J

In your vision, what are Bards supposed to be the masters of?

**Song and dance.

Will reported broken quests be repaired? (some have been broken for months and have been /bug, petition and email reported)

**I certainly hope so, eventually. We fix them all the time.

To whom do I report game bugs (broken programming)?

**Please use the /bug command.

Will reported game bugs be repaired? (some have been broken since beta and have been /bug, /petition and email reported)

**I certainly hope so, eventually. We fix them all the time.

What will you do to help Melee classes in the expansion? Items are nice but with all the caster classes getting 20 spells or so do you think Maybe they will give some new Combat skills or abilities more than maybe just the 2 that the rumor is.

**I can't answer specifically yet, sorry, but we've got some pretty cool things planned (and in testing now).

How much do you plan to have pathing improved for EQ? Will we ever see a situation where mobs take the most direct route between point a and point b in an open area rather than zigzagging? If not, will we see drastically improved pathing in EQ 2.0?

**We continue to enhance pathing here and there (we've hopefully fixed one of the bugs related to disappearing mobs recently). That said, the way pathing works in general isn't like to change in this incarnation of EQ.

Is it possible to make a command like /bag head arm chest ect. for pvp servers, this would make it harder of naked pk's to make a profit pk'ing when people can hotekey all their armor.

**We've heard this idea several times, and like it in general. It would, however, require quite a bit of work to implement and we don't plan on it short term.

Are there "legendary item" (aka fiery avenger) yet to be found via quest?

**The Fiery Avenger has been found. There are currently no other FA-type items in the game for the other classes, but it is our intent to add them over time.

Are some quests only initiated if you hail or talk to a NPC in his native or non-common languages?

**I don't think so, no, but I certainly think that would be cool.

Are there plans to put in Player controlled NPC's for quests/storyline enhancement? Perhaps they could be attained through a "guide-like" application system and follow a ongoing script written by Verant's quest/ history developers. Not guides as they are too busy with customer complaints.

**No plans, sorry.

Is anything in the works to explain better the history of Norrath other than picking up bits and piece's in quests, both on the God level and within the day-to-day goings-on of Norrath. UO's town criers and GM controlled in game news boards come to mind.

**Those are great ideas, and we hope to do something like that in the future, time and resources permitting.

Noticing a lot of people are complaining of lag and Zone disconnects, is it possible this is due to the security elements you have put in place since you are changing things around often to keep programs like ShowEQ from working. Seems a lot of these complaints peaked after you started thwarting programs like ShowEQ and other hack programs.

**No, no relationship at all. We wouldn't do anything to stop showeq that would jeopardize our playerbase.

When is the next new non-split server going to come up?

**We hope before the Expansion launches - that's the plan, anyway.

Kunark: What are you and the team generally shooting for when you're thinking of moving characters from being able to level from 50 to 60? I.e. comparative power to famous mobs? Would this be considered a "crowning" level with a very powerful new ability for each, or just an upgrade of 10 levels?

**Probably somewhere in-between. A level 60 player is certainly more powerful than a level 50 player, so much so that we feel it will be very attractive to keep playing beyond 50. That said, level 60 players are still going to get beat on pretty hard in the depths of higher level dungeons.

You're going to revamp existing unused dungeons (Runneyeye, Kerra ridge) so people will go there - what is the basic scheme in which you plan to go about this?

**Primarily more and better loot, some of which may come from existing dungeons, and some of which might be new.

I'm concerned that by playing on an older server I'm going to eventually reach a level where there's nothing I can fight because everything is camped solid. What is being done about this?

**We're launching the Expansion which will increase the size of the world by over 33%. We are revamping existing dungeons to make them more attractive. And we intend to keep expanding the world after the Expansion.

How's Star Wars Online shaping up? (I suspect we'll be unable to play Jedi or own/use ships. Any comment?)

**Can't say much other than it's going to be an incredible, ground-breaking game.

Will tracking ever be made more useful, and will rangers ever be able to track better than druids/bards (they currently cannot)

**Yes, but I don't have a time frame.

Will rangers ever become the best at archery, because currently warriors are better (not including strange new ranger only bows, but trueshot and raincaller dmg ration is outdone by DW)

**I'm not sure this is accurate, but if it is, I think we'd rather that Rangers be better at archery than other classes, yes.

Why do so many quests involve CAMPING for a specific item? Wouldn't it be easier to make larger quests involving the gathering of more items, from a wider area with slightly easier drops. That is in the one to two hour range. Not everyone can play in minimum 8 hour blocks.

**Totally agreed, and that is something we've come to learn over time. Our plan is to still have rare items in the future, but have obtaining those items require smaller and more blocks of time.

Why can someone do the same quest over 100 times if they want and still get the item or XP.

**Because it slows down the absolute consumption of content, which is very important for MMORPGs where people plan on playing them for months, even years, as opposed to just a few weeks.

Why are city guards allowed to be killed, session after session?

**Because they are NPCs, like any other. We have upgraded them recently, and will continue to do so if necessary, as a deterrent to people farming them and interfering with newbies.

Why are there many people outside of PAW and so few inside PAW?

**I think Paw needs more work, and we will return to it after we revamp more zones.

What is your stance on TWINKING?

**It's both good and bad, sort of a necessary evil. It upsets some players, yet it increases the replayability of the game significantly. This is one of those topics, btw, that I could write an essay on - I don't think I can accurately explain with just a few sentences, sorry.

Why do casters upon death have access to their SPELLBOOK and yet a melee does not even get a rusty weapon?

**So they can help themselves and other players, both casters and melees, with corpse retrievals.

One year after release why are rogue skills STILL so lacking?

**Loaded question. It is our opinion that after we revamp poison that Rogues will be in pretty good shape. We made numerous enhancements to Rogues over the last year.

-Brad
