No Increased Healing for Warriors
by Chris - 03/27/00 15:52 EST
I'm not going to comment on this, just post it. Brad has this to say about Warriors getting enhanced healing power.

The reason is our Vision(TM) of a Warrior (which certainly isn't original, but rather is quite traditional): They hit stuff with weapons, and they get hit.
They're not supposed to have any magic skills.

They are supposed to be dependent on others for healing, magical protection, travel, etc.

It's all part of the class interdependence that is key to the game. Giving Warriors the ability to heal themselves beyond Bind Wounds would, in my opinion, blur the distinction between classes and harmfully homogenize class balance.

Bottom line, if you want to melee and still cast spells, play a hybrid.

-Brad

Twinking in EQ
by Chris - 03/26/00 21:58 EST
Brad recently commented on twinking (where you give a low level character all very high level equipment).

Personally, I don't twink any of my characters, but that's only me. I enjoy the challenge of getting each one their own gear.

Here's a thought:
What if twinking, or actually the trickle down economic effect of items (both characters being 'twinked' items for free AND the prices of 'better' items lowering daily because of the constant influx of items into the economy with nothing taking them out) was there to HELP the casual gamer?

What if we knew the hard core gamers would head out and conquer the game over a shorter period of time, able to commit more hours and willing to face more dangers?

What if we knew eventually, as more causal gamers came to EQ, the better items would trickle down to the more causal gamers, making it easier for them to play EQ because they are, essentially, more powerful?

*shrug*

It's just a thought.

(personally, I have mixed feelings about twinking and it's long term effects on MMORPG economies and game balance. I think I could probably argue both pro and anti-twiking very effectively. We definitely have people from both philosophies on the EQ team, which is a good thing.)

-Brad

Tuesday, March 21st, 2000

Patch Message
by Chris - 03/21/00 14:52 EST
The following message was added today, after the patch.

March 21st, 11:00am
-------------------

***UPDATE MESSAGE***

This morning's update consisted primarily of server-side changes to improve performance of the game engine in general. However, there are several changes to note:

*Spell Changes*

- Skin Like Nature has had its damage shield removed and has regained its regeneration component. We're certain this time :)
- Many Shaman buff spells have had their recast delay removed. You can now cast them on multiple people without having to wait between each cast.
- The Wizard spell "Rend" was improperly saving versus cold. It now saves versus Magic.


*Bug Fixes and General Enhancements*

The spawning problem in Solusek's Eye (SoldungA), related to the spawn of the Clockwork gnomes, will be fixed later today in a zone patch.

The +/= key will no longer take screenshots.

You can now adjust your clipping plane by using the SHIFT/- and SHIFT/+ key combinations.

You can now view the Message of the Day by typing /motd.

Corrected a bug in the /follow process that would cause your character to loop around to the right at times when following someone.

Corrected a bug that would cause item-loss associated with trading a container marked LORE.

Corrected some grouping bugs that had the potential to cause the client to crash.


*Feign Death and Pets*

The Feign Death spell will no longer wear off. This change had been made due to an issue that was discovered that allowed Necromancers to solo creatures while feigned and AFK. The change had some unintended effects, as we discovered that there are a few cases where someone could be legitimately feigned for a longer period. Instead of having the spell wear off, any summoned pet will automatically suicide 2 minutes after its owner feigns. This is the solution requested by most of the players who voiced an opinion, and should serve the same goal as the original change.


- The EverQuest Team

Where Will the Servers Be In 5 Years?
by Chris - 03/16/00 12:57 EST
This is a question that I've been pondering for a bit. Thankfully someone else asked. Looks like we could be playing EQ for some time to come!

>>>Something dawned on me the other night. With Verant making more games in the future, what would happen if they shut down the Everquest servers ? In other words, say in 6-7 years, Verant has another breakthrough game and starts to concentrate almost exclusivly on it, they need the severs, so the take down the EQ servers to use.

It's safe to say that we won't be using the EQ Server's for anything else. The rate at which system resources (processing power, memory, etc) increase is only rivaled by the rate at which programmers can consume them. Its fairly safe to say that in 6-7 years, the EQ servers won't be powerful enough to run any game that we would come out with at that time.

>>>If that ever happened, this game would 100% unplayable (well almost...we could still play the tutorial). I mean I can still play games from 15 years ago IF I wanted to. I could still install something from my floppy drive and still play it IF I wanted to (like the oringinal System Shock, lets say)

Though we know that EQ won't last forever, the concept of it going away is still a bit out there to really comment on it. EQ is still growing a year after release. In fact, well over 60% of the people who ever played EQ are still playing, so we can't even forecast a reliable figure for the average subscription length.

For these reasons, we haven't even begun to consider downsizing or exit strategies. It will probably be several years before we can even hypothesize about it.

-Gordon

PoA Q&A
by Chris - 03/15/00 13:51 EST
A few questions about the Plane of Air were asked on the Gameplay Forum, and Abashi answered them all.

>>>1. Was the zone in debuff part of the original game plan or a change made to prevent ppl from accessing the platforms out of order?
It was a measure to prevent people from zoning in with levitate, which then enabled them to get to islands out-of-order.

>>>If it was a preventative measure why was the level of the islands not altered to prevent ppl from moveing up out of order? (Besides a new download)

The new download is a big part of it. Changing world geometry leads to having to download the entire zone again. The other thing though is that our people who are capable of doing world geometry are tied up for a while. There are also more problems than just allowing people to get to islands out of order, it's letting them get down to an island that they've already been to.

>>>2. Since the new change people are no longer able to access the starting platform once the get higher up. (You used to be able to float down) You have to jump off and gate back up. Costing a player upwards of 250p per trip. Are the costs to get into sky going to be changed to reflect this new demand?

Not at this time. Perhaps if we see an excessive shortage of platinum, we might reevaluate our money sinks so that they are in line with the means of the players.

>>>3. What is the average amount of cash that a lvl 46+ player (ballpark it) has on his person? Most folks that i know usually have between 500p and 1k.

Off the top of my head, I'm not sure. I suppose it depends on your class. I know a few enchanters with over 30K in the bank. I know that as a solo high-40s necro, I can make over 200pp per hour, and I have 3500pp in the bank.

Considering that there are auctions out there for hundreds of thousands of platinum, I think there's probably still enough of it out there.

>>>4. We spent around of 15000p - 18000p so far this week getting to gorgolask platform in PoA for a 30 person trip. Thats 500-600p per person so far. Is this the target Verant is looking for?

Considering that the only mandatory cost in the Plane of Sky is the component to zone in, with everything else being optional for short-cuts, it sounds ok. Of course, the entire zone is supposed to be the pinnacle of difficulty.

>>>5. Do the above figures encourage farming of items to gather easy cash for these trips?

I think that people are probably depleting their reserves before resorting to farming. They can also start selling some of the items they find in the world for plat, rather than making them trade-only. There's a bit of this going on currently, and overall the economy is getting healthy.

>>>6. I get the feeling that PoA is a work in progress. Should we expect any other "high impact" (high impact to me was the "anti buff" patch) changes to the zone or is it getting close to verants "vision" for PoA?

I won't call any zone perfect, but we think we have it pretty well figured out. That's not to say that we won't have to make a change or two should no one ever be able to reach the top, or should some loophole be found that makes it entirely too easy, but it's looking good at present.

>>>7. One last comment/question... If I had been given a choice of loosing buffs or moving the islands I would have chosen the latter. I feel being able to levitate in PoA... with all the mobs that can cast blowback and gravity flux... is useful for keeping yourself in PoA and preventing damage to yourself. Is it possible to have the islands adjusted to prevent people from exploiting levitate? Another 30min patch makes me no difference if the change is a good one

We don't have any plans at present to change zone geometry. As mentioned above, we have all the people who are capable of that tied up for a while.

-Gordon
