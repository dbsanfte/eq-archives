Reduced mana regen rate?
by Chris - 05/02/00 11:04 EST
Someone posted that they have noticed a reduced mana regen rate now.

I can't say that I've noticed this in Kunark. I do notice that I use more mana though :)

There has been no intentional change, however due to the number of people reporting it I'm not ready to dismiss it just yet. However, I highly suspect that the observations are due to a number of things, not related to the actual rate. For instance:
1. Perception: Mana above level 50 continues to increase at a rate much like it was before, however the mana regen rate does not increase on the same curve, giving the perception that it takes longer to med, even though you are still getting the same amount of mana back per tick. This is something that people also notice and claim as they level up or gain +INT/WIS/MANA items. They increase the amount of mana that they have without increasing the regen rate in a linear manner.

2. Watched Pot: The addition of Kunark has caused people to spread out more. The net result of this is that the ratio of people to targets has improved. Where a group used to be at full mana the next time it was their "turn" to kill a particular spawn, they are now staring at their mana bars while a surplus of critters walk by that they could be killing.


3. Placebo Effect: The "Placebo effect" also causes the "Watched Pot". Someone, perhaps due to one of the reasons mentioned above, perceives that the mana regen rate has changed, and comments on it. This causes other people to become sensitive to the issue, causing them to pay close attention to their mana bars, giving the impression that it is taking longer to regen.

4. Of course there is always the good old fashioned "bug", which shouldn't take too long for us to figure out as soon as I can corner the programmers I'm looking into it this afternoon.

-Gordon

Guard killing and faction
by Chris - 05/02/00 10:58 EST
It will be interesting to see what Verant does this time to prevent guard killing.

>>>It is getting a little tiresome in Butcherblock trying to find a guard being that everyone is camped and murdered the minute they spawn. You can see the bodies of newbies at the guard stations because they were foolish enough to believe that they could get some back up there with a battle gone bad. Why don't you make the killing of a guard a faction hit that so that the individual is automatically KOS in good cities. That would curb this practice immediately.
The goal was to have each city have a faction associated with it, making it possible for someone to upset one city and remain in good standing with another. In some cases it's apparent that this may not have worked just like we would have liked it to, and we're looking for different ways to respond. We'll probably look at making some of the guards a little less appetizing.

One thing to remember is that the goal of the faction system is to embrace the idea that no one really believes that they are evil. For instance, the dark elves do not think that they are evil, they are just uncompromising in their application of their morality. They probably feel that the high elves are evil.

More on Iksar monks
by Chris - 05/02/00 10:54 EST
Iksar monks are the source of quite a few debates. Abashi explains some of the advantages and disadvantages of being an Iksar monk.

Previously, with only one class capable of becoming a monk, balancing the monk class on the basis of race was not difficult. However, the inclusion of the Iksar, with many different racial attributes, has made this a bit harder.
Infravision and regen make life much easier for the young Iksar, which leaves us with a need to validate humans as a monk class. In addition, monks are the only race that get an AC bonus based upon their race. This leaves them already getting one just by virtue of being a monk, and now another because they are also an Iksar.

In this case, we decided that in addition to including the expected XP and faction issues, we've also made it so that Iksar monks will not be as "good" at *being* monks as humans. It's not by a large extreme, but human monks will always be better at doing what monks do: dealing damage.
