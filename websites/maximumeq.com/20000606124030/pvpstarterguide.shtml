PvP Starter Guide
A thoughtful person who plays on a Player vs Player server sent us in this PvP Starter Guide.    Enjoy!

Rallos Zek

This is intended as a guide to players new to the Rallos Zek server. Although I’m a newbie to Everquest my character is now approaching 8th level and I suspect I’m no longer quite as easy a target as I used to be ! The primary reason for writing this was that having read much of the advice from experienced Everquest players it strikes me that a lot of that advice was less useful on Rallos Zek.

Rallos Zek is a player versus player server. Therein lies the problem. There aren’t that many places where you are really safe from attack by another player. Despite this there are a number of methods that help you survive .

Firstly as a newbie get to know your way around your hometown and as normal establish yourself with a Guild if possible. This is no different to normal play but getting used to the locations in the City is more important than usual. Pay close attention to where the Guards are located and don’t anger them. When all else fails they may be your only friends. For that reason I try to keep my faction fairly high with them. Always remember that the most dangerous creatures out there are other players.

Once you are ready for a spot of adventuring make your way to one of the City Gates. I tend now to approach these with some care. Use of the Who button will at least get you familiar with those who are online. As you get to know people you will be able to better discriminate who are player killers . That might not be obvious at first but you should get better informed as you become more experienced.

You will see from the Who button that virtually no-one is listed as anything other than Anonymous. The only exceptions to this are those who want to advertise they are very powerful indeed , and those who don’t know how to change it with the General button. Make sure you don’t advertise your class, level etc otherwise you will definitely attract some company.

Make sure you have selected Run rather than Walk. I can’t see any slow moving newbies lasting long to be truthful. It also advertises that you don’t know what you are doing .

It is also worth looking around and seeing who is out there. More particularly see what they are up to. Those hunting are probably less of a threat to you ( maybe ). Anyone loitering around for no apparent reason might be more of a threat. Even at this early stage I would pay attention to what Guilds players belong to. You should be able to recognise player killer Guilds soon enough. Just as importantly you should soon be able to recognise those Guilds that are Anti Player Killers. It is well worth talking to those who are from Anti Player Killer Guilds . ( Make sure its by way of telling ) Some will be happy to hunt player killers down, others might reward you with items or buffs .

I would advise soon getting into the habit of considering most of those around you . At first they will likely all consider red. However it is good practice to establish as soon as possible who is a real threat to you. Admittedly at first that is everyone .

Those who are newbies too are worth talking to. At later levels they may be your friends . establishing at an early stage who to trust is important.

At later levels you should also keep an eye on what people are hunting . That sometimes helps differentiate their level and class. Usually by now someone will have been over to check what you’re equipped with. You will be warned that they are doing this. It is worth knowing that the typical reason behind this inspection will be to establish what loot they will get for killing you !

On occasions though it will be so a high level player can pass you something they don’t want.

Whatever it is you do want it even if only to sell it !

The subject of what to carry is a difficult one. At first you are so easy to kill that even if someone just wanted your Guild tunic you wouldn’t last long. My advice would be not to go out adventuring carrying anything much , and expect to lose whatever you are carrying. To that end the bank is well worth using to store items you want to preserve. Later on you’ll be better able to protect them.

As soon as you can , invest in a bag. Basically anything contained in a bag can’t be looted from your corpse. Your normal line of defence when under attack should be to run towards the Guards as soon as possible. If that should fail then bag up whatever you want to keep before you die. This tactic tends to work better when you can afford a bag ( obviously ) and when your character can stand combat for more than a few hits. Bagging items in combat isn’t easy so I would recommend practicing it.

If you really feel threatened I’d recommend bagging items just as a precaution.

The running whenever attacked seems to work for me and I have to confess to never having been player killed. However there are methods of making it more effective. For a start get really used to your surroundings. This is obvious advice but if you get lost on your way back you will never make it back. I tend to look fully round before attacking anything. It gives you an idea of what other creatures will be on their way to assist the one you are attacking . It lets you know who is stalking you , but most important of all it familiarises you with your escape route. The weather and visibility can change almost instantly. Knowing your exact surroundings will help you get back when the rain hits. Times when running won’t work are if you get caught by a root spell ( in which case you’ll need that bag ) or if you get cornered. Oddly enough most of the Inns etc found outside the main Towns are especially dangerous. The Guards and occupants don’t often help out while there is usually only one way out…………..

As usual consider everything before attacking it , even the same type of creature can vary a good deal in strength. If you get into trouble my advice would still be to run at an early stage. If you wait and win chances are another player will finish off an easy target. This is the complete opposite of how you might play on another server , but is sound advice.

Once you have made your first kill it’s tempting to immediately loot , again I’d still advise at least looking around first. In more difficult areas I have to admit to not looting at all , just getting back to a safe area as soon as possible. If visibility is poor for whatever reason, or you don’t have night vision it is sometimes worth not looting in any case . Fire Beetles in particular will remain as Navigation Beacons if left unlooted. Any strategy that helps you return to base after hunting is worth adopting.

Once you have repeated this enough times you should fairly rapidly gain in levels. That doesn’t mean that the methods that worked for you as a newbie don’t work now. There will always be more powerful player killers out there . As a higher level target they would also assume that you will be carrying more valuables and even magic items. As you venture further out my advice would be to remain cautious still . Rather than running for the Guards ( there probably won’t be any ) make sure you know how to reach the nearest zone. From that zone of course you will need to know how to reach those Guards if your attacker is persistent. Player Killers are more likely to succeed if you panic under attack. Having an escape route already planned will help you flee without panic.

At higher levels it also often pays to group. However you will invariably be better off grouping with anyone you know and trust, preferably someone you recognise from those early forays when you should have been noting who wasn’t a player killer. Groups offer some protection from player killers but should not relax their guard. If low on mana and health after tackling a difficult target they are still vulnerable to attack. Although for best efficiency you might want a smaller group the larger one’s are much less vulnerable to attack by another player. Again this is contrary to how you might play on a standard server.

There will inevitably be times when an all out war is raging at the city gates. That advice to approach the gates with care still remains . Even now I see players stroll out into a major conflict without realising what they have got into. A major conflict or a very aggressive local guild might seem to threaten your enjoyment of the game. However if you cater for that eventuality it is at least less frustrating. For a start I would recommend investing in some trade skill or other as soon as possible. Basically find something useful to do without going outside at all. If it really isn’t safe to venture out practice swimming or fishing , its safer and one day you’ll find them useful. A trade skill allows you to also make some money. There is even the faint possibility that you might enhance your popularity. After all will you be player killed by the character you earlier brewed some mead for ? Using a trade skill to make friends is no bad thing. Other skills such as tailoring are useful for armour etc . When someone loots your patchwork armour you can then at the least easily replace it.

At an early stage get used to bandaging yourself ( F1 to target self ) and invest in bandages. It reduces downtime and you should bear in mind that the periods where you may be able to hunt without interference are limited. If you aren’t equipped with bandages go shopping when it isn’t safe to go out.

I can’t help the feeling that using all available skills is more useful on Rallos Zek than normal. Most fighting skills don’t need a lot of investment as they automatically increase with combat. The one’s worth thinking about are those oft neglected one’s. Take missile /throwing skills. At higher levels these are recommended to bait a creature that can’t be easily reached for some reason . But on Rallos Zek you can easily get into the situation where it is unsafe to venture far if you have attendant player killers. ( To disrupt you hunting they don’t even need to be that high level )

With a missile weapon you should be able to choose the ground you will draw a creature to. Being just a little nearer the Guards can allow you to continue hunting while avoiding too much interference.

Sooner or later you are going to upset a higher level character than your own. The best way to do so is to wade in and support a character who is under attack . Best of all is to kill the player killer to make yourself really popular with the locals. Unfortunately it pays to decide whether its actually worth defending someone who is being attacked . If it attracts unwanted attention to you , and they aren’t really all that bothered where does that leave you ?

If you do become a target life is going to get interesting . For a start I would immediately assume you will at some stage get killed. A more powerful character will easily do so especially if they pick their moment. You cannot always defend against a really persistent attacker. I tend to bank or bag my valuables just in case. I also try to note at what times that player tends to be online . They can be easily avoided if you know what times they’ll be around. Remember they may even be in a totally different time zone so its worth considering. You can of course move areas completely , which actually isn’t such a bad thing anyway. Make sure you bank everything first and bind yourself to the new area as soon as you get there . If your home town has more than one entrance it is also worth simply trying the other end of town. Usually one area will be quieter than the other.

Sooner or later you will come up with your own methods and strategies. I have no doubt that the methods I have used will not suit all . However when you are at the stage where you are adopting your own techniques I would argue you are no longer a Newbie.

As a good example of me remaining cautious I’ll sign off without my character name ! ( I’d be a definite target otherwise )

Anonymous , Rallos Zek .
