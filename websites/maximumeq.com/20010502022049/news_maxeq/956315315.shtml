Patch Message
by Chris - 04/18/00 07:05 EST
This patch message was added a while after the servers came up yesterday:

April 17th, 4:00pm
-------------------

EverQuest: The Ruins of Kunark (upgrade) should now be arriving on doorsteps around the world. Please note that attempting to create an Iksar character will not work until the expansion zones are officially opened within the next week.

We ask that if you attempt to create an Iksar character out of curiosity that you do not proceed past the "History" page (the page that speaks of your lifestyle) in the character creation process. We appreciate your cooperation.

*Today's Patch*

Today's patch consisted of preparations for the launch of Kunark, as well as some new art. The following spell changes were also included:

- Corrected a bug with a new bard song that allowed them to charm anything, such as deities and dragons.
- Added a lesser included wisdom effect with "Brilliance" as well as a lesser included intelligence effect with "Insight". These spells will no longer stack.


- The EverQuest Team


Plane of Sky Items to be Revamped
by Chris - 04/18/00 06:59 EST
All I can say is, damn straight.

Most of the items received via quests in the Plane of Sky have been an utter joke. Level 30 players are able to obtain equipment that is superior to what has been received.

Hello all,
I've noticed that quite a few people are upset about the quality of some of the Plane of Sky items that have been found so far.

Part of the problem is due to the fact that the most powerful items still have yet to be acquired. Once you start seeing these items come out of the plane, you will understand what I meant when I said "There are items in the Plane of Sky that make other planar items look like rusty weapons".

The other part of the problem is that some of the items that have been found so far are indeed underpowered. Since we were entering uncharted territory with many of our ideas for these items, we erred on the side of making them underpowered, thus avoiding the problems that occur should we have to downgrade them later. That said, while some of the items that have been found so far are appropriately powered, many are not.

Though I will not comment on any particular item at this time, we will be reevaluating the Plane of Sky items in the near future, probably after the initial excitement from the Kunark release.

-Gordon

Uber-Resist Mobs
by Chris - 04/18/00 06:54 EST
Ahhh... this explains those uber-resist mobs that you get every once and a while who just refuse to be snared, stunned, nuked and DoT'd.

>>>Snare resists increased?
We haven't made any global or specific changes to the way resists work in a *long* time, except to lower their possibility via code. An example of this would be how wizard's spells are resisted less after they hit level 35.

Specific NPCs on the other hand are adjusted frequently, and not to mention have a chance of being boosted dynamically if the NPC makes a good roll. It sounds like the second part is what happened in your case.

-Gordon

Ranger vs. Cleric Quest Armour
by Chris - 04/18/00 06:42 EST
It seems that everyone looks at everyone else to see what someone else has, and not what their own class already has.

>>>The Cleric class is suppose to be a plate armor class, and the Ranger is a chainmail class. And from my understanding plate armor is suppose to be superior to chainmail. That and I'm all for my healer to have more AC.
This is correct, and that was the motive behind our changes. Now we realize that there are pieces of armor that the ranger can wear that use the plate texture, but that is *really* chain-based, and was set to use the plate texture since it looked better.

There needs to be an advantage to being a plate class, and that's why the cleric armor was boosted to bring it in line with the hybrids.

Plane Drops Still Not Balanced?
by Chris - 04/18/00 06:39 EST
This seems to be an ongoing battle with high level players and Verant.

>>>Fear is still dropping ALOT less than hate.. I think the problem is that instead of actually revamping fear when they moved (bard, cleric, enchanter, magician, warror, rogue and shaman) stuff to hate, they took out the drops of the before mentioned classes and left the mobs.
Interestingly enough, we got the impression over the past several weeks that people found the drop rates in Fear to be acceptable, and hate unacceptable. Prior to that, the general feeling was that drops in Fear were bad, but Hate was great. At that time, we adjusted both of them in opposite directions. This time however, last patch fixed Hate, but didn't change Fear.

I'm starting to think that the problem at this point might be coming from the standpoint that "equality between the planes" hasn't been acheived.

The truth is that both planes are now engineered to drop the same amount of armor (on average) in the same period of time. They are basically equal now with possible exception to a surplus of cleric armor that might be dropping in Hate due to public reports.
