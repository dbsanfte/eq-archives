Stats Finally Explained!
by Chris - 04/09/00 21:22 EST
Brad McQuaid finally released what stats do in EverQuest. Personally, what was said is what I thought the EQ community ever wanted to know.

In working with the people doing the strategy guide for Kunark we decided to provide them with general information as to what the stats do.
I'm going to post them here.

This is a change in the sense that we were not going to discuss them or release more info. We changed our minds.

That said, we're not posting any formulas, and it's absolutely true that some stats have considerably more direct impact on gameplay than others.

We're posting this for clarification, and because previous explanations have not been accurate (manual, etc). We will not, however, engage in debate and our stance on how much some stats affect game play is that it's by design to inhibit some aspects of twinking. Are there better attribute systems out there? Yeah, but we're not going to change something as fundamental as a stat system in EQ after the game's been out for more than a year.

Anyway, here you go:

DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills; how hard you hit with bows

STR: determines how much you can carry; influences maximum and average damage; influences how quickly you learn many offensive skills

STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long you can hold your breath

INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all mages, as well as shadow knights

WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells (charms in particular)

AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much damage you take when you are hit

-Brad

High Int'l Shipping Explained
by Chris - 04/09/00 18:44 EST
I was a bit taken aback by the $40 USD to air ship my RoK expansion (needless to say, I chose the $10 USD option of ground).

Brad explained why the price is so high for non-US residents.

The intent was to give international players an option to get RoK quickly, preferably on or before the 24th, so they could play right away.
In the past international players have had to wait quite some time to get EQ.

We wanted to give them the option (buy directly, albeit at a high price due to the quick method of shipping, or wait for the boxes to get to the various countries).

The prices are 'high' for two reasons:

1. The shipping is fast so you get it on or around the 24th.

2. We don't do a ton of international shipping and therefore don't have access to big discounts you might find at amazon.com or some such. We basically had to use dhl/ups type methods.

I think we should have been a lot more clear about this, and I apologize for not explaining this stuff and our intent beforehand.

-Brad

Why Are There Zones?
by Chris - 04/08/00 22:45 EST
A player recently asked why there are zones in EQ, while there aren't in Asheron's Call.

Massively Multiplayer RPGs have a long development cycle compared to other games, and the graphics engine is the first thing that is designed. Back when development started on EQ, the development team had several goal for operation of the game. For example, they wanted to minimize the effect of internet latency. They also wanted to have each "area" have its own look-and-feel, with its own set of appropriatly placed NPCs.
At the time, the "Zone" technology was the best technology available to meet the goals of the development team. Of course since then, new technologies have been developed, and though it makes sense that we would take advantage of them in future products, EQ will always have zones. You just can't change graphics engines on the fly post-production.

-Gordon

More RoK Expansion Upgrade Clarifications
by Chris - 04/08/00 22:24 EST
This is going to be a support nightmare, nobody seems to get how this works (although hopefully they will soon, it's fairly simple).

To clarify the different purchase options:
If you buy the UPGRADE, you can use it to flag your CURRENT account for Kunark. This will allow you to access all Kunark-related content (including levels above 50) with all of your characters.

If you buy the RETAIL package, you can do one of two things:

1. You can use the key to create a NEW account, where all characters on that account will have access to Kunark. If you do this, you will receive a free month of play with the NEW account, but it does NOT authorize your CURRENT account to access Kunark content.

OR

2. You can use the key to upgrade your CURRENT to access Kunark content in the same fashion as the upgrade. If you choose to do this with the RETAIL package, you will NOT receive a free month of play, and you will not be able to use the account key to create a NEW account.

Isn't There A Wall Here?
by Chris - 04/07/00 04:07 EST
I always wondered about this...

>>>Why is it that when you are inside a cottage, house or building creatures can attack you through the wall even though you cant see them. This basically means in places like East Karana you cant take refuge almost anywhere. Why shouldnt you be able to get in a house and hide w/o a lion invisibly attacking you through the wall? this applies to griffins and other creatures as well. Other buildings in other zones too!
Not really a bug. In order to reduce the amount of "lag" that can happen due to internet latency, then engine handles object collisions client-side. Most houses and such are objects (rather than world geometry) and are subject to this.

It also has the benefit of eliminating a good number of issues that would have to be dealt with in order to limit possible exploitation. For instance, if this wasn't the case, someone could sit inside a house and cast upon a creature that couldn't fit through the open door.

-Gordon

New Change in Licence Agreement
by Chris - 04/05/00 20:06 EST
There is a new message on the patch server. I'm very glad that Verant realized that what they were doing was wrong, to spite what the player base said in a poll.

***ANNOUNCEMENT***

Ok. We put the poll in, and with roughly 15,000 people participating the poll came up with 83% of the people being fine with us running the check for cheating.

DESPITE THIS POLL we have decided that it's the wrong thing to do. Enough people have convinced us that it's chipping away a little too much at people's privacy EVEN if they do consent for us to implement this policy.

Therefore, the change to the EULA will read as follows:

Solely for the purpose of patching and updating the Game, you hereby grant us permission to (i) upload Game file information from the Everquest directory and (ii) download Game files to you.

Now, before anyone wonders exactly what this is, let me explain. Technically speaking we probably should have had this language in there from day one for you to consent us to even download new game files to you in the first place. We apologize for not realizing that we should have gotten this consent, but live and learn.

We can admit when we make mistakes, and I believe this is a case where we owe an apology to our Playerbase. In our haste to try and thwart people from damaging the game we went overboard.

There will be absolutely no scanning of anyones computer for any reason other than the normal patching process (which won't do any sort of checking on what you have running).

Regards,

John Smedley
President and CEO
Verant Interactive, Inc.
