MSNBC Article about Everquest
by Geoff - 12/29/00 16:34 EST
MSNBC has written a little article regarding the advent of MMORPG's. Check it out.

EverQuest is a traditional role-playing game in many ways. Combat is still an integral part of it, but bloodshed is not the ultimate goal, and the teen-rated game isn’t gory. Here, characters interact with each other, defeating enemies, trading items or giving advice. “The attraction of online games is people, and lots of them,” says Carl Fenley, a 31-year-old gamer. “Online games, like EverQuest, are kind of like the bar Cheers—it’s always open and everybody knows your name.”

Thursday, December 28th, 2000

Alan comments on the idea of a key-ring
by Geoff - 12/28/00 14:17 EST
Alan mentioned on the boards that an easier way to handle all your keys may be coming.

"I'm finding it harder and harder to find inventory space for every key that I pick up along the way. What I suggest is that you place an npc where he will combine your keys for a fee. I don't see too many gameplay issues with this request and shouldn't be too hard to do in code. Please look into this."

We’re looking into something like this. I don’t know what the implementation will be, or if it will happen at all (though I imagine it will, since I don’t think we want to rule out making any more soulbound items...). Hang on until we get back from vacation in full force and let’s see what we come up with.

But, well, you don’t need to carry all of those soulbound items with you everywhere you go.

Alan
