Monday, May 8th, 2000

Old froglok models in Kunark
by Chris - 05/08/00 13:45 EST
Someone recently asked why the frogloks in Kunark don't have an ungraded model, like the goblins. Brad McQuaid answered this one.

2 resons (sic), really.
1. We wanted to nice mix of old and new models in the Expansion -- blending familiarity and newness has always been key to EQ (including the setting, the creatures, etc.)

2. There's always finite time in creating a product. Would I like a new froglok model like there's a new goblin model? Sure! Heck, I'd like there to be tons more models... but one has to draw the line somewhere in terms of how much content you can get into a game in so much time.

Overall, I'm very proud of how many distinct models are in EQ, both in the original release and in the Expansion -- I don't think any other 3D game even comes close to the variety we have. The EQ character model team has a LOT to be proud of.

-Brad

Friday, May 5th, 2000

VenrilSathir faction in kunark dungeons
by Chris - 05/05/00 15:14 EST
It appears that faction is starting to make a bigger difference in where you fight, and what you do in EQ.

With the number of posts about dungeon factions in Kunark, I wanted to take an opportunity to address them all at once:
Yes, it is correct, fighting in several dungeons in Kunark will lower your faction with VenrilSathir, and thus make conducting business in the outpost in the Overthere a bit rough. This is intentional, and is not a bug. It's also necessary for the political environment of Kunark to make sense, and our stance on it is firm. There are consequences, the importance of which each person has to decide for themselves, for killing everything that moves.

We're not forcing anyone to fight anywhere, or to stay out of any particular zone. Like all faction issues, it is your choice whether or not that faction is important to you. This is the same choice that players of good alignments had to make when deciding whether or not to kill things that give them bad faction. It's a personal choice. Being KOS in the evil outpost does not put the spells that are available there out of your reach. You certainly have the ability to have someone else, who is not KOS there, complete the quests for you. Evil races are KOS throughout Faydwer as it is, and people get along fairly well there. Kunark is not meant to be an encapsulated environment that people never leave. We expect people to have to go back to the mainland from time to time to get supplies and such. If you make yourself KOS to the outpost, there are other ways for you to get into Kunark without going through it.

So just to summarize, the factions are intentional. I suggest that everyone consider their options carefully before making a decision on how to tackle the continent.

-Gordon

Restrictive equipable equipment
by Chris - 05/05/00 15:08 EST
A lot of items are being found that are very limited in not only what classes can wear them, but also what races. Gordon explains why Verant is doing this.

This is another case where it is not our goal to make everyone the same. We don't want everyone of a given level to be clad in the same equipment. Equipment that is able to be widely used by a large number of race/class combos also encourage camping, something that we would like to see go away.
Restricting items by race and class works towards this goal.

-Gordon

Manaskin dissapointment
by Chris - 05/04/00 11:36 EST
Manaskin, part of the damage absorbtion line of spells available to Wizards and Necros (and to a lesser extent SKs) has a mana regen component. The spells uses a peridot to cast.

A level 52 wizard recently timed just how much time it regens. Apparently it's next to nothing. Abashi had this to say.
