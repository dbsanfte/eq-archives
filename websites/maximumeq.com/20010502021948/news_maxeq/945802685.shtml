Gordon Answers More Complainers
by John - 12/17/99 08:34 EST
What a job Gordon has, answering the cornucopia of whining players that infest this fine game. Here is what he had to say about non-caster binding and other beaten to death topics.

Forum Poster:
If Verant were to let casters bind anyone, anywhere, the gameplay of EQ would be greatly enhanced. For one, casters would not have to wait for their tanks to return to the dungeon, creating much less downtime and adding to the enjoyment of the game. In addition, melee classes would no longer run the very high risk of being killed while returning to the action, and being forced to suffer an even higher XP loss than the caster.

Gordon:
This is a very old argument. I could talk about the fact that death is supposed to be inconveniencing. It’s supposed to be something that you really don’t want to happen. I could talk about how this design is just as inconveniencing for the casters as it is the melee types. I could talk about value added to druids, wizards, clerics, and necromancers who all provide post-death services to characters. I could talk about balance issues in regards to having it so anyone can die and be right back in combat 30 seconds after it happens. I could talk about a lot of things.

When it comes down to it, some classes can do things that other classes can’t. That is the simplest explanation I can give without going into how a change in bind would cause a dramatic shift to many dynamics in the game. Some people don’t play melee-types just due to this disadvantage. Other people don’t play casters just because of the fact that a caster can’t take a hit. It’s all a matter of personal preference. Is a given disadvantage too large for you to play the class? It’s up to you to choose.

Forum Poster:
Second, why can't Verant stick to their deadlines? John Smedley reported that a new server would be up in early November. Of course, after we make demands when the deadline has come and gone

Gordon:
We have had a tendency to be overconfident with our estimations, and that is not intentional. Believe me, with the amount of flack we catch when we miss a deadline, it would be much easier to get it right the first time. Sometimes though, schedules must be adjusted, and there is no way around that. I should mention that John Smedley said that a new server would be coming up “soon”, not “in early November”. In addition to getting a rein on our overconfidence, we need to be a little less free with the term “soon”. We are working on this and you should see some definite improvement.

Forum Poster:
What makes it even worse is that Verant never bothers to inform us why they have missed the deadline. . It is a known fact among players that Verant consistently lies about their deadlines

Gordon:
As far as the new server goes, I told you that we missed it, and I told you why we missed it: Someone that needs to be here cannot. What else would you like to know? Yes, we missed the deadline. I first said it would be up by the end of this week, and had to move it off to early next week. But as to the suggestion that we somehow had some malicious intent in missing the deadline by a day or two at most, that’s just silly.

I hope this clears things up. "


Greed Is Not Gordon
by John - 12/17/99 08:25 EST
Yes, it's a bad play on words, but here is what Gordon had to say on the greediness of some players in EQ.

"Hello there, I don't have any specific comments right now, but I did want to let you know that there are some very good thoughts here. Yes, I think we did underestimate greed's effect on gameplay. We *hoped* that people would prefer to "do" a dungeon, meaning fight their way through it, not just run in to get ItemX. Many of the changes we are making now are to try and shape the game back into what we (and the majority of the players) wanted it to be at release, removing the influence of greed. Despite some of the short term pain, I think we're making progress for the long term. "

Brad Speaks on Crowding
by John - 12/15/99 09:32 EST
From the newsgroups:

Newsgroup Poster:
"Your group walks the road less taken, Brad. Solusek A is generally fairly empty, even when the server is overly full. You make a good point in that there ARE dungeons for the 20-40 crowd that are underutilized (permafrost, paw , sola, mistmoore)... but what you forget is that there are REASONS why they're that way."

Brad:
"Actually, that was exactly the point I was trying to make (I wasn't trying to imply there aren't crowding issues -- there are, and we're launching a new server soon, as well as exploring whether we could combine Talon and Valon, thus freeing up another). "

Newsgroup Poster:
"Solusek A, for example, is a huge maze. It trains fairly easily, there are a fair number of casters, everything runs, and it has lava. Even with a map, the only time I would go into SolA is invisible, to run straight to an item spawn spot and start camping. On the other hand, SolA *is* reasonably itemized, unlike Paw2. :) "

Brad:
"From my perspective, the jury is still out on Paw2 -- if it needs item upgrades, it will get them -- I just think it's too early to condemn the zone like some people are so quickly willing to.

As for the difficulty of Sol A, I find it a moderately difficult zone, and both a challenging and fun one. Same for Permafrost (Mistmoore even more difficult). I also find it a great place for exp (which is primarily why we were there the other night). I don't think every zone should have the same difficulty rating (in fact, even if I did think that, there's no practical way to insure they would have a similar difficulty rating -- too many variables). I think the general solution for more difficult zones is to add incentives in terms of treasure in order to entice players to go there. But even then, many players simply prefer the path of least resistance, and are willing to put up with crowding rather than confront the extra challenge.

Also, to answer another post in a similar thread, the reason I wasn't in Paw is quite simple: I wanted to avoid the crowds. With the zone changed, there were a ton of people there (expected), and I (and my guild) tend to hang out in the less crowded, more difficult zones.

I do look forward to heading to Paw soon, though. "

Gordon Wrinn on Moving Characters Between Servers
by John - 12/14/99 09:18 EST
People have been asking to move their characters to different servers almost since the game was released. Here is what Gordon Wrinn, Verant PR Guru, had to say on the subject:

"I certainly understand where you are coming from. There are many dynamics involved in moving characters, with or without equipment, and we've discussed both. If we allow them to move with equipment, we run the risk of messing up the economy of the destination server. If we allow them to move without equipment, we run the risk of messing up the economy of the old server (as they give away all their gear).

In addition, there is also the subject of unique names.

These are not necessarily insurmountable problems, and it's possible that it might be tackled in the future. However we'll have to see how our resources look after the expansion. "

-Gordon

Dec. 8th Patch Message
by John - 12/08/99 12:14 EST
December 8th, 7:00am
--------------------

The following general enhancements have been introduced with this patch.

- /Pet No Taunt and /pet taunt now works
- Bard Levitation is now working as intended.
- A number of spears are now available to the Shaman class.
- Dazzle and Entrance (enchanter) have had their casting times reduced.
- A number of NPC guards have received an increase in stats.
- Screaming Terror is now functioning as intended.

*Zone Specific Enhancements*

As previously mentioned, Paw becoming a mid-level dungeon is imminent. So heed our earlier warning!

Both Fear and Hate planes have been enhanced, in terms of how often loot is dropped by NPCs.

*Race Enhancements*

Barbarians, Ogres and Trolls may now slam magic mobs when equipped with magic shoulders.
