Customers Versus Verant
The following reader submitted letter was sent to me in response to the article posted here.  My comments are in italics.   I have not corrected spelling or grammar.

Aaronica's Comments

Greetings

After reading your editorial on the subject of "Verant Vs. Players" I'd just like to make a few comments, and I hope they are of the constructive sort.

On the issue of lag tendencies within the game:   

The other night, as we were enjoying another evening of gameplay, Verant decided that there were some issues that needed to be remedied within the game immediately, so they began to take down servers. As each server (or two, as they began to do later on) was taken down, my husband would go linkdead. Then, he'd have to attempt to log back in.  Each time a serer would go down, this would happen. So, he decided to go to the chat and ask if anyone knew that this was occurring. There were at least 15 people in the one chat room complaining of the same problem. Yet, no one would acknowledge it.  We wrote Verant, they say... it's not our problem, it's your ISP.  I can see that it could be the ISP to a point, but this continued all night, as they took down EACH server...so we left, and went out instead of playing a game that WE pay for.

[Ed: I cannot understand how either an ISP or Verant could be causing this problem.  I suppose an influx of people trying to login to a server that was down could affect the chat/login server, but unless you were zoning I'm not sure how it would affect you.  If your husband went linkdead and not you then I tend to believe that it was something on your husbands machine that was causing the problem.  Strange indeed.]

Lag on the server is a rampant issue..it isn't getting better, and as they increase the servers, it is not decreasing either the population on the current servers, or the lag we experience.  I play with a cable modem, my husband does not.  When I get bad lag... I know it's not my ISP. 

[Ed: Your logic is a bit faulty here.   Cable modems are frequently lagged if the node you are on is saturated.  This can happen easily.]

What bothers me is that Verant seems to want to decide to lay off all blame on themselves, and say it is the customer, or they just ignore the issue all together.  Yes, some zones are far worse than others as far as lag goes, but you can see the server side lag.  One solution to this problem is to not put up a slew of new servers, but to create different routers.  Right now, as far as I understand, everyone, all 50,000 (or more now) of us is being routed through one server.  That's rather pushing it.  I can't imagine how bad the lag is for some of the people who play in places as far away as Germany, or Australia or France!  Sometimes the lag is so bad for me, I just give up!  

[Ed: I may misunderstand your point.  The Verant world servers are situated on different backbones.  To my knowledge there is only one login server.  I believe that each time you zone you must connect to that server.  If that is the problem you are pointing out I agree it can be annoying to wait to zone.  However, most zoning time is machine based and not login server connection based.  A fast machine zones much quicker than a slow one for instance.]

If they know they have a log-in server problem, why don't they fix it?  Many of the problems that we, as customers feel they have, they won't bother to fix because they probably feel that we won't wait for them to fix it, and would complain about there being more down time.  If more down time in the short run would be better game play in the long run, I for one would sit and wait.

On the issue of overcrowding within the game:   

The other night I was in Oasis of Marr, probably one of THE most used zones within all the game.  Thankfully, I was just running through, but I decided to see just how many players were in the zone.  All said and done... 115 people.  I just laughed to myself, and wondered how ANYONE was accomplishing anything. Granted, it can be done, and I hunted there myself when things were around the same level of overcrowding, but I am not even playing on Fennin Ro, or one of the other completely over used servers.  The server I do play on usually runs about 1900 per a night.  I would like to comment on the number that you posted... 1800 per a server. I found it interesting, as in the published information before the game became such a huge success, was 1000-1500 MAX on a server. 

[Ed: I have never seen those numbers published.  Where are they? I'd love to include that reference in my article.]

I think it's almost funny that now they wish to push the number to 1800.  Oh well. I can't stop them from allowing 3000 people on a server, but I would stop playing. You are right on so many points.  role-playing, although I am an avid RPer, even in EQ, doesn't happen, and you don't get to advance any faster by RPing. But I would stop playing if the game lost all fun. Right now the camping is so rampant, it's pathetic. People Kill Steal left and right because no one wants to venture to the "hard" zones.  To me, the game limit should be at 1500, and I am one of the supporters for a cap in the game.  Even though you aren't.... here is why. 

I know for a fact that all of my friends that play the game have many characters on many servers.  If there was a game cap put into place, I would just go to another server. I don't have characters around for nothing. I have characters around I enjoy playing.  Yes, perhaps I wouldn't get to play with John and Chris one night, but I could definitely make other friends.  We had to make friends to begin with, you can make some more...and I for one do believe that the game play would be improved with a limit cap in place.  But, that's just my opinion.

[Ed: I have heard many people voice the same argument.  Here is why I think it won't work.  I may be wrong. =)  

Though you have characters that you enjoy on many servers, I am guessing that most people do not.   EQ is a very time sensitive game.  You must really play for 2 or 3 hours to make any noticeable progress.  If I was forced to spend an hour finding a good group my play time is reduced too much for my liking.   The more compelling scenario however, is this, what happens when all servers reach their cap and you cannot login at all?   The average number of players is over 1500 already.  Five new servers added yesterday wouldn't even bring numbers down that low.]

I for one use the unused zones to hunt in. It's the only way I can get anything accomplished on my server.  I would love to be able to sit out in a zone that is open, and easy to use...but I can't.  With over 100 people in Easter Commons, and 75 in Western Commons daily on our server.. it's just pathetic.

Verant, in my opinion, could most definitely afford to place other routers up to decrease log in problems, and to decrease lag within the game.  Maybe some don't see different routers as a solution, but I do.

[Ed: I guess I just don't understand what you mean by router.  You are not referring to it in the hardware routing packets sense, or at least I don't think you are.  I, for one, do not have login problems.  I do have server crowding crowding problems though. I apolgize for not grasping your idea.]

This may sound pompous, but as far as the class imbalance (and I play an enchanter for my main character... I could go on and on about being "weak" compared to all the other casters... :) )  if you are going to complain about it, play another class.  We all know there are problems with the classes... but in one way, that is what makes the game so interesting.  If you want to power level, everyone knows play a druid (I HATE DRUIDS! stop killing me :P) if you want a challenge, well my friend, I would definitely suggest an Enchanter or a Rogue.  There are a lot of classes to chose from, and until Verant fixes itself, play another one if you are that unhappy with your current one.  I for one would rather see all the other problems fixed before this one, because to me, this is the least of them.

There are  many, many more issues to cover, but I've gone on for long enough.  In general, I want Verant to KNOW it has problems. To KNOW that the customers are NOT satisfied, and most are really unhappy.  Their customer service base is very, VERY lacking... would it kill them to get a 1-800 number? I know they are making a ton of money off of us!  They don't have emails for complaints in genearl, and whenever you have a problem, not only are you paying for the lack of ability to play, you have to pay for a long distance phone call as well?? ugh.  They need to get organized. They wanted to be the best out there, they got it...they have the awards for all the "Best ofs..." out there, now it's time to do what you can to KEEP THEM!  Start paying attention to the customer, and as the old addage goes.... "the customer is always right!"

[Ed: I have to agree that the customer service is lacking. Imagine the idiotic calls they would get to a 1-800 number though.  Customer service is a topic I will touch on in another article.  Oh, the customer is not always right. Especially when there are 170,000 of them all screaming for something different. =) ]
