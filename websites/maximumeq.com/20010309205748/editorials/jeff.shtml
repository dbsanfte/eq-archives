Customers Versus Verant
The following reader submitted letter was sent to me in response to the article posted here.  My comments are in italics.   I have not corrected spelling or grammar.

Jeff's Comments

I do like your editorial very much. It is well thought out and breaks out two major key points very well. I do have a couple of points/comments I would like to make regarding this editorial.

I agree that one problem with the amount of people on the server is that some zones are heavily populated while others are left with only a few people. One reason I have seen for so many people to be in a zone is auctioning. This is especially true in a few zones such as Freeport, Greater Faydark and Qeynos to name a few. I recently saw a discussion taking place about the possibility of adding a zone dedicated to trading. I think this is a wonderful idea, create an auction zone. Maybe have vendors there selling all products, including crafts items, each of the items needed to practice crafts and a bank. Maybe have it set up like a bazaar to give it atmosphere. That way those guilds that want to hold a bazaar or fair of some sort have a place to hold it, where people go to buy and sell things anyway. I am not sure where it could be put, since travelling across the continent has now become all but impossible for newer characters. Maybe put it off Freeport, since that is supposed to be the center of everything anyway. I think this would alleviate a lot of population issues, especially in the newbie zones.

[Ed: I believe that an 'auction zone' would alleviate the problems that occur when a guild runs an bazaar.  Those problems include increased lag, zone crashes, etc.  I think that people use Greater Faydark, Oasis, and like zones for auctioning because they are crowded to begin with.  If Lesser Faydark was crowded I am sure people would go there to auction. I think that people would still go to Oasis and Greater Faydark to auction even with an 'auction zone' in place.  The main reason? They can hunt while they sell or wait for that rare item to be auctioned.

If my idea of making zones more even in respect to experience and loot can be implemented (and it is very difficult) auctioning will be spread a bit thin perhaps. How could this be remedied?]

As for the problem with cheating, I am not a Verant programmer so do not know how difficult it would be to modify the way that EverQuest runs. From other programming experiences though, it seems that such a core programming change would be difficult to implement. I am sure that if Verant is serious about discouraging cheaters then they have people currently working on a way to stop them problems with these hack programs.

[Ed: I am sure that they do.  I hope they can succeed.]

I would definitely like to read some more editorials just to find out others opinions on a variety of other topics, especially class balancing, quests, and other similar topics.

[Ed: Class balancing may be next.  Thanks for the letter Jeff.  We appreciate the input.]
