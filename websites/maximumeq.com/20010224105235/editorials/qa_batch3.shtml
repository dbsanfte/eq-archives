This is part of a series of questions that originated on the Gameplay forum on the Official EverQuest web site directed at Brad McQuaid on Sunday April 2, 2000. Brad offered to try and answer as many of them as he could.

Brad, being a longtime RPGer yourself, do you REALLY believe that understanding the basics of stats (not even exact formulas) reduce the appeal and longevity of an RPG? I understand you don't want players to concentrate on the numbers, but this "wall of silence" regarding even general questions is taking things too far.

**Yes, I really do think that. When I played AD&D the DM tried very hard to keep us from hard numbers and such, opting instead to try to create a more immersive experience. And it worked.

Thought you were going to OSI. When will that happen and who will carry on the Vision?

**That was an April Fools joke put on by one of the websites I think. I am extremely happy at Verant and plan on staying here for a very long time.

Are you ever going to embrace Open GL?

**We already are with our newer engines (Sovereign, for example). Back when we were working on the EQ engine, though, it just didn't make sense - Glide and D3D covered all the bases. That's still true to an extent, and we don't plan on adding Open GL support to the EQ engine.

Currently an INT of 200 for a caster is the ceiling for purposes of adding via multiplier to your mana pool. Will this limit of 200 go higher in RoK?

**Can't say yet, sorry.

I would like to know what you mean by balance. (I personally think that any class should achieve the same goal by different ways, either as fighter in close combat or as caster with magic)

**I think what we mean by 'balance' could also be turned into a detailed essay - there's just so much to the topic. In a nutshell, we want all race/class combos to be appealing, but not necessarily exactly appealing. We don't want gross variations in how long it takes a character to advance, or to obtain things. We want decisions on how to play EQ to be as much subjective as objective.

Are there any plans to enhance the ability of PCs to essentially "take-over" zones or cities to a greater extent than already possible? If not, why?

**We really don't have the game mechanics or zone makeup in place to handle a 'take-over' as you describe.

What was the original vision of Warriors, specifically, tanks in general? How well do you feel you've met that vision?

**You can read the class descriptions on www.everquest.com. Essentially, they're supposed to have the best weapons, the most HP, and be the best tanks. I'm pretty happy with Warriors right now.

How do you foresee the role a tank (warrior specifically again) will play in the expansion lands/levels?

**Pretty much the same overall as the rest of the game.

Can you state your opinion on not allowing binding in cities by NPC's (say the POD who does nothing, but stand around on blue servers) As servers become higher level, one can waste not only minutes, but hours waiting for a bind in small (Rivervale).

**Waiting that long has not been my experience in-game - I can usually find a bind very quickly. In any case, relying on other classes is part of the interdependence we designed into EQ. Needing an actual character to bind you is important because you have to go out and talk to people, ask them favors, etc. We feel this supports an environment that fosters making friends and alliances with other players.

Hehehehe, I think you got more than you intended to get, eh?

**Yes and no. I figured there would be quite a few questions asked, but you guys never cease to amaze me

Anyway, is the Bard song Kellin's Lucid Lullaby ever intended to be fixed? It currently has a 3 second duration and a roughly 60% resist rate. Even those that are in favor of it the way it is admit that it requires a dedicated healer to keep the Bard alive while using it. One resist causes the monster to go berserk on the bard and instantly interrupt the song, and then the Bard is more or less dead because all the monsters are
going to be un-mesmerized in three seconds.

**Don't know, sorry. That's definitely a GZ question.

Also, is it intended that Bards don't get any magical instruments until mid 30s or higher? If not, please examine the quests that give the unfound/missing instruments, they may be broken.

**I'll ask GZ.

Abashi said players don't get indoor sow or selo's or any kind of speed buff because in beta they caused a lot of trains. How about Mobs not getting indoor sow etc? IMO sow'd mobs cause more trains then any sow'd players. If it is just to prevent trains please remove the ability of mobs to cast sow indoors.

**I agree with Abashi, however that's not the biggest reason we don't allow SOW indoors. The biggest reason is SOW and similar spells were originally intended to only help with travel. They were not meant for kiting and such. Since dungeons, relative to outdoor zones, are much smaller, we designed it such that 'travel' spells wouldn't work there.

How about adding 3DNow optimization?

**It's already in - the engine uses 3dNow instructions.

Is it within the vision to ignore all bard posts/concerns except the least important for two months straight? So far we still don't have any class specific weapons and only two non-planar, non-dragon instruments that have an effect (and one is from Kedge and extremely rare).

**I'll ask Abashi why he hasn't responded to 'bard posts' if indeed truly he hasn't.

Will there EVER be a "/who guild " to list all the members of a guild currently online (and displaying tags)? I think this would be a great tool for guild leaders and officers for organizing events. Heck, you may already have something similar implemented. If this is the case, please excuse this waste of time

**You can already get the listing for some guilds by using /who all . Obviously, this method is sub-optimal and doesn't always work. Adding better who/guild functionality is on the programming list of things to do.

Any plans on adding an NPC that can reduce the weight of shadow Knight Quest Armor? It seems that this armor was designed with only Troll and Ogre Sk's in mind.

**I'll bring the idea up.

Please comment on Trade skills and what you had in mind when you made them.

**They're there to offer players an alternative to adventuring, and they're also there to bolster the player-driven economy, create item and money sinks, etc.

Any plans to make baked food last longer than store bought or even add some stat points?( i.e. str bars , mana crunchies etc.)

**Unfortunately, probably not, at least not short term. We'd really like to have food with differing strengths and durations, and had planned that to be the case before launch. Some programming directions we went in, however, made it such that implementing varying food durations and strengths a very complex task and so I don't see this happening.

What good is a high alcohol tolerance?

**Alcohol Tolerance is a trade skill that's really there purely for RP. There's no practical purpose to increasing it.

If EverQuest is a grouping game, with a certain soloing ability, why is it the strong pet classes (Necros, magicians) are given the ability to both tank and use powerful magic? I do not understand why meleers have to resort to casters to keep them alive, but the opposite is not true. You must be aware the power of pets at higher level, isn't a pet a last line of protection? A servant? why is it given the power equivalent to some of the principal melee classes? In hp, ac and attack?

**I have to disagree with your assertion that melee classes cannot solo - some can. That said, some mage classes are just as good at soloing, probably better.

Has the clip plane slider bug been identified and if not, what information do you need to identify and fix it? Bug: Increasing the slider between the midpoint to the extreme right in outdoor non-forested zone does not increase visibility distance. It used to work.

**No, internal testing and the programmer involved are still looking into it. I appreciate the information.

I don't want to get too much into game mechanics, but it just seems odd that the entire FOCUS of the game mechanics (grouping up to take on more powerful critters) impacts some classes so negatively.

**There are definitely some classes that need to group moreso than others. This is by design.

When is druid armor being put in and why isn't it in already?

**I don't think we ever promised druid armor per se. I think it is safe to say, however, that more class specific quests and items are on their way in, especially with the Expansion.

Any plan to increase the number of spell slots? 8 slots to memorize spells is not enough.

**The 8 slot limit is there so one has to use tactics depending upon the situation and upcoming battle, deciding which spells to memorize and which spells not to.

Will you ever shorten the time it takes between starting the EQ.exe file and entering the world of Norrath?

**We have a few times, and if any additional ways pop up where we can optimize things, we will. At this point, though, I don't know of any ways we haven't thought of. WARNING: the zones in the Expansion are much larger than previous zones and slower machines with less RAM will take a while to load them - zoning time in general will be going up with Kunark, not down.

Will more items be added to the Smithing skill? Are there items that can be smithed now that PC's are unaware of?

**Not sure if there are any items players are unaware of, I'll try to ask. As for trade skill upgrades, they're definitely coming.

Why are mobs not given missile weapons? Seems to me that they way to fix all kinds of problems you guys are having is to make it possible for mobs to attack from a distance if they are not casters. Kiting isn't a problem when a mob stops and crit hits yah from behind with a bow for backstab-like damage (no agi bonus either.) Centaurs have the ability already, there damage just sucks like a Ranger's does...

**Some mobs DO have missile weapons. Are you suggesting that more should, and that we should increase the damage they can do with ranged weapons?

What do you think is the most important thing (as a programmer/game designer) you've learned designing and maintaining EverQuest?

**That a lot more players than I ever thought would follow the path of least resistance and play the game the absolute easiest and most efficient way possible. I would have thought, for example, that players would embrace a slightly harder dungeon with slightly less appealing loot perhaps slightly farther away to avoid crowds. Boy was I wrong.

If you could change anything about the way things turned out, or the way something happened along the way to where EQ is now, what would that something be?

**There are quite a few areas where EQ failed in a sense, even though we're extremely pleased with the game overall, are damn proud, and consider it a huge success. I guess the biggest one is that people don't 'do' dungeons as much as they 'camp' single locations within a dungeon. I wish we could go back and radically revise the way dungeon spawning works.

Some time ago, you mentioned that you were going to try to get a "history" put together for player consumption. It would be nice to have that for "fitting into" the world we are supposed to be role-playing in. Any status?

**Good question. I believe Abashi was supposed to be working on this - I'll ask him about it in the morning.

What is the "vision" for each of the character classes - a "sound bite" that advertises the essence of the class?

**Please see the class descriptions on www.everquest.com -- for the most part they're pretty accurate and sum up the classes fairly well (boy, gonna get flamed for this one &grin)

Are there any areas of the game, in zones which have been around a while, players have yet to discover?
Are there any creatures in the game players have yet to see?

**Hrm. I doubt it. None spring to my mind, anyway.

Are there any plans to expand group features in the game? For certain activities, i.e. Planes and Dragon runs, 20+ people are required. This causes issues with communication and looting rights. Maybe maximum group size could be expanded in certain situations?

**We've tossed around the idea of linking groups together, enabling better communication. No short term plans to do this, but we like the idea.

Any chance certain items in game will be made rechargeable?

**Aren't some already? If not, there will be some in the future.

I know you must hate this one, but: Could a concise definition of all Attributes (str, stam, agi, dex, cha, int, wis) please be given?

**No, I won't get more precise than we already have, multiple times, sorry.

Is some attempt made to mathematically quantify the relative usefulness or uselessness of spells or abilities that don't directly affect combat, or are classes balanced more "by feeling"? In other words: Would you call class balancing more of an Art or a Science?

**Both. We have internal programs we run to test ideas out, weapons, spells, etc. We do a lot of math to make sure certain ideas are balanced. But there are just as many times that we make decisions based on the Vision(TM) of what we think should happen for subjective reasons.

The vision is frequently cited as the reason for making changes in the game. Do you have a Vision statement for EQ that is a detailed, written statement? No sound-bites, but a "position statement" of sorts that is used as a guiding principle for development? If not, would you consider writing one and publishing it?

**Yes, we have internal documents that talk about the vision, or mission statement, behind EQ and our other games. I don't think we want to publish internal documents, but if there is really a demand, perhaps I'll write an essay on the Vision(TM) one of these days.

Could you add a /broken command to enhance the current /bug command? This new option would allow people to "report" and EverQuest to "compile" broken quests ...(like the assassin in Qeynos not dropping [black wood chips] broken since beta)... and cosmetic stuff like typos without the information being hidden in bug reports.

**We have an email address (which escapes me at the moment - doh!) that we'd prefer players use to report problems with quests. Abashi, if you're reading this, please re-post the quest help email addy.

Could the language skill be used to enhance the taunt skill? You would, for example, get a bonus to your taunt skill if you use your opponent's language (after achieving a certain skill level). Running with this, could people learn language skills from the creatures they are fighting.

**Sweet idea - I'd never heard that one before. We'll toss it around.

It's been said many times that one of the reasons druids don't have quest armor is that we run around in wolf form once we hit 24. What if the armor gave us different tints for our animal form or, better yet, allow us to have different forms. I know that all animal forms are not in each zone, but what if the animal form was a random polygon from the zone's existing animal table.

**Actually, the reason you don't have quest armor is that the quest armor was supposed to make up for the fact that we removed Rubicite armor from the game, and druids couldn't wear Rubicite in the first place (in other words, we put in quest armor for those classes that COULD wear Rubicite).

Why doesn't EQ have a quick quit? (not /quit which requires me to click again and again, exit, yes I'm sure, exit, yes I'm sure) I have a time limit on my ISP and like to try and reconnect real fast.

**Good question. I'll ask the programmers how feasible it would be. Like /quit, though, I don't think it would generally be a good idea to use, in, that you'd remain in the world a bit after using the command, subject to attacks.

Why do we have to click agree (to the disclaimer) each time when log in?

**To protect us legally.

Why are there 4 splash pages to log in? We all know who made the game after all, wouldn't one page suffice?

**When you have multiple companies involved in the creation of the game, they pretty much all want splash pages shown all the time. Honestly, though, I just click ESC really fast, 3 times, and it doesn't bug me.

Do you believe that the lack of non-durable items in the game is a key factor in the need for "money sinks"?

**Yes, but I don't think that's necessarily a bad thing. We want most items to be durable, and now that we've added some quests that consume items, other consumable items (potions and such), as well as other money sinks, we feel the economy is in pretty good shape. I don't think there's any real need to make items wear out in general.

The Shaman's Alchemy and the Rogue's Poison skills appear to be the only "unbroken" trade skills in the game. The products do not stay around forever and create a supply glut in the long run because the are "consumable."

**I do agree that as we continue to update Trade Skills in the game they need to involve more and more consumables. We should have made it that way in the beginning, of course, and as you've noted, Alchemy and Poison, both of which have been updated significantly since launch, are based on the consumable principle. I think it's safe to say future revisions of many other trade skills will be handled similarly.

Why do quests give so little experience? Abashi stated that the reason for this is that Verant doesn't want players repeatedly doing quests for experience. I just do not understand this position. Aside from the name, why is an endless period of "camping a single mob" superior to completing a non-trivial quest?

**Many quests do give out decent experience (they didn't always, be we updated many of them several months back). The problem is that it's hard to control who is doing the quest and then to award 'proper' experience for the character, depending on his or her level. I guess this is a deficiency in the quest system, but then putting in quest level limits isn't something we're too keen on doing.

The majority of the players (over 70%) now under the Equipment Loot system (reference the risk poll Gordon initiated on PvP servers) overwhelmingly prefer Exp loss in PvP to Item Loss in PvP, why is Exp loss NOT being considered as a Valid option in the next patch or "ever" for that matter?

**I think Gordon still has plans for making PvP even more viable, but most of the solutions require quite a bit of coding, and so they have to compete with other enhancements.

Planes armor is all no drop lore, however in Fear the Shaman and Cleric belt are just lore and droppable, why do you allow armor or weapons droppable at all from the planes?

**No clue - I'll ask.

Overcrowding is a huge issue on the older servers even with Kunark many servers will still be overcrowded do you plan on bringing more servers up when the expansion goes online? I'm not talking one server every two months but more servers to help allow more gaming time w/o the pressures of crowding

**We plan on launching four more servers short term. As soon as we have fairly solid dates, we'll try to announce them.

Are the mage spells posted on castersrealm and the like really real? Or are they just some joke like the early April fools thing. (I've heard the answer about not believing that stuff but I want to know if its truth.

**I don't know which post you're referring to, sorry.

Are there any plans to change the mage's phantom armor line so it's useful?

**Don't know, sorry.

Since rogues have crit with thrown weapon and Rangers have crit with bow, can Paladins and ShadowKnights have crit with bash?

**I'll bring it up at the tuning meetings, but I doubt it - we're fairly happy with where Paladins and SKs are at right now.

Was wondering why the armor in EQ is not damage absorbing? My reason for asking is that this would help differentiate the weapons (i.e. pierce does the class armor piercing damage). Also would allow you to diversify the classes more.

**It would be great to have different types of armor that responded differently to different types of weapons. Perhaps one day in the future we could implement something like that.

Will hybrids ever get unique upgrades/abilities, or just pull off their parent classes? Will we see new spells and skills in Kunark?

**Being hybrids of other classes, I doubt they'll get unique spells, other than the traditional special abilities some of them already have (lay hands, harm touch).

Would you consider switching the character development of melee classes to getting more different skills at more levels as opposed to getting new and better gear?

**Funny you should ask that… we're doing something very similar to what you're asking for with the Expansion for the pure melee classes.

How do I go about getting a job on the dev team?

**Check out www.verant.com for information on how and to whom to send in resumes.

Will there be anything for players below level 50 in the expansion other than new areas and items? Spells, skills, etc.

**No, sorry.

Are there any plans for more of a symbiotic link between the Magician and their pet, (especially control-wise)? It'd be nice if it actually followed me when I used /pet follow me.

**No plans, but it's a neat idea.

Do you ever intend to address the spawn in Plane of Fear and the safe spot inside the zone to prevent farming of items?

**Probably, yes. We'd like to address that issue in some capacity.

You said: **To preserve a sense of mystery and exploration in the game. Ok, so isn't this sort of a catch 22? I can't know what to put stats into a character creation until I explore the game, but I can't explore the game until I have created my character.

**Yes, by design. You're supposed to start out with a character and learn and explore both the world and the game mechanics. That may mean at some point you want to start another character to better use the knowledge you have gained playing the game previously.

Why does Verant think that spending 8 minutes meditation or spending an equal amount of time for health regeneration fun?

**Downtime between battles is pretty traditional. It allows players to talk, re-group, cast buffs and other related spells, head to the restroom, etc. That said, I wish there was more to do during downtime, more gameplay involved.

Are you doing this (responding to all these questions) because of the recent deal with Lucasarts and because of other online games coming up soon? OSI wasn't very attentive to its customers until a game called EverQuest started beta. Coincidence?

**Yes, coincidence. I'm not aware of any other MMORPG coming out in the near future, are you? Is it even remotely possible I'm doing this because I care or must there always be some hidden motive (greed, fear, or some other nasty emotion)? I explained why I'm doing this in an earlier batch of answers - take it or leave it.

Instead of randomizing loot, which doesn't make sense as you've said, the fire giant warlord crown SHOULD drop from the Fire giant warlord, why don't you randomize more spawns?
For example instead of the Goblin Knight who drops the BIBS spawning in the same place all the time, have him spawn in a random Dazed Goblin guard spawn point. This would encourage "doing" a dungeon instead of camping the spawn point.

**Agreed, at least to a point. You'll see more of this experimented with in the Expansion zones.

One of the great draws of a graphical mud is to see your character evolve and develop graphically as well as statistically. Why is it that most classes have a huge range of robes/armors that change their look, while Monks and Druids must look like "newbies" for a long, long time?

**There is a limitation of texture memory (which used to be much more restrictive, btw, but recently 3D cards have been shipping with a LOT of memory, thank God), so we were limited as to how many texture sets we could put in the game for player characters. We were able to get around this limitation to a point by using 'tinting' which allowed us to tint a single texture set multiple colors without affecting texture memory. The more uniform the texture, the less 'bad' it looked when tinted. Monks, having lots of skin showing, are the least tintable without things looking bad. Anyway, in the future I hope we have the resources and time to add more texture sets - heck , you can never have enough variation in a game like this, I agree.

You have said that there are many planned fixes to a lot of this stuff. Would you ever consider posting a list of planned fixes in advance? Not a timetable, people will ***** if you don't do it on time, but a priority list so people know what is being worked on in the future and don't whine so much?

**We have an 'In Development' section on www.everquest.com that we just haven't been using. That's definitely our bad - sorry about that. Part of the EQ Live team's responsibility post-Expansion will be to keep that page up to date.

Why did you make the role of the magician and wizard so similar?

**We did? I have to respectfully disagree on this one.

With many of the characters 'getting married,' will you ever implement instead of the 'pet' that follows the character around, an offspring?

**Hrm. Involving 'children' as player characters or even NPCs at this point in MMORPGs… well, we're afraid to, honestly.

Enchanters get the instill mana line fairly recently and it has proven to be useless (with the exception of Wu's gauntlets). Are there any plans to add more items for the skills (in a gradual process) or will everything be added at one time?

**Definitely a GZ question there, sorry.

This might be a question for GZ, but why don't enchanters get a level 49 illusion? Also, will enchanters get any illusions after level 50?

**Another GZ question.

Has the idea of houses in the game completely vanished or is it still planned for the future?

**It's completely vanished, at least for this incarnation of EverQuest.

I've noticed that a few of the questions you have answered indicated that the fix or change to the bug and or enhancement would be implemented via the Expansion. For those people who do not buy the Expansion, how will these changes be made or will they not be made?

**The vast majority of global fixes will be available to everyone. More specifically, many of these fixes will be made in a global file everyone will get when we launch the Expansion.

Any chance of giving Cleric's a bash skill when they equip a shield?

**Doubt it - they're supposed to be a limited melee class and primarily casters.

Why no Halfling Rangers? With warriors and druids in the race.... it would also help with a guild closer to East Antonica...

**That's a good question. I'm pretty sure we discussed this years back, but it's hard to remember. Essentially we felt (and feel) that the number of races that could be certain hybrid classes needed to be limited. Also, traditionally, we just couldn't come up with an image in our mind of a ranger Halfling

Will Verant ever look at locating some servers overseas in the future? Has the feasibility of this been looked at? Being an Aussie I'm just curious.

**Very unlikely. The way the EQ infrastructure has turned out it's much easier for us to administrate when servers are local. In any case, with EQ being as latency tolerant as it is, and with ping times not being a big issues in terms of gameplay, we haven't really felt the need to host servers remotely.

EQ isn't really a role-playing game is it? It's a game based on the principles of addiction outlined in current psychological doctrine. It's based on positive reward and positive punishment. Pavlov's Dog, so to speak. It's designed to keep people addicted for as long as possible. That is the vision, isn't it?

**Heh, um, no. It's certainly designed to be addictive in the sense that any good game is. EQ has its heritage in all the great text MUDs of the past, many of which were very addictive and a hell of a lot of fun. Perhaps some of the early MUD pioneers were evil, scheming psychologists with a hidden motive , but we just wanted to make a damn fine graphical MUD that was fun to play for months, even years.

Why were the Jboots made more difficult to obtain in terms of camp time and level range when the reason you changed them to a quest was because of complaints of that nature?

**Actually, the reason we changed them from a campsite to a quest was because of the complaints of people fighting over a single campsite and the customer service issues that arose because of that.

Would it be more realistic if someone who has looted a timed-out guard's corpse received the same appointed faction loss as if they were to kill them?

**Well, we really don't want to tie looting in with faction hits, which is what that would require.

DO you honestly think gnome newbies should get away scott free with selling their own guards equipment to their own merchants?

**Heh, probably not, but there's no code in place to make merchants selective as to what they'll buy and what they won't. I wish there were.

-Brad
