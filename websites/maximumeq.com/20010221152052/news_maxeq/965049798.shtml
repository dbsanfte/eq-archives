Ranger damage output increase
by Chris - 07/14/00 11:03 EST
Yesterday, Abashi mentioned that they are working on increasing the damage output of Rangers in a thread that was about Rangers getting too much attention from mobs. Of course, this would only add to the present situation, so Abashi clarified what he meant.

Personally, I don't think this won't work, especially against summoning mobs.

>>>now correct me if I'm wrong, but wouldn't higher dmg output make it WORSE for us?
It depends on how we do it. In this case the plan is to enhance damage output from archery under certain conditions, giving rangers an incentive to back out of melee range for a few seconds.

If rangers can make a *big* hit from time to time at a greater delay rather than a *bunch* of smaller hits at a lesser delay, the desired affects might be obtainable.

Agro radius while sitting/looting
by Chris - 07/14/00 10:58 EST
I always thought it was a nice feature. I often have to wait to sit to med with my cleric if I stun a mob. Just part of the strategy I feel.

I recently found out that the Aggro Radius on MOBs was raised when a character is sitting down or looting a corpse. I find this absurd! A challenging game is funner than one with no challenge, but this is purely annoying. The game is already very challenging, but having the Aggro Radius go up when you are most vunerable is so annoying it is not even funny. I find it somewhat sadistic to leave the game this way. A player that is annoyed is not a happy player. Leave the game challenging but PLEASE take this feature out...
I've always felt that it was a nice touch. Creature "x" is too afraid of you to risk a conflict while you are alert, however decides that he might take a poke at you if you turn your back.
-Gordon


/con and invisiblility
by Chris - 07/14/00 10:56 EST
Although the post was directed for rogues, I think this is something important for everyone who uses invis (so pretty much everyone :) ).

Only creatures that have faction that determine what they will and will not attack give messages in /con indicating if they do or do not see you. Some things in the game will attack anyone, regardless of faction, and these things are always "scowling".
If you check hill giants while invis you'll see the same issue. It's working as designed.
