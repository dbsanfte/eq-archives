Caution about Veeshan's Peek
by Chris - 04/27/00 10:44 EST
I don't agree with this, but here it is.

Even if you die from a bug, get a corpse stuck in a wall, etc, you can't ask a GM for assistance in this very high level zone.

Just for a bit of clarification, back when the planes were brought online, John Smedley commented that people just don't die in planes, they die hard. Veeshan's Peak makes the planes look easy. There exists a non-negligible chance that not only will you die there, but you might not be able to raise the necessary forces to allow recovery of your corpse. It is *very* dangerous.
In order to avoid any appearance of impropriety in the zone, as sometimes follows when we've assisted in corpse recoveries in other zones due to claimed bug-deaths, we're not going to risk that for Veeshan's Peak. Veeshan's Peak is incredibly dangerous, and in order to guarantee that everyone is operating under the same set of conditions, we've elected to make the zone "enter at your own risk".

That said, there are no known bugs in the zone that would cause the death of a character, and it was tested extensively in the Ruins of Kunark beta. We will certainly investigate and eliminate any bugs that are reported, but we won't be reimbursing items or experience, nor will we be moving corpses or people within the zone.

-Gordon

>>>Should you not put this in a patch message?

I don't want to put up a patch message that "forces" them to view the info. I expect that the info will spread to those concerned anecdotally prior to anyone entering the zone. There is also sufficient in-character warning in-game about this as well.

I’ll give it some more thought though.

Backstabbing Pets
by Chris - 04/26/00 09:51 EST
I could always rely on Garn to take a beating. I guess if Garn becomes a wizard on me, I'll have to watch his hit points a little better :).

Up until the level 50+ pet spells, all pets were based off of warriors. We now have the ability to base pets off of other classes, and that's what's been done here. There are now rogue pets, wizard pets, and monk pets, which represent additional abilities for the pet-users above 50th level.

Rogues need not worry that a backstabbing pet somehow reduces the desirability of their class. First, the pet does not have the backstab skill that a rogue of that level would have, so they won't hit as often nor will they do as much damage. Furthermore, they don't have all of the stat bonuses that increase the rogues effectiveness. Certainly they can be buffed up, but they'll never come close to having a real rogue. Finally, rogue pets will never have the ability to double-backstab as rogues do.

Rogue pets won't be picking locks either, and there are quite a few places to do that in Kunark.

-Gordon

Taunt explained
by Chris - 04/26/00 09:35 EST
Interesting, non-tauntable monsters.

The skill level associated with taunt interacts with your level to determine how likely the taunt is to be successful. It does not control the level of success. A successful taunt will *always* make the creature shift to you, assuming that it can be taunted (Yes, some creatures are untauntable).
Taunt makes a creature hate you just a little bit more than it hates the next guy on the list. In other words, multiple successful taunts, or taunting a creature that is already attacking you doesn't do anything extra.

I hope that clears up how Taunt works.

Warrior HP after 50
by Chris - 04/26/00 09:31 EST
What Gordon says makes sense, but we needed someone to explain it to us. Nobody has exactly made level 54 yet :).

Number of hitpoints gained per level is assigned on the basis of race, class, and a multiplier based upon level. The multiplier increases in steps every X (where X is a value based upon race and class) levels. When someone hits a level where the value of X is increased above that of the previous level, the character will experience a HP "pop" over that that they usually receive. This in essence "makes up" for all the extra hitpoints they would have received if the multiplier was that way all along.
The Palys, SKs, and Bards all hit a step of that sort at 51, causing some of them to get upwards of 100 HP for the level. This doesn't mean that they are getting 100HP per level, just that for that level the number of hitpoints received "made up" for the hitpoints they would have gotten if the multiplier had always been at that level.

Warriors operate on a different schedule than SKs and Palys, but the curve will always favor them. For instance, warriors should see a fairly substantial hitpoint pop when they hit 54.

It's important to remember that 51 to 60 is not considered a "new" game. What I mean by this is that algorithms that take level into account and improve something every Y levels don't start over at 50+, they continue from where they left off. For instance, if someone has an attribute that improves in effectiveness every 4 levels, and his last improvement was at level 48, he won't see the next one until 52. This might make it appear that someone else who gets an improvement every 10 levels, and got their last improvement at 41, and gets their next one at 51 somehow gets something before the first guy. It's not the case, it's just the way that the math works.

I hope I haven't confused the issue further

-Gordon

Virus in .doc EQ files
by Chris - 04/25/00 11:39 EST
It might be a good idea to scan your HD now. I had a few readers tell me that their virus scan software picked it up, but I thought it was just older anit-virus software (old anti-virus software has picked up non-existant virii in the past).

It appears that we did patch two MS-Word '97 format Doc files that were infected with a relatively benign macro virus.

These viruses can only infect other files (and then only other Doc files) if they are opened in your word processor, so people who didn't open them prior to right now need not be alarmed. We at Verant proactively work to eliminate the possibility that things such as this happen, however some safeguards were unintentionally bypassed as party of the Kunark opening. We've closed this minor loophole and customers should not have to worry about anything such as this happening in the future.

The files have been cleaned and they will be replaced the next time everyone runs the "Kunark Patch" option on the patch server, which they should do prior to attempting to open either of those *.DOC files.
We would like to extend our apologies to everyone on this matter.
