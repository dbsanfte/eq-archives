Gordon comments on Cleric Nerfing
by Geoff - 11/11/00 15:17 EST
Not nerfed, he says, just "fixed"...

Lately, clerics have been the subject of an unending string of nerfs everything from MoK to Donals Arms.

We'll have new helmet models with Velious. That said, I don't think that the changes to Mark of Karn or Donals arms constitute any real "nerf" to the class. Both issues represented something that created untenable problems in game that had to be addressed.

You decreased the power on a cleric-only item and a cleric-only spell. I think by most people's definition that's a nerf.

If something goes in that's undoubtedly overpowered, I don't think that it's a "nerf" to repair it. While I'll accept people calling things that are borderline "nerfs", there are some things that are just common sense. If we put in a new wizard spell that's supposed to do 1600 damage, and someone accidently makes it do 16000, is it a nerf to fix it?

-Gordon


Alan comments of another confirmation box
by Geoff - 11/11/00 15:14 EST
Today's lesson: Always know what you are selling.

Arg. I just sold my staff of the observers, my most prized possession, to a vendor, instead of the cracked staff that I had intended. (That's what happens when you put down your staff to pick up your crude stein)... Ummm... could we get a confirmation box to help prevent this type of obviously WRONG sale?

I don't think we need another confirmation box.

The one you get for dropping things was added to prevent people (bards) from dropping things during a hectic combat. I really see no need for a confirmation box when selling. If you wish, just right click everything before you sell it, sort of like a personally generated confirmation box.

Alan
