EverQuest's Economy -- An Editorial

The economy is EverQuest is definately unique. According to Verant, it is a "player run economy". One of the main problems with the economy in EverQuest is that very little money or items leave the game, but lots enter it on a daily basis.

How many people have you heard of destroying that Short Sword of Ykesha, because they have something new and better?

Or how about someone spending a small fortune on a steel breastplate because it has slightly less weight than a bronze breastplate?

You won't, and you haven't.

As more money enters the game, it becomes worth less (not worthless, but it has almost reached that point). Very little is in the game to take money out. Almost everyone has seen it happen if they have been playing EverQuest for a long time.

Back in July '99, it was unheard of to see anyone selling bone chips, much less many people buying them. Now, a stack goes for between 3pp and 5pp.

Master Smiths used to be sought out for their banded armour. Now, the market is so flooded with bronze that it almost goes for less than what banded used to. Hardly anyone even wears banded anymore.

Every day, the supply of money and items is increased, yet the demand isn't rising. Prices are falling drastically. With prices falling so much, who wants to buy leather or steel from the vendor? This makes the problem worse, because the money that was once spend on armour and leaving the economy, isn't being spent anymore.

The "player run economy" is like a monopoly for the high level items however. Very specific spawns carry very specific items. The frenzied goblin knight is the only MOB in the game that drops the Blackened Iron Bastard sword. The Ghoul Savant is the only mob in the game to drop the Enameled Black Breastplate.

More players are obtaining high levels with their characters. To get the best equipment, you must visit the spawn and try and get into the group holding that room. If you were a warrior trying to obtain a Mithril 2-Handed Sword to upgrade from your BIBs, gone are the days that if you were the only person who needed it in the group, you would automatically get it.

Now, pure casters are rolling (the typical means of deciding who gets the uncommon and rare item drops, by rolling a magical set of dice) on melee class equipment and vice versa. Although sometimes the reason is sheer greed on the player's part, often it is so that the drop can be traded for what the character really needs.

There are several things that would help reduce and possibly eliminate these problems.

More "high level" items could be purchaseable from vendors. The Bag of the Tinkerers was an excellent step. What is required is more items such as this however to pull more money out of the economy.

There should also be alternative items for all levels. Currently, at high levels, the best 1H Slash weapon is a Short Sword of Ykesha. There isn't really an equally powerful 1H Slash that is obtainable at the same level. By adding items to the game that are equal in power, but different, player's have a choice. It would lower the price of the high end items so that they are obtainable through money, not just trade. Of course, then there is the risk of flooding the market with powerful weapons.

Putting rare drops on common mobs would also help a great deal. It would prevent "item farmers" who monopolize a spawn for that rare drop they want to sell off again and again. (ed. it would appear that Verant is doing just that in the Ruins of Kunark zones from several reports and rumours).

What it boils down to, is that money is worthless. A barter system has taken its place. Who wants to sell something for 5,000 platinum, when they already have 20,000 in the bank, with nothing to spend it on?
