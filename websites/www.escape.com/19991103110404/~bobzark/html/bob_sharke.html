THE QUESTION MARK KID



 he was a young dingleberry
who thought he was something smooth.
the question mark kid.

his thoughts, wild coyotes, prowled . . .
a bit awkward at 19,
his minds tomato squished . . .
wherever he would kick its rind,
bald and fat construction workers
mockingly called his name.

the question mark kid
wandered a barrio
between himself and others . . .
a curmudgeon on the exit ramp,
cutting round a nameless bend,
bleary-eyed, chock full of zens
peeled right off a silver screen . . .
the sand pebble luster of steve mcqueen . . .

 

HOT PINK SATIN PANTS
 

dippy little boy scouts hoping for a chance
hang around and talk about her
hot pink satin pants.
swimming in a sea of sin,
she can make their compass needles
pop right up and spin!

if anyone is standing there
she doesn't know and doesn't care . . .
while she looks askance
insect worlds are creeping toward her
hot pink satin pants.

 

RAW UNVARNISHED WRITING

 stealing from a feeling something
not even remotely attractive or appealing . . .
the very fleshes molting,
oozing pus and blood revolting . . .
lightning rods long from lighting . . .
symbolize the awkward silence bourgeois types prefer
raw unvarnished writing.

slumping down the page one finds
screwballs who are going blind
(meanings etched between the lines)
whose clumped up squalid revelations,
plundered shitty informations . . .
klowns dispersing silly string on the slanted sidewalks gather
those who insist on a san francisco mist,
round the plastic lawn chairs blather . . .
all of this purposeless,
of course it couldn't matter,
raw unvarnished writing.

grinches grinched in mini-skirts . . .
left and right they clutch and claw beneath a mournful tree . . .
mister munged up frankenstein bringing life to lifeless forms,
barely long enough to see
a squinch of peter lorre in defiance of the norms . . .
everythings coming to a head
wracked by what the brains turned up . . .
a flimsy whimsy looking stained . . .
rockets launching concepts he would rather have repressed . . .
reality by its collar squeezed and rendered
beyond the ping of consciousness . . .
who's the cat that won't cop out?
who knows what it's all about?
upside down schnabels he cannot stop igniting,
in his heart there's conflict,
on every side guerillas fighting
raw unvarnished writing.
