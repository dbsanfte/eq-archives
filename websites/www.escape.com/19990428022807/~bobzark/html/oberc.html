    Godamnit!  Get his camera!  You!  Yeah, you fuckhead!  Can't you read?  You got his camera?  Good.  Get the film.  You got any other film on you, fuckhead?  Search him.  What do you mean we don't have the right?  Shit, man, we got all kinds of rights.  And it's you that fucked up here, buddy.  Not us.  Jesus, signs all over the fucking place and still they take their pictures.  Are you under arrest?  Shit!  This fuckhead wants to know if he's under arrest!  What do you think fuckhead?  Any other film on him?  No?  Good.  Are you going to get your camera back?  Shit.  Hey look, I don't care how much that camera cost.  It's not my money, man.  And it wasn't me taking those goddamned pictures!  You want what?  A lawyer?  You want to know my name?  You aren't thinking, fuckhead!  Not thinking good at all.  People disappear in this town at night.  All the fucking time.  The last thing you want to do is make some fucking trouble.  Give him back his camera.  what do you mean you want your film back too?  Jesus, fuckhead, you're really getting on my nerves.  Now go on!  Get the hell out of here before I lose my temper!

SMILING

     He's in some kind of hurry.  Shoving, pushing, making his way through the packed crowd.  This is rush hour traffic.  Trains pull in, pull out, in only seconds flat.  People are saying things:  Hey buddy, what's the hurry?  Got a hot date or something?  Jesus, buddy, take it easy!  Then there's a scream.  A yell. A sound of sudden urgency.  He's got my purse!  Stop that guy!  He's got my godamned purse!  And we look at the woman.  And we look at the guy.  And we look at each other.  And we smile.  It's like a fucking miracle!  A ray of light landing on our heads!  A blessing from the heavens.  Because the fact of the matter is we can't do a thing.  And nobody wants to be a hero.  So he runs through the crowd.  People get out of his way.  Let him through.  Make an opening he can use.  And the woman yells so loud her voice bounces off the walls.  Stop him!  Somebody stop him before he gets away!  And we smile.  All of us together.  Like one big happy family.  And the guy fades into the crowd.  Starts to look like us.  And he's probably smiling too.  And the woman yells.  Stop!  Stop that man!  And she looks confused because he's gone and disappeared.  And she yells.  But there's no energy behind it.  And we smile and smile and smile while nobody does a thing.

dave leon
