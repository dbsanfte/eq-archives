PANTHERS LEAPING AT THE MOON

(for Fred Hampton, Apprentice Bunchie Carter)



Oakland with tree limbs
    reaching at throats,
entangling vocal chords
    in silent death.

  panthers running
       against the fog, light
    came through.
         Bad as in excellent
    up through the
        streets of Chicago along
 the roads of Detroit.
  
         breaking down the fences
     that held them captive,
strong teeth and paws
         lashing out at
 the nightsticks.

America called them
     animals.  We call them
     freedom fighters,
justice getters and
      griots kicking
 street knowledge
      and culture.
the police and
  state were the animals,
        sub-machine gun villains
    trying to keep us
        broken niggers forever.
 

by Willie Abraham Howard, Jr.
Atlanta, Georgia
USA
