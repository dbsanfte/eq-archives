THE QUESTION MARK KID



the question mark kid would go looking for america,
trying to stomach the unreality of it all,
thinking in the middle of a sterilized strip mall,
the only thing he ever wanted was to find himself
sliding down the bannister of dreams

and so he went at nineteen,
hitchhiking zoned out scenes,
a befuddled boy wonder muscling up exit ramps and lonesome loops of highway,
a smidge descended from james dean.
 

FOR THE UNDERGROUND CRAWLSPACE REVIEW

a limousine that flew out of control
flopped around a telephone pole,
shook pedestrians, left some dead.
the street was looking up blood red.
people said that limo driver he was not at fault,
another swerved and hit him.
made that limo jump around and come apart with a crunch,
from that flattened limo the driver got up very slow,
lost his front teeth, it so happened
the guy in the pickup was drunk.
he had no injuries of course
the next day he'd forgotten where he was or what he did.
copcars everywhere fire trucks too,
scads of gawkers all around most clamoring through
me in an underground crawlspace, lighting up while doing this
for the underground crawlspace review.
 

UNFINISHED WRITING

all dumped out, this fruitless salad of chaotic utterly confused unfinished writing, how adorable it seems after dusting off a symphony of decomposed unknowns, this obscure and uncared for collection could be the most insignificant non-essential incomplete manifesto of unfinished writings ever,  should i live long enough to finish writing it.
i would have to come up with a reason,
a terribly logical underpinning that would make this something
less than unfinished writing.
finding myself cartoonlike, impossibly hunched over a desk
putting an old leaky pen to the paper,
every stroke echoing implausibly,
a little doctor frankenstein in bringing life to lifeless forms,
a pinch of peter lorre in defiance of the norm,
i find theatrical moments flowering in my brain,
characters words and images that cannot be contained,
things beyond all measure, concepts i've expressed
with a straightface dragged from the creepiest recess,
getting off a nightmare roadway, stopping short of consciousness, still unfinished writing . . .

dave leon
