Nebiros
Human Enchanter
 
History: To My Esteemed Collegue, Neclo Rhelsar: I am sending you another pupil, old friend. His name is Alstand Trask, from a middling merchant house that only recently discovered his aptitude for the Gift. He seems a bit rough around the edges, but which of us were not? Regards, Magus Galan

To my friend, Magus Galan: Rough perhaps I was, but I do not remember being such an arrogant snot before I had even mastered the must rudimentary of concepts. I will grant you that he shows promise, but must you send me every young apprentice who seems touched by the Warlord? I suppose I shall endure. Cheers, Neclo Rhelsar

To my friend, Magus Galan: I thought I should give you a small notice of the progress of our newest pupil, young Alstand. He has shown interest in some areas of magic that we both consider highly unsavory. No, not necromancy - I would have killed him myself if I detected that. Nothing we have banned, just 'questionable' areas. Namely, texts on the history of the Teir'Dal and their religious practices. He explains it away blithely enough, but I am watching very closely. Your friend, Neclo Rhelsar

To my friend, Magus Galan: My pupil has undergone a very strange transformation. Recently, his unhealthy obsession with the ways of the Teir'dal had tapered off, although I was frequently surprised by the depth of his knowledge on rather obscure elements of their religious beliefs. Full of the vigor which I have come to associate with a gifted student such as he, he announced his attention to take a visit to the Karanas, to seek out and question the Treants on some particular dogmatic snippet of Karana. After cautioning him of the danger involved, I consented. Honestly, I suspected he was running off to try some ritual that was beyond his ability - which of us didn't do that, in our young days? I reasoned it would be healthy for the cocky lad to discover his limitations. When he returned, however, he was quite gaunt, and seemed very tired. Whatever he has done has exhausted him, in body and spirit. He is currently resting, but if his condition deterioates, I shall immediately send for you. Your friend, Neclo Rhelsar

To my friend, Magus Galan: He is gone. I take full responsibility for the unfortunate events that have transpired in the few weeks since I last wrote. All seemed to be well; he recovered within a few days... or, at least his body had healed. I feel now that his mind must have shattered from what he attempted, for he has gone utterly mad. I do not mean that he rambles as a lunatic; he has become a madman with purpose, lucid in speech and thought, but utterly delusional. He had come to believe that he was no longer human, and insisted that I refer to him as Nebiros. I vaguely recognized the term from Teir'Dal as the word for a liar, cheat, or deceiver. When I asked him why he would take such a name, he grew angry, and ranted that his 'name' meant something entirely different in Elder Teir'Dal, and had been bastardized to a derivative meaning. As I had mentioned - mad. I hoped it would pass, so I asked who he was, what he had become. He surprised me, then, by contradicting himself - he insisted he was fully human, and seemed to be terribly saddened by this... almost terrified. He spoke to me no more for several days, began drinking heavily, and simply sulked. I found it rather childish myself, and hoped it would soon end. It did. Rather abruptly, he told me that I knew nothing, was useless to him, and he would seek out those who could help him 'chronicle deeds of Hate and Plague', or some such nonsense. He mentioned the name Lord Faileas, which sounded familiar to me - some miscreant that you may know from Qeynos? A BloodSabre? A bandit? He then left, despite my pleas for him to wait for a cleric who could help heal his mind. All in all, strange, and very unfortunate. He was, despite his overweening arrogance, a very promising enchanter.

Faileas' View: Perhaps it's quiet insanity that drives him away for such long times. Perhaps not. I have heard no news of this enchanter for quite a while.

Envy's View: I do not know much of Nebiros. In fact, I thought him a Teir'Dal necromancer for the longest time. Odd..

Sugan's View: Sorry, we haven't met.
