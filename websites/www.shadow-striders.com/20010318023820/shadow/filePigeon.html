Pigeon Callust
Human Enchanter
 
History: Pigeon is the spoiled-rotten "daddy's girl" of Trenon Callust, head Wizard of the Bloodsabers. Her younger sister's name is Bloodrose, and together they're known as the 'Dread Sisters'. Bloodrose is a Necromancer, and unlike Pidge, was ignored growing up. Pigeon gives her sister some credit, but rolls her eyes at Rose's angsty hatred. They lived their entire lives under the city of Qeynos in Bertoxxulous' Temple. Despite Pidge's terrible manner and abhorrantly immature personality, she has some strange god-gifted charm.. and uses it to her advantage as an Enchantress. Pigeon ALWAYS gets what she wants.

Pidge was there when Faileas' father brought him down, and the two became er, aquaintances at best. She liked to tease and taunt and pick on him, and he hated her. It was through Pigeon's cruel endeavors that Faileas became a Shadow Knight instead of a necromancer like his father. (probably secretly likes the guy, but hey? who doesn't?) She was also present when Malgorm came to be, although she didn't spend much time around him. Pidge thinks Malgorm is a wierd, scary guy. She can respect that.

Recently on his return to his hometown, Lord Faileas met up once again with this infamous brat. Somehow, she weasled her way into the Shadow Striders; through ceaseless annoyance most likely.

Although stubbornly refusing to call him Lord, she does perform a worthwhile service to Faileas. Pidge may be a headache to people considered 'friends', but she's a positive terror to those who don't agree with her view of life. A real plague to THOSE people. Namely, Wind Riders...

Faileas' View: Don't get me started. If it weren't for the politics surrounding her father, our lovely Pidge would have died long ago.

Envy's View: Snooty, unintelligible, selfish... watching her with Nuryn is amusing though. I've come to theorize that perhaps their madnesses balance each other out?

Sugan's View: I cannot STAND this brat! Can anyone? I swear, if there's anything we all can get along about, it's Pigeon.
