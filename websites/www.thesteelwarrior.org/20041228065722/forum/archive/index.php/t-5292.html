What is "riposte damage" and why is it such a big deal when dual wielding?
Iskandar
01-20-2004, 01:20 AM
Riposte = avoiding an attack and counterattacking at the same time. Warriors get it at level 25. Mid-high mobs have riposte as well.

The theory is that, since two fast weapons attack more often than one 2h weapon in a given time frame, you will be riposted more with dual wield. It was a widespread concern in the beginning of PoP but has been more or less debunked and trivialized since then. In other words, it's something to think about if you at 4000 HP are tanking an 800 hitter, but dual wield does not automatically equal suicide.

A mob can't attack 11 times in a round. The most you will get is 4 +kick/bash, and flurries...

Repostes are from you attacking, and thus are not occuring during the combat round, but can occur at nearly the same time, close enough to make it seem that they are part of the combat round, but they are not. If you had attack off, or were out of range, etc, they would not happen, and are thusly not part of the mob's attack round.

For that matter, kick and bash are not part of the attack round, as they are on a seperate timer from the attack delay. They just may happen to occur at the same time as a attack round, but are not part of it.

Hell, same goes for flurry.

If you mean the greatest number of attacks that can occur in the same 1 second period, then its 14...

4 attacks from the mob
2 from its flurry
1 from kick/bash
4 from repostes of your attacks
2 from repostes of your flurry
1 from repostes of your kick/bash/slam

If you want to see a mob's ripostes against you, you have to turn the hitmode for your misses to "Full" or whatever, not "Abbreviated" or "Number only." This option is in the right-click context menu for your chatbox.

Also note that when it says, "You attempt to slash a_mob but a_mob ripostes!" the line immediately BEFORE that indicates that riposte attack against you. Like:

a_mob slashes YOU for 1000 points of damage! <---this is the riposte
You attempt to slash a_mob but a_mob ripostes! <---but it tells you on the NEXT line here

Personally I would think it should be the other way around, but I guess that's just the way the EQ combat engine works /shrug
