Twoaxe
Well some of the things I did while two boxing was

1) Be sure to have a hotkey with /target Warrior's name on it. Of course this is mainly for PLing as in your case they'll likely be grouped and it won't be such an issue.

2) On my cleric I bought those right click Donal's vambraces. You can stand and mana free chain heal for about 200hp every 9 seconds with little to no aggro. Once again, not much help to you until your cleric hits 45th - but someone else might find the info handy.

3) You're cleric doesn't have to have versatile healing spells up since you'll only be healing your war (assuming you don't group and two box). In that case it leaves room for some utility spells such as nullify magic and more importantly - an extra stun slot.

4) Root and atone are nice to have up as well. If you get too many you can just park one out of aggro range and atone the bugger.

5) If you've messed up and over pulled and find yourself in trouble. Drop the warrior and make sure the cleric makes it out alive.

6) Bring lots of vodka or Gnomish spirits etc...

Well not much help really but perhaps someone will add to this useless list...

Brutul
Keep those DAs up. When my cleric gets aggro, I just DA and sit down and start beating on it with my warrior. 9 times out of 10 by the time DA wears off you will have aggro on the warrior. I don't have a lot of macros, just my 3 heal spells I keep memmed (divine light, celestial healing, and complete heal). I don't have a /target warrior hotkey, I just use F2. When I'm 2-boxing in a group I insist on reforming the group, both so the warrior is in the usual F2 slot, and so the group is in the same order on both screens so I rarely have to look at the cleric's screen at all, just watch the health bars on the warrior screen and hit the appropriate F-key and the appropriate heal macro. My heal macros go:

/sit off
/cast X
/g Complete Healing %t
/cast X
/cast X

The group message is very important because I don't look at the cleric screen, so if I start a heal on the wrong person I will see it on the warrior's screen. The two extra /cast X lines are to make sure the spell gets off if you fizzle (this is horrible with celestial healing).

My hotkeys go

1-Complete Heal Macro
2-Divine Light Macro
3-Celestial Heal Macro
4-Divine Aura
5-/assist Brutul
6-Snare neck
7-Root
8-Nuke
9-Divine Barrier
0-Gate

As someone else said, 2-boxing alone with single-pulls a cleric is the easiest thing in the world to 2-box. Playing both tank and cleric in a group can be pretty tricky though, since both warrior and cleric have important stuff they have to do right away if a multi-pull comes to camp and multiple people are getting beat on. You can't really ignore either one, so sometimes you have to choose between healing someone, and trying to taunt off of them. Takes some practice, and your group will most likely wipe out in situations they could have survived with a "real" cleric in group.
