A post in another thread got me thinking about this so here is the rant.

Server changes are NOT fair and balanced for all classes. Casting classes get to keep all their spells upon server transfer, while melee's get a big fat zero. I cannot think of any reason why this would be the case. I would really like to hear the reasoning behind this policy from someone at SoE but I'm sure that will never happen.

A 60-65 chanter with KEI, a cleric with HoV, Mage with epic returned even wizards with massive damage nukes or druids with soloing abilities can quickly rack up ALOT of plat in which to reequip themselves.....and we all know how cheap pure caster gear is. Even Wis caster gear isn't anywhere near on par with amounts needed to equip a tanking class. Knights need only acquire some decent armor (still admittedly hard) and they have their spells to hold aggro. The warrior need to not only acquire new aggro generating weapons (argueably some of the most expensive items in game) but they need haste, and high AC/HP gear in order to just do their job.

WTF was Sony thinking with this? Even a monk can use his fists as decent weapons until he gets better. A rogue can find some low end gear and piercers and as long as they don't steal aggro the only thing a group will notice is that xp seems a bit "slow". The Warrior who is gimped out by a recent server transfer will become IMMEDIATELY noticeable. He'll be unable to hold aggro, unable to deal with aggro when he gets it from his lack of AC/HP's and will cause the entire group to fall apart.

This is just ridiculous IMO. If the goal is to keep the character from benefiting monetarily by selling gear then just allow transfer of NO DROP items. I'd rather a have few "holes" in my gear than be buck ass naked. Maybe allowing a pure melee either No Drop armor OR weapons would at least allow them (especially the warrior) a stepping stone in which to get started on the new server.

Allowing casters complete access to spellbooks and giving pure melee nothing further reinforces in my mind that Sony is blind to the needs of melee classes and ESPECIALLY the warrior.

/rant off
