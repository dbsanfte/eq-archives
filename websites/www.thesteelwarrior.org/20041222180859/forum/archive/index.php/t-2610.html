Assumptions:
Our current dilemma as warriors is the fact that our agro abilities are vastly inferior to those of knights' barring high-end weapons, while our tanking ability is only somewhat superior. As a result, warriors are not desired for exp grouping over knights because once you weigh the pros and cons, in the end the scales are still in the knights' favor. The once-untouchable high ground for warriors, raid tanking, is slowly being encroached on by knights as well (it's often to have a knight tanking all the mobs up to the uber on a raid, with the warrior reduced to only tanking the uber mob itself, it being the only one which requires defensive).

Reasons:
I'll focus on stun here, but for shadowknights simply replace "stun" with "snare" and you'll have a comparable picture. The reason for such a high disparity between warrior and knight agro generation is that knights have an entire line of spells - stun - which generates tremendous agro. They can cast nothing but their pre-50 stuns and still lock agro over a tank any day without every worrying about running OOM.

Solution:
Nerfing stun agro would be bad. Not, I couldn't care less about a paladin shedding crocodile tears over being unwanted; I myself use CHoS/SotB, and they both proc stun. Hence, I wouldn't want to nerf stun agro. No, what I propose to do is this:

MAKE STUNS GENERATE NO AGRO IF THEY ARE RESISTED OR CAN'T AFFECT THE MOB

How would this help? It would reduce the agro disparity between warriors and knights by cutting off their cheap-on-mana, fast cast/recast lvl 20 or 30 stuns and restricting them to using high lvl stuns to taunt high lvl mobs. Paladins have 2 stuns which can affect mobs all the way to 65, and 2 which can affect mobs up to 61. By making the low lvl stuns not agro mobs too high to be affected, they'd be forced to use the two high ones and actually work for agro, IE taunt. Most importantly, their edge over warriors would go from vastly superior to somewhat superior - kind of like what our tanking is to theirs.

Result:
Knight agro would still be superior to warrior agro. When OOM, they'd be the equals of warriors as far as being reduced to DPS/taunt for getting agro; when with mana, tier2 and up knights (fighting 62+ mobs) are down to 2 stuns: lvl 64 one (200 mana/24 sec recast) and the 62 one (65 mana/12 sec recast). This retains the snap agro since they can chain cast 2 stuns as soon as a fight starts, but removes their ability to take agro at will from anyone by being casting stun after stun, non-stop till the cows come home, making everyone exercise agro control. I mean, I did it with my cleric once when he was 62, chained stuns without ever swinging, and kept agro over a 60 warrior (did it in PoD where mobs are gimp and won't kill cleric). The agro advantage thus retained equals the hp/ac advantage a warrior brings without being superior to the point of making it meaningless.

Currently, a warrior in a group often means the group working at less than peak efficiency (with a knight, they can do anything with NO regard for their agro) - monks hold back, rogues bs later, slowers can't chain malo or mez/slow/cripple because they'll get attacked. This is one way the hp/ac advantage gets buried in the long run. Cutting back on that recklessness a little brings the knights more in line with warriors as far as group desirability, while retaining the key agro-at-will advantage knights have always had.

Thoughts?
