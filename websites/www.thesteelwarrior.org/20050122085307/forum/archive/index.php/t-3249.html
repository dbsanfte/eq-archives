Surprisingly, with the release of LDoN, there appears to be a new choice in BPs for many warriors. Of course there are always the obvious choices, but never before has a top quality BP been available to so many. But, how good is the LDoN BP? Is it worth getting if you are in a raid guild? Here's a quick break down.

1. Raex's Chestplate of Destruction
AC: 100
Skill Mod: Riposte +8%
STR: +30 DEX: +25 STA: +30 AGI: +25 HP: +250
SV FIRE: +20 SV DISEASE: +20 SV COLD: +20 SV MAGIC: +20 SV POISON: +20
Shielding: 3
Effect: Echo of Anger (Worn)
By far the best BP a warrior can hope for. Best HP/AC, great stats, and an effect that is actually useful. Unfortunately, this is only available to the very elite.

2. Chestguard of Enchanted Marble
AC: 85
STR: +25 DEX: +20 STA: +20 CHA: +12 WIS: +25 INT: +25 HP: +150 MANA: +150
SV DISEASE: +30 SV POISON: +30
Effect: Vengeance VII (Worn)
Second only to Raex's in HP and AC, also have a nice effect. Augmented can almost have 200 raw HP. DR and PR are nice additions since they are hardest resists to raise. Too many hybrid stats, but I guess you can't have it all. Worst part of this BP is the pain in the ass it is to get. Not many guilds are willing to farm the Rathe encounter.

3. Great Shadow Platemail
AC: 80
STR: +25 DEX: +25 STA: +25 WIS: +15 INT: +15 AGI: +25 HP: +125 MANA: +125
SV FIRE: +30 SV COLD: +30
Effect: Vengeance X (Worn)
The rarity of this BP is it's biggest drawback. It also has many hours of camping a VT and Emp key as strikes against it. The stats make it very worthwhile with the Vengeance giving it the boost over the LDoN BP.

4. Royal Attendant Breastplate of Sand (augmented)
AC: 69
STA: +13 WIS: +10 INT: +10 CHA: +11 HP: +180 MANA: +100
SV MAGIC: +12 SV COLD: +12 SV POISON: +12
or
AC: 69
STA: +13 WIS: +10 INT: +10 CHA: +11 HP: +140 MANA: +100
SV MAGIC: +12 SV COLD: +12 SV POISON: +12
Effect: Vengeance III (Worn)
Lack of an effect and the slightly lower AC keeps this from moving up a slot on the list. Still very good stats and unreal HP. Available to anyone with the time to complete around 80 LDoN adventures to purchase the BP and necessary augments. Also, 30hp can be substituted for Vengeance III for those who are light in the worn ATK department, still giving you a 180hp BP with a nice effect. This BP in either form is likely the best BP that the majority of warriors will ever see.

5. Heavy Yttrium Chestplate
AC: 75
STR: +20 STA: +20 WIS: +20 INT: +20 HP: +125 MANA: +125
SV FIRE: +30 SV DISEASE: +15 SV COLD: +15 SV MAGIC: +15 SV POISON: +30
Effect: Yttric Ventus (Worn)
Excellent BP with very good stats. This BP is also a bit rare and a pain to get access to. HP and Effect are nice, but not quite up to the level of the BPs ahead on the list.

6. Kizrak's Chestplate of Battle
AC: 63
STR: +10 DEX: +9 STA: +15 AGI: +6 HP: +115
SV DISEASE: +15 SV MAGIC: +10 SV POISON: +15
Effect: Shield of the Arcane (Must Equip, Casting Time: 12.0)
Excellent BP and surprisingly easy to aquire. Mold is tradeable and mob that drops it is up every 6 hours. Good resists and handy effect for the times a shammy is not around. Great all around BP that is available to the masses.

7. Breastplate of Speed
AC: 60
Skill Mod: Dodge +5%
DEX: +15 STA: +10 WIS: +8 INT: +8 AGI: +15 HP: +125 MANA: +75
Effect: Fury of the Vah Shir (Worn)
Not only is this BP rare, but it lacks resists and the stats leave a little to be desired compare to other BPs. Most people capable of getting one probably are maxed stats anyway, but no resists at all hurt it. Very nice effect because it leaves a lot of options when you don't have to consider where your haste is going to be.

8. Breastplate of the Void
AC: 65
STR: +25 DEX: +8 STA: +25 WIS: +8 INT: +8 AGI: +8 HP: +110 MANA: +75
SV FIRE: +8 SV DISEASE: +8 SV COLD: +8 SV MAGIC: +8 SV POISON: +8
Required level of 51.
Effect: Heart of the Void (Must Equip, Casting Time: 2.5)
Very similar to Kizrak's with a nice effect and good stats. While pretty rare, this is becoming available to a lot more people as more guilds become Ssra capable. Not much of an option to guilds that have progressed to VT+ due to heavy competition.

9. Constricting Cuirass
AC: 68
STR: +20 STA: +20 WIS: +15 INT: +15 HP: +125 MANA: +75
SV MAGIC: +15
Recommended level of 63.
Effect: Vengeance II (Worn)
Again, lack of resists and huge pain in the ass to get this BP hurt it as a good option for many warriors. Most guilds capable of killing new Inny haven't bothered. Effect is nice, but not enough to push it up on the list.

10. Farwater Plate Breastplate
AC: 65
STR: +12 DEX: +10 STA: +8 WIS: +8 INT: +8 HP: +120 MANA: +50
SV DISEASE: +15 SV POISON: +15
Very good stats and tradeable make this a very nice BP. However, farming enough components is a huge strike against this BP. Also, lack of a good effect makes this a stepping stone BP to some any one of the BPs higher on the list.

Honorable mention:
There are also quite a few BPs that are still very good but not up to par with the above. Some are pretty easy to get - Chestplate of Vindication, Champion's Breastplate, Breastplate of the Myrmidon, and Warlord's Breastplate are all obtainable with small numbers of people and several can be MQ'd. Breastplate of Eradication is also a good option for the non-uber warrior. Each of these is a viable option, just not quite the quality of the higher end BPs.

The reason I used the fully augmented LDoN BP is because of the easy of aquiring the BP. If you take just the LDoN BP without augments, you could get that in a week pretty easily - only 28 adventures on normal setting. Doing 3 a day you would still get one in under 10 days. When you consider the diffculty in just getting in to the the zones most of the others drop, it's not unreasonable to say you would spend a comparable amount of time as you would getting a fully augmented LDoN BP just to get in to the zones. If you add the 40hp any type augment to any of the other BPs listed below the LDoN BP, you would still end up with less HP and a whole lot more work in getting a key and an augment.

Nothing even comes close to touching Raex's, augmented or not. I didn't see the need to include the max possible HP on it.

At the level that someone would be considering BPs of this caliber, they would no doubt have maxed STR/STA/DEX/AGI with just a few buffs. I wouldn't include the augment on the HY because of the difficulty in getting th HY to begin with. Obviously if you already have one then it would make sense to augment it and work on other items, but if you are looking for an upgrade from a lesser BP you need to take in to consideration getting the BP.

So an argument can be made that the HY is the better BP for pure stats, however I ranked the LDoN higher because of 2 reasons. First, you have the option to go all out HP with 210 if you are maxed on worn ATK, and second because it is about 10x easier to get than a Seru BP. No bane weapon farming, no annoying quest to get a key, no guild raid, no DKP compared to pick up groups a few times a night for a month. If the HY was easier to get I would have probably swapped the 2, but for many people HY is not even an option.

VT bps are not that rare.

To give an idea on BP rarety, we got :

- 6 Great Shadow Platemail / 17 Aten Ha Ra
- 3 Breastplate of Speed / 8 Thall Xundraux Diabo
- 3 Heavy Yttrium Chestplate / 14 Lord Inquisitor Seru
- 1 Breastplate of the Void / 19 Vyzh`dra the Cursed In order for a VT/Ssra BP to not be that rare, one must first hunt in those zones to begin with. Once you get to elem level, chances are you will never get any of those BPs. Also, while it would seem your guild has had pretty good luck in getting BP drops, I don't think the same could be said for most others. Also, when you figure out the amount of time it takes on average for one of those BPs, it makes LDoN all the more attractive.

Great Shadow Platemail - 1 every 2.8 weeks
Breastplate of Speed - 1 every 2.6 weeks
Heavy Yttrium Chestplate - 1 every 5.3 weeks
Breastplate of the Void - 1 every 19 weeks

Coupled with the fact that LDoN is a sure thing and the amount of time you would need to spend farming keys to get in to either Seru or VT and again the LDoN looks a lot nicer to a lot of people.

With that said, keep in mind that people value stats differently depending on their current situation. I am sure there could be all kinds of arguements made as to the order of these BPs, this is just my personal opinion on the order I would rank them. About the only think most people would agree on is that these are the top 10 and I bet some people would even argue that some of the honorable mention BPs should have been on the list.
