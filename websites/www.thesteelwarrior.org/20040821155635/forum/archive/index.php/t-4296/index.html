Battleblade
Others have discussed this both on this board and PoN. I think the idea warrents further discussion.

Mob variation can be used to make the differences between the three major tank classes more important. This would allow increasing Warrior survivability without making Knights no longer useful.

Knight's could gain spells that would make them more valuable in a mixed mob environment that would not make Warriors useless in turn. Warriors could gain new unique (to the 3 tank classes) disciplines (immune to magic, stonestance, etc.) that would give them a place even in the presence of greater Knight utility.

Mob variation in a zone can be used to encourage two tank groups (8 person groups might be needed if this were done) where perhaps a Warrior's beef is an advantage against a mob that is immune to magic and hits like a truck while the very next mob has a huge unremovable damage shield that a Paladin might tank with stuns while non-melee in the group kill it.

What mob abilites are there that might favor one tank class over another?

I'll start a list, feel free to add to it.

AE Damage - Paladin
Undead - Paladin, SK
Not affected by spells - Warrior
Unaffected by stun - Warrior, SK
Mana Draining - Warrior
Unremovable damage shield - Paladin
Rampage - two tanks
Self bluring - Knight?

As a separate exercise, new, underutilzed, or abilities in combination would also be worth exploring.

A modest start:
DD Reposte (Reverse Damage) - two tanks
AE Heal/Summon pets - Paladin, two tanks
Knockback/Whirl until you Hurl
Fear/Summon - two tanks
Knockback/Snare - Paladin, two tanks
Hits hard and repostes - Warrior (new discipline - Counter Reposte), Knight (new discipline - Shield Reposte)
Unstunable/unslowable - Warrior

Jack up a lvl 30 mob to the point where it's melee is insane for it's level, and a necro/druid/shaman/wizard can still kill it just as easily as he used to, all you've done it make it so that a melee class has no hope of killing it solo. "Mob variation can be used to make the differences between the three major tank classes more important. This would allow increasing Warrior survivability without making Knights no longer useful."

An interesting idea, but I'm now sure how they could get it right, and apply it properly.

Case in point:

"AE Damage - Paladin"

Why would a paladin be a better tank than anyone else on AE damage, if the healing is there? In fact, the first time I ever encountered AE damage in EQ was against some dragons, and the warriors usually tanked them, back in the day.

"Undead - Paladin, SK"

Again, not really seeing how any class is a preferred "tank" against an undead boss. In fact, in this instance, a warrior might be the better tank, so you can put the paladins behind the mob and get them a nice DPS boost with their anti-undead type of abilities.

"Not affected by spells - Warrior"

Heh. Maybe. Depends. I mean, a lot of boss mobs already aren't "affected" by the aggro generating spells, but the aggro is still there.

"Unaffected by stun - Warrior, SK"

What makes warriors and SKs better choices to tank giants, a mob that is already unaffected by stuns?

"Mana Draining - Warrior"

Heh. Alright, this might be an instance where melee classes are better are being in melee range.

"Unremovable damage shield - Paladin"

Eh, a warrior with a big fat 2hander that has good aggro proc or aggro augment on it can probably do the job just as good as a paladin.


"Rampage - two tanks"

Kind of already exists, doesn't it?

"Self bluring - Knight?"

There is an encounter like this isn't there?

I dunno. Like I said above, the IDEA is an interesting one. But the application within the framework of everquest's combat system, will be the huge obstacle.
