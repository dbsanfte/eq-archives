I imagine this isn't anything that new, but why not just dance 'em around in place?

If your raid is half disciplined at staying behind the mob and you do a slow keyboard circling of the mob (sidestep right, turn left) I find the raid has a tendency to push the mob in a tight circle, usually more or less staying in the same place. We call it dancing the mob.

If you have to tell people to stay behind the mob, this won't work. So you need folks who aren't total muppets, but this isn't that complicated a thing to do. The only time we bother cornering a mob anymore is when the mob has some knock back or other AE that makes it necessary. Or maybe when the geometry of the situation doesn't allow for even the slight movement of the mob that dancing it requires...

Makes for more fun in the tanking too. Cornering, you back him into the corner and go for a smoke. This way you have to actualy pay a bit of attention.
