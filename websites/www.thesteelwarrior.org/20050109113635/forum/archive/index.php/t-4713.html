The normal formulas used to determine mob melee damage are:
mob melee damage = DB + ((RND(20)) * DI)
min hit = DB + DI
max hit = DB + 20*DI.

If the change manipulated DI as well, by subtracting 1 from it.

The higher the value for DI is, the more a mob is characterisized as a heavy hitter (because DI is multiplied by 20 when calculating the max hit) since the dfference between its min and max hit is large.

Low level mobs have smaller values for DI and high level hard hitting mobs have a greater value for DI.

In fact, the higher DI is (and heavy hitting mobs have greater DI's) the lower the reduction% becomes.

---- **** ----

So, I can't believe this is what's being done. Instead, what I believe (hope really) is what is being manipulated is RND(20), which I'll call the RM (Random Multiplier).

If they are changing the 20 to 19 so that the formula becomes:
mob melee damage = DB + ((RND(19)) * DI)

This has the effect of changing a mob's maximum hit from:
max hit = DB + 20 * DI

To:
max hit = DB + 19 * DI

Since there are usually 20 possible damage values returned, this has the effect of reducing the max hit of any mob by 4%

min hit = 52

This is probably the answer. What it does is reduce the maximum hit that can be taken by a Warrior by DI. The higher DI is, the greater the HP reducion and the closer the reduction% comes to 5%.

It's worth noting also that the melee damage formula is linear and DI acts as the slope. The higher the value for DI, the steeper the slope and the greater the difference between the minimum and maximum hit. Mobs with a high DI produce "damage spikes" where a maximum hit stands out from the background of median hit. This change slightly reduces the amplitude of those spikes.

It's possible to have a mob that hits for 3000's and has a low DI. For such a mob, the damage spike would be quit small as well as the damage "mitigated" by this change.

Please note that nothing I have seen indicates any reduction vs. DD, DoT or procs. Since these are pretty common in high-level boss mobs and make up the majority of the damage a non-MT Warrior would take, it inaccurate to state that Warriors are getting 5% mitigation vs. high-level mobs.

In the case of a 3000HP high DI mob this is like having another 500HP's in your maximum HP's. In the case of a mob that does appreciable damage with DD, DoTs, or Procs it's far less. For lower level blue con experiance mobs in LDoN Easy (for example) it's not going to amount to much.
