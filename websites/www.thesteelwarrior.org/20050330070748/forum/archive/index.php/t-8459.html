RZTW has about 1.4 million hit points. You have an effective time limit to produce this damage. Wave 5 is the point of certain failure (6 minutes). You should not count on anything going right after wave 3 (4 minutes).

Your raid needs to sustain 5000 DPS to win by wave 5. For a high chance of winning (i.e. by wave 3), you should aim for 8000 DPS sustained.

The average player who is contributing DPS is probably doing 100 DPS at this stage of guild advancement. Assume that 10 people are devoted to keeping the tank alive and do no DPS. Also assume that kiters and charm-masters contribute no DPS (they probably do contribute a little, but it averages out).

Take your amount of DPS contributers, multiply by 100 DPS, and subtract 20% to account for attrition. That gives you a DPS figure for your players. Now you can figure out how many charmed pets you need to bring the total up to 7000-8000 DPS.

Charmed pets will do about 500 DPS each on RZTW, when you factor in DPS lost to charm breaks. Necro-charmed wraiths bring down the average DPS a little.

Note that each charmed pet is almost equal to 1 extra group of players in terms of DPS. I encourage you to have everyone charm who can do so: druids, necros, enchanters. Every pet should have snare (to facilitate charm breaks), haste, pumpkinhead, mage weapon, mage haste item. Your raid members should be able to arrange this without micromanagement, once you reach the pit and start pulling pet mobs from Glykus area. The masters' ONLY job is to stay alive and control their pet. If they die, their pet will tear up the clerics. They should not cast on RZTW, and should stay at long range from their pet, and manage their own charm breaks.

Charmed mobs go a very long way if you have a smaller force. 50 people with 10 charmed pets will probably beat RZTW on their first try. (25 DPSers + 10 pets - 20% attrition = 6000 DPS = win in 4 minutes.) The difficulty of this encounter is not the healing, the difficulty is in generating enough DPS.
