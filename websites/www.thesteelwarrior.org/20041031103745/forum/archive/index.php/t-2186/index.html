Bashiae
I'd definately suggest the Featherwood hands down for a variety of reasons.

First, lets ignore the cost factor, because although that's a big factor for many people, we'll assume anyone having a hard time choosing would spend the extra money for the Stonewood if they decided it was a big enough benefit.

That said, the simple fact of the matter is Featherwood's stats have a much larger impact over the Stonewood for Warriors.

The Featherwood has 15 AGI, which while many warriors may have it maxxed, not many that haven't gotten a 'raid awarded' piece of loot equal or better than an elemental bow (I.E. non-elemental flagged Warriors) are going to have maxxed AGI.

Stonewood, on the otherhand, has 15 STR, which is arguably the easiest stat for a warrior to max out given all the buffs available and the commonality of it on our equipment.

They both have 10 STA, so that's a moot point.

No one cares about the INT/WIS/MANA so that's moot as well.

The 35 SV Magic or Disease is a huge factor in my eyes. If you look at the mobs you fight over the course of a week, for example (both raid and exp mobs), you'll find the VAST majority require SV Magic over SV Disease. Only a few select mobs/zones have mobs that use many SV Disease attacks (remember, slow has Disease counters but saves to Magic), so the 35 SV Magic, added onto your already gained artificial SV Magic as a Warrior, will shoot you up a great deal and give you a fair chance at resisting many Sv Magic spells without buffs or with very few buffs (Save Magic from a priest or ENC.) This definately points towards the Featherwood.

The effects on the bows, which seems to be the biggest factor for most people arguing for the Stonewood, is really not that much of a factor for either. As pointed out, the 10 Atk will give you *hardly* any actual damage increase (remember, your STR is likely to be maxed in most cases if you're at all decently equipped or buffed), and the 2 HP regen is certainly not going to offset anything nowadays; it won't make the difference between that CH landing or not landing when you're getting hit for 500+, nor will it negate ANY AE spells that you'll be taking in the 60+ game. The only thing it's good for is farming greens with your Fungi or what not on as well, and regenning (slowly) after a res.

The effect on the Featherwood, honestly, doesn't really matter either. Who cares if it lowers your agro, how often do you use a bow to 'increase' agro anyway, other than initial agro in which case you're the only one on the hate list to begin with? Never. And if it does happen to proc, which is very rarely as it is, you'll do 75 damage from it assuming no resist, which is fine and dandy. Certainly doesn't hurt.

I don't know about most Warriors, but I assume it's similar to me, and when I pull 95% of the time I use my Featherwood and the best range arrows I can, and I generally have to shoot a few (2 - 4) arrows to actually land one/get the mob's attention, due to pathing the arrow, geometry of the area vs. where the mob is, etc. This brings up the other factor, is that the Featherwood has a significantly lower delay, which means you can shoot arrows in sequence much faster than with the Stonewood. Since you aren't going for damage just to gain agro/pull, you'll want the fastest thing you can to spit out 3 or 4 arrows and grab the mob as fast as possible.

I think that covers most everything. Really, the only factor in my eyes are the SVs and delay; if you can afford both and swap them out then great, otherwise I'd strongly suggest the Featherwood.
