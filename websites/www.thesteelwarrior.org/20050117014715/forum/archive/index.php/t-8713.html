Of course the carrot has to be always out there, otherwise people start complaining how there's nothing to do. I'd say more bored people end up leaving compare to dissatisfied people.

As for the content being untested, an encounter is only as good as the people testing them. I have noticed that players, as testers, are very quick to wimp out on anything remotely difficult. Talk all you want about wanting things to be a challenge, requiring skill, or whatever, but ultimately that means 'I want this encounter I can beat." To use a more concrete example, I don't see any significant movement to lobby Mshas to be more difficult even though they're obviously too easy for the loot they drop. On the other hand, anything that is remotely difficult is quickly labeled as broken. Sometimes the stuff really is broken, but people give up too fast, so I'm not surprised devs set the bar too high and ignore the pleas for a while to see if it is really broken, or just the usual whining. Since encounters that are too easy are bad for everyone (for devs their content gets used too fast, for players you get bored, and you'll get competition hassles if it's not instanced) where as encounters that are too hard are only bad for players, it's not really surprising encounters typically start out too hard. In an ideal world everything would start out just right but I don't think it's even possible, especially as long as encounters are tested by players who would rather have something that's too easy as opposed to something that's too hard.
