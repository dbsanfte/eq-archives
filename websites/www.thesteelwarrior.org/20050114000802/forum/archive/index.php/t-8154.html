The basic reasoning is a warrior can never have too much agro.

Therefore, in dual wielding setups, 2 weapon with 2 agro procs should produce the maximum amount of agro possible. The recommended route is 2 weapons with built-in Enraging Blow type procs (if not a built-in hate proc, use a built-in stun proc) with Anger 3's (or an Anger 3 + Ykesha augment).

Not only does having two procs on each weapon increase your potential agro (a good thing), it increases the chance of procing sooner in a fight, allowing slows, etc. to land sooner without stealing agro. As the knights have shown, this is good for xp...and of course, on raids, it's equally as good.

Mathematically (please realize this is simplified and is not 100% accurate for EQ)

2 weapons x (450 hate proc built-in each, stun only) = 900 hate
2 weapon augments of 600 Hate each (anger 3) = 1200 hate
Total agro potential 2100.

2 weapons x (0 hate proc built-in each) = 0 hate
2 weapon augments of 600 Hate each (anger 3) = 1200 hate
Total agro potential 1200.

Once again, this is overly simplified and I'm very sure some more experienced EQ veterans would rip this formula to shreds if I didn't repeat that this is not an accurate formula, this doesn't account for procs per minute, nor does it account for the agro potential difference between main hand and off hand...nor does it address dps agro, which is generally not large enough to make up the difference in potential agro...just to mention a few holes in it. However, it's easy to see with this that 2100 is almost twice the agro as good as 1200.

If I recall, 1 point of hate is supposed to be equal to 1 point of melee damage. Therefore, if your weapon was 100 / 20 or so, but no proc, you 'might' be better off augmenting that weapon...but you won't be able to spike your hate with a quick 400 - 600ish agro proc.

That being said, if you want to be different, if you can hold agro with current setup without any problems, feel free to augment any weapon you want, you just have to realize you won't have the highest possible agro you could have...and unless you are signficantly better geared and AA'd than your casters or hybrids, they are managing their agro at your level rather than their mana (per fight).


EDIT: Reread your question. AFAIK a weapon with two procs will not proc less often than a weapon with 1 proc. I know it definitely increases the odds of having any proc happen sooner. I believe (but no evidence to support) that a weapon can have both procs go off at the same time (or same round). I apologize if my original response was unhelpful. :)

Seems to me a Weapon witha proce already bult in like a copper hammer, would proc the additional hate aug less.

The innate proc and the augmented proc on a weapon fire at the same rate. They are subject to your dex and the slot you have equipped the weapon. So, if you have max dex, each proc on your mainhand weapon will go off 3x per minute. Repeat, BOTH procs will go off 3x a minute.

So -- you're MUCH better for aggro purposes to follow the advice in this thread; namely, anger augment a weapon that ALREADY has an aggro proc. The two easiest weapons to acquire that fit that description are the Copper Hammer and the Sword of the Bloodsworn.

PS -- the info about dex and proc-ing can be found in the library, I believe.
