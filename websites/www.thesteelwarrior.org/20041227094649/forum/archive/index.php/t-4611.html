A few things of note.

Openings are broken down by Offensive Openings, Defensive Openings, Speciality Openings, and Error Openings (NPC Fumbles).

Offensive Openings are triggered by meleeing.

Defensive Openings are triggered by tanking (taking damage/being hit).

Specialty Openings are triggered by Kick/Bash.

Error Openings are triggered by the NPC screwing up.

Warriors are supposed to have the highest chance to trigger Defensive Openings. Currently that is not working properly (I parse twice the Offensive Openings as compared to Defensive Openings atm).

Several skills are currenly not working and/or being reviewed for efficacy and real world value.

I didn't have Flurry up as a Hot Button when going through my first round of data gathering. It comes up on an Offensive Opening and I THINK a Specialty Opening. I just left it blank for the time being.

Rage and Rallying Cry only open via an NPC Error Opening at the moment, one more opening will be added to both of these in the near future.

The data gathered comes from busting my ass for hour upon hour upon hour as well as harassing developers for hour upon hour upon hour and cross referencing Lucy.

Everything you see here is subject to change, leave the whining to the professionals.

One more thing of note, personalized sounds will be added to these openings. That is, you will be allowed to link .wav and .mp3 files to the different openings for a unique sound cue. Start rounding up the Homer/Cartman soundbites ASAP.

Also, the abilities will each have their own unique particle effect. I was PROMISED a Flying Flaming Rallos Zek Axe. Ok, I lie... but it needs to happen.

in the right directions for adding utility to warriors.

Those of you who played camelot know what "protect" is. Protect is a daoc tank ability that works as follows. The warrior targets a cleric, or wizard and clicks protect. From then on, until canceled, the warrior is "protecting" that person. Basically the way it works, is the warrior consumes a certain % of the hate the person they are protecting gets.

So a warrior could "protect" a caster in the group, let them know, and that caster knew they could pump up the dps quite a bit - because the warrior was protecting them. Basic idea here is simulating the warrior standing in front of that person and protecting them from harm.

The warrior is contributing dps THROUGH the wizard. They are teaming up. Simple, yet brilliant.

Rally cry is similar to that, but again still far underpowered to be bothered with in its current form.
