I was tossing this idea around The Dojo today given the upcoming 'Melee Re-whatever' and wanted to put it up for discussion here.

First, let me state that I'm not a big fan of converting mana on items into something a warrior can use. In the sense of Everquest, it just doesn't mesh with me. I would rather convert something the warrior is already supposed to have in surplus.

With that said, I want some form of Conversion system for Taunt/Hate.

Let me explain how this works:

At level 20 a warrior is given the innate ability to turn their health into hate (they take damage while dishing out hate). As the warrior levels, they are able to more efficiently turn their health into hate and additionally, increase the raw amount of hate they can generate.

The hate generated can be used up to 150 range. You can set various hotkeys for varying amounts of hate. For example, at level 65, a warrior is able to utilize up to 70% of his total hps at a 75% conversion efficiency ratio. Thus, for every 1000 hit points he converts (he takes 1000 damage), he can generate 750 points of hate.

This sets up some interesting scenarios. A warrior could have a number of Conversion hotkeys. Common grind at level 65 would probably be around a 400 hp/300 hate conversion, while tank switches could focus on massive conversions of 5k hps at a time all the while carefullly balancing that CH or bombardment of SLs to land right after a conversion goes through.


What this does for gameplay:

At experience levels, it balances out the playing field for Warriors in the area of Taunt/Ranged Taunt. Heavy useage has drawbacks limited by Health and mana of the healer. Careless, clumsy warriors could easily kill themselves in a Conversion. Poorly managed groups where wizards are chain nuking, for example, will result in overexhertion, and quite possibly warrior death and eventual group death.


Less reliability on CH:

As you're using conversions, depending on how you have your own hotkeys setup (remember you can setup your conversion to anything under your cap, so the possibilities are massive) you may find that you will have to do a lot of quick thinking in large/bad pull situations and raids, which will naturally require large and fast landing heals to come into play. The more skilled warrior will be able to multi-taunt, and still retain a careful balance among the groups healers.


Warrior Focus Items:

Conversion Efficiency and Enhancement FOCI. HP regen on grinds given more meaning.



Now remember, this is all just off the top of my head so I'm open to any improvements or ideas, and I still have no concrete conversion rates for level 20 - 60.

Also of note, for this to really work, Warriors would require a more appreciable hit point advantage than they have at the moment (something which I believe is in the works anyway).
