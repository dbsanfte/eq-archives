Hmmm. Helpful tips are a little more difficult. I'll assume you're main tank in an experience group, you don't know everyone in your group, and you are not pulling (though in places where you can pull, then pull). Good tanks do certain things, in my opinion:
they have a good idea of the capacity of the mobs they are facing (which are casters, which are not; which are toughest, what order the mobs ought to be killed in, etc);
they get a good sense of the aggro range and respawn rate of the mobs they are facing (even if they are not pulling);
they position mobs well, and not just randomly;
they communicate well with the people they are grouping with;
they adjust quickly to the strengths and weaknesses of their group;
they always know where their groupmates are and where the mobs are;
they understand what other classes need from a tank.

If I'm in a group with pet classes, I usually tell the group that I will position mob so that its back is to pet X. It's a lot easier for folks to park their pets in a good spot than it is to re-position their pets in mid-fight. Also, it gives other melee, particularly rogues, an idea of at least where you hope the back of the mob will be. Usually it allows me to face my casters, which I find useful for various reasons (though that's personal preference to some degree--many fine tanks prefer to face away from their casters).
Understand /shield and use it when it makes sense to do so.
Announce mez breaks, and break cleanly. If enchanter wants you to break mez on a certain mob first, then do as they say. Otherwise, you pick.
In LDoNs, often a puller has to go through a door to get to mobs. I usually stand next to the door and keep it open, so the puller doesn't get whacked a couple extra tiems as he waits for the door to open. This should be obvious, but lots of people don't do it.
If someone else is higher than you on the aggro list, taunt first, then use incite line and kick (successful taunt will leap you over the aggro of the other player, then the incite will sit on top of that; if you do it in the reverse order, either the initial hate of the incite or the taunt is wasted).
Ask periodically how mana is doing, especially in a pick-up group or a group where a non-cleric is main healer. Concerning whether a group is ready for more mobs, always vote with your healer and crowd control assuming the casters are reasonably good.
There is always some bottleneck or other that will limit the speed at which a group can kill stuff. Sometimes that will be crowd control, sometimes dps, sometimes healing mana. Whatever it is, know it, and do whatever you can to adjust.
Sometimes it is very tempting to switch mobs, but if you're MT avoid it if at all possible. The main exceptions are if you don't have an offtank and a caster is getting pounded.
Sorry if all this is obvious to you. There's other stuff, but mainly your two jobs are to keep aggro and take a punch. If you do those things, that's basically your job. If you can do the other things to make a group more productive and fun, then the people you grouped with before will actively seek you out to play with. The good thing about doing the little things is that the better players you meet will notice long before the idiots will; the good players will want you in their groups, so over time the groups you get are better and better.
If I've done an LDoN or any other group with someone who I've noticed is good, I'll send them a tell complimenting their play at the end of the group/adventure. That also helps over time.
