For the last 6 weeks or so I've had in my bag o' tools a Windblade, Great Staff of Thunder, and a War Marshall's Bladed Staff.

I use the GSoT for when I have to keep aggro. With good DEX for procing, it works the best of all three for aggro, plus packs a nice punch to boot. The only drawback (and it's mostly a minor one) is that its proc has a small knockback effect, so it's not the greatest tool for locking down a mob.

I still use the WMBS a lot, especially in single-group exp sessions. It's got a slightly better ratio than the GSoT and is fast. If the GSoT is a little stingy with procs, the WMBS is a great alternative.

The Windblade I used last night a bit for the first time in over a month. The hits are impressive, but it swings so slowly that it's very situational. For example, last night I was not the MA, nor was I one of the TM's. My job was to avoid aggro and do damage, and the Windblade helped with that.

In literally 99% of all situations I've found myself in during the last six weeks, the GSoT or WMBS have served me well and I've not felt the need to take the windblade out of the bag. I find that I am asking myself if I shouldn't be selling the Windblade and getting some better armor with that money.

If I could only have one of these three weapons, I'd pick the GSoT for overall functionality, with the WMBS a very close second. I'd pick a Windblade over a non-uber dual-wield combo (yes, even over a frosty/whatever combo), but that's about it.

There are some situations where I can see the Windblade being top of the heap, such as against riposting mobs, but lately that's been less of an issue for me (though my warrior isn't in tier 2 PoP yet).
