FFX2 does not have a story.

You just run around on errands forever and eventually some thing is going to destroy Spira and you stop it. That's basically the whole game. Throughout the game you'll find out snippets of what happened 1000 years ago between Bevelle and Zanarkand but that's about it.

I personally couldn't stand Yuna and most certainly not Rikku, but if you don't mind them, it's not a bad game.

The battle engine is the standard Final Fantasy faire. You use dress spheres to change classes which gives you an illusion of doing something matters even though you'll quickly discover White Mage + Dark Knight X 2 or even just Dark Knight X 3 will own anything. The game is real time but all that means is that you can get slapped while choosing which spheres to switch to before you get the Dark Knight spheres. There's a standard level up systems but the real powers come from the class that you're in.

FFX2 just had too much class abuses you can pull. It has no story on purpose. The point is that you as Yuna are just on a big scavenger hunt around Spira while looking for clues of Tidus (not that you ever found any), and you happen to uncover the fact that some evil ancient force is going to destroy Spira, and then you stop it.

Xenosaga is way too tedious. The game can't really be considered hard because you've an infinite supply of HP/EP items (for all practical purposes, I never got close to running out even on the 45 minute Proto Dora fight). The game overuses physical/ether immunity and since you've to pick which techs to develop it is very easy to have a party that simply can't damage the boss. Simeon comes to mind as a ridiculously easy battle that takes way too long. The story is overvalued. There actually isn't a story. It's just a bunch of people saying they've this huge plan and you're all merely pawns in it. Nowhere in Xenosaga Episode 1 do you actually even have a clue what's going on because the powers that be are still holding back their hand. All you're doing is just running around and saving a planet from getting blown up, but that is obviously just the beginning based on what you see on the ending. You don't even know what half of your party really are (Shion, KOS-MOS, Chaos). The game is coherent enough to follow, but I really can't call what happens in the game a plot when it's obviously set up to sell later episodes. The game deliberately fails to explain anything.

Kingdom Hearts I really enjoyed... even if it gets repetetive after a while around Neverland. Hallow Bastion and The End of the World are just beautiful looking worlds.
