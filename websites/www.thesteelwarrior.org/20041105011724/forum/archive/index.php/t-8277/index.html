Sassinak
GoD zones are definitely a different pace of healing, and it will really take some getting used to by the healers. It's not a place where the cleric starts the CH at a prescribed % of your health, at least not once you get past Riwwi. The mob damage is ridiculously streaky, which means the healing must be just as streaky. You will at times go for 20 seconds without taking any real damage, then suddenly you'll need a couple fast heals chained together to save your ass.

Sony started this trend of streaky damage in POP, made it streakier with Hard LDON, and even streakier again in GoD. People could still cope with CH in Hard LDON, but in most of GoD it's the fast expensive heals that rule: Supernal Light and its upgrade, Holy Light. Divine Intervention has also changed from being raid-only to being a valuable single-group tool, once you get to Vxed and beyond.

One additional note - Tribute was a new feature in GoD, and I think the mobs were designed with the assumption that you would use tribute, just like Luclin and POP were balanced around AA's, and LDON was balanced around augments. Tribute takes the edge off of these encounters (HP/AC for you, missing focus effects for your casters, etc.), regardless of your opinion of such arbitrary money sinks.

IMO the vxed and tipt drops help to skew that magelo, if you take them out and replace them with the next best thing you can find in GoD, the magelo should look a lot like a well geared bazaar magelo. Of the 800 hp remaining and the AC, you're likely to be able to make a good bit of that up with a few bazaar upgrades (RZ helm, better rings, a smattering of other minor upgrades like neck), and for the rest of the difference will be made up a lot more easily by one groupable content from past expansions (especially ldon hards for loot).
