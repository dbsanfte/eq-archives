Giving us provoke is not a case of "We're giving you B, so we can now take away A, to keep you even". Right now, pre-provoke, warriors are so far behind in agro that for provoke to replace procs *I would say for the majority of warriors, stun procs on weapons such as CHoS and SotBs are their only reliable means of agro*, it would have to be implemented a LOT differently than it has been running on test server, such as by having the hate DoT part of it running for the duration of the encounter. Right now, with procs, it is possible for a tank to ghetto mez tank a few mobs at once. Remove procs, and you would somehow have to find a way to have that once again possible. As for the LDoN procs, yes they are great, and I can't wait to get them. However, at this time, over 3 months after LDoN came out, I have a grand total of 900 adventure points, and the vast majority of those are in Mistmoore. My playtime is fairly limited between work and school, and it seems that the evenings I am on, most of my time is devoted to guild events. While I think I'm in the minority here probably, I also feel that there are a fair number of people in the same situation as me.

I personally think that Sony will leave the proc stuns alone. I'm sure that a stun proc can quite easily be coded differently than a cast stun spell, if they aren't already *just going by the spells cast by the mobs in HoT, which are already classed as procs, and so are immune to casting interruptions, etc... just shows that procs can be coded differently, imo*. One reason I think supports this, is the fact that the majority of stun proc weapons are late additions to the game, and have been made readily available. While I suppose Sony could have implemented that as a temporary band-aid cure, for some reason I don't think they did.

One possible idea is to have Sony scale up the mana requirements of the spell, based on the caster's level. For example, if a level 60 is casting a level 9 spell, have it cost almost the same amount of mana as the level 60 *or closest to* version of that spell. Combine that with the spell having no effect on the mob for virtually the same amount of mana, and I think that would fix the problem. Not sure if this is doable or not though.

Anyways, just some thoughts.
