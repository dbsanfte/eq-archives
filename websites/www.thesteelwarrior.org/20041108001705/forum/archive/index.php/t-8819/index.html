wandor
Wow. Let's give some facts instead of wild conjecture.

First of all, there is a specific formula that determines how many points of exp a particular mob gives. The basic formula is:

mob level x mob level x ZEM = exp gained

The ZEM is the zone experience modifier. On top of the basic formula, you get bonuses added to the base number. You get additional bonuses for fighting mobs near your level, and higher bonuses for fighting mobs that are yellow and red. The higher the level the mob is, the higher the bonus is. This explains why a level 62 toon gets more exp per mob than a 65 does killing the same mob. The mob will necessarily be higher level in comparison to the 62 toon so they will get a larger bonus. While that sounds like a good deal, a group of 65s can kill mobs at a faster rate, and they can fight higher level mobs more effectively, negating any advantage a lover level player can get. The fact that the raw experience goes up exponentially as mob level increases means that you are much better off killing higher level mobs for maximum experience gain.

There is also a group bonus that starts when you have at least 3 people in your group. The best bonus being when you have a full group of 6. When you have 6 people in the group, the exp is still divided by 5 and then awarded to 6 people, so in essence each mob is now worth 20% more than if you killed the same mob with 5 people.

Knowing this, we can tell that there will be plenty of pre-OoW exp areas that will still give good experience. The mobs in PoFire at the 2nd castle and the Field of Gib are 65+ so the experience there will be good. Same with mobs deeper in PoW, Tipt, Vxed, KT, and Qvic. I would assume that there will be zones in OoW that are good exp as well.

Now, back to the age old AA vs. level question. Before OoW it was generally believed that level > AA for the many advantages that one gained from getting additional levels. I am a firm believer in that still today and I am sure that the same will hold true in OoW. Character level relative to mob level has played an important factor in the combat engine and the resist system. The fact is that the higher your level is, the more favorable the outcome against any particular mob. While you can certainly gain quite a bit of advantage by gaining AAs, the amount of AAs you would have to gain to receive a similar advantage as gaining 5 levels would take a lot more raw exp to gain in a given amount of time.

Get your levels. They are far more important than any AA skills you could get in the time it would take you to level to 70.

Notes on exp modifiers:

- Merely by grouping with anybody (doesn't matter size of group), you automatically negate any penalties of race or class, and this experience isn't taken out of anybody else's experience in group.

- there is a bonus to exp based upon members in group, and 6th person basically is free (so there is no difference in how much exp you'll gain in a group if you go from 5 people to 6 people).

- If the mob is within a few levels of you (I think it's 3), there will be an exp bonus, this is calculated independent of what other people in the group get. So for instance, if your group is killing all whites / yellows, and you're level 60, other people are all level 65, you will gain bonus, but they won't .

quote:
"As of this update, groups now gain 20% to 80% "group bonus" experience for having two to five members.

Full, six-person, groups now see an additional bonus. Their experience is only divided five ways, yet they still gain the 80% "group bonus." For those who think in terms of pie charts, it amounts to a larger piece of a larger pie. "
---end quote---

So formula is actually pretty complicated versus how it first started out.

The exp needed per level was basically level x level x level x (racial mod) x (class mod).

The exp per mob was basically level x level x unknown factor x zone exp mod. This is how it remained for a while, until they started making additional steps to the formula.

Now, there are more steps that werent' present when game first started, if character is in a group

group exp mod (takes the amount of exp gained and multiplies it on an amount based upon number of members in group). This is applied to everybody in group.

Individual exp mod:
- You get a bonus based upon race and or class which basically cancels out any penalty you may have had.
- you get a bonus based upon level of mob vs. your current level.

So basically it goes in several steps when calculating exp, let's take an example of smoeone in a 6 person group, let's say everybody is 65, and one person is level 60, and the mobs being fought are level 61, let's say we're hunting in PoI with a 44% exp mod bonus (normal ZEM is .75, so this will be 1.19), and your class is a iksar shadowknight (1.2 racial penalty, 1.4 multiplier for being a SK).

Per kill:
base exp: (*Mob exp squared* 61 x 61 = 3721) * ZEM (1.19) = ~4428
group exp bonus: 1.8 * (mob exp) 4428 = ~7970
divided 5 ways: 7970 / 5 = 1594 per group member.
Now we have counter race and class: 1594 * 1.6 (they add together not multiply) = 2550 exp
lastly, mob level vs. player level bonus (unknown, but let's hypothesize and say 1.5?) 2550 * 1.5 = 3825

So in this instance, the exp for this character gained would be 3825, however the racial penalty and class penalty doesn't apply for AA points, but does apply towards regular exp. Say you wanted to find out how much a 65 human shadowknight in same group got in AA exp: (3721 (mob exp) * 1.19 (ZEM) * 1.8 (group bonus)) / 5 (group split) * 1 (race & class, equals one since AA) * 1 (for mob bonus) = 1594

So, in this instance, the 60 char is getting twice as much actual exp. Should they switch this to AA exp only however, you would see the exp drop from 3825 to 2390 actual points gained towards AA exp, which is still a fair margain over the 1594 pts a 65 would get in AA.

I tested this out with my war (56 when starting, 58 currently) and cleric (65) combo. At first ungrouped, war would gain about 3.5% AA per kill, or about 1.5% raw exp as he was very close to some of the mob levels (such as against an archaic model). Later tested same combo against archaic model, but grouped, cleric only got about 1 % AA after a kill, and war only got around 1.8% of an AA). Conclusion was since character was a dwarf war, there was no inherent advantage in grouping with cleric (other than 20 % bonus for 2 people in group) as far as cancelling out racial penalties / class penalties, so I have the cleric ungrouped and have been able to get the exp very fast.
