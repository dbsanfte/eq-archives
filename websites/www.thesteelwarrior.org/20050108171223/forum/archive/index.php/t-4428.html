Having some time to read over the posted changes, and give some thought to questions for the chat, how about we compile a definitive list here?

Would be nice if everyone able to attend would be able to take in the same (fairly) comprehensive list so that the most important questions we as a community come up with have a good chance of being asked.

Compiled from posts - topics chosen based on frequency of related questions, in no particular order of importance. There has been some (minor) rewording and not all questions are included (covered by another or merged into a single question)

If I missed any ... post again :D

Warrior concerns (Agro!)

Since adequate (as judged by potential groupmates) survivability and aggro control are fundamental tanking characteristics, are Warriors to be tanks or are they being re-defined as Rogues (special attacks) that fight from the front?
The single biggest issue facing the warrior community has been agro generation as compared to knights. That is, warriors have a very short range unreliable method of gaining agro compared to knights ability to generate and control agro at range and at will. If all “openings” are random occurrences, how do these changes address this issue?
Will agro produced from the endurance stun ability produce more hate than the cast stun spells?
Are there plans for further changes to hate generation? If so, do they include a means independent of the RNG and/or triggerable for warriors to generate hate


Itemisation

Will there be "Endurance Points" retroactively added to existing items?
Will there be any conversion for mana to "Endurance Points" for existing items?
If so, have you considered the effect this will have on those who have focussed on gear without mana?
Will there be focus effects on items to enhance Endurance usage as there is for mana? Eg “Flowing Endurance”
From where will items with “Endurance Points/Effects” be obtained?


Regenerating the Endurance Pool

How can we regen endurance?
Which classes will get "endurance regen" spells?
Will melee get their own endurance regen abilities in the same way casters get their own mana regen spells? How will this be implemented? (AA, skill such as Improved Bind Wounds etc)
Will some classes regen endurance faster or slower than other classes?
Will there be a way to turn endurance into health to lower downtime?
Will endurance regen be stat based? Eg higher STA = faster regen
Will sitting help?
Will there be a regen difference between standing still and running/fighting ?


Disciplines

Will disciplines still have the same duration as they do currently? Or will they end when the endurance pool is emptied? Or whichever comes first?
Are there any plans to add new disciplines, or implement current discipline upgrades?


Availability/Ability Restrictions

How accessible will these new abilities be? I.e. as with spells, will the majority of them (say, up to level 60) be available from trainers/merchants in the Plane of Knowledge? Will more time/effort (read: high-level raids) be required to obtain the more rare abilities?
Will there be any race/deity/level restrictions on any of these abilities and, if, so, what will they be?
What particular skills will be special to the warrior/monk/rogue alone and how do you see them as promoting a warrior/monk/rogue to groups over another pure melee?


Using Them

Are we using hotkeys, or an instant-spell type system? If they are hot-keys, will the hot-key window be expanded beyond 10 keys/page?
Do these "opening" messages appear in the chat window or some new XML window?


Stats

Which stats will the endurance pool be based on?
Will these stats affect the endurance pool equally or will their influence vary; if it varies, how?
Will stats influence the costs of abilities at all?
Will pure melee's be able to upgrade the cap for the stat, which will define the endurance pool, via AA points to 355, like casters can for wis/int?


Mechanics

How often are openings going to appear?
Will some openings not be random, and produced at (limited) will? E.g. on a successful Bash,
Will de-buffs like "cripple" or other special de-buffs be available which increase the rate of opportunities?
Will all the skills made available depend on "openings"? Or are those only certain skills, while others depend solely on Endurance, or solely on a timer, or both? (or maybe on anything else? like stats)
What part will character versus mob positioning play in terms of the frequency/type of openings that appear? E.g. In front versus behind versus ranged (archery)
What kind of skill trees are we looking at? Diablo 2 style?
Will training of one combat skill type prevent you from training another type?
Is some form of Book of Tactics being thought of, for keeping acquired abilities? Or will the abilities be available directly through a List, like it was made available for casters recently?
Will the "stun" attack mentioned in the example be treated as a melee stun or a spell stun?
Will a "snare" type of attack leave an icon? How long will it last?
How long will the AC de-buff attack last? Stacking issues?
You said some openings will apply to all people in range, does that mean that skills of one class will have some kind of synergy with skills of another class e.g. allowing two or more people to link skills for greater or more effects ) OR does is simply mean it is a time where everyone around can use their skills all at once?
Following on from this, will there be potential for renkei style skillchains like found in FFXI? Ie., say the warrior does a special move which is immediately followed by the monk executing a related move which allows the rogue to execute his move for a large damage bonus.
Will the "openings" and small timeframe for reaction, be based on the client side, or the server side?
How are the "Openings" going to work during high lag situations?
What current Melee tools will be changed to make way for this change?
What will be the effective range for these special abilities? Will we be able to use them at max melee range, or will they have a shorter range, such as taunt and disarm?
Will any races get an innate bonus for endurance in much the same way that trolls and iksar have innate hp regen bonuses?


Miscellaneous

When will this system be put in place? I'm not looking for a definite date, but are we talking a few weeks or a few months here?
How will you be testing the system prior to introducing it?
How will this affect Hybrids in a direct way? I.e. Will hybrids get the same endruance disciplines as pure melee? Or are their existing disciplines getting endurance pools only.
How do SoE expect these changes to affect the warriors’ groupability/ability to perform the main tank role?
Will mobs be able to do this too?
Will there be any new AA added to the game associated with these new combat enhancements? If so, what kind of AA?
Will certain Melee AAs or current skills be linked to Endurance?
Will the Warrior AA Warcry be modified in order to act as a sort of Group Paragon/spirit of wood that refreshes the Endurance Bar over time such as 36 or 48 seconds?
Will existing skills such as Disarm, taunt, kick, backstab, flying kick(+other monk moves), Instill doubt, etc, move to this new system, and as such require endurance? Or will they remain on their current timers?
Will the current content of the game be tweaked a bit, to be more compatible with the new powers available? (cf Velious for casters) Or is the plan to keep the change small enough to fit into the current content?

This isn't a question to ask, but rather a recommendation on how to approach the chat session and the new system in general.

Don't go into this using the mentality that Sony has to deal directly with the current issues as they exist today (ex: taunt.) They may not give us snap agro, but that doesn't mean they won't fix the current balance issue. I agree, snap agro is huge the way the game currently works - but that doesn't mean the only way to balance the classes is to give warriors snap agro. Changing how the game works is also another viable alternative.

Also, I think we need to be open to the possibility the function our class serves may change slightly. Maybe knights will remain the best choice for initial agro, but maybe warriors will be able to give the group a big advantage by taking over tanking once the mob is engaged - therefore making both classes desirable.

I'm not saying this is how the system will work - at this point I don't know. But I am saying we should be open to possibilities and focus on class balance - not necessarily changing how specific existing features work.

Rather than taking the easy solution of tweaking the existing framework, Sony has decided to upgrade the entire combat system - taking some ideas from the newer games, and adding some twists of their own. In my opinion this is very ambitious and far thinking approach by Sony, and bodes well for the future of EQ. I say this because this approach is not the cheap way out and shows they see a future for this game. In return, we need to have some flexibility and patience in our thinking.
