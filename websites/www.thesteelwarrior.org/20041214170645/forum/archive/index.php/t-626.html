People want efficient healing, and having defensive always available at a toggle would make certain that no other class(paladin, sk) ever see tanking of an uber mob unless all the warriors are dead.

So arguments against toggle defensive for me are :
-Current defensive allow for more tank transitions on tough mobs or on successive mob farm which imo is good for tanks in the guild.
-It allow sk and paladin to be tank at a lesser efficienty but still at a manageable efficienty since a non defensive warrior is very similar to a paladin/sk. Having defensive available all the time would ensure that paladins/sk are always inferior tank by a very large margin, which is bad for class balance.

On the other hand, having offensive available at a toggle would imo be clearly overpowered in the current game. Offensive is by far the best dps disc in the game by a very large margin. The penalty for using offensive are that you do not have another disc available and that as a warrior with no mean of aggro evasion, if you aggro or if the previous tank die, you risk of dying very fast since most mob can kill you in 1 or 2 round under that disc. These 2 penalties would be inexistant under a toggled /disc.

Actually, thinking about it more, in the current state of the game having all these disc available at a toggle would be quite retarded. You would see a constant spam of warrior changing state. For example, every fight warriorswould enter defensive, then switch to offensive as aggro is established. MT lose aggro? 5 spam of tank going defensive, followed by 4 tank going offensive 5 seconds later.

In term of dps it would also mean that high end warrior would outdmg monk all the time and rogue most of the time. It would probably be more fun thought indeed, switching very often, trying to avoid the riposte for 4.5k and doing more dmg then all the other melee or so.. but it would not be very balanced.

I don't really feel that warrior have that much a problem at the lvl I play at, I'm certain we could use some improvements like most of the classes in some area, but I would really be against this solution for the reasons I stated.
