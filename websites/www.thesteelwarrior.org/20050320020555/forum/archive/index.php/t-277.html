my group now is 61 war (me) 62 cleric, 61 enchanter and 62 shamman. We flew through the 50s but 60+ the low dps realy starts to make most fights insanely dificult. Our DPS isn't terrible, a few levels ago we did do the PoJ trials with the shammy in the group so it isn't that bad. But as soon as you hit the harder mobs of tier 1 and anything in tier 2 you will find that your lack of DPS makes the groups boring. This is where you make more friends, rogues, necros, mages, wizzies, and some monks can realy fill in a nice 5th/6th spot.

It all depends on where you want to go. We now have a long friends list of all these classes and while tier 2 is still hard to tank as a 61 warrior with 5600/1200 group only buffed hp/ac (as in we don't run around finding buffs outside the group) it is doable. This is farther then you are realy looking though.

Try the Hobgoblin camp in PoN. That seems to be the tier 1 litmus test. Yes there are tougher mobs and harder 1 group encounters then that in tier 1, but if you can constently pull that camp, with the occasional add (unmezzable unrootable) without trying to hard then you are doing ok. If not, you need to rethink your group. There are dozens of options concerning who you can add to make the group work. For a while when the enchanter and shaman couldn't play I was doign a 2 cleric, necro, mage, bard, and me group; just stacking damage shields and letting unslowed mobs impale themselves... worked well.

Again, its all about what you want to do. I get the impression that you are just worried about cranking out the exp. In that case, forget the shaman, grab 3 rogues and take only the safest camps you can find.
