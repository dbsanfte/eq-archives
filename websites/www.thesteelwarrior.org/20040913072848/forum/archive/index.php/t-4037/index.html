Telen
Wizards are a powerful solo class, and at high level they have minimal downtime. But the reality is all that you do is cast spells, and usually you are focused on direct damage. Occasionally you stun or interrupt or snare or root for crowd control, but mostly it's just sit, stand, nuke, sit, stand, nuke. Or if you have a horse, nuke, nuke, nuke. Of course there are pauses or concussions cast in there, but it's pretty repetitive. I grew to hate grouping with my wizard. In a group all I did was nuke and try to time my stuns to prevent gates. Usually someone else was on crowd control. It became boring. Now I only enjoy soloing with my wizard. That is more of a challenge. Going into fairly high level dungeons solo, like Droga, Nurga, Veksar, Lower Guk, and I find soloing in these places to be much more fun than grouping. I have to handle crowd control, I have to keep the mobs at bay so I can nuke, I have to worry about getting aggro, I have to so all the things I am not needed for in a group. Wo am I rambling. My point is that for me the damage of the wizard was the initial allure, but then I realized later on that is all that wizards do, nuke, over and over.

Warriors have a much more difficult job. We oftentimes get to pull. We always get to tank. We have a diversity of weapons for different situations. We have to work at keeping aggro and keeping mobs off the casters, keeping mobs on us to keep others alive. We have to move around to stay on mobs. A wizard just stands there and nukes. A wizards hardest job is keeping aggro off them.

The classes are nearly exact opposites. A wizard is all offense. A warrior is mostly defense (although we do ok dps). I think it's the allure of doing something completely opposite from what I did for over 3 years that got me into a warrior.
