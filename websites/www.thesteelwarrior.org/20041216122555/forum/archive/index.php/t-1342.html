The only problem I see with this is that you are assuming that the only Avoidance checks are based on agility. This is not the case at all - in fact, the effects of AGI on your displayed AC value are quite small.

When you equip a piece of armor (say raw AC 10 for argument's sake), your displayed AC goes up by more than just 10. If I remember correctly, the raw 10 AC is added to your mitigation, and the extra value is avoidance (which is why monks gain more displayed AC for equipping an item with the same raw ac stat). How much AC you gain in avoidance is determined by your class and Defense skill.

In theory, if you did manage to tweak your numbers so you got the same displayed AC with different levels of avoidance and mitigation, it should average out to be nearly identical in terms of damage taken over time. That's why they simplified the displayed value in the first place.
