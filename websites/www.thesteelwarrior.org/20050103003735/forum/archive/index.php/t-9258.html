10-07-2004, 02:17 AM
The only problem with Aura of Runes and Savage Onslaught is the endurance they require. From a pure defensive point of view, using Stonewall as soon as it refreshes is obviously more effective than using Aura of Runes, and Fellstrike likewise is better than using Savage Onslaught. This means you'd never use either of the discs unless you've so much endurance you can use both of the offensive or both of the defensive discs. Given the lack of endurance regen, a lot of time you simply don't have that luxury.

From eyeballing (it is hard to parse the exact benefit of Savage Onslaught) I'd put Savage Onslaught at about 100 DPS, compare to 200+ DPS using Fellstrike. I think it's fair to have Savage Onslaught cost as much as Fellstrike. I understand Savage Onslaught isn't meant to be Fellstrike 2 but currently there is almost no compelling reason to ever use it unless Fellstrike is down and you still have 5K+ endurance sitting around.

On the other hand, Aura of Rune could use a significant endurance cost reduction. The proc nature of it prevents it from being a reliable damage mitigation. I certainly can't imagine Aura of Runes ever beating Stonewall in terms of damage mitigation in any serous scenario so it should cost less than Stonewall (per second).

Aura of Runes has proved to be the most min or max random thing I've ever seen in this game so far. I've never seen it in the middle, only all or nothing.

The very first time I ever used it was while tanking XTC. Stonewall was down, so I decided to hit this for the hell of it since I just got it that day and wanted to see how well it worked. It proced constantly. It was up for every other, near every, round the mob threw at me, mitigating 400 damage way from nearly every round. I praised the disc. I shouted into guildsay at how wicked the new disc was. The "rune effect" buff was constantly flashing in my song window, the second it would come up the mob would knock it down, then it would proc right back up again.

Next time I used it was on some low 100 damage hitting mob. I thought, "Hell, with how much this thing proced the last time, I'm gonna be near invulnerable to this little guy for a full minute". It did not proc once. Not once at all.

Next few times I used it, it proced once in the entire minute.

Few more completely void of a single proc uses at later dates after that.

One time in Grieg's end helping my friend powerlevel his little mage, I used it and was completely invulnerable to the mob for 1 minute. Proced constantly again like it did the first time I used it.

I've used it a lot off and on since then. Everytime I've used it, it either didn't proc at all, proced once or twice in the entire minute, or proced constantly. Never in between.

I've seen it proc constant, almost every round, while having 129 DEX. I've seen it not proc at all, not once, while having 315 DEX.

The proc hasn't averaged well for me at all. It's either been all or nothing. Constant procs, or hardly any procs at all (far more no procs at all than hardly any procs). It doesn't seem to be tied to DEX at all like normal procs are either from what I've been able to tell so far.

This proc is nice when it actually procs, but it's a horrible "gamble". I'd never use this proc if I had furious or fortitude available. It's a complete gamble whether it does anything at all. It's a last chance, the very last thing you'll want to fall back on. While it could save your life one time, the next it could do nothing but drain endurance.

The drain it takes on endurance isn't really that big of a deal I don't think. I mean, after all, I'm never gonna use the thing unless I have no other discs left to use with my characters life on the line. So it could use my entire endurance bar for all I care, cause if it actually works I've been saved and have no discs left to use endurance on anyway. But if it doesn't proc at all (which has been often) I'm gonna have whatever endurance that's available after a rez anyway... so it's endurance cost is moot as far as I'm concerned.
