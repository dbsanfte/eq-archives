From page 2 of this forum, which was stolen from the magelo forums:

HP = TRUNC[ 5 x ND ] + TRUNC [ Lvl x LM x ND ] + TRUNC [ TRUNC [ ( STA + TRUNC [(STA-255)/2] ) x Lvl x LM / 300 ] x ND ]

Where the ND is the multiplier bonus they use (Natural Durability, Physical Enhancement, etc ...) and where the term TRUNC [(STA-255)/2] is always 0 or greater (i.e. when STA is below 255, it is considered equal to 0). LM is a class and level dependent multiplier as indicated below:

Warrior:
Level 1-19: Levelmultiplier = 22
Level 20-29: Levelmultiplier = 23
Level 30-39: Levelmultiplier = 25
Level 40-52: Levelmultiplier = 27
Level 53-56: Levelmultiplier = 28
Level 57-59: Levelmultiplier = 29
Level 60-65: Levelmultiplier = 30



so from this formula you can derive that at 60+, ever point of stamina (up to 255) is equal to TRUNC((LVL X 30)/300), which is 6 (trunc means to drop the decimal, so at 65 1 stam = 6.5 which = 6 hps still)
this 6 per stam point is then multiplied by your ND modifier, which is 1.XX (XX being your AA point adjustments, like 1.2, 1.3, etc). The end result is also truncated.

every point over 255 stamina is halved, but since its truncated, it basically takes 2 points to have a gain, so 256 stam = useless, but 257 should be TRUNC(6 X ND) more hps.

or you can adjust your magelo, same thing.

edit: also because of Truncation, 1 point of stamina may only be worth 6 hps (up to 255, ex. TRUNC((1X65X30)/300)= 6hps) but 10 points of stamina can be over 60hps TRUNC((10X65X30)/300)) = 65 hps
