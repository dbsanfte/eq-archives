I ordered for a few reasons:

1. I have several RL friends doing this game. Some are quitting their time-enabled EQ characters and selling their accounts now (while they can actually get decent ching). Some, like me, are going to raid in EQ and play in CoH to see how it is.

2. CoH has a system that allows a level 40 to group with a level 20 character. The level 20 is called a "sidekick" which fits perfectly with the superhero genre. If there was one frustration for me and EQ - the inability to group with a friend that plays 10 hours a week and I play 30 hours a week - this was it. The level 20 hero will have their abilities scale up to (for example) level 35 when in a certain distance of the level 40 hero.

3. I can tell you that the play is extremely smooth and well done. The UI is very clean, IMO. The character generation is absolutely fucking awesome - and that aint my thing - my thing is the play mechanics.

4. Instanced and scaled encounters. Not only are the encounters scaled by level they are scaled to your "archtype" (similar to class). Most quest stuff seems to be instanced - no kill stealing.

5. Some will say there is no loot. But, well, there is...they have things that are called inspirations and enhancements. Each superhero has abilities - a kick, a mind control, whatever. These abilities scale with level and have slots for enchancements(similar to slots from ldon stuff but abilities not items). You get enhancements from completing "story arcs" (quests). Enhancements "attach" to the abilities thus making your hero stronger - so, no boots of ass kickin - just a +50 damage enchancement that attaches to your kick ability.

Then there are inspirations. Think potions. One shot modifiers that last a short time.

6. My 12 yo son is into comic book stuff. I intend to play with him and have fun.

7. Starting on the ground floor. I came in way late into the EQ game - I don't like that.


The one big innovation I point to is the ability for the power gamer and the casual gamer to group. Its pretty cool.

I would say this game will only mildly hurt EQ if at all. Now WoW -that's a different story.

A couple of clarifications should be made about CoH:
Yes, having a character be a sidekick will allow a lower-level character to join with a higher-level character for some fighting fun, but regular (non-sidekick) grouping is within a very limited range of levels (just three, near as I can see).
City of Heroes is distributed by NCSoft (the Lineage people), but it was developed by Cryptic Studios, not NC.
The pre-redesign CoH game (the redesign took place well over a year ago, before anyone was ever in a Beta) was an almost complete copy of Champions (the pen-and-paper superhero RPG). It has since gone more "mainstream", with generic classes and logical power sets being grouped together. It's still fun, though.
Personally, I expect that CoH will hold my interest marginally for a while, at least. From the point of genre and personal character involvement, it does not compete with EQ and should not be compared as such. WoW, on the other hand, is a direct competitor, though I'm waffling on whether I'll try that one at all.

Level 7 Science Scrapper, Level 6 Science Blaster, Level 5 Magic Controller, Level 4 Technology Defender here. What I've seen so far is low end solo play. Damn work!

Anyways, let no one fool you - with decent skill, ANY class is solo capable, and ANY class is capable of soloing missions. In a system where 3 same level mobs are the equivalent of one PC for power, soloability is huge, at least up to where I'm at.

Missions can have a cookie cutter feel to them - my two Science guys both got undead missions early on, and the levels were VERY similar. Then the game throws you a curveball, like the mission my Technology Defender just did, where the map had three floors, and actually stuck the hard mobs in the front of the map - the end boss was green, but the lieutenants by the door were yellow. Whether this is beta bugginess or deliberate, still a bit jarring.

Mobs are plentiful to hunt in the city zones, and the system of experience sharing helps to avoid the dread KSing. The fact that random mobs drop the Enhancements and Inspirations, and that there is no such thing as 'rare loot' also helps.

Making a character is just a complete blast, and if EQ is where you came from, you'll be doubly impressed by both the equipment variety and the color options open to you. The choosing of powers seems a bit restrictive, but when you hit level 6, it gets REAL strategic. Do you opt to open up the third power set where flight and miscellaneous other good powers reside, or do you stick with your base power sets? Do you open enhancement slots on your bread and butter powers or do you extend the exotic ones? The choices, while simple, are vast enough that even if you find someone who picked the exact same origin and archetype as you AND the same powersets, chances are you two won't have similar abilities, and even if you did, both would be different based on your level and enhancements.

There is a definite progression feel to leveling as a new toon - both in the quick acquisition of powers and the constant desire to keep upgrading your enhancements. The fact that you can 'outgrow' your enhancements gives you a good reason to keep building influence - if you're unlucky in drops, you can always buy the enhancements that give you the edge.

Graphics are good and solid, Max Payneish to my eyes. Very little lag, except for the time they stress tested by putting 5200 on a server at once, heh :eek: The fact that even city zones are instanced is a great help. Can't find any Hellions up? Go to the monorail and switch zones! Too much lag in Atlas Park? Go to Atlas Park 3!

It's not perfect by any means, but it is definitely worth a look if you

- are tired of the gear grind
- play in shorter spans than the average EQ player
- dislike camping
- like comics

Wompem Ondahed
I have been in CoH Beta and the NDA has been lifted. If you are unfamiliar with CoH, relax, we all are.

REQUIREMENTS:
800mhz P2 or better. Geforce2/ATI 8500 or better. 128 ram. I know many of you are saying an ATI 7200/7500 will spank most Geforce2's, but it has to do with 'OpenGL' support in the gfx card and drivers. Even at minimum computer specs, this game is very playable, and with more memory/cpu/card power, it only gets better. You can choose Video settings, and right now I have everything maxed. It looks like a neon wet-dream ( ok, maybe not that good, but still ).

Character Creation:
First you choose your Origin ( Mutant, Magic, Science, Natural, and Technology). Origin is important in what kind of missions you get and what sort of Enhancements you will use ( more on Enhancements later). Next you select your Archetype, which is basically Class in EQ. Archetype determines your choices of powers: Blaster, Tanker, Defender, Controller and Scrapper. This may sound like a limited system, but each Archetype chooses their primary and secondary power sets in the beginning. There are tons of combinations just among these.

Costume Creation:
First you choose your sex/size. Females from 4' to 8' tall, males the same, and HUGE size males ( Hulk, Juggernaut etc). muscle size from Lean/Athletic to Musclebound bodybuilder. The choices are pretty great. You have literally millions of combinations for costumes. I have yet to see two people who look the same unless they are trying to clone a trademarked comic hero.

Starting Out: Instanced Zones-
You start out in Atlas Park, an area of the city with huge monuments to the heroes of the past and present. Each zone has multiple instances of itself. Atlas Park has AP1-5. If AP1 is too laggy, choose AP2 or higher, chances are you will easily find an instance that has an acceptable amount of Hero to Villain ratio for killing.

Changing Instances:
To change from one Public instanced zone to another you can goto a zoneline to another area of the city, or hit the subway/train station. When you cross the line or click the door you will have a quick pop-up menu asking what zone/instance. It is all very simple. Plus, you zone way quicker than in EQ.

Contacts and Missions:
A contact is a friend or acquaintance you have been introduced to. You will recieve missions from them ( quests ) as well as be able to buy Enhancements/Inspirations ( more on those in a bit ). Some missions are to 'arrest/defeat' 10 specific kinds of criminals, such as Hellions and Skulls ( petty thugs addicted to 'Supradine', a drug that gives them temporary powers etc ). After completing a mission you return to the contact. You get XP and Influence ( cash ) after every mob you defeat, as well as mission completion xp. You can also XP by saving random ppl in distress on the street.

Enhancements and Inspirations:
An Inspiration is like an EQ potion, but it is instant casting and lasts 1 minute. Never fear, combat lasts from 3 to 20 seconds. This game is fast paced and fun. You can have up to 10 Inspirations at a time. Don't be afraid to use them, as you can purchase a new one with your influence.
Enhancements are gem icons you can link to a specific power. You can get 'Endurance reduction, 'Dmg Increase' etc etc. It is a very simple and complex system. These enhancements can be traded to friends for free, in exchange for other enhancements or influence or inspirations. You can also sell them back to a store and get influence to purchase a different one.

Death:
In CoH they use Rikti Technology left over from the war ( aliens ) to summon a hero to a hospital a moment before they die. Basically if you fall over you can choose to spend a Revive Inspiration, get Rezzed by a friend, or click the hospital. You can also trade while you are down, so a friend can loan you a Revive Inspiration. Near-death creates an XP Debt. Basically you can not loose a level because that is silly, but instead you will spend half your xp earned to pay off the current debt. It isn't as bad as it sounds.

Travel:
To travel in CoH, everyone starts with SPRINT, a minor endurance cost to run faster than the average human. At level 6 you can choose a minor travel power, such as Hover, Combat Leaping, or Teleport Foe ( reverse CotH). At level 14 you get a major transportation power: Super Leap ( Hulk style Leaping on buildings ), Flight, Teleport Self, Super speed, Invisibility etc etc.

Getting New Powers:
Every other level you get to choose a new power from either Primary or Secondary ( those you started with ) or from a Power Pool ( those you get later in life ). Between these levels you get an Enhancement Slot, where you can put 1 more enhancement on a current power. It is a very cool system, and you always feel like your character is growing, because he/she is.

Old Powers:
Older powers never become useless. They are a part of your character, and remain a valid power through their development. They will get more powerful with age. this is a relief, as you will not feel as though you have to save choices for later in life.


Anyhow, thats it from me. I am long winded, but I hope this gives you a good preview of City of Heroes.
