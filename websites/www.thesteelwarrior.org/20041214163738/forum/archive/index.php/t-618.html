I see two areas of dmg that need to be looked at and both are lopsided towards casters IMO.

1. Sustained damage. Most high level casters are able to sustain a safe, continuous flow of dmg through spells that is still WAY more than what most melee can do. Some melee come closer than others, but all pretty much lag behind the casters in this area.

2. Burst damage. Casters do even more damage at higher risk if they throw caution to the wind and fire away -- the amount of dmg they can do is awesome when you factor in their AA skills, focus effects and such. Melees rely on disciplines primarily for this (at least non-tanking warriors do) -- fire off an offensive disc and you get a nice burst of damage, but again, this still lags behind what casters can dish out through spells.

So really both of these need to be looked at separately.

Sustained damage they can fix by changing damage tables (my personal preference), skill caps, damage bonuses or something like that. Since this is for regular damage you do all the time there should be no added defensive risks associated with this type of change.

For burst damage they could fix that with discipline or AA changes, but for this there should be greater risk added to be able to use it.

Spell is 2740 damage for 640 mana.
Actual mana cost = ~(0.8)640 = 512 because of specialization and SCM3
512 mana / 15 mana per tick = ~34 ticks to regen the mana used, or about 204 seconds.
2740 damage / 204 secs = 13.4 dps
Multply that by 1.4 or so for focus effects and AAs = 18.8 DPS
Very rough calculations, I admit.
