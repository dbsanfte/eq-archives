Hi all,

After a gruelling quest I finally completed the warrior epic last weekend thanks to lots of help and patience from friends. :)

After the ooh-ing and aah-ing over how cool it looks (which it still does :p ), obviousely I was wondering how it fared in combat. So far the best I can say is I have mixed feelings about it. Rage of Vallon obviousely should be the prime attraction, but I wasn't expecting it to solve all aggro problems overnight.

If it lands it certainly seems to turn the mobs head at you, more so than any other 1 hander I've had, but here is the problem. It doesn't seem to proc much and doesn't seem to annoy mobs much if resisted. (Even with dex buffed up to 260, I have yet to see a significant increase in proccage). Even when it does proc, I have noticed aggro is fairly can be lost to another melee who will most likely be doing more dmg than you are. At 61 I seem to hit for about 95 in mainhand max, with full buffs on. Offhand seems to be around 55-60. With a good string of procs the dps doesn't seem to matter much, but procs are just so unreliable.

As far as I can tell I was able to hold aggro more consistently with the Greastaff of Thunder I used before i finished the epic. The reason I see for this is that next to the high aggro proc on that it is also churning out a real good amount of DPS very quickly.(max hit for about 190) The problem with most 2 handers seems to be that it will aggro if you get a string of good hits, but is lost quickly with a few misses. The staff is fast enough for that not to matter. I was going to sell it to buy a CoF, but not so sure now

Sooo, despite the nice worn effect on the blade of tactics, I figure it may be a good idea to replace it at some point. Stuff like the Shortblade of Lightning or Fleshgrinder seems to be good for damage, but I kind of doubt it would match the staff for dps with red epic in offhand. My guild is not yet capable of killing the PoI dragon or killing in NToV so I 'll need to either buy something or get a nodrop 1hander that is not too hard to get. The Willsapper came to mind, but Vaniki is heavily camped obviousely. Basically I'm wondering what other warriors at this point have used in combination with their red epic that turned out to be a good combo.

Don't get me wrong, that final combine in Freeport was one of the best feelings I have had in EQ, and the epic is a source of pride to anyone who gets it (which after all is what the game is about; achieving something together), but three expansions later it is clear it is a weapon from a different era.
