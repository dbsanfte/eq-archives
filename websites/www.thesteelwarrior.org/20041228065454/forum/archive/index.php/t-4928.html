Everyone really needs to stop calling the mitigation change 5% as much as possible. Aside from it causing upset to some knight players (not all, just a few 'vocal' ones) it also doesn't seem true.

For clearer example of why it's not, imagine a mob with a damage bonus of 9000 and a DI of 50.

This mob's min hit would be 9050 and max hit of 10000.

5% would take off 500 from the max hit.

However the mitigation change that we got would in theory seem to just take off 50 from the max hit so it would look like this:

On a warrior min: 9050 (or 9000) max: 9950

On anyone else min: 9050 max: 10000

Unfortunately we don't have any mobs that hit this extreme to try it out on.

Of course on a high DI low DB mob the change really shines.

Trying 1000 DB with a 450 DI:

On warrior min: 1450 (or 1000) max: 9550

On anyone else min: 1450 max: 10000

In this case, nearing 5% :)

I haven't seen anyone confirm yet about whether the change effects min hits.

It is not meaningful to discuss how much mitigaiton 5% translates to actual savings because you'd have to know what the mob hits for on average (and possibly a distribution of its other hits as well) and obviously no one's bother to parse that.

If you want a meaningful approximation, take a run of the mill PoP mob that hits for 100-600. Assume it always hits for exactly 10 DI. The mitigation bonus of 5% figures out to be 3.7% more HPs under this situation compare to a Paladin with identical stats. It'd be really strange if you can actually notice the effect of having 3.7% more HPs but it is nevertheless there so just don't worry about it.
