First I want to point out some math probs in this thread and then some thinking.

I am not saying I am for or against the 20% idea, but I didn't take the posters initial idea the way a couple in here are vehemently knocking the poster's idea on.

A 20% improvement is not = 20% less damage from a mob. So lets get that straight.

As a "numbers" professional(CPA) let me run the numbers what 20% improvement in damage mitigation means.

These are numbers chosen for simplicity to illustrate, and does not reflect actual in game mob/damage mitigation tests - but just to illustrate.

Mob does 1000 damage, and warrior mitigates 50 of that damage. This is our example of warriors currently.

If we take 20% of the warriors mitigation, as suggested from poster, this = 10 points of damage(50 X 20% = 10). Added to warriors original mitigation then = 60(50 + 10) total points of mitigation if additional mitigation was awarded.

Our original warrior shrugged off 5% of the damage(50 / 1000 = 5%) OUR IMPROVED 20% MORE MITIGATION WARRIOR shrugged off 6% damage(60 / 1000 =6%).

In terms of actually taking less damage it equates to 1% change.

Some of you are thinking 20% less damage from the mob and that is an entirely different ball game. If we calculated the improvement on the warrior for 20% total damage delivered and not mitigated then that would equate to 200 points of damage added to his original 50 points of mitigation or a 400% increase.
