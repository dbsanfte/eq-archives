Now that the basics of the class skill caps have been shown, lets get down to business.

First lets debunk the Melee DMG myth that wars way out damage knights....

Now it has been parsed time and time again that the 10 skill points diff between 240 and 250 a war has in piercing equates to less than 10% of his overall DPS (close to 5% really from Ranger Glades or the Outhouse, I forget which.. been a while) and assuming average geared level 65 war in POP (Cultuaral + NToV armor, some ssra, between 7-8K buffed) his DPS using a decent 2 hander is maybe 100 DPS (talkin Windblade quality here guys.. not BoW), but.. since he cannot hold aggro with that hes now using something like a BoC (if lucky) or more likely a frosty/new-yak/wavecrasher/snowchipper (insert yer fav aggro weapon that can be bought or camped here) so DPS is more in the 60 range with max haste and decent attack buffs, thus the War will usually do MUCH LESS in the DPS category than a knight in the group... since the average Knight can take advantage of the 2 hander bonus and keep aggro lock while the average Warrior cannot and must dual wield. It has also been shown that the disparity of 10 skill points in double attack is so small that it cannot be accurately determined by parsing (falls well within the STD error). But even giving you a whopping 5% for the minimal riposte and double attack skill differences, knights will still come out considerably ahead.

Thus in XP GROUPS (and raids if war is tanking, esp with def which cuts our dps by 40% or more) Knights will outdamage a war by a considerable amount. (if avg war does 100 DPS with a 2 hander the avg SK would do 12% and the avg pallie 17% less or 88 DPS for the SK and 83 DPS for the pallie MELEE DMG, THIS IN NO WAY ACCOUNTS FOR ANY SPELL DMG) Thus you have the war trying to hold aggro at 60 DPS, the Knight holding aggro at 83 DPS depending. Take another 5% off for riposte and double attack and you get 78 DPS, still considerably higher. Also note this is simplistic view on DPS but still generally accurate way to look at it and holds true for the parses I have done myself and seen from others.

This is also accounting for equivalent weaponry... when it seems Knights have a much much wider array of weaponry that outclasses most war 2handers avail to them, but, lets keep it equal and stick with the windblade which is usable by all tank classes.



Now lets move onto the "Wars have much more AC than knights myth....

I will agree... in equiv gear... A warrior will probably have more AC than a knight while both are UNBUFFED. Is that number "Much More" as most knights would have you believe... I say no. Is it the knights fault.. no, there is ZERO skill cap diff in defense skills so ac for ac we are identical, the problem here stems from poor itemization, most of the gear that Wars can wear and would want to wear and prize for the AC and or HPs are also wearable by knights.. and also have mana on em... thus... the higher up into the game you go... the less and less of an advantage warriors will have. Given identical gear, a knight and a war would have identical AC. This is purely a matter of gear and lack of itemization for individual classes.

One advantage Knights have on top of their War parent class: Knights can also self buff and usually close or even come ahead of the equiv war on AC. Some of these buffs even stack with the buffs both classes would get from clerics/shammies, etc...



Now, onto the HPs diff... knights and warriors all have the exact same AA's available for boosting their overall HPs, so that is not a factor. Wars get a bit more HP per sta but that is minimal after 255 sta is reached, most of a wars HPs will come from raw HP items later in the game once all tank classes are 65.
