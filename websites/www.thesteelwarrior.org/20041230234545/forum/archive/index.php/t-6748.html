Well, each ability has it's place...

For warrior HoTT is the best bar none.

My Shaman main can't live without inspect buffs II (allows you to see how long is left on each buff... invaluable for timing).

Delegate mark npc is nice for chanters to communicate CC better, allowing them to let everyone know the mez breaking order.

Delegate MA also helps CC run smoother, and helps ensure people are on your current target incase they /assisted too soon (Seen a couple of LDoN's go awry when a pet class made this mistake and sent the pet running into a herd just to add a good 3 mobs to the current pull). The ATTK difference would be nice though on a melee heavy group, so I put offense enhancement on my to get list... though not that near the top.

find path to PC has a pretty crappy range, making it not as useful as I imagined it would be... Really not all that much use with this one. A fun toy sometimes, maybe even a little helpful moving a group through an unknown area, but certainly not a prime choice.

Spell awarness is God imho. I'd put this just behind HoTT. When you get use to this (and when your group get's use to it) this can really save you alot of headaches. Getting a big fat red flag pop up as a mob is trying to c-heal or gate is pretty dang helpful.

I haven't gotten target's health, and in all honesty, I can't see much use for it. This may be nice for a shaman or chanter who are slowing/mezzing the adds to still keep an eye on the action, but as a person who's main is a shaman, even then, I still don't think it would be anything more than eye candy

Inspect buffs II (only if you group with shaman often... kinda worthless for most other classes)

See here (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&postid=84474#post84474) for my post on Leadership Abilities cost and effect. If you read the whole post you will see things like . . .

I disagree with the usefulness of the utility skills, most of these are things that good players know/do anyway. I'll break it down . . .

Mark NPC/Delegate Mark NPC - The only real use I see for this is marking the mob to be attacked or mez'd on a multipull so that the chanter can mez quickly. My guildie chanters don't seem to have a problem with simply assiting me then targeting the other quickly, just not an issue.

NPC Health - All the melee should already have this in their target window if they're assisting properly. For casters its very very simple to setup an assist MT button(something they should have anyway) and then use the toggle last two targets key to switch back and forth between mez'd mobs or heal targets and the MT's current mob.

Delegate Main Assist - Dear god, if you don't have a key "/assist (insert MA name)" which you change to the MA everytime you join the group, you don't need to be grouping with me. Again for casters the toggle last two targets makes assisting the MA to check their target without losing their current target very simple.

Inspect Buffs - If you're too lazy to tell me when you need a buff, I'm CERTAINLY not going to inspect your buffs every 10min to determine if I need to rebuff you.

Spell Awareness - This could have some usefulness, though in general if you don't know what castertype you're dealing with by the first spell they cast then you're just not paying attention. If its a cleric and its under 40% health and it makes the healing sound when casting, its probably chealing. If its a chanter and it makes the same sound, its probably dispelling. Just about any other class and you can assume its gonna nuke or debuff you.

Health of Target's Target - WTF is this? If you're healing a group member(stop me if I sound like a broken record) just use the /assist MA + toggle last two targets method. if you've targeted a mob, then most likely this would display a group member, HELLO PARTY WINDOW. The only good I see this doing is quickly ascertaining the first PC on a mez'd mob's agro list, hardly seems worth the points when targeting the mob and using /assist does the same thing.


However I now have HoTT, Inspect Buffs 2, and Spell Awareness, and I can say I love all three.

HoTT just owns, I won't play without it now, I know if I lose agro before the mob even turns around, I know WHO I lose agro too, so I can decide if they can take it and I can just Incite/Taunt till I get it back, or if I need to AET to keep my cleric or chanter alive, this is especially huge in KT/Ikkinz where the mobs can splatter casters pretty quick.

I use IB2 to check my buffs before named, see if anything is close to fading. I also use it when I form a group for group rebuffs, to tell if I need to click a BSS with only 10min on it in favor of a SoT . . . things like that.

Spell Awareness is actually just as good for the ability to see what your fellow players are casting as it is for mobs. For mobs, I've watched mobs get hasted and DS'd in Ikkinz and it helps to know those kinda things, as well as the obvious cheals, charms, slows . . . ect. For players, I LOVE seeing when slows are inc, whether my slowers are debuffing before slowing, what heals are inc and when without the need to watch for /gsay hotkeys.


Overall I'd say get HoTT before anything else, other than that its kinda up to you. I actually maxed HE and OE before I touched the utility AA's and I regret not getting HoTT sooner. I'm getting ready to run outta decent stuff to buy though . . . I have 76 LeadAA atm giving 100hp, 50atk, 30mana, HoTT, IB2, SA, I'm going to finish out Mana Enhancement and then get Delegate MA then I'm kinda lackluster about the rest.
