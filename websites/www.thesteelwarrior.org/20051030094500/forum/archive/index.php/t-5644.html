Using the new information about proc rates, here is the new and improved all inclusive warrior usable weapon listing. This is for all known weapons through the end of LDoN, this DOES NOT include any GoD weapons as of yet. We can update that list when we get that information.

ALL warrior useable weapons NOT on the list:
If you do not find the weapon you are inquiring about on the list below, then you can expect a proc rate similar to this:

For mainhand weapons:
305 DEX - 2.0 ppm
205 DEX - 1.5 ppm
105 DEX - 1.0 ppm

For offhand weapons:
105 DEX = .5 proc/min
205 DEX = .75 proc/min
305 DEX = 1 proc/min


As far as we can tell, all proc augments and spell procs follow the above rule for proc rates. Augment proc and spell proc rates DO STACK with a weapons innate proc rate. If you have a standard weapon, augment it and cast a spell proc on it, you will get approximately 6.0 ppm. When adding procs to weapons with innate procs, each successive proc will have an insignificantly smaller proc rate due to the way weapon procs work. Each time a weapon swing, it starts by checking for an innate proc. If the innate proc does not go off, then it checks for an augment proc. If the augment does not go off, then it checks for a spell proc. Since a weapon can only proc once per swing, as soon as 1 proc does go off, the remaining procs will not even be checked.

Non-standard proc rate weapons - warrior usable:

Ishinaear Xiall 2.06 ppm
Acrylia Handled Broadsword 2.06 ppm

Fiercewind 2.1 ppm
Ivory Hilted Cleaver 2.1 ppm
Obsidian Scimitar of War 2.5 ppm
Darkblade of the Warlord 2.6 ppm
Greatstaff of the Four Winds 2.6 ppm
Bloodfrenzy 3.1 ppm
Blasphemous Blade of the Exiled 3.3 ppm
Barbed Fishing Net 4.0 ppm

It should be noted that all weapons with an increased proc rate of less than 10% will have such a small increase in procs that it is virtually impossible to tell a difference without parsing many hours of combat. The difference is also small enough that it could mistakenly be attributed to standard deviation or a 'streaky RNG'. For all intents and purposes, the exception list for warriors is basically Obsidian Scimitar of War, Darkblade of the Warlord, Greatstaff of the Four Winds, Bloodfrenzy, and Blasphemous Blade of the Exiled. The Barbed Fishing Net is a throwing weapon, it cannot really be included. The remaining weapons do not have an increase large enough to be noticed.

Lets look at some key items from the shadowknight table and compare them to Wandor's numbers:

| 3820 | Barbed Fishing Net | 100 | 0 |

| 22907 | Furious Hammer of Zek | 530 | 0 |
The zek hammer would be 2ppm * 6.30 = 12.6ppm

Seems to make sense. I assume Wandor calculated his numbers in the fashion? or were they from parsing?

NOTE: Keep in mind that percentages equate to % / 100. So 100% = 100/100 = 1. 90% = 90/100 = 0.90, etc.
