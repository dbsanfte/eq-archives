Are you running around to zones or porting between ldon camps and ooc'ing or just sitting in one spot for most of the time with LFG on? I am not trying to suggest you aren't looking hard for groups, but I see a lot of peeps with LFG on that are afk for 15 minutes or so. Ultimately forming your own groups is by far the easiest way to ensure a group in my opinion. Start with a healer and a slower. There are almost always others looking for group that you can pick up.

One other suggestion, many people turn down exp groups right and left "because that zone isn't good exp or that camp doesnt have any drops I need, etc." so take what you can get. The group you turn down today may be the one that doesn't invite you to where you WANT to go tomorrow. Also, be flexible as to your role in a group. I do a lot of pickup groups in LDON and many times a plate tank is stuck on being the main tank for the group. If I get in a group with 2 tanks I always give the other tank the option to be main tank or dps/offtank/puller/whatever else is needed. Who really cares who is main tank, you already are in the group :) Buy/camp a ToD and offer to slow for a group until they can find a shaman/chanter/bst/bard, etc. Little things like being flexible help a group and can go a long ways on getting repeated invites from the same people.

stratys
A few other suggestions:

1. Explore other avenues of finding a group. On my server, use of the LFG tool is basically nonexistant. We have the "plane" channel for those LFG in PoP, the "LDoN" channel for those LFG in dungeons, etc. There are also zones like Plane of Tranquility where it's not uncommon to find people /OOCing or /AUCtioning for groups.

2. Take the initiative. Instead of sitting there with LFG waiting for a /tell, start looking around at who else is LFG. I can't tell you how often I see tanks, healers, CCers, pullers, all of the same level range, all LFG, but no one stepping up and taking charge. Even if someone DOES step forward, you are then left hoping that they will choose you as part of their team -- if you are the one starting the group, you have the luxury of already being part of the group. Even if there's not a full well-rounded group available, see if you can snag a healer and one or two others and start killing something while you keep looking for more. This can be an important way to break out of a rut, because there aren't that many individuals who tend to be the ones who start up groups; it may just happen that most of them currently have tanks they tend to group with, and that may be why you aren't getting asked to groups.

3. Network. You remember that last time you had a pickup group where things clicked pretty well -- the healer was on the ball, the puller did a good job of maintaining a steady flow of mobs, etc? Do you remember their names? Do you think they remember yours? Maybe so, maybe not. You can save yourself a lot of time and frustration later if you make a point of maintaining a friends list of people you enjoyed grouping with. At the end of an enjoyable pickup group, if you say "hey, this was fun -- do you mind if I put you on my friends list? I wouldn't mind grouping again sometime" you will accomplish a few things: first, it's a nice compliment to the people in the group, second, the responses you get may give you some idea how responsive they are to grouping with you again; but most importantly, it may also inspire a few of them to remember to put YOU on THEIR friends list... and to keep an eye out for you in the future. (even if you have a bad pickup group, usually there are one or two good players, and you can do this in tells just as easily) If you do this for awhile, you may find that you hardly have to go the "LFG" route at all, as you will develop a wide circle of friends that enjoy grouping with you.
