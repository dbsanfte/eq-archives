The Provoke line will bring Warrior aggro control past the 'good enough' line for experience groups. It won't be identical to Knight aggro control, and there will still be some things that Knights can do that Warriors cannot. However, a Warrior will be able to keep one mob glued to him, and have a credible chance to truly tank two mobs at the same time. This is a huge increase for Warriors.

In the LDoN (normal) missions I play in, if 4-5 mobs are pulled, I cannot gain aggro on all of them quickly (if at all). A couple usually get mezzed or rooted off to the side and I grab 2 or maybe 3 within the first ten seconds. A Warrior will be able to grab 2 fairly reliably in these situations without using AE Taunt.

With the increase in Warrior aggro control, some of the advantages that a Paladin tank had are minimized. Warriors will get quick, reliable aggro on single pulls. Mobs will be able to be slowed on incoming. Other classes will be able to open up early in the fight. Warriors will be able to use more damaging weapons combinations without drastically damaging their ability to tank.

Look at the healing situation for instance. With the ability to glue single pulled mobs to the tank, there is no loss in healing efficiency due to ping-pong aggro. The mob can be slowed on incoming, reducing the amount of damage taken from an unslowed mob. The tank is the only character that gets healed. In most camps, a single Cleric or Druid will be able to keep up easily. Since other classes can open up earlier and Warriors themselves will do more DPS because they are using more damaging weapons. Fights will be shorter, further reducing the amount of healing needed. A Cleric could feasibly stun the mob early in the fight to reduce unslowed damage even further.

As for decreasing the re-use timer on Provoke, I think it can wait for a while. Let's see how the changes in total affect balance, and let the initial euphoria/disgust/whatever wear off so that we can really analyze what is happening here. I understand wanting to use the initial opportunity to get everything you think you might ever need. But I don't think it is always the wise way to go.

No, the changes do not address every aspect of every situations that could be viewed as an advantage to Paladins. Yes, Paladins will be able to do everything they could do previously. However, there will be less reason for a group to wait until they find one instead of grabbing LFG_Warrior_01 instead.

In closing, I think the changes are more than most Warriors expected. I think they do a good job of addressing the most glaring Warrior weaknesses without being overkill. I think that Warriors will be much more group-friendly in most situations, including LDoN normal. I think that Paladins will eventually lose their disproportionate share of experience group tanking roles. I would expect, for example, the average BoT experience group to not consider passing over a LFG Warrior in hopes for a Paladin after these changes have had a while to get settled in.
