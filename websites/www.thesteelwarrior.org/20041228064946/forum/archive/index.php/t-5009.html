This is basically what the stats do for a warrior :

STR is nice to carry items and do a teeeeeeeny tiny little bit better DPS. Gives small bonus to Blacksmithing. Partly governs Endurance pool.

DEX gives a small bonus to proc rates and helps how fast you get skill ups with offensive weapon skills. Small bonus to Fletching I think ? Partly governs Endurance pool.

AGI gives a bit on the AC side, but the effect is small indeed. It also governs how fast you get skill ups with defensive skills. Partly governs Endurance pool.

STA is good, you should know why already. Partly governs Endurance pool.

INT / WIS is good for tradeskills in general, whichever one is the highest. Fletching and Smithing excepted afaik.

CHA is good for Divine Intervention and shopping prices.

Any stat under 75 (human baseline) gets a severe penalty.
Any stat at 100 or higher gets a REALLY small bonus to aforementioned skills.
Same bonus seems to apply at 125, 150, 175 ... etc .. but the increments are so small it have been hard for anyone to document them.

Shamans have done a LOT of parsings on stat buff effects and generally found them ... useless.



Summa Summarum :
-HP & AC is still your biggest worry.
-STA gives HP, so it's nice.
-Resist are important, especially if you raid.
-All other stats are pretty much insignificant.

There's one other thing that AGI does.

You take a big hit in AC when AGI drops under 80.

Also, when your HPs drop to a certain percentage of total, I don't recall exactly what the percentage is but it might be where you go berserk, your AGI goes down by a third.

So, when fighting, it's helpful to have your AGI at 120 or more. That way, if it gets cut by a third, you're still at 80 or more, and you don't take the AC hit.

In practical terms, that's meaningless to your average overlord, but for lower level warriors it's important, because you want to make sure you keep your AGI above that magic number (buffed).
