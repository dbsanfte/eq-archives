So about halfway into level 57 I said "you know... screw this, let me try to change up my talents slightly."

What I did was juggled my 31 arms points a bit and configured it to have 5% 2h damage and 5% polearm crit at the sacrifice of Anger Management and 2 points of Tactical Mastery and 1 point in charge. These points moved to polearm crit.

Wow...

+5% crit has totally changed my gameplay for some reason. I was doing approx 110ish dps with my previous polearm on level 51 ogres and had a hard time effeciently soloing evens (would have to bandage/eat far too often). With crit talents and switching my golem (high ac +defense) helm to my old ornate mithril helm when soloing for a total net change of +6 crit %, I brought my avg dps up to 180ish (not incredible I know but...) on level 51s and more amazingly: 110ish dps on +0 mobs!

I have no clue why this should happen with such a slight tweak but it literally changed my solo play. I immediately finished my felwood quests, finished my ungoro quests in record time (thanks to my much higher dps on low cons) and then moved on to Winterspring and really plowed through most of those quests in one night solo.

Anyway, this was two nights ago and now I'm level 58 and I just hit a HUGE HUGE power uptick with the ability to finally equip my BRS gear. Going from some lame polearm (Smoldering Claw) to Blackhand Doomsaw alone was like a gift from god (and the new HP/ac doesn't hurt either).

Basically, this reinforces gear makes and breaks warriors solo. However, I learned a dramatic NEW lesson that is more valuable I believe for those unable to plow through instances and raids frequently:

Spend the gold and respec to get a higher crit rate if you can. Try swapping out some +STR for +AGI if you got some items available. Experiment with dropping *some* HP/AC for +crit% gear.

Give it a shot and see how you do, I know it made a huge diff in my overall DPS (much much more than I would expect). I went from a little over 10% crit (base crit + slight agility boost) to close to 17% crit when I noticed the huge difference.

Btw, if you have a good solid two hander it takes a *really* good set of dual wield weapons to give you a net dps increase by "mortal strike swapping" just because of the innate penalties of dual wield mechanics.

On low con mobs, your hit/crit rate is significantly higher so it mitigates much of the DW penalty and you really start closing in on a much higher dps from dual wielding in addition to a higher rage gen.

So my suspicion is unless you have a *sick* dual wield setup:

- just use your twohander even when not mortal striking

On low cons:

- parse it out, sometimes dual wielding might be higher raw "auto attack" non-MS/overpower dps than your raw two handed dps. Typically your dual wielded rage gen is going to be higher so there is a point of overlap where your rage gen from dual wielding allows you to fuel your mortal strike rage sink faster than simply swinging your two hander.
