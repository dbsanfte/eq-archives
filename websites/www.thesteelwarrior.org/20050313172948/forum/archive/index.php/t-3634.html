Proc rate is independant.

Here's how I understand it. Example using hypothetical numbers:

Your weapon has effect: ProcA (combat)
ProcA = 2 procs/minute

Your weapon has effect: ProcA (combat) plus Aug:Add ProcB
ProcA = 2 procs/minute
ProcB = 1.99procs/minute
Total: 3.99 procs per minute of two types

Your weapon has effect: ProcA (combat) + Spell: Add proc: Call of ProcC
ProcA = 2 procs/minute
ProcC = 1.99procs/minute
Total: 3.99 procs per minute of two types

Your weapon has effect: ProcA (combat) plus Aug:ProcB plus Spell proc: Call of ProcC
ProcA = 2 procs/minute
ProcB = 1.99 procs.minute
ProcC = 1.98 procs/minute
Total: 5.97 procs per minute of three types

ProcA rate is determined by the hardcoded value attatched to the weapon (assumed here as 2/minute).
ProcB rate is determined by the hardcoded value attatched to the spell (assumed here as 2/minute).
ProcC rate is determined by the hardcoded value attatched to the augment (assumed here as 2/minute).

The values slightly under 2 are meant to represent the fact you can't proc twice on the same melee hit, and the supposed priority order is latent then spell then aug.

Proc rate/minute has been parsed as independant of weapon delay or haste. Increasing number of swings effectively decreases the proc-per-swing rate, to keep proc-per-minute rate the same.
