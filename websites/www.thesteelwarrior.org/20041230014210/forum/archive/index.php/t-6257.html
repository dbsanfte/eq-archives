So, I did something stupid last night. Two-boxing my warrior and my beastlord on a couple of LDoN missions, I accidentally flipped to the wrong hotkey page on my warrior. And spent one and a half of those two missions without a taunt key. When I finally discovered my error, I had already spent all of my endurance trying to compensate for my suddenly bizarrely bad aggro--and thus effectively did the last half of the LDoN mission without Incite.

Which gives me a big red face, and some first-hand experience with Taunt vs. Incite.

Bottom line? Taunt is essential. And Incite does virtually nothing for my aggro in practice.

Now, I've got some odd and probably atypical advantages. I use my beastlord's slow to pump aggro for my warrior, making Taunt effectively a plus-600-or-so-hate command if it works the first time. And we're only doing LDoN normal, so it's acceptable for the Beastlord to be tanking for a few rounds, even without defensive AAs.

But working without taunt, it was literally impossible to hold aggro. Even using Incite twice per mob. Even when my weapons (CHoS/SotB, un-aug'ed) proc'd twice near the beginning of the fight. The mob would still ping-pong to virtually anyone else in the party rather than stay on me.

With Taunt, but with no Incite? I don't think I lost aggro more than twice in the remaining 25 minutes of that LDoN mission, and that only for brief moments and only to an early snare by the ranger, or a resisted slow by my beastlord.

Incite is nice, if you're pulling; by the time the mob gets back to camp, you've got a free 500 hate on it. But honestly, that's not enough (even with the first-melee bonus) to make a slow-on-incoming not pull aggro. It's not enough to let the ranger snare whenever he wants to. In short, it doesn't actually change the pattern of the fight in any significant way.

If I'm waking a mezzed mob, and I miss my taunt, I have a choice. I can wait around until one of my taunts is successful, at which point I don't actually need Incite to hold aggro. Or I can Incite and then break mez, at which point it promptly heads over and beats on the Enchanter.

When I was playing without Taunt, it got to the point where I'd triple-slow a mezzed mob with my beastlord, so that at least it would pull onto the beastlord when mez broke, rather than onto the enchanter.

Playing without Incite? If I taunt it successfully, after the chanter has tashed and mezzed it and the beastlord has slowed it, *nobody* is going to pull aggro from me, Incite or not.

*sigh* I really wish that they had given us a version of Incite that cost 3x the endurance, or had a 1-minute reuse timer, but that did more hate than a tash or a slow or a mez. Something that could actually be used, even if only rarely or at high cost, to ensure aggro for the warrior.

Because as Incite stands, the most it ever does for me is to make it possible for me to be a little more sloppy, a little less careful. It doesn't actually change anything about a typical LDoN normal fight. And I don't think, from my experience, that it would change that many things about other XP-grind fights.

Hear that, SOE? Give us an Incite that costs four times as much END, that has a two-minute cycle time--and that gives us 800 hate instead of 400. That's what I call a useful ability.

Experimentally,

My advice is to stop trying to make incite into something more than it is.

Incite isn't meant to place you at the top of the hate list. Incite is meant to help you stay there AFTER you reach the top of the hate list. And it does a good job of that.

In general, there is no point in inciting a mob that you are already meleeing, unless you are at the top of the hate list. Your melee and procs are much better aggro-over-time than incite.

There are a few times you might want to use incite when you're not at the top of the hate list:

- When an add is rooted/mezzed, incite it early on. That way your hate is ticking up on the mob while it sits there untouched. Re-incite each time it is re-mezzed/re-rooted. Once you get around to engaging that mob, or if the root/mez breaks, chances are good that you'll be at the top of the hate list already.

- When a mob is being pulled, obviously, incite is your ranged aggro skill, and it works fairly well for this.
