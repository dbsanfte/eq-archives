Battleblade
A dozen times I have read posts from Plaladins and Shadow Knights that Warriors have to be limited to disfunctional aggro control because Survivability + Snap Aggro on Demand + Ranged Aggro "Breaks the Game".

For purposes of this discusion I'm defining Survivability as sufficient AC, HPs, and mitigation to tank effectively.

It's pretty clear that Knights have the Survivability to tank in experiance groups without causing the Main Healer to request frequent med breaks. Their Survivability + Snap Aggro on Demand + Ranged Aggro allow the experiance group to kill faster and with less risk.

This would seem to bolster the claim that Survivability + Snap Aggro on Demand + Ranged Aggro "Breaks the Game". I suppose Knights believe breaking only the non-raid portion of the game doesn't count.

Let's concider Griegg. A Paladin MT's. Is he Breaking the Game? No? Why?

How about AoW, Seru, Rallos Zek, Finnien Ro, or Quarm?

What we have now is obvious. When a Paladin uses Survivability + Snap Aggro on Demand + Ranged Aggro to "Break the Game" it's OK because it's either the non-raid portion of the game (doesn't count) or it's the raid potion of the game and it doesn't count because..... because...???. Gosh, I don't know why!

Surely it's not because they aren't really MT's. We've already agreed all three plate wearing tanks are and should be MT's.

The only possible explanation is Defensive. Yep that 3 minute discipline is the only rational reason.

So I have a proposal. Give Warriors a unique skill that is scaleable and provides Snap Aggro on Demand and Ranged Aggro (0 sec Stun? Call it Deadly Insult or something?) that is available only when Defensive is not in force.

The "Breaks the Game" objection is easily overcome. Without this ability, Warriors are beefy Knights with disfunctional aggro control and no spells in an experiance group environment where their extra beef is not required.

While some knights do come here posting wildly defensive things, others really are trying to forward the discussion of what might provide Warriors some balance. For example, below is a quote from Knight/Warrior Class Balance thread (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=3093) that I made on September 18th. There are other examples of Paladins making valid contributions to discussions on this board as there are Warriors who make valid contributions to discussions at Paladins of Norrath.

"Graderr mentions the idea of 2 aggro abilities on a 30 second timer. I think the amount of aggro he uses is a bit high (600 – 650 hate), especially on the ranged version. But I like the idea in general. I would like to see the melee-range ability have higher aggro, enough to counter one cast of slow. The ranged version should have enough to get aggro from the puller, but not enough to counter slow. Maybe half the aggro of the first ability. These abilities should be WAR only.

This would give the Warrior a way to get aggro focused on him/her on incoming, and then immediately cement it once the mob gets to camp. It would give Warriors the initial aggro of a Paladin casting Cease on incoming, then Force of Akilae once the mob gets to camp. Knights would still retain an aggro advantage because our stuns recycle faster, but the advantage would be much closer to the Warriors advantage in HP/AC/Mitigation than is the case now."

The ideas discussed in that quote might not meet anyone's wishlist of what should happen, but they certainly not made by someone who is not interested in discussing balance. Notice my post mentions both the things that Battlebalde discusses in his post.
