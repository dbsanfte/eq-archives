If you made it so Knights cannot cast indefinitely you would probably have to do it to other classes or limit mana regen massively which would kill enchanters group desireability some.

To me the answer lies in making the damage taken to a warrior SIGNIFICANTLY less than a knight on a given mob OR dramatically reducing the casting side of a Knight. If a knight is 75% warrior and 25% cleric, make the knight take 133% (4/3) of the damage that a warrior would given the same AC and skill progression (ie, both capped defensive skills). Although it would be nice to up the warrior damage mitigation, it would upset raid encounters too much I think. The more feasible solution is to retune Knight's mitigation like monks were. More than likely this would kill Knights for exp groups and therefor is not a viable solution.

A more realistic possibility IMHO, would be to retune spells and enforce level limits (ie no lvl 9 spells being able to be cast on lvl 65 mobs) and give Knights 25% of the mana pool of the parent class of a given level and gear. To go along with this mana regen should reflect the 25% caster as well, ie KEI granting 14/4 mana per tick as it should affect only the caster portion of the Knight.

When I started EQ it was true that Knights could out aggro warriors but they had to med sometimes when tanking AND required more healing than warriors. Today I rarely see one med and CHeal is a CHeal, doesn't matter if it lands for 5k on me and 5.5k on a Knight, the mob is dead and we are both starting full health again for the same cleric mana.

The above ideas are too game changing to ever be implemented but I think that the caster side of Knights has gotten way too far out of hand. When you see magelo's of warriors with 8k hps and knights with 7.5k hps AND 5k mana you realize that somehow Knights became 95% warrior AND 75% parent class. Don't complain about the %'s either, I made up the parent class one but the above would result in a 9.9k Knight vs 10.4K Warrior or 95%. The point is that % Warrior + % Parent class >>>> 100%

To balance the classes you need to bring the Knight (although hybrid really) package down closer to 100% in my opinion. Changes to the warrior class would be very difficult to make after the defensive discipline was added to the game as it seems most raid encounters for appropriately leveled and gear players are centered around it.

I will also say that when I started EQ, ROK had just come out. I was dumbfounded that anyone would play a hybrid class as knights and rangers in particular seemed to be laughed at. I have no desire to see them return to that state, but I think the original caster penalties for hybrids were on the right track as far as balancing. They may have been too severe but IMO some are needed with the prevailance of all/all gear with massive hps and mana and wis/int on them.

I'm of the belief that it is entirely possible to prevent spells which have no possibility of landing affecting aggro while simultaneously NOT nerfing procs.

It's really rather simple actually.

I have a 50's necro, and have an "undead lull" type of spell. Some undead mobs are too high level to be lulled. When I attempt to cast on them, I get a message "Your target would have been unaffected". It simply put prevents me from wasting the mana by stopping the spell from being cast.

On the Sullon Zek server, it is impossible to cast beneficial spells on members of an "opposing alignment team", you are prevented from doing so and get a message.

What I'm getting at is that it is indeed possible from preventing spells to be cast in the first place. Spells which would not affect a target can be prevented from being cast, while procs of the same spells are easily unaffected, because they aren't "cast". You would have essentially eliminated stupidity like stuns, and fears which do not affect mobs from being chain cast to build up insane amounts of aggro, which is NOT their function, while retaining the aggro generation of weapons which proc similar effects by design.
