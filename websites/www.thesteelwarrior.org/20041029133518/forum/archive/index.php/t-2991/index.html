Sounds to me like you've got 2 different 'instances' on your raid force. That's rather strange considering there was a PoTime reboot last that came with the LDoN patch.

Best hypothesis I can make is that the instancing information wasn't wiped it the patch respawn of PoTime, just as PoTime instances suually persist through regular patches. If that was the case and the first person to zone up from your raid, didn't complete every stage the night before, your raid would be registered to the new instance of PoTime, yet all the raiders who got to P4 the night before would still be registered for the old instance. If one of them had clicked up to PoTb first, all would've been fine as the new raiders would've joined the existing instance rather than creating a new one of their own.

As it stands now, some members are flagged to P4 with the old raid, and some are flagged to only P1 with the new instance. You're fucked for a week, basically. Since a respawn should intuitively also clear instancing flag, you might get your server GM on the case, and they might be able to reset the instance flags. The other option is to wait until the new instance despawns, then your old raid can click up, along with everyone else who didn't click up, but everyone who did enter the new instance is screwed for 5 days or so.

All this is hypothetical, but it fits with the evidence. It may be the same on other servers but they didn't notice it if the first person to zone up was fully flagged from the pre-patch raid. Best practice is always to send your most consistant raider up first, just in case.
