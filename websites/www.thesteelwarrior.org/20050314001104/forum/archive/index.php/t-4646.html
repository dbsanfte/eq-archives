As I understand it:
1: Discs are unlinked and tied to endurance
2: Using a new disc cancels any currently active disc/opening effect
3: Using an new opening cancels any currently active disc/opening effect.

So from first impressions, a tanking warrior will follow this timeline:

-00:02 Provoke on inc for 100 hate
00:00 Engage mob: Disc furi for immunity to frontal attack and extra swings for agro while you back into a corner
00:07 Disc stonewall for 3 mins of 45% mitigation
03:08 Disc defensive for 3 mins of 55% mitigation
06:09 Disc evasive for 3 mins of 50% avoidance
09:10 Oh noes! No duration disc! Finger over fortitude button if you think heals won't land!
10:37 If you survived those 87 seconds without a disc, stonewall will be up again. Rinse Lather Repeat for as long as endurance holds out
On top of all this, there's latent 5% mitigation

Oh, and you won't even bother with openings because adding a 35ds for one tick will cancel your 3 minute disc. HPs, AC, veng, shielding, avoidance, etc. are ALL now secondary to endurance pool and/or endurance regen. Get ready for wholesale re-equiping, because unless fights last so long that endurance runs out (ie: VT) maintaining a disc that reduces damage by 50% is gonna be worth so much more than a couple of thousand hp.

If discs drain endurance too fast to chain them like this, then opening based endurance gains won't be used in case you need to chain some discs later in the fight. It's the same vicious circle that made 90% of Kunark discs redundant for the past years

It seems I'm missing something major here that this hasn't been mentioned before - am I just stupid?
