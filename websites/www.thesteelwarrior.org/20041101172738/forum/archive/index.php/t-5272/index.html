Ahz
First I'd like to say that both E and Y are fine programs, and I in no way wish to slam their authors.

I did some investigation, and I believe the differences in what I expected versus what was produced is due to damage shields, mostly. Both Y and E seem to include damage shield damage in the DPS. This isn't wrong if you are interested in your total effectiveness. If you want to know how your weapons performed, it is way wrong.

Here are some observations:

The more I compare Y and E, the more similar their numbers. There is some fuzziness here and there, but this is to be expected.

For Y, the DPS on the summary screen doesn't match that on the Fights tab. The Ofensive [sic] DPS on the fights tab pretty much agrees with my parser (82.43 vs 85.2 respectively) if you include all damage shield and weapon proc damage.

E gave a DPS of 82.9, nearly agreeing with Y and M also, again if I include all weapon procs and damage shields.

Y didn't include my Epic's 100pt DD, E did. Otherwise they seem tp produce pretty similar numbers.

For E, the total fight time on the stats tab doesn't agree with the total fight time if I group and combine all the fights, which are 35:05 vs 36:03, respectively.

I'd venture a guess there is a way to remove DS damage from DPS calculation for both these parsers. Then I'll be good to go. If there is no way, then neither of these tools is appropriate for rating weapons unless the situation is very carefully controlled. It's next to impossible to test weapons perfectly against live-fire mobs, due to group and buff differences, etc. But we can do adequate testing. But the DS has to go. Having an extra 50 ATK is one thing, being credited for a 90pt DS is quite another!
