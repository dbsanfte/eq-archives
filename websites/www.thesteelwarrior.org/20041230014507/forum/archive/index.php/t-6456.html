Im confused by this statement. If its a successful taunt then you are now on top of agro list, why should it matter what the enchanter had cast or is sitting. It "was" a successful taunt.

Aggro from sitting appears to be a constant value that is added to the hate list last. A hate modifier. The tuant function doesn't know of this value, and this value is not added to your hate when you successfully tuant.

If the chanter had 1000 hate, and he sits, his hate has this modifier added to it. I don't know what the modifier's value is for sure, but let's say it added 500 hate for example. So standing, the chanter is 1000 hate, sitting he's 1500 hate. When you tuant, it only gives you 1001 hate. If the chanter is standing all is well. If he sits, the mob bee-lines for him. You get the enchanters raw hate plus one, and the "is he sitting or not" modifier is added after everything else is said and done to the hate list.

After a short period of cleaving time, you've built up enough aggro to overcome the chanters hate even while he's sitting.

You see it all the time I'm sure. A wizard nukes, but doesn't draw aggro. Then he sits and the mob beelines for him. The wizard quickly stands and the mob runs back to you. The wizard might test his sit aggro then at that point, and do a quick sit-down stand-up to see if the mob turns in his direction for that split second, before deciding it's completely safe to plop back down. You've got more hate than the wizard does standing, but not when the sit modifier is added into the total. After a few seconds of cleaving you'll overcome the two added values, and the wizard can sit (or the smarter option would have been for the wizard to have not nuked so early heh).
