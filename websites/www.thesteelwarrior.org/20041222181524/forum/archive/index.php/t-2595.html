One, CS in this game has been trending downward at least since I started playing three years ago.

Two, anyone who has been around long enough to remember the old large scale GM events remembers that the basic plot was this:
Low level zone gets invaded either by one tremendously nasty creature that kills everyone (remember Faydedar in LOIO?) or by a horde of less nasty creatures that accomplish the same goal (the classic overrunning of WC by werewolves, which I think they used to do every couple of months just for giggles). After the resident PCs are all slaughtered a few times, high level players show up in force and kill the beasties, and get the loot.

They trained us to act like this. They also instilled even more distrust of their CS reps by doing this shit.

Nowadays, someone seems to have finally gotten the idea that slaughtering the players indiscriminately does not make them grateful. So they try "roleplaying" events. And run smack into a basic problem of GMing a live event - if you have more than a handful of players involved, you lose any ability to direct events coherently.

What I think would be really, really cool, given that LDoN is bringing us instanced Dungeons, is to have them stage one group events. You zone into your instanced dungeon and find that one of the npcs is being run by a GM. Maybe he's there to help you, or to ask your help. Maybe hes playing the boss mob, and presenting you with an intelligent challenge rather than the lame EQ AI. I think this would have a lot of potential, and would be much less prone to being fubared by the usual horde of idiots.
