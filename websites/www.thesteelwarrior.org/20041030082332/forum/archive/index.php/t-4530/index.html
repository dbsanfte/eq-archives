Anker steadfast
Level 30 = 50% max haste
Level 50 = 74% max haste
Level 54 = 84% max haste
Level 59 = 94% max haste
Level 60 = 100% max haste

If you have two Haste buffs on, the one with the biggest Haste component will overrule the other buffs haste component, but both buffs will add other stats if they have any .. like +ATK, AC, etc. etc. Same goes for Item Haste, biggest one prevails.

Also, Haste increases your attack rate, not directly lower the delay but a good approximation on delay CAN be calculated.

Delay / (1 + (Haste% / 100) ) = hasted delay

So let's say you have a Centi Longsword (27 Delay), wear the SCHW (22% haste) and have the Aanya's Quickening (64% haste) buff on you. Let's say 86% haste for simplicity. The full calculation, step-by-step, would look something like this :

27 Delay / (1 + (86%/100) ) = hasted delay
27 Delay / (1 + 0.86) = hasted delay
27 Delay / 1.86 = hasted delay
14,51~ delay = hasted delay

To the best of my knowledge, EverQuest actually use floating numbers niternally on delays, which is why I haven't rounded it up or down.

Formula taken from the Compendium of Haste Data (http://cloud.prohosting.com/~talone/HasteData.htm).

Anker steadfast
So then for a 65 warrior with a 41% haste item, and from a pure haste perspective (i.e. not including other stats), there is no advantage to a 68% spell haste over a 60% spell haste, is this correct?

Correct, unless the 68% one also have other stats such as AC / AGI / ATK and such.

And are these additive? e.g. [41% item + 68% spell] + 35% song = [~100% (item/spell cap)] + 35% song = ~135% total?

No, strongest one overules the weaker.

So in short :
(Strongest item haste + strongest buff haste) capped by (haste cap + strongest overhaste) = your haste

So lets say 41% item haste, 70% buff haste, 10% overhaste on a level 65 character.
(41+70 = 111%) capped by (100+10 = 110%) = 110% haste

I hope it was clear enough. :)
