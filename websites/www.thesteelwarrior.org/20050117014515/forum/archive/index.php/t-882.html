What you really need to look at the the stats of the items, forget about the haste, they cancel out.

For comparison's sake, I use a Cloak of Greater Pernicity and a Spiked Seahorse Hide Belt
-----------------------------------------------------

CoF
10ac 50hp 9dex 9agi 15svF

Putrid Belt
13ac 65hp 10dex 10agi 10svD
23ac 115hp 19dex 19agi 15svF 10svD

---------------

CoGP
12ac 50hp 10sta 25str

SSHB
10ac 25hp 4sta 4str 4dex 4agi 1svALL
22ac 75hp 14sta 29str 4dex 4agi 1svALL

--------------------------------

23ac 115hp 19dex 19agi 15svF 10svD
vs
22ac 75hp 14sta 29str 4dex 4agi 1svALL

CoF/PBoRH: 1ac 40hp 15dex 15agi 14svF 9svD
CoGP/SSHB: 14sta 29str 1svM, 1svC, 1svP
(the stats above is what each combo has over the other combo. IE, cof/pborh has 23ac and the cogp/sshb has 22, so 1ac is listed for the cof/pborh)

We'll say you're 60, and get 6hp/sta. (giving 84 hp from the belt)

CoF/PBoRH: 1ac 15dex 15agi 14svF 9svD
CoGP/SSHB: 44hp 29str 1svM, 1svC, 1svP

The 15agi is about 3ac. Giving the top combo ~5 more AC than the bottom. (~5 because sometimes items say 10ac yet you gain 14 or something like that). We'll say that 5ac = 14hp.

So, here you have it, with the stats fully weighed out:

CoF/PBoRH: 15dex 14svF 9svD
CoGP/SSHB: 30hp 29str 1svM, 1svC, 1svP

Note: the 29str bonus could quite possibly make or overtake the dps difference of the 2% haste you wouldn't have using the cogp/sshb combo.

The spell Haste, which is on almost all haste items, does indeed go up in level, and that formula given for Haste is used to calculate the haste given from an item. The thing is, it's not looking at YOUR level, but rather a level assigned to the item.
Each haste item is assigned a "level", which is used to calculate the haste. A 21% haste item is given an effective level of 20 (since the formula goes level +1 for percentages), whereas a 41% haste item is given a level of 40. I don't know how you'd actually gather the item's assigned level tho. The highest assigned level that's known is 40.
I believe this works for all worn items (a level is assigned to the item), but I don't know of any worn effect items who's effects scale up like Haste does.
