More attacks does not increase your proc rate.Actually this is not exactly accurate. More attacks due to a change in relative weapon delay does not increase your proc rate. More attacks with no change in relative weapon delay does increase your proc rate.

Proc rate is based on the delay of a weapon. If a weapon has 20 delay, the combat engine expects that weapon to swing 30 times in 1 minute. Of those 30 swings, there will be 2 procs or a 6.6% chance to proc on any given swing.

Now if we add more attacks without changing the delay of the weapon - ambidexterity, PB, flurry, rampage, ferocity - you still have the same weapon delay so you still have a 6.6% chance to proc on any given swing. The difference is now instead of 30 swings in a minute, you have 40 swings per minute. This could change your proc rate to 2.6 PPM. This phenomenon can be seen by using an Earthshaker and rampage, procs out the wazoo. Of course the numbers are purely made up, but the theory is accurate.

Unless I misunderstood him? I think you misunderstand. Lower delay <> more procs. When relating weapon delay to procs, all that a change in delay does is change the % chance that a particular weapon will proc on a single swing. This is because most weapons proc 2 times per minute. In order to calculate a weapon's chance to proc on a single swing you would divide the number of procs per minute for that weapon (2 PPM for almost everything a warrior swings) by the number of expected swings in 1 minute.

SoD = 2 PPM, unhasted 18 delay = 33.3 swings per minute (60/1.8)
Chance to proc = 2/33.3 = 6% chance per swing
41% Hasted SoD = 2 PPP, 41% more swings = 46.95 swings per minute
Chance to proc = 2/46.95 = 4.2% chance per swing

Now lets say that you ding 60 and now you get triple attack. The delay of your weapon has not changed. However, due to your new skill, you swing your weapon 10% more than you did before. This is where it gets tricky. Since the delay has not changed, your SoD is still going to have a 6% chance to proc on any given swing. Since you are swinging more than the expected 33.3 swings per minute unhasted, your PPM fgoes up.

unhasted 33.3 swings per minute X 10% extra sings due to triple attack = 36.6 attack
Each attack has a 6% chance to proc, so 36.6 x 6% = 2.19 procs per minute

When you start adding more skills/AAs that increase the number of attacks your weapon makes, your proc rate will continue to rise. Of course this is pretty simplified for the sake of getting the point across.
