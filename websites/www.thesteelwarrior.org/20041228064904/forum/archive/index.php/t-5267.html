The only difference between any weapon in the game, is 1/3rd of the damage which comes from your base offense skill, and your damage bonus.

So to rank any weapon in the game, all you need is the formula

Weapon Damage * 2 + Damage Bonus / Delay

+

A damage modifier to predict the DPS of the weapon against any mob in the game.

This damage modifier has some factors in it. How much haste do you have, what level is the mob you are fighting in relation to you(base defense), about how much AC does the mob have(we can use an average per level of the mob), how often does your offhand weapon swing in relation to the mainhand, how often do you double or triple attack, what damage table is your class on, what is your offensive skill, what is your attacking skill, and any other modifiers such as Accuracy.

Since everyone seems to be parsing the same level 61 mob, and the attack/haste is relatively similar for everyone, the damage modifier would be relatively similar for everyone.

At 41% haste, against a level 61 mob, with around 1550 attack, the modifier is about 25 dps per 1 value of the weapon.

For the offhand weapon, due to a lower number of hits, 1 dps is subtracted for every .5 value of the weapon.

Basically, the simplified explanation of how attack works is this.

The first checks made, are the riposte, parry etc which determine whether you can even attempt the hit or not.

Secondly, is a skill check based on your skill in offense which goes against your opponents skill in defense. This determines if you will land a hit or not, and accounts for 1/3rd of the damage + the Damage Bonus portion of the hit. Classes with lower base offense, would have a lower hit percentage because of this, regardless of their attack. Your hit percentage would decrease against higher level mobs with a higher defensive skill, and increase against lower level mobs with a lower defensive skill. At Damage * 2 + Damage bonus, you will get a huge amount of hits, because this is when the skill check portion of your attack is landing for max damage. Since the values seem to overlap, you have a higher percentage of hits at the maximum and minimum values.

Thirdly is a skill check based on your attacking skill. For instance if you are using a 1h Slashing Weapon, this check would be based on your skill in 1h Slash. This is also where I believe the Time mod Accuracy works so you would not see an increase in your hit percentage from Accuracy, but rather you would notice a slight increase to your DPS with it. After it is established that your hit will land, Accuracy along with your weapon skill would help to boost the chance that you will do additional damage that hit. After that check is passed, your Attack comes into play and goes against your opponents AC. Basically, on quite a few swings, your attack never even comes into play. If you were to fight a really high AC mob, you would tend to hit for the Damage bonus and up to 1/3rd damage from the skill check portion of the attack.

Your max hit is likely tied directly to your base offense as well. At lower levels, you wouldn't have high enough offense to hit for the max possible.

I'll make up some numbers as an example. Let's say the skill check portion of an attack works by rolling your offense skill against your opponents defense skill.

We can use a scale, say 1-1100 for my example.

1-200 would be a miss, from 900-1100 your skill check portion of the attack would hit for the maximum value it can.

When you first start off at level 1, you might be around 350 just the same as your similarly leveled opponents. Each time your offense skill goes up, you would gain 3 points on this scale.

You would take your 350, against your opponents 350, and let's say you both roll a dice and the difference would be the value used to determine whether you miss or hit and if you hit, how much you hit for.

Since you start at 350, and your opponent has 350, and the difference has to be 201 in order to hit, the odds are against you landing a hit so when you first start out, you would barely be able to land a hit.

As you continue to play the game, your offense increases and in my example, it would increase at the rate of 3 per 1 point of offense. If you gain 5 offense per level, you gain 15 points over your opponent, so the odds continue to increase that you will hit your opponent. Let's say around level 20 you would have around 100 skill in offense. That would increase your total by 300.

You are rolling out of a possible 650 now, and your opponent, say he is a blue con mob 5 levels below, is rolling out of a possible 575. The difference needs to be over 201 in order to land your hit, but as you can see the odds you will hit are gradually improving.

If you were to fight an opponent 20 levels above you, say with a defense of 200 he would be rolling out of a possible 350+200*3=950

So the odds of you hitting an opponent 20 levels above you, are pretty slim. You would have to roll out of your possible 650 against his possible 950 and beat him by 201 in order to land a hit. It's not likely.

The reason your damage would be lower, is because it's impossible for you to hit for max damage with your offensive skill. If you are rolling out of a possible 650, and the maximum hit requires a value of over 900, it's impossible for you to hit it.

Weapon Damage * 2 + Damage Bonus / Delay will give you a value.

That value, will accurately rank any weapon in the game. I didn't make the formula up, you can find it if you look at your hits. When you use the formula for offhand, just remember not to use the damage bonus, since an offhand weapon does not have one.

Hammer of the Timeweaver
26 Damage * 2 + 13 Damage Bonus / 24 Delay = 2.708
Edge of Eternity
20 Damage * 2 + 0 Damage Bonus / 20 Delay = 2

2.708 + 2 = 4.708 is the value for this dual wield combination

Flip the weapons,

Edge of Eternity
20 Damage * 2 + 13 Damage Bonus / 20 delay = 2.65
Hammer of the Timeweaver
26 Damage * 2 + 0 Damage Bonus / 24 Delay = 2.167

2.65+2.167 = 4.817 is the value for this dual wield combination

EOE + Hammer = 4.817
Hammer + EOE = 4.708

So, someone would do more melee damage by EOE+Hammer as opposed to Hammer+EOE. Which isn't entirely obvious just by looking at the weapons. Obviously, other things need to be taken into effect such as proc damage. If proc damage is relatively equal, you would do more damage with the EOE in the mainhand, as opposed to the Quarm hammer.

When comparing the values to 2hers, the only difference is the offhand damage is very slightly off. Almost unnoticable unless you want to figure out the DPS of weapon. What I have found to work when figuring out the DPS, is simply to subtract about 1 DPS per .5 value of the weapon. So if the offhand weapon had a value of 2, once you figure out the DPS, you would subtract about 4 DPS from that figure. This is only if you want to get the DPS though. Just to rank the items, the values given above are fine.

Weapon Damage * 2 + Damage Bonus gives a number. Graph your hits with any weapon, and you will notice a huge excess of hits at this value. That is because it's the maximum value your skill check allows. Basically all damage done by this formula, has absolutely nothing to do with your attack, it's solely based on your base skill in offense. Whether you hit a mob or not, has nothing to do with your attack.

All additional damage done after this formula, will be the same for everyone with the same skill in offense, and similar attack.

The only factor in determing the DPS of a weapon, is how much of a modifier to use. I used 25 above, and 25 only works on the above formulas with an equal skill check, and relatively equal attack. For example, Monks would need a higher damage modifier, Shadowknights and Paladins would require a lower damage modifier in order for their dps numbers to be accurate.

The simple way to find out what the modifier is, is to just use an existing parse of a similar weapon, and divide the DPS of that weapon, into the value of the weapon. Then use that modifier on any similar weapons.

For example.

Coral Hilted Spike Driver, is 13/19 with a 13 damage modifier.

13 Damage * 2 + 13 Damage Bonus / 19 Delay = 2.053

Let's say you know that you can do 35 DPS with a Coral Hilted Spike Driver, with your haste, and your attack. Not that it does, im making the number up for an example.

You would take the 35 DPS / 2.053 value of the weapon and your modifier would be 17.05

Let's say you weren't sure, and wanted to get a Bone Spiked Crusher from Grummus. It's 12/18.

12 Damage * 2 + 13 damage Bonus / 18 = 2.0556

Due to the higher value, the Bone Spiked Crusher, would very slightly edge out the Coral Hilted Spike Driver. You are curious how much of an upgrade it is...

You take the value, 2.0556 and multiply it by your modifier 17.05

Your new DPS would be 35.048, since you were already doing 35 DPS with your current weapon, it wouldn't be worth it to get it since its only a .048 DPS upgrade.

The only thing you need to know when finding out the modifier for a Dual Wield combination is that the offhand weapon is slightly overreported, so when figuring out weapon DPS, subtract 1 DPS for every .5 value of the weapon.

Pretty much by knowing the value of one weapon and its DPS, you can find out what your DPS would be with any similar weapon quite accurately. That's extremely useful if you ask me.

To be able to calculate the formula, you would need to assign a value to everything involved. Add it up, then it would produce a number and that would be the modifier.

If you simply wanted to use this formula as a tool, it's still extremely accurate in ranking weapons, and telling you the weapon DPS if you use the correct modifier. Just simply take the modifier from some existing data that you know, and relate it to any similar weapons.
