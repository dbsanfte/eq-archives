Eriatha Egan
02-01-2005, 01:13 AM
Even with a full spoiler though, the zone takes a month or two to finally get down if everyone in your guild is going in there completely raw. What I mean is, any kind of spoilers you get for Uqua only get so far. It's going in there and actually executing it that really matters.

You want some tips for Uqua though? You're gonna need about four or more 9k+ tanks with hopefully well over 2100ac once buffed. Trash mobs start off doing ~300dps and slowly work their way up to doing about 600dps. Yeah, they get mean as hell, and you just have to deal with it. There is no room for error in Uqua. If your tanks and healers aren't on the ball and someone dies, it could mean a fullscale wipe. If one person dies, a 1300 hitting spirit spawns and usually goes straight for the casters, killing them, and spawning another 1300 hitting spirit. Basically, it can set off a chain reaction and there's nothing you can do to stop it once it starts going. There is no room for error in Uqua. You either play Uqua accordingly, or Uqua will play you.

You have to figure out where the traps out. Advancing into the zone like you're the Red-Fuckin-Army is a bad, bad idea. Traps spawn anywhere from 3-5 mobs, so it's a good idea for your pullers to learn how to stagger them so you're not dealing with the entire trap at once. Oh yeah, don't be afraid to succor back to zoneline after you move up to give yourself some more room. . .

MAX YOUR ALCOHOL TOLERANCE. I cannot stress this enough. There is an AE that goes off mid-twin fight (Rage and Furious) that makes you drunk. If you're like 150+ AT, you won't get affected by it. If you're caught mid-fight drunk, your dps goes to shit, your manapool goes to shit, and your evasion goes to shit. If you're drunk, you're a liability. Do something about it.
