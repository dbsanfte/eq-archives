EverQuest Chat on November 24th at 6:00pm - 7:00pm pacific time. Melee System Enhancements!
You've been asking for them and now we're ready to discuss them. We're taking a big departure from what many of you are used to when it comes to combat. With that in mind, here is a brief summary of these exciting enhancements to better help you target your questions to us during the live chat. Don't worry if you miss the chat, we'll post the logs on EverQuestLive.com


Increase the contributions melee classes provide in a group setting
Address the current deficit between the pure melee classes and other classes
Add more depth and potential for skilled play to melee combat
Provide a system that encourages attentiveness and decision-making skills, without focusing heavily on twitch gameplay

Take advantage of an opponent's weakness. See an opening, strike home for the kill!

Monks, Rogues and Warriors will gain the ability to recognize certain weaknesses in an opponent's defense. These "Openings" will be a variety of types and can occur based on several different situations during combat. Be quick though, an opening will not present itself for long. Strike quick with a new combat skill once your character recognizes an opening. These new combat skills are being designed just for this combat system revision. Using and maintaining these skills expends the character's Endurance (a new statistic, calculated from the character's basic stats).


Here's a combat example:

A warrior is serving as the tank for his group, standing in front of a goblin. While battling the goblin, the warrior makes an exceptional parry and is presented with an opening. An indicator appears with the "opening icon" and the word "Parry" next to it (this information also appears in the chat window).

Now is his opportunity to strike back after the exceptional parry! Only certain combat skills can be used to react to a parry opening. Other types of openings will allow the use of other skills. Some skills may be usable in response to more than one type of opening. Also, your position in relation to the target can change the effectiveness of certain skills.

In this case, our warrior knows that he can use one of three combat skills, each having some merit. One might stun the target, one might increase the hate generated by the warrior for a few rounds, and one might make it easier for everyone to hit the NPC.

In this case, our warrior elects to try to stun the goblin. He presses the appropriate button and luck smiles upon him as he successfully executes the strike, leaving the goblin standing dumbly for a combat round.

Increasing Your Endurance and Learning New Skills


Increase Endurance by increasing attributes or by acquiring special items that directly increase your Endurance pool
Find tomes or instructions manuals and take them to specific trainers to learn new skills
Choose your skills from skill categories (such as Tactical) containing skills focused to a purpose
Most openings are available only to a single character, but some are available to all nearby characters
Disciplines will be transferred to this new system
Existing Discipline timers will be reviewed, and several will probably be set to their own timer
Disciplines will require an Endurance cost
Hybrids will receive a limited Endurance pool for their disciplines
There will be other changes as well. Keep your eyes on the Test server and our update messages for more details as we move forward.

This new system is much more flexible than the existing disciplines. It will be relatively easy to add new combat skills, as well as new skills that are not limited to openings. This system creates the possibility for tremendous enhancement in the future, allowing groups a large number of options when it comes to melee combat.

I'm happy because they seem to have spotted flaws in similar mechanics in other games.

EQOA warriors had "real spells", and their power pool was the same as the casters mana pool. They even had targetted buffs, IIRC.

DaoC style system had swing-based openings. You had to activate your style before the next swing. Styles also boosted damage by 100% or more, which meant that non-styled combat was gimp. Combat typically dissolved to pounding your "any time" style key. They hacked on the ability to select 1 other style to activate conditionally, but there wasn't any real tactics: you didn't have time to think tactically.

There was an important asymmetry here, and I think it is the core of the real warrior/hybrid balance problem.

The combat style system changes this. Naked warriors will have useful and intrinsic abilities that a hybrid cannot duplicate (I hope).

And lastly, it opens up some additional symmetry between casters and non-casters. Now, non-casters will have the same "every expansion bring on new abilities" that casters have had for a long time.

This is the perfect answer, if SoE implements it properly. Let's use some imagination, and pretend Sony would be willing to do this:

Openings
Monks, Rogues and Warriors will gain the ability to recognize certain weaknesses in an opponent's defense. These "Openings" will be a variety of types and can occur based on several different situations during combat. Be quick though, an opening will not present itself for long. Strike quick with a new combat skill once your character recognizes an opening. These new combat skills are being designed just for this combat system revision. Using and maintaining these skills expends the character's Endurance (a new statistic, calculated from the character's basic stats).

Ok, these are RNG based, melee only actions. They are only executable 1) after a certain situation (ie exceptional parry in the example) and 2) once the melee has learned a skill. While they are subject to the RNG, we're still unsure how exactly they'll work, and what other peramiters outside of Parry. For imagination's sake, let's say it can activate when you Parry, Riposte, Block, Critical Strike, Bash/Slam, Taunt, Proc, or Disarm. We've been told that once an 'Opening' appears, we can then select a skill under a certain category (ie Tactical) to activate an action.

For example, say while Disarming, an "Opening" appears. You could then select skills from categories that work with that condition.

Disarm
Tactical: Stunning Disarm: Lvl 50 (6 sec stun - 50 Endurance), Hesistation: Lvl 30 (20% slow for 60 seconds - 100 Endurance), Disempower Lvl 60 (-10% AC for 36 seconds - 500 Endurance)
Offensive: Advantage: Lvl 55 (+100 Atk 36 sec- 200 Endurance), Flash Strike: Lvl 20 (3x damage next attack + blind - 100 endurance), Power Slash: Lvl 65 (9999 damage, 2HS weapon only - 3000 Endurance)
Defensive: Defiant: Lvl 50 (+15% mitigation 60 seconds - 300 Endurance), Magicbane: Lvl 25 (+50 vs. Magic - 90 seconds - 100 Endurance), Dancing Feet: Lvl 65 (+30% avoidance 45 seconds - 500 Endurance).
Agro: Humiliate: Lvl 55 (Increase hate 600 - 100 Endurance)

This in my opinion is a great idea, if the system can be worked out for flexibility and there is a large enough selection of skills to enable. It would also allow for specific skills for specific situations (ie Defensive only when taking damage, groupwide/AE skills when in DPS role, skills that activate when below 50%, skills that activate only when Endurance is 100%, and so on). The randomness of the openings should be offset by both the power and the flexibility of the skills. It's here where groups should be considering whether to take a melee vs. a hybrid class. Do you take the guaranteed snap agro of a knight with their spell flexibility, or the increased power and superior tanking Potential of a warrior. Or, do we take a beastlord for guaranteed DPS or a warrior (!) who has the potential to recognize openings?

Disciplines
Disciplines will be transferred to this new system
Existing Discipline timers will be reviewed, and several will probably be set to their own timer
Disciplines will require an Endurance cost
Hybrids will receive a limited Endurance pool for their disciplines


Here's hoping the devs do this right. But lets take a look at a possible "What if" scenario. Putting discs on their own timer means the ability to activate it individually more frequently, or perhaps simultaneously. Let's be honest, enabling warriors to Defensive and Evasive back to back, or at the same time would be a huge boost over knight classes. With this new system, it's a possibility (will it happen? who knows).

Now let's take this a step further - with potentially better reuse times and individual timers, you can use Fell Strike in a DPS role. Say the refresh is 6 minutes vs. 30 minutes. Then you can use Aggressive in the same role, increasing your DPS. Chain using Defensive, Evasive, Furious, and Fortitude could be an option depending on refresh times.

Now let's go even further and combine /disc with "Openings". What if there is a riposte "Opening" that Quadruples damage on riposte for 12 seconds. Couple that with Furious, and wow! How about a Defense "Opening" with increases mitigation +25%, while you're already /disc Defensive? How about a critical "Opening" that makes every crit into a double Cripple for 36 seconds, coupled with Mighty Strike? The potential for "Stacking" of abilities is only limited to what devs want to enable, and balance.

Non-Opening Skills
It will be relatively easy to add new combat skills, as well as new skills that are not limited to openings.

The potential for skills on the Endurance-use list that can be activated outside of "openings" on demand is huge. It's a perfect spot to stick a ranged taunt + hate ability! Sure it costs Endurance, but a small price to pay for on-demand, non RNG, hate boost. Warriors mitigation not balanced with knights? Just add an Endurance based, non opening skill with a +% mitigation boost. HP difference not great enough? Add a +X% hp boost skill which drains Z amount of Endurance/tic. This category of Non-Opening skills is where the biggest tweaking will occur I'm guessing, since it can be activated on demand, and doesn't rely on getting lucky with the RNG.

Is this the "hybridization" of melee? In a sense, yes. But it is a valid solution to the balance problem in my opinion. Of course, if SoE fails to implement solutions that address the main warrior concerns with this system, it really isn't a fix, is it?

I'm willing to give Sony a chance with this system. However, with such a powerful, customizable melee combat system, they have NO EXCUSE not to get it right. Mess it up, and bye-bye EQ for me. Cause if this doesn't work, nothing will - the system is too flexible for it not to work, if SOE wants it to.
