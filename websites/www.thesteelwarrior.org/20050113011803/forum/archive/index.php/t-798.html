As someone who in the past few months has used the Windblade, War Marshalls, Greatstaff, and now finally epics, I have to say get your epics if you can. Here is my reasoning:

Windblade: Too slow... but I did enjoy the damage it did while I had it. However, it didn't taunt enough for me to be effective. Was good for when I was just a DoT and not MT, but that wasn't very often and I didn't have the resources to have both the Windblade and an agro setup at the time. Ditched it to get...

Greatstaff of Thunder: I was rather surprised by this. For being a neutered version of the yak proc, it taunted very nicely. My big issue with this was that the proc did retain the knockback component, and I found mobs being pushed around too much. This may be a matter of opinion, however, as I am severely anal about mob containment. It usually falls on my shoulders to make sure mobs stay away from obstructions and accessible, and I just felt I could not do my job with this weapon. I was almost sad when I did sell it (because again it does taunt nicely), but the person offered me 20k more than what I paid, which I used to buy....

War Marshalls Bladed Staff: This weapon was a dream for me, on a DPS standpoint. As a wood elf, I always envisioned myself using speed over raw power, and this fit in well with that. Didn't do much for taunt, and so I used it mostly when I wasn't MT (which again isn't often, as there are only two MT's in my guild atm). I liked it more than the Windblade for when in DoT mode. The opportunity came up, however, to sell the staff and put it towards the green scale for my....

Epics: A little background to my anecdotal evidence: I regularly group/raid with a 65 rogue with all these crazy offensive AA's and insane upper-tier weapons (mainhand is 16/19, offhand 14/18, both with procs). He does some silly DPS, and you can imagine the backstabs. Normally he ends up tanking as much as I do because of it (the fact that he has more HP than I do and can tank just fine I place in the back of my mind to preserve my sense of self worth ;)). However, once another warrior in the guild got their epics, he told me he only has to evade after pulls, if that. He since repeated that sentiment about my own performance with the epics. That in and of itself was total justification for what I paid for the green scale (I won't say what it was... it's in another thread if you want, I'm still sorta embittered by it ;)).

Is my DPS now less than the first 3 options? Indeed. BUT, the DPS of my group has increased because Mr. Rogue can now open up the floodgates and poke away. He has parsed upwards of 220 dps now against exp mobs, whereas before he had to hold himself down to 150 or so and still tank half the time. If we are just looking at DPS to taunt, then we are competing at the same level as the rogues, monks, rangers, and wizards of Norrath, and we are not gonna beat them at their own game. And we can't just look at our solitary performance, we have to look at how our ability enhances the performance of the group, since warriors so very rarely solo. I try to make my choices to better the total throughput of the group, rather than my personal parses. Just my 2cp. ;)
