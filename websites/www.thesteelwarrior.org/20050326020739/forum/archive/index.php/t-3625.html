Umm, I wouldn't say your weapons are the problem. I tanked Vindi one time at lvl 59 with Priceless (13/20, no proc) and a Frostbringer (12/22, decent proc). Holding aggro on mobs while defensive REQUIRES cooperation from raid members. People in raids where the content is challenging must let a warrior keep aggro while defensive. I will give a couple of suggestions but these are for your raid party and not so much for you.

1. People with pets need to keep them on a short leash. The couple hundred pts of damage that Jibober does BEFORE assist is called isn't worth screwing up the placement of the mob for the raid. And they will screw up placement if a pet engages before assist is called. Vindi doesn't cast spells so there is no way Pet_01 tried to defend its master from an AE and there is no excuse for pets engaging early.

2. Melee may need to cycle attacks in order to avoid aggro. On boss mobs when I started raiding it was standard practice to go with 10 seconds of atk on followed by 2 seconds off. My weapons always lagged behind my guildmates so I could push this more than they could. This pretty much solves holding aggro off of other melee types in the raid.

3. People must follow directions from the raid leader. There is no excuse for anyone besides the puller/MT/SA to be off the wall in your case. IMO there are only two ways some of these people will ever learn to follow directions. First is to let them die and know the raid might wipe. Second is to trans their butts out before the pull so they can't screw it up. If either of these happen often enough they will get the message. Make sure that people are paying attention on all mobs. Make them be on the wall for clearing the mobs too and tell the puller to not pull until everyone is where they should be. Start out nice in tells with peeps but escalate to /rs Hold pulls until Clueless_class_02 gets into position and ENFORCE it. Idle threats are your worst enemy.

4. People must pay attention to what is happening in the raid channel and their surroundings. I get annoyed at having to spam a raid channel 10 times with a message like LET ME GET THE MOB OFF THE WALL. People just quit paying attention sometimes after assist is called. Usually it is a pet pushing and pinning a mob high up a wall. If people aren't paying attention I let them and their pet die and resume on with the mob.

5. If you are drawing aggro from damage or spells, stop attacking or nuking. Let the MT regain aggro and wait a while before resuming. You may need to adjust the amount of damage you are doing by increasing pauses between nukes or turning off atk periodically.

6. If you get aggro, do not run around like a chicken with your head cut off trying to get away from the mob. You screwed up ( unless you are healing/debuffing), so stand there and let the MT regain aggro. Running only results in the MT getting the "your target is too far away to taunt" message and the chance to wait for taunt to refresh while you are turned into paste.

7. Do not explore zones when you are on a raid. I have been wiped far too many times by people who "wanted to see what the zone looked like" and wandered off while on a raid. They inevitibly aggro mobs and run back to the raid. The semi conscious ones will try to die not too far from the raid knowing they screwed up and can be drug back and rez'd. Unfortunately a healer usually notices them taking damage and heals them, thus bringing the train into camp.

So many of these skills have been lost or never learned by many high level people today with the advent of the exp changes lately.

Now for the MT, I would offer some minor suggestions as well.

1. Make sure you use the best Magical arrows you can for tagging. Ones that actually hit and do damage. Same for Rampage tank.

2. Use Disarm skill. It causes aggro, so use it everytime it pops.

3. Increase the amount of time you attack a mob before you call assist if you are having trouble holding aggro. Our DPS goes into the toilet when we use defensive.

4. Damage shields on Vindi are your friend. Figure out the best combo you can get and let him help kill himself.

5. If you lose aggro do not be afraid to tell the raid to stop attacking so you can regain aggro.

6. Test your raid group when clearing trash mobs on waiting for assist. Kill a couple mobs calling assist normally and wait a long time before calling assist on a random mob hopefully soon before the boss is pulled. Many tanks are predictable on calling assist at x seconds or a mob at y% health. Make sure they aren't getting lucky on when they are guessing you will call assist.

7. If someone jumps assist /tell them and be nice about it to start. Everyone makes mistakes. If the problem continues stop attacking and back away from the mob. Tell the healers to let them die if necessary until they understand what the assist call is for and what it means. Rezing one person from dieing to a trash mob is infinitely easier than rezing a raid because they ping ponged the boss mob. It is amazing how clear concepts become while waiting for rez effects to wear off :)

8. Pets are not exempt from waiting for assist, nuff said.

Yes, these may seem harsh to some, but when people are raiding content appropriate to their level it is better to use good raid skills which can include sacrificing a person or two so the raid can succeed. I cannot count the number of times we lose a MT because a healer switched to someone who drew aggro and the MT got it back but missed a heal because the healer was healing someone other than the MT.

In my guild now, I have offered to be a sacrificial tank on boss mobs. Our warriors have AETaunt and usually if you dont have a heal coming before you start taking damage it can get ugly, so I have tried to get a spot defined between the first and second assist as a sacrifice tank. I would aggro mob and die while CHeal chain starts on SA who aetaunts when I die. Human nature is tough to let a friend die and so far the healers haven't embraced this idea yet but as mobs get tougher they might.
