Brabble
Yeah, here's the the way I'd figure it.
Melee aggro is not going to keep you ahead of the casters forever. It might help you keep ahead of some other melee dps folks, but they have to be way way ahead of your melee hate before they jump over your incite button.
So even if your melee hate production is a bit lower than someone else's at the beginning stages of a fight, you should be okay (assuming someone else is not trying to gank your aggro): your melee hate+initial arrow tag of mob (where possible)+incite+proximity aggro will tend to keep you up over a slight melee hate advantage by someone else.
So you hang on until a proc. And what you want at that point is the biggest hate proc you can get.
In this case, the droga.

I was in Velks the other day farming velium with a shaman and a cleric. All spiders early in are DB to us, but not especially dangerous. The cleric was not too busy so she started to melee. she was using an (unaug'd) CHoS. I decided to try something: I equipt a Blade of Insanity (13/21, proc itching madness --resist debuff 5 on a bunch of stuff, minus 30 on the disease counter). In my offhand I put in Bloodguard wand of walking dead (hmmm, 16/26 procs Guard II, which doesn't seem to have much aggro at all).
So, at this point, given that I have an offhand weapon, I'm doing more melee hate than the cleric.
Not surprisingly, I'm holding aggro as long as neither weapon procs.
If the cleric's CHoS procs before the Blade of Insanity I lose aggro, but that's a duh. The BoI would probably steal aggro from the CHoS if the situation were reversed and it proc'd first.
The thing is, that if the BoI procs and then after that the ChoS procs, the cleric *still* steals the aggro.
Now, this experiment is not parced and way way too short to be scientific. but I was watching the proc messages pretty carefully, and of course also who the spiders were hitting. And CHoS proc=spider instantly jumping the cleric.
I needed two procs before the cleric gets one proc for me to hold aggro, period. And needing twice the procs to keep up the mob from bouncing certainly looks like you're swiming upstream against the RNG.
I don't know the relative hate value of itching madness vs. rune, but for my money you have to have a pretty darn big jump in the melee hate to make up for a proc that is markedly inferior to that of the CHoS. Obviously I have no problem playing with different weapons, but I'm going with the best proc weapon I've got when the mobs are tough.
And no I did not let my cleric get below 85% health, lol.
