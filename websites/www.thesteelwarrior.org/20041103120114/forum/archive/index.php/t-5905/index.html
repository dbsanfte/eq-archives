the fb shield is supposed to increase all spell and bash aggro by 40%.

lets say your duel wield setup is an anger3 augmented GBoS and a CHoS.

2 anger3 procs from primary = 1200 hate per minute
2 force strikes from primary= 900-1000 hate per minute
1 force strike from secondary= 450-500 hate per minute

all for a grand total of ~2800-2900 hate per minute based on procs alone assuming the 2/1 primary/offhand rate.


now ... assuming that the shield does in fact increase the hate from all spells by 40%, lets look at some numbers

2 anger3 procs (840 each) from primary = 1680 hate per minute
2 force strikes (700 each) from primary = 1400 hate per minute

all for a grand total of 3080 hate per minute from procs alone.

now i know the dps would be a good bit less (somewhere in the neighborhood of cut down by 30-35%), but on the same note the aggro from bash (albeit small .. but still there) is increased by 40%. in this case, the shield is actually generating more aggro than an offhand weapon would be, while at the same time increasing ac, decreasing ripostes, and giving nice hp/stat buffers. adding another anger3 ldon proc to the offhand weapon would balance out the shields "overaggro", but lacking an induced proc in the offhand weapon, it looks like the shield hate is actually stronger. With the new WA aa's, further increasing the proc rates of weapons and augments, it would seem to make the shield have that much more of an advantage over duel wielding. (i mean really ... theres just a point where more aggro is kinda ridiculous ... if you lose aggro, then somebody other than yourself is doing something wrong).

the reason i bring this up is i picked up a stormrider carapace tonight. i havent got a good 1hs to burn my anger3 proc on yet, but i'm looking to one hopefully in the future. duel wield will be, of course, my primary choice for ldon and xp (hell and anger3 GBoS should give me more than plenty aggro pre-elemental .. and even in the elementals for that matter), but tanking bosses on raids, hard ldons, and rampage tanking, would see me using this entirely different setup. if the shield is working "as intended", it would seem that this item is heavensent for the boss tanking warriror.

sure, an anger3 BBB, BoW, SEWS, Vanazir is ideal for maximal dps .. nice stats, while maintaining strong/crazy aggro, but not everyone has access to such fine weaponry.

are my calculations off? plz poke holes in my "findings", and can anyone verify from experience whether this shield is worth my time to put to use.

ps- and plz dont knock the shield saying the aggro isnt as good if youre sporting an augmented BF and BoC combo, because i wouldnt expect it to outaggro very highclass aggro weapons. i just want to know if it will hold up with your average mid-tier raiding guild and occasional pickup open raid tanking situations.

I remember seeing a thread here about this where 2 essentially identically equipped warriors using BoC were using a FB3 shield and found that at FB3 you get more total agro than dual-wield. The FB3 warrior always got agro off the dual-wielding warrior.

Our problem is not having sufficient agro, but rather getting sufficient agro quickly. For that, you need PPM or procs per minute and in that respect, dual-wield can't be beat. You could tank with a rusty sword and keep agro once you had it thanks to other classes (Wizard nukes, you taunt, Wizard concusses).

AoW is a bad guage of agro since he has so very many hitpoints that he essentially works as if he were undead attacking the nearest player regardless of total agro since it's all a drop in the bucket to him. As long as the warrior was closer than everyone else, it didn't matter how much agro the warrior had and by the time it did, the warrior will have had a successful taunt anyway. All AoW shows is that the warrior was the closest to AoW and everyone else was further away.
