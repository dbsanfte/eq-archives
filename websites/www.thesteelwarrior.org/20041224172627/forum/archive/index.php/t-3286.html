NPC: a blood raven

Max Hit: 266

Max hit while shielding:
2hs w/ 50AC: 199

5 AC: 197

35 AC: 191

199 = 74.81% of 266
197 = 74.06% of 266
193 = 72.56% of 266
191 = 71.80% of 266

Damage reduction %:
2hs w/ 50AC: 0%
No Shield (HB/BoC): 0%
5 AC: 0.75%
15 AC: 0.75%
25 AC: 1.5%
35 AC: 1.01%


I don't have any shields over 35 AC to test with. But if you want to do some testing to add to this here is what you do...

Root a blood raven in PoN (near river, near zone in just to right). Put warrior just out of melee range of raven. Take other PC and put them in melee range and make sure they are aggro. Shield the PC. Have the PC sit down. When sitting the NPC will always take the exact same damage (max hit). Try this with each shield and without a shield. Post results.

If you are feeling more adventurous you can try something that hits harder...

Summary:
The AC of your shield DOES effect the damage taken while shielding.

Your AC does NOT effect the damage taken while shielding, only the person tanking.

You lower the damage from shielding by approx 1% per 10 AC of your shield. Thus 10AC = 1%, 20AC = 2%, 30AC = 3%, 40 AC = 4%... etc

This is supposedly capped at 50 AC from shield for an approximate damage reduction of 5%, which means you take 70% damage while tanking, and the shielded PC takes 50% of the damage. This still needs testing though as I do not have a high AC shield to test with. This 'cap' may be a rumor/myth.


Commentary:
In order to make this make enough difference to actually be useful, the % of damage reduced by AC needs to be increased. Doubling this % would make shields considerably more attractive than they are currently. In it's current state this % savings can be considered near unless or unnoticable.

A current example:
NPC that hits for 1000.
No shield: 750 Damage
30 AC shield: ~720 Damage
50 AC shield: ~700 Damage

Assume you take 8 hits on a /shield. This results in a total damage reduction of:
No shield: 0 Damage
30 AC shield: 240 Damage
50 AC shield: 400 Damage

And a damage taken of:
No shield: 6000 Damage
30 AC shield: 5760 Damage
50 AC shield: 5600 Damage


Using shield on any NPC that hits for 2000+ can generally be considered suicide w/o a special setup just to heal the /shield'er.

Doubling this % would result in the following:
NPC that hits for 1000.
No shield: 750 Damage
30 AC shield: ~690 Damage
50 AC shield: ~600 Damage

Assume you take 8 hits on a /shield. This results in a total damage reduction of:
No shield: 0 Damage
30 AC shield: 480 Damage
50 AC shield: 800 Damage

And a damage taken of:
No shield: 6000 Damage
30 AC shield: 5520 Damage
50 AC shield: 5200 Damage


This doesn't seem overpowering, but certainly does make this 'gimmick' closer to being useful than it is in it's current form. Increasing this even beyond this would be desirable, perhaps to 5% per 10 AC.
