With just the 60+ you can farm Halls of Testing quite easily. Thats the place to go for lots of upgrades for everyone. Other suggestions would be Vindi/statue, Doomshade, Rumblecrush, the big stone guy in Ahkeva (forget his name atm)...basically any velious mob, and most of the luclin mini boss's you should be able to kill with practice.

gulug
Ur looking at close to 50 60+ peeps. Id chase all those who are in the 60s to work their way up to lvl 65, then start doing their aa's.

if you can manage to field 40-50 65 peeps, then i think you have a huge range of options.

obviousy many of them will be suffering for AA's, but most of all the old world pre-pop/SoL stuff should be doable.

start with vindi first, hes the main hurdle you should clear and the benchmark by which you can gauge ur ability v other mobs. if you can nail vindi easy - and theres no reason you shouldnt with those numbers, then get him so he goes down to you real easy i.e everyone knows the strat etc. then you can work up to the other kael guys like statue and tormax.

I would definitely recommend you do HoT. Once youve cleared it out a few times, most of your 60s should be looking ok for armour, HoT resists are nice too. Also, 30 lvl 65s should be able to manage most of the BoT towers, so id farm those on a regular basis too for the ornate drops, GSo4W for dps for your melees, etc etc.

once ur starting to look reasonably equipped, then the natural progression would be to move onto NToV. you could probably skip HoT andmove into NToV but theres a lot of lessons which u can learn in HoT which will help for NToV and wont be so punishing on mistakes.

At the same time, dont forget all your SoL options - rumble, doom, grieg, Itraer etc. they are all doable. just a matter of mastering basic raid strat e.g. prox aggro, rampage tanking, CH chains, AE spells etc.

You also have a few PoP options - grummus & PoI dragon should be doable with those numbers, although u may have a slightly harder time on mobs like MB & AD, but even then, theres no harm trying because even if you fail, you will learn a lot.

Just make sure you have some direction in ur guild, set raiding plans , some proper form of loot distribution and that everyone is after the same goals i.e. raid days take priority, exp on own time and lvls > AA.

Two Things you should get down.

1) A succesful switch from one of your warriors to another without getting anyone killed once defensive drops.

2) Clerics that can count to 4 and push the Complete Heal button in order.

Once you can do those two things, you can kill a ton of mobs.

Some things that make fights EASY !!!!

Lots of Clerics with good mana regen...
A ton of DPS. (Rangers with AM 3, level 65 rogues and wizzies)

with 47 people 60+ if you have two really strong tanks, and 4-5 clerics, you can kill anything listed there. One of the hardest will be the avatar of War and you can definitely kill him with 5 clerics, 2 8k hit point tanks and 41 strong DPS members...

Like everyone says, give it a try. The best possible thing you can do is start placing your little 'moles' everywhere.

Make sure you have trackers in all the important areas looking for mobs.

I.E. a ranger in Akheva ruins, a spotter in Umbral plains, a non-kos dude in Zlandicar's lair (he starts out indiff) A druid or ranger in Temple of Veeshan to check on named, a level one rallos zek ogre in Kael looking for mobs, a CoV faction guy sitting at Klandicar's spawn point.

Making sure that people have assigned alts to each of these positions and are checking them frequently on second comp is a real key to success.

I agree with the above. You should certainly move from planar to Skyshrine armor which will bump resists, hp and ac. While in HoT farm not only the armors but the Dozekar symbol/tear quests (these items, in my mind, are as good as some of the stuff in ntov/vt) ... 38 ac bracer with 100hp and 40 percent haste, etc.

Practice on Vindi. Once you can kill him fairly easily you should also be able to smack Dozekar in ToV to finish the above quests and be able to farm Guardian of the Seal in the Hole (easy to get to by DA dropping down for his 15ac 85 hp ears and FT2 caster gloves + the new quests).

Once you are this point, before akheva and ssra I would get to know and work on the Burrower Cycle in The Deep and buff up your caster/healer ft - ft5 chests, ft3 range, ft2 rings etc. This is a great start to developing your own strats for rings involving wave type encounters.

Good luck.
