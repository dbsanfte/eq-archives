honestly you need to have a bit more leadership and restraints/rewards in your guild to solve the leaving problem.

with that in mind; start off small..y'all are 50+...do some kael raids. get a list going of your guildmates; and farm the hell out of the joint. get the leadership roles set out and organize organize organize !! plan raids. talk to other guilds whom are around/close to your levels/size and work out a schedule. farm the gear. promote those willing to lead; reward those who put their time in. with that in mind you'll be in HoT/planar/wherever you want to go.

believe me, it works. when i joined my old guild, Divine Legend, on Terris Thule server..we were barely in Hate/Fear and we were all scattered/scrambled about. In the course of a year they're now one of the top 5 or more ( been a while since i actively played on TT ) guilds on the server; progressing through VT now; still jamming the planes in PoP...and have made quite an impression on everyone as a guild who paid their dues and now are reaping the rewards.

In closing; you could use some gear now..but i'd start looking towards the future of what you'll be doing once you get these items. Sounds like your guild needs a bit of structure and the rest will all fall into place.

Yep, leadership is the most important part of guild advancement. When I joined my guild a year ago, we were rocking Kael arena and plate cycles. We also were making a lot of trips into HoT. We were smacking vindicator with 6 groups and felt bad ass =)

We were making small advances, but not really going any where. About early or mid may, we decided we were done with farming HoT, and having Vindi or the PoN named as our biggest fights. Instead, our leaders and members buckled down and we started to do a lot of advancement work.

Now we are working on getting as many people as possible ST keyed. We went through and decimated Aary, Lord Vyemm, Lord Koi and Lady M in NToV on our first run. We pimp smacked Tunare on our first run. We pimped the PoS Lords on mulitiple occasions. In a couple of monthes, we went from HoT/Vindi farming to being capable of killing mobs on a much greater scale. The driving force behind this was my guild's officers and our raid leaders. They worked their asses off to plan and strat mobs, and our raiders took responsiblity and played smart.

Organzie a plan of action. Set goals. Figure out the steps you need to take to accomplish your goals. And don't give up. Hopeflly you can retain some of those 60+ people. Soon, when others hear and see how you have a plan, and that you are taking steps to succede in that plan, you'll have some of those 60+ comming back. People want to be part of something focused in EQ. Set a goal, and work on it, and the people will come. And then the raids and the gear.

I would strongly suggest you go kill anything and everything that does not chew up your little guys with AE's...

I.E. Rumble, Va'dyn, Vindi, Doomshade, Statue, King Tormax

Grab a couple extra clerics or 58 Shaman/Druid Healers. Your warrior with proper buffs will probably max out at around 7800 hit points.

That is more than enough to kill the mobs listed above if you can get 5-6 clerics (the tougher fights), one reliable 61+ slower and some DPS.

Any DPS :) (Borrow if you have to)

So while you are waiting around for the guild to level up, Go work on:

a 100 HP neck piece (Va Dyn)
a Vindicator Breastplate (duh)
a 70 hp 15 Stamina Cloak (Rumblecrush)
a Vindicator Boots (duh)
a 41% Haste Earring (Doomshade)
a 50hp 10 Stamina Bow (Doomshade)

Different story here, but something to think about in time...

a 75 hps 10 stamina set of boots (King Tormax)
a 100 hps 12 stamina helm (King Tormax)

Remember that the higher you go in hps, the further up the list of items you can go. Start with Va Dyn. Then move to Rumblecrush and Vindicator. Once you have a strong cheal chain and your warrior is topping 8k hit points, you may be able to survive KT as a single tank long enough to take him down.

No AE = attrition fight.
More clerics = longer fight.
Higher Hps = more room for error.

The poster above is right to suggest CT. You will clean up in there. Just dont be afraid to try some of the raid targets too :) You have about 1700 more hps than I did when I did Vindi for the first time. I probably have more DPS than you will, but I really don't see that being a huge issue :)
