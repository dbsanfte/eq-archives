Haste merely decreases the time between rounds of attacks, making you swing faster overall. This doesn't increase proc rate as you're still swinging the same number of times per round, it's just the rounds take less time to cycle.

Ambidexterity actually increases the chance you'll swing that offhand each time the delay recycles independant of haste, it makes your attacks per round higher. Punishing Blade does the same, not sure with Flurry, but possibly the same.

Small difference, but it seems the proc rate is altered by the time between rounds, not how many swings you're doing per round (as there's no delay between the different attacks in the same round for each hand, they happen immediately). Haste lowers this delay, so it therefor lowers the proc chance.

I don't understand. How does haste not give you more attack rounds.
Well, it's fine that you don't understand, because the post you were responding to is explaining it incorrectly :)

What appears to happen is this: For primary hand, every time the weapon's delay timer is up, a proc check is made. If the weapon proc passes the check, the weapon procs, if not, then the aug proc check is made (assuming you have a proc aug), if that fails, then the innate proc check is made( also assuming you have one). You can only have one proc per round (round being defined as the set up swings that occurs when a weapon's delay time expires), and the precedence is weapon, then aug, then innate, as mentioned above. The number of swings that occurs in the round is irrelevant, you have the same chance to proc on a quadruple round as on a single swing. The check is per round, not per swing.

For the off hand, it is the same as above, except that first a DW check has to be made before the round occurs. Having Ambi increases the chance of making the DW check, hence increasing the number of "rounds" with the off hand, without decreasing the effective delay of the weapon. That is the key.You have a base chance to proc, dependant upon your dex. This is scaled to your effective weapon delay, so that regardless of your haste, your number of procs/min remains the same over time. Ambi increases the number of rounds without decreasing the effective delay, which means that you get additional chances to proc. Fero and PB only add swings to the round, but do not actually increase the number of rounds, and so doesn't affect proc rate.

The way I've always heard it explained, and what seems to have worked for me, is this:

Anything that increases your number of swings in a given amount of time, without changing your delay, will increase your proc rate. If you're using a BoC offhand, with no haste lets say, your delay is 20. This is your delay regardless of whether dualwield fires or not; your BoC has a 3.33% chance to proc on any given swing. (Once per minute proc rate offhand / 20 delay = 1/30= 3.33%) What ambidexterity does is make dual wield fire more often, thereby increasing your total number of offhand swings in any given time while maintaing the SAME delay. So ambidex increases procrate.

Stuff like PB, flurry, etc etc should also increase your procrate, but very very slightly. You have a small percentage chance to proc on any given swing, and pb/flurry/etc only give you a few percent more swings per minute. These numbers are wrong, but let's say your primary weapon procs on 5% of your swings (30 delay weap, no haste) and your flurry AA gives you 10% more swings in a minute... your proc rate will only increase .05 X .1 = 00.5% more procs per minute. This increase is pretty small, small enough that with EQs RNG, it's pretty much insignificant.
