Trial of Execution is the one I've always done. Basically a timed wave event where you have to kill all the mobs before Executioner gets to the platform and performs an execution. The three keys for us were:

1. Have MA select targets very fast, and move from mob to mob quickly
2. Know which mobs are mezzable and which aren't. Believe the stone guys are not.
3. Having someone that can banish summoned creatures is a big help.

Im sure there are many other strategies, and some of this depends a lot on gear level. Mainly get high DPS together, use /assist and switch targets fast, use banish if you have it and are running out of time on a wave.

Once the waves are killed you will have to kill the executioned which is not too tough. When I first did this we would have a ranger or someone kite the Executioner around the room while rest of group medded. Not sure if this is still needed. Again, depends on level of players and gear level.

1) Have a mage in group if possible, if not get mage in guild to DS EVERYONE...45 pt DS is HUGE when the mobs have about 10k or so HPs.

Just jump into them and see what you can do. Most of them just require killing either everything or a certain mob as fast as you can, and if you fail you can start over right away. Really if you have a group of 65s you shouldnt lose once you figure out how the trials work.

To figure out how the trials work listen to the emotes during and before the trials. For example during the trial of strangulation or hanging or something you get an emote "the prisoner starts to choke" when a mob named something like "spirit of strangulation" spawns. If you kill this mob fast enough you see an emote that says "the prisoner can breath again" if you dont get some emote like "the prisoner dies". After this just repeat 4-5x when the prisoner starts to choke again and win. Not exactly rocket science.

a couple things about the executioner trial:

1. the stone mobs are not mezzable, so get them first.

2. although it rarely works (and may only be possible in the first wave or 2), the stone mobs can be banished by the mage/druid spells...if we get more than one stone mob the MA takes one and the mage/druid tries to banish.

3. whatever you do, don't let anyone die - you'll need all the dps you can get (I'm assuming you don't have uber types to make up the dps loss.)

4. after the last mob there is a named to kill (it spawns on the platform.) This mob should be slowed, and hold your defensive disc until you get to this point. (i.e.; don't go defensive on any of the waves...wait till the single mob at the end)

5. think it's already been said, but the MA must target quickly. We don't wait for assist calls, but we do wait for the MA to get a new target.

Good luck! (btw, the trials are a lot of fun!)

DON'T USE BANISH - IT'S A WASTE OF TIME.

We did this recently in my guild, flagged a ton of folks.

I played my 65 wizard for dps. We had a decent 65 war and 65 cleric. We failed once out of about 10 times. We kept the same core 3-4 people and added 2-3 others each time to get everyone flagged. Do it that way. Don't try to just throw a bunch of different groups together and expect to win. We typically had the fourth be any decent level 62+ dps class and the last two just basically had to provide decent dps and we were golden. You need a good tank and cleric because the cleric needs a good mana supply and is also going to get hit.

We had the casters move to the back corner and the tank start in the middle. The mobs would all get on the bank and the casters would assist. I don't recall how the cleric kept aggro off him from the other mobs, we had an enchanter that handled that most of the time, I think the other times it was the cleric and other casters with root based crowd control or the tank using AETaunt.

I then tanked the trial myself as a 61 warrior (might have been 60) with no defensive AA's. We didn't have a lot of dps. We made it through second try, but it was hard. I was taking a serious beating, especially from the boss at the end. I'm sure it would be easy for a 65 warrior with a few defensive aa's, but it wasn't easy for me at 60-61. I'm sure a 65 knight in ornate or equivalent or greater could tank it just fine.
