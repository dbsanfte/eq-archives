First Zek = Quad1000 , Dispell, INSANE FRONTAL AE (cone) Sorry Emilia, but this is one of my biggest pet peeves in EQ - there is no such thing as a frontal AE. Not understanding how AE works usually ends up getting people killed. There are only 2 types of AE - Targeted and Point Blank.

Targeted AE - this type of AE the spell is cast on a target (the main tank) and the area of effect is measured with the MT at the center of the effect. This is usually the type AE that people call frontal. The problem is that when you corner tank a mob and push it onto the MT, the AE effect will reach well behind the mob and people will be hit needlessly.

Point blank - this time of AE emminates from the mob itself. The mob is the centerpoint of the effect and to avoid this you have to exceed the range of the AE from the mob.

Both types of AE have a 360 degree area of effect, the difference is where the center of the effect is.

Tallon Zek likes to warp away from the raid (and bring back adds). Has anyone found a good way to counter this?
Both Zeks are "leashed" to the room and corridor. If they get close to the stairs in the end of that corridor they will warp back to the room and bring the decorins left there. Usually this happens because someone can't control his aggro, combined with some awful pathing (he runs to that part of the corridor and then comes back or warps if he goes past the leash).

Only happened to us the first times we did, after that people were a tad more careful with their aggro.

Saying "its a cone" will get them running away as soon as he is facing them.
But also will give them the idea that they are safe as long as he's not looking their way, which is false. You can still be hit from behind if the MT is close to the mob, as in cornering him.
