Sargasso
In an attempt to NOT beat a dead horse, I wanted to take a few minutes to examine what I feel are the severely overlooked issues with regards to the “casual” versus “uber” playerbases.

From reading posts in prior message strings I gather there are two main issues that the more casual player has with the current makeup of Everquest. Please correct me if I am wrong about these two generalities.

1) casuals want access to gear that more closely approximates what the uber raiders have access to
1) casuals are unhappy that content is essentially cut off from them to progress to without being in a raiding guild.

Let me tackle the first issue first by asking a question.
WHY, do those that for the purposes of this discussion, want access to gear and augments better than they can currently get from LDON’s and single group content?
I think this is an important question to ask. I am asking this from the perspective of someone that has been both a casual player and am now currently in an Time/Uqua guild.
In the most basic and non biased view…Gear is and should be all about NEED. Stop and think for a minute….why do the Ubers have access to the best gear. Because the NEED it. They need it for survival. The massive HPs and resists available on high end gear along with the effects are all NEEDED to survive progressively harder RAIDS. The gear is designed specifically with this in mind. You simply cannot survive the incredible melee DPS and the nasty AE’s the high end mobs produce without this gear…and that is NOT the case in almost ALL single group content…and in my mind THIS is the difference.
So I go back to the WHY do the casuals want the same (or close to it) gear that the ubers have?

Gear availability should be appropriate to the content you are tackling…what is it that you NEED to progress through the content YOU are doing. I think if those of you that are in the “casual” status among us were to really stop and think about it objectively, you might realize that you don’t NEED the same type gear that the hardcore raider does. You might WANT it, but that doesn’t mean its appropriate the you should be able to get it.
Let me use GoD as an example since it is the newest expansion. Is GoD tough? Damn straight it is. Can you manage to progress through GoD without being elemental and time geared….of course you can….you just can’t do the RAIDS. GoD has a lot of really nice content and this is where point #2 above comes into play, but folks there is so much you can do in GoD with the best Ldon gear available…and LDON gear is available to ANYONE. Yes you must put in your time in LDON to get the best gear, BUT its doable in short chunks of time for everyone. Gear yourself in LDON gear and augments, buy a couple of other drops in bazaar and you are ready to move to the lower end GoD zones.

Now there is this train of thought that says “GoD is strictly for the ubers,” yet this is utter hogwash. Look at this post.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=8214
Ciae did a heck of a nice job showing us all just where to hunt and what to kill to have a shot at some VERY nice gear, some of which is close to being on par with elemental gear. Yes it may take a while to gear up to move further this way, but casual folks say they don’t mind putting in the time to get what they want or need, they just can’t do it in 6 hour chunks. If that is the case then this is perfect for you. While this is NOT the same gear that drops in Time plus zones, its still upgrades over LDON gear. Its still gear that you NEED to progress further in single groupable content. Farm this stuff then start doing Tipt/Vxxed for better gear and augments. Tipt/Vxxed drops elemental quality loot and its all single groupable. Once you have beaten Tipt/Vxxed and farmed it for gear, move to Kod’taz and do the same thing. Gear up with the gear and augs that drop in KT and then try your hand at the single groupable Ikky events. Are they hard? YES…but they are single groupable and if you have spent the time gearing up in the prior GoD zones they are NOT impossible. Move into Yxxta from KT and farm the drops there…that drop off trash mobs.
Now..most likely this is where your progress in GoD stops as a casual…but c’mon that’s a LOT of content for you to play with and since you will be farming it for gear as a single group, it will take you longer to do that raiders thus giving you plenty of content until Omens comes out. Omens is supposed to introduce a TASK system that will give you even more to do…and I’m sure there will be exp zones there as well.

Now you may say “this is all well and good” but that doesn’t change the fact that my progress stops far short of where an uber raider’s progress stops.
This is true…but you have to understand that EQ has two player types to cater to…casual players and hardcore raiders…thus they have to create content for BOTH. The problem is that the casual player cuts HIMSELF off from half the content by choosing to be casual. This is not an indictment of the casual player in any way..its just logistics. The raider has all the content available to him because he either chooses to raid OR has the time to do so, thus he can both raid and single group. It is your CHOICE to remain casual, forced or not by circumstances.

If you are a casual player, ask yourself this question….what would you do if suddenly all zones were open to all players? Lets say Sony opened up Elementals for starters. What would you do? Do the casuals realize that the elemental planes offer very limited single group content and are designed MOSTLY for raiding? The single groupable content is designed there for those that have been raiding and have the gear to handle it. Should Sony dumb down the zones so that the casual player can go there for EXP? Do you realize that even if you had access to Elementals you would get almost no gear there without raiding as there isn’t much that drops off non raid mobs (yeah a few things but not much)? Aren’t the hardcore raiders allowed to have content designed for them TOO?

In my opinion Sony has actually done a pretty nice job of designing expansions for both casuals and ubers. There is so much to do its ridiculous. As a matter of fact, prior to the release of Gates, the last two expansions were designed with casuals in mind (LoY and LDON) and those that were finishing PoP had nothing to look forward to if they were hardcore. Gates was definitely another raiding expansion, BUT there is a TON of content to do if you are casual, and much of it is very fun..you just have to give it a shot.

For all of us, I hope we will in the future ask ourselves this question when we complain about gear, augments, spells, etc and the access we have to them….ask yourself “DO I NEED THIS OR DO I JUST WANT IT.”
Its kind of like the difference between being a HAM amateur radio operator and a FM station operator…what type of equipment do I NEED to do the task at hand.

Thanks for listening.
