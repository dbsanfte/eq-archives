Brabble
Been wondering lately what non-warrior alts some of you have and most enjoy playing.
I've got one non-mule slot left on my account & I'm trying to figure out what might be interesting.
The two alts I most often play are a shaman and an SK. Neither suits me as well as my main, but both are very interesting.
Of those, the shaman is pretty seriously into making his group function with max efficiency. He does a lot of thinking. The cool thing about him is that no matter what the make-up of his group, he can make the group more efficient. Usually what he does is not all that flashy, but I've noticed that the best players in any given group are usually the first to notice the effects of a well-played shaman. (Oh, AND he can SoW himself and OMG gate r00ls!).
The SK (a troll) is a hoot because for various reasons he's very relaxing to play. Mobs hate him, he hates mobs. He dies easier than my warrior did at his level, but he cares less, heh. I'm not sure I'd like him as much as I do if I really wanted to build an MT, but he can tank and yes I admit it I get a level of enjoyment from Harm Touch all out of proportion to its actual effect.
It seems to me that at one point it was very common for warriors to have a WIZ alt, but I don't know if that's still true.
Obviously clerics and chanters are much in demand. Haven't tried a chanter; have a cleric but for some reason playing him hasn't really turned my crank so far.
Guild needs, in this case, are not a concern--just looking for some hands-on experience from warriors about alts they've enjoyed. Might give some weight to an alt that is not expensive/difficult to gear up (I probably won't twink this alt much if any).
And yes I have a warrior alt already, thanks.
I realize that to a large extent a class that is a blast for one person might not suit another, but given that, what do you guys think?

Battleblade
I have a 56 Druid, 53 Paladin, and a few sub-30 twinks.

The Duid is intended to be for tradeskills and tracking mobs. I probably will level him up some more since his heals are pretty weak at his level. Other than that, they are nearly a perfect non-melee class. KEI, FT, and focus items have a huge impact (especially focus items).

The Paladin is also a joy to play, but the fall off in level 50ish groups is dramatic. From 50 to 56, I'm pretty well convinced a well equipped Paladin can run rings around most Warriors. At that level, the raid game isn't hugely important and that advantages of the Paladin class are almost overwhelming. A VERY well-rounded melee class with no (afaict) obvious deficiencies.

I've played low level Ranger, Bard, Enchanter twinks. Primarily this is in order to learn a little about how classes really are in groups and how some players can leverage advantages and paper over dificiencies. I've learned a bit, BUT

It seems to me the low and mid level game is dying off. If you see 2 characters fighting, it's almost always someone PLing a twink. Groups are not easy to find.

Other than Vox and Naggy, there is very little "old school" EQ left. A shame really. I'd kinda like to see SoE improve Old World group and raiding opprotunities, double the ZEM, improve loot tables, improve drop rates substantially, and protect that content only from bottom feeders. IMO there needs to be EQ for alts, twinks and casual players where guild progression takes a back seat to just logging in and having fun.
