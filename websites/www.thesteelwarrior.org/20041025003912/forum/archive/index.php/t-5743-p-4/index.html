First, I am a tank. Any warrior that thinks i get picked for a group because of my "utility" is sadly mistaken. My utility is always 2nd in value. My ability to pull is rarely needed, and when it is, there are 4-5 other classes that can do it as well or better (LDoN anyone?)

I do have good burst DPS, but it's only useful for one fight per hour. I can provide some mana to my casters, but that is not what wins me a spot in a group. What wins an SK a spot in a group is his/her ability to tank...to hold aggro and take damage reasonably well. Period. The same is pretty much true for Pallies, all LDoN-normal laugh encounters aside.

When you are talking about RAID encounters, I have no desire whatsoever to be an MT with my SK. It's not my job. It's not my aspiration. I want my warrior brethern to perform that role. I'm pissed enough that rangers, monks, rogues, and even bards, for the love of God, can tank better than me on a raid boss, but that is another issue. Warriors have their rightful place on raid encounters.

But, despite all the claims to the contrary, 1-group trials are NOT raid encounters. The may unlock raid content, but they are still 1-group encounters. And to succeed at a 1-group encounter of the kind we are seeing in GoD, you have to pick a damn fine group. And when the top knights on the top servers (NOT my gimp server) are complaining about the same damn thing, I find reason to worry. I do NOT have the gear yet to be tanking Trials 7-9. I am fine with that. But my gear is on par with almost every warrior in my guild...slightly below some, slightly above many.

When it comes to single groups, you have this problem. No matter how much you like to point out the "utility" I and other knights bring to the group, that is NOT why we are invited. If a warrior is tanking, knights are generally only invited because they are a) friends or b) nobody else can be found to fill that utility role that is better....i.e. spot healing, pulling, or whatever. When you only have 6 spots, and you have a trial as hard as the Ikkinz trials, you simply can't afford to choose a gimp utility class to fill that role unless you are giving them a mercy spot (in order to advance your guild), they are your friend (really just a variant of a mercy spot), or you simply can't find anyone else. Necros are better backup rezzers. Druids, and even shammies, are better backup healers, with the other utility they can provide. Bards, monks, rangers, druids, clerics, enchanters...blah blah are better pullers.

I have not imagined that I am not being invited to Ikkinz trials. I have not imagined that every damn knight in my guild is not being invited to these trials. When I got my Tipt flag, it was a mercy flag. I was invited because a group member had gone LD, and I MIGHT could corpse summon them if they showed back up later - they didn't and I reaped the benefit of a flag. Pretty much every knight in my guild received their Tipt flag as a "mercy flag". They were invited to join along with a ringer group that was there for the sole purpose of getting our guild past the trial.

I don't want to be in a mercy group. I remember how warriors felt being invited under the same circumstances early on in the PoJ trials. I really felt terrible for them. Even when we'd take a warrior, the knight would tank everything until the Executioner. I remember distinctly having this conversation with my warrior friends that were better geared than me....Joe, I know you have 2k HP on me, but I'm gonna be tanking the trash because I can hold aggro and your sucky ass can't. It sucks, but it's the only way we can win these trials.

We've done a 180 here now. I hate it. I don't want to tank every mob in the game, but content that was designed for 1 group I think I should be able to tank given the appropriate gear. Yes, I should farm PoTime before I can rightfully tank a lot of stuff in GoD. I have no problem with that. But my PoTime geared SK buddes over at shadowknight.org are saying they can't even tank this crap without relying on a shit-load of healing and even more luck.

Frankly, I hate the idea of a defensive-type discipline. I am equally sickened by the idea of making the content EASIER. I like it hard. I loved doing LDoN hard missions even when I could barely handle them. I actually like the fact that SOE put in some progression content that doesn't require 72 buffons running around like idiots pretending that they're doing something l33t and important.

However, there is such a thing as taking things too far in the other direction. Sure, 1-group trials SOUNDS COOL, but when you effectively render 98% of the other two single-group tank classes almost completely usless on those 1-group trials, something is seriously wrong. And let me make myself clear. In single-group encounters, knights are NOT add-tanks. We are MTs. Warriors and knights should both be equally-viable tanks in single-group encounters. Period! And here is why...

On a raid encounter, everyone has a job. The encounter is large enough that everyone can play a role. Paladins can be effective spot-healers, and God knows they rez alot on the zerg-fests we call raids sometimes. Knights make great off-tanks on raids. And we are completely and totally happy with that role. Our utility on raids has been great ever since the Luclin. We have very few complaints. (FYI, SKs do NOT want a raid MGB buff version of AoH, so quit telling us we do!)

But on single-group encounters, we are not good enough at our "utility" roles to justify filling up a precious spot. Especially when the group makeups are so severely restricted in GoD. It's not like LDoN normal runs, where you can have a ranger be your primary healer. Even warriors need spot healers in GoD. You have, at minimum, 4 spots on these trials that are completely taken by specific classes. You have a warrior MT, at least one cleric main healer, a spot healer, and a slower. There are precious few classes that can fill the role of spot healer and slower.

As an example of just how our gimp utility becomes in such a harsh environment, consider the fact that bards and beastlords can very effectively act as primary slowers in pre-GoD single-group encounters (LDoN hard/normal, PoP, etc.) But how often do your special ringer groups bring a bard or beastlord for a slower? They don't! Because the difference between 50% or 65% slows and 75% slows on mobs that do 1000 DPS is HUGE! Therefore, a bard that normally has utility value as a slower pre-GoD, has almost ZERO utility value as a slower in GoD.

I am convinced that the real problem is not warrior mitigation, defensive, or any of this. The REAL problem is that these stupid ass trials should not be tuned towards 1-group. They should be retuned for 2 or even 3 groups. This would allow knights to then perform what is basically a raid role, but in a smaller setting. And I'll bet my life that the vast majority of us will be quite happy with that role. No defensive needed. No gimpifying the trials. In fact, the trials would be harder, because they would be tuned for 2 groups.

With two groups, you could have a knight or two along as an offtank, and benefit from their other utility value. With two groups, you could really open a can of whoopass by requiring the 12 people to control several mobs at once. An enchanter would still be required, but a bard could perform reasonably as a backup mezzer/slower.

The problem is that they used the wrong damn model for GoD trials. They used PoJ trials, which were tuned to be easily handled with lots of easily attainable gear and levels. What they SHOULD have used as their model was.....drum roll....the hedge maze event in PoN. In this event, you have 3 groups max that have to control multiple mobs along the way, with a final "big" boss at the end. That big boss was not originally knight-tankable, at least not without overgearing. Defensive warriors were badly wanted by the vast majority of guilds to tank that final boss. But knights could very effectively offtank the rest of the way. And I do not remember hearing any protesting by reasonable-minded knights about how we should have been able to tank the final boss. Sure, even I can tank him now, through overgearing, but at the time I was trying to get flagged for PoNb, I could not have realistically done it, and I had no problem with that.

So, fix the issue by retuning these trials for at least 2 groups. No reason to make it a huge zerg-fest. 12 people still keeps things interesting and requires a lot of teamwork.

Problem solved!
