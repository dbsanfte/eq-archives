Well, even before those two were things like Phinigel Autropos and Master Yael in the Hole.

Master Yael is gone now. Phinigel is always a fun fight. People definitely need to pay attention too.

The only thing I would say is that level-restricted raids are really tough things to try and do for your guild.

They are pick-up mostly because people level at different speeds.

at the levels you begin to think about Naggy and Vox (i.e. all 52) I would start by trying to break into the Arena in Kael.

You can get armor as rewards and learn to work with an MA, etc...

The other real raid type things to try are Fear and Hate. (hate has been gimped, but you can still learn a few things.

Just be careful that you have a backup plan if you get stuck Dead in Fear.

Plane of Growth is similar but a little harder to those.

Personally, I would put all my money on Kael. Learn to have a good MA, some good offtanking for any adds, splitting PoZ's, Learn to slow and debuff without dying.

Once you know Kael really well, you can Move to Halls of Testing. Same idea as Kael, but you have to learn how to work with Area Effect Spells from Mobs.

Holding everyone back till you get 18-36 people all level 52 to kill the dragons is not really the best thing for your guilld.

Let your faster exp'ing people lead the charge into Kael and beyond, you won't regret it.

The revamped Skyshrine is a very good place to learn and push your limits. The mobs scale up smoothly from very easy level 35ish trash at Wakening Land entry (like shin knights in LGuk) and get slightly tougher every time you move to the next room or corridor, eventually ramping right up to level 62 mobs at the top. At any given level, Skyshrine mobs are probably the easiest mobs in Velious for that level. There is very rare loot to be found, but do it for the practice. Bring a map.

Phinigel in Kedge is also an outstanding suggestion, but I would not try that until your average level is at least 45.

For level 37-45, you really need to consider how you will get your corpses if you get wiped. Lower guk dead side should be workable, invis to undead works on most mobs there, you can dungeon crawl to ghoul lord. Velks is also nice, most spiders don't see invis. You can also go to sol b, and camp the efreet for GEBs. I'd be wary of crawling deep into sebilis or similar places, unless you can afford to summon a lot of corpses. Long CRs really bite, they can turn off a lot of people who are depending on you to lead them and show them a fun time. You seem to be looking for learning experiences, so be sure to stress this to your members, and plan ahead for the worst case, so you know what to do when a wipe occurs.

I'd like to say one thing first: Any list with Gorenaire and Phinigel on the same level isn't helpful to a guild working their way up unless they started a raid guild with pure 60th+ level members.

I'd like to add in an agreement about ToFS and building to Kael plate cycle. Kael arena is actually somewhat easier than the plate cycle if your cautious and time repops but the drop's are so rare its mostly a time waster until your able to clear most of it between repops.

One thing is that when your members hit 47th (keep 1-2 levels range in case of death) then consider going to the Plane of Hate. It's very good aa xp for me at 63rd so likely good xp even in a raid at that level and the new Hate has all the old armour and new drops of quest items (rare). It should upgrade a number of people over time and corpse runs and pulling are dramatically easier than before. Stay at the zone in and just keep pulling.

Hate will also provide a lot of gems for cash and jewelry tradeskill resist items. Consider keeping some minor gems to cover peridot costs and coffins at raids and rotate Blue Diamonds/Diamonds to people needing resist items made. This can get contentious though.

One thing I noticed no one seems to mention is Sebilis. Getting keyed at 37-45 isn't hard if careful and a buffed tracker is available. Your raid could easily sit at the zone in and pull to there until higher level when you can fight towards some of the front loot camps.

From what I remember back then your people are more likely to be upgraded by getting 500pp and shopping in the bazaar than most upgrades you can see drop. So gems and cash from zones like Sebilis and Hate can be nice. Also learning to value resistances after you get hit with an Ice Comet is a learning experience useful later on in raiding.
