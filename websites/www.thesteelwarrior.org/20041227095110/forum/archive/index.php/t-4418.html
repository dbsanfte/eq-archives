Bah! We're all guessing, and I am too lazy to really look it up as well. My warrior was level 20 a long time ago, so my recollection is sketchy, but here goes my remembrance/guess... :p

Your damage is capped until level 20. If your weapon is a 6 damage 1-hander you'll never notice that there was a damage cap; but you would with a 14 damage 1-hander. Likewise for 2-handers. I recall that the best weapon to get your skill up was an Axe of the Iron Back (16/37) because before level 20 it always let you hit your cap of 33 damage, if I recall correctly. Problem was, if you stuck it out with the 16 damage 2-hander at level 21+ you'd still not really notice a damage increase, because your modal damage was still around that same 33 damage mark. You'd notice the increase with a 21 damage 2-hander, though.

Now...level 30...if I recall correctly (and I'm real fuzzy on this one) before level 30 you could only get three attacks maximum per round, even if both dual wield and double attack fired off at the same time. At level 30+, you could get 4 attacks off in one round. Of course, this meant nothing if you didn't *both* dual wield and double attack.

Then, later on, they added the primary hand damage bonus, which starts at level 28 and works its way up from there (+1 at level 28, up to +13 or +14 at level 65). This has nothing to do with the original game's damage caps, it's just an added bonus for being a melee class.

Feel free to correct or expand on my comments since, like I said, I'm kinda fuzzy on this myself.
