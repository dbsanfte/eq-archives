In the interest of getting at the deeper, historical foundations of the current predicament, I decided to do some research on the history of the paladin class.

The results were surprising, to say the least.

What I found was evidence that paladins were known at least as far back as the Chandela dynasty, in Khajuraho, south Asia (present day India). That's not totally surprising, since a long tradition of inscriptional sources suggests a group associated with horse riding appeared in the Indian subcontinent area at an early date.

But how do we know they might be paladins? A close art historical analysis may provide the answer:
