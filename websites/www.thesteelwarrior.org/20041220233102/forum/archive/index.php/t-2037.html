There are a lot of great tanks out there, but as a group calling assist seems to be a shortcoming every bit as bad as monks not FDing off aggro (this applies to paladins and SKs too, not just warriors).

I agree completely with what Hulanta said btw. The best sequence I've seen of this kind goes simply:

"Engaging %t, WAIT FOR ASSIST" (on big mobs)

This alone halves the chance the MT will die in the first few seconds of the fight on a big mob.

"Assist me on %t" (not necessarily called by the MT)

As for me personally, in exp groups I always call assist. Lots of reasons for this, but two of the most important are:

1) It helps let everyone know when it's safe for them to start hitting the mob (and helps them realise this isn't necessarily the instant I stand in front of it or plink it with an arrow).

2) It reminds everyone *I* am the assist, not the puller, and so when breaking mez etc they're already in the habit of waiting for my assist call.

But overall, it's staggering how many tanks feel it's frustrating, unnecessary and offensive to suggest they should call assist, and then blame their groups or raids for the result if it goes wrong.

It's up to the casters to know how much aggro they can get away with.
