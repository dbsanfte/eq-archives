Been my experience running both druid and a shaman, snare and slow seem to be on the same level of aggro. Only difference is usually the shaman is casting slow right at the beginning of the fight, where as the druid will wait till 80 or 50 to snare. A druid can still pull aggro very fast on mobs that do not like to be slowed very quickly with a few resists to snare. Not all mobs are build the same though, some mobs are strictly proximity aggro, while some mobs seem to care more or less about snare or heals. While I am not sure exactly where dd's are in on the aggro scale, however I do know that dd's are higher in aggro then say a lifetap. Lifetap's seem very low on the scale when it comes to aggro. Rain's seem to be fairly low on scale vs a straight dd. I have nuked at same time I have done a rain, rain did more damage in same period of time, while nuke pulled aggro. Resisted slow/snare seem to generate more aggro then if they had landed. DD spells still pull what seems to be the equivielent as if they had landed. And not all direct damage spells seem to be built the same, I beleive the wizard line generates slightly less aggro then some of the other classes in terms of aggro vs amount of damage dealt. I do not believe it is all 100% based on amount of damage dealt, but how that damage is dealt.
