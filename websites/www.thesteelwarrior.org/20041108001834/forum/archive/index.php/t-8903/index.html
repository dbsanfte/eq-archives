To keep game balance no more than say 4 should be useable at once and in the interface should look like the way pet buffs are displayed as small boxes along the right that a hold over would bring a name up and allow you to click them off so your minor abilities would not take buff space. Hordaek
I agree with the issue of End regen, but I don't think the solution is solely one of enhancing warrior End regen either innately or with items. To be sure, it would be nice, maybe even needed, if there was a focus type effect for End Regen similar in fashion to mana regen, but that would be only half the approach IMO.

For all you oldtimers like me, been playing both warrior and cleric since Nov 2000, remember when Stamina was actually an issue? Remember those fast pulling places like CB, Unrest, KC, Seb, Guk, etc when your little yellow bar would go to zero and your attacks slowed and you couldn't jump anymore? There was a "cure" for that then. It was called Extinguish Fatigue, a Clr lvl 19, Dru lvl 29, Sha lvl 39, Enc lvl 44 and Rng lvl 54 spell that replensished 90 stamina. It was a one spell line; no upgrades to it have ever been added; when melee stamina changed to endurance, no changes were made to the spell or upgrades added to those classes' spell repertoire.

There NEEDS to be limiting factors in combat encounters; a limit or weakness of one class compenstated for by an ability or strength of another class. Risk vs reward and cooperative gameplay are fundamental to the game. Those limits are currently, pretty much, healer mana and tank agro, and back in the day melee stamina. What's the point really, if a tank, of any class, can get instant and unbreakable agro allowing nukers to nuke, shooters to shoot and backstabbers to backstab and dotters to dot at max DPS and max pull rate without regard to risk or consequence?

I say to the developers, treat End like Mana thereby being consistent in approach. Clerics, (using as an example, since I also play one), have Mana Regen (formally Flowing Thought) and mana pool increasing items available, have innate Mana Regen (Mental Clarity and self buffs), mana regen and mana pool size alteration from another classes' buff(s), (Enc clarity line and Bst Spritual line), and direct mana replenishment (Nec and Bst spell lines).

So far, all that's been done that I know about, is adding End to items to increase the End Pool and a couple AAs for End regen (Heightened Endurance (GoD) and Fitness (OOW)). Where's the class interdependence stuff? Where's the End buff (either separate or part of an existing line)? Where's the End "heal" (Extinguish Fatigue line needs a desparate upgrade)? Doesn't have to be much; can even be mana expensive if needed to preserve encounter pace and risk. But there should be something, if only to be consistent.
