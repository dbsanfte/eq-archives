The whole point of having a DKP system is to put the power to upgrade in the individuals hands. When you start adding in a bunch of exceptions and special rules, you are taking away from the system. Of course, this also assumes that the people who are asking for the items are not retards. You have to have faith in your guildmates to make intelligent decisions - pallys and SKs pass on bidding for BoWs for instance. We have set values for all items, if you want something you put your name in.

BTW - what makes you think casters upgrade primary/secondary less often? Sure if you are comparing someone who tries to get every single weapon that drops, but seriously, no class needs to upgrade their weapons every 2 weeks. Every class has weapons that are considered the best for the level you are at, if someone wants to take every possible upgrade between what they have now and what the best your guild can get, then that is their business no matter how much of a waste of DKP it is. You have to have a plan and stick with it, not take everything that is a slight upgrade.

Krowbar
For us melee weapon costs are capped at 1/4th of the armor cap. So currently the most expensive item is 60 dkp which very few items actually reach. (Quarm Earring, Bertox mask are the only ones so far.) So the most expensive weapon would be 15 dkp but I dont recall any weapon going for that much. Vanazir is 14 dkp for example. This doesn't apply to caster weapons. Also weapons taken as range slot get the range slot cost.

The costs dont scale however. The not-so-end-game calibre items tend to go for a lot less. Rotting fist for example was 6 dkp. Periodically an officer goes through and reduces prices but this is not retroactive. Weapons without a proc and/or low hps go for 1-3 dkp tops. Also, some weapons did go for free. Wrath's edge was awarded to MTs for 0 dkp as it was useful for tanking emp (when we thought the damage done mattered for aggro).

Melee tend to upgrade weapons more than any other item because our whole game tends to revolve around them. Casters are not bound by this need as they rely on spells which are distributed free to everyone in order of total dkp worth. It's pretty much an obligation to the guild to make sure your gear is optimal, especially for the MTs.

Gerald
We have a pure bidding system. We earn points for every boss killing we attend. Most raids are worth 2 points. Some big raids (gods) are worth 5. We kill anywhere from 1-8 mobs a night (depends on what's up of course). Items are usually started off at a minimum bid appropriate for the item. Weapons are the same way. Because it's an auction type system, at one raid a weapon could go for 150 points then at the next 230 (this happened to me unfortunately). Usually though the first time something drops it goes for its highest amount. Each raid after that where item drops it usually goes for less but there are exceptions. It usually pays to be patient, but you can only be so patient in the end before getting the itch to spend points. This is the best points system in my opinion.
