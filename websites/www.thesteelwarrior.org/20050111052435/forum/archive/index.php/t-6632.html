My two cents:
I think of tanking along two lines. First, it's a verb: the MT/MA tanks the mob, which means select the mob, aggro the mob, position the mob, control the mob, make it possible for everyone else in the group to max the damage they can do to that mob.
Second, tanking is a noun: it's a function, and a group function at that. Mostly, MT is the active part of that function, but other melees sometimes have to actively control their aggro generation, shamans have to time their slows, wizards their nukes, etc.
A necessary component of this function is that the casters not get hit and go splat. None of the other components of tanking can happen efficiently if casters die.
The original post here concerned a specific situation: a group with an enchanter, two warriors, and multiple adds. MT has aggro on active mob, enchanter has mezzed stacked mobs, SA has END and incite and taunt up.
If I'm MA here, damn straight I want SA to taunt the first mezzed mob. If taunt works, go ahead and incite. I don't know that incite by itself after a failed taunt is going to help you enough to jump you over the enchanter on the hate list; I'd be inclined myself to save incite until after a successful taunt.
Phantron is correct to point out that if all goes well, the SA will be dumping his taunt and incite down an empty rat hole. But, if all goes well, the costs of that appear negligable to me.
Of course, things don't always go well. Ideally, all mobs wait patiently for the MA to get to them in a proper order. But if they don't, the next best thing is if those "free" mobs hit the SA--far better that than anything else they might think of doing, such as splatting the enchanter. I'm assuming here that in fact the SA can stand getting hit by these mobs for a bit if mez breaks/wears off--at least until mob is re-mezzed or MA finishes his mob and comes over and picks up the "free" mob.
The only potential costs I can think of are that the time the SA takes to taunt/incite/run and then run back over to help MT will probably cost a round or two of combat damage from the SA; also, between the time SA taunts mezzed mob and the SA's taunt refreshes, theoretically neither of the tanks would be able to taunt an unexpected add off anybody. But I would think that in an LDoN these costs are a small premium for the insurance against (even the unlikely) chance that the enchanter will get hit.
Anyway, the SA here wouldn't offend me as MT if he taunted the odd mezzed mob while I was busy. Now, if he keeps taunting the mob I'm tanking, or he runs into the next room and pulls some more mobs, or loots dead mobs in the middle of the fight, or just stands there motionless in the same place he's been for the last five minutes, or tells the enchanter or healer how to do their jobs in /g without having ever played a caster, or tells other group members that their gear sucks....well, you know there are a lot of ways an SA can bug me more than taunting a mezzed mob.
