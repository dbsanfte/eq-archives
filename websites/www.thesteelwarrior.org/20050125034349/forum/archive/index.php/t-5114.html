In another thread going on right now, someone said they knew their DPS was higher with some weapon combination X and they knew this because they could see it, it was so obvious. Someone else said, "Nuh uh, appearances are deceiving, you don't know sheeet unless you parse it."

I posted that I was SURE my CHSD and Claw of Lightning outdamaged my GSoT. I proved it to myself by abusing Mr. Regen (Kromriff Recruiter?) in Kael.

Then, days later, I started parsing logs. And I found that my best 1h setup does like 70-75 DPS, and for one evening at least, my GSoT was 103 DPS.

I have no explanation of how my 1h setup can indeed kick most of the snard out of MR. Regen, and my GSoT can't, while the GSoT parses up to 50% more DPS.

So, a word of caution for my fellow log spelunkers. You must be very careful when parsing and comparing results. Are your buffs the same? Do you have the same groupmates, and are they debuffing consistently? Are your datasets made from parsing the same mobs in the same zones?

I have enough points for an Anger3 augment, and I can't decide where to put it. If 2h to keep Riposte damage down, then
...GSoT with its DPS,
...Frostreaver with its nice ratio and low-speed, which reduces riposte, or
...2h epic which would sport double-aggro (Tempting!)?

Or CHSD to make a sweet fast double-aggro weapon?

Because I parsed poorly, all I know is that I don't know squat, and I can't decide what to augment! The only thing I know, is that I don't know anything.

Blah, rambling and lamenting.

Morals of the story are:
1) parse before you make decisions, and
2) log parsers don't give push-button answers. You have to set up your test environment very carefully to get meaningful results.

There is a really easy way to get consistent meaningful parses.

Rules for parsing:
1. Always use the same mob.
2. Always parse completely unbuffed.
3. Try to use a mob that doesn't hit back, there are a couple you can use.
4. Attack from behind so you can a pure parse with no special attacks effecting your results.
5. Always use the same equipment for an accurate comparison.

The "too fast" point on a two hander has nothing to do with ripostes, though they are a factor that has to be calculated for. Instead, at delays of 27 and below, the damage bonus on any two hander is a whopping 14 at level 65. At 28 delay, the damage bonus jumps significantly(Sorry, don't recall humbers off hand), and therefore, your damage jumps accordingly. Thus, 28 delay is the fastest 2 hander desirable for DPS without large amounts of vengeance.

And I would completely disagree on parcing methods and the number to look at!

First off, don't parce something that you don't XP on. 1) Its a waste of time and 2) you then have to figure out how attack/ac interact and what the reletive ACs of the mobs are (between your test mob and your XP mobs).

Two, don't parce for DPS, its about 100% useless and makes data harder to make consistant. Look at average hit and for average hit/delay when comairing weapons. (Since you are not going for DPS--you don't have to worry about haste buffs.)

I would rather have fuzzier number collected under my playing conditions than more percise numbers collected under conditions that are nothing like what you play with.

What you should do, while parcing and XPing and try and parce the same camp. With reasonably sized samples, mob variation should balance out and you might beable to see how DPS varries with mobs (how does Damage/swing compair between Krigers and brann's or what have you).
