I have recently seen, on a few boards, questions about targeting. It really disappoints me when I see warriors answer that question with something as inert as "F8". If, as MA, that is your only tool, you’re screwed. The "mouse and click" method ranks a close second on how NOT to target quickly and efficiently.

First, is there already a post on the boards that I’m missing that describes the preferred methods?

If not, then maybe these notes will help some of the up and coming…

The following only apply to the MA…

MA = Main ASSIST. By definition, you are the primary /assist. This is not to be confused with MT (i.e. Main Tank). These are two different functions that warriors can (and usually) perform.

As MA, your raid is looking to you for their target. If you are late, they have a tendancy to target ad hoc, and kill off mezzers. Not to mention they may grumble, escalating to full blown screams over time. If you are early, those that /ass often will be on the wrong target. Timing is everything.

Here are some of the techniques I use to get the correct target quickly.

1. Target your puller. When they call a mob, start /assist’ing them. If you have multiple pullers, set up hot keys. I like to keep those on about bank 8.
2. When you see multiple mobs incoming, make your decision quickly. Either stay with the one you received from “ /ass [puller]”, or switch…but do it before they get any where near camp. Your mezzers are probably /ass’ing you to see what the MA target will be then start trying to mezz the adds. Try to be consistent, it helps your mezzers as you gain more MA time. And they may even learn to like you.
3. Once the first target is low on health (10-15%), I’ll turn off attack, and start “looking” for the next target, without changing my target. This gives me the chance to decide what is next without releasing my target and causing confusion to those still on the “correct” mob.
4. If I do NOT have agro, I may even go stand next to the mezzed mob that will be next. Chanters love that! Gives them a clue as to the next mob before your call.
5. Once I see the “You have gained raid XP” message, or the kill shot message, IMMEDIATELY grab your next target and engage, breaking mez. The breaking mez part is important here. It allows everyone around to know the next target, that’s its loose, and to /ass you. The mezzer knows not to re-mez, which can cause serious agro issues, and allows the tanks that should be taunting an easier time.
6. Rinse, Lather, Repeat.


Notice at no time did I mention “F8”. "F8" is OK in XP groups, in raids, it will fail you given enough time. So how do I get the next target? During the "looking" phase, look to see where your least experienced mezzer is pointing. Thats where I start "looking".

Have fun ripping me up, as I know some of you don’t believe as I do. That’s OK. But please don’t answer targeting questions with some blanket “F8” stupidity.

F8's ok if you get in the mob's face first and it works fine for those mobs. If you're doing Bertox or some other event with a buttload of mobs in camp at any one time (VT anyone?) -- then random F8 is ass. Why? Answer this simple question:

Three mobs are in camp. One is bard slowed and mezzed. Another is Chanter mezzed, but not debuffed yet. A third is beating on a chanter after they had a mez resist. Which mob should the raid kill next?

As MA, even when we have multiple MA's and I only have 20 people following my target, I feel like my job is to pick the best possible mob to kill next. I'm keeping track of the puller channel, mez and debuff spell messages, the healing channel so I can tell when someone is getting bashed on that shouldn't, etc.

Just hitting F8 once a mob dies will often get a mob BEHIND me, not the one I'm facing. In some zones, F8 simply doesn't work. So really, if you rely on it, you will be hosed at some point.

And yeah, train your raid force not to assist until you call it. Something I do to "help" them learn: every now and then I click on something, wait 1s, switch to another mob, and call attack. When I see mez drop on the wrong mob, I know exactly who wasn't following instructions and get a chance to publically humiliate them. ;) Of course I don't do this in critical situations, but it's proven to be very effective at getting folks to stop spamming their assist key.

Something I found really useful to teach myself fast targetting was to make a chanter alt. I played a chanter to 56 and lemme tell you, raid CCers deserver a lot of credit when they manage to lock a pull down fast. It can be super hard just xping, especially with mobs who have super tiny "to click" areas (skeletons!).

Have seen problems with F8, so I use the assist button religiously. During a raid or major group, minimal communications are desired. I only want to hear from the puller, mez and mana reports. All else should have been worked out before.

On long pulls or bad visability, the assist button gains addition importance. When the puller is inbound, I will begin clicking the assist until I trigger on his mob. He is now close to being in range. In this situation, I will hail the target as an alert/wakeup for the casters.

In breaking mez - if time permits - and based on things I learned here, I will taunt the mezed target 3-4 times, hail it and break it. That way, I have the target in hand and don't have to do the target/Enchanter/Tank relay race...

If we have mulitple mezed mobs, I have the puller break off the fight at about 10%, locate and target the next most dangerous mob. Then when the main target goes down, I can use the assist key again (turning off attack- or not depending on time and circumstance) and begin taunting. This way, I don't have to be trying to make future decisions while trying to fight.

It's good practice to be prepared for the worse - you may get it, and then is too late to change tactics or discuss the problem.
