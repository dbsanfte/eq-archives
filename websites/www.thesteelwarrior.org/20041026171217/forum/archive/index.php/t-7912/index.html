Concerning the original poster's questions: no, don't worry about STR. Until you max your stats out, STA and DEX are more important.
Mainly what you want in weapons is aggro generation. There will be times when you are not the main tank and you will want to be able to do what damage you can without attracting too much attention from the mob. But very often the top damage per second weapons are not the ones that give you the eye candy crits. Look around the site for lots of threads discussing weapons.
If you've played a shaman for a while you've probably been in groups where you are the healer. You've noticed it's easier to cast spells when the mob is hitting the tank and not you (heh), and it's easier to heal some tanks than others. You want your warrior to be the warrior that is easy to heal and who does not let his casters get beat up.
Yoarik
The warrior's Mantra:

AC is God, HP's are saints.

If my enemy hates me most, my friends will live.

If my friends are alive, I will not die.

Our job is to keep the target angry at us, and survive it. We survive it in two ways, minimize the damage done to us, and maximize the amount of damage we can take.

The art of being a warrior comes in how you accomplish these basic tenets. It is possible to get to 50 not knowing this, but you won't get much farther than that.

Stamina is your key attribute, Sta> than all other attributes.

AC> HP because not taking the damage is easier on your healer than taking the damage.

Defensive AA's come first, because things like Finishing Blow and other damage enhancing AA's are the province of the Rogues and Rangers of the world. Real tanks take more damge than they dish out, we are relentless and armored and cannot be stopped.

Taunting and Aggro come before offense. The exception is that you increase offense when it has a significant and direct bearing on aggro. The base damage of a weapon is a significant aggro factor. Punishing Blade adds to your 2H aggro by increasing the "base damage" of your weapon. Hastened Instigation is a great AA because it gives you a "no fail" taunt more often.


Lastly it is our job to make fun of lesser plate wearing classes... Just like it is the Shamans job to make fum of lesser chain wearing classes.
