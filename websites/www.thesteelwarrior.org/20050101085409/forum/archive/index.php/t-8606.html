I'd reccomend you never use stonewall except when your dps is actually important (which is almost never). Stonewall kills any motility and utility you might have in crazy situations via the snare. Defensive is no more or less aggro ... so i'd just stick with that.


Imagine the following: You are corner-tanking mob-01 and pop stonewall. 2 necros arent paying attention (hell any 2 casters) or the RNG just plain hates you and you lose aggro. The first caster is summoned and immediately killed. Your boss mob then charges off to the casters to catch the other over-aggroing sonufagun. Now lets pretend this mob has ae rampage and fires off a rampage or 2 while in the middle of the casters ... meanwhile you are snared trying to hop-skip-jump your way back to him to taunt/aetaunt ... trying all the while to get a few lucky procs in.

This happened to us on a RZ attempt a while back on the fake RZ. A premature burn was called without solid aggro established and RZ charged the casters and ae ramped once or twice. Needless to say we failed. Our warrior was stonewall and could not regain aggro or even chase after RZ. You may think ... well our casters are smart and our tanks have great aggro ... but any caster can peel aggro without too much effort on an all out burn ... and even the best geared tanks are subject to the RNG screwing them over. This is especially true once your necros start getting focus items for their dots and have their crit-dot aa's. I'd much rather let the casters crank it up knowing that i can handle a poor situation than making everyone play it safe so i can double my already weak dps.

We don't allow our tanks to stonewall anymore in raid situations. Leave stonewall for xp groups when your dps actually moderatly matters.

Stonewall is not all that bad and quite usefull if ur skilled at using it, but definitly not for the lesser skilled. While on raids your dps will not matter and so mobility is mroe important (as there are more people wacking there mob, there will be more push). Stonewall really shines in exp groups, especially those in tight quaters such as ldons.

In ldons the group is going to be close together so u dont have to go far if you lose agro, to get the mob back. In high difficulty 65 Ldons (especially when the healer is less experienced of has less manapool, etc.. and especially when were running out of time) i chain cast stonewall, beacause the healers have to heal less and i still do the same damage, which actually counts in a single group situation, also u will usually kill a good number of mobs before your group moves up each time, allowing you to burn a stonwall, then move and by the time ur setup again and ready to kill more ur only a min or two from being able to use stonewall again.

Some mob repositioning is possible with stonewall, instead of walking straight back, you can walk at an angel backwards and this is the same speed as walking forward while snared, with a little practice u can walk side to side and angled backwards so that while u dont walk straight back u can still backup.
