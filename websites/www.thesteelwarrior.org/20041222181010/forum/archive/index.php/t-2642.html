We all know the evils of mashing the taunt button (I hope). It has been brought up that a possible exception to this is when tanking in a raid situation, where it may be possible for a person to have more hate than you and not have agro because of proximity, and since you can’t know exactly when this is happening, mashing my be the best way to piggy back up in agro.

After reading a few enchanter posts, I realized that there could be another situation in which it is helpful, when using a charmed pet in the group. Enchanters have noted that if they send in a hasted charmed pet, one that is doing massive dps, they really don’t have agro problems regardless of tank class, because the pet is actually keeping agro on the tank. The pet has generated a huge amount of agro from doing major dps, but the mob still hits the warrior, because a mob will always hit an NPC over a charmed pet if one is in melee range. Of course, if charm breaks, then the agro from the charmed pet poofs, and the mobs may start going after debuffers and damage dealers, unless the tank taunts. When he taunts, he may be able to piggyback on the pet’s agro, and prevent the mob (not the charmed one) from going after others if charm breaks. In this case, mashing taunt may very well be advantageous.

A fairly specialized situation, to be sure, but thought I would bring it up. I found the idea of using charmed pets to help with agro very interesting.

I've said it before, about 3-4 times already, and I'll say it again.

Beastlords are your friends!

That technique doesn't just work on charmed pets, it works on ALL pets. Pets cannot be MT while any PC is in range. Pets hold their own spot on the hate list, meaning they can be above you on the list, but as long as the pet's owner isn't in range, it'll attack you instead.

Why beastlords, you ask? Why not necro or mage pets?

Two reasons:

1) Beastlord pets are designed to hold aggro. BSTs get a line of procs which not only do damage, but add hate as well. The higher versions (51+) have a stun as well. The reason this technique is also noticed on charmed pets is because charmed pets do a lot of damage, which gets aggro. Beastlord pets proc...a lot. Spirit of the Storm, the 53 pet proc, has a 200 hate increase, and a 1.5 sec stun. It goes off...a lot.

I group with a chanter, and she dies fast on a resisted slow. She learned that after seeing two procs, it's pretty much safe to slow, because even if the warrior can't hold aggro, the pet will.

2) Beastlords can slow, and guess where they'll most likely be while they're slowing. Yup, right next to you, or at least in range, because they're going to be attacking as well. This is a personal annoyance of mine, but it's much easier to regain aggro if you're not chasing it halfway across the zone to the slower. They can also live longer on a resist, thanks to their mini-stonestance disc they get at 55.

Pets are your friends, remember that...just make sure the owner isn't sitting when the pet first attacks.

No, not just when it is only you and the pet. The pet is going to out aggro just about EVERYONE, so tauting everytime it refreshs is going to only add to your hate every time. In fact, it is MORE valuable if you have other melees, since casters are really only in danger of getting aggro when charm breaks, but melees will get aggro anytime they outaggro the tank. Here, I'll illustrate

Warrior: 500 hate
Rogue: 400 hate
Wizard: 400 hate
Pet: 1000 hate

Warrior has aggro, then rogue BSes
Warrior: 600 hate
Rogue: 700 hate
Wizard: 400 hate
Pet: 1500 hate

Rogue gets aggro, warrior taunts, wizard nukes
Warrior: 1501 hate
Rogue: 800 hate
Wizard: 800 hate
Pet: 1600 hate

Wizard goes on a nuking spree
Warrior: 1700 hate
Rogue: 1200 hate
Wizard: 1800 hate
Pet: 2500 hate
Now at this point you see that technically, the wizard has the most hate of any PC, but the warrior keeps aggro because the pet has the most hate, but the mob will always attack a PC before a pet. Mashing taunt will help insure that the mob never turns on the rogue, and that if charm breaks, the mob will stay on the tank.
