Shamans are actually really durable. I needed an ogre shaman to imbue some jades for me, and talking to people isn't my strong suit, so i PL'd one to 29. Beornegar
In good AC gear (totemic or better), your shaman will actually be a half decent tank until about lvl 24 or so. After that you'll want to start trading AC gear for wisdom gear, because your fighting style solo will be root/dot/run/re-root, and your role in groups will require you to get as much wis/mana/regen as possible to keep up with buffs/slows/debuffs/dots depending on what your role in the group is (Shamen are extraordinarily versatile).

Actually, you can effectively tank up to low 50s in old-world zones. Properly equipped (which can actually be done fairly cheaply now), a shaman's a slow tank, but still a tank. My shaman's been played both ways (wisdom and root'n'rot, and AC/HP/In-the-face) and HP/AC ended up being the route with less down time and more fun.

Fun > all, ya know. There are a lot of shaman who swear by their GEBs and WIS gear. Whatever works best for them.

Think of it like this: If we warriors can tank it unbuffed and unaided, so can a shaman once it's slowed. Shaman get as much use out of HP and AC items as we do. I just wish STA meant as many hp to them as it does to us (roughly 3hp/STA). I still tank a lot, particularly if there's a gimp ST in the group. Frequently, my dog and I can have the critter near dead when the rest of the party offs theirs.

The Shaman's Crucible (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/) should be able to help -- if you can ever get shaman to agree on anything!

You can also join serverwide.crucible:crucible if you have any questions in-game.
