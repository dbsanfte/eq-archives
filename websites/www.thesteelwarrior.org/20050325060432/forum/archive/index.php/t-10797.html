arms/fury builds to 60 are fine. once you start raiding a prot-heavy build makes sense. 5 in shield spec, 5 in toughness, and 3 in imp shield block will give you a lot of tanking power - anything after that is nice but not crucial.

I will say that after having concussion blow for a week I am not sure if I want to give it up for anything.

re: dual wield

you get a sort of mainhand bonus or an offhand penalty, whichever way you want to look at it. your offhand damage will be the DPS of your weapon plus not much more. your miss rate will also increase, but generally your rage generation will increase as well. I prefer 2h for pure damage but everything is situational. for some people DW makes a lot of sense.

as for hotkeys I keep stance-specific skills in the first bar of each stance. skills like shout, rend, hamstring, and so on are in my second bar.

With a hybrid build, you lose out on roughly 1% mitigation, some protection hate abilities, and stuns (Which are nonfactor for me.) It's not really half assing it as much as you think, and here is why:

The major tanking difference between the hybrid and full arms/fury build comes from heavy armor plate. The very good heavy armor plate (Which every warrior should strive to get for heavy tanking), is typically including good amounts of + defense on it. Sadly, +defense gear is conterproductive to one of furies best skills, enrage. So in order to enjoy the trees full benefit, you have to pass up on an easy 600+ armor, in addition to what the toughness provides.

The hybrid build can retain a good portion of it's offense, while gaining the majority of the benefits of the protection tree armorwise without being counter productive.
