Alchork
An opinion:

SOE isn’t stupid – they realize there is a problem with melee balancing just like many from each class does. However with the current game engine, there isn’t much they can do about it. Spells are significantly easier to add than melee skills so all the classes with a spell book continue to advance. All the major melee improvements over the last few expansions have come from better itemization and procs – things easily added to the game.

The true core of the melee problem cannot be fixed with the current engine. They’ve announced that they are working on a new one, and I’ve heard rumors that it will be released around Christmas. I am positive they are fixing/designing the system to handle the melee balancing as well as planning for the future. However SOE is a business and they will do things on their schedule to optimize earnings.

While many other games have been dubbed “EQ-killers” in the past, they have not lived up to their hype and have had problems such as lack of real content or severe buggy code. SOE (logically) has timed their updates/expansions to coincide with the release of the newer games – always giving people a reason to come back/stay.

There is nothing coming out in the next month or two that will draw and significant portion of the player base away – there is no major financial reason to fix anything right now. Many people I know are still playing EQ due to nothing else being out there and the major time investment they’ve made in their character. Horizons is a few months out and WoW is coming mid next year. The next big game that is receiving all the hype is WoW, and unless Blizzard screws up it is in position to be the next EQ-killer.

SOE sees all the excitement on the message boards about WoW – and while the game possibly might not live up to its expectation as the greatest ever - as a company SOE would be ignorant not to plan for its coming and do whatever they can to retain their customers. EQ has an advantage over any new game as it is difficult to leave your character behind for a new one in an unknown game – because of this time investment, SOE can even release buggy/incomplete code and people will still continue to play.

The new engine will come out with some melee balancing and perhaps some perk for casters like being able to see mana as a number. This will be timed to combat Horizons’ release mainly to keep the player base happily paying/playin. The next expansion will be timed with WoW (unless it takes longer than expected) and will contain some significant advancements in skills – perhaps new melee techniques or more caster spell gems – all to make the game fresh and exciting. The customer will then stay with EQ and SOE continues to rake in the cash.

I’ve seen melee balancing threads on almost every class forum. I really don’t think there is much more that people can say about it anymore. It all continues to be the same arguments presented another way. Nothing is going to change in the short term – and while I’ll sympathize with the ones currently on the short end of the stick, getting upset about melee balancing isn’t going to do much of anything - the EQ community as a whole sees the problem. SOE sees the problem and they are fixing it - however it takes time to create the new engine and it won’t be released until marketing needs it to be. At the moment there is nothing else out there to steal their business.
