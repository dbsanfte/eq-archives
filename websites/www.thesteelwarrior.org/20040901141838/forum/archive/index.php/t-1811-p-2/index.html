First let's define aggro. Aggro is a trait of an NPC which concerns which PC that NPC currently wants to turn into a pile of goo. Since it is part of a computer program, it clearly is determined by a mathematical formula.
Aggro = Hate + HateBonus

This is the source (I believe) for the common opinion of how Taunt works. In brief, Taunt:
1) Is a triggered skill that can fail/fizzle
2) Assigns the PC an amount of hate to the targeted NPC dependent on the status of the NPC's overall hate
The important points are that Taunt gives no hate bonus, and does not effect hate bonus in any way. The amount of hate given relates to both points. Point one says it gives either zero hate, or an amount determined by point two. Point two says that you receive enough hate to match the highest hate that the NPC has plus "just a little bit more."
This is in line with the developer's comments. This is also in line with the testing I have seen, though not the flawed observations made from said testing. The observations failed to consider the hate bonus.

Each time an NPC attacks, and only then, it will decide who to unleash that attack on. This is what we call aggro. Aggro will not change between attack rounds, which spell casting also counts as. At the begginning of each attack round, the NPC analyzes each PC that is on it's hate list. It adds the hate, and the hate bonus for each PC, and whichever PC has the highest total is the PC that "has aggro" and receives this round of punishment.

About a year ago we did extensive testing of taunt. This is what we discovered.

Taunt matches your hate to the highest person on the list plus x amount.

The mob will only switch targets if your hate exceeds the highest person on the list + y amount.

X < Y

This means that taunting alone is never enough for a mob to switch targets everything else being equal.

I assume that there is another factor that is causing the mob to switch targets on the magic 4 taunt number. Most likely this is proximity.

Effective hate = personal hate / 1+(distance/z)

Effective hate changes with proximity, but personal hate stays constant no matter where you are.

This means that the further away you are the less likely you are to pull aggro.
This also means that if you build alot of hate, and move close to a mob... you can pull aggro.

However, the mob doesn't bounce around constantly with every hit because you have to exceed the current targets hate by a certain amount to pull aggro.

I wish I could find my post from a year ago where I went into every specific situation that we tested to prove all this.

Our tests involved a cleric, ranger, wizard, shaman and warrior.

A quick test you can do for yourself is get a snaring class.

Have them snare a mob once, then have the warrior taunt once. The mob will keep attacking the person who snared, until they move out of melee range, where the warrior will get attacked.

Now try the same situation and have the warrior taunt 20 times. See how much damage the wizard takes to pull aggro.... multiple taunts don't do anything to add aggro. This is why if you have aggro you should never taunt it does nothing.

Save taunt for when you need it. Those 6 seconds can be life or death for your shaman/cleric/enchanter if you have wasted taunt at a bad time.
