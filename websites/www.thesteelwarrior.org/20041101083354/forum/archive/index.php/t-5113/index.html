Yep, I certainly have several different weapons for different roles. I have a pair of dual wield agro weapons, a 2H agro weapon, a DPS 2H weapon, a slow weapon, a snare weapon, and a lifetap weapon.

For determining what weapon is better, I go by if it improves my current weapon setup for a specific role. Since agro is the hardest to quantify, I look at both the agro potential of the damage/delay, as well as the agro potential of the proc. For example, if I was looking at a 11/20 weapon with no proc vs. a 12/22 weapon with a debuff proc, I'd choose the proc weapon for agro. For most of my weapons, stats are an added bonus, but not necessarily the main reason I choose one over another. It's not an exact science though, and personal judgement comes into play.

The best thing I think you can do in your early levels is to work on all of your weapon skills, 2HS, 1HS, 1HB, 2HB, and piercing. At earlier levels I don't think procs are much of an option. I remember the first aggro weapon I got was a blackhand berserkers axe, which procs pique which is a great aggro proc. Lots of people say at earlier levels venomous axe of the velium brood is great for aggro procs and is fairly inexpensive, although either of those weapons will be out of reach for you for a little while. For now I would get the fastest weapons you can find, of course ratio is also important, but at your level faster weapons will equal faster skill raises and better aggro. Go with the 2HS or 2HB with no proc when you don't want aggro. My point I guess is to get a weapon of every type.... or at least of say 3 types.

As to specifically address your questions, yes I carry different weapons for different roles, as listed above. There are a great variety of weapons with a great variety of different procs and ratios for different situations.

I would say the most important aspect of a weapon depends on what you want to use it for. If for aggro, then you want speed and you want a proc that draws aggro (hate, stun, dd, slow, snare, dot, etc). If for just dps without aggro you want ratio, and preferably a 2H. It just depends. Stats are important, but it is a weapon after all, and the ratio and procs are more important than stats. As you get better weapons they generally tend to get better stats, but I personally would not choose a weapon with stats over a weapon with a better ratio.
