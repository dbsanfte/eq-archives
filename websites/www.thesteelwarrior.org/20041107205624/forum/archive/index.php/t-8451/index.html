Fitemore
There is a strong, unfair bias against knights. My old guild *frequently* used knights to tank trash mobs on raids in the EPs. Knights can't tank the boss mob; they can tank everything else. Remember: KNIGHTS HAVEN'T BEEN NERFED. Warriors were upgraded. It's a very, very different thing... the PoP content hasn't changed.

Knights can use spells to hold aggro, and to get initial aggro on incoming, much more than warriors can. In addition, they do have spells. Warriors tank better but as a tradeoff have less options. The degrees of these tradeoffs is up to debate, naturally.

Summation: If you want to tank *everything*, be a warrior. If you want to tank but have other options, be a knight.

Voltana
I have a fairly well equiped 65 SK. I >>LOVE<< playing him. BUT, lately I have found that the content that the people I hang with want to do he can no longer handle effectively. He has all his defensive AA's and could only upgrade his equipment by raiding in the EP's which he is not flagged for. So I switched to a Warrior I had access to. She is 65. Has slightly better equipment but only 1/3 of the AA's the SK has. She still has 600 more HP than the SK without having any of the +HP AA's. With aggro weapons and augs she can hold aggro almost as well as the SK could (although it IS dependent on the roll of the dice on those procs) but even in her current AA-lacking state can seriously out-tank the SK.

If you are looking towards being main tank on 65+ content the Warrior excels and the SK falls a bit short. If you just want to be a melee class that is versatile and fun to play pre-65 content go with a Pally.

Knowing what I know now, as much as I love playing the SK I don't think I can recommend that anyone start one due to their lack of value in the high end game content. If you are going to spend the time and effort to level a character to 65+ you would want to be useful and needed until you no longer wanted to play anymore. SK's seem to top out much sooner than Pally's and Warriors remain viable right to the end of the game.

Something else to consider though, Knights are much more fun to play until they reach content they can't effectively tank anymore. For me, the War didn't really hit his/her stride and become fun until I started doing stuff the SK couldn't do as well.

As always, your mileage may vary. :)
