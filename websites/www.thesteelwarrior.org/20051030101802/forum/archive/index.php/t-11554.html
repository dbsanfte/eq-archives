It all depends on Seal of Command, which procs at a set rate that ignores weapon speed. It procs an instant attack chance on hit that does Holy damage, but is like hitting twice. Slower weapons proc at a higher rate per attack because its based on procs per miinute instead of percentage per attack.

The problem with this build is luck. A paladin has to get lucky with the procs and crits, where as a MS warrior can MS consistantly and just needs crit luck. So a Paly built like this might all of a sudden pump out tons of damage at once, like weapon attack crit and a SoC proc and crit in the same way a warrior might crit an attack plus MS. This particular build is difficult to duel, because they have enough healing in one fight to keep up with the 3-4 lives and do comparable damage to you as a warrior. Since you will both be trying to stay in melee range, chances are SoC is gong to proc on you. Their weakness come out in open PvP though, as a lack of mana means this build is useless healing anyone but themselves, they still cannot catch anything, and when they do get close, they need luck on their side for the proc.
