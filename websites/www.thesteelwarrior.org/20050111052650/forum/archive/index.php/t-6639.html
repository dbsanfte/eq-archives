There are far more issues here than people are posting.

Point 1. A wipe will hurt your guild morale and make people want to log. Not using your best tank on something borderline(tier 5 time) or fairly new (ikkinz uqua) is idiotic.

Point 2. There are many many mobs to have lesser tanks practice on, for example any elemental mobs + tier 1-4 times. Instant 3 mins of risk free fight.

Point 3. Your tank is the most important person on your raids. A good mt can make your raid a breeze, and lack of can make your raid a clusterfuck nightmare from hell. A good warrior has his buffs set, has control of the situation, knows the encounter + aes + cures he will need during it. He knows the capacity of his rot + patch healers, if he needs vie + spellshield + clarena. He knows what buffs to drop if you need an emergency quiescense chain during a fight. He sees when di goes off and immediately calls for a new, using furious to fill in the skip slot in the rot if needed. He calls aes, knows ranges, knows if ae heals will be needed or not, knows the mana requirements of the rotation given a certain fight time on a given mob. Knowing what mobs to off tank, what is mezzable, what is immune, what mobs your chanters are going to target first, how much they hit for, who are they going to target first, and how fast will it take you to gain aggro off them, will you have to shield to save them if they dont get heals spammed.

Point 4. A tank going afk and doing the minimum is a piss poor player. People i want in my guild are people who recognize opportunities to rescue raids from near wipes. For example a 4th or 5th warrior who ae taunts and furious's during a tank switch to help save the second tank while rot gets on him, or a tank who ae taunts and fortitudes to eat adds on a mob such as keeper of the altar. A good warrior has a handle on his gear, he upgrades weaker items first, but has an eye for an important item, such as something with shielding or spellshield that is only a slight upgrade, but may make a world of difference on a certain mob.

There are many other things that make a good warrior, but those are the basics and its far far away from hitting autoattack and taunt. A good warrior has the capacity to make everyones life 100 times easier.

It all depends. There are times when I could see issues when how Raid Leaders designate tanks mainly because of misinformation and misunderstanding the value of HP's and AC - Such as if a raid leader soley looks at HP's and not AC(and sometimes this does happen) in determining a line up. This can work in pre-pop where the caps are reached but beyond that the value of AC cannot be underestimated. Exampl: I can have 200 more AC than another warrior but be side stepped because the other warrior has 100 more hps. Ofcourse not all raid leaders do this.

That said I do think skill has merit in the entire scheme of things. Quick response rate to raid contingencies, knowing what to do when things do go wrong, using the appropriate discipline for the encounter, whether to use fortitude or furious, evasive or defensive, altering equipment for more AC or more HP, how and when to break mez, etc.. --- are all factors of a skilled warrior vs. a non-skilled warrior. All this assumes ofcourse that the tanks have equally stable ISP's etc(if you have a 12hp/2100AC buffed tank that goes LD every 5 min then that probably isn't going to be your MT even if they have the best AC/HP set up).

I do believe a melee class is probably more suited to do a line up than someone who has no experience in melee matters(atleast on difficult encounters) -- though sometimes this doesn't always happen.

I personally would choose a more skilled warrior with inferior equipment over a less skilled warrior up to a point. If the difference between the two is great then equipment will win out unless the better equipped warrior is a complete moron(which can happen). Having experience with the two warriors in the past also plays a huge part in the decision process. If you consistently find the less equipped warrior performing better than the more equipped warrior then it's either skill separating them or a case of the plastic magelo syndrome(The better equiped warrior may not in fact have the stated AC/HP values or all those wonderful augments etc.)
