You know, I figured that since the melee damage and shield blocking had been on the site for at least 6 months now, and the new expansion is out of the way, I had kinda assumed that they would actually have started to look at these things.

While looking more closely at the upcoming updates and future plans sections of the EQ site, I'm come to realize something.

It's been six months, and they haven't even *STARTED* the development cycle for these fixes yet.

On the upcoming updates section:

Here are some things we're working on for the November update: (Please note: my producer is making me tell you that in addition to this list, we'll still be bug fixing and maintaining the live game and that, while we hope to get each of these features in, nothing is certain. But this is what we're working on at the present time.)

Self explanitory. This is what they're working on. The fact that they're not sure if they can get all the features in shows that that's a high load list... there's no dev time left to look over anything else

We're still planning to take a look at improving the damage capabilities of melee classes, as they've fallen behind their magic-using counterparts at the very high end of the game.

We're planning on reviewing the possibility of giving an innate chance to block when using a shield. We've been hoping to do this for a while, and it should be getting a realistic review in the near future.

In other words: We haven't started the development cycle on these items yet. We're too busy with the Upcoming Updates lists to spare any time for the Future Plans. We'll get around to this eventually... maybe... but don't hold your breath.

It has become clear to me that all the Future Plans list is, is another one of those carrots. It's something to keep the player's interest and hopes up about what MAY happen. Not only that, but these plans that they have may just disappear with no explanation or reason, such as the upgrades to the drops in the lower tier planes, and the upgrades to the lower tier god's drops... that plan quietly disappeared, and I don't seem to recall ANY upgraded drops in the Planes that they stated. To top it all off, these plans rarely reflect actual priorities or plans for the game. For example: how many items on the Upcoming Ipdates list actually appeared on the Future Plans list at any time? The targeting, /consider, newbie help, they obviously have had plans to put these things in, why was it sprung on us instead of being put in the Future Plans list before they started the development cycle to get it in in an Upcoming Update?

So, to sum up:

Future Plans is only a carrot... a ploy to keep players hopeful about needed changes to the game. These Plans do not reflect their priority on the development cycle, and in fact may very well be the lowest priority of anything they're working on. Not only that, stated plans may disappear without an explanation or reason given, and items that are changed in the game rarely appear in it... items such as making the game more friendly for newbies, or targeting and /con changes don't even show up on the Future Plans list at any time... they usually go straight to Upcoming Updates, so they are extremely unreliable as to determine what actually is going to happen in the game.

Future Plans is just another example of poor communication with the customers, and something that instills false hope. It HAD a lot of promise when it was implemented, but it's shown to be just another waste of time, especially since they can't be bothered to update it with ANY new information, they just copy and paste it.

I have NEVER seen another company with worse customer service than SoE.

I find it rather silly how blind many people are. Look around, melee tweaking isn't being put off. It's already happening. Just go to the bazaar to see it. Do a search for piercers, see how many are currently for sale that come from the likes of Veksar, the revamped Droga. While you're there, look at a few of the new weapons designed for warriors as well. Then stroll over to an LDoN vendor. More rogue piercers with backstab modifiers. And anger and yak procs for warriors, procs you can put on weapons that already have procs.

Monks are still a bit left out, of course. I'm thinking that's because SOE isn't quite sure what to do about them yet...nerf pacify/FM, make monks better than those abilities, or redefine the role of monks?

Of course, people also like to talk about utility. Anything powerful here isn't going to happen. So this will really just be eyecandy. Like if they put disciplines on seperate timers. It won't be a huge power increase. It'll even include a nerf or two (evasive and defensive would get longer reuse timers, guarenteed, to balance out the fact that you can use both). But it would also free us up to use things like furious more freely or mighty strike for the fun of it. Not huge power increases, but definately useful sometimes, fun at others. Would be good changes, but they'd be minor in the overall scheme of things.

Then people have zaney ideas, ones that aren't thought out. Like the idea that warriors should have more hp. That just leads to mobs that hit harder. Which means caster that get hit die faster. Which means casters don't want to get hit. And that means...knights. It's self defeating. The only way warriors come out ahead that way is if warriors have more hp and so do the casters...basically everyone but the knights. And that's better done by just nerfing the high end knight only stuff a bit, rather than mudflating most everyone. Shields are another example. Warriors don't have an issue being the best tank. They are that already. So even if a shield blocks, big deal. You still lose aggro over it, which is something warriors don't like. Unless of course knights can do it also, in which case as Phantron suggests, it'll just make knight tanks more appealling. Now they can tank more mobs. Added to that, if it's useful outside of raids, it has the same issue as more hp. Warriors tank better, mobs hit harder because of it, non-tanks die faster if they get hit, non-tanks want to not get hit. Unless, of course, non-tanks can also benefit from shields, to keep them in balance with how they are currently to warriors. Which winds up being about the same as nerfing knights, while taking a chunk out of warrior dps at the same time. Still self defeating.

Anyway, except for changes to help monks, I'd expect more of the same, some itemization changes. And maybe, if we're lucky, some eye-candy that won't really change much but might be fun. And why would that be the case? Because classes are not that out of balance. That doesn't mean no changes. It doesn't mean there shouldn't be changes. It just means don't expect anything major or rushed.
