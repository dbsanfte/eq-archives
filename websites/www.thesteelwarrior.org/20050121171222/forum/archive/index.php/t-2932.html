My observations from our 2 LDoN adventure runs last night:

We started with a normal difficulty Mistmoore adventure with a group of all 65s (warrior, cleric, druid, bard, enchanter, ranger) who are all well equipped, but mostly below elemental equipment level. We got an assassination assignment;it was a piece of cake. Nothing hit too hard (300-400s max), and pulls were quite easy. On the boss, the bard and I went LD as soon as we pulled, and the cleric died immediately thereafter, and the ench, druid, ranger still won without too much trouble.

Later that night, we got cocky and decided to try a hard difficulty Takish-Hiz adventure. This time we had all of the same people, except a 65 monk instead of the 65 ranger. We ended up with a collection quest and needed to find 28 statues. Umm, wow; this was a _lot_ harder. Almost everything hit for 900ish. 2-3 pulls were not uncommon and pretty tough to deal with. Resists weren't too bad, but the main problem we had was a lack of dps (and that's not really a bad dps group). We wiped once, and partially wiped one or two other times. We ran back each time and eventually ran out of time with about 17/28 items found. It appears there are a few enchanter charmable mobs (some of the Va'Dyn style golems) if you're willing to deal with a pissed off ex-pet hitting your enchanter for 900. We killed one of them very early on, and then later regretted it as we really needed the dps. Once we found another, it was too little, too late. Certainly not something I'm going to want to do all the time, but it's great to have a hard challenge available when you're feeling cocky. ;)
