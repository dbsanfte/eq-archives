This command gives you the ability to act as a shield to your target. You get to absorb some damage intended for the person you are shielding. Friedrick
06-30-2003, 04:10 PM
To activate it you type in the following

/shield playername

You have to be fairly close to the player you are shielding at all times. When you shield the player the following happens.

Playername gets hit from the mob for 50% of the normal damage the mob would do to them.

You get hit from the mob for 75% of the normal damage the mob would do to Playername.

The mob does 125% of the normal damage with it being split between you and the player you are shielding.

This can give a player enough time to get healed or you enough time to regain aggro before the other player dies.

I have been experimenting with it in Plane of Nightmare and first impressions are that it makes the mob mad at you (although it could be coincidence that it turned back to me at that time small number of samples). A group of RL friends and I have been trying to see if a lvl 46 shammy could slow in PON so my shield key has gotten some use. So far it seems to be fairly useful in emergencies but it has a short duration. Fortunately, it has a short refresh time though as well.

Another thing to note is that you cannot use /shield or be the target of a /shield if you are using a discipline.

I always have a shield hotkey made in exp groups with the enchanter's name on it. If they draw aggro from a slow, I taunt. If the taunt fails, I /shield them. I haven't lost a 'chanter in an experience group for a long time.
