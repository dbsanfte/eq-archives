Sangiovese
As mentioned, mana sieve the gaters... and also have your nukers ready to nuke hard right at the end. Sudden death is a guaranteed way to stop a gate :)

One thing is that you have to make sure people are assisting and on the same mob when you have multiples in camp. If 1/3 of the raid is attacking the non-gating mob (that should only have an offtank on it) then the lowered DPS on the main mob (the gater) will give it more of a chance to successfully gate.

In my opinion, HoT is where you learn to raid. You have to deal with gaters, some decent DPS output, adds, and AEs. And like you mentioned, you'll also learn to recover from a wipe. The ability to do a quick CR and get back to raiding (rather than have a wipe end the night) is an important element of raiding.

Congrats on moving in to HoT. Before you know it you'll be trying your hand at flurry drakes in North. :)

Anulien
In addition to gating mobs, another tactic used is keeping them somewhat close to their spawn point. HoT is very easy to pull and position mobs. Many times things can even be engaged where they stand as your healers hide around a nearby corner. If a mob does not travel far from its spawn it will not gate. Somewhat tricky if you get white wurm spawns at the entrance, but if you position them against the wall at the top of the stairs they will not gate. The downside of the positioning at the entrance is if you are too close to the stairs you will get wandering adds, if you are too far then there is a chance the mob will try and gate.

Also, white wurms cast cold based AE. Red cast fire based ae. Some white wurms will try and heal and sometimes strip buffs from a single target. All white wurms will try and gate. All red wurms will enrage.
