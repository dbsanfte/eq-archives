Here's some data from a parse setup I did a few months back on the rogue forum, sans the backstab data.

Using a Gold runed mithril knife (16-19 +1 fire) primary and Sharp gem formation (16-21) secondary, 1306 atk, no buffs, 41pct worn haste, lvl 61 parse mob:
Duration 11,433 seconds
Total pierces 14,828
Total damage 843,735
Average pierce 56.90 damage
Pierce dps 73.79

Same weapons and setup except with 1561 attack (255 more attack than setup 1):
Duration 33,084 seconds
Total pierces 42,907
Total damage 2,869,008
Average pierce 66.86
Pierce dps 86.71

17.51% increase in average pierce
17.51% increase in pierce dps

So with those numbers, every 14 attack = 1% dps increase on this particular mob.

It depends on the expansion. In PoP, the diminishing returns is beyond 1900 attack, at least for a rogue. Most GoD stuff hits too hard with too few hp so there's not much available for sustained parsing.

I've never bought in on the idea of just potential damage // aggro. I see it as a combination of current damage along with potential damage, plus any +hate or -hate procs. When I'm doing an LDoN and a tank pulls 3-4 mobs and they'll all on him, I'll lead off the fight with Kyv Strike most of the times - since it's a 100% guaranteed backstab to land, usually 1 or 2 mobs will turn and start hitting on me since he hasn't built up aggro on them. I've tested the same thing w/o using a disc and when I've whiffed on a backstab, the same mobs won't turn. If the potential damage was the same as actual damage, this situation would have the mobs on me both times. So either it's a combination of both or the potential damage for aggro theory doesn't fly.
