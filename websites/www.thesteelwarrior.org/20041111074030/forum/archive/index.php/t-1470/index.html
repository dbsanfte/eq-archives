Mitch Megablond
Where to start…well I think all players of pure melee classes (monks rogues and warriors) would agree that we are currently unbalanced compared to the other classes, we have frankly fallen behind in every area that counts, namely:

DPS
Utility
Desireability
Raid functionality

This gradual erosion began in Luclin with the advent of FT items and horses and poor itemisation and has continued at a rapid pace since.

After a lot of thought on this matter I have come up with some proposals which I would like the communities feedback on, and though this i'm a monk I’d welcome warrior feedback as well.

Some of my points pertain exclusively to monks and for that I apologise but I do not have the depth of experience of warriors with which to fully address all the genuine concerns I know they have. Most of what I will address does concern all melees equally though.

The areas I have identified as being of most concern are:

Itemisation
Buffs
Utility
Disciplines

First I’d like to look at itemisation, it has long been prevalent to have multiple classes able to use the same items, lately these have tended to be split into their sub-classes of mnk/bas or pall/sk or into slightly wider ones of perhaps warr/ran/pall/sk/bard/rog. There are also of course the all/all items (more on them later).

The difficulty with this system is that classes in EQ are measured in 2 ways if melee, HP and ATT (AC is mainly just for bragging rights unless insanely high). Now the caster classes are measured in not 2 but 4 ways, HP, DPS (as melee) but also in FT (mana regen) and Mana (mana pool size).

This system means that when we see an item which has HP, Mana and perhaps FT and a spell focus as well that the casting classes receive a –double benefit- from the same item as opposed to a pure melee. Think of this using the following analogy, a Wizard ad a Warrior both go do the same job for the same amount of time but when it comes to collect their pay the Wizard gets double the money of the warrior.

This double benefit to casters is clearly unbalanced but there is a simple way to address it.

Firstly All/All items should be replaced with items that have generic stats for HP, Mana and show the effects that would apply to the class using it, let me illustrate with an example:

Staff of Balance
Primary secondary
35/30
+5 to all resists
Melee HP 150 Mana 0
Caster HP 50 Mana 100
Hybrid HP 100 Mana 50
AC 15 Str+10 Int+10 Wis+10
Melee : Vengence V
Caster: FT 3
Hybrid: Vengence II & FT 1
Classes: All/All

As you can see the item delivers the same total benefit to each of the classes that can use it but does not reward one class in more ways than another, hybrids (bards, pall, SK, Rng) get a mix of the 2 but still only receive the same total number of HP/Mana that the item has to offer.

As far as making this work there are 2 options, either the engine identifies the class who wields it and adjusts accordingly or, and probably more simply, each zone has a NPC that you can hand the item to and it will cycle through the various types until you find the one for your class.

At a stroke this mechanism would remove a large part of the melee caster imbalance that exists in the game today. To apply this retrospectively to all items is probably too large a job so lets be realistic and say apply it to all PoP zones and future expansions plus certain other high end items as SOE sees fit (Emp Loot, Seru Loot , VT loot etc).



The next part of my melee fix involves buffs, specifically HP buffs. I’m going to use as an example a made up buff as below:

Buffage
Target receives 1000 HP
Allowable targets : all over 46th level


Now this buff looks innocuous enough, a basic example version of HoV or Virtue perhaps but its exactly this type of buff that has crept, insidously into the EQ psyche and caused such damage because it weakens the advantages of melee over casters in most situations. You see a melee should always have more HP (assuming same level of play) than a caster and it should be a good sized margin, frankly there is no way that a similarly equipped druid or cleric should be within a couple of hundred HP of a fully buffed rogue or monk, it just shouldn’t happen that way because high HP are one of the advantages of such classes when compared to a pure caster.

Lets use some more examples to illustrate this point: (all examples at 65th and only approximate)

Unbuffed HP

Warrior 7297 (example uses 250th placed warrior on EQ rankings)
Palladin 5970 (example uses 250th placed palladin on EQ rankings)
Rogue 5216 (example uses 250th placed rogue on EQ rankings)
Monk 5289 (example uses 250th placed monk on EQ rankings)
Druid 4165 (example uses 250th placed druid on EQ rankings)
Wizard 3449 (example uses 250th placed wizard on EQ rankings)

Fully raid buffed HP (assumes 2500hp of buffs)

Warrior 9797
Palladin 8470
Rogue 7716
Monk 7789
Druid 6665
Wizard 5949


Unbuffed warrior is our baseline for comparision
Unbuffed the Palladin has 81.81% of the HP of the warrior
Unbuffed the Rogue has 71.48% of the HP of the warrior
Unbuffed the Monk has 72.48% of the HP of the warrior
Unbuffed the Druid has 57.08% of the HP of the warrior
Unbuffed the Wizard has 42.27% of the HP of the warrior

All those ratios seem fairly inline with what you would expect but now lets look at the stats buffed:
buffed warrior is our baseline for comparision
buffed the Palladin has 86.46% of the HP of the warrior
buffed the Rogue has 78.76% of the HP of the warrior
buffed the Monk has 79.50% of the HP of the warrior
buffed the Druid has 68.03% of the HP of the warrior
buffed the Wizard has 60.72% of the HP of the warrior

As you can see the buffs have dramatically altered the ratios in favour of pure casters whilst making only very small changes to the palladin monk and rogue. To be specific the following increases have happened:

Palladin % HP increase 41.88
Rogue % HP increase 47.93
Monk % HP increase 47.27
Druid % HP increase 60.02
Wizard % HP increase 72.48

The above figures clearly show the huge imbalance happening here and remember that the higher the amount of HP buffs you have the greater the imbalance becomes. These type of generic buffs eat away at one of the primary advantages of the melee classes, namely their HP advantage.

There is a simple and elegant way to resolve this, buffs need to add HP based on class, something like this:

Warriors get 120% of Base HP given by the buff
Palladins and SK’s get 110%
Monks rogues rangers bards get 100%
Bst Clerics Shaman druids get 90%
Chanters Wiz Necro and Mage get 80%

By doing this you would maintain the class HP advantages which were intended from the very start of EQ and upon which all our most basic concepts of balance began.

To give you an idea of what this would do to the above raid buffed figures:

Fully raid buffed HP (assumes 2500hp of buffs) as things stand

Warrior 9797
Palladin 8470
Rogue 7716
Monk 7789
Druid 6665
Wizard 5949

Fully raid buffed HP with proportional buffing

Warrior 10297
Palladin 8720
Rogue 7716
Monk 7789
Druid 7539
Wizard 5449

The wizard now has 52.92% of the HP of the warrior fully buffed, still an increase over his starting point of 42.27% unbuffed but not so much as to threaten the melee advantage noticeably.





The next topic I’d like to discuss is utility for the pure melee classes (monk rogue warrior)

As things stand the pure melee has little to no utility at the endgame or before, sure we can buy potions and have the odd little clicky effect on items but basically most of the very basic most simple utility in EQ is not available to us and just like melee binding (thanks soulbinders) and no travel (thanks PoK books) its time to give the melee a few totally harmless and unbalancing bits of utility that DON’T AFFECT any of the other classes in any way at all but simply make our life that little bit easier to live with. Remember, the game is about fun, not hassle.

To this end I’ve come up with an idea to give utility to melees. All these apply only to the melee themselves, cannot be cast on others (OMG she said cast) and are subject to the normal casting times but do not have fizzle rates or mana requirements, such as the clickable spell effects we have on items.

I suggest a series of Innate abilities starting at level 58 and ending at 65 with one given each level and utilising the Spell icons to use.

These are just my first thoughts on this so look at it more as a concept that as a hard and fast proposal:

Level 58 Regeneration (lowest level version)
Level 59 Levitate (requires components, lowest level version)
Level 60 EB )requires component, lowest level version)
Level 61 Heat sight
Level 62 Bind (subject to same limitations as casters and really not a big deal since soulbinders)
Level 63 Damage shield (lowest level version)
Level 64 Invis to Undead (lowest level version)
Level 65 Invisibility (lowest level version)

All would be identical in all ways to their normal versions and be subject to a fixed rcast time of perhaps 10 minutes.


None of them impinges on any group utility of other classes as they are self only, they are also very low level so of only tiny impoact to a character of those levels but nonetheless the monks rogues and warriors out there would, I believe, love this kind of ‘cantrip’ ability and it would take away some of the small hassles we deal with every day whilst hurting no other classes in any way at all.






Lastly I’d like to discuss disciplines, frankly the melee disciplines are totally messed up, you have gems like defensive and utter crap like the monk assassination one, every discipline for pure melee needs reexamining and some (and here I focus just on monks) need trashing and replacing.

Edit: deleted monk specific stuff so as not to bore the Ogres :)


Summary

That’s pretty much all I have to say on the subject of melee balancing so feel free to discuss or disagree but take any flames elsewhere and lets have some sensible debate.
