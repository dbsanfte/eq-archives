Gundahar
As more warriors you get, as harder it's to gear them up. When you are in a common dkp guild and have to compete with other classes, you have to play more than your average guildmate who isn't depending on stuff as you are.

So how to deal with it, when you already got a bunch of them ? First build a core with the diligent ones who are raiding every day and let them do tank duties in rotation - it raises their motivation when they feel needed and they learn to replace each other. Ignore the lazy fucks with higher absence and exclude them from every task. After a while one or another of these may get disatified and hit rl. Once you shrinked your class to a reasonable size, set high requirements for new recruits, not only about stats but also on playtime. It's better to discourage someone than creating wrong expectations and end up with some casual player who is of no use and just blocks a spot in raid the days he shows up or grabs loot what would be more effective on someone else.

Being class leader for more than a year now and this method works quite well. We have 6 active warriors and one on break, all geared up with nearly similiar stats and a very high attendance. I won't add another one as long as he don't fit among his mates.
