You are right, though you don't say it, that I don't provide a context for the lastest one. Here it is: the devs speak again (http://forums.worldofwarcraft.com/thread.aspx?FN=wow-general&T=1123852&P=2) .

When fighting against mobs that do not dodge or parry, those events still actually happen, but are translated into "misses" by the UI. While there are some other subtle but important differences between humanoids with weapons and/or shields and other types of enemies (like beasts, elementals, etc), the translation of "dodge" and "parry" into "miss" is an important factor in ensuring that fighting a giant spider is relatively equivalent to fighting against a humanoid of the same level that is using a weapon/shield.

and a bit later

Another key factor players should be aware of when considering the 5% base miss chance is their position relative to the enemy. If your character is positioned in the 180 degree rear arc of the enemy, any of the times that the enemy would have dodged/parried if you were in front of them are translated into "misses" instead. This can often lead players to believe that they're getting more then their expected number of "misses", when in actuality it was a combination of misses, dodges, and parries.

So yeah, we get lied to. Past that I haven't drawn any conclusions. The tighter testing of the second sample may help.
