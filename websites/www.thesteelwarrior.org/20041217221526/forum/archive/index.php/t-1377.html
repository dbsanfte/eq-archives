c.) Finally add the /rude animation to taunt.


-- Warrior taunt is broken -- part 2: Why do I need to whisper in its ear?

How many have ever seen the broadcast 'Mob_01 is too far away to taunt' How close do we have to be? You would think melee range at first, but I have been in auto=attack and never too far away to hit, and still too far away to taunt? Why do i need to be closer to taunt than to stab with a dagger? Why can I not taunt from some range? yelling insults, rude gestures etc... There are several soloutions to this, give taunt a fixxed range, give it a scaled range according to skill level, give it a range equivalent to melee range. Any change would be better than what we have now.

To Fix this I suggest giving a scaled range to taunt -

Warrior taunt skill caps at 230. With higher skill it would be nice if we had higher range. I would suggest start taunt with a range of 5 and then scale the range up to 130 at level 65.

-- Warrior taunt is broken -- part 3: why wont this stupid sword thingee work?

Warriors are slave to the RNG for aggro generation, always have been. It is one of our huge disadvantages, previously we had only two ways to innately generate aggro, DPS and Procing weapons. Procing weapons are very streaking and extremely unreliable for hate generation. Relying on them can work great and it can also leave you waiting forever for a timely proc. DPS is also streaky, being as we are normally in front of the mob, at least when we are doing our job, we get riposted, parried, dodged on a much higher percentage than those hitting from the rear.

To fix this I suggest giving warriors innate aggro generation on attacks.

Add innate hate generation to every attack a warrior makes. EVEN misses or riposts, etc.. if resisted spells get aggro why do misses not? This innate aggro generation would be equivalent to the damage bonus on the weapons we use. this aggro+ would be added every attack, hit/miss/avoided, procs would then become a bonus and saftey feature a luxury for the elite, not a requirement for the masses. This would allow warriors to use our skills, not rely on dps which is highly situational or procs which are highly susceptable to the RNG to gain and maintain aggro.
