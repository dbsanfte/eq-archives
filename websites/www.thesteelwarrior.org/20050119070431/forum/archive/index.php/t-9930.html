I honestly don't remember what I was geared like back then, but obtaining level 70 is not all that difficult, if that is your main goal. The question should be asked whether or not you want to have a completely rounded character or not, though. You could theoretically reach level 70, and not do a single other AA, but you would then be sorely "under-skilled" compared to the majority of all other level 70 tanks out there. I'm assuming that you are not in a raiding guild, due to your low play-times, so you are going to be really limited on your gear options, since most of the "high-end" gear will be unattainable to you. You will also be limited on some higher xp zones due to flag/key issues. But OoW has some excellent areas for you to group in that would rival xp that you'd get in elementals/KT/Qvic. Your best bet will probably get a group of friends that you enjoy xp'ing with, because if you aren't looking to advance your character AA-wise, etc. your selection of groups will be limited.

So you are 64 now. You are getting to the part of the game where the long time players are running about. They won't keep a non-aggro holding tank around very long. The groups at 65+ levels looking to do exp/aa grindage are wanting a tank that holds aggro so that they can dish out the big damage. If you can't keep aggro then it's seeya when you grow up. Sometimes you think you are holding good aggro and really it's the group not willing to do major damage because you don't hold enough aggro. That means the dps classes are using small damage to make up for your lack of aggro. I know this sounds a bit harsh, but at these upper levels alot of players really generate major amounts of aggro using dps abilities and they really don't want to be the tank. That's your job if you can do it well enough. The clerics at these higher levels tend to be "super" healers so the exp groups aren't so concerned about your hitpoints as your ability to hold aggro. You might see some concern about hitpoints and ac on raids, but you have a long way(gear) to go before you reach MT for raids anyway. The most you might get is Ramp Tank or backup/off tank. If your guild is willing to gear you up(AC/HITPOINT) then by all means do your best to be the "Tank". If your just looking for groups willing to do some exp/aa then get yourself some super aggro abilities so they can kill faster and you will get invites alot. The nice thing about warriors is we can make up for AC by using our disciplines alot "evasive/defensive". The two things you really need are the aa's ID5/LR5, and the enraging blow on a weapon. That will place you in the competition for being a good exp group tank.
