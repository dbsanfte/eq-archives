I'm almost to the point of buying some Tak gear now. Murphy's law, it's the last camp I have to unlock other than Guk. Once I start getting augments, I'll pick Vengeance over regen. IMO, at least for myself, regen IS negligible, even more so than Vengeance. For myself, this is how I view it.

Take a raid situation. If you are tanking, you'll go from full HP to dead, if it happens, in at most a few seconds. Time for 1 or maybe 2 ticks of regen, at most. So I view it as you're only really getting 1-2 ticks of worth out of that regen aug. I've been purple-clubbed 2 times that I can ever remember, and about 2 weeks ago I caught a CH at 0%, where I was for probably about 3 seconds that seemed to last for a year. That's in 3 years of playing. If not tanking, such as taking AE damage while playing DPS... Usually, at least in my own exp, group heals are used in this situation. This means that the person doing the healing is going to be doing their healing time based upon the needs of the person in the group who's HP are melting away the quickest. Rarely is that the warrior in the group. We are already the best equipped to survive AE; regen just makes us that much better.

However, with added attack; that attack bonus is cumulative throughout the entire length of the fight. If you get 10 melees on a mob, and each melee has increased their DPS by anywhere from 5-20 percent *just numbers pulled out of my head, not sure how much extra attack ads to DPS*, that mob is going to be dead a lot quicker.

In a non-raid situation, such as LDoN, regen is even more useless to me. Right now, LDoN is far and away how I spend most of my EQ play time, and it's usually with the same group. As it is right now, cleric mana is never an issue, unless in the rare case of an extremely bad pull with 4 or more mobs. Hell, our cleric is our puller... not because of the needed paci or anything, but because he's bored. Extra regen won't do anything at all in this case. However, getting those mobs dead even a few seconds quicker is at least SOMETHING, minor and trivial as it is.
Khumak
The DPS increase from 10 ATK is going to depend on what weapons you're using, what your haste is, what your previous ATK was, what buffs you have, etc. So there's no way to say that any number of ATK gives some static percentage of DPS increase because it's different for everyone that doesn't have exactly the same ATK, buffs, haste, and weapons.

If my ATK was 2100 and I raise it by 10 that's going to increase my DPS by a lot less than someone who's sitting at 1200 ATK and raises his by 10.
