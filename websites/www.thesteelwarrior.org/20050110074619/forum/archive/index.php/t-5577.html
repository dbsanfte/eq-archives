Let me explain the situation as best I can. As mentioned already, warriors don't stack that well. They're DPS sure, ubt not compared to other classes, and you only need so many TANKS in a guild. Notice the word tanks is capitalised, there is a reason for that. As you know, warriors are just about the most gear-dependant class in EQ. Furthermore, in the raiding sense, warriors absolutely need large quantities of good geasr to be tanks.

Therein lies the problem. It's not that guilds don't want warriors. But, most have enough of their own, warriors which they need to gear up to make them better tanks. They don't want to add another person to that list which they will have to gear up in order to make another good tank. This is why you see the staggering requirements for warriors many guilds these days have.

On the other hand, what most guilds don't say on their recruiting page is that they are amost ALWAYS looking for tanks. By tanks, as I've mentioned before, I mean very well-equipped warriors who can step into a guild and be MA, SA, etc at least as well as their top few warriors if not better, without requiring a plethora of additional gear to do so. In other words, let's say, hypothetically, that I, with my current magelo profile, apply to an elemental guild, a guild thats maybe taken Fro, hasn't been in the planes all that long, and is still feeling out the EP's. NOw, let's further assume that they have a large amount of warriors as is and are not recruiting warriors. Do you think they will take me?

WHile this is of course a case-by-case basis, I will bet hte answer most likely will be yes because I have as much HP/AC/solid agro-generation as everyone of their warriors if not more. I can instantly step in and tank for them as effectively if not moreso than their current members. Now, this kind of analysis of course holds true for practically all classes, especially melees, but I would bet it holds moreso for warriors and maybe knights than anyone else, simply because they are so unstackable. IT's an unfortunate circumstance because getting the gear is very tough, but thats the way thing go unfortunately.

I'm the officer in charge of recruiting for my guild, which is in the elementals and doing ok so far. I'm also a warrior (duh), and am often (I'd go so far as to say 'usually') the MT for our raids. We have 7 warriors total in the guild and I plan to add one more once the right person shows up. More than that I won't recruit, simply because having 7 or 8 warriors means you will have 5 online at most of our raids, and that's enough. Adding more means more bored people, and in my experience bored people aren't having fun, make things less fun for the rest of the guild, and often end up doing stupid shit as a result of their extreme boredom. I play this game for fun, and I'm not going to recruit people that I know will be bored out of their skull and quite honestly are only showing up to earn DKP and get loot.

Similarly, we have 6 shamans and are not recruiting any more until one of them leaves the guild or the game.

We have 12 active clerics, and I figure we'll take 2 more then I'll stop recruiting those too, again because people tend to get bored doing nothing but CH rotations all the time.

The honest truth is that a guild in the elementals is in the top 30% of their server, if not more. There's another 30% of the server or so that would like to be in the elementals, and some portion of those are willing to leave their current guild to get there. The result is that guilds can pick and choose who they recruit, and if you're in a position like mine you find that your "job" for the guild is to turn people away more than it is to help them get into the guild.

There are some guilds don't mind that they hit 72 people all the time, and often you can -- with enough flags and AA -- get into them with no problem. At that point you find you're the 15th warrior on the roster and you literally have nothing to do. They not only wouldn't notice if you weren't there, other people in the guild are actively annoyed because you *are* there. They'd rather you didn't show up, actually.

That's the environment you're operating in when you want to apply to any elemental guild. I often get the "my girlfriend is a cleric and will come with me if you let me apply" line. Personally, I could care less, if I have recruitment closed for a class, it's for a good reason. I have this ranger bugging me to let him apply, and saying things like "oh and I see you need SK's, I have an SK friend that will come with me, and a wizard friend, and a cleric, and...". We have 10 rangers, and if Brad himself were to apply to our guild with his ranger I'd probably have to tell him no.

My personal opinion is that anyone trying to break into that stage of the game is far better off finding a guild that is doing ssra and tier 1 stuff, thinking about getting into VT, and grow from there. You're a lot less likely to be just another number on the roster and way more likely to have fun along the way. Of course you won't get uber overnight that way, and if that's your goal then I wish you the best of luck in your search, but I can confidently tell you that guilds like mine are not interested in you.
