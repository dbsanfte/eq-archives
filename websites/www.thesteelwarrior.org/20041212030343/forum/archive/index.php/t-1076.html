To answer you question, yes you can and will use evasive sometimes. There are threads about it and avoiding hits can be better than cutting the damage if you are tanking. I would always suggest though that you use defensive on a mob if you are MT'ing for your guild the first time you try it. Steady predictable damage IMO is better for your healers than the more unpredictable evasive damage. After you size up a mob and get it down, experiment with evasive and see which works better. Keep in mind though a lucky streak on the mob usually means dead tank though.

When my guild was farming Sleeper's Tomb, we usually had two warriors alternate tanking. We used evasive over defensive for the trash mobs simply because it refreshed faster. Keep in mind this was before POP and the lvl 60 limit being upped. We usually had 2-3 groups and hopefully an enchanter.

youre right in theory brutull, I was vastly oversimplifying.

let me put it another way.

In exp groups re use time is a very real issue. In a raid you just swap to another MA while your disc recycles. In an exp group this isnt a possibility.

"Smooth" damage is very important for a CH chain. With a single cleric healing you, the absolute rate of damage taken is more important , IMO , than the spikieness- an unslowed mob, connecting often, and proccing often, can kill you under certain situation (e.g. a bad pull, while the cleric is being hit, etc.).

And finally, AE taunt + evasive is a very real, practical crowd control tool for when you get, say, 4 mobs in camp unexpectedly. AE taunt + defensive is not likely to allow you to survive that situation in my experiance.

I agree that its situational. But I stand by my statement that for the most part evasive is better in exp groups. (of course there are mobs I make exceptions for. non proccing hard hitters get defensive, etc.)
