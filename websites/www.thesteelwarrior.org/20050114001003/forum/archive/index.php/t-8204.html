Much depends on where you are fighting and what your group is doing. Also, it would perhaps be helpful to know who you are losing aggro to, and at what point in the fight.
I can tell you one thing: if you're at level 53 and you have a couple of rangers in your group who are swinging ChoS the mob will look like a ping pong ball bouncing from flipper to flipper.
If you are in an LDoN, try to be the puller if you can--any situation where singles are possible, try to be the first person in your group to do melee damage. If that's not possible, position yourself where you can tag the mob with an arrow before other melee start whacking it. Ask the puller to pull the mob right to you; I think you get a slight aggro edge if you can land a round of melee before you use the incite line, and you'll have an easier time of it if you're not running across the dungeon to get in range of the mob.
If one group member is ganking your aggro prepeatedly, set up this hotkey: /shield ganker'sname. Shield him and you might get aggro back.
Long term: work on saving points for anger augs, try to increase your DEX, and consider Weapon Affinity AAs when you can get them.
And just grit your teeth and try to be an otherwise cheerful groupmember. In some ways the 50s game is rough on warriors.

Don't underestimate the advantage of a few arrows when pulling or on incoming with a good bow, I can get two shots off of 100-200 damage total on average.

Additionally, don't hit Incite before Taunt. I originally did this as I read incite to be a hate dot and I figured the sooner I fire incite the sooner I'll have more agro. However, as I read somewhere on board, hitting taunt as soon as mob is in camp and then hitting incite will hopefully put you on top of the hate list + the amount of hate caused by incite initially and then you can turn on your auto attack to ramp up as quick as possible and stay on top of agro list.

It also depends on what class you're losing agro to, it's quite common to have agro issues with beastlords or slowers that need to adjust how soon the slow based on how quickly their tank generates agro. Also, if you are not level 65 yet, you'll find that the AA mentioned above, in addition to casters getting AA in Spell Casting Subtlety will help.

Without a doubt, if you can do LDoN's at a reasonable point rate (i.e., 51 points for a normal win @ 65 and 105 for a hard) get some sort of proc augments for your weapons. Anger 2/3 or Ykesha if possible, but even a small dd augment for 50 points is better than nothing (just don't expect as big an improvement).
