Necromancers are the class i can speak on the most. Many overlook them for burst dps although with recent expansion additions they have gained alot of this.

When debating dps, the biggest factor is how situational you are going to consider. What is burst dps? i usually consider this a two to three minute encounter - nice and sweet where discs do a large portion of the dps.

With the addition of things like wake the dead and Swarm of Decay, Lifeburn etc necromancers have gained a large portion of dps. Necromancers can stack near 2k a tick if they are using every dot/recourse possible to them with focus, and thats not taking into account critical affliction which adds a nice chunk overall to that figure. 2k a tick x 10 ticks a minute = 20k dmg a minute dots, pre crits. Now ofcourse you have to figure in that is situational, for the first minute you are only usually doing around 6k(dot/recourse) + your max hp(LB) +pet +SoD. This is due to the fact that for the first minute you are starting your stack, first 6 seconds you only get up to one tick of your highest dmg dot, 2nd tick you get first stack'd dot plus the second, and so on. With a nicely equipped necromancer it is very feasible to break 13k + critical affliction + pets in the first minute.

Necromancers shine in sustained damage on top of that dps... every minute after that keeping just recourse on without factoring in pets or critical affliction you can keep around 18k in dots on per minute.

Understand that SoD pets hit change over 300 an arrow, and 3 of them are summoned instant cast - although with a thirty minute refresh on the ability it really only factors into the first minute in most cases. Summoned pets on the otherhand do have nice sustained aggro bumping the necromancers burst and sustained dps up that much more - although in alot of sitautions pets are not used.

So basically in a 3 minute fight an elemental necromancer that knows what hes doing, NOT factoring in resists of the mob but also not factoring in critcal affliction can lay down around 50k dmg + pet + SoD + WTD. In a 3 mintue fight thats 278 dps w/o even factoring in the three pets hitting for 300ish max, the pet quading for 80ish max, or the WTD pet quading for 80ish max when hes not casting spells, or critical affliction. All this added on is a very nice chunk of damage, but this is even more situation to try to throw in a solid number due to mob ac, normal pets being used, and random hits of mobs.

But like i said, necromancers shine in the ability to also keep this dps up... being over 100 mana per tick raid buffed with a large mana pool it is quite easy to keep all dots stacked on a mob casting them as they drop as long as there is enough buff slots on the mob to make this possible.

I think alot leave necromancers out on burst dps just because you dont see many necromancers landing shiny crits over 6k, but they parse very highly in my experience.

Not quite right. It's more like... 16 classes. One tank, one healer, one slower, everyone else fighting the same bland pissing contenst called DPS.

This simplifies things a bit since crowd control, pacification, and pulling are also important skills. So classes such as enchanters who not only can slow, but can handle pacification and crowd control, as well as DPS with charm, are heavily desired. Also note that clerics who fit into the healer role can pacify. So those classes are very heavily in demand. Monks get the pulling benefit to add to their DPS, and SK's get the pulling benefit to add to their tanking. Wizards have high DPS but evac capabilities as well as stun, crowd control. Druids have heals and evac to add to their DPS. Rogues have lockpick to add to their DPS and SoS for corpse dragging. All of the DPS classes have other abilities to augment their desirability to groups, but when it comes down to it, when those three primary slots are filled, tank, healer, slower, any other class can fill the remaining slots.
