"Blood grooves" are different and most peeps wouldn't recognize 'em. Fullers are to lighten a blade and help its flexibility.

Blood grooves are holes in the blade itself that follow the idea that a wound tries to close immediately with a clean cut/stab. Blade enters flesh and stops. Flesh tries to close. Flesh ends up in the 'holes' in the blade. Blade is yanked free and the flesh inside the holes is torn, leaving the wound ragged and rough.

They also hinder a blade's structural integrity, creating unnecessary break points. Makes for a shoddy weapon that you find in the hands of a street thug who wants to look extra mean without the pointless doo-dads protruding everywhere.
