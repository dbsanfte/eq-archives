The only really important decision is to pick
1. A warrior
2. Sumpin else.

Congrats! You chose the correct answer!

Seriously, if you focus on AC/hps most of the time you'll be best off. In the upper levels of the game, the armor with good AC/hps tends to max your melee stats out. But until then, a lot depends on what sort of armor is available to you (according to your budget and whether you are in a guild, etc).
STA is nice because it adds several hitpoints per STA; DEX is nice because it helps govern how quickly you gain skill with a weapon and, later, influences the proc rate of weapons.
But for the most part, the armor with the best AC is the best armor.
There are a couple of exceptions to the AC > all rule early on. Two classic young warrior armor sets -- crafted and crustacean -- are an example. In some slots, crafted has slightly more AC, but the crusty armor has so much STA that it more than makes up for the AC difference.
Other than that, early on try to make sure that your stats rise above 75, or you incur a noticable penalty.
Very occasionally, you might face certain casting mobs that require some sort of resist gear -- magic, cold or fire, typically -- but those are special cases.

Good luck with your new warrior!
