My view is exactly the opposite, at least, when tanking boss mobs.

Spike damage is what kills MTs on bosses. I'd take a steady 3k/round melee every time, over a 0-4k melee/round, averaging out at 2k/round. Why? Because you have to be healed for 'worst case' at all times. Although you'll get lucky a lot of the time on an avoidance based tank, average spike damage will be a lot higher than on a mitigation based tank. Assuming healing isn't fubar, a mitigation based tank will have less spike damage death than an avoidance one. Proc damage may appear to happen less, but that's irrelevent - it's the rounds a quad gets through that will kill you.

If spike damage isn't a danger (eg: exp groups) it doesn't really matter as much.

Well, there comes a time where clerics simply can't heal for max damage on a non defensive tank. Quad 3k melee +AEs for example. When that happens, you can only attempt to reduce the number of times you get hit for above 8k between CHes. Other times (eg: VT/Ssra) the fights are so long, you have to balance damage with rotation frequency. You heal less often, knowing a back to back max round would kill your tank, but you can't heal fast enough for long enough to make an insta-rot viable. It's all about balance, and IMO a steady mitigation which reduces 'danger zone' spikes makes for a longer living MT a greater proportion of the time.

I concede that I'm probably taking a onesided view on this due to having pretty much left procing mobs behind now, as well as the either/or debate being somewhat moot in my recent experience, since you have to have max defensive AAs to tank PoTime (just Inny and Qualm to go! whoot!). That said, I've always taken the 'steady' view with regards to tanking, being a mitigation based tank, and preferring conparable stable DPS based agro over unreliable procs wherever the choice is close. I know I'd take a shielding mod over an avoidance mod every time for example, and preferred Agnar sword over HG/BoC in offhand.

The whole discussion is fairly hypothetical (yet interesting), since as soon as you finish one, you'll start on the next, so the period of one without the other will be fairly short for an active MT. I'd also put SE above all these defensive AAs, just like AET. The purported 70% reduction in stuns is huge for maintaining agro, and agro should always be a MTs primary concern unless it's minimal such as incremental DPS gains from offensive AAs.
