After some head scratching, I'd tend to agree with Xebokan concerning the MM line of really cheap LDoN armor, now that it can be sold back. You'll be able to get a couple of pieces of this before your level gets so high that it's not really level-apropriate.

The real progression path for warrior gear is to get whatever you can as cheap as you can, lol.

Write down the stats of all the armor you are wearing now. Go the bazaar & search by slot, race, class, religion and your budget. Scan the possibilities. Seek AC, STA and hitpoints. Buy the best thing you can get for the buck.

I'd think of it as "layers" of gear classes rather than sending you out for specific armor types. The first layer for you is gothic-frosted-sandblasted + crafted + crustacean. the crusty is the best I think in most spots, but the differences are worth researching. The Gothic stuff should sell for about 100 pp off vendors near forges; the crafted and crusty will be in the bazaar and it just sort of depends what folks are dumping them for. I would imagine 100pp per piece would be a pretty good deal (the gems used in the crafted armor in some cases cost more than that, so if you see someone selling it for that, they ain't making a profit), and some will cost more, but this varies by server and even over time. This stuff would be an upgrade in some slots, but you'll have to replace as much of it as you can almost as soon as you get it.

Armors like steelwrapped and barbed ringmail are potential bridges between crusty and cultural. Again, price varies, but you're looking at the 2000 plat range.

Your cultural armor (Helanic Tundra Armor if you are Tribunal), is really nice stuff and will carry you from whenever you can afford it until you can replace it (you can be wearing it at 65 in many slots). This will cost a few thousand plat per piece.

Some LDoN armor is better than cultural, but you don't really build up LDoN points very fast until you are in your 60s (perhaps you know this already).

You could potentially upgrade your weapon set up for free by going through Dawnshroud peaks to Maidens Eye. Zone into ME but stay at the zoneline and ask politely that, should a goranga spiked club drop near the zoneline, that you be allowed to loot it. It's no-drop, so people might let you have it. It's primary only, 1HB, 12dam/23delay, er, 5 AC I think. You could then move your short sword into your offhand. Be both polite and careful if you try this, though.

Good luck
