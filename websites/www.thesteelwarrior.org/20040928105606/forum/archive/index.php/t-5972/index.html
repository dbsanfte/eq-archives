I've got a level 56 warrior and level 47 shadowknight, but I haven't played either character in probably a year, so I'm not exactly up to speed on class changes. My question is this: in a 3-box trio with an enchanter and a shaman, which character makes the most sense?

I've got some good gear on my shaman, so it's not a huge problem now, but it will only get worse as I fight tougher mobs.

So I need a tank that can hold agro and still put out some decent DPS. With good agro, my shaman can generate very decent DPS via his stackable DOTs and dogdog. With the enchanter, I can handle adds without too much problem, and can lull/pacify, so pulling isn't a huge issue. I don't charm with the enchanter, so he doesn't really do much for DPS right now.

I just haven't played either the warrior or shadowknight in so long that I don't know which character makes more sense. So I'm leaning toward the warrior, but don't really want to jump back into that if the SK is a far superior option. On the shaman crucible, the consensus there is that SKs make the best partner for a shaman, but that opinion may have been formed prior to the recent warrior taunt upgrade. With the enchanter (have clarity II now at level 57), my SK would probably always have mana, so maybe that option makes more sense. I have some decent gear on warrior already, and have about 60k in the bank, so I could probably score a CHoS/Bloodsworn combo for agro - unless there's a better option. I've already got a Greatstaff of Thunder for 2hb agro.

Any advice or similar experience you coud give would be appreciated.

Thorvak
I personally run a team of 6 chars. Its not easy, takes alot of getting used to, but its nice to always have it there when i need it.

Personally i recommend warrior, which is what i use. I scrapped my paladin for warrior just due to the fact that warriors are better at taking damage - period. With good weapons and knowing my own slow times etc, aggro is not an issue. With the new aggro discs, aggro procs, and nice weapons its not too difficult to keep up on aggro.

With this setup i can take down mobs that i could not with my paladin - i dont raid much with my warrior, so buyable or two groupable items are the best hes going to see - with defensive he can stretch those hp alot farther then a paladin with fewer hp ever could.

Now i would imagine an SK would have higher damage output, but i dont think it should be taken as strong enough of a difference either way to make your choice - a tank is a tank, not a dps class. Overall i dont think that will make all that much of a difference. The dps classes in whatever group you are in are goign to do the LARGE bulk of the damage, and if your using a tank alone to kill mobs it will take you forever either way. So i woudl say, make your choice based on defensive/aggro abilities.

Sk's will have faster instant aggro - aka faster slows/nukes.
If you want the utility, and are just looking to have a tank for normal exp group - go sk.

Warrior will take the hits better, and eat up less cleric mana(forget not Defensive/Stonewall).
If you want to be able to tackle larger mobs easily without getting raid quality gear, go warrior.
