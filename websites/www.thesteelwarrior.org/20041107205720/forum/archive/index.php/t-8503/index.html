I understood your point correctly and responded accordingly. Just because the UCMJ defines those words as they do, does not mean it should be so elsewhere. Those terms were defined in the UCMJ for the purposes of legal terminology. Definition in legal documents and proceedings are commonly used in other places also, however, a definitoin in a legal document does not proceed other definitions except within its bounds. The fact that legal documents commonly use definition is reconition that a word can have other equally valid meanings. Definition does not exist without scope. In thise case, mitigation and extenuation definition in the UCMJ is limited to the scope of the UCMJ.
