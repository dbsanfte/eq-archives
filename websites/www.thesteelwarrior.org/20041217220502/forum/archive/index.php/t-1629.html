Some great ideas here. Here's one I think might address the issue. I got the idea from seeing how the bard epic adds to the effectiveness of their songs. I was talking with my bard officer and thought "wish warriors had a reason like that not to bank our epics about the time we got them".

Hey Verant, change our useless epic sword(s) into something that actually adds to our abilities:

Gauntlets of the Warlord (hands slot)
AC: +25 Str: +20 Sta: +15 Dex: +15 Magic Resist: +10 Fire Resist: +10 Cold Resist: +10 Poison Resist: +10 Disease Resist: +10 HP: +100
Effect: Rage of Zek (combat) (1. decrease HPs by 175; 2. Increase Hate by 500)

Other than the AC, the stats are from the Jagged Blade of War.
I thought about putting 41 haste on it too, like the rogue or ranger epics, but that would probably be too powerful.

Anyway, the point is this: with this in your hands slot and procing as if it were in your primary slot, it adds aggro procs ON TOP OF the procs from whatever weapon or weapons you are using.

While we would still be slaves to the RNG to a point, this would increase our aggro generation ability without giving us the snap or assured aggro that Verant doesn't want us to have because it would make raid aggro too easy for us.

This would increase warrior aggro ability for levels 50-65 without making things so easy that it would change the risk versus reward dynamic Verant obviously wants to keep. As a warrior goes from a Frostbringer/Defiance combo through the BoW, his aggro generation will be increased across the board from the point where he earns his epic.

Those of us who already have our epics could throw them in a forge for the new item.

Interested in hearing your thoughts.

Glow
