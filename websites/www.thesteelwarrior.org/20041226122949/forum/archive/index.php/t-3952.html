Smithing will almost always be cheaper to buy from someone else than to skill up and make the item yourself.

Right now, you would be looking at the LDoN armor, which is about a 175 to 210 skill item. Costs a smith of that skill about 100 to 150p to make a piece of that armor. Probably sells for cost plus 10 to 40 percent. (currently I sell my wifes skillup goods at about cost).

To get to 175 (where fine steel plate maxes out) would cost... maybe 5k? Maybe more? Then factor in all the leather padding you farm yourself you could have sold to make money, and then you are looking at a higher opportunity cost.

If you are interested, www.eqtraders.com is a more fleshed out site for this sort of information.

Oh, second side note. If you arnt hitting 255 str with focus, I would advise putting off smithing for a while. Doing skillups without a max stat is like tanking with a few armor pieces missing. You can do it, but its not the best way to do it.

If you like the idea of being a smith, by all means do it.

I started smithing at level 7, way before I knew anything about the game. It was important to me for role-play reasons, and as soon as I realized there was a smithing skill I started to learn it.

Well, some people do manage to get to 250, I know one person who has done it. I'm at 207 myself.

First off, consider those people who sell leather padding for 30p a whack. All it takes to make padding is 35 tailoring skill, and farming time. If you are willing to spend the time, this could be a source of income for you. Get a large tailoring kit and make yourself a skinning knife. Then go to east karana and kill lions. Combine HQ and MQ pelts in the kit with the knife to make LQ pelts (it's the only source of LQ pelts I know, never seen a LQ pelt actually drop anywhere). When you have a few stacks, go to ferrott, to the NE corner of the zone. Be careful of bouncers on the way there, but once you get there the corner is safe. Kill the spiderlings for spiderling silks. They drop a number of different types of silk, which is annoying, but this is the best place to farm them. There are actually 2 areas of spiderlings, one in the NE corner, and one just south of the druid circle, a good zone map will come in handy. Combine 3 silks I think to make a thread, then combine thread and pelt to make 1 pad. At 25p a pop, 3 stacks would make you 1500p, a nice windfall if the most plat you've ever had is 320. Do this a few times and you will have plenty of plat to buy smithing components such as blocks of ore and molds. If you are patient, you can improve your gear as well.

Getting smithing over 100 isn't too hard or costly, banded armor goes up to 115 or so. After that you have to make ornate chain for a while, which requires bars of precious metals, the same ones jewelers use. Generally you can sell back what you make to a vendor for 75-80% of what it cost to make it, it's failures that really hurt the pocketbook. I strongly recommend buying 2 opal encrusted steins (+25 chr each) to use when dealing with vendors. You can generally get them from someone in the bazaar for 50p each or so. Also, a geerlok automated hammer is a must. This will boost your skill a bit, which helps cut down on failures.

The reason people say to raise your str is that it improves your chance to gain a skill point. It's actually the difference between your str and your skill level that determines your chance to gain a skill point. For example, if your str is 180 and your skill is 105, the difference is 75. As you gain skill the difference lessens, and the chance to gain a skillup drops as well. There are "break points" in this progression, points where if you gain 1 more point you will notice that your skillup rate has suddenly dropped. If you then gain more str you will again skill up more easily, until you hit the break point again. Obviously, if your chance to gain a skill point drops, it takes a lot more combines to gain each point on average, and it costs more plat, so maxxing out your str helps reduce your time farming and combining, and the amount of plat you spend.

Once you finish ornate chain (the gold tunic trivials at 155, don't bother with the platinum ornate, failures are too expensive) you move on to fine steel (bracers first). Here is where you need all that leather padding, one pad per combine. If you buy the padding from players it will add a lot to the cost of gaining skill. Not counting padding, you can make FS items for 10-15p per combine after selling the results back to a vendor (averaging in failures). If it's taking you 10 combines to gain a skill point, then you can see that it's costing you 100-150p per skill point. You will make FS items from 155 to 188, or 33 skill points.

From 188 to 212 you can make ldon armor fairly cheaply if you farm your own temper components. The best source is goblin bones, in the ruj dungeons, getting 4-5 of these per mission is about the norm. The superb padding you need is sold in pok, 25p each.

The other option is to make shadowscream armor, which requires you to do the progressive smithing quest in shar val, which took me about 8 hours. After that you do a lot of farming in hollowshade moor and in twilight sea. There is a pretty good writeup on this on eq traders. It can be found here:
http://www.eqtraders.com/articles/article_page.php?article=g99&menustr=040100000000

There are a few mistakes in the article about the HS war. To start, the sonic wolves start in the east, and the owl bears in the NW. Second, killing the named of a race will often cause another race to launch an attack upon them. Third, I've farmed the zone a lot, and I haven't had any trouble with the war getting bugged. Fourth, you don't really need to "manage" the war once it starts, you won't care if bears or wolves have the south islands as you need substances from each. As you farm it, from time to time the islands will change hands from bears to wolves and back, this is fine. If the bears or wolves completely take over the zone, the merchant camp near the entrance to shar val is taken over by a boss bear or wolf, killing them will reset the zone. The boss bear drops a FT1 range item which is nice for casters, I'm not sure what the boss wolf drops.

Also, swirling shadows are no longer NO RENT. They are also tradable, and sell well in the bazaar, if you want another source of income. The shades are a bit tougher than bears and wolves though. If you have a group, they are probably decent enough XP for someone in their 30s.

To start the war, kill the grimling boss in the south. Then kill the grimling defenders that pop when the war starts, so the attack force (bears or wolves) can get into the boss tent. When this happens all the grimlings will depop and be replaced by bears or wolves. Once you get teh war started, you don't really care too much if wolves or bears have the south islands, you need to farm them both for substances. Generally you will get 1 drop per 6-8 kills. Typically I 2-box this with my cleric and warrior. I set my cleric up with point blank AE spells (yes, clerics get a few). I then run around with the warrior and aggro a lot of mobs. Levitate and sow can help here, because some of the wolves and bears are fast. When you start getting a lot of mobs chasing you, if they surround you they can make a scrum pile with you in the middle, which will keep you from moving. Generally once I get 40-50 mobs chasing me I run to the cleric and AE them. Typically I'll get 5-8 drops per train, 40-50 substances an hour. It actually takes as much time to clear all the corpses as it does to pull and kill them, the owls and bears drop a lot of shit, although perfect owlbear pelts are useful for making a weight reduction bag.

Shadowscream armor is actually REALLY CHEAP to make, it costs like 2p per combine because the only things you have to buy are flasks of water and ore to make metal rings, and banded molds. You do have to do a lot of farming, which is easiest to do at high level when all the mobs are green to you. If paying 25p per superb pad is more than you want to spend, then you can just go make shadowscream instead. Actually, you could probably make SS armor instead of fine steel, and save yourself 5k plat, although you will end up farming more.
