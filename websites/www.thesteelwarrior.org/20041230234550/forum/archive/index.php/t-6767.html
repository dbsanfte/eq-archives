Some quick rules for dual wielding:

1 - The delays are seperate for each weapon: putting a faster weapon into your mainhand will NOT cause your offhand to swing more often.
2 - For each 10 delay, unhasted, it takes 1 second to swing. A 25 delay weapon, unhasted, will take 2.5 seconds between attacks.
3 - If your offhand has a delay of 30, every 3 seconds it does a check against your dual wield skill. If you meet the skill check, you will attack with your offhand. It will then do a check against your double attack skill, etc.
4 - Rule of thumb is to put that faster weapon in your primary hand, but only if the two weapons are similar in their ratios. If you have one weapon that is significanlty better than the other, but slower, it may still be better to put in your mainhand.
5 - At level 65 with max dex (305), the average ppm (proc per minute) is 2ppm mainhand, 1ppm offhand. This can be altered by AA's such as Weapon Affinity and some rare weapons have an innate change to their proc rates. But for most weapons it's the same.

The poster said he just trained dual wield. The damage bonus that is the reason that people say to put the fastest weapon in the primary slot doesn't do jack until about lvl 50. The damage bonus is added to your mainhand each time it hits, so the faster weapon swings and hits more therefore getting more bonus damage over time than a slower one.

Until around lvl 50, put your best ratio weapon in main hand and your second best in offhand or use offhand to train other one hand weapon skills like piercing, blunt, etc. At some point you will find it advantageous to have one person in a group maintaining the attention of the mob so that is the only person getting healed. At this point you may start looking for weapons that have a proc that upsets the mob and makes you the focus of their attention. When this happens the ratio of the weapons becomes less important, although it is still a consideration.

Good luck and enjoy the warrior.

A couple of things.
1. as pointed out above, you do have a damage cap until level 20, so the wurmy will be a better weapon after that (for a while) than it is before lvl 20.
2. Speedy 1-hand weapons are nice in the main hand, partly because in the early going, you'll have trouble training all of your weapon skills (you'll level too fast). As Wandor points out, the more swings you take, the more "chances" you have to improve your skill.
3. Eventually, procs become the significant difference in the ability of weapons to hold aggro; however, as late as your late 40s it's not a huge part of the game, because most of the best weapons are not usable at that point, and because your DEX will almost certainly be far closer to 100 than 300.
