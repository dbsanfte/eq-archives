I'll be playing it. I'm having a ball with this game. I'll probably be on pinnacle but haven't decided yet. You can all probably GUESS my character's name! Hahahahahahahahah. Now at least my username HERE makes a bit of sense. :P

The game's been very fun for me. Don't want to go into a really long complex post, but so far the content has been just good enough to keep me coming back for more. Sometimes it's REALLY good content. Sometimes it's a boring grind (but eh, all MMORPGs are designed to be grinds I guess). The powers are very fun. The costume creation is intense and worth the price of the game alone, heh. The RP aspect is actually playable.

My biggest gripes are: Communication is difficult. They need something a bit smoother. Maybe I'm just used to EQ, but it's very hard for me to play AND talk at the same time. And that needs to be addressed, as MMORPGs THRIVE on the social aspect. Other biggest gripe is the mobs you're on a MISSION for are never around when you need them. I think it's coded that way. But it is frustrating. I take a Kill-10 mission and then poof the mob type I need 10 of are all gone, replaced with something completely different. Because of this I tend to stick with go to place X and do Y missions.

Character advancement is actually there. There is a carrot chase, and that I didn't expect. So that's a real bonus. Loot (well what passes for it in CoH) is much more evenly distributed in this game than EQ.

My biggest gripe with the powers/loot/advancement portion of the game is, if you make a mistake you can't go back and undo it. In EQ this was never an issue (a warrior trains Bash and doesn't like it's not really a game breaking thing) ... nor in Star Wars Galaxies (as you could simply get rid of the skill tree and start training a different one). But in CoH, once you choose, you're stuck with it. And sometimes it's hard to choose which would be better (travel powers for instance are difficult ... do you want to superleap? run like the wind? or fly there?)

Anyways, I've already rambled like I said I wouldn't. Sorry. It's a fun game. I like it. I'll be playing it.

Hmmm. It's hard to stopy playing to post about what it's like. Heh.

Anyways, I have two characters (and just started a third). All of them are fun as hell. The missions are exciting. And get more exciting when grouping/teaming up with fellow heroes. The storylines are actually interesting, for a video game ... and go way beyond what EQ normally gives you. Then again, the storylines have to be in CoH. There's no UBER-sword to speak of (though there is loot, of sorts). The driving force has to be the story.

The level grind is there, that's for sure. But I think they really hit the mark with this game. You can play casually and still feel like you're winning, and getting somewhere. Like even if you're mired in your teens or whatever, you're still completing missions. You're still thwarting crimes. You're still being a super hero, every step of the way.

Costume design is fun. It's got its limitations, but the foundation is there, and it's quite a foundation. You'll find yourself wanting to design new toons quite often, heh. I know I do. It's why I've almost filled up all 8 slots, mostly because I wanted to see how cool this or that would look.

It IS TRUE, TAUNT DOES WORK in this game. It's one of the better powers young tankers (and scrappers) get.

Tankers can be pretty damn impressive in this game. They don't do impressive damage, mind you. So don't get your hopes up there. But good lord can they take a beating, heh.

I don't play a tanker though. I play a controller, and in my spare time a scrapper. Still, the room for individuality within each archtype is pretty impressive too. The combos you can come up with really do give you a lot of breathing room, so not every tanker is alike.

There is no BEST character design. There is no best strategy for completing a mission, at least not in the first 10 levels. :P

Anyways, I've rambled enough. The game is fun. It's got me semi-retired from EQ, and if it stays this fun, it'll be more permanent retirement.
