There are two ways of looking at this, I think.
Emilia has done an excellent job of outlining the first way of thinking about these pieces--that is, of the armor sets in these groups, which are best in each slot?
The second way of looking at upgrading is referenced by Alisdahre, who points out the value in upgrading for the biggest boost given the gear you currently have.
In general, I'd progress along these lines:
1. Know yourself: What stats most need improvement on this character? Are STA/DEX maxxed? How are AC/HP, not just in an absolute sense, but relative to each other?
2. Know your gear: identify the best and worst slots you have and become knowledgable about the relative costs of upgrades in the slots that lag.
3. Know your power: Can you get access to drops outside of LDoN? Which ones? Where is your guild?
4. Know your pocket book: how easy is it for you to generate cash?

I'd say that it would be helpful to know not just how these pieces compare to each other, but how they compare to armor pieces that are in a class below these categories.
This can get pretty complicated pretty fast because of the variables involved, but let's take a simple example: you level to 65 and you're wearing cultural BP and legs. You're trying to figure out which slot to focus on upgrading first. Everything being equal, I'd focus on the BP first. Neither the cultural greaves nor the BP is at the same level as these other armors, but the LDoN BP is a far superior upgrade for you than, for example, the ornate legs: Ornate legs 45AC, 90 hps, clickie STA boost; Helanic Tundra legs 40 AC, 10 STA, 80 hps (bleh, sorry don't know RZ stats off the top of my head, but the AC is the same on them, I'm pretty sure). Yes, ornate would be an upgrade, but the 69 AC bp is a much bigger upgrade over cultural's 51AC bp, and the LDoN bp is going to last a long time.
Anyway, that might be a less real-world example than I'd like, but my point is that in addition to the relative value of the possible armor out there, you have to figure out how many LDoN points you have, what your armor is like, how easy it is for you to get pp, where your guild is in it's progression, and perhaps how many raid/loot points you have.
I'd also say that it doesn't hurt to have at least a vague idea what your friends need/would like to advance their characters and to help them if you can. My personal view--and, to my credit, one which I have announced in /guild -- is that it's all about me and my needs. Luckily, people thoguht I was kidding: the longer my friends are in the dark about that, the better for me. Anyway, it's much easier to talk a guildmate cleric into coming with you to a boring or dangerous camp for an armor upgrade you need if, every single time that cleric opens his inventory and sees that he has his Epic, he remembers that you were the guy tanking those boring epic camps for him.
