Very nice skill.

This is not aggro lock by any means. Not even close. According to Lucy it is 165 instant hate and then some sort of effect that lasts for one minute. Not sure what this is. Some have speculated that it is a hate dot of some sort. When you cast it on yourself, it doesn't put an icon in your buff box, but you do get a "wore off" message after one minute.

This is just awesome for pulling or tagging mobs off of the puller. 150 range is enough to not bother with your bow in most places. Using it some 2-boxing my cleric last night it was also very handy for aggroing adds that came to camp, or tanking multiples without leaving your current target for more than a second. It's also no damage, so you could presumeably use it to build some hate on mezzed mobs by switching targets for just long enough to hit the provoke button. Didn't try this, but I'm almost sure it wouldn't break mez.

Someone I was talking to in tells mentioned that it would be nice for splitting multipulls on mobs on raids as well.

This skill has a ton of possiblities, and is easily as good as all the other abilities put together.

To answer some questions:

1. Grongl and I tested the "competing provokes" issue last night in Kael. Partially because I wanted to test if the Raex BP I was wearing would give me a greater "provoke" value than him who wasnt' wearing one. Well, provoke lands from both warriors, and in the first instance he got it off right before I did, and the mob went for him, which could be attributed to the "first come, first serve" theory of initial aggro giving a bonus. The second time, we got it off about the same time, and he got hit first, but the second I started meleeing it came to me.

2. Yes, provoke does indeed trigger social aggro. I pulled all 3 PoZ's in the building in Kael arena by provoking the middle one.

3. Provoke is not affected by Furious Bash shields (no glowing red).

4. Provoke cannot be used while sitting

5. Provoke from max distance WILL pull a mob from a pet that has damaged the mob 70%. I was with an Enchanter who DC'd a giant and killed down a mob to 30%, one provoke at max range brought the mob to me. I can't wait to see if this happens on raids with 20 pets, half of them giant tigers and a mob in a crappy place.

6. Provoke seems at this time to, for the first 10 seconds after it's used, disable the ability to press other openings skills. The opening comes but the buttons dont come up. I feel this is a bug which will be worked out before the 16th.

7. PvP doesn't seem to have any ability to use openings and when provoke is used against an opponent in PvP, it messes up your own provoke timer.

I'm still doing pretty extensive testing on this, as some unanswered questions that linger in my mind are:

- Do the stun attacks generate aggro? I am leaning toward no, since they use existing attack types (Kick/Slam).

- Missing on special attacks: I don't like waiting for openings and then missing on the special attack.

- Opening duration is pretty good right now, it's long enough to give you a chance, without being stupid long.

- Opening *frequency* is still way too low.
