The issue I see here is that your suggestion, while interesting, does not go to the heart of the problem, which is that war aggro holding ability is completely RNG-dependent (taunt, proc), whereas pally/sk aggro holding ability is reliable and spell based. Hmmm...some interesting suggestions.

As a general rule, and from a software engineering perspective, I think it likely that the developers want to avoid adding complexity to a system that is already massively complex and, in all likelihood, poorly understood in its current entirety. This is bad practice because, first, it results in a higher probability of instability due to unanticipated code interaction, and second, it makes the system as a whole much more difficult to test, which again leads to instability and other problems. After all, if the developers attempt to add highly nuanced skills/discs, then the law of unintended consequences comes into play, and we could see class imbalance occur in an entirely different area, such as Sarelin alluded to.

From that standpoint, backing code out is a lot more attractive - which I think is why we see so many nerfs and so few really innovative (i.e. code-complex) additions. Easy to code a new high level heal spell that works the same as the others only heals more HPs - not so easy to introduce an entirely new mechanism for a new *type* of heal spell, for example.

So if you are a developer who actually wanted to 'fix' the problem, I think it highly likely that you would choose the path that leads to less complexity - not more.

Also, though nerfs suck for the classes that get nailed with them for sure, I would not agree that nerf = bad. I would say that class and game balance is what you want to strive for - nerfing is in general a less complex and problematic way to achieve this in a lot of cases, which is why we see so much of it.

But hell, you could easily argue that by adding an overpowering, class imbalancing ability to pallies, you have implicitly nerfed the warriors without doing anything at all to the class. So now do you compound the problem by adding more complexity to offset the lousy idea you implemented that started the entire imbalance chain in the first place?

To put it simplistically, if pallies used to score a '5' and war's a '10' on aggro, and you 'fixed' that by making the pallies score what you thought would be a '10' but turned out to be a '15' - now do you want to increase war's to '15', but in so doing risk boosting them to '20' at which point the pallies start screaming again? Or would you be better off backing ths pallies back down to the '10' where you intended them to be?

I have to agree with Sarelin that tuning UP war's skills is very dangerous - it would mean we might benefit as much then as pallies do now - and the goal is not to make warriors rule >> all, but to even the playing field between us and the hybrids.

I think also that Sarelin is on to something in regard to pally low level stuns - virtually mana-free (I'll get arguments on that one I know) aggro generation is a major part of the problem. That way, downtime is incurred whether you choose a war or a pally - IF you do not choose to practice aggro management as a caster (which is another hugely important thing to ancourage in my view), and this would have an impact on what class groups would be willing to go with for MT and on how soon and how often the casters chose to use their aggro generators. You would also be more selective in adding casters - instead of going with just any lvl 65 wizzy or chanter, you'd want one who played their class competently in the context of the game mechanics - this is nothing but good, in my view. Keeps the e-bayers at...well, at bay.

The pally could still have a near guaranteed aggro holding ability, but there would be a significant and explicit cost associated with it for all to see. So the pallies would still have something of an edge in that sense, but war AC/HP advantage is itself a slight edge, along with AE taunt, and so would tend to offset to some degree and bring the classes into much closer balance.

And from the coding perspective, tuning down the low level stun aggro should be a very simple way to go about things - you are simply changing the hate number they use when doing the calculation, I would imagine.

In my view, this idea appears to resolve two of the major issues we are seeing - not just pallyaggro-beats-waraggro-handsdown, but also the follow on problem of enabling casters to indiscriminately cast whatever they want whenever they feel like it - it's a winner.

Lotta fermentation of ideas here - I like to see it. As for specific suggestions, I would stipulate as follows:

The problem as I see it is not that warrior aggro is broken. If warriors are 'fixed' to be able to hold aggro anywhere near where knights can, then we would have 2 classes (3 counting the SKs) that unbalance the game - make it less challenging than it should be, etc.
