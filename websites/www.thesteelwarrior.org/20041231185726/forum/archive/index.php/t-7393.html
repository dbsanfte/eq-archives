For those that don't know Unyielding Stance is an Invulnerability tanker power that increases resitances to Smashing, Lethal, Knockback, Hold, Sleep, etc. but it also immobilizes you. I've read a few posts on the official CoH message boards saying that US is the power to have. I'm lvl 24 and I haven't taken it yet, I'm still very reluctant because of the immobilization portion.

I was wondering if anyone here had any experience with it and what they thought of it.
Glatius2
06-01-2004, 12:26 PM
I've had it for a few levels, and I find it quite handy. There are particular mobs, such as the Tsoo ink men and ancestors that stun you frequently. In unyielding stance this pretty much disappears. The only time I've ever had a hold, stun, or knock back actually land was once a consigliere in Independence Port managed to land a hold on me, but he was extremely purple and it may have just been an extreme level difference that allowed him to do it. And as I'm sure you know, that when you're stunned or held, your defenses drop meaning you take a lot more damage until you snap out of it. Unyielding stance can have a huge impact on surviving encounters against these kind of mobs.

If you have taunt and/or provoke, while in unyielding stance you can still quite handily do your group job of focusing the attention of the mobs on you. The only problem you end up having is that sometimes your knock back attacks will knock a mob out of range and you end up having to wait for it to come back in range. Or very occassionally a mob will hover just outside of your melee range, waiting on you to drop unyielding stance, but this is by far the exception. And sometimes when a mob decides to beat feet and run from you, you'll have to risk dropping unyielding stance to chase them down, but normally that isn't too much of a problem. And if you simply click off unyielding stance, or even just click temporary invulnerabilty on which will auto disengage unyielding stance, you can move immediately, although unyielding stance does have a bit of a refresh timer, so you should be sure that's really want you want to do first.

Using unyielding stance I very easily will tackle a group of yellow tsoo ink men along with a light red ancestor and come out on top. Without it, I very probably would be stunned so often that my defenses would be off more than they would be on and I'd probably have to run if I didn't get defeated altogether. I really recommend that you get it at your earliest opportunity. It can really make a major difference in certain encounters.
