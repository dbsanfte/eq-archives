GSot4W is a phenominal DPS agro weapon. I usually get 5-10 seconds of whacking on a mob before my guild finishes up the previous mobs and works on mine. Since you're not relying on procs, the agro is consistant and highly effective. I notice FAR less agro bouncing between me and a fellow warrior who uses a CHSD+GBoS. The flip side is, the longer the battle, the less effective at keeping agro the GSot4W seems to become because eventually other guildies will catch up to you in damage output. The weapon is great for exping and trash clearing due to the short duration of the fights, but I only find it to be decent at best for boss tanking.

Still, I wouldn't give it up if my poor little halfling life depended on it. :)

Unfortunately the list is pretty complete. About the only weapons left out are the Troll remanufactured Yaks.

The best "entry level" aggro weapons are probably Copper Hammer of Striking, Great Blade of Storms and Greatstaff of Thunder. If these aren't available the Troll remanufactured Yaks can be substituted. Blade of Strategy can be used in zones where it isn't resisted. Cord Hilted Spike Driver is probably the easiest to obtain step up.

Since I'm at the next stage with aggro weapons, I'm going to provide my experiances -

I'm very happy with my CHoS at level 65. I use it with a BoC instead of Blade of Strategy, Ichorflow, and Trident of the Deep Sea which I also have.

I use the Acrylia Handled Broadsword and Jalen's Katana when I'm fooling around with green/light blue mobs or soloing to kill time - they are not aggro weapons IMO.

I like Gaudralek a lot. It is my only 2H aggro weapon. In PoP I take a lot less damage with a 2Hdr. While I've been in many groups that believed it held aggro much better than my 1Hdr set up, fellow guild Warriors believe the opposite is true. I've tried a Furious Bash shield and have not been happy with it.

If there's a lot of competition for aggro weapons you'll just have to be patient.
