Seems there was a little bad planning, and a little lack of execution, and obviously bad luck starting the CHeal rotation.

If I was leading the raid, and I had a knight class in addition to the MA/SA/TA warriors, they would have been designated as offtank for any adds on the pull. The fact that the SA was taken out of the rotation to offtank shows lack of planning. Essentially they pulled the SA out and stuck you, an inexperienced raider, as SA on what can be a tough fight.

Here's how it should have happened as I see it.

Pull comes, Vindi + add. Since a knight has already been assigned to tank adds, the Guardian is agroed and offtanked, and slowed (since what else are the slowers doing?). Since the offtank has had a backup healer already assigned (someone not in the rotation, such as a druid or shaman), the add becomes a non-issue.

As the pull comes in, and the knight hits the guardian, the MT goes Evasive (Vindi is an evasive fight, check the library for details on tanking boss mobs and why evasive is better for this fight) then hits Vindi with their Provoke line. Here the CHeal rotation starts, with another non-cleric providing spam heals until the CHeal lands. Vindi is engaged by MT. Spam heals land, next cleric in the rotation casts, CHeal lands, and so on.

The fight is now on. The MT/ST/TT are all engaged now, and the CH rotation is stable. DPS classes engage, yada yada.

Now say there is a hiccup in the CH rot, or Vindi gets lucky with a max hit quad and goes down. Vindi's gonna be making a Bee-Line to someone who's probably not the ST or TT. Without AE Taunt to grab Vindi back, the ST has to be quick or risk losing folks. Here's where it gets tricky. The ST needs to taunt Vindi or get him back before anyone important dies, hit their evasive, and hope the CHeal rotation has switched fast enough to him. In addition, the spam healer now has to quickly target him and get him spam healed with smaller heals until the rotation lands. Tank switches on Vindi are a pain, but if everyone is ready for it, it's fairly painless.

Now lets say Mr. SA forgot to hit their disc - well they're dead now and the TA needs to step up. Vindi will likely be eating DPS classes now, so again it's disc/taunt and pray the rotation switches.

It's pretty easy to compare what happened to you. The SA became an offtank, meaning you were instantly added as SA for Vindi. Either the MA didn't /disc early enough (if you're not /disc'ed before Vindi hits you, you're gonna be dead) or there were no support healers to keep him up until the rotation began. A good rotation begins very early with Vindi, since even with /evasive a tank can be dead in 10 seconds with bad luck. When Vindi came, he should have been targeted by you, so no need to worry about targeting when the MA went down. I would want the SA to engage as quickly as it is safe to do so. That means you be in position, ready to start whacking. There was no assist called because the MT was dead before the rotation ever landed. Once the first or second CHeal was in, I expect the MT would have called the assist. Before then, the rotation just isn't stable enough and that's priority #1 in this encounter.

What could you have done differently? Probably nothing that would have saved the raid. But there are a few mistakes I did notice. First, Vindi should have been targeted before he was engaged. Next, when the MT went down, you better be on your disc, and fast. If Vindi gets on you without a disc up, as you learned, there is zero chance of the rotation getting set up on you before you die. I'd go Disc-->Taunt-->Provoke-->Tank Switch Message. As SA on this fight, I'm fighting with my agro weapons in my hands. Things happen fast, and you won't have time to switch weapons if the MA falls.

Well, that's about it. Tough when things go wrong on a raid, but sounds like you learned a lot about the fight, which is good.

Back in the day, we used to wipe on Vindi too; he is the first real raid-level mob a lot of people try and the tactics you develop on him are usefull for many later raids. It is amazing how much simple hotkeys and a raiding system will imporve raid successes.

First off, the pull requires the monks/bards to know how to split him from his pets. This requires some practise, but even with a pet he isn't too bad.

The MT should be positioned shrunk/illusioned in a corner either inside the arena, or near the ramp leading out.

Pulling Vindi to this spot is a critical moment, and this is where many many guilds will get it wrong first time. Pets will often jump him so unless the users have the /pet hold AA, they should be killed. Bards need to stop singing at this point too. In fact noone should even move at this point and they should be well out of the line of sight of the MT and where Vindi is coming from.

Clerics should positioned as far from the MT as possible and are given an order to start the Cheal chain at the right time; aka start too early and Vindi will instantly kill the Cheal chain, start too late and the MT will die. Often a HoT (heal over Time) is used to keep the MT up untill the Cheal chain lands.

On inc, the MT shoots several arrows at Vindi and if everyone is doing the right thing, this will bring him to the corner; Defensive should then be hit and these days Incite on incoming is a usefull tool. If Vindi does hit someone else, someone screwed up and the MT has little choice but to move out and tank him away from the corner after which the melee will need to push him back to the corner. Once aggro is secure, the order can be given to assist either the MT on the boss mob or the MA for clearing possible adds.

If the MT should die, either a ranger goes in and uses Weaponshield to keep him busy for 20 seconds untill the SA can taunt and the Cheal changes to him or he uses Area taunt followed by Furious, followed by incite and defensive.

This in a nutshell is basic raiding tactics and this needs to become second nature to a guild; especially in PoP where encounters require handling lots of adds at the same time while still MT ing the boss mob or dealing with dangerous AoE spells.
