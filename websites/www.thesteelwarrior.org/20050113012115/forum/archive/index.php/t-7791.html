We have a simple system like Cimmar's. Items are determined based on a formula that gives a value from the stats of the item. X hp = Y dkp, and soforth. Raids are determined by the average drop (in dkp) of that mob divided by the average raid (usually 50 nowadays). That gives a number of pts awarded for that kill. We take logs right before engaging and everyone who was there when we win is given those points.

Theres no auctioning or point reduction at all, you have to pay the cost of the item, or you don't loot it. Highest point total gets it. If nobody'll pay the cost, it rots. Sometimes it sucks to see some nice things rot, but we can point to the numbers whenever someone has a problem with something - it's all in black and white.

The point at which unpriced auctions break down is with restricted class items. Knights would get their weapons dirt cheap, whereas warriors would generally have to bid against most other melee classes. Leather classes would pick up their class restricted armor cheaper than plate. Then they would have more DKPs left to spend on the all/all items as a result. You also get to a point where someone finds DKPs worthless (so low it doesn't amtter, or back for a 'sightseeing' trip before retiring again) so they can bid as high as they want without penalties.

We use cDKP (current saved DKP) to distinguish loot order and a fixed pricing system. Sure, there are a few annomalies in pricing, but generally we're faily good at pricing things after this many years. If anyone was to try and vastly unfairly price something, the rest of the guild would be able to overturn it anyway. Show up to raids and earn cDKP, buy something and spend cDKP. Show up lots and loot little and you rise up the ladder for the nicer, more sought after items. Loot lots or show up sporadically, and you have to wait for the top folks to loot/pass on the best items.

For junk stuff we use gDKP (gross DKP) which is your attendance over the last 3 months (cDKP divided by total possible DKP over that period). This means the currently most active people get first refusal on the toys, crappy random augs or quest stuff.

The 'best' system is a perfectly run merit system, where the loot council is implicitely trusted by the rest of the raiders, unfortunately they are few and far between, as every decision is open to interpretation 50 different ways.

As for the trash loot, what little there is from raids gets looted by officers and goes to buy dots/ems/pearls etc., as with the tradeskill stuff. There's rarely enough to be worth 'stealing' as you suggest, and hasn't been droppable raid loot for years (Velious and ele tradeskill stuff)

If you don't like they way your loot is distributed, go find a guild that does things the way you want. Come back and complain when you've raided solidly for a year to get into PoTime and a bard on a rare visit wins the first BP with a lucky roll, then vanishes again.
