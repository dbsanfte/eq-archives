Raaj
These questions were raised on the board in various places, they were asked and got the following answers:

Q: An soe rep was quoted from eqlive as saying shields rased the ac cap. Is this referring to a softcap, or the obvious raise in AC by adding a higher ac item?
A:"Shields do indeed raise the soft cap by the amount of AC on the shield. We were also looking into the possibility of allowing people who use shields to get a chance to block with them. "

Q: Is the timeweaver pet nerf intentional?
A: "There was an issue with the NPC's skills related to recent skill changes. It will be fixed for the next Live update."

Q: Any updates on augment removal?
A: "The base system will be on Test today. Which features a new window when you click on the Augmentation Sealer world container. The functionality to remove augments using new solvents is in, however you may not be able to get the new solvents just yet. Design is still finalizing that process. In addition to this, the process to delete augments has changed slightly. You don't need a specific solvent and you delete the augment you want by selecting the slot.

Btw, the augment refund guy will be removed. This won't be a way to get refunds for all your unwanted augments.

The price for the solvents is going to scale based on the value of the augment. "

Arathena
No. We're using one word to describe 2 different effects. There is the AC-ATK breakpoint, which is how the AC softcap is usually described. Further, for each class, there is a single number beyond which the AC displayed over this number is divided by a constant when actually applied to find the effect of AC - the linearity of the formula changes above a certain AC value. I will hereforth call this a 'codecap'

For example, let's make the completely unfounded assumption that the warrior AC codecap is 1500 AC, and the constant of new linearity is 4: All AC over 1500 is effectively divided by four. To get the same gains that you got going from 1400 AC to 1500 AC, you must now go from 1500 AC to -1900- AC. The shield AC bypasses this effect, however, and equipping a 50 AC shield at 1500 AC means you only need to go to 1750 AC to get the same effect as you would going to 1900 AC without the shield.
