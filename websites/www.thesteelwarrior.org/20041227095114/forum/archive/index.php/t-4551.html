5. Always work on upgrading your gear. Its an endless cycle, but you can't stop! =)

Good luck, and enjoy tanking! I know I do!

Taunting right after a debuff lands is excellent aggro, since the debuffer most likely have more aggro than you, that early in a fight.

The messages for a few of the most common debuffs casted early in a fight :
Shaman Slow ........ (XYZ yawns)
Enchanter slow ..... (XYZ slows down)
Tash ................... (XYZ glances nervously around),
Cripple................. (XYZ has been crippled),
Malo ................... (XYZ looks very uncomfortable),
Mala ................... (XYZ looks very uncomfortable)
Asphyxiate .......... (XYZ begins to choke)

If you are the one to call out assist and the likes (not all guilds have the tank do that, for whatever reason) make sure your hotkeys are easily seen and to the point !

Example of a bad hotkey :
/raid verily I say, this %t is bugging me and I ask of you to assist me.

Example of a good hotkey :
/raid >>>>>>>>> ASSIST ME ON : %t <<<<<<<<<<

The second one is MUCH easier to spot and the message is short.

If you have read around on other guides, you wil most likely already know the basis of the rest, so I won't repeat stuff like that here.

All good advice. My biggest pet peve is the push. When I'm MTing, I call all the shots. Be vocal, be aggressive...swear if that's what it takes to get their attention. Learn to use the push to your advantage. It's not easy, every once in a while I find myself with my back to the wall and I'm getting the "you cannot see your target" message. The more experience you have the less often that will happen.

Hit points aren't as big of an issue as most people think. As long as you have a solid amount, the healers should be able to make some slight adjustments and have no problems after that. 4.6k unbuffed is a good number for a level 60 warrior ( this puts you right at 6k with virtue ). Anything above that would be a bonus. Keep in mind I'm saying you can comfortably tank most mobs with this many hps, however, you can never have too many.

An above poster mentioned taunting immediately after the debuff. I do that on reflex now...as soon as I see "target yawns" or "target slows down" my taunt finger hits. Regular shaman slow is 3 second cast and chanter slow is 5 second cast. You can even watch when you see "Chanter_01 begins to cast a spell." and time your taunt off that. It helps to know when to expect the spell to hit.

As to being the main tank (which I love doing :))

A few pieces of advice:
1. make sure you have clear, easy to read hot keys for: (1) what you are targetting, (2) when people should assist, (3) when the mob is being pushed wrong, (4) when you are ready to flip the mob, and (5) when the mob enrages. Hot keys allow you to communicate quickly and effectively without losing focus on what you are doing.

2. before the fight always make sure everyone understands where you will be and where they should be, and if there should be any pushing. Be especially careful with pet classes. The pets should stay home until the mob is positioned. (and I wouldn't allow them in a lot of cases unless the caster has the pet hold AA)

3. have everyone practice controlling the push on easy mobs by; (1) pushing in a specific direction, and (2) fighting at max range to minimize push. I'd also combine this with controlling a mobs location by: (1) pushing in a circle, (2) pushing laterally and flipping to keep it where you want it, and (3) pushing the mob in a corner (you facing out...everyone else on the mobs back.)

4. get people used to always waiting for your assist call by intentionally waiting longer than normal (or calling very quickly) when fighting trash mobs.

5. watch for the enrage call...nothing more embarrassing then killing yourself on the mob when it enrages, then listening to the ensuing wipe... (been there, done that :p )

6. when you pick a boss to target, search on this board, monkly business, allakazam, etc. on the target name and see if there are tactics articles/posts.

Good luck!!

positioning is huge.

There are a lot of methods, but when you cant corner pin, its your job to keep the mob were rogues can backstab and rangers have LoS.

My method is to drill it into the head of all melee and pet casters that they need to be push the mob INTO me. directly behind or a ~15 degree either way from the rear. this allows me to simply sidestep to correct rotation, and every once in a while call "SWITCH" and turn the mob 180. in this way i can march the fight back and forth along a line keeping all the arrows, bolt spells, and rogues landing on the target.

Using the target circle helps too. download or creat one that works for you, but i find this a great help for mobs that leap or fly, cuz i can see where the mob is actually centered on the ground at any given time.

Also when initially engaging and or jumping in when a tank dies, make sure the rotation is landing on you before you taunt.
watch for debuff messages and as soon as they go , hit taunt (then burn AE when taunt fails).

always know the encounter, and have defensive or evasive (depending on the mob) ready to go, and engage the disc right before you grab aggro. When a mob can 1-round you and you forget to go defensive before you engage, it can mean the whole fight.

another great thing for high end tanking is a LDoN spell from guk called Bulwark of Vie. This is a 1600hp max, 10% damage absorb cleric spell. This thing is amazing. make sure you have BoV and DI both on you, and have a cleric refresh them as they drop during the fight. Anyone not using BoV in their raid encounters should go power their clerics thru guk. i believe its like 640 or so points.
