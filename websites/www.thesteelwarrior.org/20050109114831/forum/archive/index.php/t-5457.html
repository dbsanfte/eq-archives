They suck because they are way too restricted, and in many ways. To name a few:

- Get more than 36 people showing on at raid time? Too bad, some of you have sit out

- Get less than 6 clerics showing up? Too bad, come back another day

- Cannot replace, nor add people along the way, thus everyone have to commit for 3-4 hours. This is very bad.

- Deathcount = teh suck. Beating them within the time limit is enough of a challenge.

I doubt anyone could disagree with the above. Further problems with these raids(more open to debate):

- Retry on failure is too long. When you lose a raid due to deathcount or timeout or whatever, you should be able to come back the next day and try again.

- Deathcount + the long retry time + the less than steller rewards = Very poor reward/risk ratio.

- Several of these raids have what I would call "crunch" points, or places where one mistake can cause a critical fail. While this might be fun for the gamedesigner point of view, from a player point of view it makes for a very frustrating experience. Example=the Everfrost raid where you have to protect the corpse of some NPC. This corpse will be ripped to shreds by an attacking wave of NPCs in 1 minute. Fail to stop them=get ported out=wasted 3hours+wait 5 days=#¤&¤#%

- And finally, yes IMO most of the PoTime level raids needs a slight downwards tuning. The rewards simply do not justify making them so extremely demanding as they are. I could understand it if the rewards were better than PoTime, but they are not. Therefore it makes no sense to have PoTime being _much_ easier to beat than LDON raids.

If the designers would look at a few of the points above and make some adjustments, they could easily make LDON a much nicer and more fun experience for the player. Because the raids themselves are OK imo, I had fun the first few times on them all. But the parameters(deathcounts/retry times/difficulty level etc) desperately needs finetuning.
