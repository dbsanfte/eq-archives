"When is a warrior able to Quest for his next set of armor?"

There is a set of warrior only armor quested through the Twilight Sea in Luclin. It's called Soldier's Armor. The quest pieces are all tradable but many pieces are annoyingly hard to get due to the spawn cycles of certain Luclin mobs.

The armor is roughly equivalent to Crafted, except it probably has a few more HPs due to having more warrior centric stats (read= STA) overall than Crafted.

A lot of warriors do not deem this armor worth the effort because many of the mobs are hard to find, and hard to kill at the levels you would be running around in Crafted.

The next step up would be Champion's Armor, given through the dwarves in Thurgadin after you collect gems in Velious and corroded armor drops in Kael Drakkel.

Then Skyshrine armor quests.

Then Kael Armor quests.

Then Ornate.

Then Elemental.

This only refers to warrioir "quested" armors.

ALSO consider that there are levels of PURCHASABLE armors from the Adventure Merchants, and the 760 point armor pieces are quite good when compared to Velious quested armors.

And also consider that there are sets of diety specific plate mail armors that tradeskillers make, if you're looking to get armor upgrades and/or collect armor sets.

I would tend to avoid burning faction just yet in skyshine.

the path of least resistance is to get a few levels and start kael mini-raids. the arena is good for all armor quest drops and great exp at your level. the armor drops are relatively slow. at some point, you can also do the manor house in kael. you trigger a little ring and plate drops at a high percentage. farm the quest gems in SG and in velks. all good progression at this point. grab your friends, schedule a weekly min-raid, lots of fun for the whole family.

alternatively, you have the option to get cultural smithed armor. this is race/deity dependant so I suggest a little research on eq traders for stats and components. to pursue this path, you will want to farm the components and find a friendly smith who is willing to make the armor. this stuff is trivial > 250 skill so you really want a GM smith. Some smiths who are skilling up > 218 <250 will do free combines if you provide the materials. remember that there will be a high failure rate. find a smith chat with them work something out. Regardless of deity, bracers are probably the most worthwhile acquisition and also the cheapest to make from the smithing perspective.

i wouldnt set my sights way beyond thurg quest just yet.
