Brabble
10-29-2004, 09:25 AM
Yes, you holding aggro on tough mobs is your job, and by extension, the job of your group/raid/guildmates.
Of course there's nothing wrong with you holding aggro on trash mobs that you're trying to steamroll, either, but really you're just trying to get them dead fast. Pallies and SKs can generally take the hits from these mobs and they can ramp up aggro early, so if they gank you, all it means is that other dps folks can crank the damage faster. That's not a problem.
Now, if your casters are ganking aggro, trash or boss, that's a problem, because the increased damage on the mob is not worth the increased risk of caster splat.
So there are two tracks here: you maxing your aggro through weapons/AAs/augs/other little warrior tricks, and the group/raid in general learning how to modify their aggro generation according to the situation so as to max damage within exceptable levels of risk.
There are a few squishy elements to maxing your own aggro, but in general the ability of everyone to play efficiently is much more fluid and harder to get down to "perfection." Since the power of everyone will change over time, this is something that has to be an ongoing process for any guild/group that plays regularly together.

WA5 will fix your problems, regardless of what is in mainhand or offhand.

However, your Bloodfrenzy will gain a higher benefit from WA5 than the epic will.

Base proc rate:
Procratemod on BF is 55 = 3.1 Procs per minute mainhand
Procratemod on Epic is 0 = 2 procs per minute mainhand

Add WA5 at 50%:
BF = 4.65 procs per minute, an increase of 1.55 ppm
Epic = 3 procs per minute, an increase of 1 ppm

(Note: if you keep the augs the way they are now, the epic will win out. If you plan to mainhand the BF, move one of the A3 augs over.)
