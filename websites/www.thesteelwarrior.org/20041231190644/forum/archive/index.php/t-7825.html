Based on the best data available on AC and damage mitigation, which is the various parses shown by Brael throughout GoD, the following hypothesis can be made which seems to be supported.

AC provides a "range of linearity" in which the value of AC at the low end has no real advantage based on mob atk. Once in the range of "effective linearity" the advantage to AC seems to follow a very consistent rise, until we assume it eventually levels off (this is semi-confirmed by Kahvok's post confirming the existence of a 'softcap', which has yet to be found, even up to the 3kAC levels.

Hitpoints are only a valid factor in mitigation when they are completely renewed at a given interval, or on the very rare instances when you are "purpled". The value of additional hitpoints declined substantially when the Complete heal spell had a cap placed on it. The value of hitpoints declined even more substantially when faster, incomplete heals such as holy light became the order of the day. The value of 200 hitpoints that are not constantly refreshed becomes much lower than a scaleable benefit that adjusts itself with a mob's DI number.

Let me give an example.

If the average DI goes down by say... 0.2 with 36AC, then with a mob with a 50 DI (quite common in GoD), you save 2.5 points average per hit, but what's most important is that mob damage doesn't go down by 2.5 point intervals but by DI=1 intervals. Any net gain tends to mean that you take a few 50 point less hits, which pays off over time. As the DI figure goes up, the benefit of AC goes up as well. Hitpoints remain a constant, and unless they're constantly refreshed, their value is that of a "reserve". While a 'reserve" is certianly valuable, at the point where we're referring to, which is GoD content, Vxed+ in particular, there is a reasonable assumption that with the gear, the hitpoints on gear are already factored in.

If I give myself an additional 200 hitpoints and buff to 11,800hp instead of 12,000 hitpoints, that's a 1.7% increase that doesnt' scale, that isn't "always on" (in other words, I don't always use those 200hp unless I come within 200hp of death, which rarely if ever happens) and which dont' necessarily get refreshed when healed, EVEN WITH complete heal. If I give myself 36AC, it's "always on", the benefit is not one that relies upon refreshment to see any value, and it scales with the DI number, providing greater benefit as the mob's DI number increases.

Putting a "AC=X hitpoints" number onto anything is not a wise choice, because there's too many variables in too many situations to make that meaningful. Doing an "AC= X hitpoints" has always been foolish, but assuming that you did so, it is simply crazy to apply velious era calculations (1AC = 6hp) to today's EQ when exp mobs have DI numbers that eclipse raid mobs in that expansion.
