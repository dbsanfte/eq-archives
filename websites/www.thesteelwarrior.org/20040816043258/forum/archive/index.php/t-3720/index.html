This is not an unusual perspective. And I won't even tell you you're wrong, but consider the following. Currently 2 out of 3 tanks have a fairly significant aggro lock early in an encounter. The 3rd does not. Does it make sense that 2 should have such an advantage over the third barring a counter balancing advantage of equal significance? I think we would probably agree it doesn't. And that counter balancing advantage argueably doesn't exist for the majority of experience group scenarios, at least not to a sufficient degree.

So we can either reduce the two high aggro tanks aggro generation to a lower level, which seems to be your inclination, or increase the third's to something closer to the other two's. Now, SoE has recently finished up with a pretty major caster balance program, correct? And in this they granted the casters a great deal more ability to utilize their mana for longer periods and with greater impact. If they proceed to reduce the aggro generation of tanks to the same low (read Warrior like) level, what do the casters do with all this new ability they've been given? They can't use it to maximum effectiveness, given the reduced aggro control. So they get frustrated knowing that their character is being systematically gimped whenever it groups with one of the plate tanks.

Does this sound like a scenario whereby tanks in general will get more groups? Or does this sound like a scenario where casters will find ways to utilize their fullest potential without the involvement of plate tanks at all? Me, knowing how ingenious the human mind can be when it want's to be, expect the latter scenario to be more likely than the former. Casters won't just hold back more with all plate tanks and learn aggro control. Instead they'll find ways to bypass the need for tanks altogether.

And we'll be able to welcome all plate tanks to the LFG queue at that point.

It is safe to say that absolutely EVERYONE agrees that the ability to generate aggro as a knight far exceeds that of the war. Here is where the arguments start...knights have closed the gap on the pure tanking ability (in terms of hp/ac/mitigation - excluding "defensive") to such a point that the Warrior is the last alternative in most cases for the "tank" role in a group.

Some warriors want more hp/ac/mitigation so that the gap between them and knights parallels the "aggro generation" gap that exists.

Some warriors want more aggro so that the gap between them and knights parallels the "ability to tank well" gap.

Some warriors feel that doing either of the above will unbalance the end game encounters and, therefore, a nerf is necessary to bring the two in line.

Some warriors just want some newer cool shit in terms of skills and innate abilities to play with to make them again a unique class capable of a distinctive role besides the end encounter defensive MT.

The Warrior on the other hand has a much harder time attaining and KEEPING aggro. It is my firm opinion that, from playing both classes, that the only thing needed to really balance Melee and put them in their proper roles is to increase the taunt ability of the WAR. The Paladin, although less hitpoints in many cases, is the defensive tank. They should be the secondary tank in most situations (I think Undead would be the sole difference). The Warrior is a class designed to be able to take damage and run at the forefront of the armies. They should be able to keep the aggro better and manage it.
