Just the opinion of a 65 Shaman that has grouped with the 65 warriors in the guild many times... don't touch agi AA. Even tho it has an effect on endurance, having max Agi won't really do anything for you. As a shaman I never cast agi unless asked for and I have the mana. Tho I also always drop Ferine Avatar on the main tank, off tank and the DPS melee's in that order (in regular XP groups anyway). FA adds 140 str, dex, agi, and attack so that maxes most melee's stats even without the Str and Dex from Focus.

You have to have 6 general AA's before you can move into your more important ones and Run3 is the best of them IMO. Every class should get it first. Regen3 certainly never hurts and is probably the second best one to get, perhaps STAMINA instead of regen as you won't always be grouped with someone that can buff it. As for if you can tank with your gear... it's hard for me to say as my shaman doesn't main heal in bot unless the cleric/druid is afk or they went down. At least I try not to have to. Most likely you'll be able to tank (having a good slower will help a lot, whether it's shaman, chanter or even beastlord) and will only get better as you gain some of those AA's. Don't shrug off PoV AA xp either. If you limit yourself to BoT only, you might have tough time finding groups sometimes. XP at the wall camps in PoV is still good.

By the looks of it you shouldn't have a problem in BoT groups. I'd suggest avoiding South wing if possible, however, due to the random procs of 500 damage the giants there throw off (obviously a few badly times ones can put you down quick, before your healer notices what's happening). On the otherhand, if you're group is strong enough (I.E. They know how to play their classes, not necessarily know the zone inside and out) you should be fine. I'd HIGHLY suggest getting a group to do the BoT Courtyard. The mobs there are basically wuss (hit for 550ish, half of them are melee half are clerics) and you can nearly single pull em all with a bow/whack on the head. Additionally, because they're so close together it's a very good spot for fast exp as you just rotate around in a cycle. Find a pattern and go to town. It's highly unlikely you'll clear faster than repops unless you have a CoD charming Enchanter with you or a great deal of DPS elsewhere. And you don't have to worry about trains or adds or any of that other crazy stuff. If you do go to the Courtyard, however, I suggest moving clockwise, and keep in mind if you agro mobs through the walls, they will only go through the center opening if they're moving from section to section in a clockwise direction (if counter-clockwise, they fly all the way to the middle and will agro the four gargoyles, which can be difficult if you're not prepared).

As for your gear, it looks quite good, and I'm guessing you probably get around 6970 HPs fully buffed? Chances are you will get into groups with only a Cleric for HP buffs, so if no shaman I highly suggest you bring a few hundred plat with you, and go bum a focus and stamina off a nearby shaman (and brell's if a pally is around). That'll give you an extra 1200 HP over just Virtue, which makes a world of difference in BoT in my opinion.

Oh yes, and Run 3 is certainly the best buy for the first tier of AAs I believe. Once you get used to a character with Run 3 (or now Run 5) and then play a character without, it boggles your mind how slow they feel (and are).
