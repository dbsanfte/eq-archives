to my way of thinking, there's a qualitative difference between a guy who agrees to group, shows up with /lfg over his head, then suddenly says "gotta go" and disbands--and someone who tells us he's going to want to do something else pretty soon, and when that opportunity develops he stays long enough for a replacement to be recruited.
The first guy is likely at any second to gimp my experience and that of the rest of the group. He's wasting my time, and he's doing it because he thinks his time is more important than five other peoples' time.
The second guy, though, is just in my group because we're handy to him and he's handy for us, and his offer to stay until the replacement gets here means we're in no way gimped by his l33t offer somewhere else. If it's a pickup group, that's what happens.
I can't tell you how much time I've seen wasted by almost full groups waiting for the perfect class to arrive. half the time the "perfect" class guy turns out not to be able to play very well. Or he says he's coming but 30 minutes later you're still waiting. Or he shows up in 20 minutes with /lfg over his head. Bah.
