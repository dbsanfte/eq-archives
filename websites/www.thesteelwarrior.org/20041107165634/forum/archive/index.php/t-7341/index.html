I read the latest articles in PC Gamer on EQ2 and WoW. All the stuff I don't like about EQ is still there. Unless the coh expansions are totally lacking I aint going back to loot-centric crap combat anytime soon.

You know, at one time I didn't mind the loot-centric concept too awfully much. Yeah, there were degrees of gear, but the differences between a mediocre geared character and a uber geared character weren't huge. Yes, the uber geared character could take a bit more damage, could deal a bit more damage, and was generally a bit more efficient. But the mediocre geared character was still at least functional in 90% of the content they were ever likely to see. While it was an advantage to be uber geared, it wasn't a huge draw back to not be. But not anymore. Each and every expansion SoE has made gear that is more uber than the last expansion. While the mediocre geared character has basically stood still for at least 3 expansions. There are huge chunks of content that even if the mediocre geared character managed to gain access to they wouldn't be able to function in acceptably. This is most particularly true for tanks.

Now why does SoE seem to think it's okay to essentially dead end the gear availability of an entire segment of the player base while feeding another segment of the player base better and better gear each and every expansion? And why do they think that the player base won't realize that this is happening? And why do they think that the section of the player base unwilling or unable to be their raid slaves will be happy with paying for expansions where huge chunks of content are either unavailable to them because of flag restrictions or because of gear/AA requirements?

It's completely unrealistic on their part. Almost to the point of absurdity. Yet there we are. And I don't think SoE is going to realize the error of their ways until or unless the game collapses around their ears. I really don't. Remember how in PoP there was such a big deal made when they went through and removed a lot of the flag requirements for a large number of zones? About how the uber geared whined and cried about how "their" zones would now be over run by the idiot lowbies and the undergeared who shouldn't be there? It seems as though they felt that by their being willing to be SoE's raid slaves they were entitled to exclusive content that should not be shared. It wasn't only that they should have gear that made them superior in all ways, but in addition that they'd earned the right to have exclusive access to a large chunk of the expansion, even though everyone, uber and mediocre alike, were charged the same price for it. Yet SoE seems to have taken this concept to heart and designed the last 3 out of 5 expansions (assuming OoW follows the same path as GoD) specifically with the concept of continuing to overly reward the raid slave uber players while denying the mediocre players progress except at becoming their group slaves in LDoN.

It isn't so much loot that has ruined EQ, it is specifically SoE's approach to loot and the willingness to overly reward raid slave ubers while under rewarding or effectively NOT rewarding the run of the mill player. It's gotten to the point that if you aren't a raid slave, there's no point in playing because there's no path of progression that doesn't tie you to literally hundreds of missions in LDoN.
