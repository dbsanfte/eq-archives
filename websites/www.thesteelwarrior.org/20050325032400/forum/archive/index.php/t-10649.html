I can't speak for Krun, but I can speak for my experience in Marudon/Zul Farrak/Temple of Atal'Hakkar. I think the word is efficiency.

In an instance the goal is to maximize kill rate while minimizing risk. Kill rate is not determined by how quickly your group kills a single mob or cluster of mobs, but rather by how many mobs you have to kill before you have downtime to sit and drink. Usually the one person in the group who's mana determines this is the priest.

A protection spec tank boosts group efficiency by soaking up damage more effectively and by controling agro more reliably. Better mitigation = less heals needed on the warrior. More agro control = less emergency heals tossed on the DPS classes, or less chance of the priest getting mobbed.

The low hanging fruit in protection spec can help with this -- shield spec, toughness, maybe anticipation. The better skills, however are higher up in the tree. Defiance, improved revenge, concussive blow (yes many bosses are immune, but you have to clear a dungeon to get to the bosses).

I've not done the highest instances yet, but I have done runs where I was main tank (protection spec) and others where a higher level arms-spec was main tank. My regular priest could surely tell a difference (and now she insists I tank regardless of who else we have in the group).

It isn't a question of whether an arms-spec warrior CAN tank these zones. It is a question of how do you maximize the efficiency of a group, and in general that is better served by soaking up damage well and controlling agro with certainty rather than by adding a modest amount to overall group DPS.
