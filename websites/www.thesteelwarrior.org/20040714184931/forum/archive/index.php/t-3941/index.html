Firstly, give warriors a 1.4 ratio to HP on worn items. Before AA adjustments.

Basically if your item says 100hp then a paladin gains 100hp, a shadowknight gains 100hp, a monk gains 100hp and a warrior gains 140hp.

Most warriors I have seen at the highest end, are wearing 3k from +HP items.

This would be an improvement of roughly 1500hp on top of what you already have, which would then be adjusted by AA.

Two, do the same for AC, I am not sure of your base line, so I cannot tell you what the figure should be, I can only say that around 2000ac at the highest level unbuffed is normal, and it should be nearer to 2200ac unbuffed.

Also need to be sure that there is no AC Softcap that would negate any raise in AC, it must have an impact.

Ok, lets say these were implemented.

What tanks do we have?

1, Warrior.
Highest overall HP, highest Overall AC, No Utility. Taunt from Key and weapons only. (excluding right clickies which are generally saved for raids)

2, Paladin
Closest to Warrior HP, not quite so close on AC, HP Buffs, Stun, Group heals and Snap aggro.

3, ShadowKnight
Still quite close to Warrior HP, no quite so close on AC, ATK Buffs, Snare, Mana Infusion and Snap aggro.

4, Monks
Lowest Overall HP, Lowest Overall AC. In the region of 4k less HP, and 600ac less AC at the very highest end when compared to a similar warrior. No Utility, No taunt other then from weapon proc's/DPS.

Is the monk a threat to the warrior in their capacity as a tank still?

Yes Knights are still a major threat, but is the Monk a threat to your role as Main Tank either in adventures/groups or in Raid situations?

Even with the same levels of mitigation, with 600ac seperating us, that is quite a substanstial difference, and will equate into quite a difference in damage taken ingame.

With 4k less unbuffed HP, thats a very large drop off. It would make the Warrior a by far more efficient tank for almost all situations.

What would it mean to me as a monk?

I could do what i have done for 3 years. Tank. I could be a viable alternative...if the group could handle it.

I still won't be tanking any raid level mobs.

What else does this mean.

Well.

With an increase in HP and AC as large as this needs to be.

I think an increase in Mob DPS would also need to be taken into account. I am not talking your average XP trash mob, I am talking about raid level content.

With a larger AC to mitigate the damage better, a larger HP to survive a larger single round of combat, a raid encounter mob could have his DPS raised accordingly to keep level of difficulty as it is.
