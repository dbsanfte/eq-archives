Pretty much, yes. As long as the entire screen updates, you're fine... but if you play like a racing game where there's always a speedometer in the exact same spot, etc, there is a risk of burn-in during long sessions. I've seen projection TVs that have been used for heavy gaming marathons with no issues, and I've seen TVs that get burn-in just from the black boxes around widescreen DVDs after a month of use. It's not a garaunteed "THIS WILL RUIN YOUR TELEVISION," but the risk is there.

And that's only for rear-projection TVs... the newer LCD and DLP models don't have the same type of problem.

If its a new tv turn the contrast all the way down and leave it on for a week. When you aren't using it make sure to change it to a channel without a news ticker or logo in the corner.

This will "break in the screen" helping to prevent future burn in from high contrast viewing/gaming.
