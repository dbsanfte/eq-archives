aary is the test to see if your guild has the skill to get in and isnt a bunch of zerging noobs. most of the big fights is placing your casters so they are hidden from AEs while keeping your tank positioned to he's in range of the healers. a few of the mobs you'll need to learn push control and wall pinning. also, you want to have some one calling out the AEs for the casters to run in, cast, and hide. usually you have about 10 seconds to do this once an AE is fired. you can have 1 of the tanks or othe melee people call for the casters to go. another little trick is to have the melees back out at 50% health to the spot healers and call for a spot heal, druids usually work for this. you dont want your healers running out there and taking AE damage. then they have to burn mana healing them selves. bards and pallies are probalby the most important classes for dealing with AEs. try to get a bard in the MT group and 1 pally in as many of the melee heavy groups possible.
vyemm is probably the only exception since there is no place to hide, but he's not that bad anymore. just use fast heals instead of CH.
since no one mentioned the flurry drakes, killing them can some times be harder than the named mobs. you'll need a 3 to 5 warrior defensive chain. come to think of it, the flurries should be alot easier now with the disc change, you can now open up with furious disc while the debuff chain gets them slowed. basicly, you tell teh raid, warrior_01 is tanking, monk pulls in a flurry, raid kills it, and then you tell the raid, warrior_02 is the next tank, and so on.
just in case you didnt learn this in HoT, make sure everyone is standing when mobs are inc.

Flurry drakes are:

Any NAMED ROAMING drake, such as Cyndor Lightningwing (the black named drakes guarding the Lord/Lady dragons are pets, they are NOT flurry drakes), and ANY drake that has a capitalized defender name such as "An Onyx Defender" "A Emerald Defender", etc... if the word defender is there but not capitalized its is not a flurry drake. Blue ones *will* gate so mana sieve them, I don't recally if any new stuns work on them, but I don't believe they do.

Flurry drakes hit for 750, with low delay and flurry a lot (which is why they have the name flurry drakes).. They are slowable and have a lot of HP compared to the other drakes. Have your debuffers land every attack debuff they can on them, will make them nearly trivial at your level. However, even slowed they pump out quite a bit of DPS.
