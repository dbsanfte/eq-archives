I know how you feel Isk. As much as I love the fact that WoW is very solo-friendly and so casual, I do understand there is a benefit to harder progression. Unfortunately, it's not very easy for a computer game to reward people based on skill while also giving a raid-game reward.

Raids, due to the amount of people present, usually boil down each class to their place in the holy trio of MMOGs (Tank, Healer, DPS). And with that large amount of people, there is either going to be plenty of room for error where skill isn't needed as much, or every person is going to have to be at their best 100% of the time for you to succeed. This is one main reason I fail to be impressed by the idea of a raiding-only end-game. I don't believe raids can ever be balanced where it isn't either a cake walk due to the room for error or require a "hardcore only need apply" club to succeed because of no room for error. As you take away from one side, you start giving to the other, and I personally don't think a middle-ground actually exists.

One area of frustration I've noticed many people having is in the aspect of skill. You've said so yourself, it is completely frustrating to have a situation where a low-skilled player is being rewarded the same as a highly-skilled player because the raids are so easy that it's really more time-spent-based instead of skill-based. Reminds me somewhat of the arguments between raiders and non-raiders in EQ, where non-raiders wanted to be rewarded for their time...just like raiders were. I knew many raiders who were less skilled as clerics than I was, but they had high end gear because they were willing to tag along on raids and be reduced to a CH-bot.

Certainly a "hardcore only need apply" will weed out the lesser skilled and reward the higher skilled for their efforts, but this setup almost always requires a large amount of time spent as well. Any raid leader will know that being able to gather 24+ high skilled people is very hard. Heck, being able to keep track of a large number of people is difficult by itself, although skilled players make things easier in this regard. It seems that large raids and long periods of gaming go hand-in-hand. What guild wants to gather 40-60 people for a raid, for it to simply be over in an hour or 2 (other than individual players who need a hand in a step of a quest)? They'll take that large group of people and do several targets, making it a long raid.

I'm one of those people who believe that, overall, single-group encounters are just as difficult as raid-group encounters (sometimes more) due to a lack of numbers/resources to draw from.

Is it possible to have a system where skill is the major factor in being rewarded instead of merely time spent playing?

The tibermaw clan will reward you with new legs if you exterminate the infected enemy. Slay 2,000 of the enemy and you will shift the balance of power and be rewarded.


Now THAT I like! :D Going after single targets with a group of 40 people just seems cheap, and lacking skill. I prefer odds closer to 1 to 1 than 40 to 1...unless that's 40 enemies to 1 player. :D

I think it is possible to reward skill of the players instead of time spent, but developers just can't seem to come up with ways to achieve it.
