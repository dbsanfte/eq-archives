Niafix
If you haven't figured out why you're falling through the wall, it's because monsters have a bit of pushback when they melee, just like a PC, and in most cases they do quite a bit more pushback. Well, when you back yourself tight into a corner, this melee pushback will push you through the wall, and make you fall through the world.

Obviously there are 2 simple fixes; The first being not to corner the mob. That's right, you heard it here first kids, not all beastmonsters have to be cornered in order to win. In fact, none do! wooot.
Secondly, if you really do want to corner the beastmonster, you could try backing into a corner, then pressing the forward arrow key, or whatever key you have bound to the forward movement action. In most cases, especially with larger beastmonsters, you can still effectivly "corner" without being completely backed into the corner. It works wonders :)

In most of the corner MT fights I've managed to work corners by doing side strafes out of the corner and then popping myself back in (yes dangerous). This seems to reset the MOBs "center of geometry" back out of the wall so I can position myself between it and the corner.

I suspect the real problem here has to do with collision detection of mobs vs that of players. With the "new" collsion a mob can have its center of geometry more deeply pushed into objects than a player can. Perhaps naively I think this is probably a simple fix in wideing large model mobs center of geometry numbers. This would prevent them from going "farther" into walls than a player. They could also just not allow mobs to be pushed through players (see functionality that keeps a .5 elf from running through a barb PC).


As far as the getting summoned into a wall crap, falling through the world and dying is just plain sloppy work on SoEs part. I think what is happening is that the mob is having its center of geometry actually pushed through the wall object....it then kicks in the code for OOR/Summon...the problem of course being the player then gets summoned into a solid object and then the default code "for return to zone safe spot" routine kicks in. This routine of course forgets about the z axis calculation (which calcs falling damage) and you wind up dead as you are put to the "safe spot" (it gives you the gajillion pts of damage as the z axis differentional calc has a null because it wasnt kicked of legitimately). 3 bad code functions written together kicked off by an untested piece of new functionality.

Any way my 2 coppers on this...I'd hope that Soe has some analysts thinking through this set of events rather carefully.
