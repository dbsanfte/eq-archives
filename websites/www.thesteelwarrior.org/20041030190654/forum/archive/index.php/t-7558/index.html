Because it does only affect bash. Example:

FB working:
[Sun May 16 19:51:48 2004] Your Blackstone Buckler glows red.
[Sun May 16 19:51:48 2004] You strike through your opponent's defenses!
[Sun May 16 19:51:48 2004] You bash Innoruuk for 113 points of damage.

FB on any spell I've cast in months:
[Sun May 16 19:51:05 2004] You begin casting Clinging Darkness.
[Sun May 16 19:51:06 2004] You regain your concentration and continue your casting.
[Sun May 16 19:51:06 2004] Your target is immune to changes in its run speed.
[Sun May 16 19:51:06 2004] Your target resisted the Clinging Darkness spell.
[Sun May 16 19:51:06 2004] Your Grimror's Boots of the Plagues flickers with a pale light.
(note the lack of glowing shield)

It doesn't affect procs because of the way focus items and procs work together. As Sassinak pointed out, a focus item will only affect a proc if you can scribe and cast the spell being procced(proc'd? whatever). For example, when I used to use a Heavy Bludgeoner (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=22889), the proc was affected by my improved damage focus, because Siphon Life is a spell I can cast. Since warriors obviously cannot cast any spells, none of their procs can be focused. Furious Bash used to go off on spells, but it was changed quite a while ago.
