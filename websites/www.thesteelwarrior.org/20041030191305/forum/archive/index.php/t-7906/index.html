Brabble
The reason you see folks like Wandor and Haass pretty consistently maintaining that DW > 2H has to do with maximum aggro generation. To generate maximum hate you need to generate maximum procs and to generate maximum procs you need the maximum number of proc "slots" and to get the maximum number of proc slots you are looking at two 1-hand weapons.
Probably the reason your guild is leery of the GSoT being used for " a long time" at lvl 65 is that as various classes get AAs & better gear their ability to ramp up damage (and generally, the hate they generate) accelerates. The damage/hate that the *other* people in your group can generate is what makes a GSoT in the hands of a lvl 58 or 59 warrior a much better weapon than the same GSoT in the hands of a 200 AA warrior who is grouped with similarly advanced necros, wizards, rangers etc.
But: the GSoT is a very very nice weapon when you can start using it, and for sometime after that. It has a very nice aggro:cost ratio, and that's not insignificant for most 55+ warriors, who in fact need to upgrade all kinds of gear seemingly all at once. This weapon gives you some breathing room in the 2H category for a while.
You can tank with this thing for a good while if you know how to tank. All of the strikingly poor tanks I've seen were poor because of what was going on between their ears, not because of the weapons they held.
It's hard to see why most warriors wouldn't want to have this weapon when they reach the lvl they can use it. Before the GSoT becomes a liability, you'll have access to better weapons. Before your casters start getting dead on a regular basis, you should be able to sense when the GSoT's aggro protential is losing ground to your groupmates' ability to unload on the mob.

I'd like to add some reasoning why I still use the GSoT, as the same reasons may apply to you at some point.

First, I cant buy pricey stuff (10k+), and my opportunities to get upgradeable weapons are infrequent (due to mostly raid drop chances).

Also, since I'm usually 3rd at best in the tanking order for my guild, and the 'chore' of doing all defensive aa's first... I spent some aa's in offensive abilities to make me more dps capable on raids.

Ferocity, Weapon Affinity, Punishing Blade, Tactical Mastery ... these have helped with better procs, not to mention more/better swings in general (which is the basis for aggro generation, to the best of my understanding).

I've been building my aa's more so defensively over time, but I have personally found it more comfortable to avoid excessive ripostes with the 2H, provide better dps on raids, than dual wielding.

Even within my own guild I see that I'm one of the few (if not the only warrior) that leans on the 2H for tanking (or offtanking on raids, depending on mob).

It is all situational and depends on preference. Find out what works best for you.
