High-end PoP itemization seems rather hit and miss. Some items increase the power of the characters massively, while other items intended for characters are laughable. Now that we are seeing some of the end of the expansion loot, I feel compelled to complain.

A few items have a massive and direct increase in caster power.

Wizard Legs have a massive impact on Wizard DPS, which is their only real role in the game at this point. Let's say these increase the ability of what Wizards do by 50%.

Time's Antithesis and Serpent of Vindication also grant an incredible power increase to Shamans and Enchanters. Shamans have an item that allows them to cast slow for no mana cost with the ability to recast it instantly, as opposed to the six second recast on the real spell. Enchanters get a slow they can cast in half the time as the regular spell, once again with instant recast.

Even druids are looking at an item that allows them a mana free version of Swarming Death, a spell that normally costs 350 mana.

My question is this: Where is the warrior item that increases their power by 50%? What allows us to tank 50% better? Why do we see other classes get massive increases to their overall power and see very minor upgrades to what we do?

Those questions are rhetorical, really. I know why they can't make us 50% better at what we do, as once again the risk of "trivializing the game OMG" is greater when you increase the power of our class. If the designers of the game can not make us significantly better at what we do through itemization, then they need to create another role for us that does allow us to get significant power increases. Otherwise the effort we make to improve our characters is rather meaningless.

And don't try to tell me how great AC/HP are to build up and how wonderful that feeling is. Last time I checked I was #5 on eqrankings for warriors on Hp and honestly, I don't give a shit about HP/AC anymore.

Utility is usefulness. Utility is intrinsic value.

The warrior's utility is his ability to absorb more damage than any other class while holding a mob's attention. This isn't to say that the warrior is perfectly balanced. I'm not saying that the warrior's utility is well designed. But "tanking" is a warrior's utility. It's why, when no warriors are present, raids are called off. I agree with the general feeling of warrior discontent and can understand why many in the class want something more, but you cannnot deny that this game is designed around the warrior and the cleric relationship. Everyone else augments these roles.


The big concern lately is that the knight has become a substitute for a warrior in experience groups (but not on most raids). This is something designers had to struggle with as they were doing it. They didn't want ONE class to be the only viable exp-group healer, so Shaman and Druid core utilities (healing) were augmented. They didn't want warriors to be the only viable tank for experience groups so they augmented knights. This augmentation marginalized the cleric and the warrior in exp-group settings, but they're the undisputed kings of the high-end raid.
