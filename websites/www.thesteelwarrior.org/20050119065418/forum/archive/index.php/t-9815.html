Very few people have tested the actual yak procs versus the anger3 procs, and those that have done so have found anger3 to be marginally better.

As for the arguments that stun > anger3/EB ... you basically have people testing one set of criteria ... assuming that said criteria equals other criteria (or at least behaves similarly) ... then assuming that you can apply the same principles across the board.

IE:

Assume Baze bellow = anger3
Assume Paladin spell aggro = yak proc aggro

Paladin spell aggro > Baze bellow therefore it stands to reason that yak aggro > anger3 aggro.

Here are the problems:
1. Baze bellow has never been conclusively proven to work as well as an anger3 proc ... or even work at all the same, especially considering how much about the ability seems to be unknown.
2. Paladin spell-cast stun aggro has never been proven to be equivalent to proc stun aggro. Before you get your knickers in a twist here, the case-proof earlier stated was the tash orb. Once upon a long time ago tash orb = ench tash for aggro ... people (warriors in particular) duel wielded them for mega aggro. Verant changed the way it worked so if you test a tash stick today vs any ol' enchanter spell you'll see that they are not the same. This at least casts reasonable doubt that there is some disparity btw spell cast and proc. All of this even assumes that all stun spells have equivalent aggro. Perhaps the ykesha proc itself has a different innate aggro component than a paladin/cleric ...? The world will never know.

None of these experiments were testing the procs themselves, but rather similar things compared with other things ... which somehow work out to prove that yak somehow is superior. I find this odd as it contradicts every single real world (fantasy world) encounter i've ever come across. It just plain don't work that way.

The dps/aggro equations we have out there are not perfect, so any calculations involving said equations need to be taken with a grain of salt. They are good equations ... just not definite and/or perfect.

If you want to see for yoursellf a simple test costs no more than 3-4k. Go out and find a buddy warrior ... pull off your augs and slap them on a fine steel weapon. Go fuck around for a few hours and report your findings.

As for stun aggro scaling with levels ... not sure who started this rumor exactly, but i've run the tests both with slowed warriors + weighted axe proc vs proc (for a proc on the first swing of first round) and over time with FS weapons on mobs ranging 55-73+ throughout PoP, GoD, and a few OoW zones. Never did high level mobs react any differently ... and yes some of them were well over 100k hps.

Go test it for yourself and/or pick whatever you want. Don't blindly follow or assume every nugget you find here is truth. Just (for fucks sake) make sure you are testing proc vs proc ... anything else is irrelevant. Until someone somewhere runs a series of tons of tests between the two procs showing yak to be conclusively better, i wouldn't run to the corner store to cash in your anger 3's just yet.
