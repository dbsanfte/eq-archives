Brabble
I think it depends on generally how you're planning to play. If you see yourself ultimately moving into a raiding guild and doing tough content, well, I'd roll a warrior. If you want to tank in groups, and don't mind off tanking on raids, then a knight class is good.
The alt I've put the most time into is my SK. Since I'm quite limited in my play time and in a casual guild, the SK has turned out to be kinda nice. He solos better than my warrior, and that's always handy if you have say, an hour to be online. He also can tank dark blue mobs for any guild group of similar-level characters no problem. It's very very nice to go from a warrior main to a tank class that really has zero problems with aggro and can FD pull. And his pet is amusingly obsequious.
But, when the chips are down, warriors are the better tank. I just wouldn't roll one unless I wanted to group pretty much all the time.
Warriors are expensive to gear up if you are starting all over from scratch. If you have a bankroll, you'll have a much easier time in your mid 40s-mid 50s or so when suddenly you'd like to have haste/droga weapons/cultural-level armor and you can't even solo a hill giant.
I can't speak to the advantages of Paladins, because the only Paladin I have is a mule, lol. They do seem to bring with them some group benefits, and they can be handy in a small guild.

Concerning not choosing an SK because you're not "evil at heart" -- my other alt is a Shaman. He spends some time each week in PoK or newbie zones ninja buffing people (CHA and SoW at the vendors, for example; regular buffs in newbie zones). I don't have a large enough sample size yet (I'd like to get data on the outcome of thousands of buffed characters), but I can tell you that the three classes most likely to thank me politely for buffs in PoK are, in order: SKs, enchanters, clerics.
Dead last? Warriors. I suspect that warriors aren't the rudest class in the game; perhaps they're simply concentrating on not accidentally selling any of the 5-10 back-up weapons they're carrying in their bags, and hence don't notice the SoW & CHA landing, lol.
When my SK alt was in his newbie zone, and a bit later camping lizards in Feerott, he never asked for help from anyone, but on a number of occasions 60+ SKs went out of their way to help him. I don't have numbers on this, but it was markedly the case that SKs were more likely to help than other classes, and that holds true for all of the baby characters/classes I've fiddled with.
That's SKs' dirty little secret: most of them are pretty nice, lol.

Battleblade
I'm not sure I'd make a Warrior if I was rolling up a new character. I'd probably make a Paladin.

For your first 56 levels or so you won't be raiding. Excluding raiding, there isn't a mob in the game that can't be tanked by a Knight. The spellbooks ARE a free bonus, especially up to about level 60 - take the book and say thank you. Heck, Warriors get almost nothing at all until pretty late when they finally get Defensive (Dual Wield? Giggle - in the upper 50's Windblade beats DW droga weapons afaic). Take da book.

SK's have Fear kiting and while it's ok and kinda fun those tools are fundamentally useless after level 60ish. Paladins can solo quite nicely. Early on Pacify is helpful and Root and heals never become useless.

My feeling is that if you are a casual player, a Paladin can't be beat - heals, Res, Root, and buffing does NOT suck, not at all. If you are a hardcore player and intend to raid, it's still hard to do better than securing aggro without having to use special weapons.

Even with the Paladin 1.5 Epic repaired and set to what was intended, mid-level Paladins have a hard hitting (Diamond-tipped War Javalin in quality) epic, Narandi's helm, FT on their ornate mold boots, Mana Preservation on their ornate mold arms (I think), Benevolent Girdle, Blade of Disruption, Muramite's Shield, Dragorn Commoner's Ring, etc.

A raid Paladin at level 60-65 just coming out of Ssra/Seru/VT in combination with OoW drops IMO can not be equalled by either a Warrior or SK in Tanking ability, spells that support that tanking, and equipment that supports both tanking and spells casting. They have soloablity, near group tanking preference, and a solid raid role.

IMO, there's no contest - make a Paladin.

Warriors > Knights in EQ today.

Actually that's not the whole story. "Knight" means "Paladin or Shadowknight" and let's face facts: shadowknights are no longer worth considering in the same category as paladins or warriors. Basically, you take a shadowknight if the content isn't too challenging and you can't find one of the two tank classes you actually want. That's why, for every level 70 SK, there are at least 3 level 70 paladins and at least 3 level 70 warriors.

(Digression: Personally I believe that the reason SK's have fallen so far behind is that they don't have a prominent messageboard community and there are few prominent SK "activists" - while both paladins and warriors have more than their share of advocates. Some would say whiners instead of advocates, but I'm not quite that cynical.)

So let's get real. The competition is between warriors and paladins, shadowknights don't really come into it.

Well, there are three environments to consider: Raids, xp groups, and solo.

In raids, there's no contest. Warrior > paladin, end of story. Raid guilds need about six decently geared warriors, but by and large they can make do with one paladin twink with MGB.

In groups, there's a contest. Paladins can situationally out-tank warriors, depending on the value of stuns in a particular environment, although warriors do undeniably have the edge when the chips are down and the high DPS red-con named gets pulled.

But there's more to the group choice thing than tanking. If paladins and warriors can tank roughly equally in many situations - which is, by and large, close enough to the truth - then the question as to who's best in the exp group tank role becomes, which of the classes does more DPS?

And that's a no-brainer, paladins are one of the lowest DPS classes in the game, while high end warriors are probably somewhat too close to monks.

Soloing, both classes suck so badly it's scary. Easily the worst caster class at soloing is cleric, but clerics can still solo a whole lot better than any tank.

Conclusion: If you're going to make a tank, make a warrior.

This will switch back to pally following the next round of tank class rebalancing, though.

The only real nice thing about Paladin is that they're probably the lowest maintenance class in the game (high HP, can heal self) so if you're throwing a bunch of guys you just recruited to an encounter, a Paladin is most likely to survive the longest given that all the guys you recruited obviously have gear inadequate for the encounter.

But Warrior survivability is also pretty darn high, with extra DPS to boot. A high end Paladin might be able to perform certain things better than a high end Warrior, but none of these are vital roles that the Warrior can't do competently.

That said high end Paladins do stack pretty well on raids filling out the this-and-that roles, but so do Warriors. But when it comes down to it, I'd rather have a Warrior over a Paladin on a raid due to DPS. If I need someone to heal, just replace the War/Paladin with a Cleric instead (assuming you can find one).
