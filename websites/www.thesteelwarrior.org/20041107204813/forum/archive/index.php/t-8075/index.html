most info sites are out of date.

/shield is available at lvl 30 as a normal skill.
to use it : target a friend in need of protection and tape /shield (works great when hotkeyed, think of /assist to swicht rapidly from the mob to your friend and vise versa)
what it does : it cut in half the domage receive by your friend and you get 1.5 time the rest (ie the overall domage is greater : 50% + 75% = 125%).
limitation : it can be used only if both you and your target are not currenctly using a discipline. Morover it has an short duration and a very short radius : you must teach your friend to stay in your melee range if they want you to use your ability !
bonus : it has an aggro built him that help often, but not always, to recover the top postion from the hate list (assuming you were on top before the little caster stealt aggro).

Note : there is AAs that increase both its duration and radius, and that decrease the reuse time.

Widjit,
/shield is sort of a stealth ability. Most of your other innate abilities are automatic (dodge, riposte, etc). You can disarm, but it has essentially no use value against mobs.
The rest of the stuff you'll have to pay attention to have to do with your /discs and your AAs, but there's good info on the warrior specfic elements of those on this site, and you are familiar with the general concepts.
Can't think of any "ninja" abilities like /shield that can slip in under the radar, to answer your second question.
To my knowledge, no one has tested the "+aggro" element of /shield, but it has been widely observed that /shield does appear to pop you to the top of the hate list sometimes (caster gets aggro, you run over and shield caster, mob jumps on you). You can also use it as a kind of gesture at other tanks who gank your aggro when you are supposed to be main tank.
