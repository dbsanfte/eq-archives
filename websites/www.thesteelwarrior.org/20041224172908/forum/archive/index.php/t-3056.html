Before Kunark, Knights and Warriors struggled to complete their planar armor:

Warrior - Indicolite Breastplate +35 AC, +15 Str, +15 Sta, +15 Dex
Paladin - Valorium Chestplate +30 AC, +13 Str, +9 Sta, +9 Wis

In general Warrior armor had better AC/HP's and Paladin armor had WIS.

Twin Y'Kesha's were l33t among Warriors while most Paladins used Ghoulbanes.

By the time Kunark was in full swing Paladins were sporting Deepwater Breastplate +40 AC, +10 Str, +10 Agi, +10 Wis, +50 Mana and a Fiery Avenger

While Warriors proudly wore a Cobalt Breastplate +45 AC, +12 Str, +12 Sta, +12 Dex, +50 HP and weilded a Sebilite Croaking Dirk and Blade of Strategy.

In general "high-level" Warriors had more AC and Hp's than Paladins and owned the first "Hate" weapons in the game.

Paladins had small mana pools and their best heal was Superior Heal.

As a result, of this Warriors became the prefered MT's in raids and groups. Many Paladins who weren't in advantageous guild situations gave up and either retired or made another character. Others toughed it out and accepted an offtank role in raids and largely grouped with friends and guildmates.

Pre-Kunark: Rangers were the cool melee

There was no real advantage to having a warrior over a ranger and the ranger could spirit of wolf you... Weapons were very bad, most armor had no hps.

Kunark: Warriors were the cool melee

Hit point advantages were there and superior AC gave warriors there short day in the sun.

Velious: Things were pretty well in balance IMO

The armors you could get crafted by the giants, dragons and dwarves kept warriors in an advantageous position. SoDs and BoCs allowed warriors some decent agro control.

Luclin: Monks were uber for the first half of it

Monks were soloing like crazy for AA points. Monks could avoid damage like nobodies business until they were nerfed to where they should be and everyone who made a twink monk that later became their primary hold a grudge til this day. Items that were all/all, or melee/all hurts warriors a lot.

PoP: Knights own, Bards are ridiculous for soloing, warriors and monks blow (in general)

In the original game warriors, especially troll and ogre warriors who maxed STA, had a helluva lot more hp than paladins or shadowknights. Something like 2400hp vs. 1800hp with top end gear. Even if the race were the same, the hp gap was pretty big; say, 2200 vs 1800.

While hp were significantly higher, AC was closer: a paladin with his self buffs had about the same AC as a warrior.

In addition to much much higher hp, the warrior did a shitload more damage because dualweilding fast 1-Handers just did a shitload more damage than any 2-Handed weapon.

In ROK, the hp gap came down a bit but warriors still had tons more hp.

For tanking in xp groups and raids warriors were preferred over paladins and SKs.
