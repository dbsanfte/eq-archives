I'm not a raid tank. Much as I'd like to be a raid tank, I don't have time to join a raiding guild. So my AA intentions are a bit non-standard.

Specifically, rather than optimizing for hit points, I'm going to optimize for mitigation. I already have enough hit points that none of the mobs I'm facing can one-round me. And I'd rather take less damage over time, and take it more smoothly, than have more HP to brag about. :-)

So here's what I'll be buying:

CA1/CS1/ND1/PE
CA2/CS2
CA3/CS3
LR5/ID5
ND3
Sturdiness 1
PD3
Sturdiness 2
offensive AA's

Basically, unless you're fighting raid mobs, even against LDoN hard you're better off mitigating more rather than having more HPs to soak unmitigated damage.

And hit points are to a certain extent fungible with gear; mitigation, much less so (that is, it's easier to get 10% more HP from your gear, than to get enough AC to mitigate 10% better).

*shrug* I'm a long way away from any of this, of course; three more AA, and I'll have the first line up there finished out. But this is my plan.

I wouldn't have aug'ed Gaudralek in the first place.

It's significantly less DPS (and melee-damage-hate) than a GSot4W, which should be trivial for your guild to acquire. It's effectively the same DPS as the GSoT, and worse melee-damage-hate; and the GSoT's stun-plus-dd proc is dramatically better for hate than Gaudralek's dd-only proc.

A case can be made that the 40 AC and 15 HP make it a better tanking weapon than the GSoT (or 50 AC more and 25 HP less than the GSot4W), but I'd argue that the significantly greater delay and weaker aggro make it too much less effective for holding the mob on the MT.

Any particular reason why your guild hasn't obtained a GSot4W for your MT to put that Anger III into?
