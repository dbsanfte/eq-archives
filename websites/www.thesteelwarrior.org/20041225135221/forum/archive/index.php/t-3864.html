I play a SK as my main and I have to say, we have to try hard to not generate a lot of agro. I agree with the posts that the SK in your group should be pulling with cancel magic, arrows, or just straight proximity. If he is using ANYTHING else (taps, dots, debuffs, whatever), no warrior with comparable gear is ever going to hold consistant agro against him. Also, he should ALWAYS feign just before telling you to tag the mob with your bow. He should then stay down until you have landed a couple rounds of melee on the mob. After that, you both need to use proximity to control agro and he shouldn't cast or taunt for the remainder of the fight.

Also, he was using an ebon mace, which is a 19/24 one hander. That alone generates a fair amount of dps agro.

I don't want to start a holy war but agro is the defining characteristic of the SK class (hit points are the warrior's defining characteristic...whether SOE has balanced that correctly is not something I want to talk about, since I'm on a warrior board, hehehe). Anytime a warrior is trying to hold agro over a SK, you are trying to do something that he is naturally better at so you both need to use every trick you have to make it work.

I also play an SK, and it is most likely that he was pulling with a snare or a tap, which can generate some good initial aggro. However, he needs to learn to know when to take aggro and when not to. A well played SK knows these things, and has just as many ways to clear his aggro as he has to gain it. Casting a spell from the Voice line will increase the aggro that the warrior gets for each action taken against the mob, and feign death is always the fastest and easiest way to clear aggro and let the MT take it over. I know when I'm not MT it's not just the MT's job, but also my job as puller (when I am pulling) to make sure that he gains aggro and I lose it as soon as I get the mob to camp. In certain cases (IE in places like BoT where slows and such have to be quickly placed) when I am pulling, I will hold aggro until all necessary spells are in place (usually around 10 -15 seconds, which takes on most occaisions one complete heal to me) while the MT builds up aggro greater than the casters will have, then FD to clear mine and allow him to take over. But needless to say (I apologize for my rambling) he was doing something very wrong in the case you were involved in, and needs to rethink how he plays his character, so don't worry about what you were doing, I'm sure you would have held aggro flawlessly paired with a well played SK (But that comes with time). Good luck to you in the future, and keep on tanking.
