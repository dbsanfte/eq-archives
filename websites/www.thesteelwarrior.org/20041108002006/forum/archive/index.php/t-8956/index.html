Good question, Haas.

My wife and I play EQ ~4 hours a night most days of the week. When we aren't online, we often spend some time reading about the game and our classes on various boards. So, in terms of the time we invest in the game, there's nothing casual at all about our EQ experience. I assume that's the point of Baadan's "hours played" question.

Yet, despite the significant time investment, we still consider ourselves "casual" players. So, the defining quality of "casual-ness' isn't the amount of time you put into the game.

In my and my wife's case, what makes us 'casual' players is the fact that we're not under anybody else's orders. Nobody tells us where to be at a certain time on a certain day, or how many and which AA's we must have by a certain deadline. Nobody is laying out a progression for us to follow. If we aren't enjoying some zone or encounter, we just walk away from it. That's the way we want to play, and the price for that freedom is the gear and zones we have access to.

Now, choosing to be a casual player doesn't mean you aren't devoted to improving your character. I grind AA's and pursue gear just as rabidly as my friends who belong to raiding guilds. The difference is that I understand that I'm pursuing a different set of rewards.

Sure, there aren't raids happening all the time that I'm on, but if I am on, and a raid is happening, and I feel like going, then I'll go; but if there isn't one, no biggy, I'll spend awhile looking for/forming a group to do a hard LDoN or Sol Ro or GoD or something, or, <gasp> perhaps idly sit around talking with friends or discussing something in a serverwide channel.

The divider, I think, between a casual player and a non-raider is Sol Ro Tower access. With this, one can no longer be considered a non-raider because of the amount of kills needed to get here. To become a casual raider and not a casual player one gets EP access. Any progression further than this and you are not casually playing EQ.

The real test of whether or not you fit into this thread is really whether or not you follow a mandated schedule or play at your own whims and advance because a raid was happening and you felt like going, not because your guild says that RZtW is going down at 6:00pm EST and you are to be online from 5:30 to 8:00 that night.
