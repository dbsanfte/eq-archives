Mobs in the deep are all dark blue to 62 (as well as 56), hit in the 150s, no other abilities (except for a few casters).

Second piece of information:

I got to play warrior in Droga Saturday. What you did is basically piggy back agro off the enchanter.
The idea isnt really that new as it rather commonly used when several tank classes are in a group.
Works usually very well for me when paladin's stun and i taunt off them (usually no agro problem for that fight).
There are exceptions of course like a wizard landing a 6k+ crit right at the beginning but thats rare enough and if he is bright he will wait 6 seconds after i got agro assuming my taunt is up then.
There are some problems with the concept though.

1. Mobs that run faster than PC's or later on run 3.
I think everyone loves NPC's using spirit of the wolf while PC's cant (and thats not that rare).

2. Caster type mobs
Dont you love getting dotted, snared or rooted on pulls ?

3. Mobs with high resistance
Try pulling in Plane of earth for example, a beastlord (we had no other slower and no debuffer either at least at first) had to try slow up to 8 times til it sticked.
Our chanters were complaining a lot about resisted slows as well.

If you taunt off the chanter you will have a sure agro compared to the rest of the group except the chanter who will have to try slow again.

I have basically no problems with agro in Tier 1-3 or sol ro.
Elementals are another matter though as described earlier but its depending on your group setup.

If you watch your chat spat closely for the slow, tash, malo messages, you can make almost certain you have and keep agro.
What you cannot see though is resists and thats where warriors usually have problems.
