This is taken from future plans at the eqlive websight

We're still planning to take a look at improving the damage capabilities of melee classes, as they've fallen behind their magic-using counterparts at the very high end of the game. Finding the proper solutions for this situation will take some time. Some options being considered are:

Improve melee damage-increasing disciplines
Improve Planes of Power melee weapons

At the "very high end of the game" every single melee is at their innate ATK cap. It seems like it would be easier to raise the atk cap then it would be to revamp every weapon in the database.

1) It would also make some of the items droping have more value.

2) This would go a long way to corrrecting the obvious problem with elemental melee armor.

3) Could acheive their origional goal of improving melee damage

I'm unsure if archery damage from rangers would need to be adjusted but aside from that I'd much rather have this solution then the ones suggested.

Torrin
Yes, I hear that sometimes. My wizards suck. Of course, those people have yet to ever show me any parses.

DPS readings for casters is worthless without considering duration. I can wait until a mob is 50%, cast strike of solusek, then cast another immediately, and have parsers read 700 DPS. In order to accurately measure caster contribution, you need to look at total sum damages.

However, for a moment, lets just pretend nukes have no aggro (hah) and chain cast Strike of Solusek. Assuming focus speeding thought, maximum spell haste AAs, zero latency (impossible), no resists (laugh), and an average nuke of 115% (fury of ro focus), that would be 370 DPS. With full crit AAs, damage increases about another 20%. Thats 444 DPS. Hardly "easily twice the dps of a rogue"

Last night I parsed a rogue over the course of 72 seconds do 290 DPS on the Avatar of Earth. This rogue wields the dagger off of Solusek. (i.e. not the best) This rogue beat the highest wizard in damage by about 500. The wizard had pants and connected with 6 full damage strikes of solusek, and crit once. That is hardly "sucking"

Don't look blindly at the DPS number your parser spits out. Consider all the variables.
