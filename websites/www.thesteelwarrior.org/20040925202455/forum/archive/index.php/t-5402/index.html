Read an interesting article a while back, might have been an interview with someone like Brad McQuaid so someone else out there might even be able to provide the link...

The essence of what was said was that most games are designed to run well under the current set of hardware - games like quake / unreal etc are made to look great and run great now and people will buy them and then next year they will by the next version which will look and run great on whatever hardware is out then.

MMORPG's however are designed and developed to look great when running on hardware that comes out next year because the graphics engine the game isnt being developed to be superceeded next year, its being developed to be played for several years with the same graphics and because of this they want to build a graphics engine that will stand up against the competition they will have next year and the year after.

Because of this each time a graphic engine upgrade is done on EQ it is designed to run well on systems that arent out yet or are currently too expensive for 90% of the player base, with the expectation that in a years time hardware to run it well will be standard and it wont be until 2 years time that it is starting to look old and out of date.
