We all lose aggro to a slow or occasional snare/debuff/dot as a mob is pulled. The only way I ever keep aggro through a slow is if I get a proc prior to the slow, and then use incite, which depending on the proc, gives me a total of around 750-850 aggro. With WA4 and 3 aggro procs, this scenario isn't uncommon.

If you start meleeing a mob, and don't get a proc, I would not blow incite, nor should you ever use incite as a mob is running towards you. First, I'm still doubtful of whether soe has fixed the aggro bug if a mob is far away or you haven't done damage yet. Also, if you use incite, and then the mob is slowed, you'll still lose aggro, and a succesful taunt or a proc is needed to get it back. If you taunt, it is basically like you never used incite, because you'll be at 701 aggro, where the slower is at 700 or so. Rather, this is what I do on pulls in groups:

Mob is pulled and running towards the group.
Shoot an arrow or two and have the puller run directly past you. 9 times out of 10, the mob will forget the puller and start whacking you.
Start meleeing, and hope for a quick proc.
Shaman/ench/bst casts slow. If you have not gotten a proc, let the slower get aggro. Use taunt to get aggro, and THEN use incite, so you are 400+ points above slower on aggro list. If you got a proc off prior to slow, use incite asap after the proc, and you will maintain aggro.

Also, if you have a good low-lag connection, and/or the slower has a message saying slow is being cast, you should be able to taunt quickly after slow lands, before the mob is out of your range. If it works, you'll keep aggro, use incite, and get complimented for your uber aggro control =) This works especially well on larger mobs like in BoT, where you can taunt from a good ways away.

When a mob is incoming, hit it with an arrow, and incite as soon as it is in range. If you have time to get off another arrow before it gets to you, the better.

I play both a 65shm and a 65 enc, and i know how frustrating it can be to try to hold slow aggro off of them with droga weapons, especially enc's. Really, if you do the above, it comes down to the slower ~ not yourself. For shm, they need to start casting malo AFTER you start melee attacks, then cast turgurs or balance after spell refresh. With haste on, even w/o a proc landing, a warrior with no offensive aa's can hold aggro off slow this way with only CHoS/SoTB - melee aggro alone. Enc's on the other hand carry a bit more aggro on debuff/slows. Tash is fast cast, and brings more aggro than malo - so if an enc is casting tash fast on incoming under the same situation they are going to get hit. If on the other hand the enc waits a good 2 seconsd, casts tash (faster cast, dont lose much time over malos waiting then casting) then immediately boggles and slows - they will not pull aggro.

Even with droga calibur weapons it is possible to hold aggro off debuffers w/o landinga proc ~ if a shm/enc in a group continually pulls aggro off you at the initial debuff, THEY should be casting debuffs a few seconds later. If you encounter a debuffer that is doing this, ask him to start standing within melee range of the mob when he slows - yes, he will gain proximity to the mob - but you will be able to get it off of him faster, and also gain the ability to /shield them easier - and alot of the times bringing their attention to their aggro fixes the problem anyhow.
