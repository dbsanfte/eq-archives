"It looks like warriors are going 0-19 while others are going 0-20."

It's always been my understanding that the DI range was 1 to 20, not 0 to 20. I'm not a tank, but I do still get hit a lot (I'm a cleric), and I've always calculated DB/DI values from min/max hit like this (calculating the formulas from scratch):

DB + DI = min
DB + 20DI = max

19DI = max - min

So:

DI = (max-min)/19
DB = min - (max-min)/19

Is this not correct? I've never heard anyone say that the mob's min hit was it's DB value... which it would be if the interval was 0-20. I can't seem to find the post about this on this board.

Also, since EQ is based on a d20 system, it just makes sense that it is 1-20.

Now, if after this change warriors *do* get hit for the mob's DB value exactly, then they just subtracted 1 from the interval, so the range would be 0-19 instead of 1-20. Plus, that just seems the simpliest thing to do, in my opinion (I'm a game programmer as well, and it's pretty simple to just go put a -1 somewhere).

And yeah, Min and Max hit on a given mob has always been the same for every person/class (not counting defensive skill being up), regardless of AC, hps, or anything else. AC, etc, doesn't affect how hard mobs hit; it just affects how often they hit hard.
