Here is what I've gleaned from quite a bit of reading on the subject of agro and hate. I haven't done any parsing myself, I do too much analysis at work to spend any amount of time analysing my past time. Sources are too numerous to mention, so if it looks like your idea it prolly is. Here are the factors that will help you in avoiding or maintaining agro so everyone can do their job with maximum efficiency and safety.
==============================================
SUMMARY (if you don't want to read the nitty gritty)
If you WANT agro:
=> Damage the MOB first to get the Hate Bonus.
=> Stun/blind/slow/snare as often as possible.
=> Use provoke preferably right after a successful taunt.
=> Use a weapon that procs, preferably a stun.
=> Use a fast 2H weapon
=> Dual weild fast 1H weapons with a good ratio that procs stun and/or hate.
=> stand close to the mob
=> taunt

If you DON'T want agro:
=> let someone else (preferably MA) hit it first
=> avoid stuns (use in last 20% of MOB health)
=> slow/snare/debuff after slight delay, and or pause between them.
=> avoid fast 2H weapons, especially ones that proc
=> stand back from the MOB while fighting
=> don't sit
=> be extra cautious if you are 5 lvls or more below the MA
=> don't taunt

==============================================

The Nitty Gritty:

Hate Bonus: First Person to do damage to a MOB gets a hate bonus, details are sketchy but it's significant. If you don't want agro, BE SURE the MA hits a new MOB first. Be aware when engaging a mezzed mob that MA may require 3 (or more) taunts to get a success, meaning a delay of up to 18 to 24 seconds while the MA taunts.

Hate list management (most effective hate generators to least):

Stuns, including weapon procs (send you right to the top of the hate list):
These seem to be the best way to get a MOB's attention. Essentially a succesful stun moves you strasight to the toop of the hate list just like a successfull taunt (and it does it EVERY time not every 3rd or 4th time like taunt). If you don't want agro use stuns judiciously. If a MOB is unslowable stuns are useful to help lessen melee damage to the MA. If a MOB is slowed the any additional interuptions are not worth the added agro so don't use stuns on slowable mobs. If the mob is a caster hold stuns till last 20% of MOB health the MA should have enuf agro by then to compete with you.

Slows (including weapon procs) -
Second bestway to tick off MOB's. If you are 5 levels or more below the MA you should wait till the MA has had a few seconds to generate some damage agro.

Direct aggro procs (warrior provoke line and weapon hate procs)
These add direct hate and are very usefull when used right after a successfull taunt that put the MA on top of the hate list. Warrior provoke line, in addition to a direct hate boost, also has a Hate Over Time aspect adding hate every tick for 10 ticks. For example Incite (lvl 63) adds 400 hate plus 70 per tick for one min. Provoke line has a 30 sec recast so a Warrior can throw in an additional 400 hate every 30 sec, but this all costs him Endurance. Hate proc'ing weapons/augments are just more icing on the cake.

Snare
Snare has a significant hate generation capability. Suficient to allow a chain casting Druid to maintain agro from an entire group of melee who are just attacking. Just like Stuns it seems Snare sends you right to the top of the hate list every time.

Less effective are:

Stat Debuffs (cripple, flash of light etc) details are sketchy but they are nearly as effective as snare and may actually be in the same category as far as the MOB is concerned. Use with caution.

Direct Damage
DD's and weapon procs - adds hate equivalent to total POTENTIAL damage (if a 1000DD is resisted it still adds 1000 hate).
weapons - POTENTIAL damage (damage plus bonus). If you look at two-hand weapons you'll see why they generate so much agro. A 30dam 31delay 30bonus weapon generates 60 hate every swing of the weapon; this means a hasted player is generating about 60 hate every 1.5 seconds or 240 hate per tick. Compare to a one-hand weapon with 13dam 20 delay 12bonus which generates 25 hate every swing or about 150 hate per tick. Off-hand weapons don't get bonus damage so a 13/20 would generate approx 75 hate per tick, total for DW 13/20 weapons is 225 per tick - not considering procs.

Heals - generate hate equal to 2/3 of healing (at last report). 1000 heal = 666DD.

DOTs - basicly function like weapons.

Proximity
Closer makes you more noticable. If you want agro stand REAL close, right on top of the mob or sometimes even inside the mob. If you don't want agro stand as far away as you can and still do damage.

Level
First, as level increases, Player agro radius decreases. This means if a mob is running towards the group he will enter the lowest level groupmembers agro radius first - and beeline straight for them. Sitting reduces your level to (1) which is why mobs run in and whack any caster who is sitting down.
Second, lvl does seem to affect the hate list some, so given a choice of two player who are close to each other on the hate list the MOB will take the lower. The larger the gap the greater the effect. Players who are 5 or mor level below their grps MA should be extra cautious.

Taunt
Why is taunt way down here at the bottom? Well here is how taunt works, on a successful taunt the taunter gets an equivalent amopunt of hate as who ever is at the top of the list, plus one hate. This means on a brand new untouched mob a successful taunt generates one point of hate. Furthermore, taunt is only succeeds about 1 in 3 at lvl 60, meaning the MA only gets bumped to the top of the list on avg every 18 seconds. This means a lvl 1 No0b with a 10DD can generate more hate than a lvl60 warrior's taunt all by itself. In spite of this, taunt can be an effective tool when used in combination with all of the above.

Hope this helps!
