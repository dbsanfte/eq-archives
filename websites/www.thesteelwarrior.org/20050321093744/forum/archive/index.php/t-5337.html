A joke:

A man and woman are sitting in a bar talking about what they would or wouldn't do for enough money.

The man asks, "would you sleep with a total stranger for a million dollars?"

The woman thinks about it and says, "Yes, I guess I would."

The man asks, "would you sleep with me tonight for 100 dollars?"

The woman says, "of course not! What do you think I am?"

The man says, "we've already established that; now we're just haggling over the price."

So, how many of you would sell your uber time-equipped accounts for 1,000 dollars? Not very many, I suspect, since you could probably do that and haven't.

How many of you WOULDN'T sell the same account for one million dollars? Show of hands? Anyone?

So now we're just haggling over price.

So, I actually went and looked at the IGE site today for the first time and checked the prices. hmmm. Let me think about this for a minute. If I devote my limited playing time to just buying and selling in the bazaar, even with my (relatively) limited bank roll, I can easily make 20 - 30 K a week - probably more - maybe even a lot more.

So, what if I had 6 computers with 2 accounts per computer and just worked the bazaar on 12 servers, AND did it full time, AND had a much bigger bankroll to start with. Could I make a million plat total in a week? Yeah - I think could; I think that's actually conservative. So now I'm making a thousand DOLLARS a week with an ongoing cost of something like $160 a month to pay for the accounts.

Are there smarter and faster ways to make money with the game? I think there are - PL'ing chars to 65 and selling the accounts, farming expensive drops, etc. etc. As long as you had the initial resources in terms of characters and plat, it wouldn't be hard. Throw in friends and family members and it probably gets a lot easier. And keep in mind, this is all without any cheating whatsoever.

So, could I possibly make $2,000 a week by playing the game full time? It's probably doable. That's more money than I make in my full time job. I have 4 kids. I have bills.

Let's see - integrity of the game vs. my real life family. Guess which one wins.

I consider myself a pretty decent and honest person, but if I thought I could increase my income by 30 percent or more by playing this game and selling in-game resources, I would do it in a heartbeat. How many of you would not?

I'm not enough of a risk taker to actually do this. I don't have the in-game resources to do it now and I'm not willing to risk investing real life money to try it; plus I don't know if the market will actually hold up (i.e. Sony might go into the business themselves).

But if I thought it had a decent chance of success I would, with no moral qualms whatsoever.

So, as much as I, as a player, tend to dislike Yantis and the whole idea of buying and selling in-game stuff, I can't cast any stones.

Gotta admire the guy; Wish it was me.
