The more i think about it , the more im convinced that the current suggested AA path for warriors has a lot to do with our problems.

Warriors are "expected" to take a full defensive path through the AA's , and only when thats done should they be takeing offensive AA's. Meanwhile other damage dealing class's are takeing AA that improve thier output. If things were equal at 0aa , there certianly not at 100aa.

Ive seen a lot of posts about how this AA doesnt really do that much , or this one sux cuz it only adds 3% dps. Maybe what people dont realize is that warrior's have tons of diff AA many that help offence , many that help defence , and some misc ones. Sure 3% dps increase isnt a ton , but how bout 3% from 6 or 7 different AA , its starts to add up.

I strongly feel that if warriors balanced out their AA , some offence some defence , then there would be far far less posts about aggro problems. I personally did it this way (being kind of a rebel i guess , wasnt gonna listen to others tell me how to take my aa heh) and i have really never had the aggro problems that i read about. I rarely rarely have a problem holding aggro when exping , even when shaman slows early,useing taunt and /sheild .

Another part of the problem is that a large percentage of the good warriors out there (gear/aa/weapons/skill) are in plane of time or the elemental planes . The left overs are whats left to tank for the exp groups in PoV , tactics which is where many of the problems posted speak about. Now you may say it goes the same for any class , but there is a difference. A stun spell works the same for a poorly geared lowertier paladin as i does for a high end paladin , the same cant be said for a similar comparison of warriors.

So maybe we are digging our own grave without realizeing it . Anyways , something to think about... out.
