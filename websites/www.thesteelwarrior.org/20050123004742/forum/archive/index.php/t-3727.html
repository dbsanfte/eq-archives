This isn't an answer to what you asked (I'm not a warrior and don't know if there are any upgrades between CHoS and BoC), however, I thought I'd suggest that you not to give up on LDoN so quickly.

My standard group (the group I run in 99% of the time) is 5 characters run by two people. Me (SK and rogue) and a friend (monk, shaman, cleric). We aren't in a guild and have mostly bazaar buyable gear (heck, my rogue is running around with 800ac because I spend all my money on ac/hp for the SK).

We got destroyed when we first started LDoN. We wiped twice on the first adventure before we even killed a single mob. I think we failed the next five or so adventures after that before getting our first "overtime win". Shortly after that, we started winning occasionally and we're prolly winning 50% of the time now.

It took us a little while to get the hang of the LDoN zones. Once we started to get a good grasp on mob placement and pull strategies, things started going a lot smoother. I suggest doing assassination missions or slaughter missions (slaughter are probably the easiest).

You might not want to hear any of this but I still think it would be worth it to try LDoN a few more times. For unguilded players with limited pp, LDoN seems like the best way to catch up with some of the raiding guild players.
