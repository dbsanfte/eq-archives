For any warrior that wants to tank in difficult boss fights, gnome RZ with points in CHA is best.

Gnome => can corner without shrink
RZ => cheap and excellent cultural armor
CHA => divine intervention goes off regularly

When tanking a boss mob, MT needs to stay up - divine intervention gives a partial/complete heal when you get close to death... i.e. you can protect against the spikes that might normally kill you.

If you're looking at a non-raiding warrior, your best bet is a fatty race with your points in dex. You won't be having DI cast on you, you won't have the amazing high-stat PoP armor, and the fatty races have useful innate advantages besides the high stats (regen for trolls, no frontal stun for ogres). Especially for XP groups, not being subject to stun from the front is a really handy advantage. Oh, and worshipping RZ is critically important; it's extremely difficult to find armor for the non-visible armor slots that matches or betters FH armor.

If you're looking at a raiding warrior, your starting stats are truly irrelevant, and regen is not a significant factor (not when you're getting a CHeal every two seconds). So Trolls become uninteresting, and Ogres remain interesting only because of the no frontal stun. On the other hand, on a raid you should have no problem getting a few shrinks and an illusion, so being a gnome is less useful for cornering than it would otherwise be. But what god you worship is almost irrelevant; it's trivial to raid for better equipment than FH armor, so the period where FH is a big advantage is really brief.

Which means that it really comes down to no frontal stun (Ogre, great for tanking hard-hitting mobs where it can be hard to hold aggro if perma-stunned), higher AC (Iksar, +50 AC or so at the highest levels), or higher CHA (half elf or wood elf, 75 charisma vs. a gnome's 60 charisma).

Honestly, the 23 CHA that an ogre gives up even to a gnome is a significant factor in DI, and the 38 CHA advantage of one of the elven race is just huge. On the other hand, I suspect that at the highest levels of the raiding game, getting CHA up to 280 (or even 305) should be relatively straightforward, between high-stat gear, augments, enchanter buffs, and bard songs. But I don't play at that level, and so I wouldn't know.

My choice? For a non-raiding warrior, an Ogre worshipping Rallos Zek seems obvious. For a raiding warrior, either an Ogre worshipping Rallos Zek (might as well), or a half elf worshipping, eh, RZ if they can, otherwise it doesn't much matter. And since charisma can be compensated for, but 100% immune to frontal stun cannot, I'd probably go with the ogre. But the half elf would be a solid choice, and I may well be wrong to prefer the ogre for a raiding warrior.

Gnomes get no exp bonus, only halflings do, so the fastest-leveling warrior would be a halfling. Halflings are also small enough that they can corner well. Ogres, Trolls and Iksar get an exp penalty (I'm not sure if barbarians do anymore as well, but they used to have a small one).

The problem with shrunk big races, like ogres, trolls and barbies, is that even shrunk there are a few places where for some reason their "wide" geometry still gets in the way, even shrunk. That's why sometimes you'll see the big races being changed to "thinner" races -- like elves -- before they're shrunk, and why there is a demand by large tanking classes for the Crown of Deceit (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=11277).

My warrior is a barbarian, and he's very good in exp groups, but if I had to do it all again with the intent of making a raiding warrior, I'd go halfling or gnome.
