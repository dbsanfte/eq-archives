Yes, just about any brand will do; just try to stick to one of the major brands that were listed previously, and try to not go too low end. The better boards tend to be easier to set up and maintain. The old saying, you get what you pay for applies to MBs.

Since there are so many different models, chipsets, sockets etc, I reccomend you decide what features you want and pick a few models that meet those needs. Then go to a site such as http://www.tomshardware.com/ and search for the models you want via the price grabber service. Nice wayt to get hardware.

Personally, I am fond of MSI products, and use a MSI 865PE Neo2 motherboard with a 2.8 GHz pentium with 1 G Ram. I think this may fit your needs not sure. It has been a while since I bought it, but the chip set is a good match for your speed of chip, the later ones do not get you much more unless you want the new PCIExpress socket for the new graphics cards. The better MBs also make updating MB drivers easy.

With that graphics card, make sure you have a godd power supply. Again a name brand such as Antec, preferrably 400w. If your power supply is weak you can get some odd behavior that at times is not obviously the power supply.
