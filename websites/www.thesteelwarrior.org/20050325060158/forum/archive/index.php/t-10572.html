Before we make things FAR more complicated than it necessarily has to be, I looked into this a while back and found that..

Deflection = ListedShieldValue@BaseStrength, +1 for every +10 Strength over Base

As long as the damage being received is greater than the value you block for, the actual Deflection remains constant.

Given Marshall's initial scenario, "Say my warrior has 200 str and a shield "32 block" value .. what will be the added effect of the 200 str to this value of 32?," Marshall will Deflect 32 + [ (200 - BaseStrength) / 10] every time he receives more than that amount of damage.

Block will never Deflect more than would leave the damage taken at 1 however. If one has a potential Deflection of 25 (Shield: 19, + 64 Strength: 6), and they are hit for 14 damage, the Combat Tab will list 13 damage having been Deflected and the Warrior will take 1 damage.

In testing this I started by removing all my equipment that affected my Strength for the first test. In an hour of testing at my Base Strength it became clear that the value being Deflected was constant at the value listed on my shield unless what I was fighting was capable of striking under what I was capable of Deflecting. I began adding Strength slowly and discovered no change until I broke +10 over Base, at which point I consistantly blocked 1 over the value listed on my shield. This pattern continued over +20, +30, +40, etc as I've leveled further and upgraded equipment since the initial tests.

This has looked consistent all through the 40's, but I have yet to repeat the test across a wide range of levels so am unsure whether the amount of Strength necessary for each extra point of Deflection scales similar to the affect of Agility on ones chance to Dodge.

As far as I've noticed from occasionally glancing down at my combat tab this has appeared consistent for Green, Yellow, and Red conned enemies. I have not thoroughly tested across a wide difference between my Warrior's level and the enemies level. When Block takes place it has always appeared to result in the full value being deflected, or however much of that full deflection would result in 1 damage being received.
