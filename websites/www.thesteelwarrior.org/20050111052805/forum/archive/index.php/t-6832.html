Would you mind explaining this a little better? Are you referring to the Voice line or something else and if so, what did you mean by overriding proximity or initial aggro?

Proximity agro:
There is bonus agro (temporary) based on how close you are to a mob. You can test this (I did) by getting initial agro with someone, not attacking or casting with this person, then with multiple castings of the pure hate (not the hate percent voice line), you will pull the mob off. I estimate this proximity agro is somewhere between 500 and 2000 hate depending on the mob.

Sit agro:
Similar to (and possibly related to) proximity agro. You can move far enough away to not get sit agro (when if you sat closer you would get attacked at least one round until you stand.)

Initial agro:
Every mob has a different amount of initial agro, that in the best way I can describe it, the first person to obtain agro will retain.

Melee agro:
Much effort has been put into melee agro, which puts it around DB+WD per swing (hit or miss.) So, say a BoC, CHSD, with max AA, a warrior can generate (max) 175 hate per second.

The description of when you can not pull the mob off the enchanter, cleric, etc. Many of these spells (slow) are significantly more than 700 to 800 hate. It doesn't surprise me that a 500 hate spell should/could easily pull a mob off someone. It does not make the spell not function. From every test I have made with a real Shadow Knight toon, these function.
