Fitemore
The idea behind damage bonuses was to balance out higher delay weapons to lower delay ones. Before the changes, you basically wanted the fastest weapon possible so that the damage bonus would be applied more often. In theory, the scaling up of the damage bonus on high delay two handed weapons allows them to do similar damage to a similar ratio faster weapon. I haven't looked at enough comparisons to know how it actually works, but I'd think you'd never want to go for a worse ratio slower weapon. I believe the advantage is in DPS still lies with the faster weapons, in general. The "sweet spot" for 2 handed weapons is at 28 delay, where you have a damage bonus of 35 at level 65, while 27 has a damage bonus of only 14.

Daabu
Damage bonus applies to minimum and maximum hit, before innate mitigation comes into effect.

Assume two weapons with the same exact damage, but different delays so that the DB is different. A weapon with 50dly has a Dmg Bonus of 51. A weapon with 40dly has a dmg bonus of 42.

The average hit of the 50dly weapon will be 9 greater than the average hit of the 40dly weapon, as well as the minimum and maximum hits.

It has been estimated that the "sweet spot" for a 2h delay is at 28. Anything less and you take a significant hit to your dps. Anything more and your dmg bonus isnt being applied as often.

Rule of thumb: for DPS, pick the best ratio'ed weapon you can. If there's two weapons with very similar ratio, go with the one with the weapon with the lowest delay over 28.

trying to answer the question about whether the 31/28 with 35db is "like" a 66/28 ...

assume minimum damage of 1 and max of 2 x 31 so average damage is about 31 (the damage rating). in that situation you would be correct about the effect of the damage bonus. now in many encounters the distributions is fairly linear between 1 and 2 x damage. there are however also some hits that go up to something like 4 x damage and there are crits as well. besides that the distribution is not necessarily linear within that range anyway because ATK vs mob AC will skew that distribution higher or lower. so it all depends.

i would suppose when targetting deep red named mobs you might be able to say the damage bonus is even more important than that equation makes it (since average damage is skewed seriously downward) but for the large majority of blue con mobs the damage distribution will be skewed upward and the damage bonus will take less significance than that equation states.
