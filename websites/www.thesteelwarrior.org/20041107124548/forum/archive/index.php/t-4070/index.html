Before I tell you all that you are right..itemization is all out of whack and a knight should never be prefered to tank endgame mobs (I don't see why it shouldn't be doable, I do however think it should be so inefficient that a warrior is undeniably the choice for it)... You need to just let go of the "Hybrid" model of looking at the melee classes who can cast. It is no longer a viable means of debating. The trend has been to move away from that..as much was stated back in velious.

You also have to understand that creating agro is only an indirect part of your job. It is vital, but it is NOT what you are told you are best at. In fact, you will never, ever be best at it, regardless of how a knight is nerfed, unless warriors are upgraded in such a way that no class can outagro them. There is also no reason to believe that warriors should be the best at agro or that knight agro is contingent on how good warrior agro is.

Warriors still take damage the best, even if some of you keep implying that you don't. Those of you who are intellegent know this, you just question what that really means. And rightfully so.

Now, I'm going to tell you the same thing that rogues were told when caster balance came up-the class "description" means jack and shit. It has never mattered for any class. Casters faced that painful reality awhile, now its your turn. Roles evolve, classes evolve. To an extent, you are right when you say that your class hasn't. The problem is, SoE seems to have finally realized something when it comes to balance: No class can ever universally be the best at a given job, or classes that fill the same role are useless. The trend away from the old idea of "hybrids" as gimp version of both their parents has gone, leaving some unfair remnants of that past (buffs for example). There was a time melee dps was far greater in both theory and reality then caster dps. This made casters irrelevant and unwanted. What happened? We now have fights that have high priority on range and burst dps.. and some that allow for more endurance type fights (though, the ammount of ft/spell ft a caster can get may be unfair at this time). This gives a role to all types of classes and forces guilds to incorporate them all.

What does that trend mean for warriors? Probably that you need to stop thinking of yourselves as the only people to tank raid mobs. If this continues, (and itemization is fixed) there will be mobs that *need* warriors superiors mitigation and ones that *need* knights superior agro. There is nothing innately wrong with that, it just hurts some warriors feelings. In my mind, this approach is the fairest to all classes, it does mean though that itemization *must* be fixed so that paladins can never do fill both roles.

However, we run into a problem that no one has been able to solve. How do you fix itemization of things like hit points when it is something that everyone needs because of ae's? Get rid of the ae's? Ok, but then you have to increase mob dps and the tanks hp, while lowering everyone elses. Of course, you would lower knight hp in relation to warriors. Warriors ability to take damage should always, always be significantly better then knights, knights better then chain melee, chain better then leather, leather better then cloth.

We are at an idealogical crossroads though. Do we keep the format of warriors always being the best tanks and limiting the kinds of encounters we have, or do we accept that knights are also legitimate tanks that have different strengths and go from there? I believe that every class should be useful, in both raids and xp groups. That SoE should design a variety of encounters, each with different needs, so that which class is preferred is based on the circumstance, not some static mold.

Don't take any of this as me downplaying what is going on now. I am not. These are very significant changes in approach and how they pan out will be interesting to see. I don't think having a class that is universally the best is good for the game though-even if that means paladins shouldn't be it. It wasn't good when that class was rogues or monks, so much so that most casters were ditching their dps casters to make them, and it shouldn't be warriors (or knights) now. Dps classes should be roughly the same, each having strengths depending on the encounter, and so should tank classes.

Personally, I like my role as an offtanker. I ,for some odd reason, would rather do that then be the MT. I think its a good role for knights, but how do you fix itemization and not make it so warriors are the ones who take that job from the knights? The only way to do that is to bank on their agro, but many of you want that stripped too. What balance are you hoping for? (I'm not saying or asking if you think there is balance now) Is it simply that knights are only an unhappy, forced choice on those with no other options? Or are you willing to allow them an important role?
