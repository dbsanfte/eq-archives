There was a thread a few months back (I'm too lazy to search for it right now) that proved that Damage dealt has nothing to do with the aggro obtained.

If I recall, aggro is determined like so:

Every WEAPON has a set Aggro-per-swing value (although not discovered).
Every ABILITY has a set Aggro-per-use value
Every SPELL has a set Aggro-per-use value.

In other words, Backstab has a high Aggro not because of how much damage you deal, but because you're using the ability. Backstabbing for minimum damage yields the same amount of aggro as backstabbing for full (unknown if a crit is an increase in aggro or not, but I'm going to go out on a limb and guess no).

While one weapon may proc less than another (someone mentioned BoW), the increase in aggro was not based on the extra damage taking place of the procs, but that the blade itself yields more aggro every swing.

The testing was done by using normal aggro testing (casting known aggro spells, then meleeing without taunting until aggro bounced back). Every time, a given weapon took the same number of swings (hits or misses, didn't matter), to obtain aggro.

This means that the most important aggro buffs are Dex (for proccing, obviously), and haste. ATK makes no difference on your aggro.

The most important tanking AAs by means of obtaining aggro are Weapon Affinity, Flurry, Ambidex/PB. Tactical Mastery and Combat Fury make no difference on your aggro.

Therefore, you can conclude that, while the aggro number for a weapon swing is not currently known (like unknown70 values are):

DPS + dmgproc or no proc > less dps + aggro proc.
