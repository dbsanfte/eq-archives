We all know slow weapons get higher AP bonus per attack than faster ones. The highr your overall AP, the more this is exagerrated. I recently switched to sword and picked up a Massacre Sword, a 3.8 spd sword with about 45 DPS. If you are familiar with my work on Single Strike Damage or SSD, I wanted to compare the SSD of Massacre to Dreadforge and Doomsaw. http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=11552 What I found out was surprising.

My base AP is 618 and I have a Massacre Sword of the Monkey with 16 agi bonus and 17 stm bonus. Also compared is a Massacre of Strength with +26 Str

At base AP - 618
Massacre Sword of the Monkey, 3.8 spd - 341.7 SSD
Massacre Sword of Strength, 3.8 spd - 355.9 SSD
Dreadforge Retaliator, 3.7 spd - 358.2 SSD
Blackhand Doomsaw, 3.5 spd - 343.5 SSD

With Battle Shout up +231 AP for 849 AP
Massacre of Monkey - 404.4 SSD
Massacre of Strength - 419.6 SSD
Dreadforge - 419.3 SSD
Doomsaw - 401.4 SSD

With Battle Shout and Blessing of Might, +231 and +223 AP for 1072 AP
Massacre of Monkey - 465 SSD
Massacre of Strength - 479.1 SSD
Dreadforge - 478.2 SSD
Doomsaw - 457 SSD

When fully buffed with a battle shout and a blessing of might from a Paladin, I would have a higher SSD with a Massacre of Str than with Dreadforge or Doomsaw. The .1 and .3 faster speed of doomsaw hurt them that much for single strke damage against Massacre. I would not say Massacre is a better weapon, but it shocked me how close it cam in terms of damage potential. I will not argue the merits of sword spec vs axe spec, because after using sword spec, I think its about even. Dreadforge and Doomsaw are better weapons, but not by much in my opinion. The only thing going in the Doomsaw's favor is the proc which can be devastating, but its placement in the arms tree for specialization leaves something to be desired. The dread forge gains some damage over my massacre of the monkey and 1% to parry, but the agility and being human brings the crit bonus of sword up to match the 1% of Dreadforge, and the sword adds 170 HPs. So it seems to me to not be that big a difference, and that I should start working my mining and saving mats for reaper than wasting my time in instances only to lose rolls to paladins on weapons that aren't that big of upgrade. In pve raids I would use sword and board anyways and I lose some soloing ability do too Massacre's lower DPS.

The reasoning behind this is that I think SSD is more important for warrior than DPS, because of burst damage. In PvP you rarely get to sit there and auto attack someone. You are almost always cc'd in some way with small windows to do damage in. It makes DPS almost totally irrelevant. Not entirely, as you might get an extra swing in here and there, but enough to make up for raw damage you get from slow high damage weapons. In this regards, a green weapon, the Massacre Sword, seems to stack up respectively well against some highly touted PvP weapons, because its slower than them.

Its kind of silly really. Its an old argument, but blizzards instant attack system really hurts a lot of high end weapon, there should be no way a 45 DPS sword should even come close to the damage capability of high end weapons. Instants should really be based on DPS*4 so that the fast rare weapons would actually be useful to have.
