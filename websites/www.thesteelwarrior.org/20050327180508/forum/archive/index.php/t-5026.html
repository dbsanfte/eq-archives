Originally posted by wandor
I would imagine enchanters prefer rescue missions because they are only killing as many mobs as necessary to get the rescue mob to follow you, then only killing the ones in the way on the way back out.

Actually, you have to kill a set amount of mobs to get the captive to even spawn.

The captive will stop even if a mob is on the other side of a wall, and in some of the dungeons you have to follow a long path to *get* to the room on the other side of the wall.

In my 72 wins, I've found it good practice to treat a rescue like a slaughter. Clear well, use your map to determine if side passages need clearing to prevent the "behind the wall" hangup, and just stroll out with the captive.

No, bards are the win of they dont use that crutch at all. Learning how to really pull works out much better than relying on lull.

Basically, mez, fade, and highsun are 20x faster and more reliable on hards then lull. Rarely use highsun even. Mez is the single best pulling tool.

2 spawn, mez one and buddy runs to camp. Slow buddy as its inc, switch over to warrior fast to intercept! then remez the intial one as it is running into camp 3 full ticks later.

3 spawn, do the same thing and either mez the 2nd free add as its inc and fade at camp for a single, or let 2 run toward camp timing it so mez the second free one is mez'd in camp, then lock down the intial one in camp 3 ticks later.

etc and so on. Much much faster than using lull and having to keep doing it untill it lands (resists a ton in hard) or a failure and aggroing 6 mobs in a room and having to blow all your mana.
