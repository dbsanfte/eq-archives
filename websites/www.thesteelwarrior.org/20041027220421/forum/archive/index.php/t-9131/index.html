Personally, I use the figure 1AC = 4HP. Some people go even higher. say 1AC = 6HP, but I tend to fight mobs that AE alot, and AC doesn't help you as much there as HPs does. Anywhere from 1:4 to 1:6 is usually the accepted formula.
Brabble
Within the Ac/hps tradeoff, I tend to look at visible and non-visible slots separately. AC on visible slots tends to be relatively cheap compared to hitpoints; hitpoints on nonvisible slots seems to be cheaper than AC. (Bleh, I'm not saying that very well, but perhaps you get my meaning.)
So, when I'm trying to balance bang-for-the-buck, I almost never look at one slot in isolation (for buyable gear). The exception might be when I'm contemplating a piece of really really good gear I wouldn't have to upgrade for a long time.

Mainly focus is on PoP so figure getting where AC is really needed.

Ac is linearly beneficial all the way up to 2700/2800 ... probably even further but i dont know anyone personally who can hit such numbers. I suppose the obvious answer is to pick the best of both worlds. After you can raid buff to 9k-10k hps i would say it's time to swith the focus into taking damage better rather than piling on more hps.

I'd have to agree with the above poster though. AC is cheap in visible slots (relative) ... hps are cheaper in invisible slots generally. Just find a balance within your budget/ability and run with it.

10ac augs from butcherblock (5 total) are priceless. You can also get 15ac augs in ferubi fairly easy (2 total) and i think even 1 in Wall of Slaughter. I'd put those in all your slots where you cant put solid coals of prowess.
