Daoga,

I did Ixiblat Fer a while back and I was really nervous about attempting him as I did not want to blow it. Here some more info, sorry I don't have more, but I've only done him once.

I 6boxed the following:
65(60aa) warrior - ended up pulling Ixiblat (arrow)
65(40aa) enchanter - DC'd Gylton as pet & 70% slow on Ixiblat
65(37aa) necro - w/rogue pet
65(22aa) magician - w/rogue pet
65(39aa) cleric
65(39aa) druid - died on the pull because I cast a debuff

I pulled with an arrow in the end and walked him back, hugging the zone wall. I was able to avoid other mobs this way and easily outran Ixiblat with sow (might've been only run3, cannot remember, think it was just run3)

I fought him right at the Skyfire zone area, as it is plenty big and I figured I could run the cleric to the zone if things went overly bad.

Everyone was in full Skyshrine (SS) armor at the time, no epics, nothing overly great (war had shortblade of lightning(off) and copper hammer of striking(main)). I went defensive off the bat.

Things to keep in mind, which almost cost me the encounter:

1) Stay in range of the cleric's heal spells, or better yet, keep the cleric close to you. The cleric CH spell has a fairly short range compared to other quick heals, so you may just want the cleric to run in near you to heal, then back off. If possible, I'd simply keep your main cleric near you while another, further away, heals just him. The duration should be short enough that you can do this no problem.

2) Get FR up there, but it really isn't his big killer, his melee is. Get the mage damage shield on you (Adds FR), get druid circle of seasons (or protection of seasons at that level, whatever they have).

3) Once you spawn Ixiblat, he spawns ACROSS the zone from the guy you handed into. I didn't know this. Basically, if you hit the giant fort entrance and keep that to your back and go straight out, you will bump into Ixiblat's spawn. He's huge, very berry orange and ready to stomp your guts into the potmarks of Norrath's landscape, you won't miss him ;)

4) With the new changes to EQ, I would (personally) pull him with Incite (or closest to it) to get aggro up there. Reapply it constantly. I would then furious with a high dmg 2hander to further aggro (once in position), you may wish to mightystrike him, if the dmg is ok at this point. Then hit defensive.

5) If you have an enchanter with DC, you can DC Gylton or one of the other wurms in the area as they are low enough. That will do quite a bit of dmg for you.

Finally, here are the stats from my single encounter. I was quite nervous and didn't nuke much as I was so tense, I could only concentrate on <taunt> and <ch> macros.

If you've done tier 2 planar mobs, you should be used to hits for 500 and know how to deal with that. This fight isn't really any different.

If you haven't, well, your defensive disc will make it feel like a normal Tier 1 planar mob.

Couple of quick ponts if you are overly anal:

- If you pull to SF zone, you can hide casters from the AoE behind the rocks. It is a directional AoE, so MT in front, other melee in back.

- Slow is the key (just like any planar mob). I'd rather have a dedicated slower than wait for a BST proc, but you'll have the healing power either way.

- His HPs should range into the 20k iirc.

- Chaining Furious and then Evasive/Defensive discs will pretty much get you all the aggro you need for the fight.

- You have plenty of overkill. The 59 epic mage can nearly solo him. For that reason I'd have the shaman come for the extra buffs and reliable slow.

Unless the healing gets fubared royally, I'd be surprised if you lose.

I've done him before with 5 people...2 clerics, 1 paladin, 1 shaman and 1 ranger, all under 62. (Shaman was actually 3boxing the ranger and cleric) Done him many times, in fact, on my cleric and beastlord in my present guild.

If you get 5100 with 59 bst and 56 cleric, with proper cleric/shaman buffs you should hit 5500-5700 or higher easily.

The AE isn't exactly AE...it's a small targeted AE around the MT (no such things as cone spells in EQ, they're all just targeted AE).

My recommendations:
If it's your guild's first attempt, go ahead and pull him to SF zone. You can also fight him on his spawn, but the smaller adds from roaming wasps are annoying if it's your first shot. Get a ranger over near his spawn point and hit him with a debuff (no damage) to get him to chase the ranger. It's a really long run to SF zone, and the ranger will most likely outrun Ixiblat (he's slow). Make sure not to outrun him as Ixiblat can turn and start running in another direction.

Since you sound as if no one in your normal guild has done him before, and you've got 3(4) clerics and a druid, I recommend a celestial before engage and then spam small priest heals on the tank as he engages off the ranger, just in case, until Ixiblat is slowed. (The 62 cleric technically should be enough just by himself after slow and probably before though, IMO) After that, just normal cut-n-paste debuff/slow/debuff and whittle the guy down. Position and keep everyone behind him so they don't get hit by the targeted AE, and just beat him down melee and nuke, keeping agro on you. One dead elemental, one piece for cleric. Fire nukes will definitely land, btw.

Honestly, the only "hard" part is the initial engagement (when agro is questionable), it's cake after that. I'd recommend the 65 ranger tank if he's PoT equipped, but if you want the experience (a good thing) you should. Once you've done him once, if your healers are actually paying attention and at all skilled, they should be able to figure out how much healing is necessary for you on any subsequent kills.
