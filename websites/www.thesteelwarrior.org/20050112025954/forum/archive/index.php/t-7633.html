It's a little different with regards to raid bosses, because (unless they're already trivial to you) nobody can generally tank them entirely reliably, hence MT rotations, regular occurances of 1-rounding, etc. Also, when tanking on a raid, the value of the knights' secondary abilities is nil compared to one's ability to take the punishment from the mob. The fatal flaw with defensive (and it being a warrior-only discipline) is that it essentially caps the max damage of the mob, rather than just reducing the frequency of max damage hits.

For the sake of illustration, assume a designer is creating a new boss mob, weighting the DI and DB values in such a way that using defensive results in a 40% reduction in the damage per hit of the mob (not atypical). If it was to hit a knight (or other class) for 1000 damage per hit, for example, it would do 600 damage per hit to a warrior.

Clearly, a mob doing 2400 (600*4) damage per round would be trivial for a top-end warrior using defensive, so the mob's base damage would have to be set pretty high to be a danger to a warrior under defensive. Assuming the warrior has around 12.5k hps raid buffed, the mob would need to do around 3150 damage per hit while he is using defensive to have any chance of killing him outright in one round (more than that, really, taking into account BoV and shielding). This same mob would normally do 5250 damage per hit without defensive. There is no knight that could take such a beating, and likely won't be for several expansions to come (if ever).

Imagine how mobs would be tuned if instead of defensive being simply 0.5*DI it simply shifted the frequency of high DI values lower in such a way that it had the same effect on overall DPS (about a 40% reduction), but still allowed for the possibility, however much reduced, of the mob still doing a quad max damage round. If that were the case, the boss mob above would still be a real threat to the warrior even if its max damage without defensive was just 3150. However, that mob would now be in reach for a knight to tank, though they'd need a great deal more healing still than a warrior, and still have a higher risk of being killed too quickly. Healing requirements overall would drop though (and less dependence on an army of clerics is a good thing).

Even a mob that hits for 1600 and flurries (1600*8=12800) would still be a danger to that warrior, and yet defensive would still greatly improve his survivability compared to an equally geared knight (who would be one-rounded by a max damage flurry). With the way defensive works now though, the warrior would only take 960*8=7680 damage at most, trivializing the mob. And there are plenty of VT and pre-elemental warriors (even LDoN and bazaar-geared) who can reach 8k hps buffed.

What this illustrates is that defensive as it is now makes a mob that would kill a warrior without defensive doable, even easy, simply by pressing a hotkey, even if their gear is completely outclassed by a knight for whom the same mob would be challenging and extremely risky to tank. If the mob was slightly tougher and only hit for 2000 and flurried (16000 max), it would be suicidal for a top-end knight to tank, while trivial for an equally-geared warrior using defensive (9600 max). Even worse, if the mob hit for 2500 and flurried (20000 max), defensive makes it possible but scary (12000 max), while knights would need several expansions worth of gear beyond GoD before they would have a chance of tanking the same mob.

I know someone is going to chime in and say that a top-end geared knight would have such a high AC that mob DI values are going to be skewed much lower for them than a VT or elemental geared warrior, and they might very well take the same average DPS from a mob as a VT or elemental warrior using defensive. However, this isn't about average DPS, but rather the worst-case scenario of a mob connecting on all of its attacks for a round or two for full damage. That VT or elemental warrior has a chance to survive this worst-case scenario, while a top-end geared knight would die outright. AC just gives you a better chance of avoiding the worst-case scenario, while defensive guarantees that you avoid the worst-case scenario.

The problem with mob damage continuing to scale up as it has been is that the amount of gear required to overcome the advantage that defensive gives to warriors gets pushed further and further out of reach. A mob needs to dish out 20k damage (or more) in very short order (one round, or one round+flurry, or two rounds unslowable), in order to be a threat to a warrior with 12-13k hps buffed. That's a 7-8k disparity in hps that a knight would need to make up to even stand a chance at tanking that mob. Meanwhile, a warrior's gear can be further and further behind that of a knight, and yet still be able to make up the difference with defensive.

It's too late to change the way defensive works now (it would be a trivial adjustment to change the way defensive itself works, but would be a massive nightmare rebalancing mob damage across the board to compensate), and anyway warriors would pitch a fit even if they would still take the same average DPS after the change as they would before. This is why the only solution for class balance is to give knights a reduced form of defensive (30-35%). There is simply no way around the fact that gear upgrades cannot keep pace with the growing disparity between a defensive warrior and a knight, and any spell-based solution (that doesn't just mimic defensive) that is sufficient to really close this gap would make knights massively overpowered.

Even so, as mob damage continues to increase, the knight form of defensive is going to have to increase marginally as well, and even approach full warrior defensive at some point, probably when they're sitting at 25k hps while knights are at 20k hps, assuming the game survives that long. :P 30-35% for knights vs. 50% for warriors is a 15-20% difference, or a little less than 3-4k potential damage from a mob that can do 20k in one round. The mobs that are going to be a challenge to warriors in the future are going to be hitting significantly harder (at least twice hard to be a challenge to a 25k hp warrior). That 15-20% difference against a mob that can do 40k in one round will be 6-8k, and we're back to where we are today.
