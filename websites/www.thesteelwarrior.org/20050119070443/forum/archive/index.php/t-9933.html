You can riposte on any swing you take against the front side of a mob. You won't get riposted from the sides and back. Flurry is just a third attack added to a successful double attack already made. If you succeed at double strike you might flurry for one extra attack. This applies to all flurry aa abilities. Flurry only applies to the Primary wielded weapon. You could in effect flurry for 3 three hits and double riposte for 2 hits for a total of five hits in consecutive order. You can add a kick or bash/slam for a sixth attack and procs have their own timer.

I don't know how procs affect the flurry double riposte sequence since each proc type will have its own effects to your combat. The aa description for flurry is a bit misleading but it means that a third attack can happen if you land a double attack for total of 3 attacks. Yes, your third attack could be riposted but not until you successfully landed a double attack first. A riposte on the second attack from a double attack will end the sequence and lose the chance for flurry(third swing). You still get the chance to riposte though and possibly double riposte on the mobs attack after you swing if its swing comes after you swing(slowed).
