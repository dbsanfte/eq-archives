I can't speak for Time+ SKs myself, however, there's a handful of things I see that could easily be done to put more effectiveness in our spellbook aside from /discs.

1: Debuffs: Currently my best standalone Debuff, free of dots and recourses, is a level nine spell, Despair, which reduces target attack by 5. With a one minute DoT attack, I have a 40 str, 28 AC debuff. I have a 40 atk debuff with a group recourse attached. Yet my class description states I can slow and cripple my foes with dark magic - But all I can do meaningfully is snare. A real debuff, one that shaves off 5-10% of incoming damage, would go a long way towards establishing a reason to exist beyond tanking, for both raids and single groups. Combine this with the AA FD that they've betad in multiple expansions, and spell selection issues resolve themselves. This has the advantage of being something to do besides sit on our ass in a small group with a warrior, as well as stacking with another tank's mitigation abilities. The disadvantage is that you still really only need one or two SKs on a raid for this extra debuff.

2: FD/Pulling fixes. Death Peace is a great spell, don't get me wrong. However, the pulling SK has fallen vastly behind his counterparts. While the combination of FD, snare, pets, and if you're lucky, the CoS(An item which hasn't dropped since velious), are useful, they pale in comparison to the power of instant FD + Phantom Wind, as well as the power of a skilled bard. SK FD is particularly fragile, vulnerable to stuns, casters, AEs, etc. Monk FD bypasses this first by being instant, and the later ones by way of AA - no such AA exists for SKs. In many places, these drawbacks can make us standbys. Granting the monk AA to FD through casting, and fixing the currently apparently broken Persistant casting AA would push many of these issues aside sufficently.

3: Spell DPS: This should be a significant source of our damage output, and should probably be sustainable as 30% or so of our total damage. Yet the tools given to create this are significantly crippled. Our highest damage pure nuke parses out at an anemic 14.7 DPS if chain cast, while being disease based - A resist never debuffed by other players, except by shaman preparing a disease slow. Our top lifetap reaches 47 peak DPS, but is not in any way sustainable, costing nearly as much mana as Supernal Remedy or Cheal for a 470 point tap. For raw spell DPS, our best choice is a -Kunark- era lifetap. We are asked not to use our DoTs on raids, or our 160 per tick, 6 tick dot might block a necro's 300+ per tick dot. Other dots are even slower and lower damage. In groups, by the time aggro is stabilized, this DoT runs at best 5 of 6 ticks, more commonly, 4 of 6. Recycle times on our spells need to be drastically reduced - For every other class, mana is the limiting factor on DD DPS, not a forced 45 second cycle. Let us empty ourselves out in an orgy of spears. Add disease to the Malo/Malosi line. Institute more fast cycle dots. While they're at it, reconsider the mana effiency of our spells such that they do not assume the full necro lich cycle. As a sub set, the pathetic greeness of the pet makes him use mage and other proccing weapons roughly as well as a wizard's familiar. Mobs that are light blue to me are yellow to red for it, and it cannot even reach a fraction of its damage capacity.

4 AAs: Quite a few of the available AAs for SKs actively harm the SK. Cheif amoung these is Spell Casting Subtlety. No other class in the game has AAs that water down their innate skills or other AAs. Yet Weapons Affinity actually weakens Soul Abrasion. Many other AAs are broken or pointless. Leechtouch is right down there with Warlord's Tenacity, only it also wastes what could have been a 7-10k nuke, while being relatively resistable. I have yet to channel a spell through a stun, despite having bought Persistant Casting the day of GoD's release. These need the obvious fixes.

All I can really do is pound HT and hope to take the bastard with me.

Not all of these should be done together, but something should get done to move the SK upwards. Many of these problems existed earlier in PoP, but with warriors aggro in shambles, we were an alternative when a paladin was unavailable.

I think the main reason knights won't get a /disc defensive from SOE, even in a watered down version, is that SOE is aware of what happens when three classes perform the same non-stacking function in the same way: one or two of the classes stop getting groups and have a potentially large problem advancing their characters and are much more likely to stop paying to play the game.
In other words, the position that knights should get a form of defensive requires agreeing with two separate premises:
1. Knights need better burst mitigation
2. Knights should have improved burst mitigation using the same general means as warriors use to mitigate damage.

A person might well be open to #1 while being opposed to #2.

Arathena's post a while back identifies certain situations in which SKs have some trouble performing certain functions: multiple mobs on pull and extremely tough off-tanking situations in raids, for example. Seems reasonable to me to at least look at these situations (particularly in the case of SKs) to see if they need some skill/spell bumps. However, plugging in the warriors' means of handling these situations blurs the differences between the classes in way that could lead to some unfortunate outcomes for the game, I think.

If different classes approach the same problems differently, there would be a much stronger argeement for functionality in all three classes. If, on the other hand, they all perform their main (and, basically, only) task in the same way and present the same set of risks and rewards associated with performing that central role, then it seems to me likely that the player community itself would tend toward selecting one of those classes over the others in almost all situations.

Certainly there are systemic problems with strengthening, say, SK's ability to tank by adding a spell or two to their books. For one thing, the main drawback to using spells in a tanking situation for SKs is not that they lose dps, but that the spell might never finish being casted. Defensive works no matter how hard the mob is or how many mobs are hitting you. But SKs have a problem in that just in those situations where their innate damage mitigation needs a boost from their spellbook, their spell book is likely to become less functional on a practical level.

Still, whatever the problems of making an adjustment through spells or through some /disc that takes a different approach and has different drawbacks from defensive, the advantage is that any such change is less likely to blur the distinctions among the three plate tank classes.
