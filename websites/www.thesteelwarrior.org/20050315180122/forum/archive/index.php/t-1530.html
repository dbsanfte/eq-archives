They all proc an AE wind attack which hits for successively times for low damage. I think around 100 to 150dd per hit. This attack has a rather low radius, casters can easily move out of AE range and still be in casting range to heal etc. The attack can be resisted, I'm not really sure anymore but I think it's magic based.

Originally only the sea giant minis in PoS were using this AE attack but when we last did the PoS minis about a month ago suddenly all of them, forest and desert minis too, were able to use it.

We just got 2 down two days ago, two of the desert ones.

I tanked them as a 60 warrior with 6k3 hps buffed and a bit over 1300AC. We had only 2 65 clerics and one 60 shaman as healers, and mana was still ok at the end of both fights (Well at least one fo the fight :p).
From my log, max hit under defensive was around 700, max hit without was 986. They rampage, sometimes flurry, and enrage at the end of the fight. And they also procs these lovely litlle storms that spam hits for 200 max for a short period, then poof.
Best strat is to keep the giant away from casters, at max range from healers. 2 reasons for that, the first being to avoid aggro of the spawned storms on the casters, and the second being as I learned painfully on the second named to keep the healers away from the rampage area, or they will be pushed out of heal range ...
We had almost 3 groups to kill them, with 2 wizards 1 mage 1 monk and 1 rogue.

Each of the different giant mini types (ocean, forest, desert) have their own fun little proc that you need to be aware of. IMO the Ocean Giants are the biggest pains because of that fookin fd proc ability that they have, plus they seem to hit harder. Desert Minis proc a 1k or 1500dd, but nothing too much to worry about other then that. The Forest Minis proc is ridiculously useless against a warrior, but make sure he stays off your casters as they proc a mana tap. If all you want is to farm them for your medals I'd say stick with Forest and Desert minis when you can, they're the easiest.
