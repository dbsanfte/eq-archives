Tsaotsao
There are other courses around online gaming. Georgia Tech has one that covers "Virtual Communities". While we are all playing a game here, think about it a moment. You are working side by side with guildmates, groupmates, etc, to accomplish a goal. Someone in rural Georgia can interact with someone from New York, London, or even Sydney, Australia, like they were sitting right next to each other.

Sure, this is just a game now, but what possibilities does this hold for the future? What can be learned about our experiences that may later be applied to Doctors mapping out a plan to treat a rare disease, or researchers solving some great mystery, or even diplomats discussing issues to prevent war?

I know it sounds like a stretch, but it really isn't that far away.

20 years ago, it would take about an hour of effort to send a picture from one person to another over the internet. Now it takes a moment. What will things be like in another 20 years?

Ultima Online used to show how many player-hours had been spent. The numbers were staggering. If all those people spent that same time trying to solve the problem of world hunger, the world would be a much happier place for many.

Think about it.. by playing online games like this, you really are a pioneer of sorts.

I know.. it sounds hokey. But we are defining the world we live in.
