The recycle time for slam and kick is the same. Slam has a higher skill cap (it works off bash skill, bash caps alot higher than kick). The only way I'd use a shield is if it were a furious bash shield, and even then it's situational. Keep kick skilled up for times when you're in under effect of an illusion (wolfform, tricksters) and can't slam, but slam is the way to go for most situations. And you're wrong, your job is not to get and hold aggro. The druid's and ranger's job is to try hard not to take aggro from you. Any idiot with a mana bar can pull a mob off a warrior, their job is to manage their own aggro so that you take the beating. If your gear is lacking that will make it harder for them to do that, but it's still their job not yours. Anyone who tells you different is either misinformed, or has gotten lazy from grouping with paladins and sk's and their insta aggro spells. :) If you are using your best aggro weapons, whatever they may be, and taunting the mobs when snares or heals cause aggro, and making use of positional aggro when possible, then you are indeed doing your job. Your friends need to do all they can to help you keep the mob's attention or just resign themselves to the beatings they're going to take.
