There is some info here:

http://forums.worldofwarcraft.com/thread.aspx?fn=wow-warrior&t=38845&tmp=1#post38845

The gist of that thread seems to be that strength is better than agility when talking about sustained DPS increases. I haven't done any parsing to validate the info, but it seems reasonable. There are, of course, mitigating factors to be taken into account when deciding what you want to focus on.

It seems to be accepted (on those boards at least) that the following are true at 60:

1 Strength = 2 Attack Power
14 Attack Power = 1 DPS
1 Agility = +.04% change to crit/dodge

Agility appears to become less effective as your level goes up, with strength being the inverse.

I generally look for strength on my gear, but I won't turn down agility where I can get it. Since I don't PVP currently and spend the majority of my time in instances (with a shield) or duoing with a healer, burst DPS is less appealing than sustained for the majority of my gameplay. Depending on how my playtime is spent when I cap, I may start going more balanced or even towards agility.
