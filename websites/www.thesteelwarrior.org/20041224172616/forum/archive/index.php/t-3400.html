Stuns always had that much aggro. You nailed it on the head in your post though... they weren't desired because they were mana sponges.

VI siginificantly improved their ability to take damage (tank), so now they have both the ability to stun aggro lock (which they always had) and the ability to tank as well as a warrior (for all practical purposes) in an exp group.

Also, aggro lock wasn't as important back in "the old days" because mob DPS wasn't as high. If a mob wasn't slowed immediately it wasn't a big deal... heck, in most places slow was a luxury and a group could do just fine without it. And, if the mob beat on a chanter for a few seconds it didn't usually result in a dead chanter.

Knights also received several other fixes to make them more playable/desirable in groups, including new spells, mana and exp penalty removal, and greatly increased DPS through the two handed weapon change and itemization.

I hope that answers your question as to why knights are highly desired now when they used to be the whipping boys of EQ.

Of course, many feel that the "fixes" went too far and now the situation is reversed, but that is covered (read: beat to death) in the class balancing and outhouse forums.

Here's the list of major boosts to knight abilities since December of 99, as least as well as I can remember them. It's been a long time, so my order and timing compared to expansions is no longer reliable, but I'll try anyway.

Pre-Kunark:
-Knight HP is given a boost to bring us to a level between that of rogues/rangers and warriors. Previously, a naked level 50 rogue would have more HP than a knight. He still out tanks us, though.

Kunark:
-SK Lifetap progression is altered, so that the level 60 tap is 338 HP, rather than 150.

-Harm touch is given a damage boost post 40, and moved to the lifetap resist table. HT has ever since remained in relatively close
proximity to a high end wizard nuke.

-LOH is given a serious boost in damage healed, approximately doubling it.

-Hybrid mana pools are -greatly- enhanced. First, our mana-per-stat-per-level is set equal to casters, removing the nine level penalty. Second, our spells are set to cost the listed mana cost, not twice it, and in the case of the SK's lifetaps, three times it.

Velious:

-Hybrid /discs are introduced. Rangers and bards win big. Knights don't get much.

-Knight defensive caps are raised from 225 to 252. We can finally out mitigate, but not out dodge, a rogue. Warriors still out dodge, out parry, out riposte, and flat out out avoid and out mitigate us. This is as it should be.

-The Great Two Hander Change. This is described above, but the -real- bonus is a seperate, nearly concurrent change.

-Hybrids are flat out given detrimental spell haste on all dots, stuns, DDs, taps, snares, etc with a casting time of 3.1 seconds or greater, not including items. Further, the act of casting a spell no longer causes 'next melee attack' timer to restart. Previously, wielding a 45 delay weapon meant I would swing 4.5 seconds after my last swing, or 4.5 seconds after finishing my last cast. Now, it means I will swing 4.5 seconds after my last swing, unless I am casting at this time, in which case I will swing immediately after the cast is complete. The timer then starts again at 4.5 seconds. This means that I can cast 'between' melee attacks.

Luclin
-Our taunt(WTF?) skill and dodge skills are raised, though still inferior to warriors.
-Stacking knight buffs are introduced: Brell's Mountainous and Cloak of Akheva.
-Cease and Desist are created, as are Torrents.

PoP
-High end paladin stuns are created, allowing stuns against 55+ mobs.
-Fear stops working as fear for exp mobs, and starts working as super-enraging-blow, thanks to a cap put in place to keep necromancers from soloing Vox and Nagafen. :rolleyes:
-Spell lineup allows SKs to consider themselves much more viable damage classes, to a degree.
-And, of course: Fast, heavy hitting mobs vastly inflate the importance of slow and hard burning exp groups.

I think that's most of it. Correct me if I'm wrong. :)

I've heard the number 300 thrown around for stun agro. OTOH, I don't recall seeing any actual testing, so its anyone's guess how accurate it is.

Paladin agro generation has and hasn't improved since Kunark. It was still possible to get agro, but it wasn't as easy to smother a target as it is today. This can be traced specifically to two things.

First is the availability of more than one lower level stun. Pre-Luclin, paladins didn't get their first stun spell until level 30. Then the next one came at level 49, and once Kunark was released, a third stun was made available at 52. Each of the higher level stuns cost significantly more mana, particularly since there was:

1.) No KEI
2.) No Flowing Thought

So once you fired off one of those spells, you were in for a longer wait while your mana regenerated - and you'd have to do it standing up at 1 point per tick (3 minutes just for casting Stun). An enchanter in the group changed this, as Clarity and Clarity 2 greatly improved mana regeneration, but if you didn't have them in the group or easily available, then you weren't going to have Clarity. The half hour duration meant that you couldn't buy it beforehand and expect it to last much longer than the time it took to reach your camp spot (if you were lucky).

Cease made things easier, although it still didn't make it perfect. I play a level 60 paladin, and with an FT1 item, I still need to watch my mana if I don't have KEI (which happens sometimes). I can generally hold agro with Cease, but it isn't hard to start spending it faster than I get it back. It'll take a while to run my mana down, but it will happen (even if I don't start using heals to keep other characters topped off with hp, pacify mobs, etc...).

KEI is, I think, the single biggest reason for paladins to be so much more effective these days. This can best be illustrated with my experiences with the new paladin stuns. No one in their right minds uses the Quellious' Words of... spells for agro generation. They consume too much mana (and they also have that annoying push 10' factor which drives melee classes nuts). The effective post 55 mob level stuns are Force of Akera and Force of Akilae. Akera is available at level 53, and works on mobs up to level 61. Akilae is available at level 62, and works on mobs up to level 65 (there's also the Divine Stun AA, which is manaless, and has a refresh timer on it). While I don't like to do so, sometimes on the higher level mobs I will switch strictly to Akera, which has a mana cost of 70 (compare with Cease at 15 and Stun at 30 - oddly enough, Akilae has a lower mana cost than Akera, and since Akilae was around before Akera, no one's quite sure why this is). Its difficult, and I have to be very careful with my mana (which means that I'm pretty much not casting anything else), but it is possible to tank almost strictly using Akera (you're going to need Cease from time to time even if there isn't an emergency - if there's emergency, then Akera alone won't do the trick) - provided you have KEI. If you have KEI, you can almost do it with just Akera. If you don't, you can't. With Akilae it should be very doable (since Akilae has a lower mana cost), although the lower level stuns are still needed for emergencies. Recast times make it impossible to chain stun a mob without the lower level stuns.

And then I end up in one of those high level groups that sees me tanking mobs of level 62 or higher, and promptly switch back to Cease and Stun. After all, if Akera doesn't actually stun the mob, what's the point in using it? Might as well save the mana for something else.
