Honestly I'm not that wonderful at playing MT and main healer at the same time. I'm really not. So why the hell can't I find good clerics far too often? Oh, some have been good, but a surprising number have not been.

Common examples of late:

1. The "40% CH cleric" cleric. This cleric starts their CH when you're at 40% health, no matter what mob you're fighting or where. Are they playing EQ from 3 years ago? Has their only grind zone been BoT with tanks overgeared for the zone? If the content is what I'd consider remotely challenging now, the damage spikes are bad enough that you sure as hell better not wait for me to hit 40% health to start the CH. Has it occurred to you that you can't heal the same way for every single grind mob in EQ?

2. The "Never has mana" cleric. This cleric frequently burns through all their mana either through a total lack of math knowledge (how many hit points per mana are you healing?) or because they wait too long to start the heal, much like the 40% CH cleric, but the latter are bright enough to realize that they waited too long and must now use a faster heal. Of course those fast heals cost more mana, heal less, and when overused you frequently hear "lom" from the cleric. They may also burn huge amounts of mana on unnecessary group heals. Back in the day I called them the "divine light" clerics after that damn spell. It cost only a little less than CH, only healed something like 900 hit points, and yet I had some clerics who soon went "LOM" using it to heal me even after combat in easy grinds. Learn a little math prz.

3. The "Chuck an early direct heal" cleric. Many clerics don't seem to comprehend aggro after 65+ levels and a bunch of AAs. Yes, since I have a cleric myself, I realize sometimes you have no choice but to chuck a direct heal on someone right as the mobs hit camp and it sucks. But sometimes I group with clerics who frequently throws that early heal on me when it's not needed. Delaying the heal a bit or doing elixir followed later by a direct heal would have been plenty, or actually getting Divine Arbitration and using it. Instead they keep chucking that direct heal as a mob hits camp, get aggro, and die or at least waste more mana healing themselves and make me chase the mob around.

Bottom line. Any comparably leveled single boxed cleric should be a better and more efficient healer than my 2 boxed cleric run almost entirely from hot keys on my old 600 mhz pentium III computer. Sadly it seems many are not and that's not to brag about my "l337 ski11z" (sp? :P) at all. It's just not that hard to be better than that.

/grumble
