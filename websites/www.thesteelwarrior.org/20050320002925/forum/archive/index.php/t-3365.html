Beastlords can slow and give group mana regen that stacks with everything. Plus at higher levels they can haste as well. 'nuff said. ^_^

Beastlords can survive longer than any pet class if the pet dies (unless the Necro FDs of course, but even that can fail) due to level 55 disc. All three classes can give mana regen in some form (Mage rod, Bst SP and Paragon, Necro Mind Wrack and twitch).

Honestly, each class has it's powerful points. If you had all three in one group, they'd completely tear anything and everything apart (plus pet groups rock).

Mages have variety of pets, each with differing abilities, plus a huge number of summons and different nukes.

Necros have a huge control over battles and hp/mana, especially undead, and snare with lifetaps and everything cannot be underestimated (Necros are only pet class with snare...and I mean REAL pet classes, the ones with level 60 pets 61+).

Beastlords have group mana regen, haste at later levels, slow (high enough to be a main slower 60+ in PoP), their pets are very powerful with procs that hold agro, can melee, and they can play shaman at times too.

Just have to look at the capabilities of each class, perhaps go check out the class boards and read up on what the class can do. That's the only thing I can say. Some people like the simplicity and raw power and control of the Necro (only one pet buff to worry about), others like the power and melee/pet of the Beastlord, and others like the overall versatility of the Mage. It's really whatever the person seems interested in. Some people don't like the myriad of buffs available to Beastlords. Some people don't like all the summons Mages must keep up with. Some people don't like the volatile nature of the Necro with their hp->mana spells and lifetaps.

Need to find the pet class that more fits with the person.

I played a mage to 64.

The magician's superiority in pet summoning doesn't come in a be-all-end-all form, but a versatility of pets to choose from. Mages, as we all know, can summon fire, earth, water, and air. Each pet has its own distict advantages and disadvantages, and post-50 each pet takes on its own class. Speaking post-50 of course, both earth and air pets are warriors, fire pets become wizards, and water pets become rogues. Each pet also has a high resistance to a certain element as well. Air pets are highly resistant to cold. Earth pets are extremely resistant to disease. Fire obviously to fire. Water pets are highly resistant to poison. Don't take my words for immune though. Certain mobs will still land the respective elements on the pets if they're high enough level and have a high enough resist check with it.

As far as interclass comparisons go, mages have the upper hand is nearly every situation. Mage pets have much higher ac, much higher attack ratings, hit harder, and can have higher hp depending on which pet the mage summons. The fastest hitting pet goes to the beastlord actually. Warders hit the fastest out of all of them which is also why they have the most push. Survivability wise, the beastlord's 64 warder is about on par with the magician's 61 air pet.

High end balancing issues the mage class runs into:

1) Pets don't scale.
2) Magicians have no way of reducing their agro.

Mages get Reclaim Energy (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=331&source=Live) at level 1. It kills the pet and gives the caster mana determined by the hp of the pet (it's not a straight 1:1 conversion, tho...I forget the formula).

At around level 50+, the hp of the mage's pet is so high that it actually gives more mana back than it took to cast it. So a mage can therefor just cast a pet, reclaim it and get an overall increase in mana. This is Pet Canni, cannibalizing their pets just like shamans cannibalize their bodies. Just repeat this several times and a oom mage can be FoM in no time, especially with 61+ pets. Then you add in the AAs for Faster pet summoning (50% less) and componentless summoning and it just makes it even faster. The overall downside to this is the Mage must kill their pet in order to do it, which could hurt depending on how much equipment and buffs it has. However, if the Mage gets the Suspend Companion AA, they can just suspend their current pet and do their Pet Canni, then unsuspend it.

It's been around for quite a while, since Kunark I believe, when the pets became high enough in HP to give more mana back, and there's been nothing done to nerf it, only improve it. You have to remember that not having a pet for a mage is a huge cripple, a mage is very vulnerable while they're doing this. That and unless they have the Suspend Companion AA, they still need to summon the pet and buff it and give any items they may have to it. That's enough of a downside, IMO.
