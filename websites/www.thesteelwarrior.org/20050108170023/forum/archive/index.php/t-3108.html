I'd just add that the stuff about the door is unnecessary, imo. I just wouldn't bring KoS people at all -- make them earn their faction to go on the raid. If you have to have KoS people, make sure they keep away from the door.

You really would like a good slower. On gating mobs you'll need people who can seive mana and especially a coordinated melee push. Positioning becomes a big deal in ToV. You want to keep mobs stable at the beginning, to keep them away from the casters (keep casters around the corner from the mobs to avoid the AE), and pushing into some furniture can be really bad. But when it comes time, every melee needs to hop to the same side to interrupt the gate.

Avoiding the AE is pretty important. If the mob can be hailed with his name appearing, generally speaking his ae will get you. Casters need to monitor where the mob is and be responsible for finding the corner away from the AE, though the melees need to be smart and not push mobs into casters.

HoT mobs will aggro anyone who drags, so you can only drag while FD, so only monks, sks, necros, and sos rogues can CR. Someone needs to get a HoT map, preferably one with spawn points listed.

Breaking the pit is the hardest part I think. Generally you can seperate them by creeping someone forward and sitting along the far edge of the wall approaching the pit. Hopefully you'll find the point at which one aggros but not the other. Make sure there's no pit roamer when you do that. A small person sitting face against the wall under the tapestry facing the pit can actually see through the wall into the pit and give warning about roamers.

Belwolf,

I figured I'd throw in my thoughts on HoT as I have a semi-different perspective vs most people that have already posted.

First and most important, visit
http://www.geocities.com/waldi_waldmeister/

This is a decent guide to HoT and I found it very useful. Next, a little info on how I've done HoT. I 6 box and first visited HoT when I was barely over 60 (war/enc/clr/dru/nec/56 mnk). I found at 60 that my warrior could proximity aggro a single guardian at the initial 'pit'. Over time, I've found that a monk is not required to pull a lot of the area (there are some areas that a monk would greatly help, but I simply avoid them). I dumped the monk in favor of a 60 mage. At level 60, 6 boxing, I found it very very hard to even do the entrance mobs. I had basically full thurgadin armor from all my Kael time so I was ~5k hitpoints. I had to go defensive on the fights due to their damage output and not wanting to get my enc smoked.

I leveled up and came back at 65. I can very easily do HoT, now that I understand some of the spawns and how things work. Between the mage damageshield, druid protection of seasons and enchanter magic protection spells, my resists are 240fr/200cr/200mr which prove plenty high (coupled with my level) to resist the dragon AoEs. Pay close attention to positioning as that is the key in this dungeon. ALWAYS postion your casters around a wall, even a corner, so that they avoid the aoe and only take dmg when they move out to cast on the mobs. ALWAYS push the dragons into some sort of corner to keep them pegged and from moving around. Having only 6 ppl, basically 1 melee and 3 pets results in my pushing of mobs not stopping gate reliably. However, I have my enchanter focus solely on mana tapping... the torment series of spells are you friend, they are quite simply > HoT dragons. One enchanter can easily keep any caster in HoT from casting gate, simply by keeping their mana tap dots on the mob throughout the fight.

Finding where to place casters is easy if you pay attention to the map on that webpage. Keep that concept with you and apply it throughout the dungeon and you'll do great! Keep in mind that if pets are setup to hit a mob through a wall, they do not take AoE damage either ;)

Keep in mind that red dragons are pure melee, white are casters. I'll let you figure out the drakes, they're fun to play around with.

Some additional tips to get you past the first pit, from there you should find everything else easy to figure out. In the pit are always two dragons, at 60 & 65 I could single pull by arrow OR by creeping along the farthest wall from the other pit mob and proximity aggro. There are two roamers shown on the map, they roam THROUGH the pit. They are roughly 10-15 minutes on their passby on the pit. So, once you see one of them, you'll see the next roamer in roughly 10-15minutes. Eventually, they can both end up roaming through the pit at the same time, so simply keep tabs on them. The roamers are always wyverns/racnars. They are never dragons (dragons cannot fit through doorways well, keep that in mind) I don't think I've ever seen a drake as a roamer through the pit, but I could be wrong.

Here's a brief rundown on how I do HoT:

1) Put casters on left side of wall (as I'm facing pit).
a) Pull right mob and push into right corner at pot.
2) Put casters on right side of wall
a) Pull left mob and push into left corner at pot.
3) Wait for a roamer. If roamer comes from right side, wait until the door closes as there is sometimes another roamer there that will aggro. Pull this roamer up to left side of entrance.
4) Wait for the final roamer. I typically pull him to left side.
5) Drop into pit and head left. I go left because I feel it is the easier side, all single pulls for me.
a) Pull dragon at end of hallway, you can see him. I fight him in the little crook near the doorway (pot there) and keep casters inside of the door.
b) Pull dragon in the first door on the left. I push him into the corner near the door, sometimes he can be pulled through the door btu usually he gets stuck and resets, so I simply push him into the corner at the door. Keep casters outside of door and just open it to nuke him. SOMETIMES the next mob will aggro on pulling this dragon, I've had it happen twice, no idea why. So be prepared for that.
c) Now, go through this first door and take your first left. There is drake there.
d) Now keep to the left and go around the bend, you'll find a wyvern/racnar type.
e) To the right of this spawn is a dragon in the hallway, not the one straight ahead, but to your right. He is basically at teh 2nd door in the first hallway. I pull him and fight him up against the wall and place the casters in the U shaped portion so they avoid AoE.
f) You're on your own. Good luck and enjoy, this gets ya started.

Final tip... I'm heavily reliant on pets for melee dmg, so, DO NOT have pets up when pulling dragons. Their aoe causes the pets to aggro even if casters do not take a hit... the range is huge. Now, you have pets bashing the mob and it makes positioning it very very hard. Recalling the pets is also hard, due to the aoe going off a lot. The aoe also rips pets apart, so it is best to summon them during the fight if you require their added dps. I go through great pain to keep them behind a wall. I always have pets up for racnar/wyvern/drakes as their pushovers in comparison to the dragons and positioning is not a big deal with them.

To give you the carrot... plate drops are amazing. I've gotten 3 full sets of plate plus 3 more sets of gauntlets/arms/bracers in the same time it took me to get 1 complete cloth/4 gauntlets/arms/bracers/boots and 2 helms/1 bracer/2 legs/1 hands/2 boots in leather. I spent roughly 2 weeks there of moderate raiding to get this. I found that making it a certain distance in, then succor'ing out to redo my way in was the best bet for me, but I'm only one group. Also, not being kos to CoV is a good thing as it makes CRs much much easier. Doing the mage coth is so very painful and time consuming.

Good luck and happy journeys

Line of Sight comes into play as soon as the door opens.

Without it open, if a mob gets close an AE will agro everything.

With it open, any mob fought BETWEEN the firepots will agro them.

Any dragging in between the firepots will agro them, door or no door.

Any mobs fought on firepots are not in LOS, and do not agro.
