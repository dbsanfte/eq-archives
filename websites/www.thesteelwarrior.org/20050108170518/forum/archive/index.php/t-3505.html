Do as before on TZ, but make each group self-sufficient in terms of healing, i.e. give each group a priest of some kind and tell the group members to know where their healer is. As pointed out the key to this encounter is spreading out your raid force. You do not want Tallon to turn one direction and immediately AE 50%+ of your raid force. Personally what we do is divide the groups up as evenly as possible to the 4 corners of the room. Then we use a couple paladins chain casting stuns that attempt to keep him looking at a wall between two corners. TZ has a pretty hefty knockback on his melee so at times he likes to turn suddenly as well as his shadowstep. But if the tanks know what they are doing (and people are smart enough to move if something goes wrong) there is no reason for this fight to get messy. And what I meant by people being smart enough to move, if you notice TZ facing you that means it's time to get out of the way pretty simple.

As for VZ it is a pretty interesting fight the first time you go through it and if you haven't already read up on the encounter I would hate to ruin the surprise. Most people will tell you that the VZ encounter is very easy and that is true if you know what to expect, just don't go in expecting to just attack and have VZ fall over dead.

On TZ don't have all your tank types just blindly rush him, if they do one proc and they all get throw away and TZ will start arrowing your casters till they run back. Instead have several plate classes hover around arrowing until he procs the ones currently engaged and sends them flying. Soon as they get proc'd on send another one or two in and have them do everythign to get agro until they get sent flying. Should be easy to continue like this and have minimal procs going off on your casters who can't take it much.

VZ have a wizard cast an eye that is sent between the waiting VZs, have your tanks /assist him on call to get your targets, the wizard can easily then target /assist each tank to make sure they are all on different ones. And always always push them away from the center of the room.

Wasn't talking about resisting his AE's. The best way to 'resist' an AE is simply to not get hit with it to begin with. I said 'deal with' the AE, not resist it.
