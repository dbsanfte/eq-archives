Brenlo,
I believe nearly every Warrior agrees with almost every word in your post. There are two exceptions IMO.

1. The "Holy Trinity" remark was inaccurate and inapropriate.

2. Group staffing flexability should not result in versatility trumping focused superiority uniformly and without exception in GoD.

The so-called, by Knights and their apologists, "Holy Trinity" does not and did not exist in GoD trials. Crowd control/slowing, for example, was handled by Bards, Enchanters, Beastlords, and Shaman. In the sewer trials, Warriors and Knights could tank. In Vxed and Tipt, Warriors and Knights could tank while groups made good use of Pookas and Rangers with Weapon Shield.

It is I think one thing to conceed that some Gates content was too hard, not the way it was intended, and that it will be adjusted.

It is an entirely different matter to use the phrase "Holy Trinity", the destruction of which was used as justification for expansion after expansion of major upgrades and class blurring that culminated in Knight preference in normal experiance groups and Knights being fully capable of MTing all of the content in the game at the end of PoP.

Secondly, class bluring is the most single destructive direction taken in the game over the last several expansions. Yes, forced 6 person content must be doable by all. Yes, all classes need near equal acceptance in normal experiance groups. But class identity must be maintained in the game and class differences should mean something.

I'm sure the developers are well aware that trials do not consist of only the boss mob fight. Make that fight equally doable by Knights and Warriors and it's very easy to arrive at Knight preference if their spellbooks make the 2+ hour fight to that boss mob quicker and easier.

Some classes, particularly Warriors and Clerics, chose to be the best in a narrow focus area of the game. Knights can not tank as well as Warriors in ALL groups, else their spellbooks provide extra value that assures them group preference. Shaman and Druids can not heal as effectively as Clerics in all group content, else their versatility makes them prefered.

In my view, even group content needs to be scaled in difficulty. With some portion providing groups with strong motivation to get a Warrior to tank and a Cleric to heal. As new content becomes revealed by player progress, the previous few Warrior and Cleric prefered mobs become more doable by others, and some few new Warrior and Cleric prefered mobs become revealed.

Thus it has been for as long as I can remember 8).

Phrases like "Holy Trinity" might be soothing to some Knights who want to see Warrior survival advantages relavent only for 3 Warriors in a guild, only on raid day, and only when applied to 2 named raid mobs. However if such a condition is arrived at by "sharing" defining Wariror abilities or by making them not applicable anywhere in the non-raid evironment it would be at the expence of Warriors.

Please understand that I'm not "attacking" you for using the phrase "Holy Trinity". I am pointing out that it doesn't apply AND efforts to insure focused classes like Warriors and Clerics have no useful advantages in their area of focus has in the past resulted in Cleric "bots" and /lfg Warriors.
