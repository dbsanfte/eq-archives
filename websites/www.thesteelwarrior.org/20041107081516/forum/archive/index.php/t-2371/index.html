We're in POV this morning, pulling and exping fine having our 65 ranger tank for us... she's put some time into her defensive AA's and it's working out okay since we have a cleric healing and a shaman slowing.

Finally she's gotta leave for work. We've made plenty of paper-tank jokes about her and she's laughed it off, really we've all been impressed with how well she did after the Paladin left.

So we're looking for a replacement and we see a 64 warrior LFG. He's in a good guild, good reputation, nice guy, and I like warriors. I like anyone who doesn't wiggle their fingers and speak the heebie jeebies. We pick him up immediately. When we see him run in range we're all relieved at the fact that he's wearing great gear too.

So we get our first pull.. I land a great backstab a lil too early and it jumps to me. I quickly evade and remind myself to wait a bit. Shaman goes to debuff and slow and the monster immediately jumps to her. Clerics goes to heal and it jumps to him.
Shaman says without any hint of malice or anger or frustration as she apologizes to the group "Ah sorry, forgot we had a warrior tanking now."

After that we slowed things down a bit, monster still jumped at the shaman on every pull though, even waiting. I started off like I usually do with warriors, wait for a bit and wait to backstab until after I've landed an evade or two. Things went fine after that.

Talked with the cleric a little bit, a guildmate, about the situation and he agreed that it was goofy. He offered that he's seen the warriors in the top guilds on the server hold aggro pretty well at least.

But what about everyone else?

It's not a problem reserved for level 65 characters raiding the top 5% of the game's content.

Hope they're addressing this and all the other puremelee concerns along with the 1278 spell changes on Lucy over the last few days.
