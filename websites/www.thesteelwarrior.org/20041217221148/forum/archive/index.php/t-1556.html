This has been mentioned before and your assertion is close but not quite right.

The reason why Knights are so strong is because Casting in general is so strong compared to Melee. Mana pools and regens are just a symptom of this. Just as much as some want to blame Knights for superior agro control the other half of the problem is that caster have to generate such strong effects to survive. And as Glatius says, making them frail is what got Warriors a black mark in the first place. Removing more mana and making them weaker won't make them choose a class that even has a hint of agro control iffy-ness.

The key, and many do miss this on all sides, isn't just the fact that Knights have stellar agro control because of spells. The key is that Knights have stellar agro control because we have so many attempts to gain agro control.

During a standard exp group, I will load 3 offensive spells (two of which are probably stuns, one of which is probably a root) and 3 defensive spells (usually 3 heals). This plus 2 aa skills (Divine Stun and Hand of Piety) and good old Lay Hands are some 9 actions where I can not only generate agro but I generate an extremely group friendly effect. Any one of these activated can advance me on the agro list. And then there is Taunt...

So there is the problem. The number of attempts a Knight has to regain control far outnumbers the way a Warrior has to regain control. Is it any wonder why they are perferred in groups? If one wants to argue that SOE needs to reduce the number of attempts a Knight can make kind of ignores the fact that Warriors don't have enough attempts in the first place.
