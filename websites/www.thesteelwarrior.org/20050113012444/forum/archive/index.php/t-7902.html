SKs and Paladins are not the same thing. It's not that the category "knights" has no validity, but bleh, warriors need to be careful starting sentences like "Knights want...." It's an odd combination of posturing and whining that is singularly unproductive.
Poorly defined problems are also starting to really bother me. If you fail to define a problem precisely, your chances of proposing an optimal solution trend rapidly to zero.
Look over these threads. Hardly any space in the discussion is given over to a description of any given problem, its scope, its severity, its range/situationality, etc. A problem is gestured at, then IMMEDIATELY a bunch of solutions are presented at length, then people start flaming each other at even greater length.
BB's suggestions are weighty precisely because he is precise.
My own view is that SKs are getting the short end of two sticks: they are not a heavy tank, but in some significant situations it's hard to see how they make up for that with their damage or utility. A rational discussion of what the range of these situations are must come before a tidal wave of solutions.
Let me give a couple of examples of things that look like possible problems but which, in my view, are not.

The other day my SK was soloing a dark blue mob. I started the fight at 80% health. When I killed the mob, I was at 100% health, mwuhahahahahaha. My warrior would have been hard pressed to solo that mob at all under those circumstances. Other posts on this board have pointed out situations where in normal LDoNs the cleric goes LD and the SKs keep fighting single pulls for a while without a healer with little problem. The other day my warrior was in a normal LDoN pick-up group. Cleric pacified a crowd of mobs, I tagged one. Pac failed, the whole roomful of mobs came to me. This game is about groups of characters working together. SKs, it seems to me, have a potential set of problems that mostly stem from poorly tuned scalability in their spell books rather than lack of /disc defensive. But again, such circumstances have to be precisely defined if a sufficient solution set is going to be found.
