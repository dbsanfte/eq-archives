It is not really DPS that creates most of your aggro in the first place. It is number of swings and type of swings. Take a character with 2 duelwield rusty swords and take him to a mob that requires magic items to do damage to the mob or banes and let him swing at the mob for 1 minute. He will have done 0 damage but he will have very solid aggro on that mob due to the swings themselves and will take a bit to get the mob off him with swings that do damage if he were to stop swinging or with heals and debuffs etc. His swings would provide more than enough aggro to allow for healing and debuffing and others to attack the mob though with rusty weapon delays I wouldn't say anyone couild go all out on the mob :P . Swings alone make up about 85%+ of your meele aggro (Not counting procs) while the actual damage you do makes up for abut 15% on average. The types of swings you attack with also determine aggro. 2 Handed swings have more aggro per swing than one handed weapon swings. The exception of this rule is two handers that do not recieve a twohanded bonus seem to create aggro inbetween one hander aggro and twohander aggro. It is very close to double the aggro on a per swing basis between a 2 hander and a one hander thus a fast 2 hander like a 32 delay BoW actually creates more aggro just thru swings alone than 2 20 delay weopons will, especially when you count in flurry and other aa's that make you swing more since on a per swing basis the aggro is about double when the delay is not.
Very good example of agro based on swings is Seru. We killed Seru using a BoW warrior. If damage was most of the basis for aggro then there is almost 0 chance of that warrior holding aggro over the course of a 45 minute fight (20 people 3 clerics I think) based just on the hate proc alone. Hell if they didn't proc before the first heal/debuff landed they would loose aggro immediately if swings were not the greater portion of your aggro basis and damage was.
Those aa's make more swings and thus more aggro. That is far more important than increasing your chance to take a repost by a miniscule percentage on any given meele round. People who say that getting offensive AA's is bad for a tank need to stop being silly and look at the good effects and the bad and realize increasing a whole raids DPS by 10-20% due to others not having to worry much about aggro is MUCH greater than taking a grand total of maybe 2-3 extra reposts over a 4-5 minute fennin ro fight or whatever mob. Hell the DPS increase to the raid alone will shorten the fight enough that the total number of reposts taken over the course of the fight will almost certainly decrease in total.

Do you people who say the AA's are bad use a haste item? If so why? Obviously those haste items would be much better on all the other characters since it makes you swing more and thus get reposted more.
Do you get chanter haste when you tank? If so why? Let the chanters save their mana since you obviously don't need this spell on you as it will jsut get you reposted more.
Those are exctly the SAME thing you are talking about with the aa's. More swings = more repostes thus if you are actually getting haste from the chanter and are actually wearing a haste item you are just a hippocrit really.
Any warrior who said "I won't buy the more swings aa's cause I get reposted more even though it would make my aggro 10-15% better " would certainly not pass their probation or be given a probation in my guild or 99% of the higher end guilds I know of.
