Battleblade
Many Warriors and Knights alike have commented on automatic trial version assignment as a system that gives Knight kiddie versions of the trials.

I believe LDoN Hard gives us a hint that Hard does not have to mean a high DI boss mob needing a Cleric and a Warrior to kill.

So, suggest trials that would have different group staffing requirements and still test group readiness. I'm going to cheat and start with those in LDoN with some modification.

Assassinate - high DI mob. Always appears in the same place. While getting to the mob can be challanging, it's not a major part of the test.

Capture the Flag - tracking, navigation, and the ability to avoid unnecessary combat while dealing with combat that can't be avoided is the challange of this trial. Scouting, tracking, invis, and analytical ability gets you through this trial. The flag mob wanders.

Slaughter - wanderers and fast re-pops makes add control the challange. Add-type tanking is an asset. Some mobs are rootable and mezable/some are not. Good decision skills are key.

Smackdown - 5ish high damage output/low HP mobs must be found and destroyed. Since they have low hit points the ability to unload damage quickly as well as to find and reach these mobs insures success. Low HP nuisence mobs complcate things by being a drain on mana.

Just some ideas. I'm curious that if such trials were devised, could they be a valid test of readiness, allow all classes to participate in trials, and provide some variety that tank your way to the high DI boss mob does't provide.

The Sewers trials suggested that this was the way things were going to be in the trials beyond, but it hasn't been. Frankly since everyone may have to pass every trial, a choice of trials is needed for each or more variety and an open slot for those that don't provide much value for a given trial.

I think the "equally challenging" part is the problem in LDoN. People flock to the best risk/reward - not the easiest. (otherwise they'd never enter PoP ;) )

Rescue missions (I've done a number of them) seem to be significantly harder because you have to clear the way before the target will follow you out - then you have to add in the time to get the target out once you've found them. Assasinate is easier than rescue because you don't have to get out, but the downside is you have to fight a named mob with NO CHANCE of a drop. Both of these missions also need trackers to find the named mob you are after.

Contrast those 2 with collections and slaughter missions. No tracker needed, just jump in and kill stuff till you are done. If you get a named, you get a drop.

I generally only play collection and slaughter now because that's the best risk/reward (by far IMHO.) If they changed the reward on the other 2, I'd play them for variety even though they are slightly harder.
