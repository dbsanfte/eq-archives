As a poster said just have someone snare him a few time to keep aggression on him. After that happens you can run people anywhere in his room and he will not attack them, unless it's the person who snared him. Just keep in mind that aggression does decay over time, so it would be best that the ranger/druid refresh the cast every once in a while.

I would turn him so he would be facing the very back wall when I MT'd him. We would setup all the healers along the back wall. All the nukers and melee would stay on his side, NEVER let ayone get behind him if you face him towards the back wall. As someone already said just blast heal, do not try a CH chain. He should not take longer then 2 - 3 mins to kill so mana should not really be a problem even with chain heals.

05-25-2003, 09:40 AM
My guild did him for the first time last night. Is a very fun fight, vyemm hits for 1200 post defensive so you will burn a lot of mana trying to keep a MT alive if you do not do a tank rotation. Grummus slow does land and is helpful, but he still hits and reposts enough to burn your mana out if you are a guild that actually is in Ntov to get the upgrades and dont have the DPS to put him down too quickly.

Rotating tanks is easy enough on him as his knockback gives you a nice window of opportunity to do so, just call the switch after an AE and your gtg. Just make sure to call out a warning to the healers when the MT is at the 30 second mark.


A bard Playing AE magic resist will definitely help out and give you some resists to the KB so that you can keep pouring on the DPS. The only problem we had was having our healers get stacked up in the back after a KB and be stuck and out of heal range, but we had every offhealer assisting to keep the MT alive with quick heals so we were able to not have any slip ups.

Took us about 9 min with 34 people and 2 flawless tank switches. This is an extremely good training fight for getting your guild used to doing tank switches and handling mobs with Knockback attacks.

Most important thing to note,DO NOT position people where walls intersect into corners.If the people hanging out in an area like this get KBed they will all funnel into the corner and stack on each other...

Ever zoned into pok from the nexus and found yourself stuck amongst 5 people who afked while zoning? Same thing .

When those people stacked and stuck happen to be your healing team your fucked.

When you stand up top of his lair with the ramp slighty to your right you want to have yourself straight across on the opposite wall,You want your Melee to be down below you right to the left of where the ramp opens up into his lair.And your Healers/nukers of to the left of the melee along Vyemms side.

When He KB's you,and he will he will turn immediately to the melee,since they are directly to the left of the ramp opening,they will just hi the wall their backs are against when KBed,your casters are still alongside Vyemm and as such are not being hit by the KB.If you are lucky he will have summoned you back to him just as you land from the KB,Take a couple steps backwards to turn him bout face away from the melee and continue tanking....

If you can,Tank switch.Even with the slow he can put out some decent DPS on you,and if casters get caught in the AE you may go a little longer without a heal than you would normally like=P Defensive is a bonus in a fight like Vyemm....
