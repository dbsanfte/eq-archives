Vigdis SangCaru
10-01-2003, 03:14 PM
I honestly think the knight class is clueless as to what the problems are

We gotta stop with the class-wide paint brush. It only makes us look bad. From reading that thread I could see that several if not most of the SK's understood the problem and a few even had some pretty good ideas on fixing it.

From the mouth of one SK:

1) Put all discs on separate timers. This would give warriors something to do during battle and allow them to fully use some of the more fun abilities they already have. With this, a warrior could take different roles during battle depending on what type of disc they currently have up.

2) Get rid of defensive and give warriors inherently better mitigation abilities. Wariors are the arms masters of EQ doing nothing all thier lives but focusing on thier ability with arms and armor. Why shouldn't they be noticeably better at it than anyone else? Asmuch as a 20% mitigation advantage over all other classes doesn't seems far out of line and would help minimize the retuning required after the loss of defensive. This would give warriors a strong advantage in tanking over a knight. This mitigation advantage could also be pared down depending on any snap taunt abilities given to a warrior. i.e. more aggro control = less inherent mitigation advantage and vice versa.

3) Give warriors some fun discs that are valuable to a group all on seperate timers so warriors aren't stuck using one all the time. An abilitie like group shielding where a warrior gives his entire group enhanced mitigation abilities for a limited time would make a warrior very desired in LDoN runs where adds come fast and furious and would balance them with the group abilites of SK and paladins. Another ability that would work well is a gorup atk bonus simulating the tactician role of a warrior directing his group in thier attacks. There are obviouly a lot of idea that could fit here and would make a warrior valuable in any group even when they are not tanking.

4) Make warrior abilties scale throughout thier lifespan. Right now, there are very large and obvious parts of a warriors's career where they gain nothing. Levels 40-50 are just chaff for a warrior since they only gain level inherent bonuses while other classes get new spells etc.. Levels 61-65 are the same way. Why not during these levels warriors gain some of the discipline abilities previously mentioned? This doesn't mean that a warrior would get a new abilities every few levels since some of the discs could be simple upgrades of a previous one. However, to make the class fun the more new and unique abilities added the better.

5) Warriors need some utility and fun to do abilities just to make the class more useful when a group isn't immediately available and just plain more fun for the player. Just giving clickies does not cover this area since why should a warrior have to do a long boring camp or pay an exhorbitant amount for an item that any caster could buy at a vendor? How about enhancing warrior regen out of combat? Say if a warrior has not engaged in combat for a set period of time, say a minute, they begin to regen very fast. They could not use this ability if they have attacked or been attacked within the time mandated. With this, warriors could solo for experiance or gear with some effectiveness.

6) Along this same line warriors could be given the ability to root or snare on a limited basis. Obviously these abilites could be overpowered in some ways in the end game if taken as they are now but the root and snare warriors get could be modified just for them. These abilities could be discs and would follow the same outlines as the abilities previously mentioned. An ability like AE grapple could represent the warriors tactical control of a combat area and would effectively AE root or snare (with no aggro component on the snare possibly). These abilities could be balanced with reuse timers or damage components on them such as how shaman dots and pre-61 druid roots are balanced now.

Take for example the situation where a group suddenly gets a lot of adds, the warrior could AE root for a few seconds to give the group's CCers time to work. However the root just damaged all the adds so a resist could very well kill any overeager CCer. Care would have to be used to balance how fast true CC can be achieved with how much damage the warrior is taking. The resist rare on the root could be a further balancing tool of this ability. AE or single target snare would follow the same lines though removing the aggro component of the snare would be suggested to limit its insane aggro abilities.

7) Give warriors the real ability to rampage and flurry like warrior mobs do. The PoP AAs warriors currently have are a waste of time and don't present any AA customization option for warriors. Right now, warriors above all other classes have a set AA path with little to no deviation possible. Warriors absolutely have to focus on defensive AAs to be useful Nothing else mattters with the exception of AE taunt and that only because it is guaranteed taunt. How about rampage as an active ability on a set timer or maybe just as an ability that could be switched off and on? On adds this would give the warrior and his group the idea on whether to CC adds or just take them and possibly raise the group's DPS with the warriors possible ramp damage. With this, warriors could gain some appreciable situational dps. In particular, this ability would be of value during some LDoN runs and disatrous during others. Warriors just gained some customization and required intellect to play.

True flurry as an AA or given ability would not be hideously unbalancing for a warrior since it serves only to increase thier dps with aggro only affected by damage and possibly more swings per round. Having this ability on a timer seems silly since warrior mobs in game alway have it on naturally. Why shouldn't warriors, being the truly ferocious combat masters they are, be able to flurry inherently or as a passive AA? The check for rampage could be dex based the same as mobs and could be balance tuned by the proc rate.

And the bottom line is I understand that SKs don't wish to be relegated to gimpiness like they were in Kunark. I also understand that warriors ought to given an advantage in tanking that just isn't present right now. Again, I don't know exactly how to fix it. But at least the SKs are offering up ideas, whether bad or good.
