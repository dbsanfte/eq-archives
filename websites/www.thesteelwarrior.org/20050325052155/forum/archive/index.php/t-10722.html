Corruption - Doable with fewer numbers. This is more about the raid focusing and prioritizing what class needs to die next. If the raid is all over the place, then they will win.

Endurance - Prolly doable with 45-50 as long as you have 3 clerics per tank. It's no secret, the Ancients kill themselves, hence why it's called the trial of Endurance (~25 minute fight). You can pump all the dps you want on the ancients, but it's not going to move their hp bar. Focus on kill the adds (except for the defenders which are mezzable) and keeping your tanks alive and you're golden. This, however, is a little harder than it sounds. The Ancient's dps can be a little spikey at times, and the difficulty in this trial is really because the adds move almost faster than you can see them. The second they pop, they're halfway across the room. It's pretty stupid imho.

Specialization - Depends on your progression. If you're Txevu level and have beaten Noqufiel, then it's probably doable with slightly less than 50. If you're like my guild who is just now realizing the importance of higher end GoD loot, it's 54 or nothing, and no we have still not beaten Spec yet. Master of Specialization has a metric fuckton of hitpoints. My guild is pretty dps heavy, and after 12 minutes he was still at 25%. I'd guess he has upwards of 4 to 5 million hitpoints with some pretty crazy regen.

Foresight - It's about the guild following directions. The raid itself gets harder as more die off simply by design. It always assigns tasks for 7 individuals at a time. If more and more people are dieing off, there are fewer and fewer to assign the tasks to, and eventually it gets to the point where you're getting spammed with tasks left and right, and of course you die. This trial would be easily doable with lower numbers, but you're optimally going to want a full raid (54) of no two boxes.

Hatred - Control the ferans and you win /shrug

Never attempted adaptation.
