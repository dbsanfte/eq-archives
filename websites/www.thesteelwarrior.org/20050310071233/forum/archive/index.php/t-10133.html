Recently, I picked up 2 new accounts and am currently in the process of levelling up 2 new toons so that I can 3 box with my warrior.

I decided to go with a Shaman and a Ranger, but during some guild brainstorming, the Beastlord/Cleric option came up. I ended up doing a quick pro/con comparison and here is what I came up with:

PRO (Shaman/Ranger):
Tracking and Snare available
Balance (Shaman provides slow/healing/buffs, Ranger provides good DPS)
Shaman slow is better at Beastlord slow
Easier to manage characters (/autofire makes a Ranger cake to box)

PRO (Beastlord/Cleric):
Resurrection available
Balance (Beastlord slows/buffs/provides DPS, Cleric has virtue and CH)
Better buffs and heals, making harder content easier and more available
Pacification indoors (i.e. LDoN)

CON (Shaman/Ranger):
No resurrection or indoor pacification (soloing LDoNs difficult if not impossible)
Content restricted due to limited healing

CON (Beastlord/Cleric):
Pet push! God, I hate pet push
Managing positioning for 2 melee classes can be difficult (I'm constantly fidgeting with my positioning on the warrior) especially with pet push

I'm sure I'm missing some stuff. In any case, I've levelled the Ranger/Shaman to 25/24 respectively, so I'd really rather not start over if there's not something glaring that I'm missing. I know that I have a ways to go before I'll be able to do anything worthwhile with them, but once the shaman gets to 65 with some healing AAs, and the ranger gets a decent bow and EQ / AM3, possibilities will become available. These chars are really only for me to be able to do something besides greenie farming when friends/guildies aren't available to group with, so I like to think that I'll be content with 3 boxing a BoT camp or DH expedition with them.

As a side note, I plan to keep the warrior at 65 until I can get all of my non OoW defensive AAs, HP AAs and WA5 (about 90ish more AAs to go).

Suggestions/advice/feedback would be appreciated.
