Eoden
I found that the resists came with the gear as I progressed through content. Raiding velious you will get a few nice svall items - Vindi BP for example. By the time I was raiding Luclin my resists were all near the 100 mark - this was good enough at the time.

Gear from raiding Luclin with a few nice VT bits had my MR close to 200 and the rest of my resists around the 150's and now being close to finished in the Ele planes my resists are all near the 200s.

Im not sure what you are expecting. There is alot of nice bazaar buyable gear out these days, some of it superior to what was raided for 2 years ago, but you can't expect to XP to 65 and 100AAs and jump into the high raiding game and have the required HPs, AC or resists without working through some of the earlier raid content. Ssra and ToV / Kael geared people up to do VT. VT geared people up to work through PoP Flags and the ele planes gear you up for time.

As you are now you should be fine to swap in one or 2 bits of gear - a froggy crown or something, get a couple of resist buffs and your resists will be sweet for ToV AE's or kunark dragons etc - 100 unbuffed used to be the target for resists for those zones. From there you'll start to pick up a few items that upgrade you in HP/AC and also have good resists so you wont need to swap gear in and out.

When I first tanked halls of testing dragons I was swapping in 2 8svall rings, 2 BD bracers and a froggy crown. Sure I lost a few hundred hps and a hundred or so AC, but if you werent the one taking melee beats it saved healers mana so was well worth it. If you are the one tanking then you'll have heals landing on you anyway so resists arent as important (unless the mob fears).

the problem with warriors is that if you were to swap in resists you'd probably lose HP and AC or other stats. if resist gear had desireable tanking stats we'd probably use that gear all the time. when i was MT for my previous guild the only resist i worried about was MR, since most of the odd effects check against MR. the majority of the other spells that check against fire and cold are usually DDs where the majority of posion and diseases are generally DoTs. basicly, while i was tanking i had the full attention of the cleric chain so the extra damage from DDs and DoTs werent very noticable through a CH rotation (we had a very tight chain). seasons, GoD, and tribunal, are usually good enough for general raiding. in the event that your up against the odd mob that has some AE or proc that checks against a specific resist, a bard is usually enough to top you off or at least get you past 400.
