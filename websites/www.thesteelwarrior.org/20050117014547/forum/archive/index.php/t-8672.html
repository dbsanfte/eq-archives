Most skills in EQ aren't checked as a cumulative sum. For example let's say you have stalwart endurance 3 (~70% stun resist), and you've 5% stun resist, you don't resist a stun 75% fo the time. What happens is 70% of the time SE3 will fire off, and then you resist the remaining 30% stuns that do get through 5% of the time with the stun resist mod.

I'd imagine TM and Strikethrough interact the same way. Let's say TM3 = 50% to ignore special defenses and you've 10% chance via Strikethrough, we don't get the full expected benefit (50+10 = 60%), but instead you'd get TM3 50% of the time, and then strikethrough 10% of the 50% TM3 failed to work, for a total of 55% special defenses negated. Note that the order those are checked doesn't matter whatsoever.

To illustrate this lets assume 1,000 stunning attacks against you...

Of the 1,000 attacks Stalwart Endurance has the following effect....
Stuns Resisted - 700 - 70.00%
Stuns Not Resisted - 300 - 30.00%

Of the failures above Stun Resists has the following effect...
Stuns Resisted - 15 - 5.00%
Stuns Not Resisted - 285 - 95.00%

Total Resists - 715 - 71.50%
Total Failures - 285 - 28.50%

And for those not good at math, it works the other way.....

Assuming 1,000 stunning attacks against you.....

Of the 1,000 attacks Stun Resist has the following effect...
Stuns Resisted - 50 - 5.00%
Stuns Not Resisted - 950 - 95.00%

Of the failures above Stalwart Endurance has the following effect....
Stuns Resisted - 665 - 70.00%
Stuns Not Resisted - 285 - 30.00%

Total Resists - 715 - 71.50%
Total Failures - 285 - 28.50%
