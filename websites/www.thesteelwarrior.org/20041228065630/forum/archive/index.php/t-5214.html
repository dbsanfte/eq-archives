"dispelling mobs were not expected by me, so preparing for that with more stat items is a great suggestion. "

The best way to deal with a dispelling mob is to have an insta clicky item. Be it a bracer of the hidden, or a shrunken goblin skull earring, or whatever. That way the buffs you want to keep stay up. The buffs you don't care about and can replenish with the click of a button are the ones that get dispelled.

"How will the group I join up with be able to really distinguish how I'm going to tank, especially if they're recruiting me for the job based solely on level? "

As someone stated before, let the healer know how many hit points you're working with. Then it's their job to adjust. Not much you can do about it, heh.

" I can't help but think that the dungeon itself it more difficult than I'm used to as well, which contributed to me struggling. "

The hardest problem in Guk isn't the dispells. You'll encounter dispelling mobs in each and every dungeon of LDoN. Guk just has them closer to the entrances. Which means you encounter them earlier and depending on mob distribution/collection item distribution may have to deal with more of them during your adventure.

(Case in point ... Mistmoore, everyone's favorite LDoN zone has a ton of casters in their dungeons, and some do indeed dispell. However, the casters are almost always packed in the back of the zones, and by the time you get to them you only need like 4 more kills or 2 more items or whatever).

Another thing to consider is that unless you were having to tank multiple monsters at the same time, or you were tanking high risk versions of the guk frogs, they're not that hard to tank completely unbuffed.

I pull in LDoN a lot, and oftentimes I stand there, taking a bit of a beating until the "add" is mezzed.

The above poster mentioned haste may have been an issue. Which is certainly possible since there are quite a few shamans in Guk.

But, still, Guk ... don't be intimidated by it. You'll encounter the exact same situations with the same types of mobs in Tak, Rujark, Mistmoore and Miragul's. It just won't be spooky frogs and annoying beholders.

And for the most part you can stand up to those mobs buffless, IF your healer and you are on the same page.

Good luck. Have fun.
