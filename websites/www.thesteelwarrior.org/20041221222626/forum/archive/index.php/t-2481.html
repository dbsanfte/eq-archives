As Valeris and Kaesorn mentioned, /shield is something that everybody sees. It's amazing how often people see me shield them and say things like "omg you're the first warrior I've ever seen that uses shield!". Sure most of the time the person would have lived without /shield, but people see that, and appreciate it. Use /shield and use it often. Use it anytime someone besides you is getting hit and you're in range. Don't worry about saving it for a dire situation, it comes back in 3 minutes or something.

Little things like that are the difference between a good warrior and a mediocre one. People say warriors are easy to play but hard to play well. The reason for that is they've grouped with warriors that don't use taunt effectively, that don't use /shield, that stand there for 10 seconds when someone pulls aggro off of them, because they're watching TV or cybering instead of paying attention. That then yell at everyone in their group for not knowing how to play. That spam the group with links of all their uber gear and then still can't hold aggro worth a crap. That don't turn mobs so pets can hit them in the back. That don't want to pull, and get trains every time they do. That won't adapt to different slowers and healers. The list goes on forever.

If you want to get pickup groups, know how to hold aggro, with proximity, taunt, procs, bash, and watch for things like slow messages to know when to taunt. Don't be afraid to pull and know what you're doing when you pull so you don't train your group. When you are pulling, do it fast, be off like a shot the second the mob in camp dies or starts to run, but stop if you hear your cleric start a heal on you. Do everything *fast*. Shoot arrows at mobs on inc if you're not pulling, hit them with bash and taunt the second they get to camp, stand close to the slower, watch for slow messages and be super-quick on the taunt button. Be ready to /shield if the taunt doesn't work. Be cheerful and pleasant in group chat, don't yell at people even if they suck, just give them helpful hints and if they don't listen, put them on your "do not group" list. Know about gear and always be looking to upgrade, through quests or the bazaar or whatever. Put in the effort to maximize your character, even if your guild doesn't give you access to uber stuff. Don't try to get more than your share of the loot, and make a point of splitting everything equally.

All this stuff will get you a reputation as a kick-ass tank. People don't remember exactly what you did, but they remember that grouping with you was a lot more pleasant than that noob warrior they grouped with that couldn't hold aggro and yelled at everybody. I've had guildmates ask me "how can you hold aggro better than XX warrior who has better weapons?" It's all these little things that add up to you being a great exp group tank. People DO remember things like that, and it WILL get you groups. And the best groups are with that really smart cleric or shaman that remembers you and sends you a tell for another group when they see you looking for one. Good players remember other good players, and try to group with them again. It goes both ways.

If you think playing a warrior means hitting auto-attack and mashing taunt then you are not playing your warrior very well, and shouldn't be suprised you have trouble getting groups.

So there you go....Brutul's Guide to Getting Groups Even Though You're Not a Paladin.

EDIT: Oh, and to avoid ninja'ing Grimmlok's thread...Yes, /shield DOES make you a hero, every single time you use it. After all, you are sacrificing yourself for the good of someone else right? All warriors deserve to have their ass kissed profusely each and every time they heroically hit their /shield key. Spread the word.

Here is a nice easy way to be able to shield someone at the drop of a pin. This is how I do it, it takes a few keystrokes and you need to watch your target bar but you can do it without loosing a swing on the mob.


First of all, in the keyboard settings of the options menu... there is a cool shortcut that will let you toggle the last 2 targets. I personally bound this key to the "Q" button. It is right up near my assist hotkey (number 1) so I can switch targets really frickin fast.


Second of all, unless you excel at typing I recommend a /shield hotkey. It doesn't need a name in there, just /shield. Name it "The ass saver" if you please. I personally don't use it, because I just type out shield, but if you have problems typing somewhat fast, then I reccomend it.

Ok so the basic procedure is this; The monk pulls a_badass_mob_001. Yay, it's in camp now, you have an unslowed mob beating on your ass. Chanter tashes, but luckily doesn't get agro. But! Problems arise. Mr. Joe_Blow_Chanter slows and grabs agro. What is a warrior to do?! Well, you hit your taunt key (if it is up). Oh no that failed! The chanter is taking a beating, and his rune is gone, so what do you do? Assist the mob, hit your shield hotkey, and then hit the "Q"Button to get back on the mob. (if you assist the chanter, you may agro something he mezzed, or he may have a group member targeted or whatnot)

That, imo, is the way to use shield. It's quick and easy and efficient. I find it a habbit now, that I'll assist the mob when I see the slow message, only to find myself targeted and bash my "Q" button to get back on the mob.


Hope this helps some warriors in their quest to /shield (we all know it's /hero, though).
