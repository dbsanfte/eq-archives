Assuming max, or close to max dexterity, without WA, you will proc main hand ~2 times per minute, and off hand ~1 time per minute. With WA5, those number are doubled.

Knowing this, to get an average of 1 dps, you need 30 points of main-hand DD proc, or 60 points of off-hand DD proc with no Weapon Affinity, or half those numbers with WA5. With WA5, plus Ingenuity, minus resist rate, a 150 DD proc will do ~10 dps in main hand, and ~5 dps in off hand.

Those numbers will be almost totally unaffected by haste level.

For a straight damage augment, you have to decide how many points of damage = 10 or 5 dps. I don't know the calculations for warriors off the top of my head, but I kow for rogues a 1 damage augment > all proc augments for dps in main hand.

Of course, no damage augment is going to come remotely close to matching the hate-building power of Anger/Ykesha augments, or the healing power of lifetap augments.

it's HIGLY dependent on your HASTE, and WEAPON, and AA, and SKILLS, and LEVEL, and MOB, and MOB's POSITION !!!!
if you swing 155 times per minutes, you will get more results as if you swing 21 times. If you want to know what a DM aug will do to you, you first have to know what is YOUR average hit per second (hps : you have to take into account the miss/dodge/parry/... of the mob) and YOUR average DI multiplier (avg_rng : your avarage domage minus your min domage divided by your domage listed on your weapon). Thus if you increase your DI by one, you will increase your dps, from this weapon, by hps*avg_rng.

it's your toon, thus it's your job to calculate it.


BTW, 150DD is 7.5DPS in primary with 255+ DEX, WA5, and CE0, without counting crit and resist/immune.
