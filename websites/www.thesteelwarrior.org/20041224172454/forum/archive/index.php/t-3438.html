10-06-2003, 11:46 AM
As player gear/abilities catch up to content more and more classes can tank effectively. It didn't take too long after PoP came out that well geared knights could hold their own tanking in most PoP situations. Now, as things drag further along, well geared and played characters of the other hybrid classes are tanking well in much of PoP/LDoN.

Mudflation always favors generalists over specialists. Warriors are taking the brunt of this. There are few situations that require a Warrior. If the situation does not require even a plate tank, and the group is geared well enough to deal with the healing, Beastlords and Rangers can tank just fine.

This to me points out the fallacy behind nerfing knights. You will just move the target one step down the ladder. Warriors need to be improved more than knights need to be nerfed. Also, the situations that are risky enough to favor a Warrior should be more rewarding. Right now there is very little single group content that actually favors a Warrior once you average about 100 AA in the group.

AA is a huge problem for encounter and class balancing. The difference in power between a 65 character with 0 AA and a 65 Character with 150 AA is profound. Yet both characters get the same amount of experience from a given mob. There is precious little benefit to face tougher content when you can race through mobs designed around a level 65 with 0 AA at a rapid clip. LDoN emphasizes this currently. Normal missions are actually challenging for a group with average level of 64 and 6 AA. A group of 65s with 250 AA gets the EXACT SAME encounter though. Of course they can tear through it like nobody's business.

Nerfing knights doesn't help this situation one whit. Increase Warriors to be competitive for groups in that situation (read: aggro increase at least), and add content that favors a warrior with a level of reward that makes people choose to do it.
