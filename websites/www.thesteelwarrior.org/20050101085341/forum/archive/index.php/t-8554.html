08-23-2004, 08:11 AM
I agree with Kailyn; if this warrior is your main, you can't keep up gear-wise with the alt of a 65 mage who can farm several thousand plat a night.
Focus on what you can control: donig everything to aquire and hold aggro; positioning the mob, being aware of adds, reacting immediately ifyour casters get hit, stuff like that. Pay attention to mob pathing and respawn rates. If you level in OT or DL, learn to pull (proper pulling in outdoor zones like these involves picking up on mob pathing, characteristics, topography, knowing the strength of your group, and so forth). Do what you can to make your entire group's performance better.
My only experience playing a healing caster is with a shaman, but I can tell you that at that level I worried much more about whether the tanks could play than what their hitpoints were. Pull rates and even mob selection can be adjusted in most zones, but there is no adjustment for dirt-stupid play, and stupid will get a group killed way faster than a tank whose hitpoints are a tad low. Few things more irritating than being unable to cast because three adds are beating on you while the full cultural twink warrior is mindlessly tanking one mob and completely ignoring the spammed "ON ME" messages filling his chatbox.
The best check for your relative basic mitigation/survival level (AC, hitpoints, other minor stats) is your healer. In one or two heals, a caster will pretty much know how many hitpoints you have. After a mob or two, he'll have a good idea of your relative ability to mitigate damage. If you have some caster friends/guildmates, simply ask them how hard it is for them to keep you alive. If you're getting hints that you're a bit weak, head to the bazaar and search for upgrades by AC, looking for the best upgrade bang for the buck.
