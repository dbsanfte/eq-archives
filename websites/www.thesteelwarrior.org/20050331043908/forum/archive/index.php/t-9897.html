Once you get defensive stance, its easier to hold aggro. I mainly use it when I am tanking in groups. Here are the abilites I use for getting and keeping aggro:

Demoralizing Shout - AE attack debuf, can chain cast.
Sunder Armor - Armor debuf, can chain cast, our main aggro ability. imo this is the WoW warriors incite
Rend - DoT that generates threat and rage. works very nice when applied to multiple mobs
Revenge - a counter attack that is pretty good aggro. Becomes available after you block (like the shield block ability ;-)
Taunt - good old taunt. Works like EQ taunt. 10 sec refresh. gets resisted a lot when the mob is higher level.
Disarm - just got this so I've not used it much, but its decent aggro against mobs that use weapons.

I'm not 100% sure how good the aggro is on stuns/interrupts but so far it seems decent. So add Shield Bash to the above list if you use a shield (I do).

If you have tactical mastery (which you should), and you're able to start off with charge, do that and then there are two things you can do. If your group tries to start attacking immediately and not give you any time to get aggro, fire off mocking blow and then switch to defensive stance, demoralize, and start sundering. If they're smart and wait two or three seconds to start attacking, just switch to defensive after charging and demoralize followed by sunder. If you're having problems with aggro, you might want to fire off a sunder first. Taunt if you lose aggro followed by a sunder if possible. Once you have aggro, if you have revenge talents I would recommend using shield block followed by revenge as much as possible, throwing in your other abilities every once and a while. Switch back to battle to hamstring if needed.

If you can't start with charge, pull with your gun/bow and try to get enough rage to sunder as soon as possible.

This is a very general guideline to follow, as each situation is different. I can use this strategy to hold aggro the majority of the time I'm grouped.
