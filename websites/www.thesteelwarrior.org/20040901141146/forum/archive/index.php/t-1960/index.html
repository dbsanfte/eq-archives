I'd like to offer a solution to a small part of the warriors vs knights class balancing issue: knight aggro lock. Reading the other threads everyone agrees that aggro lock at the high level game is unbalanced, lame, and trivializes (or contributes to the trivialization of) exp groups.

Paladin Solution:

- Lower level stuns landing on mobs that are immune to them cause zero aggro. If its a DD / stun combo the aggro is based only on the DD portion.

- Take away the flash of light crutch. Not sure if higher level mobs are immune to blind, if so "immunity = zero aggro" should fix.

- up Force of Akilae to 150 mana cost or so.


Shadowknight Solution:

- Cut the aggro from Sk's casting darkness spells to be DOT based only. Have the mobs ignore the snare-component aggro.

- Maybe up the mana cost of Terror of Thule a bit.

I think these few simple changes would mitigate the aggro lock problem in exp groups. My solution would make aggro lock still possible, but would cause downtime for the knight to med up after. To attain similar levels of performance they currently have knights would have to mix self-buff procs, weapon procs, taunts, and aggro spells. It would open up knight weapon selection to situationally favor high aggro procs on weapons instead of just doing the dmg/dly math. Members of that group would have to pay attention to what spells are cast or wait a fixed time before unleashing all types of hell on the mob.

Note I am not trying to address whats wrong with warriors in this thread. Im just trying to address what I see is wrong with exp groups at the moment. Casters have been dumbed down by the aggro lock knights give to the point where they dont even think before chain mashing buttons. Bring back aggro management to every class.

I agree with Aeka. Downtime is not the answer IMO. The way to balance the situation is by looking at the 2 primary requirements of tanking. Aggro control and healing efficiency. The two should be proportional. If a knight has 90% of the healing efficiency that a warrior has then a warrior should have 90% of the aggro control that a knight has. If the knight has 50% of the healing efficiency then the warrior should have 50% of the aggro control.

All other abilities and any effects given by spells other than aggro control are things that boost utility but not tanking ability. They should be balanced against each other separately. So while LoH, root, FD, etc all contribute to the overall desireability and usefulness of a class they have nothing to do with tanking. They should be balanced against things like /shield, increased DPS, defensive, differences in available equipment, etc.

So leaving out concerns about heals, defensive, etc, and just focusing on XP group tanking we have knights with nearly identical healing efficiency and vastly superior aggro control. For both to be desired as XP group tanks 1 of 2 things need to happen. Either both need to be roughly equivalent at healing efficiency AND at aggro control, or one needs to be CLEARLY superior at tanking and the other needs to be CLEARLY superior at aggro control. That addresses the desireability of warriors vs knights as TANKS.

Once that is resolved then you compare the other differences between teh two classes and add or subtract from abilities and spells until they balance out. So if spells give a clear advantage to knights after tanking is considered balanced then add skills, DPS, etc to warriors to compensate. If warrior DPS gives warriors a clear advantage then add spells, DPS, etc to the knights to compensate.
