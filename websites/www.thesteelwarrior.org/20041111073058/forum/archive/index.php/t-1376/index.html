QuirtEvans
Looking at posts recently, I'm absolutely amazed at the obsession with HP among the warrior community.

Don't get me wrong, I agree that HP are crucially important. But the focus on HP to the point of ignoring every single other stat seems to me misguided.

With the arrival of PoP, AC has become critically important once again. Pre-PoP, there was an argument that there was a "soft cap" on AC. Whether that was true or not pre-PoP, it's certainly not true in the PoP zones, it's been demonstrated that very high AC continues to provide benefits versus PoP mobs.

One recent example of the obsession with HP was when someone said that they wouldn't wear a stone visor because it doesn't have any HP on it. Excuse me? 20 AC, 10 STR, 10 AGI, a couple of decent sized saves, all in the face slot, and you'd wear something with much less AC and substantially smaller stats because it has a few HP? That seems silly to me.

What you need depends on how you play the game. I two-box quite a lot with a shammy. When I am two-boxing, it doesn't matter whether I have 3K, 5K, or 10K HP .. because my shammy's cheal is only 1800. Really, the place I need the extra HP is on the shammy, because the warrior never, ever dies unless the shammy dies first. When two-boxing with a shammy, damage avoidance, damage mitigation, and more DPS are all more valuable to a warrior than more HPs.

And if your AGI is under 120, goosing your AGI over 120 would be far, far more valuable than those last few HPs, because of the way AGI and AC interact when health starts to get low.

Yes, more HP is better. All else equal. Heck, HP may even be more important than any other single stat. But the singleminded pursuit of HP without considering the effect of each piece of equipment on all of your other stats is stupid. It's a multi-variable analysis, and it needs to be treated that way.

I chose 5% to make a point.


Let us compare 3 rings :

AoW : ac30 hp100
Algae : ac10 hp135
Moss : ac18 hp145

Which of the 3 is better ? Well lets take a look :
For a 9000hp full buffed warrior wanting a new ring
(Items give +50%ac as normal bonus, and +17%hp as AA bonus)

AoW will give : 45ac and 117hp
Algae will give : 15ac and 158hp
Moss will give : 27ac and 164hp

So
AoW = 9000+117hp AND 45ac
Algae = 9000+158hp AND 15ac
Moss = 9000+164hp AND 27ac

But how do we take into account the AC gain ?
As we saw before 100ac = -5% dps, lets assume again that
lowering the mobs DPS by 5%, is equivalent to having 5% more hp.
(I know it doesnt work like exactly that but lets assume it)

AoW = 9117 x 0.45 x 5% = 205 hp
Algae = 9158 x 0.15 x 5% = 69 hp
Moss = 9164 x 0.24 x 5% = 110 hp

This means, that AoW will give you 205 "Virtual HP" because of its ac.
If you want to "translate 30ac" for the 9000hp warrior its equivalent to 205hp.
So ac30 100hp = ac0 305hp statwise (assuption of course)

So the Final Comparaison for the 3 rings on a 9000hp fully buffed warrior
That is looking for a ring is :

AoW = 305 hp gained (real & virtual)
Aglae = 227 hp gained (real & virtual)
Moss = 274 hp gained (real & virtual)

Remember this is based on 2 very big assumptions :
* +100 ac = -5% dps taken
* -5% dps taken = +5% hp (virtual hp of course)


Now the choice between AC & HP is delicate. You need both
because some mobs will just SHRED you with low ac.
But HP will help you take hits.

In the end i tend to believe that as your max hp above 9K buffed
Ac becomes much much appealing for 2 reasons

- You gain a LOT more "Virtual HP" from dps reduction
(Thats if you believe DPS reduction = Virtual HP gain)

- AC helps a LOT reducing max hits (spikes) and spikes are killers in PoP

More HPs mean you can take more hits, true. But more AC may also mean you can take more hits, if it lowers the average value of the hit. Even if you have a cleric chealing you, there is some tradeoff between AC and HP .. there is some point at which lowering the damage you take is better than increasing the pool of HP against which damage is calculated. What that tradeoff is, I don't know. But certainly 50 AC > 1 HP, and 50 HP > 1 AC. So we can all agree, I think, that there is a point where one or the other is optimal, it's just a question of where that point is. Conventional wisdom used to say 1 AC was roughly equivalent to 10 HP, but I get the impression that many warriors seem to think that 10 HP is worth more than 1 AC these days.

Frodlin7th
I suppose I'll put it another way.

If someone claims that a single stat is always more important than another, they're probably wrong and should be dismissed.

There's different areas of this game, and there's times when one thing is more important than others.

For instance:

AC is less relevant against mobs in PoP that have an innate proc than those that have only high melee.

This does NOT mean that because those mobs exist, that AC is worthless or unimportant. It DOES mean that if you spend a majority of your time fighting such mobs, then you will probably see a greater benefit from hitpoints as opposed to AC. You may not though...

Let's say this mob has an innate proc which isn't affected by AC *and* fairly heavy melee as well. The combination of the two can kill you... at this point, minimizing one of the two attack types could be superior to simply piling on the hitpoints if it's possible to minimize one without minimizing the other. Is the innate proc resistable? Well hell, if that's the case, then resists > hitpoints on that mob.

When Cursed wasn't trivial.... I used to sacrifice a LOT of hitpoints AND AC to get my disease resist up.... and kicked his ass every time wearing indicolite boots and those gloves from Fungus Grove and a few other things. Resists were significantly > HP *and* AC on that mob before level 65 and multi bard songs and tribunal buffs.... FOR ME.

There is NO WAY that one can say that "every time such and such is better than such and such" unless you're talking about adding on stats that are maxed.

A good rule of thumb is this:

Until you close in on 1250AC, then AC is probably going to have more of an effect on you than anything else.

Once you near that point, then you can start thinking about swapping in some hitpoints and trying to balance things out.

Don't go overboard with hitpoints at the expense of AC and you'll be fine.
