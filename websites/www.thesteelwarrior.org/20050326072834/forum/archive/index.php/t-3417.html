I 4 box all the time and have been doing so for about 2 years now. I don't use macroquest or any other simular program.

The only real secrets to boxing 4 at once are:

1 - Class selection - Some classes do not lend themself to boxing. Bards are impossible to utilize anywhere close to full potential. I found that Shaman's at lower levels (when you have to chain cast canni) are difficult as well. The classes I box are Warrior, cleric, enchanter and mage. All 65s now with 300 to 375AA depending.

2 - Hot keys are your friends. The more repeated actions on your hotkeys the better. For example, while I slow with my chanter I almost never tash (getting agro on the chanter is a pain). So instead I have the first hotkey per pull on the mage as:

/assist Raivene
/pause 2
/pet attack
/cast 3

The cast 3 casts Mala on the mob. This is almost always enough debuff to allow the slow to hit.

The chanters hotkey, which is pressed right after the mages is:

/assist Raivene
/pause 2
/pet attack
/cast 2

Where /cast 2 is slow.

Animimation empathy is very nice to have on a boxed chanter btw. The chanter pet does some decent damage even if it can't tank worth a flip.

3 - Desk real estate.

Can't stress this one enough. I use 15 inch flatpanel monitors, smaller than normal keyboards and trackball mice. This reduces the footprint of the equipment to keep it manageable. Fitting 17 inch normal monitors or normal drag around mice would require too much space to be efficient. The desk itself is a patio bar lol. A two tiered desk that allows the monitors and mice on top level and the keyboards on the bottom. Works well for me.

4 - practice practice practice. At this point I can "solo" camps like arm/sup in tactics (great place to get sellable loots), any of the bots wing minis (still cannot take a tower boss with just my 4 sadly...) can use them in xp camps in earth a etc. In LDoN I can win normals on pretty much every map. Hards I can do, but usually will bring friends 2 box along with me and we win all of those usually (it's rare we lose anymore). I generally do NOT win a hard with just my 4, but I can get to and beat the named for loots pretty easily depending on the map. But that doesn't happen overnight. The more you box the better you become. Learning the tricks of your particular make up takes time. It took me a while to figure out that in most zones a warrior + mage = FD pulling. You just have to work through how to best utilize your toons.

4 or more boxing does not require programs such as Macroquest. It's fun to do, requires a lot of coordination and skill (IMO in anycase...;)) and the best thing is you NEVER EVER LFG.

I've done 3 or 4 here and completed them in 50 minutes and that was when I was pacifying my way along to 'ease' the pulls. I put everyone on autofollow on my cleric, this puts all the casters a good distance behind the cleric, who is always behind my warrior. This helps keep them out of harms way. I have 6 boxed Mistmoore several times, and found it to be fairly reasonable. I had some timing problems at first (failed first 5 while fighting boss mob), but last few have gone much smoother. The ECommons dungeon is pretty good also, but I've not done the others enough to form an opinion.

As to "how" to play multiple chars, I use 2 keyboards/monitors/mice, each hooked to a 4 way KVM switch that has hotkey swapping (Omnibox). Pressing scroll lock twice then computer number (1-4) switches to the appropriate computer. I have been using that and the built in EQ macros almost since EQ came out. I started with 2 boxing back in summer 99, and have slowly progressed to more (I recently 9 boxed Dozekar).

True, you don't get near the efficency from multiboxing as you do from different people playing, but then I never waste any time LFG. I just camp where I want to hunt, log on and get to it.

And, like someone mentioned above, I get bored just playing 1 or 2 chars anymore :)
