Found this (groups.google.com search 'beetmi') on the google usenet archive, thought it might be of interest to some people, especially those who put a lot of faith in displayed AC as being a meaningful guide to how well you take damage:



As an experiment, to examine the relationship between an EQ char's
displayed "AC" stat and actual combat results I created a L1
Human Paladin and provided him with a Finesteel Shortsword, a
Large Lanter, and a full suit of Banded Armor. Our hero's
stats are str85, sta100, agi75, dex75, wis80, int75, cha85.
The key stat here is AGI, which I wanted at the "norm" where
there are no bonuses or penalties.

For Beetmi's first mission as an armor test pilot, I sent him to
East Freeport were there is a Soulbinder and an almost always
empty newbie yard. A very nice additional feature of EFP for my
test is that "a fire beetle" is always a L2 mob (I have conned
easily over a hundred of these with various lowbies over the
last 2-1/2 years, always yellow to L1, even to L2, blue to L3
thru L5). I "fought" one or more "a fire beetle"s to 13 of my
own deathss, and killed a couple besides. I mostly just let them
kill me, using Lay Hands to prolong the agony, in order to get
a fairly large number of attacks logged. For these first results,
Beetmi had a Defense Skill ranging from 1 to 3, giving a displayed
AC ranging from 154 to 160. He was hit 650 times for 1234 damage
(1.90 avg per hit) with 177 misses (21.40% misses), for a net
damage per attack of 1.49.

Next, our hero was permitted to "test" his shortsword for a while.
Once he was L4 with his Defense skill maxed at 20 he was sent back
to EFP to engage some "a fire beetle"s again. This time he was
sent in naked, with a displayed AC of 59. He was hit 296 times
for 1078 damage (3.64 avg. per hit) with 404 misses (57.71% misses),
for a net damage per attack of 1.54.

To recap, comparing an "AC" of over 153 derived almost entirely from
armor to an "AC" of precisely 59 that was entirely intrinsic I saw
dramatic changes in the chance of "a fire beetle" to score a hit
and to the average damage per hit, with less than a 5% difference
in the average damage per attack between the two test cases.

I seems to me that this easily recreated test demonstrates quite
clearly that your displayed AC cannot be considered a "stat" by
any reasonable definition. "AC" is nothing more nor less than a
rough guide to your passive melee defensive capability computed
in a way that differs substantially from the mechanics used in
EQ to resolve melee combat. The evidence suggests a two-step
combat reolution in which the chance of an enemy scoring a hit is
affected primarily by your Defense skill while the amount of damage
done by a hit can be mitigated by your armor.

And a follow-up from the same thread:


Aonalin, secretly known to himself as Beetlfuud, volunteered
to test the The Emperor's New Armor (tm) at L1. Added to
the results produced by valiant cousin Beetmi we now have
a somewhat more detailed picture of the effect of armor
and Defense skill on combat.

Both characters are human paladins, they wear no jewelry,
carry no shield, have no buffs cast on them, and are either
naked or wearing full banded armor. character stats are:
str85, sta100, agi75, dex75, wis80, int75, cha85

The following tables show Defense skill, displayed AC, and
the results of attacks on the characters. All reported
attacks are by "a fire beetle" a L2 warrior-type mob that
scores 1 to 6 damage on a sucessful hit.



Armored results:
Def AC Hit Miss Hit% Dmg/Hit Dmg/Atk
---- -------- ---- ---- ---- ------- -------
20 201 480 617 43.8 1.47 0.64
1..3 154..160 650 177 78.6 1.90 1.49

Naked results:
Def AC Hit Miss Hit% Dmg/Hit Dmg/Atk
---- -------- ---- ---- ---- ------- -------
20 59 296 404 42.3 3.64 1.54
0..5 10...21 387 104 78.8 4.23 3.34


As you can see, there is no substantial change in hit%
between naked and armored for equal/similar Defense skill.
There appears to be a significant reduction in Dmg/Hit
associated with increased Defense skill. And there is a
very large decrease in Dmg/Hit shown when the character
dons armor.

Comparing the Armored Def (1..3) results to the Naked
Def (20) results is especially instructive. In light
of this I see no value at all in comparing displayed
AC between two characters unless their Defense skill
is near identical. It's pretty clear that displayed AC
is pure eye-candy and does not enter into combat
calculations.

Below is a further breakdown of the Naked results for
low Defense skill. You will see here a noticeable
decrease in Dmg/Hit associated with a relatively small
increase in Defense skill. Note that the difference is
very close to 1/2 the difference between Defense (0..2)
and Defense (20). Although seeing half of the change
in Dmg/Hit ocur over a fairly small portion of the skill
is not inconsistent with the many reports of large changes
in effectiveness when moving through "sweet spots" in
various areas of the game mechanics, the relatively small
samples (248 and 243 attacks in the two groups) keep me
from giving too much weight to this given the notorious
streakiness of the EQ random number generator. Still,
I find that interpretation of these numbers far more
believeable than the conspiracy theorists "level" mantra
(I simply cannot imagine what they think Defense skill
does, to keep bleating about "level", especially when I
know of no indication from Verant or other informed
sources ever that level per se affects passive melee
defense).


Broken-down Naked results for Defense (0..5):
Def AC Hit Miss Hit% Dmg/Hit Dmg/Atk

0..2 10...14 196 47 80.7 4.41 3.56
