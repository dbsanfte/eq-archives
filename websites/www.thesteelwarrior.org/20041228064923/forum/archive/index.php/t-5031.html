If you're low in the tanking order for your guild, working on offensive AAs will not help you move up the order. The top two warriors in my guild have max defensive AAs and the other warriors are all working on them.

As the warrior class lead in my guild I'm usually the one who chooses the tanking assignments, and when our max defensive AA warriors are not around, the guys who have made the most progress on defensive AAs will get the nod to tank for us over the guys who've been doing offensive AAs.

Most people play warriors because they want to be tanks, so unless you really prefer not tanking and don't mind being pigeon-holed as a non-tank, you should be working on defensive AAs.

If your agro is so horrible that you feel you must get some offensive AAs to help, the ones I would suggest are Tactical Mastery and Ferocity. These offer the biggest DPS increase for only 9 AA each. Working on CF, Flurry, double riposte, ambidex, punishing blade, all have lower damage increases for the AA spent or have limited use (ambidex and pb being specific to weapon type). Get Tactical mastery and/or ferocity and go no further until you finish ALL defensive AAs

I grind exclusively in LDoN. I have very limited playing time, and cannot raid; therefore, Adventure Points are my only path to truly excellent gear. When I started getting serious AA points, I spent them on offensive AAs.

Why? Because for normal LDoN missions, defensive AAs add literally nothing. Our standing LDoN group is an enchanter, a warrior (me), a beastlord (also me), some healer, and two DPS. Even with just beastlord slows (the chanter being busy maintaining a charmed mob), things don't kill me particularly effectively. And on the rare occasions that I did raid with my guild, I was already more than sufficiently equipped for any targets the rest of the guild was able to tackle.

The only thing I needed (remember, this was before Provoke) was a bit more aggro. And, of course, a lot more DPS. So I spent points on Ingenuity (consciously wasted, purely for fun and the nominal DPS increase) and Tactical Mastery (slightly better aggro while tanking).

I wasn't going to be fighting raid mobs (no time to join a raiding guild), and we weren't going to be able to tackle hard LDoN adventures (enchanter too fragile, no uber-guild guildies to help), so there was no reason for me to get even ND3, much less CA/CS/etc.

Then our enchanter joined an uberguild, and suddenly hard LDoN began to look possible. And Provoke came out, answering the aggro problem quite adequately. And suddenly, I found myself wishing I'd put those points into ND/CA/CS/PE rather than offensive AAs.

Do I think that offensive AAs are a reasonable starting choice? Hell, yeah; if my chanter hadn't gone and gotten all oober on me, I'd still be buying levels of Combat Fury and Punishing Blade. But I do believe that the defensive AAs are superior for anyone seriously trying to improve their utility as a warrior.

It's just that not everyone needs to improve their utility as a warrior; and especially for those who focus on LDoN, their utility as a DPS source may be of greater importance.

I'm not unhappy that I spent the points on Tactical Mastery; I still believe those to be well-spent simply to make aggro while tanking easier. When I turned on full hit/miss reporting, I was shocked how large a percentage of my attacks were being blocked, parried, riposted, etc.

But from now until I've maxed my defensive AAs, I don't expect to be spending any more points on offensive ones.
