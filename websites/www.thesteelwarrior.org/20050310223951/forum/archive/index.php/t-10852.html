I'm beginning to think that the 'proximity' hate mod on DoN mobs is higher than say, PoP mobs.

I tanked a lot in DoN again last night and with this thread in my mind, and my 'feeling' about aggro, I played around with some stuff (all-be-it in a non-scientific way).

Tanking for a group with a BL as the only other melee, I noticed that more often than normal the BL would be in the HoTT window. If I stepped 'into' the mob when that happened, more often than not and in fact, most of the time, I would regain aggro. If I just kept tanking at the same range (pretty close to the mob anyway, I always strive to be the closest person from habit) then it would take much longer, and often a couple of procs before I would regain aggro.

I tried this quite a few times, either moving into the mob a bit closer as soon as I lost aggro, or waiting until I regained it normally, and most of the time moving into the mob got it back right away.

I also tried moving the mobs by backing away (they don't summon). I did this both with and without the BL being near the mobs, and in about 80% of the cases the mob started attacking the nearest toon without following me. Sometimes that was a low-aggro druid or the cleric, toons to which I would be far ahead of in aggro terms normally. This wasn't your normal 'i'm a caster NPC so when you move I'll start casting and not follow', these mobs turned immedatiately and hit anyone in range. They only moved to me when no one else was in melee range, or a few times when there was. But most often, they really wanted to stay put.

I watched another warrior tanking in a DoN mission (BoC / CHSD) while I played my Shaman, and he had the same issue, he couldn't move mobs, once they were in position and had a couple of toons next to them, if he moved out of range they'd stay put and switch to what appeared to be the nearest target.

I tanked in some non-DoN zones (WoS for example) with exactly the same setup and some much higher DPS characters in the group than when in DoN, and aggro worked exactly how it always has. I held it 99% of the time, I could move mobs happily even when they were next to other toons, and I could stand at almost any range as long as I could hit them and aggro would stay on me.

DoN feels different - and if what I'm seeing is correct then it smells like the proximity hate bonus is massive.
