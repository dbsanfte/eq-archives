Only testing I did on an unaug'd GSoT vs. an unaug'd GSo4W was at PoV golems one long afternoon.

My personal feeling was that the GSo4W was better agro pre-slow and debuffs (usually enough to allow the slower to not gain agro, providing they were slowing at an appropriate time). However, further in the fight the GSoT (thanks to its procs) would be better agro. The longer the fight, the better the GSoT would hold agro for me.

I also noticed that my GSo4W was downright horrible in BoT for holding agro vs. high DPS classes (such as a GSo4W wielding monk and EQ/AM3 ranger) and against many mobs it wasn't even strong pre-slow like it was in PoV. My GSoT was noticeably better for agro in BoT in all situations I came across.

Lastly, in a 58-65 LDoN, my GSo4W held agro equal to the GSoT with a larger damage output.

My conclusion? The higher level mob you fight, the less effective the GSo4W will be for agro, and the more important the GSoT's proc becomes for holding agro. With my own testing, I realized it wasn't worth putting an agro proc on my GSo4W. I've kept it for a strong DPS weapon, since I have enough opportunities to use it as such. I don't doubt that an Anger III GSo4w would be a good agro weapon, but it just didn't fit my play style to go with that setup.

a gso4W +A3 performs better then a BoC+3/CHSD combo, and at least as good as a Gbos+3/CHSD+3 comboThere is no way in hell that a GSot4W+A3 can comepete with a BoC+3/anything+3 combo uness you don't turn on attack. There have been multitudes of parses on this subject and the fact is that DPS does not account for near as much agro as Hate/Stun procs do. You might "feel" you hold better agro, but parses prove otherwise. I have a far superior DPS weapon than a GSot4W and it isn't even close to the agro of my BoC+GBoS BEFORE I got the augs.

Look at it this way - DPS agro has been calculated at around 1 damage = 1 hate. If you have a typical war set up and use CHoS+SotB and put an anger 2 proc on each, you end up with:
2 CHoS innate PPM = 800 hate (figuring stuns at 400 hate or so)
2 CHoS aug PPM = 800 hate
1 SotB innate PPM= 400 hate
1 SotB aug PPM = 400 hate

That's a total of 2400 HPM or around 40 DPS worth of hate. In order for your GSot4W to equal the hate of the combo above, it would have to do 40 DPS more unaugmented, or 20 DPS more with an A3 augment. While it is a good DPS weapon, it's not 20 DPS more. Once you start to figure in WA, the gap becomes even larger since 2h weapon don't benefit as much since the raw increase in number of procs is lower.

I thought it was pretty well established that a weapon's damage bonus was significant in determining its aggro, and that aggro was measured per swing rather than per point of damage. This favors the fastest 2 handers that fit into the 28+ delay category, of which GSo4W is one of the best. So while a Windblade might have the same DPS as a GSo4W, it will produce less aggro, because its damage-bonus-to-delay ratio is worse.

From experience I can report that a GSo4W + 50 lifetap has nearly the same aggro as a Jagged Shard of Frozen Flame + Anger 3 / Great Blade of Storms + Anger 2 combo, and will steal aggro from the latter combo about 1/2 of the time unless incite is used to widen the gap.

It seems to me that the GSo4W is the highest DPS-aggro weapon you can get before a BoW, and putting Anger 3 on it should be extremely effective. Those who say "no way", have you actually tried it? I'm not putting Anger 3 on mine, but I'd love to hear of firsthand comparisons.

I actually hesitate to recommend the GSo4W as a pure DPS weapon, due to how I witness it routinely drawing aggro off of unaugmented BoC's and Anger 3'd weapons. To me it's a logical choice to convert into an aggro weapon.
