Kailyn
It indeed was one of the worst games I betaed....

But...now that they have been out for a while, adding and fixing and patching, and have teh rights back from Simon and Schuester Interactive (I think)--who forced them to go live way too early--or less than the price of 2 months of regular EQ, you can get a very pretty game that has a lot of depth.

At the moment they are setting new on line records every weekend, though I don't think they have yet hit the cirtical mass to really have the game take off.

While it is fully PVP, it takes more than a bit of time to get to the point where you want to PVP. Though the makers of the game are trying to come up with a place where the lightest ships in the game (frigates) can fight with one another without being stomped by bigger ships.

At the moment the game is more about economics than combat, as the player density increase, I think the PVP parts will become more important.

An important fact about EVE, I think, is the original devs are still working on the game, and as dedicated to it as they ever were. The vision in still in the building.

Since I have come back to EVE, I have mined maybe 3 times. If you do try it, do as much as you can for your starting agent, as he/she will teach you a lot about the many and complicated systems in EVE.

I would encourage you to try it out, its cheap, in some ways caters to the casual player, and if you find a good corp, very social.
