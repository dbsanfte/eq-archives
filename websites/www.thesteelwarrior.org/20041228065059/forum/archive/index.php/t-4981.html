12-29-2003, 05:46 PM
Avoidance has proven difficult to quantify for comparisons. Shielding is very easy... direct reduction of damage taken from a mob's damage bonus.

Avoidance appears to provide direct AC. Whether this AC is equal to normal AC or better is a difficult prospect to assign numbers.

One of the questions you might want to consider is your position in the tank order. If you are not near the front of the line for new weapons, then you might want to consider emphasizing Combat Effects to maximize your aggro or enhanced proc-based DPS. If you are up front, then go for damage absorption such as Shielding and Avoidance.

Strikethrough is DPS only. Aggro is by swing, not by damage dealt, so an attack that should not have hit will create no more aggro than one that would have been parried or riposted. It's groovy for XP groups and parses.

Even when DI is not 0, shielding effect still gets greater as you accumulate more. Suppose a mob hits for 1k-3K. Take 4% shielidng at 0% and 96%, even if mob hits for max you're looking at 3000 to 2960 versus 2040 to 2000. It's still 40 damage reduced in both cases but it is a greater reduction as part of the mob's total damage, and this improves the efficiency of heals.

Also with more shielding the effect of our innate 5% mitigation becomes better. If a mob's DB is 0 then essentially 5% mitigation is the same thing as taking 5% less damage so the lower you can reduce a mob's DB, the more we get out of our innate mitigation.
