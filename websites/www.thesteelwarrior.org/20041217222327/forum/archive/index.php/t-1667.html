Here is my two coppers, for what it's worth.

First of all, yes it is the tank's job to work at positioning the mob in a group. However, as a Warrior this isn't as easy as it first sounds. Many times our aggro isn't strong enough to get the mob to follow us around like a lost puppy, particularly if pets are present. So we'll back up, and the mob turns on the Monk. Or we back up and the mob turns on the pet. It's a serious pita.

And consider, other melee move the mob as they attack, so eventually, even if the positioning started out perfect, it gets all screwy. Rinse and repeat the problem above with the mob not necessarily following as we try to adjust.

It is the job of all melee who aren't tanking to get behind the mob, particularly in PoP. A riposte from a PoP mob just means more mana has to be used. And it makes it much easier for the tank to stay in contact with the mob and position it most effectively when they don't have to compete with other melee chacters in the front. I don't think I can even begin to explain how frustrating it is to be working your ass off trying to get the mob lined up so everyone has a clear shot at the back only to have someone in your way so you can't move in the direction you want/need to and to keep getting out of range messages as they move in front of you. So even if the tank doesn't have the positioning exactly right, the other melee need to compensate. It is after all a team effort.

With regards to Clerics who like to melee, that's fine. However, they need to behave like all other melee and attack from behind. I have found that most Clerics, who are unfamiliar with melee in general to begin with, have a tendency to crowd the tank in front of the mob, just making my life and the life of other tanks more difficult.

This is primarily from the point of veiw of group tanking. Raids have other considerations as well.
