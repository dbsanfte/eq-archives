Kraydak
To hell with provoke, agro is a fucking SIDESHOW at the moment. Is provoke nice? Yes. Is it useful? Yes, heck, it would allow many warriors to move away from bows in the range slot. Will it do anything to *fix* warriors? Not even close. Warriors have slid into the same position as monks: we NO LONGER HAVE a role (ok, we are better off than monks in that we can still point to a role and ask for it, I have no clue what should be done for monks, not a one at all). We used to be the best tanks, then we were the best raid tanks. Now, nothing. Oh, we managed to keep up a good front for awhile, one of the more succesful swindles in EQ history. But the lie has become too large to maintain. The problems are so freaking much deeper than mere agro. And the changes in test do *nothing* to adress the real problems.

To see it in a different manner, lets define N to be the number of max melee rounds you can eat from a mob and survive. If N is 0, you have no business tanking it because you will get 1 rounded. If N is 1, then you NEED a CH landing after each and every melee round. For *some* mobs things change at N=2, but by and large, its the same, you need a CH after every other melee round. N=3 is the real transition, and N=4 you have entered the xp grind regime where AC and defensive skills actually mean something. Or to put it differently, a PLed twink magician w/ defense and doge skills of but 6k hp full buffed and an 11k warrior can both tank a 5.8k max round mob. And they'll need exactly the same healing rotation. In raid scenarios, the only difference between tanks that actually applies to healing required is increasing N. Of course, for an N of 3 or higher you might as well run it as an N of three, because anyone can get scrap a 5 second CH rotation together. And really only moving N from 0 to 1 will have much effect at the high levels because with todays raid sizes, a CH rotation timed to every mob melee round is close to trivial.

To hell with knights, the whole tanking picture is FUBAR. With 1 CH/melee round being easily obtained, all that matter for raid tanking is HP total, and only differences of the order of 30 or 50% (depending on whether you talk about the higher or the lower total) actually have a decent shot of making a difference. With knights being a mere 1k behind warriors at best, it is impossible for devs to balance mobs to be able to 1 round knights and not warriors. The expected variation in hp totals due to gear will swamp any reliable gab.

Do you honestly think anyone on test atm will give us enough "utility" to match knights? We sure don't have a functional tanking edge. Doing so would require rebuilding warriors (and monks, and even rogues) from the ground up. A simple add on like the current opening system doesn't even qualify as a bandaid.

The damage interval multiplier reduction will also do approximately nothing. We will still require *more* healing in an xp group situation (taking 4% less damage taken does not begin to compare to a semi-afk SK's lifetap buffs, much less a drunk paladin randomly hitting heals and giggling about it). And as the above example showed and effective hp difference to actually make warriors better raid tanks has to be at least around 30%. Four percent just isn't going to cut it (for an 11k tank, it amounts to a 500 hp buff, oh frabjous day), and 30% would require rebalancing all the mobs in EQ and would cause far too many problems (only warriors would be able to tank, which would cause problems of its own). What we actually need to have happen is something like giving mobs a small (2ish percent) chance of landing a critical melee round agianst non-warriors: auto double attack/dual wield/flurry/rampage/hit/strikethrough and 1.5 times damage. That would actually reduce knights to N=0 on many mobs WITHOUT completely screwing with game balance in the same way that capping the DI multiplier at 10 would (or allowing 10+ minute /def runs, or anything similar). And it'd even help warriors in xp groups (non-VT level knights would think twice before tanking Krigers/LDoN assasinate mobs, for example).

But yes, I do not think that the current changes on test can, even if done perfectly, actually balance anything. It'll just serve as an excuse to do nothing with the core problems. Sad.

-Kraydak
