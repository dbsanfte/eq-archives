Nope, more swings may (may) increase proc rate, but more hits does not. You can (and do) proc on misses. Proc rate, on most weapons is set in terms of 0.22 X delay for a percent chance per swing to proc. Note that delay IS modified for haste...so the faster you swing the less likely you are to proc. I don't know if the proc engin is smart enough to take into account PW. That is a question to post on Shaman's Crucible, as they have a VERY nice test on this there.

For non-trivial content, warriors do still have the lock on raid tanking. For most pre-pop content, raiding means keeping the MA alive while DPSing the bad guy to death.

A typical heal in a CH chain lands at half health, so the only risk is in a damage spike killing the MA. Warriors with defensive smooth the damage spikes, making a MA down - and a potentially failed raid - less likely. The game is now all about minimization of risk, and a defensive tank - for non-trivial content - minimizes the risk.

Ergo, raids still require defensive tanks, and pretty much always will for non-trivial content. Until they remove the CH chain this will always be the case. I'm fairly certain that the guilds that are doing the 5th set of time trials are NOT using knights for tanking the bosses (Bertoxx, CT, Inny).

Now, if you are not a raiding MA, then offensive AAs may be a better idea. But the perception remains that you could be a raid MA (and defensive/evasive is still huge for raid content). So defensive AAs seem to still be a good idea. But if you don't raid, who can say.
