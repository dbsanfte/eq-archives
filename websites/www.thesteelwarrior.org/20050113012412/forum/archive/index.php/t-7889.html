Yeah, warriors are about 90% based on their gear. We don't get spells. We get a couple of disciplines that'll only save us if we have good gear. That last 10% that we're based off of is AAs. Skill is a surprisingly small portion of a warrior's job. It's kinda sad, but very true. It doesn't matter how well I know how to play my class, when I tank a mob that just hits too hard. The mob will kill me if I don't have enough hp/ac/AAs. The majority of skill that comes into play for warriors is CHOOSING the proper gear and focusing all your energy into upgrading your gear.

Casters get spells as nearly an automatic update every few levels, which work even if they're neked. A warrior on a corpse run can easily die to green KOS mobs, because we simply don't have any way to defend ourselves without gear. Skill comes into play mostly for those with utility... Knowing when to root a mob, when to mez, when to debuff, when to kite. A warrior has the lowest utility of any class; all we do is aggro a mob and tank it. Our entire purpose is to do our defined job and be the best at it.

People are not always kind when you choose inferior weapons or armor because it's showing that you aren't good at being a warrior. When the warrior community makes suggestions, we really know what we're talking about. I've been playing a warrior for a long time, and if I tell you that the SBoD is shit, for example, it's based off of many parses and years of personal experience combined with the endless experience of the other warriors here. We are gear driven, we know our shit, and if you ask for advice and ignore it, we will rip you apart.

Lastly, here is why warriors matter in an experience group: We DO take less innate damage. We generate *enough* aggro to do our job. If a named pops, we can tank it far better than knights. Lastly, we have a couple of very powerful "oh shit!" disciplines and abilities to help keep us alive. AE taunt + furious + defensive (when furious ends) has kept me alive a ton of times when a knight would have still been taking full damage.

Anyhow, sorry for the long winded post. We are the warrior community, we know what we're doing and ignore our advice at your peril.

If I land my successful taunt on the mob, and BOTH the knight/pally and I drop attack, I won't necessarily keep the mob on me right? Because DOT damage and Stun effects can have a time-effect ratio correct? Same with snare?

Only dot damage adds aggro over time. Stuns work once and leave no buff icon, and any debuff that has no dot component will only generate aggro when it's first cast. So unless you're taunting off of a shadowknight that has an active dot on the mob, you should keep aggro. Do note that proximity DOES play into aggro, and if the knight steps closer than you the mob may turn.

And furthermore, if a warrior and Knight / pally are similarly equipped, is it true that the knight / pally will NOT lose aggro if they don't want to? Because I would like to do a few experiments of my own on this issue.

Well, that's true and untrue. Knights can use low level spells for aggro, which they can basically cast indefinitely due to the low mana cost on them. Warriors simply cannot compete with the on demand aggro generation. Think of it this way: Stuns and pure hate procs are what warriors use to generate aggro, coupled with incite. Knights can cast similar aggro spells whenever they want, more times per minute than we will proc. I don't speak for the time+ crowd, but warriors simply can't compete with the aggro generation of spells cast as often as knights can cast them. If they don't want to lose aggro, they won't.

That being said, taunt means that we can, on a success, match their aggro no matter how ahead they are. A successful taunt + incite will take aggro from them for a few seconds. Warriors are the KING of pingponging aggro; it doesn't matter how much aggro someone can generate, we can steal it.
