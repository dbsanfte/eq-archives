Now that I've clarified the topic, I'll post my comments.

I believe that AC should have the same value across classes. It should be an absolute across classes similar to they way HPs are across classes. It doesn't matter if its a warrior or a wizard, if they have 4000 hps then they can take 4000 points of damage. AC should be the same way. There should be no ambiguity to it. A 1500 AC wizard should have they same DI distribution that a 1500 AC warrior has. Obviously if the warrior will make a better tank because he has more hps than the wizard, but IF they have the same AC and HPs they should tank equally well.

This is not to say that there should be no AC differences between classes. Class differentiation should come from itemization and class multipliers/skills. The warrior would have a vastly easier time reaching 1500 AC compared to a wizard. Warrriors have a class multiplier to their HPs and AC that is much larger than a wizard. Also, warrior gear has more AC and HPs than the same tier wizard gear.

Yes this means that I think that the innate warrior mitiagation bonus shold have been a larger AC multiplier instead of a hidden DI mod. I can imagine why they did this though, to keep player from being able to quantify the warrior advantage in a real way. (read: knights whould have fucking lost it if warriors would have been given an even larger AC bonus)

In short, if I group with a wizard with more hps and AC then, hell, they should be tanking. No matter if its 1000 AC or 2000 AC.

Just to be clear to some of those that just started playing EQ last week:

1) Defense skill is used in calculating mitigation *** AND *** avoidance AC.
2) Parry, Dodge, Riposte, Block are all used to increase Avoidance.

4) Shielding decreased DB (one could say a form of mitigation.)
