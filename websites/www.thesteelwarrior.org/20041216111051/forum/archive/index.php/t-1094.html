I mean this to be less a rant and more a bit of constructive thought. Do not beat me with the /large trout if I go over anything that has been beaten to a pulp already :)

I was discussing arrogant paladins and their chain stunning ways with my fellow warrior Cyrz in my guild warrio channel. Between the usual curses and wishes for the death of one thousand rangers on to each chain stunning paladin we tried to come up with ideas that were practical enough to take the insane edge off of aggro that paladins have while chain stunning while not completely crippling one of their critical spell lines.

One thing that came up early was giving the stuns a jolt style effect. Jolt being that nice aggro reducing spell a smart ranger uses so he's not turned into jelly in green tights. The idea certainly has it's appeal. It would force a paladin to depend much more on taunt. Of course taunt being broken it would land paladins and shadowknights in the same boat as warriors. And even Sony would be hard pressed to ignore all three tank classes having a broke aggro skill. Of course the down side of an aggro reducing stun would be a paladin could chain stun without fear of retribution from the mob and effectively limit its ability to do damage. That would become an unbalancing trend as any paper skinned caster preferes a non-moving, non-attacking mob to one that you have to hope taunt keeps intrested.

So the question became how would you balance stuns if they became aggro reducing even to a small degree of 100 negative hate points? I threw out the idea of raising mana cost and recast time. The thing I've seen over and over in groups with guildmates and pick up groups is paladins memming two to three stuns and chain casting them until they run out of mana. Generally I've seen a paladin do this for four or more mobs until they run out of mana using clarity spells. Add in Pot9 and Beasty crack and they go for much longer. So with or without adding a jolt effect it would seemingly make sense to raise mana cost and recast time to make chain stunning less effective and lead to a greater dependence on the taunt button. And when more people stat relying on a busted skill its bound to get fixed quicker (This of course assumes that warrior taunt is the exact same as knight taunt, I never dug into the exact mechanics :)

You might notice I do not mention shadowknights in this post even though they have incredible aggro abilities. In my experience of noted that most shadowknights understand and appreciate aggro a lot better than paladins. Growing up as a class with feign death they learn to appreciate gaining and losing aggro just like a monk or a necromancer would. Many shadowknights grow up being pullers and learn to dump off aggro and a mob onto a warrior. Feign death is also one of the ultimate hate killers on a mob. A smart shadowknight in an experience group or a raid who picks up aggro knows to immediately hit the ground and play dead. Paladins don't have this luxury as all around do gooders who'd never lay down and play dead......they just train :P

I'm all for beefing up warrior aggro. But instead of continually stacking blocks it seems to make more sense to level things out. The raising of recast time and mana consumption seems the most agreeable route to me. Sony has always tweaked spells around to their liking. They are easier and less drastic a change than a game mechanics change in general.

Also as a side note paladins and shadowknights are invincible when it comes to aggro. I my self on several occasions have pulled and maintained aggro off of several knights on my server both with and without my Blade of Carnage. And not merely as an accident. I've openly challanged knights to do their best to maintain aggro while I attempt to pull it off. And this means paladins triple stunning and even one shadowknight who gave himself Voice of Thule! (I needed the BoC for the Thule guy naturally ) All I'm saying is that its tough but possible. Aggro control isn't all mechanics but it takes skill as well.

Oh, just so this fits with the forum......nerf paladins!

Being the Cyrz mentioned in this post, I should point out some things.

Trast did mention that the SK and Pally he was testing against was TRYING to get aggro.

I will rant and rave about nerfing paladins and such with the best of them, but my real, true desire, is to give warriors an EFFECTIVE, TRIGGERABLE method of TEMPORALIY gaining aggro.

Let me explain. In any experiance group I am in, aggro is a big issue in the first 10-15 seconds. I will call these the Golden 15 seconds. During that time, you often see Slow, perhaps Tash, Perhaps a form of Malo, and perhap some DoT's landing. These spells all lose some of their value each second they are delayed being cast. You also are very likely to see the first heal or heals landing in this time.

2 of the 3 tank classes have the ability to increase their aggro generating abilities during this time, and therefor allow Slows, Debuffs, and DoT's to land EARLIER, with LESS aggro concerns, and therefor allows those spell to be MORE effective. Since slow can land earlier, the first Heal can be delayed, perhaps allowing that heal to be a more mana efficent Cheal instead of maybe a Supernal line.

Because of this, the 3rd Tank Class has been developing the rep of being the less effective Tank Class in Exp-Group level encounters.

This, in effect, voices my ONLY real concern about Balance between the 3 tank classes. Relative DPS, etc, etc, etc, I don't personally care about. Long term aggro, current Mitigation and Advoidance Tables, etc, I don't see as a problem. The single, solitary failing point, the single most AGGRAVATING issue I see with my warrior, is the Inability to gain aggro during those golden 15 seconds, and as such, my diserability and preformance in exp groups.

Also, Trast, in reference to Taunt- Warriors have a higher skill level cap on Taunt. From Memory, Warrior Skill level is 230, and SK/Pally is 200?.

In reference to this issue, to provide the Dev's with Hard Numbers to get them to perhaps consider a change, or perhaps to even prove us wrong (I doubt it, but I am attempting to be totally impartial here)- I'd lied to see or do a small study in aggro.

Take two similiarly equipped tanks- The best defination I would have for similiarly equiped would be both tanks perhaps belonging to the same guild, and have gotten their weapons with that guild.

Put both these tanks in an exp group. Tell each tank to TRY and maintain aggro. Logfile the fight, and see who gets swung at MORE often over the course of say, 100 fights. If the Knight gets hit or missed 5000 times and the warrior 500, that should be indicitive of an aggro imblance. If it wasn't such a massive undertaking, I'd even break it down to see who has aggro during the first, Golden 15 seconds of the fight more often.
