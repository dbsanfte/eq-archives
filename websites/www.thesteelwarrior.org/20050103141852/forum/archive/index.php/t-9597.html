Ok, it's very simple if you want it to be. Sure there is caps, RNG, etc. but it simply comes down to this.


•Combat Agility will cause you to be hit less often.

•Combat Stability will cause you to be hit for less on average.

Both have been parsed to reduce overall damage taken by the same amount. This is for plate tanks, other classes will benefit from Agility more because they don't get hit enough times to benefit from added mitigation as much as someone who is constantly tanking.

The percentages have been parsed and debated extensively, but it's simple.
»These are the most important AA's you could buy. If you dont want to take my word for it, I'm sure my post will be followed by plenty that agree with me.

On a side note ... though they both decrease the raw damage taken over time from melee by the same percent, Combat agility/LR etc have a hefty advantage in the newer expansions.

Mobs cannot proc on a miss ... therefore the more you are missed the less chance there is that you will take the extra 100-3000 damage from that one proc. Since EP+ PoP, GoD, and OoW like to have regular xp mobs (as well as raid mobs) that have a variety of different combat effects, the avoidance line of aa's might prove to be slightly more beneficial than the raw mitigation. They are both subject to the random number generator and lady luck, though, and you can STILL take full rounds of damage even with everything capped. With everything maxxed (including the OoW ones) tanks on average should take about 40% less damage.

Max em both. They are crucial.

LR5 capped (assuming you have PE) equates to about 17% less hits taken. Add in the OoW stuff and you're up to on average 22% less hits taken.

22% less hits taken = 22% less procs averaged out. IF (big emphasis on the if) you fight in places where mobs have innate procs ... 22% less damage taken from procs adds up to a whole lot less damage taken.

On boss mobs who proc for 3k and can do so twice in a single round (Reparm) it increases your survivability far more than 22% mitigation ever would. 22% less procs + 22% less hits on average.
