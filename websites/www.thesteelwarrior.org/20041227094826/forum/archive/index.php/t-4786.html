This patch, the new endurance bar and discipline system will be going live. The extra warrior mitigation and provoke line will be in there as well. As you may have seen on the monk board, we're holding off on the opening system for now. We'd like to get more time to test and tune those skills and the system.

We've made some more changes to the disciplines. Here is how they now stand:

Timer 1
---------

Evasive - Level 52. 3 minute duration, 7.8 minute refresh. Increases avoidance and decreases to-hit.
Defensive - Level 55. 3 minute duration, 10.5 minute refresh. Decreases damage taken and damage given.
Stonewall - Level 65. 3 minute duration, 10.5 minute refresh. Decreases damage taken and reduces movement rate.


Timer 2
---------

Furious - Level 56. 2 tick duration, 40 minute refresh. Riposte every hit.
Fellstrike - Level 58. 5 tick duration, 25 minute refresh. Increases damage per hit with minimum of 4x base damage.
Fortitude - Level 59. 2 tick duration, 40 minute refresh. Avoid all melee attacks.


Timer 3
---------

Charge - Level 53. 5 tick duration, 25 minute refresh. Improve hit rate on melee attacks to 100%.
Mighty Strike - Level 54. 5 tick duration, 25 minute refresh. All attacks will critically hit.
Precision - Level 57. 1 minute duration, 30 minute refresh. Increases accuracy of all melee attacks.
Aggressive - Level 60. 1 minute duration, 30 minute refresh. Increases damage done on all melee attacks.


Timer 4
---------

Resistant - Level 30. 5 minute duration, 30 minute reuse. Increases all resists up to 20.
Fearless - Level 40. 1 minute duration, 30 minute reuse. Gives immunity to all fear effects.
Spirit of Rage - Level 61. 2 tick duration, 30 minute reuse. Substantially increases the amount of aggro you generate against NPCs.
Healing Will - Level 63. 1 minute duration, 5 minute reuse. Increases regeneration substantially. Cannot be used while in combat.



The combination of the increase in duration of the short, high-damage disciplines, the decrease in duration of Aggressive and Precision, and the removal of the negative aspects of the latter two brings the potential damage increase for warrior disciplines to where we want it for now. It's obviously a mixed bag for some, but the separate timers should allow much more use of disciplines than before.

We may make further changes to the disciplines and timers based on feedback for the next patch, but this is what will be going in on Thursday morning.
