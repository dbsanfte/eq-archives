Aye, the "elementals" are the Planes of Fire (Doomfire, the Burning Lands), the Plane of Water (Reed of Coirnav), the Plane of Air (Eryslai, the Kingdom of Wind), and The Plane of Earth (Vegarlson, the Earthen Badlands).

The Plane of Fire is an awesome zone. The exp is insane with LOTS of mobs to pull, plenty of one-groupable minis to kill with fast respawns and awesome loot, and LOTS of raid targets. The problem is, because Fire is so easy to move through due to the fact that almost nothing sees through invis, almost all of those raid targets are annihilated within hours of pop, lol.

The Plane of Earth is also widely used as an exp zone, but has raid targets aplenty through rings. The problem is, you NEVER know when the rings are up. My guild is 0 for 2 for spawning the correct final boss mob on these rings. We've gotten PHs every time. It's seriously one of the dumbest things ever.

The Plane of Water is a lot like Fire with great exp, lots of minis, and lots of raid targets. However, being entirely underwater, it's generally the least populated as far as exp is concerned cause fighting in water sucks. :) The only time you'll really find people here is if they're with their guild raiding. PoWater is freaking awesome though. I honestly thought I'd freaking HATE it, but it's a beautiful zone and you barely have to clear anything to get to your raid target that you want.

The Plane of Air is entirely different. This is not an exp zone, but if you're the masochistic type, you can. The entire point of the zone is to kill the four air avatars - Dust, Wind, Mist, and Smoke. Each of these avatars drop the required pieces to create the key that ports you to Xegony's island. To spawn the avatars, you must successfuly complete a small "ring" on a different island. For Wind, Mist, and Smoke, this really isn't all that difficult...
