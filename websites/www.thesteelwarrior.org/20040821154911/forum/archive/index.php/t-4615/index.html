Valeris
1)Gear choices,AA abilities etc etc Nothing magical, compare magelo's of people around your level for options then use allakhazam or similar to find out where to get 'em.


2)That's pretty much it til the changes go live, time your taunts, don't mash, work on good group awareness, identifying which mob is the priority, positioning relative to healer/slow so you know where the mobs headed if you loose control, use of proximity for aggro, piggybacking hate from high DPS pets, watching for slow/debuff messages so you're prepared etc, etc. 'Pushbutton ability' wise there's not much 'yet' you can add.


3)Opportunities open up during combat at infrequent intervals, you will have a choice of abilities depending on the opening type, hit the appropriate choice of skil before the opening 'fades' and continue. Depending on choice of ability you get a different result.

Limited use ranged hate ability (provoke) offers a one time hate increase without requiring an opening, not snap aggro like a knight but an additional aid.


4)If you hit the correct ability it gives you a little bit more than if you don't. :p

As far as what to do now - see 2) Abilities help but it's only if you are an active and aware participant in the group that you'll tank 'well'.

Mhila
What I did when I was leveling up... Stats really are not that important because more often then not, you can get the buffs you need to max everything. Try to get your Stamina to 195 unbuffed so that the shamans who you'll be grouping with can max that stat at 255 giving you the most possible hit points from the buff. Other then that, you want as many raw hit points on your gear as you can possibly get. There is really no problem trading off some of your strength gear for hit points. Strength is by far the easiest stat to buff.

Once you get over level 60 and can start raising your stats higher then 255 you'll want to add to your stamina so that stam buffs will just max you. At level 65 I try to keep my unbuffed stam at 274 so that the right click stam buff on my greaves will max me at exactly 305.

Thats just how I do it. I'm sure there are many other ideas which may or may not be better for you.

As to the new melee changes, I haven't played with them at all but I'm not all that excited about becoming a caster... I'm holding judgment till I see them for myself.

Buun Sofsteel
All of the above represents good advice. Equipment is the single most important factor as it plays such a vital role in the task of a true warrior, mitigation and aggro. Since these are the most obvious, I will throw out some less obvious small things you can do to get better.

Good warriors are also masters of positioning. These may seem like small things but they will make you appear experienced and polished in a group setting.

Little things relative to positioning are always appreciated. If you have a slower make sure they are close to you so you can "taunt" if you lose aggro and not get a "too far away message" if you don't hit it fast enough. Save taunt for when you need it. Have plenty of arrows and always tag on inc to get yoruself on the hate list.

Turn the mob to put pets at its back if possible (for better group dps - some pets actually backstab) and all other melee if it is an LDoN enrager.

If you have a ranger (using bow) or a rogue keep the mob out of the wall. They will love you for it. You get an overzealous monk, a beastlord pet and a mage pet all in the same group and you may have to use "sit" aggro to get it out and centered. If they are pushing it into the wall just back off and sit down.

Seperate mezzed mobs. Although it is easier now to target with the target ring on an inc of two or three, once mezzed move the mob you are fighting away from the other to get a clear seperation so your CC can tash and slow it while mezzed easier.

Always call the target when more than one mob. When no CC kill healers first. If CC ask the chanter if they would prefer you kill casters last, some like to play with their sieves.

Make sure your healers cap your health between pulls. I dislike the cleric who leaves me at 60 percent between pulls as it is not as efficient to CHeal me. Inevitably a three pull will get the cleric throwing a fast heal on me before they are all controlled and taking some aggro.

FOOD - a warrior should never be in group asking "hey does anyone have some water". Go to the bazaar and buy Halas 10 lb Meat Pies (1-2 pp each and Tormentor Stir Fry is too expensive for main food for me) and Grobb Liquidized meat (5-7pp) and always have a steady supply. I carry regular rations/fizzlecutter and I switch out when I am tradeskilling or LFG.

FOOD is the PERIDOTS of warriors.

Nothing big or important there. The other guys got the important stuff these are just things you pick up with experience.
Ealsur Helevorn
Please, what did you mean by piggybacking hate from high DPS pets?

Taunt puts you on the top of the list + a very small amount of hate, right? So, Beastlord, Mage (mostly with stunning weapons) and other pets, can generate more agro than you, cause they have innate procs (added by their masters) or summoned weapons that stun, have DDs, and of course, generate agro. You can see a Mage pet chain stun a mob with a few procs, and that is some big agro, but since you are tanking and are near the NPC, the mob is on you, but still, the pet has the higher agro. Piggyback the hate of the pets, means that you taunt and 'hop' over the pet's big agro, that is, of course, over yours, and which puts you, way higher than the other meeles/ casters :)

You can use this tactic with BST and Mage pets the most, other classes pets might not generate as much agro with their procs (unless a Mage gives those pets some stunning weapons I guess).

I think that kinda covers it :)

zito
One thing I found handy, as I think a lot of tanks do, is to make shield hotkeys for your group. Depending on the situation, I have up to 3 shield hotkeys. One for the healer, 2nd is for slower or crowd control, and 3rd key is sort of a utility key... usually I use it if there's both a shaman and a chanter in the group, or if there's a squishy caster of some kind who might need it :p Mainly tho, I always have the healer and the slower hotkeyed.

Something else that might work for you, is shaman potions. I don't know if your shaman does alchemy, but a number of those potions are damn handy, especially in pickup groups where often as not, you can't get all the buffs you want. I usually keep sta buffs, damage shields, invis, lev, and gate potions on me. At the very least, they can be really handy when you have to travel through a zone to join a group that is already at a camp. Groups love, and tend to remember, players who come equipped like that and don't need the group to send someone over to invis them, etc.

As other people have said, positioning is huge. Pet classes love having a tank who compensates a mob's position for their pets. And as someone else said, separating the mobs on mass pulls is nice for everyone involved, as it not only helps your crowd control, but it also helps the rest of the group avoid breaking mezzes. Ask the chanters you group with which mobs they want deaded next. Seems they all have different styles of playing, and until you get to know a person, they often seem to want it done differently. Biggest thing is, be adaptable in different situations... there is never a case of one particular thing/style/whatever always working the best in every single situation.

I'd comment on your gear, but magelo is down, and other ppl seem to have done it well already. But, the basics are pretty much the same... raw HP, AC, saves are all important, as to a lesser extent are stats. I wouldn't worry too much about your agi stat, as parsing has shown it really doesn't make that much difference in avoidance. As for the cha.... well, no accounting for NPC tastes somedays, is there? :p

-Edited to add- As Valeris says below, once you have the Copper Hammer, just train up that skill as you can; higher dexterity helps that. You'll be getting most of the aggro from that weapon from the proc, and the proc will go off no matter how weakly you are hitting the mob. If you can, keep all of your weapon skills trained up. It can be frustrating to get a really nice weapon that you have very low skill in, and you have to take the time and train up that skill before you see the full benefit of said weapon.

Good luck !

One further note about pulling mobs off the wall ... you will sometimes have to stand in the center of the room hollering 'BACK OFF NOW' repeatedly before the party dials in. Especially with non-pet class pets (ie: Shaman spirit wolves), whose masters are yet learning the knack of controlling a pixel critter. (And you may have to howl to get them to take /pet attack off their assist key.)

Sitting in the center of the room also pulls MOBs effectively, but if the party health is good I like to remind the group why they need to pay attention from time to time. With regular group mates this is more effective in the long run. But with p/u groups you may just wanna hold their hands as they won't learn quickly enough and may only remember that they stole aggro, or that the MOB got walled, and blame you (and thus, becoming your future naysayers when your waiting for a p/u invite).

One further note - experiment with your camera modes (iirc, F9 is the default key) ... I almost always am using the chase 3rd person camera, set overhead and back in a nearly isometric viewpoint. I can see all or most of my party at camp this way, and when I am line the mobs back to the casters, I have 3 cues available to me that they are casting - the text message, the spell particles, and the casting anim. (If they're smart, I have the fourth cue in my /gsay window ;).) It's at the point now that I'm pretty certain what the spell is by the animation and the timing alone.

I love positioning the mob, it pulls me into the group dynamic and I enjoy the game a lot more since I started paying more attention to it.
