One is spell haste, one item haste. They stack.

The effect on the gauntlets, however, is useless if you are grouping with an enchanter, a shaman, or a beastlord. The spell haste from the gauntlets will not stack with spell haste from a player (although it will stack with bard song haste from a bard).

Three kinds of haste: item or tribute, buff spell, and bard song. They stack with each other, but never within the same kind of haste.

Original effect on ornate gloves had marvelous visions, which had a sta effect in addition to the haste effect. They placed the haste portion of the MV effect on a different slot so that the buff would not get overwritten with a higher lvl of haste (ie SoV). They nerfed the effect to only 30% spell haste but never altered the haste slot, so the icon stays up even though a higher lvl of haste spell is cast on you.

Not all bard songs stack like that. Half of them count as spells, half as v3 (stackable with spell) haste. You can go to Lucy (lucy.allakhazam.com) if you are curious about a certain spell. A few examples - Warsong of Zek (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3374&source=Live) is Spell haste. Rizlona's Call of Flame (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3362&source=Live) is v3 haste (sometimes called Overhaste, since v3 can go over the haste cap) and will stack with spell haste.
