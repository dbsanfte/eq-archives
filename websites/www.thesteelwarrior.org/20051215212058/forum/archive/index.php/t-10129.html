I've been playing EQ2 since release, and while its a good game, I still have my memories from the WoW stress test which keep haunting me.

How's the game coming along so far? I'm looking for opinions from active WoW players. This isn't the outhouse, so try and keep the EQ2 vs EQ vs WoW stuff to a minimum. I'm interesting in your like and dislikes about the game. so really, anything not specificly class related is game.

I've had plenty of time to play EQ2, and while I tihnk its a good game, I remember my time in beta with WoW. Maybe it was just the "honeymoon" phase, but I just remember that game kicking ass. I had similar hopes for EQ2, but the game changes so much post 20, that I'm not sure this is the game I want to play. For reference, heres a few things to keep in mind that I don't like about EQ2. I've played a hunter to 22 or so in beta, pre-talents and EQ2 and WoW play very similarly up to that point. I'm just wondering if WoW takes the same direction at the higher levels as EQ2 does.

So, here's my biggests concerns with EQ2, mostly post level 20:

1. Soloing is insanely difficult. Solo encounters are few, and the xp rewards are nearly non-existant. It was possible before to solo green and blue group encounters for nice, but not stellar xp, however they patched it so that a)group mobs are much more difficult and b) what was blue before (and a very tough battle mind you) is now green or grey.

2. Quests aren't all that great, granted the xp is much better than EQ quest xp was, but you have to drag a full group along to complete even the easiest ones. Quests rarely use solo encounter mobs as objectives, even though they easily could. What happens is that the quest turns green before I am even able to complete it for lack of groups to drag along for the quest.

3. There is essentially only 4 classes, so all the sub-classes kinda run together within the same archetype.

4. You can't help other players without them giving up all rewards for the encounter. I can't even taunt a mob off a dying noob unless the break the encounter.

5. Travel is a pain in the ass. I spend more time running from group to group than actually getting xp most days.

6. I feel like I have to level up so I can do what i really want to do...quest.


I know that many of you haven't played EQ2 so you may not understand all that I was talking about, but really I'd just like to hear how the game is after you've gained a few levels. Does it change much after 20, 30, 40? I've been raiding, to try and garner some excitement out of EQ2, but I'm beginning to think that either I'm just in a really bad level range or the game is going in a dirrection that I don't agree with. I like to group, occasionally, but I can't stand being forced to. In EQ2 I have to be grouped for 90% of the quests that I've come across, and I've done over 175 different quests last I checked. Does soling change significantly in WoW as you level is up, is my main concern, though I would like to hear any of your opinions, good and bad, about the game right now.

I've been playing WoW since release, and I'm having a blast. I'm trying to remember how I felt about EQ when it came out, and I think I've gotten more of a "wow" factor from WoW then I did with EQ. (and I really liked EQ when it came out)

My main character in WoW is a hunter (32), and I've also got a warlock (16) and mage (6).

A few thoughts on WoW:

1. Xp is faster when soloing, although a fast group can do ok - especially in instanced dungeons.

2. I haven't worried about xp...I have to much fun just playing the game and I know eventually I'll hit 60 and raiding and such.

3. I miss having a chat window for each person I'm talking to...on the whole, most people seem less chatty then in EQ. (probably due to the next 2 points)

4. The pace of the game is much faster then EQ. By faster I mean mobs spawn quickly so there is no such thing as camping. Also, quests are generally small/short, so I go in, do the quest, then run back to the NPC to find out what they want next.

5. Related to #4, there is almost no downtime for things like regenning hp and mana. (as an example, on the Blizzard boards I read a post about a high level mage whining because it took 24 seconds to go from no to full mana :rolleyes: )

6. The quests are well thought out and tie into the lore of the game. Also, it's pretty obvious what the quest giver wants, and roughly how to find it. (although actually accomplishing the task can be difficult)

7. Very little death penalty - you lose a little time and some durability on items. This makes me much more likely to experiment and see what I can do as opposed to being super cautious because if I die I lose 2 hours (or more ;) ) of xp.

8. It's a new game and there is downtime as they work out the kinks. I think they've extended the trial period by 3 or 4 days to compensate the players. My total down time is probably less than 10 hours - of course this will vary significantly depending on when people like to play...I don't usually get in until 8 or 9 PM EST, and by then any issues are normally resolved.

9. I've also run into a few bugs (got a problem with stabled pets atm), but nothing I can't work around, and not significantly different from EQ today.

10. Travel is slower then with port stones in EQ, but I don't mind it because of the transport methods. (boats, zeps, bats, wyverns) Once you open up the flight paths, travel isn't really an issue - again, not for me because the method is very cool, and has a good immersion factor.

11. I can't define exactly why, but I like tradeskilling in WoW - and I hated it in EQ. Maybe it's because I can make items that are generally as good or better then what I see as drops from mobs?

If you liked beta, my recommendation is to give it shot...the first month is free, so you'd only be out the purchase price if you decide it's not for you...and you can get to higher levels in a month to see what that aspect of the game is like - if you want to concentrate on levelling (or just play one toon).

3. Down time is a problem with the Warrior class. I'm basically bandaging or eating after every single battle when soloing, to regen health. 4. I can't fight elites solo. Pretty much any elite that is still green it going to beat the crap out of me. A 0 to -2 elite will own the crap out of me, period, end of story.

Otherwise I pretty much endorse and agree with what most people have said about the game. It's an excellent game. In many ways better than the EQ I remember. And one that will probably hold my interest for a long long time. I just hope they make a few adjustments to Warriors at some point.
