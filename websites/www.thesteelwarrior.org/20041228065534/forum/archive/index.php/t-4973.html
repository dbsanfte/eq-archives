I prefer to Taunt before I use incite. Becouse taunt will bring you to the top of agro. and if you are lower then top hit incite and then taunt. any hate before the top agro slot is waisted.

While that sounds reasonable, in practice I find this a bit problematic. Taunt doesn't put you very far ahead of the top of the hate list. So if taunt is the only thing giving you aggro (Which of course it's not because you're attacking and bashing/kicking/slamming/whatever) then as soon as someone else looks at the mob funny you lose aggro again. And if that happens to be right after you've hit taunt, then you have 6 seconds before you can reasonably expect to get aggro back. (even longer if taunt fails, or the caster has low HP aggro by then) And for those poor fragile casters, 6 seconds worth of beating is probably going to mean death.

At the beginning of the fight, I use Incite/Kick/Disarm (I don't have slam, and I don't use a shield, I use two proccing weapons), and save taunt for when I lose aggro to a slow or a nuke. True, Incite by itself is less than slow aggro...BUT...between incite, it's aggro over time element, and they hater you're building with your attacks, there's a good chance of holding aggro even once slow lands.

Remember though..aggro management depends on the entire group. if you've got a chanter or shammy slowing on the pull, or other casters nuking or DoT'ing on the pull, you are going to have a very tough time getting and keeping aggro.

As for hotkeys, I think my setup is probably a little out of the ordinary. I mapped all my hotkey keypresses to the numpad. I'll try to do a crappy little diagram here:

1 2 - -
3 4 - -
5 6 - -
7 8 - 0
9 -

The first 8 hotkeys are mapped to the 8 keys on the left half of the numpad, 9 is on the big '0' key, and 10 is on the big 'ENTER' key. I originally did this shortly after making a bard. It's easier to twist songs with my right hand on the numpad while doing moving around with my left hand (I use WADS for movement), but then I realized it's just plain more convenient and more comfortable with all my characters' keys mapped like that.

Here are how my keys are setup for the typical xp group.

~ = Autoattack
1 = Incite
2 = Kick/Slam
3 = Range
4 = Taunt
5 = Defensive
6 = Furious
7 = "/g Incoming with [ %T ]"
8 = "/g I am engaging [ %T ]"
9 = /assist, /shield (to shield my target's target)
0 = /assist

I have a separate page for raids which is mostly the same except the incoming and engaging macros echo it in /g /rs and /say, and the engaging key adds "ASSIST ME NOW."

It might sound weird, but I like my numpad hotkeys. For one it's easier to visualize the hotkey bank on the numpad rather than in a horizontal line on the regular number keys. Hard to explain I guess. Try it out if you like.
