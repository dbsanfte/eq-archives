I think warriors should get an ability (innate, not 'clicked') that randoms a chance of tackling the mob when it runs off towards someone else, if the mob has been taunted succesfully / not succesful by the warrior.

Also, a check should be made if the warrior gets an attack of oppertunity.
This idea comes from D&D, on which most of EQ is based anyway.
If the mob focusses on a different person, and moves, the warrior/tank gets an attack of oppertunity. (A free strike) Make this strike like a crippling blow, maybe less damage, but at least a decent +aggro. More like "Hey mister, don't ya be thinkin' 'bout doin' that!"

Tackle should be like an attack of oppertunity, but briefly stuns the mob. And, when the mob is on the floor after a succesful tackle, the warrior should get a chance to keep the mob down. (By either bashing, slamming, cripple, crit and whatnot. But only if the warrior hits the mob)

Also, warrior should get the aa (class) to perform a 'knockdown' (like in D&D)
This requires the use of a 2Handed weapon, or, maybe a succesfull bash/kick/slam.
When knocked down, the mob should suffer an AC penalty to all melee attacks (for each and every melee class - and by this also boosting the want for groups to get more melee in the group)

So basically:

-Warrior taunts, but mob still runs off something like 3-4 sec later: warrior gets a chance to tackle.

-Moving mobs suffer a attacks of oppertunity. This counts for all melee classes, or maybe only War/Pal/SK.

-Warriors, elite fighters that have chosen not to spread their specialisation out over magics and the art of fighting, should get more little tricks. PoP brought many kinds of tricks, like rampage and double riposte, but warriors should also get knockdown. The fell an opponent with a mighty, well aimed blow and dazzle the opponent a brief while, causing the opponent to be less agile, and suffer an avoidance penalty / mitigation penalty.
This period can be prolonged by succesful bashes, kicks and slams. - warrior only-(Duh, mob on the ground, how is he going to dodge a kick, this isn't the matrix or max payne)

This doesn't 'nerf' palladins, they can have their stuns. They gave up a lot for it.
This doesn't give warriors 100% secure control of aggro, but it improves the chance to regain aggro, if they lose it.
It's contructive for the warrior class, since it doens't blur classes, it gives warriors a specific advantage. It's not quite as good as some stuns, or snares, but it's a warrior-style approach.
Now, imagine a warrior AND a palladin in the group. Say the palladin tanks, since he has better gear, or the chanter still prefers a pally because of the aggro-security. The warrior can still smash the mob down, and make it more vulnerable for a brief period, improving the effectiveness of monks, pallies, SK's, warriors, rangers and rogues. (like cripple, but not magic)

Attacks of oppertunity are actually quite realistic: the mob runs past you, not really paying attention to you. Why not stab em in the back, or put your foot out eh?

I don't believe it will overpower melee, because it's actually a bit underpowered compared to nukers. (Nor do the effects last very long, maybe long enough for ANY melee to catch aggro and not our cloth wearing brothers and sisters)
AND it doesn't blur classes and steal from other classes, it DOES however make certain combinations of classes more wanted (war + melee, both pal and war in a group....)

I thought long and hard on a way in which you don't nerf other classes, don't really make warriors stronger, but fix aggro and group desirability.
All I had to do was read the D&D manual.
