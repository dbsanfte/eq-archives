I decided to borrow a friends SK the other night to test out the differences for myself. I ran a parser for 90 mins or so on both chars. DPS with SK using Blindfoe, and me using CHSD/Primal 1 hander was pretty much equal, although obviously the warrior had a lot more ATK. Both are level 65, neither have any offensive AA. My warrior has an 1800hp advantage when buffed.

Aggro generation-wise, well with the SK I needed to cast a maximum of 2 spells and I could lock down aggro for the whole fight. Generally 1 darkness line spell on inc was enough, occasionally I chucked an extra DD or tap to make sure. Other classes in the group were at various times shaman, necro, cleric, wizard, ranger, enchanter and mage. The necro stole aggro once when he got a bit stack happy, and the shm took 2 or 3 hits when slow was resisted more than once.

When I switched to my warrior, aggro was back to its usual random self. I was getting a lot of taunt failures and the debuffers were taking a lot more damage. Generally once the mob had been beaten down to 60% or so, or if I got a proc or 2 from hammer early on aggro was no problem.

I personally was shocked at how easy it is for a knight to lock down aggro before the mob is even in camp, and how little they have to do to maintain it. I am lucky enough to have a pure hate proccing weapon on my warrior, and even so the difference between warrior and knight aggro is like night and day.

Tanking wise, there was no noticable difference in the amount of healing required. My warriors 1800hp advantage was unnoticable. The cleric did have to use the odd fast heal on the shaman when I was tanking with warrior though.
