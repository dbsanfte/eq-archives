I am new to EQ, playing for about 3 weeks (lvl 14 Barb War) on Morden Rasp. I dutifully read a number of boards and was looking for a way to make some platinum. Hailing from Halas (that sounds stupid) and having Everfrost at my doorstep made spiderling silk + low quality pelts = leather padding an attractive option. I plugged away at the spiders, bears and wolves, built up my tailoring and went to work. Even assuming that I would be producing 100s of these eventually, I put my first batch up at the Bazaar, thinking I could sell for 25 to 45pp each. Nope, I did a search and found one guy with 400 at 25p, and some other ones between 20-24. I just wanted to sell them, so I listed at 19p. I left my guy on overnight - nothing. The next day at work, nothing. It took two-and-a-half days for them to sell. I am almost sure that I was the lowest-priced seller the entire time, which to me means that no one bought these during that time. So much for my cash cow. From what I read, you normally can't keep these in stock. What is going on?

I do have a theory. Because Morden Rasp is a new server (I think March 2004) there are not a lot of "rich" players that can buy the padding or, for that matter, the armor that is produced by using it. I also assume that there are not a lot of 40+ level players who can throw the $ around. What do you all think? Will this change? Am I just impatient? I know know that, for the near future, I won't be selling padding for over 24p, until the 400 at 25p go.

On a related note, some of the prices at the Bazaar are ridiculous. Two examples -- I was looking to buy the Blackened Alloy Bastard Sword - checked the boards and saw that 125 to 150 would be fair. Went to the board and saw about a couple listed. They were priced at 300 and 425p. I came back the next day and saw one listed for 250 - even though I thought it was too much, I was going to buy it. After shutting down my trader, I went over to the guy and found that he had upped the price to 270! I didn't buy it. (The happy ending to the story is that I was able to pick it up in the POK for 100p -- great sword, although it looks a bit wimpy).

Second (and worse) example: I wanted a couple Orc Fang Earrings. Checked some boards and saw that I would be lucky to get them for 30p each or 50p for the pair. I WTB at the POK, no luck. Went to the Bazaar, did my search and saw 10 traders selling about 40 earrings. The highest was 450p, but the lowest was 200p, FOR ONE ! You must be kidding me. The stat bonus is OK, not great. I do not think I will wearing any stylish ear-wear any time soon.

Is this typical for new servers? Has there been EQ inflation? Are there any other comparable earrings for a warrior? (On a related note - from my mid teens to 30, what should I be trying to get? I like the Newbie Quest Armor, but I should probably be looking to exchange it, right?).

This board is terrific, and thanks in advance for your comments or help.

Hi guys,

One thing to remember about new servers is that supply will be very low. Supply of not only items but money. EveryQuest experiences an infinite amount of monetary inflation (since no or little money is destroyed) and an infinite amount of item inflation because there is no such thing as scarcity (except for mobs to kill), also known as mudflation. The only way EverQuest introduces scarcity is by the simple difficulty of attaining items and no drop items. As the server matures, better items are attained easier by the "uber" folks, and then farmers come in. Then, everything of that type that is replaced by the new item(s) falls in price.
