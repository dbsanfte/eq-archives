VT is a mixed bag.

For smaller guilds, Vex Thal is a great place to equip your guild to prepare for PoP encounters as they are, for the most part, designed for large amounts of numbers. Chances are almost everyone is going to get a piece of loot in a VT raid if you're raiding with 35.

If you're in a larger guild, the hassle of keying up for Vex Thal may drive you completely insane. You don't need VT gear to be successful in PoP, and the amount of guilds that have defeated Rallos Zek without a drop of VT gear is growing (albeit slowly, most guilds do VT regardless). What also tends to be common in large guilds is the average person receives less loot over time. In a place like VT, this becomes a problem as everyone tries to hold out for Aten loot.

What you really want to ask yourself is this:
-Do you want wade through the shit filled sewer that is Luclin to smell roses on the other side? Or do you want to climb that difficult mountain for that awesome feeling of accomplishment that is Planar Progression?

Poosha
First things first: You'll need to be able to kill the Emperor. For this you need:

1) Bane weapons for all your melees (tanks, arguably, can ignore this as they'll likely be using their aggro weapons in spite of not doing actual damage). Corrupted Shissar (bane) weapons are made by combining a Shissar weapon (drops off Elite Shissars in Ssra) with a Greenish Metal (rarely drops off named skeletons in the mines of Ssra... bring a PBAE crew and pack a lunch, heh). The combine can be done in a normal forge and is no-fail.

2) Key to the Emperor's room. This requires a Taskmaster Pouch (off taskmasters ans wardens in the basement of Ssra), an Idol (from Comm1 in Ssra), a Ring (from Comm2 in Ssra), and an Insignia (from Generals and Advisors up on the 3rd floor of Ssra... bring a raid force and spend an evening there, you'll get ~20 per trip). Combine the three items in the Taskmaster Pouch and voila: key to the Emp.

Once you have a decent force with Emp keys, your guild can actually take a little breather. Check the Emp regularly to see if he's up, and kill him when you can, but in between you can spend some time killing PoP Tier 1 bosses, Luclin bosses (for decent loot plus Orbs of Luclinite, which you'll need later), or returning to Velious to farm some decent drops (NToV, AoW, Sleepers, etc.). Meanwhile your members can spend some time in groups farming shards for so-so xps, and get to know each other and help each other out.

Each member will need:

1) 10 shards from various parts of Luclin (some soloable, the rest one-groupable).

2) A Glowing Orb of Luclinite. Many bosses in Luclin drop these, and these bosses will always drop one orb per kill. Orbs also drop from some named mobs in Ssra, so have your members tracking pops while they're farming their Emp key pieces.

3) A Planar Rift, which results from killing the Emperor. Each Emp kill will net the guild 36 Rifts, so you'll probably have to hit him a few times to get your guild outfitted, depending on your size.

4) An Unadorned Scepter of Shadows. This is a mini-quest in Akheva Ruins whereby the person will need to give an Essence Emerald (Necros make these with their Sacrifice spell) and a Wisp Stone (mage summoned) to an NPC.

Combine the 10 shards in the scepter and it will create a 2-slot container. Put the Orb and Rift in the container and hit combine. Voila! Your key to Vex Thal. :)


Is it a lot of work? Hell yes. My guild is finishing up with Velious currently, and we're actively preparing ourselves for VT. We started shard hunting about 3 or 4 months ago, well before we were capable of defeating the Emperor... before we were in NToV in fact. Our guild leader posts a message to the guild each month stating what our current objectives are, and each month he raises the number of shards that he expects most members to have collected. It's now at the point where probably 50% of our 93 person guild has 8-10 shards, most of these people have their orbs, and our Emp key collection is growing steadily.

We decided to take this steady approach to VT keying instead of the usual "This month is VT month! If you're not in Ssra while online, you will be guildremoved!" method. It has resulted in far less pressure on our members (although some do find the process overwhelming, heh), and it allows us to accomplish other goals at the same time, such as beating 99% of Velious and making our first brave attempts at Tier 1 bosses. Some would look at us and say that we have no focus, but I would argue that our goal is just different than most... we want to see VT (and beyond), but we don't want to be overbearing to our members in our quest to get there.

Will VT be worth it? My opinion is yes. The sense of accomplishment at having beaten an entire expansion will be well worth it to me. If your goal is reaching Time as fast as you can, then you should probably skip it, but if you're just looking for challenging and rewarding experiences then I'd guess that VT should be on the list of things to do. :)
