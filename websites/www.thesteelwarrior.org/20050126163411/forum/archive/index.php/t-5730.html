So my guildleader got a Blade of War last week, first one in the guild. The next day on a raid he was MA on yard trash and I found that I had to bag my bloodfrenzy or I was pulling aggro constantly. Now it wasn't really a fair comparison because I was using 2 EB weapons with Anger III and I have WA3. He hadn't auged the BoW yet and has no WA as far as I know but it got me thinkin.

Back when the BoW first came out, it was considered the best aggro in the game. It was a lower proc rate than BF/BoC...say 2 ppm vs. 4, but the extra DPS was enough to make up for the 2 lost procs. I wonder if augments have changed this.

For example, with an auged BoW you should be able to get 4 ppm without weapon affinity, but with an aug'd BF/BOC you should get something like 7, so now you're giving up 3 ppm to use the BoW.

Assume that WA5 is a straight 50% (little early to say this is 100% true but it seems about right) and now you get 6 ppm from the BoW but 10.5 from dual wield, so you're giving up 4.5 ppm to use the BoW. Is the extra dps from the BoW enough to make up for 4.5 less procs a minute? I kind of doubt it.

Now I know that there are other reasons to use a BoW, dps and lower ripostes specifically, but I have to think that with augs and Weapon Affinity the edge goes back to BF/BoC from a pure hate standpoint.

Any thoughts?

I used a BF/BoC combo in the past. When you get proc's, aggro is very solid. I use a BoW/Anger3 weapon now and noticed that I get aggro from the swing moreso than swinging a BF/BoC.
