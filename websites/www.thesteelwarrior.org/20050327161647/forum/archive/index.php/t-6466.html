uh. copied from my other post:

If you haven't figured out why you're falling through the wall, it's because monsters have a bit of pushback when they melee, just like a PC, and in most cases they do quite a bit more pushback. Well, when you back yourself tight into a corner, this melee pushback will push you through the wall, and make you fall through the world.

Obviously there are 2 simple fixes;

The first being not to corner the mob. That's right, you heard it here first kids, not all beastmonsters have to be cornered in order to win. In fact, none do! wooot.

Secondly, if you really do want to corner the beastmonster, you could try backing into a corner, then pressing the forward arrow key, or whatever key you have bound to the forward movement action. In most cases, especially with larger beastmonsters, you can still effectivly "corner" without being completely backed into the corner. It works wonders

AE rampage monsters and knockback monsters can be treated very similarly. The MA would stand at a corner, but a few steps out, so that he is in melee range while backed up all the way. If he gets knockbacked, he would just move back up to his spot. For AE rampage monsters, what works is having the nukers facing away from the mob, pushing him out toward the melee, and the melee pushing him back in the corner, if one side is out pushing the other, then some of the melee can angle toward the side, or a nuker can face toward the monster and help the melee. Take a look at this picture, it describes what I'm talking about.
