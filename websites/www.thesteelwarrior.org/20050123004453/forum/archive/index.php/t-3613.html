Tools for the solo warrior:

1- Fungi. You got that. It's a start.

2- A good haste item. You need to improve DPS.

3- Clickable haste. In other words, Eyepatch of Plunder. So that you can stack spell/song haste on top of your good haste item.

4- Truncheon of Doom. Past level 50 it starts to proc. And it's invaluable because of this.

5- A lifetapping proc weapon. I used the Black Acrylia Halberd at those levels, but the bloodpoints also help. Basically, what you're doing is reducing your enemy's hit points while increasing yours. This is very useful.

6- If you forego a lifetapping weapon, make sure you forego it for a very GOOD DPS weapon or weapons. To toe to toe you need to kill the mobs as fast as possible.

7- Invigorate BP. Cobalt, or Champion's or whatever. It may take awhile due to long cast times and large number of hit points most warriors have, BUT ... you need a way to get back to 100 percent health. Bandages at those levels max out at 50. And the fungi regen is SLOW on a 3 to 4k hit point warrior.

8- Buffs. Whenever you can, get buffs from people, like Temperance or whatever.

9- Damage shield. Regal Band of Bathezid, the Shield of the 8th, and Kiva's Blistering Flesh. Let the mob HELP you kill it.

10- An escape route, and a means to use it. SoW potions. A horse. Run 3. Whatever. Just be able to run away from the mobs if things get bad.

10a- If you are gonna run faster than the mob, remember that jousting can work, as can bow kiting. Nowhere near as effective as a ranger bow kiting, but runnign away from a mob when you're low on HPs, allowing your fungi to regen you, and still keeping the mob's hit points going DOWN, is a good thing.

Another thing to consider ...

Proc augments.

LDoN offers a set of lifetap proc augs, as well as Guard augs. Both are useful for soloing. However, to get them you need points in adventures, which means you need to group.

But it's something to consider.

Good luck.

Have fun. But be prepared for large amounts of downtime, even with all the fun toys.
