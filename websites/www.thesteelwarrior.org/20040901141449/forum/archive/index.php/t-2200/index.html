I think that since there is a way for the casting classes to specialize in a form of casting. That warrior could perhaps do something along the same lines. Someone mentioned to be able to specialize in a specific skill but I think that is actually not enough. I suggest the following. Adding these in addition to the already available AA like CS, CA, LR, and so on.

Specialize Defense:

Through the need to be a sturdy member of the fighting force, we have learned how to teach you to be able to Increase your Defensive skills above all other skills through practice and training. Specializing in this skill will allow you to avoid and mitigate signigantly more damage than your average warrior. Along with increasing your avoidance and mitigation as you become more skilled in your specialty you will begin to emit an aura that will cause the creatures about you to become extremely agitated at your very presence.


Specialize Offense:

Although a hearty race designed to take a blow we have learned that some of our profession prefers to be able to inflict more devastating damage than that of their peers. We have obtained knowledge on how to teach you to focus your Offensive abilities enabling you to be quite formidable. Specializing in this skill will allow you to increase your damage per hit and decrease the number of misses per combat round.

Ok, I have a question to those who posted that it should not be an AA.

Do the caster classes have to spend an AA when Training in Specialization?

Answer: NO

At level 20 or 24 (not sure) a caster can choose one to the casting skills to specialize in. It is done through their class trainer. Some of the skills are specialization evocation, abjuration, divination, and conjuration. Every skill can get up to a max of 50 only one can go beyond the skill of 50. For example a Wizard would choose Specialization Evocation since the majority of a wizard’s damage spells are Evocation based. So at level 21, during a fight or after casting an evocation based spell (garrison’s mighty manna shock), a wizard might see this:

You have gotten better at Evocation (128) …………... This being the basic innate skill.
You have gotten better at Specialize Evocation (53)… This being the CHOSEN skill

So, in retrospect, A warrior of appropriate level having the ability to TRAIN AT THEIR CLASS TRAINER the skill Specialize Defense or Specialize Offense, would see a message like this during a fight.

You have gotten better at Defense (128) ……………..This being the basic innate skill.
You have gotten better at Specialize Defense (51) …. This being the CHOSEN skill
