Hmm....I've been having a mage in the group cast mala first...will casting a debuff with my shaman actually reduce the amount of hate he gets from slow? If so, this may solve my 2 boxing problem.

No, I don't think I was clear enough. Reducing aggro directly isn't the idea here. The purpose of casting MalXXX before the slow is 2 fold. One to reduce the amount of resists, thereby making it so even if the shaman does pull aggro, he can live thru it. Most shaman have a decent amount of HP/AC and AA's, they can take a punch from a slowed mob and walk away from it.

The second reason is time. By waiting until after you have touched the mob to start Malos, that's a guaranteed 5-7 extra seconds for you to build aggro..both thru DPS, and procs. That's huge. Some mobs don't give a fuck, and will seriously rush the slower at any point, but the majority of mobs are easily controlled in this manner.

I don't know a whole lot about slows and resists, but I know that the shammy in my group pulls aggro MUCH more often on resists (and keeps it). But checking the Spell Details for Tigir's Insects on LUCY, I am assuming that if it doesn't land, the Hate modifier isn't applied and the shaman takes the full dose. And he has to cast slow again . So if he can land slow on first cast, that's a big plus for him. If I'm getting this correctly, whoever can debuff Magic resist the most should do so - one advantage to the shaman doing it is that it extends the time for you to build aggro before the slow hits, but on the other hand the hate from both is on the shaman. I suggest experimenting with tactics.

No shaman in any of your groups is using Tigirs, that's the AE slow. Turgurs is the spell most of your shaman should be using, and it has no negative hate modifier, so that's not something you have to worry about. The negative hate on Tigirs is just there so a shaman can actually survive slowing 4 mobs at one time with the spell.

I don't think resists themselves are the problem. I've held aggro thru resisted slows just fine. I think the problem stems from the fact that slow adds X amount of hate, which is already a lot for warriors to overcome. A resist means that you have to add that X amount of hate again, and again, and keep on doing it until you get it right. Malos'ing every mob may not seem like the most mana efficient route for shaman, but it increases their survivability and reduces ping pong exponentially by reducing the amount of aggro a shaman has to put on a mob to do their job.

So, ask your shaman to either Malo/Malos/Malosinia first to both debuff the mob and kill some time while you try to work the happy proc magic, and if you have someone else doing the debuffing, ask the shaman to wait 5-7 seconds or so before he starts the slow. You'll survive it, and so will he.
