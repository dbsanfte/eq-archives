An average of roughly 5 * DI is probably about as low as you are going to get in terms of damage mitigation. So at roughly 6 DI, the warrior tanks about 9% better in this parse, assuming the difference in average DI is valid and not an artifact of too small of a parse. As the DI portion becomes more and more of total damage, that percentage savings goes up. It will never be a dramatic difference, but will go up *slightly* as average hit goes up. It isn't a static advantage.

The absolute lowest the difference would be (assuming the same hit %) is:

([trunc(1*DI)+DB] * 52)/(trunc[(1*DI*0.95)+DB] * 50)
[trunc(22.1+102) * 52]/[trunc(21+102) * 50] =
(6448/6150) = 5%


and the maximum it would ever be is:

(trunc[(20*DI)+DB] * 52)/(trunc[(20*DI*0.95)+DB] * 50)
[trunc(442+102) * 52]/[trunc(420+102) * 50] =
(28288/26100) = 8%

That of course assumes the warrior and the knight get hit for the same multiple of DI on average AND that the hit rate listed in the parse is pretty accurate. (hit rate is pretty easy to get close since it is a binary set) So against this mobs at this AC level, we can guess that a warrior will at best about a 8% advantage and at worst a 5% advantage, assuming the exact same mitigation table. Not really earthshaking, but there are a couple of assumptions in there.


I dont even want to try and compare against scaling relative DIs (6.3/6.1). ;) Firstly, the parse isn't really long enough to give us any confidence in that small of a margin (some statistics guys would have to pipe up on this one, I don't know how to judge confidence in numbers or a standard deviation). Secondly, we have no way of knowing if the average DI for the warrior and knight would stay constant relative to each other.

Would be interesting to see a 1300-1400 AC warrior and knight do this exact same parse. It would also be interesting to see a parse against a mob with a higher DI relative to DB as is common in newer content.

It's alot more complex than saying warriors have a 9% bonus, which this parse seems to indicate. That bonus is going to scale across several factors and can be both smaller and larger than this parse would indicate. I know this parse was not meant to be an authoritative answer to the question of "what is the difference between a warrior and a knight" and I hope it isn't taken that way either and we end up seeing "9% difference!" plastered all over threads for the next 6 months.
