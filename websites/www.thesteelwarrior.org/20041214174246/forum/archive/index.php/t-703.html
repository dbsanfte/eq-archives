Over the past few months ive been reading different threads on possible ways to "fix" the broken warrior. This for what its worth is my take on the situation.

When fighting once I do have a solid lock on a mob it takes a bit to get it off me. The problem is getting that solid lock in the first place. At the moment im engaging mobs and effectively soloing the mob for 14 or more seconds at times before I call for assist. This is even worse when breaking Mezz.

So here is my basic idea for a fix. The creation of a new discipline, to become available from level 45. As a working name I would call it First Strike. The basics of it would be a single blow where your weapon had double damage for 1 single hit with a hate proc attached to it. It would work on a 5 minute timer and would only land on a mob that is at 100% health.

The theory behind this is fairly simple. To generate enough agro with one hit to give you a better than 50% chance of keeping agro when breaking a mezz and better than 80% chance of maintaining agro from a slow or such which lands within 14 seconds of engaging the mob.

Like I said this is just a basic idea at this time and would probably need balancing out. But this would certainly make my job a little easier.

maybe nonsense I dont know.

See, this is what I mean by a basic idea. I knew there would be little things I had overlooked. In this case I overlooked 2 things.

First:- Damage caused By the puller, and
Second:- Damage caused through a bow peel

Now if i figure things right, most monks usea thrown item of some description to pull and most warriors who are using a bow to peel off have a fairly decent Bow. So I think an allowance of 300 points should be fine.

Why am I trying to be so specific here. Because I genuinely believe this to be a good idea. I want to submit this to the Dev team via e-mail. Yes it may be a complete waste of time, but time is something I have and i'm willing to at least give this a try.

But before I do do this I want to get it structured so it is balanced properly. So it doesnt become overpowering in any way. The specific goal of this is to do what taunt doesnt do. Hell not even hate procing weapons are able to do what i'm looking for here and that is having a better than 50% chance of holding agro for more than 5 seconds, without having everyone i'm grouped with sitting around like a dodo for 15-20 seconds while I generate the agro before calling for assist.

This has to be set up so it can only work at the very start of the engagement and cannot be used midfight. The idea here is simply to replace that 15-20 seconds on normal mobs and to reduce the time spent building up agro on mini's and boss mobs.

I'm not looking for something thats 100% here. I want there to be a chance that a character will get agro if they cast a big agro spell. I just want the odds tipped in my favour.

I can see why you'd want it for slows, but breaking mez? I very rarely have problems keeping aggro after a mez. Here's what I do... Only works if you're level 55+ for stun kick.

1. Go behind mob
2. Taunt, disarm
3. Hit kick, then immediately turn on attack
4. The stun SHOULD land if your kick landed, mobs get stunned easy from behind. You have another combat round and taunt before they get unstunned.

You should have relatively solid aggro if you do that. I was tanking a ring event last night in umbral plains, and I used that strategy to switch mobs in under 5 seconds when the previous one died.
