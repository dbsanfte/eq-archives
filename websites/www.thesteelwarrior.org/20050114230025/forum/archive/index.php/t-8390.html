The other day while goofing around in Vxed I found something out about Chaos Claws that I didnt already know: it is not a proc, it is just an innate ability that was given to some mobs.

Have someone with snare go fetch a noc in Vxed, you stand way away from the mob on the pull. Have your group debuff the crap out of it and root it. Now since you havent done anything to it you can walk right up to it and it will completely ignore you, it'll just point at the person it hates the most. Now watch the Chaos Claws spam fly by (It wont hit your group members in this situation because it's range 20).

The frequency of Chaos Claws seems to be tweeked depending on what zone you are in. In Ferubi or Barindu for example, the mobs that have it dont seem to spam it like Vxed or Tipt. I'm assuming it was put in the game as a counter to the slow line, slow doesnt affect how often a mob hits you with claws. And I'm guessing that Cripple doesnt affect it since it is not a proc.

And yes, I already know that you uber warriors could care less about it, but for us non ubers it can hurt a lot. Chromatic plus -150 resist adjust means that i have never once resisted it (resist buffed or not). Spam 650 in Vxed can really add up. I'm already getting torn up pretty badly by trash Vxed/Tipt mobs, not being able to resist spam claws is icing on the blood cake.

Not that any of this makes any difference, I was just mildy amused to see it was being spammed when the mob was rooted way away from the group.

It's not a proc. There's two ways to check this:

1. It lands on misses: Now this in itself isn't enough to state that it's not a proc because the current understanding of inherent procs only landing on successful hits *could* be wrong, although it's not ever been proven a single time in six years of EQ.

2. It lands out of melee range: This seals it. You can be outside of melee range and take a chaos claw. It's simply a single target effect without a cast time.

Trample is worse than Chaos Claw IMO, though.
