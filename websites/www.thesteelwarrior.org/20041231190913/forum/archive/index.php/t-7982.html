heh, actually i just did. i have *maybe* an hour a day tops to play anymore, so i twinked out a new war and just solo when i need my fix. rolled an ogre who worships RZ, here's my reasons:

-frontal melee-based stun immunity
-worshipping RZ lets you wear feirce heraldic cultural armor @ lvl 46+
-high innate str and sta make it easier to cap those stats (especially for a player who doesn't have time to raid or camp much). leaves you with just dex and agi to focus on stat-wise. cha isn't a factor as i won't be needing di.

that's mainly why i chose that combo. excellent for the low-mid-non-raiding 65 game...especially when i'm looking to mainly solo or just screw around with my extremely limited play time. if you're going to end up raiding a lot and playing a lot of the very high end game, things change a bit. you'll usually need gnome illusion to corner tank as an ogre which eats a buffslot. you're going to want to invest in a shrink clicky for just about ANY indoor situation. and with stalwart endurance AA, any other warrior race can build up a 70-80%(?) stun resistance from ANY side.

definitely not the best race for everyone, but it's the best to meet my needs. my main is a 65/150ish aa dorf. for the shorties!

I play an ogre warrior, it has its pros and cons:

Pros:
- Frontal stun immunity. I cannot praise this enough. I played a troll SK for a while and holy hell I cannot see how anyone puts up with that garbage of constantly being stunned while tanking. Trying to cast is even more frustrating. But hey that's me.

- Stats are decent for starting out.


Cons:
- The "ogre screen shake" that you get when you attack a mob makes me dizzy. It is exaggerated compared to playing a smaller race. I tried a gnome warrior to pal around with some friends and it was just simply a joy to play. I didnt have the earthquake effect going on all the time. Lots of people dont even know this problem exists unless they have played many different sized melee classes. Shrink doesnt completey negate the screen shake, I've found that it has more to do with your current model. My screen still shakes a ton while triple shrunk ogre, but isnt nearly as bad when illusion gnomed.


- Ogres are freaking HUGE. If i look straight ahead while tanking, odds are I cannot even see my target, I have to look down to see the mob. Cobalt bracer / illusion fixes all of these problems so the point is moot.

- XP penalty for ogre. Shrug. I dont notice it.



If I had it all to do over again, I would choose an ogre warrior with an insta cast clicky, permanent illusion gnome with no buff icon :) Seriously, I'd still pick ogre because I cannot deal with stuns all the time, but I dont play my ogre without being super shrunk or better yet, illusion gnome or dorf.

I think, based on looks, the wood elf warrior looks the best. The plate armor just looks the coolest to me. Unfortunately, you get a big loss of stats to go with it. Once you get to be lvl 65 with a hefty dose of AA, the loss of stats doesn't really matter. You'll buff out to 305 stamina/strength, etc. regardless. But until then, it will be harder to find groups and harder to get your HP up to respectible levels.

If it's a female....well, I gotta go with the dark elf on that. The flamed plate stuff looks real nice. Wood elf and half elf are also nice to look at.

If you want to go on what really counts... stats.... you pretty much should go with the Ogre.

If you want the best of both worlds (good stats and good looks), stick with the Barbarian. I didn't want to deal with the looks of the Ogre (vanity) so I went Barbarian, and would do it again in a heartbeat.

As far as diety, that's primarily a function of the race. I am a barbarian and went with Tribunal. If I had it to do over again, I'd have gone Rallos Zek. The actual cultural armor is almost exactly the same for both... however, RZ armor is a little easier to come by in the bazaar (at least on my server). This probably has something to do with the fact that Helanic Tundra armor can only be made by a barbarian smith. I don't know if the same kind of race thing applies to RZ. I wouldn't think so because barbarians can wear it too, but I don't know. And because it's more plentiful, it's probably a little cheaper as well.

Do not under any circumstances make an agnostic warrior. Cultural armor is too much a good buy for the buck and you can't afford to cut yourself out of that. While I'm doing my best to replace it with no drop stuff now, it has served me well for many levels.
