I'm sorry that happened to you, Beornegar. Seeing something like that always makes you wonder what that person is thinking... of course, now we know he was thinking "I just got screwed over by that #$%#$% spinetwist rolling and winning on a parchment in my last group, no way am I sitting on this melee weapon -- I need it to sell so I can buy the spell I didn't get from that parchment!" :D Just kidding of course... but there's an element of truth to it -- we've all seen loot distributed in ways that we thought were unfair, and it makes it that much harder to be fair and unselfish the next time you have a chance for something.

No class or category ("caster", "melee", "hybrid") has cornered the market on outright greed; it's spread pretty evenly among the EQ population. Unfortunately, greed multiplies. It only takes one or two people acting out of greed to taint a whole guild/group/raiding party.... once people see that loot is going to the selfish instead of those who would truly benefit most, there is little reason for them to continue being selfless and abstaining when they don't "need" the item.

I play primarily a chanter and cleric (I do have a 50 warrior, and my wife plays a 65 warrior), so I have a pretty good chance to see all sides of the loot issue. Let me assure you that for every story you have about a caster rolling for melee items, I can trade you a story about a tank doing the same on a caster item. This is aggravated by the fact that most camps don't have an even distribution of loot for the various classes; NBG seems a bit less fair if you KNOW that nothing will drop that your class "needs". It also loses a lot of its appeal when you see people abusing it: I will never forget the warrior who called NBG on a pair of boots, and when asked to link his old boots claimed he was wearing *nothing* in that slot (sure enough, he was suddenly barefoot -- funny he hadn't been a few minutes before!); It was eventually pointed out that one of the other group members had been present when he had claimed the very same boots a short time before.

In general, I'm in favor of any system which gives everyone a decent chance to get something nice, and spreads it out as evenly as possible. I would agree that NBG works best when you like and trust the people you are with; otherwise, /random is often much less prone to stir up resentment.... but above all else, have the discussion and settle the issue of how loot is to be resolved BEFORE something valuable drops. If you don't agree with the system, don't join the group.

Landrain's idea has some merit -- if you are going with a NBG system, there's no reason why the duplicate spells that often result from parchment turnins shouldn't be /randomed among all parties.
