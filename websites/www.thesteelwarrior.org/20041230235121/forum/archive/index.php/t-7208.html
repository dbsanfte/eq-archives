Flair, concerning where the formula's came from:
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=3789

That's a really long thread and for most of it people are testing, guessing, re-testing in different ways. Essentially the "testing" involved casting a known amount of hate on a mob, then having a second character hit the mob with the weapon being tested until the mob turned on the the second character. The most obvious thing was that it didn't matter how many times a weapon missed the mob: the mob turned on the same number of swings each time (with any given weapon), not on the same amount of damage--so, a weapon's aggro was clearly based on some sort of potential damage, and not actual damage. Then people spend a long time attempting to reverse engineer the formula to describe the way in which weapons of different dam/delays were behaving.
The formulas I used above were presented in the thread; of several formulas suggested, these seemed to best fit the data. They were in fact off a little bit (< 5%, if I recall); they're approximate, but came pretty close to predicting how many swings it would take with a given weapon to turn a mob given a set amount of hate.
