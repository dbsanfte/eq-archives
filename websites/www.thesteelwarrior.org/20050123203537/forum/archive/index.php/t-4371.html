My understanding, from back in the days when people were using Longsword of Eth. energy:

Elemental damage is first checked to see if it's completely resisted or not (on each hit). With Longsword of Eth. this was +5 magic damage, so MR was checked. If the magic successfully landed, then it would add a random ammount to the weapon's damage value, a random value from 1 to 5, or zero to 5, I forget. This resulted in the sword averaging like 1.5 extra damage to it's base damage value (from the +5). This always seemed rather a stupid way to do it to me, but there you have it. So to recap, it didn't add +5 to the damage, it added a random number 1 to 5 IF the resist check was made.

So. . .my prediction is that on mobs that are not cold resistant, you'd average about +1 to the weapons base damage value. The undead bane portion would be a separate additional ammount to undead.

My understanding is that elemental damage, if not resisted, acts like the damage bonus for level. It adds to the total damage after all other calculations. Bane damge is added to the weapons damage before any other calculations, provided you are fighting a mob of the proper type. i.e. a 13/20 weapon with Bane undead +2 is 15/20 vs undead, but a 13/20 weapon with +2 fire is just 13/20, with 0-2 damage added per hit.

elemental dmg (cold fire magic poison disease) are added to the base dmg of a weapon, if the target does not resist it at all.

bane dmg are added to the base dmg of a weapon, if the target type matches the bane dmg type.

both of them are nothing related to the dmg bonus of the weapon. they are added directly to the base dmg of the weapon.
- for example, if u assume PoFire mobs dont resist cold base dd at all, a 15 20 1hs with the augment u listed, will be working as a 17 20 1hs against the mobs, and working as a 18 20 1hs against horse, as they (additionally) are undeads.
