Brabble
I tend to agree with the other responses; you're doing fairly well.
Some random comments, starting with some stock suggestions:
1. Start going to the bazaar at the end of every other session or so and spend a few minutes searching your armor slots one by one by race, religion, class, and AC. I'd key on AC, because you're getting to the point where you'll need more of it and most armor out there with good AC will also have other stats. Do one search for 0-500pp and one for 500-2000 pp. Start tracking prices and get a feel for the market on your server. Take notes.

2. Your STA earring would be a good place to start looking for an upgrade.

3. You don't list any other weapons that you might have. If the only weapons you have are 1H slash, you might want to work on finding useable stuff in other categories. My standard suggestion for 1H blunt is for you to go to Maiden's Eye. Stay near the zoneline and /ooc that you are looking for a goranga spiked club (primary only, 12 dam, 23 delay, AC+5, STR+2), if anyone is letting one rot (they're no-drop, and the folks fighting in this zone will have better weapons). You should get this weapon for free, and you could use it in primary and move your graverobber's to secondary. That would train 1HB and marginally improve your offhand ratio. And 1HB is good because eventually you'll probably want a Copper Hammer of Striking. Be very careful while you are in ME.

3.A. For similar reasons, you might look around for a good 2HB. Before you can afford the droga one-hand weapons, you can get by in your late 50s with a Greatstaff of Thunder (2HB) for aggro. The Greatstaff of Thunder is not usable until (um, I think) level 56, but it costs only 100-400 pp and will hold aggro in the ballpark of weapon set ups that cost 10K+ plat. It's nice if your skill is trained up in 2HB, though the skill only influences the damage you actually do to the mob, not the aggro you generate.

3B. You might look into the price on weighty polearms (2HS, 29 dam/34 delay, AC +15, AGI +15, Fire +10, hps +35). Haven't priced them since the recommended level fix, but they used to sell for less than 100 pp on my server.

3C If you want to bother with piercing, check the prices on jarsath Tridents (1h piercing, pri/sec, 11 dam/22 delay; STR +5, DEX-5, STA +5, AGI -5). It's warrior-only and also used to go for well under 100 pp. The combination of piercing, warrior-only restriction, and the fact that it lowers DEX and AGI keep this weapon low in price. But it's fast (in fact, you would lose nothing in the aggro department by putting it in your mainhand and using the Graverobber's in the offhand) and until you start using proc-ing weapons the significance of -5 DEX is completely trivial. You can use this weapon until level 50 if you use all of the other tools available to you for aggro generation. Check recommended levels on all of these before you buy, since I haven't used these weapons since the fix.

4. Cultural armor is going to be something you want to look into eventually. Start checking prices now to get a feel for what you need to save up for. It's going to be a while before you can afford it if you are getting all of your pp from fighting and farming. when you search the bazaar for your cultural armor, take note of who is selling it. Go to EQ Traders and check to see what the subcomponents are for this armor. If you can gather significant quantities of the subcomponents and can make friends with a smith, you might get this cheaper.

5. Haste. You'll soon need some. Be prepared for sticker shock. there's a list of haste items in the library; start looking it over.

6. while saving your LDoN points for the truly useful things (anger augs and top-end armor) is certainly the best plan long-term, you might find that the gothic armor isn't cutting it sometime before you can afford cultural armor. Some of the 150-point MM (Butcherblock) armor is a potential upgrade. There's a decided tradeoff here: that armor is not going to last you very long, and you already know how hard LDoN points are to accumulate at your level. But this is an option if around level 50 you start feeling like a lighter tank than you want to be and you're really broke.

7. If you don't know how stat bonus food and drink work, find out. Then get some. Certainly use Halas 10-pound meat pies, as they're cheap and fairly common. Grobb Liquised meat is also often available and affordable.

8. Rallic pack quest is something you *might* be able to do youself, if not now, then fairly soon. If you can get a buddy, so much the better. It's a nice bag. The grimling runner appears outside the Grimling caves in Ten. Mountains (be careful on the run out there). The perfect (I think it's "perfect") owlbear pelt drops off owlbears in the owlbear caves in Shadeweavers, and both the run to Ten mnts. and the owlbear cave would probably be a lot safer for you with a friend or two along. But, research the quest, as the reward is useful and the quest is fun.

Perhaps I'll think of more later.
