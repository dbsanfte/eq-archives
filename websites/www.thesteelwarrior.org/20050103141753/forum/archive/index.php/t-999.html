Well I feel a little silly posting about this, but I have had a blast this last weekend on Anarchy Online.

For those of you who do not know about it, AO is a MMORPG set in the far future. You are deposited on the planet of Rubi-Ka where you chose from 4 races (a "normal"
human, a fast but not that tough Opifex, eg see elves from D&D, a Smart but not strong at all Nanomage, EG, Erudites, and the Atrox which are big and brutish but not that smart) You then chose from one of 10 or so (can't remember right now) professions, such as the Enforcer (Warriors), the Soldier (Ranger or other high damage types) Martial Artist (Monk) Agent (Rogue/Sniper), Fixer (kinda like a Rogue, but with debuffs and teleports) Nano Technician (straight up nuker, even has root as a low level spell) Engineer (kinda like a mage with a robot pet) Bueracrat (Enchanter) and a few more that I can't remember right now.

Once you have your race and profession picked, you chose a side in the conflict on the planet. you can work for Omni--Tek (the evil corporation who is mining the planet for its resources) the clans (rebels, disgruntled omni workers, fighting the corporation for its freedom), or the Nuetrals. Each side has a starting city (two for the nuetrals, though you don't chose which one you start in, its based on current server loads) and multiple (identical) training yards to level up in early on.

It was a little hard to figure out the controls at first, but it eventually became rather intuitive (once the initial shock wore off)

Once you enter the world, you can open your inventory and equip your starting weapon (a solar powered assault rifle for the soldier), if you have any nanos (spells) in your inventory you can try and learn them, but you will probably get a message saying that you must increase your skill in certain fields before you can learn that program.

Skills in AO are very different from EQ, you don't learn by doing in this game. You have a certain number of IP (i think that stands for Increase Points) and each skill can cost a different amount of IP to raise (the higher your skill the more it costs per point to increase) All skills have a limit as to how much each can be raised. Class defining skills (assault rifle, heavy weapons, ranged combat initiative for the soldier) can usually be raised 5 points per level. Less specialized skills can be raised 4 or even 3 points per level AND they will generally cost more to raise then skills of your class. So while a soldier CAN become very adept at casting nanos, it will cost him alot of IP and in the long run may limit his growth in weapons skills.

Twinking is interesting in this game it seems, because each item (equipment/armour/implants/nanos) has a level of skill associated with it. for example in order to equip some armoured sleeves, I had to have an 18 in SENSE and a 17 in AGILITY so in other words, I couldn't equip them til I was around level 4.


If I can figure out how to take a screenshot, I will post a few.
The game is really quite beautiful in its landscapes and detailed in its models. It runs really well on my system which is rather crippled right now due to some memory going bad.

What really made me sit up and take notice though, was how overwhelmingly this game reminded me of early EQ. Not in its style of play or genre obviously, but in the fervor and utter devotion that the dev's have toward the game. They aren't the big corporation like Sony sitting back and soaking in profits (not yet anyway) and they are actively trying to make the game better day by day. It was really rather refreshing when within the first 10 mins of logging in my first char, I was personally greeted by a GM/GUIDE (called ARK's) who helped me to understand the basic operations of playing! Can you imagine that in EQ? It only happened on the first char, so I'm guessing its once per account flagged but the GM said that if I needed any thing else, I could send her a tell or just /petition if she wasn't on.

All in all it was a good experience.

Now, just so some of you don't think I am quitting EQ, this was a 7 day free trial that I activated. it runs out on Saturday, after which I will most likely never play it again. I just got tired of a few things playing Donnel, I didn't want to play an alt, but I wanted to play a computer game, and it was nice to see the perspective from another MMORPG.

Thanks for listening, like I said I will post some SS's when I can.

AO in my opinion was "the little game that could".

Like you mentioned; AO was pretty much the biggest most anticipated Sci-Fi genre MMORPG in a long time; and how it was built up in advertising and the world it was created in should have been the game Sci-Fi nuts ( including myself ) would have went to in droves.

Note that I used a lot of "should" and "could" statements. I got sucked into the game; tried and tried and spent forever in Rubi-Ka trying to convince myself "oh the game will work one day, it's just new". My first days in RK were awesome when I could move. Literally. I was a clan MA; hung out in the backyard for a while killing leets and birds and the occasional Omni...I mean for the first 6 levels of a game where you're basically trying to kill yourself every 5 minutes to learn how your character works...it was an excellent idea. Even when you died there..all you did was go sit near the reclaim booth and wait for your gear to be sent back.
Now; after level it was time to go run around in the outdoors. One of the most fun places there was the Beach where I went up to level 35 or so ( from level 6 )..learned more about the game, even met a few people who were as big of a Sci-Fi nut as myself, and we were actually "immersed" in the game. We started our own guild; went on some PK hunts for clan points and to talk smack amongst our felow clanners....but than something happened in the game.
I won't go on a personal tirade about how totally fuxx0red MA's are in AO ( who knew a lazer rifle or whatever were better than fists in a 24th century world )....but the whole mechanics on how borked some classes were in comparison to how another class were so much overpowered than the others and interdependancy soon became nonexistant really started to make people wonder just how the game was going.

You played UO. Remember the ganking tamers had in comparison to everyone else? That was AO. AO was one of the first games where levelling wasn't the sole reason to progress; you had the PK option which under level 150 was fun fun fun, after that the game just dried up. There was no where to go. GM-type events were pretty much the same as EQ ( either total rapefests or not worth going to )..the storylines and powers that be on both sides of RK were non existant except for the 10-12 solid RP guilds who kept the Omni-Clan figth alive...and people soon came to the point where it wasn't even worth logging onto.

I gotta admit though; on paper AO was, is, and probabally still could be the best damn game in the Sci-Fi genre ever released...but in the online universe where it existed it was the biggest piece of coding trash ever released. I know maybe i set my expectations too high. Fanboi maybe in it's early days? Probabally. Still; Funcom made a lot of great ideas ( travel on the Whom-Pa was to cool; vending machines with "cool" clothes; reclaim booths; apartments; storyline ) which made the powergamer just as happy as the occasional player/roleplayer just as happy...but the gameplay itself plus the individual skill systems and IP implementations and chaos it made if you spent your IP's wrong, and than the entire one-zone feel of the game which was probabally about 10 years too early to ever implement ( PC's still have a bit of trouble with the massive size of RK )....made it an exercise in futility for most if not all.

I Wanted AO to work. I wanted my release from EQ and lose myself in the genre that I enjoy more than anything ( i'm a big sci-fi nut; dragons and orcs bore me :D )....but AO's downfalls and problems just outshined anyhting it's bonuses ever could do tenfold.

I'm glad AO is fun for you though. It is a fun world to lose yourself in. I jus wished it worked enough to where I would still be in it with you; either PKing you or fighing along side ya.
