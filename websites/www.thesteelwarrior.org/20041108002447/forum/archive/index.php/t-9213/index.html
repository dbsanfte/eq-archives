czai
Keep levelling.

Gather your friends and do PoJ trials (for the free AA).

Exp in the PoS desert and gather the medallions you need for the PoTactics alternative flagging quest. This gives you a 2nd free AA.

Lead a BoT mini raid and flag yourself for the zone. 3rd free AA. Back in the day, I tanked the minis with velious gear and 4 clerics, I maybe had 6k hp. Can be done now with 1-2 clerics on me, adjust your numbers as needed.

Also, if you can get in on anymore flagging raids (Grummus, TT, etc) those are also free AA's.

If you're really wanting AA's, stop at 60 where you can go solo/duo in Velks for decent AA xp when you can't get a group.

If you have friends who are compatible with you, hit up PoTactics pit and crank out the XP. 2% per AA is not a lot in PoV, you'll get that when you're 60 too. In the Tactics pit you can get about 3% per kill.

Good luck.

The prices of many of those items have plummeted after the release of OoW. The earrings are now going for less than 10k, you can find some damn nice armguards of repentance for relatively cheap, less than 20k on drinal, and there are a couple pairs of nice gaunts that drop in OoW zones that are going for nice prices in the Bazaar.

I have found shoulders to be one of the hardest slots to fill for warriors. You could always do the Strength of the Elements quest but the AC on them is bleh. I prefer Horn Spiked Shoulder Plates but they're spendy if you can even find them for sale in the bazaar. As for your back, I just bought a new Tainted Clock of Treachery for 20k. They're going for 40k now, but maybe the initial flood of them dropped the price.


As for the levels..... it's a really sticky situation. It's easy to say that levels are better than AA's, because they are. But If you are anything other than a cleric, trying to get a group past 65+ without a title is gonna be a bitch. Personally I stopped a bit at 62 and ground out 18 AA's just so I could get a veteran title. It was really easy because I was getting 1-2 AA's per night in PoV. After that it was never too hard, because once they see you have some defensive AA's under your belt and you've been around a bit more, groups will be more willing to give you a shot. Good luck with your decision...... being a new warrior is never easy.
