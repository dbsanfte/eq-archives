As everyone suspects, you are getting splattered when Velk runs out of mana. Once he goes oom, he is a beast. He hits harder than Vindi and will out DPS Vindi if he is not casting. Worst part is, he doesn't tell you he's lom so you don't find out until he rapes you like a prison inmate. After doing Velk doezens of times to get robes for our wizards, these are the simple rules we came up with for Velk:

1. DO NOT mana tapping him, the longer he has mana, the better.

2. DO bring lots of wizards. Giant Bane is almost no agro so they can easily chain cast without fear of agro if they cast from max range. I have personally done over 60k damage on Velk in a 3 group kill.

3. DO NOT fight him in the throne room. If you get him on the throne - which will always happen in there, you lose a ton of DPS to cannot see messages and he will summon people into the throne and squash them. Fight out in the hall somewhere.

4. DO NOT start the fight with defensive. He casts a lot early in the fight. Hit defensive when he gets 50-60% so if he does go oom you don't get splattered from the sudden increase in DPS.

Something I tried last time was this hotkey: Never, Never, Never, Never, count on a hotkey with a /pause to do anything important. Never. I can't stress this enough. The problem is that /pause is directly effected by the amount of lag you are currently experiencing. Say you mash that hotkey on inc, then the mob gets turned around and you are now facing the raid, lagging your machine or you get a big lag spike. If there is a 5% difference in the time it takes /pause to fire off the next line of text warning the clerics and SA that defensive is wearing off, that's a 10 second difference. Plenty of time for you to get smashed and the mob to go wild on the casters because the SA was not expecting agro for another 10 seconds.

Something else to consider - the MT is the single most important person on most raids. It is your job to position the mob, keep it in position, keep agro, and make sure you are the only one taking damage. There is no way that you can really pay 100% attention to the mob if you are boxing a cleric, particularly if you are boxing on the same machine. You want to break that habit now before you get too dependent on your cleric because the tougher the mob, the less effective you will be when it counts.
