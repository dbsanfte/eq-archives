aspit
Which is better in regard to shielding or avoidance really depends on your own personal parses. If you have ever taken an economics course envision the supply demand curve as a shielding/avoidance curve. Where at one end of the spectrum there is a mob that avoidance dominates, and on the other end shielding dominates. At the center is a breakeven point, which for quarm as i posted before is about 10 avoidance = 4 shielding. You would have to do this for each individual mob since each mob will have its own seperate curve based on innate hit rate ( which should be fairly constant, i havent parsed any god mobs yet but i can only assume), and mobs damage bonus. Mobs with a higher damage bonus make shielding relatively more effective, as the effect cuts a higher amount based on raw damage off of what you are taking. A mob with a relatively low db would make avoidance far more desireable as the shielding effect does less and less as the mobs db shrinks. Again its very easy to calculate a break even for avoidance and shielding based on a parse of a mob. All you need is a damage bonus, an average hit, and a hit%. Also remember that any avoidance lessens the benefit of shielding, as you are getting hit less, so remember to take that into consideration.

Edit: You could make a chart with shielding and avoidance on the y axis and mob damage bonus on the x axis. Obviously this would ignore the effect of higher hit rate but it would give you a rough estimate of which is more effective vs a certain mob. You could then do the base calculations for each values and connect the dots forming 2 overlayed curves.
