Friedrick
In my opinion, the worst possible place for a second tank to be is 2nd on the aggro list for a hard hitting boss mob (up to elemental level, no experience with anything higher). The reasoning behind this is that your main tank just died with a heal wheel on them. If the mob turns immediately on the second tank before they can hit furious/defensive/evasive and aetaunt (in that order), they will most likely be a stain along side the MT. You need time to /disc and get the heals coming in and I have watched tanks in my guild die because they got aggro before they and the healers could react.

I usually keep my aggro weapons in on raids for boss mobs, BUT I cycle attacks like crazy. With quad procs you have to or the RNG will put you too high at some point. Tank transitions are fairly trivial with AEtaunt and Furious anymore except for the healers imo. There is no reason that you have to be that high on the aggro list with aetaunt. I see from your Magelo that you have it and I would assume any tank in a raiding guild would have it as well.

It may seem cruel and heartless to purposely watch a guild mate die, but to save a raid it can be a necessity. There is nothing more frustrating to see the healers all "healing Target, healer_02 next" cause by the time they targetted who had aggro and tried to cast they were dead. Heck, I have offered to be a sacrificial tank before to my guild to grab aggro and die so that they healing could switch to the true second tank and buy them precious seconds. I would rather get rez'd in after a win than CR with the raid. The healers /veto'd my idea though.

Anyway, just my thoughts on it, so take it for what it is worth.
