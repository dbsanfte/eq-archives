I have been reading up on this new technology, and it sounds very promising. It uses the 110V power outlets already in your home to create a network. Right now, it can get around 14 mbps, 3 mbps faster than 802.11b WiFi. Still alot slower than wired, but with no added hassle of wiring. This seems like a great option for those of us who are struggling with the range of our wireless networks and do not want to tear the walls apart having wires run through.

I have heard of this system, but have no experience with it. My guess is that you would see a lot of packet loss with it. Power wires typically cause a lot of interference with data lines (I have had to travel to sites to fix industrial equipment when the only problem was that someone replaced a data line and ran it in a trough alongside a power cable).

With that said, it is possible that since in this case the power line is carrying the data signal and it isn't just an external influence, that there is no/negligable distortion, or they have a decent solution for cancelling it out.

Best case is find a place that has a good return policy and try it out. If it stinks, you should be able to see it immediately.

Sweden is one of the countries furthest ahead on this field and the biggest problem seems to be the synchronization between power-frequens and data-frequens.
The synchronization is quite hard to do and require some expensive gear, which is why you would probably not see it as a homeLAN anytime soon.
Sweden tried to use it as an ISP solution and are still trying doing research in the area to improve the technology to a reliable and up-to-date working state.
