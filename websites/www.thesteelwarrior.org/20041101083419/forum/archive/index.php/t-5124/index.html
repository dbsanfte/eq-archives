Resists are irrelevant. Aggro is the same whether it is resisted or not. You have mentioned resists as being a bad thing several times now, so I think you are not clear on this.

paladiin
Ah, think I figured it out (the agro thing that is).

a) You're hunting in either non-pop zones, or Tier1 PoP zones. Holding agro in these zones is almost entirely different than that challenges you will soon face in PoS/PoV/CoD. At 55, if I remember right, you are now able to enter without a flag. In these zones, getting mobs slowed immediately is not a huge issue. The hardest you're going to get hit is upper 200's.

b) You are hunting with mostly burst DPS classes, not beastlords, monks, rogues. With the exception of the paladin (who's not likely going to get agro unless they are stunning) and the Ranger (who has more agro potential but at 55 isn't going to be a huge agro magnet unless they really want to be), you're fighting with DPS who come into play later in the fight. I'm sure the DPS casters are not nuking until you've already got a huge solid lead in agro. Melee DPS cannot afford to wait until a mob is at 75% before engaging.

c) Your support class groupmates (61 cleric and 65 enchanter) are overpowered for what you are hunting. The 61 cleric can easily keep you alive in non-PoP and Tier1 PoP with low-agro HoT spells while you generate enough agro to let the 65 enchanter slow. Also, it's highly unlikely your chanter is getting many resists on slows or Tashes.

In all honesty, with a group like that, hunting where you are, you could probably hold agro with a pair of SSoY's and still be just fine. It's no surprise your DPS weapons are handling the situation so well.

Since your group can all get into PoV/PoS now, I'm guessing it won't be long until your group is moving up a tier for xp. Tier1 XP really starts to fall off at mid-50's. When you get there, you'll be looking at xp mobs that hit 400's and 500's. It's here where I really started to hate getting riposted - there's nothing like that extra burst damage when you're sitting at 20% to make your day. It was then that I bagged my dual wield setup for a 2H solution, and it wasn't until I hit 65 that I even attempted to use dual wield again.

They'll also have more hit points. That means if you are using pure DPS for agro, you'll have to do even more damage than you are doing now so the mob doesn't bolt after the slower when slow lands. That means you're looking at taking damage longer, putting more pressure on the cleric, which means more agro for them too. Nukers will start engaging earlier too (earlier in terms of mob hp %). You might also be hunting with DPS melee in their upper 50's to 65, who are going to make your DPS look like rusty daggers.

When you get to this point (keep in mind this point is today for you - you're just a zone in away from this scenario), you will quickly notice your current preferred weapon setup is going to be very shakey agro.

Also, don't forget about LDoN. I'm guessing once you're 58 your group will be doing plenty of LDoN work. The DPS weapons aren't going to cut it. I have a GSo4W which is downright awesome DPS and I can't use it safely in LDoN to tank (though I can in PoV). I have to use my agro weapons, and hope for early procs. The time limit in LDoN really pushes people to get their DPS in early and you need to be able to cope with that.

The game's going to take a few giant steps in just a few levels for you. Be prepared.

In regards to the resists, it's in reference to agro generated by resisted stuns and immune to stuns. 6dps in a 30 second LDoN fight is 180 damage, or less than one overall round of combat of the fight. In all honesty, my CHoS could proc happy flowers for all I cared, as long as I got the stun agro for the proc. They DD from the CHoS is icing - it's nice, but it's not the reason I use it. The agro generated from the DD is really fairly insignificant overall.

As for the haste, depends what your current haste is. If you're at 31% already, you might be able to wait. CoF is nice, and CoM is nice too. Keep in mind that if you hunt with that 65 chanter a lot, you're going to be at or very near your max haste anyway. Personally I'd wait until you're doing higher tier PoP and LDoN and see what you need more, agro through haste or agro through weapon selection.
