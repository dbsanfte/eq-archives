Sassinak
On another thread, someone described him as a BoT Kriger with an AE. That is pretty accurate. There are three main ways to fail this encounter: 1) you get early aggro on Seru and/or fail to position him correctly, 2) you aggro too many adds at some point and get overwhelmed, 3) you lack sufficient healing to run the battle indefinitely.

The healing situation is fairly straight forward. Bring enough healing mana to keep your tank alive against a BoT kriger indefinitely, and some extra healing to account for occasional heals to everyone else.

Positioning is solved by patience. There will be a large number of trash mobs to clear around Seru's perimeter, which take ages to kill; you might even run into respawn issues; patience is a virtue for this. Eventually you will have an opportunity to enter Seru's chamber without trash aggro. Position Seru inside the main room, against a pillar around the wall. If he's 2 pillars away from the corner of the room, then I believe the healers in the corner won't get hit by AE, and the roamers outside the door won't assist.

Adds arise when people are poorly disciplined about staying away from doors, or if Seru ping pongs due to poor tank aggro (Seru's stun can make aggro nontrivial). This again comes down to patience, making aggro control and survival of all raid members the top priority, rather than trying to burn him fast. The tank doesn't need bane weapons, he should use his best aggro weapons instead, because he needs the aggro.

With the patience and positioning taken care of, Seru is practically a free source of terrific loot.

Balfamor
Here is my old raid template for LIS:

Meet up at the enterance to Sanctus Seru (near DSP zone), form groups, and buff up. Then, the first group will teleport into the Arx and dispatch of the first few guards around the port in area. After that, the key holder will gate back to the enterance of the city (bound there), get invited by the next group and port them into the Arx with the rest of the raid party - Rinsing and repeating until all of the raid is inside the Arx. We will then make or way upstairs and to the maze enterance, killing whatever guards get in our way. The Praetorian guards within the Arx are pretty tough and hit hard, but they can be slowed and mezzed. Then we will make our way, single-file, through the maze (no mobs).

=> Map of the Arx Maze (http://www.thekreator.com/graphics/arx_map.gif)

At the end of the maze is a teleporter pad which will take us into Seru's inner sanctum. The sanctum is basically a large square room with a door on each wall, and a hallway that circles the room. We teleport into one of the corners of the hallway where there are roaming and static guards. Seru himself is in the center room along with a guard posted at each door. We will kill the guards in the hall and one of the door guards from the room, and then head to a corner of the room without aggroing Lord Seru, who is in the center of the room.

=> Diagram of Inner Sacntum (http://www.thekreator.com/graphics/seru_sanctum.gif)

Once we are situated in a corner of the room, we will be free of agro and can buff up again, load spells, and prepare to engage Lord Seru. The room is lined with pillars along the walls, which will be a key strategy to the fight. As the diagram indicates, the MT will back into a pillar with Seru facing him and away from the rest of the raid with other melee attacking Seru's back. Behind the next pillar (to avoid AE) will be the healers and non "line of sight" casters, and behind the next pillar (to avoid AE) will be the nukers and other "line of sight" casters. When the fight begins, it is necessary for the MT to establish strong agro before anyone else engages, so no sitting, nuking, attacking, etc. until called for by the MT. Once the raid does engage Seru, it is just an endurance fight to control agro and preserve mana while pecking away at Seru's HP.

Some Info on Lord Inquisitor Seru:

=> He has near 1,000,000 HP! Once engaged, it is not uncommon for it to take anywhere from 45 minutes to an hour of pure combat to kill him. This is why it is very important to conserve mana throughout the fight.

=> He hits pretty hard (in the 500-700's) and can flurry, so we will need a solid CH chain with spot healers. Luckily he can be slowed though, and because of the length of the fight, he will need to be reslowed/debuffed as they wear off.

=> He has an ability called Stunning Strike (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/spell.html?spell=1968), which stuns his target for 20 seconds! It can be resisted with a high MR, but the MT will find himself stunned through most of the fight, and if this stun lands early on before strong agro is established, it can be bad news for the raid.

=> He has another ability called Torturing Winds (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/spell.html?spell=2061), which is an unresistable non-directional AE curse that if lands on you does 300 damage every tick until it is cured or you die. This is why we have as many people as possible hiding behind the pillars to avoid it. It can be removed with Remove Greater Curse (single target) and with Radiant Cure I (group), so if we have enough healers with these abilities, then we can have more melee with bane weapons engaged.

=> He is completely magic resistant, and can only be harmed by melee with Seru Bane Weapons (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/quest.html?quest=1997) and casters with fire, poison, and disease based spells. If melee do not have bane weapons, or if we do not have enough healers that are able to cure his curse on them, then melee other than the MT will hang back with the casters to avoid the AE and sit tight or help with potential adds while Seru is engaged. A damage shield on the MT and pets that can proc some damage are also helpful at slowly eating away at his HP.
