Conclusion - Since Paladins always have at a minimum their self-buffs, the actual Warrior HP advantage over a simularly equipped Paladin is either very modest or non-existant in most groups.

In order to have even a very modest HP advantage, Warriors must purchase Virtue and Brells before grouping (and not die) or only join groups containing a Cleric and a Paladin.

In ALL other cases (think LDoN pickup groups) a Warrior HP advantage does not for all practical purposes exit.

I was reading a thread on another board when suddenly it occured to me....

What happens to the Warrior/Paladin HP gap as a result of Paladin buffs? (This is not going to be a perfect analysis since I'm tired and don't feel like taking into account Strength of Tunare).

For purposes of this discussion, let's assume a simularly equipped Warrior and Paladin with the Warrior having an 800 HP advantage.

When grouping with a Cleric, a Warrior has a 450 HP advantage over a Paladin grouped with a Cleric if and only if the Warrior has BSS (and I've never heard BSS buffing advertised or being asked for in /ooc in PoK) or has a Paladin in his group [800 HP - Aura of the Crusader (350 HP Paladin self buff)].

If a Warrior is in a group with a Paladin then the Warrior HP advantage is 450 HP's [(800 HP - Aura of the Crusader (350 HP Paladin self buff)].

-- Edited to make clearer --

- When a Warrior is grouped with a Druid of a Shaman and does not have Virtue or BSS he has a HP disadvanrave compared to a Paladin in the same group

In a group with a Druid as the main healer, no Paladin or Cleric and the Warrior not having Paladin or Cleric buffs -

The Warrior gets 743 HP's [Po9 (618) and maybe Strength of Tunare (125)]

A Paladin gets 1805 HP's [Heroism (425), Symbol of Marzin (700), Brell's Stalwart Shield (330), Aura of the Crusader (350)]

Making the Warrior HP (dis)advantage -232 HP's (800-1032)

When a Shaman is the main healer, the results are simular.

Even if the Warrior has Virtue or is grouped with a Cleric, he has a maximum 450 HP advantage and only a 120 HP advantage if he's not buffed with BSS vs a Paladin buffed with Virtue, etc.

In order to maintain even a paltry 450 HP advantage over a simularly equipped Paladin -

A Warrior must either group with a Paladin or purchase Virtue (if a Cleric is not in the group) and BSS before joining a group that doesn't contain a Paladin.

Is my math and logic totally wrong or is the Warrior HP advantage largely non-existant in most groups?

Maybe not as situational as you might think. I was thinking about this earlier and thought about it. Boredom struck me, I decided I wanted to see the actual natural hp difference between the two classes.

I loaded up magelo and created a 65 human warrior and a 65 human paladin. I gave them matching equipment, including a BoW (even tho I know a paladin wouldn't normally be using it, it's the best weapon I could think of that both could use. I punched in their respective cultural armor for the armor slots, and the difference in hp was about 750hp. Okay, not that bad, considering the warrior cultural probably has more hp considering it's Rallos Zek armor.

Then I wanted to see the differenc using the high end LDoN armor, to see what effect being plate class/all would have. so I loaded the Royal Attendant armor of Sand into both. The results were rather...intriguing.

http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=823956
The Paladin

http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=823951
The Warrior

I used BoW and solstice earring for purely keeping their equipment identical...the dream profile for the warrior has a couple equipment different. I emphasized stamina equipment, as that's partly(or mostly) where the hp difference lies. I realize most of this equipment isn't going to be normal for a warrior/paladin, but realize that shortcomings can be made up with augments (this IS LDoN armor, after all). The paladin only has 143wis, so he'd probably make a couple of decisions differently unless he made up the wis with augments.

With identical armor, and 241sta (equal starting stamina too)...unbuffed, there's only a 470hp difference. Changing the FT1 earring it becomes 500hp. Sure, with stamina buffs or slightly better stamina differences it might be as high as 550 or even 600, or even slightly higher with Natural Durability.

I know ornate probably gives more, and I'll work out the ornate difference later, but since LdoN points are so easy to farm at 65, I'd be surprised if either the warrior or the paladin did not have at least LDoN equipment.

The only "provided" requirements to this I can see if that it requires the paladin to have all their spells (mainly the 64 self buff and the 65 hybrid buff) and it requires both people to have the Royal Attendant equipment. This is intended for the casual gamer, the people who do not have the time to go raiding for elemental access/gear all week. The people who use more bazaar-bought equipment.

Not a pretty picture. I realize that warriors and paladins might do the non-visible gear differently, but I wanted to see how big the hp difference was when the gear equation was taken as much out of the equation as possible.
