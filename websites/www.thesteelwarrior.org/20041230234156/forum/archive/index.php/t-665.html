Wubao + PoN = my own personal nightmare. It's the one zone I actually get a lot of groups in.

Heh.

So let me assuage your fears right now ...

Aggro is PING PONGY in there.

It just is.

I've pulled aggro off of BoC wielding warriors in there before. Quite by accident mind you, but it's happened.

Uh, things go remind your group about are:

1) Proximity Aggro. Sometimes the mobs are small (nettling wraiths or blood ravens come to mind) or their hit boxes are small (the crocs for instance). So you end up standing on the mob's toes and accidentally pulling aggro off of the tank. This is something that people somehow seemed to FORGET about between level 20 in Highpass and level 62+ and they tend to react like it's the tank's fault. It's funny too, because you never see people doing this in Temple of Veeshan, where it's almost second nature to stay at max melee range. But put them in PoN at the docks and pull a croc and suddenly there's people all up on the mob's HEAD trying to get inside the mob for some odd reason.

Another thing to remember about proximity aggro is these fights may take awhile or certain spells with knockback components may be involved. THUS, there's a lot of PUSH going on. Like say when I fight Fiendish Consorts ... the spider gets pushed around a lot. And so proximity aggro sometimes rears its head in the middle of a fight because the spider's been pushed up, then down the hill we're fighting it on.

2) Casters need horses. Sitting will get AGGRO ... a lot.

3) Clerics HAVE mana pools. Don't be afraid to use them. What I mean by that is, SLOW, if it lands too early will usually get aggro, and then you use mana healing the slower AND the tank. It's just easier to wait, fight the mob UNSLOWED longer and NOT have to heal two people. BUT, some clerics get wonky and complain about having to use TWO CH's instead of one. Or 3 CH's instead of two. They forget how much extra mana they're having to use keeping the monk, the rogue, the shaman AND the TANK upright.

People come here for EASY XP. And sometimes when they have to actually WORK for their XP they get all in a huff and blame the tank.

It's kind of funny to watch sometimes, but sad in a way too.
