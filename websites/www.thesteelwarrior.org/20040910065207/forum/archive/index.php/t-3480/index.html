Gonaden
We all know that Pallys and SKs are much better at aggro control than warriors, but of the two, which one is actually the best?

All the time the class balance issue is put in such terms as "warriors versus knights" or "warriors versus pallys/SKs", but I have a suspicion that SKs might be basking in the reflected glory of the superior aggro-control of Paladins in order to boost their own credibility as xp tanks and thus gain groups easier.

From my experience of grouping with both classes, Paladins with their stun spells are head and shoulders above the SK line of aggro spells, but what do you all think?

Are SKs not as good as Pallys? If so, how much better are Pallys? And how much better are SKs than warriors really?

Shadowfrost
I think if you took an equally-geared paladin and SK and they had an aggro competition to see who could taunt off of whom, it would be a close run thing. The paladin would probably win eventually, but it would be closer than you think.

I also think that a competently-played shadowknight can hold aggro even if you have a slower who slows on inc and a group full of chain-nuking casters. In other words, a group won't notice the difference in aggro holding; they're functionally equivalent.

To the cleric or druid or enchanter who's making a group and picking a tank, either knight class has the desired effect. They both guarantee that the casters won't be thumped.

I think that shadowknights irritate warriors a bit less than paladins do, when you get warrior and knight in the same group. Paladins buff the warrior's hp but shadowknights buff the warrior's aggro, so an intelligent warrior prefers the SK. Besides, while both classes create a lot of aggro just from doing their jobs, the shadowknight can lose it again while the paladin cannot.

Thinking casters tend to prefer shadowknights over paladins as well, because the shadowknight doubles as an FD puller and snarer, does noticeably more damage than a paladin, and also contributes a significant amount of mana regeneration (equivalent to FT10, roughly).

65 SK's are relatively rare.
