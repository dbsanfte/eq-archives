If the server economy is borked, then doing a couple of the crafted quests will make sense to look into (that is, get the drops & buy the gems from vendors).
If you do check out crafted, you'll be in South K. You can solo at the spires around splitpaw. Gnoll hide lariets, which drop off the gnolls there, are an underrated aggro weapon, especially once your dex gets up a bit. Gnolls also drop a mask that is a light source and might be something you want to keep for a CR run, and a +CHA neck item. If you are selling lots of loot over time to vendors, then do yourself a favor and bump your CHA before you sell (make friends with a shaman, because they have +CHA spells). It's a small thing, but everything helps.
See if you can find some tradeskillers around. You can farm spider silk (tailors), and spiderling silk (smiths) and low quality, high quality and medium quality hides from EK lions or from rockhoppers (smiths again, also some tailors). You can either sell these in the bazaar or trade them directly to a tradeskiller who is skilling up on armor that would be an upgrade for you (fine plate would be an upgrade, heh).
The idea of learning what stuff is needed by tradeskillers is a good one.
The Centi weapon you have will work for a while on that server, I think.
There are some warrior only weapons that might be a bit cheaper than general weapons; though I know nothing about your server's economy, you might check them out in the bazaar: Jarsath Trident (11/22 primary/secondary piercing, STR 5, DEX-5, STA 5, AGI -5). The combination of negative stats, piercing, and (I think) warrior-only on that weapon tends to reduce price. But you'd be interested in the damage/delay aspect. It would make a good primary weapon and you could put your Centi in the offhand & bank the Lightcrawler thingy for when you get big and want to show newbies what you had to use back in the day. Also the Sarnak battlehammer (2HB, 30/40). Long ago it held aggro surprisingly well for me at your level.
You might research the Goranga spiked club (12/23 1HB primary only, AC +5, STR +2). YOu can't get this yourself, but it's a no-drop item in Maiden's Eye that sometimes just rots on mobs near the zoneline. You *might* get one for free if you ask politely to loot any extra, but be careful getting there and be careful not to get aggro or you'll be making a long CR. If you can't do it now, keep it in mind for later.
Finally, go to EQ traders and research fletching. You'll be able to make usable arrows and a bow in a relatively short time for not much cash. You don't have to pursue fletching in the long run, but minimal effort will at least get you a bow and arrow set up suitable for pulling/tagging mobs, and every little bit helps.

Do not vendor any blue diamonds you come across. They are a component in cultural armor and players will pay you far more than a vendor.
Also, if you do go to the Beholder's (the Gorge off EK) and kill evil eyes, you might try selling the ivory to players rather than vendoring it. Ivory is a component of Tribunal cultural armor and as far as I know (bleh, haven't really checked lately) it is not sold by any vendors. Not as much demand for it as blue diamonds, but you're much more likely to come across it, and any smith who needs the ivory...well, needs the ivory.
If you go to Ocean of Tears, bring a fishing rod. Fish while you med up (assuming you'll need to med). There's a component of human cultural smithing that can be fished there. Not a big demand for it, but you never know.
If you go to Everfrost, do not vendor any of the mammoth meat. It's a component of Halas 10-pound meat pies, and is not regularly stocked by vendors. The meat pies, over time, have become an uncommon method of skilling up in baking, but some bakers still make them. You won't get lots of plat for the meat, but it belongs to a specific category of tradeskill items that it's hand to have around incase you meet a tradeskill addict. If you see a baker looking for these things, give them to the baker for free. It's a very good way to meet characters who are far, far richer than you are, and believe me, that can be handy.
