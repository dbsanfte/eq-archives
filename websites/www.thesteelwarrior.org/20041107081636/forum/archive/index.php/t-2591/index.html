Right. If you consider the various healing abilities of the priest classes it might by something like this, on a scale of 1 to 10.

Cleric: 10
Druid: 7
Shaman: 6

Now if once you factor in slow and KEI it might look something like this.

Cleric: 10+
Druid: 8
Shaman: 7

And if the game is designed in such a way that you need a healing value of 8 or better just to get by, then clearly the Clerics fit that bill nicely and a Druid can, under the right circumstances, but a Shaman would have a hard time. Which is pretty much the current situation. However if there ever comes a time where the value of healing required drops down to say a level 6, then Clerics will be overpowered in healing for the environment. People won't need all the healing or even a substantial part of the healing a Cleric can produce. Druids and Shamans would be able to replace a Cleric just fine. And since a Cleric doesn't bring a whole lot to the situation other than more healing, their desirability as specialists will be lower, since a specialist isn't required, and more adaptable classes would become more desired.

The current situation with Knights and Warriors tanking is like this.

Warrior: 10
Knight: 8

Except that the environment really needs an 11 or 12 to tank without slow. Once slow is factored in, only about a 7 is needed to tank acceptably. Which means that the Knight tanks well enough while the Warrior tanks better. But the additional tanking of the Warrior really isn't required. And given the fact that his complete specialization isn't necessary once slow is factored in and isn't enough when it isn't, there really is no reason to take a Warrior over a Knight given the extra things a Knight can do, unless you just want a bigger tanking buffer.

However, once you consider aggro control differences you begin to realize that even with the larger tanking buffer the Warrior represents, he's still more of a liability than the Knight because he reduces the effectiveness of everyone else in the group while not bringing anything you "have" to have.
