Monks have a different damage table than warriors. Even with TM3 and Flurry etc, I don't think you'll be able to consistantly outdamage a monk from the front.

2h: Specially considering you might have a 60/38 (1.58), 45/30 (1.5), 35/23 (1.52), or 48/32 (1.5) weapon. But the Monk will be using something like 42/28 (1.5), 54/30 (1.8), 35/20 (1.75), 60/38 (1.58), etc.

1h: You might have a 20/20 (1), 29/30 (.97), 38/35 (1.09). The monk will have a 26/24 (1.08), 23/20 (1.15), 20/19 (1.05), etc.

So you'd need both a DBotW and EoE (or a Hammer of Timeweavers) to come close to a monk w/ a Shinai and Hammer of Timeweavers.

And the 2h story is even worse, as we share the VDD w/ Pal/SK (and I can't imagine, but some guild might give one to even a monk). Still the VDD is far behind the Firey Staff of Zha and Staff of Transcendence.

That's not including the fact that the monk DD procs are generally higher than warriors (by 50+ damage).

"do super-high attack values really totally negate the lack of decent damage bonus?"

Yes, it makes a big difference. Zha's is really useful at that level of the game, with those levels of ATK.

But for monks not at that level of the game, Caen's or Rune Etched Bamboo Bo still have a slight edge over the Zha.

In any event, monks get Return Kick and Double Riposte so IF monks could stand in front of mobs again (which in LDoN they can), AND those monks bother to train those AAs (a really big if, since most monks rate them VERY low on the list), then their DPS from the front gets AA boosts too. Just not flurry and Tactical Mastery. But the damage table difference is still there.

/shrug
