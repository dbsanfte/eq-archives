Dakkin
1) Yes, in my experience low health and sit hate are temporary to at least some extent.

You can't taunt off a low health person at ALL if they've got solid taunt (i.e. if the mob follows them when they move), but if you pound the taunt button and they subsequently get healed you don't seem to get a massive hate boost. At least that I've seen.

One solution but only for REAL emergencies is to carry a few root nets around with you. Keep one in a main inventory slot patched through to a hotbutton, and then if the cleric gets low health aggro pop a root and hug the mob. Root proximity aggro will let you get taunt off anything except a pet. It's temporary though, wears off when the root breaks.


2) Pet hate is tweaked so that the mob ONLY notices it if no PCs are in melee range.

A pet with taunt on may be high enough on the hate list to out taunt casters and get taunt if you move out of melee range, but you'll only notice it when you try to move the mob. The instant you back out of melee range taunt shifts to the pet since there's now no PC in melee range of the mob.


3) Yes, sadly mage pets often get and hold aggro much better than the average warrior. They taunt, do surprising damage and often cast good hate inducing spells. They just can't taunt off you when you're in melee range though.


4) If you have better hp / AC than the knight, then yes it can be more efficient to taunt off them, or (with an SK) get them to hold aggro through slows then FD a little later if you haven't successfully taunted off them.

Unfortunately, they can not only gain snap aggro at the start, but keep piling on huge aggro-inducing snare or stun spells right through the fight for much more hate than you.

So if they have anything remotely near your hps and AC, unless your damage dealers are good monks who can guarantee max damage with zero aggro it's often more efficient to let the knight tank so everyone else can do maximum nuke/dot/melee damage.

I hope this helps - very interesting thought-provoking questions there. It often surprises me how technical tanking is, and how much I still have to learn as I go along :)

Beastlords have a line of buffs that add a proc to their pet (also adds 75dex as well), these procs not only do pretty mean amount of damage, but many of these procs carry a +hate modifier, allowing the pet to hold agro through the BSTs slows.

Easy way to gain agro...let the pet attack while only the warrior is in range. The pet can't taunt off the warrior while he's close, and it "helps" you gain agro, since it's holding agro over the slow also (as well as doing damage). I'm surprised more people don't do this...

4) This is also known as "piggybacking"...many SKs use this method since we can lose the agro after warriors have taunted off, allowing us to use our spells again to do damage without fear of over-agroing. Don't know about paladins tho.
