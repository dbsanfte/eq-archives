I spent a ton of time in Seb. I will concur that mobs there run, and when they run, they run fast, and they bring back lots of friends. I don't think you can single pull at 55-56, if I recall correctly you can start sometimes getting singles at 57-58. But you should be able to handle two mobs, which is what you should get on all the entrance pulls.

The biggest problem you will have is dealing with runners, root is only marginally effective. If you can spend some plat on a silken whip of ensnaring. I use one in situations like that and in mainhand it usually procs pretty early, so I can swap it out for a better weapon, and snare lasts a good while. Kunzar Ku'juch is also ok for this, but dark doesn't last nearly as long and the ratio isn't as good, but it's cheaper, not sure of the proc rate on it. Of course with either there are going to be mobs you just don't get a proc on, but if you have either one you will be much better off. Sure your dps will be lower, but the most important thing in Seb is controlling runners.

Lots of mobs see invis, you won't be able to run to exit with invis, you will get seen. However, make sure your war has max hps, toss him a celestial heal, gate your cleric, and run for the exit, you should make it without a problem if you know the way. Or buy a DA idol before you go and use a charge of it to make for exit. Make sure no one is camping exit, as that is a somewhat commonly camped area due to the ease of escape if things go bad. Also, if you have an invis potion it will help because you won't draw aggro right off the bat, you should make it a ways at least.

If memory serves, all knights and bugs see invis.

Cleric lvl 34 pacify, or 55ish wake of tranquility, should be huge here for getting single pulls. Sit the warrior down and use the cleric for pulling. Pull with annull to ensure that the mob doesn't have SOW. Cleric stuns are probably your best way of stopping runners. A snare whip is useful but not reliable. Remember your cleric has bash too, which doubles your opportunities to interrupt casters and stun runners.

In case you haven't figured it out yet, your cleric is your primary character in this duo when you head into dungeons. Your warrior is just a DPS/taunt pet. Use all the tools that your cleric has at its disposal: MOR, hammer pet, protection of vie if you have it. At 56 you will get your first proccing hammer and good yaulp, and this combo will add a lot of DPS to your duo. At 56 your cleric's total DPS may nearly match the DPS of your warrior. Don't just sit the cleric in the corner like a bot :)
