Klozz
Both zones have some faulty aggro issues. The more difficult mobs (hit harder, more hps) definitely reside in Veksar. Chardok is difficult to navigate without a rogue, as you need certain keys to get into the various wings. Chardok is almost a progression type of situation, as there is only wing you are able to access at first (without a rogue that is). The key that can be obtained in that wing allows you to access the next wing, and basically each wing allows you to access the next wing until you finally get access to all four wings.

Both zones are very much worth exploring. However, if your server is anything like mine, both are very empty, and will require you to bring your own group. CR's are also, potentially, very messy in both zones.

Lathen
If all you want from Veksar is the soulscream belt, then I'd say Veks is "easier" than Chardok. Both have their challenges.

The camp where you find the soulscream belt is easy(ish) to get to with a single group whose members are in the mid-50's. Once there, you need to be able to handle a camp with fewer than 7 static spawns and no roamers. If your group can suppress the urge to "scout", then you get to control the amount of danger you handle.

I don't really have proof of this, but Chardok just feels harder to me. I admit freely that my experience in Chardok is tainted by a Royals raid I led earlier this week. We wiped the raid twice (I hate the queen -- she STILL has my epic drop) and spent more hours in the zone than I'm willing to admit (though we DID get our cleric and enchanter fixed up for epic drops).
