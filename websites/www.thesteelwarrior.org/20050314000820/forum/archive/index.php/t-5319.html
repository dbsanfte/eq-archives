I have done about 90 hard adventures all with the same group: Pal, Sk, Rog, Rng, Clr, Enc. Our gear is mostly LDoN and high end bazaar, and the average aa of the group is about 300.


I would say Ruj, Mistmoore, and Miragul's are all of comparable difficulty. Miragul's seems to have more spots with 3+ pulls, but in general seems easier than the others. If you can do Ruk okay, then Mistmoore or Miragul's shouldn't pose a problem.

On the other hand, Guk and Tak are considerably harder. Guk just has annoying monsters, bad pathing, warping mobs, and 3-4 pulls all over the place. Tak is hit and miss. Sometimes you get a fairly straight forward map with sparse mobs and you can just tear through it. Other times, you get small corridors which lead to rooms with 4+ mobs that when you pull, aggro the 3 mobs in the neighboring room and you have a mess. In my experiences Tak has been the hardest for high risk.

Originally posted by Besrikarle
Rujark annoys me because I find them to just be harder mobs in general. They seem to hurt me more, and damn sowed mobs suck ass from a pulling perspective.

Expect every sowed mob to be hasted as well, since Ruj is loaded with shaman mobs.

Tak is full of druid mobs instead, so it'd probably be easier to have your cleric land a Kings on the mob (I pull with it in ldon-easy; low mana, casts fast, kills DS and is low enough aggro so that a good cleric puller can drop the mob on the bard without taking a single hit) instead of dispel. Either way, a high DS like bracken is just as bad, if not worse than haste, since it either kills all your melees or your group's DPS, while still causing the tank to take damage faster.

It's just that a mob that hits for 988 gets a much bigger increase in damage output from a 50% haste than a mob that only hits for 380.

Guk's blind pulls are made a lot easier with a mage/wiz/bard/holgresh beads, once you get used to the fact that Guk mobs have a larger aggro reaction range than any theme (Ruj is close, but ruj has a lot more room for mobs to be spaced out). There's also a wide range of classes spread out, meaning you have to be prepared for mobs with haste, DS, dispel, mez and charm (chanters), DS (necro), sow and haste (shamen), and CH (cleric). If you use lull to pull, remember that all of those classes gate (clerics usually only gate at 70<mana<400, though), and the lull-line was recently nerfed so that its random extension (it used to last anywhere from 42 seconds to indefinitely, but usually closer to 90 seconds) no longer happens. All you get on a cleric/chanter lull spell is 7 ticks now. SCR should work with it, focus effects don't.
