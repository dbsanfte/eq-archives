GraniteMG
This has been my policy, so figure i'll throw a word in edgewise.

1. Point in Each
2. Physical Enhancement
3. CA
4. ND
5. CS

I did it that way for multiple reasons. I started off with a point in each because the return was the greatest % increase in tankability to aa cost. Same for Physical enhancement.

Now as for the 3, here's my breakdown.

CA - I really do believe avoidance is near godly. It's streaky, but so is combat stability. Combat stability definitely ISN'T a fixed amount, it sometimes goes off and sometimes doesnt, the difference between the two is that with CA you avoid contact procs. Now I think damage reduced is usually more efficient than hp, because you require less healing, and it makes every spot heal on you that much more efficient. Remember that complete heal isn't the only heal thats ever going to land on you. With CA and LR maxed I was able to tank hard LDoN's with 6500hp, where as a friend with 8100hp was too taxing manawise. Less healing = good.

ND - I chose hp before mitigation because of AE damage and proc damage. Mitigation doesn't do anything for either, though I was tempted to go mitigation first just to make those spot heals more efficient.

CS - CS and ID seem to be doing more for me now that i'm finishing them up and have higher ac, it's an increase in your mitigation, and when you're already getting good mitigation from AC i think it really helps to level it out. I think people who tell you mitigation aa are better for you when you have low AC, aren't really crunching the numbers and are assuming the mitigation aa will make up for your AC, when in fact I think they do a better job of complimenting it.
