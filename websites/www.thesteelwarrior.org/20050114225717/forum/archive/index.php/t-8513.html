Your post is a little vague, so it's tough to say what to do.

First off, do you have the following AAs:
- AETaunt
- Combat Stability 3
- Combat Agility 3
- Lightning Reflexes 5
- Innate Defense 5
- Stalwart Endurance (depending on your race)
- Natural Durability

Usually, for MTs, those above AAs are considered the essential ones to have.

I'm not sure if a BoC + A2 + Furious Bash Shield is enough to generate and hold aggro. I seriously doubt so, unless the rest of the guild has pittance for dps / aggro. If you have Weapon Affinity 5 AA, maybe, but I still doubt so.

Try using another aggro weapon with the BoC, then, when you feel you have enough aggro, switch to BoC + Shield then.

As for Defensive / Furious, it also depends on the mob, and your AC / HP level. Certain combinations mean Defensive is enough, and you can save Furious for other 'oh sh!t' instances. Other combinations make it imperative to open up with Furious first, then Defensive / Stonewall.

Go to the Library forum on this site and read through those posts...there's lots of great tanking info there. On the Skills forum here I think there's also a stickied post on AA's - although that might be in the library now. The only changes I'd make from what Tiej suggested is getting Physical Enhancement after CS/CA and getting ND earlier - although ND really depends on how you are on HP for the mobs you plan to tank.

Another good info source is Alla's - go to the page for the individual mobs and see what strategy guides people have posted.

As to which discipline to use - it depends (and the 2 places listed above are great starting points to find out what's best.) In most cases I lead off with Furious, then either Defensive or Evasive depending on the mobs DI and procs. However, in some cases I lead with Fearless or Rage (forget the name...the agro increaser ability.) The bottom line is you need to adjust your tactics depending on the mob.

Also, I think there are more details in the Library, but make sure you know how to MT using multiple approaches (cornering, slow circle, flipping, etc.) Note: this is as much of a learning experience for everyone else in the guild as it is for the MT.

Oh yeah, and don't hesitate to communicate - especially concerning raid discipline. (i.e.; yell at someone if they are screwing up) It's much better to yell and get the problem solved than it is to wipe and get frustrated.

Good luck!!
