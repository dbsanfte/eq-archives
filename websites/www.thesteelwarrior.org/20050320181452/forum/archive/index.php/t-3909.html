I guess we're talking about level 55-60 characters here, which is where I've personally seen DPS classes swinging Droga weapons.

To a warrior, the Droga weapons are pure aggro getters, and that's that. The stun effect is incidental; nice when it interrupts a caster, otherwise, yawn-worthy. You see, when a warrior wants to interrupt a caster, she can kick, or bash, or slam. What really matters about those procs is that they create hate.

To a monk, the Copper Hammer of Striking is a chance to interrupt casting. (Yes, there's the Dragon Punch AA skill, but that's a way in the future for most monks who would use a CHoS.) The only other weapon they can realistically get in these level ranges which interrupts casting is a T-staff.

And in all honesty, if Mend (and later Stonestance) is up, then it sometimes makes sense for a non-pulling monk to take aggro.

However, if grouped with other people who can stun and it's important to focus aggro on the warrior, then I'd say your monk should be bagging her CHoS.

Rangers using Droga weapons is something I don't understand at all, but then, I've never played a ranger past level 25 or so, so I won't judge them.

As for the "carrying around spare weapons" - that's about the player, not the class.

The type of person who reads messageboards about their class is usually the sort of person who's interested in acquiring and sharing knowledge and techniques. That puts them in the upper couple of quartiles of EQ players almost automatically.

There are plenty of pickup group weekend warriors out there who have one or two weapons on their person in total - maybe another if you count their bow. Those aren't the sort of people who visit their class messageboard, though; if they had, they'd have learned about the benefits of having all the funky weapons in their bags and would probably have made the effort to acquire them.

I'm no great fan of monkly business, but if you popped over there and asked, I'm sure you'd find that most of the posters had at least one stun-proccing weapon in their bags at all times - because they're the thinkers. But in a pickup group, you'll get plenty of monks who are one weapon combo wonders.
