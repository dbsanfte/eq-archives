I have always looked at it like this, a 14/18 weapon with +4 magic damage is just that. but the damage still makes it a 18/18 weapon. Just remember the normal damage is mitigated my ac, the magic damage is mitigated by resist.

Mitigation for your attacks is about 40%, or 60% success rate. Most people average the elemental damage as 50% success rate which is why anyone would prefer normal damage to elemental damage.

Take HoH (most mobs have high MR), a 14/18 weapon, with +4 magic damage is going to have about 40% of the normal damage mitigated while about 75% of the magic damage mitigated. But if you take the same mobs in HoH, and say the weapon was 14/18 +4 fire damage. (average fire resists on HoH mobs). Then add in that a druid is in your group always casting hand of ro (-72 fire resist). Then your weapon with still have the normal 40% mitigation on your melee damage, but almost no mitigation on your fire damage.

So in some cases it can me nice to have the elemental damage, but most of the time the consistency of normal melee damage is more dependable.

I remember talking in game to Dinian who was parsing his DoD, and he said he was getting a 19 base damage more than anything else...although I don't remember what he was parsing on.

The way you can tell what a weapon's damage is on average is simply by parsing your weapon wherever you fight, then looking at the hit range between its base damage and its base damage + elements damage (if you're parsing from the main hand, you need to factor in damage bonus). The number you see the most is going to be the acting "mode" damage (i didn't use average is because you'd have to do some multiplication and division, and I dont feel mathy today).

So---to parse your Sparkling Short Sword:
stick it in your offhand, put a non-slash weapon in your main hand, and spend a day parsing (it can be in various ldons, Earth, GoD, wherever). Then see how many slashes you get for 14, 15, 16, 17, and 18. There's your damage curve.
