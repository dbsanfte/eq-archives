Something I would like to point out to the people that say there is no more softcap. By the nature of the way the combat engine works, there is necessarily a soft cap. Due to a mob always having a minimum hit, the slope of the line when you graph AC/average hit is always going to flatten out as your AC becomes high enough that the majority of hits are for minimum.

The trick is finding the point at which the gains for more AC are not as great as the gains for more HP.

I've just been tossing this around today after reading this post. But basically it looks to me to be something like this:

DMG = DB + (x+(y*(av-ac) / 20) * DI

Where x = random number 1-20 (damage intervals)
y = random number 0-20 (can still hit for min so 0 included)
x = max(20) anything over 20 will return 20 or max dam.
av = an attack value assigned to each mob
ac = armor class value based on your ac

This will push the damage curve to the right given a higher attack value for a mob (they hit for closer to max damage more often). And push the damage curve back to the left with more AC ( they hit for less more often).

This would mean that each mob would have a different AC softcap, but that number could be determined given enough sample data.

I can explain the formula if needed, but I think it is somewhat self explanatory. Let me know what you think.
