The misconception about the softcap is that once you hit it, you shouldn't focus on AC any longer. The reason this is a misconception is that AC has a SOFT cap, not a HARD cap. You still get additional benefit from more AC, it is just not as much as before the cap. For example, 1300 -> 1400 might result in a 4% mitigation increase, 2300 -> 2400 results in a 1.5% mitigation icrease. Still a 100ac increase, but not nearly as much of a mitigation increase, but it is still worth getting.

In theory, there will eventually come a time when the increased mitigation from AC has reached a point that the returns are no longer worth it. We haven't reached that point yet, and at this time I don't think anyone can reach a high enough AC value to test it accurately.

Actually, about this AC thing, I prefer to speak about breakpoint or inflexion point not cap (either soft or hard).
For me, a cap is a limit that can be achieve but not gotten through. and thus a hardcap is linked to the mumber of bits that are used to store the value (an exemple would be the code I work with : it can't compute fluctuation below 1E-13 because of the rounding error of the calculator). on the other hand I see softcap as a limitation given by the programer, not the machine (an example would be the haste in EQ which is certainly calculated like this : Haste = Min (haste_v1 + haste_v2 , 100% ) + haste_v3 , thus without haste_v3 buff nobody can get above the softcap of 100% haste).

How about this for proof:
The cap on AC in the Velious era wasn't a soft cap; it was a hard cap that had been there from day 1. After a certain point, which differed for each class, the benefit of more AC didn't just diminish - it dropped to nothing.

The change I referred to, just before PoP, changed that from a hard cap to a soft cap. You get a percentage of the amount over that soft cap. Shields increase both your total and your soft cap, making them more effective than any other item with equal AC. Your mitigation AAs, level, and class also affect the cap and the percentage return for AC over it.

Separate from this, there are diminishing returns if your AC is much greater than the NPC's attack. This is due to the nature of the formulas that produce the probability distributions that have been well documented on this board.
