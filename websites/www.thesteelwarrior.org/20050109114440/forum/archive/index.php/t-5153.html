According to this post here (http://pub147.ezboard.com/fmonklybusiness43508frm1.showMessage?topicID=43499.topic) on Monkly Business, the Opening System as it appeared on Test will not likely appear in the game in that form.

I wasn't in favor of this change. SoE intended it to provide pure melee classes with more complex gameplay and to enhance their groupability. I felt that the random utility it provided wouldn't be much more benefit than weilding a weapon with the proc you actually needed and the dancing star power-up aspect would be tough to deal with (playing a Warrior has a fair amount of complexity already).

Which brings me to my suggestion - provide a limited number of weapon buff disciplines that overwrite any existing weapon buff with a short duration (1 minute?) buff sharing the same refresh timer (3 minutes?). I'm thinking a 0 sec. stun for interrupting casters, a modest (30%) slow, Tash, Ensnare, +1 DMG etc.

Most of these things are on weapons already and many warriors swap them in or out as needed. Selecting a Weapon Buff Discipline is less messy than the Openings System and the right 4 or 5 weapon buffs could give Warriors a LOT of flexability in raids and enhance their experiance groupability without being unbalancing.

Ah, I know what your problem is. You use the numberpad on the right side of your keyboard for your hotkeys, don't you? On mine, I use the numberpad for my spells (1-8 for the corresponding spell gems). When I first got my discs on my beastlord, I was attempting to activate the disciplines when I hit the first 4 numbers (for Slow, debuff, haste and heal).

If you look up the keyboard assignments in the Options window, you'll notice they assigned numberpad 1-8 for the combat abilities in addition to CTRL 1-8. Just go in and remove the numberpad associations (should be as the alternate hotkey) and you shouldn't have any more problems.

If nothing else you should check to see what the combat window hotkeys are being activated from, that should help.
