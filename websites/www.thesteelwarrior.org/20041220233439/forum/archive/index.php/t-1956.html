What do you mean by "good" in your raid definition.

The main reason people raid (and play this game at all) is for character progression. There are 2 ways which characters progress in EQ. The first is levels and AAs which you gain in XP groups not raids, and the 2nd is for gear (and in PoP flags) which is the goal of raids.

Courtesy of mudflation there is very little (if anything at all) that you could 'raid' for with 30ish level 20-40s that an upgrade couldnt be bought relatively cheaply from the bazaar. Because something difficult for that number of mid level characters would be trivial to a high level group or a high level soloer in some cases loot valueable to characters of this level would just be farmed by higher level characters so there isn't really any raid content focused for players in the mid levels.

If by 'good' you are looking to enjoy working on team work and doing something a bit different by getting your whole guild together then I'd suggest looking for a dungeon targetted at XP for those a few levels higher than you are.

The Tower of Frozen Shadow could be a good spot because much of it is keyed and a fair amount of loot is no drop its unlikely the loot dropping mobs will be cleared by farmers, also clearing each floor looking for keys makes it more of a quest completion raid than a loot raid.

If you have a decent number of 40 somethings another fun place to try could be doing a fire giant raid in Nagafen's lair or even an efreet lord raid. These were the end game when the level limit was 50 so in a way they are designed as raid level encounters for level 40-50 players.

I realise that you are probably planning this as a fun event and its not all about phat lewts, but it can be a limiting factor to your excitment when the peak of your lower guk raid is disposing of the Froglok king and the reward is the once mighty and now 200pp piece of bazaar junk Mithril Two Hander, or you crawl your way there and find some level 60 paladin sneaked in ahead of you and solo'd your raid target.
