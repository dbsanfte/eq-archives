How many of the people who answered agro control only play a warrior at the lvl 65. Unless you play a lvl 65 slower or cleric who has a lot of power to decide what type of tank will they invite, then you are not qualified to answer this poll.

My main's a 65 cleric, so presumably that qualifies me to answer this impartially. :)

Being on a different timezone to the majority of my guild, I do a lot of exping in pickup groups; I've achieved level 65 and over 100aa with random bunches of people.

I don't often have a choice of tanks, is the truth of it; or I get real hard choices, like unguilded 61 war with Baron title and a pair of Defenders Lightblades -v- 65 pal with Duke title and some wierd-looking 2hs with a particle effect.

I tend to choose to group with the first tank I find, unless I know for certain they're a retard, without regard to the warrior/knight debate. If the warrior's having aggro issues, as is sometimes the case, I'll get out the roots.

But setting aside the gritty realities, let's imagine some perfect world in which I have the choice of paladin, shadowknight, or warrior. All are level 65, all are in reasonable but not exceptional gear, and all have reasonable reputations; I don't know any of them personally; and I'm in the fortunate position of having to choose one.

I'll group the SK first any time. The paladin can tank and hold aggro, but there isn't anything else she can do that I can't do myself... and most exp groups contain a cleric. The shadowknight brings different abilities to the table which the group doesn't possess "by default" (snare, FD pull, FT10) as well as formidable damage output.

The exception is if I'm going to one of the undeady bits of HoH or CoD, where the paladin trumps the SK. But under those circumstances, I think I'd try to take both of the knight classes.

Usually life isn't like that, though. I do have a good friend who's a 65 SK, but if he's not on or doing something else, I can't group with a SK, because there are a such tiny number of them on the server. So it's paladin or warrior, and as far as I'm concerned, that's just a matter of who's available.

In a regular exp group environment, the art of grouping with a warrior is very straightforward: Some warriors can hold aggro, and others struggle. If you're grouped with one who's struggling, well, clerics have a fast cast, low resist root line. Mem it, use it, learn to love it. For a hundred mana or so, your warrior will get two or three taunt checks after slow and before root breaks, and that's really all that's needed.

(Sure, the knight can hold aggro without costing me a hundred mana, but I'd spend that extra mana anyway, keeping the knight standing during the fight. Warriors do tank noticeably better.)

That breaks down with unrootable critters, but then, the Train of Valor Wasteland isn't a place where I would choose to group anyway.

The correct use of root will enable a monk to tank in PoP. I've done that more than once, at times when we just couldn't find a tank at all.

Part of the relative downward movement of the warrior relative to the knight has been the change in PoP itemization in favour of two handers. That's meant that at the lower end, knights have caught up with warriors in terms of DPS; or even overtaken, where the warrior's having to use single-handed weapons with procs for aggro but the knight can hold aggro with spells and swing a Windblade too.

The answer to that problem may lie with adjusting the damage bonus for single handers, and possibly with adding a damage bonus (smaller than the primary hand bonus) for a weapon in the offhand.

Any offhand damage bonus should be warrior-only, of course. Rangers, monks and rogues with offhand damage bonuses would be scary!
