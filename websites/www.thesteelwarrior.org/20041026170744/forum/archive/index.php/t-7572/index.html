3. Customer service at SOE ought to be set up not just to handle critical issues, but to deal with trivial-but-easy-to-fix stuff.

This is a common business situation: Customer has a problem with the product.

This is how excellent customer service would work: This customer should easily be able to determine which customer service person to talk to about the problem and have easy access to that company representative; the company representative should be well enough trained to assess and understand the nature and severity of the problem; the company representative should either be able to address the problem or quickly access the person who can make a decision about addressing the problem. The decision should be made quickly on an issue like this and the course of action decided upon should follow soon after that according to a determined schedule; this decision and a timetable for fixing the problem (or an explanation of why the problem cannot be addressed) should be quickly communicated to customer; once the problem has been "fixed," a customer service person should check back with the customer to see if the fix worked.
