I can't beleive I'm adding to this discussion. :-/

I switched to warrior when Kunark came out. My roommate joined me in EQ when Kunark came out. I have some knowledge of how both classes worked at the time- Not total, of course.

As I recall, we were low-mid 40's around the time of most of the Hybrid changes.

Some of the changes, from the top of my head-

Manapool/Spell Cost- Spells cost more for a hybrid to cast then the parent class, AND Hybrids had a smaller mana pool. I misremember which they changed, I beleive it was the spell cost. Either way, they essentially got a bigger manapool.

Melee/Casting- Casting a spell reset the melee timer- IE, with a 40 delay weapon and zero haste, you would swing, cast a 3 second spell, finish casting, then wait 4 seconds to swing again. This was removed- Now you Swing, Cast a 3 second spell, wait 1 second, and swing again.

Spell Selection- All Hybrid spells were spells the parent class got. An SK would have no/few usefull DoT's, for example, as a necromancer would have a higher level version on the target, and the SK's would not be able to land.
Some more spells, and some Hybrid Specific spells were added, and have continued to be added, to the point were not long ago the necromancers asked for and received a Shadowknight spell (Death Peace, I beleive?).

Exp- I was a rogue pre-kunark, so again, I have some knowledge, but again, I admit the very real possibility that I am wrong. I was wasn't around for beta, but do know people who were.
The story I got was that Hybrids, as envisioned, would end up more powerfull then Warriors, but payed for it with needing more exp, a la mana RPGs. Very Early (I am unclear if this was late beta or early live game) this idea was discarded, and warrior HP was risen, and critical hits were introduced, etc. However, the Hybrids still required more exp to level.
The exp was changed.

Skill Caps- It was decided that Hybrids needed upgrades to more firmly establish there Melee role. "Knights" were given a raise to there Defense skill cap to match warriors, and Rangers has Offense raised to match warriors. NOTE- I do not mean all Defensive Skill caps, a la riposte, etc. Everytime I mention this someone points out that dodge, riposte, and parry all cap at lower levels. I mean specifically Defense.

What is my point? None of these preceding examples are likely to happen again. A return to Kunark is impossible- SoE has changed, and the roles of Hybrids have evolved. You can't go back to the point where a warrior was REQUIRED for an exp group. The point where, even if a Hybrid was just as good as the warrior, he would still slow exp due to the penalty. If warriors got everything that any warrior ever asked for, today, a paladin or shadowknight COULD still tank- They simply couldn't in many cases in Kunark. You can't have the Holy Trinity to the point you did in Kunark.

Now, lets look at what people really mean (At least what I think you must mean) when they say "Kunark"- They don't want Warriors to Overshadow Knights. I think most of the Sane(r) Warriors, Paladins, and Shadowknights ALL desire that- Not to have any of the tanks overshadow the other, but each to be able to host there own in all/most situations and be desired to group and raid, while still having some differences.

Of course, all of the Sane(r) people also realize that no agreement will ever be reached... There will always be "Well, you have LoH!" "Well, you have Defensive!" "Well, Pallies can Solo Gore!" and we both mutter "NERF!" when we see a SK crit for 7k+.

However, I also beleive that most people, if they take an unbiased look at the tanks, will have to admit that warriors do seem a bit lacking at the moment. Whether they need significant upgrades, minor upgrades, or simply more gimmacks to give them some keys to press in battle (IE, to shut us up... :-) depends on where you see that line of balance to be.

But Cazic H Thule on a Stick! Quit Screaming Kunark!
