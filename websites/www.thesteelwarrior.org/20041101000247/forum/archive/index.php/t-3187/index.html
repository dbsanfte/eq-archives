I stand corrected, Zito. I ran my second test last night and indeed haste did not have any effect on my weapon's proc per hour rate.

I had always been under the impression that when your weapon swings, there is a chance for it to proc but this indeed seems to be incorrect. Weapons seem to proc independent of swings and also it seems that the weapon proc rate is halved if the weapon is in off-hand.

I ran 2 tests, first I fought using Copper Hammer of Striking in primary and Sword of the Bloodsworn in secondary and I removed my haste cloak and I did not use Swift like Wind either. I ran this test for about 3 hours and the total fight time ended up being 1 hour and 27 minutes.

The second test I put my haste cloak on again and was using Swift like Wind all the time so I had 96% haste on all the time. I swapped the weapons so primary was Sword of the Bloodsworn and secondary was Copper Hammer of Striking. This test was to verify the 'proc rate is halved if in off hand' theory.

In first test CHoS procced 133+8 = 141 times (hit+resist) so it procced 141/1.45 = 97 procs per hour which is darn close to the last test's 96 times per hour when I was using 96% haste.

In the first test SotB procced 56+5 = 61 times so it procced 61/1.45 = 42 times per hour which is pretty close to the last test's 45 times per hour with 96% haste rate.

In the second test where I swapped the weapons and used 96% haste again the total fight time was 2 hours and 7 minutes (about 4.5 hours play time).

The primary weapon, SotB procced 215+23 = 238 times so the proc rate was 238/2.12 = 112 procs per hour.

The secondary weapon, CHoS procced 90+10=100 times so the proc rate was 100/2.12= 47 procs per hour.

I think to get a more accurate proc test, you should have at least 1000 procs in the sample, but that would mean about 10 hours or effective fighting time for primary weapon and 20 hours for secondary weapon.

I think that this small sample gives good enough indication that at least haste does not do anything to weapon's proc per hour rate and also that weapon's proc rate is about half of the normal if wielded in the secondary hand.
