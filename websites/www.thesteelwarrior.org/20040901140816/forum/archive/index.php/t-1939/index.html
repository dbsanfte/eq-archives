Esvee
Hi all,

Rather than stating the obvious points about warrior taunt lacking in the face of spell casting hybrids, I 'd like to take this discussion in a slightly different direction by discussing what exactly it is that defines each of the melee classes.

Monks are skilled fighters that hone their body into a deadly weapon. To this end they get class-defining abilities such as dragon punch, flying kick etc which allow them to inflict a lot of damage. They also get utility in the shape of mend and most importantly feign death, a skill that makes them the most skilled pullers in the game. In a pinch they are able to tank, but they don't solo well. Their equipment can help them inflict more damage and protect them on a pull but even with their bare fists they are quite powerfull.

Rogues are a heavy damage group oriented class. They use piercing weapons and poisons as befits the role of an assassin. They gain the unique ability to backstab which allows them to inflict massive amounts of damage without expending mana. They gain unique abilities such as pick lock, disarm trap and hide and sneak. Rogues are as good as unable to solo and they cannot tank well at all. A rogue is dependant on weapons for damage but can function in average armor.

Rangers are a mix of the druid and warrior class. They can track prey and are skilled with bows. As they grow in level they develop into a powerfull offensive class. They gain a lot of utility in the shape of their spells and unique abilities such as track and forage. They can heal themsleves, snare and fear animals. If they choose their targets carefully, they are quite capable of soloing. In a group they can tank at lower lvls but not at higher lvls. They can function in our outside a group in fairly average armor as long as their weapons are quite good and they have their spells.

Paladins are a mix of the cleric and warrior class. Their role defines them as holy knights. To this end they gain the ability to lay on hands, which unfortunately has very limited uses. They gain a range of heal spells allthough these only really work during downtime and this is really their only real form of utility. They are masters at keeping monsters hitting them due to their array of stun spels. They are defined as tanks in a group and they can fill those role fairly well. They are able to solo undead (Hmmmm, I thought they were holy?)if they choose their targets carefully. They can tank fairly well with only average weapons but they do need good armor. There are quite a few good weapns available to them at the higher end which help their otherwise poor damage output.
Wait a minute.....Exactly how is a Paladin a holy knight capable of protecting his group like this? Where are the holy swords that are able to destroy evi, not just undeadl?Sure he can hold aggro, and he gets group heals, which become pretty useless.....More on this later.

Shadowknights are defined as part warrior, part necromancer. They are evil knights, bent on inflicting pain and destruction. To this end they get the unique ability to Harm Touch, which turns out to have limited uses. They are able to lifetap and cast dot spells on their enemies, which do reasonable damage. They get a large array of spells that make monsters hate them (Why would a shadowknight get this?) In groups their role is generally defined as tanks. They are able to inflict more damage than Paladins, but the damage output remains reasonably poor. They are able to solo very well and gain a lot of utility such as invisibility and feign death and a by and large gimmicky pet. They are able to function on poor armor if solo and with poor weapons in a group. Even so, there is a large array of good weapons availabe to help their damage output.
Wait a minute......an evil knight that tanks and protects his group mates? Where are the evil powers of darkness and dark swords that inflict huge amounts of damage on their foes? More on this in a minute.

Warriors are the basic melee class. They are masters of armed combat in all its forms. They gain the unique ability to.......um.......help me here........I know this one.............er........

Crit? Crits are so random and so usless it is incredible. Besides which any class can now crit due to AA.

Berserker frenzy? O please, it hardly makes any difference due to you being mostly dead when it starts working and there is allmost no noticeable benefit whatsoever.

/Disc defensive? Every other melee class gets discs, that don't fly.

O, I know, /shield. Wait a minute.....why would a warrior want this? It is more suited to a Paladin surely?

Ok on to the next thing. We gain a lot of utility in the shape of.......?
Bind wounds? Everybody can do that.......at least as well as we can.
In a group a warriors role can only be defined as a tank. His damage output does not justify him being able to fulfill any role besides that of a tank. He cannot function at all outside a group. He needs both good armor and good and very rare weapons to fill this role effectively.
Why would anyone want to play this class? It does nothing any of the others can't do and is basically just very average. Where are the monstrous battle axes, cries of war that strenghten your allies in battle, the devastating blows to your oponent that severly disorient him and weaken his ability to fight?

A couple of solutions that have been given in the past is to either increase the amount of aggro a warrior can generate allowing them to at least fill in the only role they have or to allow them to do more damage, giving them some other utility other than tanking. A, yes, but that would imbalance us with monks and rogues......

I am lvl 64 and I never duel wield anymore. Sorry, but unless I get a PoI hammer (a rather rare drop again) to complement my red epic I know I'm only going to hold aggro on my 2h epic or Greatstaff of Thunder due to the huge advantage in damage 2h weapons have over duel wield. Ok, then let's increase the damage duel wield does to put it ahead of 2h again making it fairer to all pure melee classes. What? Knights wouldn't accept that? .....O dear.......

This to my mind is the problem......all melee classes have now become so similar to each other in terms of damage output and in some cases tanking ability that it leaves those classes without spells and skills at a severe disadvantage. Making all classes even more equal with regards taunt, damage and tanking in my opinion is only going to make matters worse.
The only solution is to make it so that melee classes can COMPLEMENT each other.

Shadow Knights ought to be a DPS class, not a tank. It fits their role a lot better. They should be churning out the kind of damage a rogue does with the aid of powerfull weapons and spells. To compensate, their defensive caps and the armor available to them should be lowered significantly. In the end this would even benefit them when solo as they fear kite most of the time anyway. In a group they can cast aggro spells on the tank if they are to keep those and they can churn out huge damage, allowing them to complement both Paladins and warriors.

Warriors ought to be what it says on the tin, Masters of Armed Combat. To this end they should have some of the most powerfull weapons available to them and certain class defining skills, a couple of which I have outlined below.
The idea of critical hits is sound; certain parts of the body are very vulnerable. But the implementation in EQ is terrible. So what to do? Give them an array of special critical strikes, either on a timer (preferred) or randomly generated like a proc, obviousley the best skills coming at the highest lvls.

LEG STRIKE: when succesfull, this attack acts a lot like a snare or in some cases root. It immobilizes an enemy for a certain period of time.

ARM STRIKE: This acts a lot like a slow, but doesn t last as long. Will this upset shamen and enchanters? Well,no, a warrior still can't mez, heal or buff, but in high lvl groups at least warriors would gain a nice utility, possibly also making druids more attractive as group members for example.


CRY OF WAR: A lot like a bard song in that it gives a small haste and atk boost to your group. It woul be nowwhere near Shaman haste but possibly something like 20-30% for 1 minute.


ARMOR BREAK: An ability only the high lvl warrior gains. A well placed strike severely damages an enemies armor, forcing it to take a major hit to ac. This ability will make it much easier for the other classes to hit an opponent too.

HEAD STRIKE: the deadliest of a warriors abilities, possibly only gained at lvl 65. This serves to severely disorient an enemy, significantly affecting their ability to cast spells and stunning them for a significant period of time. Obviousely, this should not be maid too overpowered so as to make most encounters trivial, but it should be a powerfull skill that is worth looking forward to.

None of these skills would work on raid lvl mobs, however using them would induce a significant increase in aggro ( I think you d be pretty upset if a guy with a big club whacked you very hard in the noggin) This would not take a lot of code to program in (hell even shield would have taken more code I think ) and if balanced correctly it would still not make a warrior overpowering. We still wouldn't be able to solo, even with these skills due to them being high lvl and the fact that we can only bind wound after our fight, making our downtime hideous. If we gain these skills, I see no reason to increase our dps significantly either.

As for Paladins, what should they be? Their role should be defensive as it is now, but even more pronouncedly so. They should gain /shield, not us and possibly a better version of it. And maybe a skill such as holy flash of light which can cause a group of monsters to run away in terror. Maybe even a holy flash that acts like a ghetto mez like necro's have Screaming Terror. They should have the unique ability to save their group from dying when things are really going badly.

Another change I would like to see implemented is the complete removal of bind wound caps for all pure melee classes alltogether. All spellcasting classes with the exception maybe of clerics are able to solo to lvl 40. Will it overpower us to be able to patch ourselves up after being jumped rather than dying at low hp from another mob? It will give us the ability to solo some mobs slowly while waiting for a group, at least at lower lvls.

In my opinion with the right mix of classes it should be possible even for an all melee group to be able hunt for reasonable experience with the right mix of skills. I have lost count of the amount of times I have been sitting around with a full group waiting for a cleric to show up so we can actually do something worthwile. Clerics will still make it easier to do certain things, but it should at least be posssible to gain experience, even at higher lvls without having certain classes in a group. This is the only way Verant could possibly promote grouping.

I would like to hear other high lvl warrior's opinions on this and if the ideas were to be refined and prove popular wether it would be worth presenting them to Verant as a general request from the entire warrior community.

Esvee Aendland
lvl 64 Warlord
