Eoden
I started tanking in PoV at 61 with about 4khps and 1200AC and in groups with a cleric and either a shaman or beastlord that would buff up to around 6.5k which was plenty for tanking. With just a cleric I was sitting around 5.4k which can make things a little loose.

I think level of the cleric, your ability to maintain agro and the skill of the groups slower are all as important as your max hps.

Currently I have 2 characters I am XPing with in PoV, a warrior (ding overlord last night) and a 64 enchanter. My girlfriend plays a 65 SK.

Heres a few of my observations~

With the SK tanking (she has about 3khps unbuffed, so usually sits between 4.5k and 5.5k hps depending on the group make up) as a chanter I can land slows on incoming and not draw agro, this means that she rarely ever has to tank unslowed mobs (mad FD skillz so always single pulls).

As a warrior tanking I usually have to tank an unslowed mob for a bit longer, but the extra hps mean this isnt really a problem. With either shaman or bst buffs and a good cleric Ive had plenty of groups run smoothly with slows not landing on mobs till half way through the fight.

Couple of things to add tho, if you are light on HPs and there isnt a Shaman or BST to buff you probably want the cleric to be above 60. At 60 and below the pre-PoP quick heals dont have the bang for buck when things go pear shaped.

Really your ability to control agro will be the defining part of how well you tank in PoV. Extra HPs are nice for when the cleric has to land a quick heal on the chanter meaning the CH for you will be coming in late, but if you can manage to keep the mobs on you at all times this shouldnt be an issue, and if the cleric only needs to focus on keeping the tank up and not healing the rest of the group then even 5khps buffed will see you right.

For tanking I had a rogue parsing tell me that my 2 hander (epic 36/41) was out damaging my FG/Red Blade combo, and I definitely noticed it was easier to hold the mobs attention with the 2 hander.
