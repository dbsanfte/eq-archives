You know, I've been thinking about this a lot, and there's loads of conflicting stories still floating around.

The theory I came up with is one of angle. That if you get the mob to come in on a perfect 45 degrees then you'll have no problem, whether you're large race or what not, as long as you're shrunk. However, if you're a bit off, that's what is causing the problem.

Think of the mob as a square block (from what I know, the "bounding boxes" on where you hit a mob are all square). Now think of a corner. If you put that square block into the corner on a perfect 45 degree angle you get a nice perfect triangle of where you stand and where the mob is limited to. Now, if you shift either corner towards you a little bit, then that's when you get pushed out.

This seems to me what happened when cornering Doomshade. I quite literally got shoved out of the corner and the mob was behind me.

Anyone else have any other experiences like this?
