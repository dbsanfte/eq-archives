There are several reasons why the prices got so messed up.

At first it started with LDoN, less people were doing "classic" dungones and PoP stuff, so nobody was really farming anything. Supply decreased, demand stayed relatively what it was before, so prices increased a bit.

Then there were several tradeskill exploits that allowed you to make an incredible amount of money from nothing, and people were macroing it pretty much 24/7, then buying anything they ever wanted. That decreased supply significantly for people who actually earned their money, so people who still farmed learned about this and adjusted their prices accordingly.

Not to mention that some of the sales people out there (yantis, IGE, people who sell at playerauctions, etc...) took advantage of the situation, and macro'd MILLIONS of plat, bought up all the high end items they could, no matter what the price, continued making more plat...the end result being that anyone who sold items/and or platinum for real life cash suddenly had millions of plat AND high end items to sell. Eventually SoE caught on and banned accounts of players who were using the macros, which took platinum AND incredibly rare items out of the market.

A significant amount of people who exploited didn't get banned, and thats what the casino was put in for, so they could dump all their cash into it in hopes of getting an insanely rare, or even no longer obtainable item.

At least, thats my understanding of the situation, I may or may not be correct...
