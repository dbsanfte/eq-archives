Gnomos
Hook up with all your healers and go off in a quiet zone (like qeynos) and PRACTICE. What you want to see is a CH land on you every 2 seconds, like clockwork. Work with the CH rotation until you get this down. You probably need to do this with the rampage tank too, you have to keep them both alive, or rampage will eat your healers after it eats the RT.

Log all the cast messages and the messages that say "You are completely healed." Examine the time stamps on them in the log file, figure out where your mistakes are, and fix them. When you can achieve 2 seconds consistently, and maintain it long enough to kill him, then you are ready.

Eriatha Egan
There are multiple ways to pull Bert, really. The adds in the center room don't assist him, thank Brell! :P

We pull him with a warrior hitting Fortitude and running back to the raid force. Bert's melee range is HUGE, so it's generally not a good idea to tag him with an arrow un-disced and run as he's liable to off you in a heartbeat with your back turned to him. On the plus side, Fortitude blocks rampage damage whereas Furious does not. I know it saved my ass once as Bert ramped twice in a row before he was Crippled. With RT already set, MT grabs him and begins tanking. Once Fortitude ends on the RT, the RT hits Defensive/Stonewall and begins meleeing away.

As far as Ramp healers for Bert, we use three. Since I play my own cleric as a second box, I'm often the RT for this event (And for a lot of events). CH is NOT a good idea, lol. It's simply too slow. My guild leader also 2boxes a cleric, so he's healing me as well. We just add a shaman or druid to the fray and RT healing is handled pretty well via quickheals.

Slap a bard in with the tank group for the extra AC from the songs plus damage shield. A 180-200 DS on MT and RT is worth 40-50k damage alone.

Ok my guild just killed bert for the second time recently after 4 wipes on our first 4 tries, after the third wipe we tried a new strategy which works very well for us. First off your guild should try going over this strat once when SoC is not up. But anyways our basic strat is to kill all the kings at there spawn point. You need to know your way around the basement well and your guild needs to pay attention. The first step is to kill mobs until first king spawns. At the point the guild leaves the middle area and never returns to that point, that is very important. When you reach the first spawn point have your guild wait at the bottom of the ramp. Have your puller on the opposite side of the spawn room. The puller tags the king and all the adds and runs back away from the guild. At this point the spawn room is empty and the entire raid force moves into the room. The king usually 99% of the time returns solo because all of the adds corpsecamp the FD monk back in the hall. Engage the name and kill him, have chanters watching for returning adds during the fight though 99% of the time we killed the king before any adds returned. At this point it is very important that you have mapped a route to the next spawn point and can get your entire raid there without ever going in the middle room. Do this for all the kings and it saves a ton of time from trying to pull adds from spawn room and then pulling king after, plus you don't have to worry about repops in the middle room.

As far as Bert is concerned just fight max melee, if your having trouble keeping your MT up even if your rot is good i'd suggest having a chanter chain cast runes on him and plenty of spot heals.
This strat has worked well for us and good luck killing that bastard.
