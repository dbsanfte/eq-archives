New warrior didn't AE taunt or the AE taunt was out of range. It wouldn't have bounced if he picked up the solid aggro. Also you want to AE taunt BEFORE the person at the height of aggro dies if you're going to do so. The normal way I handle it (And we don't do called MT switches normally so it's slightly different) is as follows:

Old_Tank dies/announces disc is down.
New_Tank announces he's taking aggro over.
All clerics in loop switch to New_Tank on their next loop
All Rangers/Paladins/Druids/Shamans target New_Tank and begin casting their fastest casting Heal spell.
All other melee's turn off attack
New_Tank hit's defensive disc.
New_Tank waits 2-3 seconds for heals to start landing / CH to start getting near done.
New_Tank hits AE - Taunt.
New_Tank insures he has 5 - 15 seconds of solid aggro.
New_Tank calls for melee's to attack the target again.

You need to let the heals stabilize BEFORE taking aggro, and you need to ensure that it doesn't bounce around too much.

Mithaniel Marr does not rampage. It may say he does, but he doesn't actually do it, like almost everything in PoEarth and Xegony.

With PoP it's pretty much pointless to talk about a tank switch. You're not going to switch a tank just because the disc is down because that creates more trouble than it's worth, and you certainly don't know when the previous tank is going to die. Just hit AE taunt when you're getting heals. Who cares who else the mob is killing? That even include clerics since usually the number of clerics usually vastly outnumber the number of well-equipped warriors. Everyone else doesn't even matter if they die. Actually it's a good thing for anyone else to die because that's not another warrior or cleric dead.

If you have confidence in your cleric's ability to react or the mob's inability to one round you, it's actually pretty trivial for a backup warrior to ensure he's the next person on the aggro list when the MT dies. For example, if MT is using a Blade of War and so are you, assuming you don't steal aggro you're almost certainly #2 on the aggro list, unless you've some zealous nukers (in that case, it's actually better since they buffer for the tank switches). In fact it's so easy you actually want to avoid being #2 on the aggro list because generally speaking you can't count on clerics reacting fast enough to save you if you get aggro the moment MT dies.
