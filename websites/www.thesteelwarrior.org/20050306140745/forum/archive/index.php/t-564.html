From the perspective of somebody who plays another class primarily, I think warriors need help.

I've always like the warrior class, and I've probably had a dozen warrior characters over the (almost) 4 years I've played. Sturgis is the latest and highest level of those. I really feel sympathy for my warlord friends who sit around practically begging for something to do, and have often times taken them off to duo someplace with my main.

And, contrary to how a lot of people think, I do think warriors should be able to solo. I guess it's the old D&D thinking in me, but I always looked at the classes as the pure classes, and all the rest. The pure classes are Warrior, Wizard, Rogue, and Cleric.
Each of these classes have one core funtion that they do better than anyone in the game (that is, they SHOULD). Warriors fight. Wizards nuke. Clerics heal. Rogues stay alive...I guess. Each of these four classes SHOULD be the ones that are known as being good soloing classes..because they are the basics..the purest classes in each area. Of course, they shouldnt be able to walk in and kill a god or dragon single-handedly, but they should be able to handle your everyday exp mobs as easily (or easier) than anyone else.

So, if you dont mind a non-warrior brainstorming a bit, I'll tell you some of the things that I think would make warriors better. And, Varaho, dont pay attention to that thread over on the Crucible...some of the things posted there these days are stupifying. A lot of us shamans actually realize our class is far from broken and other people need some attention.

- One of the first things I noticed about my little warriors was the 1/2 point of magic resist each level. This is cool concept..the warrior class having a natural resistance or aversion to magic..but nothing has ever come of it. Some ideas:
a) give warriors twice as many points of magic resist if they train those AA's that add to magic resistance (General)
b) give warriors an AA ability that gives them a large permanent magic resist boost..say 50 MR for 3 or 4 AA..maybe let them train it 2 or 3 levels even (Archetype or PoP)
c) give warriors an AA that makes them immune to harmful magic for a limited duration - say 30 seconds, with a reasonable re-use time (Class or PoP)

- Stuns. I dont know if they're a big deal to you, they've never been a big deal to my barbarian. But, some shamans seem to care about the ogre no-frontal-stun thing. I never have, but none-the-less warriors live and die in the trenches. Why would they see birdies if they got clocked a good one? Leave that for the robes.
a) give warriors a cheap, passive AA that makes them permanently immune to stun, from any direction..and by cheap, I mean 1 or 2 AA at most (Class or PoP with no prerequisite). Get rid of Stalwart Endurance

- Damage. Rogues and monks should not be the melee damage kings. Warriors should be, in all aspects of melee combat, the masters...well, maybe give evasion to the rogues cause I'm not sure what else to give them. Warriors should not only be able to take more damage than anyone, but I feel they should have no equal in dishing out damage as well. If that were the case, some of the aggro problems would be taken care of (not all). Some ideas:
a) Make Fell Strike discipline a 7 minute re-use. Right now, a 58+ warrior can do 12 seconds of planned 4x damage per half hour. A rogue can backstab every what...10 seconds? So, in a half hour a rogue can do 180 backstabs. How many attacks do you make in 12 seconds? 20? 30? 50? These are very general numbers, of course, but a rogue with simple backstab alone can get almost 4 times the number of 4x damage attacks as a warrior with Fell Strike. Combat Fury is just icing on the cake for that rogue.
b) Or, as an alternative to the change above; give warriors a discipline (or skill) with similiar re-use time as backstab that lets you throw out one combat round of 4x damage...call it weaponmastery, or something.
c) make Area Taunt usable at the same interval as normal taunt, you could have one hotkey to taunt, one for area taunt when appropriate
d) monks and rogues both have haste disciplines; why dont warriors?

I could probably throw out more ideas, but this has gotten pretty long and my attention span is short. Please dont flame me for innaccuracies or misperceptions, I already admitted I'm a newbie as a warrior.

I think the that point really isn't that Warriors can't solo. It's the fact that we are the most dependent class in the game who must absolutely without a doubt have a group to accomplish anything worth doing and we've had our group desirability blown to crap.
