The relative value of certain stats depends on where you are in the game, and what sort of gear you have. AC and hitpoints--well, you just never get tired of having more. As for ATK, I've never picked a piece of gear based on ATK. I can't imagine a situation where I would, since one's ATK generally seems to take care of itself if you get better gear.
For a long time, STA ranks right up there with AC/hitpoints, because until you cap out STA, STA *is* hitpoints. In my experience, though, STA for a long tiime is actually easier to get than DEX (if you get the best AC you can, you'll be getting STA but not necessarily DEX in some armor lines), so for some warriors at some points (55-65?) dex may be lagging behind STA, which can become an issue with proc rates and hence improving your chances of good aggro early. It's pretty easy to see what an extra 15 STA gets you; it's harder to see that +15 DEX does anything at all. However, you can easily see the difference between the aggro of a warrior with max dex and WA vs. a warrior with <max dex and no weapon affinity.
AGI, ATK, STR...well, they just aren't that big a deal. If you're not encumbered, you're strong enough. the only time I ever pay any attention to STR is if I'm smithing for a skill up.
For advanced tanks doing tough mobs, DI is sometimes and issue, and its success rate is a function of CHA, and CHA is kinda hard to max, so you see some folks get a little interested in it at certain points in their careers.
Various resists are the same: situational, depending on what mob you're fighting. Not at all uncommon to see Magelos of warriors with a bag full of weapons and another bag full of resist/clicky stuff. I don't recall ever seeing anyone packing something to improve ATK at the expense of AC/Hps.

There is a cleric 'buff' called Divine Intervention, which lasts for about 10 minutes or so, and gives you a % chance to 'proc' so to say, a Complete Heal if your health gets below a certain point (think 20% of health not sure). Now the chance for the DI to proc is a combination of cleric AA's and your charisma, hence you see tanks that use DI regulary equip charisma gear, get charisma buffs and pref. also have a bard singing +cha grouped /w them.
Smart use of DI and the AA's / Tank's charisma can be the difference between a tight healer rotation, or the more usual cleric-zerg fest....
