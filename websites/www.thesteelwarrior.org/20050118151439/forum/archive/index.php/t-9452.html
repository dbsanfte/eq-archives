It seems to me that OoW generally is a tad too easy, at least compared to Txevu/Tacvi.

The first 3 MPG raid trials we could easily beat on either the first or second attempt. Corruption+Tactics+Mind took some more attempts, but in reality once you learn them, any guild with Qvic access could probably beat them, its not gear but tactics that count on them.

And the absurd thing is that IMO insano snake in Tacvi northwing is STILL harder than any of the 6 MPG raid trials. Its very illogical that there are encounters in Tacvi that are harder than the MPG trials, yet the MPG trials yield 50% more and statwise MUCH better loot pr kill, and also 2 very much needed level 70 runes.

It also bothers me that entire guilds can now skip the progression and not do Zun or learn Tacvi. It should simply not be possible gearwise to beat MPG raid trials when you have skipped Txevu/Zun/Tacvi, or at least it should put you at a big disadvantage. Most of the class specific weapons are now trivialized by easily obtainable MPG trial loots. Hell, we had trouble selling the (for us new) mindreaper monk club because our monks have obviously already noticed that there are new much better easily attainable options for them out.

So what should they do to fix this disparity in risk/reward?

Well they could start by making Tacvi bosses drop 3 loots each. As mentioned earlier many of the class specific drops there are now triviallized by MPG loot, so they should start adding a third "general purpose" loot slot on each boss there. It should be usuable by many classes and slightly worse than MPG loot, but they should consider putting some more unique click effects there(which was the plan from the beginning as I understand).

The reward for having learned Tacvi SHOULD at the very least be that you could use that zone for at least gearing new people up quickly, much like you previously could with PoTime. But thats not really possible atm I think. Raiding Tacvi requires every bit as much concentration/focus from the raid force as raiding MPG trials does(especially the north/south wing snakes), so its not "relaxing" raiding like PoTime was.

And finally I want to mention that I do find the MPG raid trials to be some of the finest/most fun encounters I have tried recently. But they really need to fix up Tacvi a little, because otherwise it will become the End-Game zone that most quickly become abandoned in EQs history so far.
