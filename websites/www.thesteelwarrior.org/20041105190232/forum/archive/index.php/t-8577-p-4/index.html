We just had our meeting. I can never remember the in-game names of the all the guys but it included design leads and code leads and a couple of other people who routinely work with the spell code & planning.

We talked for a bit about why the code on currently on Test is not what we want, and decided to remove it completely because - as many others have noticed - that particular solution was not a good one.

We discussed the problem again to verify, again, that this is indeed something we felt needed to be changed. Everyone there agreed that the situation still had to be changed for the many reasons we've gone over before. I specifically asked about possibly delaying this until after Omens is released and the designers were adamant about the potential for abuse in new zones was too much of a risk.

So we discussed the options. Went down all of the ones proposed so far and we wound up agreeing that what I'm about to describe is the best solution we can come up with that has a minimal effect on the other non-kiting uses for the songs. Hopefully most bards will agree. I think it's a pretty reasonable change given the problem we are trying to solve.

After the change, the bard PBAE dots will simply not cause damage to be applied to an NPC if the NPC is moving. All other spell effects will remain active and continue to be applied to the NPC, it is only the damage that will no longer be applied. To be clear: The bard will not have to stand still, this is the NPC that has to be stationary for the damage to be applied to the NPC. In other words, an NPC that is running after a bard who is playing PBAE songs will not take damage from them. Note that this *only* applies to the unlimited target count AE songs that bards have, and all other spells will be unaffected by this. Also, there will be no changes to the range of these spells resulting from this.

I sincerely hope most of you can understand the need for this change and will not be too frustrated as a result of it. Our goal is to keep the game healthy and it is difficult to make decisions like these because clearly a lot of people will disagree and think it's a bad thing for us to do this and we want players to have fun while they play EverQuest, but it is our responsibility to make these tough decisions in the hope of - and with the belief that it will result in - a better game that is more fun for everyone involved.

I would like to thank those of you who took the time to read my (many) posts on this subject as we tried to find the best solution and contributed to the debates. If it weren't for everyone providing feedback we probably would have wound up implementing something that would not have been as good of a solution as I think this one is.
