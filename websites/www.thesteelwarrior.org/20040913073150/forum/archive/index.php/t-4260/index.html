OK. I initially supported the entire Warrior cause. I myself am a 65 Warrior with a birth date of August 5, 1999. He is, and has been my only main. I am not E-bayed. My guild affiliations and status in the game make no difference.

The funny thing is, is that 90% of the Warrior population has no idea about the issues. They want everything from drastic DPS increases, insane mitigation increases (overly insane amounts), to things like a never-fail taunt button and a maximum possible hit on a Warrior.

Talk like this is counterproductive. Talk of nerfing another class is even more counterproductive. Asking for game-unbalancing things is plain stupid.

For instance-
-Permanent agro lock by a Warrior would be game unbalancing. A never-fail taunt would do that.
-A maximum possible hit on a Warrior is just plain stupid as well. Mobs should have a base damage table, from which final damage is calculated using the standard

Damage = DB + X * DI

Where X is a random integer between 1 and 20 and

where AA’s and class mitigation are figured in.

First of all, I will say that no class needs to be nerfed because of this.

Additionally, I think DPS is pretty much on target when juxtaposing most melee classes.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?threadid=3803

This being said, there are a few things that need to be addressed for Warriors-
-The taunt button is entirely based off of a random number generator. Warriors have zero ability to gain agro by any means other than hitting that button, or praying that a weapon procs.
-As soon as Knights (or any other class for that matter) break a certain HP barrier, it is no longer necessary (or even a good idea) to use a warrior as a main tank in raid situations. Virtually similar AC and HPs ever-encroaching a Warrior’s HPs mainly due to poor itemization make this possible. Not only do their HPs get ever closer to Warrior HPs, but the mana present on the same items further increase the utility, longevity, and tanking ability of said Knights. In addition, the on-demand casting of spells makes aggro control (and thus raid success) trivial for Knights.

Thus, a Warrior’s only role –to tank– has been eclipsed by other classes. A Warrior is no longer a necessary class in Everquest.

If I had a working answer to these problems, I would be working for SOE right now. Unfortunately, I am a peon, a cog in the wheel. Of course, however, I do have a couple of suggestions.

1) Apply a Succeed/Failure Message to the Taunt Button. Make this go in effect for every class. This way we can all see if taunt succeeded other than just seeing a mob ignores us.

2) Give Warriors an additional ranged taunt. Make this an innate class skill that has its success tied into the Taunt skill. Scale a raw value of hate to this taunt appropriate to the level of the Taunt skill. Do not make this an overpowering taunt, or a means of gaining overpowering aggro. Make this have a short refresh timer. Thus, this could be used to help with mob initialization and increasing Warrior aggro over time if so wanted. This would lower the need of EB weapons and the like for tanking.

3) Make us more desirable for tanking in raids by increasing our mitigation. This is very tricky. Here are a couple ideas-
-Get rid of defensive entirely. It is a crutch. In accordance with this, drastically increase Warrior mitigation against mobs that are of higher level than the player. Do not increase it to quite the level of defensive, but make it permanent. This would make Warriors more desirable for tanking because they mitigate that boss mob damage better than anyone. This would not hurt Knights in EXP groups. Knights would also be able to tank as well as they always have against boss mobs as well, so it is not a nerf. A Warrior has one basic role – to tank. Lets remember that.
-I have seen plenty of other posts regarding this, a lot of which have viable answers as well.

4) Fix itemization. Items of parallel quality are grossly in favor of hybrids. For instance:
Warrior:
http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=17930
Paladin:
http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=18172

Hybrids get much more use out of such poorly designed items. “A 10 HP difference is a lot,” you say… Well, add in the mana into it and it becomes a powerful item. Not to mention, it has the exact same Aura and comparable armor class.

These are some of the problems I observe. Tonight, after the serverwide channel was moderated, I left it. Many many people in there protesting for Warriors are asking for stupid things. Many people arguing for both sides don’t even know what they are talking about. It is sad because I want this to succeed. I, however, am not going to associate myself with a mob of uninformed, overzealous people.

That being said, I wish luck and fortitude for all the people on this endeavor, hope for the best, and will continue playing the game without interruption or personal protest other than that which I have posted.

Might be worth discussing what tanking "well enough" means; here's the criteria I personally use:

For exp grinds:
-can be healed by a single cleric, druid or shaman w/o chain casting and/or excessive mana regen breaks.
-can hold aggro well enough to allow DPS classes to do a sustainable amount of DPS output w/o significant pingpong
-can gain aggro quickly enough to allow debuffs relatively early during a fight.
-generate a respectable amount of DPS to keep the pace up.

For boss mobs/raids:
-has enough HP/avoidance to allow a reasonable delay CH chain/patch heal rotation to suvive an entire fight.
-can gain aggro quickly enough to prevent early debuffs/heals from getting casters splattered.
-has enough AC to reduce median hits from mob to lower part of the distribution.

Sound pretty reasonable?

Warriors (barely in some cases) meet those criteria now for most encounters, and are therefore fairly well balanced to the existing content in EQ. The problem is that knight classes meet all those criteria about as well as warriors do- without even using their spells! With the spells added in, they are far superior to warriors, especially in exp grinds. And this doesn't even touch on the OTHER utility and capability they bring to their groups...

When SOE's nerfed classes in the past, it's been painful but effective. When they've buffed up classes, they've almost invariably overcompensated. Warrior's don't need buffing to do what we are supposed to- and now that knight classes can do the same things, they need to be trimmed down elsewhere to compensate, or have their ability to tank w/o casting eliminated.

All just my own $.02, obviously- comments welcome, other opinons respected, flames tolerated. ;)
