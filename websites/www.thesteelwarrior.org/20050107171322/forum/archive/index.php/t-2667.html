Ditch the splitblade, it sucks ass. It'd make a nice ranger weapon (if they could use it), but it's crap for a warrior. Grab a SoD and you'll be set. If you want 2handed, Greatstaff of Thunder is pretty much the best buyable 2hander.

The reason you are getting some shit from people is that if you were at the point where you could earn the weapons yourself, you would know most of this. People get angry when people who haven't made it to the point that they could actually get the items without cash go and sound, no offence, fairly noobish.

As for Bloodfrenzy and Hategiver, both are leaps and bounds above BoC, but both are no drop. So since you're not on FV, you won't be able to buy them.

For how exactly procs work. Procs can randomly go off whenever you swing. The percent chance of it happening on a given swing gets less with more haste, and better when slowed. So in a large parse, you should have the same number of procs per minute while slowed, buffless, or hasted.

700 hate is just that, 700 hate. Damage and spell hate other than pure hate spells are too complicated to be able to say how much damage or whatever 700 hate is. Additional factors are distance, comparative level, weapon type (1h or 2h), class, etc. There are too many variables.

Taunt key. Each time taunt is successful it puts you at the top of the hate list, plus a little bit. If it fails, nothing happens. You do not get a message distinquishing between the two, unless you are fighting a "speaking" mob, which will say "I'll teach you to interfere with me Tauntersname" when you succeed. There is more to it, as there are modifiers (same as above, plus whether you are attacking, and some others). So, as proven in a different thread, taunt may take you to the top of the hate list, but not always to the top of the modified hate list, so sometimes it takes multiple successful taunts (until the "little bit"s all add up to above the modifer) to finally get aggro.
