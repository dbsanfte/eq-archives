Ah, also note that this feature of taunt implies certain things about how it ought to be used.
Spam taunting does not help you build significant hate if the mob is already hitting you, for example. That means (for me anyway) that if the mob is hitting me, I let the taunt button refresh and keep it ready incase the mob *stops* hitting me.
Also notice that it implies a proper sequence in the order that you do certain things. On a mezzed mob, for example, it makes more sense to taunt until you get a success and *then* use the incite line, because incite hate comes in set units. If you incite after a successful taunt, then the taunt leapfrogs you over everyone else's hate, and the incite hate will be added to that. Suppose on a mezzed mob you are 1000 units of hate below the top character on that mob's hate list. Let's also say that at your level, the incite line adds 600 hate. If you use the incite line first, then get a successful taunt, then your hate will be 1000+ some small amount. But if you taunt first, you jump to 1,000+a small amount, and then when you hit your incite line you will add 600 hate to the "1,000 + a small amount." (The incite line numbers here are just sort of made up for illustration purposes.) For similar reasons, if you are trying to pull a mob off a caster, taunting first then kicking/slamming get you marginally more hate (assuming successful taunt) than first kicking/slamming then taunting.
That's what I think, anyway.
