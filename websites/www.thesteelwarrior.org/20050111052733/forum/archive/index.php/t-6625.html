Small increases in mana (or in this case end) over time have always been better in practice than in theory. Since this isn't in-game, I can only go over the theory aspect. I'm not a warrior, so some stuff could be wrong.

Heightened Endurance keeps endurance running in two forms; Regeneratively, like FT is to mana, and Mitigatively, similar to Mana-Preservation effects but time-based instead of being percentage-based.

Regeneratively:

HE3's regenerative effect is easy to calculate: 3 end per tick, 30 end per minute. In theory, here on this board, that is negligible. Taking a longer-term approach, that 3 end per tick gives an extra Incite (250) every 84 ticks (approximately) - 8 minutes and 24 seconds. Over a 45-minute LDoN mission, that's 1350 extra endurance; just over 5 free incites. Nothing earth-shattering, but for warriors having trouble inciting everything in fast groups such as LDoN, this can help tip the balance. Over a longer, sustained xp group (doesn't matter where or what level of content), in a span of 3 hours, that 3 end per tick adds up to 5400; about 21 and a half incites. Keep in mind that extra end can be spent on more than incite, and even warriors with high endurance pools, and/or don't usually have problems managing incite, can make use of this. I'm just a cleric, but I can see the value of things like Fortitude+AE Taunt on a bad pull every once in a while (Fortitude > Furious in that if the server is lagging a little behind, a riposte at the exact moment an add is mezzed can break that mez), a defensive-type disc if there's a named or even if the group healer is hurting for mana but the group doesn't want to stop pulling, a free offensive disc for fun, etc.

Discussing HE3 in theory, here on this board, 5400 end over 3 hours can still be seen as pretty negligible. In practice, in the field, this is primarily the exact same argument for casters and Mental Clarity; and that little bit can be pretty significant. I've never met a caster that didn't notice their first point of FT. Raid-buffed, getting over 100 mana per tick, I'll still notice if I'm missing something small in comparison like Spiritual Dominion, or if I'm Mind Wracked, etc. Every little bit really does help (at least in terms of mana), and HE3 is the only little bit that increases endurance regen.

Mitigatively:

Going beyond this "FT for melees" idea, Heightened Endurance also helps while a warrior is using discipline(s). Other than the paladin self-only regeneration line of spells, there are no spells that cost nothing to cast, but cost mana over time. Unless I'm reading Lucy's raw data wrong (the page detailing discs, costs and timers here seems to have been lost temporarily, I think it used to be under "Trainin'"), discs are exactly that, which allows HE3 to mitigate part of the cost of using them.

HE3's 3 endurance per tick mitigation, while using a longer defensive disc, adds up over time, just as it does regeneratively. Reducing Stonewall's upkeep from 11 end per tick to 8 cuts the total cost of the entire disc from 330 to 240. This mitigation is a constant over time (3 per tick) and so the 3-minute discs will always save 90 endurance; 96 combined with Furious/Fortitude.

If nothing else, for tanking named mobs where a warrior is using multiple disciplines, HE3 mitigating the cost of every disc will somewhat help prevent the warrior from running out of end while the disc is still active, and possibly allow the warrior to follow the defensive-type disc with Charge - if every attack lands, then that should be up to 30 seconds after defensive drops that riposte-spikes are still not an issue. Charge's upkeep is 42 per tick (45 minus the 3 from HE3), and so the savings from mitigating the cost of defensive alone should be enough for at least 2 ticks of Charge.

HE3 seems to make a difference both in raids and groups, but its actual value in practice, and whether or not it's worth the 18aa to max it, would depend on your current setup and current needs.
