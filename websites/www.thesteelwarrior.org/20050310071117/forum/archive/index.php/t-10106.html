From what I understood it to be, SH had the priority customer service, the reoccuring GM events, and the opportunity to have better interaction in the game....... basically everything that was promised to everyone when EQ went live. One thing was better items dropping in the same zones as the non-legends zones but you would never see them anywhere other than SH, and I believe there was something about the first person to loot it had the item named after him/her.

Instead, the high-end guilds were forced into a GM-enforced spawn rotation (I remember the ultimatum that TR sent to Sony that they were no longer abiding by it due to supposed favoritism for their competitors), testing and retesting of revamped zones before they went live, and poor implementation of promised CS to their higher paying customers. Legends eventually came to be the expensive way to server transfer with all your gear intact. Say you wanted to go from Fennin Ro to Povar, so you'd pay for a transfer from FR -> SH, then SH -> Povar. Of course this was before the actual straight-to-server-with-all-equipment deal.

Veeshan has recently seen an influx of Legends characters applying to CD. You can tell where they are from because their name has a title from partaking in some event or quest from Legends.
