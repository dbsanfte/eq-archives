03-13-2004, 04:05 PM
As you progress through GoD I think you will find dual wield offers great benefits. With Weapons Affinity 5 and hategiver, BoC both augd with anger3 its a rare case that I dont proc in the first 8 seconds of a fight.

With the mobs in the sewers, vxed, tipt all hitting for 1100+ and fast I need at least a cleric chain casting CH. Theres no time to "wait till warrior gets aggro", its game on the second the mob shows up. Dual wield offers less lag time between procs, and higher statistical chances of an even distribution of procs.

In my case with CH landing 8-10 seconds into each mob arriving in camp the rare case where I didnt proc EB is offset by the frequency I can AE Taunt (every 10 min) and save the cleric.

The downside, obviously, is eating a ton of riposte damage. You need to be quick and if you eat a 5 x 1250 round and CH is gonna be late just run big circles around the cleric. With dual wield and weapons affinity you have enough aggro to be able to run around and not have it pong.

Dual wield is also better when mobs come screaming into camp at mach 10. Missing that opening flurry with a 2 hander if mob isnt in range can be the difference of a dead cleric / blown trial and a win.

Thats my $.02. I love my DW setup, and my clerics love it too. :)
