I keep Supernal Cleansing, Yalup IV, Healing Wave of Prexus, Touch of Nife, Shackles of Tunare, Cease, Force of Akilae, and Quellious Words of Serenity loaded 90% of the time. I get experience in LDoN Normal, BoT, Tactics, and the like.

Supernal Cleansing is used to heal someone who has taken damage but is not in real trouble. It is well balanced with it's relatively high mana cost, and long recast time. My healing rate is really dependent on who else is in the group and the mana regen situation. In a group with a Druid healer and a BL and KEI I can heal a lot, while more commonly I might drop a heal every other fight or so.

Yalup IV: This is a STR/ATK buff. It lasts 18 seconds, so must be reapplied a lot. The moderate benefit is balanced by stacking issues and the constant attention needed to keep it active.

Healing Wave of Prexus is a group heal for 600 HP. I prefer it to Wave of Marr (a larger group heal) because of the lower mana cost, and the fact I like to cast it before a LOT of damage is done. The spell still has a high mana cost to balance its use.

Touch of Nife: This is a classis oh @#$#@ spell. It heals 850 HP for 400 mana, but it casts in 1 second. I use it when someone needs healing NOW. This is a great example of a well balanced spell. It has enough HP to make a difference for most non tanks and is fast enough to use in an emergency but costs a LOT of mana for the effect. I might not cast this for a long time, then cast three in a row in a trouble spot. Certainly the mana cost is too high for sustained use.

Shackles of Tunare is a fast casting root. It is very good for ghetto crowd control. I use it mostly when there is no mezzer in a group.

Cease: I frequently pull with Cease. The fact that it doesn't actually stop the mob is a benefit in some pulling situations. I also use it to build hate on mezzed mobs without breaking mezz. I will not argue with those who feel the aggro of this spell is a problem on mobs that it cannot actually stun.

Force of Akilae: I cast Akilae as soon as the mob actually hits camp to stun the mob before slow lands and to cement aggro. I will also use it to add hate to a mezzed mob on occasion, as well as to interrupt casting. This spell is level appropriate, and has an appropriate mana cost. It was designed to be one of a high level Paladin's main tools and it is.

Quellious Words of Serenity: I use this at the start of a fight against a tough mob (Kriger, named, etc) to mitigate damage. I also use to to position mobs and to interrupt casting. The spell has a large mana cost and long casting time to balance it.

There are a few problems with your perspective.

At 65 we have 9 different stuns. We don't have 9 different stuns at 9, 20, 30, 40 and 50. Just as with any caster, most spell lines are updated and they use the improved versions or they use the lower level spells for specific situations. Clerics use lower level heals as well as their latest ones depending on the need. I don't play a strict caster, but I would imagine they have lower level spells that they use for some things and don't automatically delete a spell when they get a more powerful version of it. Snare and root are lower level spells but still useful at higher levels.

Most paladins don't use stuns much when not the mt because they don't want to grab aggro. When fighting a caster I switch out to 1h and shield so I can bash and load an extra stun that actually is capable of stunning at higher levels or another knockback. Just because the spell is there doesn't mean it is used non-stop in every encounter. To me, it is like a weapon or a tool or a specialty piece of armor or jewelry. I switch out weapons to suit the mob and I switch out spells to suit the mob. Do you carry just one weapon? Do you get rid of one weapon as soon as you get another? I have 4 shields. I switch them out depending on what gives me the greatest use for the encounter. Surely one shield is sufficient. I have 3 two-hand weapons, 3 one-hand weapons, 1 blunt and 1 piercing. Surely I really only need one weapon since I can only use one at a time.

How many responses did you have to your survey? Not a lot. Mostly because most paladins didn't feel like getting their throats cut. I assure you most paladins use a stun to interrupt casters and most groups are greatful for any interruption of some deadly casts or particularly a gater. Nearly every encounter I have been on that has a wicked caster or gater, people are reminded to use whatever they can to interrupt the gate. I'm sorry but is the removal of this tool really going to help warriors? It is something that is used in specific situations for specific purposes. I know of no knowledgeable paladin who uses knockback stuns on a regular basis, but when they are needed they are invaluable. Just because something isn't used all the time doesn't mean there is no use for it. I don't use my resist gear all the time, but I have no intentions of throwing it out.

Once again. If you plan on getting rid of all but level 65 stuns, do you also think the same spell effect that weapons proc should be deleted?

I didn't answer the experince portion of your survey. I am usually in PoS, PoV or if I just have a short time to play I may park in Veks and solo a few mobs. Experience isn't very good but it's some movement. For me, it would be impossible to even solo that without stun because it is a ghetto slow and interrupts some of the casts.

Why should that spell be as effective as it is? Does any class have a spell that is effective at higher levels than when it was first awarded? If you are going with the argument that only stuns should not be effective then are you willing to delete all other spells that could get aggro? Do you take away snare? How about root? Flash of light? Tash? Slow? Heal?

I watched a monk continually grab aggro from a well equipped, knowledgeable, warrior who was a main tank for an upper end guild. What spell are you going to take away from him that gives the advantage back to the warrior?

In reality as it is now, just about anyone could take aggro from a warrior if they wanted to. Most people who know what they are doing and have been around for a while have learned aggro management. Our instant society we have been blessed with recently allows a knight to get instant aggro instead of waiting for the warrior to establish aggro and casters can unleash everything they have immediately instead of using a little common sense.

Guilds can level a character to 65 with an epic in less than a week if they are trying to prove something. I fully expect to see guilds "tanking" high end encounters with every single class there is just to prove they can do it and make a little news. Just because it can be done doesn't mean that's the way the real world operates.

I have seen many groups recently that are strictly pet classes. Not a single cleric or tank of any kind except the pets in the bunch. Does that mean that clerics are the next dinosaurs on their way to extinction?

Any logical solution to the problem has to take the entire situation into account.. Not everyone levels to 65 in a few months. There will still be casual players who take years to level so the lower end content cannot be gutted. Not everyone belongs to the elite guilds so they may still have to use someone other than warriors when theirs aren't available.

Improve the warrior. Make him the main tank. Improve his aggro control. Lower hp on the knights if needed, but keep in mind some guilds depend on these people to tank out of necessity. I don't mind being the OT or the back up tank since that was the idea to begin with, but neither do I want to spend every day in Veks solo.
