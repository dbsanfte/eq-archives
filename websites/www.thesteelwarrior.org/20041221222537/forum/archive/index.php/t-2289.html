From One of the most important posts you have never seen (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=1541) :

Max hit for primary hand is calculated by: [((Weapon Skill + Strength)/100) * (DMG)) + (Level Damage Bonus)]

Max hit for off hand is calculated by: [((Weapon Skill + STR)/100) * DMG]

Not sure if that formula is still considered 100 % accurate, but it should at least give you a rough estimate. Overall, strength adds a small amount to your DPS.

We all know that ATK is affected by three things.

1) Your weapon skill with the currently equipped weapon.
2) Your Strength.
3) Buffs/Effects which directly increase ATK.

I believe ATK is comprised of two parts; chance to hit and damage table modifier.

The chance to hit is pretty self-explanatory -- I believe only the weapon skill affects this part of ATK. The Damage Table Modifier on the other hand is affected by STR and raw ATK buffs. If you parse yourself for long enough you'll find that there are a certain number of distinct values you can hit for with a given weapon. This is your damage table for that particular weapon based on it damage rating. The modal hit is the number that comes up the most frequently. The damage table modifier shifts your modal hit upwards on the damage table. So the higher your DTM based on your ATK the higher your avg hit will be and the higher in turn your DPS will be.

my 2 cents.

I tested this myself (mostly with backstab at the time, but later with the warrior).

Cannot recall exact numbers but I think with windblade my max was 306. Point is... with no strength buff or attack buff I never landed a 306 in hours of logs.

With my strength buffed up I was landing 306's semi regularly. So one may jump to the conclusion that my max damage was raised by increasing strength.

However, I took away the strength buffs and instead used raw attack buffs. My max hits were still 306, same as with strength buffed.

Now, someone could make the argument that both strength and attack raise your max, but I did a further test with both strength and attack buffed and my max remained at 306 (tho I hit for it more often).

Looking at it logically, it seems safe to assume that my max was always 306, but without buffs my attack was insuficient to realistically reach it. I suppose you could assume there is a cap I am hitting, but while I dont have logs, I do know things like Hand of Ro and killing greens has allowed me to hit for max even without strength and attack buffs.

It really comes down to semantics. You can say strength raises your max hit, and in cases be correct. But that is not really how it seems to work. A more accurate way of saying it may be that raising strength increases your odds of landing a max hit.
