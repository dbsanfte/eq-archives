There are alot of topics about the upcoming melee changes, but I wanted to start a post about things we should expect to see, a wish list of sorts, for the 3 melee classes.

What I'm looking for are suggestions/help for Warrior skills as well as Monk and Rogue skills. I assume that there will be some "general" skills that all 3 melee classes can learn, as well as class specific skills.

We know that some skills will be activated and others will require an event and then a twitch based player activation. We know that not all skills will work for all events, and that position will affect how effective the skill was (no specification that position will open any new skills)

Known Skills- Warrior
Stun
some sort of aggro dot (hate over few rounds, perhaps increased hate per combat hit?)
some sort of Mob AC decrease (makes it easier for the group to hit the NPC)



Skills we'd like to see:
Warrior
Activatable, ranged aggro - believe this is self explanatory

Some avoidance/mitigation effect, Event = Dual Wield check
While dual wielding, you can perform an "exceptional off hand strike" that when you twitch the button, prevents mob from Riposting for X seconds. This would help prevent spiky riposte dmg while dual wielding. Hopefully, while dual wielding, the skill goes off enough that the riposte dmg from dual wielding is reduced down to what it is using 2handers. This helps War DPS and aggro by itself, and may even give some warriors a reason to buy the Ambidexterity AA.

some sort of cripple affect, activated
akin to /shield in spirit but different in use. Skill is used on mob A, warrior flings himself in front of mob, reducing the mobs damage output but increasing warriors damage received. it can be 50/75 just like shield is, just without the akward /targetting issues. hell just call this shield and call it a day.

obviously some sort of rage effect, self haste

some sort of temporary stat increaser,

perhaps some sort of grapple/snare effect?

something that allows a check to dodge attacks from behind?

all warrior effects that generate aggro should be just as effective from all angles. being the king of combat should mean something.


Monks
I don't know much about monks. I would assume that most of their effects should be DPS increasing, and that they should be more effective from the rear than the front, perhaps 75% are rear attacks and 25% frontal? the 25% frontal being more about raising their AC/ability to take hits and less about raising their DPS? as long as the frontal ones do not suck completely or making them vastly superior at tanking I don't see a problem with it.

Probably most important monk skill which would give them a role again would be this:

Comatose, event = Feign Death (or rather, its only activatable from FD position, but it always activated, no need for an 'exceptional' FD)
wipes Hate list, costs a metric shit ton of Endurance similiar to Fading Memories.


Rogue

Rogue attacks should be primarily about raising their DPS. They should be most effective from the rear arc.

an example defensive skill for them:

Evasion, event= parry/riposte/dodge (ensures can only happen if rogue is being attacked)
if an exceptional dodge, parry, or riposte occurs (any of the 3, so more chances of occuring) then rogue can twitch click his evasion skill to reduce his hate by XXX amount.

example offensive skill:

impale, event = backstab
twitch button hit and gives chance for rogue to immediately backstab again (ungreys button). because backstab is on a timer this 'exceptional backstab' will not go off that often but due to RNG its possible for 3 or more backstabs in a row (extremely rare).


Anyway, please add your own thoughts about skills that Melees should get to even the playing field against all classes. remember Melees are unbalanced compared to Hybrids, which are weak compared to Casters. The end result should leave all classes balanced against each other, which requires melees to be brought up above where hybrids are now, not just "equal to."

Monks, as described by Scott Hartsman are supposed to be the most effective melee damage from "any angle."
