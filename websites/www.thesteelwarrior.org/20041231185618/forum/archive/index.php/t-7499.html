Sit aggro will pull a mezzed mob to the 'chanter when the mez breaks, even if the warrior has hate prior to breaking the mez. Casters should know how bad sit aggro is, by the time they're doing Hard LDoN missions. Ping-ponging off mob from tank to caster and back to tank is caused by the caster auto-standing when hit (which removes sit aggro and puts the warrior back at the top of the hate list).
Enchanters should stand during mez breaks; good enchanters will stand relatively near the mezzed mob during mez breaks, to minimize ping-ponging.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=112046#post112046

Base range of /shield is about 25, 30% more with Extended Shielding. Range with Extended Shielding is about 36. All measurements in "loc units".
Sassinak translates for us: "36 units is probably similar to the typical radius of an average LDON-type XP group (one third the range of CH). That will be much more useful than the default range of 20 or so."

Rampage list may be set when the mob first rampages, rather than when the mob is first engaged. Thus if the mob rampages after the MT attacks, but before the MT takes aggro, the MT may end up at the top of the rampage list, and the RT may end up second. Since mobs won't rampage on the person they're aggro'd on, the mob ends up rampaging on the RT as planned--until a tank switch, at which point the MT (who is still on the top of the rampage list) starts taking the rampages again.

###### Discussion, Opinion, General information:

Don't rush to the Planes to level up to 65, as you'll have a hard time tanking there, and you'll be stuck spending all of your 65+ time grinding there anyway. XP up to 65 in more fun zones that won't give XP when you're 65.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=111151#post111151

Berzerker DPS is not exciting. The original poster's question, about how much DPS is increased by the 2-hander offensive AA's, remains unanswered.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=111344#post111344

Combat Stability AA does not reduce max hit, just average damage.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=110874#post110874

It's two, two, two threads in one! First thread: importance of stats vs. AC/HP. Second thread: DPS vs. hate procs for holding aggro.
Thread 1:
DEX adds aggro (more procs), STA adds HP, AGI adds basically nothing, STR increases DPS slightly. Leave regen for the necros. ATK is more useful than stats, but much less important than HP/AC. CHA is essential for a raiding warrior.
80 AGI gives you 1% more avoided hits.
Some debate over the importance of resists, but most people say they aren't critical for the MT. Unless you don't have a bard.
Buffs make maxing stats easy, even with Planar Power. But maxed stats unbuffed are also useful, in that they free up buff slots. Don't worry about STR/DEX, because you'll have Focus up anyway.
Make sure you have 35% haste or better; haste is essential to aggro.
Thread 2:
There is MUCH debate on DPS vs. procs as the key to aggro. DPS weapons may have a small reliability advantage early in a fight, but hate procs are better for lucky snap aggro, and seem to win in the long term (i.e. after proc rates average out).
Haass says that hate procs >>>> DPS for holding aggro, and that ripostes are no longer a good reason for using two-handers instead of dual wielding. He's usually right about things.
Brabble says: "It's possible that an aug'd dps weapon might be a reasonable choice when, as Var points out, you know that the fight is going to last a very short time, since until the RNG decides to smile on the DW set up, a really good dps 2-hander will have the aggro advantage. Obviously, early procs on the DW would really lock down aggro, but those fights would be balanced by some number where the DW didn't fire off for a while. And of course, an Anger III proc on the dps weapon is nothing to sneeze at."
Brabble (who is very quotable) also says: "Given the number of variables here, I'm not sure how you could avoid comparing a bunch of apples to a bunch of oranges, some watermelons and a kitchen sink in this equation, let alone eliminate all variables from in-game fight situations."
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=106485#post106485

Don't ask "what's a good AC/HP at my level". Post a Magelo, and people will point out weak spots for you to improve.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=110543#post110543

No way to improve Taunt success rate. Lathen says: "One of the developers stopped by in the past few weeks and confirmed that the only factors which affect the success of taunt are 1) your skill level in taunt, 2) your level, and 3) the target's level."
Plumppoopsie says: " BARD Mez, Taunt (fail or success), incite, break.
CHANTER mez, Taunt (fail or success), incite, taunt if first failed, break (so if 2 fail in a row, the agro generated from the incite,will give you agro.)"
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=109964#post109964

Lots of different theories on which raid AA's to get first, but no consensus. Lots of people like Health of Target's Target, others like the +50 ATK to every melee in the raid, others like the +100 HP to everyone in the raid. They all sound good to me.
One point that was made: since *everyone* buys HoTT first, it might be better to buy something that fewer people choose, to minimize useless overlap.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=107598#post107598

There is no AC/HP number to be a "good tank". It depends on the level of content in question, and defensive AA's matter a lot more than a few hundred HP.
If you must have numbers, Sassinak says (all numbers are unbuffed):
"For LoY, I'd say 1200 AC and 4k hp.
For PoP tier 2 or easy LDON, I'd say 1300 AC and 5k hp.
For PoP tier 3, I'd say 1400 AC and 6k hp.
For PoP tier 4 or hard LDON or GoD tier 1, I'd say 1500 AC and 7k hp."
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=109568#post109568

You can only flurry once per round, and only off the primary hand. With maxed Flurry AA's, you flurry on slightly less than 3% of all combat rounds.
Flurry appears to happen only on rounds where you double-attack or triple-attack, and only on a percentage of those rounds. Raging Flurry may either be a straightforward increase in the chance to flurry, or an increase only in the chance to flurry following a triple attack. Sassinak reports that Flurry always adds two attacks, not sometimes adding one and sometimes adding two.
As per Sassinak's post (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showpost.php?p=109690&postcount=45), you can both triple-attack and Punishing Blade in the same round, without flurrying. You can also PB and flurry in the same combat round.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=105375#post105375

More discussion of "good" AC/HP. 1350/6000 unbuffed is more than enough to do hard LDoN. 1300/7000 buffed is enough to do hard LDoN's, according to Thraxnor. 1590/8200 buffed makes hard LDoN start seeming "easy".
10AC invisible-armor-slot LDoN augments are great (and cheap). The Loyalist Shield of Honor quest is good AC fast (50/75 on the back slot).
Tribute is a great way to get that last bit of AC/HP. 150 HP costs 120 tribute per hour, 47 AC costs something like 180 tribute per hour.
Editor: it's a 10-minute round trip, with slow zoning, from the Nexus stone to the tribute master and back. And it's not hard to find things with decent tribute values--try the LDoN armors that people make to practice smithing, or the Centi weapons. There may also be better deals I don't know about, but those are easily enough to make tribute a reasonable option.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=101851#post101851

"

###### Old News (things most people already knew, but some people clearly didn't, because they got asked again):

To raise archery: shoot at incoming mobs, shoot at other groups' mobs, shoot at the permarooted mob in Warslik's woods, bow-kite the named desert madman in South Ro, shoot the Nexus Scion (may not still work).
Hussar gives details: "Ssolet Dnaas in Warslicks woods. Loc is pos 4011, pos 400. He is lvl 60, so still very blue at 65. He is perma rooted. I just took a Bloody Ancille's Pouch and a bunch of Purified Crystal (the component, can be bought at any LDoN Magus). Capped archery and throwing fast. For throwing, I used Bracer of Hammerfal, which doesn't require a component."
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=97894#post97894

Ignore CHA (except when marketing) until top-end raiding (i.e. Crustacean armor is just fine).
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=111020#post111020

Making a Troll warrior: put points into CHA if you intend to raid, and pretty much anywhere if you don't. AGI over 75 is crucial. Some people like STA, but it's easy to max; some people like DEX, but you still don't proc all that much more. Editor: I put my Ogre warrior's points into DEX, and my only regret is that I didn't put them into CHA. For the fatty races, STR/STA are far too easy to max IMHO.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=110916#post110916

Warriors can't beat equivalent Knights in a duel. Just forget it.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=109646#post109646

LDoN difficulty level is based on the group's average level. Average level 63+ gives 51 points; average level less than 63 gives 34 points. Selecting "Hard" moves you up a difficulty level; "Hard" less than 63 == normal 63+. The break points are every 5 levels:
63 and higher
58 - 62.9
53 - 57.9
48 - 52.9
43 - 47.9
Level of dungeon is set before entering zone.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=110740#post110740

Warrior "innate 5% defensive bonus" is implemented as a 0.95 multiplier on the DI (Damage Interval) of the mob, reducing the DI component of each hit from the mob by 5%. Editor: but leaving the DB, Damage Bonus, component unchanged.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=111166#post111166

Finishing Blow is based on getting a critical hit on a fleeing mob. Caps at level 59, making it pretty useless. Editor: especially with OoW upping the level cap to 70.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=111506#post111506

Defensive is better for MTing high-DI mobs. Evasive is better for MT-ing high-DB mobs and mobs that proc a lot. Stonewall is better for XP groups because it preserves your DPS.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=108432#post108432

AGI is not exciting. Worry about it last.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=110266#post110266

More chattering about Earthshaker. Read it if you care. I don't.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=110279#post110279

Nobody really knows about stacking on Spellshield/etc. effects, but the +%skill modifiers cannot raise your skill above the "hard cap" of 252.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=104884#post104884

To make Magelo work right, update spell skills and charm percentages.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=7310

Damage shields have no aggro.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=7240

Ogre frontal stun resist is neither granted nor removed by illusions.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=7190

All skills cap at 252. Tradeskills thus cap at 240 if you have one of the +5% skill items.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=7179

Equipping a shield mitigates the damage taken with /shield. Multiple /shields on one person do not stack.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=7165

Lots of advice on keeping weapons skills up. Summary: get haste, get dex buffs, swap in low-skill weapons in groups when the fights are easy. Editor: or just find a pet caster with /pet hold, /duel, and leave your avatar beating on their pet overnight.
