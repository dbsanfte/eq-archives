Well maybe I should clarify:

My previous setup Vanazir+Ykesha aug was what I would call solid steady agro. If I kept attacking and kept in range of mob I could keep agro fine. There were however things I could not do; most notably drag mobs around with me.

With Anger3 augged Darkblade, once I get that first anger proc I know the mob is mine. I can drag it whereever I want it.

This is a huge benefit. For instance in undead trial the difference is very noticable. Common scenario: 4 skeletons spawn and rush some guy. I run over to the skeletens and taunt+attack one. With vanazir+ykesha I could stand there and hit for 5 seconds and then try to back off to drag it out of the crowd only to have it turn around and beat on the nearest person instead.

With darkblade in the same scenario, I run up there, taunt+attack. Then I just wait for the first anger/anger3 proc, once I get that I can now bring the skeleton with me whereever I want.

This knowing you have agro after a proc is really nice when you are concerned about placement and like to keep mobs separate.

Its possibly I could have achieved similar effect with Anger3(instead of ykesha) in vanazir, but I am not about to go back and re-aug it to find out :p
