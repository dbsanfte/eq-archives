I've been thinking about this, and I'm afraid I have to completely disagree. Forgive me if I was taking this statement out of context and it was in referance to a specific condition, but it looks to me like you feel this is in general the best way to do it.

Let's look at it on a case-by-case basis:

1) Incite is insufficient to gain aggro (someone is ahead of you by more than 400 points), but taunt succeeds. You go to the top of the heap +1. Net hate gain: highest + 1. (ie. exactly the same as before Incite came in, so essentially wasted endurance)

2) Incite is sufficient to gain aggro, taunt succeeds. Net hate gain: 401. You basically leapfrogged yourself (assuming this is possible, I don't believe it was determined one way or another), thus wasting taunt.

3) Incite is insufficient to gain aggro, taunt fails. Net hate gain: Who cares? The chanter/cleric/other-random-robe is paste.

Now, take the possibilities by using taunt first, then Incite:

1) Taunt succeeds. Net hate gain: highest + 401. You've taken the lead and given yourself a nice pad.

2) Taunt fails, Incite is sufficient to gain aggro. Net hate gain: 400. Hurray, Incite made up for our old inadequacy. ;)

3) Taunt fails, Incite is insufficient to gain aggro. Net aggo gain: once again, it doesn't matter because you are now a low-DPS monk until taunt recycles. :P

Feel free to point out anything that I've overlooked... but from where I stand, incite then taunt seems to have a good chance of wasting one skill or the other a majority of the time. Flipping them around seems to be using them to the most beneficial effect.
