Demorgoth
Can't find the link any more, but I believe it's fairly widespread accepted knowledge that SoE (Absor maybe?) stated specifically that taunt was hard-capped at 230 for all classes.

Either way, it's a direct mod to s specific skill. In the same way as +kick boots effect only the kick skill, a taunt mod would effect only the taunt skill. Meaning a very slight increase in success when you press the taunt hotkey, and nothing more. It would not add any raw hate to taunting, not would it effect hate generated by procs or anything else, just like a +kick mod wouldn't effect attack speed or mainhand dps. They're simply not connected in any way.

Ciae Redemption
All skills have a hard-coded cap of 252, so the 10% taunt mod would put you there (actually 253, if it weren't for the hard cap). What that taunt mod should theoretically do (based on some of the descriptions for taunt on this board) is increase taunt's success rate. And only that.

How much of an increase the mod provides, however, is anyone's guess. Most skill mods do very little; an example, from a cleric's perspective since I play one, would be the 8% mod to Specialize Alteration on the Elemental BP. All it does is reduce the mana cost of all alteration spells by a sliver: 1.8%.

I'd imagine your taunt mod basically does the same; increase the success rate of the specific skill, but by too low an amount to actually make a difference.
