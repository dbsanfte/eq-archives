Lets break it down to simple reality:

With Furious, any number of things could cause your back to be to the mob, be through fear, bad push, or simply turning a little too far on accident. Its not a safe, reliable discipline for staying alive. You cannot proc on a riposte, and if you use a 1h weapon, its pretty close to worthless. The only real purpose for Furious I know of is in GoD events, with only one group and requiring high damage mobs dying fast.

Now on to Fortitude. You don't gain a ton of ripostes. Boo hoo... You get full, 360, melee immunity. For anything involving 18+ people, its superior. Warrior DPS is not a meaningful raid factor, especially with tanking, but it is in one-group trials.

Edit: Yes, Fortitude also gives Rampage and Flurry immunity, while Furious won't. Yet another advantage.

Here's my rules:

If I am the MA, and the mob is being pulled, use FURIOUS. Extra swings are more aggro no matter how you look at it. A mob is not going to kill you from flurries alone in 12-18 secs, and your healers SHOULD already be healing you at that point, and you should already be in relative position as the mob is being pulled. In a controlled pull, furious is superiour, hands down.

If I am the RT, use FORTITUDE. No contest.

If I am in "Oh Shit" mode, I hit AETaunt (if needed) and then Fortitude. This is for when the cleric is late on a heal, the raid is wiping and I need to move some mobs for a cleric to get out, or in general something is just messed up.

I cannot really see any reason to not follow these rules, but I'm sure there's a few exceptions (I've only done ikkinz1 single group and EP raids)
