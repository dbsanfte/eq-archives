I do it on a 1.5GHz, 512ram, 128mb 5600 Ultra so it doesn't take a super machine to pull off. I normally don't have Luclin models on, and I turn off others hits, spell effects, and do /showname 1. I also turn down clip plane and sky if I need to but for the most part I have very little lag except when you'd expect it. I have run 3 accounts at once during the Fabled camps and didn't have much trouble with a decent amount of people crammed into a small space.

The toughest thing about playing on one box is that the cursor will freeze if you zone on one char and alt tab to the other. A way to avoid that is aiming your self at a zone then alttabbing to the other char so you zone in the background instance which keeps control of your cursor, but makes the zone take longer.

Also, obviously, everything happens faster on the focused instance so when loading up the second char, make sure to keep that instance focused or it will take forever to load.

I use EQwindows and 3 box regularly on one PC. Same advice as above, low detail, sound off, spells off, models to a minimum. Each EQ session will take around 300Mb, runs reasonably well. Always remember the "lag" people talk about on the boards isn't actually network lag, its lack of frame rate, with a fast enough graphics card most of it can be eliminated.

Also setup some hotkeys e.g. cleric

/target tankname
/pause 5
/cast 1 (CH for example)

You need the pause as it can take half a second to target.
