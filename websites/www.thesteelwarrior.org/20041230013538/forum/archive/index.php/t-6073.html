I'd say there are basically two things you can do for newbie armor.
1. Buy a mix of crafted and crusty armor (or anything else in that price range that gives you similar stats) in the bazaar; simply search by slot and and price and if some is available, it'll pop up. Either armor will last you for a while. Haven't priced the stuff for a long time and I play on Fennin Ro, but I would think you'll pay 100-200 pp per piece for most of it.
2. Create your warrior & do the newbie armor quests.
Although option 2 takes a lot longer (and probably only delays the crafted/crusty buying), it does offer a couple of advantages. First, it costs your main very little, if that's a concern. Second, by the time you finish/get sick of the newbie armor quests, you'll be used to the basic mechanics of what a warrior does, and if you don't already know the starting city, you will by the time you finish. You'll also know whether or not you want to keep playing him, probably.
I completely agree about picking either RZ or Tribunal as your deity. Cultural armor will eventually be your friend.
If you like doing tradeskills, warriors make decent smiths; if you want to do that, I'd consider making a human warrior, as they have a relatively reasonable cultural skill-up path in smithing that is not available to, for example, Barbarians.
Really, though, race doesn't matter much; the religion thing does, at least at the moment.
It sort of depends on how you are going to approach this warrior: if you want to level him fast and can afford to twink him a bit, pick an RZ or Tribunal worshipping race in a starting city you already know well, get him crusty/crafted, some relatively fast weapons in each category (1HS, 1HB, 2HS, 2HB, piercing), solo the newbie grounds, hit blackburrow at level 6 or so, then PC (bleh) then M Seru, then High Hold Keep (bleh) and by then you'll have sorted yourself out a bit.
If you're not so concerned with fast leveling but would like to see more of the game, then pick a race that can worship RZ or Tribunal in a city you don't know, and do the newbie quests and go forth to adventure.
I play a Barb; he was my first character and I picked him in complete ignorance, but I'm very happy with him.
