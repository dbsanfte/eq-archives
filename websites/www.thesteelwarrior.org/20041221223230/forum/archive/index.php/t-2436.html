Being that my bard is a main, I feel that I should explain how haste works properly:

Item haste
Spell/Song haste v1
Overhaste v2
Overhaste v3

On my bard, I can have haste from my item haste, 60% haste from Warsong of Zek, 30% MORE haste from Rizlona's(Vah Shir haste is in the same line, and doesnt stack) and 10% more haste from Ervaj line.

However, the Ervaj line tops out at an additional 10% haste...which is rather minicule, and that song twist slot is best served in nearly all cases by twisting another song, which is why you almost never see it being sung.

I find myself mostly twisting Haste, Haste v2 and wind of marr, as the extra regen and mana is FAR more efficient than a few more % haste.


Bottom line, you may have:

1)Item
2)Spell/Song (ONLY ONE)
3)Battlecry of the Vah Shir/Warsong of the Vah Shir/ Rizlona's Call of Flame
4)Melody of Ervaj/Composition of Ervaj

Any and all other forms of haste you may see in your buff box are simply providing a stat buff, or wasting a buff slot.


As for slow:

There is NO slow that stacks with ANY other slow.
ANY slow OVER-RIDES ALL haste, i.e. a mob hasted 50% and slowed 5% will hit at 95% of UNHASTED speed. Dont believe me, go to seb, tank a shammy frog for 1 minute unslowed, but make sure he is hasted, notice the damage taken, then slow him a few %, and notice that you take FAR less damage.

A mob hasted with Celerity 50% haste, and slowed 75% will hit at 16% of his hasted speed (25% of normal), not 75% of his normal.
