Nayl
Lately, I have found myself wondering more and more, "What the hell are they thinking?" when it comes to this game. Today I thought it through a bit more, and came to a conclusion - SoE is planning for EQ to fail, and they have shifted their resources and financial stake to EQ2.

I've seen the arguement many times now that EQ1 is the golden goose, and that SoE would never let it die. But I think the goose has a terminal disease, known as mudflation, and that SoE has recognized this, concluded that the game can't be effectively expanded much more than it already has been, and will not be able to compete with the new generation of games like WoW, CoH, Vanguard, etc..

Why do I think SoE has decided this? Because its the only conclusion that fits their otherwise inane behavior. Consider their recent moves:

1. Since the last full sized expansion, PoP, we have had LoY, aimed at new and casual players, LDoN, aimed at casual players, and GoD, which masquerades as a full sized expansion, but has less content and depth than any previous expansion, including LoY and LDoN. The focus is on retaining casual players, enticing new ones, and fooling the hardcore into hanging on just a bit longer.

2. The continuing moves to make the low end of the game easy and quick. New characters arrive with weapons equipped and almost a dotted line to follow from their starting point to 10th level. Paludal caverns insures they will quickly reach 25th, by which point they have started to form the social attachments that should keep them hooked into the game for a good long while to come. And even if that fails, LDoN will provide them with the kind of arcade style experience that this game has steadily devolved into.

3. The total evisceration of the CS program. Outsourcing to India, removal of server GMs, and a complete lack of any way to contact a living human about any CS matter in less than geologic time. Somewhere the calculus has been made that the percentage of disgruntled customers this loses < the money spent on retaining them by treating them like human beings. Long run, I think this will hurt their bottom line more than they expect, as it alienates their long term customers who can remember when EQ CS actually existed.

4. The New Graphics Engine. Right now its a buggy pita, but their aim is for it to ultimately consume less resources and require less CS. Here again they have miscalculated, as the monkeys left behind when they moved the real coders over to EQ2 have repeatedly made things worse, not better. If postings on boards like this are to be believed, a significant number of players have quit or gone into semi-retirement over these problems, having the outlandish expectation of basic playability from a five year old game.

5. Class balance pandering, and the lack of any coherent approach to it. The recent round of melee balancing was, I think, the last gasp attempt at actually fixing these issues. Fortunately for Warriors, their first pass at it did a lot to fix us. Unfortunately, the monks and rogues mostly lost out, as the dps improvements they should have gotten were largely lost in the now-defunct "openings" system. Endurance, in the end, is most significant in that we now get to rot augments for it that drop in GoD because of how utterly useless it is to have more of it.

Speaking of pandering, the current dev team reaction to the Paladin and Shadowknight complaints about becoming eunuchs in GoD is very instructive. Their proposal to "fix" the problem by having trials adjust to the classes entering them indicates two things - one, they didn't think any of this through beforehand, and two, they have no fucking idea how to actually fix the problem they have created. The people who might have a chance at coming up with a real fix are in the same place as the real coders - EQ2

6. GoD. I know I mentioned this in 1, but it really deserves its own paragraph. GoD is the personification of the big SoE lie, namely that EQ1 will just keep on keepin on. This one is close to home for me, my guild got its first Quarm kill less than a month before GoD opened. At first we figured that our timing couldn't have been better, since we had just finished the old content when the new content arrived. But it quickly became clear that GoD was not about more content. What its really about is making you jump through more hoops toward the illusion of more content. Just check the mobs you see as you move through Natimbi and Qinimi, the first two zones of the expansion. Because you will be seeing them ad-infinitum as you move further in.

Right now we are working on finishing the Ikkinz raids in Kod`Taz. No one knows why we are doing this, other than that it appears to be what we should be doing to progress. The loot tables here are extremely shallow, and we are seeing quite a few rots already. The stuff that doesnt rot is comparable to 4th and 5th tier Time, but difficulty is much higher. And as the weeks go by, we keep seeing changes to these raids to make them even more difficult. Add to this the fact that these raids tend to run 3 hours +, and the fun factor is at a all time low.

The overall effect on our guild is increasing attrition as people leave the game altogether, or migrate to the "most uber" tier of guilds that are currently having a more elite brand of un-fun one zone further into the rat maze. We are gradually being pushed in the direction of recruiting under-flagged folks from lower tier guilds, which will shortly result in us going back to killing elemental gods and minis for loot and flags for these folks. This isn't progression anymore, this is a treadmill with a painting of a carrot hung in front of it.

Well, its late, and I've rambled a bit. But my point is this - the fat lady is warmed up and ready. And if you think otherwise, you are only buying into SoE's feeble attempts to occlude the obvious - they are cutting us loose.

in seeing these hundreds of posts by people detailing their problems to tech support im noticing something. im noticing the techs are bewildered. they are asking constantly for dbg and dxdiag files and so on. they also are admitting that they dont know when many of these issues will be fixed but are obviously immersed in them.

it will take time. i can tell this because ive seen the enormous diversity of problems.
