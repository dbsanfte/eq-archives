John 3:16

No, it's not in reference to a computer virus. But this passage did get me into a philosophical frame of mind, once or twice. John 3:16 gets me thinking about a Tree and a Monkey.

A Tree?

The Tree of Life
== According to the writings, Man has tasted fruit from this Tree.
== Suppose this Tree is loosely related to a "decision-making tree"? i.e. Tree of Choice = Tree of Life?
== Each branch of the Tree could represent choices to be made in life.

But Khemo ... where'd the Monkey come from?

Introducing at least three aspects of every human being
== The Old Man (or Old Woman) ... we will affectionately refer to this aspect as "The Monkey"
== The Soul
== The New Man (or New Woman)

FIRST ASPECT -- "The Monkey"
== The Monkey is the "Id-Ego-Superego" that makes up the "You", the current perception of the self ... he/she is also the person everyone else sees
== One of the primary functions of the Monkey is to make choices and decisions. The Monkey makes choices by selecting different branches on the Tree to swing from
== The physical form of the Monkey is perhaps a temporary housing/vessel for the Soul?
== The Monkey is a descendant of those who have eaten fruit from the Tree
== Is the Monkey a glorified selection and sorting tool for the Soul's "final destination"?
== Being a creature of time and mortality, there is no "life after death" for the Monkey. Death obliterates the Monkey aspect. However, choices the Monkey has made in its short lifespan may have been imprinted somehow on the Soul.

SECOND ASPECT -- "The Soul"
== the bridge between the Monkey and the New Man/Woman
== the Monkey is vaguely aware of the presense of the Soul ... but the Soul is beyond the Monkey's meager senses to perceive with any certainty.
== The Soul is eternal, enjoying an existence beyond the concept of time ... it always will be and, perhaps, always was.
== The Soul requires a vessel to interact with a physical plane

THIRD ASPECT -- "The New Man/Woman"
== The New Man/Woman is NOT a descendant of a "taster" of the fruit from the Tree of Life. This new being is a brand new creation generated some time AFTER the passing of the Monkey.
== This new being is built to last ... he/she is imbued with the ability to survive eternally in any environment ... in a "life after death". A perfect new home for the Soul.
== After the New Man/Woman inherits the Soul from the Monkey, is this new being able to make decisions? If so, do these decisions appear to matter or change anything?
