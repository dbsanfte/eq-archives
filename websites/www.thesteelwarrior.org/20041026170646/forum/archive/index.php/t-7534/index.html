Graal the Dorf
In terms of parse duration, it's easy to get an accurate, long duration parse without having to worry about defensive. Turning on /disc defensive does a very specific, quantifiable thing. We can use our knowledge of what defensive does to figure out the difference between ANY warrior using defensive and not using defensive. The only point to parsing is to find 2 things: 1) average hit in terms of DI, and 2) the percentage of attacks that end up hitting the tank. Once you have those 2 pieces of information, you can figure out what the damage taken would be under defensive by simply modifying DI the exact same way defensive does.

Test Warrior - Crap gear - 1193 AC - No disc
Test Duration: 7:40
Total Damage: 78,561
DPS: 170.78
Hits: 307
Miss: 216
Hit%: 58.7
Max Hit: 496
Min Hit: 95
Avg Hit: 256

All you have to do is figure out the average hit in terms of DI and you can find out how much damage this tank would have taken under defensive.


DI=21.1
DB 74
Abg Hit: 256 (8.6*DI)

Under defensive that means his average hit would be

raw DI is 22.2
DI under def is 11.1
(11.1*8.6)+74=169

169/256=0.66

170.78*0.66=112 DPS under discipline *in that exact same situation* That is the difference that *would* have been manifested if he had hit defensive then.

EDIT: that 112 DPS number just happens to (coincidentally!) be very close to the reported DPS for when he actually did use defensive. You can basically take the modifed parse and average it with the actual defensive parse for an average of 114.66 DPS from a 13 minute, 48 second parse.
