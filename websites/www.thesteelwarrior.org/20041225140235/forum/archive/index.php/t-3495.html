I have no references for this it is just the way I believe it to have been coded. I know that both CA and CS 3 parse out to 10 % avoidance and 10% mitigation, and afaik LR and ID are 1% per lvl each, which means in the end you end up with:

CA/LR removes 15% of melee dmg directly when maxxed
CS/ID removes 15% of the dmg from successful hits, or about 12.5% of total possible damage, assuming that CA/LR is maxxed since the avoided CA/LR hits would have been reduced by CS/ID in some cases. You still get a 15% dmg removal on all the hits that arent covered by CA/LR but CS/IDs relative worth imho goes down the more CA/LR the tank has.

The inverse is not true though, CS/LR in no way alters the dmg removal from CA/LR.

Either way they both stack and they are both worth maxing.

I am pretty sure the formula for mob to hit chance is:

(MTHA)
-------------------------------------------------
(MHTA + avoidance * avoidance modifier)

where:
MHTA = mob 'to hit' ATK, usually something like level * 5, but affected by mob-specific bonuses, buffs and debuffs, etc.

avoidance is the avoidance AC reported for a character using the &debug=true magelo trick. Based on defence skill and agility, it ranges from 480 to 520 for level 65 warriors.

avoidance modifier is what you increase when you buy CA and LR. Note that these don't increase your displayed AC, but clearly do do something. The avoidance modifier is the figure mentioned in the aaxp text, e.g. CA3 is 10% bonus, so avoidance modifier = 1.1).

This formula:

goes smoothly form 0% to 100% chance based on relative mob power

produces results matching some old parses I did showing that a level 1 mob hit the back of a 50something melee 1% of the time, a level 4 4%, and a level 12 11%. For higher level mobs, the hit percentage trailed off behind the mob level.

If this formula is right, then the affect of CA and LR will vary depending on how much the mob was originally hitting you.

214/(214+500) = 0.30, 214/(214+550) = 0.28
400/(400+500) = 0.44, 400/(400+550) = 0.42
600/(600+500) = 0.56, 600/(600+550) = 0.52
800/(800+500) = 0.62, 800/(800+550) = 0.59
1000/(1000+500)=0.67, 1000/(1000+550) = 0.65

These look like plausible results, but I haven't seen a good parse of CA that removes the effects of dodges etc by having the mob attack from behind.

So in general, CA3 drops the mobs hit percentage by 2 or 3 percentage points. This has more effect on mobs with a low %chance to hit, in the best case dropping 10% off incoming DPS. It won't have any real effect on damage spikes.

Bottom line: CA is best when tanking blue or lower mobs. I suspect the opposite is true of CS.
