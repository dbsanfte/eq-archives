Jat5
Hopefully we can avoid the whole magic pj’s vs. a Buick strapped to my chest bs. It’s completely irrelevant. The only thing that matters is the mathematics behind each class.

The Monk class was once balanced upon the same mitigation table as warriors using items with significantly lower ac and a smaller hp pool. Meaning they could take a hit just as well as a tank class with crappy gear for a short period of time requiring more heals.

After a couple expansions worth of really stinkin poorly balanced itemization the monk class got too close to tank class ac / hp and started causing problems, for which they got smacked down. Unfortunately, itemization errors were corrected with a change in the classes core functionality that doesn’t scale fairly across levels, but it helped in the short term.

Now, with mobs hitting much harder and more reasonable itemization, you are looking at monks having ~ 1700 ac and ~ 6k hp’s compared to warriors with ~2200 ac and 9k hp, with knights weighing in around the ~2100 ac and ~7k hp mark. (eq rankings) It’s not surprising they would be complaining about getting the double whammy now that they’re back to the low ac / small hp pool situation once called balance with higher mitigation.

Who could take the damage better used to be important, now the main thing is who can honk the mob off faster. We know how that stands at the moment, adding monks into the mix means adding a class without spells or eb weapons and no taunt button, and as broken as it seems to be at times it still works when it works.

Knights and Warriors need some balance, Monks aren’t in the ballpark at the moment. If improved mitigation is on the table that’s fine as long as the ac / hp numbers give the advantage to tank classes. Avoidance would fit the class ideal better, keeping them squishy but streaky would be kinda tough to work out since avoiding a 600 hitter is better than mitigating one.

In the end, balancing 3 pure melee classes based on offense / defense is always going to leave one somewhere in the middle ground. Monks went from not quite as good to not nearly as good defensively and have remained not nearly as good offensively. Put that with the general melee problems and you’ve got a class with lots of venting to do.

All I can say is try and keep the discussions out of the flame pits, it does none of us any good.

Aggro is the main issue with Warriors. Improved aggro is needed and it currently looks like we're getting it through itemization. Something less subject to the RnG would be nice, but we'll take what we can get.

Itemization is the absolute worst way to deal with balance issues. As time has shown again and again, it just takes one or two stupid devs to mess up class balance.

Warriors need more class-specific flash abilities. Monks are nothing but flash. Rogues have backstab and genuinely useful non-combat abilities. Warriors are about as impressive as a brick wall. Admittedly, that's our class function, but we need a way to visably demonstrate just how good of a brick wall we are. Shield is cool, but it's too underused to make an impression on the player base. More hitpoints would be nice. Not because we need them (although the gap between warrior and Shadowknight/Paladin hitpoints is narrowing), but just because a massive number of hit points looks really impressive. Maybe a class-only upgrade to Planar Durability?

Maybe a Warrior-only improvement to critical strike? This has traditionally been one of our flash moves, but it's more or less been taken from us by giving the AA ability to all classes. I've even heard someone mention that Rangers crit more than any other class (I assume he meant with a bow, it would be really really sad if Rangers crit more than Warriors in melee). Maybe an innate class-ability aggro proc that we get around level 40 on every crit. Give it some sparklies and chat-box spam for high visability. Ok ... it's a bit corny, but we need some sort of class ability that combines utility with high visibility.

Yes, yes. Crits were ours first after all. :D Having our crits add extra aggro is a good idea. One thing I always wanted was a message others can see when Flurry and Rampage go off. I was very disappointed when I found out that only I would see the Flurry message. I was tempted to hold off on that until after defensive AAs just for that fact alone. I know it would be more spam, but there's a lot of spam already and many ways to filter it.

Hmm, how about an extra chance to crit when attacking from behind in addition to the extra aggro thing? This might seem to infringe on rogue territory, but I don't think so. Having a real penalty for turning your back on a warrior (above the mobs inability to dodge/riposte/parry, etc.) is one way to represent our role as a tank. Note that the extra aggro from crits would kick in and so it is not something that would really be a dps boost as the mob would feel the pain and turn back to the warrior.

I could see that being a potential problem on raids, with the non-MTs getting aggro when they don't want it, so maybe add this as a discipline or triggered AA? That might work well. You turn it on, indicating that you are going for the soft spots that cause a lot of pain, rather than just doing damage in attacking from behind, to get its attention, and the mob focusing on someone else allows you to be that precise with your attacks.

I never saw taunt as what its name implies. It might work that way for relatively dumb mobs, but an opponent that's supposed to be highly intelligent, whether some low level caster in a lvl 20 zone or a boss mob, is not going to say, "Oh, I better kill the warrior first because he insulted my parentage!" One part of a tank's ability to hold aggro is supposed to represent his/her ability to be a physical barrier between the mob and whoever he wants to kill at that moment. The other part is that the warrior convinces the mob that he/she is the most dangerous threat. The mob would not turn its back on someone wearing a lot of steel with lots of sharp pointy things to stick in it, for that would be a bad idea.

Anyway, just the 2 cents of an unwilling warrior retiree. **having to work for a living sux sometimes**
