For movement I use the alpha keys on the left side of the keyboard (a,s,d,w, and q,e for strafe). This keeps my left hand near my most often used hot buttons (2 thru 4 for me).

To swtich stances, I changed the default Ctrl-F1 for defensive to the numpad's '+' key, Ctrl-F2 for Battle to the numpad '-' key, and Ctrl-F3 for zerk to the numpad '*' key. This keeps my stance changes right by my mouse hand.

To switch weapons, I reconfigured the F9 thru F12 keys to activate sidebar hotbuttons where I have my weapons hot buttoned for switching. Its layed out like so:

F12 - 2h weap
F11 - main hand 1h
F10 - off hand 1h
F9 - shield

This puts me into any weapon config in 1 to 3 key presses. The only real pain is going from 1h+s to dual wield. I have to equip my 2h to unequip my shield, then equip my main hand, then my off. Its not perfect, but once you get used to it, it works pretty good and doesnt break when they upgrade the client (*cough* cosmos *cough*)

My left hand stays focused on movement and my main hot buttons while my right hand focuses on looking around, switching stances, and switching weapons.

So far this has worked very well for my style/tastes. ymmv.

For clickies or long cool down abilities/items, I use the mouse mainly to click on them.

My hotbuttons always seem to be shifting around, but it usually looks some thing like this.

1 - melee attack (mainly for visual indication that im in auto attack).
2 - rend
3 - sunder
4 - overpower/revenge (twitch key)
5 - hamstring/shield block

the rest vary so much its not even funny.

I also put the Shield Bash in a sidebar hotbutton and assign it to the numpad '0' key (numlock MUST be turned on for it to work). I've found that to help a lot when I see someone casting, its really easy for me to reach over with my mouse thumb and hit that big old key. =)
