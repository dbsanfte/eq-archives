This change was detailed in a Dev Journal (not a patch message.)

The exp given is based on two factors:

1) Average group level (and if you outside 5 levels of this level you do not get bonus exp.)

2) Your level compared to the mob (also if you are outside 5 levels you do not get bonus exp.)

The bonus was from 190 percent of "normal" exp to 290 percent of "normal" exp. (Kill a level 60 mob at level 65 and get 1.9 times normal mob exp, i.e 1.9* ZEM * mob level * mob level; kill a level 65 and get 2.9 * ...)

Now this being said, they ALSO change the whole system (but did not detail this change.) They changed the back end so that there can be mobs of the same level in the same zone killed by someone that gives DIFFERENT amounts of exp. So this is to say they can break the standard ZEM * Mob level * Mob level mode on a mob by mob basis. This is prominent on quest events that spawn massive numbers of mobs back to back (i.e. many elemental events.)

The old rules of how exp is distributed in a group (based on level of players) are still valid. By this I mean if you have a level 60 in a group with a level 65, the level 65 will get MORE exp each kill than if the level 60 were a level 65 player. It isn't much (1.5 percent more; (65+5)/((65+5)*4+60+5) compared to (65+5)/((65+5)*5) in a 6 person group with 60,65,65,65,65,65.) In this environment, even with losing a couple percent exp to the level 65's, the level 60 can gain on average 30 percent more exp killing in zones with mobs from a min of 60 to 65.
