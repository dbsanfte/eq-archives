I'm not sure it affects you at all, but the AC softcap figures in here.

Since I have no idea at all where the softcap begins, I picked an arbitrary AC goal - 2000AC and resolved that I wouldn't put 40HP for example in a slot where I could put 10AC until I exceeded that goal.

As I gained access to better gear and filled up all the slots I could with 10AC LDoN augs, I began looking at items and augs that carried above average AC and decent HP's. Probably the best example of this is my Platinum Cloak of War 40AC 185HP's with an Opaque Saphire of Bulwark 20AC on it. That's works out to a 60AC 185HP cloak.

Unless you are able to obtain the very best equipment in the game, I think that's a more rational approach. Choose an AC and HP goal, get all your 10AC and 40HP LDoN augs and as you upgrade try very hard NOT to sacrifice AC for HP's or vice versa. A 10 AC 125HP cloak sucks, and so does 20AC 80HP cloak. It's sorta immaterial trying to decide which sucks more.

I ran some numbers on the top 10 (in some cases the top 20) authentic mageloes for these levels: 55, 60, 65 and 70.

At level 55, if you sort by top AC, the average of the top 20 warriors is: 3.17 hitpoints/AC.
Sorted by hitpoints, the average of the top 10 lvl 55 warriors was 3.3 hps/AC.

Level 60
by AC, top 10: 3.36 hps/AC
by hitpoints, top 10: 3.56 hps/AC

Level 65
by AC, top 20: 4.22 hps/AC
by hitpoints, top 10: 4.43 hps/AC

Level 70
by AC, top 10: 4.42 hps/AC
by hitpoints, top 10: 6.75 hps/AC

There are some caveats here: for example, two of the level 55 warriors have over 120 AAs, so I'm pretty sure they're, uh, not typical. Also my sample size isn't the same for hitpoints and AC lists in a couple cases, mostly because I just got tired of using my calculator. The smaller samples in the "sort-by-hps" categories would, if anything, make the hps/AC higher for those figures than if I'd sampled the top 20.

Also, of course, the AC listed on a piece of gear gets a boost when you put it on a warrior, so a theoretical 10 AC/60 hp piece of armor will not result in a 1-to-6 ratio when you put it on.

At any rate, these warriors are, relatively speaking, adding hitpoints faster than AC as they go along. Even the warriors who are AC heavy are adding hitpoints faster than AC, which results in a trend toward a higher hp/AC ratio as warriors advance in level.

It might be that OoW is having some effect on the "spread" between the ratios for the level 70 warriors. If you run out and start slapping on the relatively high hitpoint gear from that expansion, you'll get higher hitpoint ratios than have come in other expansions. The other possibility is that I got tired and punched the wrong numbers into the calculator.
