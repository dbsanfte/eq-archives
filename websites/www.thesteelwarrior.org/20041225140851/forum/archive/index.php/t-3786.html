Could be as simple as having one "list", but one check uses highest value in the list, the other check using the order of the list. i.e. something akin to (but obviously entirely different in detail than) this:
player:hate
toon1:2511
toon2:4673
toon3:7596

Toon3 would (all other things being equal) get melee attacks or DDs/dots (i.e. has highest "hate"), but toon1 might get targeted for debuffs, etc (i.e. first on list). Same list, different checks, kinda like rampage. Nothing I've observered conflicts with one list being used, it's just that people (myself included in the past) seem to assume it's a SORTED list, which isn't necessarily true at all... and from a coding perspective almost certainly isn't.

Ever since the NPC spell casting revamp last year, mobs have been doing this kind of thing. Any kind of "crowd control" spell that the mob uses is typically cast upon someone who is NOT at the top of hate list. It doesn't mean you've lost aggro - in fact if you assist the mob you'll still get your warrior on target, even though the mob is temporarily casting on someone else. Bottom line: mobs like to root/snare/slow/dispel/debuff people who are lower on the hate list. Anyone who has spent much time in BOT, Tactics, Droga, Sebilis, Dalnir, etc. in the past year should be familiar with this.
