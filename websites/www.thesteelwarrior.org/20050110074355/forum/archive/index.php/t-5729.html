It's 'Lord' Yelinak, not Lady lol. Anyway, since the revamp he has guards on a ~20 min timer (I think) that you need to clear first. Also, if you can see Yelinak, then you are agro. Even though he is rooted, his agro range is huge. If you end up wiping, make sure that whoever camped out waits until he is at full health before logging back in or he will summon people and kill them one by one.

His AE is about 300dmg, cold based, and is a mana drain DoT. Make sure your clerics are out of range of the AE, if they are outside the gate they should be fine. He also procs a 400dd. I don't remember if he rampages, it's been a while since I took him down. Melee is triple or quad for 700, so pretty wimpy.

Watch the push! If you push him (which will happen) make sure you constantly call for a switch because if he gets in the water you are fucked. Clerics won't be able to heal anyone under water, and if they go into the water they will be in AE range.

Another thing that helped was all of our wizards had dragon's bane (2000 dmg wizard nuke, unresistable on dragons with more than 100k hp, cost of 2 peridot per cast, 400 mana, 4.5 sec recast if iirc). Long quest involving camping Myga in western wastes.

As for HP I think he was originally around 350k hp and then the revamp put him at 500k hp.

That's about all I can remember. It's been a while since I fought him.

Aurthur
02-17-2004, 03:08 PM
Just getting to him is some what frustrating... the Crusaders are such bitches

Just CotH passed the Crusaders...they don't drop anything worthwhile anyway.

Yelinak proc's a knockback on the MT somewhat randomly, so your MT will need to be ready to run back forward to re-acquire prox aggro. That means no Stonewall. The AE is timable, so if you don't trust ranges with the ramps, just have someone call it out 10 seconds prior and have everyone hide either in the water or outside the gate. The timer on Yel's guards isn't as short as 20 mins...so unless you completely putz around you shouldn't see respawn. The AE is also curable by RC, so it's a good idea to have an RC'er in every group just in case...and it will overwrite KEI and VoQ, so have an enchanter with ornate pants ready to run around if necessary for cleric groups.

Yelinak has a lot of hit points, and in the dozen or so times we've taken him down I've never seen any noticable push like one poster mentioned. The MT will need to have 2 extra buff slots open because most likely he'll have 2 effects on him from Yelinak, Mind Cloud and something else.

He doesn't hit all that hard, just relatively fast and is unslowable. Good idea is to have your debuffers debuff him, then the whole raid camp out to reset aggro, then log back in and immediately establish rampage tank order with arrows...then charge.

Good luck, it's a fun combat.

We do Yelinak routinely with 20 to 24 peeps. SOE, Lev, or DMF required due to ice floor. The many times I have tanked LY never saw any push.

When we did it, we ran everyone just inside the door, then immediatly left and right into the water (all levi'd) the healers went all the way around to the back corners on either side of the ledge where the click out is. The healers floating just above the water. all melee where on the bottom of the ramp on either side ready for the melee call in.
