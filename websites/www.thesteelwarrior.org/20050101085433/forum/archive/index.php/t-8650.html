Obviously it sucks because you don't use it, right? :rolleyes:

Radeon cards are actually pretty good, and until these last couple batches of new cards, it used to be if you were a serious gamer, you went with ATI. Nvidia finally caught up, and now that they're on par, its more of a personal preference based on card features and such.

Nvidia has pretty much guarenteed compatibility with all games, and last I heard the latest and greatest card(s) from them will feature DirectX 9.0c hardware features, which means more pretty effects or more effecient renderings of existing ones.

ATI was always about the raw power, and if you turned on AA/AF (Anti-Aliasing/Anisotropic Filtering), you'd get a minimal drop in FPS. ATI has always handled high resolutions and advanced effects better, but such performance comes at the cost of some stability. The drivers aren't the best around either, but they're improving. Nvidia cards start to choke when you up the resolution, or turn on stuff like AA/AF, which is another advantage that ATI cards have.
