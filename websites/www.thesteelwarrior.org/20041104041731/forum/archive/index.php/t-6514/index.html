richardkent
Frig,

You should keep on moving down in PoTactics to the pits. Basically, the room you were in, overlooks the pits. You can get down there the quickest if you levitate / invis and head to the far right or left sides (tunnels there). You can also work your way down to the pits the 'long' way by continuing past the room you were in and working down the hallway off of that room. It takes a little bit, but if you are bringing the DC pet from the zone-in area, you pretty much have to head down this way. So, it is pretty impossible to pull the pits to up top due to all the mobs in the way and their respawn cycle. Perhaps if you had a raid force you could train to them, but one group, no way.

Once in the pits, you keep the whole area clear and you have a chance of a named spawn. Also (I believe) just killing mobs (pigs perhaps?) results in a 'stampede' occurring. Basically a mass of level 65ish pigs spawn and make a sorta train through the tunnel areas inside the pit. If you fight in a pit corner, you will be fine as long as you aren't fighting right at the tunnel. A zonewide message occurs, something along the lines of 'you hear the pounding of hooves' and this is your clue to be on the lookout.

There is 'a stampeding piglet' inside the pack of pigs and he qualifies as a named that can drop ornate. I have no tactics on pulling him as I just avoid it alltogether.

I have not gotten any ornate off the named that I've spawned in the pits (Zelrin Morlock - 4 drops of war magus arrow guard) after roughly 20 hours in the pits, keeping pace with respawns and pretty much only 2 mobs up at any given time, due to the clearing pace.

Last but not least, keep in mind that the 'a young war boar' is DC'able by enchanters and druids.
Sassinak
In Tactics, it pays well to move down to the pit (or to one of the tunnels directly off of the pit) as a starting point. You can jump down and survive, if you don't lag badly while falling, but it is better to use levitate.

The easiest mobs in Tactics are in the bottom of the pit. The only prerequisite is crowd control since there are so many of them. It's better XP per hour than BOT.

The ogres in the surrounding hallways & balconies are the next harder mobs. Those are what you were killing on your first trip. They are nice for kiters, but are less efficient XP than the pit.

The elite/decorin/gindan/hindan ogres are the hardest. They are only found in the Zek wings and top floor. Avoid them unless you eat hard LDONs for lunch.

Qaladar
Anival the Blade does not spawn in the pit.

Simple way to pull the piglet is camp the south tunnel.

When you get the emote for stampede, everyone hug the walls. Have an enchanter mez the piglet as it runs by. Nothing else will aggro. Wait till all the boars go by (make sure they're all gone... theres a lot of them) then engage and kill.

Piglet can drop some ornate pieces as well as other decent drops (WMBS, Tactician's Bulwark, etc.).

Named in the pits random spawn... Enraged War Boar, Zelrin Morlock can also drop some ornate pieces and at least Zelrin, maybe Enraged too can drop glyphed runes.
