Much debate on AC vs. HP. Some say AC >>> HP until around 2khp. Some say 1 AC = 4 HP, 6 HP, or even 10 AC. Best advice from the thread, in your humble editor's opinion: choose a formula that works for you, and then *stick with it*. Don't try to eyeball each particular item in isolation. If you've decided that 1 AC = 4 HP, then that 3/100 Shaderock Amulet really is better than a 15/35 collar, and forget how miserable looking at that pathetic 3 AC makes you feel. If 1 AC = 10 HP, then the 30 AC Yellowfang Earring is enormously better than some 8/60; specifically, it's more than twice as good. Again, ignore the fact that it does *nothing* to push you above 7k, or 8k, or 9k, or what have you--if 1 AC = 10 HP, then the Yellowfang is *amazing*. But, people being what they are, most would choose the 15/35 collar and the 8/60 earring, for a total of 23/95, rather than the 3/100 and 30/0 for a total of 33/100. Pick a formula and stick to it, and you'll at least avoid that mistake. You may choose the 3/100 and the 8/60, for a total of 11/160; or the 15/35 and the 30/0, for a total of 45/35. Depends on whether you believe 1 AC = 4 HP or 1 AC = 10 HP. But at least you won't make the sub-optimal choice of preferring 23/95 to 33/100.
Some people say that you should get a certain minimum amount of HP (9k seems like a common comfort zone), and then get as much AC as you can while keeping above that minimum HP. Lets you max CHeal effectiveness with a bit of a buffer for error, and in situations where you would need to mitigate more (like bosses), you'll generally have lots of healing to patch you up and make mitigation-over-time less important.

Much debating back and forth about the effect of shields on the AC softcap. Fortunately, Kavhoc helps us out with one of his delightfully straightforward comments:
"The cap on AC in the Velious era wasn't a soft cap; it was a hard cap that had been there from day 1. After a certain point, which differed for each class, the benefit of more AC didn't just diminish - it dropped to nothing.
The change I referred to, just before PoP, changed that from a hard cap to a soft cap. You get a percentage of the amount over that soft cap. Shields increase both your total and your soft cap, making them more effective than any other item with equal AC. Your mitigation AAs, level, and class also affect the cap and the percentage return for AC over it.
Separate from this, there are diminishing returns if your AC is much greater than the NPC's attack. This is due to the nature of the formulas that produce the probability distributions that have been well documented on this board."
My God. It's full of stars.

In approximately 10-hour parses, WA1-5 gave the following increase in procs:
WA1: 7.83% increase / 2 AA = +3.92% per AA
WA2: 14.47% increase from base = 6.64% more / 4 AA = +1.66% per AA
WA3: 26.27% increase from base = 11.8% more / 6 AA = +1.97% per AA
WA4: 40.95% increase from base = 14.68% more / 8 AA = +1.835% per AA
WA5: 52.04% increase from base = 11.09% more / 10 AA = +1.109% per AA
So WA1 is clearly the best buy, and should be purchased immediately. WA2 is adequate, and WA3 and 4 are both solid. WA5 is expensive and bizarrely unrewarding, and should probably be postponed until late in your AA purchases.

The following are affected by ND/PD/PE:
1) Base hit points as calculated from your STA (including STA buffs and STA on your equipment)
2) tribute-based HP increases
3) +HP on your equipment
The following are not affected by ND/PD/PE:
1) buffs that directly add HP (Virtue, BSS, the HP part of Focus, etc.)
2) Sturdiness
The multipliers for ND/PD are:
ND level 1: +0.02
ND level 2: +0.03
ND level 3: +0.05
PD levels 1-3: +0.015/level
PE: +0.015, but only if you have at least ND1.
So if you have ND2 and PD1, you'll have a total bonus of .065, or a 6.5% increase in those parts of your HP that are affected by ND/PD.
Formula for total HP is thus:
(base HP + hp from all STA including buffs and equipment + equipment HP + tribute HP) * ND/PD/PE bonus + raw HP buffs.

Merc points out that DKP will put all/all gear into the hands of classes in the following order: BST, ROG/RNG, caster DPS, tanks. Also points out, though, that this problem is self-limiting; a guild whose MT's can't handle the next level of content due to a shortage of All/All items will just end up farming the current level until the tanks have those items. In fact, your friendly editor points out, a merit guild with its quick advancement will often move forward long before the current level is farmed out, leaving every class *but* the tanks under-geared. A DKP guild will progress more slowly, but ensure that everyone gets good gear, and that the farming is efficient (lots of killing at a level already mastered).
Most guilds seem to give 0% to 50% DKP to boxed toons.

Do Tactical Mastery and Strikethrough affect %chance to hit? They don't affect your "to hit" percentage, but they do affect (a little bit) how often your hits land. Since Tactical Mastery and Strikethrough don't stop the mob's riposte damage, just hit them through it, these aren't so great for warriors.
