On stuff that can reasonably one round kill you, HP is probably more useful than AC. If it was somehow possible to get say 20K HP but 1200 AC, then that'd obviossly be a bad idea, but it is simply not possible to end up with stats like that. Unless you decide to replace all your gear with leather all/all items you're still going to end up with a reasonable amount of AC for the given encounter. Also you will never be able to get so much AC that you can just expect mob to hit min every hit or even half of the time, and even if the latter was possible, you cannot guaranteed you won't die from a spike. On the other hand it is quite possible to get enough HP so you can never get one rounded.

On stuff that I feel that cannot easily one round me (which is everything except Xxeric and Keldoven right now), AC generally provides better savings in the long run. However one round doesn't just mean 'mob max X 4' because in practice such a round never happens. You sort of just have to experiment and look at how you die and see if it's really a one round kill or not. Xxeric is a very good example where HP > AC because you can't even get enough HPs to guaranteed to survive one round.

Of course shielding/avoidance work against each other.
((PS i know the numbers I use to illistrate are not right but the way it works is))


Avoidance works first, then Shielding.

Ex. of Shielding weakening of Avoidance:
Say a mob has an Hit of 1000.
a Person whose avoidance allows them to miss a hit
Person with Zero Shielding just saved 1000 points of damage
Person with Thirty Shielding just saved 700 damage

So obviously, the avoidance saves the person with no shielding more damage than it saves the person with Shielding. in essence, the shielding weakened the value of the avoidance because your not saving the full blow when avoidance fires your only saving the shielding reduced blow.

Ex of Avoidance weakening Shielding
Say a mob has a hit of 2000
A person with 30 Shielding would be taking 1400.

Now over a course of a fight that person gets hit 100 times. Thier shielding saved
them 60000 (600*100) damage

If that same person had avoidance, and avoided 2 of 100 hits. That person's shielding would only save them 58,800 (600 *98) damage

in essence, the avoidance weakened the benefit of the shielding because the mob missed sometimes which means the value of the shielding is gone.


Of course they still work together in that the sum of the two is better than either.
Mob Hit 500
Mob Normally Hits 100 times in a fight
Shielding 20 (save 100 per blow)
Avoidance say 5 extra misses per 100 hits

No effects: 500*100 = 50,000 damage taken
Shielding Only: 400 * 100 = 40,000 damage taken
Avoidance Only: 500 * 95 = 47,500 damage taken
Shielding + Avoid: 400 * 95 = 38,000 damage taken

No, Im not saying they are bad. But Im saying since they dont stack linearly
you would have to consider thier reduced effect when you consider how much AC and how much HPS they are worth giving up.

Take a simplistic example. Honed Trinket of Protection from Qvic that an xp group can get. it has relatively low AC, and relative low HPs, for someone in Qvic+ group. However, 2 shielding can be worth the AC/HP trade.

But it is a little more complicated when you look "full picture". IE if a tank had say some specific number of avoidance (say 50) then the 2 shielding might no longer be saving them enough to be worth the AC/HP loss of wearing that ear.

Or it might change thier own personal "value" rate
Like a person with Zero Avoidance might Value 1 shielding as 50 hps
and a person with 50 avoidance might value 1 shielding as 40 hps

Both Stack, Both are Good. But since there are really 4 variables AC, HPs, Avoidance (Both +Avoidance +Imp Dodge Types), Shielding.

((Of course you have the same kind of thinking with AC too. High AC reduces the value of Avoidance, Shielding.))

In the end, remembering that they dont stack linearly is only really important
to tilt your own tradeoff values when comparing gear on all 4 categories.
AC, Hps, Avoidance (motd or imp skills), and shielding are all win.

But you simply can't come up with 1 exact tradeoff amount because they dont stack linearly.
