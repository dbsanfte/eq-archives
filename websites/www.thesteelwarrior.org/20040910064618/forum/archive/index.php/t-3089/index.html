Frodlin7th
Having played around pretty much exclusively in the LDoN environment since it's release thus far, I'd like to take this opportunity to share my preliminary views on tanking and "class balance" in the arena of tanking in the environment of LDoN.

Normal Adventures:

Mobs do not hit very hard, nor do they represent a grave threat to groups in "normal" adventures. Furthermore, most "normal" adventures seem to be optimally done with multiple tanking rather than the older method of "Crowd Control, then kill 1 at a time".

It seems esier overall for a warrior to find a spot in an adventure group in LDoN than it did in PoP, because there appears to be a lesser resistance to multiple tank groups.

In guild based groups, which is all I've done (I fear an LDoN pickup group), guildies look for a "tank" and the one who shows up at the adventure site first and is the least burden on the group will be the one who gets the spot just about every time.

You want groups? Learn the fastest way to get to every wayfarer's camp, and you'll get em.

Get out your snarewhips boys! If you don't have a snarer, you're gonna need them, as mobs run early and they run fast in a couple of the themes and the aftermath is bloody indeed.

I have some concerns though, and that it with the balancing of mid 50's "asassinate" adventures. Bosses are a tad too tough in those adventures relative to player power, and require a more "fine tuned" group to succeed. A mob hitting a 55 player for the 500's is a significantly more difficult opponent than mobs hitting level 65 players for the 800's. While benefitting warriors with /disc defensive, nonetheless it isn't really 'balanced' with the rest of the expansion.

There's a couple more "class balancing" concerns that I currently have.

The first has to do with mobs low avoidance skills. I spent a bunch of AA's in "Tactical Mastery", and in LDoN it seems to be going for naught, as mobs have relatively pathetic avoidance skills, reducing a nice advantage I've had in tanking DPS.

The second has to do with Undead and SU3. I'm sorry but it is simply wrong for 2/3 of an expansion to undo what warriors have had as one of the sole things that separates them from their Paladin cousins, which is a DPS advantage, particularly while tanking. If mobs have low avoidance, mitigating the effectiveness of Tactical Mastery, and at the same time, a preponderance of Undead, playing to the strengths of Slay Undead 3, then I have a problem with it, as there's no "warrior friendly" alternative.

Just prior to the release of PoP, there was the whole proposed Slay Undead change, and this I fear is the reason they were considering implementing it, it had nothing to do with PoP but with THIS expansion, and had I KNOWN that there would be a situation like this in advance, I probably wouldn't have supported the positions of paladins at the time, where there would be no tanking advantage AND no DPS advantage, while there still being a major aggro advantage.

Hard Missions:

I'll withhold my judgement on these missions while they work out the changes on test.
