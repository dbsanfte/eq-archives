The best zones/areas for this kind of stuff :
-Low graphics intensity (for those with lesser computers
-Wide open spaces (preferably outdoor)
-Mobs with a high aggro radius who Assist each other
-Mobs run at similar speeds and stack well
-Striaght melee mobs
-Zones with low player traffic


Bad zones/areas:
-High graphics intensity (bad lag)
-Closed indoor zones with lots of obstacles (griegs)
-Mobs with low aggro radius and/or dont have a wide assist berth
-Mobs run at different speeds
-Caster mobs
-Zones with high traffic


The biggest problem often isnt the ability to aggro enough mobs ... it's the ability to safely clump them all together. If everything isnt very damn close, then you wont kill it all and/or it will kill you first. Casters are the devil.

One thing I find odd is that although I'm rampaging twice as many mobs vs a 4-keg turn-in, the named end up at 40% either way. Anyone have any idea why that would be? I tried facing the side walls of the cave, thinking they might be getting pushed too far away on the rampage, but that just left them close to beat on me sooner after the 'page. ;)
The simple answer is that you can not rampage/hit/proc on a dead mob.

So, what happens is, you kill all of the mobs by the (random number) 30th mob either way, so you while you are doing double the total damage, you don't actually proc any more because you doubled the number of mobs.

Basicly, the game creates a list of mobs to hit and proc on in order, so it hits the first mob, if you proc it calculates the damage, then goes to 2nd mob. Hit 2nd mob, calulates proc damage and goes to 3rd... gets to say 30th mob, procs and calculates damage, Kills all of the mobs that you have killed, tries to go to 31st, realizes 31st is dead and so on.

Hope that helps, and I still haven't figured out how it sets the order. Wondering if by agro, or perhaps by spawn time. haven't had time to play with it to figure that out =)
