More of a couple of things that are REALLY important to know about the zone rather than where the leet drops are and what not.

Starting with the most important thing: The zone in tunnel. This is perhaps the MOST dagerous place in the zone, the 3100+ hitting mobs not withstanding. The reason is there is a serise of deep alcoves along the tunnel that spawn mobs. Many people (including me, mty first time though) do not notice them. And if you are invis they will not agro. However the frist time someone visible runs down the tunnel, they will agro.

OoW non-named mobs don't hit particularly hard (probably no more than 600) but they can quad and are reletively fast attack. And darned near everything is sow speed or faster. A not inconsiderable number summon too. You will not go wrong to assume that everything can run you down and summon you. And if you are paricularly lucky you will find one that procs infected bite-- a 100/tick dot that lasts for 50 ticks--or a 5k dot.

So if things go bad in your group in WoS--your healer goes LD, is stil rezing, what have you--most of the time 1) you will not make it to the tunnel 2) if you make it to the tunnle you will not survive the extra 4 adds in it 3) if you survive all that and hit the zone line you will probaby die from the dot. Additionally, for some reason groups like being dragged to the ZI for rezzes and what not. So if you run for it--most likely you will just die tired and maybe train near dead folk at the ZI. Perhaps even your brother warriors who don't have invis to make the run up the tunnel.

So if things go bad-take it like hero. Minimize down time. Get everyone else out, pop defensive and have everyone else do their damnest to get out...BUT MAKE SURE THEY INVIS FIRST!

While others are calling it the new KC--really the zone in is more like the bridge in Lower Guk. We warriors are probably just no going to make it so we may as well make sure everyone else does.

Make sure, too, that you educate your groups about the mobs that spawn in the tunnel. And that casters can and should bind in WoS.

The second thing to know about the zone (and its high number of named) is that many folk come for the loot only. Make sure the loot rules and the target (ie XP or loot) of the group are known to everyone. I am still learning the zone, but I think most camps will have a shot at least 1 named if not more. Be clear on loot rules and focus.

Expect to be trained--and its probably by accident. Anyone who has pulled in WoS while there are more than just a couple of groups in it has probably, at least once, brought their pull to the wrong camp. It can even happen with named. I have been trained by a druid who had at least 2 mobs and 2 named--by accident.

My own experance has been incredibly variable there--sometimes with very little change in group composition. Murphy lives in this zone and at times you will not be able to get him off your back. Other times you will be able to grind for hours, get multipul named and begin to feel you are in your own instance. Good days here are particularly good, bad days mean a lot of time at your spawn point and idiots training the tunnel (see above).
Frostpaw
Wanted to add a couple things.

1. Due to the outdoor and open anture of the first half of the zone, pulls can be long. These mobs are very very fast, and can run you down at times. One type of mobs, the Bazu proc/cast something called Bazu Grasp that is basically a root. If you get hit with that far from the group, weigh your options of calling group to your aid or taking a death for the team. Decide if they have time to help you, or if it could just lead them out into the open and end up a total wipe due to adds or whatnot.

2. If at all possible get a tracker. So what else is new, hehe? Lots of nameds here that roam and spawn in random spots. Having an alert tracker can help increase the number of nameds you can grab.

3. Trash on the Nobles causeway side of the zone is Blue to 66. Mob level goes up if you go past the wall. Lots of mobs in the ruins past wall that are white and some yellows to 66. Nameds are yellow and red, and include some raid targets that you want to steer clear of as a single group.

4. Most things here do not see invis. However, there are a few nameds that do, one of which you pass very close to when heading through the wall on way to MPG. Also, the last part of the tunnel to MPG has some cubby holes in it that have see invis kyvs inside em. It is close enough to MPG zone that you can get agroed and still make it to MPG zone np. As always, call your trains.

5. Travel to and from. a Very easy run from PoD's in Draniks through Nobles Causeway if invised. I have yet to find anything in Nobles Causeway that sees invis. Im not saying there aren't any, but i have never seen one running to WoS. Also, could someone confirm there is a port spell for porting into WoS. Do not know details of how to get spell at this time.

Some of the mobs in the northern half of the zone hit pretty hard if they get a bad round in.

The Elite Nocs (PH's for the nameds) proc Chaos Claws, just like in GoD, and have a chance to proc on every hit. So it's possible to take a Quad round of 600 + 710 x 4 damage per chaos claws proc, which is in the 5200hp range in a round. You'd have to be extremely unlucky, but it's possible.

The Elite Kyvs are just mean and have a fairly high attack rating. Expect to get hit fairly hard before they're slowed.
