As a general rule if in my early 50s I picked up a one-time load of plat I would consider things in this order:
1. Haste
2. Weapons
3. small stuff (hang on, I'll explain)
4. Armor

In most cases, I'd look for something that could carry you to 65 without having to be replaced until you have access to at least some mobs that drop items you can use.
You also need to pay attention to opportunity cost. For example, 36% haste is better than 31% haste, but the difference might cost you 30K--even if you have 100K, that's problably not worth the armor upgrades you could get.
I put the weapons in there because you can afford the Droga set and it'll last you a good while. You buy them and you don't have to worry about your aggro set-up until you can loot better.
I listed small stuff next because some of it is pretty cheap: stat food, for example. Your one earring is pretty weak, so you might search the bazaar for anything cheap with AC.
You are right to be thinking cultural armor for the big slots. I assume that you realize that your face and back are relatively strong, your gothic, crusty and crafted are ready to be replaced.
To make cultural armor, a smith needs infused rings. Each ring requires a blue diamond; these rings are the most expensive part of the job. Bracers, collar, boots (heh, I think), and the visor all all one-ring items. Pauldroons, vambraces, gauntlets, girdle and helm are 2-ring items. Cloak, breastplace, greaves are 3-ring. Get yourself a list of the AC, hps, & stats of each item, then see what gets you the most bang for your buck given what you are wearing now. If you are in a guild, ask around to see if there's a smith who can do FH armor.
I wouldn't be in a huge hurry to spend the whole 100K before I took a good look around the bazaar and had a very good idea what your server's economy is like.
