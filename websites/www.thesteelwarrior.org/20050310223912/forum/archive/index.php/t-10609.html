Ok, the space thread has gotten me into a "sciency" mood.

So for those interested in this topic I will go over it a bit here as best I understand it and then I will put a link down below to the site where I got a lot of my info.

Antimatter is basically a different form of matter. In Einstien's famous formula E=MC^2, or in lamens terms the desk your computer is sitting on is actually pure energy stored in a material form. Antimatter is the key that when touching matter unlocks its hidden superenergetic properties. Just to give you an idea of the shear amount of power 1 gram is enough make some of the world's largest nuclear bombs look like a hicup.

1 kilogram is aprox 2.2lbs
1 kilogram is 1000 grams
1 gram = 0.00220462262 lbs

get the picture?

your desk probably has enough pent up energy to blow this world clean out its own arse and then some.

now i am sure your thinking wow what a great energy source (and yes its very clean energy). but the problem is that we have no known pockets of antimatter for us to use. we have to produce it. the process of producing it is essentially expending a similar amount of energy to make it.

aka, antimatter doesn't break the golden ratio. which is to say it cost more energy then it produces. but then no energy source known to man has broken that ratio. the simple fact is that oil, wood, etc are all energy productions that give off less useful energy then we used producing the energy in the first place.


Now you may be asking why the hell do we do it then?

the answer is we move energy around in conveniant forms, lighter, smaller, and more powerfull is better. So as you can see antimatter is potentially a great energy source. The only problem is that at the moment it takes quite a bit of upkeep cost containing the antimatter and preventing in from going poof BOOM. Luckily we have only been working with antimatter on a particle level thus far producing anti-protons and anti-electrons etc...

thats in depth enough for me you can check here (http://livefromcern.web.cern.ch/livefromcern/antimatter/) for the rest...

Antimatter exists as a synthesizable material - There's multiple laboratories that are capable of producing both positrons - anti electrons, and anti-protons. These can and have been stored for the short and mid-terms in magnetic containment units. Production in quantity is an entirely different matter, however. So is production and storage of anti-atoms: While a positron and an anti-proton can come together to make anti-hydrogen, this is an electrically neutral, and thus uncontainable, structure.

Currently, like many other objects of pure science, it's a matter of sheer expense that prevents development of anti-matter technology from the science.
