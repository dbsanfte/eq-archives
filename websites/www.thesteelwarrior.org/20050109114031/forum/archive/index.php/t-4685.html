Changed that one to 33% cost so now it compares as follows:

Assume a group that looks like war/monk/monk/rog/rog/cle.

DPS for this group is around 1000. For that 33% endurance the warrior would be contributing 100 dps for 18 seconds, or 1,800 damage.

Compare this to the personal Flurry one which would do an average of around 774 damage per use.

Flurry useable 5 times = 774*5 = 3870 damage.
Call to arms = 1,800*3 = 5400 damage.

So call of arms would be better with a really efficient group such as set out above, similar in a more average group setup, and worse if the warrior was the only melee in the group.

As far as endurance costs being too high, I worked backwards. Lets look at flurry again while adventuring.

Assuming similar rate of regen as mana (lets say 10 minutes from 0 to full) we have 774 damage for 3 minutes worth of regen. This is 6.5% DPS increase if that is all you used.

I don't think they really wanted this system to be a massive DPS upgrade for the melee classes, so that is what i was aiming for (5-7% dps increase if the melee went mostly all offensive dps abilities over the long term - adventuring etc, and 13-15% dps increase on raids if they only used offensive abilities and blew their endurance as fast as possible).

Also one element that has been lost from casters is choice. At high levels, casters no longer have to choose what to do. Spells have such cheap mana costs compared to their pool and regeneration, they can pretty much 'do it all'. They're only limited by casting time.

I think there should be an opportunity cost to useing a DPS ability. You should lose a certain amount of utility if you choose to go DPS abilities.

Take my comments as coming from a level 63 warrior who is LDoN/tier 3 pre-elemental, and is mostly an exp group warrior. I'm sure my take on this is very different than that of an elemental or time-enabled raiding warrior.

First off, the only thing I really like about the system you describe is "no openings". Beyond that, all the other stuff, and especially the sample abilities you've defined, are either too weak or too powerful; there's not a lot in the middle ground or compromise. For example...

-- Guard III: 33% innate damage absorption sounds great, but let's face it, it's overpowered. It wouldn't be if you could turn it on and off as needed and it ate up endurance, but having it be innate (which I'm assuming means passively on all the time, given it costs nothing to maintain) means you just gave every level 62+ warrior an additional 33% mitigation. If I think that's a lot, I'd think Sony would be having kittens with that level of additional mitigation.

-- Muscle Spam II: -35 ATK debuff for 18 seconds at level 63? It won't even feel to the mob like a mosquito bit them. By this time, druids can debuff upwards of -180 ATK, and that doesn't seem to do much to the typical tier 1-3 PoP or LDoN mob. Chanters and shamans probably do even better. -35 ATK isn't even a tickle, except perhaps to generate around the same miniscule amount of hate.

-- The recovery line looks like a bone thrown to the soloing warrior. It's only powerful if you're not moving around much, which means it's only good for downtime. The overwhelming majority of exp warriors (and all raid warriors) will have someone healing them, as well, so most of them won't take to "sitting and medding".

-- Vivication: 200 a tick regen for 5 ticks at level 53 is more powerful than the cleric's heal-over-time spell (180 a tick for 4 ticks; they don't get better till level 59). Yes, it costs a lot of end, but it's awful powerful, comparatively speaking. This again seems like a bone thrown to the ability of warriors to solo.

-- Lunging Bash: It's been a while since I was level 9, but I don't recall a 10 damage bash being that wonderfully powerful. In any case, it's certainly not worth ~25% of your endurance.

Anyway, you have some good ideas, but I think the details are too sketchy and need some moderate to heavy tuning. For someone like me, who exp's mostly and isn't end-game, a lot of this is not better than what SOE seems to be offering us with their system, albeit the "no openings" thing would truly rock. I can see how this would be a better system for end-game raiders, though.
