Generally the issue boils down to asking people to watch their aggro control.

Sadly, PoP has made knight MTs aware that with mobs hitting for hundreds every round, the only way to turn a groupful of ignorant Dreadlands graduates into a safe place to exp is to trivialise aggro control for them by spamming the mob with aggro spells, which is an area a warrior can't compete in. It's also, unfortunately, the most efficient way to get fast exp.

The result is that many people are now genuinely ignorant and/or incapable of learning how to control their aggro.

One thing which is rarely mentioned though: If there's another plate tank in the group, not casting spells much but they keep getting aggro with about the same frequency that your taunt button refreshes, you have a stealth taunter in your group.

Politely asking them not to taunt (and kick, if you see them kicking), once per fight, is the solution. They'll either stop, or get upset and leave the group which works just as well. If they say they're not taunting, keep asking and then when they make an issue of it tell them they're getting aggro with the exact frequency that the taunt button refreshes, and then continue asking them not to taunt.
