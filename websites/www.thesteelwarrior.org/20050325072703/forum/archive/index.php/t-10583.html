Well, the type 4 yeksha aug (slimestone fragment of shock, I think) is lore, so if that's the one you're thinking of, you can't have two.
Anger III out of Mir is not lore, but heh costs 1150 points.

The general answer to your question is, I think, this:
All the points you get for wins in LDoNs go into one pool. All your purchases draw from this pool.
But, to buy a specific item/augment at a camp, you must have earned enough points at that specific camp to "unlock" your ability to purchase it.
So, if the Augment of Greater D'oh! (procs Rage of Homer) comes from a Guk merchant for 100 points, you need to have earned 100 points in Guk to get your first one. If it's not lore, though, once you earn 100 points in Guk you can use the 1,000 points you've earned in Mir to buy 10 more.
If, on the other hand, you have 90 points in Guk and 1,000 in Mir, then no Homer for joo.

Why on earth did you want ykesha augs? Oh yes, you wanted to do more damage, hmm. I guess you understand that playing a war is the wrong class for dishing out damage. You might consider the anger2 aug from everfrost ldon. The recommended level for the aug will decrease all stats including damage for the stats on the weapon equipped until you reach that level.

Say for instance you equip an ldon ykesha aug and its recommended level 65 and you are 51. Your weapon that is equipped with it might have about 20% less stats. Soo, for a 10 damage weapon its now 8 damage. Say you have a 20 damage weapon, its now about 16 damage. Say you have a 50 damage weapon, its now 40 damage.

The reduction of stats is not so bad when you reach closer to the recommended level for the item. These augs (ykesha) make your weapon a recommended level weapon. If you want further ideas on how this effects you, go look up some weapons in the bazaar and see how recommended level 65 affects a weapon at your level.

I would suggest the ykesha 100dd and angerII augs. I had them both for awhile and found angerII was better aggro on both weapons. I thought that ykesha would be a nice damage aug and aggro, but the necros and wizards found my proc damage amusing at best. You may soon realize that you are no longer level 10 and everyone can deal out the damage. There are damage classes, with AA you can do some ok damage. The DPS classes with some AA do booku damage.

Get the copper/bloodsworn and put anger2 on em, you set. There is a nice anger proc weapon from the dragon in POI. Its a raid mob, drops cord hilted spike driver (no drop) and with a ykesha aug , youd get anger and damage and stun. That's the holy trinity of aggro procs.
