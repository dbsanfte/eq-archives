Frodlin7th
His "ae ramp" is more like Infuriate in that it hits a certain limited number of people.

Some people are still under the impression that there's only 2 types of rampage in EQ, single target and AE. The lack of understanding of rampage is sad, but I suppose kind of cool.

Here's a couple of things to keep in mind about Rampage:

1. Single Target: A list is kept based upon the order in which aggro was gained. Regular single target rampage will hit one additional person per rampage.

2. "AE Rampage": Kind of a misnomer. Instead of a linear "rampage" list where a single target is hit, this type of rampage simply hits everyone that's in range who's performed a hostile act on the mob or a benevolent act on someone who's performed a hostile act. (Casting a beneficial spell on someone on the rampage list is considered a hostile act). Standing near the mobm or "proximity aggro" is 0 hate, and thus does not place you on the rampage list or subject to being hit, thus making "AE Rampage" not really AE, but an unlimited multiple target rampage.

3. Limited Multi Person Rampage: Suled Dar's Shade, and any mob who "Infuriates" does this. An undisclosed number of persons on the rampage list get simultaneously hit on rampage, but it's not everyone on the rampage list. This is the one mistaken for "AE Rampage" but it's not. Infuriate has the benefit of giving advance warning prior to it happenning.
