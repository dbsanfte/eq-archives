Well, I have been working through 20+ people in our guild for getting PoS/PoV flagged, and I swear by AoE (Area of Effect) groups.

Here is my group makeup:
1) 65 Warrior (me)
2) 65 Cleric (my wife)
3) 65 Enchanter
4) Wizard/Mage
5) Wizard/Mage
6) Anyone on Norrath who wants to be flagged :)

So far this group has not failed a single time.

We all get into a small group in the center of the Exe room, with me sitting down. When the mobs pop, they all run to me from sit aggro. The enchanter chain stuns, while the AoEers nuke. My job is to target the named mobs first, the rocks second, and the fiends last.

With this group, the hardest part is the first mob in each wave. Once the first mob in each wave is dead, the second, third and fourth are low on HP's from the AoE and I finish them off one by one.

The key is to target fast and keep all the mobs in AoE range during the wave.

On our last trial run, just for fun, I did not hit Defensive on the Exe, my wife asked me if I was defensive, and I told her yes. She was sweating wondering if the Exe had been beefed up in some way, and I just kept laughing.

I finally got thru the trials last week with a pickup group(first person in my small family guild flagged), so it is doable(group makeup was 61 war, 62 cleric, 65 ench, 62 shaman 2 62 mages), if you are patient and willing to accept
alot of failures. From what I have seen, for your role as the tank what you need to do is this.

1) Buy DS potions, both you and the enchanter at least should use them.

2) Target fast. I moved the select closest NPC key to the delete key on my keyboard, so it was right by the arrow keys and handy for me. I would switch targets actually before mob had completely dropped, unless it was close. Let the pet's finish it off.

3) Have a "I am slowed" hotkey. Make sure a priest has a cure up when you go in.

4) Don't panic. If your enchanter is AE tashing and AE stunning, he is going to get hit much more than you. Cleric should know that and be prepared. Nothing you can do to keep him getting hit, so let it happen. I did not get my first in fight heal until we where on prime executioner, but with all the chain stunning, and therefor mobs not hitting anyone much, cleric was at 95 % mana once we got there.

5) And do not get discouraged. I took a month off playing my Warrior due to shear frustration with trying to get flagged. I came back and third try in first trials group got thru. Only one person in the group was over 62(the enchanter), the group included a shaman(who are not normally considered a DPS/good trial class), only the cleric and enchanter where already flagged and it was not even close, in fact it was easy.

Most of the(many) failures I had came from either poor coordination with the group(takes a couple tries to get it right), not targetting fast enough(simply cannot afford lost seconds of DPS), bad luck(4 rocks in one wave pounding the enchanter to a mush) and groups without enough DPS.

One tactic we found that worked quite well was to have a decently geared cleric and a shaman with at least Torpor.

Load up damage shield on the cleric. Cleric sits on horse near the tank and pops a high agro heal on him. The 3 MOBs not being tanked turn on cleric. Shaman torpors the cleric and the cleric (if needed) can drop a divarb and/or group heal.

Normally the cleric only needed torpor really, cause stuff is dying very fast and we were always assured that the last couple MOBs already took 15-25% damage from DSes.

This completely eliminates the need for a chanter, and in fact when we used a chanter, we normally had them nuke with insanity instead of doing any normal chanterly duties.

1 war
1 rog
1 druid
1 cleric
1 Shaman
1 anything

With that set up, we were often bored by wave 2 or 3 because it was just too simple.

I will say tho, that most were mid 60's and we maximised our buffing. Melee running 1500+ attack and tank at 7500 hp or so. Also the druid is maxxed nuke crits, tossing out the 1550 nuke.

But.. it was SOO easy, I imagine a lower level group could still do it fine.
