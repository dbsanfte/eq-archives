IMO anyone who is not in a raid guild should focus on getting enough HP and AA to tank high risk adventures. Once you can successfully complete high risk adventures you will have a hard time going back to the easy ones. Not only do they have some very nice loot for people who are not in raiding guilds, but the adventures are a lot more fun because they are actually a challenge. I suppose it is fun to pull mobs in bunches in the easy dungeons, but eventually that will get boring and you will want to do the hard dungeons anyway.

Personally, my warrior is only doing easy adventures until I can get him enough AA to handle the hard ones. The loot, exp, and AP are much better than the easy adventures.

And before anyone says that you can do easy ones faster, keep in mind that there is a 40 minute minimum so at best you can only do 2 easy ones in the time it takes to do 1 hard one (if you use all of the time). Once you get good at doing the high risk adventures, you can usually complete them in 60-70 minutes or 1.5-1.75 AP per minute as compared to a best possible 1.275 AP per minute for normal risk dungeons. As long as you can complete a high risk faster than 80 minutes, they offer more AP per minute spent in the dungeon.

I would continue along the defensive path in order to make hard adventures a better possibility. Like Draganetti said, if you plan to stay in easy dungeons then go ahead and do offensive. If you plan to move on, get the defensive AAs, you're going to need them.

11-04-2003, 02:48 AM
LDoN mobs hit weak enough that you probably don't have to worry too much about defense, unless you get multiples.

On Hard things have so high of an attack that it's unlikely your defense will have any effect on them to be meaningful, at least for the lower end crowd.

On LDoN I think offense is more important than defense. First of all these missions do use up every bit of DPS you can have (assuming you're not in a powerful group just bulldozing everything). Second defense in LDoN is relatively easy but aggro is NOT. The mobs are just harder to aggro by any method in general (even Knights, though it doesn't affect them nearly as much). While no one knows for sure if more offensive AAs helps aggro defensive AAs certainly do not help aggro whatsoever. At the very least you should have Stalwart Endurance because able to resist stun helps a lot with aggro, especially initial aggro, since you getting stunned as a mob incoming is almost a guaranteed way for the slower to get ganked.

I can't imagine doing LDoN without AE Taunt. There's a lot of times I wished I had Hastened Instigation too. 30% faster AE taunt means you can use it roughly 40% more often, and it seems like there's always a situation to use for AE Taunt, even on normal (though that's more just a good idea in principle. Even if the mobs can't kill my party, I like to know that I can still get aggro if it's needed). I still don't recommend getting Hastened Instigation but if you do LDoN a lot, it is certainly a viable choice.
