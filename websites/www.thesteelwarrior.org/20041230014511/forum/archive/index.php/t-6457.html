60% haste would take a 20 delay weapon to 14 delay.

Here's how it works:

100% haste doubles the amount of attacks you make which means that your weapon delay is cut in half.
Half of 20 delay is 10
60% of 10 is 6
20 - 6 = 14

To get 12.5 delay you would need 75% haste.

Edit:

Yes, with a 20 delay weapon you will swing 30 times per minute with your main hand.

60/2 = 30
60/3 = 20

I wouldn't look at it in swings per minute though as most mobs die in about 30 seconds or less in xp groups.

2nd edit: (I really need to answer questions in one post :D)

If a mob has a delay of 30 and you cast 75% slow on it then it generates 75% less attacks on you.

30 delay * 1.75 = 52.5 delay

So where as haste will double your amount of attacks at 100%, slow will halve your number of attacks at 100% (don't think it's possible to slow more than 75% at this time)

I'm afraid I don't agree with your numbers, Payn.

You say that a 20 delay weapon, 60% hasted, is 14 delay (that is, swings every 1.4 seconds). Your formula is (delay - ((delay / 2) * (haste/100))).
20 - ((10/2) * (60/100))
20 - (10 * .6)
20 - 6
14

I say that a 20 delay weapon, 60% hasted, is 12.5 delay (that is, swings every 1.25 seconds). My formula is (delay / (1 + (haste/100))).
20 / (1 + (60/100))
20 / (1 + .6)
20 / 1.6
12.5

Well, let's look at that. A 20 delay weapon, unhasted, swings 30 times per minute. With 60% haste, it should swing 48 times per minute (30 * 1.6).

48 swings per minute is 1.25 seconds per swing, or a delay of 12.5. Not 1.4 seconds per swing, or a delay of 14, as predicted by your formula. A delay of 14, in fact, with a swing every 1.4 seconds, gives only 42.9 swings per minute, or a haste of only 43%.

Look at it a different way. Your formula can be simplified mathematically as follows:
delay - ((delay / 2) * (haste/100))
delay - (delay * haste)/(200)
((200 * delay) - (delay * haste))/200
(delay * 200 - delay * haste)/200
(delay * (200-haste))/200

Notice that at haste = 0, this gives the original delay (as expected). And that at haste = 100, this gives 1/2 the original delay (also as expected). But at haste = 200, this gives a weapon with zero delay.

200% haste does not make your weapon have zero delay. It makes your weapon have 1/3 the delay it originally had, so you swing 200% more.

Now, plug 200% haste into my formula. (delay / (1 + (haste/100)))

delay / 3

Which is exactly as predicted. I believe the original poster had the correct formula.
