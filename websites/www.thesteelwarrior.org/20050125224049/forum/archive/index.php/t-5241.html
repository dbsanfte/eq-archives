>Originally posted by EmiliaEQ
>>Now 36% is a lot better than 28%, but with ench haste, that difference is
>>minimal.
>8% is a large fraction, which I would not consider minimal, personally.

Mmmm. Careful, there. 8% greater item haste does not mean 8% greater damage. You've got your base damage (100%), your enchanter haste (68%), and then your item haste (28% vs. 36%). Total damage is 196% vs. 204% of base damage.

Although this may be wrong, the sources I was able to find indicate that the haste cap (modulo bard songs) for level 65 is still 100% haste. So it's actually "Total damage is 196% vs. 200% of base damage."

Which is only a 2% increase in damage. While noticeable, it's certainly not the 8% you mentioned.

Note that this (the haste cap) also implies that a 32% haste item is just as good as a 41% haste item in any group containing a 65 enchanter. Kind of useful to know, given how much easier it is to get a 32% haste item than a 41% haste item for us non-raiding types.

Sony has always had a bizarre relationship with haste. Think about the weapons that were top of the line when the CoF was new. 7/28 with a proc? From Nag himself, a 9/25 piercer with a summoned-only proc? 9/25?

Consider the difference in DPS between a rogue wielding a 9/25 and a rogue wielding a 17/19. It's not just twice the damage or three times the damage.

Now imagine a haste item that would provide the same advantage. A 120% haste item? Maybe 130% haste?

Availability for haste items is bizarre, too. 9/25 piercers or better now drop regularly off Luclin trash mobs. Imagine if 36% haste did the same. Nobody would bother killing Vox and Nag at all, because there'd be no market for their loot.

Haste items have not been subject to mudflation because Sony has not used them as the pull to make people buy a new expansion. They've used new spells, new AAs, new levels, new weapons. But they haven't used haste. Mudflation happens for two reasons: an economy where no money or items ever leave, and SOE's need to have each expansion be slightly more powerful than the last to draw players in.

If Sony ever needs to sell an expansion that nobody wants to buy, all they'll have to do is put 36% haste, no drop items on trash mobs at the zone-in. They'll sell one copy of the expansion per active EQ account.

I hope they never get that desperate. It's nice to have at least one kind of item in the game that's still a challenge to acquire. On the other hand...it's more than a little silly to have the best haste you'll ever need dropping from mobs that I could solo without breaking a sweat.
