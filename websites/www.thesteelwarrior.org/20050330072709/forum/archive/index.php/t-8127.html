Find a monk (I prefer them to bards, they often pull faster).

Outside that, it really depends on your theme and a person's pulling skills. I have been pulling for 65 levels with my warrior, from old Kunark dungeons to GoD. I trust my pulling abilities over most non-FD classes. (and over quite a few FD pullers who use FD as a crutch for sloppy pulling technique).

The enchanter, ranger, and shaman should all be able to do a better job than a warrior though. Ranger pulling with snare and root should work fairly well if there are 2-3 mobs. If there are three, the Ranger snares, runs back to camp with adds, tank peels one add off to fight, enchanter mezzes the first add, then mezzes the snared mob when it mosies into camp. Ranger uses WS to buy time as necessary.

Shaman can use root, slow, and ae slow to pull, although I would not recommend this unless the shaman has had a lot of practice.

A decent enchnater makes a pretty good puller, and I have seen some tear up Takish using AE spells. But an average chanter usually just sucks at pulling hard.

When I pull hard ldon, and the group is a bit suspect on the CC or heals or slowing, I usually pretarget the adds with a hotkey saying "Please assist to Mezz %t on Inc". If its alot of adds, sometimes I ask the people who can root/and or snare to "Please assist to Root %t on Inc" on the other mobs. The whole key is to delay the mobs a bit so the chanter can lock them all down, you don't need singles, you just need to control the amount of dps on the tank at the start of the fight. Unless both tanks are geared to the gills, I would not recommend off-tanking much because it just eats up healing mana. Better to ghetto CC.

Btw, two warriors trading off MA with defensive, and using /shield on each other before the mob is slowed, and in between defensive refreshing, can make most LDON hard trivial.
