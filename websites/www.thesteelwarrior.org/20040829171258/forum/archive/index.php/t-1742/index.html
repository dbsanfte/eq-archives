vova
How about making Berserk more meaningful? Besides a STR boost and a better chance to land crippling blows, it should do more.

Here's the proposal:

[] Form of Implimentation: AA Skill with three levels

[] Name: "Taunt of the Berserker"

[] Conditions for Activation: Can ONLY be activated when the warrior is "Berserk" (below 40% health) and the skill refresh time has elapsed. Activated by hitting the "Taunt of the Berserker" hotkey which refreshes at the same rate as the regular Taunt hotkey and works as a regular tuant whe a warrior is NOT berserk.

[] Prerequisites: This should be a PoP or Class ability.

[] Effect level 1: add 250 hate to warrior's hate level per successful taunt while Berserk
[] Effect level 2: add 500 hate to warrior's hate level per successful taunt while Berserk
[] Effect level 3: add 800 hate to warrior's hate level per successful taunt while Berserk



Comments

1. It updates the now out-dated and nearly meaningless "Berserk" skill.

2. It addresses the XP warrior's shortcomings vs. knights. It would be meaningful in EXP groups where a warrior can be kept berserk for longer -- improving a warrior's balance vs. knights in XP group settings.

3. It is meaningful in raid settings but the difficulty of using it rises in proportion to the DPS of the adversary. That is, it scales with encounters.

4. It comes with RISK and TRADEOFF: To use this skill, a warrior will need to make a judgement call about the use of taunt. If a warrior does a poor job of timing his hitpoint fluctuations or if healers are overzealous, he ends up with a regular old taunt and may have just created an issue if someone steals aggro right after his regular old taunt. People will get eaten for 6 seconds. If he manages to get a tuant off at below 40% health, he gets a nice hate bonus.

5. It increases interaction between classes on a raid. In order to use this skill MORE, heal rotations would need to be designed with more care on some mobs so that the warrior isn't simply fluctuating between 90% and 100% health. On other mobs (Reparm, for instance) it'll simply be a matter of luck. Besides the repercussions for clerics, it'll also add value to the AETaunt skill, making "Top of the List +1 hate" a bit more meaningful.

6. It should be tied to AA because 800 hate is a number of entirely different scale when applied to a level 35 warrior vs. a level 60 warrior.

7. It's a boon for everyone who creates aggro on a raid. It's not JUST a boost to warriors, it's a boost for everyone who relies on a warrior to get and hold aggro.

As someone who has to prioritize development requests on software projects, I know that the smaller projects that tweak issues with existing systems are often more attractive prospects for development than hastily devised and far-reaching changes.

IN other words, I think it's important to be realistic about requests and win one battle at a time.

Very simple. It's more taunt. Most of the suggestions have to do with taunt. This is one way to give warriors MORE of it but also impose trade-offs that will be attractive to designers from a balance perspective.

I believe that this would be a very sizable amount of added taunt.

AT the low end, in your average xp-group mob in PoV, a warrior's going into berserk about one or twice a minute. With the right AAs and good timing, he's going to be able to add the equivalent of one extra EB proc per minute on average, a significant boost to the aggro of the average non-EP warrior.

At the high-end XP group setting in a camp in PoEarth of PoFire, warriors hit berserk more often and therefore have more chances to add an extra 750 hate. He needs it because his xp group partners are also much more capable of dishing out damage.

In raid settings, the new skill scales with the difficulty of the encounter and adds value to the skilled warrior who uses taunt judiciously.
