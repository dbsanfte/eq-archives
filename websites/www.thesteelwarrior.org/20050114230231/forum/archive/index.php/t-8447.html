Most are slowable but some are not. For the non slowable ones, It can be a challanging fight. I had around 8kish HP and 1540 AC and almost died twice. Fortitude was the lifesaver both times.

Best thing you can do in a high-difficulty group, and one of the main things that separates skilled groups from unskilled, is talking to your cleric. If your cleric is decent, then with the wide damage spikes of Gates mobs, your cleric's healing strategy will always be getting on-the-fly adjustments. Saying little shit like 'unslowable' when an abandoned worker (Sewers) or 'hits hard + ramp' when a noc is inc in mountains means your healer isn't going to be surprised when you take damage faster than normal. Eventually a cleric will learn what mobs need adjustments for, but it takes a few mobs (and a few surprises) if the cleric has never been there before. Then there's weird mobs like tipt cragbeasts, where you'll kill a dozen or so of them and they'll all do normal damage, then get one that does double or more than that for the entire fight, for no reason at all - not hasted, not buffed, nothing.

I've done sewer2 with nothing but a paladin healing (well overgeared, but still possible), quite easily. Sewers are really varied, and Plant is by far the hardest.
