It is time to break out the Sscaley parse, from this (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=2259) thread in The Library. The following quotes are from there:

Which is to say, that going from ID0 to ID5 (on Valorian Guards in HoH) will net you an average 15hp/hit mitigation - which is roughly 15/2.87 = 5.2% decrease in damage taken. Given the linearity of ID cost, I'd wager a round of beer that it's intended to give you ~1% of damage per skill level (or ~4% for an increase from DI1 to DI5, for comparison).

Going from LR1 to LR5 will increase your avoidance by 3%, from 32% misses to 35% misses. Miss+Hit at LR1 is 85.6% of the swings, while Miss+Hit at LR5 is 85.8% of swings. 32% of 85.6% is 37.4% of damage avoided while 35% of 85.8% is 40.8% of damage avoided - or about a 4%-point increase in damage avoided.

From this little test, I would hypothesise that ID and LR are about the same efficiency in reducing total damage taken - however, LR will make you get hit less which can affect any innate proc related additional damage/stuns.

IIRC, CA3 has about an equal effect as LR5, and CS3 has about an equal effect as ID5. CA and CS cost 12 points each to get that effect, while LR and ID cost 15 points. You will want them all, so order is not hugely important, but if you are a min/max type, probably the best order is CA3, CS3, LR5, ID5.

As always, all hail Sscaley for his awesome work giving us this information!

01-19-2004, 11:23 AM
It's not quite so cut and dry, Ooga. Yeah, you may avoid that extra 950 hit.. but chances are, you didn't avoid that 950 hit, you avoided that 250 hit. And the next hit you took was 600 instead of 950. Rather than being straight off the top, both AAs are adjustments to the bell-curve, shifting their peaks favorably for us. Because they're equal shifts in the peaks, they, over a time-weighted average, cause the same reduction in incoming damage.

Let me try to explain it this way:
A mob would have made 100 successful hits on you, for an average of 100 damage each. Thus, you take 100 x 100 = 10,000 damage. With the 15% total of CA 3 and LR 5, he makes instead 85 sucessful attacks for an average of 100 damage each, for 85 x 100 = 8,500. With the 15 % of CS3 and ID 5, his average attack drops from 100 to 85, meaning he hits 100 x 85= 8,500 damage. 8500 = 8500, thus in the absence of the marginal effects of stuns, procs, and channeling, the two AA are equal, for any class, in terms of damage reduction. In any case, both these shift averages, not the actual numbers.
