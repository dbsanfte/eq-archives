Had a good guide written and accidently lost it. :-/

Get a map of NToV, I'm running off memory, but it should be fairly good.

Ok, your NoN KoS Mage can run into NToV, up to the corner right before Aaryonar. The hatchlings are Non KoS, the bigger wyvern guards are, Ikitar and Eashen are. Might use an SoS rogue to scout out and help the mage get there.

CoH your non-kos, get a decent force there, then get your KoS in and kill the hatchlings. At this point, for a new guild, I'd recommend pulling Eashen and Ikitar. You got a good corner if you can kill em faster then hatchling respawn right there, and should build confidence.

TIME THE HATCHLING REPSAWN. I think it's 27 miniutes, but have someone knowing when it will happen again.

Then do an about face, put the clerics on the stiars leading to Eashen/Iki, kill the hatchlings, kill roy's guards, kill the hatchlings, kill Roy. (Roy is Aaryonar). Positioning is important, you need Roy to never AE your casters.

Oh, minor point- At one point, maybe still, Roy's AE was a bit buggy. The client would think it went through walls, the server would not. That meant casters could get hit by it (on the client) and lose all their mana. Get your casters cheap insta clicks (Kromzek survery scopes, telescopes, etc) if they don't have. Clicking one of those forces the client to update with the server, and will get your mana back, but ONLY if the "buggy" AE hit them, not the real one.

Does that make sense? Here it is simple. Casters get insta click. If you get hit by AE and lose all your mana, click it. You might get mana back.

Ok, again on Roy, I dunno how Pacification, etc, will change this, but when we did it, it was very hard to get Roy w/o his guards, or get his guards w/o Roy. There are two Wyverns near the bottom of his steps, with an hour repop, easy to get w/o Roy. There are two more up near Roy, with a 3 day? repop. Try to get them single. If you get Roy as an add, I highly recommend putting a tank on Roy, and concentrate on the named Wyvern(s). Once you kill the two of them, you can recover from the wipe and get Roy single, and that's a lot easier, IMO. Your Mileage May Vary.

Kill Roy, drop down the back steps, and end up in a safe "pit". This is an excellent spot to camp if it's now too late for your raid. You are just about to start the easy part of NToV.

Either now or the next day, start by clearing to the right. You are looking for Lord Feshlak, he's perma rooted. Again, watch for Flurry Drakes, a Green Flurry drake is, IMO, the most likely thing to wipe your raid ATM, including the named fights.

Once you clear to Feshlak, you should be able to use Incite to build godly amounts of aggro before you engage. Casters can hide from his AE on a ledge to the right.

Next you clear to and pull Dargarn. We usually put him where the casters were on Feshlak, and put the casters where Feshlak was.

Continue clearing right, for another rooted named, Lord Kriezlak, or Special K. He has ice leading up to him, watch the slip and slide. Casters, IIRC, just stay up the ramp a bit to advoid AE.

Continue to the right, you should be moving around the circle and heading North, then West at this point. Get to a 4way. To your left (South) is Vulak Trigger. Stay out. Ahead (west) of your is Lady M (Rooted) and Lord Koi (Not). To the right (North) is the Old PoM zone. Dunno if still there, don't play with any little wierd looking things. Pull Koi's guards, and Koi. Pin Koi near a 4 way corner, with casters around the bend- I usually put my casters in the North Tunnel, and Koi in the West.

Move up to Lady M, she is in a little cubby. Really obvious place to put the casters, and important, she silences.

Back to 4 way to move North, turn right, keep going straight. Your target now is Jorlegg. Pull him to a corner, finish him.

This ends the Easy Section of NToV, really. For a new raid, this has been a long day, you can camp in Lady N's room up ahead, if you move into a little cubby on the right, out of sight of Lady N. You'll see what I mean, it's right where Jorl spawns. There are other safe spots, some might be better, this is just what I used to use.

Next day, or same day if you are really motivated and moving along at a good pace, I'd recommend going for the Triplets. These are Cekenar, Zlexinak, and... uhm.... Sevalak? Check the AE's, these are not an easy pull, etc. Hopefully your Morale is high from the previous section of the raid. You should get at least one of the Triplets, even first time, but I forgot which is the easy one. :-)

Can also try Lady N. Rooted, does a long spinstun. Annoying Fight. Remember, this is the hard part of NToV, don't be discouraged if you don't win first time. You already are more powerfull as a guild, can always come back next week and do the rest again. For Lady N, the safe spot I mentioned earlier is a GREAT place to camp a pally or necro or something for CR.

The other one I left out is Lord Vyemm. IMO, Lord Vyemm and Lady N are the two toughest fights in the normal NToV clear. Vyemm does a wierd knockback that will have your careening off walls. DON'T let him face the lava, or that's were half your raid will be.

What else? Melee Range to cast spells. Watch out for Flurry (AKA, Rape) Drakes. Don't get frustated on Roy and give up, the Feshlak to Jorll segment is easy. It's almost like they said "Well, we can't put 7 dragons worth of loot on Roy, but we can put 7 easy dragons after Roy..."

Don't get discouraged when you wipe. How you handle wipes will detirmine if you are NToV material or not. When you wipe, the first question that comes to your mind should not be "Who went wrong?" but "What went wrong?"

AE got the casters? Find a new place to put them or the mob. AE Ate the melee? Find some way to better their resists, or heal the AE damage. MA died? Find him some more HP, or AC, or tighten up the cheal chain. Clerics Ran OOM? Find em some Mana Preservation, or more clerics, or more FT gear, or Up your DPS so the fight doesn't last as long. Got adds during pull? Find a way to control them, off tanking, kiting, killing the add, eating the wipe, recovering and trying again with the add dead, whatever.

If you can do that, and can, as a guild, not mind eating the deaths to learn that sort of stuff, there is nothing in EQ that can stop you.

Just a few minor things to add: If it's blue, white, or a chromodrac, make sure enchanters and bards are actively manadraining it. While depriving the druid and cleric mobs of their mana will increase the DPS of the drake or wurm in question, gating mobs suck. Period. Facing down two Rape Drakes and a dozen assorted lesser drakes and wurms, while fun, will teach you a new meaning for pain, uniquely unduplicated in my experience.

Another hazard I note unmentioned is the blue drake proc. All blue drakes in WToV and NToV proc a dot called 'Thunder Blast'. To a pure melee, it's just another some hundred point a tick dot, but to your casters, it's a hellish manadrain. Keep these off your healers and slowers at all costs. Further, always be ready to change who's doing the actual tanking, though assist can remain one person. Slime Mist on a warrior or Thunder Blast on a knight cripples aggro generation abilities, and even with RC3, curing them is hit and miss.

Speaking of Rape Drakes, they hurt. Alot. Worse, they always seem to come in packs. Take all your 60+ warriors. Put them in a rotation. Warrior 1 to the first Rape Drake. Warrior 2 to the second. Warrior 3 to the third, and so on, until you close the loop. Why the rigamarole? You want defensive on every last one of these. As a 7800 HP SK, I die once for every two Rape Drakes I tank successfully. As a tank, these are one of the game's major peaks. The tank will die on this one. Aggro loss is a -huge- prossibility. An AE Taunter or a suicide knight is a smart thing to have pre-arranged, you really don't want these loose on your casters.

Depending on how your guild breaks, you may also have to deal with rampage wyverns, which are only really fun in mass pulls.

Good luck. While NToV's much easier now, it's still a pretty potent testing ground for your abilities as a raid force. It's the challenge that makes it fun.
