No, I'm not overly serious about this, but I did have a stray thought which might produce some interesting changes to the paladin agro things (no comment on shadowknights, however, as I don't play one).

Oddly enough, its one that wouldn't affect warriors at all, although a lot of other classes would have screaming fits.

The big complaint about paladin stuns is that Cease can be used to generate a good chunk of agro and with KEI up, the mana can be regenerated in just one tick. Very nice if you have KEI. If you don't have KEI, its another matter entirely, and the mana useage pretty much requires the constant use of Cease to keep from putting too heavy of a drain on mana. I have a 57 paladin main, and while I'll be the first to admit my gear isn't the most optimal (its actually somewhat subpar right now, imo), but I suspect even better equipped paladins would need to keep an eye on their mana pool.

(and for the record, I love Cease, not only because its easy on the mana pool, but also because when I pull, I want to generate a nice bit of agro without leaving the mob standing out in the open any longer than necessary - which is a problem with Stun)

The thing that seperates KEI from all of the previous group mana spells isn't the amount of mana regeneration (although that is pretty high). Clarity II has over 75% of the mana regeneration. What makes KEI different from the other spells is the duration. The other clarity spells only last for a half hour. KEI lasts for over two (iirc).

One possible solution?

Kill the duration on KEI. Maybe not bring it down to a half an hour, but a lot less than the current number.

Of course, the problem with that is that over half the player population would scream bloody murder, but I think it is an interesting solution to the problem (although knights with lots of FT won't be hit nearly as hard) - even if it does have only the proverbial snowball's chance of coming about.
