Couple helper ideas for ya-

-use clicky snare items to build up good aggro on the pull, might allow you to use a good 2h if you have one (to reduce ripo dmg). Snare glands or sceptres are best.

-2x shrunken skele form seems to be the smallest player size obtainable for warriors (only smaller I know of is ench minor illusion), very good for cornering mobs. Note- AoW is a tall mob, and distance is checked from your toon's "chest" to it's "chest" (i.e. where arrows hit it)- AE taunt checks z-axis, so have backup tank(s) lev'ed if they are shrunk, so they will be "in range".

-Divine Intervention works; use it if it's available, have your clerics read up about it over at one of the cleric forums.

-Big damage shields help a ton, especially if you are low on dps; he's gonna hit you a LOT more than you are gonna hit him.

-Watch out for enrage. ;)

RdT.

This mob is extreamly simple if you have decent healers with a tight Cheal Chain. The reason he is so simple is he only hits one person if you position him correctly. The following tips will make this encounter tivial.

First make a group with 5 clerics and a necro that can feed the group. This should be the Cheal chain group. Make a second group of your extra clerics/druids/shamans they will patch heal during the fight depending on how well your Cheal chain is doing.

Second make a group with your MT and 2nd, 3rd, and 4th tanks and a Enchanter that can project Illusion wood elf. You may need a shrink class in this group if non of your tanks have a shrink item.

Third explain to your guild what max melee range is!! Nothing can mess you up more than a idoit that turns AoW around so he starts reposting your DPS classes to death.

Turn your tanks into Wood Elves and shrink then as much as you can.

Now just have a monk or ranger with WS pull it to corner and then once the Cheal chain starts on you AE taunt take argo and establish a decent amount of argo before calling in your DPS. Once DPS is in makes sure they fight at Max melee range and this will be a very easy fight.
