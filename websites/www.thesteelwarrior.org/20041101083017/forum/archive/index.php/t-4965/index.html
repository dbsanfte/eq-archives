Ok, goes a bit like this when choosing weapons :

Potential Damage + procs + augment

Now, what most should know about procs while dualwielding, is that offhand have much lower proc rate, half of what the primary hand have as far as I know.

So a dualwield setup will not proc twice as much, nor have much higher damage potential, thus in some cases a 2H will be better aggro, depending on what wepaons you have available, especially in the case of GSoT since that is actually a pretty nice weapon (with horrible graphics though).

On the other hand, there's more potential in the dualwield setup, for the simple reason that with Two Anger proc augments, there's a higher chance of getting a proc earlier, which means a lot .. and not only in terms of snap aggro.

You do, however, take more Riposte damage as has been mentioned, and it is more costly to keep 2 weapon slots upgraded than just one.

When you look at proc rates for an individual, the DEX is irrelevant because the proc rates will still have the same ratio whether or not you are at max DEX or not. Unless you are weilding one of the very few high proc rate weapons, you can assume that at max DEX a 2h weapon will proc 2 times/min and with a 1h set up, your mainhand will proc 2 times/min and offhand will proc 1 time/min giving you a ratio of 2:3. If you have less than max DEX, you will get a lower proc rate, but you will still have the same 2:3 proc rate when comparing 2h v. 1h set ups. Basically, your 1h set up is going to proc 50% more than your 2h set up.

The Gsot would be expected to proc at twice the rate of the main hand 15 to 31 delay (following the scaling proc therom).The GSoT would have more procs per swings because it swings less, however, you will still end up with 2 procs per minute. The reason 1h weapons have an advantage when it comes to procs is because there is an additional weapon proc'ing. Offhand weapons tend to proc 1 time per minute in addition to the 2 times per minute of the mainhand. That translates to a DW set up proc'ing 3 times per minute while a 2h set up will be 2 times per minute.

Personally, I used both options at different times, depending on the situation. As a 63 warrior, with no AA's other than Run3/Regen1, and no Ykesha/Anger augs, here's how I see it.

I felt that the dual wield combo gave better long run agro, particularly against blue con mobs. That means for most xp grinds, I preferred the CHoS/SotB for agro. I would find myself losing agro too often using the GSoT in LDoN's, especially if I got a dry spell without any procs. The dual wield combo seems to proc with more frequency, and the agro was noticeably greater in my opinion with this setup in LDoN's. My GSoT is bagged 100% of the time in LDoN, and xp situations outside of higher tier PoP.

However, I've recently begun hunting in HoH basement, Drunder Pit, and PoV golems, and personally I felt the 2H was better for several reasons. First, all the mobs where I'm hunting are extremely upset by slows it seems. I found that these even/yellow con mobs were less likely to turn on the slower if I used a 2H at the beginning of the fight instead of dual wield. Can't explain why other than it just seemed to be safer for them if I used the 2H. In addition, ripostes from these mobs are at the point that they are painful (potentially 500+ damamge) and I feel the 2H is worthwhile to use to decrease this damage output. It is possible that the level difference comes into play here as well.

Recently though, I have begun using a Greatstaff of the Four Winds (41/31 75DD proc) as my 2H solution. It started as my DPS weapon for when I wasn't tanking, but on a trip to PoV golems I gave it a try, and the agro it generated was surprising. It easily outagroed my GSoT (unless I got a lucky streak of procs with the GSoT, which didn't happen often) and was a better solution than dual wielding in this situation due to heavy riposte damage. I realize it's not fair to compare this weapon with the bargain that the GSoT is, but it sure was eye-opening to see how much agro can be generated from a fast, high damage weapon even without agro procs.

I think a smart warrior works out what works best for them and when. If you can't afford (or can't camp) the CHoS/SotB, I certainly think the GSoT is going to be fine for you. However, as I mentioned, I personally feel agro is better in LDoN's and other xp situations when I dual wield. In these situations, I am not satisfied with the GSoT's performance compared to the CHoS/SotB. One solution is not vastly greater than the other, though both options have pro's and con's that makes having both available worth it.
