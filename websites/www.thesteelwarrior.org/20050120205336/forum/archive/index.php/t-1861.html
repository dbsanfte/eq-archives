Like others said, you dont need 140 or more people. There's very few things you cant do (pre-PoP) with 4 groups of high level, highly skilled people..or less.

The most important things are; a good leader, a disciplined team, and levels. Having uber gear helps, but can be overcome if you have these three things going for you. And with these things, the gear will come eventually as your accomplishments grow.

And a lot of it starts with the leader, since there seem to be very few good leaders out there who can hold a guild together a long time.

A good leader has to be successful. Nobody is going to follow a leader that leads them into failure after failure. He has to set goals and accomplish them time and time again.

A good leader has to willingly and happily sacrifice his own needs for those of the guild. He knows the more he gives to the guild (in time, loot, whatever), the stronger they become.

A good leader does not pull rank on his members, unless absolutely necessary. If such need arises, it should be done privately whenver possible - not in front of the team. This is probably the hardest thing for most leaders to learn and practice. If a member challenges the leader openly during a raid, then it must be dealt with immediately and harshly. If a member challenges the leader openly at any other time, the leader must let him have their say. If the leader is truly a good leader, he will not need to defend himself because his team will defend him.

A leader can, and sometimes must, be a tyrant in action. But if the leader is benevolent and generous off the field, he will earn his team's trust and loyalty and they can forgive and will come to understand his actions on the field.

Here's what I'd say, which probably has been covered.

1. Develope your core. Don't go heavly recruiting now. Take your core of people and work on gearing up in Kael/PoG. Arena can be done with one group, even pre60. PoG is a two group sort of thing for lower levels. Work on developing that core group of people who. Try to stage leveling events, like go into an old, under used dungeon, and have groups sit at camps for a time. The more you work on develping your core of people, the more others on the fringe will see, and want to be part of. That will inturn cause them to level/AA to be with part of that group.

This will increase the size of your core, and your ability to take on new targets. To help facilate that, have some folks level up some tracking alts. PL to 39 can be done in a few days. At 39, ranger track is pretty good, and they will be able to find if/when mobs are up. If you diversify your targets, then you will see a lot more interst in raiding and advancement.

2. After you have gotten your core out there, and if you have a good rep, you will start to see a lot of outside guild folk, and unguildied people wanting to raid with you and apply. When you get to this point, make sure you have a good system for recruitment. In my guild you need a number of member and officer votes to be accepted, and those aren't increidbly easy to get. An applicant must also be sponsered by a member as well, therefor, it's the members responsiblity for the applicant. We have a high degree of accountabilty in our recruitment process, and it has given us a group of wonderful people who work well together, and are a riot to be with =) /hug guild

You'll see yourself grow and as you grow, your raid targets will get better, and your members gear. Don't forget the importance of xping though. As has been said, levels are the most important upgrade a guild can have. Level 65's can do alot more things than a level 60 can do, spells aside.

Now that you've gotten to this point, remember this very important rule, have fun. If you are able to keep your members happy, and having fun, you have done yourself and your guild a huge favor. My guild isn't really a raiding guild, even though we do it often, and usually extrememly well, we are a social/family guild first. We have a lot of people who love to tradeskill and chat and hang out more than raid and level. We also have people who are more hard core, who raid all the time if they can, and xp as much as they can for AAs. We are still able to fully fuction as a group even with these diverse wants from the players. I /salute my officers for the job they do. Set your style and stick to it. We've had folks leave for more uber raiding guilds, but we've also had some of those folks come back to us as well.

One last point of perspective, how I think about my guild. If I wasn't in my guild, then I would have probably quit some time ago. I love the folks in Sword of Fate like family. Our guildleader have a yearly get together that really reinforces that to me. I would never leave my guild, unless there was a horrible, fundimental break down of leadership, membership, and the quality of a group we are. If I ever did leave SoF, I'd more than likly quit in a month or too. The friendships I've made are vastly more important than any loot item.

If you can have your members truly feel like your guild is your home. If you can have new members come in, and they feel like your guild is their home. Then you have created a really wonderful guild, and something to be proud of. With time, and some good leadership, you will be able to over come many encounters. Those encounters will be made all the sweeter because you do it with your friends.

Sorry for the long sappy post, but that's what I'm in, and it has worked incredibly well. As a group of friends, we are able to find about any mob we want up, attempt it, and we usually succede. Raiding can happen with good friends, or it can be done by hardcore players. Just have fun while you do it, and you'll be ok.
