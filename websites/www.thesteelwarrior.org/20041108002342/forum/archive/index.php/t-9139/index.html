Alisdahre
With Z-axis issues he wouldn't be getting the messages then would he? If he's getting the messages, than the game did indeed register the mobs coordinates vs. his coordinates as being within range and taunted them successfully.

I've AE tuanted and had mobs not turn as well, but from what I've seen it's just a symptom of how tuant works. You go to the top of the hate list plus one, that's it. But, there's proximity aggro and/or sitting aggro adjustments to the hate list to consider. I've had 5 mobs in camp before, where 3 were very close to me... one I was actively tanking and it was beating on me, two that had other targets but were up against me (closer to me than my buddies they were beating on), and 2 more mobs that were closer to other people than they were to me. AE tuant registered all 5 successfully taunted (5 messages), the one I was on stayed on me of course, the two that were next to me flipped to me, but the 2 that were a little ways away from me kept right on beating on my group mates cause of proximity aggro. I've done this a number of times, and even walked over to those mobs that didn't turn and had them immediately turn to me... then upon stepping back again they immediately turned right back to my friends again. Proximity. Some mobs seem to respond more to proximity than others, while there are other mobs that respond less to it, sometimes not responding to proximity at all.

Proximity aggro is most likely like sitting aggro, in that it's an added adjustment that gets tacked on after everything else is said and done to the hate list. If you taunt a mob off someone, tuant only, and pull aggro, if your groupmate you pulled from sits down, the mob will go for him again since his sitting aggro is greater than the "one point" lead you have on the hate list. I'd imagine that proximity aggro works this same exact way. Your AE tuant was most likely successful on all of the mobs that it said it was successful on, just that these after-the-fact hate adjustments that tuant has no effect upon were greater than your "one point tuant lead".
