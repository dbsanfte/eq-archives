The linking of /disc timers together was a cost. Use one skill now costs not using another immediately. The question becomes, is the cost too high? Wouldn't just the fact you can't use the /disc you just used again during cooldown a cost?

I view /discs as a failed attempt to add flexibility to Melee. I'm not sure if it should be scrapped in favor of something new but the way /discs stand they are unwieldy and for the most part unusable.

I always thought Warriors should be the only class that can be Offensive and Defensive at the same time. To this day I still do not see the point of crippling offensive DPS when doing defensive (they should have done a movement modifier if they thought defensive was too powerful). I still wonder about the lengths of the reset time. SOE controls the game...why can't they tune monsters to take into account faster reset times on skills?

The point is no matter how fun or cool a skill is, if its too hard to use its unused. What is the justification for having a /disc with short duration? What is the justification for having a /disc with a long reset time? If a /disc has either restriction then that means they are powerful? By most accounts, /discs with short durations, long reset times, aren't that powerful compared to things Casters field.

So I say if you are playing with the idea of revamping all of the /discs throw every preconceived notion out the window and start fresh. Make Fellstrike (just grabbing one at random) solid from the start and make the costs reasonable to be used instead of saved. Warriors should be actively looking to use a lot of their /discs during raids and exp groups instead of holding them back for the rainy day that might never come.

Eureka! What is the one thing pure melee don't have that every other class has? You guessed it, SPELLS! Why not model disciplines on the spell model, give us the space usually reserved for spell gems, put in a new "spell" vender in PoK who sells sheets outlining melee disciplines, and melee then take the sheets and study them (ie, put into their discipline book, which just happens to look like a spell book by the way) and can then commit their discipline to memory. Memorized disciplines would be represented by (surprise) Discipline Gems. By clicking on a discipline gem, that discipline is activated,and all other disciplines are greyed out. When an objective reactivation timer is reached, the discipline gems become active again and ready for reuse. Just a thought, maybe providing an easier way to organize disciplines in game, as well as a method of introducing new disciplines without having to change the entire dynamics of the game (the same way they introduce new spells), and we can use graphics already available in game. Hell, I could see a whole new avenue of quests to get new discipline styles not available from venders. If the developers really got froggy, they would untie all the disciplines from the same timer and give each one individual timers, just that when a discipline is active, all other disciplines are greyed out. By the way, this discipline model would only be available to pure melee, hybrid melee would have to rely on the old model for their disciplines (the benefit of not having a spell book).
