Couple things.

1. You NEED to keep Thelin alive. Just before a wave he will bend down to pick up a dagger. That is the signal to the party that mobs are about to spawn. Folks need to jump on the mobs asap or they will beat on Thelin. As someone else mentioned, the number of mobs gets progressively larger until the final wave of 7. Note that they do NOT always spawn right near Thelin. Sometimes they specifically spawn on the other side of the little space (aka, near where the healers are watching usually).

You need good crowd control. If you can swing it try to bring two enchanters. One will be relegated to mezzing/blurring Thelin during the combat so he doesn't wander off and doesn't draw any agro from the mobs you are fighting. The second will be in control of mezzing the adds (unless you prefer to off-tank them; that is what my guild often does just because they are all show offs). Dazzle works for Thelin. It also works for some, but not all, of the mobs.

If your tank has AE Taunt, I'd recommend he hit defensive and blow it on the final wave and not wait for the boss. Couple reasons. First, he'll probably get it back by the time you actually engage the boss. Second, that final wave is just plain NASTY on the CC and healers until its locked down. An AET will give the chanters the necessary time to hopefully lock adds before they start jumping clerics.

2. It takes a LONG time to get to the first wave in the maze but the distance between waves gets progressively shorter.

3. You CAN clear the construct room of mobs before you engage him. The construct does not spawn until Thelin enters the final construct area (near the water-thingy). If your chanter mezzes Thelin outside the room, the rest can pull the four mobs and kill them separately. Then the chanter blurs Thelin and lets him start the event.

The maze can be long and boring. But the first time through is always fun I think. G;'luck!

if you have 1 enchanter, dedicate the lowest level shaman, or druid in a pinch, to get agro off the enchanter by spamming greater heal and superior heal on them, even before they take damage.

Ive done this with one enchanter, and was the shaman doing it. Basically its a force multiplier for the enchanter, because it keeps mobs that get proximity agro on the enchanter, OFF the enchanter, and on the healer. Thats why you want the shaman, so they can take the beating from the level 60 mobs while the enchanter mezzes them. Doing this, we had 1 death, and that was an int caster that wandered into the kill zone right before a pop.

DPS wont be a concern really in success, the mobs all are slowable, and tank fine after slowed. you may want to pull the birds from the fountain area to just corner him so all the melee can just wail away though.
