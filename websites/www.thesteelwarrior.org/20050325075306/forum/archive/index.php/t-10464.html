01-31-2005, 06:03 PM
In my opinion, warrior has been vastly enjoyable most of the time and incredibly depressing/annoying some of the time. After much analysis and response from the development team, much of the hardship is due to key bugs that shipped that impact our "rage generation." Rage in WoW is the bar that powers our special abilities.

Rage generation against multiple enemies in defensive stance with a shield works exceedingly well right now. This aligns well with personal experience as my favorite experiences have been doing deep dungeon runs with my guildmates in 5 person expeditions.

Rage generation in a solo environment is actually quite manageable up till about level 45 I would say. Getting the absolute best weaponry available to you is key to solo execution due to rage generation issues. If you are going to play in the Horde then working with a Shaman using a totem to give you a proc for a chance at multiple swings will REALLY make a night and day difference.

I would say the point at which rage generation is simply not enough to keep warriors competitive with other classes in 1-1 solo scenarios is around the low 50s. It gets quite annoying and you will find yourself soloing against mobs that 3-4 levels below you to keep the gameplay and xp gain tolerable. Bandages and quality food is key here to reduce your downtime.

One thing is for sure: warriors shine in duos or groups with a person who can heal you. There is nothing more satisfying than equipping a gigantic two handed axe, charging into battle, switching to berserker stance, using the whirlwind attack to lay down huge damage on every mob with huge "splat" critical hit sounds going off :)

On the whole, play a warrior if you like to be a heavily armored player with the ability to do damage and take damage as the situation demands. The class will be getting some badly needed bug fixes "soon." So now is an excellent time to start!
