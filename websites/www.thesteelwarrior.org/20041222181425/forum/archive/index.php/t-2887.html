I think a lot of the discussion depends on definitions. Can paladins solo? Well yes and no. They can heal themselves, have good mitigation and have limited crowd control abilities. These abilities go a long ways towards mitigating their mediocre DPS. Do Paladins solo better than Warriors. Unquestionably. Can Paladins solo effectively, as in for reasonable experience gain over time as opposed to a 'better than nothing way to fend off boredom'. At lower levels, possibly (although less likely now that KEI has been fixed) particularly in old world zones where current equipment is very powerful. At higher levels maybe, particularly against undead. There aren't many high-level undead in areas that can be soloed though.

Can a 65 level Paladin solo antiquated content that was uber 2 years ago in high-end Elemental/Time gear and with AAs placed in the optimum abilities? Sure, it's been demonstrated. Does this in itself make them effective soloists? No, not by a long shot.

Should a Paladin last as long in a fight agaisnt Gorenaire as a Warrior? Without healing, certainly not. However, since a Paladin can heal themselves, they will definately have an edge against a low-damage (compared to level) mob like Gorenaire. With LoH added to the formula they can effectively double the advantage they already have. It's the duration advantage that's killing Gore. (This is nothing new either, Paladins have always had this advantage, if not to as great a degree as they have it now).

It's a cool thing for Paladins. It does nothing for Warriors. It says nothing at all about the balance between the two classes. It's mainly just an example of how powerful high-end gear and AAs can be compared to older content.
