I wouldn't attempt to value it vs other stats on a point to point basis, but look at it this way: AC is a chance of mitigation. HP is there to keep you up longer as the damage makes it through the mitigation (and avoidance but.. yanno..).

Shielding is defined, constant, definite mitigation. Shielding aug = 2% less melee damage. How much AC do you need to add to compensate for 2% mitigation?

And HP... well... you'd have to look at how hard each mob you tank hits to really value it vs shielding. 100 hp vs 2% shielding on a mob that hits 2k? Each 2k hit turns into 1960 with 2% shielding, so get hit 3 times like that and you've already done better than a +100 HP aug. I'd be hard presed to find a situation where any HP aug > shielding aug.

Hard to put numbers to it, but I think it'd be crazy not to take one of those augs once you get the opportunity to. :)

Sythrak
03-28-2004, 08:11 PM
Semi easy obtainable spellshield gear (If your a Time geared guild) :

1.) Glyphed Prayer Ring - 5% (1 group obtainable)
2.) Fleshbound Shroud of Aggression - 5% (2 group or less obtainable)

Raid spellshield obtainable :

1.) Armguards of the Brute - 4%
2.) Prismatic Ring of Resistance - 6%
3.) Belt of Temporal Bindings - 5%
4.) Blood Glazed Helm - 4%
5.) Tactician's Shield - 5%
6.) Various spell shielding augs from Ldon or Yxtta - 2-3%

On average if you've been doing GoD and Time for awhile, you can get around 10% spellshielding in items. Helps alot if you have to tank the mobs with the nasty high damage chaining procs. Much more usefull than shielding. But of course that's only situational, as I can only think of a few mobs you'd go with spell shielding over normal shielding. Mix and matching them to get whatever you can for both effects though makes for a more versatile tank.

Edit : Didn't mean to derail this. Always have shielding augs over 20 hp augs or whatever. Shielding gives small returns depending on the mob but it adds up as you get more.
