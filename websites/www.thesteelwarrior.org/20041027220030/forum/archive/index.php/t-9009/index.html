Eriatha Egan
I would decide your goal path after your first run through the Plane of Time.

The PoTime gods aren't exactly, shall we say, easy. They become relatively easy after you have farmed Time for a while, but to walk up to Tallon Zek the first try and say, "Sup biotch! Gibborz me your pl0otz, matie!" isn't very likely given the circumstances of his strength vs ANYTHING you've ever tanked before.

Now, I don't want to give you the wrong impression either. A lot of your success will depend on how many numbers you can support on your first Plane of Time run. Ask yourself these questions:

1) Do you rely on several key alt-mains for the success of your raids?
2) Do people in your guild have a tendancy to lose interest after the first initial victory? Meaning, can ya put out 72 EVERY time you raid Time?

The reason I write this post is because I have seen a radical change in my own guild as we entered Time. I now see them in an entirely new perspective. My guild is very large, and we have a little over 100 people Time flagged. However, we cannot seem to form more than 55 for a Time raid. Why is this? It's because the people who are alongside you raiding Time are really the people in your guild who actually care.

Now, to actually get to the real point of this post, what I'm trying to say is... see how your guild performs on their first (and second) shot through the Plane of Time. This is because there is a real fine balance between the numbers present to take on these gods, and the gear required to actually defeat them. They're monstrosities in comparison to any elemental mini. They do twice the dps, have twice the hitpoints, spawn adds in the process, and have AEs that make any elemental AE look pale in comparison. They require everyone to not only be on their game, but to be well equipped to accept the challenge. If you are unable to field more than 55 like my own guild, then you're going to have to take a good hard look at where your weak spots are (gearwise), and work on them. For my own guild, it was the fact that we had relied on two primary alt-main clerics for so long, and never once bothered to equip them sufficiently. They crumbled very early in the rotation, and we failed miserably because of it. We have now changed our outlook on awarding gear because of that (and our second run was, by far, a much greater success). If you can field 72 on your first try, then you will be able to get away with not having everyone so top notch equipped. Still, your early success in Time will have A LOT to do with how much you geared up in the elementals. Even if you think you did, you may find yourself repeatedly going to back because Time has a real nasty way of exposing the weak spots within your ranks. :P

Because of this (and finally getting to the conclusion of this post), while your longterm goal will remain unchanged, you *might* find yourself benefitting from a few short term upgrades from Phase1-3 in the process, simply so you can kill Phase4 and beyond. That is exactly the reason they are there. =)

I admit to a few mistakes in choosing Time equipment. First of all, get the Veil of the Inferno, its a 180hp clicky buff that stacks with everything. That alone is about 2-4 loots worth of hps on average going from elemental to Time gear.

Next, piss off your friendly neighborhood ranger by picking up a bow off Tallon Zek, expecially if you are the main tank.

The rest you have to look at what you managed to acquire in the Elemental Planes. You could probably pass on Armguards of the Brute if you have Raex Arms. The Tier 2 gloves are better than Raex because of stats and the strikethrough, but would not be worth spending DKP if you have Raex. Same can be said for CT legs, Helm of Flowing Time, and Boots of Despair vs Elemental stuffs.

Sadly, Rallos Zek is going to drop several items you will want. If you can hold out week after week, pick up the Darkblade, his melee ring, the BP and the shoulders. Again, do not take the mask from RZ, wait and get the Bert mask. Get the boots off bert.

My guild had it pretty easy going through Time. I think we beat Quarm for the first time on our 4th or 5th run. VZ and Inny tend to be sticking points, but with a little bit of asking around, I am sure you can find the strats to take them down. With the levels now capping at 70, things won't be nearly as difficult for your guild.
