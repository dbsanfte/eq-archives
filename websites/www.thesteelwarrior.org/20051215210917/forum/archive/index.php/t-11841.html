I got in a huge argument in hillsbrad yesterday. There was a 60 mage maxed out on gear that plays there all the time. He is constantly giving orders on general chat and no one listens to him. So he gripes about and says everyone else will suck in battlegrounds.

This irritated me to no end. He is never in the raid group and apparently moves in a 5 man group. I told him plain and simple that of course no one will listen to him and its because he is not providing any leadership. I got a little long winded, but jesus, quit complaining about people not following your orders if you are not going to step up and be a LEADER. Being a leader is more than telling peoplpe what to do. You have to recruit and organize them. You have to gain their respect which requires you respect them too. You have to give them guidance and let them realize that you have their interests in mind too, not just your own.

Guess what, that is not going to happen in a 5 man group. Only the people in your 5 man group will listen to you. It does not matter how much you know about what to do, people will not listen to you unless you do the previous things. Guess what that means, giving up some of your own glory and playing ability to do those things. Yes you can get more CP in a 5 man group that plays together than in the raid. But if you want your side to play well, you have to lead them. It means gettign in the raid, helping organize it, ie make sure every party has a healer, that complimentary classes are in same party, give some instruction on tactics, create subordinate leaders in te groups so yu can focus on bigger picture, and they can lead their groups the same way you do an in instance.

It takes some effort and means not always being able to be in the middle of the action. People will follow you though if you show them your are interested in helping them instead of just using them.

Needless to say the argument got heated and he called me a jarhead (i am the army infantry, not the marines) and told me to go back to Iraq (he did later apologize), and I attempted to remain as civil as possible, in the hopes that others reading would take some of these lessons and learn a little. Yes its a game, and you are more than welcome to play it however you like, but if you want the cooperation of others, you have to make some sacrifices.

This is not directed at anyone here, as I just needed to get it off my shoulders. Please don;t complain about people not following orders if you are not willing to step up and be a leader though. Remember, just ecause what you are telling people is the right thing to do, doesn;t mean they will do it.
