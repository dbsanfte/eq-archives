This is incorrect. If Spell + Item haste is capped at 100% (May not be true), and bard overhaste exists at 10%, then a 40 delay weapon would have an effective max hasted delay of -4. As this represents attacking 0.4 seconds [B]BEFORE[\B] your last attack, and our EQ characters do not travel back in time in order to pre-attack, we have a contradiction, and the equation must be discarded.

The proper way to view haste is as that % addtional attacks per time unit. If you have a 40 delay weapon, you get 2.5 attack rounds per 10 seconds. If you have 40% haste in addition, then you get 2.5 * 1.4 = 3.5 attacks per 10 seconds, or an attack every 2.85 seconds, making your hasted delay 28.5. At 80 % haste, you get 4.5 attacks per 10 seconds, an attack every 2.22 seconds, making your delay 22.2. At 100% haste, you get 5 attacks every 10 seconds, making your delay 20.
