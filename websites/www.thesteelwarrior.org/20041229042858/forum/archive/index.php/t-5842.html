The thread that you linked is flawed because the flurry parses were done with dual wield. Since the offhand can't dual wield you get a lot of extra attacks that can't have flurries associated with them. The best way to parse flurry rate is to use a two hander.

I have flurry 3 fero 3 PB 3 and I parsed one of my logs from last night doing LDoN. During the fighting I was only using a two handed weapon.

From the log I had

Total Attacks 3,959
Total Flurrys: 59
Ripostes: 146

So to figure out the flurry rate first I removed the riposte attacks from the total number of attacks (since ripostes can't flurry) then I removed the attacks due to flurries from the total attacks (since we are trying to figure out the number of extra attacks that I would have seen without flurry trained)

This gave me a total of 3,695 attacks with 118 extra attacks due to flurry (59 flurrys * 2). Or an extra 3.19% swings from having flurry 3 trained.

NOTE: I believe that if I did not have ferocity or punishing blade trained that the percentage of extra attacks due to flurry would have been higher, but the absolute number of attacks due to flurry would have been the same. This is because PB and fero increase the average numbers of attacks per round, and flurry can only trigger once per melee round. So you are increasing the number of attacks without increasing the number of chances to trigger a flurry.

Callon,

What Sulvar is saying is that the original post you had listed attacks using two weapons. Well, the off hand weapon can never flurry, ever. So, if you base your numbers off the # of total attacks and do not eliminate the number of off hand swings, then your percentages are off.

Example:

115 main hand swings
50 off hand swings
15 flurries

If you just go on those numbers you'd say 15 flurries per 165 swings or 10% increase in swings due to flurry. However, in reality, you should be 15 flurries in 115 attacks. This put you at 15%.

If you just use a 2 hander, you eliminate the need to remove the off hand swings. Of course, if you use 1h blunt and 1h slash (etc) in each hand, you can filter then out easy enough.

So, according to Sulvar's numbers he sees a 3.19% swing increase for Flurry3. Perhaps flurry6 is ~6% or so.

p.s. my numbers above are make believe and just used to illustrate some differences and get the point across. I did not check any numbers, just made them up on the fly, so please forgive any inaccuracies.
