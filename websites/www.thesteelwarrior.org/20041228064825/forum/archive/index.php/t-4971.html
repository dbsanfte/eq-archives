I tag with incite and only taunt "if" I lose aggro.

The reason why you want to taunt (successfully) before using Incite is that basically doing it the other way around your not maximizing the amount of aggro you can generate. Example:

Mob inc...

1. Shaman casts Malo/slow for 600 "Hate"...

2. Warrior sees mob beeline to Shaman and Incites for 400...

3. Shaman STILL has 200 "Hate" over you and is presently getting the everliving crap beat out of him/her... So you taunt (successfully) and it turns...

4. Now you have 601 Hate... and have to hope that no one does anything that will cause it to turn away because a) Incite is gone and b) Taunt is gone (and unreliable)

Now lets do it the other way...

1. Shaman casts Malo/slow for 600 "Hate"...

2. Warrior sees mob beeline to Shaman and taunts (successfully)

3. Warrior now has 601 "Hate" and the Shaman is sitting on his mount munching on roots or something and debating on whether to DoT or not...

4. Warrior uses Incite to "pad" his Hate... You now have 1001 "Hate" and your basically sitting pretty saying things like, "JESUS LOCK~" and "Got nukes?" to your group mates...

Basic jist of it. This is of course not counting Anger line weapons and effects... Speaking of which... If taunt DOES fail than I find it very handy to Incite AFTER an Anger3-type proc.
