The AH is difficult to judge regarding what will and won't sell. Basically it takes some knowledge of what's pretty commonly available at that level range and what is somewhat hard to acquire or better than average. If it's common, it's vendor trash for sure. If I check the AH and there are already half a dozen of them on sale, typically it's not worth my time. If it's hard to acquire it might be worth trying to AH it, once. Only if it's a blue or purple item are you almost certain to have a sale.

And as you go up there will be more of a market, because people aren't leveling as fast and therefore they're changing gear within their range more frequently. If I'm going to go from level 20 to 25 in two or three days, I'm not likely to worry too much about upgrading my gear a lot in that time frame. I might make a change at level 20 and then another at level 25, but not much in between. If I'm going from 50 to 51 in that same time period, I'll start paying more attention to my gear. I think most people are like that.

Also people need to put buy out prices on their items. In 8 hours a lot can happen. And I don't know about you, but I'm not particularly inclinded to want to camp the AH waiting to out bid any of my competition. Nor am I inclinded to want to have to interrupt what other things I may be doing to pop over to the AH and up my bids. Many times if there is a buy out price and the current bid isn't too much under that, I'll just pay the buy out price, because it saves me time and annoyance. People who expect to just have bid prices are probably in for a rude shock, not everyone is interested in bidding.

However, overall I agree with Haass, the AH can be a huge money sink with very little return, if you're not very careful about what you bother to try selling. I made that mistake early on and learned from it. Either vendor the trash or give it to a guild enchanter for disenchanting, you're better off.

Before you put something up in the AH ask yourself, if I were such and such level how much would I likely spend on this item?

I usually only sell about 1 in 3 items I put up for sale. The things that don't sell I just eat the deposit and vendor. However, the money I make by reselling that one in three items easily offsets the deposit lost on the items that don't sell. Yesterday I sold a level 30ish green 2H sword for about 1.5g, would have sold to a vendor for maybe 60s. Blue stuff goes for bucks in the AH. I sold a blue 2H level 30 hammer for 3g.

Oh, and be sure to search on the item you are selling first. If there are any like it, set your minimum bid below the other item. Also don't set your buyout so high, make it about 50-75% over your minimum bid or higher if it's a blue item. Don't expect to make a lot on green items... but you can sell at a higher price than you'd get from a vendor.
