dafrog, do you know what we mean when we say "potential damage" and "actual damage"? Because when you refer to that as a semantic difference, I begin to suspect that you don't actually understand what we're talking about.

It's not just a difference of nomenclature. "potential damage" and "actual damage" refer to two different things.

Suppose I had a 100/10 weapon. That weapon is *amazing* for DPS, right? It's a "great DPS weapon". It does lots of DPS. It has high DPS.

But "DPS" is an imprecise term.

Against the Emperor, that weapon does no damage at all. Takes no actual hit points off his hit point total.

So we're really looking at two different concepts, both called "DPS".

One concept, which around here we call "actual DPS", is only measurable vs. specific mobs. Against a BoT boss, that 100/10 weapon might have an "actual DPS" of 1000. It takes 1000 HPs off the BoT boss's health every second. But against the Emperor, that same 100/10 weapon has an "actual DPS" of zero. It takes no hit points off the Emperor's health, no matter how long you swing it at him.

That's "actual DPS".

Now, let's look at what we call "potential DPS". That's the amount of damage you would <I>expect</I> the weapon to do, all other things being equal. That 100/10 weapon has a "potential DPS" of 1000, whether you are measuring it against the Emperor or against the BoT boss, whether the mob it is being wielded against has an AC of 10 or an AC of 10,000, whether you have an ATK debuff on you that means you miss 9 out of 10 swings, or an ATK buff on you that means you hit 9 out of 10 swings. Even though the weapon does <I>no actual damage</I> to the Emperor, it still has a shockingly high "potential DPS".

That's "potential DPS".

They are fundamentally different concepts.

What people are saying is that the thing that determines melee aggro is "potential DPS", not "actual DPS". That 100/10 weapon will do an <I>amazing</I> job holding aggro against the Emperor, even though it never does even one HP of damage to him. That 100/10 weapon will hold aggro against a mob with such high AC that you never land a single blow against it.

Yes, it is a true statement that a weapon with "better DPS" will hold aggro better than one with "worse DPS", in general. But given a choice between tanking the Emperor with a 10/20 weapon with Emperor-bane +10 (effectively, a 20/20 weapon), and tanking the Emperor with the aforementioned 100/10 weapon with no Emperor-bane at all...
...the 100/10 weapon will hold aggro far, far better. Nearly 10 times better, in fact.

Even though it does no damage to the Emperor at all, never removes even one HP from his HP total, has absolutely no "actual DPS".

Does this help clear things up for you?

Off the top of my head, the following situations benefit from an understanding of potential vs. actual DPS:

1) tanking a melee-immune mob. A bad bane weapon will tank more poorly than a good non-bane weapon. Since the tank's job is to hold aggro and soak damage, not to do DPS, the MT for a melee-immune mob is better off using the best potential DPS weapon he has access to, rather than the bane weapon.

In particular, a dual-hate-proccing non-bane weapon will definitively out-aggro an equivalent non-dual-hate-proccing bane weapon, even if the bane weapon has significantly better ratio.


2) Dealing with high-damage-rating, low-damage-bonus weapons. Actual DPS tends to be proportionate to ((2 * damage rating) + damage bonus), while hate tends to be proportionate to (damage rating + damage bonus). So the damage rating contributes much less to potential DPS than it does to actual DPS, changing the math about which weapons are best.

Consider a GSoT: 31/30, DB 36. Its actual DPS will be based on a weapons quality of (62+36)/30, or 3.27. But its potential DPS, its "hate quality", will be closer to 2.23.

Compare to a GSot4W: 41/31, DB 37. Its actual DPS will be based on a weapons quality of (82 + 37)/31, or 3.84. But its potential DPS, its "hate quality", will be closer to 2.52.

The GSot4W is less of an improvement over the GSoT for hate, than it is for DPS. Mind you, it's still better. It's just less better. :-)

That's part of why low-delay (below 28) 2-handers are so bad for warriors. Consider a 30/20 2-hander. Its DPS quality is (60+13)/20, or 3.65. Better than a GSoT. But its potential DPS, its "hate quality", will be closer to 43/20, or 2.15. Significantly worse than a GSoT.

Summary: there are two situations where "actual DPS" and "potential DPS" are usefully/significantly different.

1) when the mob is immune to actual DPS, potential DPS is the relevant factor to calculate by

2) when the difference between ((2*damage)+bonus)/delay and (damage + bonus)/delay is significant enough to make one weapon markedly better for aggro than another.

There may be others; those are the only two situations I was able to think of, off-hand.

I wish that SoE were aware enough of their own mathematics to put "DPS-weapons" into the high-end game for warriors; 50/25 2-handers, for example. 4.56 quality rating for actual DPS, outdamaging darn near anything. But only 2.56 quality rating for hate, making it much easier for a BoW-equipped or even hate-aug'ed GSot4W-equipped MT to hold aggro.

By the way, I just did a quick back-of-the-envelope calculation for VDD. 60/38, DB 40. DPS weapon quality 4.21. Hate weapon quality 2.63, or only slightly better than a GSot4W.

Mind, there may be some small multiplier (1.25 or so) on the hate formula, so it would end up looking more like (damage * 2 + bonus)/delay for DPS, and (damage * 1.25 + bonus)/delay for hate.
