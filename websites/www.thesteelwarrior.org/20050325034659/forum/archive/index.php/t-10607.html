What makes a warrior a very good choice in WoW is how dedicated you are to learning the game and strategy. I started as priest, got to 22, and realized there wasn't a damn pally or warrior that new how to hold agro. So I made my Pally lvled to 44, and realized my group needs a warrior. So i am now playing awarrior and love it!

First off there is a large portion of the gaming population that have never played a mmorpg before. This is important in the fact they don't comprehend group tactics. Therefor if you are capable of learning to change stances and using abilities in those stances then you are levles above the rest of the community. I like to comapre this to Ebayers in EQ1, you just knew the ones that didn't have a clue, same here.

Paladins have very good single mob aggro control. Thier multiple mob agro controol comes down to Consecrate, a very mana intensive AOE low damge holy spell. If you are playing a Pally and your encharge of agro control with this spell and your other abilities you will have no mana left after tackling a group of 4-5.

WoW is different that past MMORPG's because they didn't create the 1-pull 1-slaughter game. There is next to no mez, and if there is it's like 30 seconds on opponet with no ability to lock down and control. They wanted to create a chaos type of game, where groups were constantly doing something to survive. The only lockdown I have seen is Rogues on casters, when you have a good rogue, casters mana bar should never move.

I say the above because that is where Warriors are needed. They have an AOE taunt and the ability to hold 4 opponents at bay all the while doing damage.

My Pally can tank, as a retro spec 44lvl pally I can dish some pain to my opponents. But once I have more than one, its an outlast kinda thing. My damage output is pretty constant, so that vairable doesnt change. A warrior on the other hand will change his damage output, so instead of stopping to attack to heal myself. My warrior just ups the damge output. Concidering the changes that have been made, I'm doing more now. So my Warrior can tank too, I really personally feel it comes down to player concept of the game, and learning the tools.

For example, quick switching weaposn to gain more agro, then unloading with a big 2hander for more damage, switching to sword and board to interupt casters, swithing back to DW for rage, 2h for big damage. Using nets, marbles and candles in my earlier days to get the advantage, and of course specing Arms/Fury for the battle warrior. Even though some of our abilities people don't think are worth much, using them is key, battle shouts, rend, sunderarmor (this one is big help) and of course being a warrior make you very equipement dependent.

So try both, stay away form the wow boards, and play to win. there is plenty of help out there if you ask.

Peace out
