You dont have to do all of the trials in one session (god that would be horrible, considering how long some take =p ); when you finish a trial and get a temporary flag, you have to go back to Barindu and hail the Scribe (near High Priest) and say "issues", and it becomes a permanent flag.

Think of temporary flags as something like checklist flags from PoP, you wont lose it, and you can do the trials out of order.

You will need to complete the previous trials to get a permanent flag for whichever trial you complete though; for example, if you finish Trial 3, you wont get a permanent flag for it until you finish 1 and 2 also.

Basically the way I see it is as follows:

1. If you have done Smith flag, you will need to say 'what tend' to the npc in Vxed to get your flag. Hailing Scribe will then get you the perma-flag.

2. If you have not done Smith yet, Hail NPC will suffice, but you will not be able to convert it to a perma flag until you have completed Smith flag.

3. If you did Vxed and Tipt, but do not have Smith, then you will have 2 temp-flags, and Hailing the Scribe and High Priest will do nothing, until you have done the Smith flag.

In a way, Smith = Saryn, Vxed = MM, Tipt = RZ.
