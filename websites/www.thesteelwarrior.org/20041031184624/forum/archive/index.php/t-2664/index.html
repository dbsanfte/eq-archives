After studying some posts about procs on this board and data in Lucy, and my own observations in game, I have what seems to be a consistent theory on how procs occur:

1) Player proc checks occur every two seconds or so
First, player procs are different from NPCs. NPCs proc on a per hit basis. That is, every time the NPC swings and hits, there's a chance the proc goes off. That also means that if you haste or slow an NPC, or evade the blow, it alters how often they proc

The studies I've seen on this board indicate that players are different. Base player proc rates appear to be constant for most weapons, regardless of whether the player hits or is hasted. While I haven't seen much discussion about this, my own guess is that what happens is, for most weapons, every two seconds or so your character automatically attempts to proc his weapons. The chance this proc works is based on your Dex, and your off-hand weapon also has to make an off-hand weapon use check. Note that this rate is constant, regardless of weapon delay. So in general a one-hander and a two-hander proc at the same rate per minute, for example.

If you examine the raw data on weapons in Lucy, you'll notice that there is a "casttime" stat. For most weapons, this stat is 0, but for some it is a -1. The casttime appears to be a modifier to the delay between proc checks. So whereas most weapons check for procs every two seconds or so, weapons with a casttime of -1 check every second. Thus weapons with casttime -1 proc about twice as often as most. Casttime -1 weapons include black acrylia weapons like the black acrylia rapier and the cleric's Hammer of Damnation (and if you've watched either of these weapons in action you'll have noticed how often they proc).


Some interesting aspects of this:

- Two-handers and one-handers proc at the same rate. So with the warrior epic, for instance, the 1-hand Blade of Strategy in your primary hand and the 2-h Jagged blade of war proc the same number of times per minute on average.

- You can ignore weapon delay when it comes to procs. Black acrylia rapiers proc at the same rate as black acrylia battle axes. Likewise, Blade of carnage and blade of war should both proc enraging blow at the same rate (although I can't test this out personally).

- It should be obvious, but if you are dual wielding the primary weapon will usually proc first and proc more often than your off-hand, since you need to make your off-hand weapon check to proc it. This comes into play when, for instance, you want to proc a snare or slow weapon ASAP during a fight.


That's about it. Afraid I don't have personal parses to make this a formal study, but feel free to post info, parses or comments about the theory. Like I said, this is the theory I'm currently working under, and it seems to bear out when I watch my own proc rates. But if someone has parse evidence to the contrary, I'd be interested in seeing it.

Remf da Troll
Hi all-

Think this might be in the library in some form, but here goes again. Think this is all correct:

-there are two different types of procs; innate and weapon based. This is true for both mobs and players.

-weapon-based procs can occur on a miss; "innate" procs (most mob procs, player effects like call of sky) can only proc on a "hit".

-weapons have a "baseline" chance to proc; 2h baseline chance *may* be higher than 1h, but further parsing needed to verify.

-each and every individual swing has a chance to proc, with the base rate modified by current dexterity.

-some weapons (bloodfrenzy, cleric hammers) have modifiers to their proc rate that increases it. Some suspicion other weapons have reduced chances, not well documented tho.

-for weapons, the chance to proc PER SWING is modified by the effective delay of the weapon *for that swing*; haste reduces the chance to proc, slow increases it. This results in a generally constant proc rate over time, modified only by the number of actual swings (dual wield, double-triple-quad attack, flurry, rampage) vs. the number expected based purely on delay.

-for innate procs, proc rate is proportional to number of hits, modified by dexterity. It is unknown if effective (haste/slow) delay impacts innate proc probability the way it does with weapon procs, but the reduction in "hits" due to fewer attacks is probably the predominant factor. Fewer swings = fewer chances to hit = fewer hits = fewer procs. Avoidance has the same type of impact.

Think that's most of the story anyway. The most procs I've personally seen in "one round" of attacks is 13, which was done on a 65ish mob AE pull via rampage.

RdT.

NOTE: since (most/all?) EQ "success or fail" and "extent of success" checks are based on the RNG output generated using a variable seed value, and all checks seem to be the same "polarity" (i.e. high = succeed / low = fail, or the opposite- it's arbitrary but consistent), multiple checks for success/failure in a small time interval are probably NOT truly/statistically independent. I.e. the seed used impacts the RNG output, and it is unlikely the seed value is updated on a per-event basis. Empirical evidence suggests it, or part of it, gets updated on a zone-wide basis about once per tick, which explains how seemingly unbelieveable events like quad-flurry-crits or procs occur, despite the fact they'd normally happen about once in the time before a proton would decay...
