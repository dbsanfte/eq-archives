Vendak
Do what I did to confirm PP Length (I never believed the "Omg dont hail it or it starts the depop timer" just like I never believed the "Omg dont sit in the frenzied ghoul's room, it's anti-camp code!").

Simple method, kill said God/flag mob. /con his corpse and watch the corpse timer.

Watch in glorious awe as the timer turns to be 10 minutes for most Planar Projections. Give 1-2 ticks leeway (6 or 12 seconds) in variation because it's dependant on when you killed it during the servers "tick" cycles. But it comes almost exactly 10 minutes each time. For the lamen, that means when your flag mob reaches 20 minutes left to rot from the 30 min alotted.

You can hail it, give it cookies, whatever you want. Each Planar Projection works like clockwork, it cares not what you do to it. I believe Aerin`Dar's PP is 15 minutes. TZ and VZ's is 10. Terris' might be 10. Those are the ones i've timed. But like clockwork each time. I know where this rumor stemmed from. It's from raid leaders like myself who tell their raids not to hail the projection until *gasp* we've finished the job.

Point in case, Terris Thule fight. Usually I want the kiddies to kill the 4 gargoyles (as TT is burned down prior) BEFORE everyone starts making the mad rush for their precious flag, so we don't have needless deaths and we, well, finish the script. Some people fail to see this logic and assume its some big bad voodoo "omg dont hail it or it'll eat you" crap.

Don't read into the projection stuff too far. It's a static depop time for each given projection. It has no bearing of when or how you hail it. I know it isnt a projection, but the Carprin Deatharn flag mob stays up for what seems at least 20+ minutes. Same with the gnome for Manaetic Behemoth. And i've stated the two different Planar Projection timers between say TZ and AD. Just time it against the corpse rot as I said, there may be 6 or 12 seconds leeway like I said due to the tick-system in place (this applies to ALL timers, ask any ENC who has Mezmerization Mastery where this flaw in the system is blatently apparent).

Hope this helps.

-Vendak
