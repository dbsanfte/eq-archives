I know the consensus is that defensive AAs are the way to go, since the "job of the warriors is to take damage, not deal damage". However, I never hear the flip-side of what the benefits of offensive AAs are by comparison. The obvious answer is they generate more aggro.

If I recall, there are posts here that say having all the offensive AAs increases your dps by 40%. So that would also increase your dps aggro component by 40%, on top of increasing the total number of double attacks, flurries and off-hand attacks you get per minute. These likewise directly increase the number of procs you get per minute.

Therefore you could potentially be looking at a sizable aggro increase by investing in offensive AAs. Obviously, if you are having trouble holding aggro, this mitigates that problem. But even if you aren't having much trouble holding aggro, increasing your aggro allows your groupmates to further go "all out" against the mobs. For example, the extra aggro might allow your casters to each throw in an extra spell or two per fight and the monks and rogues to avoid having to spend time feigning or evading to help toss back aggro occasionally. Finally, the extra damage you do makes the fights a bit shorter, and therefore more mana and time efficient.


So while I'm not saying you should neglect defensive AAs, I think a reasonable argument can be made that a warrior who takes some offensive AAs before completing their defensive AAs is not necessarilly losing anything significant, and might even be helping their group fight more efficiently if their defenses are already high.

I just always found it ironic that I see so many warriors complaining about our aggro, but then ignoring the AAs that can increase aggro. Why not take a look at strengthening the weak area of the class a bit? Just something to think about, I guess.

Frankly, the problem is that there are 2 roles for warriors... The raid warrior and the exp group warrior. In a raid, the warrior is assumed to have enough time/buffs/whatever to get aggro. Therefore having more defensive AAs means that the warrior is more likely to survive, while eatting less healer mana.

In an exp group, defensive AAs aren't nearly as vital. Important, of course, but you're fighting for aggro with people that generate much more than you easily. In this situation, any method of holding more aggro helps a ton. This combination means that offensive AAs are more useful.

I personally think I'm fine in exp groups. However, with even just half the defensive AAs, I was getting eatten far too quickly on raids. Therefore my focus was entirely on defensive AAs and I will be getting offensive AAs when I get the chance.
