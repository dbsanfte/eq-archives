Karnos Darkmere
Time for a paladin to put things into perspective.

What would happen if paladins were stripped of their aggro-holding potential or if the average warrior became as good at keeping aggro as paladins? I'll tell you what would happen: paladins wouldn't get into XP groups. Nobody invites paladins as healers; nobody invites them as damage dealers; nobody invites them as buffers/slowers. Paladins are invited to tank, pure and simple. Take their one major ability away from them and you have an utterly broken class.

Now, one might say a paladin unable to hold aggro isn't any different than a warrior with his current set of abilities, but that is nonsense.

A warrior and a paladin with equivalent gear will always result in:

-- The warrior generating a good deal more damage (much higher skill caps; much better offensive AA abilities.)

-- The warrior having higher AC (warriors-only armor affords more AC than equivalent paladin-only armor.)

-- The warrior having a significantly greater HP total (at 60th level and beyond, warriors get about 18% more HP per point of Sta than paladins; furthermore, warrior-only armors afford a lot more pure HP bonuses than paladin-only gear.)

The paladin is able to buff himself and other people with HP and AC, certainly, but in the high-end game, past the point where paladins are able to solo, who cares when those buffs don't stack with superior cleric, druid, ranger, beastlord, and shaman buffs:

-- Aegolism and Virtue override all cleric buffs paladin have available at any level. Spells in the Spiritual Vigor and Strength of Tunare lines don't stack with any Brell's buff, and how many players prefer Brell's over Spritual Vigor.

-- The paladin-only buff Yaulp IV doesn't stack with Spiritual Strength, a clearly superior Attack buff, rendering Yaulp IV effectively useless.

-- Valor of Marr, one of the worst haste spells in the game, doesn't stack with any other haste spell.

-- Aura of the Crusader doesn't stack with Protection of the Nine and similar mana regen spells.

What remains?

-- Pacify spells can't affect enemies above 55th level, so high-level paladins aren't better pullers than warriors.

-- Paladins have two stuns which actually work on enemies above 55th level, one of which is prohibitively expensive, making it usable not even once every fight--and that when the knight has KEI.

-- Paladins have a nice set of healing spells, but none of them is even half as good as the equivalent cleric heal. There is no paladin 75% CH, mind you.

-- Paladins do retain their self-only proc buff, but even with Pious Might in effect, they don't even come close to the damage output of a warrior.

-- Paladins do enjoy the ability to CH themselves once every 90 minutes. Once every 90 minutes. Try counting the number of CH's you need while tanking though a 90-minute period. And let's not forget, LoH is frequently interrupted (forcing you to wait 90 minutes for it to refresh) and can't be used when the knight is stunned.

Not a great deal, eh?

Even given only the above information, warriors are obviously ahead of paladins in the 55th+ level game, except for the one major fact that paladins hold aggro so much better.

Now figure in that awe-inspiring array of disciplines warrior enjoy, in particular Defensive and Evasive, and the fact that paladins have on four disciplines, all of which are effectively useless (Sanctification, the one single discipline which might have made a significant difference under the right circumstances, has a duration of a ridiculous 10 seconds.)

Yes, I can see how taking away paladin stun aggro would make things so very balanced.

At the bottom line, there can only be one "best" tanking class in XP parties, and right now, it is the paladin class. Warriors continue to reign supreme as tanks in the raid department, a part of the game where paladins have never been popular except as group healers against enemies with AE DD effects. There is, in other words, room for both.

Now, if you were to hand the XP tanking crown to warriors, paladins would, for the sake of balance, have to become the kings of raid-tanking, and that would imply taking away the Defensive and Evasive disciplines from warriors and passing them on to us knights. Is that something you would appreciate?
