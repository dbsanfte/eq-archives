My advice to you is to not take the stereotypical attitude that a warrior's ability to tank, solo, or PvP depends on its talent build. (at least not until 40+)

While Im still ignorant of how warriors work once you hit 60, in the process of leveling 1-38, Ive heard a lot of "this is how to be a warrior" and "warriors cant do this" rants and witnessed many of them being disproved.

A good example of such is the general attitude towards warriors in PvP. The other day I grouped up with 3 mages to complete a non-ellite quest. These guys were built for dps and assumed that it was all that mattered in pvp. When we had all turned in the quest they asked if I wanted to duel assuming that any warrior, especially a warrior with almost all sta gear and a prot build, would be a pushover. It turned out that I was probably the hardest opponent that they could have chosen. They all tried to kite me and nuke me into the ground but their dps builds were so inefficient that when they hit low mana I still had a good 30% of my health left. With imp shield bash and concussion blow the duels were easily won at this point.

Anyway...
Im not trying to say that my build is good against dps mages. What Im trying to get across is that when people assume that there are only certain ways of being effective at a task they are usually wrong. Obviously warriors can be very speced out with our stances and talent trees and I think that blizzard intended to create more ways of being effective at tasks by giving us these specs instead of restricting certain specs to certain tasks.
