Problems I have with the leadership points:

Let me preface this by saying I'm both the guild leader and raid leader for my guild. I'm not the MT (usualy 2nd tank or ramp tank) but occasionly if the MT isn't on, I do that as well.

1) Technical problems (bugs and bad coding)
A) In every raid I've been on, both with my guild and guesting with others, the raid leader has also been a group leader. However, you can not accumulate raid leader and group leader xp simultaneously. This makes sense, but in order to maximize the xp for your raid, it's now neccesary for the raid leader to not be a group leader. By itself, this wouldn't belong in this section except...
B) If you transfer raid or group leadership to another person, you continue to receive the raid/group leader points, they don't. This is at least how it works in raids, not sure if it works the same in xp groups. This means that if you're forming the raid and you want to maximize the xp, you can't simply form your group and transfer group leadership to another player. Instead you must first raid invite the person you want to lead your group, then have them invite you. It's a pain in the ass and adds one more thing to worry about for a raid leader that's already dealing with other issues.
C) The raid leader xp bar is broken. I'm thinking it's just not correctly updating itself. When the xp bard fills up, you actualy have somewhere around 1/2 level and it will stay full (and unmoving) until you get enough xp to actualy ding.

2. Gameplay issues
A) xp groups fighting over who gets to be the leader is not fun. Restricting these points to just one person makes no sense unless the person designated leader (as opposed to the person that actualy runs the group) is inane.
B) OMG please give 0 group leader xp to group leaders in raids. I beg you SONY, do not give them points for leading groups in raids. I have more than enough issues to deal with while making groups as it is, I don't need 20 people asking me to be the group leader while I'm trying to make balanced effective groups. I'm having flashbacks to Vellious when everyone tried to be the KS group and get all the xp and I had to deal with that.

How to fix the system in one easy step:
Take all the group leader bonuses and make them AA's instead. Let those that enjoy leading groups buy those AA's. Then when we form groups instead of fighting over who gets to be leader, we can instead figure out who the most effective leader would be based on their points. People that enjoy leading groups will have the best group leader skills, those that don't won't bother buying them. Take the raid leader skills and make them drops from boss mobs. Make the drops semi rare/uncommon and either have boss mobs drop either a) an item that is redeemed with your trainer for a certain number of points or b) a tome like the melee discs that can be turned in for a specific raid leader ability.

Here's my take on leadership experience. I will refrain from working on any leadership experience until I have finished up most of what I consider the important AA's introduced in GoD. I might even consider knocking out a few more in PoP also before I tackle leadership points.

My reasoning is this:

I reduce my AA experience by possibly 20% for choosing to acquire group leadership points.

The value I gain from choosing leadership experience only kicks in if I am selected to be leader for a group. So, it may very well be that the leadership abilities I have earned may not be used at all at times. Whereas regular AA's are being used all the time whether I am selected as leader or not.

The value of someone elses hard work and effort for earning leadership experience is shared by the group, so basically I share in the rewards anyhow even though I didn't put out anything for it.

By not choosing leadership experience I don't consider that an obstacle to me getting a group because for not having chosen leadership points I have gained AA which is permanently available that adds to my effectiveness.

I could see where getting leadership AA early on would be an asset if you were consistently grouping with the same persons and you were always going to be the leader. This would allow you pretty much 100% use of leadership points spent to be used. Everyone would benefit for it, but you would have been the one to take a 20% loss in your regular AA acquisitions.

I think they can be fun, and have value. However, for me I see some real nice AA's out there which will keep me busy for awhile. Until I have acquired these AA's I probably won't focus much on leadership AA.
