First, you generally want the faster of your weapons in the primary hand, especially with both being the same delay. Next, use the formula 1ac = 6hp = 2sta (each sta should give you about 3hp, give or take). Keep upgrading your gear accordingly. Strength isn't terribly important, neither is dex or agi. Ac, hp and sta are king.

Grab some 6ac 65hp player made rings if you can, they tend to be fairly cheap for the benefit. Bazaar shop for other upgrades.

Okay, Karn, the places you'd probably get the most bang for your buck in upgrading armor are your greyhopper slots, your fingers, and your chest. You'll have to look around a bit to compare exactly what gets you the most AC/hps for the buck, but luckily you have some options.
first, go to the bazaar and search by slot, race, religion, class and some budget amount (let's say in your case, 1K). In each slot, write down the best AC item for <300 plat, <500 plat and less than <1000 plat (you might do separate budget searches to make that easier). Then go to the dwarf smithy merchants in PoK. A good number of players skill up on smithing in that place and sell the items they just made right back to the vendors there. Look in particular for LDoN armors (gothic, sandblasted) and see what's available, then check that against the bazaar prices.

Oh and before you buy stuff from merchants, spend 40 pp or so and get 2 opal encrusted steins. They are +25 cha each, and will soon pay for themselves even if you don't do a great deal of buying from vendors.

If you see LDoN armor on the vendors, you should be able to get, for example, a breastplate for around 100 plat. The gothic steel breastplate's stats are:
AC: 27
STR: +3 STA: +4
SV MAGIC: +6
WT: 5.5 Size: SMALL
Class: WAR CLR PAL SHD BRD
Race: HUM ERU ELF HIE DEF HEF
IKS FRG

As you can see, this would be a big boost to you, and several upgrades should be within your budget.
Shop carefully; take notes and compare what you can get per plat for each slot. I'd pay little attention to anything but AC, hitpoints, and STA on armor, but when competing pieces are close in AC, do weigh the other factors. Don't get in a hurry to buy stuff in the bazaar until you have a good sense of what your server's economy is like.
