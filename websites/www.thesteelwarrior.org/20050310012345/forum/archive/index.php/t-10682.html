Question for you all was what order do you think is most benificial?

Deflection is one of the best Talents you can get to increase your survivability.

Tactical Mastery is VERY handy, especially if you'll be expected to tank a lot or will do PVP some.

Anger Management is somewhat useful. It definitely helps in the rage department.

Improved Overpower is great, if somewhat unreliable (because of Overpower, not the talent).

Can't spear for the rest of the Arms stuff.

Cruelty is very nice. It will up your DPS by about 5%, which will also help your threat.

Anticipation is not going to do much for you during the process of gaining levels, wouldn't bother until later.

If you're planning on using a 2hander most of the time (I assume you are with MS), Toughness will probably help to offset the lack of shield. Really depends on what you think your role will be though.

Improved Revenge is pretty nice, but how often do you think you'll use it?

I know it seems like I'm down on Protection, but I'm really not. Those particular skills are just sort of "meh", except Imp Revenge.

In fact, I generally do what I expected, offtank aggro on multi-pulls and beat down mages while the Palys deal with harder hitting targets. This has worked fine for me thru RfD and SM recently.

My latest plan for Talents is very geared toward soloing. Basically, 31pts in Arms for MS (which I love) and 20pts in Fury for Enrage (very nice ability with 2h weps). Basically, I dropped Anger Management and a point in TM to get the 2pts I needed to max Enrage. I barely notice a difference in rage management. Just 5pts less rage when I switch to Zerker Stance.

Okay, enough about me. If I were playing up from lvl10 again, I'd drop my first 5 points in Cruelty. Then 5deflect, 3rend, 2TM, 2overpower, 3deepwounds, etc. At 40 respec to get MS and have 5cruelty by lvl45. From there I'd go Booming Voice, Imp. Shout, Enrage. That is just to max solo ability tho. By the time you're in the upper 50s you should know your usual role in groups/raid well enough to respec yourself into whatever warrior you want to be.

Lets see general advice: Get your Whirlwind Weapon as soon as you can, preferably in your low 30s with help. DO NOT buy gear every 2lvls like others have told me to do. I started buying the Lambent Scale set at lvl15. It wasn't useable fully til 22. I saved tons of money by planning gear in advance and not making impulse buys. The Lambent Set held me over til late 30s just fine. By then I was looting upgrades. Chief Brigadier's should be the next set you look at unless you can hold off upgrading til Heavy Mithril at 40-42. I started buying the full Heavy Mithril set at lvl30. After that, I saved for a mount. Last nite I dinged level 40 with a full set of Plate in the bank and over 100G to buy my mount. It took me one morning of farming to get enough money to buy all the class abilities I neglected from 32-40 (class trainer). For professions, I chose Mining + Enchanting. Mining is the most lucrative profession as long as you never take up smithing. As for Enchanting, I've never enchanted a single piece of gear. I disenchant my old Soulbound stuff I've replaced and any green/blue items that don't sell in the AH. I got my last round of enchants completely free b/c I was able to supply all the regeants!

That's alot to take in, but best of luck to you and enjoy yourself!
