The actual quest is probably one of the easiest you'll ever do. You'll probably finish everything up to camping Heratius Grolden in about 6 to 8 hours.

Camping the Vampyre tower is where it gets nasty. Heratius spawns on the balcony to the right if you're looking from the mountain behind or way to the left if you're inside the castle looking at the balcony from within. He hits for 160's, flurries, has 24k hp, relatively high ac, and lifetaps for 200pts. He will stay up for roughly 25 minutes after he spawns IF he is not taken out by the top floor named that roams in the process. Remember, Heratius is a traitor vampyre. The third floor named (Trix or something) will attack him and the centurion will add.

Upon Heratius's death spawns Valdanov right smack in the middle of the balcony. Valdanov is level 62, immune to melee unless it's vampyre bane or undead damage. He has 48k hp to the dime, which I will tell you how I concluded later on. He will double for up to 370 and has a random 1k lifetap proc. He is a necromancer, so he will try to cast dots AND HE WILL FEAR YOU Overall, he's pretty mean, but once slowed and crippled, even a shaman can keep the MT alive. Killing Valdanov will require anywhere from 6 to 12 people depending on their abilities. He really isn't that hard, but he can be without the right mix of people. Melee will do pathetic damage against this guy cause the weapons that hit him are 10/23 piercers.

Now, here's the deal though. Overall, the quest sounds pretty easy, right? It is, but camping Heratius can be very time consuming. I've sat there waiting for him to spawn for 40+ hours so far and expect to put anther 40+ before I see him spawn. He spawns roughly twice a week at no set pattern and stays up for a very short amount of time when he does.

Mages, beastlords, and necros are the bane of Valdanov. He is stunnable. Just make sure the pet receives the vampyre bane weapons as well, as they will not hit him even with the new PoP weapons that have +3 magic/fire/ice damage to them. Have the mage use the air pet. Have the beastlord load his warder's most useful stun. Have your necro go to town.

And HP - Because I've camped him for so long, I've gotten a pretty good feeling of how many hp each mob has. I've noticed that all the named have 12k hp. Always. I then started doing the math. I noticed Centurions have around 3k hp, generals have roughly 6k hp, named have 12k, Heratius Grolden has 24k hp, and I tested Valdanov with a 1k nuke from my mage and it took away 2% of his health. That would mean Valdanov has 48-50k hp on the spot. I was wondering too. People had said anywhere from 40 to 100k. At least now we know a definite amount.

Ok, I finished the quest last night. You won't believe this, but I think I have the formula for Heratius Grolden's potential spawn time.

****THEORY, NOT TO BE TAKEN AS FACT****

He spawns once at least every 60 hours. Within that potential 60 hour timeframe, his chance to spawn is every 3 hours. My examples to back this up:

The following are his spawn times for the week:
-Monday ~4pm
-Thursday ~2am
-Friday ~9am
-Saturday ~10pm

The difference in hours to these spawn times divided by 60 all roughly end up in multiples of 5%, hence why it is my opinion that his chance to spawn is every 3 hours. Now, before you guys go nailing me to the chalkboard in bad mathematics, these are approximate spawn times that I received mostly by word of mouth. Heratius will stay up for 25 minutes while Valdanov can stay up for 45 minutes. Necromancers often solo the castle and even solo Heratius, but will leave Valdanov up because he's simply unsoloable. Heratius will NOT spawn while Valdanov is up, so that adds roughly an hour discrepancy between the spawn times.

Now, using this formula and a wildly good guess, I predicted on Friday that Heratius would spawn at roughly 7pm on Saturday evening. To my suprise, he spawned exactly 3 hours later.
