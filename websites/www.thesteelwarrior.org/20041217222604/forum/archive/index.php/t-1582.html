Originally posted by Lessiter1
I feel like I keep repeating myself, but a while ago, (1-2 years? just after CT was revamped.. went in the same time that the "slows can be removed by a cure disease" fix went in.) slow agro was changed considerably because Shaman/enchanters were getting pasted by the high level mobs in CT.

As a side effect, the agro on a slow proc weapon was reduced significantly. Willsapper, wavecrasher, ToD, etc..


No it wasn't.

Well, sort of with the ToD, but here's what happened. Disease counters were added to shaman and enchanter spells so they could be cured. This made aggro on them insane. For some reason disease spells had higher aggro than other spells. Consequently, they lowered the aggro on disease spells so slow aggro would be similar to what it was before the change (side effect was also nerfing the aggro on the low-level SK dot).

This ONLY happened on shaman/enchanter spells. Breath of the Sea (wavecrasher proc) and Energy Sap (willsapper proc) are not shaman/enchanter spells. They never had any disease counters added to them. They are not cureable (good to know in PvP). They never had their aggro increased or reduced in any way.

Other slow spells (like the ToD proc which is an enchanter spell) still have plenty of aggro. I'm sure that any warrior that groups in PoP knows that. They just don't have the insane aggro that they had for a few days after the disease counters were added. Slow procs are still great aggro, and also very useful when grouping without a slower. Willsapper and wavecrasher are both great aggro weapons, the same as they have always been.
