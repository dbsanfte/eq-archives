This build is specifically for PVP and is intended for a Level 60 Warrior. I don't think it would be prudent to start trying to build this until you're 60 because you lose quite a bit of dps from the Fury shouts.

So let me quickly state the pro's and cons :

Pros

-Maintain a good natural crit rate or mastery for whatever weapon you choose.
-Have as much AC as a Prot build, excluding the defensive skills and mastery.
-Get a great skill : Concussion Blow (In my opinion, outweighs the usefullness of Sweeping Strikes or MS for PVP currently)
-Get yet another stunning skill, although hard to dish out - Improved Revenge
-Get to use Bloodrage more often with less danger because of Improved Bloodrage (lowers the health cost of the skill)
-Get passive resists against Charm (Fear?) and Stun. Though I don't exactly know how resists work with those stats.
-Have some of the useful attack skills Arms tree : Improved Rend, Deep Wounds


Cons

-You lose quite a bit of DPS from not getting the Fury skills (If you were Fury Build) and Improved Battle Shout. You also lose some DPS from getting only 4% of 2H Specialization.
-You don't get Mortal Strike or Sweeping Strikes
-Concussion blow has a 45 second cooldown (Ridiculous for a talent I think)
-As a previous Prot build, you'd miss out on higher tiered protection skills like : 1H Mastery, Improved Disarm, Last Stand, etc.


Coming from an early prot build, then to Arms/Fury, I realized quickly that a Warrior can be useful in any stance for PVP. The key factor in winning though, as a warrior especially, is disabling your opponent. Though you lose a bit of DPS if you were Fury or some of the duration talents for your Prot skills if you were Prot build, you do maintain a good crit rate that is both important in PVP and PVE. You also gain the use of concussion blow, which, even with it's long cooldown, can actually turn the tide of battle especially in smaller confrontations.

The 10% more AC will also undoubtedly help in any situation. Not to mention the extra Charm and Stun resistance.

Improved Revenge, although a quite isolated move, would actually snap off often because classes like Rogues would dodge quite a lot. I actually think Improved Revenge is better than Improved Overpower. I mean, the stun is 5 seconds.

Again, this build is only viable with the current patch and only when you're already level 60. Go Arms/Fury if you wanna kill or Prot if you wanna be the instance king before then. You're not gonna have the talent points to get the most useful skills till you're 60 anyway.

Which leads me to another topic/complaint -- Tactical Mastery is a necessity now... WHY do we need to have it as a talent? Can't it just be inherent to free us a few more talent points?

Again, this is a suggestion of mine. I've used all the skills I have above when I was in different builds. I find that those skills were the ones I used and relied on the most when I played in those different builds. Of course, if you prefer different weapons or skills such as 1H mastery, you can modify the build to still give you the best aspects and not lose it's integrity.

This build has a crit/Arms edge because in my time playing, I've found that especially in PVP, the Arms tree is one of the most useful trees to have with you.

If this has been done a lot before, sorry for missing out on people's suggestions. Really though, on my server, most people are Arms/Fury, Fury/Arms, or Arms/Prot. This build is technically Arms/Prot, but it doesn't go deep enough into any tree to get the 'Ultimates' that most builds usually do. I actually think our 'Ultimates' are mediocre right now at best. But of course, you guys can show me how MS or SS should be used in a PVP battle and I'll try it. From my experience though, Bloodthirst was probably the talent that had the most noticeable effects.

I know... or hope that the Warrior will get some good changes coming and that could turn this whole build obsolete, but currently, I see it as being very useful especially in PVP.

Suggestions? Ideas?
