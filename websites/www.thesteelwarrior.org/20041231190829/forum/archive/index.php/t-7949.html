The weapon with better ratio should be in the main hand. With today's available gear damage bonus is a small portion of your total DPS and consequently aggro (it is not even clear if damage bonus even affects aggro). When dealing with 2Handers, the weapon with higher delay should be used (1hander rarely has enough variation in delay to matter). Why? Let's say you have a 60/40 and a 30/20. You'd think they produce the same amount of aggro, but actually the 60/40 is better, because assuming aggro is a linear function of damage, then let's say 60 damage weapon produce 500 aggro per swing, then the 30 damage weapon produces 250 aggro per swing. However, on your first swing, the 60/40 starts with 500 aggro as opposed to 250 on 30/20, and the difference is considerable when dealing with snap aggro. Also, since procs are scaled to delay, the 60/40 also has twice as high a chance of getting a proc on the first swing as opposed to the 30/20. They may average out to the same aggro over time but the weapon with same ratio but higher delay actually produces considerably more aggro for the initial parts of a fight.

DB + damage way overestimates the effect of DB.

At level 65 the max hit is about 6Xdamage + DB... so even if you hit half of that on average, that still gives you 3Xdamage + DB to work with.

Obviously, the multiplier can go up or down depending on what kind of attack buffs you get, worn attack, and what kind of mobs you're fighting (mostly against Luclin mobs).

If you assume you've a 20 delay weapon, max haste, assuming 50% hit rate (low estimate) and 100% double attack (high estimate to cancel out), you get 1 hit per second, or 13 DPS from damage bonus out of your entire dual wield DPS. If you have Sinister Strikes, add about 6 DPS to a total of 19 DPS from damage bonus. On the high end warriors can do 200 DPS easily so only 1/10th of DPS comes from DB. So let's say you're not so uber so your weapon acts like half the ratio of an uber (i.e. 32/26 Demerix becomes 16/26, which is certainly obtainable) after factoring in all the non-uberness, that still leaves you about 100 DPS with DB attributing to only 1/5th the DPS.
