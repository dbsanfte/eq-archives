Battleblade
At my level I don't use ToD in groups much anymore, but when I do:

(Works best with a Ranger also trying to slow). Acquire aggro with the best possible aggro setup and Bellow every time it pops. Switch in ToD. Look for resisted Slows. Get two resists and check mob's HP's. If the mob still has high HP's swap in a Journeyman's Walking Stick. As soon as you get a debuff, swap in ToD again. If you end up killing the mob unslowed with ToD, don't bother trying to slow that mobtype again.

So the short answer is ToD's not a good aggro weapon. Believe me, if there was a Tashini aug that could be put on ToD I'd do it so fast yer head would spin. But Anger III or Force of YKesha is like putting a racing saddle on a plow horse. Those augs belong on the best aggro weapon you can lay yer hands on - not ToD.

It's somewhat surprising what it can slow, but like any weapon that procs you might not see one in any given fight. If you can't take the beating from the mob unslowed, then ya need a slower.

ToD is teh win soloing easier blue mobs - like Kael Arena.

Fitemore
A ToD is decent aggro, but only if it procs obviously. When DCing with my cleric in Sol Ro, if I didn't get a proc fairly early I could lose aggro from the cleric's nukes and healing. With WA5 and something like anger 3 or ykesha, I'm sure it would hold ok aggro. It's definitely not a premium aggro weapon, but it can suffice if the slows are really worth it. Also, definitely switch it out after it slows the mob. 3 minutes should be enough time to kill most things, and it's just not good enough DPS or aggro to keep up always. Augment it only if you NEED to slow the mobs and you NEED to hold aggro at the same time.
