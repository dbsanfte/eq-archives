It *is* quite possible that he has been stunned right at incoming, in which case he'd have almost no aggro. Lets assume a worst case scenario, where he only had one round of attack to build aggro before he got stunned. No procs yet would be likely, and he might not even have had a bow shot. He might, or might not have gotten off his Bazu Bellow (level 69 version of provoke line), but we'll allow for it. That's still not a lot of aggro !

Using the hate formula that Valkargen supplied in the thread, we get a formula something like this :

Mainhand = (D + DB) x HM
Offhand = D x HM

D = Weapons Damage
DB = Weapons Damage Bonus
HM = Hate Multiplier

Vanazir, being a 2H weapon, is therefore a Main hand weapon.
(it has a Damage Bonus of 47 at level 70 according to LUCY)
Thus you get :

(60 + 47) * 1.2 = 128.4 hate per hit

If he's unlucky, he doesn't roll a double attack either and Ripostes don't count as aggro AFAIK.
So now all he has is what his bow gave (if he used one) + Bazu Bellow + 1 round of attack.

Lets say he used a bow with real low damage (ie. low hate too), or he skipped it on that one and opted for Bazu Bellow right away instead, we remove that part. We already saw that 1 round of attack with a 2H weapon can be as little as 1 hit and no proc = 128.4 hate. And Bazu Bellow is 600 initual and 100 every tick afterwards for 1 minute.

That's 128.4 + 600 = 728.4 hate as a minumum, probably a tick as well ... so 128.4 + 600 + 100 = 828.4 hate.

I do not know how much hate a heal generate .... but if it's half the amount healed (that's what I've heard once long ago, for direct heals), you could have had 1648hp / 2 = 824 hate.

That's awfully close, *and* you did it more recently, so that might affect the AI to pick on you too. Offcourse, this is all speculative, since we do not have the source code and all that. And most of this hinges on how much aggro you get from the spell.

Yep, as your experience points out, it's hard to generate a one-size-fits-all reading for aggro in different situations.
Warrior aggro is generated in various ways, including (and this is not in any particular order):
tagging incoming mob with range weapon
using the incite line
potential damage from melee weapons (and slam/kick)
weapon procs
proximity to mob (closer= >hate)
taunt
certain AAs (area of effect taunt and weapon affinity, two take two quite different examples)

Notice that most of these require some time to build; a warrior with decent aggro-generating tools will almost always have much more solid aggro late in a fight than he does in the first few rounds after he engages the mob. Incite is instant, but procs are come in streaks and are progressively more important to warrior aggro as the warrior advances. The warrior in the original thread could face the same mob twice, try to do the same things in the same order, and your fate as fast healer could be different.
Say, in case one, warrior tags incoming mob, incites, gets in a couple of rounds of melee, gets a nice proc, a couple more melee rounds and then your heal lands. Very likely, the mob stays on the warrior & you live.
But in another case, warrior tags incoming mob, incites, mob stuns warrior on first round, warrior recovers, gets round of melee, gets stunned again, doesn't get a proc, your heal lands...oh oh. If the weapon here has a fair proc, it even doesn't much matter whether the warrior's got a two-hander or two one-handers in the second case: neither will be procing or adding potential dps hate if all the warrior is seeing is a stun message and a spinning room.
Neither you nor the warrior did anything different; the random number generator simply doesn't shake your hand the same way each time.

Theoretically with this warrior, there are three avenues of cutting down on the likelihood of the second scenerio:
1) the warrior can increase AC/hps/resists/AAs so that he lasts a little longer before he needs a heal and is less likely to be stunned (and thus gives his weapons more time to proc/add potential melee damage)
2) the warrior can increase the quality and number of his weapon and hate augs
3) The guild/group can attempt to use a Knight for ramping early aggro as Anker suggests

Depending on the mob and random number generator, none of these things is a lock, though. If they do start being a lock for a given mob, it's quite likely that you'll just move to a tougher mob and have a new/improved set of problems.

Of course, maxxing any aggro reduction tools you have (including AAs) wouldn't hurt, but I doubt any lack of AAs on your part or the weapon the warrior was carrying was the "only" thing that caused the results you experienced.
The fastest fix of course for you as a patch healer is to quickly volunteer to be in a different group than the main tank, lol.
