Ok, I’ve read through these posts the last few days until I, actually, could not blink my eyes. Of course, there has actually been absolutely no progress in the debate of warrior balancing. So, I decided to come up with a healthy number of simple yes or no, fact or fiction statements and, questions. Mind you I am referring to the average player here, not the end game player, nor the occasional player. First I’d like to begin with my facts.

Fact:Warrior’s taunt is broke.

Fact: Having to depend on chanter + hate spells or weapon procs is a crutch.

Fact: In an experience setting, our disciplines are hardly used enough to be considered as a utility or group benefit.

Fact: The average Warrior DPS is nominal in fact quite laughable at times.

Fact: If a Warrior is not tanking he is dead weight and can be replaced by ANY class.

Fact: Perception is everything, if it is perceived that the Warrior class are second rate tanks and has less than adequate agro management, they will get second rate exp.

Fact: Warriors have no utility thus killing any sort of “plate” stacking unless it’s a group of friends or there is no other option at the time of group creation.

Fact: Warriors are COMPLETELY group dependant for any kind of exp.

Ok, a few fictions.

Fiction: The only job, in an experience setting, for a Knight should be to tank. Numerous roles are out there just the knights are primarily selected for the tank slot.

Fiction: A Knight’s spell book doesn’t land them groups. (I have been in countless groups that have enlisted a Knight for their utilities be it the SK FD or the Pally Heals.)

Fiction: Knights need to be nerfed.

Fiction: All Warriors get to tank Boss Mobs.

Fiction: All Warriors have access to the weapons or PP needed to effectively tank in an experience setting.

At last a few Questions.

1. Why when grouping with a War should the other members of a group have to hold back.

When I play my 65 warrior with 70+ AA I get to play ALL OUT I am holding nothing back because if I do I lose agro and players Die. But in order for the group to function correctly and smoothly the other players have to hold back. Yet when they play with a Knight tanking, they get to enjoy the Full power of there class by going all out on every mob.

2. How does nerfing the Knight class fix Warrior agro?

Nobody likes a nerf; the Knights in this game pay the same amount of money every month to play this game just like me. I chose the path of a Warrior because it was my choice no one made me do it. But as this game has evolved there is a disparity in the two classes that have given the knights an advantage that is what needs to be addressed. Warrior agro needs to be increased some how, smacking the Knights with a nerf bat will NOT fix this.

3. Why is it now harder for me at 65 with 70+ AA and slightly above average weapons to hold agro than it was at 40 fighting Drolvargs in DL?


And finally a summary:

I love playing my warrior I have slightly above average weapons average AA and I have been playing for quite a while. There have always been class disparities between the plate tank classes. But, the current state of affairs is simply frustrating for the Warrior class. Why is simple. The uncontested agro management of the Knights has left the Warriors far behind when it comes to exp groups. Even grouped with skilled players it is a chore for a warrior to get and keep agro. The warrior can only maintain agro IN AVERAGE SETTINGS at the expense of the rest of the group members holding back. A warrior brings NO utility to a group if it already has a tank. All MELE only characters are completely gear and group dependant. It is time for the war class to be addressed by the Developers. We need to put down our silly pre pubescent petty arguments and seriously come up with a tangible fix that would address our core needs as warriors.

Aeka
I really meant no disrespect. I seriously see Taunt as working exactly the way as it is designed. The problem is it isn't good enough any more.

The only reason Taunt exists in the game is because the original EQ engine doesn't support server side object collisions. All collision detection the player sees is done by the client (which leads to some early specular hacks). There is now som rudimentary enforcement of "space" but it is far from complete.

So what does this have to do with Taunt? Well the very idea of tanking goes something like this....


Burniator ---> <----Aeka {some distance behind} Glassjaw Enchanter


Simply put as a tank you want to put yourself between Burniator and Glassjaw. How do you accomplish this when "space" is not enforced? Enter Taunt!

Taunt simulates blocking the monster from reaching whatever it is it really should be attacking. In a sense offering yourself as the only target. Taunt failures represent "misteps" in covering your friends.

Because of SOE's "black box design" philosophy on Melee no one is really sure of the mechanics of Taunt. Best fit description of Taunt works something like this.

1. The monster remembers who did what on an agro list. It is ordered by who does the most anoying stuff.

2. Players as they interact (read: try to kill the monster ^_^) they can move up and down this list.

3. The monster selects a target to attack from the top of the list. If the monster can move it will close the distance. If the monster can summon at the moment then it will summon. If that target is "unavailable" it will select the next one on the list.

4. What does Taunt do? Taunt if successful will make the monster ignore the list and replace the Taunter at the target to attack.

Taunt itself appears to not appear to add hate. It only tells the monster to ignore the agro list for a moment.

This may not be exactly what happens behind the scenes but it seems like an accurate model and accounts for a lot of behavior seen in the game. It isn't perfect because it doesn't explain certain choices the monster makes (when does a monster summon? when does a monster move? where does low health figure in?). In any event Taunt is a powerful skill. We take it for granted every day a skill that basically rewires the simplistic AI of monsters.

However even as powerful as Taunt is it sucks that Warrior's mainline strategy is to use it constantly. It should have been a "last resort" instead of a "first attempt".
