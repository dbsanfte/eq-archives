I think there is a world of difference between diminishing the number of Clerics required on a raid and making Clerics irrelevent by class blurring. The same thing is more or less true of Paladins.

I've no problem at all with SoE making raid encounters that don't require 10 Clerics or 5 Warriors. However that does not necessarily mean that 10 healers with all their healing AA's, deep mana pools, FT15, and healing focus items can't be required. Nor does it mean that 5 Tanks within a few 100 AC or HP's of theoretical maximum at their progression level can't be required.

All too often threads like this are used to try to drum up support for class blurring or the removal of pure class specialization advantages. Frankly, I've been on successful raids in KT+ without 10 Clerics or 5 Warriors. Those guilds that require more often aren't near the apex of preparedness in the first place.

It's not a class balance issue. It's an encounter design and ready for the content issue.

Besides, would you *cough* have a quota system with 1/16th of the spots in every guild going to each class. Or would it be more reasonable for SoE to design content to reflect the percentage of players at each progression level of each class. If there are 500,000 Warriors and 10 Beserkers ready for Uqua, shouldn't Warriors be more needed than Beserkers in Uqua?

In simple terms you think too much healing, tanking, and damage dealing (giggle) is being done in these encounters and that pure class specialists shouldn't be the best at their narrow focused specialzations.

I think the complaint is more about the fact that SoE is focusing so much on small numbers of bosses with massive DPS output to make encounters difficult rather than designing encounters that require the effective use of a wide range of abilities and classes. An encounter with 2 bosses that can't be split and both put out 3k DPS is no more difficult than one with a single hard hitting boss plus some mezable and unmezable OT mobs, some of which cast curses, mana drains, dispells, memblurs, etc that have to be countered by a variety of classes. The difference is that the first encounter requires 2 warriors and 10 or more clerics. The rest really don't matter, they're just DPS. The second type requires lots of CC people, lots of healers (notice healers does not mean only clerics), lots of tanks (this includes knights), lots of support classes for cures, refreshing junk buffs, AE heals, etc.

The first type of fight requires zero effort to design and success is determined by a vary narrow focus on heal timing, aggro control, and how well you have your main tanks geared up. The second type depends on having a well balanced raid force where everyone is doing their job.

Requiring 20% of a raid to be clerics is as retarded as requiring 20% of any other class would be. Changing that does NOT require making shamans or druids or anyone else heal even close to as well as a cleric. It just requires a little more imagination designing encounters.
