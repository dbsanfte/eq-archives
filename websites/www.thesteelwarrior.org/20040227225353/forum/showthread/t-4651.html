evilsofa
Don't forget that when you're doing the math about how much you lose for each recipe, you need to account for your failure rate. "Assuming no failures" will get you a number that is actually incorrect. amd can be significantly far off the mark.

For lowlevel recipes like we're talking about here, you can assume the player has a skill level high enough to get the maximum success rate which is supposed to be 95 percent. This means that, on average, out of every 20 combines you should fail 1. I've done a lot of hardcore tradeskilling and I can tell you that you can take this to the bank - you might see lucky streaks on short runs of less than 60 or so, but in the long run you WILL get that 5 percent failure rate.

Therefore, a recipe can appear to give a small profit for each combine "assuming no failures", but if you account for a 1 out of 20 failure rate, the recipe will actually lose a small amount of money.
