Brabble
I agree that surrounding the general rule are a number of other considerations.
If you're talking about bazaar gear and bang for the buck, sometimes you can get farther by considering multiple slots at once. Typically, you're not going to get huge Ac upgrades from rings and earrings, to take an example, but they can be a very good place to get hps and resists, which might allow you to consider a higher AC/hps ratio on some other piece of armor.

I actually think that in many cases the harder balancing act is to figure out whether to use your resources to get an upgrade now, or hold out for a bigger upgrade later, a problem that exists outside of (or in addtion to) strict AC-to-hps ratios. Generally it's more "efficient" to hold out for the best upgrade you can, but obviously you have to be geared well enough to do your job no matter your progression point, so everyone has to undertake some sort of balancing act calculation.
lokkak
Many accept the unwritten rule of 1 AC = 6 HP. I generally use this rule when considering upgrades.

For most of GoD, "1 AC to 5 HP" to "1 AC to 8 HP" is the spread. The "percent reduction for damage taken produced by the increased AC" is worth "the amount of HP you need to survive to the next heal" with these ranges.

Having less AC means you WILL take more damage on average, so having enough additional HP to soak up the increase will be the tradeoff.
