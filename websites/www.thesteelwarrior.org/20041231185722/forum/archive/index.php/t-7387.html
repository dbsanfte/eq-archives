The SK strategy goes like this. Assuming no HT (it's basically impossible to win against a SK that uses HT as a warrior), a SK can do maybe 70-80% of your HP damage in straight melee before he runs out. Hola-

Here's some ideas for you to "level" the playing field a bit. Expensive, but worth doing if you need it.

-Since you have a shammy, you have a ready source of IG toe potions (stinging wort?) Anyway, an instant cast 300pt heal that can be made into 10 shot, 3k in a second or two if you click fast. BIG equalizer to the pally/SK, since you can probably carry more potions than they have manapools (i.e. 1 10 slot backpack = 30k hp). Expect to be called a cheater tho, they won't like it. ;)

-If you are rich and/or elemental tagged, consider getting a driftwood compound bow (50 pt. mana tap)... it'll help, but bow procs are hella rare so don't count on it. There's also a dagger (insanity crawler fang?) that has the same proc.

-fight the SK at the BOTTOM OF A SMALL VALLEY. shadowstep ALWAYS places the caster at a location equal to or lower than the starting location that has NO higher elevation between the start/end loc, and never goes through walls or terrain of any type. I.e. if you cast shadowstep at the bottom of a pit, you don't go anywhere. You could also get your own shadowstep item, war useable boots drop in the vamp castle in teb mtns (fairly long cast tho).

-use a bio orb to blind them (cast time isn't too bad, good range), snare glands and/or sceptre of forlorn to snare them, or crystal webs to root. Forlorn totem of rallos zek (stonebrunt drop) is also a decent right click stun, tho I'm not sure it'd stick to a level 65 toon.

-get a pet to help follow them and/or interrupt their casting. Easiest one drops in uguk, but is extremely low level elemental (brasier of some type, from summoner), can also do gnollslayer quest for a level 30ish shammy pet. I'd imagine all the old sk pet summoning teeth are gone by now.

-Amulate of necropotence + 2x shrink = very tiny skeleton, MUCH harder for them to joust you and keep track of where you are at. Won't stop them casting on you, but will make meleeing them much easier. Shammy illusion potions for small races also work if an AoN isn't an option.

-duel in a water zone like the ferrott; pc's can't cast into or out of water. Watch for casting message, jump in or out as needed. Cheezy but works and evens out the playing field considerably.

-Also cheezy, but amazingly effective vs. fair weather duelers, is fake emotes that look like /discs being used. :)

-and weapon selection... ToD for slowing, tash stick for debuff, there's some nice dot weapons out nowdays, stun procs are good, and if you can get one, so are lifetaps.

Just some ideas.

You might be able to take advantage of the fact that these guys play on a blue server and play easy PvP classes, so may not have much skill. Their gear may not be PvP appropriate, etc. First of all, pick one of the two classes to prepare for. The Paladin for example...it's probably the one that's easier to prepare for. Have a suit of gear that is solely dedicated to your having good MR and hardcore AC, HP, regen etc. Kite with the manatap bow whenever the mana DOT isn't on him and joust otherwise with a high damage high delay two hander. If people at your level have high resists as a matter of course, you can ignore resistable proccing weapons in favor of pure damage, AC etc. Damage shields are nice but in a jousting fight probably not that useful because hits on you will be rare and for high damage. I always remember blue server tanks (and tanks played by crappy PvP players) having bad resists. I remember owning an even SK with my Wizard, a fight which favors the SK, mostly because of resist differences...his Spears bounced off me and my nukes landed on him.

The only thing a Paladin will have on you really will be stuns, buffs and massive healing which is equivalent to many more HP...and channelling too. Your advantages are slightly better DPS, better AC and HP unbuffed (not counting heals), innate MR advantages, and being better prepared for the class matchup/terrain/PvP in general.

One tactic will be to win through boredom. The fight will be long and boring, and since the Paladin has the inherent advantage and is usually the better duelling class against most anyone, he will be overconfident, and especially as the fight goes longer and longer will be more and more likely to do something stupid. Don't let this happen to you and try to get him to make mistakes and capitalize on them. You can use psychology too by taunting him and not letting his taunts get to you or affect your game.

Terrain should help too. The more extreme a zone, the more of an element of unpredictability there will be, and the more of an advantage there will be to someone who knows the zone well...Lavastorm would be good like Remf suggested, and zones with aggro mobs or ones that you have high faction with could help too. If you have high faction, that's kind of unfair, but if the mobs are just generally aggro then they are equally a factor to both of you and it's fair but an advantage to someone who's more prepared/experienced. Knockback items are good for extreme terrain fights.

Also, in general, make sure you have your PvP skills. Know how to joust, and practice often in duels against any melees and hybrids you meet. Have instaclicks to protect your buffs against dispells, have dispell items, have instacast items, have items that cast or proc cool spells like Snare or Blind, be prepared to be disarmed and be fast at redrawing your sword or axe if it happens, be able to swap weapons or items quickly as you need to, have emotes that look like discipline use or that say you died or whatever, make sure you disarm the opponent, etc etc etc. Make sure you dispell the hell out of him because he's going to be highly buffed. You might be able to dispell off his heal over times too.

Warriors do have more killshot ability than Paladins because of crits and cripples, especially when dual wielding with fellstrike as was already mentioned. I remember jousting a Warrior with my Ranger on Tallon Zek and being utterly surprised by his Weighted Axe crit killshot. I went from like 30-40% to DEAD if I remember correctly, though it's been a long time.

Also, if you get him to Lay on Hands, you might want to begin prolonging the fight while whittling away at him with the manatap bow and any DOT proccing items you can find so that you can regen your HP back to full. Using Lay on Hands is for the purposes of the duel a nearly permanent penalty for the Paladin, unless the fight gets REALLY long, which might happen for Warrior vs Paladin now that I think about it.
