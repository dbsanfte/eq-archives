Second, I always assumed that Stamina was the end-all-be-all stat for warriors, but in some of the discussions here and especially on the EQLive boards, I see people talking about a somewhat more balanced approach with at least a nod to Dex, Agi and Str. 95% of all warriorrs will never need charisma. It's useful for the very top of the very top. Even at the stage of the game I'm at, I could never see burning the 2 emeralds for Divine Intervention on a regular basis. If you needed charisma, you'd know by now (IE : You'd already have your time+ guild lined up and just ready for you to roll up your toon so they can tag you, flag you and PL you).

For everyone else, make stamina your primary concern. Some hippies are gonna try to tell you that all stats count because of your endurance pool, but that's a load of crap. For levels 1-50, you're not gonna be able to use the endurance fast enough anyway, and by 50, you should be getting close to the stamina cap anyway. Worry about AC, HP and Stamina first, everything else after that.

Brenin,
Haass is correct concerning diety options, the role of charisma, STA, and race.
If you like the look of a dark elk, roll 'em up. Celestias's comments in this regard are also good.
I'd stress the diety armor factor, particularly given that you plan to get to 50 fairly quickly and seem to have the means to do so. Rallos Z is probably the best choice, primarily because that cultural armor is as good as it gets as a cultural set, and because sets that are more or less equivalent, such as Tribunal, are less often made by smiths.
With PoK [Plane of Knowledge] vendors and the bazaar you just don't have to worry much about faction to deal with everyday bank/gear/travel issues.
I think your idea of spending 3-5K early and saving the rest is reasonable. You can get individual pieces of armor from the crafted, crustacean, or LDoN sets (Gothic, Frosted) for around 100-200 pp each and they will easily carry you to 40ish; you can start looking at the cultural armor and get a feel for the prices on your server and pick up pieces of it that are good deals as you go.
I haven't leveled a warrior since level restrictions were imposed on weapons, so I'm not able to give the best advice on which weapons will be best for a low-level warrior. I believe that the centi weapons (short sword, long sword, hammer) do not have level restrictions, and would be fine for you to start out with. There are slightly better weapons that cost more, but those are quite usable. If you don't know what level restrictions are or how to tell how they affect a piece of gear that you are loooking at, find out from your friends before you buy stuff, heh).
At level 50, if you are substantially in cultural armor and have a copper hammer of striking and sword of the bloodsworn (CHoS and SotBS around here) you'll be better geared than most warriors, and it will be gear that can carry you to 65 (and basically until you are able to fight mobs that drop better stuff).
One thing that you'll need to think about is that although 20K is a great leg up, it's not enough to outfit yourself at level 50. Save your plat as you level, and keep an eye out for good deals. Also keep in mind that eventually you will want some form of haste, which can be got from worn items, augments and/or tribute (casters will also cast haste on you, but in addition you want worn haste through your gear or, later in your career, tribute).
Warriors basically do one thing better than any other class in the game: absorb/otherwise deal with damage from mobs. That's your funtion as main tank, and it's the primary reason groups and raids want warriors: you get the mob to beat on you and you alone while your group/raid kills it. When Haass says AC, hps and STA are the important things to focus on, that's why: all of them directly relate to improving your ability to perform your primary role in a group or a raid. To do your job, you have to generate aggro--that is, be the character that the mob wants most to kill. That's why on this board you see so much discussion about aggro-generation: if the mob ain't hitting you, you can't be doing your job as main tank.

Ask questions here if you need help with anything. this is a great site and some of the folks here know a tremendous amount about the game and the warrior class.

I'd also suggest that you not spend all of your time pre-level 50 getting powerleveled. Do hunt in groups before then so that you learn how to play a warrior in a group before you get to level 50. Try to hunt in indoor and outdoor zones so that you are comfortable in either type of zone.

Help your friends whenever you can. To progress as a warrior, you will need the help of your friends, so return the favor cheerfully.

Finally, you are a warrior, so never whine; it's shameful. Bitching, of course, is different matter entirely.

I have played a Wood Elf warrior for 4+ years now. The only regret I have ever had in his initial rolling was when they put out cultural armor. I'm agnostic, and the racial armor for elves sucks. For a while I really had wished I could wear that RZ armor.

Dex/Agi/Cha are worth concerning yourself with a little. However, with your starting stats as a DE you should be fine on dex/agi. Cha is only usefull for raiding, and even then I have DI fire quite regularly with just a shaman buff on. Other than that, cha is just usefull for buying/selling, and you can get two steins for that in the bazaar for next to nothing (Opal Encrusted?).

Starting stats are almost irelevant. The only advantage Ogres have over any other race today is stun resistance, and with some AAs you can overcome that as well. Pick the race that you would enjoy, don't let anyone tell you any different.

As far as twinking goes......Find yourself a decent haste item and a pretty good set of weps to start out. Make sure to get at least one item from each skill set to keep that skill up. 1hs/2hs/1hb/2hb/pierce You don't need a good one from each area, but at least decent ones to keep your skills up. You never know when you're gonna come across a really good item, so you wanna have the skill up as you go instead of spending forever trying to raise 2hb on blue mobs.

Dunno about prices, but I would find a good haste item, a few good weps, and as much RZ armor as you can afford.
