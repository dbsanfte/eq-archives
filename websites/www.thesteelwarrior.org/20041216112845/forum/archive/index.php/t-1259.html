It does kind of seem like SoE considers mana some kind of freebie, or semi-irrelevancy. Why they should value mana at 1/10th of a hp (or whatever that pretty low value is, assuming they consider warrior and paladin PoP charms balanced) is hard to see. Even Grimmlokk's sort of proposed ratio of 3-4/1 is *substantially* more than now, and would in fact end up with what some might think of as crazy looking charms.

Just as a thought experiment: A 65 paladin in normal gear has what? 3k mana? 3.5k mana? If there were a correction from 1-10 hp/mana to 1-3 hp/mana, that would mean adding 700 hps to the average 65 warrior?

1/10 ratio = 300 hps = 3000 mana
1/3 ratio = 1000 hps = 3000 mana
difference = 700 hps

There are 14 slots, so 700/14 = 50, so the charm should have 50 extra hps on it (assuming the correction, etc).

Anyway, I'm not proposing adding 700 hps to every 65 warrior, or even 50 to the charm, but I don't think it's crazy, from a class balance perspective, to consider seriously changing the relation between hp and mana. Changing this relation would be a generalized way to balance caster and melee classes.

By the way, I see from EQRankings that well-equipped paladins are easily breaking 4k mana, and some have over 5k mana. There are 1522 paladins in EQRankings (of all levels, unfortunately). The modal paladin (position number 761) in that list when sorted by mana, has just over 3k mana. I think when the non-65s are eliminated the number would rise to around 3500, but EQRankings doesn't let you do that, and I didn't feel like copying and pasting all that data.

EDIT: Okay, one important point. The 1/10 ratio is derived simply from the difference in the pop charm, standing in for gear in general. However, the average warrior has maybe 800 hps more than the average paladin (less in my guild though - I have 500 more than our guild knights), who still has his average 3500 mana. On that basis, SoE is valuing mana at 1/4 approximately of an hp. BUT, that's blurred by gear, like charms! They appear to have two standards: innate and gear. One could argue that it's itemization that's been killing us, or that fact that paladins are looting armor with 90% of the hps and mana at a substantially higher ratio than they used to get, or do get innately. Dunno if this all makes sense.

EDIT: Okay, I created some magelos: Nakedwar and Nakedpal. Both are human, level 65, with no AA. Nakedwar put 20 points into sta and 5 into dex at creation; Nakedpal put 10 points into sta and 10 into wis.

Nakedwar - 2637 hps
Nakedpal - 2202 hps 1300 mana

That's 435 hps for 1300 mana, or about 1/3. Obviously that's a lot bigger than the ratio on gear, and suggests that warriors are losing ground, hp-wise, relative to the growth of hybrid mana on every piece of gear looted.

Itemization, or the fact that paladins are getting a ton of extra mana relative to additional warrior hps, is certainly a big part of the class balance problem.
