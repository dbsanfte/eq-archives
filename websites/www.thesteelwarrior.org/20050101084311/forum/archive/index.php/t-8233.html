If you want to take and hold aggro, you need to pay attention. I know, it's not the most popular advice here but warriors do have the tools to hold excellent aggro if they choose to employ them. This is mostly germane to trials and exp groups of course, in raids the aggro-lock becomes far more a function of equipment and strategy.

Yes indeed, some of this is things like hate-proccing weapons, intelligently using taunt and bellow and such as well as simply having the stats and AAs to do the innate things even better. A lot of it however is simply being attentive and proactive. Tag on incoming, not after a pull is running around camp. Save taunt for when it will help, don't just chain it meaninglessly. Use your discs at appropriate times and know what to use when and precisely what effect they will have. Hold position when aggro is fragile, proximity is not only useful for rooted mobs. Have various weapons available and know what to use when and realise that short of the very high-end, there are often different aggro setups for different situations. Also pay attention to when you are losing aggro and perhaps why. If it is healer aggro because of your AC/HP that can be addressed differently than slower-aggro where perhaps it is over-aggressiveness on the slower's part.

Most of all though, communicate with your groupmates about aggro if there are issues. It is (generally) in the group's best interest to have you maintain aggro from start to finish, so let them know how best this can be accomplished. Don't freak out though if someone occasionally takes aggro temporarily. A good group should be floating on that edge anyways, not gimping itself to ensure 100% aggro-lock at all times.

Hmmm.

Disarm will put you on the hate list if you are not already there. But whatever hate it generates is completely trivial. It's not an aggro tool, as far as I know. The only time I use it is when I'm farming and want to amuse myself. Or when I'm in a pick-up group with someone who is ninja looting. Then at least I get the weapon if the ninja looter doesn't notice the disarm, heh.

And, uh, yes, concerning aggro. Bash, slam, kick, melee, incite line and procs are all additive. That is, they add to whatever hate you have built up, regardless of what anyone else has done. In theory, if you were far enough behind in aggro, you could bash, do rounds of melee, incite and all of your weapons could proc and you still wouldn't have aggro if someone else had generated more aggro that that already.

If, on the other hand, you successfully taunt, you will have aggro--you'll jump to the top of the aggro list plus some small amount. If you successfully taunt and then do all those other things (bash, melee, incite, proc) immediately before anyone else does anything, you have aggro from the taunt *plus* the hate all the other things generate.

Thus, if you are in mid fight and taunt and the incite line have both refreshed, and you lose aggro, you are better off hitting taunt first, then immediately hitting the incite line.

However, if a bard is pulling a mob into camp, you may find that you can draw aggro by shooting the mob with an arrow. If the mob comes to you, save taunt, but go ahead and use incite to get an early jump in hate on everyone else.

There may be an argument for holding back on incite in that situation to see if the slower is going to yank the mob off of you onto himself, but someone else will have to make that argument.
