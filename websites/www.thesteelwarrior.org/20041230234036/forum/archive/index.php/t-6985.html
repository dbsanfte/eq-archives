Barney explanation:

In general, defensive is good. Tanks usually die to damage spikes, and defensive makes the damage spikes more survivable. Also, there's no aggro penalty while defensive.

If the mob's minimum hit is very high (i.e. a significant portion of its max hit), then defensive won't be very effective, so you probably want evasive instead. You see many such mobs in Luclin.

If the mob gets a bonus effect for hitting you (i.e. nasty procs or flurries), then defensive won't reduce those effects, so you probably want evasive instead. You see a few such mobs in POP.

Basically, up until this stuff was figured out, everyone assumed that defensive was the way to go on everything. It was such a huge deal to be 55 and get defensive, and no one really gave 2 craps about evasive.

Once we figured out how damage bonus and damage interval worked, and what each of the disciplines did, we found out that Evasive was definitely equal in value to defensive, and it could be argued that it was superior.

When I did need to use a disc, it was usually evasive, because of the recycle time on it being a little faster than Defensive.
