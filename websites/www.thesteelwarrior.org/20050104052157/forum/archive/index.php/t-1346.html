Haste that you recieve from a worn item will not stack with another worn item. I.e. Cloak of Flames 36% and Spiked Seahorse Hide Belt 34%. You would get 36 % haste from the COF and nothing from the belt. Enchanter haste and bard haste will stack along with a right clicky haste or a proc haste. IE if an enchanter casts haste on ya and a bard is playing the haste song it will stack with your worn haste.

"Enchanter haste and bard haste will stack along with a right clicky haste or a proc haste. IE if an enchanter casts haste on ya and a bard is playing the haste song it will stack with your worn haste."

To clarify ...

One Item haste will stack with ONE spell OR song haste effect.

There are two bard songs which skirt this issue making it confusing. But they are very high level and are different from your normal bard songs.

So, if you have Cloak of Flames (36 percent haste) and an enchanter casts Anya's Quickening on you, the cloak and the spell will stack. If your group's bard starts to sing Jonathon's Whistling Warsong, the ATK portion of the song will stack, so you'll see both icons up. You WILL NOT get added haste from the song however.

1 item. 1 spell or song.

To make matters even more confusing, before level 60, you have an 85 percent haste cap.
