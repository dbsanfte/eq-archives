I have always sought after a way to compare weapon set ups without having to parse log files with a third party program, and I think I have finally done it. I spent about an hour or two working on this, so any feedback would be greatly appreciated.

Below is a formula to determine the efficiency of a 1H dual wield set up, the only value here I am not 100% sure on is the dual wield percentage, going off of feedback from other warriors I have approximated the value at 62% at level 65.

DMG1 = Primary Weapon Damage
DB1 = Primary Weapon Damage Bonus
DLY1 = Primary Weapon Delay

DMG2 = Secondary Weapon Damage
DLY2 = Secondary Weapon Delay

0.62 = Dual Wield Percentage at level 65.


(((DMG1 x 2) + DB1) / DLY1) + ((DMG2 x 2) / DLY2) * 0.62) = Weapon Efficiency

Now given this formula, I will use Blade of Carnage in Primary and Scepter of Destruction in Secondary as an example.

(((15 x 2) +13) / 20) + ((12 x 2) / 18) * 0.62) = 2.963

The result is a weapon efficiency of 2.963, sounds like a nonsense number, but it's in fact used in comparison to another dual wield rating. I will use Great Blade of Storms and Cord Hilted Spike Driver as a comparison. These two weapons have a combined efficiency of 3.113. Now you simple divide one number into the other, 3.113 / 2.963 = 1.05, this means that the GBoS and CHSD does ~5% more damage than the first set up. Everyone still with me?


Now here's the useful part, comparing 1H dual wield set ups to 2H weapons.

The formula to determine weapon efficiency for a 2H weapon is much simpler.

((DMG x 2) + DB) / DLY

For a 2H weapon I will use Blade of War, which results in a weapon efficiency of 4.156 in comparison to the efficiency of the first dual wield set up efficiency of 2.963. Simply repeat the step above and divide 4.156 by 2.963 and you end up with 1.40, or 40% more damage with the Blade of War. I have tried this method on all the weapons in my arsenal and the results have been dead on so far, most people I have computed efficiency for I have gotten right on the dot as well.

Now I know this post drags on, but I think this is a definitive way to compare weapon set ups. The only variables I can think of that are not factored in are AAs such as Flurry, Punishing Blow, etc. But until we get hard numbers for those abilities, they will only throw off the formula.

Please note that dmg*2+db is NOT the average (or mean) hit. It is the MODAL hit. There is a difference. The modal is the number that you hit for most often, it is not the average. If you hit for 10, 50, 50, 25, 100, 50, 120, 50, then the mean (average) hit would be 56.875, but the modal hit would be 50.

The reason that we use the modal hit in weapons comparison is that it is easy to calculate and fairly constant for a given weapon. Average hit will vary quite a bit depending on buffs and mob AC, but modal hit is pretty constant (at least pre high end, not sure how it works at 65 + huge ATK increases, or against mobs with obscene AC where your min hit might actualy become your modal hit). Since damage in EQ tends to be heavily weighted towards the modal hit, it ends up being a decent approximation for comparing one weapon to another, but it is NOT a predictor of DPS.
