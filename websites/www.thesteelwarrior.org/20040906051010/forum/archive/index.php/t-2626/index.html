I can see three epics that stand out:- The Rogue epic (15/25, 41% haste and a fat ATK boost are great for the effort involved), the Monk epic (100hp hand slot with 40% song haste is pretty good for the effort), and the Cleric epic (which pretty much all clerics past 50 still have to do, even if they're bots). Other epics, like the Bard and Ranger ones, deserve a mention as items that are still useful.

The problems with the epics are the great variability in the difficulty of the quests - Shadowknights and Mages have it very rough here - and the fact that a few of them still have effects that are hard to replace, so some classes really have to do them while others do not.

The role of the epic quest has changed, and most players would agree that epics should not be the pinnacle of raid loot. From the point of view of a game which is going into its sixth expansion, your epic should represent a worthwhile item for the "casual" player to strive for, but not required.

Personally, I'd use the Cleric/Rogue epics as a benchmark for the rough difficulty of the quest. Both epics can be completed from start to finish with a single group. In terms of the Warrior epic, that would mean that the Fear and Sky drops should be replaced by solo camps (which might be for a reasonably rare spawn), and Sev should be triggerable in the same way that Ragefire and Faydedar can be triggered. You can do the Queen in Chardok with a single group so that encounter should stay, but memory says that the Queen's drop is not guaranteed, although it should be.

In terms of the stats and the effect, I'd use the Rogue epic as the benchmark for non-tanking melees. 15/25 with Seething Fury, useable in the offhand once you get a better main hand. The knight epics ought to be marginally but not significantly better than a Windblade (so say 50/43 with 75hp and 25mana), and the warrior 2hs epic should be similar but with 100hp.
