One of the core problems in a mmorpg like EQ is the constant influx of new stuff into the economy. Every X days a new Blade of Carnage enters the economy, and it stays there permanently- unless there is a system to suck items out.

One analogy designers use is of a bathtub. You have a faucet which is the flow of new items in and a drain to suck items out. Unless the flow in is as fast as the flow out, your water level in the tub will rise. Items become too common, and some become valueless.

The lack of any drains for equipment in the EQ economy leads to ridiculous situations like the Greatstaff of Thunder going for 100pp or the War Marshall's Bladed Staff going for 800pp. These are items that 3/4 expansions ago would have been considered godlike. But because there is a constant flow in of these items, and only a small and constant base of characters to use them, their value is zero.

People groaning and passing when a GSoT drops is a design failure on Sony's part. The same camp drops an ornate bracer mold worth 50k or a weapon worth 100pp.

Sony had a great opportunity with the new tribute system to fix this. A tribute system would provide a drain for too common drops and a minimum price for items- "Should I get 2kp in bazaar for the weapon or 3kp tribute points?"

The initial implementation incorrectly priced weapons, such as 27k tribute for GSoT, but it had the right spirit- people were turning in items for points. The recent patch has destroyed that aspect of the tribute system. No one will turn in items at their current tribute prices:

-GSoT 800 tribute
-weighty polearm 50 tribute
-mace of the fallen crusader 50 tribute

for some of the items that I think are seriously undervalued. With too low tribute values for items, tribute becomes a plat system, not an item drain.

The design idea should have been:

-casino to drain plat
-tribute to drain items

But too low tribute values will lead to a worthless and unused tribute system.

I have to disagree that the tribute system is impossible to balance. It requires answering one question- "What is this system for?" For my mind it would have a simple answer- "To soak up the inflow of high end gear."

Once you make this decision, balancing is pretty easy:

1. Tradeskill gear should be worth very little. People can generate this stuff on demand, and the whole point here is to suck stuff up, not generate more.

2. Old content stuff should be worth very little. Who cares if there are 50 Maces of the Shadowed Soul floating about, people only farm City of Mist while powerleveling their alts. Make MoSS worth 100-200 tribute, and people will keep trashing them.

3. Equipment that your average level 55+ character would consider wearing should be worth the most tribute. PoP drops and level 65 LDoN drops should be worth real tribute. Enough that people will use the system, and that it will keep the prices of the droppable top-end gear high.

Example: Make the GSoT worth 3-5k tribute. This will keep prices of a damn nice piece of gear out of the trash level. And it will make people happy when they see one drop in BoT.

Example: Make those too-common knight PoP weapons worth 2-4k. There is no way a character should be able to pick up a 1h weapon with almost a 1-1 ratio for 50pp in bazaar.

Aye, it does all depend on the purpose of the system, and from where I stand it seems like Sony is having a conflict over what this tribute thing is supposed to do.

I mean, I can't imagine that the sole purpose of it was to give players a boost in power... they know that mudflation isn't helped by such things. Thus, it should be about getting items out of the bazaar and removing them from the world altogether... seems pretty clear to me that this should be the ultimate goal.

What I find funny about it, though, is that the high-end people need tribute far less than the rest of us. For instance, the Vengeance and Flowing Thought tributes don't give anything once you have those things maxxed with items. Likewise, Haste and Focus effects don't stack with existing worn items at all... it just takes the best. So, a warrior with maxxed Vengeance and Haste will only feel compelled to spend a fraction of what a lower-levelled warrior would spend.

Of course, the other side of the coin is that these "uber" players don't see nearly the same benefit from the new system as those of us who don't have these things maxxed.

So, what happens? Ubers, who are generating a lot of these high-end droppable items, don't need the points nearly as much as lower levelled folks. Thus, they stick them on their bazaar mule just like before, except now us lower folks have more of a reason to buy them... either to use or to hand in for points.

It's a clever system, but I don't think Sony has done a good enough job of determining its true purpose... it's more like an experiment.

EDIT: One thought did just occur to me... this *does* allow uber players to swap out, say, one or two vengeance items for something else that has more ac/hps, while maintaining the same Vengeance total. That's a good benefit, for sure.
