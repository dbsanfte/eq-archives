People like the windblade because they see the 50dmg and think that it must be kickass damage as a result. People pay many many times more for a windblade than they do for a war marshall's bladed staff... because they are ignorant/stupid/etc. Granted, the windblade might be better in a few situations, but as a warrior, it is too slow for aggro, and too expensive to be a situational dps weapon.

Now, as for aggro weapon upgrades.. unfortunately, the best route is the old, boring, disheartening CHoS/SotBS combo. These are the best buyable aggro weapons short of SoD/BoC, and are dirt cheap for the amount of aggro they generate. You can also take a group of friends to droga and camp them both for free, and make decent plat/exp while doing so. If you think you can get a raid together to go after some mobs, possible targets might be Xanamech, the dragon in PoI that drops a 13/19 with 450 hate proc, two of the BoT tower bosses (Auliffe and.. Einride?) Auliffe Chaoswind drops a 41/31 75dd proc 2hb which is a great dps weapon for the efforst, and Einreid or whatever his name is drops a 14/18 with stun/dd proc (Greatblade of Storms). All of these mobs are good targets that will give you better weapons. Other possibilities might be vallon zek, for his scimitar, or avatar of war, for a BoC.

Once you do get a good aggro setup that you are happy with, you will eventually want to augment one or both of the weapons with an aggro proc. Anger2, Anger3, the guk stun/dd procs, are all pretty good for aggro. Let me tell you though, you do NOT want to waste an aggro augment in a weapon that doesn't already have a good proc of its own. a frostbringer with anger2 will outaggro a 14/19 noproc, with anger2. Get a 1hs/1hb/1hp with a +aggro, stun/dd, or dd+ac debuff proc, and augment that.

What is the attraction that people still have for the Windblade?

Well, for me, it's a few things. First of all, it's now become a status symbol (WIndblades are too expensive, you should just buy this piece of shit instead). That's fine. Makes me look like I paid a lot of money for mine (which I didn't, but that's okay, everyone can think that). Second, if I'm actually using 2h, I'm using it for a purpose, and that purpose is to avoid collateral damage, specifically damage shields and ripostes. Swtiching from dual wield to a 28 delay weapon to me seems retarded, especially since my dual wield combo outdamages my 2h anyway. You may be able to argue that the War Marshall's staff is better DPS, but there's no way you can argue that the windblade prevents more. Finally, I just don't like that naginta. Windblade may not be the best graphic I could have asked for, but I'd much rather be carrying around a big ol' axe if I have to be carrying something. One of my first memories of EQ was seeing Kalorn Iceblade in freeport, looking badass with his full crafted and his Executioner's Axe. I'll never forget it, and it kinda shaped how I want my characters to look in EQ.

Is it overrated? Probably. But you have to understand that the main reason that a lot of people who have them are still using them is that when PoP came out, it was THE weapon to have. There simply wasn't anything else you could get cheaply that could compare to it, damage wise or aggro wise. People were finding that they could actually hold aggro when they were cranking out 500 damage a round from it. The people that had them back then probably still have them because there's just not a good alternative for it. Could I switch to WMBS? Sure..but what would be the point? I already have a windblade, the extra 0.0001% dps that some people claim from a WMBS isn't worth the hassle.

Oh, and by the way, tossing a red epic into your setup would be a bad idea. Your weapons are fine. I wouldn't be too concerned about them, altho I might switch hands. Bloodsworn proc should be no problem being more than 250 hate.
