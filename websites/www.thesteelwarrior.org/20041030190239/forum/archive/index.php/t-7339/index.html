Mourneblade
This was actually a question I had recently until I parsed my DPS with proc weapons vs. non-proc weapons.

DPS was slightly higher with the proc weapons so I answered my own question. Yes, procs make it "appear" that you are swinging less, but if you look at the actual damage output there's no change in swing rate. Hope that helps!
Derrict
Depending on the type of procs, your dex, any combat effects / weapon affinity, proc damage can be a huge boost.

I did some before/after parses of damage output of adding augmented DD procs. I added 150dd poison to primary and 125dd fire to offhand. 305 dex unbuffed, WA5, 0 combat effects:

73,969 second duration

500,434 damage from rujarkian venom
208,951 damage from flaming blade
----------
709,385 total augment DD damage, 9.59 dps

Add in natural procs:
542,100 damage from assassin's blood
388,570 damage from call of time
----------
930,670 total natural proc damage, 12.58 dps

Add in all 4 procs:
1,640,055 total damage, 22.17 dps or 10.17% of my total damage output (I'm a rogue).

Edit: forgot to add these numbers are overnight parses, unbuffed, so the proc dps % would decrease with attack and haste buffs. Witn normal buffs, proc damage of total dps drops to 7%.
