Well, considering the worthless pile of crap that the "working as intended" thread has turned into (thanks, Guyfencin), I figured that I would just start a new post, so it would actually get looked at.

Now, I agree, there should be NO way that taunt would be 100% sucessful on higher level mobs.

However, why not just make it exactly like tradeskills??

Here is a chart, with "trivial" levels, and the sucess rate at them, with varying skill levels: Tradeskill Trivs (http://www.crimsonsnow.com/pdfs/charts/zeralenns_trivchart.pdf)

Percentage of success = (skill - (trivial x 0.75) + 51.5

Now, replace "trivial" level, with a mob taunt-resist level, based on level of the mob, and a variable, so that a level 70 mob would have a "taunt skill" triv of, say 335. That would be around a sucess rate of ~30% with a taunt skill of 230.

Some things to note about tradeskills:

1) You can NOT fail on a triv 200 points lower than your skill. IE, you would have 100% sucess taunting a level 1 mob.

2) The higher you skill, the higher the chance you have of suceeding on a triv that is higher than you skill level. IE, a level 25 warrior would fail to taunt a level 30 mob a lot more than a level 65 would fail a level 70.

3) Once your skill is high enough, you have a 5% chance to fail on a triv at or below your level, up to the 200 points below rule, at which point it becomes 0%. This would make 95% sucesses on trash mobs.

Now, you could then vary the variable by hand, to make certain mobs more resistant or less resistant to taunt.

Example: Leve 70 mobs, one with a taunt mod of 4, another with a taunt mod of 5. Mod 4 = exp mob, mod 5 = raid mob. You would have a 71.5% chance of taunting the level 70 exp mob, and a 19% chance of taunting the level 70 raid mob. This would allow for raid mobs that are level 55, that are relatively taunt resistant, by giving them a mod of 6 or so, making them slightly easier to taunt than the level 70, if thats what the devs would want.

Anyway, I'm bored at work, and looking at tradeskill stuff, and thought that might be a change to push, as it would be a better, more tunable, yet familar system (as its used already).

Any thoughts?

This system accomplishes absolutely nothing. Currently, system only depends on taunt skill and level difference the mob, and it might look something like this, assumed taunt skill is maxed:

You Mob % to taunt
65 1 75
65 65 50
65 70 25

Now you propose adding a taunt resist level, which would end up like this:

You Mob 'To Taunt' % to taunt
65 1 X 75
65 65 Y 50
65 70 Z 25

Where X, Y, Z would be set to ensure the same level of taunt success as before.

Changing the system does not change the result. There is no indication whatsoever that the % to taunt a mob will ever be changed, because there's really no reason for it to be changed. Different systems still have to implement the same success rate.

And if by some miracle SoE decides that there is something wrong with the % you can taunt mobs, it can be implemented in the current system. For example you can say just treat any taunt on a mob light blue or lower as an AE Taunt level skill.
