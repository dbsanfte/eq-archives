3. Mudflation blew up bigtime. I hit 12k+ today with the new shaman buffs, that's an obscene amount to me. On a slightly related topic:

Why don't they revamp some of the older zones? I picked up Warcraft 3 the other week and read some of the history -- they really got into it. Whereas with GoD, and even, in part, PoP, the underlying story wasn't really there. With OoW it's seemingly gotten worse. "Let's put these cool looking mobs here on a different faction than those yahoos over there!" I understand I'm being fairly quick to judge, and perhaps the bigger picture will come out as it did with PoP and GoD, but I just dont get the sense that the game is "healthy." It seems it's reaching out and overextending. Don't get me wrong, I really like OoW, but the RPG part of the game has peeled off, leaving it mainly an MMO.

Would be nice if they went back and tweaked a few zones in a way that doesn't interfere with smaller, casual/family guilds moving up through an actual progression, but gives different challenges maybe with a bit of storyline. Like revamping part of Kael (adding a 'b' section) or Skyshrine, or adding onto Sebilis and Crushbone.
