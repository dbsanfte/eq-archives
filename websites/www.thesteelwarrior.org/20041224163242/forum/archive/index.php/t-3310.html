How about taking the discs, unhooking their timers so that they operate independently (save combinations like defensive and evasive), and instead of having the outdated command line interface for discs, take the unused spell gem bar for pure melee, and allow the placement of 8 disciplines in the spell gems. These gems would refresh when that discipline is available for use again. Also, disciplines could be coded to block each other discipline, so that no two could be active at once. A "cool-down" period could be coded, much like spell refresh gems are now, so that no discipline could be activated immediately after another had worn off.

This would not only allow more use of disciplines, it would make it easier to see when the disciplines are available, easier to activate a wider variety of them, as well as opening up macro slots.

If they re-coded the disc commands into spells with instant cast times, it would allow disciplines to be modified and balanced as easily as spells are today, and would also allow them to add new toys, in the form of disciplines, in each expansion, just as new toys in the form of spells are given to casters, each expansion.

I'm not really partial to this idea. I'm sorry, I just don't like it. Maybe I'm being silly just because it makes pure melee look like bards. I have this nagging feeling that ultimately it would degenerate to that. "Dude! Start twisting defensive and evasive!" I have real trouble imagining our ogre tanks dancing the mobs to death, although that would certainly be fatal. The other nagging complaint is that bards have, in the past, had severe problems whenever spellcasting is manipulated by the devs. They get treated like some special case stepchild, and their songs are always broken once or twice with every little tweak the devs make...for days. I really don't want to see you guys in that boat...which you would be, if you weren't actual casters using spell gems, with all that common code implied, but rather some special case like bards.

While re-using all that pre-extant UI real-estate is tempting, when i think about it, the one right thing is for melee to have its own part of the UI that casters find useless. The opposite of the spellbook. The opposite of the mana bar. The opposite of the specialization skill.

Because in addition to my nagging qualms above, I don't really think that a spellbook and spell gems really lends itself well to describing someone's mastery of combat. You should not be limited to 8 choices, just because there are 8 gems, and to change them you have to sit down and read a book? That is if you ever got more than 8 choices, which by level 65 you really should.

Can you imagine Jackie Chan asking the bad guys to wait 5 minutes while he reads a book to remember how to kick their asses?

So, this is the part where I should probably give an alternative.

I have a humble idea (http://pub148.ezboard.com/fpaladinsofnorrathfrm21.showMessage?topicID=583.topic) of how I would attack the question of how to make pure melee scalably extensible with expansions, and give them more than one trick. In effect, to give melee depth.

In the link above, I outline a few ideas on a base system that would allow for SOE to produce new and different skills for each class with each expansion if they choose to, similarly to spells. In which pure melee get an ability to excercise these skills, under a time limitation imposed by a statistic you can influence through equipment (unlike disciplines or /shield today), and have that ability augmented by items, and buffs...similarly to mana bars. And an over-arching skill that, when finally in an environment where a warrior can choose wether to be better at one thing more than another, he can do so. Similarly to specialization.

This, to my way of thinking, is the Right Thing (tm), even if SOE will never devote the development time to it.

Even if you aren't interested in the Right Thing (tm), but rather the Thing You Can Get From SOE Easily (tm), I hope I have at least given you a different direction from which different new ideas can be launched. Because I don't think spellgems are it.
