Is it possible that aggro is a composite of several different things, and that we're sniffing around one tree, when we've really got raccoons treed in two or more different trees?

For example, the initial tests seemed pretty convincing that hate was a fixed amount (within a small range) per swing, and that it was related to a weapon's damage/delay stats.

Then, it became evident that that theory is not entirely consistent across a wide spectrum of weapons (because the theoretical model doesn't mirror what we all know from practical experience). So there are two possibilities:

1) The original theory is completely wrong, and any semblance of validity is a startling coincidence.

or

2) The original theory is very close, thanks to a fortuitous use of weapons that are highly representative of the model, but there is some other amount of hate coming from somewhere that is too complex to reverse-engineer.

For instance... perhaps the hate IS based on the weapon as originally postulated, but there is an additional amount of hate being generated using a different formula which scales in some way that is undetectable with the small sample of weapons tested thus far? Maybe the amount of damage being done does generate hate, but depending on some variables (like weapon damage, damage bonus or delay) has a multiplier which practically negates the effects of added hate from damage done. Or maybe each weapon has some hate multiplier, and most weapons have a multiplier of 1.0 or very close to it.

I think the best way to proceed would be to continue to conduct these tests with a vast variety of weapons, and tabulate the pertinent information: damage, delay, damage bonus, number of hits to take 1 ToT's worth of aggro, total damage dealt in that time.

There's also one other factor to consider, and that's decay. Does hate decay over time? Perhaps the variations seen have something to do with how much time it's taking to steal aggro. For instance, with weapon A you take aggro back with 10 attacks, but weapon B seems to consistently take 40 swings to gain aggro. Assuming the times are different there, is it possible that if hate decays over time, you're really not stealing 1 ToT of aggro, you're stealing (1 - (Decay Rate * time)) ToT. For longer periods of time taken to gain aggro, the amount being overcome might be considerably less than the fixed 510 we're taking as granted.

So let's say hate decays a 10 hate/second (just for sake of argument). Furthermore, we'll assume that 10 attacks with weapon A takes 10 seconds, and 40 attacks with weapon B takes 25 seconds.

Weapon A:

Hate Overcome = 510 - (10 hate/sec * 10 sec) = 410 hate to overcome decaying hate

Weapon B:

Hate Overcome = 510 - (10 hate/sec * 25 sec) = 260 hate overcome.

This kind of variation could account for differences observed as well. I'm not aware of any studies done as yet that conclusively prove or disprove whether hate decays at all.
