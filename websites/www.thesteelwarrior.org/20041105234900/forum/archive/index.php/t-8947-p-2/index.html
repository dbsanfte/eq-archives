To formulate the problem in a way that isn't so offensive to raiders: Let's say that there's a raiding guild with good players in it. They're in a position to recruit another good tank. They're not interested in having idiots around, so they have a vetting process. Some of them know an ungilded warrior who is a good player. Perhaps he's been in a small guild before or a less advanced guild at any rate, but at this point he's free to move to a more advanced guild. He's interested in applying to this guild, because it would afford him access to the next level of the game--tougher mobs, cooler fights. This warrior applies to the guild, is tested, and is accepted.
And you know what happens next: the first month the good warrior is in the guild he sees some significant upgrades and he gets them significantly faster than he was getting lesser upgrades before joining the guild.

The issue for casuals isn't that the guy shouldn't get upgrades. It's not that he isn't a good player or that he isn't working hard or that the guild isn't working hard.

The issue for casuals is that this warrior didn't suddenly become more skilled by clicking the "accept guild invite" button. Over time, sure, he'll get more skilled because good players, whether they are casual or raiders, tend to get better when they face new mobs. But that first month what changed was not his effort, but who he was standing next to. The effort/reward ratio changed dramatically for this warrior. Not because he's working less or afk or any of that, but because his rewards are better-- perhaps dramatically so.

I really don't think thoughtful casuals begrudge this warrior his upgrades. Certain issues, particularly with PoP flagging, do cause casuals grief, though, and have almost since the release of PoP.
