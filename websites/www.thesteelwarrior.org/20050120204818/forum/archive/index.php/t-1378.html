You really don't want any guild that would take a 47 druid and a 34 warrior right now, for a couple of reasons. Don't take offense here, and realize that this is what usually happens in these situations. There are exceptions, but this is the norm.

First of all, any guild that can currently do what you want to do (planes+ stuff) doesn't need characters that are that low of level. If you do manage to get in a guild that does that stuff, you're going to find yourself being left behind a lot because you're too low to contribute and actually end up being a liability on that raid. End result? You're not going to have fun until you get into your 50's.

Second, if you join any guild appropriate for your level, there is a solid chance that guild is always just going to be a guild for chat and will never be able to do the things you want to do. This isn't a bad thing. Friendships are important in EQ, but if you truly have aspirations of doing bigger and better things, a family guild is just going to hold you back. You'll get attached to the people and stick around doing nothing for longer than you normally would, just because of "your friends." Case in point -- I came back to EQ a couple weeks ago, and found that my guild had split off into another guild essentially. Damned near the instant I got back online, I had 4 people in the other guild come back to mine because they know that I get things done and their guild wasn't doing shit except be a green chat channel and some more letters over their head.

Your best bet in your situation is to get into your mid 50's and then start looking around and seeing who's raiding what on the schedule you play on. Find a guild that does things when you want to do them and then try to form a relationship with them. If you find a guild now, chances are you're not going to be doing anything until your mid 50's anyway, so just keep plugging along and worry about it later.
