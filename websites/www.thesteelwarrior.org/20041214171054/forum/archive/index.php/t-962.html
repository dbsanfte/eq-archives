heres they ones i have -

Assist call - i have one for melees and casters but tend to use only melees and even then only infrequently now as our guild melees are meant to manage their own aggro.

Target - just a MA targeting %t - i find this useful as a heads up to people to know whats incoming or when 2 mobs are inc so the SA knows what to offtank.

SA OT - if im MA - i have a macro telling the SA which target to offtank. If im SA< then ill change this to say SA offtanking %t so people know what mob im on.

Back off - Basically a disengage and back the hell away from the mob and let me get aggro/position - for occassions when u have an add or MT goes down or pets are misbehaving and you need to reposition mob or get aggro.

Defensive - to let em know when im hitting defensive.

autoriposte - to let em know when im doing combo of AE taunt and autoriposte - for occassions when the sht hits the fan and i need to buy everyone time to cast evac.

MA switch - for MA switches ill have one telling the clerics that im just about to hit AE taunt/defensive in x no of seconds and to start spamming the heals on me.

proc - to let em know when wep procs. helps the shams /enchs to time debuffs and melees casters to know when they can unload a little bit more. tend to only use this in intial rounds of fight and if its a harder mob that shams are holding off slow for.

/shield - if i have a primary class in my group then ill put em on hotkey shield.

thats about all i can think of atm.

I don't use macros for anything except for calling an AE. They're annoying and don't help at all imo. Who cares if you just AE taunted? If things were that bad, clerics were probably already getting pounded and put DA up so they won't be able to heal you right away. Ok, you're defensive ... congratulations. There's already a message that anyone who matters is going to see, I don't need more spam in my window. If you have to micromanage the raid that much, you're already screwed. It's better to put in the effort to get their heads out of their asses so they can figure out what to do in the future without 4 bazillion macros flying across the screen. And if you're the MA and people can't use /assist anyway, they need to learn and you shouldn't be switching targets.
