lokkak
A couple of points:

1) Exp is based on the difference between your level (and the average
group level) and the level of the mob. If you are soloing and kill
a mob at most 5 levels below your level, you gain around 90 percent
(multiplied by 1.9) more exp. If you kill mobs at most 5 levels
above you, it is 190 percent (multiplied by 2.9) exp. This was
explained in a dev journal, but the numbers (1.9 to 2.9) come from
observation.

2) The BEST I could find (aa rate) at level 51 was killing most of
the zone with AE'ing weapons in City of Mist. DS, buff with a
cleric and druid, get lev, project earth elemental form from enc,
and argo as much of the zone as you can. Use vie, and heal over
time, with CH's. This is not "easy", but does produce an AA an
hour grouped with one level 30 character (who got more than a level
an hour until I stopped at level 43.) Expect to die once or twice
an hour (I did) and the deaths can be rather annoying.

3) I experimented with AA rates for a long time. Comparing AA rates
against level 65 characters to determine that if you are MORE than
5 levels below (or above) the average group level, you get no bonus
(the 1.9 to 2.9 multiplier above.) So, you need to be level 60 to
gain the bonus when grouped with all level 65s. Being level 60
will give MORE exp to the level 65's (refer to Sony's Dev journal
where group division of exp is explained.)

4) A level 60 in a 65 group gains 25 percent more exp than a level
65 in SolRo (all level 65 mobs.) Level 61 is 30 percent more in
SolRo. This is measured over time by comparing the time between
AA dings of the level 65's and the level 60/61s. The majority of
the time I grouped in SolRo, PoE/PoF/PoW. PoV killing the level
67 named for AG water was also nice EXP for level 61's.

5) Now, that being said, most people level 60 or 61 are not worthy
tanks. Primarily because they do not have 100 AA (as was said
before.) If you compare a level 60 with CA3/CS3/PE/ND3 to a level
65 with identical AA's, they will compare favorably in tanking
abilities. The level 65 will have approx 200 to 500 more HP
(depending on if the 65 has PP and PD AA's) and at low HP (below
8k buffed) 500 hp makes a big difference in "how often you die per
hour."

6) Also, if you ARE lower level than 65, you will experience severe
level prejudice. You will find it hard to find groups. Your best
bet it to START the group with a dual box Cleric, Enchanter, or
Shaman (someone very important.) I found that once people GROUPED
with me they were more likely to ask me to join their groups in the
future. But in the end, I would still recommend leveling to 65
unless you are severely behind in AA's from your friends (this was
my primary reason for remaining lower, I need to catch up on some
AA's.)

In short, unless you like hardship, go level 65, then AA.
