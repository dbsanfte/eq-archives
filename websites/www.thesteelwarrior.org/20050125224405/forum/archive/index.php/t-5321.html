Personally, I want the best of both worlds, or at least the largest balance I can find. High HP but low AC will get you pounded hard (lots of max hits) in PoP. Low HP but high AC is going to keep your cleric lom from inefficent CHeals.

So I try to look for items with mid-high AC and a nice chunck of pure hp. However, I usually either group with a shaman or have a bot shaman to buff me, so my STA is usually maxed. Therefore, +HP > +STA for me.

To me, it just seemed your AC was a little behind and could use some attention.

For example, on the legs, say you went with the shinguards. You give up 6STA, gain 30 pure HP and 14 pure AC. The total hp will be close to a wash but you'll pick up an adjusted 20AC approximately. Remember, pure AC is not 1-1 with your displayed AC.

For the BP, you lose 7 stamina, but pick up 20 pure HP and again, 14 pure AC. You'll lose a total of 20hp adjusted, and gain 20AC adjusted in return.

For the necklace, you'll pick up 5AC unadjusted, even out on HP, but pick up 3STA, so you're about 8AC and 15hp ahead (adjusted).

Just those 3 upgrades would be about a -5HP, +48AC return. I didn't list other stats (STR/AGI/DEX) and you'll probably drop a minimal amount on those. Another good part is you've switched STA hp's for pure HP's, giving you more headroom for STA buffs to help out.

But personally, I don't usually do all that math, or any AC=HP rule. I take a look at where I'm hurting, then try to find the biggest overall boost in important stats I can find, then fit it in my budget.
