I think there are a few things you guys are either not aware of or have not considered.

First of all, the amount of total up time a group has is no where near as high as you think it is. This was discussed countless times over at Graffe's with people claiming 90+% uptime groups that basically never happen. At 70% real uptime, the group has a "fast pull" feel to it, and usually require someone pulling before the current mob dies. The highest up time parse anyone was able to produce where on the order of 80-85% up time and those groups where on an insane pace to keep up with that pull rate. I think if you actually do some parses and calculate your actual uptime, you'll be quite surprised.

LDoN normal risk adventures are likely to produce the highest up times, and I would guess those will be in the 80-85% range with a high risk adventure doing closer to 60-70% uptime for a very good group. I would be surprised to see any group pull 95% uptime because the most important person in the group wouldn't be able to keep up - the healer. Clerics get less mana regen than any of the INT classes and generally have less mana than INT classes so they generally run out of mana faster.

Second, sitting is not nearly the problem that some people are making it out to be. On inc everyone is standing anyway, wizards for emergency CC. On multiple pulls I will often snare the pack on inc to keep the warrior from getting worked over by 3+ mobs. This will give the chanter time to get the mess under control without the healer having to blow a wad of mana keeping the tank and puller alive.

The maintenance for high uptime is pretty minimal. No matter how often you're fighting a mob, the total amount of time you're spent fighting them is still the same. So mobs do not do more DPS just because you're fighting them more often. Unless you're doing stuff like pull 4 mobs at once and then call this '100% uptime', there's really not much sacrificed. For example even though your monk is not doing damage 30% of the time, it's really a minimal DPS loss. If he stayed and did damage for the other 30% of the time, he'd still have to spend the same amount of time after the mob is dead to fetch the next one. So, you're expected to miss his DPS for the duration of the pull anyway regardless of when he goes out to make the pull. Assuming the monk doesn't go so fast that you end up having to mez/offtank an extra before the first one dies, the pulling time cannot be considered a maintenance cost for uptime because the monk needed that much time to pull anyway.

Now, one way you can avoid this is if your group always run from mob to mob. Then you can say that the monk pulling is a cost, because he could've just run together with your group (running time cannot be avoided anyway so might as well use it for pulling). But this only works on Normal IMO. Hard is a bit too risky for the group to just charge into a room.
