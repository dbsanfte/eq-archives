Since there is no clear-cut definitive answer, the good news is you can choose whichever you want.

Until I dinged 65 I used my GSoT exclusively in xp situations where I was tanking, simply because I was xp'ing in zones where riposte damage was taking a sizeable chunck out of me (BoT Krigers really hurt). I also used my GSoT in a Vindi kill where I picked up tanking duties when our MA died, a fight where I want to take as few ripostes as I can. Agro was not amazing, but it was enough to get me to 65. I totally agree that the further into the fight, the better my agro lead got with the GSoT. My only concern was agro pre-slow, and the GSoT has always performed iffy for me in that situation.

Last night I was in HoH basement, where the mobs hit more a more reasonable 500ish. About an hour in, I switched to my CHoS/SoBS to experiment a bit. My pre-slow agro improved significantly, and I believe I only lost agro once in an hour on a slow (where with the GSoT I lost agro approx. 2 out of 3 times on slow, and only held agro through slow if I got a proc off beforehand). The riposte damage I was taking did not feel terribly bad, and I in fact was avoiding a nice chunk of the ripostes. There was no noticeable difference in terms of the heals I received each fight (65 cleric healing).

The point? I think the GSoT would probably benefit most from being your first anger augment. I feel that it needs a little "umf" that an Anger 3 augment would certainly bring. It would help with initial pre-slow agro by doubling your chance of proc'ing pre-slow, and would nearly assure rock-solid agro deep in the fight. An augmented GSoT with anger 3 would be my raid MT weapon of choice as well.

I think I am planning on going Anger 3 with my GSoT, and Ykesha for my dual wield setup. It seems the cheapest way for me to be covered for both xp groups and LDoN (where I feel dual wield is better) and raid and BoT groups (where I feel GSoT is better suited).

Over the weekend I picked up a Sword of the Bloodsworn augmented with Anger II in the bazaar for 20k (gotta love FV). Anyway, gave it a spin, SoBS main/CHoS (with a wimpy 50DD proc aug) offhand, in HoH Basement last night. Grouped with 65 necro, 57 shammy, 61 pally, 57 bst, 65 Cleric. Haste was 31% + Celerty.

The DW combo was enough to hold agro from the shammy trying to slow the majority of the time (maybe lost it 1-5 or 6 times). He rarely would get slow to land on the first shot due to level, so there was usually at least 1 resist before slow landed. In that regard, I was very pleased.

However, the combo would lose agro to the DoT'ing necro further down in the fight, nearly every fight. The dual wield didn't seem to generate the hate to keep up with the massive DoT damage the necro was doing, likely due to lagging behind in DPS. I would see the mob take off after the necro, even after several stun and hate procs. I even lost agro the the BST a couple of times, which frankly surprised me.

In terms of Riposte, at 7k buffed the riposte damage of HoH mobs is fairly insignificant. I'm sure there were a few more per fight, but I was avoiding and mitigating well anyway, so it was not a concern.

Switched to an unaugmented GSoT to compare. Again, the slower got very little agro (except for one time where they slowed earlier than normal). The necro still was able to grab agro late in the fight, but it seemed to happen less often - there were more fights where I went straight from pull to kill without any agro loss. Didn't lose agro to bst at all. However, on the fight where the shaman got slow agro very early, I struggled to gain agro back quickly, and he ended up needing a heal before I could get the mob back on me. That did not occur with the dual wield setup, as I usually either got a successful taunt or one of my 3 hate procs to go off before the shaman needed a heal.

Overall, it was interesting for me to see how well the GSoT performed unaugmented vs. the Droga weapons, one with Anger II and one with a 50DD augment. As much as I hate to admit it (since wielding a GSoT gets me no hotties), I'm pretty convinved I'll be going GSoT+Anger III. A Droga dual wield setup would likely need Anger III in each, or possibly a combo of Anger III and Ykesha augs, to compete with a GSoT+Anger III in my opinion.

As for a GBoS+CHoS combo, I'd expect a GBoS to be somewhat better than a SoBS due to better damage, which might tip the scales when comparing vs. a GSoT.
