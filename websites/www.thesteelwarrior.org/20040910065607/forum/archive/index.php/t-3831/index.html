The reason it's being nerfed is best explained by crowd psychology. All opinions vary over time, and there's even a particular shape this trend takes, time and time again.

When PoP was being created, bards were at their low point, in terms of perceived power. Thus bards were helped in PoP.

At the same time, monks were at their high point, and were nerfed during PoP.

Now the two trends are reversed.

Bards are getting nerfed because right now, bards are perceived as being overpowered. That's the only reason. It isn't based on logic. It's emotional. Logic only kicks in after the emotion decides what direction to take. The choice of the 32 manasong over something else was made by the logical part of the brain. But the decision to nerf something was entirely emotional.

Don't dismiss this trend just becase it's based on emotion. Emotion is much more powerful than logic.

A class is overpowered, by definition, if it is displacing other classes out of their assigned roles. Thus bards are not overpowered. The only class bards ever displace is monks, and only in the role of pulling...and monks aren't supposed to be pulling in the first place. How many fantasy works include a martial artist that...pretends to die over and over and over again near a group of bad guys until all of them get fed up and go home but one? The argument that SOE ever intended FD to be used for pulling is ludicrous. This is ignored by monks because, again, emotion rules logic, not the other way around.

The designers emotionally "know" that bards are overpowered and that they need to be nerfed. Their rational brains kick in to help decide what to nerf. Nerfing 32 won't cause a huge outcry that nerfing something bards like to do would, and helps satisfy the urge to nerf SOMETHING about bards, because there's no arguing against an emotion that is telling you that something needs to be done.

This is showing a greater capacity to use logic inside SOE than in the majority of businesses that exist. This is a GOOD thing, that it's 32 manasong that's the focus of the nerf. It's not a fun role, it doesn't trash the bard class, but it lowers bard power while leaving the parts of the class that people actually enjoy doing, alone. Bards lose power but don't quit, which is good, because there aren't many bards. Compare this with the monk nerf where they wanted some monks to quit and play something else, because there were far too many.

If it were possible to override these basic emotions, we would no longer exist as a race. It's emotions that drive the human race to improve and grow. Without them, we'd all come to the logical conclusion that there is no purpose in continuing to live, since there is no logical reason why life should exist over its lack of existance. It's emotions that drive us.

While the basic emotional cycles can't be changed, the strength of each phase can. What we, as bards, do and say on the boards, at fan faires, and in game, can make the negative impulse spike deep or shallow. It can change what direction the emotional impulse will take (32 manasong vs. resists vs. fade vs. whatever).

It's monks that are the primary driver of anti-bard sentiment right now. It's monks that should be our focus, in terms of what happens to our class and what happens to theirs. Right now, monks are already fixed, at least by the definition they use for fixed (being able to pull better than bards), thanks to environmental changes on LDoN raids (indoors, high MR), and even hard adventures (indoors, massive lull resists). Unfortunately this isn't well known, because a lot of guilds don't do LDoN raids and few bother with hard adventures, and it certainly hasn't penetrated to the emotional, limbic portion of the brain. Thus the monk emotional calls for nerfage continue. This is the sort of pattern that leads to the large spikes then corrections afterwards, instead of a nice smooth increase to the proper value, or decrease to the proper value, with no overshoot.

The logical brain may make the perfect change, balancing everything out, but the emotional brain doesn't yet believe it. The logical brain is forced into making more changes until such time as the emotional brain finally catches up. At which point, the logical changes went too far. One can use the argument that the majority of monks don't know about LDoN raids/hard adventures, but that certainly doesn't apply to the designers, and it's they who are nerfing 32 manasong. The emotional wave is still in force. They will keep making changes until the herd is satisfied.

The next expansion will be a PoP-like expansion, with new AA's and levels (at least, according to rumor). This is the expansion that will probably overpower monks, and nerf the hell out of bards...unless the emotional trends reverse.
