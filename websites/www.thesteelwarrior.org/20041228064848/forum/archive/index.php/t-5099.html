But it's possible to have other goals than "the most powerful character for the least time played". And one such goal is the one Shaikyerbooty holds: to be the most effective character in an XP or LDoN group at each level.

And for that goal, AA >> levels. A level 55 with max archetype AAs is going to be wonderful to group with in level 55 experience groups, and utterly annihilate level-appropriate LDoN missions. Sure, that person won't be as powerful a character as the person who (in the same /played time) dinged 65 and then got just ND3. But for the mobs and missions and levels they're at, they're far more effective.

Or, to put it another way: the only way in which the "levels >> AA" rule applies is to minimize time played per character power. Now, that happens to be my goal in the game; I have limited playing time, and I enjoy being as powerful as possible. But it's not everyone's goal; in specific, it's not Shaikyerbooty's goal.

By the time Shaik gets to 65, he'll already have the 100 or 200 AA points I only wish I had. He'll have his amazing LDoN armor. He'll walk into Hard missions that I don't even dare attempt, and "crush them like grape."

Mind you, it'll take him another year or two to get there. And by the time he gets there, I'll have (in the same playing time) ground out 200 or 300 AA points. But he will have accomplished *his* goal, which is to always be far more powerful than the things he is fighting. Just like I will have accomplished *my* goal, which is to be as powerful as possible, as quickly as possible.

I think you're both right. You just have different objectives, and thus of course your ideal methodologies differ.
