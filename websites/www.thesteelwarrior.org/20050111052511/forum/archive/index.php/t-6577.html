So the other night, we do some named encounter in Yxxta for the first time. Named itself isn't that hard, but we fail to clear out everything else in the building before engaging, and wipe to adds. No biggie, we wipe and recover all the time, its part of the game. Except this is GoD, and our surviving rogues and necros are shortly mulched by wandering mobs that see SoS. So now its time to spend 40 minutes having everyone run back to KT, doing a CoH of the entire raid to the Yxxta zoneline, and then doing a CS of the raid inside Yxxta. Fun, fun. Oh the fun.

And this is not an isolated incident. I think since we have been raiding GoD, we have probably had to do half a dozen CS's to get a raid going again. The expense isn't a problem, we've got enough plat from selling spells during PoP that we can keep this up till the sun grows cold. But it got me to thinking - when was this anything like normal? I remember occasional coffin jobs back during Velious if we wiped deep inside HoT, or if we wiped to Trak. But CS as a matter of course? We used to CS only if we were stupid enough not to get a cleric camped or a necro FD in time. And even if we did wipe totally, there was almost always a chance to get the corpses dragged out and rezzed up in reasonably short order.

I suppose PoP might have been this way without the graveyards except that SoS worked 99% of the time in PoP. This isn't a return to an older, harder, more challenging EQ. This level of tedious difficulty is something new, with no reason behind it other than to slow down progression by making failures a time consuming cluster fuck.
