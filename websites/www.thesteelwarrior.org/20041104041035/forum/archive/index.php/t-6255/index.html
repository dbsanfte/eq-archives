Sangiovese
I've always wondered... when a caster casts his own Invis spell on themselves, they have fixed duration, but when they cast it on others, the duration is random.

They are different because they are different spells.

If a caster uses the same spell to invis himself that he uses to invis you, then the duration on him will also be random. But casters also get a line of self only invis, which has a fixed duration.

As an example... my mage learned the spell invisibility at level 8. And the duration on that spell is random (max of 20 minutes) no matter if I cast it on myself or on someone else. but at level 54 I got the spell "veil of elements" which is a self-only invisibility. And it has a guaranteed duration of 10 minutes.
