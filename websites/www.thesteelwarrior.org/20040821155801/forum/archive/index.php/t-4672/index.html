Battleblade
More players group in LDoN than in any other environment. LDoN Easy is selected by the majority of these groups. LDoN is the only source of certain spells and all augments.

LDoN is a timed event. Players are motivated to complete an adventure as quickly as possible. Even if they complete too quickly to start a new adventure immediately, the down time can be used for other purposes.

Excess survivability is not required in LDoN. Any tank capable of absorbing the rather low damange produced by LDoN mobs without being a burden to heal can MT.

Warrior aggro control was perceived as a deficiency in LDoN. Let's assume that the new Provoke Ability Line makes Warrior aggro control acceptable. Let's also assume that no benefit is gained by greater then acceptable aggro control.

If Warriors are acceptable MT's in LDoN based on adequate survivability and aggro control, what motivates groups to select them when other classes also meet these criteria?

DPS, Disciplines, and power-ups.

Lets rate Warriors based on additional attributes that assist a group in completing an LDoN adventure efficiently.

Warrior - 100 DPS, marginal effects from disciplines and power-ups

Paladin - 85 DPS, buffs, heals, Root, ranged aggro on demand

Shadow Knight - 105 DPS, snare, FD, ranged aggro on demand

Ranger - 105 DPS, snare, Root, light heals, ranged aggro on demand

Beastlord - 110 DPS slow, snare, Root, ranged aggro on demand

Looking at this (and feel free to correct it), my perception is that when selecting a MT to do LDoN Easy, Warrior desireability will be very dependent on the impact of their power-ups.

Superchum
"Warrior - 100 DPS, marginal effects from disciplines and power-ups"

I think you really have to wait and see the changes live before you characterize warriors like you do above.

You already classify effects from disciplines as marginal. And yet you don't know how the new system will affect the use of disciplines. What may have once been marginal, may now be quite useful.

The biggest limiting factor on discs was that melee classes saved theirs up for some phantom time when they would absolutely HAVE to have the active.

Monks would save stonestance for when pulls went bad. Warriors would save defensive for bosses, nameds, or some other oddity.

And thus, the end result, neither class would use their discs regularly.

The change to discs, is intended to get us to actually break that habit and use our discs. Use them even in casual XP group situations.

So, give em a chance before you write them off.

In regards to the power-ups ... well two of them already demonstrate a LOT of potential for the warrior class.

Increased mitigation will help in XP groups a LOT. Even just 5%. It's noticable. And it's something a warrior can point to and say "Well, I take LESS damage than Knight_01."

That helps with both the perception issue and with the actual data of the situation.

Then there's Provoke. Which I assume you classify in the power-up category ... BUT, if on your list you're going to have these other classes with "ranged aggro on demand" you need to really give credit where credit is due.

Provoke is, also, ranged aggro on demand.

So I would just hold off on classifying and quantifying warrior desirability in LDoN at least for a few weeks.
