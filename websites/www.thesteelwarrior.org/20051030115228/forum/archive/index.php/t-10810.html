"So .. perhaps's STR = Procrate as well as teh other things that have been Identified with it?"

No, spirit use to determine proc rate, but they took that out. Proc rate will no longer be effected by any stat. Strength adds a small bit of block, (pathetically small, and no amount of strength can be considered "protective"). The 100 str buff from crusader proc will raise your block by a whopping 5. Nothing to write home about. Strength is for attack power.

Now i'll disagree with most posters because I min-max defensively.

Stamina is nearly useless in non raid situations. Even going for non stamina gear I can get to near 7k full buffs and with an imp pet. The top heal doesn't come close to that unless it's a critical heal. The only time stamina is better than Armor, Defense or agility is if you're fighting straight casters.

Out of Stamina, Defense, Armor, Agility, and +Dodge/Parry Percentages, stamina is the only one that doesn't increase the value of a heal.

Since mitigation via armor scales, armor gets less and less useful the more you have. So as you go along just check for what offers the best combination of mitigation/avoidance.

Things to go by:

10 Defense = 0.8% Avoidance + Variable (the variable is the 0.4% block it adds, which of course mitigates a varying percentage of the incoming damage)
10 Agility = 0.5% Dodge
Armor scales (always check to see how much an armor gain will effect your mitigation by mousing over)
1% Dodge is always better than 1% Parry (you can not parry a creature behind you)
1% Parry is always better than 1% Block

Quoted for truth. You need enough hp for most PvE situations to where your healer dosen't have to panic. For PvP, you need as much as you can get without crippling your damage or crit rate.

Really, there are about 4 different types of "regular" plate late game.

Heavy armor + defense
Stat plate
All around + crit
All around + defense

The all around + defense stuff (Golem skull helm, stonegrip gauntlets for instance) is probably the overall best stuff to go for. You don't get too much of an overall damage reduction from the heavy armor plate, but you do lose out badly on stats.
