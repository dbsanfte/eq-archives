This thread answers your question.

http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=5883

The meat of the information was posted by Wandor when he wrote...

More attacks due to a change in relative weapon delay does not increase your proc rate. More attacks with no change in relative weapon delay does increase your proc rate.

Proc rate is based on the delay of a weapon. If a weapon has 20 delay, the combat engine expects that weapon to swing 30 times in 1 minute. Of those 30 swings, there will be 2 procs or a 6.6% chance to proc on any given swing.

Now if we add more attacks without changing the delay of the weapon - ambidexterity, PB, flurry, rampage, ferocity - you still have the same weapon delay so you still have a 6.6% chance to proc on any given swing. The difference is now instead of 30 swings in a minute, you have 40 swings per minute. This could change your proc rate to 2.6 PPM. This phenomenon can be seen by using an Earthshaker and rampage, procs out the wazoo. Of course the numbers are purely made up, but the theory is accurate.
