Brabble

Greetings Kalanu.

First, I think Fitemore is probably on to something concerning the ability to interrupt higher-level casting mobs. Assuming that if players will eventually be able to level to 70, then mobs will get to be higher level than they are now, mob interrupt could be even more important. Obviously whatever tools are given warriors to interrupt higher-level mobs have to be carefully tested so that two conditions are met: 1) the tools actually do something; 2) the tools are not overpowered.

I agree with Vikken's point about non-diety cultural armor.

I agree with Mokor that quest armor ought to be looked at.

In the case of Vikken's and Mokor's statements, I think both point to very large and very broken elephants in the corner. Neither tradeskill armor nor questable armor has kept up with mudflation. Examine the advice given to young warriors on this board when they ask "What useful armor can I quest to improve myself?" or "Where can I go to kill for an upgrade?" Over and over the answer is: no quests are worth your time, and nothing you can kill will significantly improve you faster than going to the bazaar will. In fact, most of the useless quests in the game were originally designed to require that a warrior bring a group (at least one) with him. That was hard enough to do when the quests were new and the armor was useful. Now you can't really even suggest it with a straight face.

The way new players get their stuff nowadays is to farm trivial mobs and sell stuff in the bazaar, or wait for an older player in their guild to give them something. Imagine the difference in the quality of game play between two games: one in which you can go out and kill for, quest for, or tradeskill your own armor that is useful at the level you can obtain/make it -- and another in which your class is markedly poor at soloing and the way you get better gear is to kill trivial things for hundreds of hours and then log a bazaar mule on and go play some other game. We don't know what the first game would look like, but the second game is called Everquest.

Older players of couse just twink their alts and spend a week or two leveling the twinked alt up. No point in them doing any quests either.

LDoN's armor structural progression actually looks good on paper, until your level 25-52 character actually starts doing the adventures and you realize that the reward rate is so far below the adventure point cost of the armor sets that you simply cannot outfit your character that way. This is not a matter of how much effort you are willing to put in, it's a matter of mathematics: a level 28 character who does 10 adventures and wins all of them might get two levels, but he'll only have 20-25 adventure points. (Not sure my figures are 100% accurate here, but they are close enough). He certainly will not be upgrading his armor at a level-appropriate rate this way: in fact, the harder he tries the faster he will level beyond his ability to upgrade his armor!

Nor is tradeskilling going to help much. On and off for about a year and a half I've been doing an experiment with a Barb warrior: I wanted to see if he could, without twinkage, loot and make his own armor at a level-useful rate. The answer is not really, no. In theory, the answer of course is yes, but on a practical level, the answer is a decided hell no. The BD cultural armor is, even now, darn nice. But there's very little between fine plate and tundra/fierce.

So, okay, even granted that essentially all of the sub-ornate armor quests are so completely impractical that almost no one does any of them, and that with mudflation any mob a character is likely to kill won't drop useful armor outside of raids (where the drop rate will be dismally low), and even granted that tradeskills have become something that bored level 65 characters do to help SOE remove excess duped pp from the game, it is reasonable to ask: so what?

The so what is this: the game doesn't have to be all about farming and buying stuff in the bazaar. If SOE is telling itself that the reason new players are not coming into the game is because the market is "mature" I think they're letting themselves off the hook too easily: people aren't coming into this game because the means by which one advances one's character preclude adventure and necessitate repetitive, boring farming. And, that's the same reason high level, long-time players are tempted to leave the game too.

Older players bang into similar walls, a bit sooner if they can't play 30 hours a week, a bit later perhaps if they can raid for six hours at a stretch on multiple days of the week.

One thing that olders players do not do to have more fun is to make untwinked alts for the pure pleasure of lower-level quests and adventures. Why? Because just as such paths are difficult for new players, they ain't that much fun for more experienced players, either.

The quest system in this game needs a total re-vamp, and however it's done, it ought to acknowledge the effects of mudflation. And the tradeskills need to serve some function other than removing plat from the player economy.

My sense is that nowadays almost all the quests that are still being done are either progressive, or soloable. Nearly all of them also result in a cool piece of gear. SOE needs to analyze the characteristics of the quests that people still do, and match them against the characteristics of quests that either are not done at all or are done only by high-level characters farming twink gear. Stop designing quests that have the characteristics of the hundreds of quests now in the game that no one completes. Designing quests and tradeskill items no one will bother doing except perhaps as a curiosity is a waste of labor resources for SOE. They drive up SOE's expenses and fail to increase revenue, and the opportunity cost is clearly that some skilled programmers are failing to address other aspects of the game that could be improved.

I happen to think that if a quest requires a group or two or three of players, then no one will do the quest unless: A) the reward is so good that guilds will want to organize themselves to help individual players get that specific item, or B) the quest might reasonably result in rewards for multiple classes of characters. If a quest requires a tank and healer and a dps melee, why not have the quest result in at least the chance of rewards for all of those classes? why is it always the case that the 11 people who help someone on a quest are doing the quester a big favor? Why couldn't the quester be doing the others a favor by setting up a quest whose final fight might result in rewards for the quester's friends?

You asked a simple question about gear, and my answer is that the structural aspects of enormous parts of the game need to be revamped in a systematic way.

As for specific positive suggestions, those are of course much more difficult to come up with.

If I were in charge of the game, though, I think I would take a hard look at the whole system of drop/no drop gear. Dumping a bunch of better tradeable gear into the game actually won't solve solve the structural problems I've discussed. If warriors get twinked now with BD cultural, then they'll just start twinking with the better "new" stuff and the structural problems I've pointed out will remain the same, only the itemization will have changed. Higher levels will farm trivial content or be doing the same raid 60 times in a row, and new players will farm trivial content.

Let me repeat myself for a second: if I were in charge of the game, I would take a hard look at the drop/no drop system. I'd explore the possibility of some sort of progressive quests/tradeskills/fights that would allow characters and classes of all levels to transform their low-level quested/made/adventured gear into better and better gear as the character went out and fought/explored more and more.

As it stands now, dropable gear drives endless farming: low-level characters farming trivial or near trivial mobs over and over to raise the money to buy gear from higher level characters who themselves are killing trivial or near trivial mobs to get that gear. Meanwhile, no-drop gear is either multi-quested (for fees) or is sort of discouraging to the person who fought for it, wears it for a couple of levels, and then has no use for it and can't do anything with it except destroy it. Or let his bot alt loot it in the first place.

There needs to be a third way.

I went over the Warrior Class Issues List over on SoE's Warriors board and was kinda disappointed. A couple of things stuck out a lot:

Kick/Slam - there're two issues and it doesn't seem like SoE understands: Damage and the ability to stun (chiefly to try to interrupt gaters).

Shields - even if shields were made more useful to Tanks by for example giving them a chance to block, Warriors do not generally have the kind of quality DPS 1 handers available to Knights and by going from DW to 1H + shield aggro procs are reduced. Since a Warrior might well want to equip a shield when tanking, this aggro reduction is a concern.

Crits/Cripples - didn't make the list. Crit damage, particularly a minimum Crit, is not very high. With as hard as mobs hit now going Beserk and hitting Mightystrike is pretty useless - a Warriors is either going to die almost immediately or get healed which means of course those intended Cripples become mediocre Crit spam.

Groupability - didn't make the list. Not long ago SoE announced that the Phase 2 Melee Update was not going to happen. This update was intended to improve the groupability of Warriors and to make palying a Warrior more interesting. Warrior groupability issues still exist. In brief, the other two Plate tanks bring various abilities into groups including buffs, heals, Root, stun , GRC, etc. or the ability to FD pull, "snare", use a pet, and to do damage in a variety of ways. Warrior capabilites may include greater survivability and little else. Should Knights get "burst mitigation" or should most experiance camps never require a Warrior's greater mitigation, then Warriors have little to recommend them to groups compared to other Plate tank classes.

As one of the people who frequent these boards that is *not* Time, EP or even PonB flagged, I just want to make sure that you keep our interests in mind as well. To me, some of those would include:

--Increased Weapon Selection. Off of my head I can come up with less than a dozen weapons that have any sort of proc usefull for aggro holding. I am one of the fortunate few to be able to afford something better than a Copper Hammer of Striking/Sword of the Bloodsworn combo due to having a 65 main and having played so long. But when a good 2/3rds of the warriors on a server are using the same weapon setup, I think something needs to be changed. Either remove the need for proc spams to keep aggro on higher-up mobs or decrease the aggro generated from other classes, casters in particular, and crit-dots even more in particular.

--Lack of dependance on deity. True, a warrior will function just fine if he is agnostic or worships someone that isn't the Tribunal or Rallos Zek, but they are certainly at an advantage, and quite a large one at that. A few people touched on better armor that was non-deity, I am basically just saying that that is a good idea.

--Access to more lower-tier raids. Someone mentioned redoing the 7-day spawn timer that was prevelant throughout Velious and parts of Luclin, I support that very much.

--Higher selection of items, not more of a certain item. To me, it's sad that there is such a small selection of items for a particular slot. Not only does this make it difficult to sell an item sometimes, it also leads to the price being driven into the ground. The fact that I can buy a Greatstaff of Thunder for 50pp right now is very, very sad.

Perhaps it would be a good idea to re-evaluate the No Drop tag on such a large amount of items. As the content expands, items from older expansions should become droppable, in my opinion. Not all of course, but some. Items like a Windspeeder, Claw of Lightning etc. would fit in well with todays market, and would perhaps increase incentive to go to those zones again.

These are obviously not all warrior-specific concerns, but with the degree that our gear defines our character, I believe that the people who don't have the time or the want to do heavy duty raidin g should have a chance to get really good items aside from a Loyalists Shield of Honor. Perhaps we could get more indepth quests that yield items more around the 125-150hp area, but that take time/tradeskills/smaller raids to do...ALA Velious and all of the quests from Thurg.

Just my opinions on such is all. Have a nice day.
