I don't really think that's a valid argument at this point either. Yes, the stats, ratio, and risk versus reward are perfect for being 1.5 and quite on track for casuals/non-ubers/non-Time+, but the effects (both the Incite reduction and the right-click) are not at all what I'd considering "rocking additions" to our class, even for casuals. (And take a look at my Magelo if you doubt where I'm coming from, I'm not an uber by any stretch of the imagination).

The fact is, the Incite reduction is *nearly* useless; it won't make a lick of difference for a 65 Warrior exping in LDoN nor a difference for a 70 Warrior tanking Txevu boss mobs; the reduction is simply too marginal to matter. (That doesn't even touch on the endurance issues inherant, which I think is a completely different topic).

The right-click mitigation is at least somewhat useful, and I wouldn't be angry or even completely against leaving it alone, as long as the Incite effect is altered come patch time. However, that said, it needs to be noted that the effect is actually more of a benefit to the Warrior's group, rather than to himself. Again, even for a casual, regardless of whether you're raiding or not, the very minor amount of mitigation provided will not make a noticeable difference regardless of whether you're exping with mobs that hit for 400 or raiding against a 5k-quadder. The effect will not last long enough and/or reduce damage enough to even be worthwhile for the Warrior, however, I would argue that it will produce a benefit to the group, providing them a slight benefit from being attacked when/if you lose agro, another mob pops, adds come, etc. The most obvious benefit of this right-click effect is for raid mobs with harsh AEs for non-tanking Warriors and their subsequent groups. The 15% chance to resist a brutal AE is much more useful than the mitigation, and in fact, I would be quite in favor of altering the click to provide only a 15% chance to resist incoming detrimental spells for the Warrior's entire group for a fixed duration (say, 1 minute) and provide no melee damage alteration. The 2.0 could be upgraded to produce higher chance to resist, longer duration, and/or melee damage mitigation, but the key is a reliable and useful effect in nearly all situations. The obvious downside, of course, is minimal use for exp groups, but even then, nowadays many named mobs you'll be pulling with your exp groups have some sort of AE, so that could still prove useful.

However, the point is, while the weapon is certainly great for non-ubers/casuals, and I won't argue that at all, the effects, especially the Incite timer reduction, needs some serious consideration (which it appears it will get per Frod's recent posts).
