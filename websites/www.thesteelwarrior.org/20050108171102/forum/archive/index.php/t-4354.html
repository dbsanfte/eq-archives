I'm a cleric who pacify pulls in LDoN.

When do I pull? I pull when (a) the group makeup is such that pulling is more efficient than charging, and (b) I'm the character with the least damage output in the group and therefore I'm the one who can be best spared to do the job.

How do I pull? In such a way as to ensure that the group is fighting at least one monster 100% of the time wherever possible, and using Mark of the Righteous, which generates near-zero aggro.

It's fairly safe to assume that if you were worried about pulling, you're in a group with no mezzer, so what I have to say below is based on the assumption that the group is dealing with adds by rooting them or by just slow and ignore.

How do I feel about pickup group warriors pulling? Frankly, unless I know the warrior, I don't like it at all, and I sympathize with the cleric who wanted to pacify. I've seen enough warriors fire an arrow and go, "Umm, lots" just before their health drops like a stone and eight monsters come charging round the corner that I'm leery of pulling warriors.

The problem is that sucky warriors frequently insist on pulling in LDoN. As a class, warriors like to take charge, and I think they're working on the theory that if they get initial aggro, the chances of them hanging onto that aggro are thereby increased.

Now, pulling is largely about the abilities, experience, and knowledge of the guy behind the keyboard - and unfortunately, while many pickup group warriors are able pullers, others just tend to stick an arrow in the nearest thing without the faintest idea of how many monsters will come when they do it.

As a cleric faced with a pickup group warrior you don't know, it's unwise to assume that your warrior is a competent puller. After all, warrior is a class which lacks any of the key special abilities for pulling.

In fact, I can't think of a class which is less well qualified, by nature of its innate abilities, to pull than a warrior. Maybe mage, although mages can use pet pulling in creative ways; but pretty much every other class has some ability which would be helpful, even if it's only sneak or root.

So those warriors who are skilled at pulling - and they certainly exist - have become skilled despite the limitations of their class.

As for that "assist me and pacify my target" thing - henceforth to be known as "assify" - that's just pathetic. Melees who do this are generally unaware of key information, like how long before the paci wears off, which monsters are paci-immune, or how much paci mana their cleric can afford to blow. If you need a room pacified, kindly get the hell out of the way and let me pull it, please and thank you.
