I've been on a ton of messy Behemoth wins and losses, and one very clean win. I'll explain the difference on the clean win, as I saw it.

In my experience, 90% of the reason raids wipe is because of the chaos that ensues when people try to run into the Behemoth room after he's awakened. Multiple AE's, large aggro range, etc. mean that the rush into the room turns into a zerg, as Behemoth attacks the wrong people, the right people don't get heals, and the wrong people are thrown out of range.

On the clean win that I was on, the main raid force was positioned in the Behemoth room IN ADVANCE, before he awakened. As soon as the spider groups began their thing, the main force moved into position and waited (and waited). So when Behemoth finally woke up, the tank and everyone else were already positioned properly, aggro was obtained properly, and the fight proceeded like any AE boss encounter.

Yes, it's a very vulnerable feeling to be standing in the danger room, next to the sleeping Behemoth, while the raid's life is in the hands of the spider groups. But so what? If the spider groups mess up, then your raid is borked anyway, regardless of your strategy. Put the tank and healers in the right place BEFORE Behemoth wakes up, and you remove 90% of the chaos from the event.

I'm with the zerg proponents on this fight. If you have 50+ people and they all go all-out dps on him, he will die in about 30 seconds tops. Hell a weaponshield ranger could tank that long with no heals.

I have done him with 2 different guilds and the 2nd one does him a whole lot better. What we did on him was position groups around the hallway to kill spiders. If you're trying to 3-group him then yea you will need to work a little on positioning, but with 50+ people, by the time you got him positioned, he would have been dead if you just went full dps on him. You're only decreasing your chances of winning by screwing around with him when he has so few hps. We do push him toward a corner with the MT in front of him, but by the time he gets to the corner he's at half health and dropping fast.

Do make sure your nukers know to nuke from melee range if they think they have any chance of getting aggro though, because ping-pong will kill you.
