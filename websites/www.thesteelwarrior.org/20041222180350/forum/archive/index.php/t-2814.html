I managed to get my hands on a level 65 Shadow Knight and a level 65 Paladin to do some testing on hate generation (Thanks beta server!!) and came up with some interesting results.


Test parameters:

Shadow Knight using 1 spell:
Terror of Thule (600 points of pure hate, along the same lines as our EB procs.)

Paladin using 3 spells:
Stun (lvl 30 0.0 sec stun with no dd.. 1.5sec cast with 12 sec refresh)
Force of Akilae (lvl 62 4.0 sec stun with no dd.. 1 sec cast with 12 sec refresh)
Quellious' Word of Serenity (level 64 10.0 sec stun with 108dd.. 2 sec cast with 24 sec refresh)


The Shadow Knight, with no weapon equipped, would the bring the mob to the test area with proximity agro, then cast Terror of Thule (600 pure agro). The SK would then duck to avoid riposte and stand there with attack off.

We ran 5 tests:

1.) Paladin cast Stun(level 30 0.0sec stun) until the mob turned on the paladin.

After repeating this with 4 mobs, it consistantly took five(5) 0.0 sec stuns to turn the mob off the Shadow Knight. Total time: ~67 seconds.

2.) Paladin cast Force of Akilae (lvl 62 4.0 sec stun) until the mob turned on the paladin.

After repeating this with 5 mobs, again it consistantly took five(5) 4.0 sec stuns to turn the mob off the Shadow Knight. Total time: ~67 seconds

3.) Paladin alternating between Stun and Force of Akilae with just enough time in between casts to see if the mob turned on the paladin. (Cast Stun, wait for the stun to wear off, cast Force of Akilae, wait for the stun to wear off, wait for Stun to ungrey, rinse/repeat)

After repeating this with 4 mobs, it consistantly took five(5) stuns, regardless of the stun duration, to turn the mob on the paladin. Total time: ~34 seconds

4. ) Paladin cast Quellious' Word of Serenity (level 64 10.0 sec stun with 108dd, knockback:1) until the mob turned on the Paladin.

After repeating with 8 mobs, it consistantly took three(3) 10sec stuns+108dds to turn the mob on the paladin. Total time:~78 seconds

5.) Paladin didn't begin to cast on the mob until the Shadow Knight had tanked for 3 minutes. Paladin cast Quellious' Word of Serenity (level 64 10.0 sec stun with 108dd, knockback:1) until the mob turned on the Paladin.

After repeating with 3 mobs, it consistantly took three(3) 10 sec stuns+108dds to turn the mob on the paladin. Total time: ~ 4:20 seconds



So, after the testing, it seems that, regardless of the duration of the stun, whether it be 0.0 4.0 or 10.0, they generate a set amount of agro. (Which appears to be between 121 and 149 points of agro)

If a stun is between 121 and 149 agro, the Paladin casting Quellious' Word of Serenity (level 64 10.0 sec stun with 108dd, knockback:1), proved that the DD damage does at minimum .15 point of agro and at maximum 1 points of agro per point of DD. I'll see if I can narrow this number down any more in a few days.

Test #5 showed that hate doesn't decrease over time if there is only one person on the hate list. I'll go back and run another test to check if hate diminishes over time with 2 people on the hate list

Personally, I think you guys are mistaking initial aggro, with "sticky" aggro.

When a mob is on a person that mob has a resistance to change targets. It doesnt have anything to do with being the first person to aggro the mob, it has to do with being the person that mob is currently aggro on.

When I first did my AE taunt tests, I saw this and attributed it to AE taunt adding top hate + some extra hate. In actuality, AE taunt just adds top hate + 1 and sticks the mob on you, but for someone else to taunt it off, they have to overcome the "sticky hate" factor.

In my tests I had a cleric pull a pussling in CoD with 2-3 stuns. I would then AE taunt off the cleric. The cleric would then cast DD spells to regain aggro while I did no more hate.

The cleric could not pull the mob off me with 1 90 point DD nuke, but could pull the mob off me every time with 1 160 point nuke.

I believe sticky aggro to be between 90-160 hate, that is when a mob is aggro on someone, you have to overcome that persons aggro + 90-160 hate to make the mob swap targets.

Best bet to test aggro of stuns would be to pull with a 3rd party with a good amount of aggro and have the pally use stuns to pull the mob off the 3rd party. Then repeat the test with a SK, using the thule line to pull the mob off the 3rd party.

For instance. Ranger snares mob a 10X and runs to camp. Pally chains non DD stuns until gaining aggro. Ranger snares mob b 10x and runs to camp. SK thules mob until gaining aggro.

That seems to be a plausible way to take sticky aggro out of the equation.

Taunt per the Pencil and Paper rules... .. quoting myself here from another post..

Taunt

Taunting is the art of angering and annoying opponents so that they attack you in preference to all other opponents, even if thats a tactically unwise decision for them.

Check: A Taunt check is opposed by the target's Sense Motive check. Favorable and unfavorable circumstances weigh heavily on the outcome of a tuantcheck. Four circumstances can work agains the taunter and they are the following

1. The target is already attacking someone else.
2. The target is of animal intelligence or lower (they don't understand what you said or are deadset on finishing one person off before the next)
3. The target is above animal intelligence but cannot understand the taunter. (different language etc...)
4. The target realize that attacking the taunter may be tactically unwise.

Each of these circumstances adds a bonus to the sense motive check of the creature being taunted, as listed below

Taget's Condition Bonus

Attacking someone else +10
Animal intelligence +5
Doesn't understand the taunt +5
Tactically unwise +10

Taunting is a move action. A successful Taunt check indicates that the target MIGHT (yes the book says MIGHT) move toward the taunter --- depending on the targets intelligence and ranged attack options --- but certainly focuses all of its attacks on the taunter for 1 round (and possibly longer if it closes to melee). The target attcks the taunter as intelligently and effectively as it can, but if it knows it cannot attack him, it is likely to ignore the taunt. Targets affected by more than one character's taunt skill will attack the character who taunted tem most recently. If the taunter damages a target on the same round he tries to taunt it, he recieves a +5 circumstance bonus to his Taunt check.

Scenario: be advised this is based on a D20 system and not 100's and over 300's for stats.

Karesh, a warrior with Charisma 16 and 8 ranks in Taunt, sees a wolf attacking her wizard companion 75 feet away. She knows she can't reach the wolf in one round, so she tries to goad the wolf into coming to her instead. Karesh first fires an arrow at the wolf to get its attention, striking it for 7 points of damage; she then makes a taunt check, rolling a 9 and adding her taunt bonus of +11, and her +5 circumstance bonus for having damaged the wolf this round, for a total of 25. However, the wolf gains +10 to its sense Motive check because it is already fighting something else, +5 for not understanding Kresh's taunts, and another +5 because its animal intelligence, the GM rolls a 6 on the wolf's sense motive check, so, with its +20 bonus (and +1 for its wisdom score), it gets a total of 27. The wolf keeps attacking the wizard. If karesh had rolled an 11 or higher, the wolf would have turned to attack her isntead, most likely for the remainder of the combat (unless it were then taunted by someone else).
