- if multiple quests are available only one may be active at a time,

Some of Niclet's issues might go away, and I'm not sure four hours of lfg mean the game is borked, but certain problems for the casual warrior are stubbornly systemic in this game, and I don't see any easy solution to those problems.
The primary issue revolves around a simple fact about how people play this game: some of them play an average of eight hours a day, every day, and they've been doing that for five years, and they've been doing it in large groups. Other people play in one to four hour slices, a few times a week, and they tend to do it with smaller groups of people.
The result is an extreme variety in warrior character development, and that variation is greater than differences in level would make it appear.
The problem here is that the game cannot simply stop giving the 8-hour-a-day folks something to do, since obviously they'd quit if they didn't have something to do. But the construction of sufficient challenges to amuse folks with that much game time and who are in large guilds creates a cycle where the rewards are greater for greater and greater challenges, which provides these folks with more power, which generates a need for yet greater challenges.
Eventually, some of these challenges become significant enough that a good many casual players will never be able to meet them.
An associated problem for casual warriors in casual guilds is the disparity between how fast they can get things done as opposed to how fast warriors in raiding guilds can get the same thing done. If one of the barriers to getting a drop is the rarity of the drop, for example, many guilds simply hit the mob night after night until the drops come. For casual guilds, however, it may take many days or even weeks to make a second attempt.
I don't see these issues going away, because I think they are systemic.
They don't mean that casual warriors have nothing to do. They do mean that casual warriors thrash around a good bit at various points attempting to get inferior gear to that available in much less time to raiding warriors.
Personally, the relative efficiency of raiding warriors and their access to better gear bothers me not at all as long as when I get into, say, a pick-up group, I can do a good job tanking. I often get tells from people I've previously done pick-up groups with. Even if I'm not lfg, sometimes they'll ask me to join them, and a good many of them are raiding folks (often playing an alt). As long as folks in raiding guilds are taking the trouble to seek me out when they do pick-up groups, I figure my warrior is able to do his job.
I know for sure that he is modestly geared, but I figure it doesn't matter as long as people who have seen me tank want me to tank for them. But at some point, I think, my lack of access to all but the marginal end of gear available to warriors will result in a steady decline in my relative tanking ability. At some point, I either have to change my real life, or be gimp, or stop playing the warrior. The first two choices are not acceptable, and I'm not looking forward to the third. I could, as another poster has suggested, play a necro or some damn thing that can solo. But nothing beats playing a warrior. And all of you know why: when you're not playing one, or on one but not fighting, warriors are frustrating odd little toons. But when you're tanking, all that other stuff just falls away.
That's the carrot: maintanking a tough mob, and doing it well.
It's the only carrot.
All other non-player aspects of the game are merely sticks and the illusions of carrots.
If the real carrot goes away because I can't gear my warrior well enough to do his job well, then no more game for me. And that's the central issue for me as a casual warrior.
