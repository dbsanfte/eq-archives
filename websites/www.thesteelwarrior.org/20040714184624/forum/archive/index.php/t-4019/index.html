It's actually not important how much HP each AC is worth when you look at the class abilities broadly.

Suppose warriors has X HP and Paladin has 0.9X HP, and we interpret mitigate 10% better as warriors takes 10% less damage. Then, when a Paladin takes 100 HP of damage, it'd take 111 HP do the same to the warrior. Hence, the warrior X HP is really worth 1.11X HP if both classe are taking the same damage.

So now we get Wariorr at 1.11X and Paladin at 0.9X, which is roughly the 80% ratio that as quoted.

If you use the recent monk parse Brodda Thep quoted (monk 1800 AC and Paladin 2100 AC), using mob DB = 200, DI = 40, then the Paladin was taking 82% the damage as the Monk. If an uber monk has 10K HP, then that means the Paladin had effectively 21% more than monk HP, which is 2100. In reality it's better for warriors because the more HP you have, the more effective AC is (taking half damage at 10K is like having 10K more HP, as opposed to taking half damage at 1K is only good for 1K extra HPs).

This would seem to indicate 1 displayed AC = 7 HP.
