Assume that you are in a group where the level 30 mob_012 is attacked by your band of level 30ish (warrior, ranger, wizard, shaman, druid, monk).

ALSO assume that the taunt button is now pushable twice as often as in the past.

The warrior watches while the monk pulls the beast into camp and feigns death (no hate from the mob now), the ranger snares (generates imaginary number of 100) and the shaman slows (generating imaginary number of 150).

The warrior hits attack, misses and then hits taunt and gets a partial success of 70%. This puts 105 hate on the warrior and the mob is on the shaman at this point.

Then the wizard hits the mob with a mighty nuke (generates imaginary number of 400) and is now on the top of the hate list. The warrior swing and hits the mob for 40 points of damage (generates imaginary number of 40 + the 105 already from the partial success on the taunt for a total of 145) and hits taunt and gets a partial success of 70% which would be factored from the wizard who is at the top of the list with 400 which would be an additional 280 hate + 145 = 425 hate!

Woohoo! Even though the warrior did not succeed fully on either taunt, the added hate from receiving part of the agro that a fully successful taunt would generate gives the lucky warrior agro for a time.

To make this an honest scale, with low taunt skill, the warrior should have a success rate similar to:

40% fail
30% partial (10-50%)
20% parial (60-90%)
10% Total success

But that with a low skill.

At level 65 with maxed taunt skill it should be more like:

20% fail
25% partial (10-50%)
25% parial (60-90%)
30% Total success

This is a much lower success rate than our normal success of about half. (I don't have the exact percentage parsed, I don't parse). However, with the extra hate generated by the partial successes, a warrior can still command pretty nice agro.

However, I know that this ONLY works when the warrior is NOT at the top of the hate list and that the taunt button does NOTHING when the warrior has agro. This would continue to be the case. However, this new way of computing what happens when you hit taunt would mean fewer times when the mob will turn on you in that initial push of the button, but the ability to grab agro better when the mob has turned on a ranger, wizard, cleric, enchanter, shaman, etc and you have pushed the button twice or thrice.

Battleblade
Hulanta's model seems to me to be more in line with reasonable mob reaction than the current implimentation. Even if stripped down to barebones it's superior to a binary result. For arguments sake:

Mob is infuriated - top of the Hate List +200 Hate
Mob is angered - +200 Hate
Mob laughs at your puny insult - nada

Making the current implimentation worse perhaps is the way the RNG might be being used -

A random coin flip has a 50% chance of coming up heads totally independant of the previous results and while there can be statisically improbable runs, each flip is random.

However Taunt should not be a coin flip - it's a skill. It is a lot closer to a baseball player with a .500 average. The batter may have slumps, but he's also a profesional with techniques designed to put him back in the groove and break slumps. Can you imagine a baseball player with a .500 average going 20 at-bats without a hit? It's quite unlikely.

A method to simulate a skill vs. a random flip is easy to impliment. Instead of each coin flip being an independant event, previous results are stored and used to modify the odds as a hot streak or slump lengthens. This is intended to produce expected results (eliminate unlikely runs not generally found in a skill).

The higher percentage of sucess for a skill, the less likely a long run of failure should be. And, as Hulanta points out partial success is more skill-like then a binary system in the first place. Our baseball player is a skillful hitter. He may not always hit a homerun - but a single, double or triple is a good effort and is recognized as such.

It should be relatively easy to establish if tradeskills and Taunt are implimented as skills or coin flips. The length of runs is a dead giveaway.

Now it may very well be that since the RNG is run on the server side this becomes more complicated, but it's not undoable. It's also quite likely that such a system exists in EQ (it's been known for 30 years) and players merely take note of slumps.

Warrior at 145, wizard at 400. 70% taunt here gets 178 points, not 280, for a total of 323. Wizard still getting hit.

What ya missed id that taunt puts you at top of the hate list counting from where you are, not from the bottom. In other words, a warrior who has nearly no hate and taunts from someone with 400 hate gains 401 agro and gets attacked. A warrior with 300 hate taunting off someone with 400 gets 101 points of agro for a total of 401.

This can be observed in game quite easily: when someone takes agro from you mid-battle (after you've been building up hate) and you taunt successfully, you should get all their agro in addition to yours, which is near the top too, giving you a massive amount of hate that totals nearly double what the top agro was before. This should give you SOLID agro after a successful taunt; instead, you often lose it in the next combat round, indicating the amount of hate that partoicular taunt added wasn't much since you were so high on the agro list to begin with.

Taunt, once again, simply gives you the difference between your current position on the hate list and the top position on the hate list +1. 70% of that still won't help, because as you get closer to the top of the hate list the number needed yo put you on top of it gets smaller, and taunting for less than that number gives you an even smaller amount, never enough to hit the top.

The idea of a partial taunt succeeding in getting the taunter agro won't work; partial taunt, the way taunt works now, would be a lot like a cosine curve - it gets ever closer to the x and y axis, but never actually touches it.

Another idea Hulanta presented has some merit, that about taunt refresh being halved. This is good, if only because it gives taunt more times to succeed (fully). However, the way taunt skill works now, partial taunting won't work - not unless the skill is reworked to add a substantial and fixed amount of agro.
