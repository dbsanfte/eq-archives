I was lfg in BB a few weeks ago I actually heard that in OOC. With the ease of LDON adventures and classes overlapping, I didn't think there was any such thing as a NEEDED class anymore. After reading the posts on Pallys, only one thing really needs to be done and a couple of things I would like to see done.
The thing that would solve the aggro problems is not give us more but give the Pallys less. Increasing ours would probably screw up some high level raids from verants point of view at least. When a 58 pally can keep aggro from my 63 warrior and by keeping aggro I mean glued, something is seriously fucked.
Stun needs to take away aggro instead of increasing it. Of course the mob will beeline to the girly man in the shiny armor but then the battle changes. The mob gets hit by stun and in a semi-conscious state recieves a moment of clarity. He thinks to himself "was that a shammy that just slowed me?", or " Did someone just heal the guy that I was beating on?", or "Is that a rogue behind me or is someone just glad to see me?". The mob would refocus his attention on the people who are making the battle go bad for him. This would penalize a chain stunning MT and make him fight like a man. Stun would have to be used in an emergency like a gating mob or Ch'ing one, but they should not be rewarded for using it.
Actually, the way taunt is set up for warriors seems to be ok. Using proximity, I can usually get back aggro in an aggressive LDON group in one refresh of taunt. This should keep the casters on their toes, keep roots memed, keep backup healers prepared, keeps everyone awake. The way the pallys have it now is just plain too easy.
Two other things I would like to see added is a melles ability to pacify and maybe a rez potion. The mellee pac could be like an entice or something that would lead a mob from his post without alarming any mobs in chain range. "psst c'mere". This could have a limited effect in that you could use it only from a short range from real aggro range. If mob came within real aggro range while still in chain range, then the chain would activate. Using this would take some skill and practice but I don't know if it could be coded in a patch. I think it would be a less effective pacify but at the same time take away from the Pallys NEEDED status.
Rez potions would be cool to have but I don't think they would ever happen because they would make difficult raids easier by letting someone with fd have some potions instead of camping clerics. In any case this would take some utility from our Pally friends. I'd like to see some profit margins built in for the shammys like 80 plat to make or something but really I think clerics would have fits if this went thru.
I know that overlapping utility is the desired effect for LDON and it is very nice that i can whip up a sucessfull group in 10 minutes from a myriad of classes. but the fact that a warrior is this good at getting groups together should emphasize the fact that we need to regain our MT status. LFG/on and pray.

Let's not get so focused on stuns as to forget that a hybrid can get agro with many other spells too, they just use stuns cause they're easiest. Flash of light is terrific agro; it acts like a fear if EVERYONE moves out of a blinded mob's melee range, but how often does that happen? Same goes for heals, they agro great too. Calling for a stun nerf won't work, because they're far from the only agro machine in a pally's spellbook.

Only two approaches I see working are:
1) increase warrior agro to match, or
2) rework hybrid spells so that they're all separate, paladin-only spells and then make them generate vastly reduced amounts of agro. You can't change the spells as they are because the change would affect a lot of other classes

Option 1) seems a lot easier.

I think the break point for a level 65 LDoN adventure is having an average group level of 63+. If the average group level is under 63 a Paladin will be able to Pacify pull most, if not all, of an adventure. Meanwhile if the group level is 63+ the Paladin will not be able to pacify anything except perhaps a DC mob.

Paladins are formidable tanks, and the perception of them is even greater for undead environments. It is not surprising that a group felt they needed a Paladin, even though it is quite unlikely that they actually required one to succeed. In this case perception is greater than reality. The amount of undead in the Mistmoore adventures has been significantly reduced recently. Shadows and 'zombie rats' are no longer undead. I find the mix of undead in most Mistmoore adventures to be under 50% now. I don't even load my undead proc or damage spell there any more. So the reality is that a Paladin is not required for a BB (or any other) adventure, yet the group felt that they needed one.

I can see how this is infuriating to Warriors. Let's hope the upcoming changes help balance the situation a bit.
