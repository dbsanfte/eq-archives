Sometimes, I think I'm the only one who reads what I write. And I even wrote it on your thread!

http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showpost.php?p=169331&postcount=90

You know a game is fundamentally flawed when a player can happily and easily level to 60 solo or with a small group of friends of any random class assortment. The game is designed for casual play. Casual players praise the rested state, the fact that they can log on an accomplish something in a matter of minutes, the fact that quests are broken into quickly-completed steps. Even the instances are all completed within 2-3 hours, tops.

But wait, those casual players reach 60. Here is what they will see. They cannot complete quests at all unless they five-man instances (with the right assortment of classes) some of which that take a solid 2-group raid 3-4 hours; with a five-man it takes minimum 5 hours. They cannot log in and immediately feel a sense of accomplishment because even in solid guilds, which our happy casual players probably do not have, getting the right numbers and classes takes time.

We are looking at two radically different games. And unlike EverQuest, where at least players sort of learned the score as they leveled, here the "end game" will come as a complete surprise.


I have no idea where you get the concept that the progression goes from solo to raid to group to raid. Where exactly in the 50-60s do you raid aside from "final" instances (BRS/Strat/Scholo/MC/Ony)? I would hazard a guess that "gearing up" in those instances never ever ever involves single groups. You don't single group, for instance, BRS unless you need quests finished - and you'd do that before you gear up. In any event, if that's been your progression, so be it. I don't think that's the typical experience. I think its solo/group ---> raid with absolutely no inbetween. Which is the problem that serious players have been bitching about since beta. The game simply doesn't teach people how to be useful or efficient in a group/raid so by the time we get to level 60 and we have to group ... some of us end up with real fucking wacko losers in our parties with zero etiquette or skill.
