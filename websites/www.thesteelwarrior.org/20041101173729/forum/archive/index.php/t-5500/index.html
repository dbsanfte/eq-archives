Welcome to the SteelWarrior boards Colcannon!

As suggested the best thing for you to work on is your jewelry as it is probably the easiest to do at first. The suggestion of valorium rings of Gallantry, Blood earring of rage, etc. are great, but you will find they are way out of your price range at 6k and 15k each on your server. The best thing to do is check the bazaar for particular slots you want to upgrade and search based on the stats you are after. Example: search for rings+hitpoints and see what is available. Find something you want/like and then do some research where the items are available/dropped and or how much they are. Once you determine that an item is farmable by your group then simply head there and get the item.

Once your group is over 55+ you could try to tackle the King in Droga and the Seer to get yourself a copper hammer of striking and a sword of the bloodsworn; both of those one hand weapons
are probably the best you will get until you join a guild that tackles SSRA/PoP zones. I believe you already have a Greatstaff of Thunder ( right? ) so you are set there for 2hander. It really is the best thing you will get until you are guilded.
At 55+ your group should be able to start tackling the arena in Kael for armor upgrades as well if you want to try that route, altho the armor available from LDoN points is far superior to anything you will receive in Kael once you accumulate the points to spend. I really doubt you will ever come up with the coin to purchase the cultural armor mentioned here. Unless you are into tradeskills to the point of getting grandmaster level skill in a tradeskill ( which takes some serious coin to achieve/ and farming materials ad nauseum ) money is only attained by selling off an item of value won via a roll in a group.

Also remember you have a tremendous resource in a friend online who has a 65 warrior who went through exactly what you will in the 50's ( the 50's very hard for warriors IMHO )

Hope this helps.

Heya Col,

I would save the points for the bigger ticket items you have mentioned. I also wouldnt worry to much about resists as you can request resist buffs in group and also you are not going to be running into anything that requires you to have sufficient resists until you are guilded anyways. The first real resists required place you will encounter is likely to be in Temple of Veeshan in the Halls of testing: which is a guild raid event at your current level ( most guilds generally request that you be at least level 55 to raid there ). Once you have developed your gear/resists/hit points and AA's it is possible to single group the Halls of Testing ( i have done it ) althought at that point you really dont want the armor drops except for alts or selling as a MQ.
Your focus right now should be on acquiring hit point/AC items. If these items have +stamina it will help you now but above 62+ you will be hoping to replace as much stamina stuff with Pure raw +hit points items. My opinion is the sooner you get adding +hit points/AC stuff the better off you are.
As for AA i agree that you should do run3 till it is done then switch to Full exp until you are 65. Experience at 65 comes easier as you are fighting in the higher end zones with greater experience. Example: A decent group in Plane of Valor in the inner cave I was able to get a full AA point every 80 Minutes.
The other thing that can Help you out is to Focus on a weapon type for now ( perhaps wait until you are 55 and can use the GSOT ). See what weapon style you prefer to use ( 1 hand vs 2hand )and sell off the other weapons for some cash that you can use to upgrade other gear slots.

paladiin
Tons of fun upgrade options for you! And if you're planning on going to PoP soon (which is probably likely due to having higher level friends) you're going to want to upgrade fairly soon.

Here's the quick list of what really doesn't need upgrading soon: Helm, Velium Fire Wedding Ring (you can go to 65 with it if needed - I did), Arms, Range, and maybe shoulders. Pretty much everything else needs to be gutted and redone, before doing serious PoP tanking (excluding PoJ junk).

What to do, what to do for upgrades? Sure, if you're loaded with plat your upgrade path would be fairly easy. But sounds like that's not an option. So we're looking at "bang for the plat".

Earring: Jagged Bone Hoop. 6ac and 40hp on a shoestring budget - easily under 1k, and I've seen it for as low as 400pp. Black Sapphire Electrum Earring is another cheap option (2ac 35hp). I personally use (since I'm perpetually broke) a Forgotten Ebony Earring (10ac 50hp) which is a new drop in Velks which is campable with a group at your level (though pretty rare).

Neck: Ribbon Choker (7ac 45hp) should be fairly cheap, as is the better Amulet of Mummy Wrappings (12ac 45hp and lots of stats). I have personally seen both of these at 500pp before on my server. The Amulet would probably be worth going higher if you have the plat for it.

Face: Grobb Hide Mask (7ac 30hp 2sta) or Crystal Spider Eyes (8ac 35hp) are two inexpensive upgrades for you.

Finger: Cheap-o Velium Fire Wedding Ring until you can get the highly recommended Valorium Ring of Gallantry.

Wrists: I'd recommend keeping an eye out for cheap Thurg MQ's - I know on FV you can put together the Champion's wrists for ~500pp.

Hands: Again, a Champion's MQ would be perfect here, if you can find one for a good price. I personally have sold corroded plate gauntlets for 100pp. Worn Zraxthril Gauntlets (16ac 30hp and stats) are a minor upgrade and should be fairly inexpensive as well. There are also some LDoN gauntlets such as the Sandstorm Fragment Gauntlets (22ac 40hp and stats) which if found for cheap would be an option.

Chest: Your current piece is passable, but I'd suggest saving to take a step up. The Architect's Bejeweled Chestplate (54ac 60hp 5sta) is very nice, and is usually between 5k and 10k. Save your plat and watch the market for this one, and it'll be a great piece for the price for you to grow into.

Back: Crinose Cape of the Werewolf (12ac 80hp and stats) is very nice, and will likely only run you a K or 2.

Legs: Kylong Greaves have dropped quite a bit in price (35ac 45hp and stats), or you can probably camp them with a group. Would be a nice upgrade for you.


Just a few ideas for cheap or fairly easily obtainable upgrades. There are always bigger and better items, but these shouldn't run you more than just a few thousand plat for some fairly decent upgrades in both HP and AC.
