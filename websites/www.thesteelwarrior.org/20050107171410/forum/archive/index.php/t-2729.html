Even using the incredibly gimpy FBSS as an example, look at it this way...if a haste item gives you 21% more swings, can you honestly say that replacing it for a belt with 25 AC and approximately 113 HP's (including STA boost) is going to lower the amount of damage you take by 21%?

I know I'm kind of comparing apples and oranges with that example, but you get the idea that the benefit you get from 21% more swings of your weapons far outweighs the benefit you get from 25 AC and 113 or so HP's.

Especially in raid situations, 113 HP's is maybe a quarter to one third of an average hit from a raid mob. I can count in the fingers of one hand the number of times that I can honestly say that an extra 100 HP's saved me from death. I *can* say with total confidence that 21% more swings in *each and every fight* has helped me immensely.

Now, use a more normal haste percentage that you would have if you're able to use a Heavy Rubicite Belt (level 51...say a 26% or 31% haste item...they're getting pretty cheap these days...certainly within the price range of a heavy rubicite belt, if not less, and some of them have good stats as well), and the odds are even more in favor of a haste belt.

The only reason to replace a haste belt is because you got better haste from a cloak or some other item in another slot.

I must concur: haste > all
