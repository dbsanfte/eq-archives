You may be okay one on one, but that really isn't a judgement measure of your pvp prowess. Consider when it's 5 on 5, or 10 on 10,etc..

The only thing that really matters is dishing out as much damage // or // having the utility to distrupt the other side. Arms and to a lesser extent fury warriors have the damage spiking ability that counts in pvp, protection warriors due to three basic issues really don't have the gang-buster utility that is required.

Diminishing returns and the lack of an instant stun greatly impact your distruption role. Given the fact that the best way to generate that initial rage is with a charge, and said charge is a 1.0second duration stun, any subsequent stun you use on that person is reduced 50% each application. Our stuns don't really last long anyway, and well, this just makes them very laclustre.

Weapon chains (and the rare gauntlets) negate one of the most effective counters you could have for a melee class, disarm. You can have all the talents in the world to extend this to 10 minutes but the moment you face a smart melee whom doesn't care about some red demonslaying glow you're at a decided disadvantage. Now barring all of this, even still your disarm really doesn't ammount to much. Most classes will just run a bit or also use their utility means to negate your advantage untill such time as the effect fades.

And the most important aspect of a protection warrior is also the most serious hampering issue you could have in pvp - taunt, or lack therof. The entire point in specing protection is to tank better then anyone else, but without an ability to focal that damage to yourself it doesn't really matter. Taunt, (perhalps just improved taunt) has to have some type of pvp utility even if it is just a 1~ish second forced target change from another to yourself. Without it, well you know the state of prot in pvp...
