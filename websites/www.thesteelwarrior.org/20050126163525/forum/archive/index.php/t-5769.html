Well, it's unlikely that you'd get that many failures in a row.
But a coin has about as much chance coming up heads as tails and if you actually flip a coin 100 times you'd be surprised how many sequences of 5+ heads (or tails) you get. In fact, if you are presented with two lists of coin flips, and one is a record of 100 actual coin flips and the other is made up by a person trying to replicate randomness, the list with the longest series of same-side results is likely the "real" coin flip list. Few people trying to replicate a two-result random list will insert a series of 7 or more straight "heads" or "tails." But if you flip a coin 100 times, it's likely to happen.
Anyway, my main point is that you can expect some failures (maybe not that many, but some), so be prepared for them.
