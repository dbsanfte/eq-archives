Rueeliar
Not a terribly bold statement at all. First, I stated outright that they were estimated. The figures I did were assuming certain things:

1) Warriors attack an average of 1.71 times per round with their mainhand weapon
2) Melee hate is Damage Rating + Damage Bonus
3) Stuns are estimated at 400 aggro
4) The warrior in question will have Echo of Anger
5) Raid conditions (100 percent haste, no bard overhaste)
6) The zone does not have a proc modifier
7) Weapon Affinity, innate proc mods, and Combat Effects add arithmetically

Brael believes there is a multiplier for melee dps, but I have yet to read the support for those when I was awake enough for it to settle in, so I have set that aside for this. I have heard many people in my guild express a belief that certain zones have altered procrates. I have not seen a parse to support this so I will again ignore it. A warrior with Combat Effects out the wazzoo and the expected Weapon Affinity 5 should have the same number of extra procs from these effects as procrate enhancements would be illogical to code multiplicitively for the potential of abuse by Combat Effects afficionados resulting in ludicrous procrates, otherwise (Example: Combat Effects 10 giving 7 percent, WA5 giving 50 percent, and DBoW giving 30 percent resulting in a total of 87 percent increase, not 109 percent increase, etc.)

Assuming these, we have:
Anger proc = 1225 hate (stun and DD will be augmented by EoA, direct hate will not)
Darkblade at 58.63 attacks per minute and 51 hate per swing
Estimated Darkblade melee aggro ~3439 per minute
BBoB at 66.19 attacks per minute and 56 hate per swing
Estimated BBoB melee aggro ~4263 hate per minute
Estimated difference in raw melee aggro ~824 hate per minute

The Darkblade has a 30 percent procmod. At 2ppm normally, the proc mod will average (nice and simply) an extra 0.60 procs every minute. The value of the missing 0.60 procs would be approximately 735 aggro. (Note: Most parses for procrates were indicating a procrate slightly higher than 2.0ppm, usually closer to 2.08ppm, which would mean 0.71ppm difference, for 870 hate instead, almost on the button for the difference.)

This would indicate an 89 hate per minute difference in the aggro output of the weapons in favor of the BBoB, which also would benefit tremendously in situations where stat debuffs render the warrior's Dex below 305, where procrates suffer but melee dps aggro is unaffected.

Unforseen factors and my refusal to get off my ass and look up the numbers and record them to the proper decimal places leaves the estimate with a very high potential for variance, but would indicate that they are very close. Feel free to poke holes in my numbers. I left plenty of room for it.

Rueeliar
I was assuming that procrate modifiers are compiled additively, and not multiplicitively. My reasoning behind this is that each piece of Combat Effects would have a greater return for having more of them previously, creating a very interesting and difficult to overcome overusefulness for a single effect. No other effects in the game have increasing value for highest increments of equal size over lower increments of the same amount. For this example, let's pretend that there are pieces of gear with Combat Effects 10 for every slot. With eighteen non-weapon slots to fill, that would mean Combat Effects 180 if you had them all. Every 10 points of Combat Effects increases procrate by approximately 7 percent, iirc?

If the modifiers were added together, you would have a procrate bonus of 226 percent of the base (0.07 x 18 +1).
If the modifiers were added multiplicitively, you would have a procrate of 338 percent of the base (1.07^18)

Adding in WA5 and the Darkblade's innate modifier would then rendered them as follows:
Addititively = 306 percent
Multiplicitively = 659 percent

Hence, I believe they are compiled addititively to avoid that kind of absolutely silly potential, and the 30 percent mod on the weapon would add 30 percent of the standard procrate to it, no matter what other gear was being worn, or .6 (or ~.71) procs per minute.
