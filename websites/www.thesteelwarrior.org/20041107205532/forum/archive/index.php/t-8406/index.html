Haass
Regen 3.

Listen to me here, and follow my logic. If you put points into stamina, you're gonna gain about 30-40 hp. THat's HP that's most likely not going to save your ass. By contrast, regen 3 will regen those HP in under a minute. Furthermore, if you ever are able to max your stamina with your gear, you're gonna feel pretty silly for wasting those points. Your regen rate will likely never reach capped status, and it will be useful for the rest of your EQ life.

You don't look like a raiding warrior where your max HP matters. You look like a warrior who does some LDoN or casual grouping a couple nites a week, and regen 3 would benefit you FAR more in that environment than a couple extra HP. If this is the case, and you do some PoJ or LoY on the nites that you play with your friends, you're going to be far happier with an increased regen rate than you will with 30 extra HP.

Lots of people tout metabolism on this board, but lets be serious here. Halas meat pies are fuckin 50pp a stack absolute tops, and they last a good 2 weeks. Do you REALLY want to waste hard earned XP on that? If you do, that's fine, but I can see far better things to put points into than cheap ass food.

Brabble
Lol, the problem here is that most of these are very very marginal, and they tend to get more marginal as you get stronger.
The case for Run 3 is that it works indoors, doesn't take a bluff slot, and you don't have to think about it.
Regen begins to look pretty marginal as you get more hitpoints, but again it's always on and 30 hps a minute running all the time never completely disappears (as does the benefit of STA).
If you want to think of them in their best light, both of these can make you marginally (and innately) slightly harder to damage. Run 3 can help you avoid getting hit once in a great while; the regen is basically a tiny mitigation factor in your favor whenever you are at <100% health. /shrug.
One of my warriors, who is kind of an experiment, quested jboots before he was 52. Since he hung out a while at 52 to tank Vox & Naggy, he had some AA points building up. Since he had jboot sow, he put his generals in regen and first aid. Also he does tradeskills, so he's farming mass green mobs as fast as possible; an innate ability to replace hitpoints can be handy, cut down time, etc.
In one sense, first aid is stupid because I suspect he'll eventually want to go back and get Run 3 for long indoor crawls when buff slots are at a premium.
But at least in his 50s, having Regen, first aid, the (somewhat irritating) focused will discipline and maxxed bind wound means that he can regen more than 300 hitpoints a minute. Fairly useful in the mid 50s, though progressively marginal as time goes on. Not nearly as useful if you don't farm or never hunt in small groups. A couple of my good friends play a mage and necro respectively, though, and sometimes to relax we hunt together. A capacity to regen hitpoints is handy for me under those admittedly narrow circumstances.
One aspect of first aid that is more helpful than it used to be is the fact that you can bw to 100%, and depending on your level and bw skill, you can bandage for about 50 hitpoints per bandage. No, this is not like winning the lottery, nor will it make you irresitible to wealthy & attractive & intriguingly flexible objects of your desire, but it does have a couple of small utilities:
1. When you do multiple LDoNs with a shaman, and you get buffed up by him at the entrance to the dungeon, lots of times there's a bit of downtime while casters med for a minute or two. In your 50s you can improve the efficiency of your shaman's canning by 2/3rds to 1/3rd, depending on your respective levels.
2. At any point after a tough fight when a med break is called, you can do the same for shaman or necros, or you can touch up anyone's hitpoints who won't have time to get to 100% during the med.
3. In some places, depending on the pull rate, you have about enough time to do one bw between pulls. Again extremely marginal, but for necros and shaman it's free mana.

How's that for spending too much time thinking about things that don't really matter?
