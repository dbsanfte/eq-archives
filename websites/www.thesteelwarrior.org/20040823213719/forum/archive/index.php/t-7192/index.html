Brabble
To answer the original question: after the Trolls lost their home town, I was thinking about making an alt and I finally decided on a Troll SK, precisely because I thought trolls might well have the most disadvantages of any SK race. Erudites were in there too, but they don't have an exp penalty. So, I picked a Troll because I couldn't figure out why anyone would still play one, and I thought it would be interesting to solve the problems associated with Trollness. And, eventually, eat some halflings.
There is no logical reason to make a Troll.

Back when I started started EQ, I only did minimal research before picking a race (hell, before picking a class).
I picked a Barb because they could wear both medium and large armor, which I thought might come in handy. They could slam, which I thought would be handy. They had a good newbie zone and starting city (lol, no, not PoK). I thought it would be easier to play a race that didn't have as many faction issues as evil races.
Once, those were pretty good reasons for picking a Barb war as a first character.
Briefly, once I realized that Barb wars had no newbie armor quests (well, not back then) I thought human warriors looked like a good choice too. But oh well: I tailored myself some leather armor, then smithed banded, then chain. I couldn't see in the dark, but I learned to back through the cave to BB. Killed hundreds of gnolls to tailor the gnoll cape (bleh, forgot what it's called) and then turned that in for a magic (!) piercing weapon and killed wisps for a Greater Light Stone (woot!).
Even pre-luclin I'm not sure race mattered much; all had benefits and all had drawbacks--you just had to "fix" different aspects of each character. Now race doesn't matter that much, but for different reasons--you don't have to spend a year leveling to 50; post-luclin expansions have largely dropped faction issues and PoP trivialized travel problems; the shared bank slots and the bazaar have trivialized lower-level gear-up problems. The regen patch muted any differences there, the 55 kick stun muted slam advantage, and so on and so on. Not only have differences between races been muted, but entire newbie skill sets have disappeared (remember practicing sense heading, lol? Remember meeting characters who spoke in languages you couldn't understand, heh? Remember when Blackburrow represented a substantial risk that you would fall into a trap to the bottom of the zone and lose all your gear?).
I wish that I'd also started characters in Freeport and somewhere on Faydark too, not because I wish I'd played those races, but because I would have had a chance to play in more of the classic young-player zones when they were still full of groups looking for more.
In my view, as well as adding significant content, expansions also tend to have the effect of eliminating content.
If I were able to make one change to the game right now, I'd make it impossible for a character to use a PoK book before he was level 30. I realize the many drawbacks to this idea. I'd still make it, though.
