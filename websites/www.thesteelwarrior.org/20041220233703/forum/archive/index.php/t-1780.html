In a group with players lower level than I am, they DO get more AA % per kill than I do (presumably because the difference between their level and the mob's level is smaller, therefore they get a bigger bonus). Unfortunately, they also drain on the group's resources and ultimately lower our kills/hour rate when compared to a character of higher level.

So, if gathering AAs as fast as possible is your plan, then (assuming that you can get in the same groups/hunting zones) you'll pick them up faster if you are lower level.

However, level is more important to your character's overall ability than a few AAs (HPs, resists, damage, aggro, etc...). Although your toon will be getting AAs faster, you'll be less effective in your groups and on raids. So essentially, if you stay at a lower level, you'll be gimping your character while you do that. And people understand this... a level 65 LFG warrior will be picked before a level 63 almost universally.

I normally get an AA every hour and a half to two hours when exp hunting. It is my experience that the benefits of being higher level (able to hunt in better exp zones, and being a more capable toon in your groups) allows you to more than make up for any loss of "level difference bonus" from gaining levels. At least for toons that group.

I am sure that some people who solo or duo/2 box have valid situations where it is better long term to stay lower level and reel in the AA from mobs that would otherwise turn green/light blue. But for people that prefer to hunt in groups, it is better to level up, and move to new hunting grounds.
