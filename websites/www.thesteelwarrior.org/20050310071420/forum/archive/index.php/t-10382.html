* ISS is a 0 gravity light-station in low altitude.
---- 0 gravity : human being can't live under a given thresholds of gravity (out of memory, this threshold is 0.2*g, g being the everage earth gravity) without slowly loosing its health. At the end death can happen. thus human being can't stay for an extented period of time in the ISS.
---- light-station : it has very weak radiactive protection, making dangerous the life on ISS, with possible death in case of unpredicted major gamma-ray explosion.
---- low-altitude : makes travels cheaper, but increase the cost of keeping the correct altitude. Moreover there is an increased chance of collision. But the station profits of the magnetic field of the earth to increase its radiactive protection (from high-energy charged-particles only).
---- it is mainly use for studies of long 0-gravity exposition. plus some observations.

* a moon base
---- the average moon gravity is under the thresholds of livable (out of memory, moon's gravity is around 0.18*g). thus human can't lives for an extented periode of time (longer than on the ISS, but still finit).
---- a buried moon basis would gain radiactive protection from the whole satelite.
---- the moon is really far from earth (few days of travel) increasing very much its cost of exploitation. Moreover between the moon and the earth there is two 'rings' (don't remember their name) that are filled the all the high-energy charged-particles that are captured by the magnetic field of the earth making their crossing dangerous for living being. All the Apollo missions crossed theese rings two times each thus a human being haven't cross them more than few time, but creating a persistent basis on the moon would oblige spacenautes to cross them much more, increasing their chance of developping cancers.
---- it can't be used for 0-gravity studies, thus ISS is still needed. the space observation can be automatised ala Hubble thus doesn't need human being on the moon. the underground can be studied with robots, ala Mars pathfinder, thus doesn't need human being on the moon. the earth observation is made easier from low altitude, thus it would be dumb to do it from the moon. Creating a 'first step' into space travel (with or without life on bord) is a good thing to do on the moon. However it would be more efficient to do it from a space station (ala ISS) that gravits around the Moon in orde to not have to take off twice (from the earth and from the moon). Moreover a 'tanker station' or 'dock station' doesn't need a human being for its maintenence.

So all in all, there is very very few scientific purpuse to build a staion ON the moon, and even less to make it livable.
