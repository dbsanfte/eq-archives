*BZZZZT*, not correct Solinar, you've added the unmitigatable minimum hit into the mitigation calculation. In a situation where the average hit is well less than twice the damage bonus of course mitigation doesn't look very effective, but that situation really isn't representative of real world content. Now, lets look at it properly. Defining:

Mitigation coefficient = (avg - min) / (Max - Min)

C_Mnk = (87.55 - 46) / (157 - 46) = 0.374
C_War = (76.5 - 45) / (156 - 45) = 0.284

So actually, the monk is mitigating:

(C_mnk - C_war)/C_war = 31.7% worse than the warrior....

And this is in the so called "diminishing returns" regime where both characters aren't that far from minimum hits....

Now, this mob has a DI/DB of 111/(20*46) = 0.12. I.E. if you were tanking this uber mob (add a zero on the end of all the damage!) you'd use EVASIVE, not DEFENSIVE :p.

Take a more "normal" PoP type mob with a DI/DB = 0.25 and high enough atk that warrior mitigation is about ~40%, and predict.....

monk mitigation = 1.317*40% = 52.7%

(Yes, this is a guess, but its probably biased towards making the monk look better than he really is... the warrior is further from the distribution tail of diminishing returns now.)

Now, lets compute the ratio of average hits:

War = DB * ( 1 + (0.25*20) * 0.4) = 3* DB
Monk = DB * ( 1 + (0.25*20) * 0.527) = 3.635*DB

So.... on average the monk gets hit 21% harder every slap.

Doesn't look too rosey does it for the poor old monk, and remember, that's a fairly conservative estimate of the difference in mitigation.

Also, we haven't touched the most important aspect... the probability of lethal damage spikes, which is massively greater for the poor monk (and that would still be true even if monks avoided alot better than anyone else, which they don't except for the extra from block!). Or the difference in HP etc...

Anyway, that just shows what we all know, monks (*without a chain of clerics permabuffing them with bulwark of vie and 6 quarm loots/full phase 5 gear!) get slapped a sodding lot harder than any other hybrid or melee.

You take less damage from Saryrn because Saryrn melees for less than Fennin Ro. How much DPS a mob does does not determine what disc you should use. If Quarm hits for 3800-3801 it'd obviously be a good idea to use evasive over defensive. If Quarm hits for 0-6000 then obviously you want to use defensive. Min hits cannot be mitigated, ever, so the bigger the min hit is relative to the max hit, the more useful it is to avoid a hit then mitigate a hit.

For a more practical example, Doomfire Darkfiends hit somewhere around 1000-1300. Now let's say warrior gets hit 50% of the time, and our mitigation is so good that we always get hit for min. So we take 1000 damage 50% of the time, which means we take on average 500 per hit. Now let's say we have a monk, they don't mitigate anything and always take max hit, but they only get hit 40% of the time. On average, they take 520 per hit. As you can see in this example, the monk not mitigating anything at all didn't put them that far behind the warrior that mitigated everything, because the min hit was high enough that the saving from avoiding more hits outweighs mitigation.
