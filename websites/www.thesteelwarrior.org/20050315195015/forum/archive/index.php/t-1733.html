The amount of exp a mob gives is not magic, there is a specific formula that determines the numeric value a given mob will yeild. The simplified version of the formula (before all the exp changes) is -

(Level x level x ZEM) / # of people in group = total exp per person

Where ZEM = Zone experience modifier. The ZEM is a value given to every zone in the game. It can range from 75 for most outdoor zones, all the way up to 119 for most PoP zones and 160 for some n00bie zones.

Since the changes have been made there are a couple of bonuses that also get figured in to the total amount of exp you get. Now there are bonuses you get as the mob level increases relative to your level. The bonus increases as the mob gets higher with a peak at 3+ levels higher than you.

Also, there are the group bonuses to consider. The more people in your group, the larget the bonus. Currently, a full group gives an 80% bonus over baseline, and moreimportantly, the exp per person is figured by dividing total exp by 5 instead of 6. This means that adding a 6th person to your group results in a huge gain due to the bump in bonus while the divisor stays the same.
