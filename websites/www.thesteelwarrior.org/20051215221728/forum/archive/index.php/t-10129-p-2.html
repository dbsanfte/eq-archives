Sooo....back on topic :p

Vikken, I'd say that for the most part WoW is not plagued with any of those problems you mentioned. I am playing a 38 Night Elf Druid and a 22 Human Mage. I find the game insanely fun.

1. Soloing is always do-able and the experience is rewarding since people should always be mixing their soloing with their questing - even if the mobs don't garner a ton of exp, the end result of the quest likely will. I can believe that soloing with a warrior class may be more difficult, but I don't think it would be along the lines of EQ2 solo difficulty (then again, keep in mind that the highest level character I got in EQ2 was lvl 7!). I can tell you that some classes solo with far greater ease, but that has always been the case. My druid solo's beautifully. My boyfriend's paladin is a regular solo champion. I often see hunters and warlocks soloing.

2. Quests are fun, interesting, often rewarding, and often soloable. I know that the quests tend to be the same (gather, kill, explore), but there are often twists put into them that make each interesting to me at least. Personally, I love the explore ones or the ones where you have to click on world objects. I'm not sure why I'm so tickled by that, but I am. :p Being able to solo quests is also great. In a line of related quests, the last ones will probably require a partner or a group, but there is such a plethora of quests from all over the globe that you can complete solo ones any time you can't find a partner. Also, I've noticed that as we gain levels folks in WoW are much keener on grouping up to complete quests (and the system really rewards grouping for quests since everyone can loot a peice on a named mob, for instance). This makes it not painful both because you can easily fill your time with solo quests while waiting for a partner or group to come along and because you can complete the quest with the group relatively quickly.

3. The classes in WoW are unique, which is good, but there is no holy trinity (at least not by level 38). At least three different classes can tank (Warrior, Paladin, and now with the direbear changes, Druid), at least two classes can heal (Priest and Druid, usually also Shaman and Paladin), several classes can CC (Druid, Mage, Priest, Hunter, Warlock, Rogue), and most all classes have some utility that differs from everyone else. That said, I would have personally liked even more variety in class makeup...but what can ya do?

4. Having played beta I'm sure you already know that you can always help other players in this game and they would never lose the reward for the encounter unless you managed to "help" them before they attacked the mob.

5. Travel...ahhh....I'm growing to hate travel in WoW. My Druid can get around relatively fast because she has Cheetah form and she can bind near Stormwind, yet still get across to the other cont. fast since she can Teleport to Moonglade with rides to Auberdine. Running around on my Mage is just painfully slow. At 40 this all changes and I haven't personally had the money problems that others mention. Since around level 30 I've hovered around 35gold, and I'm paying to train up Leatherworking on my druid and tailoring and enchanting (ug) on my Mage. Then again, depending on the class you pick, you'll never have that problem anyway. Warlocks and Paladins get a quest mount at 40, undead Mages also get that mount, Mages can teleport around by 40, Hunters get cheetah form for themselves and others by 40, Druids get cheeta form.


So far WoW is going strong and I don't see the end coming any time soon. I do wonder about the viabilty of high end play. But you know what? I and my friends are having a blast right now, which makes the present monthly fee more than worth it.
