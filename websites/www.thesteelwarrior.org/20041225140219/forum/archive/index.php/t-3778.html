Holy crap....

I cannot believe what I'm seeing here.

ALL that is being asked for is a message indicating success or failure of a skill. There is *no* other skill in EQ that doesn't give a success or failure message, not a single one. If you can know with intimate detail from 300 feet away how much damage your dot did on a given tick, then it's only logical to know whether your taunt was successful or not.

The "mystery of the game" argument is amusing to me because taunt is the ONLY "mystery" in EQ, and there's no justifiation for it. It's MUCH more justified to not have a sneak/hide message indicating success than to not have a taunt fail/success message.

You are in the face of your opponent, you are looking into his or her eyes, you should be able to tell if he or she reacted to your derision/actions/goose/slap/etc. I know that if I get into someone's face in RL I can tell a LOT easier whether I pissed them off with a witty comment than if I were to try to sneak up on them being able to tell if they could see me or not.

Finally, the meat of it. I suspect that, just like Brutul's post showed, taunt works funny... and our propensity to analyze these things would show taunt failure rates as well as show that taunt doesn't necessarily work the way we want to think it does. This is very likely the reason we don't see a taunt success/fail message.
