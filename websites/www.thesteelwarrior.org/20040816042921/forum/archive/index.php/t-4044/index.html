Possibility number one: The paladin can hold aggro better than a warrior. Possibility number two: What brings the most to the group? The paladin brings moderate (nothing fancy but moderate) DPS, aggro lock, group heals, an extra 330 hit points for all, lay hands, spam heals, emergancy heals, root for emergancy crowd control, stun to interrupt casting. The warrior brings about two to six hundred more HPs and some DPS maybe a bit better mitigation. Which would you pick?

Possibility number three: Who can make my job easier? The paladin makes everyone's job easier, saving the cleric on down time heals, saving the cleric from having to heal anyone else, allowing the cleric to use DArb at whim without risk, and makes it so the cleric practically has to heal no one but the paladin. If anyone else gets hit a Touch of Nife and/or a group heal or two should remedy that.

Possibility number four: What speeds up the rate of exp? Certainly not the warrior with his bind wound, decent DPS, and lack of firm aggro lock. In Planes of Power the itemization has thrown the whole reason warriors were superior out of whack with the narrowing of the gap between warrior and paladin AC/HP. In Velious/ Kunark, warriors were "it" because they could take the hits and give them back long enough for the clerics to land their heals. It was very simple...the paladin couldn't. It wasn't mana efficient in raid situations. Now adays the paladin tanks like a warrior, has aggro lock like a paladin, and heals just short of a cleric. Not only does this speed up the rate of pulls, but the paladin's lack of DPS is made up for by the group's DPS increase. The paladin beefs up the abilities of the group to their maximum, the warrior hinders them.
