Merkell, I just want to clarify something for you: Infra/Ultravision and light sources (e.g.: lightstones) do different things.

Infravision makes it so a night-blind race (human, barbarian, erudite) can see in the dark; murkily, but they can see, especially at a distance. Infravision sometimes tints everything a brownish-red in the absence of light. This is what you experienced with the glowing band, and it is what you will get with the Cobalt Helm as well. Don't buy a cobalt helm if you don't like the way you see with the glowing band.

Ultravision is better, but it tends to make everything you see bluish, and it is not a light source. Still, a great improvement over infra and literally like night and day over night blindness. There is a kind of ultravision known as truevision; this is simply ultravision with added perks, like seeing invisible.

Light sources, like greater lightstones, actually illuminate the area around your character with "natural light." They do not illumniate much of anything over distance, so night blind races remain night blind when they try to see past 50 feet or so.

The only way to get the "full effect" is to have both an infra/ultra item for distance, and a light source for close-up natural light conditions. I'm not sure which items that warriors can use also act as light sources, but I know several shields that do (shiny brass shield, charred guardian shield, others). I believe Allakhazam's sometimes lists if an item is a light source.
