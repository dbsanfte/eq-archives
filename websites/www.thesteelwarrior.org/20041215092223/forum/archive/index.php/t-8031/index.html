As expressed above, yes you can do them, but you need to have group members who are better geared than you to succeed with ease. With tank hitpoints on the low end of the spectrum, two things will drain cleric mana quickly

A. Low DPS. If the cleric has to blow 3-4 cheals on you per mob because your group can't whack it down fast enough, its going to be a pain.

B. Poor slowers. If the mob is being slowed when it is at 50% health, your cleric mana is going to be stressed. Personally, I prefer shamans, bst, and then enchanter for slowers in that order. Enchanter takes longer to slow, and often are quicker to go down if they get agro and/or resists (this of course varies with the skill and gear of the chanter).

You can help your group survival rate immensely by using your defensive and furious discs to save your ass during a late cheal/ double pull. Also, snare is extremely effective in Hard ldon. Snare allows you to min-kite the mob if the cheal is late or you get a nasty damage spike. (just keep stepping back out of melee range, don't run around like some noob caster) Snare will also reduce the number of times you blow taunt because the mob has ran out of distance after the slower or the idiot with no agro control in the group.

One last piece of advice, try to group with a secondary healer when first starting ldon. It will help out survival immensely. Also get topped off by your healer in between pulls. You run the risk of dying or getting cleric agro when you start fights at 75% with <8k buffed.

Even with my current gear and AAs, hard ldons are a challenge, and rightfully so. Given full buffs, you should be capable of tanking in hards, but I suspect the first 1-2 might be very challenging. Try the ldons in a consistent group, and you will see that your coordination gets better over time. Some thoughts after going through some hard ldons:

1. Necros are a good addition. With the ability to mindwrack casters and do other fun stuff to undead, they are good to have around.

2. Auto attack OFF. Can't stress this enough, with a dual wield combo ripostes can be a pain, and I actually learnt when to time turning auto attack off in hard ldons. With anger 3 procs, you should be ok for aggro to stop attacking for a while.

3. Endurance issues. Oddly enough, given the rate mobs are pulled in my hard ldons, I actually have enough endurance to use all my discs over time. In easy ldons, my endurance bar gets eaten up fast from just using Incite. So if your endurance is decent, don't be afraid to blow that Defensive or Fortitude and even Mightystrike/Fellstrike discs to help your survival.

Hope you enjoy Hard ldons. They can be a pain, but they can be a lot of fun once you get the hang of things :D

I have a regular weekly group that does LDoN for 4 hours. Assume that all the "regular" group attributes apply (we're good at working together, we know how each other thinks, etc).

Since "ubers" comprise a good part of the group, we're an exception, but this info may help other tanks out there looking to do LDoN.

The cleric has FT15, over 300AA, and is in a post-Time guild
The enchanter has FT15, over 400AA, and has retired from a post-Time guild
The necro is in Magister (top guild on EMarr), and has more hit points than I do . He pulls for us.
We also have a beastlord that's about the same on AA as me (~100) and a druid that's a little behind (~70).

We did normals until I had worked up LR5 and ID3, then we tried our first Hard. This is how we handled the Hards until I finished "growing up".

- treat every pull as a named (but without using discs). Set up good aggro before asking them to assist, make sure the adds are taken care of, keep your finger hovering over that AET hotkey (you DO have AET, right?), use 2h weapons to reduce ripostes (if that's in your weaponry)...etc

- make the puller work singles out. Our enchanter is a badass - he's hands down the best enchanter I've seen in 4+ years of EQ. However, we used that as our "ace in the hole" for when the stuff really hit the fan.

- adjust your buffs to concentrate on hp/ac and hp regeneration. Yeah, I said regen. I added Regen, Druid HoT (lasts about 3 min), Cleric HoT to my buff lineup when planning slots. Since Cleric and Druid HoT's stack, on top of regen and BST Crack, you can get some serious hp's coming back to you, enough that your regen during a fight actually matters.

- bring a tracker. The only REAL, practical advantage to doing Hards is the loot from the named. Sure, you can get "more xp" but since even Hard xp is crap (/rude nerfed zones and the developers who make them), that's not a draw. And the rush of doing hards wears off after 5 adventures of no named at all. /cry

- stick to challenges you KNOW you can win. We will spend 30 minutes getting a Collect 23-27, rather than add to our risk by doing a rescue (got screwed too many times), a slaughter (it's rough killing THAT MANY mobs in a Hard adventure, unless your group has major DPS), or an assassinate (O.o we *might* could handle the boss at the end, but why worry about it?).

Your stats on your Magelo are very similar to where mine were at the time I started. You've got the equipment to survive, if you can find the team to back you up. I do recommend 2 priests, for backup heals - and even then you'll have some rough spots.

Good luck!

Sandro: If agro is really a huge problem, you can have a helpful group use the following techniques:

A) Have the bard/chanter mezz the mob while it is incoming, debuff and slow it, and after slow sticks, taunt the mob until you succeed, then break mezz. This is very slow, but it is very safe, as the only risk is a resisted mezz.

B) Same as A., but have members root and snare the mob during incoming, then have the mob slowed, then engage. This is safer for an enchanter than A. but might involve mini-kiting by the slower. This takes a bit of cooperation from all members of the group, but it is safe, if not ugly.

After you get a successful taunt on slow, you shouldn't be losing agro that much, if at all.
