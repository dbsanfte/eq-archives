Kinda depends on what level you are. There are some permarooted mobs here and there, but obviously they have to be at least light blue to you.
If you are low enough and have a friend who can root, you can find a high-hitpoint, non casting mob (say, a giant) and have at it.
You can also ask to tag along with a group and shoot arrows at their mobs.
As for improving your skill ups through pulling: If you are in your 30s or 40s, go to an outdoor zone like Overthere or Dreadlands. Bring a 150 range bow and 150 range arrows and pull. These zones are, I think, falling slowly out of favor, but an excellent bow puller in these zones can be a tremendous asset to a group.
If you are in your 50s, and you like a challenge, find all your guild clerics who are working on their epics. And all the human smiths in your guild. Offer to pull and tank Chromos for them in skyfire: both of these groups of players can use drops from these mobs (though they can get the drops off other mobs as well). Chromos dispell and part of the terrain in that zone is hideously steep (next to the OT zone) and adds are very hard to avoid unless you pay attention to your business. But: it's interesting pulling, interesting tanking. Very good training for your young casters, too: if they pull aggro, they lose their KEI. Makes an impression on them, heh. Or: leave the smiths at home and take the clerics and pull everything except chromos. Other mobs drop the cleric stuff and it's also very challenging to avoid chromos. the cleric epic is pretty good and clerics become quite fond of tanks who help them with their epic quest.

The reason why this happens is because of two things, distance and aggro list.

As you may/may not have noticed in PoP, when you go too far from a mob, it stop chasing you, even tho you are still on the aggro list, it won't come after you. The problem is, because of the screwiness of aggro, when a mob gates,and/or gets too far from the aggro'd PC(ie: the tank), it still has it aggro'd, however because it is too far away, it cannot run after him/her. So when another person tries to aggro it, its still "aggro'd" to whoever is at the top of the list, so until you become top of the list, it won't come after the puller.
