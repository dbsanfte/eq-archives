Since you only get hit 50% of the time (roughly), 1% more avoided is actually 2% less damage taken (49/50). So, on a 10K HP warrior, 1% damage avoided is the same as having 200 more HP.

The effect is even more significant under evasive if the amount you avoid from avoidance is constant, i.e. if you get hit say 33% under evasive, then (32/33) = 3% less damage taken, or 300 HP on 10K HP warrior. However, since there are mobs with elevated to hit in GoD, avoidance won't work quite as well on them.

Shielding and Avoidance both factor into survivability. It is really not practical to talk about spikes anyway because the only conclusion you can get when worrying about spikes is no warrior should ever be alive. I view both ways as effectively increasing the HP I have, and if I get one rounded, then that's just the way it is. I try to get both, though haven't had much luck with avoidance (probably shouldn't have taken a Deathwrap of Rites... maybe I'll blow some DKP on the near rot Plat Cloak and rot VZ range).

For sake of simplicty, let's say a mob hits 1k-3k, 50% hit rate, on a 10K HP warrior. Then each avoidance = 20 HP (1% avoided = 200 HP = 10 avoidance). Assuming that a warrior never dies in less than one round (because it'd be utterly pointless to tank such a mob if it can kill you in 3 hits), we'll assume you can expect to take 4 hits before getting a heal, then each shielding % = 40 HP. Of course, shielding's effect is lessened when you don't take 4 hits in between heals, due to whatever reason (usually due to the mob missing).
