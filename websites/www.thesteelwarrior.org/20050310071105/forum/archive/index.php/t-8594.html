If you're just starting out as a warrior, I suggest being one of the large races, which allows you to slam (i.e. bash) while dual wielding.

Maybe it's just the people i've seen/grouped with, but too many large race warriors use kick instead of slam. I've found that slam is tremendously useful. It can mean the difference between a particularly annoying caster mob successfully getting off a CH or gate versus an interrupt, courtesy of slam stun.

In the end, however, it's your character, play the game to have fun and to hell with everyone else. Welcome to the boards, as a relative newbie to EQ myself, I've found the information here to be tremendously helpful. Enjoy!

He's right, it's not an issue now as there are a bunch fo cheap and useful lightsource items around.
But it once was a real irritation to my first Barb warrior, lol. Backing down the cave to Blackburrow, getting smacked by gnolls you can't see...grrrrr.

There's a thread somewhere (tavern?) in which I asked about what alts people here have. Responses were all over the place; I concluded that there wasn't much correlation between a person with a warrior mindset and any give alt class. Wizards are fun because you can, as Wandor put it once "get shit dead fast."
If you like tradeskills, make a shaman or an enchanter with the same race/deity as your warrior (hmmm...humans can't be shamans, though). Or, make a completely different race/deity and start in a different cities, because it's kind of fun to learn a city/newbie zone with a new character.
Note that warriors and rogues are not at all very efficient soloists after a while. Wizards probably do a bit better, but you might consider making a class that you can use to solo for those times when you want a change of pace from grouping or need to efficiently farm something.
