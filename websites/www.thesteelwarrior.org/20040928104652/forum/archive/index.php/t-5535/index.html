Originally posted by Firth
I have to ask what you meant by this. I bought a pair of 512 sticks and didn't see dual channel mode in my bios. I tried Turbo Mode, and it just reboots me on start-up. Also, a search on the internet for turbo mode produced no information as to what it does. Any tips?

First we'll start with what Dual Channel mode is. DualDDR is effectively a 128-bit interface with two 64-bit controllers working together. Latency is lowered as well by this method. Think of this scenario: The first controller can be gathering information, while the second controller is publishing it. Basically, both hands are working independently of one another, while still working towards the same end result. Therefore, latency is effectively cut in half.

Second, as long as you have a MB that supports Dual Channel mode, it will be automatically enabled as long as you have 2 or 4 slots filled with ram. Be carefull here though. If you mix and match Ram to fill the slots, this can actually decrease the overall performance.

Third, "Turbo Mode" places agressive timing on the ram, which you're ram probably can't support which is why it crashes out with Turbo mode on. You need higher quality Ram that is up to the task for that. When you shop for ram, look for it's CAS Latency, It will have numbers next to it in the form of something like 3-4-4-8. The lower those numbers are the faster the ram can be accessed which lowers your latency.
