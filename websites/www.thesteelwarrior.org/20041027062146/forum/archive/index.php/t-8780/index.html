Traask
Not fortunate enough to be in closed beta, I was able to secure a spot in the stress test.

Warriors in WoW really do get what they deserve.

We can Dual Wield. (At lvl 20) Use 2H weapons (After 30 skill points - around lvl 8 I think) and we can use 1H+Shield.

We also have 3 "Stances" Battle Stance (Balanced) Defensive, and Beserker.

Each of the 3 styles generate Rage (Tank Mana) for special moves, and Special Moves available are based on the Stance.

Its a viable solo class provided you are willing to invest in materials to reduce sitting and healing, but when we are outnumbered by worthy adversaries, we die.

Each style has a different strategy, and allows the adaptive warrior to hone his/her skills in excelling in close combat.

Blizzard have finally got warriors exactly where they should be, up front in the thick of things, either dealing it out, or taking it - the good warriors adapt to the situation... the bad ones run, or get rezzed.

To all my fellow front line fighters, this one gets a two thumbs up.

In other words, Warrior should never be viable DPS options compare to real DPS classes, because that's not our role. You want to do damage, then you lose your ability to survive. It's that simple.

Even in EQ, I recommend recruiting Warriors for DPS due to their sheer survivability. It is far easier to pick up a random Warrior with decent AAs, give him new weapons, and have his highest HPs out of all classes tough out the AEs (though honestly, Paladins would be even better) as opposed to picking up a Wizard or a Rogue and know that they need a ton of gear before they can even survive the duration of any reasonable AE intensive battle.

We can roughly measure a class's effectiveness at dealing damage as DPS X survivability (live longer, deal more damage). Problem is that in all nontrivial (i.e. stuff you expect people to die) situaiton, Warrior's survivability actually allows them to be top damage classes. When you consider how high the Warrior's survivability is, a Warrior shouldn't even have 1/2 the DPS of a DPS class because otherwise a Warrior would end up doing more total damage.
