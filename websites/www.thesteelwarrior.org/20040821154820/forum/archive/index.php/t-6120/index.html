exeter
We're here because we love warriors - despite the fact they are tough to level and incurr some humiliating comparisons to other classes when it comes to our core competency in tanking.

But in talking about our plight - we might consider discussing the cleric's plight in the same breath.

Clerics used to be the best healers in the game bar none. In addition to being the unquestioned healers - they had qualitatively different abilities such as rez (Knights was poor by comparison) and soul binding (before NPC's served this role).

Of course they lost of all this: shamans and druids have comparable healing once they reach the 50's; NPC soulbinders; and other classes that rez to a comparable % at the high end of the game (Pally's, Necros). Clerics have lost out to hybrid classes the same way that tanks have - probably worse.

To compound matters, while the sheer ability of the cleric to heal in a single shot may still be superior to other classes - his partner - the warrior - does not display a clear hit point advantage beyond hybrid classes to really illustrate this fact (correct me - but warrior hp's about 5-10% higher than Knights or SKs at high end of the game).

The height of humiliation is that with the addition of new zones (PoP) the cleric - warrior alliance no longer works. They can no longer duo. No slow. No kiting.

If we want to talk about "stand-down" and other expressions of dissatisfaction - we might do so by joining forces with what used to be our perfect class fit - the cleric.

We might go so far has to say pure classes are dying in EQ.

Depends where you are in the game, Gaylon. I don't mean to be insulting when I say that I don't think progressing through the GoD trials yet, are you?

If you're chugging through PoV and BoT and maybe trying your first few LDoN Hard adventures, then I don't doubt that to you, cleric looks like a pretty powerful class within their specialized role.

But in GoD, there comes a point where a cleric can't keep her group healed. You reach a moment where you can't progress without two healers - and many people are doing GoD trials not just with two healers, but with two clerics because of the insane healing output requirements.

Why should a single cleric be sufficient healer for an equivalently-geared group? Because clerics give up almost every other possible advantage in order to be able to do that. A cleric can only do one thing well: Protect, nurture, heal, and when necessary rez the group on which she's totally dependent. If you ever ask a cleric to interact with the actual game environment - tank something, kill something, move from place to place, whatever - you'll see how weak and helpless the class is outside that specialist role.

The situation's analagous to warriors. Warrior is the class which gives up massive amounts of utility and flexibility in order to be able to do one thing really well: Tank the big hitters.

Look for a moment at the disadvantages of being a cleric. It's the only class which has the drawbacks of both casters and melee... unlike a caster, a cleric lacks the basic utility (invis, see invis, eb, levitate, etc.) to move from place to place without help - but she still gets the defensive skills and hp of a caster rather than a melee. My 65 cleric's got 1300hp naked and a dodge cap of 75.

A question you could ask, both of clerics and of warriors, is: Why on earth would anyone want to play a class which is totally dependent on others in order to do anything meaningful, and is valueless compared to other classes anyway unless you're fighting the really big hitters?

The only reason you'd play a class which is that limited, is to be clearly the best option (and in places, the only option) for the job.

But a single cleric can't DO that job any more.

Now, I know I'm talking to an audience of warriors. Raiding warriors - which includes most of the warriors who're progressing through GoD - tend to be accustomed to having a vast harem of clerics trailing round after them when they're tanking. Most of these warriors don't see anything unusual about having four, five, or six clerics who're doing nothing else apart from keeping them, personally, standing up. So they don't see a two-healer group in quite the same light that clerics do.

But the fact is that when you have six group slots - and one of those slots has to be a warrior, and another has to be slower/CC with a strong preference for chanter, and two of the others have to be healers - then it really sucks trying to get a large number of people through a large number of trials. And the bottleneck for guilds is always either warriors or clerics - but the difference is that one warrior is enough. One cleric is not.

I'm afraid that the idea of single-group mobs that are REQUIRED for progression but also need more healing than one Time-geared cleric can put out is simply retarded.

There are two possible answers. The first is to relieve the pressure on the clerics by making druids and shamans a better alternative as healers - and that entails making some fairly large upgrades to clerics so they can do things which aren't healing, hp buffing, or rezzing - and the second is to up the cleric's healing output so that one cleric is enough to do the job.

But in GoD - the CURRENT expansion - it's certainly true to say that all the healing classes are underpowered when compared to the mobs.
