People think "One Rounded" but its the most flawed & stupid concept ever.
Its almost impossible for most raid boses to kill you with 4 max hits in a row.
What gets you killed is a bad series of 8-12 swings, which is 3 or 4 rounds.
(Thats assuming healers didnt do anything wrong)

As for AC vs HP dilema here is what i haved used for a long time : "Fake HP conversion"
Lets say you buff up to 2000ac 10000hp.
And assuming the linearity of AC gains, 100ac = 2.5% DPS reduction.
Going to 2100ac , is like gaining 2.5% x 10000 = 250hp.

That essentially means, that every HP you have becomes more valuable as you increase AC.
Because since you will get hit for less, you wont need as many hp to survive.

Get a 0ac 50000hp warrior to tank Jihru 1.0 with 2 clerics.
He will die, because he's going to be "Over DPSed"

Get a 3000ac 8000hp warrior to tank Jihru 1.0 with 2 clerics.
He will live, because he wont be "Over DPSed" any more.

Of course these are extreme examples, but they are easy to understand.
Essentially you need both. I'm an AC & Shielding freak.

Why ?
Because if i take 1 less point of dmg, thats 1 less point of dmg that needs to be healed.
And 1 less hp i will be missing at the start of the next round.
Of course i need HP, because when i run out .. i'm dead

In many situations, there is no AC vs. hps tradeoff: an upgrade will upgrade both stats.
However, I tend to be more active looking for AC than hitpoints. Most non-tanking classes in the game have an exaggerated view of the role of hitpoints (that is, hitpoint total on a tank is by far the most frequently thought-about measure of a tank, often necessarily at the expense of properly weighting AC and AAs). But I've noticed that some very very bad things can happen if your AC is simply too low to mitigate a mob's attack. Basically, lots of the people you play with will think you need more hitpoints--and that's great, because you do need more hitpoints and you need help to get them. But the AC you have to advocate for yourself (or, you do more often).
In terms of upgrading efficiently, it's a big help to know what potential upgrades are out there, the relative difficulty of getting them, and the relative strength/weakness of the specific pieces of armor you're wearing.

Well the tradeoff is quite simple really. With evasive you still can get hit 4 or even 8 times in a row with a non-negligible probability, and those you get hit for whatever you get hit for. With defensive you will be hit that many times in a row more often but each hit will be reduced by the 50% DI calculation. Anything more than 8 straight hits (assuming no flurry) should be considered a serious problem with healing and issue of healing can overcome any amount of mitigation anyway.

If you're not worried about dying to spikes Evasive appears to be better (at least based on old parses), but taking less DPS overall doesn't help much if you die earlier than the guy taking more DPS since healing mana is usually not a significant issue. To be fair, most mobs you don't get to refresh either discs so you might as well go with Defensive always... but I like to have my disc up quicker just in case it's needed for something.

And Anueks that hit for 1000-1050 in Txevu are obviously meant to punish defensive. It's no different than the old Kyv named that miss 1% while you're using Evasive. For the Anueks you might as well put a monk to tank them since the best you can ever mitigate is a saving of ~200 HP per round.
