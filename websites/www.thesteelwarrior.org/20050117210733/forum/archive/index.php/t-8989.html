Well, in White's defense, he was probably touching on dps because I brought it up. I brought it up not in terms of pally vs. warrior, but in terms of switching a bst for a pally. I think the importance of melee dps in some 50s groups is under appreciated, and I've noticed that in experience grinds where the composition of the pick up group changes over time and goes from high melee dps to low dps the fights take longer, which means the mobs do more damage, the adds are up longer, mana falls faster, etc. It's not like there's a magic point at which this happens, but you can get situations where the group members change and (in this case) the mobs get harder and suddenly you have to adjust fast or die. In a pick up group, that often means you're dead.
In that narrow sense, a group's melee dps has an effect on the tank/tanking. Any given tank will look better in a high dps group assuming the tank can hold aggro.
Nokatsu
09-23-2004, 05:05 PM
Unmanaged adds are far more of a problem in hard LDoNs than they are in normals. This problem gets worse the higher the level you are. Successful groups for hard LDoNs require limited (ideally no) adds on the pull and a good plan for managing the adds (Enc or Bard mez, root park, whatever).

Tanking 2-3 normal mobs is far different from trying to tank 2-3 hard LDoN mobs.

That said, there are some weak paladin tanks. Playing my berserker alt I had one adventure where no matter how hard I tried not to I was stealing aggro from our paladin MT. The paladin was using a 1HS/shield setup, and didn't appear to be using much in the way of aggro spells or taunt. Even using Diversive Strike my DPS was enough to take aggro.

Communication before and during LDoN adventures is important, especially for pickup groups of players with different skill levels. Avoiding problems in the first place is great, but correcting problems so there are not repeated is necessary. If you see a problem, try to propose a solution while making an effort not to alienate your teammate.
