A rough estimate of dps comparing to an ornate greatblade (weapon that I use often still) can give something like this :

A 35/31 14 bonus dmg 2-hander would be exactly 74% of a 35/23. A 34/31 13 bonus dmg will be lower, let's say 70%. You have to remove punishing blade. Let's say it's something like 65%.

Depending on buffs, with an ornate greatblade I see myself doing between 110dps up to 130 dps. That usually exclude avatar and it's around 175 vengeance.

65% of this is between 71.5 to 84.5 dps. This generally fit with my excel damage calculator. Proc will amount for at best another 3 dps (2 proc per minute), probably less.

Is between 70 dps and 85 dps good for a 1-hander in this case scenario? It's pretty good, probably just below the monk xegony 1-hander ratio not counting proc at 20/19.

In this case to get the ornate greatblade dmg, you need an off-hander able to put out 38.5 to 45.5 dps or so. The only off-hander I see able to do this would be itself as the best off-hander theorically, but it cannot go in that hand. I doubt even the xeg monk 1-hander can go that high in my example.

So to me just looking at the theory, I don't see how this weapon can do greater dps then an ornate greatblade (assuming an also good off-hander that war can use). Ornate greatblade is less dps then the really high end 2-hander like bow/stone etched/the 60/38 from time, etc. Even if you give this calculation a very high margin of error (which is unlikely), this weapon will never do 10% higher then a Bow. But usual disclaimer, I could be mistaken and a few good parses would be nice!

Not saying it's not good, but that's dps only.
