If you can wear Fierce Heraldic, do so. Every slot. Visible armor (head, arms, wrists, legs, boots, hands, chest), invisible armor (waist, back, neck, face, shoulders). At about 5k per slot if you shop around, you're looking at 65k for armor.

Then buy a CHoS, a SotB, and a GSoT. Now you're looking at, perhaps, 20k more. 85k total.

That gives you your one-handers aggro set, and your two-hander aggro set.

You are now approximately as well-equipped, modulo a worn haste item and your jewelry, as anyone outside of a raiding guild can expect to be. Go enjoy a game that just got way, way too easy for you. :-)

Oh, and the remaining 15k of your 100k can buy you two Valorium rings, so all you really need to fill in is haste and earrings. Use tribute haste instead of a haste item, and ignore your ears.

I'm not aware of any right-click haste bracers for warriors, so you may already have a suitable worn haste item (all of the wrist-slot worn haste I'm aware of is high-end, 41%ish haste).

The GSoT (Greatstaff of Thunder) is the best underpriced weapon in existence. :-) 55+ only, 31/30 with a stun/dd proc that pisses mobs off quite nicely, and drops commonly enough that even after the Tribute system was put in, they're still going for 1k or thereabouts last I looked.

Check your bracer. If it is worn haste, you're golden.

If not, I'd still say wear the FH girdle, buy a bunch of low price, high-Tribute-value items in the bazaar, and run off Tribute haste until you get a better haste item. *shrug*

Also remember that 31% haste is enough to cap you with enchanter spell haste, so unless you plan to do a lot of soloing (in which case, you want to buy Ornate gloves for the clicky-haste when you can afford them, which you can't yet), you really don't need a 36% or 41% haste item.
