Battleblade
No, Knights shouldn't get the Incite line.

There's a fundamental debate that's been going on in EQ for almost a year that covers most class changes between Knights and Warriors. Put very simply, there are two competing views:

1. Knights are Tanks. Versus all content except raid boss mobs (and Knight should be sufficient to tank these in a pinch as well) Knights and Warriors should be interchangable. IMO, this focuses the comparason of Knights and Warriors on a single aspect of the Knight classes, Tanking. Spellcasting that does not directly affect combat while it is happening would be free. The ability to cast on others is free. SK FD pulling, and much of the Paladin spellbook are bonuses.

The most generous Knights propose Warriors get utility to offset the value of spellbooks beyond that which allows Knights to tank like Warriors. IMO this would trade the reason I chose to play a Warrior, unmatched survivability when tanking the most dangerous creatures in Norrath and being the Primary Tank, for a psuedo-spellbook vastly inferior to for example the Paladin spellbook. I eschewed the spellbook and being a melee/caster hybrid to be a 100% Tank and best at Tanking.

Less generous Knights want an avoidance/mitigation/auto reposte discipline and to tank every mob outside of raid boss mobs just like a Warrior. If Warriors want Tanking superiority, let the Warriors that raid have it on raid day and when tanking a raid boss mob only. If they wanted a 90% res, heals, GRC, Root, etc they should have rolled a Knight.

*** There is an alternate view ***

2. Warriors and Knights are different classes that should be fun to play and have viable roles in every environment with distinct abilities that justify their existance as seperate and valued classes. Traditional roles should be preserved and it is acknowledged that classes are valued for the sum total of their capabilities.

No Incite for Knights. Incite is lovely for tanking more challanging mobs. It's not so good when there are adds leaking into camp 2 at a time and you must quickly change targets.

IMO Knights should have a rapid-fire aggro system that allows them to promptly switch mobs and to pull them off a beleagered Enchanter. Don't say AETaunt please - it's absolutely useless when there are 3+ adds in camp. The refresh of Incite and especially AETaunt insures Knights have superiority in rapid-fire aggro.

IMO, Paladin aggro generation meshes well with a raid CC/Combat Medic/Add Tank role and is generally fine. Should a Paladin wish to build aggro more effectively and not be as dependant on stuns and a consequent loss of DPS, then they can start looking at weapons with an aggro proc and YKesha augs. The rarity of Knights in general trying to acquire aggro weapons suggests that they are not nearly as dependant on them as Warriors. The only improvements (and they aren't afaik needed) that should be concidered for Paladins is the ability to use the LDoN Anger augs and a 0.0 easily resisted stun component for the Divine Might weapon buff line.

SK aggro generation could use some work. IMO and consistant with the SK Class Description they should be able to cripple mobs with debuffs (there's an aggro tool) and terrorize it (there's another). Ideally, they should be able to direct the aggro they generate to another PC with a short duration buff (they should be the masters of aggro manipulation and aren't) freeing up their ability to do reasonably high DPS in a variety of ways.

Cloning class abilites like Incite blurs class differences. It is anathma to creating classes with "distinct abilities that justify their existance as seperate and valued classes."
