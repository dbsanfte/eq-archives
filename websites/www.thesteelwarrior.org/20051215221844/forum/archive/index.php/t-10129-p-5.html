There are about 1 million characters, not accounts.

Consider even FF11 has 2.5 characters on average in Japan and it costs $1 for each character beyond the first one, it's not exactly a stretch to assume the average player has at least 2 characters. Now obviously wowcenseus isn't able to capture every player in WoW but 500K subscribers seems like a reasonable guess based on the # of observed players.

From what I know WoWcensus simply does a ton of /who (since /who is limited at 49) to cover the entire server (e.g. /w 1 troll shaman and so on) whenever someone runs it and then updates the people it pulls to the database, adding any new person it sees. This won't get everyone on the server, obviously, but there's no reason to believe that the alliance just happens to be all on whenever anyone runs WoWcensus so the alliance to horde ratio should be pretty accurate.
