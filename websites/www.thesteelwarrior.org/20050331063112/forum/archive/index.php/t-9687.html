What is the death penalty?

Well, the original death penalty was basically nothing. You could run back to where you died from the nearest graveyard and you can self rez yourself for no real loss other than the time it took to run from the graveyard. Then there's the spirit rez where you ask the NPC at the graveyard to rez you which previously would cause an exp hit but it wasn't too big of a deal if for some reason you really needed a spirit rez. However these were the two original penalties now it's different.

Now on deaths you take a 10% durability hit on everything you're wearing and that's in your current bags. The spirit rez has changed from an exp hit to a 100% durability hit and I think some sort of rez sickness(I hardly ever use the spirit rez so I'm not sure, usually isn't necessary though) Take into account when you do corpse runs you're in ghost form which means you can't see npcs and nothing attacks you. Once you're near your corpse you'll be able to see where npc's are just so you can find a decent space to rez in without being attacked right away if possible.

I've been in the beta since May and I am having a blast. Some notes from my experiences -

1. You can group or solo at any level with any class. After 2.5 years of logging on to EQ and knowing that I could literally do nothing until I had a group, being able to log on, see that no one in guild or friends list was on, and then go do something useful was AWESOME. Really, I grouped when I wanted and had fun or went solo and had fun.

2. There is a definite change to the game as you level. Quests get a bit harder and often are more time-consuming. Early on you have a lot of quests packed into small zones. Later you spend more time travelling. Not better or worse, just different.

3. Exploring is fun. Exploration xp, hidden quest givers, trainers, and merchants.

4. Instances are fun. Your first instance opportunities come early (mid-teens for Horde, about 20 Alliance) and are much different from non-instanced gameplay. There are a number of dumbasses who seem to think that you need perfect groups for instances. You don't. If you don't have a warrior, priest, mage you can still do instances. It may be a bit harder and you might have to play smarter but it works.

5. Tradeskills are useful. This will be a big shock to EQ players (it was to me). You can have 2 Professions/Tradeskills (which I dislike but hey, nothing is perfect) and they are all generally useful. Mining/Smithing, Mining/Engineering, Herbalism/Alchemy, Enchanting/'whatever you want' are all very useful for warriors. IMPORTANT - Cooking, Fishing, and First Aid are NOT Professions so you can get those and still have 2 Professions. This confuses people who are new to the game.

6. Talents. You have a lot of customization through talents in WoW. Some classes are almost like 3 different classes, depending on talent builds. I actually think that warriors have one of the weaker talent builds, not in terms of power, but in terms of how the class customizes depending on build. There is not a huge difference between an Arms build and a Fury build although both are different from a Protection build.

7. Politics. One of the things that I love about WoW is that it does really cut down on guild politics and interclass bickering. Most of us remember the huge warrior vs paladin flamewars on these boards. That just is not an issue in WoW. For a couple of reasons. One, everyone can solo so there is not a pressure to fill a limited slot in a group. Two, any class stacks in a group. Have 2 warriors in a group? Fine, one can MT while the other offtanks and snares. Have a warrior and a paladin? Great, have the warrior MT while the paladin offtanks and heals while your priest or druid nukes or does melee. Or have the paladin MT while the warrior offtanks and snares. It all works. You could have a group with 4 warriors and a paladin if you wanted, sure it would be weak at some things but it could work pretty well.


Common complaints -
1. I can't use all of my skills when I'm grouped!!! Boo-friggin'-hoo. My 51 warrior had 3 stances with 12-key hotkey bars that were totally packed, plus sidebars with more skills. I couldn't use all of my skills on one mob soloing. Get used to not using everything at your disposal, you just don't have to and later on, you just can't. Given the choice, I'd rather not be a 2-button chump ever again.

2. You are always doing stuff so you can't chat.!!! Never had that problem. It was harder to chat in instances but for the most part, especially while soloing, you can go at your own pace and chat as much or as little as you want.

3. What can you do at higher levels??? It is hard to get bored in WoW. Between quests, instances, raids, hero classes (when that gets implemented) there is a lot to do. Throw in homeland defense or raids on the opposing faction and there is always something to do.

4. My warrior can't beat a paladin in a duel!!! Too bad. Paladins are tough as hell to kill, especially for a melee class. Then again, warriors will rip through other classes. PvP relies a lot on player skill but there is a certain rock-paper-scissors aspect to it as well. Deal with it.
