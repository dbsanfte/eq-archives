Sounds like you have been playing UO since you mention "template". There are no templates in EQ - All warriors are more or less alike and people expect you to act in a certain way in groups. Skills go up with use, so if you hit a monster with a sword, your 1 handed slash goes up togeather with your offence. The skill cap for your combat skills is: 5 + 5 x level, so at level 5, it would be 30. Your 1 handed slash is at 30, so I guess you have more or less been using your newbie weapon all the time.
Try find a mage and convince him to summon some bandages, since they will be your friend in the early levels (they're used togeather with bind wound skill).
Save your practice points for now, until you get more insight into skills. They can always get handy if you neglect a skill like 2 handed slash or blunt or you want a kick start in duel wield or double attack. If you're interested in trade skills, spending 20 prac points initialy is recommedable too.

I suggest you familiarize yourself with www.eqatlas.com and www.allakhazam.com. EQAtlas has maps for all the zones that you will spend time in, for the foreseeable future. Allakhazam is a treasure trove of information about gear, quests and the beasties that inhabit the different zones.

Train Sense Heading. Remap it to your left or right movement key. Sense Heading is your friend. (Thus speaketh the Perpetually Lost Ogress). Train one point in Swim. If you pass a body of water during your travels, jump in and swim. Train taunt and mash it when you are soloing. This is not how to play in groups, however.

The easiest (safest) way for a level 5 Barbarian Warrior to get to the Bazaar is:

1. Find the PoK book in Everfrost (I think it is on a EQAtlas map). Click on it.

2. You are now in Plane of Knowledge. A lot of people hang out in PoK while LFG (looking for group), or to do banking, or to get buffs. The PoK books have made travel in EQ very easy. EQAtlas also has a map of this zone, so you can get familiar with where the different books are located, and where the different vendors are located.

3. Find the Nexus book in PoK. There will usually be a ton of people clustered in front of it. Click on it.

4. You are now in the Nexus. Take the corridor to the south (turn right) and you will enter the Bazaar.

If you wish to look for equipment while in the Bazaar, type /bazaar. You will get a window to pop up where you can either type in the name of the equipment you are looking for, or type in the race/class/equipment slot/stats and it will filter the appropriate equipment for you. When you find something that you want to buy, highlight the name of the trader and click the "find trader" button on the bottom of the window. A "mystical path" will take you to the trader, and you can purchase your item, just as you would a NPC vendor.

If you wish to sell items in the Bazaar, you need to purchase a trader satchel (less than 2 platinum) and then stand in one of the trader stalls. Type /trader. A window will appear in which you can set prices on any goods inside a trader satchel, and then add them to the list of goods you wish to sell. Now hit the "begin trader" button. You can go AFK, and your trader character will sell your stuff for you.

I sincerely reccomend that you do NOT vendor sell any low/medium/high quality animal pelts, or any spiderling or spider silks. People use tons of these items for trade skills, and will usually buy them in the Bazaar, rather than going back to the "newbie" grounds to kill the critters themselves.

I also reccomend that when you outgrow the twisty canyons of Everfrost, you venture down to Blackburrow. This will be your first real dungeon. Know it, like it, and save the gnoll fangs.

"and save the gnoll fangs."

There is a captain of the Qeynos guard in, heh, South Qeynos (a town south of Everfrost, south of Blackburrow, south of Qeynos Hills).

This captain wants the gnoll fangs.

Give him them one at a time (do not stack them).

He will happily reward you with faction, a very good amount of XP if you are level appropriate to be killing the gnolls, and an orb of something or other (forget the name, moonstone crystal maybe?).

Take the orbs to Fish's Tavern, also in South Qeynos. Go to the back. Find the bartender in the back of the place (forget the name, you can find both of these quests at Allakhazam's). He will reward you with more faction, more XP (but not much more) and some ale for giving him the orbs.

Sell the ale, get some money for foods.

Or drink the ale and be manly.

Have fun. That's how I spent my early levels.
