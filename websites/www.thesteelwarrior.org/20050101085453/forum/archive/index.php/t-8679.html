Read the Class Descriptions (http://eqlive.station.sony.com/library/classes.jsp).

The Warrior Class Description is pretty much word for word exactly what appears in the game. We take a getting hit during battles better than any other class, but we are extremely group dependent and can do little else except very average melee DPS. Think of the Rocky movie where he's getting beaten to a pulp, sticks out his chin, and says "That's not so bad. Is that all you've got?"

Unfortunately the SK Class Description begins getting less accurate after level 55. They are supposed to take away a mob's will to fight,and their ability to do so effectively. They are supposed to fill them with fear. They are supposed to take advantage of this by doing some serious damage to their hapless opponent. Like I said, Verant and SoE later did not to my mind make the SK Class Description a reality much past 55 and for no good reason what-so-ever.

However SK's are evolving into good pullers, are less group dependent than Warriors, and like Warriors do average melee damage. Somewhat distressingly, a signature piece of equipment, Circlet of Shadow, which is extremely useful when pulling no longer drops in game and is overly expensive to acquire.

SK's can tank, but are not as effective versus the hardest hitting mobs. My opinion is that they are extremely group friendly - they can bring one mob at a time to the group and are very adequate tanks, but they lack a real class defining ability or set of abilities. SoE would be improving the game by reading the SK Class Description and making it so.

Read the boards. There's a lot of information available.

Some people really like warriors; some think warriors are boring. I'm never bored playing my warrior (well, uh, not while I'm actually fighting anyway).
I'm also never bored playing my SK alt, and in some ways he's more fun, though I'm not sure whether that's a function of the class or that when I'm playing alts I'm usually just fiddling around in a less-tense environment than when I'm tanking with my main.
The life of a warrior involves certain hassles: the combination of being a strikingly poor solo fighter and being heavily gear dependant means that starting in your 20s (if you don't run a trader) and in your 40s or 50s gear expenses are pretty high if your warrior is your main and you're trying to both keep aggro and tank for groups of players who, in many cases, will be well geared. I think lots of warriors stall out in their 50s, and that's why (though I have no real data to back that up).
But: all groups need a tank, and warriors are the best tanks for the nastiest mobs.

SKs have some advantages: you can solo better than warriors (though not nearly as well as some caster classes) and pre-50s SKs have some really nice little tricks up their sleeves. Warriors really do lack any sort of utility, whereas in the early phases of the game, SKs have some.
Unfortunately for SKs, Battleblade's assessment of their 50+ situation is correct. Their relative utility declines and, as mobs hit harder and harder, SKs lose traction as tanks relative to warriors and paladins. The tools that SKs have for reducing the amount of heavy damage they take either don't work at all against higher mobs, or don't work nearly as well as warriors' discs and pallies' stuns. At the same time, SK damage-production and utility to a group or raid that is facing hard-hitting mobs are not especially impressive. So, life begins to get frustrating for SKs on a level that a decently geared warrior does not currently face.

But, you know, you're just going to have to check them out yourself, as previous posters have mentioned.
I wouldn't pick either of those classes unless you are fairly certain that you want to play a lot in groups and are willing to cultivate friends, as both classes are built with group fighting in mind.
