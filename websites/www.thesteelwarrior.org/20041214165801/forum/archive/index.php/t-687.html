I'm having my doubts.

You learn allot of interesting things if you sit down and test them.

For instance, if you taunt to pull a non-kos mob it gives you less aggro than when you pull it using an attack. This makes sense mechanic-wise because taunt simply puts you on top of the hate list + a small amount, whereas if you get the initial contact against a mob it generates additional hate.

You also get to learn nifty things such as how mob hp relates to spell aggro, the less hp a mob has the less aggro they get from spells.

As a bonus you may get to see a druid get beat up by lower level mob as you sit around for 5 minuets disarming said mob. :D

Some tests that need to be run is take a few mobs of similar/same hp totals in different zones and use those +hate spells Vs the high aggro spells such as snare, stun, tash, ect.

In the belief that both beg and disarm add some aggro my taunt hotkey is set to:

/attack
/do 3
/attack
/do 4
/do 5

On my Abilities Page (not combat, abilities) 3 is beg, 4 is disarm, 5 is taunt.

Why do this?

Well if I am not top of the aggro list then the instant I am about to hit taunt I want to get as close to the top as I can so taunt has the greatest chance of working. Therefore I always bash/kick then hit this key which begs and disarms before taunting. I can only provide anecdotal evidence to support any belief that it works but I do honestly believe I am getting aggro from mobs that are not on me easier than I was by just taunting.


The only thing to remember is that if you have a scripted defensive or evasive button (like one that counts down the time left on the disc) then while it is running you cannot use another scripted hotkey, however you CAN use a scripted social. So keep your social panel handy at all times!

Does disarm add hate?

The answer is yes. It most certainly does. This is easily seen by walking up to an indifferent mob and hitting your disarm button... armed or not, it will attack you no matter what.

Taunt to pull mobs..... this is a good way actually to test taunt effectiveness if you have some time, taunt an indifferent mob.... when it turns on you, it's because it was successful.
