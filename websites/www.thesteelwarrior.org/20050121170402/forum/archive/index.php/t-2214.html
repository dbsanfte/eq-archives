It seems like with PoP there has been a lot more guild jumping than of recently. Anyone can go into BoT and farm decent gear and level up 200 AA's to be top picks in the local top guild's recruiting station. Gear is simply the largest limiting factor (As it always has been) and people will jump ship regardless just to get it.

Now, successful guilds most likely have the following in common:

1) They constantly provide a challenge to their members by outlining and following a long term set goal.

2) Run by a guild leader who is fair, level headed, and supremely dedicated.

3) Consists of members with similiar dedication to the game and devotion to the guild that they're in.

I guess you could probably call it the three D's - Direction, Dedication, and Discipline. A guild must have all three of these traits in order to become successful. Vaz, it sounds like your guild fell apart because people lacked Dedication.
