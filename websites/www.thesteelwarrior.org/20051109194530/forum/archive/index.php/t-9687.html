So far I'm having a lot of fun with the game.

One concern I have is with grouping. The players do so much DPS and the mobs have such low HP's that standard EQ grouping strategies don't really seem to apply. Everyone tries to go all-out DPS, even the healers. Because of this, the few times that I've grouped to finish some quest it's been a complete cluster fuck. I expect this will improve as the game progresses and people learn their roles.

One of the things I really like about this game is the quest system ... I can literally spend 100% of my time working on fun and interesting quests that actually advance my character. At any one time you can have 20 quests added to your quest log, and there seems to be a decent level of overlap, i.e. you can work on a coupleof quests at a time.

But ... because the game is so quest-centric, there really isn't much grouping that happens. Unless you have a regular group of friends you play with, expect groups to stick together for 30 minutes or less. As soon as someone accomplishes their quest objectives they leave. This is different in an instanced dungeon of course, and then you can expect to be grouped with the same folks for a couple of hours.

The devs recently introduced spell reagents, item durability, and a new death system that reduces item durability upon dying. So far people really seem to hate it, myself included. It will be interesting to see if it sticks around for very long.

What is the death penalty?

Well, the original death penalty was basically nothing. You could run back to where you died from the nearest graveyard and you can self rez yourself for no real loss other than the time it took to run from the graveyard. Then there's the spirit rez where you ask the NPC at the graveyard to rez you which previously would cause an exp hit but it wasn't too big of a deal if for some reason you really needed a spirit rez. However these were the two original penalties now it's different.

Now on deaths you take a 10% durability hit on everything you're wearing and that's in your current bags. The spirit rez has changed from an exp hit to a 100% durability hit and I think some sort of rez sickness(I hardly ever use the spirit rez so I'm not sure, usually isn't necessary though) Take into account when you do corpse runs you're in ghost form which means you can't see npcs and nothing attacks you. Once you're near your corpse you'll be able to see where npc's are just so you can find a decent space to rez in without being attacked right away if possible.

Overall, I enjoy the game a lot personally just there are still significant changes being done when it's supposed to be coming out in two weeks. Paladins essentially got their whole playstyle changed around with the latest patch. Take advantage of the open beta if you can get in(they're not giving out keys anymore atm but maybe later)
