Try equipping a non-weapon (ie: infinite delay) in main hand, and if offhand swings are tied to mainhand delay, you shouldn't swing offhand at all.

In short, for offhand weapons the delay and damage are irrelevent on their own, and pure ratio is what's used to compare

Because RATIO and DAMAGE are two different things

Saardan is wrongly saying having small delay weapon in offhand does not help dps. get big damage weapon The 'damage' part of the 'damage and delay' on a weapon, on it's own, is irrelevent.

50damage 150delay for example, whilst having a huge 'damage' componant, only has a 1/3 ratio.

10damage 20delay for example has a much lower 'damage' componant, but with it's higher 1/2 ratio, it's a much superior offhander.

Saardan's posting suggests that the 50 damage weapon would be superior offhand due to it's higher 'damage' componant, but is false due to him not understanding that dual wielding is completely dependant of offhand delay, and has nothing to do with primary hand delay. A 3dam 6delay weapon offhand would be almost identical offhand dps to a 50dam 100delay offhander.

In short, the best weapon for your offhand is the one with the highest ratio: damage divided by delay.
