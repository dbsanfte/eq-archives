Haass
This "counter" aggro theory is interesting

It's not really a theory, it's a really well established fact. It's why shamans have such a hard time doing damage....because of all the DR/PR counters on their most efficient spells.

Basically a 'counter' is this...it's a little placeholder on your character that represents the strength of a certain spell. For example... Trakanon's AE adds 9 poison counters. Now take a look at the spell "Cure poison." Cure poison removes 1 poison counter per cast, so it would take 9 casts of Cure poison to remove Trakanon's AE. By comparison, Counteract Poison removes 8 poison counters, so that would take 2 casts to cure that same spell (8 from the first cast, and then you still have 1 counter left, so you need to cast again).

For the most part, the higher taunting DoT's and effects have counters added to them...Poison or Disease. Boil Blood line has to be annuled, and leaves no counters on the mob, so it's fairly low aggro. By comparison, Bane of Nife is 10 poison counters...it generates quite a bit of aggro. Even the disease DoT's like Scourge and Pox generate a fair chunk of aggro, even tho their damage is less than the poison lines, just because of the disease counters which they place on mobs.

So, for effects, if it has a poison or disease counter, chances are, it's really bitching aggro. If it doesn't, then you might not have as good of luck.

Haass
Thanks for that info...I'm still a little confused though. If my sword adds 9 poison counters to a mob, does it only affect the dot from the sword? Or does it also lower poison resist for other casters? Or are spells which lower resists and spells which add counters totally different?

They are completely different concepts, but they both can be in a spell.

Spells that add counters just mean they can be cured by using the appropriate cure. Spells that lower resists will say so in the spell description. That doesn't mean that they are mutually exclusive however.....the ______ Decay line for shamans lowers DR, and adds Disease counters. That means that everyone has a better chance of landing spells on a being with that debuff on, but if you wanted to get rid of it, a cure disease would work.
