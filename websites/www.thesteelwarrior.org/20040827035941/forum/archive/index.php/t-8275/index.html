Guess I will ask the obvious question. How many machines? That matters more than most other things. And how are they equipped?

More machines = easier

On one machine you would be a fool to do anything other than something like warrior/cleric or druid/slower and you likely wont be able to do GoD (the lag for me is horrible on my 3 box in GoD when the zone is empty except for my total 6box) and this is the best machine Dell makes with 4GB and bband

On two+ machines your options are open but if you are gonna do GoD you have to have warrior / druid or cleric / slower, anything else is risky unless nicely equipped

The one major disadvantage of pet classes for dps is the push, if you are boxing 2 or less machines then put the warrior on the solo machine so you can reposition him to hold aggro

My setup varies but is gonna be in the next week:
3 machines, 6-7 total active accounts
3box - monk on top (tag pull), cleric, wizard
2-3box - chanter on top (CC), shammy on bottom, ranger for tracking/dps (if needed)
1box (laptop) - warrior


In the end compare # of machines, projected content, desired/current equipment, and come up with a configuration (to include what goes where) that works. And abuse the hell out of your hotkeys, they are essential! If you want to do anything quickly you will likely have /assist and /follow hotkeyed to your puller (whoever that is gonna be). Hotkey your heals as well.

Its also far easier if you overpower your content to offset your complexity (in short dont expect to 3 box things that a grp of 3 can kill unless those three likely have a 75% to kill). You can ride the edge once you get the hang of it but things that a normal grp can kill easily at my equipment level are more challenging for me as I keep flipping back and forth (course 3box is far easier than 6). The ultimate challenge to your box is anything that drops your tank fast (i.e. GoD) as lag is your enemy. If doing that kind of content then your cleric needs to be keyed to the tank (less nuking) and likely on top (top active box).
