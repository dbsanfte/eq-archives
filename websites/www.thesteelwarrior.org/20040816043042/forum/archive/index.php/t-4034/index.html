The problem with just hp advantage is that it doesn't scale with buffs. Lets say unbuffed a war has, oh, 20% more hps than a knight. So, just to throw out numbers, lets say that's 7K hps for a war. That means a knight will have about 5800 hps (5800 * 1.2 == 7000 roughly).

Now, lets cast some buffs:

Focus: 544
Kazad: 910
P9: 618
BSS: 330
Total: 2402

So, now that 7K warrior has 9402 hps, and the 5800 hp knight has 8202 hps.

Remember that 20% advantage in hps? It's now dropped to 15%. The more hps each player gets the less this advantage becomes, too.

This is why giving a HP boost is not the answer. And being a cleric who reads the cleric boards I can explain all about the referenced warriors make better tanks type threads (which are pretty common) these threads are different than the paper tank threads, that's a completely different topic. From a cleric standpoint, warrior is THE tank. Sure, SKs and Pallys do pretty good, but require a ton more healing. And no I'm not only comparing them to my warrior but other warriors I've played with over the years.

Warriors general do still have more hps and better mitigation as the same AAed Pally / SK. Sure they can keep aggro, but so can a wizard ... question is do they really need to or should they. Typically in LDONs we do more than one mob at a time, Pallys and SKs will ALWAYS need more healing than any warriors in the group, even if the Pallys and SKs have more AAs (or at least claim to). And some of them I believe as I've known them a while and seen them around for over a year, you don't play a character at 65 for a year without gaining AA.

On the same mobs I'll heal over time anyone not a warrior when they are a bubble down, then they usually need another heal by the time the fight is over. Where most warriors will only need the heal over time or even just a CH every few mobs. Most knights whether pallys or sks want to keep aggro, and while the warrior really doesn't care, it drives my cleric nuts... why should I spend twice as much mana if not more to keep alive a knight when I could have a warrior instead? I've also played with plenty of warriors with out EB or Anger 3 procs that keep aggro majority of the time and get it right back after the mob aggros on the knight after a spell.

I don't know about ya'll but from a cleric's standpoint where I calculate and pay attention to how many hps people in my groups have and how quickly they go down on slowed and unslowed mobs: warriors as a rule have a couple k more than knights. They may SAY they have more hps, though only a few I've seen have lied. I don't know if it's my server or the people I know, but there's an obvious gap in the hps of a equal warrior and knight. With my clerics healing AAs done and extended enhancement to make heal over time last longer, the 10 second cast and the rate at which knights go down compared to a warrior CH is useless on anything but a warrior.

My hubby's shaman usually uses his 1400 quick heal on knights when we've several mobs, while I keep my heal over time on them and keep an eye on everyone else's health including the warrior. Forget what we were doing but the other day after a session with 2 warriors in our group and he was throwing out heals and nukes to use mana. Afterward he said "I nearly cried when my heal on both the tanks didn't even do a full bubble."
