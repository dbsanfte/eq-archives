This is going to be somewhat long, there is no short way to say this. This has been the result of a signifcant amount of contemplation. Any who would reject the below or choose to flame are advised to read the work in its entirety first. If you think after reading the below and you think, "These changes would result in my class, group, etc. being impacted unfairly" then feel free to say so and explain why you believe that. If you would reply, "what you are *really* trying to do is..." simply remain quiet. I intend to do exactly what I sate.

My goal is thus:

To make changes to the game, classes, abilities that results in an overall better game with each class being more evenly desired than they are currently. This does not mean that certain classes, groups or approaches are all 100% equal. As content and the remainder of group/raid composition vary this is impossible without reducing EQ to a 'Gauntlet-style' simplicty.

As even pure content changes to the game are now considered 'nerfs' if said changes make a class or classes less powerful/desirable there will no doubt be those who consider the following 'nerfs'.

Many of these people may be my fellow Paladins.

******************************************************************************************************

TANK changes (Knights are used to indicate BOTH Pal and Sk, War will be used to indicate warrior-specific changes, where the term 'Tank' is used it indicates all three of those classes).

PREMISE:
A. All tanks have the same primary purpose. That is to be the recipient of a mobs attention and attacks.
B. Because this role is EXCLUSIONARY (one tank prevents another tank from recieving said attacks in an absolute fashion) its balancing is more complex and needs the greatest care.
C. 'Inherent' tanking shall indicate passive and ever-present game concepts such as HP, d/p/r, DI factor, offense skill cap, defense skill cap, damage tables, da/dw caps/rates, etc. and all AAs that modify same in a passive manner in a direct manner or provide a benefit of equal kind (e.g. Flurry increases the amount of melee attacks War obtain and is considered, HA/HG affect the healing rate of Pal, etc.)
D. I am intimately familiar with Pal and War. I am not as familiar with SKs in practice with the same breadth and depth. Therefore my proposals for their class are more conjectural and subject to a greater error in misunderstanding the practical nuances involved with SKs.
E. Although it is possible that fundamental mob/encounter changes could take place that would alleviate the need for most or all of the below one must accept the facts of history. For years 'tank stacking' has been asked for. It hasn't happened and is unlikely to do so on a broad scale since each method used to date would result in unwanted consequences that meet or even exceed the benefits of doing so. Hence class changes are advised.



CHANGES:

1. The Stalwart Endurance (imparts a large degree of stun resistance) and Tactical Mastery (provide a noticable amount of innate strikethrough) AAs are currently War AAs. They are, however, fundamentally speaking TANK AAs and give important benefits to those classes when they exercise their primary role. Thus Knights should be given these AAs at the current War cost and effectiveness.

2. ALL Hybrids would be affected by this next change. Changes would be made such that hybrid melee is unaffected by the casting of DETRIMENTAL spells (casting said spells would not pause the execution of a melee attack). This allows their offensive spells to scale appropriately with their melee. What occurs now is that while 'pure' melee/nukers DPS scales linarly with their acquisition 'hybrids' face a sometimes minimal gain due to the melee lost during spell casting, interruptions, fizzles, and resists. This results in an undesirable 'step' scenario where a new offensive spell is of great importance early, then of minimal use later. This is due to the problem of having to choose a 'middle point' for the balancing. This makes the balance linear and fairer to all classes in an overall context. This may require current rebalancing of spells, resuling in a decrease in effectiveness for hybrids from their 'chain-cast' levels in exchange for sustainably constant effectiveness in the arena of melee AND cast.

3. All BENEFICIAL single target spells cast by hybrids shall default to SELF when either there is no target or an NPC targeted. Beneficial spells would continue to pause the execution of a PC melee attack as it does now. The current tab re-targeting encourages 'twitch-play' and fast client/server sync more than any skill on the part of the player.

4. Paladin stuns would be changed to being no longer than 4.0s in duration (e.g DWoS down from 10s currently to 4s). Most group mobs should be stunnable. Exceptions, even frequent ones can, and SHOULD take place however. For example, encouters that are intended to be tank, healing, debuffing, or dps challenging (etc.) should certainly not be stunnable such that they are preferentially 'stun-locked'. However entire zones or areas should not be stun immune in a blanket attempt to 'make them hard'. These changes make 'stun' mitigation present, but limited in magnitude and scope.

5. All tanks use the now 'warrior' mitigation table. Gear, player skill, and visible differences in skill caps, AAs, DI mods, etc. distinguish tanks, not mysteriously hidden tables.

6. ALL hp, ac, and hp/ac buffs that are cleric spells are REMOVED from paladins' spell books. This eliminates the balance problem of paladins carrying around their own 'free' hit points when competing against the other tanks, especially in clericless groups.

7. Paladin 90% rez is REMOVED. A paladin provides a disproportional strength on the lower end in groups with druids instead of clerics for primary healing. One of these is the presence of a 'good rez'. Paladins retain an 'emergency CR' rez of a lower level (50% xp or less).

8. Warriors innate DI modifier is changed to 0.9. (the default is 1.0, War current DI mod is 0.95). This means that a warrior through innate mitgation will take 10% less damage from the DI portion of a mobs attacks than a knight does on a per-hit basis. This is not 'subject to the RNG' but instead represents a very visible and consistent ability to absorb damage better.

9. The Defensive discipline is changed to take ( into account. It is changed from 45% to 40% which will result in a defensive warrior retaining a net 0.5*DI, which is its current net value.

10. Knights obtain the defensive discipline. This operate very similarly to those currently used by warriors and will reduce the DI by 40% as above. DI for knights then go from 1.0 (default) to 0.6 while using Defenisve. More clearly put a knight will take 20% more damage from the DI portion of a mobs attacks than a warrior for each hit. Knights additionally obtain the evasive discipline. This functions identically to the current warrior discipline, however it is lessened in effect so that the same net relative change in tanking ability is maintained (i.e. the higher DI of pal causes the same DI mod to result in a higher net defensive DI. Since the 'miss' portion of tank defense is the same, the evasive modifier would be lessened to maintain the desired relationship of a warrior taking less damage at all times when in a the same disc.

11. Warriors obtain new endurance based skills to provide some of what is commonly called 'utility' as well as provide more self-survival. These skills will all be on the same 30s timer and provide the following skills: stun (4.0/65, would have the Divine Stun-level of very low hate), snare (mr check, 0 mod, 30s), and root (mr check, 0 mod, 30s). These skill would have a modest endurance cost but would be short range (30ft). Healing Will is altered to provide a better down-time healing rate. The reuse will be cut from 3 minutes to 1 minute. Endurance cost/upkeep may also be modified (I do not have date on end regen rates to give a more definative value here). The idea here is not to replace the desirability of the clases that provide stun, snare, root, or heals but to make a warrior (and his group) less burdened by the absolute absence of those abilities and provide some 'oh no!' coverage as mobs flee, gate, etc. This makes them the only tank who has all 3 of those abilities (pal lack snare, sk lack stun) but their application is limited.


What is NOT changed:

1. Knights do NOT receive AE taunt or any other Disc/AA/skill other than those listed above.
2. Knights do NOT receive a 100% avoidance disc of any kind. Warriors remain the only tank with this ability.
3. The current HP values are not altered. This includes Sturdiness remaining.
4. Warriors are not nerfed in any way whatsoever.


RELATED CHANGES:

To further this 'priest/tank' balance Druids obtain a 90% rez. Clerics obtain a self-buff that amplifies both the damage and the mana costs on DD spells by approximately 20%. They are also given a smaller DD that is fast enough casting (but modest in damage, ~half of their normal DDs) to actually use. Druids still provide snare, regen, DS, better damage, and the rare charm/DC ability. Clerics still provide the largest CHs and the more robust overall healing but are not now the sole source of priest-rezzes but will have some added offensive ability that makes their addition in a 'non-healing-intensive' setting less overtly redundant than it is now. There are other proposals I have for classes, but I wanted to focus this post on the tank issue primarily. Thus I am only outlining the other class changes that I believe are important to mention as they are integral to the balance changes I propose above.


RESULTS:

1. The paladin no longer becomes "the obvious choice" for groups with a druid as the sole primary healing class due to the currently present buffs and rezzes. The desirability of BOTH SKs and Warriors for said groups is increased for such settings.
2. The knights obtain a more linear relationship in survivability as a result of all tanks using the same 'table'.
3. Knights are able to better use their abilities when tanking. The ability to 'stun trivialize' content is reduced.
4. The warrior remains supreme in 'pure tanking' by way of inherently more hp, better avoidance, and now even more visible mitigation benefits.
5. Warriors obtain modest skills such that they are not as beholden on others to the same degree that they are now for some emergency situations.
6. Knights have 'tanking critical' defensive disciplines, but lack other skills such as AE taunt and an auto-avoid discipline thus limiting their power in that regard. A /disc warrior will remain > a /disc knight. A non-/disced warrior will remain > a non-/disced knight. However a /disced knight will tank greater than a non-/disced warrior. Furthermore relative gear becomes more important element in considering which tank is 'best' in a setting.

Barring some tuning required for SK-specific alterations it is my contention that the above will serve to better balance tanks (and their resulting groups) not only NOW, but throughout the progression of the game's content.
