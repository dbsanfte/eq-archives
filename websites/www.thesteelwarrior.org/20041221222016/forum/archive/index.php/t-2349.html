Hey fellow warriors!

I know that taunt has and forever will be a much-discussed topic, but I have a new twist to bring up (at least new to me). Im a level 44 warrior, and was xp'in in DL this weekend. I noticed and interesting trend, which was perhaps just a random number generator thing. What I noticed is that it seemed that I had more succesfull taunts when I was right on the mob....that is, the closer I was to the mob when I actually hit taunt, the more likely the taunt was successful.

Now I know that proximity based aggro (at least on certain types of mobs) has been well-documented, however I've never seen anyone test a proximity-based taunt before. Try it out sometime...if you're not already standing on the mob's feet, go ahead and move in (on large mobs, yes, you are actually "in" the mob)...see if you notice a difference. Perhaps some of you tester types can run some "scientific controlled" tests to see if my theory is all washed-up.

I don't know that there's a way to scientifically test it, but the fact that closer proximity to a mob draws more aggro has been well known for a long time. Anyone who's tanked for a long time has more than enough evidence from their own experiences to know that if they want aggro, they need to get their butts right up against that mob. And make sure the bloody rogue stays at max melee distance! :p

you may have also noticed that if a mob is rooted, it automatically goes for whoever is closest. In that case proximity is everything. That's just another reason earth pets really piss me off, especially when fighting in tight dungeon corridors. They keep rooting the freaking mob and sometimes a squishy (caster) is sitting too close....

The only real significant proximity aggro dealing with non-rooted mob I've seen is whether you're in melee range and not. I think if you get far enough away from melee range there's a bigger reduction in aggro but that kind of distance do not concern us. As long as everyone else is also in melee range proximity aggro is fairly unnoticeable (unless mob is rooted). It's still a good idea to be closest to the mob but don't count on just being close will get the aggro you need.
