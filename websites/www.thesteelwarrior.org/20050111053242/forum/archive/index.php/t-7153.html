Either an attempted hit misses or doesn't miss. When the data is presented with this value correctly displayed, the outcome isn't nearly as "outrageous" for monks, and it shows why a monk when taking a hit worse thn others still takes less damage than others.

I dont think this is entirely correct.

If you have Strikethrough (item of AA), you will notice that you can miss after hitting through a mobs parry/riposte/dodge/block. This leads to think that defenses are checked before the base Hit or Miss chance. At least they are for players, but we can assume this works for mobs the same way.


- Monk defensive skills and their chance to activate on any attack: Dodge (4%), Riposte (4%), Block (12%).
Total chance of any skill activating = 4 + 4 + 12 = 20%
- Warrior defensive skills and their chance to activate on any attack:
Dodge (4%), Riposte (4%), Parry (6%).
Total chance of any skill activating = 4 + 4 + 6 = 14%

Now lets take a Mob, and say he has a base 50% to roll a Hit, and thus also 50% to roll a Miss.
This means that a Monk's defensive skills will help him avoid 10% of the attack that would be hits (20% total chance of any skill activating x 50% of the attack being a Hit). The other 50% of the time a Monk's defensive skills kick in, the mob would have missed anyway, so them kicking in doesn't make any difference.

Same for Warriors: their defensive skills will help him avoid 7% of the attack that would be hits (14% total chance of any skill activating x 50% of the attack being a Hit)

So actually the defensive skills of any class count only for as much as the base To Hit chance of a mob (50% in my example).
This does mean that the apparent 6% gap between the 20% of a monk and 14% of a warrior, is actually closer to a 3% gap in Hits being avoided due to defensive skills.

Correct me if I'm missing something.

IMO, there is a hierarchy. The more versatility, utility, and caster-like a Tank class is, the less focused on tanking it is as a consequence:

Warriors - the Primary tank, default raid MT's, and offtanks difficult hardest hitting raid mobs requiring Defensive. Able but not called upon to tank most adds.

SK - a default add tank/the DPS Knight - MT's raid boss mobs only when Warriors are unable to because of special abilities or such mobs become trivial. May tank difficult hard hitting raid mobs and/or Rampage Tank.

Paladin - a default add tank/the Priest Knight - same raid duties as SK's plus group spot healer.

Ranger - an auxilary add tank with damage at a distance as a main focus. Often a back up to Knights in add tanking duties.

Warriors, SK's and Paladins are the most common group tanks with others getting that opprotunity for less than the most challanging group content.

That's four classes all jockeying for limited raid tanking opprotunites and wanting to preserve their turf. The role is overrun with Tanks, sometime tanks, and those that enjoy tanking when they get the chance :D.

Others might ocassionally add tank or be a group MT, but that isn't their primary role. They should not be especially well suilted to perform those roles.

Monk are actually quite good at tanking for not being Tanks. This is a concern for Knights and to a lesser extent Rangers (they aren't entirely reliant on tanking to have a group or raid role) who ARE Tanks and believe they shouldn't have significant "competition" from classes that aren't.
