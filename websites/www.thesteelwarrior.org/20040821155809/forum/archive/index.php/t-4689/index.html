It's a deceptive line of thinking. I have seen it mostly expressed as something along the lines of 'The heal chain heals at 0 sec delay anyway, having 5% mitigation will make no difference'.

While this is partially true, the big glaring omission is that there are a lot of mobs that can one round a tank regardless of how much healing is being done. 5% mitigation has a significantly more than 5% chance of preventing the death of a tank during a damage spike. It's like having 5% extra hp, but with the added advantage that, unlike 5% extra hp, it doesn't decrease the efficiency of CH, and is useful even in situations where the mob has zero chance of one rounding the tank.

For xp group situations where healing is ad-hoc it's a straight 5% saving in healing, as well as extra survivability on sticky pulls etc.
