You will definitely want to group with a healer.

Shaman - Slow/dots/utility
Druid - Ports/snare/damage shield/nukes/utility
Cleric - Best healer/best hp and ac buffs/Rez/least utility

The druid and shaman will have more trouble healing you until the high 50s but they have other nice things to offer like SoW and Invis that will make your life as a warrior more pleasant. Plan on some downtime with the druid or shaman in your 40s and early 50s, but in the upper 50s and 60s druids become very good healers and slow is very powerful.

Cleric is my personal favorite, but most people here would say shaman.
Khazzuz
09-17-2003, 09:53 PM
My warrior was the first character I created when I started playing a bit over a year ago. My only piece of advice is that if you start with a warrior expect the going to be tough and a lot more work than other classes. I would say that I managed to survive with below average equipment on my warrior all the way up til my low 50s, mostly because I found some friends and people tended to like me :) The fact is that you will not be out in Rathe Mountains soloing HGs any time soon (like those damn droods) to be able to afford all of the upgrades that a warrior needs, and we are indeed the most equipment dependent class in the game. If you're willing to accept the fact that you will most likely not be geared as well as some 65 necro's twink, and that that fact will be glaringly obvious for our class unlike some others where you would hardly notice, then by all means go with the warrior. Not to talk you out of it, I consider myself now to be a much better player of my warrior for starting from the bottom up with him, I believe in a lot of ways I learned far more about how to correctly play the game by struggling along. Good luck to you.

Khazzuz
61 Warrior on Povar

ps my intent was not to bash the warrior class, but I think most people can agree that access to good gear is indeed a limiting factor to starting up with a warrior.
