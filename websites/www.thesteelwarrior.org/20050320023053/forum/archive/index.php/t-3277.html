I snooped around a bit in my EQCLIENT.INI file (Word of Warning : Back it up if you are gonna mess around with it) and found the following bits in it that I think might have an impact on visual quality :


[VideoMode]
BitsPerPixel=32 (this one is a no brainer, more colors, more detail - Options are : 16 and 32)


[Defaults]
DoProperTinting=TRUE (makes armor color not color the skin, nice to have)
LoadVeliousArmor=TRUE (This one should be cancelled by another option, but I have it on .. just in case some NPC shows up)
LoadArmor17=TRUE (I believe those were used for Velius class armor sets, not sure though and still have them on. :p)
LoadArmor18=TRUE
LoadArmor19=TRUE
LoadArmor20=TRUE
LoadArmor21=TRUE
LoadArmor22=TRUE
LoadArmor23=TRUE
LockWindows=TRUE
ShowDynamicLights=TRUE (used together with UseTnL this noe makes for much better lighting .. often turned off on raids.)
VideoMode=2 (I have no idea what this line do !)
VideoModeBitsPerPixel=32 (dunno why this line is in the file, should be covered by BitsPerPixel in the VideoMode section. However, options are 16 and 32)
UseD3DTextureCompression=TRUE (the less loading, the better, might have a slight negative impact on crispness .. but not much as you have seen)
UseTnL=TRUE (use hardware to do coloring of light)
MipMapping=TRUE (makes textures align better, most noticable on ground and water textures)
TextureQuality=1 (My guess is that this is the option you need to turn on)
VisitedLuclinOptions=TRUE (no idea on this line)
TextureCache=TRUE
LoadVeliousArmorsWithLuclin=FALSE (Not sure how big a difference this makes)
ShowGrass=TRUE



Hope it helps.
And remember, back up that ini-file before messing with it. ;)
