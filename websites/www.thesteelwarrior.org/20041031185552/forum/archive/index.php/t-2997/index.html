I thought I better throw in an explanation of Guard and Spell Guard for those who do not already understand how these works..

Essentially the proc will buff you to mitigate an additional 5% of either melee or spell damage (Guard vs. Spell Guard) for 5 minutes or until a set number of hit points have been absorbed.

That means that the level I versions will last 5 mins or until you take a TOTAL of 1,000hp damage (5% of which is 50), the level II version will last 5 mins or until you take a TOTAL of 2,000 damage (5% of which is 100), the level III version will last 5 mins or until you take a TOTAL of 3,000 damage (5% of which is 150) and the level IV versions will last 5 mins or until you take a TOTAL of 4,000 damage (5% of which is 200).

Hope that helps.

Finally here are my opinions on these procs. I'd love to hear other people's too...

Mirror: Since I do not play on a PVP server, Mirror is worthless to me.

Spell Guard: Since I will never try to solo casters, this has no solo utility. Caster's aren't common enough that I'm going to worry about 5% damage reduction from nukes. This proc might be useful on a raid weapon 'built' especially to combat high-level caster mobs with targeted rather than AE spells. In most of those situations, however, there should be an ENC present to cast the Bulwark of Aelendor spell (or however you spell it) so this proc is IMO worthless.

Guard IV: This has some utility in xp groups because it is unlikely that you will convince your CLR to spend Peri after Peri to maintain the Bulwark of Vie on you (which lasts 36 minutes or upto 16k total damage) but, IMO, its true usefulness would be when soloing. Ultimately, it's only saving you 200hp a fight but you could choose to think of it as adding 200hp to your total pool.

The Icicle Line: I don't like that they messed up the resist on these. It makes me nervous. Plus, I am confident in my MR procs. I know MR is often resisted more but it's rare that I'm not in a group that lowers MR and on those mobs where all my procs are resisted.. Oh well, then I DPS a little bit lower. This might have some utility in PVP or soloing.

The Vampiric Line: I haven't thought too much about these because the info is so vague. LTs are supposedly slightly less aggro then stuns. These have obvious advantages in solo situations.

The Ykesha Line: This is where I really become uncertain. I would love to add the DPS and spell interupts of Ykesha to my weapons but aggro is my main concern. For my own part I think I am leaning towards Ykesha for my weapons. [EDIT: Also, the old Ykesha proc had a level limit on it. These do not appear to according to the data in Lucy's]

The Anger Line: I wish there was a way to know how the Ykesha stuns compare to +300 hate. If anger ensures aggro more often I would choose this, if not augmenting Anger is a waste of DPS and spellcasting interupts.

Demorgoth
Not quite true (or ambiguous at least)

You need to get a certain amount of points in a theme to unlock the items in that theme's teirs. Once unlocked, you can use points from anywhere to buy them.

eg:

You have:
300 points from Guk
400 points from Everfrost
600 points from Butcherblock
Total = 1300 points

You cannot buy items from the 500 tier in Guk or Everfrost, but you can buy from the 500 teir in Butcherblock. You can buy 2 items from the 500 point tier from Butcherblock if you wish, leaving yourself with 300 points left.

If you then went out and gained 700 points in Mistmoore (total points now =1000), you could buy a 500 tier item or two from Mistmoore, or another one or two 500 point items from Butcherblock
