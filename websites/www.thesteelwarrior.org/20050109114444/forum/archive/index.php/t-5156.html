The new Inny is in an entirely different league than Maestro. I've seen guilds who rocked Maestro hardcore fall flat on their face to Inny. That's not to say you can't do it. No way. He's just entirely different than Maestro is all.

Inny starts off in merchant form. This is the fake one. By killing him, you start the true Inny script and he will appear in the pit behind you. The fake Inny has the same damage output as the real one, however he will completely dispell your main tank. He quads for 800's very quickly and AE rampages. I'm pretty sure he also has the Avatar of Power AE (Dispell + Gravflux) as well, but it's been a while.

Thing to take into consideration:

-Inny can be pulled so that no adds will come. This is not a rumor as my guild has done it. He is tethered though, so you need to figure out where that correct place is. ;) If you pull past the tether, he despawns. :(

-When the revamped Hate first came out, it was considered an outdoor zone. It was then later changed to an indoor one. Inny has an annoying AE, but the zone is indoor. Just put two and two together here. :D

-Inny is an age old mob and shares one drastic thing in common with them. It's kind of annoying at first, but your *cough* debuffers *cough* and casters *cough* need to be *cough* in melee *cough* range in order to *cough* land spells *cough* on him. *cough* Gah! It's dusty in here. :p

Other than that, it's a pretty long fight. Inny has a ton of freaking hitpoints, but overall really isn't all that hard. Good luck! The loot rocks.

The type of adds and number that spawn initially is random. They can all be mezzed except for the rock golem thingies. Kill those. . .but leave the rest mezzed. You'll need 4 ench/bards to be safe. You *can* kill some of the mezzable ones too, but if you kill too many, they'll just start respawning.

Inny himself is quite easy, much lower dps than maestro for example. His AE rampage is annoying especially to casters, though at least once it was kindof buggy for us (hitting some people but not others). We usually did mostly ranged attack on inny, especially in the first half of the fight where we're locking down adds, and trimming the number of adds down a bit. Basically, if you can protect the MT and clerics from the adds, it's a done deal. The evangelist that becomes targetable at 25% or so is quite easy (with alot less dps than inny to my experience. . .a couple druids can heal a /disc tank fine).

If done properly, inny could be taken out without much trouble with 40 non-elemental geared people. It is very chaotic until the adds are locked down, however, and can easily turn into a giant goat screw.

By the way, we never cornered him, as we like to use rangers throughout, but we did have clerics healing from the other side of a wall.
