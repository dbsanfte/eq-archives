Arena summary: 2004-03-30 12:24 am to 2004-03-30 1:17 pm

Arena summary: 2004-03-29 11:30 am to 2004-03-30 12:24 am

Wandor proposes a test for HoT spells giving aggro to their caster when they heal you: get a HoT cast on you, aggro a mob, wait for the HoT spell to heal you, zone out. See if the mob goes after the healer. Editor: Great idea! Now someone go do it, and make sure that the HoT actually heals you at least once before you zone.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?postid=94137#post94137

Read Sassinak's post on using /shield during a boss encounter. It's too damn good to summarize. Go. Read the original. Shoo, now.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?postid=94182#post94182

Ogre stun resistance is not removed when under an illusion. SE may have some small utility for ogres (as when running away, or an unexpected add hits you from behind), but isn't as critical for them as other races.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=6408

Stonewall is good for single groups (no offensive decrease), but Defensive is better for raids. Strafing while in Stonewall moves at full speed, so there's a good work-around. Stonewall is good against mobs that snare you anyway, and bad against mobs that do a knockback.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=6404

Chance of success at Dual Wield/Double Attack (with and without AA's).
Successful Dual Wield: 85% (90% with full AAs)
Successful Double Attack: 66.7%
Triple Attack: 0.05%???? (I read this but it seems awfully low)
Double attack confirmed at 66% for rogues with Ferocity 3 AA.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=6407

The success of Kick at stunning mobs is more visible when they are casting or running. It is generally agreed that a Kick that lands will almost always stun a stunnable mob. Bash is better, it is theorized, not because it is more likely to stun when it lands, but because it is more likely to land (higher skill cap).
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&postid=94132#post94132

Tiejinnie reports that if a HoT spell (cast on you before you aggro a mob) actually heals any hit points on you (that is, you took damage, and the HoT heals some of it), this will aggro the mob on the caster of the HoT spell. The simple presence of an HoT spell does not give aggro to the HoT spell's caster, but the action of the HoT spell does. Editor: thus an HoT spell can put a cleric on the Rampage list.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&postid=94051#post94051
