1) Get a better UI. I use Cosmos myself. Having an extra 12 mouse clickable buttons avalible all the time (and Cosmos can be configured to have even more) means that I use more of my abilities.

The stuff I use regularly are on the main, keyboard accessoable bar--attack, rend, suder armor, charge, thunder clap, hamstring, demoralizing shout, sheild bash (!@#$% casters), overwhelm, battle shout, gun and potions.

Then on the extra bar I have my "Oh Shit!" buttons--cleave, taunting blow, shield wall, bandages, canabalize (which I never remembered to use before I got cosmos, it was a pain, and occasinally dangerous to use) etc.

Heroic Strike and Slam have fallen off the tool bars...

It also runs timers on buffs, so you can see, and plan for battle shout to fade.

2) First Aid. stacks with poitions, is a Heal Over Time--so you can fire it very early in a fight. Also firsts aid requires NO materials other than bandages, so you can make more in the field, as you go. Also a stack of bandages is 20, not 5.

A freebie for the undead: Eat your kills. The cool down on canabalize is a mere 2 minutes--so between every 2 and 3 fights you can munch down on your humaniod victims. I find that my cooking is 7 because I am always eating my kills.

Beside what better fate can there be for the Scarlet Brotherhood?

The Expert First Aid book is in Stormgarde, and it's easy to get to it. As a gnome, I slipped through a gap in the wall on my first visit, but you can also go in through the front door. At the earliest opportunity after entering through the front gate, turn right - if you go straight onwards, you will meet with the Syndicate. This meeting will likely be brief.

Keeping taking right turns (you should now be surrounded by Alliance and not Syndicate) until you see the Prince's house - the vendor with the book, Deneb Walker, is on your right. He may be a little obscured by the overgrowth that covers the little building he is in. He also sells the Mageweave Bandage manual. Be sure to pick that up.

The bug fix to the channelling bar is annoying, but I still think the skill is worth the effort. Hey, we're warriors - we should be used to being the finesse class. ;)
