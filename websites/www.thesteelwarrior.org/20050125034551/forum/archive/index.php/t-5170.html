According to Lucy, the Guard line is 5% mitigation, for 50, 100, 150, and 200 points of damage, total.

Guard I (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4384&source=Live)
Guard II (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4385&source=Live)
Guard III (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4386&source=Live)
Guard IV (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4387&source=Live)

Compare to Bulwark of Vie, which is 10% mitigation for 1600 points of damage, total.

Bulwark of Vie (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4091&source=Live)

It's not formatted prettily, but basically the 2nd number is % reduction, and the final number is total HP absorbed.

I have no idea what the first number is.

Spells with effect #162 absorb normal damage, and effect #161 absorb magical damage (from procs or nukes)

Bulwark of Alendar (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4076&source=Live)

This is a very nice buff. It stacks with the spellshield line, and can really change an encounter dramatically.

For example, Emperor Ssraeshza, has an unresistable 4000 DD proc (does a few other things too, but the DD is what we care about)

Bulwark of Alendar absorbs 20% of this, or 800 points, making it a 3200 DD, and Spellshield then absorbs 700 of it, making it a 2500 DD. Because it absorbs 1600 damage total, your Enchie only has to cast it on you every other proc, which is possible even if the enchie is on Mez duty.

In this fight, taking the 4000pt proc and making it a 2500pt proc makes healing on the MA substantially easier. It is much less likely for the MA to die if he happens to be at "low health" when the proc goes off. In fact, it almost trivializes the encounter.
