Why are you dieing? Are healers fizzling? Out of range for a heal? Eating AE's that stun/interrupt the heals? Are you dieing at the start of a fight? Dieing by adds before they get mez'd/offtanked? Is the heal rotation slower on you since you have more HP's than the others?

One thing to keep in mind, if you are the MT, no other tanks are going to die as much as you. You will be the one fighting an unslowed mob and you will ALWAYS be fighting the most mobs. Some heal rotations are adjusted mid fight if they aren't working correctly. Backup healers don't necessarily spam heal sometimes until the MT goes down and they think, hmmm, maybe I should heal instead of nuke/dot if I want to live.

Figuring out why you are dieing is key to solving your problem. But, keep in mind, the MT is always first to die for tanks and have the most chances for a RNG to screw them through heal fizzles, healer LD's, lag, etc. since you are tanking the most.

Here is something to keep in mind ...

All pre-SoL mobs where all killed with level 60 warriors sporting around 6khp with 0aa.

All SoL mobs where killed with level 60 warriors with under 8khp and not nearly as many defensive AAs as are possible now.

If you are dying a lot with 8khp+ and 1400ac with a bunch of AA at level 65, then your healers are blowing it. We routinely do the tower named at night with a group of friends, and we have no problem keeping a single tank up against them. We use anything from a 7khp SK to a 7500hp pally to a 8500hp warrior. We usually use 3 clerics with a druid and a shaman gap healing. There is no reason you shouldn't survive mobs with similar damage output.
