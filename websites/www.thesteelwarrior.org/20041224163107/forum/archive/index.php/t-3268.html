First make sure everyone else isnt just mashing their taunt button constantly :D

Proximity aggro can play a large part in this. If you are low on health going into a fight with your warrior partner and dont want aggro, dont hit taunt (duh) and stand as far away from the mob as you can and still hit it. Have the other warrior get as close to the mob as they can and still hit it.

If you have aggro (and dont want it) turn off attack, wait til taunt lands from other warrior then either wait a few more seconds before attacking again or back up, wait a few seconds and start bandaging.

Hope this helps. :)

Umm, I dont' mean to be making light of your situation, but at lvl 11 a druid can tank mobs so does it really matter at that level? I don't really remember aggro problems at that point in the game but I probably pulled for most of the time though and it seemed to me that the first person who aggro'd the mob usually tanked. Enjoy the game for the time being and just focus on becoming a good tank using skills, not gear.

Not until the late 30's to early 40's do I remember starting to have issues with aggro but usually this was against people who had gear well above their level and we doing much more damage than me or were taunting or casting high aggro spells like rangers snaring on incoming. I finished getting crafted armor at 40 (/waves goodbye to the bronze) and was using weapons with ratios like 10/29 and 11/25 (which I thought was uber at the time). I now regularly see better weapons given to pets.

I really do think you will have some problems until you accept some things in EQ.

1. Almost any class can generate more aggro on demand than a warrior using mana.

2. Aggro weapons rely on the random number generator to favor you because they are all based on proc's. We don't have superior dps weapons to other classes that are warrior only.

3. Taunt fails ALOT

4. Even if you obtain some good aggro proc'ing weapons, they probably won't proc until lvl 40 or so.

Now, with that being said, how do you get/maintain aggro? First if you want aggro, engage the mob early. Pull if you can. Get as close to a mob as you can. Use disarm when you get that skill as fast as it refreshes. If needed, ask your group mates to wait a few seconds before attacking a mob so you get a headstart. Ask group mates to root the mob and crawl into its armor with it. Make sure you are the closest to it. If someone else pulls shoot the mob on the way in with arrow(s).

LDON I am afraid won't be the answer you are looking for either although I enjoy the expansion so far. The adventures are timed so the group really doesn't have much time to let you solo the mobs before engaging. But, groups with multiple tanks are very viable in LDON so that part is good for warriors.

Enjoy the warrior class as I know I have.

By the time I finish writing this you could/should be level 12.

By the time you actually read this, you could/should be level 15.

And that's without any PLing or powergaming taking place.

Your levels fly by at this point in your career. So don't sweat it so so much.

In regards to some of your questions though ...

"But when one of us is lower in health, the other one cannot taunt off him/her."

HOW low on health exactly? Remember, your HP pool is much smaller at level 11, so low health aggro is actually something that happens.

"I can no way taunt off a Paly or a SK who is often the puller when we are in a group - unless the Paly or the SK backs off intentionally."

Two things:

1- Having others back off intentionally works. And should be used as a strategy. It works at level 11. It works at level 40. It works at level 65. If you're grouping with people who don't want to back off if/when they get aggro, you're grouping with the wrong people.

2- Paladins, SK's, monks, Rogues, Warriors, Druids ... you all tank about the same at level 11. I wouldn't worry too much about not being a tank in a group at those levels. Worry about it when you're playing at a level of the game where you can't ding 3 levels in a couple of hours. Where kills take longer than 15 seconds and do not yield two blue per kill.


"This really does create a little frustration for the group."

Why would this create any frustration for the group? It's EVERYONE'S job to manage the monster's aggro and who it pays attention to. If your paladin friend can't be bothered to turn off attack, then your paladin friend needs to get better gear and start tanking.


"So I guess it's really more efficient to let the knights be the MT - since everyone can go all out without having to back off."

At level 11, going all-out is really not anything to worry about.

Applying high level tactics to this level of the game will skew your gameplay experience.

For ME ... at level 11, I was camping orc 1 in EC. Our BIGGEST problem was crowd control. We had two choices ... get a crowd control class (enchanter or druid) to help us with single pulls ... or load up on melees and offtank.

In scenario 1, it didn't matter who tanked. We just had to kill the mob. In scenario 2, EVERYONE tanked.

Seriously ... you shouldn't be worrying about this stuff at level 11.

"So what are the solutions at my LVL?"

SOLUTION: Level up.

"Should I get certain weapons that would ensure I would out damage and out agro the knights?"

Something to remember: Scepter of Destruction procs at level 1. Get that, you'll be fine. Most mobs at level 11 will be half dead with just one proc.

Something else to remember: MOST weapons won't proc until the mid-30s or higher. So don't sweat procs.

Something else to remember: DMG BONUS does not exist until level 28.

Something else to remember: YOUR DAMAGE IS CAPPED.

You know what one of the most effective weapons at level 11 is?

Blackened Acrylia Blade.

Or, ye old classic, Jade Mace.

Or a Centi sword of some kind.

Don't sweat your weapons. Not at level 11. You're going to give yourself an ulcer, because you've got all of your 40s, 50s and 60s to worry about aggro, weapons and inferiority tanking complexes.

"Can someone recommend what I can do at my lvl?"

SOLUTION: LEVEL UP! Have FUN. Don't worry about this nonsense until is actually should be an issue.

Jeezis ... does it really matter who's tanking that Orc Lego?

Well, since you didn't mention if it's your first character, or your buddy's first character too, and how much platinum you have to spend .. or what expansions you have, it's a tad hard to say what to d and what not to do. For now I will guess that you have bought the box set with all expansions except LDoN and are a new player.



Once a character gets sufficiently low on HP (less than 30 % or so) a monster will frenzy on him/her and pulling off aggro on that person is next to impossible and only seen once in a blue moon on the second sunday of the week.

The trick is to switch before one of you get that low, switch aggro when one of you are around 50% HP. This is done by letting the one who got down to 50% to stop Taunting and stop attacking, while the other attacks, kicks/bash and Taunts all he can - The MOB should switch to the second tank within 1 or 2 combat rounds. :)


As for weapons, Crystalline Spider fang have been mentioned, But Centi Shortswords are also wonderfully cheap (approx 40 pp I think ?) to get in the Bazaar. And don't forget, at level 13 you get dual wield (just train one point into it, it goes up quickly by itself) which also helps some on damage output.

Unless you decide to use two-handed weapons offcourse.


Now, the Bazaar as I mentioned, is a zone on the moon (which is called Luclin, the moon that is) where players can set themselves up as traders and sell their loots. As I mentioned, lots of people sell the Centi Shortsword for a cheap price and quite useful at 12 Dam, 24 Delay. Not to be confused with the Centi Longswords which are 14 Dam, 27 Delay and quite a bit more pricey.


Anyways if you have Luclin expansion (qhich is quite likely), get you and your buddy to Paludal Caverns and start in the easy end (towards Shar Val, there's newbie maps which can be called up with backspace, in the zones between Shar Val and the Cavern and POK book teleport thing and all that jazz, if you have that expansion, so easy to find) - Kick ass exp in there for your level. :)

Oh, and POK teleport thing is if you have the Planes of Power expansion, which is a tad less likely if you made a boxboy, but still quite possible. The easy way to figure out if you have it, is to go to the zone you started in and find an odd wooden bookstand and click on it ... if you are transferred to POK (Plane of Knowledge, a friendly zone) then you have easy travel access to many places and shops within easy reach to boot.

Oh, and the POK book thingy is noted down on the Newbie maps and all, should make it easy to find.
