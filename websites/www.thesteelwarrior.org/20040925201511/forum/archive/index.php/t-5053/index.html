Well met Gunrack.

Alot of what you are asking is kinda a personal preference. It really all depends on what you know and what you dont. Do you have the time to learn code outside of what you already know? Does the "purpose" and "need" of your site allow for on-the-fly learning? These questions only you can answer really.

Personally I started coding webpages using Notepad. Nowadays that is terribly ineffecient. However, it gave me a strong background in hand-coding and that is a great benefit to have in the world of WYSIWG editors.

Of those types of editors, i have used 2, FrontPage and Dreamweaver.

All I am going to say is this I HATE FrontPage. To me Frontpage is a glorified Word program. This is great for beginners I feel .... but very lackluster for the professional. IMO, Dreamweaver is the way to go.

Ok -- so we have your editor established, now lets move onto server/language technologies. This again is a personal preference.

Until Recently I always used Javascript and HTML. That was it. For what I was doing i did not need anything else. Nowadays I am learning ASP, PhP and XML (although barely :)).

As I can only speak for myself, if the site you are designing allows for you to experiment with anything that you are not used to, then by all means go ahead and give them a shot. There is nothing like figuring things out by experimenting. Also, trying to use server/languages that you have not used before on a real project is the best way to learn.

Up until recently I never needed database oriented content for webpages I worked on. Now I do. However, the server that will be working with is IIS (microsoft) and therefore I will be using ASP technologies. If I could I would like to try PhP also. However Php is mainly used on Apache (linux/Unix) servers, and I dont have access to one atm.

So, if ya wanted to mess with ASP/PhP you gotta know what your server allows and that will help with your decision. Oh forgot to mention, ASP/PhP is the method of HOW the client communicates to a database located on the server. So both of these languages require a database (Access and MySQL are the most popular) to be located on the server, but the type of server determines what language to use.

OK ... lets move onto frames or noframes.

I have always had the moto of "Simple Elegance"TM when it comes to page design. So I try to keep things as effeciently driven as possible. However, sometimes the client does not care about effeciency OR the subject of the site does not require effeciency, and in those cases, go crazy :) Also, effeciency does not have to mean things look boring or canned. I can make a site that looks great .. pleasing to the eye, downloads fast, navigates easily ... all while staying effecient. I can also do the opposite if needs warrant them ... but I will stick to effeciency if at all possible.

Back to frames :) ... Again personal choice. I will always try to stay away from them if at all possible. Frames, although easy enough to manage with software like Dreamweaver, can get complicated and I try to keep everything as simple as possible. You can acheive frame usebility and not use frames by taking advantage of library items and table setups in Dreamweaver (this is not by any means a Dreamweaver oriented idea and in fact can be applied to whatever editor you choose, its more of a ideaology then a software capability FYI, although some software helps more then others :) ).

Also, since this is on the same subject ... try to think of your pages and the way you navigate them in a modular way. Meaning, down the road when your site has exceeded more pages that you can easily handle, how hard would it be to change site navigation across all of those pages? Using libraries and other "such" thinking can make life easy when dealing with webpage maintenance. For example, by using libraries I can easily change navigation of 5000 pages just by changing one library item. (Frames can do this too -- but personally I dont like them).

On the same note .. I also try to stay away from drop-down menus and other fancy coding. Like i said before ... there are places for these types of navigation to be used ... but I try to stay away from them if at all possible.

For me, embedded tables are the way to go for layout of any site. Graphics, navigation, anything and everything, can be organized with clever use of tables. With tables there does not have to be use of frames, drop-downs, radio buttons, etc. for navigation.

Well I hope I answered all of your questions. I am sure you will have alot more of them. As I said before, these are some of my preferences. I am sure others will agree with me on some things and flame me for others :).

Basically it comes down to this:

1. Use what you know.
2. If site allows for it, experiment (great way to learn).
3. Keep things simple, allows for easier maintenance in the long run.
4. Modularity is your best friend.

Come to think of it ... I probably am over-complicating this if your only considering web designs around EQ/Guild oriented material. However ... it will go a long way in helping that too.

Good luck and have fun.

Gunrack -

The 'Dream" you are talking about is very much possible. Its the modularity thinking that can help achieve.

Like i mentioned before, you can do this is notepad, but use Dreamweaver it can make things million times easier.

Let me say first that I am glad you realized what you needed and that using what you know already is plenty to get you through. Often times I see grandiose ideas that are far out of reach of some people. On the same note ... many times they can do what they are trying to do --- just takes a little "outside of the box" thinking.

In any case, what you are dreaming about is referred to as "libraries" in dreamweaver. You can create a library item (which can be anything; i.e. text, pics, flash, etc.) and then insert that item to any location on any pages that you wish. Then, when updates are necessary all you do is make changes to the library item and its gets changed on ALL pages that the library item exists on. WEEEeeeee.... its fun!! :D

Personally I make library items for the constant things throughout the site that might need drastic changing. For example ... you can make a library item (I do this all the time) for your site's main navigation. Stick it on all pages (and make templates for super-easy page creation) and voila ..... page navigation across 5000 pages can be fixed/updated just by correcting the library item (I seem to like this example :P ).

You can also make library items for footer info ... quotes of the day ... damn near anything really. Its the modularity of library items that makes them supreme. With some slick thinking you can use them in alll kinds of ways.

You should pick up a trial version of Dreamweaver (along with all the other Macromedia products) and try em out. I bet you will love it as much as we do :P
