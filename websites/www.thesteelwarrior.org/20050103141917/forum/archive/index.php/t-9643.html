I think it's likely to not make much a difference most of the time, a Berserker might need more burst healing but in the end the fight is over faster so it balances out.

You get stat boosts according to class and equipment and character traits but since everyone gets pretty much the same boosts you will obviously be most successful if you choose a race appropriate to your class. I have seen an Erudite tank and she didn't have the margin of error of the bigger races but it worked out, she kept aggro and (often) lived and that's all that matters.

Note that, at least at the low levels, fights are almost uniformly short and brutal. If things go wrong it can snowball in a hurry and before you can type, "/g RUN" it's all over. As the tank in EQ2 you WILL die unless you're fighting trivial mobs, regardless of race. Don't get discouraged, death isn't really that big a deal unless it comes in bunches.

Yeah, that's a thing that I think a lot of people still haven't gotten used to. All fighters are equal tanks, barring bugs (like deflection not working for a while for monks brawlers), just choose what you like.

Berzerkers do a bit more dps than guardians, and its true, not by much though. DPS does hold agro quite well, even better than some of the taunt abilities. However, the only people that you are really going to be fighting for agro are other fighters. Agro is not much of an issue as it is in EQ. The tanks were given plenty of tools for this job and they work.

There's a big debate that berzerkers are more useful because DPS stacks and is generally more useful in a group than defense buffs. I've only made it to 22, but I don't see why its an issue. Guardians get plenty in return for less DPS.

I can't overstate how good the guardian buffs are. I can make a wizard tank if neccessary. Our buffs stack with each other and its a snap to protect someone from too much damage. Warriors get intervien, which is relatively weak, but is purely concentration based. Most importantly, it does work, you can even be behind the mob beating on him and the buff works so lang as you don't get too far from your group member. Later on you get ally and some other buff. These things are priceless. Tank stacking is not an issue, at least for gaurdians. We can tank without even having agro.

These buffs really work if you give them a chance. They are get at getting agro back from a wayward caster, and great for tough encounters because you can spead the spike damage off the main tank and help decrease the likelyness of them being one rounded.

In short, we all tank just as well, but each fighter has other abilities to differentiate themselves from other fighter classes. Berzerkers do better damage, and guardians can protect anyone in the group.

I chose guardian, and I'm very happy with my decision.

Well, perhaps I didn't post clearly enough. I wasn't suggesting that the guardian intervein/sentinel the MT in normal group situations. That would be inefficient, unless the group is way undercon. In that caseits more like a single group raid.

The damage spreading spells, like intervien, sentinel, and stand firm really come in useful when taking on raid mobs. Most raids that I've been on have a MT that is higher level than the encounter. I have tanked a few as well as secondary tanked raids where this is the case, where no one will do the raid unless the tank is some arbitratry level above the mob. This is rediculous, and is not what is going ot be happening as people get higher level. Players will have to deal having an undercon tank just like the rest of the raid.

So, one way to deal with this is to have your tanks protect the MT. Each healer is assigned a tank, just one tank for them to heal. The MT is going to take th brunt of the damage, but with the other guardians spreading the dmaage across player, essentially, you have tank with much more hitpoints, since its very unlikely that the tank will be killed in ONE HIT, the damage will spread to the other tanks, because they each have a chance to take the damage instead of MT. They each have healer ready to heal them also.

So, for instance, you could have the MT a berzerker, which holds agro much better than a guardian, wears the same armor, ans simial hitpoints. Then have your secondary tank a guardian, he has all his protection buffs on the berzerker, intervien/sentinel/stand firm/ally. Have a healer on each, one being a shaman doing wards on the MT and spot healing the guardian. There's very little chance of the MT doing down, and almost no chance of the berzerker losing agro, since very little raw healing power is being used on him, and berzerkers are just awesome at keeping agro. That leaves you with no less than 8 DPS spots to work with. Nothing I've come across can deal with that much pure DPS, but if you need to you can have 1 or 2 spots for extra healers for ae damage or whatnot. But chances are, from my experience so far, you'll have more than enough DPS to kill the encounter before ae dmage kills a significant portion of your dps, if any at all.

More than one tank in a group is a waste. Two different types of healers are usually a good idea for most groups though to keep things moving. My thoughts hae olways been that xp gain will come best from going without a none dps than with one. Anything less just means more downtime, and slower xp for those that like to do that sort of thing. Myslef?
