I don't think you're doing too badly. No one is going to mistake you for Supertwink the DestroyerofZergables, but you'll be able to do you job for a bit.
If you can run a bazaar trader, I'd consider farming silks and hides. Go to EQtraders, then go to their message boards, then to the tailoring and smithing sections for some tips on where to farm silks and hides. Everfrost used to be a prime spot for farming spiderling silk (a subcomponent of player-made smithing stuff) but after the newbie armor quests were introduced for a wider range of Barbarian classes, the spiderling loot table was changed. Now I believe that the common thinking is that Shadeweaver's is the best place to go.
If you can't run a trader for some reason, you might be doing about as well as you can with your farming. I spent an ungodly amount of time at your level Killing wisps and turning greater lightstones in at the North K. gypsy camp (to female gypsy who's name starts with an "M"). she'll give you a "book" that you need an empty enventory slot for, and you can sell it to the other gypsies in camp for 9-10 plat. But, that was a long time ago and I don't know that it's close you your best farming option nowadays. The best thing is if you can run a trader.
Generally, in terms of classes of gear, I'd say your armor options coming up tend to fall into about four categories:
1. Player-made LDoN armor such as Gothic & sandblasted. Check prices in Bazaar, but also check the dwarf vendors in the smithing building in PoK, because players who skill up on this armor often do so in this building and often simply turn around and sell whatever they are making to the vendors next to the forge. Vendors in turn will sell pieces to you for about 100 pp.
2. Crafted and crusty armor. In theory, you will soon be able to quest some of the crafted pieces, but as with so many quests in this game, doing so doesn't make any sense. You can find these pieces sometimes in the bazaar for about the cost of the gem subcomponents. The common thinking is that crusty holds a slight advantage over crafted, but you can compare several armor sets here: http://members.cox.net/radhazzone/rad_armor.htm
3. Player-made stuff. I note you are wearing some old-school player-made armor (fine plate collar, ornate coif). Fine plate and this chain stuff are still fairly common skill up products for young smiths (though the plat. chain is a little unusual; most smiths I think do gold rather than the plat). Sometimes you can find a smith working on this and get a deal on it. I don't think I'd buy it from a vendor, though; you can check but I'm 95% certain you'll get more bang for your buck with the LDoN armors.
4. Odds & ends. The game is full of various items that are not "standard" sets of gear but which might plug a gap or two. As a Barbarian warrior you can wear all kinds of different stuff. Search in bazaar by class, religion, race, your budget and order the results by AC (but also look at STA and hitpoints).

Players skill up in jewelry will also sometimes sell their products in the bazaar, so I'd check your finger slots every once in a while; as with the visible armor slots, you want to start getting a feel for the price of warrior-wearble gear on your server, even if you can't afford to go on big shopping sprees quite yet.

I'd also seriously consider saving up for two opal encrusted steins, which you can look for in the bazaar. They add +25 CHA each, and will pay for themselves in better prices from vendors in very short order. On my server they are around 20-25 plat, I think (haven't checked for a while).

I think your weapons are okay at the moment. You might start pricing things like the Centi hammer (15/32, which is slow, but not a bad offhand at all and it will train up your 1-hand blunt skill) and maybe when you hit level 21 something like a Sarnak Battlehamer (2H blunt, 30/40, sometimes cheap because it's warrior only; yes it's slow but I used to be able to hold aggro with it surprisingly well and it will slowly work up your 2H blunt skill). Be careful when you buy weapons because of the recommended level thing. I'm out-of-touch with which weapons this has and hasn't affected, so just be careful. Another weapon I used to use was a Centi short sword (1hS 12/25 and +5STA), still quite a usable weapon.

If you are still broke down the road (say, in your early 30s) and you do some hunting in outdoors zones like the Overthere and later the Dreadlands, here's a pretty old-school thing I used to do to hold aggro as an untwinked warrior. For this to work, you have to have a Weighted Axe and you have to be the puller, and you should have SoW. On long pulls from camp, you initially hit the mob with the weighted axe, which is a 50 damage, 150 delay (!) weapon. It will do eye-candy level damage if you actually hit the mob, and it will generate a lot of aggro for a single hit. One the way back to camp, switch out the Weighted Axe for your faster regular weapons. Ideally, you cycle through the weighted axe's 150 delay while you run back to camp, and you're ready to go when the mob arrives. As soon as your delay recycles, you can tag with an arrow too. Again, before you get excited, make sure that the effective damage on this weapon actually works at your level. And note this weighted axe pulling will not work if the pull is short, since you'll be standing there with the mob in camp but your delay will not have refreshed yet.

Okay, I'll stop boring you. It looks like you're making your own arrows, which is good.

High quality ore is needed to smith the kind of ornate chain that you are wearing (the coif). It's sold in limited amounts on vendors (if memory serves), or can be made by melting down fine steel weapons.
The LDoN armor's bottleneck has to do with tempers, which drop or are foraged in the LDoN.
The plate armor almost certainly has more AC than the chain (I'd say it does, but actually I've never compared them). So go plate if you have a choice.

Smithing can be fun (er, for those of us who think it's fun) but it's a very very expensive way to get your armor. You'll get the best armor a smith can make faster if you sell farmed smithing components, take the cash, and pay a smith to make you stuff.

This is the best smithing guide I've ever seen: http://www.crimsonsnow.com/pdfs/guides.html

I agree with teh comment that life in the 50s can be rough on the warrior main who has few resources.
Partly that's because in your 30s and 40s you can really do your job well with relatively inexpensive gear.
But suddenly in your 50s you need expensive weapons, expensive armor, and expensive haste and it will seem like you need them all at once. You'll start doing the math on the cost of what you need, and you'll compare it to the rate you can farm plat and that will discourage you, heh. But hang in there.

Also, don't be discouraged by the difference in your current bank account (you say the warrior has 15 pp) and some of the weapons listed above. Just use them as a list to check prices against.
Also, be careful to look everything over carefully before you buy it. Things like the bedleg club is really fast--same delay as a lamentation--but it was one of the weapons which was affected by the recommended level changes. At your level it would only be a 1 or 2 damage weapon.
