When I am not the puller (and that is always a good thing :rolleyes: ) I will tag with an arrow, hit Incite and that will usually "pull" the mob right to me. Then I start to hit the mob and use taunt just to be sure I have aggro locked in. I will refresh Incite maybe once in a group situation and two or three times during a boss fight (depending on how quickly it's health is going down) but I like to save a little for when nuking begins in earnest. And save Taunt for when I actually lose aggro.

When I'm tanking a boss mob I will put up Defensive if I'm forced to stand in an open area and the push may get out of hand. But if I can get my back into a corner, and movement is not an issue, then I'll use Stonewall. And, yes, you can use Incite with Defensive, Stonewall or the other "non-taunt line" of Disc's as long as you have Endurance left.

For building up a lot of aggro:

Let a ranger pull the mob, go weaponshield, and spam snare hotkey (rangers get an AA that lets them snare for zero mana).

Right as Weaponshield is wearing off, but before the ranger dies, use AETaunt and pop furious -- if the ranger dies before you AETaunt, you lost a lot of aggro. If you get a successful taunt off before he dies, you did your job well (the ranger may still die if he continues to aggro after your taunt, of course).

Furious is popped because once the mob is hitting you, every riposte you make builds your aggro up even more.

When you're down to the last 2 seconds of furious, start spamming your defensive/stonewall key. You should not take any damage without having a discipline up.

You should now be set for aggro.
