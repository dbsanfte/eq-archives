I've seen a whole lot of posts talk about how Knights have *always* had superior taunt ability. This is simply not true. My first character was a paladin I created in May 1999, and retired him December 2000 at level 52. Back then, taunt was merely a function of DPS, and dual wield was king. As the 1H bonus scaled up dramatically after Kunark was released with the new level cap of 60, the disparity grew. The DPS disparity between knights and warriors was fairly large... where knights might do 90% of the damage that warriors do nowadays, back then it was more like 70%. We are also talking about the days before KEI and 15 mana stuns. Mana was a luxury and only used in sparse quantities. A paladin could outagro a warrior for 3 minutes, then when the mana tank was empty, the warrior was agro king until the paladin had time to med.

The turning point which made paladins such good with agro was the introduction of the level 9 stun, Cease, which cost a negligible 15 mana (entered game during SoL era). Now when grouped with an enchanter, a paladin could chain stun for indefinite periods, applying extra agro on demand. Fast forward to the present, in the world of BST crack, KEI, the druid Glades line, FT... paladins now have massive mana regen, and stunning to maintain agro really isn't a drain on the mana pool. And with the long duration of KEI, paladins don't even need to be grouped with an enchanter anymore to enjoy massive mana regen.

So anyways, just wanted to point out that the knight's claim to being the kings of agro wasn't true for the first 2 years of EverQuest. It was merely situational agro, not sustainable over indefinite periods of time like it is today. The game has changed a lot in the past, and there is no reason to believe it won't continue to change in the future.
