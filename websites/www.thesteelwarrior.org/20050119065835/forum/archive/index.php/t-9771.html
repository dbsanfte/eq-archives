Well, to some degree this is always the test: if the weapon holds aggro in your regular group, it's usable. So, test it and see. When it stops holding aggro you'll know it, your group will know it, and few things will convince a tank that his formerly okay weapon is no longer cutting the mustard than looking at grey-out incite buttons, greyed-out taunt buttons, the back of a mob and the plunging hitpoints of other members of your group.
But, until that happens, weapon X will work.
When I'm trying out some weapon I do try to check with groupmates I know well. I want to make sure that I'm not standing there tanking, thinking, "heh, this weapon holds aggro okay" while what's really going on is that my groupmates are having to throttle back what they do *because* I'm using some new weapon.
You can also sort of ninja test a weapon if you notice that you're in a pick-up group with folks who are better geared/more advanced than you are. If you hold aggro okay with your "regular" set up, you can switch in your test weapon; if they gank your aggro when you're using weapon X, it's a good bet your regular group is going to be giving you the same problems once they get to that gear/power level.
