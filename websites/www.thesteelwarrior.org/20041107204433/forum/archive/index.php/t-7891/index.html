rysingsun1
here's what to expect from a warrior with good agro setup:

1) if he pulls with arrow and incites on the inbound the mob can be consistently slowed approximately one second after contact without drawing agro. if this is not the case then he is probably not inciting on the pull.

2) even if he does so he will soon lose agro if he gets no procs. this is why most warriors insist on 4 stun/anger procs.

3) a full minute into a fight agro is difficult to draw off a properly setup warrior. if you can easily draw agro that long into a fight he has a poor agro setup. MOST fights you can start chain nuking long before that. but its dependent on whether he procs and on how much offensive/taunt aa he has.

4) if anyone besides the puller frequently gets hit before the warrior than that person probably got hit before incite was used. even undead proximity agro will rarely override an incite.

5) it entirely possible its taking him several seconds after contact to turn attack mode on. such a situation will cause any debuffer to draw agro in spite of an incite. once the caster gets agro if this same warrior is slow to reposition himself on the mob it can ping pong for a long time.

so my suspicion is that the warriors you speak of either are not inciting they have poor positioning skills or reaction time or they dont have good agro procs. obviously you dont want to go into a fruitless crusade to set straight every agro-poor warrior you ever group with. asking them meekly to incite on the pull will probably get you the most improvement for the amount of faction hit you could take with them. telling them to abandon their split blade will likely land you a bigger faction hit. but if you group with them regularly and they appear to be open minded i would say the advice to give is to abandon any weapon without a high agro innate proc and to exhort them to work toward ldon anger procs.

by the way there is one almost foolproof way to find out if they are inciting on an ldon (assuming they are mt) without asking them. if they use offensive disciplines a lot they are probably not inciting much. reason this is true is there is not enough endurance regeneration to support an incite on every pull AND a lot of offensive disciplines.

For what it's worth -- and I'd agree it might not be worth much -- my own unscientific observations match Haass's: I'd rather hit the mob before using the incite line.
According to the theory I have in my head, what *should* work best is to incite at maximum range, since the sooner you incite the sooner the post-initial hate of the incite can start ticking away. But that runs counter to my experience. If I tag incoming mob with arrow, autoattack & slam, then use incite I *appear* to get better results. And if I"m pulling, I'd always tag mob with arrow for initial aggro then hit incite. You can shoot a mob with an arrow and still have time to incite, but often if you pull with incite initially you don't have time to shoot the arrow before the mob's on you.
The only reason doing melee damage initially might work better is if for some reason initial melee damage and aggro is "seen" differently from pure hate damage. I don't have any actual evidence of that. But that 's the way it looks to me.

If the original poster is never seeing the incite-line message when he's with the problem tank, I'm suspicious of the tank. Yes, if the tank is pulling and using incite on the pull, you might not see the first message. And, yes, if the incite refreshes late in the fight and the tank has solid aggro, then I can see the tank saving it for the next mob. But I can't see the tank never having incite available if fight goes on for a while and the tank does not have solid aggro.

The other thing is the possibility that the "good" tanks the original poster plays with have a significant AA and possibly even a level advantage over the tank that's having problems. If you are a caster and get used to the aggro generation capacity of an advanced tank, you could be in for a surprise when you have to throttle back for a less AA'd tank (an effect that can also be heightened by gear differences).

As for trying to tell someone how to play their class without offending them, heh. Very difficult. My shaman alt has on a couple of occasions been able to successfully give non-offensive advice to warriors, but only after warrior asked a class-specific question and I was able to establish that I knew what I was talking about by precisely and accurately answering that question in a tell. Then, as an aside ("it can also help when the MT does X early in the fight....") I can mention something that I think the tank should be doing right now.
