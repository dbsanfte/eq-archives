This analysis deals SPECIFICALLY with the part of the game known as "The Planes of Power", and as such, with the exception of portal stones in PoKnowledge, is limited to the "high level" of the game. If you have a desire to discuss non-PoP, or general warrior things, then please do so on your own thread.

The State of Warriors in PoP:

It can be said that warriors focus on three areas. Melee damage output, Melee Damage absorption, and The generation and sustenance of an opponent's attention (aggro).

Melee Damage Output:

Planes of Power has been *somewhat* kind to warriors in this regard. By not having the ridiculous AC on opponents, melee damage seems to be meaningful when compared to the previous expansion.

The 50's: Damage output is fairly good, and many new weapons are available at relatively decent pricing from the Bazaar. With a minimal investment, warriors seem to be doing just fine in the DPS department.

There is indeed a drawback to this, and it's the matter called "mudflation". What mudflation is, is the increasing power of weaponry to "the masses" which obsoletes previous choices and gear. The reason that "mudflation" has become particularly problematic in the Planes of Power is because the influx of weaponry seems to follow a fascinating trend, which is that there are very few droppable pieces of gear in the early planes which a member of a group hunting there would put on. All "worthy" gear seems to come from areas which are 2 tiers higher than where people are hunting for experience. This cheapens the experience of hunting, from one of multiple rewards, in both experience and equippable items, to one of the acquisition of experience points only.

This problem is significant, one of the most problematic areas in PoP in my view. Let me say it in a different way.

I hunted in Sebilis, and in Howling Stones when I was getting to 60. There was itemization, and experience. You had the "double reward". The thing was, there were items in those zones which you were WILLING to put on. PoP loot has been *extremely* problematic in this regard. Generally speaking, items which you have acquired have been found in Tier 3 zones, and by the time you were fighting there, the items that dropped (and quite rarely at that), we in essence auotmatically "bazaar trinkets" to you. They are almost NEVER equipped by those hunting there. They make their way to the Bazaar, where people who WOULD make good use of them grab them. These people are in generally in the mid 50's, and they immediately benefit from those items. On the other hand, what it THEN does is places a standard on their next upgrade. They know at this point that in order to even DUPLICATE what they have, they'll have to level throughout the planes into Tier 3 and still won't be rewarded, because they already HAVE the reward from that plane. It then turns the experience of Everquest into nothing more than advancing the little blue bar and yellow bar of experience.

60-65 DPS: It's good, perhaps the biggest problem being the gap in gear, and lack of upgrades available without raiding. Two things happen in this level range. Your level vs. mob level goes up, and you gain 2 damage bonus increases (one at 61 I think and one at 64). Again, DPS overall is quite acceptable though with the exception of weapon availability.

A word on the lack of upgrades without raiding: This is very important to a warrior in particular because it is universally accepted that warriors are the most equipment dependent class in EQ. Raiding should *always* grant the biggest payoff, but the alternative to raiding shouldn't be *no* payoff, but a smaller one.

65+AA DPS: Warriors are *not* hurting at all in this area. We do substantial damage, and aside from some itemization things and the viability of dual wield (which does need to be looked at and fixed), we do very well, particularly raiding warriors. Most warriors are quite happy with their damage output.

In relation to other classes from a Damage Output perspective:

Warrior vs. Rogue: We seem to be ok

Warrior vs. monk: Monks are a little weak at the moment in relation to warriors, and should have some sort of DPS output improvement.

Warrior vs. Ranger: Seems to be Ok and acceptable

Warrior Vs. Knight: Knights are slightly too high in relation to warriors, there needs to be a more clear difference between the two. Also, the itemization favoritism toward knights is simply wrong to the point of humorous.

Warrior vs. Beastlord: Seems reasonable

Warrior vs. Caster: Seems reasonable

Damage Absorption:

The biggest problem area here is warriors' damage absorption in relation to other classes. They're not *enough* better to compensate for their clear weakenesses. This area cannot really be undererstimated.

The reason that warriors were so far ahead of the other "tanks" in Kunark and early velious was two reasons.

1. The cost of Complete heal was higher than the cost of ancillary heals.

2. Mana was finite and med times were a reality for groups.

With cleric mana being not an issue with the advent of KEI+ and FT, the argument of tanking efficiency is out the window. The only reason for tanking efficiency to be a big deal is to conserve a finite amount of mana, but mana isn't really that finite any more. Because of this, durability differences being not very large pale in comparison to other benefits such as aggro control, ancillary abilities (such as infusing your paty members with mana for even LESS downtime).

On the whole, warriors should have a distinctly larger melee damage absorption advantage over others than they currently do. The point of difference should be noticeable, not a little.

The Itemization Aspect:

Warrior only armor pieces offer BARELY greater protection than their Knight Counterparts, while the knight counterparts gain HUGE pluses in terms of manapools, which is the source of their power. This is... as simply put as I can say... totally unfair to warriors, and needs to be addressed. If I'm two dimensional, and my competitive counterpart is 3-dimensional, if the sum of my two dimensions is far behind the sum of his three, then he has an unfair advantage over me and this is wrong. Also, if the ancillary pieces available to my competitor in every slot are identical to those available to me, then the only difference is in the class only armors, where my "advantage" is swallowed up and overshadowed by his "advantages"... there NEEDS to be a greater defensive spread between warriors and all others.

Taunt:

Simply put, this is the most screwed up area of EQ. Warriors are a relic of the past because they cannot reliably and AT WILL get and maintain aggro, while their competitive counterparts CAN with no cost other than a little mana, which in a world of KEI, is again, infinite.

Notice please, I am not saying that it's broken because warriors and their groups have to work at aggro, but because they do AND their competitive counterparts DONT.

Balance it, fix it, it MUST be done, it's screwed up beyond measure. All other areas of being a warrior are meaningless if the warrior is perceived as not being able to perform this basic task while others are. Either bring the competitiors down to our level, or bring us up to theirs, I dont' really care. i suppose I'd prefer the others were brought down, as it wouldn't make the game even more boring. Whatever it takes FIX IT.

The mitigation advantage is VAPORIZED due to the aggro imbalance, because a warrior has to tank unslowed mobs for longer periods of time, and because with the cost of ancillary heals (Sup Remedy, Sup Elixir, and Sup Light) are either the SAME or GREATER mana cost than complete heal, making a warrior a LESS efficient tank, not only from a safety perspective, but also for a mana cost per hour perspective.

I think what the big problem that people aren't seeing is this. You're right in some regards. Aggro has historically been a strong point among knights, and a fairly weak point among warriors. This was made up for though historically, with a rather large spread in tankability between knights and warriors, as well as a rather large DPS spread between the two. Complete heal used to be a fairly expensive spell when mana pools were much smaller than they are now, and mana regen wasn't nearly what it is today. Finally, the dire need for a mob to be focused on a single tank wasn't as crucial as it is today because of environmental reasons (i.e. encounter design). It didnt' used to be that when one party member got hit, that it would cost the same mana as complete heal (supernal remedy and elixir), or in some cases MORE (Supernal Light), and oom was a reality as well.

The difference in tankability is no longer wide. As a matter of fact, the difference in tankability is SO small that there's many cases when a Paladin has more HP/AC yadda yadda than a warrior because of equipment. What this means is that there's no real inherent "class" advantage, but merely a "class advantage when at equal levels of equipment". In other words "A warrior" isn't inherently better at tanking than "a paladin", but disclaimers must be placed on it. "A warrior in equivalent level gear is somewhat better at absorbing damage than a paladin as long as he or she can get and hold aggro".

The act of the matter is that many of my guild's paladins are significantly better at taking damage than many warriors on my server. There's no inherent "class benefit". There's the HP/Sta calculation and the avoidance skill level difference. The avoidance skill level difference has been shown to mean thata warrior gets hit approximately 1.5% less often than a knight. That's one and a half hits difference out of 100.

What that does NOT compensate for is a multitude of ways to prevent melee damage from occuring (stuns) nor heals, multiple "get out of jail free cards" (DA/LoH), nor heals, group heals which fit PERFECTLY with Divine Arbitration, and so on and so forth, or Infusing your party with mana multiple times per fight, FD, and so on and so forth along with PERFECT aggro control.

What I'm getting at is that in "the tradeoff" we're getting ripped off. What we have in terms of "advantage" (a hp/sta calculation and 1.5 less hits out of 100) does NOT compensate with what either knight class has, and to be utterly crippled in "at will" aggro generation makes it even worse.

No, I dont' think anyone's arguing for warriors being "the best" at both tanking and controlling aggro, I think what we want is parity. We want to have a "total package" which is equal to the "total package" that our competitive counterparts bring.

Monk - DPS, Feign Death
Rogue - DPS, Shroud of Stealth
Warrior - Tanking

The Warrior in this example does one thing. He is unrivalled at tanking, though others can do it well enough for all intents and purposes.

The Monk in this example does two things. He is middle of the road for DPS relative to the rest, and Feign Death is of questionable value to a group, particularly since anyone can pull for xp in PoP.

The Rogue in this example does two things. He is near the upper end of DPS, although still behind a few of the other DPS classes. SoS is of questionable value to a PoP xp group.

All in all, pure melees need to bring more to an xp group than they do today.
