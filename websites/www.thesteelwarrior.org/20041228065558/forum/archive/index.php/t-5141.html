Personally, I taunt...if successful, I then kick, attack, then disarm. If the taunt is not successful, or if the mob still turns from me after mez is off, then I use incite to try and get the mob back to me. Often, however, I'll get a stun from the kick then time enough to generate enough aggro without Incite.

Also, occassionally, when we're dealing with difficult mobs in camp, even after crowd control is established, I'll briefly target off mobs and hit them with Incite if it's charged to begin the aggro building process...and let the hate...flow...

Given no other overriding concerns, I will generally attempt to taunt a mob 3 times if I don't get a success. The little bit of extra time is worth it if you can avoid going through rezzing the chanter and all that that entails.

Peeling off and attacking a second mob is extremely situational. Who are they attacking? If they're on a Shaman who's trying to root park them, then the shaman can take a few hits - I'll give them some time to try and do their job but keep on eye on their hitpoints. Secondly - who are the other melees? - if they are capable of occupying the original mob and taking some damage for a while, then you have more freedom to go help someone else. And lastly, what's the impact on the group? I like to keep everyone alive if possible, but, in general, letting your cleric die is going to have a lot more impact than losing a chanter or a mage, for example, so I may take additional risks to save the cleric.

If there is a second mob rooted or mezzed, I will always peel off the first one as soon as it runs (unless it's unsnared and is too close to other mobs), and begin to deal with the second one.

Finally - always have a plan. When I'm in pick-up groups (and I'm always in pick-up groups) where mez MAY not be available for whatever reason, I always make one of the first things I ask 'what's the plan for adds?' You'd be surprised (or maybe you wouldn't) how much it can help just having everyone aware of the general plan, and how often it appears that no one really thought much about this until it was asked.

1) Breaking mez
As has been said, it depends. Ideally, you'd taunt until you get a successful taunt, then break mez with attacks. Poof, you have aggro. If you toss in your berate after a successful taunt, you probably won't lose aggro at all until the mob dies. If you don't want to wait the extra 6 seconds each time a taunt fails, a quick risk evaluation is in order. Is the enchanter/bard going to able to take a few hits while you try to regain aggro? The answer invariably depends on the damage output of the mob in question, and how well equipped said mezzer is. In normal xp groups like in BoT for example a reasonably equipped bard can take a few hits, as could an enchanter with a fresh rune - but you really don't want this to happen. The extra time is worth it when the mobs hit really hard. In a normal LDoN I will generally just taunt once, hit incite, and attack. If taunt fails, I'll wait a couple seconds while incite ticks, and then break. Sometimes this works, sometimes it doesn't but in a normal LDoN there isn't a lot of risk for the most part. In summary, wait for a successful taunt, optionally use berate, then attack and you're pretty much guaranteed aggro. When time is of the essence, analyze the risk before making a decision.

2) Changing targets
Changing targets once you've engaged is almost always a no-no, especially when you have a chanter or a bard on crowd control. Let them do their jobs. You'll likely just confuse the people that use you for /assist, break mezzes, and get people killed. Even lacking a mezzer, you will almost always have at least one class that can root-park and/or snare kite an add should the situation require it. As Xenephex mentioned, the best strategy is to know what you're going to do BEFORE it happens. Make sure everyone knows what their roles are, and what everyone else's roles are. The wizard may not know that he's expected to be on root detail, or the necro might not realize that you need him to snare kite an add unless it's already been established. Like you noted, switching targets to engage a priority target, like an unmezzable/unrootable/whatever is acceptable, but everyone has to KNOW. This is a hotkey I always have on my first page:
/g I am engaging [ %T ] - Please assist me NOW
/rs I am engaging [ %T ] - Please assist me NOW
Do whatever suits you, but if you HAVE TO change targets don't do it without telling your group.

3) Weapons
Your weapons sound fine. They should last you a long while.

4) Toggle targets
In options/keyboard/targeting you can bind whichever key you like to 'Toggle last two targets' or whatever it's called. Myself, I have it bound to CAPSLOCK. I use WADS for movement, Q to target the nearest NPC, E to target the nearest PC, and TAB to toggle between myself and my last target. Bind the toggle key to whatever is most convenient to you. It only works for two targets - the last two that you have actually targeted whether you acquired the targets by mouse, nearest NPC key, /assist, or whatever. It's a useful key. Bind it to something that you won't have to search for, and play around with it.

5) /Shield
Essentially this takes 50% of the damage that a mob was going to do to another PC, and applies 75% of the original damage to you. Say a mob swings at your shaman for what would have been 200 damage. Since the shaman is being shielded by you, he only takes 100 points of damage, while you take 150 points of damage. This can be a useful skill for saving the hides of your more fragile partners. Personally, I use /shield every time taunt fails, assuming the skill is available at the moment. My hotkey is like this:
/assist
/pause 4
/shield
This will assist the mob that I just tried to taunt. The '/pause 4' is there only to compensate for the slight lag that the /assist sometimes takes to actually switch targets. Once /shield goes off, hit your toggle-last-two-targets key, and get back on the taunting train. As has been said, disciplines and /shield don't stack. I believe the message it uses in-game is something like, "Neither parties in a /shield link may be using a discipline."

Hope some of this is useful.
