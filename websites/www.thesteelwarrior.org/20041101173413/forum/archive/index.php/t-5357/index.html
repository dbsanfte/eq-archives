Totally 100% innacurate.
I have a Blade of War (2H not a Time weapon) with Anger on it.

Now as to the original question... Really hard choice.

The augmented dual wield combo should last you for a long long time.
And provide superior agro.
And the agro gap will widen in favor of the dual wield as your dex raises.

With only 150 dex, most of the agro comes from you swinging at mobs.
Not the procs (since they are very rare).

When your dex raises, your proc rate will go up.
The "Agro Per swing" will however remain the same.
While the "Proc agro" will almost double at 305 dex.

That leads me to believe, that if atm with a really low low proc rate
the dual wield combo is better (you say it was)
With a normal proc rate, the dual wield combo will outperform the 2H.
Since you will be optimising dual wield advantage (more procs).

So the question i ask you ...
Want the best agro ? Go back to dual wield + Augments & slowly raise your dex
Want good easy agro ? Stick Ykesha or Anger2/3 on the GsoT

If a 2x 15/20 with no procs will generate the same agro as 30/30 with no procs.
Adding procs to the equation, can only favor Dual Wield.
Because DualWield, will by nature proc more.

Khazzuz
I use the CHoS/SoTB combo, mainhanding the hammer. My original intent was to augment both with AngerII procs, mostly because I considered 2 procs of 400 hate to be better than one proc of 600 hate for a lesser amount of points. I'm sure someone would like to disagree with that but that was my feeling. I augmented my CHoS with AngerII and found that the three procs I now had along with incite was in general plenty of aggro for most situations I can come across. I still haven't augmented the SoTB figuring I can afford to spend my points in better places. The difference with the single AngerII proc was however night and day. So I guess my answer to the question is one AngerII proc is more than likely sufficient for someone who is guildless and probably doesn't do more than LDoN or doesn't expect to go past tier3 PoP in the near future. My advice would be to slap the AngerII on the hammer, use it for awhile, and if you still feel like you need more aggro, then you can purchase a second augment.

As far as the riposte is concerned, I still carry the GSoT around in my bags. One thing I've noticed about the DW combo is it is far superior in terms of the initial aggro. My biggest problem, even with incite, when using the GSoT is the short term aggro when the mob is getting slowed. If I find I'm in a situation where riposte is eating me alive over the course of a battle I DW through the slow, establish solid aggro, then swap in the GSoT to save the healers some mana. This is mostly of course in PoP or when tanking raid style named.
