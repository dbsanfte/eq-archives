Payn
Basically take 6x where x=base damage and add the dmg bonus. That's how much you can hit for max. Then factor in the delay, that's how you can get ratio.

No haste:

skarlon (50/40) = 8.525
exe axe (61/50) = 8.34 w/o haste proc, 11.12 w/haste proc
mith2h (48/40 effectively 48/28 with 41% haste) = 11.75

Let's say you have 36% haste worn:

skarlon = 10.75
exe axe = 11.36 w/o haste proc, 15.5 w/haste proc
mith2h = 11.75 (sword haste of 41% overwrites 36%)

So with max haste (100%) you get:

skarlon = 17.05
exe axe = 16.68
mith2h = 16.45

and with bard overhaste the ratios scale accordingly.

Skarlon is apparently the best for dps and the Faerune effect certainly is nice as well :)

Correct me please if I'm way off :D

Gront
With a 36% haste item, you're still best off with the Exe Axe unless you have spell haste on you. It doesn't have to proc very often to be better than the 5% better haste on the Mith2h.

With max haste (you have a 36%+ haste item, and a top haste spell on you), Skarlon > Exe Axe > Mith2h. With only item haste, Exe Axe > Mith2h > Skarlon. With no haste, Mith2h > Skarlon > Exe Axe.

Btw, I prefer to compare weapons by modal (most common) damage: (2*damage + bonus)/ratio. However, these weapons are all close enough in delay that the order that results is the same.
