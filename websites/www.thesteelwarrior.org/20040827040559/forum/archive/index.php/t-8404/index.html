well I am not able to answer all your question, so I will give you only the few I know :

* how to know which motherboard you have ?
---> it's writen on it : for exemple on mine it's writen MS-6330 v1.0C this stend for a MSI MB for AMD thta you can check here (http://www.msicomputer.com/product/p_spec.asp?model=K7T_Turbo_Lite3) (yep it's danm old too). the detailed spicificities of your MB is given on the net to know if you really have to change it when you upgrade your stuff.

* How do I check my RAM, and what does it mean?
---> there is a note glued on it. mine is 256MB PC 133 SDRAM. 256Mb is obviously the size, PC 133 is the freq allowed (100 or 133 Mhz bus speed : which is the speed of your MB ~> see MB web page for tolerances), SDRAM means its a Simple Double canal Ram ie there is 1x128 connection. there was RAM EDO (64 connection), then 128 connection thus Double RAM = DRAM, then 2x128 connection (two paralell row of 128 connections) thus DDRAM. at this point DRAM has been called SDRAM, the fisrt letter S or D stend s for SIMM and DIMM.

* How do I check the RPM of my hard drive?
---> I don't know, try to get you HD is your hands to look on top of it for its serial number and try to find the web page of its manufacturer to get its RPM, size, Buff size (more important than the two previous), canal speed (100Mz, 250Mhz,...) and canal type (EDO, ATA, RAID, ...).

Arathena
One of the best things you can probably do is find a small to mid sized local shop that build custom computers, and have a long chat with a sales tech before you buy anything, especially of the technology labels confuse you. Make sure it's a mom-and-pop type shop, and you can probably get someone who at least knows that you need to put tab A in slot B. The big advantage to this is that you can take it back in a 15 minute drive, and you can talk face to face with someone who knows what they're doing. It'll be more expensive than piecing things together off a dozen vendors, but the much lower aggrivation is -WELL- worth fifty bucks if you've got a good shop.
