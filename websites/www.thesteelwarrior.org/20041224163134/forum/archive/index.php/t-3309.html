A system like this would allow SoE to tailor items for pure melees in general and then use multipliers to custom fit them to each class. It would also allow them to do the same thing to casters and hybrids. Let's take it a step further and look at what they could do with multiple modifiers.

My system included the method already outlined in addition to categorizing the effects on equipment and instead of putting a specific effect on it, you assign a generic type to it which is defined by your class. Here's an example of what I mean.

Say the devs make a BP with 75 AC 150 hps 150 mana 10 to all stats and resists vengeance 8 and mana preservation 4. This obviously is a good BP for any class but is by far the most beneficial to a hybrid. Let's say you want that BP to automatically customize itself to any class that puts it on. First we assign some base values to it.

Say 50 AC 100 hps 100 mana 10 to all stats and resists and a type 4 effect

Now we give multiples to each class and define what type 4 means for each class.

Warriors 2xAC, 1.75xhps, 0xmana, 1.5xmelee stats, 0.5xcaster stats, 1.2xMR, 0.9xother resists, type 4 for warriors = Vengeance 8 + 4 worn DS

Paladins 1.8xAC, 1.5xhps, 1.3xmana, 1.4xmelee stats, 1.2xcaster stats, 1xall resists, type 4 for paladins = vengeance 8 + mana preservation 4

Monks 1.5xAC, 1.4xhps, 0xmana, 1.5xmelee stats, 0.5xcaster stats, 1xall resists, type 4 for monks = Vengeance 10 + 10% extra flying kick damage

Wizards 0.6xAC, 1.0xhps, 2xmana 0.5x melee stats, 1.5x caster stats, 1.2xFR/CR, 0.9xother resists, type 4 for wizards = FT 4 + 20% reduction in aggro from spells

That would allow that 1 BP to have the following effect for just the classes I listed:

Warriors: 100 AC, 175 hps, 0 mana, 15 melee stats, 5 caster stats, 12MR, 9FR/CR/DR/PR, Vengeance 8 + 4 worn DS

Paladins: 90 AC, 150 hps, 130 mana, 14 melee stats, 12 caster stats, 10 resist all, Vengeance 8 + mana preservation 4

Monks: 60 AC, 140 hps, 0 mana, 15 melee stats, 5 caster stats, 10 resist all, Vengeance 10 + 10% extra flying kick damage

Wizards: 30 AC, 100 hps, 200 mana, 5 melee stats, 15 caster stats, 12 FR/CR, 9 MR/DR/PR, FT 4 + 20% reduction in aggro from spells

To some extent they're already doing this with augmentations. It doesn't adjust automatically to each class but you won't have a warrior sticking a mana augment on anything and you probably won't see a wizard sticking a vengeance aug on anything...
