News services are frequently sued in court for slander (the oral communication of false statements which harm an individual in their reputation or character) or libel (the written communication of false statements which harm an individual in their reputation or character, althougth it can also include non-written communication of a permenant nature such as pictures, graphs, and so forth).

Of course the truth is always a defense against charges of slander or libel, so as long as the news service can show that there was a reasonable expectation on their part that what they said or printed/produced was based on facts, the odds are extremely good that the 1st admendment "freedom of the press" rights will get them off. What it amounts to is that in order to successfully sue a news service, you have to show that the news service did not use reasonable judgement and had no reason to believe that the information their story was based on was true.
