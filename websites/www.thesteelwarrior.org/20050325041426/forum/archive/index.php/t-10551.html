This came up in a thread on the offical boards. Just for the record here, in case it comes up:

Average Damage Avoidance = (1 - dodge)(1 - parry)(1 - mitigation)

Mob hit rate is ignored or assumed to be constant between classes (if you make cross class compairisons).

As a note, since multiplication is associative, it dosen't matter if parry or dodge are checked first, the math comes out the same. Factoring in block is a non-trivial matter, since it can be activated via skill and mitigates an absolute (rather than relative) amount of damage. That is probably the next step.

Kailynn at 54, without shield avoids or mitigates 53.52% of incoming damage. With shield and in defensive mitigation rises to 65.91% of incoming.

There is an assumtions that defensive = 10% mitigation which I have not tested myself.

There was a bit of a storm over the fact that rogues appear to tank better, but the analisys did not inclue block, agro management, defensive stance, or that rogues are far mre vulnerable to damage spikes (due to high avoidance and low mitigation).

PS Excel is great for figuring ADR.

As Kailyn said, the term mitigate and avoid and their variants are, for lack of a better term, technical terms we use to describe precise mechanics in games like EQ and WoW. Mitigation is how hard (damaging) you are likely to be hit. Whereas, avoidance is used to describe how often you get hit.

Mitigation is generally better for tanks because it makes it easier for healers to time their spells in order to heal the tank before they die without wasting mana though each can be just as efficent over time. AC determines mitigation and as such tanks have the best (highest value) AC out of all the classes.
