Regarding EQ2, and power inflation in EQ1:

SOE wants to start a new world, from scratch, to fix all of the built-in balance problems, and to eliminate four years of mudflation. The players want to retain their friendships, and their investment (often quite considerable) in their characters.

But that investment is not primarily embodied in gear. If you stripped every character in the game of all items and plat tonight, by a week from today the top Time guilds would be back killing Time mobs, hardly the worse for wear. Hell, I could re-equip my character to nearly the same level he is now in something like two or three months, if I were serious about it, which is nothing compared to the time I've put into him in the past.

The investment is embodied in time (levels + AA) and organizational skill (Planes access and LDoN raid wins being the easiest measures).

So. Create EQ2. Forget about selling it as a separate game; sell it basically as an expansion to EQ1 ($30 or so, and covered by the same monthly fee you're already paying for EQ1). Let players play either, or both, as the spirit moves.

And when you create an EQ2 character, you get a number of bonus stat points (assuming stats mean anything in EQ2), or AA points (assuming there's an equivalent to AA points in EQ2) based on your EQ1 accomplishments. You know; an extra starting stat point for every level above 55 on your highest-level character, plus a stat point for every 5 AA, plus 10 stat points for Elemental plane access and 20 stat points for Time access, and 5 stat points for ever having one a Time-level LDoN raid. Some formula like that.

So that guilds can painlessly move themselves, part- or full-time, to EQ2. So that players benefit in the new game proportionately to their efforts in the old.

SOE's win? They get to move people to a new game, one that is more balanced, easier to maintain, more fun for players, and will retain more players longer.

The players' win? They get to painlessly try out a new game, stay somewhat leet if they were already leet, and incrementally persuade their friends to move over to the new game with them if they decide they want to stay.

Eventually, the bulk of players will have "voted with their feet" for either EQ1 or EQ2, and momentum will bring most of the rest along. If SOE has done a good job with EQ2, most players will eventually move over there. And if a few stay in EQ1? Consolidate servers some, cut back on support and new expansions, and wait for EQ1 to die a slow death by attrition. There's always EQ2 waiting for anyone who gets lonely in the "old world".

Just my $0.02,

The thing I always somewhat liked about the old EQ was that you could reasonably fit a very large amount of people into a very small space, IE: the entire 40+ crowd of every server into two zones, lower guk and solusek B.

There werent any other options, and although at times it seemed like it, it wasnt that bad really.

The zones were designed with multiple seperate camps per unit of space, the mobs were fairly easily seperated into different camps, the camps were of a somewhat progressive nature as far as the requirements to actually be there, IE: lvl 40s would be exping around the entrance, but you bring a 45 group in there and they go maybe fight down to the second level and exp there, bring some 50s in and they will work on getting some SMRs or something, bring in the real big dogs and they will go for the real live and undead bosses.

Seb was the same thing, 50s at the entrance maybe the bridges, 55s over at bonecaster or gang, 60s would be down at shrooms or juggs where the real rewards are in the zone, and bring enough 60s and maybe you could try trak.

Zones these days are relatively vanilla, and there are very few camps per unit of space, think plane of valor, everything on the vast space of land between the 2 water boundaries and before the castle remains unkilled ever, however the topside castle walls have frequent groups and so does OC or IC, the problem here is that these camps are all completely equal, there is really no group that can fight at one camp that cannot fight at another camp. No real progression in camps either, you gain nothing by camping IC over OC, maybe a chance at the sleepwalker but thats about it. This applies to all of POP exp zones that are tier 1 or 1.5 (valor/storms) for the most part.

BOT is somewhat in the line of the old seb/lguk in that weak groups will fight at the entrance, better groups might go in some and include some kriegers in their fighting, and better still groups would try their hands at the wing bosses, and bring enough people and maybe you could try a tower boss or two.

The problem in BOT is its relative to total lack of seperate camps, there is really nearly no reason to camp any side other than earth side unless there is a group already killing all of the earth side. Also no other side matches the easily pullable 4 kriegers to one camp, other wings barely have so many kriegers together as there is just in the earth side. If there were more kriegers in the other wings, then more people would be able to reasonably fit into BOT without feeling total suck.

The comparison between lguk and BOT then being that lower guk has probably 10 decent camps to support 10 decent groups and give them reasons to actually be there for anything other than exp, whereas BOT has only one such camp the mentioned earth kriegers.

Another POP zone that sucks is torment, there is not one single camp in this zone that offers anything other than exp for a single group, none, and if there is one somewhere that I dont know about then its either 1) insanely difficult to get to, not like high lvls clearing a few trash mobs meant for lower groups to get to the real camp, or 2) some very close guarded secret, and I mean very closely guarded, such that nobody ever heard of it outside of DEVs.

If the POP zones were designed like the old school dungeons with natural progression in mind then it wouldnt feel so much like you were just plain stuck in trash zones if you had no claim to one of the very few camps in a real zone.

If all pop tier 1 zones were made this way, I mean with a progression path that must be cleared to get there so that you just cant walk to the better camp, and increasing rewards as you progress through from one camp to the next, then these tier one zones would be wildly more popular than any other zones in all of POP as far as exping goes.

I dont want outdoor zones, kiting is bad, maybe one expansion zone designed for solo dru/wiz that is akin to POV to keep them from trying to kite in a real expansion progression zone. I want to pull the lvl 50 mobs at entrance, move in some, then pull the 52s, then the 54s, then the 56s, once I get to this point I want a large amount of branches to go out, each to seperate camps, one camp per branch, the branches will then have trash mobs be 58 at the front, and 60 for a mini camp in each branch that gives either spell or item rewards that are useable for anyone fighting here, then I want one big room with one big lvl 65 boss with two lvl 62 guards, and two guardian bosses which are lvl 63 with lvl 60 guards, all 3 named should be linked with their guards in such a way that no guard or named can ever be split from the pack of 3, if one is fought they must all be fought together, mezzing is fine for the boss guards but not the boss. All three bosses have a decent sized loot table, with approximately 6 items droppable, two for each class type, tank, priest, or caster. Such that at each big boss room there is 6 possible and USEFUL items for a group that goes there, and if a group were to go through the whole zone and clear every mob maybe 3 or 4 times then they should have a really good basic set of equipment made up to start the next bigger set of dungeons.

Killing the lvl 65 bosses in each branch would then flag you for the next higher tier of the same type of dungeon layout (but wildly different mob types and scenery).

There would follow a sort of dungeon to dungeon progression, with say 3 tier 1 dungeons, and 3 tier 2's, and 3 tier 3's, etc.

Thats what I want to have in EQ again.
