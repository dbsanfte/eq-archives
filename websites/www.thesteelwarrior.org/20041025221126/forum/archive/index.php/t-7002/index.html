Brabble
Since the last patch, I've been spending as little time as possible in PoK (outrageous zone-specific lag, crashing, computer lock-ups). I'm not saying the zone is bugged, but when I want to play the game, I stay the heck out of there.
Long ago, back when such things mattered, I got to ally in Qeynos, and so I've been banking and selling and tradeskilling there and in Halas.
The other day I was in South Qeynos and no one else was there. The zone, of course, was lag-free, and I was struck by the care that had gone into the design of the place, the weird bits of npc dialog, the texture of the buildings, and so forth. I thought about how much of the game I haven't really played--I haven't done most of the quests in Qeynos, and I guess like everyone else I can't imagine doing them now, as they are impossible to do if you can use the reward, and completely without reward if you are high enough to finish them.
And yet, especially considering how long ago this zone was designed, someone really worked hard on it. The design of it is far richer, for example, than PoK.
It's as if the game were not originally designed to be a rack-em-and-stack-em slasher movie filmed in colorless dungeon corridors.
And no one is there. It's like WK, except it's a major starting city for new characters. Or, anyway, it used to be. Back when there were new players.
Yeah, I knew on an intellectual level that this was going on. But I found it disturbing to actually be standing in Qeynos, completely alone, on one of the oldest servers in the game.
SONY's got bigger problems than whether or not they should nerf GoD mobs.

Unfortunately even VI didn't get to really polish things. I was most impressed by this game when my youngest son made a Halfling Druid. OMGoodness, Rivervale! The music! Guards in Zoro costumes (sometime later I saw Bristlebane and finally understood the tricksie nature of Halflings)! Noobie quests galore, selling Acorns, begging for a combine for those uber studded boots, Piranahs in the pond, MANGLER!!!! And a mandatory run through Kithcor to reach the greater world beyond. Oh my!

Kaladim, Kelethin, and Nariak had flavor, but not to my mind quite the same depth. That to me was the initial charm of EQ. A different world, a different atmosphere, and in some ways nearly a different game all due to your choice of class and hometown. Sadly, this aspect of the game soon disappeared in one size fits all raiding and a limitled number of single class quests that didn't take a guild to complete.

I've looked at the other games, but to my mind they don't have anything near the fit and finish of the original EQ.
