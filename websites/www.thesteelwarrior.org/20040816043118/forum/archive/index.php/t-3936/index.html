Howdy,

I have a very retarded idea to fix warriors. It's so retarded, in fact, that it's something the Sony Dev team might actually implement. But first, lets come up with some Universal truths here. Even if you completely hate my idea, I'd like everyone to accept my universal truths or atleast give your opinions on their "truism" so that it can help us come up with a better solution.

1. With itemization how it exists today, War, Pal, and SK are all well within 10% hp/ac. This is Good enough for any of the 3 tanks to take hits in groups/raids. Sony will not go back through from LDON augs to Velious loot and make adjustments, it's a pipe dream. Additionally, the length of time it would require to add new War only items from Velious to augs in order to artificially recreate the standard of War's being far ahead of other plate classes is just too much to be cost effective from a developers standpoint. Itemization will remain how it is. As much as you hate paladins/SKs complaining about the 'dark ages of kunark', it might be time for warriors to realize that the Greatly Superior HP/ac advantage myth is also a relic from kunark as well.

2. The Solution to the problem should leave Warriors, Shadowknights, and Paladins on equal footing for grouping. The decision to take 1 over the other should be simple, such as a need for a FD puller/extra dps in a certain camp, grab the SK, the extra healing provided by group/targetted heals, take the Pal, the extra durability and yes utility (Disciplines, shield), grab the warrior, etc. These extra advantages should be equally attractive for most areas. ie. Paladins should not be forced to only group in Undead zones due to SU3, however, even in Undead zones, they should not be so preferred over either SK or War that those 2 classes are LFG while groups wait for a paladin to log in.

3. The solution should not change the way warriors work at raids. As it exists now, boss mobs are at first tanked by warriors (with pals/SKs have other essential non MT roles in raids), at the point where the encounter becomes trivial/easier due to either smarter tactics, proliferation of that encounters loot to the raid (farming better gear), etc, Other tanks can MT the encounter. The solution should keep this standard. Therefore, the solution should not greatly increase the hps or mitigation of Warriors, which would either allow not-as-skilled/geared players to higher content faster, or would require significant retuning of boss mobs from HoT through LDON time lvl raids. not going to happen.

4. The Solution is not more HPs. It does not matter how many hps you have if the monster never hits you. It sucks, it really does, that hybrids get more benefit from a 200hp/mana item than melee's do, it really really does suck. But converting mana to hp for melees is not addressing the problem at hand. You could give that lvl 57 warrior tanking in PoI 30k hps and it still would not help them tank. Regardless of how many hps you have, it still requires 400 Mana for a cleric to heal 7,500 hps, yet to heal other members of the group (say the enc who got slow resisted) costs more than 400 mana. Even if warriors today are placed on a superior mitigation table than other plate classes, to such a degree that they take 50% less damage than either Pal or SK(better than perm defensive without the DPS reduction), it would be more mana efficient to Cheal the paladin twice instead of a Chealing the War once and Supernal Lighting the Enc once. Sony will not go back and retune the mana costs of all spells to make it any better, especially not with the Mana regeneration at the levels they are today.

5. players in general are going to continue forming the most efficient groups for exp/hour. It's just a fact. nerfing Paladin/SK Aggro lock will simply drive players to other ways to maximize their exp/hour. Making all plate classes as inefficient as warriors as they exist today will lead to AE groups, pet groups, etc, as the norm, which leaves all tanks LFG. When exp groups need a tank, Beastlords, who completely avoid the slow-on-inc aggro issue by providing the slow, will become the new best tank. You may already be seeing this now. The solution is not to nerf knights just like its not to nerf BLs. Lets face it. The game is dumbed down. It has been for a while. LDON didn't change that, it only reinforced it with lull pulling being the standard for your average retarded player to mow through normal slaughters like they were going out of style. The true differential between casual gamers, joe everyguy players, and ubers is still high level raids. Always have been. This is another reason why the warrior fix should not change how raiding works today. The art of aggro management still needs to exist, if only for raiding purposes.


With these 5 somewhat long winded truths, I propose my solution to plate tank balance:

raise the taunt cap of Paladins and Shadowknights to that of warriors. this places all plate tanks on the equal grounds as far as "riding aggro" when its necessary to do so.

Add a warrior skill hotkey, call it improved taunt, spit in the eye, or whatever. give it a range of 100 or so, and have it be a pass/fail skill check with a 95% chance of success when maxxed. Give it a refresh time of 6 seconds. The skill should be interruptable by being stunned. On success, this skill adds 400 or so hate to the warriors aggro.

Flag all raid level boss mobs as immune to this new warrior skill.

With this new snap aggro for warriors, groups should be able to slow on inc, heal, burn, etc, as well as they can with either a Pal or SK tanking. Its chance of failure/interrupt is about equal to the chance of a pal/sk spell being interrupted, which keeps them on equal grounds since this skill is "mana free" aggro. The difference between Snap aggro with Pal/SK and Warriors now is that Warriors can do it mana free every 6 seconds, while pal/SK can chain cast at the beginning of a fight for improved initial aggro.

By a careful combination of Taunt and improved Taunt/AE taunt, or Taunt and Stuns/heals, or Taunt and taps/dots/dds/direct aggro spells, Warriors, paladins, and Shadow Knights should all be capable tanks in Exp groups as far as both Aggro generation as well as damage mitigation.

Knights have always complained that if Warriors had snap aggro then they would be inferior, but would they really?

DPS tests show clearly that SK> War > Pal for damage
Itemization places all plates as capable of tanking exp groups, with Warriors 10% or so ahead.
Group utility functions place Pal/SKs > War for secondary jobs in addition to tanking (heals/taps/FD/paci pulling/loh/ht/etc).

In all honesty, if warriors had Snap aggro that was as reliable as knights today, I'd still prefer a paladin as MT if for nothing more than for group heals and LOH. But that's just me. I know plenty of people who will still swear by SKs as I do ppl who still swear by warriors. Giving warriors snap aggro will not make knights obsolete.
