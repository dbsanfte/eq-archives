For hards, non-time warrior's don't pull.

If you are able to tank singles in hards, then you can pull. It takes some time to learn the layout and how adds work, but this is what I do.

- Pre-target the mob you want mezzed. Tell your enchanter to assist for mezz target, and make sure they stand behind you when mezzing ( otherwise they'll get assist aggro on the lot of them ).

- Get a heal over time when pulling. Once your group gets used to this the cleric will do it automatically and not cause delays. This will buy you some time until that first heal gets off.

- Use your discs. If you get more than you can handle, don't hesitate to use a disc. You don't need to disc on named in LDoN, and defensive/stonewall has such a short refresh you can use it and have it ready for the next bad pull, assuming you don't do two in a short period.

- When all else fails use furious/fortitude. Save this for emergencies as you will probably only be able to do it once or twice per adventure.

- East Commonlands adventures are by far the easiest for a warrior to pull. Do them until you get used to the mechanics of a hard risk LDoN.
