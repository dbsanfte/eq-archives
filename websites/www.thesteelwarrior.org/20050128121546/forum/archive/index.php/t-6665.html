It's always seemed wrong to me to buy either pp or characters; in both cases you're paying money to avoid playing part of a game. But I could avoid playing for free, lol.
However, the reasons some people sell pp should be obvious. The reasons some people buy pp are a bit more complicated.
Scan the boards here for the answers that lvl 30 or 40 newbies get when they ask what armor or weapons they should be looking for. "Cultural" comes up all the time. Before the recommended level bug was fixed, we'd tell people to go buy CHoS. We'd advise warriors that by the time they were 45 they should have a 31% haste item. then someone else would say 31% sucked--41% would be better, lol. While it may be true that a lvl 60+ warrior can farm thousands of pp in a single day, that's just not going to happen for the vast majority of "first character" warriors, who are strikingly bad at soloing and at the same time heavily gear dependant.
I have a feeling that Pallies and SKs mains who hit their 50s have it almost as bad.
Of course, most toons in their 30s and 40s are alts on most servers. Pretty easy to hop on your main and farm a few thousand pp to twink the alt and then lvl as fast as you can. It seems logical enough to farm pp for your twinks or to do the buy/sell thing in the bazaar, until you realize that it's even faster to buy pp online.
Buying pp is a logical extension of powerleveling, twinking, refusing to level up in any but the highest-zem zones, and so forth.
A couple of months ago I decided that in my spare moments I'd play an alt, and that the alt would be untwinked. I had a troll SK mule (at one point all my warrior's mules were Pallies and SKs, since who better to hold a warrior's bags?).
It's been an eye-opening experience. First, I met a player who has gradually become one of the people I most respect in the game: he's been playing since 1999 and his highest lvl toon is 49. He spends almost all his time helping people and, obviously, almost none of it leveling (except when I can con him into fighting with me--he's an excellent player). He does what he does because he enjoys it and it makes the game more enjoyable for others. You don't see that every day.
The other thing I've noticed is that although I've purposely had to level very slowling in order to let the SK get his own gear (thank Inny for the Troll exp penalty!), his equipment is pretty marginal compared to just about everyone else because on my server just about everyone else is twinked. Fairly often after a while in a pick-up group, a player who is good enough to recognize that my gear is decidedly untwinked will offer to give me some piece of gear they have somewhere. Of course I refuse, but it's been very nice to see how many people will offer to help.
The last thing I've noticed is this: the last time I logged on the SK, within a couple of minutes, I had tells from five different non-guild people asking me to come play with them. I decided to spend my time helping the person on the CR who needed my "find corpse" spell. No exp, no plat, still not Uber. But I did have fun and I got a free SoW out of the deal (yep: dead toon was a ranger, lol).
And, no, I'm not really sorry for the length of this.
