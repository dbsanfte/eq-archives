After level 30, you kinda run out of high ZEM zones to level in. But I have been having fun PLing a couple clerics in ToFS lately. Steelrain,
A couple things about your post suggest to me that you're not twinked or being powerleveled. Also that this might be your first character, and you don't have lots of pp. If that's the case, then the following advice might be useful (and if it's not the case, just ignore me and eventually I'll go away):
1. Scrape together whatever cash you have and head for the bazaar before you go to PC. Do searches on all weapon categories (one-handers and two-handers) filtering for warrior and your race and the amount you can afford to spend. You'll have a damage cap in effect until you're level 20, but search around. You'll find something weapon cheap & better than fine steel.
2. If this is your first character, mix and match your hunting: experiment with indoor and outdoor zones. Learn the pathing characteristics, abilities, level ranges, spawn rates and locations of the mobs you hunt.
3. Group as often as you can stand it. Yes, you will meet many many annoying people this way, but you'll also meet a few very cool folks. And you'll learn how groups work and how they fail to work.
4. Get a bow & start making your own arrows. Learn to pull with it, especially in outdoor zones. (requires fletching skill: look at EQ Traders online for tradeskill info). For a long time range>damage for your bow as a pulling device. I'm suggesting this because two of the better places for the non-pl'd, non-guilded tank to get groups later will be the Overthere and the dreadlands. These zones do not have experience bonuses, but if you become known as a good puller, two things will happen: you won't have to wait more than two minutes for a group invite, and as puller you control the the rate at which mobs arrive in camp. These two things are important if you are in a position of having to rely on pick-up groups, because after you can no longer solo efficiently, the two practical factors that are most likely to slow your experience are: First, you can't get a group. Second, people in your group keep dying.
To get a group, you need to go to popular zones: Blackburrow, PC, the Aviaks in South K., High Hold Keep, the Overthere and the dreadlands, for example.
Many pick-up groups will have members who seem to think that the fastest way to get experience is to pull faster and faster. Actually, the fastest experience is to pull at the maximum rate that particular group can kill mobs without getting killed (death in pick up groups cause rez and re-grouping delays and often lead to the break-up of the group, all of which slow experience far more than anything else). A puller who can hit the risk/reward curve perfectly will be much in demand. Another reason to pull if you are untwinked is that as puller you'll hold agro better than if you try to take it from someone else pulling.
Soon--by level 16 or 18, for sure--start learning as much as you can about the issues your casters face. What buffs do they have? What spell components do they need? What problems do they face? Always do what you can to help your group fight better.
No matter what level or zone you fight in, one of the best ways to make a pick-up group fight better costs absolutely nothing: when you see someone do something good, compliment them in /g. It's often shocking how much more fun and effective a group is after a couple such compliments.
Finally, if you happen to be on Fennin Ro, let me know & I'll find you and lend you a hand.
