How else do explain no taunt fail message but not getting aggro?
If they have unnatural aggro, via being the puller, or the first to damage the mob, or are sitting, or are too close to the mob (or you're too far away), this can happen. There were taunt tests a while back that suggested Aggro is built up into two parts: Initial Aggro and modifications, and Pure Aggro (via spells, melee, procs). Taunt only checks one of them, so if someone else has a lot of aggro via pulling the mob with a damage spell or something, it will not be taunted.

Have a shammy or necro just PILE on the aggro sometime, and see how many successful taunts it will take to get aggro back. Or try to take aggro from a stunning pally :)
Both of these scenarios involve "aggro dotting". Casting a dot on a mob will continually add aggro -- and a taunt alone will NEVER take care of it. Chances are, you're taunting, getting aggro, then losing it immediately next tick when the dot damage is factored in. Paladins who are stunning, likewise, will undoubtedly get another stun in after your taunt and steal aggro back.

All taunt does is put you at Whoever_Has_Highest_Aggro + Small_Number, and makes it REAL easy to have your aggro stolen back.

I've had successful tuants not turn the mob to me a number of times, but it doesn't have anything to do with tuant not working I'm sure. It has to do with the other people beating on the mob and the slow moving EQ update packets. Tuant only puts you 1 point ahead of the top person on the list. That person's very next cleave puts them right back ahead of you if your melee isn't outdoing their melee. Since EQ doesn't send you packet updates every few milliseconds like a game of Quake does, you don't always get to see that the mob spun to you, then spun back to the other guy quickly. You just see the illusion of him facing the other guy through the whole ordeal.

That's where incite comes in. To give you an actual lead on the hate list above the person that was previously at the top of the list, once you get there yourself with a successful tuant.

A successful taunt won't get low HP aggro off. I have tried AE taunt enough times to know it doesn't work. The modifier is temporary so you get whoever is highest (probably yourself) + 1, and then it compare that against the low HP aggro person + ~10000 and hits the low HP aggro.

It's like how a successful taunt on a mezzed mob doesn't stop the mob from hitting an enchanter if they're sitting down, because you get Enchanter + 1 aggro, but the enchanter is higher because he has his aggro + sitting aggro, which is temporary.

Now, if you somehow had about 10000 more than the person low HP aggro, you could get it off him, but it's pretty unlikely in most situations.
