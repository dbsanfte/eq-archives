With some tanks in PoV, a cleric can start a CH at 25% in the fairly certain knowledge that it will land early. With others, you can start a CH at 60% and still be taking a risk.

PoV is a break point. Level 65 tanks with 100+ aa can still go there to earn big experience, and sometimes they do. Equally, level 55 tanks with 3aa and less than 5khp can also go there and sometimes they do. It takes a cleric a few critters to figure out how badly the tank's getting owned.

Equally, level 65 clerics with 180aa still go to PoV for exp; I did, yesterday. Level 55 clerics with 3aa can also go there. A higher end cleric can play it a bit cooler, because she'll have tools like Divine Arbitration or Supernal Light to cope with any sudden damage spike; a level 55 cleric has only one spell which can produce the hp/second to cope with a double 500 + proc, which is CH.

A high end cleric can compensate for a low end tank and vice versa, but lower-end tank + lower-end cleric against PoV critters is a total party wipeout waiting to happen.
