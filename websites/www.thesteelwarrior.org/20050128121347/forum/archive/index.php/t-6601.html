Another thing to consider, and I rewrote this to try and write it more clearly.

Like the others said, if you're using a dual EB/AngerIII combo and switch to a raw Blade of War, you're going to be experiencing a huge reduction in agro, or at least temporarily. If we were to compare a Blade of War to a Bloodfrenzy/Carnage combo without any augs or weapon affinity just like it was when PoP was still fresh, chances are the BF/BoC combo would outagro the BoW until certain AAs were attained by the warrior.

The question is, do you have your offensive AAs maxed yet? The truth is, and I can speak from experience, the Blade of War doesn't really shine until you get your offensive AAs maxed. It really delivers a huge amount of weapon agro with its 48/32 ratio, especially over time. When you start boxing in Flurry, Raging Flurry, Ferocity, and Punishing Blade, then the BoW will really start to shine.

My advice currently is this though. Since you already have a dual EB/AngerIII set up, it looks as if you're probably not having agro issues at the moment, so getting a Blade of War would be like trading out your 450hp 5.0 Mustang for a 405hp Z06. Yeah, one looks a hell of a lot better, but the fact is, both will drive you down the road at obliterating speeds and both will get you where you want to go. In the meantime, I would save your DKP for Raex slots or other snazzy elemental pieces. Once you've attained your offensive AAs and have another AngerIII aug in the bag, *then* I'd bid on the Blade of War. Without these, I think you'd be seriously let down in your expectations. The weapon is unbelievably amazing, but it's not "The Magic Answer". ;)

I also had BF BoC before my BoW. That was before ldon or GoD was out. Back then, BoW was far superior dps AND slightly better agro (imho). The difference was: With BF BoC you HAD to rely on procs to get solid agro and these procs were too rarely. High dps casters or very fast debuffers stole agro from you pretty frequently. With the BoW you got slightly better agro and the main advantage agro wise was: You had a pretty straight agro line. Regardless of procs, your ench knew he could tash after 3 secs and slow after 3 more secs. The procs were more like of a bonus than the main agro source.
BUT today:
-We have augs, increasing our proc rate by 100%
-We have WA, further increasing procrates by 50%
1h weapons profit 50% more of these changes, especially a bloodfrenzy.
Sinister strikes also adds some dps to 1h.

Today you usually have at least 1 proc within the first 5 secs and tons of procs over time.

However the BoW still has many advantages:
-Very high dps. Only highend EP / time weapons will deal out compareable or better damage. Adding your own riposte damage which become a real factor when duoing unslowed mobs or generally in expgroups.
-Relyable agro over time, regardless of procs.
-Fewer Ripostes. Yeah, imho: I dual wield EoE(yak) BF(A3) for agro EoE(yak) Stone etched mallet(none atm) for dps 95% of the time. I love the amazing agro of the first combo when fighting mobs that hit very fast for low ammounts of damage (<2k) but there are still mobs where ripostes can be pain in da ass: Quarm (slowable, VERY high maximum hits, where one round + one riposte can kill you with realtive ease), Ikkinz single group bosses (2200 quad, one riposte becomes a real factor when your cleric uses a ~4k heal), etc...

I think you don't have to regret your choice. I think i would have done the same (<-- weapon freak.), if no other MT with roughly the same stats like you was wielding some sucky weapons. (passed our first BoW on one of our MTs who was using HG+ epic (/shiver) when all others had BF BoC)
