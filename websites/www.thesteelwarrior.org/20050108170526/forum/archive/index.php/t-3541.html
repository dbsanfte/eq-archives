I think Bumamgar is partially correct. I have some Time gear and plenty of AA so I just have a good time doing adventures with my friends and leveling my twink while I get him enough points to buy a BP.

For the middle of the road people, LDoN actually could result in some very nice gear, much higher quality than would otherwise be available. However, you have to plan your missions and know what you are buying to make the most of your adventures. I think it requires too much planning due to having to have points in the specific mission type you are doing. I think the system would benefit greatly from a few changes to the way the difficulty is determined and the way vendor loot is purchased.

I would change:

1. Difficulty - I would make the difficulty level either a 1-5 or 1-10 choice. It would be simple enough to set a base line level 1 mission and a maximum difficulty mission, then set everything in between to be a % based on the min/max.

2. Loot - I would change the way you can purchase loot to be tiered. Rather than having to do adventures in a theme, you would have to do adventures of a particular difficulty. Points would work on lower tiered loot, but no upward. If you want the best items available on Tier 10, then you have to do difficulty 10 adventures to get those points. The loot would be level based so that the rewards available for doing difficulty 10 adventures at level 30 would not be the same as doing them at level 65. However, the points would be usable in the proper tier as you level.

3. Adventure points - all adventures would result in the same points for a win. The points earned would be the same for every adventure at every level, but they would be associated with the difficulty.

4. Loot cost - I would lower the cost of loot across the board with the biggest reduction at the lower levels of loot, with just a small reduction in the top level. Make a level 1 difficulty top item cost 800 points and a level 10 difficulty top item cost around 1200 points.
