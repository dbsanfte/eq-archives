Velk
01-21-2004, 10:04 PM
thought Flurry had a chance to trigger on a successful triple attack, and that that chance went up with the extra AA. Am I wrong?

Yes.

Flurry only works after a succesfull double attack, and has nothing to do with triple. Triple and flurry can both occur in a single round ( obviously with the respective rarity of triple and flurry both of them happening at the same time is a rare occurence ).

This is from extensive parsing of a slow 2handed weapon using flurry 6 with no punishing blade AA.

Evidence for only working on a double :

Single hit percentage didn't change from flurry 0 to flurry 6.
There were zero occurences of a triple attack with a flurry message.

Evidence of triple and flurry both occuring independently :

A small proportion of 5 hit attacks with flurry message ( compared to the large bulk of 4 hit attacks with flurry messages ) in roughly the same ratio as double -> triple attacks. ( roughly because we are talking about a miniscule fraction of attacks here ).

I just did two parses for proc rate, and included flurry information. It is posted in the proc rate thread, but the relavent information was:

Parse 1
Katta Banker from behind. Ornate Greatblade (2hs - delay 23).

No buffs. Worn 41% haste. 1286 Atk. 203 Dex. Parse length: 293.7 minutes (just under 5 hours)

PB 3, Ferocity 3, Flurry 1, Ingenuity 3.

94 Flurries (.32 flurry per minute)


Parse 2
Katta Banker from behind. Primal Velium Battle Axe (2hs - delay 44).

Only buff was avatar - I procced it before starting, so it was on constantly during the parse. Worn 41% haste. 1461 Atk. 286 Dex.

Parse length was 5 hours 41 minutes and 20 seconds.

PB 3, Ferocity 3, Flurry 1.

70 Flurries (.21 flurry per minute)

discussion
Flurry rate obviously scales with weapon delay, unlike procs. The more you swing, the more you will flurry.

If this was not the case, we would see similar flurry rates with the two weapons (or a higher rate on the primal 2hs if stats like dex matter). Instead, we see the Ornate Greatblade (much faster) having a much higher flurry per minute rate.

When I get a chance, I will go back through both logs and determine a "number of swings per flurry." If dex or the other stats that avatar boosts up matter, then the primal two hander should require fewer swings per flurry. If the stats don't matter, then I would expect the number of swings per flurry to be the same.
