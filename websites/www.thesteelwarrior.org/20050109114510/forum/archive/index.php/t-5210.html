Unclebong summed up zerkers pretty well with these two words... "offensive warrior."

DPS is higher than a warrior, aggro generation ability and tanking ability is lower than a warrior. With a tiny bit of utility added. But aside from the minor utility they possess, they are pretty much just an offensive version of a warrior.

HPs are about the same as Pal/SK. Obviously mitigation is nowhere near as good due to the chain armor.

My berserker is up to level 52 and I have been tanking for groups since I was a baby 'zerker.. but I see those days coming to an end. As it is now, I tank great in normal LDoNs... I'm OK as a tank in PoJ, but I'm more of a drain on cleric mana than a real tank. And in PoI I can tank in an emergency but I require too much healing to be an efficient tank in a group there. As I move to zones where the mobs hit harder and harder, the lack of mitigation will beome even more important. So, no need to worry about a 'zerker taking over your spot as tank in a group. We will most likely make good offtanks and emergency tanks when the real one dies - but not a threat to compete for the job.

As for aggro. I have been able to hold aggro without any real issues in every group I've been in that asked me to tank. The only trouble comes when breaking mezzes. One stun isn't enough, and I have no taunt key. And with a 12 second reuse time on the stun, who has time to wait around to try it again?

The other side of the aggro coin... I can also lose aggro as well.

Only thing i can say is that i would rather be grouped with a monk. They seem to have the same dps and ability to generate agro though the dps, but monks can fd while berserkers can't and just take a beating pretty bad.

You were playing with a lousy berserker. Berserkers get a "distracting strike" line for aggro reduction. It gives an immediate drop in aggro, and also reduces aggro over a duration. If you had a 'zerker constantly taking aggro from you, they weren't using this ability. Personally, if I am not tanking I use it early in the fight, and depending on the aggro generation of the group's tank, I refresh it as necessary. The only time I have ever had problems with unwanted aggro was one night in PoJ with a warrior who had no aggro procs (not even 1) and who did not use incite.

Also, the stuns DO generate aggro, but it doesn't seem to be on par with pally stun (I have a 52 berserker and a 54 pally, so I can compare pretty directly). It also seems that the snare is less aggro than ranger snare (based on the fact that when grouped with a ranger who was particularly forgetful about snaring mobs, I would snare them and never take aggro, and on the rare occasions when he did snare, he always got a love tap or two in return).
