Yet another warriors opinion….

I leveled my warrior to 38 up to around the time that Kunark was released. I then quit playing EQ because I noticed some disturbing trends having to do with my RL responsibilities / EQ play time ratio. Well, I’m back into EQ now and have been playing my warrior again for about 2 months.

It’s been interesting coming back to the game after a 2 year hiatus. Back then, warriors were THE tank, and paladins were an amusing class that you rolled if you wanted to solo or something. Now paladins are the uber tanks (funny how things change). I’ve had my own depressing experiences with several pallys getting groups while I sit around with my /lfg flag up, and it’s not fun. I’d consider rolling a paladin and starting over but I have no interest in going through the newbie grind again. Besides, I rolled a warrior because I wanted to be the beastly animal that picks his teeth with the bones of his victims, not the holier than thou pristine wuss that is a paladin (no offense to the pristine wusses out there).

Anyways, for what it’s worth, this is my perspective coming from someone who hasn’t gone through the progressive slide into mediocrity and ultimately coffee maker status that is the warrior, but rather had an abrupt, overnight wake up call.

Knights need to be nerfed, or at least their ability to generate aggro through stuns needs to be nerfed

There, I said it. I’ve lurked these boards for more hours than I care to think about, and don’t claim to be the expert that many of the old-timers here are, but this is the conclusion I’ve come to. All the many other well thought out ideas posted here are interesting, and would certainly make warriors more viable for groups, but I think they result in more mudflation of skills and abilities without addressing the real issue.

I believe the real issue is aggro-lock. This is the game breaking, boredom instilling, tactics eliminating monster that SoE created with the stun line of spells that paladins have. I don’t believe that those stuns are “working as intended” for pallys, I think they are just an unintended consequence, and SoE is terrified of pulling out the nerf bat, particularly with viable EQ alternatives on the horizon. Another issue is the fact that most other classes have come to take this ability of paladins for granted, and would raise bloody hell if they were forced to go back to real aggro management. All the aggro-proccing weapons out there are SoE’s attempt to nudge warriors back into balance with paladins, but given the nature of procs, they will never equal the instant-aggro abilities that paladins have that is most sought after by xp groups looking for the best xp / time ratio they can come up with.

I don’t see any other simple, viable, practical solution, and apparently neither does SoE, or they would have dealt with it by now. The aggro generating procs on weapons are an admitted band-aid fix by SoE while they try to TRULY resolve the issue.

Aggro produced by paladin stuns should be reduced (maybe by extended re-use times, I don’t really know) to put paladins on a level playing field with warriors. I’d be satisfied with that – putting pallys lower on the aggro generating list would go a long ways towards putting them back into irrelevant amusement status which I don’t believe anyone wants.

The balance would be maintained by the (admittedly not huge) utility advantage that paladins have with their spell book (post stun nerf), balanced by the slightly better mitigation / meatshield abilites of warriors and their albatross /defensive ability.

Itemization and the shrinking melee stat gap between warriors and paladins is puzzling to me, but I don’t think that’s nearly as big an issue as the aggro-generating stun issue, and to be honest with you I really don’t care that much, just so long as warriors can go back to at least equally viable status with paladins for tanking.

I know that this solution would not be liked by all (particularly paladins, of course), but I think it’s what needs to be done to not only fix warriors, but fix the larger issue of instant aggro-lock.

That’s my 2 cp worth. Feel free to call me an idiot if I’m borked here, but I don’t see any other solution.
