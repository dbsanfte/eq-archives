(Disclaimer: I was really angry when I posted this, so there's some fairly colorful language, but I took out the *most* colorful parts).

How does one test the effectiveness of taunt?

In order to do it, one must attempt taunt over a decent period of time, and have a mechanism which allows you to see if it's successful or not. In many areas, a successful taunt is verified by the following message:

"I'll teach you to interfere with me Target"

If your taunt fails, no message, if successful, message.

So, logging a couple of hours fighting against mobs who give this message can give you a reasonable gauge of how well the Taunt skill works.

In light of VI's exp revamp, this is an important thing, because a warrior in a group needs to be performing their function against even con mobs if they're to contribute to justify their group spot.
