So, I wasn't able to find any data on any boards about how much ferocity actually increased DPS, and naturally, I'm curious about such things, so I parsed it on ye olde katta banker. If you don't like me doing it on a green mob, I don't care, do it yourself. The parses are about an hour to an hour and a half long. If you think they're irrelevant because they're not 3 and a half months in duration, I don't care, do it yourself. I'm posting this for your and everyone elses benefit, don't bitch at me about my results or the way I chose to go about doing it because quite frankly, I don't give a damn. The results are valid for me, and that's all I care about. I just figured some people might like to know.

I have 2 weapon setups, Sceptre of Destruction/Copper Hammer of Striking and my Windblade for 2h. I took parses of each before I started on the Ferocity road. At that time I had Tactical mastery, Amibidex, Fury 3 and Flurry 2. All parses are done on the same mob from behind. All tests are done unbuffed, 36% haste.

First set, melee DPS only, Dual Wield setup. 58.77 DPS according to Yalp.

Dual wield Setup after Ferocity 3 -- 61.45 DPS. Net increase of approximately 4% DPS. Obviously sample set is only an hour long. Still, 4% DPS for 9 points seems to be the norm based upon accepted values of 4% DPS increase for Ambidex.

Second Set -- Windblade before Fero 59.03 DPS

Windblade post Fero 3 -- 60.95. Difference is approximately 3%.


Conclusions -- The only thing I can conclude from these parses is that Ferocity 3 is basically shit. Keeping my DPS within an acceptable margin of error is not what I'd call a very good AA skill. By the end of level 3 there should be a very clear difference between having it and not having it, and in my opinion, there's really not. I wasn't really expecting a whole lot, but this was less than even I expected. Perhaps some views will change on how important people view this AA, as all I see everywhere is "Man, you gotta get Ferocity and PB, those AA's are SWEET." This one definitely doesn't appear sweet.
