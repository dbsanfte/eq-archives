Throag
Try this :

Have monk first tag the RC
Have ranger get aggro with snare and bring it to camp
Warrior tag the mob with an arrow.
Now you have 2 choices : The monk FD -> ranger is on top of aggro list, and then isn't affected by banish and will NOT be banished but the warrior is 2nd on aggro list and on top of the "banish list" (since the monk is FD and ranger has aggro) and WILL get banished, exactly the same with rampage.
2nd choice : Monk does not FD, ranger is still first on aggro list and is not affected by banish, but monk is higher on the 'banish list' than the warrior (because he entered aggro list before the warrior) -> monk WILL get banish and warrior will not.

Try this and I can tell you it will happen that way, when we were still doing rathe I could predict with 100% certainty who was gonna get banished by following that logic.

You can believe that it's random and you cannot do anything to prevent banishing, but in that case, I can tell you you're wrong.

Pling
I have pulled about 150 coincilmen if you count all the wipes (and our 3 successes) and my estimation is that the puller is punted in about half the pulls, 40% is the snarer punted and 10% is someone else.

They usually punt early (in like 30 seconds) so my advice is to have 3-4 peeps get aggro on every pull BEFORE it reaches the Tank (example: bard, monk, sk-snare, necro-snare) and have one or two of these four be quite near the councilman on inc to get it to use up his punt on them rather than on the MT.

Never, never let a non-fd class aggro a councilman on inc. If a druid snares or chanter tash and gets aggro + punt, the coincilman will move towards the punted and that may bring it close to offtanks.

Rarely a offtanker will get punted, but it can happen if the time drags on, so be ready with some extra tankers.
