Now I've told you what issues are not important, here's one that is: SONY is under a certain kind of pressure that will get more pronounced over time. The pressure is this: the longer the game exists, the greater the "spread" in the advancement among characters/players, even characters who have put in a good deal of time. SONY has to have something for cutting edge people to do, and those zones/mobs have to take the top guilds some time to work through--at least enough time that developers can come up with the next thing. That's why flaging was thought up in the first place.
But eventually (and this possibly happened as early as PoP) keeping cutting edge folks busy will require content so difficult/time consuming that some other folks will likely never get there.
At that point, SONY has to start supporting at least two games--stuff for the strong guilds to do, and stuff for the "casuals" to do: and you get a bifurcation in expansions so that some are GoD/top-end and some are LDoN/casual. The problem is, of course, that unless SONY is clever (or tricky), casuals will see less and less reason to buy the GoD-type expansions, and raiders will see less and less utility in LDoN/casual expansions. Suddenly, SONY risks having to produce twice as many expansions (with twice the development and production costs and risks) to service the same population of customers.
Telling casual players that they should expect to see smaller and smaller percentages of some expansions (even after those expansions are "old") is basically an argument that casuals shouldn't buy the new expansions, which is basically an argument that SONY's revenue/cost ratio should go down, which is basically an argument that SONY should have fewer resources for developing new content, which is basically an argument that SONY should produce new content for raiders at a slower rate or with less careful design or with inadequate testing before an expansion goes live.

Thank goodness the inadequate testing thing hasn't happened yet! But be careful what you ask for.
