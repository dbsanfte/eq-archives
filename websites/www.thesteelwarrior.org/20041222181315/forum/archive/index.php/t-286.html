BBB works great.

Great for getting aggro, great for holding aggro.

I built my character around using 2h weapons, and bought offensive aa's specifically for the purpose of greater aggro control and they paid off. Many of the people who whine and cry their 2h's have no aggro on this board haven't so much as bought flurry from 3 expansions ago in luclin much less the multiple PoP offensive aa's availible to them, and still wonder why any other warrior will out perform them.

I have used my BBB to tank everything up to and including the Elemental Mini Bosses in PoP (excluding the actual gods themselves as I had since upgraded to a BoWar as of the time of my personal engagement with them)

If you are lucky enough to get a BBB I would reccomend you make an effort to get the most out of your 2h and Train Punishing Blade 3 and Ferocity 3. Personally I also finished Tactical Mastery and am in the process of finishing Fury/Flurry, which will add even more aggro.

When using a 2 hander as your primary aggro control weapon there are a couple things to be aware of in order to make the most of it.

2h Weapons can generate so much aggro not only because of their proc, but because of the potential for massive DPS bursts. Mobs notice when a ferocity/pb/flurry warrior is frequently swinging at them them 3/4/5/6 times a round. If you aren't main tank and you are going full throttle into a mob with a BBB without turning attack off, you're either gonna be target #1 when he dies or you are going to start pulling aggro off him whether you want to or not. It's just the nature of this weapon, it is an aggro magnet, and I've had to learn my lesson the hard way hehe.

The flipside to relying on it's frequent bursts of potential damage as well as a proc for flat out aggro control is that if you are fighting a mob that frequently has you stunned for long durations or has you slowed by 90% for long durations between cures, you lose that edge you had with DPS aggro, and a dual EB warrior is going to come out ahead due to the fact he will proc more, even if his DPS is in the toilet too for the same duration. This goes for BoWar, BBB, SEWS, N'T, GSoTS or any other aggro 2h out there.

Part of knowing how to use a 2h right is knowing how to manage your aggro, both when you want it and when you don't. So obviously if you are going to fight a mob that procs a 5 second stun frequently you will want to structure your tanking around the stun and learn to adapt from the standard strategies used on most boss fights. An obvious example of a mob that procs stun relentlessly and really screws with 2h users are the Generals in PoFire. You're better off using evasive on these guys (not only to avoid the 3kdd portion of the proc) but also to avoid the constant stuns associated with having them proc on you every other round they land their hits. You will notice a huge difference on these guys in particular being defensive or being evasive as far as aggro on concerned.

So to sum it up, if you want to use a 2h, prepare yourself for them and make an effort to train your any aa that will add extra swings to your attacks, the difference in aggro is remarkable, and well worth putting an effort into. If you haven't even put a single point of aa into a 2h skill like Punishing Blade and you want to stand around talking trash about 2h weapons, you're just uninformed and unaware of your real potential. BF/BoC users can put less effort into their weapon skills and still maintain aggro due to more frequent proc rates by default, but that in no way means 2h users are anything less than godly when they have a good weapon/aa and mange their aggro correctly. We will do more DPS, Hold the Same aggro, and take less damage with a 2h weapon at the current stage of the game.

I also wanted to address the issue about hit/miss/dps aggro.

It's my personal opinion that all melee based hate is based on the ratio and POTENTIAL damage done from your weapon (excluding procs, we're just talking dps hate) moreso than ACTUAL damage done.

I also feel Discs are completely ignored when calculating hate and that hate is based off of the potential you would have to do damage based on the number of swings you have for a round, rather than the damage done for that round. a warrior with a BoWar swinging 4-6 times a round with full offensive aa, but using evasive stance and missing a huge % of the time won't be any worse off than a warrior with the same aa's who isn't using the disc, because they are still generally swinging the same number of times with the same ratio weapon.

Someone mentioned they can't get aggro with their BoWar if they miss the first 10 seconds, well if you are SWINGING thats all thats needed to generate the same aggro as if your hits were landing. More swings = more POTENTIAL dps and so it creates more aggro. I actually LIKE it when I am trying to break mez and I miss for a round or two before I actually land a hit and break the mez, because all those misses are adding on alot of extra hate before the mez is actually broken.

AA abilities like Tactical Mastery and Combat Fury and Fury of the ages will add a lot of DPS, but they probably won't add any extra melee HATE. Unlike aa abilities like Ferocity, Punishing Blade, Ambidex, FLURRY which require extra SWINGS in order to contribute to your dps. It's the potential damage of the SWINGS that is causing the hate, not the damage itself. SO even a full SwingDPS-AA warrior using evasive who is swinging and missing a whole lot more than a NonSwingDPS-AA warrior NOT using evasive the SwingDPS warrior will most likely have all the hate, even though disc'd to miss more, causing their actual DPS done to be significantly lower.

I never get extra hate going full aggressive on a mob (using a non-hate/non-rune proccing weapon) even thoguh I am doing 50% more damage as opposed to those times with the same weapon where I am attacking in normal mode. The same aggro management rules still apply though if you are in a fight that requires you to cycle attacks, but in some fights you can go full agressive, and you will notice that you aren't pulling aggro any differently than if you were attacking without a disc on. The only difference is that ussually once you are aggressive you are less likely to want to turn attack off because you are on a timer for how long you can keep up the DPS, in these cases you may start pulling aggro just because you've left your attack on for so long, the same would happen even if you weren't disc'd

All in all the majority of our aggro is caused by procs, but heavily manipulated by the potential for huge melee DPS. Weapon ratio plays a big part in this, as does your current haste/slow level and the number of swings you attempt in a round. Warriors who swing more with higher ratio setups will be hated more than warriors who do more crit/tm damage with the same weapon but less swings but both doing similar dps.
