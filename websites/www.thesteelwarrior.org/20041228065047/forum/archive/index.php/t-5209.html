It is hard-coded to average 2x per minute. So whether you are a monk with triple attack at 65 using speed focus hasted at 110% (with bard song for example) and using a base delay 18 weapon, or a cleric using your summoned hammer, unhasted, swinging once every 5 seconds or so...

My theory on procs is that it runs in the background while you are attacking. This would make it independant of your weapon delay, and such things as haste or slow. The swing would be the catalyst.

Auto Attack On

You have 3 procs, you now have 3 seperate checks being made in the background as long as you are attacking.

When a proc meets the check, and is set to go off, it attaches itself to your next swing, whether that is .01 second, or 2 seconds away. If you swing and the target is out of range, the proc would be lost.

This would also explain why you would not be able to proc on a riposte. Since the proc rate would be tied to when an attack is activated by you, turning attack on, or hitting an attack such as shooting an arrow, a reflex hit such as a riposte would not be able to generate a proc simply because your character has not activated your attack sequence which starts the proc sequence. Basically, you aren't checking for a proc if you aren't attacking.
