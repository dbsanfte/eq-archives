Eumerin
The difference is that the problem with warrior agro is a much more difficult problem to fix than a simple hole in the paladin spell line-up.

Sure, the devs can make a make an across the board change and say that warriors now generate ten times more agro as they previously did with every swing. Yes, it would "solve" the problem from the warrior PoV (i.e. they wouldn't be overlooked as tanks), but it would also put the paladins back to Kunark (no idea what it would do to shadowknights).

What the new paladin spell means is that on the odd chance that you actually group with a paladin (and as a paladin, I love grouping with warriors - I get the early agro build up with a couple of stuns, and then I let the warrior taunt it off me. It uses the advantages of both classes, and means the slower doesn't get turned into paste if the taunt takes a bit to stick.), you don't have to go looking for the mob every time the paladin fires off a stun to interrupt the level 57 caster.

Yes, warrior agro is a significant issue, but that doesn't mean that development in every other facet of the game should come to a screeching halt while the problem is examined. Simple fixes for simple problems, like the new stun spell, should still be implemented as they crop up. The big issues (and warrior agro is hopefully the biggest right now) will be looked at regardless.

Edit: Regarding the "stun nerf" rumors.

This has been circulating around for a while. The first trigger for the rumor was the inclusion of the comments in the spell descriptions. Spells now include descriptions that tell you what the spells do (or at least give hints - my alt had to educate a paladin the other night about the fact that Lull was useless once combat had started). The descriptions of the stun spells mention which levels the stuns no longer stun. The initial assumption by many when this popped up on Lucy (while the descriptions were still on Test) led some to believe that the lower level stuns would have no effect on mobs over 55. i.e. they currently don't stun high level mobs, but some thought the spells would no longer be able to be cast - much as some spells (such as Lull) simply will not start to cast if the target is too high of a level. Then after people were able to check the spells on test, they found that the spells could still be cast, and therefore generate agro, on any mob in the game.

There was a minor change, although its pretty inconsequential. Whereas low level stuns could previously be used to stun/interrupt level 55 mobs, now they can only be used to stun/interrupt mobs up to level 54. No idea on why that happened, and it doesn't mean anything to agro.

When Force of Akera turned up on Test, some people once again believed this would herald in a stun nerf. The new stun doesn't damage mobs (one problem with QWoT, the other post 55 stun that's available in the 50s, is that it damages mobs, meaning you can't use it to gain agro on mezzed mobs). However, its now gone live, and the old stuns still seem to be working the same as always.

It is possible that the devs are planning on eventually implementing the "stun nerf", and the introduction of Force of Akera is the forerunner of this, but the new stun honestly does fill a gap in the spell line-up, and its possible that's all the new stun was for.
