What I remeber about proc rates for various weapons: proc rate for most weapons is based on a number of procs over time with a fixed dex. That is, if I remember right, at max dex(255 I think it was at time the tests where done) all weapons tested proced about once every 30 seconds over time. Hasted and slowed, that figure remained the same. Weapon delay did not change that proc rate. Some weapons do have a higher proc rate(cleric hammer), and so it is generally believed that proc rate is variable for different(more recent) weapons. I will look on shaman board in a bit and see if I can dig up that old test,, but that is as best as I can remember it.

Edit: found it: Rate of Weapon Random Procness (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=10861)

The conclusions drawn from this test are, to quote:

-The number of procs is determined by time, regardless of the weapon used. In other words, you will get the same number of procs over time, regardless of the delay of the weapon.
-Hastes and slows do effect the number of procs per swing, in order to keep the number of procs over time constant
