Well then shouldn't you be gaining X agro every time you successfully taunt?

Because X~1

Let me elaborate.

Let’s say, because it is easy to think in round numbers, that you generate 100 points of hate every 3 seconds. ( Mind you this is an outrageous number to just pull out of my ass and in actuality the number would probably be much smaller)

You can taunt once every six seconds. Also for the hell of it, lets be generous and assume that taunt generates 50 hate when it works ( ~50% of the time on blues )

So you whack a mob for 6 seconds and taunt the mob successfully and you have 250 hate.

That sounds nice?

No.

Spells generate 300, 400, 500, 600+ hate per cast.

Not good~

That means if someone casts one of the huge hate spells they are going to get their asses handed to them. ( BTW the huge hate spells tend to be the ones you want to have cast on a mob, such as slow and other de-buffs. ) Let’s say slow has 600 aggro.

Now then :

So now you have 250 hate and the slower has 600 and is getting eaten alive after just six seconds with the mob.

You cannot taunt for another 6 seconds and only generate 100 hate every three seconds so...

6 seconds:
You have 250 hate slower has 600. Slower is being eaten

After 9 seconds:
You have 350 hate. Mob is still eating slower.

After 12 seconds:
You have 450 hate. Taunt fails ( 50% and you lost the roll ) mob is still eating the slower.

after 15 seconds:
You have 550 hate. The poor slower is still getting chewed on despite the heal that was tossed on them.

After 18 seconds:
You now have 650 hate Vs the slowers 600 woot! Problem is the slower is now suffering from low hit point aggro and the mob finishes them off.

( The average caster will not live that long against a pop mob fyi )

Now then lets save taunt till the slower slows. Best case:

6 seconds:
You have 200 hate, the slower is getting eaten cause they have 600.

You taunt and make the roll: You have 650 hate the slower has 600 and all is good in the world.

Mind you: you can still fail the first taunt but a 50% chance right off the bat is far better than having to wait 6 seconds before getting the same.





DISCLAIMER:

These are not the exact numbers. And any numbers will vary from mob to mob depending on the mob and/or zone that said mob is in.

Please note: This ONLY refers to raiding scenarios when there's fairly large numbers of people engaging a LOT of high aggro behaviour from a distance. It does NOT refer to experience groups, more melee oriented raids, or anything else whuch doesn't involve high distance aggro.

1. It is absolutely proven beyond a shadow of a doubt that one can be highest on a mob's aggro list and not be getting beaten upon because of distance. Don't believe me? Go get yourself on top of a mob's hate list with 10 mother melees around and try to move the mob. You CANT DO IT by yourself unless it's immediately post-summon.

2. IF you are the main tank and are NOT highest on the mob's hate list, but are the highest among those within "aggro proximity", then a successful taunt ABSOLUTELY adds numeric hate to you, it places you above those higher than you yet far enough away to avoid engagement.

3. Every time you press the taunt button, it does NOT guarantee a successful taunt.

4. The more times you press it, the more likely it is to be successful once.

5. If someone exceeds the range/aggro quotient, the mob WILL move, particularly post summonning placing everyone nearby in danger of either AE's or Melee damage.

6. It is MUCH harder to re-establish lost aggro than it is to maintain a position on top of the aggro list.

7. It is also MUCH less likely that a mob will be in taunt range for your convenience if he runs halfway across a zone to pound on a mage or wizard, leading to the possibility of wasting your chance to taunt because of range issues.

8. If you think that you're on top of the mob's aggro list simply because he's beating on you, explain that to the wizards who get summonned while you're getting beaten upon.

9. If you had the opportunity to place yourself higher on a mob's aggro list and avoided that opportunity, out of stubbornness then you're a jackass.
