Shadowknights are unholy warriors who use fear, pain and disease to attack all that is good in the world. Shadowknights are primarily a melee class, able to wear plate armor and wield many kinds of weapons.

I'm not even going to respond to some of the rants and insults regarding paladins, but I would like to post just one more opinion. I chose a paladin as my main character when I started because the idea of being a fighter and yet also being able to support my companions appealed to me. I didn't want to be mt because I didn't want that responsibility most of the time even though the third character I created was a warrior. I didn't want the responsibilty of being a cleric and knowing people would die if I made a mistake even though I made a cleric as my fourth character. I want to be the knight templar. The one who does battle against evil and particularly the evil undead and serves the needs of her companions.

I don't cast better buffs than others although Brells is nice if there isn't a beast buff. I was not created to replace casters, but rather augment them occasionally.

I don't heal better although I can group heal to take the pressure off the healer. I can lay hands on the mt, cleric or chanter if there is an emergency. I can step back and cast a quick heal or a spot heal if needed. I am supporting my healer, not replacing them.

I can't nuke a mob although I have a very few spells that do direct damage they are basically not worth casting. That's fine. I didn't create a caster, I created a fighter.

My utility, in my opinion, lies in the fact that I have some versatility. I can help spam heal the mt in a raid. I can step back and keep someone alive with healing for a short period of time if needed or, as stated before, I can help with group heals.

I can cast cease and draw immediate aggro when the puller is bringing a group in and OT until the MT is ready for my mob. I can stun a mob distracting it long enough for ch to land on the tank or until tank can get aggro off caster. In the absence of a slower I could stun enough to keep the tank from getting pounded to dirt. Or, big if here, if we have a tank who has confidence in his abilities and my timing is right, I can cast a stun peridically during the fight that simply slows the mob's dps without stealing aggro. I can spin stun to reposition a mob that might be in a wall or too close to the edge. It's part of the joy of being a knight to me to be able to use stuns, root, heals creatively to make the group work more efficiently.

I'm not sure the call to nerf the stuns is going to solve this dilemna. It will make the paladin less desired and therefore open up more spots in groups I suppose, but it will also make the groups work much harder. If putting more challenge back in the game is the desired result then that might work.

It seems to me, the biggest problem is aggro control. I have seen a total of one post mentioning a warrior and a paladin working together with the paladin getting the snap aggro and then passing off to the warrior. Perhaps this isn't the perfect solution, but I do know it works. The beauty of this to me is that it shows characters who have figured out how to work together to utilize both of their classes to the utmost. I do hope Sony does something soon to improve the warrior's aggro ability and possibly add some other things to increase their inate warrior abilities.

I noticed on another thread a paladin said he joined a group as the last member and they made him mt over the better equipped warrior. I would have had to find some excuse to not mt. I have done that before and it just means I have to pay a little closer attention to aggro and either divine favor someone or distract the mob. If I wanted to be a mt all the time I would play my warrior.

I guess the point I am making is that not all knights want to be warriors. Many of us enjoy the support role, but part of that support is being able to tank when called upon. Many guilds don't have the luxury of having a collection of high end warriors and so the beastlords, paladins, shadowknights and rangers become our tanks when the warriors aren't available. The solution is not nerfing one class to make the warrior more desireable because then you will just have to go down the list nerfing the rest of the classes to level the playing field. Paladins are not the enemies, they are your brothers and sisters in arms and should be there to fill in the gaps in the lines and even step up to the front if the gap is there. The solution is to adjust the warrior up so he is the front line tank of choice and the classes all work together to complement each other. When all the parts, like fingers on a hand, work together to do something great things can be accomplished.

At the end of the day, it's still nice when we all have a job and we are all needed and not just needed when the groups are scraping the bottom of the barrel and you are the last possible option.
