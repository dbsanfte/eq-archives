ALSO the LDON "Royal attendant BP of Uberness" (as I saw refered to here on this forum somewhere).... is basically tailored to ManaTanks.

What cares about that bp? Me, I'm gunning for an ornate one. Bit less ac, better hp (once you slap a 40 hp aug on it), better effect. However, if you do want to compare the armors, there's at least one bright side to it from a warrior perspective. They have to choose from a lot more crap for the type 3 slots. While we've got a smaller list. So at least there's some cost to them...it's one area where they don't get casting and melee power handed to 'em on equal amounts to both parent classes.

Any advice at all?[/B]

Play what you enjoy. Me, I love my warrior. She's cute and effeciently sized and she kicks butt. She's about your level, 58 right now.

Does she have problems? Yah, getting that initial aggro sort of sucks. When there's a big train, well, don't have AE taunt yet, so I feel a bit optionless, especially when I'm used to playing an enchanter. And because I'm a gnome with all initial points into dex and follow Brell instead of Rallos, well, my hp isn't what it could be.

But on the opposite side, I've got good aggro weapons (the droga ones), a good 2-h (windblade), backed up with my dex, which is further augmented by a 30 point self dex buff. My hp is improving with sta augs and the acquisition of more ornate. And I'm salivating at the thought of Anger III procs on my weapons. I love defensive, makes tanking nameds a snap. I'm also prone to exclaiming "go, go gadget gnome!"...I can buff my dex by 30 which stacks with non-garou/ndt dex buffs, buff my str by 50 (55? cobalt boots +30 from clockwork observer), 40% spell haste, 30% spell haste (the 40% one takes recharging), slow, SoW, snare, and a few other things.

If there's a knight in the group that's well equipt, I'm willing to let them tank. I recognize the snap aggro they can attain and, besides that, I do more melee damage. So letting me clobber from the back is better than letting them pansies do it. Likewise, it frees me up to assist in CC a bit. A quick taunt and I can pull a mob off the chanter or cleric, without doing damage to the mob. And sometimes for fun I crank up my dex, wield my aggro weapons, and let my weapons pull aggro off the knights. And then they ask if I'm using taunt and I say "no." And that surprises them, hehe. For a tough mob, well, then we can do the old hand off trick, where they build up lots of early aggro and I take it from there.

When I do tank, I try to do the things a good tank does. I position the mob nicely, keep pets and the clueless players at it's back. I use taunt as needed, not mash it. I do my best to encourage others to play up to my strengths, not my weaknesses. That means, for instance, nukes from the group, not DoTs, because it's 1 taunt to recover aggro from a nuke, but the next tick of a DoT will take it away again.

Basically, I rock. I undo some of the damage done to the warrior reputation by others.

Anyway, there's a few things I hope they do to improve warriors, but I consider them tweaks more than anything else.

1. 20 hp augs that go in the same slots as the 20 hp/mana ones, but cost less points.

2. A revamp of disciplines: I want them turned into 0 cost AAs. That'll seperate their timers, give nice buttons to use to use 'em, and provide a nice window where you can see what they do, which are up, and when you get them, as opposed to the current crappy /discipline. The downside would be a static reuse time that doesn't decrease as you level. That hurts defensive/evasive, but since you have both available, having the reuse time be 2x what it is currently at 60 wouldn't be a big deal. Would increase the diversity of what we can do and add a bit more fun spam.

3. Sense Heading removed completely from the game. If it's going to start at 200, it's pointless to have the skill. Just make the compass work right all the time, always give us arrows on the map, and add a /heading command. Then use that sense heading skill slot to give us warriors Intimidate. Sounds like a useless skill, sure, unless you're familiar with it. Against pre-55 mobs, it makes solo'ing a bit easier. Against 56+ mobs, well, they are fear immune, but fear is good aggro. That's where the skill would really pay off.

But, anyway, I love my warrior for what she can do. I enjoy her more than my paladin. He's useful, but not as much fun.

And so that's my advice, if you love your warrior, play him to the best of your abilities. Do what you can to offset your weaknesses. And since you can't rely on cheesy spells to make up for incompetence, be competent.
