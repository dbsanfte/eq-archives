1 displayed AC is somewhere between 5-6HP if you've at least 9000 HP buffed. Obviously this only applies to tanking (AC don't reduce AE damage at all).

How much an AC is worth is actually dependent on how much HP you have. For example if you only have 1000 HP, then if you add enough AC to cut down average damage by half, you'd only gain an effective 1000 HP. Now if you have 10000 HP, for the same amount of AC you basically gain 10000 HP.

Generally speaking most gear are balanced between both. Since 0 AC 150 HP item or 70 AC 0 HP item don't really exist, it's usually just common sense and pick the best balanced item.

First of all, the people that continually say HP > All with no clarifying information do not offer anything useful. Obviously HP are needed but to just claim HP is the only thing worthwhile is just plain retarded. 100AC and 10HP is clearly superior to something with 10AC and 30HP despite the latter having more HP. But what if the numbers are closer? At some point, there is a shady area where at first glance it is not so easy to tell which stat to sacrifice for the other.

Here is my 2cp on that matter:
Parses have indicated that there is an aproximate mitigation gain of 4 percent for every 100ac ranging from 1200ac all the way to 2000+. This is going to vary slightly depending on the mobs DI/DB, but lets say on average, damage taken is reduced by 4 percent per 100 raw AC. Now your ability to tank is also dependent on HP, but every tank has a different amount of HP when buffed. So to determine the HP/AC optimal tradeoff value, you take your target raid-buffed HP (for me that will be about 7khp because i currently have about 6400hp buffed) and calculate 4 percent of that. For me, that 4 percent of 7k is 280HP. We then equate this 280HP to 100 raw ac. 100ac=280HP ----> 1AC=2.8HP

It makes sense that it would vary for each tank because the same amount of HP will deliver a smaller percentage of gain the larger amount of HP the tank has. So for example, if the tank has 9khp buffed up, AC will be relatively more important.
