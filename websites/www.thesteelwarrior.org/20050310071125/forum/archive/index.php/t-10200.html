Its just a matter of multitasking.

8 Computers, 2 monitors/keyboards/mice. Each group of 4 computers is plugged into an Omniview KVM switch hooking them to a single monitor/keyboard/mouse (you can get 8 and larger capacity KVM switches if you want). I have one keyboard on the desk, and one on my lap. Pressing scroll lock key twice then 1-4 switches to the appropriate computer. Thats the hardware side of things for me.

The actual mechanics of play for me are as follows:
1. Keep characters on the same computers - ie, MT is always lower KB/comp 4, cleric is upper KB/Comp 3, etc
2. Keep chararcters same position within group - MT is alway at F2 position, ST at F3, and Enc at F4
3. Single pulls whenever possible - Having to keep mobs CC'ed really lowers DPS and makes things much more risky
4. Extensive use of social macros: /Assist MT, /Pause 5, /Cast 1, /pause 5, /cast 1
5. Keep buffs in specific order - this way you can tell at a glance what needs refreshed on which character.
6. Keep aggro on 1 char.
7. Position mob so you don't have to move other chars. Ideally corner every mob, if no corners available, then a wall works: MT in front, rog behind, and all pets and others pushing mob straight into the wall.

By following 1 and 2, it is very fast to switch to a healer, target character, hit heal macro and back to another char (under 1 second for whole process)

Once an exp spot is "broken" and I can get single pulls, the pulls work like this:
1. Pull with war, Incite/taunt and head back to group
2. Pal attacks mob
3. Mage sends in pet and casts MALA on macro
4. Shm sends in pet and slows
5. Cleric starts CH (may do this sooner if taking lots of damage)
6. If slow doesnt stick, start another CH with cleric and further debuff and reslow
7. Attack with other pets/chars
8. Chain nuke on all casters until mob is dead

My normal build out for RCoD named for example is: War/Pal/Cle/Mag/Shm/Wiz in my "exp" group, then I usually have Enc nearby for buffs and occasional CC, along with some extra DPS from any 2 of Rog/Nec/Dru/Mnk or maybe a bard for manasong

The main thing is to grow into it. I played 2 characters almost from the start back in spring 99, and just kept adding to the mix.
