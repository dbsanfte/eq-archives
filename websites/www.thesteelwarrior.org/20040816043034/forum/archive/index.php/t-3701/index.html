OlavSkulcrusher
I agree with Bunnie. We want real fixes, not band aids. Valeris is also correct, that hate procs were supposed to be a band aid. In Velious

I've said it before, that aggro is the achilles heal of the EQ combat AI. So much of a group or raids tactics are made or broken by aggro control. Thus, classes that can control aggro >> classes that cannot. If a wizard actually has to watch his aggro, he'll put out substantially lower dps. If the shaman or enchanter has to wait for aggro to build before slowing, the cleric has to stick the tank with fast heals to keep him up, potentially adding med time, not to mention potential aggro on him. So, if you stick any tank class that has near perfect aggro lock in front of a mob, the game's AI is essentially taken out of the equation and the game is dumbed down in the extreme. Only pulling is really relevent then.

The taunt skill works pretty much exactly as intended. Whether that is good enough is certainly debatable. What isn't working as intended is the combination that exists now. Knights' tanking abilities being so nearly equal to a warrior's in exp groups, and their aggro abilities being near perfect lock.

Because of this, I think it would probably be better for the game to nerf knights' aggro and boost warrior's aggro up to the point where they just about meet in the middle. But I also think that SOE is about 0.0001% likely to do that right if they try (that is equalize the balance instead of just reverting another class to suckage). So, I suppose the only real solution is to boost warrior's aggro to just a bit under knight aggro.

And this has to be done WITHOUT band-aids such as hate procs. Personally, I hate even the idea of hate procs. (pun intended) Using itemization to keep one class where it is supposed to be relative to similar classes isn't inherently bad, but it should be something systematic, rather than something slapped onto random items. The higher AC and HPs of WAR only armor in the old days (crafted and Indo, for example) is an example of doing it reasonably well. Telling warriors, 'Hey, you'll get the advantages of your specific class, but only when you get this or that item,' isn't acceptable.

Specific items should enhance your ability to do your job, not make it possible. A whole set of WAR only items across whole zones and levels of the game that have some built in advantage for warriors (more AC/more HPs than equivalent armor available to other plate classes) isn't the best way to do it, IMO, but it is at least acceptable.

In summary, I think that anything they come up with that is a half-way decent solution is still a ways off. We probably won't even hear about it until it hits the test server.
