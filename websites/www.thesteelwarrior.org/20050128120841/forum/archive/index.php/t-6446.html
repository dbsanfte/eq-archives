Originally posted by Kilaara
When you do not have problems holding aggro in your group WHY do you want to have more aggro for less damage?
Because having "no aggro problems" just means that you have cautious nukers. In most groups, the DPS nukers are *always* holding back to some extent. Accept the fact that you, the warrior, are incapable of producing enough aggro to let them use their mana arbitrarily.

If your usual group has no such DPS members, then sure, focus on your own DPS. But in a typical balanced group, reducing your own DPS by say 10% to get better aggro might make the group as a whole increase its DPS by 30%.

I remember the taste of humble pie when my wizard friend explained to me, very patiently, that he was incapable of going below 40% mana in a group with me, because if he used his mana any faster then he would take aggro and die. It took me a while to really accept that he was not exaggerating. That was a year ago when he had negligible flowing thought. (Clearly, I had negligible aggro to match his negligible flowing thought.)

Every time I got a weapon or aggro upgrade, he and my druid friend are the first ones to notice it. They're constantly toeing that line where my aggro sits, seeing how much DPS they can fit in below my own aggro limit. They typically notice it long before my healer friends notice it.
Neksnappa
04-05-2004, 02:54 PM
I'm also gonna throw in the fact that warrior aggro, other than incite, is based solely on the RNG. I know for a fact I'm not the only person who has had 1 aggro proc on 1 mob, followed by 10+ on the next. (This with wa3, and 3 aggro procs). For every extra aug or level of WA, your chances of getting a proc early on in each fight rises. Eventually, with wa5 and 4 aggro procs, you average something like 1 proc every 5 seconds. This seems godly, but again, I guarantee you will see fights with only 1 or 2 aggro procs. It will happen much less frequently than if you had lower wa and a few dd/lifetap procs over aggro procs.

Best case senario is to have either a good DW combo (16+1/20, 17/20, etc are good for vt/early ele and lower) or a nice 2hander. These are the weapons you should put the pure dd or lifetap proc in. The other weapon setup should have weapons that proc taunting blow, enraging blow, stun/dd, and even things like ac debuff (I love my OSoW off VZ). Put anger/yak augs in these, and by all means, only spend 510 points if you see an upgrade coming your way soon.
