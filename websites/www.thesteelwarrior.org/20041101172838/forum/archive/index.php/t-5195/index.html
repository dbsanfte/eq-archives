Uukla
You will want your defensive AAs and Anger 3 on your primary weapon. If you dont plan on going elementals, then your challange as a warrior will be to tank Hard LDON (in the current environment). This means not only taking the damage of Hard LDON, but holding agro. Spell Guard and Guard, while being "nice" do not do as much for accomplishing your goal of doing your 2 jobs as a warrior. Holding agro and taking damage.

The reason I say you will want anger 3 on your primary weapon is that I really cant say that ANY proc augment is worth it on your secondary weapon. It will proc point 8 times a minute. For 1150 points, I dont know that it justifies an Anger 3 augment, while if you put a 125 DD proc on it, you are getting a whopping TWO dps out of it. Again, points better spent elsewhere.
If you truely felt that Guard was worth it as a buff, then putting guard on your offhand might be worth it, as the infrequent proc rate would probably not matter so much.

My own experience with Guard and Anger is they proc at about the same rate. Guard adds zippo agro though (it acts like Vie line and not like Rune).

I would caution against putting 1150 pt investments (guard 4 and anger 3) into droppable weapons. Odds are at some point you will want to swap out and sell them (especially as you are close to GSoT usage range).

Uukla
I have an agro weapon primary, and when i put anger 3 on it, I started getting complemented on my agro. IMO pre elemental people dont get weapons that are good enough for you to say that you can hold agro "good enough" without an anger 3 on them.

As far as the Guard on your offhand weapon. In someways im dismissive of it. But in others, I think about it this way. Guard = 5 levels of innate defense (each level of ID is 1 percent less damage), and if people think its worth it to pay 15 AA points and you get that level of mitigation for putting it on your weapon... shrug, it might be worth it, because its certainly no worth putting a DPS proc on it.

BTW, please dont talk about varying proc rates. Its been proven many times, proc rates from LDON augs are 2 / min onhand, .8 offhand. The onhand will increase with dex, the offhand wont.
The reason why Guard and Spell guard would "proc a lot" is that they are buffs with duration, and as long as they proc once per the length of the duration, then its always on.

Friedrick
My first instinct would be to put Anger III on a GSo4W and not look back. Obviously you have quite a ways to go to get your hands on one unless your guild is much higher level and better equiped than yourself (no offense intended btw).

With that in mind I would buy nothing atm. 1150 pts is a lot of points to spend and time will tell if you need better mitigation via armor or guard or better aggro. Apparently I take it neither is an issue atm in LDON for you. Aggro and mitigation have their thresholds and sometimes enough is enough. More aggro won't help you if you don't lose it to begin with and more mitigation only helps if it reduces the number of cheals you need. If after every mob you are at 50% health the cleric probably heals you back to full. If you were at 55% health the same would more than likely be done as well. If you were at 80% though you could go another mob before getting healed more.

With all that being said, if you are a warrior and tanking most of the time, it is my opinion that you cannot go wrong with aug'ing a weapon with Anger III, I love mine. I would just make sure the weapon I aug'd would be used for a very long time for that investment of LDON points.

Kitre Halfax
I had quite a dilemma myself, having acquired both a Blade of Carnage and a Greatstaff of the Four Winds. However, I can't advise you about the offhand. I use an Ancient Prismatic Mace offhand, since the proc rate keeps up Avatar, oh, 80% of the time, when I'm dual wielding, though the Mace is not augmentable (none of the Avatar proccing weapons are, sadly). An Anger2 modified Blade of Carnage is more than sufficient aggro for every situation I have run into, including LDoN, Raid-mob tanking (Storms named, Bastion of Thunder named and Tower named, other various "boss" type mobs).

If you acquire a massive aggro primary, and slap AngerX into it, you'll rarely find yourself on the short end of the stick - if it's a 2h or a 1h. The added advantage of the 1h is that your offhand can provide some utility, at the sacrifice of your DPS. The advantage of the 2h is well documented - fewer ripostes, more dps, etc etc.

In the end, weigh your hard earned points against the possibility of saving cash/winning randoms/otherwise acquiring weapon upgrades. If you're going to be a big Adventure Point spender, sink your money into the weapon that does the most attacks (2h or 1h primary).
