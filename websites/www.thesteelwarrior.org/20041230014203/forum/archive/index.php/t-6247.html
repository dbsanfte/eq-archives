I experienced some weird taunt behavior in Droga this weekend. It seemed inconsistent with what we think we know about taunt.

Here's the situation. I'm hydra, a 65 warrior and a 65 shammy, no one else with me. (Exp is crappy that way, but I was afk on and off, so couldn't do a real group.)

When I am hydra like that, I use the shammy to pull. The shammy pulls with slow and comes to my camp spot, the mob follows, the dog and the warrior engage, and off we go.

So here's what happened, pretty frequently. The mob comes in, the warrior and the dog start attacking. The warrior taunts, and, if taunt is successful, the mob turns to the warrior.

Except that, several times, AFTER turning to the warrior upon a successful taunt, the mob turned back to the shammy for several seconds. The only thing that was happening was the warrior and the dog were attacking the mob. The shammy wasn't doing anything (other than the slow it had already cast, long before the successful taunt, and other than the dog attacking).

So I say to myself, hmm, taunt just puts you barely at the top of the aggro list, maybe I had bad luck, maybe the warrior missed and the dog hit, and the dog put the shammy back at the top of the aggro list.

Except that wasn't what was happening. I watched the swings, the hits, and the misses, and it wasn't that the dog was hitting earlier or faster. Most of the time, the warrior was connecting first (after the taunt), and just as often (the warrior was using a GSo4W, but the haste item makes up for a lot of the bigger delay and the lack of dual wielding).

Another interesting observation. If the mob turned back to the shammy, it usually took until the next taunt for the mob to turn back to the warrior. Even though the warrior was hitting a LOT harder than the dog (as you might expect). And, after the second successful taunt, the mob NEVER turned back to the shammy.

Please note, I was not using incite, because recapturing aggro wasn't all that important, once they are slowed the shammy could tank them himself. (When I am PLing my rogue in Droga, I root them and put the shammy close, so the mob will concentrate on the shammy and allow the rogue to backstab.)

This behavior seems totally inconsistent with what we know about taunt. I thought that maybe it was the fact that I was using a two-hander, but the GSo4W can sometimes steal aggro from even a high level warrior dual wielding, if the other warrior isn't inciting and doesn't get an early proc. If I can almost match aggro with a high level dual wielding warrior, I should be all over a shammy pet (level 58, iirc). And, if that was the problem, it should have happened at least once in a while after the second successful taunt. Except that it NEVER happened after a second successful taunt.

Anyone have any thoughts about this? Am I missing something, or is there another (possibly minor) factor in taunt that we have overlooked?
