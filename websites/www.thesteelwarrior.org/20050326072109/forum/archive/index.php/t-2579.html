The thing with NToV is that it doesn't take very many runs to gear your guild up. There is a TON of droppable loot. As a general rule I'd say that all the previous posters are right. You will want to try NTOV in order to stretch your raid experience, take on new encounters and gear up your people.

I do think, however, that with the advent of POP you have alternative paths available to you. For example, you no longer need the speed items as you can pretty easily upgrade from Xanamech. You can also get items from the POS mini and uppers. If you are capable of doing Xanamech, I suspect you are capable of at least doing Auflie and Einride and probably all the tower bosses in BOT, all of which will drop good upgradeable stuff that fills many of the slots otherwise upgraded in NTOV. And if you work within the confines of POP (kicking in Vindi, IV, THO, Burrower, RC, Servitor, Korocust, DS, etc) you can basically skip NTOV entirely. In addition, since many of these encounters have "tricks", AEs, etc., you really don't lose much in raid skill experience.

So, in short, I think its up to you and your guild. If your guild will actively pursue and track the mobs in POP, Luclin, Velious, I'd think you can just leave NTOV. But it is a lot easier and more satisfactory to upgrade a bunch of folks in 6 hours in NTOV than many days elsewhere.

And in the end, I'd never make a decision based upon faction. When I joined my guild they managed to trash every single one of my Velious factions within about a week. It is the individual raidmember's responsibility to raise faction when needed (and its damn easy!) - let the guild gank any damn Velious mob it wants because ultimately spreading the love is a good way to upgrade everyone.

Sassinak
08-21-2003, 11:40 AM
I believe flurry is an extra set of main-hand attacks. So potentially, a mob can swing 3 (mainhand) + 2 (offhand) + 3 (flurry) = 8 times in a single round, plus perhaps a bash as well.

Of course, they'll miss a lot of those attacks, and their chances of actually making 8 attempts in a given round is low, but you'll see lots of 5 and 6 attack rounds with mobs like these. When they hit for 700, and have a delay of about 1 second, like those flurry drakes do, they can still put a world of hurt on an 8k tank.
