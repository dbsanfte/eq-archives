Don't take a full 30 second clip all at once. It's best to take smaller clips ata time, then piece them together into a video editor. You can also reduce the number of FPS, do half screen and turn off sound. Those will help reduce the file size.

I use Adobe Premiere Pro to piece my footage together, but you can use the free Windows MediaMaker 2 to do it (available from the microsoft update website).

1 - Don't use software based capture if you really want to put together and edit movies. You are better off with a hardware capture to video tape. Two reasons. It wont clutter your hard drive. And you dont have to worry about starting and stopping recording. Just pop a tabe in and record. You en up with more to work with and more is good.

2 - One you compress the vide it can not be edited or should not. The uncompressed source is best to edit with. The final product you compress not the source.

3- The source or uncompressed video is extreamly large. By takeing what you need from vido tape instead of all the video on your harddrive you save disk space. You will be working with gig's worth of vido just to make a few min movie.

4 - many vido cards vido out does not work at the same time as there monitor out therefore it's almost useless to use. Many gforce cards are this way.

5 - There are many methods of capturing the video. Quality is a function of how much money you spend on equipment.
