<<.. but never had the courage to level a caster on my own from lvl 1 to 50.>>

courage? no harder to level a caster than a melee. just different. i played a druid for 3 years before i finally started really pushing my warrior. why did i pick warrior over say a shadow knight? mainly because i wanted the most opposite class i could get so that when i was bored of one i could go to the other and not just have more of the same thing.

rolling a warrior when you already have a ranger is asking for a lot of overlap. rolling an enchanter would be a world different. and you learn as you go so you dont need to worry that they are a mystery class at the moment. one big difference you would notice is you dont contribute much direct dps and yet people will fawn all over you. same goes for bard. same with cleric. druid wizard and mage are sort of a compromise because you do a lot of dps with any of them and so still feel sort of ranger-like. not to mention their spells are sort of similar.

whatever you do dont canabalize your ranger for essence emeralds. you may be frustrated now but you may regret later you sacrificed him. i agree that you cannot judge the endgame by what comes in your 50s. they are just very different.

one last bit of advice. form your own groups. dont wait for others to invite you. there are a lot of people out there who to put it one way "have been bitten and inflicted by the shyness monster" and will not start their own groups to save their lives. those who do start their own groups get very inflexible about who they invite. for example i always start my own groups and i am extremely partial to casters.

here is a funny example of me starting a group a few months ago. my cleric gf wasnt on and there i was my crummy no-agro self as i was. there were a few other melees lfg so i invited them all into a sort of social "holding group" (all melees ..HAH) and told them anyone who got an invite do not hesitate to grab it. funny thing was in 10 minutes i invited a chanter to our holding group then a shaman. the ranger and rogue had found another group by that time so there were now five of us and what do you know? a cleric shouts "LFG!" and we snagged her. we had me two other melees shaman cleric and enchanter and everybody was glad for our great group. and it fell together practically by accident simply by starting with a "leftovers group".

which brings me to another conclusion from all this. discouragement breeds failure. when we are discouraged we break down socially. its a fact of human nature. and its self perpetuating. its hard to be proactive to get out of the rut because discouragement pre-empts us from taking the steps necessary. that group of leftovers that turned into such a good group in only 20 minutes started from me feeling discouraged. by getting the couple other melees into my group it got me talking and gave me the impetus to give others tells to get me out of the rut i was in that day. give it a try!
