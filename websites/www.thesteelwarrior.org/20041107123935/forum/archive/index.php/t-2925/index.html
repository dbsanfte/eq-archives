Imagine for a second you were managing a huge online rpg like EQ.

You want to keep the classes balanced, but you have given up on getting feedback from the "masses" because its often conflicting, and/or overpowering suggestions etc etc.

So if you are looking down at the game from the executive chair how do you objectively determine if classes are actually balanced, without going into the nitty-gritty details?

One (primitive) way would be to ask the database guy to give you weekly/monthly reports about class distribution. Aka, how big a percentage of the population plays warrior, how many play paladin, etc etc.

If the numbers show there is twice as many playing warriors as there are paladins, would you then do anything to make warriors more popular? Probably not.

Which brings me to my suggestion:

Go delete all your warrior bazaar mules/un-used twinks and remake them as another class, so you do not contribute to these statistics!

I think alot of people by habbit simply choose a warrior when they want to make a level 1 bazaar mule or whatever. I know I do(back in the old EC days STR mattered, so having mules be warriors made sense).

A brief survey in the AB bazaar showed 12 level 1 warrior mules in the bazaar, as opposed to 6 level 1 paladin mules.

Depending on how SOE does their statistics, these mules "could" be contributing to giving a wrong picture about the actual popularity of our class.
