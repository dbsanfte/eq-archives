The prevailing school of thought on this is a very simple line of understanding, and has been supported at multiple Fan Faire events by Verant themselves.

All Tradeskills base skillups off of the highest of the applicable stats - wis, int, or in the case of some skills, dex or str (skill dependent). That means if you have a 255 wis and a 106 int, your wisdom will be used for calculating skill ups. If you are smithing, and you have a 280 str, but only 100+ wis/int, your str will be used (str is the secondary stat for Smithing).

This has been tested thoroughly and proven repeatedly in controlled environments.

Note that your stat has NO IMPACT on your success rate - skill alone affects how often you succeed / fail. This is where a Geerlock will help you out - having the geerlock equipped will increase your effective skill level for success - but not for trivials. That is to say, you will check for "triviality" of an item (as in, can you still skill up from it?) using your natural skill, but your success rate is checked against your modified skill (your_skill_value + 5%). The advantage here is that use of a geerlock tends to result in fewer failures, overall, which can result in a lower overall cost (due to sell backs, or availability of more inventory to sell to others).

There is still a school of thought that Wisdom affects your success rate - this is an old rumor, and there is no reproducable evidence supporting this.

As recommended above, all of this information is available on
EQ Traders (http://eqtraders.com/) , on the forums and in the individual board links. A thorough perusal of that board will answer 95% of your questions. I recommend spending a LOT of time there if you are going to invest any of your gaming experience on tradeskills.
It is widely regarded as the one-stop tradeskill website by tradeskill junkies (like myself).

Oh, and to address an issue of ambiguity in your original post, your class has no impact on which stats you can use, or which tradeskills you should attempt. Some classes are better suited for certain stats (priests tend to have higher wisdom - thus it makes sense for a priest to max out wisdom if doing tradeskills, but they can use any of the applicable stats they choose) or for certain skills (enchanters tend to represent the larger portion of 250 Jewelcrafters, but such is not always the case).
As a warrior, you may find that using the secondary stat (str for smithing, dex for JC, fletching, MAYBE Tailoring - this is unconfirmed as of yet) may be easier for you.
