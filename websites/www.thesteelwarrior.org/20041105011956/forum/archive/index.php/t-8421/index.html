If you want a break from farming and you have some friends, here are some possibilities for fighting for better armor than you have now. I'm not listing them in any particular order.
1. Lower-level LDoN stuff. Even the 150-point stuff from BB would be an improvement and give you a couple of levels of breathing room. The drawbacks are that you won't use this armor for long, and you're spending LDoN time that could be going to augments/top end armor. The benefits are that you don't have to worry about the random number generator & rare drops; you are fighting rather than farming; and that you can actually get enough points in a normal adventure at 53 so that you might be able to afford this armor.
2. There are a couple of Cobalt drops in Karnor's Castle you might be able to fight for with a few friends: the boots (AC 25) drop off the Caller of Sathir in courtyard 1; and the gauntlets (AC 25) drop off Drolvarg captain, also fairly near the front of the zone. Drawbacks: your current haste is in your hands, it's hard to get a group to go to Karnor's nowadays, and the drops can be irritatingly rare.
3. Thurgadin armor quests (Champion's armor, I think the easiest of the Velius quest armors to get, though it's not the best of them). You go to Kael with some friends, kill giants and keep your fingers crossed that the quest component drops. Drawbacks: drops are kinda rare, you have to have the right faction with the dwarves in Thurgadin (but that's not too hard to get--just whack giants), this is not the greatest armor in the world, and these guys are not hill giants--you do need some friends. But, the armor again would be an upgrade, and you'd get to fight for it, and sometimes you can actually convince people to go to Kael.
4. Go to Crypt of nadox and hope for steel wrapped stuff to drop. All the usual drawbacks to hunting for your gear apply, and you will need friends, the more the merrier. But, go to Alla and search for "steel wrapped" for the stats.
5. Go to Veksar with a group and hunt there. Be careful, methodical. Research the zone before you go. Feral Lord Gulok drops kylong shinguards and the rotting (rotted?) shopkeeper the soulscream belt. You can really use a shaman getting in and out, some mobs are casters, adds are easy to get. but all of these problems have solutions.

I'd mention the draconic shoulders, but Caramaj did in an earlier post.

I'm not saying that doing these things will actually get you a suit of armor any faster than farming. But you have a puncher's chance of upgrades. Veksars is an interesting zone; it and Kael may provide the casters amongst your friends with some stuff they can use too (nadox may as well, I just never paid attention).

In these places life would be easier if your friends were in their upper 50s rather than their upper 40s, and of course if they are good players, that's a plus.

If you're hurting for cash and looking to do extensive farming ... grind to lv 55 somewhere and buy this stick. It's much better than wasting 8k on 1 weapon which by itself isnt nearly as good as the staff. You need to buy lots of upgrades, so i'd concentrate on what will upgrade you the most for the the least (best bang for your buck).

It's aggro is impressive and its dps is on par (and imo a little bit better) than the droga combo ... and you can buy it for under 200p if you keep your eyes open.

That said, looking at your magelo i would concentrate on a full set of fierce heraldic and a pair of valorium rings. I would also look into buying a solstice pack. That earing is quite the bargain for only 3-4k.

For farming:

Fungus grove is good xp and loot at your level (need a small group)
Velks is a decent place for xp and coin
Farming Hill giants in Rathe MTs is easy plat (although boring)

ps- Full suit of Fierce heraldic and a GSoT will do more for your aggro, dps, ac, hps than anything else. The Valorium rings are nice stat boosters, but i would work on a full set of FH before i threw cash away on the rings.
