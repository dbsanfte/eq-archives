With those two problems highlighted I consider soloing defensive spec good when compared against other Warriors. And just because I'm protection doesn't limit me in weapon selection. I keep a set of other good weapons to two-hand and dual wield. I was Arms/MS to 60. I recently switched to a prot build as I started raiding. I solo stuff slower but the rate at which I bandage/eat has decreased. I'm also more effective against NPC casters - sure, my DPS has dropped, but I can keep them locked up with stuns for a good portion of the fight. putting a full five points into 1h weapon spec has helped DPS quite a bit.

I definitely recommend grinding it out to 60, doing what quests ya can but generally avoiding raid instances unless your tanking duties are minimal. once ya hit 60, well, it's your call. ya don't have to go Prot heavy, either - a dozen points will get you some extra armor and extra shield blocking, which is most of a Prot warrior's raw mitigation.

Honestly full out prot is a joke of a talent path. The tree defining skill is not used much at all, the stuns are neutered in seconds due to diminishing returns (thanks charge) and the myriad of abilities do not enhance your tanking ability in the same way that MS / fury builds enhance your dps. This disparity is why you rarely see any full prot warriors outside of raid mt's. The price you pay for a tad bit better tanking overall is very high considering it destroys your soloing ability and pvp prowess.

Yes I can solo mobs easily, it just takes forever and a day. This is not an efficient way to solo, no matter what anyone tells you. DPS > defense in that environment, faster you kill things the more xp, faction and loot chances you get. Downtime is almost non-existant with bandages a-pleanty avaliable, and if you're not grinding on humanoids for bandage drops it's your own fault.

Pvp is much the same story. I see a prot warrior coming at me on my alts and I just point and laugh. They have next to no dps, and if they give up the shield that they invested heavily in then there goes all those points. I'd say a battle chicken is more dangerous then a protection warrior in the all out fights.

The sad fact is that you can get most if not all of the "worthwhile" prot talents with only 15 points and still retain mortal strike. The tree needs major work in the 20+ point range. Shield discipline, imp shield bash, conc blow, imp disarm -- it all needs to be overhauled.

Deep Wounds - 3/3 points
