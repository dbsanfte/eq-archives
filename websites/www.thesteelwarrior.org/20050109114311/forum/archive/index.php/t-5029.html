In the original game, one of the advantages to picking a warrior was the fact that there was a fairly significant advantage of innate MR over the other classes, as Warriors got an innate bonus in magic resistance each few levels, while (I believe) none of the other classes enjoyed the same bonus.

With all of the new spells that are cast by mobs having large negative modifiers, as well as resistance increasing items being tens or hundreds of times more available now than when the calculation of MR added to warriors was made to balance in the game as it existed back then, the minor bonus that warriors get has been rendered insignificant. Back then, the innate resistance given to warriors could easily account for half of the warrior's total resistances, while now it can easily account for less than a TENTH of their total resistance.

I think another thing the designers have to look at is restoring the resistance advantage that warriors had in the origincal game, but which has been slowly rendered obsolete, by the same things which have necessitated the implementation of disc changes, the addition of incite, and the addition of openings.

I suggest a change along the same lines of HP and AC... just like the addition of a 50 hitpooint or 50 AC item does not add 50HP or 50AC, but rather, somewhat more, so should warriors get rather more out of a MR addition than what is stated in the item's description.

This addition would just about finish the update to the original warrior to balance with the realities of game play today.
