You should really consider how much of a commitment you want to make to tradeskilling if you want to do it for profit. Currently my skills are 244 pottery, 240 JC, 228 brewing, 210 baking, 200 fletching, 176 smithing, 174 tailoring. Even with some of the big ticket pottery items available to me (elemental items) I still go through plats faster than a fat kid eats chocolate cake.

For 5k you can actually master several trade skills (100+), but you aren't going to make any money at that level. To be profitable enough to have a good cash flow you are going to have to GM a skill so you can make the items that sell. Brewing is the cheapest skill to GM, but it is also the least profitable. JC is actually pretty cheap as well if you can get high enough wis/int and cha over 110. JC is a pretty good skill if you can get ahold of the rare PoP drops to make the good items. You are a ways off from being able to farm any of those drops at your level so you would be stuck buying them - which of course cuts into your profit considerably.

Fletching can be done inexpensively if you farm all the components you need to make acrylia arrows. Plan on spending weeks (months is more realistic) farming enough components to get your skill over 220.

Smithing can also be done somewhat inexpensively if you make shadowscream armor. Again, the down side is that you will have to farm the ingredients for months to get enough to get your skill high enough to make it worthwhile.

All of the other trade skills are going to cost many thousands of plats to get high enough to make the items that sell. Expect to spend at least 40k+ to get in the 220-230 range in any of those skills, even if you can farm your own ingredients.

On top of all of this, you have to consider what is happening with the player made item market. With the completion of the aid Gimil quest, there are droves of elemental guild level people that are taking all of their trade skills to 220+. It cost a ton of plats to do this and they have to finance their tradeskilling somehow. As a result, high end elemental trade skill items are popping up all over the place now and as more and more people become capable of making the items, the prices are coming down quickly. On my server, elemental bows are down to 150k (air bow) and 220k (earth bow) and there are always 2 or 3 bows for sale in the bazaar. A month ago, you would be lucky to see 1 bow for sale in a week and 300k+ was the price.

Of course this does bring up yet anothr option. You can still make money from lower level trade skilling if you know what to make. Take a look at the market on your server and figure out what is selling well. Then figure out what sub combines go into making those items. You can make a killing selling things such as celestial essense, various tempers, and various dyes among other things. Find out what the most common methods for skill ups are for high level trade skillers and farm the components they need. Make cod oil for tailoring, farm pelts for tailoring, farm acrylia and velium pieces.

I think you will find that you can make a lot more money selling trade skill ingredients than you can doing the actual trade skills, unless you are prepared to commit 10s of thousands of plats and many weeks to GM a skill.
