Unless a change has been made to the Feign Death code, I recall power leveling a friend's Enchanter with my level 60 monk back in the day, by beating down the mob then going FD once it's low on health. The Enchanter was able to nuke it a couple times and get solo experience. With a 65 Shm to buff/heal/slow, your monk should be able to tank and beat down everything that you'd want to exp your cleric off of, including all white, yellow and red mobs.

If the FD tactic doesn't work any longer, you might also consider the zoning technique, depending on your setup and zone times. With a monk and a warrior, however, you can camp everyone near a zoneout with high level (compared to the cleric) mobs nearby, pull with the warrior and beat it down to nearly dead, Root it with the shaman, zone the Warrior, and then finish it with your Cleric for full experience. While the Warrior is zoning in and out, the Monk can be beating on his mob to do the same thing - rotating them should provide constant experience. A real pain in the ass, certainly, but it's an option.

You can also use Atone tactic, once your cleric hits 34, by beating it down and atoning until agro is clear. However, this can be both unreliable and very slow depending on your luck. Remember to use /assist on the mob to check it's agro and make sure it's clear before finishing it off with your Cleric.

Good luck!

-Bashiae-

Definitely continue the damage shield route. Even when you are restricted to single mobs at a time, there really is no faster way. I suggest Kaesora library area, the mobs there will be mostly yellow-white at 30, and it's an XP hotspot. If you need higher level mobs, the Xalgoz area is a few levels higher. As soon as Kaesora's not good enough, go to City of Mist.

At level 40, group the cleric with the beastlord instead, sit the cleric in a corner, and go mow through easy mobs with the beastlord - City of Mist will probably suffice for that.

Atone is great for aggro tricks, but it's too often resisted and too slow recast to use as a powerlevelling tool.
