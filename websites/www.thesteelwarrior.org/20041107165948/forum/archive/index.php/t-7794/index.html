I did a little research on DS a while back on the EQHunter forums...

DS Infos (http://forums.eqhunter.com/viewtopic.php?p=16023#16023)

All that stuff was for rangers, however, I'm thinking that a warrior can bump up that DS even more...

Firstly, Bladecoat is a ranger/druid only self buff that isn't present in any clickable form, so you will need something else for Slot 3 DS. Through a quick perusal of Alla's Spell database, I found 2 items that both have a clickable Bramblecoat (Slot 2: 18 AC, Slot 3: 3 DS)

Golem Spines (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=24090)
Spiked Chain Mail (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=8362)

Unfortunately, the Golem Spines are Lore and No Drop, so only a 1 time use item then you get to go find more; also, according to posters on Allakhazam's it looks like the Spiked Chain Mail no longer drops :(. In running some final numbers, I'll go ahead and assume the use of the Golem Spines on an unslowable boss mob, and if you feel that 3 DS for 1 buff slot isn't worth it please feel free to just remove 3 DS from the total number ;)

Secondly, as a Warrior you have a much better Slot 4 buff than a ranger would have access to in PoTime Saryrn's Girdle of Intense Durability (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=21888) which gives a quite phenomenal 15 DS in your Slot 4, a 9 DS increase of a Ranger's 6 DS in that same slot. Considering the use of Bramblecoat this would net a Warrior an increase of 12 DS over the ranger total, bringing your DS up to 321.

One thing that wasn't counted for a Ranger's DS, but I will include for Warrior raid purposes here, is the Druid AA Spirit of the Wood. This is a 5 tick 55 DS Slot 8 AA effect that is often MGB'd during raids due to its wonderful 250 a tick HP regen. If this were chain Massed by 4 druids the effect would stay up roughly 2 minutes, not sure how AA duration effects this so I will proceed with base numbers as the amount of DS applied isn't affected. This 55 DS stacks with everything discussed previously for a total of 376 DS.

A final note, and since I don't know anything at all about Warrior /discs these days, this might just be taken with a grain of salt :) Allakhazam's lists a Slot 6 level 45 Warrior discipline called Barrier (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/spell.html?spell=4589). I know not wether this is actually in game, and kind of doubt any warrior would take this over a defensive disc of some kind, but... the range given for applied DS is 25@45 to 33@70. Doing some quick math (70-45)/(33-45)=25/8=3.125, meaning that every 3 levels gives another point of DS. At 65 this would give a loss of only 2 DS for a total of 31. This also stacks with everything including the Druid MGB Spirit of the Wood, bringing the total up to 407 DS.

The bard modifiers and such were gleaned from perusal of the EQDiva forums and asking many questions in the serverwide.bards channel. I couldn't find any hard data on song mod caps, so I went with the highest possible I could find. The final number might have to be adjusted down slightly.

Hopefully at least some of the research I've done will help you guys in deciding if DS is worth it or not hehe. In total, between Mage DS, Shammy Potion, Bramble Coat, Shield of Torment, Vengeful Guard, and Barrier, you are looking at 6 buffslots taken up by DS alone, but possibly when fighting Monk bosses the DPS acheived from ~400 DS might be more than worth the loss of a few buffs, but that would definitly be at the discretion of the user. Good luck all :)

Couple of points to check out on this...

A Damage Shield does add aggro.
Yes, it is damage so there is some small aggro with it thus the AA for some casters to INCREASE damage shield aggro.

Change your thinking on the DS from Damage Shield to Damage over Time.
How long does your damage shield last? How many times is it going to damage mobs? How much total damage do they take? In most situations, getting hit 20 times total in 1 fight at even a measely 25 hp DS is 500 damage that is unresistable to the MOB. That's a free babby nuke. DS lasts for 5 fights, you did 2500 damage to the mobs for the cast of that 1 DS. Not to bad for even a gimp damage shield and you'll note the numbers of fights and damage per is fairly low.

Look at it in reverse too... When you run into a mob with a healthy damage shield up and nobody dispells it.. every melee in your group loses about how much HP from it? The DS you use is 99% of the time a higher DS than the one used by the mob caster. Yeah, slowed, the mob takes less damage but still, if you're looking at mana efficiency vs effect, you probably don't have a higher damage potential spell in your book than a damage shield. Gimp as it may seem in the short run, it lasts a long time and is unresistable damage to that target for max effect every time it hits your tank.

All in all a damage shield isn't a bad idea. They are cheap for what they do.
