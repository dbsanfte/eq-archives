=============================================

Oh, yay! I've been wanting to blow off some steam for some time now. There's a lot of stuff that people do constantly that have eaten away at my temper over the years.

1. People who have to announce it to their guild, friends and the entire zone EVERY SINGLE time they level. When it's a milestone level that gets you an uber new ability or popular spell, that's fine, but what I call "ding spam" is easily one of the most frustrating little things in EQ. Paludal caverns is a nightmare, because you can level every ten minutes in there. I turn off /ooc and /shout when in PC for whatever reason.

2. People who can't seem to comprehend the use of the /lfg tag. This goes both ways. You either have dumbasses that spam the zone every five seconds looking for a group (and they never put their tag on) or you have dumbasses that spam the zone asking; "who all is lfg?" While I think you need certain expansions to actually open the lfg/lfp window, I seem to recall that simple /lfg tagging came out way back around Velious. There's no reason beyond willful stupidity for so many people to overlook the use of the /lfg tag, or do a /who and see who in the zone is lfg. What's even worse is when you put your /lfg tag on AND do an /ooc or /shout saying you're looking for a group, and two seconds later the same jackass who was spamming the zone before still asks if anyone is looking for a group. Which leads to the next complaint.

3. People who won't say that they want a specific class/level when they continuously do zonewide inquiries asking who is lfg. This increases the spam from the people who have been trying to get a group, because the group seeking players ignores them due to their class/level and keeps on spamming. If you want a cleric, say so. If you want a wizard, say so. Or, stop spamming and do a /who to see if any of the classes you want are even in the zone or lfg.

4. Monks that demand autosplit to be off (in casual groups, not ldon or raid groups). This makes it hard for the people who spend most of their time medding to break even, especially clerics, who are expected to cast hp spells which cost components. It's no fun to race the warrior for loot scraps everytime a mob falls (no offence, but I've found that for some reason, warriors are the loot whores of the group about 75% of the time). If the monk can't stand destroying copper/silver whatnot, then the group needs to figure out a fair way to distribute the loot, so that everyone can buy the supplies and/or spells they need. Which leads me to my next complaint;

5. Warriors who speed-loot mobs before they even hit the ground. Yes, there are other classes that do this (usually melee), but even when EQ first came out, warriors were the worst about it. Maybe because they never have to worry about mana or songs or even health in a group...I'm not sure why so many warriors are so greedy. Here's a tip: sharing loot benifits everyone. It helps the cleric afford the stones so that they can give you those hp buffs you crave, it helps the shaman buy the attribute buffing spells you constantly spam them for. The worst case I've ever seen was a warrior who told me I had to buy some mage words that dropped in our group. He was looting everything in sight, and when I asked for one little thing that I needed for research, he told me he'd sell it to me. I told him to fuck off and solo, if that was how he was going to be, and I kicked him out of the group.

6. People who are too lazy to put one or two more letters in a word to spell it properly. If someone is flooding me with "Plz, thnx, u, 4, h8t, etc" then I will put them on ignore...unless they are just learning how to type. Most of the time that isn't the case, and people are just lazy.

7. People who think that you owe them all of your time when you stop by their hunting group and throw a few buffs on them to be nice. I actually had some little punk cuss my druid out the other day because the warrior in his group died while I was afk. He was pissed off that I didn't just pee in a cup so that I could be their healing bitch (people forget sometimes that other players are actual human beings and still have body functions and require nourishment). The moral of the story is, if someone helps you out of kindness, don't take that to mean that they have any obligation to stay around and keep helping you. Be thankful for the free buffs and don't whine if you pull more than you can handle and the benefactor isn't around to pull your arse out of the fire.

8. people that bitch about clerics taking a few swings in a fight now and then. No, they aren't going to do big damage even with their summoned hammer or a delightful orb, but they have a right to do more than sit and heal over and over again if they can still do their primary job while meleeing a little bit.

9. Clerics who are so interested in meleeing that they forget they're the healer and don't do their jobs. These people should have made a paladin if they wanted a full-fledged "battle cleric". Nobody wants a cleric in the group that thinks they're Conan or Xena. Meleeing is fine and dandy, but when you can't do your job right, it's time to sit on your ass, med, and pay attention to people's health.

10. Druids and wizards who quad kite inconsiderately. I don't mind someone quadding for exp/loot, but when they monopolize a zone and make it impossible for anyone else to get exp or money, or when they run their mobs over other people, it's time for them to be banned. There's no reason in the world why quad-kiting should cause zone disruption, if the kiter knows what the hell they're doing and has some manners.

Well, that's it. There's plenty more, but those are the top ten things that have been bothering me almost as long as EQ has been live.

Thanks for letting me get that off my chest.
