This isn't massively complicated really. There needs to be a viable progression path for raiders and a viable progression path for non-raiders.

I don't like the argument that raiders "work" for their gear and therefore deserve better. Raiding ought to be fun in itself, and raiders should raid because they want to - not because they have to raid in order to get the gear which'll enable them to get a pickup group. That's just bass ackwards.

I also don't like the argument that non-raiders should be able to get raid-like gear without doing anything particularly challenging.

For casters, Velious struck the right balance, with the Coldain Prayer Shawl quest. That was tough and time-consuming, with a real story, and it had realistic requirements for the reward. It was obtainable by anyone, regardless of guild or flagging requirements. Not putting something similar in for pure melees was an error: you ought to have been able to exchange your Coldain Prayer Shawl for a weapon or piece of armour of equivalent value. The 10th Ring wasn't the same because it required a guild.

LDoN got it wrong. LDoN Normals are too easy, and too time-consuming, and the rewards are disproportionately skewed so that pure melees get less out of LDoN's than other classes, and there's too little story. Obtaining LDoN gear is more about patience and play time than about overcoming challenges.

GoD was better, because anyone with enough LDoN victories and enough guts to eat the deaths along the way can get to Ikkinz. You don't need a guild or PoP flags to do it (I speak from personal knowledge). There's a story to it and there's variety in the encounters.

Ikkinz is disproportionately tough compared to KT/Yxxta, though, and it's the new "glass ceiling" for non-raiders. Ikkinz ought to be progressive, rather than three zones of roughly equal difficulty.
