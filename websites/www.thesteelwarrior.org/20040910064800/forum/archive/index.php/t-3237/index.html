Boleslav
LDoN bosses in level 65 Normal dungeons are over level 65. The only way a Paladin can stun that mob (and actually stun it) is with the Divine Stun AA. Divine stun is a short, low aggro stun, so there would be no 4 – 6 seconds for others to figure out what happened because of a Paladin stun. Also, Divine Stun has quite a few pre-requisite AA, and most Paladins don't get it until the 100 – 150 AA mark. I have 50 AA, and no Divine Stun, for example.

When I pull LDoN bosses, I use Nullify Magic, and follow up with a stun as the mob gets close to camp to solidify aggro. In this situation, it looks like you were rooted while the mob was still at his spawn point, or at least only partially on his way toward you. If casters were to cast on the mob before I dropped the stun on it, or overcast before I got a second stun off, it is quite likely they would have drawn aggro too. If one of them got hit enough to get into low HP territory it would be over for that squishy. But most likely I would have regained aggro after that.

I do agree that the Warrior is in a bit of a helpless situation here. About all you can do is to let folks know you are rooted, remind them to come to you, fire off AE Taunt (if they come to you), sit, and pray. Not much. As a Paladin, my first thought would be to root the mob, then continue stunning it to gain/hold aggro until my root or the mob's root wore off; a much more active role.

A LDoN assassination boss is actually a situation where a Warrior's increased mitigation and /defensive do matter though. I have been smacked down three times by LDoN boss mobs, out of the 9 I have tanked. (By boss I mean the Assasinate target, not normal LDoN named) A Warrior with comparable gear and AA to me would probably been able to survive, especially if they activated /defensive. By and large I have to agree with Warriors who say the difference in Warrior AC/HP/Mitigation/DPS does not make up for the Paladin's spells, especially when mudflation has made it that almost every experience mob is tankable by Paladins as easily as by Warriors. This is just an example of where Warrior's advantages are valuable.
