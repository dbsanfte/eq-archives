Here is the part that is interesting:

As previously said I didn't expend extraordinary amounts of dkp for gear with avoidance mods, but since most of the gear was at near rot levels I decided to gather some and figure out why anyone would put such a low return effect on endgame gear. This parse was done with much less noise and higher maximum avoidance to better pinpoint what exactly the mod does for an enchanter.

Details:
- Mob was a stoneshapen gladiator; level 65 rooted mob in Forgotten Halls. As close to a real GoD mob that I'd care to parse against atm.
- Attack was off, and I was facing the mob at all times.
- Buffs of importance to this parse were Virtue and Shield of Maelin.
- Healing was done by my cleric alt.
- Agro was maintained during the entire fight using tash.
- Mob wasn't slowed.
- Each dataset lasted as long as my cleric had mana or I died, whichever came first. There were 3 datasets for each gear setup and each was consolidated into one.
- YALP was used to gather some of the info(total damage, attacks, hits, misses, dodges), however, the times listed are obtained by starting at the first attack attempt by the mob and ending at the last attack attempt for each dataset.
- I have max def skills and AAs, and I have included the magelo for gear setups below:

------------------------------------------------

No avoidance mods
Magelo
305 AGI from SoV

Total time: 2802sec (died zero times)
Total damage: 621829
Mob DPS: 221.9
Mob attacks: 2685
Mob Hit %: 1965 - 73.18%
Mob Miss %: 672 - 25.03%
Mob damage per hit: 316.54
Dodge %: 48 - 1.79%

------------------------------------------------

65 avoidance mods
Magelo
305 AGI from SoV
5 raw AC higher than the other setup, the number is significantly higher because the avoidance mod affects the shown AC

Total time: 3796sec (died once)
Total damage: 781588
Mob DPS: 205.9
Mob attacks: 3665
Mob Hit %: 2439 - 66.55%
Mob Miss %: 1156 - 31.54%
Mob damage per hit: 320.45
Dodge %: 70 - 1.91%

------------------------------------------------

Difference
- The healing for 65 avoidance was far far easier than the one with 0 avoidance.
- For 65 avoidance an enchanter avoids ~6.63% fewer hits.
- Dodging as well as mitigation effects, shown by Mob damage per hit, is negligible.

Conclusion
In stark contrast to the previous parse one can easily see that the returns one receives from the potime avoidance mod is decent. The difference is easily explained by the noise from the varying AGI levels and higher mob hit % of the PoE parse as well as the stacking effect of avoidance. AGI will affect how often a mob hits you. A mob that hits 100% of the time hitting for 99% of the time is barely differentiable, whereas a mob hitting 2% of the time then hitting 1% of the time just had his dps halved. Drawing from the previous concept one can see how much better the avoidance mod works the more one gets.

Again, this mod has nearly no effect at low levels because we have low defensive caps and are hit quite a lot. However, when the supply of avoidance is substantial enough to get lots of gear with the mod, then it's a worthwhile purchase.

Shielding makes the amount of DPS mitigated by Avoidance less but the % DPS reduced is still the same.

For example let's say a mob just always hits for 1000 50% of the time every second and call that 500 DPS.

If you avoid 10% (using 10% to mean you now get hit 40% of the time) you've reduced DPS to 400 DPS, a saving of 100 DPS or 20% DPS.

Now if you have 50% shielding (just so numbers are easier to calculate), you get hit for 500 50% of the time for 250 DPS. Avoiding 10% more reduces the damage to 200 DPS, or 50 DPS saved, but it still shaves 20% DPS off. Regardless of what a mob's hit distribution/DB/DI is like, avoiding 10% more always means you live 25% longer (assuming a mob's to hit is fixed at 50%).

Although in the second case the amount you mitigated is less, in both cases you reduced damage by 20% which translates to living 25% longer due to avoidance.
