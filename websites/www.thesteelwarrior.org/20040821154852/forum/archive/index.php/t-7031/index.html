1. In post 87 of this thread, Glatius 2 asked why I thought the problem of knights getting groups was "not theoretical." Actually, the problem that I was refering to as "not theoretical" was the way that the fate of knights and warriors are linked. Look, relatively early on in EQ players noticed that warriors had the best mitigation. For years, warriors were the prefered tanks in most situations, and over time that perception grew stronger. To the extent knights' primary function was tanking, that made it difficult for them to get groups. When expansions began to show up with mobs that could whack a caster in one or two rounds, though, suddenly the shortcomings of warrior aggro became so dangerous that knights became the prefered tank for most of these mobs. So, after months of warriors lfg and a standdown, warriors got better aggro tools and unlinked discs. Suddenly, warriors could hold aggro. This, coupled with GoD's unfortunate level of tuning, certainly made warriors the prefered tank against tough mobs, and has *perhaps* made it more difficult for knights to get exp. groups. Thus I maintain that the link between the "playability" of warriors and knights is not theoretical, it's historical and it has manifested itself over and over and over. What happens to one class of tanks affects all classes of tanks.
1. a. My first point here is not that at the moment one class or the other is overpowered. My point is that changes to warriors influence knights and changes to knights influence warriors. I think the knight and warrior communities should be able to agree on that. If we can't, then we shouldn't be talking about knights at all in this thread.
1.b. If we agree on that small point, then the next question is this: are we in this together, or are we going to keep calling each other whiners and fucktards? By the way I ask this question you can tell what I think. In my view, it's vital for warriors that knights have recognized and valuable roles, both in groups and raids. And the reverse is true as well. When classes are out of balance, the history of EQ suggests that eventually (after a painfully long time) the underpowered class will be strengthened, sometimes to the point that the other class is suddenly undesirable. Or the powerful class will be nerfed. Neither of these outcomes is optimum. Endless rounds of nerfage and shifting disequilibrium are bad, and they're bad for all three classes of tanks.

2. The next question then becomes: At the moment, do knights have recognized roles that get them groups and raid slots, and the tools to do their jobs once they're in a group or a raid?
2. a. I happen to think this is several questions. The answer might be different for characters below lvl 55 than it is for characters who are 65 with 200 AAs. It might be different for group situations and raid situations. I suspect that sometimes when people disagree about the answer to this question, they do so partly because at different levels of this game, there are such huge differences in characters, mobs and zones that the level 60 folks are simply playing a different game that the lvl 65 max AA folks. I will give you a small example. Before the melee changes, I leveled an untwinked warrior from 30 to 50, mostly in Kunark zones. At the same time you might have sat for 2 hours as a lvl 65 warrior lfg in PoP, I would zone into OT or DL, shout I was lfg, and simply start running toward popular grouping spots. Within 90 seconds of zone in, I'd have an invite. Often multiple invites. It was quite literally the case that I spent 20 levels never having to wait more than a minute and a half for a pick-up group. Then, suddenly, I hit level 50 and hours of lfg in PoP. I didn't change, but the game did.
2.b. The first step in solving a problem is to get a good definition of what the problem is. Only by defining the problem can you even tell if the problem exists. Some knights obviously believe they have a functionality problem. Apparently some warriors disagree. What we should be doing is coming to some sort of understanding of what knights think the problems actually are, and the levels/zones/conditions underwhich they manifest themselves. Instead, what happens is that we jump from "Hey, I have a problem" to endless specific proposals for solving the problem (up to and including proposals that would basically put various classes in God Mode), interspersed with some people saying there is no problem and other people objecting to the proposed changes and other people claiming that the real problem is something else and offering different solutions, and other people calling the first guy a whining fucktard. If we want SOE to take our suggestions seriously, we have to have serious suggestions about defined and demonstrably true problems. We do not all have to agree, but it would be nice if we were disagreeing about defined issues. then the nature of our disagreements can be defined as well.
2. C. Underlying some of this, perhaps, might be a class definition issue. Warriors are quite fond of telling knights that "You've never been able to tank the boss/hard mobs in new expansions right away." Well, okay. Are knights supposed to be able to do this or not? This is a game design issue, I think, and it ought to be an issue that SOE can answer clearly. If to main tank a boss mob in one expansion, knights need gear from the next expansion, well, that should be made clear. If that's not what is supposed to be happening, well, that should be clear too.

3. Over time, both warriors and knights are going to get both new powers and harder mobs/new expansions to deal with. Basically it seems to me that knights' use value comes in two categories: damage mitigation, and utility/spells. I've offered the opinion that the problem with improving knights by focusing on increased mitigation (by which I mean innate mitigation and AA/disc effects that would be shared by warriors) is that it threatens to create a situation where warriors and knights leapfrog each other over and over in terms of mitigation and hence effectiveness as tanks. If knight utility becomes a trivial part of their value because direct damage mitigation is exponentially more important than everything else, then they are functionally warriors. Certainly among the most serious players, there is a powerful tendency to min/max everything. If warriors and knights do the same job in the same way, one class will be better at it. The other classes will have more or less dramatic game play issues.
3. a. This is why I say that I believe that for all three classes to have a valued place in groups and raids, knight utility has to be strengthened. For all three classes to have a role, the classes have to be different, not the same.
3. b. I could be wrong about this.
3. c. Even if I'm right--or especially if I'm right--balancing the classes by making them different is going to be extremely difficult.
