Grhar
I don't think it would be difficult to iron out exploits:

- Make it so that the warrior must be on the target's hate list to successfully summon. For example, a warrior could not summon an unengaged mob since he would not have agro.

- Flag any mob that can be knockbacked with a paladin / cleric stun spell as "summonable". If stunning classes can already position mobs, letting warriors do it to the same mobs would not be exploitable... if anything, it would merely be spreading the "positioning" ability to another class. Stunners would have the ability to "push" mobs away, warriors would have the ability to "pull" mobs.

- Make the effect radius larger than /shield, but less than a common healing spell. IE --- something reasonable.

That right there eliminates any exploits with regards to pulling & positioning. Any ability can be exploited if it isn't properly tuned... that's why we have these things called *patches*. If you think the idea is silly, that is a valid stance... but whether or not it could be exploitable prior to tuning is irrelevant.

In general, I think it would be a great skill to add to the game. Warriors could use a unique ability. Monks have feign death, mend, disciplines. Rogues have evade, stealth, lockpicking, pickpocketing, and disciplines. Warriors have /shield and disciplines. Summoning is already coded to a degree in the game. Tons of level 51+ NPC's can already do it, regardless of their class. If you want a roleplaying reason for it, shouldn't warriors have the ability to prevent an opponent from disengaging before a conflict has been decided? Warriors would simply be calling their opponent back into the fray, instead of letting them pick on more vulnerable targets.

I don't really like the idea of a +agro effect added on to it. Warriors have always been lacking in terms of generating snap agro, and changing that might not be a good idea. Instead, maybe "stun" the summoned mob for a nominal period, say 3 seconds. This would give the warrior time to get in at least a few swings, and perhaps a bash/kick and taunt. Or maybe let a summon make an attempt at taunt. So if the shaman draws agro on a slow resist, the warrior taunts unsuccessfully, when the warrior summons the mob another taunt attempt is immediately rolled, without having to wait for the button to refresh.

I think it would be tons of fun to see warriors summoning mobs off clerics. "You will not evade me!" It wouldn't step on the toes of knights, and would help to make warriors more viable tanks in PoP experience groups. It's raid impact would be nil as ubermobs are unstunnable, so they would also be unsummonable.
