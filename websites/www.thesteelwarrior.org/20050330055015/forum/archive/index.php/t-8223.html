It removes 9 curse counters from up to 5 slots. RZTW is a great fight to show this example of counters and slots. Say you're the MA, and you get both the proc (18 curse counters) and AE DoT (9 curse counters) landed on you. Then, someone casts Remove Greater Curse on you (9 counters x 5 slots). Say the Proc is higher on yer buff list. it removes 9 curse counters from that "spell slot" (leaving 9 to go), then it looks for any other slots with curse counters. It finds the AE DoT, and removes all 9. Now its got 3 slots worth of cures left. Finds no more slots, RGC ends. Now the RGC'er casts again. It cures the Proc now since it only has 9 counters left.

Hope that makes sense. But each "Slot" is basically one buff when considering cures.

The way RGC works is it removes 45 counters in 5 sets of 9. Basically the cure has 5 attempts to cure it, each time removing 9 counters at a time. It will definitely reapply the 9 counters to a debuff if that one has more than 9 counters. The way it's set up means only that it's allowed to cure more than one spell at a time.

This is different than say, Abolish Poison (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=97&source=Live), which only has one attempt to cure 36 poison counters. AP can only cure one spell per cast, because the 36 counters are lumped into the one set. The upgrade on the other hand, Antidote (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=1525&source=Live), has 4 attempts to cure 64 counters (4 sets of 16), so it can cure up to 4 spells.

Ex:

You get 3 debuffs. One has 3 counters, another has 21 counters, and the third has 8 counters. RGC will apply it's 1st set to the first one, the 2nd, 3rd and 4th set to the second one (as 18 won't cure it, but 27 will), and the 5th set to the third. This will cure all three in one cast.

You can test this yourself with a shaman using the spell Pure Blood (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3693&source=Live). Have them slow themselves (16 disease counters), then cast 2 of their low level poison DoTs on themselves (Envenomed Breath (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=434&source=Live) and Tainted Breath (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=277&source=Live) are good for example). Then cast Pure Blood and it'll cure all three.

So yes, Sedition will be cured in one cast. I now return you to your regularly scheduled strategy discussion.
