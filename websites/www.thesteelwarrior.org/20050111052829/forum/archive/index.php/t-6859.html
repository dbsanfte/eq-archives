- The entire lower end of the healing spectrum should be rebalanced. Rangers, Beastlords, and to a lesser extent Paladins and Shamans, and even the SK and necro are slowly having their healing abilities being made less effective, and in some cases irrelevant, by the vast increase in damage output over the last few expansions. It's gotten to the point for ranger and beastlord healing has gotten laughable. Heals for 400-500 when mobs hit for several times that in a single hit? <shakes head> let's make their healing actually make a difference again. Shamans are in dire risk of losing their role as secondary healer, their heals just aren't big enough any more.

- Paladin stun caps should be raised to reflect the significant increase in NPC level. Paladins used to be able to actually stun mobs that they fight on a consistent basis, and now they are finding more and more content at the very high end that is immune to their stuns. Extremely high end Paladins can many times not stun their mobs, while middling paladins can nearly always affect theirs with their stuns. This should be rectifiied. Stuns should ALWAYS have an effect on any trash mob. Only "special" mobs, such as named, giants, dragons, and others, should not be effected by stuns. Not having significant amounts of the mobs stunnable in single group content, JUST because of their level is not right.

Paladins and Shadowknights should regain the ability to become invulnerable to or significantly reduce melee damage for a short time, just like just about every other melee class in the game does. There's just no reason not to have it, and they have more need of it and more of a reason to have it than many melee types that DO retain that ability. This is especially important, due to the high melee damage output mob design that SoE seems to currently favor. DA and Harmshield don't cut it, due to the fact they often times are counterproductive. A Paladin or SK uses HS/DA in an emergency, and one of their party memebers is most likely going to die due to the aggro issues. Solution? Add a autoblock disc or spell to the hybrids with the same limitiations as furious/weaponshield and the like.

Change Cloak of Luclin to stack with BSS. It's a self buff that should be treated the same as the self buffs that the other classes have. Currently BSS stacks with just about any class's self buffs... except SK's, and there's no real reason for it not to stack, due to the fact that just about, if not every other class's self buff DOES stack with it.

Hybrids really don't need a whole lot of changes or upgrades. Things like these, however, would go a long way to solving these problems.
