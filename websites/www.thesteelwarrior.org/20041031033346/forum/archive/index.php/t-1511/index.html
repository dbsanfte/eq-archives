Bashiae
Sounds like you can do it. I'm assuming since you're talking about 4 - 7 clerics, you'll have at least 4 groups of misc. other players there in addition. I've not killed Xanamech yet personally (13% without a death with 18 people, four clerics; when we pushed him over the wall and he despawned a few minutes later :/ ) but it's close enough to understand what it takes. In all honesty, the only challenging parts are the CH chain prior to slow, then balancing damage with healing mana.

This last attempt, when he poofed, was our second try with a much better strategy. I'll try my best to explain it, as it worked for us, but of course everyone is different and I imagine your force is a bit different. We start with everyone in position; the main tank (Warrior, not a ranger) right in front of sleeping Xana, Clerics at max CH range plus two steps forward, debuffers (shm/enc/brd if you have one, we didn't unfortunately) right behind main tank - everyone else behind clerics. Once gnome is just about to reach dragon, tank throws up the disc and CH chain starts (I have approx. 8400 HP buffed as our main tank, 140 AAs, so adjust accordingly), which we used a 3 sec rot with four clerics until slowed. Slow landed very easily within about 8 - 10 seconds. Then let warrior build some agro, and all melees/casters/backup healers hug either the left or right wall and move around to the back of Xana and get in positions. On our guild message board, we had a diagram, but try to imagine Xana is a clock. Melees engage from 10 and 2 at MAX melee range, and their *same group* healers/casters stand at 10 and 2 behind said melees, just within CH/Celest range and two steps forward.

I won't go into all the details, but his 3 AEs (AE unresistable snare, AE stun/300 DD in waves, and AE 800 DD) are different ranges and center around different targets. The stun/DD, and the reason most people say don't use a CH chain, is shorter than CH (especially with extended range) and is centered on the main tank. Just remember that, and you can spend the ENTIRE fight with only the main tank taking the AE stun/DD. The AE snare is the same range as the stun, but centers around the dragon, so expect all melees to be snared. Not a big deal, but the main tank will have to keep moving and pay attention to keep in position in front of Xana or he'll turn around and screw up the positioning/AEs. It's 60 or 65% snare as I recall, so it's extremely difficult to backup very well. I found it better to turn left or right and slide back and to the side to keep in front of him, but sometimes had to turn around and simply walk forward. Lastly, the 800 DD is larger range than the entire room, so expect everyone to take it, but it's only once every minute so very easy to heal with backup healers and clerics not CHing main tank. This would also be lowered greatly by having a bard of decent level/equipment, but you may want the bard with CH clerics if you're low on clerics rather than backup healers.

Which brings me to my last point. Our tactic was, using four clerics, once slowed we broke off two clerics and left two out front of Xana out of AE stun/DD range, to CH chain the MT. We used an 8 sec rot (he hits for 1390 max, but not very often just remember he can, like any mob, spike for a high round or two), alternating, but many times one CH could be cancelled as the heal would not be needed until the next CH landed. You'll have to communicate between clerics, but it can be done fairly easily, even with lowered damage from 3 groups of non-primaled people.

That's it. The only other thing to remember, which we failed, is to have EVERYONE switch sides (exact same positions) at one time during fight, so dragon doesn't get pushed out of the room and up the wall. With that tactic, we got him to 13% in approx. 7 minutes, and would've finished the fight with two CH clerics around 20 - 25% mana (level 65, FT 6, SCM 3) and all other healers extremely high on mana (most nuked near the end when it was looking good).

This fight, in my opinion, is all about positioning and maintaining it for the duration of the fight. If you can find a way to explain that to everyone, it will GREATLY reduce your mana spent on healing and greatly increase your chance of success.

Edit: Forgot to mention, and might be useful information to you, that our raid of 20 was average level of 62 or 63 (lowest 57), with key classes being 65 (namely tank and shaman).
