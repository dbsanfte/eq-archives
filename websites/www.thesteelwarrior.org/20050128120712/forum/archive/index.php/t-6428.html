I think you correctly identify the best weapon set-up among your choices.
And I think you can fairly easily hold agro with that set up until your late 40s.
The basics of tanking haven't changed. Get agro, position mob, kill mob. Do not let mob kill your casters.
Work on all your skills.

Some things have changed though. You have a new tool to build hate: the provoke/incite line. It's very very nice.

You will find (perhaps you've already noticed) that many old-school zones for leveling are pretty much empty now, and you might want to look around on this site for suggestions on that. You can also do /who all <your level> lfg to find out where most folks are experiencing.

If you stopped playing before Luclin, go to the bazaar. It's the place for shopping for gear.

If you stopped playing before the Planes of Power expansion, you'll find travel stunningly easy and that a good many faction issues at lower levels moot.

When you're able, start doing Lost Dungeons (LDoN) fighting. You'll find it different from the old Kunark days.

Here's a thread on "basic" positioning when you're main tank. Some of it is not so basic, but do look at the pros and cons of positioning yourself with respect to pets and whether you're going to face toward your caster or away from them: http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=4690&perpage=15&display=&pagenumber=1

since you'll get to level 30 far far faster than folks did in the old days, here's a thread on the /shield ability that you can use at that point: http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=6067

here's another thread, on PC. I'd ignore what I say in this thread about South K. If you want a group in your mid 20s, go to someplace line M. Seru then HHK or maybe LDoN. PC really is kind of a mess--there are lots of dirt ignorant players there, and groups fight more like streetbrawling than with any sort of organization. If you need a break from it about level 20 go to Neth. Lair. Not very many folks there, but very often they know how to play. After a couple levels, you can get groups in M. Seru, then HHK. By that time you'll know folks and be more sorted out: http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=4906

Good Hunting.

The answer to another of your questions--will groups accept you if you don't have the best dps weapons--is yes, they will.

Warriors are gear-dependant, and better gear will make it easier for you to do your job. But a large majority of players know that the role of warriors is to tank and they also know that tanking is important. That will get you an invite to groups looking for a tank.

Some warriors have a problem getting invited *back* into groups with people they've grouped with before. But those warriors are probably not being ignored because their gear is substandard. They're probably being ignored for one of the following reasons:
1. They're immature, annoying human beings who are no fun to play with. Often, they feel compelled to demonstrate some sort of unresolved psychological insecurities that they have, and these insecurities almost always involve bodily wastes, human sexuality, and male and female primary and secondary sexual characterisitics. Nor do they find interesting or unusual ways of demonstrating these insecurities--no, not only do they have distasteful emotional problems, but they manifest these problems in predictable, boring ways. Even their typos fall into predictable patterns. They make other people feel like unpaid babysitters.
2. Without demonstrating that they know how to play their class, they start telling other people how to play. They bitch the exp. is too slow. They want to endlessly ramp up the risk the group faces because after all they routinely solo harder mobs than this. They make decisions that get other characters killed, and they don't even know they've made the mistake.
3. They never ask about mana levels and when casters say "LoM" or "OOM" the warrior completely ignores them. They explain to shaman that buffs don't do anything; they ignore "a <mob> has been slowed" message from enchanters; they tell druids their healing sucks; they tell rogues that monks are better because they can pull; they tell the monk that rogues do more damage. Well, you get the picture.
4. They simply fail to notice that a mob or mobs is killing the casters.
5. They loot far more than their share.

My guess is that you won't have these problems, and thus won't have problems getting groups and finding people who want you as their tank.

In fact, if you are cool to hang out with, if you learn how to tank and pull when necessary; if you're smart enough to learn spawn rates and the characteristics of the mobs you're hunting; if you take the time to compliment good play when you see it; if you learn what other classes actually do for a group and then do what you can to make your group fight better--if you do these things you'll have no problem finding other good players who will go out of their way to help you get good gear.

Your ability to increase the dps that your group does by increasing the dps of your own weapons is surprisingly flat. Your ability to increase the dps of your group by playing your warrior really well is exponentially higher than increases in the damage you'll ever do.

Don't get me wrong--gearing up is a major concern for a warrior. Ultimately you have to hold the attention of the mob and you have to be able to take a punch. But for quite some time your gear will not be the critical factor in your ability to get groups. By then you should have a lot of friends who want you to tank for them so badly that they'll spend a lot of energy helping you fight the mobs you need to fight for better gear.

Weapon with agro proc at 0 skill doing no melee damage > higher damage weapon without agro proc at 250 skill

Proof: Gnoll Hide Lariats/Silken whip of ensnaring

Damage agro really is worthless, the only things that matter on agro for warriors is swings and procs. If you depend on damage to hold agro at higher levels, you're going to end up with the slower sitting when the mob is at 0% health (after slowing at 95% and doing nothing else) and he STILL runs after him/her. Happened to my adventure group last night several times.

As a healer, I don't care if you stand there with attack off and only do damage when you riposte the mob, as long as you can hold agro through everyone else debuffing/killing it. Your DPS doesn't matter to me, nor to almost anyone unless you're soloing it...only holding agro and having enough hp for me not to burn my mana down chaining CHs constantly to keep you alive while the rest of us kill it. Once you get your agro abilities up to snuff, THEN you can start working on getting more DPS output with my blessing (I'll even find you groups so you can get AA/gear).
