Podling
I know a way to balance most warriors without a) nerfing knights or b) trivializing content. This will work for nearly all situations, some high end raid encounters not withstanding.

To explain, let me lay out what I think the problem with warriors is currently.

It's not snap aggro. Warriors have never had snap agro. Knights always have. This wasn't always a problem. The problem is the massive leap in DPS that mobs have received, and more specificaly the massive increase the damage intervals mobs use to figure the damage on each hit.

In the good old days, a warrior was the ideal tank. No, they couldn't hold perfect aggro, but if the cleric or chanter took a round or two of damage, it wasn't a huge problem. However the hitpoint gap between tanks of all kinds and non tanks began to widen in the luclin era and increased even more in PoP. To counter the increased HP's that tanks had, mobs had to hit harder as well. The AC gap also increased along with the AC softcap. This means that while tanks of all type advanced relatively equal to mob dmg output, the ability of other classes to withstand a round or two of combat decreased dramaticaly. Paladins and SK's became the preferred tank because it was no longer feasible to have your casters take a round or two of damage. Meanwhile the gap between warrior ac/hp and knight ac/hp diminished leaving the warrior with only his discs to seperate him.. and compared to knight utility spells (heals, buffs, fd, snare) this wasn't much.

Most warriors seem to be clamoring for some sort of snap aggro. Not me. I don't want to be a knight, I want to be a warrior. More importantly, I want the knights to retain that unique ability.

What I want is a way to for casters to feel comfortable knowing that standing between them and the mob is a human shield ready to throw down his life to protect them. Enter the /shield command. This was a good concept in theory, but lousy in practice. First, the range on it is much too short. Generaly the only time I need it is when the mob has run to the casters and by the time I'm in range to /shield, it's too late. Second, unless I plan to redo 5 hotkeys every time I group up or untarget the mob I'm supposed to be tanking w/ an assist, I have to type out the command by hand. By then, it's often too late, and I'm a fast typist. Third, the fact that it has a limited duration and a reuse timer is inane. It makes no sense.

Here's how it SHOULD work. I have /shield off or /shield on. If /shield is on, then I will automaticaly /shield anyone in my group/raid that is taking damage from a mob I have targeted if they are in range. The range should also be increased somewhat. In addition, this should generate some aggro as the mob tries to push me aside to get at the caster. This would be far more representative of a warrior leaping between his friends and his foe and shielding them from harm. It would also encourage the casters to run back to the tank if they do get aggro.

If more than one warrior has /shield on, then only the one closest to the person getting hit will activate. There are other issues to consider, such as it shouldn't be possible to shield the MT. Perhaps only let casters and priests receive shielding?

Granted, for some raids this won't fix the problem. There will be mobs that still require snap aggro for a variety of reasons. Let the knights tank them. I don't need to be the preferred tank class for all situations... just for most.

Warrior aggro is fine. I have no problems holding aggro in exp groups or tanking on raids.

Knight aggro is fine. Knights can get aggro-lock because they have VERY high aggro spells that take very little mana to chain cast. Knights *should* be able to out aggro warriors.

The only remaining class-balance problem I see is the gap between healing a warrior and healing a knight is not big enough. All Defensive/Stonewall do is put the gap a little higher than it should be for 3 minutes. I would prefer that SoE delete those two disc from the game and tune warrior innate mitigation +30%. That's less than Defensive, but it would create a permenant healing gap between warriors and knights. They get the aggro-lock and we get tanking. That's balance.

An example of the tuning I'm talking about is on LDoN hard Adventures, an EP group requires cleric healing for both knights and warriors. Properly tuned, warriors should require druid-level healing while knights would still need clerics. The tradeoff would be aggro-lock vs lower-healing. Knights could still do their tanking jobs just fine, but warriors would be more appealing for groups that don't have clerics.
