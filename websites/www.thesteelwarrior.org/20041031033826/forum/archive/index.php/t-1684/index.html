Solinar
Well you have to balance two things.

1) The strength of the guilds main tank affects the guild on nearly a 1:1 ratio, while the strength of any other single member of a guild affects that guild on a 1:60 ratio (or however many are in your guild). Put in different terms, double the strength of your MT and you can nearly double the strength of your guild as a whole. Double the strength of any other single person, and you increase the strength of the guild by 2-3%.

2) You cant just give everything to a MT because its an upgrade. It hurts your guilds morale to do so. So you have to balance whats best for the guild logistically with whats best for the guild's morale.

wandor
I would agree that you should go for the biggest upgrades your guild is capable of. No point in getting a 5ac 10hp upgrade on your boots when you can get a 20ac 100hp upgrade on your helm. One thing that helps a lot if to do an upgrade search at Allahkazam's (might be a premium option, but its cheap to buy and worth it). Just enter all of your gear and have it search for upgrades with what ever criteria you think is important. I use AC x5, HP x2, STA x5 and select my upgrades that way. Look where your items fall and what your guild is currently capable of as well as what you expect to be capable of in 2-3 months.

Once you have a list of upgrades, order them by the relative increase in stats per slot so you get the most bang for your DKP buck each time you take an item. Be sure to make a plan and stick to it. All too often I have seen people loot a minor upgrade on day, then get what now amounts to another minor upgrade a couple of weeks later because they where impatient. They could have save themselves DKP and given an upgrade to a guildie had they waited for the bigger upgrade to begin with.
Gobbo
The first thing I do is look at the 'modifiers' list for HP in magelo. It lists the HP in each slot from highest to lowest. Then I go and do an advanced item search at eqmaps for each of my lowest hp slots - you don't need a membership to do this. You can check the boxes next to the items your interested in and hit 'compare' to get all the stats in table form. Anything that you don't know where drops, copy the name and paste it into an Allahkazam's item search to get where/what drops it.

The second thing I consider is how long this upgrade will last. The ideal situation is that I will only start replacing DKP-bought items after every other slot has DKP bought items in it. It has been pretty easy for me, up untill now, to do this - my guild is at the stage where we have done everything in luclin and are starting towards the ele planes. Its hard to decide whether to get the low-hp or low-ac raex slot items (which are big upgrades) in VT, or hoard points till we start getting ele armor. It doesn't help that we have been getting shit drops on belt/back/face/earring in VT. Not to mention the lack of bloodfrenzy.

On whether or not I take loot - if you are picky enough to only get upgrades that you won't re-upgrade for a long time, then take everything you want. Like someone said earlier, the tanks' gear is the most effective upgrade for the guild overall.
Kellaen
Decking the MT in minor upgrades does help the physical side of your raid, but you have to remember there are what, 40+ other people who's thoughts a gm / loot handler has to take into consideration for things.

That said as the others have suggested, run magelo and check your modifiers by hp. Work on the lowest slots first, keep ac in mind when you make said choices as well. Since you are basically your guilds raid MT talk it over with your officers, leadership. Talk it over with the rest of your warriors, and guildmates. Most loot problems come about from lack of communication, and there are times when a slight guild advancement may not be worth the personal anguish it can cause.

Something to think about. If you want my opinion you don't seem like a loot whorish type at all, or you wouldn't have come here to ask. Your helm / neck jump out over all other slots currently, i'd work on them first if possible. Good luck to you and your guild.
