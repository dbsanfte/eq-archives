Haven't been to WoS yet, but from what you're describing it sounds like it could easily be compared to Rathe Mountains. I know there are other zones like that, but I had a good bit of personal experience pulling there about a year/year and a half ago while playing my druid.

In a zone like that where it's first come, first served, I would say just try your best to be fair. If you see a mob aggro someone and start running after them, back off. If the mob is just standing still or wandering around at their normal pace, plink em with an arrow and pull away.

There's always going to be a gray area in pulling in a zone like that due to the fact that not everyone's computer/connection speed is the same. On your screen it may look like you've aggro'd first, but you really didn't due to lag. The same could be said for the other person of course.

As far as named mobs go....again, all you can do is try your best to be fair. Technically you cannot "camp" a named. The first person(s) to engage has(have) the right to attempt killing the mob without others interfering. Of course, imho at least, it's always best to respect others' camps, as that is no doubt what you would like if the situation were reversed.

Basically, it just comes down to the most basic principle in life(at least it's the most basic principle by my way of thinking), which is to treat others as you would like to be treated. Do your best to be fair-that's all anyone can do.
