I registered on these boards pretty much only to reply to your thread.

As others have said, LDON gear is great. go for the 1492 breastplate in nro, and I would suggest getting some 760 point EF armor just so you can built up fast. (You can always sell it back later pretty much to get some more nro stuff).

I understand you're not raiding and that you might not have a whole bunch of bazaar cash - but even with the items you've bought, you could have bought items with more HP on them for the same cost (at least on my server).

For example:

What the hell are you doing with a cobolt bracer? No HP. - buy a new one (but keep the one you have for shrinks if you want). Fine heraldic, anything, just get one with HP.

-You have a 30hp breastplate. Now, I KNOW you can buy that architect BP from the nro ldon (or you can just get it by doing nro ldons they are common) for about 4k on my server. Those have way more hp (and sta) than your current bp.

basically to sum this up:

---Buy new items to replace items yo have with low or no HP (bracer, bp etc).

Everfrost LDoNs are great and the armor is almost as good as North Ro. Additionaly EF is where you get your hate augments. Most EF armor is only a few ac and 15 HPS less at about a little over half the cost.

EF is also a LDoN where CC is optional. 2 Good tanks are usualy more then adequate if no bards or chanters are available. Most of the time (like East Commonland) you can power your way through. I enjoy setting groups up for EF (ESP since I have a Shaman and BST friend who is always group with) Just need a healer and I can usualy adjust the group from there.

So my advice is go to EF get a healer (Cleric or Druid) and slower then make another warriors day by inviting him to Tank Mezz. After that just see whats available (remember necros at this level can mezz those skellis)

For NRO LDoNs (65) remember that mages were made for it (Higher nukes against summoned, mezz 80% of the mobs, even charm)

This is from my experience and other may have better advice. Don't be afraid to fail. After making a few groups you really get a feel for what class combinations work.
