Warriors do not suck in PvP. I spent four years on a PvP server, I know.

It used to be that casters ruled, root could often stick, most spells would stick. Then came the damage nerf, spell damage was reduced 33%, or 1/3rd. Casters still were superior in PvP. Then came blue diamond resist gear and suddenly the playing field was level, with melees even having a slight advantage. Then came insane resist gear and very high hp gear.

Now, at least at the highest levels of the game, casters spells just get resisted. Nearly all the time. A shaman doesn't have a prayer of sticking a dot. A wizard can only stick a lure, and not always even that. A necro can rarely stick anything. A mage, hehe, good luck. An enchanter, well they can always rapture, but good luck killing a melee with an enchanter. Casters suck in PvP. Melees own them. Melees resist everything, have tons of hps, and hit very very hard, especially with discs. Warriors are no exception, a high damage 2H weapon, especially the 60+ dmg weapons, coupled with mighty strike, or mighty strike while berserk just rocks.

Comparing warriors to sk's also is unreasonable. Comparing warriors to bards is also unreasonable. Bards and SK's own in PvP. The next broken class is enchanters only really due to rapture. The rest of the classes take backseats to them, and I'd have to say a warrior is better than a lot of the other classes, better than a rogue, better than a monk, better than a shaman or wizard or mage or zerker. Sure, not at the top, but nowhere near the bottom.

I'm also not talking about abilities like manaburn or lifeburn or strong root. A wizard with a good enough mana pool with strong root and manaburn owns, as long as they are only facing one combatant. Also, let's compare PvP indoors vs outdoors. In a tight indoor zone a caster doesn't have a prayer. Outdoors it's closer to a level playing field, but the melee still has the advantage.

Lets dispel some myths.. lot of crazy out-dated assumptions running around.

While other spells like snares and roots and unresistable malises may never stick on you with high resists, the newer generation of upper end nukes have a very odd resist code. Sometimes it doesn't seem to matter what your resists are. This applies for clerics, enchanters and mage firebolts in my experience.

I've heard testimony from a good team bard, that at 500 Fr even, mage bolts land far more then wizard lures. My experience with this kind of phenomenon has been similar.

Some previous posters hit the nail on the head... in most duels yes, you lack the utility and lockdown power to bring someone down and win. PvP in EQ is FAR more fun in group type settings however.. because individual class balance issues break down and the experience begins to resemble more along the lines of what the game was designed for... groups. Picture a warrior solo on a raid.

Sure, some lame things like manaburn and harm touch are still around and hopefully will be addressed as there is an entire PvP forum now brain storming on the SoE boards... but there's a lot a warrior has to offer.

With a Plankton sword you are a monster. If you aren't in a position for that, then get a Fabled Exe Axe. Between every pass through or attack click your pumice. Just do it. Dispelling is unresistable, available to all classes and puts someone on the defensive. Caster on a drogmor already casting a spell? Too bad.. can't renew those instaclickies.

With AA's that increase your chance for those two handed swings/flurries, you can potentially level many classes with a lucky round from a Plankton sword while disc'd, or not.

If you have access to Vex Thal, pick up a Bow of Shadows. It's proc is a -1000 resist 1000 point mana sap. I've seen warriors and rogues kite paladins around with these things.

Always carry with you the proper potions to cure tash and other poisons and diseases. Have a alternate set of hotkeys and macros just designed for PvP... Crimson Potions, cheaply purchased anywhere, will shut down all but the most brutal enchanters.

It is very frustrating to see people give their upset 2 cents on PvP while not exploring any options as to how to better their experience of it.

There is a PK Warrior named Hdaorfx on Rallos Zek who is time geared and brutal. I've seen 10 people chasing him and hitting him not be able to bring him down for over a minute, and he nearly killed a bard who got too cocky while taking the onslaught. It was hilarious when he put his back to a walk and flipped on his riposte discipline when reaching berserker range. People just backed away and waited.

You do have something to bring to the table... or you can just be content to whine about what you don't have *points you to the monk board*.

Its easy to say PvP has no place in EQ, PvP isnt fair, EQ wasnt built for PvP etc. but people still do PvP, why? cos its fun and we all play EQ because it entertains us. I barely spent any time PvP till I was lvl 65, the occasional duel with a guildy or a friend but wasnt really interested. Then one day I was bored in the bazaar with LFG on and wandered into the arena part, started duelling and it was fun - it opened up another way I could have fun playing EQ.

Ofcourse I sucked to start with, had read posts about how to joust and what tactics to use in PvP, but its all pretty irrelevant until you get in there and try it. Now Im getting better and anyone who thinks warriors suck in PvP just isnt trying hard enough.

Dress wearers - easy. Get a big 2 hander and whack them whenever they stop running and try to cast. Only caster who ever gave me trouble was a necro with a SoW'd pet (thx pre-nerf fabled jboots - shouldnt happen again)

Monks and Rogues - easy. At the same level of equipment with a decent 2 hander you have a HP advantage, an AC advantage and you arent giving up alot in DPS.

Rangers - haven't lost to one yet. They dont have the HPs to compete, they dont have the AC to compete and they cant land spells on you. Shamans - Spent 15mins duking it out with a time geared shaman, he couldnt get slow to land, but he kept Q up and I couldnt kill him, we called it a draw.

Druids - A good well geared druid who knows his shit can be pretty nasty, the ones who try to snare and dot you are easy pickings.

Clerics, Bards and Paladins I havent had a chance to duel yet, SKs own me and Beastlords are like a monk / mage combo in PvP - lost to one with the full time gear package, havent lost to any others.

PvP is fun, PvP as a warrior is even more fun because people will tell you how much your class sucks and then you get to own them (SK's not included). Don't knock it till you have tried it.

FWIW in the Tribunal Melee royal rumble recently 1st and 2nd were warriors, 3rd was an SK. Sure tactics and team work are very significant in those events, but with warriors placing 1st and 2nd we obviously don't suck in PvP.

I'm on VZ. We're faced with PvP every night, every raid, every exp group and every zone just getting to an exp group. Hence I think it is very relevent to our class.

We are the least important class in mass pvp, when zoning into a zone to take control for a raid there is often advanced warning of the situation, and a guild will be waiting at the zone-in point to engage and try and take you out. Best move here is to send in a group of warriors and monks first - they take the major brunt of the initial attack and if the guild isn't properly hot-key prepared these melees will end up on most of your enemies target as the real damage dealers start to zone in and take them out.

Secondly, hot-keys are extremely important, have the raid leader pick the most important targets first i.e. clerics, chanters, announce the target and all engage the key pvp classes one at a time. I've seen raids with half the force of our enemy take out better equipped forces simply because we were better organized.

Never use 1h weapons in pvp becuase DS's will eat you. Stick and move err.. joust - keep moving all the time. If you've zoned in first and have 6 or 7 people all focussed on you keep them running around in chaos while your casters all assist eachother and take them out.

In a group situation where you might end up losing your exp spot, the best thing you can possibly hope for is that the group attacking your group will be dumb enough to focus on you first. Second best would be for them to all be on different targets. Always assist eachother in your group -- just have the puller use his inc. message and assist. As previously stated, well organized pvp'rs can beat better geared poorly organized ones.
