I do not enjoy being vocal on in-game issues here on the boards, but there is a reason for that, and it is this reason that I come forward here today.

My fellow warriors, here within this community we have a conglomerate of some of the most skilled warriors that exist within the game. Here, more than anywhere else, is a place where a warrior's voice can be heard, but I ask you this...

Look at our boards. Look at the topics in which the most grave matters are spoken of, watch how we speak and argue, curse and complain. Like children, we do not dive to the heart of the matter, as warriors should do, instead we revert back to some primal state. This site, this board, is a representation of the warrior body as a whole, a single voice. When you speak, it is not only you speaking, when you voice your opinion on this board you speak with the body of the entire class. I ask you now, not as a whole but as individuals, to stop and read those posts again. Now, imagine it said with your voice, without your consent? Is this not defilement in every sense of the word? We are taking what influence we have and smashing it against a stone wall, because we insist on acting like children. We abuse the very power and opportunity that this board gives us. We have a blade, but instead of using it, we dull it on the rocks of shallow complaint and pettiness

So I beg each and everyone one of you, stop for a moment, and look objectively at warriors and their position in the game. Stop for a moment and think about that post before you make it, stop and think if you're acting fairly, and I implore you to consider the fact that you use each and everyone participant of this board's voice when you speak out here.

If we are to have change, we must understand these things, and then present a united body free of these childish elements which sicken me and (I am sure) so many others. Every person in the world has the capacity for maturity within them, they must only be honest with themselves in order to find it.

To fight, we must first be one. Think on it.

I get groups pretty easily, and after I have grouped with somebody a time or two, I often get tells from them when their groups need a tank. I can hold aggro. Sure I don't keep it 100% of the time, my group members have to pay *some* attention to their aggro, just like they always have, but it's not like I can't tank, or I'm causing a lot of downtime for my group.

Taunt works pretty darn good now. I was in Fungus Grove last night, killing level 60 bugs in there (deep red to me at 56). I was tanking. I was "mostly" holding aggro off of a 65 ranger (that's 9 levels and 56 AA points he has on me, but my group still felt I was the appropriate person to be tanking). The enchanter was pulling with slow, and it never took me more than 3 taunts to get it off of him. A lot of times it worked on the first try. Is that perfect aggro control? No. Is it a horribly broken class that can't do their job? No.

On raids, warriors still get all the glory. Paladins and SKs tank adds, but they melt like sugar in the rain if you put them in front of high-end boss mobs. Aggro or not, there's no question who's gonna be tanking the boss mobs, it's warriors.

Here's the whole beef with warriors. In experience groups...sometimes...we have trouble getting aggro quickly. With a warrior tanking, there's a better chance that the slower will take a couple hits and it will take a few seconds for things to settle down and get the mob on the tank. During this time the cleric might have to cast a fast heal on a shaman or enchanter and waste some mana. In this situation, a paladin or shadowknight can get aggro right away. They have a little less hit points, but a lot of clerics feel that the better aggro control more than makes up for it, so given an equally equipped and aa'd paladin and warrior, they would take the paladin. I have never heard a cleric say that they refuse to group with warriors, or even that they don't like grouping with warriors.

Folks, that's a minor imbalance. That is not a horribly broken class. That is not a reason to quit your warrior. That is not a reason to daily post bitter complaints on what used to be the best EQ class board on the net.

Any Dev that reads these boards is going to see the outrageous hyperbole that is posted here daily, go back and look at the facts, and come to the obvious conclusion. "These guys don't know what they're talking about". Those of you that consider it your mission to save warriors by daily posting your exaggerated gripes are not helping the warrior class. You are turning what used to be the board other classes came to to get away from the childish whining on their own server boards into just another whine-play forum. No dev in their right mind would read half the crap on this board and take it seriously.

Please try to keep your posts to valid concerns and make sure you are making intelligent, rational posts and not just whining. This board would be a much better place if you did.

While I would largely agree with Brutal's examination of the situation, I don't believe that is truely the source of the problem (the whole slow agro business), I think the following is really the heart of the issue-

I think mainly mana regeneration has unbalanced the game and brought problems for pure melee to the fore.

Back in the day, casters had to sit for mana. Groups had to wait to pull until the casters had enough mana for it to be safe. You wanted to preserve all the mana you could because it was the blood of your group.

You also had to watch your agro, if you over cast, you couldn't sit because you'd get agro and die and therefore slow down the group, because you were low mana or dead (wasting time, et cetera)

Today...

KEI, Flowing Thought, HORSES, et cetera.

So, casters have "virtually unlimited mana", I honestly don't remeber the specifics of the last time a group I was in had to wait for mana, but I think it was right after tanking a named in BoT. Before that, I do not recall any instance of waiting on mana.... where as back in the day every few pulls you'd need to sit and wait at least a little to be safe.

HORSES - you don't need to worry about sit agro in outdoor zones... oh wait... the best zones to XP in 60+ ARE outdoor zones.

Other things that have gone to unbalance the classes are the variety of spells that have been added to the casting classes with very little in the way of "new" being added to melee.

Melee have gotten- /disc. itemization. AA. /shield in the case of warriors.

Casters have gotten - itemization. AA. Many upgrades to old spells, and new spell abilities.

Hybrids have gotten - itemization. AA. /disc. Many upgrades to old spells, and new spell abilities.

Playing from day 1 (well, darn close to day 1, if you all recall NeverQuest *caugh*) just thinking about the way the game has progressed casters tend to get all the new toys melee do (cool new items) and then some (Spells). I think this, more than anything else, has led to the current situation, the pure melee just doesn't have a mechanism to add nifty new things like spells can do for other classes.

The other change is obviously better itemization for hybrids (specifically knights) since the origonal game. Combine the changes to itemization, the mana regen, the lack of sitting agro, the outdoor dungeons and you end up with a lot of discontent for pure melee classes and warriors in specific.

I am hoping that the next expansion will be something of a "golden age" for warriors, making groups head back to the dungeon crawl, where warriors seem to shine the most (because casters need to watch their agro and sit to med instead of horses)... although I am not getting my hopes too high, and until then I believe I will focus on an alt or another game.
