I play a 70 Warrior and a 70 Cleric in both groups AND in raids. I was originally going to play a Shaman as a two-box, however the healing power of a cleric is eons ahead of any other class. You can, and rightfully so, outheal any other class any day of the week, and you do it in both efficiency and speed. Also, Divine Arbitration is a fucking godsend for when mobs come into camped supercharged, and no Light, Remedy, or CH is fast enough to keep you alive.

Overall, a cleric really isn't all that hard to 2box. It does require you to pay attention and keep your group alive as well, but you can play a cleric entirely from one set of hotkeys. It's not rocket science, and they're immensely powerful classes. What you do not get from 2boxing a cleric though is any sort of DPS enhancement. You can cast a reverse damage shield on the mob, but that's about it. You can nuke too, but cleric dps, overall, is pretty unsubstantial. A shaman could run circles around a cleric any day of the week.

What do you get by playing a shaman? Shaman heals are pretty decent, but the overall healing power of a shaman is 3rd on the totem pole (Clerics 1st by a large margin, then druids, then shamans). You can slow, obviously, which despite being an extremely old shaman "function", is still the most powerful tool they have in their arsenal. Those mobs in Riftseeker's hit freakin' fast as hell unslowed, haha. The new OoW buffs are total "pwn". Wunshis, Fortitude, Might, and Sense (and if you're lucky, the new Champion spell too) are totally badass. Even Ferine Avatar can make up for a lot of lacking stats if you're unlucky to have a full set of Raex yet (which most warriors do not have).

The most powerful aspect of a shaman? If you play them right, they hardly ever run out of mana. :D

Why do I play a cleric over a shaman? Well, my friend plays a shaman, but when you consider the amount of hitpoints a warrior has and how much of a beating they take, it's a shitload easier to keep yourself alive using a cleric than it is a shaman. Also, I frequently participate in my guilds/guild's (just switched guilds) CH rotations. I've grown to really adore the cleric class, and if I ever quit my warrior (which I never would), I would most definitely play a cleric. I love the class now. :)
