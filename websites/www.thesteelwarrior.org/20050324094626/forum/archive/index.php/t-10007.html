To be more specific about the mobs in OT, There are basically 2 types of each mob...

Succulents - Thorny and Spiked
Sabertooth - Tigress and Tiger
Cockatrice - Stoneleer and Stonegazer
Sarnak (warrior types) - Berzerker and Knight

Each of the first names are the lower level version of the mob. For instance, a Thorny succulent will always hit less than a Spiked Succulent, etc. At ~30 you should be able to solo the Thorny Succulents, Sabertooth Tigresses, Stoneleer Cockatrices, and Sarnak Berzerkers relatively easy. In addition to that, Sarnak Berzerkers all drop War Braids. Collect 4 of those and turn them into Captain Rottgrime for more EXP and faction.

To expound on some comments made; you want to stay away from Luclin primarily because the mobs there tend to have higher ac and hp values, and slightly higher dps output.

Anything old world will be good for you, because stuff was just easier back then. You say that you are done with Solusek A, but have you tried the Gnomes? They should be good until level 35 at least, and the ZEM for SolA is very high. Basically what you want to do is run through the hidden wall, which is near the wooden bridge guarded by 4 goblins over lava. You want to come out the entrance that has 2 doors and 2 goblins standing in the proceeding room; the door to the left leads to a zone line to SolB. Since you're soloing, you're going to have to worry about runners, so what I would personally do is clear all the greenies/light blues straight and left from the zone in, then hook a right go down some stairs and you'll start seeing gnomes. Pull those back to the zone in, and when they start to run you won't have to worry about adds from runners. Just watch out for the caster gnomes, as they pack a whallop.

As far as gear, cultural will be pretty ok for you as you level, as long as you can arrange to have your non-visible stuff to make up for the differences. Since you are a true lowbie, I'm assuming you don't have a large pool of resources to work off, so make sure you save everything to sell and whatnot. At level 46, consider looking into a pair of Valorium Rings. Mosquito Leg Earring should be relatively cheap by todays standards, and you can probably look into camping a Shell Necklace from Natimbi if you can find a healer and slower to go and hang there with you once you're level 50ish. 15ac 50hp and regen, not a bad necklace. Also look into doing the katta shield quest, the specifics are all over the quest forum of these boards, 50ac 75hp for a quest that (albeit long camps) isn't incredibly difficult.

As you get some more gear, here's a pretty encompassing experience path that you might consider taking, staying in high ZEM areas all the while:

Level 30-35 Solusek A Gnomes.
Level 35-40 Difficult choice with the disappearence of Mistmoore for lower levels, you might do ok in Solusek B, you might also consider soloing a little in The Overthere, just stay away from the Sarnaks.
Level 40-50 City of Mist, start at the zone in and work your way deeper as you get stronger.
Level 50-55 Plane of Innovation or Plane of Disease if you can get groups, Velks if you want to stay solo.
Level 55+ Can't really solo much anymore by this level, but you'll probably be able to find some groups at this point. Velks will always remain constant, albeit slow, experience past level 55, but the returns diminish greatly.

Mid 30s, to 40, the Yeti cave in Dreadlands is a decent spot. They have a tiny aggro radius, so single pulling them is a no brainer.

Late 40s, and early 50s, you might try Sea Furies in Ocean of Tears. They've got around 5k hp or so, but they don't hit very hard at all. It's not uncommon to see shaman and druids out there who might not mind a partner.

In your 50s, it's hard to beat Velks for soloing.

Restating some of what has been already pointed out ---

It is good to see questions from all levels of warriors on here, not just the big boys!

I have been playing EQ for a while at a laid back, non raiding type of pace (work schedule, type of gameplay desire, and PC gear some of the hardcore raiding issues) and since September of 2000, I am now a level 65 warrior with 37 alternate advancement points and counting. Guess what ---- I can still solo for xp (aaxp now)!! Some of that is stubborn, hardheadedness on my part because I like doing things a bit differently and some of it is just because I don't try very hard to find groups. I don't turn down groups, just don't often try to find them.

Anyways, back to your question about soloing as a young warrior........

I think the absolute key to soloing as a warrior (and warrior excellence in general) is learning. Learn the types of mobs, spawn points, do they run at low health, innate procs / ds / casting, how hard and fast do they hit, etc. After you identify this information (through in-game play, your mates here at the steelwarrior, various web sites and such), you then need to figure out how to best equip yourself to beat them down before they send you on that free port to bindpoint.

Some of the best soloing tools are still either beyond your resources or level for procs - (Truncheon of Doom, Silken whip of Ensnaring, Bloodpoints, fungi tunic, etc). This makes things harder but not impossible. You might give Allakhazam a whirl and do some searches there for weapons with slow / snare type procs, as well as gear with self buffs especially damage shields. Find things that could be attainable in the near future and make you a list of goals.

In the meantime, here are some actual spots to try :P

Again, in FM -- goblins near The Mines of Nurga (SEish corner) and the entrance to the Temple of Droga (NWish corner). Not sure on level range, since they have been green to me since I remember. Decent cash, up to 5pp and low end gems possible, xp, and most importantly ... FACTION and faction turn in items (green goblin skin and Mountain Death mineral salts). There is a mob inside the tunnel complex near the Burning Woods zone (goblin traitor) to check faction against. The faction to note is the Brood of Dizok. This applies to the sarnaks inside Chardok, a dungeon reached through Burning Woods. A very handy ring to be had as a result of a quest starting here.

In Timorous Deep, you could check into the ogre island in the NW corner of the zone. The Great Oowomp is a resident here, too. He might be a bit tough for a solo warrior in his 30's however -- darn shammy / necro NPC's are mean.

Unrest Estate might be too green for you, but worth checking into. Many mobs here don't run at low health, because they are undead. Not running at low health cuts both ways --- they keep swinging at you until one or the other is dead.

Lizards outside of the Temple of Cazic Thule (in the Feerot) might also be worth checking out, as well. PoK book right here in zone, as it is in The Overthere.

If you have Omens of War, always try to keep a task going. The xp and cash rewards for those aren't bad for the risk involved. I get just over a yellow aaxp from medium tasks now, as well as 116+ pp -- and can solo many of them.

Finally --- make friends!!
Even though I don't raid much, lots of people I have met in the game do. As they come across gear that no longer fits them, many will send the old equipment to their friends as gifts or at steep discounts from market prices. The vast majority of my gear has come as discounted hand me downs from friends or as a result of saving coppers and frugal shopping in the bazaar.

Good luck to you.
