I think your tactic(s) will rely heavily on your force at the raid. As I recall, when Paffa pops targettable (third wave, or fourth?) it's exactly as a final wave of adds spawn around him. I always had my raid force stand far back against the wall outside of his room (in the first room there where you can see his torso basically) just prior to targettable Paffa/wave popped, and I'd have a second and third tank assigned beforehand for the purpose.

Being the MT, we'd split the mobs prior so that my mob was dead well before final wave/Paffa targetable, then there's a couple options. Then I'd do as Fafnir stated (without riposte, since this was before multiple discs) and plunk the closest pusling pop, which would *usually* bring 2 or 3 of them (sometimes just 1 too, depending how you pull - try not to run too close to paffa or he'll smack you a few times and make you require a heal).

As soon as those first few mobs are out, you will then have agro on Paffa, and immediately instruct your second (and third, if applicable) tanks (rampage tank(s)) to run up and shoot and arrow/cast on Paffa. This will almost always agro the final mob(s) in his room; then they can bring them down to the rest of the raid.

At this point our tactics varied as we experimented, but with a raid of about 18 people most of the time (one CoD pet), we found the most efficient tactic was to kill TWO of these first puslings (since they can pretty much be killed before the final pusling pull is locked down by your Enchanter/bard/what not.)

During this time of fighting the first few puslings, since you have already established the Rampage tank (will be either your only second tank who pulled, or perhaps the third tank if you chose to use one; only really required if you want to take less risk and *may* lose someone during the fight), have debuffers do their thing on Paffa. Then when MT is ready to charge, give the call, run in disc/AE Taunt, and go to town. Suggest that you stick your back to the back wall so any pets you have will be attacking from the backside. This also forces your healers/casters to move into the room slightly to keep things spread out for your CC to keep things under control.

That's about it. You may or may not see a second wave spawn depending on your DPS, but if so, remember they will almost always go straight for the MT right off (or whoever has agro from Paffa the instant they pop) so make sure he has spam heals ready. However, due to the risk of agroing 4 puslings on a single healer, I reccomend using another Warrior if available to AE taunt the adds off the healer/whoever gets subsequent agro from the spawns. This Warrior can then /disc furious if necessary to allow time for lockdown. I DO NOT suggest you fight any of these mobs. It's a waste of time and resources and increases your risk greatly - they will despawn when Paffa dies, so why bother. You shouldn't have trouble burning him down after 50% health anyway, since he's stationary and all.

Have fun and good luck!

-Bashiae-
