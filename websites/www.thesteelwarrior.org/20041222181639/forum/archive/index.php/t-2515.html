Originally posted by Phantron
I doubt using just 1 EB weapon will beat BoW in aggro no matter what your other weapon is, since 2H weapons are supposed to proc about the same as using 2 1H weapons with the same proc (Primal 2H procs more often than Primal 1H for example). You will not outdamage BoW DWing so the only reason to not use BoW is if you don't want the aggro.

That is incorrect. Two-handers are not supposed to proc 'about the same' as 2 one-handers. Two one-handers will proc on average of 1.5 times more than a two-hander. There are also a few (very few) DW combos that will beat out a BoW DPS wise. Aggro wise? Iffy...

Consider that with about 1650 ATK and max haste you'll be doing about 150 dps ON AVERAGE to experience mobs with a BoW while tanking. Use that as the baseline. A SEWs will put out a bit more DPS. A Vanazir a little more than a SEWS and a good chunk more when MTing fast hitting ubers (assuming your riposte AA are all maxed).

And btw your proc rate is directly tied to your dexterity and has nothing to do with the number of swings you take (easy to test, get slowed, parse; use no haste, parse; haste, parse). Two weapons = more chances to proc over time, naturally. When someone tells you that a certain weapon procs like crazy or another weapon procs like shit, it is because the weapon itself has a mod that figures into the proc formula either negatively or positively, i.e. a Howling Orb might be telling the server to check -200 versus dex per swing (which would explain its asstastic proc rate).

With all that crap said, assume that on average a warrior with 0 CE will proc a BoW 100 times per hour on average (this number is actually fairly accurate). With 15% CE, expect to proc 15 more times over the course of an hour, i.e. 115.
