Read this (http://steelwarrior.xwarzone.com/showthread.php?s=&threadid=13) thread. Very informative. Essential info for warriors.

To give you a very quick overview, mob damage prior to calculating for AC mitigation (that is, mob's ATK compared to your AC) is explained by the formula DB + x * DI.

DB means Damage Bonus and DI means Damage Interval. All mobs have these two numbers set for them. The 'x' variable is a number between 1 and 20 -- exactly what it ends up is determined by the mob's ATK and your AC.

An example is Cazic Thule whose DB is 200 and DI is 20. That means his minimum hit is 220 and his maximum hit is 600. See how that happened? 20 times 20 is 400, plus the DB of 200.

Now, defensive is a DIRECT manipulation of the DI, not the DB. It basically divides the DI by two. Point being, if the DI is relatively low, mitigation (which is what defensive does) will be minimized.

So the basic theory is that you want to use evasive (which is avoidance versus mitigation) on those mobs with a smaller DI, since you'll be avoiding the entire thing, DB AND DI, as opposed to only mitigating an already small DI.

As a general guideline, anything with a DI less than 25 or so is probably better off being tanked evasively. Though there will always be exceptions, of course.

Actually it's not the PoP avoidance AAs that swing warriors towards Evasive lately.... it's their already huge mitigation.

Tanks with CS 3 and ID 5, sporting VT or Elemental armor (let's say 1700-1800 AC), already mitigate the damage of most mobs down very well without using any disc at all. You really need to parse a fight or two against a particular boss to see how you're doing for sure, but if you're already mitigating more than half a mob's hits to minimum damage, you'll probably get more benefit by just using Evasive.

My personal guideline is this - Evasive cuts down about 30 percent damage, period (this may be swayed a bit higher on mobs with nasty contact procs, since Evasive helps avoid them and Defensive doesn't reduce their damage). I parse one or two fights against a mob using Evasive, and see how my natural mitigation does. I look at the difference between the Average damage I took from a hit, and the mob's minimum damage. Using Defensive instead would reduce the average hit by half of that difference... if that amount is greater than 30 percent of a mob's max hit, I'm better off using Defensive.

I give Evasive a lot of love, but I tank primarily Luclin targets at this point, so it's appropriate. I've heard that Elemental tanks mitigate pretty damn well with their 1800+ AC, and it can also be beneficial to use Evasive there, even with the huge DI mobs.
