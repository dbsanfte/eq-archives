Cleric perspective: I think there are several sources of this problem.

After 14 adventures, I haven't seen that taunt is noticeably worse in LDoN; while there may be a small issue there, a lot of the "problem" is that people behave differently.

(1) No horses in dungeons. Casters sit down to med, therefore pulling more aggro from the gitgo.

(2) Time pressure. Whether it's a slaughter, an assassination, a collection or a rescue, you still have "X" minutes to kill "Y" monsters - so your casters, feeling that pressure, don't think have time to sit around waiting for your tank to build aggro. In their minds, they need to get doing damage right now, rather than sit around with their thumbs up their asses waiting for the tank's weapons to proc. And yes, that attitude will pull aggro off a knight as surely as it'll pull aggro off a warrior.

(3) No fear of consequences. As a cleric I've spent a fair bit of my time in LDoN tanking unslowed adds, but it's not like tanking in the planes... those "normal" setting critters melee like wizards and I mitigate most hits to ~80. To me, pulling aggro isn't lethal, just mildly irritating.

I set up quite a few pickup groups and of late, I've consciously taken warriors over knights on those occasions when I'm fortunate enough to have the choice. I prefer the extra damage output a warrior brings to the table and I'll accept that the casters are going to get thumped sometimes, because it won't kill them anyway.

Warriors hate this, but I've taken to chain-casting Destroy Undead at times. If I just stand next to the melees and open fire with both barrels, nobody's going to get seriously hurt because that mob will be dead too fast to do any appreciable harm.
