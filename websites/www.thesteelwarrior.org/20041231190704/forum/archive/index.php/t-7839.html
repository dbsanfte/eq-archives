Gunnar makes the *very good* point that looking ahead to upgrades that are likely to be available, and holding on to your DKP until the optimal point, gives the best results. "The thing is availability and long term planning is > applying a standard ratio to all drops."
Brael has always gone AC over HP, and likes the results.
Phantron says to use Bulwark of Alendar against procs, not Evasive.
Apolyon says: "GoD + Bard + Psalm of Veeshan + Singing mod + Singing mastery 3 + Amplification = 500MR", with which he resisted every single one of MM's procs.
Many people feel evasive gives better overall mitigation, but defensive reduces the risk of lethal spikes.
Some mobs, like Aneuks, are designed to require Evasive/make Defensive useless on them. Know your mobs.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=117181#post117181

Much good debate on AC vs. HP--go read the original. Quick summary: once you can't be one-rounded, AC is king. AC is harder to get than HP; HP goes up steadily with each expansion, but AC doesn't seem to by as much. Frodlin says that AC helps all the time (by reducing healing load), but HP only helps in the crisis when you very nearly die.
The change from a CHeal-dominated world to a splash-heal dominated world makes HP less important and AC more important.
Kailyn points out this is only true when your HP are far enough above the 7500 CHeal cap that clerics can safely land CHeals for full.
Emilia says AC gives more efficient tanking, HP gives more room for mistakes.
Phantron points out that at the high end, the best items have both high AC and high HP.
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?p=117322#post117322
