A more accurate formula for comparing weapon damage for 1-handers is:

primary hand actual effective damage rating is
((damage x 2) + damage bonus) / delay

off-hand actual effective damage rating is
(damage x 2) / delay

A 13/20 has a primary-hand damage rating at level 65 of
((13 x 2) + 13) / 20 = 39/20 = 1.95

An 11/18 has a primary-hand damage rating at level 65 of
((11 x 2) + 13) / 18 = 35/18 = 1.94

So the 13/20 will do more damage in the primary hand than the 11/18.

In the offhand, the 13/20 does 26/20 or 1.3, while the 11/18 does 1.2. The 13/20 is still better.

But now consider a 3/10.

In the primary hand, the 3/10 does
((3 x 2) + 13) / 10 = 19/10 = 1.9, or nearly as good as a 13/20 or an 11/18

but in the offhand, it only does
(3 x 2) / 10 = 6/10 = 0.6, or markedly worse than either.

Specifically, the damage bonus favors low-delay weapons in the primary hand, and the lack of a damage bonus in the offhand makes pure ratio the determining factor.

The damage rating for 2-handers relative to each other uses the same basic formula of ((damage x 2) + damage bonus) / delay

However, 2-handers and 1-handers are not even remotely comparable by these formulas, since there is no reliable algorithm presently available for taking into account dual wield success rates, Ambidexterity, Ferocity, Punishing Blade, etc., etc., etc.

But you can compare two 2-handers quite adequately using these formulas.

Run the numbers sometime. You'll be astonished how close a GSoT comes to, for example, a WMBS or a Windblade. Certainly close enough for the proc to make up the damage differential (and provide much better aggro to boot).

Hope this helps,

Assuming I understand such things correctly, damage per second (dps) calculations are a bit different from agro calculations.
Gront's post above considers dps.
Here's a thread on agro:
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=5864

That thread is based on one that's in the library on agro generation tests, which you should also read. Folks like Wandor & Haass would be able to correct me if I'm wrong, but my understanding is that there are three cases:

1. Agro from primary hand 1H weapon is based on this formula:
(damage + damage bonus + 1)/delay
The higher this number, the better the agro. This does NOT weight procs or any AAs, etc; in all of these formulas, these other modifiers of agro must be judged separately.

2. Agro from 1H in offhand position:
This is purely a function of the damage/delay ratio--the higher, the better the agro.

3. Agro from 2H weapons is based on this formula:
(damage + 2H damage bonus + 1H damage bonus)/delay

Read the post I link above for another link to a chart that lists damage bonuses for both 1H weapons and 2H weapons (1H and 2H bonuses are different).
To use Gront's sample weapons, but this time analyze them in terms of agro generation rather than damage output, I get this for a level 65 player:

Primary hand, 13/20 weapon:
(13 + 13 + 1)/20 = 27/20 = 1.35

Primary hand, 11/18 weapon:
11 + 13 + 1/18 = 25/18 = 1.38

What this means is that the weapons are pretty close, but in the primary hand the lower delay weapon comes out slightly ahead over time in terms of agro generation in the main hand position.

In the offhand position, since agro is simply a function of the weapon's ratio, the 13/20 weapon = 0.65; the 11/18 weapon = 0.61.

Thus, 13/20 generates more agro than the 11/18 in the offhand position. That suggests that the best agro generation would be to put the 11/18 in mainhand and the 13/20 in the offhand.

Again, even assuming I'm correct here, procs, AAs and discs can play a big role in the total agro of a weapon set up. If you are raiding, your weapons probably have procs on them, so keep in mind what Payn said about that.
Agro is more critical if you are main tank; dps if you are second tank or for whatever reason don't want the mob on you.
