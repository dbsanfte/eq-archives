The warrior is the tank. So if you want to be a Tank then be a warrior. Yes SK's can get aggroe faster and drain clerics of all their mana faster. But a good SK would not be taking aggroe while there is a warrior in the group. You need to make sure you use your taunt and disc everyfight . Make sure you have anger proc weapon or two and you can hold aggroe. Since the new disc and tech. have been added playing a warrior is how I always hoped it would be.

Take the road of responsability and get all defensive aa's and you will far out weigh the value of Pally's and Sk's as tanks. This of course will cause your time of anguish to be extended, due to your low dps.

After you get all your defensive aa's start working on the dps aa's and you will soon see Warriors can contribute as dps. Increasing the warriors ability to strike through or to strike more times in any given melee round will make a huge difference in your ability to hold aggroe. You will also find you don't need to hit that taunt quite as much.

Fast weapons with high anger augs are a must. If you are a warrior with 200 aa's and high anger aug procs with low weapon delay speed, Aggroe should never be a problem. A pally or SK would have to be really trying to get aggroe or depending on circumstances may just be in their aea of strength such as fighting undead. I was discouraged about the warrior for a while till I met another warrior Rafather. He is a great example of the dsp potential of a warrior. We did an LDoN together and being the main tank in most of the groups I can tell you this warrior with maxed out aa's was indeed uber.

After tanking with Rafather I realized there is light at then end of the tunnel for me and if I can stick with it I will be most pleased with my warriors abilities. No memming spells or worrying about mana , just pure innate abilities carrying me through a battle. And if trouble arrises I still have my disc and tech.'s The simple but powerful way of a warrior is the life for me. :)

If your just going to be hanging around one group - SK might be a better choice. Depends on where you want to hunt of course; I'd pick a warrior if you guys were just planning on being outdoors. Also depends on your group make up. If your going to have a beastlord to slow, a monk to pull, druid as healer, and only have one caster in the group - that's a good set up for warrior since if the others do catch aggro they won't die in a few shots - if its a group with one cleric, one tank, an enchanter and 3 wizards - well, SK/Pal is the superior choice.

SK's do have a nice line up of spells at their disposal which can really help if you've only got 6 friends you'll even see. Your group might not have a necro (corpse summons), or a monk (FD), and the loss in tanking ability won't be so signifigant that you'll have much longer downtime. Add to that, the aggro that SK can generate is better than warriors, so your healer will spend most of his mana on you can not the wizard.

All in all - SK bring more to the table than warriors do - although on raids its nice to have WELL GEARED warriors doing the fighting since they do take damage better and also can close the gap on the aggro advantage of knights. But you're not going to do that, and are only taking this guy to mid-level.

Mid-level for warriors IMO is the worst because the advantage of tanking isn't felt when the mob only hits for 50-100 and aggro is a major issue for warriors around that level.

I'm on MR now, so send me a tell if you have any questions.
