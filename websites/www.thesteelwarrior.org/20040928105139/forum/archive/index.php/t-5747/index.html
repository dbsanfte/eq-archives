When I built my box, I started out by watching the newspapers. If where you live is anything like where I live, every sunday (give or take) the big electronics stores like Fry's, MicroCenter, and CompUSA send out inserts with their sales. I'd just watch those for a good deal on a processor/mobo combo, or a good video card, or some other piece of gear that you're looking for. As an example, I got my athlon xp pro 1700+ and MSI mobo for 100 bucks...a friend pointed out the fry's ad, and we both did some checking on the mobo being offered, and it was a sweet deal.

Once you get a board and processor you're happy with, I'd start checking around for other bits that you want. newegg is great for good deals on graphics cards if you get tired of waiting for sales. So yeah, that's how I did it, and how I'd recommend other people do it.

Nayl
$2000 for a gaming computer is a lot. Here's a rough breakdown:

CPU - P4 3.2 or AMD 3.2, roughly 400$ - Im not familiar with the latest wave of processors, but my impression is that the 64 bit thing is not going to actually be useful until the software catches up, say in a year to two years, by which time it will be time to upgrade the hardware again...

Motherboard - ASUS is the best, imho, whichever CPU you go with. Get the best one appropriate to your CPU, will run close to $200.

Video card - ATI 9800 XT - Currently the best. Driver issues reportedly long since resolved. About $450

Sound Card - Not sure what to tell you. I got a Sound Blaster Audigy 2, and had installation problems that resulted in a HD reformat. Got it right the second time, but it still is buggy with some games. If I had it to do over again, I might go with an onboard sound chip, sound isn't that crucial to me with games. Sound Card if you get one = $150

Memory - Get two 512 mb sticks of the best 3200 ram you can. Corsair is good. About $300.

Hard Drive - Get something huge, like 160 gb, you won't ever use it all, but its stunningly cheap. Make sure it has a 8mb buffer, and uses a SATA connection. Western Digital, Maxtor, Seagate - about 150$

DVD/CD drive - For about $150 to $200 you can get a optical drive now that will read and record every format of optical disc out there. At this point, the best bets appear to be Pioneer and Lite-on.

Case and power supply - you have about $200 left of your $2000, I would suggest something aluminum with lots of fans and a good power supply - Antec, at least 400 watts. Lyan makes some nice Aluminum cases.

Advices for best cash/performance. Never buy latest CPU/GPU. Buy stuff which was latest a month or two ago. That way you loose very little in performance and save quiet a bit of cash.

Choice of mother board is one of the most important ones. Its called mother board for a reason. In this day and age I'd go for stability and ease of tweaking. I like ASUS.

I strongly advise against ATI since they never have been known to update their drivers and its the most important thing about graphics card. I'd go with NVIDIA. Of course if you planing to upgrade every year or so thats enough story

Soundcard- stick with SoundBlaster cause they have been THE sound card for games for last 15 years. Althought they almost never update their drivers.
