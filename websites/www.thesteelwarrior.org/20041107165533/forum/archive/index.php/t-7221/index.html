It's a pretty easy concept. When you die rather than loosing experience you incur a debt. At level 10 a single death incurs a debt of 200 points. So using one death at level 10 as an example, this is what happens. You start off with 200 debt points, each time you kill an NPC, who for our example will be worth 40 experience points, rather than the whole 40 going towards your leveling what happens is 20 experience points goes towards you leveling and 20 experience goes towards paying off your debt. Which means that until you pay off your debt completely, you have to kill twice as many NPCs to gain the same amount of experience towards leveling as you would normallly.

Or put another way, until your debt is paid off it takes twice as long to level.

I think my highest amount of debt at any one time was 720 points or there abouts. But I make it a point that when the going gets tough, the tough get going... :P
