once again, Haste theory :
1. 0% Haste ~> no swing speed change.
2. 100% Slow ~> no swing at all, thus it's the limite we can't go further. We have to wait an infinite delay before a swing hits.
3. 100% Haste ~> you swing twice as much (obvious, isn't it)
4. we can go further above with 200% , 300% , etc ... to infinite haste (continuous hits)

this give the formula : Length_of_Time_of_a_Round = 0.1 * Delay_of_the_Weapon / ( 1 + Haste% )

so with a weapon of delay 18, the normal round lapse is 1.8s ; at 50% it goes to 1.2s ; at 100% Haste it goes down to 0.9s ; at max overhaste it achieves ~0.78s with 130% Haste ; and on the opposite at 70% slow it goes up to 6s. it this time periode can can successfully swing / double / triple / flurry depending on your skills and wielded hand (afterall you are always successfull at simple swing with your primary hand :) ).

Haste doesn't decrease delay, it increases the number of swings you get in a certain time frame. Right now I believe the fastest you can get is 130% or so with a bard play Rizlona's. If you want to know what your delay figuratively is, use this formula: 100/(haste%+100)*weapondelay. At 100% haste you get 100/200 (.5)*weapon delay, thus cutting the delay in half.
