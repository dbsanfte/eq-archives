Tubthumpa, definitely get AE taunt next; it's one of our best aa's and I would rate it higher than getting max defensive first..

The reason TM isnt considerred a defensive AA is because parses seem to indicate that it does not negate riposte by the mobs; i.e. with TM while you can strike a mob on a swing by you that would otherwise be only riposted by the mob, the mob's riposte still lands on you. In otherwords, your swing may land through a mob's riposte, but the mobs ripose still lands on you.

It's still a good offensive aa though; it helps nicely with establishing initial agro that might otherwise be avoided by the mob's defenses. Btw, TM does nothing to help avoid being stunned; you want stalwart endurance for that. Whether an ogre need's s.e. depends on if he's getting shape changing illusions like NDT, or using a woodelf crown or guise of the deceivers etc for shrink/corner tanking I suppose, but I"m not positive whether you lose your innate frontal stun immunity in those illusions as an ogre since I'm a barbarian.

The top offensive aa's so far to me were ferocity, tm, and flurry; if you use 2h you might want to get pb3 before getting flurry due to flurry's high point cost, if you dual wield you may want to get ambidexterity first for the same reason. While fury of the ages3 is fun, Raging flurry 3 is definitely better to get first dps and agro wise imho. Since I primarily dual wield, I'm waiting on PB3 till after I finish planar power.. I'm a bit disappointed in PB tbh a feeling shared by someone else in another thread.

I don't know if this will help at all but here goes. I currently have around 250aa's.

After Defensive I'd consider the following:

AE Taunt - Every tank must have.

If you use 2hs, PB is a must have as well as Flurry + Raging Flurry
(With PoWaters 75dmg weap that looks like BoW I can average 150-200dps with those aa's)

To tell you the truth I didn't notice any difference with the Combat Fury and FoA so I'd wait on those.

Ferocity is a must have for either 1hs or 2hs as it helps with double attacks and the more successfull double attact you have the more your flurries will happen.

Ambidext- If you use 1hs over 2hs this is a must have as it increased mt dps 10%.

I personally noticed a huge difference with Tactical Mastery. As someone posted above it is good for offence and defence. The offence is you can bypass mobs ripostes, blocks etc.. and do more dmg. You can attack through enrages but I don't recommend it that much as you do not bypass every single swing. The defence part is you avoid ripostes from mobs and take less dmg. Let's face it swinging a BoC and Great Blade of Storms you swing ALOT when hasted hence have more chances of Riposte's.
:edit Just noticed the post stating that the riposte's still land. Even thou it's been parced I noticed a huge difference when taking dmg from mobs in normal grind groups in PoV as well as BoT and higher PoP zones. I believe I got TM3 bbefore ID's or LR's and went from 2 ch's per mob to 1 ch per mob.

Stalwart Endurance is a great AA to get as well if your in the top 3 of your MT ranks. If not it's not that important to get before the above mentioned aa's. Although it helps alot with BoT grinds or grinds where mobs stunn alot.

Ingenuity is just a fun aa to get if you have proc weaps other than EB proc. It's fun watching my GBoS proc for 358 crit spell and watch the noobies go wtf wars can crit spell proc?!? Yes there are still some. It's also fun as I have augmentations on both weaps(yes I got bored) on the BoC I have 50hp lifetap that crits for 100hp heals, and on the GBoS I have 50 fire dd 100crit which enables up to 4 procs off my 2 weaps. I can usually hold complete aggro off the 3 wars in the guild that use Blade of Wars.

I guess the main question is what weapons are your preference 1hs or 2hs, then choose from the descriptions above for your next aa choice.
