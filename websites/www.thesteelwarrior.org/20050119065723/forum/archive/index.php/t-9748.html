Actually, Tribute haste does go up to 41% haste and at 37 points per 10 minutes, it's easily sustainable with normal vendor trash and other loot that is so common that it doesn't sell well. For example, the tactitians bulwark as already stated goes for 11k(49 Hrs) tribute, but there are tons of other items out there that go for decent tribute like "a dragorn greatblade" which is a very common trash drop from OoW that goes for 250 tribute(just over an hour of tribute haste) or you can sell it for about 10 plat or so to a vendor, various GE drops which go for a 2-3k tribute(9-13 Hrs) to include "Book of Impracticallity which goes for 15K tribute (67 Hrs of haste). There are tons of drops out there that are so common that they are next to impossible to sell in bazaar, yet are the type drops that you can not sell to vendors even if you wanted to, yet they still have a tribute value that is decent. I'm a big fan of tribute haste and if you do the math on how many hours you play per week, you can see how long it will last you. Use "ALT+U" to open up your tribute window and you can turn your haste off if you are setting up in trader for the night or doing tradeskills, turn it back on before you fight. then upgrade your weak haste slot item to something substantial.

It's a really important item to have worn haste. I get the tribute haste thing. The ldon haste aug is definitely worth your time to get. I put mine in my NRo Helm and it's been there a very long time. There's a nice earring from doomshade in Umbral Plains(luclin) with 41% haste. I suggest finding a haste item as soon as you can that is worn haste. The ornate gloves are spell haste and give a buff icon when used. Spell/Song haste will stack with worn haste. Shammy buffs 50% haste and chanters buff 66% haste usually at level 65. This would give you 86% or 100% haste if you get the 36% haste aug. The aggro factor is a big part of how many swings you are taking.

The cloak of flames is a common 36% haste back item that is nice for new warriors. The price in the bazaar makes it a wishlist item, but soulscream at 31% is a good alternative. If you can do a hard 65 level ldon in Everfrost there is a No Drop back slot 36% haste item that drops there. It's possible to hold aggro with an 18 to 20 delay dual wield combo(Droga). The haste cap is 100% without the bard overhaste song. The value of haste over 80% is really not that important if you are dual wielding 20 delay and lower weapons. EQdiva.com has alot of good number crunching of haste.

I am speaking from my personal experience. I dual wielded 18 delay weapons with no procs before the aggro disciplines came out. Speed of weapons will gain alot of aggro. If you are level 65 you are going to need 36% haste to compete with all the other players in your group. The haste buff should be the same for the group since it is usually by a chanter/shaman/bst/bard in your group. So if you have little worn haste you will need fast weapons (20 or less delay).

There are some good two handers with special procs for massive aggro, but the two handers usually have large delays which require high haste numbers to keep aggro. The velium gemmed greatsword is a decent enough two hander with a low delay which makes it great for two hander aggro. It's the slow axes and swords with over 30 delays that really see the benefits of haste as compared to the low benefit of low delay(20 and below) weapons.
