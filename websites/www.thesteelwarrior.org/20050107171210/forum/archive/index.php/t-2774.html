This topic's probably been beaten to death but here goes anyway.

62 warrior here in a small guild which just took down Vindi for the first time. I was MA and after giving myself approx. 12 seconds to build aggro before calling assist I managed to hold aggro the entire fight without a single ping pong. Took about 4-5 min to drop him.

I used a Great Sword of the Bloodsworn 30/32 procs Acrimony 100hp/mana dot, basically identical aggro to a GSoT.

What I'm wondering is if it would be worth duel wielding something like a BoC/CHoS over the GSotBS. Yeah, the BoC combo would be much better aggro, but I can only imagine how much the extra riposte dmg would add up to on mobs like Vindi.

The way I see it: BoC/CHoS better aggro but more riposte dmg which leads to clerics going oom sooner. And our guild somewhat lacking in dps we really need for them to have mana for a long fight. There's also the possibilty of dying to the extra riposte dmg which could lead to a wipe.

GSotBS: Less aggro but also less riposte dmg. Of course, there's more of a chance I'll lose aggro which could lead to a wipe as easily as dying from riposte dmg.

And so I'm torn, should I go for the better aggro with BoC/CHoS or go for less riposte dmg with GSotBS?

Any advice is much appreciated :)

The problem is you can not think this way.

Dual wield = More agro = Shorter fight BUT more ripostes
Need more heals (so lotsa clerics)

2H = Less agro = Longer fights BUT less ripostes
Need more heals (so lotsa clerics)

Going 2H or 2x1H wont change the amount of healing needed.
Specially in your case.

A ripose in PoP is from 2000 to 3500 dmg.... Thats from 25% to 30% of the HP of a tank....
(worse case scenario)

A single ripose from a mob you tank will never be 1/4th of you HP.
So they will have no real impact on how well you tank.

Want my opinion ?

Dual wield while on defensive to try and maximise agro generation.

Once disc drops put a shield if survival is a real problem for you.
(u wont find a 2H >> 1H+Shield) at your level.

Something not mentioned here yet - Vindi's aggro is highly based on proximity (as are some other boss mobs), so you may not need the best aggro weapons for him if he's properly cornered and the other melees fight at max (or near max) range.

That said, I agree with the others. You don't need to worry about riposte damage so much that you should disregard a good dual wield aggro setup pre-PoP. A good aggro 2hander is nice, but most likely not a life or death matter. Just use whatever you need to keep the mob on you.
