I haven't seen it posted anywhere, but about 2 weeks ago SoE (or Ubisoft, I'm not entirely sure) closed the Korean Everquest server and moved everyone there to Maelin. Sony has been totally silent the entire time; the only way people on Maelin found out about this was from the Koreans themselves. One night people started appearing on the server with boxes for surnames, like so:

http://www.eqguild.net/guilds/valoranp/korea1.jpg

http://www.eqguild.net/guilds/valoranp/korea2.jpg

Most of the new xfers with the surname boxes have since changed those surnames, and with Sony's total silence on the matter, word hasn't spread much at all. Many people on Maelin still don't know. Maelin is a unique server (nearly everyone, including myself, transfered here with a raiding guild when the server opened), which is very quiet - we don't have a server board, and unguilded people are pretty rare - with a population that, even with the new xfers, we're still the lowest population blue server (though we have 3 guilds in time and another due soon).

Considering their situation, the Koreans have been great. They're *extremely* polite to the locals, and a lot of them are still fairly low level since the Korean server only had Kunark/Velious/LoY enabled. Most of them are a little angry at Sony, since they claim they were told they'd be moved to whatever server they chose, and they're learning fast that GM's don't exist on small servers - my own horror story being the time I waited 38 DAYS before my petition was ninja-deleted without an answer (<3 /viewpetition command).

What I wanted to highlight, though, by posting this thread, is that if EQ's customer base does take a huge dive, from WoW, Sigil or otherwise... don't expect much of a warning when servers get merged.

And of course, there's certainly significance in the fact that SoE actually closed a server.
