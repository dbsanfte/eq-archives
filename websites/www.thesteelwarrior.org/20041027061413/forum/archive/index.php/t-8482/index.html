Tarran Loreweaver
First of all, I'm talking about NPC flurry, not warrior flurry.

I've noticed over the past month or so that often when an a mob flurries I will get hit by it, even though I am not the one tanking. It seems to happen most often in phase 1 trials in Plane of Time. Best way to explain everything would be a quick example I suppose, so here goes:

Example, Warrior_01 is tanking Named_Mob in a trial.
Named_mob executes a FLURRY of attacks on Warrior_01!
Named_mob tries to hit you but misses.
Named_mob hits you for 980 damage.
Named_mob tries to hit you but misses.
You tell Warrior_01, 'Did you just get hit by that flurry?'
Warrior_01 tells you, 'No'
You tell Warrior_01 'I didn't think so, because it hit me, sorta like ramp or something'
Warrior_01 tells you, 'wierd'

I move away to go root park some other mob in the trial and while I'm out of melee range:
Named_mob executes a FLURRY of attacks on Warrior_01!
You tell Warrior_01, 'Did you get hit by that flurry?'
Warrior_01 tells you, 'Yeah'

As you can see, I'm getting hit by flurries that were obviously meant for some one else according to the flurry message and HoTT. If I move out of melee range, I stop getting hit by them and I will no longer get hit by them after I move back into melee range. From what I can tell, this only happens to 1 person per mob, so it isn't working exactly like rampage list. If the 1 person moves away, the flurries go back to the tank and seem to stay there for the rest of the fight.

If I had to guess, I'd say this is a bug. But since it could potentially be used to players' advantage, and I've posted about it now, I'd imagine it will get fixed soon.

So anyone have any thoughts on this? Anyone else ever notice this happening? Anyone know why it happens or what causes it?
