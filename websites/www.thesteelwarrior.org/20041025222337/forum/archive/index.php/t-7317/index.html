I left the warrior at 61 and 128 AA, then leveled to 65 with these around 50 group leader exp points (Health3, targets target, MA ring, spell awareness.)

So, the cleric lost around 26 AA (or more due to group leader exp loss of 20 percent from warrior) compared to the lower level warrior. In other words, I gained 28 more AA from being level 61 than what I would have had if I leveled to 65.

Also, I can say at level 61 and with ND3/LR1/ID1/PE I could tank 95 percent of the mobs that level 65 groups tanked. I even did hours of parsing against a level 65 with identical AA, identical ac (exactly the same value), and identical AGI to find no statistical difference between level 61 and level 65.

What I believe it all comes down to is this:

1) If you can get groups regardless of level, stay at 55 until you have the level 55 AA done (CA/CS/ND) then move to 60 and finish level 59 AA.

2) If you depend on pick up groups there is not option, you will get exp FAST because you will be LFG less.

Also, one factor may have been that at 55, I have roughly the same gear as I do today. I am 9335 buffed (no tribute or group leader exp) with 1780 AC buffed. I had around 8300 HP at 61, and 7500 ish at 55. So the warrior was slightly twinked.

At 65 I have to be in a really good group to manage an AA an hour now (or better; vxed is probably the best exp) and it has to be fast killing group. When I was level 55, I could easily manage an AA an hour in PoV and Hobgoblins in PoN.

Edit: of note, you want mobs to be within 5 levels up or down of the group members and the average group level. If you kill a mob that any member of the group is more than 5 levels away from it, someone (maybe all) will get less exp than normal. So, if you have a level 55 and a level 65, you can only kill level 60 mobs to have both gain their normal exp from killing a level 60 mob. You also may want to consider the average group level, since in this case it was 60, but if you had several level 65's and one level 55, the level 55 would not gain as much exp as he "could" if you kill as a group a mob higher OR lower than level 60.

In short, there is a whole lot of complicated parameters to this equation. That leaves the path of least resistance of leveling to 65.

Its very simple math. The xp cost of an aa is a fixed number, something like 24million xp regardless of level.

A group of 6 level 65's will put out more dps than a group of 6 61's (comprable gear, etc, etc). Stats, level, everything is higher. In other words a group of 65's will kill the same mobs faster than a group of 61's, this is fact.

So in other words if the mobs the groups are fighting are within the 5 level range the 61's will get more xp per kill and most likely get xp faster. However, if the 65's are fighting mobs that are within 5 levels of them they will also get the bonus xp. So you HAVE to compare apples to apples here. Now if you also account for the fact that farther in pop zones have better xp mods and the 65's (the case example was without a guild to flag them) have access to these better xp zones there is no question that levelling to 65 and then aa'ing is faster than aa'ing before levelling as long as you pick your xp spots intelligently.

Jerseth,

There is a fallacy in the arguments being made here... there is more content accessible to a level 60 character than a level 65 Character. The content that is accessible to higher level characters can be "keyed" and thus is still accessible to a level 60-62 character. The content that is no longer practical for a level 65 character (diminishing xp returns) remains that way. The alliance we are in already has main tanks who are 65 and have a lot of AA's (Yethuy, Ayida) The mobs you are fighting will not change significantly by getting all the way to 65, but there is a difference in the number of XP's you get determined by your relative level to those mobs. The higher the mob is compared to you, the more XP you get. Since AA's are a fixed number of XP's, if you are killling the same mobs you get more XP killing the same mob at a lower level. Since the group is not relying on you for DPS, and your buffed AC will exceed diminishing returns for most content, achieving a certain level (around 62) and knocking off your AA's will in the long run take less playing time than going straight to 65 first.
