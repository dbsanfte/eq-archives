I'll try and explain it.

There are two parts to a mob's damage, DB and DI. DB is the minimum hit that a mob can hit for. DI is multipled by a number between 0 and 20 and added to the DB to arrive at what the mob hits you for. Note that this works slightly differently for warriors with their -1DI advantage.

Hit = DB + (DI*x) where x is a value between 0 and 20.

Assigning random values of 250 for DB and 50 for DI, shows that a mob can hit for 250 to 1250.

Now, Shielding works on the DB of the mob, and the DB alone. With 10% shielding, the DB is reduced by 10%. So for the fictional example above, 250 * .10%, = 25. 250-25 = 225.

So.

A warrior with no shielding in the above fictional example would take:

250-1250 per hit

A warrior with 10% shielding would take:

225-1225 per hit.


Hope this helps.
