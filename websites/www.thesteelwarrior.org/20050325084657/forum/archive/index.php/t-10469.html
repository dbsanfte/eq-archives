Hamstring Hit move out of melee for a second turn back run past hit in the process continue on past while your weapon refreshes run past MS Hit you get the idea. I can go toe to toe with equal lvl or lower cons using my normal Dual Wield Weapon switch tactics but not very well vs higher lvl warriors. Disarm is the bane of Warriors especially if they use a 2hander. Duel wield warriors dmg is cut drastically but they can still hit with the off hand weapon.

Paladins are the most annoying in PvP their dmg is usually not a big factor but their healing and immunity buttons will drive you crazy. These fight tend to be long and drawn out, but if you watch for the right moments warriors survivability can really increase. First off I charge if possible build rage and MS as soon as I can this cuts their healing effectiveness and drops their health a good chunk. Then I switch to dual wield my hits are smaller and the dmg evens out making them comfortable with the timing of when they need to heal. I use as little rage as I can during the time I drop the middle portion of their health. At about 30-40% I've built up alot of rage I switch weapons and MS then Switch to Berserker and WW this usually drops them to around 10-15% health just enough for me to Execute the last of their health. If my hits don't land as hoped I don't panick.. at this time they pally is cuaght off guard. Sometimes they manage to get their "I win" i mean immune button off so I back up and Bind wound this usually causes them to attack me after only one weak heal to themselves since I used Mortal Strike. Then I continue to MS Execute if the fight gets close and I'm loosing I will pop Intimidating Shout then back up for a 3 second stun in berserker stance and the fight is over. Lay on hands is annoying but they cant use it that often so 9-1 isn't a terrible record for a warrior whom some of you consider to be the weakest PvP. Also you can pummel if they try to cast healing spells in combat.
