Firstly: At about level five you can visit a profession trainer in a city and learn trade skills, ask a friendly Guard and they will point you in the right direction.

Secondly: In the Capital Cities for your side, there will be Weapon Masters who teach you different weapon skills, additionally they train in different weapons at every capital so you will have to travel about to find them all. Good news is that the guards can point you to them.

Thirdly: Trade skills are different than in EQ, You will need to get up to 35 skill before you can attempt a recipe that requires 35 skill, but you won't be able to fail it once you can make it. ( Unless I am just an extremely lucky guy, and I don't have that kind of luck :D ) Skill-ups are easy as well, just find some cheep orange recipe and make them at one per skill up until they yellow out, then move on.

Hold up here, to unconfuse people that may be reading in:

You train per weapon type.

I.E. Two handed sword and Two handed Axes are separate skills not the same 2hs skill like in EQ.

Axes Swords Maces all have one and two handed variants (two separate skills each). Then there is crossbows, guns, bows, staves, daggers that you can train in. All of those are at level 1 IIRC. At Level 20 you can train in dual wield and Polearms. All of those are skills you can learn to use.

You can specialize in a weapon type via the Arms talent tree; Axes, Swords, Maces, or Polearms. Using a talent for Axes, Swords or Maces will get you the benefit with both 1h and 2h types. You need 20 points in arms tree (min level 30) before you can spec Axe, Sword, or Mace, and 25 points before you can spec Polearm.
