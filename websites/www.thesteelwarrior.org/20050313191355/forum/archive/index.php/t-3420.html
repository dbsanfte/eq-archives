Hey Warriors,

As an owner of the shield, here's my speculation/theory/observation (no parses though) about it over the last 3 months of using it very regularly.

It is half-useless (more on the useless side).

The mob doesn't flip to me when I bash for 120+ crit. There may be occasions where the mob did flip to me when I bash but it is so so sooooooo rare that I may have not noticed.

I have 3 weapons that I use, Blade of Carnage, Blasphemous Blade of the Exiled and a Prismatic Staff.

When using Blasphemous Blade of the Exiled, I hold aggro quite good because we all know that weapon procs alot. It's because of its proc that I am able to hold aggro quite decently. Downside is it has low HP (50hp only) and ok stats only.

When using Blade of Carnage, I sometimes hold aggro good, sometimes hold aggro bad. It's all about the BoC's proc. If it procs early, well good for you, the mob loves you. If it doesn't proc, you'll depend on taunt.

When using Prismatic Weapon, pray your taunt works or your team members get beaten on.


Good sides about the shield, you have fantastic AC. Trash mobs tend to not max hit you more, clerics complete heal me twice only instead of 3 times when exping in Plane of Fire first castle mobs. A wizard once commented that he could chain nuke a few times but not everytime. So the focus effect may have some effect on the mob but I have no way I can prove it.
As stated on another thread, my high AC can help migate some rampage hits especially from Warlord Gintolaken aka. Wipelord.

I have MTed Solusek Ro, Sigismond Windwalker (PoAir) and Blazzax the Omnifiend (PoFire) with the shield. I owe my aggro still to the Blasphemous Blade of the Exiled's proc and Orb of the Sky effect (Call of the Sky).
What I would like to try in the near future is MT General Reparm and Xegony and see how well the shield combo does compared with a Blade of War user.

So does the effect work? I would say more on the No side then Yes.

If they ever want to make shields workable for a tank, they need to have an effect that compensates for the obvious DWing/2hander benefits you're giving up. Yes, a smart raid could compensate for the lack of agro from not wielding in the offhand, but why would they? There has to be enough reason to make that extra effort.

Maybe a latent 50% spellshield.
Maybe a latent Bulwark of Vie.
Maybe a latent 50% extra agro (like BP or SK line).

None of these would be worthwhile using 24/7. None of them would be worth using in your regular DPS group. If you're fighting a mob with high melee dps, you switch in the second shield for lower dps, lower agro, but a noticable increase in melee tanking ability. Next mob has some nasty AEs, so you switch to the spellshield shield instead, still with reduced agro, but you don't get hit so much by the AEs. Maybe a skilled warrior could DW, switching in the shield when the timed AE is about to fire. Next boss has a slow/stun. Now you need more agro, so in with the third shield. 2 months later you've killed the first mobs a few dozen times, gotten a good slice more hps and AC, so you don't need the shield any more. Switch back to DWing or a high agro 2hander.

As it stands right now though, shields offer a negligable benefit in terms of ac/hp over dual wielding, at the expense of 25-40% less agro than an offhander. Than ac/hp difference is worthless if you're taking more ripostes than a 2hander.
