I have been meaning to post this for a while, but didn't want to start a new thread. I feel a new thread with a clear title will help catch the eye and allow others to post their own experience so that our community can make an informed decision.

After two weeks of testing I am sad to say that I prefer the GSoT aggro over CHoS and SotB. This is with my regular XP group ( me, 65 cleric, 65 rogue epic/primal, the other 65 rogue epic/primal, 65 necro, 63 ench). We know each other well, and they know the limits of my aggro. After many many hours of the 1h combo I was beginning to get a complex. I just couldn't hold aggro off the rogues for more than a few seconds.

I later tested this with a same cleric and chanter, and a pickup group of 58 mage 58 wizard and 61 ranger ( Swiftwind + 14/22 sword) . Each of these classes were pulling aggro off of me, so after an hour I switched my GSoT in and they rarely found themselves tanking. I am not a taunt-masher, but when using the 1h combo I found myself having to use taunt as quick as it refreshed due to others tanking instead of me.

I will say this, I may have to augment my AA's to be more 1h friendly before I pass swift judgement. My current offenisve AA setup is this:

When either CHoS or SotB procced, mobs only turned 1/5 times. I really do hope to improve my 1h aa and try again, because I enjoy dual wielding after many months of Windblade and then GSoT.

I also weild these 3 weapons with my now 59 warrior. My usual group consists of 59 war/65 wiz (both played by me), 65 cleric, 65 bard, 65 ranger and a 65 mage or 65 enchanter taking the last spot. We usually will hunt in CoD or PoS. I have had vastly different results with my weapons, but I think that is because I don't have any AA at all.

In my experience, all 3 procs have very good agro. The GSoT does proc a pretty good amount, but no where near the rate that the 1h combo does. When I was using 2h exclusively, I found that I had a bit of a hard time maintaining agro on an early slow. If I got a quick proc, then no problem - agro right back on me. I also found that I could not be as liberal with my nuking. As long as I had a couple procs, I could blast away.

With the 1h combo I have found that establishing good agro is faster. Not only is the DPS slightly higher, but I tend to get more procs per mob with the 1hers. Once I get a proc or 2, I can usually unload with the wizard

While none of these set ups is as good as a chain stunning pally or a 2x EB warrior, any of the 3 is still far better than was available to the casual non uber player just a short couple of months ago. For the effort or price involved in getting these weapons, they just can't be beat. Any warrior who does not have EB or a GBoS should carry these 3 weapons.

I don't know why in god's name someone would want 4x the reposte damage from 1h, when 2h does better dps and can achieve comparable aggro. The 2h only does better DPS than the 1hers if you have 2h DPS AAs. If you have no AAs or you have both 1h and 2h AAs then they parse out pretty equally, with the edge going to the 1h combo due to the procs. You should parse how much damage you are taking from riposte some time. On exp type mobs and the bosses that someone wielding these weapons is generally going to be tanking, the difference in damage is not worth the difference in agro. By the time you are tanking bosses in PoP where the riposte damage is significant, chances are that you will be weilding better than any of these anyway.

but I do find that the proc on the GSoT doesn't seem to get resisted as muchThis seems to keep coming up in this and other threads. If you parse it, you will find that the resist rate for each of these procs is exactly the same if you are parsing on the same type mob. Take a look at the resist adjustments, they are all are checked against magic and none has a resist check modifier. Any difference in reist rates for a period of time would be due to the RNG. Also, keep in mind that the stun and the DD portions of the SotB and CHoS procs are independent. A mob can resist/be immune to the stun and still have the DD land for full.
