Head to Crystal Caverns and run through the orc encampment. Everything should be green at 54, you'll get lots of little gems, velium weapons and other assorted vendor trash (leave the picks and shimmering velium rubies alone, they aren't worth anything). The big money, however, comes from the small pieces, bricks and blocks of velilum. Check the bazaar for prices on your server, establish a competitive price and set up a trader overnight or whenever you're not playing.

I was struggling myself at that level until I started hitting CC and farming it. I'm still nowhere near rich, but my gear is a whole lot better now.

Keep in mind that only the miners drop the tradeskill velium, and the watchman and foremen drop the weapons. Foremen can also drop the Skull Cap, Oracles drop a nice cloak, and Inspectors/Herbalists occasionally drop some random bazaar fodder as well. All of the pieces are lore, though. I usually just stash 'em in the bank and use 'em for a few easy points of tribute, as it's not always easy to sell newbie gear on my server.

Best of luck to you. =)

the options listed above are pretty good ones.
Well, I posted in the other thread about ice giants in Everfrost, which you should be able to handle. Get virtue the first time, see how it goes. Bring bandages. But the giants are on about a 7-minute timer and drop anywhere from 5 plat+ fine steel+ minor vendor items to 30 plat + fine steel + vendor items.
If you do start farming stuff, do yourself a favor and save anything that says it can be used in tradeskills until you figure out what it's for. Some giants drop items for the tempers in cultural smithing: ice giants, for example, drop essence of winter. Tribunal cultural armor requires two of these, and they are not common drops and not sold by vendors. Other giants droop other essences for cultural armor. Might be a bazaar market for 'em.
Poisen-making stuff often isn't worth anything, but any tradeskill item for smiths or tailors, particularly stuff that's not sold by vendors, is a potential bazaar item.
On your way to and from the ice giants, whack any mammoth you happen to see. tusks are heavy, but they sell well.
Also, start surveying any friends you have to see if they do tradeskills. You'd be surprised how eager your friends will be to help you go camp the soulscream if you given 'em half a stack of some drop they need for their smithing or tailoring or even alchemy. Not to mention the free buffs, free potions, etc. they'll eventually be pestering you with.
