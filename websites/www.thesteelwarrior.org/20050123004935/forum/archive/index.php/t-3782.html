Heh, I remember a long time ago, it was believed that 2handers were given a higher chance to proc...wonder if that's really true or if it was only because of the slower delay that usually were alongside the 2handers.

Anyways, on the ToD....Originally posted by Ealsur Helevorn
A proc effect can change based on your lvl? Never heard of that *shrugs*
The proc of ToD is 50% slow, whatever your lvl is, if your lvl would affect the proc effect, a 65 warrior would land a higher slow than 50%.

It might scale with level, but that doesn't mean it can go higher than 50%. Tepid Deeds (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=185&source=Live) is the proc on ToD...it scales up to 50% at 60. You cannot slow any higher than 50% from it, the spell won't go any higher...but if you're under 60, it can slow under 50%. If a spell proc couldn't scale, as was said, the Waning Light Katar proc (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=917&source=Live) would land for 84 damage every time...instead it lands for less if you're under 65 (81 at 62, goes up once per level).

On the other side of the issue...Originally posted by Crystara
Wavecrasher and Willsapper slow % scales with level, starting at like 1% up to their max at level 60, the proc from ToD is always 50% reguardless of level. Not exactly....Willsapper can scale, Wavecrasher cannot. In order to scale it must be coded to scale in the spell description...Breath of the Sea (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=1978&source=Live). It's a set 20% every time. Now take a look at the Willsapper proc Energy Sap (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=1960&source=Live). Scales from 20% at level 1 to 35% at level 60...about 1% higher each four levels. If you somehow get a Willsapper before then, it'll still be equal or higher than Wavecrasher (provided it can proc that low)
