Brabble
My standard advice is to go to the bazaar, carefully search each slot by AC, race, religion, class and start with a reasonable price range: say 2K. In your case, I'd just search by AC. Take notes on the cheapest AC for the buck (not only the price, but the other characteristics of the gear--resists and hitpoints).
You need more AC. I haven't played a small warrior so I'm not entirely sure what's out there for an agnostic dwarf.
A lot of warriors aim for around 20 AC/ level. That's not hard-and-fast, but it will give you something to shoot for.
I think you might find some upgrades in your armor slots for 2K or less in price.
You probably also ought to buy a Greatstaff of Thunder, which you'll be able to use in a level or two and which is cheap aggro.
Partly what to do next depends on how many friends you have and how much you play. You could start pounding LDoNs out, saving points for augments (aggro augments) and maybe some armor.
You might also dedicate some time to farming (I'm assuming this is your main and you don't have lots and lots of cash). Other posts here can give you some ideas about farming spots.
Concerning AAs: the first most folks get is Run3. After that, perhaps Regen 3. You need 6 general AAs, and most of them aren't worth that much. Run3 is still handy at lvl 65; most of the others are not worth very much at that point.
If you are as broke as you look, though, at some point I think you might consider putting experience grinding on the back burner until you can get some plat for upgrades (or put 90% into AAs until you get Run3 at least).
If you are in a guild, I'd talk to the other warriors in it, assuming there are higher-level warriors in the guild. They might be pretty happy to help you out.
Lathen
General Gear Advice:
Go immediately to the bazaar and search for "heraldic" with your race as one of the criteria. Once the results come back, be sure you're looking at the pieces that won't conflict with your diety. Now, buy as many of those as you can afford. Start with your waist, if the girdle is available. The heraldic will carry you well into your 60's. Other posters will have more specific suggestions for particular slots; my advice is for a general way for you to upgrade "everything".

AA Advice:
If I had it to do over, I wouldn't turn on AA experience until I got to 65. You get such a major boost in your effectiveness just from dinging up that's it's amazing. Furthermore, the general AA's (with the exception of Run Speed) are not worth the time you pour into them while you're in your 50's. That hard won exp pays better dividends if it's invested in levelling.

My 2cp -- your mileage may vary.

Brabble
Ah, yes, I suggested the Greatstaff of Thunder because you have a lot of expenses coming up, it will be a jump in aggro for you. You could also go to the bazaar and get the Copper hammer of Striking and Sword of the Bloodsworn, but again, I don't know how much plat you have and if resources are tight the GSoT is a great weapon for dirt cheap. If I had 10,200 plat and I were you I would not spend all of the plat on a Droga weapon: I'd spend 200 on a GSoT and dump the rest into either haste or AC. (You'd have to save more for the haste, though).
If your weapon skill in 2hb is low, it will only influence the damage you do, not the aggro the weapon produces. So you won't be endangering your aggro by skilling up in a group situation, though your dps might fall a bit. You'll get skill ups pretty fast, though.
If you don't get an aggro weapon then pretty soon your group mates are going to be yanking the mobs off you right and left. Or they will have to throttle back their damage output, causing the mob to stay up longer, causing you to take more damage, causing your cleric to be grumpy.
You can level to 56 for defensive, but again, if this is your main character you will find that you can level faster as a warrior than you can make plat for gear upgrades. I don't think you want to get much higher than lvl 56 while you still have less than 1000 AC.
I think Frostpaw's idea about Kael raids might be worth a look. There's a quest that starts in Thurgadin for Champion's armor that you might pick up some random drops for on a Kael raid; nowadays most of the folks on such a raid will have better gear than that and there might not be many folks rolling on the drop you need.
And yes, you do need some haste; there's a post in the library about warrior usable haste items. Check the bazaar prices on the stuff. I'd look for 31% haste, which obviously isn't as good as 36% haste, but is close considering it will cost half as much.
