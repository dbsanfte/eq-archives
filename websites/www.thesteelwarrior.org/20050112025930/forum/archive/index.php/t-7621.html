Bleh. Let's try this another way.
What elements of this problem do we agree on, and for which do we have shared and certain knowledge.
I imagine that:
1. We agree on the proc rates for all three weapons.
2. We know approximately what hate each proc generates
3. We know that over a long period of time, the proc hate of the 1handers > than the proc rate of the GSoT
3. We should agree that, no matter what we think about the various estimated potential dps fomulas, the testing that Brodda and others did clearly shows that weapons generate a set amount of hate per swing based not on actual damage, but potential damage.
4. We know the damage and delay of these weapons.
5. We should be able to figure out how many swings a given warrior would take with each weapon combination over a given period of time.

What we can't seem to agree on is the potential hate generated per second by the "potential dps" element of these weapons.

What I'm getting at is this; There are two possibilities for any given warrior:
1. The non-proc hate per unit of time of the GSoT > the non-proc hate per unit of time of the SotB + CHoS.
2. The non-roc hate per unit of time of the SotB + CHoS > the non-proc hate per unit of time of the GSoT.

If #2 is the case, then the two 1-handers exceed the hate generation of the GSoT by every measure, and would always be better.

If #1 is the case, then we need to know the difference in dps hate generated per minute by each weapon set. Then we'd have a known dps hate advantage of the GSoT. We ought to be able to figure out the "average" proc hate advantage per minute of the 1-hand combination. At some point, even if the dps of the GSoT is higher, the 1-handers' procs will overtake the hate of the GSoT.

The first question becomes when that occurs.

I think it makes sense to look at the average of the proc hate, even given the streakiness of the RNG, because the streakiness is a different animal than the average: the average will always be true; streakiness is a risk factor.

The second question is a bit squishy, but the answer has dramatic in-game consequences: how often does the dps advantage of the GSoT (again, assuming it has an advantage) -- how often does that advantage matter?
The only time the GSoT's (theoretical) potential dps hate advantage really matters is when someone else in the group exceeds the 1-hand hate by less than the amount of the dps hate that the GSoT has generated.
Here's a theoretical example: 10 seconds into the fight, neither weapon set has proc'd yet. The 1-handers have generated X dps hate. The GSoT has generated, say, X + 10 dps hate. Now, if a caster lands a spell at this point that generates X + 100 hate, then BOTH warriors will lose aggro. If the spell hate is <X, neither warrior will lose aggro. The only time a GSoT warrior will enjoy a practical aggro advantage is when a spell lands that generates hate somewhere between X and X + 10.

The smaller the GSoT's dps advantage, the less often that advantage will have a practical effect before the proc advantage of the 1-handers starts adding hate in, say, 450-unit chunks.
