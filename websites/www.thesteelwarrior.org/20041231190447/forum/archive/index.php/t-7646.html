Actually, Sinister Strikes is static and not percentage based. Weighed in at ~5 dps as best I recall.

If I recall correctly, the additional damage done by Sinister Strikes scales with the delay of the offhand weapon. Again if I recall correctly, the damage bonus is 1/3 of the offhand weapon delay, or 2/3 of the max-hasted offhand weapon delay. Since weapon delay is measured in 10ths of a second, that's 6.67 dps (if you made your Dual Wield check 100% of the time).

I'm recalling (please, everyone, poke me if I've misremembered the numbers for any of this) that with maxed Ambidexterity, Dual Wield succeeds something like 80% of the time. 80% of that 6.67 dps is 5.3 DPS.

So Northerner's approximation seems about right. Sinister Strikes should add approximately 5.3 DPS when dual-wielding.

My understanding, however, is that with maxed AA's two-handers are still better for DPS than 1-handers, so unless Sinister Strikes can be shown to improve aggro, I'd be inclined to avoid spending points for an AA that doesn't provide DPS when you most need it.
