I've had DirecTV for years, and the only times I've ever seen it lose signal is during particularly heavy fucking storms. IMO if your satellite is losing signal just because it's sort of cloudy or raining, it needs to recalibrated. I personally spent hours on my roof setting that dish at precisely the right orientation and signal testing until it was perfect - I don't know how dedicated a company installer will be...
An advantage over cable is that you can often get several channels in both EST and PST time zones, which I guess is really cool for catching all your prime-time shows and sports games and such, but I don't really watch TV except by accident, so I can't vouch for that myself.
