A few things to consider.

Vah Shir have to be agnostic, so no diety armor to twink with. Not sure which other races can't worship RZ, but depending on what you planned to twink him with, that may be a factor. This is really only a twink issue, as dropped gear without race/diety restrictions surpasses all the race/diety limited armor.

Barb, Ogre, Vah Shir all have issues cornering mobs in some locations (even fully shrunk)... easily solved via illusion - but something to consider if this toon will grow up to be an MT for raids. Gnomes don't need shrink or illusion to corner, not sure about halflings. Everything else will need at least shrink in some corners.

Slam is nice for interrupting, but post 55 kick also can interrupt, so the advantage is only while levelling.

As mentioned, Ogres have frontal stun immunity. Very nice. Advantage is partially negated at high level with stalwart endurance aa.

Halflings and Vah Shir can sneak. Handy for some questy things if you're in to that.

Gnomes can tinker. If you're into that kind of thing, it's a nice side benefit.

But like other people have said before... in the high end game, gear is the great equalizer. My guild has MTs that are Ogre, Wood Elf, Barbarian, and Vah Shir. All of us do just fine, and any differences in our tanking ability is gear and AA related, not race. We had a halfling for a long time, but he moved to a different timezone guild that fit his playtimes better - he was just fine as well. Play what will make you happy - the days of fatty tanks being far superior are long gone. Race really just doesn't matter. Well, except for the fact that Vah Shir get all the ladies... 'cause we're so damn sexay!

02-02-2004, 11:31 AM
One of the posts above suggests (probably with a measure of humor involved) that Fierce Heraldic armor "owns" Tribunal. In fact, the sets are substantially the same. I'm pretty sure all of the pieces have exactly the same AC. I *believe* that FH armor as a set has 5 less STR, 10 less WIS (heh), and 20 less STA than Tribunal. On the other hand, FH has +20 DEX, +25 AGI, and +15 hitpoints over Tribunal.

The Tribunal's WIS advantage, of course, is completely worthless; the basic differences are that you get more STA with Tribunal, and more DEX and AGI with FH.

FH is probably more commonly available in the bazaar, and thus perhaps a bit cheaper if you intend to buy all of it that way. But in both cases to get some pieces you'd probably be far better off to contract directly with a friendly GM smith, especially if you can bring them some of the more expensive components (blue diamonds come to mind) and some of the mob-drop only temper components.

Thus, if you are creating a warrior from scratch, and intend to twink him with player-made armor, the best plan is to ask the smiths you know which set they can make (if the race you're choosing has a choice). If you earned your plat in a legitimate fashion, checking bazaar prices and asking smiths you know will not seem like a big time sink. If you (and I don't mean the original poster, just any generic you) bought all your pp on Ebay, well, it really doesn't matter what you do (other than avoiding at all costs telling your smith that you bought the plat, as someone who took the time to GM smithing may well decide that he doesn't want your Ebay twink ass embarrassing his armor).

Both sets of armor are so good (AC of 327 and hp 820 and 835 respectively, if memory serves) that if you are wearing them and are lower than lvl 55, any problems you have staying alive are very very very certainly not being caused by your armor.

In either case, by far the most expensive thing to do is to contract with a smith to make you a set that your character cannot wear because you got the name of the armor wrong, lol. Believe me, it happens.

Oh, if time sinks do not frighten you, any set of armor you can wear you can probably make. All you have to do is a few thousand smithing combines and a little farming. How hard could that be?
