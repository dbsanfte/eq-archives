I haven't seen any guides posted, so here it is:

First off, Tactical Mastery is a MUST. You cannot live without this trait. Retaining rage when changing stances allows you to play to your fullest extent.

Second, spamming Heroic Strike is a sin. Don't do it. That little extra damage may seem nifty, but there's a myriad of other useful skills you can use. Cleave and Whirlwind being two notable examples.

There is one thing you must let your group know: you have to pull. If you don't pull, keeping the aggro will be hard. Even if you have paladins, you're the main tank. It's much better that they stand to the side and keep you healed. The group is best off focusing it's attacks on your target.

Sunder Armor is a great skill to keep aggro. Taunt has no effect if the mob is already fighting you.

For lv30 and below:
Start the fight off by charging into a group of about four, then use Demoralizing Shout or Thunderclap to at least tag them to you. From then on, use Cleave when there are two or more. If you lose aggro and you're still in Battle Stance, use Thunderclap to blow your Rage away on keeping a little hate, then switch to Defensive and taunt the mob back to you if you haven't gotten the aggro back.

Lv30/36+:
Charge in, switch to Berserker stance, use Berserker Rage once all the mobs on you and whirlwind. Bloodrage, Cleave, Demoralize, Whirlwind again. From then on, just use your wits. You can Cleave, Sweeping Strike, Demoralize, or whatever. The key is to use powerful area affects to hold aggro. The more you get hit, the better - free rage.

My personal suggestion for weapon choice would be a slow speed, heavy hitting two hander in later levels since both Mortal Strike and Whirlwind are both instant-cast skills that are based on your average weapon damage. Using a high-range weapon will optimize these two skills.

These strategies aren't necessarily meant for a two-man crew. It's for holding aggro in large groups.

In Zul'farrak, at lv43 (all the mobs are elites with at least +2 levels against me), I was able to hold aggro off of a lv48 rogue rather consistently. The only times it ever wandered was if someone targetted the wrong mob at the start of a fight.


For fighting one-on-one, it's really anyone's game. Personally, I prefer a slow, heavy hitting 2h with Improved Overpower, Mortal Strike, and the line of the Rend tree. Initiate with a charge, then demoralize/disarm (if they have a weapon), and rend. Follow-up by overpowering any dodges and Mortal Strike when convenient. Again, this was lv43, but against lv44 elites.


Added: I was also a protective spec til 41 and relied on Concussion Blow/Improved Revenge. I switched over to Arms and I've seen quite a considerable leap in damage.

1) Rend now does a healthy chunk of damage in DoT for only 10 rage (133 at lv43).

2) Pairing Improved Overpower with Deep Wounds and Impale is an awesome combo. It's like a free 150 damage DoT with a two-hander.

3) Tactical Mastery and Anger Management - nothing needs to be said here.

3) Weapon specializations are both very nice, even though I was an idiot and took Two-Hand spec thinking it read "Improve chance to critical strike with two-handed weapons."

4) Sweeping Strikes - 30 rage for 5 cleaves, can't get much better than that. Some say that Cleave/SS still stack, allowing you to do double damage to both targets.

5) Mortal Strike feels like one of those skills that warriors should have started out with. Basically, it's a beefy version of Heroic Strike that is instant-cast and halves healing effectiveness. Solo, it's awesome because of the great, quick damage that can be delivered. In groups, it's a wonderful way to get some aggro while dealing damage and blowing away some Rage to switch stances when you're over 55 rage. In PvP, it really takes the edge off of the healer types.

If I'm tanking with a healer:

I'm almost always in defensive mode, sometimes I may switch out to charge to get more rage or to zerker for whirlwind etc. Just depends.

If there are pet classes tell them to turn pet taunt off. Most people don't realize that Warriors are at their best when all mobs are on you and you're getting the crap beat out of you.

You need to pull. Pull aggro is very important in WoW, as is who gets the first hit.

If I get several mobs, I demo shot immediately, and then also try to tag at least one each with a hit and sunder if I have the rage. As was mentioned Cleave is also nice aggro in groups, so is thunderclap (its also and AE snare), if you're in battle stance.

In def mode with shield i'm usually shield blocking/revenging, and also keeping sunder, demo shout, battle shout, rend, and disarm as much as the situation and rage allows.

If I'm in dps mode:

I'm usually in Battle/Zerker stance. I always let the MT pull, and I never charge or even engage before he does. I try to charge to get rage, then bloodrage, then sweeping/cleave like a beast. I'll toss the occasional thunderclap or heroic strike in there too. Usually the MT is sundering, demo shouting, battleshouting etc., so I usually don't need to do this, but again its fluid so sometimes I do. If we have lots of lower level mobs (or just cause its fun!) I'll switch to zerker and enrage and whirlwind. I kind of feel that cleave and sweeping are more dps with several mobs and me not tanking though. The key here is keeping my rage up by charging and bloodrage as I'm not getting hit that much.

Good talents to have, sweeping is great, TM is essential if you're changing stances, etc. For defensive tanking I really like improved revenge. I'm mainly an arms spec but I do have ant 5, and toughness 5, which really help with AC and defense.
