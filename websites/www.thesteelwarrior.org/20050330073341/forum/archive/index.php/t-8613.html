May I ask why you are pulling the mob past the group?
Whatever the reason, if you run a mob by a sitting caster, the mob might jump the caster.
s far as using a bow on a rooted mob, usually not, I'd say. My guess is that the mob is being rooted for crowd control purposes and that there's more than one mob in camp. Kill the unrooted mobs first, then the rooted one, unless for some reason the rooted mob is a nasty caster. There are occasions when a caster might root a mob to allow for some sort of aggro control--the closest player to the rooted mob will get hit by the mob until root breaks, regardless of the mob's hate list. Generally, though, when crowd control is not an issue, it's best if the mob is not rooted, because a rooted mob will keep fighting until it's dead. If the mob typically runs at the end of its life, then instead of running it will stand there and beat on melee characters, causing more damage to the group than it would otherwise.
As unclebong said, a mezzed mob will not hit you, so if you are standing next to it, it'll just be standing there, drooling. Make sure the group knows that you and you alone are breaking mez; taunt until you get a successful taunt, then hit your incite line, then autoattack & slam mob & call assist (the last being optional, since mostly people will join you once the mob starts hitting you).
You are pulling casters the right way. If groupmates are jumping the gun, make hotkey that says /g Incoming %T PLEASE WAIT FOR MY ASSIST CALL BEFORE ENGAGING MOB!"
And then another hotkey that says, /g Please ASSIST ME in killing %T now.
If you play with the people in your group much, they'll eventually fall into a pattern and you won't have to direct them so much.
And, before pull you can always stand where you want to pull the mob to, and tell your group that you will pull to this point, and want to fight the mob here.
