The fact that a Knight controls his input into the group is precisely why knights are favoured over warriors - because their aggro and spells are a 'sure thing' whenever they are needed.

Even when a spell fizzles the knight can cast again within a second or two. When one of your skill opportunities comes up it can and will fail on occasion and then you wait until the next opportunity comes up in 10 seconds or 10 minutes to have another go at influencing combat.

The knight is in control throughout, you are never in full control, people prefer the sure thing to the 'wow, if we're really lucky he might do some cool stuff' character.

THAT's why RNG abilities suck big time in comparison with stuff within your control.

For random occurrances to make a big enough impact that people will take you over the sure thing will require the bonuses to be absolutely unmistakeable, not 'stunning a goblin for a combat round' at some unknown point during the fight.
