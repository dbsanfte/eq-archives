I was just thinking about something...what does a warrior need to do to be classified as a "good" warrior? Well, a good warrior knows his opponents, knows methods of getting aggro, pays attention, and knows how to use that taunt key properly, not like some paladin, etc. Well, to us warriors anyway.

But in the end it really comes down to how the group or raid works together, and the warriors equipment. Think about it. In a regular group a warrior just has to tank(or pull also), and that mostly depends on the group making sure the aggro stays on the warrior, the warriors weapons, and his hp, ac, and AAs.

Let's assume the warriors knows how to taunt, uses shield on the casters, has AE taunt, always uses kick/slam and disarm, and uses a gsot. If there is a monk with a really high DPS weapon pushing out insane DPS and doesn't bother to FD or stay at max range, the warrior can't do much to keep aggro. An overnuking wizzy will mess things up too. We can only taunt once every 6 seconds, and casters landing high aggro spells more often then that will surely get them beat up. And besides, taunt doesn't always work! There's more things a group can do to keep aggro on a warrior then a warrior can do to keep aggro.
Oh, by the way, let's say the warrior has a very good weapon, a BoC for example. Not only a warrior with that will do more DPS then one with an average weapon, 1 EB proc will keep him on top of the aggro list for quite a while even with a bad group.

And is it the warriors fault he takes more damage then another? One may be in a guild who's raiding the elementals, walking around with his 7k unbuffed hp, 200 AA, and a BoW, another may be in a family type guild that has just defeated Vindi, with 4k unbuffed hp, 30 AA, and a gsot. So, one will require a cleric healer in PoV and a good number of heals, possibly a med break once in a while, and the other can easily do with a shammy healer in the same place, and the shammy won't break a sweat healing him. So, because one warrior is in a good raiding guild and the other is in a low end family type guild, one is "better" then the other? Assuming both know how to play their class well.

And on a raid when main tanking some big mob, there's very little for the warrior to do in many cases. Heck, look at IV in akheva! The MT just has to get positioned correctly and then he has to tank blind for 10 minutes. Sure, in some fights the warrior has to actually do something, but most of the time the warriors job is very simple, and how well he tanks is only determined by his hp and the CH chain.

So...to a smart warrior, a good warrior is one that plays his class well...but to everyone else, a good warrior is just one who has good gear. Heck, some people don't even care if someone is ebayed. A friend of mine was telling me that he met this great tank, he's an SK and has over 9k hp(buffed) and stuff, elemental gear. I told him he's ebayed(and he is), and he said he's still a great tank because he still did good DPS and took far less damage then others(only thanks to his gear). I was really not happy to hear that.

ok...i'm done ranting, for now. Tell me what ya think, or flame away :)

There's a lot of things a "good" warrior does to differentiate himself or herself from average warriors that isn't related to gear.

1. Is the warrior conscientious as to the positioning of mobs to give others a clear shot at the mob's back?

2. On multiple undead pulls (high proximity aggro), does the warrior position himself or herself such that he or she is the one taking the majority of the beating from uncontrolled mobs.

3. If 3 mobs are mezzed, one just dies, and 2 are active, does the warrior hi f8 and break mez, or oes he actively seek the live ones?

4. Does the warrior use /shield? (This is the one thing that will differentiate you from crappy warriors easier than anything else)

5. On splitting mobs, does the warrior try to control the initial split via use of Rampage or AE taunt, or does he just pick a target and let the chips fall as they may?

6. Is the warrior prepared in advance with arrows or other range attack items?

7. Does the warrior have a knowledge of how to get from point A to point B without assistance? Does he or she carry with them appropriate potions (Invis/Gate/etc) to not burden the group?

8. Does the warrior have a variety of utility type weapons to compensate for a linkdeath of a party member, or the lack of a specific class? (Any warrior with a Journeyman's Walking stick is better than one without, and it's not about 'l33t raid gear, they run 400pp in the bazaar). Warriors with a variety of procs are of greater value than those without, get them. At a minimum, a journeyman's walking stick, a silken whip of ensnaring, and a wavecrasher will make you more valuable than the next guy.

9. A warrior who is impatient (I'm bad at this) turns people off who have better alternatives to the warrior.

10. You exude your level of self-confidence. If you think you're badass, others will likely agree. If you think you're a pud, others will likely agree.
