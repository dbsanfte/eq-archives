It depends where, how, and what you're exping on (as well as your role in your guild, if you have one).

I did defensive AAs first except for two exceptions: AE Taunt (of course) and Tactical Mastery, because I was duoing a lot of experience with my shaman (thus I was a major source of the "group's" damage). From what I've seen Tactical Mastery provides the best "bang for the buck" DPS increase, although from the front only (not an issue grouped with my shammy, since I'm in front of the mob 99% of the time).

I did TM1-3 after doing ND3, PD3, PE, CA3, CS3, and LR3 and don't regret it. If you do full fairly standard groups most of the time you should get all of the defensive AAs first IMO though.

For order of offensive AAs this also depends on how you fight:

Offensive AAs that work all the time:

Crit AAs, Ferocity, Flurry AAs

Only works with 2h (very nice if you have a good two hander IMO):

Punishing Blade

Only works with dual wield (good if you MT and use dual wield for aggro):

Ambidexterity

Only works from the front/sides:

Tactical Mastery

Only works while tanking (note difference from just being in front of the mob):

Stalwart Endurance

Only works with procs (and other clicky effects):

Ingenuity (mostly a toy AA from what I've seen)

Note I also didn't include a few such as Planar Power, Double Riposte, and Rampage.

Anyways, that's almost as broken down as you can get with warrior offensive AAs. It's up to you to decide which ones would be most effective for you. What's most effective for another warrior may not be most effective for you :)
