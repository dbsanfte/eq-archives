Don't believe that crap. This is how rampage works -

Old world rampage is very easy to control once you understand it. The rules are set in stone and are always the same. Every time someone comes up with some "mystery" rampage it turns out that other factors came into play and rampage did indeed work as advertised. These rules apply to all PRE-POP rampage mobs. With the advent of PoP, mobs gained the ability to AE rampage so the rules have changed, but they are still the same for pre-pop mobs.

Rules for rampage:

1. The rampage list is static and is not changed by zoning or death. The only things that will change the rampage list is a successful memblur, camping out, or on rare occassions when FD successfully memblurs.

2. The rampage list is set in the exact order that players get agro.

3. Rampage hits only 1 person each time it triggers. The person taking rampage is subject to a full normal round of attacks. If the mob quads and bashes, 1 round of rampage will be a quad + bash (if bash is up).

4. Rampage NEVER hits the person who has current agro, even if they are #1 on the rampage list. If the top person in rampage range has agro, it will hit the next person on the list that is in range.

5. Rampage has a range and it is different for every mob. Some mobs, the range is smaller than max melee range, other mobs the range is damn near the entire zone.

6. Rampage never his someone who has successfully FD'd. When the person gets back up they will resume their place on the rampage list unless they got lucky and the FD memblured the mob. This is why monks and SKs are great rampage tanks. If they miss a heal, they can FD until a heal lands.

7. Rampage is a proc. Cripple helps reduce the number of rampages a mob does.

The best thing an up and coming guild can do is to learn to manage rampage on a rooted rampaging mob - IV in Akheva is a really great training mob. You can see the rules in action, the results are replicatable.

Keep in mind that these rules only apply to pre-PoP mobs. AE rampage is a whole different beast.

Well from that quoted section he has a lot of things right but also a lot of things wrong.

Rampage list isn't limited to 3-4 spots. A whole entire raid will get on the rampage list all you have to do is agro said boss. The way the rampage list works is the first person who enters it ( through agro ) will be rampaged unless that person is also the highest person on the Agro list. If the same person is in the top possition on the Rampage and Agro list then rampage will hit the SECOND person the Rampage list.

Rampage can also have a range associated with it. Some mobs can rampage across the whole zone while others need to be in a certain range to hit their target. One person can be at the top of the agro list and then get knocked out of the mobs rampage range and then the next person down on the Rampage list will get hit by rampage.

The rampage list can only change through a memwipe from my experiecen. That's why monks / pullers, who would be #1 on the Rampage list since they agro'd the mob first, aren't always the Rampage Tank. If the puller brings the mob in , FD's and his FD is succesful in performing a memwipe then he will be removed from the Rampage list.

I don't know about the whole dying and being rez'd back to the top of the rampage list so I can't comment on that.

That's Rampage how I understand it and I am almost certain that's how it works.

Just a few observations.

Despite what most people think, the hate generated does have an impact on the Rampage list

False. The amount of "hate" either from procs or damage has zero effect of Rampage. The rampage list is set from the initial aggro and members are added in the order they piss the critter off.

Example:

Aggro Emp Ssra with your rampage tank, then have MT take over. Rampage will stick on the rampage tank until one of a number of criteria happen:

1. Ramp tank moves OOR
2. Ramp tank dies
3. Mob dies

Once one of those three criteria are met, rampage switches to the next person who got aggro after the initial tagger and MT. Generaly, thats the first cleric who had a heal land when MT was aggro.

We got got our asses kicked first trying Emp way back when because our healers would get the ever living shit kicked out of them by rampage. So we began using *every* monk we had to stonstance him spawn to get at least 3-4 people + MT on his hate list.

Now, the rampage list IS static in my experience. I have vivid memories of ramp tanking Creator in early Ssra, only to be rezzed and instantly die to rampage again. Rampage list is completely cleared only when the mob dies (or is unaggro but I'm not completely sure on that).

Best way to control aggro, is have your ramp tank tag the mob in, or if its a rooted critter have the ramp tank toss a few arrows or something at it before MT engages.

As long as the ramp tank tags the mob on inc BEFORE it aggros the MT (thereby getting clerics on hate lists), MT can pull off 1-3 arrow shots without problem and leave the ramp tank to soak up the rest.
