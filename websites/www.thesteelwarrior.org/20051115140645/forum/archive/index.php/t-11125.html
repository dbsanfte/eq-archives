"Am I just stuck in EQ mentality that procs are important for aggro? Is proc aggro so insignificant in WoW that 100 hps from sta on a weapon is better than an aggro proc? Is a Timeworn Mace really better than a Frostguard or an Annihilator?"

I use frostguard + icy chill for bosses I know aren't immune to slow, or for mobs that primarily melee, but no saying the proc will go off much before they're dead.

In the long run, procs dont really mean crap. I use a timeworn mace + crusader to min max defensively. Can switch in frostguard + icy chill for dual wielding or to slow hard meleeing mobs (albeit people like prince are immune, and a resisted proc generates 0 aggro).

We aren't pigeonheld to crappy aggro weapons in this game. If I wanted I could hold aggro with a toothpick and a rubber band. So go for stats or dps, and who cares about weapon proc. Even in defensive, our dps contributes. Locking down 4-5 mobs aggro I still have a ton of rage to heroic strike, or whatever else I feel like doing these days.

Also remember 90% of bosses and 10% of non-bosses use de-aggro abilities, so it all comes down to using our skills. Taunt, revenge, shieldbash, etc to get and keep aggro, relying on a proc in a 30 second fight will destroy you as unlike EQ you aren't getting and building aggro, you're getting aggro then getting it back, takes precision, not random chance.
