Taunt works by putting you at the top of the hate list. It does not add any hate if you're already on top, but if you are not, it bumps you up to top (aside from low health agro of course). Because of this, the ONLY time your taunt is not being wasted is after someone takes agro from you. I generally don't use any macros or mash my taunt key, but do this: As soon as I see tash hit, taunt. If taunt doesn't work, be ready to /shield the enchanter. As soon as slow hits, taunt.

If at any time you see a mob summon someone (you will not evade me, <insert name here> !!!), Taunt. If at any time you see another group members health bar dropping, taunt. In other words, any time you remotely believe you might have lost agro, go ahead and taunt. If you time taunts right, you will rarely lose agro, and your group will love you.

Mobs run fast, and if i'm standing there fighting and the mob gets slowed it will sometimes be beating on the chanty far away before i'm able to throw my unused taunt at it. Worse, sometimes i taunt but the mob is already too far away!

Two points here:

First, you need to be paying attention to your text window - watch for messages indicating when an aggro generator lands. Also, ideally, your casters are CALLING their casts (insist that they do). This way, for example, shammy calls "casting slow on mob_01" - now you know to watch for the message 'mob_01 yawns' to appear in your text box and are rdy to hit taunt if the mob suddenly turns (you'll get so good you will know right when the msg is about to appear - and catching the mob in the act of turning to maul the shammy and ripping his attention back to you WILL get your skills noticed and appreciated).

Same goes for any aggro generator (slow, tash, nuke, etc). You can look up the messages that will appear under the spell info on allakahzam - get to know them well and your groups will notice that you keep the mobs off of them and invite you back. I redirect all spell messages (and nothing else) to one of my text boxes and I watch that box like a hawk so that I can keep track at all times of who is doing what in my group - this is a key part of being a highly aware tank, and a highly aware tank is one that will keep his or her group alive and xping a helluva lot more than one who is not.

BTW - a lot of casters only call when their casts LAND - I ask the ones I work with to call when they START the cast so that I can be on the lookout either for a resist (no msg) or a stick (msg appears). Works very well. The idea is that, as warriors whose aggro is not near guaranteed like pallies and SKs, we have to use every trick we can to build and maintain aggro. Asking your group members to do their part to help you keep them alive is not asking too much, after all!

Second, tell the casters in the group - especially the ones who are not practicing (or even aware of) good aggro management and just can't seem to wait to start casting aggro generators - that they need to be CLOSE to you (just beyond max melee is good - you don't want them to proximity aggro the mob unnecessarily) so that when, as inevitably must happen, the mob turns on them and your taunt fails, you have the best chance to both protect them and regain aggro by:

1. Using as needed the /shield <insert caster name> hotkey you should have made when the group formed, and/or when group members changed. Note that shield does not work beyond a certain (short) distance, so if they are further away than that distance, you cannot protect them.
2. Continuing to generate hate by beating on the mob instead of missing hits while running after the mob who is running after the silly finger wiggler who has probably panic'd and is very probably about to die. Caster needs to stand there and let you peel the mob off of him via damage aggro and taunt (when it finally pops back up again) - and running may even put him or her OOR of cleric heals. Depending on the situation, I often can get aggro back even before taunt pops back up again due solely to the dmg I am doing putting me back on top of the hate list. In short, if they run they'll only die tired.

To sum up, if the casters are too far from you, then you cannot shield them, your taunt is useless, and you are not generating hate from damage or proximity to regain aggro. If casters in your group can't understand that the safest place for them is relatively close to your side after you explain things to them, then you need to find new casters.

As for taunt/hate list, the consensus seems to be that there are two ways to move up the hate list:

1. incremental quantities of hate building up from melee dmg, spells, procs, etc - this appears to be additive. Say your melee damage has built you up to 400 hate and shammy lands a spell causing 500 hate - shammy is now 100 above you (and mob turns on shammy) until your melee damage or a proc or whatever puts you back on top.

2. taunt: this is not a quantity-of-hate added per se like above - rather, taunt 'pushes' you onto the top of the hate 'stack' - so there is no amount of hate like 30 or 100 or whatever associated with taunt. When successful, it simply puts you 1 above whoever was at the top before you taunted.

Also, most tanks I know do not mash taunt when MTing a raid, since if you have aggro already it does nothing for you, but if this works for you, feel free...

If your group spells is on, you can pretty much always tell when someone has a high chance of getting aggroed, i.e. the first two "Enchanter begins to cast a spell" and the second "Shaman begins to cast a spell" in just about any pulls.

Get ready to taunt right away as soon as the spell lands, even if it doesn't look like it's summoning/running after the slower. If you see "XXX yawns" or "XXX slows down" taunt right away. Remember that your kick does stun and you might've just stunned it so it looked like it's still on you. It never hurts to taunt after a tash or a slow. If the spell is resisted, then you'd have to guess when it happened (since you don't see the resist mesage). Usually, I taunt again after a few seconds after when a slow normally would've landed. As long as your timing isn't horribly off, your taunt will be up again when the second slow comes in (especially for Enchanters since the slow takes a while to cast) so you don't lose anything.

Likewise taunting when any premature big heals or big nukes land is always a good idea. Even the most suicidal of people usually don't do two consecutive huge aggro spells in a row, which means you shouldn't have to worry about taunt not up for the second spell. It might not do anything. It might fail. But it might also work and save your group a deal of mana. Even unslowed, there is often enough time between a mob is aggroed and a mob is ready to attack again for you to taunt it (since taunt is instant). There are lots of time I'd see a caster summoned while sitting down and a successful taunt brings the mob right back, and the caster would sit smack by the mob as if nothing happened. Oh yeah, if someone is summoned, always taunt too. :)
