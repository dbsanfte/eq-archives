In brief: if you target a player and type /shield, or type /shield Playername without targetting them, then you will take part of the melee damage that the other player receives. The other player receives 1/2 damage and you receive 3/4 of their damage (yes, this means some extra damage is produced). You have to be really close to the person you are shielding, basically within arm's reach of them. You don't have to keep them targetted, though. You can continue fighting and doing everything else you normally do, as long as both of you stay close to each other.

Shield lasts 12 seconds and recycles every 3 minutes. Use it anytime a fragile party member is getting beaten on. You will be able to use it every 2nd fight or so, if needed. It's more useful once you reach the level where your healers have Complete Heal, because then there's an actual mana advantage to having one person take most of the group's damage. At later levels you can get AA's that let you extend the duration up to 48 seconds and increase the range. Equipping a shield is supposed to reduce the extra 25% damage produced when you /shield, but as far as I can tell, this has always been broken.

Everything Sassinak said is correct as far as I know; I'd also add three more points:
1. You can hotkey this like so: /shield <your slower's name>. If mob jumps that player, you just run over to them, mash that button, and you never have to untarget the mob.
2. Shield very much appears to have a taunt element to it. Quite often you will find that the mob turns back on you immediately after you hit your shield hotkey. I haven't done controled tests on this, so I can't tell you exactly what the hate component is. And, the history of players' understanding of game mechanics is a history of people who were "sure" of something they hadn't tested, and were later found to be wrong. Nevertheless, I'd be shocked if /shield didn't add to your hate.
3. You will have to tell your groupmates how this works, at least at first. You do have to be close to the character you're shielding, which means that your casters can't be running around in circles or doing other stupid things when they get hit. They just need to stand real close to you. When you shield someone, everyone can see a message something like, "So-as-so begins to use you as a living shield"--so it does look like you're doing something, which I have found has a calming effect on the more nervous casters. Shield can also be used to help enchanters who are trying to control the mob by re-mezzing it (and don't want you to be hitting that mob). The damage you absorb helps the chanter last a bit longer.

I can see the rationale. Shield is better than defensive for the person receiving it, and not much worse than defensive for the warrior that's doing it.

A fully slowed mob only swings twice in 12 seconds. With you diverting half of the damage, and the other person having CA3/LR5, it is easily possible for the other person to survive much longer than 12 seconds, even on mobs that hit for 1300+.

Taunt does not work very well in SolRo and later zones. Having shield last for 24 seconds is a huge help I've found, and I can see a definite reason to further extend the timer. Often the 24 second shield wears out before I can get aggro under control.

With shield effects running 1/4 of the time (48 seconds per 180 seconds), each warrior could preemptively shield someone who tends to draw aggro, before it even happens.
