I am the raid leader of a casual, non-raiding guild. All of my guild members are working people with time constraints. Amost all of us have been in this game since it started in 1999. My guild can normally sees 12-20 on a weekday, and can field 4 groups for raids on the weekends. Almost every main is 65, has lots of AAs and is very well-equipped from PoP and LDoN. I am a 7k+ unbuffed warrior who has pretty much everything I can get with my guild.

As the raid leader, I have to sit down before the weekend and plan what we will do. My constraints are: less than 4 hours per raid, can't require keying or flagging, must allow for late additions and people who need to leave at short notice.

In short, I am a serious casual raider from a small guild. I have no idea what proportion of the EQ playerbase I represent.

Lately, planning Friday raids has grown tougher and tougher. None of the recent expansions has offered anything for people like me. Very little pre-PoP that is unkeyed has anything to offer that would excite anyone in my guild. And we're sick of pre-EP PoP where we are stuck. Where the hell do I take my people on a fun Friday night?

You need to be able to feel like you can progress in games like this. This simple fact is that for serious casuals like me, there has been very little progression in the last year. There're just glass ceilings everywhere. And I'm sick of hitting them.

Blizzard just has to say "size-scalable content for all levels", and my guild will be out the door in a shot. And this saddens me, as I'd love to keep playing EQ, but there is nothing left.

Honestly, I'd hit GoD a little more than you might presently be considering. Although raiding Ferubi for random drops (and a few of the nameds should certainly be very, very possible) will provide you with more bang per raiding hour than anything else short of just splitting into LDoN groups. The biggest problem is that there are less "targets" and more simple clearings. Still, taking three somewhat independant groups into Ferubi would provide the AAs, the potential for *excellent* random gear and (properly coordinated) the potential for good gear/augs through some of the nameds.

Sadly, LDoN might be your best progression option though. Smart players that are undergeared can still mow through LDoN easies or (with some effort) hit up LDoN Hards for upgrades and points towards upgrades. I'm a pre-elemental Rogue and have pulled a number of LDoN hards as long as there is any CC to speak of (multiple root classes or multiple healers, although ideally a bard/chanter or proper puller is nice) and we've tanked them with pre-Ssra knights comfortably as long as people are paying attention. I've yet to hear of an LDoN-orientated progression guild but it honestly makes more sense than the old Luclin/VT cycle in terms of RvR these days. And no, I don't mean the raids at that point in progression.

Other options include out of the way targets like Korocust or even odd ones like BoT towers (easy with masses I guess but quite doable with strategy and some finesse). Luclin targets as a whole are not so good anymore imo, given the potential in GoD for smaller forces to at least get *good* gear, even if more randomly. Ironically, I'd consider Dain/KT/AoW to be better RvR even today than Luclin for the most part. If you can do NToV, it still offers pretty nice RvR also, although you'll deal with tons of rot and a short raid with in-and-outs is poorly suited there.

I would avoid the key-and-raid for ST though these days. It honestly doesn't provide anything of real value anymore, primal included.
