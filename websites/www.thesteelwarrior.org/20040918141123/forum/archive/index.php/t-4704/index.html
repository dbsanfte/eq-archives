Smackadabut
I am sure that something to this effect has probably been brought to the surface in the past, or when LDoN first was introduced, but I have a serious quarrel with LDoN.

I am sure you are all familiar with the "trapped" chests, boxes, and what-have-you. I have also noticed that nearly every class has some special ability to see if it is trapped, how it is trapped, and disarm the trap.

What are us warriors supposed to do about this? Just stand there, and pray someone doesn't set off the trap? Or do we just smash the daylights out of it, like we did to the former owners of this object?

I seriously think we should have the common sense as warriors to at least look at it, to see if we should bash the crap out of it, but our mighty creators have apparently deemed us too simple-minded to take on such a task.

Apparently warriors such as myself are too dumb to figure out what the difference between a normal box and a trapped box looks like.

Am I alone in feeling some sort of frustration over this? Or are we supposed to be nothing more than meat shields, existing only to take damage and scratch our heads when asked a question?

/rant off
Wompem Ondahed
If you are looking for RP justification of having a sense magic skill or something like that then I think you may not find a good one. We as warriors do not spend our time learning about magic, but instead about all things combat. In a roleplay sense we would recognize the more common detrimental spells being cast because everywhere we fight we see some finger-wiggler chanting the exact same mumbo jumbo and then we feel burned/sick/poisoned etc.

Traps are the land of the rogue, and I would leave it to them. Since magical traps are now in use they are the land of the magic user. When no one else can get into a box for whatever reason, I let them zone out after the adventure, then I sometimes go mad-nasty on the box. I could die, I could get drunk, I could just synge my eyebrows, but I won't endanger my group to do it. We have an ability to open these, but truly it is a last case scenario.

Btw, /inspect a box and it will tell you if it has magic runes or whatnot. In the beginning it would sometimes set off traps, but for the last month I have not had one go off because of /inspect. Think in an RP sense, you are inspecting it ( shaking it to hear a rattle, or running your fingers along it to see if it has magic dust on it etc etc). Would you really try to get into locked safe with a crowbar if you thought it could be rigged with dynamite?
