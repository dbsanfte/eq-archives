1: Nope, there can be as many instances as you want, up so some number that hasn't been discovered yet. I doubt there's a acheivable limit since it's just saving progress rather than any large amount of data.

2: New instance starts the second the first person in the raid zones into Phase 1. All people in the raid who aren't already flagged with another instance, get flagged with this instance when they zone in. Anyone already flagged with a different instance can't zone in.

2a: When can the next guild go in?
You can't have 2 instances running at the same time. A new instance can only start once the current instance finishes. If you zone into P1 and straight out, it's an hour wait for the new instance to start. If you time-out halfway though P4, a new instance can be started immediately with P1/2/3 up. Remember though, this is ONLY for new instances - you're still flagged for 6 days, so if you were to go back immediately, youd go straight to P4 once you complete P1, with whatever gods you'd killed still dead.

I *think* it's 14 hours from the start of a complete clear before the zone's ready for a new instance. It's possible for two guilds to go on the same day in different timezones, but prolly safer just to go on seperate days.


3:
Monday/Tues: Guild 1
Weds/Thurs: Guild 2
Fri/Sat: Guild 3
Sun/Mon: Guild 1
Tues/Wed: Guild 2 etc...
That's the easiest way, provided you want to know in advance. We've got 2 guilds in Time on Zeb. We're farming the entire raid in 5 hours or less, the other guild are progressing, and are up to Quarm now. Basically, we get priority whenever we can start a full respawn instance, the other guild get it the other 5 days when we can't go back. Works fine.
Monday: Guild 1
Tues/Wed/Thurs/Fri/Sat: Guild 2
Sunday: Guild 1
Mon/Tues/Wed/Thurs/Fri: Guild 2
Monday: Guild 1 etc.

4: Whoever got to PoTime first, is probably clearing fastest. Let them do their 1-2 day clear whenever it's up, and then you have 4-5 uncontested days left before they bother going back.

Least I think that's how it works
