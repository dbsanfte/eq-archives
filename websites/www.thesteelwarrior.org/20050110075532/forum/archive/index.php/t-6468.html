The problem with comparing avoidance and shielding, is that it will obviously vary from mob to mob. Let me do some simple math for an example. The numbers are just made up because i have actually never parsed quarm, but he is very simple since his db is an even 1k. Slowed quarm has a delay of 51 or 5.1 seconds i believe, just assume 1 round every 5 seconds. So 12 rounds per minute, 4 swings per round, not counting bash/kick/rampage. 48 swings a minute and an average of 10 minutes to kill him. I belive our best time is just under 8 mins. So now you have 480 total expected swings during a 10 minute quarm fight. I have shielding 14 and avoidance 30. Shielding 14 will cut every HIT by 140 damage, and avoidance 30 will cut his hit% by 3%. Assume quarm has a hit rate of 45% over the 10 minute span and his average hit is say 1900(these are numbers i made up, i dont have a recent log in front of me). Assuming 480 swings during the fight duration, 45% or 216 will land with avoidance 0, 42% will land with avoidance 30. 3% of his 480 swings equates to 14.4 hits, round down and multiply by 1900 which is his average hit. This gives you an estimated damage avoided through avoidance of 26,600, or 44.33 dps(also remember quarms dps is only about 500 to begin with).
Now lets calculate shielding. 216 times 140, which is the value each hit will be cut by with shielding 14 will give you a pure shielding value of 30240 or 50.40 dps. However you have to take into consideration 3% of those hits will not land because of your avoidance. 480 times .42(your hit% post avoid30) gives you 202 hits at 140 per hit or 28280 (47.13 dps).
As you can see the higher your avoidance, the less the value of shielding becomes as a mob simply misses more. Now lets equate 10 avoidance to 1 shielding.
Avoidance 30 Shielding 14
44.33 dps 47.13

Avoidance 10 Shielding 1
14.78 dps 3.37 (based on 30 avoidance, scaled to 10 it is 3.52)

So on quarm 10 avoidance is equal to 4.20 shielding independant of armor class.

One warning is that shielding dps is NOT linear based on increasing avoidance, as higher avoidance will cut your shielding dps since the mob will now hit for a certain % less based on the avoidance value.

I hope this helps to clarify how you can calculate the breakeven on mobs using their db values, hit%, and average hit values.
