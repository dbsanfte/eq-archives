For a max level warrior with 230 taunt a good sample size to determine success and failure is the following

(((Zvalue)^2)(p*q))/e^2

Where zvalue represents confidence level - 99% confidence would be 2.575

p = percentage of of occurances that show up as true
q = 1 - p
e = error rate

Thus, for a +- of 1% error rate on a 99% confidence level with the most conservative possible scenario(not having a basis before hand of % occurrences being true) you would have the following:

Sample size = ((6.63)*(2500))/1 = 16,577

The 2500 is derived from p = 50% or maximum unknown.

Thus, testing taunt on a known level mob 16,577 times will yeild the appropriate sample size for percentage determination.

It must be on the same level mob for the results to be representative.

sorry if this is too much math for some, but sometimes I see people sitting for hours and hours hacking away on a mob or doing test results and the 100,000 + samples provide nearly zero improvements in a conclusion.
