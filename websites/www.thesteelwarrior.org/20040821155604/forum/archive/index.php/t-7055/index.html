Posted this on the EQLive forums, crossposting here to see what ya think.

There are very few things in the game today that are as unbalancing in the game as defensive and evasive.

These two abilites are so powerful, content in the game is balanced in the game specifically so that it is challenging, WITH the use of defensive and evasive. Along with clerical complete healing, it has been breaking the game for years now.

Nowhere is this so apparent than with the recent GoD issues. With content being designed FOR the use of defensive, the other two tanks are in danger of being marginalized in favor of warriors. This situation is exacerrated by the ever more commonly placed defensive requiring mobs that are placed in new expansions.

Defensive is overpowered and unbalancing. In their quest to make new content challenging, the devs are breaking the game. Content that is challenging for knights to tank, is trivial to a warrior with defensive. On the other side of the coin, content that is fine for warriors with defensive to tank, is excessively difficult for knights to tank. Defensive is breaking the game.

What is on the lips of many players of the game, is giving a lesser form of defensive to knights, so that they may be given an improved ability to tank mobs that are balanced for warriors with defensive. I feel that that is the wrong approach. I believe that for the health of the game and for the ultimate happiness of the players, it's time to get rid of defensive and evasive in its entirely. These problems are just going to keep occurring over and over again until the problem is fixed.

Here's how I feel it could be done.

#1: Give warriors a permanent further increase in damage mitigation and avoidance, so that the remain clearly the best choice for tanking nameds. Not nearly as much as defensive and evasive give, of course, but enough to offset the fact that defensive is gone, but that this increase is on a permanent basis. I feel that a 7.5% bonus to mitigation, and 5% to avoidance, is a balanced amount to give.

#2: Rebalance named mobs by reducing the DB and DI. Reduce these numbers by about 25%, so that while they hit a little bit harder to a warrior with no defensive or evasive but improved mitigation and avoidance capabilites, that is offset by the fact that they will still be getting hit for that 3.5 minutes later, when the pre-changes warrior would have defensive wear off, and be taknig the full force of the mob's attacks.

#3: Compensate the loss of defensive/evasive from the warrior's ability set by giving them some abilities that allow them to contribute to the group in places where the warrior's superior ability to take damage is rendered irrelevant and useless. After all, in most groups, as long as the tank can take damage "well enough" to not die, they don't really care HOW much better he is than the next guy. This is why you hear beastlords, rangers, and monks tanking in groups in content that is considered trivial. They tank "well enough", but they add significantly to the group in other ways.

Specific suggestions for the warrior ability sets are short duration (think bard song) affects that are endurance based and add some affects, alone or in combination, to the group:

War Cries:

-minor attack and/or v3 haste buff (Warrior's getting the morale of the group up with a rallying cry)
-minor HP and mana regen buff (10HP/tick, 5mana/tick) (warrior causing the hearts and minds of his compadres to race and flow quicker)

"Called Shots" with a heavy endurance cost, possibly replacing the melee attack for that round with a single hit)

snare (IE, tendon slicing, charley horse, smash the kneecap)
Short duration stun (boot to the head, smash the solar plexus)
armour smash (no damage, but AC reduction)

etc, etc.

Removing defensive/evasive from the game in favor of a permanent, lower bonus to mitigation and avoidance would mean that content could be balanced much easier now, and in all future expansions. Warriors would still remain the undisputed king of taking damage, without taking it to such extremes. Mob damage would be kept down to sensible levels, enough that knights can still take the damage being put out if they are required to. The extra abilities given to warriors would give them a reason to be in a group that doesn't really care about having the beefiest tank possible.

These aren't firm or complete numbers and ideas that I'm putting out, by any means. What this is, is an initial idea, subject to lots and lots of ideas, balancing, and testing, but ultimately to the betterment of the game.

I don't believe it's even too difficult to rebalance it, when put up against the fact of exactly how much it can do for the game. You're only, after all, changing the NAMEDS by probably very close to a set amount for each, not every mob. Probably don't even need to adjust all of them either.

Defensive and evasive has been breaking the game for years. for all of it's usefulness, it's time for it to go, I think.
