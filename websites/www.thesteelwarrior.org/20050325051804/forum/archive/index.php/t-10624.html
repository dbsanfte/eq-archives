I haven't done any of the tasks, I 2-box a cleric as well (I'm a beastlord though, so I have slow). I'd have to say that the suggestions of Droga and Griegs end are among the best for easy decent experience and semi-decent loot for tribute/vendor/some bazaaring. One of my more favorite spots lately however has been Noble's Causeway. There are several very very nice camps for 2-boxing there. Even with my poor tanking skills, my cleric (who is also gimp beyond belief) can chain CH's on me and rarely get below 70 mana (I use a drogmar for the cleric, SA and cleric self buff for mana regen-he rarely ever has C). Even without slow, a cleric should be able chain CH on you through most anything in Nobles (I've had a few nameds bounce slow until they turned to run, so if I can tank one unslowed, it should be considerably easier for a warrior). There are many camps in NC that have at least 1 and sometimes more named or PH's within a close proximity and they drop some decent things to include Runes as well as well as some of the minor OoW Tier 1 quest armor no-drop peices (wrist/boots/head is what I've found so far in NC). My normal routine when I log on is to go to Nobles Causeway with my cleric, find an open camp, throw up LFG and start killing and getting EXP and runes while I'm waiting for a better group in MPG, RCoD or RS. I've found that I *can* 2 box in WoS, but it causes more pinching of my seat cushion than it is worth. Nobles Causeway is decent exp, non-trivial mobs, and some decent loot, especially while waiting for the nicer camps in the higher zones.

Get an extended C for the cleric, and you should do just fine in Nobles Causeway. And unless you bring a slower with you, take advantage of some DS potions for the extra DPS since you'll be battling unslowed.... just start those CH's early... I know for me, I will start a CH as I get the mob in camp even before I've taken damage if my first slow bounced, then continue the chain as necessary.

I like camping in Bloodfields (when it's not lagging to high hell there). At the Dranik's Scar, it's essentially soloable mobs but downtime is required every so often. The exp isnt phenominal there for a group of two, but if you go to the RCoD side, there are more blue cons.

The mobs are dragorns, and seem to drop magnetized / augments at a low but steady rate. The cash in general is nice there (I think I've made close to 1k/hr from the gems etc that drop, plus I average one magnetized/aug per hour).

If you fight in the middle area, they're easy to get in single or 2-pulls, they're easy enough that even a non-EP warrior could probably take them with a cleric bot, and they con low-dark-blue to a warrior.

Buffs are nice, but not necessary. If I were you, though, I'd either use Kizrak's gaunts or get some sort of haste. Clicky DSes are nice as well.
