Flair,

The way I test this is fairly simple. I started this with the simple question of "Which is better aggro, Norge`Tal or BBB?" The common thought was that rune was quite good aggro, so it stood to say that the BBB was really good. The Norge`Tal is also quite good aggro, with a 3-fold effect: A direct damage, a stun and a knockback.

To determine how well "a weapon is at aggro" one simply must run a series of tests with 2 people, one with each weapon you'd like to test, attack a mob, and record who is hit more often. Over a fairly lengthy period of time, it will become clear which weapon assists in the generation of aggro moreso than the other. Both dont' have to simultaneously proc in order to make this determination since the question is really an over time question, but short durations between procs is also helpful for a "bang for the buck" or "bang for the proc" perspective.

We used a melee mob at the zone in to Halls of Honor, which was pulled by a 3rd party, both characters simply used attack, with no taunts or anything else. The Norge`Tal was surprisingly good at generating aggro, as the mobs were beating on the Norge`Tal equipped toon 81% of the time over the BBB equipped mob. Once this was changed to BoC/CHSD vs. Norge`Tal, it changed, as the Dual Wielder then had aggro 70.4% of the time. We're unsure whether the increased rate of proc, the increased swing rate due to delay/Dual wielding, or whatnot was what was responsible, but it was adequate.

The Great Blade of Storms turned out to be *surprisingly* better than the BoC, in that by mainhanding the one weapon without another equipped, the GBoS held aggro over 100 fights, a surprising 88% of the time. What was particularly fascinating was that the GBoS when procced closely to the BoC in time would in every single instance draw aggro from the BoC.

In my experience, the GBoS is WAY better of a weapon than a BoC for a warrior, and frankly the most underrated weapon in EQ.

Arathena
From earlier testing, Niafix, particularly that performed by Brael, it seems that aggro from melee DPS is based not on the 'real' dps, which is what gets adjusted by the addition of attack, but rather, upon the 'potential' DPS: Every swing you make generates aggro as if it was a hit for max. Meanwhile, the addition of vengeance, etc, does not increase that maximum hit, it merely skews the curve of an unbuffed character towards hitting for that maximum. Therefore, Vengeance should have little to no effect on the actual aggro generated, however, a superior maximum damage per delay unit will out aggro the inferior maximum damage per delay unit. It's likely that the modal damage per delay can also be used to judge this, though less accurate, it's much easier to calculate.

Based on modal damage/delay, I'd expect the procless aggro of the GBoS to outperform the BoC, at 2.2777 repeating vs 2.15. Both Enraging Blow and Stuns are rather large chunks of aggro, still, given the choice, I think I'd lean towards the weapon which performs better under the circumstance of shitty RNG luck. Granted, the edge is small, but it's there, and it maintains itself out to a max damage multiplier of 6-7 times the weapon damage, which is out around where it is at 65, based on what scraps I can observe myself
