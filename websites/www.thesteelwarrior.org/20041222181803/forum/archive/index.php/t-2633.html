This is the big problem...casters and many others want to have the freedom to nuke like crazy and have the security knowing that aggro will not be an issue. They can gain this sense of security with a SK or Pal better than they can with a warrior, and often rely on that as the basis for their opinion.

As for me I usually do pretty good as a tank, but its entirely up to the groups willingness to allow me to do my job. I am not above stopping a group and telling them what will work better, and many times I have done so when problems become too much.

I get a lot of pick up groups so I experience various situations where one group I have what seems perfect aggro control and zero aggro recourse to caster. An hour later, I can have 2 people replace and all of a sudden the aggro management is totally chaos...and the other 3 people before wondering what is so different to cause such a change in aggro management.

As a tank I am nearly always fully buffed, as I can log on a 62 shammy, or a 65 cleric for the best buffs available for experience groups. My gear AC/HP wise is pretty good, but I do not own a BoC or an EB weapon. I am probably right at 8k hps or within a hundred of reaching it fully raid buffed...exp groups I am around 7800...my AC is 1550 to 1600 ... sometimes I switch out stuff if I pull. I am usually fully hasted in my groups, and my Dex is always maxxed for procs etc.

I am an avid believer in positioning, using taunt at the right times, and switching out weapons during the fight for different reasons. I also work to allow proper placement for rogue and ranger if in group(backstabbing and Walling of mobs).

As for being on both sides of healer and warrior let me tell you my perspective on aggro management. A warrior can do a good job if people will let him.

I don't mean this to offend you or your scenario. However my biggest frustration occurs when I group with someone who also is trying to manage aggro while I am main tank. It usually happens at the onset when a SK or BST want to generate aggro early on to protect the slower and take the aggro.

A warrior has a refresh timer on taunt, and in my humble opinion that taunt button works. When I am in a group I expect to be the first person(other than the puller) to engage that mob straight out. As the Main Tank I want that mob hitting me and only me. After I hit that mob and a couple seconds later my assist key goes off I fully intend to keep aggro. When slow lands, and if mob turns to slower, I fully expect that my taunt button is available and ready at that time to turn that mob right back on me. 9 times out of 10 that taunt works. If I lose aggro to the slower/caster it's because my taunt button was not available to me when I needed it(yes there are times when taunt will fail or the caster gets resisted and retries and the warrior is at square one again). If I fail the taunt or I don't have the button available I have to rely on positioning, DPS, procs till that refresh timer returns or I make a judgement call on AE taunt.

When someone else wants to play aggro managment they are blindly working against the warrior....making the warrior look like shit for aggro management.

There have been times I blew taunt on an overzealous SK or BST, and got aggro back only to lose it again on the slower, only to wait a few seconds to refresh or blow my AE tuant to again have the SK or BST take the aggro again thinking that the warrior sucks and that they are doing some wonderful thing.

I am not knocking SK/Pal/BST by any means. I have grouped with all of them and sometimes a couple of those classes at the same time very successfully in groups. And they can act as snap aggro for crucial moments that can save a life.

However, it emphasizes a point that a warrior has more issues do deal with for aggro control than a SK or a Pal, and more opportunities for erros and people have to understand those issues to allow the class to function right or to respect that class as it is currently.

In my opinion some of this was the original approach to how aggro management was intended...making people think to play.

But, conversly I feel SK/Pal literally have it way too easy in the aggro department and it undermines this game as it stand currently. You can't give a class marginal difference in tanking ability coupled with a trivial ability for aggro control.
