One thing EQ has proven beyond a shadow of a doubt is that it is not possible for a MMORG design team to pump out quality content at a pace that keeps the "hardcore" raiding set satisfied. Although I loved the idea of playing a rogue (was poison master too... expensive as hell and completely useless, but fun), the reality was painful both in early and late game: early game, you spend your time waiting for your HP to get back to normal between EACH FIGHT. In the late game, I used to spend like 3 or 4 hours waiting to group, as rogues ended up being the persona-non-grata (sort of) class there.
My guild being small, I finally quit because playing meant waiting more than anything else.

- In WoW, I sticked to the non-caster stuff and am playing a dwarf warrior. I never NEVER had so much fun with my EQ rogue so far (ok, might be because I'm new but hey, much of Wow stuff are exactly like EQ if you ask me).
The playability is excellent but most of all: the FUN is there all along. Sitting waiting to get your HP back is no longer a bore, as 1) you don't wait long and 2) you have easy ways of getting back HP in early game, even without a priest to heal you. Bacsically, even in solo you chain-kill easily which was something impossible in EQ.

As for "the warrior being crap-class etc etc" I think not. I'm grouping with ppl my level and I have the feeling of being really efficient.
Besides, skills and talents in Wow are THE good idea and make warriors much fun to play imho.

To sum up (early game comparision only so far): 3 hours of EQ=30mn of Wow and that only is a major improvement, as you can avoid spending all your RL time playing.

I do agree that WoW is kind of a bridge between traditional MMO's and games similar to Diablo 2, but for me, Diablo 2 became boring when LoD was released because LoD made the game far too easy. In patch 1.06, it was extremely hard to level because the only way to effectively boost your experience at 88+ was to run Hell CS+Diablo. I did a lot of team CS+D runs when levelling with my friends, the end result was garnering #1 amazon, #1 sorc and #1 paladin realmwide on the ladder (hardcore).

When LoD was released and the ladder wiped, I saw people at 99 within a day or two. It didn't require teamwork, you could solo your way on Baal runs. It was disappointing and boring.

I really do think the point about community within a game is very well made. The problem is striking a balance between being able to progress with a small number of people, and requiring too many people. Right now, I feel EQ requires too many people. Ideally the raids should give a choice -- one type should be balanced around a quantity of 30-35 people, the other should remain at 54 cap. Yeah, yeah, this would take some time to implement, but it would allow smaller guilds to progress. They did pretty well with DoN which allows solo/duo quests with some group quests involved to round it out.

Getting to 46 in EQ was a huge accomplishment that took me several months of steady playing (back in mid velious), but I'm currently 46 in WoW and it took much less than several months. I think that Blizzard is leaning too far into the Diablo 2 LoD side and not enough into making levelling supported within a community. While I feel you should have the option to solo your way to 60, I wish there were more opportunities to get to know the server. Then again, I am the kind of nutjob who enjoys playing underdog classes (lvl 50 nightshade - Mordred represent), so take this with a grain of salt I guess. Levelling on WoW doesnt have the pizzazz I wanted, though. I'm hoping once PvP is refined and new dungeons are released, the endgame will start to look more rosy, but for now I prefer EQ over WoW.
