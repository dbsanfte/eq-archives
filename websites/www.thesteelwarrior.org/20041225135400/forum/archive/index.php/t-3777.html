What if a fix for warriors (and pure melees in general) involved us having mana? At this point, I'm starting to think that the only way the three mana-free classes can be fixed is to give them mana and abilities that are similar to spells.

I'm brainstorming as I write, so this may not be the most coherent post ever.

1) Eliminate all the warrior disciplines and get us our own mitigation table. Worst case scenario, eliminate all but the four 3 minute disciplines and put those on a timer that is the same length as the current Evasive timer.

2) Add a series of new droppable and buyable manuals that contain knowledge of new 'techniques' (melee equivalent of spell scrolls) starting around level 45. 45 since up until that point, melees see new skills every so often and the caster/melee balance is still pretty good. Also, add melee class vendors in the PoK library for parchment/rune turnins for 61-65 manuals.

3) Add a series of new 'techniques.' Castable, use mana, are subject to focus effects, FT, the whole shebang. Technique effects could include increased aggro buffs (warriors), aggro reducing buffs and jolt (rogues), self healing/mana regen (monks), short and long duration damage increasers (all 3), short and long duration hit chance increasers (all 3), short duration boosts to dodge/parry/riposte/block (all 3), self only poison/disease cures (monks), reduced spell damage (warriors), crippling strike (i.e. short duration slow/snare [all 3]), vicious wounds (dot for rogues), bull rush (push on demand [warriors]), short duration self and group attack buffs (warriors). Etc, etc, etc.

Give the monks the best damage increasers, give rogues the best hit chance increasers (plus the dot for extra damage), give warriors the best of the crippling strikes.

4) Make warrior mana stamina dependent and rogue/monk mana dexterity dependent. Give us AAs to boost those stats to 355 like the other "pure" classes.

5) Add channeling skill and limited meditate skill.

6) Boost warrior piercing to 250 (just on principle)

Advantages - makes upgrades far, far easier since spells are much more adjustable than skills or disciplines. Gives more options for the formerly manaless classes both now and in the future.

Disadvantages - will piss off the purists. It is mana, with all the attendant difficulties if you run into mana sucking mobs or need to meditate.


Anyway, just a thought, figured I'd throw it out. Personally, I don't care how SoE fixes the pure melees. I don't care if it involves mana or no mana. I don't care if they blatantly rip off other games. I just don't want to have to play the EQ version of Sloth from the Goonies, locked away in the basement and laughed at until muscle is needed then shoved back out of sight.

As a hybrid (a class that deals with spells and melee), its a more interesting game if there are two bars. Balancing between them both adds a layer of complexity and management.

If the Stimina suddenly starts being important there also adds another layer of complexity to help balance out items. Notice how many items have a focus and an effect but melee items only have one? Well when Stamina starts being important you can introduce new lines of focus. Vengance IV on an item isn't that interesting but what if it had Stamina Conservation IV as well? Now you are attacking a little bit better AND your advance melee move doesn't take so much Stamina.

A system of "advance melee" powered by the useless Stamina bar is sound on paper. The other half of the equation is coming up with skills to trigger that are worth a darn and somewhat fit in RPwise.
