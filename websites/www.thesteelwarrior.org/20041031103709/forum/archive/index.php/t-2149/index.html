Friedrick
Dividing damage by delay does provide useful insight and information. This is especially true for your offhand which doesn't get a damage bonus. All other things being equal, this is a good judge for an offhand weapon (comparing two non proc'ing weapons for instance).

For your main hand there are more factors that you must look at like proc's, your damage bonus, etc. As you level you get a damage bonus applied to each hit you score with the main hand only. Therefore the more this gets applied (all things being equal) the more damage you do. For instance, a 10/20 weapon (ratio .5) will outdamage a 20/40 weapon (ratio .5) because you hit more often and have the damage bonus applied more.
