I think in addition to the factors so far raised for and against putting some AAs in before going to level 70 are a couple other things to consider.
First, there's a large group of warriors who were level 65 for a long time, because it was the max level; they had time to get a bunch of AAs, and thus the expectation of other players was that a level 65 warrior would tank like a level 65 warrior with some AAs; I don't think that expectation is entirely gone.
Second, it very much depends on who you're tanking for. Are your friends your level, level 70, or level 60? If your friends are level 70 and you are level 65 and you are trying to MT for them in groups, you're probably having some hard fights. On the other hand, if you're level 65 and you're tanking for a group of friends who are level 60, there's much less cost turning on the AAs and letting them catch up to you before you level.
third, I'd evaluate my Augment situation with respect to LDoN points. Anger III/yeksha augs are still important to warriors, but getting enough LDoN adventures to get them can feel like you're going to be doing collects until the sun burns out. So, ask yourself what level is easiest for you to talk the folks you know into doing LDoNs.
Fourth, the size and strength of your guild is a factor. If you're in a non-raiding guild, or casual raiding guild, you might find that gearing up adequately to your level is in fact more difficult than leveling. If you have acess to a strong solo/farming class that can earn you some plat for warrior gear, you're in a much different situation than if you're trying to farm plat with your warrior. If your guild is strong and you're not MT, the gear/level issue might not be as hard to keep in balance, and you may be more valuable to your guild if you go right to 70.

Do neither. I'm well aware of how XP works, and for me, at that time, 50/50 worked well. I earned several AA while earning one level. Over time it doesn't matter if you do 100% xp for half the time and 100% aa for half the time, or 50/50 for the entire time, you still earn the same amount of xp in both real xp and aa over that period, but one of them, for me, at that time, moves both bars and keeps me more interested.

The reason people say its better to do 100% xp to one area or the other isnt because it wastes xp or direct time overall. Its because levels and AAs are awarded at caps (e.g. You dont get any benefits of the next level or AA until you have 100% of the bar.)

On a 50/50 split, it takes you twice as long to earn each AA and level individually than if you had 100% to one or the other even tho the total time spent earning your total AAs and level is the same. That means if your goal is something like Area Taunt and CS3 before level 65, at 100% to AA first you could have the benefit of *all* those AA while your grinding XP to level and not take any longer to do it over all.

Basically, dedicating 100% xp to one area or the other gives you the benefit of the specific area sooner at no loss to the time it takes you to split them.

Now if you just want to see both bars moving at the same time, more power to you, but it really is more efficient to do one or the other.

I run a cleric/enchanter hydra while my wife plays a warrior. In the last month and a half or so I have spent a lot of time grouping with her and other tanks up through mpg and this is my opinion.

Casual content up through BoT can be tanked by casual warriors or raid enchanters. All they have to do is break 5k hp buffed and be able to keep agro.

Sol Ro and up, basic defensive aa's start becoming more important along with ornate or better gear. Less then that and grinding is difficult. Omens up through NC is about the same.

WoS and RCoD is where things become noticably more difficult. Mobs become closer on par with Fire Castle mobs. There level is higher and most casual tanks have a difficult time without some aa and good gear. My wife just now became able to reliably tank WoS mobs on the west wall. I would recommend at a minimum ornate gear and basic luclin defensive aa (CA, CA, ND, PE). AoE taunt and the like are gravy.

FU camps (MPG side) are noticably harder and casual tanks will have a very hard time regardless of aa. At this point most of the tanks in decent groups are from elemental raiding guilds, have matching aa and usually 68+.

MPG, the only good tanks I have grouped with there have been from time+ guilds or else raid main tanks from less guilds. The stuff here is too nasty to expect a casual player to tank without elemental level gear and the PoP defensive aa. Even then it is going to be difficult without a good cleric.

These are my basic expectations from a tank at these locations. Yes, there are exceptions to the rules. One of the best groups I ever had in WoS was with a non-raiding 66 ranger who just happened to have max defensive aa and a good bit of Omens armor to make up the difference.

As for when to stop leveling, my opinion is that it depends on your guild. Exp slows down in casual exp zones noticably at 68 and it gets worse at 69. Unless you are fighting in MPG or higher there isn't much reason to level past 68. High end raiders will get more benefit from the levels in a raid situation and can group in the places where exp is good at 69. For casual players, you are doing yourself a favor by holding at 68 while exp is still good everwhere and your abilities are sufficient to keep up. Grind out the 100+ aa you need and then try to join a high end guild.
