Effectively, the dungeon is populated by a dungeon determined by your parties average level. If your average level, for example, is 47-52, you'll get a level 50 adventure. If it's 53-58, you'll get a level 55 adventure, or numbers very close to there. What 'high' risk does is bump your average level up by five for the purposes of populating the dungeon. If your average is 60, you'll get a level 65 dungeon. If your average is 65, you get a dungeon tweaked for level 70s.
