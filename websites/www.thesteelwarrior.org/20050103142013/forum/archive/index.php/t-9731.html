Pet peeve here, but...

Lag is caused by slow/latent connection. Objects don't update properly due to lack of information from the servers. This is not a local effect, others on the same connection will experience the same problems.

Framerate problems are different. They are caused by local problems with your machine. The screen will stutter or lockup when a lot of graphical effects are going on in-game.

First, up your memory to 1 Gig. Todays OSes and games will run much better when you give them room to breath. 512 is the minimum, expect it to perform as such. Not sure how much memory is on the 5500, but 128 is the minimum you want to be playing at. Games take up huge huge ammounts of memory on high quality textures.

TURN OFF SHADOWS if you really need a preformance boost.
Turn off vsync if you dont mind a little tearing.

I'm running "high performance" setting on an ATI 6800, AMD 1900+ MP, and a 1 GB DDR. The only changes I made were enabling 2x AF to fix a driver bug with ati cars and eq2 and enabling vsync. I could run at "balanced", but I like the added framerate buffer for smoother graphics. I very rarely have framerate problems except in the most extreme conditions.

I don't run EAX, its a resource hog with my audigy. I use miles, which works good with my headphones.

If you are really having problems check your drivers (agp, video, sound, etc..) and make sure they aren't having issues. The most updated drivers don't always have the best performance. I ran 44.03 drivers on eq for a long time with my old NV 4600 because of preformance issues with the latest drivers. If your really concerned about performance, then I suggest you look into the best drivers for EQ2 for your hardware.

Last thing of note that can't be stated enough. disable all other running programs. This included tray applications. Typical resource hogs are anti virus programs, quickstart applications (real player, quick time, etc..) and file sharing. Basicaly, every program running is less cpu and memory for EQ2.

If you share a connection with other computers, understand that if anyone is doing a lots of downloading, expecially kazaa/shareaza/etc type programs, they are eating HUGE ammounts of bandwith. Disable those applications while you are running eq2.

On my comcast cable connection I can handle 5 or 6 (thats as many as I've every had) eq/eq2 clients running with absolutely no lag. One file sharing program, and the lagfest begins.

Hope all works out well for you and your GF.

Few things from a friend that plays EQ2.

Defrag your hard drive. I know some people say that no matter what your problem is, but playing EQ2 with only 512 meg of ram you will be accessing your hard drive a *lot*. Look at the light, it is almost always on. If your drive is nearly full or significantly fragmented, that is going to slow down your HD swapping considerably. My friend got a considerably performance boost from this, and he was only about 30% fragmented.

Get more RAM. The more memory you have, the less you have to access the HD, the faster things will go.

Have also heard others say that performance gets worse the longer you play, so log out and back in once every couple hours maybe.

Most of the issues, I've seen with EQ2 (I have a lot of rl friends that play on varied setups) are related to heat build up. I have friend that kept having a problem of performance degrading over an hour or 2 and he'd have to reboot to get his performance back. I sugested that he take his case off and put an fan blowing onto the MB and video card. Problem solved, he's looking into a better heat solution now.

I've also seen artifacts and wierd stuff going on with a few other computers. These were heat related, the case off test works wonders. its important that you put a desk fan or something blowing into the case and not just take the case off. your trying to runle out heat and the best way to do that is to remove as much of it as you can.

I've also seen the game stutter every few seconds, read the boards ans you'll see a lot of people with this issue that are completely certain its a video card issue. While some people may be having the nvidia stutter issue, I suspect that most are experiencing hard drive problems. In my case, I had a scsi controller on the verge of going out (raid controller = pain in the pocketbook) that was causing randomly slow zone loads, and poor performance from the client while playing, especially while traveling.

I put in a ide hard drive as a temporary solution, and problem solved. I noticed a similar issues, though less appearant, on another friends computer. He had a stock OEM, cheapo, drive that replaced with a newer, faster drive, and problem soved.

The game loves memory, and even at 1GB (not that much now days, i know) I still get a lot of hard drive accesses. An disk issues will be found by the game client and slow your preformance. Check your page file activity, and check event manager for hard drive errors (read or write) and chances are you are having disk problems. Either the controller, or more likely, the cheap drive that came with your "value" computer. OEMs like to slap in lasts years hard drives in an effort to save money fro thier value line of computers. I suspect that a large portions fo peoples problem with the game stem from latent hard drive solutions. In my case, I bought a $30 hard drive (only 7200 RPM, 40GB) from circuit cityand now I'm gtg.

Check your case temperature, check your disk activity, if either seems high, chances are that that is your problem. Wel, except if your justing seeing wierd geometry or corrupted graphics, then its almost definitely a video card issues and you should definitely be checking heat.
