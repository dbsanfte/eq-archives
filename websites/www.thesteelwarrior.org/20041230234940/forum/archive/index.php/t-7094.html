A few things:

1. Agro - I think the problem is, those weapons in your magelo are not good agro weapons. The cheapest option is Greatstaff of Thunder (also a 2 hander like the Windblade, but much better agro) - at about 100 plat. The cheapest dual wield option is the Droga weapons - Copper Hammer of Striking and Sword of the Bloodsworn - combined these run about 12 to 15k. (the prices I listed are on Emarr) I had good luck with both of these combinations - and both easily beat a windblade when it comes to agro. I haven't used the weapons in your magelo, but from everything I've read, they suck for agro. By the way, you can sell the weapons you have now, buy both of these combos, and have plenty of plat left for armor upgrades ;) .

2. Taunt button - don't mash!! :p Only use taunt if you lose/don't have agro. The reason is, you want it available immediately if/when you lose agro. What I do is hit taunt when the mob gets to camp (puts me on top of the hate list if it's successful), then immediately hit Incite (adds 400 hate, which now puts me 401 hate above the next person on the list.)

3. Other agro advice
- get anger augments - at least for your primary weapon. For me, I know I'll be upgrading soon so I only bought anger 2 for my primary, but it's made a huge difference. I'll go for anger 3 in your primary once you have a weapon you think you'll keep for a long time. By the way, if your group doesn't mind the knockback from the Ykesha augment, it's a better choice than anger 2 since it's the same hate and has a DD component (I mentioned it to my group and, well...the reaction wasn't pretty :rolleyes: .)
- have multiple sets of weapons - I have a 2 hander and dual wield setup. In some cases I seem to get better agro with one than the other. Also, I primarily dual wield now, but if we're pushing things I'll switch to a 2 hander to save on ripostes.

Nim, for the style of playing that you do, augementing your Windblade with Anger 3 would probably be a good idea.

If you do that, keep in mind a few things:

1) You will need another dps weapon for when you are not tanking (I.E. War Marshalls Bladed Staff)

2) The Windblade with Anger 3 will not be enough aggro with *most* level 65 groups.

3) If you desire to raid more, you will need a much better aggro set-up.

If you are duoing with a healer, the Windblade with Anger 3 should do fine. And keep in mind that most of the responses you see on this thread are from warriors who are looking to achieve maximum aggro. Play the way that fits your style, not anyone elses.

There is nothing wrong with having good looking weapon(s) and valuing high DPS. But you also need a functional aggro weapon in your bag, such as a GSOT, for when you're in a situation that calls for it (i.e. just about any group).

I agree with your assessment that aggro doesn't matter much in a warrior-healer duo (I'm assuming your GF is some kind of healing class). It's pretty safe for them to sit back and watch you kill the mob, even if they COULD outaggro you by doing more.

This will not fly once you expand beyond the duo, however, and you should not try to make it work. As soon as you add a third person (who is almost guaranteed to outdamage you), your role switches from damage dealer to damage sponge. Accept that, break out the GSOT or CHOS, and let the third person do their best.
