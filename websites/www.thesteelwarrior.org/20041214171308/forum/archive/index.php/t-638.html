Taunt

Taunt is a skill that checks many factors that only SoE know about. However, I believe its based on:

1) Skill level in Taunt
2) Level of Mob
3) Level of aggro between you and the person the Mob is currently beating on

If successful, taunt will put you at the top of the Aggro list and the mob will start to hit you. Note, this effectively makes taunt useless if the mob is already hitting you (your already at the top of the aggro list)

Tactics for taunt

Only taunt when the mob is NOT hitting you. Otherwise you waste 6 seconds or so before you can try again.

Stay close to the Mob, proximity helps aggro, more so at higher levels, but its good form, so learning now helps later.

Aggro

Aggro is what people term how much a Mob hates you, if you have aggro you are somewhere on the list. The number 1 on that list is who the Mob is hitting on.

Aggro is generated in different ways:

1) Hitting a Mob (DPS aggro, or damage per second)
2) Taunting a Mob (related to the skill)
3) Kicking/Slamming/Bashing. These generate aggro, though how much /shrug. Not a lot I find (Maybe bash/slam, but being a Halfhooman I dont do it often)
4) Proccing a weapon (Proc = Process, the firing of a random effect on your weapon) Certain procs have more aggro than others, highest being (in no order) Slows, Stuns, Tash
5) Sitting. Some mobs dont like you sitting, can be useful to postion a stubborn mob.
6) Some warriors feel Disarm/Beg also adds aggro.

How to taunt

My personal belief is that taunt works best after hitting a mob, so if you lose aggro to an over-zealous caster, wait till you get a round of attacks in then taunt.

Factors affecting taunt

1) Root. The spelll Root and its variants will cause a Mob to hit the closest target, regardless of who is number 1 on the aggro list, make use of this to get up close and personal on the mob
2) Low health. When characters get below 40pct health, some creatures frenzy and automatically attack that person, no amount of taunting will stop it. Best tactic is to root, or heal the person (or both)
3) Stunned/Runed. If your stunned or runed some Mobs tend to ignore you and hit other people, deal with it.
4) Dual Wield vs 2handed weaps. At the lower levels, dual wielding two 1hers generates more aggro than 1 slow 2her. At higher levels and better weapons, the reverse is true.

Other uses for taunt

1) Breaking mes. If a mob is messed, I generally taunt 1-3 times before breaking. Other factors come into play of course (has the chanter blurred? Rooted? Anyone <40pct health?)
2) Annoying Paladins and Sks that have taken your livelyhood from you.

When not to taunt

1) When your not the MA/MT (Main Assist, Maint Tank). If your not the one the healers are concentrating on, DONT TAUNT!

Training Taunt

1) Although VI have said they changed it, I would still visit your trainer and put 5 points into it.
2) Use against EXP giving Mobs
