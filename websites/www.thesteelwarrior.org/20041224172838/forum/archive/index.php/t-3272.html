I'll cut and paste the original post here for those Warriors who have lost both arms in combat. ;) With the discussion thread info I should probably modify it slightly (namely in regards to the way Taunt appears to work, and the amount of Hate the mage recieves when he/she sends in a pet), but the basic premise is the same in any case.

The basic idea is that Pet "movement" AI and Pet "choose target" AI appear to respond differently to the Hate list -- mostly due to the "always attack a nearby player" change a while back to stop people from using DC pets in Kael to tank mobs like Vindi and the AoW.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------

I wanted to start a discussion on this topic to establish its validity and find errors in this theory.

Presumably, pets maintain their position on the agro list of a mob, no matter whether or not a player is near. The NPC agro code will always choose to attack a player over a nearby pet, but will return to attacking the pet if:

A) The pet's position in the Hate list is higher than any other players in the area.

and

B) The players on the Hate list are all out of melee range.


Example Hate list of mob BadGuyX:

Character:.....Hate Value:.....Player?.....In melee range?
Jobober..........800................No............Yes
Cleric1...........750................Yes...........No
Warrior2.........700................Yes...........Yes
Paladin3.........400................Yes..........Yes
Mage4............0....................Yes..........No

...in this case, the Warrior gets agro, despite the cleric being higher on the hate list! The reason being that the mob chooses to stay near the character on the top of the Hate list, but attacks the nearest player within melee range ...due to the fact that they are a player, and are within melee range. ;)



Thus, the theory is:

* The main tank will effectively keep agro as long as a pet in near proximity is higher on the hate list than the highest player is.

If this theory is true, then the DDD pet may be extremely useful, particularly if equipped with (for example) stun whips and a dex buff. (I think 61+ pets may generate enough agro to out-agro the DDD, however -- but thats 61, and DDD is 56)

In a sense, a high agro pet can potentially be as useful as some degree of "reverse" Spell Casting Subtlety for the entire group, at least for the mob(s) engaged by the pet.

In addition, a highly dex buffed, stun whip equipped pet may be very useful to send in with the "agro building" tank, on raids using that strategy. The mage who sends in the pet gets added to the Hate list, but maintains a hate of 0 (and thus sticks to the bottom), unless he/she stupidly sits down and raises their temporary Hate above that of the tank and pet.


The idea of pets generating more agro than a warrior may be foreign to those who play in a lot of 65 groups with uber taunt weapons, but there are a lot of lower level warriors in the game who have a *lot* of trouble generating more hate than your typical pet from a mage of the same level.
