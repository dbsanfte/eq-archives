Parsed the first half of our PoTime raid today. Thought maybe someone would find some real-world numbers(as opposed to katta banker parses) interesting.

Proc damage included.

Pet damage included in owners damage for pet classes.

DoT damage NOT included.

Total Damage is the most interesting number.

DPS = Total Damage / Total elapsed time(From DA / WS until mob dead).

Thus the DPS reported here are typically lower than EQCompanion/YALP reports and cannot be directly compared, since those parsers divide with the time since YOU did your first damage.

All mobs were killed Vindi/AoW style in a corner.

I don't see how you can completely ignore DPS. If one guild is more DPS heavy than the other, then a mob is going to drop faster, hence less total DMG for everybody in the parse.

I agree that sometimes the accuracy of DPS is questionable, but I do not agree that you can totally ignore it. It doesn't matter how long a fight last or how "DPS heavy" one guild is over another because the raid as a whole has to do basically the same amount of damage (extra damage due to regen is the only difference) to kill the same mob.

The reason DPS is completely irrelevant is because you have to use a common fight time for everyone - from the time the mob is engaged to the time it dies. That means that everyone other than the MT who is the first to engage is going to have the wrong fight time. You can't take in to account melees turning off attack to keep from getting agro, monks FDing, casters waiting until 95% to engage, time spend dodging AEs and other such variables that take away from the amount of time an individual spends doing damage.

Regardless of the fight time, total damage in comparison to the others on a raid is the only effective comparison. When comparing 2 different raids, you want to make sure that they have similar total fight durations.
