When POP came out I was just hitting level 60 with my warrior. I enjoyed the new zones a lot, but I quickly got disenchanted with the Warrior class and our inability to hold agro when compared to the Knights. Prior to POP it wasn't really much of an issue to have a little (tiny?) bit of ping-ponging in and experince group: it wasn't pleasant, mind you, but the mobs didn't hit hard enough to make it lethal to the party.

My solution?
I bailed.
I quit EQ for a couple of months, and when I eventually came back I went back to playing a paladin instead of my warrior.

Fast forwrd to the present: my paladin is level 65 with 80ish AA. the paladin is fun to play: he's a decent tank, he's versatile, he's fun to play in experience groups and on raids...did I mention that keeping agro with a paladin is child's play?
;)

...but I still miss my warrior.

I've got some friends who are in the 56-60 range who I haven't been able to join for LDoN adventures because of the LDoN 7-level limit....so I have dusted off my warrior.

I had to re-equip him a bit, but the core of his gear is still fairly solid (almost full SS). I bought a few fairly cheap pieces to round out his armor, and picked up a GSoT for 400 plat in the bazaar (they were 30K when I quit playing my warrior :) ).

Its pretty amusing that a 400 plat weapon holds agro better than my old combo of a pair of Fleshgrinders...at the time I thought Fleshgrinders were pretty cool.
:)

Anyway...I've done a few LDoN adventures and I've been having a lot of fun playing my warrior. I like the challenge of holding agro...which is something I never thought I would hear myself say.

I think the current state of the Warrior class in EQ is MUCH better than it was when POP was released: the addition of some very nice 2H weapons for DPS and the LDoN Anger procs ahve been a very welcome addition to the warrior class.

Have these changes 'fixed' the warrior class balance problems? No, but they have helped...and some progress is better than no progress at all.
