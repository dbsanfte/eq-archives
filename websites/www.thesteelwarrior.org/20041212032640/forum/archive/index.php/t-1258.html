I remember back when I first started playing EQ, every person I talked with assured me Paladins were 75% warrior, 25% cleric... Shadowknights were 75% warrior, 25% necromancer... and Rangers were 75% warrior, 25% druid. Most websites described it that way.

There was (and still is) an intrinsic problem with that. In a game that involves grouping, if the 'other half' of your job was being filled by someone, you were basically a sub par version of whatever the over untouched half was. In a group that can prosper when everyone fills their specialized roll, being a 'hybrid' or jack of all trades just isn't rewarding when someone or many people do your job better.

This isn't so much a problem with the DPS classes. You can't have enough DPS. Luckily the many variations of monsters and DPS makes it so you are benefitted from varying types, even if one class clearly outdamages another (though Burst damage is in season with PoP). You can only have so many tank.

It's interesting to see how the issue was addressed. From most accounts I've read on several boards, it seems more and more like SKs and Paladins are now 98% warrior (because regardless of how they began prepared to do the job in the original game, it's the roll they've had to fill as a plate/tank class)... and the other percentage of that 100 is negligible and debatable, but it is sweet wonderful solid aggro so who cares. You don't invite a SK or Paladin to the group for an AC tap or a resist poison buff... it's because they can tank all but a very small amount of encounters without the problems of a warrior. We'll say that small amount of encounters is that magical 2% warrior that's missing from the Paladin/SK equation.

Rangers kinda lucked out in this field.... they were segued into a damage role (along with the traditional snaring/peeling/rooting job they've always had), and are kind of omitted from this job fight Warriors, SKs and Pallies are now in.

Here's the problem.

If Warriors are given their 25% edge back.... you're left with hybrids that have no function in a well designed party. At the same time, if nothing happens Warriors get, or rather continuing getting, the finger. "You get no fun stuff and you're no better then those guys. Hell they aren't even penalized in XP anymore so they can have just as many skills and AA as you do".

We saw what's happened with Monks. They lost their ability to tank and have become rogue-lites. In PoP pulling is such a menial task, that even though a monk is well equipped to do it, many classes can do it and often faster. Monks can and still do the job because they've done it for so long that if you're a monk that makes it to PoP, you probably know how to pull. It'd be nice to see monks compensated in some way for their not wearing plate armor. Nah they're just screwed for it, but that's another gripe for another board.

If the next expansion suddenly makes it so that Warriors are the 25% more of a warrior then Paladins and Shadowknights again, what role will they have? Rogue-lite? I'm not sure if my group needs weaker cleric buffs or heals when we've already got them. And I certainly don't need my corpse found...

If anyone wonders why they haven't given us warriors a step up to compete, it's because they can't... not because they've forgotten. Too many promises have been 'made' to start breaking hearts now.

Maybe we'll get something cool like a big new sword. That'd be nice. I like swords.

-Mahhj
