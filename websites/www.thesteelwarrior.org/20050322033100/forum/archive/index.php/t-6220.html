Been thinking about the year to come, and the various MMORPGs that are going to debut. Same time, I'm looking at how SoE has dealt with competition in the past, and I think we might see some things this year that would have been unthinkable a few years back. So, just for yocks, here's a few predictions on what we will see in EQ in the year to come:

1. Epics V2.0 - the Berserker Epic and the Fabled <whatever> drops foreshadow this, imho. Fabled mobs are a brilliant idea, they tap right into the nostalgia of the long time players. A chance to upgrade our epics would have the same effect. At the same time, the clearly out-of-line stats on the Berserker Epic have already generated a lot of griping, which SoE can then appease and look like good guys by providing Epic V2.0 quests. More than likely, these new quests will be tied to a new expansion, maximizing the bling factor.

2. Expansion #1 "Legacy of Lost Dungeons" - Aimed at lower to mid level casual players, will be a combination of LoY and LDoN, with lots of instanced small raid content. For whatever reason, SoE has decided that 72 man raids are a bad thing, and will continue to force feed us 36 and 54 man raids. New over-powered mid-level spells for all the casters, twink weapons for the melees. A new system of portals to the last few remote zones will connect Highpass Keep to Western Wastes to Burning Woods to Rathe Mountains.

3. Expansion #2 "Steel Cage Match of Discord" - Trash mobs that hit for 1500 and have less HP than a Crushbone Orc. Level limit will be raised to 70, trivializing 80% of GoD progression. Single group flagging trials that will require Berserkers to succeed. Flag progressions that make getting to the Elementals look like getting keyed for the bottom floor of Befallen. Each copy shipped with a fresh syringe.

4. Two more old world zones revamped. My candidates are Mistmoore and Kedge Keep, since they are the most underutilized dungeons I can think of. Mobs will be elemental level hard, loot will be appropriate for 40th level twinks and will have a recommended level of 60.

5. GM events and upgraded customer service will magically re-appear in the months before and after the opening of WoW. Similarly, the Devs will appear on the melee class boards again at about the same time, but refuse to discuss class balance.

6. Progression in GoD will remain broken for three more months, at which time it will be fixed in stages so as to allow Afterlife to complete GoD one month before the release of Expansion #1.

7. EQ2 will be an overgrown bunker-buster bomb. EQ itself will suffer serious and permanent subscriber losses as its players escape to WoW and Vanguard. EQ will finish the year with about 75% of the playerbase it started with.

First I'll weigh in on your predictions:

"1. Epics V2.0 -"

Maybe. That's actually not a bad prediction. The fabled stuff is going over pretty well. People are at least talking about the zerker epic. It wouldn't be a stretch for them to finally give in to the players who want this. They've made worse decisions.

"2. Expansion #1" and "3. Expansion #2 "

If they up the level cap, it'll be in the very next expansion, not two expansions down the road. Outside pressures from competitors will be a huge catalyst in them wanting to rev the treadmill up again. Unfortunately, the game "design" for the past few expansions has been more and more like a first person shooter than an RPG. You know, beat Bad Guy A to get to Level 2, so you can start working on Bad Guy B to get Key to Level 3. That's a big time departure from Everquest's original "vision" and so, I don't know how your predictions are going to hold up.

"4. Two more old world zones revamped. "

I agree with Mistmoore. I disagree with Kedge. Here's why: The pathing and the aggro in Kedge would really work against them as they revamped it. As you've seen in all their other "revamp" work ... they can change the polygons but they can't change the framework of the zones. And Kedge would be a tweaking nightmare.

"5. GM events and upgraded customer service "

The cynic in me is screaming to agree with you on this point. However, as we've seen with their bold new steps in the area of CS lately, it's more than likely that their customer service is going to continue to go into the crapper. Look for CS to steadily become invisible in the world of EQ. In fact as EQ2 launches, EQ is going to become more and more and more automated with its CS. That's the direction they're going in.

"6. Progression in GoD will remain broken for three more months"

Heh. That's not really a prediction. That's more stating the obvious.

;-)

"7. EQ2 will be an overgrown bunker-buster bomb. EQ itself will suffer serious and permanent subscriber losses as its players escape to WoW and Vanguard. EQ will finish the year with about 75% of the playerbase it started with."

I quoted all of this one, because it's the one I agree with the most. Which is odd, because for the most part, I've been in the "EQ hasn't been killed yet" camp.

But this time things are very very different. The game, for me, has peaked. It's beyond boring now. And I myself am looking at other games to fill my time. Not even MMORPGs. Just other fun video games.

And as I mentioned above, the actual style of the game has shifted so much recently from "the vision" to the "beat monster A to get key B to go to place C" style of game. And that style of game isn't what I wanted to play when I started with Everquest.

Which gets to Vanguard. The VISION. If anyone can beat EQ at what EQ does ... it's the guys who created it in the first place.

So don't count them out. Especially with MS in their corner. That should really help them with keeping that game working on odd system requirements and the like.

And Worlds of Warcraft, also, has a strong group of people who could give EQ a run for its money. And graphics are the last worry I'd have with that game.

In short, I don't know if they'll KILL EQ as others are thinking, but I certainly agree with your prediction that they'll cut into the EQ market like you say.

What do I think will kill EQ? EQ itself. The devs are out of ideas. They stats are beyond pointless. New gear is gaudy beyond belief. The carrot just isn't fun to chase anymore for a lot of people (me included). That's dangerous for a game that hinges on the carrot chase like EQ does.
The competitors won't have much to do with killing or keeping EQ afloat. It's all SOE themselves. They need to rethink the directions they've gone with EQ. Remember why people liked this game in the first place (or at the very least ASK the people who have left the company to join other companies, why the customers like the game so much back then, before the new employees got there).

:P

Sorry to ramble. I liked your post. And your predictions.
