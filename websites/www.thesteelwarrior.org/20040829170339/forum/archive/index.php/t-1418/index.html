Current State of the Warrior from the perspective of an Enchanter

I stopped playing my warrior at lvl 25, so I cannot speak AS a warrior, but being one of the classes that is perhaps as interested in how well the tanks do their jobs as the tanks themselves, I thought you might be interested in my perspective.

In short... warriors have problems. As a newly dinged 59 enchanter I cannot speak to the 'high end' game, I have never grouped with a warrior in even a single piece of elemental armor, nor a BoW. I get to group with the 'mid range' warriors, the ones in Thurg gear, the ones in a few pieces of cultural if they're lucky and picked a diety. I've rarely gotten to group with warriors with EB weapons, if I'm lucky they'll have some sort of proccing weapon but most of the time no dice.

As of right now, if I am forming a group, or my opinion is asked, and am looking at tanks, if both a warrior and a paladin are LFG at the same time, I go with the paladin unless the paladin has a horrid reputation, then I'll look for an SK. Not because I don't like warriors, because I do, many warriors are fun folks to group with, not because I have any 'angst' at warriors, but because a paladin or SK MT allows me to get the SAME reward in terms of XP/hour as a warrior with LESS risk in terms of aggro/death. There are no concrete benefits to me as an enchanter with having a warrior MT over a hybrid. The ONLY potential benefit is that a warrior is more experienced at the best ways to break mezzes than a paladin/SK is, but with a little coaching 99 percent of all hybrids can do even that better than a warrior.

Now I hear people say 'but warriors have more HP, more AC, therefore they're better' to which I say, I have less HP and AC than any tank, and its MY silk wearing buttcheeks that get the loving attention of the mob when I get aggro. If I have to group with a warrior MT I am perfectly capable of altering the way I work and avoiding getting aggro, but why should I have to? Why should I work at 60 percent to enable Joe_Warrior00 to hold aggro when Jim_Paladin00 or Billy_Shadowknight00 allow me to go 100 percent? If I have a hybrid MT, I can concentrate completely on landing slows, tashs and nukes, and mezzing the rare adds. With a warrior I need to load up my aggro related spells and my slow-refresh boon/tricksters in order to help the warrior keep and hold aggro and still operate at a good efficiency. In short, a warrior as MT requires MORE effort in relation to the SAME reward.

Color me lazy, if I can get 1 dollar of reward for 60 cents of effort, I'm going to be ALL over that in a heartbeat. That's human nature, and the ONLY thing keeping some warriors as MTs is that there is still a perception among people who don't really pay that much attention that a warrior is required as MT. I've had groups where we had both a warrior and a paladin in them, the group wanted the warrior to MT, but on the first fight the paladin immediately stole aggro and never lost it despite the best efforts of the warrior, supported with Boon/Tricksters and Horrifying Visage... so on the next pull the paladin was MT, because he decided he wanted to be. After a while, the warrior sent me a tell upset over the entire situation, and I couldn't help but agree, that paladin got a group DESPITE not being expected to be MT, and still took that job over leaving the warrior who's ONLY realistic contribution to a group is to MT to sit there and play with his two handers, pretending to be useful.

What needs to be done? Warriors need SOMETHING to make the REWARD for having a warrior MT match the increased RISK and the increased EFFORT of having a warrior MT for the rest of the group. I don't care if this is increasing the DPS of the warrior, increasing mitigation for the warrior, increasing taunt for the warrior, or nerfing the bejeesus out of knights. SOMETHING needs to be done about this, I already am seeing warriors spending hours on end LFG in zone after zone, I've seen lvl 60 warriors LFG in PoN/PoI for over an hour, while a 53 paladin walks in, shouts once, and gets a group immediately, and these warriors are from reputable guilds and are known to be good players... It is completely wrong for a 'pure' tank class, one whose ONLY selling point to their group is their ability to tank, to be LESS desirable to groups than any hybrid. Hybrids get versatility at the expense of specialization, the specialists should always be better than the generalists AT THEIR SPECIALTY.

I'm an enchanter, my only 'melee' characters now are a paladin twink, a SK twink and a monk twink, none are above 36. I live and die by aggro control, I slow too soon, LOADING... PLEASE WAIT. I tash too soon LOADING... PLEASE WAIT. I nuke too hard LOADING... PLEASE WAIT. I sit down too early LOADING... PLEASE WAIT. Having a tank that can, by god, tank and hold aggro while tanking is CRITICAL for me to be able to do my job, NOTHING at all in a tank matters at all compared to that, because if the tank holds aggro, a cleric doesn't have to waste mana healing my robe-wearing buttcheeks at all. Paladins and SKs can do this. Warriors can not. That is a problem, and that needs to be fixed.

Friss, how am I going to GET the AA's and levels to be at the level where it no longer matters if a warrior can't get instant snap aggro, hrmmm? I *know* how to work with a warrior, when I am grouped with a warrior MT I do NOT usually get aggro, but that is because I am having to hang back, be more careful, watch my timing with greater care. So we have an unslowed mob whalin' away on the warrior, necesitating an earlier CH and possible cleric aggro, so I can slow without needing to worry about getting beat on myself... if that paladin over there can tank the mob at 90 percent of the warriors efficiency, and I can get the mob slowed faster in consequence, the difference comes out in the wash.

The warriors advantages of HP/AC, IE taking the beating, are not the end-all and be-all of tanking. If the mob is hitting me instead of the warrior, I don't care if the warrior has 10 million hp and 1 million AC, I don't. That's the problem, with a knight I *never* have to worry about mistiming a cast or casting to soon or in an indoor dungeon sitting to med too early, because the knight has aggro locked down tight. With a warrior I *do* have to worry about that.

So the question becomes, is the BENEFIT of having the warrior MT EQUAL to the BENEFIT of having a knight MT. I respectfully submit that the answer is 'not right now in XP groups'. Especially in Tier 1 planes (that I am restricting myself too on my own even with the opening of Tier 2, due to AA/Levels) there is NO drawback to having a knight as MT compared to a warrior, and the knight allows the rest of the group to generate more DPS, allows me to do my job with less stress, and things go smoother. Thus the BENEFIT of a knight MTing is higher than the BENEFIT of a warrior MTing, when they should be equal net benefit to an XP group, 3 minutes of glory on a raid for one warrior doesn't translate at ALL to the XP setting.

Do I think this is 'right' and 'good'? Heck no, warriors NEED to be balanced vis-a-vis knights, they need to be AS attractive to the average XP group for the MT spot as a knight without needing to go through contortions and jump through hoops. A warrior in bazaar bought gear should be EQUALLY effective and efficient a MT as a paladin in bazaar bought gear, not less, not more, equal. Right now, the warrior is NOT equal.

Do I have a solution? Not really, I'm not a warrior, my highest tank class is my 36 SK, I'm not qualified to say 'this is THE ONE TRUE solution' to the problem. I can SEE the problem, because I'm the one who gets to have to deal with the results of the problem. My one proposed idea evidently has more holes than a brick of swiss cheese (the enrage idea I had in the Outhouse section) *chuckles*
