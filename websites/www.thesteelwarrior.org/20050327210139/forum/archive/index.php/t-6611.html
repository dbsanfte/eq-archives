My guild farmed this for the CHSD and haste neck item for a while as its a very easy encounter and fun as well. The encounter starts by walking Nitram (and keeping alive) to xanamech and then killing the dragon.

We run this as a chaos raid -multiple tanks clearing the way with an ench wrangler that mez/mem blur's nitram along the way as we walk him towards Xana. You will need a few items to hand to Nitram to start the event. The only problem we run into enroute is occasionally leaving Nitram too far back and he runs into respawn, which is really not an issue.

Once you get to Xana's loc, have the ench keep Nitram on ice while the raid finishes up buffing. Then everyone gets in position leaving a path for Nitram to get to Xana. You will need to have the dragon face away from Nitram if you want to keep Nitram alive for the factory flag. So the MT will be opposite the door and uses incite/ae taunt to get the dragon facing where he wants it. Get a slow on xana as soon as you can as it makes life easier. Also be warned that nitram will summon Xana to him if he gets to far so don't be surprised when the dragon moves on you. Lastly, if anyone aggro's Nitram during the final kill, he will not hand out factory flags.

Xana has 2 ae's that are annoying. An AE snare and dot. One of them can and will interrupt heals so short heals like SR are much better. We usually have a paly set to loh along with a DI in case the healers get interrupted before the slow gets landed. We also have at least 2 druids MGB SOTW and 1-2 clerics MGB EE.


Your guild may have a few problems at first, given your average level and will need to make adjustments to find a tactic that works for you. Code started doing this with about 30 folks lvl 60-65 and now are doing it with about 14-18 lvl 65. The good thing about this encounter is you can do it often as it just requires Nitram to be spawned.

this is the strat i used when i was doing this raid for my previous guild.

tankline up is set up behind xanamech (before he's activated), rogues in front and other DPS on the sides as evenly as possible. pet classes place their pets under xanamech. the healing chain (or spam chain, whatever they use) set up on the left of the tanks, just out of range of the stun proc not too far from the wall. the debuff team moves into melee range. xanamech casts an AE snare, so if your debuff team is across the room and suddenly pull agro it's ganna take your tanks some time to walk AAAALLLL the way over there and reagain agro which will most likely kill the agro hi-jacker and everyone around him. once everyone is ready you release the gnome (i assume an enchanter is mezzing/bluring him). xanamech is activated and usually bombs his AE right away, tank spins him around (so the gnome doesnt get wasted by ripos) pets hammer away at him from underneth, debuff team gets to work, healing chain spams away, assist is called and hopefully everyone is evenly placed. you dont want him moving around too much since everyone is snared and there are no flat walls to pin him on.

xanamech quads for 1400s max, and uses 3 AEs, an 800DD -300 fire resist, procs a 300DD centered on the tank with a 1 second stun -200 magic resist (200 range, you want the healers far enough to avoid this) and uses an unresistable snare.

Additional notes from experience:

Put away the knockback weapons
While its funny to see Xana do his own version of 'whirl till you hurl', it just plain sucks for maintaining aggro and keeping casters and healers out of his AE range. Not to mention Xana getting some ripostes in on Nitram.

Do not use F8 to target
Had a guildie caster that just simply didn't raid, or be online, very often. It was discovered after the fact, that he had been casting his nukes on Nitram during the battle, instead of Xana. This led to ...

Do not /q out
Guild killed dragon, but pally got aggro from Nitram. Pally /q'd out, got the LD tag, and became an NPC. Then he killed Nitram in short order. Very bad.

Otherwise, make sure you've all got resist gear (FR and MR) and any bards you can get will help tremendously.

**This is my experience and is not a bible. Use it at your own risk**

I have ran this a few times as my shammie.
When you get out side the windmill. Do your prelim buff. Assign one tank as the main assist 1-2 more as alt assists and off tanks for the clearing. Enter the zone and have everyone asist the MT and just slaughter the path to the gnome. Clear around him and them move on.
Have your Chanter and turn in person have a timmer. The respawn is 18 min if i remember correctly. Wait 10-12 min or untill your raid force gets just out of sight if they area high dps and slaughtering everything fast. Then start the gnome walking. If the gnome gets to close to the raid just have the chanter mez (40 sec one) for tunnel areas and the 90 sec one if they are in a big open area clearing out. Once memblur should work after each mez.
Once you get to the opening to the finnal walk up to the gate keep clearing up to the gate and then inside the gate. Mez the gnome just before the finnal tunnel up and after everyone is rebuffed (if needed) then blur the gnome and let him go.

Set up...
The asigned tanks MT 2MT 3MT get behind the dragon. Clerics off to the right as your looking at the dragon with your back to the gate. Have them target the MT and get the proper distance from him. Pets under the bellie and have all your nukers off to the left as you look at the dragon and have them target a pet and use it for range.
Here i have the Slower/tasher off to the side a little so if they draw argo they should not get anyone else in the line of fire of the AE's.

So if your looking at a clock. The gate is 6, the MT's are 12, the clerics area around 3-4, the nukers are around 8, and the Slower/Malo/Tash 'er are around 10-11 ish area.

Perfect fight...
When the gnome goes in a cleric hits HoT on the MT. The MT will argo the dragon. Spam heals go off on the MT at 50% (this depends on the HP of the tank) 98% the 2MT, 3MT attacks and the debuffers start debuffing around 95 %... Pets will be auto argo because of the AE's unless they are AA'd parked. At 90% the tanks light em up, at 85% the nukes start dropping. The nukers should know to space them out to not draw argo but if needed tell them one nuke every 5%, Then at 30-40% ish burn him down.

The clearing should take 30min to 1 hr depending on your DPs. The dragon should drop in under 1 min any longer then 1:30-2min and you run a high risk of wiping and the clerics going OOM.

**EDIT**
NO AE"S SHOULD BE USED unless it is a heal or heal like action.
**mages like to use their rain spells and that is a NO NO!!**

**This is my experience and is not a bible. Use it at your own risk**
