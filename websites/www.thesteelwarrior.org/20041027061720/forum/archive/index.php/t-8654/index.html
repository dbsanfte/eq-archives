I don't remember how the "haste cap" was arrived at originally, but I don't think this is true. At least it is commonly assumed that a 36% haste item with VQ is just as good as a 41% haste item with VQ which is just as good as 45% haste item with VQ. There doesn't need to be a "cap" for spell haste, because haste spells don't stack with each other, so the highest level haste spell percentage IS the cap.

The common wisdom now is that the max haste you can get from a haste spell + a haste item is about 100%, with ver 3 bard haste stacking on top of that, so the only advantage of having a 45% haste item is a little added DPS when you don't have a haste buff. As far as I know there hasn't been a recent test of this, and one would probably be worth doing, but as far as we know right now I believe the 100% + bard number is as good as you can do.

I do know of a certain group of uberguild rogues that think 45% haste is very important, but I couldn't tell you if their opinion has any basis in reality.

Cast slow is considered the same as haste in that if you have a slow on you haste spells are ignored and the slow modifer is used.

So as an example from total swing rate not added swing rate compared to other item hastes in there spell haste group:

Normal Hasted
45 = 200 = 1.01% more swings than 40%
41 = 199 = 0.51% more swings than 40%
40 = 198

Normal Not Hasted-
45 = 145 = 3.57% more swings than 40%
41 = 141 = 0.71% more swings than 40%
40 = 140

With 20% slow
45 = 125 = 4.17% more swings than 40%
41 = 121 = % more swings than 40%
40 = 120

With 50% slow
45 = 95 = 5.56% more swings than 40%
41 = 91 = 1.11% more swings than 40%
40 = 90

With 80% slow
45 = 65 = 8.33% more swings than 40%
41 = 61 = 1.67% more swings than 40%
40 = 60


It can be clearly be seen that at higher hasted rates it matters less but when you have 50% slow on you there is close to a 6% differance or about a third of the effect of Echo of Anger.
