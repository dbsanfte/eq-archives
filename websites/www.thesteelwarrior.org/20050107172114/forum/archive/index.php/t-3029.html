Interesting thought game: Armor conflicts in LDoN.

Look at the high-point-cost armor pieces for each of the various sets. They're arranged as Plate, Chain, Leather, and Cloth.

Plate (war/pal/shd/clr/brd): Three classes that compete for a single group spot. Not much sense having both a warrior and a knight, unless you're fighting undead and want the knight for dps. Clerics and bards do not conflict, as they fill separate roles.

Chain (rng/rog/shm): No conflict. The two dps classes "stack" with each other, essentially, as groups could benefit from having both a ranger and a rogue. The shaman is a separate role entirely.

Leather (mnk/bst/dru): No conflict. All three classes fill separate roles.

Cloth (wiz/mag/enc/nec): No real conflict. Some could contend that wizards and mages compete for group spots, although since they both offer dps, there's no reason not to take both.

Isn't that weird? There are certain classes that compete to fill a single group spot (Cleric / Druid / Shaman healing, Monk / Bard pulling, Enchanter / Shaman slowing, Enchanter / Bard crowd control, Warrior / Paladin / Shadowknight tanking), but of those "conflict" classes, only the tanks have identical armor sets.
