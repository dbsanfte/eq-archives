A successful taunt without getting aggro doesn't mean that you didn't go to the top of the hate list. Successful taunt essentially puts you equal in hate to the person you're taunting off of. If that person is a melee or has a dot active, they can easily get aggro back before you even get hit by landing another swing or getting another tick from their dot. If the person with aggro is doing nothing to the mob and not sitting or the puller then you should always get aggro with a single taunt.

The 4 taunt thing is do to an aggro bonus from being the first one on the aggro list. If you pull with one character, and taunt with another while autoattack is off it will always take 4 successful taunts to get aggro. After the initial successful taunt however, you can turn on autoattack and you will have aggro in one or two hits. This was the main discovery in the library taunt thread (there is a summary post linked in the original post if you don't want to read the whole thread.

It had occured to me that maybe they had upped the +hate amount on taunt to cover that bonus and let you get aggro from a puller with one successful taunt, but I did a quick test last night and the 4 taunt thing is definitely still there, which only confuses me more with regard to the other tests in this thread. If the +hate has gone up, then the bonus for pulling has too. I seriously doubt that this is true, so this is even more of a mystery now. You can still pull aggro off of a puller who used prox aggro with an arrow or two, so if taunt is 1/4 of that aggro it's very very tiny.

We are definitely missing a piece of the puzzle here. I can't explain holding aggro with 4 or 5 taunts over a stun.
