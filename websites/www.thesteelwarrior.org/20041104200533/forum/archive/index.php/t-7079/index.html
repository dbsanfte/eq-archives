Brabble
Until relatively recently, the "recomended level" on weapons was not actually operational in the game, which meant that you got the full damage rating on any weapon you could equip. That has changed, however, and in some ways made weapons without recommended levels much more valuable for younger characters.
The reason everyone says haste is important that haste increases your aggro. Likewise, low-delay, high-damage weapons do the same thing.
The wurmslayer is slow, but using it at your level is far from a disaster. I say that because at higher levels, the procs on weapons, your AAs and haste become progressively more important components in holding aggro. If you have a lot of plat, haste is available to you now, but procs and AAs are not really a factor for you or anyone else you're grouping with for a while.
Get haste if you can, but whatever your haste, use the next 25 levels or so to learn how to use the other methods available to you for holding aggro. In general, some of these methods are:
1. Be on the lookout for low-delay, relatively high ratio one-hand weapons.
2. Use your taunt key well. For a long time we've been under the assumption that a successful taunt put you at the top of the mob's aggro list, plus 1. Recent tests discussed on this board have indicated there is a possibility that taunt has a cummulative aggro component--some set amount of hate. However, I think your best bet is to avoid punching the taunt button when it refreshes, provided that the mob is already hitting you. Save taunt for when the mob stops hitting you and starts hitting someone else in your group.
3. Slam or kick the mob every chance you get. It will be a while before you face mobs that have a dangerous riposte, so use these special attacks whenever they refresh. Two exceptions to this: If you are a large race, and can slam, note that slam will interrupt casting mobs, and it will stop running mobs for a round or two. If you are in a group with classes that can stop running mobs, then the timing on your slam is not that critical; but if no one can stop a running mob, sometimes slamming them at the moment they start to run can help (marginally) prevent a mob from reaching some of its friends before it dies. On casting mobs, you may want to time your slam so that it has a chance of interrupting the mobs' spell casting.
4. Hit the mobs early. The group member who hits the mob first has a head start on aggro. Warriors have limited tools for pulling, but learn to pull as well as you can, and get a good bow. If you are not pulling, make sure you have an assist hotkey, and try to position yourself so that you can "tag" incoming mobs with an arrow before they get to camp. At minimum, it will put you on the hate list behind the puller, and sometimes it will cause the mob to turn on you.
5. Use the provoke/incite line. It is really a great tool for aggro. The three best tools you have in early levels for aggro control are good weapons, provoke, and the taunt button.
6. Always try to be as close as possible to the mob you are fighting. Proximity aggro is seldom a critical factor at your level, but it never hurts. Start getting in the habit of using every single tool available to you.
7. Be aware of who is in your group, and where all the mobs are and who is getting hit. All the aggro tools in the game won't help a tank who simply fails to notice that two mobs are killing his cleric. Stupid casters can get themselves killed no matter what you do, but it should always be a goal of the main tank that no caster dies before you do: it should be very very difficult for a mob to kill anyone in your group without killing you first. If the healer in your group dies in a fight and you live through it, you should analyze what happened and what you could do differently in the future to prevent that from happening.
8. Understand how crowd control works, especially Mez and root.
9. When you hit 30, set up a hotkey for /shield. Find out how it works, and use it when you lose aggro to a groupmate who cannot take much damage. I happen to believe that in addition to it's main function, /shield has an aggro-generating component, but I haven't done any testing on it. Nevertheless, it is a nice tool whether or not it generates hate for you.
10. I'm probably forgetting something. Find out whatever it is that I'm forgetting, and work on that too.

Brabble
Dropable haste usually ends up in the guantlets, belt, or back slots: you'll have to give up something to put haste somewhere. The good news is that you'll be replacing all of these slots eventually, and none of them are hugely superior to the others at the moment. That should leave you free to get the best deal you can find; just look around and start keeping track of what different haste items cost on your server so that you'll know a good deal when you see it.
And I would say that you are intellegently geared right now for your level.
Check posts in library for potential quests. Most quests are more for fun than cost-effective rewards. In addition to contemplating gear, you might think about this: read posts on this site and notice what people say about the things that they've done that have been the most fun. Often they are not what you'd expect: running across Antonica just to see it, for instance. Spend the time to do some of these things.
Go hunt. Don't worry too much about how fast you're leveling (leveling is good, worrying about it is not); work on your character development (skills, etc) and your playing skills.
You're good to go, looks like to me.
