Common psychological bias. When something is more rare (the BoW) humans tend to ascribe more positive benefits and a greater value to that item.

In experiments conducted by many psychologists across many different age groups, this effect is always seen. In one variation of the experiment, test subjects were asked to rate the flavor, quality, and tastiness of two different jars of cookies. The first jar of cookies had ten cookies in it. The second jar of cookies had just two cookies in it.

"The cookie in short supply was rated more favorably than when it was one of ten. The cookie in short supply was rated as more desirable to eat in the future, more attractive as a consumer item, and more costly than the identical cookie in abundant supply." -- Cialdini 1984.

Identical cookies, two vastly different reactions to them depending purely on scarcity. I'm sure the test subjects would have replied, "but I tasted the difference with my mouth. How dare you tell me that the cookies are the same!"

That's what's going on with the BoW. Guilds work LOTS harder to kill RZTW and therefore there's a natural tendency to justify that work by assuming that RZTW loot is superior to other comparable loot.
