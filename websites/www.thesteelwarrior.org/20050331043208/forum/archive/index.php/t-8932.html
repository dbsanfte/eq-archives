Cut and paste from rpgexpert:

Runes for Omens Spells

There are five types of runes you can obtain through Omens Of War to trade in for 66-70 spells.

Here is what they are:
1) Minor Muramite Rune: Level 66 spell.
2) Lesser Muramite Rune: Level 67 spell.
3) Muramite Rune: Level 68 spell.
4) Greater Muramite Rune: Level 69 spell.
5) Glowing Muramite Rune: Level 70 spell.

They are all turned into the same guy, he is SW of the zone in to Dranik's Scar. His location is -1650,-40. (Basically, go southwest and look for the red con mob).

They all drop from various named, the higher level the named, the greater the rune will be. Obviously, the level 69/70 spells will drop off of raid-encounter type mobs.

You will never receive a duplicate spell. You only will get a spell you need from turn in.

The port spells for Druid and Wizard are very easy to get. The components will drop from random trash mobs in the Bloodfields (crystals and parchments), and in Wall of Slaughter (blood and parchments). You need 3 crystals/blood per 1 parchment for a single spell.

09-20-2004, 01:32 AM
Proccing a 400pt rune DOES make a huge difference, even if its only 4 times....

Remember what happens when you raise your AC: Overall, you may take 20dps less...but its not like every second you're getting hit for 20 less, its that maybe every 10 seconds you are hit for 200 less.

Proccing a 400pt rune 4x in a minute effectively makes you take 27dps less in that minute. It's not huge, but it is comparable to the difference between having Symbol/Nine/WOG versus just having Symbol/Nine. And in fights where you're barely surviving by the skin of your teeth (general reparm, or the Diabolic Destroyer for a lot of people, for instance) -- it could mean the difference between a wipe and success, for sure. Rune proc is the reason I went with a SEWS instead of a BoW -- my SEWS will mitigate damage better than a BoW, even though the BOW has more AC on it.

Also remember that a rune proc is an aggro proc -- a 400pt rune should give you around the same aggro as an Anger2 proc, I believe (I dont remember for sure, and I'm too lazy to look it up).

Would I like to see the procrate increased, or perhaps the damage sponge increased? You betcha...but its a disc I would use often (assuming it will stack with my SEWS proc)
