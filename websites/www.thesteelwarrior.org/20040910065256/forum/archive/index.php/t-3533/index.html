Oloth
Although I'm not a 5 year vet. of EQ and of the class warrior... I've often come to wonder why there is so little weapon diversity in EQ... Everything has seem to come down to Enraging Blow type effects. I have yet to see such a case warrior might base weapon selection vs. encounter... In the same way that casters might for DD's or DoT's. I might consider that an excellent way to add a little spice to Pure melee, but then again the community might not want spice??? Would we prefer the works on everything consistancy or a greater weapon selection that might add few more factors to an encounter?

Just a few ideas of my own...that might make it worth while to have a weapon bag....

1. Increased Proc rates vs creature type ( undead proc weapons vs undead... disease based procs vs mobs weak against disease.. bane weapons...etc..)
2. Worth while bane or + dis/fir/col/mag like 5 on 1h 10 2h where the dps increase would be significant.
3. More multi component procs ( like Anger (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=3624&source=Live)) With a variety of effects that would be benificial vs a type of encounter) These could range from mana sieve type to large spell runes...
4. Weapon Foci... worn effects that increase the effectivness of either procs (in the increasing rate or effect) Casters get spell foci pure melee get weapon foci. (Btw it think it would be fair to exclude hybrids from best effects ( as I believe they should have been excluded from caster foci best effects since their are not specialized in either)) or hit rate (hit/miss ratio) or even discipline extention (defensive/aggressive) or further reduction in reuse by 20% etc...

Add Augments to the mix and I'm sure there could be even more room for creativity... Now.... then again like I mentioned earlier maybe the community would not be in favor of this (after Seru/Emp type encounters). That was an encounter disign mistake and obvious timesink.. I would be always be in favor of non-specific weapons, but am suggesting an itemization that would make it worthwhile to attempt to attain specific weapon types.

Now EQ is a bit of an old dog at this point and maybe it's too late to impliment something this dynamic in nature. Although it might be worthwhile to make more dry runs in future expansions with a full implimentation in EQ2 where there are no legacy issues.

So am I in left field here? or Should things stay static (Stick with what works, don't rock the boat...)
