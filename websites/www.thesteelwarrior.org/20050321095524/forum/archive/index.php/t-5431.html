Lately while waiting on a spawn or competing for one against guilds with overwhelmingly more numbers it makes me think if it's worth doing the 'low numbers' thing.

So I'm wondering what you guys think. Do you guys think the big volume way is it? Or would you rather go with just enough or even lower than adequate and get more glory? Just curious.

Zerg was a unit in Starcraft. People used them early in tha game to create "Zerg Rushes" that would overwhelm an opponents defenses with chaep-fast to produce units. This actually existed in numerous games (C&C etc) before Starcraft but the name "zerg" came to mean in eq:

1. Guiilds that use alot of people to kill an individual mob
2. Guilds that would res stick people during an encounter to just throw bodies at a boss
3. Guilds that just plain have alot of members

I've always preferred the term SimCorporation for item 3. There are CEOs, HR and logistic issues with such massive guilds. You also get the same reduction in personal respopnsibility, bureacracy and other attributes you find in most large companies. Note: this is not necessarily a derogatory term as yopu also get a deeper resource pool and less dependence on any individual.

All that being said, I recommend the original poster do what best fits their needs/objectives in the game. There are pros/cons to both approaches.

I've been in both.

I was in a guild called Forge of Ro for several months with the absolute finest EQ players I have ever played with. Loot was aplenty as there were only about 40 total to equip. The problem was, we couldn't do many encounters because we:

1) Didn't have enough numbers on at one time.
2) Didn't have enough numbers period (PoP content).

The guild had class though and I enjoyed every single damn bit of it. Victory was placed on the backs of the individual and it was an awesome feeling of accomplishment when you won. Some of my best EQ experiences were in this guild.

I'm now in a guild that raids with 60-70 called Veneration X. This does, indeed, have several advantages. For one, you usually don't have to worry about not enough people showing up. Even if just over half the guild signs on Friday night, that's still 40ish to raid with to go off Emperor Ssraeshza, for example. Two, it is true, you can plow through PoP content. This doesn't mean everyone can jack off as they please, as PoP encounters aren't really designed to allow that, but it does keep morale high when you kill MM one week and RZ two weeks later. There are some disadvantages though.

1) The individual sees less loot because, quite simply, there are tons more people to equip.
2) Takes fawking forever to get everyone backflagged. :p
3) Takes a long time getting people keyed for events such as Emp and VT.

As far as us Warriors go, the primary tank in the guild I'm in now came from an elemental guild. While he wears no Raex, he's quite well equipped with VT gear and a few non-Raex elemental pieces. He's a hella solid tank and a damn fine player.

I'm happy and have enjoyed the experiences of both.
