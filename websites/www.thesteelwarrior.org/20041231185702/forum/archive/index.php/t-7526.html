Look up:
http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=6806

The general consensus I've found is that, assuming you're using one type much more often than the other, go for the hand-specific one.

If you find yourself dual wielding much more, get Ambidexterity, then flurry.
If you find yourself going 2h much more, get PB, then flurry.

If you use them both an equal amount, get Ambidex and PB, then flurry.

While flurry in and of itself is going to be a better skill once its maxed out, it requires you to get CF first, then has 6 levels to work on. You'll get more bang for your buck, and sooner, if you do ambidex or PB (or hell, do em both! 2+4+6+9 > 2+4+6+3+6+9+3+3+3)

You will notice an increased procrate going from 0double attack to maxed double attack. You will notice an increased procrate getting ferocity, flurry, PB/Ambidex.

There's only one problem with this post: the original parses are no longer available at the links it provides, and the math in the post (unless I am very much mistaken) is flat-out wrong.

You'll note that in the first parse the off-hand proc (non-melee) fired 732 times over the course of 02:11:36 which is an off-hand proc every 77 seconds.

In the second parse, the off-hand proc fired 1476 times over 04:02:50 which is an off-hand proc every 47 seconds.

But 2:11:36 is 7896 seconds for 732 procs, or 10.79 seconds/proc. or 5.567 procs per minute. Given that the normal offhand proc rate is 1 PPM at 305 dex, I'm simply not believing that he was getting over 5 PPM from his offhand. And given that, his whole assertion becomes questionable.

Similarly, 4:02:50 is 14570 seconds for 1476 procs, or 9.87 seconds per proc. That's 6.07 PPM.

His numbers show ambidexterity changing the offhand attacks from 45.24% of all attacks to 47.37% of all attacks (9086/(9086+10999) to 11865/(11865+13185)). That's a 4.7% increase in offhand attacks ((47.37-45.24)/45.24), or a 1.5% increase in total attacks ((1.4737-1.4525)/1.4524). Unless I screwed up my math somewhere (which is entirely possible).

Yet the author describes a 7% increase in offhand attacks. So once again, we have a disparity of numbers.

In any case, I find it difficult to believe that a 7% increase in offhand attacks can move you from an offhand proc every 77 seconds to an offhand proc every 47 seconds.

The numbers just don't add up. Unless someone can provide either the original parses, or point out where my math is wrong, I think we're going to have to move this back to the "unproven" category.
