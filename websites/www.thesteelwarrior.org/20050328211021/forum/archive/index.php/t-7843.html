Aggro procs are good, and are mainly slows, snares, direct hate, stuns and ac debuffs. When DWing, the offhand ratio is the only thing that really matters. For example, a 10/20 is as good as a 20/40 offhanded. For the primary hand, speed *does* matter, so sometimes a lower delay weapon is better than a better ratio, higher delay one.

For armor: ac is good, hp are good. I recommend a 1ac = 1sta = 6hp formula as a rule of thumb. Other stats just aren't significant enough to matter, as long as they're over 75. Regen and attack are normally overrated and overpriced, though 15 more regen, like on a fungi tunic, is very nice below like 60.

For skills: Don't mash taunt, save it until you lose aggro. Taunt won't help if you already have aggro. Max all weapon skills, or at least get them near max. You never know when a new weapon will pop up. Use incite line after a successful taunt, not before one. If you can't handle the mob coming at you, don't be afraid to use a defensive discipline (if you're high enough level).

More general advice: Know a little bit about every other class. Know what they can do, so you can act to compliment it. Know when a caster roots an add so you can get out of melee range of it. Know which classes have the buffs you want, if they're not offered immediately. Know how to react quickly to changing situations.

Just want to dispell this myth, pets do NOT push any more than a PC. This is parsed on the beastlord site, by Coprolith, link.

It is true pets do not push more. However, it is the cumulative effect of multiple pets all pushing from the side that causes issues. Once a mob starts moving in a certain direction, the pets will eventually all be on the same side of the mob. This can create a huge push. In other words, once a mob starts moving in a certain direction, it will accelerate in that direction as all the pets will align themselves on exactly the same side.

Just as if 20 PC's were all attacking a mob from exactly the same direction would cause a huge push, the same is true for pets. However, it takes only a matter of a second for a PC to reposition themselves. It is much more difficult to reposition a pet. Given this, most pet classes do not take an active role on repositioning their pet, instead leaving it to the PC's to contend with countering the pet push. Which means if all the pets are on the mobs back, PC's must move to the mob's front to counter. Which is the last place most melee want to be, due to ripostes.

It is for that reason that pets are blamed for push. And rightfully so. However it's not the pet owners fault, that's how the game mechanics work. Which doth suck mightily.

Until SoE builds some intelligence into pet positioning, this will always be a problem. Personally I'd like to see some pet controls that let the pet owners reposition their pets without the pet owners having to move. Something like /pet attack (mobs)front, /pet attack (mobs)back, /pet attack (mobs)right, /pet attack (mobs)left or equivalent buttons on the pet window to do so, or both so you can either use the pet window to reposition or hotbuttons, whichever is your preference.
