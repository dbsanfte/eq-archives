You should not assume that increasing your ATK makes the same weapon produce more aggro. The blade of war holds aggro because of its outstanding damage-bonus-to-delay ratio. This happens to give it good DPS as well. It also holds normal aggro against immune mobs like Seru, even though it does 0 DPS. This should tell you that aggro is not dependent on DPS, but is only dependent on the properties of the weapon.

So no, if I'm tanking and tight on buff space, I don't take ferocity or ferine avatar. To summarize the question about ATK tho, DPS is irrelevant to agro. The agro equation looks like:

1) # of swings, modified by damage rating
2) # of procs, modified by agro from the proc of course
3) agro modifiers like Thule and Raex BP
4) Taunt, incite, etc

so is aggro actually any harder to hold when you have defensive discipline running?
No, it's not.
Higher ATK equals more potentional dmg, but apparently that doesn't enter the equation?
Correct. Higher ATK only makes your hits trend closer to maximum, which is thought to have no bearing on aggro.

How much aggro per swing is based on your weapon's damage bonus.
How many swings per combat round is based on your skills and AA's.
How many combat rounds per minute is based on your weapon delay and haste.
How many times you miss is based on your level vs. the mob's level.
