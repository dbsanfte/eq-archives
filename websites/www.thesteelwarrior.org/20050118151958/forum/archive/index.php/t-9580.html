Honestly when it comes to aggro 2handers, I'd rather use a GSoT over an edge of envelopment. No innate aggro proc = just about worthless from a tanking perspective. In a random pickup group you might be fine with an edge of env, but get a dps class that is ele+, let alone high end GoD, and they will have aggro 90% of the time.

With WA5 and an anger proc, you'll get 3 anger procs per minute, and 3 100dd procs if using an edge of env. A GSoT would get you 3 stun procs instead of the dd, which is over 1000 more aggro per minute from procs. True, dps would be lower, but I doubt it would be enough to compensate for the proc. With 6 aggro procs per minute on a 2hander, you'll average 1 proc every 10 seconds. The odds of getting an early proc (which is where we have aggro issues) is much better than if you only had A3 (3 procs per min, 1 / 20 sec).

Until you get really really nice 2handers, 40/30 + aggro proc or better, it's almost a given that your dualwield setup is better aggro. If you've got the plat, by all means get an edge of envelopment; at least mobs will die quicker while the ranger in your group tanks.

Ygnir, an unaugged edge will NOT be a good tanking tool. It's just that simple. A bazaar rogue, bst, monk, ranger, or caster will out-dps you more often than not, which forces you to incite as soon as it refreshes. If the slower gets a resist, or the snarer casts at 50% because oow mobs resist it so much, you will lose aggro.

Pure DPS is a terrible method to maintain aggro. We are not a dps class, we don't even come close to decent dps until time+ weapons. Any dps class with ele or better gear, or epic 1.5, or whatever, can and will steal aggro just by doing 50% more damage than you. The solution to this is aggro procs. If you can choose between gaining 10-20% dps to use a 2hander with no innate stun/debuff/anger proc, or have a weapon with an aggro proc of its own, it's obvious which is better for a warrior.

Relying on the incite line on every pull works, maybe. You shouldn't have to count on it though. If you can keep aggro without it, and only use it when you have an unlucky string of 20 seconds without any procs, then yeah use it. But using it more than once per mob will result in you losing endurance really fast. With a good chainpuller, you'll be out of endurance in less than 20 minutes. Then how do you keep aggro?
