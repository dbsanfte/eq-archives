Coprolith, the meaning of whose name I am *not* going to discuss with you, determines (and displays graphically) that EQ's RNG is not, in fact, streaky at all. Woot stats geeks.
Battleblade points out (although not in these terms) that the problem is not that the RNG is a *bad* RNG--it's precisely that it is a *good* RNG. Truly random behavior includes outcomes that violate people's statistical intuition, like "I have a 60% chance to succeed at Taunt--how can I *possibly* have failed Taunt 3 times in a row?!"
Your friendly editor posts on this at some length.
