Selling tradeskill drops is by far the quickest way to make money at low levels, and as mentioned above you will be able to make armour for yourself once your skill gets higher up with the non-imbued versions of dorf plate.

The armour you are looking at smithing at the moment is good for your level, but you could probably come by equivilent or even better in the bazaar for very reasonable prices.

You could also try questing some, take a look at http://everquest.allakhazam.com and read up about the crafted armour quests.

In the Bazaar have a look for Crustacean armour, crafted armour and Ry`gor armour. Also do a search on dwarf warrior gear with hit points, put in the price you can afford and see what is available for each slot, gear with high +hp is good to get.

Another thing to do is head over to http://www.magelo.com and create a profile for your character. Post it here and Im sure you'll get lots of advice on where to make the best improvements to your gear.

As mentioned earlier smithed armour is a stepping stone to you as you gear up and there are several other paths you can take, as long as you are having fun with your character thats whats important. Level 30 isn't too far along to re-roll your character if thats what you want to do, but in the long run you wont see any big advantage from being a brell worshiping dwarf vs an agnostic one. (if you reroll as something that can worship Rallos Zek its a different story though...)

When they first introduced the newbie armours, I said what the heck and made a human warrior in freeport. I did all the quests (took a while) and was the proud owner of a full suit of Steel Warrior armour. I bashed wisp for a while and then proceeded to Paludal Caverns at level 16. I had little problems tanking here and when the xp became trivial I moved on. It was here things began getting hard. All the sudden I bacame a papertank and a manaspunge and I ended up deleting the character (it was an experiment after all).

The best you can make with smithing would be banded armour. Banded armour is much like newbie armour: it will take you to your early 20, but after that it will start to hurt. People expect higher standards with regards to hp and ac today, because they're used to twinks and because the market is flooded with cheap items. The problem with your smithing approach is that smithing takes alot and I mean alot of plat to get even near worthwhile. The trivials on culture armour are very high. My advice to you, is forgetting smithing for now and buy some budget armour in bazaar. When you get into higher levels, more plat will find its way to your pockets and you will start being able to afford hobbies, which is what tradeskills basicly are.
