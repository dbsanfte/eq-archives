Warriors and knights are on the same damage table. Monks are on a different table, not sure about rogues.

To answer your question: A warrior will have higher DPS with his weapon than a knight of similar skill/gear/AAs. Undead are an exception to this for paladins with the Slay Undead AA, and high level SKs can buff their attack to warrior like levels (don't know if this can be sustained though). This doesn't take into account other offensive spells. In any event, the difference at 65 is not large.

Simple answer to your question: The warrior will deal more damage due to having a higher attack due to having a higher skill. However, I doubt that the difference would be significant.

Spell or item based +atk *seems* to only affect damage distribution (chance to hit max damage or higher on the damage distribution spread), and not affect accuracy. Accuracy *seems* to be affected primarily by weapons skills, and/or offense rating. In that case, the warrior has the "to hit" advantage, and only the 'chance to land for or close to max damage' advantage is really minimized via atk buffs.
