Mynka
01-15-2004, 01:44 PM
I have noticed that pally's are the worst at controling their agro

This is so absolutely true. It may be by design however. SK's (as opposed to Palaldins) are built around aggro: getting it when they need it, keeping it when they should have it, and losing it when they shouldn't have it.

For example...

On my SK I have specific weapons that I use when I am MT, as well as a specific spell set. All designed around keeping and holding aggro. I switch out weapons and spells for different sets when I am not MT (i.e. if there is a warrior in the group better suited to tank than me). Should I draw aggro I just back off or FD. IMHO this is the vision of what a Hybrid should be. Tank if there is no Warrior, DPS and utility when there is.

The problem is (as far as I know, I've never played one) Paladins don't have the ability to shed aggro like an SK does. Yeah, they can back off, but if they've just dropped 14 stuns on a mob, that's not really going to work like FD can. Of course the over stunning is attributed to a poorly played Paladin. But unless the Paladin has leveled up with a Warrior odds are they have been MT most of their career, and this is the only way they know how to play.

The bottom line is a lot of Hybrid tanks (there ARE some SK's that fit into this category too!) don't understand that just because you can take aggro doesn't mean that you should.
