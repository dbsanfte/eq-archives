And, while the subject is heading off into its own tangent, I thought I'd go off and explore the depths of my puny halfling brain myself!

The subject is raid level Warrior weapons.

I had been giving myself some time to think about this, as I often do while either taking a shower, or running my nightly 4-mile course. With the advent of OoW and the introduction of the new Epic 1.5's, the entire weaponry progression of PoTime/Ikkinz raider gets completely thrown off, but yet some classes still remain unchanged. This is inevitable with expansions that old weaponry becomes outclassed. We saw this in the BoT towers in conjunction with Ssra Temple, and we saw this some Ikkinz weapons, though not all, when compared up against their PoTime counterparts. However, a new trend has arisen, and I think it's offset the balance, not necessarily in some classes being overpowered, but simple game mechanics not being taken into consideration.

Fact is, all PoTime+ content is essentially balanced based on the Warrior having a Darkblade. That is fine, as when the droprate of the Darkblade is put side-by-side to the percentage droprate of, say, some other class weapons out of PoTime, it's balanced.

Introduce the epic 1.5 quests.

For most classes, the epic 1.5 represents an item that's basically equivalent to about a Tier5/6 PoTime item, and yet you can achieve the new epics for less than a 100th of the work that it takes to get a guild to PoTime. No biggy, expansion mudflation is inevitable, and things like this are to be expected. But here comes that mechanics flaw I was talking about. If the majority of the guild isn't even bothering with most of the Time weapons, and are simply skipping straight to the Epic 1.5's (such as my own), what does that leave the position of the Warrior class?

It's not that I believe this is an extremely critical issue, but it does tie into the fact that the Darkblade is an amazingly powerful weapon (We have 1 so far), and all content beyond the Plane of Time is essentially balanced based on the Warrior wielding one. The problem I am seeing is this: The Warrior 1.5 is an incredibly solid weapon for the sheer amount of work involved, but it is designed in mind to be a complementary weapon at best. It is absolutely NO replacement for a Darkblade or even a Fleshbiter as many of the other classes epics replace the function of their PoTime weapon with ease (Exceptions to this rule: Shamans and Enchanters for the clicky slow and Clerics simply because the Cleric 1.5 is a shield).

The observations I am seeing in my guild are as follows:
-Blade of War users, like myself, can still keep agro against this "New Breed" of dps classes, but it's obvious that the agro line is way too close for comfort. I never had problems holding agro against a Rogue wielding an Ifir/GRMK combo with my Blade of War. Put a Fatesealer and JoS in their hands and it's like returning to the old Warrior days of frustration. Why not look for an agro upgrade? Quite simply, outside of the Darkblade, it doesn't seem like there is one.
-We once regarded the SEWS as a viable alternative to a Blade of War given its stingyness for dropping, but so far in my observations, a SEWS wielding Warrior stands no chance against holding agro from a primarily epic wielding raid. The Warriors in my guild who have a SEWS are starting to refuse MA duties. There just isn't much else to give them. =/

Basically, it boils down to this. The Epic 1.5's are upper Time quality, and while a Rogue can happily acquire a Fatesealer and not have to worry about a JTB, a Knight can get their 1.5 and not necessarily worry about getting a Greatblade of Chaos, etc etc, the Warrior gets his epic, puts it in this bag, and says,

"Gee, this'll be nice to use with a Darkblade."
