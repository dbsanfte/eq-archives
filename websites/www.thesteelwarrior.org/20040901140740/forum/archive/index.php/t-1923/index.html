Sangiovese
So it looks like SOE is going to stick with weapon procs as the means for warriors to generate aggro. At least they are adding some weapons with +hate procs for the lower to medium end game where there previously weren't any.

Unfortunately, that means we are stuck with the same problem... it works fine after we have some time to build up aggro and get a few procs - but we are still at the mercy of a random event. If a Knight needs aggro, he casts a spell and he gets it (whether the spell lands or not doesn't matter). If we need aggro, we pray for a successful taunt or a weapon proc (taunt does nothing if it fails... the proc we have zero control over).

So how do you fix it? The "taming your weapon" suggestion I think was on the right track, but wasn't simple enough and started adding too much.

So here is my idea. At level 50, give warriors an ability called "weapon mastery" or something along those lines. It gets a button in the UI and has a refresh time of one minute. When you activate the ability, it forces a proc of the weapon in your main hand on the next swing.

Other than being able to force one proc per minute, procs are otherwise unaffected.

The reuse time would drop by one second per level, for a 45 second refresh at 65.

This would allow us a small "snap aggro" effect that would scale up depending on gear... but an extra proc every 45 seconds wouldn't be something that I think would overpower us... and Knights would still have an advantage because they can still chain cast.

I don't mind knights having an advantage over us in aggro control. I DO mind being completely unable to do anything other than "hope for a proc" to gain aggro.

Thoughts?

Panazule
The best solution I have been able to come up with to address our snap aggro issues while not trivializing raid encounters was to base the aggro on the level of the mob.

In PoP it's safe to say that any boss mob is going to be 66+ (and even that is a low figure).

If we were given some sort of a AA that would give us a taunt button that would only effect mobs under level 65, even if it has a chance for failure, would give us some form of snap aggro that would effect our exp groups but not take the challenge out of a raid encounter. The counter could be 1 min, 45, 30 basied on a 3/6/9 AA or something like that.

I'm a warrior and even I see the downside of giving us the ability to snap aggro without failure and no restrictions. It would take away much of the strategy and skill needed for many things in EQ.

The only way I can see maintaining this balance and fixing our situation in exp groups is by taking the level of the mob your fighting into account.

Some people have brought up the idea of giving warriors other skills such as a "Group Shield" aa, that would shield your group members while they were getting beat on so you could get aggro. While in theory and in roleplay maybe this is a good idea, I just don't think that the average exp group is going to want to bother to have to rely on it.

As far as stepping on toes, I don't really think giving us a snap aggro that we must work for via AA is bad. A Paladin still has his group heals and the ability to chain cast his snap aggro as well as LoH in a pinch. A SK still brings such things to the table as group mana taps, pulling abilities, chain casting aggro spells, group heals, lifetap proccing weapons with aa's that make them crit for well over 1k a shot, and so on.

My point is even though a warrior has more HP than a SK/Paladin and a slightly higher defense, a SK/Paladin has other things that significantly make them attractive to a group for exp even if warriors were bumped up to be close to their aggro abilities. Let's face it, once your hitting 7,8,9k hp anyway, there is little matter, the only difference is what percentage the cleric starts their ch. As long as the group is chain pulling, whats the difference if the cleric is at 80 or 40 mana the entire time.

It's certainly not a black and white issue because making the wrong decision here could drastically change the way EQ is experienced but it's not a situation that cannot be solved. Whether SoE bothers to solve it is something that I wonder about.
