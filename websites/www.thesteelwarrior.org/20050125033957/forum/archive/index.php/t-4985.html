Would watch bazaar for this set of armor (search gothic). Cost to a smith to make it is ~60-100p per piece. On most servers it is selling between cost and double cost but I think on Stromm it might be a bit higher since there is more market for lower level items. Each piece of this set has decent AC with STR, STA and SvMagic. Unfortunately no raw HPs but the sta is good and if you find a good deal on it, it might be good for you. Specially good from this set is face slot - hard to find an AC 10 face elsewhere. Also shoulders seem popular (I used to smith it).

Runnyeye zone has some nice AC items that I'm guessing are common. Should be good xp while you hunted but not sure that trading HPs for AC is a good or bad idea. Belt at least is pretty good - Black Alloy Girdle (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=6024) AC: +8 Sta: +5. Zone also drops a nice caster neck so might be good place for your guild to spend a few days. Whatever no one will wear can be vendored for cash to buy more upgrades.

Permafrost has some nice items although only one that I really thought good for a warrior. Might be another nice place to explore with guild though. I remember having a lot of fun there and there are a lot of named in there with decent drops. XP and Vendor loot was good if nothing else although you might be too high for good xp here.

Unrest is incredible xp at your level. Bronze armor drops are common so plat is good there and there are some named drops that might be worthwhile. If you go with a group you trust, have a druid or wizard bind right outside the zone and then self port somewhere to sell and gate back to /split. You should all make a lot of plat in a short time that way. This is probably my favorite zone in the game. Take your cleric there and he/she will love you forever. :)

Just realized Tak theme has a 20 point set of armor that isn't too bad. Not all the slots would be upgrades for you, but it might be worth looking into. You could earn those in a couple days worth of adventuring and they would be nice fillers until you got better upgrades. Example: Sand-Coated Gauntlets AC:12 Agi:2 DR:2, Jeweled Guard Hoop (finger) AC:4 HP:25 MR:5. Shoulders from MMC for 16 points: Savagefang Spaulders AC:6 Sta:3 CR:10. From Miragul (EF) for 16 points: Cursed Cloak (back) AC:5 Sta:4 Cha:4 Agi:4 CR:4 and for 20 points: Frosty Jeweled Necklace AC:4 Dex:7 Sta:7 HP:10. You can go to the LDON Merchant Database (http://www.squidbox.com/skp/eq/ldon/list.cgi) for a searchable list of all the LDoN drops and search for whatever slot you feel you most need to upgrade. If you can find an upgrade for the amount of points you can earn in 1-2 days I think it would be a great deal for you. Anything that will take much longer then that prob isn't worth it at this point since you will be upgrading again soon.

Back to the original poster's situation, being level 25 and on Stromm where prices are more f***ed up that just about anywhere:

At level 25 you might get a drop off a named in a LDoN adventure, but getting 1-2 points per sucess you'll be leveling faster than your points will add up. I'm basing that on my LDoN experience with my upper 30's Beastlord who graduated from getting 4 points on his first normal level 35 (I think, forget the level range of the group, may have been 30)adventures to getting 10 on a hard level 40 one. Took him just about 2 weeks to save up 72 points to get his pack shrew spell, and in that time leveled from 38-45 as a Troll with 20% exp penalty. My rambling point being...once you do have 20 points saved up, you'll probably be close to level 30, so that's 5 levels for one piece of armor.

Runnyeye is a great zone at that level and was my favorite. Blackened Alloy pieces drop off random mobs for your plate and chain classes, Dark Muslin for leather and silk classes,, as well as fine steel weapons deeper in. Plus there are several named. One drops the venerable Blackened Alloy Bastard Sword (Ahhh memories:)) Sporalli Gloves are 9% haste. Black Alloy Medallion is a caster's dream neck with Mana Preservation II, we camped that for my Shaman when he was level 31. A complete list of Runnyeye drops can be found here (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/itemzone.html?zone=27)

Permafrost named seem to skew their drops toward casters a bit more, but they'll want equal time too. I had 4 other people that I played with daily when I first started, we just all saw we were on at same time and grouped. We went to Perma at level 14-16, was our first dungeon experience ever and got rolled so fast. Can a person be panicked and have the time of their life at the same time? Was just great! Here (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/itemzone.html?zone=47) is the list of Perma drops. Permafrost is slightly higher level than Runnyeye acording to Alla, and both give a healthy experience bonus.

Najena I never got to, so can't talk from experience, but should also be good and it has a key system like Befallen. When your groups average high 20s Sol A and Splitpaw will be fun and good places to make some cash.

Definately gather a solid group to do your crafted armor quests. Take turns each day and do a different quest for each memebr of the group. A great way to develop bonds in the game.

I wish you well
