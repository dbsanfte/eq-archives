Personally, I own a 1981 Corvette that i've been working on since december of 1999.

While I prefer the sound and torque from a nice V8 powered Muscle car, you have to give a little respect to anyone who can pull 12 second quarter miles out fo a honda Civic. My only problem with the ricer crowd is that a very large portion of them just don't seem to "get it". You can buy a few year old Honda for 10k, dump 20k into it and turn high 12's or low 13's or you can by a mid 80's camaro for 5k, put another 5k into it and easily be in the low 12's.

I use to work with a guy who's goal was to make his Ford escort ZX2 faster then my friends 400+hp 2000 Camaro SS. Can it be done? absolutly. How did he go about achieving his goal? first he put on a big mouth body kit. Cong him adding 800lbs worth of useless crap to his escort. Next he put a wing that looks like he took it off a sprint car on the trunk. Yea, more useless weight. Then he cut a hole in his airbox so the car would breath better. At least that one might add a horse or two but not enough to be noticable. Then he spent $1200 on a cam and exhaust change which netted him, on the Dyno, a 12.5 horsepower gain. I can change my oil or put in a new set of plugs and wires and pick up 12.5 horsepower. Next he added a 4 inch tachometer to a pillar pod which, last time I heard, he still hadn't connected yet so its just for looks. It doesn't look cool... he put on a 6 inch exhaust tip which he swore was worth 10hp then He swapped in a new set of rims and tires which were still slightly to narrow for my tastes but they were at least better looking then stock. when I moved to california he had picked up about 14 horsepower over stock, spent well over $5000 for it and was running the quarter mile in the mid 16's. The guy with the SS camaro he wanted to beat had just broken into the 11's with a new best time of 11.9.

My corvette cost me $6,000 when I bought it. I've got around $2,000 in a new interior, complete rebuild of the front and rear suspension, swap of the TH350 Tranny to a built 700R4 and a set of 3.55 gears for the rear end. next I'll spend about $3500 on a new Chevy ZZ4 fast Burn Crate motor 385hp/385ft lbs Torque stock then another couple hundred on a new Carb and a set of headers for it and I'll be running mid 12's without any power adders (Turbo, supercharger, N20) for a grand total of about $12,000. I could get by a whole lot cheaper if I rebuilt my original motor instead of buying a crate motor for it but this way I still have the original motor if I decide to sell it some day and the next owner wants it. I know two people who have this same setup in 80-81 corvettes and both run 12.5 consistantly, get 24-28MPG on the highway and pass california Smog tests with flying colors. Not to mention, very few people will tell you a riced out civic looks cooler then a stock 68-82 body style Vette:)

As for resale value, go check the prices on any old musclecar then check what riced out civics are selling for. I saw a Big mouth kit, giant wing, turbo charged Eclipse sell for $7,000 a few weeks ago because the owner couldn't find a buyer at the $17,000 he was originally asking. The car was for sale for more then 10 months until he finally had to take what was offered. Putting all that useless garbage on a car does nothing for resale value unless it really is as fast as it looks. Most however, aren't.

With all that being said, there is an old Plymouth laser running around here where I live that runs high 12 to Low 13 second quarter miles. It looks stock runs reliably according to the owner and is just a very badass ride. The guy also knows cars so thats a plus.

I give all the respect in the world to ricers that make thier cars fast and know what they are talking about when you ask them what they've done to it. Its the guy with the Autozone edition Honda civic reving his 1.8ltrs of screaming bumblebees at my Vette at stoplights that I make fun of to no end.
