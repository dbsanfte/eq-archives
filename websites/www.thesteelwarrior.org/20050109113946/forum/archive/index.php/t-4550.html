I know I am going to get flamed to the high heavens because of this post, but I wanted to give my opinion about the Paladin - Warrior dispute. BTW, incase you didn't catch the nifty title, I am a Paladin.


Disclaimer: Paladins are finally balanced /wave Kunark. Warriors are not


First off, the incoming "opening" change to pure melees I think is a step in the right direction, but somewhat of a crappy change. This opening still leaves warriors at the mercy of the RNG (that sounds familiar...), therefore still having snap agro shitty. Now, I am not saying that Warriors don't need an upgrade, they definitely do, but the upgrade can't be crazy. Here is what I am trying to say.


Paladins need to stay the masters of agro. We must be able to sustain agro longer, and generate agro faster than Warriors.


Before you go apeshit, and go OMFG stupid greedy Paladin fuck off, let me explain. If Warriors can generate as much/more agro as/than a Paladin, the demand for Paladins will fall to Kunark-era levels. Yes, Paladins can patch heal, have stun mitigation (which is a moot point on raid mobs, and a fair amount of exp mobs BTW), rez, buff etc. That is all nice and dandy. But, when it boils down to DPS (on live mobs, dead mobs are up in the air) and HPs/AC, Warrior are much greater than Paladins (except at *possibly* the higher end game. The Bulwark of Vie line is fucking ridiculous - Paladins *should not* be tanking high-end mobs like Time RZ).


However, Warriors DO need an upgrade. What type of upgrade should Warriors get? Something that is not at the mercy of the RNG. AE Taunt was a step in the right direction. Give Warriors something that is snap agro. Not as much as say, Cease, but at least something that can be a halfway-decent substitute for Paladin stun. Make an ability that uses the endurance pool that can be used at any time. Something like a shout, which *can be resisted, but that resist can generate agro, just like Paladin stuns.*


Now, I'm not saying that giving Warriors a shout thing should be the only fix. Warriors should have a utility like SKs (group taps, recourses) and Paladins (group heals, HoTs) have. Maybe something similar to a Diablo II Barbarian Warcry (while not infringing on Bards, they've been fucked over too much :P) - have the Warrior's "powerful shout bolster the resilience of his comrades," causing a brief increase in haste.


Basically, Warriors need, IMHO, two things: A snap-agro generator that isn't random (sorry, an Anger X wouldn't help Warriors), and more utility in groups.



Let's please try to keep this as a civil discussion, but if ya want to, flame away :D

should we compensate for this advantage by giving warriors manna? no, because thats not in the spirit of what a warrior should be. its bad enough that the differences between races are so small and that deities make almost no difference at all. we certainly dont need to increase the overlap in the characteristics of classes as well. this is why i like improving the mitigation (and MAYBE the dps) on warriors as the solution for the balance issue.

also i would caution about overdoing any improvements to the point where it only causes the other melees to complain that they have now gone to the bottom of the list on desirability. there is a fine line between being overpowered and being underpowered. consider that a cleric at level 60 is probably only 20 percent more manna efficient on heals than a druid of similar aa selection specialization selection and equipment. or a wizard only about 20 percent more manna efficient on nuking than a druid. the point being that a 20 percent improvement on either of these areas by a druid would make clerics or wizards utterly devestated in their group desirability and the same would happen to palladins if a warrior were improved by 20 percent in areas that would be a direct competition with palladins.

though its all guesswork to some extent im expecting to see a four percent improvement to mitigation a comparable improvement to relative dps (warrior compared to knights) and maybe a 10 percent improvement to "hate generated from activities other than dps" (which could result in a loss of taunt as much as negative 20-40 percent) and that if true will result in a dramatic difference in perceived value of warriors to group (which people will take a long time to become cognizant of) without it becoming so pronounced as to make them overpowered in the minds of the masses. and so it should be adequate. i would love to see warriors become the new superclass but lets face it i only think so because im a warrior and if i were any other melee class i would be justified in loathing that idea. i think the extent of changes forcast for our class are more or less where they ought to be.
