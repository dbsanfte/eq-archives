In my mind, this is how I see how the combat formulas stack up against each other to determine hits and damage.

AccuracyATK + rand(n) vs AvoidanceAC + rand(n)

AccuracyATK is determined by offensive skill values and dex.
AvoidanceAC is determined by defense skill values and agi.
The rand(n) adds a random roll into the calculation for a probability distribution.

In my mind, + accuracy items add directly to this AccuracyATK value and + avoidance items add directly to the AvoidanceAC value.

Of course, only after a higher roll on the AccuracyATK value will a successful hit be determined. Then a separate roll is made to determine how much the hit was for in a similar equation.

DamageATK + rand(n) vs MitigationAC + rand(n)

DamageATK is determined for offensive skills and str.
MitigationAC is determined from defensive skills and AC.
rand(n) being there of course to provide probablity rolls.

Its this comparison that gives the damage distribution probabilities in line with what we already know about damage calculations. In other words, DB and DI fit in here somewhere.

So damage ditributions would then be modified by +ATK items on the damageATK side while standard + AC items work on the MitigationAC side to determine damage distributions.
