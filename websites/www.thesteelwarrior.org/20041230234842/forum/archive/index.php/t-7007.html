Should I bother training in bash to use a shield, when I'm just going to get dual weild?

The only thing you might want bash skill for is "ghetto bash" where you swap in a shield for second to stun a caster or runner. Most people don't bother with this anymore since they made kick stun at level 55.

How many weapon forms should I train in? Can I get by with using 1 handed slash and dual weild?

You will eventually want to max them all, but probably won't be able to keep up in your early levels. Try to get everything to at least 100ish and you will have time to catch them all up in your 40s. Don't spend any points on melee skill, just let them go up on their own. Put a single point into things like parry, riposte, dual wield, and double attack, but otherwise just save them for things like tradeskills or if you need to pump up a skill you have neglected to use a new leet weapon.

How hard is it to bring up 2h handed? It seems very very slow to me....

It is slower than 1-handers. Try to use the lowest delay weapon you can get your hands on to get skillups.

Ah, it's important to work on all your skills, but for a long time most of your skills will not keep up. Work on the skills, but don't stress that they are not all maxxed. Even if you spend a lot of time soloing in the first 25 levels, you'll simply level faster than you can train all the types of weapons that a warrior can use.
A significant percentage of the best weapons you will get will either accidentally fall into your hands, or will be weapons that you see and realize are extremely cheap for their quality. When that happens, it's nice to be able to do some damage with the weapon from the moment you pick it up. Also, new weapons are introduced into the game now and then, so it's difficult to predict ahead of time what skill will be "best."
Weapons do two things for warriors:
1. They let you do damage.
2. They help you keep aggro.

Now two important things that you should know: first, your weapon skill is directly related to your ability to do damage with a given weapon type. So, if you have a maxxed 1HB skill, you will do the most damage possible within the potential of the 1HB you are using. If you have a skill of 1, you'll miss a lot with the weapon and not do so much damage.

Second: some pretty careful tests have suggested that the amount of aggro you generate has nothing to do with the actual damage you do to the mob with a given weapon. That is, it appears to be the case that if you pick up a 1HB and swing it at the mob, the hate you generate with that weapon will be the same (or functionally the same) whether your skill is 10 or 100. The mob hates you based on the *potential* damage of the weapon. (This is sort of an oversimplification, because later you will use weapons that have a considerable portion of their hate coming from procs, but for some time you don't have to worry about that).

On a practical level, what this means is that if you get a good weapon but have a low skill with that weapon, you can use that weapon safely for aggro purposes. You will not endanger your casters by using a weapon that you don't have a maxxed skill in. You will, however, do less actual damage with that weapon until your skill increases.

The more often you swing a weapon, the more "chances" you get to improve your skill. That's why 1-hand weapon skills seem easier to raise than 2-hand skills: not only do 1-hand weapons tend to be faster than 2-hand weapons, but once you can use weapons in both hands, you'll be swinging two 1-handers at once. Another factor that helps you "learn" a weapon type is your dex. If you group with a shaman, for example, you can ask for a dex buff and it will help.

Again, generally speaking, the more advanced your warrior gets, the more important it is that you are on the top of the mob's hate list (that is, that you generate more aggro than anyone else in your group) and the less important it is that you are doing damage. That's because other classes produce far more damage than you do, and the gap widens as you get higher in level. Early on, the damage you do can be pretty important to your group, but even as early as your 20s, you are far more valuable to your group for your ability to take damage than your ability to do damage.

It will probably be the case that your best aggro set up will be to use two 1-hand weapons. Eventually, you might feel like switching to a two-hand weapon when you are main tank is bad, because it puts others in your group at greater risk of getting hit. I'd make two points about this: first, be on the lookout for times that you can use your 2-handers. Sometimes you'll be in a group and not be the main tank, for example. Every once in a while, you can ask your group if they would mind if you worked on your 2h (or whatever) skill. If you've been in a group for a while, and all is going well, and people know you're a good tank, they will seldom say no, in my experience. Anyway, you have the right to ask, certainly.

Good luck.

I am the self proclaimed skilling up champ... Got 6 new toons in the last year that I've gotten up to at least 45, and every one of them has all their weapon/combat skills maxed. Here's some tricks pre-level 45 to get those skills capped:

1. Dexterity governs your skillups, so if you have the extra plat, buy some dex items to swap out while your training up (there is a cheap +15 dex neclace, mushroom veil is like 12 dex... stuff like that). Since your PLing, you don't have to worry about getting smoked with weaker gear, so swap these out at the beginning of each new level till you cap all your skills.

2. Go to a shaman and ask for Infusion of Spirit, and Dexterity (and altuna if you want the hps too). Say "NOT HoS". HoS has a built in dex component, but the single target dexterity buff won't stack with it. Infusion has the same built in dex component, and Dexterity will stack, giving you an extra 50 dex. If you also grab altuna, you net a few more hps than HoS anyway. Now mind you, IoS is the best kept secret in the game (Imho) so alot of shaman wont have it, and alot won't even know what it is... just do a /who shaman in pok and start sending tells asking for it and dexterity, and offer to donate pp and come to them. it's always worked for me =). But make sure to call it by it's full name. If you just say IoS, they'll think it was a typo and HoS ya... trust me I've had it done about 100 times, and I just don't bother with the abreviation any more.

3. Get a set of low delay weaps for all your weapon types. Don't waste alot of money here getting good ratio weapons, Just get good delay. These will only be used at the beginning of each level until you cap the skill, so don't fret about the DMG (shovel for example is an excellent 2hb training weap. only 27 delay for a 2hb, and usually only 100pp or so).

4. Find a training ground zone. Example: After level 30 or so Magus Seru becomes terribly trivial, with the mobs barely hitting you... Yet the stonegrabbers stay Drk blue till 37-38. This is a perfect place to go practice with your training weaps.


5. Find a punching bag. I can't remember his name off the top of my head, but there is a mob just outside the entrance to the warrens (painel side) that is a drk-blue con well into your 40's and he swings for a max of 19 dmg. He also has tons of hps, making him perfect for practicing. Only drawback is his 400hp HT he starts off with, but after that it's gravy, so no biggy. Plus you're right by a zoneline, so even if you didn't have a healer, you could fight him till ya get low, zone, and bind up. Zone back in and start again. If he dies it's an 8 minute respawn (which isn't too bad) but you could easily avoid it by just zonning in and back (the zone to the warrens is actually a really fast load to) and go again once the insane regen is done healing him heh.

6. Find a good shaman friend. This is really the best meathod, but not available to everyone. If you duel a person, you can get skillups off their pet. So, find your shaman buddy, have them whip out the appropriate level dog (their lvl 61 pet spell is still drk blue to a 65) and /duel. There are several reasons you want to do this with a shaman. One, their dog blows donkey nuts. it doesn't innately duel wield, it misses about 10 times more often than it hits, it's standard hits are for half as much damage as any other pet of comparable level... it's just all around a sad thing to behold... perfect for whooping on. #2, the shaman can keep you set with Dex buffs to help the skillups come along. #3, the shaman can haste you, increasing your swings, and in turn, increasing your skillups. #4 if either you or the dog gets low on health the shaman can heal ya both, so you never have to stop to bind up, or to recast a pet. Now some people suggest going to an arena with the shaman and doing it there, but I find the other people just make it annoying, so it's not worth it. Your shaman can still group with you, heal you, and buff you while dueling (it's really stupid, but it's true) so just go to any old zone that's safe and /duel.

Anyway, hope this all helps
