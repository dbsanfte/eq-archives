I'd suggest using an SK as well. The SK may not be able to take on as big a hitter as the warrior, but with the SK you can at least pull them to even try in the first place. For example, if you ever want to attempt to box high risk LDoNs, it won't happen without a good puller. A warrior puller is going to net you many 4 to 5 mob aggro chains, all hitting for around 990. You won't keep the warrior alive long enough to mezz, nor slow, all of that. The SK will net you singles, with the occasional double if you decide to go with it, through feign pulling.

An SK/shaman/randomDPS can win high risk LDoNs. A warrior/any healer/randomDPS can't even begin to attempt one. The warrior combo can take bigger single mobs, but that's just the problem... you need a way of getting them singled, or at least very thinned depending on how hard the mobs hit. While pacify works good in some areas in the case of a warrior/cleric combo, feign will work in far more.

Alisdahre
Ps. How do you reckon wizard and mage dps would compare in this situation?

Either would be very nice. It's a trade off kinda, more "now" damage vs. slower consistant damage with some extra frills thrown in.

The wizard offers some serious burst damage, and a huge convinience factor with the ports. The wizard is the master of nuking, and can drop a mob very quickly to defuse a potentially hazardous situation. The best burst damage there is. You can also get the entire group out of there in a pinch. There's little chance of running all 4 characters to the zone out if you get into trouble, but holding off the mobs for 10 seconds for an intra zone evac is real handy to have.

Myself, I think that the mage brings more to the table.

The wizard offers quicker and bigger "now" damage, but the mage wins in the DPS department "over time" if you ask me. The mage is the master of stored mana, summoning items to boost others, and more damage over time through consistant use of the pet on top of a decent amount of smaller burst damage.

With the mage you can have a nice big 45 point damage shield on the tank at all times constantly adding DPS (which I would stack Mark of the Righteous from the cleric, plus a potion for those "need as much DPS as possible situations". Can get 112 DS going with all these.).

The pet will always be adding constant, unwavering, computer controlled, stick to that mob like glue no matter who it runs to, DPS even if you need to forget about the mage for the rest of a tense fight to consentrate on other things. It's easy to neglect the wizard and just forget about him for a while if things get too complicated all of a sudden while boxing (which is also why I feel they're so easy to box with, most of the time you're not gonna lose a battle if you just forget about them for a few). With the mage, at least his pet was still adding DPS the entire time while you're forced to neglect his nukes. Kind of a fire and forget constant DPS that you don't have to worry about, it's always there once you've started it. A computer controlled pet is not going to lose track of the mob.

Then there's also the offtank thing someone else just touched upon. The pet can keep a mob busy for a bit while the chanter takes care of other things.

The mage can summon a number of items to boost the SK's pet, the necro's pet, the shaman's pet, and his own pet, adding yet more constant DPS to the total mix.

The mage can summon other foci items for the other casters in the group if needed. Modulation rods are nice too in a mana pinch.

Also, something else to consider, though it may be rare circumstance. The pet can take rampage damage instead of some of the squishier members of the group. I've played a warrior/cleric/wizard combo in Ferubi before, and couldn't keep up with the mana use. The oh so tender cleric and wizard took those rampages. With the shaman and mage pets present, you can set them to take that rampage instead, taking some of the fast work out of the boxing all of those characters. The pets will heal up fairly fast after combat, and leave the healing classes a bit more leeway and mana. I've got to barrow my friends characters if I want to seriously attempt a lot of that stuff, cause even though the warrior/cleric/wizard classes are "individually" more specialized at their jobs than the SK/shaman/mage combo, the latter combo's diversity offers far more in the end... making things the former combo can't even attempt, easy.
