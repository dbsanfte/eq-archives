Little trick to get around the cap for one hand P for warriors. Can do the same for other classes that have lower caps in certain areas and higher in another - monks and H2H comes to mind (or beasts and 1HB - one of them)

Anyway, notice that if you are maxed piercing/2HB and you put a lance in your hands you get a lowered attack than if you put in a 2HB or 2HS. You cant get around that.

Putting a dagger in you PRIMARY hand does the same thing over a sword, BUT if you put a dagger in your secondary it does not lower the attack. Its been over a year since I parsed it, but when I did I found that having a dagger in the off-hand did not lower DPS when compared to a sword/club of the same ratio. I think I was using a 10/18 for both but forget exactly the weapons. Then again, it might have been that dagger on one of the rhags - I cant exactly remember and I dont know if SOE even fixed it.

I would think they didnt but I have used a dagger in a long time, so investigate and get yourself a nice off-hand if it works for you.

Brabble
What Mokor said -- that agro does not depend on the damage that you actually do -- shoudl be comforting to young warriors attempting to practice a wide variety of weapons.
An 11/22 1HS and the "same" 11/22 piercing weapon should generate the same agro even if you've maxxed your skill in 1HS and your skill in piercing is 15.
I suspect that most people who have problems holding agro with practice weapons are having that problem because their practice weapon isn't as good as their "favorite" weapon.

If your main is a warrior in his 30s or 40s and you haven't bought pp online or been given pp by some friends, then you are broke. And that's another reason to practice a variety of weapon skills.

On my server you can get a Jarsath Trident (pri/sec 11/22 STR +5, DEX -5, STA +5, AGI -5) for under 60pp. it's cheap because it's peircing and because of the negative stats. But, for around 65 more pp you can get a shimmeriing partisan (pri/sec, 17/39, STR+10, DEX +20, Fire +10). Yeah, it's pretty slow, but pop it in your offhand with the trident in your mainhand and you should be able to hold agro fairly well in your 30s/40s--at least, I did the last time I tried it with a warrior (just *before* the provoke line was added).

As for people yelling at you for practicing your weapons: if the weapon you are using is significantly slower than your regular weapon, they might have a point in the short run. But if you regularly group with these people, and your practice weapon is okay, then I'd just explain to them that for you to make the best warrior possible, you're going to have to work on a lot of skills. If you're using taunt and the provoke line properly, then a 11/22 mainhand weapon should be generating enough agro for you to do your job in your low 40s even today.

At that level, almost any other class can steal agro from the warrior if they try. As a warrior you don't want to restrict the dps of the group, but beyond a point the other players simply have to learn do do as much damage as they can without grabbing agro. If they spent a lot of pp on some uber proc weapon--well, okay, now they have agro. Duh. Maybe they should bag *their* weapons and break out a non-proc'ing, higher dps weapon, hmmm?

If you explain what you're doing and why, and they're not willing to reduce the agro they're generating, and they're unhappy that you're practicing your skills...hmmm. I guess I'd find someone else to play with who needs a tank.
