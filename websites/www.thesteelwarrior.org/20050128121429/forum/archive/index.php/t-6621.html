Being a warrior is like a wierd sickness =)

I've played a warrior for three years, and I always come back. I've tried 3 different SKs and 2 paladins. Its the attitude that makes the warrior. The good warriors are stubborn, knowledgeable, and confident. Warriors have a limited amount of tools at our disposal to do our jobs. Knights have it much easier with their spells to not only keep agro, but to have some utility. We trade off that utility for our higher hps (not always though), and our tanking disciplines. We are SUPER gear dependent, so being a warrior isn't cheap. While I really haven't experienced the "group stealing" of knight classes, some groups are really preferntial to knights as the tanks so they don't have to worry about their agro.

So the question is, are you willing to go down a tough, mana-less road? Do you want to tank the big nasties? If you are, get behind your warrior 1,000%, learn as much as you can about your class, as well the the mobs/encouters you will be fighting. And find some money =)

Good luck in enjoying the game =)

My first character (and still my main) is a warrior. I also have a shaman and an SK and a beastlord.
have no opinion yet about the beastlord (he's too little yet).
The SK is a troll and I enjoy getting on him and going around killing stuff and, when possible, eating it. He's untwinked, and I've noticed that it's easier for him to hold aggro with cheap gear. Harm touch is fun too. he has a number of ways to help a group, even if he's not MT. His ability to generate lots of hate pretty fast makes him an excellent offtank. And something I don't see mentioned much but that I believe is true: an SK and a warrior who work well together make an unbelievably good tanking team. The SK doesn't mitigate damage as well, though, and every fight I notice it. He's relaxing to play, but it gets down to this: when I go somewhere where there's a good possibility that things will get nasty, I wanna be on the warrior.
I'm very impressed with the shaman. Pretty durable for a caster, and can do a bunch of different things to erode the mob and increase the power of a group. You have to pay attention and think strategically with a shaman. It's very weird to be doing nothing but medding part of the time though. I have to remind myself to sit down, and sitting down just seems wrong.
And then there's the warrior: expensive to gear up, hard to hold agro, minimal pulling abilities, hard to travel, very little extra utility. Yet once the fight starts, there's nothing that compares to tanking, and no tank as tough as a warrior. You manage aggro, position mob, watch push, keep an internal running estimate of everyone's mana, notice mez order, and always always think about what comes next. Eventually I've found that even my lack of snap aggro is oddly attractive: it just means that I have to pay attention to what I'm doing and everyone else is doing and where the mobs are--aggro is something you manage by thinking three deep into the mob order. When the chips are down, I don't want to be staring at a screen with a bunch of stupid spell gems blocking my view.
