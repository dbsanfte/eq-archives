search the boards (since im too lazy to find it for you) and locate a few good discussions on the split blade. i had a split blade through most of my 50s and then i sold it for agro weapons. this is my take on it (and most here will agree) ...

its a fine soloing weapon. its even good for doubling up with a cleric. but its not good for tanking in typical groups. yes the 20ac on it is good but... your slower will have to wait longer to slow the mob so all the mitigation (what is it one percent?) that you get from it is lost several times over by the excess damage you take waiting for the mob to be slowed. and that mob wont be slowed for a while once your slower learns that he must wait to slow to avoid getting whacked. your enchanter will wait to tash too and your nukers will wait to nuke. some of them will despair that the waiting means they cannot make use of their manna pool. mobs will ping pong and the cleric will waste healing manna and the excess heals may lead to the cleric gaining agro too. all and all a frustrating situation for the whole group.

all good warriors learn that an agro proc is not enough. FOUR agro procs is the standard. and with a split blade you automatically are limited to three. and until you get your agro proc augments you are limited to a rather scary ONE proc (!). i know it hurts to lose the 20ac. i know you love the 14/19 stat on it.

even with four procs a necro stacking dots can gain agro at will. even with four procs a beastlord slowing on incoming will get hit frequently. most consider it a worthwhile tradeoff because they mitigate decently. even with four procs a cleric will find himself rooting mobs to keep them off him if 3 or more mobs come in on the pull. but it sure beats what you go through with say 2 procs. like most warriors with good agro ive had many compliments that i keep agro "pretty decently". its a compliment that when you get it you will forget about the lost dps of the split blade (which by the way isnt much better than a copper hammer on account of the lack of 179dd stun proc that the hammer has - its debatable if it is any better at all).

dont let others get you down by making fun of your split blade. i used one for a long time. i know the letdown of selling it. i DID like the 120k it fetched me. but you CAN still sell it for 100k or more on most servers. it might plummet in price after oow depending what drops in that expansion. sell it while you can. it is THE favorite weapon of many plat buying twinks who equip their warriors before they learn the class.

another word on procing weapons. not all procs produce big agro. dd procs dont produce much. the copper hammer is loved because its stun/dd proc DOES produce good agro even if resisted.

I have to agree with all here and say the SBoD is great for looking good and MAY be good DPS but since you are a warrior DPS is not your primary concern....aggro is. Your job is to get it, keep it and let the mob hit one person. DPS in a group for the warrior is secondary.... if everyone in your group is getting the snot whooped out of them will increase med time for the healers and decrease XP regardless of how much DPS you are adding.

The Gold Standard for aggro until you're raiding mobs for something better is the Copper Hammer of Striking and Sword of the Bloodsworn. These weapons, with some skill at taunting, will carry you through nicely. Add anger augments to them as you can. Both of these weapons proc and yes will sometimes be resisted. Resisted procs DO add aggro. (ie: Think about a caster casting a slow that is resisted.)

Stay close to the mob when ya fight...get in their face so to speak. Do NOT repeatedly mash the taunt button if you already have aggro...if you do, odds are when someone else gets aggro, you will have to wait until it refreshes to taunt (which can seem like an eternity). Notice what the people in your group are doing to catch aggro ..... is the ranger snaring at 95%? is the necro slapping on 3 consecutive DoTs? is the caster slowing and then sitting down prompty (my personal favorite)? Ask them in a tell politely to alter their behavior..."I notice you're getting slapped around alot. I am doing everything I can to keep the aggro. Do you think maybe you could snare a bit later?" Rogues can EVADE and sometimes have to due to their DPS. If you are doing everything you can (have the right weapons combo, taunting at appropriate times, staying tight to the mobs) to keep your aggro and you're convinced that there is nothing you can do to improve the situation (including sending tells to the repeated offender), let the group know you are doing everything in your power to hold aggro. Shortly after this point, I tend to "lose" my taunt button when aggro is snagged by the same person again. I tend to find it right before they have low HP aggro... but if it's a really bad day that person seems to run out of HPs before I can find it again. The "Returning to Home Point" message seems to serve as the only heads up that some will listen to and most often their behavior will be altered upon their return to the group.

One factor that may make some of this advice seem strange to you is that at level 52 your Dex is reading 126. That's fair dex for your level, but as your level/gear/AAs get higher, procs will become more and more obviously important, and you will find that the best dps options available to you simply don't keep up with the dps aggro that other classes can put out if they don't throttle back their damage.
You dex influences the rate at which you proc, in other words, and at low levels and low dex you might have to wait a bit for the proc to happen. But as you advance, you'll have no dps option available to you that will reliably hold aggro.
This, coupled with the going price of your weapon, is why people are telling you to sell it and buy the much cheaper Great Staff of Thunder and the droga weapon.
If I were you I'd find out whether or not your cousin would be offended if you sold the weapon. If not, sell it and buy the others.
If it would bother him (or her) then return the weapon as soon as you can equip a Great Staff of Thunder, and save up for a droga weapon (or, if you have enough to buy the sword, buy it now and return your cousin's gear).
If a rogue swipes your aggro, taunt mob; on taunt failure, immediately /shield rogue. Sometimes that helps to pop the mob back onto you.
