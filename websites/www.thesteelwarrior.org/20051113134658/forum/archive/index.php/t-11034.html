for critts, it doesnt matter, critts you do x% so thats x% damage bonus no matter the speed.
For attack power it doesnt matter either, attack power is calculated in dps.

It does however matter when you use Overpower, MS or Slam; extra attacks based on weapon damage. The more damage (thus slower) your weapon is, the better these instant attacks do for the rage they cost.

When using "on next attack" abilities like heroic strike however, fast is better as it costs you less rage. Rage that you could have gotten by not using a special on that attack.

Fast weapons do create more dodges, critts etc, thus more overpowers, flurries and wounds. These are all based on weapon damage however so therefore it does not matter.

When tanking, a fast weapon will give you more often the opportunity to use revenge for good agro.

Enrage benefits more from a slow weapon as it lasts for x strikes and your weapon speed does not influence the number of enrage activations unlike flurry.

You can however take all the good things and none of the bad ones. Use fast weapons (DW if you want) for your main attack to genereta as much dodges and critts as possible. Swich to a slow weapon when these abilities like enrage or flurry are activated or when you want to do an extra attack based on weapon damage.

Eh, no they won't. The slower weapon will have a higher average damage per swing, even though it's overall dps may be less.

Attack is applied in a linear fashion throughout whatever weapon speed. Lets take an example of 50 dps for attack power.

A 1 delay weapon will add 50 per swing
A 2 delay weapon will add 100 per swing
A 4 delay weapon wil add 200 pre swing.

Because of this, slower weapons not only pull more damage per hit due to the way their dps functions, but they get ever more of a boost from the way attack functions. Needless to say, this is irreliavant in a straight up dps comparison, but when you factor in instants, the harder hitting weapon comes out ahead.

Now, in his example I couldn't say what would be better overall because I'm too lazy to crunch numbers, but if you have 2 equal dps weapons, the slower one is better for both damage and attack power distribution reasons.
