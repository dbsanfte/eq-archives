When my guild got into elementals, we started using an "offtank group" to handle run of the mill offtank duties while clearing.

It usually has 2 clerics, 2 warriors, a pally, and a shammy in it. This group can easily keep three adds on ice for as long as necessary.

You could easily substitute pallies and SKs for the warriors in this offtank group... but our philosophy is that knights have a lot of other things they can be doing for the raid... while a warrior that isn't tanking is pretty much just gimpy DPS. So we use our knights in other roles and let our warriors handle the offtanking.

If you are expecting people to offtank these mobs without cleric support because the clerics are "focusing on the MT" then you're pretty much setting yourselves up for rezzing a lot of dead offtanks. During clearing, the offtanks are going to be tanking mobs that hit every bit as hard as the the one that the MT is tanking... and they often are not debuffed like the MT mob. Building an offtank group solved that problem for us. We have tanks, slower/debuffer, and healers... everything you need to control an add. The pally adds versatility in that he can help healing or tanking... depending on how many adds you're taking care of and how hard they hit.

Frankly, mezzing mobs is dangerous, and a waste. First, it puts enchanters onto the hate list of a mob in a big way. Second, a resist can very, very quickly kill an enchanter and move the mob into the heart of your raid. Some mobs being very hard to target can turn this into a very bad thing. Thirdly, it's a waste of DPS. Any DPS class can generally out aggro a tank while putting the hurt on a raid mob. Having 2+ active mobs in camp allows your nukers/dotters to split up their targets without having to waste mana lowering their aggro. It also wastes the enchanters' time and mana, which they could be using to nuke, debuff and buff.

Obviously, if you don't have the tanks or the healing, mez. Use it more as a last resort though.
