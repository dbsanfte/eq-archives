Hand of the Maestro - Go to Hate, Hand of the Maestro is almost always up. Kill him. Think it drops every time.
Dragon Scales - Start tracking dragons. Or the bazaar. Norclav the ScaleKeeper in the Hole still drops the red one. Think there's a way you hand in a red and get a green?
Spyroc Wingblade - Need a guild capable of getting to the spiroc island in Sky. If you're all level 55, then it may be a challenge.
Ball of Everliving Golem - If you can break the entrance to Fear, you can take a Golem.
Ancient Blade - If you have a group of 65's the Queen of Chardok is single groupable. (Drop into king room and PBAE!!! wheee!! ) If you're all level 50-55, bring a raid force. The Queen fight isn't actually the hardest part, IMO, its getting through the entrance hall of the palace. Something like 15 low 50's mobs that you could get all at the same time.

If you're going to do this, I suggest you spend some time trolling around on the web looking for write ups on all of these encouters. Each of them has a bunch of stuff to them that you can't all cover in one message board post. Depending on the level of your raid force / group / whatever you may need to pay attention to every last detail.

So, sad as it is, Yoda has a point. Guess it depends on how much 'credit' you get for your first epic when you go to do epic 1.5/2.0. If any. As well, if you're in a younger guild maybe the new epics are out of reach for now and you want to get it done 'just because'.

Lathen
If all you want is a weapon, then you should skip the first warrior epic quest. As has been proven with parses that you can find all over this board, the CHoS and SotB combination are far and away better aggro weapons than the two swords. (I did this in my late-50's.)

If you want to undertake a quest that is truly epic in scale and class appropriate, then you should do the Coldain Ring quest! It's a hell of a lot more fun, and if you "general" the final battle, you've proven yourself to be one hell of a warrior. Also, you'll have no trouble getting people pscyhed to fight the Ring War. In fact, you'll have to turn people away once you trigger the war in Great Divide. (I won the ring war last night on Stromm and am STILL stoked.)

If you want to do a quest that requires you to take reluctant guildmates to annoyingly difficult places (Sky, Fear, and Chardok), then you should start epic 1.0. Be warned, though, that you will probably get stuck on those last three drops along with the vast majority of epic-questing warriors. (I am stuck on those last drops.)

Let us know what you decide!
