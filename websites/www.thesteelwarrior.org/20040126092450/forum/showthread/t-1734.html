Valeris
One of the biggest reasons the aggro question is a problem is the high population of tanks all competing for one spot in a group. Most other classes stack in groups much better than Plate Tanks.

The only way I can see to truely address this issue is to continue the 'generalisation' (druid, shaman CH etc) of abilities into the arena of the Tank classes. In it's most radical form I feel it should be possible for a mix of Tanks to group together without requiring a 'supporting' healer/slower class. It would always be more efficient to have such a class in group but the option to do without should be there. Nothing is quite so irritating as LFG and being stuck sitting on the sidelines because you CANNOT do a thing without a certain class. When groups all have their one tank the extra's should be able to make their own way...

Warriors obviously currently have least Utility and thus the lions share of my suggestions pertain to my class. All the figures are basically educated guesswork as to what seems fair to me in relation to other classes competancies. All abilities should be considered as maximum at lvl 65, if balance requires and the abilities are deemed neccessary before the lower 60's then these abilities should be scaled down as required. Here's some that sprung to mind. The first will probably cause a stir...



Warrior Ability : Cripple

The Warrior attacks a creature crippling it's ability to attack, in so doing he leaves himself exposed to attack.

A successful Cripple will SLOW the mob by a certain percentage (around 50%) for a short duration (1-2 mins). Reuse time of 10 seconds between retries. Use of Cripple will give aggro similar to a similar power slow spell and penalise the warrior with 1 minute of mitigation penalty equal to that caused by use of /disc aggressive.

The slow effect is fairly generous as it needs to be to make a tank group effective, the high aggro and increased damage the warrior will take if he remains the centre of the mobs attention serves to prevent casual useage. Defensive and evasive would probably need to be disabled during the period of vulnerability.



Warrior Ability : Storm of blades

The Warrior becomes a whirlwind of steel relentlessly attacking the creatures defenses.

Duel wield, Double and triple attack success chances would be significantly raised for the duration (3 mins) of this ability (someone with a better knowledge of the actual numbers atm would have to determine how much :p ) with a 6(?) minute reuse time. Activation of any /disc during this period automatically cancels this ability. Nor can the ability be used during the active period of any disc's operation. I believe it should be activable during the cooldown period however.

Serves pretty much as an alternate /disc aggressive and a means to up the average DPS of a warrior in a group without making a blanket change in balance. The low reuse time means it can actually be used during exping. The ability is balanced by the additional riposte damage the tank would be exposed to if the mob is facing him/enraged during the course of the ability and the inability to use emergency defensive measures without loosing the dps . Would primarily be used as a rogue type rear attack (i.e. not tanking)



Warrior Ability : Berserk Fury

The warrior builds himself into a fury, shrugging off blows and returning them tenfold to a variety of opponents. By the end of his fury he is weakened and exposed.

Activable at any time. /discs in operation and other abilities are immediately disabled. For 30 seconds the warrior may attempt to riposte all attacks directed at him in a 360 degree radius. His riposte chance is increased by 50 - 75 %. All attacks that successfully hit the warriors current target (including ripostes) are considered criticals and are applied as AOE damage to mobs in melee range. For one minute after the effect wears off the warrior suffers the negative aspects of both /disc precise and /disc aggressive (Hit more for more damage). Reuse time of 20 minutes. No other abilities (except /shield) may be used until the cooldown has expired.

This is primarily just a fun idea :p . Practically there are many reasons that this ability will not see widespread use in normal exping and it's doubtful anyone would consider it appropriate on raids :) But for general coolness factor when it all goes wrong i think this is worthy of inclusion :D



Warrior Ability : Surge of Adrenalin

Adrenalin surging the the warrior ups the pressure on his attackers defenses.

Short term Haste (40% at lvl 65 for 5 mins with a 10 minute reuse), stacks with item haste but not spell, song haste (except Battlecry/Warcry lines). Stacks with all other warrior abilities except group haste (see below - Timers linked).

Minor DPS advantage gained when no other haste casting class is available. Adds a little flexibility for minor soloing etc.



Warrior Ability : Call to Arms

The warrior encourages his allies to greater efforts.

Short term group haste (20% for 5 mins with a 10 minute reuse), stacks with item haste but not spell or song haste (Except Battlecry/Warcry lines). Stacks with all other warrior abilities except personal haste (see above - Timers linked).

Minor boost to all group members in the absense of a haste caster.



Other tanks : Paladins

Paladins in a non-efficency based group are very well equipped at this time as they stand, however they cannot at this time support an entire group in an exp situation. To address this I think it would be acceptable to extend the CH spell to the Defender/Healer class. Perhaps limit it to the druid incomplete heal if the clerics scream over loud. Druids themselves stack in too many roles for me to consider this a nerf of their grouping capability and the new spells in pop available to clerics reduce the importance of this Level 39 spell to their own attractiveness.

It hardly affects soloing at all since the likelyhood of getting a 10 second cast off uninterrupted in melee is particularly unlikely and in any case when a bard 12 levels lower than myself can kill multiple (blue to them green to me) mobs in a fraction of the time i can kill them with a fraction of the risk i think worrying about soloing is pretty dumb :p .

Paladins could perhaps gain other minor perks to aid them in specialised situations (AoE Undead nukes etc) to provide a little more flavour.



Other Tanks: Shadowknights

I really know too little about Shadow knights to make suggestions concerning them. Although one option did come to mind when visualising the all tank group...


Spell Line : Waking Nightmare

The target is trapped in a waking nightmare, unable to react to the outside world until such time as it fights clear of the nightmare or the trance is broken by external stimuli (getting hit).

A short term (45 second ? ) moderate mana cost Mez which can be used in emergencies for crowd control. Would require heavy maintenance costs in terms of concentration and mana to hold mobs for a long period.






There are many other ways in which we could allow warriors to tweak group performance via special abilities that should probably be explored. I've primarily concentrated on filling gaps left by lack of another class but it should also be possible to provide options to mildly augment a group that already has the key classes.

The concept i kept in mind when envisioning those above abilities themselves was that they should not be more efficient than a specialised classes ability in that area, that they should not take the balance too far wide of whatever SoE's current idea's of balance should be (i.e. all abilities cannot be applied for permanenently and most of the powerful ones require the warrior to be operating in a support role with another tank taking the hits).

In some ways the psychological impact of these abilities and the fact that they are activable and are thus noticeable to other party members is more important than their ultimate effectiveness. When a warrior activates an ability he is making an active contribution to the group, not simply hitting attack (not many people bring a parser to an exp group) and the group can see him making that action - the sense of making a contribution of that shouldn't be underestimated.

These idea's should not be considered a complete solution, i think this type of generalisation alongside some of the other idea's put forward would provide a greatly needed freedom from the one shot role currently 'enjoyed' by plate tanks in general and Warriors in particular.

Given that warriors would still be the most group dependant class ( I can't really see a rationalisation that allows warriors to heal themselves or solo effectively since they're all about being in the mobs face) something would still need to be done to offset the current knightly advantage (yes i still want rid of aggro lock :p ). Not least because the trivialisation of mob control would make the idea of these warrior abilities less likely to fly - but i guess i could live with playing second fiddle so long as I now had some meaningful role outside the MT spot.......
