A lot of people are afraid to venture through Vxed and Tipt, and it's a huge damned shame.

The first time I went through Vxed and Tipt, I had less of everything that the popular opinions here dictated I should have. I was around 7.8khp, 1500ish AC, and like 45aas. I didn't even have LR5 yet, I was hovering somewhere around LR3.

The real challenge of Tipt and Vxed is that you need to have people that you know and trust to back you up. I tanked both Vxed and Tipt, with those subpar stats, straight through Heartstriker Jhiru. While I did rely heavily on the DPS of the charmed pet, I was the one tanking those beatass mobs, and I didn't do a poor job of it.

GoD is the first expansion that begins to wean clerics off their CH at 65% mentality. Damage in GoD is far too streaky to rely on the ten second cast time, and even now when I go back there with 9khp and 1800ac, I still can't take CHs on those mobs. Sometimes I'll get wacked for 4k in straight melee + whatever I take from procs, and sometimes I'll get missed 4 times straight.

Having said that, I would personally encourage any tank to try these trials if they can bring a group of people that are used to playing with them. If the Cleric has a feel for your HP, and the Enchanter is skilled, there's honestly no reason to fail these trials if you persist. Plus, they have some kicking upgrades you may want to look for, particularly greaves and earring off charoov the trickster, hp augments off enraged maternal cragbeast, and bracer of the heartstriker off jhiru, if you're lucky enough to see it drop and beat out the cleric on the roll heh.

A few things anybody would want to note before seriously tackling these trials:

Enchanter pets are gods here. However, don't let your Enchanter charm a stonemite; they go into summoning mode below 96% health, and that's super bad for recharms. Pookas and cragbeasts are ok, ravagers are actually a LOT faster (runspeed 5 at least) but dish out more DPS: snare is a must for any charming here. Make sure your enchanter is timing charm, don't pull if it's getting close to 7 minutes and 30 seconds on charm (mandatory charm break at that time, CoD won't last longer than that ever.) Do NOT let the pets tank, most mobs here proc a snare that is very short and will overwrite druid/ranger snare, thus creating an issue on unexpected charm breaks.

Build your group as well balanced as you can. If your enchanter is charming, don't let him slow, bring a Shaman along. Have a class for snaring, and if possible a seperate class for pulling. If possible, make it a monk, necro, shadow knight, or bard for pulling - in that order, monks are best here, then necros, shadow knights and then bards. Lots of stuff can't be lulled here.

Avoid fighting in the narrow mountain passes. Try to pull as many mobs as you possibly can to a roomy area before proceeding. If you find it necessary to move up into the passes to fight, try to fight at a bend, not where the trail is straight.

Ikaav mitigate melee damage heavily, and reflect themselves midway through the fight. Ikaav and Hexxd type mobs spawn pets upon aggro (Call of Chaos is the spell script you will see before the mobs spawn), but poof after 12 seconds. Note: pets that are spawned before you aggro, will not poof. Those are real pets.

No CHs here, not even with a secondary healer. Spam heals all the way, unless you're remotely close to 10khp. Then CHs but you'll still need a spam healer. Shaman, Paladins, and Druids work well for this purpose.

Mobs run here! Make sure they get snared. The last thing you want is a runspeed 5 ravager finding a few of his high DPS friends to come back and splat you.


Don't let popular opinion dictate how you can play the game. A good group setup and skill account for much more than the stats on your inventory window in these GoD trials. When I did the trials, it was supposedly suicide for me to even try them.

That being said, I fully agree - these are not places for pickup groups. I wouldn't even try them with people I don't know. Remember, they are progression content, they're not to be taken lightly.
