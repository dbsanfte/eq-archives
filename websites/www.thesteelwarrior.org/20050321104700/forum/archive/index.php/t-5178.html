I played the snot outa FFXI (in beta and release).

IMHO the biggest detractor for me is the lack of English Speaking specific servers, coupled with an overly restrictive grouping system and well - after 2 month's FFXI accounts closed and removed from the hard drive.

Let me expound...

1) Lack of English Speakers
While I don't have any issues with people of foriegn nationality or other languages, basic communication barriers suck ass. You play on the same servers as the Japanese do. All of these servers have large Japanese populations of high level characters, so there is not the "race" to level up as a new server opens (which can be a fun aspect of a new MMO IMHO)

2) Overly restrictive grouping system.
While you will solo (with about any class / race combo - some better than others but) to about lvl 10 as standard, once you start grouping, especially in the mid teens and getting progressivly more restrictive as you go up in levels, you will be forced to group with people within 1 or 2 levels of you for the "best exp" (and why get anything but?)

On top of that, there is the "holy trinity" aspect of what classes you have to make an ideal group, and well, it just makes forming a group (of which you probably won't be able to talk to most of the people you would like to invite) a big pain in the ass

3) Lack of end game
I've talked at length with very experienced english speakers who had played the game, and once you hit max level, you basically hunt notorious monsters (like rare spawns) for loot, help others level, but there aren't much in the way of big raid type targets like the plethora of stuff to hit in EQ

4) Game looks sweet tho and has solid, non buggy systems
Just wish they had new servers for English speakers and a more open grouping system - skill chains, sub jobs, auction houses, lots of quests - game is cool but ultimately didn't hold me.

EQ: You know and love (or not love) it.

DAoC: Nice option. I beta-tested and then played about 6 months into the release, finally coming back to EQ because most of my guild came back as well, and the world was pretty small compared to EQ. Also, Realm-vs-Realm (their version of PvP) wasn't my cup of tea. Things have improved since, I hear, with two expansions and some special ruleset servers, including an all-blue server that allows you to travel to any and all realms to do adventure in. (I am, by nature and play style, an explorer, not a killer, so I prefer blue servers where I can travel the zones extensively.)

EQ2: Looks like a really different beast. I'm following its development, and this is the one I'm likely to end up in, methinks.

SWG: I was in Beta and thought it sucked big time, then they just up and released it, and the peeps who were not in Beta seemed to generally enjoy it. Some of the peeps in Beta who made the transition wondered why we weren't playtesting what was released, because it was apparently better. Although it draws people in, it seems to have a more limited lifespan than EQ, with many of the peeps I know having already tried and cancelled it after a few months, as opposed to many of them being in their fourth and fifth years of playing EQ.

Anarchy Online: Sucked at release, much better now from what I hear, though I didn't give it much of a chance and cancelled after just one month (this was at the time of retail release).

City of Heroes: Just entered Beta and not a lot of info, but I find the lack of "I don't care about the NDA, this game sux!" posts encouraging. Very genre-specific, though...you gotta like playing superheroes in colorful tights.

WoW: I am in that apparent minority who could care less about Warcraft and its ilk. As such, I have no particular desire to play this and will reserve any judgment on its qualities until those who have played it extensively in Beta pipe up and give us their feelings.

FFXI: Played it for a couple of weeks, even bought a gamepad to play it because the mouse and the interface are quirky at best. Very pretty to look at, but not a whole lot there is you're used to what EQ offers. Cancelled it after two months, but only because I didn't get to it in time to cancel before I was charged for the second month.

Planetside: SOE's MMOFPS is pretty dead ("I'm not dead yet!" "Yes you are, you're only kidding yourself!"), but I still log into it maybe once a week to see what's up and to get myself shot up six ways to Sunday. Finding a large battle outside of prime time is, at best, a challenge, and since the only things you can kill are other players (no NPC's to shoot at...hint, hint, SOE, it would make it more fun if I could hunt down giant lizards when there's no one to shoot at), it can get pretty boring unless you play at primetime exclusively.

Eve, Earth and Beyond, Horizons, AC2: Don't bother...some of these may not even be up anymore. Horizons is brand-spanking new, actually, but I was not impressed with it in Beta, and apparently not many are impressed with it at release. I always thought of it as MMORPG-Lite. I will say this for AC2, which I played for 4 months after release: It was a beautiful world to explore, graphically; there just wasn't a heck of a lot to do in it and ultimately that doomed it. I hear it is still online, with just one server.

YMMV, of course; these are just my observations.
