Brabble
The answer to your question: "Would I be a detriment as a tank at that level?" is no, you can tank just fine at level 32 with what you've got on now. Will you mitigate damage as well as a twink in full cultural? No, but then you don't have to, either. If you've been paying even casual attention to what tanks do as you leveled your shaman, then you'll be a better tank than most 32 warriors who have never fought anywhere except in the chaos of PC and HHK, never ask healers about mana levels, never pay attention to LoM messages, can't time spawn rates, have no idea how to position mobs, fail to notice that two mobs are beating on the cleric, don't have any idea what /shield does, etc etc.
Right now, your armor is fine and you can get by with it for a few levels if you have to; In 10 levels you'll need to boost your AC.
In 15 levels you'll really need to spend some plat on significant upgrades (to cultural armor levels). At that point, upgrades get much more expensive.
If you don't have boatloads of pp now, you might consider saving now for a bigger upgrade later, rather than marginal upgrades now.

I'd look in library at the haste item post and see if your shaman would farm something--he could solo for several of the minor items, I would think. At level 32 your warrior doesn't need it, but around the low 40s it wouldn't hurt to have some. And a significant haste item (let's say 31% or better) can be expensive, and is something you'll also need to be saving for (or make it a goal of your warrior to raid Vox/Naggy when he gets high enough, then hope you win the roll).
I'd get a bow and some arrows (doesn't take long to fletch arrows suitable for tagging, so I'd do your own).
Halas 10-pound meat pies would boost your dex over 100 and add hitpoints and STA. Very cheap.
