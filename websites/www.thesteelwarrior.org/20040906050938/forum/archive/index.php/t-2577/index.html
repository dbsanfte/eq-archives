Principle interests taken from Raaj's poll:
Improve Taunt, upgrade HP/AC/Mitigation/Avoidance, Aggro DoT, Disciplines, +Hate, New Skills, Itemization, Specialisation.

Each interest fits under 3 Main Categories:

Skills - Disciplines - Warrior Advancement

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Explanation of Each Category

Skills - Add New Damage/Hate, Defensive or Utility type skills
- Upgrade Old Skills (Kick, Taunt, Disarm etc.. )
- Make a New Skill system (skills that take account of stamina, stats etc...)
- Advance the warrior's damage/defensive capability through new passive skills.

Disciplines - Add New Disciplines at certain levels, or in the form of "quests".
- Upgrade the Old Disciplines, changing duration and reuse times.
- Upgrade the Discipline Mechanics, changing way timers are linked etc...
- Add new forms of Disciplines like Auras or Stances etc...

Advancement - Heighten the reward Warriors get from Items. (hp/ac bonuses, ways to use mana etc...)
- Upgrade the equipment with Melee Focuses, Effects or New sorts of Effects etc....
- Make new Items Warriors get through quests and drops.
- Advance the Damage Tables.
- Upgrade the Warrior's Status(Mitigation, HP, AC, STA etc...)
- Bring in New caps to skills (Taunt, Kick, Parry etc...) as well as Specialisations.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is what we will do:

Post your ideas that concern each of these categories, and ONLY your ideas. Don't start discussions here, start new threads in the War Room if you want to do so.

The lists will be links to the posts and threads that have details/discussions on it.
At this stage, it doesnt matter if people agree or not with ideas. If you don't agree with any ideas in a certain Category, then don't vote for that Category in the futur poll :)

During a few weeks, everyone will have time to read the ideas that concern each of the three Categories. This way, everyone has a pretty good idea of what the Category stands for.

Once enough days have passed, I will make a poll, and everyone will then choose ONE Category.
The Category with the highest vote, will become the center of interest.

Once we got our Center of Interest (possibly make a new Board specially for whatever that interest will be),
we can start the deep discussons, spring out new ideas, ditch unacceptable ones and try to avoid the unavoidable flames ;).
We'll work that big mass of muddy ideas together, polish them out a bit, and sculpt something we'll all be proud of.
Then we just need to submit our piece of art to the Devs, with a whole community backing up the idea, they'll hopefully listen.
I would also suggest to submit the idea to a couple of other communites, and see what they think of it.

So for now:
post ideas, detailed or not. Bearing in mind that there will be no discussions about it on this thread.

Posted this in a discussion on my guild boards, but wanted to lob it over and see what you guys think of it as well:

Balancing tanks is extremely hard. They are just as unwanted in multiples in a group as enchanters and clerics, yet, there are three classes that fill the role as "tank" instead of just one in the other parts of the "trinity" (tank, cleric, enchanter, for the non-old-schoolers). This has been rectified some by making druids and sometimes shamans able to do the cleric's job, and bards and sometimes shamans able to do the enchanters job, but there is still more competition for the tank position.

Warriors today are no doubt underpowered. I will gladly turn down a 65/0 warrior in favor of a 60 knight for a LDoN, and will take a 65/0 knight over the 65/100 war for PoP xp. Yet, I have been along long enough to remember when paladins and sk's were nearly useless as tanks. In the old days, I as an enchanter tanked better than a paladin, using my color stuns. No joke.

If we buff warriors too much, knights will very quickly go back to being a third rate option. If we buff warriors too little, they will remain subuptimal as they are now.

What is important when discussing tank balance, is to be clear on what we actually want. Do we want warriors to be the superior tank in every situation like they used to? Warriors might, but most of us don't. I honestly think it is fine the way it is now, that knights are preferred for xp groups, but warriors are preferred for raid kills. Although of course, that has to be with equal characters, not the way it is now that I prefer a knight five levels lower for some things. Warriors are still by far the preferred raid tank, but paladins and sks have been catching up big because they get as much hp from gear as warriors do, and the innate warriors bonuses drown in the huge amount of raw hp gear. The percentile difference between warriors and knight hp today is ridiculously low.

What I want to see:

1. Warriors need a way to build aggro FAST. With a good Paladin or SK tank (or even ranger:)) I can start casting slow before the mob gets to the group, and never get aggro. The cleric will save nearly a whole CH on this, more than making up for the lower hp. She also won't be forced to patch heal me, drawing even more mana. Warriors have to get better at this, although they should probably never be quite as good as knights. Being able to slow before 10 seconds have passed would be cool though.
Suggestion: A 2 minute timer, 5 second duration discipline that turns the warrior bash skill into an auto-hit, 1 second planar stun, 500 (random number, might be completely wrong) aggro bash. Will reintroduce the shield to the game, which would be cool. It would also require skill in the form of ghetto bashing and timing to make sure you hit. Things that reward player skill = good.

2. Warriors need a SLIGHT improvement in keeping aggro once they have it. This is far less critical than point 1, as most well geared warriors are good enough here imo, as long as the other players in the group know their job. Poorly geared warriors don't have much here though.
Suggestion: The hate shield sounds interesting, particularly in combination with the above skill. This will let poorly geared, probably less skilled warriors use both skills and gain the benefits, without having to do semiadvanced tricks like ghettobashing. I want the hate shield to be less efficient than for instance a BoC/RZtW sword combo though, as warriors with strong aggro weapons do well enough here already, and we want to avoid turning all those weapons into useless junk. Point 4 below MAY remove the need for this improvement.
EDIT: The "hate shield" was an idea from the guild thread (and probably here) where the warrior would get an aggro bonus for each time the mob hit him, much like a damage shield except with aggro instead. This would only apply if the warrior was holding a shield.

3. Warriors need a bit more hp. The difference between warriors and knights here has shrunk quite considerably. We don't want to give them too much of an advantage either, but a small boost is indeed needed.
Suggestion: No idea. Probably a certain percentage additional increase to the raw hp recieved from items. This requires LOADS of testing and rebalancing though. And if warriors get a 20pct increase that gets lowered to 10pct a week later because it was overpowered, you know they will be screaming NERF!

4. Warriors need a bit more damage. They are currently outdamaged by knights, which seems just unfair, seeing as knights are better at utility. It didn't use to be like this.
Suggestion: The last few years, two handers have nearly catched up to dual wield in efficiency. Both itemization and the damage bonus changes are the cause of this. I think a simple change exists that may be enough, and almost guaranteed won't be too much, so it can be implemented in the next patch without much to worry about: Apply the damage bonus to the offhand as well. This will help bring dual wield closer to it's former glory, helping the struggling rogues and monks (and non-archery rangers) as well. This is in my opinion the first, riskfree step towards pure melee balancing. After this hopefully gets implemented, we can take another look at warrior damage and see how it stacks up to knights.

5. Reduce stun aggro slightly. It is just insane. I love it as an enchanter, but it has to be downtuned a bit to bring it more in line with SK aggro.

Hopefully some combination of the above will bring us to a world where:
- Warriors tank raid encounters significantly better than knights. Heck, I don't mind if they are a must-have class for the main boss. Knights can be offtanks.
- Knights are slightly more wanted as maintank for xp groups than warriors, but a warrior superior in level and equipment will be more wanted than the inferior knight.
- Warriors deal slightly more damage than knights, making them attractive in multiples. Knights will still be at about the same level of attractiveness because of their utility.
- Paladins and SKs are two sides of the same coin, equally useful but still different.
- Ghettobashing is back ;)

P.S. Ghettobashing, for the new unaware players, is a technique where you switch in a shield in your offhand to bash, then quickly switch your weapon back in. It was frequently used by small race tanks of all levels before kick all but replaced bash from level 55+.
