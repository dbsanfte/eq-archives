Well, we've all had our experiences with LDON now, and where as on a whole I tend to like the expansion, I beleive it is going to make matters worse for the plight of the warrior.

1) Weapon Augmentations are nice....but all of the good aggro producing ones(stuns, snares, slows, Hate procs) are all usable by any classs so I beleive this will do very little to help in this aggro area since many of the same procs are good Direct Damage procs anyway and will be used by most classes for increased DPS.....

2) While we concentrate our points we earn on augmentation our weapons with slows, stuns, hate procs, as well as DD in order to try to increase our aggro generation. Paly's and SK's, who worry not about aggro spend their points augmenting thier armor with HP augments and AC augments.....further closing the gap in these areas.

3) A couple things I have noticed in my experiences in LDON that could make matters worse for warriors going LFG for hours on end once everything gets settled down and people start to become choosey about their groups:
a)The only mobs that tend to need Defensive is the "Boss" Mobs on the seek and destroy missions.
b)Since it is hard to tell what situations are gonna arise in these type of dungeons, The more flexible your character class, the more multi-facetted your char's abilities are.....the more desired it is. So a class that can FD, Tank very well, Snare, Dot....hold a mobs aggro like glue WILL ALWAYS be desired over one that can...well just tank. Because of this I see the problems we first start seeing in PoP becoming even MORE Widespread in LDON.

Overall I like LDON.....but the above things I mentioned will only make problems worse for the warrior....hopefully making the Hybrid classes non tanking abilites more useful(as LDON DEFINITELY DOES) is SOE's first step to bringing everyones tanking abilites back into balance....we'll have to wait and see.

Warning, hehe Paladin perspective here:

Aggro control is a bit more difficult in general in LDoN, but since the mobs don't hit so hard there isn't as big a penalty for losing aggro. There is a thread over at Paladins of Norrath where folks are asking if Stuns are less effective in LDoN than elsewhere. I don't buy it, but some Paladins do.

Knights are the 'preferred' tank in PoP, and groups are seemingly punished for taking a Warrior as tank. In LDoN, Knights may still be considered the preferred tank, but groups will not be 'punihsed' by taking the Warrior. In this respect LDoN is an improvement for Warriors over PoP.

I have to agree that the fact that Warrior abilities do not use mana is a help. All casting classes have to watch mana use in LDoN. There is pressure to kill mobs as fast as possible, so people tend to cast more. This leads to rationing of mana after the first few encounters. Warriors do not have to worry about rationing except for Defensive, and that should only be necessary for 'boss' mobs on some encounters (like the one that rubbed me into a greasy spot on one adventrure.. SPLAT!).

In the adventures I have been on, off-tanking has been important. There has been a Warrior on 2 of my 4 adventures, and they were vital to the groups' success. One of the times I was MT, the other the Warrior was MT. I preferred the Warrior being MT, as it left me more free to help with CC by root-parking and off-tanking. In the groups where there was no Warrior, a Warrior could have taken my place and the group would have succeeded just as well as with me there.

LDoN does not address the overall class balance issues that have been well documented. It does provide a situation where groups should not feel encumbered by taking a Warrior though.
