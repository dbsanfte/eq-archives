1. Being Iksar has not made me less of a warrior than any other race. I still have comparable AC to plate warriors, 1047 unbuffed at lvl 55, which is not bad considering I am not twinked and earned everything I own. The only place being Iksar is a problem is lower levels where your gear is not as good as plate warriors.

2. Later in the game, post lvl 46, the world opens up to you for armor. PoH armor is great, Legionnaire Scale is better than Indicolite IMO. Stats are more in line with what we need. The AC is a bit less but you make up for it with your natural AC bonus. You don't like PoH, try PoG armor, Iksar and plate war useable. You both get the same AC from the armor, THEN you add on your bonus. Same goes for the rest of the Velious armor. The plate restriction appears again in Luclin as UP armor is not useable if I am thinking correctly. With diety armor now, that is also a restriction, being Cazic worshiping only. Granted FH is really nice to have, but just remember what people did before it came out. That is what you would have to deal with. Yes there is Cazic armor, but not worth the time or money to get it. Pretty much anything else is fair game for armor.

3. Finding armor in the beginnig is a bit difficult, which just means you need to be smarter. You don't get the easy way out by getting crusty or cobalt like most people go for, or is it FH now, been to long since I was in this situation. The Trooper Scale Armor quests will do just fine. They are not to difficult and fairly decent. My suit of it lasted me till I finally got some hate drops.

4. Exp penalty is the same as a Troll i beleive. Not to sure as I never even knew there was a penalty when I first created my war and never cared once I found out about it. Exp moves fine through the lower lvls and continues going fine for me in the higher lvls. But don't let the penalty hold you back from making the choice to be Iksar. The rewards are great once you get to the higher lvls. Of course if you are going to be in elemental armor you are pretty much the same as everybody else, only you have higher regen. If you're not going to be a power gamer, then you will be ahead of the rest of the non power gamers.

Plus, you look badass, that is enough reason to be Iksar alone.

1. No, being an iksar does not hurt tanking in any way. You can shrink for positioning if you choose to, people can usually spot you in a crowd, etc...

2. It will matter to you in the later game if you do not:

A) Join a raiding guild that can do High-Ssra level stuff or better.

B) Play LDoN at all, or just enough to barely get armor.

3. Your armor in the beginning is not that bad. It is not that easy, however, to upgrade from there until level 46ish. You get the trooper scale at first, then your next upgrades will be Legionnaire or Thurg.

(if you can get lizard on Thurg faction, you can MQ a full set for them that will take you up until your 50's and 60's)

A couple hours in the Platehouse in Kael with your lizard taking faction and looting stuff for MQ will do wonders for you.

4. Your penalty is real. It hurts. It is decreased by being a warrior. Don't trust anyone to tell you exact percentages, as they cannot truly be trusted.

IMPORTANT!!! The only person who will be hurting as an iksar warrior (happens to be me HA HA)

Someone who loves to tank higher stuff but doesnt want a raiding guild.

Someone who cannot get access to tons of Platinum to buy ornate, etc...

Someone who cannot devote tons of time to playing for AA's, etc...

LDoN has normalized play for level 65 iksars who just dont want to join raiding guilds (IF THEY PLAY A LOT)

P.S. one of my biggest gripes about trying to stay up with the joneses as a casual player trying to keep my lizard in front of the big mobs was cultural armor.

This has been somewhat negated by LDoN armor. Just realize that you will have to get to level 65 and be able to crank out the points to get it :)

LDoN Miragul's 760 armor is comparable or better than Fierce Heraldic. So one or two adventures a day for 10 days will net you a new piece at level 65. This is a good thing.
