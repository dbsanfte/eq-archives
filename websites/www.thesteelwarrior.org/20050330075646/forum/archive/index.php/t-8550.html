Avoidance adds a flat amount of equivalent HP to your tanking stats. For example if you've 10K HP and you get hit 49% instead of 50% due to avoidance, that's the same as adding 200 HP for all practical purposes. The effect of shielding, on the other hand, depends on what the mob hits for. You can estimate it by (# of hits you expect to get hit before you die) X DB X shielding %. But this number varies greatly depending on the mob. For example on the Aneuks in Txevu if you have say 13K HP you can expect to take 13 hits before you die, so the value of shielding is 13 X 1000 X 1% = 130 HP per shielding. Of course, the Anueks are a pretty extreme case. I'd put each point of shielding at about equivalent of 50 HP versus most ubers.

1% more hit avoided is worth between 200-280 HP (depending on what your max HP is) if using the assumption mob to hit = 50%. I think parse says 10 avoidance = 1% hits avoided so each avoidance would be about 20-24 HP. Of course, avoidance tends to come in larger #s than shielding.

I seem to recall the % avoided is constant under discs so that means the effect of avoidance under evasive is multiplied. If you assume you get hit 33% of the time in evasive, then you're looking at 1% avoided hits worth about 300-420 HPs, so 1 avoidance = 30-42 HPs.

Avoidance is almost guaranteed to be better in the long run versus shielding except against extreme cases (mobs with extremely high DB and low DI) but there just isn't that many good tank items with avoidance on them. You end up sacrificing other stats for avoidance, whereas there's almost no bad tank item with shielding. Thus my gear setup favors Shielding, but I'd certainly try to get avoidance if at all possible.

Someone check my math here but according to what I've seen avoidance is almost always superior to shielding.

Let's run some numbers here:

- I'm assuming that 10 avoidance = 1% more misses.
- to make the equations look simpler I'm defining x=1d20(DI)

10 avoidance = 0.01(DB + x)
1% shielding = 0.01DB

For 10 avoidance to equal 1% shielding you have 0.01(DB +x) = 0.01DB so x has to equal zero. So we know that 10 avoidance is always better than 1% shielding because for them to be equal, DI would have to be zero.

The most shielding I've ever seen on an item is 5%.

10 avoidance = 0.01(DB + x)
5% shielding = 0.05DB

For those to be equal, x must be 4 times DB so DB has to be 20% of the total damage. So any time a mob's DB is less than 20% of it's total damage 10 avoidance is superior to 5% shielding.

To me that makes it fairly obvious that avoidance is almost always superior to shielding.

Do your clerics heal you based on the mobs average damage? Answer is hell no or you will die a lot. They have to heal with spikes in mind not average. Thus in a lot of ways Shielding is superior to avoidance. Shielding is guarenteed to lower the total damage a spike can do. Avoidance only lowers the chance of a full spike but when it comes, and it will, you will take a harder spike than the shielding would. I would wager that 99% of you in GoD die only when a mob gets a spike past the spam H Lights.

Lets take a mob that has db of 500 and a DI of 125
If you got 25% shielding you are protecting yourself from 125 damaged per swing with the shielding. Having 50 avoidance would make him hit you less but when he did get a full quad on you, you would take 475 more than the shielding person in that quad even if all hits were for minimum or if they were for max. Basically in the end it comes down to living thru a bad spike when it comes cause no matter what your avoidance you will still get full quaded sometimes and those are the times you live or die based on your mitigation not your avoidance.

Overall I look at it like this.
Shielding is superior for mobs that have high enough spike damage that your clerics do not use c heal at all.
Avoidance is superior for fights with lower spike damage to the point that c heal is actually used as the standard heal instead of spam style lights.
