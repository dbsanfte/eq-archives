Nothing specifically targeted towards Warriors. However LDoN is a lot more flexible in terms of group composition than PoP is. Agro lock isn't as critical an issue, so even though warriors are still gimped, they're gimped in a friendler environment.

On second thought, one thing that is cool for Warriors in LDoN is there are a number of weapon proc augmentations that can be bought directly with adventure points and added to pretty much any weapon currently in the game. Since LDoN is supposed to allow multiple procs on a weapon, this could make some good agro weapons.

Unfortunately I think, from a game mechanics standpoint, classes are relatively balanced at this time (balanced in the sense of 'all classes are functional at all level ranges' not balanced in the sense of 'all classes are roughly equal in power and utility'). What's really hitting warriors hardest is player perception of their abilities (at least in exp groups). In a game like EQ where the game mechanics are hidden as much as possible from the player base, eye candy maneuvers have a disproportionate effect on player's perception of the class's viability. Paladin's Slay Undead ability is a good example of this. Even though intensive parsing has repeatedly shown that the ability gives a relatively negligible increase in DPS against undead, the eye candy effects of a 5K slay has given rise to the occasional rumor that its an unbalanced ability.

Better agro management is definately a good thing, but what Warriors really need is a couple of eye-candy maneuvers that provide a perceptable benefit without having to be parsed for 6 hours. Player perception is more of a hindrance than game mechanics.
