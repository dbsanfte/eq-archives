Wubao,

Resisted spells DO generate aggro, but it's not the same as if the spells land. There's one direct benefit to SK's getting a numerical hate spell, and that it that one can pretty clearly measure and parse aggro for the first time. It has been shown that aggro on resisted spells is numerically lower than that from landed spells even on non-hate based spells, but I think we can all be pretty clear that a spell which has as it's function numerical hate will generate less aggro resisted than one which lands. Granted, it'll produce some hate, just not at the same level.

For proof, I offer only the following, which admittedly is anecdotal: When CT was first revamped, the taunt skill was completely disabled in the zone, we had to totally rely on procs to generate aggro. The other thing is, that the lizards in that zone are almost 100% fire resist if not 100% FR. Since the red epic was a fire based spell, it was kind of a double whammy to warriors, who could neither land their epic proc, NOR taunt. I played around a lot with different procs to see what worked best among the weapons I'd had at the time, so I pulled out every proc weapon I had at the time, and found that Veldrak's Shortblade was #1, Gnoll Hide Lariat was #2, and Dirk of the Dain was #3.... lagging WAY WAY WAY behind was resisted red epic proc.
