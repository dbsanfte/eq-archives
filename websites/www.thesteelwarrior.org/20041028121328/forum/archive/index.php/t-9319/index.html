I have been contemplating how EQ is on its death bed for many days now. Think for a second, back a few years ago, heading to Seb to level where skill mattered and mudflation wasn't outrageous. Think back to when the game wasnt desensitized and 90 people were bartering in the EC tunnel. Think back to going on an early Nagafen raid and winning, or when an uber person was a sight you saw every couple weeks. Back when 100k was an outrageous amount of money. During these times there were no flagging, AA points, or armor dyes. Anyone could pick up the game on a mediocre connection and a 56k modem. I remember logging into the game to find over 30-40 people in Oasis and the same number in LoIO. I remember being deathly afraid of spectres, the Sarnak Chancellor, and sand giants. The neverending class debates in LoIO and a warrior in crafted and an FBSS was a hardass. During these times, the game retained its fun. It felt like a world full of wonder, intrigue and mystery. Back in these times, the game was fun.


Now, however, the game is simply about ready to meet its end. The Bazaar is worthless, easier, but just not as cool as the tunnel or NFP. Now, thousands of people have over a million pp and kunark class armor sucks. Think for a second that you just bought the game, had installed it, and were ready to get to the high levels to go after the "fun" content. Well, these days, no "noobs" are running around anymore. You may see a few people in the low level zones, but chances are theyre being PLed in their twink gear. If you wanted to reach the upper levels of Everquest today, you would have to ding level 70, get all your planar progression flags, obtain millions of plat, or do thousands of LDoNs. Then, you must work on AA, then GoD flags, then head to Omens. It is impossible for someone to start from scratch and catch up in Everquest today unless they play 8+ hours a day, and VERY FEW people have the time to do that. The amount of timesinks before people can have access to high level content anymore are just absolutely ridiculous. Just having to level to 70 to get there wouldnt be THAT bad, but people would then have to get flags, tons of AA, and gear to get in a good high level guild. This is just one of the ways the low level playerbase will be alienated and the game will eventually fade away with no influx of low level players.


The next point I would like to being up is mudflation. With each passing expansion, the effectiveness of itmes increases drastically. I mean, christ, berserkers have a 100/40 axe now. SoE just cant keep pumping out the shoddily constructed expansions with new and more "uber" items...before long people are going to be soloing velious and luclin raid mobs. This is absurd.


The last point I am going to bring up is the lack of imagination, detail, or creativity in today's zones. Think back to Karnor's Castle or the multilevel dungeons such as Sebilis or Chardok, in which the creativity used in creating them is unreal. Zones such as guk with some fun tricks like traps (pits, etc.) and excellent use of textures and creativity concerning mobs and the wars amongst them. There are very little places in everquest today that could've rivaled heading down to assassin in a risky mission to obtain yourself the much fabled FBSS item. I'm convinced this is why Sony did the "Fabled" events during its 5th anniversary, to be well received by the public. I can guarantee you if they revamped seb to an elemental type zone, people would flock to it simply because it is fun to play there. It seems challenging and mysterious and the mob diversity, cool camps, and extreme attention to detail are what made the "Verant-Era" of Everquest unique. I view SoE as the multimillion-dollar conglomerate who is attemping to squeeze every last bit of the money out of the game as possible with little regard to its customers. Sorry If I don't want to go to GoD only to fight strange alien monster-things over an over again in the same old bland zones. It's just not the same.


I know this is a long rant, and I could go on. However, I believe I have brought my point across. Today's Everquest is not the same game I played a few years ago...a game that was based on companionship, challenge, mystery, and fun. Today's Everquest is personified by the driven desire to become "uber" at all costs, elitist pricks, and unfun timesinks amidst unimaginative, buggy, and repetitive content. I am just hoping the Everquest 2 or World of Warcraft can renew that original EQ feeling of wonder and excitement that has been lost with the current phase of the game.
