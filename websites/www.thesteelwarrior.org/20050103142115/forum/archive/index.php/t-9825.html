Rage Efficiency Guide


PvE is all about rage efficiency, this guide is meant to help show which abilities are more efficient for solo and group play. Against tanks its useful info since the fights last long enough for efficiency to come into play. Against casters the higher damage the better in general so efficiency goes out the window. The skills that have their damage based off weapon damage have 4 data sets, this is to give you an idea on efficiency based on how much dmg you do per hit. One handed weapons will hit for 50's, high end 2hers closer to the 300 depending on mob AC and level etc. Also these numbers are based on lvl 60 skills.


Bread and Butter - Every bread and butter ability of the warrior needs to be revamped at least slightly. All of them except Rend fight against the concept of rage in one way or another.

Slam - weapon dmg + 87, 15 rage 1.5 cast. Slam has been changed so that the slam timer interrupts your current swing time so by using it you take at least 1.5 seconds longer to swing your weapon. This results in slowed down DPS from your normal attack and slowed down rage production making it MUCH weaker than these numbers indicate. The talent for this reduces cast time by 0.5 seconds for 5 pts and is pretty much a complete waste right now.


50 dmg - 137/15= 9.1 DPR
100 dmg - 187/15= 12.5 DPR
200 dmg - 287/15= 19.1 DPR
300 dmg - 387/15= 25.8 DPR


Mortal Strike - 2 times weapon dmg, 30 rage, 6 sec cooldown, reduces healing effectiveness by 50% for 10 seconds. One item of note with MS is that if you miss it takes all 30 rage, other warrior abilities only take 2 rage when missed.


50 dmg - 100/30= 3.3 DPR
100 dmg - 200/30= 6.7 DPR

300 dmg - 600/30= 20 DPR


Rend - 196 - 10 rage, 21 sec, cannot crit and isn't modified by AC. Rend is one of our best abilities but it doesn't scale well at all at level 60. As you get raid weapons its damage is going to look worse and worse as it never goes up, sort of like mage spells without +damage gear, same problem.

147/10=14.7 DPR
196/10=19.6 DPR with talents

Heroic Strike - 124, 15 rage next attack. HS makes your next attack generate no rage. The higher dmg the base attack does the more rage you lose out by using the ability. This makes it particularily weak with high damage weapons. With a raid level 2h weapon hamstring is more efficient than HS. When it crits it does do double bonus dmg but keep in mind a crit normally generates a lot of rage so its still not efficient. I gave 4 data sets to show the difference in rage cost based on weapon type, bigger dmg is more rage which is less efficiency.

124/15= 8.3 DPR, 124/12= 10.3 DPR with talents

Largescale PvP - Warriors don't really shine at this for the same reason other melee don't, when its 40 on 40 you end up sniping at each other from range, when a warrior charges into the fringe they get wasted by all the people hanging back. They are decent for the final push of a fight but generally it makes sense to just protect your casters.


1v1 PvP - Warriors dont' really shine here either. We have too few options for breaking root/snare, we don't have particularily good escapability and our burst DPS has been reduced a lot. We do good against other melee though Paladin can be rough and we are ok against fear classes since we have some fear removers but against anything with a root/snare its a frustrating battle. We also do ok against anyone we get the jump on though a well played mage is always a pain to deal with as a warrior.

5v5 PvP - This is where warrior shines, small scale group on group stuff. The battle is small enough someone can keep us CC free and our extra durability makes enough of a difference that people don't try to focus on us early in a fight so we get to do some sustained dps and we offer some nice utility with shield bash etc as well.


Warrior PvP Abilities

Tactical Mastery - Talent in arms tree, lets you keep some of your rage when switching stances. I consider rank 3 in this a must have for a pvp warrior as you will need to switch stances depending on what you are fighting and being able to switch stance to intercept is incredibly important when you are jumped while fighting.

Intercept - Zerk stance only, requires 15 rage, 30 sec cooldown charge that works in combat with a short stun to it. The big problem with this ability is you need 15 rage for it and you start with 0. That means either drinking a rage potion in which case you can't use any other potion during the fight or using blood rage which does 600 dmg and takes 10 seconds at level 60. I still use it but its not as strong as it would sound.

Charge - battle stance, 1 sec stun, generates rage, 15 sec cooldown This no longer works in combat, that means if any caster hits you with an instant you can't use it and are stuck with intercept. Its still useful in that as long as you are within charge range nobody can run away from you without a speed enhancer.

Berserker Rage - Zerk stance only, makes you immune to fear for 10 seconds, with 30 second cooldown. This is one of those abilities that make us decent vs fear classes.

Mortal Strike - 30 rage, 6 second cooldown. This is still our big burst DPS ability, since the majority of our other damage abilities need to be reworked its still your best bet for the damage warrior. Nice burst DPS but the 30 rage cost is VERY expensive. This also cuts down all heals on target by 50% for 10 seconds which is very useful in pvp.

Execute - 15+ rage. This is a finisher move that only works on people under 20% health, does very good damage though it will drain all of your rage.

Shield Bash - 10 rage, 12 second cooldown. Interrupts casting and prevents spells in that school from being cast for 6 seconds. With the imp shield bash talent this also silences for 3 seconds. You can macro a weapon swap in order to use this.

Overpower - 5 rage, does weapon dmg + small amount to target usable after they dodge. This is a rogue killer, especially if you have the talents to improve it. You can macro a weapon swap to a 2h in order to use this.

Disarm - 20 rage, 1 minute cool down. Disarm opponent for 10 seconds. This is nice as well, most people don't do weapon chains and this will cut a melees dmg down significantly for 10 seconds.

Intimidating Shout - 25 rage, 3 minute cooldown. AE fear for 8 seconds. Breaks when target is hit. This is the warriors best getaway ability and is useful in group on group as well. Using it does not turn off autoattack though, so turn that off before using it.

Hamstring - 10 rage, snare target for 15 seconds, maxes at 40% snare, 50% with talents. One of weaker snares in game but it gets the job done.

Piercing howl - 10 rage, talent in fury tree, AE snare for 6 seconds, not directional and longer range than hamstring which is nice.

Demorializing Shout - 10 rage, AE atk debuff, useful against melee classes.

Rend - 10 rage, relatively weak DOT but its still one of our most rage efficient abilities so I always use it. Will stop rogues from stealthing and stops anyone from doing a full bandage which is nice.

Retaliation, Recklessness, Shield wall - These are all 30 minute cooldown abilities that are great when you have them up to use it. Shield wall makes you take 75% less dmg from all attacks, recklessness makes you immune to fear and makes your attacks crit and retaliation makes you do a free attack back every time someone hits you in melee. They all share the same timer, so use one and they are all down for 30 minutes.

Sweeping Strike - 30 rage, 30 second cooldown. Talent in arms tree. This is our most rage efficient AE ability. It hits one extra target for each of your next 5 attacks.

Whirlwind - 25 rage, 10 second cooldown. This is the 2nd best AE ability, it hits 4 targets for weapon dmg which does decent AE dmg. Its pretty small range though and only usable in zerk stance which makes you take 20% extra dmg.

Concussion Blow - 15 rage, 45 second cooldown. Talent in defensive tree. 5 second stun, makes your next attack generate no rage so in reality it costs more than 15 rage.

Deathwish - 10 rage, 3 minute cooldown. Talent in fury tree. increase your dmg 20% and become immune to fear for 30 seconds, you also take 20% more dmg from attacks.


Items - You no longer can swap armor in combat so some of these aren't as useful as before.

Ornate Mithril Boots - Right click to remove an existing root and make you immune to root for 5 seconds, 1 minute cooldown I think.

Glimmering Mithril Insignia - 10 minute cooldown. Requires 225 blacksmithing. 30 second fear immunity.

Nifty Stopwatch - 30 minute cooldown. Increase movement speed by 40% for 10 seconds.

Free Action Potion - 2 minute cooldown, makes you immune to root/snare/stun for 30 seconds

Net-o-Matic - 210 Engineering req. 10 second root with 10 minute cooldown. Sometimes ends up rooting yourself instead. Still a nice item.

here is my experience by class including who I think holds the edge. - means the warrior holds edge, + means the class has the edge,++ means I think that class wins even if the warrior gets the jump on them and = means the fight is pretty even.


Druid++

Warrior just does not have the tools to kill a druid. If we get a snare they can change to travel form and outrun us, if we use a root stopper they do the same. They can kite us ad nausium. We just do not do the burst damage to be able to kill a well geared druid.

Mage++

Blink is greater than any of our abilities now. If I get the jump on a mage I stand a small chance as I can charge, they blink then I can intercept, they root and I use root breaker and I have 15ish seconds to kill them assuming they don't get a poly off on me, which they usually do. However that all leaves 0 rage for special moves and honestly a mage with shield up can last that long vs a warrior with 0 rage. If mage gets the jump on me I'm pretty much a sitting duck.

Paladin++

They do as much dmg as we do, they take hits as well as we do, they have 3 lives. Warrior does not have the burst dmg to take a paladin down anymore so we have to run them out of mana by always keeping MS on them so their heals are innefficient. It generally doesn't work though unless you have a gear advantage.

Shaman+

If I get charge off on shaman I stand a chance against them. Without charge I have to use intercept and their survivability and DPS is high enough that I'll end up losing the fight. They control the tempo of the fight with their movement abilities and instant snare.

Hunter=

I haven't done this fight enough to judge hunters but I think if I can get to melee range with them I stand a good shot at winning.

Priest=

We get a lot of anti fear tools which are nice but the priest can dispel most or maybe even all of them. MS makes his heals inefficient and we do have enough burst DPS to take down a priest so this is a fight that can go either way.

Rogue-

Warrior have a decent advantage against rogues but we can still lose this one, especially if they get the jump on us. A lot of it comes down to who gets more crits and how often the rogue dodges(the more the better for the warrior for overpower).

Warlock-

Anti-fear4thewin. Seduction is the one thing I really fear about with a warlock. I haven't fought vs the new pets so maybe its different now, don't know. If a warlock gets the jump on me they can still keep me chain feared and kill me.

So there you have it, 3 classes destroy warrior, 1 has an advantage, 2 are even and 2 have a disadvantage. To me they still fall at the bottem end of balance, most classes have an equal number of advantages and disadvantages
