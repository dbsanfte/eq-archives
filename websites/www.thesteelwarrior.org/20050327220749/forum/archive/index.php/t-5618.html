Although for some reason your Magelo link wasn't popping for me, having tanked Vindi and having all your defensive AAs (good wo/man!), I'm assuming you're around 7000 - 8500 raid buffed HP? Regardless, anywhere in that range and I totally agree that NToV would be a great place to go next. Frankly, I spent a great deal of time farming ToV (West ToV to be precise, we were on Kael faction) and long story short, it's really not worth the time investment for the outcome. That's not to say it should be avoided like the plague, but like you said, if your guild wants to raid rather than do one or two group things, it's really pointless to 'raid' West ToV/Halls of Testing. The drop to kill ratio is much too low, and chances are you'd see a great deal of rotting armor before anything else.

The other huge factor that most people forget, is farming ToV for armor does not increase your damage output in anyway. Technically, the longer you last the fight with better armor, the more damage you do, and I'll give into that. However, you won't see any weapons unless you kill Telkorenar or Gozzrem (and frankly they just drop weapons for your WAR, SHD, and PALs that are equivalent of 2500pp weapons in the bazaar, and I'd argue Gozz and Telk are harder than a majority of NToV dragons). Long story short, in my opinion the only people who'd REALLY benefit from farming ToV for armor (whether Halls of Testing or West ToV) would be your main tank(s) if it's upgrades to their current equipment, and clerics to maintain longer CH chains. Everyone else doesn't really gain much benefit save the BPs and Leggings to some extent, and their rarity is way up there.

That said, I'd strongly encourage you to do as you say, and head into NToV, ESPECIALLY if it's undercamped on your server. Take your winning force that went against Vindi, and do Ikatiar the Venom - He's really almost a duplicate of Vindi (though I don't recall him rampaging, but better assume he does just in case I forget - there's something about him that makes him slightly easier than Vindi, but it's been a long time and I don't recall exactly what it is. Either no rampage, tiny bit lesser max hit, less HP; something like that).

Anyway, he drops a very nice cloak, some mediocre armor (Ancient Wyvern Hide), and a so-so rog (and shd) dagger, among other things. Not the greatest loot, but he's just an intro to NToV and is good warm up for the raid. Makes sure everyone's on their toes and what not. As for the strategy, I'll leave it mostly to you, but he can be corner tanked right nearby his spawn (the stairs leading deeper into NToV) just like Vindi, without adds as long as you pacify/clear his room before you pull him, and watch for agro through the doors on the right where the mini-drakes are and on the left where a large group of wyverns are.

Anyway, depending how your Ikatiar fight goes and how much mana your clerics had left over, add another group of 6 if necessary, and go for Eashen of the Sky. He's just up those stairs near Ikatiar and to the left, and he can be pacify pulled out, or monk/sk/necro pulled, once all the mini-drakes are clear. I'd also suggest pulling him to the corner near the stairs where you fight Ikatiar, because you can easily hide casters/healers from his AEs while fightning nearby, and he can also be cornered so long as you're shrunk properly.

His loot is quite fantastic; I'd wager you'll be hard pressed to get a drop from him for many many kills that wouldn't be well used by someone. The big difference is he does 400 damage AEs during the fight, which will only hit your melees if you position correctly, but it will slowly drain them down. This is a good fight to practice longevity - make sure your melees know it's their job to keep themselves alive, by hoping out of the AE/fight when they hit 40% or whatever you designate (I suggest not much lower, because you don't want them to get low health agro), and to go the 'healing station' for a heal or two. If you have them, I suggest Shaman's heal over time spell, Quiescence, if you have one, otherwise Shm/Dru are good for healing melees as they need it. Also remember to get everyone regened before the fight as it helps a bit.

Then comes Aary, and he's probably the hardest part. The actual fight isn't really that hard, because he only hits a bit harder than the previous two mobs, and is slowable, but it's pulling him and 'locking him down' that is tough. You basically have to ensure you pull him in such a manner that his AE doesn't hit your casters (most importantly, clerics), or they'll drastically lose mana and unless you have one or two superior clerics with lots of FT, they won't be able to last the fight most likely. There's lots of methods to doing this, some better than others, but I will impart that his guards are Pacifiable by a 65 enc/clr, thus making the pull very trivial - it's just a matter of timing and positioning. Don't forget the small drakes spawn quite rapidly, so once he's in battle, you may have to designate people to offtank the small drakes as they repop on you - it won't be all at once, so just have offtanks grab them when they spawn, and have either all melees or all casters or a combination you deem necessary switch to killing the small drakes. There's very little chance you'll see two waves of small drakes during the fight.

As mentioned, since he's slowable, if you keep clerics/casters out of his AE at all times, you shouldn't have any problem keeping your main tank up with one or two clerics, even with minor FT/mana pools. You can also switch out tanks if necessary with AE taunt - using evasive, you can guarantee pretty much 9+ minutes of evasive tanking on him and still ensure he goes back on your Master tank after the second tank's evasive runs out (first tank starts with evasive, when it goes, second tank AE taunts and does evasive, when it goes, first tank AE taunts to get him back, and evasive will be nearly refreshed again on him.) Should be a significant portion of the fight.

Anyway, I could go on and on, and there's obviously tons of guides to NToV out there, but if you'd like anymore detailed info feel free to PM me here and I'll do what I can. I'd rather not blabber on too much (even though I always do).

Hope that helps,
