sounds like a combination of factors to me, several good points have been made about different factors that could contribute to your losing aggro consistently

1. Casters skill and aa-I played a necro and cleric and trust me SCS3 DOES matter, as does timing your cast. If you are consistently pulling aggro off a tank, you adjust on the spot, you address the issues with the tank later as they most likely arent instantly fixable (ie he knows how to do his job but its just not working for some reason).

2. Heals needed-With the recent changes to the hits in GoD, maybe you arent good enuff anymore. I had to face that fact about myself in GoD post sewers, you do have a few hundred more hp and about 150 more ac tho so i cant say thats it.
Might want to pick up Tactical Mastery3 to reduce riposte damage from 2hand weaps. 1100 a riposte makes spikey damage and eliminating some might allow cleric to heal a little differently and pull less aggro. Heal over times on inc help, but they can put cleric on ramp list early so be careful. Have you considered going evasive on some of the trash? might also help with need for spam heals that draw 2 tons of aggro.

3. Your offensive AA-ambi, Ferocity, flurry, WA5, punishing balde, etc are really useful for aggro. Increasing swings at a mob or increasing proc rates both help aggro. If you dont get a lot of AA, do what i have done so far and buy them but get them according to cost. Like start with the cheapest ones and work on all of them in order of increasing costs. Im up to the ones that cost 6aa each now. So far i have flurry1, ferocity3, punishing blade2, and wa2. I can tell i hold aggro better now than i did before, no doubt at all.

4. Your methods of maintaining aggro-seems generally agreed you are doing things right here. Arrow in mob on inc, taunt, incite etc

you cannot compare yourself to a pally for holding aggro, they have aggro procs on demand(stuns) and any knight can keep aggro off any warrior, at least until the mob kills them :P
half the aggro responsibility is on the tank, you must do all you can to get and keep aggro, but half is also on the others in the group to manage their aggro. never ever forget that

without being in the group and observing what happens firsthand, its hard to say really what is going wrong
