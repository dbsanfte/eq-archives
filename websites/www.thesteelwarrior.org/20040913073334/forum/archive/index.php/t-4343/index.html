Well, from my own observations playing both a warrior and wizard, I believe aggro/hate to work like this:

Nukes casted at any range give the same hate.

Some mobs are "stubborn." They will not charge the highest aggro person unless that person has a significantly higher amount of hate built up over the tank, if that person is out of melee range. So you could still be tanking the mob, yet not have the most hate. Some mobs just don't want to switch targets without a lot of provocation. (especialy time gods) Sometimes a caster can nuke a mob, and not be its target out of melee range, but the moment you enter its melee range, it'll switch to you. If you step back out again, he'll go back to the original tank.

This would explain the "initial aggro" people talk about. You don't get more hate for aggroing it first, its just the mob doesn't want to switch targets easily.

Conversely, I've noticed the opposite. Some mobs are "dumb." Sometimes you can nuke a mob, train it over someone who did absoluetely nothing to it, and the mob will attack that person instead of you, simply because he is in melee range and you are not. Nuking/aggroing the mob more before hand will make the mob continute to chase you instead of attacking the person you pull over.

This explains "undead" aggro. Those mobs are just mindless and attack the person closest to them rather than run to the one it hates most. Undead/animal mobs tend to be dumb.

There is some question whether if you are tanking, but don't have the most hate, if taunt will copy the hate of the person who does. This is REALLY hard to tell, but I tend to lean toward no. From my experience there seems to be a couple different variables in operation here.

First is The hate list is just a straight up list of all hate generated from spells and damage done by characters to a mob.

Next for some mobs there is the Rampage list... this is just the order in which mobs aggro on a person in chronological order.

Finally there is the aggro list. This is a list of targets that the NPC will actually attack, either by spells or melee. This list is the one that I believe is HIGHLY affected by range.

Anyone who says range has no affect on a mobs target is not being observant. Velious mobs are notorious for having a highly range affected aggro. get out of melee range and the mob wont hit or chase you, yet will keep summoning your ass, etc.. stuff like that happens a lot. Saying range doesnt affect aggro is like saying range doesnt affect the chance of spells landing, ask casters about mobs you need to cast on from melee range to land, they do exist. It is not all mobs, but there are many...

Here is my belief as to the way it works...

When you do a mob affecting action, you are placed on the hate list. this can be proximity aggro (the least amount of aggro possible), damage aggro, spell aggro. All of these add you to the hate list. The order you are added gives your rampage list number.

So now we have a large number of people on the hate list. How does the mob choose a target?

Well it could simply choose the target with the highest hate value and attack, thats the simplest method. So the target with the highest hate, could be:

a) Out of Melee Range
b) A pet
c) In melee range

Here is where some AI coding enters the equation, If (a) does the mob summon? and is the summon timer up? if so, I bet that the mob summons the target, else the mob will charge the target If (b) The mob checks to see the total number of targets of the list, It has been shown that pets will tank, only if there are limited PC's available to attack, so if there are more PC's on the hatelist than some number X, the mob ignores the pet, and goes to next target. Finally (c) If in melee range, the mob attacks the target.

So what if not the simplest method is used?

What if the aggro list is a modified hate list? Where the aggro value is equal to the current hate value, modified by range? So now the hate list has some additional fields. Range to target, and then a modified hate value. I am under the belief that aggro decays on mobs by range at a somewhat exponential rate. There is some critical distance where the hate value decreases dramatically. So now we have the same situation as above, except instead of using the pure hate numbers, we now have an actual aggro number, this number is the value of hate modified by the distance from the mob and some equation. This now leads as above to the same 3 situations above. So the target with the highest AGGRO, could be:

a) Out of Melee Range
b) A pet
c) In melee range

Where the same 3 actions as above can happen. Summon, chase, ignore, attack..

This also leads to some Mob AI programming. Does the mob summon? Is there a delay from when a target is on top of aggro list to when the mob summons or chases? Again I know different mobs are definately coded differently. Some mobs will break and run in a split second, others will stand and fight summoning transgressors.

Want a good example? General Rapeme in PoF. I have tanked him many a times. Not once has he ever run away from me. And I have continuous battle spam of him hitting me over and over, yet he does summon and kill ranger, wizards, necros all the time. If range had no bearing he would have run them down and attacked, instead, he keeps attacking me, until his summon timer up, summons the poor over aggro schmuck, kill him in a swing or two, then continues laying the smack down on me. Yet there are other mobs that will break off of the tank and run a hundred yards to get that silk panty wearing finger waggler.

Next there is Undead/Animal aggro, where the mobs will target the nearest target, pretty much regardless of actual hate or aggro. If you are closest to the mob you get hit. Intelligent undead usually dont use this they use above, mostly dumb skeletons etc. (taunt appears useless here)

Last there is rooted aggro. When a mob is rooted, mob will attack the closest target im melee range, while summoning the highest target on its aggro list. Taunt is useless here...

Want good proof that aggro decays by range? Go to BoT, go to deep, aggro the mobs at the telepad. then port to courtyard, then back to the foyer. No mobs come running... now slowly move towards the teleport pad.. at some range XXX that mob will 'remember' you and come running. this is the critical range for aggro. Another example, is 'out running aggro' if range has no bearing on aggro, how can this ever happen?

Depending upon how taunt works, Is it pure hate values or is it aggro values, there can be arguments for and against the wait-and-see method of taunting and the mash-button method of taunting. Some mobs one method may work best, some mobs a different method may work best. Is there any reason to believe that there is only one way of being a warrior that is best?
