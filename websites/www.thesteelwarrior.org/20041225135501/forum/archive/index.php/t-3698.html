Kind of an old post, but just thought I would add some things to it.

Definetly as others have said, keep your weapon skills maxed or at very least reasonable. You never know when the next hot new weapon that every other warrior on the server will be using will come out. You also don't know what type it will be - maybe it will be piercing.

Also, you need to understand warrior aggro, DPS, and utility (a big part of warrior utility is through weapon procs and clicky items).

I know some warriors who raid who don't understand the DPS and utility aspect of the class at all, and to be honest I find this very sad. The DPS part on raids can be especially annoying when you main tank a mob and your other warriors don't have a good non-aggro DPS two hander to use. Some use their aggro weapons and die, or end up turning off attack a lot, or rarely even attacking at all. IMO unless you tank 100% of the time part of your duty as a warrior is to have at least a decent non-aggro DPS weapon setup.

Regarding warrior DPS too, at least one warrior I've talked to had the following attitude "I don't do as much damage a rogue, so why do I care about DPS?" Well, if things are relatively even, rogue DPS is higher of course but it's not so high as to make warrior DPS insignificant. You also can't make a guild's melee setup consist of 1 warrior and 19 rogues. If the warrior is busy with work/school, you're screwed :P. So you need multiple warriors. This means there will be times where you have one warrior tanking and the others not tanking - they might as well do the best damage they can. If they can't get at least a decent DPS setup they're dead weight IMO.

Learn to use as many (all would impossible) of the tools and strategy available to the warrior class as possible.

Warriors, we aren't just turn auto attack on and mash the kick or slam key and taunt key when we lose aggro anymore...

Originally posted by Alacor
... I know that the 2HS gets some awesome damage bonus at higher levels, dependant on the weapon delay, I assume. Will Dual Wield with Double attack out damage a single 2HS weapon?


Actually, damage isn't the only thing to consider. How much aggro you generate is another factor. And at really high level areas, how much riposte damage you take can be a factor too.

In general, from what I've seen on my own parses and here, good two handers outdamage good one-handed combos. With augments, a quad-proc might come close, but two-handers still seem to have the edge.

For instance, during some of my LDoN missions I recently parsed out some things I own to compare damage. I got something like:
1) Narandi's Lance - 63dps + 6dps dot proc
2) Greatstaff of Thunder - 61dps + no damage stun proc
3) Augmented Delightful Orb of Forgotten Magic + Augmented Copper Hammer of Striking -
Combined 57dps + about 12dps from procs = 69 dps


So as you can see, both of my two handers outdamage my one-hander combo, not counting the procs. But when the procs are included the one-handers are doing more. Still, note that I could, if I wanted to spend the money, get a Windlance and probably do about 75dps, plus more if I added a weapon augment to it. My one-handers would have a tough time beating that. And the twohanders further get an edge once I max out things like punishing blade, double riposte, and flurry.

Two handers also have an advantage in that you take reduced riposte damage. This really isn't much of an issue until you hit the higher planes, though.


Aggro is another matter. Because stun and anger 3 procs generate so much aggro compared to weapon damage, quad-procing stun/hate combos are probably the way to go to get the most aggro. The damage you lose by using these weapons is probably outweighed by the massive aggro the procs generate.


Just some stuff to consider.
