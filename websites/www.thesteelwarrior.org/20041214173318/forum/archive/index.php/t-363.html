I don't think different mobs have different checks for aggro, I think it just depends on how much damage you have been doing, how many procs you are landing and what the rest of the group is doing at the time. Sometimes you are going to have bad streaks where you miss a lot while someone else is having a lucky streak and doing lots of DPS, you will likely lose aggro then. Sometimes you hit a lot and proc early and often, then you will be very unlikely to lose aggro. The best defense against this kind of inconsitancy is to raise your attack (via strength and maxing weapon skills), raise your dex (for more procs), and equip the best haste gear you can get your hands on.
As for the idea that dps does not contribute toward gaining/holding aggro, that is just false. I've played around with the evasive disc XP grinding in the planes, and I've found a very clear tendancy. It goes something like this-Mob comes in, I tag it and engage. I taunt and swing away hoping for an early proc to help hold aggro on the slow (I rarely have trouble holding aggro after the slow), once slow is on I put up evasive disc to save the cleric some mana. If there are no undisciplined casters dumping their mana onto the mob I have no problem holding aggro till the end of the fight. Evasive last for something like 10 minute, and this is where the trouble comes in. When the next mob comes in, evasive is still up. I engage the mob and do very little damage with virtually no chance of a proc. When the slow comes in, I have little aggro and the mob jumps. Takes me a while to gain aggro back because my DPS is so low, and my chances of a proc are small too. By the next fight evasive is usually down, now I can do some damage and maybe get an early proc. Now I am holding aggro again. Clearly DPS has a prominent roll in the gaining/keeping of aggro.
