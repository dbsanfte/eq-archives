Hussar
10-23-2003, 07:55 PM
You will always critically fail on a creature that already has you on it's hate list.

After reading that, I decided to run some tests. I had 2 chars, warrior and rogue, mob is a guard in EC. Warrior pulled with prox aggro then ducked for the remainder of the test, rogue meleed with no main hand (non weapon in main) and empty fist in offhand. After a few punches, rogue had aggro and ducked also. Warrior taunted until he had aggro, rogue hit again to grab aggro back (one hit, so he was a tiny bit higher) and ducked again for remainder. Warrior then proceeded to beg (0 skill for the entire first test). After 40 begs, mob was still on rogue, though at beg number 23, the guard did give an aggro message. I then did the exact same test to a scarab (just to do a test against a mob that you can't be successful on), and I had the same results, minus the aggro message. Though I did get skill raises up to 4 on the scarab.

My conclusion would be that the only critical failure on the first test was number 23, and the rest were just normal (non aggro) failures. Basically, 40 tries, at the beg skill level that is most likely to get you a critical failure, and the mob didn't even glance at me over a rogue who was only a tiny bit higher than me in aggro.
