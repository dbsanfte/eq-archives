There are three types of Velious armor for Warriors. Champion's, Myrmidon's, and Warlord's. Each armor requires a different faction in order to complete the quest involved. Champion's armor requires good faction with Thurgadin Dwarves, Myrmidon's requires good faction with Sky Shrine Dragons and Warlord's requires good faction with Kael Giants.

The quest drops for Champions drops in Kael, in the arena, around the bank, and at the plate house (or ice house as some call it). The ones for Myrmidon's drop in the Halls of Testing in the Temple of Veeshan and the ones for Warlord's drop in the West Wing of the Temple of Veeshan and uncommonly off the yard trash in the North Wing of the Temple of Veeshan.

The faction necessary for Champions is only high amiable iirc while for Myrmidon's and Warlord's ally is required. Since Kael Giants are on the exact opposite faction to both Sky Shrine Dragons and Thurgadin Dwarves, you pretty much will be KOS to Kael Giants to do either Thurgadin or Sky Shrine quests and KOS to either Sky Shrine Dragons and/or Thurgadin Dwarves to do Kael quests. Gaining ally faction with either the Dragons or the Giants is usually a pretty long involved process, not to be taken lightly.

Chances are you will start your quest armor collection with Thurg armor. It is the easiest to get and the faction is cake to get. Most people progress from dwarf faction to dragon faction since killing giants gets you both. This means you will upgrade from Champion's to Myrmidon's since it is the path of least resistance.

Once you have gotten to the point of killing dragons or when your guild decides to no longer be on a particular faction, you might want to replace some pieces of Myrmidon's with Warlord's. Doing the faction switch is not hard, but it is annoying and tedious.

Also, the cultural armor is a very good alternative in many slots. Unfortunately you are agnostic so you cant wear Fierce Heraldic, so you will be stuck wearing Brellium (dwarven made). While a little harder to find Brellium than FH, the price is usually a bt lower and the stats are still pretty good.

The best suit of armor will be a mixture of Myrmidon, Warlord, and Brellium in your case.

There are some other reasons to follow the progression as well as I envision it was intended by the Dev team. Going Thurg-->Skysrhine-->Kael is a time consuming endeavor but its difficult to go any other route (not impossible, just difficult).

Kael is easy to start in, mobs are relatively easy compared to the other two alternatives and you work on faction for Dwarves and Dragons at the same time. By the time you are tired of Kael, your guild will probably have some necessary upgrades for melee and casters to be able to survive in HoT (halls of testing, easiest wing of ToV). The Dragon faction you recieved up to this point allows you to walk past many mobs that would probably wipe out your groups without the armor you have gotten as well.

In HoT, people usually get their first dose of the AE reality that will forever play in most high level encounters from then on. This armor though IMO is the best you will get quest wise from Velious. Sure the Kael turn in armor is better for tanks but I can get 10 x the amount of casters to go to HoT for SS armor because it is better for them than Kael. Also, the Kael armor isn't that much better for tanks anyway.

Kael turn in armor usually happens as a result of farming NTOV/WTOV for named and the items they drop. Few guilds I would imagine farm it for the sole purpose of farming armor. One fortunate byproduct of farming WTOV/NTOV for named is that you gain Kromzek faction in the process (needed for the Kael turn in). Also, the yard trash mobs drop the armor like my plate BP. But most guilds wouldn't be able to farm NTOV/WTOV without having, the AC/HP/RESISTS/INT/WIS/MANA from the SS or Thurg armor.

The game has changed some since I was last in TOV, the cultural armor being the most significant IMO. This may have changed the game significantly as to the progression I am not sure. But, if I were wearing full cultural I probably wouldn't try to farm the quest armor though either.

Bottom line is, most guilds need some of the Thurgadin armor to survive HOT to get SS armor. Then they need the SS armor to survive getting the Kael armor (which is a smaller upgrade to melee than Thurg-->SS was). The faction issue also plays a role in it as well. Casters generally want SS armor. To get Kael armor for melee, you hurt your COV faction needed for SS armor. This caused some major issues in the guild I used to belong to.

If you check out my profile you will notice I wear one piece from each of the quest armors so I do have an understanding of what it takes to repair faction for them. Kael wasn't bad when I did it, took me a week or so of soloing in Skyshrine and some in GD for dwarf heads. I was KOS to Kromriff and Tormax but allied to Kromzek when doing turn in.

Sorry for my rambling but I hope some of it makes sense.

Hope this helps you out, Donnel.

I did Kael Arena with two groups initially. We had the following:

6 - Warriors - MT was 55 with frostbringer/wavecrasher and junk armor (cobalt at best, enameled black chestplate and the like). 2 warriors at 53 and the rest 50/51 tops.
2 druids - 1x54 and 1x52.
2 clerics - 1x52 and 1x54.
1 enc - level 55
1 monk - level 52

We had a decent time in there, killed fairly slowly but we did pretty well. Pulling two mobs would end up in a wipeout most likely, but we got a lot of singles as the mobs are setup like that in the arena (good spacing). I would say we cleared about 7 or 8 mobs in a cycle.

Now I do arena alone with
1 war - 56 with swiftblade of zek/shortblade of lightning, 22AAs adn full thurgadin armor
1 druid - level 55
1 enchanter - level 59
1 cleric - level 58
1 necro - level 57 (amazing damage output on her, the necro make a huge difference for me)
1 shaman - level 48 (casts on nothing, just there for buffing and sucking what little exp you get there)

I can now clear all single pullable mobs in the arena, so that would be ~10 mobs before respawns (just enough time). So, yes, the arena is easily doable with a single group. It works out best if another group camps the other side of the arena and you have a monk so you can pull/split the Kallis trio. This allows you to get another 2 mobs on the sides and if creative pull Temple area mobs (not for the faint of heart).

What to get for the intermediate levels? I would save your pennies and get PP to purchase some cobalt. The arms and boots are great items and you'll hang on to them for a bit. Technically, the cobalt arms are better than Thurgadin arms due to the shielding buff (AC wise). You could also shoot for some Hate raids and try to get armor that way.

Of course, being level 35, you're pretty low, hehe. Try hitting Temple of Droga for some fast experience, but you'll need a monk at that level plus a really really solid group. I think Scarlet Desert was some pretty good exp around that level too. Not to mention hitting Guk with a good group.

Good luck on your adventures,
