This is how our guild does Emp, others can elaborate or offer better:

1) Someone invis'd goes in and targets the 8 mobs not including Blood or Emp. Four are mezzed and Enchanters get those targets for mezzing when the fun starts; they hold that mezz the entire fight (roughly 30 minutes for us). Four are slowed, these require four offtanks to hold them the entire fight. You don't kill these mobs as they will respawn instantly, so they must just be tanked and not killed. No one else but invis'd person should be in the room or even in LoS yet of Emp.

2) Chanters and Lockdown group go in. (LD group is the four offtanks and two "insurance" backups.) Offtanks grab their mobs and drag them to one of the cubby holes on the sides of the room to keep them out of the way as best as possible, while the mezzed mobs stay where they are.

3) Once Lockdown is solid, everyone goes in and kills Blood. This is usually not a long fight. Once he's dead, you have about 5 1/2 minutes I believe until the Emp is even targetable. The mezzes and lockdown mobs must be maintained all through here.

4) Emp becomes targetable, kill the King Snake! Bane weapons are still a must as far as I know, but proc's still work so if your melee has no bane, have them whoop out the proc'ing weapons.

5) Once Emp is dead, you still maintain the mezzes and tanks, and Shissar Wraiths will spawn. These are what drop the rifts needed for your Vex Thal keys. You'll get a total of 40 rifts off all the wraiths.

6) Award loot, do a mass TL out, and you are done. Make sure offtanks know to hit Yes fast or it'll be summon corpse time for them.

Note: someone else would know better, I don't remember, but the names of the 8 mobs determine which ones are mezzable and which ones are slowable, making them easy to find and get the targets back to who needs them.

We wiped our first two times trying him; first time LD group got broken, LD mobs went rampant on casters, and second time our MT died because of slow heals. Once we got the pattern down and people started to actually keep attention on the fight, it wasn't hard.

When I see a bane weapon like dmg 6 bane: +7 ssra does that mean it's a 13 dmg weapon against ssra or a 7 dmg weapon against ssra and 6 dmg against normal mob

Anytime you see +bane damage, you add it to the base damage of the weapon. In this instance, Shissar bane are 6 damage, 7 shissar bane, and 20 delay. Against any non-shissar, this is a 6/20 weapon. Against a shissar (and Emperor), however, it is a 13/20 weapon and performs exactly the same against a shissar as, for instance a primal (another 13/20 weapon), would against any regular mob.

Secondly, any time you add +bane to a weapon, the entire value of the base damage + the bane damage is calculated per hit. So, for instance, say you had a 16 damage, 1 shissar bane, 19 delay weapon, that would work as a 17/19 weapon against Emperor (even despite the fact that 16 of that damage is "none bane", which Emperor is normally immune to). All it takes is +1 bane to make the entire weapon effective against that particular mob.
