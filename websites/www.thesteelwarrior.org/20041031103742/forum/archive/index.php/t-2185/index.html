wandor
You are asking 2 different things. By DW you will double the number of ripostes you take, but that still amounts to a 12% increase in TOTAL DPS taken.

A typical Tier 1/2 mob probably does around 150 DPS. If you switch to DW, you will see an increase of around 12% total DPS taken AND your riposte rate will about double (increase 125%).

If you separate mob damage and riposte damage, you can do the math to see what you end up with when switching from DW to 2h.

150 TOTAL DPS = mob damage dealt + riposte damage

Since the parsing show that riposte damage using 2h accounts for 10% of total damage taken:

150 TOTAL 2H DPS = 135 dps (Total mob damage) + 15 dps (total riposte damage)

By increasing total DPS taken by 12% by switching to DW:
NEW TOTAL DPS = 150 TOTAL 2H DPS + (12% * 150 TOTAL 2H DPS)
NEW TOTAL DPS = 150 TOTAL 2H DPS + 18 DPS increase
NEW TOTAL DPS = 168 DPS

By doubling the riposte rate by switching to DW:
NEW TOTAL DPS = 135 dps (Total mob damage) + 2.25*15 dps (total riposte damage)
NEW TOTAL DPS = 135 dps (Total mob damage) + 33.75 dps (total riposte damage)
NEW TOTAL DPS = 168.75 dps

As you can see, a 12% increase in DPS is going to be nearly equal to increasing the riposte rate 125%. At the level of damage an exp mod deals, this is largely insignificant. When you are dealing with a boss mob that does 1500 DPS the increase is much more pronounced.
