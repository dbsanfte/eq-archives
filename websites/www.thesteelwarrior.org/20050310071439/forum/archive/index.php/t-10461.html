Hmm. From the way you word your post I suspect you are hearing aboutthe desperate low numbers some people are claiming. Frankly, no, it's not worth it, not to the folks you'd be playing with.

If you want to see Time, then log on and guest with someone on a raid, enjoy it, then go back to whatever you're having fun with now.

Besides, if you've seen Earth, Fire, Air, Water and Drunder, you've seen 90% of the zone, from the architecture to the mobs to the ecology. Saryn and TT aren't that different from what they looked like in the PoP progression and they're too tall to see them easily anyway. Quarm's proportions are badly artistically rendered and he's WAY too big to see effectively. If fact, if you're a cleric you'll never see him anyway except on the pull, perhaps for a second or two.

Getting a cleric "twink" apped, guilded, geared, flagged, and expecting to have that person around long enough to justify the time and effort invested in him only to have him go "ok, I got to Time and I'm bored again, bye" is debilitating to folks, both the resources mentioned and their morale.

People need to raid because they enjoy the process as much as the goals. People who are too "goal-oriented" in games like EQ burn out.
