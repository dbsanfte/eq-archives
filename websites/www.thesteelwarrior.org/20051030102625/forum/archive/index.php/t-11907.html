1) a WoW warrior is more like an EQ bard. once you get used to the cacophony of juggling abilities, managing rage, and switching stances, you'll find that most encounters are fun, not overwhelmingly difficult. experience helps. also, end bosses hit you so hard that rage is in constant supply. often I have some rage left over, even though I dump 30 rage every 6 seconds with MS.

for what it's worth, the fact that you're finding flaws and working to correct them is A Good Thing.

2) granted, all else being equal, a Prot-heavy warrior will have a defensive advantage tanking anything, but IMO the returns are too small for the sacrifices. my guild's primary tank is heavy Prot and yes he usually tanks the first attempts on anything, but consider this - not too long ago, a guild on a PvP server killed Ragnaros with nothing but 31/20 warriors.

also, a 31/20 (or even a 31/5/15 like myself) can lay the serious beatdown. get your crit % up and invest in a really nice 2h (like an Arcanite Reaper) and you'll compete with the rogues in long fights, especially if you can score multiple Executes.

3) no, level in of itself is not the most quantifiable measure of skill and knowledge, but it is a straight number that can be correlated to some level of experience with the class. probably I've learned as much during 60 as I did in 1-59.
