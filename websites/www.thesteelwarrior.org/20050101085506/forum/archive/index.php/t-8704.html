Paen
08-30-2004, 02:13 PM
DB = dmg bonus, DI = dmg interval

For example a mob that has a DB 1000 and DI 100 his min hit would be 1100 and max hit would be 3000. Each hit is calculated DB+DI(1-20) so if the mob rolled a 7 his hit that round would be 1700 (db of 1000 + DI*7)

The only exclusion to this is warriors roll a 1-19 instead of 1-20 giving us our innate 5% dmg mitigation.

Paen's description is good...the way i was able to grasp easily was thinking in old D&D terms:

- Assume a die 20...it can random from 1-20.

- the DI is the damage per hit die. So in paen's example from Die roll of 1 (100) to Die roll of 20 (2000)

- the DB is the added damage per die (a constant). So in Paen's example D1 (100+1000= 1100) and D20 (2000+1000)

Good stuff and kinda intuitive when you think like you are rolling dice...sorry for the rehash but I figured if this helped me understand this it might help others.
