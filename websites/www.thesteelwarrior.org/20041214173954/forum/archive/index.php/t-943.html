With the resurgance of posts about warrior agro, more and more hybrids from both knight classes have come here to express their opinion. Aside from the usual "you have defensive" "you have raid role and we don't (false btw)" and the exagerated notions of our HP, AC, and mitigation advantages (which we don't have, but not the point). These reactions are nothing new. However, as of late, there has been a whole new wave of people saying "You want our agro abilities, we want defensive," or "you getting our agro abilities will kill our classes or trivialize the game" These are the people this post is directed at, you know who you are, I don't feel like quoting everyone of your posts.


My point is simple. Sensible warriors here "do not want the same agro abilities as you". We don't, really. We want improvements in our agro abilities, but if you notice, none of them are still even remotely close to bringing us to your level of agro generation. If you haven't realized, there is a massive gap between warriors and knights in terms of agro (I group paladins and SKs together here because I know while SK's are slightly better in this, they are both FAR above warriors now). And, there are plenty of ways to fix agro and narrow the gap, some of which you've seen mentioned on these boards, that would improve our abilities noticeably without treading on your territory.

Let me repeat, sensible warriors here don't want the agro abilities of knights, and most of the posters here are indeed sensible. Most of us feel your agro, especially on the paladin side, since SK's were designed to be agro magnets, is much too powerful. we don't want the ability to gain agro at will in any scenario under any circumstances, because that indeed would be game-breaking. No, most just want the ability to pull agro off a slower or CHer in an XP group, if done properly (and don't tell me thats easy to do now, or even possible because thats BS considering how often taunt fails).

Think of it as a figmented scale. Going low low to high, imagine SK's are 105, Paladins are 100, and warriors are 50 on a scale of the agro abilities of either class, respectively. If warriors got a boost, and went up to 65 or 75 on the scale, we receive improvements, but are still not treading on the abilities of either class. I know, some will respond "well, if you get agro abilities to bring you closer to our abilities, we want fixes to bring us closer to you." To that response, I would say, how much closer do you wish to get? We already share similar defensive and offensive tables (tables, not skillcaps). With every expansion, the HP and AC differences become gradually smaller, especially with the introduction of paladin or SK-only spells. Even DPS gaps become narrower with the introduction of more and more great knight weapons into the end-game. The lone feature we have left is defensive, our 3-year old bandaid for not having separate mitigation tables, that is really over-rated and not frequently used in XP groups.

Think about it before posting; we want agro improvements, but not your agro.

I continue to think that leaving warrior aggro and pally aggro alone, and simply making pally aggro more expensive in terms of mana would make the most sense.

This offers groups a clear tradeoff: great aggro (pally) = more downtime for medding, OR higher risk (war) = consistent xps over time with less downtime. Since xp is what it's all about in the problematic scenarios we are discussing (tier 2+ xps groups), more skilled groups (including chanters who actually know how to manage their aggro) can use a warrior with very low risk.

Chanters who have never learned how to properly manage aggro (and there are loads of these out there), and who are the main problem in my experience in terms of demanding pallies instead of warriors, can still choose pallies, only this way they will not be rewarded for not learning an important skill. But they won't be overly penalized either since the xp rate for either type of group should net out pretty close.

Additionally, this would cease what is in effect a penalty against those of us who like some challenge and risk in our game play, because it sharpens our skills, in my view. And it also does not overpower the warrior class simply in response to an already overpowered pally aggro ability.

Bottom line, I don't want warriors to be broken like pallies are now broken, even though this break would be in our favor, as it is currently for pallies.

Is this a nerf? It all depends on your definition, I guess. I think it's a reasonable way to address the pally/war snap aggro imbalance that the vast majority of class boards, including this one, seem to have reached a consensus on.

Further Down the Line

In the next month or so we will be implementing some changes designed to bring more fun back into the game. Our experience with Planes of Power has taught us a lot about what we did right and what we should avoid in the future. Building an online game like EverQuest offers us the unique opportunity to see how customers react to our ideas and to adjust them if needed. We'll be taking the opportunity to put some of our experience to work to improve the play experience in EverQuest.

1) Partial Zone Revamps.
These consist of improvements in the experience gain, monster density and/or item rewards in some of the older zones. The goal is to improve the playability and reward for adventuring in these zones without completely remaking the contents of the zone. We plan to do several of these, and we'll keep you updated as we move through these. Currently we are working on a partial revamp of Veeshan's Peak.

2) Experience Range Adjustment.
When Planes of Power was released and the level cap was raised from 60 to 65, the range of NPCs that characters in the new levels could kill to gain experience was smaller than it is for characters of lower levels. This was done to encourage characters to fight more challenging enemies and allowed us to increase the experience reward given for that increased challenge. Unfortunately, this has limited the locations available to 61+ level characters for experience gain, as well as making it more difficult for solo players to gain experience.
This is something that we don't like, and it has had a negative affect on the "fun factor" involved in playing in Planes of Power. To correct this, we will be increasing the level range of NPCs that will give 61+ level characters experience. They will have approximately the same range of NPCs available to them as they did at level 60, and should be able to gain experience in some of their old hunting grounds, as well as in Planes of Power zones.

3) Planar Progression.
Planes of Power has provided us with valuable insight into the group and guild dynamics that are involved in large scale games. The way that we had expected guilds to advance through the story of Planes of Power has become a roadblock for some guilds, and some members have fallen behind and found it difficult to catch up. Obviously this was not our intention. We want players to play with their friends and guild mates.
To correct this issue, we will be removing the flag requirements for entry into the following Planes of Power zones:
Bastion of Thunder
Plane of Tactics
Halls of Honor
Plane of Nightmare
Plane of Storms
Plane of Torment
Plane of Valor
Crypt of Decay
This will make the majority of Planes of Power zones available to everyone, and should allow most guilds to travel and adventure together. We understand that those that have already earned the entry flags for these zones have put some effort into that accomplishment. We will be giving a reward to those that have done so, though you'll have to wait to see what that reward is.

4) Items in the Planes of Power
We will be looking at the quality of Planes of Power item rewards and improving them where appropriate. Items for many of the lower tier gods will be upgraded and more rare items and NPCs will be added to the following zones as needed:
Crypt of Decay
Plane of Torment
Halls of Honor
Plane of Tactics
Plane of Innovation
Bastion of Thunder

5) Class Balance
We are going to look at improving the damage capabilities of melee classes, as they've fallen behind their magic-using counterparts at the very high end of the game. Some options being considered are:
Improve melee damage-increasing disciplines
Improve Planes of Power melee weapons
Additionally, we're looking at the concerns from players about their spells, and changing them where appropriate. The goal here is to remove annoying issues with spells, not necessarily to increase caster power overall.

- The EverQuest Team
