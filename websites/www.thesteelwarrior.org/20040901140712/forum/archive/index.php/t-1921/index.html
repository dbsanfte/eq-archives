Daemonwynd
I'm not entirely sure where the best place to put this thread is, I'm just tossing it here for now. Mods can move it wherever.

I was thinking the other night.

I think that a good part of the reason why so many people are passionate about problems with their class, is because they have invested a lot INTO their class. If they have a class such as a warrior which arguably has turned innto a class that is weaker in relative pwoer than the other tanks, they're going to make a fus about getting the problem fixed, they're not going to go to the other, more powerful classes, and start a character of the class that's perceived to be more powerful, because they have invested too much time INTO their original character. Really, "powerful" can be interchanged here with the word "in demand."

Pure melee also in PoP have, until the very highest levels of PoP (IE SolRo/RZ/Elementals and above) pretty much gotten the shaft in terms of itemization and improvements to their class and their abilities, especially compared to the upgrades in spells that all casters have gotten.

I think what this is very likely to turn into in terms of class choice in EQ2, is that classes that are seen to be weak in EQ, will pretty much translate directly into classes that are NOT picked in EQ2. For example, I believe many warriors will pick Paladins or Shadowknights. Druids will pick Mages if they want a pet, Cleric to be a good healer, wizard to be a good nuker. Rogues and monks will be rarer, clerics and enchanters will be all over the place.

IN EQ2, I believe that since people won't have the time invested into their characters as they have in EQ1, people will reflect, with their class choices, the community's general perception of what classes are good and what suck in EQ1, and to a lesser extent, what classes they feel will get upgrades and new toys consistently, and what classes will not get anything.

I'm curious, what does everyone else think of this little speculation of mine?

According to normal logic, yes. But, this is Everquest, a game where it is normal for a hybrid to outank a class whose sole purpose revolves around tanking. What I'm hinting at, is that logic does not apply to Everquest.

True, some will say that, once you've killed all the gods, where else is there to go? Well, personally, I think there are still several angles that can be explored here.

1) Keep in mind, not all the gods have been killed yet. In fact, arguably the two most powerful ones have yet to even be discovered. Veeshan is obviously one. Considering the fact that players have already defeated most of Norrath's dieties, and the final stage in PoP progression is Plane of Time, I had a thought. Up until this point, Everquest has existed in a consistent time-frame, as in, gamers never travel through time, to past or future. The concept of time really isn't even an issue (not talking about time limits on encounters or anything, i'm talking about in-game time).

So, with the completion of plane of time, and the deafeat of major Norrath diety, why not launch an expansion enabling gamers to travel through time? I mean, the expansion starts off with an immensely wize sage who forsees the destruction of Norrath. However, the detrcution of Quarm, and completion of PoTime has shattered the fabric of time, allowing adventurers the ability to travel through time and prevent the events that will eventually lead to Norrath's annihalation from occuring.

Rather than gods, gamers can combat the mystical characters that helped created lore of Norrath, and participate in the events taht helped shape the planet's history. Trakanon's prophecy, the original war of the dieties, the chaining of the sleeper are just a few of the possible encounters...


Yea, I know, my idea probably sounds stupid and non-sensical. But, it's just designed to illustrate that tehre are still paths that Everquest can go through at the high-end game without allowing the game to just "be beaten"
