Let me give an analogy in terms of druids and clerics.

In Velious and early Luclin, druids were not healers except as a last resort. The only real option the group had in terms of healing was cleric, and druids were grubbing around for those heavily-contested DPS slots.

Since the introduction of Tunares Renewal and Karana's Renewal, druids have lost a lot of ground against other classes in terms of DPS. (Not all of them are completely happy about that, but the evoc druid is a dying breed; you just can't have a situation where there's a required group slot for a healer and only one class of healer to choose from, it's inequitable. Equally you can't have a situation where one of the primary group healing classes is also a primary group damage dealer, but the other isn't.)

The situation between druids and clerics should be that a cleric offers the big buffs and the big heals, plus noticeable damage output against undead targets, so that in any situation where you're fighting the big hitting critters or the undead, cleric is clearly the better choice. Druids should offer buffs and heals which are adequate for a less challenging environment, plus noticeable damage output against pretty much everything, so in any situation where you're just grinding against non-undead small hitters, druid is clearly the better choice.

(That's probably close to the reality of the position at the moment. Once you have an "adequate" amount of healing in your group - i.e. your healer is keeping up with constant chain pulls - the choice between druid and cleric comes down to damage output.)

Well, the situation between knights and warriors should be that the warrior offers the big tanking and the big damage, while the knight offers tanking which is adequate for a less challenging environment, plus situational damage output (e.g. paladins -v- undead), plus better aggro control.

(That's also close to the reality of the position at the moment, the challenge being that the group's concern about aggro control has become exaggerated, owing to the fact that PoP exp critters can kill a non-tank in a few attack rounds.)

The problem warriors have at the moment is partly that their damage output is too low relative to knights (which also has an impact on warrior aggro generation). Dual wield should be noticeably better damage than two-handers, as it has been throughout the majority of EQ; fixing this would come a long way towards solving some of the issues that monks and rogues are suffering from as well.

The second part of the problem is that the knight's ability to soak damage is too close to that of the warrior. You could fix that through warrior hp or a separate damage mitigation table or whatever, but warriors need to soak damage noticeably better.

However, I don't see that solving those two issues will solve the overall problem. What will really fix warriors would require those two issues to be solved, plus the introduction of real big-hitting critters with a real incentive to kill them.

Learning how to control aggro is, and should be, the price for grouping with a warrior. What's missing is the reward (in terms of increased exp and item drops) for doing that.

This is where I have high hopes for the dungeons in LDoN. (I'm not in the beta and may be wildly off base here). I'm hoping to zone into one of those dungeons and meet level 63-66 critters that hit for 1k and nuke like artillery pieces, right at zone in - and see them get tougher (hitting for 2k+) as the group advances - and to see real benefits from choosing a warrior as opposed to a knight to tank them. And there has to be a real incentive in terms of exp and loot to kill these things.

I also see mana-draining critters in PoP, and these are underused; I'm hoping for more of them. Show me a mob that procs a 1khp/1kmana drain and I'll show you a mob that I want a warrior to tank.

In short, despite what Absor says about class balancing not being tied in with expansions, addressing game content has to be a part of the fix.
