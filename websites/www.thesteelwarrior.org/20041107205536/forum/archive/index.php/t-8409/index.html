Plenty of guilds apparently have direct communication with devs, and basically know when and what changes will be pushed live to the endgame zones. This does constitute a competitive advantage, because it allows them better to schedule their time/raid days.

Let me give you an example:

Before the recent Inktuta patch I was talking to someone on another server that had basically a run down list of all the exact changes that would occur to the Inktuta encounter BEFORE they went in: from the new minimum amount of people required to be near exiles, to very specific and detailed information about the changes to the final Noqufiel encounter, in particularly information about the fake version becoming stationary and stop summoning when it is about to explode. And that players would have 5 seconds to move away to avoid the explosion damage. That is the kind of information it otherwise might have taken a raid or two to notice.

I dont know the origin of that information, wether it was from summit board or leaked to a guild, but I do know that at the time it was hugely valuable at least for me, since I then could stop scheduling inktuta raids knowing that the "toned down" version would be going live with next weeks patch.

So basically there are 2 types of competitive advantages you can get:

A) Knowing when patches/changes to zone go live, allows you to make more effective use of your raiding time, than guilds that are not privvy to such information

and

B) In the case of very specific encounter information such as the above, you can formulate a tactic for dealing with the encounter before even having seen the changes.

Personally I think A) should be information privvy to all. It was good how Rashere gave public notice on the eqlive section when the Tacvi zone was patched, this is the way to do it. I think they should extend that concept, and maybe make an area of the board where they can also post when minor zone patches go in.

Information of type B) I dont EVER think anyone should be given. It should be enough to state that a zone is being patched, nobody outside SOE should need a spoiler list of the exact changes before they go live.

But generally speaking its a balance act. I think its cool that devs talk with players through summit boards and other forms of communication. They just have to be aware what kind of information they share, and not go overboard with details(and if they do, it should be on a public forum where it is for all to see).
