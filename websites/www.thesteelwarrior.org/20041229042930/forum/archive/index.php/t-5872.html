A couple people have mentioned that when you are tanking on a raid, you're getting healed anyway, and the damage is minor compared to the melee damage you're taking. That's true, when you're talking about something that is purely a damage based AE...

But a lot of AEs also include such nasty things as slow or major stuns. In the case of these types of effects, healing has nothing to do with it - if you don't resist, then your ability to maintain aggro has just been hurt, and hurt badly.

I don't think you can make a blanket statement about how important resists are to a raiding warrior. For some encounters, they really don't make much of a difference. For other encounters, it is either resist every single time or fail.

For the original poster... in the 50s I really wouldn't worry too much about resists. Focus on AC and HPs. But (there's always a but) I would also have a bag of resist gear, to have handy when the situation calls for it (like if you're fighting mobs that AE a lot and you are not the MT).

When I was in my late 40s, I wanted to go on Vox raids, so I invested in resist jewelry. Unfortunately, very few of the hapless horde that showed up for the pick up raids I attended had even minimal resist gear, and all of those raids were frustrating failures, as the raid had a tendency to lay down and die to Vox's AE.

Then I joined a raiding guild, and my focus shifted to AC and HP, since I wasn't going to get a tanking job unless I could actually take a hit. My standard gear's poor resists were made up for with all the buffs I got on raids, and even more, by the levels that I gained to be on a par with the rest of my guild. Going into HoT at 53rd, I was getting cooked, and was probably nothing but a mana sponge for the healers. By 55th, I was doing a lot better, and by 60th it was just about easy.

Somewhere in here, SoE decided that the monsters needed some help in all of EQ, and jiggered resists, making blue and higher con casters truely fearsome, while greenies became much less of a threat. I began actively looking for disease resists in the mistaken belief that this would protect me from the shaman slows in lower Chardok. Still don't quite understand why its magic resist, but cured by cure disease... At the same time, they changed npc casters so that they had a more sophisticated AI and a bigger spell book. (I really wonder if any of the SoE devs every took an appropriate, non-twinked group through SolA, or Lower Guk. One wizard is a serious threat. Two probably means someone is going to die. Three, and you are looking at a wipe.)

As my guild started working its way into PoP, I found that my resists were both beginning to take care of themselves from my standard gear (this trend actually started when I got my Vindi BP) and to become less significant as the dependence of our raids on Bard resists became more and more pronounced. So I continued to look at AC and HP first. As we entered and progressed through Time, a new standard emerged - high enough resists so that you could hit 500 without a bard in your group.

Now we are in GoD. And I am stuck with a bit of a problem, because of this prismatic resist stuff - my cold resist sucks ass. Everything else is 200+ unbuffed, Cold is about 130. Guess thats what all those unused augment slots are for...
