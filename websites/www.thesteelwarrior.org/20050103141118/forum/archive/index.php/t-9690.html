First impressions:

1. Quest system owns.

Browse the web, make sure you absorb all the quest info you can. Not only are quests immersive and user friendly: but you can also easily DOUBLE the amount of exp that you're getting. Seriously, fill the quest log and just go out, at one point the other night I was getting hits from 4 or 5 different quests for every mob I killed.

Some cool quests I've done so far:

a. the quest to become a rogue -
The harbormaster in fp was 'cooking the books' with the ship logs and i had to penetrate the harbor office and steal both his private and public logs by searching his office: Zone in to my own instance, sneak past a few guards, start searching his office, find one log. oh no! a guard is coming! Hit sneak again and he comes in the room and stops, looks around. It's clear he's not leaving so I 'ambush him' (cool rogue skill from stealth mode, knocks the mob on his ass effectively putting him out of commission for a few seconds) then stun him with 'cheap shot' (a knee to the groin) - at this point I whip around and backstab the bastard, etc.... once he's down I continue my search, find the log, stealth mode back out - return to the quest giver and i'm a rogue.

b. Access quest for a zone off of the commons (name slipped my mind):

Shady npc in freeport tells you about the zone, he can't get in because some people are researching in there and he's not on good terms with them, directs you to talk to a guy in commonlands near the zone in.

The guy is talking about some research process they are doing in the zone, states he won't let me in unless I kill some of the giants in the area to prove my worth.
After killing the giants, he explains that they are going to hold a ceremony to present me with a key. I then return to the shady guy in freeport who advises me to betray them at the ceremony, kill their leader, and take a key for myself and him both...

Sooo, my friends and I zone in to the instanced ceremony, slaughter the bastards, kill the leader (who drops nice loot) and another named (also drops nice loot) then I get the key for the zone, as well as a helmet (they offer plate, leather, chain and silk versions for this helm as a reward) and for that hour I spent doing the quest I got about 75% exp in the level I was in: probably 40 of that came when I did the final "hand in" for the quest. (again, quest exp OWNS)


I've been playing with a group of friends from eq and we've been seriously kicking some ass. We're only playing part time as we don't want to interfere w/ eq 1 but still are able to make great progression.

As far as the performance:

I also have fx5700 (256 mb) - 512 ram - I turn the visual options down all the way and it's completely playable, no lag here. It's a brand new game, to play on full settings you will need a new card, that's just the way it is. Still, it looks good enough to me (I didn't buy the game for the graphics).


The game is not EQ, with a new graphics engine as some would suggest. It's a totally different game (although there are a few similarities)

I was laughing the other night when I found an ogre in freeport named Boomba, who sells pickles. (sound familiar?) - Well he actually talks now <insert booming ogre voice here> "My name is Boomba, and I sell pickles. If you want to talk, find a Teir'Dal." - it would appear that the price of pickles has gone up considerably over the past 500 years, so I didn't buy one.


All in all, I'm liking it pretty well, still going to try out WoW when it comes out, then make a final decision from there.
