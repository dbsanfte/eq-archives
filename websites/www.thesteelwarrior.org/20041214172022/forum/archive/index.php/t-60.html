Apothes,

The general consensus that I've seen is basically this:

The Warrior vs Knight aggro generation gap is too large in relation to the warrior and knight durability gap.

Some people, such as Phantron, etc. believe strongly that the "standard" should be a lot closer to current warrior aggro generation than current knight aggro generation. In essence, that knights' aggro generation should be toned way down, or that there be some significant tradeoff in terms of medding time to maintain the level of aggro generation which they enjoy. Their position is that the easier it becomes to maintain aggro on a tank, the more boring and rote this game becomes and the less skilled people have to be to play it.

Other people on the other hand, desiring not to see another class get nerfed, think that raising the level of aggro generation to closer to that of knights than it currently is is the answer.

There's a 3rd group who believes that the key to this is to increase the "durability spread" between warrior and knight to a noticeable level making groups choose between the *obvious* (i.e. not theoretical, but noticeably) better "tank" and the one better at aggro control.

I tend to fall in line in a small way with the #2 group, but with a spin. I think that the problem with aggro generation has been identified as being something that happens early in fights in PoP, where a slow cast or resisted can mean a splatted shaman or enchanter, or a heal can mean a splatted cleric.

I believe pretty strongly that once that has been identified as the issue, then solutions can be brought up to compensate or deal with it. For instance... I personally have proposed the "hate arrows".... fletched arrows which add a specific numerical amount of hate, but through a non-stackable "debuff" that leaves an icon similar to Donal's BP (Ranged attack means it's limited to a range, and can't be used during normal combat with any efficiency, and the "icon" meaning that it cannot have an additive effect whereas a warrior kites a mob around with a few arrows ensuring "aggro lock"). Others have proposed "skills" such as hurling insults which do essentially the same thing.

In either case, most of the objections raised are accounted for. Does it trivialize high end raid encounters? Nope, not unless you have been doing high end encounters without figuring out how to do aggro transfer pulls through the use of weaponshield, which is signficantly superior to any of the proposed methods. Does it make warrior "taunt" too strong? Nope, it simply adds a single tool to assist in generating aggro where it's needed. Not assuming someone else's aggro such as in the taunt skill, but generating some of your own. Finally, if done through reagent itemization (such as hate arrows), it's available to all for a small price, and doesn't rely on AA points spent or anything like that.

I also tend to agree with many of those who have spoken here that making taunt *too* reliable would certainly make my playtime boring, and I wouldn't want to have it so easy. On the other hand, I also don't think i want to see the spread between knight and Warrior in terms of the aggro gap be as glaring as it is today.

There's no question that the aggro difference between Knights and Warrior is too large, and that only leaves two solutions. Nerf knights, or buff Warriors.

Nerf Knights is the right solution when you consider game integrity & design, assuming that EQ was never intended so that you can cast a level 9 spell and hold the mob's attention forever.

Pros:
1. Nerfing doesn't break the game. The Paladin stun line can be gone from the game tomorrow and the game will still function. Yeah Paladins will have it hard controlling aggro, but they still bring plenty of other benefits to a group. I don't know much about how to fix SK since I can count on the one hand the number of SKs I have grouped with.

Cons:
1. People cry over nerfing.
2. Groups will actually now have to learn how to control aggro, and from my grouping experience, this is really hard.

Suppose we buff up warriors instead:

Pros:
1. Warriors are happy, and since there are a lot more warriors than Knights, that means everyone is happy (relatively).
2. Further, your group is now happy too.

Cons:
1. Make the game more or less trivial on the XP level (even though it sort of is already, but it doesn't need to get even less trivial).
2. Knights might not be able to get a group anymore. If you don't need Knight for aggro, what else do you need one for? Warrior tank better and do more damage. If the mob is always on the warrior, you really don't need significant crowd control abilities.

The aggro problem is perceived in the sense that it's only bad because people think it's bad. It's obviously there, but you never needed Knight aggro if you have a good group or even just a group that will listen. But because Knight aggro exists, people become reliant on that and you'll find yourself in a group where the wizard tries to pile as many nukes as possible and wonder why you can't get it off him, even though the game is never intended to be played this way.

And like I said before, it's easier to fire the warrior than fire the group. Most people tend to prefer to blame on the warrior's inability to keep aggro (which is stupid, as warriors have no means of generating extra aggro besides taunt. Even EB weapons aren't aggro on demand) as opposed to their own inability to control aggro, and there is only one of you but potentially 5 of them in a group.
