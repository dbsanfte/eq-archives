Sassinak
03-29-2004, 06:44 PM
When two warriors are using shield,

- one person receives 1/2 damage (this is a little better than defensive, and is a good thing)

- the other person receives 3/4 damage (this is generally worse than defensive, almost as bad as them tanking it on their own).


There doesn't seem to be much point in having one warrior take 3/4 of the damage while still having to heal another tank.

If the warrior who is eating 3/4 damage has the most hitpoints, then they ought to be tanking in the first place.

If the warrior who is eating 3/4 damage has superior AC/shielding/etc., then it's totally wasted while they are shielding.

The only situations that I can think of to /shield during a boss fight are:

1) if the warrior with the most hit points is unable to get aggro for some reason. Then he can still take some of the melee damage from the person who has a lower life expectancy.

2) if shielding completely eliminates the need for a healing rotation. It might be easier/safer to heal two tanks through conventional healing instead of coordinating a rotation.

3) if the person who has aggro is in danger of dying (e.g. the healers are silenced), and it would be really bad if they died. Then shield them and risk your own life to save theirs.

4) if most of the group/raid's DPS comes from a damage shield. Shielding makes the mob take twice as much damage from damage shields.

It's an ability, not a discipline. You can use it every 3 minutes, as long as neither you nor the other person are using a discipline. Type /shield when you have no target to see info about it.

It's short duration, but extendable with AA's. It's also short range, but that too is extendable with AA's. The people who never use it are the people who never spent AA's on it. 2 AA is worthwhile to double its duration.

In exp. groups I prefer to set up hotkeys with /shield <slower's name> /shield <cleric's name> and/or /shield <enchanter's name>. Basically, whoever might get hit but can't really take a punch.
In raids, that's not practical because of the number of different folks who could, in theory, need to be shielded, so the hotkey set ups mentioned above will work.
Shielding does add to the damage output of the mob, but it cuts in half the damage taken by the character the mob wants to hit. I believe there is also a hate component to it--so when you shield your cleric or shaman or enchanter, there is a better chance the mob will turn right back to you, which is really what you want. But it does also give whoever is getting hit a little bit extra time (how much depends, of course, on how tough the mob is and how wimpy the caster is).
Because of the fact it doesn't stack with discs, it seems to have limited use in terms of shielding other tanks. If another melee picks up agro, you can /shield him and give healers more time to keep that melee standing, and give yourself a better chance of pulling the mob right back on you.
As far as shielding the main tank...hmm. If MT is out of discs in the last few seconds of a fight and there's a real question that a heal might not land in time, you could try it.
But post lvl 55 I would think that such situations would not be that common.
I like /shield, but the people you raid/hunt with have to know what it does--you're going to have to explain it to them, including the bit about the fact you need to be close to them to do it.
