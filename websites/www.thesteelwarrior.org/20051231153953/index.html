TSW Becomes a Vanguard 'Affiliate'
Dec 24, 2005 - 10:01 PM - by Raaj
I recently shared some emails with Nick Parkinson, the Community Representative of Vanguard: Saga of Heroes. They are interested in having TSW as a part of their affiliated sites along with the Druids Grove and the Safehouse. Now I realize there's already a "plate" site out there for Vanguard, but they don't have the following or membership that TSW has, or the good name and reputation for factual information. At last glance we had ~10,000(after our last purge of inactive accounts). With their userbase not nearly as large or established as ours, hopefully we can bring some new blood over to the TSW community, especially in the Vanguard section.The number of active users I quoted above of course includes Everquest mainly, but I know many EverQuest players are going to be playing Vanguard. It also includes a large number of World of Warcraft players. Mr. Parkinson himself was a long-time reader of TSW before he went to join the Vanguard team, so it is not as if we are some unknown community of gamers. He's quite familiar with our community.

These are preliminary talks of course. I'm in the dark as much as you as to what this might mean. It's possible that being "affiliated" means that we're officially endorsed as a class-site, or it's possible we'll just be mentioned in a link on their page. TSW is, and will remain, an EQ-Oriented site as it has since the beginning. That doesn't mean, however, that we cannot embrace change. Being the holidays, I wouldn't expect much news until after the celebrations. If anything, it should increase traffic on the site, which is good for everyone. Perhaps some of you will meet old faces you haven't seen in years from EQ-past. People change, they move on just as in life, but that doesn't mean we can't keep in touch with them. This community has grown far larger than everquest, as evidenced by the rambunctious WoW boards that have been opened since its release. It's a place to keep in touch with one another and a place to share adventures - wherever they may take place. Rest assured that TSW is not going anywhere.

Regardless of the outcome of what kind of 'affiliation' this is, I'll keep ya'll updated on anything that I hear about Vanguard, SoE, or any of the other companies that contact me in order to reach the community here. I'm not TSW, you all are. You've kept this place alive and well for years now and I'm not going to let it go to waste. I've been honest with you up to and through the IGE attempted buy-out, and that trend will continue. Like I've said before, this isn't my site, I simply figure-head it and keep things working along with the help of the moderators and sHORTY the server d00d. The community makes it what it is. Together we've fought off the likes of platinum sellers, cheaters, hackers, and ez-board pocket gougers, and it seems our time to shine as a gaming community that cares deeply about its hobbies may have finally come. DoD Loot Upgrades
Nov 14, 2005 - 7:38 PM - by Raaj
I receieved an e-mail from SoE in regards to some DoD Loot changes that are going to take place. There's some good information in here for anyone interested.

Included is a short Q&A with the EQ team, which you can read below.

What were the thoughts behind the decision to retune the Depths of Darkhollow items?

After DoD launched, we saw a lot of feedback from players that non-raid loot rewards weren’t appropriate for the difficulty of the content. Carefully evaluating the cases players brought up led us to audit the model that has been used for itemization for several expansions now.

The old itemization model was based around the top end single group gear being roughly 50% of the stats of top end raid level gear. This worked fine when it was first implemented, but as stats have increased, so too has the gap between raiders and non-raiders. Since content is tuned with player power in mind, this has caused a growing gap in the difficulty of expansion zones, in turn leading to the problems players have been reporting.

After a lot of evaluation on the potential impact, we decided to change the itemization model to bring single group gear closer to the level of raid attainable gear. Beyond providing more tangible upgrades in the expansion, this change should also have a number of positive effects on the game as a whole and is something we will continue to use when making items in the future.

How many items did you change?

These changes affect roughly 1000 items from the Depths of Darkhollow expansion.

Does this retuning involve anything other than changing the items?

Yes. In addition to looking at many of the expansion items, we also took a look at the difficulty of the zones and missions and made adjustments to provide a smoother progression of difficulty through the expansion. In many cases, lower level missions early in the expansion became a bit easier and higher level missions that are further into the expansion became a bit harder.

If we have previously done a DoD mission and deleted the one-time completion reward, will we be able to get it again after it is upgraded?

Yes. We changed the way the mission reward mechanic works so when you complete a mission it will now give you the completion reward again if you don't already have that item anywhere on your character (including in the bank). This will allow players who have previously done the missions to return to those missions and get the items again.
