To change the distribution of hits with CS/ID, NO CHANGE to the AC cap is needed. All that is required is a several hundred cell look up table, and a term that says, 'Oh, yeah, look 15 cells to the lower of whatever the randomizer tells you.' This has ZERO relation to your AC. This has the effect of an additional subtractive term in the mitigation equation, and is both the lazy and the 'sane' way to implement something like CS/ID. From dev comments and in game observation, there are TWO different, really poorly named onset points in the AC effectiveness curves. The existance of one does not, in any way, preclude the existance of the other.

The first of these caps is reached at the early luclin level, by dev comments. Let's call it 1300 AC. Beyond 1300 AC, the value of AC vs atk changes. 1000 AC and 1000 attack are directly comparable. And here's the really important part: However, past 1300 AC, each AC point is worth a hard coded, linear fraction of what it was before 1300. Assuming that for the plate tanks, the conversion factor is 2, 2100 AC is only comparable to 1700 Atk, not 2100 atk! Every point of AC is still valuable, and is, in fact, more valuable, as you now need more of it to keep up with ever inflating mob attack values.

The second cap is the edges of the mitigation tables: At some point, you will sufficiently exceed mob attack, and see little to no return from addition AC against that mob and lower attack mobs only. This is because no data beyond this point exists.

Just some thoughts, and a bit of clarification of what I was saying earlier-

For each expansion, there is a mob (or possibly mobs) with the maximum level + attack combination in that expansion's zones. Assuming no hardcoded exceptions due to special buffs/etc., that mob defines where the maximum "knee" in the mode hit vs. AC curve is for that expansion. It also seems logical, and agrees with casual observation, that "typical" atk values for the highest level "regular", pure-melee mobs in an expansion has a set value. This combination of level + atk sets the average/typical "knee" in AC vs. mode hit- for *that* expansion.

Note that this is a background phenomena, purely a result of game mechanics (max/typical atk) + designer decisions (max/average mob level). On top of this baseline, you can add non-linear AC vs. ATK, various mitigation implementations, etc. And there is nothing to say that several expansions couldn't share the same max/typical mob level/atk values either...
