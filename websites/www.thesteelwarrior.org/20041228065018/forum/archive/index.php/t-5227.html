STR and STA are sooooo easy to raise up for warriors, there really is not much point in spending your staring bonus points on those. I have a n ogre warrior and I started with all my points in both DEX and AGI, if I recall I set them both to 85 with the bonus points.

Now that was along time ago and I didn't know much about high end warrior stuff. If I had it to do all over again I probably would have gone all CHA and AGI -- those are two very hard to raise stats. In general warrior gear has no CHA on it at all, and AGI is probably the least available one of the 4 prime melee stats. Why CHA you ask? Well CHA is used for Divine Intervention and that will save your ass over and over again when you are tanking.

To give you an idea, with mostly high end luclin-era gear with some pre-elemental PoP stuff mixed in my actual unbuffed stats are as follows (not accounting for stat caps):
STR=359
STA=323
AGI=195
DEX=229
CHA=52

As you can see I have an over-abundance of STR and STA since those are capped for me at 305, yet I still do not have enough AGI, DEX and especially CHA. I max all of STR/STA/AGI/DEX at 305 with buffs easily. If you look at my magelo you'll see only one item that has CHA on it -- that's how hard it is to come by. I'm gonna have to get LDoN augs to raise up my CHA and spend 15 AA to raise it as much as I can.

So for STARTING STATS I would go for either CHA or AGI or a combination of both. Then once you start playing your char go with high STA gear until you max your STA at your stat cap.

Don't worry about resists at all until you get to your 50s. Then you'll want to make sure you can get over 100 to each resist unbuffed with resist gear as a good start. Eventually you'll have goals of 150 and 200+ to each resist, but that comes much later. +50mr will NEVER be > 100ac, your job is to tank and as such you will face a ton more melee damage than you ever will get from spell damage. AC/HP > resists just about always for warrior who is tanking. When you are not tanking, then you can make some sacrifices for greater resists.

As for AC and HP, you need healthy amounts of both, but shoot for about 1200ish AC and then focus on HP. This is the "soft cap" around which most non-PoP mobs show diminishing returns for having higher AC. When you get to level 60+ and start doing more and more in PoP you'll need to get that AC back up.
