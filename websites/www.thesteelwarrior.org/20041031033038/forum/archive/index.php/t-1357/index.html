wandor
You are really asking several questions here. Let's address everything so you can make the best decision for you.

I mean, if I can make anywhere near the cash at lvl 65 by farming that i could with, say, 250 smithing
Well, first of all, to get 250 smithing is going to cost you a ton of plat to begin with. I don't care what anyone says, trade skills cost a bundle to get to 250 with very few exceptions. You can get brewing to 250 very cheap, but then you really can't make much off brewing unless you are dealing in massive quatities. You can probably sell Grobb Liquidized Meat or Kaladim Constitutional but you will have to make hundereds of stacks to make good coin.

All other trade skills are going to cost you a bundle. Most you can get to the 180-200 range pretty cheap, but you won't have very good success making items that sell well at that skill level. Once you get to that level you either have to spend a truck load of plats for skill ups (40K+ for the cheap ones, 200k+ for the expensive ones) or, you will have to farm greens endlessly for weeks at a time to do your skill ups inexpensively. Plus you have the disadvantage of not having 355 int/wis with your normal gear. You will have to invest in some tradeskill gear so your skill up/attempt ratio doesn't kill you.

The problem with most trade skills is that you need to be a GM in a trade when a new popular item comes out. If you don't get in early, you are not going to make nearly as much money as the guys who get 10x the normalized price the first few weeks something sells. Not only that, but the trade skill market has gotten hammered by people doing the Aid Grimil quest. Instead of 1 or 2 people in a guild doing most of the trade skilling, now you have dozens of people who all have 220+ in every trade.

Basically, there are very few good options when it comes to trades now. The first is to have very high skill in all trades so you can cash in on what ever the latest trend is. You need to be able to make what ever the hot seller is regardless of what skill it takes. It really helps a ton to have Tactics or Elem access because the best trade skill items are mad from drops in those zones. You will have to farm them yourself because anyone in those zones knows what the drops are used for and knows how hard they are to come by, so they don't make it to the bazaar very often.

The other way to make money on trades is to cash in on the laziness of the people who are working their trade skills. It doesn't take much research to figure out what trade skill items you can sell for a good profit. These items usually take considerably less skill to make so you don't have to spend a fortune getting your skill high enough to have a good success rate. You still have to farm a lot of green stuff, but at least it is pure profit. On my server spider silks (10-20pp EACH), silk swatches (20-30pp EACH), Imbued emeralds (30pp each), Celestial essence (5pp each), Leather padding (20-30pp each), Acrylia pieces/blocks (25-100p each), and Velium pieces/block (25-100p) each sell very well.

As for making money at 65, Sebilis is one of the best zones to do this in. Tons of cash drops and the occasional loot to sell. You will have a hard time finding another place that will yeild so much plat.

However, there is a better option at 65. With a small group (3-6 toons) there is a ton of stuff you can do that drops gear that will sell for a lot of plat. Would you rather spend hours making a few kpp in a night or would you rather spend 30 min-1 hr each night and make 10k+? You have to set aside the time to check certain spawns (having a dru/wiz port bitch helps a lot here) and you will have to track spawns so you can get an idea of when mobs are due, but this is not very hard to do. Even if you only get 1 target a week, you can still make a ton of plats if you pick the right targets. I will leave the target selection to you since you will have to determine what mobs you can effectively take down with small groups.
