I recently posted this to other messageboards, so I find it kind of amusing to come across this post. Anyhow, here is what I posted.

Each attack is broken up into two portions.

The first portion deals with your skill. This is the portion that checks for a riposte, parry, block, determines if you are going to hit the mob or miss, includes the weapon's damage bonus, and 1/3rd of the damage.

How do I know that the damage bonus is attributed to this portion of the attack? Simple, it is part of the formula I have found to work.

This formula is, Weapon Damage X 2 + Damage Bonus.

The answer to this formula stands for the maximum possible hit based on the skill check portion of your attack.

Let's use Ragebringer as an example. Ragebringer has 15 damage, with a damage bonus of 13.

15X2+13=43 which would be the maximum hit from the skill check portion of the attack.

15X6+13-x(in this case x=1)=102 which would be the maximum possible hit for a normal attack with a Ragebringer in the mainhand. I will explain the max hit formula later in the post.

The Everquest combat system seems to overlap values, so the 3 most common attacks you would get with a Ragebringer would be 0, 43, and 102 damage.

0 Represents a complete miss on the skill check portion of the attack, when this happens the attack portion of the hit never goes through.
43 Represents the maximum possible value on the skill check portion of the attack, along with a complete miss on the hit portion of the attack.
102 Represents the maximum possible value on both the skill check portion, and the hit portion of the attack.

Due to the fact that through extensive parsing I still haven't seen that much of a change in the first portion of numbers, no matter how low or high my attack goes we can assume that this number is relatively constant.

Whether you even hit the mob or not, and 1/3rd of the damage is likely determined by how your base offense skill compares against your opponents base defense skill on a roll.

1/3rd of all random damage done each attack has absolutely nothing to do with how much attack your character has. I say random damage, because the damage bonus applied to each hit is not random.

There is a possibility, which is the reason I am sharing this information, that your skill in that particular attack(Piercing,1h Slash etc) is also used in this portion of the attack. That is what I wish to find out.

The second part of an attack is your attack value against your opponents AC. This only comes into play, if the first part of the attack goes through. The first part is relatively constant because your base offensive skill is a constant.

Which basically means no matter how high your attack goes, you still won't hit for max all the time. The higher your attack goes, the more often the 2nd portion of the attack is for max. Since the first part is based on a constant, it would take that first random number + max value for the 2nd part, which would be your total hit.

On an augmented vanazir, the maximum hit is 424, the skill check portion of the attack equates to 170 damage(65 damage+40 bonus) so all of the attacks between the skill check, and 170 don't change. So if 5% of all of my attacks do around 100 damage out of 170 on the skill check portion, even if my attack was so high the 2nd portion of the attack did max on every hit, it would still only put 5% of my hits around 354(100 out of 170 + 254 out of 254) damage, as opposed to the max possible of 424.

So, your attack has reached diminishing returns when the first portion of your numbers is directly proportional to its equivalent max attack value on the 2nd portion of numbers. Not when all of your hits are for max damage, but most/all of the hits on the 2nd portion of your numbers are doing max damage.

This formula also provides the formula to determine the maximum hit allowed for a particular weapon.

Your offense against your opponents defense = Weapon Damage * 2 + Damage Bonus.

Your Attack against your opponents AC = Weapon Damage * 4 - X where X is a counter variable.

For every 10 damage on your weapon, X is increased by 1. Keep in mind that your weapon starts at 1, instead of 0.

So, From 1-10 damage X is 0.
11-20 X is 1
21-30 X is 2
31-40 X is 3
41-50 X is 4
51-60 X is 5
61-70 X is 6
71-80 X is 7
81-90 X is 8

So, the simplified version of the max hit formula is this:

Weapon Damage * 6 + Damage Bonus - X

The actual formula is broken up into 2 steps,

(Skill check: Weapon Damage * 2 + Damage Bonus) + (Attack check: Weapon Damage * 4 - X)


Just as this information has been useful to me for attacking, it can be just as useful for tanking. Basically, 1/3rd of all random damage taken would depend on your base defense skill. The first part would likely deal more with avoidance, while the second part would be the mitigation part.

As well, my theory is that the skill in the weapon you are using, is part of the 2nd check. Once your offensive skill determines that you are going to hit the mob, it's your weapon skill(piercing etc) + your atk against your opponents AC that determines whether you will do any additional damage, and if so, how much. It would be possible to gain a skill up in Offense and Piercing in the same swing since they are both being checked each swing. This is where I think the various weapon mods come into play, not by increasing the chance to hit, but by increasing the chance to do additional damage after the hit is determined, basically an increase to DPS. I would also tend to believe accuracy would be on the second half, as opposed to the first, so accuracy would not increase your chance to hit, but would be a minor increase to DPS as it would increase the number of times you do additional damage in the 2nd portion of the equation.

Your theory on when attack starts to hit a soft cap is wrong as far as I can tell too.

All of the hits between Damage Bonus and Damage Bonus + Weapon Damage * 2 are constant. Every single attack has to go through that damage range. So, rank the percentage all attacks hit in that area. Completely remove all hits above your "magic number" from the parse and focus solely on the hits in that range. 40% of all of those hits, will be for max with 252 offense against the vast majority of mobs you fight.

Now, do the exact same ranking on the other half of the equation. Remove all the numbers from the magic number and below, and focus only on the hits in the 2nd half. What percentage of those hits are for max?

At 1620 attack, its around 23.5% of all attacks in the upper portion are for max. In the first half of the equation, you hit your magic number around 40% of all possible hits in that area but are only hitting max 23.5% of all hits in the second part of the equation. Obviously there is still room for improvement past 1620 attack.

I'll make it easy for you to find. Take off 100 attack worth of gear, then parse a mob for however long you want. Notice the number of times you hit the magic number, and the number of times you hit for max. Now put that 100 attack back on, and repeat the parse. The number of times you hit the magic number will not change, the number of times you hit for max will increase by several percent. Add another 100 attack, the number of times you hit for your magic number will not change, and yet again the number of times you hit for max will increase by several percent.

There is a noticeable change in the number of times you hit for max each time you increase your attack but the number of hits at your magic number, does not change. Interesting.

Guess what attack value the number of times you hit for max stops increasing? It's a steady increase all the way up to a certain attack value. I'll give you a hint, it stops increasing right around 2150 attack.

This means, if you parse your attack at 2150 and look at the number of times you hit for max then increase your attack to 2350, and look at the number of times you hit for max, there will be almost no change, whereas increasing every value from 1300 to 2150 produced a steady increase. That is diminishing returns on attack.

Why is that number significant? Well, because at that attack value, around 40% of all hits in the upper portion hit for max damage. Directly proportional to the 40% of all hits in the lower portion that hit for max(Magic Number).

I did some experimenting with Mippo's theory that damage is done in two parts.

My assumption was that the first part of the equation (2*damage+DB = max hit) was distributed across damage intervals just like with mobs, with a probability that was fairly equal across all intervals except the low end (where the probability is lower than the average) and the max hit (where the probability is much higher, on the order of 40%). My 2nd assumption was that the second part of the equation (4xdamage-X = max hit) was distributed across all values from 1 to 4xdamage-X-1, and the probability had a negative slope. The 0 and 4xdamage-X values had a higher probability than the average across the other values.

Example: Assuming the weapon's base damage is 31, 0 had a probability of occuring of ~13%, values 1 thru 120 had a probability of ~1% down to ~.4%, and value 121 had a probability of ~5%

If you take the convolution of the first function across the second function, you'll see a histogram that is very close to the charts posted by Kailyn, which confirms to me that Mippo's idea for the formula is on track. Now how the offensive skills determine the probabilities of the two functions is anyone's guess until we get more parses like Kailyn's with corresponding ATK and skill values.
