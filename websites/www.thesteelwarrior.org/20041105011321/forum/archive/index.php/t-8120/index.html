Sassinak
Agreed with the previous posts. The newbie quests usually top out at level 15/ if you do them without assistance and are untwinked. Do them. They are great XP and they guide you to the best places to XP at each level. As you get toward level 20, the amount of money that you earn from monsters will jump a lot, and you'll be able to start buying gear in the bazaar for your non-visible equipment slots and weapons, items that cost a couple of platinum each. At level 20, or earlier if you have friends, you can begin working on crafted armor quests. This set of quests will take you through to level 35 if you do the entire armor set, and will take you to places that are appropriate for earning XP. As you level from 20-30 your income will soar as mobs start dropping platinum pieces, and you'll be able to buy better equipment in the bazaar to replace the gear/weapons you obtained at levels 1-20. This equipment progression can take you smoothly to level 40 or 45 without requiring the charity of others.

Texreb, if you are in Qeynos, go to Blackburrow; I believe that you can still find folks fighting there. If you are in Freeport, you'll soon be able to get orc and dervish camps--a few months ago there were still enough people hunting in the commons & Northern desert of Ro/oasis that getting a group there was possible.

And, if you can find hunting partners, I'd hunt in those zones. Because, really, if you grow up in Qeynos you should know what the elite ledge in Blackburrow is; if you grow up in Freeport, you should spend at least a day or two at the Orc Highway.

The newbie armor quests are good, and I'd do them. You'll find that the drops for some of the stuff you need are potentially frustrating to find, but do as much of it as you can stand.

I seriously doubt you'll be able to get groups together to help you do much of the crafted quests, but some parts are easier than others (bracers, for example). Again, I'd do what I could on these quests.

You might also look at http://www.eqtraders.com/articles/news_page.php
That site is an excellent source for info on tradeskills. In general, tradeskilling is not very efficient (costs you more plat than it's worth) but two might be interesting to look into: fletching and fishing. fishing actually pays for itself in fairly short order, though it's a low-return activity. Fletching costs some money, but almost right away you can make arrows that are better than what you can buy for the same amount of plat. (If you hunt in Blackburrow, you should be able to loot a very low-level bow and some arrows from the gnolls there, by the way).

As you problably already know, there are a good many details in this game, heh. they cannot all be explained in a single post. I'd very much encourage you to ask questions here as you go along. Try using search, of course, but if nothing turns up, post your question and someone around here will try to help.
