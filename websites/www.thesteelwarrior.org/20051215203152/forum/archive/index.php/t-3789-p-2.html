Great thread! I really hope that you can follow this up with more tests to get a solid estimate on aggro per swing based on damage rating and damage bonus. One useful formula you could derive next would be aggro per second:

Aggro per second =
(Aggro per swing / swings per second) + (Aggro per proc / seconds per proc)


Note that seconds per proc is roughly 30 for most main hand weapons. I'm not sure of the off-hand proc rates off the top of my head, but they too are fairly constant. Odds are the maximum normal damage/5 is a damage procs aggro, plus any stun aggro.

Hypothetically, using the damage bonus + damage estimate for two handers and 1.25*damage + damage bonus for one-handers, for instance, at 65th level all one handed weapons would do about 1.25*damage + 13 aggro per swing. So, estimating +10 delay = +one second between swings, aggro per second would be roughly (1.25*dam +13) * 10 / Delay. For a two-hander, it would be (dam + dam bonus) * 10 / Delay.


I'll be very interested to see some follow-up numbers to see how this all pans out.

Playing with the numbers a bit, the formula DB + 11 * MINIMUM seems to be a reasonable fit for all types of weapons. Sounds like the kind of thing someone might have hacked in as a guess of average weapon damage (11 is close to the average of a 1 to 20 roll).

MINIMUM is the minimum possible damage for the weapon ignoring damage bonuses, i.e. RND(DAM/10), so 2 for a 15 dam weapon.

dam delay DB@65 MIN DB+11*MIN

If Kaledan's theory is correct, it suggests a way to help with our agro as compared to casters. The formula given was a reasonable approximation of the damage per swing pre-Kunark, but since, with the improvement in damage tables, skill caps, raw attack, str etc... it *drastically* underestimates damage. Accordingly our melee agro per melee damage ratio has fallen dramatically. As a lvl 65 warrior w/GSo4W and no worn attack I'd estimate my average damage being min*30+DB rather than min*11+DB. Using the min*30 formula would literally double my melee agro generation.

Being more realistic, if the formula holds, then as a rule of thumb melee agro per melee damage (which we might as well call APD, and as warriors want to maximise) is highest for 28+ delay 2handers, followed by dual wielding followed by delay 27- 2handers. VERY slow and VERY fast 1 handers violate this, as might VERY slow 2handers. The DBotW, for example, would absolutely SUCK at melee agro generation for a weapon of it caliber at 57 agro/35 delay on Kaledan's scale or well under a 2 to 1 ratio, worse than, say, 35/20 for a BoC. A GSo4W would do much better at 80/31, and while that discounts an offhand, even an EoE would only have a 22/20 offhanded, and assuming your offhand fired as often as your main (hah) DBotW+EoE would have all but the same melee agro generation as a GSo4W.
