Wednesday February 27th, 2002

  • Well how's that for taking a break from updates? Way too long I know but I have been super busy. Went down to Salt Lake City for a week and a half to cheer on the Canadian Olympic Team. Looks like my cheering worked though Canada finished 4th in medals and soundly defeated the Americans in Hockey. But enough RL stuff. I'm back in EQ land now. My account has expired so I have to get it going again but I should be around by this weekend. The pic of the week features Scalis learning from the Skorpiki masters.

    Friday February 8th, 2002

  • A group of Champs made a foray into Chardok this week. They got fairly deep I'm told and had a number of close calls. Way to kill Sarnaks! Dracyal was also promoted to the rank of officer, congrats to him. Have a good weekend.

    Monday February 4th, 2002

  • Last night saw the Champions raiding the City of Mist to complete Sasraaz and my epics. This was an ambitious plan but definitely a doable one. Unfortunately when we arrived the SK mob Lhanrc wasn't up. Sas was bummed I'm sure but didn't show it. We then went to assault Lord Rak' Ashiir. We had an initial wipeout then a regroup before we were finally able to slay the bastage! We went after Neh'Ashiir after that to complete my epic but that resulted in another wipeout so we called it a night after that. Well Cakaris thought that he'd like to upstage us so with his new copy of Torpor he set off on a rite of passage for all uber lvl 60 Shaman - The Soloing of the Dragon Yeldema. Cakaris soloed the baby dragon and the screenshot of the week proves it. And wait that's not all, I'm not out of quality news yet (that's what happens when I don't update the page for a couple weeks =p) I have officially passed on the torch of leadership of the Champions of Cabilis to Ishy Drackos. Ishy has been my right hand man from day one of this guild and is the perfect replacement for me. He has passion and fire for this game. I have been playing a lot less lately and rather than feeling guilty about it I'd rather just assume an officer/webmaster role. You shouldn't notice too much of a difference in the day to day stuff. If anything there will be more events and the guild will get stronger. I haven't retired though, you'll still see my ugly mug around Cabilis.
