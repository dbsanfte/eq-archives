Welcome to EQ Spoilers guide to Planar Progression. This guide is designed to help anyone progress from a tier 1 guild to a Plane of Time capable guild. This guide will not only guide you through the pre and post-flagging process but also gives information on the gods that you will have to kill along the way, as well as walking you through the sub-quests. This entire guide is built at run-time from our databases, so this information will always remain current and will not go out of date. For a short time this guide has been made free to the public, but eventually it will return to a Flashlink only feature. One quick note, we assume that you know that you always need to hail the Planar Projection or other spawned flag NPC after each boss mob encounter. Players must be in the raid that killed the boss in order to obtain the flag.

NOTE: This page may take a while to load due to the dynamic way that it is built and the size of the content.

Tier 1 Planes

 The following are the easiest of the Planes. Recently the flags required to enter tier 2 planes were removed from all zones except for Drunder. You will still need these flags to progress to the elemental planes and the Plane of Time, however.

Plane of Disease

Search Results Ruins of Lxanvom Entry  Submit Info

This is the quest which allows one to gain entry in to the Ruins of Lxanvom(aka the Crypt of Decay).

It begins by asking Adler Fuirstel in the Plane of Tranquility, "what ward?". This will give you a character flag which you need to open the castle gates.

Put together a raid and head in to the Castle. You must first kill Gryme, the Crypt Guardian. He will drop a key. The key unlocks the door to Grummus' area. This is guarded by Aramin the Spider Guardian who has a loot table.

After Grummus is killed a Planar Projection will spawn. Anyone who was in the raid can then hail the projection to receive the flag for Grummus. After receiving the flag you should then jump down the hole in the next room that leads to the Ruins of Lxanvom.

You must then hail Elder Fuirstel in the Plane of Tranquility who will ask you to kill Bertoxxulous.

Level: 70-70
Sociality: Social
Classification: Boss

Description:
- Spawns in one of several different forms, each with their own AE attacks. This includes damage over time spells for each resistance, knockback and area effect silence randomly.

- Immune to Slow

Do not let him stay in the center room or the spiders will almost certainly blow up and destroy him. Have a monk or shadowknight pull him out of the room and feign death, then memory blur him.

Search Results Trial of Execution  Submit Info

This is one of the trials given by the Tribunals in the Plane of Justice. I am going to give different spoilers for each trial since they are pretty difficult. Completing this trial grants access to the Planes of Valor and Storms.

When you enter this trial your goal is to protect the prisoners from being executed. An executioner will spawn on the far end of the room. Mobs will spawn in gorups of four and will attack you. Until all of the mobs are killed the Executioner will inch closer and closer to the prisoner. If he reaches him then the prisoner is executed and your trial has failed. Each time you kill a mob the executioner will reset to the back of the room until the next wave spawns.

The key here is kiling the mobs that spawn as quickly as possible. The first wave the executioner moves very slowly so you can take your time. By the time you get to the fourth wave you will need to kill the mobs at a superhuman rate in order to prevent the Executioner from killing the prisoner.

After the fourth wave you will then need to kill the executioner. He procs a spell that will lower the targets armor class, and hits hard and fast. He will spawn immediately after the last mob on the fourth wave is killed and attack whoever is near him.

The key here is a high DPS, teamwork and conserving mana in the early portions of the trial.

After killing the Executioner each group member can loot a Mark of Execution which will allow them to complete the of the Mavuin's Case quest.

Keep moving along and you will reach a secret area that Thelin will open. Inside are two Sinister Nightstalkers and a big raven. From there the encounters keep stepping up in numbers and strength. There will be some 6 and 7 mob battles. Be sure to loot the corpses because they will drop Dagger Pieces which you will need.

After killing him hand in all of the pieces that dropped to Thelin. The reward for this quest is Thelin's Dagger.

The Construct of Nightmares also has a random loot table and will drop an item when killed.

After handing in the pieces Terris Thule will spawn but be untargetable. It is not yet known how to kill him but it is assumed that the dagger is bane vs. Terris Thule.

Hail Thelin to be teleported out and have a character flag placed on to you. This character flag allows you to enter the Crypt of Terris Thule, which is entered through the castle entrance guarded by the two Foreboding Guardians.

To complete this flag you must hail Elder Poxbourne and Adroha Jezith in the Plane of Tranquility and then kill Terris Thule in the Crypt of Terris Thule.

Search Results Terris Thule  Submit Info

Level: 70-70
Sociality: Social
Classification: Boss
Known Habitats: Crypt of Terris Thule

Description:
The key to the Terris Thule encounter is knowing what is coming. With a little strategy this is the easiest of the tier 1 bosses.

* At 75% hit points Terris Thule will spawn an army of Butterflies. These don't hit that hard but there are many of them. AE mes DOES work on these though, and so does stun. Best strategy at this point is to have everyone other than the main tank focus on eliminating the butterflies. Then get back to Terris Thule.
* At 50% hit points Terris Thule will do a massive AE dispel. Everyone within range will lose the majority of their buffs. One approach to this problem is just before Terris reaches 50% to have everyone other than the main tank, healers and a couple of nukers move out of range. Then nuke him below 50% and once the dispel has gone off to rush back in to the room and rebuff those who need it. Another appraoch is have MGB buffs ready for as soon as this occurs. I would recommend the first scenario, but keeping MGB ready, just in case.
* At ~35% the four gargoyles in Terris' chamber will spawn. These hit hard, are unsnareable, unmessable... but are stunnable. Rather than engaging these it is recommending that you have paladins kite them with stuns, or by shadowknights with aggro spells. They do not move quickly. While the gargoyles are up it is important to make sure that nobody drops too low on hit points, or they will get low hit point aggro... and the gargoyles hit hard.
* Hits for up to 620 per attack.
* Single target proc does up to 720 points of magic based direct damage.
* Flurries.
* Terris Thule is slowable with magic slow.


Tier 2 Planes

 The tier 2 gods range from incredibly easy (Vallon Zek) to the difficult bottlenecks (Bertoxxulous and Rallos Zek). All of these gods other than Rallos Zek are required to enter the Tower of Solusek Ro with the exception of Aerin`Dar, who is not required if you use the 85% flagged rule on Mithaniel Marr. You will need to kill Aerin`Dar with the majority of your raid though, so for the most part he is a requirement. Rallos Zek is not required for the Tower of Solusek Ro, but is required for the Elemental Planes.

This guide considers the Plane of Torment as a Tier 3 plane because you need to have killed Bertoxxulous in order to do the flag in order for Saryrn.

 
 

Drunder, Plane of Tactics

The Plane of Tactics is the only tier zone that still requires a flag or a key quest to enter. It is the home to three gods (in order of difficulty): Vallon Zek, Tallon Zek, Rallos Zek. You can kill all of the gods here with only having the key, but you will need the Manaetic Behemoth flag to progress through the planes. You are also going to want to complete the Zek Bane Weapons quest at least for your main tanks so that they will be able to tank Rallos Zek. This quest requires that you kill Tagrin as well. Vallon and Tallon Zek are among the easiest gods in the Planes of Power. Rallos Zek is bar none the toughest encounter pre-elemental planes. He is the bottleneck for elemental access.

The Rallos Zek encounter is by far the most challenging pre-elemental event in the game. This is the type of event that will take numerous attempts to complete. You must be prepared to spend a good period of time learning each portion of this event. Even though you have likely killed Tallon and Vallon Zek before, these versions are different, and arguably difficult.

The area of this event is the center hall on the top floor of Drunder. This is not down in the pit area. Fight your way up to the staircase and you will come to a T intersection. If you follow the T it makes a square with branches split by double doorways. There are also a couple of branches to the northwest and southwest, but you can ignore those.

To begin the event you must killed two named Decorin's in this area. They must both be killed within a few minutes of one another. One is on the southeast corner guarding the doorway to the large chamber. The second is to the northeast guarding the entrance on the other side.

Different guilds handle the pulling and killing of these mobs in different ways. You will need to decide what approach is best suitd for your guild. Some pull these mobs to the steps and kill both. Others pull to one side of the tunnel or the other. Most guilds do mesmerize at least one of the named Decorins though so that you can kill them back to back.

Once they are both killed the timer to kill Tallon and Vallon Zek begins ticking. You have around 30 minutes to kill both of these mobs. Tallon Zek spawns in the southeastern corridor. He is similar to the "real" Tallon Zek in the zone with his PBAE resistance debuff, his powerful AE that centers around the target and slows them. His damage output is also similar (heavy). He is not rooted though, and does not shadowstep. As a result your going to have to tank him. Holding aggro on him is difficult for a warrior to do, be warned in advance.

Vallon Zek spawns in the center room (between the two double doorways that were guarded by the named Decorins. He has a heavier damage output than the other version and hits for up to 1053 per attack. He has the same aggro reduction and increasing AE as the other version, so be prepared for some aggro problems. The difference is that he will only split once and only in to two pieces. One of the pieces (the real one) will be unmessable. The other can be messed.

After conquering both of the little Zeks your timer is reset and you have more time to work with again. You must now engage the fake Rallos Zek (named simply Rallos Zek). This version is similar to the real Rallos Zek, but without the adds.

He hits for up to 1074 per attack, and has an AE damage over time and Aggro Reduction spell. He is slowable with magic slow. This makes holding aggro on him difficult. You can avoid this though by meleeing or casting at maximum range and with some careful placement. You are on a time limit here so you need to have a wizard cast AE teleport to zone in as this fight begins, but do not accept the box yet. When Rallos is at 60-70% he will depop and spawn in the pit as Rallos Zek the Warlord. This is the real Rallos Zek. At this point everyone needs to accept the teleport box so you can quickly regroup and engage.

Once he is engaged adds will begin spawning throughout the pit. Some guilds kill the adds, others kite the adds. Some are messable, those who are not are snareable and rootable. Crowd control is key here, but the longer the fight goes on the more adds will spawn and the more difficult this process will get. As a result you need to drop Rallos Zek as quickly as possible. If you are kiting the adds then the longer the fight stretches out, the lower your chances become. If you are killing the adds then you will likely just have a single person tanking Rallos Zek, with a set of healers and you will rely on charmed pets to do the majority of the damage. The rest of the raid will handle the adds.

It is not uncommon for people to kill Rallos Zek and then wipe to the adds. For this reason it is recommended that you have players bound outside who can cast spirit of eagle and invisibility. The planar projection here sees through invisibility so you can hail him while invis. Some of the wraiths see through invibility though so be careful. while doing this.

Search Results Agnarr the Storm Lord  Submit Info

This is the Agnarr the Storm Lord event. In order to reach this event you must have the at least one member of your raid who has completed the Symbol of Torden quest. You then use the Symbol on the electrical pillar in the courtyard area to unlock the door. After this point for a short period anyone else can walk through the electricity to be ported to the Agnarr area. If anyone without the key clicks this pillar the Gargoyles will come to life and kill them, so only the player with the key should click in.

The Agnarr event unfolds in three different levels.

The first level is fire themed and features a named boss, a few guards and three portals. The portals will spawn additional mobs throughout the fight, so they must be destroyed. Wizards can disintegrate these portals, so they are a huge plus. If you have no wizards with disintegrate (gasp) then you can attack the portals manually. The boss of this level is Evynd Firestorm who has a DoT that is a fire resistant debuff and is a curse(so it can be cured). It is generally a good idea to kill the portals first, then the adds then the boss. You of course will need to have your main tank on the boss during this time, with a ch chain. Some players will mes the adds and kill the boss first. Either way works.

After clearing all of the mobs on this level you hail Askr the Lost and follow his dialogue to port up to the next level. Each person will need to speak to him.

The second level is meteor-themed and features a boss, some adds and three additional portals. Just as the first level you can disintegrate the portals. The boss on this level is Emmerik Skyfury. He has a nasty area effect spell which does up to 1500 points of AE damage. He also has an area effect silence attack. Once again you should kill the portals first, then the adds then the boss.

After completing this level you can again speak to Askr the Lost. Ask him "what storm". Only the raid leader needs to do this. A portal will then open behind Askr. Stand underneath the portal and target it. The hit the 'U' (Use) key to be ported up top.

The third level features Agnarr himself and more portals. The center portal on this level can not be destroyed.

Agnarr hits for up to 1400 per attack, but is magic slowable. He has a point blank AE which stuns the target, spins them around, does 800 points of direct damage and another 1440 points of damage over 1.8 minutes. This spell is unresistable but only has a radius of 50 feet. Agnarr is snareable and can be kited until you take care of all the portals and adds to begin this event.

During the fight Agnarr will spawn the four mini bosses(Jolur Sandstorm, Ekil Thundercall, Oljin Stormtide and Hibdin Cyclone). He will spawn one at 99%, one at 74%, one at 49% and one at 24%.

The first boss is going to come immediately, while your fighting all of the adds and the portals. The most common tactic to handle this is to have some players assigned to kiting Agnarr and the tower boss while everyone else kills the portals. Then kill the adds as you have been doing, then work on the tower boss. After that everyone should get on to Agnarr until the next mini-boss spawns, and rinse and repeat.

You can avoid the AE with careful placement. If you place the healers at the bototm of the stairs you can then pull Agnarr close to there around the corner. This will block the AE from healers or others. You must be careful though not to push him in to them. When deaing with the tower bosses you will want to keep them seperated from Agnarr to avoid low hit point aggression.

The final mini-boss (Hibdin Cyclone) spawns at 24% and should be kited until after Agnarr is killed. This is because he is unslowable and will require some healing power to kill. Take him down after killing Agnarr.

After killing Agnarr you must run upstairs and tell Karana that you 'will walk the path of the fallen' to receive your flag.

Search Results Crazed Norrathians  Submit Info

This is the easiest of the three Halls of Honor trials. It gives a flag that is required(along with the other three trials and the Aerin`Darr flags) for entrance to the Temple of Marr.

To initiate this trial you must speak to Aleksan Garn. He is found in the left basement closest to the zone in. Once it is activated A Custodian of Marr will spawn and all mobs in the three rooms near the trials area will despawn. You must kill him to initiate the trial. He isn't too hard but does have a lot of hit points. His purpose is so guilds can't activate the trial simply to prevent other guilds from doing trials.

Once he dies maidens will spawn in each of the three rooms in the basement. Six Crazed Norrathians will also spawn in those rooms and will try to kill the maidens. You can not allow this to happen or the trial will fail. Likewise if any of the Crazed Norrathians are not aggroed during this trial(or if they gate) you will lose the trial. Once all of the Crazed Norrathians in a given room are killed a boss for that room will spawn. The bosses are pure melee but they do hit very hard and are unsnareable. The first boss hits for up to 1153 per attack.

The popular stategy here is to place kiters in each of the three rooms beforehand. When the custodian spawns have them all ready to engage the mobs and take aggro. Fight the custodian in the middle of the room with only melees out there. When he dies the Norrathians will spawn where he is being killed, you do not want this to be near casters. The minute the Crazed Norrathians spawn begin running in circles kiting and have one player(preferably a shadowknight or monk) pick the Norrathians off one at a time and bring them to be killed on the sides of the room. When the last is killed and the boss spawns, it will not aggro, so you have time to prep for him.

Once one boss is done, move to the hallway of the next room and begin pulling those guards one at a time until you have reached the final boss.

Tower of Solusek Ro

This is where things really start to branch. In order to gain entrance to the Tower of Solusek Ro you need to have killed all tier 1-3 gods in other planes with the exception of Rallos Zek and Agnarr the Storm Lord. Once you have done this it is time to gain entrance to the Tower. Your flags have likely been done out of order at certain points, so we will guide you through the entire process.

Speak to Seer Mal Nae`Shi in the Plane of Knowledge. She is found in a building near the stone to the Plane of Tranquility. You will first need to sit down and then say, "what guided meditation" followed by "unlock my memories". This fixes your flags if they are out of order.

Next you need to speak to Grand Librarian Maelin in the library. You will need to ride the elevator up to the top floor to find him. Tell him that "I have new information" followed by "I have new lore". Keep doing this until he gives you access to the Tower of Soulsek Ro. If you have not yet killed Rallos Zek the message here will be that he wants you to destroy Rallos Zek the Warlord. If you have already killed both Rallos Zek and Agnarr you will be given (by repeating the same lines) access to the Planes of Air, Earth and Water at this point.

To receive access to Doomfire you must kill Solusek Ro. This is a tricky process as you need to have killed all of the mini-bosses in the zone to gain access to his chamber (Arylixir, Rizlona, Jiva, Dresolik, Xuzl). After killing them you receive the flag by using the teleportal behind their room. It is possible to do an 85% raid at this point, but it is a tricky multi-step process. Solusek Ro is not as difficult as many of the other gods required to this point, so once you gain access he should be no problem.
