Character Creation:

The character creation of the magician is one of the most hotly debated topics within the class' community. The choices are Human, Erudite, Dark Elf, High Elf or Gnome. I will start right away by saying that all of these races will make a fine magician, and it is sometimes quite a lot better to make your decision of race based more on your personal preference than for statistics. Each has its advantages and disadvantages. Humans are the only race that will not make an excellent magician for a few simple reasons. First of all, they don't have any higher than average starting statistics. For a magician, Intelligence is the most important stat of them all and Humans start with the least of all of the options. Secondly they are blind at night, possessing no night vision whatsoever. Although this is not a huge deal, at the beginning of the game you will find that this is extremely frustrating. Sharing that lack of night vision is the Erudite. However the "high man" counters this disadvantage by starting with the highest inherent intelligence stat of all of the available classes. Statistically, this is the best magician to go with. Another disadvantage that must be noted is that the erudite starting zone is what most would define as horrible and the rest would agree is not ideal. Next in line is the Dark Elf who has the number two spot for intelligence and also has strong agility stats. Furthermore, Dark elves have Ultravision which lets them see almost perfectly in the dark. A huge disadvantage of this race is that they are hated in most of the world. Gnomes have slightly less intelligence than the Dark Elves, but are good. They are small creatures (which is good if you play on a PvP server) and make very good magicians (my gnome mage retired at level 56). Lastly, there is the high elven magician. Although their Intelligence stat isn't exceptional, it is noticeably above average and the royal elves also enjoy the benefits of high charisma (make more money at lower levels selling stuff). They are very well liked around the world except in evil towns, despite their arrogance. They have night vision and have Greater Faydark as their newbie zone -- one of the best in my opinion.

So the question becomes which of these choices to pick. I would suggest either a Dark Elf, a Gnome or a High Elf, although the human and Erudite can do very well. Humans and Erudites share one feature that I cannot stand -- blindness! Humans have sub-par stats and while Erudites blow away all other classes in terms of Intellect, they start way out of the way of everything and enjoy very poor stats other than intelligence and wisdom.

One of the most important features of character creation is picking a race which will fit your and your character's persona and that you will enjoy. If you enjoy being a bit on the evil side then Dark Elf is a great choice and is perhaps the best all around choice. If you like being just and good, or love role playing characters that spout off holier than thou speeches randomly for kicks then a High Elf will suite you very well. If you want to be a neutral, fun, tiny character (with a big nose) then a gnome would be a great choice as well

Stats wise, no matter what race you are, place all the points you can into INT and then place the rest into STR. The reason we place the rest into Strength is because really, the other stats aren't going to matter very much. A higher strength will help you carry slightly more for the rest of the game and will help you hit a bit harder when you are whacking newbie monsters with a stick / staff / club / dagger / insert choice caster weapon here.

The Magician's Role:

Level 1 to 4:

Levels one to four are the same for most classes. You will find yourself a weapon, cast to pull monsters and whack / cast them down. Rinse and repeat. This will be a very quick portion of your life.

Level 4 to 11:

These are some of the toughest levels for a magician. They have pets and can begin their careers as excellent soloers. However, their pets haven't yet come into their own yet and are still relatively weak. You will still have to "help" them by tanking a bit.

Level 12 to 50:

You have bind. Go out and explore the world. During this stage you are arguably the highest damage dealer in a group (non-twinked). You combine a very strong pet with your attack spells being the second strongest in the game. In a pinch you can take down any monster you are fighting fairly handily. You are an excellent soloer in these levels and in groups act as a damage dealer and utility caster (most of those summons are useless. A few are very good) .

Level 51 to 60:

At the higher levels the magician's role changes and is not always completely clear. Wizards pull far ahead of the magician at higher levels and this continues up until level 60. Druids start to be able to go nuke for nuke with the magician at these levels (apparently Verant decided they should be the second best nukers in the game, not us) and to make this worse the power of the magician pet starts going down. While before the magician pet was an extremely strong creature it becomes more of a tactical aid than a reliable tank. The mage epic weapon is extremely hard to get, however the spell you receive allows you to create a weapon which summons the epic pet -- a very formidable force. There is a huge power difference between a magician that is epic and one who is not.

Magician PvE Tactics:

Level 1 to 3:

There is no real tactics for this level other than where to fight. For High Elves and Gnomes the best place to go at level 2 is the first area of Butcherblock near the greater feydark zone line. There are skunks here who are level 2 and will bring you're level up quickly. They also drop glands that are worth about a gold and a half. By the time you reach level 4 you will have enough money to buy all of your spells. Dark Elves and humans should hunt in the Commonlands, mostly the eastern ones where there are lots of low level critters of all levels to fry, burn and smack. Erudites should get to another place (such as the Freeport or Felwithe areas) as soon as they can. Friendly wizards and druids should be able to make this an easy prospect.

Level 4 to 12:

You get your pet, and these levels you will start to see how powerful an ally this pet will be. Make hotkeys for the important pet commands as follows:

/pet attack or /pet kill -- these commands will make your pet attack your target.

/pet follow me -- this will make your pet follow you until it is aggroed by a monster. When the agro is up it will return to you

/pet sit down -- this will make the pet stationary and it will regenerate health quicker. If it is aggroed it will defend itself and when the fight is finished it will sit wherever it ends up.

/pet back off -- this attempts to back your pet off a target. This can be used when a creature is being mezzed, when it is taking too much aggro, etc.

/pet report health -- the pet will tell you its health status as a percentage

/pet get lost -- the pet commits suicide. Very sad event. Casting reclaim energy is often preferable.

Play with your pet and you will learn its strengths and weaknesses. At these levels you will find that you will need to help your pet out by taking damage sometimes.

Level 12 to 50:

These levels are very soloable but you will begin to find that grouping is a lot more fun and productive. You are a very strong member of the group and will probably be doing most of the damage in the fight. Gradually leave home and go out and explore the world. You will have a lot of fun playing in different settings with different people! Find a guild! Do some quests. This will be the majority of your character's leveling life! Your tactics in these levels will be to balance your pet's attack with your casting skills. You will want your pet to keep the monster's attention while you can nuke enough to kill it before it kills your pet. In a group situation your pet represents the majority of your damage output. It can be used as a crowd control mechanism and your mana is best used in emergency situations. You will summon bandages and food for your group and will become the best friend of rangers everywhere with your summon arrows spell.

Learning to control your pet is of great importance because in the later levels a stray pet in a dungeon will get you and your friends killed. Many groups out and out ban pets in high level situations because of this and you will have to either suck it up or convince them that you will be able to control it well enough.

Level 50 to 60:

These levels are similar to the upper 40's and although you will see a decline in your relative power you will still be a strong force. You will have a variety of useful new pet features (the backstabbing water pet is very very nice) and some class-defining utility spells (Call of the Hero is a good example). You will live and learn in these levels. Getting your epic weapon will be a challenge, but will arguably be a necessary challenge. Although you can get it as early as level 46 this is as good as impossible unless you have a large amount of sway with an extremely powerful guild.

Magician PvP Tactics:

Level 1 to 3:

PvP at this level isn't PvP. A bunch of level 4 mages / wizards / etc with doubly strong spells just kill you. Ignore them and get yourself to level 4.

Level 4 to 7:

Send your pet in and nuke your heart out. Try to be hard to find and if versus a melee move around and let your pet tag them. when the melee fights your pet nuke em more.

Level 8 to 43:

You now have nukes, bolts and shocks. The shocks will interrupt enemy casting and the lower level ones are hard to resist when you get up in level. A basic strategy versus a caster is to use a low level shock to interrupt their spell every time and let your pet do most of the damage. when they are close to dead finish them off with the big nukes.

Remember that with Bolts you have range on your side. If you are initiating the attack, open up with a few long range bolts. A nice thing to do is to face away and fire the nukes. you can get a few off before the first hits and it could be enough to bring them down to a dangerous HP level. Then just send your pet in to finish them off.

Level 44 to 60:

Various strategies are going to apply here. By now pets take a long time to kill things (although vs. casters pets can still be very effective, especially the earth pet's root (if it goes through) and the 50+ water pet's backstab

Resistances, especially magic resistance are very high meaning your shocks will be less effective. Bolts are still effective. Scars of Sigil is IMHO one of the best PvP spells in the game. Use the aforementioned aiming away bolt trick with this fast caster... cast 4-5 of them and by the time they know what's happening they will be dead.

General:

You will learn the best strategies as you play and every situation is different. Mages will not be the most successful in a duel situation. As a PK'er they are superb and they can defend themselves in a pinch. Never neglect resistances even if your opponents are low. Remember to always cast your resistance buff and always try to malise your opponent down. As long as you are adaptable and learn what spells work best against which opponents (you will find on the Zek's that you are always fighting the same people), you will do well in PvP as a magician.

Magician Epic Quest:

This is the magician's epic quest. The spoiler is given in no nonsense format.

It also requires that you have already completed the Shovel of Ponz and Broom of Trilon quests(see related quests).

1) Visit Rykas in Lake Rathetear(+950, -650). Say that you "wish to learn the tale of Magi'kot" and he will give you a Token of Mastery.

3) Kill the Bloodthirsty Ghoul in Lower Guk and loot the Torn Page of Magi'kot.

4) Kill Enraged Dread Wolves in Kithicor Forest(night spawn) and loot the Torn Page of Magi'kot.

5) Kill Tentacle Terrors in Unrest and loot the Torn Page of Magi'kot.

6) Give the Token of Mastery to Jahsohn Aksot in West Commonlands(he patrols).

This was meant to give some dialogue but we have already done his task so simply hand him the three Torn Pages of Magi'kot that we collected and he will give you Words of Magi'Kot.

7) Kill Fairy Guards in Lesser Faydark until you get Power of Earth.

8) Kill Elementals in Solusek's Eye until you get the Power of Fire.

9) Kill Raveners in Kaesora until you get Power of Water.

10) Kill Gypsy Dancers in Mistmoore until you receive Power of Wind.

11) Give the Power of Earth, Fire, Water and Wind to Walnan in Lesser Faydark. He spawns near +1850, +625.

He will give you Power of the Elements.

12) Now head to Najena and kill the Magicians until you receive the four torn pages of earth, fire, water and wind.

13) Turn the four pages in to Akksstaff in Najena. He will give you Words of Mastery.

14) Now you turn in the Power of Elements, Words of Magi'kot and the Words of Mastery to Rykas in Lake Rathetear.

He will give you Power of the Orb.

15) Kill Undertow in Kedge Keep until you get a Blazing Wand.

16) Kill Neh'Ashir in the City of Mist to get a Torch of the Elements.

17) Kill named wurms in Burning Woods until one drops Burning Embers.

18) Turn the Blazing Wand, Burning Embers, Power of the Orb and Torch of the Elements in to Jennus Lyklobar(wanders around) in Skyfire Mountains to receive the Element of Fire.

19) Kill Slixin Klex in Burning Woods and loot the Dirt of Underfoot.

20) Kill Magi P'tasa in the Plane of Hate until you receive a Staff of Elemental Mastery: Earth.

21) Hand the Broom of Trilon, Dirt of Underfoot, Shovel of Ponz and Staff of Elemental Mastery: Earth to Tiblner Milnik in Firiona Vie.

You will receive the Element of Earth.

22) Kill Trabul Earthstrider in East Karana for the Rain of Karana.

23) Kill Phinigel Autropos in Kedge Keep for the Staff of Elemental Mastery: Water.

24) Kill Captain Rottgrime in The Overthee for the Tears of Erolisi.

25) Hand in the Rain of Karana, Staff of Elemental Mastery: Water and Tears of Erolisi to Jinalis Andir in Dagnor's Cauldron(-450, -2050) and he will give you the Element of Water.

26) Clear the 7th island in the Plane of Sky and pray that something drops the Crown of Elemental Mastery.

27) Kill elements at the entrance of the Hole until one drops an Elemental Binder.

28) Kill Quillmane in South Karana and hope for a Pegasus Feather Cloak. Quillmane is a very rare spawn.

29) Hand the Crown of Elemental Mastery, Elemental Binder and Pegasus Feather Cloak in to Kihun Solstin in the Plane of Air and he will give you the Element of Air.

30) Turn in the Elements of Earth, Wind, Fire and Water to the Master of Elements in the quest room of the Plane of Air(spawns after getting your Element of Air) for your Summon Orb of Mastery Scroll.

Scribe and cast it for your Orb of Mastery.

Magician Spell Discussion / Analysis:

In this section I will list the important' spells of the level in the order that they should be bought. A brief description will also be included. The first 3 or 4 spells in each level category should be purchased right away.

Level 1:

(i) reclaim energy - get mana back by "unsummoning" your pet (necissary later in the game)

(ii) Summon Food - summon a loaf of break

(iii) Summon Water - summon a flask of water

Level 4:

(i) Elementalkin Earth - Your first earth pet. As you go to level 7ish HP becomes very important wiht your pet, and the earth pet will serve you best in that way

(ii) Burn - your next damage spell. DD for 11-13dmg

(iii) Gate - return to your bind point

(iv) Summon Brass Choker - summon a focus item to conserve components (useful as you summon a lot of pets)

(v) Summon Bandages - makes you a melee class's good friend

(vi) Other Elementalkin's - Your first water, fire and air pets

(vii) Summon Wisp - summons a lightstone. Higher priority for Erudite and Human magicians.

(viii) Sense Summoned - helpful for later when fighting elementals is very efficient.

(ix) Fire Flux - First AE spell. Not necisary at this level.

Level 8: (Level 8 is a very important level to get all the spells up to (x)-(xi))

(i) Elementaling Earth - Still the best pet for the level IMHO. HP's are still paramount especially considering killing yellows is still very possible (air with high agility will still get hit if the monster is higher level)

(ii) Flame Bolt - your first bolt spell. Does a max of 47 DMG.

(iii) Shock of Blades - your first shock spell. Does about 35 DD. Also interupts casting.

(iv) Invisibility - lets you be invisible. VERY IMPORTANT

(v) Lesser shielding - More HP, More AC, More MR.

(vi) Renew Elements - Pet heal for 50hp

(vii) Staff of Tracing - give it to your pet to ensure slightly higher damage output.

(viii) Shield of Fire - Damage shield for 5-6 dmg. Extremely important to cast on your pet.

(ix) Summon Linnon Mantle - focus item to increase buff duration (saves a bit of mana)

(x) Other Pet Spells

(xi) Eye of Zomm - scouting eye. Useful for exploring unknown areas. No longer is aggroed.

(xii) Dimentional Pocket - Summons a 4-slot bag. Advantage is that everything in it becomes weightless. Disadvantage is that when you log or go LD everything inside it goes *poof*. Use with extreme caution.

Level 12:

(i) Elemental of your Choice - Start playing around with them. Find one that fits your playing style. You will want to start buyign all of your Elementals

(ii) Bind Affinity - changes your bind point. Essential to travelling

(iii) Burnout - pet only buff which adds haste, str and ac

(iv) Ward Summoned - Efficient DD spell vs. summoned monsters. (35-41 dmg)

(v) Other Elemental Spells

(vi) Summon Tarnished Bauble - decreases agro on your DD spells by ~10%.

(vii) Other spells in the level (none are particularly important)

Level 16: (By this level you should be buying every spell every level -- just a matter of the order)

(i) Minor Summoning of Choice - Again get your favourite but play will all of them. By now your pet should represent a good portion of your damage output and you shouldn't be going anywhere without one.

(ii) Shock of Flame - Newest dammage spell. Good stuff at 97dmg, especially considering there were no all purpose DD's last spell round.

(iii) Shielding - More HP, More AC, more MR. Again, very good idea to have this on ya at all times.

(iv) Phantom Leather - increases HP regen slightly, and improves AC.

(v) See Invisible - finally see your air pet at all times!

(vi) Summon Heatstone - for blindies this gives you infravision. You are now caught up eyesight wise. Everyone else... don't bother

(vii) Summon tiny ring - Focus item to reduce casting time on some spells (non-dd)

(viii) Other spells this level. - Misc. usefullness.

Level 20:

(i) Lesser Summonings - Yes. All of them. From now on.

(ii) Bolt of Flame - 148-152 dmg isn't too shoddy!

(iii) Renew Summoning - 220hp restored to your pet can help.

(iv) Shield of Flame - Damage sheild for 8-9dmg and a +15SV Fire to boot! Cast on you, your pet, your best friend and your worst enemy. Love for all!

(v) Elemental Shield - Boost Cold and Fire Resistance up by 20 each. You are going to be fighting tougher and tougher casting mobs as you get higher up in levels and without good resistances you are toast.

(vi) Expulse Summoned - 98 dmg vs. Summoned monsters. Sure you can do a lot more damage with your new bolt, but Expulse is a lot more efficient than Bolt of Flame. Also, Bolt of Flame won’t do much good against monsters in say, Solusek’s Eye.

(vii) Summon item spells - There are a few excellent ones this level. Summon waterstone is a good spell to get because if fighting in a water area having an instant enduring breath is a lifesaver. There is a new focus item to increase your damage output (Jade Bracelet) and you get your first arrow summoning spell. Arrow summoning is wicked for your ranger friends! They will appreciate the free arrows. The summoning spells which are questionable are the spear and armor summonings. Both of these are good only if you are going into a newbie zone. The leather armor is nice to give away to some helpless newbie, but it unfortunately poofs when they log. The spear is laughable but looks cool when wielded by your pet.

(viii) Rain of Fire - your first alright Rain spell. For those hunting multiple orcs in Highpass this spell could be useful. If you have several monsters very mad at a tank unload this ON THE TANK. Yes. On the tank. This will do 2 things. A) it will reduce agro because you aren’t directly casting on a monster. B) you will hit all monsters surrounding him. This spell has the potential to do about 600 damage (assuming all 3 waves hit 4 monsters for full damage).

Level 24(From now on there are some spells which you will need to research. I will not go into the details of researching here, as that is for another guide. You can either do the research yourself or get a higher level mage to do it for you and pay them)(Another note about level 24 is that your spells will look different, having many more particle effects than before. Yay!):

(i) Summonings - Yes, again. All of them. Fire will be tougher to get because it is research.

(ii) Shock of Spikes - This new Shock spell is the next in your line of good damage spell-interrupting DD’s. It will hit for approx. 172-176 and should become your staple attack spell.

(iii) Major Shielding - the next in your line of self-buffing spells. This one, like its predecessors adds more HP and more AC as well as better MR than the ones before it.

(iv) Everfount / Cornucopia - These two spells summon 20 water and 20 food respectively. It makes your life oh so much easier than casting summon spells 20 times every time you enter the game. A must have for any mage.

(v) Malise - This spell decreases all resistances but disease by 20 points. It is a very useful spell, however it also generates a lot of agro. Use it with care, but learn to use it effectively often!

(vi) Summon silver choker - This is another focus item to conserve expendable components. Although it once again only gives a 10% chance, it works on all spells from level 49 and lower.

(vii) Flame Flux - this is another AE spell that magicians get. This type is called a Point Blank or PBAE spell. This means that it hits all monsters close to the magician. This is very dangerous when used against a group of mobs because they could easily all turn on you. Best used to finish off multiple weak mobs or to impress newbies by killing 12 bats at once!

(viii) Staff of Runes - This is a useless weapon being 9 dmg / 36 delay. However, the nice thing about it is the charge of cancel magic that comes along with it. If you think you might need an emergency manaless cancel magic then you should probably have one of these sitting in your inventory as a backup. Heck its not lore (as far as I can remember) so summon a D-pocket and stick a few of them in there. Will be weightless and full of summoned items that don’t matter much! :)

Level 29:

(i) Greater Summonings - Get em all (getting the point yet?) Earth and Water are both research only so you will have to pay a bit more for them.

(ii) Burnout II - an upgrade to burnout. This raises the AC, Strength and Haste of your pet by significantly more than the first burnout.

(iii) Inferno Shield - Next damage shield. It does 13-15 damage over the time that you will be using this shield. It also adds 20 to fire resistance to boot. Putting this in perspective, if your tank(s) get hit 30 times in a fight (very reasonable at this level) this spell will do just under 400 “bonus” damage to the monster. For 80 mana this is a very efficient spell.

(iv) Dismiss Summoned - 140 to 162 damage versus summoned opponents. This spell becomes important because it is the only DD spell you get at this spell level. This means that the lvl 29 to lvl 34 stretch is most efficiently done fighting summoned monsters. This is an extremely effective spell if you can find a good number of opponents that are vulnerable to this spell.

(v) Phantom Chain - This is the next spell in the line of Self Only AC buffing, regeneration increasing spells that magicians get. This one increases the AC from between 12 and 14 and increases health regeneration by 2HP per tick (6 seconds)

(vi) Expedience - This spell is a J-Boots buff for pets. Very helpful in making sure your pet doesn’t’ get lost too far behind you.

(vii) Rain of Spikes - along with earth pet’s root this spell is possibly your ticket through level 29 to 34. If all three waves hit your target, you will do 273 damage to the monster. (91 per wave). Very risky, but possibly necessary as you go through these difficult levels.

(viii) Summon item spells - As normal there are a few useful summons and a few not-so-useful summons. Summon Coldstone summons an item which grants ultravision to the holder of it. This is particularly useful for blind races. Leather mantle summons the upgraded buff duration focus item and phantom chain allows you to summon up a set of chain armour for your favourite newbie. The Chain is also helpful for chain-classes during corpse recovery in a pinch. Finally, sword of runes. Only real use of the sword is to give to your pet if you are fighting summoned creatures.

Level 34:

(i) Minor Conjurations - All of them (once again!). Fire and Air need to be reasearched. Sorry :(

(ii) Blaze - Finally a new, rather powerful DD spell to bring us back up to date. It hits for a max of 276-295 and is fire based.

(iii) Cinder Bolt - 319 to 336 damage max makes us a damage force to be reckoned with. This is a nice upgrade to get, although it is disappointing that it is also a fire based spell.

(iv) Greater Shielding - The next upgrade. You know the drill by now. More HP, more AC, more MR

(v) Nullify Magic - Upgrade to cancel magic. Basically a stronger version with a better chance to do its thing with less casts

(vi) Summon staff of symbols - Another terrible weapon with a semi-useful effect. This staff has 4 charges of see invisible and the mana works out to be cheeper than casting see invisible 4 times. Not to mention that you don’t have to go and re-memorize the spell, etc. Also the weight isn’t a problem with…

(vii) Dimentional Hole - a 6 slot bag that reduces weight to zero. This is good for keeping your summoned staves and such and not having to worry about their weights or them popping. Also a good place to store food that you summon! :)

(viii) Summon Shiny Bauble - Another very important focus item, this one is the upgrade version of the agro-reducer.

(ix) Summon Shard of the Core - your replacement for the coldstone. Does same thing but adds poison and cold resistances.

(x) Monster Summoning I - this spell is next to useless. It summons a pet that is worse than the elementals you summon. The only plus side is that it takes a random skin from the zone’s texture memory and makes that the pet. This means that you can have a pet that looks like a Sand Giant, Griffon or measily Rat. Your choice.

Level 39:

(i) Lesser Conjurations - Air and Fire are research only. I don’t need to tell you that you should get all 4 do I?

(ii) Barrier of Combustion - damage shield for 18 to 20 dmg. 25 fire resistance. Sweet stuff. If your tank gets hit 40 times in a fight (very likely at these levels) this spell will do a minimum of 720 damage just as a bonus. For 100 mana that is sweet!

(iii) Expel Summoned - 261 to 273 max damage allows very efficient killing of summoned monsters. However, this is once again the only DD spell you get this level. Making the level 39 to 44 stretch very difficult.

(iv) Summon Companion - Summons your pet to your location. Great spell when your pet is lost. Also good if you want to summon your pet at the beginning of a dungeon, tell it to stay there and then summon it when you are where you want to fight.

(v) Rain of Lava - your next rain spell. Same as always, more damage. This one does 516 in 3 waves (172 dmg per wave)

(vi) Summon Twisted ring - Summons a casting time reducing spell. Same as before

(vii) Summon Ring of Flight - Summons a ring with 2 charges of Levitate

(viii) Summon Phantom Plate - Summons a set of plate armor for a corpse run or to make your favorite newbie look decked out.

(ix) Dagger of symbols - It summons a dagger with a few resists on it. Give it to your pet…. Won’t make a big difference but every 5 resistance points counts.

(x) Flame Arc - your next PBAE spell. Once again it is very good for impressing newbies doing 171 to 181 dmg to everything surrounding you. You can now efficiently impress newbies in crushbone. Congrats!

Level 44:

(i) All your conjuration pets. Only Fire is buyable - you’ll have to research the others.

(ii) Shock of Swords - your next spell in your summon shock line of spells, and it’s a beauty. 600 direct damage. It’s a huge leap in power for your character.

(iii) Arch Shielding - Next in the line of HP / AC / MR buffs.

(iv) Elemental Maelstrom - A DD / Dot spell for mages. Good for adding that extra little damage kick. It does 250 damage and then 350 more in 5 ticks (30 seconds)

(v) Elemental Armor - Upgraded fire and cold defense spell. This one gives 30 to each save.

(vi) Phantom Plate - Next in the line of HP regen and AC buffs. This one does 3hp per tick healing and increases AC by 17-18

(vii) Malisement - Decreases all saves but disease by 37 to 40! Huge spell. Lots of agro though.

(viii) Summon Opal bracelet - increases damage output on spells level 54 and lower by 10%. So, your shock of swords is suddenly doing 660 dmg instead of 600.

(ix) Summon Modulating Rod - Summons a rod with a health to mana charge. Helpful in a very desperate pinch when a small amount of mana is needed to finish the job. These items are Lore so only one can be given to each member of the party.

Level 46 Spell (This spell is received as the final reward for the magician epic quest above):

Summon Orb - Summons the orb of mastery allowing a magician to summon the epic pet. Just for kicks I want to see a level 46 actually get this. The pet they summoned would be red to them hehe. This is quite probably THE defining magician spell and the power difference between having it and not having it is rather large.

Level 49 Spells:

(i) Greater Conjurations - Get all the pets, you know the drill. This time only Earth can be bought the rest must be researched.

(ii) Lava Bolt - 810damage. ‘nuff said.

(iii) Burnout III - more haste, more strength, more AC. Your pet is now officially a killing machine.

(iv) Shield of Lava - A 25 point damage shield. 25 Fire resistance. In perspective, a tank getting hit 50 times in a fight (again, reasonable for this level and higher) will give you a bonus 1250 damage in a fight. Not bad for 120 mana!

(v) Banish Summoned - nice dmg to mana ratio for a spell. This one does 585 damage for a cheep mana cost of only 250ish.

(vi) Summon Elemental Blanket - summons a shoulder item which gives +15 to Magic, Fire and Cold. Very useful for further buffing your stats. With natural resistances and magician spells and summons you can boost your MR, CR and FR to about 70 with no items. Pretty impressive!

Magician Pet Discussion:

Earth Pet

The Earth Pet is the meatsheild. He has the most HP and the most strength of any elemental. This is a good elemental to use throughout the first half of your life. Another very important advantage of this pet is that it casts a root spell. Root is an important spell that is not available to magicians in any other form. Post-50, this pet is modeled after the warrior. It has very high HP and hits quite hard.

Water Pet

This is the well balanced pet of the lot. It has the most balanced stats. Good agility, the second best hitpoints, etc. It also casts an ice damage spell which can make the slight damage edge in a fight. The post-50 version of it is a true powerhouse. It keeps these qualities but is also modeled after the rogue, which gives it the added ability to backstab. This is a very useful skill and creates very high damage output.

Fire Pet

The fire pet is an interesting situation. Pre-50 at the high levels it is arguably the best pet. It has strong attacks, as well as an incredible damage shield. However, post-50 it is thought to be somewhat useless. They turned it into a wizard which doesn’t do much melee damage but chaincasts fire and lightning spells. Solo it becomes useless and in a group it is underestimated. Pre-50 it is wonderful. Post-50 experiment with it in a group - you might like what you find.

Air Pet

The Air elemental starts out weak and becomes better and better the more you use it. Most people think of it as the weakest pet, because it has the least hitpoints and strength. However it makes that up by extremely high agility, dexterity and speed, as well as a great stun spell. Post-50 this pet stays the truest to itself hitting up to 5 times a round for almost 60 damage per hit, kicking, bashing and stunning all the while.
