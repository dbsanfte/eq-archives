Pet Classes Rejoice! - AD (Stranax)

Hey Everyone,
Just got some great news for all you pet classes out there. As a part of the caster re-balancing the latest topic being addressed was pets. It was just announced today that there are some great new changes to the way pets function.

1) Pets will now treat any mezzed creature as dead. This means all hate will be cleared from that monster and your pet will not attack it under any circumstances. This is great for Mages and Enchanters especially -- Enchanters will not have to worry about their uncontrolable pets breaking their mezzes and Mages will be allowed to bring along their other half more often.
2) Pets will now be neutral to everything. This means if your pet wanders off, monsters will not attack it. Bad pathing while walking through a dungeon, plane, etc will no longer cause trains. However, if your pet runs off attacking a fleeing monster, it is still considered hostile. This will also help mages and other pet classes as big groups will "allow" them to bring along the pets with less worry.
3) a)Pets now take NO experience from you or your group as long as they don't do 100% of the damage. This means as long as any member of the group does 1hp damamge to the monster the pet will take no experience.
3) b)If the pet does 100% of the damage it will take 75% of the experience
3) c)Old rules apply for Dire Charmed pets because they are so powerful. If the Dire Charmed pet does 100% damage it still takes 75% experience.

This is great news for all pet classes. Magicians, Enchanters, Necros, Shamans, Beastlords and Druids will all directly benefit from these changes. All players will enjoy a benefit, as pets will no longer train and these classes can be that much more effective in certain situations (not to mention the extra experience!)

News For 6/2/2002

Advanced Searches - J.C. Smith

I have received a few mails about this so I figured I would point it out, especially since the majority of our users are only accessing the daily updates. We do have advanced searches but it appears that we didn't do the best job of highlighting that in our menu as we've had a few people ask us if we were planning on adding the option. The bold text that reads Atlas, Bestiary, Equipment and Quests will take you to our advanced search menus.

From these menus you can filter your searches down to a fine detail. We also have a wide variety of sorting options. For example in our quest database(the most complete quest database on the internet) you can not only search for quests in a certain level range for your class only, but you can also sort those results by Level, Rating or Alphabetically. Through our equipment advanced search you can sort results by Armor Class, Damage, Damage per Second, Speed, Weight or Alphabetically.
