We realize that becoming a member of the subscription network Flashlink is going to isolate us from certain readers. Since the banner advertising market collapsed for gaming web sites in December of 2000 gaming sites(including our own) has struggled mightily just to pay for their own server bills. Despite this many readers mistake gaming web sites pleas for donations as a sign of greed. This is not the case, and we will do our best to help you understand that in this document.

As many of you know the banner advertising market collapsed in December of 2000. Since that point database intensive web sites such as EQ Spoilers have had a very difficult time covering their server and bandwidth costs. From July of 2001 to January of 2002 our gaming web sites cost us an average of $150 every month out of our own pockets just to cover server and bandwidth fees. From January of 2002 until May of 2002 our web sites have lost an average of nearly $300 a month in server and bandwidth charges. When you consider the many man hours that go in to running these web sites on top of the money lost. There is simply no way to expect web sites such as ours to remain free. It is an impossibility.

So we were left with the option of putting multiple pop ups on every page and hoping that we could manage to cover our own server bills or going subscription or partial subscription. We choose to go subscription and choose Flashlink for various reasons which we will document below.

I think it is honorable that sites such as Allakhazam have attempted to go partial subscription. We thought long and hard about this route, and we may still offer some more free options in the future, but for the time being the only thing free is news. Why did we make this choice?

The answer is simple. When a site is partial subscription the vast majority of people will not pay. Yet they will visit every day and eat up a sites bandwidth and add to the server load. This is the reason that Allakhazam's runs so slowly despite shelling out huge amounts of money every month to run multiple servers. Our current cost for bandwidth is $4 per gigabyte of bandwidth. This bandwidth goes fast. The end result is that those who pay for the service have to deal with slow server access because they are not only paying for themselves, but for everyone who accesses the server.

So once we realized that subscription was the only viable solution for us we were left with the option of charging a small subscription fee which would be good only for our internal sites(currently including EQ Spoilers and Shadowbane Legends with a DAoC Spoilers coming soon) or to join an existing network. Rather than charge you a small fee to only access our web sites we decided to join Flashlink for various reasons.

First and foremost we feel that it is what is best for the gaming community. Web sites were struggling to meet server fees before, but with UGO's recent changes these sites are going to find themselves in an even worse position now. As a result more and more web sites will be going subscription in the coming months. News sites and other less strenuous web sites will be able to hold out longer than database heavy sites such as ours, but everyone is struggling. The gaming community is going to have to accept this or they will be presented with an empty web site community in the near future.

In the struggle to survive the majority of the sites that have gone subscription have gone on their own. Taking the Everquest community as an example EQMaps, EQ'Lizer, Allakhazam, Ilia's Bestiary, EQ Atlas and Everlore all have subscription options.  Currently EQ'Lizer, EQ Maps and EQ Prices are members of Flashlink. EQ Atlas, Ilia's and Allakhazam are members of a second alliance of web sites. Everlore stands alone.

Allakhazam's fine network includes their Everquest and  Dark Age of Camelot sites for $10 every 3 months or $30 a year. Flashlink allows for as little as a one month subscription for a mere $3.45, four months for $9.95 or a year for $24.95. Flashlink also represents a growing network of web sites. Each of these represent fair options for your gaming dollar.

In the end we choose Flashlink because it allows thousands of current subscribers to instantly access our site free of charge. We also choose Flashlink because they are currently the one network who is actively looking to expand their web site and that will take a large variety of web sites. In the coming age of subscription gaming this is beneficial to the community. The more unified this community can become when it comes to subscription the better it is for the end user.

That having been said we understand that subscription isn't for everybody. If you can not afford $3.45 a month for a one month subscription of $2.08 a month for a year subscription, or if you simply do not want to pay to access a web site then we understand and we would refer you to one of the free web sites out there. At some point we may add some more free options to the web site, but in the current market we have to take steps to ensure that our new network launches before it sinks.
