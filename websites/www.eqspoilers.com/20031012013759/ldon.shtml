So you have ordered Lost Dungeons of Norrath and are ready to hop right in to the action. Unfortunately you have no idea where to begin. This guide is designed to present that information to you. It is displayed as a FAQ.

What is this Dungeon Instance Hoopla All About?

The idea of dungeon instances have been around for years. I touched on this subject earlier this year in my Evolution of the MMORPG article as well as in my Reinventing the MMORPG article several years ago. The basic idea is that when a group of players enters a dungeon they receive their own instance of that dungeon that they do not have to share with anyone outside of their group or raid.

There are many advantages to dungeon instances. Respawns are no longer necessary which means no more backspawn or trains from other groups. Because the dungeons are meant to be crawled through and explored camping is no longer an issue. You don't have to camp a particular mob hoping it will drop the item that you need. In fact you are unable to because once a monster is killed it will not return. This means that you can crawl a dungeon the way you did in pen and paper games and that competition among other groups of players is no longer an issue.

How Do I Receive a Dungeon Adventure?

The first thing you need to do is head to your home town. In each town there is a new NPC who you must hail to receive a sort of character flag. There will be yellow text. From there you need to head to the East Commonlands Wayfarer Camp and speak to Bastre. Tell him the following phrases in order after hailing him.

"adventures"
"favor journal"
"morden rasp"
"farstones"

Each phrase will give you yellow text. Once you have completed all four you receive an adventurer's stone. You may then take a new adventure at any time by visiting one of the Wayfarer Camps. These can be found in Butcherblock Mountains, East Commons, Everfrost Peaks, North Ro and South Ro. You must then form a group. There is a minimum of four players per group and all must be within 7 levels of one another. Once the group is formed the group leader must right click on the Adventure Recruiter to be presented with options for your adventure.

Once you have been given an adventure the entrance will appear on your Adventure Window. You have 30 minutes for your party to reach the dungeon entrance and enter the zone.

How Long Do I Have To Complete an Adventure?

After entering the zone you have 90 minutes to complete an adventure. Once you have completed an adventure you are allowed up to 30 minutes to explore the dungeon further if you so choose. Raid adventures by contrast, have a 3 hour time limit.

What Types of Adventures Are There?

There are currently four basic adventure types: Assassinate, Collect, Kill or Rescue. Collection missions require that you collect x amount of items to complete the adventure. Kill missions require that you kill x amount of monsters. Rescue missions require that you kill the NPCs in a dungeon and then escort the NPC safely to the exit. Assassinate quests ask you to kill the boss of the dungeon. He is spawned by killing monsters there. The group leader will be able to check which adventure you are given and decline any type that he does not desire. It will take 1-2 minutes after declining an adventure to receive a new one.

You can use the Adventure Window at any time to get a list of the goals and how close you are to attaining them.

How Do I Receive My Award?

For each adventure you will receive a number of points. The number varies by the difficulty level of the adventure and the level of the group. You can then spend these points on items, augmentations, spells, etc by visiting an Adventure Merchant. These merchants will only display the items which you can afford when you browse them. We will be attempting to compile a complete list of rewards in the coming weeks. These rewards will be listed on this page.

Monsters also can drop items during each dungeon. Augmentations are randomly dropped on any mob throughout the dungeon. Each dungeon also has a chance of spawning one or more rare NPCs who will each drop an item from the rare loot table for that particular dungeon. Mob drop quality is based on the level of your party and on the difficulty setting.

How Good is the Experience?

It's generally decent, but not on par with elemental experience.. A level 65 group can expect about an AA per adventure. Each adventure has a 90 minute time limit.

How Can I Teleport Between the Wayfarer Camps?

You can teleport between the Wayfarer camps by using the Magus NPC in each camp.

How Difficult is a Dungeon Adventure?

The difficulty level varies by the level of the group and by the setting chosen by the group leader when an adventure is assigned. In general the more difficult an adventure the more points you will receive upon completion.

How Far Does the Level Range Stretch?

All group members must be within 5-7 levels of one another to be given an adventure.

Traps and Chests, Do I Need a Rogue?

With the Lost Dungeons of Norrath a new emphasis has been placed on traps and locks. You will find locked chests and doors throughout dungeons. If you stumble upon a trap you may find yourself in a bad situation. This adds a new element to the game which has been missing.

Rogues and bards are the #1 option for finding and removing traps and unlocking doors in LDON. Because all groups will not have a bard or rogue at their disposal though, a new line of spells have been given to druids, shaman, necromancers and wizards. These spells allow casters to find and remove traps and open locks. These spells require a component that is sold at the Wayfarer camps.

How Do I Receive a Raid?

You receive a raid in the same way that you receive an adventure. You must form a raid of 18-36 members and then you have your raid leader speak to the Raid Recruiter and choose a raid. Raids have a 3 hour time limit instead of 90 minutes, and vary greatly in difficulty, with the most difficult raids being able to pose a challenge to Plane of Time geared guilds.

What Are Augmentations and How Do I Use Them?

Augmentations are a new type of item available in LDON. Augmentations are used to improve the stats on existing items. Most items in the game will now be available for upgrade through Slot-X augmentations. Each augmentation has it's own specific enhancements and have a restriction to the type of items they can be used on and the classes who can use them.

For example the Flawless Blue Jewel Augmentation grants +6 AGI. If you wished to add +6 AGI to an existing item in your inventory and it accepted a Slot-1 Augmentation you could combine that item and the Flawless Blue Jewel in an Augmentation Container.

There are a wide variety of Augmentation effects from stat enhancements to Flowing Thought, Regeneration or Weapon Procs. You can find augmentations both on monsters in LDON dungeons and through Adventure Merchants.

Can I Remove an Augmentation?

You may remove augmentations from an item by placing the item and solvent in to an Augementation Container. The augmentations will be lost, however. You are not able to salvage them.

What New Spells Are Available?

Druids, shaman, necromancers and wizards have been given spells to detect and remove traps, as well as opening locks. We are still compiling the complete list of LDON spells for our database. We will post it here once the spells have been entered in our database.
