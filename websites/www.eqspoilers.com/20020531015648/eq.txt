<font color="#D2D200"><p style="margin-left: 8; margin-right: 8"><b>Why Hybrid Only?</b></font>
<font size="2">- <a href="mailto:jcsmith@amazinghideaways.com" style="color:#ffffff">J.C. Smith</a></font><font face="Microsoft Sans Serif" size="2"><br>
<br>
Absor gave the reasoning behind only hybrids receiving the Natural Durability, Combat Agility and Combat Stability spells in <a href="http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/Forum1/HTML/000583.html">this thread</a>. In a nutshell...<br><br><blockquote>Let’s assume that a theoretical hybrid was 75% as effective as a warrior in each of those melee aspects (the warrior being the base at 100%).<br><br>If we boost the warrior’s ability by 5% (of the 100) he gains 5%.<br>If we boost the hypothetical hybrid by 5% (of the 75) he gains (gets out a calculator) 3.75%.<br><br>So the warrior with one level of the skill is 105% and the hybrid is 78.75%.<br><br>So rather than the hybrid being 25% less than the warrior, he’s 26.25% less…<br><br>As you add the levels of these bonuses up, you end up increasing the gap between the hypothetical hybrid’s melee skills and those of the warrior.<br><br>Now there was indeed much discussion about this, because if you look at it the other way, the hybrid is still 75% of the warrior (105 x .75 = 78.75). But in the end we decided to allow the hybrids to purchase a little more effectiveness.</blockquote></p>
