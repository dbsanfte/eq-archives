Character Creation:

Character creation for the enchanter, like any other class, is one of the defining moments of a character’s life. It determines where they begin their existence, it determines what race they are, it determines what base stats the character has and lastly (and obviously) it determines what class they will be. The class of the character is of course the most important. However, class tends to be something that people have already decided on. If you are reading this, there is a good chance that you have decided on creating an enchanter. There are five racial choices for your character and each one can make it as an enchanter. However, when push comes to shove, on stats there are good and bad choices when it comes to making an enchanter. I will say right now that these are my opinions. All of the races will make good enchanters, and at high levels these differences are much less obvious to the point where many feel that they disappear. I have always and still firmly believe that although stats can be evened out, that initial advantage that some races have never quite burns out. At this time I will say that humans, dark elves and gnomes make very good enchanters. At the same time, High Elves and Erudites make superb enchanters.

Human Enchanters have a very distinct advantage over their other human magi counterparts. Where as necromancers, magicians and wizards all have but one starting stat that is important; enchanters have two - Intelligence and Charisma. For this reason, Human Enchanters do very well. Although they start blind (something that forever bothers me and prevents me from playing a human character) and with less intelligence than the other racial choices, humans enjoy the second highest natural charisma of the races available. To add to this, humans have the most balanced of the other stats, giving them by far the most strength and stamina. This allows them to carry more loot (important when trying to make money) and gives them a few extra HP, which is marginally useful (being able to take that extra hit before mez goes off is a lifesaver).

Dark Elves and Gnomes are really in the same boat when it comes to being an enchanter. They both share high intelligence but lack when it comes to charisma. This becomes more and more evident throughout their lives. Both have various advantages and disadvantages, the most obvious being that dark elves are evil. However, as you will soon find out that isn’t as bad as it seems for an enchanter. Very quickly you will learn how to safely walk in any city.

Erudites make amazing enchanters. They are plagued with two problems that are both fairly easily solved. The first is the blindness factor that is shared with humans. It can theoretically be solved as early as level 4 with you get illusion: half elf. However, walking around as a naked half elf isn’t always fun. It is better solved at level 8 when you get both illusion gnome (robes will show up on gnomes) and illusion wood elf (and we all like seeing naked wood elves right?! ;) The second is their starting location. That side of the world sucks. I personally can’t stand the Qeynos area. I much prefer the protection and love of Feydwer or the familiarity of eastern Antonica. However, there are people that swear by Blackburrow for the beginning of life. Either way, you have to get off Odus - it’s a train wreck, and easily the worst continent in the game (yay for suckyness!) Get a teleport and a bind and you should be fine - Erudites aren’t loved but they aren’t hated either. They are blessed with by far the highest intellect and the third highest charisma, just behind humans. Many will argue that statistically, this is the best enchanter class to go with. I find it difficult to argue.

However, my personal favorite, and the favorite of a majority of enchanters is the High Elf. Many people say that there are only two things that the high elves do well - Enchanting and Healing. I don’t agree that they are the only things they do well, but I will concede that they do these two things better than anything else. For that matter, they do these things better than anyone else as well. As a high elf enchanter you are given the royal treatment growing up in Felwithe, night vision, a great newbie zone, a great starting location with plenty of leveling options, enough intelligence to do well, and better charisma than the lot of them. Many people will also argue that this is the best enchanter class to go with. And once again, I find it difficult to argue with them… especially considering my enchanter is this race.

Lastly, religion must be discussed when it comes to the enchanter. Don’t dedicate yourself to one god. Please. Just don’t do it. For some reason illusions don’t cover up religion. Maybe we wear huge medallions saying, “Tunare is my Mother!” or “Innurok kicks ass!” Either way, sometimes illusions just aren’t enough, so chose to be Agnostic. If you don’t believe me, try being a High Elvish Enchanter who worships Mithanial Marr, put up Dark Elf illusion, and try walking onto Dark Elf turf. Have fun retrieving your corpse (try invis this time!).

The Enchanter’s Role

Levels 1 to 4:

The role of the enchanter is the same as every other class from 1 to 4. You go solo, you find small animals and you hit them with whatever you got. Don’t group, and get to level 4. That is your role =).

Levels 4 to 16:

This is when you should do the only soloing of your life. Your pets aren’t all that bad even though you can’t control them. You should learn how to use mesmerize and control a crowd. Just because you CAN solo it doesn’t mean you shouldn’t learn how to group. From level 16 on it is basically your only viable option unless you are planning on getting killed a lot and leveling slowly. At level 12 you get charm. You will die from this spell, but you need to learn how to use it effectively or else you won’t get anywhere near your potential. You should solo a bit but in groups your role is to learn how to control a crowd with your tools.

Levels 16 to 50:

You give mana enhancers to whoever needs them, you give haste buffs to your tanks, and you control the crowd by any means necessary. That is your function. Learn it, live it, it is your job.

Levels 50 to 60:

The high levels for enchanters are basically the same thing. You become an important buffer as well as debuffer because without hasted party members you won’t survive. You also have “emergency heals” with your rune series which can keep the tank around long enough to get that complete heal he needs. Crowd control continues to be immensely important and we get some wicked spells for dealing with high end mobs, including a fixed duration charm and mezzes which work on monsters that weren’t mezzable before.

Raids:

You have a few jobs. You have to make sure everyone has Clarity II or whatever your best crack spell is at the time. You have to make sure all the tanks have the best haste you can give them. You have to make sure that you do everything you can to control a crowd. In a boss mob situation, same thing with buffs. You should do your upmost to get tashan through to lower the resistance. Then you do what you can. You’ll probably die quickly anyway.

Enchanter PvE Tactics:

Level 1 to 4:

Open with your DoT Spell, Follow with strength debuff and hit it to death. (or stab.) Simple as that!

Level 4 to 16:

Play with your pet. Summon it, buff it however you can. Hit a monster and let your pet go at it until your pet is half life. Keep your DoT spells eating at it. When your pet is half life go in and melee with it to absorb hits until you are half life. Back off and finish the job. You should be able to consistently solo this way. In a group a key thing is to explain to them how mesmerize works. Until they understand this they will keep messing things up over and over and over again. Explain to them how to /assist the main tank, all of this stuff is necessary. It is one disadvantage of the Enchanter that we need to school our groups at low levels on how to play with one. The advantage, of course, is that after they understand exp comes a lot quicker and they see how good we are.

Level 16 to 50:

You are a buffer and crowd controller, never forget that. You might start getting DoT spells that do a good amount of damage and you might get DD’s that can pack a bit of a punch. However, you should never use any mana on attacks unless you have more than 75% mana, everything has been controlled and is stable and everyone has all the buffs they need and want. If that is the case, feel free to pop a few nukes or a DoT. However, you will find that there is always something to do crowd control or buff wise. Also, don’t neglect that you are a crowd controller. Don’t be forced to become a clarity and haste whore - it is easy to do if you are lazy or not careful.

Level 50 to 60:

You get powerful spells and some key new abilities that you need to learn to use. Your epic weapon makes giving the best haste in the game easy, which is a nice break on the mana. You get more and better choices for mana regeneration. In terms of crowd control you have to have more focus than ever. These high level mobs are extremely dangerous and one bad move from you could kill your entire group or raiding party. They also become capable of extremely high damage output with Dire Charm which allows them to perma charm a monster. At high levels this has the potential of making the enchanter both an extremely valuable utility caster, but also a high damage dealer. As always, this comes with a price - if it fails, you are pretty much guaranteed to be dead.

Enchanter PvP Strategy:

Level 1 to 8:

There is no real PvP at these levels. Instead it is higher levels easily ganking lower levels. Get yourself to level 8 ASAP for some better options.

Level 8 to 16:

Have a pet up at all times. This will make their life harder. A good strategy is to tashan and then chain cast your level 8 nuke. If it keeps hitting their life will go down and if you time it right they won’t be able to get off any more spells. Versus a melee it is hard. Get your pet on them and cast Whirl to you Hurl and other stun spells.

Level 16 to 60:

You have the advantage of Mana regeneration. One option that is very dangerous but effective - charm a monster and sick em. If you can keep It charmed and on them you will be able to crush them easily. Another option is to do the level 8 to 16 strategy. You’ll have more mana now due to breeze / clarity / clarity II.

At level 51 with my enchanter I was able to kill 4 people simultaneously. It is very difficult but that is the sort of PvP task a skilled enchanter is capable of. My basic strategy is as follows: Have several mesmerize spells ready for use including the AoE one. Start by casting an AoE mez on them if they are near each other. This will freeze them for a bit. During this time you should cast tashan on each of them. Now, start giving them mesmerize to keep them down. One after another mez them and then cast your highest level tashan spell. You should now have them all mezzed and debuffed. Take the weakest link (normally a caster) and nuke them. Follow it immediately by another mez on them and then sit down to gain mana back. Re-mez or dazzle all of them to keep everyone frozen. Repeat this, always gaining your mana back. When one falls you go to the next and then the next. It is extremely difficult and I sugest that you start doing this by facing one opponent and then two and then three. When I faced 4 it was not by choice and I was extremely lucky. Don’t waste time looting them unless there is someone that is going to ninja or they are bound close. If you don’t know, mez everyone, loot and then gain some mana back while everyone is mezzed, for even 2-3 mezzings of everyone. This will allow you to gain enough mana to take down the second person quicker.

Enchanter Epic Quest:

This is the enchanter's epic quest. The solution for this quest is described in the quickest and most straightforward fashion that we could come up with. As a result you won't find much on the storyline, you will simply find out what you have to do to complete the quest.

1) Give a Shiny Metallic Robe Rilgor Plegnog in Ak'anon. The Shiny Metallic Robe is dropped by the Ghoul Arch Magi in Lower Guk.

He will give you a mechanical pen in exchange.

2) Visit Reania Jukle in the Qeynos Aqueducts. Ask her where you can find the ink of the dark and she will give you an empty vial.

3) Buy a Quill and Paper from Qeynos.

4) Travel to Lower Guk and charm the Ghoul Scribe. While it is charmed hand him the empty vial and he will give you the Ink of the Dark.

5) Hand the Quill and Paper to Chrislin Baker in West Karana. She will give you a bandit sash for your efforts.

More Importantly this will cause a bandit named Thracken Griften to spawn at loc +800, -11500. He is level 55 so bring some friends.

Kill him and he will drop a White Paper.

6) Turn the White Paper, Ink of the Dark and Mechanical Pen in to Stofo Olan in Erudin and he will give you a Copy of Notes.

7) Travel to Burnign Wood and seek out a Sarnak Imitator. You may need to kill some sarnaks near the fort to get him to spawn.

Hand the Sarnak Imitator the Copy of Notes that you received from Stofo.

He will give you Jeb's Seal.

8) Tell the Sarnak Imitator that you need a sack and he will give you an Enchanter's Bag.

9) Gather some friends and head to East Cabilis. An NPC named Vessel Drozlin spawns there. He is level 60. Kill him and he will drop a Xolian Rod.

10) While you have your friend's together head to Neriak Third Gate and kill Verina Tomb. She will drop Innoruuk's Word.

11) Gather even more friends and head to Chardok. This time you must kill Prince Selrach Di'zok. He will drop a head.

12) Turn in the Prince's head to Joren Nobleheart in Felwithe and you will receive the Chalice of Kings.

13) Head to Ogguk. At loc +230, +400 you will find a pair of Large Muddy Sandals(unless someone else has taken them already).

14) Take these sandals to Bozlum Blossom in the same zone. She will give you a Scribbled Parchment.

15) Take the scribbled parchment to Brokk Boxtripper in one of the stone huts. He will give you a Gift.

16) Take the gift back to Bozlum Blossom and she will give you Snow Blossoms.

17) Combine the Chalice of Kings, Xolian Rod, Snow Blossoms and Innoruuk's Word in to the Enchanter's Bag.

18) Travel to Overthere and turn the combined bag in to Modani Qu'Loni. He will give you a piece of the Staff of the Shissar.

19) Tell Moldani that you need a bag so he will give you a new Enchanter's Bag.

20) Head to the Oasis of Marr. You may have to kill some Sand Giants for a while to get Cazel to spawn. When he spawns kill him and he will drop a spoon.

21) Head to the Overthere. I don't have a loc for this part so be prepared for a headache. You must find a key that will spawn on a mesa in the canyons. Pick it up you will need it later.

22) Head to the Crypt of Dalnir. At location -20, 50 on the third floor you will find a Lost Scroll.

23) Go to the Plane of Air. You need to get the Book of Charm and Sacrifice. It is found on a table at loc +1300, +550.

24) Combine the Scroll, Book, Key and Spoon in to your Enchanter's Bag. Hand the combined bag to Clockwork VIIX in Ak'anon.

He will give you the second piece of the staff.

25) Head to Firiona Vie and pay a visit to Nadia Starfeast. Ask Nadia for a bag and you will receive an enchanter's bag.

Next ask Nadia for a diamond, sapphire, ruby and emerald(one at a time) and she will give you one of each.

26) You will now need to head to four differnet locations and charm then hand in(while the mob is charmed) one of these gems(a different one for each mob) to them. You will then receive an item with the mobs trapped essence in return.

Here is the list of mobs to turn in each gem to:

Diamond - Spectral Librarian in Kaesora
Sapphire - Faelia Goldenwing in Skyfire.
Ruby - Wraith of Jaxion in the City of Mist.
Emerald - Impaler Tzilug in the Overthere.

27) Combine the enchanted gems in your Enchanter's bag and return them to Nadia Starfeast in Firiona Vie. You will receive the third piece of the staff.

Ask Nadia for a new sack while you are here.

28) Gather a good collection of friends and head down to the Hole. You need to visit Polzin Mrid. Hand him Jeb's Seal(that you received way back when). He will give you some instructions and return the seal back to you.

29) While your in the Hole kill Kinde's Ghost. He will drop the Essence of a Ghost.

30) Kill the Wraith of the Shissar in the Plane of Fear. He will drop the Head of the Serpent.

31) Go to the Plane of Hate and kill some Forsaken Revenants. If you are lucky one will drop the Essence of a Vampire. If you are unlucky, well try again soon.

32) Head to the Field of Bone and kill the Tangrin. He can be found on the island. He will drop the Sand of the Mystics.

33) Combine the four items you have just received(both Essence's, the Head of the Serpent and Sand of Mystics) in to your Enchanter's Bag and head back to the Hole.

Hand them in to Polzin Mrid and you will receive the final piece of the staff.

34) Head back to the Burning Wood and pay a visit to the Sarnak Imitator again. Ask him for a sack if you do not already have one.

Combine the four pieces of the staff in to the Enchanter's Bag and hand it in.

Congratulations on the Staff of the Serpent.

Spell Discussion:

Before I begin this discussion I would like to note a few things. First of all, the usefulness of a spell is entirely my opinion and other people might have differing opinions. Secondly you should at every level try to get every spell. This is merely a synopsis of what the enchanter spells do and in which order I believe it is best to purchase them. If you are filthy rich, the order doesn’t matter because you will get them all at once anyway! Also, if you have the opportunity to buy a spell, take it, don’t worry if it is “out of order.” Every spell counts!

Level 1:

Shallow Breath - This spell comes with your enchanter and essentially does 5 damage in a short time. Use it to pull your victims.

Minor Shielding - Also comes with your enchanter. This one raises HP, AC and MR. Keep this line of spells up at all times for the rest of your existence.

Weaken - this spell becomes very useful to you, removing 15 STR from your opponent. It makes you take less damage. Period.

Pendril’s Animation - your first pet spell. Buy some tiny daggers and watch your first few levels become a lot easier. This pet will do a ton of the work for you.

Strengthen - cast on your and your pet to increase your damage output slightly.

Reclaim Energy - Cast on your pet to un-summon it and get a percentage of the mana back. Useful when your pet is near death and you need mana.

Minor Illusion - This is very much a PvP spell. Although it roots you in place you become a small object such as a coin purse, a sword or a tree. You also generally become safe even in hostile areas. Why attack a tree? This doesn’t always hold true, however.

Lull - I’ve always found the lull series of spells to have only minor use. This is generally used when you want to pull only one of two mobs. However, if the lull fails you have both of them and you are in trouble. Always cast this on the further of the two monsters. If not, the running monster will agro the lulled monster. If that doesn’t make sense, just take my word for it, or learn it the hard way hehe.

True North - points you north. I suggest sense heading or a compass instead.

Taper Enchantment - decreases time remaining on a random buff / DoT, etc. Extremely useless at this level.

Level 4 (Quick note about level 4. This is the level where you become an enchanter. Every single spell in this level is important and 100% necessary):

Gate - Teleport to your bind point. This quite possibly may be the most used spell in your life next to invisible.

Suffocating Sphere - New DoT Spell. This one does 10 dmg. Use it, live it, learn it.

Juli’s Animation - Next pet which is bigger and badder. You’ll be glad you have good old Juli.

Tashan - Lowers your opponent’s Magic Resistance. Makes most of your spells stick / stick better.

Mesmerize - The bread and butter of your existence. This spell (when successful) freezes a monster for 24 seconds. The kicker of it is that you cannot attack it either. As long as it is not taking damage it will sit there frozen not able to do anything. Start practicing this spell because it is your most important one. You will also have to educate your group on this spell. You will start to hate your pet for breaking this all the time ;)

Color Flux - Area Effect Stun spell. You will get more of these. When you get 3 of them you can effectively keep 4 mobs perma-stunned. This is an incredibly useful enchanter series that is very underused. The only reason that you shouldn’t be using this often is if you play on a PvP server. In that case, I’ve been ranting about the stupidity of some PvP rules forever (AE hitting group mates and pets is my biggest beef)

Invisibility - Makes you invisible until you attack or cast. For now at this level you can pretty much go anywhere within reason and walk right past the monsters. One very important note is that it has a random duration between 24 seconds and 20 minutes. And it ALWAYS starts fading when you don’t want it to.

Enfeeblement - decreases target’s strength by 18-20 and AC by 2. Makes you hurt a lot less. You’ll like it.

Illusion: Half Elf / Human - Important if you are blind or evil. Lets you walk around in relative peace.

Haze - Adds 5AC to you. Could make the difference and will make you get hit lighter when you do get hit.

Fear - Ok, so many of you will be asking why I put this last. Fear is an incredibly useful and powerful spell. In fact with a druid or ranger (9+) grouped with you, you suddenly become and incredible pair. Mana. That is the problem. At level 4, 40 mana is huge. Like a bubble and a half to 2 bars of mana. It just isn’t worth it until level 7 or so. When you reach that level it becomes a godly spell.

Level 8:

Chaotic Feedback - You finally get your first Direct Damage Spell. Right now your Evocation will completely reek. I mean, completely, 100% reekage. You’re going to have to spend some points in it and cast this spell a lot. It will eventually catch up. This like all of our DD spells are “All or Nothing” which means that they either hit for full damage (48 in the case of this spell) or they are completely resisted. These spells also have a stun component to them. The good news is that when it goes through it will interrupt spell casting. The bad news is that the stun component makes it easier to resist, and remember if it is it is resisted all out.

Lesser Shielding - Upgrade to Minor Shielding. More HP, More AC, More MR.

Root - Another bread and butter spell, this one although more commonly used to keep a monster in place while you nuke it (you aren’t a wizard. That is how THEY use root, not you) this spell is much more useful as a crowd control spell. How so? Root has a check to break every tick (6 seconds). It also gets a break check every time you do damage. Furthermore it has another check every time it is stunned. So, every time you nuke it you give it two additional chances to break free. If you root it, leave it there and keep it “out of the fight” it has less chance of breaking and you have done more good.

Myrcil’s Animation - The next pet. You know the drill.

See Invisible - See all those people that thought they were hiding from you.

Alliance - make people like you even more! Cast this on an NPC and everyone on that faction will like you a whole lot better. Use this in combination with illusions to help survive in hostile waters.

Eye of Confusion - if nobody is within melee range the monster will begin randomly running in random directions. It is fairly entertaining to watch and allows you to make a quick getaway.

Cancel Magic - Use it to take away buffs. Ie. If you begin fighting in lavastorm in a few levels, some things have damage shields. This takes those away making you or your friend’s life easier.

Bind Sight - Step into someone else’s eyes. If you are in a group, step into the puller’s eyes so you can tell your group what is coming and when. If you want to figure out NPC patrols, jump into an NPC’s eyes. Heck do it to a random person to keep tabs on them.

Illusions: Gnome and Wood Elf - more illusions. More fun. Yay!

Sentinal - The laggiest spell ever. Do this - go to your favorite bank, cast this spell and run around the general area for a bit. Repeat a few times. Then just run around. Continuous fog of particle effects :) It doesn’t have much other use. It can be cast down a hall to give a few seconds warning of a train in a dungeon. That is about the only practical use of it

Soothe - see lull. Same problems, works a bit better and on higher level mobs.

Enchant: Silver and Enchant: Clay - These are both only useful for trade skills. If you do them or have friends that do them these spells are useful. If not, you will get little use out of them.

Level 12:

Charm - use it, learn it, live it. It is arguably your most powerful spell at this point. It is also the most dangerous to your health. It could be the difference between you living and dieing, between soloing yellows and dieing to blues. I will be creating a separate charming guide sometime in the future when I have finished all of the class guides. Some useful info: /pet attack - sends your pet to attack. /pet back off - wipes your pet’s hate list. /pet sit down -- sets the pet in one place. /pet follow me - makes the pet follow you.

Choke - your next DoT spell. This one does 80 damage in total over 30 seconds and decreases STR and AGI by 7-10.

Bind Affinity - this spell allows you to change your bind point to almost anywhere. Some zones and areas are exceptions. You can also bind friends in certain areas with this spell (as a rule you can bind them in city zones although other areas to bind do exist).

Languid Pace - This spell is the first slowdown spell that the enchanters get. This decreases attack speed by 30%. This means that you can effectively reduce damage done by the target by 30%. Very helpful spell (note that shamans get better slow spells so if a shaman is in the group, you should let them handle this one)

Memory Blur - This spell wipes the hate list of the monster. This has two huge uses. First of all, if a monster is going ape on a caster or healer in your group, or even on a tank who is close to dead, cast this on the monster. IF it succeeds the monster will stop being mad at everyone and the next person who hits him will get his attention. This also works well in Enchanter / Rogue combinations with non-aggressive mobs. Backstab, mem blur. Backstab, mem blur. Works well. The other use is for powerleveling characters. The enchanter gets the monster almost dead and mem blurs it. If it works the hate list is gone as well as the damage list. This means that the lower level can give the final blow and get all of the exp.

Serpent Sight - All you blind people how have infravision. Congrats.

Whirl Till You Hurl - One of the most uniquely named spells in the game, the usefulness of it is debatable. There are three schools: The people (like me) who swear by it in the low levels and think it is a wicked spell; The people who just whine that it was severely nerfed 3 years ago and refuse to try it out; and the people who say it plain old sucks either way. The spell roots and stuns the target for up to 12 seconds or until the monster is stunned again. Combined with an animation this spell rocks up until level 22 or so (IMHO).

Kilan’s Animation - The next summoned pet. Should still be used often despite getting charm. This is a safer and more reliable alternative, although not as powerful.

Intellectual Advancement - This spell decreases your fizzle rate. Very helpful.

Ebbing Strength - Decrease target’s strength by 25. This starts to become an excellent PvP spell. In combination with the DoT spell you can now reduce strength of an opponent by 35. This will not only make melee’s a lot less effective but an opponent carrying a lot of loot will be bogged down by this spell to the point where they start moving very slowly. Versus monsters it is also very effective, and in combination with Languid Pace you can now seriously diminish a monster’s damage output.

Enduring Breath - Takes away the need to breathe. Requires fish scales though :(

Illusion: Dark Elf, Halfling, High Elf, Erudite - More enchantments to make more people like you :)

Mist - Adds 8AC. Although this isn’t as good as the Cleric AC buff it helps your tanks in a pinch.

Thicken Mana - As far as I know, this spell is completely useless.

Level 16:

Enthrall - I place this upgrade to mesmerize first on the list because crowd control really is the enchanter’s bread and butter ability. This mez lasts a lot longer - 48 seconds! You should be using this from now on and will find that you use this spell into your high levels at times.

Breeze - The lesser version of clarity, this mana regen spell was added with Kunark. You will be hounded for this spell from now on, but it is a great ability to have!

Quickness - You are now a melee character’s best friend (hmmm maybe clerics still get their love more than you). This spell increases the rate at which weapons swing by 30%. It is a significant improvement, although it tends to be mana consuming.

Chase the Moon - Fear upgrade. Useful in a fear kiting group or when you are in trouble!

Shielding - The upgraded shield spell. This one gives more AC, more MR and more HP :)

Rune I - The Enchanter heal. This requires a component (Cats Eye Agate) and absorbs up to 50hp damage. This can be used in a pinch to keep someone alive while a healer can get a spell off. This is also a good idea to keep on yourself at all times. 2 Gold per cast is kinda expensive for you right now but it will get to the point where it isn’t too bad anymore.

Mesmerisation - Area Effect 24 second mesmerize. This is both powerful and dangerous. I can’t stress how cool it is to stop a train, but if some get resisted you have a lot of monsters mad at you all at once!

Sanity Warp - Your second DD/Stun spell. This one does 95dmg as a maximum. When you first get it it will be slightly below that. As with all the DD’s enchanters get, this one is all or nothing and is resisted more often than we would like.

Shalee’s Animation - Another uncontrollable pet. This one has a chance to be a duel-wielder so try giving it an extra weapon :) With your melee buffs you can really make this animation pretty strong! (30% haste means 30% more damage output)

Levitate - Half floating half flying spell. This spell lifts you off the ground and makes you able to run on water, lava, etc just fine. It also helps on rough terrain because you won’t fall for damage. Unfortunately it isn’t a flying spell so you will float downwards to about 3 feet off the ground whenever you levitate off something high.

Disempower - Lower your target’s strength, stamina and AC by up to 20, 15 and 9 respectively. This is a GREAT PvP spell, especially in combination with your DoT and weaken spells.

Invis vs. Undead - Does exactly what it says. Makes you invisible to the undead. You can’t be invis to dead and alive at once though :(

Illusion: Barbarian, Dwarf, Tree - Good for adjusting various factions. Tree makes you neutral to pretty much any animal or undead.

Enchant Electrum - Useful for jewelcraft. This turns a bar of Electrum into a bar of Enchanted Electrum.

Level 20:

Tashani - Upgraded Tashan. This one does a maximum of 23 MR taken away and is once again unresistable and unleashs mad amounts of taunt :)

Color Shift - Area Effect 6 second stun. Combined with color flux this is a great group tool and solo tool if you are in a bind and about to die. 6 seconds is a LOT of time to make a getaway.

Intellectual Superiority - Decreases Fizzle rate. Always a helpful ability.

Feckless Might - Your next Strength Debuffer. You are a strength debuffing machine! Again, a very under-used and excellent PvP ability. This one takes away up to 30 strength.

Sympathetic Aura - Increase your Charisma (or anyone else’s for that matter) by up to 18 points. Great for when you are charming, mezzing, or buying / selling. As you might guess that means that you should have it up at all times :)

Berserker Strength - A single target Melee Buff and 50hp RUNE. The upside is that it requires no component. At this point though a 50HP buffer isn’t much at all. However it is still worth it if it saves your tank and it doesn’t hurt to throw it on now and again if you have extra mana.

Sisna’s Animation - Next animation. You know the drill. This one always duel wields (sweet)

Endure Magic - Increase your MR by 20 points. This is especially important on PvP servers, if you are fighting casters or if you are using AE Mez spells.

Cloud - Next AC Buff. This one gives an extra 11AC max. Once again, good to have when a cleric isn’t available.

Benevolance - Upgrade to Alliance. Nuff said.

Illusion: Iksar, Troll, Ogre -- More various illusions. The Troll one gives you slightly higher regeneration but you become atrociously ugly.

Shifting Sight - You inherit your target’s eyes and gain infravision :)

Crystalize Mana - Another trade-skill related summon.

Level 24 (Yay, new Spell effects!):

Beguile - Your next charm spell. This one is good against monsters up to level 39.

Alacrity - 40% Hate. Very nice. You are still a melee’s best (or close to best) friend, and this just re-affirms your position.

Rune II - The next in the line of emergency damage absorbtion spells. This one can absorb up to 118 damage and costs a bloodstone.

Major Shielding - Next in the common magi line of Shielding Spells. This one once again gives more AC, more HP and more MR.

Tepid Deeds - A monster that is 50% slowed does 50% less damage. Very useful for when a shaman isn’t handy at the time.

Chaos Flux - 150DD is getting weaker and weaker for your level, but you aren’t a nuker. However, when push comes to shove and something has to die, this does the job. Its all or nothing and has a stun component as usual.

Sagar’s Animation - Animations are getting less and less useful, however they still useful in a soloing situation.

Strip Enchantment - Takes off up to 4 enchantments. Excellent for PvP and against buffed NPC’s.

Illusion: Skeleton / Earth Elemental - More illusions. Warning - most guards don’t like these two.

Invigor - Give someone back all his or her vigor. Limited use spell, but necessary in some circumstances (Kedge raid anyone?)

Enchant: Gold - Turns a bar of gold on the mouse to an enchanter bar of gold. Used for Jewelcraft.

Level 29:

Clarity - Why? So Newbies can bug you of course! If you don’t know why this is the number one spell for this level, go back and play a warrior.

Augmentation - A super melee buff combining haste, AC, Agility and stamina regen. It is also long lasting.

Haunting Visage - Throw this on your tanks. It increases their hate output so monsters hate them more than you and your caster buddies. Its always a good thing.

Suffocate - 263dmg over a min and 48 seconds as well as up to 20 strength and agility removed for that time… who doesn’t like this spell?

Listless power - remove up to 35 strength and agility as well as up to 18 AC. With this and Suffocate you can take strength down by up to 55 and agility down up to 55. In PvP these two spells together are absolutely amazing. 55 strength will make most people overweight further decreasing their agility and bringing them to a slow crawl.

Enstill - This is an upgraded root spell which will bring your target to a halt for up to 96 seconds. Its basically a poor man’s Enthrall, yet it works surprisingly well in a trained group.

Feedback - Your only damage shield (other than fire elemental) which does 11 damage. Helpful when there is no mage or druid around to throw out a damage shield on the tanks. This spell DOES NOT stack with illusion: fire elemental (at least it didn’t a year ago when I was playing my enchanter).

Ultravision - Give yourself the best night vision available. Better yet, give your poor blind human friend the best night vision available.

Obscure - Increase AC by up to 14. Although not as effective as the clerc’s AC increasing line, this is good when a cleric isn’t around or to buff the casters to save the cleric a bit of mana.

Illusion: Air / Water Elemental - These two spells give you elemental form and bonuses. The Air Elemental has a built in levitate (no more batwings!) and the Water elemental has built in water breathing (no more fish scales!)

Nullify magic - remove 2 enchantments. Alright so you are wondering “Why this over Strip enchantment which removes 4?” Here is the simple answer. Strip enchantment removes UP TO 4 enchantments. This means it could be 0, 1, 2, 3 or 4. Nullify magic either takes no enchantments or 2 enchantments. This is good when you want to take off a DoT spell or a snare or root but don’t want to have a chance of wiping out half of your buffs either.

Uleen’s Animation - Those animations are getting less and less useful. They are still good for certain situations (fear kiting) but not for much else.

Dyn’s Dizzying Draught - This odd spell is like whirl to you hurl, but worse. It is easily the more useful of the two. Not only is it very often resisted, it takes a ton of mana, is easily broken and now drains mana (causing more resistance checks). Just… don’t use it.

Curse of the Simply Mind - Debuffs INT and WIS stats. Only useful in PvP as far as I’m concerned. However, there have been rumors that Wisdom affects your chance of resisting mind-altering spells such as charm. In this theory casting this first would increase the duration of a charm. I’ve never noticed a difference, but feel I should mention this so I don’t get flamed ;)

Clarify Mana - More trade skill mana summoning junk stuff. (can you tell I’m not into trade skills?)

Level 34:

Entrance - Upgrade to Enthrall, this is your latest single-target mez spell. This one lasts 72 seconds if nobody fiddles with it :)

Radiant Visage - add 30 charisma to yourself. Helps with the mind-control spells.

Rune III - This is going to be your most used run spell. It is an emergency absorber for up to 230 damage and costs a jasper.

Anarchy - Next in your DD/Stun series of spells, this one unleashes a fair punch of up to 233 damage

Greater Shielding - More HP, More AC, More MR. Same deal.

Insipid Weakness - Debuff your opponents for up to a brutal 51 STR. Combined with your latest DoT this could cripple them by up to 71 Strength, Easily enough to encumber most people.

Boltran’s Animation - At this point, the animation is only good as an extra damage dealer in the group. It is a lot more efficient now, because it will not attack mezzed targets making it more useful at higher levels.

Illusion: Fire Elemental - Get yourself a fire elemental look as well as up to 16 damage in Damage shield goodness.

Mana Sieve - Make your target lose mana. This is a great spell for PvP and When you have several enchanters sieving an Ultra (Boss) Mob to make sure it can’t cast anything.

Gift of Magic - Increases your target’s mana pool. Not a bad buff to have :)

Cast Sight - See through your target’s eyes with Ultravision. Yay!

Enchant: Platinum - More trade skill enchantment spells.

Level 39:

Cajoling Whispers - Charm monsters up to level 45

Celerity - Making your Tanks his 50% faster gives them a 50% boost in damage and taunt. Always a good thing.

Calming Visage - Makes your target generate 10% less hate.

Immobilize - The next spell in the root line, this spell is quick casting and holds a monster in place for about a minute.

Insight - This nice buff increases both wisdom (up to 14) and intelligence (up to 10).

Aany’s Animation - The next animation, once again, good for extra damage in your group.

Invoke Fear - Your newest and coolest fear spell, this one can last up to 42 seconds. Excellent for group (or partner) fear kiting.

Resist Magic - Because you deal with so many mind altering spells, some of which are AE, it is always good to keep your magic resistance high. This spell helps with that, adding an extra 40MR

Gravity Flux - Mostly used as a joke spell, this is actually a very good PvP spell. If you find someone with low magic resistance, forget about the mez - nuke formula, start chain casting this puppy. Every time they land they will take considerable damage and when they are low, just mez, get some mana and nuke them down for the kill. Don’t try this on rogues, monks or Vah Shir (all of which have safe-fall abilities)

Shade - Increases your target’s AC by up to 17. Again, helpful when cleric is low on mana or not present.

Mind Wipe - There really is nothing like seeing 6 monsters stop in their tracks all at once and forget why they were so mad in the first place. This is a REALLY fun spell for showing off in newbie dungeons and watching everyone Ohh and Ahh when the train walks off scratching their heads. It is also somewhat useful in the real game where you can try to get normally non-aggressive mobs off your group. It is just an Area Effect Memory Blur spell.

Wandering Mind - A mana drain over time spell, this takes 120 mana over a period of 2 minutes and gives it to you. Helpful for increasing your mana regeneration.`

Illusion: Drybone / Spirit Wolf - These illusions make you a skeleton or a Wolf. They both come with some bonuses (unfortunately, SoW Isn’t one)

Pacify - Reduces agro range and gets resisted a lot. Another useless spell in a useless spell line.

Distill Mana -More tradeskill stuff. MORE… MORE… MORE (it never ends!)

Level 44:

Tashania - Decrease your target’s MR by even more with this extension of the tashan line. This one decreases it by up to 33MR

Incapasitate - One of the PvP Mother spells, this baby decreases Strength and Agility by up to 55 points and AC by up to 24 points. It really does incapasitate people.

Weakness - Combining this spell with the last one creates devastating effects. This spell decreases Strength by 60 points. Lets just take a scenario here on PvP. Take an average Warrior of this level. Lets say he has about 200 Strength. Now with these two spells alone, every pound of weight over 115 is going to encumber him. Add to this the 55 agility he lost before and he is going to be as good as snared. Who needs Root?

Color Skew - The next in the color flux line of spells. If your enemies aren’t too magic resistant, wit this and the previous two spells you can take a group of enemies out of the equation indefinitely

Discordant Mind - DD’ing isn’t an enchanter’s specialty, but 528 damage in a single nuke at level 44 is fairly respectable! Less than 75 damage less than the mage’s best nuke at this level. Also has the all-or-nothing and stun components

Arch Shielding - upgrade to the HP/AC/MR line of self-only buffs. Use it, learn it, live it.

Boon of the Garu - How would you like to get an extra 40 ATK, 100DEX and a 27pt lifetap proc? How about werewolf form? Well then this spell is for you. Cast it on your tanks and they’ll love you forever. This is one you have to keep memmed due to 3 minute recast. The spell has a 6 minute duration

Shiftless Deeds - Slow the target by 65%. Good for PvE and PvP

Brilliance - Increase INT by up to 14 and WIS by up to 6. This won’t stack with any other INT/WIS stat buff.

Pillage Enchantment - An excellent debuffing spell. This one murderers 4 enchantments on a consistant basis!

Yeogreff’s Animation - Your next pet. Buff him up and send him in, this one can do some serious damage for a “weak enchanter pet.”

Summon Companion - Summon your pet to you. Helpful when it gets stuck or lost.

Rune IV - Another rune spell. Although this is a lot more powerful than rune III it also costs a lot more. The required component, a peridot, costs around 10pp. I don’t use it a lot, but for the wealthy enchanters around, you’ll be loved!

Extinguish Fatigue - Completely restores all vigor. Go Kedge raids!

Illusion: Werewolf - Turn into a werewolf. Guards now hate you but on the up side, you look cool!

Enchant Velium - Enchant a bar of velium for (you guessed it) Tradeskills!

Level 49:

Allure -your next charm spell. This one is effective on monsters up to level 51 (imagine having a level 51 pet at level 49? Hehe)

Dazzle - 96 second high level mez spell. This will once again become your bread and butter spell. This knocks enemies out cold as most fights don’t last anywhere near 96 seconds.

Gasping Embrace - DoT for 710 damage in 2 minutes and 30 STR and AGI debuffed in that same time frame.

Swift Like the Wind - 60% haste, a melee’s dream. This is an amazing spell that should be thrown on all your tanks until the next upgrade to it.

Blanket of Forgetfullness - Known as both the high level mem blur and the ultimate train stopper, this AE mem blur works fairly reliably versus higher level monsters and almost always on lower level mobs (we’re talking 99%+ of the time)

Adorning Grace - Increase your Charisma by 40 points. Helpful for charming and all those other fun mind-bending spells.

Berserker Spirit - Get 40STR and Lose 20AGI. The other upside is that you get a bonus, componentless 200hp Rune. This is good for twinking melee characters.

Illusion: Imp - Hey, its new and cool to be an imp (plus you get to float!)

Paralyzing Earth - Your latest Root spell. This “poor man’s mez” can last up to 3 minutes, making it very useful in a well oiled group in an open area.

Shadow - This upgrade to the AC line of buffs is worth a maximum of 20AC. While not as good as the cleric’s AC buff it helps them with the mana or if they aren’t there to do that job.

Kintaz’s Animation - Your level 50 pet. Although the dumb thing doesn’t do what you say most of the time, it does defend you when it gets whacked. With a weapon and swift like the wind it actually isn’t half bad. It hits for a max of 52 and with quadding and such can be a real asset, especially in duels. Enchanters are always amazed the first time they see their crappy pet take down a giant :) Everquest Trivia: Kintaz is Zatnik spelled backwards, as in Geoffrey Zatnik the spell designer for EQ.

Group Resist Magic - Figure it out.

Reoccuring Amnesia - Casts Memory Blur every tick for 4 ticks. After its all said and done you’d hope the creature would have forgotten everything. I’ve never found it all that effective.

All the tradeskill crap (not gunna list em all)

Level 50:

Improved Invisibility - Invisibility that won’t randomly drop on you leaving you to be brutally slaughtered at an inopportune moment.

Level 51:

Levitation - Levitate spell with no component requirement

Everlasting Breath - Underwater breathing spell with no component requirement.

Collaboration - Upgrade to the alliance line. This one increases your faction standing with said NPC quite considerably.

Theft of Thought - Steals up to 400 Mana from a target. The target must have a mana bar (ie. No Warrior NPC’s!). Very good PvP Spell versus other casters :)

Wake of Tranquility - Harmony that can be resisted. This means that you are almost garunteed to have a slew of angry mobs chasing you. I can’t think of any reason that you would EVER want to use this spell. In fact it is resisted by MOBS 40 levels lower than you most (like 80-90%) of the time.

Level 52:

Trickster’s Augmentation - This is a great buff to throw on your main tank. It gives them imp form, levitation, 30 Fire resistance and makes them proc a 75DD + taunt effect.

Rune V - This next rune spell is incredibly powerful. It gives up to 700HP in buffer and costs the same component as Rune IV. This is a lifesaving spell. It has a big taunt factor to it though so get ready to stun the monsters when they come after you.

Boon of the Clear Mind - Group Clarity. Makes your life easier.

Color Slant - The next in your color line of stuns, you can take out groups of monsters indefinitely when you use them all properly. This one also has a mana drain component.

Fascination - AE 36 Second mez. I tend to dislike this spell because it normally leads to certain death. However, this works fairly reliably on light blue monsters and is ok if you are in a situation where you want to save a newbie friend or whatever.

Level 53:

Aanya’s Quickening - Upgrade to Swift like the wind. This gives a killer 64% haste and should be given to as many tanks as mana can afford

Boltran’s Agacerie - This is your latest Charm spell. It caps at level 53 MOBS in Norrath and level 51 MOBS in the outer Planes.

Cripple - One of the holy grails of PvP, this spell is deadly on its own but unspeakably good when combined with other spells. Alone it debuffs STR and AGI by up to 75 points, DEX by up to 65 points and AC by up to 33. When combined with other debuff spells this renders a melee character completely helpless.

Recant magic - Upgrade to Pillage Enchantment. This should be your buff-stripping spell of choice. Also an excellent PvP Spell.

Level 54:

Clarity II - A big and well deserved upgrade to “crack” clarity II (or crack 2) should be cast on yourself and all other mana-using classes asap.

Beguiling Visage - Self only taunt reducing spell. It effectively halfs the amount of taunt you generate.

Glamour of Kintaz - This is a high level mesmerize spell. It is effective on level 55+ mobs and carries a powerful memory blur with it.

Dementia - Your latest all or nothing nuke / stun spell. This one gives a punch for 675 damage.

Shield of the Magi - The last spell in your personal HP / AC / MR line of spells. This final version gives a maximum of 250HP, 25MR and 32AC.

Illusion: Vah Shir - It baffles me why you don’t get this illusion until level 54 considering you can become any other race much earlier in your career. I guess that Shar Val not KOS’ing any race does help. What is more, the spell only drops in the extremely high level zone Ssra Temple making this a spell that most will never have.

Level 55:

Zumaik’s Animation - Your final pet spell! This beast of a pet hits for a max damage of 54 damage and when fully buffed can really tear through things.

Gift of Insight - This spell increases your mana pool by 100 mp and increases your mana regen by 1 mana per tick. Always a helpful spell to have on you.

Memory Flux - This is an upgraded memory blur spell that has a very low resist rate and can get you down on the hate list quickly. It has had mixed reviews in application, with some enchanters swearing by it and some swearing at it.

Largarn’s Lamentation - This is a single target 8 second stun spell. It has a lot of good applications and is resisted a lot less frequently than the AE stun spells. It also doesn’t have the chance to start a massive train in a multi-floor dungeon.

Wind of Tashani - an AE tashan spell. In my experience this spell should be renamed “suicide”. It has a huge taunt effect and gets multiple monsters extremely mad at you.

Level 56:

Augment - 45% haste, 18AC, 35 AGI and Stamina regen built into one long lasting spell. ‘nuff said. Next.

Torment of Argli - This is a DoT spell for 960damage over approximately 2 minutes. It also debuffs Intellect and Wisdom by 21 points over that time period.

Horrifying Visage - Increases target’s taunt output by 30%.

Overwhelming Splendor - Increase your Charisma by 50 points. Helpful for sticking all of those mind control spells.

Trepidation - 36 second high level fear spell. This one works on higher level monsters than your last fear spell and will last longer on high level monsters a well. At this point in the game, keeping a monster feared for the full 36 seconds isn’t very realistic unless it is a low blue.

Level 57:

Forlorn Deeds - This is a killer melee debuff when a shaman is not around. It reduces the attack speed of the target by 70%, effectively making their damage output only 30% of what it is capable of.

Umbra - Your last AC spell, this one gives an extra 23AC. It is once again helpful when there is no cleric there for AC buffing.

Enlightenment - Increase your target’s (or your own) INT and WIS by 21 points each. This is very good for both casters and hybrids for stunningly obvious reasons.

Tashanian - This is your last single target tashan spell. It takes off a maximum of 39 MR and helps you land your other spells bigger and better. It also carries a big taunt so be careful!

Level 58:

Wonderous Rapidity - This is the single best castable haste spell in the game, how could you not like it? This spell gives a whooping 70% haste to anyone that it is cast upon. You will be forever loved.

Bedlam - Melee buff increasing strength by a whooping 50 points but lowering agility by 25. It also has a built in componentless 350 damage rune.

Glamerous Visage - This spell gives your target 18 seconds of reduced agro output (30% less). It is good to cast on a wizard about to do something big, or perhaps a rogue who seems to be getting a bit too much attention from his backstabbing ways.

Dementing Visions - This is a low-resist enchanter DD / spell interrupter. It does 675 damage.

Fetter - Your last root (aka poor man’s mez) spell! This one can last up to 180 seconds if you don’t bother the monster much. It can be effective with a well trained group in an open area and lasts much longer than a mesmerize spell.

Level 59:

Rapture - This is an almost unresistable mesmerize spell which lets an enchanter keep one monster down pretty much indefinitely. However, because of re-cast time and duration as well as the high mana cost this spell would not be able to keep two or more monsters mezzed.

Asphyxiate - Your last DoT Spell. This one breaks the 1000 damage barrier for a total of 1010dmg over 2 minutes. It also has the effect of crippling STR and AGI by up to 40 each. Combined with Cripple you can take down your opponents Strength and Agility by 115 points each. The nice thing about this is that for anyone carrying 100 or so lbs worth of stuff it will make them either unable to move or close enough to it that it won’t make a difference.

Gift of Pure Thought - This one is a group clarity II. Easier on the mana and quicker than buffing everyone sperately

Level 60:

Well you are here. You made it to the height of your career. Get the ridiculously rare ancient spells and you will be in an elite club of level 60’s who have every spell available to them. Congratulations! Because some level 60 spells are so difficult to get the order doesn’t really matter. You should get them all ASAP!

Koadic’s Endless Intellect - Kai is one of those Holy grails. It is the ultimate Everquest drug. It is essentially not only clarity III but a group clarity III with some extra bonuses. This baby increases mana regen by 14 per tick, increases INT and WIS by 17 each and gives you an extra 250 mana in your pool. People pay 50pp+ for this spell on some servers so you could probably making a living being a drug dealer. All hail the Luclin expansion!

Dictate - This is a fixed duration charm. Yes you heard that correct, fixed duration. Once under the influence you have a guaranteed 48 seconds before this monster comes to beat the crap out of you. Enjoy!

Ancient: Eternal Rapture - this upgrade to rapture is either completely or virtually unresistable. It lasts around 30 seconds. This spell only drops in the toughest of Luclin zones

Gift of Brilliance - Adds 150 mana to your pool and gives you an extra 2 mana per tick regeneration. I’m not sure whether or not it stacks with KAI.

Ancient: Chaotic Visions - A very low resistance nuke that does 775 damage. Easily your best DD spell. As always it interrupts casting and is all-or-nothing.

Speed of the Brood - This group buff is incredibly powerful. It gives everyone a 66% haste (second best haste spell in the game), increases agility by 40 points and gives some armor class. It also lasts 30 minutes compared to wonderous rapidity’s 20 minutes. Don’t forget this does it to EVERYONE in the group (or target group).

Visions of Grandeur - An upgrade to the augment line of spells this one lasts 42 minutes and gives 58% haste, 40 AGI, 25 DEX and 15ATK. Unfortunately it doesn’t stack with the Shaman buff Avatar.

Wind of Tishanian - Renamed: Upgraded suicide. This AE tashan does 40MR damage to each monster in the radius and successfully gets every single one of them to beat the living shit out of you. The only non-suicidal use for this spell would be in a group PvP situation.
