Men are from Ogguk. Women are from Kelethin.

EverQuest appeals to male and female players in different ways, and motivates them to continue playing EverQuest for different reasons. Female players find the social interaction of the game more appealing than the male players do (link). Male players, on the other hand, enjoy the sense of power the game gives (link). Several respondents articulated this difference:

He is really turned on by the power and prestige aspects of the game, whereas I use it as an extended chatroom. So we had some conflict over me getting twinked out purely because I was a real woman. [f, 19, dating]

It's something we both enjoy, but we tend to enjoy it seprately. I enjoy it because of the social aspect, while he just enjoys the game itself. [f, 23, engaged]

Men like this game more. Men tend to be more agressive and therefore are driven by item acquisition more than women. My wife has not shown much desire to play EQ even though she has a very simular personality to mine. She is not driven by the goals within the game; even though in real life she will be driven to complete a project and carefully add to it to make it better. The women that I have met in EQ are almost always people who want to play the game for social interaction. To them the game is not about getting items or even levels, its about talking to friends while they do it. Something very interesting I have observed; men who play women tend to level much slower than men who play men. They are more social and less driven to get one more item. I have found them to grow frustrated at the long camp times for items they really need. [m, 26]

Female players are attracted to the social aspects of EQ, whereas male players are more drawn to the ability to feel powerful. Thus, it is not surprising that female players are more likely to group with people they've grouped with before (link), while male players are more likely to be goal-oriented (link). As an example,

For instance, I will rather play in a group just for company, even if the exp gain is minimal, whereas my partner tends to literally AVOID other players. [f, 23, engaged]

Moreover, we also find that female players are more likely to be in officer or leader positions in guilds(link). Female players are also more likely to participate in guild events (link) and to enjoy being in guilds (link).

In a sense, EQ is mainly just a game to male players, whereas for female players, it is a place where real friendships can be made. Male players look for hunting partners, while female players look for chat partners. For male players, communication is the side-effect of coordinating group strategy. For female players, EQ is a huge chat-room with a lot of other things to do. And thus, female players are more likely to feel that their EQ friendships are comparable, if not better, than their real-life relationships (link). Because female players are more likely to be forging real relationships, they are also more likely to feel that a lot can be learned from EQ (link).

Female players are also more attached to their characters, and are less likely to sell their characters for money than male players are (link). This is probably because their characters have emotional relevance to them from the relationships that have been forged with it, whereas for male players it is a game, and there is less emotional attachment to their characters.
