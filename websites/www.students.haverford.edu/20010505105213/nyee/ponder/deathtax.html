Death and Taxes


        Mandatory organ donation at death would certainly cause a public uproar. Side-stepping the moral question of whether dead people have rights, let's take a different perspective to this scenario.

        At one level, we seem to resist the idea because it is a mandatory theft for the greater good. We don't like the idea that we have to give something up without a choice. But realistically speaking, taxation is both these things. More important and relevant to this scenario is the reality of death taxes. Our estates are taxed when we die.

        Now here is a very amazing pair of thoughts. It is without doubt that the taxable estate is of some value, whereas the organs of a dead person are of no value to his immediate family (unless they sell them, but most don't). Yet the former is accepted without any overt resentment, and the suggestion of the latter would cause an uproar. It seems absurd that we would give something of value away willingly, and fight to keep something that is useless.

        But consider a scenario where the government gave you the choice between the two. Either your estate will be taxed or some of your organs may be taken. Would most people still choose to be taxed? Is it the case that nothing in life certain but death and taxes?
