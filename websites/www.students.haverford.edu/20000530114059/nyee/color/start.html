First of all, I'd like to thank the 232 people who responded to my questionnaire. It felt a little harder to get people to respond to a study where their interests were not the primarily element under investigation. I had to advertise the questionnaire in more places, and on the way learned a little more about what works and what doesn't in terms of this kind of advertising. In any case, I'm very happy with the number of responses I got and it reassured me that 20-30 minutes of knowledgeable advertising on the Web can get you over 200 responses consistently.

Like the RPG study, I started out wanting to make a presentation that would be easy to understand even for people who had never touched psychology or statistics. As I began writing up the results though, I realized that these constellations of small, yet significant, correlations make it hard to make satisfying statements that are specific or of high certainty. So in the end, I added sections that simplified matters and wrote up a sample reading that demonstrates how this might work out. People who have studied psychology or statistics will probably share the same sense of mixed excitement and frustration as I had in performing the data analysis.

If you are not acquainted with psychology or statistics, read the Introduction and Method. Then skim the Results and go on to the Sample Reading. After the Sample Reading, you will have a much better idea of what the numbers in the Results page mean.
