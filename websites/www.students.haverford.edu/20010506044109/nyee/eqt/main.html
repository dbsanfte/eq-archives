EverQuest Study

 Nicholas Yee '01, Haverford College

Thank you for participating in my study of EverQuest. This study began on 10/06/00, and is part of my senior thesis research in psychology.

The purpose of this ongoing study is two-fold. First, I want to understand in greater depth some of the dynamics and interactions that my previous studies shed light upon. More importantly, I want to build a database of EQ players that will allow me to analyze responses across all the surveys you have completed. This database will also allow me to research finer questions because I can target a specific subset of the EQ population.

As I understand more of a certain aspect of EQ, this knowledge will prompt new surveys targeting that area. That is why this study is ongoing. You will be invited to respond to surveys as they become available. The strength of this study will rely on being able to refer to your responses across different surveys. Thus, your continued participation will be deeply appreciated.

I would also really appreciate it if you could spread the word of this ongoing study by telling EverQuest gamers of this webpage address. I am trying to include as many EQ players in the database as I can. These forms will be up until May 20th 2001.

Please direct all comments and questions to Nicholas Yee.

Old surveys and questionnaires that are still open for responses are listed separately for the convenience of returning participants. New participants should feel free to complete them.

Current Phase (April 2001)

Questionnaires

Demographics: 27 multiple-choice questions on both in-game and out-game demographics.

Addiction: 21 multiple-choice questions on different aspects of addiction to the game.

Etiquette and Morality: 12 multiple-choice questions on whether you think certain behaviors and actions in the game are right or wrong.

Elements of Appeal: 30 multiple-choice questions on what elements in computer games you find appealing.

Past Phases

Personality Inventory Inventory Set 1: This personality inventory set will allow me to analyze your responses in terms of personality dimensions, and to connect this data to past studies I have done. (10/6/00)
