Here's the math. It'e pretty self explanatory.

Abolish Disease = 6 seconds cast + 2.25 delay. Total time to cure group = 47.25 seconds

Disinfecting Aura = 2.5 seconds cast + 2.25 delay. needs 2 casts = 9.5 seconds. total time to cure group = 54.5 seconds.

Nadox = 7.5 seconds cast + 2.25 delay. Total time to cure group = 17.25 seconds

Adding in any spell haste simply makes Nadox even better compared to the others, because spell haste does not affect the recast time, the Nadox cure route has 1 lot of spell recast delay, the others have 5 or 11 in order to do a full group cure.

Lets add in 15% beneficial spell haste and see what we get....

Abolish Disease = 5.1 seconds cast + 2.25 delay. Total time to cure group = 41.85 seconds

Disinfecting Aura = 2.125 seconds cast + 2.25 delay. needs 2 casts = 8.7 seconds. total time to cure group = 50.25 seconds.

Nadox = 6.375 seconds cast + 2.25 delay. Total time to cure group = 15 seconds

Add in network lag - and you favour nadox even more, as you only have ther lag twice, as opposed to six or twelve times.

Honestly, TRC has two difficult things you have to conquer to beat it.

The first is the pulls. That's worked out by your pull team without the raid even being present, generally, but it takes a mix of skilled skilled debuffers & taggers, and often a cleric out there rezzing them as they go. I've seen a magician/shaman/druid three man team pull the whole thing, but they're not normal guys; it usually takes bard shenanegans and some necros snaring & feigning.

The second challenge is the time involved. If everyone on a large raid, like 72 PoP-style large, knows what their part is, then the event can be done in about a hour. A *hour.* Your enchanters basically don't get to afk at all for that time unless you have extras that can cover the slack, and the clerics don't get much rest either. I think most raids beating TRC for the first time take more like *2* hours. It's a pretty long time to not-****-up.
