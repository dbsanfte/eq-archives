Edit: Leap-Frog Method explanation cut from the language learning guide on my guild's website

3. The Leap-frog method.
This method can be used with a friend if neither of you have high skill in the language you want to know.

Group with a friend and teach them what language you know by spamming them (they can learn up to 2 skill points higher than yours). Then they spam you until you are higher and you repeat the process

To switch languages with hotkeys, write the line "/language x", where "x" is the number of the language you want to switch to.

Leibeast I believe I know the answer to that one. Language skills max at a level of 100. The text you are trying to read can be coded to be trivial at over a skill of 300 now. Try clicking on the text several times to see if you can get a good "read" on the text. If you are real interested in deciphering the text you can fall back on the old brute force method characters used to use before anyone mastered all the languages. If the text is above your skill level to read each time you click on it you will get a slightly diffrent translation. Just keep clicking the text and after a while it will start to make sense from the bits and pieces that are translated correctly. If you click on the text repetedly and get the exact same translation every time it probably is a bug or "feature". Hope this helps. Good huntin'. :)

As far as I know the maximum skill you can achieve for any language is still 100. But the programers have the ability to set the trivial level far higher than anyone can achieve. Like in smithing even with a skill of 250 there are many recipes with trivials higher than can be reached. With languages the diffrence between your skill level and the set trivial determines how much of the message will be gibberish. The true test is to repetedly click the message, if it changes it's just set higher than you can achieve. If it stays the same and you're at a skill of 100 it's bugged and ya should send in a /bug report. Hope this helps, good translating. :)
