Perelandra
As a Mage who 2boxes a shaman, here's what works best:

Mage Pet:
Burnout 5 (mage spell)
Focus
Primal Essence/FA
Agility (only if pet is tanking)

Shaman Pet:
Focus
Primal Essence/FA
Haste

Never bother with haste on a mage pet unless he is very low level. Burnout 5 has 85% haste on it and while your haste will stack, only the higher will take effect.

How stats effect pets
Strength: Makes pet hit for max more often
Stamina: No effect - don't bother
Agility: AC/Pet gets hit less
Dex: PROCS - MAX THIS. If the pet has proccing weapons, the higher you can get his Dex the better.

A pet with summoned mage gear will have a significantly higher DPS with buffs than without them.

On my Cleric I NEVER used to buff pets, but recently I picked up my long abandoned Necro cause I wanted to try some LDoN adventures with her. It was on one of these LDoN adventures that my view on buffing pets changed completely.

Our mission was an assassination. We had tough goings through the mission, our healer was a Druid and she was having a bit of a tough time keeping up on healing our 2 Warriors. I was twitching as much as I could and slowing undead.

We were about 3 minutes from completing our adventure. The Enc paci'd the room and we pulled. Bad news, 2 inc. Chanter mezzed one quickly and the Warriors attacked our target. The Druid healed one of the Warriors and drew aggro which the War's could not pull off in time. Healer dead. A few seconds later, War 1 dead. I tried my best to keep the other tank alive, but my little heal spell was not enough. Tank 2 is at 30%hps, Enchanter goes LD. Mob 2 wakes up and heads straight for tank 2, I feign death hoping that my pet will stay alive long enough to kill our target and rack up a success for us. Tank 2 goes down. Basically our whole group died except for me and the LD Enchanter. My pet is at 40%hps and dropping fast with 2 mobs beating on it. I check our targets hps and he's at 1%!! He goes down, we get the successful adventure notice (to the cry of many "WOO HOO's!" from my fallen group mates). The add kills my pet and wanders off to it's spawn point.

The lesson I learned? The Druid and Chanter dropped all the buffs they could on my pet. Keeping it hasted, HP/AC buffed and thorned. Yes it could be seen as a waste of mana, but if my pet had not been buffed and kept buffed we would have failed our mission for sure. Now my Cleric makes sure to keep all pets buffed to the best of her ability.

I'm sure it can be argued that if they had not been keeping buffs up like they were we could have moved through the adventure faster and it would not have been so close, but... It was a bad pull that caused the wipe. It wasn't our pullers fault, he had been doing a great job all night. Bad pulls happen, and you'll never know when you need that pet to tank for you...
