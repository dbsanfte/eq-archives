Tragik
It really depends on what you're going for. A lot of named in Veksar can be singled out of the room (without FD pulling) and for the double pulls you can use root to pull one, get them both slowed and work them down slowly with pet, epic, LDoN dot and the occasional ice nuke.

If you're looking for shaman usuable gear you can try:

1. Chef camp for the Kylong Darkmail Girdle.

2. Highborns (rough mobs 58 - 63) for the gauntlets.

3. Shop camp for the shield and tunic (haste belt and 2hb also drop here - there are two floors of mobs in the front shop camp. Right off from the golems).

4. Temple/Warlock camp drops another belt with right click summon companion (works from inven) along with a 1hp.

5. Hands drop an affliction efficiency 3 ring and a named Warlock in the Theatre drops a spell haste 3 ring.

6. There is also an FT2 necklace that drops in the sewers. Stats are so so and I never bothered going for it.

Plenty of other camps also. You always have the option of hitting a couple of the easier rooms for just the loot drops to sell then buy what you want in the bazaar. Except for the nobles dogdog can tank just about everything here. Nobles hit for up to 464 btw, depending on level. You'll need to torpor tank these and the fight will be looooooong if you're sporting average gear (didn't look).
*EDIT: Just reread your post and realized you don't have torpor so I would really recommend skipping these if you're solo.

Anyway, good luck. If you're just after loot and farmage fun then speed of kills really doesn't matter. Carry a couple of ITU 10 charges with you for the undead and remember that aggro and assist ranges are very small in Veksar. Common sense can often make it clear what order to pull in for singles without having to resort to root or VP pulls.

Goblins and Golems are the only live mobs as well. Everything else is undead.

*EDIT: I usually do this place with my necro. The combination of snare (for pulling/splitting), undead slow, undead mez, superior pet, fast taps, fire/magic dots, forceshield make this much easier then doing the same with my shaman. As a shaman I recommend grabbing a nec/bst/mag or any of the tank able melee classes like pal/sk/war/mnk/rng.

Tragik
Breaking shop can be fairly easy if you get lucky on a long lasting root. When you enter from the golem area you'll pass through 2 rooms with a single mob in each. Kill these then proceed towards the 3rd and final 1st floor room with 3 spawns (2 mobs + PH/named).

The way I split these is to move into the room just far enough to cast on the mob closest to me. Root him and the middle spawn will come alone. Sic the pet on it for some breathing room while you land slow and drop a root on it to keep it focused on the pet. Now edge back into the room and slow/reroot the 1st mob and finish killing the "add".

That's it, room is broken. Kill the parked mob next then pull the PHer/named. Once the room is cleared move to the bottom of the steps and setup permanent camp right there. This way you only have to deal with the 3 room pops and nothing else. Remember to keep tight control over the pet if you don't have /pet hold. Some of the spawns do HT and cast and having the pet run into melee range too soon can be hazardous to your health.

The chain tunic drops off the named on the 2d level of the shop. To single pull those use the same tactic for the 1st floor except you're going to root the middle spawn and aggro the mob closest to you. Kill him at the bottom of the stairs, then pull the 2d (rooted mob). The PHer/named on this floor is placed out of assist range so he'll never add to a pull here.

Last bit of advice on this camp. Take the loots you obtain here including the tunic if it drops and sell it all in the bazaar. Pickup some Tae Ew with that cash. Better stats and even though it's purchased armor you still earned it fair and square. Get the haste belt to drop and that'll pay for a damned near full set (the good pieces anyway).

Good luck to you.
