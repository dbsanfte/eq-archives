Baz4
To get "full points" (meaning level 65 adventure points):

The group as a whole must average 63.0 or higher


So - if you have 5 level 65's in the group and then add the lowest level that can accept an adventure with them (57 or 58 or 59, forget which) you will average more than 63.0, and you will get the "full points."


The flip side is that, if you want to get the next easier level of adventure, you need a group that averages less than 63.0. For example, if you have a couple of level 59's ya want to take through an adventure, and decide to play it safe by getting the level 60 adventure, then don't add too many level 65's to fill out the group.

65+65+65+65+65+58 = 383 / 6 = 63.83 avg level (will be level 65 dungeon)

65+65+65+58+58+58 = 369 / 6 = 61.5 avg level (will be level 60 dungeon)

65+65+65+65+58+58 = 376 / 6 = 62.67 avg level (will be level 60 dungeon)

63+63+63+63+63+62 = 377 / 6 = 62.83 avg level (will be level 60 dungeon)

63+63+63+63+63+63 = 378 / 6 = 63.0 avg level (will be level 65 dungeon)

Dalarian
I'm curious as to how the number of players in your group can effect the dungeon? Say going in with 4 people instead of 6?


Have some experience with this as most of my current shaman's leveling time has been in LDON.

The only thing that seems to affect the dungeon difficulty is the group's average level and whether the dungeon is normal or hard. 4 person groups get the same number of mobs etc. as 6 person groups. I don't notice fewer mobs with less people or more mobs with a full group.

As for what average level do you get the 'next hardest' dungeon at, its either x2.5/x7.5 or x3/x8, not positive on which. So doing a hard dungeon at 53(get the level 60 dungeon)/58(get the level 65 dungeon)/63 (get the 65 hard(70) dungeon) is really a pain, doing them at 52/57/62 is much easier. At 51 in a hard most mobs con blue/even with the nameds conning even/yellow.
