taraddar
AC is definitely useful. Gone are the days of ac not mattering and hps being the key factor in survivability.

In high end pop encounters, IE elemental planes, the tanks are finding that ac is as important if not more important than hps now. Ac is having a very definite impact on how hard a mob hits for.

For example my guild was in PoF last night killing some named. One named we where killing hit for up to 2924 (ouch that really hurts) and rampages every round even when crippled. Our mt was getting hit for an average of around 1200 a hit. Our rampage tank was getting hit for an average of 1900 a hit. Thats a pretty significant difference for a ~200 point ac difference. I've seen reports from furor and other high end guilds doing the gods and such that it continues to make a difference threw 1900+ ac.

With that said how ever I'm not sure how realistic it is for shaman to try and get thier ac up to that high of a level. If we could it would definitely be worth it. I have no trouble beleiving that 1350 ac or some other simular achievable number for a shaman can make a significant difference for shaman only lower end mobs like BoT or PoV mobs etc. I don't expect to be tanking that mob we did in pof last night ever tho =)
