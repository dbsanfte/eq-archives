Warador
Our slows are checked against magic to land, but add disease counters. They did this so that our slows won't get dispelled when people dispell mobs and also when a tank gets slowed by a mob a cure disease will get rid of it instead of having to dispell the tank and possibly stripping his buffs in the process.

Until you get malo or malos my advice would be to cast your slow before you try malosinia (or whatever level spell you're at) and if slow gets resisted then toss on a maloxxx while waiting for your slow to refresh. I say this 'cause you have the same chance of your slow landing as you do your maloxxx. With malo or malos the opposite is true of course (they're unresistable).
Jinheim
It's all in your head. You are expecting ID to help you slow, so when you ID and then slow lands, you attribute it to ID, and when slow bounces anyways, you right it off as an exception to the rule. This is the same kind of thing that makes people think charisma helps you slow, agility makes you dodge more, etc... I bet if you thought about it for a second, you could find about a billion real life examples of this same thought process at work (big one: religion ;) ). I assure you that turgur's is 100% magic based as far as resists go. It has disease counters, but it definately checks against magic.
Barliman
Warador's post summed up most of it. Disease debuffs do absolutely nothing in terms of your chance of landing our higher pecentage magic based slows (Togors, Turgurs, etc). If you feel it does it is because you've thought that way since level one. At 56 I hardly ever had to debuff a mob to slow it so you probably just haven't noticed it yet but as you level you will see many times even normal experience group mobs will need some debuffing. This will become extremely evident as you start raiding.

For example long ago when I had just reached 60 I was raiding HoT. I was one of two shaman on the raid but didn't have malo (unresistable). So the other shaman would malo and I would slow. Things went extremely smooth for the first couple of hours and I was usually landing slow on the first try. When the shaman with malo had to leave it got extremely bad without malo. After 60 minutes, 3 dead mobs and two wipes we had to call it quits because no one could get the mob slowed. I decided right then that I would spend 100% of my time working on saving up for malo and after about 6 weeks I bought it for 60k. The next week I went back with malo as the only shaman and everything went great.

For now as Warador said don't worry much about debuffing because malosi has the exact same chance of landing as slow. The only instance I'd really debuff first is if you can't take the beating of the mob unslowed. Malosi has almost no agro so you can cast that all you want until it lands and then slow to avoid agro of chain slowing.

I use the Slow first - Malo if resist - slow again technique frequently. But another technique is to have both Turgur's and Togor's loaded, and if Turgur's bounces then throw Togor's. Gets you a lot of aggro, especially if both are resisted, but gets the mob slowed a bit faster.
