Valsuvious
really most of the fights depends entirely on your equipment and such, especially on something that summons.

for this fight I suggest that you pull him from as far away as you can with MD or malos. When I pull named, I liked to use MD and then vp when i can, using my max extended focus to enable me to have an extra split second to debuff or whatnot. After MD hit VP when you can. Then I do all the rest of debuffs.. using malos and slow and putrid decay if I feel like it. Then, I start doting him. I would suggest using at least one disease dot.. your best one or maybe even two of them. He won't normally summon until say 96 - 97% so you can get a tick or two off on him before he will summon. Depending on how many you use, use your best magic dot after that.

Me, personally I usually always use four dots, sisslak, bane, yoppa and blood. This will drain the mana just a bit, but it will also killhim quicker. The hardest problem that you will have is he will summon every 4 seconds, so there is no way that you will have time to back away from him and then get a dot off in time. Therefore, your only form of dps is to let him attack you. I would stick your best HoT on you first, making sure that you always have it up, and then apply your dots. If they run out before you can refresh them, start with your best ones again first no matter what. Even if you still have one of them on him and by the time that you finish casting another one, that other will run out use the best one. It's more important that it's always on tthe mob, instead of keeping up the weaker ones and leaving the best ones out of the fight.

use spirit pets early. Fight will be shorter if you do.

for me it's a lot about gear. I solo RCoD named, but I don't take them for granted. Haven't really had a problem yet doing any of the NC named, but like I said.. it's mostly about gear and AAs.
