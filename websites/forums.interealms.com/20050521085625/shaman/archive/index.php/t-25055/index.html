Yeah. At level 10 if you are going to follow the path of Jeweller, Sage or Alchemist you select the "Scholar" tree. From level 10-19 anyone in the Scholar class can scribe any of the books labeled "Scholar" or "Alchemy" or "Jewellcraft"

Once you hit level 20, you select one of the three branches and are closed off from the other two professions forever. (With the exception of still being able to make the 10-19 recipes.)

The reason I already classify them as "Sage" and "Alchemist" is because my Barbarian Shaman has worked on Runes and Essences exclusively since level 10, and my High Elf Summoner has worked on Spell Scrolls exclusively since level 10.

Thus each has a greater skill in their given field at this point. (Since the sub combines and final combines take so long to do, I haven't bothered to increase their skills outside their field much, I'm trying to level them in adventuring at a reasonable rate too lol!)

One last reason is that as a Barbarian I get +5 to my Chemistry skills as a racial trait at level 18, and my High Elf gets a +5 bonus to the Scribe skill at level 18, making them naturally more adept at making the items in their profession.

I could have overlapped their skills, but it would have taken more time and effort, and wouldn't have been as efficient in the long run.

Hope that long winded explanation helps!
