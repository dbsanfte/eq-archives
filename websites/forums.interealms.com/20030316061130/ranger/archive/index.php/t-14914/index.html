Yup, must be blue or higher for skill increases. Good way to raise Archery I think is to load up on arrows, or carry stacks of parts and a fletching kit to make 'em as you go. (1 stack of components makes 100 arrows but only takes up 4 slots in a bag) If you're out soloing and you need a med/heal break, ask someone close by if you can shoot their mobs. That's basically how I kept Archery maxxed until dinging 51.

AC is important until it's over 1000 IMO, then focus on HP...you get hit less and take less hits = less gating to bind at the expense of exp :)

Drienne
Skill increases are on any mob light blue or higher. The higher the level of the mob, the better chance you have of gaining skills. In other words, light blues have a very tiny chance of skill increase, blues are much better (with higher level blues increasing your chance), white is better than that, etc. There are a few good places to gain skills. In SRO, there's a female DE mob that wanders around. Something beginning with A. She's blue all the way to 60, and non-aggro. So you could follow her plinking arrows all day if you like (you can also practice your other offensive weapon skills). Secondly, the Nexus Scions. They're level 63 (I think), and you can use ranged attacks without fear of being hit back. Unless they nerfed it, you could sit at a nexus spire and toss arrows at them for skill increases there as well. Just be sure to stay out of melee range. They hit for 2000+.

No the shot does not need to hit for you to get skill ups. And shooting half a bag of arrows is frankly a drop in the bucket of how many arrows you will need to shoot to max the skill eventually.

Also, I would be >very< careful about using the scion as a shooting target. As its a rooted mob that doesnt summon, a GM might not look kindly on you shooting at it. Also if a bard happens to show up and sing an AOE song that hits you, the bard will be on the scions aggro list. Meaning the scion will attack the bard if the bard gets into melee range.

Just shoot at other peoples mobs, or find a nice area and bow kite some blues for a day or 2.
