Kianor
My first main was a ranger, he's 61. I started him back in May 2000, and levelled up through the "rangers suck" era, hoping things would get better by the time I hit 50+, and things did.

Here's a little history: When Kunark came out, the level cap was raised from 50 to 60, and some skill caps were raised accordingly. However, the tank hybrids defensive skill caps were raised, and ranger's offensive caps were raised, but rangers at that time got no defensive skill caps.

Rangers went from being a semi decent tank pre-kunark (although obviously behind warriors and knights) to being unable to tank, and having to go full AC gear to survive. This is when "ranger down" became a common cry. Clerics hated rangers tanking, and even when they weren't tanking, rangers had all the agro from dual wield and no way of removing it. This is when AC became popular for rangers, and replaced dex/agi, hehehe.

However, a number of tweaks since then, such as defensive skill raises, the jolt spell, and much better gear from Velious through to today means that rangers do not die nearly as much as they did, though they're still living down the reputation from the post-Kunark times.

Velious brought new lines of spells for rangers, and Luclin brought AA, in particular archery AA.

With spells and melee, they outdamage warriors with similar weapons, but with archery AA, and a decent bow, they can outdamage rogues, while remaining at a distance with little agro, and in some cases staying out of AE range. This can be invaluable at some raids.

Most rangers who've been around a long time are quite happy to step in and tank if necessary. Some of them even welcome it.

Sadly, a lot of rangers these days are people who started a ranger alt when it became clear how powerful and useful archery AA was, but who still remember the whole "ranger down", "paper tank" phase.

I still laugh at rangers with the "Baron" title who insist on using archery in exp groups though.
