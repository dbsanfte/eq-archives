Players crafted items are definitely better than all drops with the exception of some quest items, some one time drops (otd) and the stats the dropped/quest/otds might have. The last issue will be resolved when spell crafting is finally put into the game. The primary issue here is that most players don't understand how the condition, durability, quality and bonuses work on items. Quality is always at least 90% from player made items now and an upcoming patch will change that minimum to 94%. This affects not only the effectiveness of the item, but also the longevity of the items. Most drops are only 85% quality. Looking at a piece of armor with an armor factor of 100, the dropped armor would actually only provide 85 AF while the 95% quality player crafted would provide 95 AF. The 95% quality player crafted armor would degrade at a much slower rate also, saving you a lot of money in repairs and replacement costs. Condition, the attribute that degrades as an item is used, also affects the effectiveness of the item in the same manner as the quality does. So that 85% quality dropped armor that has degraded to 90% actually only provides 76.5 AF. Since the "breast plate" and leg armors supply the majority of AF the user actually receives from the armor set, many users will always buy player crafted armor for these slots and then use drops, quested items, otds, etc. in the other slots to gain the attributes they want or need. This will change dramatically when spell crafting is available.
