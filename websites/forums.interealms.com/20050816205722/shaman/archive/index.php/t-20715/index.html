veeshanMurtak
The reason slow mitigation was implemented is that SOE wanted certain encounters to do at least a specified amount of melee damage.

Getting past that will make that encounter much easier, especially if you allow for full slows.

That may be ok in groups - noone cares if one in twenty Poohkas gets slowed fully. But it will make some raids depend on an early slow crit for survival.

Whats worse, the easiest way to not make raids depend on slow mitigation is to make each individual MOB last a much shorter amount of time so the overall impact of a lucky slow is less.

Trust me, we do not want slow crits. ;)

Giac
Crits on slows are retarded for several reasons:

1) Encounters would have to be tuned with crits in mind

2) All a Shaman would do is chain casting Slow hoping to land a crit during fights. Needless to say that this takes time that we have none of

3) Everything that is slowable for 37.6% or more would end up being slowed for more than a not mitigating mob can be slowed currently

4) You are practically bypassing slow mitigation for every mob that isn't *slightly* affected by Slow.

5) Clerics and Druid would get better heals to be able to heal for the new mobs with adjusted dps. The Shaman class would not.

6) More mobs would be flagged Slow immune

I could probably list another 150 points. I can't believe this needs to be explained.
