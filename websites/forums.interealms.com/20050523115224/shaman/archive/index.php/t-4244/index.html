He had a rather long post on the Necromancer board (but it has since been wiped) where he explained why certain spells had been dropping less frequently than they should have been. I don't remember the details, but something about the algorithm not spitting out enough high numbers or somesuch. Apparantly the code looked fine at first glance, which is why they had never bothered to actually test it before. Once he tested it and saw that they were dropping less than they should, it took a while to track down the problem if I recall correctly.

When he figured out how to fix that, the "fixed the spell drop rates" patch went in. My understanding, based on this old post, was that this bugged rarity was removed for all kunark spells (unless you are of the "malosini should be malo" camp, which is a different bug, if it is a bug at all)

Of course, spells that are on fewer drop tables (like Malo, Torpor, etc.) drop less commonly because they drop from fewer mobs. But they are no longer disproportionally rare within the tables they drop from. What was once a double-whammy (you could only get it from a few mobs AND the loot table was bugged so they spit out less often than they were intended) is now a single whammy (you can only get them from a few mobs). Not that they are common drops now, but neither is any other particular spell on that table.

What anecdotal evidence I have seen supports this. Of course, it is only anecdotal, but this guy has earned some good faith I think.

Of course the single whammy problem gets worse and worse because the numbers of level 60 shamans increases every day while the number of mobs who can possibly drop the spells remains static.

Anyway, I didn't mean for this to degenerate into another spell drop thread. DoT stacking is news enough, I think. If it goes through, I think it will be a really wonderful improvement.
