Pantheon's Dream
© by David Kong, March 2003

Last night, while the world sleeps, I dream of you
In that split second when dream collides with reality,
That special miracle moment where wishes could come true.
You stand before me with your childlike eyes, lovely and sensuous
And at that moment, my heart stops as I tremble before your flawless beauty

Only to your beauty, would I have surrendered my universe to prolong this dream
To relinquish all that is Good and Just and Pure
And embrace the senseless kingdom of false reveries
To be drowned by the sweet emotion that taints the depth of the heart
And accept your poison and torment that tear the immortal soul

For you, my world stops
For you, my heart shatters
Because of you, the world has stopped
Because of you, the world has shattered

Even in regrets, the dream still seems like richness, as I would have gladly taken in the darkness
To find eternal shelter in your shadows
And forgo the Pantheon's Promise
Ceding the Sword of Justice and walk the Path of Deceit
To be forever banished from gods and mortals beyond the bounds of time

Yet, I could dream the same dream a thousand times a thousand times, and
Still I cannot change the outcome of the dream, because in the end,
The dream shall fade, leaving nothing more than the shadow of
A memory of the distance's past and as always,
The dream shall fade, and along with the dream, you.

So here I am, bound by Destiny, and Fated
To be the god's puppet in their eternal struggles
Of Good and Evil, Love and Hate
To be the Champion of Men
Is where I stand

But, for you my love, shall forever remain to be
My joy, my grieve, my precious, my weakness, and my most splendid rose.
