Lacka
I play on Vallon Zek. When I first started on the server it too was coin +1 item loot when you PKed, but the level difference in a fight is 8 levels, where on Rallos I believe it's 4. That means anyone 8 levels above or below you is within your range to kill on Vallon. You used to be able to tell if someone was higher or lower than you with a /con but they changed that some time ago too so now when you /con someone you can only tell if they are in your range. This has greatly cut down on rampant ganking. In the old days some wizard 8 levels above you would basically zap you from behind and waste you with one or two blasts, loot your new breastplate and all your coin, and then gate out and sell your junk before you knew what hit you. Most frustrating.

It is now coin only and I prefer this. This server, originally designed as 4 teams (dark, elves, shorts, humans) is now for the most part light (elves, shorts, humans) vs. dark. There are some purists left if that is how you prefer to play.

I've never played on a blue server and can't imagine life without Pks or worrying about being PKed. It's very exciting.

Paerlith
PvP can be a lot of fun, but it's incredibly unbalanced.

PvP in Everquest was an afterthought, and most new things in the game don't consider pvp, and thus as time goes on, pvp becomes even more unbalanced.

Things that determine the outcome of a pvp fight (in order of importance)

1. Level - This affects everything. Spell and melee power, resists, hit ratio, everything.

2. Equipment - If the guy with a primal weapon hits you for 250, and your weapon only hits for 75, there isn't much you can do to overcome that. If your enemy has resists of 175+, he is mostly immune to your spells (unless you're a wizard)

3. Class - This relates to #2, since gear is so melee focused in the high level game. Melee's get 50dmg 30dly weapons, but all spells are nerfed in pvp. All spells only to 66% of their damage. (This is fair in low level pvp, but after L50, it's just silly)

4. Skill - Sadly, last on the list. Most fights I get into, I either have almost no chance of losing, or almost no chance of winning, because of level, equipment, and class. It's very rare when I get to have a good close fight, where skill actually determines the winner.
