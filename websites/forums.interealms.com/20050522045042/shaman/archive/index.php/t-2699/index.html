Sciek Spellbottler
I live in Chardok for xp typically, but FG is also a cake walk for xp. With pets not aggroing anything anymore it's even better soloing nowadays, although still boring as hell IMO.

Fungus Grove:
Find an unclaimed patch. If you havent screwed up your bug faction there, then back in the back near the Twilight portal is a good area that I find open quite often. Park yer mutt, pull with slow from max distance and hopefully get 2 or less shrooms. Typically they spend the majority of the fight trying to cast on me which is fairly easy to resist with Epuration up. (NOTE: buff DR ;)) Epic Dot, Bane, and Bracer them to death while mutt chews on their backside. It's so simple and easy as to be ridiculous.

Chardok Zone Out:
Typically not camped since there aren't enough mobs to support a whole group in the tunnel, but an easy solo spot for a shaman. Should all be dark blue still at 58, some will go light blue at 59/60. I typically invis and run up to the top of the ramp, you should be on a crest with 2 spawns behind you and 2 more just ahead of you. Pull the nearest mob behind you with slow. at 58 the other one shouldn't aggro and the 2 ahead of you (closer to the zone out) will still be out of aggro range.

Tactic here is to root the mob and slap epic dot on to whittle it down while you summon yer pet, buff it with regrow and haste. Sick DogDog and cast a bane, then follow up with JBBs until root breaks. Rinse and Repeat clearing your way to the zone out, then park pet there and pull at will. Super easy. Let the mutt tank since your ac is kinda low, just keep the mobs rooted and stand out of range while you JBB. With Epic, Bane, and JBB nukes the mobs should drop fairly fast and after slowing your pet can tank these pretty easily. Regrowth plus naturally fast regen on pets will get him close to full just standing around in between pulls. Hell, with JBB you might be able to skip the Bane and use next to no mana other than reapplying root and a heal here and there. :D

Before I got my epic, I solo'd some at 54 and 55 in the Grey. I usually stuck with golems. They aren't very resistant, and aren't hard to kill. I stayed away from the Shissar. They are as bad as the Sentient Suits in Wakening lands as far as poison and disease goes. With an epic and/or jbb they probably wouldnt be to bad though. The rock guys have way to many hp's to really want to xp grind on, although I got one down to 40% health once when I wasn't prepared to fight him (started with 50 mana and 70 health). Never bothered with the skeletons. Some were already light blue at 55.

Maiden's eye - toilers are pretty easy at 56, especially with epic. Not terrible experience I guess. I havent tried solo'ing the Gorangas, but I think they are doable. Some are fairly high level, and kind of resistant. My only advice - If you fightin ME, solo or grouped, never, ever right at the zoneline. This place actually has more trains than Karnor's, and that's saying something.

The plateau in Scarlet Desert is good xp at 56. The Revenants are still blue to the 58 shaman I partner with sometimes. We went up there camping the burning priest several times, and pulled all we could while waiting on his spawn (I think all reavers spawns are about 30 minutes)

Skyfire - there are light blue mobs to a 56, quite common on the side of the zone near the druid port in. Opposite side is pretty much all blue. Some of the mobs are alot easier than others. I aggree with a previous poster. Stay away from the Chromadrakes.
