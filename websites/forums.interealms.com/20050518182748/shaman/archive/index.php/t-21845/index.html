qhor
speaking from an end game point of view, the classes that seem to be in the highest demand, the most useful and consequently the most fun are the specialty classes, or more specifically the classes that are the best in the game at one specific application.

warriors are the best tanks in raid, farm or exp situations in the end game because of their disciplines, superior avoidance/mitigation tables, and excellent agro control (in the "end game").

clerics are, at almost any point of the game, hands down the best healers.

enchanters are the masters of crowd control and have the most useful/necessary buffs.

rogues and wizards are the masters of damage.


after those five classes, i'd say bards and shaman are the next two most desirable/useful classes because of their general utility and enhancement of the above classes' performance (bards with mana regen, overhaste, attack enhancement, run speed enhancement, etc; shaman with a decent hitpoint buff, backup haste, good slows, backup heals and FA.) bards are, in my experience, more useful and fun than shaman but more physically demanding to play well.


i think any of those seven classes would be fun and interesting. if you want to be able to solo, pick an enchanter, bard, shaman or wizard. if you want to be needed and stack well on raids, pick a rogue, wizard or cleric.


those are just my general ideas, of course. the other classes have their own merits to be sure. to reiterate what a few posters wrote above, you should pick a class and race combination that strikes you as interesting and fun, and may hold a bit of roleplay amusement for you.
