I'm already losing interest...

I don't know if it is just me but...

First of all, the game is very beautiful. However, in order to play in such beautiful settings, you need a very powerful, top of the line system. I have an intel 3.0 mhz cpu, 1G ram, Geforce 6600gt and I can only run smoothly in medium setting (balanced mode) with shadow off in most places. When grouping with 4-5 players, especially in dungeons like Blackburrow, the lag is so bad that I have to turn settings way down to minimum and everything becomes so ugly...I just don't feel like playing that way.

Secondly, for some reasons, this game feels like a single player game to me like Ultima 9/10. With locked encounters, I can't help, heal or buff anyone. I also feel that people talk a lot less in this game. I think everyone is too busy doing solo quests including myself. That might change in the future when the game gets more complex but this is how I feel at the moment.

I remeber 5-6 years ago when I first started EQ1, it was a great experience: I was so afraid of dying and lose my mino axe, so happy to get my first pair of magic boots from Runnyeye, so envy of that ranger with 2 well-balanced long swords...I can't find any of that feelings from EQ2. Everything is so well guided with compass, waypoints and detailed in game maps, it is almost impossible to get lost in places like Anotica and Commonland. The new world doesn't give me the sense of danger anymore. I am never afraid of dying now.

The freshness is dying quickly for me. The first time I stepped foot in Blackburrow the lag was so bad that it is almost unplayable unless I put every settings to lowest. Then I was thinking...now what? another $500 to upgrade my system so I can run this game smoother with a little better fps...hrmmm...i don't think so.

It has been 2 days since my last log in...the graphics lag really kills it for me. I think I am going back to EQ1 for a while...well at least my system can handle it.

EQ wasn't the first MMORPG for me, so no "virginity" is lost to it. First game in that genre of all for me was Meridian 59, shortly after that, Ultima Online. Now I don't care that much about M59, but UO was first game I really played, for a long time. Well, long for me anyway, over 2 years.

Later came EQ. I was at first exited as always, heck, new game, new things. I was invited to beta at early stage and during the beta, the game felt different. Heck, even SOE people were like real people, meaning they were there when you called, they were acting like people, listening, goofing off with you.. Me and some of my friends were first people EVER in EQ to see a dragon. Not a "real" one, no, we were in Permafrost getting XP and had one EQ Devs with us, in group, play with us. He changed into many different forms and shapes, showing to us different mobs, so we were actually the first ever to see even many other models as well beside the dragon.

At first EQ was fine, it was new and naturally it was more fun to do things together, as we all were exploring. And we werent familiar with the game. Later however it started to slowly annoy, how the person really matters nothing in EQ. How at first, up to level 20, you are something as a person, but after that, slowly and more and more, you'll become just a number among others and can't do anything yourself, not even your basic everyday XP'ing (I played warrior too). It is very annoying. How you could not really simply explore the world, if you weren't one of the classes who could Gate, SoW, Levi, invis. But, it was still all new, so these gripes were secondary, because the newness and discovery took over them. After couple of years, EQ was dull, but, it were the people who kept me in the game. I still had fun in company of the people, and I liked to do things together with them, even if the stuff we did wasn't what I liked due to the grind. And you know, I actually still am in the game because of the people. I like the people I hang out with and do things together with them, no matter how dull the thing itself may be. I don't even log really on now during non-raid days, because I just can't stand the grinding. When there are raid days, I get on and have fun with the guild, when raid is over, I log.

And EQ hasn't really changed to the better. Heck, it's even worse, as now even more people are just a number from the mass and mean nothing on their own. You still can't really explore anything if you're not one of the classes who can gate, levi, invis, SoW. Yeah you now can get the 'tools' to do this even as a warrior, but those tools work slowly and, no matter that you may now be wearing meanest armor there is and sport biggest buff there are, still die faster then newbie in Blackburrow. SoE finds it fun to add mobs into the game, who still all, everywhere, see through invis, run about 5 times faster then you and can deal over 10k damage within couple of seconds, and even more. And new expansions - all they have brought is just more and bigger grind, on bigger scale. For bigger things, yes, but still a grind.

WoW in this case is like a cool glass of drink on hot summer day. Suddenly, even as a warrior, I can explore the world. I can even solo and quest alone whenever I want it. I actually see the world, and if I get into tight situation, I can get out of it. Not always, at times I still may get too deep into it and bite the dust, and death is annoying still. But, the death isn't the agonizing thing like it's in EQ. And I still enjoy the grouping in WoW, I join any time a good group of good players and we'll go do something crazy. There's LOADS of stuff for solo person, group, groupS, raids. And it's all enjoyable, because it isn't a grind. I have my warrior over 40 and I don't have that sinking feeling I had in EQ at the same level, when I realized that as a person, I am nothing now in the game. Of course, being a working person now for years and years, I don't have as much time at hand as I used to, so my gaming times are casual still (usual gaming, not raiding :) ), but when you have to choose where to spend some quick casual time, between WoW or EQ, then WoW wins here easily. Heck, even EQ2 as there are also plenty to do for casual player.

EQ2, the lag there can be annoying. Heck, it is annoying. With some tweaking, it's working more or less OK. Typica for SOE, request some crazy configuration and let people know that their game is built for "future" technology just to cover up their own bad programming. But, once you're out of the city, the lag is bearable, mostly. And EQ2 has some improvements over EQ, which are handy. Heck, you know, warriors can actually really tank and KEEP aggro from low levels, all because this is now coming from using the skills and not tied to gear. You can even explore as a warrior, it's still dangerous and can get you killed, but it's not outright deadly and impossible as it is in EQ1. EQ2 uses alot of instancing, but this is only for good, as it ensures that the areas don't get too crowded and lagged. And also resolves issues with camping of mobs and/or items. And, heck, you can even solo if you want and that even at higher levels. Naturally, groups can still do more and faster and are more fun, but if you just don't have that time, you can do something quickly, solo. And quests, there are actually quests which are fun and not crazily annoying to do, which give you rewards you can put to use right away and also give very good XP bonus. In any case, it's a step into right direction, but all in all, not much is yet known about it, especially about high level content. I've only seen some shots about one Vox run in beta, it wasn't very detailed but what it did show was that a good and coordinated group and raid work is needed to do it.
