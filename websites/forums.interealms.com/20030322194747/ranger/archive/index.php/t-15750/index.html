Sorronn
The most critical aspect of DKP systems is that for whatever loot entering the system there has to be the same number of points entering the system (so expenditures = income).

If DKP controlled loot drops from these events (and the loot from these events is good enough that most guilds will consider using points to award the loot) then points have to be awarded for attending.

Whether it is "fair" has nothing to do with it. For the system to stay in balance requires that income = outgo. As soon as your point system fails to meet that criteria you will have either inflation or deflation and both of these are bad for your guild.

Inflation will let a tiny minority of senior people sit on massive pools of DKP and swoop in for select items whenever they want (resulting in bitterness amongst the rest of the guild). Deflation will result in either massive rottage of drops or people going negative on DKP, depending on your guild's policy on negative DKP totals.

In any case, with a point system the critical thing that keeps it fairest to everyone is to ensure that for every point entering the system there is an expense going out. In fact, some guilds won't even award fixed points for just raiding. They only award points if items are looted. They they take the total number of points looted, divided by the number of people present, and award each member present that many points.

Your "opinion" doesn't matter alot because DKP is a completely quantified system of accounting for raid attendance and loot distribution. Properly administered, it works well.

The only better system appears to be a single master loot dealer who knows every one's gear, keeps track of what has been dropped, and then awards it without any bias at all and with a goal of upgrading the entire guild. Since most master looters are not capable of both that level of knowledge and that level of selflessness, master loot systems frequently fail due to biases. Of course when they succeed they succeed spectacularly - look at FOH for example.
