The slowability chart shows him as Disease slowable only and he mitigates that. I raided him a few times with various guilds. The first time, I tried to Turgurs pretty much the whole way through the fight (we had so many people on that raid...) and it didn't land once. One tactic for controlling aggro that has worked well for us is to have the Pally cast stuns on him for about 10 - 15 minutes before engaging. Hail,

I just did Zlandicar last night. Druid, Ench, Shaman, Cleric. We had healing out the wazoo, though it really wasn't necessary. I don't think I did more than Q myself and dogdog a few times. Most of the cleric's healing was on the pets who kept getting hit with the AE (we were resisting them).

Tash makes a huge difference. Give the SK and yourself a Tash stick (heck, they are cheap enough, give dogdog and the SK pet each one too) and you should land slow soon after it procs.

I couldn't get Malosinia to stick either yesterday. After Malos, Turgur's (second try), and Velium Strikes were the only things landing reliably, and VS was hitting for partial more often than not. I wouldn't waste mana trying to cast any DoTs (go ahead and try clickies though).

I think you could do it with an SK/Shaman duo if you just stuck to careful mana management and melee. Buff your pets ahead of time (include resist disease) & keep Q or BoT on the pets & your SK all the time. Be sure to give the pets summoned mage swords & keep them alive - their melee damage will be substantial over the long fight. Use Spirit Call right after you slow so it might be up again before Zland drops (it will be a long fight - don't forget to time your slow so it doesn't wear off).

I was honestly very surprised how easily Zlandicar went down with no melees and no real nukers in our group. Next time I'd like to try soloing him. :D

Take care,
