coryn braun
I just wanted to get some input from how some of the other shammies felt about price gouging in the bazaar. Not quite sure if it's affecting all servers, but I'm pretty sure it has. Shamans are continually dropping the prices of all of our potions to outrageously low ones in an attempt to beat the other guy to the sale. I can't say I didn't see it coming, but at the same time, it's a little disappointing.
As a whole, I've felt that alchemy is one of the most unappreciated and undervalued (not to mention unprofitable) skills around - while tailors, smiths, jewelcrafters, and even tinkers are making thousands of plat (sometimes in the range of 10k-25k or more) for their products, we make little to nothing. Some potions cost exorbitant prices just to make, and to be reasonable to other players the cost we sell them at nets little profit. When the bazaar first came out, I was determined to stick to my guns and to continue selling potions at the prices they always sold at; when I got next to no business, I was forced to drop my prices to match those of everyone else. Why bother making shrink potions for 150pp to net a measly 30p profit? There's even gate potions being sold for 190pp - that's 3pp in the pocket! It's absolutely insane. In my opinion, thanks to those shaman, alchemy has been made next to useless. Potions can be made for friends and guildies for utilitarian purposes and when needed on raids, but certainly not used to make a profit. =/

How does everyone else feel about all this?

Hopefully we'll see some improvements on alchemy like we've asked for, with the upcoming balancing. I only get requests for 5 or 6 potions on a regular basis, the others are negligible. No one will even touch the Luclin potions with a 10 foot stick... I won't even bother trying to make them, considering the prices they would have to be sold at to make any decent returns. The +10 save potion would be decent if it was knocked up to say, +20, +25. 10 is a drop in the bucket. =p

Shokar
But this seems to be just as good.

I worked up a spread sheet that offers various profit margins on Potions. Now, in REAL LIFE pricing, what is marked up, say 40% for lower priced items may only fetch a 20% mark-up on the higher priced items. Some stores may sell everything at a straight mark-up...or may adjust profits as needed.

With that in mind, my spreadsheet reflects various profit margins.
What I did was take the average cost of materials, and multiplied by ten. Then, I needed to take into account failures. As everyone knows, the higher you go, the less failures you get. BUT...how to make a formula for that? Well, I figured that with a base failure rate of THREE...this would cover current failures, and past failures. In the Drug Industry, when you pay $100.00 for a bottle of pills - you are not only paying MATERIAL costs, you are paying for research.

I can make 40 sow potions usually in 42 tries. BUT, there was a day that I would have 5-10 failures in 20 tries. SO, with the base failure rate of THREE, I am taking into account materials and research.
