Landragoran Heronwing
House, you asked and I will answer. Taking your hypothetical group: 58 shaman, necro, warrior, rogue, mage, and monk.

First, you need to buy a copy of group regrowth. That's the easy part since I saw like 4 copies for sale in bazaar for under 2k last I looked. This way you can cast the spell once for 600 mana and be done with casting it for 20 minutes. It is the most efficient healing spell shamans get and it is imperative that you mem it.

Second, use the necromancer's dark abilities to their very flexible maximum. Your group regrowth hits him, saving him mana, therefore he should save you mana by contributing with the healing. Even low 50's necros have heal over time spells they can break out that are actually quite efficient mana wise, and their lifetaps counteract the damage the heals do to the necromancer . . . not to mention the dot lifetaps they possess.
A necromancer is a very decent backup healer whose damage dealing capacity is only slightly lessened by this role.

Third, be sure to slow the mobs as soon as they get to camp. Follow up with a fast root on the mob. Warn the melees (read: rogue) that the mob will be rooted so maintain max distance for melee if not the MA.

Fourth, don't epic dot anything in such a group. It's a waste of time that could be used in meditation or cannidancing.

Fifth, get a fungi or iksar regen bp and wear it. I'm sorry if you're poor, but hunting in a place like seb will fix your poverty at least to the point of being able to afford a iksar bp.

Sixth, stay on top of things and wear ac gear to minimize hps lost thru mob aggro. Keep your wis pool over 150 and you should be fine otherwise.

Seventh, establish a "lower limit" for your mana pool beyond which you need to call a med break. For me this limit is 30%, since an overpull with complications at that level might be enuf to get someone killed. I strive to keep my mana above 60% tho, and for the most part this has served to maximize my mana regen capacities.

That's only SOME of the things you can do to make it as the only healer in such a group. Given time, thought, and imagination you should be able to think of many more.

Please tell me what I'm doing wrong. I am a Level 58 Shaman. Let's imagine that my group also includes a Monk, Warrior, Rogue, Magician, and Necromancer (I don't normally play with a Rogue, but I threw them in to replace the Cleric). I keep Regrowth on myself, the main tank, and the Necromancer. I give Celerity to the Monk, Warrior, and Rogue. I cast Turgur's Insects and Root on everything that we fight. We're killing a MOB every 30-45 seconds. For argument's sake, we get an add every 5 minutes, which takes off 400 hit points from me and requires two Roots before it's under control.

Where do I possibly find the mana to cast Chloroblast? I didn't even factor in time for refreshing buffs, so I'll just assume that everyone takes a bathroom break every 75 minutes and I just go in the bucket under my desk so I can continue to cannidance.

I think that one mistake many shamans make is trying to do it all. And when they are the healer for the group they STILL try to do it all. The main tip I'd give you for that group is to let the necro do more. Necros can do way more than just dish out damage in an exp group. As already mentioned, they can help significantly with healing, especially if you're already slowing and casting regen. Also, they can mez. It's called Screaming Terror, and they get it sometime in the L 29-34 range. It's a three tick mez, which is all that a bard's mez lasts for, and a bard can mez just fine in Sebilis. If you've only got an occasional add that needs to be controlled, your puller is doing very well and a necro worth his salt should be able to handle that much CC while still asleep. ;)

Most of the rest of that group doesn't have a lot in the way of abilities to help with healing or CC, but the pets could even be used to tank slowed mobs if needed (and they wouldn't need healing unless they had to do it multiple pulls in a row). With the DPS that group would have, mobs wouldn't last long.

Also... why are you casting root on every mob you fight? If the warrior can't hold aggro, then either he needs to taunt better or your other groupmembers need to learn to control their own aggro better. Root simply should not be needed for aggro control. If it's to stop runners, that's another job the necro should be doing. He has darkness dot's with snare, and he can prevent runners better than you can. What's the root for?

As has been said, you're severely limited in what you can do by your self-imposed restrictions. But if you're going to play that way, you need to make some effort to get the things you need. You can get an iksar regen bp by camping it (with a tracker's help). You can get a lot of gear that'll raise your wisdom higher than 118 in solo camps, if that's what you want to do.

Just my $0.02,
