Juujuu
MGB is very useful, if you actually raid. If you don't, then it's a waste of points.

If you play only occasionally, and plan to get level 70, and want the AAs that mean the absolute most to you and your group, go for:
-MC3 and Canni5 asap.
-RC1 is highly useful, and only costs 2 points. One of the best AAs ever for it's cost.
-VP1 is somewhat useful. It's something that can easily be skipped, but there's many situations where being able to push mobs back is nice. It's also a root that works off something other than magic, which can be a blessing. It's an AA, so you can cast it immediately after casting another spell, which is also nice (no spell refresh delay).
-Mnemwhatever the 9th spell gem. Only 3 points? I'd have payed 30. Nuff said.
-Shaman res AA ability. Summoning the cleric back to their corpse after a bad situation and saving the group huge time getting them back? Yes please.
-Group Heal over time. Get 3 levels of this. Group HoT is hot.
-Spiritual Channelling is big too, but if you don't have the HPs to power it, it's dangerous.

Not too many other AAs are really OMG gamechanging.

veeshanMurtak
The more I use Spiritual Channeling the more I like it. Right now I consider it one of my best AAs, right besides Call of the Wild and ahead of Cannibalisation. Yes, ahead. Cannibalisation may give more mana per hour but the effect of Spiritual Channeling is so immensely powerful it feels like playing a different class. As someone else remarked in another thread the ability to chainheal for 5 minutes straight is hard to overestimate.

That being said I agree with most of the posters here as to what AAs are great. I do not really think grinding out tons of AAs at 65 is a great idea but they will not come that much slower then at 70 so at least you are not losing much in the long run.

Given your Shaman/Beastlord/shadowknight group this would be the order in which I would spend my AAs (not listing prerequisites):

1. Spiritual Channeling (Just amazing, have to wait until level 70 though)
2. Canni 5 (Because mana is good, especially when you mainly group)
3. Call of the Wild (Can be a huge timesaver, have to wait until level 70 though)
4. Mnemonic Retention (Even 9 gems is not enough)
5. Virulent Paralysis (Your regular groups features no crowd control except the little you can provide. This will help.)
6. Spellcasting Mastery (For you SCM3 equates to FT5 and 320 mana)
7. Radiant Cure (RC1 is just sooo cheap it seems like a crime not to get it)
8. Quick Buff 2 (To cut down Torpor/Quiescence/Trushar to 3 secs or near that)
9. Spirit Call (Offtanks/Supresses summoning/interrupts)
10. Healing Increase/Healing Crits (Looks like you will be the main healer quite often)
11. Durability, Avoidance, Mitigation (Extra survivability is always good, but with a Shadowknight tanking this should be low on the list)
12. Group HoT (Extra instantcast healing is always good)
13. Buff Extension (Extra healing is always good)
14. Spellcasting Subtlety (Less aggro is good, but as you have a shadowknight tanking ...)

Anything past Spirit Call are things I would not be willing to give up levels for myself. Your mileage may vary.

veeshanMurtak
What may also work for you is basing your AAs around your causes of death.

Died to getting slow aggro and getting beat down? Get defensive AAs or spellcasting subtlety.

Died to running out of mana and not being able to heal the tank? Get Canni 5 or spellcasting mastery.

Died to not being able to control adds? Get Virulent Paralysis.

Don't find yourself dying at all? Play in more difficult places until you do.

Your friend is wise; being thoughtful and cautious often pays off. But I think in this instance her fear is misplaced.

When you get a random group at level 65 there is some small danger that someone may say "What!? You're level 65 and you don't have BlaBla!?" /disband /publish-horrible-reputation-stuff-about-you

But it is actually more likely that you will get in reputation trouble for: being a loot whore; being afk at critical moments; not knowing how to play your class; not knowing how to work with other people.

As to what order you get aa. Cannibalize 5 aa may be more significant than Level 65.

Maybe not. Level 65 grants you the spell Quiescence, which even with no aa helping will be game changing for your groups. As you gain aa to strengthen it -- quick buff, extended buff, spell casting mastery -- it provides a lot of healing for very little mana.

I'd certainly say that the combination of Cannibalize 5 and Quiescence is so powerful that there is no other aa you 'obviously' ought to get sooner than that.

(There are very powerful aa available at level 70, but you won't be there for quite a while. And clearly you can't plan to get level 70 aa before you get level 65 :) )
