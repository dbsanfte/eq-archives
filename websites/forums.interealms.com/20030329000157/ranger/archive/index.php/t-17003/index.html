Jelanen
Just to go kick a dead horse some more...
There are four types of haste in EQ, you can stack different types, but not same type (ie two haste items won't stack)

1. Item haste (FBSS, Envenomed Moccs, SCHW, etc)
2. Bard haste (certain bard songs stack, some don't, but its the bard's problem, not yours)
3. Spell haste (Celerity, etc)
4. Quiver haste (Fleeting quiver, stacks with all the other hastes, but only applies to archery. Also haste still works with EQ)

Again, most people know this, but sometimes its good to rehash something to refresh the memories. The only reason I can see getting rid of the fleeting quiver is if you didn't want archery to be your main form of combat. If you wanted to be a melee ranger, then you can prolly find something better to go in that inventory slot.

Venture
The only difference between "Bard haste" and "spell haste" is when you are discussing the Bard songs that give "v3 haste" according to Lucy. AFAIK, that's just the Vah Shir songs. v3 haste will stack with other haste spells and can take you over the cap.

Spells and Bard songs that give regular haste may stack with other haste spells, but that's just so the side effects of the two can both take effect. Only the strongest haste component actually counts. A lot of people don't understand this; I see folks with Speed of Vallon up clicking their Eyepatches of Plunder like that's actually going to do something.
