ciccero
I have a troll shaman, and a troll shadowknight.

The simple, and single reason I chose a troll shadowknight was because he could have the same faction and zone access without issue as my shaman and I could take the two anywhere together without any faction issues.

I did a LOT of thinking about the different races, but in the end, the only real difference between the races is what they look like.

If you want raw starting int - you could also go with a gnome... but at the higher end game, mana is a function of INT and +mana items. Starting INT means little to nothing. Any class with equal INT will have the same mana per level, regardless of race and after a while, int items aren't that hard to find...

Ogres are nice for unstunnable and that was a tough call for me, especially considering that with karsan there as a 65 shaman for constant PL'ing heals, the troll regen was useless and an ogre would level slightly faster than a troll, plus have better starting stats (including INT).

I chose a troll strictly for aesthetics since I had a troll shaman. However, in my honest opinion for raw munchkin-power if you're wanting the best min/max you can get - I'd say go with an ogre if you have a constant shaman on hand to PL. Ogres are unstunnable from the front in melee, have great starting str/sta, so they max out faster, and have decent INT if you dump all your starting points into INT.

Total mana pool only matters if you find yourself running out all the time... but again, with the same INT - any race will have the same mana. It just might take Ogres another +20 INT in items to reach a dark-elf or gnome. However, that's more than offset by starting STA. Another option would be to get AGI just high enough to break 75 AGI and dump the rest into INT for the AGI vs. AC breakpoint.

Make hotkey: Heal SK

line 1 :: /pause 3, /target (insert your SK's name here)
line 2 :: /pause 50, /cast 1 (whichever gem you have for chloroblast)
line 3 :: /assist

The effect of this hotkey is: it switches your shaman's target to your SK, heals him, and then switches back to his target for root or cripple for aggro.

Put a DS on your shaman with a potion. Have your shaman pull the mobs with a low level cripple (not slow). Now root the mob right next to your shaman.

Switch to your SK. The mob will be beating on your shaman, the only effect is that the mob is dying from the DS. Start meleeing with your SK, whiff whiff whiff whiff hit whiff whiff whiff ... work on getting the spacing right, so that your SK is at max melee range, and keep practicing weapon skills, whiff whiff whiff whiff hit whiff whiff whiff.

If root breaks and mob flees, switch back to shaman, reroot, move shaman into range, move SK into range, continue.

Mob dies. Almost all damage has been done by the Damage Shield on shaman: no credit for anyone. Shaman has done no damage: no credit for him. SK has done a smidge of damage: You gained experience!

Your SK will take some hits when you get the spacing or aggro wrong, probably even die a few times while you are getting the hang of it. Pretty quick you'll be able to switch, hit the Heal SK hotkey, and switch back, pretty seamlessly. Then your SK is going to fly up many levels very quickly.

The keys are: keep the aggro on the shaman by rooting it very close, let the DS do the work, switch zones when the mobs turn blue.

No race has any more or less mana than any other race so long as the intelligence of the two is the same.

E.G. the mana pool for a Lvl 18 Erudite SK with 130 INT is EXACTLY the same as the mana pool for a lvl 18 Ogre SK with 130 INT.

Provided the INT (and +mana gear) is the same, they'll both have the same mana, same spells, and same abilities ... except the ogre can also see in the dark, slam/bash regardless of weapon/shield, AND is unstunnable from the front - all for a nominal exp difference.

Likewise for HP - provided their STA (and +HP gear) is the same, both the Erudite and the Ogre will have EXACTLY the same AC and Hit Points at the same level.

It doesn't matter if the stats are maxxed or not - they all max at the same numbers for everyone at the same time... as long as the stats are equal - the associated attributes are also equal REGARDLESS of race.

BUT -- it does feel nice being able to back yourself into a corner with 12 froggies whacking on you in Seb / Guk and channel that FD without worrying if one of those 12 frogs will stun and interrupt you.

The bottom line for all this ... I've NEVER personally heard anyone who made an Ogre SK ever complain that they made an ogre. I've heard lots of folks who've made other races say they wish they'd been ogres though.

For an Ogre SK though, they still start out with tons of STR/STA to put you that much closer to maxing it - and as a shaman, you can't ever buff INT. I'd dump the starting points into INT, then grab gear as needed and never look back - that will put your mana on par with the smaller races anyway, and you'll still be far ahead of them in melee stats.

I'd also recommend Rallos Zek as the SK diety for an Ogre, just because in my opinion the Rallosian Ogre cultural armor edges out the Cazic diety cultural.

I've got a 65 elemental SK ogre. Love him, great fun and very useful.

As to INT, everyone needs mana, and SK's can get low like anyone else. 20 INT does indeed matter, especially if you're an ogre, because you start with very low INT. And much of the equipment you pick up at low levels will have very little INT. When you're younger, too, you don't get full points of mana for each point of INT or WIS; that only stabilizes 10 for 1 at level 50. An item with 10 INT gives 100 mana at 50, but at 30 it only gives 3/5 = 60 mana. So the INT you DO get, you don't really get until you've been around the block a few times. So yes, you need INT.

I read and talked to a lot of people in the game about how to make my SK, and the feedback was pretty universal that ogre shamans should just dump all their beginning points right into INT. I forget if I could put more than 20, but basically if I had any extra, it must have bone into either of the two things ogres are really bad at(besides INT) -- AGI or DEX, just so he stayed above 75. But it was INT all the way.

Ogre's high STR/STA are a blessing when young. With buffs, even an extremely low level SK ogre can be max STR if not STA if he has good gear.

You might try to get a DEX buff off your shaman(or HOS) or someone else if you're not with your shaman every chance you get though. Until you get pretty good equipment, your DEX will be VERY low, like your AGI, and that will make gaining skill increases in offense and in all your weapons very slow indeed compared to those who have high DEX. It would be one thing if SK's dual-wielded, but they don't. A SK can't put a 1hb in one hand and a 1hs in the other and raise both at the same time. A monk even without high dex would have a much easier time raising offense and weapons skills; SK's in comparison swing only one weapon. Do yourself a favor and don't forget to get that buffed as often as you can -- nobody likes to "grind" skills on a weapon you might not even like when you could be using your favorite weapon instead.

As to deity, two considerations. As a 250 smith, I know the ogre cultural inside out, and the rallos zek ogre cultural is outdone by the human made version, fierce heraldic. Except, I think for the arms. Ogre made arms are very good.

I made my ogre fulvous darkscale, the cultural that cazic-worshipping SK's can wear. Bright yellow but you can dye it of course; doesn't look bad though, and the color blends in well with ornate, if that matters to anyone. Anyway, you can equip it at level one and wear it till you're 65, and beyond. It has more hitpoints than ornate, at least the hands, arms, boots, and wrists do(that's the ornate my SK now wears, for the focuses, but loses HP with each piece he puts on). Not so the BP of course.

Anyway, cazic deity will let you wear armor that is exceedingly good(more INT than rallos plate and very competitive hitpoints and other stats), and that any ogre smith can make for you. But there is also the value, if anyone still cares anymore, of having cazic faction so you can do the Greenmist quest easier. You need to be able to get into iksar city for that, and they all worship cazic. You will have to do less work to get the right faction levels to get a greenmist if you start as a cazic rather than Rallos deity.

Be aware though, that your cazic toon will be more hated than a rallos toon. It will be harder to find vendors to buy for, quests to do, and the quests you try will take much more faction work, etc. That really doesn't matter much in the game anymore, though, to most people. Overall, I'd recommend cazic over rallos.

If you powerlevel, I'd use a druid. They PL very fast up to about 40(can go beyond though, just harder), and are usually the gold standard of PL'ing up to that level. The nice thing about druid powerleveling is it keeps your defense maxed, which a low-agility ogre finds hard to learn anyway, and needs as much help as he can get on. If you PL the "drink a potion and let the high-ranking toon get beat on" strategy, you will indeed level very fast, but you will almost immediately fall far behind in your defense skill. Each point of defense, for a shaman, is worth 2.5 AC -- forget if it works the same for an SK or not. Five levels of defense per level, so you lose potentially 12.5 AC per level by not getting hit enough. This can quickly become catastrophic in its own way, as the mobs you eventually start facing are designed to be hitting someone with appropriate defense and the AC that comes with it. You can fall behind as much as 50 AC surprisingly quickly with powerleveling if you're not careful.

Some shamans who level by rooting and never get hit have the same problem. They crumple like wimps when they get hit. This breeds the famous skittish shaman, who try to wait till the mob is at 50% health before slowing, run like hell when the going gets rough, and generally find every excuse in the book to suck and disappoint their groupmates. Anyway, it's bad enough to be a shaman who can't take a hit -- a lot of people don't know better and actually kind of expect it! But a tank who crumples every time he's hit? Uh oh, incoming suckage reputation!
