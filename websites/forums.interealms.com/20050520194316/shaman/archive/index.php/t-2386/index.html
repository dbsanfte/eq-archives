AC, HP, and WIS are all nice, but they're not your most important stat.

Your most important stats are Regeneration and Mana-free damage. Think about it, the more mana you have, the more time it takes you to med it back... you are looking for a way to get your mana back faster- and that's what regen is all about.

Here are my recommendations:

1. Get an iksar regen BP. 5 hp/tick = 3000 hp/hour = 1500 mana/hour (if you're a cannibalizing fiend.)

2. Alternatively (if you can find a reasonably priced one) get a fungi staff. They definitely have limitations (they snare you, the effect needs to be reapplied every 5 minutes, and it doesn't stack with regen spells) - but it's mana-free Regrowth... 9000 hp/hour = 4500 mana/hour. Stacks with the iksar regen BP or fungi tunic, too.

3. If you spend a lot of time outdoors, get a horse. Get the cheapest horse, and turn the models OFF. Turning the models off allows you to cast SoW on yourself without it being overwritten by the horse speed effect (and you don't have to look at a horse-head all the time.) While on a horse, if you are standing still and not auto-attacking, you are medding. Even while standing. Even while casting. The ability to med while standing/casting is huge.

4. Your longer-term items are (of course) the JBB and fungi tunic.

The good news is that you have some nice items to sell. Ditch the froglok BP, Efreeti boots, and Earring of twisted leaves - you'll get good money for all of these. You could probably get nice money for your leggings and belt, too- replace them with something cheap (ry'gorr, totemic, or jarsath if you can get it for legs..... spider fur, crystalline, etc. for the belt.) Replace the boots with cheapo JB boots (few hundred plat) and the earrings with Imbued Golden Ivory Earrings (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=4930) (super cheap and super nice- 100 pp each or less). Use the resulting money for an iksar BP, horse, staff, whatever. Remember, Regen > Stats.

A couple other recommendations that won't break the bank:

Ditch the chrysoberyl talisman and get a Black Alloy Medallion (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=6020) - stats are pretty good, it's cheap (couple hundred PP, you might even break even on this one), and it will make every spell you cast under level 44 cost 10% less mana. That's a good thing!

Ditch the plat ruby veil and get an Arctic Wyvern Mask (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=5289) - you might even make a few plat on this deal. You get a nice balance of AC and WIS with the mask.

Replace your charred guardian shield with a Clay Guardian Shield (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1137) . You can probably break even on this one. You'll lose 9 WIS, but gain 15 raw AC and 50 HP - this is a good trade. A general ratio for deciding on equipment is 1 WIS = 1 AC = 10 HP- according to this the char guar is 19 points, the clay guar = 30 points. :)

Replace your platinum armband with JB arms. 18 AC > 3 WIS.

I've tried to make the equipment replacements basically pay for themselves by slot- or at least come close. I agree with Gloriana that the serp bracer and paw gloves need to go, but there aren't alternatives that won't set you back money (JB gloves for a few hundred and a totemic/ry'gorr bracer for a hundred are probably your cheapest decent alternatives.)

Hope this helps!

Taladaen
As you gain in levels, you will be getting mana back faster via canna. You only have canna 1 or possibly 2 (if your guild helps you out a smidge) and that's only so much mana per wince. Regen BP will help that out some as you'll be able to get hps for nothing. Don't cast more spells than you have to, as a pull comes in, tag it with slow and look for adds. You're not there to do damage, you're there to prevent it.

When everyone is talking about regenning mana hand over fist, they're talking about the higher levels, I still remember the 40s and how slow it was. Another thing to keep in mind is how often you're getting hit and how hard. Every time a mob beats on you, that's mana you've gotta regen the old-fashioned way. If mobs are cleaning your clock too easily, upgrade your HPs and AC. If they agro on you & don't do too much damage tell your tank to pull harder things :)

Have a monk or tank bind wounds on you while you cannabalize, this will let them experience the joy of mashing on buttons to get mana as much as you do. Have a cleric toss a celest heal on you if they have the mana & you're low or if the druid is running high on mana (yeah, yeah. I know. As soon as they have it they nuke with it but there's some good ones out there) they can share the mana by hitting you with a heal.

Get levels, it will get easier. Regrowth and canna 3 will greatly increase the rate you get mana back. Being able to take a few rounds of hits and not have a significant impact on your health will help you get mana back, too.

Hang in there!
