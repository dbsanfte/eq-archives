Hopefully this thread won't degenerate into an insult-fest, or cover old ground.

Anyway, it seems to me making changes for the sake of making changes isn't a viable strategy for a game designer. So, persumably there's a purpose behind the recent focus item change.

Removing all spells but dots from the scope of these focus items must lead to this question: Were shaman and classes who used these items unbalanced because they used them?

If the answer is "no", then what is the purpose of the change? Furthermore, balance would presumably require them, so well, you need them.

If the answer is "yes", then it gets to the title of the thread. Did these focus items permit us to do too much, making shaman overpowered with the benefit of the focus effects of these items? And not just shaman of course, but since this is a shaman board and that's what I play, that's what I call it.

I don't think shaman were overpowered with the benefit of these items, so I am puzzled at this decision. Lord knows Affliction Haste items are totally useless.

But others and Sony apparently disagree. If so, in what way do you think shaman were overpowered because of these focus effects - that is now corrected by their removal?

Xanden
I think the problem is that Sony introduced focus items as a small perk, claiming it was to compensate us for the lack of melee-type items such as haste, high-ratio weapons, etc. They never intended for them to actually be of great use to anyone, to the point where it would make a significant impact on your game.

With these, there was a significant impact. Not unbalancing, but the effects made our difficult jobs a lot easier. Casting times on our key debuffs were reduced to reasonable, useful lengths, allowing us to properly slow the hard-hitting mobs in PoP and still be able to toss in a heal afterwards. Durations were increased, so we don't have to spend extra mana to refresh a slow because the last one was just a little too short, and you had an add. Mana savings were also introduced, but I was never able to get hold of a decent efficiency item that worked for me, so I don't have any experience here.

No, they were not unbalancing. I never heard a single person complain that we got our slows off 30% quicker, or that it lasted longer.
