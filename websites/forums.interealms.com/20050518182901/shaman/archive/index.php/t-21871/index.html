(Since "W T F" is an integral part of this story, and this board filters out W T F [***], just know that I put spaces in it.)

Thought I'd share a humorous story (I thought it was kinda funny, anyway).

When they announced that they were putting in the ability to edit your own surname by just typing /surname [name], I thought to myself, "Cool, I can change my monk's surname 'cause I really don't like it."

So I gave it some good thought for a week trying to come up with a cool surname to put on my monk, who's name is Xosa. I finally came up with "Li." I don't really know why I liked it, it just looked and felt right. So I had it all settled in my head that when they put in the new patch, I'd change my monk's surname to "Li."

They put in the patch and I log in my monk to do the deed. I typed:

"/surname Li"

and it comes back and tells me "sorry your surname has been rejected. Please choose another."

W T F was the first thought that crossed my mind. I was heart-broken. I really wanted that name and I just couldn't for the life of me figure out why "Li" would be rejected... I mean it didn't make a phrase... it wasn't vulgar or demeaning... the first thought that crossed my mind was literally "W T F" because I was baffled.

So I'm sitting there disapointed, thinking W T F to myself, trying to come up with another name. Just to be stupid I typed:

"/surname W T F" just knowing that it would be rejected by the filter.

"Your surname has been accepted."

W T F!!!! So let me get this straight --- it won't let me put my surname as "Li", but it will let me put "W T F" as my surname?!?!?! As it turns out I just so happened to change my surname while in a hard LDoN with some guildees, and they saw my new surname immediately and died laughing at it.

The BAD part was: I didn't know that you had to wait 7 days to change your name again! So I had to walk around Xegony for a week with my surname as "W t f" until I could change it again. A lot of folks commented on it and even told me I should keep the surname, but in the end I knew someone would probably /petition me. 'Sides, I really didn't like the surname, although it was kinda humorous. 7 days later I changed it, but the guild still refers to my monk as "Mr. W T F" every time he logs on. :)
