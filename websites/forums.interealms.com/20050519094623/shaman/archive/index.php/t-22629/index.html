Tuesday July 20th Update

- Augments can now fit into multiple slot types. On an augment the text could read "This augment can fit in these slot types: 3, 4".

- Zoning pets will now properly save their taunt toggle settings.

- Spell damage messages done to yourself will no longer be displayed for others. Cannibalize and Mana Convert spells were examples of this.

- Summoning a Sand Giant in Oasis with Monster Summoning will no longer cause your pet the next time you zone to be HUGE! Monster Summoned pets will no longer save their size

- Pets will no longer poof when you get Call of Hero'd.

- Weapon augmentations will again proc properly

- The EverQuest process is now more friendly. There is also a slider bar to set your Max. FPS. in the Options window under display settings. This will free up more resources while running EQ. Also, when EQ loses focus it will use less resources.

- New commands for consent:

/consent group -- flags your current corpses so they can be dragged by all of your current group members

/consent raid -- flags your current corpses so they can be dragged by anyone in your raid (including people who join the raid later)

/consent guild -- flags your current corpses so they can be dragged by anyone in your guild

/deny group -- removes the flag

/deny raid -- removes the flag

/deny guild -- removes the flag



[Test fixes that are already live]

- The Ferocity line of spells will now increase your chance of double attack for classes without innate double attack.

- Soul abrasion damage is now being figured into critical hit damage.

- AA Focus effects should now work.

- Various fixes to Fading Memories

- Warrior shielding should now last the correct duration

- Spells that require spell components will not cast if you have more that 1 component in your inventory.

- Instant cast combat abilities can once again be used when you have a discipline up.

weissblau
This option is very useful because (1) it will help those who get motion sickness from very high frame rates and (2) it will limit CPU utilization, helping those who run other apps in the background while playing EQ.

Huh? Nobody gets motion sickness from high frame rates.

Typical causes of motion sickness in computer games are

1. Lag between your navigation action and the onscreen response. This is made worse by low frame rates, since the soonest an action can be reflected on screen is typically the 2nd frame after you make it (up to 134 msec at 15 fps but only 33 msec at 60 fps). Higher frame rates = faster response = less motion sickness.

2. Quickly changing, physically inaccurate movement. This is my main complaint with DMF/Lev and the bobbing effect. This has nothing to do with low/high frame rate.

3. Jerkiness in the onscreen movement caused by low frame rates. Higher frame rates = less jerkiness = less motion sickness.

4. Flickering/blurriness on screen due to low refresh rate. Refresh rate is independent of frame rate. It's a setting you can tweak in the Graphics options section. 60 hz is too low for me, 70hz and above is ok, 85 hz is great. Your monitor and video card determine what refresh rates are allowed.

If you got sick at higher frame rates then the developers were using the frame rate to peg the motion, i.e. when you signal a right turn it whips around too fast and stops too suddenly when you stop. This is unnatural physical motion, reason #2 up there.

However, tying the physical motion to the frame rate is amateurish at best. Doom MIGHT have done that, I'm sure Quake did not.

Actually, there is one other possible cause - low resolution. If you had to reduce the resolution in order to get the higher frame rate, then it's possible that you made the scene more blurry, which could cause motion sickness as well. EQ doesn't do that, so I hadn't considered it, but it could happen in other games if they render to a small bitmap and stretch that to fill the screen. That's also a Bad Idea and modern games don't do it, for the most part.
