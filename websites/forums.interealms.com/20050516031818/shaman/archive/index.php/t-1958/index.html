Bigguss
The JBB does 263 damage each 8 seconds, for a damage per second (dps) of 32.875. Realistically, it's closer to 25 dps by the time you figure in lag, root breaks, the occasional stun, etc. The JBB is rarely interrupted unless you end the cast at a different spot than you started it, or get the snot beat out of you while casting.

Incidentally, the epic DoT does less dps (around half of the JBB), but has the advantage of being able to be used on multiple mobs at a time, so if you're root-rotting three at a time, you actually will be doing more damage with the epic than with the JBB.

Shaman melee generally doesn't get into the 25 dps range until very high in the game (with uber weapons combined with haste spells and haste items.) Towards the end game, it is indeed possible to consistently melee for around this amount, if you have the right gear, AA, and buffs. It gets tougher on Luclin mobs, though, which have a notoriously high Mitigations (AC).

The JBB has the additional benefit, though, of freeing you from having to tank (and take damage.)

Also in the end-game it's possible to consistently duplicate JBB type damage through regular spells (see this thread (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?s=&threadid=885) for details.)

Bottom line: The JBB is worth it for solo'ing unless you can consistently put out 25 dps through melee and make up for the resulting downtime you'll need for heals.

Hope this helps.
