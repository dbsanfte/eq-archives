Mostly = .75
Partially = .5
Slightly = .25

Lucy takes the spdat info and presents it in a readable way, which means that a lot of the information is hidden. If you read the "Raw Data" for a spell, you'll see all the info in the spdat, including the cryptically named "base1" and "calc1". These are the key bits of info -- they say that the base effect of that spell slot (in this case, slot 1 is the slow) is such and such value, and the dependence on level is such and such other value. That song clearly shows that its effect will go up as the caster's level increases. However, because the effect will not go up by level 65, Lucy hides the level-dependent growth -- you can't reach a level where it'll matter, so why show it?

For bards, though, you *can* reach level 70, at least for the purposes of spell calculations. Jamfest raises your effective casting level, so that its effect calculates as if you were higher level. JF1 adds 1 to your effective level, JF2 adds (I think) 3, and JF3 adds 5. So, as a level 65 bard with JF3, my songs calculate as if I were level 70. However, Lucy doesn't show these calculations on the main page.

Look at Lucy's descriptions for Turgur's Insects: <a href="http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=1588&source=Live">Detailed Info</a> and <a href="http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spellraw.html?id=1588&source=Live">Raw Data</a>. You'll see that the effectiveness of your slow depends on your level; the same is true of Requiem of Time (<a href="http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3066&source=Live">Detailed Info</a> and <a href="http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spellraw.html?id=3066&source=Live">Raw Data</a>).

I have not personally tested Requiem of Time, so I cannot personally verify that the slow percentage improves with level. However, Reed's Spreadsheet is maintained by a fairly conscientious and respectable member of the bard community, and the entire community contributes to its accuracy, and this precise point was discussed and investigated by the bard community a fair while back and settled to our satisfaction at that time, so I trust it.
