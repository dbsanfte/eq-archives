LaughingWoman
I'm hardly a pro, and honestly expect to get more tips out of this thread than I'm going to give.

But in the interests of not being a schmuck, I'll stick in what I can think of, that might help those logging in for the first time.

* If you're frustrated with the mass crowds in the newbie area, explore a little to the sides. There are generally one or two easily found areas to get the quest critters, and then some more isolated areas with the same stuff where you can hunt in relative peace, with the other few people who looked for a path less traveled.

* If you're going to take up a tradeskill, do it early, and take the complementary tradeskill as your 2nd skill. Such as blacksmith/mining, leathercrafting/skinning. You can discard any tradeskill at any time (losing all skill point advances) so don't worry about taking the 'wrong' one. By taking it early, you'll avoid having to come back and slaughter/harvest in greenie areas to catch your skill up (higher level tradeskill harvesting can't be performed without meeting the minimum skill level).

* Remember that it's beta, and your characters can and will be wiped. So you might have more fun rolling up a less popular race (horde seems less popular than alliance) if you're getting frustrated with the crowds.

* Exploration is also fun, and gives pretty impressive exp at lower levels.

Looking forward to hearing some others.

You can share quests with group members.
