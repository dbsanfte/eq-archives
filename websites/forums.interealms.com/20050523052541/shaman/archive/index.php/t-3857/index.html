stygiana
A long time ago the world seemed big and wonderful, around every corner you took a new and dangerous beast was lurking. Dungeons where places filled with great danger and many wonders. I will never forget my first trip into Kurns Tower and how amazed I was, how big the place seemed and I remember that I could not wait to go back there. (even today I really like the way Kurns is set up) Some time later I entered Lake of Ill Omen for the first time and I just stood for like 10 minutes at the waterside watching the water, the sunset and the little river. Then I explored Warslik woods and battled the giants in it. I wandered into the Overthere and very very scared I took the wreck that transports you to “the old world” (calling it a ship is to much credit for it). And so I explored and grew in power, but no matter what you did there was always a new corner to take, a new danger to battle and new dungeons to explore. But alas all good things come to an end and soon the last corner was rounded and only the most remote and inaccessible dungeons where left to explore. Then I joined a fledgling raiding guild and we started to explore the places a pick-up group will never visit (at least not back in those days). Hate, Fear and Air where the places my raiding skills where trained and later the RoK dragons and even later the giants in Kael. But after a while all that was left was a mindless grind for more loot in a place that proved no real danger to the raid anymore.

And that brings us to today.

Now I am a member of a decent guild (some would rate us even as an uber guild but well that is a matter of definition), we are capable of killing almost any mob in the game and the few mobs we have not (yet) killed are just out of reach due to artificial restrictions (Hi boring key camps in Luclin) and recently it seems that every raid is just like a mindless repetition of old tricks. No matter what mob you take it al comes down to learning how to adapt to the trick they use and when that is understood the farming can begin. Please do not understand me wrong, the process of learning how to deal with a mob is one of the most fun parts Everquest has to offer (at least for me) and during those “learning” raids I enjoy myself (even we have to summon all corpses for the 3rd time that day or we have a strategy discussion for an hour)
So I just wondered why VI never made the boss mobs more variable. Why not have like 2 or 3 versions with different skills available for spawning so that you never know if you face a rooted, unslowable, pure melee mob or that you have to face a mobile, slowable but spell casting mob. In my view this would increase the challenge of the encounter.

So my questions to you are:

Do you have the same feelings about the current high end content that I have? (and then I am not talking about the level of the loot those mobs drop but just about the battle they offer)
Do you think that adding more variation in the mob can solve this?
Or do you think that by design Everquest is loot focused and this is a lost cause?
