****************************************************

Remember the rat race that was Clerical Epics? Remember camping {whatever} by yourself for a particularly nice item?

Remember that Epics were originally designed so that not everybody could have them immediately. MOBs didn't always drop a particular item (Vampire Essence in PoH for Enchanters), or were rare spawns (VS in Karnors).

So what happened say for the Rangers - if one Ranger was able to turn in something to VSR, then another Ranger could not.

If one Monk killed Gwan in PoSky, then another could not.

A zero sum, I win you lose philosophy and implementation.

WoW isn't like that. * IF * you are of the required level to receive a key quest (mostly at 12, 20 and 40 from what I've determined so far), then you get the quest.

Furthermore if 5 of the same class needing a particular guy group together and kill him? All get the item.

And if one or two die in the process before the named dies (meaning they can't loot the body)?

It's not a start over penalty. You simply wait for him to respawn (maybe 2 minutes max) and kill him again.

Trying to turn in an item that is wrong or to the wrong NPC? Doesn't happen.

There aren't multiple pitfalls designed to create frustration or confusion on the part of the playerbase. You CAN'T turn in an item to the wrong NPC, you CAN'T have an item eaten, you WON'T be sitting at one spot staring at a wall for hours on end.

But hey, I just wanted to provide that perspective. Again, play what you think is fun and what makes you happy.

The players had to do it themselves, and usually, people weren't always playing by the same rules (some were willing to train, some were not. some guilds were willing to leapfrog, some where not. some guilds were willing to blow spawns/kill under their level to hold back others, some were not).
