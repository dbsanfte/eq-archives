Lyzzt
One of the nice features of /pet hold is the fact that it acts as a backoff AND hold -- no more pounding /pet back off while a mob goes aoe on your pet.

One note on usage, once you issue a /pet attack your pet will be in kill-whatever-aggroed-you mode until you explicitly /pet hold again. I had hoped that you would not need to explicitly issue a /pet hold command after each battle to get him back to a tranquil state, but that's how the functionality was implemented. Because of this, I have a hotkey that is:
/pet hold
/pet follow or /pet sit

that I press after every battle so my pet doesn't go running off in search of other mobs to beat on that may be aggro on me.
