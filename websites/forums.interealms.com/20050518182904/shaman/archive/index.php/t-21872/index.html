Giving mages root and clerics invis is not a good idea. Then you gotta give everyone else something they don't have and weren't intended to have, and pretty soon,, you get a lot of classes locked out from groups cuz all the other casting classes can do the same things they can. You can respond with "But how would you feel if you were a cleric and this went wrong..." The answer is, I would feel fine, because I do NOT need to have a huge list of cross-classed abilities that give me the power to avoid ANY and ALL discomfort in a game that is based on risk vs. reward balance to be happy.

And as for 'how does giving clerics solo ability hurt anyone', I'll tell you. Clerics are the GODS of group desirability. Why should they be given solo power as well? I see my solo power as a small but existant offset to my now watered down grouping desirability (I also like being able to actually CAST DoT's once and awhile).

Also, even though small changes like adding invis to clerics would simply ease frustration, it is step one to a huge mess. There are certain areas that are just NO QUESTION BARRED OFF to certain classes. If you ignore this rule and add just a FEW spells in to classes that were designed with NOT having those spells in mind, you have laid the patchwork for "But I want this too, and my mage needs root, and my wizard needs buffs he can cast on others, and my necromancer could use a damage shield for pet, and, and... and its all okay as long as they are slightly weaker versions than the versions cast by their core classes...."

I just think that you should think about tweaking abilities classes already have to balance them against other classes that aren't as hard core in that ability area, rather than giving out whole new abilities.
