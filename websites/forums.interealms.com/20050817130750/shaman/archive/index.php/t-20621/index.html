Caanus Grey
I think I'll post my vote in favor of sever slow mitigation and immunity in GoD. It is important that as new expansions come out alternative classes become desireable, and a couple become nearly useless. Keeps us on our toes or something like that.

Next expansion I would really like to see 75% of the mobs proc an unresistable, uncurable version of Balance (a spell proc from Emperor guards that limits all healing to DD heals and caps those at 300 a shot). This would free groups from the tedious need to fill in that annoying "healer" slot in their groups and allow more use of bandages. For the first time ever clerics will be graciously allowed to skip out on that annoying nuisance of groups and raids to work hard on thier twinks, afterall that is what we want to play this game for, is being level 10 or less all over again.

In expansions after that I propose a 90% or greater existance of perma-rooted mobs with weaponshield discipline active from all sides at all times that proc DTs. This frees us from the melee DPS bother and allows the complete nullification of 3 classes in one expansion (go go efficiency!) Furthermore, this expansion will have to plan ahead for the fact that tank Hp will be getting to the point where they can actually TAKE a DT, and strikethrough weapons will be so common that even pets will have them, so make sure that all of the mobs have a strong knockback and root proc too.

And for the third expansion, maybe we can get away from healing and DPS completely and make mez and root return to their former glory. We can implement mobs which will only die if you can keep them completely stationary for more than 5 minutes. This should quite completely screw up anyone's understanding of what the hell they are doing in the game and finally realize the true intent of driving people back into older content.

In the fourth expansion, we can persue broader horizons and make it so that you actually only get experience when someone in your own group dies, and that experience is scaled inversely to the level of the mob. This will provide some much needed group desirability for those people who are especially adept at forming large trains of green con mobs while encouraging people to view their class skills from a unique angle as they attempt to get everyone around them dead while somehow living themselves.



I feel these changes are going to help the Everquest community at large, and should advance the concept of slow mitigation to its fullest potential by eventually balancing out all classes as "completely undesirable for grouping."
