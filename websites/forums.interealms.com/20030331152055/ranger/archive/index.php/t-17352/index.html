I'm not preaching, but I personally feel that any person interested enough to dedicate the time and effort to build a character in EQ, should have the patience and dedication necessary to do it right. To me, power leveling is sonething that should be reserved for alts (your characters other than your main or first character. Passing on information to encourage "newbies" to twink to extremes and power level is akin to buying a complex video games and immediately going out to buy cheat code books so they don't have to experience the whole thing. We are in this for the experiences, both good and bad, the fate of the game brings us. If you don't die, how will you learn from the mistake or misjudgement which caused your death, thereby making you more valuable to the game and to future group members. I know personally of 40-50s PCs that I would only group with if there were no other alternative. On the other hand, I know of lower level PC's that have been playing since the begining. What they can teach me is more valuable than blasting through a level just to go higher, with no idea of what a ranger, or other character, of that level is supposed to be able to do, to experience. When I was only level 7, I travelled the world, getting killed all over Norrath. By the time I was 11, I knew how to sneak around, evade, avoid and generally keepmyself alive through my own experiences. I was in every high level zone at least once by 9, and before 10 was travelling the Karana's by myself...verrryyy carefully, but without dying, for days on end. I didn't take on level 50 MOBs, but I taught myself how to keep away from them, just as a real ranger would have to do...just as the US Army Rangers, or any other Reccon unit, or insurgent unit would have to do in real life. An EQ Ranger should dedicate his early career to learning Evasion first. When you get sneak and hide skills, you'll just be that much better and MORE SURVIVABLE.
