Well, for whatever reason, I had a few people write emails to me to ask me to write something on the condition of the shaman class. Keep in mind that I’m speaking for myself at the moment. If you agree, feel free to post and say so. If you disagree, likewise feel free to post and say so. I will, however, make a caveat to the invitation to post: if you are not level 65, AND played in the elemental planes or through the mountain trials (vexd and tipt) in GoD or above, this post probably isn’t going to apply to you. For the purposes of this summary, I intend to focus on the high(er)-end shaman. So . . . here goes.

Where do shaman currently stand? For the most part, in a state of forlorn confusion.

The developers made a brief review of our healing hell, and responded by making a few cosmetic changes. Since the changes were made, SoE has, in usual fashion, gone radio silent and refused to answer any questions as to whether they are looking at shaman in any further capacity, or whether we are now ‘balanced.’ Hence the state of confusion. The forlorn part will probably become more evident as I go on. I’m going to break the following analysis down into several areas: healing, debuffing, buffing, mana regeneration, and DPS.

HEALING:

After weeks of shaman screaming, the devs finally analyzed healing for the shaman and seemed to come to the conclusion that we were lacking. Some changes were enacted. Our useless DoT in GoD was changed to a direct heal for 1550. Our heal over time spells were upgraded.

Rytan, the SoE guy in charge of spells, posted a couple of times on the shaman forum. Apparently SoE had a vision in regards to healing percentages. Briefly, he said:

With the new Daluda:

for the same comparative HP/second as the PoP heals:
Supernal Light: 100%
Nature’s Infusion: 64%
Daluda's Mending: 47%

[As an aside, the above is a little misleading. I think Rytan misspoke in his post. Daluda’s is 47% of the GoD cleric heal Holy Light, not 47% of Supernal light, so shaman are stuck back where they were in the beginning of PoP (pre-mitigation).]

The result being, shaman and druids are pegged to a healing percentage of a cleric. SoE declined to respond or comment to any questions regarding the base percentages. Their ‘fix’ to healing was to increase shaman direct healing capacity to pre-GoD levels. Nonetheless, I would think it should be pretty clear that underlying changes to the game make an occasional re-evaluation of the percentages necessary.

So, due to underlying changes to the game, the shaman are trying to get the developers to question the percentage heals. Apparently we were pegged at 47% heals at least as far back as the inception of PoP. That was before (a) mitigation, and (b) the change to mob hp vs dps.

The shaman have been arguing, so far unsuccessfully, that due to the widespread prevalence of slow mitigation the dev's need to examine the base percentage of heals for high level heals. More specifically, since a lot of mobs now mitigate our slow to probably 37.5% (formerly 75%!), that shaman heals need to be bumped up to compensate. Our initial estimate of a raw number was around 1800 hp base heal. We got 1550. On it’s face, the additional 250hp seems trivial, but when combined with focus effects and AAs, and chain healing, that 250hp starts to make a big difference after 2-3 casts.

So anyway, mobs mitigate. Their dps does not go down as far, but our healing ability does not compensate. No other healing class is balanced against slow (I discount the minor heals that BLs get), so it only really affects shaman. Thus, while mitigation hits all slowing classes, it really double whacks the shaman since our heals remain unchanged. That problem goes back to PoP.

Now enter GoD, where mobs hit much harder and mitigate, but have less hitpoints. Fights are over faster, so over the course of a fight, mitigated slows, or even bounced slows are of substantially less importance (except to shaman). The difference of 30% slows over the course of a 30 second fight is relatively trivial, especially since most cleric and druid heals contain an 'overheal' component--heals above and beyond the hp the tank has. GoD reduces that overheal component a lot, so much so that many cleric/druid heals hit for full efficiency. To some extent, that makes druids and clerics MORE efficient, though at the cost of some anxiety since you have larger spikes in hp! Pity the hapless tanks!

Shaman heals, on the other hand, are effectively further hindered. We were already healing for full efficiency due to a lower raw hp healing amount per cast. Now we have a situation where we are penalized for slow by having a lower percentage heal, then we're given a less efficient heal than the other classes in terms of mana cost, then we're trying to heal more damage with a heal that, because it almost always hits for full, can't get any more efficient, over the course of fights that further trivialize the impact of slow by being of such short duration. Slow saves us mana on healing over the course of a longer fight. Short duration fights, especially since we have to slow at the beginning just don't prevent as much damage.

Shaman were designed as an endurance class, and that concept is no longer relevant in GoD. Slow makes healing more efficient over the course of long fights. Thus, by slow, shaman add endurance to the healers' mana. Over the short term, we don't really save them ANY mana--they still have to heal once or twice. Over the long term, we can save them a TON of heals.

Our heal over time spells are nice. I’m glad they got improved. They allow shaman to help the main healers save mana during downtime. However, HEAL OVER TIME SPELLS DO NOT ALLOW FOR BURST DPS MANAGEMENT. I can’t stress that enough. Heck, let me repeat it for emphasis.

It’s something that is apparently ignored by SoE devs. You guys could give us a spell that does 500/tic for 10 minutes, and it won’t help with burst dps in a level of content like later GoD trials. It’s NOT average healing power that rules in an engagement. If we can’t heal a spike of mob dps, the tank is dead and so is the party. If (when) my cleric gets heal agro, I don’t have 3 seconds to cast Breath, 2.2 recast time, 3.5 seconds to cast Daluda (if I had it!). I have to chain cast tnargs/daluda and pray I can keep him alive long enough to get heal agro or long enough for the tank to pull agro.

Druids are in better shape, but I think they need to be looked at, too. DEBUFFING:

This area has a lot of different opinions. Shaman seems to have a lot of different view points about it. Originally, I was pretty indignant about the new slow on test before GoD went live. 14 levels after we got Turgurs, we got a new spell that lasted 1.5 minutes and slowed for 15% LESS (raw) than our level 51 spell. In short, Balance of the Nihil has a 1.5 second cast time (versus 3 sec on turgurs), -40 MR check (versus none on turgurs), lasts 1.5 minutes (versus 6.5 minutes on turgurs), and slows for 60% (versus 75% on turgurs). The spells are similar in all other aspects. Please note, I don’t have this spell so I’m shooting from the hip here.

What I’m guessing is that Nihil was an attempt to balance the massive DPS of mobs, the need for shaman to resist debuff on inc, and the short life span of mobs. Again, I haven’t run parses (since I don’t have the spell), but I suspect that the 15% less slow on Nihil is offset by the fact that it gets slowed faster and the mobs die faster. The net result is probably a fairly close, given a comparison of relative time needed to land the slow averaged over a 30 second engagment window. Thus, Malos/turgurs on a GoD encounter is probably equivalent to using Nihil on most yard-trash type encounters. Perhaps someone can parse and confirm. Still, if that was the intent of Nihil, I still think that the resist check on nihil should be equal to malos.

[BTW, let me digress a moment. I’ve mentioned that encounters are faster, and that mobs seem to have less hp, etc. I don’t know about actual hp on mobs in GoD, but player DPS has gone up, and if mob hp has gone up, it has not gone up by the same percentage as player dps. The net result is, outside of actual raw mob hp, that mobs die faster than in encounters in previous expansions. So, if I say average mob hp is down, it’s a relative thing. I happen to think that raw mob hp was reduced from PoP levels as well, though I may be mistaken.]

Some shaman have offered opinions to the effect that they think we should get AAs or slow abilities that enhance our slows. Some suggestions have included a passive AA that vastly increases a chance to slow, or increases a slow by a random percentage, or perhaps just reduces mitigation by one level. In terms of a class-defining ability (which we currently lack—will be discussed more below), I think that an AA that reduces mitigation by a single level would be very nice without being totally overpowering. Thus, if a mob were normally ‘slightly’ slowed, with the AA it would be ‘partially’ slowed. I think that might make a shaman a more viable alternative to some of the other slowing classes. As it stands there are a lot more reasons to invite an enchanter or BL over a shaman if a group needs a slower. They bring a LOT more to the table than a shaman does.

Some other shaman have advanced ideas regarding a melee debuff like Cripple (level 53 spell) that reduces damage in some fashion—ie, lowers max hit, further reduces rampage, etc. While I think it’s a good idea in principle, I think it serves to further complicate a tough balancing situation. Honestly, if shaman could reduce mob dps by 5% with a new spell line, then obviously the developers for end-game content would need to just up mob dps by 5%. Essentially, it’s like a whole new slow line, with all the balance/mitigation problems therein. It would also make shaman a required class for certain high damage raids—which is not necessarily a good thing. Making classes REQUIRED for encounters is bad.

I honestly think that most of the shaman view debuffing as a problem mostly because it was widely regarded as our class defining ability, around which we were balanced, and it was effectively nerfed with the implementation of mitigation. When mitigation went live, the fact that shaman were completely ignored was disheartening, to say the least.

BUFFING:

Shaman buffs have become a joke. Elemental level and above players often have ability stats maxxed. Shaman have exactly 2 buffs that are of any use at the moment: focus and ferine avatar. I’m going to guess that fully a third of shaman spells are stat buffs. One third of our spell line is now useless.

Beastlords, a class who also slows, does more dps than shaman, and has mana regen buffs and AAs, also has attack buffs in excess of our avatar—so avatar isn’t all that useful either.

Our one spell that’s still useful is Focus of Souls, and it’s useful not for its stat buffs, but solely for its hit point component.

I think I’m not amiss in saying that shaman used to be the kings of melee buffs (not hp—that’s the cleric province—but passive buffs to increase the dps of our melee). We had the stat buffs and attack buffs (something only bards and shaman had at one point). That has apparently fallen completely by the wayside. We basically don’t add ANY dps to the group passively. Get an enchanter and/or a BL and a group will be in much better shape because they can add a great deal more raw dps (melee for BLs or a pet for the enchanter), and also bring a great deal more to the table in terms of mana regeneration, or other class-specific lines (ferocity, haste, crowd control, etc.)

Some suggestions were advanced that I think make tremendous sense. How about changing some of our useless stat buffs into something a little different? For instance, one of the best suggestions I saw was to change the stat buffs into buffs that enhance effects like those on time level and above gear. Give us a spell line that provides small increases to Strikethrough, combat effects, shielding and the like. +5 or +10 to each of those wouldn’t be overpowering by any stretch of the imagination, but WOULD constitute a buff that melee would seek out.

[Side note on buffs—why is it that, after years of shaman grumbling, the dev’s finally fixed our (now useless) stat buffs so that group spells did not buff for LESS than the single version spells, and yet they felt the need to reintroduce the same irritating-as-hell concept with our group haste? SLTW (single) 60% haste. Talisman of Alacrity (group) 50% haste. Hello?]

One other thing I’d like to note in passing. Raising the stat cap is not the answer at this point. Players are buff limited, and there just is not room for stat buffs anymore even if they did anything. The return on stat buffs is marginal compared to the other buffs available. A melee class is better off with another raw attack buff or hp buff, or resist buff than a stat buff. It’s time for SoE to accept that stat spells are useless in end game, and move to a <gasp> new concept.

MANA REGENERATION:

This is actually a 2 part issue: mana regeneration and spell costs. Shaman are viewed as a mana powerhouse, and, apparently as a consequence, all of our spells are extremely mana inefficient. This exacerbates issues like healing where we are already very weak.

Shaman are good at mana regen. I challenge anyone to say differently (and mean it!). Unfortunately, what everyone seems to ignore is that our mana regen comes at the cost of everything else. We cannot cast, move, etc. while getting mana back. What does that mean, exactly? It means we’re in trouble in GoD.

Recall that fights are extremely short in GoD. That means a very short engagement horizon. Shaman DoTs are predicated on relatively low DPS mobs and long duration models (further discussed in the DPS section). On mobs that die prior to the expiration of a single DoT, DoTs become a horribly mana inefficient means of damage. So, heretofore, we could DoT and then cannibalize to recover mana. With the shorter fights in GoD, now shaman have to move to our horribly inefficient nuke/DD type spells to land something before the mob dies. While we are nuking, we do not have our DoT down-time to cannibalize . . . hence our mana regen goes to crap. The short term nature of the fights have effectively neutralized the DoT as a viable method of dps. Nukes do not have a passive component to allow us to use active mana regeneration.

As with the other section analyses, the fundamental game changes in GoD, namely changing the game to a faster-paced, higher dps scenario, has crippled the shaman in particular. Since our spells cost more mana to cost for the result on average, the reduction of time to cannibalize is doubly penalizing.

The dev’s got the right idea on our new cannibalize spell, Chaotic Pain. Unfortunately, by making it an ancient spell that no one has even seen, it does NOTHING to help balance the class.

DPS:

Wow. Where to start. I would argue that effective shaman dps is the lowest of any class out there, with clerics a close second. Clerics may shoot me for saying this, but as the primary healing class in Norrath by a HUGE margin (see healing discussion), they shouldn’t be high dps classes anyway. Their niche is healing. Shaman don’t seem to have a niche.

The reason I say that shaman have the lowest EFFECTIVE dps is that we actually look pretty good on paper. Shaman have a very high potential DPS. Someone can pull out a spreadsheet and plug in DoTs and nukes and get some insane dps numbers. For instance, just assume you can stack Bane, Blood, Ultor, and then alternate Tears and Velium Strike. I dunno what that would work out to in terms of average dps, but there would be tics of probably in excess of 400dps. Looks pretty sweet, right?

Wrong.

It’s just not possible to sustain that level of damage for a shaman. Forget mana concerns for a minute, and ignore the fact that our spells are designed to cost more to compensate for our active mana regen. So far, I have found exactly ONE tank that, in very limited circumstances, can hold agro if I cast all that. Want to know the circumstances? Fighting an unslowable named that quads, and then only AFTER the tank in question has used the furious (reposte) discipline. The tank also has heavily augmented agro weapons with anger procs, and horrifying visage by an enchanter. So, they can only do it on named mobs every 45 minutes. Not so useful.

Why is it that a wizard can blow off 30% of a mobs hitpoints in a single crit of 6000+ damage, and yet I can pull agro off a taunting warrior with a spell that does 320 hp damage a TIC. Agro on DoTs is REALLY messed up. Hey, Mr Dragon, look! Who should you attack? The wizzie that just blew two of your limbs off, or the shaman that just gave you a very mild case of gas?

Frankly, as bad as DoT agro is, the whole point is still kind of moot. The more fundamental changes to the pace of the game make DoTs useless and obsolete anyway. In a competent group (one that has a legitimate reason to be in GoD), mobs die in 35 seconds anyway. Less if there is an enchanter pet in the group. Ignoring slow and recast times, our DoTs don’t have time to run on a mob if we cast on inc. They stink for mana efficiency in GoD. DoTs are obsolete. Without re-tuning them to run in 30 seconds, or 24 seconds, they are a waste of a spell slot. Another factor completely ignored by the developers is a resist check. Why is it that other classes get -100 and -200 resist checks on their DoTs, but, even if we malos/malosini we can't even get close to that. None of our DoTs have resist modifiers, including Bane with a 30 second refresh if it bounces.

Naturally some will immediately fall back to the argument that shaman can solo. My response? So what? Why don’t you go solo in GoD? Our DoTs are designed to be slow. We can kill larger mobs, and tough ones, but we do it VERY slowly. It’s not a viable substitute for grouping. A decent (not even good) group will probably get at least 60% more exp than a soloing shaman. This is doubly true after solo experience was nerfed. Soloing is something people can do, but what’s the point? I challenge anyone to tell me that the game is designed around soloing.

So, accept for a moment that DoTs aren’t worth the mana to cast them. We can’t close with a mob since they’ll beat the tar out of us (shaman melee discussed below), so what’s left? Pets and nukes.

A lot of time has been devoted to our pets. They stink. They stink like week old goat cheese left out in the hot sun. The green-con 65 SK (same as the level 49 necro pet) pet will own our blue-con shaman pet before buffs. If both pets are buffed (self buffs by both classes), the shaman pet sometimes gets the SK pet with enrage. The shaman pet is essentially a speed bump. Unbuffed it does 12 dps (blue con mob. Your numbers will vary with types of mobs and AC of mobs). If given 2 weapons and buffed (including Ferine Avatar every 6 minutes), it can do up to 37 dps.

Why is it that the shaman pet, of all the pets out there, requires shaman to give our pets weapons to make it dual-wield? Weapons are not stackable. Nor are they light. Our pets have all the stamina (BUFFED) to take about 2 seconds of beating from GoD or elemental or hard LDoN mobs. They are going to die in a couple hour expedition. Probably several times. In an difficult group encounter, it’s not uncommon to go through 2 full backpacks of trash weapons. Carrying resist gear, and other necessary stuff doesn’t leave much room. There is NO reason for not making the shaman pet dual wield. It will still do crap for dps, but at least it’s not an irritant.

Shaman have been annoyed about that for years. The pet is nothing new. We have had to carry weapons for our stupid pet for 26 levels. Why?!? We’re not even a pet class. Our pet is at best a low level DoT. It isn’t even a significant factor in our dps. Even SK pets get innate dual wield. SKs are even less of a pet class than shaman!

Okay, so DoTs aren’t viable anymore, our pets really suck, so what’s left for our dps? Nukes, or direct damage spells. Shaman have 3 spells for DD at higher levels. Tears (a poison rain), Velium Strike (VS), and Spear of Torment (spear).

Tears: 3 waves of 650
VS: 925 base damage
Spear: 675 base damage

Tears is a rain spell. That means if you have mezzed mobs, or rooted mobs, it can’t be used. Rains are also coded differently. Spear is a poison based nuke, like tears. Neither has a resist modifier. Comparisons between waves of Tears and a like number of casts of Spear show a marked increase in resists on Tears. Current estimates are that rains are coded to have a 33% resist rate (as in 1 of your waves won’t hit the mob). While we’re at it, why do rains spells still hit the caster? A rain will ONLY hit four targets. Thus, 4 targets once, 3 targets once and 1 target a second time, 2 targets twice, or 1 target 3 times (all ignoring resists, of course). HOWEVER, even if I hit four mobs once, if I’m too close, I will still get hit by THREE waves that apparently don’t touch the mobs. Nonetheless, tears is our most efficient nuke!!!

VS takes (with refresh) just over TEN seconds to cast. Chain nuking with our largest nuke, a shaman cannot even get up to 100dps. (925/10=92.5 dps). Spear, through faster, only hits for 675 and is even less mana efficient than VS. None of tears, VS, or spear is what you would call mana efficient.

Bottom line, shaman dps is poor. Very poor. Our DoTs are useless now. Our melee and pets are a joke at high levels. That leaves only DD spells, and there isn’t a caster or priest class out there that can’t out nuke us.

Melee has gotten silly. It’s not worth it to get within melee range of a mob post Tier 2 in PoP, to say nothing of hard LDoNs or GoD. Shaman, despite our original design as battle priests don’t melee. Clerics, Norrath’s primary healing class, gets the bash skill up to 200, yet the ‘battle priests’ aka shaman max out at bash of (1) IF they are a large race. Iksar, frogluks and vah shir don’t even get bash of (1). Don’t misunderstand. Bash would be very very nice for interrupts, since shaman have no active skill for interrupting casters (if we’re really lucky dog dog can do it for us), but it’s not going to balance our dps. Shaman cannot afford to close in with a mob unless a real tank has got its attention. Improving our melee is not the answer to dps.

Summary:

Some of the boards wonder why the shaman have been screaming bloody murder recently. Hopefully the above will explain why. Right now, our class might as well not exist. The sum total of skills that we bring to the table include (a) malos (so someone else can do more dps to the mob since we can’t do any), and (b) focus (for 544 hp). Note that mages can cast malosinia as well, so malos isn't all that important.

Need a healer? For god’s sake don’t get a shaman if it’s something even moderately difficult—you need a cleric or a druid.

Need DPS? Get a monk, wizard, rogue, ranger, shadowknight, or heck, ANY class over a shaman. We can’t nuke, and we’ll suck the healers dry on mana if we DoT. Even if the tank can hold agro, we’ll be OOM in next to no time trying to DoT mobs that last 30 seconds.

Need a slower? Take an enchanter—they can mana regen, slow, charm pets, haste, and crowd control. Or take a beastlord—they have ferocity, a heck of a lot more dps than shaman, have mana regen spells, and can also buff and slow. With mitigation on almost every difficult mob, the differences between our respective slows is probably under 5%.

Every problem I've noted is just as prevelant in raid situations, if not more so. We can't even heal a group from an AE in GoD. Raid mobs are almost all flagged immune to slow. Our DoTs are so high agro that we can't afford to cast more than 1 because the consequences of turning a mob are disasterous. Most raids, my main function is to focus rez'd folks.

Right now, shaman have no purpose as a class. We bring nothing to the table that another class can’t do better. Usually much better when other skills are considered. At the last fan fair (a couple of days ago), Rytan commented that shaman slow for 5% more and that was a huge difference in dps. I’m guessing he must have been drunk. 5% isn’t a huge difference, especially mitigated. Don’t believe me? Ask a cleric if they notice a difference with a 5% slow when the mobs die in 30 seconds. Now imagine the mob mitigates (hint: it makes even LESS of a difference). If 5% is such a huge difference, why aren’t the healing percentages 100, 95, and 90% for clerics, druids and shaman respectively? Since enchanters can haste for 70%, why can’t shaman haste for 65%? Perhaps 5% isn’t such a huge difference after all when it comes to class balance. Unless, of course, it requires a developer to actually re-examine something. Then it’s huge.

I do not exaggerate when I say that fully 30% of the end game shaman I know have already quit. Don’t believe me? Go to one of the shaman boards and start reading good-bye posts. One third of my guild’s shaman have already left. I know 4 others from other high-end guilds on my server that are considering it. Most have left EQ entirely, but a couple have switched to different classes. If SoE’s goal is to phase out the shaman class, they are doing a good job.

In closing, I’d like to propose a challenge to the developers. Take a party through Trials 7-9. One member of your team must be a shaman. I recommend doing it all the way through twice. First, try it with no warrior (hint: you really hosed the knight classes in this expansion, too). Second, try it with only one other healing class besides the shaman.

Heck, while I’m at it, I’ll deliver a second challenge to the developers. Try responding to one of our balance threads and open up a discussion. [Hint: posting “Hi!” does not constitute a response or a discussion.]

So . . . though delivered in jest, Brenlo’s question is right to the point. What IS a shaman? We’d certainly like to know, too. Right now, a shaman is a waste of pixels in end-game EQ.

The % difference between Shaman slows to others is directly proportional to the amount of time the slow is running against the entire duration of the fight. There is a 20% difference in damage between Shaman and Enchanters slows but only once slows have landed. You have to include the damage mobs do whilst unslowed into the equation then take total damage difference for the full duration of the fight to calculate the effective difference.

Hypothetical situation :

Say your GoD mob with low HP and high DPS mob gets slowed at 50% health for a fight lasting 30 seconds. This means they do full damage for 50% of the fight and are 75% slowed for 50% with Shaman slow or 70% slowed with Enchanter slow.

In a case of a mob that does 1000 DPS for a fight that lasts 30 seconds then you have 1000 DPS for 15 seconds (15000 damage) and 250DPS over 15 seconds (3750 damage) the rest of the time for Shaman.

In a case of a mob that does 1000DPS for a fight that lasts 30 seconds then you have 1000 DPS for 15 seconds (15000 damage) and 300DPS over 15 seconds (4500 damage) the rest of the time for Enchanter.

The net result % difference in damage taken is 3.8%. The 20% argument requires a mob to be slowed 100% of the fight.

End of Hypothetical situation:


Including MR debuff an Enchanter can land slow faster than a Shaman so the % difference gets closer and closer to 0% damage dealt between Shaman slowing and Enchanter slowing.
