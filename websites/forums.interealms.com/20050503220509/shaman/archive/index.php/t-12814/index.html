deria
BoT is a wonderfully interesting zone for a shaman. I really like it, actually. It keeps me thinking instead of mindlessly grinding away.

In general, before I got Malos I tended to get higher than normal resists on slow. Sometimes I would have to cast it 2 or 3 times to land it. After Malos, the situation improved dramtically. I still get resists, of course, but much less often. As one of the posters above mentioned, there is also the occassional super-mob that seems to be extraordinarily resisty.

Regarding damage, each quarter has a different resist profile.

If you happen to be fighting in east, you might as well just melee and epic-dot. You can pretty much forget poison, disease, and cold damage.

If you happen to be fighting in south, Velium Strike is your friend.

If you happen o be fighting in west, Velium Strike is your friend here too.

If you happened to be figthing in north... strangely enough, Velium Strike is your friend here too.

In general, Velium Strike is your friend. :) Spear Of Torment can also be your friend... just not the kind of friend you spend alot of time with.

I find BoT is also one of the few zones where you can really use your epic alot. That's kind of nice.

deria
Regarding mitigation:

Some of the regular mobs mitigate, but very few. In general, I would say ~99 percent of the regular giants are completely slowable (no mitigation message whatsoever).

The east and west minis are slowable, with mitigation, using disease. While it may be possible to land magic slow on these, I have never done so. The south mini is technically slowable, but in practice cannot easily be slowed if the only debuffer is a shaman. A shaman/enchanter could probably get his MR low enough, but a shaman/chanter/bard combination would be best. In all instances that I've fought the south mini, we fought him completely unslowed (and just healed more). The north mini is immune to changes in run speed -- he cannot be slowed (at all, using any slow).

I've not noticed any mitigation on the bees and/or stormwatchers, but I've only fought them a very few times and may have simply not run into one that mitigates.

Regarding enchanter slow landing easier than shaman slow, I think it is simply a case of the following sequence of events:

1) malo (unresistable, mr - 45)
2) malosinia (bounce)
3) slow (bounce)
4) slow (bounce)
5) slow (bounce)
6) enchanter tashes (unresistable, mr - 50)
7) slow (lands)

The EQ random number generator is flakey, but enchanters tend to also be a cowardly bunch (just kidding) and cast tash only when they have to. I suspect that if you had cast slow after his landed, yours would have landed too.
