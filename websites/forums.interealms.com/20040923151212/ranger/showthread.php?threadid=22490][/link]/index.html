Here is a hastily thrown together walkthrough of how to set up the n50 as a Ranged auto attack.

as mentioned above it uses the "Throttle" on the pad since when you put the throttle into a position that is an "active band" then it is "depressed", when you change the position of the throttle to an "inactive band" then it is no longer "depressed".

So as long as the throttle is in the fire position it will repeat your firing macro until you roll it to the off position.

Here is the link: http://64.243.10.204/nostromo/

Belkin Nostromo game controller, hand-shaped pad with 10 programmable keys, throttle roller and a tilt-pad directional controller under the thumb. USB and a loadout manager/editor that makes setup quite easy.

I took the throttle roller, set it to use "bands" ( control in "XX" range and it executes this command) and set the forward extreme to hit my ranged attack key, and repeat every 1.6 seconds ( about optimal firing rate with my prismatic bow.)
I pick my target, select the hotkey bank for it, and roll the throttle control forward; auto archery until I roll it off.

I happen to customarily mem Eagle Eye in the same slot as the hotkey number for range attack. If Eagle eye flashes, I hold the alt key down for a sec until the next repeat of the number key, and get a cast of eagle eye to refresh the buff, then continue firing.

One caveat; make sure the things you have keyed on different banks in that same slot are innocuous, as you can spam them pretty well by switching banks and forgetting to roll off the throttle.
