I've been in Sol B for about 10 days now, and this is awesome experience for the mid 40s. Went there at 45, and hit 48 in 8 days. That dungeon exp bonus definately helps. While tanks may be LFG for 30 minutes or more, as a healer you will almost always find a group as soon as you walk into the zone.

At 48 the Kobolds in KPC are a mix of lt blue and dk blue, but i'm still getting a blue of exp every 40 minutes or so. Bat groups are even better, and although I've only been in one SS / LDC group ( MUST have a cleric along here, shaman heals aren't enough to keep the group alive) the exp there was even better. You'll be healing and buffing mostly, slow's aren't much help at least in the parts i've fought - the aggro they draw isn't worth the damage they offset, since most groups will outtank the beasts easily. Keep a dot or dd mem'd, along with root if there is no chanter to mez. But if you're healing / buffing 5 melee (and this will happen, monks, rangers, pallys and SK's seem to love this zone) you won't have much mana left for offense. My typical spell set up is root/DD/Buff/Alacrity/Cani/Chloro/G Heal/Gate.

I've won 2 bracers on rolls (+5 Wis), and several nice non shammy items to sell or trade. So, the loot isn't terrible.

Sol B, in short, is an excellent exp zone for those who like to group and aren't afraid of dungeons. Sow before entering as it's an indoor zone, and drop gate into your sow slot for a fast escape - yes, the trains can be deadly. And if you're going to die, do it at the camp and not running into some out of the way - wrong turn - cubbyhole where no one will find your corpse for a week. Plenty of monks in zone most times as there is a nice monk quest item that drops here, so if do die you can find one to pull your corpse to the zone or to a cleric with little trouble. Enjoy the fast pulls and non stop killing, and watch your exp. bar move.

Happy Hunting,
