Bronzestar
After a year and a half "clean", I have fallen off the wagon and started EQing again. Rather than hunting down my old chars and servers, I decided to start a new character (Ciarraide) on a new server (Ayonae Ro), and see how it all worked out. Here are my observations/strategy so far....

Level 1: I arrive in Halas, admire then new UI, note that I have some free spells, free backpack, etc. After dumping 5 points into 1hb, I immediately speak to Jinkus Felligan, and start off on my quest for the Initiate Symbol of Justice. For the unaware, this is a No-Drop AC3, WIS+1, MANA+3 item that allows you to summon a magical hammer. (1HB, 6/32, BANE vs undead +3). Makes early life MUCH easier, especially against skellies. Since the quest hauls you through Qeynos, I also stop in N Qeynos and kill rats, collecting Plague Rat tails until my inventory is full. Return to Halas nets me the Symbol, 12pp and change(from selling tails), and level 2.

Level 2-5: I buy all my spells, and exiting notice a new (to me) Shammie Master. Lo, and behold - a Shammie Newbie Armor Quest!!! I used to bitterly complain of the lack of this quest, and now it has been created. I collected the kit, the molds, etc, and head out onto the tundra, pausing only to buy a sewing kit and 2 each of the patterns to make a full set of Tattered Armor.

I note that Polar Bear skins are required en masse to create this armor, and assume that Polar Bear Cubs still drop them about 1 in 5 or 6 kills. I am proved to be sadly mistaken. The drop rate on these skins has been severely cut - I started keeping track after my first drop, and managed to pick up a Polar Bear Skin one in ever 17 or so cub kills. Sigh. While amusing myself in the hunt for bears, I do manage to create a full set of Tattered, and give away two others to Trivial my sewing skill on Tattered.

I park myself at the tree at the three-way intersection below the PoK book, and begin pulling every Cub, Skellie and Spiderling that walks by.
When the dust settles, and after 6 fatalities (Wolf Packs and Vengefuls) I am level 5, with a full set of Shaman armor, 4 HQ pelts, 2 stacks of silks and 4 stacks of Bone Chips. Time to head to the PoK!

Level 5-8: I head up to the PoK, aiming to sell off my loot quickly and make enough money to buy all my level 5 spells and a couple of other armor pieces to fill in the gaps left by my newbie armor. (Which is awesome, btw - better than the armor I had on my old Shammie char at level 25....). I send out an /auc in the PoK advising the availability of my items. 30 seconds later I am blinking, 50pp richer, with my inventory empty. I decide to investigate. I head to the bazaar, and find that Bone Chips are now selling for 20-25pp a stack, HQ Skins anywhere from 20-50pp each, and Silks are going as high as 10pp EACH. Inflation at work. This begins the process of collecting these items from a few newbie areas each time I am on, then selling them in the bazaar at 50% of the lowest going rate. I am never in the bazaar longer than 10 minutes as a trader, and by level 15 I have 2K pp banked.

Back to levelling. I now head to the Everfrost entrance to BlackBurrow, and begin soloing the 4 guards posted along the hallway. With a six-minute respawn rate here, after breaking spawn for the deadly duo at the entrance, you have a minute and a half to finish each combat. This area can be used to collect rusty weapons and gnoll skins for resale, but I chose to ignore these items for my nefarious purpose of collecting Gnoll Fangs. About 1 in 6 or 7 gnolls drops a Gnoll Fang, and they even stack! When I am finally nauseated by the sounds of gnoll voices whining, I gate back to PoK, and Book it to Qeynos. I seek out Captain Tillin in the arena, and turn in 20ish Fangs. I then turn in the resulting Moonstones to McNeal Jocub in Fish's Ale as well, and have achieved level eight after about 6 hours of total work.

Comments? Ideas? Improvements? I am actually level 16 currently, and will also write up the 8-16 notes if anyone is interested. Thanks for all the great info on these forums!!

-Ciarraide, Ayonae Ro.
