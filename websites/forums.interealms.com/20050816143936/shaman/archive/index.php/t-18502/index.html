Chutoi
My 2 cents, for what it's worth . . .

Sooner or later they HAVE to raise the level cap. The basic nature of the game requires it. Consider--with each expansion, they have to add new spells and items, right? Pure content works somewhat, but they need selling points for everyone, not just raid stuff, but stuff for the non-raiding folks.

So, what new spells can they offer? (If you're a shaman, nothing worth mentioning!) Look at a cleric or a paladin. They keep getting new heals of larger and larger amounts. This is a good thing since average hp on the high end folks goes up with each expansion (new equipment). However, if the level cap stays the same, then effectively two level 65 clerics are the same. Let me explain.

Cleric1--GoD geared, all spells.
Cleric2--just hit 65, not a raider, but thanks to Yantis has all his spells.

Obviously there are differences in hp and mana pools, but in terms of spells, they are exactly the same. With no differentiation, you have just trivialized any content prior to the last expansion. These 2 characters do different mobs, etc., but cleric2's mobs of choice have been utterly trivialized by the new spells.

I probably haven't explained myself well, but think--why would you have spells of varying power within 1 level? From a balance standpoint, it doesn't make sense.

Of course, that could be what SoE is attempting by making all the spells for GoD no-drop--to limit distribution to those who actually belong in GoD from a level/equip standpoint.

I don't much care for the AA argument either. If new kickass AA are implemented (obviously never for shaman), then people will buy the new kickass AA and skip the earlier less-useful AA. Same effect--making a more powerful character quickly, thus trivializing content. Arguably they could turn it into a more level-like system by making more pre-requisites of earlier AA abilities, but all of a sudden it sounds like the spec system from DAoC. One change in prior pre-reqs, etc, and suddenly you have to refund everything. That would make changing the AAs or retuning them a real nightmare.

Obviously I can't comment definitively, but I don't see how you can avoid raising the level cap. They sort of needed to do it this expansion. I think it's almost a forgone conclusion for the next expansion.
