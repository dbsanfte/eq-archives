In a zone, there are 5 types of mobs. They are all mixed in.

The Councillers have 10 k hps, and cast wizard spells with a 100% damage focus and 50% haste effect. They melee for 100 to 900. Councillers are uncharmable, and non-dot spells last for between 1% and 50% of their standard duration on them (each Counciller has a 50% to 99% spell immunity: what amount they have is random).

The Warriors have 50 k hps. They melee for 200-300, and quad/flurry. Warriors are unsnareable, unstunnable, unrootable, unmezzable, are immune to slow, but are charmable.

The Warbeasts have 20 k hps. They melee for 400 to 2000, but do not Quad, and rarely double-attack. Warbeasts are Uncharmable, and mitigate slow somewhat.

The Scouts are invisible and see invisible, and spawn off traps. The traps trigger scouts not at the trap, but near the trap (generally ahead/behind, but not in aggro range).

Scouts have 30 k hps and melee for 300 to 700, backstab, and are fully slowable.

The Guards are like Scouts, but have 35 k hps, and no special abilities.

The idea is, mobs which vary in HPs and DPS mixed in with each other. So it matters which ones you attack. Fast-burn damage gains utility (kill that Counceller *now*)
