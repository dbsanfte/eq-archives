But this reason in itself isn't the sole reason that we are struggling. We are also struggling because with the now popular straight dps fights, with the mob lasting less than 40 sec, our dots aren't lasting for full length (especially if we have to debuff prior to that). When our sole dps now goes with us being able to cast one Bane that gets in maybe 2 ticks on a mob, pet dps (which is at max 40dps) and maybe one cast of Velium Strike, we suck at dps in quick fights. Next reason is, we do not have a mana regen spell (hence another reason why beastlords and chanters will get the slowing spots before us now especially in GoD). With the emphasis now on quick fights, there are only a few factors that need to be taken a look at. What is your dps and how good is your mana regen. That is why we suck. We do not have a way of giving other classes any form of mana regen, and for ours we must be standing still while not doing anything else but hitting the canni key. DPS wise, if the fight lasts for 40 secs (that's really long for most fights too) then how much dmg do you expect to get in during that fight. Assuming that you have Balance of Nihil, you will still only be able to cast one Bane, maybe 2 VS and 1 Tears of Saryn plus dogdog. Okay, so that's 40dps from dog, along with MAX (offensive AAs maxed) 12k extra dmg in a fight, and that's only with criting all bane ticks and criting all DD casts. But, that is a perfect situation, and we know that without malos first on a mob, we aren't likely to land every spell that we cast, especially not for full. And this dmg is nothing compared to what any other class can do. Not to mention, the agro from slow and bane alone would keep the mob's attention on us.

So, what is stands.. right now our main problems are:

1. We have literally no means of dps in short fights. Too long cast times on our spells, especially our top DDs. Dots will not run the full length on mobs, even our magic based ones. And with no lull type components on any spell other than GoD slow, we get resisted far too often.
2. Slow mitigation has removed our advantage in dps fights. With our serious lack of dps and a mitigated slow, we have lost any real advantage over the other slowing classes.
3. Mana regen. With our lack of dps, we should make up in other areas and we can with mana regen. But, this is our mana regen, not others. Being able to keep myself near full mana does nothing for the real dps classes who are constantly using that mana. A beastlord can give SD, which gives everyone 8 mana regen and they can renew this buff whenever. They also have an AA that gives mana/health regen every 15 min. A chanter has the best mana regen in the game with VoQ. Shaman have nice mana regen.. but it's in the form of self regen only and only while standing still. It's active, it's not passive.
4. Heals. We don't have a problem keeping tanks alive outside of some parts of GoD. With our new heal, we are better healers, but we are not ones that can normally function as the backup healer. We cannot do the main healer duties anwhere from Vxed on, and from Ikkinz on we are suspect at best in our duties as the backup healer. I've done it a few times, but it's not something that didn't happen without the proper classes around (read: we did have a pally there healing if needed, and we had a def tank with alot of hps.). A lot of this depends on gear and AA, and NOT JUST YOUR OWN. So having a blanket statement about what does and doesn't work really doesn't apply well here.

It's probably pretty fair to say that if a shaman is the main healer in an experience group in earth, he had better have a pretty darn good tank, both aggro and defensive AA and gear as well.

And he had better not have a bad run of slow resists. And of course he should have all his mana-enhancing AA and preferably his healing and avoidance/mitigation AA coming along pretty nicely, if not already all the way there.

I've healed in plane of earth when our main healer has died, too, but if slow resists and you get aggro on you, many elemental level shamans are paste pretty quickly. Even if you get past the interrupts, you can't heal yourself faster than an unslowed mob can damage you before someone else attains aggro unless your gear is very high level. The average guy is very likely to be in serious trouble on a resisted slow -- with perhaps no rez for when things go wrong.

If you have fabulous luck and never get resisted slows, then you can do all right for a while, but then you have to take downtime to canni here and there, which a druid or cleric healer wouldn't.

Combine a class who is a distinctly third-rate healer even with all his relevant AA's -- far worse than either a druid or a cleric with hardly any AA's at all -- with a bunch of people who don't want to die and get no rez because they're counting on a healer who is both inferior AND can't rez -- and you really have no reason to be invited in as a main healer in POE.

People can if they want to, and are willing to pay the price of possibly slow pulls while you canni back mana, occasional deaths that a cleric or druid could have easily avoided without breaking a sweat, no rezzes when the deaths come that a third-rate healer can bring you.

Being with time-geared and GoD-geared shamans who have 500 AA each can unbalance the equation, but so can being with shamans freshly elemental with little or no elemental gear, or being with shamans with not much in the way of AA. And that doesn't even factor in absolutely critical factors like how much your tank has in the way of gear, AA, hitpoints, defensive AA, aggro generation capability, or the DPS of the rest of your group.

It's too hard to say whether shamans "can" or "can't" heal in one place or another without bringing in enough clarity that you render the question almost pointless.

There's such a wide variety in the levels of shamans and groups that different people can have completely different answers, and neither one from mere empty theorizing -- both earned from actual experience. What a time-geared and an ornate geared shaman have to say to each other can just uselessly bounce off each other's skulls on an issue like this, neither one really listening to the other or understanding their completely valid perspective.
