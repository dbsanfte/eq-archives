As for 2-way sattelite internet, it has to stink for gaming, or anything that requires a low latency (Low ping). The sattelite is in orbit roughly 22,000 miles up in space. The speed of light is only :p 186,000 miles per second so your signal has to travel roughly 44,000 miles just to get from your house to the sattelite and back down to earth again.
44,000 miles divided by 186,000 mi/second = roughly .23 seconds. Double that for a return signal and you have .46 seconds. This is the minimum time it takes for a ping. This does not take into account any sort of latency for equipment, routers on the ground, from the sattelite ISP's equipment, etc etc. All this means is that the rough minimum ping for sattlelite communications is at least 460ms ping (I get 200-300 over my crummy dialup at home in EQ) but does not include any sort of latency for the ISP's equipment. Realistically, it should be about 500-600 pings. Sattelite is wonderful for surfing the web, downloads, etc, but you'd be better having a dialup connection for any gaming if this is your only options. I'm not going into any debate on which is better, Cable or DSL, they both work great, and you will hear equally bad horror stories about both. I play on multiple DS-3's at work but thanks to our LAN, my pings in EQ slurrrpp... (Now, quake3 and Tribes2 is a completely different story for some reason.... :D )

I apologize for my math, I am using approximations but the concept is sound (I hope, its been a long night in seb :p ). The speed of light is only so fast, and you are communicating by means of something over 22,000 miles in the heavens.

Hope this gives some objective information on what to expect from sattlelite internet. :)
