Rodahn
Caster disciplines.

Like melee disciplines, these activated skills will, for a period of time, improve an aspect of the user's character. Refresh times similar to melee discs would, of course, be part of this system.

Also, to make it a little more balancing (and believable), give pure casters more discs than priests (like how warriors get more than rangers, paladins, SKs, etc). Further, perhaps exclude hybrids from this, or give them only one or two caster discs, since they already have a melee set.

Some examples of caster discs may be: Critical Cast (sort of like warrior's crit disc; maybe exclude WIZ from this since they already have this innate ability) -- Mana Shield (where, for a short time, casters' mana diminishes each tick to put up a self-rune) -- Conservation (when activated, this disc decreases the amount of mana your spells use by a certain amount) -- Mind Wipe (this disc, for a brief time, removes the caster from the target's hate list; maybe include the feature where the caster can re-add himself even during the disc if he casts an extremely high-aggro spell).

Anyway, probably would never happen in a zillion years, but just an idea :)

EDIT: And as far as this maybe conflicting with AA (i.e. making casters too overpowered), I don't see that as an issue. For AA skills, not only must you be the appropriate level, but you also must sink the time and EXP into them. Also, to get to the good skills (which, for casters, are few as it is) you have to earn a fairly large amount of points. Not to mention the fact that many AA skills operate on the "add a modifier to a random die roll" principle, meaning you basically have to get a lucky roll to see your AA skills activate. These discs, while short-lived, will be completely reliable.

Originally posted by Ahaman
I know some melee discipline have both positive and negative effect, like evasive make mobs miss you more, but same time you will miss on mob more also, how about caster discipline suggested? any negative effect?

Personally, I think the long re-use time and short duration are negative enough effects, but you're right, some of the more useful ones will probably have to be balanced further to be even remotely plausible.

So how about this (revisiting my examples):

Critical Cast possible downsides -- high aggro, uses same resists as spell you are casting (so if you cast a spell that has a high resist rate, Crit Cast will not affect its chance to land either positively or negatively)

Mana Shield possible downsides -- Perhaps make it a "chaotic rune" that only absors a random amount of hits for its duration (In other words, each time you are hit a die is rolled; if the number comes up as a failure, you are hit, if a success, an absorption). Maybe also design it to where, as you level, your chance to absorb goes up.

Conservation I feel should be one that has no downside other than short duration/long re-use time. Perhaps make its duration shorter and re-use time longer than normal discs. Amount of mana you save may also increase slightly as you level.

Mind Wipe possible downsides -- As I stated in original post, perhaps make it where, if you choose to cast a high-aggro spell while this disc is in use, this will cancel the mem blur effect and return you to your normal spot on the hate list. This is meant more as a means of keeping yourself alive; your groupmates or pet not being able to retake aggro before the disc wears off is part of the risk you take.
