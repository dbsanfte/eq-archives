Metra
Everyone knows that "Rangers can't tank" right?

Not neccessarily. I've noticed that (starting around level 58) I am actually (surprise) able to tank effectively, despite my lackluster gear (not even runed scout's armor yet except for one slot). The secret to ranger tanking in PoP is our ability to grab aggro fast and hold it solid. It turns out that in PoP, clerics spend most of their mana keeping the tank alive those first few seconds until the mob can be slowed/crippled/etc. Once the mob has been gimped, the amount of mana needed to keep the tank alive is relatively minimal. And the slower can't slow until the main tank has solid aggro, otherwise the mob will bounce around and the cleric will be wasting mana CHealing everyone. So the faster you can grab aggro, the sooner the slower can slow, which means less mana spent on CHeals.

For some reasons Warriors seem to have real problems holding aggro at that level, whereas a snare + immolate will stick them on you like glue. I've tanked for hours at RCY in PoD without a problem, and our shaman was casting slow the instant the mob was in range, with no aggro problems. So in that particular situation, I am actually tanking *better* than a warrior, in the sense that I'm more mana efficient for the cleric. (SKs will of course be even better.)

This only holds true, of course, for the weaker PoP mobs such as lightcrawlers and underbulks. Rangers will have a hard time tanking mobs that can kill you in less time than it takes to slow them. (The foreboding guardians in PoN are an example of a mob that I have trouble with - although if the mobs are slowed and the main tank dies I can take over and finish it.)

So this gives you even more options in terms of group roles, and can only help in finding groups.

If your main tank is a plate class who is unable to grab and hold aggro quickly, then you might be better off with the Ranger tanking. But you would probably be even better off with a different plate MT who can grab aggro better (i.e. SK or Pally). It depends on the exact makeup of the group, what is being fought, etc.

For example, if an early slow is necessary/desirable because the mobs hit hard and fast, then you lose efficiency by having the slower delay the slows to let the MT build aggro. If they don't wait, the mob attacks the slower (especially a problem if your slower is a fragile enchanter) and ping pongs and wastes healer mana, etc. If they do wait, then the MT takes more damage than necessary. If the MT needs early healing because the mob isn't slowed, the cleric may draw aggro and we have more ping-ponging and inefficiency (and in any of these cases higher likelyhood of a death). If the MT can grab and hold aggro quickly, then the slower can slow quickly, and everything runs more smoothly.

In an optimal group, there is an enchanter to tash, and the slower is a shaman who can take a few hits while the MT grabs the aggro back. If you have a non-optimal group and mobs are ping-ponging, mana wasted healing the enchanter, etc. then sometimes having the ranger be MT will be just what is needed. A ranger tanking a slowed mob might be more efficient than a warrior tanking an unslowed mob that is ping-ponging onto the enchanter. Rooting the mob on inc helps, but if your have an AM3/EQ non-MT ranger you are losing efficiency because they get 50% damage on the rooted mob.

It's all about adjusting to the mobs/zone and the class/level/skill of the players involved. No one answer is best in all situations.
