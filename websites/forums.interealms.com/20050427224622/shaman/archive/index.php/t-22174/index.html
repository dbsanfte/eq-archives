weissblau
This is for elemental-flagged level 65's.

First step, get VP3. Seriously. Join an aggro-kiting group until you grind out the AA, it doesn't take long.

GETTING TO THE CAMP. Zone in. Lev, SOW, invis. Head along the RIGHT zone edge until you get to the first castle. Go as far up the mountside as you can, then run toward the castle - the idea is to run on top of the wall, where the mobs are (none of them see invis). Some people have trouble getting on top of the wall, keep trying.

Once on top of the wall, head down the stairs right in front of you, then along the first floor until you come to a staircase in the middle of the hall there, heading down to a courtyard. Run straight ACROSS the courtyard and out the door on the other side.

Once through the door, take a hard right and hug the wall. (This is called the C1 camp on Rodcet, XP is not too bad here although not quite as good as where we're going.) There may be two phoenixes up, they will not aggro if you follow directions. Hug the right wall and run along. You will pass a little gazebo thing with many mobs in it; keep running along the zone wall. You then come to another big castle. There's a rock directly in front of the doors to the castle that I always run to and pause. There are a few see invis mobs on the plain in front of the castle, so be careful. All the mobs guarding the castle see invis.

Time your entry into the castle so that the see-invis wanderering guards do not see you. Run through the middle of the door so the stationary guards do not aggro.

Now you need to run all the way THROUGH this castle. There's a big arena-like area, kind of like a boxing ring, straight in front as you enter with lots of mobs; you want to pass this on the left. Aggro range is not that big, so SPLIT THE DIFFERENCE between this area and the buildings on your left. If you do not, mobs in the buildings may aggro as you run by. The mistake I made a few times was to try to stay too far away from the boxing ring, and I aggroed a couple of mobs from the left-hand side buildings instead. Run STRAIGHT towards the boxing ring until you can split the difference between the ring and the houses on the left.

Run all the way to the back and through the doorway into a courtyard with two buildings in it and some picnic tables. Congratulations, you are at the tables camp (c3 on Rodcet). It's a good idea to pause in the entrance to the tables area and check for aggro. Die or gate if you have any, please don't train those inside. If you don't train, have a good rep, and have an EE on you, a kind necro will often rez you in. Don't worry if it's not a 96% rez, honestly, you will get it back in no time.

PULLING. You will be pulling anything/everything in the tables area, plus Doomfire Soldiers from outside. Stay away from the caster types solo unless you have no other choice, they will break VP frequently.

Move to extreme range and pull with Malicious Decay. Without running, hit VP as soon as MD has landed. It WILL stick and WILL last to full duration, on the melee-only mobs, and the mob should not be able to get to you (use an extended range focus if you have to, they are player-made and purchaseable in bazaar).

Now that the mob is rooted, hit it with malos and then malosinia (you can try just malosinia but it may take a few casts to stick). Lay on Blood of Saryrn and Breath of Ultor, Epic, and your non-DD curse-based dots (Bane, Anathema, Odium). Reapply as needed.

Wait until your refresh button for VP is about 3-4 seconds from refreshing, then if you choose, hit it with a DD dot. This will break root about the same time VP comes up, so reroot. If you'd rather not mess with that extra dot, just skip it and re-VP as soon as the button pops. You have about a 6 second window after VP recycles before the old VP wears off. The last VP break, I pretty much just load it up with fresh dots and let it chase me around so I don't use up my VP and have to wait to engage the next mob.

14% solo AA per kill. I get Burning Affliction IV from tribute and I have CA1; I have Bane and Odium but not Anathema. With all that it takes me about 4:30 to solo one, average, with no downtime if I have KEI. I could improve on that time a bit by getting CA3 and Anathema. At that rate, I'll see an AA every 35-40 minutes.

Oh, and regarding pulling... all the mobs in the tables area come single very easily, even the ones inside the buildings. There are a few tougher pulls outside the tables area. I was under the impression for a long time that these mobs do not assist each other; they DO assist, but the range is very, very small.

The only drawback: it's BORING, folks :-( but it will buy you those AA's you really need.
