This is my summary of 6 months worth of discussion, analysis and polls on the Crucible. I will probably edit in some links to some of those later.

This post is mainly going to address the issues of elemental+ shamans. Some of the changes will of course benefit lower level or lower AAd shamans as well. Overall however I believe that most shaman problems concern the post-elemental part of the game and thus this proposal is centered on fixing shaman issues in that content.

General Shaman issues:
* Shamans have received less upgrades to every single one of their spell lines then all other classes got to their comparable spell lines for every expansion since Kunark. Shamans want at least some of that lost power back. This includes DoTs, nukes, cannibalize (already being adressed), buffs (partially being adressed), heals and pets.
* Shamans suffer more then any other class from the short fights of LDoN and GoD (and to a lesser degree PoP) as well as suffering more then other classes from slow mitigation. Shamans want increases to their healing to at least partially make up for the lost power of slow.
* Shamans still stack poorly on raids. This can probably be adressed (or mostly adressed) by the improvements outlined above.
* DoT aggro is utterly out of control. DoT aggro should be decreased to the level of an equally damaging direct damage.

Specific shaman issues
* When acting as a healer shamans rely on slow to be able to heal at all. Any resist causes the shaman to go into chain healing mode which will at best negate the incoming damage. There is no time to attempt to slow again, which is what shaman healing as balanced around. Shamans would like an emergency ability (most likely as an AA skill) to address this.
* The shaman group haste is still inferior to the single target spell.
* Shamans are still unable to shrink their pets.
* The Avatar line has an effect which is not even close to being in line with the mana and time required to use it (not to mention having to lose a spell gem to it). This is even more pronounced as players cap their stats.

That being said this is the list of specifics:
(Items of minor interest to either the majority of shamans or of low balance impact are in italics. Critical issues are bolded.)


Healing
* Spot Heal
Improve Daluda's Mending to heal 1900 damage. Increase the mana cost to 600. (Also adjust the OoW heal accordingly.)

* Group Heal
Add a shaman group HoT. It should heal 420 points per tick (same as Quiescence) and cost 900 mana.

* Emergency Heal
Add a Spirit Shifting AA in three levels (4/4/4 points at levels 61/63/65). This ability will drain 12/25/40% of the shaman's HPs and give them to his current target. The casting time should be no higher then 1 second. The reuse time should be 10 minutes.


Mana Regeneration
* Cannibalize
Shaman spells are balanced on the assumption that shamans regenerate mana much faster then other classes. As other classes have received a much larger increase to their mana regeneration over the last expansions then shamans did shaman mana regeneration is in need of an upgrade. This can come either in the form of more efficient cannibalize spells or in the form of cannibalize spells that basically combine several castings of cannibalize into one big cast, thus freeing up time for other tasks.
The current ancient cannibalize spell is a good spell, but something so central to the shaman class should not be available to a select few. Make the new cannibalize spell a purchaseable spell in PoK.


Damage
* DoT aggro
Decrease DoT aggro significantly. A DoT that does 2000 damage should not add more aggro then a direct damage spell that does 2000 damage.

* Poison nukes
Increase Spear of Torment to do 900 damage. Up the mana cost to 370.

* Ice nukes
Increase Velium Strike to do 1300 damage. Up the mana cost to 430.

* Poison DoTs
Increase Blood of Saryrn to do 400 damage per tick. Up the mana cost to 650 to match that increase. Add a -30 resist adjustment. (Also change the OoW poison DoT accordingly. Also apply the resist adjustment to lower level poison DoTs.)

* Magic DoTs
Increase Bane to do 500 damage per tick. Up the mana cost to 600. Add a -60 resist adjustment. (Apply equivalent changes to the other spells of this line.)


Debuffs
* Balance of the Nihil
Up the resist modifier on Balance of the Nihil to -55 to match Malos.


Buffs
* Avoidance
The replacement buff for our agility line has a listed effect of 7% avoidance. In actual play this seems to work out to an actual 4% avoidance. It should be improved to 10% (listed) to produce an actual 6% avoidance effect in game.

* Damage
The replacement buff for our strength line has a listed effect of 5% melee damage. In actual play this seems to work out to an actual 2% damage. It should be improved to 10% (listed) to produce an actual 4% damage effect in game.

* HPs
The OoW shaman stamina buff currently adds stamina past the stat cap. Shamans fear that stamina will suffer from additional dimishing returns past 305. If the spell ends up adding less then 200 HPs to a stat-capped 65 warrior it should be removed in favor of a non-capped effect, similar to the other buff replacements (stun resist or shielding seem appropriate).

* Focus
Increase the HPs on the OoW version of the focus line to 850. Remove the strength and dexterity components and replace them with +melee damage to match the standalone buff.

* Focus
Increase the HPs on Focus of Soul to 650.

* Avatar
The effect of the avatar line is not at all in line with its cost. Either substantially up the effects of this spell line, add a group version of the spell or substantially up the duration.

* Group haste
The shaman group haste is inferior to the single target spell. Up the haste to match that of the single target version.

Misc
* Pet
Make the shaman pet innately dual weild (currently it dual weilds once given weapons).

* Pet shrink
Make the shaman pet shrinkable again.


-- Adjust other spells accordingly" means that the spell in question should receive the same percentual improvements compared to the base spell as comparable spells of other classes get in that expansion. --

In terms of efficiency (straight damage per mana) we rank as follows:

Wizards are the tops with 4.28 dmg/mana (SoS and White Fire)
Mages next with 4 dmg/mana (Firebolt of Tallon)
Druids next with 3.97 dmg/mana. (winter's Frost)
Enchanters with 3.66 dmg/mana (Madness of Ikkibi)
Shaman with 3.08 dmg/mana (Velium Strike)
Clerics with 3.02 dmg/mana (Order)
Necromancers with 2.79 dmg/mana (Neurotoxin)
Rangers with 2.78 dmg/mana (Brushfire)
Beastlords with 2.7 dmg/mana (Trushar's Frost)
Shadowknights with 2.67 dmg/mana (Miasmic Wind)

I was a little surprised here, I expected Sony to give us much less efficient nukes to "balance" against cannibalize.

Your changes to the nukes would result in a slight decrease in the efficiency of our nukes leaving us exactly where we are. This is fine. However it neglects to take in account the changes in dps this would results in.

A simplistic look at dps would be to evaluate the potential dps from chain casting the nuke (casting nothing but the nuke in question). To calculate this, we need to take the refresh time into account. The results are as follows:

Wizards are tops with 301.5 dmg/sec (White Fire)
Mages with 222.2 dmg/sec (Firebolt of Tallon)
Druids with 206.7 dmg/sec (Sylvan Fire)
Necromancers with 130 dmg/sec (Neurotoxin)
Shaman with 108 dmg/sec (Spear of Torment)
Clerics with 89 dmg/sec (Order)
Enchanters with 86 dmg/sec (Madness of Ikkibi)
Hybrids are all less than 25 dmg/sec due to the long recast times on their nukes 30+ sec.

This analysis is not accurate, fights do not last long enough for nukers to fire off unlimited nukes (disregarding mana constraints) Instead I like to look at an 18 second window for nuking. Still sticking to one spell, we get the following dps numbers:

Wizards with 335 dmg/sec (White Fire)
Mage with 222 dmg/sec (Firebolt of Tallon)
Druid with 189 dmg/sec (Sylvan Fire)
Necromancer with 144 dmg/sec (Neurotoxin)
Shaman with 75 dmg/sec (Spear of Torment)
Cleric with 67.2 dmg/sec (Order)
Enchanter with 67.2 dmg/sec (Madness of Ikkibi)
Ranger with 42.7 dmg/sec (Sylvan Burn)
Beastlord with 41.2 dmg/sec (Trushar's Frost)
Shadowknights with 36.6 dmg/sec (Miasmic Wind)

I'm still not satisfied with this as an adequate picture of true nuking ability. Let's assume instead that each nuking class is willing to devote two spell gems to their nukes and is willing to cycle through them to achieve maximum output of damage over the same 18 sec interval. The numbers:

Wizards with 335 dmg/sec (White Fire)
Mage with 222 dmg/sec (Firebolt of Tallon)
Druid with 189 dmg/sec (Sylvan Fire)
Necromancer with 144 dmg/sec (Neurotoxin)
Cleric with 128 dmg/sec (Order and Condemnation)
Enchanter with 128 dmg/sec (Madness of Ikkibi and Insanity)
Shaman with 88 dmg/sec (Spear of Torment and Velium strike)
Ranger with 81 dmg/sec (Sylvan Burn and Frozen Wind)
Beastlord with 78 dmg/sec (Trushar's Frost and Frost Spear)
Shadowknights with 70 dmg/sec (Miasmic Wind and Spear of Decay)

Now the number start to reveal a problem. Shaman are dipping dangerous low into the realm of hybrid nuking, still better but barely. Ideally I'd like to see our nuking on par with Clerics and Enchanters, but I'd accept being somewhat below them. The changes proposed put us at:

122 dmg/sec (Spear of Torment and Velium strike)

We're still a step behind Enchanters, Clerics and Necros, but enough ahead of Hybrids to be considered viable sources of dps. (All this hinges off the assumption that while DPS from DoTs can be good, they just don't cut the mustard on the shorter fights)
