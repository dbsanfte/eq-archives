Yzen Danek
What VI really needs to learn about putting effects on armor is:

If you give pure melees armor spell effects, you need to also give every other class effects that they cannot already cast.

If, for some reason, they must put on an effect we can already cast, it needs to cast as fast or faster than the spell we already have, or save us an appreciable amount of mana. If there are no new spell effects to give casting classes without blurring class roles, then upgrades to existing spell lines work well, e.g. the cry of fire effect on Vex Thal gloves. Few enough people see each high end upgrade that granting a spell upgrade in that fashion isn't going to unbalance anything.

For rangers in particular, anything else is useless to our class. Rangers are never strapped for mana during the buffing phase of a raid. Never. Clerics, shamans and enchanters are over in the corner laying down many thousands of mana of buffs per group, and we all regen mana at the same rate. Even a single ranger can buff a full raid on one bar of our relatively puny mana pools, and be ready to go before the primary buff classes are done and full mana again.

Even PoTW on the bp would be pretty useless. This is a spell that only needs to be cast once every 2 and a half hours, that's 1500 ticks, for a mana cost of 450. That's equivalent to FT0.3. One third of one mana point per tick. On the breastplate, the flagship of the armor set. Riiight. The gloves are Vengence III, and the BP is FT0.3.

If it were me making that armor, I'd just put 41% haste on the ranger bp, and fix that seemingly ubiquitous problem I read about for rangers getting shackled to Swiftwind. Problems solved; everyone's happy. Put 'Effect: Swift Spirit' on the Elemental BP, and you're an itemizing champion.
