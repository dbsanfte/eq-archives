1. The starting zones for evil races aren't necessarily bad. In particular, the Iskar's Field of Bone is a very popular noobie zone. If anything, many good races go there and thus have to worry about being slain by Iskar guards. I personally love EverFrost (barbarian shaman, go figure) and always have...but there are other great noobie zones.

2. Evil races, particularly the Iskar with their innate improved regen, are compensated for the fact that evil faction is a sometimes a hassle. PoP makes life much easier for evils than they had it in the past because they can just book everywhere without having to avoid guards in many zones like they used to have to do in ancient times. Heck, my main in ancient times was a DE wiz and he travelled everywhere invis just to be safe and I didn't want to leave Nektulos until everything was green!

As stated, it's about game balance. Not to mention that nobody is forced to play an evil race and if you do, deal with the faction issues!

I would add that although you can book around now and hunt wherever you want as a noobie...any real noobie (new player noobie) that doesn't have a friend, doesn't know how to farm tradeskill stuff for plats, etc, might be inclined to do their noobie armor quests (if they somehow figure out those quests exist without help, which is probably unlikely, I think more experienced players do those quests than true noobs as a novelty) - which make you stay around your home zone until about 10th depending on your luck with the idiotically rare drops needed to complete noobie armor. A true noob would also be less likely to book around since they wouldn't know the system and would probably be afraid to get that far from home.
