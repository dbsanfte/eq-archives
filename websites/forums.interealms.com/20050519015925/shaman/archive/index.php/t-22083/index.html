Very short parses, obviously, but all that missing looks quite impressive at first glance. Hwoever, there are a few caveats.

The first one is that a knight can tank higher level mobs that would probably kill the Monk. That limits your choice of targets somewhat.

The second one is that Monks' avoidance is good, but their mitigation is not nearly as good as what the knights have. Hence your Monk may take little damage during 3-4 fights in a row and then get a string of hits for full damage, which will really hurt on non-trivial content when mobs' max hit is for, say, >10% of yout total HP. It doesn't make for a profitable XP session for you to cruise through 4 mobs and go 'splat' on the 5th one.

Third, unlike knights, Monks don't have a way to generate snap agro reliably. That's a major issue in many situations.

Fourth, utility. Monks have 2 major utility abilities: FD as an innate skill as opposed to the SKs' spell (fizzles, interrupts, can't cast on the run) and an irresistable lull on mobs <65, which is, in many cases, very VERY useful. On the other hand, Pallies can root/heal/lull lower level content/buff/etc and SKs can snare/self-heal/group-heal/DOT/etc, so on balance I would say that their utility is considerably higher, especially in case of the SKs.

Finally, the whole issue of DPS. At first, Monks will outdamage SKs and especially Paladins, no question about it. However, at higher levels SKs gain access to good weapons and KEI and can chain cast for a long long time, so their damage output comes close to that of Monks. I believe that Paladins stay behind in the DPS department until the end, but I don't really know the end game, so can't comment on that.

Anyway, I am reasonably happy with my Monk's performance (although more AAs wouldn't hurt), but I am also starting to build a SK as an experiment. We'll see how they stack up once the SK gains 60ish levels :)

Welp, I'm of the old school "shaman/monk" train-of-thought, just 'cause I love both classes. I duo my monk and shaman not only by themselves, but in xp groups and raids. The good thing about a monk is that you "stack." If I go into a group, and there is already a monk, so what? We both do helacious damage, so no prob there. If I were a SK/warrior/pally, and there was someone else in the group go tank, welp, I don't stack as well.

Tanking --- first, it's all about gear and AA. A wizard can tank a slowed mob with the right gear and AA. Get your hps and AC to a respectable level, and get LR5, ID5, ND3, etc... and you'll be able to tank things that other people who don't actually know would realize. My monk tanks in PoTactics all the time, those guys hit for nearly 600. That's without LR5 and ID5!

It's all about getting mobs slowed ASAP. Once that is done, the monk will do just fine. The monkie has tanked 2 PoTactics mobs at once and the shaman kept him on his feet. Monks get a few snazzy discs and mend that helps in a pinch. The discs also help when you wanna duo a named mob somewhere --- engage boss, monk hits disc, and mend when you've lost about 30% health, and by that time the shaman should have the mob debuffed.

The thing that a monk does better than a SK or Pally or warrior is damage. Mobs drop faster (thus reducing damage taken due to less time engaged with mob). Monks love shaman buffs and perform well with them. Monks are also awesome at pulling, especially dungeons which is where I practically live. FD and lull make it much easier to split mobs.

The only thing my combo lacks is snare and tracking... I'd love to have those abilities, especially in dungeons.

I also have a 61 ranger who is not as well-geared as my monk, and I can definitely vouch that a ranger/shaman combo is extremely versatile and utilitarian... I'd love to combine the ranger and monk class into one and then I'd have my perfect combo. :)
Shadrak
I'll be a bit unorthodox here. Obviously lots of support for the plate classes, mostly shadowknights, it seems. I'll preface all of this by stating that my first love was my monk, born a few days after EQ went live way back in April of 1999. He's still around today, at level 59 :p

I'll vote for magician. The mage air and earth pets have excellent agro - better than a monk by far. The air pet procs a stun that grabs a mobs attention quick. Once that stun goes off once, I can slow without problem and throw a Bane of Nife in right after. Mobs really don't like that stun.

Mages are possibly the easiest class of all to two-box. Just set up a macro to assist and pet attack, and a few more for nukes, rains, malo, damage shield, etc. They require almost no attention, because compared to shamans, they basically generate no agro at all. I can happily drop 2k nukes with my mage while my poor shaman takes a beating from landing a 1500-hp DOT that will take 42 seconds to expire.

The 59+ mage pets also improve significantly if you've got a pet focus item. The easiest to obtain is the Diamond Prism of Companionship, purchased from the North Ro LDoN camp for 760 points. With this focus, or any of the higher focuses, mage PoP pets regen at very high rates, hit harder, come pre-equipped with plate armor, and mitigate damage better. The level-61 air pet generates excellent agro, does respectable dps, and avoids very well, while the level-65 earth pet has oustanding mitigation, beefy hitpoints (~6k shaman-buffed) and decent agro via his root procs. In my opinion, both of these pets make very viable tanks. When they're tanking slowed mobs, they make outstanding tanks.

Combine that with the other benefits a mage brings.


The second-best nukes in the game (virtual tie with druids)
Excellent damage shields for older content
Virtually the same malo line as shamans to help spread out agro
Summoned armor to significantly improve dogdog's AC and add over 500 hitpoints

Summoned weapons that proc a 140hp dd spell - no more getting to your hunting location only to realize you forgot to stock up with rusty daggers for our dual-wieldless dogdog.


The shaman, meanwhile, adds all the utility the mage lacks, most notably crowd control. Poor mages can't even root, for goodness sake. If their earth pet proc doesn't go off at the right time, it's hello-train. But teamed with a shaman, their pets perform better (marginally improved via stat buffs, more notable improvements via direct hp and AC buffs) and last longer (thanks to slow, assuming you're fighting slowable mobs). It's a very symbiotic relationship.

The mage/shaman duo is very viable, especially if, like me, you won't have access to raid-quality items. I'd much rather have a bazaar-equipped level 65 mage as a shaman partner than a bazaar-equipped level 65 shadowknight or monk. You'll get MUCH better dps, very good agro, and excellent durability.
