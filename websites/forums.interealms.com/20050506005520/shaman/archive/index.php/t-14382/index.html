Bigguss
I know this sounds lame as hel|, but it worked for me.

Turn everything down- underclock, RAM frequencies, video card clock, AGP multiplier, yadda yadda. See if it works (much to my surprise, that fixed it for me... tested by leaving a toon in the bazaar 24/7 for a couple days.)

If that works, it's just a matter of turning one thing on at a time and re-trying. Eventually you'll get the config that your machine can handle.

In my case it was a heat thing. I was faced with underclocking- well, really undoing some of the overclocking I'd done before- a couple hundred Mhz, or getting a new heat-sink/fan unit. Since I'd picked out the HSF unit special to be extra quiet, I undid some of my overclocking, and all is well.

It'sa pretty quick test- just turn everything way down- if you crash to the desktop again, you know it's not anything that tweaking setting will fix and you can move on to other potential problem areas (bad RAM, software problem, Direct X, driver goofiness, whatever.)

If underclocking fixes the problem, the problem isn't with EQ- it's with your hardware/heatware. I should mention that when I had this issue, everything else (Neverwinter Nights, Quake 3, Wolfenstein, Max Payne, yadda yadda) all worked fine... only EQ caused the problem.
