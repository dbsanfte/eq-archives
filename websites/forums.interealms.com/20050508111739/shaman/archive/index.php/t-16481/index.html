Jinheim
You can make a lot of money via tradeskills, either by farming ingredients and selling them to those that would use them, or by working a tradeskill up and selling products.

Your best bet for farming is going to be farming acrylia, rockhopper hides, othmir furs, and that kind of stuff. Check eqtraders.com and your local bazaar to find out what is in demand and sells well.

For working your own tradeskills up, it obviously depends on how much capital you have. You can make a ton of money off of fletching, for example, but you're going to need to sink about 100k in to it in order to be able to make anything worthwhile. On top of that, you'll have to buy the ingredients to make the good stuff since you obviously won't have elemental farming abilties at level 52.

Pottery is easier than that. You can get to max skill for around 30k or so, but you're only going to make a few K off of each successful combine. Still, I am guessing a 30k investment is a bit much for you. I sure as hell didn't have that much money at level 52.

The best thing for you would probably be baking. A few hours a night for a week spent making misty thicket picnics will get you to 250 skill. It will cost you about 2-3k to get there, but if you sell the picnics you made, you'll make up for the loss. Once you get 250 skill, you can just keep making picnics. Make a few hundred of them and throw them on a trader (a mule that you can keep active 24/7 helps a lot, but it still works if you just leave your char up overnight). I find that if I keep my trader constantly stocked with picnics, I make about 1-3k a day. This obviously isn't a lot of money, but it is nice steady income. In addition to picnics, buy all the cheap hero parts you see in bazaar and make hero sandwichs. Surprisingly, they have a much higher success rate than picnics. They cost a lot more per combine, but they are quick and easy to make, and sell for a lot more (100pp per sandwich, vs. 5pp per picnic).

I make most of my money just by selling drops I get while exping, but tradeskills have been a decent addition to my income. It might not be for you, though. I have a very high tolerance for doing tradeskills (I actually enjoy it, go figure), but I have a very low tolerance for going to newbie zones and farming greens for plat. Hill Giants might work better for you, though.

All in all, though, you should consider just biting the bullet and working on levels. It is much much easier to make money when you're 65 than when you're not.

Whatever you decide to do, good luck.

The biggest way to get more plat is to *stop* buying things.

Basically, the biggest problem I see people having when they don't have any money is not the money coming IN it's the money going OUT.

pp drops practically *everywhere*. Sure, there are some camps that yield better pp than others (such as the excellent examples above) but most any camp at all will yield up some sort of loot.

Stop spending money on alts. Stop spending money on stat-food. Stop spending money on those 'little' upgrades (500pp here, 400pp for this, etc). Treat your bank like a one-way street and only put money in for a couple weeks and see what happens.
