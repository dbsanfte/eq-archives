[QUOTE='Gaack'] Guess people dont want to call it animal kiting, or panic kiting huh?[/QUOTE]

Nah, not a moron, just ignorant. Ignorance can be fixed - stupidity is forever. :)

Anyhow, jibes aside, fear kiting isn't specific to one class. Necros and SKs get generic "fear" abilities, usable on all types of mobs. Clerics and paladins get the ability to fear undead mobs. Druids and rangers get the ability to fear animal mobs. In all cases, the concept is the same: fear the mob, and chase after it, meleeing/casting as you go. Thanks to the ranger/druid limitation of only being able to fear animals, and it not being that obvious as to what mobs are animals and what aren't (e.g. why aren't spiders and beetles considered animals? why are griffons?), a guide like this is quite useful to us.

Hope this helps clarify. I'm eagerly awaiting completion of the guide.
