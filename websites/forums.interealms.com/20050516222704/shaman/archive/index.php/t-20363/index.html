Chutoi
Corlathist, while unarguably true, the fact that raw numbers are increasing is kind of moot.

As long as the percentages are held firm, the net effect is zero. It still takes XX casts of shaman heal to get the tank to full. Honestly, it's a moot point.

The telling factor in this expansion is twofold. First, absent the spells actually on test atm, the shaman heals did not get increased to maintain healing percentages in comparison to other healing classes. The second factor is currently more important since the first factor has been fixed (at least on test): underlying changes to the game make an occasional re-evaluation of the percentages necessary.

Due to underlying changes to the game, the shaman are trying to get the developers to question the percentage heals. Apparently we were pegged at 47% heals at least as far back as the inception of PoP. That was before (a) mitigation, and (b) the change to mob hp vs dps.

The shaman have been arguing, so far unsuccessfully, that due to the widespread prevalence of mitigation the dev's need to examine the base percentage of heals for high level heals. More specifically, since stuff now resists about 50% of our slow to have a net slow of probably 37.5%, that shaman heals need to be bumped up to probably around 1800ish. I'm not sure what that equates to in the heal distribution, but let's just call it 50% instead of 47%. Someone who's got more time can actually do the math and give us a correct percentage later.

So, mobs mitigate. Their dps does not go down as far, but our healing ability does not compensate. No other healing class is balanced against slow (I discount the minor heals that BLs get), so it only really affects shaman. Thus, while mitigation hits all slowing classes, it really double whacks the shaman since our heals remain unchanged. That problem goes back to PoP.

Now enter GoD, where mobs hit much harder and mitigate, but have less hitpoints. Fights are over faster, so over the course of a fight, mitigated slows, or even bounced slows are of substantially less importance. The difference of 30% slows over the course of a 30 second fight is relatively trivial, especially since most cleric and druid heals contain an 'overheal' component--heals above and beyond the hp the tank has. GoD reduces that overheal component a lot, so much so that many cleric/druid heals hit for full efficiency. To some extent, that makes druids and clerics MORE efficient, though at the cost of some anxiety since you have larger spikes in hp! Pity the hapless tanks!

Shaman heals, on the other hand, are effectively gimped further. We were already healing for full efficiency. Now we have a situation where we are penalized for slow by having a lower percentage heal, then we're given a less efficient heal, then we're trying to heal more damage with a heal that, because it almost always hits for full, can't get any more efficient, over the course of fights that further trivialize the impact of slow by being of such short duration. Slow saves us mana on healing over the course of a longer fight. Short duration fights, especially since we have to slow at the beginning just don't prevent as much damage.

Shaman were designed as an endurance class, and that concept is no longer relevant in GoD. Slow makes healing more efficient over the course of long fights. Thus, by slow, shaman add endurance to the healers' mana. Over the short term, we don't really save them ANY mana--they still have to heal once or twice. Over the long term, we can save them a TON of heals.

DoTs take a long time to work. Cannabalize/mana regen depends on our ability to take the time to do it. Burst healing and dps is now king, but shaman are at best pawns at both of those areas.

For illustration purposes, shaman got hit in the face with a large fish--not once, but several times.

Slow is mitigated: You have been smacked with a Salmon!
Your PoP heal isn't balanced to compensate: Smack!
You aren't given new heals in GoD: Smack!
More mobs mitigate: Smack!
To progress you need to finish trials with lots of unslowable nameds: SMACK! SMACK!
Shorter fights on non-named further reduce the effectiveness of slow: Smack!

I've rambled past the topic, but really, it's not the raw numbers that is the issue. It's a more funamental question of adjusting the percentages to account for significant changes in game mechanics. That analysis by the devs is still lacking.

Brrakk, mobs 'mitigate' slow. You'll see the following messages:
Target yawns
Your slow was mostly effective
Your slow was partially effective
Your slow was slightly effective

Our estimates of effect are as follows. The first is a full slow, so hits for 75% (normal turgur's level). The second is only 75% effective, so slows the mob for (.75 x .75) = 56.25%. Partially is 50% effective, so slows for 37.5%. Slightly is 25% effective, so slows the mob for maybe 18.75%.

The effect is to lessen the effect of slow on the mob. The mob is not as hindered by our spells.

Let's see why that "balanced in the brave new world" group of war/war/clr/clr/ench/ench is better than the "balanced by any reasonable meaning of the word balance" group of shaman/druid/paladin/bard/wizard/rogue.

Tanking: The brave new world group is better because an ornate equipped warrior is >>>>>>>>>>>>>> a 2200+ AC knight on the bosses because of the asinine disciplines added in Kunark. Hell, a ranger is a million times better. Everybody with a clue has been saying these were game-destroyingly imbalanced since the day they were put in (usually to the detriment of warriors actually, so this is not an anti-warrior rant).

Instead of fixing the tanking problems that they created with this craptacular move, the made them worse by shifting to small DB/large DI model for the mobs in an effort to prevent all the +avoidance stuff that they put in the game from making them too easy, which drastically increases the value of these disciplines and greatly increases the chance of damage spikes on the knights.

CC: Yeah, a couple enchanters should be much better than a lone bard. In fact, they should up the CC requirements and ditch the mez immunity in a lot of places to keep the game exciting for CC classes.

Damage: The enchanters in the brave new world group -- in addition to providing the best CC in the game as they should -- also obliterate not just the wizard and rogue, but the entire old school group combined in the DPS category and at minimal risk (because there are 2 of them). Mmmm hmmm.

Healing: 2 clerics should be a little better than a shaman + druid + paladin. They should not, however, be overwhelmingly better. While the shaman brings slow (largely irrelevant in a world where most bosses are unslowable, and in any event the job can be done with your enchanters) and the druid brings dps (which is an utter joke compared to the enchanters'), the clerics not only bring better, faster heals that can minimize the risk of your tank dying to a damage spike but they also bring the most valuable utility skill in the game to the table (rez), which is greatly magnified in importance in GoD. The old school group has only the paladin for rez, and considering the fact that she is the one most likely to die (see "tanking") one spike (which has nothing to do with skill, only the luck of the RNG) and it's lights out.


If they want to make an area that suits oddball group compositions like Magician x6 or warx2, clrx2, enchx2, that's fine. But not in an area that everyone needs to progress through.
