Shabura Naarteban
Right now there are 2 sets of problems. First is the in-game problem: Shaman upgrades are marginal, and the class is being marginalized. The second is the in-forum problem: People don't believe there is a first problem.

I'll address them in the other order, because if we don't think there is a problem, we can't even talk about fixing it.

Shaman who aren't in elemental+ gear and guilds say things like:

And, yes, I would say that any class that can solo (with ease based on the tales posted here) in the elemental planes isn't very likely to be getting any kind of significant boost in power. How many classes can solo the elemental planes with ease? How do you boost a class in a significant way without making it more powerful as a soloist?

But the problem is that almost all casters can solo Fire (nothing summons - its no harder than soloing Velious mobs, just longer). And no one, shaman included, can solo the other 3. I think a lot of folks have missed that point, but to reiterate ... the reason we can solo redcons in the back of Fire is one reason and one reason alone. They do not summon. That's it, has nothing to do with the power of slow, or our amazing dps ... anyone with root (or snare) can do it.

And, sure, there ARE things we can solo that others probably can't, like Zelnithak in Umbral Plains. However, the "wow, that's old content" argument aside, we cannot do so -efficiently-. Being able to take 2 hours to solo a mob does not indicate the strength of a class. For a class to be strong, it needs to do things in a useful setting - either grouped or on raids, and in a timely manner. If your measure of greatness for a class is what you can solo given infinite time, PLEASE do not take part in the discussion; your values are not in line with Sony's, and your opinions will confuse the issues - no offense.

Instead, we will look at how the shaman compare to the other priest classes, to beastlords, and in some cases to other classes.

1) Mass Group Buff utility.
Having a mid-combat useful MGB option is important, because it allows multiple members of a class to have a valuable contribution. This works to counteract marginalization - specifically, it works to ensure that more than 1 of a class is useful for a raid.

Clerics have Ethereal Elixir. Druids have Protection of Wood. Beastlords have Paragon of Spirit. Shaman do not have a equivelent.

2) Healing
Since the dawn of Everquest, the 3 wis casters have been the masters of healing. Clerics, by nature, are the superior healers; we have no right to compete thus.

CLASS....................FAST heal/LARGE heal
Cleric.....................2750/7500
Druid......................1750/4680
Paladin....................850/900*
Shaman..................1300/1950
*group heal

As it stands, we are distinctly third in the healing race, with paladins reasonably close, and circumstantially preferable.

3) Direct damage
While not intended to be a strong point of the shaman class, the issues involved in dots and the speed of PoP events makes direct damage valuable.

3A) Standard nukes.

CLASS....................Damage/Mana (Efficiency)
Cleric.....................1100/365 (3.01)
Druid......................1550/390 (3.97)
Enchanter...............1100/300 (3.67)
Ranger....................610/220 (2.77)
Shaman..................925/300 (3.08)

Again, of the priest classes, we are decidedly third, and are not far ahead of the frontrunner hybrid class. Additionally, we are greatly eclipsed by Enchanters, a class formerly referred to as a "low-dps" class, and for which direct damage is also not meant to be a primary focus.

3B) Non-standard nukes
Rains have proven to be a reasonable source of damage for shaman. However, our rains are still not particularly viable.

Considered as if it were a standard nuke, a 3x 650/450 rain provides 4.33 efficiency. However, maximally efficient rains are rare. More commonly, onyl 2 of the 3 waves will hit, and this also serves to better approximate the more limited situations in which the spells are available. This equates to a 1300/450 nuke, with a 2.89 efficiency. In other words, unless all 3 waves of the rain land, our best rain is in fact a poorer mana-to-damage ratio than our nuke! To its credit, it is more time efficient, which is why it still gets used.

Certianly some people will take umbrage to the means by which I considered the rain damage. That said, a justifiable direct comparison would be to compare our special-situation direct damage to other special-situation direct damage (such as undead nukes).

CLASS....................Damage/Mana (Efficiency)
Cleric.....................1200/300 (4.00) (undead)
Druid......................1200/300 (4.00) (summoned)
Shaman..................1950/450 (4.33) (rain)

We do thus have the highest damage and efficiency use-restricted nuke among the priests. However, as noted above, the damage (and thus efficiency) of our use-restricted nuke is questionable. Whether or not there are sufficient undead/summoned mobs available to warrant the other classes' nukes is not an issue that I am prepared to discuss here. Regardless, the net result is that our lead here, as it stands, is a small one.

4) HP Buffs
Buffing is an essential part of this class, regardless of its potential to relegate shaman to bot-status. Because of the current state of equipment, the only buffs that make a significant impact are +hp buffs (and, to a lesser extent +ATK buffs)

CLASS....................+HP buff
Cleric......................1405*
Druid.......................618
Shaman...................544
Paladin....................330
*or 910, stacks with druid.

Even at this, our purported strength, we are third among the priest classes, with a hybrid not far behind.

We do not even perform well in regards to other, less directly valuable buffs. We have a +55% movement speed buff. Rangers get +60%. Druids get +65%. Shaman -do- have the best poison and disease resist buffs in game, but the quality of that buff is lower than the equivelent spells received by other classes. Talisman of the Tribunal is +65 pr/dr. Protection of Seasons is +72 fr/cr. Guard of Druzzil is +75 mr.

5) What else is there?
Well, despite all the complaints in that regard, we DO have the best slow. Malos is the best broad-spectrum debuffer. Quiescence fills a niche role for sustainable heal-over-time. However, these should not be used to justify our huge shortcomings across the board in other areas, because they are matched by other, equivelent effects. We have slow, true - but druids have snare, and clerics stun; all these are mob management effects, and each class is the best at their particular one. But beyond this, there is a clear conclusion ... slow (especially in the era where it is no longer uniquely tied to shaman), malos, and quiescence are insufficient to justify the number of key feature in which shaman are 3rd among 3. "But you have slow" is simply inadequate compensation. In Kunark, certainly. Post-PoP, no - and doubly so because this "key" spell that seems to hold back out other spell progression dates back from the very first expansion. We are the only class that still depends on a Kunark spell on a daily basis, with no possible upgrade.*

*Wizard fans will note that Concussion is a Kunark spell, and the only generally available wizard de-aggro spell. However, despite its relative scarcity, there IS an extant upgrade, Ancient: Greater Concussion. That's not a viable upgrade for most wizards, but it does exist. Shaman do not possess even this option.

6) Where to go from here?

It is difficult to say. I could set my opinions about the relative priority of these problems, and which ones I would prefer shaman to be boosted in, but another player would likely view them differently. In the end, it IS up to Sony, like it or not, to dictate the balance between the classes in this manner. It might behoove us to propose specific spells to "fix" some of the inequities; it might not. At a minimum, I would posit that we should demonstrate our unified support in the desire for SOME change, that our dissatisfaction is not that druids heal better or that enchanters nuke better, but that our ire is drawn instead from the volume and consistency with with we are supplanted and marginalized. That, more than anything is how to unify the shaman community. It might not get us the exact change each of us would want - but at this point, a rally for change in and of itself is perhaps our last, best motivating force.
