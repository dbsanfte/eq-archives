The first thing I want to point out is that about half these spells are Target Type: Unknown(34). For reference, here is another spell with Target Type: Unknown(34) that is already live: Reebo's Greater Cleansing (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4287&source=Test). This is the spell that we use to disarm cursed boxes in LDoN. If you try to cast it on something besides an appropriate box, you get something to the effect of "This spell only works on the cursed." This makes me believe that all of the target type: Unknown(34) spells will be the only spells that work on entire species of mobs. From here on out, I'm going to call these mobs "cursed" because I am frankly very sick of typing Target Type: Unknown(34) :)

Here are some trends I noticed:

1) Most classes got an upgraded nuke that does exactly 10% more damage. Necros, and Shamans are the only nuking class (not counting paladins or bards as nukers) that do not get a new nuke. Enchanters get a new nuke, but it actually does less damage than their old nuke. I expect this to be fixed and go live at 1210 damage.

2) The three primary dotting classes (Shaman, Druid, and Necro) all get dots which do dismal damage compared to what they would normally use. The new druid and shaman dots do about half the damage of their corresponing precedent spell, and the necro dot only does a third. However, the druid and necro dots are set to type Unknown34 targets, whereas the shaman dot is set to normal targets. I expect this is an oversight and will go live as an Unknown34.

3) Possibly the most important thing I have noticed: Enchanters do not get a type Unknown34 slow. We get a 70%, and beastlords get a 55%. This means that if the expansion is full of cursed mobs, enchanters will no longer be viable slowers, and shaman slow is once again significantly ahead of beastlord slow.

Even if cursed mobs are only found in certain raid content, this is still a good thing. It gives us something that we can do and enchanters cannot, and we do it significantly better than beastlords. Like being the only class that gets a disease slow, this is a great thing and a great boost to shaman desireability (unless of course I am completely wrong on what Unknown(34) means).

Another thing I would like to point out is that no one except bards (reflect song) is getting anything special. Everyone is just getting minor upgrades to the same crap they have had for years. Before you rant and rave about how shamans are being nerfed by not getting anything new or different, take a good look at what everyone else is getting. Like GoD AA's, GoD spells look pretty lackluster all around:-\ I hope a lot more spells go live than just 2 per class, and we get a lot more AA abilities.
