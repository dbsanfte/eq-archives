Like i said, it's a big issue on the PvP servers. The GMs on PvP servers are constantly being flooded with /petitions that are usually a result of people exploiting in PvP to kill players, or getting them killed by mobs. People on PvP are CONSTANTLY doing illegal things to kill each other. As a result, the GMs watch more closely what people are doing, and will slap warnings and bannings if they catch you cheating. It takes to long to respond to /petitions in order to catch THAT cheater, so they have to catch the cheaters while it's actually happening some other time.

The mechanics of the strafing... When EQ was designed, the world was developed based on squares, each square being a set size, say 5 feet for each square. The distance that you can run was designed with the intent of running strait forward, so eash running step moved you 1 square, in this case 5 feet (further of course for jboots, sow, bard, etc), to get you to the next square. When you strafe, you are moving diaginal 1 square instead of strait 1 square. The problem is that when moving diaginal, you have to more 7 feet to get to that next square instead of the normal 5. This in itself does not seem like a problem, until you put it into a chase scenario. The person in front is strafe-running, so they are moving at 7 feet per step, but the mob chasing them is not smart enough to strafe, so he is stuck running at the normal 5 feet per step. Basically you are taking advantage of a speed quirk to get away from a mob. Falls into the same category as using a pathing issue to keep the mob from catching you. In PvP it works pretty much the same, although the other player may be smart enough to also strafe, it's still using a design bug to intentionally go faster then the designers wanted you to.

The GMs probably don't watch for it on the blue servers, because those servers don't have all of the same problems PvP do. So even though the GMs may know about the issue, they are not watching for it because they are not getting /petitions about it every 6 hours from some new person that was PKed using it.
