Glor, I considered length when I wrote the original post in response to the clueless on that thread. The problem with condensing further is that you leave stuff out.

Let's face it, shaman have a lot of problems--addressing a few would be balance. Addressing all of them would be overkill--ie, fix DoTs (properly)and nukes aren't as important. Fix slows and heals aren't quite as unbalanced. Fix heals and slows aren't quite as unbalanced. Etc.

Not to mention the fact that when we mention a problem, we have to document it with anecdotal evidence of some kind. We're working against a pretty skeptic crowd.

As to the solutions, that has it's own problems. Finding a middle ground where we can all agree on a suggested fix is gonna be hard. You'll note I shied away from making suggestions. Even if we do suggest fixes for all of the above, it runs into my earlier point--fixing all of them would be unbalancing, and making that many suggestions for improvements would make us look greedy.

In the end, I figured a description of the problems was sufficient for that forum. I haven't checked back in the last few hours, but last I did, I didn't see many people trying to offer counterarguments.

Splitzer
Here's an attempt at a basic outline of our current problems. No new ideas, just outlining what Chutoi's already said here (http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/Forum4/HTML/056591-9.html) with a bit of commentary inspired from other posts scattered around on the forum.

Melee


I personally have little to no desire to melee, but lots of us do. To that extent, we should at the least be able to keep pace with a cleric's melee abilities.


Nukes


As it stand right now, shamans are the only casting class lacking a 1000+ dmg nuke. When you take the long casting time built into our highest nuke, we're out performed by even a hybrid (rangers).

The resist rate on rains is completely out of line, to the point where they are unreliable at best as a source of damage.


Heals


We lack true battle heals. While our heals can help during downtime, they're no where near strong enough to help when facing mobs that can easily do 2,000 points of damage in a single round. This goes for all three of our top heals (Tnarg, Kragg, and Quies)

We lack a group heal. Every class with heals gets some form of group heal, with the exception of rangers and shamans. We are the only priest with no group heal of any kind.

We're out performed by a hybrid class yet again when comparing overall healing abilities. A paladin gets burst heals, group heals that heal almost as much as our single target, and a duration heal that's only 50hp less per tick than our best.


Pets


Every pet but ours can dual wield without having to be armed first.

Our pet does roughly 12 dps base. With full buffs and weapons, we can squeeze that up to about 38. For reference, that's about as much as our level 56 poison dot. Hardly enough to bother with.


DoTs


On paper, we can do massive damage with our DoTs. In game, that would get us summoned and killed (either by the mob of the tank out of frustration) in about 10 seconds.

There's an inordinate amount of agro generated by poison DoTs

Mobs generally don't live long enough for our DoTs (even Bane) to take full effect. Disease DoTs are useless for anything but soloing since raid targets (the only thing that lives that long) have a limited number of debuff slots.


Slows


Mitigation reduced the gap between enchanter, beastlord, and shaman slows to a point where any difference is negligable at best.

Our slowing process takes forever Mobs almost half dead before we are able to get them slowed since a slow casting debuff is required to land Turgur's reliably. Other classes get resist mods (bards), fast casting slows (beastlords), or fast casting debuffs (enchanters) to compensate for this.

Mitigation reduced the effectiveness of our slows to the point where extra healing is required. Our heals weren't increased in proportion.


Buffs


Current gear makes it easy to reach stat caps, rendering our massive selection of stat buffs obsolete.

We are towards the bottom end of buffing hit points, out performed by druids, clerics, even paladins and beastlords.

Our premier melee buffs (the Avatar line) is less desirable than the equivalent spell given to a shaman hybrid class (beastlord) due to the stamina, resists, and higher atk rating. It doesn't have the stacking problems that we encounter with Avatar either.


Mana Regen


Our comparative mana regen has been steadily decreased over the past several expansions.

Enchanters and Clerics (proposed for GoD) get a free bar of mana every now and then. The strength and variety of mana regen buffs, combined with the availability of flowing thought and mounts has steadily increased over the past several expansions. All of this reduces the gaps to the point where, although good, our regen isn't nearly as spectacular as it was.

Our cannibalize spell line takes precious time to work. Other classes get passive methods of mana regen.

Cannibilization alone doesn't create enough mana when only available every three minutes.

Between signifigantly higher spell costs and high resist rates, any advantage we have with mana regen is used up.


GoD Spells/AA


No one wants a new disease DoT for any purpose other than soloing, which has been discouraged more and more by current game design.

The new Cannibalize spell is LESS efficient than the one we currently have and amounts to roughly 1 mana more per tic.

No one will use the proposed slow in its current form. Recast is too long, duration is too short, resist mod isn't enough, or the slow percentage is too low. The only good part about it is the fast casting time.

20 AA points for something that should be patched in as a bug fix for free (pet buffing) is absurd.

DoT crits cost more than the equivalent AA for nukes, while delivering the same performance.

The pet AA's just suck in general. We're not a pet class.
