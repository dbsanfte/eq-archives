I want to be a battle priest.

For the first 2-3 years of this game, that's what we were. Our damage was found in the strength of our arm and the power of our dots. But it was also amplified by the melee of our group, because our buffs magnified their dps, making them stronger. We weren't the strongest healers, but we could manage.

Today, it would be nice to see this type of priest reimplemented.
(1) I wouldn't ask for dual wield or double attack, that's a bit over the top. But give us bash. We melee'd more than clerics ever did, and it'd be extremely useful to the shaman who enjoy melee'ing today.
(2) Upgrade our buffs with real combat effects that melee actually want. By doing this, you upgrade shaman shaman dps indirectly, through the group we are apart of.
(3) Fix dots! Make their agro comparable to nukes, up their mana efficiency because they don't always last the full duration, make them resist on a per tick basis, fix itemization to make dot focus effects more available, and then give us comparable AA to crit like the nuking classes. Anything less is just wrong.
(4) Upgrade our debuffs. Malos does not need a long recast. Cripple is worthless. We are slotted to have the highest mana costing root with the lowest debuff of any class in the game. It's a single spell, but in this spell we see the injustices present in every other spell line. Just have to ask myself where all this hate is coming from...
(5) Why can hybrids outnuke us? Up velium strike's damage by 150 dmg, reduce it's cast time to 6.25 (same cast time as all other priestly nukes) and call it a day. There's simply no valid reason for why this would be overpowering.

The changes necessary to turn us back into the battle priest we knew and loved when we played this class are not daunting, overbalancing, nor are they even particularly hard to implement. They're what every shaman for the past year has been clamoring for, and it's simply intolerable that Sony thinks they can overlook this class's most fervent desire to fell useful and wanted once again.

I want DoTs redesigned to mesh with the modern game.

I want debuffs/buffs that mesh with the modern game.

I want debuffs/buffs based on the BoV/PoC concept that expand the utility for a well played shaman.

For example:

1) A BoV type slow with the following characteristics:
- relatively large damage buffer
- fast casting
- a small resist modifier
- slows by 10%
- is not affected by slow mitigation
- stacks on top of all other slows for a max of 75%

2) A BoV type slow with the following characteristics:
- very small damage buffer
- very fast casting
- no resist modifier
- slows by 40%
- is not affected by slow mitigation
- stacks on top of all other slows for a max of 95%

I would use the former in groups to make shaman superior to other slowers. It could be used in raids to shield tank switchs or in a chain to reduce healing requirements by a %.

I would use the latter as an alternative form of CC. Not as good as mezzing, but effective enough in most circumstances that a group can do CC content without a chanter. It wouldn't displace a chanter since the spell would complement a chanter if present, making CC that much more secure.

There are a lot of things that you can do with spells designed around the basic concept behind BoV and PoC. I'd like to see Sony show some imagination and revitalize buffing/debuffing classes that way.

Shamans serve as priests to the tribal races of Norrath, able to draw upon the power of the spirits and focus it for their own purposes. Shamans are primarily a magic-using class, able to wear chain armor and wield blunt weapons and spears.

Shamanic magic has two main focuses - enhancing the mind and body of the shaman's allies, and weakening or damaging their enemies. A shaman's enhancement spells are always in demand, massively improving the strength, stamina, health, and other physical aspects of the shaman's group. Shamans also are able to heal their allies and have excellent health regeneration spells.

The weakening and damaging spells in the shaman's arsenal are tremendously effective in battle. The shaman attacks his enemies with diseases and poisons that cause massive damage over time, while using cold and ice magic to inflict damage directly. A brutal line of crippling spells can slow a monster's attacks, hamper their resistance to spells, and lower their physical prowess.

Shamans are a well-rounded class with strong abilities in both group and solo situations. An experienced shaman can turn the tide of any battle, bolstering their allies while weakening their enemies significantly. The shaman is an excellent class for players who enjoy working in groups
