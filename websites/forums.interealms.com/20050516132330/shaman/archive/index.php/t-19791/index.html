Rascalan
Just wanted to give you a method we use for raiding with multiple shaman.

The obvious reason for assigning duties is we have so many roles in raids. I hate switching out multiple gem slots just to buff one or two people. Sometimes the same shaman will get overwhelmed.

We try to assign groups for differant shaman. In the buffing role we will usually have 6 shaman on a raid. 3 will be assigned to groups for buffing roles.

For example if we have 6 groups shaman 1 will be assigned groups 1-2, shaman 2 groups 3-4 and shaman 3 groups 5-6. No matter who dies or asks for buffs we have the assigned shaman take care of it. We also though require only group leaders to send tells for buffs. This limits the amount of tells.

Those shaman basically that are buffing are back up heals as well so the gem slots are buffs and heals.

The other shaman are overlapping debuffs and offense. They usually are casting Malos, MalosiXXX, and slows. They will also RGC, and do cures based on mobs we are doing.

The back up buffers are usually assigned to either a caster group for heals. The offensive and debuffers assigned to heavy melee groups. They would also FA a group of rangers and or rogues for instance. We also assign out a specific shaman to disease slows only. We will often cast off the disease slow and tugurs at the same time. Just in case the mob is not really liking regular slow. Especially if its a new encounter.

It is really as creative as you wanna be with the raid leading. The targets are preassigned and we usually have very little overlap. We try to switch it up so people aren't always stuck slowing or healing. All and all it makes for good raiding and accountablility.

All of this is always decided and done in a shaman channel so we can keep track of what is going on. We try to limit the MGB and always have a back up with at least two who have it up in case we wipe or have to redo annulled buffs.

People know thier roles and follow in suite with what is needed.

Tyck
The tactics you're going to use will be so situational that its hard to say that any one way is the one true way.

Here are some of the situations that we've gotten so used to that we usually just 'deal with it' without going into debates in the channel anymore.

1. High turnover encounters.

Some encounters (paticularly planar) can have a very fast turn over of secondary people. They die like flies because of adds, AoE's, curses, what have you. We usually assign 1 standing singleton buff for each shaman. Focus, STA being the two that are MOST needed, followed by IOS (for the clerics) and then AGL, and Diaku. As people get rezzed in each shaman drops his buff on him.

2. 1001 adds encounters

There are many encounters where no matter how good the pullers are you have adds. Maybe dispite their efforts or because they are trying to pull as many as they can to 'beat the clock'. For these encounters we use a free for all system. Each shaman is going to debuf each-an-every mob that he can. Yes, there is double debuffing. But usually it is kept to a minimum because as we debuff we call it in channel. Better too much debuffing than a full speed mob cutting down its add tank and chowing down on the clerics.

3. Boss Mobs

Boss mobs that can be slowed... must be slowed. By hook or by crook. It doesn't matter if you have 2 or 2 dozen shamans... Somebody has to get that slow in. We typically have 2 shamans who have max spell hast casting Malo at the open (just because it does fizzle way too often) while everybody else is pounding slow. Called in channel we sometimes see multiple successes... but if the mob is slowed... its always a good day. And dont forget the Cripple chaser. You didn't need those Hitpoints anyways.

4. Backup healer

Never... ever.. let your shaman be the only healer in a raid group. It completely negates his debuffing ability. He cannot debuf if he cant take a few thousand points of damage and a Rampage or two.
