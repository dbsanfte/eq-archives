Elured
Coats do not stack, though higher level Coats will overwrite lower level ones. That is why you could not have Barbcoat and Thistlecoat up at the same time.

Thistlecoat (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=516&source=Live) is 4-6AC in Slot 2, and 1DS in Slot 3.

Barbcoat (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=516&source=Live) is 11-12AC in Slot 2, and 2DS in Slot 3.

Shield of Thistles (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=256&source=Live) is 5-6DS in Slot 1.

If a spell has the same basic effect (DS, AC, HP, Haste, etc.) in the same slot as another spell, those effects will not stack.

Also, there are some special stacking rules. For instance, Strength of Tunare, for some unknown reason, prevents the Cleric spell Divine Barrier from landing (which usually ends in a dead cleric).

Additionally, Strength of Tunare (and it's predecessor, Strength of Nature), have "special" stacking slots. In Slot 2 for Strength of Tunare is "Stacking1 v2 204: Hitpoints(400)" which is *supposed* to prevent Brell's Mountainous Barrier from landing, amongst other things. However, for whatever reason, this does not work properly. Strength of Nature, on the other hand, works properly in this regard and is one of the main reasons that these special stacking codes were put in place: Lots of rangers getting fed up with losing Strength of Nature and it's combo-upgrade, Warder's Protection, to a piddly little 150HP "upgrade" from a passing paladin.
