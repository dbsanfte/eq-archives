Weyalan
Duelling is not really something you should ask Red Server Shaman about - we don't in general, duel a lot. Duels are so formal, and stifle creativity. Many duels require no buffs before duel starts, but no shaman on a PvP server ever goes into combat without buffs on - just like no melee class would go around without armor / weapons. Many duels are fought in a predetermined small area - this does not suit a shaman who generally has a movement speed advantage over most classes. Without resorting to lame tactics (training mobs, for example), a freeform PvP has a lot more leeway for creativity

Here are a few things to think about.

1. Instant Click items:
Things like SoW booties, Grim Aura Earring etc. If you are allowed to pre-buff for the duel, a caster will probably try to strip your buffs with cancel/annul/pillage etc...Using insta click buffs allows you to protect your important buffs. More insta clickers = better. Make hotkeys for each one.

2. Buff management:
You want to try to have your first couple of buff slots free so that any nasty spells that stick to you can be cancelled (or pumiced) without having to cancel your useful buffs.

3. Potions:
Potions like damage shields, resist potions, stinging wort potions can give you a significant edge in PvP. I would have the high level DS potion as a bare minimum against any melee class (and agains a monk, they own)

4. Spell slots
Free up as many slots as possible by having useful clickable items. For example, earring of cleansing, earring of purity for cure disease / poison. The more spell slots you can have for other spells the better. Even consider getting the neck that summons a pet

5. Nasty items:
Snare is a winner in PvP if you can land it - the easiest clicky snare item is the webs from crystal cavern - head there and get a few. Farm the Divine Aura item that drops from the boss orc in Highpass Hold

6. Mana conservation.
Against most casters, he whos mana lasts longest wins (not true against necros, but not a bad rule of thumb). Remember that it takes much less mana to heal/cure/remove a spell than it costs to cast that spell. Shaman are not particularly good at handing out massive damage, but we can win by outlasting. Concentrate on keeping yourself healed and cure all DoTs etc, and you can be sure that you are burning up less mana than your opponent. Once a caster's mana is gone, they are easy meat.

7. Know your enemy.
Tactics that will work against a monk might be useless against a SK, etc. Against any caster aspecially snaring caster, get your resists up the wazoo (use potions to raise resists more). Dont try to toe-to-toe melee a melee class. also, as a general rule, if you arent actually casting a spell, do not EVER stop moving. Try to get competent using 3rd person view if at all possible because it will help you against nimble opponents. Know that a rogue is pretty useless if you can get your back protected by a corner or wall. A monk is much less dangerous if he cannot knock you back to interrupt your spells.


8. Strafing
In its simplest form, strafing is simply using the Ctrl control button and left or right arrow keys with autorun on, which makes you run diagonally and sideways at the same time. "Circle strafing" is more difficult, but useful. using the mouse to pan one way while using the arrow and control kets to move sideways in the other direction will make you move in a circle facing inwards. So you can circle strafe from an opponent's frant to their back without turning your back on them. Its tricky, so practice it.

9. Use the terrain
If it is a steep terrrain, levitation and runing around can hurt a non-levitated player badly

running at full tilt up a hill and making a sharp turn can cause a "ghost" imagewarp that makes it look as though you are still running up the hill whereas you are actually not - this can buy you time to get a spell off, or can just be useful in shaking an opponent off allowing you to change spells, cast slow heals etc.

10. Practice makes perfect.
PvP is so different from PvE. Try to practice as much as possible. Go to an arena with friends and PvP against each other again and again and again...just because you get your ass handed to you the first time doesnt mean you arent gonna be able to kick their ass after the 20th time.

I could write heaps more, but there are a few things for you to think about.

Here's a couple tips I guess just throwing stuff together in no particular order:

Always malos first.
Never use disease dots in PVP. DR debuffing takes an extra spell slot, and the dots themselves are slow (129/tick for BOU vs 320/tick for BOS). See above, they're already malosed so PR is down... (note: I do not have VP, I really have no experience using it. You may need a DR debuff to use it effectively, I have no idea - I'd still recommend against using DR dots though)
Stack multiple PR dots on melees. If you can land the big 3 - Blood of Saryrn(320/tick), Scourge of Nife(254/tick), Bane of Nife(214/tick) - well you're doing 788 damage/tick which is immense.
If you do land PR dots on someone, and see them going to cure them (a melee stops to cast) get in their face and do your best to not let them channel.
Don't waste time with cripple. The effect is negligable on melees (most good ones have max strength without avatar, let alone with it - not so high for dex/agi but you get the picture).
Don't waste time using your epic. 10 second cast time is immense - against a melee you will likely get interrupted regardless of your AC/channeling AAs.
Spiritcall is the caster killer. If you manage to root a caster, and get all 5 pets engaged on him, he/she is very likely going to die. It is almost impossible to channel vs max spiritcall - this includes heals so use it to kill healers.
Kind of an extension of above, but against healing classes you kind of need to wear them down to the point where you can root/spiritcall them, and then do enough damage in 1 minute to finish the kill. It can be tricky. The only time I bust out a cold nuke or rain spell in PVP is when trying to finish a healer under these circumstances.
Keep Tnarg's memmed, not just torpor/Q. If you don't, then any opponent that can maintain more damage than 300 per tick will kill you eventually.

Some good info so far. Here is my basic pvp summary.

For 1 on 1 pvp I keep the following spells memmed. I know most of my enemies so strategies vary depending on a persons skill, class or gear. (Skilled enemies that I know have high resists will often have me using pumice before I start casting on them.) In mass PVP I don't dot so my enchanters can mezz people.

Tnargs
Quiessence
Cani
Paralyzing Earth
Malicious Decay
Malos
Scourge
Bane (will substitute Blood when I get it)

I lead with malos and malicious decay. Then I VP them or root them if they still resist VP. Once rooted I send the dogs. If it's a melee type I un-mem malicios decay real fast and mem cloud of grummus (lands a lot more often than turgurs in pvp) and slow them. After I slow a melee I get both dots on them and go in and usually melee. If it's a caster type I like to use Rabid Bear at this point. I can proc my mace a lot in a fight and usually kill them off before they kill me.

I used to keep my top spots open to dispell dots, but I don't go that route anymore. RC works much better. I keep 2 insta clicks in my top 2 spots and I make sure to keep them up when I get dispelled. Sow boots and AoN are perfect here. Skeletons are harder to target so this piece of gear was made for pvp.

Bards = Unless they are AFK don't even bother. I hate them with a passion.

SK = A well geared SK will usually tear a shaman up 1 on 1. They only need to get you to 75% before they click that magic button. SK in my guild whacks me fast and furious for 500+ a hit. Life taps are unresistable I think.

Wiz = If you can keep yourself healed while they try to get you below 75% you have a chance. Keep Q on and try to get them rooted. Send dogs and dot them. Go Rabid Bear and beat them fast.

Necro = You can dispell lifeburn.

Paladins = I've tried to kill some of the paladins in my guild so many times I've lost count. (before we were guildmates) I think they are unkillable. A couple of them always run around with up to 10k on them at all times as a prize if someone actually does kill them. They just don't die. Lesser geared paladins I can actually kill. Takes a long time though and their stuns make it difficult.

Shaman = I once fought another shaman for over an hour. Another time I died right fast because I accidentally un-memmed malos instead of another spell.

Clerics = Annoying to fight. They can't usually kill me, but they are hard to kill themselves.

Rangers = Since the ranger nerf, they are now 10 times easier to kill. VP and disease slow them and it's all downhill from there. Unless there is water in the zone, in which case the few annoying rangers I fight will swap out their scuba gear and play the water game with you for hours.


Other Melee = Malos, malicious decay, cloud of grumus, dot, melee, keep rooted while you canni and heal and loot their corpse when they are dead.

Have fun. PvP is what keeps the game exciting for me.
