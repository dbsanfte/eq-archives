How am I supposed to win a PvP encounter without a dot? Burst of Flame? I guess I could win with tanking and torpor... or talk whoever I'm fighting to fight with one hand behind their back.

1) Why can't a DoT's "source" be marked when it is cast? Something as simple as "from player" and "from mob" should be enough to fix this problem. I'm sure this has been discussed previously, but why has it not been implemented?

There is nothing there that can possibly kill you other than other players. Why not make it a "no exp loss" zone? Basically, if you are in The Arena and you die, it was probably from a player. Of course, if they implemented item #1 this would not be an issue.

Bodabag,

My understanding is that there are a few different scenarios where dots will cause exp death.

1) In a duel. I dot someone in a duel. He kills me, so the duel is over. A short while later, he dies from the dot. The game doesn't see that he was dueling any more, so assumes that he was killed by a mob and gives him a real exp death.

2) In the arena. I dot someone in the arena. He runs out of the arena with the dot still on him. He keels over and dies. The game doesn't know why he died, since he was not in the arena, so it assumes that it was from a mob, so it gives him an exp death.

I'm not sure if dying in the arena from a dot of a person who is no longer around (like #1) will cause exp loss too. I think so, but Henora says no. I'm don't know.

I think there are other variations on this theme, but in general the idea is that the person on the receiving end of the dot is no longer fighting the guy doing the dot, so the code doesn't know that it was a duel. If I dotted someone and killed him while I we were both still "fighting", I think he does not take an exp loss.

Or at least that's how I understand it.
