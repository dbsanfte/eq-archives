Trevurg
I'm also primarily a single-group shaman, and often the only healer. Here's my thoughts:

(We'll assume the traditional MC3 / Cann V):

Radiant Cure.
-----
You need to be able to cure maladies, quick, without targetting or caring too much about what they are.

Virulent Paralysis
Spirit Call
-----
Your best crowd control abilities. VP will let you put an add aside to deal with later, Spirit Call will do the same thing, add a hell of a lot of push, make life really hard for casters, and do 4-5k damage over 60 seconds. All for no mana. :)

SCR/SCRM
-----
Buff less often. If you're the primary healer, buffs are something you want to do as seldom as possible. Also makes Torpor last longer so you can bring yourself back after Cann V for less mana.

Healing Gift
-----
I generally try to heal at about 50%. This puts the tank up to 80% or so. If I get a crit heal, it puts them to full health. Also, crit heals are fun. Fun is important.

SCM
-----
Haven't gotten this far yet myself. Do more stuff with less mana. Sounds good to me.

Healing Adept
-----
Heal a little better with every direct heal spell cast. Percentage based, so effect not incredible considering our heal not incredible to start with. Still probably worth buying.

Not sure where I'm going after that. :)

It's both actually, at least IMHO.

It's not so much how much mana you have, as it is how quick you can regenerate mana back. It reduces downtime considerably. Two nights ago in PoV at Golems, we took a med break after a druid healer and myself were both at 20% after a bad pull. I Cannied back up to 100% with the help of my drogmor and stoicism, while the druid on the horse only managed to get back to 40% herself.

Likely the druid has more mana than myself. At 57 I only have 189 WIS, while she likely had somewere around 250 (didn't think to ask at the time). I figured however I had just over 4k mana total, and while I was ready to pull, we were still waiting on the druid to med up some more. So I rebuffed and Cannibalized to full once more by the time she was ready again.

So more HPs help there when it comes to Cannibalization.

However I feel HPs are more important in staying alive. Slowing, especially when resisted, generates HELLUA Aggro. Which means you're going to get beat on. If you want to stay alive long enough for the MA to get that MOB off you, you need the HPs to take a beat down. Those same HPs just also come in handy when it comes to Cannibalizing back your mana afterwards.

So HPs serve something of a dual purpose, both of which are very important.

Not to derail the thread but you couldn't have been more wrong in that bit if you tried.

SCM is a 10% mana savings on everything and is constant. There is no check to see if specialization goes off or not. The description of the aa for scm is compleately wrong.

Your boots on the other hand and all mana pres items are a random percent up to the max, in your case 15%.

Assuming you've got spec alteration as your main one you save ~22% from that and scm and then another ~8% from your boots on average so you end up saving ~30% on average.

The numbers are whats been pulled out of eq by programs like showeq which I have linked to in the past but i don't have infront of me right now. Could probably find them on the forums tho in the last thread about scm.

As far as the healing aa's go I've gotten myself up to a fair number of aa's now (260 and climbing) and I have enjoyed the healing aa's. Q is great for the efficiency with scrm and my crown but it's very slow. Tnargs with maxed healing aa and a Heal IV is useful especially on a crit. I wouldn't put them in my first 100's but it definitely has made me alot more viable as a healer. I've even had a few lucky crits on raids that have probably saved the mt's.

As far as difference in aa/focus effects for shaman and thier roles I see 2 main areas where I'd see some difference between them.
On the aa side of things the obvious one is MGB. I find it very handy for shaman on a raid but obviously fairly useless in soloing or standard groups. Most other aa's hve good uses in both situations. Any buff related aa's work on torpor/q and that alone makes them worth while even while soloing for alot of people. ND/LR we get hit raiding or grouping or soloing so useful for all of the above. Healing aa's are far enough down the list on any of them that by that point it doesn't really matter.

As far as gear the main difference I'd see would be soloing vs anything else. Obviously soloing your gonna want the afliction lines, Burning affliction, affliction efficiency etc since those are gonna be majorly used spells soloing. In raids or groups your gonna tend towards the more general mana pres, buff extension and maybe healing or improved dmg focus effects.
