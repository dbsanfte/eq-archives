Sithrax
This kept coming up on the "Skrew shammys" thread, so I thought I would clarify things in a new thread.

People keep stating that beastlords buff better than shaman. I think there is some truth to this argument, but there are situations in which a shaman is a better choice.

(All spells listed as if cast by a 65.)

Shaman uses:

Focus of Soul/7th = 544 hp and 70 str/dex
Talisman of Boar = 60 STA
Spirit of Bih'li (outdoor only) = SoW, +15 ATK
Swift Like The Wind = 60% haste (or ToA, 50% group haste)
Ferine Avatar = 140 ATK/STR/DEX/AGI
Talisman of the Diaku = 45 STR, 35 DEX


Beastlord uses:

Spiritual Vigor = 225 hp and 40 ATK
Talisman of Kragg = 395 hp
Infusion of Spirit = 50 STR, 55 DEX, 45 STA
Spiritual Dominion = 9 hp/mana per tick
Celerity = 50% haste
Ferocity = 40 STA, 150 ATK, 65 MR/FR/CR/DR/PR


Please note that Talisman of Kragg and Spiritual Vigor STACK. That is why BLs often prefer their buffs (Kragg/SV/IoS) to Focus - the 40 ATK and 76 additional HP are really much better than 20 STR and 15 DEX you would get from Focus. Ferocity and Ferine Avatar stack, but clearly Ferocity is better. We have no analog to SD, but it's obviously a great buff.

Some things to consider:

1) SV doesn't stack with the paladin Brell's line, so when pally buffs are available, Focus is a better choice for max HP.

2) All those buffs are pretty mana intensive for BLs, who generally have smaller mana pools than shaman and do not have access to the cannibalize line.

3) The beastlord buff series takes up 2 buff slots (SV + Kragg) vs. 1 for shaman (Focus).

4) Shaman have the SCR/SCRM AAs, while BLs do not. This makes it possible for us to keep FA on a lot more people than a BL can keep Ferocity on.


Personally, I think it's inappropriate that a hybrid can outbuff us in so many situations, but it's not the biggest issue we have right now. We haven't been the "King of Buffs" for a long time, unless you count stat buffs.
