Not including Affliction efficency III here, because I don't know those figures:

A pox/plague combo costs 730 mana for 3305 damage.
A bane/eb combo costs 745 mana for 2780 damage.

Difference: 15 mana more and 525 damage less.

Since pox wears off close to or just before the shroom dies, one can assume that most of that damage is delt, with a little bit of plague's damage remaining to be delt. Certainly not 500 of it... so that extra 500 damage must be made up somewhere, through jbb or having the dog chew on it.

Torpor makes a big difference in this camp; but kraggs is better than nothing. I did this when I had torpor, but no other AA's besides the minimum leading to canni 5.

What I did, to earn canni 5, the safest way--you might have to adapt without torpor-- is pull with slow. The shroom starts casting on me, which makes doggy run to him. If the shroom is still in the patch, I'm screwed; suddenly six shrooms are on dog, then on me. So I park dog a ways away from the patch.

Anyway, on incoming, I cast epic. The shroom screwing around casting spells usually gives me enough time; I get many more interrupts when he's actually stopped casting and finally run up to me.

Then I cast pox and plague. I canni and get a bit of mana back. When mob is at half health, I pull another. Dog will generally stay on the first one and finish it. When the second one is half health, I pull another. I JBB and canni whenever I can, just using my judgment. Torpor(kraggs, whatever) comes in when necessary.

When I got in my rhythm, I would generally keep three on me at once. It takes a good bit more concentration in the ghetto gear I had -- 2400 fully self-buffed. Still, I have done four and even five at that level before, and at one point remember doing seven when I had an insanely messed up train from some noob ranger running around aggroing junk.

Anything over three takes a great deal of concentration in comparison.

The reason for doing multiples, as someone indicated, is that your heals can be fast enough to keep up with the damage of multiple shrooms unless they get multiple 200's on you with their spells -- by the way, they shroom a LOT more than they used to, by far. The camp has become far more difficult lately.

Another reason, though, is that you want to keep those spots empty and working on their respawn. Then go pull some other patch maybe. :) Sometimes it's worth putting a real strain on yourself and killing at a pace that is uncomfortable for you if it means you'll get the maximum number of mobs that are around and eliminate downtime. FG can be crowded at times, but often there is a patch here or there that is unmolested. The faster you kill your mobs, the quicker you can go get the extras before others kill THEIR mobs. Better that you're busting your own chops should give you a shot at extra mobs and exp/hour than that you take it easy and sit around with nothing to do.
