Long time lurker, first time poster.

I've been reading every thread on this board for over a year now. And to be honest, I'm somewhat disappointed by the conclusions that some of you have reached about the state of the shaman. Oh, I agree that the class is essentially broke in GoD, but I don't agree that healing is the answer.

Improved healing, seems to me to be duct tape on a broken system.

Let me lay out my thinking, and perhaps you'll see my point.

Shaman have two classes that can basically fill our role. Beast and Enc. An Enchanter brings the following to the table:
Slow, buffs, crowd control, and excellent (boarding on overpowered) charm dps.

A beast brings slow, buffs, and average to slightly above average dps.

As we all agree, slow is nerfed. Its a moot point in GoD. Higher dps+large heals will kill mobs. So the enchanter, beastlord, and shaman have slow removed from the debate table. Just forget about it.

Whats left? Well, for the enc we have buffs, cc, and huge pet dps. For the beast we have buffs and melee+pet dps. For the shaman, we have buffs and healing. I agree, shaman dps is useless. In good bot groups dropping mobs in 20 seconds, its always been useless.

I can understand the next logical step here. Make our heals more effective. But I don't understand the path taken to achieve that end (more healing). Our buffs and dps are pointless and non-existent for ele geared players and fast encounters, respectively. So, since we can't slow, can't damage, and can't really buff, we have healing left... so.. more healing, right?. Well, if I wanted to be used mainly as a healer, I'd have played a cleric.

Why aren't we clamoring for buffs? They too have become useless, just like our healing. Why aren't we clamoring for dps? It too, has become useless.

Well, I can answer the later. Adding significant dps to a shaman would probably overpower them. You'd be a one man killing machine in any older content. But buffs? Our role is a support class. So let us support the other classes.

My suggestion would be to stop clamoring for heals and start clamoring for buffs that make a difference.

An enchanter brings mana, cc, and huge dps to a group via charm. Why can't we bring minor healing and buffs that mimic the results of the chanter's and beast's dps and buffs?

By that, I mean, why aren't we asking for buffs/AA's that provide uses to players that A) need dps B) need healing and C) already have maxed physical stats?

Why aren't we asking for focus effect buffs? In the same way that a war can activate defensive disc to reduce damage taken, why don't we ask for an AA or a spell on a long timer that makes all party members (or just the MT) take 30% less damage for a brief period? That doesn't over power us, but it allows us a place in high end encounters that are over in 1 minute or less.

Or, why aren't we asking for long term buffs that increase the effectiveness of groups? Like, a long term attack increase buff like FA, but that affects things that can't be touched by gear? God-Like gear has made our physical stat buffs worthless in the end game, so why not let us buff chance to hit, chance to riptose, chance to dodge, 1hs, 2hs, backstab, etc...

Give us a way to support the other classes, that adds overall dps again like STR buffs used to. Give us short time burst dps via FA like spells that can compete with a chanters charmed pet dps. Thats not overpowered. A chanter in bot has her pet forever if DC'd. That pet probably increased the groups dps by 25% (total guess), so why not give shaman buffs that increase chance to hit and weapon skill to the point that the groups overall dps increases 25%?

Balance us in the areas that used to mean something: buffs and dps, by bringing the two concepts back together again. Make buffs actually add overall dps, and then the shaman will find his role again.

In the same token, I'd rather see our heals balanced by a new line of defensive buffs (chance to dodge, chance to riptose, short term damage reducing shields, etc..) than just straight up increasing healing.

The shaman for me was always a balance between nerfing the mob, strengthening the player, and damaging the mob. Over time our ability to both strengthen the player, and damage the mob, has disappeared, and we (Enc, Bst, and Shm alike) are all feeling the full effect of no mob nerfs in GoD. Give us back the ability to strengthen the player AND damage the mob, but by introducing spells that do both. Buffs that truly add pure dps to a group of ele geared players, and buffs that truly reduce mob damage, not by nerfing the mob, but by toughening the player.

In that way, I think we could have a system that allows the shaman to remain the diverse, fun class that it was. Attempting to address our current useless state by making us clerics isn't going to cut it.

Going down the aisle...

BUFFS:

Let these once again be THE Shaman staple. A Shaman was the guy who strengthened his tribe through channeling the spirits of gods / ancestors. He would improve their skills, their luck, their fortune through his prayer and sacrifice. So, with this being a large part of shamanism, what does it give the game?

Clerics are the big HP buffers, right? With htem, the tanks can take a heck of a beating. With Shaman, we should be the avoidance / mitigation buffers. Let Druids fall between us with their HP + AC buffs. But for real, many stories of a shaman infusing his warriors so that arrows always miss, so that spears and swords bounce off. Give us hefty, meaningful AC and defensive buffs. +% (or raw skill) to dodge, block, parry, defense, Rune-type spells every couple of spell levels, Spell / damage sheilding, to absorb a set amount of damage. Keep resist buffs, but make ours better than the other casters. We're the only priest that gets all five resists as a package, might as well capitalize on that, as well.

Yes, folks, my vision of the Shaman is to make them mitigation kings, rather than healers or slowers. Not saying that we should be able to make anything invulnerable, but we should have a noticable impact on damage taken.

In addition, give Shaman the Talisman spells. Raw, stackable HP's. Doesn't even have to be as many as it is now... i'd simply like to see my finger-wrigglers live longer

Of course, ya gotta get your tribe ready for war. To this end, I would toss on skill buffs / mods for Attack rating, Offense, and weapon skills. Either have fewer, or have them be not as good as defensive mods - After all, Shaman ARE defensive.

And then, lastly, we have our "general" buffs. Your lev / EB, UV, maybe even some low-scale proc buffs (Would actually see these as better suited for enchanters). Another thing the chanters can keep - Haste. Screw haste. Maybe give us some stacking haste at high levels, if any at all.

And folks - Notice, no stat buffs. Unless they wanna revamp the entire stat system, it's all just green eye candy. Even newbie quest gear is better than what most high-up folks were wearing back in the start of the game.

Moving on...
HEALS

Ooooh, the big one. Well, there's no question that shaman were healers. Big thing is, rather than simply pray, or administer medicine, they would do both - The medicine is given as a form of prayer, and then the prayer empowers the medicine to do its work. Weird, primitive, and here's the odd thing, it seems to actually work better than just tossing drugs into someone. Anyway...

Shaman shouldn't have big heals. They should have meaty, fast heals, however. At LEAST let us have an edge over Paladins in the heal department. In addition, the regeneration line is very appropriate, as is the Torpor line.

An alteration we could do, ties to the "resist mastery" thing, as well as how we already have the best cures. make shaman cures lingering. They do their job, one hit removes however counters, but it stays there like a HoT, healing a small amount of damage and providing a nice resist mod for it's cure type. And, ending this - Do something to detrimental magic spells so that we can cure them without stripping off beneficial buffs? Heck, if it has to be an archetype AA, so be it, it's just a gripe of mine.

SPELL DAMAGE

Okay, we have two kinds here, DoTs and DD. First the DoTs.

Shaman does not equate voodoo witch doctor (though voodoo witch doctor can equal shaman.) Your classic shaman isn't going to want to poison the soul and body, even of an enemy with his divine Gifts. oh, he'll use them against his enemies, more on that later...

Now, though, I can see SOME DoT capability. Honestly, Magic and Disease make the most sense. Unfortunately, in practice, both are fairly useless. Scrap the DD component fully, give us swift damage and low resists, maybe as many DoTs as a druid gets, alternating between disease and magic, with Magic-based coming out on top.

For direct damage, it actually makes more sense than DoTs. A shaman calling the wrath of his spirit allies on a foe (or even an ally as a "ZAP! see, that was stupid" kind of thing) works just fine. I can live with our current DD's, except for two things - One, they're almost entirely cold-based. Mix in some Fire and Magic, make us kinda wizzy-priests, with way less damage in our nukes. Two - Their cast times suck. Can't think of shamanic reasoning why they shouldn't be slow, but I can think of a million game reasons why our fast-casting DoTs are often better than slow-casting low-power nukes...
