As far as why is nerfing KEI on low levels a good idea... Despite what you may think, there IS a level of competition in EQ no matter who or how you play. We all play with the same limited content and that by itself forces some level of competition for spawns and xp camps whether you are a solo player or raiding with your guild.

With the recent easing of the levelling treadmill, there has been a rather massive influx of higher level players and due to some pretty poor design choices, the areas where those high level players can hunt for xp or loot has shrunk. Put two and two together and you have some massive competition for xp spots at high levels while a TON of zones designed for lower level players go utterly ignored. How many people that started playing EQ within the last 6 months or so even know what Dalnir's Crypt looks like? Kaesora? Splitpaw?

The argument that many newer players aren't as good as the old school players stems from this as well. Those dungeons were once good spots to xp as well as taught you many valuable lessons about your class and how to best work with others in difficult situations while you levelled up.

There's a problem though, at least in the short term. There simply aren't nearly as many low level players out there to populate all those areas and make them viable zones that you can go LFG and pickup a group for xp, at least not now. The KEI nerf is a good step in the right direction as well as the increase to group xp. Now the final step, IMO, would be to adjust the xp mods in those difficult dungeons compared to the stupidly easy spots to balance the risk vs. reward. Another good idea IMO would be to implement Graveyards in some of those tough lower level dungeons to remove what has always been probably the biggest deterrent to xp'ing in those areas.
