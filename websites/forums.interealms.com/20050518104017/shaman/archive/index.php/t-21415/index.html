Recently, the EverQuest II website (http://www.everquest2.com/) was updated with new spell information. Some creative ideas are at work. What is interesting is that more than one type of priest can cast resurrection (Legacy of the Eidolon) and that all priests will be able to mitigate damage, a buff castable on a playing character (Ethereal Ward), with the shaman class having ability to buff all members of the group with damage mitigation buff (Umbral Coterie). Effectively, the shaman class has been converted into a combat buffing class with the ability to resurrect members of a party. I suppose that instead of slowing a NPC, the shaman shall now choose two routes: (i) to become a caster of several damage mitigating battle buffs on a combatant, or to become a caster with ability to deal some formidable damage to a non-playing character. The Mystic class may receive ability to deal damage (which I believe is only proper). It remains to be seen how closely the EverQuest II shaman class will follow EverQuest I's shaman class.

The class is described as to "call upon the ancient spirits of the land, using protective wards and healing to invigorate their comrades. They remove afflictions from their companions and turn the vile magic back upon the enemy."

The Mystic class is described as those who "seek a symbiotic connection with the spirits of their ancestors, petitioning them to bestow the power to heal the injured, invigorate the weak, and enhance the capabilities of their allies when on the field of battle."

The Defiler class is described as those who "enslave the spirits of their ancestors, harvesting and corrupting their power to use against enemies. This stolen energy can also be used to heal and boost the capabilities of the defiler's allies."

Any shaman will be able to use a Spear, Flail, Hammer, Mace, Staff, Sling, or Thrown Weapon and wear medium Brigandine armor. While the defiler class key knowledge will be in Vehemence, the Mystic key knowledge will be in Fetishisms. Both will share Sphere of Power as their secondary key knowledge. Shamans also have a base knowledge of Talismans and Malisons. The Priest class overall has a key knowledge of Inspirations and Judgments.

I was a little worried at first when I heard that mitigation buffs would not be exclusive to the shaman class, but this allows the shaman to pick up a different niche. Maybe we might develop some interesting skills. Who knows, the shaman may still be the premere debuffer. Only time will tell.

Since I feel like rambling and commenting on this light/dark path discussion...

The Q'ero (Peruvian) Shamanic tradition instructs about three paths:

Campo Gandero, in the Uhu Pacha, the under (or inner) realm, the left side of the mesa, the path of the Magician;
Campo Medio in the Kay Pacha, the here/now realm, the center of the mesa, the path of the Shaman (or Healer); and
Campo Justiciero in the Hanaq Pacha, the above (or outter) realm, the right side of the mesa, the path of the Mystic.
Obviously, Shamanic teachings extol the virtues of the Middle Way, being present in the here and now, as opposed to being too focused on the inner or outer ways.

That being said, yes, anyone can choose to work for the good of the planet (Pacha Mama) or for more selfish goals - hence the "light" and "dark" connotations apply for those on any of the paths.
