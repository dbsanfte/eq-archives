Is this zone specific? Is this MOB specific?? Something you need to turn on somewhere??

Yes, some zones have a modifier and give better experience. At lower levels, dungeons typically give better experience than outdoor dungeons. Higher level mobs will give more experience. You can turn on alt experience by clicking the alt experience button in your inventory. You can then set the slider from 10% to 100% of your experience going towards AA experience (by factors of 10%). I believe you only have this option at level 50 when you can begin accruing AA experience.

Are different AA's better for different Races? Are they based on the type of player you are? Solo vs. Group? Root/rot vs. melee?

The beautiful thing about AA is it helps alleviate "cookie cutter shaman syndrome." Until we're all like Mudcrush and Ceno with 300+ AAs, folks are getting a chance to "specialize." You can maxx out your healing AAs and be a better healer, or maxx out your nuke AAs and nuke for more damage or maxx out your defensive AAs so you are more sturdy. So, yes, it is based on the type of player you are and whether you solo or group.

Most folks follow this AA path:

General - begins at level 51 (or 50?) - must put at least 6 in general before you can move on to arch abilities (6 points gives Baron/Baroness title). Most folks choose 3 runspeed 3 regen. All abilities are 1 point per level in this section.

Arch - begins at level 55 - must put at least 12 into arch before you can move on to class abilities (12 points in arch gives Venerable title). Almost EVERY shaman puts 12 into mental clarity since it's a prerequisite for the can 5 class ability (incredibly powerful mana regen). In the arch section, the levels are 2 points, 4 points, 6 points. So, to earn mental clarity 3 for example, you will spend 12 points...2 for level 1, 4 for level 2 and 6 for level 3.

Class - begins at level 59 - must put at least 24 points into class abilities to earn your Elder title. Nearly everyone goes for Can 5 first becuase it's just that good, it costs 5 points. The abilities here have random costs...5 for can 5 (prereq of mental clarity 3), 8 for Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery (prereq of spell casting reinforcement 3), 9 for mass group buff, etc.

PoP abilities - some fun stuff here, read the descriptions in the link I posted for more info

finaljustice
AA is simple and complex.... Until level 51, you've always had regular EXP at 100%. Mobs give EXP and AA EXP. These are two different pools, you can't get 100% of both of them. As you raise the percentage of one pool, you lower your percentage of the other. Therefore, if you want AA, it behooves you to go to a place where the mobs give better AA than they do regular EXP.

As you die, you lose EXP, Regular EXP only, not AA. Therefore, even when you decide to go hard for AA, you should keep at least 10% regular EXP going. And remember, AA's are to enhance your class, they aren't more important than levelling.

Talk of Can V can be dizzying, but don't let it divert you. Can V is a fantastic skill, but it only enhances a well-set up Shaman. Without Torpor/Quiescense, it's usefulness is diminished. If your HP's are low enough that every time you use it you get low health aggro, it's of limited usefulness.

You'll get it. You'll be a powerful Shaman, but not tomorrow. It'll take time, effort and knowledge.

This experience penalty applies whether you have exp set to regular or aaxp.
