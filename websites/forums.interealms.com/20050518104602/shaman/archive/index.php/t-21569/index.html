From my experiences, this is sometime caused by your anti virus software running in your background...most of them are set up to auto check anytime an application wants to access a folder...so when you command your comp to open a back pack and switch out your SOW booties for some beer, for example, and you get lag, its cause your antivirus is checking to see if EQ really has busisness accessing your EQ file folder....thus a touch of lag.
Either turn off your anti virus program while playing EQ or change some of the programming settings in it by telling it to not bother checking EQ's access; this is usually done by simply adding its folder to a "safe"list.

Weissblau, you can tell Zone Alarm that EQ changes frequently, and then it will not ask you to check the box again.

Right click on the zone alarm icon in the task bar.
select "restore zone alram pro control center"
select the program controll tab
scroll the list until you find EQGAME.EXE (I'm at work so I cant be sure but I think I'm right)
right click it and select the "changes frequently" option

Then so long as you give EQ access at least once it wont ask you after each patch.

I also do the same for the patcher files. Again I can't remember what they are called but I think it's _everquest.exe or something.

Zone alram is a great little firewall for gaming it blocks most crap, but is simple enough to get working with games.
