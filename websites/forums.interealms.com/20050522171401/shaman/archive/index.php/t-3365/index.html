Inqubus
If you want to be the most annoying class to play on a PvP server, choose bard. Hard as hell to kill, and hard to die by them, as they'll always just run away if you get a leg up on them, or it'll take them forever to whittle you down only to heal all the way back up. Rangers are pretty good at PvP if played right...hell, any class is good if played right. The only class I wouldn't play based on firsthand experience is a mage. If you do start a mage, stop around 20, as they are still high in the power curve PvP wise at that level against untwinked players. After that, it's just pure frustration. Took me 2 years to get a mage on VZ up to 35, took me about 22 days /played to get to 52 with my shaman. Survivability is key.
Alminah
The really good bards I've met says they need pretty good connection to be able to play real good. It's not possible to twist more than 3 songs and switch instruments back and forth at the same time if you lag or have a slow connection.

Also, they have all redesigned their keyboard and have stuff on the strangest places there... heh... so they can use the mouse for moving and looking around. Works very good for bards but I have no idea how it would work for a shaman if you plan on switching between the chars.

If you see no problems with this, I think bard is a good choice for PvP. They are very hard to kill for most everybody else, and they can do some pretty impressive stuff. It's also one of the few classes I've met running around solo out in enemy-land ;)


Rangers are very fun in PvP and tracking totally rules when others try to hide. Rangers become lethal with levels and archery in the high end game, well, the most common advice for others is 'Run!'


I'd say less downtime for a bard to level up with a rogue, but in the high end game both bards and rangers are two of the more lethal classes when played by a skilled player.

Shadowknights and paladins are both strong classes in PvP. At 60th, with DOT heals and your Selo's speed pet, you're virtually unkillable as a paladin, and as a shadowknight, you can lifetap to your heart's content and deliver a 1k+ dmg HT as the killing blow. Plus with epics and/or higher end weapons, you dish out massive damage in PvP.

Plus either class makes a good tank while the rogue is beating away from behind, when you're just leveling up.

I'd even suggest the SK/Paly duo instead of the rogue. A friend of mine (who plays a 59th level Cleric) and I made an SK and paladin twink, and we do pretty well as a team. We each dish out good damage, he has stopping power when people try to run, and my heals help when we're outnumbered.
