The mobs have about 4000hp each and you have 25 secs to drop each to make it through to the next round. Mages are gods of the exe trial and make sure you get you mage some star rubies (68pp) each to cast on the rocks.

Our typical group when getting through a friend would be a big tank that can keep agro. SK/PAL/WARRIOR, 62+ cleric with SE (due to is low agro on the spell), Mage, Shaman/Bard/Enchanter, Rogue and the person we want to get through.

Our shaman tactic is as follows. There are 4 mobs that pop. The intention is to use DS potion + Mage DS on the MA and 3 of the mobs beat on the MA (DS does wonderful damage). The shaman will slow one of the mobs off and solo it. The cleric will SE the MA to delay the first big heal bomb and hence try to avoid any agro.

This works rather well. As the other 5 finish off the 3 mobs, Shaman and alpha puppy beat on the 4th. Bane + Ebolt + Hasted pet with any 2 1hand weapons will drop a mob in the same time as the other 5 drop the 3 other mobs.

The key to any fight is to get rid of rocks ASAP. 68pp per banishment and loss of xp on that mob is well worth the effort. These are enchanter mob and they toss around slow like candy. It must be prevented.

We had 6 named pop on the way to exe one time and our cleric still finished the fight with 60% mana.

Dandi
I just finally made it this weekend, with 63 warrior, 61? mage, 61? cleric, 62 chanter, 61? monk, and myself at 61. Took us three tries, but the first was my VERY first trial attempt and I made some dumb mistakes. Second try the mage LD'd on the first wave, so we didn't even really try.

I didn't bother attempting to slow, Tigir's or otherwise. I blasted with spear of torment (you'd probably want ice strike rather than blast of venom, for mana conservation purposes) and tossed sup heal to help the cleric keep mana up. I topped off everyone's health between waves, to avoid healing agro, even though it sometimes meant doing my mana recovery during the actual waves. The mod rods were a BIG help. On the boss I landed slow first or second try after Malo, then epicced and settled in to heal, slinging just a few DD's during the whole fight. Cleric was using CH only, so I started tossing sup heal when the tank was half life to keep her alive until the next CH landed. It was close, I was OOM (after going OOM and canning enough for a couple more heals) by the end and the tank had only a couple bubs left, but we won. :) With the additional levels of most of your folks, I'd say you should be able to do it. Don't feel bad if it takes some tries to get the hang of it. It takes some practice to get the rhythm and the tactics down. And it's great exp in the meantime, so you're hardly wasting time even on failures.

If you're worried about resists on the final slow, you might want to invest in Mystical Aptitude potions that raise your effective casting level by 3, giving you the same chance of landing as a level 61. With the mage to Mala (or whatever their unresistable one is), you should have a good shot at it.

Edit: Forgot to add that our chanter was mezzing. Unless your paladin is really good at pulling stuff off fast, I don't think you or the cleric would survive the beating you'd take before the first couple mobs go down. I'm fairly well equipped but nearly died a couple times in our first attempt after I broke a mez (doh!) or took healing agro early on. If your cleric has to heal you from low health more than once or twice, he's not going to have enough mana for the final fight.
