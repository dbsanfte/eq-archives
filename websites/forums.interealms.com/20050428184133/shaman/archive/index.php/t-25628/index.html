Kryscha
I've done a fair bit of 2-boxing in WoW lately and it is a lot busier than in EQ. You can still be quite effective, but you really need to work on charcters that aren't both busy. I've tried to DW when in a group, and it is pretty much a waste of time. Because everything moves so quickly, you spend so much time ensuring that both characters are where they are supposed to be, that you barely contribute as well as if you were playing a single character.

Don't get me wrong, you *can* be effective in a group, but I would suggest two casters as they are much easier to control. (no melee chasing mobs for instance) On the other hand, if you DW only, without grouping with others, you can control the flow of things and become quite effective. The only thing I found is that instances are harder because of the 'twitchier' gameplay required in WoW, such as juggling healing/tanking/taunting etc. Get a few unexpected adds and things go downhill fast. However, as with everything, you get better with practise :)

For instance, I DW a warrior and priest, and I'm using that combo for a couple of reasons. First one, the priest isn't mine, but is my regular duo partner or DW char if they're not online. Secondly, the strong healing combined with strong tanking capability means that I can still be fairly effective even switching concentration between two characters. Initially when I started doing this, it felt much much more hectic than EQ, but over time I've got used to it and in controlled situations I can really go to town without even looking at the priest keyboard, just one hand on the numbers, nuking and dotting, and my concentration on my warrior's screen.

If you can team a more passive class with an active class it should be fairly easy; (ie rogue/priest) I haven't tried two active characters yet, but I'm sure it's doable, but may simply be less effective. (ie, missed openings etc)

I've also tried Paladin / Warlock and it is quite a powerful combo. (sorry all you RPers :D )

I can't really answer if shaman/hunter is a good combo as I haven't played either class above level 15 or so, and that was a while ago to boot.

Macheras
Beware the warrior/priest combo. Sounds great on paper, but be prepared for the priest tanking most of the time due to healing aggro and other high threat spells (dots, debuffs, etc.). And warriors are ill-suited for getting aggro back.

I don't want to get into the details, I've posted a couple other times about warrior's weaknesses. Some people think it's like EQ, but it's not. Taunt will last exactly 1 second and then the mob will go back to what it was doing, namely, beating on your priest. Some threat generating abilities exist but they are largely ineffective unless aggro is carefully managed.

That said, priests can actually tank quite well with Power Word: Shield and Renew. My wife (55 priest) has no problems fighting elite mobs her level or higher whereas my warrior runs like a schoolgirl (no offense to anyone!) unless the elite mob is 5-6 levels lower.

My two cents. :)
