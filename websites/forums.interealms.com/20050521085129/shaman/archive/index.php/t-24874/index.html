LaughingWoman
Levels: 7 on evil, 5 on good
Class: fighter (bruiser), scout (bard)
Server: Blackburrow
Enjoy: Sure, when the servers are up. ;)
Pros: Pretty, pretty pretty. The spell effects, the models (for the most part), the creatures (goblins are FANTASTIC). Lots of quests with worthwhile rewards (i.e. GEAR), and I'm kept pretty busy. The intro newbie island is great, and I hope there's a lot more like it in the rest of the game, tho I suspect that wont' be the case. STAY ON THE ISLAND. Don't be in a hurry to ring that bell. *grin*

Cons: Server performance and uptime (fer crap after the first day, when only preorders and people who had it shipped overnight were logged in).

Bugs - since 90% of the people play good, evil quests and content seem sparse and buggy as all get out. In general, there are a LOT of combat/ability bugs that should in no way still be in a live release. Track becomes bugged and stops bringing up the track list, and I don't think tracking works at all, since when I select something and hit track, nothing happens. Some of my characters tend to stop fighting and put away their weapons for no discernable reason in the middle of a fight. My scout has a tendency to turn her back on her target when I'm in 3rd person, and is unable to fight until I go back into 1st person to face the creature.

Jury's still out on the game itself. It's hard, though, not to compare it to WoW, which is 20x more complete and polished in open beta than this thing is at release. Not surprised, but disappointed.

Other than the bugs, they're vastly different games, and IF they get the bugs worked out, I'm likely to keep an active subscription to both, providing gameplay doesn't become significantly less enjoyable than it is at these low levels.

Sciek Spellbottler
14 ENC on Mistmoore, fairly fun so far but not without it's frustrations as well.

PROS:
-Looks are obviously a biggun if you have a system that can show it all.
-Gameplay is pretty good, fights and crafting are more interactive with the combat wheel and the little things that go wrong during crafting that you have to respond to.
-Levelling so far seems at about the right pace and if you don't want to just grind you can do quests which, if nothing else, diminishes the feeling that you're grinding.
-Tons of quests and a wide variety in types of quests that I've found.

CONS:
-Good/Evil split forcing players apart in a game that has no PVP doesn't make sense IMO.
-Crafting is frustrating as hell to get started in. Could use a lot more in-game explanation.
-Lack of good explanation of the combat wheel/HOs and how they work in game.
-Seem to be a lot more bugs in game still than WoW (nothing major that I've encountered, just not as polished)
-System requirements soo high.
