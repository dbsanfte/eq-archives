Track Scanning:

I have created a hotkey for myself that I think is quite valuable. It performs a macro that tracks, sorts to normal, then sorts to consider, then finally settlles on distance. I find it a great tool as the tracking window changes to the different sorting methods without pressing other buttons. I use it to Scan for rare mobs, or mobs that are above the average mobs in the zone. These are often the loot droppers that the raid or group is searching for.

The ScanTrack Hotkey:

line 1: /doability 3 (note: ability 3 is my tracking)
line 2: /pause 20, /tracksort normal
line 3: /pause 20, /tracksort consider
line 4 /tracksort distance


The result:

When I press the key, track window pops up and switches to /tracksort normal for the latest spawns. 2 seconds later the window resorts to consider to get the biggest mobs on top. 2 seconds later it resorts again to the distance sorting method. Of course if I spot something of interest as I scan, I can always refind that mob on my tracking list and go pull it for my group.

The limitations:

While this 6 second macro performs, you can not hit any other palletted hotkey and you cannot execute a slash command. However, keystroke shortcuts still work. During this 6 second macro, it is important to know that while the ORDER of the mobs changes on your list, newly spawned mobs during the macro are not added to the list until you press Track (or ScanTrack) again.
