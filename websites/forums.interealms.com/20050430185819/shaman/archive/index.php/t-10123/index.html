If you want to stay in the old world, East Karanas and Unrest are two spots that come to mind. EK has a lot of blues at that level and you get to practice dodging giants!
Unrest might be a bit rough as that level, you're a bit high to solo outside the house and I don't know if enough people still go there to group inside. It's a pretty good intro do dungeons though and it's a lot less painful to learn about trains in your teens in Unrest than in your 40s in Karnors.

I like to solo most of the time myself, because I have kids and go AFK without nitice all the time. AFK = Away from keyboard

I would recomend that you do spend quite a bit of time in groups to learn the skills you will nead to participate in a group. Shaman play significantly different within a group and it can take some effort and practice to get the skills down. Also, I would tell you group that you are very new to EQ. Most people will be more then happy to help, encourage, teach, a new player who is honestly trying to learn.

Solo play can be a very efficient use of time but it is not nearly as fun as participating in a good dungeon crawl with a group of skilled people that are all pushed to the limits.

This board is also a great resource. If someone tells you somthing and you don't understand, then just come post a question about it on the crucible and we have a great crowd of people willing to help. Just try and make your question clear and with details. Even if all the advice is not the same, at least you can gain insight into how different shaman aproch the same problem.

Have a great adventure.
