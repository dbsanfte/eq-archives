* Vah Shir (cats) and froglocks are now player races
* Tons of new zones which make leveling easy at the lower levels
* armor dyes - hot pink troll love for everyone!
* CT was revamped to be a high level dungeon
* shaman/cleric now are able to use a stone to gate back to their home cities
* Horses! Lizards!
* Low level casters don't have to look at the spell book to med anymore
* If you die before level 10, you respawn with your gear
* Gnome Shadow Knights and Paladins and Halfling Paladins and Rangers!

* The ability to link item stats for other players

- LDoN is totally new to me. I've heard it talked about with the instanced quest stuff, but does it all require a group? Can I do quests solo? I doubt I'll play enough to even bother if these are the same old retarded camp quests that EQ always had.

Requires a group to get a dungeon instance. And by group they mean 3. So you and any 2 others and you can get an instance and go to town.

There is a 7 level spead max, so if you are 60 you can group with people up to 67(65) and down to people at 53 in instanced LDoN's. But, a 52 cant group with a 60. So, take the lowest level member of your group and +7, and the highest and -7, and where those numbers overlap is your grouping range.

There arent really quests involved with LDoN; its all pretty straight killing and loot. There are 4 different adventure types you can choose from (slaughter x many, collect x many drops, rescue X NPC, kill x boss mob) with slaughter and collect bing the easiest and therefore most commonly done.

There are 2 different dificulty settings; Normal and Hard. Hards (IMHO) arent worth the effort though they have double the point value upon completion and better drops because I can run 2 normals in the time it takes to run 1 hard. And in Normans a shaman functions fine as Main healer/slower; Hards will see too hard of hits and mitigation that make you heal now as effective and therefore probably lacking.

I love LDoN's, but thats because I hate "grinding" in one spot in one zone for hours on end. Thats not why I play EQ. Adventure, conquest, loot, and friends are why I EQ; all those are found in LDoN for me.

You get "points" from successful LDON missions. You can use these points to buy gear or augments to gear.
