Darjeeling
Sorry long post...
I came back to EQ and shamanism with hopes that the game had become more of an interesting exploration and adventure game than when i left (early PoP). Unfortunately it seems the game is actually worse. Here's a few gripes...

Dungeons
Dungeons used to have the potential to be a thrilling tour of the unknown, or unpredictable, with the promise of great rewards. The zones reeked of stories and character. Everybody knew the camps and the names of the mobs. Some of them were classic game experiences... most Kunark, a lot of Velious, and even parts of Luclin.

Now, once you have done just one LDoN you've pretty much seen it all. Why do they call these things adventures? They should be called races. The focus is on beating the clock, not adventure. Does anyone actually remember the names of mobs in these zones? LDoN dungeons have as much character as an instanced cup of coffeee. You don't EXPLORE LDoN, you ENDURE it.

GoD has been debated to death, but suffice to say, people still are unwilling to go there, unless they have a perfect, well geared, group containing an ideal mix of chars, and have 4 hours ++ to spend. The risk of many hours wasted time is so great because of the punitive cost of dying. Not because of exp lost, but because of wasted time.

Also, after having been fed the PoK insta-port and PoT insta-zone, SoE turns on their head and makes you run through multiple dangerous zones in GoD just to get to the place you want to fight. Didn't SoE introduce the PoP conveniences because everyone was sick of 30 min multiple zone runs and 2 hour naked crs?

Gear
EQ has become so equipment focused, people now pretty much MUST guild and do raid encounters to get reasonable upgrades in a decent space of time. This has lead to a massive schism between the haves and the have nots.

For players that can't or don't want to join big guilds, the alternative is endless hours of LDoN farming. No adventure, no exploration, just grind points.

Whereas the bazaar used to be a source of upgrades for players, pretty much any piece of "good" gear is now no drop. As a 65, once you get a few pieces of ornate, there is nothing to aspire to buy. On top of that, the economy is incredibly broken, with vast sums being asked for equipment that in many cases is just not worth buying. East Commonlands and later the bazaar were always interesting to hang out in just to watch what was for sale.

SoE has focused so much on making EQ a gear driven game, effects you would expect to see as spells or combat abilities, or even dream of having as spells or combat abilities, are put on items. Case in point: shamans have been pleading for new buffs that actually have an impact. What do we get? More stat buffs and the promise finally of a few lowly effects that top end items already have, such as strike through, damage mods, mitigation, avoidance, and which are better.

Class balance
Clerics are so far ahead of the other priests in healing, you need a ratio of 3 clerics to every other priest to raid. You can get by with less, but why would you? Groups for hard content do not get started without a cleric. Again, you can do harder content without a cleric, but why would you?

And for hard content, you still need cleric, enchanter, and warrior. As someone said, the trinity can do content at level 65 that the other classes need to be 70 to do. Other classes just can't tank, heal, and deal as much damage as these 3. Even after 5+ years...

Overall, if you look at the core skills of the 16 classes, possibly half the classes in EQ are essentially downgrades of better templates. If they removed shamans, mages, sks, monks, druids, necros, berserkers from the game, what would you really lose? FD, malos, and twitch? Why not just give these skills to beastlords, enchanters, and clerics, respectively, and make everyone switch over?

Tradeskills
Even if you spend hours and hours tradeskilling, have skills at GM level, there is almost nothing worthwhile to make unless you want to do long, boring, nonesensical quests that require multiple skills at GM level, and in many cases, drops that you can't get unless you are in the high-end content anyway. I'm a GM alchemist, and have multiple skills at ~200. I get asked to make gate potions, which are useful, but about to be made obsolete in Oow, and occasionally shrink. Why bother?

Not a Game, it's a Grind
Very few people play EQ from 1-64. They grind these levels so that they can start having fun at 65. I've played 2 classes to 60+, 4 to 50+ and many through the 30s. Frankly, it's a chore. There is very little point to having most of the content in Antonica, Kunark, and Velious. Nobody bothers to go there except for the odd weird expedition for spells or gems or something. Most people level in about 4 or 5 zones.

Then once you get to 65 you have the joy of grinding AA. And then if you want to raid, you have to grind flags, which is another major timesink. And all the while, there's the carrot of better better gear, zones where loot drops like candy, just ahead. One more flag, one more raid, then we'll be there.

Anyone who remembers an old bitter poster Eduin, probably remembers him for his vitriol. I remember him for a one of his more impassioned posts where he explained why he played EQ. He wrote he played EQ to do fantastical things in a fantastic environment. Too bad nobody at SoE seems able to understand this or to see beyond the bottom line to the dream.

LDoN dungeons, i agree with you on. they really are more of a race than anything. if you're doing a normal adventure at level 65, there's no drama whatsoever. basically, you go in and grind them out, and most of the fun you have is social. great time to joke around with folks and get to know them. high risk ldons are considerably more fun, imo, because they involve actually playing your character correctly, and just enough of a chance of death that you have to be aware of what's going on at all times. as complaints go, though...well...LDoN was basically a response to the player community. people were saying that as casual players they wanted opportunities to gear up competitively without having to join a big raid guild or do a lot of raiding. soo...viola, there it is. the first part of the solution for casual players. fantastic place to get aa xp, meet and socialize with people, and either gear up or supplement your other gear. i personally am friends with a 9600 hp, 1700 ac Shadowknight who is geared entirely from doing hard ldons and wearing a few tier 1 and 2 PoP raid drops.

GoD...have to join a raid guild? pfft. i'm in a guild that's working right now on tier 3 raids in PoP--not yet SRT flagged as a guild--and i'm doing work here on my own. organizing groups for trials, xp groups, etc. i'm not uber...i also am geared from tier 1 and 2 raids and ldons for the most part. BUT...there's some nice twists to GoD. first and foremost...in every zone there are random chances for some sweet drops off trash mobs. you go there and xp, and WHAM....jackpot. additionally, i am *glad* they haven't yet put up a book to port accross these zones. they retain as much or more character than the old world zones. awesome story lines, bad @$$ named, whether minis or raid encounters, and talk about exciting! high octane, heavy offense fights! the mobs don't have huge hp pools, but it makes every fight feel like an explosion. either that mob is going down fast, or you are. woooot! additionally, there are some very cool quests attached to the expansion, and the trials and aa themselves are easily worth the cost of it. BUT...IMO, one of the most FUN things about EQ is the chance to form up with a bunch of other people and work as a team to coordinate an effort and take down some Big Nasty Raid Encounter. and this zone DEFINITELY has that to offer. great new styles of raids, awesome events, etc.

gear as a focus? absolutely. i have no problems whatsoever with EQ being gear oriented as a very large part of the progression. for one thing, making many items no drop means you are having to work for most of them. sure, people can point out examples of doing jack and getting them, but if you're in Time, deep into GoD, etc, you have earned your gear at that point. one way or another, you're spending time and energy aquiring them, and there SHOULD be a big pay off. ldon gear, same thing. whether you're grinding out normals with drop in groups, or hammering out high risk ldons for faster returns, you're earning your gear. additionally, it's awesome to me seeing that i can be standing next to 20 other shams and none of us are geared identically from head to toe. in fact...in most cases there may be 3-4 common pieces, but we will be equipped differently from each other on most slots. i LIKE that. focus effects, for casters, are icing on the cake. lots of fun customizing with them. offensive sham? critical affliction 3 aa, burning affliction 4, extended affliction 4, improved dmg 4, nuke aa's. woot. not the most effective way to utilize a shammy in some cases, but that sure is fun. additionally, i LIKE that at this stage of my character development, i really can't go to the bazaar and upgrade any of my gear. i would MUCH rather go raid for gear than farm pp, and i like the idea that in order to have better gear than me, someone has to have worked at aquiring it. nothing would annoy me more than having some level 55 shammy with a lot of real dollars to spend go buy plat, deck his toon out in better gear than mine, and throw it in my face that he had more hp and ac than me, with more focus effects.



as for having to join a big raid guild, nah. on my server, i'm one of the leaders of a group called AOR (ayonae ro server...ayonae's open raiders), which is a group that helps people by running open raids on PoP flag mobs so that we can backflag folks, get people a kick start on their flagging, etc--and in the process, give repeat raiders chances to win some nice drops. i just became one of the leaders about 3 weeks ago, and in that time have helped people get over 1000 flags and at least 80 items without the help of a raid guild. we have a channel people join, and they go do whatever they want...they keep an eye on the channel, and when a raid is forming for something they want or need, they drop in and get flag or loot. additionally, a lot of them get to meet people who ARE guilded who are backflagging or just going to help, and are often able to find guilds with schedules that work with their own. in some cases, though, we have gotten people SRT flagged without a single flag from their guild...all open raids. not common, mind you--MM, KoS/Saryrn, and Bert are rare runs, but they happen.




as for the grind...well...no pain, no gain. i, for one, LIKE the grind. i LIKE knowing that i have to work to get where i want to be. all the time i spend doing something that's NOT a major event makes me appreciate the big events that much more. i like going out, hammering out some AA or ldon, doing GoD trials, hanging out whacking minis in BoT, etc. all of those are aspects of the game, but in my honest opinion, they are smaller facets that help you prepare for the bigger deals...the most fun part of EverQuest, by far--raids, and killing named mobs. whether big raid mobs or minis with lots of juice or notoriety (ie...Frenzied Noc Corpsethrasher in Barindu...have heard a few people swearing about him and his crackheadesque rampages), there's very little more satisfying in game than smashing one of these things. that is why i play EQ. you mention doing fantastical things? doesn't get much more fantastic than the idea of fighting Quarm in the Plane of Time, does it? or working your way through the awesome Breakdown in Communications quest (GoD) which takes you through everything from full on raids to mini trials and even busy work, all while following an awesome story line. even the standard Plane of Nightmare Terris Thule raid is a hoot, and the story line for the Saryrn raid string is cool. there are LOTS of fantastical things to do. there are so many story lines and so much detailed and themed content that it almost makes me dizzy. i LOVE this game, and the more i play and discover, the more i love it.


i'm a bartender on Pacific Standard Time. i also have a wife and daughter that i spend a lot of time with...if someone with my schedule is able to explore this much and get this much done, there's no reason anyone else can't. it basically comes down to whether you're aggressive and dynamic, or the type of person who sits in a zone with his "LFG" up and waits for people to send him tells, all the while complaining that he can't get groups. make it happen. if you want to do something, get yourself there. i realized a month and a half ago that i had zero flags. none. not even PoJ trials. soo...now i'm almost SRT flagged. because i started doing drop in raids, met some folks, found a guild that had a schedule i could work with, and even took over leading the open raids so i could continue moving things along. GoD trials? i have put together every group i've been with out there myself. sitting and waiting doesn't get you anywhere but frustrated.


pick something to do, and do it. it's there waiting to be done, and YES...it really is fantastic.
