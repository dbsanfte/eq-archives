There may be some selection bias - people who run the experiment and don't like their results (due to fearing some sort of nerf) might not post them.

But, that seeming unlikely, with what confidence can we say that the likelihood of landing slow for beastlord and shaman is the same? (which seems to be the reasonable assumption by default, given the spell data available on lucy.)

I've never done any serious statistics, so let's just say that the true p_shaman=.118

Now, let's get the sample variance S^2 for the beastlord data, 44 1s and 277 0s (N=321) (the mean is .137). S^2 = (1/320)*(44*(1-.137)^2 + 277*(0-.137)^2) = .1187

From that we compute the t-statistic, t=(.137-.118)/sqrt(S^2/321)=.988 which corresponds to less than a 90% chance that there is in fact a difference in the true rates for landing shaman and beastlord slow - in other words, not very impressive, run this experiment again 10 times and you'd get this result by chance once even if they were the same

Or, go to http://home.clara.net/sisa/t-thlp.htm which indicates that with 95% confidence, the difference lies between 7% in favor of beastlords to 3% in favor fo shamans (i.e. it could be that shaman slows land more often than BL, it just hasn't happened in this small data set)

Or, using 38 successes out of 321 for shamans (by the percentage) and 44 (by observation for the beastlords), with 95% confidence the difference in a run of 321 slows should be between 24 more landed for the BL and 12 more for the shaman (if I'm not wrong in guessing that this sort of repeated bernoulli trial approaches a poisson distribution in the limit?)

That's just a long way of saying that the data so far is inconclusive =)
