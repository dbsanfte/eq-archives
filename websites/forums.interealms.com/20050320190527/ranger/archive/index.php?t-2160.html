In a nutshell, approximately every 250,000 years, according to solid scientific research, the Earth's magnetic pole flip-flops (for reference, the magnetic pole of the Sun flip-flops approximately every 11 to 13 years and did so last year). Until now.

According to the data, the strength of the magnetic field at the poles is about 10% less than it was 25 years ago. Conversely, there is an area of Africa that is currently experiencing a magnetic field bubble that points in the opposite direction as the polar field. If the African field continues to grow in strength, and it's growing a lot faster than scientists originally thought, it may indeed force a polar shift within the next 200 to 500 years.

Weaker magnetic field = more solar particles reach the surface of the Earth = global warming. There you have it, folks. It has nothing to do with us.
