Targuran
I just wanted to confirm what has already been said and add a little.

I've successfully duoed 4 Gwurms at level 61 with Stoicism (no malo/torpor).

3 times with 62 SK
1 time with a 60 Pally (I pulled for this fight and yes they are VERY slow)

Landed malosini either first or second cast every time then slow first cast every time.

Fight took approx 7-8 minutes (remember to reslow after about 5 and a half).

I just healed with Kraggs when at 50% with SK and 60% with pally. Used epic dot, and Stoicism + Canni 4 on myself when not healing.

Never went below 80m the whole time and ended all 4 fights at about 90-95m and full health.

I would also recommend a fire resist buff for the tank as Gwurms proc a AoE fire DD.

Edit: Also wanted to add, I never had a problem with gaining agro any of the fights. I imagine with a warrior as your tank, it may be a problem initially.

As a side note, I was not able to single pull the elder wyverns at 61 but the SK was able to at 62.

Myself (60 shaman) and my wife's 56 warrior are able to duo these pretty easily. I've pulled about 15 of these and have not had a Turgur's resisted as of yet. I pull with Turgur's, and when I finally coax him back to our camp spot, I run around like crazy while the warrior gains aggro. At that point, send in the pet, epic dot, pox, and JBB. Reaply dots as needed, med if you need to and continue JBB when you don't. I keep CB and Kragg's loaded for heals and I don't think I'm much below half mana by the end of the fight (I always get kei before trying this). Neither of us are twinked - warrior has around 1125 AC with 4k hit points, and I have a lot less than that :) Warrior is using Frostbringer and Black Acrylia sword of some kind in off hand.

I've tried casting Bane, but the warrior seems unable to keep taunt, so I use Pox instead which is significantly lower aggro. Once the warrior has aggro, she's never lost it with me casting Pox, epic, and JBB.

Hope that helps.
