However, the dynamics of the game are totally different for non-US players, in a way that US players seem unable to comprehend. No, I can't just grab a few dozen of my closest L60 friends to whack target_X, because the server population in my timezone is so low. There just aren't any high-level clerics or warriors around for tough encounters, because those classes need groups to level, and so on and so forth.

No matter what the reason, be it timezone or the implicit PvP that comes from guilds going all-out to keep spawns to themselves, there's probably not much point in focusing too much on what you don't have and can't get. Set your own, more realistic goals and expectations.

In my case that has meant getting another account and starting to 2-box, and I'm amusing myself by levelling up alts in the times when my friends aren't on to group with. It's interesting to find out first-hand what some of the issues other classes have are, and making characters who can explore zones I never did (or can't) with my main.
