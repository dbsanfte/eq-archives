ciccero
If you're willing to quest a bit, the spear of fate is a rather nice main hand weapon. No AC on it, but it does have great stats, resists, and a clicky DoT (albiet fairly short range).

At 53 you can solo everything you need to do the spear up until Rak'Ashiir himself and the child's tear.

Do the quest up through test of patience, then do the test of wisdom (killing Talbien and Glaron in Rathe Mtns) over and over turning in Woe, Envy, and Marr's Promise to the wandering spirit until you're max ally.

Then all you need is to turn in your child's tear (or find a shaman still able to MQ it with you), bring a mage friend or two and a cleric and tag along with some folks hunting reavers in CoM (or bring more folks if you wanna kill the reavers yourself).

Once Rak spawns, park a couple fire pets beside him (make sure mages have low fire resists beforehand), turn in the tear, take the first AE, mages /pet attack, and you slowly back away to a neutral corner once no-longer agro, then when sure not agro, make your way back to the rest of the group.

Kill Rak, loot scales, get spear. Not too hard, takes a day or so for faction, then whenever you can set up a MQ tear or fear raid to get one, and ever how long it'll take for Rak. In my opinion, for the level of difficulty in obtaining it, it's easily worth it.

If you already have a child's tear, you can finish your epic within the week if you can muster the 3 or 4 folks it takes to help kill Rak. I'd suggest holding off on spending more money and go ahead with the epic.

ciccero
Rak's not screwy. When they made the pet resist buff changes a couple months back it changed the way pets' resists worked vs. him. Pets use either their own resist or the resist of their master, whichever is higher... however, fire pets get an invulerability that isn't considered if the pet uses the master's resist.

If the mages summon their pets with high resists (and/or possibly buff their resists later), then the pets will use their master's resist rates instead.

Therefore, before summoning the fire pets, and during the fight, just make sure that mages have very low resists so the pets will default to their own natural resists and Rak can't hurt them with his AEs. You'll still need to heal the pets from melee damage though, so make sure the pets are hasted, muzzle'd, symbol'd, HoS, etc. Load 'em up and turn 'em loose.

I got my 2nd shaman epic the other night (maybe 3 weeks ago-ish), and we took him with 2 mage pets no problem at all.
