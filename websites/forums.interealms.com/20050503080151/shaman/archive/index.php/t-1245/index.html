Bigguss
The determining factor for your xp is likely to be how fast you can kill monsters, rather than the split of xp between you two. Even if he's getting most of the experience, if you're helping at all, you guys will be killing tougher monsters than either would be alone, bringing in lots more experience.

When grouping, definitely slow everything, keep him healed and buffed, and if you've got mana, drop a poison DoT (assuming it will last the whole fight- otherwise a nuke is probably more mana efficient.)

You'll get a little more group-friendly at 24 (regen, better buffs and DoT, cannibalize) and a lot more group friendly at 29 (better slow, quickness, better heal, invis, even charm animal can be neat if hunting in the right place.)

Andrewe
They changed this a looong time ago... about the time that hybrid exp penalties were removed.

The split in experience between the two of you will be about equal to the ratio of your levels (so about 40:60). At 22 I was grouping with a 32 and making about a level every two hours or so, so I don't think it really gimps your experience. This was with your exact same situation (lower shaman, higher monk).

Just have them find something that's blue to them, itll probably be yellow/light red to you. Keep them healed and buffed. This works particularly well with any melee class.

That whole "higher person gets 90% of the experience" wasn't really true, and to the extent it was, was totally removed when they took out the hybrid exp penalties (which has got to be more than a year ago).
