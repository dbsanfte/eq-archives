Verdandi:

Disclaimer: I'm not an expert at the spdat, but this is what I've been able to discover...but I haven't tested ANY of this in the game.

Bottom Line up front:

The *effect* Concentration increases Mana by 2 per tick.

The *effect* Greater Concentration increases Mana by 3 per tick.

The *effect* Mystical Infusion adds 50 mana to your mana pool.

The *effect* Greater Mystical Infusion adds 100 mana to your mana pool.

The *effect* Mystical Aptitude apparently does the same thing as the 12th level Enchanter spell "Intellectual Advancement", increasing your casting skill by 3. It does NOT increase mana by 4 per tick.

I've highlighted *effect* since we can't be sure that when we drink a potion it is actually causing the effect of the same name...it would be possible that Verant is trying to mislead us, by using dummy entries in the Spdat. But I doubt it.

Discussion:

First, the entry for Mystical Aptitude is COMPLETELY different than the entries for Concentration and Greater Concentration. So chances of it givin back 4 mana per tick are slim-to-none.

Lets talk about "Mystical Aptitude" and the Enchanter spell "Intellectual Advancement".

Both have some of the same statistics:

Category: clarity
Attrib : 70
Base : 3

The only spells that have '70' as the first attribute are:
Intellectual Advancement (Base1 is 3)
Intellectual Superiority (Base1 is 6)

The *effects* Mystical Aptitude and Focused Mind both have 70 as an attrib, Focused Mind has a Base of 2, Mystical Aptitude has a base of 3.

My guess is that 70 is the code for fizzle rate (or possible effective level of caster) and thus it's modified by 3.

Side note: The Dragon effect Feeblemind gives you a negative 200 to Attribute 70...so I doubt that '70' is effective level.

I suspect enchanters will be able to tell us more.
Edit: In the newest version of the spdat viewer, 70 is mapped to Casting Skill. But we still aren't sure what 'casting skill' actually is.

Concentration and Greater Concentration:

in the Spdat is a field for "Description", and then several fields for Attributes, Equations, Max Level, etc...basically all the real values that get the job done.

Mystical Infusion, Greater Mystical Infusion, Mystical Aptitude are all missing descriptions. Thats part of the reason it's so hard to figure out what they do. However, there are values in many of the columns, and some are pretty obvious. Ex:

Attribute4 = Total Mana
Base4 = 100

Its a pretty good guess that the effect is to increase your total mana by 100 (that was Greater Mystical Infusion, btw).

Edit: Descriptions are now available, no changes from what we already knew, however.


Concentration and Greater Concentration both have Descriptions:
"Increase Mana by 3 (L1)" , but the 'real' values are missing...If you compare Concentration with Flowing Thought 2 you can see what I mean. Both have the same description:
"Increase Mana by 2" but Flowing Thought 2 has the following values:
Attribute2 = Mana
Base2 = 2

Clarity has the same entries:
Attribute2 = Mana
Base2 = 1
Max2 = 7
Calc2 = Min + level / 8

Concentration has no values for Mana at all...It doesn't appear to do anything! The more I ponder, the more I think that it is a dummy entry in the spdat, and the when you drink the potion you are getting some other entry in the spdat.


EDIT: I guess I should have used the newer version of eqcaster (the spdat viewer)...Concentration does have values for Mana, they are just buried way at the end.

Attribute7 = Mana
Base7 = 2


EDIT: I should have downloaded the newer version of the spdat viewer first, and saved a lot of time. Aplogies to everyone who was forced to read through my ramblings.
