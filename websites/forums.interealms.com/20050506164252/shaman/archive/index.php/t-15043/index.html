Xatrei
A couple of things...

I two box my necro / shaman combo, but on the occasions that I decide to just play one or the other, I usually don't have a tough time finding a group with either. It's a bit quicker to get into a group with my shaman, but I'm normally not LFG for very long on either. The "jobs" of the two classes are obviously very different and require a different playstyle for soloing, grouping and raiding.

Many people have a pretty narrow view of how necromancers hunt. Sure, necros can fear kite (at low levels), and they can aggro kite, but that's hardly all they do. As others have said in a purely solo situation, they can charm, they can pet tank, they can tank slowed mobs themselves (even in PoP), and of course they can root cook. It's all situational, and it's having so many options that make necros such a powerful class. In group or raid situations, they bring this same flexibility into play.

Necros aren't just relegated to being mana batteries on raids. Necro DPS is substantial at high levels. Wise raid leaders let the necros do their thing and only employ mana pumping situationally when there is no other smart option. Taking necros off of DPS is akin to asking the rangers to stop shooting arrows in order to chain cast chloroblast.

Someone said that necro gear is cheaper, but I couldn't disagree more. Int gear, much like wis gear for a shaman, takes a back seat to HP and Sta for a smart necro. While it might be really cheap to get int alone up to the cap, you'll be doing so with junk gear that leaves you with next to no HP. GOOD necro gear is every bit as expensive as GOOD shaman gear (perhaps even a bit more expensive).

I love both classes dearly, but If I were forced to choose between one or the other (a decision I would loathe to make) it would be the necro. That's just my take on it, and given the same options others would choose the shaman. I'd play both depending on my mood that day and you'll find yourself gradually gravitating towards your favorite, which is the one you should focus on. If you have the option of dual boxing both, you'll find that the pair make an incredibly powerful duo.
