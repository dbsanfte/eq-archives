I couldn't hope to win an LDoN without using my fast-acting shaman poison DoTs, for example.

So I need a tank that can hold agro and still put out some decent DPS. With good agro, my shaman can generate very decent DPS via his stackable DOTs and dogdog. With the enchanter, I can handle adds without too much problem, and can lull/pacify, so pulling isn't a huge issue. I don't charm with the enchanter, so he doesn't really do much for DPS right now. But my shaman is swimming in mana, with constant Clarity II and eventually KEI.

I just haven't played either the warrior or shadowknight in so long that I don't know which character makes more sense. I know warriors got an upgrade to their taunt, but have no idea how effective it is compared to SK spells. I also don't know which character will put out higher DPS - I assume the SK would with a lot of spells, but casting lots of spells is tricky while 3-boxing, especially with a constantly-canni-ing shaman.

So I'm leaning toward the warrior, but don't really want to jump back into that if the SK is a far superior option. With the enchanter (have clarity II now at level 57), my SK would probably always have mana, so maybe that option makes more sense. I have some decent gear on warrior already, and have about 60k in the bank, so I could probably score a CHoS/Bloodsworn combo for agro - unless there's a better option. I've already got a Greatstaff of Thunder for 2hb agro.

I'm posting here mostly to get the advice of shamans who've either 2-boxed/3-boxed similar setups with either a warrior or sk, or who regularly group with one and know their strengths. I've already posted this question on both the shadowknight and warrior boards; amusingly, both class boards seem to think the other class is a better option. The grass is always greener, I guess.

Any advice or similar experience you coud give would be appreciated.

You want an SK. Damage should actually be comparable between your monk and a similarly equipped SK, plus the shaman and the chanter should be able to do pretty much whatever they want to the mob. Make a hotkey to cast Clinging Darkness repeatedly and you should have no trouble with aggro.

Gearwise, you'd probably find the war a bit easier to find good armor for and the sk easier to find a good weapon for.

Tanking ability-wise, wars do have a small advantage over SKs in their ability to take damage. This advantage largely dissappears in your situation though, since you don't have a cleric (no CH) and you'll presumably be fighting xp mobs, not raid level stuff that would require disciplines.

I twobox a shaman and an sk, sometimes threeboxing with my cleric, and it's an extremely effective combination. The only downside is, it's hard to go back to grouping with other people once you start.

Macnair
I use a SK for my two-box tank. I find it works well for me.

When you two- or three-box, you can't give 100 % attention to any char. I expect that it is rare that the two are equal partners. Instead, one of them becomes the Main that you give the bulk of your attention to, and the others become Pets: you can give them more complex instructions than what you can tell Kenober to do, but your basic plan is for them to do their job while you are actively working your Main character.

For me, the shaman is the main and the SK is the pet. I pull with the SK, and then I hit his aggro hotkey: he will now stand there, hold aggro, get pounded on, and do decent dps, while I switch to the shaman to do slows, heals, and damage.

That two-box combination is very workable. I like it a lot.

You're working three. But it sounds like your chanter demands very little of your attention: it tags along, provides buffs and occasional crowd control. So I would expect you would have no problem with the SK as the third member of the trio.

The advantage of the warrior is that it is an easier pet to bot than the SK. But you probably don't need to have the easier bot: you can probably run the SK as a bot reasonably well.

The advantage of the SK is that it can do more things for you. Because it is a bot, you won't get the dps that an aggressive SK can generate: but even as a pet it will give you more than a warrior bot can.

BlargX
The difference in aggro between a warrior and SK is the difference between having a virtual guarantee of it and hoping for it. Playing your warrior and shaman well won't change that.

If you want to do the toughest mobs around, go for a warrior. They have the defensive disc and lots more hitpoints and better mitigation. They are also more needed for most raids, if you like raiding a lot. You'll never come anywhere close to the options regarding pulling that you do with a SK though. Or have the ability to just FD your tank and gate your casters, letting you try things over and over, as long as it takes, without absorbing a single death. With the warrior, once you engage, you're pretty much looking either to win or for a rez.

Another great thing about shadowknights is that you can summon your corpse with them when you DO have a bit of bad luck or screw up. And they're great for exploring -- they can invis to undead and to use regular invis. If you get a pre-nerf circlet of shadow, they can re-invis right in mob's face practically and not get caught doing it. That combined with FD means -- and the final escape hatch of corpse summoning -- means you can do a lot of exploring and messing around with a SK -- something people with monks often enjoy a lot -- I know I do on both my monk and SK.

The SK pet and your shaman pet add a fair amount of damage over time. It's not a huge burst, but over time it's a nice little addition.

You won't be duoing/trio'ing mobs that are as tough if you use your SK, but overall, you will have much more flexibility in pulling and fighting, and I think a lot more fun.

My SK is up to a bit over 7700 HP with raid buffs wearing only his general bazaar stuff including ogre cultural fulvous darkscale and ornate on hands, wrists, and arm. I actually have to give up hitpoints to wear the ornate over the fulvous -- it's very good stuff. You can get a pretty decently geared tank pretty cheaply these days; cultural prices have plunged, and a war marshall's bladed staff costs about a quarter to a fifth what a windblade does on my server -- yet it outdamages the windblade.

Anyway, good luck. I loved my monk who I played as a main for a while, and I love my SK now. Either makes a good match. I wouldn't want to give up FD pulling, so I abandoned my own warrior at 52.
