This past Saturday, my girlfriend tells me she is going to do some charity work with her company. Sounds great, no worries. She then proceeds to tell me its in Harlem. OK, still so far so good. Now I ask well when are you leaving and how are you getting there? She tells me she is leaving at 7am, driving into the city and taking the subway from there.

For those who do not know, Harlem is uptown in Manhattan (NYC). It is a lower income area and is predominantly a black and hispanic community. It was my understanding that it was not a great place to be, especially for a young girl by herself on the subway. I told her no way is she going by herself, I had thought she was meeting people at work and all going in a group.

Long story short I went with her. We got there with absolutely no problems. We were not sure we were going the right way after we got off the subway and I asked 2 guys passing by if they knew where the street we were looking for was. They were both very polite and helpful.

The area was clean in general, especially by NY City standards. The buildings were a surprise to me; great architecture! Some really stunning churches in particular.

Anyway we got to where we all had to meet up and set off to the local YMCA to start painting. Everyone we met involved in this was great. All the people from the community were saying thanks to us. It was awesome. We had a good turn out and we got the job done by about 2pm. Had a great lunch donated by deli, and had a good time doing something nice for some neighborhood kids.

I felt so damn guilty for worrying about letting my GF go alone to Harlem. I feel that if she said, “well we are going to do work in Midtown”, I wouldn’t have said a word about it. I know better now but does that make me a bad person? I mean, her mom and dad called to see how she was doing. She told both of them where she was going and they didn’t say a word.

I don't think you were wrong at all. Here's a possible flip side for you to know that you're not alone.

I'm a competitive shooter. Not to drag guns into this, but my wife and I shoot Sporting Clays. We have a club with a very good safety record. Anyone is welcome to join the club, we have all walks of life, ages, genders, whatever.

We had a tournament a few weeks ago. Normally, we get a local youth organization to score for us, as it frees up the members to enjoy the tournament. We donate a sum to the organization that pays for uniforms, trips, whatever. Plus the competitors all chip in at the end to tip the trappers for their service, which also goes to the organization. This time, we had a local high school football team. It was my job to show the kids what to do, what to say, and how to do their jobs. Only one of them had been there before.

About a half dozen of the kids were african-american. While talking with one of the chaperones, she told me that these kids were scared out of their wits. I realized why as I was asking. A bunch of young black men coming out to a club back in the woods filled predominantly with middle-aged white folks carrying guns.

At any rate, I made sure all the competitors treated the kids with respect and kindness. There's no place for being rude to someone trying to help you. The competition went off relatively smoothly, the trappers did a great job, and everyone had a fine lunch together afterwards. As I spoke to them, every one of them asked me to try to get their team again next time.

It's human nature, nothing to be ashamed of. I know that I would have a problem with my wife going to certain portions of our city at night alone.
