Sciek Spellbottler
If you're a casual gamer, join a guild to do stuff with your friends and make new friends. If you're a serious powergamer, join an uberguild to see the endgame and reap the uber rewards of that. If you're somewhere in between, find a guild that fits your playstyle and see some parts of EQ that you aren't going to see in a pickup group. If you have a bunch of friends playing already, form your own guild and add the new friends you make as you play.

There's no one reason to join a guild. I would advise anyone to turn down any random guildinvites ppl send after a single grouping or even worse those tools that just run around spamming you with guildinvites because you're the right class/level. Be choosy and find out what the guild goals are and see if they fit with what your goals in EQ are. Meet and get to know some members of the guild and see if they are the kind of people you want to associate with on a fairly regular basis in EQ.

In the end, it should just feel like a place you belong and making the decision to join, ideally, will be an easy one.

I joined a guild for friendships. I’d met a couple of people whom I enjoyed grouping with and I was eventually invited into their guild. It was a small guild with more of a family feel and we did very little in the way of raiding. But it was always fun to log into the game and participate in guild chat even if I wasn’t grouped with anyone. EQ can become very lonely if you don’t have any friends.

One of the downsides of a guild, at least for me, is the politics that can sometimes arise. The problem we had in our guild was that some members expected the guild to deal with personal issues that had nothing to do with the guild. We were, of course, there for support, but when in game romances start to cause problems within the guild something is wrong. In the end the officers got tired of dealing with it all and there was nobody to take over, so the guild disbanded.

I’m currently in a larger guild that does a lot of raiding. There are a lot of nice people in the guild, some of which were from my old guild, but I still don’t feel at home. Being a member of this guild could prove very advantageous for me in the form of equipment upgrades, something I rather desperately need, but I think the pace is just too hurried for my taste. I miss the laid back feel of my old guild.

If your happy grouping with friends and don’t feel a need to change then don’t worry about joining a guild. But having spent a good amount of time both in and out of guilds, I find the game more enjoyable within one. The secret, of course, is finding the guild that’s right for you. But once you do it can truly be a game altering experience.
