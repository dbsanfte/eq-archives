Ok I just tried my "count the hp healed" methodology suggested above. I did observe on some trials the "rollback" of the last tic when the server client syncronized, as noted above. Here are the results with Q (350 hp new version) +SCRM only. My standing regen was 10hp/tic, so subtracting 60hp for that during the Q heal itself. The data is hp at start of Q landing, hp at wears off message, and hp on the next tic after wear off:

Trial 1: 1065hp > 3585hp > 3245hp (rollback to 6 tics)

Trial 2: 382 hp > 2902 hp > 2912 hp (7 tics)

Trial 3: 182 hp > 2702 hp > 2712 hp (7 tics)

Trial 4: 130 hp > 2640 hp > 2550 hp (7 tics)

Trial 5: 88 hp > 2608 hp > 2618 hp (7 tics)

Trial 6: 96 hp > 2616 hp > 2266 hp (rollback to 6 tics)

These results seem consistent with what is reported by others using the count the tics method. This suggests the 6 vs. 7 tics is a timing thing, which could be used to further extend Q with an Extended Blessing 4 item as some others have observed.

Another thing I learned is that if I start casting Q in the middle period between displayed "tic" updates of my hp, the timing is such that I get the 7 tics. To get 6 tics, I cast Q starting right over the top of the tic. Of course, this may vary with lag in a given zone situation, and internet connection. I was doing this in a low lag zone (FV tunnel at book).

Bottom line, for someone like me with SCRM but no Q focus item, Q is now a 2100 or 2450 heal. Not bad at all.
