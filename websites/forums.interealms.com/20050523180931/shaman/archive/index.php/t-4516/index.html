Kriel Darkbane
Where do you guys usually exp, and what general tactics do you use? Do you kite or fearkite outdoors, root rot, or do dungeons with a fairly traditional tanking strategy (with a shaman or pet tanking)? It's a little hard to answer the question without knowing more about the tactical situation.

In general, I'd say to (1) not feel bad about your pet. Free damage is free damage, and once he's summoned he's free damage until he dies; (2) heal as needed (3) what's wrong with blasting away? If mobs last long enough, use your dots. If he has Bane of Nife, you're golden using Envenomed Bolt. If he's stuck with Envenomed bolt still, then you can just use nukes. If you have or can get a JBB, just click away. If not, then use mana for it with poison or cold nukes. (3) Malise line - you're looking at a lot of spell damage in that group, so I'd think resist debuffing would be useful if you're not already doing it. Even the small/cheap one can make a big difference. (4) Pulling could be a good choice, but only in outdoor or easily pulled zones. A higher level puller is generally a good choice in hard-to-pull areas, due to level aggro effects. (5) If the mobs live a long time (close to two mins), you can use Plague as well.

Well, as others said - if you're with friends, who cares about wringing every last DPS out of the game? I'd much rather do a guild xp group than a pickup group - not only is it more fun, but I don't have to worry about the quality of the other players I'm with. (Not saying pickup groups are trash, just that guildies are a trusted variable already)

As for two shaman in a group, it's pretty much all been said. Talk, divvy up what you're doing. Pets and JBB stack, and are mana free damage... different dots are stackable. And if you don't have an enchanter along, might take a look at some little-used spells. Torrent of Poison (is 55, and Snap is 52, I realize) can be good damage, and stacks. Blinding a mob won't set it running, like Fear does, if it's engaged - it'll just keep it fighting whatevers nearest, probably pets.

The only place I've found where it's really, really boring being an extra shaman is someplace like ToV, where every fight is a single MT fight. An xp group without a FD puller is likely going to have multiple mobs, so there's plenty of room for extra fun.
