BlargX
I'm liking SCS quite a bit now. I'm a huge fan of LR5, and noticed it make a really dramatic difference to me. However, if SCS keeps you from getting aggro, depending on what you tend to do with your playtime, it might obviate the need, or at least push back the high priority, of LR5 and similarly the combat mitigation AA's. If they don't want to hit you in the first place, you won't need to worry what happens when you get hit, as much.

Of course, if you're in groups where aggro control isn't good, or just find yourself getting hit all the time or are soloing, then SCS won't be enough to let you avoid getting LR5 as quick as you can.

I'm a big fan of radiant cure, for people who raid a lot or just group a lot where group members tend to get slowed or hit with DOTs, etc. Some mobs group slow everyone, and that drops down your DPS a lot, sometimes enough to put your group's survival in danger. Any DPS and crowd control you're doing gets interrupted by doing lots of individual cures, too. So being able to radiant cure as much as you can is I think often very valuable.

VP -- I hated it at level 1. Thought it was ok at level 2, definitely much better. And at level 3, I like it well enough.

I'd do all the things like quickbuff, scm, scr, scrm ages before I did anything else but canni 5. All those work well with each other to really dramatically enhance you. The other stuff is playing with toys by comparison.
