Kellenial
The other day, my wife (56 rogue) was logged in before me resupplying. She had on her LFG flag hoping that by the time I finished what I was doing in RL and logged in (about 20 minutes top), she'd have found us a group. As it turned out, she received a request for an Everfrost LDoN. She was ecstatic that she actually received a tell for a group! However, when asked if they had room for me (55 shaman), they said no--that they were looking for DPS. She was a little dejected, but I told her to go anyway...that I'd find something to do once I got on. (I ended up helping a guildmate with camping an epic piece.) Turn out that the group was a paladin, 2 rangers, a cleric and 2 rogues.

But it got me to thinking about how I felt. At first I was a little angry and upset. How could they NOT want a shaman. We can slow, buff and, for a mid-50s LDoN, heal adequately. Then I thought that, what I was missing here is the plight my wife deals with daily as a rogue. There are an overabundance of DPS classes for groups. Most groups for mid-50s prefer someone who can slow (which ended up to be my function in Hate that night). Add to that the tank and healing class, that leaves 3 spots for everyone else. No wonder there are nights I'm at work and she calls me frustrated that she can't get a group.

While this is most likely not new info to everyone else, or to me, it made me realize this all over again. And while many see the elemental+ game as being particularly hard for a shaman, to me this incident helps me to see a little of the other side of the coin--when I have things a little better than my wife's rogue.

Funny how that appreciation of things comes from getting rejected by a group? :)
