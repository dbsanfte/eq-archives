The dogs are N A S T Y, and if you get something that summons it hurts. A lot. Personally I prefer the Ferubi zone in (on the Qinimi side). The first 5-8 mobs in the place are pullable solo and nothing summons (go go root rot), and they die fast. You can tank in teh face if you want some action, but make sure those suckers are slowed before they're anywhere near you!
jzak
I've soloed in KT and Yxtta (west entrance). Hynids, nocs, cragbeasts do not summon in Yxtta. I think ukuns are rootable and don't summon also. Mastruqs are sometimes immune to root. I think all kyvs are immune to root. Ra'tuks summon and flurry. The ones you can kill have about 40% less hp than doomfire guardians and give between 9-12% AAXP per kill. Use MR-based roots as they seem less resistant to MR than to DR. VP3 breaks early most times. My problem is finding a steady flow of mobs though.

Shabura Naarteban
At least some, and possibly all, hynids and cragbeasts in KT summon. You choose poorly (if unknowingly).

The ra'tuk in KT are safe, if you can deal with the enormous melee box and flurry. The ukun likewise, although they have some scary burst dps when root breaks. I've heard good thigns about soloing the aneuks, but haven't tried them myself. Kyvs just move too fast to be a good solo target - possibly if you had a means of assured escape from combat, and gambled on the first root landing. Ikaavs and ixt should just be avoided. Golems are fairly easy fights when single, as many of them are shaman and will waste time casting Bane of Nife instead of hammering your skull in - but they have many more hp than typical KT mobs.

I can't speak as well for Yxtta soloing. There're a couple of druids that tend to set up shop there, so I havent' gotten much time alone with the locals.
