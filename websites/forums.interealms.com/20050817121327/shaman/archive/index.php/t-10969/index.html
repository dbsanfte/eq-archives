That guy had a ton of nerve. I would let him know it. I think you've been warned the kind of behavior you can expect out of him in the future. If he can take advantage of you, he probably will. I hope the other people in the group weren't also your guildies, because if they were, you're going to take a lot of abuse in that guild.

Generally, people who hang around to buff should get firm commitments at worst, and be in the first group to go in if possible, since people often "suddenly have to log off for an emergency" when they've gotten what they want out of you. So if they said they'd come back and tag you after they passed, it's wiser to count on it NOT happening than happening. Usually it won't happen.

You want to know the most ruthless and practical thing to do? Put an /AFK tag over your head and don't buff anyone who is not first buffing your group with any missing buffs. Reasons?

First and foremost is that once people have your buffs, they don't need you, and there's precious little charity to go around in the game. If your first group falls apart, you will have nowhere else to go because you'll have buffed everyone else in the waiting area already. Who needs you know? Almost anyone would be better DPS than you. What's the point of inviting you?

Second reason is that your buffs actually DO have value. That's why people want them. What that value means to you when your buffs are on other groups than your own is that that group has an increased chance of succeeding in the trials. If they go before you and lose, you wait 10 minutes before the next group can go in. Not bad! But, if they succeed, thanks in part to your buffs...now you have to wait 30 minutes for the trials to reset so you can get another shot. Way to go, buffs! Thanks for TRIPLING the time I have to wait! How about if you've buffed three groups? Well, if they all pass, you've now turned a quick business into an extra full hour of waiting for your shot. Meanwhile, people will likely come in to form more groups and you'll buff them too, right? Great -- now let's say your group fails. You go to the back of the line behind the guys you just buffed. They win! Grats you on the extra 20 minutes wait your buffs might have had a very real hand in making possible.

Sounds like a cold, kinda jerky way of looking at things, eh?

Never said it wasn't. In fact, I always buff everybody. I do a lot of things not in my best self-interest.

But...just be aware that it's not always in your best interest.

And frankly, if you're feeling kinda like you're being taken advantage of by friends, guildies, strangers, whatever...why help them do whatever it is that bugs you or makes you feel mad or left out? Remember the saying, "familiarity breeds contempt"? As long as they can get all you have to offer without giving you anything in return, a lot of people will think you're the best thing that ever happened to them, and love to take advantage of you whenever you give the the opportunity. Maybe you might even enjoy that type of thing; some do. But they'll appreciate your help and buffs a lot more when they don't have them than when they can just get them for free and then kick our a** out the door with a snicker.

This post is a very important illustration of the effects of repeated lackluster expansions on the power of a shaman. If we had kept pace with other classes, it would not be an issue. Yes, shaman buffs can help other classes, but in the case of these trials, shamans do not bring enough DPS to the group to be desireable.

This is as true of the most uber shamans as it is of the rest of us. When I was getting flagged, Chukzombi's magister group was ahead of us. It had two shamans in it (pretty sure Mukas was one, can't remember if the other was Chuk or Warren). Both shamans were decked out with the latest VT gear, both had ancient scourge, etc. Their group failed at least twice while I was there, maybe even more.

When I got flagged, the only thing I did was heal and sick my dog on mobs (didn't have the 61 pet then). My nukes were a waste, slow was barely effective. We had a wizard and druid nuking, an SK tank, a rogue, and an enchanter for mezzing. As it was, I was barely able to keep the tank alive with Kragg (didn't have Tnarg), and was unable to save the enchanter.

I've done a few trials since then to try and help others get through, but found that the best thing I could do to help was buff then get the heck out of the way. It is gratifying to see a group that almost wins without your buffs be able to win with them, but disturbing that you can't do any more than that to help.

The fact that the only thing people want from shamans in the PoJ trials is buffs is symptomatic the loss in relative power in the class. I seriously doubt that LoY will rectify this. Perhaps the promise of additional priest class balancing by SOE will help out here.
