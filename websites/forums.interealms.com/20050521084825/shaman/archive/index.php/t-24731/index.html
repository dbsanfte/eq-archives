Again, none of the melee damage modifcations are applied to the "End Damage". Damage in EQ is split up in Damage Bonus and Damage Interval.

Damage Bonus is a fixed value(weapon or mob base damage). Damage Interval is another set value. The end damage calculation is a result of Damage Bonus + 1-20*Damage Interval. The number(1-20) the Damage Interval is multiplied with is random(like rolling a die), except that the aggressors ATK vs. the defender's AC heavily factor in there(closer to 1 for a high AC tank against a low ATK mob and closer to 20 for a low AC tank against a high ATK mob).

An example:

Assume a mob has a Damage Bonus of 200 and a Damage Interval of 100.
A mob will hit for 200+100(1*100) minimum and 200+2000(20*100) maximum.
Only the Damage Interval portion is affected by any damage modification effects.
In this example if you charmed a mob with above traits and cast Might on it the min and max damage would be modified like this:

200+105(1*100*1.05) for the minimum and 200+2100(20*100*1.05) for the maximum

Pet "weapon damage" can be calculated as follows:
(Max_Damage/Min_Damage)/1.9
(if you give a pet a weapon with more weapon damage than their innate ability, their damage will go up).

The pet's damage bonus is (Min_Damage - WeaponDamage/10).

Or, in other words
Min_Damage = Damage_Bonus + Weapon_Damage/10
Max_Damage = Damage_Bonus + 2* Weapon_Damage

So, take a pet that hits for 30 min and 106 max.

(106-30)/1.9 = 40

So, the pet has a 40 innate "weapon damage" and a 26 damage bonus.

Toss Champion on that pet, and the weapon damage goes up to 44.

We get a min damage of 30, and a max damage of 114.
