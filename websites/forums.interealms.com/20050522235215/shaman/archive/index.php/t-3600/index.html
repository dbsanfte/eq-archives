CrustyOldShaman
Shamen and druids don't stack well on raids, and as secondary healers their capability for healing on raids is inadequate. However, any significant increase in their ability to heal would make Clerics redundant in exp. groups. This is my idea for a solution to this.

It requires two components:

Firstly, a new spell - called 'Empathic Heal' - and secondly a new AA ability - called 'Healing Empathy'.

To use the AA skill 'Healing Empathy' you target another healer and click the hotbutton. Duration is until you perform another action.

The spell 'Empathic Heal' works as follows: by default it uses the same amount of mana as Complete Heal, but only heals about the same as Superior Heal (ie. its useless). However, with another healer focusing on you using 'Healing Empathy', you will heal for considerably more and for the same mana before. With two healers focusing on you, you will heal for pretty much the same as a Complete Heal.

The idea behind this design is:

1. None of the healers using the AA skill are using mana in the process, so a group of three could - by switching roles - all contribute effectively and all get to perform the main healing task. So shamen/druids will stack better.

2. It's not much use in an exp. group as it requires too large a proportion of healers in the group to be effective.

Well thats the idea. Does it stink? Does it form the basis for a better solution to the stated problem? Would anyone want this? Or am I just dredging up an idea that has already been dismissed?
