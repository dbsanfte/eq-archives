Gardeltron
Much has been said about priest healing, so I did some caclulations to compare the role that shaman slowing has on the amount of mana spent by the different classes. My model is relatively simple. Without going into the details on how I derived it, I used the following formula to calculate the average costs of healing:

Total mana spent = debuff costs + [(mana cost x mob dps x fraction slowed) / amount healed] * fight duration

The term in braces "[ ]" is the rate of mana expenditure on healing per second.

I used this equation to compare the incomplete heals of druids and shamans to the complete heal of clerics. In each case, the mana cost for the spells is 400. Without showing you the graphs (I'm no Thott), here's some conclusions I drew:

1. Because they heal more per cast, clerics are always more efficient than druids and shamans, as expected.

2. A shaman healing with slow initially spends much more mana than a druid healing without slow because the shaman incurs debuff costs (Malos + Turgurs = 650).

3. However, the longer the fight lasts, the more similar mana costs become. If the fight lasts long enough, a shaman will recoup the mana lost due to debuffing by having to cast fewer heals. Conversely, when mobs die quickly, shaman healing is extremely inefficient compared to other priests because the debuff costs are paid for every mob.

4. The time it takes for a shaman to recoup the mana lost on debuffing increases dramatically with slow mitigation. In fact, with sufficient degree of mitigation, a shaman cannot ever recoup this mana, so a druid will always be more efficient.

Here's an example of my calculations to give you a feel for what happens with slow mitigation. Assume a mob doing 100 dps, then we have the following mana costs:

Fight duration = 1 minute

Cleric mana cost = 320
Druid mana cost = 813
Shaman mana cost (no mitigation) = 957
Shaman mana cost (25% mitigation) = 1188
Shaman mana cost (50% mitigation) = 1419

Fight duration = 2 minutes

Cleric mana cost = 640
Druid mana cost = 1625
Shaman mana cost (no mitigation) = 1265
Shaman mana cost (25% mitigation) = 1726
Shaman mana cost (50% mitigation) = 2188

Although these are theoretical calculations, I think they are fairly conservative, since actual mana costs for all 3 classes will usually be much higher for a number of reasons. Essentially, mitigation removes us from being a viable healer in groups because we cannot keep up the the mana costs even with cannibalization. I noticed this when I first tried to keep mobs healed in Plane of Sky, and later in BoT.

The bottom line is this: shaman healing is extremely weak when we have to debuff mobs before each encounter. This weakness is compounded greatly with mitigation. It is most severe on mobs that have high dps but die relatively quickly. That is why PoP-type mobs, with high dps and low hp relative to older expansion mobs, have marginalized shaman healing and slowing ability.
