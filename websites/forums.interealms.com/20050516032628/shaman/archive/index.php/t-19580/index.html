xorekill
Short and sweet from a TZ shaman.

If, and I do mean if, you are serious about going to a PvP server, I would recommend you start a character on whichever server you choose, your options are as follows ...

Rallos Zek - From lvl 6 forward, anyone in a 4 level range to you ( 4 above, 4 below ) can kill you. If you die, they take all your coin and one item. Only exception is if the item is in a container.

Tallon, Vallon Zek - Pvp Teams...similar to Rallos, except the lvl range is increased to 8 ( 8 above, 8 below ), and only coins can be looted. You may only pvp against someone not on your "team"

Shorts - Halfings, Dwarves, Gnomes
Human - Humans, Erudites, Vah Shir, Barb, Frogs
Dark - Iksar, Troll, D. Elf, Ogre
Elf - High elves, Half Elves, Wood elves

However people on opposing teams CAN heal / buff one another, leading to instances where you must face out of range healers the same level as the person you are fighting, who you can NEVER do anything to. Most high end content is locked down by guilds composing of all four team members. On TZ, you WILL NOT be able to enter Time and not be immediately killed unless you are a member of the top guild Pandemonium. That guild does not accept ANY new members who do not meet strict guidelines ( including already being Time flagged ) and they prevent the progression of other guilds as well. And the rest of the guilds, by and large, do the same with everything below them.

Sullon Zek - Deity based teams, No pvp range...a lvl 65 can walk up to a lvl 6 and gut them, and there is nothing you can do about it.

If you MUST play on a pvp server, I would recommend not transferring, but starting fresh. In that way, you are not paying 75$ to find out you do not like pvp. It takes less than an hour to hit lvl 10 and begin participating.

Generally speaking, most pvp is won before it is began. How? By gear. Very very few people are new when they start on a red server, and most are severely twinked.

BST are effective in pvp, but only so long as their pet remains unrooted / mezzed / stunned / feared. Without your pet, you're a sub-par monk and shaman. Possibly the best classes for pvp are SK's, Necro's, or Bards. Shaman like myself, have a hard time killing due to the fact our nukes are no where near overpowering, and our dots can be easily cured away.

www.everquestlive.com has more detailed information about the red servers...but before you transfer, make an alt on the one you want and see for yourself.

Weyalan
Can only speak about Vallon Zek really.

On VZ, at the top end it is not really a "teams" server any more, as almost allt he top guilds are 4-team. It is more of a guild v guild server. There is one guild (defiant) that is top dog, and a handful of 2nd tier guilds (Aduentus, Ancient Dawn, Zephyros, Aerist). Then there are a whole lot of 3rd tier guilds...too many to name.

The 1st and 2nd tier guilds do a lot of mass PvP over control of zones.

At lower level, PvP tends to be more 1 on 1 or 2 on 1 or small grp pvp. Unfortunatley, at the lower levels, PvP is dominated by alt characters who are twinked up the wazoo. No matter what you do or how good you are, go up against somebody who has spent 10000000 plat on gearing up a low level twink and you are going to get owned! I have seen level 38 players with a "baron" tag and an epic - they have de-levelled just to "own" in PvP

The above may sound depressing, and certainly don't expect to come to a PvP server and not get very dead very fast a lot of times, but sometimes, just surviving PvP can be a lot of fun!

Also, there are zones that are kinda known for PvP and zones that it is very rare, so once you get to know where the action tends to be, you can basically pick how much PvP you want to do.

I am in a third tier guild at the moment, and do not PvP all that often. I get killed more than I kill, but I savour my victories, or even my narrow escapes. I don't get upset when I get beaten, and I wouldn't leave PvP servers now.

A couple of things to bear in mind.
Resists are super important on a PvP server. Many people carry around "resist gear" to swap in for PvP (which I think is a waste of time unless you are actively the aggressor, in most PvP, if you get attacked, you will not have time to do much gear swapping or you will be dead).

Instacast clickies are very important for PvP. You always put your "important" buffs at the back of your buff line-up, and if you are getting stripped of buffs (annul magic, etc) you need to be able to instacast "junk" buffs on the run.

In one on one PvP, Necros are hard to beat, as are bards. Shaman suck at killing anything, but are fairly hard to kill.

In group/mass PvP Shaman are much more poweful - Malo / Malos can be a real deciding factor. In mass PvP it is usually the Clerics, Chanters and Shaman who get targetted first (ouch)
Weyalan
You will, unfortunately, learn very little about a class up to level 10. You will, in particular, learn diddly squat about PvP to that level. In my opinion, no class starts to be defined until its mid 20s.

On Vallon Zek, PvP starts to get "balanced" around mid 30s - befoer that, the level difference (+/-8 levels to PvP) is just too much. But it doesn't take long to get to mid 30's these days.

It is difficult to give specific PvP tips because strategies depend on your class, your opponent's class, levels, gear, etc. A few general tips are:

Potions are your friend. Crimson potion (vendor) and Stinging wort (shaman alchemy) break mez. Damage shield potions are great against certain classes. If you aren't a snaring class, clicky snares are great for PvP and aren;t hard to farm.


Get used to checking who is in zone often - you will get to recognise names of players or guilds, and can prepare (for a shaman, PvP can be a very different spellset from PvE)

Don't get upset by PvP ever - there are griefers, immature idiots and FeFs who will try to get under your skin, will use lameass tactics, will train you, will do every stupid thing in the book, and then shout or /ooc afterwards. Its just a game, and don't let them have the satisfaction of seeing that they have got to you. Don't lower yourself to their level - if you get a rep as a dishonorable PvPer, life can get pretty limited. Enjoy the PvP but do it right and do it honorable (otherwise go to Sullon Zek haha)

Choose your guilds carefully. Guild reputation is important. You can get a bad rep just by association with a dishonorable guild that can stay with you even after you leave that guild.
