Me (55 shaman), a 54 warrior and a 62 cleric went to PoG to mess around and basically see how tough it was.

We were able to fight rather well there with just this group. Be sure to not let the mobs run when low on health, getting adds will suck since you have to (in our case at least) be done with all aggro when the PoG's respawn (22 min timer i believe).

What we did was zone in... aggroed the mob at zone in, killed it, waited for the pog's to attack us. I root parked one while slowed/dotted/pet the other and let the warr go to work. Repeat for the second PoG.

In the remaining time before PoG respawn we pulled to zone wall. Ended up getting wiped due to a runner, root parked adds that we couldent finish before the PoG respawn.

A few quick tips on PoG and named trees:

All of the named trees summon a particular type of mob to "aid them" when they are aggro'd.

There are four named trees.

Ail the Elder
RumbleRoot
Fayl SomethingorOther
Treah Greenroot(?)

Do NOT mess with Ail or RumbleRoot unless you have a powerful raid. Ail AE silences. RumbleRoot has a powerful AE dot. People kill them when they're clearing for Tunare, and otherwise generally avoid them.

Treah is easy. No AE, and he's slowable. Your small raid can probably take him, if you have at least two clerics. And you might not even need that. Heck, a 65 pally and shaman duo might be able to take him, although I wouldn't want to promise you that (and it'd take a loooong time, he has HP).

Fayl is similar, except that he's not slowable. Your raid can probably do him as well, with that good a tank.

Treah summons sylvan protectors. Fayl summons all the little wooden tree guys that generally wander east of the river (forget the names, it's been a while, hehe). Ah, the Entolling Whatzits, that was it.

They summon help when they are aggro'd. What this means: they shout for help, and all the mobs in the zone of that type RUN to the spot where the Tree was when it shouted. So, the trick is to aggro the Tree a substantial distance from where you want to fight it, and then lead it away quickly. If you have cleared a path first, you can pull the Tree away before any of its help gets close enough to actually aggro on you. If you get too far away during the pull, you'll have to reaggro the Tree, and it'll call for help again, leading all those mobs to run en masse to the new location. It's important to avoid that, or they'll end up close enough to possibly find your raid.
