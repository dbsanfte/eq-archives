Actually MacroQuest is so much more than just a macro programmer. Initially F-H used to make their own offset programs to do these hacks, but since MQ2 is so robust as to allow plugins, they decided to take advantage of MQ2 because it has the built-in ability to circumvent the "checks" that Sony has placed into EQ.

The "checks" watch for changes to specific offsets, for example the speed offset: there is a piece of code that checks the value at that memory offset and reports it to sony ever so often (30 second or something like that), MQ has functionality that takes care of this reporting so that you are not caught when modding the speed offset.

That is only a small example of the offset protection offered by MQ.

The reason you only see the small things on the FH forums such as speed, eb, fisheyes are because those are all that are released to the "level 1" people (people that are newly registered, or people that havent been able to contribute). Once you become a trusted member there are 3 more levels (level 2, level 3, and o.0), as you gain levels, access to the higher level boards open up. It is here you will find the bigger hacks such as changing your ID (so you can effectively take over other mobs and speak through them etc...) along with a multitude of other offsets deemed "to risky to be nerfed" and not shared with people lower on the access ladder.

No, i dont cheat in EQ (which things like this most certainly are) but I am a programmer by trade, and I am amazed by what the MQ community was able to do with EQ. Things like this interest me and therfore I am rather familiar with the project.
