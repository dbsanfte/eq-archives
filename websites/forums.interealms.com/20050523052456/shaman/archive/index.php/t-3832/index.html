Vorpatril
Bamana, I had the exact experience that you did. When I hit 60 I traded my fungi for Torpor and it took a while to adjust. While torpor is great, there are downsides that requires a change in tactics:

- Can't cast torpor while running. This prevents the tactic of running in a big circle while regening some health.

- Long cast time == interrupts. This has resulted in 2 deaths for me. I would get down to 40% health or so and start to cast torpor... a few interrupts later -> dead lizard.

- Fungi does not go OOM. This has only happened once to me so far, but the Torpor mana cost is something to be aware of.

- The snare of torpor basically commits you to stand and fight until it wears off ( or you click it off ). This can be bad if you are rooting some nasty, you torpor, root breaks and it comes to beat on you. Granted, you are being healed insanely, so the mob should not kill you.. but depending on your circumstance, the snare can be bad.


I never had mana problems while soloing anyway, so while soloing, torpor has not made a big difference. I mean for dull everyday grinding - not for uber mob fighting. I've found torpor most useful for:

- Uber mob fighting. Here I mean basically anything that can summon you and beat the snot out of you. Read any of 4 billion posts on this board to hear about how to solo these with torpor.

- Duo fighting. Where I have fallen in love with Torpor most is when fighting as a duo with my monk partner. Since he usually has aggro, I don't have to worry about interrupts or anything as I canni down and torpor up. Every few minutes I toss a torpor on him when he gets to 40% health or so. We can be fighting 3 or 4 mobs at once and I never get below 60% mana. It's so easy, it's kinda sad actually.

- Raids & AE mobs. Understandably, melee types do not appreciate being torpored in the middle of a big fight. But torpor is great for casters and healers in a fight with AE mobs. The additional healing helps the clerics concentrate on the CH rotation ( or just healing tanks ) and the heal over time function nicely cancels the DoT function of a lot of upper level AE attacks.

Some miscellaneous ways to use torpor:

- Preemptive torpor. A mob is inc and he's big and nasty and your job is to slow him. Cast torpor on yourself right before you cast slow. He rushes to beat on you, but torpor gives you some time for the tanks to get aggro back.

- Stopping that annoying puller who keeps pulling too much. Keep torpor on him and he can't pull so fast. ;)

- Healing folks who were just rezzed. Clerics seem to love to heal newly rezzed folks, which strikes me as inefficient. In a CR situation, I would assert that Torporing the newly rezzed is more mana efficient/time efficient for the raid as a whole than having the cleric use his mana to do it. Actually, I would extend that further. Given that we have canni + torpor, shamans are always going to have more mana than clerics. So it behooves a raid to use shaman mana in place of cleric mana whenever possible. Let them med up for rezes ( if not epic clerics ) or for buffs while you handle CR healing.
