Spell Awareness, or Spam Awareness as some guildees call it, has its good points and bad points.

Good: working on a new encounter? Not sure what you need to cure that ae/dot/etc with? It Helps. We learned a type of God mob was self hasting repeatly during fights and on pulls, so one of the toons that wasnt doing alot at the time, stayed on removing that haste as mobs came into camp while the other shms stayed on debuffs. Result - fewer ppl died. In short, this is going to solve encounters quicker for you, identify what classes mobs are quick, and reduce deaths.

BAD: The spam. You cant turn this off, its an active aa. Not a passive one like Health of Targets Target. This absolutely needs a toggle switch to turn it off for the group or for the individual. You dont need this ability on 24/7. There are times you need it, when you do, you do (think vp). And when you dont need it, the spam blows hard.

Juujuu
Get HOTT asap. It's beautiful. Absolutely the first Leader AA I recommend anyone get.
-You can target the mob you are killing and see right away who he's aggro on and whether they need the heal or not if you are assist healing on a boss mob or something.
-You can see the health level of mob in camp if you are healing anyone that's targetting the mob.
-You can see who's slacking and who isn't when raiding if you target various people to see how quickly or even IF they are targetting the trash mobs that are being brought to camp.

I've got /findpc as well. It's got it's uses.
-You have the same functionality of the golden path to the target as you have in the Bazaar or home town, but the person must be in your group.
-If you have framerate problems travelling and need to cut down your clipping plane, but know the landscape, you can findpc your groupmember and just follow the golden path and adjust it for the places you know you have to move a bit off path. Running through the trees in PoV is an example of this for me.
-If you raid the plane of time, it makes it incredibly easy to find where your group has run to for prepping for the next trial.
-If you party with people that perpetually get lost, this skill is wonderful.

I've got delegate main assist as well. It's ok, but not super amazing like HOTT. It's still very worthwhile to get though.
-It's the final word on whether you assisted the main tank or not. Can't argue with a targetting circle as long as the main assist stays on target.
-I've played an enchanter a bunch, and I know the pain of broken mezzes. For example, 2 mobs inc, you mez one the moment it its camp, broken mez, GREAT!, anger level rising, repeat. When you see the target ring, you know what NOT to mez to make things easier for yourself and for the group.

I've seen spell awareness. I really like it. It's the next thing I'm getting.
-You know exactly what spells NPCs are casting. You know can tell the class of an NPC mob right away on many spell casts (ie malo for example). You can tell what buffs NPCs cast.
-You know exactly what spells group members are casting. You can tell if someone is slacking by not casting any useful spells or whatever. You can see what spells someone is using if they are getting too much aggro, and if you know alternatives can possibly suggest something else.
-If you like being knowledgable of what's happening around you at all times, get this.

Our main raid leader has NPC health too. It's ok. It's fairly redundant with HOTT and targetting box, but still has some uses.
-If you have a mob damaged but many that are being CCed and not hurt so much, you can see which mob should be next to be killed.
-You can see the health of the main target mob even if you are off healing other people
-Its only 2 points, so don't expect anything super amazing....

I've seen mark NPC in use as well. It's useful for specific circumstances, but not many.
-Best use ever that I've seen is to mark your Charm Pet so when it breaks, it's super easy to retarget because he's got a permanent targetting ring.
-Other uses for it involve CCing multiple mobs on inc, and giving instructions on what to CC ahead of time.
-This will sort of pass for Delegate Main Assist if you like clicking your hotkey a lot.

I haven't ever seen Inspect Buffs in action. I can see it being useful knowing how long till certain buffs are dropping. Best use would be for FA.
Offense enhancement stacks with everything, but is pretty marginal benefit. It's like 9 attack per level.
Health enhancement is pretty marginal benefit. It's like 30 health per level. No big whoop when you have multiple thousand HPs and mobs hit for 400-1200 depending where you hunt.
Mana enhancement is pretty marginal too. It apparently gives you something like a 50 larger mana pool per level. *yawn*

The Leader AA stacks with all forms of +attack, so it shouldn't matter if they are near their cap or not.

As for the benefits of +9 attack per level of leader AA, it's pretty marginal.
Unfortunately I don't recall the exact numbers, but from what I remember reading, +10 attack will give a melee roughly 1% more damage output over their base damage.
This sounds fairly accurate to me considering I remember parsing avatar to do roughly 10% more damage (+100 attack).
I'm not online right now, but I think offense enhancement had 5? levels to it, which caps it out around +45 attack, or about 4-5% more melee output for your melee members in your group.

Personally, I don't think spending 4+5+6+7+8=30 leader AA for 4% more damage output of the group melees is worth it compared to the other abilities you can get.
