Roles.

I think every Shaman has their own perception of what their role is in a group. I feel that I am a versatile class. I can slow, buff, debuff, cc and heal.

Slowing: We have the best slow in the game. This is one of our bread and butter roles.

Buffing: Focus and Boar is a staple for most. Most of our buffs are saught after.

Debuff: For me, my general routine is: malos + slow on incomming mobs. Most times, I do not worry about having malosinia memmed if the group has high DPS. Sometimes I will have Cripple memmed if there is no chanter present. Cripple could be a lifesaver if a mob you are fighting has a nasty proc or hits hard. Getting that Dex and Str down is a must.

CC: Malos, Slow, and Root Park. I do this a lot in LDON adventures. If the mob cannot be rooted and is slowed, we can tank them with only Qui up - normal setting of course.

Healing: With out Healing AAs, we can be a decent healer. I never saw Shamans as a healer class, but once when I was asked to heal on a raid, I was amazed at the power we possessed. Granted, we cannot do it for long period of time, but we can get by with Qui and T Mending. There has been a few times I have grouped in Earth, and I was the Main Healer or another Shaman was the main healer. Qui is critical, and I would kill, beg or steal for that spell. If you thought your world changed with Canni V, welcome to Qui!

When I group, I am usually a slower / buffer / healer. If there is a chanter present, I usually ask them to slow for me, but this is not necessary. I hardly mem my nukes anymore. They take too long to cast, and the dps in groups I am in are usually high. Same goes for DoTs.

My normal spells I have memmed are:

FA
[Util]
Slow
[Util]
Malos
Regen
Heal
Qui


In the end, we have many possibilities to choose from. Some nights I am bored as hell, and some nights I am at the edge of my seat. Nothing is more exciting that getting a crit heal when the MT is at 4%. Well, exciting for you - not the MT :P

Try new things - if you need to work on your melee skills, ask the group if it is alright for you to work on them once your duties are met. OR, you can enjoy your free-time. Depends on what you want out of the group / game / etc.

Xev_Grammar
Lately I've been seeing my shaman's role as cleric/enchanter bodyguard.

True I malos/turgur mobs, and true I buff the group and add indirect dps with Ferine Avatar, but the most important part is that when an enchanter resists or a cleric gets agro, I slow the mob and heal the enchanter/cleric. Even if slow resists, at least I will be getting agro enough so that they will regain control of the situation... if I cannot get agro then at least I am buying enough time with heals for the tank to gain agro and peel it off the person in danger.

We have a unique crowd control ability aside from chanter/bard/necro mez: Virulent Paralysis 3 is the equivalent of pulling an add away from someone who has agro (can be person in danger or yourself), moving him several paces away, and rooting him with a low-chance-break root for two minutes, but without all the agro and damage. It saves so much time over trying to pull adds away and rooting them, and it saves your arse when you are slowing and they are getting in your face after resisting.

After completing those tasks, my role is tears of saryrn or velium strike or bane, and keeping ferine avatar/haste up on tanks and maybe pets.

If grouped with a druid healer, Quiescence at level 65 should help them with their job.

If not grouped with any healers, be glad that there is a beastlord doing the slows. Just assist with Malo (although probably not necessary since the enchanter is tashania-ing, unless you have nukers), like someone suggested, and keep people alive, helping with slowing and rooting when multiples come. Feel free to nuke and stuff, but the group will rely on you for the strong burst heals (ala Tnarg's) when things possibly get bad. No real need to overwrite 65% slow with 75% slow, since you are the main healer... unless you're struggling with mana. It would be a lot easier if you were 65 so that you could heal very well with Quiescence and not anger the melees with Torpor.
