Brohg
47%. Counting full 75% slow landed 10sec into a full 1min fight, Shaman need heals 47% as good as clerics to resist the damage from the same mob.

That 47% is the observable ratio for the heals we have, so (without confirmation) we believe that's where the number originally came from, since it would be a coincidence of monumental proportions if someone throwing darts got that consistent a number.

The balance issue is that the 47% number was arrived at before slow mitigation, and with much longer fights than is the current norm. Slow is, obviously, more powerful the longer it's on a mob. That % healing effectiveness hasn't been revisited since mitigation was introduced in PoP and exploded in GoD.

Bigsnack For'Mobs
Shaman healing in the past and the proposed changes are based on bad reasoning. The amount of healing a shaman can do should be set regardless of the amount of slow. Is a cleric or druid's healing overpowered because there is a slower in the group? Obviously not. But if a shaman is slowing and healing we are considered overpowered.

The proposed changes to adjust shaman healing based on the amount the shaman slows is both cumbersome to implement and continues the flawed reasoning. If there are 2 shaman in the group, should the healing shaman have enhanced heals if the other slows? What if a beastlord/chanter/bard slows? If one shaman slows and heals while the other does buffs and dps should that make a difference in the amount of heals the first shaman does? Of course there is no difference but this nonsense has been around since Kunark.

If shaman are to be the number 3 healer that's fine. Just pick a number and set heals accordingly. Trying to adjust for slow makes no sense in actual game play. Certainly not at higher levels where everything mitigates slow and all slowers are equal. At lower levels where everything is fully slowable it might have some sense but don't continue it past about level 50.

My personal feelings are that I have no interest in being a main healer and think it is a waste of shaman abilities to do so unless there are just no healers available for a group. I'm very comfortable with a support role in healing and would be more interested in a 1 second casting emergency heal around 1300 hps adjusted up for focus and crits.

I'm actually very pleased with where shaman are at currently. Although there are a number of tweaks needed, there is really only one major flaw and that is with the availablity of Nihil. Come on SoE, everyone knows, and you even have admitted, that GoD was a major fubar. Make Nihil or a comparable spell reasonably available for a 66 to 68 rune in OoW or to be purchased for crystals in DoN.
