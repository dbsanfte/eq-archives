Tordail Farstryder
Take a moment and read this page (http://home.cwru.edu/~srp5/Gate_Tiers.htm). That's the guide I used, and I was flagged for Kod'Taz, Ikkinz and Yxxta earlier this morning.

Some advice:
1. Get yourself geared in augged LDON/bazaar gear if you do not have access to elemental armor.

2. Max your healing AA's (HA, HG, AHA, AHG). Also be sure you have buff extension AA's ... sewer trials and Vxed/Tipt runs can make for a LONG night, and often there isn't time to constantly rebuff. I started doing sewer trials and Vxed/Tipt runs at around 150-175 AA. LR5 helps.

3. I'm still leery of pickup GOD groups, but that could just be my server ... I will urge you to take along a group of people who have worked together before and with whom you play consistently. GOD isn't really a place to go experimenting with new folks unless you're sure they're reliable. The slightest mistake will kill you and wipe your group.

4. If you are not at the top of your game or close to it that night, stay away from GOD. CR's are a pain.

Best advice I can give is go with people you have grouped with before and are comfortable with, and have fun.

Dont group with people who are skittish about risk, they will just make your experience there miserable.

Make sure that you carry an ample supply of invis potions with you even if you can cast invis. They are insta cast, and if cast when your current invis starts blinking you dont break invis, which is very important if you happen to be traveling through populated areas. I think I have died only once in GoD from invis dropping, and that was because I was too busy chatting to pay attention.

Keep an eye open for see invis mobs. Once you become familiar with the zones you are playing in you will know which these are and where they are found.
Grizlor
Definitely on the healing AAs. Also there's a couple things you need to know. Certain mob types have special abilities:

Noc (Purple guys with funky teeth): procs Chaos Claws, RAMPAGES. hits harder than other mobs in the area by 10% or so.

Kyv (Archers): procs Chaos Claws, has VERY high ATK. They usually see invis, and run really fast. In some areas they are unrootable.

Turepta (Spiky Turtles): Nothing too special really. High Defense.

Stonemite: Usually immune to root. Procs a long lasting DoT spell which you.

"Flying" Flies: Immune to root.

"Walking" Flies: nothing special really.

Goos: Nothing special.

Mastruq: Around 30%, they do this "Dark Aura" thing which is a self melee rune and absorbs 90% of all incoming melee damage. having someone to nuke these guys at the end is handy. Usually Warriors or Shamans, though they can be clerics too.

Ikaav (Snake things): These have an innate rune which absorbs 50% of all melee and nuke damage. It's annullable. A lot of people forget this for the DD in the first ikkinz trial. Annul Snakes! they also summon a single Ukun pet thing when you engage them, which will always have 50% slow mitigation, and hit for usually 500ish. If you keep the pet mezzed it just depops when the owner dies. Sometimes they procs a reflective aura which reflects nukes back at the caster. can be Wizard, Magician, Enchanter. Chanters will cast Anya's Quickening on anything in range :p

Ixt (Goat Women): Summons 2 pets when engaged, which will depop when mezzed. Procs a 200dd with a 2.0 sec stun on the tank.

Ukun (Dog things): Usually see invis, especially in Vxed, Tipt, and KodTaz+. Procs Chaos Claws, also can go into a frenzy which I think is AE ramp + a DoT on everyone near it when they're low health.

Ra'Tuk (Big tall dudes with axes): FLURRIES, procs Chaos Claws. Lots of HP. Enrages.

Cragbeast (Yellow Lizard things): Procs a DD/Stun on the tank. has a large hit box.

Pookas (Green winged dudes, in Vxed/Tipt only): Snarable, don't summon. Commonly charmed for nice DPS.

Golems: Unslowable in Yxxta and sewers, slowable elsewhere. I think these have high defense. nothing really special otherwise. Can be Warrior or Shaman.

Taelosian dudes (Ghost or otherwise): ghost ones are undead. Oftentimes are casters.

Aneuk (Dudes with the chains and the mace and one eye): Casters more often than not, usually shaman or cleric.

Hynid: in Vxed/Tipt they all see invis.

That's about it. if anyone wants to correct anything be my guest.
