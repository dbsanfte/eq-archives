I believe mitigation mitigates your slow by a percentage, rather than having a slow "cap". I could be totally wrong here, but I am just putting out what seems to make sense (or else everyone would just use their lowest slow, and BL's slow would be as effective as shamans on mitigating mobs).

So it would look like this: you cast a 75% slow, and the mob mitigates it by, say, 70%. your left with a slow of 22.5%.

Now if your little beastlord buddy were to slow the same mob with a 65% slow, and the mob mitigates by 70%, effectively, the beastlord would be landing a 19.5% slow.

I believe this is the reason why everyone complains about slow mitigation. On a non-mitigating mob we have a 10% differenceon our slow, though this is actually a misnomer... our slow landing at 75% makes the mob swing at 25% of their current attack speed. BL's 65% slow makes the mob swing at 35%. the difference between a mob swinging at 25 vs 35 is actually roughly 28.5%. that's a significant difference in damage output.

Now back to that mitigating mob...
Though the 3% difference in slows scales up just like the 10% did, its a HUGE difference. Ever learn the dramatic effects of compound interest? Well, lets just say with a mitigating mob, your interest is not compounding nearly as much.

Now you have the shaman slowed mob (slowed by 22.5%) swinging at 77.5%, and the BL mob (slowed by 19.5%) swinging at 80.5%. Believe it or not, this is only a difference of 3.8% in actual damge output.

To make a long story longer, lets say you all were fighting a mob that pumped out about 8,000 dps per minute (just pulling this number out of a hat here). If it was a non-mitigating mob, the difference between the shaman's slow and the bl's slow would be 2280hps of your MT, saved every minute.

However, on a mitigating mob, the same 8000 swinger slowed by a shaman instead of a beastlord is only hitting your tank for 304 less damage a minute. A VERY trivial amount.

Anyway, I believe that is how slow mitigation works, and why it really screws us in comparison to others who also have slow. If I messed up at any point in my math (or with my general assumption) please feel free to correct me (I am a troll after all).
