weissblau
I often have to go LFG on a raid, either when the raid is first forming or before/after a COH when the raid temporarily breaks up. I hate getting tells from random people for LDON groups or whatever while the LFG tag is up.

I played with the LFG window and set some filters, but they didn't seem to work. Last night I finally figured out why (I was setting the filters and then using /lfg on instead of clicking the LFG ON button). So since I finally got it right, I thought I'd share.

Here's the scoop.

1. Type /lfg to bring up the LFG window (don't use /lfg on).

2. Click "Additional Filters"

3. Click "Only include these guilds in my search results". Use the ADD button to add your guild or the guilds in your raiding alliance.

4. Click "Only players who match my posted filters can query me".

5. Close the additional filters dialog

6. Click the LFG ON button on the LFG window. NOTE: YOU CANNOT SIMPLY CLOSE THE LFG WINDOW AND USE THE /LFG ON. The filters will not apply if you use /lfg on.

I tested this last night with my non-guilded trader in the bazaar, and I could not see my character as LFG even though I had the LFG tag on at a raid.

I guess every guild has their own system for grouping up. In the past, raid leaders in my guild would create groups and spam the raid channel with the group leaders and who they should invite. This method took a LOT of time.

Yes, I know what class/level everyone in our guild is, with the exception of perhaps a few that have joined in the past couple of weeks. However, we always go LFG upon arriving at a raid site. The raid leader gives a raid invite to individuals, usually healers, as raid group leaders, and those individuals are responsible for forming their own BALANCED groups out of the LFG population.
