Gunderson
This isn't specifically a shaman issue but I'm a shaman and this is the board I call home.

Remember in pre-kunark Everquest, how cool it was that evils had to find a way around various obstacles due to faction, like the neat tunnel system in Freeport and such. Even in Kunark it had the matching good and evil towns with pros and cons to both sides. What ever happened to that part of the vision? it was a good part.

But in the last 2 years, mainly since PoP but it started before that, faction just doesn't matter anymore except for a few specific quests.

Back then I used to wonder what it was like to play an evil, it seemed like a whole different game. I can't imagine a young player wondering that now.

Does anyone else miss it being a big part of the game? I don't mean it should be an overwhelmingly big part of the game, but there should be some difference in the "lifestyles" of evils and goods in EQ and not just everyone hanging around all clubby in PoK like it's one big good/evil university campus.

Faction gave the game flavor, is what it comes down to, it made the game more of an immersive experience.

And yeah I"m not talking about PvP at all. I'm only talking about the main player versus monster game that most people play.

Mafia dudes live extremely well for guys who mostly have next to no education or skills, or even ability to get along in the world at all without resorting to brutishness and violence. New evil dictators and bosses of all types pop up all over the world every minute, and millions more wish they could be there too, doing the same thing. They often live quite well. Go to any workplace and see how often it's the one who kisses the most butt, spreads the most rumors, and rats out his fellows who gets the promotions and the raises.

Faction is stupid if it only works one way. The evils should have a more complete world of their own in games, just like in real life. I don't think we'll turn our children into satanists that way, but I do think we'll be more responsible about just throwing up pointless timesinks one after the other when EVERYBODY has to do them.
