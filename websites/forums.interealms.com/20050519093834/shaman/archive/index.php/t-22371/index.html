Gunderson
So often on this board we focus almost exclusively on the needs of high level shamans with an assumption that we're all 65 or close to it, I've been guilty of that myself.

But I recall when I was raising my shaman up over the last 2 and a half years that I found the mid-high levels pretty frustrating, there are some big holes in the spell books for characters in the 40s and 50s.

Specifically what I suggest Sony add is:

Group Talisman of Altuna
Group Harnessing of the Spirit
Group Stamina (as in the spell called Stamina)

The shaman is primarily a grouping class, at least that was my experience. Younger shamans need some solid group oriented spells, it's kind of ridiculous that shamans only really get major group oriented spells once they are in their late 50s when most of us had to group for 57 levels to get to that point.

By comparison, Druids (and I have a druid as well, this isn't druid bashing) get group versions of all their Skin spells including group skin like nature. And Clerics of course get group temperance etc.

Group Altuna, HoS and Stamina at level 50 or below seems only fair to help our struggling lower level brethren. It won't imbalance the game in any way, or take away from any other class, it simply will make things less of an unnecesary pain in the butt for them.

It will allow your level 50-57 or so Shaman to feel more useful and able to do the job that every other class in the raid/party assumes he can do. Most people were shocked when I told them how few group spells I got when I was that level.

I haven't mentioned spells below those because I haven't yet looked into what is needed, probably some even lower level group spells would help a lot as well without making things too easy or imbalancing things.

Thanks for listening. :)
