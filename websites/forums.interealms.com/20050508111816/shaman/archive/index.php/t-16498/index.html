Also the difficualty of the missions is directly related to the levels in your group. Most everyone knows this so far but not sure alot of people realise the affect one the difficualty. I think were people are running into alot of problems is that they are forming groups with alot of support classes that are 5-7 levels higher then the front line classes taking the brunt of the NPC contact. (AKA: Tanks/Meele) Having one person of a much different level does not ussually change the Average level (used to determin Mob Level) enough to make them harder to kill for the lower levels. However, one you get 2-3 people that are 4 levels higher then all the rest then the difficualty of the mission starts to increase by alot. This is most noticeable around the 55-63 level range. The problem is not so noticable when the groups are top heavy level wise but when the levels are Low level heavy.

Basicly be careful adding non-melee that are more then 4 levels higher then your highest tank. Other wise your tank is going be possibly fighting mobs that hit harder then they are able to handle.

One of the things I’ve discovered is there can be a huge difference in difficulty between camps. Thus far I’ve always grouped with the same 61 warrior and 61 necromancer. So half the party has remained the same. Five of my first six adventures were from the South Ro camp, fighting frogs, and the other was in Mistmoore. And my last two adventures were from the East Commons camp. Since I usually fight in Guk, that’s what I consider as average. During my one adventure in Mistmoore I found the mobs to be much harder and packed much closer together. And our last two adventures from East Commons have proved incredibly easy. The mobs were not as hard and they were spaced far enough apart that it was difficult to pull more than one or two at a time.

We’ve had levels as high as 65 and as low as 58 in our group and I can honestly say that I haven’t noticed any difference in the difficulties in our adventures based upon the makeup of our party. If there was an increase or decrease in difficulty it wasn’t great enough for me to notice.

The other factor that has made a great difference in our success is learning a different play style in the adventures. We no longer take the time to pacify mobs. We pull as soon as the last mob drops and we deal with however many adds we get. I’ve found that when we have party members that know how to play their characters we always do fine. We once had one mob escape and bring back friends. I think we had about nine mobs on us at one time, perhaps more. We each did our jobs, I slowed each one and helped with heals while three of them slowly beat on me, and not a single party member died. And I must say it was a huge rush.

However, we’ve also gotten party members that don’t know how to play in these adventures and they can really slow you down. Last night we had a ranger who only wanted to shoot his bow and would run from mobs. And we had a bard who refused to stop mezing the mobs even after being asked many times not to and to allow our pets to chew up the adds. Thankfully we were in Rukarkian Hills and the pulls were slow and easy. Otherwise I think we could have had some deaths.

I think these new adventures require people to learn to play their characters differently. And it requires them to play them well. If even one group member can’t carry his/her weight it can cause you to lose the mission and may cause deaths. Hopefully people will either learn how to play in these dungeons or decide not to enter them at all.
