timberbee
The Bandits of WK really should not be overlooked in the guide.

There are four camps in the series.
The first starts at the easternmost bandit farm, which is fairly much due east as you come out of the tunnel from Qeynos Hills.

From there proceed westward, and slightly north, there are two more bandits stationed by some Birch trees at the base of the hill,

occasionaly you will get 4, if you happen to hit a change of guard.

At the top of the hill is a Bandit encampment. Numbers are fairly random as they will occasionaly break off and Run to man certain spots in the wilderness or Run to the other bandit groups.

The third band is to the north, and westward, at the northern base of the hill, amidst some rather large trees.

There is no permanent encampment here, and, again, numbers can vary. It is on the Westward, downsloping side of the hill (gentle slope).

From there follow the bases of the hills south and west.
Where the bases of the east hill meets the base of the west hill follow that west ridge up,
over the crest and south westward will be a spot overlooking the Ogre shrine with a number of bandits upon it.


AT lvl 30 I no longer pick up aggro from the entire camp when attacking, except for the Brigands.
Those will aggro if you attack any of the bandits in their immediate vicinity.

This really increases the efficiency of the hunt.

with SoW, dcent faction (which you get from killing the bandits), and 6 10 slot, 1 8 slot and 1 6 slot bag I make about 190-210 pp per 2 hour session.

For me a session has ended when I get 54 sashes.
As there are three merchants just north east of the bandit farm, selling Bronze weapons, shields, etc is a breeze.


The spawns are fairly quick,
four camps in a row
a vendor close by
not a lot of people hunting

just comes down to an easy 90-100 pp per hour.
Best plat I've made at 30, by far...

East Karana has a few good cash sources.
Most obvious is the Spiders. Crag Spiders drop up to 8 Spider Silks but commonly about 3. Carrions drop 2 regularly and up to 4. Place holders for both are the Casm Crawler snakes which are easy to kill to keep the spawns running fast. I sell these for 4-5pp in the Baz and they are snapped up fast. Can rack up 100 silks in 4 hours if you push hard, netting you 1k in cold hard Plat for the effort
Hill Giants spawn here sometimes and not much need be said about them :)
Griffs of varying levels can be ok for cash and sometimes drop Gems.
A nice little NPC that i noticed recently is Theoris Bladespur. Spawns every 30mins or less (not exactly sure) Drops up to 6pp in cash and ALWAYS drops a FS Dagger and either a FS Sword or Rapier. Total Cash form him is upto 15pp. Cons LB to a 50 Ranger and is an easy kill. Cant be rooted or Snared though. BTW he is a Rogue so dont show him your back or you WILL know all about it. 221BS everytime he has snuck up on me :( He has a sister in South Karana, same class, same level, same loot.

Forages for here are Plains Pebbles which are slow sellers to Tradeskillers and Tea Leaves. These are used for Qeynos Afternoon Tea so they sell for a tidy profit.
Even better is if you can get Brewing to 200 (not hard or expensive to do) and make the Tea yourself. 1 Leaf makes 6 Teas and the going Rate on Karana is 15pp per Tea. I sell at 12pp and they go like Hot Cakes.

Hello everyone, I am new to the Glade and new to EQ in general, so please forgive me if this message seems presumptuous.

I thought I’d share what seems to me to be a fairly decent spot for us low-level types to make 25-40 plat an hour. The Grikbar Kobold camps in Steamfont are quick and easy for a 7-10 level Ranger to clear.

The drops you are looking for are Grikbar Kobold Fur and Kobold Teeth. Both stack and are worth around .85 and .5 of a PP each. In addition the Kobolds also drop various cloth armors and Spring Water worth around 1-2 gp each. These prices are from the druid ring merchant beside the camps.

A quick 10 minute cycle of the camp consistently produces 5-10 plat worth of fur and teeth and they appear to be on a fairly quick spawn cycle. Only the Shamans cause downtime.

I realize that 25 plat an hour is nothing to you exalted folks but for us newbies, a few hours there makes the difference between having leather armor and something quite nice from the Bazaar (and I’m enjoying the Swiftblade I bought with my ill-gotten loot as well).

Hope this helps someone else my level.
