LaughingWoman
The number I've heard thrown around most often is 30DPS per pet. Situationally, these are very handy. You're really not going to notice their DPS in normal *shudder* exp grinding. You'll call them, the mob will die a little early, and your dogs will stand there, useless, and poofing before the next pull.

When do these dogs shine?
1) Casters. Yes you can circle the dogs to cut down on push, but if you make sure to pile your dogs on the same side, that push can be used to help interrupt NPC casting. Talk to your MT, convince them to push in a circle (this is especially beneficial when partnering or in small groups).
2) Partnering / small groups. That extra damage makes a huge difference if you go for max recycle.
3) Adds. Those dogs can tank adds. They will ENRAGE, just like your trusty pooch. Use them to burn each mob down, or use them to distract an add for their duration. Just remember, if you want to get max use out of them, you can do it without killing the chanter, just root and poke, or root and sit - if you must.

Other points of interest - I've seen the dogs split up and go for different targets on occasion. I think it's just when one gets hit by said mob. The ONLY time they'll attack something that hasn't damaged you is when you first summon them. I have NEVER seen them break mez. If you have to cut and run for the zone, there is absolutely nothing (save for a defensive warrior, maybe) better at holding critters off you than this skill. That's three critters who have to be knocked all the way down, and then hold out through enrage, before they'll come after you. That's a lot of time to get to the zoneout and still not end up training anybody. ;)

They will attack whatever you have targetted, regardless of its current feelings towards you.

If you summon your pets and put them on mob1, while mob2 is rooted in the corner, and then mob1 dies before SC wears off, they will not attack mob2 if all it has been doing since they were summoned is stand in the corner. However, if mob2 hits you or casts on you, they will go after him as soon as mob1 is dead.

Basically it works just like your normal pet, except you can't control them. They will defend you when you are attacked, but you have to be in more danger than just being on the aggro list.

Short list of points I have learned about Spiritcall:

1) It is a great way to pull mobs when solo'ing and you know they will resist slow before Malo.

2) Their hate list is set up as..
a - The mob you had targeted upon activation of ability
b- Any mob that hits you/casts on you WHILE THEY ARE OUT.

3) Their target is determined as: Whichever mob is on their hate list and CLOSEST TO THEM, they will hit. (this one I am not 100% certain on yet, but have yet to see an exception)

4) /shield and Enrage are the 2 most useful things about the pets. But the damage is free and pretty nice too.

5) You can circle the mob to place pets in a manner that they won't push. Fortunately most raids you will then see the mob pushed by the PCs right over your pets to force them all onto one side anyway, then they will blame you for all further push.
