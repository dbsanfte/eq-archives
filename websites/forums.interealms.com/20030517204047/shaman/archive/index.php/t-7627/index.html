See my magelo.

Under profiles there's "wisdom shammy". that's my pottery outfit. You'll notice the epic is swapped out to make room for my pottery geerlok, so I had to make up the extra wis loss there.

Chipped bone bracer, polished bone bracer, velium jade veil, velium jade rings, golden effriti boots, acrylia studded cloak, foreman's tunic, or acrylia tunic, at one point I was using soiled breechcloth for legs for the tradeskill suit, jade earrings, black alloy medallion for the neck, totemic helm, eye of the griffon for range.

Remember bearform gives you 5 wis. Greater bearform gives you 10. Stat food - I use misty thicket picnic and queynos afternoon tea- give you 10 wis. Just don't eat them :). (I stuff myself while I tradeskill). So my wis outfit only needs to go up to 235, greater bearform and stat foods do the rest to get me to 255. I prefer to get 255 without KEI, as it means I'm not wasting kei trying to do endless clicks on wu's, but if you're starting out, remember kei boosts you 25 wis.

Good luck tradeskilling. I'm on my 5th shawl, working on my 6th, all tradeskills are over 150 except smithing (133), 180 in alchemy, 222 in pottery, and I just trivialed wu's in tailoring.

The amount you spend in your wis suit pays off when the cost of a combine goes 80 plat or more... which it does, fast, especially in tailoring.
