The reason archery isn't viable to most people as a skill before 60 is because they don't focus on it. If you give up your nice melee stuff for +dex gear and pass on expensive weapons and armor and save your money up for a DN bow or one of similar quality, then archery will surprise you with it's combat effectiveness. But the common assumption in EQ is that it's a secondary skill, and that's how the treat it.
That being said, there's a serious lack of good dex stuff and good bows out there for lower level rangers. But especially starting at 51 (double damage on non-rooted, non-moving mobs) it truly does become a viable alternative to melee fighting in over 80% of your encounters.

Well, if archery is the only overriding reason for you to be a ranger over a paladin, then you probably want to be a paladin. For the most part, bows are used the same (i.e pulling mobs) for both classes until rangers get trueshot disc at 55. Other than the trueshot disc, the only real difference is that rangers get crit shots with archery -- which don't amount to that much more damage pre-50. Granted a ranger can bow-kite mobs much better using snare, most rangers prefer to solo using fear-kiting methods.

The things that sold me on a ranger vs paladin are dual-wield, SoW, and tracking -- and I have no regrets whatsoever. I prefer to be more of an offensive fighter vs a defensive one. On the flip side, paladins get better AC, Hitpoints, heal spells (and rez later on), LoH, and spells/weapons geared towards undead mobs.
