Actually, dispite the complaining that VI does, EQW is NOT against the EULA. EQW is not a hack and does not alter the game itself or any of it's functions or calculations, and it does not capture or trojan any information. It's simply a video utility that allows you use EQ in a window instead of full screen. It's not any different then the newer video cards and drivers that let you have 2 or more monitors active and switch between them. It just lets you do it with ONE monitor/videocard instead of having to invest in all the extra equipment. The reason VI complains so much is because they are afraid some hacker is going to take the EQW engine and design something that DOES start breaking the EULA, and they won't be able to block or track it.
