BlargX
Unfortunately, you can't really "learn" judgment. Not in 3 hours or 300.

You can, if you are reasonably alert and creative, learn to juggle priorities and more consistently make the best choices under pressure. But for anyone, that's going to take being exposed to lots of different situations, enough so that one after another of the "set" strategies people tend to adopt and never veer from get proven deficient, and they have to think through how to adapt -- or find someone else in their group or raid to blame, perhaps? I think we've all seen that plenty of times.

So sure, firing off a spell is a no-brainer, but knowing when and how to do it best isn't, and it's often easy to tell who is good at their class, who sucks, and who is REALLY good. And who cracks under pressure and who doesn't.

EQ is full of people who think they're fantastic players. But the percentage who are, even after level 60, is smaller than one would think. Sometimes excessive pride is even a short-cut to telling you who to watch out for.
