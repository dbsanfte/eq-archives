First off I wanna say Hi! I have been reading these boards for awhile now to get my little 54 shammy into shape. One problem I had though is that my computer seemed to freeze every 30 minutes or so, and I also seemed to get data corruption in my files ( if I do a full file check it comes up with a plethora of files ).

After getting severely pissed off last night and pondering the idea of buying a new system I decided to try and find the problem myself. So the first thing I decided to do was look for a way to check the validity of my memory. I went to http://www.memtest86.com/ and downloaded the tester... loaded it on a floppy and reboot my system.

HOLY CRAP!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

Lets just say my little pc2100 512m stick failed, and it failed ultra hard!

The problem is in cheap memory ( which I admit I bought ) .. seems that when the computer is writing all those 1's and 0's the bank next to it can sometimes inherit the new data ( bad bad bad bad ). So I had another old stick of pc2100 256m laying around and I popped it in... tested it and got 0 errors. Played EQ and Star Wars Jedi Knight for hours last night and not a single crash! Bad part is I lost 256m of memory, but the good thing is im not freaking crashing anymore.

Anyway just posting this because im a happy camper now, and im hoping I can help some other goofs like me who thought they could save an extra penny on their memory. Oh also I have read that the memory may not be bad in all systems, but I have an Athlon and they are notoriously bad about system noise... so dont throw it away or anything till you can try it in another system.



That site for those that didnt wanna read all that is http://www.memtest86.com/ ... all you need is a floppy disk ( or cdrom ) and your good to go.

EDIT: Also does anyone know of any good moderately priced premium memory? I wanna buy Crucial, but a 512m stick of pc2100 is like $89... kinda steep for broke mofos like me.
