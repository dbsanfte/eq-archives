ciccero
I just *need* to have little **POW!** and **KABAAM!** messages popping up in bright colors all around me.

Jenavyra, you can EASILY do this with /bind commands.

Take whatever the name of your power is and bind it to a keyboard, or hotkey macro.

e.g.

/bind 1 "powexec_name Jab$$local BAM!!"

/bind 2 "powexec_name Slash$$local **KAPLOWIE!!**"

/bind 1 "powexec_slot 1$$local **POW!!**"

/bind 2 "powexec_slot 2$$local **KABAAM!**"

and the interface supports a lot more than that.

I have a lot of neat things like that in my setup.


/bind f "powexec_name Sprint$$powexec_name Hover"
-- this sets my keyboard so that hitting the "F" key will switch me between hover and sprint and ensure only one is running at a time... when sprint comes on, it switches hover off, and vice versa - to help me save endurance, and I can do it with one key.

I have my mez and heal buttons set up similarly.

2 is my mez hotkey.
/bind 2 "powexec_name Mesmerize$$team MEZZING --[ $target ]-- HANDS OFF"

/bind 0 "powexec_name Healing Aura$$team INCOMING HEAL, GET CLOSE"

macros work roughly the same, except you have to name the macro instead of binding it to a key on the keyboard.

/macro Taunt "powexec_name Taunt$$team TAUNTING [ $target ]"
ciccero
You can also use /bind and macro in your hotkeys or keybinds to change your text size, color, text bubble colors, etc.

Some folks have some super-complex binds... where they actually have a key bound to use accuracy or healing inspirations if they have any - just by the keyboard key. That's very useful for considering them as potions and setting up a key to use that specific type of potion if you happen to have one. They have them set up to use whichever potion is highest strength first until exhausted, then look for the next tier of heal potion, or whatever...

On the issue of enhancements here is how they work.

Enhancement come in three types Training, Dual Origins, and Single Origins.

Training are useable by all 5 origins. (Magic, Tech, Science, Mutant, or Natural.)

Dual Origins are shared by 2 of the above types and Single Origins are specific to one type.

Training Enhancement give 12% to 15% bump in a powers effectiveness. Dual origins give ~25% and single origins give ~ 33%. (These percents may vary a great deal based on the focus of the enhancement. Cone area increase might have a different bonus progression than healing enhancements.)

Each enhancement has a level value. This determine whether a character can benefit from that enhancement and to what degree. Enhancements can by upgraded by combining them with an identical enhancement. This is reflect by placing a '+' symbol after the level indicator for an enhancement. Only 2 upgrades can be made to an enhancement so at best a level 10 enhancement can become a 10++.

A 10++ enhancement is identical to a 12th level enhancement. A 14th level enhancement is identical to a 13+ of the same type.

To gain the benefit of an enhancement you must be within 4 levels of it below you and 3 level if it is higher than you. So a 12th level character can use 8 to 15 level enhancement.

I have a SO damage enhancement and a DO damage enhancement on my Frost Sword power. Without enhancements it hits in the low 30 range bit with these enhancements it strikes in the low 50 range.

The right enhancement makes all the difference in the owrld on your powers.

Warcry is wrong then. According to the Dev in charge of how powers and enhancements work, the progression is:
Training: 5 or 8.3%
DO: 10 or 16.7%
SO: 20 or 33.3%

It depends on Type. Out of the 28 kinds of enhancements, only Range, Cone Range, Damage Resistance, and Defense Buff use the lower progression.

Now, the way that some enhancements work, those percentages are not as obvious, especially when you multi-slot the same kind of enhancement. Reductions (recycle time, endurance use) are on a diminishing returns scale...ie: You cannot put 3 SO endurance reductions into something and use that power for free.
