Shandy Orcbane
Shandy's Rules of Spell sticking:

Future Imperative Rule
If it absolutely positively MUST, it will... next time.

Assurance Clause
If you didn't mal* or the enchanter didn't tash because "these are wimpy," it won't.

Associative Property
If you're raiding a mob with a higher level shaman, and it cons blue to him, red to you, his will bounce, yours will stick... but only after his bounced.

Law of Diminishing Returns
If you're raiding with a shaman of lower level, your spell won't stick until theirs has.

Axiom of Primacy of Lesser Effect
Grouped with somebody that can do what you can, only not as well? Yours will bounce, theirs will stick... and then yours will stick.

Advanced Tenet of Lesser Efficacy
After tiring of having their slows, etc, overwritten by you, your companions will stop slowing, and debuffing. Your next cast will then be resisted, causing you to be summoned and squooshed.

I think those are the primary ones.

I'd agree with 55+ for HoT. If you have a 60 shaman to cast malo, and an enchanter to tash, you may be able to help with slows around 52-53. However, you'd get hit hard by AE and overall you'd probably be more hindrance than help.

After 55 you'll have some chance, malosini will give you a boost once you get that. A lot also depends on who else is along - got a bard to sing resist songs and ooc? A mage to malosini? Chanter to tash? If so, then you will do much better and could maybe help sooner. If you have none of those you'll not be much use until 58 or so.

60, malo, and torpor are the real breakthroughs for HoT. With malo slowing is sooo much easier, and with torpor you can keep yourself and the other casters alive from AE effects.
