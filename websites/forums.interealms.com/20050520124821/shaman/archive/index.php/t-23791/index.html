The argument is that certain races get benefits that enable them to survive better, and therefore die less, and therefore level faster. To keep things balanced, these races should pay an exp penalty.

But there are plenty of inherent racial benefits, all of which can be utilized to level more quickly. Why is it that some of them are singled out for the exp penalty?

Halflings get innate sneak/hide: which means they can avoid dying in certain places, and therefore they can level faster: and they pay no exp penalty for this.

Wood elfs get innate forage: which means they can gather items to sell to other players, make money earlier than other races, and buy better armor sooner, and therefore they can level faster: and they pay no exp penalty for this.

Humans get good faction most places: which means that the guards are more likely to step in and save them from dying, and therefore they can level faster: and they pay no exp penalty for this.

Vah shir get innate safe fall: which means that they can jump off walls and escape from mobs in certain places, and therefore avoid dying, and therefore they can level faster: and they pay no exp penalty for this.

In addition: twinks get lots of regen, armor, great weapons, and they can level up incredibly fast compared to any genuinely new player: and they pay no exp penalty for this.

In addition: All races now get improved regen while sitting. All races now get improved bind wound (while seated, to 70 pct of health). So the advantage that 2 extra regen gives you is a smaller advantage than it used to be.

So why is it, exactly, that various races get certain advantages, and they should not pay any exp penalty; but other races get other advantages, and they should pay an exp penalty?

It is just bogus to say, "When the game started five years ago, the racial exp penalty was the rule: so live with it."

Because: the game has changed 1000 other rules in the meantime. The rules in this game change with almost every patch. Because the reason for the racial exp penalty rule is no longer valid, it's one of the rules that should be changed.

If you disagree with that -- if you think the reason for the exp penalty rule is still valid -- then you have to say why. And not simply point out that it is an old rule.
