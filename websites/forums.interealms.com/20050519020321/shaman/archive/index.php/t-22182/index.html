Thorzion
I firmly believe that there is a set of hard coded agro numbers. You brought up cripple, which is a prime example; no counters, but nearly the agro of slow.

Ive always thought that agro on dots was calculated in the following way:

DoT is cast. A set agro is generated from a successful landing of the DoT spell; this agro is simmilar to the agro generated by a direct damage spell. After successfull landing agro is calculated, a measure of the counter agro is assessed. We know that poison counters generate much more agro than disease counters (I believe hard coded to be), with curse (magic) counters not far behind (explains cripple/slow agro effects).

All that in the first tic of the DoT. Then, the next tic is counted. The same rules apply as the initial tic, so the landing agro is calculated, and then the cumulative agro from the counter type is added again. Have you ever had a mob ignore you on the first tic of a dot but glue to you on the second (and subsequent) tics? I have, and this is the only way that I can make sense of it.

Third tic: landing agro is assessed AGAIN. Counter agro is assessed AGAIN. At this point, the agro you generate is moving in an exponential curve, simmilar to the following pattern:

Successfully landing the DoT-----L+X where L is the landing agro
Tic 2 of the DoT----------------L+(X*X)
Tic 3 of the DoT----------------L+(X*X)*X
Tic 4 of the DoT----------------L+[(X*X)*X]*X

You can see where it goes from there. In effect, you are multiplying the entire agro schedule by itself every time the DoT runs an additional tic.

So...if this is the case, why do disease DoT's generate so much less agro than poison/curse DoT's? Well, a few years ago SOE removed most of the agro from disease counters. There is still a healthy bit of agro generated by disease countes as evidenced by slow agro, though this could be easily explained by postulating that specific agro numbers were added to direct slows, but not to all spells that added disease counters.

In this way, disease dots calculate the landing agro every tic, but its simmilar to DD landing agro and generates an exponentially smaller amount of agro than the other DoT types. (Fire DoT's seem to follow a different set of agro rules. The level 1 (level 9 ranger) spell Flame lick generates a healthy amount of agro; it generated ehough agro to be used in tier 2 planes by a ranger playing "rabbit". Yet subsequent fire dots generate what seems to be much lower agro. I have been in groups where necros have let 2 Pyre's (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=2885&source=Test) run, and yet I grab agro after the second tic (only 640 damage) of BoS) Why?

This is all pure conjecture of course, because SOE will not release hard data as to what happens agro-wise when a DoT is landed, mainly because they are unsure of what is happening themselves. Rytan has mentioned in numerous occasions that DoT agro is broken and that he does not know where it is broken...one of the pitfalls of having one person entirely responsible for the spell system.
