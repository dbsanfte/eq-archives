Chutoi
Chances are good your MT already has defensive on for flurry mobs anyway. I'm not sure if anything under 60 flurrys (honestly can't remember). Flurries are usually 4-6 hits by the mob, I think. As you can imagine, if they hit for 400+ damage . . . ow.

Cripple has been said to reduce the number of flurries. In my experience, I'd agree--crippled mobs do seem to flurry less--but I haven't really done any tests on it to find out.

Flurries appear to have a chance of happening randomly on the mobs attack--sometimes you'll see 2 or 3 in a row, and sometimes it won't flurry for a while. Obviously, if the mob is slow, it also can't flurry as often as it has to attack to have a chance of flurrying.

If you can, always cripple and slow a flurry mob. One minor note, I'm sure you noticed that cripple taunts pretty well, too! In raid situations, you may want to have the non-tashing enchanters cast cripple to help spread out the agro more.

Enrage typically only show up when the mob is around 10% hp left. It lasts like 15 seconds or so, and if your tanks in front of the mob don't turn off attack, they will probably get owned. This is especially true for 60 warriors and monks. Can we say triple attack, hasted, with a 12/17 weapon? Auto-riposte hitting them back at 400 an attack will kill the tanks in moments. Most tanks are aware of the problem, and know to watch for it and turn attack off. Lag is the problem with enrage. People can die before they even see the message if they are lagged. I don't believe there is any way to stop enrage. Just stop melee in front of the mob for 15 seconds--spell casting isn't affected.

Rampage--there seems to be 2 or more types of rampage. Some of the luclin mobs seem to do it differently. Controlling it is a royal pain in the ass. The two types seem to be:

(a) the highest people on the hate list are hit when the mob rampages (rampages are random)--may be top 2 or 4 people--almost always top 2. One of the people is out of range, it picks the next highest on the hate list (only summons the highest person on the hate list usually).

(b) the first people on the hate list (in range). Some of the mobs seem to remember who hit it first, and rampage on those people. As you can imagine, this may be a problem as bard songs and healing can get your bards and clerics up on the hate list early.

Until you learn how a particular mob rampages, make sure to identify the rampage tank early and keep them alive. Have them announce in /gu "I HAVE RAMPAGE" and keep them alive at all costs unless they're a gnome. You might have a cleric in the heal loop as the next rampage target, so it's best not to let the rampage targets change through death or out of range problems. If you get a wizzie or something with REALLY low HP, you may need to let them die (serves em right for attacking to fast anyway!). I don't think any debuffs reduce the chance of rampage.

Bezerk. I think only tanks go bezerk, and only when below 40% hp. They get a small strength bonus, and hit more often, and also have a chance to critical hit (up to 4x damage I believe). Not positive about the bezerk numbers as I don't play a tank class very often! :)

Hope that helps!

Flurry - mob gets 2 additional attacks that round.
