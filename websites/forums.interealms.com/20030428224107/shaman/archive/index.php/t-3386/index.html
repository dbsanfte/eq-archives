Well, there are actually 2 ways to increase your EFFECTIVE alchemy past 180.

1. Philospher's stone - adds 10% to alchemy. 180+18 = 198. Assuming this effect actually works (there was a patch that indicated once it had been fixed), you should do better on high level potions. I haven't noticed a big effect on Luclin potions, but hey, I don't have that kind of pp to test it.

2. Alchemy mastery - this is one of the "stellar" shaman AA skills. It has 3 levels, each of which increases the probability of making a successful potion. Supposedly this is how to "trivialize" making Luclin potions. However, AC1 costs 3 AA points, AC2 requires 6 AA points, and AC3 requires a whopping 9 AA points. I know of no one who had put any AA points into this complete waste of time skill. The bottom line is, who cares if you can make 10 dose clarity potions for 1600pp instead of 2400pp?

So you want ubah alchemy skills? Then head out to Greig's End for you philospher's stone, then spend the next 6 months compiling AA points. You'll be the envy (or more likely pity) of your guild.
