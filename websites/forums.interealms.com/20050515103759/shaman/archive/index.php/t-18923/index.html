To hear a lot of people talk on this forum, you would think that shamans are one of the worst classes in EQ. You'd think we were gimps who can do nothing except slow, and we are almost eclipsed there by enchanters and beastlords. This simply is not the case. Shamans have been and still are a very powerful class who can use that power in just about any situation.

That being said, I still think we need a little tweaking to make us better. We need something that makes us more adept at slowing. Right now, our only advantage over an ench is that our slow casts faster. The 5% difference does not matter one bit in an exp group (although the marginal difference can be the difference between life and death on a raid). As of right now, one of our current GoD spells gives us a fast casting 55% slow with a -30MR mod. I think this spell is an excellent idea and we should have gotten it a long time ago.

I also think we need an upgraded poison nuke that casts just as fast as spear of torment, but does the damage of velium strike. The long cast time of velium strike makes it almost unusable in ldon groups, and spear of torment just does crappy damage.

Dots need to be tweaked so that they land easier (in pvp and pve), and they need to generate less aggro.

We need some form of group heal. I'd be very happy with a group quiescence AA that we could cast every 15 minutes or so, and MGB.

We need more atk buffs. I feel that shamans should be the master of melee buffing, and enchanters should be the masters of caster buffs. However, with both the best haste and the best mana regen buffs in the game, enchanter takes the #1 slot of both. It would be unfair to enchanters to, at this point in the game, switch haste lines with them, so I think we need some form of atk buff that stacks with avatar (bih'li doesn't count). Also, if endurance regen is as important for melees and voq is for casters, and shamans get the best endurance regen buffs, I'd be happy there.

Do that, and shamans would be perfect.

Anyways, as for why I want it. With an 18% detrimental haste item (not the best, but better than most people have) my turgur's is 2.5. With BoR it is 2.2. I'd guess with the best haste item, you could get turgur's down to a 2 second cast time. Remember, though, you don't always have blessing of reverence. You usually do, but not always.

A 1.5 second cast time is much better than a 2.2. Faster spells channel easier than slower spells, obviously, but a spell this fast is exponentially better because you can cast it in between swings, making it much much easier to get off if you are being hit.

The resist check is also nice. It isn't a huge modifier that makes it near unresistable, but -30 is noticeable. For comparison, it's the same resist check as on greater immobilize, and I know that spell has a better resist rate than turgur's does, although it isn't a huge difference.

So basically, we have a fast casting slow that lands easier than turgur's. Unfortunately, we can only use this slow every 30 seconds, and it barely lasts that long. In addition to that, it is a noticeable inferior slow to turgur's.

Why would I want this? Backup. As we all know, turgur's has a short but annoying recast time. If you bounce a turgur's, you have to stand there for 6 second while you wait for it to refresh. During these 6 seconds, chances are pretty good that you are getting smacked by whatever resisted your slow. Once turgur's finally does refresh, you've got to channel it through these hits. If your slow bounced once, then the mob probably wasn't malos'd, and malos is fairly difficult to channel through an unslowed mob. Thus, your chances of landing turgur's on the second try is the same as the first. So, you've got a reasonable chance of getting resisted again and having to wait another 6 seconds to try again.

When I get a resisted slow and aggro, I usually hit VP (assuming I got aggro). This, of course, knocks to mob off of me and roots it, leaving it to be debuffed at my convenience. I can then go back to slowing other mobs which are currently loose and attacking my group. Of course, VP has a 2 minute recast time, so I can only realistically use it once per pull. If I get aggro again while VP is down, or VP gets resisted, I just have to deal with it and try to channel a slow.

This is where the new GoD slow comes in. I definately would not be using it as a replacement to turgur's, but rather as a supplement to it. I'd use Ancient Balance as my backup slow when turgur's bounced. Turgur's gets resisted, and instead of waiting 6 seconds to try to channel it, I have another slow that is ready to be cast, is much easier to channel, and has a significantly better chance of landing. It'd look something like this:

You begin casting Turgur's Insects.
Your target resisted the Turgur's Insects spell.
Ldon_hard_mob_01 hits YOU for 980
Ldon_hard_mob_01 hits YOU for 980
You begin casting Ancient Balance.
Ldon_hard_mob_01 yawns.
You begin casting Turgur's Insects.
Your Ancient Balance spell has worn off.
Ldon_hard_mob_01 yawns.

The 30 second recast isn't a very big deal, because this would be something you'd use in an emergency to save your own ass, basically like VP. Like VP, though, it has other uses besides self preservation. You can use it to slow the mob that is attacking the cleric, or to quickly snag aggro off of someone. If there's two mobs inc, you can turgur's one, balance the other, then go back and turgur's the second - all with no time spent waiting for turgur's to refresh.

I don't see anything bad about this spell, except that it will take up an already valuable spellslot.

We used to be the ultimate melee buffer/debuffer. Give me back that role and I'll be happy. Perhaps take some of the high end gear effects and give them as spells. Things like strikethrough, combat effects, or accuracy. Let my spells have a noticable impact on a melee class's dps, mitigation, and avoidance. Maybe a spell line to increase melee damage by a set % or aids dodge rolls. There are LOTS of ways to give us buffs that make a difference. Same thing goes for debuffs. If I can positively affect an attribute for a player, I should be able to negatively affect that same attribute for a mob. If I can make a player hit more, for example, I should be able to make a mob miss more. Maybe something like a reverse Bulwark of Vie--cast it on the mob and every hit from that mob has 10% of the damage taken off. Currently, the only melee debuffs that do much of anything are slows. There's a huge range of territory here that we could expand into.

On a seperate note, a lot of our power has been diminished simply by the speed at which mobs die in a normal xp group. At this point, we're not lacking in high numbers for total spell damage. We're lacking a way of dealing signifigant damage in short fights. Our most damaging spell, Breath of Ultor, has a higher base damage than any wizard nuke, including Strike of Solusek. Exactly 666 more than SoS, interestingly enough. The problem is that the fights are too fast for ANY of our powerful dots to be of use. If an average xp group's fights were lasting 2-4 minutes instead of 30-45 seconds our damage output would be a lot more noticable. Of course, if we started tossing Ultor, Bane, and maybe Saryrn on a mob, we'd pull agro off even the best tank. Some way to allow us to use our spells effectively would be necessary. An on-demand agro debuff would give us a way to do this while still being able to gain agro for root-parking or off-tanking. A hate modifier for our dot's would accomplish the same thing, whether built into the spell or given as a Spell Casting Subtlety style AA specifically for dots.

All in all, I think we're all just asking to be given back a concrete role to play in groups and raids. Something that we can point at and say "this is what I do well". If that role is to be support, then make my support actually do something.

I actually am posting in spite of myself—and it’s gonna be a LONG post. These threads tend to be excruciatingly painful to read, mostly because the people posting have no clue what they’re talking about. (No doubt some will immediately point fingers at me.)
My bonafides, such as they are—my main is a 65 shaman with 557 AAs. I’m the raid leader of a time enabled guild, and yes, we have cleared time in under 6 hours. What does that mean? Besides the fact that I’ve played a shaman for a good long while and that I’ve had to memorize most of the abilities of other classes, not much. However, it does mean that I have had to play almost every class out there at some point. Those on which I have spent the most time are druids, enchanters, clerics, warriors, monks, rangers and wizards. I’ve spent the least time playing beastlords. (For the class of trolls who can’t refute something so resort to insults—yes, I have a life, a girlfriend who is entirely too good for me, a job, and I do exercise. )

First, I’m going to take a moment to be elitist—one of the earlier posters was exactly right. Shaman are comparatively very powerful at level 60 (compared to other classes). HOWEVER, from levels 61-65, especially with the inclusion of AA abilities, the other classes take a major jump in power. Shaman do not. Shaman are very static in power from 61-65. That leaves them noticeably behind the curve on balance. I am going to be talking about end game shaman (level 65). If haven’t played a level 65 shaman, and aren’t correcting something I say about a class other than a shaman, you don’t have any business replying. My response to you is, “talk to me when you’ve experienced it.” Shaman have not had a significant upgrade since Kunark.

Shaman are not hybrids. We are a priest class. Originally, near as I can tell, our role was to be sort of a battle priest. We trailed slightly in healing, but had higher melee caps. That was useful up until about level 50ish when mobs started to beat the tar out of us.

Fast forward to today—clerics now out melee shaman at the end game. Out of curiosity, I dueled a cleric (weapons only). I was using a 1hs 23 del, 32 damage with a 450dd proc (-350 resist check on DR). The cleric was using his summoned hammer. Same hp, same haste on both characters, close to the same AC (I was higher!). The cleric won. Clerics get the slam ability too. Only large race shaman get slam—and it caps at a skill of (1)—or maybe (0). I forget. Other shaman have no ability or spells to stun a mob. I think I can safely say the ‘vision’ for shaman no longer is that of a battle priest. What about the rabid bear AA, some may ask. If you spend the AA on it, and the max AA to hasten it, you get 5 minutes of better melee every hour and 20 something minutes.

Let’s look at nukes a sec. Shaman have 2 types of direct damage (dd) spells. One is a rain which pisses of chanters and gets resists you have to use to believe. After malos, I usually get at leave 1 of the 3 waves resisted. While I’m mentioning it, why is it that it hits FOUR targets once, or 1 target 3 times? However, if we cast it on four targets and stand close, it hits four targets ONCE and will still hit me for all 3 waves? Love those game mechanics. Oh, yeah. Rain spells can’t be used next to your without nuking yourself, and it the mob moves or is pushed out of the rain effect, it won’t get hit.

The other line is direct single target nukes (cold and poison lines). We have one fire nuke (our first nuke at level 1) and never got another one. Our best nuke is 925 damage (cold) but takes 7.8 seconds to cast! Do the math and you’ll see that RANGERS can out nuke us—they can just cast twice and do 1400 damage before our spell gem has even refreshed from the universal 2.2 second delay between cast times. Our poison line does less damage, though is marginally faster. Our dam/mana ratio is pretty awful too. This problem is exacerbated by the GoD spells currently on test. Somehow Shaman are the only class not getting a larger nuke. Clearly we aren’t nukers.

Okay, so we aren’t nukers or melee. What about healing? One of the biggest fallacies floating around is that shaman don’t need to heal because we have slow. Wrong. PoP mobs, or LDoN hard mobs hit hard. Rumor has it that GoD mobs hit harder. I don’t care if it’s slowed or not, or if you have a time equipped tank or not—mobs get lucky and can get good hits in on everyone. A single max double hit (1 round) in a hard ldon does more damage than our largest heal—and the cast time on that heal is 10 seconds (and isn’t affected by spell haste). That doesn’t, of course, address the fact that people take damage BEFORE it’s slow. Our heals are significantly worse than a druid—for the same mana.

I could go into a long analysis of fast and slow heals, but the bottom line is . . . shaman are lacking. I don’t know of any 65 shaman that use our 10 second (in)complete heal of 1950. Everyone uses tnargs—base 1300 heal because we can get AAs and focus items to make it . . . close to the BASE druid fast heal. Our heals barely touch a tank with 10,000+ hp. We have no group heals. Even a druid gets an AA ability to heal the group. Paladins are getting a new nearly 1k single target heal (1 second cast time!) in GoD. Not to mention that they OWN for group heals. Strangely, in GoD, clerics, druids, and paladins are getting new heals. Shaman get a new heal over time that does an extra 60 hit points of healing every six seconds. Hate to break it to ya Sony, but even slowed PoP mobs do more than 300 OR 360/tic. I shudder to think just how effective we’ll be in GoD (the image of bailing a leaky boat with a spoon comes to mind). Okay, so we’re not very good healers (a hybrid WARRIOR class out-heals us!).

How about pets? That seems to be the vision for us in GoD. We’re getting lots of new pet abilities. The shaman community has done LOTS of tests on pets so far. Base dps on dog dog is 12.7 dps. If we carry around backpacks of rusty weapons and give it two weapons (since shaman have NO items to summon a weapon), it can suddenly dual wield. Fully buffed and weaponed, it can do around 38 dps. (all numbers dependant on AC/level of target, of course—these targets are blue con mobs at 65). Just for reference, our level 56 poison DoT does similar dps. The only class with a pet worse than ours is the single level 55 druid bear pet (which is so patently bad as to not really even qualify). Shaman pets are just bad. Okay, so we’re not even CLOSE to a pet class. Our pet qualifies as a low level DoT at best. (BTW, thanks Sony, for giving us a ton of new pet AAs in GoD—I’m sure it will get our pet up to maybe 45dps for only 150AA pts. /sarcasm off)

How about DoTs? All of our DoTs are stackable—we can do major dps if we stack 8 DoTs on a mob! Now . . . uh . . . oh wait. I’d have to unmem slow, cannibalize, heal(s), malos . . . erm, nm. Our top 3 DoTs can do decent dps. Blood, Breath, and Bane—maybe up to 1000/tic. Of course, in a group, you’re tanking if you cast all that. Not to mention it has to be a pretty piss-poor group if the mob lasts long enough for you to cast all those. Well, we can solo—but solo exp was nerfed pretty hard. We have to root/rot too, since we can’t melee mobs (even slow) in PoP effectively—see discussion on our heal over time spell (quiescence) above. On a good note, we’re getting a new DoT in GoD. Currently, it’s yet another of our disease DoTs—whoops. Did I forget to mention that our disease dots take 2.1 minutes to run? Unless you’re soloing, when was the last time you were in a group that took 2.1 minutes to kill a single mob (besides a named or raid level mob). Our entire disease line of DoTs is useless in a competent group. It’s a complete waste of mana—you’re better off nuking with that slow-as-heck nuke. But look at the bright side—we get a NEW useless DoT. /cheer. In GoD we at least get crit DoTs, never mind that it costs a heck of a lot more (30AA last report) to get them. Who cares if it cost casters a lot less to get their crits. Screw the DoT classes.

Well, now, let’s come to the big thing—slows! Shaman are the master of slows! Sort of. Beastlords cast it faster. Enchanters cast it almost as well. Shm-75%, ench-70%, bst—65%. Current parses of mitigated slow (ie everything endgame) seem to indicate that the above percentages are reduced proportionately. Let’s look at mobs ‘slightly’ effected by slows. That means it’s about 25% effective. Multiplying it out yields: Shm-18.75%, ench-17.5%, bst—16.25%. Not too impressive is it? Don’t worry! Shm can heal! (We’ll just ignore the fact that shaman healing was last changed before mitigation was put in!) So what if mobs are doing almost their full unslowed damage! Shaman can slow so they don’t need heals! Right? On a good note, we get a new slow in GoD—it does 60% slow instead of 75%, costs more mana, has a TWELVE second refresh (cause we never get more than 1 mob in camp), lasts 42 seconds instead of 6.5 minutes, and will fizzle more because it’s level 65 instead of 51, but it’s NEW! I’m in a rush to memorize it, let me tell ya!

What about buffs? Our stat buffs are vital for those end game people who need the additional 2 strength to hit their cap (hint—there aren’t many who aren’t well over the caps). What about avatar? +140 attack and stats? Well, the BL (a true hybrid—the total twit who called shaman a hybrid class needs to be shot to improve the gene pool) can give more attack with their buff. HP buffs? Shaman can only do 430hp unless the target has less than max stamina. BLs can do about as well.

Mana regen? A lot has been made over this, but it’s getting deflated constantly. Enchanters and (in GoD) clerics get a free bar of mana every once in a while (depends on how many AAs they spend). The FT cap is pretty big. Horses. Paragon, SD, VoQ, Bot9, clicky buffs—thinks ain’t as far spread out as they used to be. Shaman mana regen is good, but not god. Since DoTs are apparently meant to be out primary mode of damage (never mind agro issues), I also invite you to go look at the mana costs on those—it’s obscene. They also get resisted a lot.

Someone tell me again how overpowered I am because I can solo Lodizal or Yeldema. I hate to break it to you, but so can other classes. Hell, a warrior on our sever solo’d Yeldema before PoP came out!!!

Does any of this start to make you wonder why some shaman are annoyed? In GoD we get a DoT no one wants, a slow no one will use, and a ton of pet AAs for a pet that sucks rocks. We’re getting a new cannibalize spell that is LESS efficient than the one we have (it’s more damage per cast but gives a lower percent of mana back—nets to about 1 more mana per tic). We can’t keep up on healing in a group because one resisted slow and our tank gets pounded to heck and we’re reduced to chain healing with no time for another slow on hard mobs. We get no group heals, even like the 40+ minute AA for druids.

So to all you pundits screaming, “overpowered”, I say you’re morons. Hybrids out heal us, melee out nuke us, and we’ve got more pathetic ‘skills’ and spells on the way while everyone else gets upgraded.

Oh well. At least I can go solo Yeldema if there aren’t any warriors camping him.
