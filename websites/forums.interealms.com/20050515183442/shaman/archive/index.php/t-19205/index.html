Tordail Farstryder
I call everything.

Hotbutton 3 is my "[ %T ] has been SLOWED by 75%" button, and I've never had a complaint about spam. I'm getting into the habit of only calling slow on a LDON mass pull ("The mobs near [ %T ] have been SLOWED by 50%" on button 4), since we usually go pretty fast pull-wise, and calling a slow on every mob just gets spammish. If I'm dealing with a tank that I've not worked with before, I macro every time, regardless. I also always macro both the malo-x on the named, and also macro the slow when it lands.

I am always main slow on raids, and I *always* macro the malo-x and the slow on every mob I cast them on.

I think one of the keys to avoiding spam on a slow message is to keep it simple. Just bloody say such-and-such a mob has been slowed and be done with it. There's no need to say "+~+~+~+~ %T ~+~+~+~+ has been SLOWED *@*#$%*!!!!" or some such. The fewer symbols and crap you use, the better off you are, IMO.

I also always keep the mob name toward the front of all my macros and what I'm doing to it in caps (SLOWED or MALOS-ED) so folks can just glance down, catch the name, and know what's been done to it. There is no hunting for the mob name or deciphering symbols.

When it comes to mitigated slows, I forego the hotbuttons and simply type "PARTIAL SLOW ON %T" or "%T IS MOSTLY SLOWED" and leave it at that ... that lets folks know they're not dealing with a full 75% slowed mob. I've never gotten a complaint, though a few have asked if there's any way to estimate the slow % on a partially or mostly slow mitigated mob.

I am of the opinion it is a waste of time and space to call slows.

You are a shaman, you are going to be casting slow. Period. If there is a case where there are two shaman and you are resisted and want help on a hard hitting mob so you do not have to wait for the spell to come available again, that's cool. Set up a hot key for him.

I think calling every slow really makes no sense, calling fails that is another thing then back up people can provide help to you, or other non slowers can adjust accordingly. That is a good practice and one I personally use.

In groups with strangers I will say at the start that I do not call slows, and at what percentage I will be slowing on average. I let them know I will call fails only.

So for me.

Reasons to NOT call slow:
1) There is already a slow message.
2) Tanks know when slow has landed, any tank that can't is in my opinion a poor tank.
3) Back up slowers know when slow has landed if they are watching damage out put and mob animations. If they do not, send them a tell, why clog up group chat with your I have slowed message?
4)There is never an action that is waiting to occur until after I have landed a slow. It is not like a main tank calling the it is okay to attack. People want me to slow. They want me to do it as soon as possible, they want me to do it without stealing aggro. They are not waiting for me to do it BEFORE they do something. SO no one needs to hear it.
5) In cases where multiple mobs are pulled I slow ALL the mobs. I don't wait for people to break mez/engage the add, so an enchanter can die because I didn't slow when I had the chance. I slow. I am a shaman it is what I do, I slow things down. So I make sure everyone knows I am slowing all adds. However if you are a shaman and you are NOT slowing all adds you might want to start.
6) Healers know when you have cast slow, they can tell by the tanks health bar I am sure. I know I can when I play my cleric.

Reasons TO call slow:
....

I can't think of a single one other than maybe point of interest, or piece of mind. But if your a good shaman piece of mind comes with you doing your job right consistently.


In closing however it is a matter of opinion, and preference. If you want to call it I say call it. If your group wants you to stop. Stop. Or debate with them why you think it is a required action.

I will never call slows unless requested to do so specifically, and in raid situations where that is the current instruction.
