Ok, I know that's a smart ass example, but there is some truth to it. If you find that experience in a group is painfully slow, there are a couple things you can do:
1) go to a better zone. Basic rule of thumb, the tougher the zone, the more exp a mob gives (ie a lvl 25 mob in Kedge Keep will give you more experience than a lvl 25 mob in LOIO).

2) get a smaller group. Yes, there are incremental bonuses for each party member, but if it's going slow with 6 full members...the next time you are in zone, try and get a 4 person group, or just a 3 person group.

3) pull more monsters. Unless the people you are playing with are experienced players, they are still getting comfortable with their roles at lvl 34...so they tend to play cautiously. Try to get the puller to pull the next mob when the current mob is down to 30% health, for example.

I personally went through my slump around lvl 18 - 19. I got burned out and took a break for a bit...but now I'm loving being a shaman.
House
At Level 35, unless your Defense is maxed, I'd strongly recommend against pet kiting, except for the rare white/yellow MOB you need to kill for a quest or something, where tanking just isn't possible.

Everquest is a repetitive game, and much, much more so when soloing. Playing a Monk isn't going to be any different as far as this is concerned. What makes it interesting is grouping with other people, and the strategies that emerge from working with other classes. Exploring new areas and fighting new MOBs maintains interest as well. I agree that full groups are generally not the greatest for experience; 2 or 3 person groups seem to be the best.

Here are some suggestions to break the repetition:

1. Use Invisibility to solo deep in a dungeon. Kiting generally doesn't work too well indoors and you won't be able to fall back on SoW and the zone line when you get into trouble.
2. Try duoing with every single class. I can't think of any two duos that require exactly the same from a Shaman.
3. Pull for an indoor caster group. Perhaps it's just me, but I enjoy the challenge of pulling and it's a great way to learn a zone.
4. Move through a dungeon, rather than just camping one spot.
5. Even if you choose to solo outdoors, make it a goal to max your Defense (200) by Level 40. If you've been kiting, this is guaranteed to change your play style.
