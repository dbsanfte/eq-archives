At 55, you're at the breaking point in difficulty settings. You're big enough to get the 58+ difficulty settings, but still small enough to qualify for the 50+ settings. The differences in mob DPS vs hitpoints is significant at those levels. That could be why your healing is starting to show strains when trying to keep the group alive. That will also greatly affect the lull level of the mobs, which is probalby why your Paladin couldnt lull in one of the adventures you mentioned.

At 58 you get a much bigger (though MUCH slower) heal which will help take some of the strain off. Level also GREATLY increases the resist rate of your spells. 55-59ish is just one of those tough areas for shaman. You have the potential, but just havent quite grown into it yet. Hang in there. Besides the late 30's to late 40's I think this (53-60) is one of the toughest in a shamans life.

Your gear is pretty nice. You have much nicer gear than I did at your level (I didnt see my first FT untill I was 54 and Luclin came out), and a pretty nice mix of HP and mana stuff, so I dont think you could do much better unless you have 100K that you arent doing anything with. Must make sure and land slow before engage though, as they tend to hit pretty fast.

But losing LDoN's isnt all bad. Yeah, you dont get the points, but you do get the exp and that is really the name of the game anyway. The point spread in LDoN is terribly and HORRIBLY unfair to lower level adventurers. By the time you can afford some arnor pieces you're already well past them in terms of bazaar purchasable gear. Spells are another matter, and many times you wont get a spell untill you are well past the level when you get the spell. As I said before just try to grin and bear it. It sounds like you are doing the best with what you've got to work with and are just working against a deck thats stacked in the other guys favor. taraddar
It's a combination of factors that have mostly been pointed out already.

1) Ldon runs are getting harder. The exact mobs you get in a dungeon and therefore the difficulty of the adventure is dependant on the average level of the group. Essentially there a level 20, level 25, level 30 etc dungeons all the way up to level 70. Selecting a hard mission bumps you up one step. So group with an average level of 25 doing a hard will get the exact same thing as a level 30 group doing a normal. Knowing which dungeon your going to get is important especially if your looking at a hard. The difference between a level 50 dungeon (low end for what you could get now) and a level 60 (a high end hard) are going to be huge.

2) Shaman aren't particularly good healers at that point especially if your trying to do multiple things. Druids really aren't much better off at that point either. Thats one range where clerics still hold a really good lead. Later on shaman become very effective healers vs easy content because of slow and out highly efficient HoT spells and Cann ability for mana regen. Especially as gear and aa's improve. You'll probably want a druid or cleric around for heals and you'll probably need to help the druid heal.

3) Your also at kinda a weak point for debuffs. Mobs are starting to get resistant but you still don't have malo for an unresistable debuff. This can make landing slows difficult especially if your in a hard dungeon for your level.

In general just make sure you know what to expect for the ldon mission and don't try to do too much and you'll do much better I think.

Hail Jahvah,

I hope you don't mind the perspective of a Ranger, who has made his share of mistakes, but has also had a few unorthodox successes.

I love your examples because it seems in each of them there is a chance for victory (and also three of them have at least one Ranger in the group :) ).

I don't think you did anything wrong, tactically, in these adventures. On the contrary, you seem very competent. I think it's more, "How could the group members have better coordinated their efforts?" It's great to be a part of a group that works well with each other, and is willing to try different strategies.


Concerning the first adventure:

You are the lifeline in this particular group. Once you go down, all is lost. But 2-boxing the Ranger, gives you the opportunity to coordinate the Ranger's and Shaman's tactics. Please try this -- have the Ranger cast Tangling Weeds (fast-casting Snare) and Flamelick upon each mob on incoming, and repeat for each time you have to cast Slow because of a resist. Your Shaman will be able to cast Slow very early, and also be able to sit immediately after each cast. Patience is important for this tactic. Don't cast Slow on a mob until it has been first Snared and Flamelicked.

I have 2-boxed my wife's Shaman quite a few times, and have been very successful with this tactic. I know that without her, I would have a hard time taking on even a single mob. But with her, tanking 3 mobs is possible, once the mobs are slowed, and if she doesn't get aggro'd in the process. On 3 pulls, her mana does get depleted because of the need to heal me, but we end up surviving and being able to progress. On single pulls I take little damage because of the early Slow, and am able to heal myself afterwards, conserving her more valuable mana.


Second adventure:

Stillborn group? Hmm, maybe not. :) Not much DPS in the group, but the Paladins can heal themselves between battles, and have Lay On of Hands for emergencies. They also can tank, lull, and grab aggro very quickly. It would be interesting to try it.

My wife (Shaman), stepson (Paladin), and I (Ranger) did quite a few adventures with very few failures, when we were in our 40's. Always MM Normal Collects, and it was always slow but steady. Haven't been able to try this since my stepson left Norrath.


Third adventure:

A valiant effort on your part, but there was probably nothing that you could do to save the group.

It may be more reliable to have the Enchanter Pac Assist the Paladin on pulls. I think too, if the Paladin pulls with a low-level Stun, the Enchanter may be able to Tash on incoming without drawing aggro, giving you a greater probability of landing a Slow. Not sure about the amount of hate generated by Pally Stun spells, though.


Fourth adventure:

/em hangs his head in shame.

If the Bard was being hammered while waiting for the Main Tank to grab aggro, then the Main Tank (Ranger) was not casting Tangling Weeds and Flamelick on incoming. If that was the case, you probably ended up having to cast more heals than necessary, and had to wait longer before being able to cast Slow and sitting.

Also, the Main Tank could have maintained aggro by recasting Tangling Weeds every few seconds. I don't think the other Ranger would have stolen aggro in this case (proccing weapons or not).

The Aggro Ranger should also have put his proccing weapons away. I assume he was using a couple. If he ever did draw aggro, he would only need to stop attacking for a couple seconds.

Concerning multi pulls -- at least one of the Rangers should have been able to Snare and kite the adds away from the main group until the first mob was defeated. Also, I have seen some impressive Bards fight one mob, while keeping another mezzed for the entire fight.

And finally, about the Rangers' mana and healing ability -- they can't keep anyone healed during a battle, but three Rangers can fairly quickly bring a group up to full, between battles. Every minute that they have full mana pools, while you are Canni Dancing to get yours back, is wasted mana overall. As a Shaman, I would heal only enough to keep the group members alive during the fight and ask the Rangers to heal everyone to full afterwards. And if you are forced to Canni, ask them to keep replenishing your health. Much better to have you at 100% mana and them at 20%.


Again, tactically, I think you are doing everything very well. It's ironic, I think a group would have a hard time surviving a 4-pull without you, or at least a Druid or a Cleric. Yet, I have never seen Shaman single-handedly save a group without having at least one other member perform crowd control or at least draw most of the mobs' aggro onto themselves first.

Planning, as a group, for bad pulls is the most beneficial thing I think you can do, especially in unorthodox groups. And it feels great when everything works out. But if the group doesn't work well as whole, I think the only thing you can do on bad pulls is wait for someone to engage the adds, mezz them, or at least kill a couple mobs before you can do your thing. I ask my wife to hold off doing anything, if she thinks she will end up having 3 adds on her. If the group can at the very least kill off one of the mobs first, she has a chance to heal others and slow, and end up saving the group.
