Rorgg
That no-penalty in cities is patently false, and apparent if you understand how the XP system works. The formula for XP needed for a level is

C * L^2 * r * m

Where C is some constant, L is the level you're going for, r is the racial modifier. BTW, a lot of people have weird ideas on what this is. Here you go:
1.2 = Troll, Iksar
1.15 = Ogre
1.05 = Barbarian
0.95 = Halfling
All others = 1

and m is class modifier. This is 1 for all classes but warriors and rogues, but I've forgotten what their modifiers are, they're between 0.90 and 0.95 IIRC. When they removed all the "penalties" for classes, they left the "bonuses" for warriors and rogues.

Now, I quote penalty and bonus because you actually GET the same amount of XP. This is a function of the level of the mob you kill, the ZEM (Zone Experience Modifier), and the number of people in your group. So, if you're a level 30 troll killing something, you're getting the same XP numerically a level 30 froglok would get, it just looks like less since it's represented to you as a percentage of the amount needed to progress to the next level.

Ok, lesson over. My opinion on this is that any of the races can make a solid shaman, but I see significant tangible benefits to Iksar, Trolls, and Ogres. The only other benefit you're looking for really is lower XP required to level, and if you want that, go ahead and play a cat or frog. I don't much see a point in barbarians except that a lot of people think they look cool.

Of course, since you can really play any race effectively, if looking cool is what you want, go for it. With PoP, faction's really pretty much a dead issue, it's of extremely minimal effort to render any "bad faction" issues meaningless by choosing your hunting zones.

For the record, I play an Ogre, if I were rerolling I'd either go Ogre again or Troll.
