Predicus
Leveling today is a shadow of difficulty of what it was in the earlier expansions, and I think it has caused quite a few problems that some people do not realize. The common voice of knowledge of all classes is to level to 65 then work on gear/aa's- we see it in our threads and I am sure it would not be difficult to find it in similar threads on other class boards.

Problems that I see that are related to the "easy" leveling:

- Players not knowing how to play there class.

A lot of people that I have played with have a common viewpoint: the 30's begin to define what the class will become. In the past, these were the training levels where you learned the basic skills on how to play your class: healing, tanking, mana efficiency, mez control, etc. We are seeing players today use the 50's-60's to learn about their class which is horrible. You should know how to play your class by then.

- Players not bothering to equip themselves for harder content.

I think there are 2 factors to this: one, that players, in the endless zerg to 65, do not take the time to upgrade their equipment because it slows down their grind. The other issue I see is that the developers speed up the leveling process, but the equipment drop rate is the same as it was before. Basically, people bypass the equipping process by trivializing the content faster [xp grind].

- Flagging, Keys, and Instancing

There are a lot of players out there that complain about the semi-new zone restrictions created by the above 3. People point out that, in the beginning, EQ did not use these strategies to block content, so why are they using them now?

The kicker here is that EQ was using another way to block content- levels. Hell levels and low xp rates made sure that the gaming populous was spread out in content- it took literally two weeks of active xp grinding back in the day to hit level 60 from 59. We don't see this in the game today because leveling was made easier. So why are we seeing the use of keys, flags, and instancing now?

When I log on during the bulk of EQ traffic [8-10pm CST], I will see 30-45 players in BoT, 15-30 players in Tactics, 5-20 players in HoH, 6-12 players in CoD, 20-40 players in Sol Ro, 15-36 players in PoE, and 6-18 players in PoFire. The majority of these players are here for xp, and are individually blocked by flags. If the flags were removed the majority of them would attempt to move to PoFire simply because it has the best xp modifier. The total number of players in the zone would be.....
