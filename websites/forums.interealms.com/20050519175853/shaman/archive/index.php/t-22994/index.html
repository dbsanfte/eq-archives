There is also a "Minimum damage modifier" (see, innerflame discipline and the berserker time bp).

I believe the "damage modifier" modifies the maximum hit, but leaves the minimum hit untouched. And the "minimum damage modifier" affects the minimum (surprise :-). So, to get a true damage increase you have to increase both the damage modifier and the minimum damage modifier (like the berserker bp, which adds 15% to both).

The damage modifier does not apply to the weapon's damage bonus either. When my monk uses innerflame (100% damage modifier, 400% minimum damage modifier) his hits go from 464 normal max to 891 normal max.

Tugela
The damage modifiers apply to the base damage component of weapons, not the damage bonus. There was a discussion about this some time ago when the latest iteration of these spells was released on test. Because the damage bonus is not included, the maximum increase in damage would be for a max hit, but it would be less than 10% (probably around 8.5-9%, I forget the exact number). As your hits are mitigated, the dps increase will be less since the ratio of base damage to bonus damage will decrease. A min hit will have a 0% increase for example.

Basically it means that the effects on these spells will be most useful on mobs with low relative AC and least effective on mobs with high relative AC.

The minimum damage modifier on some discs affects the damage bonus component of your dps, so it is not affected by these spells.
