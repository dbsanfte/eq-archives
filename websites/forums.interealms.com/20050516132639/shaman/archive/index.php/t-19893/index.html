We encountered this mob last night doing a Fear raid for epic drops. He was NOT FUN.

He's DB to 65, has around 125k hp (not entirely sure about this), and doesn't hit that hard (200 or so and doubled), but he has a wicked AE mez that has an absolutely insane range. These add mobs were green to 65, had very little hp, but hit surprisingly hard (200 or so) for the fact that they were green.

The AE mez was just EVIL. It lasted around 30 seconds or so on the average, was absolutely unresistable, and the little monster kept chaining it. After mez wore off, we had about 4 seconds to act before getting re-mezzed. Needless to say, I spent that time memming Malicious Decay and a disease dot, then dotting myself in those short windows when I could move. After those who could self-dot freed themselves up, the 5 of us who could self-dot became tanks, healers, and crowd control.

It was a very wild and hairy battle. Ireblind and his spawned adds killed a necro, cleric, and druid during the course of it, and our tanks were knocked out of the battle 90% of the time because of the mez AE. His spawned adds proc'ed Shadowstep just like Scarelings, at one point flinging me from the WW gathering point to the other side of the hill the entry portal stands upon. The two shamans in the raid became the tanks, while our casters who couldn't self-dot used their precious unmezzed time throwing fast-casting nukes on Ireblind. We primarily just nuked/dotted him down, but since he had so much hp, this became a very long fight that wasn't without its casualties on our side.

We had the joyous luck of dealing with Ireblind in an area where we had already cleared it out. Had we had to deal with Ireblind when he spawned off a scareling in an area we were in the process of clearing, things would have been VERY messy. If I'd have been shadowstepped out into an area and got aggro, I'd have been knocked out of the Ireblind fight because I wouldn't bring a train back to the others. The way they've put Ireblind into Fear is devious, and it's going to get raids wiped. This is a very effective and quite EVIL way for SoE to make Fear a very dangerous place again, even for 65's with Elemental+ gear.

From the experience we had:

--he seems to be umezzable/unstunnable/uncharmable. Our raid chanter certainly seemed totally powerless against it, focusing on nuking down the mob. I'll have to ask her if she managed to get off a mez, she didn't get back into the battle until late because she didn't put a strong enough dot on herself (yah, you have to dot higher than regen to break mez...I did the same thing to myself using Sicken til I was warned and upped to Scourge then clicked off Replenishment. Didn't want to take any chances, hehehe). Our chanter is a darn good one, so good she was the only chanter we took and the only chanter we needed, so I'm very sure that she tried mezzing the mob after she got back into the fight.

--Nobody in our group managed to land a root. I didn't try, but I'm sure the druid tried to snare AND root. The fact that the druid kited the mobs out of the battle to give the shamans time to heal some people told me that either they won't land or she had really bad luck.

--Ireblind's spawn adds seemed to have the same immunities as Ireblind himself. I do remember one point where our chanter attempted to chain stun, but the spawn adds all raced off to chase down and kill the necro.

There are two main suggestions I can make for Ireblind.

The first is to make sure you have at least 5 people in the raid capable of self-dotting to break mez. The regen/dot alteration might not let crimson potions work, someone will have to check. In our raid, we had 5 people who managed to break the chain mez and fight, and those 5 managed to defeat Ireblind with help from the others during those brief windows when they could act. Ireblind hits like a sissy and the only danger his adds pose is their Shadowstep proc, but his AE mez makes him virtually invincible if you don't have a way to counter it.

Defeating his AE mez is your biggest priority, because it's the one thing that makes Ireblind such a dangerous mob to fight.. Take that away from him, and he's just another notch on your belt.

One stopgap concept some people might want to consider is a cleric with the share health AA...if the cleric can break mez early, he could use that AA to break everyone's mez except the most injured people just once...but that one time might be the key to turning the battle in your favor. ;)

The second suggestion is, if he pops off a Scareling in an area you're in the process of clearing, you MUST pull him back into an area you've already cleared. His spawn adds will shadowstep the people who can self-dot and fight, and they'll bring more adds if they get aggro running back to the battle. This aspect of this mob makes him even more dangerous to battle, given the insane aggro radius of the mobs in Fear.

Just like everything else, I'm sure we'll all adapt to Ireblind and overcome, but until then, he'll certainly make Fear raids much more exhilerating than they used to be. ;)
