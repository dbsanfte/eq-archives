Meener
1.) Tell all applicable companies about what's happening. Most have resources to deal with this, they will flag your account.

2.) Put holds on your credit cards, bank account, etc. Vendors can confisgate a credit card that is being used on a locked account. Get all new cards with new numbers.

3.) Change all your passwords, code words, etc. on all your accounts. Do you do internet banking? If so, change your password asap.

4.) Get a credit report, it will tell you whatever else is going on. You may have had loans taken out in your name. Your bank can help you get these.

Having an identity stolen (if that's what happened) really sucks, and unfortunately you have to go after each robbery individually after it's resolved. Get your Social Sec'y # taken off every piece of ID that you can. Insurance cards, for instance, use your social. Get that changed, it's totally unnecessary.

A side thought...do you use PayPal? They have a record of siphoning funds out of people's accounts. Track the cash and find where its going, it may lead you somewhere.

There's something I've learned through a friend's experience. You should worry about this when your post office mail sits in some sort of mailbox that isn't locked. This is especially true for people living in apartment/condo complexes.

Scam goes like this:
1) you open a new account
2) Bank sends you debit cards
3) theif sees the cards/knows the cards are coming etc. and takes the envelope
4) theif watches your mail for the next piece of mail from your bank - it is almost certain to be the pin number mailing confirmation.
5) theif bleeds you dry.

Happened to a friend of mine - she eventually learned that it was her brother in law. They caught him on the video camera at an ATM - apparently they keep that footage stored somewhere...

My advice? Get a PO Box if your mail isn't secure - sometimes friends and family have mean streaks and you have no clue they exist.... Sad but true...
