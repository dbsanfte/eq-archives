CrustyOldShaman
I think a lot of our disagreement at this point comes from 2 things:

1. The inadequate way in which I expressed the point I was trying to make.

2. The differences in people's playing experience due to their personal cirumstances.

What I see in LDoN is a change of emphasis. After PoP came out I had what I may come to regard as the 'golden age' of EQ. I was one of those who stayed at level 60 and stayed out of PoP: for the first time in EQ, I - and the group of people I play with - were able to do real dungeon crawls in dungeons full of dark-blue mobs. I didn't level to 65 until after the mob exp level nerf was removed.

I had hoped the instanced dungeons in LDoN would allow me to repeat that experience. Only this time it would be even better as the dungeons weren't designed to have to respawn for other people, and more variation in mobs and dungeon geometry were possible.

What I got instead was what I consider to be a clumsy attempt to 'spice' the game up by making it a fast action game (ie. by using a timer to force you to do things quickly, rather than do things with precision). If I wanted a fast action game I'd go back to playing Quake.

The design rationale in LDoN seems to be based on the idea that forcing people to hurry makes it exciting. I disagree with that: doing the same repetitive things with little thought for 90 minutes is boring no matter how rushed you are. All that happens is that the in group banter that was part of the fun in EQ is now lost for me, because we don't have time to do it in LDoN.

Instanced dungeons have the potential to serve us far better than that. The dungeons mobs can be balanced to fit the precise level/equipment of YOUR party so it should be capable of providing a challenge. With the settings as they are in LDoN we could only get that at the moment by doing the 'hard' setting, and then we'd fail on the timer and miss out on an essential part of the reward system every time.

I'm sorry if I annoyed anyone by recommending you to go try out Quake. I did say that I've played Quake a lot myself so it was not intended as an insult.

What I got instead was what I consider to be a clumsy attempt to 'spice' the game up by making it a fast action game (ie. by using a timer to force you to do things quickly, rather than do things with precision). If I wanted a fast action game I'd go back to playing Quake.


I find the timer in LDoN a lot less annoying that I thought I would. Usually it just isn't an issue (and the people I group with are far from uber) but those few times when you win an adventure with less than 5 minutes less or even when you barely loose one have turned out to be a lot of fun. Plus there's the added bonus that the old zones are a lot emptier. We actually had Veksar all to ourselves the other day (and walked out with about 30k loot).

As for the leader board, who really cares? You can't use it to factor out the really clueless people since you only see the top 100 and as some others have pointed out, I can see reasons why people would have a bad loss record even if they knew what they were doing. I kinda like the fact that it lets me keep track of my own adventures but I'd never consider using it to decide if I'd group with someone or not. Basically, the only downside I see is the people worried about taking a loss and screwing up their rankings but I try to avoid that type of player anyway.

Me - shaman
1 x Plate Tank (Pally or more often War)
1x Cleric
1x Mage / Bst / Bard
make up rest of group with melee if possible.
As long as your puller is half competent and you dont get more than 3 or 4 mobs in camp things should be fine. We normally finish most adventures with 30 mins or so to spare. I am now 22/25 MMC and dont get overly stressed when doing adventures - I dont think that I have ever checked my rankings. Friendly banter, repping in and out, inviting friends as reps were needed, collecting people from zin - but I had one of my rare occasions where I had 4 hours to spare playing EQ.

I am an unguilded shammy and TBH LDoN has made zip difference whether I get groups or not, it is just more of a defined time period of playing.
