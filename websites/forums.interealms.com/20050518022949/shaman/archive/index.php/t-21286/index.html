Serepi
There has been issues with this ever since the concept was introduced. It's not intuitive, but here's my best understanding of how it works.

Raid forms. You must have a minimum of 32 flagged people in the target zone, to even attempt it. To the extent you have more than 32, you will be allowed more "85 flags" per the 85/15 ratio. However, the instant you put one non-flagged person in raid, the raid 85 ration is now set. Increasing flagged people in the raid will NOT increase your ratio. So, if you have 32 flagged in raid, and someone zones out to get the non-flagged, and invites 1, then you've set your cap (32*.15 = 4.8 = 4, I believe it rounds down.) Even if 30 more flagged join your raid at this point, you will only be allowed 4 85 flags, unless you reform the raid to reset the 85 cap.

The reason most people have the 85er's wait outside is because you have to be in the target zone. That's why sometimes after a near wipe, etc., some 85er's get the 3 min warning clock, because all the flagged are outside the zone, waiting to get ressed.

I think the cap is static to avoid abuses in 85ing. That's my understanding of how it works, ymmv.
Chutoi
Serepi is correct. You need a minimum of 32 flagged people to get unflagged into a zone. It will not work with keyed zones, only flag zones--thus you cannot use the 85% rule to get people down to lower CoD for bertoxx, or to get anyone into PoEb for Rathe.

The base rate is raid x .15 for unflagged, once you hit the 32 flagged minimum. I think numbers are actually rounded up for a maximum of 10 people unflagged in a full raid of 72, but I may be mistaken there.

Getting everyone in is a real nightmare. Have everyone zone in and make a raid in the appropriate zone. Then, once you have all flagged people in a raid, zone out and invite NO MORE than the right number of unflagged. If you get someone who tries to click in due to death (before rez box pops), or can click in because of a LD, etc., it becomes a real nightmare to track.

God help you if you are using the 85% rule and corpse summoning to do a Xegony run. Just don't even invite unflagged until you have summoned everyone else up and completely disbanded the raid and reformed it up top. Then send a rogue with a key, or an evac class back down who doesn't mind eating 1 more death for a summon.

When you change zones, don't be afraid to reform a raid. It may speed things up.

taraddar
I'm pretty sure that the whole thing is just slow and buggy.

As an officer I frequently handle this and it's universally a pain in the rear. I can also say with out a doubt that Serepi is wrong about the number of unflagged being "set" as soon as the unflagged person is in the raid. On numerous occations we've had unflagged in the raid before we even get 32 or had addition unflagged get in as more flagged show up.

There seems to be 2 main issues that cause problems.

1. Raids will flat out become bugged. Seems to be related to people leaving raid, possible only ld, while other people in the raid are zoning. The wrong combination just seems to bug out the raid.

2. While the number allowed in is dynamic it seems to be a bit on the slow side in catching up. So adding 20 more flagged people isn't going to instantly allow 3 more unflagged in. It will often take up to a couple minutes before it lets them in.

How you do things kinda depends on where your going. For HoHb we typically get everyone flagged to run in and form up raid and then send someone back to HoHa to start inviting people to thier group and sending them in and then moving them to thier group once they are in HoHb. Almost like doing a coh chain. This also helps to ensure that everyone is actually flaged for hohb.

For things like Plane of earth, water, air. We typically just form up groups outside and zone in together.

There are some reports of people leaving, dying gating what ever, and then zoning back in normally eatting up additional spots. I'm personally not sure if thats actually the case or just more related to the previous 2 problems. Raids getting bugged or just taking time to catch up can definitely seem like that.

Another interesting fact. While your raid is in Plane of tranquility unflaged will be able to zone into zones they aren't flagged for. Not sure the exact dynamics but we've had numerous occations where an unflagged will acidentally zone into PoWater and be the only person in zone even tho they aren't flagged. It seems like they can zone into any zone that you would be able to support them in when the whole raid is in PoK.
