afreeman
Clerics get the short end of the stick in a lot of cases, IMO.

Think about it: besides the ability to rez, shammies and druids are often 60-70% as effective as a cleric. Add a druid AND a shammie to a group and the cleric will only get logged when someone dies.

Consider CHeals: in most cases (read exp groups, minis, etc.), that CHeal is going to land for less than the 4.7k that a druid can heal, and often even less than we can heal for. Even considering the extra 15% efficiency of cleric heals, a competent druid with one of us as a backup healer should have no problem keeping a plate tank up in all but raid encounters.

Consider buffs: virtue is about 1500 HPs, while focus + sta is (depending, obviously) usually about 600. The Druid's Po9 is about the same at 618. And they stack. And don't forget about regen, which is a dramatic amount of healing for the mana in an exp group.

Consider debuffs: shammies reign here with slow/cripple, but druids also get useful debuffs like the Ro's line which cuts a mob's ATK and AC. These also stack. Clerics get nothing like this, except for maybe fear (if you consider that a debuff).

Consider DoTs/Nukes: while modest by necro/wizzy standards, druids and shammies can both add meaningful DPS to a group. Clerics, on the other hand, cannot realistically nuke unless you are fighting undead, and their undead-only dots are not terribly powerful.

Consider other utility spells: druids can port, snare, and we can both cast sow. Clerics don't even get regular invisibility.

Consider mana regeneration: druids and clerics both get several self-buffs that increase mana regeneration, but the druid's are about twice as good. Po9, for instance, regens 8 mana per tick. And of course, we bury them both with what is arguably the best mana regen in the game.

Consider solo ability: I won't rehash well-known facts about druid and shammie soloing feats since these are both well-documented. Note however, that clerics are handicapped in both the efficiency of their DPS spells AND their rate of mana regeneration, which should make it pretty obvious that clerics can't solo for ****, unless the mob is undead. Hell, there isn't even any recognized progression of zones in which clerics to move to as they level up, should they choose to solo. Druids at least have been fairly successful at petitioning for a couple places here and there (e.g. ME, CS, etc.) where they can quad.

Consider rez ability: they can do it, and we cannot. (Although Pally rezzes are a mere 6% off at end-game).

These are all situational factors, and so its hard to reduce them all to a single percentile of efficiency, but I don't think its far-fetched to state that druids and shammies are about 2/3-3/4 as effective at what clerics do, while bringing many, many other benefits to the table.

Does that seem worth the ability to rez? Well, in a raid encounter, yes, it does. However, it seems to me that they give up a lot to get that extra 25% in effectiveness.

Maybe that why, in my experience and guild, we generally only see clerics at events that are going to be a close enough fight that the extra 25% of so in HP buffs and heals is really going to matter. Other than that, someone usually leaves a cleric camped out somewhere nearby to rez the dead.

How many cleric bots do you have in your guild? If its anything like mine, that number will easily be 4-5 times the number of other bots.
