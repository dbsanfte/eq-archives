Jinheim
The time has come for me to start working on my healing AA's. In a previous post, I decided that HG wasn't really worthwhile, but HA would be really nice. Since then, I have gotten Quiesence and it has changed my mind. Here is what I am thinking:

With Q, SCRM, and SCM3, I very rarely need to use Kragg's Mending. Before I got Q, I had to use kragg's for regular, controlled healing. I figured that HA would be better because it would be better to have larger heals than the occasional crit that would normally be wasted because tanks were only down 2k HP.

Since I have gotten Q, I realized that HG is probably a much more viable AA. The MT in my group has Q on him at all times and its usually the only healing he needs. Whenever someone else gets slapped around a few times but doesn't keep aggro, they get a Q to heal them up. Kragg's never comes in to play. However, when a caster is getting beat or a DD just can't use aggro, I need to use tnarg's.

Tnarg's with HA3 should do 1430 points, I believe. 1430 vs. 1300 is not going to save someone's life. However, if I had HG3, that blast heal has a high chance of critting. A 2600pt heal vs. a 1300 is a HUGE difference and will most likely save the person's life.

So, in summary, I am thinking that going the HG route will be more beneficial for me since it will make me significantly better at blast healing, whereas HA would be a poor choice since the 10% bonus is not likely to save anyone's life, and I no longer use kragg's.

I am just looking for other people's opinions on these AA before I start spending points.

Goldark
I often find myself patch healing on guild raids against boss mobs. In those circumstances I think the chance of a crit heal is more useful than the steady but small gains from healing adept and could well save the tanks life.

In xp groups I'd probably say the opposite. I generally use my heals to help out the cleric with mana, or if the cleric goes LD, or a caster gets hit. In those cases the damage is normally fairly predictable and I'll heal once they are 1300hp down. A crit would most often just be wasted.

Having said that, I think FinalJustice has a point; there are other more useful AAs out there. Although, I'm not so sure I'd include innate stamina 3, metabolism 3 and and spirit call 3 amongst them.

Being able to cast quiessence/torpor and Tnarg's in half the time, every time, is more useful to me than the small, random chance of a critical heal.

The extra hp from ND3, damage mitigation from CA3, and improved chance of channeling that crucial slow from channeling focus are probably all higher on my list than healing AAs. Once you have those though, and the nice PoP ones (I'd suggest SC1, VP1 and RC2 or 3), then the healing ones do look nice.
