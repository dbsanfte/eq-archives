Winterfury
I was asked by izmalis and jdthomas in another thread to elaborate on how my exp group does this, hitting all four of the BoT tower bosses in about an hour, hence this thread. It's not rocket science, and many of you reading this will have either done it or are very familiar with the common techniques that we're using to get it done.

Group composition:

Tank (ours is a pally, I'm guessing he's over 9k buffed)
Druid
Cleric
Enchanter 1 (TD3, max CHA)
Enchanter 2 (same)
Shaman (<---- that's me)

All 65's, none time-geared or significantly e-geared, most of us are making our way through the planar progression (I explained last night to the tank how to finish his BoT key quest and I myself am not yet flagged for any elemental zones). Although I was just accepted into a Time-flagged guild last week, this isn't something I've done with any of my guildmates, it's just a group of friends who started off as a pickup group and eventually got into a nice little groove doing these together. But we are a tight team, having done most of the GoD trials together and now Kod'Taz. We know each other well and we know our roles and we often come to do this after our respective raiding is done within our guilds.

Druid's role is to Harmony our safe passage past most trash mobs, to snare pets, and of course heal. My role is to keep the pets MaloSINIA'd, Slow and heal. That's it, in this configuration I have zero DPS. Chanters are responsible for getting a pet each with one given the additional role of CC.

We meet in the safe room and buff up. Then we invis up, run up the Fire steps, run up some stairs, around a corner, and to the portal taking it up into the upstairs stormriders area. Once you're in the upstairs stormriders area you simply choose a direction. SE takes you to Air; NE to Fire; SW to Water, and NW to Earth. Run invis past all the stormriders and come to some wide steps leading up to a false door, with zone portals on the left and the right. The left returns you to BoT zone in, the right takes you into the relevant elemental wing (btw, someone in the group needs to have Enchanted Ring of Torden). This works for all 4 directions, they are basically symmetrical.

We usually start NE to Fire. We enter the zone in start running up. Druid leads us in, using Harmony on all giants along the way, running constantly, taking all the appropriate ports until we get to Thunderclap's room. We rush in and survey the situation. We charm two pets, eliminate any other giants in the room and set up in our positions. I stand immediately below the boss facing his platform, giving myself maximum distance but close enough to hit him with a spell. My job is to pull Thunderclap with Malos, hit him with Malicious Decay while he's running on the upper platform for my ass, and have him Grummus'd before he hits the ground. Pally then aggros him and moves to the left bottom corner of Thunderclap's platform where he engages him into the corner. Pets move in, (making sure that the any non-summoning variant hits the boss first to take ramp), CLR, DRU and SHM spam heal the tank. About 50% I re-slow. Takes maybe 10-15 minutes for total encounter. Loot and run to portal out.

SW to Water. Important to invis up here and keep it up at all times. DRU Harm's any see-invis and we run, run, run till we hit the portal going into the final boss room. When we enter this portal, Chanter 1's job will be to take the kriger on the left as pet, Chanter 2 will take the right, tank pulls any others and engages. We eliminate all mobs between us and Icestorm, except possibly the ones on the right as you face him, since we'll be out of their range. We get into position with the tank moving into the cubbyhole on the left nearest the boss, and me positioned at the wall space between that cubbyhole and the one next to it further away from the boss. Tank pulls boss, I Malos, MD, and Grummus. Pets are sent in, CLR, DRU, SHM spam heal. Reslow at 50%. Kill, loot and portal out. 10 to 15 minutes.

SE to Air. No invis needed here, just Harm all the way up the stairs, taking each portal till we hit Chaoswind's room. Pet up two nearest mobs, kill the rest and prepare for fight. Chaoswind is unslowable, so my job is to keep the pets healed, SINIAd and spam heal the hank. Chaoswind has an AE stun so it's critical that the healers stay beyond the fourth tile from the center and all be equipped with ER IV items. Kill, loot and portal out. 10 minutes tops, this one is easy.

NW to Earth. The trickiest one. Again, invis up and harm our way to the the room just below the stairs with the sky's. We'll invis to a corner and prep for some fights (kind of like moving in to Tables) and take 2 pets and clear the rest. Tank runs ahead and starts clearing the skys and eventually brings Hailstorm to us. He is unslowable so again, I heal tank and pets. Kill, loot and portal out. This one seems to be the hardest and if we ever wipe, it's here. 15-20 minutes.

All told, we're out in and out in about an hour usually with 2-3 pieces of Ornate (Tunics, Greaves) and many other nice loots (I got my Cap of Drowned Souls and Glowing Orb of the Stormwatcher here, both off Thunderclap). Other drops are Great Blade of Storms, Greatstaff of Thunder, Wicked Shank, and always a few Glyphed Rune Words and SPs.

For those who are familiar with pet management this is probably stating the obvious but it's worth pointing out some simple concepts that may be news to folks that are new to charming. First, enchanter needs to have a hotkey that announces when pet breaks. I'm pretty familar with the aggro spam associated with each of the usual pets so I'll know when they break but it's still good to have the hotkey announce msg. Soon as it breaks, DRU snares and announces, I MaloSINIA and announce success (when I see the "uncomfortable" message), chanter tashes and THEN charms. If charm occurs before sinia or snare attaches we break and re-do pet. Also, whenever we're heading into critical situations we'll break and re-do pets. Chanter keeps a distance from pet and as long as it's snared they're pretty safe. Important to NDT pets in this situation so as to avoid ambiguity while they are fighting their kind.

Needless to say we can only hit whichever bosses are actually up, and if we encounter any groups working their way up we disengage and leave. In all the time that I've been doing this I've never run across another group, although there have been times when one of the bosses wasn't up.

It's a hugely fun encounter and I always look forward to taking on these bosses with our group. The loot is nice, but the real reward comes from the incredible rush associated with knocking these guys down on their own turf. Although we're all good players with decent gear, there's nothing really uber about this team or their gear, it's just a matter of knowing the terrain, knowing the enemy, and knowing each other. Fun as hell.

Hail,Okay, now my interest is seriously piqued. My group of friends just got hammered trying Glykus for the first time, though I thought we had a decent strat.
I've been whacking Glykus intermittently as a trio with a druid and enchanter. None of us are in the EP, & I don't think any of us have any pieces from VT.

Enchanter charms a zek (any) and we clear pit trash until a frenzied initiate pops. Then we switch pets to the frenzied, and transfer mage weapons (swords, mask, belt) to the frenzied initiate. Frenzied's flurry and have more hp than standard zeks. You can take out Glykus with a normal initiate, but a frenzied makes it easier.

Whack the back hall wanderer (respawns about 27 min I think) and then the 2 corner spawns outside the pigpens. Clear ALL of the stables as fast as you can once that's done. When you have cleared the stables completely (minus Glykus of course), move the pet to just outside the stables, a few steps beyond the border. Break mez, reapply Malosinia, Tash, and Snare, let pet get to 100% and have druid at full mana. Recharm frenzied, refresh FA (ench charm pet also has Focus, Strength of Diaku, and Pot9). I generally take over all healing duties during the last half of the pigpen clearing so druid can be near full when we're ready to pull Glykus.

Enchanter pulls Glykus (with Tash I think, not really sure) to the pet. The boar pets don't spawn right away, so walking Glykus to the frenzied isn't too dangerous. Frenzied engages Glykus and enchanter runs back to the area near where Glykus spawns. Enchanter then kites the boar pets around the back of the stables (he's in imp or air elemental form so he's floating with FoE on for faster movement rate) while frenzied pet is killing Glykus.

I Malos as soon as pet has engaged, and cast Turgur's at about 95% Glykus health. Cripple goes in about 90%. From there I assist heal only when Glykus rampages on the frenzied pet. Druid covers at least 80% of the pet heals. The important thing is to make sure the frenzied is at near full health before Glykus enrages, because the pet can drop 30-40% health extremely quick, and the enchanter doesn't always have time to back his pet off during the enrage.

This method takes a very skilled (not to mention gutsy) enchanter and a well-heeled druid (in terms of manapool). I feel relatively useless except for my buff contributions and some assist spam heals. I think pretty much any 65 shaman could be used as the third person. My almost 400 aa's don't do much, a 65 shaman with Canni5 and not much else could probably fill the shaman role just as well. I could easily be replaced by a cleric honestly as you can manage Glykus even unslowed with a Chealer in my opinion. (Maybe I shouldn't say that here, but it is true.)

Overseer can be done using a similar strategy with 4 people, merely adding a cleric to the mix. Shaman does NOTHING to get on the aggro list however unless someone dies to keep the hate list on Overseer limited to 4 people (to prevent him from switching off the frenzied pet). I feel REALLY useless for this kill most of the time, as all I'm really contributing to are marginal buffs and some help in clearing the trash beforehand :p

We've been trying to do Tagrin with 4, but haven't managed it yet due to some unfortunately timed charm breaks.

Oh, if charm breaks during Glykus or Overseer, druid immediately casts Exodus. The ench can recover using refresh mana AA (puts him back to FM) as soon as he/she loots all but bridle off corpse. Second attempt can happen if you don't delay without needing to reclear the stables.

Sadly, none of us really need these drops, so they are pretty much randomed among us. I've bought a manastone and a fungi tunic as toys with the proceeds from my winnings recently, and still been able to give away some very generous gifts to guildmates. :cool:
