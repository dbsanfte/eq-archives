OK, here's my rundown, having done this several times.

Allakhazam quest guide is at http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/quest.html?quest=2219, please refer to that for all dialog.

1. Make sure ALL party members have the preflag (say "Tortured by Nightmares" to Adroha Jezith in Sickbay).

2. Make sure NOBODY HAS ANY AE NUKES MEMMED. Bards should not play AE damage songs. You do not want Thelin damaged. Personally, I do use AE slow; sometimes I get Thelin aggro, but memblur fixes it.

3. Form up at the maze, near ZO2 in PoNightmares. It helps if you have an evac'er in each group.

4. Group leaders hail Thelin and follow the conversation, I think it ends with "I am prepared" or some such.

5. At this point, all groups are ported in to a maze. DO NOT TALK TO THELIN YET. The question is, are they in the SAME maze. There are THREE COPIES of the maze, all identical but at different (x,y) locations in PoNightmares. If you have groups in different mazes, someone needs to evac, run back, and talk to Thelin again. It may take 2-3 trips to get everyone in the same maze.

6. Raid leader talks to Thelin again and he starts moving. He goes a long way before anything interesting happens. Have a chanter ready to mez/memblur Thelin at all times. He will eventually path into a dead end with a single werewolf standing in it. Kill the werewolf (Thelin will help). Thelin then continues into the corner to pick up a piece of his dagger. When he does, some mobs will spawn, I think about 2-4 of them. It's critical to pull aggro off of Thelin as quickly as you can; I like to use AE slow here.

7. Thelin will continue walking. Any time you need a break for rez/rebuffs or for medding, just mezz/memblur.

8. Rinse and repeat step 6 4 more times, except the number of mobs increases every time. I think the last time we had 8-10 mobs spawning. You do occasionally run into ravens in the maze that have nothing to do with the mob-spawn cycles.

9. Now Thelin will head to the central courtyard. We always mez him at the entrance to this, pull and kill the birds inside, and make sure everyone is medded up and ready to go. Then let Thelin go; he will walk to the fountain and the Construct of Nightmares will spawn. CoN is a fairly conventional melee fight, just keep the MT alive and knock him down.

10. At this point, loot the dagger off the CoN and hand it to Thelin. Terris Thule will spawn; Thelin goes over to her and has a conversation, then she despawns. Now hail Thelin to receive your flag; you will also be ported out of the maze with the same hail. Note that all loot is droppable except parchments, so you should be able to distribute loot outside.

Hail,

If you can field 2 groups of skilled people, you can do it. Grab another group of scrubs just to fill out the 18 man event, but don't have any of them in key positions. Solid CC and a well informed raid party make this event a breeze.

If you are concerned about the event, here's what I recommend for a little advance recon to help things go smoother.
1) Go visit the Hedge yourself beforehand. Bring a friend or two if you want, but really you can do this all alone.
2) Once inside the Hedge, don't start Thelin up (since you are alone), and move throughout the maze carefully.
3) Kill every static spawn you see.
4) Mark the location of each static spawn point on your map using the map editor. Be sure to SAVE THE MAP after making modifications to it.

I did this for soloing purposes (so I wouldn't stumble into spawns), and EVERY time I've gone back on a Hedge raid, the map I marked all the static spawns was the map we were given. My impression is the other two maps are only used if there is another Hedge event going on at the same time (with a different raid).

Knowing where every static spawn is means you can run ahead while Thelin is walking, aggro the static and bring it back to the raid for a quick kill. This will save you time and make the waves a little more manageable, as several of the statics are placed at or near where the waves spawn.

The waves are triggered when Thelin crosses certain points in the maze, and are always at a dead-end.

Be sure to kill the 4 birds in the corners of the final courtyard where the Construct appears. Do this before letting Thelin move up to there just to be safe. The Construct has a lot of hp (comparing to the normal Hedge mobs) but really doesn't hit too hard. If your tank has 9khp, I think a single cleric would be fine for keeping him up.

Unless you are really low on DPS, I wouldn't recommend using pets that aren't controllable or ANY pets for people who have any kind of connectivity issues. Especially no enchanter pets, since they will likely incur Thelin aggro periodically through the event. Linkdeaths often end up leaving the LD player's pet whacking on Thelin. If this happens during a wave, it can cause you to fail. A hasted pet with mage toys can do some serious damage pretty quick. :p

Best of luck to you. :cool:

Grafsha McDaughts
If you want to prep the key players (tank, chanter, cleric, etc.) you can do a walk-thru of the maze. Nice if you have the zone map with the hedge included. Refer to it during Thelin's walk and you can tell how close you are getting to the main event-fight ... but I am getting ahead of myself.

If you do the quest to the point of being transported into the hedge just leave Thelin alone. Don't hail him. Then just walk thru the hedge maze (using map) and work your way to the center. There are a static spawns you can kill on the way to get a basic feel for mob difficulty. You will not spawn the multiple mobs on this test walkthru.

This is just a way to get folks who are unfamiliar with the area a chance to get past that and focus on the group spawned mobs during the real event.

To my knowledge there is no way out of the hedge maze except to complete the even and hail Thelin other than (1) gate, (2) port or (3) die and wait for body to pop in PoN GY. So if you take a non-gating class they need a gate potion or add a druid/wizard to your tour group.

Shaman can solo this zone area also. There are few posts scattered here with details on doing it.

Good luck - for a young guild, this is a fun event. Good practice for getting folks to pay attention, focus and react to multi-spawns quickly.
