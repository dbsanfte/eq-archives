Actually, 700 dps over a TIC is remotely possible. He'd be dumb as a stump to try it, especially on any mob in Natimbi (except maybe the cragbeast queen if someone could actually hold agro).

To do it, if I ran the math right, you'd have to have 5 DoTs running and then tears, then nuke. If everything hit at the same time, you could get (with focus items, assuming no crit DoTs or spells) a damage of somewhere near 825 dps for that single tic. However, that's horribly misleading--dps is best viewed as an average, not a number to be picked at random from the best tic in a fight. It's not even close to sustainable. That line of spells will cost a well equipped shaman probably 40% of his mana, and absolutely insane agro. (if you're just picking your best tic, a wizzie with a focus could probably get 1200 dps in a particular tic--with a crit--that's why dps is best considered an average over several fights, and not in terms of a particular tic).

For about a 3-4 minute max sustainable dps with a time equipped shaman, you could perhaps reach 500-600dps, maybe a little higher with some crits on DoTs and nukes. That is ONLY in a situation where agro wasn't an issue, no interrupts, no summons, no resists (HA! since we have no resist modifiers on our spells!). It also assumes a mob that will last long enough for you to land 4 DoTs and start nuking.

Basically, I can think of about 2 mobs in EQ that we could do that on--and we'd have to have a particularly beefy tank with tons of agro procs and augs, and who fired reposte at the start to hold agro.

So, yeah, he's lying through his teeth, misunderstood the question, or is just flat out wrong!

There are a few widely used definitions of DPS.

a) DPS is the amount of damage you do from the moment a MOB is aggroed until the moment it dies, divided by the amount of seconds this took.

b) DPS is the average damage you do over a certain large span of time, including downtime.

c) See A except you instead take the average for a large number of MOBs, preferably while maintaining a constant level of mana.

C is what is most often used. B contrasted with C is a nice way of gauging how much downtime affects you. A is what is usually used to compare 1) burst damage and 2) DPS on bosses.
