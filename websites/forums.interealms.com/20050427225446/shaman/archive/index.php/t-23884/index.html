I believe I understand why you think you may be earning AA faster at level 62 than at level 62+. I also believe that you are mistaken. While it may be true that you can get slightly more AA exp per kill at level 62 (because you are closer in level to the mobs you are killing), I don't believe you fully understand how much more quickly you can kill based on power increases from level 62-65. The biggest gain you'll see is the removal of your group's cleric or druid. Consider how busy you are now trying to single target focus and replenishment on your group, malo and turgur (bounces fairly regularly still probably) for every mob, and all the while contributing minimal DPS. I could never main heal for a group in BoT at level 62. At level 65 I was fully capable of functioning as both main healer AND main slower for that same group with get this... ZERO downtime. You'll be able to ditch the cleric or druid and replace them with another DPS class. Kill rates go up significantly. At 65 in BoT you'll still get 2-3pct a kill, your downtime will decrease and your kill rates will increase. Don't say you need a cleric or druid for the named, that is BS. I've main healed most of the named in BoT (outside of towers). Your ability to function as both main heal and slow will shed a whole new light on LDoN, PoV, BoT, OoW. Now mind you, you can't do this with a level 62 warrior tanking that group in BoT. It just won't work. LEVELS are the key. They improve your mitigation table, lower your resist rate, increase your hp/manna pool, allow you to scribe more powerful spells, raise your group desirability.

Think about what the fastest you've ever earned an AA was. 2 hours? 1.5 hours? 1 hour? 30 minutes? What was the average level of that group. I've seen an AA tick by in 30 minutes working the top south camp in BoT. We cleared all the way down to the bottom of south before re-spawns. Everyone was level 65. I was main healing. Mobs unslowed. I couldn't have done that at level 62. Quiessence is a level 65 spell. Yes, the mage in my group was charming. But guess what, he couldn't do that at level 62. Call of the Arch Mage is a level 65 spell.

There are possibly some good reasons to do AA before level for each class (though I'd argue not many as have others above), but saying you get AA faster at level 62 than you would at 65 is not one of them.
