Lets look at the facts and not the sentimentality.

1. The company that produces everquest and supports it is not a non-profit organization, and Sony is a publicly held company as far as I am aware.

2. Publicly held companies are responsible to be profitable for their stockholders.

3. Like any company, there will be a bottom line with budget goals that need to be met.

4. The company in question has many expenses that are part of the ongoing support of this game, including, but not limited to, hardware maintenance, hardware upgrades, network support staff, content developers, office administration, management, custodial staff, marketing, coding, artwork, internet connectivity, research and development, legal fees, lease, distribution fees, and electricity to make everything run.

5. It is a sure bet that at least the costs associated with electricity, wages for at least much of the staff, and the building leases, along with addition of staff and hardware, have gone up over the past 3 years.

6. Up until this point, even though these other things have gone up in cost for them, they have not raised the price to us.

7. Increasing costs with the same subscription rate translates into lower performance on the ratio of money going out compared to money coming in.

8. They are raising pricing by a moderate amount to keep budgets at their target levels.

Really, if you look at your own situation, you have probably noticed that your rent, your utility bills, and damn near everything else that you pay for has gone up in price over the past three years. This happens to companies as well. To make up for lost revenue, a price increase is sometimes implemented. It is very simple. If these people that run the companies are given a bottom line to meet and they do not meet it, people lose their jobs after a while. It is the way economics tend to work. Now 3 dollars is not that much. It is surely more to some people than it is to others, but that really is not important here. The facts are that their costs have increased, and to make up for it so have ours. I personally think that they made a HUGE snafu with their timing of this however. They would have been MUCH better off announcing this right before the release of Luclin, or at least before the Legends server came online. With legends being more expensive, many people are pointing at that as the catalyst. While there may be SOME truth to that, I think that mostly it is due to pure and simple economic strategy.
