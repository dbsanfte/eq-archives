It does matter.

When the first person dies, you all share 1/6 of the debt. But if that first person releases to their bind point, then they won't share in the loss of all the rest.

If everyone releases when they die, then the last man standing will eat all 5 of the previous people's deaths and SOLO his own Xp debt.

He will have easily a half yellow bubble more debt more than the first person that died.

It's really bad form to release when your group is wiping, unless you are near the bind point and can come try to help. (Plus, if you are in the same zone when you die and are nearby, you will still share in the debt.)

Sudedor
There is no difference.

In a group situation (full group) when one person dies, the debt from that death is split evenly among all 6 players. (assuming the system is working as intended). So, if you are the one who dies, you accrue only 1/6 of the debt you normally would.

If all 6 of you die (you being one of the 6) you would accrue 6/6 of the debt you would normally accrue. Well, yea, duh, you died.

Now, compared to the EQLive system, it's the same thing. In EQLive, when you die, you simply "lost" the experience. Gone. You had to "re-earn" that experience. You lost between 6 and 10%, possibly losing a level in the process.

Now, with EQ2, you never "lose" experience. You go in debt. While in debt, you are still *gaining* experience. You can level up while in debt, gain spells, etc. The only difference is that half of your experience goes toward your debt, while the other half goes toward moving you forward.

So, in EQLive, you had to earn a strait 10% experience to recover from a death. In EQ2, you have to actually earn 20% to recover from a death. The difference is that when you pay off your debt in EQ2, you are also 10% farther along toward your next level.


Example:

A player in EQLive is at 80% toward his next level. Dies. Now he's at 70%. In order to get to 90%, he must earn 20%.

A player in EQ2 is at 80% toward his next level. Dies. Now he's still at 80%, but has debt until 90%. He must earn 20% experience to get to 90% since half his experience goes to debt and half to experience.

At the end of the day, both players had to earn 20% experience following their death in order to get to 90%.

There is no difference.
