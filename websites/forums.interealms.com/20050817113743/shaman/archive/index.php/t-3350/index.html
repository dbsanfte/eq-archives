Bah, one old way of making a shaman be effective in an AE group in FG is the following. Keep in mind the method works in many zones, but the results vary depending on your mana regeneration and how much damage you take from mobs.

We used to do this with a 4 shaman group, or shaman+bard+cleric (ae boots) +mage (pets+occasional AEs), shaman+bard+monk+monk, and a variety of other group makeups.

The role of the shaman, if there's only one shaman, is to pull with slows, and do an occasional heal. The limiting factor is of course your mana regen (you need at least 1000 mana per pull if you use turgur (I recommend tirgirs, saves mana), and pulls are between 1-2 min, so work out the details). The faster you regen mana, the faster you can pull, and as a result the more exp you will get.

It's simple really, basing on the simple fact that a group standing still is a group wasting time, so you want to have your group engaged at all times. The AE method just add a little bit more, why not keep 8-12+ mobs in camp and taking damage, instead of just one?

Viola, there ya go. The basics of shaman AE, where the shaman's kept busy with searching for pulls while trying to keep his mana up, and trying not to go out of range so mobs you pull won't give exp when they die.

This works best when you have a mob base of 40-60+ for a 4-6 person group that spawns every ~15 min, which makes FG sort of ideal for it. However, it's a lot of work, since you need to know exactly what to pull next, make sure to never be out of range, and constently keeping an eye out for your mana.

With less work but a better group, you can get similar exp in CT or even Ssra, and better loot. Also, you won't be restricted by not having enough mobs to pull.
