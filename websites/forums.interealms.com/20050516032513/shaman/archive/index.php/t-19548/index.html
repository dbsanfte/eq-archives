I completely disagree.
Go 100% aa or 100% regular exp. Doing anything else forces Sony to split exp numbers in half to go to two different exp bars. What's that mean? Invariably there will be rounding whenever the exp # is an odd number. Which digit gets rounded and in which direction? Is it just the 1's column? If so, maybe it isn't a big deal, but we're talking about potentially losing a eensy teensy piece of exp on EVERY DAMN occasion experience is gained. No thank you.

I generally go 100% regular until I'm happy with where my bar is, then go 100% AA. Sometimes I will just set a timer for 30 min and run full exp and then switch back.

Before GoD info was starting to filter out, I heard a nasty rumour that we might get additional levels, so I filled up my 65 bar completely. Right now it generally sits 1-2 blues short of being full (depending on how much raiding and dying I've been doing). When it goes more than 2 blues down, I just go full regular exp for one LDoN adventure, and then turn it back to AA's.

I don't trust the random number generator string, and I don't trust Sony to round rather large numbers properly without taking shortcuts (truncating instead of rounding up???) that might possibly subtract from the maximum amount of experience I should be getting. YMMV.

Something to think about anyway. I am a strong advocate of doing your OWN splitting by switching from 100% in one category and then flipping back and forth as your desires dictate. ;)

I aim to keep it around 15-20%, I've never undinged yet. Anything more than that and you've got potential AA points you could be using, sitting there as wothless xp.

I stay on 100% aa until I get my next target (e.g. current target is LR5, 15AA pts total). When I reach that I will go 100% std xp until my buffer is back up to 20% or so.

I feel this way I reach each target faster but still have minimal risk of going back to 64. Anytime you split AA/XP you are making to take longer to reach either target so I like to hit one target (aa skill), then the other. Similarly, switching away from AA when you have 2aa unspent say, is a waste. Get the rest of the AA for your next skill, then switch to xp.

Just my opinion of course though - I can understand some people may be worried about going back to 64, but I honestly can only think of one time in 6 months that I've not got a rezz, and a couple of times when I've taken 2 deaths in a row (but then both of those times got 2 rezzes right after).

If you're like Einno and have 360+ AAs then I can understand putting more into regular xp, but for me (120aa) each aa point is still very valuable to me.
