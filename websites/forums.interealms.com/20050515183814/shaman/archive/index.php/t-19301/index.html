Bottom line, Sony wants everyone to play more and longer. The only way to ensure this is by making it so that you can only get critical spells and items through hours of adventuring. The beauty of LDoN and now GoD is that you can not buy your new spells, you have to grind to get them.

This is both good and bad. It eliminates the "chance" element of the parchment and rune turn-ins which can be very frustrating and gives you a set goal for attaining the item in question.

However, it can also be frustrating for us (me included) who rarely have time for 1-2 adventures a night and therefore it takes several months of play to get the items we need.

Worse, because points have to be earned in the specific zone in which the item is sold, it takes MUCH longer to get the item than if you could use points earned anywhere on everything. I understand why they did this, again it makes us play longer, but it is a royal pain to wait for a group in a specific zone (and no, I don't have a guild or complete group of friends that are always on when I am and ready to do an adventure in the zone I need).

So, bottom line, this is good for some of you, those with the time and friends to get a group into an adventure in a specific zone when needed, but not good for those of us with more limited time or resources. We all know how bad pick-up groups suck.

Sony will generate their revenue, and unfortunately, some of us will never see the new spells. If they were droppable it would go a long way in solving the problem, I could farm pp or trade items for them, but alas, that would not give Sony the additional hours of my time that they are looking for.
