The thread asking about our innocence in gaming, got me to thinking about my first forays into the lands of Everquest.

Growing up in Everfrost, and making it out to the South Wall the first time, only to be killed, time...and time again, by the Orc Shaman...(got my revenge over and over later on)

Finding my way blindly thru the tunnel to Blackburrow...the first few times, getting so turned around in there, I ended up back in EF, only to start all over again.

The LONG LONG run across West Karana to NK...that one made me feel like I had actually accomplished something.

Completing the first quest for Totemic Armor.... I had made it out into Lake Rathe, and was lev'd over the water , just exploring. I forgot, that on my pointer, was my Mythril Breast plate. I forget now, why it was there. Well, needless to say, my mouse decided to act up, and by the time it was working again, my BP was on the bottom of Lake Rathe, and after searching and searching, I finally gave up. I think most of my guild had pee'd their pants several times over while laughing at me as I explained what happened.

My very first foray into Fear. Barely into 48....my heart was pounding as I zoned in invis, trying to find where everyone else ran to.

Winning my first piece of armor from Hate. The only thing I never got from Hate, was the BP.

The Hell levels. Before I really hit them, I can remember out in EF, at the South Wall, dying 17 times in level 16...Then level 36 hit. I dang near gave up ever getting out of that level. In the space of 24 hours, I died 12 times. Nothing I did was right. When I dinged 37, after some help from some good friends, I think I went out and got drunk in real life to celebrate.

I have to admit, that when it came to dungeons, I was the biggest sissy of them all. Scared to death of dying, getting lost, falling... and needless to say, my sense of direction was lousy, even with SH. And dont' even get me started on /loc

This is my 3rd computer since I started playing EQ. I won't be upgrading for a while as this one is a 2.5 P4... as a matter of fact, I have 2 now. I started EQ on a 256 P1 with 64mg of memory, and cheapy video card.

Hearing about the unbelievably fearsome and dangerous planes of Hate and Fear, when they first opened. Multiple group wipes, tremendously difficult, almost impossible corpse recoveries, the very toughest game challenge at that time. To this day, to my disappointment, I've never visited either zone.

Seeing the first warrior epic, and being so incredibly impressed by those cool glowing blue and red swords.

Camping Cazic-Thule with pickup groups for a chance at a rubicite drop (never saw a single piece). CZ would have 100+ people in those days, and the rubi breastplate was rumored to have a regen quality and was therefore valuable beyond measure.

Getting disease or rabies from miserable mummies in Befallen, and actually sitting and waiting for it to wear off for probably 45 minutes. I hated asking for things from other players, even something like a disease cure was a major inconvenience.

Seeing a troll warrior in full rubicite and dual jade maces, and being absolutely STUNNED.

Seeing Aradune in that funky green armor and flaming sword in Greater Faydark. Yes, I actually saw him once.

Realizing how much mobs hated bards, when my poor bard friend kept getting whacked in our Unrest groups. He died so much it was almost funny.

NO ONE got rezzes, because no one was level 49 and the lower-level partial rezzes wouldn't be introduced for a year or more. Death sucked, big time. No ports, no SOW, and no rezzes. You die, you start running back to your corpse.

Getting clarity as a monk and thinking it was something I needed.

Losing my corpse in Greater Faydark and shouting for help finding it, having absolutely no idea where it was. A kindly bard finally helped me locate it, to my tremendous gratitude and relief.

When my bard friend got a minotaur axe, and was just kicking the crap out of things with it.

After Kunark coming out, running (without SOW) my monk to Dalnir's Crypt for that no-drop monk armor (can't remember its name) and maybe a Sarnak Earring of Station. Getting my friend killed by the Kly when we tried to get back out. He dropped an ice-comet on us, the first time I'd ever seen that spell cast.

Being an over-powered, dual-wielding 8/20 fighting baton monk.

Reading about the seemingly-unfairly-difficult ice comet quest, where wizards had to find runes spread out all over Norath, including one under the ice in Everfrost Peaks, which only spawned on server-ups.

Seeing my first Fiery Avenger on a high-elven paladin and being extremely impressed.

Being THRILLED when my beta-ranger's sense heading or *even better yet* forage skill would go up.

Spending hours fishing for rusty daggers and sandals in beta so I could buy a SHORT SWORD for my beta ranger, which was like one delay better than the RUSTY SHORT SWORD I had been using. It wiped out my bank completely - probably 4 or 5 pp in Qeynos. I had to run all the way from Surefall Glade - my home - through Qeynos Hills and into Qeynos to buy it - the first time I'd ever been there.

Being charmed by that undead skeleton in Qeynos Hills and killing my druid friend and then dying myself after the charm wore off.

Wondering why the heck people were fighting over Hadden.

Writing down the text from each NPC I spoke to in Qeynos, and then running all over the Karanas, trying to doing all the
quests they handed out, thinking the rewards would be vast.

Getting pureed by griffons, griffennes, and griffawns in the Commonlands and Karanas, and HATING Verant for putting such high-level mobs in a newbie zone.

Doing my monk sash and headband quests, and hoping Verant would soon fix the next quest in line (black, I think - to my knowledge, it was never fixed).

Camping Aviaks in S Karana for the infinitesimal chance to loot a precious black pearl, and falling out of that stupid birdhouse and dying, even as a monk. Safefall - who needs that?

Roaming through these now-empty zones and feeling sadness for days long gone.



In case you can't tell, my memories of the early days of this game are very fond. This was, and still can be, a magical game, friends. Some of us, myself included, lose sight of that from time to time. Take a step back and look at the world of Norrath. It's still quite remarkable.
