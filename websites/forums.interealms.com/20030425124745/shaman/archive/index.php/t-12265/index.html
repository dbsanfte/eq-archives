Shabby
Not sure what level you are, but if you are in a camp where they are resisitng your slows, cast a debuffer 1st--Malo or Malosini. If there is a chanter in the group, you may want to have them tash. After the defuff, cast yer slow. If there is a Beastlord in the group, they will often land a slow 1st, then you can delay a tad more before landing yours (hence letting others build more aggro 1st).

Even though I tend to slow immediately on pulls to camp, I do not usually get summoned until the 2nd or 3rd resist of slow. If you are getting summoned after 1st resist of slow, maybe your tank is using a very slow 2hander and has not built up taunt yet? Not sure from your post how many resists you had before summons.

As for what happens after you get summoned, I usually stand there and take a beating til the mob is taunted of me, then I wait a minute or two before attempting to slow again.

Even with summoning mobs (especially with summoning mobs?) I find that a quick root will do wonders to help keep the aggro where it belongs. I'll still stand behind the tank's shoulder while I cast, though. It's important that the critter not be standing inside you as you cast, since a lot of your aggro depends on how close you are to the mob.

I try to position myself so that if the mob wants to hit me, it has to stand directly in front of or on top of the tank, giving the meat shield the most proximity-aggro that I possibly can, while avoiding ping pong.
