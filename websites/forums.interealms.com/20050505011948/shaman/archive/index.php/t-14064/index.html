finaljustice
The list is
Ally
Kindly
Amiable
Indifferent
dubious
apprehensive
threatening (percentage of mobs will attack)
Scowling (mobs will attack unless they are green and don't have 'always attack'coding)

Other that attacking, the only three things these cons effect are whether merchants will sell to you at all, selling price for items and whether or not you'll get offered or can complete certain quests.

Killing just about anything that effects a faction positively, effects at least one other faction negatively. It's always a tradeoff....

Plus, there are 'hidden factions,' due to diety or lack thereof affiliation.

BTW, beware of Factions. You might think yer in good with the Scaleybutts of Cabilis if you fight in Seb, but that's only ONE faction in the city, you'd still be KoS with all others.
Jaruden
Actually warmly is in there between kindly and ally.

Also, merchant mobs are always bottomed out at dubious. You may actually be in the "scowling" category for a merchant, but he'll con dubious to you. They used to not do that, so you'd have merchants go chasing kos people around, get out of place, and refuse to sell to anyone for days. Quite a pain, so they made merchants nonkos.

There is a point system for factions, I don't know it off hand, but scowling is the largest range. Killing some mobs give you bigger faction hits than other mobs. For example (not real number) killing the king of ak'anon might lower your faction with him 1000 points, but killing a clockwork sweeper might only drop you by 5. In general the negative faction hits for killing things are always larger than the positive ones.
