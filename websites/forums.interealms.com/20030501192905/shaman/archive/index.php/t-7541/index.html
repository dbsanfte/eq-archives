Yeah, I know...that title makes you just say, "Well, DUH! Haven't you figured that out yet?"

I recommend not practicing alchemy if you're doing in hopes of greater potions later. Alchemy has continuously been a very low priority for Verant. Unless you really need to make SoW or DS potions for people, the rest are a waste of time and money. Don't think about shrink and growth potions anymore...you can get something from a tinkerer that do both of those. Gates? Get one of those new PoP belts.

Let's look at the history of alchemy here:

They swore up and down that alchemy was working in the game, because they'd insta-create a shaman and test it. It wasn't until later when someone there actually made a some lower level shaman and leveled that character that they found out it really wasn't working. That tells me NO ONE there was playing a shaman. Shortly thereafter, the first alchemy went in.

After some complaints about the way that first round of potions worked, alchemy was revamped. The first set of alchemy potions was taken out, and it was replace with the second round of potions.

There were some really big complaints about the replacement potions too. The majority of potions aren't sellable to people mainly because of the negative effects of them.

So, Verant kicks in more potions....but they're not NEW potions, they're the OLD first alchemy potions put back into the game. There are a couple of new potions in there, but nothing all that interesting.

PoP comes out. One new item discovered: Quintessence of Knowledge. It's a low level item that is needed for a gating belt. Nothing else has been discovered, unlike the massive amount of new things that were added for the rest of the tradeskills.

Verant considers this a very low priority.

We need to start seeing things like BOOKS in the game that give us a hint of what to do, rather than make people do all two-herb and three-herb combines.

We need new potions without negative effects, and more interesting effects.

We need to have better buy back rates.

We need a reason to skill up to 180. Gate potions are largely obsolete considering the number of gating items out there.

For a tradeskill that REQUIRES you get to 60 just to top out at 180, it sure should offer a better pay off than it does.
