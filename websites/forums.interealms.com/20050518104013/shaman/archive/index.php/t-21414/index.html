Naagruz
With the first epic there was not a massive gap between high-end raid players and the rest of the population so it was pretty easy to throw together a quest that everyone could chip away at and eventually finish with a few friends helping out (not talking about the faction method here). Most of us picked up a SoF at some point, and it was not an easy bit of gear to replace for several expansions.

The epic 2.0 is "designed for people at the upper end of the LEVEL (65-70??) spectrum" and comprised of "a combination of solo, group, and raid content", but the real difficulty lies in making an epic that is worthwhile to hardcore raiders and not an impossible quest for more casual players. How do you eclipse a weapon like the TA (Time's Antithesis) and yet not hand out too weighty a reward to Joe Casual?

I scraped together a few ideas and this is what I came up with:

1. Epic 2.0 needs to be multi-slot. This might be just primary and range, but more slots = more utility and tends to keep items from being replaced easily. I probably would have kept my first spear around longer if it was able to be thrown in range.

2. Epic 2.0 needs to be improvable by several means with the same ultimate result. Through raid flags, LDoN points, and/or mini-quests (ala iksar cudgel) perhaps. This gives everyone an option and maybe weighting content differently would allow the epic to keep pace with raiders and still keep other players happy. While raiders may arrive at the final result quicker, other players can rest assured that they too will get there some day.

3. Epic 2.0 needs a novel effect(s). Anything from a badass buff to a DoT O' Death or supa-slow mod that we won't have access to in any other form. This gives the epic more utility and possibly makes it worth getting just for the effect if nothing else.

I almost threw some sort of slow spell on as a "needed" item, but if the epic is able to be tossed in range, that really ain't necessary.

Yes my line of thought may be a bit myopic, but does anyone have some useful input? :)
