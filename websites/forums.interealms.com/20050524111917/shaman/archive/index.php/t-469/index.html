Uggok
There are people who don't have 4 hour chunks to devote to the game and only to the game every night, for these people soloing is a great option. For me, oftentimes being glued to the screen all night is not an option, and joining groups just to go AFK for long stretches every half-hour or so is just plain rude, so I solo a lot during those times. When I do have a fairly uninterrupted stretch for gaming, I usually raid.

I never found it to be particularly boring. What makes EQ fun is the other people, and /gu and /tell work just as well when you are soloing. The new chat channels make it even better. Of course, any one kind of playing (solo, group, raid) gets boring for me if I do it too much.

Also the xp is just plain faster solo than most grouping (not duoing), especially if you have any amount of set-up time. (log on, kill kill kill, log off vs log on, LFG, beg for port to another dungeon, LFG, get group, get to camp, leave as soon as you get there when 3 people have to log...). Even without that factor, a 6 person group is going to have to kill about 5x as many mobs to get the same xp over time as a soloist. In lots of places, the soloist can sustain a kill rate of a mob every 2 minutes, but not many places can support a group killing a mob every 12 seconds without running out of mobs.

Anyway, back to what this shaman actually asked. At 59 I had very good luck in these 2 places:

Katta is very very good xp, rarely camped, easy to go AFK if you gotta. Easy. No loot.

SolB imps/freeti. Harder, chance at decent loot (GEBs), stay on your toes. I don't know that this camp would have been viable for me at 59 without fungi/epic, but you might be better than I am :)
