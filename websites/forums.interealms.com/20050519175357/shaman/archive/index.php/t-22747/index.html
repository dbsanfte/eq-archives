Well I logged onto my shaman to do something or other, and got a tell asking to do an LDoN. Of course I jumped on it, and headed to EC. Along the way I asked what role I would play, healer AND slower, back-up healer and slower, or just slower. I was a bit disappointed when I was told only slower. Luckily for myself and the group, I was only slowing. The monk was a zerg puller, which is normally no problem except for the uncanny number of resists even after malos. I fumbled my way through, feeling badly for having given in to altitis for so long.

The group was a one-shot thing, but I noticed another group looking for more, and asked if I could join. Joined the group, buffed us, and we started. Few resists even without malos this time around, had several single adds from proximity aggro from nearby rooms, which I announced I was offtanking once all were slowed. Did well, did ghetto CC, took over heals when the cleric was LOM after a big pull, meleed as well as landed a few fast DoT's. Felt good to be back on my game, and even better to be able to adjust quickly from playing a gnecro to a wizard to a warrior to a beastlord and back to shaman within only hours of each other during my days off.

It's almost 8AM (and bedtime for me) so I'll hit the rack in a few, and wake up to try my hand at some soloing with my shaman, something I haven't done in awhile. For today, my philosophy will be to just do what I want to do regardless of bugs/brokenness/lack of TLC from Devs and let the game evolve around me, even if that means I never finish PoP oreven get started on GoD. Odds are, I won't even be able to participate in OoW, so I won't even buy it unless that changes.
