Well, you really didn't say what kind of content you were doing. Also, who will be playing this character?

If the bard has the right click DS he could throw that on the wizard, then add some cleric/shaman buffs and porcupine in unrest to 30 fairly fast.

The other notable damage dealers would be a ranger, druid for nukes/ds/utility and ports, and monk.

If I had to choose, I'd go with wizard. Hard work at first, then just sit him in the background while he gains easy experience and helps with ports/damage.

Jukax
If it was my group, I would choose an enchanter. If you guys want to gain the most xp in the least amount of time or want to do things that are quite challenging for a single group, go with an enchanter. Bard song + clarity / KEI makes for a group with virtually no down time whatsoever.

If travelling is a big concern for you, go with a wizard. Wizards are also great in a pinch because they can add a whole lot of damage in a very short period of time.

I personally wouldn't choose a druid over a wizard because you've already got a secondary healer in the shaman, and the druid's damage shield, while nice, has greatly diminished returns because of the shaman slowing.

A monk / ranger or another rogue would help you kill faster, yes, but with an enchanter's speed and clarity, I don't think it would make much of an appreciable difference. A warrior in your case doesn't make much sense to me, as the SK can tank extremely well at level 60 and can hold agro like nobody's business. A mage or beastlord can do a lot of damage, and with the pet changes coming up would be a fearsome addition to your group, but don't really measure up to a wizard or enchanter.

In short, in my opinion the key to a good group is diversity. It doesn't really matter what you pick in the long run, as you sound like you already have the foundations for an extremely solid group :)
