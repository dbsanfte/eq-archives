Bigguss
Benefits of turning the horse models off (so you know what will and won't work.)

You can keep your sow speed instead of the horse speed (the speed of the cheapest horse is slower than SoW- turning off the model removes the horse speed effect... so you can cast SoW on yourself, click your epic boots, get snared by the fungi staff, etc.)

You can duck (to interrupt spells)

You don't have the acceleration effect

You don't have to see the horse head all the time

You also keep the automed feature. Basically, when you're standing still and not attacking, you're medding (you don't need to sit down... and you can med while casting/JBB'ing/etc.. If you swing a weapon or move, it doesn't count... but if you're standing still, you're medding... this is huge. One quirk, I've noticed that sometimes the mana bar doesn't update from automed until you do something to trigger it. For example, I'll stand still for a minute and the mana bar won't move. I'll refresh my SoW, and my mana will go UP two bubbles (because it's just now refreshing from the past few minutes.)

By the way.... if you have the fungi staff, the epic boots are your best friends. Click off staff effect, click boots, ready to go...

By turning off the model you lose the safe fall bonus that comes with the horse

Whether the model is on or off, you'll go through a lot more food wihle on a horse than while not riding one. They eat a ton.

Hope this helps.

Grippa
What Bigguss said is essentially the whole story with regards horses, but he missed one point.

When on a horse, you regenerate at the STANDING rate. If you turn off the horse models, you get standing regen whether you are sitting or standing. I can confirm also, that with models off, you DO meditate when standing (but not if moving or attacking).

Essentially, sitting has no effect on your hp/mana recovery when you are on a horse.
