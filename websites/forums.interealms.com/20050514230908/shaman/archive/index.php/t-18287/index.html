Kamidari
You have enrage right... That's when a low hp mod will riposte all frontal attacks, so the MT (and anyone else in front) has to be quick to turn off auto-attack or they'll kill themselves.

Flurry is just extra attacks (up to 3, I think) in a melee round, so the mob can kick out some serious damage if it flurries a lot. It just hits whoever it's currently hitting though.

Rampage is more funky. It's a kind of secondary hate list that the mob keeps. It's basically just based on the order the mob gains hate on anyone, and doesn't change. Whoever's on the top of that list, but who isn't on the top of the main hate list, get hit when the mob flurries.

For instance, MA pulls a mob, and cleric heals him. MA gets on top of rampage list, and cleric is second. When the mob rampages, it hits the cleric, because he's the top of the rampage list who doesn't currently have aggro. If a secondary tank then takes aggro from the MA, the MA will get the rampage damage on the next rampage, because he's above the cleric on the rampage list. That's probably about as clear as mud, but I tried. ;)
Jinheim
Rampage is based entirely off of WHEN you get on the aggro list, and has nothing to do with how much aggro you have generated. Thus, casting malos early on a pull will get you high on the rampage list. The first person to do anything to aggro the mob will be the person getting hit by rampage UNLESS they are the mob's current target, or are out of range. If for some reason the #1 person cannot get hit by rampage, then it hits #2, unless they aren't a valid rampage target, in which case it hits #3 and so on and so forth.

Basically it goes like this:

1 - Puller
2 - Whoever got it off the puller (usually designated tank)
3 - Whoever hit it next (usually main tank)
4 through 15 - clerics and debuffers.

A lot of the times, having your rampage tank die can be even worse than having your main tank die. A new warrior can just AE taunt and take the place of the previous MT, but you can't do anything about the rampage list except plan ahead.

Coccyx Oracle of Thule
Flurry is a combat ability where, the mob who is flurrying will double his normal number of attacks for a given combat round.

Example Avatar of War.

In a given combat round, he rolls a successful double attack check, gaining a chance at 2 hits. In that same combat round, he also rolls a successful flurry check, gaining an ADDITIONAL 2 chances to hit in the same round.

This gives him the potential to deal out (1154 * 4 = 4616) damage in a flurry round, assuming all 4 hits land and do full damage.

If he failed the double attack check, but passed the flurry check, he would have the potential to deal (1154 * 2 = 2308) damage in that round.

If you were unfortunate enough to have a dual wielding AoW (Don't think it's possible, but it's a good use of the example), and he were to pass his double attack check, dual weild check AND flurry check, he could potentially deal out (1154 * 8 = 9232) damage in 1 round and probably wipe out your raid.

This is all applied before any avoidance and mitigation is calculated on your tank and is therefore only POTENTIAL damage.

So a mob who flurrys, instead of having 1 to MAX_ATTACKS attacks per round, will have 1 to (MAX_ATTACKS*2) attacks per round.

Rampage list is static with a couple notable exceptions. The order on rampage list is compleately dependant on order of aggro and nothing else. If your the first one to aggro you could stand doing nothing and you'll stay on rampage till you die. You can become an invalid target tho. Feign, DA, Out or range, Out of zone (aka dead) all make you an invalid target and make rampage move on to the next valid target on the list. You will how ever get back on rampage as soon as your a valid target again.

If you can compleately clear yourself off aggro, which is what fade does every time and feign can do some times, it will take you off the list. One thing that can cause the rampage list to change over time is that the list has a limited length and there seems to be some process of dropping low aggro people off the list to be replaced by higher aggro people.
