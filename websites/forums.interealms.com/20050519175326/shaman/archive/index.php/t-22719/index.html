Slein Jinn
A very large number of Shadowknight spells, including many of our most used spells, are on disease resist. When it comes to adding resist adjustments to the spells, our spells are generally equvilant to other hybrids' (whose spells are on other resists).
For easiest comparison, look at SK spear spells vs. ranger nukes (Ice or Fire - it makes no difference).

Disease is the only resist that is not debuffed by malo spells. Although another line exists to debuff disease, it is rarely used for a number of reasons. This puts shadowknights at a disadvantage in relation to other hybrids (and to an extent, all other classes), as our spells have equvilant resist adjusts, but the resist is debuffed less, meaning we are resisted more.

As a result of this, many SK's have been campaigning for resist adjustments to be added/altered for our spells or for an AA to innately add resist mods to our spells or various other things along those lines. This is the wrong approach, however - a bandaid fix. It would then mean our spells are potentially less resisted if the Malicious Decay line is used, and is simply the wrong approach to fixing the problem regardless.

To fix the problem is quite simple - add disease to the Malo spells and remove or replace the Malicious Decay line. This isn't nerf to shamans - it's a boon, as you would achieve with one spell what currently requires two. It also fixes one of the major gripes of the SK community. A win-win situation for both classes.



The only noteworthy problem I can forsee is with the stacking of the new OoW spells - namely Putrid Decay, Scent of Midnight, and Malo (assuming it were to debuff disease). Currently, Putrid Decay and Scent of Midnight both debuff Disease and Poison, stack with Malo, but not eachother or Malicious Decay. This seems like it might be another problem, though (perhaps you've discussed it - I havn't had a chance to follow other class's boards as closely as I'd like lately due to RL schedule and computer problems).

Traditionally, necro Scent spells have stacked with all other resist debuffs (and debuffed fire also) and the Decay line has debuffed only disease, thus the above marks a notworthy and seeminly unwarrented change for both spell lines. It also means that disease will potentially fall further behind still without the ability to stack the Decay and Scent lines. Also, in the absence of a Necro, it will give poison a potential advantage over the other resists.

I'm not going to suggest anything regarding that issue at this time though.

EDIT: Did some searching and it appears that it has been discussed, with no conclusive results... Guess we'll have to leave that in the unknown category for now, unless someone wants to link something more revealing than what I've found.



Note that it could be argued that the fact that the Decay line is unresistable starting before the introduction of unresistable Malo spell would be a problem, but I can't forsee it being a serious one. If it were deemed a noteworthy issue, it could be fairly easily resolved by leaving the lines separate pre-60, however.

Now to do that, you have two options:
1) Split the mage and shaman spell lines, giving shamans a line that debuffs all resists, and leaving mages on the current line.
2) Add a disease debuff to the current sinia line, giving mages a new ability.

Problems with these:
1) Since (at least in my guild) mages are often the ones debuffing with malosinia (since they get the right click version of it), if malosinia continued to overwrite the new and improved malos you'd have to either talk to your guild and get the shamans to take that over, or you'd lose the benefits of having this disease debuff. Asking the mages to give up debuffing might make them feel like they have even less utility (I don't play a mage, I'm not sure if they'd be happy to give that up or not, but it's something to think about).
2) This gives mages a new ability, making them even closer in resist debuffing abilities to what shaman currently have, further eroding any edge that shaman might have.

Possible solutions:
1) Let malosinia and malos stack, so if a mage cast malosinia you'd retain the disease debuff aspect of malos. Note: If you did this you wouldn't have to split the spell lines, but I think shaman would still want them split (for convenience), in which case make the new shaman malosinia overwrite mages malosinia and malos.
2) Same solution as 1 but do not split the spell lines. This does add stacking issues for malicious decay though, as you wouldn't want that to overwrite malos either, leaving the possibility of having three buffs on the mob just to debuff resists from a shaman.

I personally like number 1 better, including the stacking of malosinia and malos, and splitting the spell lines. If I were you though, I'd also ask mages what they thought, as I'm sure they'll have an opinion either way.
