Kkrush
Face it. Myself, Geddar, Final Justice, Axejst, ...you name it, the old shaman are tired of fighting the fight. The war has taken it's toll on us, and we are shell-shocked. Exausted and worn, beaten and torn, we pass the heavy burden of our roles onto the younger, the "next generation". As we old Bears strap our spears to our sides for the last time, never to wield them again, as we allow Vobtik, our mighty and trusty warhound to roam again a free spirit, and as we close our spellbooks, to collect dust and yellow with age, we have to wonder about those who take up the slack. Will they augment our allies and disempower our enemies as we did, or will they find better ways to do it? Will they understand what we were all about, why we loved our place in the game? Do they feel the same, or do they play a misguided choice, regretting their path?

EQ still going strong, after all this time...amazing. All the friends I still have, and the ones I left behind, those I cared for or at least managed to get along with...I can only hope a strong Spear of Fate is behind them, bringing out the maximum capability of his allies and standing beside them, crushing their foes with detrimental forces only we could bring about.

For those greying shaman, bent and old but with the fight left in them, and those young, with fire in their eyes and chanting in ancient tongues....please take care of the friends we left behind. It is your Spear that supports them, your shoulders that bear the burden, and may Rallos Zek give you strength to get off that last heal, the final debuff and park that last add.

It's all up to you, fellow Heyokah. We are trusting you to care for those we left behind, so we might rest easy.

SnowwSilverhammer
In bear form I am old and majestic, An elder in title and in truth.

I walked the permafrost when times were new. Before the age of the gods and before the fires of heaven scoured the land.

I battled bear cub and goblin, with nothing more than an old stick and the knowledge that my calling as a shaman would be strong.

I walked in the night when it was truly dark. Before the vengeful bards took shape and wandered the icy plains. Before crafters learned to make the tundra rings of ice into armour. Before the lands were mapped and weighed and measured.

I have killed Hill giants with only my mutt and my spirits around me when the deed was considered impossible but for those of the dark arts. I walked Into lower Guk when the place was fabled and new.

When Kunark was discovered, I carried the long arm of northern Justice to the sarnak. And to the undead in the ruins of Kaesora. I watched as frost froze the ichor of the emporer of long dead seblis in his spectral form.

I have wandered and led in the icy wastes of velious. Friend to dragon and slayer of giant. Much like the long times past in the mountains of rathe. I was charmed and cursed in the Sirens grotto in the first day of walking that blasted land. I have raised my spear in salute of The Darkpaw and slain Yedelma in honarable combat. After many had done so before me. And many would come after me.

I walked the Moon of luclin, once again delivering the Justice of the tribunal. And trying to rid the land of shadow. I have braved the scourge of the temple of Ssra, and walked in the airless depths of the grey itself.

I have met my lords and protecters in the halls of Justice. I have stood before them and been Judged. And not found wanting. I wander now in the Halls of Honor, and the plane of tactics Proving myself with the stregnth of my arm and spirits and with the friends of all my past arrayed behind me.

I am the tip of a spear aimed at the neck of the boar. The speed of the wolf inside me, the pack baying at my heels. I am a caller of spirits and a weilder of poisen and disease. I cripple and call upon the insects to weaken my enemies. I am a healer of my people and of all the races that stand with me. I am the growl of the Kodiaks that still live on the northern plains of my home.

It has been a long Journy. One of fun and fright. Filled with laughter and pain, as old friends fall by the road and new friends pick up the pace.

But one thing will endure. The memories of the Silverhammer. Standing in the firelight, looking out over the frost. The memories of the good and evil that I have come across in my jorney are still strong. And thankfully they are not at an end. My spear is still in my hand. And the Hammer of spirits is still only a reach away.

Justice will rule in the far north for some time to come.

Trebaar
As I consider all that has been said
I ponder on the runes wind-written in the snow
Feeling old I was before these things I read
And now the Shaman spirit sparks and starts to flow.

Sixty winters long and biting cold
I have endured, and one beside.
But at the ancient Shaman's words so bold
I am but young, and as a Shaman will abide.

There's much to hunt, and seek, and learn
And lands I have not dared to brave
And though the spells of evil wights may burn
I'll stand by friends, and fight, and seek to save.

On pages tattered, bent and stained
I read the stories of the great and wise
As firelight flickers, hunts explained
And stratagems that others did devise

All these and more I store against the day
When I, with such a journey still to go
Will need them, then my debt of thanks I'll pay
As each sweet blow falls crippling on the foe.

So take heart, all of you brave souls and good
There are still Shaman proud and learning fast
True to our friends and calling, as we should
True also to the memory of the past.
