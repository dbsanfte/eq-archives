veeshanMurtak
Amen.

Designing onesided content leads to classes being marginalized in that content. Designing a whole expansion that way will leave some classes lagging behind.

For all purposes there is a single MOB type in all of GoD (and in way too much of EQ) - it deals a lot of melee damage, it has few HPs. The only variables are slowability, rootability, charmability, mezzability and stunnability. All these abilities have one thing in common - they avoid damage. Apparently this is bad, so you have to make MOBs immune. Of course if the content was varied there would not be a need to do this.

If you have a swarm of 6 MOBs slow, stun and the likes are inherently devalued, while the value of AEs, offtanks(including pets) and group heals goes up.

If you have a MOB with huge regeneration the value of DPS goes up.

If you have a MOB with a ton of HPs the value of DoTs goes up.

If you have casters that actually do damage the value of stun goes up.

And if you actually combine all of those in a single zone you can have every member of your group have an area they shine in and an area they suffer in.

Of course it would make the copy/paste design of LDoN and GoD harder.

I think the big difference between the success of PoP Vs the utter disappointment of GoD is the lack of character advancement. When PoP came out, doing the lower tier zones at level 60 with the then equipment was difficult for the majority of folks. Sure, the content was hard, but through items/raiding/equipment/aa these things were at least eventually conquerable.

For GoD there is seemingly little way to prepare and improve yourself for the new content that is offered. The new AAs do not really offer a significant power improvement (in offense or defense) that would help the average player feel they could accomplish a lot.

Now they are planning on a new expansion with new levels and AA. Things that should have been added when GoD came out. There are arguments that increasing levels is bad, but I disagree. Sure it can trivialize older content, but it is required to give players the needed sense of achievement that it is possible to improve themselves! The best thing they could do is come out with a expansion that would wipe the floor with 95% of the best equipped players in the game but that offered a way to improve your character so that the content would be conquerable.

I really hope SOE takes a look at some of the new games coming out and implements some of the features from them. I have been playing CoH recently and the fact that a group of players can effectively and quickly tackle a group of 12-24 mobs is incredible. And the down time does not suck. Perhaps Sony can learn a thing or two about increasing the pace, while keeping the challenge intact.

With this new expansion and increase in character development, I think a lot of concerns that affect shaman currently have the opportunity to be dealt with. Hopefully this next expansion will be good, or else I foresee even more people leaving the game.
