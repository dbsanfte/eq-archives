This feedback is written from my viewpoint as a Grandmaster Fletcher (205), Ranger and Grand Master Archer. I understand acrylia doesn't even drop in the Acrylia Caverns but only in the Tenebrious Mountains.

Most bow users are Rangers; in fact our 55th discipline revolves around it and our Luclin spells and AA skills too. Yet I heard conflicting feedback from the last Fan Faire, someone said archery is not an important form of combat for rangers or anyone and that a possible epic mark 2 for rangers would be a bow? We have countless concerns with archery: line of sight, vertical targeting, mobs in walls, no minimum damage, no large delay bonus, arrows disappearing etc and no one is willing to treat this as important as its not seen as a primary form of combat. And please can Rangers have a 252 Archery cap.

To decrease my frustration and increase my enjoyment of the game and enjoy roleplaying my Pathfinder Elf I have some ideas on helping listed below. I don't think these are major changes and don't just impact one Trade skill. The Coldain Shawl quest is a great example of how I believe you want to improve the usage of Trade skills. Here's another from the viewpoint of a Fletcher/Archer.

Recipes :

Chisel (Smithing), Ore Splitting (Smithing)
Large bricks and blocks of acrylia are good for no recipes I understand and are sold to vendors. Could there be a Smithed item called a Chisel, which splits a Large Block into 2 or 3 large bricks? A large brick into 2 small bricks? A small brick into 2 small pieces? You can combine to create blocks so why not the reverse? This could also work for Velium.

Ceramic (Pottery), Velium (Smithing) Arrow shafts
As Smiths can already make acrylia and steel arrow shafts could there be a recipe for velium arrow shafts? Also one for potters to make ceramic shafts?

Acrylia, Velium (Smithing) and Ceramic (Pottery) arrowheads
Steel arrowheads make 10 magic arrows why not these 3 materials? Therefore arrowhead receipes are needed. The ceramic ones could involve longer range? Could the velium be enchantable to enable a velium shard proc?

Steel, Acrylia, and Velium (Smithing) bow staves plus Ceramic bow staves (Pottery)
In the Bowyer book bought at Merchants it has always mentioned Ceramic and Steel Bow Staves as being more powerful than Darkwood. These were never introduced. Why not add the recipes for staves for the four materials above? The Velium one could even be enchantable first? This would also encourage other trade skill use by Fletchers who can't afford the 244pp or 390pp for Darkwood or Shadewood bow staves? BTW you need to update that book to include Shadewood for the book version bought on Luclin?

Steel, Acrylia, Velium and Ceramic bows (Fletching)
The logical step from the receipes above for the staves. For example one material results in lower damage lower delay? Another longer range less damage more delay? Another larger damage more delay (the normal) and enchanted velium could even make a bow with a plus to dex or archery skill? All other combinable items then can be added, cams, strings, planes. So a person could go for super fast low delay high range or max damage long delay longer range etc. Some of these weapons may need minimum strength to use? Or minimum required level or archery skill? The enchanted velium could mean velium proc on arrow hit? (up to 12 dam?). The new recipes for the Shadewood bows give you different ranges depending on the number of cams used, this feature could also be introduced on these stave bow recipes.

Bow Cams
Bow cams either bought or made by a tinker decrease a bows delay and also its damage. This would vary the resultant bows created for staves of lower quality than Shadewood. Again in line with what has been happening with Luclin items. This does not make uber bows.

These receipes make what ore drops for Acrylia and Velium more flexible in its use. It gives 3 trade skills more receipes. I know I would start pottery if these items were in the game. Fletching provides a bow user more choice and they need situational bows rather than the one they have. Certain Luclin no drop weapons are situational why not Trade skill items?

I hope these suggestions are useful and I'd love some of the them to be introduced. If you want more information contact me directly or my fellow rangers at The Ranger's Glade website for their views.

To be perfectly blunt and honest, Verant's tradeskill system has always been fairly weak, time consuming, and in general, a pain in the ass. Ultimately, many of the trade skills don't provide you with anything that you can't get simply on your own through raids and such.

What does Verant really need to do? I know some people are tired of hearing this, but Verant really needs to look at how Dark Ages of Camelot applied tradeskills. DAOC has some awesome ideas about this - when I was playing DAOC the tradeskill system was the most impressive thing about it to me. In DAOC, the tradeskills were actually USEFUL....so useful, in fact, that crafted armor and weapons were frequently better than anything you could find even in dungeons until you got to a high level (which in that game was past 40th).

Some of the things that made the tradeskill system in DAOC great:

1) While practicing your tradeskills, you would create things, and actually sell them back to the NPC merchants (if you wished) for a small profit. EQ has always been dead set against this and have quickly nerfed anything of the sort. A rather disappointing stance, I must say.

2) Things made with tradeskills in DAOC (namely armor and weapons) were enormously popular because of the high quality that could be crafted. Think about this for a minute....in this day and age, who bothers to buy banded, fine steel plate, or any of the imbued armor? Who bothers to buy crafted or imbued weaponry? No one in EQ does. Why? Simply because a player can spend less, or even get for free, something better on their own by dungeon delving and slaying monsters. Verant has made dungeon delving and slaying monsters the only really vialble way to aquire equipment. Remarkably linear, and limited, thinking on their part, and a rather huge disappointment.

3) Most of the items in DAOC can be made with materials that can be found on the merchant NPCs in town. Its still very time consuming, and still challenging, but you normally don't have to travel through the four corners of the world gathering rare components for one item. In EQ, this is becoming very commonplace. To make the situation worse, EQ is making players who wish to raise their skills to be able to do it via only one way, and that one way usually involves making a product who's components are either rare, or/and are scattered about Norrath and must be hunted for.

I sincerely hope that Verant has learned some valuable lessons on their tradeskill system. In EQ2 I would love to see the tradeskill system a vialable alternative to aquiring items, weapons, armor, etc. by dungeon delving and slaying monsters for equipment. Its much too late for that now in EQ, but a diverse and useful tradeskill system would be a boon to their future endeavors in EQ2 by making tradeskills an alternative to the linear "hack n' slash" system EQ is based totally on right now.
