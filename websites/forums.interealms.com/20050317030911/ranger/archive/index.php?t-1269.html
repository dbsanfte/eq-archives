(From off the top of my head)

You might be an old DOS DInosaur diehard if......


- you go out of your way to avoid the use of the word "Folder" and encourage others to do the same.

- you view the folder as a graphical representation of a Directory.

- you install a game and automatically rename the directory to 8 character's or less with no spaces purely out of habit but mainly because you feel safer about it.

- You have personnal issues with the defualt install directory of C:\Program Files\....

- you remember when windows first came out and thought of it as a fancy menu system for users too stupid to learn DOS.

- you can tell the difference between IBM DOS and MS DOS; and you thought 4DOS was pretty cool.

- you can remember loading various TSR's into your autoexec.bat

- you know what a TSR is.

- your thankful you know longer have to make boot disks for Extended and Expanded memory configurations but at the same time curse windows.

- you can build and configure a new PC with nothing but a single floppy disk, and have it running without never inserting anything Windows related into the machine.

- you remember when 3.5 floppys came out

- you remember having to park a hard drive.

- you remember when a 20 meg hard drive was ALOT of storage.

- you know what a Hercules graphics adapter is.

- you remember flipping dip switches to configure your machine.

- you remember when upgrading your computer involved installing a Math CoProcessor.

- you've ever configured and ran a TCP/IP connection without windows on an IBM PC machine.

- you still think of pentiem or AMD PC's as IBM clones.
