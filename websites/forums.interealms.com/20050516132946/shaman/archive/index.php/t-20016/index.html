Macnair
I was wondering where your AA would do most good.

Shaman dps has its problems in many ways. In many circumstances it can be quite significant. In many other situations it is woefully bad. Our lack of effective dps is an ongoing complaint of the shaman community. Still, in many cases there are things you can do to hit harder.

If you are soloing a lot, critical dots will add a significant amount of dps. Critical nukes will (mostly) not help you here, because you won't be nuking much: nukes break your root, and you don't want that.

If you are doing a lot of fast-paced LDoN, critical nukes will add a significant amount of dps. Critical dots will (mostly) not help you here, because you won't be dotting much: most mobs die in a little under 30 seconds, so even if you cast Bane on incoming, you won't get full damage. Plus if you have to root the add, heal someone, or slow -- sometimes you slow in LDoN, sometimes you don't -- then you might get two or three ticks in is all.

But suppose you are fighting mobs in PoP, or suppose your pace in LDoN is a little more relaxed. If you are taking a minute or so to kill a mob, you could have one dot, possibly two dots, running efficiently. Critical dots will make a difference here.

Critical dots are new, and I think they will become a strong feature of shaman dps. Over time I would expect shamans to max this out: I certainlyl intend to.

But before Sony added those in, I had found that critical nukes were already making a signficant difference for me.

When we are wanting to burn down a mob fast, I'll be chain casting Tears of Saryrn, immediately following with Spear of Torment. The rain spell does get resisted much more than it should be, but it is still the best burst damage that we have. Typically, with four chances for my combo cast to hit, I get three. Sometimes less. Sometimes none.

On the other hand, when you cast a lot of nukes, crits show up pretty often. Double crits happen often enough to be quite noticeable. And my personal best:

Macnair hits a Steelslave Surgeon for 1300 points of non-melee damage.
Macnair has delivered a critical blast!
Macnair hits a Steelslave Surgeon for 650 points of non-melee damage.
Macnair hits a Steelslave Surgeon for 1300 points of non-melee damage.
Macnair has delivered a critical blast!
Macnair hits a Steelslave Surgeon for 1424 points of non-melee damage.
Macnair has delivered a critical blast!
A Steelslave Surgeon has been slain by Macnair.

There was a brief pause. Then the monk said, "Damn, I got to petition that. They need to nerf shaman dps."

4600+ damage, all landing in about 9 seconds, is something over 500 dps of burst damage. That's only happened one time in my playing career, and the number of times when I've gotten all resists or mitigates is way too many to count. (Last week I got a crit for -- hmmm, maybe 34 hp of damage. A crit.)

Still. The one time it did happen, it was really really fun.

The bottom line for me is: you want both. Because you want to be able to hit the hardest you possibly can, in a long drawn out fight: you want critical dots. And you want to be able to hit the hardest you possibly can, in short burst fights: you want critical nukes.

This may not pertain to the majority seeking answers on whether DOTs or DDs provide more DPS but after your guild reaches Time, you will probably want to be using Nukes.

I've got a 6000 mana pool (and that can go much higher) and rarely ever run out of mana. After you obtain Times Antithesis off of Saryrn II in Time, conserving mana becomes even less important as your Turgurs are fast and free.

I've been concentrating on maximum speed and damage for my Nukes. Here's what I've done so far:

-Obtained Times Antithesis which provides free mana, zero recast time Turgurs.
-Obtained Cord of Potential from Vallon Zek II with Quickening of Solusek focus (detrimental spell haste 23%)
-Maxed Fury of Magic Mastery AAs
-Obtained Barrier of Freezing Winds shield off Innoruuk II with the focus Wrath of E`ci, (Increase damage of Ice Based DD by 35%) (nice regen VII on this as well)

On raids or in LDoN, this allows me to drop Velium Strike DD bombs continuously with a 6 second cast time. With the increased damage and crits, I hit for non crit max ~1200 and crit max 2400hps. Someone may have the stats, but through experience, it seems like I'm landing crits close to 25% of the time with Fury of Magic Master Maxed.

Even on lengthy God battles I don't run out of mana and help contribute significant damage. Note: of course you can't be dropping DD bombs all the time whenever you're needed for CC, group healing or curing etc.

In LDoN, with A TA equipped, you rarely need to Malos as slows are free and fast and if you are resisted or interrupted, there's zero recast time and a very fast cast time to avoid being interrupted again by a mob beating on you. I don't parse myself but someone else said I was doing ~140dps on LDoN raids even before I maxed AA and obtained the Increased damage shield so when people are looking for DPS on LDoN raids you can explain that you are a CC/DPS combo and get more groups : )

----------

Another reason to max out the Fury AAs is if you have a weapon that procs DD. If you like to melee you may want to try this out.

-Max Fury of Magic Mastery
-Obtain Clay Bracers of the Golem in Plane of Earth (permanent 41% haste) in Plane of Fire
-Obtain Staff of Eternal Flames 2H, 50dmg 35 Delay, Procs Flames of Ro 400 DD with -300 Resist Adjust.

Slow mob, FA yourself and have at it! The staff procs the 400hp DD like mad and with Fury AA's maxed, crits for 800hps DD damage quite often...several times a fight. Combined with the 41 haste and even your own SLTW haste, you're doing some serious damage without using mana.

I realize that this may not help all levels of shaman but it may be something you might consider if looking for ways to maximize your DPS effectiveness. I haven't used DOTs in the past year or so and have found that mobs just die to fast for them to be effective at the higher levels.
