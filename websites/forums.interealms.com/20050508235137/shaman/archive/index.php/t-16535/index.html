Hi there,

Actually you are just about the perfect level to max out both your weapon skills and your defense (which is often overlooked and slow to raise). The reason is the mobs still dont hit too hard compared to your level.

Now, I very rarely suggest soloing because I think it is a bit boring and lacking in social interaction. But.......for the next 2 levels you should take puppy and hit the blue mobs in Overthere. Just avoid the tigers - tigeress is fine. The best mobs are the cacti and the chickens

You are the tank, puppy chews at the mob. No need to slow since the more hits you take the faster your defense skill will rise. Make sure you keep dex strength and agi on you because they help raise weapon skills and defense. This will teach you a few things which are invaluable:

1)how to pull singles
2)how to keep puppy alive
3)how to park puppy and spam pet back off (lol)
4)how to cast spells when you are in the face
5)how to root park if you screw up
6)how to appreciate a fast pulling group because this will seem a bit slow

etc.etc.

This may not seem to be that important to you now, but trust me these are the situations where you learn to be a good shaman and it carries over to grouping.

Calabar
Also use the lowest delay weapon you can get your hands on. The smaller the delay the more times you’ll attack and the more chances you’ll have for a skill up. If you are soloing and actually trying to kill the mob, you may have to balance delay with damage, but if you’re in a willing group or soloing something just for skill, don’t worry about the damage. In fact, the less damage you do the longer the mob lives and the more practice you get.

At 61 I’m still trying to max out my weapon skills. Offence and defense are both maxed, as is 1H blunt, but 2H blunt and piercing still need work. I have a nice little camp of DB mobs I can solo with my puppy. The xp isn’t great and the loot is even worst, but since I’m tanking my weapon skills are slowly improving. So don’t sweat it too much if you don’t manage to max out your weapon skills every level. Do so if you can, but its not going to hurt you that much if you don’t.

The main combat skill you need to worry about is your defense. You may or may not spend a lot of time later on beating mobs about the head and shoulders with your weapon of choice. But I can guarantee that you will have to deal with mobs in your fact while you’re trying to cast a spell. The higher your defensive skill the better the chance that you’ll get that spell off. So get up close and personal with those mobs whenever you get the chance and let them beat on you. It’ll help toughen you up.

Karshak_rn
I am also wondering what you mean by cripple them? Do you mean slow?

cripple is a level 53 shaman spell that lowers ac, str, dex, agi by a large number.

At level 44 you get incapacitate which is a lower version of the spell.

These lower the damage dealt by a mob, you really don't want to be slowing them if you are simply out for the skill ups (as long as you can keep up with the healing) because you will be slowing the amount of chances you get per minute of a skill up occuring.
