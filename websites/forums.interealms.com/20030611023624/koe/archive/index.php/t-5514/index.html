Moebeus
That's a video driver issue or could be that AGP4X isn't liking something. Here's a few things to try.

Use the driver version 22.80 www.guru3d.com for the download
That is the recommended driver that VI says to use for Geforce cards.
Before switching drivers download the detonator destroyer from there so make sure all old drivers are out. Go to add/remove programs and remove any video drivers that are listed. Then start det destroying and click the uninstall button.. reboot and reload the video drivers from wherever you saved and extracted the drivers to.


If that doesn't work then check out www.geforcefaq.com and go through some of the stability things it says with settings in your bios.

As to general questions, over 512MB is only a problem on Win98. With constant errors it sounds like you have some kind of OS corruption, bad memory, or something HW related. With Ravi's if it is erroring during an install it is probably bad or the wrong type of memory. Take out all but 1 chip (assuming 256 or 512 chips) and try again, then swap around and see if it makes a difference.

Oh and if this is a new system that you bought as components then take back that Geforce4 MX 440 card, it's power is less than the Geforce 3 TI200 cards and about the same price. Those are the bottom of the barrel cards and you'll be better off with the newer model coming out soon or with a GF3Ti200 card of your choice.

Here's a review of the Gforce 4 at Tom's Hardware Guide.

http://www6.tomshardware.com/graphic/02q1/020206/index.html

He notes that the MX Series is not true DX8 compatable, in the following quote from the 'conclusions' of the review.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~SNIP~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

When you look at the three different GeForce4 MX offerings, it is easy to see that the low-end version, the GeForce4 MX420, will be a product for OEMs only. Its 166 MHz SDRAM memory will slow down the performance of the GeForce4 MX chip so much that it will hardly perform any better than previous GeForce2 MX400 cards that you can get for a mere 50 bucks.

The performance of the GeForce4 MX440 and 460 cards, however, proved to be very impressive. Especially the GeForce4 MX460, which is able to score results as high as those of the GeForce3 Ti200, or even the Ti500 cards. Both are able to beat ATi's Radeon 7500 in most cases.

Still, none of the GeForce4 MX cards has full DirectX 8.x support, because of its lack of vertex and pixel shaders. This weighs heavily when you remember the lowest priced offering out of the GeForce4 Ti bunch of cards. It's only $20 more than GeForce4 MX460.

I therefore suggest the GeForce4 MX440 for value buyers, but they should try to get the price down to less than $149. Better yet, a value buyer should watch out for GeForce3 cards. Any GeForce3 card that costs less than $190 is a bargain right now. GeForce3 comes with full DirectX 8.x functionality, vertex/pixel shaders and lots of 3D power! If you can chose between a GeForce3 card and a GeForce4 MX card for similar prices, I would clearly go for the GeForce3!

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~SNIP~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Given that the vertex and pixel shaders are one of the defining features of the Gforce4, I believe I'd take the 'I need to exchange this product, it's not compatable with my PC' approach and upgrade to either a Full GF4 or a GF3.


NOTE TO READERS: Some newer motherboards (Intel 850 and 845 chipsets in particular) are NOT compatable with AGP2X Video cards, (AGP2X runs at 3.3v as opposed to 1.5v AGP4X). If your video card tries to run in AGP2X Mode, it will burn and damage the mainboard. I've been doing a lot of Socket-478 Pentium 4's lately (Gigabyte Mainboards ROCK) and they ALL have this warning. They all insist on an AGP4X Video card.

Moebeus
Hasn't been mentioned but here are some other basic things to check.

IRQ on Video, Sound, NIC
Make sure they are all different and there is no device sharing on any of those IRQ's. Sometime USB will using the same as the Video so watch out for that if you have any USB devices.

Few other spots for drivers. www.the-ctrl-alt-del.com, www.m3dzone.com, www.guru3d.com

I'd recommend the 22.80 drivers then 27.31 if the first ones don't help.

Question: When it locks can you ctrl-alt-del and end task on EQ or do you have to reboot? If it locks and you have to reboot then it is more likely a conflict on IRQ or something. If you can end task then it sounds more like a driver issue.
