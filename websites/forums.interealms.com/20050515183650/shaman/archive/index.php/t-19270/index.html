Emmhim
It happens more and more often for me that an LDoN adventure is delayed because people prefer waiting for an ideal (or close to ideal) group. Needed is:
* Main tank (prefer paladin or shadow knight)
* Healer (cleric - druid might do)
* Snarer
* Slower (= me)
* Pacify for pulling (i.e. cleric or paladin)
* Ressing (i.e. cleric or paladin)
* More dps


But this often results in waste of time and less fun. My best LDoN adventure ever was like this:

Receive call "Want to be main healer in LDoN?". I guessed the call was from another shaman, so slowing was handled and that the person could not find a cleric or druid. Accepted and hurried to the adventure camp.

When seeing the group, I realised that the slower was a beastlord, and I was the only priest class. Group was:
* shaman
* wizard
* paladin
* beastlord
* ranger
* one more dps class - forgotten which.

The member was all 65 or close.
Not the ideal group, but close. Only a cleric or druid for healing was missing.

Collect adventure, normal setting.

On second pull, the paladin went LD. No main tank, no pacify, no LoH for emergency, less dps, no res.
Leaving the adventure was naturally not an option, so we redistributed the roles:
* Beastlord slowing and main tank
* Wizard pulling, kiting adds and dps as time permitted
* Shaman healer and backup slower

I was a little worried over having a low HP wizard pull, but I soon stopped worrying. That wizard knew what he was doing.

We flew through the adventure, succeeding with 40 minutes left, even killing a named on the process. I had more fun that I have ever had in any LDoN adventure before.

So for all the people who are going for the ideal adventure - I can understand that people go for the ideal for a hard 65 adventure, but for anything easier, don't cheat yourself for a lot of fun by having a less ideal group. The classes are not that important - it is how they are played that really matters.
