It depends on your wis.

Once you hit high 180's in wis, it starts becoming less of an issue. Before that, you probably are hurting by running OOM during battles.

Once you get a wis level you're comfortable with - a good sized mana pool that enables you to not go OOM easily - I'd go HP. It's never to early to start, and most hp gear has good AC anyway.

Str is worthless for us. We have good strength buffs, plus the rage line that stacks on top, and with that, plus str that comes naturally on my hp gear, I easily max out my str.

I know, at 33, I make a pathetic tank, but my usuall P is a Druid, and thats worse than Shamy at tanking.[/B]

With 825(ish) AC, you should be tanking extremely well at lvl 33 unless you have low HP, or you're not slowing the mobs.

You'll find that as you lvl and your slows, pets. and buffs get better, you can let Doggy tank more while you stand back and cast. (Specifically, the 34 pet is a horrible tank and the 39 pet isn't much better. Let the 44 pet tank till he's about 25% health, then you should take aggro from him. He regens hp in between fights faster than you do. My 49 pet tanks 75% of the time and the only time I even think about healing him is when I kill several mobs in a row without any breaks.)

Regen/HP > AC > WIS/Mana This applies at all lvls.

The more hp and regen you have, the less often you have to heal mid-battle. This frees up mana to dd/dot each mob more, and the faster the mob dies the less healing you have to do.

Sure, you can cast more spells before you go oom if you have a larger mana pool, but then you also have to cannibalize more and med longer to get the larger pool full again.
