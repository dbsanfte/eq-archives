Kiyrwen
I did a bit of testing in the last few weeks because i was interested in how archery works and wanted to be able to predict what kinds of hits i can get with a certain bow and arrow setup. Ok this is what i discovered and found interesting:

.)Maximum hit
For how much you can hit with archery mainly depends on your level or better your damage table. Anyway, it's quite simple, maximum normal hit is:
(bowdmg+arrowdmg)*modifier
The modifier is 2.85 from level 60 to 62, 2.9 through 63 and 64, and finally 2.95 at level 65.
I am not sure about lower levels but from my experience it is hard to hit this cap before regardless because you don't have high enough atk/dex during everyday archery use for pulling and kiting. On raids and in exp groups when you have the good buffs archery is pretty useless without Trueshot and the discipline skews the results. So you kinda "softcap" before you hit this hard level cap.

.)Minimum hit
There is no minimum damage for archery!
I have heard people claim we have a bonus of 1 point therefore hitting for a minimum of 2. I have found this not to be true, however they are not completly wrong.
It seems to depend on your bow+arrows setup, with either Primal Bow using 11+4 damage arrows and WBoRZ i have not been able to find a hit for less than 2 basehit. However using 1 damage arrows with a Primal Bow i was able to score quite a few 1s and using a Hunting Bow (yeah the vendor bought 5 dmg one hehe) i was seeing a whole lot.
My conclusion is it has probably the same reason as the 2 damage minimum hits with high damage offhanders people are reporting. Not a direct damage bonus as there is for the mainhand but rather an effect caused by the way the damage is calculated. Even though i don't know much about the algorithims Everquest uses, i just accept it is this way. :)

.)Order of bonuses
Damage calculation for an archery hit seems to be done in this order:
1.) Compute what the hit was for using whatever obscure algorithms. Round to nearest integer.
2.) Apply Archery Mastery modifier. Round again.
3.) Apply double damage bonus for unrooted, nonmoving targets.
4.) Apply critical hit modifier if you scored one. More on that later.

Reasoning:
The value must be rounded after the inital damage computation because else we could see uneven hits with AM3 (plain 2.0 modifier = double) which don't exist, they are all even numbers (multiples of 2).
Also double damage hits are always even numbers so the value must have been an integer before it was doubled else we could get uneven numbers. This just does not happen however, so it's rounded before.
AM modifier must be applied before double damage because else there could be uneven double damage hits with AM1(1.3 modifier) and AM2(1.6 modifier), which i have never seen personally.
Not sure where Trueshot modifier is applied but it's before doubledamage.
Critical modifier has to be applied last because it is possible to get uneven AM3/double damage critical hits.

.)Critical hits
This brings me to my next point, critical hits. I already started a thread about it a while ago but it was off the frontpage in general pretty quickly. Anyway, my point back then was that a single modifier could not produce all the criticals we see, not mathematically possible if it is a straight factor. You can use search if anyone really cares heh.
Now we, or I at least, always thought the critical hit modifier is around 1.72 but as i found out it actually is exactly 1.7, yes this alone does not work out but the simple solution is 8 dmg is added in the end. So it's:
(hitdamage*1.7)+8
So easy and it took me so long to find out. :\

Another interesting thing i found out is about the minimum critical hit.
At first didn't even know there was one but thinking about it i had not seen a critical below 83 for ages. So i looked a bit into it and found out it depends on your combined bow + arrow damage once again, it is:
(bowdmg+arrowdmg)*1.7+8
This was what brought me to the 8 that is added to crits actually.
It always applies no matter if double damage, AM or trueshot was involved, minimum critical hit is always this value. I also think it applies to all levels, at lower levels this will be the only critical hit value you will see actually.
Another thing to note is elemental damage is figured into the minimum crit, however as it can be resisted you don't get the absolute minimum crit if you use it for your calculation.
What it explains though is why the most common low critical hits with a Primal Bow and 11+4 arrows are 83(full resist) and 90(all elemental damage went through), additionally you can see all possible hits between, that is 85, 86 and 88 if elemental damage is partially resisted.

Well, that's all so far, just wanted to share. If this was all common knowledge screw you all for not telling me, heh. :P
