Well it's been well over 2 months and I ask everyone to ask themselves the following question.


Are they satisfied with the Everquest product they purchased in the form of Gates Of Discord or the monthly service charge that is intended to upkeep and maintain the game for them?


If not and you are curious about what actions you can take, I submit the following information for your perusal. If you have a complaint regarding SOE's services or product as substandard than what you expect, you can take the following recourse ESPECIALLY if you felt your complaints have been largely ignored.

Please note, frivolous complaints cannot be expected to be taken seriously and will only hurt your cause.


Sony Online Entertainment is a member of the Better Business Bureau (BBB). This organization is something of a consumer watchdog that ensures all members (businesses) are operating in an ethical manner. To carry the name of the Better Business Bureau is to indicate to everyone that they conduct their business fairly and attend to their customers promptly.

If you are unsatisfied with a product or service, you may lodge a complaint with the BBB and they will forward the complaint to SOE. While you may ask, "What good does that do?" here's the catch for SOE... in order to maintain their membership with the BBB, they must answer all complaints in a timely manner as set by the BBB's policies.

Here's an url of the Better Business Bureau's website:
http://www.sandiego.bbb.org/index.html

Here's an url of the BBB's membership standards:
http://www.sandiego.bbb.org/membership/standards.html

Here's an url of SOE's current charter with the organization:
http://www.sandiego.bbb.org/commonreport.html?compid=11000350


If you scroll to the end, you'll see SOE's past complaint history, which I'll admit is quite sterling. Every complaint has been investigated and resolved in a favorable manner.

Now, the reason I bring this up is:

1. I did not purchase GOD nor have I played during this entire time period between GOD's release and the current state of affairs EQ is in.

2. It appears to me that people have been complaining about SOE's customer service and that their gameplay has been greatly adversely affected.

3. This would indicate to me that people have a legitimate complaint to put forward to the BBB. Whether or not this is actually true is a matter for the BBB to decide.

So... if you feel like you have a complaint about Product Quality or Quality of Service (the monthly charge you pay), I urge you to compile the following information.

1. Billing information... That you both purchased and paid for the product (Expansions namely GOD) and services (monthly charges) that are now currently either still defective for you or has not delivered what was promised by SOE (usually a rewarding game playing experience)

STRONG NOTE OF CAUTION.... Any remarks you make about product quality should be based on as much FACT as possible, and not just your opinion. If it is opinion driven, you're going to need as much testimonial as possible to indicate clearly that a consensus of people agree that a service is not being adequately provided.

2. Provide evidence of inferior product quality. Now for GOD, this is actually quite simple, just produce Smedley's letter of apology where he clearly states that releasing GOD was a mistake.

NOW THE BATTLE... if you are still unsatisfied, you must prove that SOE has not been answering your specific complaints promptly. Remember that SOE 'DID IN FACT HOLD A PLAYER SUMMIT' in order to address this problem, they DID NOT provide a refund for lost time and aggravation however. They only provided assurances that they would look into the expansion and fix it and they implied they would look into class balance issues and fix them... all of this they also implied they would do before Omens of War but they never directly stated that it would be done with certainty.

Very clever of SOE.

So now, as a consumer you should provide proof that SOE hasn't fixed GoD to your satisfaction and that issues are still outstanding and require attention and that SOE hasn't adequately answered you.

It's not as easy as you would think, technically SOE has done everything right with the exception that they still haven't actually fixed class balance issues. But they can claim that they've been trying.

In order to have a legitimate complaint, you'll need to provide message board logs, references, and every shred of proof possible to show SOE has in fact been negligent in providing adequate customer service.

However, it could be that just a few complaints could propel SOE into at least giving you the answers you all are looking for. A volume of complaints may be even better... If a person were to compile this information here and provide it to others as proof... well, a lot of people could submit complaints.
