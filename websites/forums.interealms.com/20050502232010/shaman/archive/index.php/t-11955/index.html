Xev_Grammar
Could you post some kind of guide for Deathrow soloing?

I suggest doing this with torpor as it's quite a bit riskier without it, but still doable. Summon dogdog, arm him with weapons, buff up, and kill the first enforcer in the entrance hallway (the usual.. turgur, pox/bane/curse.. root, torpor as necessary, rebane).

After that, rest up a lil then paralyzing earth one of the punishers. Then send pet at (if you're afraid) and slow the other. Targetting is much easier now now that you can switch between the last two targets (I set that button to 'Q'). Now switch back and malosini the first because that will make paralyzing earth stick easier and turgur it as well for when root does break. Solo the second and root park whenever necessary. Then finish up the first (I wouldn't rootrot both of them at the same time because you want to split the spawns).

Rest up a lil, finish up the last enforcer.

Once all of this is done you have a perfectly split up entrance hallway to DR and there is little risk.. the first enforcer will appear after a little rest period. The spawntime on these mobs is maybe about 13 minutes.

If someone is already hunting this hallway, you may want to try DR itself. Three Cautions: there is a wraith on the left corner that is triggered if you get too close to there. If there is one wraith already there it's even worse because you will trigger yet another wraith and two will be there. I would recommend often triggering this trap to get rid of the wraith. The second caution is that the second enforcer easily aggros through the door and can cause complications. This issue can be resolved by sitting further away from the door on the opposite wall of the wraith (near the Dreamthief door). DR is done similarly to the DR hallway punisher split. I tended to root the left and turgur the right.. The third caution: if you click on any of the doors in the DR room you will be sent into a prison room where a mob will attack you.. and it *tends* to drop nothing when you kill it. One of those mobs drops a metal speckled sleeve or something if I remember correctly but that's it. If you happen upon a giant cell, remember that you need to be in melee range to cast on it.

Expect to kill maybe 2-3 rows before having to come back and kill the wraith and the hall enforcer (I try to agro the enforcer by coming into the opposite corner of the wraith)-- try your best to kill the wraith separate from the enforcer either by grabbing the wraith before enforcer repop or by not triggering the wraith before pulling the enforcer, then triggering the wraith to finish that off.

I've found that cripple lowers the damage but eventually I did this place so much that I didn't even bother since torpor was good enough to keep me alive-- I just concentrated on dotting it to death (or experimenting with rains and such).

Loot includes peridots, random junk (enforcer stones, ceremonial daggers, etc) that may sell for 50-70pp, raw diamonds/diamonds for 210pp, blue diamonds that I've sold for 400pp at the bazaar, weighty polearms which are now crap, buncha research stuff now, and ethereal parchments.

None of these mobs run.

Do not drag these mobs near any of the high guards or that big guard in the back (though I doubt you can solo your way there without a lot of back repops to complicate your return). High guards are level 60 and hit for 400-500? and in that hallway before the DR entrance hallway, they tend to come in pairs at least.

If for some reason a high guard agros you after you pull a punisher in the entrance hallway, then forget it-- come back another day. I've never had this happen but once they're bugged you're in trouble.

One last thing. Sometimes people come barging through -- be understanding -- they are probably just coming through to get flagged by Mavuin (Mauvin?). That may screw up your spawn schedule.. good luck. hehe.

EDIT: I recommend doing this with a horse.. it's harsh without a horse. Also if you're doing the hallway and someone is in the DR be wary of the second enforcer-- you may need to sit in that spot when he spawns or he will agro the group in the DR room if they're stupid (or smart) enough to stay close to the door to agro him. Enforcers (not punishers) have extremely large ranges btw. Keep this in mind when you are root parking. You can also use this to your advantage-- since they may attack you from a distance you can cast rains on them directly. hehe. But not much of an advantage, you could always have targetted your rains on your pet. I seemed to have a much easier time using dots instead of rains btw.

Also get Virulent Paralysis if you can to help with the emergency rooting (in case root or turgur's resists) .. if say you root the left punisher and it resists, and you send your dog at the second, then the first will come at you-- try to turgur him, then virulent paralysis or root the other and back dogdog off and turgur the other.. malosini and apply paralyzing earth.. then resume solo.

Spirit Call is okay to help speed things up if you seem to lag behind in the spawn schedule. Control your spawn schedule.. kill speedily or slow down your kill accordingly to adjust the spawns to the most comfortable level you can manage it.
