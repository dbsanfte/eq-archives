The thing you have to remember is that class balance is something that commonly runs in cycles. Classes go from high to low all the time, with very few exceptions. Thats why it annoys me so much when people say things like "Don't start a shaman, when you get to the end game you won't like it as much". Thats quite possibly true, but that ONLY applies if they somehow insta-transform to the end-game that week. Who know what coming months have in store for any class.

I wouldnt make any decision on the class until after OoW ships, and we see what SOE has in store for our "purpose" in life. I know there are some OoW spells out now, and they seem moderate at best, but I do not believe they are final, and I truly feel that SOME of the things we have been asking for will be addressed.

As it is RIGHT NOW, Shaman are still fairly powerful. We are still desired in groups. We have value at some of the high end. Yes, in some cases, the preference people used to show shaman has changed. This is the evolution of EQ. WHo remembers when Rangers sucked? Who remembers when Rogues were horrible? Or Knights? I agree that classes SHOULDNT go through these "semi-worthless" stages, but then I have to rememember that balance is a difficult thing, and that adjustments tend to affect all the surrounding classes in a parallal manner.

IMHO, our future as a class is bright, but it will never be as stellar as it was in our hey-day during Kunark and Velious. Thats ok, I am all good with being "one of the best classes" instead of "The Best". All I really want is for groups to say "Theres a Shaman available, hows that" and the answer is "Thats cool" instead of "Look for a BL". All I am really looking for is something more to do on raids than my puny heal or my puny nukes.

I may be overly optomistic though, but I guess I feel I have to be, because pessimism is just a miserable way to spend your days.

Trust your own advice and play what you want, how you want.

From your post detailing your EQ history, a steady one-for-all class (or the game itself over the long run) does not seem to be what you are after. Or, perhaps, it is just that you haven't found the right character that gives you consistant, long term challenge and enjoyment. Then again, maybe the game itself and how it is played will never hold your long term interest.

Powerlevel or not. Again - your choice. If it makes you all warm and fuzzy to zip into 65 and start 'playing' there, then more power to you. If spending the next few months/years working a character up slowly and 'doing more' at each level is fun for you, then again, more power to you.

If your game reality is "I really like my shaman over all other characters I have or could ever play but everyone says they are the **suck** now and I want to be liked by everyone," well, I am not sure what to tell you. It is a game. For fun. Enjoy the character YOU want to play. Play that character well. All else falls into place as you meet folks, group with them and they learn you are not one of the 'many' shaman out there. You are one of "THE SHAMAN."

While every class is limited to their abilities by game design, the better players compensate for their limitations and play a class better than it was designed to be played. In short (and this is very much a generalization) talented players don't see brick walls stopping them - just obstacles to overcome. If that is how you see your shaman (or cleric or ranger or ... ) then go have fun. Others will notice and it will be YOU they want, not just another (insert class here.)
