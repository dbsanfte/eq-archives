Living wills, no matter how explicit, are notoriously hard to enforce. The problem is that the person who most wants it to be enforced is not in a position to testify to that fact, and the one who do not want it to be enforced are generally thier most loved family.

Unless you have hired a lawyer who is separate from the rest of your family's legal counsel(s) to enforce your living will, there's not a good advocate to speak on your side in court. For example, if you and your spouse both setup living wills with the same lawyer, and when she goes into a coma, you decide to challenge the living will, her lawyer is your lawyer - see the problem?

And in the mean time, the living will is negated while the court process goes on.

The same thing happens with organ donation. If your family does not relase your organs for transplant, they can't be used, even if you have signed a donor card.

The best protection you have is to come to a real agreement with your family about what you want and how to handle a situtation. Sign the donor card and get the living will so that your spouse, kids or parents have the legal force they need to make the hospital follow your wishes, but make sure they are going to use it.

Is it selfish to take your own life to end certain pain and suffering near the end?

I don't think so. If the pain is so great the the drugs and machines to lessen it turn you into a different person than your family knew, I'd rather save them the pain of watching.

My grandmother died after about a year of illness, months in ICU, more months on a ventialtor. By the time she finally died, she didn't recognize anyone, she was in so much pain. And her body was so bloated we could hardly recognize her. She was miserable, and for the last few weeks, delirious from pain that the strongest drugs couldn't completely eliminate. We all cried every time we left her hospital room, and it nearly killed my grandfather to be with her in that hospital day after day for a year.

To see her all prepared for her funeral, with her dentures in again, her hair and makeup and meticulous as she liked to keep them and her face back to its normal size and shape was what we all needed. She was at peace, at home with The Father at last, and we were free from the pain of seeing her suffer.

I say that when it's time to go, it's time, and no one is being selfish by saving their family the pain of watching somone they love die slowly and painfully.
