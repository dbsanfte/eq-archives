Sariss
Normal and Hard just mean "What modifier to add to the dungeon". Nothing more.

Normal is "+0" and Hard is "+5".

If you have a 62.5 avg level group, you'll do a Normal (+0) adventure of 60. If you bump it to hard, you'll do a Hard (+5) adventure of 65 (60 +5).

The "HARD" dungeons... are the level 70 ones. That's what you get the 100 points for.

So a level 65 adventure group, selects Hard (+5) and ends up in a level 70 dungeon.

The other day, a group of guildies (plus a non-guild ranger) did a couple of assassinates back to back in Miragul's - the first was normal, the second hard.

We were Warrior, Ranger, Shaman, Enchanter, Wizard, Cleric. All decently equipped, all but the ranger with at least some elemental gear.

Both dungeons happened to have the same map, but the named spawned in slightly different places - the hard one being fractionally nearer.

In comparison - as expected, the mobs in the hard one hit harder, and mitigated slow, and had lots more HP. But they were typically similarly spaced, in some cases less dense, so it wasn't fatal to rush rooms (which is what we were doing both times, not pulling).

The most surprising thing to me was that the mobs in the hard one didn't really resist that much more than the normal ones - I could typically land slow before Malos in both cases, with a few resists.

We had a named in the normal one, and made a slight detour to kill it. No named in the hard one.

The normal one took us around 45 minutes, the hard one took us around 65 minutes.

If it takes your group typically less than an hour to do a normal adventure, I'd suggest you could do a hard one. I would recommend a bard or enchanter though - 3 mobs on normal don't hurt much if loose, but on hard will easily kill.

Oh, and I must say that wizard with his Eye of Zomm has been very helpful to find bosses on various adventures - much more so than rangers or druids who don't seem to know how their own tracking works. Of course a good wizard and a good ranger/druid together works best.
