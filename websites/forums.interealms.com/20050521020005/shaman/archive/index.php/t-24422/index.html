Healing has to be sufficient to keep the tank alive for the time it takes dps to kill the mob. Thus heals have to be balanced against the hardest mob a group or raid intends to hit, not the average or weakest.

Survival: (healing per second * seconds) + tank hit points > mob damage per second * seconds

65 shaman with all non-god spells and healing aas:

Burst Heal Cast Time 3.75 secs, refresh 2.25, best haste = 30% thus 2.625 cast time, 2.25 recast, one burst heal every 4.875 secs, or 5 secs.


Tnargs 260 hps
Tnargs (aas) 335.4 hps
Daludas 310 hps
Daludas (aas) 466.6 hps
Yoppa 357 hps
Yoppa (aas) 537.2 hps

HoT with AAs presumed to last entire fight and be cast before beginning of fight.

Q 70 hps
Trushar 105 hps
Serenity 136.7 hps


Q+Tnargs = 330 hps
Q + Tnargs + aas = 405.4 hps
Trushar + Daludas = 415 hps
Trushar + Daludas + aas = 571.6 dps
Serenity + Yoppa = 493.7 hps
Serenity + Yoppa + aas = 673.9 hps

By determining your maximum hps per fight, you can determine the hardest hitting mob you can attack with shaman healing.

Slow reduces mob dps from 0% to 75%, buit any slow less than 50% leaves a shaman behind a cleric head to head, and if the cleric has any slow at all from another class, he greatly exceeds a shaman once again.

Shaman to cleric healing ratio is 47/100 so an equal cleric can heal against a mob with 2.12 times the dps as may a shaman, or against a mob which hits for 1958 dps in the above example.

Shaman to Druid healing ratio is 47/64 so an equal druid can heal against a mob with 1.36 times the dps as may a shaman, or a mob which hits for 1258 dps in the above example.

HoTs may affect this somewhat. If you have the math, let me know.

Personally, I think all priests should be equally able to heal 80% of level appropriate single group content, and the current balance is heavily skewed.
