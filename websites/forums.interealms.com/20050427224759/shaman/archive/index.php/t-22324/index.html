Poguk
Welp, at least some of the shaman whining has dipped a bit with some of the new changes. I was never one to find my shaman lacking in anything. I have quite a few characters.... a 65 shaman, a 65 monk, a 61 ranger, a 57 wizard, 50 beastlord, 35 necro, etc... 3 of those toons are pretty damn powerful, with a 4th toon knocking on the door.

So I've seen the many sides of classes, and trust me --- monks and wizards are hard as hell to level up due to their group desirability. Never really had a prob with my ranger for some reason...

I can play all these toons very damn good. You'll never get a complaint about my monk's role in groups, he can pull like a mofo and then do some serious damage in-between.

I can --- with a straight face and brutal honesty --- say that not one of my toons kicks as much ass as my shaman, and the higher the stress-level, the higher the danger --- the more the shaman goes supernova. Some of the stuff I've been able to pull off with the shaman has been nothing short of amazing, including tanking/soloing the final 10% of a HoT dragon after it had wiped the raid and having less than full health and mana. One dragon standing, one troll standing --- sit down, Mr. Dragon.

Most of my playtime is 2-boxing my shaman and monk, who I consider to both be my mains. The monk is good at damage, pinch-tanking, and of course, pulling. Very strong character and fun as hell --- but when the **** hits the fan the monk can pretty much do damage 'til he can no longer do damage and then save his own ass by FDing. Meanwhile the shaman is standing in the middle of all the carnage trying to work miracles as he's getting torn to bits.

Last night, the shaman did it again.

So you all know about those high risk LDoNs? Where ya need a full group of level 65s, including an incredible cleric, super-beefed-up tanked, and some helacious DPS... and the mobs hit for 1000, mitigate slow, and usually have to be pulled by only a monk or bard? Make 1 mistake and you can lose the entire adventure? Okay, so if yer PoTime geared, this doesn't apply, but to us mere-mortal, casual players high risk adventures are pretty damn tough. This is our big-leagues.

A crew of shaman and monk (2-boxed by me), pally, cleric, warrior and necro were blasting their way through a high risk EF adventure last night. We got to the halfway point (45 mins) having achieved 16 of 26 items, which is actually pretty damn good.

That's precisely when a nasty storm rolled through the NE part of the United States, effectively taking out the necro and cleric for the rest of our adventure. They went LD, never to return... although the cleric remained on the phone with the pally keeping tabs on us and tried repeatedly to log back in. I also had received a few messages from the necro via EQIM that he was trying to log back in, but neither of them were able to make it.

Normally in a high risk LDON, when yer snarer AND cleric go LD, you sit there and went for them to return. However, we received news quick that these 2 might NOT be returning, period. Having had such a good start on the adventure (and being one win away from getting my new haste aug), I bucked up, said to the tank "you ready? Pog is gonna be yer new healer" and started the chain of pulling again with my monk.

We plugged away for 30-35 mins, taking out as many as we could. Pog was like an over-clocked, overheated processor, fizzling and crackling --- every mob that we pulled he had to malos, slow, and cripple and then strain his hardest to keep the tank or warrior on their feet and trying to keep buffs refreshed. Definitely pushing the limits of his abilities. Shamans aren't built to heal in high risk LDoNs. These dudes hit for ~1000 and mitigate slow. We're not talking Karnor's Castle here.

But we were doing it. At one point, I even fooked up and pulled TWO mobs. Somehow we pulled that off without a death (although a big drain on mana) using root and the shaman's dogpack of war.

With about 20 mins left we went to a system of my shaman debuffing and throwing Heals-over-time while the pally medded to heal the warrior while the warrior assumed MA duty. We just kept chugging along slowly, taking one mob down at a time, and thanks to a fortuitous drop-rate, we were getting closer and closer to an improbable victory.

Finally with 10 mins left, the necro came back and barely had time to get his pet up when the last mob we killed dropped the 26th and final item. Success! With 10 mins left!

That's right --- 4 toons (no cleric or druid or true-CC) collected the final 10 items in a 35 min period. One mistake --- even one death --- would haved been our end and we managed to pull it off. It was probably the best 105 points I ever got. The 3 of us were ecstatic for what we had just pulled off. Any other time, we woulda packed our bags when the 2 went LD...

The shaman was the hero of the day... even with very good single pulls by the monk and tanking by the pally and warrior... what the shaman was doing (healing, debuffing, buffing, CC, and extra DPS) was effectively doing the job of 3 characters. No one there thought that the shaman could maintain that level, and for that matter even keep a tank on his feet against a single 1000-hitting mob, let alone allow chain-pulls.

It was astounding. The warrior said "How the hell did we pull this off?" and the pally responded "it was all about the SHM powah." And the warrior /nod'd in agreement.

For us mere mortals down here in PoP and high risk LDoN, the shaman is still a very powerful being and this is proof of it.
