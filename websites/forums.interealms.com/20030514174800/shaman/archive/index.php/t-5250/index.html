They're spaced far enough apart that you can pull them single. Pox works, haven't tried to land bane on 'em. Pox, epic, dog and bracer + the occasional torpor when single. When 2 boxing with my necro I can kill them all no problem. If I have KEI, I can even spring a few of the nearby traps looking for a Boulder or Stonehand while waiting on repops, otherwise I spend downtime canni'ing for mana.

They're easy enough to slow - I like to pull with malosini and slow them as they catch up to me at camp. I don't really need the malosini to land the slows, I just use it to cut partial resists on the jbb. Spend some time running around invis near the camp to learn where the traps are so you can avoid them. There are several good spots to pull the revs to that are well away from the traps.

If I have the camp to myself I get pretty sweet exp solo or duo, but when it's taken I'll usually pick a nice quiet spot and pull trap spawns and blue skeles. Doing that I can *almost* match the exp / hour (aa) of the revs.

In your specific case, you and Draag duoing should be able to tear them up now, but I'd wait for torpor to try them solo. Cblast may be enough, you can try and see. I suspect though that the amount of mana consumed casting cblast would cut into the exp efficiency too much for them to be worth the effort.

Try camping the royal bugs in fungus grove. Warriors are level 60, Warlord is level 62?, soldiers/priests/royal guards are all killable.

Hard part is keeping the mobs stationary so you don't get adds, have to switch push directions every couple minutes or you get herdmasters and soldiers adding. Their assist range is small and they hit for max 2 or 300.

Regardless warriors are worth 3-4 percent alt xp, soldiers a lil less.

Warlord is pullable solo if you cheat a little bit. Pull the warrior in front and to the left, and kill it. Then *root* the warrior to the left of the Warlord, you'll get the Warlord as an add. Malo, slow, and root it. Camp out. Come back and if root hasn't broken you'll have the Warlord solo. Pain in the ass but so is finding a monk :p
