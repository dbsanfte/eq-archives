Are your power management settings set too aggressively? If the hard drive is being powered down to sleep mode every five minutes, that could cause a delay.

Is it possible you have DMA enabled in the BIOS/OS but not on your hardware? I believe this can cause timeouts on each hard drive access.

Just a couple thoughts off the top of my head.
Gresgoth
It could just be that your hard drive has gone bad. I've seen this happen numerous times.

One thing you can do is download and run the diagnostic tools from your hard drive manufacturer. Western Digital has one called Data Lifeguard. The Seagate diagnostic is called Seatools. Both of these programs can test the drive for faulty hardware problems.

About 3 weeks ago I helped a friend put a new hard drive in his system. It was booting unbelievably slow, loading games slow, etc etc. Cost him about $80 for a new hard drive and now he's good to go.

Hope this helps.

--Gresgoth
