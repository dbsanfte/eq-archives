HoT = east wing of ToV
First of all, factions.
Stuff in first hallway and the three way is on CoV faction, and is not KOS to you if you are not KOS to CoV.
Killing in HoT for armor doesn't change any factions, but EVERYTHING in HoT is KOS by default.
WToV is entirely on CoV faction. You can walk through it unmolested.
NToV is a bit different. The hatchlings are on CoV faction, but that's all. The rest is on GoV, which everyone is KOS to and there is no way to raise. You get -CoV, -GoV, and some -Yeli hits in NTOV.
3 groups of 55 would be fine in HoT. Leave the other wings alone.
Armor drop rates in HoT are much higher then Kael, as you may have seen. If your guild has not raided Kael Arena, get some practice there first.

The most potent danger in HoT is gaters. Mana sieves are very important. If they gate, they will star tsummoning people. It's not fun, and it's usually a bad CR if your guild doesn't know how to handle it best, which just takes practice.
Mobs generally hit pretty light. 300ish, sometimes less for max.
Slowing them can be hard. Malo/s, sinia/s, occlusion / harmony of sound, and tash are very important. Get the debuffs in and get the mobs slowed or some of them will eat your tank alive.
You probably need some levels and Skysrhine gear to attempt Dozekar, but you can start killing the other HoT named for Doze quest pieces.
Pulls are pretty easy. I myself have pulled HoT many times, but if you can't handle adds, use a FD puller. Communication is the key to owning HoT.
White wurms, blue drakes, glimmer drakes are gaters.
Glimmer drakes are practically immune to Magic slow. Don't waste mana and aggro trying.

If you haven't been to Kael before..like I said, hit up the arena for some Thurgadin quest gear and CoV faction gear. For a guild just starting HoT, it makes it a lot less hassle to be non kos to CoV.
After that..you CAN farm Kael armor in WTOV but very few guilds do that and I don't recommend it, personally.
Once you are mostly geared up in SS gear, start farming other mobs -
Vindi, Velk, velious dragons, - kunark dragons can be good too -, doomshade, the other UP mobs, etc. There are plenty of threads on other targets you can take. Eventually you can start trying NTOV. With 65 and new spells it's a lot easier nowadays, most of it can be 1 grouped.

Pulling guides for HoT can be found on monkly-business.com btw, they are pretty good :)
Good luck :)

The question becomes one of group composition and gear. I've done HoT with 11 people and we were hardly uber, so I'm sure it could be done with fewer.

Do you have enough clerics for a decent CH rotation?

Do you have a 60+ tank with good gear and a pile of hp?

Do you have a 60+ slower?

I'm sure that people will jump in and say that these things are not needed, that it's been done without them and that's probably true. We used to do HoT without a slower (well, I was only slower and I couldn't get anything to stick) but we also had like 30 people.

There are a large number of threads on this subject that you should be able to find from the search feature.

The conventional wisdom:

- People are going to die, no matter what. Epic clerics are a big, big help
- Don't open the door
- Don't drag corpses

Other tips:

- Beware gating mobs and be ready to mana drain them
- Beware unslowable mobs
- Beware dispelling mobs
- Make sure you have plenty of FD-capable people. They are needed to move corpses as well as pull. For small or lower-level raids, adds = total wipeout.

Note:

All the above is mostly based on the assumption that your three groups are going to be closer to 55 than to 65. If you have 1 lvl 55 and 17 lvl 65, then forget it and just massacre the place. ;)

To clear up how to move corpses, the FD person targets the corpse, then FD. Then they drag it to them with /corpse, stands up and moves, rinse and repeat. The dragons outside the HoT doors wont assist if you are fighting just inside the doors if the door is shut. But if you drag a corpse while standing up, they will open the door, come in and eat you. After Zemm kills you a few times, your love for CoV faction won't be as great as it used to be.

Also, if for some reason you are just inside the door to HoT, door opens, and Zemm and his gang of dastardly dogooders comes in, stop doing anything. Don't cast, don't heal. Turn off attack, and just hope you didn't end up on the list of targets. Just stand there and do nothing.
