Sciek Spellbottler
level 20-65 is an overly large range to say simply, Monks are better or SKs are better. At lower levels up to say 40 or 45 it won't much matter either way IMO. The monk will probably shine due to better DPS. The SKs ability to tank and hold aggro won't be nearly as important at those levels.

From 50ish onwards the SK will start to shine as the better tank, but depending on where you hunt it may not really matter enough to merit the loss of DPS. Once you close in on and pass 60, the SK is the better choice for a duo partner without doubt.

Going from level 20-65, I'd say at the lower end it won't matter. Towards the middle the Monk DPS will pull ahead enough to make him a better partner despite the tanking/aggro ability of the SK. In the end though, the SK will be a far superior partner in a duo whether you're doing a dungeon crawl or outdoor xp in some PoP zone.

Monk has slightly better dps.

Monk has instant feign.
Monk has sneak.

Shadowknight has instant invis circlet which lessens the imporance of sneak.
SK has harmshield for 18 seconds which makes you 99% immortal which lessens the importance of stonestance and mend for emergencies.

SK has massive massive taunt ability.
SK has a minor group heal + mana spell to help entire group effectiveness.

To sum up quickly though:
-monk has better dps.
-shadowknight has very effective taunt spells, self heal spells, superior pulling utility, and more utility spells.

Ok, I have a decent amount of experience with both Shadowknights and Monks. My main was a human monk for a long time, and I created a shadowknight a couple of years ago. I'll speak mostly to the gear issue, which a previous poster brought up.

My ogre shadowknight couldn't be agnostic (like my idiot warrior), so I have a full set of fulvous cultural armor. For those of you who haven't checked that out, it's pretty decent armor, especially for shadowknights, because each piece gives hp and mana in addition to VERY decent AC. All in all, it's very decent armor and is EASY to find. Average price per piece, maybe 4k on Ayonae Ro.

For weapons, I have a war marshal's bladed staff for 2hs and a sceptre of lightnight for 1hb - 25/25. The WMBS is (I think) 31/28 - just about the best droppable 2hs in the game. I think I paid about 7k for it - they're always available in the bazaar for that price. I paid 2k for that sceptre of lightnight - it's so cheap because it's sk/pal only. That and a shield is a nice tanking combo for when I need the extra AC.

So I was able to fully outfit my shadowknight with extremely nice weapons and armor for maybe 50k. Throw in another 50k for slots the cultural amor won't cover (rings, earrings, etc.). That's 100k for some of the better droppable gear for a shadowknight - mostly better gear than I can get for my stupid agnostic ogre warrior at 10 times the cost. Almost all the pieces give me relevant stat boosts and raw hp/mana.

Now, let's look at the same situation for a monk. My poor human monk has no cultural armor. He has to share armor with two wis classes, druids and beastlords. The majority of his armor is wearable by caster classes and is geared toward them. I think Darrick's wis is about 180 - that comes in real handy :rolleyes: And about 800 raw mana on top of that :mad: Most of the really good monk items are OBSCENELY expensive, like flayed barbarian skin leggings for 150k. There must be a lot of monk twinks out there, which makes no sense because we suck, but the gear prices are just stupid. A 50k fungi is a REQUISITE for a monk, so figure I've conservatively spent 350k on my monk's gear to get him a decent number of hps/resists. But, his wisdom and mana are also nicely boosted :confused:

On to weapons - any long-time monk's favorite topic. The best tradeable monk weapon is the staff of flowing waters - I can't remember the damage/delay but it's quite decent. Something like 40/33. Comparable to the WMBS for the shadowknight, actually a little better. If you can find one for less than 100k you're doing extremely well on Ayonae Ro. Even the old standby, the tranquil staff at 29/30 from Kunark is still going for 15k. A good 1-hander combo is the copper hammer of striking (about 15k) plus the waning light katar (about 20k). Monk weapons are so stupidly overpriced it isn't even funny. Figure on spending 50k plus on weapons or pulling your hair out waiting for Raster for our retarded epic quest.

So once you figure in the cost of gear, you're probably looking at spending 4-5 times as much outfitting the monk as the shadowknight in comparable gear.

Once you've gotten that little investment out of the way, expect the shadowknight to outperform the monk in almost every conceivable way. The nerf to monk mitigation was absolutely vicious. Without stellar gear, your monk won't be able to take nearly the beating that a shadowknight can, and the dps difference isn't an issue if your monk died halfway through the fight. His hp will be lower, his utility is zero, and his taunt is dependant almost entirely upon weapon procs, making it completely unreliable. Monks benefit greatly from shaman buffs, however, so it greatly increases a monk's power to group with a shaman. It just helps the shadowknight almost as much, so that point is probably a wash.

If I had to do it over again, I'd have left the monk in the closet and levelled up my shadowknight. In a duo with a shaman, I think they're clearly a better option.

Don't get me wrong - my main was a monk for a long time and I love the class. But SOE has just dorked things up so much right now I have no desire to play him any more. MAYBE the upcoming melee patch will address the monk's shortcomings, but I'm not too optimistic based on what's coming out of the test server right now. We monks have begun referring to the new melee "USE YOUR SPECIAL ACTION BUTTON RIGHT NOW BEFORE IT'S TOO LATE!" feature as "whack-a-mole."

Either combo is quite powerful and fun; I've two-boxed extensively with both. I just the shadowknight is a better option, in large part because of the easier/cheaper access to really good gear. Plus, ogres are much more handsome that humies :p
