Caanus Grey
A couple items make me think someone is doing VERY well as a shaman, and I normally try to find at least one of them in shammies before I give them my vote to get into the guild (though we aren't exactly the most exclusive around)

1) you really don't give a flip if you die. Sure, you'll check for the best rez you can, but you won't whine over it.

2) Not only do you not care if you die, but you go for revenge the moment you do. Trying every trick in your book before calling the mob unbeatable by you (and with how many tricks we have, you die a few times or have close calls with Gate)

3) You know when you really can't win.

4) You have used every spell you have ever written effectively (Imbue ivory and the summon food spells are the only ones that don't apply directly to our battle. If you have, and still do, used blind, Rage line, and the AE rain when appropriate, you are well on the way)

5) Quested / rare gear that gives you an edge. I know you may thinl gear doesn't make the man/woman. But you said GREAT, not just good. You can be good on your own, but to be great, you have to be dedicated, and that means finding extra tricks. a DA idol, the free pet tooth, snare webs, anything that will give you an extra edge shows thought. And if it is rare/quested, it shows dedication

6) Ability to fill in the gaps for any one of the "Holy Trinity." That means being able to tank (including keeping taunt), being able to be main healer (without slacking on slows or buffs), and being a master at root parking.

7) backing up every class you do have, while playing every class you don't have. Just cause you are root crowd controlling doesn't mean you take heal off mem or stop buffing

8) maximizing groups. You don't jsut exp grind. you find the way to make mana last longer. Totally remove med time, expand your camps reach and get more exp. That means finding out which buffs you don't need, and convincing people that you shouldn't cast them. Finding who to have tank, and when (if you use regrowth of Dar'Khura, then the cleric is wasting group mana every second he is FH. Having him tank at just the right times, for the proper duration means less med time later. An SK that goes 2 hours without using HT is a flaw, unless you need the security in reserve. A cleric that goes 2 refreshes of DA is a waste as well.
It is all well and good to just grind away when with random people you don't know, but when with friends and guildies, no time shoudl be wasted unless you have cleared every exp mob you can possibly reach.

9) Equiping your TEAM. It is all well and good for you to be decked to the heavens. But your normal exp group should also be getting upgrades. If they can't do it themselves, you should be willing to assist since it furthers your own exp and capabilities. Consider it investing in later quests.

10) Presence. You have to be out and known. If you don't let people see that you are capable, they won't listen when you know the only way to survive something. I don't mean brag, I just mean that you don't become a "yes man" and do what everyone else asks of you. Make sure people know that you are driving at a better gaming ability than that which is known to work.
BlargX
You have to have the courage to die. Unless the monk(or SK or whatever) dies on the pull, the first person to die will almost always be you in a raid, and very often even in a group. People will be healing the main tank, but will figure that since you have torpor, you're invincible. Of course, you can't cast torpor on yourself WHILE slowing(though you can cast it before), and torpor doesn't heal enough hitpoints fast enough to save your life against really tough mobs a lot of the time anyway, but most people don't really think far enough ahead to realize that.

So knowing that, you have to be willing to step up to the plate and take a chance on dying.

I've seen a fair number of high level experienced shamans who wait a long time before slowing, same like I see chanters who wait forever to tash, because they're scared of dying. Many of them are the people who took bad advice from (usually melee class) friends, who think of shamans as pure casters and tell them to forget hitpoints and AC, wisdom items are the ticket. So the shamans become so weak that they are terrified to get in the thick of things. So...they let the other shaman around do the dying. Not that a shaman who has the proper AC/HP gear is near as likely to die but....

Besides courage, you need to be adaptable. If the chanter dies and you need fast damage on multiple mobs who are now unmezzed, could you break out the rain spell? Can you tell from watching the way things are going where the cleric tends to focus -- ignoring caster health maybe even when he has plenty of mana, so you have to pick up the slack? If you have a mage in your group, how about torporing him so he can be a spell-casting maniac with his mod-rods?

And you need to keep in mind the old stand-bys, like keeping the chanter healed so he can save all your butts, remember how thin his margin of safety can be. In the middle of all your hassles and /tells, you have to keep your head, and remember not just to keep up with your own responsibilities, but to pick up the slack from people who forget theirs.

Which sometimes means flat out ignoring the requests and cajoling and commands and extraneous blather and just getting the job done. You need the ability to compartmentalize and actively focus. You can't just hit auto-attack and go get a soda.

The great thing about being a jack-of-all trades class is that you have all kinds of opportunities to shine, if you play the game alertly. Maybe not every day, but you can pull a lot of miracles out of your arse if you apply yourself. Or, you can just try to be incredibly efficient. Considering the amazing amount of mana we can put out, we have many opportunities to crank up kill rates and make people feel safe, too.

A great shaman can make group members say, WE kick butt!! and help delight people with their own powers. He can make them feel good about themselves, maybe without them even realizing why they're suddenly so powerful today. He can make them feel good about the TEAM. And say, man, we gotta come back and do this again!
