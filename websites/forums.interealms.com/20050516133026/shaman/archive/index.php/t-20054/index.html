afreeman
My understanding is that it works out like so:

Human, high elf, half-elf, dark elf, dwarf, etc. -> no penalty or bonus
Halfling -> 5% racial bonus
Barbarian & Vah Shir -> 5% racial penalty
Ogre -> 10% racial penalty
Troll & Iksar -> 20% racial penalty

In the past, there were also class penalties (e.g. 20% for SKs and Pallies, 10% for monks, etc.), although these were eliminated a long time ago (shortly after velious was released, if I recall correctly). However, under that old regime, warriors also enjoyed a 10% exp bonus and rogues got a 5% bonus. These class-specific bonuses were not removed along with the penalties and are still active now.

Thus, a halfling warrior will level the fastest, while an iksar or troll of any class except warrior will level the slowest.

The only class-based exp modifiers are BONUSES. Class-based penalties were removed. Racial penalties are due to specific benefits. The largest bonus is for regen, then frontal stun immunity, and finally the tiny barbarian penalty, because we're just so damn segzy. So here it is:

Warrior & rogues: +5%
Barbarians: -5%
Ogres: -15%
Ikar & Troll: -20%

These are really easily verifiable by starting a noob with a buddy and getting a level together, using a UI with a % measurement on the exp bar.

So, for example, a barbarian (-5%) warrior or rogue (+5%) will end up leveling the same as a non-penalty or bonus character. Hobbit warrior or rogue ends up with a 10% bonus. Your troll is indeed going to level 20% slower than the human monk. Just keep gate up so he dies more often than you, and you should come up even. *grin*
