Ungkor
Depends on what I'm doing and how hard it is.

If I'm in a KT group, I announce heals, slows, roots, canniV, malos, and occasionally group cures.

If I'm in bot, I might announce slow on named, root if we get a bunch of adds, and if the cleric keeps healing me when I canni, I'll announce canni also.

Depends on the group also. If its total strangers, I'll usually announce more often than not, so they understand what I'm watching.

Really, if your messages are simple and without a lot of fluff

"Slowed a mob X"
as opposed to
"I HAVE SLOWED 75% >>>[[[ mob X ]]] <<<"

Your group shouldn't complain.

I've had people complain to me in groups before about messages, and depending on the difficulty, I might cut out announcing certain things. But if the content is hard, and people need to react to your actions, then not announcing is going to get a competent group killed.

Really, the only people that complain about group messages are people that don't understand how to react to other players messages.

You might say, its impolite to NOT announce slow/root/canni/heals, because its a waste of mana for you and the cleric to both fast heal the same person, its a waste of time and potentially dangerous to have both you and the ranger trying to root the same mob, its a waste of mana for the cleric to heal you after canniV, etc..

But in the end, its up to you and up to your group make-up. If you are working well together, and the camp isn't very hard, then sure, you can avoid messages and just chat. Its only when things require split second reactions, or when peoples mana is pressed to the max, that messages come into play while grouping. Or, and this depends on you again, you might announce/ask for announcements if you just plain want to get more done in a night. If I'm wasting mana on heals that land a second after CH, when I could have dropped Bane+Rain on a mob, I'll shoot the cleric a tell and let them know I'd like to see CH messages.

Some people baffle me. Especially clerics without CH messages. Its very difficult to be a backup healer if you don't know what the cleric is doing:) Even on something as simple as a bot named, sometimes I'll be with a tank who's hit points really drop fast, if that cleric doesn't announce CH, I have no clue whether to toss a fast heal if they get too low. Its a waste of mana to toss a heal everytime the tank gets to %X health if a CH is coming in 0.5 seconds anyways.

I've had monks that pull without inc messages. grrr.
Enchanters that don't announce mezz..so I spend 3 seconds trying to aggro or root a mezzed mob off cleric, while there is another mob unmezzed that could have been dealt with instead.

Tanks that won't call for assist in very hard content are also annoying. This, you might say, is overkill. The tank message. But really, when you are fighting mobs that 1.5k x 4, its necessary. I can't tell if taunt failed, I can't see if that pally's stun was interrupted (usually have spell effects turned off for raiding lag), I don't know if those anger aug's proc'd.

But yeah, if its easy stuff, messages usually aren't necessary.
