Gardeltron
People who bought GoD said they did so for new content. From initial reports, that new content means mobs that hit harder, have higher resists and more hitpoints than previous expansions. This is obivous from a perusual of the non-Ancient spells other classes recieved. Clerics and druids have more powerful heals. Wizards, mages, and druids have more powerful nukes. Even hybrid classes have more options for damage output than before.

Before GoD, we drafted The Proposal (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=19207) which outlined 5 areas where shamans were unbalanced given the then current content. In the face of the new GoD content, it is useful to revisit what we said in The Proposal and see if the upgrades we actually recieved have kept pace.

1. Healing -

Problem: Shaman healing was underpowered in many high level situations for most shamans
GoD situation: To compensate for higher rate of damage both druids (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4883&source=Live) and clerics (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4880&source=Live) recieved more effective single target heals. Clerics also received a new powerful heal over time spell (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4882&source=Live). Shamans recieved a very modest upgrade (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4899&source=Live) to Quiescence.
Result: Shamans are farther behind the healing of other priests than before GoD.

2. Mana regeneration -

Problem: Shaman mana regeneration provided very little edge over the mana regernation of other classes to compensate for the higher costs of healing, dealing damage, debuffing, and buffing required of them.
GoD situation: More challenging mobs mean higher mana costs for healing and debuffing. The one upgrade to shaman mana regeneration will be inaccessbile (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4979&source=Live) to the vast majority of shamans.
Result: Most shamans will be forced to deal with higher mana costs using existing, underpowered mana regeneration.

3. Direct damage spells -

Problem: Shamans were far behind other casters in direct damage spell output.
GoD situation: Mobs will require higher DD output, since clerics (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4881&source=Live), druids (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4884&source=Live), enchanters (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4879&source=Live), magicians (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4887&source=Live), and wizards (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4906&source=Live) all have powerful, new nukes. Even beastlords (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4876&source=Live), rangers (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4897&source=Live), and shadow knights (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4904&source=Live) recieved significanlty upgraded DD spells. Shamans recieved no new direct damage spells. (Note: even the new DoT, which is less powerful that our level 64 spell, pales in comparision to the 3 new necromancer DoTs, each with huge resist modifiers).
Result: Shaman DD ability is effectively equivalent to those of hybrid classes.

4. Slowing -

Problem: Long the hallmark of shaman power, the edge they had with slowing was removed by enchanter and beastlord spells, coupled with slow mitigation
GoD situation: Mitigation and resists of mobs to magic based slows are virtually unknown. Based on previous expansions, it is likely that each of these will be more prevalent in GoD. Shamans recieved a less resistable slow spell (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4900&source=Live), but it last a much shorter time and generates 50% more aggro.
Result: Slightly improved slowing ability in terms of intial slowing (which is now done better by bards) and on mobs with relatively high magic resistance.

5. Buffing -

Problem: Buffs available from other classes compounded with equipment mudflation marginalized many shaman buffs.
GoD situation: No new buffs spells were recieved. The introduction of a new beastlord group buff (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4875&source=Live) decreases the need for shaman buffs.
Result: Even lower desireability of shamans for buffing purposes.

To sum up, GoD presents new, more challenging content in terms of raids, groups, and possibly new solo opportunities. To keep pace with these new, more challenging encounters, all casting classes recieved substantial upgrades to at least some facet of their abilities. Shamans received virtually no significant upgrades to any aspect of their abilities. Apparently developers assumed shamans were perfectly balanced to explore this new content without any upgrades.
