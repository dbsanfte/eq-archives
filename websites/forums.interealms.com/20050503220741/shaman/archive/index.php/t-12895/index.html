greyfish
I have a 60 cleric on another account that was my main. I started a 2nd account and played around with a druid and ranger before settling in on my shaman. He's 43 now and I used the cleric to PL him with buffs and rezzes. Unless you have a steady group that you play with, it's a pretty mindless role to play. There solo abilities are awful, even with my shaman buffing him (quickness, nimble, deftness) cleric with hammer of divinity, yaulpV (maxed offense/defense). I'm currently 2 boxing drunken pirates in Dulak and it's faster to just cast 2 Judgements than to bother with melee.

That said, from your post it appears you won't be 2 boxing. If your starting a new toon and are interested in the fun factor of the character, let me say this about the cleric:

For the most part,
You'll never be the puller
You'll never be the tank
You'll cast HP/AC buffs and heal
You'll never effectively solo anything

It is fun to land a heal and save the group from a wipe, and that will happen.
Rezzes offer a 'good feeling' when your able to help someone out of a bad jam.
Tossing random heals on people will make you a lot of friends

Allows you to watch a good movie while you play EQ
You can fold laundry while playing EQ
You can let the dog out while playing EQ
Bio breaks don't usually require telling the group :)
I'm just kidding, but you definitely can find yourself playing 'backseat' in a group.

It all depends on what your looking to do. If you want to melee, play a war/mnk/rog/rng/sk

If you wanna cast, play a wiz/mag

Not sure if plat is a factor, but if you don't want to spend a ton of money on spells, play a monk.

I love the solo ability of the shaman. It's something I can never do (atm) as a cleric. I enjoy having a pet that doesn't depop after the mob is dead.

I hope this helps.
