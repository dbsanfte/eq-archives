Einnod
First thing you want to do is check to see what kind of RAM you have, just like warbear said, it'll have a sticker on it most likely. If not, it should say it somewhere in your BIOS, but I won't go into that..
Then you'll purchase your memory, whether it be 128 or 256.. Keep in mind that the memory you put into this computer will NOT be compatible with any brand new computer you buy nowadays, so don't spend too much money on it.
Something else to keep in mind is that the larger stick of memory should go into the first RAM slot, and the smaller stick should go into the second slot. Makes for better performance all around.

Kvak1
A computer that old will probably be using the Intel I440BX chipset on the motherboard. A chipset limitation means you have to use low density memmory modules. Either it won't boot or will only register half the memmory with high density.

Higher density is cheapers as fewer memmory chips are needed. Due to this I highly recommend finding your computer / motherboard on Crucial or Kingstons website and then going with what they recommend.
