I've played in both several small guilds and an uberguild. I always hated the seperation EQ put on people (I won't say forced, you can always choose to recruit for an uberguild, or leave an uberguild for smaller guilds).

These are just my opinions, and right or wrong, there are also exceptions to every rule. Not every guild thinks or behaves the same. I know this.

It's very hard to blend the two. If you're in an raiding guild, chances are they want you to raid... especially if you're a key class, like a cleric for example. If you take 50% of your time off to just go xp with your friends or just hang with small guilds... they probably won't like you. I mean during prime raid times. Your guild says "Everyone lets go, we're killing 'ubermob'" and you say "Nah, I'm crawling nurga" ... that doesn't always go over well. Sure, sometimes you have to have your own life or take a night off... but if you do that a lot... they'll probably question why you're bothering to be in a hardcore raiding guild.

The reverse is partially true... in non-raiding guilds... if you run around and find a mob up that you think your guild can kill, or think you can at least give a good go at and learn something... people aren't acustomed to jumping at the first sign of a mob, or racing another guild. It can take real time and real effort to get the needed resources to try. And even then, odds are a 'raiding guild in training' or even a raiding guild will beat you to it.

Rhag 1 and 2 are examples that come to mind... nice lower level raid targets... trivial for any seasoned raiding guild... yet often killed... sometimes just for the chance to get Remove Greater Curse.

The suggestion that a small guild just recruit more people and build towards being a raiding guild (even just a small one, or eventually an uber one) isn't really valid. I mean, that's how it works... you start with something and you learn and grow... how far you go is mostly determined by the people you get and who is leading them. But the simple fact is that all small guilds don't want to be raiding guilds. Sure, they wish they could kill some mobs here and there... but if they wanted to be in a raiding guild, they'd go try one.

Talent is widly spread around in EQ... you'll find some of the nicest, highly skilled, and smart people in small, more social like guilds. They could easily get into a high level guild if they wanted to... but some either have no desire to do so, or have been there and don't want to go back... are happy with where they are.

And I guess that's what it's all about... being happy with where you are. If you're just not happy... if you need loot and high end raid challenges to be happy... try out for an uber guild... if you're happy grabbing a group and crawling Nurga... more power to you.

My biggest problem in EQ, is that it makes you choose between the two. I think it's very hard to do both... chill and be social, and raid. The reason is pretty simple, raiding takes a lot of time and effort.

I consider uber guilds to be very well skilled, and to put lots of effort into what they do. Sure, some mobs you can zerg... throw 50 people at it and you might win... but i know that doesn't apply to every guild out there... they try, they learn, they fail, they come back and kick ass. They work towards harder goals, and hopefully get there.

In Everquest ... loot is a huge factor in what most people do. Some people it consumes them, some people are happy for an upgrade every once and awhile... but loot is there.

I hate the fact, that for most good loot, you have to be in a raiding guild. I do respect the work that raiders do... sometimes it's mindnumbingly boring on raids... 6 hours for 1-2 drops among 35 people... night after night.

I think people who do that effort deserve great loot. When I see someone walk by with a particle effect other than an epic... that person probably earned it.

However, I really truly think that non-raiders should have a chance at items like this. Not everyone agrees with me here... and that's okay... it's my belief.

If a monk can raid the Avatar of War for Flayed Barbie Leggings... I think a non-raid monk should also be able to quest for an item that is of LOWER value... but close.

When I say quest... I don't mean 5 minutes and your monk has Legs of Divine Uberness... and I also don't mean a quest where you have to kill something like the Avatar of War to get a quest piece.

The quest could take weeks... months... several drops.... you can even make it use the raid features of PoP... crawl a dungeon that requires 12 people ... or kill a mob that requires 15 people. But keep it small, and manageable to non-raiding guilds.

And time. I definatly am not saying you should be able to quest something in a day... just that there should be alternatives available.

I'm also not saying that every uber item out there should have a quest alternative. Just a lot of good items for any given class.

Uber and non-uber warriors alike should be able to look forward to an uber sword. One of them might get it after 3 raids, defaulted, or until they earn enough DKP to get it... the other may crawl and camp for weeks and weeks, but they'll get something eventually if they keep it up.

I will say though, that I don't want a repeat of epics. I think epics caused a lot of competition and greed. But I don't know... maybe that's what you get if you make a great quest reward that everyone can get.

I just hate that most people have to choose loot vs guild. If you're in a small guild, with the exception what you get off a named mob here and there, you're restricted to what you see in the Bazaar.

I'll end with... I had uber jealousy issues in the past. Big time. I always wanted... I always dreamed... the one day I got the chance to get uber items... and it was great seeing things I have never seen... killing stuff bigger than a giant in Kael... but I didn't love the lifestyle... and when I won big loot... I realized that it didn't really make me better... 5 more wis, 35 more mana... whoopie... I just wanted more.

Now... I just try and have fun... so far it's working.
