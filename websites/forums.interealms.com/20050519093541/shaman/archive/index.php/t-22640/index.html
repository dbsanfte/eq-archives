snapdragn
No hard numbers here, just a weird situation that I thought was pretty neat.


Ok - In Riwwi Arena there are 'gladiator' mobs (non-named) that will wander out, aggro on a helpless slave (a Yunjo) and proceed to beat their ass silly. The gladiator mobs are lvl 64+, the slaves are lvl 40.

If you sit and watch, the gladiator will stomp a mudhole in the slave and he (the gladiator) will end up a bit over 80%. But if you slow the gladiator, the Yunjo wins.


I was camping the middle of the arena when an Anuek Glyphcaller pops and wanders out. I was fighting another mob so I ignored him, he started beating up a slave. I decided I would let the slave get him down to 30-40% and I could get some easy xp.

I toss a slow (no other spells) on the Anuek so the Yunjo will last longer, and go back to dotting my mob. By the time I finish killing my mob the Anuek is down to 30% health but the Yunjo is still around 90%. The slave eventually killed the gladiator, no problems.

I was interested by this and was able to repeat it several times, each time a slowed lvl 64 mob was trashed by a lvl 40 mob. The slowed gladiator simply couldn't keep up with the innate regen of the slave.

I know there is some funky coding with mobs and regen, so I wonder if I just malosina'd the gladiator would the outcome be different?
