I'm not sure all HGs (and SGs for that matter) are the same but...

I've been soloing SGs for the last couple of levels and just recently started soloing the HGs in NK. Like I said, I'm not sure if all Giants in the "Old World" have the same hitpoints and do the same damage so I can't comment on the Rathe Mt HGs.

As far as my technique? I usually start by casting root, snare then DoT (stinging swarm). I then start plinking arrows into them like there's no tomorrow all the while keeping my distance and the above spells casted.

Once the giant is down to about half life (or I run out of arrows -whichever comes first ;) ) I start in with melee.

If my health gets down to about half I will re-root, back off, and heal myself (this usually finishes off my mana though).

Now, keep in mind that this is not a fast thing to accomplish. It usally takes about 10+ minutes to take the giant down and then another 15 or so to regain health and mana.

The only reason I even started to bother with soloing giants was to get my Sands of Ro off an SG. Since then, I only do it to pass the time in between dungeon crawls, quests and/or guild functions.

As far as plats go - SGs don't drop all that much but the HGs in NK usually drop between 20 and 45pp each time.

Hope that helps. :)

btw I'm level 45 now.

At level 30 you'll probbly need a partner but I'm find Stonbrunt Mountains (SBM) a Ranger's paradise and making excellent money there (better than when I group for HG's). This is at 38-40 though, so like I said. You'll need a partner or to level a bit first.

It's a Ranger's paradise because it's a large outdoor zone and everything is fearable. It's also very quiet so no one around to interfere with your kiting. Tracking is a must especially if you want to kill panda, and you want to kill pandas

To make money... kill Pandas. The more senior the better. Many of them drop gems or rings that sell from 50-80pp to merchants. In between you can collect components for the Wak of Frozen Sky quest (which you can sell for several hundred plat or keep as it's a rather nice weapon). You can also keep an eye out

I made about 1K here over the course of about 8 hours total. I may have been a little lucky though.

There are downsides. It's a long way from a bindspot and from civilisation in general. It's long way to zone. Until you get lev at 40 you'll get a lot of falling damage. And the adds.....

This is the worst place I've ever been for adds and I end up having to abandon every second fight. As long as I have SoW up I'm ok though, but it can be frustrating having to run and camp over and over again.

As well as the documented Wak and Swiftclaw Sash quests there are a lot of other interesting drops in the zone that don't seem to be part of any documented quests yet. Becuase of it's remoteness it is an underutelised zone and I really think there may be some other undiscovered quests there.
