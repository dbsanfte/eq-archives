For an untwinked first character, your gear looks fine to me. I would gradually start converting most of your gear to +Hit Point gear (rather than +Wis gear), but I don't see anything glaringly obvious that you need. Working on Velious armor is probably the next logical gear step for you. Kael Drakkal giant killing for Thurgadin quest armor seems like your best bet.

At 52 you can camp the Sebillis crypt in a good group for a JBB (and a Heiro Cloak), if you can find or build a group to do it. The Mushroom King in Sebillis is more of a level 60 camp in my opinion, and the Fungi is quite rare now. It is rarely camped these days because it is a hard camp with low exp and only a very rare reward.

Affliction Focus effects were recently made much less useful for Shaman. They now only work on DoTs, rather than debuffs and DoTs. I would try to get an Obulus Death Shroud for Mana Preservation 3 (Old Sebillis again - random drop off random frogs).

another thing is resist gear. i think i'll buy a crown of the froglok kings, seems like money well spent. beyond that, can anyone suggest some cheap resist items that won't detract horribly from my gear in raids?

I would not spend cash on resist gear unless you want to do Vox and Naggy a lot. If you want a froggy crown, take few people to Lower Guk and camp the Froglok king's room. Exp should still be decent at 52. Don't buy things that drop commonly in places that are good grouping spots for you.

last thing is WR bags. i have a rallic pack and a couple hand-made backpacks. what is a bag of the tinkerers, and where does it drop/how is it made? how about the evil eye bag? the bag of nil space? any info appreciated.

You can solo the evil eye in Lower Guk for an EE bag if you want one. Box of Nil Space drops in the same camp in Seb as JBB. Bag of Tinkerer's cost 5250pp. As a Shaman, WR bags are not very important to you, though. You have the best strength buffs in the game, and Barbarians have good starting strength. Tailor made 10 slot backpacks should be fine.

--> EXPLORING --> --> GUILD --> --> LOOT --> --> EXPERIENCE --> and Epic

Basically, you need to network. In EQ, just like in life, you gotta have freinds. It is time to quit soloing and farming Rathe Mountains, and learn how to be a great grouping shaman. Join pickup groups in Karnor's Castle and Sebillis. Die a lot. CR a lot. Learn how to not die a lot and CR alot. Learn how to get trained and kill the train, rather than running to the zone line. Learn how to get a group of people to work together, rather than playing like 6 soloers stuffed in a group. Also, I hear that mid to low 50s people actually group some for good exp in the Tier 1 planes.

Talk to people. Ask them about their guild. Find some people you like hanging out with. Build your own group by inviting people to join you. Try to find some regular grouping/hunting partners and set up regularly scheduled grouping time with them on a weekly basis. Find a guild of people interested in the same things that you are interested in doing, and join them. Recognize that if you want people to help you with things like your epic, you should help them on things they want and need first. Ultimately, Everquest is a game about grouping. There are better designed games for single player play.

I wouldn't worry about your gear, it's much better than what most of us had at that lvl.

I'd start saving money for the big ticket items (Fungi, JBB, malo, torpor), maybe for a horse although I personally dislike them too much to bother.

Best advice to give you is what a few others have said, start making friends! You can solo to 65 if you want but you've passed the point where you could actually solo mobs that drop equipment upgrades. Easiest thing to do would probably be to hit some of the grouping spots for your lvl and start meeting people. I wouldn't even bother with full groups, find a melee (or necro/mage) that's LFG and go duo somewhere. I always found it easier to meet people when the groups where relatively small.

You might also want to figure out what you want to do at the high end. If you want to join an uber raiding guild, screw equipment upgrades and torpor /malo, just start hunting in PoP and worry about getting to 65. If you want to enjoy some of the 50s lvl dungeons, try to find a more casual guild around your lvl that raids occasionally. Most of the coolest raiding/dungeon zones are for lvl 50-60 and you'd certainly miss those if you went straight for the high end. It all depends on what you're looking for.

My two grind spots for low 50s where OOT and the BW giant fort. BW is probably a better chance to meet people but the giants are sometimes camped which slows down exp. You can probably average about 500p there in a 4 hour camp while still getting exp. OOT is about the same if you hunt seafuries but there you can camp Gornit if he's open for really nice cash. Only other decent money and exp spot I can think of for your lvl is Ice Giants in Everfrost but I never felt like fighting the idiots there for the spawns.

Black Dire in MM isn't too difficult at your lvl, you do need a melee partner though, trying to solo him at 52 would be a crapshoot. You're gambling that your pet lasts long enough for you to stick mal-x, at which point you can rot him. I don't think I'd try him solo before 55. The shaman epic mobs really aren't that difficult. I'm pretty sure that my favorite tank and I (65sk, 61 shm) could duo all parts of the shaman epic except for Fear (we would need a rogue to get us into CoM though).

Sebilis is still the best money dungeon but you'd have to see how easy it is to get pickup groups there on your server. Don't think you can solo even Seb zone-in at 52. Velks might also decent, not as a solo spot for your lvl but should be easy to find a melee to partner with. The entrance to the Hole might also be a good solo spot for ya. Dunno if it's starting to turn light blue at that lvl.

Best of luck to ya and happy hunting!
