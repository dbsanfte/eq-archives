Shaman - (SHAH muhn).
The word shaman comes to us through Russian from the Evenk people of Siberia (referred to as Tungus by Russians). It is sometimes spelled saman, but shaman is more accurate. Generally, when used as a noun, it has no gender, however some use it as a masculine term with the feminine being shamanka, but this is actually a Russian change, the Evenk people would say shaman in all cases. Shaman may also be used as a verb, defining a state of being. For example, "he is shaman" is equally correct to "he is a shaman".
