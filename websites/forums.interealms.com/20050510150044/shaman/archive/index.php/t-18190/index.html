Helfdane
Seeing that you have a nice selection of spells available to you and that you have Cann 5/Torpor combo going on, I believe that you have 2 options that you can pursue:
1) Start saving your AA for Natural Durability; your hit point total isnt really all that respectable for your level; one cann 5 when your recently rezzed and bam you need another clicky. Your gear is nice, but Wolf Callers, while great armor 2-3 years ago doesnt cut the mustard these days (I personally have a full set of Kael banked for just this reason). Natural Durabilty will give you a few hundred more hit points and that will only go up as your gear improves.
2) Get better gear (Tae Ew Bp and Legs, Barbed Dragonscale Boots are all very very nice choices for the hit point impared) and start using your AA for flushing out SCR to full, then getting SCRM (a 6-7 tick Torpor/Q kicks a$$). You can also get CA and LR, both of which would let you get hit less, but I think that in your situation your really really in need of more hit points.
I am by no means uber, most of my gear is /bazaar type (recently acquired my BP and legs from miraguls though), and I am in a small family guild that just likes to get rowdy in chat and not get /reported :D so we dont do much more than group in LDON together, and occasionaly get wild in BoT, etc. so I do know what your talking about not raiding a lot and not having to have the raid AA selection going.
Do a few pickup raids in BoT Tower, look for random Luiclin raids, get your guild to wear LDON out (a great option for smaller 3-4 group guilds is to simply give LDOn hell till your all wearing the high end armors from there) all are great options to get your hit point total up and thus be able to cann without fear of a random death.

I agree with Donar, work on stuff that you will be using every day, all the time first, SCR/SCRM/SCM, etc. My recommendation is to save the things like CA/LR and defensive skills for later, you can avoid getting agro and getting hit and you wont use them ALL the time (mind you Im NOT saying they arent great and important, just placing them behind the AAs can get that affect you EVERY time you cast a spell). The defensive skills become particuarly unuseful when you have a knight tanking for you (I might have a different opinion on this if I soloed a lot, however I almost always group).

Im a person of convenience, so SCR/SCRM rated highest with me since I hate having to continually rebuff, especially haste. In addition, it extends the duration of your Qui/Torpor spell making it more efficient, which you just cant beat.

Slip ND somewhere in there for hp since you will be cannVing a lot. Think the order I got them in was MGB (I raid a lot-you dont, so skip it), SCR/SCRM, QB1 (for the title), SCM3, ND3. RC and VP are also very useful and i would also get them before the defensive AAs like CA/LR but I would still not rate them higher than the previous ones I listed.

Which AA ability to buy after Canni 5 really depends upon your play style. As you said, you don’t raid so MGB should not be on the top of your list. I don’t raid either so I’ve yet to save for that ability. But I do want to buy it at some point so I can buff folk in PoK if nothing else. But there are other abilities that are more useful to me and are most likely more useful to you as well.

I almost exclusively doing LDoN adventures these days. As such I do a lot of buffing. The idea of having my buffs last longer is very appealing. So after Canni 5 I spent my points on Spell Casting Reinforcement 3, which gives + 30% duration to buff spells. I’m currently saving points to buy Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery, which adds another 20% to buff spells, for a total of +50% buff duration. And though Caster’s Realm lists the cost of Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery as 5 AA points, when I look at it in game it’s listed as costing 8 points. I currently have five points saved so just three more to go. And I’m also ready to do my final walk for my 8th shawl, which in addition to Flowing Thought III also adds +20% to buff durations. So I hope to soon have +70% to my buff spell durations. That’s going to be nice.

I’m considering buying Quick Buff 3 next, for a total of 18 points, to cut my casting time of buff spells in half. I would also like to have more hit points for Canni 5, as I’m left with about 30% health when self buffed. So I may go that rout first. But whichever I choose first I’ll most likely buy the other next.

Best of luck with your decision.
