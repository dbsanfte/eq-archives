I have been extremely impressed with the interface design and ideas in ToonTown.

It's a great on-line game for children and it is a readily buffered.

My 8 year old wanted to play EQ, and initially she had a tremendous time running about. I got her a drogmar, and an EB item and a couple of twink weapons and the next thing I know her High Elf was running about in West Karanas.

But some jerk started pestering her and wanted her to give him 1000 plat or something. I saw the tail end of that, petitioned him and put him on ignore, but it worried me some.

So, we tried Toon Town, and she loved it. It does have a buffered chat mechanism, where you select phrases from drop down menus. It's not really a game any of us would want to play, but it's got it's own very dedicated core. Two people can agree to "special chat" to each other also, but it requires entry of the parental password.

Clearly a lot of adults play too, given that my 40 year old wife is totally hooked. She sees lots of "Granny Smith" and other names that appear to reflect adults.

Anyway, it's got a great design for children and lots of really creative interface tricks that I think are both easy and powerful - and that's a wonderful combination.
