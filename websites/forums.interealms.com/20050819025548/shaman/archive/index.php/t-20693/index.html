Caanus Grey
There are a two ways to view "overpowered" and sadly only 1 seems to get much attention.


A class that can do alone what an entire group tries to do, is overpowered. This had been us one time with doin dragons in WW. Come PoP it was anyone that could charm something. Now in GoD it is still those who can charm.

The other way you can be overpowered doesn't get as much attention IMO, but it is when you become indispensible.

-If no shaman is around you can still raid using an extra healer or 2, a stronger tank and an enchanter.
-If no warrior is around you have to settle for lower level content or bring a TON of extra healers (at some points not even that can get you by so you need a HUGE supply of backup knights).
-If no clerics are around, you have to settle for TRIVIAL content, or have a raid that is almost 1/3 druids.


So you can either look at overpowered as being as far as solo capability, or as far as group/raid DEPENDANCE. I personally don't think that group/raid DESIRE is a factor in overpowering. Someone will be best at some area which is most critical, even if those 2 clarifications are by a small margin. However, to have things grind to a halt in the absence of a certain class is really, really stupid.

As for being overpowered in the solo field, I don't mind that really. Those characters will farm up their AA and gear, then move on to an alt when they have nothing else to work on. Just look at bards with their swarm kiting. Most all of the high end bards I know rack up a couple hundred AA real fast, then only log in the bard for raids. If all solo ability overpowered classes were THAT far overpowered it'd make it a trivial concern IMO.
