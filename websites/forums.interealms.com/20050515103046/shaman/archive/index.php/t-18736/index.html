Like I said, a two-box duo can be stronger than two seperate players if they are strengthened by not needing to communicate. For example, the monk I group with has a hotkey that makes his cleric autofollow him, like most two-boxers do. He drags the cleric with him when he pulls, and uses it to pacify the room. He doesn't have to waste time saying "assist me and paci" and wait for confirmation from the cleric that it is done, he just does it. This saves time and makes things run faster. Also, when a puller is taking damage, you typically aren't supposed to heal them because you run a high risk of aggroing a lot of mobs that the monk was intending to FD off. The monk knows when it is safe to heal and not heal, once again saving time and reducing risk.

A tank is boxing the cleric? He knows when he's taking damage too fast and is going to need a blast heal. He also has the CH timing down to a science. If he has a different cleric each time, that cleric might over or under heal. He'll also know immediately when the cleric is under attack, and whether or not the cleric can just tank it or if they're going to need it pulled off of them.

The enchanter and druid I mentioned? Absolutely perfect charming duo. The enchanter knows instantly when his charm is broken, and thus the druid knows as well. He's got a hotkey that targets the ench and heals him, as well as targeting, assisting, and snaring. If it was another player on the druid, you still have several seconds of reaction time for the druid to realize that charm is broken and decide which course of action (heal, snare, ignore) to take. If he's just boxing the druid, he knows what to do, just from 800 adventures and literally thousands of AA worth of experience between the two.

Not all two-boxers are like this, of course, but the ones who have been playing the characters for years and have a complete and total understanding of how they best work together are.

We spend almost all of our EQ time in LDoN (in fact, we levelled up and split off the shaman specifically so we'd have 4 for LDoN). We have a 100% win rate with either of those groups EXCEPT for 2 occasions of the servers coming down and three occasions of our going LD (ISP issues); have about 140 wins now and am ranked 124 on Xegony as of last night (don't care about the rankings and wish they didn't even exist - but they can give you an indication of how much we play compared to other people).

A few notes of agreement with others:

1. I know that I 2-box my characters better than SOME people play a single character. My druid can keep me healed up and nuke without going oom and without letting me die once in the last two years (in cases where death COULD be avoided). I've had plenty of clerics mis-time a complete heal on me, run OOM because they are casting the wrong heals, etc. I've played with druids who nuke all of their mana away and have nothing when it comes time for the heals. Etc. etc. etc.

2. I know that I play my paladin less effeciently when I 2-box than when I don't (we drop our 2-box character when real people join us). The ONLY times that is noticable is when the crap REALLY hits the fan and I need to concentrate completely on one character. Even with the best hotkeys, you can't do 4 things at once. :) Just having to decide whether character #1 or character #2 should get all of the attention is tough sometimes (e.g., should my druid be evacing? should my paladin lay hands, root the mob and tell the enchanter to move the heck out of the way?). However, this gets easier with practice - it's no different than the rest of EQ in many ways; if you stay calm, you generally have time to do the right thing. Not 2-boxing just increases that time and/or removes some of the decisions.

3. 2-boxing can be VERY advantageous, as others have mentioned. I never have to wonder if a heal is coming. I never have to wonder "how much time is left on that complete heal? should I lay hands?" I can just glance at the screen and see it. As the druid/healer, I never have to wonder what the heck the puller is doing. I don't have to ask the paladin what he's doing when he's running around root parking. I don't have to the tank that I have aggro or that he's moved the mob out of my line of sight, etc. A lot of mundate tasks and communications are removed by 2-boxing - although this also happens with a group that plays together frequently.

4. A lot depends on the combination of classes that are being 2-boxed. Some "finesse" classes are just too hard to 2-box (bards and, in many cases, enchanters provide a lot of challenges so frequently that you have to ignore the other character). My druid can be run 99% of the time with one page of hotkeys that make him nuke, heal, sit down, even stand up & forage, etc. He requires only quick glances to make sure lag hasn't eaten a command, he hasn't fizzled, etc. etc. (I also have him send some tells to me so that I know that a hotkey has finished running.)

My wife's ranger has AM3 & EQ - she's basically a fire and forget missile (gamepad for "autoattack" archery). once she has her shot lined up, she can devote 99% of her attention to her shaman (sort of the reverse of my situation). Target dies and she sets up the next one.

It is much harder for the enchanter/paladin combo I mentioned. If the enchanter needs to do crowd control, she really just has to ignore her paladin temporarily.

Ironically, the hardest thing I've found is dealing with the situation where my druid is in danger - it makes it hard to decide whether to react with him or with my paladin!

I should mention that 4 of us (2 people, 4 characters) finish Normal LDoN missions in 45-70 minutes, with most of them being in the 50-60 minute range (a lot depends on dungeon layout, seeding, etc.). We are by no means uber - you can see my magelo as proof of this: http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=231378&resize=true :)).

All of that being said, if I were doing a pick up group, I would be very skeptical of someone I didn't know 2-boxing. It does take practice and I've seen some people 2-boxing who can barely handle one character as it is. They are frightening.

2 Boxing is not better, or worse than 2 individual players. It's simply
different. It has pros and cons.

I play a shaman and a druid for the 2 box.

Pro to 2 boxing:
Is that I have intimate knowledge of what is going on with the other char that no single boxer can ever have. I can see for example if the CH is landing too slow and back it up with a spot. I can see the slow resist message and spam heal the shaman precious seconds before ANY 1 boxer could possible react. Heck, on a raid think of how many times you die as a shaman to slowing a boss. I die probably 1 time for every 10 you do.

I can do things like in tier3 in time. have each defaulted to a different tank and spot and then hotkey double up on a tank that isnt getting enough heals.

Basically put, since I have 2 computers running, I have more "information" coming my way than any 1 boxer.

Con to 2 Boxing:
You split your attention. And sometimes limit yourself to how many different things you can do at once on each. IE: say you can do 3 things on the 1, and 3 on the other in a situation. Trying to run both you might only get 4-5 accomplished.

Simple truth, is that when your talking skilled 2 boxers vs 2 skilled 1 boxers. Your talking apples and oranges. There are things I can accomplish 2 boxing that the best of 1 boxers would not be able to simply because of reaction/communication delay time. On the other hand, there are things that I am unable to accomplish that 2 1 boxers would.
