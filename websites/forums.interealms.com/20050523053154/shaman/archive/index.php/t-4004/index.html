Darjeeling
With focus items combined with AA, you can get some nice rewards from superior heal.

Since the removal of the healing penalty, superior healing does 583 hps. If you have Adept at 3, that's a 10% boost for ~641 hps. If you have a Healing 3 focus item (0%-20% bonus), that can boost it up to 20% for ~770 hps. If you have AA healing gift, 10% of the time you will get a double heal, so anywhere between 1282 hps, and 1540 hps.

Also, with focus casting haste (15% reduction), superior heal's cast time drops to ~3.8 secs. So it's 0.8 secs slower than chlorblast but for a bigger bang. Chloroblast isn't affected by focus items, because it's at 3 secs already.

Add in a manasaver focus item and your sup heals cost only 225 mana. Factoring in mana savings from specialization which will give you a saving of 11% so mana cost is 200. Deduct another 10% if you have spell casting mastery at 3, making it 180 mana.

So 10% of the time you are getting an instant torpor, and all the time, you are getting half a torpor.

Not bad all up. But, get torpor.

Even the most reticent clerics and chanters I know now actively look to be focused these days. Chanters start to worry less about MR and CHA at level 60 and go for HPs. Clerics more and more follow the shaman thinking, they get their mana and AC to a comfortable level and then go for HPs, their defensive abilities are limited, its more about durability than avoidance.

MGBs usefulness to an shaman in terms of point spend is entirely personal and situational but saying don't get it because clerics and chanters don't like it is rather misleading.
