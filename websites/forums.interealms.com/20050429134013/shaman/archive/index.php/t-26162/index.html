LaughingWoman
Macroeconomics isn't a HARD topic by any stretch, but it isn't one that everyone's had much exposure to.

Pure & simple? Without the real world $ incentive, these people would NOT be farming gold in WoW. Remove that possibility and a lot less gold enters the economy. Less inflation, and your gold buys more. It's not a *maybe* thing - that's what's happened in every mmorpg with an economy to speak of.

Inflation is a natural part of any economy, but the goal of an mmorpg is to reach some kind of equilibrium, so they don't have to constantly update quest rewards, spell prices, etc.

Farmers aren't a natural part of the economy because they wouldn't exist, save for the RL $ changing hands. Hell, I guess it doesn't matter how many other people do or don't understand the impact they have, just so long as Blizzard gets it.
