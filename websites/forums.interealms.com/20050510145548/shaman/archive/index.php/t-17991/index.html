Having creatures respawn and sitting on one place repeatedly killing the same critters over and over is "parking/camping" not doing a "crawl". A crawl is clearing an entire dungeon from start to finish. LDoN does have elements of repetition but it's the closest thing to a classic PnP dungeon crawl you can find. LDoN has as much lore or story behind it as any other aspect of EQ - and how many people do you know that actually give a flying monkie's butt about EQ lore anyways? Romping through a level 40 dungeon with a raid of 65's ins't a crawl, it's a slaughter. You *might* be able to crawl a dungeon like LGuk these days with a good group or two made up of levels that would be challenged and get xp, but back in the days when LGuk was actually (over) popular there was no chance of doing a crawl there since every spawn in the dungeon was camped and you could often move freely from end to end in there without danger. I might've done it once back in the day...whereas I can do several LDoNs a day on the spur of the moment with ease.

LDoN missions have a timer and a goal - they may not be that complex but they do have goals and the timer keeps you moving. If you crawl in a classic dungeon what is the goal? What is the motivation to even finish? The other aspect of LDoN that's nice is that everyone has more potential for rewards - there are chances for augs to customize/enhance your gear, chances for drops which are sometimes very nice, and even if you get none of that you earn points to buy things you actually want. In most classic dungeons even if you crawl them with loot in mind you may or may not get said loot depending on whether mob X is up (and if you camp him you're not crawling anymore) and if that 1 item does drop only 1 person can have it.

I've played multiple MMORPGs and dungeons/content in them are basically the same. Camp, kill mob that respawns in in the same spot/pattern repeated, repeat, repeat, repeat. Every static dungeon has the exact same layout with the exact same critters at the exact same spawn points every single time you visit them. At least LDoN offers some randomness within the various themes and dungeon layouts. How are the LDoN dungeons with varying critters over time, random loot, and adjusting difficulty levels any more cookie cutter than a static dungeon that is always exactly the same (aside from the occasional named vs placeholder hell) ?

Meanwhile, /lfg is chock full of people flagged as seeking LDoN and everybody else is seeking planes.

Having read many threads regarding LDoN on various forums there seem to be very few people that dislike LDoN, but they seem to violently dislike it. That's cool - whatever you do for fun is cool with me as long as you're not exploiting or ruining fun for others. I and many others, however, seem to find LDoN to be quite enjoyable.

Most expansions bring the same kind of additions - new classes, new races, more content that is basically just more zones (more of the same), a few new interface features to make life easier, etc. LDoN brought a whole different type of content. Yes, it's like AOs missions but AO isn't that fun (point blank battles with ranged weapons that lasts for minutes at a time is downright silly) and AO missions were even more generic that LDoN is.

You can like or dislike LDoN but I still contend that it's the closest thing to a classic PnP dungeon crawl and in many ways is more rewarding than attempting to simulate a crawl in a classic dungeon.
