Gloriana
Conventional wisdom dictates that you put as much into wis as you can and the remaining 5 points into agi/sta.

Wis - mana pool
agi - more AC and helps to offset some extremely nice high level gear that has agi penalities (lodi shield and fungi tunic)
sta - more hitpoints - very important in the high end game, due to cannabalize

Cha and int are extremely unimportant stats for shaman. Str looks appealing since so many low level characters are encumbered for so long (particularly iksar) but don't...you will soon have the ability to buff str to the heavens.

Although conventional wis dictates 25 points into wis, I have found that at the high levels it isn't difficult at all to find very nice gear that is high in wis but very difficult to find nice gear that is high in sta (that you don't have to fight hte melees over).

I would recommend (and this is pretty wild) 10 points into wis and 20 into sta (or 20 points into wis and 10 points into sta).

This is the opinion of a level 60 barb shaman, if I could go back to my character select screen and select starting stats knowing what I know now...that's what I would do.

Definitely, anything but cha, int or str!

Yes, it is, Jentriken. Not saying that stamina doesnt help, but for a shaman it's not necessary to focus on it, or put points into it in creation.

Druids and shamans are both priest archetype, so we probably get the same exact stamina to hit point ratio. Let's say it's 3 hps per stamina at level 60.

3 out of the 5 shaman races are the large races and start with 100+ base stamina.

Shamans also have that nice stamina buff you mentioned, so we can keep our stamina buffed all the time.

Some of the gear we get will have stamina boosts as well, of course. Cant say shaman gear would have more stamina boosts than any other class-specific gear, but I'm sure ours wouldnt have less. Just looking at my gear; my epic gives +10 sta, RE chest gives +9, coldain gloves another +9, necklace +5, wolf caller arms +3, and wolf caller boots +6. That's a total of 42 stamina, or 126 hps. I have no +100 hp items from NToV or where ever, and I only have the small pieces of Skyshrine armor (helm, arms, boots).

Also, the original question was posed by somebody just creating a shaman. He wont even have cannibalize for some time, so converting hps/stamina into mana isnt even a reality for him. But handicapping himself with low wisdom and lots of stamina that he cant do anything with is possible.

EDIT: Also, most shaman races start with low base wisdom so it takes us longer to crack 200 wisdom than it would for a druid. Druids typically dont worry about AC and go strictly after wisdom, whereas shamans start lower and cant use (or cant afford to use) some of the low AC items that druids do (GEB's, paw of opalla, split paw gloves, et al).
