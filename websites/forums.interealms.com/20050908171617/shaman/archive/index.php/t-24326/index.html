Qhan
I haven't made EQ work on my linux machine, but mostly only because I don't play enough EQ to bother anymore (I keep a windows machine for gaming anyway). However, I have made several things work with wine (Internet Explorer, MS Office, etc).

Compiling wine shouldn't be too hard I wouldn't think. In fact, there are likely some Mandrake RPMs floating around out there, or you could set up one of the package management systems (urpmi or apt?) under mandrake and try to install it that way. If I recall, there are pretty detailed walkthroughs on getting wine installed, and they do require a good bit of config file editing, but its not anything too hard.

I wouldn't bother with the virtual machines. The best I know of is VMWare, and I don't think they have 3d support. Besides that, its not free and runs noticeably slower being inside the VM unless you really have a top end machine.

Check out http://www.pclinuxonline.com. Its just basically a blog/news site of a guy that does alot with Mandrake, and on the left hand side near the bottom he has some nice links to RPMS and such.

Also, you mentioned that money was an issue, but I would highly suggest looking at Cedega (packaged version of wine with an installer for games). I don't remember the cost, but I've seen it in action and it is very, very nice. Makes installing games in Linux incredibly easy. I think they are subscription based, and if so, you could subscribe, install as much as possible, and cancel.
