Boy o boy...

Please Please Please stop referring to these two numbers as being the "same". They are not. And I'm going to tell you why.

Example:

Mob does 100 DPS.

Shaman slow is 75%.
Ench slow is 70%.

Equation for calculating slow damage:

Mob DPS x Slow Percantage = Slowed DPS.

After slowed, mob does 25 DPS if shaman slows, and 30 DPS if ench slows. Everyone agrees that this is the way slow works. Time has nothing to do with the equation, not sure why people keep bringing it up. I'm not sure why several people on these boards think that it is appropriate to compare 25 DPS and 30 DPS directly.

The only numbers that 25 and 30 can be compared to are the original number that was multiplied by the original percent. Because the original number was 100, there is still only a 5% difference between 25 and 30. There is no 20% difference between these two numbers, because they (and here is the important part) DO NOT EXIST ON THEIR OWN. They are the products of an equation dealing with a real (virtual) world component. This is one of the fallacies of mathematics--taking it from a textbook and applying it to the real world, some things just don't compute.

Now, if I were to ask you, 30 is what percentage of 25? Your answer would clearly be 120%. If you want to compare these two numbers on their own, you can, all day, and the answer is always going to be 20%.

But when comparing to the original 100, the answer is always 5%. 20% of 100 is 20, which is clearly wrong.

Look at it this way. You have a 100 DPS mob for 100 seconds. This equals 10000 damage over the course of the fight. If a shaman slows the mob at the beginning, it will only do 2500 dmg. If an enchanter slows at the beginning, it will only do 3000 dmg. If NOBODY slows, it will do 10000 dmg. You only obtain the 2500 and 3000 dmg numbers by acting on the original value.

This is almost like a Physics equation. A mob has a Potential Energy, and that potiential is 100dps. But in the real world, the Kinetic Energy it uses can be one of three values--100dps, 30dps, or 25dps--based on what percenage slow is applied. These numbers can only be compared back to the original Potential Energy, because the 30dps and 25dps can't exist at the same time, only one or the other, therefore they can never be directly compared.

I'll use one more example. Let's say you have an engine that can go 100 MPH. But you want to test two governors (speed limiters) on the engine, to find out which will slow it down the best. One limits the engine to 25 MPH, and the other to 30 MPH. Which is better? The one that limits to 25 MPH, obviously. But I challenge you find an engineer or mechanic doing the testing to tell you that the one that goes 25 MPH is 20% better than the one that goes 30 MPH. It's not--it's only 5 percent better.

This is why there are liars, damn liars, and statisticians.

Yes, an enchanter slowed mob does 20% MORE damage than if the shaman slowed the mob. Hey, and while we are at it, the shaman slowed mob does 16.7% LESS damage than a similarly enchanter slowed mob. All 3 are correct statements. Get over it.

And for those of you who think using the 5% number makes for a better shaman case, then you are dead wrong. Let me cover the WHOLE slow process. And yes, I do realize there are some assumptions below.

Okay, let's cover the 'length of fight issue'. It's not really a 'length of fight' rather than 'length of fight vs length of slowing time' really. Somebody previously covered this, but here is our formula for the effective 'Slow %' based on time to slow and time of combat. Let's repeat it here:

Let
Ts = Time to land slow
Tt = Time total of fight
Sa = Slow Actual
Se = Slow Effective over total fight.

Se = (S * (Tt-Ts))/Tt

As we'd expect, as Ts get's closer to Tt the less S affects the Effective Slow. And if everything is preslowed (Ts = 0) then ya, the length of the fight doesn't matter, no slows are affected.

Ah, but we haven't included mitigation yet! Okay, from another thread, the current though is that the first X percent of slow is unmitigated (perhaps 30%) followed by a fixed percent mitigation after that.

Let
U = Unmitigated percent
M = Migitation factor above U
S = Slow Percent (75% for shaman, 70% for enchanter, etc...)

If (S < U) Then Sa = S
Else Sa = U + (S - U) * M

This Sa we get, get's plugged into the first formula above to get the final Effective Slow.

Let's give an example. Say a 30s fight, that takes 10s to slow with a mitigation of 50 mitigated above 30. Please feel free to try different numbers at home.

Shaman (75%):
Sa = .3 + .45*.5 = .555 (or 55.5%)
Se = (.555 * (30-10))/30 = .37 (or 37%)

Enchanter (70%):
Sa = .3 + .40*.5 = .5 (or 50%)
Se = (.5 * (30-10))/30 = .333 (or 33.3%)

Beast (65%):
Sa = .3 + .35*.5 = .475 (or 47.5%)
Se = (.475 * (30-10))/30 = .317 (or 31.7%)

Bard (53%?):
Sa = .3 + .23*.5 = .415 (or 41.5%)
Se = (.415 * (30-10))/30 = .277 (or 27.7%)

Okay, how do we compare all these. Since we are the best, let's compare to shaman.

If we just substract the slowing percent (the "5% method"):
Enchanter 3.7% (previously 5%)
Beast 5.3% (previously 10%)
Bard 9.3% (previously 22%)
Unslowed 37% (previously 75%)

Looking at these raw numbers, I would say that shaman/enchanter/beast aren't significantly changed. Sure the gap is closer but it wasn't far to start. On the other hand, the bard/unslowed numbers are reduced by half.

However, if we compare the extra dmg by using inferior slow compared to shaman (the "20% method"):
Enchanter (66.6-63)/63 = 5.7% (down from 20%)
Beast (68.3-63)/63 = 8.4% (down from 40%!)
Bard (72.3-63)/63 = 14.8% (down from 88%!!)
Unslowed (100-63)/63 = 58.7% (down from 300%!!!)

And now I hope you understand, comparing using "the 20% method", you can see JUST HOW MUCH GROUND shaman have lost. It's massive especially for bard and unslowed.
