When the the "warriors" tried this way back, all but one warrior in my guild thought it was stupid. While there were problems with the class they were far from what the hysteria going on in TSW would have suggested, most of those people got caught up in their own hype. The so called "warrior fixes" actually did very little except make a few things a little easier, and they certainly didnt resolve any of the underlying issues which exist to this day. The issues faced by warriors at the time wasn't class destroying, but it did require warriors to adapt their play style within the context of the content they were facing. The good ones did, the poor ones whined.

In the end the cycles of change caught up to everyone, and in the latest iteration of the game weaknesses that were less important became more important and the percieved tide of class favor changed. Actually the innate strengths and weaknesses of warriors/paladins/SKs didnt change at all, but the way content reacted to them did. And in the next expansion I'm sure it will change again.
