I WHOLEHEARTEDLY agree with you Ooga.

M59 might be outdated graphically, but for anyone interested in gameplay value as opposed to looking at pretty graphics, M59 is hands down the best. It may be 6 years old, but it's STILL lightyears ahead of games like EQ, DAoC, etc. when it comes to game design.

If NDS had the financial backing to do Meridian 59 II, with modern graphics/size/scope.. and maybe a few additional features, it'd totally blow EQ out of the water.

what's so great about it?
a REAL guild system, not just another chat channel. complete with guild halls, guild treasury/vault, guild status levels (that do more than just let you invite other people :p) etc. etc. It rivals DAoC's guild system from what I've seen of DAoC.

customizeable 'chat channels'. you make your own, you can customize your 'tell/send' handle with colors and ascii char's, it's not a 'graphical font' so you can actually cut and paste stuff, oh.. and this was a standard feature when the game came out, not a patch 3 years afterwards.

your char is based on skills that you improve by using them... there is no 'class' and there are no 'levels'.
There are several 'schools' that you can improve in, but there is no preset defining role that you have to play/are stuck with.

you don't have to worry about dieing because you accidently agro'd to many mobs.. if you run away.. you can actually run away.. (sarcasm)*boggle*(/sarcasm) :p most of the time you die from mobs because you gamble on whether you can kill it before it kills you, and you loose ;)

It's true PvP if you want to get involved in that (I still don't understand people who complain about getting PK'd.. if you don't open your big fat mouth and talk @$#%@ you really don't have much to worry about)

and many other things:
It's played in a window.. that you can minimize! (what? that's possible?)
Guildwars
the assassins game
faction wars
the whole good/vs evil thing (Ao3's and SoTH's)
rentable/customizeable rooms with storage
questy stuff
tournaments
probably other stuff I'm not thinking about

anyway... sure, if you not gonna play because you have a 100 Ghz processor and 5 billion megs of ram and a Gforce500 video card and can't possibly play anything that doesn't have photo quality graphics... *shrug* but if you're at all interested in playing a game that's actually fun, it's deffinately worth takeing a look at.

And yeah, I've wasted 300-400 days played in EQ, and I will probably continue to do so for quite a while, but it's obvious that the m59 designers had a clue when they were making it, whereas Verant obviously wanted to get a product on the market to make money without giving much thought to what a MMORPG should/could be.

P.S. Shea - server 101, beta -> the day 3DO pulled the plug -> NDS resurection (101 again).
P.S.S and yeah m59 was the first 3D (doom quality) orpg, it predated UO by at least a year or two.
