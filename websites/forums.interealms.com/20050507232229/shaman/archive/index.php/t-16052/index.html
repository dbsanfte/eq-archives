I like the way they indicate they are going to work it.

Age will be partly time based...this keeps it from being a "Oh look, I'm rich and powerful. I'll make a dragon twink and get him ancient in 1 day" thing. It also allows for casual players to 'age' at roughly the same rate as a powergamer. No penalty for not having an UberGuild.

It will also hopefully cut down on the number of people making dragons because they want to be 'Uber'. Juvenile dragons will supposedly be dufficult to play. Gotta work for that eventual power.

The other part of it will be a Trial of Passage kind of thing...quests that must be accomplished, a certain horde size that must be gathered, etc. to go from one stage to the other.

In theory, this should help prevent camping a dragon char and waiting until the Real Time timer kicks over, then bringing it on and *Poof* you can fly.

They stress that they want to make sure that casual players are not overly disadvantaged compared to the power gamers. Obviously, a powergamer is still going to have an edge in how much they can aquire, but at least the casual gamer doesn't gt left in the dust.

I'm liking that.
