Hmm, ok.

As most know there is a "Hate list" and a "Rampage list". The Rampage list is a pretty short one(3 or 4 positions). These 4 people will stay on the Rampage list for the whole fight, unless they die and stay out of Rampage range for a certain amount of time(Rampage list doesn't forget you as easily as the "Hate list" and you can die and get rezzed back in and get Rampaged on, if you try hard enough).
However, and here is the key point, the positions on the Rampage lists aren't static. Despite what most people think, the hate generated does have an impact on the Rampage list. The person with the most hate on the Rampage list(anyone else doesn't matter) will be on "Rampage" and get Rampaged on.
This makes you want to put a classes on it that can control aggro at will. Preferably a hybrid tank and 3 monks as they can FD the Rampage on the SK/Pally best.

Hope that helps.

Having done a bit of programing in my time, altho choosing not to do it as a living, I'd bet that they are using a what's called a linked list. It's a very common programing technique and is taught in most of your early programing classes.

The basic idea is simple. On computers accessing memory is SLOW. Processors currently range up to 3ghz (ie 3000mhz). Fast memory is 400mhz. Thats about 1 8th the speed of the processor. To make things worse memory access usually takes multiple cycles to access the memory making access even slower. As such programers commonly try to minimize access to memory when possible.

What a linked list does is add an extra field to the list that points to a line in that same list. For example take the 2 following lists.

first name last name weight
john doe 200
jane doe 120
homer simpson 300



first name last name weight link
john doe 200 1
jane doe 120 2
homer simpson 300 3


To sort the first list you have to copy around 3 peices of information per line. It's even worse because you have to read all the info on each line and then write it to someplace new.

The second list can be sorted simply by changing the link in the 4th field. So if you wanted to sort by weight it would look something like this for heaviest to lightest


first name last name weight link
john doe 200 3
jane doe 120 1
homer simpson 300 2

To get the heaviest person you would go to line 1 to the link field, follow it to the 3rd line and get homer simpson.
To get the lightest you would go to line 3 and check the link and get line 2 jane doe.

I expect the hate list is the same type of thing. The normal aggro rules follow the links to get who has generated the most hate while the rampage just follows the list in order and doesn't follow the links.

The only way to get off the rampage list is to get off the aggro list compleately. Dying doesn't seem to get you off the list. A mem wipe seems to be the only way to get off the list compleately.

I've seen it jump from warrior to warrior, but it was a function of one warrior not being in rampage range.

I've seen it jump from an SK to a warrior, then back to the SK when he stood up after feigning.

I've seen it jump from a monk to the cleric that healed him when the monk FD'd. The cleric healed the monk on inc and was first on the rampage list.

A few things I've noticed:

If you use multiple monks to tag pulls, all of those monks will be on the rampage list, but only the ones in range will be affected. So if you have 3 monks feigned out of range, and one feigned in range, only the monk in range will be affected by the rampage list if he/she stands up. If the first monk in the chain comes into agro range, he/she is going to get rampage. If the first and second monks in the chain come back, the first monk will get rampage until a.)he/she feigns, or b.)he/she dies. It will then switch to the second monk. (Insert any feign class in place of the monk and it works the same)

Every mob has a different rampage range. Vindi, for example, has a fairly small rampage range (just outside of melee range works). Rumblecrush, however, has a fairly large rampage range. (About 2-3 times his normal max melee range) Getting off rampage, but not off the list is just a matter of moving out of rampage range. As soon as you move back into range, you're back on rampage. This is why it appears rampage jumps from person to person. Here's an example:

Monk pulls Vindi. Cleric heals monk. Monk feigns. Warrior_1 tags Vindi and engages. Warrior_2 shoots a few arrows at Vindi just after Warrior_1 tags. Now, the rampage list looks like this:

1. Monk
2. Cleric
3. Warrior_1
4. Warrior_2

Now, with the monk feigned, the cleric is the highest on the rampage list and will start to take rampage damage. If the cleric backs out of range, the rampage damage will pass to warrior_2.

If the monk stands up, rampage will pass to the monk and stop hitting warrior_2.

If the cleric comes back in range while the monk is feigned, rampage will pass to the cleric and stop hitting warrior_2.

Rampage has nothing to do with the amount of agro a person has. If the monk only does 1 point of damage while pulling Vindi with a shruiken and feigns in Vindi's rampage range, he/she will still take rampage if they stand up right at the end of the fight, even after being feigned the entire time. (Monks like faction hits too!)

In the mage example, mage_1 did something to get on the rampage list and simply backed out of rampage range or died. Ie: Mage_1 nukes, Mage_2 nukes. Mage_1 starts taking rampage for whatever reason (like all the folks above him/her on the rampage list died or moved out of range) To have mage_1 switch off rampage to mage_2, the mob was either physically moved out of range of mage_1 while mage_2 was still in range, mage_1 physically backed up leaving mage_2 to get rampaged, or mage_1 died leaving mage_2 next on the list. As soon as mage_1 moves back in range, he/she will be back to getting rampaged.

Death doesn't alter your standing on the rampage list either. If you die from rampage, are rezzed back in, and move back into rampage range, you'll start taking rampage again.

edit: on a side note, if you really want to figure out how rampage works for yourself, take a group to the warrens and engage Mugglewump. He's a high 20's mob and rampages. He's pretty useful for testing out theories on rampage. I've used him to test out the above theories and they've all been sound across the spectrum, either on Mugglewump, Vindi, or in Ntov/Ssra.

Of course, this has nothing to do with AoE rampage, which is a completely different animal.

Soooo, Enrage!

Before changes made recently, pretty much every 55+ critter would enrage when they reached low health. It lasts about 10-15 seconds. During that time, as mentioned previously, the critter ripostes all frontal attacks. Mostly the 'tank' type critters did this, but occasionally the other types as well from what I remember

A fairly recent change made this no longer true. In Bastion of Thunder, for instance, there are many post 55 critters that do NOT enrage. They changed this in most of the PoP zones. Still unsure why.. Either way, now it's just specific critters in PoP that do it. Oreen, by way of example, DOES enrage, but the 63rd Militis doesn't.
