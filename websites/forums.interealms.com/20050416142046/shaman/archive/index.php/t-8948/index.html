Hehe two years ago I'd have given the same advice, but any more it is virtually impossible to put together your own machine with a price comparison anything close the the major companies.

Consider--he says you can re-use many of your own parts and keep your price down around $400. And that is absolutely true. But, you can also go buy a brand new system without a monitor from one of the major companies for about the same price. At that point, it is hard to justify the time and effort to make a new machine (unless that is entertainment to you, as it is to me :),
and you get all new components in the deal.

I wish it was still true that you can build-your-own cheaper than the retailers--I used to make some decent extra cash building high-end "tailored" machines for some gamers and other power-users--but it really just isn't true any more :(
