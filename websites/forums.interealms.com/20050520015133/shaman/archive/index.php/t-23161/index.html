shaman is actually quite useful in tipt. honestly you aren't adding a lot of speed to your run but greatly increase the safety and thus the winability of a crawl

a lot of the groups ive done tipt for have been for alts or friends and Ive done tipt with a couple of marginal groups. baseline tank is prolly a 6kish/1400 base warrior with def AA and a 5k mana cleric. if you're north of that and have a decently equipped 8k+ tank obviously you can sleepwalk through the zone but to get friends through who might be a little undergeared ive found a shaman can add a lot

ive played both chanter and shaman and say what you like i still vastly prefer shaman slow for its casting speed over tash+FD... in a marginal group like the first poster is talking about where your tank can take hits from mobs while pet destroys, but who cannot stay up indefinitely, over the course of the expedition, having mobs slowed before it's in camp saves clerics mana and allows you to keep moving ahead of the respawn, which makes doing tipt much much easier

also assuming that you're using a pet, with TD3 and good char malos isn't imperative but it does help managing pets between recharms

finally there's the issue of spot heals. while a decent cleric can keep a marginal tank up versus mobs without undue hardship, for rampage, splurt dps from nocs and **** etc shaman are really nice to have. just overall makes things smoother and safer

ive done tipt with dps heavier groups war bard cle ench rng necro and with heal heavy groups war bard cle ench shm druid

and honestly i prefer the shaman group... the pet makes such quick work of the mobs that in the long run the entire expedition seems to take about the same amount of time either way. the longest stretches are when you wait on the mobs to be pulled etc... but with a shaman things seem much smoother. mainly because you dont spend 2 hours and get up there and then die from some burst of dps from the mob or if a broken pet gets in a swipe at your cleric

about the kvy hunter, he's not hard. the key things seem to be to of course dispell and also to make sure you fight him at spawn. his dps increases at a distance from spawn so there's no point in pulling him. send in pet, have bard or whoever dispell and take rampage, send in war with discipline and in 30 seconds mob is down. there's isnt a whole lotta room for error (ie there's not a whole lot you can do to **** up) as I say we've had a 6kish 70 AA war even tank him without issue so probably the main thing is to make sure your dispeller, tank and pet all hit the named at the same time

as a chanter i have to say, pet breaking shouldn't be a problem, there's really limitless ways you can minimize the danger of pet running buck wild. all of them are things that people just get too lazy to do like taking time at the beginning to make a few heal and root macros for your chanter and pet or taking time to properly position your pet and to keep your distance from it when moving it around. but if you have someone who keeps zoning out for whatever reason, invest in a decent bard or paladin who will tab buffer the pet. there's 0 way a chanter can die with a bard in the group when pet breaks

I also have to note that for some reason the assist range on mobs in this zone seems almost buggily small... it's impossible to pull more than 2 mobs from an area that should assist 4 mobs... almost seems broken. for a puller makes the zone really boring... a string of 6 single pulls followed by rooms with 10 mobs that all come by themselves followed by 6 single pulls etc etc

It isnt you Macheras, its just the way things are for those trials, they are not pickup group friendly. And if you do get a pickup group, chances are that it will not be made up of toons that have a high probability of success. You really need the sort of social connections that comes with being in a guild to progress there without frustration. In your position its a serious problem because to do the trials you need the social structure, but to get that social structure you need to have done the trials. Sort of a chicken/egg situation.

There are only two solutions really, one is to get friends in the guild that you are trying to join to help you, or, join a less developed guild that doesn't have KT as a requirement, or at least until you have it done (that will likely make you a few enemies though, as most guilds dont like being used as stepping stones).

You could opt for sitting there LFG as well, but the social dynamics are not in your favor in that option.

The other thing to remember is that guilds that impose these sorts of restrictions on applications often do so to test apps to see if they have what it takes to make things happen for them, since those are the sorts of people they usually want.
