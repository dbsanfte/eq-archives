Ok... just tried it out. here's whatcha do.

right click on your chat window and select new chat window.

Now /autojoin XXX (xxx being the name of your new channel). Right click on the new chat window, scroll down to filters, scroll to chatchannel, and select your new channel... now it will go to this window.

Rightclick again on the new chat window. Drag down to filters, go across and select other. This will have your /time messages go to the window also. (there will be a few other random things going to this window like yells for help, but it's so rare it's really not gonna spam-flood your window at all. just be aware that the /time message does take about 5 lines so you will most likely end up scrolling this window a bit.)

now, just do what solastoru said:
line 1 /cast x (where x is the number slot that FA is in -- numbers go top to bottom)
line 2 /cast x, /pause 10 (to eliminate fizzles)
line 3 /X casting ferine avatar on %t (X being the number of your FA chat channel)
line 4 /time


Sorry if I kinda overexplained some things (like how to select a filter) I just figured that alot of folks might want to use this for other buffs (like younger folks using it for haste) so I thought it would be helpful to be as basic as possible.

Jaidin, the answer to your question is a little muddier... yes and no. As far as I can tell, the hot-keyed type abilities like inspect buff, mark npc, etc., can only be used by people in the group with GoD. To make the hoktkey you have to hit L to pull up the leadership window, select the ability, then click and hold on the little hotkey button. Yet hitting L to pull up the leadership window doesn't work unless you have GoD... so my guess is only group members with it can use it.

However, if you have abilities like find path to PC (can be used by just typing /findpc), or offense enhancement for example, then anyone in the group can easily use it. Health of target's target pops a window on everyone in the group's screen regardless of GoD, as does NPC health. So really, if it doesn't require pulling up the leadership screen to use it (i.e. making a hotkey) then everyone, regardless of expansions, can use it.

The group leader can still designate a main assist, even if MA doesn't have GoD... they can tag mobs and everyone can see it, they can inspect the buffs of everyone regardless of their expansions... The ONLY time someone other than the groupleader would need GoD is if they need a hotkey to use the ability (there might even be a way around this, to make a hotkey without using the leadership window... but I have yet to find it), for example, if I'm in a group with an enchanter, and I am leader, I can inspect everyone's buffs. but if the Chanter want's to inspect their buffs they would need to have the hotkey, so the enchanter would need GoD.

Basically, it doesn't hurt you in any way if anyone in your group doesn't have GoD, it just means they might not get to use one or two of the nifties... One exception... There is an LA that allows you to tag the mobs (basically you can put a number over their head to distinguish what order they are to be killed in). There is another LA that alows you to designate someone else to be the tagger (e.g. you give the enchanter the ability to tag the mobs in order, so they can tell the group which one's mez will break first, so you know which one to kill first). In this case, I assume such ability is using a hotkey... so whoever you give the tagging ability to must have GoD in order to actually use it.

In my mind, this ability was kinda back on the list in the first place... the fact that your chanter, or MA (whoever you were going to give tagging duties to) must have GoD in order for you to distribute it to them makes the ability dead last in my book.
