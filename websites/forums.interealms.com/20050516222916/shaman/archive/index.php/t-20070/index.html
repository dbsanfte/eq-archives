Thunderwolfjr
OK, my 2 cents. Since I'm close to your level (I'm getting ready to ding 58), I think I can state with confidence that the shaman is a blast to play. Especially at 58+, with all the great spells we get at 58, 60 and 65.

You will note that some of the higher end shaman (300+ aa's and elemental/Plane of Time raiding) have issues with raiding and soloing, however, almost every class has these same issues. At the very high end, it becomes a game of specialists, and shaman are one of the games best generalists. A blast to play for a long time if you are a casual or small guild type of player (I will never see the Plane of Time myself) but a bit frustrating for the power gamers. To be honest, at the very high end, if you are not a warrior, cleric, or very high dps class (wiz, rogue), then you are probably replaceable by another class. However, these great end game classes are also some of the worst solo'ers (except for Wizzes, but levels 1-29 are terrible for them) and most group dependent.

So for the casual player, one who is not planning on powerleveling to 65 in a week and farm Plane of Time and GoD until they hit the magic 540 aa's, the shaman is a great class to play. I can solo well, always get a group in a few minutes, and contribute to any group I play with.

Like most trips, the trick is to enjoy the ride and not just focus on getting there.

3) How are shaman these days?

I love mine. I'm rarely LFG for more than a few minutes if there's a group to be had - and if I don't group I always have something to do - so much so that I rarely play my alts. Did I mention that I love my shaman? I even solo some now even though I'm far from having a soloist mentality - now it's just another option. You might browse the last 10-20 pages or so of the forum (that may be far enough) to read the *discussions* that went on about shaman around the time of GoD release.

4) What expansions are good? There's this Evolutions thing out now. The last expansion I currently own is Velious.

Again. Opinions vary. I like having all the expansions so that I have everything available to me...but then $30 (price of Evolution) isn't a big deal to me. I think EQ Evolution is a great deal. LDoN is some of the most fun I've had in any MMORPG and it's still very popular (been out a few months now). I'm not sure about GoD yet...I bought it but have yet to have the opportunity to try it - it's less casual-player friendly than some content, IMO. It's also 50+ minimum and to do the meat of the content (the 'trials') you really want to be 65 with decent gear (not necessarily uber) and some AA (from what I've heard).

5) I haven't upgraded my comp since I quit. Have the system requirements gone up at all?

No clue. I'd imagine so. My system isn't that beefy and it runs EQ very well. I do have a decent video card though, which helps. You can get a video card that will crush EQ and pretty much all games out today for $100 (or less if you shop around).

6) How's high-end soloing these days? I remember grinding a bit of level 59 solo at the SS camp in Sol B.

I can't imagine many people soloing in Sol B these days except for nostalgia purposes. There are tales of shaman here soloing some of the highest (elemental planes) content in the game.

7) Anything else you want to tell me.

I originally played EQ at release and left days before Kunark shipped. I'm in my second run of EQ now starting just before LDoN hit. A lot has changed in EQ...some old timers don't like the changes. Personally I think EQ has become a lot more fun and offers a LOT more to do. There's so much content it's mind boggling and between classic content (traditional dungeons and outdoor zones), instanced dungeons (LDoN/GoD), planes (high level high xp zones), limited zones (those that require flags and/or level restrictions) and tons of raid-level content you have many options about how to spend your time (in addition to crafting, socializing, exploring, etc). Additions to the banking system, crafting system, trading mechanisms, and travel (Nexus, PoK) have all enhanced the fun side of EQ. And after all this time and with all these changes and additions EQ still has many challenges and many annoying time sinks.

EQ still has its exploitive side (the PLing, uber twinking, overbuffing, 3rd party character/item/plat sales, multi-boxing/botting, overpowered non level-restricted items (like JBB and CHoS) and such) but it's easy to overlook the negative and enjoy the positives of the game. (...and before anyone flames me for not liking the exploitive aspects of EQLive read up on EQ2 and notice that everything I listed is considered a flaw/exploit of EQ design by the EQ2 design team that they're doing away with in EQ2...and I love it...tyvm)
