I am Krup - friend of Grun's.

If you see us together, you can tell us apart quite easily: I have 2 eyes, hair on my head, I stand up straight, and can speak understandably (with a slight accent maybe, but women find it irresistable).

I too frequent Grun's homelands. I speak with Lynuga, but she ignores me - I'm sure she gets lost in my ice-blue, barbarian eyes leaving her speechless. I'm used to that reaction from women. I also say hi to Zimbittle, but get no response. I think Zimbittle's Int is below Grun's, as hard as that might be to believe.

I also speak with the bashers - some of them are uglier than Grun . . . all of them have Grun's manners. No matter how many times I tell them to stop, they try to look up under my Totemic Kilt. They say, "Nice skurt hummie" or "Har, har, why yoo hav on purty dress puny barbie?" Ignorant trolls! Well, I am a barbarian after all, and do not have the refined social skills of my Erudite and human friends, so my response is simply to bash in their skulls.

Sometimes they forget about my kilt, and make fun of my dog instead. They say, "Where yoo git purty poodul?"or "Mmmmm . . . that dawg look gud - speshully as pickul." Believe me when I say I try to be polite and explain that it is a wolf not a poodle, and that you're not supposed to eat pets, but it's like telling a druid not to kite. Instead, I just bash in their skulls.

Then the other day I met a basher, Oggrik was his name I believe, that was a little different than the others. He had 2 blades - one of them was small, like a toothpick. And he seemed very confident. When he said to me, "Hey puny barbie in purty skurt, yoo puppie look like pickul wif legs" I demanded an apology. 6 Malosi resists, 4 root resists, and 2 E-bolt resists later, my puppy ended up a gherkin on the end of Oggrik's toothpick, and I was one scared barbie running through the swamp with my legs chugging and my skirt blowing up in back! No wonder Oggrik kept following me. Long story short: I took a quick peek over in South Ro just to see how things were (things were fine there . . . and safer!).

So now when a basher with 2 blades, one of them a toothpick, makes fun of my kilt or my wolf, I do the right thing: I bash one of his friends' skulls in.
