Kromnik
The worms are an alternative that I will sometimes do with my SK. They're actually pretty good xp for sub 65, since they're lvl 58-59, with the named festering worm being higher. The named drops a decent resist earring from time to time, and surprisingly, I've gotten more ethereal parchments off the worms than the other mobs in PoV.

They hit pretty hard and fast without a slow, however. If you're not slowing them, your monk is taking a HELL of a lot more damage then he needs to. However, you're also going to get aggro when you slow it, and monks don't have taunt, so until your monk gets it back through damage, you're the one that's going to be taking the hits, which can hurt.

These worms were the very reason I started an SK after PoP came out. I tried 2-boxing them with my then 60 warrior. After I slowed, the worm came after me, and I had to take the damage until the warrior could taunt it off (he has a willsapper and the red epic). But, it wasn't fun, and there were a couple deaths that occurred before I could get to the gy.

Slow will sometimes be resisted. However, it rarely will be resisted on the second cast. If you cast malo first, it will always land. Problem again will be the mob is now on you, and the monk will have to beat it off you.

Some people like monks for 2-boxing. However, this is the problem with hard hitting mobs and slow. No class generates snap aggro like an SK. Their snare/dot is a bigger aggro producer than our slow, and usually even bigger than a malo/slow combo.

I'm not telling you to re-roll an sk. However, this is going to be the problem the entire time you try to two-box in PoP. You're going to be the one taking damage after slow. You have 2 choices. Take the damage after the slow and survive until the monk gets aggro back, or not slow, and let your monk fight an unslowed mob that can double for 435, and hits fairly fast. It's going to be a mana drain this way.

You might be able to kite the mob around while the monk beats on it from behind until the mob finally turns on the monk. However, this is not a fun 2-boxing technique. You want to sit on the horse/lizard and regen mana while the melee tanks.

Pre-pop, monks were an excellent choice for two-boxing, because the majority of mobs only hit in the 200 range, and that was easily survivable with reasonble shaman hp/ac equipment. PoP changed all that. If you think the worms are bad. The golems and razorfiends are worse, and double over 500. You have to slow those, or else you or the monk is going to die. These can be rooted, however.

It won't take that much time to level a taunting class to 65 (i did it in 10 days playtime, and that's not any big feat). If you want to do a hardcore PL, you could get a plate class tank to 65 in 5-10 days easily. If you want to stay in teir 2 or higher PoP, I think this has to be an option for you. Another area that is almost as good are the caves in PoJ. Mobs hit for 300 there, and won't beat you up so bad. Just keep your bearings when pulling so you don't run down the wrong tunnel.

I'm sure there are some monk/shaman two-boxers out there that would disagree. This is only my observation (I also have a 55 monk), and suggestion.

Grixx
You could also try PoN Gargoyles. They are on the far side of the river, so you'll need to levitate over and carefully (emphasis on carefully) make your way over to them. Once you reach them, there's a large grassy safe area between the gargoyle "fort" wall and the zone wall where you can sit and med all day long with no possibility of roamers or agro. Seriously, you could sit there for hours AFK safely. Once you're there with both toons, it's pretty straight forward. Gargoyles from the top of the wall are single pulls (at least they are for me). Malo, Slow, and sik the dog on them. The gargoyles hit for around 387 max, so it's a little easier than PoV. I 2-box with a shaman/SK, so I can't speak to the tanking ability of a monk on these - but they'd have to be easier than worms or golems. I usually net about 3% AA per kill on these when 2-boxing (for both characters), which I don't think is bad at all. The BEST thing about this spot? Not a soul ever comes here. It's never camped. Drops are sparse, but you'll occasionally get a Raw Diamond or Blue Diamond or Flakes of Gold (sellable for ~11pp I think?). It's a good quiet spot to grind out some AA.
