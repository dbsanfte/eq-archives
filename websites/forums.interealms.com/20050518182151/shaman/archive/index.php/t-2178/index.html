My take on the situation with shamans in the end game:

1) We need a better heal. Torpor is fantastic, but is limited to group only. That needs to change. But outside of group only, I can't think of a single time that I or another shaman has EVER been in the main tank group when we're doing AoW, Yelinak, Dain, NToV, Itraer Vius, Rhag 1 / 2, Rumblecrush, Ancient Burrower, etc. Not to mention the fact that if I was in the main tank group, torpor slows the ATK speed of the person you're healing, which does not help the main tank build agro well. My suggestion would be a level 60 quick heal spell, like an upgraded Chloroblast. Make it heal for 700 - 900 ish, and put us on a level similar to druids.

3) Pet /hold, in my opinion, is something that all pet classes should have had all along, and not have to spend 5 points to gain. I only ever use my pet when soloing, and not even that much then. Magician epic pet puts ours to shame, and necro pets are pretty high up there too. My wife plays a shaman (which I hijack and play on occasion). To be honest, I cannot think of a class that is more well rounded AND has the level of power that shamans do. Shamans rock.

My wife and I usually duo. We can do things consistently that other groups of 3 and 4 don't have the balls to try. If she played any other class, our power would drop dramatically. The ONLY exception to this is against mobs that cannot be slowed, cannot be stunned, and have high damage output. In that small scenario, a cleric would be better. For general fighting? She owns a cleric and/or druid in usefullness so much it is pathetic. Once she hits 60 and gets malo and torpor (wish us luck!) the disparity will be even larger.

I absolutely love the fact that my wife plays a shaman, if you haven't noticed.

As far as a better heal for shamans, I disagree. Shamans are already far superior to clerics in xp groups as it is. The comment that 5 shamans spamming heals on a MT fighting the AoW is missing the point. Those heals aren't there to replace hp during an average round, CH does that. Those heals are there simply to compensate for the high damage spikes that occur and to keep the tank alive for the next CH to land. Is that usefull? **** yes.

The AoW can one round any non defensive MT in the game. 8 hits for 1154 damage will kill anyone. 2 rounds of 8 hits for 756 damage(max defensive damage) will kill any tank in the game. The only thing that will keep that tank alive is spot heals.

If you have 10 spot healers (druids/shamans doesn't matter) all repeatedly casting spot heals, those high damage spikes will have a much smaller chance of killing the MT. It is also a nice way to spread healing aggro around during the start of a CH chain.

Saying that chloroblast or sup heal doesn't cut it in those situations because it is too slow casting/doesn't heal enough is like saying that CH doesn't cut it because it takes too long to cast. Those spot heals don't cut it, not by themselves. But a single cleric casting CH doesn't cut it either. There is a reason those mobs have the damage output that they do.
