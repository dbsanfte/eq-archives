Subtitles: What are Rangers Anyway? or Why Be a Ranger? OR Will Giving Rangers Self Port Ruin EQ?

Anytime changes are made to the way classes play in EQ some are happy, some indifferent, and some disappointed (and worse). Nevertheless changes continue to take place because they should. The argument that "I made it without this ability so we don't need it" is an interesting one and a great subject for someone's couch debate with another type of professional... just kidding. But after playing only one character class through level 59 I have to admit that I am forever wondering about the bland mix of ranger abilities. Abilities that are more embarassements than assists.... disarm comes immediately to mind as does hide and sneak (to a lessor extent as although terribly poor they do have their uses).... spells that come so late that they are essentially useless... In effect rangers can easly appear to be a hybrid of weak compromises with a hint of areas where we should shine but barely and rarely do.

It is my opinion that rangers have inate abilites that we must seek to exploit.... scouting, stealth, movement, range attack (archery, and certain small throwing weapons), and self preservation.

So why desire Self Porting? I believe enhancement to solo, long range movement is essential (whether done with porting or special evac from zone in to zone in....) to creating the special unique ability mix that would make a ranger more of a 'ranger.'

Rangers should be the scouts of EQ... when an individual, group or guild needs to know if someone or something is somewhere they 'hire' a ranger (hire is a figurative term here... mostly). Self porting or evacing throughout outdoors zones could be obtained as either a progression similar to druids getting their ability to port others or as a special category of AA enhancements. With self port/evacing rangers become more valued for certain functions... just like other classes have fill special needs.

In addition ot enhancing our ability to move quickly to different zones, to further make our mark as a scout we need an improvement to our sneak, hide abilites, (up the 75 to 125 or even 150), better self healing improvements (bandaging/medicines mostly), add detect 'natural' traps/poisons, add lastely improve our tracking to add specific targeting searching abilities. Really revamp the ranger so we can better exist solo and be real scouts. And lastly fix Superior Camo!

Now as you have probabaly deduced by now, I think see the self porting for ranger issue as just the tip of the iceberg. Rangers not only need to be able to get somewhere in order to scout they need to be able to survive once they get there.... and I don't mean with AC or godlike damage output, although rangers should be able to put up a nasty fight when they have to.

Heavy armor with lots of AC just doesn't fit with the plan. Rangers that scout certainly don't need more than what we have. I think rangers should stay light and dangerous (but not aspire to become tanks)... and we should have the ability to blend into the surroundings much better and to excel in distance attack with bow, dagger, axe.

Class balancing issues? Our curent spell book is a mixed bag just as we are. I think our spells should be mostly limited to self (and some group) protection, fear, confusion, observation, entrapement/hinderance to mobs (more than just animals), self mobility, stealth, short duration offense, first aid (for ourselves and others). I believe we don't need to have DOT or direct damage style spells.... just doesn't fit. On the other hand we should be able to use magically or chemically enhanced range weapons that can act as delivery devices for Necor, Mage, Wizard, Cleric (etc) enchanted ammo). Double wield, double attack yes but with slash, piercing, and range weapons. Why would a scout be good at bashing.... 2hb in particular? 2hs slashing? Heavy weapons in general? Let Warriors and monks be the masters of weaponry in general.

Let us get there fast, quiet, secretly.... move about... protect ourselves.... see danger... confuse the mobs....and kill well when necessary.

OK, time to Flame me. :).
