Top guilds had to merge to keep raiding.

The guild that merged with another was the #2 raiding force on the server. The top force would have been around a lvl 2 raiding org or so compared to live progression. The top org has always had a mash of different guilds, nothing new there at all.

Progression stopped for test at the time of GoD beta. We put raiding on hold to test....dropping the server back some. Then the changes that were made to the server made raids on time stop, literally stop dead. Pleading and begging with dev to fix the bugs or atleast acknowledge them fell on deaf ears. Our members got annoyed and some of the best players and best geared quit...to watch paint dry or some other worth while persuit. Our members tried to hammer away at god, and made progression...quick deep progression. But the bugs that plauged the server, and the frustration of watching things that have been reported over and over again go live drove most away. Atleast most of the hardcore players. There are still a few around.

It's difficult to imagin the frustration if you're a player on live, just as it's difficult for the test player to comprehend the "race" to spawns. For me personally, the biggest annoyance was reporting bugs, making sure dev knew about them...talking personally with them and asking what they needed or what we can do to help them address it only to see it go live and be blamed for inadiquate testing not only by folks on live but by development.

The relationship in the past with the development staff was top notch, we'd run any testing they asked for or needed. Forgo our raids and drop whatever to test for them, and they would do their best to keep us on even ground with the live servers as far as chance for mobs and things like that. We never asked for more than that, although at times it was offered...we declined. We simply had a desire for our "shot" as it were at the mobs that server bounces ate. Our perk was lower population, theirs was unpayed testers that have played in most cases since the inception of this game with each other. When a couple of very key members left the dev team, that relationship seemed to go away...for whatever reason. There are still a couple with dev and QA that utilized the org but by and large they are now the exception instead of the rule they once were.

It's disappointing to see what the server has become, and the quality of "tested" content now being pushed live. Granted your top guilds on live always ran into problems with encounters and things that test didn't find, but by and large in the past it seems like things got tested better than they do now. For whatever that's worth. The bugs are being reported, but there needs to be someone reading them.

I don't understand what soe was thinking, it's not like reporting the bugs was a selfish act...eventually, they all got pushed live. In the end, they lost unpaid testers 90% of which enjoyed what they considered their responsibility.
