Grippa
One method I've used for fear raids in the past, is to gather together 2 elite groups (what "elite" means is up to you!) and ask them to turn up an hour early.

Trying to break the plane with 36 people can be more trouble than it's worth, unless you are very organised. So take in the 2 groups, made up of people you trust to know what they're doing, and just kill the roaming mobs near the zone in.

You can either find a good enchanter to mezz the adds as they come to you, or the ghetto tactic is to give each group a porter, and when you're going to get overwhelmed with mobs, evac to the Feerott and start again. As long as you kill at least one mob per trip to the plane, you'll eventually clear the wnaderers. I think there are about 12 of them around the zone in area.
Csin Csity
You are going to have a VERY hard time with the break-in. Looking at your level, and assuming that 51+ means mostly <60 you are going to have to be very patient, and very conservative.

Grippa has the right idea, get your core peeps up there early and schedule enough time for a break-in wipe. Have a good friend hang out and kill cyndi while organizing groups of those who arrive for the main raid.

Raid-channel spam the plan for a west wall break-in so nobody wanders off and trains the rest of the raid. This is often the end of a Fear raid.

I'm sure things are easier now with better equipment, people with nice AA's, and the possibility of 60-65 level help, but don't get complacent. Make some Kilva's potions for tanks, make a stack of insta-heal potions for everyone if you can afford it (at least keep some in your pocket).

One thing I remember about fear is wierd resists for slow (it has been a while). Make sure everyone, particularly in the break-in group is prepared to do whatever crowd control their class can contribute. Bards and Necros come to mind as helpful and underappreciated help in this area.

Good luck..took me 8 trips to Fear to finally get my
Tear.

Last time I hosted fear we had 6-7 groups, when making groups pick 2-3 groups as break in groups. These will be stronger than the others, they should have 3-5 enchanters and a few clerics. It's a good thing to have the MT in this group, and either a) go with them, or b) send someone in that knows what they are doing, is trustworthy, has been to fear before, and can type while under pressure.

During the break in those 2-3 groups zone in first alone, they had west to the wall, be in communication with these groups. Quickly find out if many didn't make it, find out if the mobs in camp are quieted down ( mnezzed ). If the break in group is safe ( won't wipe on how many mobs are in camp ) figure out based on how many mobs are in their camp if the rest should zone in or not. if there is only a few mobs in camp and chanters have lots of mana, send the rest in to finish securing the camp. Otherwise let the break in team finish off the mobs and get ready for the mobs you will be bringing with you. After that start pulling ( use a pull group in fear, have pullers pull to group outside of camp. clr brd enc enc rng shm works well in pull if you have the clr / shm / enc to go around.

We created a channel that the pull group MT and ST were in. Our ST pulled from the pull camp to Main camp, use this channel to keep in communication when the ST can grab one from camp, when the pull group should pull more. KILL.

Remind EVERYONE if they get aggro outside of camp EVER just die and get a loc. I don't care if its only one mob. get loc and die.

Hope this helps anyone else doing the tear ( stupid golems are never up when I plan my raids ... GRRRRR )
