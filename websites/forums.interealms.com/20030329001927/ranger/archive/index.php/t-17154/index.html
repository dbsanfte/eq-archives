Lauralei
Just some helpful hints for when you head to Steamfont Mountains for the micro servos...

In Lesser Faydark on your way to Steamfont stick to the wall on your left. Don't go wandering around or you will be brownie fodder. It's no fun trying to find your corpse in there.

Watch out for the Kobold Missionary in Steamfont. He spawns in the area around the giant cog in the ground. A safe bet is to stick to the area closer to the windmills before the mino caves (you will see what I am talking about once you get there).

This is a great quest for the young ranger, and it is quite satisfying once you finish it. Best of luck.

But I guess if you don't want to take the time to run around collecting the parts needed and making stacks and stacks of arrows to riase your Fletching skill, go blow 200pp on it in the Bazaar.

At level 17, it's time to leave GFay. It's a big, big world out there, with lots of new and exciting things to do and see. Highpass Hold would be a great spot for you to call home for a while, and when you outgrow that a few levels later, step on in to Highhold Keep or head on over to Paludal Caverns.

Another hint on collecting the micro Servos: use Tracking to track down the Clockwork spiders, but be aware that there are mobs with similar name like "a rogue clockwork" and "a runaway clockwork." These are NOT spiders. The ones you want are called "runaway clockwork" and "rogue clockwork." And do watch out for the Kobold Missionary. He spawns in the little valley that the Minotaur Caves are in just past the windmills. He's a caster, so if you find yourself in a confrontation with him, DO NOT try to root and shoot him. I have removed him from my "payback" list many times but for some reason I just can't seem to take him off permanently. Same goes for that damned Cargo Clockwork, which by the way still conned dark blue to me at 41 so DO NOT mistakenly hit it thinking it's just another rogue clockwork like I did.
