OK I think everyone is reading too much into this.

First off, a Bard is going to be much more successful pulling the non-summoning mobs than anyone else. Why? because Fade allows them to skip mobs and pull from as deep as they wish. Other classes are limited to whats right there. You can't really skip the summoners and the yellows unless your a bard, I think.

Secondly, I have noticed that the mobs here break root and VP regularly. Although I have not tried to Mal Decay + VP yet, though. This creates a problem for shaman. Personally, I hate having to re-root, and I will avoid ANY mobs that break VP early for soloing.

Thirdly, I am really not trying to make a statement against bards, or whatever. I am using this exoample to highlight the difficulty of gauging content difficulty in todays EQ. As I said in an earlier post, if you make things "easier" for the less equipped people, you are merely trivializing it for the upper half. This applies to a lot of things. I did an LDoN Hard with one of these bards pulling. Collect 26, took us 38 minutes, and we never left the ZI room. Cleric, 12K tank, druid, ranger, me. Hardly uber DPS or anything. But we are all decently geared and he is an amazing puller.

People like Iz, Snoww, BlargX, are right. Its impossible to create 1 set of content for all levels of EQ players anymore. The gap is too wide. A less equipped, less AAed Bard (note I didnt say less skilled, I do not see people who play less or dont raid as "less" skilled) probably wont be able to do the Vxed thing with any efficiency. Didnt seem like a big deal to these guys though. I guess thats the point I am trying to make here.
