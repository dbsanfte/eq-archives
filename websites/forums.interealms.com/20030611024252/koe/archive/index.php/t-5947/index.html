Klydon
Probably one of the very few games I have not at least tried and probably never will..

It looks like a bit of fun, but it is very, very expensive (too expensive IMHO) and most of the battles I have observed come down to pushing stuff into the middle and hoping for the best. I am afraid I would spend too much time tweaking the rules trying to fix some of the problems I see (biggest of which the field of play rules compared to the size of the army, with their being too many units in too small of a space and thus not much of a chance to manuver much).

Depending on your budget, the piece of advice I would offer is if you are on a tighter budget, go with a higher point density/higher skill army. This should give you better troops than what the other guy has and you should be able to bring greater pressure to bear at a given point. This also has the added benefit in that since you have fewer pieces, it is cheaper on the pocket book. Part of this is dependant on what you feel comfortable with.. some like to try to swarm the other person under while others feel more comfortable dealing with those swarms with quality.

Now if you got into Battletech, that would be another matter.. ;)

I used to play Fantasy back when I lived in Albany (this is some years ago) and I had a pretty impressive Chaos army built up. (I could easily field 10,000 pts. worth of troops if I needed to.) I believe they've once again rewritten the rules for a new edition of the game, and it's one of the things I've grown increasingly bothered with by GamesWorkshop -- they typically put a lot of the higher priced models into the obsolence box to force you to buy more with each new edition.

If you're looking to keep expenses down, I'd suggest Chaos, since the standard foot soldier is about 34 points and the standard chaos cavalry soldier is about 66 points. Their character models can run from 150 to 500+. It doesn't take long to get to 1500 points.
