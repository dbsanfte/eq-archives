Wyun
Did these with a group last night for 4 hours.

53 shammy, 58 cleric, 60 monk (me), 60 bard, 60 druid, 60 druid.

Harmony is an absolute must unless you want the monk to do lengthy/dangereous splits.

They look like skeletons, but are not classified as undead.. Sorry clerics/pallies. They hate the local frogs and they fight each other to the death. Be carefull when pulling them, as if they are fighting a frog and you pull, then they ignore you and kill the frog. If you then get a pop, then you end up with them all charging once the frog is dead.

They come in 3 shapes. A warrior type, and 2 necro types. The necro types have Damage Shields. The Necro types will summon pets.

They were not particularly hard, and IF the shaman got a slow in (which was only 50/50), then I could tank and only take one bub of damage.

If no slow, I usually needed one CH.

One time on a particularly bad pull we ended up with 4 at the same time.. Cleric had to burn all his mana, but we killed them all with no deaths.

The named we encountered were a necro and a SK. Both 60th level. Usual stuff. Fear/Life Taps. The SK Harm Touches for 1K.

The Necro was not too hard, hitting for 180 ish. Took a couple of CH's, and dropped.. a rubbish caster staff.

The SK was damn hard. Hits for 270 and fast. Twice got very very close and had to burn MEND once and Stonestance once. Slow would not land at all. Dropped.. a rubbish sword and shield. Id suggest a tank with defensive.

The 2 named are on long pop timers.. I would guess a couple of hours. Our one group could clear the whole camp, and have a minute to spare between each pull.

We cleared the camp for ~4 hours, and had 4 rings drop. About one per hour.

As an aside, Iksar start off none KOS (in fact, Amiable). This goes down as you kill. I finished off indifferent after the 4 hours.

Our camp spot was near the ruins beside the lake. Zero frog adds.
