Valsuvious
Well, it's hard to justify one being better than the other. Each type will reduce the overall dmg taken by each AE/dot. So, if you had 5% spell shield and got hit by say a 1000hp direct dmg, and it hit you for full (meaning you didn't resist it at all), you would get hit for 950hps. If you got hit for a dot which takes away 1000hp per tick, and you had 5% dot shielding, then every tick you would lose 950hp. The max effectiveness on any one of these is 35, so anything over that and you will not gain any additional benefits.

Personally, I found it easier to get spell shield, and if you are doing stuff say pre-mpg/anguish then I would suggest that you maybe worry about more spell shield than dot shielding. I think that omens and maybe even the later part of GoD Dot shielding started being real useful as you started getting hit with more and more dots and they started becoming big factors. In placs like, the Adaption trial when you have the Fire dot on you you can really tell the people who do not have any dot shielding, as opposed to those who do.

Also, not sure how your guilds loot system is, but I've managed to acquire a few items that while I won't wear them, they are pretty decent spell shield/dot shielding/shielding gear that I switch out when I think it's best appropriate to.

There isn't a single focus in PoTime that wouldn't have some effect on level 70 spells and the vast majority of them are better than OoW focus's pre MPG trials or anguish. The key is that they lose 5 or 10% of thier effect not 5% or 10% total.

AKA a 30% spell extension focus that looses 10% per level working on a spell 1 level above it's cap would loose 30% * 10% or 3%. Which would mean it's 27% extension. 3 levels above the cap would be 30% * 30% or 9% so would be 21% extension. It takes either 10 levels or 20 levels above cap before the focus is totally useless depending on if it's 5 or 10% per level.
