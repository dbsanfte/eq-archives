Juujuu
Here's my analysis of WoW:

I've played a Tauren shaman up to level 15.
I've done so by doing nothing but quests. I haven't gone "grinding" xp or anything once. Ya, I have to semi-grind when I have a quest to get, say, 5 of this and 5 of that kind of feather from a bunch of harpies in an area. But it's kind of neat dodging the mobs you don't want so you can hit the ones you do want and hoping your quest item will drop. That's about the closest I've really done to grinding xp at all.

WoW features:
-fast levelling. compared to eq, you rocket up in levels. Coming from years of EQ playing, I know how to play mmorpgs somewhat well now. It's taken me fairly little time to level my character up to 15. I shot up through the level to get to 12, but from that point, it's begun to slow down since I've got access to a whole new area I can hunt in now and I'm looking around for quest givers and for the quest npcs I have to kill. You don't even notice the levelling at all. I just hope that everyone doesn't shoot up to 50 or 60 right away and get bored of it with potentially not much to do at high level.

-loads of worthy quests. every quest out there is worth doing. why? it forces you to see mor parts of the world you might not otherwise see. I think exploration is fun. You also get good item rewards for doing quests. The majority of my gear is stuff I've gotten from killing the mobs I need for my quests, trh8skill stuff i've made, or direct results of quests. You also get hefty chunks of experience doing quests. some quests are gimpy in xp reward and give a bit of cash, but they are the easy quests. The more involved ones like finding people out in the wilderness, dodging mobs all along the way, and then having to do a few tasks for them for the final reward, gives a very handsome type of reward. I'd say about 1/5 of *all* my earned xp or maybe more is directly the result of doing quests. A lot of cash is generated too.

-trh8skill system that doesn't suck. i don't mind doing trh8skills in this game. in fact it's kinda fun. i'm making armor i actually use. it's not costing me money to skill up my leatherworking. you can't ever fail a combine. you just have more/less/zero chance of gaining a skillup on making a particular item based on your skill level. that's sweet. no more fear of losing your leet item you found when trying to make a good piece of gear (as opposed to say failing archery combines to make ele plane bows from POP). I wish I could learn all the trh8skills at the same time, but alas, you can only learn like 2.

-nice seamless landscape. the landscape is beautifl. it's one huge world without zones. well, there are zones, but you don't "loading..please wait" or anyting. it just says on your screen what new area you are in. finding new areas on the map itself gives you xp bonuses to encourage exploration. you can't see places on the map in any detail until you've been there once. from the starting town whereever you are, you can start running and keep running and running and running and you pass by changing landscapes until you get to wherever you are wanting to end up. You see that mountain waaaaaaaay off in the distance? Well, if you start running straight at it, you'll end up at the base of it in about 3 minutes of running and can see the mobs at the base of it, and you can see the mountain detail slowly coming into focus as it would in real life. Sweet. Taking a "public transporation" griffon or whatever lets you see this very very very well. you take off from one city and head off to another for a small fee. You fly overtop of the landscape very quickly in real time, and you can see the mobs and players below as they pass by and you fly on to the next city.

-downtime. what downtime? actually, there is some downtime, but not much. you get into a fight, you kill the mobs. even if you are at like zero health and mana, you can completely recover on your own in about 30-60 seconds. If you are impatient you can carry food and water with you to eat/drink your hp/mana back, but I never bother. It's nice that when combat is done, you can usually recouperate from the fight in the process of running from wherever you are to your next fight. personally, i actually wish there was a small bit more downtime, so as to make it more important to wisely spend your mana, and make recovering with food and water actually needed or whatever.

-combat speed. or should I say combat speed. I don't like this. combat is over too fast. here's a typical fight: pull mob with nuke. drop my instant fire totem to nuke the mob for me. drop my instant totem to increase my strength or reduce my damage taken or to snare the mob. melee the mob for about 20 seconds. blast the mob with another nuke to kill it or make it flee. reapply lightning shield. loot up and carry on to next fight. total combat length is about 30 seconds per mob if you are fighting even cons. I don't like this. I wish the fights went longer. The spells you cast in combat should matter. It shouldn't be all about the nuke as fast as you can to kill the mob before it kills you. if you thought EQ sucked since GoD came out for fast kills, you have nothing to complain about until you've played WoW. This is my biggest beef. I have a big variety of spells I can cast during combats, but I barely use any. Mobs die so fast when you are solo, that you cast about 4 spells per combat and that's it. If you are grouping, well it's just rediculous how fast you cruise through mobs. If you thought that you were going fast through mobs before, just wait until you group with 2 or 3 poeple for example, or possibly more. When we had 3 poeple in 1 group, we were killing so fast, that if you have 1 person master looting, that person won't be able to keep up with all the corpses while the other 2 do the killing. That's sad. The HPs/mana pool of the players and mobs should be doubled, and the xp doubled per kill, so each fight is much more meaningful (with quests modified as such). The mobs can also kill you way too fast as well. If you are fighting a mob, and you get jumped, you're screwed a lot of the time. You might be able to run away at times, but it's rough. 2 even cons you can probably kill. If you get jumped by 2 mobs that are 1 level higher than you, they'll kill you before you have a chance to react. This somewhat encourages grouping, but for the most part grouping just slows down your xp. The only time it's worthy to group is if you are doing a quest and you need help to clear a camp or something of a bunch of mobs since you both get credit for the quest rewards and drops. If you are familiar with how fast you go through mobs in diablo, you kinda get the same feeling in WoW. Pull mob, cast a couple spells, hope you didn't die, loot, carry on with no downtime. It's the main negative thing I've found about the game so far.

-friends. I want some people I know that I can regularly play with. poeple from my EQ guild playing wow are scattered all over different test servers. I want a core group of people I can group with and trade trh8skill items with them to save money and make money for fun stuff like mounts and whatnot. I'm becoming a decent leatherworker, skinning my own animals I kill, but I had to pay a lot for my weapon from a vendor cuz i don't see anyone auctioning weapons, and I don't know any blacksmiths. I've love some emergency use potions because they've saved my life a bunch of times now. etc etc. Pool your cash for collective goals. Unfortunately I don't see very much unison among players. 5 times total in 15 levels has anyone wanted me to group with them. I've been grouping in total for about 1 level. You could be playing WoW solo on your own computer, and you'd get pretty much the same experience I've had so far, unfortunately.

When WoW goes live, I'll be buying a copy for sure. I know it won't take me away from EQ1. I'll be playing it on the side, since you can advance so much for so little time invested. Doing quests is FUN. If you factor in all the races you can play, and all the terratory you can explore with each race and quests you can do total, there's a LOT you can do that will keep you occupied for a long time, which is what I think Blizzard wants you to do to get the most out of the game. Plus there's raiding, but I have no experience in that at all yet.

That's my $0.02 worth.

I, like many others, am enjoying WoW. For starters, I decided play the class I foung least desirable so that I could review it throughly. For me , that decision was to play a Druid. I am very pleased with how they have represented the class. It is nothing, repeat, nothing like what a Druid would represent in EQ. Heck, even the lore behind Druids in WoW is much much more respectable. I find myself liking the class for it's own merit, I suspect I'll enjoy my retail Shaman just as much.

As for the game, I really can't say where it lacks or could be better. The game isn't perfect, but it isn't built on the rigid philosophy EQ perpetuated. In fact, for those that don't see a future for the game, think about this: Why do you want to be indefinitely satisfied with one aspect of a game, forever? Wow lends itself to allowing the player to start another character and be happy about it, especially if it's another race/class combination. You can play a character to the endgame and move on to the next fun experience waiting for you in another class.

What I really love about this game though, is the options avialable to the player. You have a choice of what you wish to tackle, and how you go about tackling it. When I log on at night, I could progress in any of the numerous quest in my log book, for the most part solo if I choose unless I'm trying to finish an elite quest. Or I can get some experience roaming around to new areas, most likely opening up new quests if around my level. I could roam around and grind in an area on the look out for tradeskill supplies, for me I am a Smith and I look for Ore mines to plunder. Because I'm on a PvP server, I can go hunt some of the opposing faction if it tickles my fancy, or maybe I can just straight up grind xp, with a good chance of finding nice loot to sell/upgrade. I don't feel, at any point, that I'm required to do anything. Everything is optional. My choice. In my opinion, that is extremily satisfying and lends itself more to the roleplaying/fantasy elements that EQ lost so long ago.

So far I've played WoW beta and EQ2 retail, and the decision, for me, is to play WoW because it is all around a more satisfying, complete gaming experience. If you're asking for more then that, then you're looking in the wrong places.

Not a flame, just a long-winded opinion :D

I feel your a lil confised about some aspects of BT. There is no upload limit on BT unless your using a client that has that specificly built into it. Infact the idea of limiting the upload is against the idea of bit torrent to begin with. The way the system works is that the more people that are on, the faster it goes. More people is more uploading power and its easier to download and share files this way. This system is far superior to the current server to client system that all MMORPG's have in use due to the number of people that will be downloading patches when they become available. With a server to client design the bandwidth is limited and the increasing number of clients will result in a split of the pipe available and in turn each person suffers when someone new comes on to download. This means that more servers are required and more bandwidth is spent to kepe up with the increasing server loads.

With Bit Torrent, as i said, the increasing load to the servers are effectivly nullified cause the load is not on the server, but on the network which is self correcting cause the more load and the more people downloading, the more bandwidth there is to support the influx of clients. Everyone shares and helps each other along. Now this system does begin to break down once the clients base depletes and there is less people to share with and at that point i believe blizzard needs ot move it to a server to client setup and eat the bandwidth, but for inital patch download there is not a better design ATM.

One thing i will enlighten you to about BT is that you have to let it sit for a good 5-10 min for it get full steam as it creates its connection bases and modifies them on the fly to connect to new available connections. But once the connections are established i find that BT is a vastly superior method of file transfer. Try it next time. Dont give up and say it sucks, let it run for a while and build steam.
