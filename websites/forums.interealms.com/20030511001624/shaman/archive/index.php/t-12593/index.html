1) Our DPS is limited to our DD's / AE's, DoT's are useless when mobs live for about 15 seconds.

2) Slow mitigation makes us pretty much useless on anything except the Executioner himself. Also, once again, mobs live for 15 seconds or so.

3) Our heals, while nice, are no match for a cleric or druid.

4) Our exceptional mana regen abilities are pretty much negated by the short duration of the entire fight - I think it was around 11 minutes from spawn of first wave to death of the Executioner.

DS potions do 30 damage on a level 60 tank. 1 potion will last the duration of the entire trial. a mob bashing on a tank for 1 minute with 30 damage DS will be half dead by the time you get to it. That's half the work done using a DS potion.

Now a shaman a fantastic asset in a trial. There is more than one way to get through a trial as a shaman. You can slow. But in my opinion this is lost DPS as we use Mage/Druid DS as well as DS potion on the tank.

The mobs hit very fast but not very hard, perfect for DS. It's 4 mobs beating on the MA which makes healing tough, but 3 mobs on the MA and the healing becomes much easier.

My guild always takes a shaman into the trial as we look after 1 mob for each round. Then the others take care of the other 3. The shaman pulls 1 mob off the MA with slow and Bane/Epic and then let dog dog beat on the mob while the shaman tanks it. When the others are finished with the other 3 your mob is pretty much dead. Bane + Epic + Pet kills a mob in under about minute.

Our typical group is SK/Cleric/Mage/Rogue/Shaman/AN_other. Where AN_Other is the new trialist.

Also note the rocks must go down first. They are enchanters and tash and slow. If your melee DPS gets slowed they game over. Druid or Magician banishment kills the rocks outright, so no xp or loot, but gets the mob out of the way fast. (Remember to re-imburse the druid/mage for the star ruby's)

Hope this gives you an idea or different tactic to try.
