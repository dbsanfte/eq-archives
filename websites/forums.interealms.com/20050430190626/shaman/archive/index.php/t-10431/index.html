I am quite comfortable with my control (slow, VP, root) , buff and healing capabilities.
My biggest issue nowadays is the pathetic damage output in the Highend game (PoP) in comparison to every other class.

Normally a one-group-situation encounter in PoP goes like this:
Mob is pulled, Shaman cast Malo, Shaman cast Slow.
While the tanks and nukers finish the mob i have the following options:
- spot healing
- dot mob with Spear of Fate
- dot mob with poison
- dot mob with disease
- damage mob with AE
- nuke mob cold/poison

Spot healing is rarely needed because normally there is always a Main Healer in the group

Spear dot wont do full damage because most of the time the mob is dead before the last and biggest damage-tick of the dot resolves

Disease dots may do big damage in theory but they take too much time until they do full damage; as with Spear dot the mob normally is dead before the spell resolves.

Poison dots are imbalanced at the moment: There is
- Bane of Nife (1698 dmg);
- Ancient Scourge of Nife (1955 dmg); only available to Uberguild-Shaman
- Blood of Saryrn (2240 dmg); L 65 spell, so only available to Uberguild-Shaman

So 99 % of the Shamans only have L 56 Bane of Nife as poison dot; its big drawback is the high aggro you gain (thats because of poison counters)
Dmg/Mana Ratio: 3.99

Nukes:
L 62 Spear of Torment 675 dmg for 275 mana; ratio 2.45
L 64 Velium Strike 925 dmg for 300 mana; 3.08

Damage is not that bad but also misses a real "punch"; every other caster or priest class has better nukes. Also the casting and recasting times are quite high. (7.8 secs for Velium strike, ugh)

This leaves the AEs:
L 55 Torrent of poison 2x2 or 3x1 rains with 540 dmg each; ratio 4.26 (3 hits); 5.68 (4 hits)
L 64 Tears or Saryrn 2x2 or 3x1 rains for 650 dmg each; ratio 4.33 (3 hits); 5.77 (4 hits)

Small problems with the AE:
- It can damage yoursefl if you are not careful
- its aera is location bound not mob bound, so if mob moves or flees it can miss hits

BIG issues with the AEs:

- RESISTS! Very often one or all rains are fully resisted. Even with Malo on the mob. No other Shaman damage spell is resisted that much.

- You cannot kill mobs with the shaman AEs. I am very sure that there is a piece of code which prevents mobs from taking damage from the AE rains when they are below a certain amount/percentage of HPs.

Ways to improve AEs:
- Get rid of that AE-rain-wont-dmg-wounded-mob feature
- Add ways to lower the resists either by changing the spell itself or by introducing an according AA skill or by new focus items/effects.
