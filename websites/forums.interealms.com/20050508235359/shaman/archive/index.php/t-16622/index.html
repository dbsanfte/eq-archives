Given:
1) Three mezzes total 600 mana from the chanter.
2) Three mobs, each dealing equivalent damage in the amount of time it would take your group to kill it. Let this damage be known as X
3) Each melee, when tanking, will maintain aggro lock. Possibly unrealistic, but it simplifies the equation, and provides a bigger advantage to the off-tanking scenario than to the mezing scenario (do you want one mob rampaging around uncontrolled or three?)

Now, suppose you mez two of the three mobs, your tank will take roughly 3*X in damage. You cleric heals 3*X damage, and your chanter spends 600 mana to cast three mezzes.

If, on the other hand, you decide to offtank each of the other mobs, the time required to kill each mob is tripled (one third of the group damaging each one), thus the mob's total damage output is also tripled. Each mob now does 3*X damage, for a total of 9*X damage dealt over the course of the fight.

Thus the cleric has to heal 9*X damage, or three times the damage that he would have to if the chanter had mezzed.
Allow Y to be the cleric's cost to heal X damage.


In order for the total mana expenditure to be the same, 3*Y + 600 must equal 9*Y.
Thus Y = 100. As long as your cleric spends less than 100 mana per mob on your tank (one CH every 4 mobs), it's efficient to offtank.

There's one circumstance (the only one I can think of) where mashing the taunt button works well as a Warrior. If you have a Beastlord pet using a high aggro proc, three or four procs will put the pet at the top of the hate list by several hundred points. (Since pets can't keep aggro if a player is in range, this isn't noticeable as a tank.) At that point, taunting even though the mob is still aggroed on you will add considerably to your hate level.

(Of course if the Beastlord pet stays on the mob through the whole fight there's no real need to hit taunt anyway. The mob won't be going anywhere and will stay aggroed on you.)
