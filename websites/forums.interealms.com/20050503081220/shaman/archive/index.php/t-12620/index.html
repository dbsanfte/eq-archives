Jinheim
As you are leveling up you want to have both decent HP and decent WIS. Many people on this board will tell you that wis is worthless, but they are wrong. When the crap hits the fan, you are going to want to have a reasonable mana pool. Cannibalizing takes time. If you don't have time to canni and you go oom, you are going to wish you had a bigger mana pool. Do not neglect one for the other or you will gimp yourself in certain situations. Strike a balance, and you will do just fine.

This is a long way in your future, but once you have canni5, wis and mana can take a backseat to HP and FT. Your ultimate goal should be to have enough HP to canni whenever you need to (2500 unbuffed is fine, 3500 is more than enough), and have as much FT as you possibly can, to reduce the amount of time you have to spend casting canni4 (FT15 is the cap, but MC3 and other effects, like lunar whispers, still count).

As for AC... I stopped caring about it a long time ago. I don't even consider it when picking my gear. I used to be an AC freak, with about 1150 AC and 130 wis. I have since adjusted myself for a bigger mana pool and more HP, and have no regrets. I've never seen anything to convince me that AC really make a difference for us at high levels. It is nice to have and all, but not worth sacrificing in other areas. Also, I can't testify to its usefullness at lower levels. It has just been too damn long, and I never really took notice of my AC and its effects as I was leveling up. Some have said that AC is more important for a low-level shamans, but I don't really buy it.

Good luck.

Radag
I think a lot of the advice you are getting is very bad for a lower level shaman.

It is reasonbly good advice for a high level shaman but all the HP & WIS advice is just not so great for your level.

This has been said more then once, but the subject is not realy that hard.

You have too resourses to use as you play your shaman.

HP and Mana.

How efficent a shaman you are depends only on what uses ore replenishes these resourses.

HP gets used up when you Cannibalize or you get Hit by mele or spells. It gets replenished by a number of things like sit / stand regen rate, regen items and spells and healing spells.

Mana gets used up as you cast spells and replenished by Canibalize, Flowing thaught items, mana regen spells like KEI.

How big your HP or MANA pool is has ZERO impact on how efficent a shaman you are. The SIZE of your HP and MANA pool does have a big impact on your safty margin. At the higher levels, some special consideratoins come into play primarily because of the AA skill Can V and the Torpor spell and also because the mobs start to hit so hard that you must have a fairly large HP pool for a usable safty margin. At lower levels these factors are not present.

AC is very nice all the way into your 50s because it mitigates damage ( HP resource ).

Also very nice items are things like the Cerimonial Iksar Chestplate that directly impove your efficency by giving you an aditional 5hp a tick. Some focus items are also nice by reducing the cost to cast spells or improving spell performance.

Another big factor for the efficient shaman is sustainable damage output. When you get to lvl 34 and start having a pet this can help you quite a lot because it improves your damage output. Also, having a nice weapon like a good ratio 2hb and a haste item can also improve your efficency.

How you play your character can also change how you balance ac / hp / wis on your gear. In general, the more you solo tank the more value you will get from ac and a good mele setup but even in group a resonable amount of ac will help you in a lot of situations where you want to maintain more agro on all the mobs the the cloth casters like enchanters and wizards.

At your level I would try and get my WIS all the way up to about 130 or 140 and then go heavy AC with any HP on the items consider a big +. When I soloed I would swing a big hammer and equip a hast item like schw. In group, mele == almost zero so the hammer and the hast item go in the bag in favor of a stat spear and a shield and such.

Untill you reach your low 50s... AC > HP > WIS.

As you get close to 60 then HP > AC > WIS.... I put wis on the buttom because it will start to go up without giving it any thaught at all as so much of our equipment will have wis on it.

The best items you can have are those that directly improve you mana or hp recovery rate or those focus items that improve you spells, or give you free extra damage (JBB, nice weapon, haste items).

I truly hope this helps you understand a little better how a shaman works. Once you get it figured out you will not need much help at all figuring out which item(s) to equip or why you are doing it. One of the reasons a shaman is so much fun to play is because our gear can and does make a differance and provides ous with a lot of things to consider. In contrast my necro can play naked at a about 75% effectivness.

--------------The Rathjar Shammy------------------------

In the begining.
--------------

First step in making a great shammy is choosing a race. Each race has its own advantages and disadvatages, but in the end game it really doesnt' matter much. I would choose a race based on your personality, not on which race will make a better shaman. Because of this belief I am not going to go into detail on what races get what ablitys. We all know evil races have bad faction, barbies are blind, and cats are perty =p. The most important part about making your new shaman is a high starting wis and good stamina / agility.

Now you go out and get levels. I can post a hunting guild later. For now, lets assume you just got 24 and you now have cano 1. From this point on, IMHO the way to play a shaman now changes. This is the section I would like to cover. Welcome to the wonderful world of armor and equipment.

------------Armor and Equipment guide for shaman-----------

Before we can go into armor and equipment I would like to say that this will not make you a good shaman. A good shaman is a good shaman even if he is nakid. I have soloed IG's in everfrost nakid at 49 while waiting for a rez on a dragon raid. Gear does not make the shaman, it only makes us better.

Part 1: The many types of shaman

Shaman can be divided into 2 main classes. Wisdom Shaman, and HP shaman. Both have strong points and weak points. Knowing what balance you need to fit your play style is what you have to decide for your self. I will only show you the way.

Part 2: The truth about wisdom

We all know that wisdom increases the amount of mana a shaman has. Well, I'm here to tell you that is technically not true. Wisdom increases the maximum amount of mana you could have. We can prove this by taking off all your wis gear, medding till FoM and then putting on your wis gear. Your mana will drop. This is because you have increased the max amount of mana you can have, but not your current mana. I know you are asking why this matters, but just bear with me, this is important later.

Part 3: The truth about Canno

One of ths shaman's great powers is the ablity to trade HP for mana. Canno 4 and Canno 5 (aa skill) are roughly .567 mana per 1 HP. our a 2 to 1 ratio HP to mana. This means at level 58, full buffed I can get about 1650 mana if I canno from full life down to almost dead. I can then C heal myself for 400 mana to almost full life and have made 1250 mana. Wash rinse and repeat. At level 60 with Torpor i can do this for the cost of only 180 mana thus making almost 1800 mana and still being full of life. Now we all know this, but using it is a differnt story. Most of us are afraid to canno down and keep pulling. This is our single most limited and greatest power. Many shaman wear more HP gear to boost the amount of mana they can canno.

Part 4: Putting it all together

So we now know why shaman need HP's and why shaman wear wisdom gear. So lets put it all together.

Step 1) How much wis do I really need?
Most people will tell you the most wis on an item the better it really is. This is simply not true. The truth is you only need as much wisdom as you can spend. If you are constantly OOM and holding your group back while you canno and med, you dont have enough wis (or you cast way too much durring combat) If you are always fom or almost fom after each pull, even multiples you have too much wis. The truth is extra wisdom does nothing for you if you are not FoM. As a shaman if you are FoM either your group is slow, or you are doing something wrong. Try adjusting your wis by swaping out some items for HP or AC and see how it works, if your still not hitting OOM then its a good change. If you are going OOM a lot more, then put some more wis gear on.

Step 2) Ok so what am I supose to replace this gear with?

Well, for one, you can't go wrong with more HP's. HP gear will allow you to canno more, thus making you have more mana, and as a side effect, requiring less wisdom. This is not to say ditch all your wis gear. Use common sense. Would I trade a no ac 10 wis ring for a 6ac 65hp ring? Hell yes i would. Would i trade a 36ac 15wis bp for a 37ac 50hp bp? No way in hell. Maybe if it was 250hp. The trick is slowly changing the ratio of Wis to HP and Sta (it gives HP too you know) Until you are running at about 70m full time. I always tell my groups i'm 50m when i'm 25m and that i'm fom at 50 mana. Basically for me, its true. And I never go OOM. AS long as you have HP's you can't go oom, its not possible. So trade out those earings and rings, and slowly move to more HP's and watch your mana pool actually grow.

Step 3) I have all this HP's now, but when I slow and a mob beats me to a pulp I don't have any HP's to canno and i'm not seeing how this helps me.

This is where it all comes together. The most important stat a shaman can have is HP's its true, but the second most important stat a shaman can have is AC. AC allows us to preserve our wonderful HP's so we can do more damge to ourselves. Everytime a mob hits you its like he is sucking your mana away. Now it is not possible as a shaman not to get hit, having a very high AC will help you avoid getting hit. The shaman AC seems to cap out around 1300ac, so thats your target. Right now at level 58 I have around 927ac unbuffed. Fully buffed i break 1k ac easy. This allows me to do massive crowd control. Unmezable mobs are a shamans best friend. Simply slow and dot till you have agro, run out and root park. If it resists root, you can just tank it. You have about the same AC as a hybrid and almost as many HP's. Now its hard to get tanks to let you do this (the have tank envy) but once you can convince them its ok and to just kill 1 mob at a time, they tend to start to love you. If the cleric complains that you are making his job hard because he has to heal you and not a tank, tell him to get bent and you will heal yourself. I have tanked 2 slowed tin men while my group killed 3 spiders, I didn't need more then 1 heal. This should really be one of our jobs. We can take a beating, we can reduce the damage out put of the mob by 75%, at that point, they really can't hurt you. If a mob doesn't hit for more then 500 damage, dont ever be afraid to tank it.

-------------Final thoughts---------------------------

I hope that if you try my tatic of High ac and HP's and a lower wisdom you will find yourself having more mana and doing a much better job as a shaman. Even if you find yourself OOM a lot with your current wis, I would suggest giving this a try. A lot of times shaman are oom simply because they refuse to canno. A tipical shaman needs to cast about 2000 mana a fight min. So you should never stop cannoing. If you are not FoM you should be cannoing or waiting for the recast of canno. Watch your actual total hp not a percent. I use an actual number on my UI so i can see how close I am, I will canno down to the point where the next canno will kill me. I do this even durring combat. Once you train your cleric not to worry about you things tend to go more smoothly and eventually the cleric finds himself FoM with nothing to do but tank and nuke.

I hope this helps future shaman.

Musicandor
Up until you are able to acquire the Cannibalization Advanced Ability (Cann5), I would have to say that you should be building your shaman WIS>HP>AC. Most pre-PoP mobs do not hit for the kind of damage you will encounter in the Planes, and your mana supply will most likely be the determining factor in whether you are able to sucessfully solo a mob. I am also basing this on the fact that pre-PoP mobs seem to less resistable to the shaman's root spell, and most of our dots in general. It is more than likely you will be able to root a mob long enough to get your heal spell off if you take any significant damage.

When you get Cann5, you are ready to Solo in PoP and at least stand a chance (in my book). Your odds of survival significantly increase if you have the spell Torpor, and even more if you have Malo. So after you acheive Cann5, you are going to want to be able to cast it without 1) killing yourself (most shammies have done this once.......once) 2) lowering your hp to the point where you draw low life aggro (certain death) and 3) Be able to cast it after dying immediately after casting torpor on yourself, and not draw low life aggro. #3 is important in raiding situations where a quick recovery is essential to completing an event or to beat out the respawn. So, after you acheive Cann5 (a great thing) your shaman building priorities need to change from WIS>HP>AC to HP>AC>WIS. Yes, it takes a lot of AA's to get Cann5, but it seperates you from those that dont have it. I would rather have a lvl 60 with cann5 in my group than a lvl 65 with none. PoP mobs just dont give you the luxery of sitting back and regening mana as a shaman. From now on, medding won't be in your vocabulary, or the stupid canni-dance we were all forced to use (which really worked well I must admit). As you progress through PoP your gear will most likely improve, and you will have access to the higher priced items to augment that. Personally, I recommend getting lvl 60 first, then going all AA until Cann5 is acquired (especially if you have torpor). After getting Cann5, XP straight to 65. This will greatly increase your HP and AC (somewhat), and will start you on the path to getting more and more HP. With a KEI and Cann5 and torpor, you can solo just about any tier 3 PoP mob and below no problems, and be able to move on to the next mob with a mostly full mana and HP bar. Remember HP IS MANA....and Medding is out.

As you progress through PoP, by the time you reach Tier 4 zones you will probably have 4500-6000 HP Fully Buffed, and be able to take a few hits, but not much more. At this, "the beginning of the endgame," your priorities are going to change again. Instead of HP>AC>WIS, you are going to want to follow this:

1) HP - Get you Natural Durability and Planar Power AA's maxed, and get your STA to 305
2) Max out any AA's dealing with Combat Avoidance, like Combat Agility
3) WIS - Get Innate Enlightenment AA maxed, and get your WIS to 355 a larger mana pool will sometimes save your butt
4) AC - Unless you have a TON of AC, comparable to a Warriors (nearly impossible at the same progression level), forget about AC. Your gear that you acquired earlier to get all that HP almost always has decent AC as well, which will be good enough to take couple of hits, but not much more. From here on out, your AC rating is only a buffer between whether 3 hits kill you, or 5. With combat avoidance you need to make the most of that gap. 5 more seconds DOES matter.

The mobs in PoP hit HARD. As you get closer to Time, the mobs hitting you are just insanely powerful, most requiring several groups, or one really well armed group just to clear normal stuff. The combat avoidance line of AA's are your best way to survive in raids. You arent soloing in Tier 4+ unless you are uber-shammy-extraordinarie with a full set of time gear and 700AA's. And even then, you are still going to die when one of these beasts hits you more than 1-2 times. Your combat avoidance will help you greatly, but it does not make you an unhittable ninja. These mobs will mess you up, and good.

Perhaps in future expansions Shamans will get a major natural AC boost, or maybe even be able to use some plate. If the developers are going to make turgurs insects so powerful to a mobs hate list, something has to give. Not every tank you group with is going to have a BoC or BoW or BF to get instant aggro. Taunt is useful for saving your butt after the mob has already pounded you once or twice, and thats about all you are going to be able to take without avoidance AA's. Would the game be put out of balance if a Shaman's defense was in line with a warriors? At the high end of the game, I think not.

Most of this is only my opinion, and critisicm is welcomed. Maybe it will make you or me a better shaman, which is what we all strive for.
