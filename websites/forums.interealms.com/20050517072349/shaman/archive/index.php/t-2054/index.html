Gloriana
Shaman are in a mixed role of casting and fighting. Melee does become increasingly ineffective, but that doesn't mean shaman still can't take hits. Whereas druids use snare to keep mobs from hitting them (their AC limited leather armor doesn't allow them to stay close to the mob for very long), shaman lack snare (except for inny worshipping shaman) and need the AC + hitpoints to take hits from mob (shaman have a wider variety of higher AC armor that allows this). Even root/rotting doesn't protect a shaman from being hit for several reasons:

*shaman dots have an intial damage component that breaks root.
* Nuking a mob via our ice nukes or JBB can break root
* Root's just darned unpredictable

Although there are many differing views on the subject, just remember that unlike other priests a shaman's mana pool is not solely determined by wis + mana items, but also by how quickly the shaman can convert hitpoints to mana. Hitpoints are potential mana.

So...

Higher AC = good, because less damage taken = more hitpoints to convert to mana.

Higher hitpoints = good, because more hitpoints to be converted to mana.

Higher hitpoints = good for a "caster" shaman, because there is more potential for mana conversion AND higher hitpoints = good for "melee" shaman.
