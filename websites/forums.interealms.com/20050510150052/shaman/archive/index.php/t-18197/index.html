Thorzion
After 59, level 60 will fly by. I went from 60 to 61 in about an hour or so in PoV at the switch camp. If you are going to stop in your 60's to get AA's, I would say get to 62 before you stop. Then you have the 61 pet, nice regen spell, upgraded focus, the best single target stat buffs, and your last heal spell.

However, it took me only about a week longer to go from 60 to 65 than it did to go from 59 to 60. (I was stuck at 59 for about a month, playing casually) Get 65 first would be my recomendation. 61 and 62 spells are SO easy to get. Ethereal parchs drop like crazy and most 61&62 spells can be had for less than 100 plat in the Bazaar on almost any server Id bet. Spectrals are just a little harder to get, which isnt hard at all. Runes are had unless you are doing Towers/elementals, but those are not uncommon either.

I think its very situational and personal for each player as to what they should do. I came back to EQ days before the release of PoP. Most of the guild I was in was still below level 60, I didnt want to outlevel the rest of the guild by a lot, and no one had any higher level spells anyway and most of the tier 1 and tier 2 pop zones were locked unlike now. Alternately, you may also have a slow leveling guild (or friends) and you may not want to outlevel them because you group with them a lot (my case).

So basically, do what you want and what makes you enjoy the game more, there really is no right or wrong answer.
Ruatonim
Haste helps all melee abilities, including normal "autoattack" melee, range attacks (bows, throwing knives, etc), kick, bash, etc.

If you dont have any useful 65 spells (Quiescence, ferine avatar, malos), it's probably not worth getting 65 asap. If you're doing LDON, then it is advisable to get 65 asap for the larger adventure point reward.

I stopped for Canni5 at levels 62 and 63. Why? Because I was playing main healer and slower for my group of friends. Canni4 was NOT enough to keep up with the pace we wanted to go at. The difference in what I was capable of before canni5 and after canni5 is astounding. Canni5 is also a huge boon indoors where you cannot use a horse.

If you don't have torpor, canni5 won't make as much difference for you most likely, but, if you have torpor waiting to scribe, you aren't going to regret getting canni5 before 65. Also, depending on where you hunt, being a lower level may benefit you if being <65 puts you within 5 levels of the mob level; this is only true if being 65 would put you out of this range.

Personally, I would have regretted getting 65 asap and skipping any AAs. Our spells, especially the upper level ones, cost extremely large amounts of mana, and not having the increased mana regen of canni5 (approximately FT30 using torpor/q to heal yourself) would have been much more difficult. I'm still unsure how I managed before getting canni5.
