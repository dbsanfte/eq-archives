While I don't have that list, I can name some tweaks off the top of my head.

First, turn on texture cache of course, set your VideoModeBitsPerPixel in Eqclient.ini to 16,
Setting UseUmbra=False may help, also, depending on your video card, setting UseTnL=False may help as well.

In your system.ini, put the line
ConservativeSwapFileUsage=1

Turn off all thsoe god-awful new models. Or at least, the half elven ones.

That's about all I can think of.

-----------------------------------------------------
Win95/98/98se/ME Shadows of Luclin players READ!!
If you have over 128mb of RAM, which we all should if we are playing SoL, read this article and apply the suggested line to your system.ini file.. It made a WORLD of difference for my system..

www.aumha.org/a/memmgmtz.htm#conserve

I have a P3-1ghz CPU, 1gb of RAM and a GF3-Ti500.. After loading SoL, my system would pause every 3 seconds for about half a second.. I was getting really really frustrated to say the least.. My disk was doing a lot of swapping and I really felt like my machine should be performing better then it was.. So I did some surfing and found this fix.. It links right to an MS article that in so many words says that all the extra RAM you have in your system is basically doing jack and **** without this entry..

First, you set Windows to manage your Virtual Memory.. Secondly, add this line to your system.ini under the 386enh section as the directions say.. I zone substantially faster, the new textures load substantially faster and I have far far FAR less lag when I am in large groups of people that before dropped my framerate down to hideous rates.. Basically, this will make SoL run almost as fast as it did before the new engine in my honest opinion..

Give it a shot, it worked for me and has done great things for a number of my guildmates as well.. Its very worth it..

Machine 2:
PIII 733

512 MB PC133 RAM
7200 RPM Ultra ATA HDD
SB Live Value
DirectX 8.1

Machine 3:
Cel700
GeForce2 MX400 64 MB
512 MB PC133 RAM
7200 RPM Ultra ATA HDD
SB Live Value
DirectX 8.1
Nvidia reference drivers - Detonator 21.83 WHQL certified
Video config:
1280x1024 resolution
32bit color depth
2x FSAA
Direct3D Fog Table Emulation disabled (unchecked)
Mip Map Detail level set to "High Quality Image"
EQclient.ini settings:
UseTNL=TRUE
VideoModeBitsPerPixel=16
UseD3DTextureCompression=FALSE
EQ Options: (the "Options" prior to logging in)
Loading all 30 character models
Texture Quality set to Medium
Using 'original' textures for old world zones, Kunark, and Velious
Windows system.ini settings:
Added the following entries to their [respective] sections
[386Enh]
ConservativeSwapfileUsage=1
[vcache]
MinFileCache=16384
MaxFileCache=45056
I also ran Cacheman 5.0 to optimize/make certain registry entries for my systems cacheing. I highly recommend it.
That's really about it. I'm convinced these settings work well since yesterday and the day before Shan's PC crashed every time I just went into Marus Seru for no more than even a couple of minutes. Some zones were still a tad laggy, but all indoor dungeon type zones had great performance. Mainly the humongous outdoor zones and the larger city zones (Shadow Haven, Sanctus Seru, etc) had a noticeable frame rate decrease. The Bazaar is a bit laggy too, but no where near as bad as it was prior to todays patch and the above tweaks being made to my systems.
Hope this info helps someone else enjoy Luclin as much as I am now.
