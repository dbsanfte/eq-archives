I've been on one Mujaki attempt, which failed. I don't know much, but here's what I saw.

It is one of the scripted encounters in Nightmare, started by Etumer (you can read the story in a series of books in the Library). You get to bring four groups into a large courtyard with some graves in it. "Servants of Mujaki" start coming out of the graves and walking to an untargettable Mujaki, where they mount horses.

Our force split in two and tried to kill as many Servants as possible before they reached their mounts. The Servants didn't seem to be all that tough (blue to 61) but there are a LOT of them: 36 total, and they start coming out of the graves two at a time eventually. The plan was for each group to pull the Servants coming out of one of the three graves, leaving the third one alone. Unfortunately the second team did not understand this and tried pulling the third grave as well, which caused them to wipe out. Both sides had to stop pulling to recover, and the end result was 24 Servants made it through.

At that point Mujaki and the Servants respawned as targettable versions. Our Monks went out and tried to split him, but we ended up with Mujaki and at least half a dozen Servants stampeding into camp. I was trampled instantly, and the raid wiped out shortly thereafter. I did hear someone say in /rsay that Mujaki had been slowed; no idea if that was true or not. In the short barrage I took, the top hit from a Servant was 473, and Mujaki himself hit for 871. No idea how many hit points they have.

It looks like an interesting encounter. I would guess the key is having enough DPS to kill all the Servants before they mount up.
