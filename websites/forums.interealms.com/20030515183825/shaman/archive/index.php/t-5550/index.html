From Casters Realm...

PET RESISTS WILL BE DYNAMIC, JULY 20, 2002
With the proposed changes to pet spell resists, will the resist check of which is used be made against the base resists or is it fully dynamic? I.e. A player has a pet up. They have 90 MR and their pet has 70 MR. A magic based spell is cast on the pet and it uses the player resist and Level check against the spell. The player then gets tashed and now only has 60 MR. On the next magic spell cast does the Pet use the Player or their own resist checks?

Also, does it always use the player Level (if it is higher) and or does it use the Pet's Level if it uses the Pet's resist check?

Pets decide which resists to use at the time a harmful spell is cast upon them. They will always use their owner's level when checking for resists.

At the time a harmful spell is cast on the pet, it will decide if it's innate resist or it's owners resist is better, and use the higher score. This is dynamic and is rechecked each time a harmful spell hits the pet.

Hopefully that clears it up!
