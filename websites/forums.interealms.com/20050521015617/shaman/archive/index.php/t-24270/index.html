Firstly, let me give you some background... As none of you probably know, I'm the recruitment officer for Cold Fury (http://www.coldfuryguild.com) and have been since it's creation over 2 years ago. In that time we've done pretty much all the progression paths-

Velkator -> Vindi -> NToV -> Ssra/AoW -> VT -> PoP pre-elementals -> PoP elementals -> PoP Time -> GoD to... where we currently are in Txevu. (And obviously playing about in Oow)

We raid 7 nights a week pretty much without fail for about 4-5 hours a night. We have a smallish roster because a) people in the guild have high playtimes or they're booted and b) we all get more loot :P

Old content (ie: anything below GoD) we do not backflag for as we don't raid there as a guild.

That's the background over and done with - let me get down to answering your questions.

Uloff's tips

General
* Set goals for the guild and DON'T look back. ie: we'll do Plane of Time until we get to Qvic or we'll do VT until we kill Bertox. Complacency is the devil and counter productive to guild health.
* Be firm and decisive in your leadership - there is nothing worse than have "wishy washy" leaders... (Members can and will abuse weakness.)
* Don't have too many officers. We have 6 officers who make all the decisions for the guild. While we listen to what our members have to say, ultimately they have to adhere to what we decide. We work as a council, of sorts. IMO, the guild as it stands wouldnt work any other way.
* The leadership should stand united. Even if your officers dont all agree, if the majority of officers agree then as far as the guild is concerned you all agree. (This avoids people playing the officers against one another.)
* Be consistant with everyone. (This is a guild breaker if not followed to the letter.) This covers loot, rules, flags, fines/penalties, etc.


Managing flags
* As a guild when we defeat a new flagging mob we wont raid the new zone until we've completed it twice. With our members high playtimes we're pretty much guaranteed to flag 95%+ of the guild doing this.
* Because we're focused on new content, it reduces the flags we have to worry about. In GoD for example, we can flag people up to Txevu in a night. (In fact, we did it last night for a new member who didnt have Tipt, Ikki4, Uqua and Inktu'ta)
* If people are happy to do it and they can't be on when we flag, people in the guild log on members who need flags. (Obviously we'd prefer not to do this though)

Guild recruits
* Don't take any crap - at the end of the day if you're honest and open about what you expect and they don't meet it, then you're better off without them. Honesty is the best policy.
* Make sure they understand all the guild rules and don't underplay them.

Complaints
* Don't let people blackmail you - if they try *regardless of who they are* warn them that it won't be tolerated.
* Try to look at problems in terms of your "vision" for the guild and not what's best for the individual. Despite what people think, you can't please everyone - so you should concentrate on what your guild was created for. It's better for people to leave than be disgruntled. (And as bad as this sounds, they can always be replaced.)
* Don't try to sort out complaints while raiding. The last thing you need is more stress. if someone is really annoyed tell them to talk to you after the raid or tell them to log and talk to you later.


There's probably more to say, but thats all that springs to mind. I really enjoy being an officer, and my guild totally rocks! (I loves them all.) Good luck with everything, mate.
