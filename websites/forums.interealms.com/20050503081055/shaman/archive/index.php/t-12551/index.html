Jinheim
HoT and NToV are completely different.

HoT is for armor farming. You have no specific goal mob here. You and your guild sit down in a corner and pull drakes, wurms, and wyverns until the cows come home. Killing here raises your cov factions I believe. You need to be ally to do the armor quests.

NToV is a raid area. It is basically a big circle with named dragons spawning all around. Some of them, like Lord Fesh, are gimps - a glorified Yeldema. Some of them, like Lord Vyemm, Lady Nev, and the Triplets (can't remember their names) are tough bastards.

You start at Aaryonar's pit and work your way either clockwise or counterclockwise, depending on what mob you're after. In your way are lots of drakes and wurms - the same mobs you find in WToV. They will lower your faction with CoV but are not very hard. The real challenge of clearing in NToV are the flurry drakes. These things are mean SOBs. It is in your best interest to wait until malo tash and occlusion have all landed, because if you get a resist and draw aggro, you are dead. They hit for about 700 and are called "flurry drakes" because they FLURRY like madmen. On the bright side, the named flurry drakes can drop a class shield - shield of thorns for druids, shield of wurms for us, etc... Very nice shields.

A lot of the loot in NToV is pretty gimp by todays standards. Most of the dragons have both really nice loot and really crappy loot. Sometimes we will clear the place out and get nothing except loot for alts, and sometimes we'll get enough upgrades for 20 people. Usually it is somewhere in between.

NToV is a really fun zone - probably the most fun I ever had raiding. I really love killing dragons, and that's all you do in NToV. Some of the fights are boring, like Lord Fesh. Some of the fights, though, like Lady Nev, are a real rush. Most of my guildies really hate Lady Nev, but I love the fight. It is very long and grueling. She has lots of HP and a nasty AE that debuffs your MR and stuns you. Fortunately, healers can hide from it. Your healers really have to be on their toes for you to win. Last time we did her, I had 5 melees in my group and it was my job to keep them all healed as well as go out and reslow Lady Nev every 6 minutes. Very intense with never a moment to relax - which is how boss fights should be.

If you need any tactics or tips for the other dragons, let me know. I remember them all pretty well.

Have fun in there, it is a great place.

Edit: To answer your original question, there is NOT any NToV specific quest armor. You will get a few pieces of Kael quest armor (Spirit Caller's) off of the trash mobs, though. The HoT quest armor (skyshrine) is Wolf Caller's.

Vorpatril
Some of this has already been explained, but for completeness:

You can hunt in HoT (east tov) regardless of you CoV faction. You will likely have trouble with the CoV mobs outside if someone opens the door if you are KOS. Also you will probably need a CoH to get there if you are KoS to CoV. The mobs in HoT give no faction hit either way. The only drops in HoT are quest pieces you turn in at SS for Wolf Caller's. So, to get the armor, you need to get good CoV faction.

The mobs in WEST ToV are on CoV faction, so if you have good faction, you can generally run around in there with no problem. Killing them gives bad CoV faction and good giant faction. They drop the quest pieces you turn in to Kael for Spirit Caller's. I've only hunted there briefly, but the drop rates seem much higher than in HoT.


North ToV is generally KOS to everyone regardless of CoV faction. Killing there reduces CoV faction. To the best of my knowledge, it does not drop the quest armor pieces (wolf/spirit caller) - at least I've never seen any. NToV is all about big named dragons. As mentioned above, the loot of these dragons ranges from fabulous to now mediocre. There is no "NToV armor" as there is SS/Kael armor. There are just lots of different pieces that are generally pretty good.

The difference in difficulty between HoT and NToV is rather large. Back in the day, most guilds started by raiding Kael for Thurg armor pieces and CoV faction, then moved to HoT for SS armor pieces, then switched factions and hunted in WToV for Kael armor pieces, then moved into NToV and forgot armor farming.

Of course, now that you can zip up to 65 pretty fast in PoP, I would imagine even medium guilds could skip all that and put a decent dent in NToV...
