Chutoi
With a random freeze-up, it honestly could be any number of things. First thing I'd check is the low-tech solution. Are you fans (CPU and video card both) working well? Overheating can lock your computer and fry it if it persists. Typically it will lock up more based on how long the computer is on, rather than how long you've been playing EQ.

Also, check the datarate in your EQ ini file. The datarate defaults at 5.0 (I think). Anything above a 9.0 is the same as a 9.0. Anything below a 3.0 won't work either, as I recall. The datarate determines the speed at which your computer receives info from the servers. Dial-up should be around 3-5, cable/DSL should be from 7-9. Datarates too low or high could also lock the game.

I'd check those 2 things first and see if they help.

You don't have to disable the sound card on the board, or in windows. All you have to do is when you first start up eq, before you enter your password select options. It'll ask for you to select your vid card, then the models you want enabled. The 3rd screen will have check boxes, one of those is "Sound". Uncheck it. Exit out of EQ, and go back in. If this corrects your lockup problems, try getting the newest drivers for your sound card. If that doesn't work (which it probly won't), you might think about disabling onboard sound and putting a newer/better pci sound card in there. I recommend Sounblaster 128, works wonderful.
