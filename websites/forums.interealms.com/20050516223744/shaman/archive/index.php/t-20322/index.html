Winterfury
And I'm glad I did. When I'm not raiding I'm spending all of my time in GoD expeditions. I've won every sewer trial and am working now on the vxed and tipt trials (hoping to be flagged for Kod'Taz this weekend), and the sense of accomplishment is quite satisfying.

The action is great, it demands the very best play from every member of the group, there is very little margin for error. The environments are gorgeous, Barindu is kind of CT'ish with mayan temples and flowing vines and there's danger in every step, the tension is palpable. The missions are a nice change of pace from the bland generic missions of ldon, and offer some interesting tactical options. I think I've won the first sewer trial about 3 different ways and each time is an improvement on the last. I can win trial 3 in 30 minutes now with the right group, and the discoveries that led to that point were very fun to sort out. Even the apprehension associated with with just *getting* to an expedition alive is welcome, it's a thrill every time. I love to hate that roaming see-invis dog in the Barindu hallway, and I've got the run to Ferubi zone hard coded into memory now from the times that invis has dropped in the courtyard or an archer has waltzed by. Hah!

Are there things I don't like? Hell yes. CR is a pain in the butt, it should *never* be the case that a wipe = game over, it's so discouraging. I don't like that they gave big raiding guilds an opportunity to completely bypass the sewer trials in order to advance to the mountain trials. Make everyone pay their dues, I say! The sewer environments could have been a lot less repetitive. I certainly agree with most of the sentiments expressed here that the "enhancements" for Shaman class are flimsy (I don't see myself buying any of the GoD AA's for a while, if ever). In fact, it's hard as hell to get any kind of rewards, the drops are so scarce. The kill-reward ratio truly sucks. There is very little to be excited about if your main hope was enhancements to your class or easy new loot.

But for me, the bottom line ultimately is the action. I came to play, and GoD offers me some of the most satisfying game time I've experienced in a long time in this game. Hah, I dont' think I'll ever be able to go back and sit in the HoH basement for 5 hours grinding again. Why should I, when I can get better experience having the fun I'm having in GoD expeditions?

Yeah, I'm glad I bought it.
