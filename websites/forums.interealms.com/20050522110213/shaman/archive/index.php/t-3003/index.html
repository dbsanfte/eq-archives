Talamay Crystalspirit
There is no class defining spell for the shaman class.

None.

Spirit of Wolf is shared by both druids and rangers. People don't recognize a shaman because of Spirit of Wolf. They don't ask shamans to raids for Spirit of Wolf.

Cannibalization is a unique ability, but it doesn't define the shaman class. As someone else mentioned, raid leaders don't say "lets make sure we have shamans to cannibalize." Exp groups don't look for shamans because they can convert hit points to mana.

Insects line (togors, turgurs, etc) is the closest the shaman class has to a defining spell, but this ability is shared with enchanters.

Torpor is an end game spell. Class defining abilities do not exclude the entire game to just level 60. Not every shaman even has Torpor to begin with. Besides, shamans aren't recognized for a great ability to heal anyway.

Malo is strong, but it doesn't define the class. Not only do mages have a similar spell (mala), but Malo isn't required for all aspects of EverQuest. It helps, but it isnt what makes a shaman a shaman.

The pet is certainly not a class defining ability. Two words: Mages, Necros.

Buffs are a central ability to the class and one of the things people know shamans best for. They're probably the closest thing that shamans have to a class defining spell ability, but most don't recognize casting buffs as their main vocation.

Heal is the cleric class defining ability.

Damage over Time spells are shared with Necromancers, who actually have more spell lines and thus better damage output with DoT spells.

Clicky items aren't unique, every class has clicky items. Also, not everyone has all the clicky items.

The shaman class is a generalist class. We can deal damage, heal, debuff, buff, cast a pet, do some crowd control and look sexy doing it. There isn't a single ability that we have that is clearly "class defining." The thing is, when someone mentions shamans, people get different ideas about what they do. The most common, of course, are Slowing (debuffing) and Buffing. The responses on this thread are an indication that the class doesn't have a single class defining spell..
