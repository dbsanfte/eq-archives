If this cheap way fixes it, then look into buying a new power supply with better fan output or adding a supplemental fan to your case.

Loramin,
Sounds like you've got a good start on the problem. A faulty RAM chip would certainly cause the problems you describe. Also, faulty video drivers (or drivers that EQ doesn't like, ie ATI) can also cause it.

I run Win 2000 on one of my EQ PCs, and have no problems. Make sure that you have the most current win 2000 drivers for your video card.

I'd re-install Direct X 8.1 just to be sure.

Also, if you weren't aware, Win 2000 is built from Win NT, which means that the latest service pack needs to be run after installing new software. Win 2000 isn't as bad about this as NT is. You can get away without re-installing the service pack most of the time, but in your case, you want to get all your bases covered to narrow down the problem, so it would be a good idea.

If you dont have service pack 2 (3 is out now, but haven't heard if it introduces any new bugs yet), you can download it from Microsoft's website (don't try this if you use a dial-up modem), or you can order it sent to you on CD for free.

Good luck!
