This is not intended to be a gloat. I know there are other guitar players on the board, some might get it. Some other musicians might get it. Or y'all might laugh at me like over the D&D Guitar Method.

OK, here goes. The first guitar I had that was able to be tuned and played was an acoustic, a Yamaha factory second called an Eterna that my folks got me for an early 15th birthday present. To put it bluntly, I HATED that guitar. It sounded good for about 5 minutes with a fresh set of strings, then it was back to being a dead, lifeless hulk. Some guitars are just like that.

I kept that one acoustic for a long time, it got me through most of college, assorted hunting and fishing trips, campfires, parties, some low-volume gigs, and impromptu concerts for prospective soulmates. But I finally sold it to a friend for $60, and was glad to be rid of it. I knew that I just plain hated acoustic guitars, they were all hard to play, and hard to sound good on.

As part of my 30th birthday present, my wife gave the go-ahead for me to find a "good" acoustic. Deep down, I figured it would be a Taylor, as I've played some of those I kind of liked. But after perusing web-sites, talking to some friends who are into acoustic guitars, and a phone conversation with a particularly good "matchmaker" of people and guitars, I ordered this:

Santa Cruz OM/PW (http://home.cfl.rr.com/amathews/ompw1.jpg)

It's a small bodied acoustic by a pretty small maker. It wasn't cheap, but I am looking for a guitar that's going to get better with age and be with me for a very long time. A friend of mine who's a pro musician in Nashville played this particular instrument for me, and ranked it above even the shop's instruments in the $5000+ category. So I gave it a try.

I got it Friday, and am completely stunned. This guitar has a strong chance of changing my life. Already, I've written the basic parts for two songs, something I haven't done in 10 years. I spent both mornings this weekend playing on my porch watching the sunrise. It doesn't hurt to play like the old one, and for a small bodied guitar it has a balance and clarity I've never heard before. It projects very well, filling up a room like a good piano.

So, while it's probably not as fulfilling as having a 5 year old, my happiness is making music. And finding something new that's inspiring me to create rather than just recreate.

Just so nobody takes my post the wrong way, I've been playing guitar all through this time, just electric. I've got a bunch of electric guitars. But I never saw a need for an acoustic until I got one again.

I admit to being a bit of a noodler on electric. It's HARD to play solo electric unless you're doing chord melody jazz stuff. Yeah, I know, Eddie Van Halen kind of invented the extended guitar solo with Eruption, but you can only listen to that for so long. So it's snippets of songs, work on scales, do some arpeggios, little chord work, another song snippet, play along with an MP3.

My wife kinda summed it up when she said that in picking up this new guitar I stopped playing notes and started playing music.

Besides, I gave up on being a rock star a LONG time ago. I'm over it. Just like all the old metal bands on "Behind the Music", I'm now in my sensitive acoustic period.
