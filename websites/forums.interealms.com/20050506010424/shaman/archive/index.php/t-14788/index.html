Well, if you're really bored of AAs, you can always do quests. My assumption is that if you have 3500 hps, you are near the elemental planes. There are countless quests to do that are fun and rewarding such as the earing quest.

First off, if you are in the elemental planes, get Radiant Cure 3. You need it for exp groups in PoE and it's very useful everywhere else.

I really enjoy ID5. There's nothing better than HoH mobs missing me twice then hitting me for in the mid 100's twice. I love it so much and don't regret it. Healing adept is also very helpful. However, I do like gift better than adept. But, it's still very useful. If you use kragg's alot, get it. With all level you do 1549 heal instead of 1300. 250 might not seem like alot, but it does help. And it's an extra 500 when you crit.

SCF is good if you are into nuking in exp groups. I enjoy it. Every single level helps and even though it's 9 points

Planar Power 5 would also be a good way to go. Each level adds about 15 more hps. It's not alot of hps total, but it sure does help.

I have no idea on channeling focus, i still don't have that and SCR3.

I can't tell you which you should go for first, it's truely all your decision. When i was deciding what AAs i needed i always asked myself " what AAs can i get to improve my performance on raids." If you get hit alot and almost die, get ID5. If you heal alot and what tnarg's to be stronger, get adept. If you want to be able to nuke more, get SCF. If you are in the elemental planes, GET RADIANT CURE!

Good luck to you and your guild.

Well, until a couple of days ago, I was under the impression that Kragg's Mending could crit, since I've seen cleric CH's crit and thought ours was flagged as a CH. But, Kragg's Mending is a percent heal (1950 or 75%) and as such, it cannot crit.

About the AA, that really depends on how you play. Personally, I'm pushing towards defensive AA since I seem to get smacked around more than I heal folks (which is not to say I don't heal when needed, but I get a lot of aggro ;)). Plus, healing AA only affect one of our heals, Tnarg's. Not saying it's not good to have healing adept and advanced healing adept but just think about how often you heal compared to how often you get aggro from debuffing and such.
