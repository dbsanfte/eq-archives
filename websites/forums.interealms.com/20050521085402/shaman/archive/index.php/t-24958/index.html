Bufr
My experience has been any class can solo. MOBs are marked 'solo' or 'group'. A MOB marked 'solo' means it is designed for the solo adventurer. No MOB will assist unless you aggro them by proximity. MOBs marked 'group' are designed for a group of players, though you can still solo them if you are high enough level. Group MOB come in two flavors singles and groups. When you target a 'group' MOB all other MOBs in that group are highlighted. There is no way to separate that group all MOBs highlighted will aggro as one. A single 'group' MOB will always come solo.

Rezzing is important but it does not save EXP, at least yet. On death you will have an option to revive and can select your revival point. If you do not select revive any priest can revive you in place.

The Pros of the Shaman are strong buffing power and our Runes. The Shaman get a spell Spectral Ward which negate's damage for 24 seconds. I believe there is a total damage that can be absorbed however at 17th my Ward has absorbed several hundred points of damage and remained up. In fact when I solo my health bar nevers moves.

Sudedor
I too have found priests in general to be extremely valuable and powerful, each in their own way. Surprisingly, it seems that the three flavors of priests are actually *gasp* balanced. They each have their own way of doing things, and so far each seems very capable of fulfilling their role. A group with two priests, especially two different classes of priest, is capable of some VERY impressive things.

The fighter archtype is exactly what you expect. They can absorb a load of damage, and seem to be a bit better than their EQLive counterparts at dishing it out.

The scout archtype varies widely at the Class level, though each seems able to fulfill the role of DPS and each one brings some unique utility. These utilities, and indeed the classes, seem to more closely mirror their EQLive parents than some of the other classes.

I have no experience outside of playing with a couple magicians.
