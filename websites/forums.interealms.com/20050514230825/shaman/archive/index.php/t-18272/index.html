Let us define DPS.

DPS = damage per second.

DPS is an average number. It's taken over a period of time. That period of time seems to be the point of confusion for most people.

1 mob agro's puller
5 shaman begins casting malos
8 mob reaches camp
9 warrior engages mob, malos lands
10 other melee engage mob
11 shaman begins casting slow
13 slow lands
16 shaman begins casting bane
21 bane lands
24 first tic of bane hits
30 second tic
36 third tic
42 fourth tic
48 last tic of bane, bane ends, mob dies

Most melee begin the fight when the mob gets into camp. Some casters that parse start in casting range. Let's actually be generous and start in camp.

48-8 = 40 seconds. 420 x 5 = 2100 damage. DPS for the fight = 52.5 dps.

Measured over a single tic (when bane is running), you are doing 420/6 = 70 dps. That is not an accurate number. If you want to compare to other classes, you have to use the same time period (ie, same base divisor) that they are using. Otherwise you are comparing apples and oranges. It just doesn't work. Yeah it's fruit, but your apple pie is gonna taste pretty freaking odd if you get the wrong thing.

Guys, it is not accurate to add up all the potential dots, add X% for crits and y% for focus items, and then throw in a nuke--and divide by 6. That is a hugely inflated number that means crap. A wizard doing that with a crit on corona flare with a good focus item gets 1200dps. It's a MEANINGLESS number. In statistical terms, it's your single highest tic of dps over the course of a fight. That's an outlier that is often ignored in figuring true averages (it's not infrequent to eliminate the lowest and highest results and average the rest--not that it would be appropriate in this case, but I thought I'd mention it).

For consistency with melee, you might as well start timing when the mob gets to camp and they can start parsing their damage.

Warriors are considered low dps. You'll be burning heavy mana to get up to 2/3 of a warrior over the course of a fight if you go with a realistic measure of dps.

So yes, shaman are low dps. Generally, however, the longer a fight stretches, the higher our dps will go. We have time to stack DoTs, nukes, etc. Time is our friend. (Don't forget, we can slow time! ;) )

Please, no more posts of, "I did 487 dps over the course of a fight!" No, you didn't. If you had up 6 running DoTs, a rain, and a nuke, you might have gotten 487 dps over the course of ONE tic.

Sorry I should have been more clear. When I think of dps I immediately think of the dps that Chutoi defined. When I think of damage per tick, I think of "hey my dot does XX damage per tick" which, as he detailed, is not the same as dps when calculated on a per mob basis.

Yes, the dps of that dot for that tick can be measured but generally is misused when claims such as "bane is 70dps" are made. 70dps from bane is accurate on a per tick basis, but dps is generally calcuated over the duration a mob lives, or per fight, hence the distinction between damage per tick and damage per second. Chutoi said it all in detail of course, but really the definitions are simple.

DPS covers anything doing damage and is calculated on the duration of a fight. Damage per tick relates specifically to DoT damage on a per tick basis to serve as a measure of comparison for DoT's. I don't think I have ever heard a rogue's damage to be stated as "1200 DPT", or the equivalent of 200 DPS.

Damage per tick is not an average and is not the same as say, average damage per tick. Bane always does 420+x, x = modifiers, damage per tick. Always. Bane's average damage per tick over several ticks will vary, but for any tick, the damage is not an average. Spear of Torment instantly does 675 damage over a course of time ranging from infinity to where time approaches zero, but is usually around 6.5 seconds (cast time + recast time). It is calculated in damage per second and is therefor an average damage over X seconds.
