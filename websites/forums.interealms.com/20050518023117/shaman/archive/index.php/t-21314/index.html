The Hyena Shaman
My favorite dungeon to look at is paw. I went past the double doors for the first time EVER (in 4 years of playing) hunting down a collector's split paw eye with my beastlord. The interior lake with it's ledges and pathways was wonderful, and I've always liked gnolls, anyway. I hope that with it being a Hot Spot, more people will get to see it.

The entire continent of Kunark is exquisite. It's immersive - The ruins aren't cut-and-paste like most of the monuments in Velious, and you can tell there's a story - Who are the three brothers in Frontier Mountains? What about the giant statue in Overthere? Or the two colossi guarding the mouth of the river in Warslik's Woods (which is one of my favorite spots in Kunark, period). What the hell is that windmill for? And who here wasn't impressed by the aqueduct in Trakanon's Teeth the first time they saw it? A lost city overgrown by a hostile jungle? Another, occupied by a dragon who cheated death? It's things like this that made Kunark the best expansion.

Velious was nice, but certainally lacked the feel of immersiveness Kunark had. But damn! It had some eye candy, huh? My first raid on Growth, i zoned in... And realized I didn't want to screw up my faction for this place, 'cause it is, hands-down the best-looking zone in the expansion.

And then came Luclin. Sadly, most of it looked pretty darned cartoony. But there's some good spots. Sanctus Seru is gorgeous. Umbral Plains and Scarlet Desert are WONDERFUL for giving you the feeling of being in an alien landscape that could look like paradise but be hell. Seeing Norrath floating overhead is the one huge thing I regret about the sky changes they did.

Planes of Power failed to really impress me. Plane of Disease was pretty damned neat, though, because you're adventuring on something's giant rotted carcass. Maybe that's where Veeshan got off to? Innovation has great "post apocalyptic" atmosphere.

LoY is great. It pulls off the whole "haunted pirate island" feel wonderfully, from the smoky water and fog, to the shanty towns and spooky Luggald critters. The take on an "underwater innoruuk" being an amorphic squid-thing ala Cthulhu was pretty good, too.

Lost Dungeons sucks. There's a few places in Miraguls dungeons that look really nice, and I DO like the "sand tunnel" variety of Takish Hiz, but I can't stand Rujarkan Hills or Mistmoore. And Deepest Guk, neatly enough, LOOKS like Guk. Which is both a point for and against it.

And now, GoD... I like the underwater of natimbi, as well as the music. Haven't gone past there yet. Abyssal looks neat, too.

But for me, well, i'm a sucker for Kunark. Every time I just want to goof around and see the world, I wind up in Kunark.
