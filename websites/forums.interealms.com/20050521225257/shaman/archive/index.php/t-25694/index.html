Samanna
Here's my understanding on a group made up of 65, 66, 67, 68, 69, 70:

Say, for round numbers, mob is L 68, and has a base value of 10,000 XP.

Each groupie starts with 1/5 of that (six-person bonus), so 2,000 as a base of XP given.

Any of the toons might have 20% going to Lead XP, and some percentage going to AALP, and also might actually require more (or less) Level XP due to race. But this doesn't affect the initial 2,000 XP starting figure for any of them, just what impact it has on the various pools' percanetages.

Every toon should get a gross 2,000 XP figure to start with. Since the level 58 toon was the same level as the mob (mob was white), that's it. 2,000 XP gross = 2,000 XP net.

Since the level 69 toon was a little higher level relative to that of the mob, the 2,000 gross XP will be reduced somewhat (mob was blue) - we don't know the real number, but let's say to 1,800 net XP.

Since the level 70 toon was even higher level, relative to that of the mob, the 2,000 gross XP will be reduced even more (mob was blue). Again, we don't know what, but let's say to 1,500 net XP.

Now, the level 67 toon was lower level relative to that of the mob, so the 2,000 gross XP will be bumped up (mob was yellow), let's guess 2100 or so net XP.

The level 66 toon was also even lower level relative to that of the mob, so the 2,000 gross XP will be bumped up (mob was yellow), let's guess 2250 or so net XP.

Lastly, the level 65 toon was lower yet, elative to that of the mob, so the 2,000 gross XP will be bumped up (mob was red), so let's guess 2500 or so net XP.

Note, however that these are just illustrative numbers; I don't think anyone knows the actual percentage bonuses/penalties for the relation between mob and toon levels.

Note also that the raw amount of Level XP required for the lower level toons to get a percent (or bubble) of XP is smaller than that required to span the same percentage of the higher levels.

I'm not even sure the gms or devs know the answer to this questions fully because there are so many things involved.

Base exp for mobs used to be lvl * lvl * ZEM (zone experience modifier) and I don't think that has actually changed. It just jumps threw about 20 billion hoops after that before it gets figured out.

The next thing that happens I assume is the grouping bonus. The more people in the group the more exp a mob gives. Going from 5 people to 6 people adds an additional 20% exp so that the last person is free. In that adding the player even if they do nothing automatically adds enough bonus so that everyone else is getting the same share. IE 100 / 5 = 20 and 120 / 6 = 20.

I beleive at this point exp is just divided evenly among everyone in the group. It used to be based on how much exp you already had which ment higher level players got more exp and hybrids (which had exp penalty) got even more but that was removed.

The next thing that happens is it starts playing with how much exp you actually get. It should at this point take off the 20% exp if you've got raid or group leader aa's turned on.

I think then at this point it applied the level bonus for "difficult" mobs. I'm pretty sure this is only a bonus for difficult mobs and not a penalty for easy mobs. When they first implimented it people complained because it was a penalty for easy mobs (got less exp for shrooms in FG than before) and they changed it around so it was always at least as good as before the change. This should actually start kicking in on "high blue" mobs with in 4 or 5 levels of your own level if memory serves and gets bigger as they con higher and higher.

The next thing I think happens is it splits what ever's made it to this point between regular exp and AA exp by what ever you have the slider set to.

Final possible thing is that for hybrids they get an exp bonus applied to their regular exp to counter the exp penalty they were setup with.

I can't say for sure this is how things happen but given all the adjustments that occur this is the way that makes sense and would seem to work out.
