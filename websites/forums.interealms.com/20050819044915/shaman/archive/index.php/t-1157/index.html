Think about what a shaman is - a priest/magician in a primitive culture.

Curing diseases and sickness. Cursing your enemies. Making the tribe strong. Knowing the old lore and preserving the oral history. Foretelling the future and gathering information about the present.

I can see elements of the first three in the EQ1 shaman, but knowing lore, divining information, and telling the future are under-represented by our current skillset.

Maybe a way of getting more information on a MOB than just its con - like its class, relative number of HP, any special abilities or resistances, etc. (ooh, I know - tell me if it's gonna drop something good! hehe)

Tracking could fall into the divination area as well - instead of following literal tracks (which is dumb anyway, how do rangers do that when they are not following the mobs?) you just divine the current location and go for it.

A Vision-like spell that lets you send your spirit forth to reconnoiter an area would be cool (for non-PVP anyway).

Those things would make the shaman more "shaman-y". Whether it would make for better gameplay or not is hard to say.
Cush
I liked the way Camelot let you specialize in a spell line, after a certain point - it let you have one 'basic' class, with completely seperate abilities. The Norse Healer, for example, could come out as the equivalent of the Shaman, Cleric or Enchanter, at high levels. (Specialize in Buffs, Healing, Mez)

What I'd like to see, is something based on race (background) and diety. I could quite easily see the Ogre's specializing in Disease spells - the Feerrott (jungle type) would be laced with disease, and they'd have to learn to heal, and use, such. Trolls live in a swamp populated with poisonous critters - again, learn from the environment. Barbarians, in the icy north, wouldn't be exposed to either of those much, so would learn more about channeling the spirits of cold. Hmm, guess there are no Iksar or Vah Shir in it, but not sure where they'd fit.

Also, diety selection should matter. The Tribunal, Cazic Thule, Innorouk, and Rallos Zek are all valid Shaman dieties - and the spells that you get should reflect that choice. RZ shaman should be more warlike, and have more combat type spells; CT shaman, those involving fear; Inny, hmm... hate type? Not sure what should fall under that, and about the same with the Tribunal - "Justice" is a nice concept, but what spells would reflect it well?

Well, anyway. My main point is, there should be a noticable difference - at higher levels - between Shaman of various races & religions. Even between those of the same race & different religions. AA skills (which should be worked in to the class) should also reflect it - Shaman of Rallos Zek should gain some limited warrior skills as they go along, for example.

Personally, I've always thought there should be more variety, based on race (stats) and religion. A fight between an Ogre Warrior and a Halfling Warrior should show completely different styles - the big lumbering Ogre, who only needs to land a few hits, versus the agile little Halfling, who keeps jumping about & landing lots of minor blows. Also, clerics should be different. Those who follow Rodcet Nife should have superior healing/res abilities, although lower offensive capabilites, versus a warrior-cleric of Rallos Zek, who trades increased combat ability for fewer spells.
