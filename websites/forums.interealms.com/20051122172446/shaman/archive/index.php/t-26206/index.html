izmalis
One concern I've had for awhile as I gingerly step into the PVP world is the effect gear has on a player's PVP competitiveness. My high pop PVE server as usual got beatdown under load last night so we continued playing our PVP alts over on Frostmane. My PVP rogue's gear is awful compared to my PVE rogue, not even in the same ballpark. I'm a little worried how this is going to work as I move into contested zones w/o an instance crew to gear me up.

The reason I'm asking this now is because of the experiences we've had lately in PVP fights and duels in GZ on our PVE server. My crew is flat out wrecking people but they don't know crap about PVP. After inspecting some of the victims it was clear to me that we're geared about 2x better than the average player who doesn't raid/dungeon crawl. Epic AH gear is no substitute for blue instance gear. Our rogue has one of the weirdest, all over the board, inefficient builds and he annihilates people- he has very nice gear.

So with that having been said- will skill matter as much as gear later on? It sure seems to me that gear buys you a lot of mistakes. A phantom blade is no substitute for a thrash blade that's for sure. I think it will only get worse as PVP raiders start showing up with full epic and legendary gear- how on earth could you possibly compete with that? It will be sheeps to the slaughter.

What's your experience so far?

At level 60 everyone is pretty much the same. Honestly the blue set gear you get from the high level instances can be replaced at very little cost by normal green items you can get in the AH.

Anyways, the only piece of gear that really matters is skill, a decent weapon helps a little bit.
