How many shaman here have completed hard adventures?

The reason I ask is because right now completing a hard adventure seems to be at the edge of our DPS capability WITHOUT bringing my shaman.

Winnning group makeup (All level 65 with 100+ AAs): Bard/Enchanter, Beastlord, Paladin/Warrior, Cleric, Rogue, Wizard.

Our gear is a mix of Cazic/Dozekar/BOT Towers (See my magelo for typical gear).

It certainly looks to me like swapping the beastlord for the shaman in the above group would be a mistake. We generally do not have a lot of extra time after completing a hard mission. And I don't think my shaman can do comparable DPS to a well equipped beastlord. There are plenty of threads talking about the DPS of our various spells, but when you factor in time for slowing/maloing mobs (resists, etc.) the theoretical numbers get degraded (Max 110 DPS for velium strike, probably closer to 50% of that over the course of a fight when you consider other tasks, so 55 DPS + say 25 DPS for the pet, total of 75 DPS). Add to that the fact that the BL can tank the mobs better than my shaman when he gets slow aggro, which translates to less mana used by the cleric and more mana for a cleric nuke per fight.

So my questions are: How many shaman here regularly participate in hard missions AND win? What does your group makeup look like? And what level gear does your group have?

Just thought I'd dig this up, just to give some non elemental players some hope for hard missions ;)

We tried two hard missions tonight, both in Ruj.

Group was all 65 leader/officers of my guild so pretty well equipped for level of the game - we have no emp/VT, and are in Sol Ro of pop.

Make up was : Enchanter, Paladin, Rogue, Ranger, Cleric , Shaman.

Lots of aa between the group, all have class title, and decent aa ( I'm middle of group aa wise at 173). Everyone is also used to hunting together, and can second guess each other pretty well, which is what helps us most; in fact it was the exact same group that we beat Trial of flame with now I think of it ;)

First mission was assasinate, we got to boss in just under an hour, Boss fight itself wasn't too bad (slowed first time after malos/tash), I spent all my time keeping Q on paladin and tnargs to keep him toppped up between CH's, took us a few mins to kill it ;) I'd estimate Overseer sort of hp with slowable tier2 boss melee, was too busy mashing Tnargs/canni to look more closely.

Second mission was kill 60 orcs. We knew we could kill a standard orc in under a minute, so as long as we kept things flowing smoothly we could probably win. We kept a smooth fast pace up, ranger an enchanter working the pulls, won with 20mins to spare.

Overall I use very different tactics and spell lay out. normal mode I can win comfortably with any manner of oddball guild groups (only thing I insist on is snare, and I'm 20-0 on normal). I just use AE slow, 2 rains and Q/Tnargs all over.

Whereas to win hard, we had a much more 'convential' solid group, I just used velium strike as only DPS slot, and setlled for FA/utility over damage, no dots/rains due to quantity of mezzed mobs. I'd keep Q up pretty much always on paladin to smooth CH's. The pace is high tempo, but the main thing we found that won it was keeping calm and not panicing ;)
