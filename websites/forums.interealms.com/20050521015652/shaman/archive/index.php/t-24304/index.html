on Rallos,

I am a bit disappointed at the PvP abilities of most shamans on Rallos. For a PvP server, it's amazing that some people are so effective at killing rats and bats till 65-- but against thinking, living opponents they tend to get chewed up.

I think this is due to the repetitive- almost mindless, strategies that shamans employ while xping. Slow, buff, rinse and repeat. Doesn't allow much room for improvisation.

So, even though this may hurt my own career to divulge some secrets, I can't stand to see my shaman brothers getting beat up constantly.

1. Shamans are not victims! I get so sick of everyone complaining that shamans are broken. Believe it or not-- to be good in pvp requires attitude more than gear. /flame all you want but I've beaten countless ebayed twinks with just dirty tricks and a little initiative. If you wanna win in PvP, think of yourself as the hunter and just go from there. Fear = mistakes.


2. Be anal about buffs. I don't leave pok without 20 buffs up (yes count em -- the max). Put your crappy buffs first and the good ones at the end. If your going up against a druid or necro- click off your first 2 buff slots and you can pretty much negate anything they throw at you.

3. Move around! Shamans who stand and tank are shamans who get killed. Levitate has won more spells for me than any other single spell. If you have a problem getting dispelled-- see #2.

4. Pumice-- your best friend. Always carry lots of pumice stones. Clickable cancel magic has so many uses-- I give it to all my pk friends for Christmas.

5. Different strategies for different situations. Remember, a player is not a mob. He will respond to what you do and try to figure out a way to beat it-- the best PvPers can adjust their strategy infinately. If someone starts to cast gate, don't start a new ebolt for example. Slam that fool!

Yeah these are obvious-- but surprisingly most of my opponents havent figured em out yet. I would list more but time is running out.
Bozer
Tips against specific classes.

Casters: No finger wiggler can heal himself. No, not even necros! When fighting a caster use one of two approaches-
1. If hes weak and scared. Just run up and slam the crap out of him and throw in melee whacks and poison dots. If you dont have slam--just gate and /tell him, apologizing for wasting his time.
2. If he thinks he is going to pk you. These are my favorite caster fights. Stand out of casting range, talk smack in ooc and call him a coward. Odds are if hes twinked he will be an idiot and chase you down-- just keep hitting your best dot or debuff until he steps into casting range. (Disease works best for this strategy, but poison makes for a quicker fight) When the spell gets off- turn and run like hell. If you do it right, you will have one spell on him and he will be out of casting range to respond. Just keep it up until he dies or gates. The trick is to make the caster think he is winning because you are running scared. Then when he gets to around 20% you gotta know when to turn around and finish him with a quick slam (to stop gate) and nuke.
Pure Melees: Kite Kite Kite. But the scared ones who try to run away-- just chase em down and dont be afraid to melee a little bit. Nothing scares a melee more than being beat down by a caster while hes taking 200 a tick from dots.
SKs: Since the darkness nerf AND HT nerf, this class is nothing to be feared. Just kite like hes a warrior with less HP. If he has low MR use malo and go for root / PR strategy. If hes twinked just stack the DR dots and let him rot.
Bards: I once made a bard because I figured they are unstoppable in PvP. Yes, bards have some amazing advantages, but the key to beating bards is to outsmart them. Don't try chasing him around in open ground! Thats like tanking an SK. Go find a cave and /ooc and talk smack until the bard is dumb enough to come looking for you. Remember that bards have different line of sight rules from casters. Many times I have landed 2 dots on a bard from behind a wall when he didn't know I was there. Also, if you wanna totally shut a bard down just keep dispelling him. He will be so worried about refreshing his selos and resists that his offense will be nil. And if he doesnt refresh selos or resists-- you can kill him with ease.

Hope it helps someone. My PvP strategy is more aggressive than any of the shamans I have fought with or against. I like to get into peoples face and melee and run around talking smack-- sometimes they get mad and usually this leads to mistakes. Good luck and hope someone reads this.
