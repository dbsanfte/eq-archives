Copy of email sent:

Brenlo,

Heh, after all the massive shaman threads, I feel kinda bad about writing you but . . .

The shaman could really use a little more communication. Sorry for the length of this email, but I felt some evidence/explanation of the issues was needed for clarification.

Right now, there is a lot of confusion over several issues. First, are we done? Was the healing changes the sum total of our ‘balancing’? I don’t mean for good, but for the short term at least. Are we likely to see any other changes?

Have there been any move to address the underlying healing percentages? Specifically, there is a fair bit of disgruntlement that our healing has been restored to only our PoP percentages. In terms of raw upgrade, it’s about half what most shaman agreed we needed in GoD—specifically, we healed 1300 with tnargs, we thought 1750 would be the lowest effective amount, and we got 1550.

The healing percentages in PoP were aligned PRIOR to the implementation of slow mitigation. Thus, Shaman were nerfed in PoP, and never got our heals adjusted after the mitigation went live. Putting us back into the same percentages doesn’t address that issue. Further, the encounters in GoD are not simply hp/dps increases—they are a more fundamental shift in game mechanics. Apparently we were pegged at 47% of cleric heals at least as far back as the inception of PoP. That was before (a) mitigation, and (b) the change to mob hp vs dps.

The shaman have been arguing, so far unsuccessfully in terms of getting even a reply, that due to the widespread prevalence of mitigation the dev's need to examine the base percentage of heals for high level heals. More specifically, since stuff now mitigates about 50% of our slows to have a net slow of around 37.5%, many feel that shaman heals need to be bumped up in base percentage terms. I would argue that druids need to be bumped up too. (Maybe 54% and 70% of cleric heals respectively?)

When mobs mitigate, their dps does not go down as much, but our healing ability does not compensate. No other healing class is balanced against slow (I discount the minor heals that BLs get), so it only really affects shaman. Thus, while mitigation effects all slowing classes, it really double whacks the shaman since our heals remain unchanged. That problem goes back to PoP.

Now enter GoD, where mobs hit much harder and mitigate, but have less hitpoints. Fights are over faster, so over the course of a fight, mitigated slows, or even bounced slows are of substantially less importance to clerics or druids. The difference of mitigated slows over the course of a 30 second fight is relatively trivial to clerics or druids (their heals aren’t balanced against slow), especially since most cleric and druid heals contain an 'overheal' component--heals above and beyond the hp the tank has. GoD reduces that overheal component a lot, so much so that many cleric/druid heals hit for full efficiency. To some extent, that makes druids and clerics MORE efficient, though at the cost of some anxiety since you have larger spikes in hp! Pity the hapless tanks!

Shaman heals, on the other hand, are effectively gimped further. We were already healing for full efficiency due to lower hp heals. Now we have a situation where we are penalized for slow by having a heal that, because it almost always hits for full, can't get any more efficient, and it’s over the course of fights that further trivialize the impact of slow by being of such short duration. Slow saves us mana on healing over the course of a longer fight. Short duration fights, especially since we have to slow at the beginning just don't prevent as much damage.

Shaman were designed as an endurance class, and that concept is no longer relevant in GoD. Slow makes healing more efficient over the course of long fights. Thus, by slow, shaman add endurance to the healers' mana. Over the short term, we don't really save them ANY mana--they still have to heal once or twice. Over the long term, we can save them a TON of heals.

DoTs take a long time to work. Cannabalize/mana regen depends on our ability to take the time to do it. Burst healing and dps is now king, but shaman are at best pawns at both of those areas.

While I’m on the subject, is anyone examining shaman dps over short term fights? I’m talking about in terms of reasonable mana-efficiency and time. Sure, shaman could stack 3 dots and nuke a mob once so that for one or two tics we do a lot of damage—but we’d be out of mana in like 4-5 mobs. Not to mention that we’d probably be summoned and dead after the first mob.

Are shaman just basically hosed for GoD? There seem to be some fundamental game balance issues on shaman at the moment, and while the healing upgrade helped, it hasn’t addressed any of the real underlying issues. It stuffed us back into a position where we were already hurting.

We can’t flop like necros to dump agro. We can’t jolt like a ranger. Channelling through a mitigating mob is luck. We can pull agro off a non-uber tank with a single DoT at 80% of mob life. We can’t nuke worth a poop. Our buffs (expecting 1 hp buff) are no longer of use.

If you don’t want to post on the shaman boards, fine. How about just replying to this email with something I can post on the shaman boards? Are shaman ‘done’? Are we ‘balanced’ now that our dps and heals are back on a PoP level? (Hint: pretty much all the other classes got a raw dps upgrade with GoD)

I know you guys have been trying to look into shaman, but can you talk to us a bit while you do it?

One last thing--thanks for being the point man! You have a rough job basically fielding tons of angry emails, and I doubt you get anywhere near the credit you deserve for a rough job!

Chutoi
65th Level Shaman of Sol Invictus
