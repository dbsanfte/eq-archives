DevvNull
This thread wasn't getting any love, so I thought I'd chime in. I'm sure most of you are aware of this by now, but a great place to get some of your earlier OOW spells are the expeditions in Dranik's Scar.

You need to talk to the Grand HIstorian who is close to the east zone wall north of the zone-in point. Simply go up to him and say 'caves' and you will be given an instanced expedition. Unlike Vxed, this expedition requires three people to start.

Once you've received the expedition, note the name. You will either receive one called the watering hole, one called the fire pit, or one called the Murkglider's pit (I think). The murkglider instance is the one you want. Once you are in the zone, you will need to traverse the cavern and look for two place-holders: a hideous rat and a tatterwing bat. The zone itself is very large, but it also follow a very straight forward path, so finding these are very easy.

The monsters in the zone consist of rats, bats, and murkgliders. I was doing this mission the other day and our tank commented, "After 68 levels I'm still fighting rats and bats. =(" to which I replied, "And you can't even solo these." Anyways, the rats and bats are non-KOS but are social and will assist against any combat. The murkgliders are KOS but undead, so the best way to find the named PH's are to either get a class that can ITU or stock up on potions. Once you find the first PH (most likely the hideous rat), you can kill spawns to get to the second PH which is roughly two rooms away. This provides some relief for the boredom that will set in. Alternatively, you can just wait for the repops to occur as the tunnels between rooms are completely safe.

The spawn time is eighteen minutes which is a nice amount of time and should give you a sufficient number of attempts at the re-pop. Named mobs at this level are pretty easy; they hit for the 500ish range and are completely slowable. I've heard mention that if a lvl 68 person initiates the expedition that the named are harder and have different loot tables, but I haven't experienced this first hand. Anyone have any information on this?

Anyways, I hope this has helped you all in your spell farming efforts. Personally, I still have yet to receive one of these spells, but I've seen enough to convince myself that I need to start three-boxing. :cool:
