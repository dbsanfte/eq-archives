I haven't really been a fan of the latest writer for mobhunter, but she raised an interesting point in her most recent post (http://mobhunter.com/000031.html).

Basically she argues that five phrases in EQ are should be taken out of use because they have many different meanings for people:

Uber: The German word for Over describes high end characters, guilds, equipment, or abilities. We hear about uberguilds and ubermobs. We use the word as insult or praise when a friend acquires a new shiny item. Uber is anything more powerful than you. Some 65 paladins with 300 AA points don't consider themselves uber. Level 25 rangers tear through Crushbone like a tornado and everyone in the zone considers them uber. There is no specific use of the word that helps describe a particular state of the game, guilds, or players. In almost every Mobhunter article I wrote for my first three months, my editor, the uber wizard Azile Ciricbane, had to edit out my use of uberguild and told me to replace it with something more specific.

Casual: Casual is the most misunderstood and misused phrase in the Everquest community. There are casual guilds who raid the Elemental Planes. Some casual players have hundreds of AA points and play 40 hours a week. Everyone has a different definition of a casual gamer. Sometimes it means low level. Other times it means non-raiders. Instead of asking for changes for the casual gamer, we need to describe exactly what it is we desire. Do we need more short-duration events? Do we need less locked content? Do we need higher power equipment available to single LDON / Ornate equipped level 65 groups? Avoid the word casual, speak in detail.

Mudflation: This ancient term gets used any time someone sees the game progress and doesn't like it. Mudflation means little but has a built-in wrapper of negativity that lets us label progression like a package of spoiled meat. Few describe exactly what they mean when they use the term and fewer can describe how to fix it. The game gets larger. The high-end zones get higher. Level limits increase. Equipment grows in power. That isn't mudflation, that's progress.

Timesink: This is one of my favorites. Timesinks describe any action in Everquest that someone doesn't enjoy. Everquest is a timesink. Reading a book is a timesink. I know one perfect way to avoid timesinks. If you aren't enjoying what you do, do something else. Before you begin telling me that you HAVE to camp shards to get intoVex Thal or you HAVE to get your Ring of Scale faction up, remember that this entire game is voluntary and the world is big enough to find a million other and often more enjoyable things to do. If you find a particular event or hunt that bores you to tears, instead of labeling it a timesink, just tell SOE what you don't like about it. Speak in details, not overused and meaningless generalities.

Class Balance: Classes, by definition, cannot be balanced. Every class is good at some things and bad in others. There is no way to balance the wide variety of skills from one class to another. How does one compare the blasts of a wizard to the slows of a shaman to the healing of a cleric? With limited combat interaction it becomes difficult to make every one of the sixteen classes unique yet not required for a group. SOE can tweak class abilities and help support those unique roles. SOE can also tune encounters so they work well with particular class archetypes as they did with knights and the Ikkinz trials. There is never a clear solution for class balance and there is no conclusion. Classes cannot be balanced without ruining the unique skills that each class has to offer.


Here on the Crucible we use these phrases, but sometimes people have very different ideas of what they mean. IMHO, "casual" means any player who only plays less than 15 hours a week. This (on my server at least) prevents such players from joining the most serious raid guilds, and thus implies a not being in an "uber" guild. "Class Balance" is just a matter of desirability. If you can never find a member of class A (*cough* clerics *cough) and class B always has five level 65's lfg, then those classes aren't blalanced. "Mudflation," to me is when I see a level 15 shaman with a full set of jaundiced bone armor he bought of a guy in the bazaar for two plat, three copper, and a rat ear. It's not a bad thing, just the reality of a game that grows.

"Timesink" to me is when players are prevented from experiencing content by unrelated hurdles. For example, some old end-game zone key quests required you to kill hundreds of green mobs, or kill the same green mob every X minutes, to get a peice of the key. Now soloing greens has very very little to do with high-end raiding, but Sony connected them to slow people from getting to these zones. Finally, I agree with Loral that "uber" has a different meaning depending on your power level, I think most people understand that when you refer to an uber guild, you mean a guild that raids end-game zones (similiarly an uber raider is someone in such a guild, and uber loot is loot that comes from these zones).

These definitions seem obvious to me, but I wonder if other people might have very different definitions. So while we all wait for the servers to come up, how do you define these terms?

Stragak
Uber: When a player passes the point from "game enjoyment for the sake of enjoyment itself" to "obsessive desire to have the mostest, bestest things". It is possible to enjoy the game AND have the best stuff, but its very rare. It is also entirely possible for someone to get great enjoyment BY having all the stuff but these are the ones that go out and start slaughtering kobolds at level 65 for steamfont water, thereby ruining the game for other players between level 2-7 that could enjoy killing them.

Casual: A player that plays to play (have fun). They log in, ready themselves, play, log out and continue life. They most certainly will group if possible, but won't give up sleep, call in sick to work or skip school to be able to raid. Casual games may in time have great stuff, but are almost never "uber".

Mudflation: The sudden "over-valuation" of items when it is discovered that they have a new use, exploit (remember shivs and tribute?). It can also cover the sudden overpricing of items in an attempt to earn enough platinum to buy the newly-discovered "uber" item that has started appearing in the bazaar.

Timesink: Anything that causes a player to spend long amounts of time doing something they feel they need to do but would rather be doing something else. Quests, camping for plat, etc all can fall under this.. even working on faction as well, even if the end result seems worth it.

Class Balance: The desire to have an ability or feature of another class without having to actually BE that class.

Mudflation - the tendency of developers/GMs to introduce more powerful items into a game, leading to a devaluation of older gear and its connected content.

As mentioned above this is not a new term but stems from the times of the text based muds. Even in P&P you encounter this kind of development. And actually, there is little you can do as a designer/GM to stop it. People are unhappy with things like item wipes. So to keep people playing (and having fun playing), you make new, better items and dangle them in front of the players. Because, face it, someone who plays a fantasy roleplaying game just LOVES big flashy toys. If you ever GMed a P&P RPG and you have seen the glee of a player getting a new toy ...

/sigh straying from topic again :P

Until now I do not know of any RPG that has managed to circumvent Mudflation. And I do not think you can.

Just a quick word why most people think Mudflation is evil. It enhances the gap between players just based on gear and produces envy from the haveless people (which may be at any level). To solve this problem more powerful items are introduced into the game and , well, guess what, more mudflation occurs.

The solution? There isn t really one. Developers/GMs need to be aware of the problem and try to find a balance between enjoyable upgrades and the speed of Mudflation. In EQ this balance is sometimes kinda ok, (LDoN + Aug - EP gear), sometimes its not (LDoN + Aug - GoD). No idea how OoW will turn out.
