Jinheim
A druid would fit your requirements quite well, but a lot of shamans have an aversion to druids - I don't know how you feel about them. They solo well and are desired in lower-level groups because of their healing and sow abilities (remember: lowbies need sow!). You are also very self-sufficient with your heals, sow, and ports.

A mage would work as well. I had a mage alt that I leveled to about 20, and I had some fun with him. They can solo just fine once you get your tactics down, but some levels are a lot harder than others (right before you get your new pets are bad levels).

Enchanters will always get a group, and learning to solo with one can be quite a challenge and very fun. Charm is fun.

Necro won't get many groups, but you are an excellent soloer and very self-sufficient.

Wizard could work as well. Their damage isn't very good at low levels compared to a twink melee, so they won't be that desired in groups, but you solo well once you get AE snare at 29.

LaughingWoman
Ewww, druid. ;) In the 51 levels that I got her through, I finally learned that I hate playing a druid. (yep, I'm slow). Really practical toon, though. Making money in RM is a breeze when you can track the giants before the farming casters get to em. But quadding was really only fun for me the first dozen or so quads. After that, there just wasn't any excitement anymore. And how many times did I forget what toon I was on and try to snare IN TEH FACE? Waaaay too many.

Mage is probably my personal pick for an alt. Summoning means you don't need to fiddle around too much with buying gear (or can focus on fewer slots) and really get to see the joy of focus items. Mages can solo equally well at varying speeds, too. You can either take on tough stuff, burn em down, have a challenge, and read a book during the downtime -- OR you can go slow and steady on blues for days on end. Mage pets do silly amounts of damage and need only a buff or two every so often. The hard part is remembering to do a point of damage so the pet doesn't eat half the exp...

Haven't played a necro, but every time I've grouped with one (other than the moron or two), they've been a tremendous asset to the group. Usually they're not LFG, so it's hard for folks to see what all they can do, but with a good necro and the right mobs, you'll start laughing at the piddly mana regen on C3 alongside em.

As much fun as bards are to play, they're just so busy, I was more tempted to play an alt where I could relax a bit - shamans are high-maintenance enough as it is.

Definitely mage.
