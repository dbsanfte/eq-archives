Loramin
Short answer:

SCM3 is as good as an Obby for Alteration spells, and only 1/4 as good as an obby for non-Alteration spells.

Long Answer:

Despite Zyphyr's claim that there are no checks, Caster's Realm (http://eq.crgaming.com/misc/pages/special.asp) thinks differently. According to it, specialization involves checks!

However, in this case Caster's Realm is just repeating Verant's line (explained at fan faires and in posts to various non-shamanic boards where the developers don't just lurk) , so I recommend checking out Graffe's Wizardly FAQ at: http://www.graffe.com/graffe1.0/library/wizardly_faq.htm . Scroll down through section "2. Spells" a bit until you get to the question on specialization, and then read up on the wonders of what packet-sniffers can uncover. Or, if you are lazy, just re-read it here:

"Q: What is 'Specialisation' ('Specialization')
A: This is an interesting one. Its an example of Verant's description having little resemblance to reality. They claim that you have a percantage chance when you cast for this skill to kick in and save you some of the mana cost. The chance is meant to be governed by you skill in specialisation for that given discipline of specialisation.

Q: What are the 'Specialisation' skill caps?
A: One discipline may be up to skill level 200 dependant on your level, whilst the rest are capped at 50.

Q: So what really happens with 'Specialisation'?
A: You save a percantage of the spell cost on every cast, the amount contingent upon your specialisation skill in that discipline.

Q: How do you know? Thats not what I heard!
A: Because someone used a packet sniffer to check it out (See ShowEQ).

Q: How much do I save per cast with a 'Specialistation' at 200?
A: 10%.

Q: And at 50?
A: 2.5%. (Can you see a pattern? <grin>)

Q: What should a wizard choose as his primary specialisation?
A: Evocation of course!"

After we ammend that last one to say Shaman/Alteration, we should prolly add this or some paraphrase thereof to our "Tips from the Shaman's Crucible". But I digress. The point is normally you save as much on your primary spells from specialization as you would from an obby shroud, and a quarter of that on your non specialized spells.

Now the explanation of SCM according to Verant is the following (taken from Caster's Realm):

"[SCM ] increases the chance that a player will successfully get a specialization roll. For a full explanation on specialization read our editorial here. The first point increases the chance of specialization by 5%, the second raises the chance to 15% and the third to 30%."

So there are two possibilities here. One, Verant secretly fixed specialization to work the way they always said it did, and they did this back just before SoL (when SCM was introduced). There's a good chance that the person who did the research for Gaffe's FAQ did it pre-SoL, so this isn't an entirely impossible theory. However, the second possibility is that VI is full of it, and that SCM just increases the amount of mana you save from specialization. This is how it works according to Zyphr, and by his account it works out to be 20% total for SCM3 in your primary skill, or in other words:

SCM 3 + Spec(200) about= 20%

Now, if VI is telling even half the truth, SCM3 should involve a 30% somewhere, but without using really random formulas I can't make 30% change 10% to 20%. So what seems far more likely to me is just that SCM follows the same progression as most other AA skills, at least purportionaly. By this logic, SCM3 (30%) should be twice as good as SCM(2) (15%), and six times as good as SCM1 (5%). So applying that back to are previous formula, we can interpret that:

SCM 1 + Spec(200) about= 15%
SCM 2 + Spec(200) about= 11.66%
SCM 1 + Spec(50) about= 5%
SCM 2 + Spec(50) about= 3.75%
SCM 3 + Spec(50) about= 2.9%

So to answer your original question:

Obby (or any other Mana Preservatio item) = Spec(200) = 4xSpec(50) = SCM3+Spec(200) = 2x SCM3(50) = 10%

Or in real words, being level 40 and having your skills maxxed means you save 10% on Alteration and 2.5% on everthing else. If you pick up a mana preservation item on top of that, you get to save an additional 10% on all spells, bringinging you up to 20%/12.5%. If you then go get SCM3 you'll save an additional 100% on Alteration spells, and additional 2.5% on your other spells, bringing you up to 30%/15%. Alternatively, if you just have SCM3, level 40+, and maxxed skills, but no Mana Preservation item, then you have 20%/5%.

So, SCM3 is as good as an Obby for Alteration spells, and only 1/4 as good as an obby for non-Alteration spells :)

Since I'm a someone, I'll start a first draft:

"How does specialization work?

Well, there's two school's of thought on this: Verant's, and the correct one. The school of thought proposed by Verant, and outlined/explained at "http://eq.crgaming.com/misc/pages/special.asp" is that specialization is a set of skills, one of which is invoked every time you cast a spell (once you've reached the appropriate level, 30 for shaman), and depending on which spells you cast the most, one school will eventually reach a higher skill (200) than the others, which will cap at (50). So far so good.

Then we get to the incorrect part. Verant claims that when you cast a spell the computer makes a roll, and depending on your specialization skill you may or may not "succeed" at the roll. If you do succeed, then your spell will have reduced mana cost, and fizzle rate.

However, many dutiful parsers and users of ShowEQ have diligently run tests to determine how specialization, and the conclusions they came up with are summed up nicely at "http://www.graffe.com/graffe1.0/lib...izardly_faq.htm" (under section "2. Spells"). In brief, they determined that there is no roll for specialization, and in fact that it simply works all the time. For your primary (skill 200) specialization, you save 10% mana on all spells you cast from that school. For your secondary (50) skills you get a 2.5% reduction in mana cost.

To their credit, Caster's Realm does have a few useful points on Specialization:

"Specialisation does not increase chance of recovering after interruption. This is based purely on Channelling. Specialisation does not make spells cast faster. This is a fixed amount."

But what about Spell Casting Mastery (SCM)?

According to Verant, the AAXP skill Spell Casting Mastery works as follows (borrowed from Caster's Realm):

"[SCM ] increases the chance that a player will successfully get a specialization roll. The first point increases the chance of specialization by 5%, the second raises the chance to 15% and the third to 30%."

However, as we already know there are no specialization checks in Everquest, so how does this ability really work? Well, according to reports from The Crucible, SCM:3 adds 10% reduction to all spells cast. However, one report from Hannduvdeth showed that for him, one casting of Khura's Focusing MGB style 51%, as opposed to 44% after SCM3. This would only seem to indicate a savings of 7%, so assuming Hannduvdeth has his specialization(alteration) maxxed at 200 (a fairly safe assumption) there might be as much as a 3% loss, possibly only on MGB spells. Then again this 3% difference might have come from equipment changes in Hann's gear in between his getting the skill as well. The moral of this story is that SCM3 probably gives 10% to all castings, including MGB, but your mileage may vary :-)

Now, what about SCM2 and SCM1? We don't have any hard figures on this, but if you assume that these skills follow the same progression as Verant claims they do (5/15/30), then SCM1 gives about 1.7% savings, and SCM2 gives 5%. However, to date there have been no tests done (on the Crucible, that I know of; if anyone knows of info from another board feel free to post a link and/or quote it)."
