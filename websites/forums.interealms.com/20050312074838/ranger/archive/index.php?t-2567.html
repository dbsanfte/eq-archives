It really sucks that so many people are interupted by the patche schedules, and especially that it's the same people over and over and over again, but you need to keep some things in mind...

First, the SCHEDULED patches are at those times for a reason, that is when the servers usually have the lowest number of players logged in. The option is to block 10k people or to block 200k people, the obvious choice is the 10k group. It sucks that it's usually the same people over and over again, but that's the way it is unless you can get everyone else to start playing during that time.

Second is the issue about them having to bring the servers down at all, when other games don't. Well think about this, EQ has been out for 3 years now, and was in developement for a couple of years before that. At the time the game was developed, this is how server updates were done. The whole system was designed the way the technology at the time allowed it to be done. All of these NEW games that everyone compares it to are NEW, they use NEW technology, new technology that lets them update servers without bringing them down. I bet that SWG is using this new techology that keeps the servers up most of the time. And I'm sure that EQ2 will have that same always-online ability, but the current EQ is simply not designed to handle that.

And just for FYI, almost all of the patches for the last 3 or 4 months have been on wednesdays. That just seems to be the day they use now. Originally, when the game first came out, it was usually on tuesdays. Then for a while it was usually on wednesdays, then on thursdays. Now it's back to wednesdays. In actuality, wednesdays make a lot of sense, they get 2 days to prepare, and then after the patch they have a couple of days fix unexpected major issues before half the country shuts down for the weekend and pounds their servers into oblivion with increased activity.
