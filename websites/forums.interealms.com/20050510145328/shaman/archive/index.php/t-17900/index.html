OK, here's the thing. I have a 58 shaman alt, on another server. I don't like to solo, although I'm quite good at it. I can do it happily if I'm close to an important level, as I'm feeling highly motivated then. But otherwise, I don't like to solo.

Since I only play him sporadically, he has no regular friends to group with. So any time I want a group, I have to go for a random pickup group. So what to do?

Solo WHILE LOOKING FOR GROUP. If your reaction to not being able to quickly find a group is to log off, you never (or rarely) find a group. Play for an hour and you'll likely find a group. Or as someone else suggested, find someone to duo with, and try to find a group for both of you. Or get a duo partner, and go somewhere like PoN or PoV (depending on class/level of partner), and duo while you're looking for more groupmembers.

If you can find a spot to start that two can get exp, and just keep adding people as you find them, you can have a group.

You should also consider trying odd group compositions/strategies. If there's 5 shamans in PoV, great, grab all of them for a group. If you can add one of any snare class to that, you'll have a fantastic aggro kiting group.

If the ONLY thing you like doing is six person dungeon crawls, then you only have one option. Find some friends that you can play with, and group with them as much as possible. It might take a little time, but build up a friends list of people who like to dungeon crawl and have similar play times. A competent group at L 58 does NOT need a cleric and enchanter to do LDoN. In fact, you don't need either one. I've had some very good LDoN groups with two shamans, no bard, no enchanter, no cleric, no druid. The best LDoN group I've had, actually, was 2 shamans, 2 rangers, a rogue and a wizard. Because the players were all good - amazing for a pickup group. But if you can find some competent players to play with, a perfect group is not required.
