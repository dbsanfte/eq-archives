Kriel Darkbane
Just go to the new guild. It sounds like a horrible evil thing to do when it's a new concept for you, but the reality is that it's just how the game works. After you've played a while, you'll see this from every angle. It just never works to stay in a guild you're not happy in. At some point, almost everyone wants to do bigger and better things than they've done so far, and very often the only way to do it is in a new guild.

Just make sure you stay friends with your old guildmembers. That shouldn't be hard if your wife stays with them. In reality, all your old guild does is exp groups, right? You can still do exp groups with them without the guildtag. I'm now completely separated guild-wise from nearly all my closest EQ friends, but I still do exp groups with people I've played with since L 20, or L 30, or L 40 (or even L NINE in one case), and I"m not guilded with any of them any more.

went thru a similar thing where I left my small guild for one of the Uber guilds - I stayed 6 months and was very unhappy - and rejoined my 'friend' guild.

I think its important to realize that as a shaman, you don't need the support of an uber guild to acquire practically anything in the game if you have enough patience, but the guild support of close friends is hard to replace.

My friend guild averages around a dozen lv 55ish people, for which I am one of 2 shamans. For my Uber guild, I was the non-60 shaman in a guild with maybe 3-4 60 shamans and a number of lower ones - and I can say that its much more fun to be THE shaman on a raid, knowing that youre needed and active, than just another gimp out there to cast the crap buffs on a dragon raid and maybe toss out some heals for group 6.

For me, not only are friends more important than end game content, but you'll actually do better in some respects since friends are more likely to sit there questing stuff with you cause theyre your friends and they know that they're screwed on a raid if your the only shaman and youre poorly equipped.
