BlargX
Oh sweet jebus! Don't even let them BEGIN to think that they're right on that score. Here's why. More than one reason so read on if you think I'm crazy.

Mages are weak and puny and scared. Almost all of them in my experience will not cast mala until the last possible second. Often not even till after malo is cast. Low aggro? They don't necessarily care.

To be fair, mages die easy. To be realistic, mala isn't gonna get anyone killed. But try getting that into the head of someone who has gone 50 or 60 levels doing two things: staying the HELL out of danger because he's a caster with garbage for armor and pitiful hitpoints AND IS THE ONLY PURE CASTER WITH NO ROOT, and ramping up his ego on DD blasts so he can crow about all the damage he did while saving up mana for it because he's doing nothing else.

I've seen huge arguments on the tiniest expenditures of mana from mages, since they want to rain spell and blast everything whenever possible, yet still want to suffer no consequences for anything they do. In short, the lamers among them are thinking DD full bore, and frankly have no desire to risk anything. It's often not, does the raid succeed, but -- Do I shine? Especially if there's a shaman around or an enchanter around to do the dying for them.

Ok, YOUR guild mages never shy from casting mala when needed. Never. Ever. Mine either. Ok, so we're agreed? I'd also like to mention that I happen myself to be not only admirable but perfect. Now that that's settled, let's move on.

Anyone with real solid experience in the reasonably high end game or anywhere close knows how razor-thin margins can be. And has plenty of personally disturbing memories of how long the corpse recoveries can be -- you know, the kind of memories you fear will somehow pass into your sperm and warp the minds of your future children. Mala is ONLY a bit less debuff than malo? Means little to nothing?

Baloney. It means EVERYTHING.

So much so that you shouldn't even be buying malo yourself -- your guild should be buying it for you. It does a LOT less for you than it ever will for your guild, by a mile. You need it for very few experience mobs, but they need it for almost EVERY mob they raid, whether the mob can be slowed or not.

Any shaman with malo is a god-send to his guild. Malo vaults them to an entirely different level. Malosini? It is to laugh. Often can't stick without malo.

Malo is a very low aggro spell, but the horrible thing about it is that it costs both time and mana both. It brings the shaman into aggro range and keeps him there. While chanters are playing chicken with tashani cuz they don't want to die(or heroically doing tashani and THEN dying) and your bards are or are not playing occlusion of sound(and many raids for many guilds don't even have a bard who can do that), your shaman is out there heroically burning mana on malo and then possibly malosini while right in the mob's face and then compounding his troubles by casting slow(or, god forbid, plague of insects). Meanwhile, the clerics are concentrating on the warrior tanking the mob. Shamans have torpor right? So they never die....? Mmm hmmm. Sure.

Maybe the only needs to cast slow once to make slow work, or he needs to cast it three times and then dies. Who knows or cares? The shaman's job is to die when necessary to get the slow in. And we die a LOT.

If your guild needs mobs slowed NOW and doesn't want to have to be held hostage to both the courage of mages and the courage AND survival of chanters, a lot of responsibility and a lot of death is going to fall on the head of the shaman. Unless a monk or SK dies on the pull, the shaman will almost always die more than anyone in the raid on slowable mobs unless your clerics suck or you just don't have enough of them. Your shaman will play an incredible role in raids on slowable mobs with both malo and slow.

Does he DESERVE malo? Hell yes, if your guild deserves malo, that is. Does your guild NEED malo? Even if you have 800 copies of mala? Try a few tough mobs and get back to me.

I know a guy who auctioned for malo for like four months. It was almost as pitiful as my auctioning for torpor. You should have seen the prices he was talking about!! Certain things don't really have a price, though, do they? Or should they? Frankly, if you need something and it costs 10x more than it should to buy it but it's your only chance, what do you do? Probably buy it no matter what the consequences, or be happy living forever without. A shaman without malo sucks compared to one he does for raids. You WILL be notably less valuable without it to any guild or any raid. Brutal fact, but that's it.

If you ever get it, do not even dream of ditching it for any price. Not even 10x what it's worth. There are literally people who quit the game because they can't get key rare spells like this one. They're not necessarily quitters by nature. Would you quit the game for lack of a fungi? A rune etched icewurm fang? 20 fangs? Money is worthless sometimes. You will never be the shaman you want to be without your key spell, and malo and focus are the KEY raid and guild spells for a shaman. (Torpor being the key solo/group spell -- focus is right up there too, but 1000x easier to get than torpor.)
