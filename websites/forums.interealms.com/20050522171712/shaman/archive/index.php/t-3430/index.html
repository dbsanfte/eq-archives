It's not the popular answer, but I think it's the best one: Buying and selling.

You can make more money in a shorter period of time doing that than anything else. Hang out in the EC tunnel, open up the Trade channel, buy low, and sell high.

Ideally you want to trade in items that sell fairly frequently (so you're not stuck holding inventory for a long time) and for a defined range (varies by server- so you'll have to do your own homework.) For example, buy an FBSS for 3k and sell it for 4k. In my tunnel rat days, I'd often use my own gear as stuff to sell, if I was sure I could buy it back fairly quickly. I knew I could buy a set of JB arms for 500 pp in an hour or two, so if I saw "WTB Shaman Gear" I'd send a tell offering my arms for 700 pp.

There are, of course, some tricks (don't buy and sell with the same character, prices vary by timezone- sometimes it's better to do your buying early in the morning and your selling during primetime, or vice versa... etc.) but you get the general idea.

Yup, it sucks compared to killing stuff... but where else are you going to make that kind of money at level 47? :)

47 is the level where it starts to become viable to solo Ice Giants in Everfrosty at the entrance to Permafrosty. You do not need great gear, but you do need to be moderately competent. At level 47, it will be fairly slow going - you will probably have to Root 'n Rot, as it is unlikely that you have good enough gear to pet n tank. By level 50, pet n tank is much more viable, and faster.

Root n Rot
Successful root n rot requires that you make good use of the terrain. As you pass under the portico into the Courtyard, there are 2 Icengiants located in alcoves to the north and south. If you pull either giants (with malosi) and immediately run out of the portico and run diagonally (i.e. if you pull the south IG, run out of the portico, and then run southwest), the IG will become stuck in the wall for approximately 10 seconds - plenty of time to stop and cast your first root spell. You can then apply your DoTs (stack as many as you can). By the time you have stacked 2 or 3 dots, the root will break, but the giant will probably be stuck in the wall for long enough to get off another root. After that, had south, and use the abandoned fallen tower to repeat the trick of getting a wall between you and the mob - this buys you the extra time you need to get a root spell cast without getting pounded. Note you will probably need to risk sitting and even cannidancing while the IG is rooted, in order tog et enough mana to finish the IG off.

Pet n tank
If your gear is good enough, it is more efficient to pet n tank. However, I would strongly recommend that you have at least 800AC (self buffed) and preferably 850AC - if you dont have 800+, try this at your own risk.
Make pet, buff with Talisman of Tnarg (or HoS if you have), Shifting Shield, chloro, haste
Buff self with same (and inner fire, stam, agi)
Pull IG with best slow spell, and immediately sic pet on IG (you may wish to pull as per Root n Rot, because it will take a while for pet to get aggro from IG - at this stage, stay out of melee range. at a safe distance sit and med/cannidance to balance hp and mana. Monitor pet's health (use "/pet report health" hotkey), heal pet a couple of times if necessary. if pet gets to 25% health, and you are less than 25% mana join the melee - the IG will aggro on you immediately. Melee IG until it turns or you are at 25% hp. At 25%hp, back away, let dogdog get aggro, heal self and rejoin (hopefully before dogdog dies).

IGs drop between 5 and 60 plat woth of loot - typically coin, fine steel weapons and gems. On average, I would expect to get about 30-35 plat per kill.
