Deian
I read the WoT series also... got stuck in the middle of book 8 and cashed out. Jordan's books are good, but after a while they begin to feel like a soap opera, with him dragging the story out for no reason that I can see.

But if you like the viking era, I suggest you pick up the 3-book cycle (all 3 combined are the size of ONE Jordan book!) by Harry Harrison: The Hammer and The Cross, One King's Way, and King and Emperor.

The books are alternate history: what would happen if, in the middle ages when the Holy Roman Empire of the Germans which spanned Germany, France and a bit of Italy and worked to basically convert everyone to Christianity had opposition? In this alternate history, a strong king unites the Viking nations (Sweden, Norway, England, etc) into an empire to rival the Christian one and which is based on the Norse religion, though it tolerates others. Sound intriguing? Heck, what if that Norse empire united itself with the Muslin empire to the south of the Christians, who in turn ally with the East Orthodox Byzantine Empire?

Everything includes huge doses of knight vs. Viking battles & skirmishes, a LOT of detailed info on the Viking society, and is easy to read. It's worth it to pick up the first book in paperback & judge for yourself.
