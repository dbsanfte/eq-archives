snapdragn
Haste: enchanter haste is better.

Slows: Shaman slows are better at the high end, during the midlevels it fluctuates a bit depending on the various levels. we get our best slow at lvl 51, chanters have to wait until lvl 57.

Debuffs: both classes have identical melee debuffs (str, agi, AC, etc). Enchanters get a unresistable Magic Only debuff. we get a resistable Magic, Fire, Cold, Poison debuff. So generally ours is superior. But at your current level it's not a big thing.

Roxxanne
In most experience group settings, it is expected that:

1) the shaman will slow, not the chanter, because we can take the beating from an aggro'ed mob much better than the chanter

2) let the cleric AC buff, because his are better, but shaman covers all other stat buffs (our HP buffs stack with cleric ones, even where his are better)

3) the chanter will haste because his are better, but you must haste if no chanter unless bard around, then discuss with the bard who will haste.

4) discuss whether shammy, druid or ranger will keep up SOW, until you get group SOW then you do it for added ATK buff on ours

5) consensus is that the disempower/listless power/incapacitate/cripple line, shared at same levels with chanter, is a waste of mana until you get to high level (50+) mobs that flurry, etc.

6) discuss with druid who will keep up regen/chloroplast/regrowth on the tanks taking damage, where needed

7) where magic resist debuffing needed, chanter will lead with their unresistable debuff (tash line) then shammy or mage (discuss, but try to get mage to do it to save you mana) will hit with malise/malisement/malosi/malosini line.

8) finally, best tip for working with chanters--never DOT a mob which may need to be mezzed! You just made the mob unmezzable.

Roxxanne sums it up pretty well, but I'll add a caveat - what she says is the IDEAL way, but depending on mana and your other responsibilities, you have to be flexible. i.e. if there is no cleric and you are acting as healer, by all means let the enchanter slow.

And do touch base with the enchanter, mage, cleric, whatever where you know your abilities overlap to make sure that THEY understand where the line is, too.

Also, if there's no chanter, you will generally be on root-park duty (or kite-park if there's room). Your slow spell is a good taunt to get the mob on you, and if that doesn't work, DOT them.
