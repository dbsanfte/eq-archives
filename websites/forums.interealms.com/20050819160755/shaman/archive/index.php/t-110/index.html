Anyways, regarding slow agro. I also noticed a bit of an increase myself. I have refrained from commenting on this, as I have been fighting mostly in CT and we all know that agro is just well, screwed in there.

So, my group went back to ToV to do HoT again, for the first time since the introduction of the changes to Slow spells. I have a bit of data on this that I can share...not real scientific, but slightly better than my usual ravings.

A little background.

I depend on the warrior or the bard in my group to proc tash for me, we have no enchanter. The warrior dual wields jsticks until tash procs, then switches to a Frostbringer and some 12/18 or 11/17 weapon combo.

So, Warrior tags and starts positioning. I jump in fast and lead with Malo. Bard plays occlusion, with just epic, I chain cast Malosini, which usually doesn't stick until after tash. When Malosini sticks, then I immediately turgurs. The rest of the melee folks come in when the first CHeal is cast, which is generally right before/same time as slow. So like 20-30 seconds into the fight... Note this stuff takes us 4-6 minutes to kill, generally.


Session 1. (March 13/14 - pre patch)
60 shaman, 60 monk, 60 bard, 59 rogue (2boxed by me), 58 cleric, 57 warrior.

During Session 1, I landed slows as fast as 19, 22 and 23 seconds after I started casting Malo and gained no agro.

One mob I had 3 resists in 60 seconds of the beginning of the fight and I did not gain agro.

On 3 mobs I had resists on Turgurs less than, or exactly 30 seconds from the start of the fight and took no agro.

We fought 17 mobs total this session.



Session 2. (March 23 - post patch)
60 shaman, 60 bard, 60 monk, 60 ranger, 59 warrior (2 levels higher now), 58 cleric.

Fastest slow was 22 seconds, which resulted in me gaining 3 rounds of agro.

I gained 1 round of agro on a 26 second slow.

No agro from 28 second slow...

I gained 3 rounds of agro on a resist at 31 seconds.

1 round of agro on a resist at 48 seconds.

Fought 12 mobs this time.


So, it is still anecdotal evidence... however, it is pretty clear to me that I was getting more agro on my last trip. With our warrior being 2 levels higher I was anticipating having less problems.

In a nutshell.

Trip 1 - slows at 19-23 seconds = no agro
Trip 2 - slows at 23, 26 seconds = 3, 1 rounds of agro

Trip 1 - 1 resist at ~30 seconds = no agro
Trip 2 - 1 resist at 31, 48 seconds = 3, 1 rounds of agro.


But then, if we want to prove things.. I could also use these same logs to show that Turgurs is easier to land now, than pre-patch. So take it with a grain of salt.

Trip 1 = 12 Turgurs Resists, 17 mobs.
Trip 2 = 2 Turgurs Resists, 12 mobs.


Proof of absolutely nothing... but something to think about it. :)
