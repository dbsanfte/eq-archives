Warrior : of the three, has the least overall usefulness in a Duo sitiation. Complete lack of utility. All he really provides is Damage and a Taunt button. Mage : Biggest potential damage output of the 3. Loads of utility eventually (not a whole lot right now but the mage needs more levels anyway). go with the warr. I hydra a shaman/warr myself. and it works perfect.

Why?

Since i'm controlling *both* chars, i know *exactly* when the spells about to go off. I watch the "casting" bar... and just as the spell goes off, I hit the taunt button on the warrior.

There's an impression out there that warriors cannot hold aggro. This is complete nonsense. What's happening, is warriors hit the taunt button ever time it pops up. That's bad, and get's casters killed. You have to learn *when* to hit the taunt button.

Another reason for the warrior. "/disc Defensive" owns. When you're by yourself, this will give you lots and lots of time to get the mob slowed and situated before worrying about heals. At L62, my warriors defensive disc timer was like, 6 minutes or somthing. When popped, it would last the whole fight, plus a little extra. Mobs that generally hit for 400+ hit me for about 80 when the disc was up.

Since your pretty much set to start playing immediatly when you hydra like that, you can start trolling for all the classes that get left out of groups, but are just *perfect* for your situation. I invite as many necro's, wizzy's, and mages that i can. Everyone wants all the shammy's and cleric's out there. And totally overlook the sheer hell casters can unleash.
