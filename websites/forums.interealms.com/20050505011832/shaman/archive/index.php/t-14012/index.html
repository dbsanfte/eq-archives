Xev_Grammar
Just out of curiosity, what is the nature of spells when you zone? I know that when you debuff or dot something, eventually you see a message that says that it has worn off.

But if you cast it and zone out and zone back in, the spell is still there, but you don't get the message anymore.

Does this mean that the spell doesn't belong to you anymore when you zone out and back in?

If so, does that mean you can Breath/Pox/Plague something, zone out, zone in, and Breath/Pox/Plague it again, stacking with yourself?

This sounds like an exploit of course. I'm just really curious about the inner workings of EQ.
Chutoi
Well, I haven't done a lot of experimenting in the area, but I think it leaves the debuffs and strips the agro and DoTs. You can check it for yourself, if you're curious, but roots, malXX line, and slows will hold, as will some others like cripple, etc. You may still get the message that XX has worn off if you zone back in and wait. I've had it happen to me. However, even if you don't get the message, the mobs will still show those effects.

As to the DoTs, the last time I can remember zoning out after dotting was back in Kael when I was trying to get faction by turning in d0rf heads. I had dotted the militia captain down to about 5%, refreshed my dots and rooted, then zoned out and back in. He was still rooted right there, but was full health and undotted.

I can't offer any experiences in PoP or LoY. I will suggest you be careful experimenting, as people are gonna be grumpy if you start parking mobs at ZI/ZO points!

Jaradis
Zoning (or dying) does not remove DoTs or Debuffs. It only removes your agro, the DoTs are still there. I have two accounts, and I can put a DoT on the mob and zone out and still watch with my wizard that the mob is still DoT'd and debuffed (still slowed/rooted).

As for being able to stack the spells... not sure. You would have to watch the rate that the mob drops before and after you cast to see if the rate of drop increases or stays the same, if it increases then they stack, if it stays the same then you just ended up over writing yours.
