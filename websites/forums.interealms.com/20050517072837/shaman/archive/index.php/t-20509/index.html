After testing on a heap of different boxes, I have found the following (personal opinion supplied ....).

Running fine = frame rates adequate for moving around in Raid Situations ..
Lag = frames rates at under about 5 frames per second that really hamper movement.

I have 5 boxes that used to be able to play EQ fine before the DX9 patch, and I since that equates to a lot of hardware, I have been playing with a few different configurations ....

I have a range of CPUs (no AMD's at this stage), but the ' lag ' issue I have shown to be CPU independant (have had EQ running fine on a Celeron 300A post patch) ....

Ok, this is the way I tested ... I had 3 variables, CPU, RAM and Video Card ... I kept 2 variables constant and changed the third .... I use 3 CPUs (Celeron 300A, Celeron 450A, P3 1GHz), 4 RAM settings (128MB, 256MB, 384MB and 512MB) and 4 Video Cards (TnT2, GEForce2MX400, GEFroce4MX440 and GEForce2 GTS) and I tested each in the Bazaar from Nexus zoneline, to the most populated Trader corner, turned around and ran back to Nexus Zoneline without SoW, but with Run3 ..

I get BAD lag on all GEForce2 cards (MX and GTS) when system memory is 256MB or less (no matter what CPU I use) ... if I boost system RAM to 512MB frame rates improve marginally....

GEforce4 MX440 cards with 256MB RAM or less are not as laggy (and are almost playable) and scale as CPU speed increases (unlike the GEforce2 cards) ....

Ok, so from the testing I have done, my conclusion is:

If your system RAM + Video RAM is less then 384MB combined, you will get nasty frame rates in general .....


These are my recommendations to people in different situations for upgrades:
- If you are using a GEForce2 based card, ditch it for a GEForce4 based card (this MAY be tweaked by either SoE or nVidia thru patches / drivers) ..
- If you have less then 512MB RAM and do NOT have a GEForce2, upgrade your RAM.
- If you have a video card that is not nVidia nor ATI (and ya having problems) the cheapest upgrade is a GEForce4 MX440, the best value for money upgrade (assuming you have the CPU power to drive it) is a GEForce FX 5900 XT (cheap and powerful) ...
