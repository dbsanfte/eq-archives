Steelbear
Getting a horse is probably the best way to avoid the sit aggro. You dont even have to get a good horse; just buy the cheapest one solely for use when you are hunting. I dont use mine for travel at all.

Some other tricks that you can use are to shrink yourself and stand right next to or behind the MT. Then if you pull aggro with slow, the tank wont have to chase it down and he can pull aggro back much faster, usually with one taunt.

Or, you can wait until the MT has the mob positioned, root it, then slow it. Long as your root lasts a decent length of time, you can sit down and probably wont have aggro anymore when it does break. Course, some of them will complain about rooting the mob because of proximity aggro...wah.

Kianor
Warrior taunt very much depends on the warrior in question.

If they're dual wielding lammys and hitting the taunt hotkey every time it ungreys, the mob will be ping-ponging around like anything.

If the warrior has worked on their weapons a little, and uses taunt timing astutely, you'll notice the difference.

Some warriors go for the best damage weapons they can afford, they like to do good damage. But smart warriors realise that taking damage is their job, and go for tauntier weapons even if it means dropping their overall damage. And there a fair few good weapons for that now, obtainable by untwinked level 50s.

But please don't slate warriors uniformly - some warriors are better than some paladins or shadowknights. The hybrids have an advantage, but there's still room for skill in the equation - a lot of it is down to the person, not just the class.
