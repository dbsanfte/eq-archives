Here's how I'd fill out that list. Not with a vague listing, but with hard numbers and fleshed out concepts.

1. Healing:
-Daluda's mending: change from 1690 to 1950 hp heal, change recast from 2.25 to 5.

2. Nukes:
-Velium strike & Ice age: reduce cast from 7.8 to 6.5 seconds. Obkin's spear of mending sees mana reduced to 300 from 343 and cast decreased from 4.2 to 3.7.

3. Dots:
-Counters on dots remain, but counters no longer determine agro. Dot agro is coded to be exactly in line with agro from nukes dealing similar damage. Dots will also now check resistances on a per/tick basis rather than when the spell is first cast.
-Ancestral vengeance sees increase in dmg/tick from 546/tick to 595/tick, putting it below the necro's curse, but making it respectable still. All curses see their recast reduced from 30 to 10.

4. Buffs:
-Ferine avatar's and champion's duration increased to 36 minutes and made group spells (non mgb'able). Ferocity 1, 5% damage modifier, and 5 endurance/tick added to FA and Ferocity 2, 10% damage modifier, and 10 endurance/tick added to Champion. Mana cost increases accordingly.
-Fo7 sees shielding 1, 3% dmg modifier (str/dex) and stats cap 55, while Wanvu sees shielding 2, 5% dmg modifier and stats cap 100. Spiritual vigor sees addition of stun resist 1.
-Upgrade to Bih'Li, make it equally un-pronounceable, but give it 63% speed (slightly less than SoE) and 35 attack. Also, Shaman group haste spells see their haste increased to the level of their single target version.

5. AA's:
-Current Alternate Advancement abilities are changed to reflect dots as a viable means of dealing damage, making a critical affliction AA that mirrors fury of magic in every way.
-Rabid bear's duration decreased from 5 to 2 minutes and given a 60 minute timer. Add a no faction kodiak illusion, strikethrough 10, combat effects 10, in addition to its current effects.
-Rabidity (3/6/9) increases duration by 1 minute and decreases recast by 10 minutes per rank on rabid bear. In addition, the 2nd rank gives puppy an innate 30 damage disease based proc. The 3rd rank increases puppy's dex by 75, the proc's damage changes to 75 with a -10 resist mod.

6. Miscellaneous:
-Make shrink mgb'able with a consent option for raids.
-Level 61+ puppies innately dual wield.
-Malo line sees recast changed from 30 to 5.
-EP+ focus effects maintain their power up to level 70. Focus effects like Improved damage, burning affliction, improved healing, etc will see their effects reduced.

That only took me 6 bullets and I gave shamans something to sink their teeth into and test/argue about.

As far as dots resisting on a per/tick basis, I see absolutely no reason why this would be a bad thing. Instead of being forced to recast a dot that was resisted in its entirety, a resist will be reflected in a single tick, which, when you think about it, is the way it should have been implemented. Nukes can be partially resisted, dots should be as well. When the game was young, I can tell you it wasn't a big deal. But today, it's absolutely necessary that the entire dot system be revamped if we hope to remain current.

I'm convinced that this change, combined with a change in the AA system to reflect dots as a relevant means of dealing damage, as well as a change in the way dots generate agro, would make mobs fear us almost as much as they did back when my friends and I were tossing around Ice Comets and Ebolts. Sony is currently 'looking into' dot agro, but for us to see any real changes, those three things must be developed at the same time.

Maybe I'm a sucker for lost causes, but I think reinstating the battle shaman isn't hopeless. A fundamental change in the way we deal damage is all that is necessary. Dots are the lifeblood of this change, but at the same time, adding special combat effects to certain buff lines, specifically offensive effects to a longer duration group FA, would amp the damage of our melee partners. You may not always be dealing that extra damage yourself, but regardless, it is part of our total dps as shamans. Toss in an improved Rabid Bear AA and an improved OoW puppy zerg, which we can use to put out nice burst damage, and I think many complaints we have about damage dealing will subside.

After all that's done, the rest of what we need as a class seems to be a retuning of certain ridiculous items on different spell lines. The recast on malos and our curses, puppy dual wielding, reducing cast time on velium strike, etc. Fundamentally, that will take us about as far as we can go in today's environment. It won't entirely solve how useless many people feel when mobs don't last more than 45 seconds, giving their shaman very little to do to a mob besides malos/slow/nuke. But that's not a shaman problem so much as it is a content design issue for us.

From dictionary.com
beta

/bay't*/, /be't*/ or (Commonwealth) /bee't*/ n. 1. Mostly
working, but still under test; usu. used with `in': `in beta'. In
the Real World, systems (hardware or software) software often go
through two stages of release testing: Alpha (in-house) and Beta
(out-house?). Beta releases are generally made to a group of lucky
(or unlucky) trusted customers. 2. Anything that is new and
experimental. "His girlfriend is in beta" means that he is still
testing for compatibility and reserving judgment. 3. Flaky;
dubious; suspect (since beta software is notoriously buggy).

Historical note: More formally, to beta-test is to test a
pre-release (potentially unreliable) version of a piece of software
by making it available to selected (or self-selected) customers and
users. This term derives from early 1960s terminology for product
cycle checkpoints, first used at IBM but later standard throughout
the industry. `Alpha Test' was the unit, module, or component test
phase; `Beta Test' was initial system test. These themselves came
from earlier A- and B-tests for hardware. The A-test was a
feasibility and manufacturability evaluation done before any
commitment to design and development. The B-test was a
demonstration that the engineering model functioned as specified.
The C-test (corresponding to today's beta) was the B-test performed
on early samples of the production design, and the D test was the C
test repeated after the model had been in production a while.
