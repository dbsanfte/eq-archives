I think there is a difference between druids and shaman on this. I group with druids and rely on them as healers (my guild has a cleric shortage). The druids in my guild will heal, if they have to, but would rather nuke and dot. Most druids that I know "don't care to be a healer."

The thing is Buz, shamans do not have effective nukes to fall back on. We can slow, and act as secondary healers. That is our real the extent of our "capabilities". My healing is based around the idea that I can slow. So I have poor quality heals, since slow makes up for it. This is all well and good until I am put in content where my slow is commonly mitigated, highly resisted, or not very useful because of the length of the fight.

If I am in that situation my prime capability (slowing) is not effective, my secondary capability (healing) is also not effective, because shaman healing was balanced around the idea that I could slow a mob to 75%. This is what screws the shamans that are trying to do high-end content. If our prime skill of slowing is useless, our secondary skill of healing is also useless.

Druids nuke well, and heal well. Shamans nuke poorly, and heal fairly (although if a mob is slowed to 75% fairly is ussualy enough). I can understand a druid not being enthusiastic about healing, if they would rather nuke. Shamans how ever, have never been nukers, and our traditional means of damage (dots) are not strong in the high end content.

When you said

"I'm much more affective using the spells designed for my Druid and Shaman to be concerned about healing a group"

I have to ask what spells those are? If you're picturing a lvl 65 shaman stacking dots on a mob and watching it’s hps melt away, you do not understand. The fights are too short, and shamans have no lure style dots, and so have to debuff before getting dots to stick, future shorting the time that a shaman has to dot. Not to mention the agro that would result from using our faster poison based dots.

What are these "many other capabilities as a Druid or Shaman" that I can do when my slow is ineffective? I guess I can root park, and use heal over times to top off hurt folks hp's after the fight. However so many classes can already root park, and the healing of any hybrid can top folks off.

I've read that shamans do not need more healing, as it would be unbalanced because of all our utility (or “capabilities” in your words). What is this capability or utility that shamans have? I'm invited to groups to slow, and heal. No one has ever invited me to a group and asked me to be the designated inviser, root parker, food summoner, or to buff the party with shrew, or even keep FA up. Slow and heal, those are my shaman’s main functions. Tormac’s pretty good at them. But when my shaman gets in a situation where his slowing is ineffective, his healing also becomes ineffective, and there is no real utility that shamans have for a group outside of this.
