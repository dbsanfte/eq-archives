House
This topic comes up a lot: if you do a search you can probably find a number of posts that pose the same question. The general consensus seems to be that AC/HP is more important than Wisdom. Our spells, particularly slows, generate massive amounts of aggro and, without any kind of memblur ability, we need to be able to survive a beating from angry MOBs. Also, we are able to convert HPs to mana, so that our HP regen, plus the AC necessary to not lose too many HPs when being attacked, is really more important than the size of the mana pool.

There is also another benefit, I think, to going for AC/HP over Wisdom. Many of our AC/HP items are melee-class rejects, and can therefore be had fairly cheaply, or involve uncontested camps, whereas Wisdom items are in high demand by Druids.

Ultimately the Wis/AC thing comes down to how you play EQ.

I'm not even close to being at the level of the game that most of the posters here are but I did/do raid enough to know that I need AC and HP when I end up tanking a PoZ for 3/4 of a fight. About four months ago I couldn't have done that, I was barely self buffing over 800AC and flat out getting smacked when trying to do bigger encounters. Which was fine, I was mostly XP grinding anyway so it didn't really matter. Once I found myself raiding it became a matter of survival. My gear needs changed as the way I played the game changed. I could not do my "job" in a raid with Int caster AC.

Now, at 56, I can self buff to 169Wis with 950+AC (before resists) in gear that doesn't take a raid force to acquire. It is working for me and for how I play the game. Your "balance" will change depending on your level and what you're doing in EQ.

In other words, there is no definate "right" answer to this particular question. The best advice, really, is to not be afraid to adjust your gear one way or the other. Pay attention to what you feel you're lacking in a fight (be that HP, regen, AC, Wis etc.) and then adjust your gear accordingly.

Good luck :)
