Kellenial
I'm sure there will be some disagreement with me on this, but here goes.

Nobody knows your play style and what works for you better than you. The best duo will be the one that best suits your personal style of playing. If kiting's your thing, tailor the duo to that; on the other hand, some find kiting to be boring. If you like battling undead, paladin/cleric. If you like root rotting, pick ones that do that well. I've seen mage/necro and rogue/necro fear kite well. My wife and I started 5 years ago as paladin/enchanter which, while not a great duo group then, has some benefits now, if either of us still played them. We currently play rogue/shaman combo.

Again, to me the "best duo" would be more accentuated by your playstyle and less dependent on the pros and cons of the classes involved.

what is your play style?

Do you want to do lots of ldons?
Bard/Cleric is a log on for instant group spam.

Want to go places and kill things?
SK/Sham is very self sufficient.

Want to be wanted in a guild?
Cleric/Necro or Cleric/Wizzy.

Its a toss up between mage (nuke but have to watch pet occasionally) and wizzy (just nuke every x seconds) that allows you to still be productive whilst attending to the precise timing requirements of a cheal chain.

Want to be a bad arse?
Chanter/Druid is great for charm kiting. Snare and evac make charm kiting much safer.

Chanter druid is also great for trade skilling, go anywhere, track and stupendous charm dps for farming greenies.

Then again, i had a lot of fun running two mages for a while. Send in the pets, when one dies just cast another.

Very decent dps.

Chanter is a good two box participant cause they can mezz. and running away is difficult when your trying to control two chars.

So in summary:
LDONS: Bard/Cleric
Duoing: Sham and SK or Pally or Warr. (the tanking gap between knights and warrs is so great now that warrs while lacking in utility are a far superior damage taker)
Guild: cleric/Nuker
Tradeskills AA whore: Chanter/druid.

Good luck!
