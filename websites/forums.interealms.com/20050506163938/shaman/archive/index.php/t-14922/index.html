My two cents: It's the problem of the person, and not the action. If you can control what you're doing and be accomodating to those who need to use the area, fine, do it.

I also agree on the special properties of HoH -- it's a small zone, it's easy to move around in invis, the graveyard is big, etc.

Here's my checklist though:

1. Can I control my kite?
2. Is there not another reasonable available camp spot?
3. Can I move my kite quickly if I need to to allow people to rez?
4. Can I see an unannounced rez coming beforehand and make allowances?

If you get 4 yes, go ahead.

The problem with the original post was that the idiot in PoNB failed on counts 2 (there's plenty of other places in PoN to kite that are just as good) and 4 (you can't usually see a rez coming in the PoN GY from a mile off) and he was unwilling to do #3 (kiter was just an idiot).

The only time I've killed in a graveyard was root-dotting with 2 druids in HoH. There was nobody else around, all 3 of us had root, we could easily have seen and accounted for an incoming rez, and the zone in area was taken, and we didn't have the firepower to hold anything deeper in, though we could have pulled the mob out to the ZI area if need be.
