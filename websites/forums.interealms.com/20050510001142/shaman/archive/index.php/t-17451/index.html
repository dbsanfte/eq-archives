Shaman's my first character in EQ, and has a lot of memories connected with it, but i'm no longer sure I want to spend time levelling one. Part of this is connected with the fact that, due to timezone, being part of a guild is largely impossible. And even XP groups are rarer than they are for many, there's simply less people on line. Great if you like soloing though.

I have a char on another server (necro) who's elemental flagged in a great guild, so I got to see that side of it, although not as a shaman. Obviously there's guesswork involved. If someone proves to me how completely i've missed the truth I would be *ecstatic*. I'm L55 with my shaman, but group with lots of L65 shaman with my necro (And the guild shaman are very skilled).

I'm not really trying to prove anything, just putting down my thoughts.

Shaman on Raids: Very Desirable.
Stackability: Average.

Raid targets are very magic resistant and have massive DPS. Thus the combination of malo and the best slow makes shaman's more or less mandatory. The shaman HP buffs, while not the most important (Cleric, Druid, Shaman, Paladin) stack nicely and are thus part of the standard buff lineup.

The damage and patch-healing abilities of a shaman are less than impressive. They are worse in both categories than their closest competition (druids) and far inferior to the specialist classes in these categories (clerics, wizards). While each shaman increases the raid power they are unlikely to shine unless they are they are involved in slowing mobs.

The number of shaman needed for slowing mobs depends on the power of the guild. PoP has a lot of fights where multiple mobs need to be slowed quickly, which also tends to mean shaman fatalities are not uncommon. This means you need more shaman available, which means more shaman get to shine. NToV and Luclin is much the opposite with their commonly only being one challenging target.

In other words if you are going to be one of the core shaman in a raiding guild you are going to be a valued member, able to camp for the night knowing you've made a difference in the successes of the guild. Being shaman number 3,4,5 or five is significantly less exciting.

Shaman Grouping: Varies, but generally low.
Stackability: None.

The grouping shaman brings one A-class ability, slowing, which is a massive damage mitigation. This combines with the shaman's healing abilities to make them a potent package in one character. The cumulative effects of haste are substantial too.

Shaman slow is most valuable in challenging environments. If the party drastically over-powers the mobs, like time-geared characters going through easy LDoN adventures, slow is less vital. Shaman have a potential strength here in that they can combine slow, haste and healing in one person which allows more slots for DPS specialists.

As the environment becomes more challenging the value of shaman slow increases, but the danger of having the shaman as primary slower and only healer also increases. Thus it is likely more than one support character will be involved. The most likely is that slow and healing will be seperated, giving the following desireability.

Healing: Cleric, Druid, Shaman.
Slow: Shaman, Enchanter, Beastlord, Bard.

The core of the problem is that shaman have no synergy with the other healer classes. In simple terms we can't give them buffs they care about. Each of the other slow classes can increase the efficiency of the primary healer in addition to providing satisfactory slow. This is before considering that enchanters and bards bring crowd control and pulling, in which the shaman and beastlord have minimal ability (ie. root / pet tanking).

If the group is in a very challenging environment, which means it's probably not an XP group, the advantage of being able to slow and patch heal rises. However CC probably becomes mandatory. Meaning you'll go with the full defensive lineup of Cleric, Enchanter / Bard, Shaman. The best group I know runs with Cleric, Bard, Shaman having retired a L60 enchanter to make way. What they do with one group many guilds consider a raid target :) I'd love to be a shaman in a group like this.

In other words if you regularly group with "overpower" groups, or "high-challenge" groups the shaman has a lot of value. The regular pickup XP group falls into neither of these categories. The shaman will generally be aware that their inclusion is an indication that the group is sub-optimal (I came to dread enchanters for this reason).

Shaman Soloing:

In a guild? Lets assume you managed to get your epic, malos and Torpor/Quiscience. You've also got excellent gear? If so you can take pride in your ability to take down some impressive old-world big-game that summons. It's not really efficient for XP though.

Not such great gear? As long as you have Torpor / Q you can still root rot mobs. Melee's not too scary if you're not getting hit. You have great mana regeneration but no snare, so things are a bit more dangerous. Still, you can solo any mob that doesn't summon, can be rooted and can be got single in an area with some room to maneuver. Certainly better than nothing, but you can't compete with the druid agro kiting with a charmed pet for safety and efficiency.

Summary:

Shaman are not rare, in fact PoP has made them one of the favored bot classes.

If you are not in a guild then getting the neccessities of being a shaman (malos, torpor, Q) is going to be painful. And without them you're gimpy.

A shaman with great gear is quite durable. An enchanter with their rune spells is durable in a different way. The runes are easier to get for a non-guilded character.

What's more you tend to get pickup groups which are generally "moderately challenging", in which either a BL (no CC environment) or a ENC (CC environment) combined with a cleric or druid main healer is probably a superior choice to anything involving a shaman. If a group wanted high DPS (such as LDoN) they'd probably prefer a beastlord. If they are facing a challenging environment they probably want an enchanter.

In essence, for the casual gamer or those forced into a non-guilded life, the shaman is a class with few strengths. And the guilded, geared and valued shaman telling you how good you've got it won't make you feel much better.

On the positive side soloing up a beastlord is so incredibly trivial after playing a shaman :)
