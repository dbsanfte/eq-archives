Fudapuda
I'll certainly admit that I'm picky about groups, so to some extent I impose self-limits. I avoid pickup groups if at all possible, and I even avoid grouping with some members of my own guild who I know have skills too weak for the area they are trying to exp in--I just don't see the point in spending 30% of my time (or more) doing CR.

And part of the problem is just basic burnout, too. There aren't too many hurdles left to leap any more--11k HP, 9k mana, 500 AAs, all leader AAs maxxed, 1750 tradeskiller (plus a baby gnome tinker at 231 presently), more cash in the bank than I could ever spend. I guess I'm just less and less tolerant than I used to be. But I still think my initial point stands: There are two "basic" approaches to grouping (If you'll allow me to be reductivist for a moment)--you either construct a group "offensively" or "defensively".

Defensively designed groups are great for shaman--your intent is to minimize the effective damage to the group, and shaman are great at that: we have nice buffs for it, we can heal, and we can Slow, root, CC, etc. Lots of defensive tools.

Any time a new expansion comes out, there is going to be a period of time when most groups will be constructed around that defensive model, because players are naturally cautious in unfamiliar territory. You don't know how the mobs will react, you don't know what environmental variables will affect you, you haven't tested escape strategies thoroughly. Defensive is the optimal approach.

But as time passes, and players learn the lay of the land, they shift attitude to a more offensive approach to group design. Increase DPS at the expense of damage mitigation, because they've learned the tolerances of the area. This is also natural, since increased DPS, if it doesn't cost you in other ways, increases exp per time period, and loot per time period spent exping.

And shaman just don't scale well in the offensive approach. We CAN'T bring to the table what most other classes can. YES, we are better off in this regard with OoW than we were in GoD, and arguably better than we were during PoP, but it isn't enough. The tolerances of MPG and RSS just aren't narrow enough to argue for our presence. And so, we lose out on group spots to those who can bring "most" of what we bring (or else it can be purchased in PoK--i.e. paying for buffs before you leave) but can also bring that higher DPS that the offensive mindset requires.

And then, to drive the nail in the coffin, you have the groups like I saw in RSS this weekend, which had a shaman bot they dragged along and camped at the camp spot. Stay there all day, just log the shaman in long enough to buff when somebody dies, or when the buff wears off. Heaven forbid we actually open up a group slot for somebody who actually plays that class as a main, when we can bot the buffs instead.

All in all, I'm just angry at the continuing marginalization of our class. I'm sure I'll get over it with time, but my three-month assessment of OoW is that it didn't fix our problems, all it did was postpone them for a few weeks, until people had figured out once again that we weren't going to be valuable to them in the min/max game EQ has become.
