I believe if you were to project this discussion forward 5 years and 65 levels later, then it is possibly identifying where EQ is now.

This got me to thinking about the solution to Class Balance and indeed whether there is a solution.

With most problem solving i try and do, I simplify first. So the first thing i did was remove the concept of Class. Sounds weird right, solve class balance by removing class :rolleyes: , but dont give up on me yet.

By removing class you basically have a load of races and huge bucket of skills and abilities. If we divided these skills and abilities into categories, lets say you end up with 30. (these could be things like melee-sword, melee-club, melee-spear, melee-bow, melee-throw, casting-heal, casting-DOT, casting-DD, pet-create, pet-charm, summon-stuff, casting-stats, casting-buffs, casting-regen, casting-debuff, stealth-attack, stealth-defence, special attack, special defence, utility-offensive, utility-defensive, utility-other etc etc)

Now that we dont have Classes, but abilities I suggest we replace the idea of Class Balancing with the idea of Ability Balancing. Ability Balancing would basically be a set of rules that would not allow certain abilities to be selected with other abilities, or for certain races. This would be wordy to outline here, but it would basically protect from having an ubah casting DD, casting DOT, melee-sword, melee-club, casting-heal god like creature. You get the idea.

Throughout the life of a character there are opportunities to select abilities, restricted by the race you are, by your current selections and by your previous selections. You effectively have the ability to customise your toon.

There would be a category which was universal, which included a set of base skills that everyone had, such as a basic melee form (bitch slap), a basic heal form (bandaid).

You could limit the selection so that you could select to train from say 5 of the 30 categories, and specialize in 2.

So, if i was of say a shaman persuasion I could select...

Melee-Spear (effectively piercing, but would include 1hp,2hp)
Casting-Debuffs (effectively malos, slow, cripple etc) - specialize
Casting-Buffs (effectively haste, frenzies etc)
Casting-Heal (obvious)
Casting-DOT (obvious)

as well as having my basic category.

A warrior type might go with...

Melee-sword (1hs, 2hs) - specialise
Melee-club (1hb, 2hb)
special attack (berserk, frenzy type stuff)
special defence (riposte, dodge etc)
Utility Offensive (taunt, aggro type stuff) - specialise

As i go up in level I am offered more skills within each ability which i would train in, with special skills available if I have specialised in that ability. In the same way there is currently a quest to change your casting specialization, there could be a means working towards changing your ability selection and specialization. This would allow for your character to adapt over time, or even change completely if you just got bored.

Titles would be awarded based on level and race rather than level and Class, however for ability specializations acheivements you could receive awards of some sort.

AA's would be based on your ability category selections.

Equipment would be based on ability selection and race.

I know i will most likely get flamed for suggesting this, and obviously it would need a lot more thought, but i really think that conceptually this solves the issue of class balance. It may also go along way to solving PvP balance.

The problem with class balancing, is too many people have an idea of what this word "balance" means. And, as one of the SoE guys said... most classes look at only themself... or maybe their closest competitor, and not a bigger picture. I am not saying NO ONE sees beyond their class... as this isn't true... but many people just look at themself and want more.

Most people equate balance to desire. You desire your class to do more dps... to heal better... to do something else...

My vision of Balance... is two fold.

First... for every ability... for example... healing, slowing, "dps"... there are alternatives. If you can't find the "best" healer... you have backups that are "almost" as good. If you can't find the best dps class... there are backups that are "almost" as good. They might shine in other areas... a buff... or something.

Second... every class would have desirability... in "raid" and "group" situations. No class should feel like they can't get a group... and no class should feel like dead weight on a raid.

I don't think EQ is balanced... but I also think a lot of people throw this balance word around when what they really want are "upgrade" no matter what it "balances"

Balance is possible... you gotta have a firm vision of what you want your game to be like... willing to accept mistakes and suggestions... and change things where needed... and to talk to your player base with a grain of salt... they do play the game, and might just have a clue sometimes.

temuchintehkhan
the idea of a classless mmpog is a vaild one and precedes EQ, actually.

UO for example has no classes, merely skills. it's an old design question, and some developers and GMs are VERY dedicated to the idea of its superiority. like those old UNIX guys. at any one time a quarter or third of the MMPOGs you see under development boast a "classless system." the problem with a skill-based system is that it's MUCH easier to min-max a skill system... ie tank-mages running around with the best possible combination of skills with little variety between classes at all. the tank-mage syndrome isn't AS enormous an issue in pvp games or in korea where the idea of a level playing field takes precedence but for most US players and designers the idea of 3000 identical guys running around isn't fun

there's also the fact that skill systems are harder to balance than class systems. there's a serious danger that you can gimp yourself if left to wide open choices. CoH (city of heroes) for example took great pride in its open system until its first time mmpog designers threw their hands up in the air at the chaos of it all and scrapped it for a traditional class system

what makes EQ so hard to balance... aside from the snide comments on sony's crappiness is the fact that from the beginning classes were never meant to be balanced versus each OTHER. there's 2 layers to in EQ. GROUPS were balanced versus MOBs. With MUDflation things are a bit different now but when the Vision lived you had no chance to take a higher con or even mob. in fact the /con function is tuned to what a group can safely take, not what a single player should be able to kill. the key roles in any fight... healing, doing damage, slowing, snaring, tanking, nuking were all split up among the class members. it was revolutionary in a genre where healing was largely done on the fly with potions, where everyone was the ultimate hero and it forced people to group and socialize (it's probably the one design decision that has led to EQ's long long life).

in fact if you COULD reliably solo an even or higher con it was bordering on exploit. and those mechanisms. (shaman charm, dots, necro pet etc) were nerfed. sony NEVER said a particular class would be equivalent to another. in fact the original class penalties attest to the fact that fundamentally they intended for hybrids to NOT be equivalent to other classes.

most good designers recognize that classes can never be "equal" and so satisfy themselves with the idea of balanced. most will say they take a rock-paper-sissors approach. in EQ it seems that designers have pretty much given up on any rational, fair game design principles and have a sort of round-robin whoever complains gets an upgrade approach without a clear understanding of overall balance. so while I'd strongly argue against the title in this thread... MMPOG can CERTAINLY achieve class balance... it is impossible in EQ: class balance is NOT achievable in EQ
