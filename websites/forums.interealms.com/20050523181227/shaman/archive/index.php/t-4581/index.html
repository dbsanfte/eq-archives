Exodus
At level 39 or so, you can head to the ry'gorr fort in Eastern Wastes and farm the armor they drop while still gaining exp. The mail they drop sells for 300 minimum on the Mith Marr server, and has some decent stats if you wan't to wear it. You can also try farming Upper Guk and maybe Permafrost at around the same level, or head to Crystal Caverns and farm the herbelist pack the herbelist drops and the Crystal covered shroud and BCS the foreman drops. Herbelist pack sells for 100pp each, but you can only carry one at a time because it's lore, and crystal covered shroud sells for 200pp, not sure on the BCS. I've also hear an oracle pack or something drops off of the oracles in CC, but I have yet see one. Hope this helps. - Exodus

I've been trying to answer the same question of how to make the cash for the JBB since I hit 44. I did levels 39-44 in Dawnshroud soloing, so the exp went fairly quickly, but I was left with about 600Pp in the bank. Then the Bazaar opened and I couldn't sell a 10 dose sow potion for 100PP to save my life, and I will not undercut the market and screw over the rest of the class that is just starting to work on alchemy and can't afford to sell their potions for 80PP a pop, which I have seen on my server, Povar.

I went to Rathe Mountains. With my less than 100% efficent tactic of pulling with slow, siccing dogdog, dotting with poison, wading in with 2hb melee and tossing in a nuke I can kill an average of three before having to take a canni break. ( I'm soft, and like seeing my health and mana full for a few seconds before heading back to the wars again...LOL) Considering the money I was making I was happy with the exp I made there. I've upgraded some very outdated gear with Jaundiced bone vambraces, helm, gauntlets and boots for about 1500pp, and still have 3k in the bank. Now I just have to stay out of the bazaar for a couple more levels until I have that built up. Only thing I might be tempted to blow some of that money on..at this point it would be almost all of it, would be a regen breastplate

I've been real lucky...only had two pulls taken, and both at least had the courtesy to apologise afterwards. And I have also benefitted from having my slow land and dogdog get his first bite in just before a druid/ranger hit one of their root/snare spells on it, helping me out. It also helps that I spend most of my time in there definately in off-peak hours...staying up after midnight Pacific, or waking up mornings from about 7:00-8:00 until noon.
