Elihu, the actual perception of people (at least in high level groups) is the other way round. And fighting against red mobs distort your perception. Our higher slows usually stick quite fine, especially if the mobs are tashed already.

The most effective way for me was always to adapt to the other classes. The higher you get, the easier it becomes. The breaking point is Cannibalize with regeneration and the practice of Cannidancing (if one uses this practice). Once you get comfortable with regaining your mana during fights, you can start spending it in different ways as the situation warants.

Try to keep up regneration all the time, try to get an Iksar Ceremonial BP, and get comfortable with balancing your Mana- and HP-bars (cannibalizing enough to get the use out of your regeneration but not too much to endanger you if you get aggro).

The rest comes with spending that excess mana, but the first step is to make that mana almost instinctively :)

You should be getting, now or soon, your first of two charm animals spells. Start learning how to manage a pet. Woofie'll be your companion for the rest of your career, and you have to get a handle on how he works. He is your tank and he won't steal your xp anymore either :)

Level 24 is very low level for a shaman, and they tend to be quite delicate at that age. What -will- change your life is the Invis spell at level 29. It allows you freedom of movement you never suspected heh.

I should point out that shamans can solo Lodizal. How many other classes can do that?

At level 60, the shaman slows mobs 75%. That means for every four hits you had to deal with before, only one lands. On top of that, the shaman has the torpor spell, which is something on the order of 1200hp healed over 40 or so seconds. In a group situation, you probably just saved your warrior's behind. In a raid situation, you might have just saved the cleric's, who in turn saved the MT.
