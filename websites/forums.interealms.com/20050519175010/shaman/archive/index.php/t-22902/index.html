Back before the men and women of Norrath discovered the moon and the lost dungeons, back before my homeland was invaded by frogs, I was a battle priest. My damage spells were not instant, they were spread out over time and they were potent. Uglies cringed when ice shot down from the sky, but when my bolt of venom struck home, they were just as terrified. The healers in my group did not melee, it would have been silly for them to do so. But me? Oh I was fierce.

Today, I don't know if it's old age or the passing of an era that saw our kind strong and virile, but things have changed. Just the other day, I saw a wizard pull down fire from the sky, fire like I have never seen before. That uglie's mother wouldn't have recognized her son after that. Our knight was strong though, and the ugly did not turn. Not to be outdone in my old age, I inflicted the same ugly with poison, thinking I would show this youngster a thing or two. It must be my old age, because not only was my poison not strong, but the ugly summoned me to his side and nearly killed me. When was the day that the gods decided that poison would anger the uglies more than fire from the sky? And why do the gods hate us so if we can not train our poison in the exact same fashion that wizards can so that our dots can do more potent damage every once in a while?

With my belly rumbling (I craved for halfling toes like a pregnant woman craves icecream) I gated back to the tranquil plane. As soon as I rifted in, I had to sidestep warriors and knights, rogues and monks running towards the bank. Thought to myself that a young shaman must be selling his craft to his melee brothers to make a penny. Imagine my surprise when I saw these melee surrounding a halfling! A halfling! The cleric was blessing them with his god's virtue. Feeling sad and lonely, I offered to buff a few casters with the focus of a real god, but they politely declined. Even the casters do not wish the blessings of my god. I remember when His blessings were highly sought after, especially by my melee brothers. It's one of the many reasons I chose to serve Innorruk as a young troll. But today... Well I now understand why the exiled priests in Neriak were telling me that so many of our kind feel dissatisfied with the current state of things. I asked them about the future and they just shook their heads.

I don't have it in me to level any more with these youngsters. We are weak today. I do not understand why the gods are displeased, but our kind desires to feel wanted like the thirsty man desires water. I pray for changes to come now and not later. My requests were not asking for more than is required, nor would they make that wizard I met today envy my poison. But if our gods listened to our kind still, fulfilling these prayers would restore our waning confidence in them.

As battle priests, if these things were given to us, I'm positive our kind would be proud once again and have the same strength in their arm that I had when I was but a lad.

OoW Shaman spell overhaul:

Poison, disease, and curse counters no longer determine agro. Dots lasting < 1 minute have a 1:1 agro ratio with nukes dealing similar damage. Dots lasting between 1 and 2 minutes have a 1.2:1 agro ratio, where dots can deal up to 20% more damage than a similar nuke before grabbing the mob's attention. Resists are determined on a per tick basis.

Rains now have an inherent -15 resist mod on them (now putting them somewhere around +10 for resist rates).

The stat cap is no longer affected by shaman buffs. If you are at 305 strength, and you are lucky enough to be blessed by a shaman with Focus of Wanvu (the name will be changed from talisman to focus), then you now have 390 (305+85) strength. The stat cap will not be raised above 305, either thru AA or levels.

These changes, in combination with the rest of the overhaul, would go a long way towards re-establishing the shaman class as battle priests and as the premier debuffers and buff bots in the game.
