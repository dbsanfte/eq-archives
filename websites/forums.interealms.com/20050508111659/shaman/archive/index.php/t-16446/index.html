Xeoc Bonespirit
I am curious how people handle the role of being the primary or only healer in groups, particularly LDON or dungeon crawls.

I have been using nearly 100% of my mana on heals and regens, but the difficult decision is buffs/haste/slow.

Slow obviously decreases mob damage output, therefore making healing more efficient, but thats lost healing mana. I know that slowing is a major Shaman role normally, but when I am 100% of the healing power of a group, I have trouble deciding when to take the blinders off of being essentially in cleric-mode.

I am looking for advice from tenured Shaman about making the decision to burn that healing mana that may be needed for an overpull, etc, on other things like slowing and haste.

It's much worse when there is no other haster/slower in group, because then the decision is even sharper, as haste/slow would greatly increase the offensive/defensive dps, but would leave me completely unable to heal 6 people through thick and thin.

Thanks for any sage tips.
Sionnach Taibhsear
The way I look at it either you are the healer or you are slowing, but you really don't have the mana or the ability of control agro if you do both.

I have only been the primary healer in MM dungeons where a good portion of the slowing can be done by the Necro. As far as the buffing goes that is no problem if you can get folks to understand that if they want to have buffs refreshed they will have to wait just a monment. Usually the time that it takes to recast buffs is only about the time it would take to kill 2-3 mobs so you are not looking at a big loss of time if you only have to recast buffs ONCE during the 90 min adventure.

I have been on two adventures with duel shaman and those two adventures were extremely successful as judged by how much spare time we had left. It is odd how different Shaman are now as far as stackabiltity compared to the old days. It used to be that 2 Shaman were a waste of a group slot. Now with stacking DoTs and mulitiple roles, two Shaman in a group can function in two very different ways. Which reinforces my point that as a single Shaman in a group you have to choose what function you will play. I basically break it down into Healer/Buffer or Debuffer/Damage with of course some overlap.
House
At your level, it is probably better to heal than to slow. If things are taking a long time to die, making slow more efficient, but you don't have the mana to slow, try using Drowsy. In general, don't waste mana on stat buffs (Sta, Agi, Dex) at low levels; HP, AC, regen, and haste are the main things to keep up.

Kalander
At your level heal and haste were the key. Regen if you can afford the mana, it saves some healing. Again dont worry about buffing unless you are full mana and looking for a place to use it (which doesnt sound like the case). Slow is probably only worth it if the mobs are taking a long time to die. Like the above poster mentioned use the lower level slow if you are going to. It uses quite a bit less mana (20 for drowsy vs 60 for walking sleep) and the difference in slow % is not that great.

Ebonfang
Slowing has always been the key to being a main healer as a shaman. Combine slowing with regens and you should be able to handle things with reletive ease. If you have 2 tanks, be sure to have them rotate aggro. The tank with the most health that round should be the tank for the next fight. Make sure that the main dps melee in your group has haste, dont bother with anyone else in the group, at those levels you normally have 1 Rolls Royce twink dealing out most of the damage anyhow. Always sit, dont ever get up to melee, no matter what cool twink weapon you have. Lastly always have root mem'd. If you see multiple mobs called as incomnig... be sure to root before it hits your melee. You will not be able to keep up if your melee are tanking multiple mobs.
