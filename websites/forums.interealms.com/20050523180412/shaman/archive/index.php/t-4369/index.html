Bigguss
Depends on where you are in your shamanly career.

Prior to 60-with-torpor, your most important gear stat is regeneration. Period. AC, HP, Wisdom, yadda yadda yadda... all of that is secondary. With nothing but a fungi tunic I could solo all day long... it's all about regen. That's why in all of the "Starting a new shaman- what to buy?" threads- the answer inevitably includes an Iksar BP, Fungi staff, or Fungi tunic. Mana regen and HP regen are similar for us, since we can convert one into the other anyway- it's just the HP regen items are cheaper. :)

Think of focus effects as a minor form of mana regen. My crystalline torque reduces the mana cost of every spell I cast by 10%- it's like getting that mana back as soon as I finish casting the spell.

I'd say the second most important attribute to look for in gear is mana free damage. The JBB lives up to the hype. I'm hoping the epic (or as I like to call it- "that piece of gear with nice stats I got through a lot of faction work" (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?s=&threadid=1333&perpage=40&pagenumber=3), once I get it... that is, /wave Glori) does, too.

For the low levels, when you'll be tanking most of your mobs anyway, the next most important stats are haste and a nice weapon (this is your mana-free damage at low levels.) This remains empowering until well into the 30s, and definitely doesn't hurt even after that.

If you're post-60 with torpor and on the AA grind, HP becomes your most important attribute, but mostly because it enables Cann5, which is mana regen.

The whole AC/WIS/HP/Resists debate is a lot more incremental and, for the most part, situational. If you're a solo tanking shaman, extreme AC/HP is probably the way to go. If you're a raid shaman that rarely gets aggro (3rd on a raid or something), resists are probably more important. The general philosophy is that it's good to be able to take a hit, and that since one of our defining characteristics is out ability to quickly regenerate mana, the size of our mana pool (Wisdom) isn't that important.

Hope this helps.

Kriel Darkbane
I'm going to speak some heresy for this board. Everyone, please sit down before reading. ;)

HP are irrelevant for a shaman's mana regeneration until they are L 60 with the Canni 5 AA. Before then, more HP does NOT equal more mana. At least, not in anything approaching an efficient manner. If you need 100 extra mana fast, it's a hell of a lot easier to have 100 extra mana from your wis/mana gear, than it is to have 200 extra hp and cannibalize a few times to turn that hp into the mana you needed 10 seconds ago (by the time you get it).

HP's ARE good to help you survive aggro from failed slows, and tanking tougher mobs when solo'ing. No question, they're useful. But, before Can 5 and torpor they don't do anything for mana regen. Once you have Can 5 a large HP pool to let you use it without low hp aggro or risking dying is clearly useful.

What DOES matter for mana regen?

Flowing thought (obviously).

HP regeneration - this affects how much you can cannibalize over time, and thus mana regeneration rate. Your total hp don't affect that at all. Clerics will very very rarely CH a shaman unless he's L 60 with tons of great hp gear (if even then), as Celestial healing/elixir will be more efficient at most levels, especially if you're not wanting the shaman to canni down to 5% hp due to potential aggro.

Armor Class - high AC means you take less damage when you do get hit, which means you can put more of your natural (and item/spell) regeneration into cannibalized mana. High ac actually helps your mana regen more than high hp, at least before Canni 5.

Kriel

P.S. Nowhere in there did I say that 200 wisdom is critical for a shaman, or that you should max your mana pool at the expense of AC or HP. I just said that HP don't do everything that is sometimes claimed around here.

Bigguss
"HP are irrelevant for a shaman's mana regeneration"

Rephrase that to "HP are not significant for a shaman's long-term mana regeneration" and you're fine.

Extra wisdom will give you a larger mana pool, which generally makes a significant difference only in situations where you're pushing your limits. Same thing with HP. In the short term (during a fight) you have a little more HP or mana to burn- in the long term, your regeneration rate (and thus your ability to give and take damage) is the same. Yes, CH efficiencies can come into play here... but if you're on a CH rotation you've got much bigger problems than the most stat goals.

Generally (it's the use of the absolute term "irrelevant" that caused the problem here) extra HP and extra Wisdom don't matter a whole lot, since you're not getting close to the limits anyway. Cann5 changes this because it puts many people close to their HP limits. Generally, if you're this routinely close to the limits, you're either supremely efficient, or not very efficient at all- and you get an exemption (feel free to concentrate on hardcore stat tweaking.)
