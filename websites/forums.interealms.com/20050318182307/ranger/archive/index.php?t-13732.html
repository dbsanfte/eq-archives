Strange Aggro Problems and What Triggers It
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
From what I've seen on this and other boards, quite a few people have been reporting odd aggro in several different zones. Well, I got to observe this today while my wife was hunting drakes for tailoring. I had her do some experimenting, and I think I see what triggers the weird aggro in certain mobs.

First, there was my wife's experience. She zoned in, starting killing drakes, and shortly thereafter, Yvolcarn, who had been halfway across the zone, paid her an unfriendly visit. Not wanting the faction hit from killing the pest, she put a safe distance between herself and Yvolcarn and gated. She thought she had been trained, but I pointed out that she had SoE up and would have seen anyone running past her. I told her about the weird aggro people had been reporting, and we decided to take a closer look at what was happening.

(Just a note -- my wife is dubious to CoV, but checking around the zone, we found that there were allies to CoV getting aggroed at what seemed to be odd times. So, faction doesn't seem to be an issue.)

My wife zoned back in, tracked Yvolcarn down, and checked who Yvolcarn was aggroed on using /assist. She followed behind Yvolcarn as person after person was forced to zone. Finally, Yvolcarn decided to head back to his perch. My wife sat next to Yvolcarn and used /assist to see if Yvolcarn was aggroed on anyone. His list was clear. Then another druid my wife had been chatting with zoned back in and started to kill a wyvern way across the zone. My wife hit /assist again, and sure enough, Yvolcarn had that druid targeted. Yet, he wasn't going after the druid in question. A few minutes later (long enough for several people to zone back in and start hunting wyverns/drakes), the druid -- who was still targetted by Yvolcarn according to /assist, wandered into "pursuit range" (i.e., the range at which mobs lose interest in you and wander off). Yvolcarn took off after the druid like a bat out of hell, with the druid puzzled as to why and none too pleased about it. The druid headed to Skyshrine and zoned. Now, instead of going back to his perch, he proceeded to run around the zone, attacking anyone who had killed or was killing a drake or wyvern while he was sitting on his perch, waiting for someone to wander into range and trigger him to move. Once again, he returned to his spawn point only after everyone on this hate list had cleared aggro (zoned, died, etc.).

Think of it as an aggro time-bomb. You get on the hate list of one of these mobs with this buggy "zonewide awareness," but you don't know it, because they don't come after you immediately. Eventually, someone pulls this mob, it aggroes on someone else, or you wander into it's "pursuit range." Now the mob is free to chase whoever is highest on its hate list, even if that person is on the other side of the zone. [Edit: Some more testing with Yvolcarn this morning seems to indicate that this last point isn't valid, at least in his case. Needing some pelts, I killed a few wyverns as far away from the pest as I could get and watched track to see if he started moving. Eventually, someone with aggro got too close to him, and he pursed them to SS. The person fleeing zoned into SS, and at that point, Yvolcarn should have came after me and a few others. But since no one was in his large pursuit range, he went back to his roost. Still, if a zone is crowded where this kind of aggro is in effect, a mob could easily ping-pong from one person to the next and end up ganking some unexpecting soul at the other end of the zone.] As that mob wanders close to other creatures it's social with, it passes it's hate list on to them, and the next thing you know, it's add city. From what I have seen in a few zones, it gets ugly FAST.

I think this could explain the weird aggro in a lot of areas other than CS, such as ME and WW. I think that if those of us who have experienced the "adds out of the blue sky" phenomenon, I suspect many of us could see the pattern above at work.

In closing, I'd like to hear from other people and see if the pattern I give above can be confirmed by anyone else in other zones. If this is a bug, Verant needs to take a look at this quickly, because it makes playing in several zones more of a pain than a joy. I certainly hope this is a bug, and not a case of "working as intended," because how aggro is working in certain zones now is nonsense.

I think it has to do with lull. This can and will cause major problems in PoP as well as the older world, as apparently there is a bug that will mess up the hate lists. I've encountered a number of situations where the lull line of spells (not to be confused with harmony or harmony of nature) will permanently bug aggro until all the mobs involved are killed.

First time I noticed this was in Crypt of Decay. The bard in my group was using lull and fading memories to single pull down the right passageway through the bubonians. We managed to snag Witchdoctor Oxkra, a named rat, and got him to 15% before he managed to gate back to his spawn point. He started summoning us and the bard attempted to use lull and fading memories to escape the spawns in the path to the zone. The bard and our tank die, the rest of us escaped and zoned out. We rebuffed and attempted to work our way back in. As soon as the bard got near some of the mobs that he had previously lulled, every mob from the area we cleared to the Witchdoctor (pretty far in) aggroed instantly, warping through the wall at once. Our tank, bard, and cleric died this time. We all tried logging out and back in in PoTranquility. The bard did some CR to get the warrior and cleric out -- he said he was able to walk up to the named spawn with invis up (none of them saw invis) and did not aggro anything. This time, he had to use lull on mobs even closer to zone. When we rebuffed and fought to that point, the Witchdoctor again charged us from maybe 800 distance units away and killed a few. We gave up on this since it's apparent that something was wrong.

Second time I encountered this was in Halls of Honor where a bard decided to lull pull and also used fading harmonies to split the guards. He zoned out at some point without having killed the first two guards... the next party to zone in was immediately charged by the guards upon zone-in. Usually it is safe to zone in without invis up, but they even charged the graveyard. A necro that heard about this and zoned in FD-ed, however after they returned to their spawn points, they charged him again when he got up. We brought a group to kill the guards, and the next spawn was fine.

Third time I saw this was in Akheva while a bard was pulling some stonemen out of Va'Dyn's lair while he was up. Apparently he was using Fading Memories and lull and hit Va'Dyn with a lull once. They decided to pull him to kill him, but failed. Every time they attempted to pull anything remotely close to his room, Va'Dyn would rush out with the rest of the room and attack them. This did not reset after they died and zoned.

Basically, this bug could be used as an exploit to prevent people from succeeding in an encounter that might normally be trivial to them. I hope Verant takes a closer look at what they've done and fix this problem as soon as possible.
