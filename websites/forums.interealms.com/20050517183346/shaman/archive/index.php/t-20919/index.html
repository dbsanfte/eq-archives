Spite
I'm enjoying Horizons.
It's geared yes, toward the less obsessed gamer. Most classes can solo pretty well, so you're not forced to group unless you want to.
The tradeskill system is more advanced than EQ, and you know exactly what you have to combine, to get an item.
Experience in trading and adventuring is explicitly stated - there are no hidden exp bonuses/penalties for one class vs another.
You can tailor your character by multiclassing, if you want.
Most of the best gear is made by players, in a player driven economy. If you don't want to buy it from another person, the tradeskill system is easy to learn and advance in, IMHO.

Yes, it's got bugs and stuff it has to work out, but EQ had more than their share in their first year.

My first main was scrapped after it was locked out of the game for over a week because the support was so lousy - I identified the problem right after it happened and asked for the fix (delete what files to fix?). It took over a week for the support staff to figure out what I was looking for. It took me 5 mins to fix after they told me which files I needed to delete.

My second main (the one I developed while my original was locked out) made a total of 52 adv levels solo. When I quit it was a shaman, but to become a shaman they needed 36 levels of lesser classes like druids.

There is little reason to group up in the game - all chars can pretty well do any skills they are willing to train. So finding folks to group with can be a problem.

The craft system is fun - for the first 20 levels in most classes. Buying a lot (when you can find one available - most of em are tied up by peopl who quit the game) is interesting - until you start to build. Buildings take tons of heavy resources and your toon has limited capacity. Once you have trained to the point where you can create those resources, it becomes a series of FedEx tasks to gather a ton of resources - the good ones are a ways away from any transportation - trudge them to the appropriate shed, create the materials, trudge the materials to the plot, spend a couple seconds installing them on the plot. Do this a few hundred or a few thousand times and you have a house or silo with no functionality - Congratz!

CrustyOldShaman
I like the crafting system in Horizons.

1) You get experience and hence levels for everything you do so progression does not depend on infrequent random skill ups - with all the frustration that goes with it.

2) The interface for crafting is second to none.

3) When creating stuff by crafting, you can choose to produce decent gear or else take the time to buy or farm the extra components needed to create top of the range gear - literally the best gear in the game. This is a refreshing contrast to EQ where dedicating huge amounts of time and pp results in you being able to produce nothing better than 'the suit of the gimp' when compared to the gear obtainable by raiding.

The housing (plots) idea, on the other hand, is a total waste. Not enough of it reproduces some of the worst camping squabbles found in EQ. Luckily housing isn't necessary so its best left to the kiddies to fight over.
