Maian62
All the same - sometimes sharing aggro (and hence damage) is essential - like when the last healer in your group goes LD in the middle of a fight.

This happened to us last night in the KC basement. If I hadn't taken aggro from the warrior - and started sharing the damage around at least one of us would have died. As it was we all survived until the healer got back on...

It can also be necessary when you have no complete heals on tap - and the druid or shammy cannot keep up with the mob's rate of damage. The thing is not to let anyone get below about 2 bubbles - or you risk getting to the point where they cannot lose aggro ...

The risk of course is if you start to get adds or respawns towards the end of a hard fight when everyone is low , and vulnerable.

The all -ranger group CAN work ok - IF you all have some sort of mana buff (at least clarity, or better still, KEI), which gives you all enough mana to heal yourselves up between fights. Hence all rangers plus either a chanter, bard or beastlord works ok too. We had a group like this with one warrior, a bard, and the rest rangers in KC. We'd let the warrior tank and the Bard would mana regen us during the fight, after the fight we would all chain cast heal on the warrior - crude but effective.

Otherwise - since DoTs now stack - I suspect an all ranger group without mana regen is better off either bait kiting or root/DoT/plinking, if not the whole way, at least until the first root breaks to soften the target up.
