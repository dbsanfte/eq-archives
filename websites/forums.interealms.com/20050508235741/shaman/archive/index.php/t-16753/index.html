LOL, Jinheim.

DoTs will help you out a lot if you're not presently using them.

Also there are a lot of things that can be done to use what we have available to maximum efficiency. For one, use your pet's hp and his insane regen (post fight) to your advantage. For example, buff up pet including with regen, pull with turgur's, if you have epic, root it, back up and epic (I *hate* how easily this is interrupted), Ebolt and reroot. Now back off and canni-dance that used mana back again while your pet takes the hits. Throw another Ebolt in again when first expires and get in there and tank now, Cannibalizing and Stoic and Ebolting as needed (not sure how many hp these mobs you're fighting have). I had JBB in the mix too, but that won't help you to consider.

Once you're done, you get back your used mana for half a minute and pet regens back to full health and you're ready to go again.

Of course, the timing might be different for where you are fighting, but this is basically what I did to help maximize everything we have available. Experiment a bit and you'll get the timing down to optimize each encounter.

There are a myriad of other different tactics you could use also, from letting pet tank fully while you sit on the horse Cann'ing, healing pet, DoTing, to plain ol' Root rotting. You'll get better and faster, just don't be afraid to mix things up a bit and try out something wacky now and again. (I used to use JBB and Flash of Light as a type of Fear, and it was timed beautifully so that I could blind the mob, JBB it once before out of range and have another JBB start casting and hit the mob on it's return after FoL wore off, and then blind it again to send it off again...all the while with our pet eating it up)

Best of luck to ya.

snatzie
I do not have JBB nor my EPIC (just lazy on EPIC... I've been "almost done" for over a year now...) But I didn't have any problems solo in Dulaks.

Here is my take... see what everybody thinks.

My advice... let dogdog be the main tank. You want dogdog to take a beating since (a) you can't use his HP for mana (b) he heals much much faster than you between fights... especially if you make him sit. Most people think of dogdog as a DOT, but he is also a meat shield... make sure you maximize both functions.

<b> Buffs</b>

You don't need all the best buffs... you mainly need HP for canni... so STA and Tuna are good investments. If you find yourself taking a beating, then bring up AC and then maybe the AGI buffs. Horse really helps you with mana and to not pull sit-aggro... otherwise you should be in bear form. Oh, and I usually had C3. By the time C3 wore off I was usually ready to gate out and sell anyway.

On the other hand, I give the pet all the best melee buffs and best haste. As mentioned, dogdog must have 2 shivs or proc'ing weapons.

Regen is a must on both of you.

<b> Tactics </b>

I would lead with Slow, then engage dogdog... let him get aggro, back up. Mob should turn on dogdog. Wait for dogdog to get a good amount of taunt going while I canni back the slow mana. Then I layer in the first dot. You shouldn't pull aggro, but if you do, just wait a sec for dogdog to build up taunt and back up. Then layer in the next dot. Canni up. Repeat dots as necessary.

I usually leaded with Plague since it lasts the longest... then followed up with ebolt.

By 55 in Dulaks, with the new pet, I was able to finish most battles ready to jump into the next. With regen up, dogdog was usually ok too, but worse case: slap a stoicism on dogdog, tell him to sit, and go pull the next mob... he will be good to go by the time your back.

<b> Other minor points:</b>

I always have root up just in case dogdog has trouble taunting the mob off me. I root and then back up.

Let dogdog take a beating... but never let dogdog die, you have too much mana invested in him. If he gets below 50%, lay a Stoicism on him... he will loose some haste, but your dots are going to kill the mob. Also, if dogdog is near death you can always lay a stoicism on yourself and step in and to take some hits. Stepping in myself is usually my second choice because then I am not canni dancing.

Lastly, my horse was the best investment I ever made. It was recommended to me over JBB... but I couldn't afford JBB anyway.
