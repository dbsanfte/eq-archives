Thorzion
Previous to the most recent patch pets, regardless of thier level, could take any buffs that were cast upon it. So a level 1 mage pet could take level 65 shaman buffs.

This afternoon a cleric friend of mine was leveling his beastlord alt. the BST is 24. I topped off the BST's buffs with level appropriate buffs and memmed the spellset I use for buffing my own pet. I got "spell is to powerfull for intended target". I tried the next step down in buffs...same effect. I had to use my pre 50 buffs on the pet as nothing else would stick.

Interested, and mildly annoyed, I headed to PoK to search out pet classes in various level ranges. I found a level 44 magician and a 45 shaman that were willing to help me out.

The level 44 magician pet was blue to the magician, so under level 44. Post 59 buffs would not stick, just like they wont stick to PC's at 44. The 45 shaman pet was blue to the shaman, so under level 45. I could stick Focus, Boar, Diaku and FA on it, even though the pet level was under 45.

Im thinking that SOE has tied acceptable pet level buffs to the level of the caster. Interesting, and as I said, somewhat annoying even if it IS a "fix" to the way it should have been. But seeing as that pets have always been able to recieve the best buffs in the game regardless of level (or at least, since I've been able to summon a pet circa 2001) this isnt a "fix" but a nerf.
