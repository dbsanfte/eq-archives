FYI, PoS and PoV are all just like dreadlands (except less interesting).

But about the trial of execution.

Add a warrior with reasonable gear to that group (6k+ hps). Cleric only heals the warrior with elixir. Warrior gets aggro on everything in sight. Warrior has max damageshields (potion + dru/mag). Don't slow a damn thing.

You are the MA for the group. You tank one of the mobs keeping torpor on yourself and keeping aggro for the rogues.

The key here is the warrior+damageshield. He soaks up the damage and the damageshield tears the other three mobs up. By the time your group gets the first mob dead, the rest of them will be at half health and you tear through them like a hot knife through butter.

If the cleric ends up chealing or fasthealing the warrior he will likely take aggro, and depending on how early in the fight this occurs, it could screw you over. That is why the warrior having good AC and hps is key.

Juujuu
The best trials groups you can make have mages in them.
My favorite trial group is Tank, Shaman, Healer, Mage, Mage, Mage. If I need to get someone through the trial that isn't in one of the above classes, I sub in that person for one of the mages.

Mages (druids too) can banish summoned mobs. This is an immediate no xp kill that still resets the executioner. You banish the rocks that spawn, and get a lot of time to med up between waves. Pet classes in general are good for trials. Necros have a good damage output on their pet, and they can nuke as well for the fast damage needed to drop the mob before executioner does his thing.

You need a main tank for the trial. He's main assist. Send pets at the assist. Mages do NOT assist him if they are banishing a mob. If the executioner gets to the prisoner, and the mob is still at only 1/2 health, you might still make it if you have enough wizards and necros and mages for the fast nuke damage.

Tirgir's insects is the biggest waste of a spellslot I've ever used in trial of exe. The only thing it ever does is overaggro mobs onto me. Mobs slowed with tirgir's seemed to 100% mitigate the slow in my experience in the trial.

As a shaman, the order of importance I have on things is:
1. If the executioner is about to kill prisoner then nuke the main assist with poison nuke
2. Heal the main healer if he's needs healing.
3. Heal casters with Torpor and fast heals. Heal tanks with 2k heal if you can to save the healer some mana.
4. Nuke stuff at your leisure depending on how easily you are going through mobs and the waves. Torrent of Poison is a great nuke if you are down to 1 or 2 mobs left for the wave.

When you get to the final boss, malo him (if you have malo), and then slow with turgur. Heal as needed.

I've done the exe trial with many different group makeups, so while DPS is the key to exec trial, you don't really need a set formula to pass it.

As a shaman, I found recently that tirgirs pretty much does nothing on the mobs from the 4 waves. I've actually chain casted tirgirs on the same mobs 3 times, until I got the Yawn message from all 4 at once... and they did not seem to slow down at all. My advice is stick to nuking and healing. Use Torrent when there are only 2 mobs left, and use your single target poison DDs to speed up the kills.

If you aren't the main healer, then it's your job to keep the main healer alive. A smart cleric will use SE (or CE) as their main heal, then CH the tank and melee in between castings. Your job is to keep him alive, and let him deal with the rest. If you have a druid, then have him concentrate on the MT and you, while you heal the rest with tnargs or torpor (on casters only).

The key to exec trial is targetting. As long as your MA targets fast, you'd be fine. Even if he/she just uses F8 it's still a lot better than wasting time trying to find the right mob.
Henora
Boinshar hits it on the head. While DPS is key, there are a few other things to take into serious consideration.

The very most important one is agro management.

Clerics should use Heal over time spells as much as possible. If they need to use bigger heals and get agro, you must keep them alive.

You need a MA who can target fast and get everyone to killing mobs FAST.

You need someone, be it your MA, or someone else... bards work very very well for this... to be able to pickup the agro on all the other mobs that the tank is not actively fighting. So they do not agro on your casters or healers.

You as a shaman should be doing what shaman do... filling the gaps. Don't slow, instead pass out damage shield potions. Anyone who is taking hits should have a DS on. Heal. If you have a bard who is taking the hits on the extras, you can use torpor on them, they are likely using AE dots and other AE spells to control the extra mobs and not contributing a great deal of damage via melee. Keep the cleric alive.

Personally I passed my trial as the one and only healer. It certainly wasn't flawless, 3 of the 6 people with me bought it to the boss, but I think with tnargs and a better understanding of the event and the strategy that is most important that I could keep an entire group alive, with luck.
