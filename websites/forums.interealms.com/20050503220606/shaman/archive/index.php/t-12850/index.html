Jinheim
General Abilities:

Run3/Regen3

Some people say to get enhanced stamina instead of these, but I think that's really dumb. You get diminished returns on stamina past 255, and that's pretty easy to hit. Before that, 5 stamina is going to give you 15 HP, which is kind of worthless. Run3 is priceless, and regen 3 will give you those 15 HP every 30 seconds, so this is a no brainer in my eyes. Get these two and leave general alone.

Archetype Abilities:

Mental Clarity 3

This is your number one priority. 1 mana a tick for 2AA (1 rank of of MC) is nice. After the first rank you get diminished returns. 4 more AA nets you 1 more FT, which kind of sucks. 6 more AA to get just 1 more FT which definately sucks. However MC3 is vital because it is a prereq for:

Class Abilities:

Cannibalize 5

This AA skill is god. Anyone that says otherwise doesn't have it. Hitting canni5 every 3 minutes amounts to about 30 mana/tick if you spread it out. That's huge. The best part about canni5 is that if you need mana and have health, you don't have to sit there for a minute casting canni4. You just hit your button and BOOM you have mana. Go straight for canni5 - almost everyone agrees on this.

After canni5, these AA's are useful (more or less in order):

Mass Group Buff - At first I wasn't going to get this AA until later in my AA path. Then, I had to buff a 9 group raid by myself without it. Then, we wiped and I had to buff them all again. Once the raid was over I immediately went out and AA'd for about 16 hours to get this AA. I am very glad I got it. You'll be glad you have it, and so will everyone else in your guild. If you aren't in a raiding guild, though, you can skip this one.
Natural Durability 3 - 10% increase to base HP, which is very nice. It isn't as big an increase for us as it is for a tank, but it is still awesome.
Spell Casting Mastery 3 - Big mana savings on your spec line spells. I stopped playing for a while and left my friend with access to my account. When I came back, he had gotten me SCM3. Since I didn't see stages 1 or 2, I got to see how big a difference it was going from nothing to MC3. I noticed a very noticable difference, and was quite pleased.
Spell Casting Reinforcement 3 - prereq for:
Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery - 50% buff extension. Once you get this, it will seem like your torpor lasts damn near forever, which makes a very big difference for IN TEH FACE soloing. With extended enhancement focus, your other buffs last a very, very, very long time. One MGB focus and I usually won't have to worry about anything except rebuffs for the rest of the raid. 11 minute avatars are great, and it is very nice for making those damned haste/regen buffs last longer. This is an underated AA that many people do not get, but I am very pleased with it.
Radient Cure - Radient Cure 1 is nice. It gets rid of lots of annoying little effects easily and quickly. I only have 1 rank of this so I can't tell you about the high levels, but many people say that ranks 2 and 3 are even better.
Quick Buff - I have 1 rank of this, and intend on getting a second. The cap on buff haste is 50%. With QB2 and a haste of mithaniel item, you will hit 45% haste. That is close enough for me. The downside is that the focus item won't work for torpor, so it will only have 25% haste. A 3 second torpor (vs 4.5) may or may not be worth 9 AA points. I will probably get it eventually but QB3 is not high on my list.
Healing Adept - Makes your heals heal for more. Having better heals is self explanitory, and is obviously a good thing.
Virulent Paralysis - I don't have this AA, but it is easy to imagine having it. I think 1 rank of this would be well worth the 2 points to get it. A fast, low-resist root can be a life saver. I can't imagine the higher levels of it being worthwhile, though.
Combat Agility - Makes the mob miss you more often. It is definately a must-have for melees, but looks to be only mediocre for shamans. When I am exping I get hit on a regular basis, but rarely is my life in danger. On a raid, I rarely aggro, and when I do, I am screwed anyways. CA3 could be worth the 12 points, but only if you are ready to invest another 15 in to:
Lightning Reflexes 5 - Basically just more combat agility. People with both CA3 and LR5 will swear by it. I believe it would be worthwhile, but 27 AA is a LOT to anyone but the most hardcore AAers.

Useless but fun AA:

Rabid Bear - Gives you double attack, 200 atk, and 99% haste for 5 minutes every 2 hours. This AA is decent for dueling and good for a laugh every 2 hours, but is more or less useless. If the devs were to shorten the reuse timer or make it a permanent improvement to the form of the great bear spell, it would be pretty spiffy. For the moment though, it sucks.
Spirit Call - Summons multiple pets to fight for you (3, 4, and 5 for ranks 1-3, respectively). This AA was lots of fun and I was very happy with it for the first few weeks of PoP. I liked to see my horde of pets, and I loved people saying "what exploit are you using to do that?" Now, though, everyone and their mother has this AA, and the novelty has worn off. It's useful as a damage boost while soloing, but that's about it. I wouldn't advise getting the second or third ranks. You only get one additional pet for too high an AA cost.
Spell Casting Fury - This would be a fun AA to have, but only because it is fun to see yourself critting. We can do decent damage with rains and nukes, and that decent damage made even more respectable by SCF. I will probably never get this AA, because I rarely have the opportunity to use my nukes and rains. I am almost always much busier with more important things, and leave the nuking to the wizards and mages.

AA's I don't want:

Alchemy Mastery - This AA gets a /rude from me. I would rather put points into innate charisma.
Pet Discipline - Makes it so that your pet won't move until you tell him to. The only use I could ever think of for this AA is when I am soloing WW dragons and I don't want my pet to run after them when I get hit by an AE while pulling. You can easily accomplish what pet discipline does by spamming /pet back off. If it was only 2 points, I might get it, but definately not for 6. Good for classes that use pets on raids, but not for us.
Healing Gift - Gives you a chance to critical heal. This is a good theory, but I would much rather have the the stable increase in heals from healing adept than the criticals from healing gift. Usually when I am healing, I am healing someone tanking a slowed mob. This means the damage being done is very constant and manageable. I usually heal them with kragg's when their health is down about 2k. If I were to crit a heal in such a situation, it would be wasted because they are at full health already. The constant 10% increase from healing adept will always work. The only time healing gift would be better is when you are blast healing someone that is about to die. If someone was at low life and you landed a crit tnarg's on them, they have a better chance of living than if it was just a normal blast. I would rather have something reliable though, so I skip this AA.
Channeling Focus - Makes you channel 15% better at rank 3. In my mind, that is not anywhere near enough to be worth 12 points. If it was 50% at rank 3, I would sure as hell get this AA, but not for 15%.
Hastened Rabidity - Makes your rabid bear usable every hour and a half rather than two hours. Screw this AA.
Suspended Minion - Lets you suspend a pet that can be called out later. Does not go with you across zones. The only use for this is saving yourself the hassle of having to resummon your pet every time you invis. Would also be good for duels. I don't care about this AA, since I hardly ever use a pet. This, however, is an AWESOME AA for any true pet class.
Combat Stability - Not that good for shamans. We don't take damage on a regular enough basis for this to be worth it, in my opinion. If you have 27 to dump on this and Improved Defense 5 (like LR5, but for CS), then go for it. I don't have those AA to spend.

Others:

Planar Power and Innate Enlightenment - Full training in these will give you a cap of 355 wis and 305 for everything else. You should train them as soon as you are hitting your current cap.

Something else to consider....

Don't just consider what the best AA skills are, also think about how easy they are to get. Thus, sometimes its best to do a quick easy AA that gives good value for the time invested, rather than grind out ND3 that will take 18 hours or more before you see any significant benefit.

On that basis I would strongly recommend RC1, VP1, and to a lesser extent SC1 after Canni V. They are all relatively cheap AA skills, and are in fact extremely useful. If you are in a big raiding guild then MGB probably comes first, but in any other case I'd get these 3 before anything else because they give the BEST VALUE. They aren't the best AA, but you can get them so easily it seems silly not to.

SC1 - think its been discussed quite a bit. Mana free damage is nice. Also useful on a CR if you get jumped, and it can hold off even tier 2 mobs for a fair while whilst the group gets its act together. Don't forget the fun element!

First, this is a quality thread. Let's keep this near the top and stay on topic (not saying anyone before hasn't) if we can. :)

I am a raiding shaman who also solos. I'm not in the Elemental Planes (nor expect to be any time soon), but I am in Tier2/3 almost daily.

There are distinctions with what TYPE of Shaman you play and what kind of ACTIVITIES you pursue in designing any AA progression guide.
There are AA's that offer a greater return on investment to a raiding shaman that offer a lesser return to a small group shaman, or one who is usually playing as a duo partner.

My advice is to choose your AA expenditures based on your playstyle & AA earning capabilities. If knocking out 8-12 AA's in a weekend isn't a big deal to do, it might make more sense to go for mastering a specific line. If getting 3-4 AA's in two weeks (due to raiding or whatever) is more what you accomplish, then spending points on some of the smaller AA's abilities may make you happier. Choosing WHICH investments to make (whether small or large) should be based on what you spend most of your time doing IG, be it raiding (sole shaman, or w/ many present - big difference), grinding, grouping, soloing, duoing, etc.

General & Archetype initial progression (RS3/Regen3/MC3) are pretty easy to pick out. But after that the Archetype waters become murky & making the choices more gets a little difficult. It is hard to judge whether a raw hp upgrade (ND) is more valuable than an increase in buff duration (SCR) or an increase in Alteration spells mana efficiency (SCM) since they do very different things. I found all of these valuable, so I max'd each of these areas. Deciding WHICH to do first isn't easy. I did SCR first (primarily w/ extended Torpors in mind), then ND (HP>all), and SCM (mana efficiency) most recently.

My personal choices for next Archetype AA's are Healing Adept (HA) & Spell Casting Fury (SCF). Since I like them both, I'll probably stagger them, bumping each one a level at a time. I originally was going to do the CA/LR next for improving my soloing, but since I'm doing less of that now & more raiding (with multiple shaman present), support healing improvements and additional DPS weigh in as being more valuable for me. I'll be leaving Crit Heals (Healing Gift), Channeling Focus, and Combat Stability to last in this section.

Class skills are (to me) pretty easy to pick out. Canni, QB, MGB, & SCRM are all worthwhile. How far to go in QB may be gear dependant.

Rabid Bear and Alchemy Mastery are worthless, and Pet Discipline is of limited use. I put pet discipline very low on the totem pole of AA's in comparison to other things available (especially with a 6 point investment requirement), but above most other General abilities. If I was still soloing often, or considered saving my pet a priority on raids (it isn't) instead of killing him off for AE fights, I might value this more.

PoP Advance:
Planar Power and Innate Enlightenment in my opinion should be invested in as your stats max out. I'm just now hitting that point gearwise when buffed, so I'll probably dump points in here soon. No sense in being buffed to a potential 300 wisdom if you are capped at 280. Small AA investments make these easy choices when you have a few banked and burning a hole in your kilt. :)

PoP AdvanceB:
RC1 should be done immediately after Canni in my opinion, possibly even sooner if you are already raiding. (Canni5>all other AA's).
VP is going to be my next investment, just to free up an extremely valualbe gem slot taken up by a root spell. Spirit Call is a fun AA I still don't have, but will probably get soon. Free damage is hard to beat, but it didn't outweigh the benefits of the Class/Archetype AA's I've invested in ( http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=642761 ) so far. Suspended Minion is nifty, but not worth 5 points when I can just cast a new pet pretty easily.

The rest of these abilities are generally dependent upon major investments in either Archetype or Class. Skip Hastened Rabidity entirely in my opinion.

Another Elder Prophet's opinion to add into the mix. YMMV. :cool:
