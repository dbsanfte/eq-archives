Loramin
The mistake that WoW and EQ2 are going to make is they will allow it to be too easy to level. Burnout won't be far off and people will come back to EQ1.

I love parts of EQ, but I absolutely hate "grinding." IMHO, you shouldn't play a game to power your toon, you should play it out of enjoyment of the game itself, and the powering of the toon should be a side effect. EQ is this way at times for me, but if WoW or EQ2 can achieve it consistently then I think they will be superior games. Just because neither game takes the EQ approach of endless timesinks such as slow leveling, not to mention faction-sinks (#@$)*#@&% dranik loyalists), rare spell drops (do you have all of your GoD and OoW spells yet?) and long (entirely unrelated to raiding) quests for keys to raid zones, doesn't mean that they are failures. It just means they need to have something for people to do once they max out their toon.

What I expect of both games is that they will be like EQ back in the day. I remember a friend telling me, when I joined during the Velious era, that I shouldn't do any quests or worry about equipment (to be fair he did twink me a bit) until 60, because levels were > all and once you got to 60 that was when the game really began. His point was that the real fun of the game came from doing the massive quests like the shawl, the dain ring, and your epic, and these were the kind of thing you did after you got level 60. So I guess what I am saying is that being able to get to the maximum level quickly is not inherently a game flaw: having no end-game content is. And while I have heard that both WoW and EQ2 let you level fast, I have yet to hear reliable complaints about either game that they have no end-game content.

I really doubt that SoE or Blizzard would be stupid enough to make a MMOG that can be "finished" in a few months, but until these games have been out for a few months we won't know. Personally though, I'm looking forward to both (WoW initially, and EQ2 if WoW fails to meet my expectations) as they both seem to focus far more on classic RPG elements like small groups instead of raids, and quests instead of grinds/camps. And if they do both run out of steam after a few months ... well, I'm not stupid enough to sell my account, so as long as SOE isn't stupid enough to delete it while I'm gone Loramin will be waiting for me to come back.

Oh, and one final thought: in beta devs often up the exp rate to get players to test the high end content they otherwise wouldn't get to. It's entirely possible that in the actual game exp rates will be "nerfed."
