RedChief
Sounds like your loot system is in need of revision. You have a system that allows and even encourages people to hoard there points and not raid unless your doing uber stuff.

The system my guild uses has specific design rules to prevent this type of behaviour. Loot Scores are determinied by multiple factors, including total raid level (75% attendence = full, 50-75% is less), loot drops on raids, and attending a raid (which is seperate from loot). So the more you raid, the higher score you get. If you have a raid level of 0 for more then a week, you start loosing points (so go afk for a month and all your points wont be around, just some). The other thing that my guilds system does is encourges people to spend there points to prevent hoarding. There are 4 bid lvls (rot, decent, good, uber) Now bidding rot, decent, or good takes a set amount of points off your total. But bidding uber will normally take between a minimum 20 points and all your points away (and you can go neg if you have less then 20; and unless you have 120ish+ points, you will use them all on Uber item). The net result is everybody has loot from the current level. Only a few people have in excess of 50 points, and they are saving on specific items (elem pants (we have 2 in a dozen god kills)). The other result is new people in the guild have gotten elemental loot very fast because every one accepts they the system is design so a few people cannot get most the loot.

We are also a guild that understand people have real lives and may be able to raid only a few times a week. You raid less, you accumulate points at a slower rate then those who raid more. You will get loot, just not as fast. Deguilding people becuase they have a real life even though they have shown long time loyalty to the guild is not only harsh and unfair, but mean and impersonal also.
Valsuvious
Stopping people from getting more than 1 loot per phase isn't a good answer. Limiting your Quarm loot to 2 per member is a good idea I think. It stops those people from just snagging up every quarm loot while no one else gets any. Installing a raid % average is a great idea. My guild uses a 40% over the last month in order for you to grab any non rotting gear over another member.

Another thing that they need to look at is the cases that I'm in right now. Currently, I'm at the bottom almost of my guilds dkp list. This was done cause I joined the guild just before we started trying to kill the rathe. When I got into the guild, my gear was lacking, and as a result, I needed every upgrade that I could get. So, 16 or so loots later, I'm at the top of our shamans in hps, even though I'm at the bottom in dkp. I don't think that this is punishing people like me, who weren't in the guild until recently. I got upgrades when I could and needed. I personally wouldn't have thought that it was right if someone over me in dkp couldn't have looted something cause he already has an item from that phase. When GoD comes out, the top end stuff is gong to need your guild to be in massive Time+ gear, so you'll be farming it for a while. You will need all the upgrades that you can get.

Basically, if you look at my guilds dkp roster, you'll see some interesting things. First, you'll see the people like me who are fairly new to the guild at the bottom in the negatives. You'll see a majority of the people near +70.. and then you'll see about 10 people who are just huge amounts above everyone else. They are the people like you who have been raiding with the guild for a very long time, and just don't need the upgrades as much. Believe me, once you start to see the stuff in time dropping, the high dkp people will start to fall back down to the ground.

Summarize: install a raid average that they need to maintain over a 30 - 60 day period in order to bid on anything, and maybe install a 2 loot per member Quarm loot policy so that everyone has a chance to gain from it.

Most guilds on my server get over 100 people on during Time nights, then maybe 60-70 when it's not their turn in Time. It's pretty normal to see a 25-30% jump in attendance on Time nights.

My guild disallows getting Time loot if you don't attend at least 50% of non-Time raids between rotation turns (unless you have a believable excuse).

Edit:

Also the officers toyed with the idea of making your 30-day attendance percentage modify your bid, if the attendance was below a certain percent (66% would have been the cut-off). Basically, if you have a buttload of dkp saved up and you only logged in to raid Time, you'd have to bid more to get an item than someone who has higher than 66% attendance for the last 30 days.

Example: If you had 50% 30-day attendance and an item for 100 points minimum dropped and if the high bid was 150 by another player, you'd have to bid over 300 to outbid.

Something like that would kill dkp hoggers who only log in for Time.

Some guilds "renormalize" their members dkp each month or so, to stop people from building up too much.
