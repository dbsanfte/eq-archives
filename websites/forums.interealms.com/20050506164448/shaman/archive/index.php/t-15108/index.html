i would sugest that you spot heal the other casters, and rebuff the folks that get rezzed once combat starts.

i've been the low man on many raids, this is what i've done.

most of the raids i've been on, the higher shamans do the pre-raid buffing, but once the sh_t hits the fan, folks start dying, and the clerics with clicky sticks start rezzing, if you see someone looting their corpse, slap a buff or 4 on them, haste, regen, sow(if in zone that allowed), don't worry abouot a str buff, rez effects wount let it stick.

if for some reason your cheal group draws aggro, maybe be the sacrifical goat and draw the aggro with a slow or something and try to run the mob back to where all the warriors are.(done this once or twice)

the most important thing is of course FOLLOW DIRECTIONS and don't draw aggro away from the MT or ST

mostly, you'll be healing or buffing.

the cheal group, if you don't know, is the group of cleric that are casting cheal on the MT (main tank). example, cl1 cast cheal, 3 seconds later cl2 cast cheal, 3 seconds later cl3 cast cheal and so on and so on. so essentialy there will be anywhere from 2-4 cheals being cast about to land on MT, all depends on how many cleric in rotation and MT's HP and how hard the mob hits.

The others have given good general advice, so I won't repeat any of that. What I'd like to add is that since this is Chardok, do NOT have a pet up unless told specifically to do so. So many Chardok raids wipe as there is a trap otw to the king and pets/players fall thru and aggro the King and his guards. Not good. You probably don't need the pet up at all anywhere in there, but if in a nice safe place, it probably wouldn't hurt. Just don't let the pet wander. :)
Gart Egilsson
A Royals raid references heading deeper into Chardok to kill the Prince (for enchanter epic), the King (for cleric epic) and the Queen (for warrior epic). A pretty classic challenging and well done raid event.

The pet should be fine if you use /pet guard here, and then summon companion as you move to a rest/camp/fight spot.

They aren't aggro, so obviously they can be parked whereever.

Royal raids are cool, while the Queen is no longer the challenge she once was, respect the fact when she dies that she's cleaned the clocks of many a raid.

Lord knows we've had a couple of necro summoning parties due to the Queen. It's a different ballgame now of course.
