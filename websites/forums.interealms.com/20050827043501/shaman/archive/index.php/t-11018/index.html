The Hyena Shaman
Advantages?

1) Faction. Honest to god, having awful faction just means you have more places to hunt. As a barbarian shaman I've ruined my faction in Freeport hunting, High Keep hunting, Ak'Anon, Felwithe, and Kelethin. I'm working on getting at least dubious in Cabilis for the conveniance of it, however.

2) Regeneration. Not really that astronomical (can barely notice it on my Ikky Beastlord), but it IS better than what everyone else gets.

3) AC bonus. Very noticable, and pretty useful.

4) High Wisdom. the +10 you have over Barbarians means that you'll never ever have to mourn the fact you can't wear GEB's.

5) You look pimpin'. Seriously.

Disadvantages?

1) Faction. However, the fact that you have SoW will save you from everyone except Ogguk bouncers and Holly Windstalker. Being hated allows you some interesting veiwpoints. You learn very quickly what NPC's dwell where in what zones.

2) Gear. Not a HUGE problem. the only thing you're likely to miss is the cheap AC of Jaundiced Bone. Everything else is either chain or race: ALL. Your Plane of Hate armor is miles ahead of Rune etched.

3) Exp penalty. This is the BIG penalty for Iksar. You lose 20% of the exp from every kill you make. It adds up.

4) Low Stamina. As we all know, Stamina = Hp = mana. Iksar get a larger mana pool with their high wisdom, but don't get as many HP to work with, at least not until some of the better gear.

5) Crap newbie zones. You'll be lucky to afford patchwork.

6) Finally, 50+ spells. These aren't sold in Cabilis. And to my knowledge, they're not sold in Shadowhaven or Plane of Knowledge, either. You have to get friends to pick up such gems as Turgur's and Malosini.
Toktuk Wartalon
The race you chose is not nearly as important as how you play. Every Shaman race has their advantages and disadvantages that you have to compensate for. I don't think I've ever heard anyone say that they wouldn't take someone because of their race/class combination, though.

Iksar just aren't as popular as other races, for some reason. They make perfectly decent Shaman. If what's important to you is choosing the absolute best Shaman race, I would personally tell you to go with a Troll. But I also think that people who constantly hop around looking to be the latest and greatest Race/Class combo will always find themselves disappointed, because balance in EQ is always shifting. If you pick a role that you enjoy playing just because you think it fits you well, you're bound to have more fun in the long run.
