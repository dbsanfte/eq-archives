Some history...

Three shamans have stood out since the early days of EQ and defined the class in the public arena in various ways.

One of the first was Tudamorf. Alas he joined the chanter cult quickly when he realized that a lot of what shamans did at the time (pre-Kunark) was bunk, i.e. buffing. He was one of the very few that supplied hard data to destroy a lot of the BS that Verant tried to perpetuate about it's game systems. (Anyone remember his AC/Agility tests, his Strength tests, and later his haste tests that made VI rework a whole section of their game because it was simply bust?)

He also tried hard to make out shamans to be a generalist class that had a very limited role in the big encounters. He had a point, but he overstated it. People did not forgive him.

The second was Stusser/Sam (who posted above, /wave Sam). He also showed with hard data that a large percentage of what people *believed* about the game was bunk.

He also could flame like nobody's business and got caught up with his own personality far too often. His protege, Eduin, took all the best tricks from the Sam repertoire. Both Sam and Eduin tried to close the case on shamans as a class, believing them to be an irrelevance in the top end game. People did not forgive them.

Which leads to Nixx. The arch-enemy of the above players. He was one of the very few who continued to shout the praises of shamans as a powerful force in spite of a lot of the data the naysayers threw at him. Perhaps his perception was clouded by the different, smaller, and possibly more suiting to shamans, environment of the test server.

But, whereas Tudamorf, Stusser, Eduin and many others continued to rant about shamans as a broken class in the end game, even after leaving the class for enchanter/druid, necro, and monk respectively, Nixx stayed a shaman through and through. He did stuff that nobody else thought possible. He slayed dragons, soloed crypts, and posted many other feats that pushed the boundary of what being a shaman is all about.

Because of this, i pick Nixx as the most powerful influence on the class. Others have been more uber. Others have taken the shaman case to VI with more success. Others may even have done some of the things he posted about before he did.

But, i haven't heard of anyone who has come close to playing a shaman and talking about shamanism with such passion and such success as Nixx.
