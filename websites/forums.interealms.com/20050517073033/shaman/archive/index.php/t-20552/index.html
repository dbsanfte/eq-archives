Actually I hadn't planned to post back to this thread, but I do want to address this point. Yes, I'm sure I understand software development quite well, and TMMM does apply here.

My point, apparently not made well, was that producing a game the scope of Everquest on the existing code base, and specifically making a major change like the graphics engine change, would require an immense team of people working together well to pull it off at a high quality level. As the difficulty of the project rises, the number of people needed to pull it off well rises exponentially, and the time needed to train all those additional people adds greatly to the cost of the project. At the size/scope of a project like the new Everquest engine it is already economically impossible to produce the product according to "good" software development principles with a goal of shipping bug-free or nearly bug-free product.

So I'm sure they resorted to what they could do - overworking their existing team. I'm sure they were AWARE that there were bugs, but maintaining two engines in parallel is a LOT of work, and they can't hire additional people due to the ramp-up and training time (TMMM principles apply here). So they switched over to the new engine as soon as it was practical and are no doubt working like slaves to get it up to the old standards.

Part of the problem is the lousy code base they were starting from. I would love the chance to develop a good code base for an MMP, one that is designed from the ground up with fault detection in place. That would be a worthy project IMO, and it would be nearly unique not only among games but among virtually all large distributed software systems if it was truly bug-free or nearly bug-free :-). But EQ was like any other game at the time it was developed - undermanned, and with an expected lifespan of about 2 years if they were lucky. Here we are 4-5 years after and its on its third rewrite.

I'm not saying it is impossible to write stable code, if that is taken as a goal from day 1. But the EQ code is so old, unstable, and crufty that I think it's pretty amazing they managed to change such a major piece and keep the game up.
