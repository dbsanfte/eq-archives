Well after reading some posts im bored and would like a good decision, i would prefer to discuss relgion but from the other posts i read that doesnt go over well. So i came up with this...should not offend anyone and be a good discussion at the same time.

The question is do we ever really know anything?

Before we can decided if we know anything, we need to establish what it means to "know" something.

1st to know something it must be true, ie you can know its raining outside, then walk outside and it turns out its a sunny day. You really didnt know it was raining then.

2nd you must believe what you know. You can say its raining outside, but if you dont believe it then you dont really have knowledge of it, you are just making something up.

There are a couple more conditions that i cant remember right now but those two are suffiencient for my cause.

The next part alot of people disagree with but they are wrong.

Is there a way to tell if you are dreaming? When you are in a dream do you know you are. People will say "sure if im flying then i know im dreaming, if im fighting monsters then i know im dreaming" while i dont agree that you are ill grant you that.

Now have you ever had a dream where you woke up and were like "whoa! that was just a dream! crazy" i know i have. They can be mundane, just waking up and going about my normal routine dreams or crazy nightmarish dreams...but ive woken up from both and been like "phew that was just a dream" or "oh ok that was just a dream". I will assume that everyone has had one of those type of dreams in their lifetime. Just one. Thats all i need.

Now when you are dreaming, what do you know? Perhaps you know that you are on a sandy beach with a hot date. But is this true? No. Your really in your bed, or on your couch, or at your desk or where ever you really are. If its not true, then its really not knowledge. So when your dreaming you cant know anything.

Now if when your dreaming you cant know anything, and you cant tell if your dreaming or not, how can you be sure if you ever really know anything.

People will say no i know im not dreaming right now, i just know. How can you be sure you wont wake up in a second and be like "whoa that was just a dream".

You cant be.

You cant be postive you know anything.

In one sense it all comes down to faith. Faith that the world your living in is the world in which you belong.

But once faith is established that certain things (in this case "reality") is true then you can work from those confines on to other assumptions and finally knowledge.

NOTE: when using "World" it can be applied as the word "Universe" in most circumstances... "World" is not just the "planet" we live on but rather the entire "reality" we are discussing.

I assume through faith that the world I operate in has certain laws and as such will behave a certain way. These are primarily refered to as scientific laws and behave the same way under the same conditions each time.

My inner mind or imagination where dreams are kept and created is another world unto itself with a different set of laws often which are changable to some extant by partly my own will.

Outside this world is a third world I assume through faith exists that would be the domain of God or some people refer to it as the spiritual world.

Within each world I do "know" things that are true and apply to those worlds in each instance. For instance I know in this reality that there is some force acting on me to hold me to the ground. I assume its gravity and that the working scientific model for gravity is an accurate depiction of this force.

In my dreams the rules can be a bit more chaotic though in many cases I have direct control over them. In most dreams I can tell I am dreaming. I've trained myself to look for things that indicate that I'm dreaming through reading various studies on dreamers. For instance most dreamers can not read while in the dream world. It is possible to have one particular key phrase be seen but books and signs are often at best symbolic pictograms of thoughts we have. If I start seeing these in my dream that is a pretty good tip off.

Secondly abnormal movement. In dreams it is common for dreamers to experience sweeping scene changes OR to see other people move from place to place in times that are simply improbable. Again these are signs that I pick up when I'm dreaming. Interestingly enough it doesn't take me long to figure out that I'm dreaming and therefore can often take control of the dream and bend and twist it to my desires. I rarely have nightmares mainly due to the fact that as soon as I realize I'm having one I'm able to take control of it and stop whatever is scaring me.

The rare time I do have a nightmare its less of a dream and more of a nighttime memory coming to the surface. Since the memory is an actual event recorded in my mind it plays the same each time and therefore I do not control it.

The spiritual world also has certain laws about it in most cases well beyond my control but I cannot discuss them here since religion is banned. But in the case of philosophy you rarely can seperate ones religious views from their working philosphical views. In the case of most philosophically aware Christians the premise which everything else is built upon is based on a faith in God and His orderly will.
