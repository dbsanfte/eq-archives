Gloriana
I love our guild's point system.

Everyone accrues points for attending raids. Active players must maintain a certain attendance percentage to stay active.

When an item drops, it has a pre-determined OBV (opening bid value, typicaly OBVs are 2, 8, 16, 32). The guild does three rounds of silent bidding to officers, with round 1, round 2 bids announced. Highest bid on round 3 wins item.

Bids are open to active guild members first. If no active guild members bid at OBV, the bidding is open to active/inactive/trials. Class only loot is determined by the class and the winner spends 2 x OBV (I spent 64 for my fabulous TA for example, a BARGAIN).

I like the system because:

* It's perfectly fair, you attend raids, you earn points, you spend them as you want
* It rewards players with high attendance without penalizing players with low attendance. Even if you don't raid as much, you can still save up points to buy your dream item
* Due to the anon bidding, it's hard to "one point swipe" an item, people have to bid what they think the item is worth to them

Some problems we've had:

*Debating if certain items should be restricted to one class (BoWs for example). We pretty much decided to let the guild use their conscience - most items best suited for a particular class go to that class first

Jaradis
My personal opinion:

A guild where the guild leader decided who got what gear based on what best benefitts the guild as a whole, without any regards to personal feelings, friendships, or individual magelos, but based on current gear needs for the guild overall, can progress at a much faster rate than a guild on a DKP system, assuming all other things equal.

Now thats not saying any non-DKP system will beat any DKP system guild. But an officer/leader awarded system where you put gear on the members that get the most upgrade/use out of it is going to gear up better and faster than a DKP guild for the most part. DKP guilds tend to have two problems: Rot and Minor Upgrades.

Rot being a problem when an item could benefit someone but because of the DKP cost they won't loot it and it rots. So instead of bettering your guild overall you end up letting items rot that someone could have used.

Minor Upgrades being where members with already good gear but also have high DKP buy items over people with much lesser gear simply because they can. So you have people that get very minor upgrades over people that would have gotten a massive upgrade. Having a 10k hp tank buy an item that gives them a 20 hp upgrade instead of the 8k hp tank getting a 150 hp upgrade is an example of what can happen in a DKP guild.

Now this doesn't always happen, but it can. I think the problem with many DKP guilds is people forget that DKP is only supposed to determining loot order, who gets first choice. Unfortunately many people feel that their points entitle them to not only get first choice, but 2nd, 3rd, 4th, or as many as they want without regard to others.


You really need to decide what is best for your guild based on the people in it, especially your officers. A non-DKP system takes very strong officers that have a strong backbone and are willing to make tough decisions.

Zaviax McCleo
My guild uses a unique system. We went through a lot of trial and error runs before finding one that seems to work well.

We give all members one point per raid (or sometimes per target if we hit multiple targets in one night). We also give people bonus points for doing certain non-raid tasks. Right now to encourage people to get their VT keys we're offering guildmates extra points if they get their Emp keys by a certain date. In the past we gave points to people who farm fungoid sap for when we were preparing to take down Seru for the first time.

Each point can be used to increase your random on an item.

Standard rolls are 0-2000 for friends and apprentices (Guildmates who have been in the guild less than 30 days)

For every point you use your random is increased by 100. So if someone was to use 5 points, their random would be 500-2000. The maximum points someone can use in a random is 18. Thus being 1800-2000.

Points are only used up if you win the random.

You can also buy items with 25+ pts. Before a random takes place the looter will ask those who are buying to send him a tell with how many pts they are using.

The idea is to give everyone at least a shot at loot when it drops while giving the regular raiders a much better chance at getting the stuff they really want.

We've had it going for about 8 months now and no complaints at all. Everyone seems to be pretty happy with it even though once in a blue moon someone will come in and win a nice item on a 0-2000 roll despite a few people randoming 1800-2000. But it's not too common.

And the 25 pt buy option guarantees even the unlucky rollers will win something eventually. :)

My guild uses a Loot Council. We based our policy off the one used by Blades of Wrath on Torvonillous. If I'm not mistaken, I believe BOW is in Time, and may even be raiding GOD content. I read it their policy first several months ago (when we were looking to upgrade from a /random system) and about fell off my chair ... really wonderful system. We tweaked it to suit our guild's needs and haven't looked back. Our Loot Council consists of our leader, our lieutenant, one officer, and five regular guild members. They link what we win off a mob, and ask for people to send in tells. In that tell, people must link what they'd like to replace and also say why the drop is an upgrade. Loot Council takes into consideration things like time spent raiding, time spent contributing to the guild, seniority, need, and a host of other things, and makes a final decision. We have had people from DKP guilds join our guild and say the Loot Council is the best thing they've seen since blue M&M's. It's efficient, it's fast, and you are certain the loot is going to someone who needs it and who has really put time into the guild. We have had VERY few disputes over loot.

As for DKP ... DKP does not take into account need. It simply awards items to the people who can raid the most ... and if you're like a lot of our guild members, it would leave you in the dust with no chance of ever outbidding someone and winning an upgrade. DKP might be a more fair system than a Loot Council system ... but it is not the best system for us. I doubt we will ever use DKP.

RedChief
Open bidding is the worst thing in any loot system. I was in a guild with open bid and watched people bid on items for the sole purpose of bumping up the cost.

My guilds current system is probably the best I have seen (but we all think our guild is the best). It addresses numerous issues that DKP systems have. It uses a silent bid useing fixed amounts (4 levels, 1, 3, 6 or 20+ points).

Rot. Nothing really ever rots. Since each item has no fixed amount, someone can always get for rot. Even trial or untagged people can get loot. Only class specific stuff like the ranger sword or bard horn in Ikkinz rots.

Marginal upgrades. If a item is a 20HP upgrade for a warrior and another warrior it was a 100hp upgrade, the other warrior should bid more. People bid what they think it is worth. If your being cheap, expect to not win items.

Point Hoarding. Because of the design of the system, someone can hoard 100+ points but high end items have a variable amount attached to them. That amount is either 20 points (a person can go neg), a max amount of 100 points (it is rare for someone to acquire 100 points though), or a final amount which is there total points minus there raid level (1 through 4) times 2. So if I had 30 points and bid on a item and am a full time raider, I would have 8 points left.

Additionally, seperat scores are used for determining who wins lower point value items. So the person with the most points wont necessarly win the lower value item.

Since our guild is heavy hydra based (when 2/3rds of clerics and 1/2 of shaman are bots) so we treat hydras at almost the same level of a main. A hydra must have x more points then the next bidder where x is the value of the item. Hydras acquire and pay the same amounts, just have more restrictive conditions to win.
