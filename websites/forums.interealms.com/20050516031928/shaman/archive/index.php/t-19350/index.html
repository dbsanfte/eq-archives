Sionnach Taibhsear
I got to thinking about how stupid the Graveyards are in PoP and some other zones. Sure it saves folks from losing corpses permanently but it really shows a lack of imagination on the part of the developers. For the most part I think waiting 20 minutes your corpse to pop is not a serious enough penalty to keep the game exciting. Death can be used as an opportunity to add texture and content to the game. Brad McQuaid used the terms Static Penalty and Dynamic Penalty to describe experience loss and corpse recovery respectively.

My thought is that instancing could be used to make the dynamic death penalty of corpses recovery part of the game instead of some minor inconvenience that just works to make the game feel more like a grind than an adventure. I agree that no one should lose their corpse permanently unless they want to, but recovery should have some element of fun and adventure.

With the Netherworld penalty a player would have two hours to recover his corpse before it despawns and “reappears” in an instanced dungeon. Of course the dungeon would not actually exist until the player accessed it. The corpse would not be a resource hogging graphic on a zone floor, but a bit of data stored on the game servers waiting for the player to start his CR dungeon. While the corpse is “above ground” it ticks off 20 percent experience penalty so that by the time the corpse despawns and is sent to the Netherworld dungeon, a full 20 percent experience penalty has been assessed on the corpse. The player with a corpse is a wraith and cannot wear or wield solid objects, cast spells (except for Necromancers and Shadow Knights and then only limited selection) or be affected by the spells of the living.

If the player does not return to his corpse within the two-hour limit, the player becomes a Revenant of his former self who takes physical form but cannot progress in the game. In other words the player can play cannot gain experience. To be restored to full function the player must enter the Underworld, battle the creatures of the Netherworld locate his corpse in a crypt and reunite his spirit with his true body.

While the player will be unable to gain experience in the world of the living, he will be able to gain experience in the underworld dungeon in “escrow” to be applied to his 20 percent static death penalty. This way the dynamic corpse recovery become part of the recovery of the experience points lost to the static penalty. In most cases the recovery trip in the dungeon will replace all but one or two percent of the lost experience. It will of course be possible for the player to enter his instanced dungeon alone, but he will be able take a regular six person group into the dungeon as long as they are within five levels since the dungeon will be of the appropriate level of the corpse. It will also be scaled to the number of people in the party. The friends will gain experience normal for that level of dungeon but have a chance at drops that can only be found in the Netherworld.

If the group fails to recover the body in the allotted time the player loses his gained experience for the crypt dungeon and has to start over. His friends get to keep their experience.

What I like about such an arrangement is that the corpse recovery is part of the game. It is not too easy like a 20-minute wait in a PoP graveyard but it is not a separate, extraordinary part of the game that holds little enjoyment and does not feel like part of the game itself. Even a player does not have uber-guild connections can go on a large pick-up raid to a dangerous dungeon knowing that even in the worst case scenario his corpse will not be lost forever. Friends wont dread getting a tell for a CR. Instead of a traditional CR this will be more like and invite to a LDoN Dungeon. Truly in most cases folks will probably be able to recovery in the two-hour window but if they don’t it wont be like the alternative CR isn’t fun.

This is just an idea and the numbers example but I think something like this could be a lot more fun than the current system.
