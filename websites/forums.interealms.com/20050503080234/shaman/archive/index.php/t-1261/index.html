Beeg:
Couple things. Its been a few years since I've been out of college, but I can't seem to follow your first two 'equations'

1) according to the mana calc over at Castersrealm, at lvl 24 1 Wis = 5 mana.

So, Canni 1 is 50hps for 20 mana.
4 Wisdom would get you the same amount of mana.
Therefore 50hps = 4 Wis. So each Wis = 12.5 hps.


Healing at lvl 24 is 60 Mana for 94 HPs. It takes 12 Wis to generate the Mana for that spell.
So you could claim that 12 Wis = 94 hps, and each Wis would then be worth 7.8 hps.

The thing that is causing problems (I think) is that these are one way conversions...you can't claim that they are equivalent...I don't have ANY Cannibilzation spell that will give me 60 mana for 94 HPs.

So to convert Hps to mana, use the value of 12.5 hps per Wis.
An 6ac 65hp Ring is NOT 11 Wisdom, but more like a 5 Wis Ring.

To consider how many Hps a Wis Item will allow you to heal use the 7.8 hps per Wis value.

Hypothetical example:

I have 100 hps in a pair of earrings. If I want to Canni that away, I get 40 pts of mana back...If I immediatly turn around and heal myself of the 100 pts of damage, I spend 60 mana points to do it, for a net gain of -20. Whoo! neat party trick. I would be better off with a pair of +1 Wis earrings if I did this. Those 2 Wis points would allow me to heal about 15 pts of damage, much better than -20.
(NOTE: I said *hypothetical*...don't flame me pointing out how silly this is).

If you examine the numbers, you will see that compared to healing, Canni is very inefficient...and that is part of the problem with the HP/AC over Wis argument. A casual inspection will show that a +6 Wis ring adds 54 Mana to my mana pool (at lvl 45) but a 55 Hp ring (the platinum equivalent) is barely enough for 1 cast of canni = 20 Mana.

This is why you can't convince the average player that you aren't a raving fool when you mention your Wis is only around 130. :)

However, the real key comes into play when you include regen, other players healing you, meditate, etc. Most casters sit at 100 health with no way to turn it into mana. No matter how inefficent we are, we still have that option. So we can be recovering both bars at once.

Hope this helps.
