Vorpatril
Your fundamental premise is flawed Armak. You say that SoE is not to blame for prices, since they are set by "greedy" shamans. Ergo, the problem is ours.

The problem is a simple one of supply and demand. The supply (set by SoE) of good spells is low, shamans are a powerful and popular class, so demand is high... therefore the prices are high.

No one is going to sell Torpor for 1k. If it was, someone would buy it immediately and turn around and sell it for 50k. Because they are evil and greedy? No, because that is what the market will bear.

The subject of "greedy" people has been beaten to death in these forums and is a comic waste of time. Individual people might or might not be "greedy"... but the fundamentals of market economics are always present and is an impersonal "invisible hand" that will guide prices to what the fundamental supply/demand curves demand.

So, if you want prices to go down, increase supply or decrease the number of shamans. Griping about greed won't get you anywhere.
