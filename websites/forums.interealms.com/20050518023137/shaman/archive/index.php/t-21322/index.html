afreeman
Its another example of shoddy programming on SoE's part.

Falling damage is calculated off the time spent in the air, which is itself a function of machine speed and how much video detail you have turned on. So, if you have a fast machine, and most of the graphics candy turned off, you will "fall" very fast, have very little air time, and thus incur very little damage.

This is also why people with slow machines were getting hit for 20k at the spires a while back. Because the EQ game engine places the character into a zone several seconds before you regain control of the character (again, ****ty programming), if you happened to land on the spire scion's head, you were treated as if you were in the air that several seconds before your screen updated. On a fast machine, this was being counted a 2-3 second fall, but on a slow machine, it was long enough to produce 20k falls for some people.
