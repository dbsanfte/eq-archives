ciccero
Torpor is a pre-emptive heal. It's not a very good spot heal for melees since it's a major snare and slow.

Guardians hit for 530+, so torpor at 300 pts/tick heal isn't really able to keep up with that on it's own.

Use torpor before the wurm ever hits you so you can have it up while you try to slow and get aggro'd -- OR if on the monk (not good since it mucks up his damage output) -- use it before he's wounded very much and let Torpor run as the wurm's hitting him to offset some of the GW's damage.

Torpor isn't really a heal, it's more of a damage offset tool. Don't use it on a severely wounded melee who's still getting pounded by a heavy hitter as it just won't keep him alive long enough to heal him.

Without counting AAs:

Torpor takes 30 seconds from cast to finish to heal 1200 damage.
Kragg's takes 10 seconds from cast to finish to heal 1950 damage.

Do the math.

Use Torpor when your target is at full (or good health), and you ANTICIPATE they're about to take one helluva beating over the next 24 seconds. Not when they're at 30 health, have already taken one helluva beating, and are still being pounded like some big GW chewtoy.
Harwel
It's totallly situational for me. Solo, Kraggs Mending is out of the lineup. Torpor for heal over time and Chloroblast for a quick fix.

Grouped, it depends on the other healers. If I have a cleric or druid (or both, /drool) in the group, Chloroblast for patch heals while the cleric and druid dish the big heals. I don't Torpor the melees during a fight. DPS goes down the crapper if you do.

If I'm the only healer, Torpor and Kragg's Mending USUALLY. Torpor myself and other casters, KM the melees. Depending on what other support classes are there, I might keep CBlast memmed still for a quick heal or there. Especially if there is a chanter, you give up your Celerity spell slot for Cblast, you'll need it to keep the chanter alive. :p I've been in groups where I was the only healer, keeping all three memmed, because I didn't need any haste or damage dealing spells. I still don't Torpor melees during battle though.

If I'm duo'ing with a light melee like a ranger or rogue, I prefer to tank, in which case I follow the solo idea (CB and Torpor memmed)... essentially you're just soloing and the light melee adds DPS in this case.

In your case of duoing a GW with a monk with the monk tanking, I would have healed the monk with KM and healed myself with Torpor.

That's just how I tend to run it... but it's all situational really depending on group composition, DPS of the mobs involved, etc...

Grats on Torpor btw. :D
