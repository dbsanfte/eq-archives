They really need to add some other incentive to Rescues and Assassinations, as nobody seems to be doing those. The best option IMHO would be to balance out the missions (not necessarily making them equally challenging, but at least equally attractive) and give the option to select what kind of a mission you want to do.

For example make the assassination targets to drop loot equivalent to random nameds, so that you get one quaranteed named loot from the mission if you choose one. The same could work for rescue, hailing the rescue target after he reaches the zone in would give you a piece of loot (or spawn a chest containing loot). Or they could have a point multiplier (regular points for collect, slightly more for an assassination etc).

Different mission types could also have an effect to the rest timer between missions. They could increase the rest timer a bit, but make them specific to the mission types. That shouldn't be the only change though, just forcing players to do the missions they don't want is not a good overall fix, but something like this could be a part of the change. That would mean that you want another collect, you need to wait, but you can get a mission of another type immediately. Implementing that might be a pain though, making it too strict could cause problems if there people in the group that have done different types of missions in their previous groups, or making it too relaxed migth cause it to be too easily circumvented by leader changes.
