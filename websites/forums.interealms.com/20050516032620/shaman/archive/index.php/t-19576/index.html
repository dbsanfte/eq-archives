Hi Ughlei,

I see your thoughts regarding Stamina/ND3 but I would recommend going for MC3 then Canni 5 first.

The reason for doing Canni 5 first is that you could make use of it sooner. Stamina is something that you can get quite high from bazaar gear and will not be far from topping out with your own buffs. I wouldn't put points into that just yet.

ND3 is a great AA set, and not to be scoffed at. Personally I wouldn't do it until after you have Canni 5.

If you are concerned that you won't be able to use Canni 5 during times you don't have virtue etc you are losing out on the opportunity that you do have those buffs by not being able to canni for several more aa's.

I am sure I am making this more complicated, so forgive my less than clear outline, but I would move MC3/Canni 5 above Sta.

jzak
I think the biggest mistake most players make on AA is buying the stat ones before they get even the marginally useful AA. I have over 300AA and I still haven't bought STR, WIS, DEX, AGI, STA, INT or CHA.

If you join a raiding guild that's at least in VT or lower-tier PoP, you're going to max out your primary stats (WIS, STA) pretty soon. Resist AAs are useful later down the road, especially if you ever get to PoTime. If you don't plan on being in a raiding guild, then I think you could skip the resists and go for stats.

Before buying stat ones, you should get: all archetype AAs, all class AAs (except AM and Pet Disc.), all PoP Ability (except Suspended Minion and Hastened Rabidity) and all resist AAs.

Korelen
AAs are highly situational, depending on your play-style.

I think most Shamans will agree with working on Canni V as soon as humanly ( or Barbarianly /Iksarly ) possible.

Run 3 -> Regen 3 ->MC 3 ->Canni V

To improve your Hit Points, I would work on ND 3. I did not do this until later in my AA Path, but I would have benefited more if I bought these AAs sooner.

If you raid, I would invest into buying MGB. Along with MGB, Radiant Cure is a life saver. No priest class should go without it. For encounters such as Grummus, you only need one rank to cure his AE.

To help with your buffs, SCR, SCRM and SCM. Longer duration and mana preservation is good on raids. There will be times on raids in which you may wipe and need to cast buffs on groups - which is a mega pain the arse.

CA/LR - I did these AAs after I finished all my Buffing Enhanced AAs were completed. I was tired of dying all the time when a slow was resisted in BoT.

Quick Buff - having a 3.0 sec Qui with QB 3 ( 50% spell haste ) is awesome. This is something you could save until you mem Qui. Quick Buff is a big commitment ( 3,6 and 9 AAs ).

If you solo, VP is a requirement. Well, not a requirement, but it saves you a spell gem where root would have been -unless you have Scaled Avatar Pants. Also, it is neat to see a mob being tossed back. :P I do not solo that much anymore, and if I do, it is usually for farming. I am sure Shaman who use this ability more can give a testiment to it's use.

Healing AAs. More and More I am seeing myself healing - raids, ldon and xp groups. I never saw the power we hold with Qui and T. Mending. Most people say we suck at healing, and to a point, I would agree, but with Healing AAs, we can close the gap a tiny bit - enough to fumble our way through most situations. I worked on Healing Adept first ( I wanted the benefit of 10% ) to add with my Scaled Avatar Sleeves ( IH 4 ). Finishing off with Healing Gift.

CS/ID - I just started working on finishing my Defensive AAs.
