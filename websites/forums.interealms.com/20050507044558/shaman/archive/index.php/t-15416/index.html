Sciek Spellbottler
Two options basically, and IMO it depends on what part of the script is causing your raid the most trouble. You can Go all out DPS and nuke/DoT/Rain or you can help the CC by slowing, split tanking, rooting adds. It sounds like you have the right idea in your situation though.

The whole script kinda spirals downhill with each mistake you make. Let an add or two get loose and they can kill off some clerics or CC people early on which is obviously bad. Put too much emphasis on CC and not enough DPS, then you won't kill him fast enough and get too many adds for your CC crew...

I'd say you're probably on the right track, toss some DoTs on him early, send in the dogs, and then get to work on Crowd Control. If things are under control, don't hesitate to toss some nukes and refresh DoTs as able.

Before the fight, don't forget to nab as many melee with FA as you can. Every little bit of extra DPS will help you out here.

Well, my guild has made it to RZTW three times so far. Our first try, we failed at 47% (he got pulled on to clerics at 80% and killed 2/3 of them with ae rampage). Second and third tries we whomped his ass. With only three tries, though, my experience is a little limited.

Here's my job during the RZTW fight: When we first get in pit, I help all the chanters and druids charm mobs from the pigpen, by casting malosinia on them all. Jing
You can actually do several different things on him and still do good. Disease, poison and cold all land well on RZtWL. When he's first pulled, I cast swarm pets on his back side to push him into corner (as well as other swarm pet casters). Then I Malos, Slow and Cripple (or we split those jobs up between the shamans). Then I land Ultor and Saryrn.

By the time all that's done, the adds are about to spawn. If they aren't up yet, I toss in a Velium Strike or 2 on RZ. Since I have Fury of Magic 3 and an ID 4 item, I can get pretty good damage out of my cold DD. Once adds are up, I try to malos, slow and root as many wraiths as I can. Don't root boars. Snare them, charm them and have them attack RZ. Rooting them will prevent this. Also, always use Petrifying Earth on the wraiths, for 2 reasons. It's the only root we have with a resist modifier (meaning it's harder to resist), and also it lasts a <b>really</b> long time if the mob is malos'd and tashed. I think it can last up to 3 minutes, and will normally last at least 1 minute with debuffs in.

Try to make sure you keep your dots on RZ. If CC is doing ok (unlikely) you can toss in some cold DD on RZ too. You want him to die as fast as possible. You can't control the adds forever, kill RZ as fast as humanly possible. Use your melee disciplines at about 90% on RZ. We corner him, so rangers can't use Trueshot. You probably already know about the AE, AE rampage and what to do to avoid those (buff FR, use max melee range).

Stick your best nukers with bards for Rizlona's (the DD bonus song). Do everything you can to get your dps as high as possible. Ferocity and Ferine Avatar on rogues/monks, too.

Luclin's Arriana
Mobs make a check against the root to break it every tick. Lower their resists, the root lasts longer. This has been public knowledge for years now.

The great part about petrifying earth is that ever since I got it, it is really quite rare for a mob to break root early (unless I nuke it). The reason why this is so is the resist penalty intrinsic to the spell.

TashX/MalX most definitely improve the duration of roots (not beyond max of course).
