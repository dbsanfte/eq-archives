Currently being evaluated – This means that a few of the development team members are looking into ways to implement the item. The Community Team plans to give updates on these types of items every 2 weeks, even if it’s just a “no change since the last update”

Currently being discussed – This is almost just like being evaluated however these items the development team is totally undecided if they want to implement or how to do so. The Community Team plans to give updates on these types of items every 2 weeks, even if it’s just a “no change since the last update”

Done – This means that the development team has implemented this item and it’s currently on live servers.

Currently on Test server – This means that the development team has implement this item and it’s currently on test server, watch for future updates for implementation on the live servers.

Waiting for community feedback – This means we don’t have enough information to make any type of decision on how to implement. Threads will be started by me to get your feedback.

No changes planned at this time – This means that the development team has decided at this time not to implement this item.

Items that are marked done will be removed from the top 10 issues lists and you as a class will be able to replace those with new issues. Watch for posts on this in the next week, your class correspondents will be helping SOE update your top 10 issues lists with these new items. If your top issues list was less then 10 issues to begin with you will be able to add more issues.

You as a class will have the option to replace items that are marked as “no changes planned at this time” with new items or leave them on the list. Please understand that leaving them on the list does not mean that they will eventually be implemented however it does mean that the class as a whole still feels that these are valid issues and would like implementation eventually. One thing to note, we will not be responding to requests as to why something is classified the way it is.

This post will be locked and updated bi-weekly.

I think the reason they responded the way they did was that they are looking for changes that will not change the class.

Here is the rub. Frankly, (there are numerous examples in game history) SOE could care less about changing classes. What they have done this time, over the course of a 'couple' expansions, is change the game without consideration of the consequences to class adaptability. Which, effectively, changes the class.

In a buffing sense, shaman cannot adapt to add more to a melee with maxxed stats. Cast all the buffs you want - if the stat is maxxed, it matters not.

In a debuffing sense, shaman can not slow an unslowable mob. Shaman can not completely slow a mitigating mob beyond their level of mitigation.

In both healing and quick dps, shaman are a 3rd rate (giving benefit of the doubt here) choice. Shaman are actually further down the list for dps and pointless to use as primary healer; very 'iffy' as a secondary healer.

This is said with the understanding we are talking about top end new game content. In older worlds, shaman are viable and useful and capable of contributing significantly. This is the problem, and we didn't cause it by just being shaman. The game changes affect us by design just because we are shaman.

SOE changed the game dynamics in ways that altered the class. The game changed the class from useful to much less so. Had SOE increased the resists rates of wizard nukes by 75% with a "100% lure resist" tossed on top of it, do you think they would of caught on by now such a radical game content change, while not changing the class, effectively resulted in changing the class?

When SOE is commenting about working on a definition and description of classes, I can only shake my head in ironic wonder. They are working on it? They don't know already? They don't have accurate class definitions they use to base game design against? (rhetorical question)

SOE faces some difficult problems. Mostly, IMO, the high end encounters need flavor. IMO, SOE recognizes the need to make new zones, new encounters and new progression different to keep the interest of players tiring of 5 years of 'same old' progression template. That is fine - no problem here. Diversity is a good thing. Problem is, the solution they have decided to go with alienates a specific class ~ shaman.

This appears to be acceptable to them. Realistically, the downside of doing this is ... what? A small percentage of the community will quit. A small percentage will continue to play their shaman. The majority will move on and make another class and continue to play. Beyond the small percentage of diehards who quit, SOE has lost no money. Of those who do quit, roughly half (IMO) will return and either live with their shaman or make another character - if they want a viable online gaming experience. All the hype about CoH, WoW, etc. has yet to go anywhere beyond the hype stage. In short, changing content to provide diversity at the cost of the shaman class (slow was an encounter limiting factor) is a small cost to SOE.

Is it nice? Is it fair? No and no. Yet, nice and fair have nothing to do with the decision. It is about keeping the 'majority' of players interested in the game. Shaman do no make up the majority. Shaman are not even, as a class, played (bots being exception) in equal player based percentage to other classes. If shaman were to walk out today, what would change? Encounters would be a bit more difficult - but doable. Shaman options are not as viable as warriors when they threatened their walk out. Warriors are a required component to level equivalent 'high end' encounters. As are clerics. Shaman contributions can be adequately, if not better, performed as secondary abilities by other classes.

Does this mean SOE has written off shaman? I think this is a yes and no answerable question. If unslowable/mitigating mobs are the future direction, the loss of shaman in the short term is acceptable. Go back to early ranger days. SOE appeared to write them off. "Ranga Down" is still a humorous comment ~ totally lacking in the truth of its reality today. Changes came. Rangers are now powerful, viable and sought after DPS. SOE did not jump in and do anything with them for an expansion or two (or three.) Then came the changes altered (er, can we say - changed?) the class significantly and made them much better than they were.

Assuming the same 'sit back and evaluate' perspective is alive and well, I expect that down the road the new niche for shaman will be found, developed and added to the game. The frustration is waiting for those changes. We all have opinions on what those changes MUST BE NOW. Now is not (again IMO) an SOE option for shaman.

Shaman will not become strong, viable healers. Shaman buffs don't need changing - the melee they are cast upon just need to be able to use them, which I don't see happening very soon. At least not until content changes to balance out the enhanced stats. Slow is gone. Shaman won't get new buffs because SOE has no idea/plan on what new buffs are needed to balance the player against developing content, much less which of those would fit into a shaman class description. I do think the official version is: "when we know what is needed we can give these spells to shaman to pull them back into the game."

Dot/nuke damage won't be initially changed, though I do expect to see an end to 2 minute disease based dots - and soon. Likely this will be in the form of an OOW AA. The bone will be bring them into line with poison based durations. The claim will be to allow shaman the ability to deal more damage faster in short duration fights. The fact poison dots don't often run full duration in these fights makes the argument silly. The majority of shaman will (as happened with the increase in 'healing abilities' bones fed them) accept it and cry from the mountaintop, "why are you whining, it is a positive change!!"

If not the above, something along the lines of a burst-dps AA will be added allowing shaman to consolidate the total damage of a single dot in a "nuke" type effect. The re-use will be long and negative consequences of using it likely huge (X10 aggro generation or some silliness.)

Over the next year, don't expect much. There will be tweaking, dinking and 'doh-ing' as shaman are toyed with to see what fills a need neither SOE nor shaman know is needed yet. Until these changes are incoming, shaman will remain in a holding pattern. Bones will be tossed, but none that have any serious impact on either the game or on improving shaman ability. At least, and this is not meant as compensation - just observation, there is lots of ingame content shaman can do and do well. Tons of it solo :eek:
