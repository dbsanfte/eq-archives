For those that haven't been keeping up to date:

In GoD we will be getting an AA called Secondary Forte. It will allow us to choose a second specialization. I assume almost everyone is currently specializing in alteration. Divination and Abjuration are out of the question (please no divination jokes, they're old and lame), so that leaves evocation and conjuration. I've been thinking about it, and these both sound like perfectly viable spec lines.

If I had been given the choice at the beginning of PoP, I would have without doubt jumped on conjuration as my second spec. At that time, I still soloed a lot, and I was still doing mostly luclin content for raids. I didn't have spear of torment of velium strike. I had even more pathetic nukes, but considerable dots. I was fighting mobs that took a long time to die and didn't resist my dots so much. As I went through pop, though, I started using my dots less and less. LDoN would have been the final nail in the coffin if it hadn't been for Bane. Mobs just die too fast, even in hard mode, for Blood of Saryrn to run full course. This makes is dreadfully ineffecient. Luckily, though, we did get Bane. It is a very powerful dot, and if you apply it early it will run full course or close enough. It is also very good for dotting an add, because it will almost always run full course. Blood of Saryrn is also good for this, and recently I have started memming it instead of malos for normal adventures (I looked back over an entire day's worth of adventuring - 8 adventures - and realized I hadn't cast malos once. I stopped memming it).

However, although I do use my dots in LDoN, I use my nukes much more. If I am busy debuffing or healing and I don't have things under control until the mob is at 50% life, it is too late to use a dot. However, it is never too late to use a nuke. In addition to that, our dots are also pretty high aggro. In most normal adventures I do, I no longer worry about overaggroing. A slowed ldon mob simply cannot hurt me, and if being the MT is the price I have to pay for putting out significant DPS, so be it. However, it can be a bit of an inconvenience. In most LDoNs, I am usually the puller. If I have aggro on the only mob in camp, I can't pull without dragging it along and thus annoying my entire group. I have to wait for it to be dead before I can start my next pull, thus slowing down my group. With nukes, I very rarely get and keep aggro.

So, typically, this is what I do in an ldon: Pull some mobs, slow them, VP one and Bane it. Then I /assist the MT and just start nuking. That's one dot vs. many nukes.

What about on raids? If you've read many of my posts, you know that I prefer the healing and support role on raids versus the damage role. Thus, I don't bother with dots. However, I do keep velium strike memmed and use it pretty often, although it is not my primary focus. The ratio of nukes to dots while raiding is, for me, overwhelmingly in favor of nukes.

As for soloing, I very rarely solo any more - it is just too boring. However, when I do solo, I use dots exclusively and I stack so many of them that it does drain my mana.

So, when I look at everything, I think I nuke a whole lot more than I dot. If I were given the choice to make right now, I would probably pick evocation. I'd only have two qualms with this: 1) Shamans are supposed to be a freaking dotting class and it just doesn't seem shamanly to pick evocation over conjuration. However, this is really just a flaw in the basic design of dots. 2) The only situations in which I heavily use dots (soloing and ldon vs. lots of adds), I find that dots drain my mana. I rarely see my mana drop rapidly from nuking, even from chaincasting velium strike.

I think when GoD comes out, I am going to wait to buy secondary forte, although I think it is going to be one of the strongest AA's we get. I want to see what type of encounters will be involved. If the expansion follows the same pattern as PoP and LDoN, and the encounters just keep getting faster and faster, I will pick evocation without a doubt. If things slow down a bit, more like encounters in luclin, I might be tempted to pick conjuration. If they give me a reason to solo again, I will be even more tempted.

So, what do you guys think?
