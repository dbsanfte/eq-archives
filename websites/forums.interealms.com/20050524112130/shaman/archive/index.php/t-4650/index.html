Slows are magic-based, so the save is versus magic. It adds disease counters so that you can dispell it with abolish disease rather than having to strip buffs off with cancel magic or one of it's upgrades (annul/nullify magic). I too find it easier to land slows rather than malosi's but turgur's has a lower resist check I believe so that's normal. If you're soloing and using your disease dots, by all means, use insidious decay when you get it. It does help. Keep in mind though, that insidious decay only helps with the disease dots. Malisment/maloXXX are needed to lower poison resists, as well as cold/fire/magic. You're gonna get resists, regardless of debuffing but debuffing does minimize the resists. Some mob have higher resists than others. If your dots are being resisted alot, try the cold-based nukes. Experiment and see which seems to land more often. Good luck. :)
Bigguss
Check out http://lucy.fnord.net - you can look up any spell and see what Save it uses (under "Resist").

Generally, slows save against Magic (except PoI which saves against disease), DoTs dave against poison or disease (depending on what kind of DoT it is), general debuffs save against magic (Malx series, Cripple series, Root, Blind, etc.)

On the CHA thing, I don't think anyone has shown definitively that it affects resists. This, combined with the lack of overwhelming anecdotal evidence leads to the general impression that CHA doesn't affect resists (except when charming- not exactly our specialty.)

One other piece of information that has helped me. Spells resist more when you first get them. For me, Togors (level 39 slow) is much easier to stick than Turgurs (my level 52 slow). It often doesn't pay to use your most current versions of spells when they have small incremental increases in effectiveness.

Hope this helps.
