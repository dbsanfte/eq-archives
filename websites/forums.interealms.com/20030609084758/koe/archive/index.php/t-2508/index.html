Ah, Dalnir *Sighs with happy memories*

My earliest grouping with KoE was in Dalnir while I was still a petitioner at roughly 30th level. Had some epic battles there, and most of KoE at the time would switch to alts to come to Dalnir.

For quite a while Dredwind (ryu's SK) and myself were often to be found grouped here, causing mayhem.

Good XP, Good Pay. It does not come cheap however. You should expect a good fight at all times. Now some background.

Dalnir is officially The Crypt of Haggle-Baron Dalnir, a former 'Chief Economic Advisor' of Cabilis. It has since become the home of an escaped sarnak called The Kly. The Kly was, during the days of the creation of the Sarnak, apprenticed to an Iksar who was executed for Heresy. He was found practicing the forbidden arts of magic. (Only Necromancy is allowed) Dalnir contains a special forge, called the Grand Forge of Dalnir, (or 'Always Works'). This forge was used by our heretic friend to create a special dagger, the parts of which drop in Dalnir. So it was here that The Kly fled, upon his escape.

The Kly has formed and trained a legion of Sarnak in the arts of magic, and has begun enslaving every single passerby he can get his hands on. These poor sods have a controlling device called a 'coersion implant' put in them, forcing them to do the bidding of The Kly.

Here you will fight goblins, iksar, dwarves, gnomes, erudites, tier'dal, goos, and of course sarnak.

These coerced types hit hard for their level, cleric keep that heal ready. They are fairly spaced, but with respect to pulling adds are common. Most PC classes are represented here as well, expect to fight warriors, monks, shadowknights, shamen, necromancers, paladins, clerics, mages, and unfortunately ---> gnomish wizards.

In particular watch the female gnomes - if they are with pet, they are mages, without pet they are wizards. The wizards hurt for this level, but can't take the abuse in return, so watch out.

FACTION: I'm sure this is a big concern to you goody-goody types by now, so rest easy. The only faction hit here is ThrallofKly (Thrall is the next step 'up' from slave - a slave could be said to be someone 'controlled' by someone else, a Thrall would be someone owned 'lock-stock-and-barrel' by someone else, as the saying goes)

Klydon
Dalnir: (In Warsliks Woods) Level 25-45. (Best is 28-34)

This is a much easier dungeon to get to than Kurns and it is between Cabilis, FV, and Overthere, so everyone can get there. Warsliks Woods is no picnic to navigate with many wandering mobs and terrain. There is a shortcut to Warsliks from the Lake of Ill Omen through the Goblin tunnels. Just invis and make a run through the tunnel from the Lake and you come into Warsliks. I recommend that some of the preparation (especially if no 29 or better cleric) include a couple of sow potions and invis potions for melee types if they have to make the run back to the zone. This will save a lot of time (and perhaps unecessary deaths), should a CR be required. While there are melee in the dungeon, I rate the casters more dangerous here. The dungeon has 3 levels and unique ways to get from level to level. 1st level is like any other, you go in the zone, you exit the zone in the same place. To go to the lower zones, you must go down a pit (lev works wonders here) and it is a one way trip. There is an exit on the second floor that will take you back to the entrance and also an exit on the third floor (right behind the hardest mob in the place). I rate this dungeon as one of the funnest I have been in. The action can be intense and a lot of fun and you will learn to work as a group.

Mob make up: 1st and 2nd floor have coerced mobs of varying types, the toughest being the cleric. 2nd and 3rd floor also have Kly. These are higher level and also consist of casters and melee. Goos and nibblers are present in the dungeon as well. The only thing that sees invis are several of the goos. Creatures will con like they see invis, but they don’t. This dungeon is rather unique in that the goos and nibblers can be non-agro. Don’t automatically assume that they are aggressive, but be prepared. Aggressive and non-aggressive con the same. Some of the coerced are SK types and have HT. I can tell you from experience that they are deadly against non melee types, particularly healers. Pets (mage, SK, and Necro) are present, allowing mages and druids to have some fun against summoned. There are wandering mobs here and the respawn rate is a blistering 13 minutes in the whole place.

Loot: Some good stuff drops here including the Sap of Piety, Practice Sword, and Monk Batons. You will likely see a large number of monks here as there are several monk items that drop here. Some guides show that the Necklace of Superiority drops here. That is no longer true.

Group makeup and tactics: Very hard to solo, even at the zone line. I do not recommend it. A well balance group is recommended and I recommend 4 at least with two being melee. The group should include 2 stunners (cleric/pally/chanter) to help deal with casters. If you don’t have them and happen to get two clerics, you will be toast as they will heal each other while beating the crap out of you. You don’t have enough firepower to kill both as fast as they can heal themselves at this level. You will either need a monk or a chanter for crowd control. If a monk is used, they should put in magic resist gear and MR buffed as they will get zotted a lot. This should even be done at the expense of AC. An evacer is also handy here as well, especially on the lower levels, given the problems of getting in and out. Pets can safely be used once a camp is established. You can pretty much camp anywhere on the top floor (away from the entrance is advisable) and I would camp near the exit on the 2nd level. There is little reason to go the 3rd level now that the necklace no longer drops. Where it was is an especially nasty place with multiple casters that like to nuke enchanters for bothering them. There are several statics near the exit on the second floor and what we used to do was to invis, lev down, head back to the exit. Once there, we would drop invis to see if the mobs were agro or not if they were nibblers. If they were non-agro, we would leave them in place. After that, we would work our way up to the room we would pull to and then pull the second floor. We would continue to check the spawns between us and the exit and keep wiping them out until they were non agro and it was much easier to exit the dungeon. The good news is that they will stay that way unless someone else attacks them or the server repops. That area was seldom camped and I think we went 3 nights in a row with the same non-agro statics back there. A 29 cleric is very handy if you plan to go to the 2nd and 3rd level as it will be hard for party members to reenter and go to the camp spot. If you don’t have this, all is not lost. Simply have a party member that is capable of casting invis and lev exit out the dungeon, meet the incoming member, do invis and lev and rejoin the party at the pull spot. One final note: many classes will have just gotten a big heal spell. The mobs in this dungeon hate healers. If you heal your tanks early, expect the mobs to throw a fit and attack you. This could be very dangerous if it is a coerced mob with a HT. If you are hurt a bit, you could go from 3 bubbles to dead from a HT.
