First up, you need to do some clean up work on your drive, EQ, Windows, and in fact most programs use a certain amount of drive space, dictated by the program itself, to swap files back and forth. Having too little hard drive space gives you a similar message to what you have above, sometimes "You have run out of virtual memory" messages too.
Second, do you have your hard drive partioned? if you do that actually decreases your hard drives ability to do this file swapping, causing lags and other problems. Only partition your hard drive if your wanting to do something funky like run Linux on a windows machine (if your gonna do that just use an emulator, works just fine).
Also check your current Radeon drivers and to see if you have the most up to date Dx9 version (9.1b I believe) a different version than what your video driver is looking for can give you headaches.

Edit: did some thinking real fast on your cleaning up problem, you really need to defragment your hard drive once a month at least, and some people recommend more often than that, also its good to wipe and re-install your OS every ~6 months to prevent corruption of files (it does happen). Most people skip that last part entirely, but gaming PCs, video editing PCs, etc tend to have more number crunching and processing going on than your average internet cruiser. If that doesnt work, you may just have a hard drive thats going bad.

Check your swap file settings, You do not have to have your swap file on the C: drive. You can move it to D: where there is more room, or split it between the 2 drives.

Clean up your C: drive! Take those music, picture, video files and move them to the other drive for storage, or better yet put them on CD/ zip disk. Uninstall anything you don't use regularly, if you really want to keep it installed then reinstall it to the D: drive.

With 512mb of ram on the system, you are forcing the system to use the swap file more for EQ. Each time you access the drive for the swap file you slow things down. I would suggest bumping the ram up to 1 GB that will prevent a lot of swapping.

I know I am getting in on this late and your problems are somewhat solved but not entirely.

I would highly recommend getting the entire Norton Systemworks package from Symantec.

It has a more than decent antivirus package(NAV/SAV - Norton Antivirus or Symantec Antivirus), a disk error resolver(NDD - Norton's Disk Doctor) and a disk defragmenter(SD - Speedisk) that is vastly superior to Window's built-in defragger.

It isn't really cheap but it is entirely worth it. The one problem is that it installs a lot of material on boot up that will waste a lot of valuable resources. Be sure and uninstall most of this stuff and leave only the antivirus.

Oh yeah, NSW also has a registry integrity scanner called Windoctor. Pretty spiffy. It helps to clean up garbage left behind in your registry and in some of the other system areas.

If you can't or more likely don't want to spend the money on NSW then Windows has a disk problem correcting program you should try. CHKDSK.

Click on Start, Run and then type in: chkdsk c: /f

The /f is important, otherwise Check Disk(chkdsk) will only check for errors but not fix them.

To use CHKDSK the volume must be locked. Which is normally impossible when Windows is running. You will get a message to that effect and be asked if you want to schedule CHKDSK to run at a later time. Answer yes and reboot. On the reboot CHKDSK will run try to find and possibly even fix logical errors that creep up on a disk during usage.

I don't want to offend anyone but I noticed the standard comment above about having to reformat and reinstall your OS every so often and that is true of many systems but only because they aren't taken care of properly. A computer system is like a house in many ways. It must have regular maintenance to remain in tip-top shape. The problem is that it degrades much faster than a house does and most users have no idea how to properly maintain it. It would be nice if more users were better educated about their systems but that doesn't excuse the fact that the systems should me more maintenance free.
