ciccero
The only problem with that idea is EQ uses 2 sets of graphics, global and local, to manage memory requirements.

EQ can't load every single graphic for every animation, class, and mob in the game into global (worldwide) memory.

Most graphics are local to the zones only, and are swapped in and out when you zone.

Global graphics are for things that might be in every zone in the game. Player character animations, illusion spells, pets, horses, etc.

In the case of shaman, we get a few global bears for the different race shaman to squeeze into, and in some cases, EQ even tries to cut-down on the storage that a global animation will use since that's the most restrictive.

Your best bet would be to petition to take out the current bear from the global graphicset and put this one in it's place if you like it better - however, that bearform in the global set is also used in lots of other zones as mobs, so that's an issue too.

Personally, I only use bearform when doing tradeskills.
