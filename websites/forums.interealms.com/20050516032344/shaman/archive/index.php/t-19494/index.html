NO PET. Useless, he just dies anyway and you burn mana trying to keep him healed.

Start at max cast range, cast malos and turn your back to the mob while casting. When it lands, take off in as straight a line as possible. When spell gems refresh, stop and turn around, cast root when you have successfully turned around (the time to turn around is about equal to the time needed to guarantee no interrupt due to movement). Root WILL land after malos, or if not just run, rinse, and repeat until it does. Hit it with malosinia at this point to reduce root breaks, and Turgur's just in case it gets in melee range.

Now cast Breath of Ultor. While this is refreshing, unmem malosinia and mem Blood of Saryryn. While BoS spell gem is refreshing, cast epic. Retreat to max cast. Refresh root. Cast BoS.

Canni 5 if it's up. Quiescence if necessary. At this point typically I have not been touched, I'm at 80+ mana and quiescing up from 60 health.

At this point, you can go the patient route and just refresh root/BoS/BoU as necessary (this will take around two full durations of BoU but will never break root), or you can accelerate things a bit by using the DOTs that break root. Turn your back to the mob while casting the DOT that breaks root and run until root is castable, then turn and root, just like you did with malos. I like to load it up with disease dots because if they break root, they only break it once every 2 minutes :-) so generally I might have three disease dots and 1-2 poison dots running, plus epic. Bane would help a LOT, I really need to get that.

Note that if you plan to go the fast route, you might want to start with BoS loaded instead of BoU - there will be time to cast only one BoU, so there's no pressure to get it in early; going the longer route means you need to get BoU and other disease DOTs in early so they can run two full durations and you get the max benefit.

There are variations - sometimes I don't use the epic because you have to get so close to cast it, and the damage for a whole epic run is worth about four ticks of BoS. Sometimes I do throw a second disease dot just to speed things up but no other root-breakers because I'm being lazy. I do throw swarm pets in there occasionally but the damage is negligible, it's more for fun than anything.

I canni4 constantly and canni5 when available. If I get hit, I reroot, back off, and Quiesce; I VERY rarely ever use Tnarg's, in fact I am about ready to boot it off my spell gems when soloing. No horses, they are too awkward for me.

I am short a lot of nice AA's, but I have some great focus items to make up for them. No, I don't have Burning Affliction, but I have a DOT extension focus that makes them last longer, excellent for soloing; a buff extension focus plus SCR1 that together make Q last an extra tick; mana cost reduction foci; and range increaser focus. Although I did all this before I had those, it's a lot easier with them.

AA's that would help on top of what I have would be to finish out the SCM and SCR/SCRM lines; Quickbuff 2-3; maybe channeling focus; LR and other defensive AA's; and for dungeon soloing where you invis between camps, Suspend Minion 2.
