Shokar
I posted this elsewhere, but will post here too. I made a pricing guide for Potions. I also give you several options too.

http://members.aol.com/shokaroffreeport/alchemy2.html

Now, here is my philosophy on potions. I can make 40 sow potions on about 42 attempts, sometimes as high as 43. Selling at 100 is a pretty nice profit, or SO IT SEEMS. My price guide, works like the medical industry's pricing guide. It does NOT cost $100.00 to make some drug, what you are PAYING for with your $100.00 is the research, and development costs.

It took me MUCHO WASTED PLAT to be able to make 40 sow potions in 42 attempts. I made A LOT of potions that I gave to my Paladin, or sold to vendors at a loss. I had A LOT OF FAILURES TOO!

So, in my pricing guide, I use the cost of ten potions, add the cost of 3 failures (development cost) then multiply that by the profit margin I am looking for.

Lower cost potions should have higher profit margins, and higher cost items LOWER margins, thus stabilizing the overall inventory control.

Feel free to use my guide, post it, publish it or whatever.

On undercutting: When a Shaman spends 1000 plat per two skill points, any idiot selling potions at greatly reduced costs is, well, an IDIOT.
