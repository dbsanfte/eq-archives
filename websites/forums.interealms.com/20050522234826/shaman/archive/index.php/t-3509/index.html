I remember when it was a common myth that AGI/DEX effected how often you dodge/block, STR made you hit for insane amounts of damage, STA was useless, etc.

The Shaman's Crucible was the first community to run empirical tests on stats. We figured out that AGI had minimal affects on AC, DEX was largely useless, and STR didn't affect damage output as much as everyone assumed. It was pretty cool watching the arguments, people would site authority as proof (brad mcquaid said DEX was important! Why would verant put in a useless stat?) and many other fallacies. It made me realize how myths were developed and how crucial logic was in debunking them, kind of changed my philosophy.

Also, I remember somebody ran a test on +ATK buffs like wolf form, and found that they increased overall damage by huge amounts - even if they only buffed ATK by 20 or so.

The conclusion was that the ATK stat was meaningless, since you could buff ATK 100 points with STR and notice no difference, yet 20 points with wolf form and have a big difference. The only mystery left was what did ATK only buffs (avatar, firefist, etc.) actually buff?

Anyone still have tests on stats lying around? My guild on Sullon needs some proof before they believe me.

Tudamorf did the ATK tests. STR was tested by several people. I remember at the time, this lead to people dismissing str as a useful stat because they couldn't find much evidence of average damage increase. I think they were expecting a greater effect from smaller changes.
