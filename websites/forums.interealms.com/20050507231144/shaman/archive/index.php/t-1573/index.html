BlargX
For your guild raids, wizard. For everything else, druid, hands down.

In exp groups or as a (solo) trio:

A second healer never hurts, even if you have torpor. Shamans are sometimes so busy slowing(and if slow doesn't stick the first time, even more so) that you have to either heal or slow, not both, until one or the other is done. With a druid around, slow with your shaman and hit a hotkey to health with your druid simultaneously.

Plus druid DOTS are very good, and their blasts are infinitely better than a shaman's.

DS shields are nice in a minor way; much less so against slowed mobs.

Druids have great fire/cold resists, which complement the shaman's great dis/pois resists. You are very well covered.

Spirit of Eagle does not suck.

POTG is a VERY, very, very good buff.

But most importantly of all, really -- a much more useful, long-lasting SNARE than wizards get. This is easily one of the game's best spells, and it's importance can't be exaggerated. It makes dungeons infinitely safer, and everything else too; and since it makes some mobs turn away and stop fighting at low health, it can prevent substantial damage over time.

Another nice thing -- your druid can use some of your hand-me-down shaman stuff. SBS, shield of bane warding, GEBS, DWB, iksar beserker club, molten cloak, etc. -- lots of shaman hand-me downs work just great for druids.

And, finally -- daddy bear gets a baby bear! ;)
