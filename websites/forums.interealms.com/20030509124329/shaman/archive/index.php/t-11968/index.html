Few points, please try to see these as constructive, I'm not a web designer by trade, but I am a designer who knows one or two rules about web design.

- Why do you have a random PoV link in your main menu?

- Lose the splash page. Telling people if they have a problem with it to just save a link directly is just going to annoy people, did you make the page for their convinience or for yours?

- Link back to splash page, one word, WHY?

- Lose the name, people are just going to think you're some kid who's just learned HTML with a name like that.

- Design a page that works in all resolutions.

- Try to avoid fixed page sizes, design a site that looks good no matter what shape your browser window is (within reason of course).

- Reduce download time, resist the temptation to fill your page with loads of images.

- Don't use Photoshop's crap image effects, they're not big or clever.

- Lose the hits counter, quality speaks for itself.

- PHP looks bad unless it's done properly.

- Never 'EVER' annouce a page until it's complete. Most people will look at your page once and never look at it again in it's current condition.

- Offer something you can't get elsewhere. Why say your site's forum is a large part of the site, when we have a far superior and far more established one right here?

- In depth spell lists are hard to come by, perhaps you want to develop that.

- Quests, another area which could be developed into something which is hard to come by.

- Don't steal EZboards smileys, they'll sue you.

- A few rules, keep it SIMPLE, FLEXIBLE and USEFUL. Never chose style over content, and be objective with your work. I'm guessing, but the site you have up currently was the first design you thought of right?

A typical example of how not to do a site is here:
http://www.eqbastards.harlo-online.com/main.html

I made this myself in a less patient day. On the surface it seems OK, then a few fundemental flaws creep in. You can't read the text easily and there's no room for content without spoiling the design layout. If I'd taken the time to sit down and draw out the site on some paper I'd have realised it was doomed from the start. Learn from your mistakes, and mine if you like.
