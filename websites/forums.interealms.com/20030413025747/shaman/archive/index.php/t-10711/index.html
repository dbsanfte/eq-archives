I for one am not ready to throw in the towell and say that I'm a broken class. I KNOW my abilities and what I bring to the table, and so do my friends and my guild, and that's all that matters. I'm not crippled by the fact that my malo casts 0.5 seconds slower comparing affliction haste to spell haste and blessing of reverence. I'm not crippled by the fact that my slow lasts 6 minutes instead of 8. I still like that blood of saryn lasts 20% longer. On longer lasting fights that's still more damage for less aggro and less mana. More than I had before these items were ever put in game.

I don't xp in pov much anymore since being flagged for other zones, unless I'm going there to solo. I fight harder things, and my dots are still viable damage). Lots of times I get chosen for groups for my dots, instead of a chanter for mez, because they know that the mobs will be rooted and rotting away while the melees chisel one down. But, even if in fast pulling fast killing areas, I can bring alot to the table in efficency, while my personal dps may be lacking in these circumstances, I can provide for a group with litterally no down time while staying practically full on mana in case the **** hits and fan, and then I'm there to play hero and save the group from a wipe. I can still fill in as tank, chanter, cleric or damage whatever the situation calls for, for the short term, until we get back on our feet.

In the last 2 years I've too made gear decisions that were based on the status quo, that later have been changed. Fungi stuff and Skyshrine Quest BP fall into this category. For example, Skyshrine BP was awarded to me before 1) our group stamina buff was as good as RH, and 2) before mgb and /tgb. Once luclin came out and these two things were put into practice, my mana savings on a free cast RH weren't consistant with the time it took to just tgb my group stamina on others. So I had a bp that was not only better awarded to rangers and rouges in my guild, since vindi bp was easier and better for me, but that also affected my consideration for other loot that would have in the long run been more suited for me.

But on the same hand, in the last two years, I've woken up to see items that I had all of a sudden given new power. My Shroud of Veeshan fell into this category. What was once a nice back item, all of sudden was godly when it was granted improved healing 3. (not so godly now that I dont use any under 60 heals, but for the months I had it before pop it was). I had even thought of that item as one of my never improve slots, until pop that is.

I'd like to see some changes to make my class better, who wouldn't? But faster malo and turgurs, I don't see as the make or break issues. I do see, getting a heal that can keep one rampaged person alive as an issue. I do see spectural's being wasted on a spell I'll NEVER cast an issue. I do see alchemy being completely gimp compared to other tradeskills an issue. I do see downscaled spells because of slow, when slow is mostly mitigated as an issue. I'd like to see curses implemented, or plus ability buffs. And I hope that while the dev team is reviewing the priest classes that they will keep all this in mind.
