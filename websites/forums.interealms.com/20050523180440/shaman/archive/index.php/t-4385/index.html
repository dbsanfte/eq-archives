Lol yeah sorry I meant it as a compliment. Shaman that spend their time during the formative years (and all classes really) will be better at their job later on. Dungeons are typically at least somewhat challenging. Outdoor zones are typically not challenging at all. Outdoor zones you always have the crutch to fall back on of SoW and lots of room to play with for running a mob around or whatever. This can sometimes be good exp out there, but it doesnt teach folks to really be solid in groups. If you always have the easy out available of just running to the zone, then if things get a little tough it makes sense for yo to just zone out and then come back in. if you are deep in a dungeon and you know that if you try to run you will get killed by the adds, that makes everyone pay attention a bit more and work better as a team. If your group can succeed and do well in a dungeon, then the outdoor zones will seem even easier to you. Anyhow, i was complimenting you and recommending that you do more groups in dungeons (many of the dungeons are bring your own group nowadays though) and hone your skills so that you can be the best shaman you can be later on.
