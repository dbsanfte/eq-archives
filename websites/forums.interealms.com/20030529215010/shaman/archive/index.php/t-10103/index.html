Level 65 Shaman here.

This was my thought process:

If you are finding that you have pretty ready access to ethereal parchments in your hunting, level up to 62 and stop. Then, if you are finding you have pretty ready access to spectral parchments in your hunting, level up to 64 and stop. Then, if you are finding that you have pretty ready access to Glyphed Runed Words in your hunting, level up to 65.

I did not level up to 65 until I got flagged for Bastion of Thunder and started killing named giants who can drop Glyphed Runed Words.

Regarding the experience thing:

Exp in zones like Valor, Storms, BoT, and CoD is so much better than exp in any other zone - it does not matter whether you are level 60 or level 65. It does not matter whether Fungus Grove is dark blue, light blue, or green to you. Time spent exp-ing anywhere other than Tier 2-3 planes will seem like time wasted. You will only hunt for loot outside PoP, not exp.

There are some decent spots in Tier 1, also. The Hobgoblin caves in Plane of Nightmare were a huge source of fantastic exp and Ethereal/Spectral Parchments for me. Take a strong group down there, though.

Of course, the most important thing is to do what is fun for you.

Uman
Shamam is right. He can get exp faster by being a lvl 60 shaman in valor in a group of strong lvl 65s.

Shamam is wrong. He could not get 3x the exp. He could get about 20% more (assuming his additional slow resists and other missing spells wouldn't slow down the group kill rate one bit). To see why, look at:

http://eq.straylight.com/exp.php

You will see that there is a huge loss of exp (half, in fact) as you start killing mobs more than 5 levels below you. You can also see that there is a slight benefit to killing mobs closer to or even higher level than you - about 5% per (your - mob) level.

If you can get spectrals, there is no reason not to ding 64. If you sometimes group without a bard or chanter, there is no reason not to ding 63 (the 60% haste spell will increase your melees DPS by 5% over celerity at least).

For a rogue or monk, it is more tempting to stay at lvl 61 or 63, because they do not see anywhere near a 5% increase in damage per level (more like 5% from 60 to 65). Likewise, a caster without access to spectral or runes is probably not seeing a 5% increase in power per level.

If your guild is in fact making serious progress toward new and harder targets, you should ding 65 so that you can get gear upgrades that make you (and your guild groups) strong enough to make up the lost exp.

In summary: you don't gain that much exp (in valor) by staying 60,61,62. At least get 63, and maybe 65. Everything you fight will be within the 5 level cutoff. You will have a harder time finding groups at lower levels (because people know you are mooching), and, more importantly, you will not contribute quite as much to your group. Don't hold back!
