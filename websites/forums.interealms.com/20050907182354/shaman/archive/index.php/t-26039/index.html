Ungkor
I don't have vp3, nor do I have clicky root, but I can easily solo two 500 hitting summoning mobs.

here's what I do.

Spell Lineup:

1. Nihili (or Turgurs if you don't have it).
2. Malos
3. Malicious Decay
4. 3 minute root (don't use 1min root)
5. Yoppa Heal (fast heal)
6. Spiritual Serenity (hot)
7. Pained Memory (canni)
8. Panther
9. Some dot depending on mob resists. (usually Yoppa)

So, you see two mobs standing near each other:

Mob A and Mob B.

(start at max spell distance from mobs)

1. Cast a hot on yourself, cast a hot on your dog. Cast panther on yourself, cast panther on your dog.
2. Target the mob you want to root.
3. Cast malicious decay on the mob you want to root (second mob aggro's and is inc now)
4. Quickly switch targets and sick your hotted dog on the second inc mob.
5. Switch back to the mob you want to root and hit VP root.

If all goes well at this point, one mob is rooted and your dog has the attention of the mob you'll be killing shortly.

6. Nihili or malos then turgurs the mob your dog is proc'ing like mad on.
7. Target the rooted mob and malos then slow then 3 minute root him. (or if VP3 don't bother rerooting now, just malos and slow).
8. Rehot your dog if necessary. Dot the mob, and either melee or heal dog, depending on how hard your dog is being hit. Remember, you take dmg far better than your dog does, and with panther on, you'll probably want to melee anyways.
9. Remember to reroot the second mob periodically, and keep a hot on yourself and hit canni the second it comes up.
10. Finish first mob and kill the second.


Basically, pulling two hard hitting mobs is all about the initial pull and controlling the second mob. Putting a heal over time on yourself and your full buffed dog before the pull buys you valuable time to lock down the add and get things debuffed.

A basic rule of thumb for solo'ing consistently is this: If my heal over time cannot keep up with the mobs slowed dps, I will not bother trying to solo them unless I really need to.

Everyone here has made really excellent points but I just wanted to add a couple comments since I solo most of the time and did so almost exclusively from lvls 30-65...

Everything they have said about VP is so true, you will love it once you get used to using it. It is truly a soloer's dream. Being able to quickly knock an add away from you without waiting for gem refresh is awesome, esp if you are tanking one and root breaks, you can just slam it back out of the way before it ever gets into your melee range most times.

When you are pondering your next solo session, you are basically forced into a primary decision that guides your strategies: are you going to tank the mob or rootrot it?

Each has its value and place. Tanking is fun and effective, more so now than ever due to leopard/panther. But with solid rootrot techniques, you can take on some real baddies, safely, as well.

If you decide to use a tank strategy then use your pet and I would recommend getting fluffy set up with conv. He will be able help you on adds a heck of a lot more. If you are rotting, don't bother with the pet unless he's needed for pulling.

Pulling with debuffs such as malos or MD (depending on if VP is ready at the moment when you pull) will help a lot because you know that you will be able to quickly get at least 1 mob parked rapidly and then move on to slowing and dealing with any adds.

Spells and efficiency: either style can be very demanding on your mana, because a) tanking you have less time/ability to cann while getting beat on (depending on the difficulty of the mob) or b) while rootrotting you are stacking up those dots alot (hint: don't forget to use our slow arse disease dots for high-hp mobs, they are very mana efficient). It is alot easier to sit on your mount and cann safely while rotting, but for high hp-mobs it can simply take longer to kill them. You kind of have to experiment to see what works best for you. I like being able to kill at a medium to high pace steadily with no pauses between pulls. This means that most times I rot them and go to pull the next just before the previous one dies.

Really, truly, believe me when I say that the torture involved in getting Bane is worth it. I see on your magelo that you have Sisslak but with Bane you will be able to kill so much more quickly and efficiently. Strongly recommend getting it!!! If you don't use tribute, another thing that can help soloing a LOT become more efficient with your relative mana/hp pool is to tribute affliction efficiency V and an extra 4FT to max your worn cap. If you have those running just while soloing, it's easy to maintain that amount of tribute and it makes a huge difference until you get focii to replace.

Don't forget to use spellsets to quickly swap dots and debuffs in and out and remember to always try to use HoTs instead of blast heals whenever you can squeak by.

Our mana/hp pools are similar and I can easily solo (more efficient to rot) 2 of the WoS murks at a time with no downtime and no risk involved. They are a bit more challenging and fun than many others in the area because they run darn close to bard speed. They are a good place to practice your newly polished soloing skills :)

For kicks and fun, go claim Drunt's camp in NC and solo it for a couple hours. Safe, single pulls and you should be able to solo Drunt with a little practice. Solo at 68, I make 7-8% AA per elite dragorn, except for on the PH/named (less here).

Brohg
Learn pet pulling.

1: Summon highest level green /con dog (Spirit of the Howler), buff him with hp & ac & avoidance & sow (or gopher if you're inside), and a cleric buff if you can get it. Hotkey /pet back off if you don't have /pet hold.

2: Stay way back, and send him in, watching HoTT on the mob you sent on. As soon as doggie pops up on HoTT, back him off all the way to you. Mob will come alone :)

Notes:
SoW is neccessary, because if doggie dies, mob agro will xfer to you, and the mobs you're trying to split from will assist and charge. This scenario, the pet dying, is why the technique is more popular with sk/necro. they can do it while feigned, so if pet dies mob just loses agro period.

The plan is somewhat touchy on casters, since they'll sometimes stop to cast and dog will run out of chase range. In the example you gave, you'd pet pull the melee mob, then after killing that go charge the caster on his spawn.

This is definately not freshman level shaman-ing. Probably 300-level if you manage to pet pull and still get the mob debuffed when he arrives where you want to kill him. There is a somewhat steep learning curve, even with it all spelled out for you, but it's a really valuable technique when you get it down.

Macnair
I have no problem soloing single mobs. Singles are a breeze for me. My problem comes when I get more than one. I don't know what I'm doing wrong.Several additional things will make it easier.

1. Ancestral aid; epic 1.5; pet affinity. This combination gives you two clicky HoT that land on both you and dogdog. Except with extremely hard-hitting mobs, this will give you one minute where you can tank 2 mobs while dogdog tanks a third one. That's enough time to get the matter under control again.

2. Virulent Paralysis 3. As others have said, this is very powerful. Used with Malicious Decay it will park many mobs with very nearly 100% reliability.

3. Swarm pets. At the highest level these will tank many trash mobs for the full minute they are alive, and do some decent damage while they are at it.

4. Developing a rhythm. The twentieth time you break a particular spawn is a lot different from the first time you do it. You really do learn a fairly intricate pattern of keystrokes, targetting Mob A, casting, targetting Mob B, targetting yourself, targetting your pet.

You don't have all those aa yet, but once you have them, the job becomes considerably easier.

Breaking a three spawn then looks like this.

Move invis into the place you want to fight from.

Target one mob, a melee mob by preference. Cast VP (or if you have learned that it is needed, MD and then VP Mob A. Mob A is punted away, two mobs are beating on you; pet is attacking one of those mobs, Mob B.

Target the other mob, Mob C, and slow it. You can now ignore Mob C for a bit; you just have to watch your own health.

Target dogdog if his health is 40 pct or lower and heal him, then slow Mob B; if his health is high enough, slow Mob B and then cast HoT on dogdog.

Cast SoL on dogdog.

Target dogdog's mob (Mob B), call swarm pets.

Target one swarm pet and cast SoL.

Cast HoT on yourself.

Mob B should be dead pretty quick, and all the pets will switch to Mob C, which has been beating on you for the last 40 or 50 seconds. Cast Bane on Mob C. This Mob will probably outlive your swarm pets, but not by much.

Now its down to you and dogdog versus Mob A, and VP is about to break. Refresh VP it if you need a moment to catch your breath. Otherwise slow it, cast SoL on dogdog, cast Bane, and watch the mob die.

I do this all the time. Sometimes it has more of a pinch: that's when you use Ancestral Aid or Epic clicky.

Good luck. :)

Gaknab
Soloing is an art. The more you do it, the better you get.

There have been a lot of good points made in this thread but one of the more important points was only touched on.

Know your enemy.

I cannot emphasize how important knownledge is. Learn which of your spells will land consistantly on which mobs. Resists are the bane of any solo artist.

Some mobs are vulnerable to Magic, some to Posion and some are vulnerable to both. Learn which DoT's work best on which MoBs. VP will hold it's full duration on some mobs but not on others. Learn which mobs you need to fully debuff and those that you don't. If you do this in every place you solo it will save you much time/grief/mana and make soloing that much faster and effcient.

There has been alot of talk in this thread about Pet Pulling, Melee Soloing and Pet Tanking tactics. All of these have thier place. However the reality is that these are only viable up to a point.

For the higher end stuff such as mobs in the Nest, MPG and Riftseekers root rooting becomes your only real option. This also applies to whatever is a "hard" zone for you and your gear level. There is only so much and shaman can handle depending on thier gear/skill level. There comes a point where MoBs just hit too hard and to fast that melee soloing and pet tanking is either death for you or uses so much mana that it's not a viable option.

IMOHO If you want to really excell at soloing I'd learn how to Root Rot in your sleep. A really good Root Rotter doesn't even let mobs touch them. You can solo much harder mobs with this method than any of the others.

The other most important skill I'd suggest learning is root splitting. It's much eaiser to kill 3 properly split mobs at once rather than trying to gain control over the same 3 mobs after pulling with malos, malicious decay or a pet.

Soloing is all about 2 things:

What's the biggest mob I can solo? and Where can I solo the best or most efficently?

If you can answer these two questions then you are off to a good start on learning what to do next.

And Finally don't be afriad to push the envelope!

Myself I solo in MPG and The Nest (Non instanced) I didn't really think it was possible to solo in either zone until I went and tried it myself. I took the time in each zone to learn which mobs need to be split, which mobs are vulnerable to which spells, which mobs summon and where I can safely kill solo in those zones.

Now about 4 months later I have a spot and cycle I do in each zone where I can hunt and collect decent plat as well as gain an AA every 25 - 30 minutes.

Take the time to learn about the mobs and the zones where you want to hunt and you will do well.
