It can be confusing.

A level 65 person in a normal level Adventure will get 51 points for success when the average level of the group is 63.00 or higher.

If the average group level is 62.99, the 65 players will get ~34 points from memory. The rewards scale rapidly downward every so many levels lower. The amount of reward given is determined by the Adventurer's level and the average level of the all the players on that Adventure.

I'm sure someone could figure out the exact formula, but I'm honestly too lazy to work out the math myself.

It would be discouraging to be the exact same level as someone else and not get the same reward. I would imagine (guessing here) that the formula uses a numerical value for the individual's cumulative experience total instead of just using their level for the formula.

Such that dinging at level 48 you might have 1 gazillion cumulative experience points. Halfway through you might have 1.1 gazillion points and at 1.2 gazillion points you would ding 49. If that's the case, the formula could work out (probably with some rounding involved) that two people the same level could be just under and just over the threshold for getting 9 or 10 points.

Even if that's the case, it would still be frustrating. :( Incentive to level isn't it? :p

It sounds like there is a total number of points set at creation of a dungeon. If that number is divisible by the number of players (scaled to lvl), then each player (of the same level) will get the same number of points. If it's not divisible, someone will receive less points.

eg: dungeon a is created and set at 7 points. 6 players complete the dungeon. 1 player receives 2 points, the rest receive 1.

This would also explain the points awarded on completing a mission after the first time limit is up. At lower levels only 1 player in the group will receive a point (ie the dungeon is set at 1 point for failure).
