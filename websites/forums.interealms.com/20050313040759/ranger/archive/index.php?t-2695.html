Let me start out by saying that EQ is an amazing game. I have encountered some really great people while playing this game and have enjoyed playing it over the past 3 years, as a 'casual' player. I created my character, Galaron, a ranger on the Quellious server, and have played only that character. I have been able tolerate some of the general problems with EQ (as perceived by me):
· Little to no story line and a lack of purpose. This is compounded by a poor quest system.
· Dungeon/area design that eliminates usage (and creates crowding in other areas).
· The occasional technical difficulty.
· The trade skills. LOL, spending hours on end doing the same thing over and over. Wouldn’t be an issue if some of the things I desired in the game didn’t require trade skills.

and the inherent problems in an MMORG:
· Great deal of 'down time'.
· Competition for limited resources (camping, competition for mobs, etc.). Especially in an environment where the population ages with no in-game mortality (top heavy).
· Encounters with people of poor character.

These things, I have been able to deal with. I'm afraid I cannot tolerate the post 50 game though. Here is what I am noticing. There appears to be little for a solo ranger to do, that interests me. The things that do interest me require a group or groups. I have been spending ridiculous amounts of time looking for groups. In the past, finding a group was not nearly this difficult. And even if I failed to find a group, I could move on to do some sort of interesting solo activity. The necessity to participate in raids to visit places and acquire items of interest is particularly distasteful. The time requirements are well beyond my ability to commit to as a casual player.

Just wondering if others feel the same as I do in this regard. It will be very interesting to see what comes of NWN.

Yzen Danek
05-10-2002, 10:07 PM
The problem, Epiphany, is that no such feature can exist. In order to be able to quickly put together groups, you need 1) a favorable balance in the number of classes represented online to fill required roles and 2) fast transportation between zones so that you can get people where they are needed. The second might be technically possible, but is undesirable if there is to be any sense of majesty or size to the world of Norrath. The first will never be possible. Even without people requiring the perfect group, there just aren't a balanced number of healers, melee, crowd control and nukers on at any given moment. No matter what the system is, the remainder will be lfg.

What's really ironic is that even people who are hard up for groups are reluctant to join a sub-optimal or unconventional group; they'd rather sit lfg at the zone than group 3 rangers, 2 mages and a necro.

Regarding rangers not being a very good solo class, I'd have to utterly disagree.
