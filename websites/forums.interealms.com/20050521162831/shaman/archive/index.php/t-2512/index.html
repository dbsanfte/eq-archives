Ok, this is different than the usual "What race" questions because he is _new to the game entirely_.

As stated above, every race has their pro's and con's. No matter what race you choose, you will eventualy be uber because you're a shaman ;)

The troll snare necklace: It's nice but it's not a game breaker. Comes in handy from time to time but that's pretty much it. More importantly why a troll is a good choice is they regen HPs faster and shaman can convert HPs to Mana starting at level 24. You do pay for this ability by having an experience penalty.

I would suggest you play a Barbarian or, if your computer can handle the graphics, Vah Shir. The reason why is you don't have to worry about being KOS (Kill On Sight) everywhere you go. You've got a lot to learn, one more thing to remember might be too much while learning the game. Play that character for about 10 levels just to get the feel of the class, then cast SoW on yourself & go wandering around. Go near some of the "dark race" cities and watch the guards come busting after you. The Ogres and Trolls have a lot of advantages (especially in Roleplaying) but you pay for that with people trying to kill you nearly everywhere. You will also have a harder time getting cash when you sell junk because your charisma will be lower.

Once you've played 10 or so levels with the light race, try playing with a dark race. Put the ogre-smack on some frogs. Watch your HPs climb as a Troll or Iksar. Once you've figured out how Faction works, _then_ make your decision on what race you want. My Barbarian is that race because I didn't know the game & was suggested to avoid faction until I learned the game better and I still play him. I also have a female Troll shaman that I play mainly for the roleplaying aspect. The regen is just gravy on top :)

I'm a lvl 21 Iksar Shaman which is my first and only character that I started a few months ago. Can't tell you about the other races, but can give you a few pros and cons of starting as an Iksar:

Pros:
Fast Regen - at first when you are soloing it reduces downtime by a good amount, but later levels you'll end up waiting for mana until Cannabalize at level 24.
Forage - although you do get summon food/drink early on it is nice not having to recast them everytime I log in and I rarely buy food. In Warsliks Woods you can even forage Bixie Berry Pies :p
Infravision - no need to carry anything special for seeing at night.
Swimming - you start as a master swimmer and it's a great way to travel!
Hunting - you can go from level 1-20 at Field of Bone and Kurn's Tower which is right next to where you start. Scorpions give great XP and you can sell stacks of bone chips for money to high level necros. Later on you can group with people or solo in Lake of Ill Omen and Warslik's Woods which are also right next to the Iksar home city.
Quests - at level 20 you can start doing the Scaled Mystic quests which give really nice armor for that level. Assuming you don't get twinked. Really easy to start your epic quest.
Look - Iksars look pretty cool, especially with the skull helmets

Cons:
Faction - no one likes you except other Iksars and player characters (well hopefully), but then again you really never need to leave Kunark until you are much higher level
Quests - the Iksar Shaman Skull Quests are a pain in the ass for a crappy weapon, but once you start you gotta finish out of principles :) Since you are KOS everywhere actually doing your epic quest is a little harder (or so I have heard)

Don't listen to all this crap about an item called a JBB (Jaundiced Bone Bracer) that is supposedly the most powerful item for shamans (or its made out to be). Its an item that you equip in your wrist slot and his a clickable feature (mana free) that allows you to cast a spell that does 256 Direct Damage with a 9 second cast time. Sure theres no disputing it is a great item but by no means does it make one race more powerful than another. Iksars are the only race that cannot use this item if you hadn't already gathered from previous posts.

Just pick a race that you like based on personal preference. A lot of people pick their race because of the way they look and I would have to say this is a good way to do it. Forget about starting stats and racial abilities and faction because if you play your shaman all the way to level 60 it makes very little difference at the end.

When placing your starting points into your stats I would have to say the main one to boost would be Stamina. Place as much points as you can into Stamina and put the rest into Wisdom. Stamina is by far the hardest stat to get up and it directly affects your hit points which become vital at the end game. The only thing I regret about making my Iksar Shaman was placing my starting points in the wrong stats (I put some in dex, str and wis EEK).

Now that you have made a shaman my best advice to you is to stick with it. Shaman's have patches where they become very tiresome to play with none being worse than levels 19-24. I absolutely hated my shaman during this time but if you stick with it being a shaman is very rewarding.

Let me highlight the reasons why I believe Shamans are the best overall class in EQ.

[1] We have a huge variety of spells at our discretion. We can enhance all melee stats, haste attack speed and boost hitpoints and AC for combat. We can increase movement speed, levitate and breathe underwater making us very durable. We have the second best Doting (Damage over Time) ability in the game making our damage output high if needed.

[2] We can have Armour Class as good as a melee making us able to take considerable damage from mobs without dying (unlike all other casters).

[3] We can debuff our opponents and lower all their melee stats and AC and slow their attacks up to 75%.

[4] We can regain our mana (ability to cast spells) better than any other class. Necromancer's can regenerate their mana fast however we can swap hitpoint for mana as well and have other mana regenerating buffs on us unlike Necromancers.

[5] We can make potions that allow us to use spells that are not in our spell book such as Damage Shields, Teleports and Mana Regeneration.

[6] We can solo extremely well however are still very sort after in experience groups. Classes like Mages, Necromancers, Wizards and Druids that also have excellent soloing abilities fall into the trap that they are not wanted as much in experience groups. Shamans get the best of both worlds in that we can Solo extemely well but are still highly sort after in experience groups.

[7] We are a key class on raids. Once again other classes which can solo well aren't considered key classes on raids however shamans are a key class in every facit of the game.

[8] Verant hates us for being so powerful and constantly improves other classes but neglects shamans (this is good because it allows us to post nasty messages about Verant).

So in conclusion play a shaman, love your shaman, be faithful to your shaman and in the end your shaman will reward you. Oh and have a look in the hunting guide section as there is an excellent post on how to play your shaman during different phases of his/her life.
