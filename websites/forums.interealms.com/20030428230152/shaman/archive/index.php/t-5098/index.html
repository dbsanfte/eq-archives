I think this is all symptomatic of a root problem that as the major institutions which used to promote stability in communities fail (ie. family, education, religion), we turn more and more to those remaining institutions (ie. government) to solve our problems for us. The point being that government -- as a manafestation of the social contract -- was only intended to allow each member of society to be productive by protecting a fundamental right to persue basic needs. Anything beynd that is an attempt to provide direction for society which used to be handled by other institutions.

For example, the state of Massachusetts has a law which forbids the sale of alcohol on Sundays. It is no accident that Sunday was chosen for this (the Sabbath); however, there was a time when the vast majority of people participated in the institution of religion which had dictated that Sunday was a day of family and reflection -- there would not have been a need for such a law for people would have observed sobriety because their religion told them to.

The less people are able to care for themselves through social institutions, the more government will intervene. This will continue until you do have government deciding what is "good" for you and what is not.
