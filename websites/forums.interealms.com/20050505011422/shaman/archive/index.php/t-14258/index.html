Jaruden
Iksar > all =P

Seriously though, it doesn't really matter, all races are equally good for shaman. Here's the lineup:

Iksar - Regen, AC bonus (about 40 ac at level 42, no more past that), swimming starts at 100, forage. Downside: 20% exp penalty, kos in all old world cities (and katta / seru), can't use jbb (level 45+ to click)

Troll - Regen, nice snare necklace, jbb (45+). 20% exp penalty, kos in most of the old world, but not all of it. Not sure on katta/seru.

Ogre - immune to stun from the front, jbb. 15% exp penalty, kos status same as troll, no extra regen

barb - only a 5% exp penalty, good faction. downsides are no infravision, no extra regen.

vah shir - I think 0% exp penalty (not sure). good faction, can sneak, safe fall. No regen, and lower starting stats than the "big" races.

froglok - hold breath longer, start with higher swimming. good faction. 0% exp penalty (i think) no regen, stats similar to vah shir. Probably the worst from a powergamer point of view, have less bonuses. On the bright side you get to do that cool flip when you cast =P

Korlace
Having leveled a barb shaman to 65, and now having my ogre shaman leveled to his 40s, I'ld have to say that no stun from the front is amazing. Very noticable during a fight, since it keeps you casting when all other races would be standing there waiting for a chance to cast. Regen is nice on the down time, but you gotta get the kill before there even IS any downtime. No frontal stun is the single racial bonus that helps you during the fight. I'm constantly amazed at how big of a difference not being stunned makes. With my barb, I was dazed and confused alot more than I had realized. To be honest, the main reason I rerolled my character and transferred my gear over was just to get this ability.

I agree, regen is very nice, but every single class can come up with a way to give you a few extra hit points at any level on down time (bind wounds). There is no spell or non AA ablility, aside from ogre's innate no stun from the front that can prevent a melee stun, end of story.

To the guy with 81 regen a tick standing, congrats. With the same gear and AA (fungi tunic + any number of aura of battle itmes + buffs), an ogre could get 76 points of regen standing, and still have no stun from the front. So your advantage is what? Less than 10% hps per tick more in exchange for even more of a racial xp penalty? Not to mention that the ogre probably has an extra hundred or so hps in the first place because of his higher starting stamina. Hardly worth it in the end game.

The typical lvl 44 shaman should have about 1500+ hps, and by 65 that shaman should have 2500-3000 hps self buffed at least (or much more for the if they've been reading the crucible forums and taking the advice here to heart). Troll/iksar regen is 5 hps per 6 seconds, so 50 hps per minute. Any 44+ shaman with stoicism really isn't going to miss those 50hps.

At lower levels for the non twink, regen is very handy. 50 hps of healing per minute to a level 10 is amazing. To a level 20 it's noticable. To a level 30, it's handly, but won't give you much of an advantage. By lvl 45, you won't notice. With the recent (last 3 months or so) game changes, only the most casual of players will stay under level 40 for too long. As you get the the end game, you're really gonna miss that no stun ability while you're trying to get that slow off as you tank a mob.

So, in a word, I'd say the frontal-stun is nice if you're a soloing shaman, if that's the path you choose to take. Otherwise, I wouldn't let that be a determining factor.
