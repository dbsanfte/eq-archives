EQPolaris

The refresh time is 12 minutes. Level 1 of spirit call will do about the same amount of damage as a bane of nife, assuming that your pets do not die. That is really not that much damage. It's nice to have as an added bonus, but I feel that other abilities will help your more for soloing. The three that I listed, in addition to defensive AA, all have a much greater effect than spirit call does.

Another thing to be noted about spirit call is that in pop, just using rank 1 of spirit call, your pets will not survive the full duration, thus dramatically reducing their damage. If you are still soloing in the old world they will usually survive the full minute, but in pop they aren't that great for soloing. Also, when grouping, many mobs do not even last a full minute so you have to cast spirit call right at the beginning of the fight in order to get the maximum amount of damage from them.

The only time I find SC very useful is when I am pulling in an LDoN group and there is a room with three or more mobs that need to be split. I just send my pets in and every mob in the room will attack them. They will survive long enough for me to pull each mob off one at a time with slow and then root it.

So, in summary, SC is really not that great of an AA and has pretty limited usefulness, although you'll probably get a real kick out of it the first dozen times you use it or so. I trained SC right after pop came out and I thought it was awesome for a few days. Then, the novelty wore off and I left it at rank1 and didn't train it any further until I'd gotten another 200aa. I often regreted spending those 4 points on SC as early as I did, and I do not regret waiting until 250+ aa to finish it.

As for Radiant Cure, it definately is not a bad AA. However, at rank1, it is extremely inconsistent. If you get rooted or snared I will give RC1 about a one in five chance of saving you. In addition to that, RC1 only takes off 9 poison/disease/curse counters. That won't cure anything except tash. RC3 will cure a slow, but it won't cure any of the LDoN traps or curses that I have come across. So, basically, the chance to remove a detrimental spell is decent if you have RC3. Otherwise, RC isn't that great and with blood of nadox you just don't need the counter-removing component. And honestly, how often are you in a situation where you need RC? It hardly ever happens to me. I would much rather have one of the three archetype abilities I listed.
