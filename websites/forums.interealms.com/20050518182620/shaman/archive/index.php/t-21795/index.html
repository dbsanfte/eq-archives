Weyalan
1. Shaman are weak in PvP, even at high level, almost any class with comparable levels, gear and number of AA can either kill us, orstalemate us. There are rumors of changes around the corner, but for the time being at least, shaman are not the PvP force that they once were
2. Levels 16 - 23 are probably the hardest levels of a shaman's career for soloing.
3. Yes, getting canni1 and regen will make a big difference to your ability to solo, but frankly, soloing for decent exp returns is never a stroll in the park - soloing is fun becasue it is risky
4. Do a search on "cannidance". Learn how to cannidance, how to hotkey it and how to love it
5. Soloing is still a viable way of levelling for a shaman, but you will get significantly more exp/hour grouping than soloing. Personally, I think it is important to do a bit of both
6. LDoNs are your friend

Grafsha McDaughts
As was pointed out, check other posts. Fungi is not the end all item at all for a shaman. Check out other gear threads.

My personal journey as a troll shaman was periods of tough going early on. While your spell lineup improves dramatically from 24 and up, the buff duration is short, the dots cost lots of mana for the level. Canni-dancing will be your friend forever (or horse canni.)

Shaman can solo well - at all levels. Well = attack mob, kill mob, live, increase xp. Learning which mobs/zones are easier to do the above is key. Kind of pointless casting dots that are resisted on mobs consistantly ... and there are a number of zones where that is just how it is.

Mellee defense/offense skills should be a priority to max at every level possible.

Grouping at least 1 level for each spell level cycle is strongly recommended. Shaman are versatile group additions. There is a lot shaman can do and based on the group makeup it is important to learn what to do with who against what. Yeah, you are going to be the slower - 99% of the time. Yet there are groups where you may not be ... as your talents are better used doing other stuff.

By the time you can get KEI you don't need KEI. It is a nice buff, but you are not a caster or druid or cleric. Canni/regen more than makes up for KEI. And, even with KEI, you are still going to be canni dancing just as much.

Once again - gear. Look up some threads on this. Think tank with a set of spells. HP = mana (more to canni.)

Soloing, I found 49 the magic number for my shaman to break away from the pack and solo effectively and efficiently. 49 spells are a major power boost. Before that, 34 pet is a big step ... though you learn quickly the pet is not your tank.

An additional bonus now, that negates the value of the fungi later on, is the increase in innate regen the longer you sit.

To answer one of your base questions: will soloing as a shaman become too easy? No ... cuz what you can solo is above what other classes of your level can solo, with a few exceptions. If you want easy, it is there. If you want challenges they are also there. Your choice. Your call.
