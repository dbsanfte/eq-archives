Binter
Actually, it can kinda work to pull a single, because so long as the mob remains rooted, it retains the social agro from its original position. The instant VP breaks and it begins to move, its new position becomes the center of its social agro.

I've used it to pull singles by hitting a mob that won't social agro on intial hit, but DOES agro when it moves by moving into the social agro range of nearby mobs on its way to you. By smacking the mob into a new position and then moving myself to a point where the mob won't agro others on the move, I've managed to pull single mobs away from potential adds.

It's not a universally useful trick, though. It only works in zones where mobs have a low agro radius, like Droga and PoFire. But in those zones where it does work, it works very well.

Want a pointless trick? While VPing the mob, after you start casting but before it goes off, turn your back to it. Sometimes instead of punting it away from you, it actually yanks it TOWARDS you. I haven't quite figured out when and how it decides to do this, but I have seen it happen enough times to get me curious as to what causes it. So far, my best guess is that it somehow involves client-server lag that confuses the server as to facing and position when the VP command reaches it. ;)

Needless to say, I think that this trick has some potential to kinda reverse-push a mob for more control over push and position, if i could figure out how to make it happen when I want to.
