Bigsnack For'Mobs
Since the NDA has been lifted I thought I'd copy a post I just mode to the EQII beta forums:

In response to a post against the rarity of spells Eviljim posted:

"What I am saying is there is not a distinction in your above argument between a spell and a weapon. Both have easily or freely available versions that are usable, but you have to achieve (in some fashion... adventure, craft, socialize... or all of them) better things. This is core to games that have acquisition and advancement as an element, whether it be an MMO or otherwise."


This is a recent development of flawed reasoning that is threatening both EQLive and EQII. The melee correspondent to caster skills is _abilities_ not weapons. There are many abilities but let's just focus on one. If a rogue had to have a high level guild raid to acquire the ability to backstab at the highest level it would be equivalent to the rare drops for spells or the "ancient" spells in EQLive. Melees get their abilities automatically instead of having to buy them like casters do spells.

Both casters and melee are equally dependent on obtaining higher level equipment and need to progress similarly to obtain higher level gear. How much healing or damage a caster can do is dependent on gear -- not on the spell that lets them heal or damage. Eviljim your argument would have merit if casters only had to cast once but they need to cast repeatedly. Their ability is measured in how often they can cast over a period of time and that is dependent on gear.

If you wish to balance casters and melee then make the melees buy their abilities and make the higher levels harder to obtain in the same way that spells are.

Bigsnack For'Mobs
Let me be clear, I am NOT saying we need to change melees to balance things. What I am arguing against is the idea that it is only weapons that make a difference to melees and therefore it is right to make spells almost impossible to get. This is bad logic and terrible design.

The worst of these design flaws is making a spell like Balance of the Nihil almost unobtainable by most shaman. This spell is _different in kind_ than our other spells and has no corresponding OoW spell. I got very lucky and was able to get Nihil and I can tell you it is very different in how it works in practice compared to Turgurs.

Even regular spells though should be reasonably obtainable. Just because a higher level spell can heal for more or nuke for more doesn't mean the caster doesn't need to have the equipment to use it over and over again. We don't heal _once_ or nuke _once_ we do it repeatedly. Without the mana pool and focus items we just can't do as much with the spells.

Both melee abilities and caster spells are the basics we need to be able to do our job and should be available. Our overall equipment relates to how well we can use weapons or spells and that is where the progression should be.
