Binter
Three things have to be done if EQ is to survive:

1: close the ridiculous gap between "uber" and "casual" players. Until they do this, they cannot make an expansion that appeals to everyone, because it will be absolutely impossible to design content in one expansion for both sides and yet make the expansion reasonable in size and content. This means that SoE will have to either A) upgrade gear attainable by casuals or B)"nerf" uber gear to make it more in-line with casual players, then adjust raid mob difficulty proportionately to take that into account. Option B would be much harder to accomplish than option A. However, to prevent a sudden "Monty Haul" syndrome, scale this new gear in such a way that casuals would have to have good gear in the first place to get access to it. In other words, create a way to progress through gear WITHOUT the flagging nightmares of PoP.

2: change the game from monotonous grind to something FUN. SoE needs to de-emphasize grinding xp/AA and concentrate more on quests that can be completed solo or with groups. Set it up so you get that XP/AA while DOING the quests. Duh.

3: stop the ridiculous mudflation and restore server economies. Much as it pains me to say this, but SoE should institute required level restrictions on ANY piece of gear that drops in a level 30+ zone, scaled so the item's rec level is 5 levels below the average mob level of the zone. In addition, accounts should have a limit on the amount of plat that can exist for any one character, say 400kpp per character and a maximum of 100kpp in the shared bank slot, but allow an exception to this rule for a REGISTERED guild bank mule (as in officially inspected by SoE and flagged to allow it to carry more than the maximum allowed plat in the account). This will kill a goodly number of duped plat money laundering accounts. Finally, SoE MUST crack down on IGE and other plat-for-money operations, because they are why our economies are so messed up right now.

If SoE doesn't do something, the game is going to fade away.
