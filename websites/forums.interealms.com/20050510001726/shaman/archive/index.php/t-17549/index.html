Juujuu
but just the act of turning someone makes the game think you aren't moving.
That would be because you aren't moving.
If you are running forward and stop holding down forward, it takes you a fraction of a second to stop running forward.
If you start turning your character right after you finish running, the amount of time it takes you to do roughly a 90 degree turn to the side is more than the amount of time it takes you to stop running forward.
Soooo, if you start casting a spell after turning a bit, you know for sure that you've stopped running forward and you won't interupt yourself.
So ya, the reason the game doesn't think you are moving is because you aren't moving anymore...
This of course only holds true on normal ground and not icy surfaces.
If you try this on ice, you'll just be sliding sideways instead of sliding straight ahead.

Actually, regarding the turning while running thing. Abasher or one of the PR people posted a long time ago that the game does not update constantly. So while you are running, sometimes when you stop, it takes a few seconds for the game to realize you've stopped as far as casting is concerned. When you turn, it sends an immediate update to the server of your new position. Which of course now also lets the server know you are stopped. So the trick to turn and have less interupts after stopping actually came straight from Abasher.

Boinshar
Assuming you are not being hit (which forces an interrupt check), spell interrupt check is based on the difference of your position when your started the spell and your position when you finish the spell. You can begin a rather long spell in a corner, run all over the room, and come back to the corner just before the spell completes (let go any direction buttons, full stop) to finish casting without dealing with an interrupt check.

When you start to move from a state of rest, it takes a faction of a second for the game to realize that you moved. When casting a spell, you can start moving when the cast bar still has 1/4 to 1/2 of a purple to go and successfully cast your spell without an interrupt check.

Chutoi
I believe mobs that summon don't start summoning until 96%. You can use that for pulling, agro control, etc. Summoning mobs are also timed on summons. Use that to your advantage if you need to root and back off. I think it's 15 seconds between each summon, but I forget.

For those of you with less well equipped groups, the tank may have trouble controlling agro. Use root. Root makes the mob turn to the physically closest PC in melee range. Thus, if a shaman has agro for most of the fight due to a poor tank, warn the melee classes, root the mob, and let the tank get in its face. Rogues can still get just barely in range (everyone calls this using max melee distance) and it won't turn on them as long as the tank is closer.

Couple of other random things--while it's not ALWAYS true, the large majority of unsnareable mobs don't run. They usually enrage as well. Enraging mobs never run. Make sure all melee and your pets are behind the mob and you can keep hitting it while the tank has to stop attacking during enrage.

Unless you are trying to hold agro, pretend you're a rogue and stay behind the mob to melee. Most of the defensive skills (for both PCs and NPCs) only work for the front of the mob. If you are behind them, I don't think they dodge, parry, or repost--thus your dps will go up. If you are running from a mob, YOUR defensive skills don't apply--they'll do a LOT more damage to you from the back--if you are off-tanking a mob and have to slow something else in camp, back up until you can see the mob without turning your back on whatever is hitting you.
