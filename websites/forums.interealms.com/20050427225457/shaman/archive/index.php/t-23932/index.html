It's hard to describe - and i'm not the kind of person who likes putting down numbers (such as XP you get per mob, XP til level..).. . frankly, I hate numbers, math, and everything associated with them. :D

And i'm a computer technician. Go figure.

In the stress test, my very first character was an orc shaman, and to get to level 10 took me ... give or take, 3 days, and that was progressing very slowly - I was checking out the sights, trying to figure out where the quest NPCs and targets were.

If you're playing the game from the start, brand new, and you dont go to spoiler sights and message boards to learn the "Uber XP Spots" (cough..paludal..), then I think that the XP curve, just hunting, is just about the same as EverQuest's. Add in quests and it speeds it up, because quest XP rewards are very significant for an even leveled quest.

I remember those days in EQ, and I remember getting my first character to level 50, I think I made it about a week after Kunark was released. :D

I dont know if it'll take years for a casual player to hit the level cap, but I can fairly safely say many months to a couple years (depending on your pace - even between casual gamers this varies alot) to get there.

Some people will hit max level in a month. Heck, in the stress test, the short time that was going, people had reached level 40 already. Frankly, I can't even fathom putting that kind of dedication into a game :) Especially, as you said, you just miss - or blatantly skip - a lot of content to do that. Like Paludal again in EQ - it's just too fast. You miss out on, well, everything.

I'm not one of the people who say EQ is a dismal flop of a game with no story or redeeming features - heck, I love EQ, that's why i played it for so long. EverQuest, especially back with Verant, had a great storyline for almost every aspect (and I love the epic quest stories, especially shaman). But, it's only there if you look for it.

Leveling does slow down at later levels. The amount of XP you need for each level does ramp up steadily. Plus, i'm fairly sure that XP gain is still artificially inflated for beta testing purposes. I'll get back with actual feedback from my experiences and not just hearsay from the boards, though, when I get to experience it myself.
