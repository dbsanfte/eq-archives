Graal the Dorf
I'm a 60 paladin and my wife is a 60 shaman.

We complement each other very, very well. We can take on mobs that a cleric/warrior, shaman/monk, or shaman/warrior combo would have no chance at. A monk is going to generally be a better partner for fast xp, simply due to damage output, but the PAL/SHM duo is much more flexible and a hell of a lot tougher. Paladins don't have nearly the damage output of monks, but the amount of mana we save the shaman by allowing them to cann constantly allows THEM to make up the difference with dots.

Generally we try to save my wife's mana for damage output and slows. Most of our healing comes from regen and CC. This leaves my wife free to stack dots, cann back mana, buff, and slow.

My wife doesn't have to worry about aggro from slows or dots, she can pile em on right off the bat. A quick stun, and the mob is mine for good.

All of our buffs stack, the only thing we are missing as a duo are ports, corpse summons, snare, damage shields, and clarity.We very rarely die.

Basically, a paladin and shaman duo is tough as nails, has most of the bases covered well, and can go almost anywhere and fight almost anything. You cannot find a better partner for a paladin. A monk might be a better partner for a shaman. It all depends on what your priority is. If you want fast xp, go monk. If you want to be able to take down big targets, go paladin.

BlargX
I've played with a lot of pallys, and really have come to love their helmet heals, celestial heals, and ability to keep a taunt.

The tricky part about shamans and pallys is that they both take radical jumps in power at certain levels. Shaman's radical jump(discounting malo) comes with torpor at 60. Pallies can seem very low damage until they get really great weapons, and if they don't have celestial heal yet either, can seem like they really don't contribute a lot.

But even pairing helmet heals with a shaman who doesn't have torpor yet can make a nice difference. And celestial heal to a pre-torpor shaman is godly. It doesn't suck to a shaman with torpor either, but it isn't as dramatically empowering. Besides regrowth, shaman heals are actually pretty miserable at high levels before torpor. Pally can help a lot with that.

When you get a torpor shaman and a well-equipped pally, you can be pretty confident. You still won't generally get the experience you will get if you were with an equally well-equipped monk, because experience groups are more about fast damage than being able to survive forever. Experience groups tend to seek out weak, low hitpoint mobs. So monk does better there. But when a pally FINALLY gets around to putting out significant damage, it's a relief to everyone, and he becomes a really good partner. Shaman casting haste on him helps a LOT.

Undead mobs help a lot, too. ;) I envy anyone who groups with paladins vs. the undead once they get their AA skills and good equipment.
