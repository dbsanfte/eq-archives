Krup
I like the WoW approach to PvP. In my guild, we call it consensual PvP - you can choose the time and place to fight. It's what we prefer as a guild; we left UO because of "non-consenual" PvP, eventually tired of EQ becuase it was one or the other (duels and arenas just aren't the same), liked DAoC and SWG because of how they implemented PvP.

One thing we've found is games seem to have a hard time being good at both PvP and PvE (player vs environment, or computer). EQ has great PvE and mediocre PvP. DAoC has mediocre PvE and good PvP. SWG had very good PvP, but the game was too buggy. We have high hopes for WoW and see a good balance (i.e. enjoyable playability)between PVE and PVP so far.

Like Iz, I learned the hard way you need to know all the rules for "consenting." Many of the quests we do require killing opponent NPCs that will trigger your PvP flag. Many opponent players will hang around these quests hoping some of the questors don't know this rule. It's all good!
