weissblau
So you're in Innothule.... Getting to FoB would be difficult and IMO not worth it. Getting to Paludal would be easier and definately worth it, when you get to level 10+. The first part of the run is the same - get yourself to NK or GFay so you can take the spires.

The GFay run is what I'd choose IF froggies are non-KOS in Freeport and Butcher, which I'm not positive of. If so, just run north from Innothule to South Ro - Oasis - North Ro - Freeport, boat to Butcher, run to GFay.

The NK run is goes through Innothule to Feerott, then to Rathe Mtns (you can avoid aggro here if you're careful), then Lake Rathe, South Karanas and then North Karanas. You will most likely need help, OR just bank all your gear and make the run before level 10, if you die no big deal. BIND in Shadowhaven before proceeding. In fact, the perfect level is level 9, because then you'll have SoW but won't lose XP for dying (do you leave a corpse at level 9)?

Once in Luclin, you can get to Paludal easily, but if you're only level 9 you will really need to go THROUGH Paludal and go to Shar Vahl. Bind in SV and then XP grind from the SV side of Paludal, it's much easier there. You will need invis to get through, beg at the SH zoneline long enough and you'll get it.

If you really, really want to go to FoB, the run from the spires in DL to the FV zone is not bad IF you get SoW, lev, and possibly invis at the outset. Go through the tunnel between the spires and the rest of the zone, then climb the glaciers on either side of the tunnel. Get as high as you can, make sure you are looking level (hit HOME), get lev up, and take off. As a small race, you won't hit the ground until you get to the zoneline; this lets you bypass ALL the nasty mobs in DL. Zone into FV and run straight through the tunnels; when the tunnel opens out, hug the RIGHT zone wall really hard and then swim across to the city. Bind at the soulbinder on the docks in FV.

Leave the main gates of FV and run RIGHT to the cliff edge. Run up the cliff edge, watching carefully for Drolvarg aggro, and hug the south and east zone edges tight until you get to the Swamp of No Hope zone. You'll have to leave the zone edge to go around the Drolvarg temple, just watch carefully for aggro there. From SoNH it's a simple straight shot up the east and north zone walls to the FoB zonein.

The PoK books really made it nice and easy to get around, which I appreciate at high levels... but it was fun accepting the CHALLENGE of moving around in the old days... I still remember the rush I got when we avoided the spiders and safely arrived in LOIO at level 17, or when we made the run from Qeynos to Freeport at 13.

Tobasko
Nurga is a dungeon accessed from Kunark's Frontier Mountains. There are five notable things about Nurga.

First, you enter the mines my jumping off of a waterfall that is impossible for some to climb back up. If you are bringing any melee friends with you you'll need to invis them to get them out.

Second, none of the mobs see through Invis.

Third it is the location of the sleeping oger for the monk Epic quest. He is non-agro and is really only natable because of the area near where he sleeps is chock full goblins that come two at a time.

Fourth, the goblins all give +chardoc faction hits. If you are going to do any of the quests in chardoc you can get a bunch of your faction work out of the way in Nurga. (and get xp for it too)

Fifth, Goblin Warders have the chance to drop the Shroud of Nature. It used to be uber, but not sells for 100-200pp in the bazaar(or 10pp to a vendor). It's lore and I've let more rot than I care to think about.

Here's what EQ atlas has to say about Nurga. It is notable beacuse it is partially wrong and may be the reason that the zone is usuall empty. LINK (http://www.eqatlas.com/kunark/minesofnurga.html)

The entrance to the Mines is kind of tricky. There is a large waterfall at the entrance from the Frontier Mountains, which is a very long, and painful fall. This waterfall can actually be climbed if you're careful, so isn't as dangerous as you might think. Going upstream actually leads to a safer way to access most, but not all, of the rest of the Mines. The entrance from Temple of Droga, however, is very one-way, and leads down into a room with goblins in it deep within the Mines, so it's best to be ready.
All of the goblins in the Mines will aggro anyone, and many of them are casters who cast damage shields. They also like to run away when they are hurting, which can be dangerous with this many creatures around.

The spiders are tougher than the goblins are, and hit hard and move fast. They also will group together to fight in that area. Located in their area as well is the Sleeping Ogre, who really isn't sleeping and will attack anyone inside of his huge aggro radius.

The Burynai are the hardest, mostly because they are in large groups and come to fight that way. Crowd control is essential, and the pathing in this area is known to be especially bad.
