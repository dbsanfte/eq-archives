Jinheim
First of all, your formula is a bit off. I don't find AC to be of much importance, certainly not as important as your mana pool. For us, it is better to have high HP than it is to have a big mana pool, but you need to strike a balance between the two. It's been a long time, and the gear available has changed a lot, so I don't really know much about gear available for you. However, it looks to me like you're doing pretty good. For a shaman, your gear isn't that important. It's much more important how you play. Don't listen to the people that are harping on you for wearing GEB's. They only do that because it is such a visible piece, and every druid and their mother wears GEB's at some point in their life, and people like to make fun of druids on these boards.

So in short, don't worry about it you are doing fine. However, if you want some new boots, I would suggest barbed dragonscale boots, which come from turning in WW dragon heads in kael. They used to be about 4k on my server, but have since risen to 8kish, so I don't know how much luck you'll have getting a pair. They served me well for a long time, though.

Uggok
Just to add my voice to the chorus, at your level AC is enormously important. regen > ac > hp for quite a while. Jaundiced bone stuff can be had cheap (except BP/leg/bracer, though I don't know their current prices).

Max hp are like max mana - not that useful in a pre-PoP xp grind. The rate at which you lose and gain hp and mana is MUCH more important than your max in each. So things that regenerate health and mana (regen items and FT [I know that FTisn't a practical alternative]) and things that lessen your use of health and mana (AC and focus effects) are much more important than +HP or +Mana.
