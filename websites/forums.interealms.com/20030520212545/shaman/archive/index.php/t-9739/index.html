Yes, little Finland has an Army. It even has a history from WWII.

The Winter War of 1939-40 saw the Soviet Union demand Finnish Territory at threat of war. The Finns declined. The Soviets, thier command devastated by the purges of the 30s, attacked and were stopped. Such was their belief in an easy fight, they invaded in Winter.

Average daily temps were in that -50C range and the Soviet men and equipment froze stiff. Two Soviet divisions (20,000)in northern Finland were defeated by a pair of battalions (1600?). While the Soviets eventually regrouped and pushed through, the Finns sued for peace and won a settlement. Post-Stalin estimates of casualites run in the 250,000 range.

Later, Finland allied with Nazi Germany to reclaim it's territory. However, after reclaiming it's territory + some strategic spots, the Finns ceased. When the Soviets countered, they pushed north of Leningrad until they fought to a draw near Vipurri. Finland settled with the Soviet Union.

Unlike the other German allies/territories- Hungary, Rumania, Czechslovakia/Poland, or the other Baltic states etc... Finland was allowed to remain independant and nuetral, though she lost territory and had to pay reparations. She still has a small army and came through the cold war well.
