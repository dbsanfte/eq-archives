Uman
I guess there's no point in hoping that anything will come of this, but I think that mistakes were made with regard to the lukewarm upgrades to shaman abilities in the past few expansions. Perhaps the wounds from the affliction focus nerf have given people a new perspective, and shamans will get another look, though.

Shamans have suffered from weak nukes, dots, and healing because of perceptions of their power due to:

1) slow
2) malo
3) cani
4) buffs

1) slow mitigation and BST 65%, ENC 70% slow

2) mage mala/malosinia yet still have perhaps the best sustained DPS

3) upgraded bst/enc/dru mana regen buffs, bard manasong, FT (non-ubers easily get FT7 - FT3 boots, 8th shawl, solstice ear), SCM, focus items ... there are (admittedly uber) wizards who nuke nonstop and do not OOM, and super-efficient 4+ dmg/mana before focus PoP nukes

4) stat buffs don't matter. bst/enc/dru have better buffs for casters, rng/bst/enc have equal or better buffs for melees. shamans only really have a stackable HP from Fo7 and ATK from ferine avatar (an expensive single target buff) to offer.

--------

What shamans got in PoP:

1) slow: a relative downgrade (slow mitigation cutting down the gap while BST and bard got upgrades)

2) malo: a minor upgrade (for a lot more mana)

3) cani: no upgrade (a relative downgrade considering the increased rate of mana consumption from PoP spells, and the upgraded passive mana regen buffs)

4) buffs: a slight upgrade (200 hp more from Fo7 from KF?) and 140 atk compared to 120atk from primal avatar

... and barely improved dots (mostly unusable in groups due to agro), nukes (pathetic in comparison to other classes), and a quirky rain that looks good on paper but works out to have too many resists

---------

What's wrong with shaman DPS from nukes, DoT, and rain?

Nukes:
atrocious efficiency for a 160dps poison nuke, or poor efficiency for a weak slow casting 100dps cold nuke (worse than bst and rng) - in the past, shaman nukes had to be gimp because of their far superior mana regen

Rains:
the resistability, lack of focus effects, and the (always-resisted-under-8-percent-health) makes them less effective on all but extremely low resist mobs. Wizards can get more efficiency out of their nukes than their rains. This is an issue for all classes, and in fact druids got a -50 resist modifier on theirs to make up for it.

DoT:
less efficient than focus nukes by any nuking class. marginal DPS upgrade from pre-PoP DoT should be okay since both can be stacked, but ... huge number of poison counters leads to a ridiculous amount of agro for nothing but damage, leading most shamans to rely upon rains and nukes, even though they are less efficient

Suggestions:

nukes:
more mana efficient at least; higher damage for the 65 cold nuke would be good

rains:
give all the PoP rains (not just druid) a -50 resist penalty and fix the always-resist-at-under-9-percent-health bug (yes, it is a bug)

dots:
we're really only talking about breath of saryrn (sic), since ultor lasts 156 seconds and is thus, in groups, very inefficient. the mana cost is too high (the druid dots and nukes are within 5% as efficient before focus items), but the biggest problem is the agro. there should be NO agro from the dot except that caused by the damage actually done, since that is the only effect the spell actually has. there is a bug (yes, a bug) due to the implementation of poison counters as a means to keep agro from the tash line high. the spell could be changed from pooison to disease counters (while keeping the poison resist, much like slows keep their magic resist) if the problem cannot be fixed directly

heals:
quiescence needs to heal more and be affected by healing adept. 450/tick, not the 300 that torpor already gave. shamans are supposed to have superior heal over time to everyone except cleric, in exchange for weaker direct heals than druids. quiescence should stack with cleric HoT.

-----

Why should a shaman's DPS be upgraded? Their distinguishing abilities that made them so desirable instead of another DPS have received no uprgade, and have been downgraded relative to improvements in the weaker versions given to other classes who also offer great DPS. With MGB, shamans don't really stack in raids. Giving them an average DPS capability would make them of average use when the traditional shaman role is already filled.

-----

OR, keep the shaman DPS spells as poor as they are now, but give real upgrades to

1) slow - a 75% slow that mitigates slow mitigation and has a -50 resist penalty - necessary to keep the same superiority shaman slows *used* to enjoy. Maybe a PBAE 60% slow.

2) malos - faster casting time, less mana use

3) cani - hastened cannibalization AA - or even an upgrade to the regular can4 line (necros get a lich upgrade, come on)

4) stats are useless. haste, casting haste (BoR), atk, mana regen, HP are useful buffs. ferine avatar should give a lot more than 140atk considering the group ranger atk buffs add up to more than that, and the 65 BST atk buff is 150atk. let shamans transfer mana to others, increase weapon/archery/backstab skills, a raw +melee dmg modifier, give them the cleric spellcasting haste buff, I'm sure you can think of something ...

----------

Fix shaman DPS/HoT or fix shaman utility. Please. Other than past perceptions of shaman godliness (soloed Yeldema - OMG!) there is no reason for things to remain as they are.

And of course, remove the str/dex spells (maybe keep single dex as proc rate for pets and clerics is something, I guess)
