Blargh
My advice is DO NOT spend a dime on alchemy. Alchemy is the only trade skill that hasn't gotten serious attention from the game controllers in the past couple years. The fundamental flaw of alchemy is that SOE envisions the skill as one of the very few plat sinks in the game and prices potion components so that only rich people can afford to have them. The problem is that in everquest, no one is rich, and its difficult to make a profit from the skill. On your server, ask any 255 jewler/potter/smith/tailor how much money they make plying their trade. Then, if you can find a 200 shaman, ask the same question.

My advice: Don't even get started. I wish to hell I hadn't.
Shandy Orcbane
I, however, am of a completely different opinion than Blargh. I went into alchemy expecting to sink cash into it, and not make much back. I was pleasantly surprised. Over the last year, I've made sufficient cash through alchemy to bring smithing to 212, jewelcraft, pottery, fletching, brewing to 199.

I also went into alchemy thinking that it was an extension of my spell book. What I can't do with a spell (such as invis to undead) I can do with a potion. I can DS, breeze, amplify a caster's level, invis to undead, and TL somebody to one of 4 zones. Now I can TL them to bind.

Yeah, it's gonna cost them, so many of these are used only rarely, but I can do it.

Some of the regen potions are quite impressive (Greater Rejuve is 40/tick for 2 mins), and Stinging Wort is a soloer's dream.

Lately, though, it's not worth my time to stock up my merchant with potions. Bozos will see the price, think "ah, so that one sells!" and then undercut by 5-10p.
