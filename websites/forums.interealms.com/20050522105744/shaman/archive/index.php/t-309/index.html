Sionnach Taibhsear
But the old Spider Spot was one of the best solo exp grinds in the game at any level.

A number of elements combine to make this a very nice experience camp. These elements are in no certain order:

Very quiet zone. This is especially nice for folks on the Bloodthirsty Punk (as opposed to Carebear) servers. A soloer has a good chance of hunting without getting Ganked.

The Mobs in the area do not roam, so once you sit down in a spot that is not within agro range of a mob you are safe. Of course you can still get trained.

At 45 a shaman has all of the spells and skills he needs to easily handle the mobs as well as travel safely in the rest of Nurga. Invis, Gate, Ultravision, Cure Poison, Resists, Bear Form, Dots and Pet. This may be another reason why many classes don't come to Nurga, as they do not have the unique set of skills that make Shammies of this level perfect for this spot.

The Spiders give great exp for their level. I don't know exactly why; it may be a combination of factors like dungeon bonus and that spiders are over-conned because of poison. I personally think that the Craig Spiders of EK are a much worse deal per level than these spiders.

The map is located at

http://www.eqatlas.com/kunark/nurga1map.html

According to this map with the first coordinate being the up/down, you want to be neg 1500 by neg 100, just right of the number 1, which is labelled Spider Caves. To your immediate left and right you should find 2 sets of 2 Briar or Bristle spiders with the infrequent Strathbone in the mix. The rough level of the spiders will be 34-37.

Getting to the spot should not be difficult as the Goblins do not see invis. You will have to becareful to avoid the random spider spawns in the Goblin areas as they WILL be able to see invis. I would also suggest that you spend a few minutes with a map and SCOUT out the exit. Nurga is a 'oneway' dungeon so you cannot simply go back out the way you came in. Of course when in doubt: GATE.

For your first level or so here at 44 or 45 you will not be able to single pull. This is fine since you should be able to land a root and pull the other mob. With Dots and Pet you should be able to dispatch the first spider before the other one breaks root or darn near. Repeat with the other set of 2 spiders. If you just concentrate on these 2 sets you should be plenty busy. Don't go looking for trouble with more.

Another benefit in this area is that you have a fair amount of room to manuver. You can always root a second and move away to safe distance. Don't be afraid to let your dog die. After all it is only mana. Better him than you.

I am sorry that this is impromptu without any editing, but I think it will give you enough info to start. Some of the stuff may be wrong but I am sure that one of the good folks on the board will have time to set this stupid Hillbilly right if I am indeed wrong. Good Luck.

Yuuki
I leveled from 40 - 47 at this spot, one of the best exp camps I've seen. Although you cannot do it on a regular basis, you can get singles (rarely) starting at 43 or 44 here. Best idea is to pull with enstill to break the camp.

I would usually take the 2 spawns by the sleeping ogre up till 45 and jbb started working, which really made this place insane exp. Once you have broken the 2 rooms, start alternating which room you pull from. This way even if you do fall behind you will usually not lose the break. After broken I would pull with slow, stack VoS and EB and tank while dogdog chewed on em. Usually I would also have to throw in a winters roar to keep the dog from KSing me.

After 45 with JBB I would pull the 2 spiders in front of the large room then start pulling from the large room. Just pull with slow and JBB/canni till dead (3 - 4 blasts + dogdog should kill them). Doing this I was able to take 8 - 9 spiders before repops with no downtime whatsoever.

I think the spiders start going ligt blue at 46. At 47 too many of them were light blue and I (sadly) had to move on.
