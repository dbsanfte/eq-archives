An exception I'd make to that is +arcane damage.
The reason I bring that up is specificly because of Arcane Explosion for mages.
If you have a +X damage to arcane item, it does 10 more damage per arcane explosion you do.
When my mage is popping off arcane explodes (instant cast due to talents) each +X is multiplied against all the targets you have.
So, if you AE 10 targets, you get +10X damage per cast.
Seeing as I can cast arcane explosion about once every second due to instant spell recast time, if that X is say +10 damage, then I'm gaining 100dps from that one item.
I haven't transferred them over yet, but I've got about +25 in various arcane damage items waiting to be equiped by my mage.
That +25 damage is going to equate to about +100 dps when I ae 4 mobs at a time if I'm doing that for xp.
That +25 damage is going to equate to about +250 dps when I ae 10 mobs at a time in an instance.
That's a huge dps boost.

That said, on adds or when they get a bad proc streak they go down faster than the prom queen. Me, I can easily tank and kill (over time) 3 even con mobs with my STAM/AC/HP/AGIL build. I use a blue shield and a dagger. Adds are no problem, my damage is constant, my dagger is a fast hitter and has more chances to interupt casters, my racial strength and stat bonuses to STR mean I use my shield better and mitigate more damage. IF I can hold aggro over the screaming wildman 2 hander shaman, I can almost totaly assure us of victory charging into a camp of 4 mobs, all higher or equal con to us.

On PvP it's harder for me to kill cloth casters because I give them more time to live, but I've never had a problem with cloth casters. On rogues, pallies, druids and even warriors I do better (consistantly) but the fights last longer and I'm usually in bad shape at the end. I linger in melee too long (I can get away with this due to LS and some decent mitigation) and I get hurt, but I win through attrition. Contrast the live hard, die fast build of 2 handers, vs the surviveability and consistancy of the 1 hander/shield, personally, I like the later.
