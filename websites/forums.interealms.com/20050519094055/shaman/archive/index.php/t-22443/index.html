If you wish to join any of the top guilds only way is to work for lv/AA. Grafsha McDaughts
I would toss in personal reputation. If there is a top guild you want to work yourself into as a member, get to know some of the folks. Group with them at every opportunity. Let them see how you stack up against other shaman - no in gear or aa, but in ability. This is a very long term project. Making friends will only make the guilding process easier when you are at the level (lvl and aa) the guild would consider you as a serious applicant.

Grind levels and aa's. If a guild has a minimum requirement its not there because they are being arses about it. It is there because without the minimum you are not able to pull your load and contribute at the bare minimum. Bare minimum. While it seems tough to meet the guilding requirements, keep in mind those who are in the guild already are above and beyond the minimum. They took the journey already (GRIND, GRIND, GRIND) to get where they are ... no one gave them a 65 300+ aa shaman.

You are asking a lot from the game/others and yourself to think that after "about a year" at lvl 61 with 7 aa that you are ready to jump into a top level raiding guild. Add to that your magelo gear and you really could take your time in a mid-level guild for the next year or so and meet your basic requirements:

1. Get much better gear than you have.

2. Learn how to play your shaman to the best of your abilities. Learn from others and learn from yourself as you try new things or do old things in new ways. Challenge yourself and your abilities until you know those limitations forwards and backwards.

Both of these are possible without being in the server's top guild(s). Finding a way to make both of these happen will, along the way, get you into the server's best guild or at least a guild you are happy with as far as gameplay goes.

If you are into raiding, there is nothing more satisfying that raiding with friends. Doesn't matter if you are doing Kael Arena or Corinav. So first find people who want to raid like you do. Doesn't matter what they raid, as long as they do.

Look at the current guild you are in. Is it a chat room, or a raid machine?

You might discover that there is a core of people in the guild that are wanting to do the same things you do. So start raiding. I'd suggest getting some cleric's epic'd for starters. Then start getting planar flagged. Take on Grummus and Xanamech.

If your guild is just a chat room, and most people have no desire to raid, then you need to find a group who does. Don't try to join the #1 guild...join the #10 or 12 guild. Find a guild who wipes to Vindi and then make them better.
