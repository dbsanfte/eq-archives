The way I look at it..

ND3 still maintains the same value after achieving Canni5.

MC3's value becomes greatly diminished as soon as you achieve Canni5 (i.e. mana regen becomes a substantially lower priority once you have Canni5).

So from a standpoint of utility, MC3 as a prerequisite to Canni5 seems like a dimished effect, since after you achieve the ability to generate ~800 mana every three minutes, you hardly notice the 90 mana MC3 generates in the same timeframe. Prior to Canni5, every little bit of FT/MC helps.

ND3 would make more sense from a non-diminishing returns standpoint, but really has absolutely nothing to do with mana regeneration. Sure, we cannibalize hitpoints for mana, but from an organizational standpoint, it seems a bit of a stretch.

I don't personally believe you can make everyone happy on this one. To a lot of folks, 12AA's are alot to spend and they would prefer better freedom to spend them on the things earlier on things they think will hold better value. To some, this will be more hp.. to others, more mana regen.. and to even others, defensive or healing AA's. No matter what you make the prerequisite, there are going to be shaman that feel they wasted 12AA's on something they feel should have much lower priority compared to other AA's.
