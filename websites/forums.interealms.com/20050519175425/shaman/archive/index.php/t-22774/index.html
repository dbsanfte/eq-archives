taraddar
In your statement you forget that the devs could set the damage high enough that it requires mitigation and therefore requires a shaman, beast, or chanter. They don't need to mitigate what we can do or make it so slow casting that it's useless. When they do that they are merely writing us out of Norrath.

Thats not entirely true. Sure they could do it game mechanics wise but it wouldn't really work well in actual implimentation. Why? Simply because the dps before being slowed would end up being too high to survive. A mob doing 1k dps slowed might be challenging to a group and thats fine if the mob is doing 1300 dps unslowed because they get 25% slowed. The same mob would have to do 4k dps unslowed to do 1k dps fully slowed and would either have to hit rediculously hard or fast to do that. Either way the mob would be nearly untankable untill slowed. Assuming a standard 3 second delay the mob would be hitting for 12k around or quadding for about 4k a peice. Thats tougher than elemental boss mobs.

Simply put high dps mobs can not be fully slowable and be viable in the game. With the latest expansions tending towards high dps low hp mobs for short but intense fights that means that slow mitigation is almost a requirement. The problem really is the exact same situation as our healing issues. In the way the game is setup today the difference between a fully slowed mob and an unslowed mob is too large for us to be viable at both ends. Either we're way over powered on fully slowable mobs or completely underpowered on slow immune mobs. The only way to fix this situation is to decrease the effectiveness of slow, IE slow mitigation, so the different ends are closer together.
