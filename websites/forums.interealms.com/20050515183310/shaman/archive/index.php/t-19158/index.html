Rascalan
I have been reading through the suggestions as they stand and some are good some are way off track. As a shaman for the last four years I know that I started the class because I liked to help people and offering my services to them was my ability to move the group forward.

The reason I started the class was because I was helping out my community and others xp faster. It was a good feeling to log in and contribute something to all the differant classes. It was a defining ability that made the class so enjoyable. The versatility of being a jack of all trades. We could crowd control somewhat, slow, heal just enough to make things work.

If a group couldn't find the right class for the make up you throw a shaman into the mix and you could always get the job done. We didn't take on the role of another character, we just seemed to fill in the blanks if that one class was not be available.

I can't really say that I know what it is I personally want besides that feeling of walking into a group and knowing that I could fill a useful role. I think all classes want that and should ultimately get it.

One thing I do know though, is that I didn't start this class for the following reasons.

If I wanted great nukes I would have been a wizard. If I wanted great heals I would have been a cleric. If I wanted to be a pet class I would have been a magician.

I do like to melee, I do like to cast, I like to solo and group. I like the versatility of the shaman cause you can do many things given differant circumstances.

I liked the class because of the subtle underlying aspects in the game. A shaman could make or break a group. By this I mean we were the fuel enjection for an already well oiled engine. Sure a group could get it done, but with a shaman you optimized the group.

By optimizing I mean we made up for the other class short comings or we highly polished them. For example if you had a cleric with a crappy mana pool and you were in a chain pulling xp group. The shaman could add slows and spot heals into the mix to compensate. Shaman could also keep crowd control somewhat with roots and slow if an enchanter wasn't available. We had decent buffs and a haste that could make things work out.

I see the shaman class as a sublte change that would give the adventurers the advantage to win. With slow, buffs, and debuffs it was an advantage to have them around. It made things more streamline and unltimately faster and easier.

That being said I guess what I am looking for is clarification now. The subtle changes that made me wanted, have now been taken away. There was no definition what I do in a group. I have to now ask what it is I exactly need to do. I try to fit into the group, rather than making them streamline. Half the spells I used to mem don't make a differance. With maxed this and that who needs buffs. With mitigated slows and resists, who has mana left to heal. When a heal is cast and it doesn't make a diffferance why cast it.

These are the things that are making this proposal so frustrating for me. The shaman was a subtle advantage to make things better. We really didn't do a big bang, but a little of everything thing to make an overall differance. That is why I find this proposal so difficult to answer. We did to many things to put done just a couple of changes to really make things worth changing. Now that other classes can take care of those sublte things we really aren't needed. I don't know what the answers are, but from my personal experience and opinion we need to address some things. The current state of the game leaves people asking exactly why is it I started this class again?

This was a frustrating class to play before, but it was enjoyable to see the differances we made. We have to change out gem slots more than any other class. We were given three differant spell line ups. Debuffs, Buffs, and utility spells. This means we had to switch out spells more than all other classes. When you get to a group you buffed, than you had to transition to debuffs and other common offensive spells. Keep crowd control utlity spells going, all while hitting a canna button. We had definately worked for the subtle differance and made the differance in the outcome.

Now we have buffs that aren't worth meming. Spells we have, other classes have too. We can regen mana, but have nothing worthwhile to cast.

I personally feel SOE has taken us for fools. We were out in the gray before and now there just isn't a clear vision why this class exists. I feel that I pretty much just wasted four years. I don't know what is more embarrasing, dying for the first time using cannabilization in a group or playing this class this long and getting this after four years.

Rascalan
