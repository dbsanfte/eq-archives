Bludletters
Now that I am in the swing of things and have been able to do multiple Adventures in all of the themes, I feel that I can comment on what it takes to win EVERY time. I have had 1 loss since I purchased LDoN and that was the first adventure I was ever on we backed out right after the first pull when the Cleric got swamped on a very weird/bugged pull. Anyway, If you Have a Chanter/Bard, Shaman/Cleric, Pally/Warrior/SK, and any three other classes you will win. As an example I put together a group last night all 65's of course. Shaman (me), Cleric, Mage, Necro, Druid, Pally. Our mission was to kill 60 Creatures in the 90 minutes. We encountered 2 Named mobs in the adventure one at the very begining within in 10 minutes and one at the end 2nd to last kill. We finished with 23 minutes to spare. This was out of the EF camp. One Tak adeventure I was in group had a Chanter, Shaman(me), Cleric, Monk, Wizzy and Pally and finished with almost 40 minutes to go. So I guess the real point of this is Have a class that can slow, a class that can heal, one to take dmg, one to put out dmg, and any other 2 you want to put in there and you will win IF you are on a normal level. Hard setting is not much different other then have a cleric as healer or 2 others, (ie. Druid and Shaman). A perfect group doesn't exsist as each theme is different. In EF most everything is undead so a Necro can mez, pacify, etc.. no need for a chanter at all. On the Ruj Hills EC Adventure mobs are spread so far apart that again parking with root has seemed to be a very reliable way of crowd control. So use good judgement and you will always win, minus a bugged pull or something else beyound your control. Have a good day.

Mistmoore Catacombs
Not bad usually. A good portion of undeads usually too. Good for pally/necros and even clerics.

Takish-Hiz
Tends towards a pain because of lots of doors with mobs right behind them. Lots of summoned mobs tho and druids/rangers/mages love it.

Deepest Guk
Least favorite zone. Most interesting but too much up and down, water, door ways and such that just combine to make it a very generally irritating zone to do. Also a fair number of chanter/clerics mobs for casters. Also has it's share of undead.

Miragul's Menagerie
Descently spread out on most maps altho on some maps the mobs tend to blend into the map. Seem to have a fair number of meleers.

Rujarkian Hills
Fairly spread out mobs in nice long halls. Makes pulls easier. Occational intersections that can be crowded but thats it. Only problem seems to be the occational sowed runner from all the shaman around.
