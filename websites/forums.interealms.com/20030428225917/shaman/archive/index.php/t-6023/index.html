My guess would be that the guy selling the potions very cheap won't be selling them that low for too long. Let me use Walmart as an example:

Walmart wants to build a new store in a city where there will be 5 mom and pop stores competing with them. Walmart knows that all 5 owners of the stores are competitive, but friendly. They have made deals with each other to all sell certain items at a set price. Because they all sell at the same price, each of them holds 1/5 of the market and they all make a profit. Walmart, however, would not be happy with 1/6 of the market when they move in. So they become aggressive.

When Walmart first opens their new store, they set the prices on some items very low, lower than cost sometimes. Because Walmart is a huge chain, they can afford to sell items at a loss for a limited time. The 5 mom and pop stores, however, are forced to lower their prices to be competitive. Walmart will continue to undercut their prices no matter how low they go though, so eventually the mom and pop stores are forced out of business because they cannot afford to sell at a loss. Then once Walmart holds 100% of the market, they jack the prices on those items up. Some of the customers may complain, but Walmart doesn't care because it has no competition. Other businesses know that if they try to move in, Walmart will just force them out again and so Walmart is safe and raking in the dough.

The person that is selling the SoW potions at 5pp below the lowest competitor knows that eventually he will force other shamans out of the business. You have already stated that you stopped making potions because it was no longer worth it to you. How much longer before the rest of the shamans competing with LowSeller00 come to the same decision. Eventually, he will be the only SoW potion seller, or close to it at least. At that time he will most definately raise his prices back up to a more normal 90pp per potion. If others try to undercut his prices, he can just continue what he was doing before and force them out. This person most likely has a lot of 2 things: time and money. He is skilling up on alchemy and making a small profit while doing so. After time, his profit will skyrocket as his competition diminishes.

Granted that this may not seem fair to everyone else, the seller doesn't care because he is only concerned in making more money. Either simply wait it out or do as you have been doing. Buy out his entire stock and resell for slightly more. If you don't buy from him, everyone else sure will. As was already stated, their is no loyalty in the bazaar. People will buy from the lowest seller every time.
