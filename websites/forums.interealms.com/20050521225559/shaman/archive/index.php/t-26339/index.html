Brohg
wow, that map is terrible, the arrows all go both ways.

Kod'Taz has great stuff. First, excellent exp, though not *quite* up to exp zones designed for level 70 (specifically, Anguish/RSS). It's better than Plane of Earth.

The mobs themselves offer a real range of challenge, but a group can handle them all np. You can duo all except Rampaging Nocs, I expect. They have varying abilities based on model type that I'm sure you're used to from Vxed/Tipt.

Very few mobs see invis, and those are very consistent. The two wandering Ukuns see invis, and the undead (the Trusik ghosts in the small area called Martyr's Passage), and wandering muramites (not animals) in the area of the ikkinz temples. Other than those few, it's quite safe to run around Kod'Taz while invisible.

There is loot to be had, though you may or may not need it. Guise of Seething Satisfaction (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68702) and Reflective Orb of Energy (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68701) are random/veryrare from any trash mobs.

The named have augments and spells. You don't need flowing thought, but you should pick up the shaman-usable 20ac augment to go in your shield, if you don't have Ground Murkglider Eye. The 35hp augs aren't as special as they were when GoD came out, unfortunately. Still, you'll want to camp named in KT if you didn't manage to get Nihil from Vxed/Tipt.

As Frozen mentioned, KodTaz access = Yxtta access. There's a short but kinda steep learning process in figuring the safe route to Yxtta, since you have to pass both see-invis ukuns, and the Martyr's Passage area. The canyon walls are your friends ;) Yxtta has Glowing Bone Ring (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68635) and Attuned Discord Earring (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68636) , both random/veryrare from trash.

KodTaz also = access to Ikkinz. Ikkinz one-group trials allow only, and basically require, one full group. There are two really good SHM drops , Bracelet of the Fearsome (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68767) from Ikkinz1 and Glyphed Prayer Ring (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68796) which is pretty common from any of the Ikkinz trials. There's an exceedingly rare earring from ikk2 with a fire focus we can't really use, but is technically SHM usable.

Ikkinz1 is Temple of Singular Might. You need tank, cleric, and a mezzer to win on Plan A. Any group not including a mezzer will have some pretty severe trouble with the final boss. Defensive/Evasive/Stonewall disciplines are Not Allowed on the boss here, just like in Tipt. Using any disc on that timer kills agro as if the tank had used DA, there is No Way to tank using those disciplines.

Ikkinz2 is Temple of Twin Struggles. You need TWO tanks, and healing enough for both of them. No big cc neccessary, the challenge is taking on two bosses at once. It was a pretty extreme challenge when folks were doing it at level 65 in elemental armor. With level 70s, shm can work for second healer, but you might need your full bag of tricks.

Ikkinz3 is Temple of Tri-Fates. Bring a knight to tank this one, if you can; the bosses (yes, ikk2 = two, ikk3 = three :p) are perma-rooted, so a knight can superagro with spells and back out of melee range between summons, to make their damage output rather gimp. Building agro like this isn't a possibility in Ikkinz2, I'm sure you'll discover why in the natural course of things ;) With a solid game plan and a knight tank, Ikk3 is the easiest of them all to win.

I think that about covers it! The progression quest for unlocking raid instances of Ikkinz is well covered in BiC walkthroughs, that includes some details I haven't mentioned, and maps. Happy Hunting!

In addition to the wandering Ukun, undead, and muramites, there is at least one wandering Kyv who sees invis and will smack you down fast and hard. I hate him; I owe him so many beatdowns it's not funny. In addition to nocs, Ra'Tuks also might be problematic to duo. Cragbeasts and Hynids are pretty easy, and with a good group you can keep your camp clear.

KT remains one of my favorite zones. It's out-of-the-way enough that it's not riddled with morons who start fights /ooc and train all over the place. Getting to a camp isn't just a blind run; you actually have to pay attention to where you're going.

Yxtta is also a favorite solo haunt of mine; save for the occasional raid, that zone is empty almost 24:7. Great group exp. too, if the primals are down and you can get into the caves.

Ikkinz trials are an absolute blast (have fun getting in!), great (common) loot ... Glyphed Prayer Ring and Type 8 35HP augs, not to mention some pretty nice random drops and tasty experience.

Best thing you can do, IMO, is get yourself a map with the various Ikkinz trials marked. Group trials are down in the valleys, raid trials are out in the big circular area (you'll see what I mean when you pull your map up). And yes, valley walls are your friends.
