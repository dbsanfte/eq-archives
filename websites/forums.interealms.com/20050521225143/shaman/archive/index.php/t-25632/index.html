Aruman
When I first started playing EQ back in March of 1999, the attractive part of EQ was the fact that it was a challenging game.

The big problem as I see it with EQ is that it is now way too easy. I don't want to see Everquest become another Ultima Online.

You used to lose a bubble and a half of exp when you died when the game first started, and you had zero hope of a res of any kind because clerics hadn't reached the level where they had an exp res. You can bet that people paid much more attention in groups back then.

Today it is a completely different story. Exp loss isn't even a concern to most people, because there is so much content with large exp gains, and also because the equipment available today is I am sure, way way beyond what the original creators of Everquest ever imagined.

I myself believe that when a game is made too easy, people become bored with it and quit playing. There simply isn't any challenge to it.

This is the reason I enjoy the game the most when I encounter challenging content, ie... guild level raid content, or pushing myself to do 'stupid' things, like trying to solo extremely difficult mobs. These are examples of what keeps me interested in Everquest, the challenges. When the game loses this aspect, is probably the time I will lose interest.

As an example: While I enjoy the ability to travel quickly to areas with respect to guild activities, I think you lose the immersion in the game with these features. The game just doesn't feel the same. You just don't see nearly as much of the game when you are able to travel this easily.

Don't get me wrong here though. There needs to be some availability to ease travel, EQ is just too huge a game to not have it, but I think there is entirely too much.

I don't think making the game easier is the answer to bringing in and retaining players to Everquest. I believe that SoE needs to get back to creating interesting, challenging content for all players. Raising level caps, adding features which require large amounts of exp grinding and so on, although 'challenging' in a sense, causes quite a bit of frustration for the most part.

IMO the continued success of Everquest will be tied mostly to the creation of content that will be enjoyable, not frustrating. This won't be easy for SoE though. It will take careful design and thorough QC to pull it off. Rushed expansions won't help. I'd rather wait longer for well thought out and designed expansions. Make the content challenging without the mindless exp grinding.

One idea: Instead of tying character advancement to grinding, create quests that are similar to (but not as difficult as) epic quests which grant characters additional abilities and so on. Make the journey to aquire the abilities, spells interesting, instead of requiring you to group for hours grinding out exp or killing the same mobs over and over in order to gain additional equipment and spells. Expand on that task system they introduced.

I'd love to once again have the sense of excitement and anticipation I had when I first started playing Everquest.

EQ today is much easier in all aspects. Time consumption can be applied to many different instances of play, but let's look at three of the bigger parts of Everquest in that respect:

1. Exp grinding
2. Questing
3. Group or Guild level raids

Which one would you rather do with your time in EQ? I would say it depends on who the question is put to, but for me, I enjoy raid content and interesting quests over exp grinding.

Which of the above are easier now compared to when EQ first started.

1. Definitley easier. Bonus exp zones make exp grinding much easier.
