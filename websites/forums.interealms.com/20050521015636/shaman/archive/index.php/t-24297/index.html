Binter
/sigh, everything has to turn into an ubers vs. casuals debate.

Hate to say it, but EQ's survival hinges on this very thing, Griz...and you shouldn't be so quick to say that.

OoW went a long way in providing some solid gear upgrades easily attainable for the casual player, because it absolutely positively had to happen. As it stands now, the gulf in power between a hardcore raider and the average casual is so extreme that it's absolutely impossible for SoE to balance the game so both of these extremes can find fun and challenging content without it requiring SoE to stick in 50 new zones.

Faced with a daunting dilemma, SoE did the only thing it could do, introduce Attuneable gear (GREAT idea, IMO) that would give casuals a chance at some excellent upgrades without destroying the EQ economy or introducing a whole new round of mudflation. But more than that, it helps close that ever-widening gap in power and allows casuals the ability to utilize zones that would have been impossible for them to function in otherwise. It was the only thing they could do to literally save EverQuest from itself.

The main concern I've always had about the guild summit and the forums is a lack of representation for the casual gamer. The progression concept may be built around the raider now, but the casual gamers still represent a majority of the players in EQ. Kill the fun of the game for them, introduce yet another expansion where the casual gamer will only be able to see/xp in 10% of the zones (PoP/GoD, anyone?), and SoE loses so many subscribers that it cuts into SoE's profits. That seriously hurts the raider's game as well, and we all suffer for it.

Simply put, raiders NEED for the casuals to be happy, as the casuals NEED the raiders to have challenging new content. It keeps the game moving forward.

Raiders should be very concerned about the state of the casual player, and not just immediately think "oh, it's another uber vs. casual thing" when this kind of thing comes up.
