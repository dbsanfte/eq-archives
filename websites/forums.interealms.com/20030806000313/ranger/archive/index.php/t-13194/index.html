Xequecal
I'm writing this guide mainly because I'm frustrated. Frustrated with people who obsess on leveling and completely neglect their gear. It may be hard for some of you to grasp this, but your gear is what makes you in this game. If your gear sucks, so do you. I don't care how good you are or think you are. Playing skill pretty much only determines who lives and who dies when the **** hits the fan, something that does not happen very often anymore with today's gimpified XP MOBs. 90% of this game is standing in front of a MOB and letting the random number generator do it's thing. If you are level 50 and still wielding Icetooth Claws, a Crystal Fiber, Banded armor, and no haste item, then this is directed at you:

STOP LEVELING! STOP RIGHT NOW! YOU ARE THE REASON EVERYONE MAKES FUN OF THE RANGER CLASS! Rangers are THE most gear-dependent melee class. Paladins, SKs, and Warriors have higher AC on their gear, more hitpoints, and a higher Defense skill that will compensate for below average gear. Rogues get a Backstab key that adds so much damage that even with crappy gear, they will still outdamage most anyone. Rangers do not have either luxury, so you MUST have better weapons to stay desired. Rangers are GOOD tanks in most situations if they've kept their gear up to par.

Now that the mini-rant is over, here is my advice:

- Panic Animal. Never, EVER use it. Why? Animals do not drop loot. If you can't solo a blue MOB with straight melee, then you need to farm plat until you can. People say Panic Animal was an amazing improvement to our class. I disagree. It lets any idiot get to 60 without any skill at all, and wearing some pretty abysmal gear. The only exception to this rule is gryphs in Jaggedpine or in NK/EK at low levels, or snow griffins in EW, because they drop money. It may be fun to get 10 levels in 3 days off rockhoppers, but you'll have gained exactly zero platinum doing it, and chances are your defense skill is 30 points behind.

- Hunt in places that drop large amounts of loot. Even if the XP is below average. Go to Crystal Caverns at level 30 and don't leave until level 39. You'll have made at least 3k in that time, more if you managed to get into the geonid camp on a regular basis. At 39, go do Hill Giants. Do them for at least 2 levels. Yes, it's boring. Yes, the XP sucks. But you'll get 10k doing this pretty easily, giving you a large equipment advantage over everyone else your level.

- There are a lot of places where you can get tons of loot, yet are largely unexplored. Oggok is a great example. The bouncers inside are easy to pull singly, and you will get 1-2 FS weapons from EACH kill. That's in addition to the platinum you will get. There's a non-KOS merchant right at the zone that you can sell to. Get a friend, and hunt here for a few levels. Sol A is another great plat farming location, especially if you're level 50 and are wearing crap. You can massacre this dungeon and sell all the fine steel to vendors in the zone.

- Don't be passive. If you are in a group and someone else is looting everything, call them on it. Don't try to be nice and just hope that he/she will stop on their own.

- Recognize what items are valuable to players and hoard them. For example, the small pieces of velium that drop in crystal caverns are very valuable to players, because they are required for trade skills. I collected 40 at one point and sold them for 400pp to a player. Also know the rare spawns and rare drops in the area you're hunting, and constantly check Track for them. Direct your group to these spawns when they appear so you have a chance at getting a nice item to use or sell. Winning the lotto on a Quillmane cloak will solve all your money problems, as high level mages will pay 50k or more for the multiquest rights to this thing.
