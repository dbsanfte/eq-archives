I've used it with groups for hmmm two years now I guess.

It is so fine for situations where the world has changed and you can't do a thing to roll back the clock to where things were before.

It is less than optimal, though, where the possibility exists for negotiation.

In the story there is no one for the mice to negotiate with, the cheese just appears or does not. Lots of situations like that in the world: culture / economics are the way they are, nothing you can do to change that.

In many situations there is someone to negotiate with. Within a marriage, in an office, in a city council, in a classroom, on a sports team. The solution there may not need to be to find a new place to get cheese; it may be to negotiate about how to arrange the whole cheese distribution process.

Are we (1) in a situation where SoE will put cheese in certain places, it's no use complaining that they are not putting it where we used to find it?

Or are we (2) in a situation where we can negotiate with them about how the cheese distribution is going to work?
