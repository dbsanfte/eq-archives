Though I've enjoyed reading threads on this fine board for about a year (which is as long as I've been playing EQ), I haven't wanted to post. All the hoopla over the upcoming existence of GoD convinced me that if ever there was a time to voice my opinions, now might be it. I'd rather GoD didn't hate Shaman, but be created in our own image:

Powerful, Mystical,
Sublime, Solitary, Social;

Complimentary and contradictory;
Beyond complete comprehension.

I am encouraged by some recent Shaman threads that make concise, concrete suggestions about what GoD should and shouldn't be like. I am glad to see similar suggestions on other class boards. I am especially glad to see positive, constructive comments from experienced raiding players that will benefit all levels of their favorite classes. I hope folks at SOE make many of the improvements to Shaman that have been suggested. However, what concerns me more is the possibility that this expansion and EQ2 later on will "encourage" even more group involvement and actively discourage solo and duo enjoyment.

As a background aside... I do not hate people. I have managed people professionally for many years. I understand and appreciate what being social involves on many levels. I have been elected to public office. I have adult children and a good life outside of EQ.

The main aspect of EQ that enables it to capture my continued interest is that it is massively multi-player. That EQ is inherently a social game does not mean everyone who plays it enjoys to group. Nor should it. EQ's design is more robust than that. The more I read that any expansion, or EQ2, will increasingly emphasize grouping, the less likely I am to buy it or subscribe. If GoD is being targeted toward elemental/time guilds as arrogance suggests, then its market is quite probably too small to be profitable and certainly of no interest to me.

There are a myriad of ways to play and enjoy EQ. Its facilities for grouping are central to most. Its apparently underlaying founding principle that most individuals are more likely to continue paying their subscriptions if they are coerced into grouping and belonging to a guild may or may not be true. It is certainly a counter-productive tactic to take with me. I appreciate that many players do not feel coerced and enjoy grouping above all else and that's great! Those of you who arrogantly retort something to the effect of "then you're playing the wrong game" simply don't understand this game or people as well as you might think.

I value the time I spend with my wife above all else. The only reason I play EQ is that it's an enjoyable way to spend time with her. So, she has her eight characters on her PC, I have mine, and we have a full-time trader account. We've played most every day for about a year. We duo most of the time. We solo sometimes. We group sometimes. We're fairly active trade skillers. We're moderately active officers in our guild. However, any game content that we can't duo we pretty much skip. We don't care that much of the game will thus remain unavailable to us as long as there are lots of fun things we can do. Anything that detracts from our ability to enjoy the game together simply pushes us closer to choosing some alternative activity. The real world is full and glorious.

We've recently started having fun in EQ with our son who started a Shaman using our trader account. He's found the time spent on Norrath with us surprisingly enjoyable such that we're considering starting up a new 4th account for him. Again, new content designed exclusively for raiding guilds won't positively influence that decision, or any of our game enjoyment, except perhaps by decreasing the likelihood of encountering a few ubers destined for the ignore list. Please don't mistake me; our friends lists overflow with most of the long-time players we've met who are shining assets to the community. A few aren't.

At my age, I have little patience left for those folks who go on ad nauseam judging how others play or spewing dogma regarding how others should or should not play. That we can interact and share Norrath with people who play for very different reasons and in very different ways from us is quite cool. That we can choose to ignore them is vital. That they can choose to ignore us is probably even more important. If EQ really is simply a game designed to socially engineer a gang mentality into American youth and waste potentially productive human resources while making a profit, so be it. I don't think it is. I wish those folks who enjoy raids all the best and hope theirs is the highest roll for the most uber loot. I also wish they weren't so frequently myopic and condescending and didn't influence future game development to the detriment of social soloers. I expect I care less about the game they play than they apparently do about mine. /em sighs.

The question is, should Sony ignore subscribers like us? If the majority of EQ players have raiding mains who have been lvl 65 for longer than we've been playing, as many seem to take for granted, then Sony probably should. Unless maybe they want to sell EQ2. From what I can tell, only Sony can actually do more than speculate (all too often loudly) about EQ's real subscriber demographics. Even they can only make highly educated and variously hedged bets about what will make EQ2 most profitable.
