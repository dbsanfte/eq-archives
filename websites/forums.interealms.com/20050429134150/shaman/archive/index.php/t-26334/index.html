Kryscha
Like Cappi said; a lot of it depends on how you spec. A warrior is definately higher DPS than a paladin, and quite noticeably so, unless you go a pure protection spec, in which case you'll feel like a paladin, only without the heals and shields.

The more I play warriors, the more I start to think they're talent trees are perhaps more diverse than most of the other classes I've played. If you primarily spec in one tree only, then your play style becomes quite different indeed. For instance, arms spec tend to do a lot more stance switching (thanks to tactical mastery) whereas someone like myself who went deep into fury and didn't get tactical mastery tends to try to pick the best stance and will only grudgingly switch if the situation aboslutely demands it.

Weapon choices also become dependant upon trees, as an Arms/MS spec will go for big, heavy hitting 2hers most times, as opposed to a fury warrior that will generally dual wield. Most protection based seem to go 2H weapons, but I haven't played that much as with that talent tree, or met a lot of protection warriors. 'End-game' warriors (I use that term to describe lvl 60 instance runners) go protection spec for best instance tanking, and since there's nothing like AA's to keep you xping once you hit 60, I suppose there's no reason to keep a fun soloing spec going.

Warriors can solo even con mobs (or even 1 or two levels higher) fairly easily, and if you can get them single, you can pretty much just keep on going with minimal downtime. Pick your equipment wisely, and don't skimp on upgrading weapons regularly. Also highly recommend being very choosy on your armour; get stamina on every piece, and I personally favour a balanced agi/str after stamina.

At level 60 you can grind on -4's or so and not rest much... mobs above 56 or so and you need to stop and eat. And elites? Forget about it if you are not fighting one that is about -8 from you.

In terms of builds and dps and such... I'm arms primarily with Mortal Strike. It's not as great as it used to be, weapon damage + XX amount (can't remember). With my hammer from the Princess in Maraudon and I mostly hit for high 200's/low 300's with MS, with occasional crits (seemingly rare) of 700-800ish.

Using a shield +Thrash Blade, I can still pretty MS for 200's as an extra instant attack, which is still good. You can fight with a shield and a 1h weapon even as Arms, imagine that. No real need for macro-switching either. Also, I often find that when grinding, the shield + 1h is a better choice with less downtime. The beat down I take without the shield and subsequent eating required goes way up with a 2h and the kill time is not that much less.

So anyway, in summary, I'm not convinced that a pally grinding xp on mobs -4 or lower (like a warrior pretty much HAS to do) is that much slower. The fact that they can consider mobs of higher level and elites should tell you a lot. As a warrior, you won't be grinding through even cons very fast, I assure you, don't be mislead.
