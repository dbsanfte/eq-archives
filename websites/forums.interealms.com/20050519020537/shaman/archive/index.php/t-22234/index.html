Kellenial
While the issues you raise appear to be valid ones, I for one am not of the mind that cancelling accounts or no longer playing a shaman are viable ways to get SoE's attention.

Cancelling accounts DOES hurt them at the bottom line...true. But for them to truly FEEL the magnitude of that, it would have to be the entire shaman community. A handful cancelling will not have much effect. If anything, SoE might view it as culling out some of the disgruntled undesirables. The reason that they started addressing GoD-related issues is partly due to the sheer magnitude of people leaving EQ over what was deemed as a very poorly constructed expansion. At that point, it wasn't just "Joe Shaman" and "Mary Magician" leaving, it was the majority of some of the more prominent guilds. With such a mass exodus, they HAD to take a look at what was causing it (and I don't think it was entirely based on the quality of the other, comparable games out there). Therefore, the trickle of a few dozen shaman cancelling in protest over lack of changes in the viability of shaman as a class most likely would not even be acknowledged.

As for switching to different class with an alt: that would take away from people voicing their concerns about this class. Most likely, it would get those people in arms over the state of the "new" class. EVERY class feels that their are certain areas that could use improvements. [Insert class here] does not corner the market on it.

SoE is slowly doing something rather unprecedented in the history of EQ: they are slowly starting to listen to the customer. First the summit, now the Class Correspondents. Following the summit, there seems to be more of an SoE presence among the various class boards as they are trying to see what we the players think and feel. The fact that SoE is coming out of their ivory tower IS a good thing. I think the best thing for us to do as a shaman community and as an EQ community is to give them a chance. Work with them. Show them their misconceptions and let them explain to us a little more about why some of our ideas might not be the solutions sought. For the first time since I started playing (April 1999), I can see them offering the olive branch to players. We have a choice. I don't see cancelling accounts or hiding in other classes to make our points about the status of the shaman class as being the right choices.

Again, this is my opinion. Sometimes I'm right; other times I'm wrong...
