Shayne716
Key to soloing the HGs before you get a jbb or horse is to park pet along the zone wall. Buff him with current hp buff, sow, and haste all others are waste of mana essentually. Equip him with 2 rusty or other cheap 1handed weapons. Some really good ones are Velium for the free dd effect on it or the gnoll whips that drop in SK and Splitpaw off the gnolls that proc stun.

Shamadruid method
/pet taunt off
/pet guard
Locate Hill Giant pull with slow, run back to spawn point sending in puppy as soon as in range, continue casting dots till the giant ignores the pet and comes after you, run along zone wall as if a druid as your dots and pet chew down the HG. If the HG stops to beat on your puppy /sit till he likes you again /stand then keep moving, reapply dots as needed

Advantages to this method less mana spent on healing and more mana left for dmging spells, less recasting of dead pets ect generally most efficient

Disadvantages need to keep sow up, epic right click sow boots are really nice for this. You will need room to work and if zone is crowded you'll need to be carefull of others. Main disadvantage is other shaman will call you a Druid =)

Root/Rot method
Self explanitory i do believe, best if no pet used as he will cost you more mana than he will be worth at your lvl

Tanking method
Fully buff yourself, summon pet equip and buff simular to above.
/pet taunt on for this one
pull with slow, turn on attack, send in pet and attempt to channel some dots.
Attempt to heal yourself at half life
if you get under 2 bubbles life attempt to root the hg and back up out of melee till you are healed, let dog tank at this point, if pet gets low life get back in melee and mob will turn back on you.

Advantages- Fastest of the 3 methods for HG to die. You will get much needed skillups trust me you want all 200 of your defense points.
Disadvantages-More risky and you will die occasionally with spell interupts. More downtime from extra healing mana used. Somewhat gear dependant.

As i'm sure you've notice versitility is one thing shaman are good at. We usually don't have one way of doing things, we usually have many that will work. Adjust this based on what you have available to gear wise and buff wise and have fun.
Personally i killed my first HG at lvl 30 using the kite method after watching druids/bards doing it. I wasn't able to tank them till about 34-35 with my pet. Mind you this was before the days of Kei and Ageo/Virtue buffs. If it can be done in banded armor it can be done in todays gear for sure =)

Once you get 45 and have the pp's to get a jbb you feel like a machine gunner and can set up camp on top of the hill and mow them faster than they can spawn =)

As an alternative hunt check out Cynthia at the Gypies camp over by Lake Rath zone. She is a wizzard so have your slam hotkey ready. My method for her was buff resists, send it pet to take first nuke and go full offensive. She drops around 5pp and drops gems that you can sell a few feet away to the other gypsies and respawns every 6 minutes if i recall correctly. She is on her own faction so don't worry about ruining gypsy faction.

Donar
As far as buffing your pet when hunting, I say "yes." I have noticed that our pets are more robust with a hp buff and the agi/ac buffs. As others have pointed out, I've not noticed any effect of sta buffs so I usually don't cast it and if I do, it's the sta buff from my bp. I don't blow mana on it, anyways. If buffing the pet is a strain on your mana, then depending on how you're using him, some (or all) buffs are optional. If you're tanking and pet is just adding damage, he doesn't really need a hp buff as he shouldn't be getting hit. If you're letting the pet tank (really iffy with the first few pets), then by all means, buff him with your best stuff.

JBB is a judgement call. It's very nice to have from level 45 to 55 and even beyond depending on where you hunt. In PoP, it's not gonna do much but in Luclin and older zones, it still has plenty of utility. It's not needed and if you can get torpor at 60, you'll kill much faster by using your dots and canni/torporing the mana back. The only reason I keep my JBB these days are for the odd mob who's really poison/disease resistant. There aren't many, but really annoying when you run across them. If you can get money for JBB while you get exp, then great. I don't know if it's worth putting off levelling though, unless you're having a lot of fun where you're at. If it's mindless plat pharming at the expense of levelling, it doesn't seem worth it to me, but it may be to you. I did camp HGs alot for my JBB. I got lucky to be playing at times when the zone wasn't crowded and I got good exp for killing the HGs, so for me, it was a win/win situation. Your situation may be different, so I don't know what to advise. Go with what you think is best. Good luck to you. :D
