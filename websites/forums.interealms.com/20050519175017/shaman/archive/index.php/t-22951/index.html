Hail,
I vaguely recall some safehouse thread regarding splitting xp/aaxp giving less total xp gain than just sticking to 100%.
What I recall was that if you have anything OTHER than 100% regular exp or 100% AA exp, you will lose a smidgen of experience due to rounding errors involved with large numbers being split into percentages.

I believe (and I'm by no means certain) that in order to save on computations that the #'s are being truncated at some level. So if a mob gives you 3425425 experience points and you are currently 48,543,286,534 into level 65 and 48,532,747 from your next AA ding (and I believe the numbers for total net lifetime exp earned at 65 is probably a much bigger #), then somewhere the SOE experience calculators are taking a shortcut when it comes to accurately splitting the mob exp and adding it to the total real exp/aa exp values.

Now we have leadership exp to also add into the mix, and I'm not aware of any thorough parses on how those splits are made. Personally, to be on the safe side, I always play 100% into either regular or AA, and never split the two. For leadership exp I turn it on when I'm leading, and leave it off otherwise, just to be safe. ;)

The amount of exp being lost in the split may so miniscule as to not even be noticed, but dangit, I don't want to lose any to a calculator (I lose enough through deaths as it is! :p ) rounding or truncating. :D I don't mind taking a chance with leadership exp because I have no choice.
