Irksome
Not a skill, it's a spell.

Level 60, friggin rare drop (not vendor) spell.

It heals 1200 hit points over 24 seconds for 200 mana (hope I got that right, or the anal retentive army will just tear me up). It has two negative side effects, snare and slow. Thus, melee types tend not to like it during a fight, but it is one of the most mana-efficient healing spells in the game (beaten only by Complete Heal).

The AA skills can work to improve it by making it cast faster and/or last longer. If you make it last longer, you can get an extra 'tick' (6 seconds), which is worth another 200 hit points. While I haven't gotten there yet myself, I think you can get Torpor to heal up to 1800 hit points by maxing out the AA skills related to duration.

Irksome
On the slow portion, it may be only 30%, but for a high level raid melee, that will be a drop from 210% base speed to 70% base speed. That is a very significant slow.

A 1% slow will over-write all haste effects. So, if you have a warrior with a 41% haste item, SoTS, and the bard song that stacks (110% haste or so), a 1% slow is a reduction of DPS by 50%. A 30% slow is reduction of DPS by 66%. Ouch.

However, if there is not cleric or druid around to do healing, I don't hesitate on Torporing. As someone above said, Torpor does not reduce the DPS as much as death does. :)
