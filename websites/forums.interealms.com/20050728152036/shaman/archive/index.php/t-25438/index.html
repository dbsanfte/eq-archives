taraddar
I think its pretty much acknowledged that AC goes in "steps". The latest thread I saw discussing this suggested there were very noticeable effects at the following numbers 1800ac, 2k ac and 2.2k ac.

I don't think this is really wrong but I do feel that is an over simplication of what is going on. Everquest actually really complicated in the way ti does alot of things. Ac may actually be one of the most complicated of the bunch.

Can I say with out a doubt that I'm right? No but i don't even think anyone at sony could either at this point. The people have changed probably several times over for most and the game has gone well beyond what anyone envisioned i'm sure. Can just look at things like class balance and the constant tweaking of any number of things to see this.

How does ac work? In a very specific cause it's very simple. The mob has an attack value. Thats compared against your ac value with a random number thrown in. The d & d analogy of taco, a d20 and ac works well here. Now just like D&D there's always a chance to hit. There also would seem to be always a chance to miss.

Theoretically speaking if my ac is significantly bellow the mobs attack value the odds are extremely high that he'll hit me and increasing my ac doesn't effect that much if any. 100 bellow or 200 bellow. Either way he's still nailing you.

By the same token if your ac is significantly above the mobs attack. Your already being hit for min or missed and adding ac isn't going to effect that much. 100 or 200 above doesn't make much difference either.

The natural result is what's called an S curve. It starts off reletively flat gets to a point and starts curving up substantially and then gets to another point and starts flattening off again. Too high or too low and it doesn't have much effect but when your going threw that sweet spot it makes a pretty big difference.

This is where the idea of steps come from. Mobs in a particular zone often share many things in common. Level, hps, etc. It's reasonable to assume attack is simular too. If all the mobs in a zone or in several popular zones have 2000 attack or what ever it happens to be then obviously in those area's your going to see great effects getting to the top of that curve at 2200 ac or what ever it is. Of corse you might need 2400 ac for the monks in the next zone or next expansion. Thus the value of ac is always dependant at least on what mob your considering.

There are other factors. Level difference seems to effect things more than just better gear and higher skills would seem to sugguest. I suspect that it adds a bonus or penalty to the equation outside of a particular range. Especially when you look at ranges where no improvements in skill are possible, IE going from lvl 60 to 65 for most classes.

In the extreme consider this. Do you think you'll take any less dps from a skelly nakid at level 70 or fully clothed? Either way your natural ac and level make it almost impossible for him to hit.

This is why I say on the one hand it can be very helpful in easier content but wouldn't consider it a substantial factor for challenging conent. We simply can't get our ac high enough to get to the bottom of that curve in challenging content. Sure ac is nice and always welcome on gear I pick up but I'd say it's only slightly ahead of wisdom. It's one of those things that just comes with good gear not what makes it good gear.
