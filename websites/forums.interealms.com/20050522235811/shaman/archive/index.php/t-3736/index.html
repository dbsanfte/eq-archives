mormegil
I've barely touched the bazaar, but I can already see this is going to profoundly change the game's economy.

The number of people actively auctioning items has gone way down, and I'm not sure if, as I suspect, this is the future of the player economy, or just the novelty factor of a new toy. I assume it's the future and more and more deals will be Ultima Online-style with NPC-type merchants.

Here's what I predict:

1) Massive influx of low-end items. Probably great for twinking or new characters. It was really never worth auctioning those 20 low-end items, but it sure seems worth it to watch TV for two hours and let people sift thru your satchel while you're afk.

2) Common items are now worthless. You'll see prices plummet on items once the seller takes note that 21 other players also are selling the same item. Having this kind of steep competiton will only heighten mudflation on everything but items massively in demand (fungi tunic) or massively rare.

3) Far fewer live auctions, which means bartering is dead. CBoL for Torpor? Sorry, pp only. Really, pp is supremely valuable now. Also, haggling is dead. You have 32,134pp for that 35kpp spell? Sorry. I suspect some people who don't want to be logged in 24-7 will /auc or use the bazaar channel, but I'm not sure how many.

3.1) The phrase "make offer" may disappear from the EQ Lexicon.

4) A wider selection of spells and items will be available at all hours. No more missing out if you don't play prime time. This, I assume, was the point.

5) Confused buyers. No more asking questions about items or seeing stats as the text scrolls by. Know the gear or get lost in the shuffle.

6) Phone bills rise across the world; servers snap under the load; computers everywhere overheat and die; and raids are cancelled because too many people are /afk to sell.

7) Finally, OMG THE EC TUNNEL IS EMPTY.
