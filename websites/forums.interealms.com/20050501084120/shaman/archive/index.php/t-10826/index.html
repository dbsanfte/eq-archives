EQ did used to be something of a roleplaying game where most things were difficult to do and not everyone was level 60.

EQ has been changed to be compatible with our fast-food culture. The game is hardly the same game anymore as it used to be pre-RoK. The game has been completely retooled because VI realised that a great many people play the game to raid, not kill the same mob 50,000 times in a row.

Before it used to be difficult to reach high level, or at least much more difficult than it is now. Look at the changes over time though - hybrid xp penalties removed, more classes being given xp rez, hell levels removed, travel removed, race hate used to be an issue, on and on. That's not to say those are bad things to change - just giving examples of how just about every major change in game dynamics has made it easier. Most changes to game dynamics since RoK or so have been geared at herding newbs into the high end game quickly enough that they don't get bored and quit while trying to "get to the fun part." VI has redesigned most of the game to put everyone on the fast track to level 60 regardless of skill. I don't think anyone would disagree with me when I say that being lvl 50 or lvl 60 used to mean you were a great player (or at least more so than it does now). Now it just means you play a lot.

People used to see the low and mid levels as part of the fun, not just some bullbleep you had to go through to get to the fun part. IMO, EQ was mortally wounded with Kunark and is still heading the same direction. For some, it's a fun place to meet people and do quests and things, but for most people anymore it's just an uber contest.

Just my 2cp... I realize probably no one is going to agree with me. But it's not the same game anymore, at least it's not played anywhere near the way it was. If you don't believe me, go down to Sol B next time Naggy is up, watch the noobs get their ageo, kei, etc., then none of them wait for assist and the raid wipe out. I realize that most people play the game to raid, but if you are going to make it that easy to get to high level, why not just enable people to start characters at level 50? (still waiting for them to sell that... start at lvl 50 for $50)

EQ is still a great game. Just not as great as the game that it used to be.
