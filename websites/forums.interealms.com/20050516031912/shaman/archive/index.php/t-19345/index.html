I 2-box a Shm/Mag frequently so I can address some of these points.

1. Use the earth pet. With the earth pet I can pull with slow and DoT once mob is in camp while rarely having to worry about aggro. With the air pet aggro is a pain and usually I wind up running in circles waiting for stun to land and the pet to gain aggro. Air pet is way too unreliable, best to go with earth pet.

2. Shm pet almost always gets aggro. I have tried shrinking Mag pet while growing Shm pet and vice versa, turning Shm pet taunt off, talking to him sternly, etc and nothing has worked. Lately I don't even use the Shm pet because he drains too much mana keeping him healed and his DPS stinks.

3. When I cast Stamina buffs on PCs their HP bar moves, the same does not happen when I cast Stamina buffs on pets. The only bit of evidence I have that Stamina makes a difference on pets is that the Mag summoned girdles have +sta. I think that is shaky evidence though.

4. I only give the Shm pet two summoned weaps, muzzle and a girdle. At a minimum give him two weapons. I wouldn't bother with the armor as his mitigation/avoidance is so bad that it doesn't seem to help.

5. I can slow, DoT and heal the Mag pet without gaining aggro. If I'm really laying on the DoTs I may get aggro on a root break, just gotta wait a few seconds for root to stick again.

IMO use the Shm to slow, DoT and heal if you are not charming with the Enc. If you are charming use the Clr because charmed pets really need CH.

Also realize that a Shm requires a lot of attention to be most effective. Mana regen is active on a Shm not passive as it is on a Clr. I'm not sure how you use your Enc other than pulling, but it seems like that setup would have 2 high attention toons in the Shm and Enc. So if you can't give the Shm enough attention then the Shm and Enc overlap too much and the Clr is a better option.

Or totally off-topic you could 3-box a Enchanter, Cleric and Ranger and be a charming machine.

I'm going to repeat some of what's said above, and contradict other parts.

I spent a few months doing little other than duoing with a mage, and here's what we learned.

Earth pet will always result in dogdog getting root-proximity aggro. You don't want to use the earth pet.

Air pet - this pet is just great for so many reasons. He's able to keep aggro from me and the dog unless I really let loose. We found a pretty reliable way to make sure I didn't end up tanking too often. Slow, of course, gets aggro - now, whether or not the air pet proc's or casts his stun, I can shed that aggro the moment the mage lands a nuke. So, pull with chanter, mage sic's pet, shaman slows, and mage nukes as soon thereafter as he can. As soon as that nuke landed, I was able to step away from the mob, who focused on the air pet. It seems that just putting the mage directly on the hate list is enough to get him to stop following me around.

Don't forget, also, that the stuns from that pet are going to significantly lower the DPS of the mob. My shaman spends a lot of time being slower and healer for dungeons - and the difference an air pet makes is phenomenal.

Pet preparation - both pets get 2 weapons, focus, and diaku - more dex = more proc's. (I would just infusion the dog instead of focus, because nothing will make him tank well - but since focus is up, I just give him that). Both pets also get muzzle and belt. Stamina buffs do nothing.

Oh, and if your mage doesn't have the focus item from Takish - get that ASAP. The regen on the pet with that thing is ridiculous. Especially when he's out of combat.

edit: I've never found that giving dogdog armor makes a ton of difference, but I believe it DOES make a difference. The mage would toss armor on the dog if he thought the situation was risky enough.

If your mage is 52 and shaman is 59, depending upon what you are fighting it is likely the shaman slow will dominate on the aggro side.

But, if you are fighting in Nurga, Seb and similar dungeons - so what. These are 59 shaman solo areas. Unless you are very under-geared, getting aggro will be a minor issue. Frankly, you mitigate incoming melee damage lots better than a 52 mage pet (or lower depending upon which pet) and Rover. Slow, dot if you want and tank. If you need to heal then root, step back, heal.

If you fighting mobs that are more in your level and high to the 52 mage, expect aggro and lots of heal time as you will have to slow to keep mage pet alive and aggro from it.
