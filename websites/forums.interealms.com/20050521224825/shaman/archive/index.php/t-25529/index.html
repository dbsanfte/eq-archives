Ah. Oops. Well, my comments were mainly aimed at the original poster. :) My point was really that they noticed a problem and tried to fix it. They may not have got it right yet, but at least they're responding.

Also, I agree with Sekin - the first 20 levels are great, so much so that I keep wanting to start new characters and try out classes. (Although I have to admit 18 is my highest level post-beta, so my experience of the "finished" game is limited.) I do look at the next 30 levels and wonder if it's as fun at 40-50 as it is at the moment. I also think I shouldn't rush to level too fast, as the content is still growing and being tweaked, and we are, as you say, still testing it for them.

One difference I see between EQ1 and EQ2 is that a lot of people play more obsessively now, and many are more interested in levelling than exploring and experiencing the world. Even my guildmates, who would all say they were "casual gamers", are playing most nights, although we all have several characters and aren't speed-levelling. When we see new zones or mobs we admire the graphics, but there isn't that feeling of excitement there was when we first explored Norrath in EQ1.

I remember zoning out of Everfrost to Qeynos Hills and being excited at a zone with different landscapes. Everything was green instead of snowy white! I remember exploring Kunark when no-one had any idea what was out there. It was thrilling in a way no computer game ever had been before.

Back in EQ2's beta, I declared I wouldn't visit the spoiler sites, and would just let the game unfold as I played. I wanted that feeling of exploring a world I got back in my EQ1 days. That lasted all of about a week into release. ;) As fun as it is to explore, there's only so much time you can spend randomly hailing people and poking around before you wonder if you're actually playing a game or ticking boxes on a checklist. I've realised no new game will ever give me the same sense of wonder, and to take them for what they are.

As many people have said, EQ1 has been around for years, has tremendous depth, and had that "first MMORPG" factor for many of us. EQ2 isn't going to recapture that no matter what it does. However so far it seems to be a good, solid game, more polished on release than many of the others I've tried, and likely to hold my interest longer than anything since EQ1. I played EQ1 for nearly 4 years, and I doubt I'll ever do that again. AO lasted 3 months, DAoC lasted 6, SWG 2, CoH 5... you can see the pattern. So far, if I pace myself, I can see EQ2 lasting much longer. I hope it does. It's not perfect, but it's the best so far since the original. I just have to make sure I don't outpace it.

Bleh, shouldn't post late at night. I'm rambling.

Actually, its all pretty reasonable up until the very last mob. Frankly, I wouldn't even bother with "Ancient Watcher" because I have to believe he's bugged.

This is a single group, instanced zone right? You get there, the mob is flagged as Group x 2 triple up, even con to a 31 Mystic.



That mob is needed for the haste ring quest. The ring is VERY nice. It can only be worn, IIRC, at 33. Stats are: Ancient Slayer's Ring of Wisdom: 9 wis 5 sta 16 health 30 power 269 vs mental 168 vs disease, effect haste of the ancient slayer, AC 49 at level 39.

Anyway, my guild farmed him for all the guildmates. There is a trick to beating him. The guy has a grav-flux that pushes you up and then back, and a NASTY rampage.

The trick is that half of your party must be at least 35 to do it. Having a 40+ tank is really almost necessary. We did it with 3 experienced guys and 3 people who needed the quest over and over each night till it was done, it takes 30 mins tops to kill the gargoyles then him.

The tank neeeds to stand in the corner, back to the wall so he cant' be gfluxed. The healers need to stand off to one side, far enough away from the barrage, but not directly behind, as that will be in the path of the gflux. Casters stand there too.

The scouts/other fighters stand directly in front of the mob at the point where the moat comes to a corner, (this will be right behind the mob once the tank engages and taunts him to turn around). And they need to be prepared for gflux. Ranged attacks from rangers are more useful than backstabbing due to gflux.

If you do it this way, you will win. The mob will be grey and drop no loot, but you'll win. It's a tough battle and things easily can go wrong and a wipe can happen very quickly.
