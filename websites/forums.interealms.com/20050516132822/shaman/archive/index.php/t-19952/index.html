Kylt
Cripple and speed do not stack.

If you get the message that "cripple will not take hold" vs an *unslowable* mob then you had better dispel the crap out of it, and fast. It is likely self-hasted or hasted by another mob.

Vs a slowable mob this wont come up as an issue very often because we generally slow before cripple, and the slow will overwrite the speed buff. Then cripple will land with no issue.

This becomes a major major issue in the Kod'Taz trials where all nameds are unslowable - you should be pulling all of these with dispel.

Chazic
Well there are several issues that could prevent Cripple from landing. As Cripple is a AC/Str/Dex debuff any spell on that mob that reduces one or more of those stats will in all likly hood block Cripple from landing. Sk, Druids, and several other classes all have spells with compontents built into them that will reduce one of those stats. If I am not mistaken I believe there are a few issues where a lesser reducing spell will block it as well due to one the other effects in that spell has been given a high level of priority on the spell stacking level. This information is just what all I have learned by casting and asking others what they are casting when I start to get bounces on spells. Though I believe the AC component is the biggest cause of cripple being blocked.
