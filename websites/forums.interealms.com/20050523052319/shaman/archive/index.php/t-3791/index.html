Cush
What race you are helps too... I think Everfrost is one of the best newbie zones, and you can make decent money as you go.

Standard advice to newbies:
Don't pester higher levels... nobody likes beggers, and some play tricks that end up getting you killed. However, feel free to (politely) ask advice - not many mind that.

Loot everything. Sell it to merchants.

If you're a barb, save up for a sewing kit and start using those ruined bear & wolf pelts to make your own Patchwork armor. Also, once you get a bit of Tailoring skill, you can make better money selling the finished armor to merchants - even over the cost of patterns.

Also, watch for quests - you can usually make some good cash or get items (for your level) as rewards.

Zyphyr
As a Vah'Shir, start talking to the merchants. There are several who will give you a 4 or 6 slot container to do a quest for them in if you hand them your Acryilia Slate (the thing you got for doing the citizenship quest).

That should give you a headstart on the container situation.

As far as money is concerned...

The newbie area down in the pit around the town is very poor in terms of cash and sellable items. I guess they figured that 99% of all Vah'Shir would be heavily twinked alts.....

Shadeweaver's Thicket is a little better. Hint - the Rockhoppers drop hides. The High Quality hides can be used by a high skill tailor to make 10 slot backpacks and as such can be sold to them for a moderate amount of money (by the standards of an untwinked newbie at least). Enough to buy a few of your level 1 and/or 5 spells.

Taladaen
HQ Rockhopper hides can be sold for as much as 30pp but I suspect the price might have gone down some. It's good to hear that you're not out there begging "Plz give me lewt" but there are creative and fun ways that will earn you some money or items from players.

Personally, any time I find someone who is an honest newbie to the game, I usually send them on some personal quests for me and reward them for their time. My usual offer is I'll buy their first 5 levels worth of spells. Try /auctioning in the zone a personal assistant for hire. Offer to run errands for them. Be creative and roleplay it up a bit. I don't know what drops in the area but in Everfrost I got my skill up in tailoring by making silk threads & silk swatches. There have been a number of times I've heard people hiring newbies to just simply do the tedium of combining silk into swatches. If there are enough supplies around to get your skill into the low 20s for tailoring, blacksmithing or anything similar, try to raise them up and offer your services. When I was working on blacksmithing I would of loved to pay someone else to keep me supplied in metal bits while I was doing harder combines.

These are just some suggestions on how you can help other players and in turn, they can help you with either cash, equipment and in-game tips.

Taabya
At level 3 you should probably be able to do the whiptail poison gland quest. Kill all the whiptail scorpions you can find in the pit and loot the poison glands (they are no drop and not stackable). Then take them to Spiritist Ragnar in the building beside the bank with the candle on it. Turn them in for some coin and padded armor. If you get armor you don't need, sell it. This is how I got money to buy all my 1-9 spells. Not hard at all, just takes a little time.
