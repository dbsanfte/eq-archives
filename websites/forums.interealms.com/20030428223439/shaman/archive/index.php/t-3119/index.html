Dandi
I have an alchemy skill of 180 and even with a philosopher's stone i stay at 180 when it is equiped.
You will not see the change in your list of skills when equipping an item that gives a bonus to one of them. The only thing that it changes is your effective skill, which improves your chances of success on a given combine. If you had a skill of 179 and had this stone equipped while making a titan potion (trivial at 180), you would still even have a chance of skilling up to 180 on that potion.

Dandi
From my understanding of the skill modifying items that once you hit max you are at max.

The rule is that no tradeskill can ever be higher than 255, either actual skill or skill with modifier. All tradeskills other than alchemy can get to actual skill of 250, but with modifiers will not be higher than an effective skill of 255. If you're using a 15% modifier at 250 skill your effective skill is still 255. This may have changed now that stats can go over 250 but that has not been officially suggested or confirmed.

Alchemy, however, cannot get to 250, so the Philopher's Stone will be more useful for us. By my calculations you will get the full benefits until you are at 230 alchemy which is still well beyond what we can achieve even at level 65.

So no worries, the stone will still give you the full 10% benefit no matter what your alchemy is at.
