there are lvls were we solo easy and then right before we get a new set of spells it gets tuffer (lvl 33, before we get our first pet for instance), but if you look over the hunting grounds section you will find all kinds of places to hunt.

must have gear? if you are a twink, buy an Iksar BP for the regen, get a nice pointy stick, or a good blunt. please don't ask about best race and the differences in them. just pick whatever you like.

balanced? erm. shaman change the most over the course of their game play, more than any other class.

we are the first class to get SoW, at 9, but we are the last pet class to get a pet; at lvl 34. we have decent melee skill till 50-60. we get root, and gate. but not see invis till 29.

our heals are decent untill you get to high lvl content, then they just don't keep up and/or take way to long to cast.

we have good mana regen thru the active method of cannibalization, but later other classes have better thru passive activities.

by 65 more shaman look at the added stats of their primary slot over the damage/delay ratio, and some of them turn into pure casters.

we have slow, and that has been our greatest gift, and our greatest curse.

as the game has changed with mobs having fewer hps but greater dps, the shaman role has become less necessary as mobs do not last as long as the duration of our spells do, and we waste mana by having a spell land on a mob that dies 10sec later. Trials and LDoN come to mind.

given enough time, shaman can kill solo lots of content. unfortunately the content is shifting to burst dps and burst heals; we do not currently keep pace in those categories. also the exp gained from solo'ing is not as it used to be.

lastly, we have a stackability issue on large events. after 2-3 shaman, you may find that the rest are not needed. and i am personally concerned that shaman may become the bot class of the future.

there are times tho when we shine and the fun factor is to the max. as the content changes many shaman are worried about usefulness, let alone the fun factor.

then again, there are shaman that see the issue entirely differently.

A lot of what you get out of a class depends on what you put into it. A good player can get the most out of a class and excel while a gimp can't get by even in god mode.

1. How well does a shaman solo compaired to other classes?
How quick/effienent is it?

At various stages, particularly at 65 once you start racking up superior gear and AA, shaman are potentially very strong soloists. Soloing efficiency varies. Some claim incredible xp gains from it while I personally find that almost any grouping crushes solo xp. The nice thing is that shaman CAN solo, and can often solo very interesting content, such that you can often farm loot or solo quests which can be rewarding in a variety of ways.

Despite shaman being able to solo, they are highly desired for groups and the strength of the shaman lies in filling in the gaps in a group and enabling a group to fight more effectively (debuffing being a part of this). I personally feel that shaman are highly suited for grouping even though we can solo.

2. Is there any must have gear I should try to get?

Opinions vary. Gear is important in EQ but in the early stages it won't be a big factor. Over time there are particular flavors of gear and some items you may want to get.

I never had excellent gear, and I still was able to do amazing things.

1. How well does a shaman solo compaired to other classes?
How quick/effienent is it?

In terms of versatility we solo very well. There are few mobs that any other class can solo and we can't. We can solo in a huge number of places and get xp and loot from it.

How quick is it? Probably about medium compared to other classes. We somewhat lack in dps, at least until you start stacking dots like mad at higher levels. On the plus side, once you get canni V (an AA skill) we have awesome mana regen, and even before that the other canni spells give decent regen. Not as good as necros (imho), but its good. Thus, whilst we kill slowly, we have little to no downtime between kills.

2. Is there any must have gear I should try to get?

To start with, a regen bp and a nice weapon, perhaps with a dd proc. Fungi for big twinking, iksar regen for something a bit more affordable. At 46, a Jaundiced Bone Bracer. Also around that time you'll stop meleeing so effectively, and might consider a horse.

At 58+, focus on getting your spells before worrying too much about gear.

3. Are you guys overall happy with the class? Do you feel that you are balenced? (as far as EQ balence goes anyway lol)
And if not why?

Up until you start serious raiding, I think the shaman class is as balanced as any other. We solo reasonably well and we can make worthwhile contributions to groups. There are patches where we are very weak, particularly some level ranges pre 55 between getting heal upgrades. At other times we do very nicely, like when we just get a new pet level, new heal or new DoT.

Problems start at raiding levels. We don't stack too well, 2-3 shaman is plenty for almost all raids, and quite often just one or two can do everything necessary. Furthermore, as beastlords get their level 65 slow and decent gear and AAs, they start to be able to take our place in xp groups, and enchanters with their charmed pets often get taken in preference to us too. However, I still find LDoN groups without too much problem - our utility as healer+slower who can also do soem cc is still valuable.


Overall - I love my shaman, still play him lots and would still say they are a good, fun class to play. Few other classes have such a variety of things to do, or such a range of options as to how to handle a given situation. We can solo, group, or raid. You can kite, tank in teh face, root-rot or get scared and gate.

What we can't do: We can't kill things particularly fast, our nukes are very poor. We can't do much on raids if you're not the no.1 or no.2 shaman. Our heals aren't enough to keep up against high end slow-mitigating mobs. We can't do much dps without getting aggro.
