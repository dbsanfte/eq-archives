Honestly depends on how your tank is equiped.

Gear makes all the difference for warriors. They dont have any spells to augment thier abilities. (they can use spells from items, true, but nothing innate and Im not counting \disc here).

If hes in banded or fine steel you are gonna have to get him almost to your level before he can really start doing his job. If you have him in some nice cultural (like Dwarven or Ogre..thats pretty inice stuff) then he can probably start making a difference at about 30 or so.

As far as weapons go..the better they are the easier everything will be.

Considering your shamans level Im betting that you are not rolling in plat right now, so your best bet is to take your shaman somewhere where the cash is good and then hunt the bazaar like crazy.

Incidentally, the best cash spot that I have found to date is Dulak. While doing my Stoicism quest I was getting about 5 plat per kill, plus weapon (another 3 to 6 plat). Gems dropped like crazy too (a blue diamond of a guardian of some sort made my day). In the 5 hours that Iwas there I made close to 1.5K. And most of this stuff was green or light blue to me. Id recomend heading there is you can.

At level 40 your warrior will get a big boost in hit points, etc. You should be able to duo well with him once he gets there.

I would encourage you to continue with your warrior rather than start another class. I have a 50 shaman that I have recently been two boxing with a 45 warrior and I have been very pleased with his tanking abilities. AC/HP gear for warriors is fairly cheap and he maintains agro very well. I know there is a lot of talk about warriors having a difficult time holding agro but this has not been a problem for me. He is also a very low maintenance character to two-box with, as you have probably already found out.
