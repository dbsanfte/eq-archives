Macnair
Health of Target's Target is very powerful. The fact that it tells you the health of target's target is a nice bonus, but the hugely useful piece is: you know who has aggro. If I've got aggro from slowing, everyone sees it: when the tank gets it back, everyone sees it. We're not guessing, we know. This is hugely useful information.

I love Spell Awareness, though some people who group with me hate it for the spam. I argue that the basic amount of spam has not changed: you always saw a message that says MobX begins to cast a spell. But now you get a useful message: MobX begins to cast a spell <Complete Heal>. After I listened to the complaints about spam for a while, I learned to say: If you don't want to know this information, filter it out or learn to ignore it. But it is a great advantage to many of us to know what spells are being cast by other players and by mobs.

Delegate Main Assist is quite helpful for the targetting impaired, who are many.

Health Enhancement gives 100 hp, that's not a lot but I got it, and I think it's worth getting.

ATK Enhancement adds I think 30 ATK when you have all of it, that's a lot of aa for not a lot of benefit, but melees love ATK. It's the one I'm working on now.

Mana Enhancement gives 100 mana, again not a lot but I think it's worth getting. I've bought one level so far, 30 extra mana.

Health Regen will add a little bit of hp regen, but I haven't put any aa into it yet.

Inspect Buffs looked to be a very useful leader skill for those of us who do a lot of buffing, and are always getting tells from people who say "I need focus" 10 minutes after you just cast focus on them. I figured it would be very nice to be able to use this and say, "Yah you need focus all right, because Fo7 is up in your buff bar right now and has 109 minutes till it needs to be refreshed." But now that everyone can inspect their own buffs and timers, these leader aa points are now a waste.

Juujuu
My purchase order I think I did:
1. HOTT first. Enough other people mentioned how good it is above that I don't need to go on. Should be the first Raid Leader AA bought as well.
2. Delegate Main assist. I love the target ring. I don't have an assist hotkey (too many other hotkeys already on main page). Makes it easy to know if I'm on the wrong mob.
3. Mark npc 1. I got this for helping on some strategic pulls. It's good for marking the charmed pet in an xp group and places like Tipt. I like additional clickies and abilities so that's why I got this so early. It's useful for targetting named mobs so you can see them when they are far away (like in KT for wandering named cragbeasts and hynids).
4. Spellspam. I like knowing what spells people casting. It lets you see what spells npcs are casting at you too. Some people h8 it and think it's too much spam. I don't think so, as you already have the "soandso casts a spell" spam. Instead, it's "soandso casts XXX". It's no more spam than you already had, it's just different more informative spam.
5. NPC Health. It's pretty marginal in it's use. It's also only 2 points, so I got it out of the way already.
6. Health 1 2 3. Can never have enough HPs. It's not a huge amounts of HPs, but its some.
7. Attack 1 2 3 4 5. I had to pick between regen, mana, and attack tree next. I never go oom, and neither do my group members even on raids. Regen is pretty insignificant when mobs are hitting you for 1k a pop. Increasing attack is always useful, especially for my fairly melee heavy groups and guild raids.
8. Delegate Mark NPC. It was 4 points to get it out of the way. I use mark NPC a lot to direct the main assist at times. I also party with other people that have leader AAs and if they want to be able to mark things, they can too.
9. This is as far as I've gone so far. I'm working on bigger mana pool next. After that will be regen. After that probably Mark npc 2 and 3. Lastly Inspect buffs*.

* Inspect buffs is so very far down on my list because you can see your own buff durations now. If you couldn't see how long your buffs were going to last, I'd probably get it at #8.
