First off, do not make a pet of your own. It'll simply be lifetap DoT bait for the necro.

Root the necros EoT. Continually move out of range of his spells. If you have a horse it'll help alot. If not, SoW vs his jboots/run3 should be sufficient.

If he's smart he'll have cure poison and cure disease potions so nothing you do will really dmg him. You should definitely outlaw potions or you have no hope other than meleeing him to death.

Your nukes will be absolutely ineffective except for Torrent of Poison and that only if he decides to stand still under the 3 waves. The rest of your nukes will easily be negated by his fast casting taps that will land on you while you're casting the nuke.

This is not a fight that's easy to win and if it's in close quarters, I don't think you'll be able to win at all. In the outdoors I have beaten necromancers in duels with the simple rule of no cure disease or poison potions. If potions are allowed you can try to kite the necro and everytime he dispells insidious decay reapply it then run again so that he never actually gets rid of your DoTs. This would be tedious and there's alot of room for error.

Shamans are pretty terrible at high-end PvP vs prepared opponents. Resist gear makes our poison DoTs, even after Malo, utter crap. They get resisted way too much. Our nukes suck. Dogdog is no good vs someone with a much much stronger pet like a necro. If you open up instant click items (which you shouldnt) then the necro can pretty much instakill you. If he has lifeburn he's going to make your job even more difficult.

That's fine though, we're a support class. Even in PvP, we're a support class :).

I know there will be tons of replies about how I am wrong and how you can just DoT up the necro or just torpor until he's OOM or about how our poison DoTs are good in PvP... but you're wrong :). There's no way a necro should be able to kill a shaman but there's also no way the shaman should be able to kill a necro. Running the necro OOM shouldn't work either as they can use liche and regain life from low-con mobs around the battle or if it's in the arena and the quarters are tight, just from you :).

Click away spyglass asap.


Dmg shield reporting on, /particle low. Watch his spells. Go offensive with melee, but never cast a spell unless it is to counter something he is doing. Remember that if you dispell the opponent while your pet has your opponent targetted, your pet will poof. Same goes for the necro.

Blind and/or root his pet. I usually opt for blind since you cant dispell it.

Tear him apart with a twohander, time the slams when he is casting. Strafe around him to hit him in the back as much as possible. Make sure you dont rip yourself apart on his possible dmg shield.

Watch out for him using two quickcasting spells like siphon strength and vortex of shadows to mess with your spell list. If he casts one of them on you, thus filling buffslot #2, make sure your spell list is full so that vortex lands at the end. Then click away sow asap. This means his next dot lands on buffslot #1. If the spell he casts after siphon strength has a cast time longer than 1.5secs it is NOT vortex and thus probably a dot, cancel sow to receive the dot in buffslot #1. Annul it and reapply sow.

Learn to recognize spells like annul, liche, all necro dots, and the drains by their graphics. Learn their manacosts and cast times. Inspect him before the fight (if allowed) to check on possible focus items.. especially dd and cast time items.

If pvp toys are allowed, have pumice, cure potions, ds potion, instasummon pet neck (from KC) and rod of malisement from Phinny (great to use on his pet). There are more out there but i'll leave that to you.

In general a shaman should tear up a necro using melee. With a haste item and alacrity buffed (not celerity, it has too long cast time) and regrowth ticking along and a fungi you should be able to wear him down. Sprinkle in epic dots and an occasional bracer to confuse him. Might be worth it to epic dot his pet, but you would have to root and buff his pet with bracer of hidden before epicing, so that his dispell doesnt cancel your epic dot. Reapply root and bracer when he annuls.

Necro and shaman are both extremely versatile in pvp. It would be near impossible to write out a comprehensive guide that covers every little aspect of such an encounter. A well played necro is very nasty in pvp, but often most necros plain out suck since they are not used to having to flex their pvp muscles to score kills.

Good luck... and let us know how it goes. Make a log please. 8)
