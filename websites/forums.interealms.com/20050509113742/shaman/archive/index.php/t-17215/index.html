Kianor
Actually, GEBs are okay at lower levels for the effect. Although, even at those levels, Barbed Dragonscale are good for a similar price. But some people like having decent focus effects.

However, I don't agree with belittling people for their choices - especially when they have no reason to know any better. I don't like the term "druman" or the derogatory comments that are sometimes made when someone posts a high-wis, low-hp profile asking for gear advice.

If they haven't come here and worked it out beforehand, shamans can get to their low 50s before realising that having all the wis in the world won't keep you alive after a single bounced slow in PoP, and start changing their gear focus.

Anyway, high wis/mana, low HP at lower levels isn't really a problem, especially if you play like a druid, which works perfectly well at those levels. But in PoP, as a slower, it starts to hurt and if you have less than 2000 unbuffed HP at 60/65 (and I've seen that), then you have serious problems.

My own story illustrates this.

I remember sitting in Highpass at around level 15 being told by a shaman who was much higher level (I think low 50s) wearing GEBs that wisdom was the most important stat for a shaman, and you should work on wis gear first until at least got it to 200. Trusting person that I am, I had no reason to disbelieve him, he was a high level! Plus, my shaman was an alt, so I never spent real cash on him.

I levelled fairly slowly, mostly playing healer to friends' rangers, until I was around 45 and my guild was desperately short of shamans, so I started to level in earnest. In my low 50s, though, several things happened - I started getting PoP groups, I switched mains from ranger, and I started reading these boards. :)

I kept an alternate profile of the gear I had before I made the switch - if the link works, it might amuse you... remember, it was low 50s, without ND3 and charm, so HP would have been much lower even than the 1764 shown: http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=94917&alt=125188 Over a few weeks, through moving some of my rangers gear to my shaman and spending around 15k (a lot for me at the time), I gained around 500hp and 100ac, lost 30ish wis, but kept around the same mana pool (hooray for +hp +mana items). More importantly, I knew what direction to go in for the future.
