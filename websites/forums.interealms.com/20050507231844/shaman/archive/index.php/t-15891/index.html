In fact, if there IS an impromptu raid for us on the weekend, it has to be somewhere like the Hole, which in truth one group can handle. For the sake of getting the guild together and socializing, we take what we can get.

Our official raid nights are Tuesday and Thursday, with Monday being our epic raids.

At least 50% of the time our raid consists of going to a zone, finding it camped. Sending out alts to an alternate zone, finding it camped. Finding a very low level zone that isn't camped and going there just to do something. These zones are not camped by groups, they're camped by guilds at the same level we are at. In the case of places such as Kael, they are occasionally camped by a group of high levels taking down the Avatar (for example) because they too can't find a place for the guild to raid.

There isn't enough content to go around, at least at our level (50 to 60). Period. We don't whine - it is what it is and we still get a lot of enjoyment out of the game.

But there is no way on god's earth anyone can say there's enough content. There isn't.

So in response to the original topic - so this is end game? Well, it's the whole game, as far as I can see. Hopefully LDoN will solve some of these issues.

Grouping, on the other hand, is a piece of cake. I get a group within five minutes of asking the guild or turning on LFG, and we rarely if ever have a problem finding a decent place to grind. This problem (as I see it) is to do with raid encounters and people's expectations. LDoN seems to have been designed exclusively to combat that issue, so kudos to SoE for dealing with it (assuming it actually works, eh).
