Both the froglok forager and hunter are level 45. They've got around 5k HP and hit a ton (117 roughly). But then, almost all the mobs in TT hit like that.

Anteil is correct: they either instantly respawn, or in their place will spawn a rare on the cycle, elsewhere in the zone. When that rare is killed, the froglok respawns.

As my Bard (51), I was able to work the frogloks with a 46 chanter and a 59 druid (root-dotting), then came back the week after with a 41 cleric, and duo'ed them. (Granted, Bards have more options for DoTs.)

If you're planning on soloing these as a Ranger (Which I've seen my brother's 46 Ranger do a couple times)... a couple things to keep in mind.

If they pass a froglok shaman, they'll get buffed. (*cough* sow *cough*).
When pulling, you'll most likely get adds. The only ones that will be 'easy' to take care of are the tigers and birds. Fear should stick easily. (Except on the nameds.)
Try to be 45+ with a Tolan's Bracer, and have a full quiver+ of arrows at the ready. Of course, this means you'll have to be a bit closer to hit them, as the summoned arrows are only 50 range.
DoTs will take a while as well. Try to have Tolan's Arms to reduce mana expenditures. Also, try to root as opposed to snare - this way the 30% decrease on DoT effectiveness while moving doesn't take effect.
Other than that, melee when they start to run, you'll feel better beating the heck out of them ;)
