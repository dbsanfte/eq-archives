Sithrax
This post is about high-risk missions only. Normal missions are so easy that I can serve as the sole healer/slower/buffer, and thus achieve high efficiency that way. My typical group for doing these missions is SK CLR ENC SHM RNG MNK. All toons are high AA - I have the lowest total at 140. We are all geared in a mix of Luclin/Elemental stuff for the most part, but no one has Time gear or anything like that.

The problem on these missions is that I feel pretty marginal. I have trouble coming up with a group configuration in which a shaman would be really desirable - you'd be more likely to settle for a shaman if you couldn't get a more useful class.

There are 5 main roles that I can see in a high-risk group.

1) Main healer
2) CC
3) Slower
4) Tank
5) DPS

At least for me, being main healer is not an option. I can be sole healer for singles if our cleric dies, and I can even handle 2 mobs at once if nothing goes seriously wrong. The pulls of 3 and 4 mobs are just too risky to have me as sole healer. When 3-4 unslowed, 990 hitting mobs come crashing into the group, that's a massive DPS burst. I have no real way to counter this - no group heals, no Divine Arbitration, no Spirit of Wood type AA, nothing. If they all end up on the tank, there is no way chain Tnarg's is going to keep the SK alive against 4 mobs. So, the healer role is generally left to clerics and occasionally druids.

CC in high-risk is all about mez. There seem to be an abundance of cleric and wizard mobs, which need to be mezzed rather than rooted. This is the province of bards and enchanters. VP would be nice, if it wasn't resisted about half the time.

Slowing is something we can clearly do. However, since our slow has no resist mod, we are stuck using Malos to make sure it lands 90% of the time. A resisted slow means you're going to have an unslowed 990 quadding mob very mad at you. This is bad news for me, and I have over 6k HP fully buffed, plus good defensive AA (CA3, LR5, CS3, ID1).

DPS - not so much. Rains are often limited in their usefulness due to mezzed mobs, so they are very situational. Our cold/poison DD is slow, and thus you are looking at ~100 dps with chain nukes. Bane is okay, but you need to Malos first to make it land reliably, and if the chanter has a CoD pet, it often will not run its full course.


Sadly, LDoN appears to be nothing more than a DPS time trial, and because of this, shaman are just not that great. By way of comparison, we did 2 missions back to back - one in which the chanter used a CoD pet, and one in which she did not.

Mission 1 (CoD pet used): Completed in 48 minutes
Mission 2 (no CoD pet): Completed in 80 minutes

Clearly, an enchanter brings a TON more to the table than we do. Since everything mitigates, the gap between our slows is even smaller than the paltry 5% it normally is. Vastly superior CC and DPS, near-equivalent slow... I found myself wondering if I would ever get into these missions without the benefit of friendship.

The only group I can see us being useful in is one that is heavy in melee DPS and has a bard to act as puller/mezzer while the shaman slows. Something along the lines of SK/PAL/WAR, CLR, SHM, BRD, ROG, ROG. Even so, I would rather have an enchanter than a shaman, because of the huge DPS boost provided by CoD. A beastlord would also be a better choice, because of near-equivalent slow and significantly better DPS.

So I have to ask... is anyone else running into the same situation? I'd like to be more valuable, rather than feeling like I should be playing my friend's rogue.

(FYI, just so you think I'm not doing everything I can think of, here's what I do when I am in this group. I use an alpha pet, maintain FA on the melees, and act as main slower. I use backup heals to conserve cleric mana and I babysit the chanter on charm breaks, and keep her pet Malos'd. DPS is dealt out via pet, using nukes/rains when there's a CoD pet, or via Bane/nukes when there isn't one.)
