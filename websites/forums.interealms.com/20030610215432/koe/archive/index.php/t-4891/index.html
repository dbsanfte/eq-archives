As far as charm, if we are talking about just two mobs it's not that tough. More than two makes it darn near impossible.

You will want three hotkeys.

1. One with your charm song and a message to let your group know which mob you are charming.

2. One with the command /pet attack (pretty sure you know how this one works ;) )

3. An assist key set to whoever is tanking in your current group.


Target your soon to be pet, hotkey 1 to charm. As soon as it sticks, use F8 to target the hostile and hit hotkey 2. Then hit F1 twice to retarget your pet (NOTE: I am assuming you are noncombat and letting your pet tank. If you are tanking as well, ignore the bold text).
When your pet breaks, it's hotkey 1 again and repeat the sequence (NOTE: If you don't have your pet targetted when it breaks, back up and use F8 again. The former pet will stick to you like glue and should be the closest hostile). I also like to have Hymn hotkeyed and fire it up as soon as I send the pet in to heal any damage I may be taking from the two during the charm break.
Whatever you do, DO NOT charm the mob you have been beating on unless your former pet is at very, very low health. As long as you haven't hit the target mob, he will turn to fight your pet. If you have damaged him in any way, or otherwise generated hate (i.e. charm), he will come after you and ignore your pet. This means that:
A. your pet is dealing very little damage while chasing the target,

B. your pet isn't TAKING damage (remember, you want to finish him off next), and
C. You are getting beat on.



A full charm is 15 seconds. Once you get a feel for the duration, you can be backing away from both mobs while recasting charm (assuming you have the room to do so) and take very little damage. With hotkeys, you can have your pet back on target in less than a second after recharming.

Charm works better than mez until level 53, especially from level 40 to 52. Crissons pixie strike (your low level mez) has a terrible resist rate. Charm goes in much easier. Once you get Song of Twighlight at 53, you will use charm less and less.

One last note, 120 CHA seems to be the best target for charming. I haven't seen any difference in running CHA higher than this, but there is a noticable difference in resist rate with your CHA lower than 120. Pick up a set of opaline earrings and 7 CHA bracers when you can. They are cheap and should put you over 120. Switch em out for AC when in a full group.

F8 targets the nearest hostile NPC. Well, the nearest NPC that isn't a pet, actually. Pets will attack now only after you are aggroed, so you have to either tell them to attack or get hit at least once for the pet to go back in.


Using F8 and a /pet attack hotkey, you can get your pet back on target very fast.


I think the key to charm soloing is having room to do it. If you can back away from your pet a bit and back up while recasting charm, you won't get hit much.


And no, you cannot recharm until charm breaks. This works the same way for all charms, as far as I am aware.


So, stick to outdoor zones where you can get some room to maneuver. If you want to avoid getting hit altogether, twist in Selos just before the break and back away while recasting. Or buy SoW potions and keep SoW up. Either will keep you out of range while you recast, F8, and hit your /pet attack hotkey.


P.S. ( Another great way to do this is to partner with a druid. If the mobs are snared you are guaranteed to take almost no damage, and a nuke or two is handy to finish off your pet after the first target goes down. Very mana efficient, almost no downtime at all.)
