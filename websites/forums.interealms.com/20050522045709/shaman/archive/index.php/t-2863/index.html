"The question is, those of you with horses, are they worth it and does it matter which one I get?"

Yes, very much worth it. Second, just for soloing no it doesnt matter and you should get the lowest one.

"I hear you can med while chaincasting JBB type stuff and with horse models turned off, you can run at normal sow speed.
If I do get the horse, I'll probably turn off the models, so I probably don't care how it looks, or the speed."

With models on you can still sow yourself. The reason people turn horse models off is because the horsehead movement and bobbing motion is totally annoying. Plus you are quite larger with models on and targeting is difficult. In addition to that, you cant duck out of spells, it takes a few moments to start/stop completely, and when you are in the spell book you have to click "done" to exit it (rather than just standing).

While on horseback you are always medding as long as you arent moving or have autoattack turned on. You will med through spell casting and item casting.

"Is there a difference in horses? How much would the lowest end horse be?"

Horses differ in run speed, looks, and cost. Otherwise no notable difference I believe. The lowest end horse is about 8300pp with 115+ cha.

"Are they tough to use? Like mounting/dismounting all that crap?
Or does that stuff even come into play with the models turned off?"

With models turned on they are tricky to use as you cannot duck out of spells, the spellbook thing, and it takes a few seconds to accellerate and decellerate. Also, targeting is particularly difficult in first person view with the giant horse head swaying back and forth all the time.

With horses turned off, you will not see any of the drawbacks of horses and will see all the bonuses (except you wont look as cool). A slight drawback though is that if you are riding a horse on a raid and other people have horse models turned on then it may impact their game.

Jarren Icewulf
IMO...get a JBB first...then save up for horse.

Horse w/JBB is awesome. Horse w/o JBB is just ok.

Only thing took me a bit to get used to (soloing) was not attacking while tanking and JBBing the mob. So what I do is wait until JBB is just about done casting, and I turn on attack so I can get a chance at hitting the mob between chain casting the JBB. Then I turn attack off, while JBB starts casting again (8 secs for JBB to cast). That way I'm medding for most of the time while tanking and JBBing. This works really great when using a Barabarian Spiritist Hammer which procs same spell as JBB (Winter's Roar, Max Ice Dmg of 263) so sometimes in between JBB casts, I swing hammer and get that extra 263dmg right after JBB lands 263dmg, which is always nice to see.

Have horse models turned off, this is just more convienent and allows you to use your SoW while being on your invisible slow horse (assuming you bought the cheap one). Though other players seeing you on your horse (cause they have Horse models turned on) going as fast as SoW, will think you are on a very expensive horse....at least peeps thought I was.
Borgax
Nothing major, but sometimes people can't trade with you and you with them. Sometimes you can't cast on a person right next to you and get "out of range" message. These are easily fixed by clicking off the horse then summoning again after. They also don't happen too often (maybe trading does, haven't checked).

Also, the horse is an actual NPC/pet/part of you and can be targetted. This is most apparent if you back up a bit, then click or hit F8 to target and you'll get "Borgax's mount", and you have to move forward again before F8 will not target your horse instead. At least this always happens for me.

None of these are a big deal, but wanted to make sure all info was available to people and were forewarned.
