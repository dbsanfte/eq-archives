LaughingWoman
When I think about the fun I've had in EQ, I'm forced to admit that my enjoyment of the game itself really decreased when I hit 65. I don't want to explore the reasons for that here, but rather concentrate on the implications. The EP-raiding-guild experience was infinitely boring (to me - I think it's great that some people dig it), so after a long break, and being faced with the truth that EQ still does hold something that I truly love and enjoy, I'm trying to get back in touch with that. A lot of people are going to think I'm flat out nuts for leaving behind a couple hundred AA's, EP flags, VT key and gear to start at level 1 again. I would have said it was impossible a year or so ago, myself. I guess 3 or 4 years of the same game will do things to you.

Here are the rules I've made for myself, to try to avoid falling into the same traps that caused me to stop enjoing the game in the first place. Because honestly, I blame the downfall of EQ 50% on the devs mucking up the class balance and content, and 50% on myself for falling into the patterns of me-too, greed, and level-grinding.

I'm writing this primarily to get the points firmly in my mind so I have less chance of "forgetting" and running off to the bazaar. :P And I figured I'd put it here, because I see a lot of frustrated people who perhaps can find some similarity between things that dragged the game down for me, and what they're experiencing.

New server - no twinking, no PL'ing, no asking old friends about what the guild is doing, no links about the gear you're not getting. I adore my old Xev friends, but there's always crossserver channels if we decide to keep playing.

No twinking - how many times have you looked up a quest item or flipped through the listings on Alla's, just to say "what a great quest, too bad the reward is so gimpy"? I'm finally doing some of these newbie quests (the new tutorial is fun if you don't need a pet during the first 5 levels, too), and the starter iksar quest claw is GREAT if you're upgrading from a cracked staff....

No bazaar - this is basically the same as 'no twinking' except you're using the exaggerated price of tradeskill components combined with mudflation to twink yourself. At level 5 you can start farming well enough to equip yourself better than the gear you'll get from level 20 quests. But my point this time around is not to have the best gear in the shortest time. It's actually to take as long as possible to get the gear I want to extend my entertainment value from the quest. Or, alternately, to upgrade as often as possible to keep that carrot in front of ourselves.

Quest, quest, quest or
No grinding - most likely, this will spell the end of the Starting Over Experiment, because we WILL run out of quests eventually (hopefully LDoN is good at low levels). But so far, these 11 levels have been incredibly entertaining because we've had a focus and a goal the entire time. Anywhere we hunt, it's for a purpose. We see new places, go to zones we've never had an interest in before. We're not leveling as fast as we can, we're not picking zones based on the ZEM (burn in hell, paludal). Since we still have a few empty inventory slots, ANY quest item is worth doing for them... which keeps us busy.

No class boards - No reading about the problems with the class in the endgame, balance issues, how screwed our spell lists will be for levels 66-70. For all I know, I would still be playing and loving my shaman if I hadn't joined a raiding guild, gotten bored, and then read about (and experienced) our dim usefulness in GoD. I'm not blaming the Crucible, I'm acknowledging that I'm not the type of person who can know that stuff and just get over it.

Overall, I'm trying to remember that I'm not racing to a high level. I'm not battling to have better gear than the next guy, and my ratio of /played time to level has absolutely zero bearing on my fun factor. Most likely, I'm about the millionth person to play EQ and realize this. I, too, have said "there's no WAY I'm starting over on a new server, I've invested way too much time in this character!"

I'm not sure how well this will work in the long run, but it's more fun than EQ has provided for a while now. If anyone else has points to share regarding helping to keep yourself from ruining your own fun, please do.
