For tanks, A SK can lifetap to help you with healing, a pally can group heal, a warrior is going to have to taunt a pray he gets the tree on him.

Any caster (bar a mage) can root the mob off. A mage can offtank with his pet (which should be focused and he should be using a tank pet unless you have an enchanter). A BST pet can offtank just fine as well.

Rangers can do fine CC, rogues... just DPS.

If you're grouped with a warrior, make sure they don't neglect their AC for all HP. All HP is nice if you have complete heals landing, but for shaman healing avoidance and mitigation are key. I know when I used to play my SK/SHM pair, I'd tell the warrior not to tank, because even though she had 800 more HP, they did jack since her AC was about 100 points below my SK. She tied herself to cleric healing by gear selection - even though her HP went down slower as a percentage, the SHM didn't heal as a percentage, as she absolutely lost HP faster.

So where do shaman clearly suck at healing? When things hit for 800s and 900s or more and mitigate slow and come in multiples. Of course, I concede the point that some shaman with time level gear with above par tanks aren't going to notice the problems that the more casual non-raiding shaman with a 65 tank with bazaar gear and 30 AAs will notice in these areas, and it gets worse in upper end GoD where shaman can't even heal one round of combat while spamming the best heal without getting a crit on every heal.

So to sum up: There really isn't a problem that I ever noticed in lower content (as long as you are of the PROPER LEVEL, which you clearly are), but there is a problem in hard LDON's, upper tier POP and a good majority of GOD, and this will probably get worse in OOW assuming no changes are made.

Goldark
A lot depends on who's in the group and who's pulling but I agree that a 62+shaman should be fine.

If you've got a bard then you should be getting single pulls, pre-debuffed as they arrive in camp. Already its 34% slowed and snared, or at least its resists reduced. Malo + slow and you shouldn't get resists.

If you've got a chanter then they can help with tash/slow, and also mezz any adds. In a pinch they can use rune to protect people.

Paladin MT? Get them to help out with group heals and emergency heals, they're actually very good at it. Ask them to stun on inc, gains a few valuable seconds to debuff and all but guarantees them aggro.

SK MT? They can lifetap at the start if things get hairy. Snare and kite. FD if its really bad and someone else can take a few hits.

Warrior MT? Should have enough hp and mitigation to take a few hits unslowed before you're ready to heal.

Ranger? Have them pull with snare, so you have time to malos and slow on inc. No problems to stick torpor on youself before the pull if aggro is a worry. They can help heals a little bit too in a pinch.

Beastlord? Get them to help slow. Can also help heal in a pinch, and offtank mobs with their pet - who they can heal remarkably well. Often, pet will take aggro if the MT backs off and pet taunt is on, which is great if you need a couple more seconds before a heal lands.

Mages - same deal as beastlords with their pets.

Wizards - masters at AE snareing and kiting, put it to use if you get adds. Can also burn mobs down fast if MT isn't looking like making it.

Monks - can still tank a little, particularly with discs and mend, and then FD before they get too low on health.

The overall message is that you CAN heal, but it does require a bit more skill on behalf of the whole group than with a cleric. On the flip side you can replace that cleric with some dps for faster and more exciting xp :) And as others have said, don't be too shy to use torpor on the MT (don't hit the rogues with it though!) at the start of a fight. They can always click it off after a few ticks if all is ok.

At level 63, Tnargs is plenty of healing to keep up with an unslowed tier1 mob until it is under control. Keep Tnargs and Kraggs memmed at all times. Hell, back then I think I even had Tnargs, Kraggs, and Chloroblast memmed since chloro took a lot less mana than Tnargs did.

A few pointers for the original poster though:

1. Malo on incoming. Don't listen to anyone who says to wait until the tank has aggro on this spell. Assist the puller and spam your malo key until it starts casting. If you do not have malo, do the same with malosini (they both have similar aggro). Then note if the mob still smacks you first and if so, leave the group or boot the MA (seriously, the malox line is so low aggro that a single arrow takes it practically).

2. As soon as malo lands, cast slow. Don't try to do a quick heal on the tank inbetwee, get that mob under control asap. As has been said before, with a malo on a tier1 mob first you will see a slow resist one out of maybe 50 casts, *maybe*.

3. Make sure you are on a mount. If not, this should be a priority.

4. I was never a big fan of Torpor on the melees when there was a mob in camp. However, with an sk or pally tank that is an option as they will generate most of their aggro through spells anyway. On the other hand, make sure you have torpor on yourself at all times and using canni4 as much as possible. Tnargs might be costly and ineffecient, but constant torpor/canni4 goes a long way :)

5. Try to get yourself and groupmates buffed with virtue and c3 if you can. Some say they are "crutches" - don't listen.

6. Beware of the dilemma you will be faced with if you decide to level to 65 and try for Q, basically that you will not be able to stay in tier1 planes (except for some very specific spots). The XP at even level 63 in tier1 is horrible compared to tier2+. Basically, if you plan to get to 65, you should move on to tier2+.

Weyalan
Nothing wrong with using Torpor.

Remember that the MT plate class is not necessarily there for their ability to do DPS. Other classes can easily maintain significantly higher dps than the Warrior (or, indeed, the Paladin or Shadowknight). The tank is there to generate enough aggro to maintain the mob's interest.

Their aggro does not really come from their dps, but from a combination of taunt and aggro proc. Rate of proccing is not effected by the slowing of attack speed associated with Torpor, neither is taunt rate.

So, although it is a commonly held misconception that torpor is not a good heal, it is, until Quiesence, our most mana efficient heal, and should be used as such.

I never use our mini CH - I find the 10 second delay just too restrictive. I got an umproved healing focus item at about level 58, and I could crank out more healing with the smaller fast casting heal(Tnargs Mending?), which, in combination with torpor, should be enough to get the job done.

In terms of mana efficiency, use Toropr as your primary healing tool, with Tnargs as a spot heal when you need a bigger hit of healing fast.

when you are in a healing role, the hardest thing is to keep up with the mana expenditure - you should never sit, and if you aren't actually casting, you should be mashing that Canni button and torporing or stoicing yourself.

I was never a big fan of Torpor on the tanks, usually if I'm slowing it's a relatively small group and I don't want to hurt the DPS. My trick to playing healer in a small group was to also pull, if possible. Pulling with Malo means that even if you get an add, you can usually park the malo'd one before you get back to camp and have it last a while. Pulling also gave me the option of putting my pet on one if it was a difficult pull. Puppy doesn't last long but usually long enough to get a pull under control. Even when it goes well, pulling with Malo means that you'll almost always have the mob slowed by the time you get it back to the group. Even at 65, if I'm duoing some place like Tactics, I still prefer to do it that way.
