Landragoran hit the nail on the head, IMO, with his 3 groups theory. I am in the sort of casual player group (47 days played on 1 character since May 2001). Chikarsis
Nice work on spelling amateur incorrectly while trying to critique someone else's post, Colal.

EQ is a very personal experience and activity, and as such I don't think it's entirely accurate to pigeon-hole people into three categories. From my own experiences, the enjoyment of the game varies as often as the people who play it. Some are end-game and loving it, while others are low-end and hating it. Still others are someplace in between with a somewhat varied sense of accomplishment and enjoyment.

The bottom line has always been as follows: EQ is what you make it to be. If you're trying to win the game, there's a very good chance you're not going to enjoy playing. If you're playing it only to give yourself something more to complain about, you're not going to enjoy playing; and so on and so forth.

Ideally, people are able to distance the events in-game from their own reality. If you're finding you're having a terrible day because guild X ganked boss-mob Y from your guild, you might need to find something else to do for a while.

There are as many variations on the problem and solutions as there are people in EQ. What works well for one happily-adjusted married couple may not work so well for the next.
