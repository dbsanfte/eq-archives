Proposal

Step 1: Identify our Expectations

There are seven player stats; strength, stamina, agility, dexterity, wisdom, intelligence, and charisma. We'll focus (groan!) on our forte -- melee stats. STR/STA/AGI/DEX. Yeah, yeah, shush about CHA and slows.

From the EQ Manual:


Strength: Physical strength; affects how much you can carry, how much melee damage you deliver to a target, and how fast you learn some skills.

Stamina: Health and constitution; affects your maximum number of hit points at each level.

Agility: Physical agility; affects your probability of being hit in combat, how much damage you take per hit, and how fast you learn some skills.

Dexterity: Coordination; affects how much damage you deliver with a bow/thrown weapon, time between attacks, how fast you learn certain skills, and how often certain spells and weapons hit their mark. Also affects some skills and Bard songs.


My understanding of these stats are as follows. Some of this is likely folklore so please correct me where I err. Please have a source to support your observations.

Strength merely raises the maximum hit value. It does not add damage to every hit, only those that land within the increased range of the new maximum. Strength does not increase your chances of hitting your target. Your attack value goes up simply because attack is a representation of two internal values; you potential to hit and your potential to do damage.

Stamina adds hitpoints based on level and class. I took a break and honestly don't know how the new endurance bar works. The old system was STA under 100 would cause fatigue if your stamina bar emptied during combat. Also affects how long you can remain underwater.

Agility increases your armor class (evasive only?). May affect your chance to perform a defense action? Also affects the rate at which you learn defense skills.

Dexterity increases your chance to proc and perform special attacks. Also affects the rate at which you learn offense skills.

Benadan
I do agree with needing a different levels being tested, but to strip gear fighting a blue con I don't feel is necessary since we are interested in changes. I emailed Brenlo about this issue yesterday here is the email.


Brenlo,
I have been giving this some thought, and it seems that a lot of the issues are point of view issues. There are calls for Ryan to take a shaman into the content being complained about on the boards. Doing that would make him no different than an Ebayed character in the content since he would lack the experience gained from learning the class while leveling, this is not intended as a slam just stating a fact. I am sure he does not have the time to do that and get his job done, which leaves him making decisions with only the behind the scenes point of view. Meanwhile people on the boards don't take into account the full scope of Ryan's duties, preventing the consideration he needs to do his job.

The solution to this is finding a way to bring the points of view together in a cooperative mode instead of an adversarial one. With this in mind, I am proposing that we define a protocol for testing and documenting the real issues that need to be addressed, and seperate out those thing that people would like to have from the real problems. What the community needs to know is what information is relevant to the issues we are raising from Ryan's point of view, parses of mob DPS with and without slow, healing ability in a fight, etc. Both sides can sit there all day long and play statistical games, and get no where without the common ground. I don't know what tools you have available in house so I will use add ons to discuss the functionality needed.
Everyone submitting data needs to provide a valid Magelo profile number. This allows the data to be sorted by level, gear, etc.
Parses of log files done with one standard tool for the summations being turned in as well as the complete log of the test. This should allow any invalid data, doctored log files, to be detected and removed from consideration.
Specific scenarios to be tested sewers, EP, tier X, ldon both risk levels, etc.
Detailed documentation of the scenario as tested, group members, classes, levels, Magelo numbers if available, etc.
There are 2 key things that I want from balancing:

Increasing the desirability of the Shaman class, and reducing the amount of time spent LFG. Actively trying to put together groups does not always work.
The ability to significantly contribute to the group I am a part of. As has been pointed out already 2 other classes bring slow to a group, and bring additional significant contribution to the group. What the other contribution is does not matter that much as long as it makes a noticable impact, see point 1. (Stat buffs are not a noticable impact).
If this system works for shaman, it could be expanded to the other classes providing a community based method of ensuring things do not get out of balance, and removing the validity of the agruements presented by those who wish to whine. While it would be nice to be able to silence those who's only goal is to create uproar, I do not see that as an achievable goal.

I feel that if SoE commits to a program of this type, the serious members of the community will actively support and contribute to it. Additionally it would help repair the relations with the community and hopefully begin reversing the hard feelings that exist.

A magelo profile gives us a base with the toon being tested. From there we use the various stat buffs we have gained to incrimentally increase from the base. Our interest in this project is the difference the buffs make after all.

Each data set should be done against a single mob type, and include a say/gsay line with the toons current stats, and a con of the mob before begining the fight. This provides a data header to ensure that all log sections being parsed in a particular run are the same.

My thought is we should start with Warriors, as they are supposed to be the main tank class so would logically see the largest benifit from the buffs. Once a sufficent data pool is collected, we change classes to Pally or SK and repeat with each. Once all three classes have been analyzed we can compare the results of stat buffs on "tank classes" as a group.

Beyond this we may want to do specific testing on DPS classes. Examples being Dex on Rangers and Rogues, STR on Zerkers and Rogues. I think Rogue testing might be a significant source of information as far as the effects of Back Stabbs go. Another insteresting test would be the effects of Dex on casters with the DD crit AAs to see what if any effect it has on the chances to crit.

I've done some analysis of parses made by others in this thread here: http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=21892

Hope it helps a bit.

With regard to your proposed parsing, I think its worth restricting it to "stable" characters in static situations. By that I mean any comparisons should be made with, ideally, only one variable.

For example, if testing strength, then the buffee's other stats and skills must remain unchanged, you should be fighting the same level and type of mob, same buffs etc. This way you can see any changes in damage and know its due to differences in strength (and the associated attack that comes with it).

I'd also surmise that buffs DO have a decent effect at lower levels. Agility is proven to give a big bump in avoidance at 75, a slow increase to ~200, and a big tailing off over 250/280.

Strength has been shown to show no change in max hits from 280-305, but a steady increase below that.

Any parses should examine a wide range of variation in the one thing they are testing. All else should remain equal.
