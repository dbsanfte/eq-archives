Jinheim
I don't play on a pvp server, but I have been a part of a few arena brawls, and have done a lot of dueling. For mass pvp against very resistant people, there just isn't much we can do. We really desperately need a lure dot. It doesn't even need to be a strong dot, we just need something that we can do to hurt people that they cannot resist. In brawls, I just hang towards the back, malos the enemy and throw out heals like mad on my friends. I am sure you pvp people know far more about this than I do, though. One thing I thought of, though. On blue servers at least, there is something strange about the resist rate on our rain spells. They get resisted in PvE more than any other spell we have, but in PvP, they will land at least partially on ANYONE. I tested it against a bard singing resists, who had 500PR. He chain cast mana drain on me while I chain cast rain on him. I got him to 40% before I was oom. I don't know if this works on red servers, and I also do not know if rains will hit people on your own team, so this may or may not be of any consequence. It also kills your pet.

If you want to duel, though, I honesntly believe we can beat just about anyone, regardless of their resists. Anyone know doesn't resist MR, PR, CR, and DR based spells is going to get demolished by me, no ifs ands or buts. Low resists usually goes along with crappy gear, which leads to low HP. Blood of saryrn and chain nuking will drop these people fast. No further discussion needed.

For people with great resists, though, it becomes much more challenging (and fun). By "duel" I mean two people, with nothing but self buffs, square off in the arena and go at it, one vs. one, until one of them is dead or gives up. I realize that such a situation will almost never arise on a pvp server. Even if you did manage to get someone alone, it takes us so damn long to kill someone, they could probably crouch-walk backwards to the nearest zone line, stop to take a piss, and then zone out still alive. The advice I am about to give requires that your opponent wants to fight you.

We have two major advantages over every other class.

The first is that we can heal ourselves. The second is that we regen mana better than anyone else. These two characteristics make us extremely powerful in a long, drawn out duel. Our greatest weakness is that our damage-dealing abilities are pitiful against resistant people, but you all know that. It is practically impossible for us to outburn anyone, so we have to outlast them.

Int casters are, imho, the easiest opponents. They have low HP, cannot heal themselves, and they cannot melee you. If they go oom, you win.

If you have good MR, an enchanter is powerless against you. The worst they can do is dispell your buffs, but you have several instaclicks, right? If you have bad MR, this guide is really not for you. You should dot yourself, though, in order to keep their mezz from sticking. I typically use epic dot since it does insignificant damage for most of its duration. The downside to this is that they can dispell it, but I usually place it at the very end of my bufflist, so there is little danger of that.

Magicians are easy as well. Their pets are not resistant at all, so just sic your pet on it, back up, slow their pet, root it, and turn your pet on the mage. They do not have any lures that I know of, so if you have decent resists, they aren't much of a threat to you with their pet out of action. Just quiescence yourself and beat them down.

Wizards are a bit tougher because they have lures and a manaless rune (epic). They also have harvest, which is sort of like a miniature canni5. It doesn't give them a lot of mana, but it gives them enough for an extra nuke or two. To kill a wizard, you have to heal yourself while they chaincast their lure on you. It hits for 706 I believe in pvp. Every wizard I have ever dueled has gone full tilt nuking me from the start. Just quiescence yourself, and throw in a tnargs when you need it. Don't let your hp get too low, because he can crit very hard, and he will do it often. All you have to do to beat a wizard is outlast him. Once he is oom it is game over for him. While he is nuking you, take the time in between heals to dispell him. Just dispell dispell dispell until you take his rune. Send in your pet(s) on him, and melee him down. If he reapplies the rune, just dispell it. Don't spend your mana trying to damage him, use it to heal yourself.

Necros are hands down the hardest int caster to kill, and probably one of the tougher classes. They typically have more HP than most int casters, they can heal themselves with lifetaps, they have an annoying pet, they have a great PR buff, and most importantly, their mana regen is on par with ours. Start the fight by putting his pet out of commission. Slow it, root it, and either forget about it or just kill it with a few velium strikes (spear of torment doesn't work very well). Most necros will start the fight by lifetapping the living jesus out of you. Just stand there and take it, like vs. wizards. Most necros throw in dots as well, so this is what you have to do: Get three instant clicks. Click all three, then click off the first one. Leave this slot empty. If he dots you, dispell it. If you have to quiescence, click the instant for the first slot, cast q, and click off the instant again. If you screw up, that's why you have radient cure. Eventually he'll start to get low on mana and will slow down. This is your chance. Dispell dispell dispell. You HAVE to take his lich form. If you don't, he will never go truly oom, just like a shaman. By now, his pet should be under control, and your pet should be bashing the necromancer in the face. Let your pet slowly wear him down. You can melee as well, just make sure to dispell his lich whenever he recasts.

That's it for int caster. Other posts to follow.

Jinheim
Other priests are a real pain in the ass, because they can heal themselves. However, they are not particularly dangerous.

Clerics are a giant pain in the ass, because they will bash you over and over again, and their stupid hammer interrupts a lot. On top of that, if they have ornate pants, they get a free hammer pet, which is also very annoying. However, these are the only things a cleric can do to hurt you, assuming you have decent MR. Even if you don't have decent MR, you should be able to outheal their nukes. If they spend all their mana nuking you, they are oom and you win. Most clerics will save it for heals, though. Just get your pet on them and melee them down. Test out their resists and see if anything can land. If it doesn't, just settle in for the long haul, because this is going to take a while. Most duels with clerics do not end in a death. It usually ends in someone saying "f this" and leaving, a raid getting called and me having to leave, or someone falling asleep at the keyboard. The only problem I can really forsee is a cleric that has a VT mace that procs manadrain, and remembers to use it. However I have never run in to this.

A well played druid is a challenging and fun class to duel. Like dueling a necro, you need to set up your first few buffs so that you can dispell their dots. They have a good CR nuke and a decent CR debuff, but you can dispell/RC the debuff, and the nuke takes considerable time to cast, so it isn't too much trouble to just outheal it. When he figures out his nuke won't land, he'll start to use dots. These are unresistable, but can be taken care of with dispell/RC. A druid I dueled had the following items: Epic (dot, resistable sometimes), Mithaniel Marr Scimitar (click unresistable? dot), one of the ES pieces (dot, unresistable), a freaking mistwalker scimitar, and a VT mace that procs mana drain. This is hands down the longest fight I have ever been in. Just shy of an hour, start to finish, even though he was oom 5 minutes in to it. These dots, while not powerful on their own, add up to do some serious damage. He was dotting me almost as fast as I could dispell them. Occasionally I would screw up and get a dot at the end of my bufflist, and just have to eat it. Eventually, though, I didn't have any buffs due to mistakes on my parts and chain dispelling on his. These dots kept me on my toes, and his mace of dark thought kept me lom and him from being completely oom. Spirit Call won this one for me. This, however, was an exceptional druid, in both gear and skill. Normally you can just outlast them.

Other shamans is more of a contest of wills than anything else. As long as you have it in you to say "Hell no, I'm not going to die!" then you won't die. Sooner or later someone breaks.

Rogues are the easiest class to kill. Use a DS potions, quiescence yourself, and back in to a corner. He'll kill himself faster than he kills you. He can't heal himself and he can't pull out a twohander. Rogues are completely helpless against a DS.

Fighting against Warriors and Monks is like fighting a rogue, except harder. Unless they're dumb, they will pull out a big whoopass twohander as soon as they realize you have a DS. It takes a while, but eventually the DS and your pet will do its job, and they will die. If you are getting low on health and their kicks are interrupting your heals, just run away, cast Q, and run around in a circle for a while until you are full health.

Bards, contrary to popular belief, are beatable assuming they do not have an entire zone to kite in. If they can just selos away, regen, and come back, then the fight will go on indefinately, because you will heal yourself as well as replenish your mana while he is running around. Assuming you are fighting somewhere like bazaar arena, though, you are going to have quite a fight on your hands. First off, if he has ancient mezz, you won't win. Just give up, it is impossible. He will just mezz you and manadrain you until you are oom. Dotting yourself won't work because he has cure songs to get rid of it (note, this applies in arena fights. I am not sure if you can cure your opponent if you /duel). Assuming he doesn't have ancient mezz, though, you can win the fight, especially if the bard is dumb. Dumb bards are probably the easiest class to kill. I had one bard mezz my pet and then melee me. While I had a DS. And he didn't sing resist song. Or his heal song. Or cure songs after I hit him with BoS and BoN.

To beat a bard, you are going to have to pay extremely close attention to what songs he is playing. Bards can do everything, but they cannot do everything at once - only 4 things. Most bards will manadrain you until you're oom, then finish you off. If this is the strategy he is using, chain cast both of your rain spells on him, and pray that it kills him before you go oom. If he has a lot more HP than you have mana, it won't work. If the vice versa is true, you just might win. If it doesn't work, don't worry, not all hope is lost. Once you are oom and he is dotting you, this is what you do: Wait until he manadrains you, then immediately cast canni4 twice, then quiescence yourself. Manadrain has a 18? second recast timer, which is just barely enough time to canni enough mana for a Q. You can fight him to a stalemate like this. Also, you can dispell or RC his dots. If he comes in to melee you, you have your DS potion (you do have one on you at all times, right?). Bard melee is rather unimpressive, and like rogues, they can't use twohanders. A combination of melee and full array of dots can be kind of hard to survive when oom, but if you can keep it up he'll eventually kill himself on your DS. Do not melee him, though, because his DS is a lot nastier than yours. Bards are definately the trickiest class to fight. If you are a great dueler, though, you can beat a bard who isn't quite up to your skill level. If he is as good as or better than you, though you will more likely than not lose.

It is a very simple strategy (albeit difficult to execute) for beating rangers: Don't let him bow you and instead force him to kill himself on your DS. There is a very fine line you can walk that is just inside his archery range, and just outside his melee range. If you watch what weapon he is holding in his hands, you can see what he is trying to do and adjust accordingly. Let him melee you and let your pet wear him down. He most likely outdamages quiescence, so when you start to get low, apply a fresh Q on yourself and kite him in the manner I described above. It can be tricky to do, but if you have played any FPS games or just have a naturally good ability to move precisely in a 3D environment, it shouldn't be too hard. Took me about 30 seconds to master. This can take a while because rangers can heal themselves, and good ones will pull out a twohander. Not only is a ranger with punishing blade AA and a nice twohander a beast for damagedealing, the twohander also means that your DS potions aren't doing much. They are, however, doing something and eventually you will win.

Beastlords. This is the class I have the least experience with. They aren't particularly dangerous, but their pet is a giant pain in the ass. Their pet is the only pet that draws its resists from the master. This means that if you have a hard time slowing the beastlord, you're going to have a hard time slowing the pet. The pet doesn't do too much damage really, but the biggest problem is that it quads and bashes you in the face constantly. This makes it damn near impossible to channel a spell through one. Against players, you can usually run past them and it will take them a second or two to turn around and catch you, giving you time to cast something, but this won't work on pets. They track you flawlessly, which means that you cannot get away from the bashing for long enough to cast a spell unmolested. You have to channel, and if you just get an unlucky string of interrupts, it can be lights out for you. This is the biggest problem I have with beastlords. If you can manage to slow the pet, you have pretty much won. Beastlord melee is not impressive at all, and their spells are all easy to resist. Just let your DS do its work and your pet wear him down.

Paladins and Shadowknights are very similar, so I will group them together. In short, an uber knight is the most dangerous opponent you can find. They use twohanders almost exclusively, and almost all high end knight weapons proc either a heal or a rune, or some other nastiness. The twohander means that your DS won't be doing much damage, and the insane amounts of worn regen on their EP+ armor means that almost all of that damage is going to be negated. On top of that, many knights also gear themselves to have considerable FT. This means they won't be going oom for a long time, if ever. Even if they do go oom, it doesn't make much of a difference. They will still be able to outdamage anything you can throw at them, just by melee alone. If they aren't oom, expect the shadowknight to be reaming you a new one with his nukes and lifetaps. The paladin can also heal himself indefinately with both direct heals and a decent HoT. Hopefully you have the MR to resist his 10 second stun. If you don't, you're royally screwed. On top of all these things, they also get damned twohanded bash, the most annoying AA of all time. I literally cannot channel a damn thing through a bashing knight. I have had strings of 15+ interrupts on a 2.8 second tnargs. All you can really do is try your typical strategy of healing yourself and praying you outlast them.

Basically, a knight with EP gear and a ton of AA is a monster. I have only had my ass truly and thoroughly kicked once, and it was by a beast of a shadowknight (he soloes gorenaire =\ ).

Their rune is more manaefficient than our nukes/rains. Potions cure our dots should they ever land. The rune keeps them from being interupted. Insta AA rune for emergencies. The enchanter drains our mana faster than we do theirs.

Their pet wins over ours since the chanter has runes for it and because chanter pets are (at least on red servers) still very bugged. In tests I have run the enchanter pet has resisted two full manabars of dots after debuffs. All nukes but one were resisted. A wizard had lures resisted. It took a lifeburn and HT to bring down the pet. Our best bet in taking out their pet is using melee and damage shield. Both which the enchanter can counter using rune and dispells.

Chanter pvp may not look very powerful, but in essence they have every tool they need to counter any and all tricks we can pull off. Automatic cast interupts using mez, pro-active healing using runes, best manadrain, best dispells, pet and nukes for finishing off. The nukes may not be top notch, but they dont need to be. The chanter wins the mana game, thus their nukes can be as inefficient as you like. They still win.

Mage:
Mostly agree with you here. Once their pet is slowed all you have to do is outheal their nukes, should they ever land, and stay wary for their swarmpets. The mage cannot debuff more than you and they cannot cure their pets slow (unlike beastlords for example).

I usually slow the mage pet, let him use his swarmpets and after that is done I'm in control. Nothing he can do we cannot counter. Heals take care of nukes, slow takes care of his pet (blind/root usually dispelled by a competent mage), annul takes care of mala. Then I stick my swarmpets on the mage to keep him busy, and while he is kiting those I kill off his pet quickly. After that it's just me and the mage. I melee better, I regen mana and hps better. The mage has nothing to counter. Pretty easy kill. We win.

Wizard:
General strategy as you point out is to go very defensive with the spellcasting. More so than against others. But by all means stick to him with the melee all the time. It's free damage for us.

However as pointed out already, their Xcul pet is a threat once they start laying on some serious nuke damage and you have to channel those heals.

Our swarmpets and normal pet is usually the tideturner here. Versus an elemental geared wizards ac our pets will still chew through his best rune and do some solid damage unless he kites them. Which buys us the time to recoup hps and regen mana.

In the end we should win by superior mana/hp regen and our melee. Surprise.

Necro:
In pvp you can usually work on the assumption that offensive dispelling is a waste of time and mana. People have 2+ instaclicks and know how to use them. You will not be able to strip his liche. Enchanters and those int casters with the Aten bracer can usually clean you out on buffs though.

The positive thing with necros is that malos overwrites dmf. I know alot of necros who arent aware of this, and they will fall prey to our nukes/dots because they never put much priority on their unbuffed poison resist.

To counter some of your arguments. At 250mr you will start resisting necro lifetaps pretty regularly. The -250 resist modifier is not working in the same way other spells do. A smart necro will use your pet to heal himself on (or use eye of zomm), thus a smart shaman wont use a regular pet against a necro. Swarm pets are still good.

Killing a necro is very much like killing a mage, only its alot slower since he will still land some of his lifetaps, and he also has two invulns. Just remember that while the lifetaps are pretty scary stuff, as long as the necro keeps on using them (450mana) you should win. You are more manaefficient. Generally just staying out of range of the necro as much as possible will prevent him from using liche to its full extent.

Something to watch out for is wake the dead. When used on the corpse of a wizard (heavy lures), chanter (nukes, tash--> his lifetaps will start hitting, mez, charm) or shadowknight (harmtouch) this ability can be lethal.

Should be a win, but very tricky.

Clerics:
Agreed. With the exception of shaman vs shaman this is the ultimate valium.

In the end it will be your pet, your superior manaregen and better melee capability that wins. Swift like the wind and avatar works wonders. If one person has ToT hammer the balance strongly tips in that persons favour.

Don't underestimate cleric nukes. Sometimes they will land for full and will crit. Don't fall below 50% hps thinking this is a pushover fight.

Also, turn on damage shield reports to make sure you are not beating yourself to death on kilvas skin of flame and the chardok ring (total 31+25 pt dmg shield).

Boring but pretty safe win.

Druid:
A druids dots are not unresistable. At 200mr you will start to resist quite a few of them. They have a -100mr modifier.

Should they land at the top of your bufflist, by all means dispell if you have the time. Otherwise a quiescence will outdo them.

Dispell ro's fiery sundering. It debuffs your fireresist and can lead to some pretty heavy nukage hitting you. While we loose mana relative to the druid by dispelling it, we regen mana faster so this is ok.

Fighting a druid is like fighting a gimp wizard/cleric. Preserve your mana and go mostly defensive. When you pop swarmpets, try to wedge into his defenses by going full offensive. If that doesnt work, back to defensive and recoup mana.

Our swarmpets and regular pet is our ace versus a druid. However, in the end its the manabattle all over again. Who has the ToT mace and who has better manaregen.

An annoying item is the druid PoGrowth gauntlets that casts a damage shield. Can be countered by pulling out the good ole JBB and laughing at the druid. Mostly a joke, but can be done.

I've had fights with druids where neither of us could use spells to kill the other. Swarmpets were just kited out of range of me. I could not use melee to kill the druid because of their damage shield. It ended in both of us just standing there looking dumb.

We should win most druid fights based on superior manaregen, but no safe bet.
