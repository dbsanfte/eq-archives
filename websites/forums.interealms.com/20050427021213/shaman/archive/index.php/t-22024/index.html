Droga is a fun spot for me as well. I take my rogue down there to duo a lot. He is currently 56, and he just rips through those mobs like warm butter.

You might want to try the flies in PoD. From ZI, go left and follow the lake until you get to a rock. Straight ahead of the rock are the flies - they are low level, 46/47-ish. You have to be careful because sometimes rats/flies hide in the water and may pull adds. The xp will be crap, but it will give you a chance to practice.

Also, the exit in PoJ is another nice spot for you as someone suggested. You can pull the mobs from the cells - these are 46/47-ish as well if I recall. There are also rats and guards that wander. Xp will be slow here.

The yard trash in PoN may give you problems since they can add on you. Plus, they tend to hit hard here - depending on your gear of course.

PoI is decent. Just start on one side and work your way around. It is a circle, and you will come back to where you started. I suggest you do not camp ZI/ZO since people have the tendency to train it.

Personally, I would try to group, with friends if possible, and see how you do. Soloing, to me, is vastly different than being in a group. Try a HoH or BoT group. For the most part, all you will be doing is slowing, with the occassional healing - granted, depends on what type of group you have.

Dassi, I commend you for asking for guidance. You can learn alot about the Shaman class by soloing, but soloing and grouping are vastly different. There are alot of posts on this board on how to group/solo as a Shaman. Also there have been many posts in this thread about places to solo. Make sure you check them out. One major difference between Soloing and Grouping that will upset the group cohesion quicker then anthing: Aggro Management.

You said you have experience playing other classes, but Slow is an aggro king. Debuff, slow, and a few DoTs and we can easily keep aggro from just about any other class through an entire fight (or until we get "Loading please wait..."). I won't say I'm an expert by any means. But grouping or maybe duoing is about the only way to learn aggro management. Now don't get me wrong with a very good tank, especially a Paladin, you can drop a slow and DoT on incomming and the Mob won't even jerk in your direction. But most of the time you will need to wait at least a few seconds to cast slow, usually the time it takes you to debuff. Either way, to avoid any hassle and make it easier for the tank to regain aggro, make sure you stand next to the tank when you slow. Again the only way to learn this is to group.

There are alot more aspects to playing a shaman, but IMHO the quickest way to not get an invite back to a group is not be able to control your trigger finger. Do your research, explain to the group ahead of time that you are learning, and have fun. And before you know it you will be playing your shaman as a seasoned pro. Good luck.
