Korelen
If you are looking into a raiding guild, the AAs I would recommend you work on are as follows:

This list is in no particular order.

1. Healing: Healing Adept, Advanced Healing Adept, Healing Gift, and Advanced Healing Gift. I tend to work on the Adept line first because you see the return on your investment immediately.

Reason: On raids, do not be surprised if you are required to patch heal. Once debuffs are done, I usually go into healer mode. A lot of times I am the main healer in my raid group, and it helps to have these AAs to extend or enhance our weak fast heals.

2. RC: I would work towards purchasing RC3

Reason: RC3 is a handy tool to have. I use it constantly in a raid or xp setting.

3. Defense: Lightning Reflexes, Innate Defense

Reason: Survivability. LR line will help your avoidance. In the early stages, you will not see a huge return if you primarily xp in PoP zones, but once you have LR 5, wow, like night and day - my opinion of course. ID line helps with melee damage mitigation.

4. Planer Power: Depending on how your attributes are, you might want to finish this. As you earn better gear, you will most likely want to re-visit this AA.

5. Resists: I think a lot of people overlook resists. Higher resists mean higher survivability. Work on these once your core ( defense and healing ) aa's are completed.

6. VP: VP is nice for crunch cc'ing or soloing. I tend to use this AA a lot.

7. Quick Buff: Depending if you have spell haste, some people only get 2 ranks of this. (I got all three ranks eventhough I had a spell haste item ) This AA helps reduce the cast time our Torpor line of spells as well as other benefical spells. Max rank is 50% spell haste for benefical spells.
Samanna
7. Quick Buff: Depending if you have spell haste, some people only get 2 ranks of this. (I got all three ranks eventhough I had a spell haste item ) This AA helps reduce the cast time our Torpor line of spells as well as other benefical spells. Max rank is 50% spell haste for benefical spells.
Clarification: Quick Buff only works on 'buffs', not all beneficial spells. Any spell that doesn't grant a buff icon (spot heal, gate) will not benefit - but they will benefit from a Spell Haste effect. This is why most people shoot for at least Spell Haste IV (15% to all beneficial spells) and QuickBuff 2 (25% to all buffs), for a combined effect of 45% to buffs and 15% to non-buff beneficial.

And spell haste is capped at 50% for any spells, and only applies to spells with base cast time of 3 seconds or more.
