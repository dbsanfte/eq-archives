Melee beastlord (pet, slows, autoattack, buffs, pet heals)
Shaman ( slows, pet, heals, dots, nukes, canni is a big attention grabber)
Enchanter ( cc is a tireless job, especially when they are only slower in group)
Bard (everything except heals and evacs)
What you really need to decide is how comfortable you are with the classes you play, how effecient you want them to be and how much work ya willing to put into your mini group. I found 4 boxing to be alot of fun, but a ton of work also. I find douing to be almost boring, and playing 1 toon unless I'm kiting is downright sleep inducing. Almost any class can be boxed well, but almost all are better off being played as the only toon.
When deciding what classes to box you need to ask yourself a few questions:
What do I want to accomplish?
how many toons am I willing to play?
What roles do I need to cover?
What classes do I already have available?
What gear will I need to help me accomplish my goals?
Is anyone going to be joining me to form a more well rounded group?
What are my play times and how many friends can I count on to help fill roles?
Once all these questions are answered then you can figure out for yourself what classes you need for the ideal box. Don't overlap abilities to much, especially if it means ignoring ignoring other areas. Groups need a few things to be successful: healing, dps, debuffs, buffs crowd control. IF any of these is really dominate it can counteract a lack in the other areas, especially healing and dps. If the mob dies fast or you can keep everyone healed all day debuffs, buffs and crowd control are meaningless. The old manaburn teams are perfect examples of this, mobs would die so fast that the fact they couldn't heal or cc for crap didn't matter.

If you want to worry about having to do things to acquire aggro, then the SK isn't an inferior choice to a warrior -- just the opposite.

Once a SK puts clinging darkness and/or fear and/or some of his other buffs(I really like the SK's AC buffs and ATK buffs) on a few times, it's not that easy to get aggro off him. BlargX
Mintyfresh, I have to disagree on your ideal combo. Not to put it down or be mean, just that I don't think it's ideal at all.

The stuns from the paladin are very nice, and can lead to taking less damage on the part of both the shaman(when slow gets aggro, which can happen even with a pally/SK tanking) and the tank himself. But when you talk about the puller being the pally or the shaman, you have to let both step aside. (Frankly, the wizard's snare makes him not a bad puller sometimes in comparison -- but more on snare pulls in a sec...)

I pull all the time as a shaman, because malos lets me get a mob to camp malosed and often slowed too before it even gets there. It's a lot of fun and often very effective. Sometimes I can even get root to land before I get hit, too, presenting a group a mob for whenever they're in the mood to get around to it. But for the tricky pulls, the FD spell really, really shines.

Both a warrior and a pally basically have to run if you get an overpull and hope not to die. If you two box like I do on the same computer, as soon as one toon zones, you have to worry about doing it with the other one -- which is tricky on a slowish system because of the instant lag that zoning creates. But even on a lightning fast computer (or multiple networked computers) and a great connection, it's just not that safe to run to a zone compared to just feigning death on a bad pull. Feign death is easily one of the best spells in the game.

If I have to run a toon out past my shaman to the zoneline(I've two-boxed druid/monk, druid/SK, druid/warrior, and shaman/with all of those), I have aggro on not one toon but two of them. If there are multiples, sometimes one gets far enough behind to start pounding on my shaman while the other is pounding on my tank who's running ahead to the zoneline - ouch! Now I've got two unslowed mobs on two different toons I'm trying to run out at the same time -- not really possible.

With my SK, I do what I loved doing so much with my monk before the monk nerf -- FD and try again. FD just lets you try so many scary things and get away with it scot free. With a tank who can't do it, you run, or die, and hope you don't endanger your other toon in the process. No matter how competent you are, it can easily be a problem.

I was duoing the metal suited guys by the zone in in POV the other day, working on my Aid Grimel tradeskill earring. I needed to kill the suited guys for their uncommon no-drop, sacred waters. It was great doing it with my SK. I pulled with snare in some places(bioluminescent orb in the tented village, and then snare after) and kept casting snare till it stuck. If I got multiples, no problem -- nobody died and nobody had to zone(and waste a ton of time and have to recast pets); I would just FD.

Well anyway, through FD, my pull got down to a single. Pallies can pacify, but they can't do what my SK can do for me in a duo. Snaring the mob allowed the SK to run it past my shaman, who could then malos it. Another run-by allowed for slow. By that time the aggro was so high on the SK that there was almost never a problem. The SK cast his AC buffs and ATK buffs as the mob was incoming and got ready to cast his lovely zeevfer's (sp?) bite spell, which adds both HP and, especially lovely, mana to the group, starting to regen my shaman's mana right away.

You have an enormous amount of control with an SK doing this kind of thing. I loved my monk, and his FD was king of the world -- better than an SK's, in fact. But... he didn't have snare. Mobs came in full bore when he pulled them, and malos/slow was much more of a dangerous situation, with my monk's hitpoints plummeting all the time...and them slow yanking the mob right off the monk. SK's combo of both snare and FD makes them an extremely potent puller in many cases.

Your third being a wizard -- excellent choice, can't argue with the DPS. But...I'd still rather have the charming ability of a druid as an option for vastly more DPS, and for the other things he can do. Against really tough mobs, or when slow just doesn't seem to stick, shaman heals can either be too late or too inadequate. Druid heals are far more potent, and having HOT's and slows from the shaman plus REAL heals from the druid makes for a very potent healing combo in a trio. A druid also adds in significant DPS that, like a wizard's, can be readily hotkeyed.

Additionally, a druid, unlike a wizard, can give your team 9's, a very potent buff that helps add not just hitpoints but mana regen too. Like the wizard, he can evac, which is more handy than a lot of people like to give wizard/druids credit for, but he can also land a superior snare, add a damage shield, cast a manaless regen spell with the right equipment, provide superior fire/cold buffs, damage shield, a nice ATK boost with his wolf form...that's an awful lot of utility.

Whereas the wizard in comparison provides superior DPS but ONLY DPS, the druid provides quite good DPS and excellent healing, as well as very useful buffs. And if he gets a pet, he's very superior DPS indeed. I've seen a druid/chanter combo mow through mobs incredibly quickly, far faster than a very good regular group could hope to do.

Anyway, while paladins provide stuns and a buff or two(their rez is nice, but usually I can find a cleric rez anyway), and wizards provide great DPS, the FD and snare and mana/hp "sort of" regen abilities of a SK, and the enormous overall utility on both the DPS and healer/buffer side of a druid make them, at the very least, easily the equal of a paladin/wizzy addition to a shaman.
