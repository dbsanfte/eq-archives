Nektar
Ok, I've duo'ed Cliff golems with another oracle, I know they can be done solo. Is it worthed? No, and honestly it's best to get a few partners.

But yes, they are soloable (at 60 with malo and torpor however), and you can easily split dragoon adds, from a cliff golem. The method I would use is root splitting. I use it all the time in AiW4 in PoM while splitting Breen and Grenn4 because there's lots of space to kite in. One plus though is the low MR on most mobs in Mischief, save maybe white stallion and Lithiniath. Hell, even Bristlebane himself has less resists than golems.

But while I was duo'ind cliff golems with this other oracle, dragoon was never much of a problem because he had low resists. Don't cause any damage to him, and just keep him rooted, he won't summon. In the meanwhile, you can just kite the golem around and just pray a malosini or turgurs lands, and again, don't cause any damage to him before slow actually hits him.

Now I do realize I've been talking about duo'ing them, but if you wanna solo them, there's one pretty safe way in doing so. Bind in the same zone, but far enough away so that when you gate, you're too far away for them to chase. That's the first step, and then you have to wait till both the dragoon and cliff golem return to their spot to ditch aggro. That or you can directly zone. This is a safe measure in case you've been unlucky landing like 20 slows the first time around, and then when you gate, you can basically regen mana full, and try again.

Ok, now say you just wanted to pull the cliff golem single, make sure to root the dragoon, and run far far away with golem on your tail. OT is an outdoor zone, plenty of space to kite in without risking high level adds, so you can run the golem far enough away so that the dragoon will ditch aggro once his root wears off. Then when you finally manage to land a slow on the cliff golem, it's smooth sailing, and if you're after them for the challenge and/or spell drops, I'd say go for it. But not doable at level 56 imo, and I'd strongly recommend that after malo, you first try to land malosini to further increase your chances of not only landing your first slow, but also the subsequent ones.
