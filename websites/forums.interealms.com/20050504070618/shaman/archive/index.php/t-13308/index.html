The fact is, that PoP is all about large guild action. Verant was finding that the content theat they had before (NToV, Ssra, VP, VT) wasn't challenging large guilds that had raiding down to a science. Because of that, they had to make a decision: do we create content for large raids or do we create content that will be gimp for FoH, Afterlife, etc...

They came down on the side of the large guilds. To make sure that the large guilds didn't have to "go back" and redo key raids for one or two people, they created the alternate quests. These quests bypass the main content, in favor of allowing the person to rejoin their friends at the higher level.

Verant has defined the endgame as large guild raids with difficult fights. If you define it differently, then they're not creating the content with you in mind. Larger raids take more discipline, more planning and more time. If you don't want to grow or aly yourself with other like-minded guilds to create large raids, then you're right, you've gone as far in PoP as you're gonna.

In your example, if someone missed the MB fight and got a tactics flag through the Diaku quest, that would be great to a certain point. The person with the flag via quest could still participate in all tactics events (killing TZ, VZ, RZ) but wouldn't be able to progress into the Elemental planes without it.

What assisted large guilds was the % rule for raids. A number of flagged individuals in a raid will allow a small percentage of non flagged members to zone in and join them. This is a huge help and allows guild members to receive flags out of order. For instance, I got my Agnarr flag before I got my BoT flag.

A large guild would still have to go back and reflag eventually. Someone attempting to zone into the Elemental planes must have every pre-req flag from the Hedge quest to Bertoxx to the ogre trial in HoH. The percentage rule does not apply to the elemental planes.
