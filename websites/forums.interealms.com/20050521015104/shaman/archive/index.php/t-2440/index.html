There are two earring rewards, one is a druid earring that non druids turn in to get THIS:

Earring of the Solstice: AC 5, STA 5, WIS 8, INT 8, HP 55, mana 55, sv fire 5, sv cold 5, sv magic 5, recommended level 51, flowing thought 1, tiny, wt 0.1, ALL/ALL, LORE, NO DROP

There is a full write up of the quest on eqtraders:
http://www.eqtraders.com/quests/potc_intro.htm

It requires 9 gifts...and the services of a grandmaster brewer, baker, potter, fisher, smith, JC and tailor. My guild did a divide and conquer approach, each person assigned a task and gathering all the stuff we needed in the Bazaar for combines. The quest looks overwhelming, but I was amazed at how quickly we could gather ingredients.

Each gift is droppable, you could technically purchase all 9 gifts, combine them in a forge (no fail combination) and turn in the druid earring for the non druid earring.

The biggest advice I could give is, buy in bulk. Scent of Marr can be purchased in EC and is needed to make celestial essence (celestial solvent purchased in Bazaar + high level research component, combined in a mortar/pestle, no fail combination). Being able to buy an endless supply of Scent of Marr is a huge plus!

Foraging is another big one. Get some druids/rangers into POG to forage the planks of oak, get some folks out to the Karanas to get cabbages and Plains pebbles. Get everyone with no trade skill ability farming iron oxides, lotus sap from FG, spider silks and spiderling silks. Get someone to Freeport to buy tons of elven wine and mistletoe.

Oh and emeralds, stacks and stacks of imbued emeralds :)

1st Gift of Elves - Demi-Sec Champagne
A 250 brewer has a 95% success rate. Cost of materials per champagne is about 35pp

2nd Gift of Elves - Mistletoe Cutting Sickle
A smith with effective 255 skill seems to succeed 1 in 3 (based on 24 combines, 8 successes). Often 1 subcombine failure in the attempts. This works out to a raw cost in materials of about 1200pp for a success.

3rd Gift of Elves - Golden Idol of Tunare
Trivial at 242 pottery skill, success rate at 250 pottery a little better than 50%. Cost per attempt about 225pp, but be prepared to provide 3 sets of components, or just pay the potter 2-4K for a finished idol (market price depends on server). 200 skill potters have about 15% success rate.

1st Gift of Man - Blessed Fishing Pole
Raw cost per attempt is about 175pp. Not a lot of data on this. Guessing about 75% success rate for a 200 skill fisher.

2nd Gift of Man - Star Ruby Stein
Raw cost per attempt is about 75pp. At 250 pottery skill success rate is about 50%. Be prepared with 3 sets of components or just pay the potter 1-4K for a finished stein (market price depends on the server). 200 skill potters have about 10% success rate.

3rd Gift of Man - Ceremonial Solstice Robe
250 JC'er has been consistently 50% on the Gem Studded Chain with a geerlock, through 32 attempts. So count on raw average cost of about 510pp for the chain. Since the chain is returned on failure of the robe, it's about 40pp per robe attempt. Could get really expensive since there are not a lot of 250 tailors out there. Guessing about 1K average for a 200 tailor. At 181 tailoring I failed 20 combines and gave up. I've heard of a 232 tailor charging 1K for success if you bring components.

1st Gift of Halflings - Lotus Pie
Raw cost per attempt is low (50pp?). I've seen people selling finished Pies for 500-1K, seems to be standard on our server.

2nd Gift of Halflings - Misty Thicket Picnic
Raw cost per attempt a few pp (approx). I've seen finished ones sell for 500pp-1K.

3rd Gift of Halflings - Lucky Cabbage
Raw cost per attempt < 10pp.

If your guild doesn't have a few grandmasters (pottery, brewing, JC, smithing, tailoring) you're going to find it a lot harder to do it all in house.

Make some celestial essences (scent of marr + celestial solvent) both are buyable and the combination is no fail. See if a high level tailor/smith needs you to do some low level stuff for them in exchange for their help on combines.

It's pretty easy to get brewing to 100 or so, and after that you can work to create mistletoe tempers which you could trade to a smith for their services. Grand master smiths want to make lots of sickles, you could help out by doing some of the "scut" work for them.

Work on getting your gem studded chain made, you only need one of these!

Nerun,

Did you describe the stats to your guild? :)

If your guild doesn't have a few grandmasters (pottery, brewing, JC, smithing, tailoring) you're going to find it a lot harder to do it all in house.

If your guild isn't willing (or able) to help you, you can start gathering all the pieces for your earring and work with grandmasters on your server who will probably attempts combines for you for a fee.

I know the quest looks overwhelming, but just start on a few pieces. I started on the champagne and with the help of a 250 jeweler and a 180 brewer, I had 5 champagnes (one for each of us and two spare for my guild) in about an hour and a half.

Make some celestial essences (scent of marr + celestial solvent) both are buyable and the combination is no fail. See if a high level tailor/smith needs you to do some low level stuff for them in exchange for their help on combines.

It's pretty easy to get brewing to 100 or so, and after that you can work to create mistletoe tempers which you could trade to a smith for their services. Grand master smiths want to make lots of sickles, you could help out by doing some of the "scut" work for them.

Work on getting your gem studded chain made, you only need one of these!

Yup, that's the plan... just farming for cash to get started :p
