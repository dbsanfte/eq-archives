This comparison is meant to be applicable to the question, "If I had to provide dps for an LDoN adventure, which one should I use, velium strike or bane?" Info all comes from Lucy and personal experiences.

bane
420x5-6 ticks=2100 or 2520 damage
490 mana
5.5 casting time
24 recast time
4.3 or 5.1 damage per mana
71 or 85 dps (dividing by casting+recast time)

velium strike
925 damage
300 mana
7.8 casting time
2.25 recast time
3.1 damage per mana
92 dps (dividing by casting+recast time)

bane with burning affliction IV (1-20% increase per tick)
2121-3024 damage
490 mana
4.3-6.2 damage per mana
72-103 dps

velium strike with Anger of E'ci (1-23% increase)
934-1110 damage
300 mana
3.1-3.7 damage per mana
93-110 dps

velium strike with Anger + fury of magic 3 (13% chance of crit?)
(note: in my experience, a crit with anger is double damage + 1-23% of half that double damage)
934-1110 damage 87% of the time, 1859-2035 damage 13% of the time
1054-1230 damage by weighted average
300 mana
3.5-4.1 damage per mana by weighted average
105-122 dps

bane pros
Once it lands, the damage is guaranteed full
You can do other stuff while it's dotting
More efficient than velium strike
bane cons
Once it's landed, the mob can't be mezzed for 24 seconds
Can be fully resisted
Agro
Not practical when mob is half dead
Since recast time is so long, can't really use it to rootrot other mobs in addition to one you're fighting
Requires a Burning Affliction IV (really hard to get) item and Canni 5 (lots of AAs) to be competitive

velium strike pros
Cold resist is sexy
Can be partially resisted so that spell isn't completely wasted
Useful regardless of mob health (unlike Bane, where it's a waste to cast if a mob is at 10% health)
Less agro than Bane (I think-- but if you keep doing it you may get agro)
More dps than bane
A shaman can generally keep up with velium strike castings with Canni 5 and Canni 4
No real worries about mezzing and switching targets and stuff
velium strike cons
Again, can be partially resisted
Can't really do anything else while nuking over and over
Requires an Anger of E'ci item (not so hard to get) and Fury of Magic 3 (30 AAs, what a pain) and Canni 5 (more pain) to be competitive

8) C * H * H * 3
9) C * C * H * 3
10) C * C * C

This was just something I was thinking about, but regarding the SoT Vs. VS discussion, assume that you had 30 seconds in which to do your DPS each fight:

Velim Strike
7.8 + 2.25 = 10.05 seconds per velium strike (except the last one, obviously, which will not include the 2.25 second recast time)

So, you could do 3 casts in ~28 seconds. That would cost you 900 mana. That would do 2775 damage.

Spear Of Torment:
4.2 + 2.25 = 6.45 seconds per spear of torment (except the last one, obviously, which will not include the 2.25 second recast time).

So, you could do 5 casts in 30 seconds. That would cost you 1375 mana. You would do 3375 damage.

Looking at this, the question seems to be "do you want to do your damage efficiently or do you want to do the most possible damage in the same amount of time?"

This is all assuming no focus items and no crits. Using spear of torment, you will have more chances to crit, but for a lesser amount.

I'm not the strongest math-wise, so I'm not going to figure out what the numbers would be if you had a focus item, or if you were to crit once during those 30 seconds.

I guess what I'm saying is that both spells have something to offer, and your preference is largely going to depend onwhat you want from the spell (unless I completely messed up the math and am now being laughed at and ridiculed by many).
