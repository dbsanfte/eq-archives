Yedak: The answer to your question is that in general at your level buffs take a back seat to pulling and healing.

If you are grouping with a monk duo partner of the same level, cast your str/dex/hps on him before the first pull, and then stick in the other buffs and rebuffs during pulls. Make sure you use either group versions, or cast on yourself at the same time to time rebuffing. It is NO BIG DEAL if the monk heads out to pull another mob without STA or AGil..each buff gives you an 'edge', none in particular is a killer if it drops (unless it's enduring breath).

Regen is important at your level...far more than at higher levels.

If you're grouping with 5 others, your buffs at this level are going to be a major mana-drain. Pick your buff targets carefully. If the wizard cries for a sta buff give it to him..once. Then simply forget about him unless you're overflowing with mana. Most people you group with (like my level 23 SK) are twinked out the wazoo, and couldn't care less about a few extra stats. My SK would need a shammy for healing, regen, haste and maybe some HP's. Anything else would be a waste. Your slows aren't really that helpful yet, and I don't really remember how you'd do at crowd control at level 22.

So the answers in brief are: Stagger your buffs out when trying to fully-buff a duo partner monk, and keep buffing managed carefully in full groups to maximize mana usage vs. utility. When you get higher levels of Canni you can try to accomodate more people at once.
