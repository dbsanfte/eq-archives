Landragoran, while I appreciate that there are always multiple ways to win any battle, I also feel that usually 1 way is preferred. And in the case of charming, charm is ALWAYS preferred in high dps, low hitpoint battles. Weaponshield, etc.. are abilities that are on timers. A charmed pet is not.

As for charm being a "core" ability.. well.. I don't see it that way. Charm has become more and more valuable as groups encounter these high dps mobs. Its a "kill them before they kill you" type game in most big encounters. I can't ever recall having a charmed pet in any group, in any zone, prior to pop (disclaimer: prior to pop, I can't recall having a charmed pet in a group that I felt was 10x the power of the group and felt it was making the battle trival anyways). Its an ability that has become a core ability. Like, if all of a sudden an entire zone opened up where the mobs could only be hurt by disease:) That would instantly make disease dotting classes invaluable.

Charm has become more valuable because of the design of the encounters.

I don't think we should nerf charm honestly. There is risk involved (albeit very minimal risk once you know what you are doing), and there are some (few) situations where it is not viable.

I do, however, think that, like wake the dead, there needs to be some limit on how much dps the pet can dish out. In addition, other competing classes (namely us:), need some boost to be a desirable dps additon to a group. I've posted before that our buffs need to add significant dps to a group (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=20638&highlight=ungkor). If an enchanter can add 500 dps to a group via a pet, can I get a series of buffs that adds 50 dps per player.. pretty please hehe.

The most forgiving parses seem to show that FA adds about 10-20% dmg to a player (if they aren't capped, etc..). Push that number to a 40% increase in group dmg via buffs, up our healing a bit, and I'll be a happy camper.

Then a group could decide "well, we can take a shaman for backup healing and a 40% damage increase, OR, take the slightly risker route and get a 200% dmg increase with an enchanter". Its not that I want charm nerfed, its that no one class can provide anything remotely equivalent to its power, and thats a problem.

Charm is kinda like wizards getting a 7500 hp, 5 second cast nuke, but it has a 5% chance to kill the user.

Enc: 70% slow, 70% haste, mana regen, crowd control, and 500+ dps
or
Shm: 75% slow, 60% haste, 500 hp buff, minor healing, and about 50 dps

Thats pretty much what stat maxed groups have to decide between right now. Its not much of decision.

Now, add slow mitgation, or immunity into the mix, and we all agree, the situation gets even worse.

But back to the original topic. I'm just not sure why shaman are crying out about charm. Since pop, its always been that way. Charm is a tremendous benefit to an entire group, but it does make all dps classes, especially dotting classes, feel insignificant.

In summary, I completely agree with this:
"Let's think of ways to bring the Shaman class up rather than other classes down"

Leave charm alone, but then give the classes that compete for that slower spot (bst and shaman alike), something to add to a group that approaches the usefulness of charm dps power.
