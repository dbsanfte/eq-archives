Champion, better haste, and 100 dps per melee in a group + 100 dps for my pet = > beastlord by far.

Take a typical group for me.
1. Cleric/Druid
2. Shaman
3. Monk/Bard/Ranger
4. Necro/Mage
5. War/Pally/Sk

Add our group Champion, add our 7% damage avoidance. Add our 60hp per tick regen. Add our attack sow. Add our 5% damage mod buff.

Add our ability to root and heal ourselves. Add that we have the best mana regen of any class save necro's, and many ways to use it, and you still have a very strong class.

Between my epic hot, my group aa hot, my single target hots, and my fast heals, I can stack a hell of a lot of healing power on the group as is.

The biggest problem remains our blast heal. When the crap hits the fan, and we haven't had a chance to stack hots, our blast heal is still inadequate against upper end content.

Our second biggest problem is the cast time, and especially the recast time on malos. If the shaman is fighting resistant mobs, and mutiple are in camp, the enchanter begins to out class us. There's no point being able to heal if your heals can't keep up with 2-3 mobs unslowed dps, and you don't have the time to slow them because A) you're being beat on, B) the tank is near death, or C) malos won't refresh fast enough to get them slowed.

Lets look at the three slowing classes in terms of what they bring to a group of mixed melee and casters.

Healing: Shaman > Beast > Enchanter
CC: Enchanter > Shaman > Beast
DPS: Shaman > Beast > Enchanter
Buffs: Shaman > Enchanter = Beast
Debuffs: Shaman > Enchanter > Beast

Thats how I see it.

Healing: Of course the shaman is a better healer than enc or bst.
CC: Of course the Enchanter is a better CC than shm or bst.
DPS: Some may argue.. but between our buffs and Panther, I'm willing to bet we bring more overall dps to a group than a beastlord does.
Buffs: Duh.
Debuffs: Slow is the main debuff yes. And enchanters are nearly equal to shaman in group content. But malos I think gives us the edge when nukers are around. Its almost a toss up here now I think. With the beast still far behind the enc/shm.

Lets look at the priest classes now:
Healing: Cleric >> Druid > Shaman
CC: Druid = Shaman >> Cleric
DPS: Shaman > Druid >> Cleric
Buffs: Shaman > Cleric > Druid
Debuffs: Shaman > Druid >> Cleric

Healing: Cleric obviously. But then we get into a more moderate different. With our hot on, our relative heal power = a druids. Its when we can't land the hot that our blast heal shows its weakness. The complete heal is still a valuabe tool though, so the Druid gets a slight edge for that. But with blast heals increasing in size, and with Crits, I notice less and less Druids using CH in groups. Raids is another story.

CC: Kinda depends on the content here. If mobs are snarable, a druid can snare kite/root when the mobs are hitting too tough to tackle directly. If the zone is compact, or snare does't work, then the shaman get the edge for being able to slow and park still, but loses if the mobs are too tough to tack hits from.

DPS: With Panther, its now the shaman. No debate there. Before Panther, it was the druid.

Buffs: Duh again. We are too versatile to be denied the top buffer slot.

Most of that assesment comes from a group standpoint. When you talk raiding, things can change a bit.

For instance, both our active shaman (me being one of them) took a week off at the same time. Our guild succesfully killed many raid target s without us. Try that without clerics or druids, and you'd be in trouble. The raw HP buffs that cleric and druids brings are of much greater importance to raids than a shaman being present. In summary, we are holding our own in groups. And I think are the top pick of the slowing classes. We are still the third pick of the healing classes though. If things get out of hand, we oftetimes can't keep pace with mob dps.
