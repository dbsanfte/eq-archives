With only four data sets from the other thread, don't have nearly enough data.

1) Some of the postings are estimates
2) Only 4 postings to deal with.

In lieu of anything better, I am working from the notion that the formula for Achery Max Damage (beofre modifiers like TS, NR/NM, etc)

(BowDAM + Arrow DAM) * ArchDamX

Where ArchDamX could be determined by one or more of the following:

Level
Skill
Dex
Str (reasoned away, see below)
Bow Delay (reasoned away, see below)

The quest is to determine ArchDam X.

Based on what VI has said, and observations, I feel that STR and DLY of the bow are NOT Factors. Not STR, because DEX has been indicated as the stat for bows, and not DLY of bow, because the only known formula that uses DLY is the BONUS of two handed melee weapons, and archery is NOT a main attack it is a secondary style attack, and no place in eq do secondary attacks get a bonus.

So that leaves Level, Skill and Dex as possible components of ArchDamX.

I looked at the posters on the last threads numbers, and using excel to compute backward, it appears that the Archery Max damage Formula is not too differenent from the melee one.

ArchDamX = (DEX + SKILL + LEVEL) /100

Prroduces numbers that are close, but not ACCURATE to peoples reported numbers, but at least two of the four samples I got were "I think" or "If i recall", what I need is more data that is EXACT.

I need 10 volunteers for the greater good of Rangerdom to do exact tests, and post them. We can pin this fomula down, but not with anecdotal data. We need to shoot greens (for true maxes) and we need to control the test. The more data the better, DATA, DATA, DATA!

The Test Methodology.
------------------------------------------

()----- Ruleset: -----()

1) Don't have any points in AM, sorry, AM is the shiznat, but messes with the test.
2) Dont use Trueshot
3) Ignore crits, if you get a crit, don't count it
4) Root the mob! to remove the double damage
5) If possible, and you have the time and gumption, lather rinse repeat with different bows and arrows, the steps below and record the different Maxes. At worst, get a terrible 1gp store bought bow and try it to.
6) repeat the steps below as many times as you feel you need to get a hit that aint getting any higher

()----- The Test -----()

1) Find a total Greenie, as green as possible, the greenest thing that can live through your lowest root.
2) Root them, negating the Non rooted code
3) Without TS, or any AM points, or archery buff let them have it.
4) Find another mob of similar level, if they are green enough the exact level is not as important, but try to be as close as possible, a level 1 or 4 should still get hit for Max.
5) Repeat until you cant seem to get a value that is any higher, the more samples you get the closetr you get to the true max. WE ARE LOOKING FOR TRUE MAX DAMAGE, not practical max damage on raiding mobs, etc. PLEASE IGNORE CRITS, crit values are applied after the normal formula.
6) repeat above if you want with different bows and arrows.

We (the eq number crunching community) THINK the way combat and the tables work is this.

1) You make a "to hit" roll against the Mob. This % is based on your Skill/Level ATK vs their Avoidance AC component.

2) If you hit, you hit for either minumum damage, modal damage, maximum damage or somewhere between, based on your STR/DAMAGE ATK vs. their Mitigation AC component.

MAX Damage, how it is calculated is known for melee.

The Tables dont effect MAX damage, they effect the frequency with which it is acchieved.

That is why monks at 60 never hit harder than Rangers with same weapon and stats, just more often, and for more DPS over the long haul.
