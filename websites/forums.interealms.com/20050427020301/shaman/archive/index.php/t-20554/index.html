Important Notes for Pit
Wraith type mobs see invis, the rest don't
Diaku mitigate slow, the rest don't
Aggro range is pretty low
Mobs resist root quite a lot even after malos, but VP is near fool-proof after MD
Oracles and wraiths can dispell (you or themselves) and root
SKs cast some dots but nothing too major
Other mobs are straight melee.
Zone out is in the near-right corner of pit from zone in.


My tactics:
Bind outside. Buff up, including levitate and invis.
Zone in to the pit, float/run down the left side to the far left corner, avoiding any undead that might see through invis.
Go through the tunnel into the small square room. There are 2 diaku in opposite corners, and one roamer mob that paths through the middle of the room. On your left are the pig pens.

The two corner mobs will never aggro if you don't go near them, so you can leave em up, or go kill em if you feel safer that way.

I move to the near right corner, and await the roamer. DR debuff it and then VP (or malos + root), slow if you feel safer with slow on (I know I do!), then just root rot til dead. Start a timer, repop will be in 27 minutes IIRC.

Once the roamer is dead I invis up and head back to the pit, looking for any named mobs (enraged war boars and frenzied initiates seem most common). If I find one, wait for a clear pull back to the pig-pen room and root-rot as before. If nothing special is up, pull anything else to clear the spawns and hope a named might pop later.

Keep an eye out for the "stampeding hooves" message - this means piglet is on the way. Finish off your current mob fast, or get it positioned out the way right in the corner and root it there. Invis up and wait for the stampede. Try to get piglet on target, wait for it to come back around at the end of the stampede and if its clear on its own, root it and proceed to root rot, making sure you stay pretty close to it at all times. More detail on other threads if you use search :) If you get adds rooting piglet you have a chance to zone out and run back fast with VP3 to still catch it... if you're lucky and zone fast.

Wraith type mobs see invis, the rest don't
That's close, but incorrect.
Tortured Souls & War Wraiths will always see invis. *SOME* boar beasts will also see invis, so /con them if you are running invis through the stables or back hall. As an additional warning, some of the Zek Initiates can see invis if they were previously used as an enchanter's charm pet - so beware any of them dual wielding swords or red flashy globes (mage weapons) while you are running around invis. I personally seek these former pets out for root/rotting to get dogdog free toys - don't pet tank them. War Crows are sometimes hard to see, so keep your eyes peeled for them when pulling. It is generally VERY easy to single pull anything from the pit if you are a little patient.

There are 3 zone-outs in Tactics. The one near the zone-in, the one in the corner of the pit, and another in the back corner of the stables. The one in the stables will be 'magically locked shut' if Glykus (the pig farmer) is up, so be careful if you are using that as your escape route. Be sure to bind yourself right outside by the stone to Tactics for gate/CR purposes.

Frenzied Initiates have ~10khp MORE than standard zek initiates, and they FLURRY. Root/rot only if you are soloing. They uncommonly drop spectral parchments. Zelrin Morlock is the named war wraith. Typically drops a spectral parchment. Can also drop gauntlets or bracer pattern/mold or one of 2 shields (neither of which we can use). Enraged War Boars are the named boar (separate from a stampeding piglet) that can also drop gloves/bracer mold or a War Crested ring, a bard sword, or a Gold Tipped Boar Horn (used in Cloak of Greater Pernicity quest). Several posts in this forum already deal with A stampeding piglet, so I won't cover that here.

Glykus the pig farmer can be done with 3 people. I've been farming him regularly now with a druid and enchanter. 24 hr respawn. The Diakus Overseer can also be single grouped, but is more difficult. Avoid them both solo. ;) Be nice to the soloing enchanters or druids in the zone, and you may be able to team up short-term with them for Glykus kills. :)

The tunnel on the left (near the stables) is much safer for soloing than the tunnel on the right. I use the tunnel on the right only for single kills when I have a clear single pull to there. The right tunnel gets TONS more wanderers going through it. Do not camp the zone-out in the pit - too many people train out there, and it is just lame to block a zone-out that is so out of the way. This will likely be your most commonly used escape route. I'd recommend zoning if you ever have 3 or more mobs engaged at once, even if all 3 are safely root parked.

Suspend Minion 2 and a clicky invis item (I use a Tae Ew Ritualist Mask) are also nice to have items for soloing here. Suspend pet and click invis to scout any time you like. I generally do this right after a kill while I'm regenning w/ Quiescence on after smacking Canni5.

Spirit Call is a great way to buy yourself time if an unanticipated add comes your way. SC the add, then root it and slow it. The dogs should take aggro very quickly. Virulent paralysis is also a great way to handle single adds.

Also be aware that most of these mobs will run at 15-20% health, so have some sort of plan with dealing with that if you aren't straight up root/rotting. All of these mobs are social, and a mob at 6% that gets a little too close to the pit may end up bringing 2-3 adds because you didn't finish him off fast enough. Clicky root is awesome for handling this.

Take care,

Root it before it comes within melee range, and then back up to max spell range again.

For DoT's, he would be using Curse of the Spirits, Anathema, Odium, Plague (preceded by Insidious decay for DR debuffing), and possibly Ebolt. CanniIII in between DoT casts, with Stoicism running most of the time. Bone Clasped Girdle (or a Coldain Military Wristguard) with Burning Affliction III on it means every DoT does extra damage, and Affliction Efficiency III (from a Band of Twilight maybe) would save significant amounts of mana. Another possible method would be nuke&root, sort of a hybrid tactic of how you would take out a Cliff Golem. Pull with Malosi, Root (level 14 version if you think Ornate pants is too much of a gift). Tortured souls don't have a super high movement rate, so you might be able to land it w/o getting touched. Hail,

If the camp outside the stables is taken, you can still do a few things.

1. Back Hall. Talk to the person/group that is there. Sometimes they won't mind you taking out the back hall area (diakus) if they are only pulling the pit. As a solo shaman nearby, you can exchange buffs w/ their group (since honestly most groups use an enchanter there in my experience) and if you have proven yourself competent (meaning you won't train them, you can actually handle the mobs there w/ no danger, etc) they usually don't mind neighbors. The back hall area is enough to keep you busy when alone. Many groups leave the back hall mobs up when pulling the pit for exp because the Diakus mitigate slow and therefore take more mana to kill compared to most pit mobs. Root/rotting them the mitigation isn't an issue for you though. :D

2. SW Corner. Alternatively, if you or the other group isn't comfortable with someone that close by, the corner closest to that tunnel (SW) is a fairly decent spot. If the other group is pulling at a reasonable rate, the normal problem of adds is somewhat lessened, and you can always train out w/o hurting anyone if things get too hairy for you.

I would NOT recommend camping the zone-out corner. In addition to causing others who want to safely leave a wrinkle in their exit plan (for those that actually care), you can end up clicking out unintentionally rather easily.

3. SE Tunnel. The SE tunnel hallway isn't a great place to camp, but it is sometimes okay for 2-3 mobs. I'll use it when I can safely single pull a named there that would be too difficult to bring singled to my normal camp spot. If you are grouped, this can be a great camp spot, because the large numbers of wandering mobs pathing through makes for 'auto-pulls' which save time in a fast kill group. Solo for extended periods this spot is very dangerous due to the frequent adds. Additionally, if Arm/Supp is camped, you have to watch out for uncalled trains to the pit zone-out, which will frequently leave you flattened. :( Stampeded also come through here, so you have to watch for the message and flatten yourself against the wall (even while engaged w/ a mob) to keep from getting stampeded. :p

4. NW or SE corners. The other two pit corners can also be used, though you will have to stay on your toes 100% of the time while you are kililng. You DO need to worry about adds coming at any moment. When that happens, I typically will root & slow the add, then 'burn' the mob I was engaged with as fast as I can. 3 or more in camp & I recommend you zone. Generally when you take one of these corners it is because there are already 2-3 other parties in the pit. When this is the case, the adds aren't as much of a worry because the bulk of the pit stays clearer than when you are down there by yourself or with only one or two other soloers.

5. Sandbox. You can root/rot or kite from this camp above the pit in the 'sandbox' area as well, though that spot is often taken by druids or necros kiting. This is pretty safe exp, though it is very boring to mer personally.

6. Stables. You can also camp the stables itself. Most pit groups leave the stables alone unless Glykus is up, and then they only clear them prior to killing him. If you ask nicely, groups will normally let you kill your first mob (root pull or you will get two) in the room outside the stables (where they are camped) and then you spend the rest of your time inside the stables proper. Keep a timer or watch the clock to make sure the respawns don't take you by surprise. ;) If Glykus isn't up, you have a zone-out very close by at the very end of the stables. Many people are unaware of this, and it means you can zone safely when in trouble without bothering the person/group camping the room outside the stables. NOTE: If GLYKUS IS UP, this zone-out DOES NOT WORK!!! It is 'magically shut' and you will die to Glykus rapidly trying to use it.

This is in some ways more difficult than the pit itself, and in other ways it is easier.
