Ok, so I solved my problem, and while I don't think it will help that many others, I figured that it couldn't hurt. It turned out the problem was with my soundcard (even though I had the nosound.txt file in my everquest directory). As soon as I removed the card, everything started working fine. It may have been a faulty card (though I've had it for a few years and it never gave me problems in my old computer), or it may have been that the card was just generating too much heat. I also think that it could have been a conflict of some sort with the motherboard's built-in sound card.

BTW, I just wanted to share another problem and solution I found while I'm at it. The motherboard on-board sound card that I had was only producing one channel audio (I couldn't get sound in my left speaker; this was why I had the other sound card in the first place). So after wading through 10 pages of tech support forum hell, I found a user with a similiar problem, who had discovered it was caused by an extra spacer in his case which was touching the motherboard and causing a short. Sure enough, I checked my case, and there was a spacer in the exact same place. So, if you ever have random problems with your motherboard, make sure to remove all the spacers that aren't actually being used (to hold the motherboard to the case).
