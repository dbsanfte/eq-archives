Have you fought many mobs that have effects that change how healing works? Maybe you haven't and maybe you have. My experience is centered mostly on the effect you find in Ssra and one that you see in GoD.

The ones in Ssra, which proc Balance of Zeb (- linked above), for example, hurt me a lot more than they hurt clerics. Let me explain.

They reduce healing effectiveness TO 25%.

cleric - complete heal - 7500 - w/balance 1875
cleric - holy light - 3275 - w/balance 819

druid - karana's renewal - 4680 - w/balance 1170
druid - sylvan infusion - 2100 - w/balance 525

shaman - kragg's mending - 1950 - w/balance 488
shaman - tnarg's mending - 1300 - w/balance 325


I'd still argue that since our heals are the smallest, we suffer the most from changes like this that cause them to do less healing. A bigger heal, even cut down to 25% is still a bigger heal...and everyone has bigger heals than us. A cheal will still land for 2k with AA, when an effect like this is on a tank. That is still significant. The arguments we have been having about our heals being weak haven't been about efficiency of heal, it has been about SIZE of heal.

Since Heal over time is a fixed amount per tick, they are not effected by this type of effect. In this way, we have an advantage over druids, but we are still far behind clerics in ability to heal around this. Since we lack an AA heal over time (like clerics, druids and beastlords have) this also makes it difficult to say that we are hurt less than other classes.

The only other heal reducing ability that really doesn't impact shamans is one I have come across in GoD. It reduces healing spell effectiveness on healing spells with a cast time of 10 seconds... so specifically a nerf on cheal and iCH. I guess it could theoretically be bad for us too, if anyone actually used Kragg's mending.

1. We get bad heals because of slow
2. Now ( 18 months ago ) there is slow mitigation
3. We should get more mobs that mitigate heals to punish druids and clerics too
