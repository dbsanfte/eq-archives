SisterLi
You can either keep 3 or 4 timers by your computer, rely on them to tell you when their buffs have dropped, or use yourself or dogdog as the timer. These are the only options I am aware of.

I prefer to use meself and Dogdog as the timer. You're a shaman, might as well run around in a full set of buffs. For the most part, you won't need them. But there have been plenty of times when the heal hits you with just a few hps left, or you are able to run with few hps because your agi stayed high enough, or the extra AC let you Shaman Mez(TM) a couple of adds.

Unless I am doing a lot of melee, I use myself to time the HP buff (Kragg, or Harness, or Focus), the stamina buff, the AC buff, SOW, regen, the strength buff and the agility buff. Dogdog times haste and dex. Before I had group versions of all of these buffs, I used to space them out aproximately 12 minutes, so they wouldn't all drop at once.

If you have Harness or focus, you can space them out even more.

SisterLi
I don't like to leave it for the tanks to tell me.

First, they notice at the most inconvenient times. Far better for me to notice on my own and to have a few to select when to cast.

Second, I try to space my buffs. The tanks will often not notice until several buffs have worn off. This creates problems with my mana supply, and then repeats itself every hour or so, because now all their buffs are wearing off at once.

Third, I draw enough aggro that the buffs are useful to keep on myself.

Finally, I do not recommend attempting to time buffs on a raid, u
nless you are just responsible for one group's buffs. It will just drive you up the wall. In those circumstances anyone who wants buffs must be responsible for keeping track.

It is not difficult to keep track of buffs once you train your mind to do it. I find that it is much like slinging hash -- you are not aware of memorizing the order, or of keeping track of what everyone's drinking, but when the time comes to bring the pitcher back around, or distribute the food, you find that you know what goes where.
