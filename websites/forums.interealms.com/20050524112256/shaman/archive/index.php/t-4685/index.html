Flash back two years:

When I was 20, most of my armor was tattered leather that I tailored myself. I crossed the world from Halas to Kaladim bearing the head of that villian Trumpy Irontoe. I travelled through dangerous moutains zones, haunted forests, seedy towns, and sailed a great ocean. I'll never forget seeing the wooden bridge to East Karana for the first time. I saw my first hill giant and was terrified he'd spot me from half the zone away.

When I finally arrived, I had to spend days fighting evil goblins (they had GREEN skin) to earn the respect of the dwarves, but it was all worth it. Byzar Bloodforge awarded me with the mighty Bloodforge Hammer. It was a 13/50 two hander, gave me +10 versus magic, and it cast light! I felt invulnerable. I also bought my first piece of banded armor on that journey, spending all my savings for a helm.

Yes, the game has changed a lot.
