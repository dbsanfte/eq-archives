To: 'EQFeedback@soe.sony.com'
Subject: EQ: Re: 500 trader limit in Bazaar

The limit of 500 active traders in the bazaar
could be dealt with in better fashion.

First we suppose that the 500 limit is actually
desired and not imposed simply because of avoidable
performance issues.

The players attempting to become traders should
join a queue to become traders dealt in first-in
first-out fashion. While in this queue they should
no longer consume food and drink, but they should
be constrained to remain in the trader areas.
This way a player could set up in the bazaar, click
Begin trading and retire for the evening, safe from
having their comestables consumed.

The current method leads to frustration and bad behavior.

Second consider the posibilities of reducing the
load on clients and server imposed by the current
trader system. Let us consider some possibilities.

A) Trades could be made directly from the /bazaar
interface, suppose that right clicking no the trader
name no longer pointed the character toward the
desired trader, but simply opened the trade interface.
This simplified trading substantially, but more
importantly it allows the next step.

B) Remove the need for traders to be online for trading
to occur. This lowers the server load by removing the need
to support at least 500 connections. This reduces the
netword traffic by these same clients. But how is trading
controlled and logged? There are several possibilities:

The /trader interface is still a fine way to control the setting
of prices. Make it that characters that log in the bazaar
with trader satchels upon their character are traders until
they log back on. (This could be limited to last such
character on an account to log in this way. This could use
a special command in /trader interface if it is felt there is
danger from people loigging without desiring to be traders.)

When players right-click on the trader name in the bazaar
window a trade window rises up, they buy items, the cash is
added to the amount that will be on the character when it logs in.

Currently, trades are logged into s log file on the client.
This capability is lost under this protocol. Although fine with
me, if it were felt such logs should be made, entries could be
made when the client logs back in.
