soupypoopy
It's a market. People ask what they think a few others may be able to pay. Once an item sells at a certain "ask" price a number of times, that price begins to be perceived as the "set" price of that item going forward.

If an item is *really* being overpriced, then chances are a seller will be auctioning it for a very long time. Eventually, he'll either find a buyer or he'll lower his price to match the perceived value of the item by buyers.

Bigguss
It's all relative.

Genreally, tradeable items are priced according to two things- how they relate in power to other items, and saturation of the market.

Relative power: A new item comes into the game- how do you determine what it's worth? Well, how does it compare to other existing items in the game? It's probably going to be worth more than items less powerful, and worth less than items more powerful. Let's say a new 9 dmg/20 dly sword drops- chances are it will be worth less than a 9/19 lamentation and worth more than a 10/24 velium warsword. Of course proc, weight, stats, etc. can change the value of an item.

Saturation: This one is simple supply and demand. The more available an item is, the less it's going to cost. Once an item hits the magic point of being more available than it's desired, prices plummet to commodity levels. Perfect example- when Split Paw Hide Gloves first came out (with the Paw revamp), they were pretty much the only tradeable +WIS gloves in the game. For those first few weeks, they sold for a lot of money (thousands of pp) since they represented an upgrade for a lot of people- and there weren't many of them. After a few weeks, people started realizing that these were dropping like candy- and prices started falling. Now you can't get 20 pp for them. Black sapphire necklaces (+hp/+mana) are another great example- they said to sell for thousands (sometimes 10k+)- now they're available for 500 pp due to saturation.

Saturation also drives down prices of like items. This happens all the time with weapons. The easy availability of Centi shortswords (12/25, with a couple stats) has driven down the cost of anything with a worse ratio. Why? Well, why would you pay more than 500 pp for a weapon when you could get a Centi for that price?

This manifests itself through the markets- in exactly the way the above posters mentioned. This is a discrete market, though, so an item can definitely be worth more to person X than person Y. Many "I HATE the tunnel!" types will sell for less and pay more for items to avoid hanging around the commerce zones or trade channels. So an item never really has a true "market value" it has a range of values that people often buy and sell for- but even that is no guarantee.
