Actually this makes more sense than it first appeared. The thing is, Walking Sleep is doing about 25% slow for only 60 mana, and Tagar's is doing about 33% for 125 mana - more than twice as much mana for only 1/3 more effect. Wow. I wish I'd realized that at level 30, lol.

The "value" of the slow depends on how long the mob lives after slow lands, as well as the melee DPS of the mob. Assuming a 50dps mob, Walking Sleep mitigates around 12.5 dps, and Tagar's around 16.7 dps.

For this to match the efficiency of a Greater Heal (300 health for 150 mana) it only takes about 10 seconds for Walking Sleep to justify itself, but Tagar's takes 15 seconds to pay for itself. So if a mob with that DPS is living less than 15 seconds after you land your slow, you're better off with Walking Sleep.

(Note: I have no idea what the real DPS of the OT or FM mobs are, so take all this with a grain of salt.)

Weyalan
Alpha dog refers to "Project Alpha"; which is an open secret within the Shaman Community

Project Alpha was the codename invented by this community for the investigation of our pet's ability to wield weapons, and the effect thereof. Remember, our pet is a wolf, and as such has paws, not fingers. Also, unlike other pets, our pet has no grphic for wielding a weapon. So, when it was discovered that our pets actually can wield weapons, it was generally thought that this was an oversight on Verant/Sony's part.

Initially, this was seen as an irrelevant oddity, because the weapon damage / delay did not seem to effect the pets DPS. However, it was then discovered that if the pet was given 2 weapons, the DPS increased significantly (in the region of 20 - 30%). When this was discovered, the shaman community were excited, but also remained convinced that this was a mistake by Verant/Sony, so initially, this was treated as a secret, on the premise that if it was discovered, the nerf stick would be waved, and the ability to wield / duel wield would be removed.

So, the early discussions never directly referred to pets duel wielding - as an attempt to keep everything secret, it was just referred to as "Project Alpha"...and the name has stuck, even though it is no longer really a secret.

Weyalan
Since this thread has turned into something of an "all about pets" discussion, I will just mention the following:

*Strength buffs increase pets damage output
*Dex buffs increase pets damade output only if a proccing weapon is being used
*Agi buffs do not effect pet's AC
*Stam buffs do not affect pet's total hp
*Direct hp buffs (talisman of altuna, for example) do affect pet's total hp
*Your pet will automatically assume the resists that yo have, so if you have buffed your own, no need to buff pet's

No responsibility is taken for blatant inaccuracies
