Chutoi
I'll apologize ahead of time, because my info is going to be dated--I did this trial before the revamp. (Back when it was REALLY freaking hard.)

The strat we finally ended up using also used the tether. Take in a high hp warrior (not even sure if defensive works anymore now--might lose agro like the tipt trial). We'd go with a warrior, cleric, druid, and 3 dps (or 2 dps and shaman). Druid has to have GoD heals.

Anyway, we'd send in the tank, and let him use furious discipline. Chain heal the tank with long distance heals from outside the room (Sylvan infusion does most of the healing this fight, as it's 200 range. Cleric's only have supernal remedy that's 200). Daluda's works well too, as it's 200 range and actually 100hp more than cleric SR.

Make sure to chain from the middle of the hall IN THE DOORWAY. If you don't the tethered (but unengaged) mob will warp onto healers for a quick smackdown, then warp back. If you heal from the doorway in the middle of the hall, they run at you and don't warp--giving the tether time to jerk them back.

Heal agro will keep the 2nd occupied. After about 5-10 seconds send in your dps and burn the first one down.

If you need to med after the first, have someone stun/tash/whatever to keep agro on the 2nd one so it won't rez the first.

Engage as quickly as you can and kill the second.

I'm not sure how hard these are now--I hear they're at least partially slowable now, so this trial is probably a ton easier. The original version was brutal.

Completed the 2nd Trial last week with a group of:

Warrior (10.5k hps)
Cleric (6500 mana, all GoD spells)
Druid (6300 mana, all GoD spells)
Monk (xeg wraps, sem)
Ranger (coirnav hammer, emp sword)
Shaman (6200 mana, no GoD heal)

No clarity, shaman haste, no BSS.

We found that the trial was significantly easier if you don't wipe the first attempt. We wiped the first time on the 2nd named mainly due to mana issues, but finally won on about the 6th attempt afterwards. The primary reason the first attempt is best is because you can setup so that you only have to fight them 1 at a time. If you wipe the first time you have to take your chances with leashing the 2nd priest or dealing with it adding in at some point.

For the first attempt we went in and killed all the adds around each priest, except 1 in the corner of the room out of line of sight of the doorway (as long as it's not the mob behind each priest directly in front of the doorwary it's fine). We then went out of the room to med back up to kill the last add and priests. Then the entire group moved into the first priest's room and setup in corner near the last add out of line of sight of the doorway. We killed the last mob and the warrior riposted/defensive the priest. No one was allowed to move out in front of the doorway into LoS of the 2nd priest. By doing this the 2nd priest never aggroed. When the first priest was dead the warrior led the way into the 2nd room where we wiped with the 2nd priest at 4% because the healers were lom from spam healing on the first priest.

The next few attempts after we CR'd were bad mainly because we couldn't get into the 1st priests room without aggroing the other over. What ended up working was leaving the cleric+druid outside the room in the doorway and having the rest of the group run into the 1st priests room and setting up in the corner furthest from the 2nd priest out of LoS of the doorway. The 2nd priest still aggroed but leashing kicked in and kept it out of the room for most of the fight. This time we had the cleric chain CH on the MA with me bridging the CH (if it wasn't bridged CH is 1-2 seconds too slow) so we wouldn't have a mana issue on the 2nd priest. This worked well until right before the 1st priest died the 2nd priest aggroed into the room, I slowed it right off and died but the rest of the group was able to kill it.

So what we learned was:
1) First attempt is the best chance to deal with the encounter on your terms.
2) Setup out of line of sight of the 2nd priest.
3) Once slowed it's better to use CH with a bridge heal rather than spam heals.
4) Even though the priests start at 12% health, they have alot hps.
5) Healers outside the room out of line of sight of the priests is good.

Of all the trials this was the most annoying.
