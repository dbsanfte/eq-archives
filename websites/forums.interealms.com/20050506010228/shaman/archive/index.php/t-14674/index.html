Sharlok_Bonespirit
Which buffs to cast really depend on where you are and what people want. Not everyone needs STR/AGI/DEX/STA all the time, but if they ask for it, give it to them. It takes far less time to mem the spell and cast it than it does to argue about who deserves what buff, etc. Your job is to keep everyone buffed, to keep the mobs slowed, and potentially, to heal. I would strongly recommend against refusing anyone a buff.

That said, I found that Infusion of Spirit, Kragg, Agility, and Stamina were very well received by everyone I grouped with in those levels. Occasionally, I'd get the odd request for a STR buff from someone . . . tumultuous strength took care of that, and I don't believe I ever got dex buff requests from anyone other than folks proccing or folks working on combat skill increases. Those base 4 spells just about covered everyone, though.

Sharlok_Bonespirit
If you're in a group with a level 60+ shaman, the higher level shaman gets buffs duty. By 60, we can cast Khura's focusing, Group Stamina, Group Agility, Group Regrowth, and group poison/disease resist buffs. Regular STR and DEX buffs do not stack with Focus. Total time to switch spell sets and buff the group . . . . less than a minute, give or take. If you're in an 'outside' zone, you can get SoW. This speeds up the buff cycle and gives you a break from having to cast 5 buffs on 6 people.

In a raid situation, you can probably work it out with the higher level shamen to get slow duty. The way I always did it was to start casting turgurs just after the higher level started casting Malosinia. The slow would land a split second after the resist debuff. The higher level shaman will probably appreciate the help with the slows, since they're going to be getting all of the buffs, and you get a big contrabution to the raid. Once you get to 58, you'll become a viable healer, so you'll have even more to do in groups/raids. I found that raids went from barely tolerable to half way fun (to me) as I leveled up and was able to assume more roles. It just keeps getting better, now.
