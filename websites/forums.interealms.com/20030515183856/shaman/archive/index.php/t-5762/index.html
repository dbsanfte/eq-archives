Tarthug
The new pet at 55 is more durable, taunts better, hits slightly harder and more frequently. In a straight up fight, he'll get dropped fast on an unslowed mob, or when fighting 2 mobs at a time. On slowed, controlled single pulls, your pet usually tanks very well for you allowing you to med, or deal death from a distance. Give your pet the right tools by tossing at least 2 buffs on it (speed, regen), but I also like to toss on a HoS for improved avg damage and seemingly better taunts.

Typical fight scenario for me is
1) pull with slow
2) root near my pet
3) epic
4) pet attack
5) additional dots/debuffs as required (IE: Bane/pox on non-pr/dr mobs)
6) jbb (in your case, additional dots and med, or just melee) and melee till mob around 30-50% depending on mob durability and pull rate.
7) root, back off, med/regen/can let wonderdog and lingering dots toast the mob.
8) go back to 1.

It eventually gets so routine that the adjustments become natural and you can pull before the current mob is even dead, or park another one dotted and slowed till wondermutt is done feasting. Epic is great for mana free damage while the mob is parked and awaiting destruction.

I think DogDog is highly under appreciated or abused. I personally buff my pet to full. I give him every buff I give myself with the only exception being Charisma. Casting HoS first then stacking Str and Dex ontop helps his damage output noticabley. I heard a rumor from a beastlord friend of mine a few weeks ago that VI broke pets damage tables in a patch, and this is to our advantage.

Correct me if I'm wrong: Shammy and BL pets have damage based on a H2H chart. Since they broke them, if you give them weapons non h2h based, it moves the pet into a different (much better) damage table. He won't hit for a larger max, but he'll sure as hell hit for max more often, and just hit more often all together. Because of this, I've been on a trend of twinking my pet. I buy up tons of little weapons under 100pp that have interesting procs, just to give them to my dog. With the pet fully buffed and duel wielding, he can tank most dark blues with just a Turgur's.

My personal tactic is:
Pull with slow
Send in the Pet
Dot if mob is beefy
Canadance till FM or Dog is 40%hp, which ever comes first
Finish off mob tanking, which lets pet regen for next fight




Note: With the Bazaar wrecking havoc on the economy you can make your pet do cool things for cheap with proc weapons. Mix and match to see what works best. I probably spend about 1k every 2 weeks on pet weapons. Some would call it a waste of money, I call it making soloing fun. Here's some other helpful hints.

Stun: stun procs are great against caster classes and work well as a cheap snare. Even against melees, repeated duel procing stun weps will help keep your pet alive longer and easier. On a ton of weapons

Root: Tired of casting enstill or immobolize to root/rot? Pick up a couple Ebony Bladed Long swords and put that pet window to use, with some mana free dogdog crowd control

Snare: So you messed up and didnt take Innoruk as you diety, no snare necklace for you. Try giveing your puppy a Serrated Bone Dirk for some engulfing darkness action, Woe works well with it too for the double DoT action. (My combo of choice in Skyfire)

DD: Puppy can crank out alot more damage with HoS stacked with Dex and sporting some cheap Ykesha or Spiritstrike procing weapons.

Avoid Iron Bound Tome at all costs, nothing worse than your dog dispelling your buffs.

Have fun :)

I'm a lvl 54 Shaman on Tarew Marr. No Epic, no Fungi, no JBB, no nothing. Here's my typical senerio while soloing.

Before the very first pull I cast Bih`li, Levitate and Regrowth on myself. I cast Regrowth and Alacrity on my pet (that's it, no HoS, Str or Dex).

I suggest you find a zone that you are pretty familiar with. Park your pet somewhere free of adds (if possible).

1) Pull with Turgur's Insects.
2) Run a good distance past pet but still within casting range and wait for mob to come. This is where levitate comes into play when I sometimes solo in OOT and run over the water past my pet parked on the shoreline. Can be done w/o Levitate, just run along the shoreline.
3) Sick pet when mob reaches it. Your pet should gain aggro cause all you've done is slowed it.
4) optional - Cast a disease based DoT since the fight my take about a minute or two. You can also cannibalize a bit if you desire. You can now sit since pet will be owning it (they're tougher than people think).
5) Root mob at about 40%
6) Toss a DD on it if Mana permits.
7) DoT and Root should break before mob is dead. But by the time root breaks mob should be atleast 20%. You may now Melee it to death along side pet.

You will notice very little downtime if any at all. If you wanna toss some melee buffs on your pet feel free. I just choose not to cause i wanna get right into the pulls after the little buffs I toss before the fight.

Goldark
Nice thread this one :) Here's my current setup for soloing at 55:

Go to JP and fight the gnolls in the North. My favoured spot is just down the pit, back to the wall. There's one spawn immediately infront of you in the tunnel before it opens into the large room. He won't aggro when you drop in though, just back off and get ready.

Self-buffs: Just about everything
Pet buffs: HoS, Str and dex from JB gear, alacrity

Pull with slow, set pet on it. Cast plague (normally get aggro at this stage, but that's no problem) and then EBolt (sometimes need to try a few times to channel it). After they have landed I root and let pet tank. I then sit back and med and canni. If root breaks or pet is getting beaten up badly I then tank for the pet until the mob starts to run. With JBB I'd tank and channel JBB nukes I think, but at the moment I prefer to regain the mana as pet is fairly effective. If needed I sometimes add regrowth on the pet.

When the mob runs it will just path into the rear wall and can't move any further. It keeps trying to run - so its like a free snare! At this point I either resume medding and canni, or go pull another, while the pet chews it up.

I normally find the DoTs fade just before the mob dies (with ext aff 3), unless I was tanking it too. It can take a while for dog dog to finish it off without DoTs, so when that happens I melee too. Oh for a JBB!

Notes:
Most mobs are warriors, but there are some shaman, necro, and cleric types. I melee them with my howling harpoon for extra stuns - will try some gnoll hide lariats on pet next!

Aggro radius is very low so single pulls are normally easy. Most I got was one add from a pop while pulling back, plus one add when the tunnel mob popped. I slowed and double-dotted the adds, and then rooted them out the way. With KEI they were easy to handle. Risk of bigger trains is practically zero at this spot.

Xp was just over 1 blue bub an hour at 55 (100% xp - 0% aa).

My gear is far from uber. Using a spine chill spear, only items of note are iksar regen bp and coldain military bracer (helps a lot). I try to get a KEI before starting too. JBB and REIF would make this a lot easier I think.
