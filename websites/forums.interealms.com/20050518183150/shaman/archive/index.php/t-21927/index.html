I am starting to collect data on AC and the effects of agi. Hopefully this data will help those who are skilled at number crunching to develop some models.

Data collection protocol:
All toons used will be warriors. Three different races will be used to establish a base line for the effects seen. All toons will not wear armor to remove that variable from the effects of ac. Toons will be leveled and the AC information collected every 5 levels. At each check 4 agi buffs will be applied and the effects recorded, then 4 debuffs will be applied and those effects also recorded. All melee will be logged as the toons are leveled, on the chance that the combat data will help others with their data collect, if you want copies of the logs feel free to send me a message.

Ruatonim
Sorry, but what exactly are you trying to prove? You are leveling a warrior and buffing it with every agility buff you have to determine what the shown AC number will be? Is there something I'm missing with why this is needed?

If you are trying to determine if agility has a (noticeable) affect on avoidance, then you need to have little to no variables other than the amount of agility on said warrior. Then you need to parse vs the same MOB for around 1000-10,000 hits at each level of agility.

You aren't going to get the data needed for determining if agility is meaningful just by leveling a new warrior from 1-whenever you get bored leveling the warrior. Besides this fact, you aren't going to buff the warrior high enough in agility (most likely) to get meaningful results until said warrior is 47+ (hint: 10-30ish agility isn't going to have much, if any, noticeable or parseable difference from base--you need increments of 50-100ish).
