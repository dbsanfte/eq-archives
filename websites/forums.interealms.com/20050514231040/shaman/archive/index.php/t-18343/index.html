Shadrak
I used to do a lot of 2-boxing with a warrior (actually, I 3-box with a druid as the third). Give the warrior a cheapo greatstaff of thunder for a stun proc and he does ok holding agro. Of course, I think the proc starts working at level 55, so that's not going to do much good prior to that level. Warriors are REALLY expensive to outfit if you're hung up on good gear like I am, so I wouldn't recommend that route. And unless you've got a get-out-of-jail-free card (like my druid evac-bot), you'll end up dying quite a bit with the warrior, who can't gate or feign.

Shadowknights are a nice option - they hold agro nicely, have some nice inexpensive weapons, and are FAR superior to warriors in one critical area: they actually have a reason to level. I've pretty much quit on my warrior because - how much better is a level 57 warrior than a level 56? Not a whole lot. There just isn't much incentive to get excited about leveling a pure melee class. That same argument goes for my level 58 monk, who I haven't played in months. It's just not very exciting to go up a level and get nothing but a craptacular discipline with a 72-minute reuse timer and 37 extra hp :rolleyes: At least a shadowknight has something of value to gain at each level - a few new spells. I know that many of those spells aren't particularly valuable, but at least its something.

I haven't tried it personally, but I've heard that necros make excellent partners. Two pets, exceptional dots, really good mana regen on both characters, both can gate, and the necro plugs some gaps in a shaman repertoire (snare, FD, undead CC, undead charm). But I think it might be tough to 2-box if you're trying to fear-kite. Others that have 2-boxed that combo are certainly better equipped to answer the concerns about how to manage that, though. It certainly looks like a powerful combination to me.

Monks are always a decent option, and are quite fun at lower levels. A decently-equipped monk in the lower levels can really put out some damage. But as they level, they get weaker and weaker unless you have access to elite no-drop gear, and they're horrendously expensive to outfit in the bazaar. But, they make a very good 2-box partner, like the shadowknight, because melee is easier to handle on the second machine and both can feign death to escape bad situations. And their mend ability and stonestance discipline can really help out in a pinch. Plus, they get lots of wisdom from their druid/beastlord gear :D

My current project is a mage. I've read several times that the mage/shaman combo is quite powerful, so I'm playing a mage at the moment. She's up to level 40 and is quite fun so far. From the very excellent mage board http://www.magecompendium.com/, I've read that mage pets even at the highest levels make excellent tanks. The level 65 earth pet Rathe's Son has, with proper focusing and buffs, over 7k hp, and he roots. That makes for a pretty decent tank, especially given that he's disposable :p The air pets stun, which can be helpful when fighting caster mobs. The water mob backstabs, if you want to tank with the shaman and let the pet get around behind. The fire pet is quite effective at lower levels with its built-in fire damage shield. Four types of pets, each with something different to offer, give you more flexibility in what you hunt.

Mages also fill a shaman weakness - nuking power. If you need to burn something down in a hurry, mages can do the trick. They have a weakness in that their DD lines are all either fire or magic based (I believe), but both mages and shaman have the malo line to debuff resistances. Mages have a very nice selection of rains and nukes to put the hurt on mobs in a very quick time. And the very effective agro generated by stunning or rooting pets gives them the ability to do so, within reason. If you want to tank with the shaman, we all know how to generate stupid amounts of agro with malo/slow/cripple/dot/dot/dot, which will let the mage burn to his heart's content :D

Throw in the mage summoned pet toys for dogdog and jabober and you've got some decent mana-free dps. Admittedly, the best toys are from level 60+ spells, but you can summon proccing weapongs, masks with item haste that stack with our spell haste, full sets of pet armor, and a waist item with 500 hp. Fully outfitting two pets with mage toys GREATLY increases their staying power, and helps a little with dps, too. Don't forget the mage damage shields on whichever pet is taking the beating.

Mages can regen mana quite effectively by using their reclaim energy spells, so downtime should be held to a minimum (only effective at higher levels, though).

Mages can summon an impressive array of focus items all the way up to the burning affliction IV bracers. They have spell haste, improved damage, reagent conservation, and burning affliction lines from 1 - 4, so you can maximize your casting power for both characters at ZERO cost.

Another nice thing about mages is they're not too dependant on gear. You can outfit a mage with decent bazaar gear for a fraction of a warrior or monk's tab.

As you can tell, I'm pretty excited about the mage/shaman combo. It's a little weak in a few areas, but every 2-box combo has weaknesses. All in all, I think it will make an excellent 2-box combination.

Just for kicks, I'm also leveling up a cleric as the 3rd character, to hopefully keep dogdog and jabober alive a little longer and take the sting out of deaths. Shaman/cleric hp buffs on two pets should make for some good meatshields, and the cleric pacifying will make pulls simpler, since crowd control is one of the major weaknesses of shaman/mage.

Good luck with whatever you choose, and remember, have fun. If you don't like a combo, try something else. There's so much variety in the classes, you're bound to find something you like.

Scalia
As for plvling, if you make a caster, unless you have a druid/mage to DS your toon, be rdy for a long walk to 51.

If he is making a Necro, then PLing with a Shaman is not that hard. Have the Necro tank for the pet for the first 30 levels, which will keep his melee (especially Defense) skills maxed. It gets a little easier when the Necro gets his DS - stick to fast hitting critters like the Needlites (sp?) in Echo Caverns. After 30, tanking gets a bit harder, but then you can just haste the Necro pet, slow the mob and watch the pet kill the mob while it's beating on the Shaman.

However, I would really recommend spending some time getting used to playing a Necro without powerleveling. They have their own set of tools, including Lich spells and DD/DOT lifetaps that make juggling HP and mana fun and challenging. Figuring out how you can split mobs with snare/FD, pet control, DD/DOT efficiency, healing (!) and so on can take a while.
