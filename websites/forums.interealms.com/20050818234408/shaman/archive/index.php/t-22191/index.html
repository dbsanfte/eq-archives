Grafsha McDaughts
Reading this post http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=22161 I was reminded of lots of similar posts as well as the tons of posts trying to rationalize solutions for SOE to consider that would make Shaman viable in groups once again.

History time: Original and Kunark tough content groups (dungeons) needed a tank, cleric, enchanter, shaman and dps/utility. If you wanted extra mana regen/best haste as well as superior crowd control to do more you had enchanter. If you wanted less incoming damage, melee buffs to add damage output and backup healing you had shaman. Cleric filled the role of increasing hitpoints as well as healing damage. Tank was meatshield. Dps was ... dps, kill the mobs faster. Utility was druid - they could take the place of cleric or shaman with heals/damage shield to compensate for lack of slowing. Ideally, with the the addition of a druid the ds contributed to fast killing.

Even with this group, there was downtime. You could only do so much before the cleric needed to med, the shaman needed to canni/rebuff, yada, yada. Experience was 2-fold: (1) naturally the increase in character experience to make the next level (not aa, there were none); (2) experiencing challenging game content with a group - generally a group of friends having fun.

You could take the above group makeup and remove each of the 3 primary support classes individually and still have a successful group. Their overall ability took a hit, but the group could still progress through content by adapting to their weakness.

So what really has changed? Velious expansion is where the devs starting attempting creativity in content design. Recall Skyshrinne version 1? Totally MR mobs that couldn't be mezzed, debuffed, slowed, nuked? That didn't last long. Orcs at the fort in EW, at the time, were monsters to fight and did, at the group level intended, definitely need a cleric as healer. To be fair, ToFS was compensation by giving a nice xp zone to those groups that didn't fit the "mold." Then the dev concept of 'difficulty' began its skewed course of introducing content that reflects very little on the core abilities of all the classes. Beyond the dynamic duo of tank/cleric or the holy trinity of tank/cleric/enchanter content no longer balances itself against the variables of a potential group makeup. Were it not for "mudflation" and the ease of getting great gear each class would feel the skewed balancing/lack of balance a lot earlier than GoD content - in encounters based on the level they were intended for. The concept of 'difficulty' is way out of whack.

So, what is my point (er, Chapter 2:) )? Rationally, and shaman are a rational bunch (though high strung at times), we continue to point out the problems in the game based on what the content is. The SOE wall we pound our heads against is their argument that classes are "balanced as intended." Frankly, this is fundamentally true ... though I would argue classes are so overlapping and over-balanced already that any more would just turn the mess into a quagmire. Shaman are the poster child for what goes wrong when balancing/adjusting classes to fit too many roles. Don't get my wrong here, I am NOT saying shaman have been adjusted so much that their balance against other classes is skewed. Just the opposite - shaman lack of balance/expansion progression while others have adapted/progressed into shaman abilities points out the inherent flaw in 'balancing.' SOE history clearly defines 'balancing' as making the lines between class individuality blurred to the point of invisibility. With the exception of one single class: clerics. Go figure.

So what to do? Content. I would like to know who this 'majority of players' is that demands encounters be 'quick.' Personally, if I want quick I can jump on my game cube, PS2 or XBox. Don't get me wrong. 10-15-20-30 minute fights belong against only boss mobs. On the other hand, 25-45 seconds fights are just damn silly. That is how long yard trash mobs in PoFear/PoHate lasted on raids with 30-50 folks ... many, many moons ago. Named fights lasted a few good minutes, and that took work/coordination to do that fast. CT and Inny took a damn long time ... and you actually thought you accomplished something when you won!

But I digress ...

Back in the day, a group working its way into a Seb camp (or holding a Seb camp) worked together to get there. Mobs took a minute+ to kill. Mob dps required the cleric (or rather, primary healer) to heal, but wasn't so massive that a druid/shaman combo couldn't work together to fill the healing role also ... given the shaman was still primarily there to slow/debuff and the druid was there to keep the tank alive until slowed. Without a shaman, an enchanter could fill the slower role - which was tough since they were also doing lots of CC. That was the balance though. Without key classes, roles could overlap satisfactorily to work the content by adapting. Note the difference here - "roles could overlap satisfactorily." Not replace completely, but rather, work in a pinch if that was all you had available.

This is still very much how the players approach the game today. It is still very viable as an approach up until serious upper 50's/mid 60's content. Note, I said serious content ... challenging, level appropriate content. 60ish though, this approach loses its viability. Except for clerics. This becomes the realm of cleric dominance. This is not a slam against clerics ... rather a slam against SOE game design. The one class that stands out above all others as being the most significantly indespensible are ... clerics. Everything is balanced around them. This is where the game gets stupid.

Since clerics can adapt to any current content (and 95% or more of it is basically trivial to a top end cleric) the dev approach to content design has become focused on their limitations only. Other class contributions have become minimized/marginalized against this. As long as the cleric can keep the dps folks alive, all is good ... or so seems the new SOE game plan. To make content "challenging" has become more concerned with "how to make clerics cast more, faster and efficiently" than the group sense of taking on a mob and kicking its butt with adaptability, initiative and utilizing varying group talents/skills. It is just blast healing/blast killing. A few years ago I used to have fun doing this type of encounter ... in DOOM and DOOM II.

So, without any parsing or statistics from me, I will leave my argument that it is not so much the classes that need to be revamped/changed/yada, yada as much as the content needs to be adjusted to allow each class, in a variety of combinations, the ability to face ALL avialable content, adapt to it, and ENJOY PLAYING because they CAN win in it!

From a shaman perspective, I say this somewhat tongue-in-cheek. At this point in the game our "buff" abilities are moot. Were content changed so that I could, at this level of the game (with the heals/debuffs available to me now), contribute by slowing, back up healing, dealing dps over the long term (IN A GROUP) ... well, I would be a happy camper more than willing to let clerics have their radically dominant healing as well as not having to worry about casting 4-6 melee buffs. How can SOE do this?

Just make encounters such that over the duration of the fight, or series of trash fights, that I am needed to slow/debuff the mobs or the cleric finds they are using way too much mana to counteract damage incoming. Instead of giving me better heals ... make clerics need my slow in their groups. Mine. Not enchanters, not beastlords ... hell, not even necro slow ... mine. The others already have dominant slots in groups (CC and dps.) (Yes, this is a selfish shaman perspective and, yes, other classes also have viable problems with newer content ... for sake of this board my focus is shaman atm. Sorry to you other folks.)

If this can't be done, then take the time to prove to me the justification for the arcade-style battles. Who the hell is this 'majority' who want 'faster killing' content? No one I know. Like most of SOE perceptions of the game ("majority of players 30-50 range" - LMAO) I fail to believe this is true, or that they have CLUE-1 what is going on with the player base. It is clear SOE has no clue that what these games are supposed to be is FUN, challenging, FUN, rewarding (risk/reward balanced), FUN. Did I mention FUN also? Not 10 minute fun, or 15 or even 30 ... but an enjoyable experience that can last for years. An ... pardon the play on words here ... EXPERIENCE!!

(rant off ... I can go back to bed now)

veeshanMurtak
PoP has seriously changed the way this game is played. Before PoP most content, even in raids, was endurance content.

There was next to nothing (even few bosses) that simply walked all over you. What got people killed was running out of mana, multiple adds or overpulls or respawns catching up with you.
I distinctly remember paladins healing just fine in Sebilis, even on the shrooms. The just went out of mana doing so. I remember taking my time with heals because everyone could take a couple rounds of melee.

Fast forward to PoP. Just about anything is capable of killing you, fast. The first priority is not efficiency but survival. You die not because of mana mismanagement but from such things as slow resists on single pulls, a single MOB getting aggro on the cleric and the likes. Overpulls are "more then we can offroot / mez in 5 seconds".

Before PoP a cleric was a better healer because he could keep the group alive with less mana and because he could deal better with adds / overpulls.

After PoP a cleric is a better healer because he can deal with the damage of an unslowed MOB.

That means that any class must have at least one "impact" capability that is useful for a single fight. Mana efficiency does not cut it - that is what you look at after you sorted out the classes that will not help you survive. Slow does not cut it, not when mitigated. Spending that much time means you should better lock down the MOB for good.

The only shaman abilities I can think of that fit the bill are haste and VP. Druids have similar problems, having heals that barely fit this category. Clerics on the other hand have CH, excellent emergency heals, group CH, divine arbitration and DA. All of these can turn high intensity fights around.

Now, you all knew this before - why is this important? It matters because prior to PoP emergency abilities were just that - for emergencies. Post PoP they are par for the course. Every MOB is an emergency. You do not time CH to be efficient, you start it ahead of time and hope it will land. You throw spot heals to barely keep up with the incoming damage, use tools like divine arbitration when it doesn't, use abilities like VP as much to circumvent the spell recast time as for the effect.

That does not mean that this style of game is worse. It just means that classes need to be redesigned to have emergency abilities, abilities that can, by themselves, turn the fight around. Efficiency we can think about after that is done.
