But there's no way to break new ground then to innovate and dream is there?

Let me contribute, and perhaps hijack the thread with my modification of the idea. I'm going to take it in another direction, but keep the same feel. Check it out

Spirit Channeling 5/10/15

Description:
This grants the shaman the ablilty for a short time to fuel his spells with supernatural haste, and negating the cost of the spells at the cost of the shaman's life force.

How it works:
When you activate this AA, after a casting time (2 secs maybe?) for the next 7/16/30 seconds. All spells you cast will have a 30/45/60% spell haste applied to it. The lowest a spells casting time can be reduced to is 1 second. All recast time for spells during the period of time in which this AA is active is reduced to 0. No mana cost is deducted from the shaman's mana pool for any spells casted by the shaman during this period of time. After a successfully casted spell, the shaman would instead take damage equal to twice the amount of the mana cost of the spell. When this AA is active it will take up a buff slot (and operate much like Quiesscense does) and may end the effect by clicking it off or simply waiting for it to expire.

So what do you think? This idea incapsulates both Kurth's idea of trading life for damage (although not so directly), but offers much more versitility then the original suggestion. I imagine that in a situation where you need bring some dps, you could activate this AA and begin chain casting Velium Strike, or use it to get that incoming crowd of mobs malos/slowed, act as super spot heals for a few seconds, etc, etc. Of course I believe the idea could use some tuning, but that's the whole reason I decided to share it with you all.

Please, critique but don't flame or bring in any other trolling to the thread.

Indalecio Galronte
@Sownow:

I see your concern, and I've been thinking about what I would set the ablility refresh rate to. I'm trying to find a number that would be low enough to go for maximum recycle and regularly improve dps or the like, but yet be high enough so you'd have to think about saving it for severe situations. If I had to take an initial stab at refresh time, I'd say 15 minutes sound like a number that meets these needs.

As for the 30 second max duration being overpowered, you may be right. What do you think the maximum duration of the AA should be? If I had to pick a number, I'd tenitively say 20 seconds would be a comfortable maximum.

Keep in mind that you're taking damage from every spell you cast with a mana cost during this time. If you have to heal yourself, you have to waste at least 1 second doing so plus the damage is factored in AFTER a spell is successfully casted. So let's say I activated the AA, and Velium Striked a couple times. I want to heal myself now, so I'm going to Tnarg's Myself. I get healed for 1300, but now since the spell has been successfully casted, I take damage equal to twice the mana cost of the spell. Tnarg's mana cost is 450, and 450*2 is 900 damage. So, I've wasted precious active AA time to heal myself for a net total of (1300-900=400) 400 hit points. I haven't even brought myself another cast of Velium Strike with that (which comes out to 600 damage per cast).

The only other way I'm going to heal myself is with Quiescence. Ok, I cast Q, it lands, I take 468 damage. Q will be able to heal me at that point, but I'm wasting time waiting for the ticks to hit. Unless I moderate my casting and/or Chain Tnarg's myself to a comfortable life total, I'm going to have to be careful. I cannot just blast away until the time runs out, I must be strategic.

Of course, someone could always take up the task of healing me during the active time of my ability, however, that's one person healing me that was supposed to be doing something else. The easiest comparison I can draw would be the Enchanter's charm ablility. Chanters can charm mobs for massive dps, but it's very hard for the chanter to keep the mob under control by his/herself. The group, or raid realizes this and aids the chanter by debuffing the resists of the mob, snaring the mob, rooting/tanking/mezzing it when the charm breaks, etc. Playstyle is altered because the benefit of having the Chanter charm is worth more then the effort of helping the Chanter with the Charm upkeep. Thus, having another person/s heal me during the active time of this ability doesn't make it broken. It merely streamlines the process of using the ablilty.

I devised this AA to be workable in a solo, group and raid setting, with obvious strenghs and weaknesses given your setting, and current situation with gear, focus effects, AA's and playstyle. The more your shaman progresses in terms of these attributes the better this AA will be.

Thank you for your comments. Others?
