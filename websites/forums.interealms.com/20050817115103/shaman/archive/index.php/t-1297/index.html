The trick is to have lots of hps before zoning in. If you know you are going to try to break it solo, get an Aego ahead of time, or at least a good couple of buffs to help you out. The HTs are gonna hurt if two skellies are up, so be prepared to self heal.

Usually tho, it's one HT skelly and one Ravenous Gnawer (sperm thingy). Also, if you zone in and dont move, you should be able to turn about and see where stuff is before aggroing anything. Make sure to lead with a slow, and use root liberally.

Oh, one other trick. U need lev up for this tho. If yer getting owned, jump off the edge and float down to the top of the house. You can camp out here, come back, and then float under the house roof to the glowing red ball which when clicked will port u back to entr to give it another try.

Actually a good strategy for a low level like you would be to do that right after you take the HTs. Then all you need to do is heal up and port back up to engage the now HT-less mobs. Without HT they really aren't bad.

Again the key is to have enuf hps to live thru the opening volley. If you don't, don't zone in.

I started going to HS at level 54, and I had a terrible time. I died sooo many times in 2 days, that I was ready to throw in the towel. Although I kept at it and eventually found a strategy that worked for me.

Zone in buffed to the gills, including levitate, sow, and invis. Also have gate memed at all times, never unmem this spell even for a second.

If 2 skeletons are up, be prepared for 2 harmtouches, however the max harmtouch from one of them is in the 500's and most of them are in the 400's. First thing to do is root them, slow them, then heal yourself. Its not fun to do this, especially at 54, and especially with no experience doing it before, once you get it down, then you are good to go though.

There are 2 problems with fighting here:
-Low hp mobs will run, and they ALWAYS run to the east wing, where you dont want them to.
-The repop on the 2 zone in mobs makes soling a pain in the ass because of the harmtouch, and the downtime it adds.

Here is a way you can deal with both of these problems.
1. Time the spawns, dont pull a golem if you are about to have a repop, you never know if you will get an add, or a runner. repop time is 21 mins if i remember correctly.

2. Always pull and fight to the northwest corner of the zone in. This will make the mobs walk all the way around the outer edge before the hit the east stairs. This gives you time to root, and reroot if it gets resisted (which WILL happen).

Single pulling at level 54 is hard, but you can do it about half the time. This is a dangerous place to solo, but the exp is well worth the risk. You can expect to die a few times, until you perfect your technique, and learn what to expect. Be ready for an emergency, and be ready to gate if you get an add you can't handle. I higly recomend using the level 14 root, because it can be cast on low mana, and it is the least likely to be resisted (i think).
