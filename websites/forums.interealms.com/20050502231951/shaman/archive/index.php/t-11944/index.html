A group can have an enchanter or beastlord slowing, but most still want a cleric to heal. However capable they are, a druid or shaman is only a last resort.

Kunark brought the rise of the enchanter. Crowd control in KC and Sebilis was so important, an enchanter would get a group instantly.

With Velious, the warrior/rogue/cleric trio became the top 3, other classes were necessary, but melees were gaining power.

After Luclin came out, with archery AA, rangers came out of their Kunark doldrums and became very popular. They still haven't lived down the "ranger down" jokes, which is probably the only reason there aren't even more rangers around, but rangers today are not the paper tanks they once were.

With PoP, and hard hitting mobs with lower HP, the shaman became more visible as a desired class. But to my view, the shaman has never been undesirable. These days, though, there are more of us around, more shaman alts becoming mains, and more shaman bots. There are more cleric bots too, for similar reasons.

The thing is, when you need certain classes for your group - a tank, a healer, a slower - then if you can't get one, you struggle to form a group. Even a shaman or cleric bot can make the difference between being able to get exp and not... or at least, convincing people to try. It is often the less desirable classes that start these characters to 2-box with.
