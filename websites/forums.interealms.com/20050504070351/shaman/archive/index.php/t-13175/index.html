Slurm
At 65 you will get a LOT less resists. And exp in pov moves pretty well at 65. You can chew thru mobs at an amazing rate, and rack up the aa.

On the other hand, I agree.. canni 5 will be the biggest boost to your power that you will see. I would get that first, then level to 65, then farm all the aa you want.

My guild has formed the opinion that for casters and priests level 65 is more important than AA points. Higher level = less resists, more mana, and the chance to use better spells (if you have them). For Melee the AA is more important. A 62 melee character with all their offensive/defensive aa skills is more powerful than a 65 without the aa.

Its a generalization, and there are exceptions (like canni 5 and mgb) but overall it does seem to fit.. at least for us.

Groz
If you are killing Wasteland mobs (Golems, Crawlers and stuff) or Razorfiends, you'll still be within the level 5 bonus in PoV at 65. The exp is still very good.

However, frogs and undead skeletons are lousy experience since you don't get the exp bonus. I duo'd a fair amount at east frogs in PoV, but the exp has definitely tailored off at 65.

Especially If you are the primary slower for your guild, after you get Cann V, it's best to shoot for 65. As I got higher level, frogs seemed to resist less often. Nothing worse than try to trying to slow bounce , both on a Raid Mob and on exp'ing mobs. With adds, you don't have time to malo and slow. And some mobs are immune to root. Having slow land may save you from a wipe.

I didn't see a big change in spell power from 63 to 64. (Only have velium strike, blood of ultor, talisman of alacrity and malicious decay). However, having Quiesence and Ferine Avatar was a huge difference.

I was tempted to de-level initially after ding 65 since i couldn't duo at my favorite spot in PoV. But playing without those 2 spells, it would just be too painful.

However, at 65 you really are restricted to tier 2+ planes for exp. I basically have 3 types of playing sessions. Grinding for AAs in the tier 2+ planes, Questing or farming for stuff (pretty much don't expect the exp bar to move for that time), or go raiding with the guild.
Barliman
I would go along with what most people have said, get MC3 and Canni5 and then move on. I really didn't notice a huge difference in slows between 63-65 when raiding. I went from 63-65 within about the last 2-3 months and in that time we've stayed pretty much in NToV and BoT towers so it could very well be different in other zones. I did however notice a huge difference in valor experience groups. Before 65 it seemed I'd bounce about 60-70% of my slows if I didn't malo first but at 65 I'd say that dropped to more like 30%. I still malo on incoming but if we have adds and I'm in a hurry just to get agro to park if nothing else I'll slow first and VP.

So anyway, personally I would go with MC3, Canni5 and then 65. The difference in slows just wasn't as huge as I thought it would be but it is still obviously noticeable and it sounds like others have had different experiences. I guess I just used to be very lucky with slows at 62-63 or I'm just very unlucky at 65. Also, the rate of AA between 62-65 isn't that huge. At 65 I started to pay more attention to what other people in the group were getting AA exp wise and it seems to be about 6% per level down to 62. So basically in the time it took me to get 100% AA if I were 64 I would have gotten 106%, if I were 62 I would have gotten 118%. Either way 18% is about 20-30 minutes in a decent group so not a huge deal in my opinion.
