Jinheim
I never use totems in pvp except for earthbind and grounding. Everything else is a waste of mana and it is too easy for the person to just hit it once and it goes away. As you get higher level you'll find that people have learned that shaman totems only have 5hp, and they will just kill them.

Against warriors and rogues, I just introduce them to the frost shock dance and kite them until they are low, then finish them off with melee.

Against casters, I charge them and drop a grounding and earthbind totem. Your melee can take down a caster pretty quickly. I recommend dagger with windfury proc on it because the interrupts will extend their cast times to a point where you can use earth shock to completely shut down their casting, especially if you have the fast shocks talent (which I highly recommend for pvp). Also, remember to purge them to get rid of buffs and shields. This is vital against priests. Their bubble goes away with one purge and then you can just annhilate them. Ditto for druids because it gets rid of their regen spell.

Hunters I treat as casters and melee them while ignoring the pet. If you have the improved lesser healing wave talent, you can heal yourself at will even when being hit by both the hunter and his pet. They just can't compete with that and are probably the second easiest class for me to kill (druids being the first).

Against paladins I do the frost shock dance and hope I have a mana potion handy because I will need it.
Walruss Touth
Learn the jump/shock technique:

While running away from a melee, jump, do at least 90 degree turn, frost shock and turn back while still in the air.

When you are jumping your forward momentum will continue so you are still headed in the opposite direction of the player you are kiting. Turning allows for line of sight so you can shock. It's kinda tricky but after practicing for a couple minutes on NPC's it should be become natural.

It makes melee characters extremely easy barring resists.

Against casters make sure you get close and circle them just when their spells are about to finish casting. This buys you more time and annoyes the crap out of them. Don't ignore your resistance totems either.

The best advice I can give about pvp, if you are not comfortable with it or unsure if you are good, is to simply practice.

Duel friends/guildmates. Duel groupmates while you are waiting for that last member or two to enter an instance or just random people at your local outpost.

temuchintehkhan
how you approach pvp depends a lot on how you've specced. the most important talents being: elemental mastery, nature's swiftness, improved nova, and improved ghost wolf. I was a shaman all through beta, and having been 60 on warsong pvp server for a good month now I have a few tips on what to do against a range of classes

shocks: be sure you have all your shocks up to date through all levels and handy on your tool bar. frostshock is your bread and butter against melee classes. although I'm partially specced enhancement, and an expert jouster/strafe dancer since my earliest day on zek in eq, following to guinevere in DAOC, your battles as a shaman in WoW are going to be won with your spells. for any melee class the way to go is to snare them with earthshock, drop a fire totem in melee range and kite them around. against any melee including paladin, you should NEVER lose if you keep your distance and kite. close in only to finish when they run.

earthshock is very very important and largely underutilized spell. in essence it's a nuke + shield bash that interrupts spell casting and silences them versus that spell school for a few seconds. for casters, particularly mages who can sheep from a distance, you CANNOT win without using this spell. typically I'll drop a grounding totem immediately to eat their poly or nuke, start a lighting bolt if i can squeeze it in and ES them when i see them start to cast. for anyone save a warlock, one bolt, one ES, a nova and maybe a swing or 2 = dead caster.

flameshock, I rarely use since it takes longer to do full damage and also since crit potential of the spell is worst. the one use is that it's out only dot. use it on rogues to keep them from stealthing and drop an earthbind.

as far which totems to use. ground is CRUCIAL. you cannot wind many fights without it. also, for an elemental build shaman like me, NOVA is crucial. i've often been jumped by 2 or more alliance (since alliance lvl 60 outnumber us like 4 to 1 on warsong) and quickly evened the odds by hitting EM + chain lightning, shocked the lower hp or lower level attacker, stomped and nova. many times this will KILL the lower HP toon and also have done significant damage to the remaining attacker. for multi-person fights. tremor is often crucial in the case of sleep etc.

Tarthug
I use a flexbar UI addon and have 48 buttons on my screen with my main combat buttons grouped in one area of the screen for less mouse movement and quicker actions in combat. This helps a lot.

As for tactics, alliance are totem nuts, and have totems as KoS. What I do is drop lower mana cost totems earlier in the fight, even those of questionable utility. I spread the totem drops out a little and melees are forced to waste combo/rage on them. Many of my opponents seem to suck at retargeting so while they waste time switching targets, killing the totem and switching back, I'm happily smoking them or healing myself.

Warstomp for Tauren is amazing. AoE stun that buys you time to self heal, ghost wolf if you're losing, or drop a 2 second nova totem to spook your foe with a burst of potent, but unsustainable, DPS. With a WS, nova and a frost shock combo (usually with a melee swing in the mix) towards the end of a fight my foe usually gives up or tries to run.

Purge is your friend. Purge gets rid of some of those paladin extra lives by dispelling seals. Purge is key on druids and warlocks. Purge is sweet goodness.

Mix up your shocks, frost to kite, earthshock on casters, frost shock to keep hunters in melee, but fireshock is the most mana efficient shock you have should you enter into a melee battle of attrition (not a situation of choice).

As for builds I put my build on a thread in the main WoW section under shaman talents. I split my points, shooting for a shock elemental/Imp GW enhancement/ and NS restoration build.
