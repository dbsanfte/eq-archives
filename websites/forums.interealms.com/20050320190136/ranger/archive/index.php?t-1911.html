I agree both that addictions will occur regardless of the source and that the source of the addiction itself is not the cause of the addiction. Nobody does something with the thought of "I'm going to become addicted to this." Most often it's a thought of "I'm only going to do this for a little while or infrequently. I won't become addicted." That's precisely(sp?) why addictions work the way they do. I'm not at all saying that it is anybody's responsibility to make an internet or a game less addictive. Addiction is a phenomenon that lies outside the realm of the details. Anything that is fun to do is addictive to some people.

That having been said, the message here is simply to take a look at yourself objectively once in a while and make sure that your favorite leisure activities, whether they're games or TV or gambling or Tiddly-Winks aren't causing problems with your job, marrage, schoolwork, etc. Most people "never saw it coming" simply because they were too caught up in the addiction to even realize there was a problem.

It's amazing to me what some people say isn't important in a marriage.

It's not important that we have the same friends, religion, hobbies, sleeping habits, etc. "What matters is that we love each other."

In most cases, love like that turns out to be mere infatuation.

It's not imperative that ALL those things match exactly, but to really live together and love for the rest of your life, it IS important that you at least have some common ground.

FRIENDS
If you don't have the same friends just because they don't know each other, that's one thing. My friends and my husband's friend had never had the chance to get together, but once they did, we had one big circle of friends, instead of his and hers.
If you can't STAND his friends, what makes you think you can live with him for the rest of your life? Birds of a feather... You can tell a lot about a person based on who his/her best friends are.

RELIGION
Not to get too deep into it, but assuming you plan to have kids, this can become a MAJOR issue. I'm not talking the difference between Cathoic and Lutheran. I'm talking Protestant-Pagan, Catholic-Athiest, etc. If you already don't like his best friends, and he has a completely different religion view from you, you're well on the road to marital hell.

HOBBIES (Often tied to SLEEPING HABITS)
He plays computer games all night. You believe in early to bed, early to rise. I hope you like spending your weekends alone.
You play tennis with this really cute young girl at your club, she seldom sees natural sunlight.
If you don't share interests in the things you do outside of obligations to work and family, can you really see the relationship lasting?

Why do people get into a marriage thinking that these "little things" don't matter? They aren't little things. People's beliefs and habits are part of who they are, and that extends to every part of the relationship.
