"The more mana the less downtime"

True. A shamanic corollary is "the more hit points the less downtime." You'll likely find the overwhelming opinion here is gonna be to not worry as much about wisdom, and load up on AC and HP gear, with a Fungi tunic leading the list.

I've gone both Wis, and AC/HP, now seem to have struck an OK balance between the 2. You will find downtime will decrease dramatically when you reach 54 and get Cann III (even though it can be a fizzle-fest). If you don't have Can II now, it may be worth your while; it certainly was for me. You'll be replenishing your mana pool in bigger chunks.

Maximum Wisdom before PoP AA: 255.

After Wis=200, there seems to be a bit of a diminishing return on Wis points vs mana. I believe the rule of thumb is 1 Wis = 10 mana at lev 60.

BlargX
Actually 1 wis = 10 mana at level 50, not 60.

A good thing to play around with is a mana calculator. Castersrealm.com and a few other places have ones. You type in your beginning wis/int and then the wis/int you have from mana, and you see how much mana you get versus your starting point. So add up your pieces and swap them in and out to see which give you the best bang for the buck. You'll find that very often items with mana on them are much more worthwhile than ones with wisdom, especially at lower levels, where wisdom does very little for you, but a few items with mana on them are really great. Good way to decide what to wear is to use those calculators until you get a feel for it. It's very proportional, you'll find. In other words, if level 30 is 3/5 of level 50, you'll find that 1 wisdom(10 mana) is only 3/5 as much at level 30(it equates to about 3 mana). At 60 or 65 you'll probably be max wisdom or fairly close to it anyway, or at least close enough for a shaman that you won't care. I didn't know that it scaled up again at 60; kinda thought it hadn't. Over 255 wisdom, though, and you only get 6 mana per wisdom point.

Kriel Darkbane
You are approaching the problem incorrectly. If your problem is long downtimes, a larger mana pool will NOT help you. All it will mean is that you can go slightly longer before running OOM, and then need to meditate for even longer before you're full mana again. What you need to do is become more efficient, either through using LESS mana or recovering mana (or hp which you can convert to mana) faster.

Options:

1) Get a horse, and exp in outdoor zones. On the horse you get meditating mana regen at all times that you are not moving or attacking - such as when you're casting or just standing there getting hit.

2) Get mana-free damage. The obvious examples are the Epic and the JBB (if not iksar), if you don't have them. With a horse you can effectively meditate while clicking the JBB - making the combination very valuable.

3) Regeneration! Get a fungi tunic if you're rich or lucky, and get an iksar ceremonial BP if you don't have one. Even 5 hp/tick regeneration is equivalent to FT 3 or so for a shaman. Very useful. A fungi is about FT 9 or 10 - huge.

4) Direct mana regen - flowing thought. Get an earring of the solstice (FT1) if you don't have one. They're relatively cheap. Get any other flowing thought items you can find/afford. Don't pay huge cash for FT unless you can get it more cheaply/efficiently than you can get a fungi. Getting a fungi is very hard if you're vah'shiir, as the vest is so rare and you can't use the tunic... but camping the vest might be a good plan regardless of race.

5) Higher AC - if you tank when you solo, then more AC will mean you get hit less and lose less HP - meaning you will be able to heal less and convert more of your HP regeneration into mana regeneration.

If you want less downtime, you need to use less hp/mana and recover hp/mana faster. The size of your mana/HP pools are almost irrelevant to that equation at L 53, as long as your pools are big enough to get the job done.

My shaman is L 50, with a cheap horse, a JBB, and an iksar ceremonial BP. He has almost zero downtime solo'ing in outdoor zones. I get about 20% of a level in an hour in BW (which will slow down when the levels slow down again at L 51). I can only dream of what it'll be like if I can add a fungi and/or Epic to that equation any time soon.

At L 60+ with Cann 5 a larger HP pool becomes a useful tool for efficiency, but until then it doesn't matter much.

BattleAxe
When soloing - reducing downtime can make hunting less tedious. There are other considerations. Do you want to maximize experience, maximize the 'thrill factor', or minimize downtime?

Maximizing experience doesn't necessarily mean minimizing downtime - consider:
10 minutes to kill MobX, but have no downtime
5 minutes to kill MobX with 2 minutes downtime
This is where you start balancing tanking while you slow, dot, and jbb; letting dogdog tank while you slow and jbb; or letting dogdog tank while you slow, root, dot, and jbb. If you're getting hit - you're losing hitpoints - no matter how much ac you have. You can minimize this loss via high ac, but you can eliminate it altogether by not getting hit. If dogdog is tanking - you may have to spend mana on healing, buffing, or regen. In your fighting - you have to decide where the break-even point is.

'Thrill factor' is different for different people, but I know I have a lot of fun fighting on the edge, so to speak. It isn't the most efficient way to play EQ to blow all your mana and be left with a bubble of health to kill one mob, but it can be fun.

General recommendations made above are still in play - maximize hp, ac, and mana in-flow. My addition is that you need to also evaluate your fighting style to fit what's important to you - max experience, min downtime, or max thrill.

Other quick suggestions for max effectiveness - if you are ever at full health and not full mana - hit canni. There is no reason a shaman should ever be at full health (unless there's an obsessive compulsive cleric in your group). If you are full health and full mana - refresh a buff and canni. Our buffs are constantly wearing down, and this is a way to make sure an important one won't fail you when you need it most.
