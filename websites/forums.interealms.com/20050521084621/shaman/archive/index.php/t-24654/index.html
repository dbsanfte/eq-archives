Giac
Shaman spells are the most resistable spells you will find in game.

A long time ago Shamans were penalized with that, because we had a spell line to lower a mob's resists. Other classes didn't have the ability to do that, so their spells were made harder to resist.
Fast forward today, many classes have some sort of debuff for the spells they are casting, yet they kept their innate resist modifications for their spell lines, while the Shaman's damage spells are still as resisted as ever.

You aren't doing anything wrong, it's just how it is for Shamans. Resists are the most limiting factor when it comes to a Shaman's damage out put(the other being aggro which is relative to the equipment of the people you play with). Unfortunately, there are no foci available in game that innately reduce spell resists(with the exception of the Spititkin Tunic's effect, which I have yet to see any parses of).

You need to find out what those dragons have for resist. Cast Malos, Insidious Decay (for disease debuffing). If you use disease dots they last longer, When you use Bane and Anathema along with the poison dots, BoN and BoS you can drop the HP down quite a bit. Honestly I always have loaded: Malos, Root, Slow, Quin, canni 4, Blood of Saryrn, Bane, Anathema and Breath of Ultor (i have the 9th spell gem from AA).

I usually use these when soloing as slowing, in my opinion helps a ton when they inevitably break root. The great thing about Bane, Anathema, Blood of Saryrn, and Breath of Ultor is they don't have a DD component to them so there is no crazy breaks in Root.

Give the Malos, Malosinia, Insidious Decay a shot, then play with what dots land on him most. Just keep using those dots and you should be in good shape and still have time to canni4 canni5 when the time is right.
