Instead of multiclassing, I'd just like to see greater specialization. Not the aaxp type, which isn't really specialization at all, because given enough time and effort, you can have all the abilities. No, I mean real specialization, where you choose an area of focus for your character, and get better at it, at the expense of other possible foci.

e.g. Ranger specialization choices.

Path of Nature's Servant - improvement to the effects and durations of buffs and/or heals, access to new buffs not otherwise available.

Path of the Stormcaller - improved nukes, access to ice based spells.

Path of the Blademaster - increasing damage bonus to offhand, innate atk bonus, new melee discs.

Path of the Marksman - increased chance to hit, new bow discs.(For example, the ability to make a 'called shot' for double damage once every 2 minutes, which wouldn't be overpowering, but could make for an amazing pulling tool solo and in small groups).

Like spellcaster's specialization or tradeskills, the early levels of specialization could be equally pursued, but past a certain level, you would need to choose one. Your choice would of course depend upon your playstyle; while PoNS would be a moderately weak spec for a solo or single group player, being able to cast a better CoP, SoN, FoN, or CoE than a ranger of any other spec would be an amazing asset for your guild.

The main thing in my opinion though is having to choose. The biggest problem I see with aaxp is that it only promotes diversity in the short term - eventually we will all have all the useful abilities.
