This has been discussed before, I know. But I have yet to see all the arguments plus and minus discussed. Maybe this needs to go into the FAQ.

I just hit 60 and am pondering whether to continue levelling, work exclusively on AA's, or some combination. The factors as I understand them:

Higher levels mean fewer resists. I can't land anything on a desert giant in PoS, for example. This is a very compelling argument for me.

Higher levels mean I resist more spells cast on me by mobs.

Higher levels mean more HP/mana and better spells, to the extent that I can get parchments/runes for those spells. I have almost all the 61-62 spells right now, so that argues for levelling at least to 62.

Higher levels mean little or no XP in old-world zones. Since soloing for XP is difficult in PoP zones, this ties me to the LFG command. On the other hand, an hour spent LFG in PoT and an hour grouped in PoS is going to net me significantly more experience than I could expect in two hours soloing in Chardok or Sebilis.

More AA's mean more functionality. Cann V changes the class dramatically and putting XP full into AA will give me Cann V faster. In addition, MGB and RC make me more valuable to my guild.

AA points come faster at level 60 than they would at 61+, killing the same mobs. (On the other hand, if I move on and kill tougher mobs at 61+, I may be able to AA at the same rate per hour spent, Idunno).
Thurdar
I find it pretty sad that you even have to ask that question. IMO, there's something badly flawed with a game design where whether to level or not is even an issue. But it is, and I've ranted enough on the subject so here's my take on your question.

Advantages of lvling to 65:
- Your character will be more powerful, more quickly if you're at least flagged for PoV/S and lvl to 65 before anything else.
- Don't have malo, torpor? It's probably cheaper and easier to get the lvl 65 versions.
- Want to join a high end raid guild? lvl matters.
- You'll get AA faster at 65 just because of the zones you can hunt in.

Advantages of staying 60-61:
- Better solo exp (or at least more places to solo for decent exp).
- You can actually get decent exp outside of PoP. Even if I can double the exp in PoP, there's still something to be said for actually doing a fun dungeon crawl as opposed to pulling singles in PoV for the rest of your eq career.

Personally, I don't consider any of the above all that important. The main question you need to ask yourself is; 'what lvl are my friends and where are they hunting'?

Since I have malo and torpor and don't have cann5 yet, I don't mind staying 61 for a while. I'm basically waiting to see whether this next expansion addresses the issues I have with EQ atm and that will determine whether I start to worry about putting time into getting flagged and leveling or bow out after 4 years anc concede that while it's been a fun ride, it's time to move on.
