Dargathi
Firstly some background Info,

Velium Hounds when they die either spawn a Velium hound, or a Glacier Mastadon
Glacier Mastadons either spawn a Mastadon or a Bronti
Bronti's either spawn a Bronti or a Crag Wyrm/Drake
Crag's spawn 1/4 Hounds. Drakes spawn an Ice Burrower.

Each mob can spawn a copy of it's self up to 4 times.

As a ranger you have two essential tools to make your life easy. Snare and Panic Animal.

Faction

There are two factions that play a part in Western Wastes. Claws of Veeshan, and Ring of Scale. try to ensure you are at least dubious to both when in Greater Wolf Form (or wolf form) CoV can be fixed by killing in Kael, GD and EW, RoS can be fixed by killing in Mistmore. RoS isnt as important as only a few of the dragons are on this faction. CoV is vital or you will be getting adds all over the place.

This guide has been written with COV in mind. it is possible to hunt the CoV mobs for great XP but thats for another guide ;o)

Buffs.

Given that this is not a level specific guide I'm not going to mention specific buffs. I would adives you buff yourself with ATK and regen in mind, also ensure you use GWF for the speed, and keep levitate memed. I generally hunt in WW for 3 or 4 days before going anywhere, and use 5 or 6 stacks of bat wings.

Targets.

The first mobs on your list should be the Velium Hounds, and Rogue Dire wolves. The reason being these mobs will agro on you from a fair distance. they are KOS to everyone, and a pain in the neck when they add. So scour the zone in a Z patten picking up any wolves you find.

Technique.

Firstly always travel Camoed and levitated. This avoids any nasty bumps on the head, and helps you avoid any unwanted friends.

With the Dogies, get to about max snare range, and pull with snare. now 90% of the time this is also inside their agro range, if snare doesent stick dont panic, hit Attack and try the snare again.

Once the Mutt is snared back up to max Panic range. then hit panic animal. once the animal is paniced then go chop it into tiny little pieces.

Now sometimes you will see pack's of up to 4 Mutt's. this is because when a cragwyrm is killed it can spawn up to 4 wolves. splitting them is easy. Dont bother with Harmony, the max range of harmony is inside agro range of the dogies.

For this get to max bow range, and shoot the closest one to you. Remember stay camoed untill you fire your shot. now usually you will get one Doggie. however sometimes you will get all 4. if this happens dont panic. hit snare straight away, the idea here is to make distance ASAP. assuming no failures thats one mob taken care of, run like hell for a while till you have distance on the remaining 3 dogies, select a new target and hit snare. Rince and repeat untill all 4 mobs are snared. this should give you distance between each mob.

Now we want to get all 4 dead, and not have to zone. If you have survived the pack this long then you can do it.

There are two approaches to this.

Approach 1 (the one i use) Fear each mob, chop one to death. it's complex, hard to remember which one is which fear but it's ddoable.

Approach 2. Run like hell in a fixed direction (say north) camp out. when you come back the 4 doggies will be standing where ever they were when you camped out. Snare has a habbit of stopping them from wandering off. Repull and snare etc one target, and bob's yer uncle.

After all the Hound's are dead and looted, then begin to work on the Other animals. I prefer to get the Mastodons first as they give the greater XP return. then go for the Bronti's.

Approach 3 If you have Endless Quiver, then you can bow kite all 4. pack them up as if you were quad kiting them, and simply launch arrows into the pack, your damage will be distributed amongst all 4 mobs..

Once one begins to run, simply circle and finish that mob off, maintaining ground on the other 4.

The risk here is ensnare running out, but hey, there is no fun without risk. (my best so far is 7 doggies using this trick took em a little over an hour with EQ and AM1)

Note Iceshackle can be snare/bow kited even after the resist changes, Stronghorn and Tantor have yet to fall to my bow.

Make sure you either have EQ, or lots of arrows before attempting this ;o)
