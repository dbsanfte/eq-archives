Some guilds are luck guilds. If you get lucky and show up to a raid that has a good drop for you, grats you get to random. Or if you are the only one there, then grats you get to loot! Yay lucky you.

Some guilds are merit guilds. If shaman A has been with the guild for a year and comes to events lots and is the primary shaman... well then they get the droppable loot first. Since they are the highest level they will be the primary slower and need better gear to take beats. Personally I think it is pretty crappy if a person comes to raids and happens to miss the 1 raid that drops an item they need and it goes to someone who has been in the guild a short time and has contributed far less.

Another type of guild is DKP/points style guild. Then you buy your gear with points earned from raiding.

I would strongly suggest you join a non-merit loot type of guild. If you get yourself worked up about loot, can't see the big picture about what gear helps who best and other merit related issues then you should leave. Merit loot assignments work best in groups of laid back people who trust their guildleaders and officers/loot council. I'm not saying you are a bad person for wanting the pants. However, throwing a big fit over the pants will just put you in a bad light and since merit looting is an entirely human equation, well it does not bode well for your future loot prospects. And it obviously makes you unhappy and this is a game, so why put yourself in a situation that makes you unhappy?
