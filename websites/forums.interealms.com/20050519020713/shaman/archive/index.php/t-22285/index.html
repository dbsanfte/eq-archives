I'm not disagreeing; however, the people that spend the marketing dollars are completely different creatures than those who spend the development dollars. And I'm sure Ongoing Maintenance is a separate sub-budget from Future Initiatives.

I work in IT in Telecommunications. I have seen a three-person team frantically working around the clock to fix one code problem while a 10-person team assigned to another project takes the day off for 'team building' (at the pub) because their project is on schedule.

It doesn't make sense to outsiders (or even most insiders), but that's just how it works. If Fred's Department supports production, and they have a particular problem, they need to fix it with internal resources. Even if Mary's team developed and QAd the thing initially; her team is already coding up the next release.

This might not be how Sony works - but I'd be very surprised if that was the case. Once a company gets big enough, people worry about their own fiefdoms instead of the end product - because they get penalized or rewarded based on their own fiefdoms more directly than they do based on the end product.

There's only one exception I've seen: Billing. If the billing system is down, and revenue stops coming in, you'd better believe everyone is going to be working together to fix it.
