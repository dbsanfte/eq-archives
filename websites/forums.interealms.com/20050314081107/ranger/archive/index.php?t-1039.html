Now the mega cool trick I've found out. I can't tell if this was game designed or if it is a bug... anyhow SPOILER WARNING so highlight the incoming text at your own risk.

[COLOR=black]First by mousing over your character you can view the remaining duration of buffs or any summoned pets you have up.

Second when using buff spells you can increase the timer that they last for by repeatedly casting the same buff on yourself or your target. For instance Charged Fist in Nature magic... simply cast it upon yourself multiple times to have a duration lasting in the thousands of seconds.

My favorite way to handle this is when you come accross a mana fountain to stand in it and then do all your buffs up til you start losing mana. This means the mana fountain has been drained for a time but now you have LONG lasting buffs. Or start gathering all those mana potions you have been leaving behind after collecting about 6 or so do a full series of buff and use up the potions for something useful.

Also warning... if you advance in Skill level of the magic type that your buff is in and you recast the buff it wipes the timer clean cause you are now casting a higher version of the buff[/COLOR]

Working as designed. Development decided upon this for bandwidth performance reasons from what I read.

One tip I can recommend is for archers. On single-player, set them to attack the strongest. Since they attack slow and then you gotta wait for the arrow to land (assuming they don't move) you should have em attack the strongest. This doesn't seem to create aggro problems.

Donkey control - when your donkey gets attacked he will not go where you want him to. ie, you cant get him to run toward your tanks so they can take the heat off him. The mule will take about 2-3 steps toward where your clicked and then turn around. So click, let him move, pause it, click again, un pause, let him move, pause it, wash rinse, repeat.
