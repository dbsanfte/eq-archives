After experiencing the encounter to the near end, I have come up with a suggestion or 2.

FIRST: what seemed to work; Mezzing then debuffing the 6 mezzables (very nicely done btw).

Dodging the AE. More people need to move though (save for the ENC and the rot healers and tank) A lot of DPS were still in AE range when it was called...makes for hectic trying to cure people...lots of confusion. People need to move more (but over all not bad)

SUGGESTIONS:

First: After all 6 mezzables were locked down, trying to get back to keep them slowed became a giant pain in the ass to run back, slow them all and then get back for the next pull. The solution that I found was have the Kick ass enchanters slow their own mobs once we started on the TM mobs. (BTW...very aweseom job enchanters.../bow)

Second: Having 3 full sham on the raid would have been ok IF we had a better plan of attack from the beginning. These mobs are HIGHLY resistant and with out the tash from the chanters, its even harder to land anything (including malosinia after malos has landed), getting SINIA ON IS IMPARITAVE!!! It also needs to STAY ON THE MOBS just like slow. SO here is a little solution that I think might work (this is from the TMs on):

This is for a 3 sham encounter btw:

Till the first 3 TM are locked down, the sham are doing pretty much their usual sham thing. But also keeping the TMs slowed while waiting for INC.

After #3 TM is locked this is how my suggestion works:

Sham #1: Keep TM 1-3 Malos(inia) and slowed once locked down, that is your primary duty..and curing clerics as needed.

Sham#2: Keep 4-5 malos(inia) and slowed, help with #3 and cureing when needed. (so TM #3 will have 2 sham on it just to be safe and make double sure both sham have enough time to keep their TM slowed with out having to worry...'@#%$ my TM 2 is resisting everything...I dont have time to get to #3TM before slow wears off')

Sham#3: Take 6th TM, then help as needed with cures and slows on other TM mobs, and the general raid as well. Having 1 sham on the 6th TM INC will add an extra few secs of un slowed...if that is a prob then sham #2 can probably slow like ususal. That would mean though that #2's TMs slow might wear off.

From my experience till we had #11 locked down, keeping all the TMs slowed wasnt bad. Resists made it hard, but untill that 11th TM, slow never wore off even with resists, clerics stayed cured for the most part and everything was ok. Doing that way though is high risk, and not recommended again.

IN A NUT SHELL:

Sham#1: Keep TM 1-3 Malos(inia) and slowed while curing

Sham#2: Keep TM 2-5 Malos(inia) and slowed, while curing and helping to keep TM3 slowed as well.

Sham#3: Alone on INC 6 with the malos and slow (mages will sinia like usual), while curing and helping with other TM slows should it become neccesary.

That covers all TM with slow, and clerics with cures.

I have also found a way to run and dodge the AE while slowing the TM mobs. Keep the TM on the water edge (as close as is safe) and run as close to the temple thing as is safe. This will keep the sham from getting AEd while running and slowing between mobs. With extended range I never found it truly necessary to get in AE range to slow (or I was just lucky and dodged it a lot). I did experiment with seeing if being close to the TM was helpful in landing anything (in my vain attempts to reduce the resist of slow)...it didnt do squat, so I stayed away from the AE as much as possible.

If you can understand what I said, this seems like a sham plan that will probably work. if you have any suggestions to add, or anything to point out...please do so. I hope that I made it clear enough

We did the rathe with only three shaman and once with two shaman. We had one shaman back in the cave with the enchanters and bards keeping the mezzed ones debuffed.

Really if you have three shaman just split the mobs up two per person or even three per person. The last one you don't really need to worry about all that much. It dies fast so you really only have to worry about the other 5.

Spacing and positioning will be a factor to the your strategy. I don't know where you space your TM mobs at, but it will be a factor on ease of range.

My suggestions on doing it....

1st is avoid the AE it only sucks your mana down. Slow using lev out on the water or even across the water. Use Radiant Cure and Remove Curse for cures. Do not use Remove Greater Curse its more mana cosumption.

Regarding remove curse. Most priest classes can get it. Make sure all your clerics, druids, paladins, and shaman have it. That is four classes that can use it. Most will be healing, but they can cure themselves if they must.

Have your shaman with the largest mana pool take the mobs that are tank mezzed first. Have them keep malos and turgurs on the mob. The new GoD slow is a great addition to fast slowing if you have it.

If you can, get full tributes for mana consumption and stay on a horse it helps with mana regen. If you are grouped with a cleric or druid have them set up a radiant cure rotation.

Other than that its pretty much up to the tanks not to die. Have necros feeding the clerics and help spot heal the tanks.

It wasn't a problem for us doing it with three shaman, but it depends on your strat. We killed the last two tank mezzed mobs within seconds of each other.

On incoming have one shaman Malos and one slow from across the water with extended range you can do this successfully. Reason I say from across the water is because it generally saves you from dying. Will slow the mob down. Only do this if you have a knight catching the mobs on inc. Slow right after they get aggro.

The only other problem after that is timing your kills on the mezzed mobs so you don't get hit with the AE's.

Good Luck...

The biggest problem with rathe is their ability to randomly remove a debuff for no reason at all. if that randomness chooses the mezz, you can be stuffed. You have to assume that the 6 mezzables will be held in the cave ok.

Cave Mobs:
We use chanters, 1 bard and 1 shaman. The shaman's job is sinia, cripple and slow. Reason is that when a mezz wears off on a councilman it will kill it's mezzing chanter and then it moves on to kill the shaman who should have massive aggro from cripple and slow. In the time it takes for the mob to do this another chanter should be able to pick it up and kill it. You could replace the shaman with a Mage (for sinia) and an mad aggro generating class, like a ranger (snare!) or paladin
Shaman Timing for Debuffs: Our main debuff Turgors lasts 6 mins, to debuff 6 mobs with malosinia, slow and cripple takes a little under 3 mins, assuming that you get some resists. So after debuffing all 6 pop CanniV, when canniV repops you should start re-debuffing all mobs


The Tank Mezz Mobs:
Pull, debuff, position and Tank Mezz 4 of the reamining 6 mobs, have 1 shaman per 2 mobs ideally. You could realistically manage this with 1 shaman on all 4 - use the same Shaman TIming as described above.


Pull TM 5 and start to reduce it's hit points. When it gets down to 10% give the order to kill all of the TM mobs. Kill the 5 TM mobs you are currently engaging. Pull, debuff and kill the last mob as soon as you have TM5 dead, let the people from TM1-4 join in when they can as they kill their mobs.

Run back into the cave and kill the remaining 6 mezzed mobs 1 at a time.

Have a bard kite the avatar around while you rezz and rebuff (5 mins max). Then kill the avatar, hail and loot. Avatar is about as tough as a countilmember
