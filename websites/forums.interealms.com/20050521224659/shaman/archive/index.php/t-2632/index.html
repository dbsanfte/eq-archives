Well, you have the gist of it right ... but don't "aim at zero WIS."

You do need WIS, it is just that WIS is least useful in the early stages, and is never as important as mana regeneration. WIS gives you 1 point of mana for your level divided by 5. So at level 20, one WIS gives you 4 mana. At level 50 it gives you 10 mana, and at 60 it gives you 12 mana. Mana regeneration via cannibalizing is a key element of shaman play, and the hp/ac are important ingredients for that.

Aim for hp/ac and let the WIS come. the Arctic Wyvern Mask at 6 ac and 5 WIS (if I recall correctly - worn it so long I don't remember LOL) is much, much better than the Platinum Ruby Veil at 8 WIS and 8 STR.

But a spider fur belt is not necessarily better than a flayed turmoilskin belt. The turmoilskin is 1 ac less, and has WIS versus the hp on the spider fur ... your needs my dictate the turmail as a better choice - for you.

I, and many other posters on these boards, do just fine with wis at, or even below 150. The most valuable item to a shaman coming up is a fungi tunic - that hp regen is a constant source of mana (yes, when/if you hit 60, and if you have torpor, it finally loses its must wear label .... personally, I am not holding my breath!)

Don't avoid WIS, look for one of the threads on formulas for comparing items with different stats.

For twinkage, you would do very well with the common jaundiced bone pieces (helm, arms, boots, gloves). they would give you outstanding ac staring out.
other slots - the AW mask is great.
get the 6 ac/55 hp rings.
I am currently wearing the 6 wis earrings, because I am still just over 900 ac with them on, and nowhere near 950 with the ac/hp rings. That will change as I upgrade other slots.
then you can look at wis on the other items. Elder wolf hide is good ac in the shoulder slot, and has 4 wis on it, and hp, for example. or mammoth/molten cloak.

and that is not a aconsensus opinion - just my take on what I have learned here and in game.

Cush
At early levels, you're almost a tank with some spells - at 30th or so, I was meleeing things when soloing... throw on some debuffs & dots, then melee & nuke in between (big slow hammer let me get off a nuke between swings).

However, the biggest thing - your needs will vary based on what role you play.

Shaman can solo a lot, by dotting & tanking, in which case you need decent AC, hp & wis.

We can also be the main healer for groups, at least up to the low 50's. As was said, you really have to look at mana regeneration... since we don't get Canni until 24th, go for a mix of AC/Wis prior to that. Once you start to form the Canni habit (like, um, hitting your hotkey while FM) and get different Canni spells, Wis becomes less important. Here's why: if you never to OOM, then you have plenty of mana, and thus plenty of Wis; so, you can tweak your gear towards AC & hp. AC to preserve your precious hp's when you get beat on, and extra hp to Canni more.

From 39th up to 51st you are going to be slowing things all the time. Togor's Insects starts at 39% slow, going up as you level & Turgurs Insects is an upgrade at 51, going up to 75% at 60th. These also get you one heck of a lot of aggro, which is where the AC bit comes in... because a good shaman will slow everything while the enchanters mez things, and stand there getting beat on until everything is locked down, or until (if) the tanks get aggro back from you.

The people talking about maxxing AC/HP gear are mainly the upper level folks, who are topping out on gear choices. At low 50's, I think I was focusing on a mix of AC & Wis - of course, that was pre-Velious, so my choices were more limited, usually having to choose one or the other to raise. With the newer expansions, at 60th (depending on what your guild is capable of) you can get gear that has a better mix of stats, and begin to focus more on HP.

My first priority would be to get a regeneration item.
Ceremonial Iksar CP, Fungi Tunic, whatever.

Then I would get a decent spear/shield and/or a 2hb.

If you have money after that, spend it on items with some balance. Consider using items with a combination of nice hp/ac/mana stats, rather than pure wisdom. Remember 1pt of wisdom is 10pts of mana at level 50. At level 10, it is like 2mana per 1 wisdom. At level 20, 4 mana per 1 wisdom.

So rather than wearing an 7 wisdom ring, which would give you 28 mana at level 20. Maybe you would consider a Jaded Velium Ring, which would give you 5ac, 30hps AND 30 mana. That is a much better combination for you. I would wear the jaded velium ring over a wisdom ring at any level.

Other examples would be, don't wear a 3 wisdom earring when you could get say an Black Sapphire Electrum Earring that has 2ac, 35hps, 25mana. Or an earring of twisted leaves, that has 2ac, 1wis, 35hps, 35mana.

Also consider a haste item. FBSS are fairly cheap now, or you may have one on your monk you can transfer over for self PLing.

At level 60, I wear the same things as some monks and other melees I know. 6ac/65hp rings, barbed dragonscale boots, working on the Spirit Wracked Cord and Regal Ring of the Bathezid quests, crystal spider eyes, enshrouded veils, black sapphire electrum earrings, gorganga idols, etc etc.

Boinshar
I went through my early 50s with about 900 ac and 120 wis. When I hit 55, I had around 1000 ac and 140 wis. I did not seriously upgrade my gear until I reached 60, at which point I had ~1050ac and 180ish wis.

It's quite possible to go through the levels with low wis and high AC, it makes you more effective in a group that pulls less than 3 mobs per pull, and teaches you the true meaning of mana conservation. For example, casting Bane is about the worst thing you can do in most groups when you know you are hurting for mana. You just spent a large chunck of your mana, and the DoT may not even run for it's full duration. You just quickened the fight by about 20 secs, and added 120 secs of down time.

If your wis is low and you are grouped, restrict your mana to slows, regen, and heals. Spend the rest of your time canni dancing. Use DoTs in groups if and only if you can keep up with a continous stream of mobs and you still have more mana than you know what to do with.

It's a different story if you primarily solo with root/dot, but if you tank solo with DoT and pet, a high AC low wis setup will still work well for you.
