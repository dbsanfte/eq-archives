Brohg
The Atlatl is 20% spell haste, reducing by 1% each level over 65 (1% being 5% of the original effect). So it has full efffect on Quiescence, but will only be 15% haste on Spiritual Serenity. Quick Buff AA speeds up buff spells by 10%/25%/50%, with no level limit.

50% is the maximum possible haste that can be applied to any spell. This limit applies to all spell haste, from AA, foci, and buffs (specifically cleric buffs, but there's also the CBQ clicky). Because of this limit, lots of shamans stop at Quick Buff rank 2, 25%, because a basic focus and cleric buff get them to the cap of 50%. You have to choose what you're aiming for. If you get a really good bene spell haste focus, then only QuickBuff2 is needed to get max haste on buffs. If you have less than the best focus, then QB3 will provide a functional pickup in cast time. Enough added haste to justify 9aa? That's up to you.

Personally, capping Quickbuff is the next thing on my list, I'm doing the last rank of OoW healing crits atm. Having max haste on Serenity is worth it to me, and I'm having a B*TCH of a time finding a level 70 bene spell haste focus.
