Cragael
Shamans were always able to scribe it. The scroll was mislabeled as DRU-only, but any pure caster with Enduring Breath (Shaman, Enchanter, Druid) could scribe it even then.

Now, why is it rarer than GRC.

Well, it is. Unlike GRC and like Levitation or the level 39 Remove Curse, it only drop from a single, named MOB. And, like Levitation and unlike Remove Curse, that MOB is in an out-of-the-way spot requiring a moderately good group, one on the average higher than level 52, to get to and to keep.

But unlike Levitation, the MOB that drops the Everlasting Breath spell doesn’t also have a chance to drop an ‘uber’, droppable, AC 35 shield/back slot piece of armor that sells for mucho plats or something like that. So groups only go kill it for the spell and leave when whoever needs it got his and or her copy, rather than trying the named spawn over and over until everyone in the group got a copy of the LORE scroll to sell.

Plus, unlike Levitation again, there is so many EB items in the game, most of them easily obtainable by the low 40s (which is the cut-off point of that level 52 spell anyway) that very few people ever even bother to put together the group to get the spell. Not to mention that the vampyre castle in TM is just a pain to fight in… tons of ‘named’ vampyre, each with their own specific immunities and a wonderfully lousy pathing job that usually insure that half of the runners will poof without experience nor loot.

A little background on this one... Trilexior is one of half a dozen or so named vampires in the coterie castle in Tenebrous Mountains. He's an enchanter who likes to gravflux, just to add to the fun. There's also an underconned 58 SK named up there you might want to watch out for, for another quest.

Some time ago there was a serious pathing bug in the castle where, when the nameds ran back into the castle from the back balcony at low life, they'd get snagged on the tombs and just poof. No xp, no lewts.

Verant claimed to have fixed this, but I don't know if they did. We took a group up there and had a lot of problems with aggro through the floor below right after that patch. Competent group, all the right levels, and it was a horror-show.

Before this, we killed Tri over and over and over and never did get him to drop the spell (when we were able to prevent the poof, that is). After the patch we stopped trying. RvR dictated it.

Good luck in your hunting of it, though. Imho 8k for that spell would have been cheap for the effort, but hey... bat wings are cheaper.
