Something that has always bothered me with the cripple line is that these spells don't directly impact casters. Plus the fact that Shaman's don't have a true castable Stun type spell (some of our DoT's have knockback but GL on getting them to impact casting of a NPC spell caster).

I understand that casters have lower AC, Dex, Agi, and Str for the most part ... in theory. But most SoE encounter NPC casters have so much AC and other related stat's that one may as well call them melee-casters. By level 66, most mobs/NPC's are going to have high stat's across the board ... casters included. In fact, I would not be surprised that 65 plus NPC midlevel mobs have maxed stat's (350 plus) across the board.

In my opinion, Crippling Spasm should have some sort of reduction in spell cast time for the target or spell damage reduction effect against caster types.

Call it a crippling spasm of the spell casting fingers.

Let me see if I can do some quick math because I'm too lazy to search for the older posts on this.

Say you have a cleric who can use SL to heal for 450 hps.
Say you have a druid who can use NI to heal for 291 hps.

And say the mob's average hit is 1200 per pop, and can quad that amount. So its about 4800 damage every 3 seconds or so. That means 1600 dps. Both the cleric and druid's combined heal rate wouldn't keep up. Now, add 75% slow. Now the mobs is doing 400 dps. And, as you can see, the encounter is now trivial. The cleric alone can keep the tank alive.

You can scale these numbers up to raid encounters too. Where the unslowed dps of a mob can drop a tank in two rounds, whereas the slowed dps requires nothing more than a standard CH chain.

I don't disagree that part of it might have been disliking how the slowing classes contributed so much, but the raw numbers are much more revealing. They couldn't keep adding dps to raid mobs without dropping any main tank before slow, while trivializing the encounter after slow.
