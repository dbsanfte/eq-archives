To the Sony Online Entertainment Developers . . .

The shaman community has been trying for the last couple of years to come up with some form of unified commentary to express unhappiness with the state of our class. This effort has met with failure a number of times, probably due to the sheer utility of the class. We can solo, group and raid—so people with different play styles have different views of the basic problems. The net result is that for the most part, we can’t agree on much of anything, other than the fact that the shaman, as a class, has issues.

Shamans are plagued with the perception that we are being punished for the powerful combination of slow and torpor back in the Kunark expansion. Each and every expansion we fall farther behind other classes in healing and damage (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=19149)

Our healing and damage is now more closely compared to hybrids than other priest classes. Our two strongest points, debuffing and mana-regen have been reduced to no advantage over other classes. It is hard to understand our role in the game. We are no longer the best debuffer by any significant margin, nor are we a good healer or a good damage dealer. We aren't really a pet class and our buffs are less and less sought after, we are certainly no longer the best buffing class of either melee or caster.

With the impetus of the current spells and abilities from Gates of Discord (“GoD”), discontent seems to have increased substantially. The general consensus is that we have reached our pinnacle of usefulness in Kunark and have wound up at the bottom of the heap in just about any measure of class balance. Mediocrity compared to the other specialized classes we can understand. Being dead last in basically all categories is a bit much of a penalty for utility.

Another difficulty the shaman are facing is that we are suffering deficiencies in so many areas, that it looks like we’re asking for the world if we suggest fixes for them! However, in terms of balance, fixing one or two of the issues would effectively negate four or five others. For instance, addressing damage per second (“dps”) covers nukes, damage over time spells (“DoTs”) and pets concurrently. A fix in one of those areas might make changes in the others overpowering. Accordingly, we decided to post a list of problems we feel need to be addressed desperately. After all, you folks at SoE have to decide what constitutes balance in the end.


Top 5 Issues as a result of the What do we need the most? (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=19072&pagenumber=1) Poll


1. Healing

Shaman healing is extremely weak when we have to debuff mobs before each encounter. This weakness is compounded greatly with mitigation. It is most severe on mobs that have high dps but die relatively quickly. That is why PoP-type mobs, with high dps and low hp relative to older expansion mobs, have marginalized shaman healing and slowing ability. We often fall into the dreaded "tnarg chain" cycle: a slow is resisted and the main tank takes so much damage we can't stop casting tnarg long enough to attempt another slow.

Shaman healing is way behind other priests even when slow is factored in. Healing needs to be re-evaluated in comparison to other healing classes, including paladins. This review should include cast time, mana cost and amount healed. Our heal over time spells need improvements and we need access to a group heal. Exp group mobs hit harder in one round than we can heal.

-No group healing ability
-Smallest direct healing ability
-No upgrades to our amount healed in our heal over time line, which we received in Kunark.
-No MGBable/AE heal, like other priest classes and our hybrid class, beastlords.


Healing Suggestions:

* Some sort of group heal or group heal over time (with MGB option).
* Some sort of upgrade to or improvement of Quiescence.
* Some sort of faster and more efficient quick single-target heal
* Bigger quick single-target heal


2. Mana Regen

In the past, cannibalize and our ability to regen mana was blamed for shortcomings in other areas, such as mana cost and duration of our buffs and manacost of heals. With the new and widespread abilities in passive manaregen for other classes, an upgrade to cannibalize 4 needs to be implemented. We are the only class forced into a position where we have to sacrifice so much to regain mana. Most other classes simply use a buff slot.

* Other classes get mana regeneration methods that are nearly equivalent to shamans
* FT and other enhancements increase mana regen rate across all mana using classes
* Mana regeneration spells are widely available to other classes
* Cannibalize is active mana-regen and has a large number of penalties when compared to passive mana-regen (can't cast any other spells while regaining mana, can't move while regaining mana, cannot engage in any social interaction while regenning mana due to constant clicking)
* Permanent Spell Slot required
* The current Canni upgrade is unsatisfactory and does not address any of our concerns.


Mana Regen Suggestions:

* New AA ability for Cannabalize.
* An upgrade or addition to Cannibalization, allowing (at the same ratio), greater or more frequent conversions of HP to Mana.
Alternately, some across the board adjustment in mana cost for our spells, considering the cost of similar spells to other classes
* Upgrade Can 4 - bigger bites, less clicking
* New Canni AA - hastened cannibalize



3. Direct Damage Spells


Our nukes need help. Even with major improvements to our DOT spells, we need a better nuke. There is no reason that hybrids should be nuking on the same level us a pure caster class.

* Under powered
* Slow casting (especially velium strike)
* Resist rates on rains are high.
* No nuke over 1000 damage, rangers can nuke almost as well as shamans. All other casting classes have a higher DD spell (cleric, enchanter, mage, wizard, druid).
* Highest level poison nuke is 675dmg (compare to hybrid nukes from POP expansion, RNG -700dmg, BST -675dmg, SK -660dmg)
* No specialized damage increasing focus for poison nukes.

Direct Damage Suggestions:
* Velium Strike changed to casting time 6.5 seconds
* Alternative, a new poison nuke. Suggestion:
Poison based nuke, level 65, casting time 6.0, 1200dmg. 400mana
* An upgrade to our FIRE based nuke line, introduced at level 1 but then abandoned, would be nice
* Keep the poison/cold nukes, up damage
* Better rain spells, ditch the 4 target limitation!



4. Slowing

In the past, it has been justified that many of the things we are lacking at (such as healing) are a result of the powerful nature of slow. This argument is no longer valid. Encounters are designed using slow mitigation and with full knowledge of the effects of slow. The difference in slows between shamans and enchanters is small, beastlords are close behind with their very fast casting slow. Other classes like bards, necromancers also have the ability to slow pretty well too.

* New slow is not satisfactory, long recast, short duration - not worthy to replace a spell in most shaman's current line up
* New spell isn't good enough to memmed along with Turgurs
* Bst and enc slows nearly as good
* Slow mitigation has decreased the advantage we have over other classes that can slow.
* GoD Slow upgrade is no good. Recast times and short duration, when paired with a nerf in effectiveness of the slow makes this spell undesirable.

Slowing Suggestions:

Reduced agro, mana cost, resist rate, and/or recast time on (an upgrade to) Turgur's Insects, which has been in its current state since Kunark was released six expansions ago.


5. Buffing

Once the masters of melee buffing, the only class that could buff ATK, shamans have seen their buffs become less and less desirable since Velious with the introduction of weapons with avatar procs, the ATK buffs from other classes and the decreasing usefulness statistic buffs. Our hybrid class should not be buffing HP, ATK, resists, stamina and manaregen to higher numbers than we can. There is no reason that our buffs should cost more mana and last a shorter duration than other priest classes.

* Stat buffs at level 65 are largely useless because of stat caps.
* Our hybrid class can buff more pure HP than we can, plus more of the other relevant stats at 65, ATK and RESISTS.
* Our buffs are shorter duration and higher mana cost than other priest classes.

Buffing Suggestions:

* Group FA
* Stat buffs that are attractive to the stat capped
* A new shaman buff of adding +10% to weapon skills, +10% dmg to procs, +10% to avoidance lasting 1 hour. As str/dex/sta is pretty much maxed by so many people at the high end making the only beneficial means of buffing being hp and resists
* Endurance buffs! Logical choice for shaman
* Buffs which go past the sta cap (For example: So is someone with 305 Stamina was buffed with Boar they'd hit 355. Same would hold true for people at level 60 with 255. They could reach 305 with our buffs. And at 65 with 280 you'd hit 340.)
* Focus augmented with the following:
+30 accuracy (lowers hit/miss ratio)
+30 combat effects (increases proc rate)
+2 shielding (lowers damage taken)
+20 Strikethrough (You swing, they dodge, you strikethrough and hit them anyway)

I am a 65 Time-enabled shaman, so my ideas are geared toward endgame play, for the most part. I've tried to think of things that would be of general benefit to all shammies, however. A lot of these aren't my ideas, either, but the effort of the entire shaman community that posts on this board, so kudos to ya... and here we go!...

<h2>Slow</h2>

The core ability of the shaman class. 99.99% of shamans agree that we should be able to slow better than any other class. That means our slow should dominate. There should not be situations where you say "A BST would slow this mob better." BST, as a hybrid, should be able to fulfill the slowing role, but its slow should not 'step on' the shaman one at all. Currently it does, and this is a problem.

We need to either slow more (80%, which I don't think will happen), or get a slow that is at least as good as the BST (1.5 sec cast, 65%, easy to land). I would propose a 1.5 second cast, 300 mana, 70% slow with a 5 second recast (same as BST) that lasts a bit longer than theirs (4 mins).

There are also more creative ideas, such as tacking slow onto something that is a disadvantage in some situations, i.e. slow+nuke or slow+DoT in a single spell (so you couldn't use it on adds). A cold based slow+nuke could be particularly interesting and good for fast-paced situations.

Might look something like:

<b>Frigid Cocoon</b>
1. Decrease HP by 1100 when cast
2. Decrease attack speed by 50%
Mana: 450, Casting Time: 5.0, Duration: 1.0 mins, Recast Time: 2

A 50% slow would be about 125mana (imo) and Velium Strike 300, so 450 is a reasonable cost for something that isn't as effective as Turgur's slow-wise and nukes for more than VS. The main thing you gain is *time*, which is one of shamans' principal problems atm. Our spells are so slow that we don't have time to cast the fun stuff. This spell gives us a bigger nuke but at a price (more mana, slow tacked on), but also would give us a new slow resist (cold).

A cold-based slow DoT might look something like:

<b>Frigid Withering</b>
1. Decrease attack speed by 50%
2. Decrease HP by 160 per tick
Mana: 400, Casting Time: 4.0, Duration: 7 ticks, Recast Time: 3

This would add some dps to our slow and work with our mana regen. Again would add another resist check for slow (cold).

I do not think either of the above spells is overpowering, although maybe need some tweaks to costs/slow pct/etc to make the idea work.

Summary:
1. Shm should *always* be better slowers than any other class
2. Make a slow to surpass BST slow in all situations
3. Tigirs upgrade?
4. Different resists or combination spells

<h2>Nukes and DoTs</h2>

I lump these together because these are our bread and butter DPS. Our current problems are that our Velium Strike nuke only does 925. Other classes may be getting nuke upgrades in GoD which will illustrate further just how poor our nuke is.

If clerics and enchanters get a nuke upgrade, we should get a cold-based nuke for *AT LEAST* what they *used* to nuke for, i.e. 1100. I have proposed a creative slow/nuke combo above. A straight-up 1100 nuke would be easy to add.

As far as DoTs go, Bane was nice, but the recast time sucks and it is resisted way too often. The aggro on Bane is about where a DoT should be, however. Ultor is only a soloing tool. We do *NOT* need more DoTs to solo with.

If a DoT is added, it should be FAST (perhaps 4 ticks or less even) and FURIOUS (at least 500/tick). Basically a nuke in disguise. Remember, we can only do this once per mob-that-dies-fast anyway.

Here is my proposal for a straight up DoT:

<b>Curse of the Kill-You-Fast</b>
1. Decrease HP by 500 per tick
2. Increase Curse counter by 18
Duration: 4 ticks, Mana: 500, Resist: Magic, Recast Time: 6

More mana, less efficient than bane, but more damage fast. Although I propose this spell, we *did* get a magic-based DoT in LDoN, so maybe it should be something novel like cold, be evocation-based possibly?, or tack on other effects (as with Frigid Withering above).

A long-lasting DoT (anything 1 min or more) does us no good at this point. We got those nicely upgraded in PoP, and it is not a place where we need improvements.

To sum up, we need burst dps, either a nuke of 1100+ or a 4-5 tick DoT doing 2kish damage. If the devs find either of these overpowering, tack them on to a slow and make them cost more.

Summary:
1. Better nuke
2. Faster high damage DoT

<h2>Pets</h2>

Our pet is, above all else, fun. I think of him like the cute dog in Nethack that follows you around. He is basically a constant baseline DoT dps of about 40. I do not think the pet needs to be the focus of attention for new expansions, nor does most of the shaman community. All we really want with dogdog is innate dual wield and an easier way to haste him. Adding 1-2 minor aa abilities devoted to pet is not a bad idea, but it should not be the focus of attention. I think most shammies can agree on that.

<h2>Melee</h2>

We used to be a battle priest. We are no longer. Clerics have taken this role. I, personally, am fine with that. I don't think it's feasible to make shaman melee in high end game anyway. This is probably a point that will divide the shaman community... but I'd say most of the high-enders (65 w/aa) do not expect to kill a mob by smashing its face in. The one possibility here is to fix Rabid Bear to be a real source of burst DPS, but, again, this is so minor compared to the other problems with the class.

One way to fix rabid bear is to have the form itself provide a benefit. My personal favorite would be that being a rabid bear makes your poison and disease DoTs and nukes do more damage, because you are, after all, RABID!! This would be similar to the wizard Iceflame of E'ci spell effect. I don't consider this overpowering for high-enders at all, since we all have damage/dot foci anyway which wouldn't stack... but for those that didn't have these foci, it might be a very nice damage improvement. I would suggest 25% improved damage to poison/disease dots/nukes while rabid bear is in effect. I'd also up the tainted bite to be at least a 75dd.

<h2>Buffs</h2>

With high-enders all stat-capped, our buffs have taken on less and less meaning. Focus and FA are all we have left. Group FA has been proposed. I think this is a great idea. In addition, I would like to see a group 60% haste for shaman, just because only the lazy of us bother with Talisman of Alacrity... so right now we labor to cast Swift Like the Wind on everyone.

I don't think there is much more that can be done with buffs, other than giving us the endurance regen (which it looks like has been done).

Group FA has got to be the most requested buff (that I see). Even if it is an AA, shammies everywhere would rejoice at its existence.

Summary:
1. 60% group haste
2. Group FA
3. Endurance regen

<h2>Mana Regen</h2>

Along with slow, this is one of the core abilities of our class. About all I can say about mana regen is, either seriously upgrade it or just leave it alone. A half-assed upgrade would be the worst possible change. If you're going to add a canni, I feel it should give the extra benefit of 10ish mana/tick when used once per minute. In other words, an improvement of 60mana.

My proposed canni spell would look like:

<b>Cannibalize VI</b>
1. Decrease HP by 250
2. Increase Mana by 140
Casting Time: 3.5, Recast Time: 3.5

I lengthen the casting time and recast time a bit, keep the hp-to-mana ratio pretty much the same, but make a genuine improvement to the hp gained. If this were an aa, I would be wary of canni4/canni6 toggle being overpowered, so I would give a reuse time of 10 seconds or so in that case.

<h2>Healing</h2>

Healing is, in my opinion, the most difficult aspect of the shaman class to nail down. With the inception of EQ, we were definitely healers. Now, we really aren't. We are identified more as "slowers", an altogether different creature. However, with the decreasing importance of slow in many situations (unslowable raid mobs, xp mobs that mitigate), it is important for a shaman to be able to keep up. I'm not proposing that we ever be able to heal as well as a druid, but we shouldn't be ridiculously far behind them.

Kragg's mending is just a bad spell. No one uses it. I would not propose any upgrade to it, as I don't see shamans as CH'ers, and most shamans on this board probably don't see themselves as CH'ers either.

Tnargs is a pretty paltry fast heal. Quiescence is nice, but other than healing our own canni, simply can't keep up with mitigated mobs. It also doesn't help on raids when you need to heal someone outside your group.

So, we need either a new fast heal or a new heal over time. Group quiescence has been suggested, and I will take a stab at what that would look like:

<b>Sphere of Quiescence</b>
1. Increase HP v2 by 250 per tick

Mana: 1000, Casting Time: 7.0, Recast: 2.25, Target Type: Group

The idea is not to step on ethereal elixir (300/tick). or HoT the group in a more mana-efficient way than a cleric. Hence 250, and 1000 mana cost. Even adding something similar as an aa would make shammies happy.

A new single target quiescence should look like this, I think:

<b>Super Quiesce</b>
1. Increase HP v2 by 400 per tick
Duration: 4 ticks, Mana: 275, All else same as Q

Summary:
1. Better fast heal
2. Better HoT
3. Group quiesce

My final caveat with respect to both heals and nukes to the developers: if you upgrade the healing and nuking capabilities of other classes without doing the same for shamans, we will fall very far behind.

<h2>Debuffs</h2>

AE Malos has been suggested as a way to more quickly slow hordes of mobs. Perhaps this is too powerful, but I mention it anyway. An upgrade to cripple is also a frequent request. Neither of these is high on my list, but different shammies have different priorities.

I do believe debuffs could be made more creative, i.e. debuff+dot, debuff+nuke, etc. similar to the ideas I tossed out there for the slow+nuke/slow+dot above.

Summary:
1. Upgrade to cripple
2. AE Malos

Well, that's about it. I'm sure there will be lots of other ideas floating around in the next few weeks. I've tried to mention everything I've ever seen anyone post on this board, even ideas that I am not personally a big fan of.

Good luck, fellow shammies =)

Just some observations on the elements of style in the proposal.

To be effective, presentation is as important as content. I feel this initial draft lacks necessary elements of both style and content.

The proposal needs to be directed to an individual. It is too easy for everyone in a group to passively ignore something generally addressed. A person named must ignore with volition.

If everyone simply mass complains using the exact same text, it will become nothing more than spam. Really, really bad idea. Much better to have one representative, and many signatories, with only one document sent.

Anything bearing an accusatory tone needs to be removed. For example: "through a medium of concentrated neglect". The overall tone should not seem like a complaint, but rather a reasonable discussion of objective shortcomings.

We do not know the motives of the balancing team as regards Shaman, to impute bad motives will not help our cause. It would be unhelpful for us to proceed on the assumption that the developers have chosen to ignore or neglect, when in fact they may have ample and (to them) reasonable grounds for every decision they have made.

Empty statements of objective opinion need to be removed, for example: "There is no reason that hybrids should be nuking on the same level us a pure caster class."

Perhaps there is a reason, perhaps that reason is the subject of great and lengthy deliberation by very important and influential members of the balancing team, whose professional reputation and standing are entwined within.

How can it aid us, then, to make such statements which could very well insult or at the least irritate those responsible for such things? There are better ways to word things.

Broad and subjective declarations need to be made specific. There needs to be objective data serving to fortify each and every complaint.

"Shaman healing is way behind other priests even when slow is factored in. Healing needs to be re-evaluated in comparison to other healing classes, including paladins. This review should include cast time, mana cost and amount healed."

Have we any data to support this? Does the data take into consideration all mitigating factors that might be relevant in issues of balance?

Wouldn't it serve us better if it was *we* who did this evaluation using objective, repeatable methodolgy so we don't end up looking silly when somebody else shows us incontrovertable data proving we are wrong?

Is it reasonable to lump all other priest classes together when making this comparison, or is it more relevant to make the comparison weighing the strengths and weaknesses of each priest class individually?

I could play devil's advocate, and handily argue that none of the complaints in this proposal are accurate. I can think of dozens of reasonable and plausible objections to every single point.

To win at a game like this, you have to show more sand than just all getting together on a bunch of words. You have to have solid, unarguable evidence.

My opinion is the cart is going before the horse at this point, and much more work needs to be done.
