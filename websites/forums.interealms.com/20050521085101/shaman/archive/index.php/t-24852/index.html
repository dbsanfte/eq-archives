Hail,

Three clerics & an 8k tank plus assorted DPS with those kind of numbers should take down Vindi just fine. I'd STRONGLY recommend you use a monk (just one is fine) or a Vindi-experienced pull team if your force isn't used to Vindi raids to make things smoother. I've had to pull Vindi as a shaman (!) and managed it, but I wouldn't recommend it to you.

Positioning & aggro control are the keys. Keep your tank in a corner & pin Vindi there (facing your tank & the corner). If you use DC pets for extra DPS, make sure you refresh their attack buff that has an illusion to reduce confusion. Assign an ench (even a sub60 one ought to be okay) to mez any trash guard adds that occur. I wouldn't bother with regular charms honestly.

Grieg is another good mob for breaking in an alliance to raiding, as is the million hp blob in Cazic Thule. Both can easily be done with a mixed bag of 3 groups. Three clerics + a tank & miscellaneous DPS can do ALOT of old content for fun. Dain, Dozekar, Aary maybe, etc. WW dragons can be lots of fun (without much danger of a wipe, though people are bound to die) for a night of raiding, and for the sub60 crowd would probably offer upgrades to those in poor equipment (alternatively the drops make pretty good low-level twink gear).

PoP events like Mujakr, Hedge, the Spider Queen, Aramin, etc can also be used as ice-breaker raids. Prototypes in PoI are easily downed with 3 groups of under 65's, and Xanamech might be a nice test as well (that can be daily until it is smooth if your folks have the patience). BoT towers could be done easily also using certain strategies.

Take care,

The short answer is yes. A level 67 tank should be able to hold aggro and 3 level 60+ clerics should be able to maintain a CH rotation on the MT (use an additional druid or 2 in your CH cycle if you are in any doubt). Your other main tanks should be the rampage tanks, and any spare druids or shaman should be tasked to heal rampage damage. If you are worried about your clerics going OoM, you can always task necros to twictch mana to them.

It is important to note that rampage,if I recall correctly, works on the basis of the order in which you enter the aggro list, so that the order in which you engage Vindi is important. I.e. Let MT aggro and build up a bit of aggro, then let your next best 2 tanks aggro as "rampage tanks" then let everyone else come to the party.

Vindi is best pulled to a corner so the tank backs right into the corner and holds aggro, allowing everyone else to attack from behind. The safest strategy is to have your raid force situated at the bottom of the ramp to the arena, actually, just around the corner past the ramp to the arena. Clear all mobs in the area, clear mobs to Vindi. Stand your MT alone in the corner just to the left as you come down the ramp, with everyone else up the blind alley a ways to avoid aggroing. Have a FD puller bring Vindi and FD. Let tank aggro to pull vindi

Vindi is a good fun mob, and is a good test of your ability to be organised. It is worth assigning a ranger or 2 to tank/kite any adds or respawns...and shaman should be able to slow any adds and keep the rangers alive

It is important to note that rampage,if I recall correctly, works on the basis of the order in which you enter the aggro list, so that the order in which you engage Vindi is important. I.e. Let MT aggro and build up a bit of aggro, then let your next best 2 tanks aggro as "rampage tanks" then let everyone else come to the party.

There's a much easier way to set aggro than this: instead of having MT engage first, have the player you want to be your RT engage first. He only has to do a single hit of any sort to top the aggro list, and THEN have MT pull aggro off him using taunt, etc. That establishs your MT as the main aggro, and the RT as the one that gets rampaged.

The best way to do it, if you can arrange it, is to just have your RT pull the mob. That sets him automatically.

And then, of course, you have to keep the RT alive. Otherwise, rampage will go to whomever is next on the aggro list, unless you rez the RT. If you rez him, make sure its outside ramp range, or he'll go right to the top of the rampage list again, and get beatdown before you have a chance to buff him.

In case that wasn't clear, here's an example: in Time, Tier 5, my guild pulls both CT and Bertox over to Rallos Zek's room to kill them. We use either a paladin or an SK as RT. So, guild assembles in RZ's room, and paladin goes down to the other end, and throws a stun at CT. This aggros CT, and sets the paladin at the top of the aggro list.

Paladin then runs back to RZ's room, leading CT all the way. As soon as CT rounds the corner, the MT taunts the hell out of him, and backs into the corner, thus cornering the mob and taking over the position at the top of the aggro list, thus bumping the paladin down on aggro, but leaving him at the top of the rampage list. Our shaman are assigned as designated healers on the RT, so we keep him alive, while clerics proceed to heal the MT, and the god dies, and much "phat lewt" is obtained.

Hope that helps.
