In that order. 2aa seems like a low price to pay to get some nice crit "eye candy". But adept does seem like a more solid choice over all. Then finish up by making the crits more frequent with gift 2,3.

Even if you choose to do it the other way, seems the benefit per aa point for level 1 of either ability exceeds the benefit per aa of subsequent levels, so doing level 1 first seems best.

Gift 1 = 2% crit chance / 2 aa = 1% crit / aa
Gift 2 = 3% crit chance / 4 aa = .75% crit / aa
Gift 3 = 5% crit chance / 6 aa = .83% crit / aa

Adept 1 = 2% better heal / 2 aa = 1% better / aa
Adept 2 = 3% better heal / 4 aa = .75% better / aa
Adept 3 = 5% better heal / 6 aa = .83% crit / aa

Bang for your buck wise, the first two levels of each are best.

Macnair
Shaman heals are situational. That means that no answer about how to heal can be right all the time: sometimes one thing helps you more, sometimes another thing does.

Shaman heals start from a low baseline. That means you want to do everything you can to strengthen them. In the end, you want an Improved Healing 4 item, plus every Healing Gift and Healing Adept aa there is, plus Quick Buff aa to cast Quiescence in 3 seconds, plus Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery so that Quiescence lasts 6 to 7 ticks.

So what order to you want to get them in? That depends on the zones and mobs you're playing, how lucky you are, and where you see the shaman's roll in the healing order.

The advantage of crit heals is, if we get into an unexpected bad situation, we could still get lucky and survive, if a crit heal goes off at just the right moment.

The advantage of steady augmented heals is, we know what our capabilities are, and can rely on them to take care of business.

So in an emergency raid situation where only the shaman is available to keep the MA alive, a crit on Tnargs for 2600 can save the day, if we get lucky enough to have one.

In a normal LDON with an ambush from a random unslowed wanderer, 1600 point Tnarg + 1600 point Tnarg + 1800 point Quiescence is enough to keep the tank alive. Comfortably. Reliably. But 1300 Tnarg + 1300 Tnarg + 1200 Quiescence and hoping for a crit is not comfortable.

Crits are fun, no doubt about it. Getting a crit heal in to save the tank's life and winning a raid encounter that you were about to lose: that's terrific fun.

But.

If you have the aa to crit 16 % of the time, that means you get it 1 out of 6 on average. So when you land in a raid situation where the shaman's crit heal can save the day, you're going to hit a normal heal instead of a crit 5 times out of 6, the MA is going to die, and the raid will be wiped. But 1 time of 6, you'll get a crit, and get to be a hero.

Any raid that is depending on the better survivability generated by 2600 hp of shaman healing -- especially, any raid that is counting on the shaman to hit a critical heal at the moment of truth -- is a raid that will fail, 5 times out of 6.

In contrast, if you are the main healer / slower in a level 65 normal LDON, 1800 Q and 1600 Tnarg means you know you can get the job done. Not because you crit at just the right moment, but because you're reliable.

I know some people who seem to be much luckier than I am. If I felt like I could count on my luck, I might have a different opinion. But as it is, I myself would rather count on the steady reliability of 1600 point heals and 1800 point quiescences.

But.

If I had very limited time and mostly spent it on raids -- which means it is going to take me a long time to get all the healing aa -- then I'd give serious consideration to maxing crit heals. If it's any kind serious raid, you're never going to be the main healer: but when it all starts falling apart, if you're still standing you will fire off a heal on the MA: and sometimes it will crit, maybe providing just enough healing for the last cleric's CH to land. And who wouldn't want to be there for that.
