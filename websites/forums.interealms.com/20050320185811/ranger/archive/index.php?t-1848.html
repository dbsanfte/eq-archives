Venture states - "Actually I'm pretty sure the EULA says that Verant owns all content you contribue to EQ, so they can do anything they want with your character and its actions."

No, it does not state that nor does Verant's EULA even remotely make such a claim.

There was a intellectual property case vs. an online provider service a number of years ago where someone posted a series of short stories on one their message boards. The author eventually sought to get them published and managed to get them sold to a local magazine. This online service tried to sue for copyright infringement citing that their terms of service gave them sole rights to characters and written materials posted on their boards. The court found in favor of the author stating that the online service did not possess intellectual property rights in that the author paid for the message board services provided and adhered to the standing regulations for use of those boards at the time. It was furthermore stated that the online provider's terms of service were a violation in trying to claim ownership of someone else's writings without offering proper compensation but that they do retain the right to use those stories for advertising purposes.

This ruling has been upheld in several other cases of similiar nature with that and other online provider services since then.


Section 11 of the EULA, only applies to character information you sumbit to their official media sites and is in reference to their intellectual property rights they have granted you license to use.

Loosely translated:

They can post screen shots of your character killing Vox et. al and use it for advertising purposes without offering you any sort of compensation.

Any information or writings of your character that you post on their official boards can be used for advertising without offering compensation.

They can mention on their official sites that your character completed action X and was awarded some title or object in game for advertising purposes. (They cannot make mention of the account holder's name without your consent though, as that's a violation of the Privacy Act.)

They can do this because your character likeness is actually made from their content. You use the choices they provide you with for appearance and equipment. You have a rented license to do so, this license permits you to write stories or use the EQ setting for non-profit purposes without their consent. As far as official events go, you consented to participate in a story/event that they created and accepted offered compensation forthwith from them for purposes of advertising.

They cannot "sell" any of this though without your consent. They cannot take your writings and sell them for publishing without your consent. Just as you cannot publish a story of your character in the EQ setting without their consent. Advertising is not viewed as a source of income but as a means of generating revenue, hence why they can use your character for free advertising. And this is another reason why they're particular about not allowing people to take copyrighted/famous names.

They cannot take your character by name and write up a unique story within the EQ setting and post it on their media sites for advertising or attempt to sell it as a story for a novel, comic, short story, or whatever. That is an infringement of your intellectual property rights.
