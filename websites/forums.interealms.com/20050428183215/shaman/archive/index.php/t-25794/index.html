Ruatonim
Paladins are hardly the lowest DPS class; that's just propaganda thrown out to avoid a nerf. Some level 60 rogue quoted his DPS as 200; my 45 paladin has a listed DPS of 154 (with a 39dps weapon) and that neglects procs and engineering toys that I could use.

The following are the only paladin weaknesses I've encountered:
1. Hard to maintain aggro efficiently and reliably (especially on multi pulls). Its usually not a big deal if you are proactive about aggro generation, but if you're lazy (or want to be lazy) its an annoyance.

2. Lack of ranged attacks. The only fixes for this are engineering (long, one minute cool down on dynamite), linken's boomerang (not sure on the cool down since my paladin doesn't have it yet), 31 pt investment in the marginally useful (mostly useless) holy talent tab for the 30 second cool down mana inefficient nuke, or fight only undead.

Yep, that's it for paladin downfalls, and they aren't that game breaking either.

Strengths?
1. Best AC in the game: plate armor, aura, talents to increase the effectiveness of both (this makes the assumption that warriors don't have similar - I really don't know).
2. Priest quality healing in most instances: very few areas need more than the ~1300 heals that a 60 paladin can provide.
3. Phys damage immunity for 20 seconds, spell immunity for 10 seconds: AND you can cast through both and melee (+50% atk speed) for 10 seconds.
4. Good DPS: not the BEST, but its certainly not the worst by any means. I'd put paladins right after mages/rogues/hunters.
5. Can solo YELLOW CON elites or lower at most levels: Anything that doesn't stun is easy, but does require *some* strategy.
