..........i. Aggro: Dots should be less aggro than nukes. period.

----b) Rains/AE: Let this be our "specialty".
..........i. Reduce the resist rate
..........ii. Increase Variety
__________1) Reintroduce our fire line as a single target 3 wave "nuke" type rain. Highest resist rate, similar to our poison now.
__________2) Buff our poison line. Give it -50pr, increase dmg. 4 target like now.
__________3) Reintroduce disease cloud. True AE, but lower damage, however easiest to land.
----c) Nukes: Bah, let the druids have them

3. Complimentary healers:
----a) Heal over time
..........i. Largest single target heal over time of any priest.
..........ii. Group Quiessence
----b) Wave healing
..........i. AE Heal in a rain form
..........ii. Single target rain heal.
----c) Weakest direct heals of any priest.

my experience, and collected info:

parsing shows that all procs are over time, but a proc can only be activated on a swing. haste has been shown to decrease proc rate.

you can increase proc rate by reaching a higher skill and an optimum dexterity level.

parsing shows that proc rate is dependant on 3 factors.

skill
dexterity
the proc itself.

proc rate will softcap w/ maxed weapon skill and 255 dex.
the weapon's proc rate is variable by proc. some weapons have a proc rate modifier on them.

Pile Driver has a 200% proc rate mod on it. (no wonder it's so valued!)

as for proc buffs, the proc rate will be dependant upon what it is coded as, because I don't believe "proc buffs" have been parsed on special weapons.
