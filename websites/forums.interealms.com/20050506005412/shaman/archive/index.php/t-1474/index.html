If the only thing thats stopping you from going to the fungi is the AC I would definatly make the switch. I wont say AC doesnt matter, but you have to see it through the law of diminishing return.

As near as anyone can honestly tell All Wis casters, and maybe Int casters to but not my area of expertise, hit a point that gets commonly refered to as a Soft AC cap.

This is not to say you CANT get more AC, what it means is that after a certain point the amount of AC required to alter the damage you are recieving is exponentially greater than the return on the investment.

I can tell you that since 60 lvl (course 60 lvl has something to do with it as well) I have noticed very little in the damage recieved after 1100 AC at 1200 I still took as much damage with a very very minor difference over a 2 hour samples soloing in the same area only adjusting ac and trying to keep agi as close to the same score so I could take dodge into some account.

The big deal seems to be just plain not being hit vs. actually lessening the damage you recieve.

Unless you find yourself soloing casters alot you really can afford to focus on Wis/mana/AC instead of resists.

I would definately shelf the Sabertooth neck for an Imbued ivory collar.

Id try and grab a pair of Coldain Skin gloves (these really arent that costly considering thier stats to a shaman)

If you have to buy the gems you might wanna let the thurg BP go and just put the cash else where, course that depends on gem availability to....

Take the boots off man. You'll get used to either pullin em out of a bag or just go without the bag. I havent ever left my boots behind, but I havent worn mine since about 52 lvl, just keep em handy taken out of the bag or putting them on if you really want em for a specific reason isnt that hard. Get a pair of Tribals (AC, Wis and resists) or the ones from ME (these arent that uncommon at the cento camp, they rot alot)

Quite frankly Id drop both those rings for a ring of winter/dezok begirding (sp)/ shadel bandit/ burning ring/ dizok signet ring of service combo

Id focus on Wis/mana/HP combos. Resists are resists your gonna need em, but you dont have to wear em. Most people I know in raid guilds (myself included) carry 1 or 2 bags of resist gear with em at all times, if your not a big raider (you seem to be focused on soloing) dont worry about the resist gear so much unless you really find yourself constantly needing it (in which case I have to wonder why)

AC will take care of itself as High lvl gear becomes available. If you arent in a situation where you are raiding/looting high lvl gear then you most likely dont need to focus on the AC side quite so much.

You need to look at items that will give you a maximum return for each slot. Sometimes you will find that sacrificing 3 or 4 ac for a total of 12 more stat points will get you alot farther. Maybe even look into one of those common 5 ac 4 to all stat dragon talisments in West Waste (they rot alot more than you might think).

Some of this info may be to much or to little but based on what you posted about soloing or spending long hours camping quests points to a Shaman that isnt in a power guild, but plays alot and wants to be as uber as he can, its going to be expensive or take you a while......

If you have access to www.allakhazam.com do a search for yourself based on what you want to focus on. I would sudgest lookin at things other than AC as a defining stat though Wis = mana, mana = mana, regen = mana, HPs = mana, big mana pool = more potential for you to play.

BlargX
I agree. My ogre shaman has used a bunch of BP's, including ikky BP and fungi and froggy and barbed ringmail and I don't know what else. Regen is king -- infinitely better than anything else unless it's time for you to bust out your resist gear.

I had my Temple of Veeshan (COV) dragon quest BP in my bag for months before I actually put it on because it had little value to me compared to my fungi until I got torpor. Once I had torpor, I didn't miss the fungi a bit. But until then(and it can take quite a while to get torpor), there is no substitute for a fungi, period. Ikky BP until you can afford a fungi. I'd go so far as to say don't buy anything else for your character ever until you have the money for either. With the nerfed drop rate of fungi's, you're very unlikely to be looting one anytime soon, so think in terms of paying cash.

As far as wearing the SOW boots, I have NEVER died because I didn't have them on. I travel with all my bag slots full, and when I see the SOW icon fading, I hit "I" to click open inventory, then open my second from the top bag, lift the boots out, pick up the TOPMOST bag and put them down in that inventory slot and click them to activate SOW, then put them in the second bag again and hit "I" to close inventory. It takes maybe two seconds -- more than enough time before the icon fades. I can do it while running, whatever, and I'm not one of those coordinated Quake-types who can circle strafe and all that; it's just that it's very easy to do. And plenty quick enough.

When I've had my top slot open, I've just don't have to lift the bag up before clicking the boots, eliminating a step.

If you are worrying about having an easily accessible instacast junk buff because of concerns about mobs that dispell, get a goblin skull earring, or just lose a single bag(my top bag is a disposable spit -- cool for making food on the run, and it only costs a couple plat so i can destroy it if I like) and put your boots in your empty inventory slot. You can even hotkey them so you don't have to open inventory at all.

On your equipment, I'd definitely get the crystal chitin gauntlets. They're plenty good enough even at 60. I have my ToV gloves, but keep them in the bank -- the crystal chitins are better. And they're only a few thou.

The crystal chitin shield is nice, too, and also usually cheap these days. AC 19 wisdom 8 or something like that, and I think maybe 10 versus cold? Kinda guessing; been a while since I wore one, but I liked it a lot. Definitely SBS should be seriously considered, too. Those hitpoints and the AC are probably better for you than more wisdom now.

Personally, I'd dump the flayed turmoilskin belt, too. Mine is in the bank. I like the spiderfur belt better because it has 40 hitpoints. That's a nice boost in hitpoints. And the belt is dirt cheap these days.

Sabertooth amulet and velium diamond rings seem weirdly out of place. Get an imbued ivory collar and either a shadel bandit ring or a AC 6 hp 65 ring(the shadel is much better). Shadel rings are actually a pretty common drop, but nobody ever wants to kill the bandits, so that's why they haven't flooded the game.

As far as the MQ'd BP, riotous health for no mana is a huge boon on raids, and you'd like the stats. But for most people, the gems are worth more than the actual thurg armor you use to make them at some point. Black marbles go for 15k for 3 on my server; it's a personal call as to whether you want to spend that kind of money, I guess. Personally, I think you would be much better off applying that 15k to the eventual purchase of a fungi tunic.

As a matter of fact, except for maybe one or two dirt cheap pieces like the ivory collar or the new ring, I would probably not buy a single thing, and just focus all my saving and earning on buying a fungi. No item you get will have anywhere near the impact on you of the fungi.

Fungi + JBB + epic = holy trinity pre-torpor

Each of those three items drastically increase your power, and together make a powerful synergy.

JBB + epic + torpor = holy trinity POST-torpor

But you can worry about that once you actually HAVE torpor. ;)

Fungi is still good even if you have torpor for some experience camps, but it isn't as insanely great as it is pre-torpor. Shamans don't really have much in the way of mana or health problems post-torpor -- and if they do, they need a complete heal or an evac to solve that problem, not anything a fungi could supply.
