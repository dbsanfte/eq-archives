So how else does this really pan out for shaman and druids?

Just looking at the regular versions, the "100/64/47" rule applies. 3770 = 100; 2420 = 64; 1785 = 47. Now if we compare cost of casting to equal the cleric heal of 3770 (note - this ignores the inherent 10% cleric heal benefit as well as any aa/focus effects; just dealing with base numbers for a bare minimum view of the variances in mana cost):

Druid: at 3.86 hitpoints per mana healed = 977 mana to heal 3770 = 32% more mana to heal the same number of hitpoints.

Shaman: at 3.2 hitpoints per mana healed = 1178 mana to heal 3770 = 59% more mana to heal the same number of hitpoints.

The discrepancy between healing is not a singular element. It is not just how much a heal "heals" it is also about how much a heal "costs." On both ends - shaman are being minimized in this role to a greater extent. Considering the cost of casting, if the situation presents itself where shaman can actually keep up with incoming damage by chain casting these heals, they will soon find themselves unable to keep up with incoming damage because of their mana pools. It is a lose-lose situation.

So, the tradition continues. The double whammy of minimal heals comes with the price of minimized efficiency. Their really is no logic to this. Minimize the ability to deliver higher value heals - that is a done deal, apparently regardless of all arguments, parses, rational, well thought out points made to the contrary. But compensate the minimization by - at the very least - equalizing the efficiency of the heal cost. Or explain why it is necessary to have this dual limitation as a part of game balance.

At the very least ... were they listening ... had a clue ... gave a damn ... the hipoint per heal:mana ratio would be equal already. It is not like this would be that big of a bone to toss. It is not like shaman are not doing anything else in a group except healing. It is not like shaman are not already strapped with the highest mana cost, lowest efficiency set of spells in the game in every other "skill" are they have.

If this were done, the shaman heal would cost 370-380 mana; druid cost 495-510. At least then there is an argument that on a basic level healing was even ... druids just need to cast more often and shaman need faster than is possible.
