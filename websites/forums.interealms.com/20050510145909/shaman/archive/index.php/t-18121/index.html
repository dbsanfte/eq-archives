Jinheim
Beastlords are annoying, but not overly dangerous (to me at least). Their pet doesn't do that much damage, but it interrupts a lot. Between the pet bashing you and the beastlord kicking you, it is difficult to channel spells. If they get you to low health and you cannot channel a heal, you might die. If you try to run from them, the beastlord has significant nukes that can bring you down. In addition to being an interrupting monster, beastlord pets are also very resistant. Any other class's pet I can just slow without ever getting resists, but beastlord pet requires malos and multiple attempts to slow. Once the pet is slow, the vast majority of beastlords forget that they have cure disease spells. I have run in to a whopping 1 beastlord that has cured his pet. The same is true of root. I have had necros and mages that were smart enough to dispell root off of their pet, but never a beastlord.

If he is smart enough to cure his pet of root/slow, then use blind on it. You cannot cure that, and with malos on, it lasts for a long time with barely any mana cost or casting time. Once you have the pet under control, the beastlord is a joke. Their melee damage doesn't even come close to outdamaging quiesence, and without their pet helping, they won't interrupt you very much. They have two types of DD spells: nukes and spears. Spears hurt if you don't have good CR, but they have a long recast time (30 seconds I believe). Their nukes aren't that strong. They have good poison dots but we can cure that. If he tries to burn you down with nukes, just heal yourself (quiescence should cover most of it). He will go oom way before you do. Use a DS potion and he will be taking as much damage as his melee is dishing out.

So, basically, a good beastlord with a lot of AA is a long, tough, and annoying fight (I hate their pets even more than knights with twohand bash). They have a lot of resist buffs (+50 to everything from ferocity, plus whatever else they can cast), and if they add that on top of already decent resists, you will have trouble landing anything. However, if you have good resists, there isn't much they can do to kill you. The pet is more annoying than dangerous, and you can always kite the beastlord just like any other melee (quiesence and run around until full health). It makes for a very long fight, but in the end you should win.
