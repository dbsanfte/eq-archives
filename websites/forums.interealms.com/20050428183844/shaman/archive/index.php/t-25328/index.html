But seriously, it's not the DPS balancing per se that surprises me. It just sounds like Blizzard is only now discovering that the system that they put in place can and will be used in fairly obvious ways to improve your PvE and PvP chances.

The first lesson in survivability that my Shaman discovered was that he could run through fairly heavily populated areas by simply chain-casting his snare totem on the run. The fact that Blizzard couldn't see the PvP and PvE ramifications of this brings back painful memories of Verant discovering in 1999 that a combination of SOW and snare could be used for kiting (!). Combined with the built-in resist adjustment on the Druids' main magic-based DOT line, Druids could easily take down low reds "with little to no danger to the player". Imagine that!

At the time (August 1999), a Verant designer stated in an interview that they had envisioned SOW as a purely travel and escape spell. If they had thought of its combat uses, he said, they would have made it unusable in combat like its higher level versions were. But it was too late to make such a drastic change all of 5 months after the release, so they just nerfed all DOTs. I remember reading the interview and thinking to myself, "Gee, if they didn't see that coming, what else didn't they see and what other basic ability that we thought was 'working as intended' can they nerf on a moment's notice and by hitting something else entirely at that??"

And that is precisely the feeling that I get reading this patch.

This minor DPS change isnt even in the same ballpark as SoE's monk AC nerfage...

The Monk nerf was a complicated and often misunderstood matter. Post-nerf Monks still don't take all that much damage over time -- see Broda's parses here (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?t=5868). However, the damage that they do take is considerably spikier (due to their high avoidance and low mitigation) than the damage taken by the plate classes. And, as you will recall, the Monk nerf went live weeks before the PoP release, when mobs' ATK and average hit numbers were drastically increased, thus leading to mitigation becoming more important than avoidance. It doesn't help that my Monk can beat 3 PoV Golems in a row with little healing required when the fourth Golem will demolish the lizard before the heals can land. Finally, Luclin era itemization with high HP/high AC ALL/ALL items gave way to PoP+ era archetype-based itemization when plate classes got vastly more AC and, to some extent, HP on their armor. Hence the triple hit that Monks took in late 2002-early 2003 and that they haven't fully recovered from since. But hey, my mostly Bazaar-geared Monk can still tank anything lower than Tier 2 PoP as well as anybody else! :)

Anyway, back to WoW. MMORPG balance is a complicated matter. Itemization, abilities, talent combinations, PvP vs. PvE balance, group roles, low level vs. high level characters, soloing vs. grouping vs. raiding -- there are all kinds of things that need to be juggled. If Blizzard's designers can't juggle the relatively obvious stuff mentioned in the patch, it makes me wonder how well they will cope with the more complex issues that they will face when the game matures. Not that it will necessarily make the game unplayable any more than it made EQ unplayable, but it is one reason why I will be keeping my EQ accounts active for a while :)
