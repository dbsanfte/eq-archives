Jinheim
We used to have a spell on test called Khura's Mending, which was like a 2950 heal with a 10 second cast time. It got scraped before ever going live. Why did they do this? Because we do not need it.

If we had direct heals just as good as a druids, then the druid class would no longer have a reason to exist. Why? Because shaman heals combined with 75% slow is about 10x better than druid heals alone.

On top of that, I do not even WANT another 10 second heal. I would have liked quiescence to heal 400 a tick instead of 300, and I would have liked to see a slightly bigger and faster blast heal as a spec spell, but a slow, clumsy 10 second heal just doesnt do it for me. There are basically two types of healing that need to be done: Erratic healing and controlled healing. Erratic healing is when a mob is unslowed, or flurries, or does anything that is unpredictable and/or causes a lot more damage than is easily healed. You generally use fast blast heals to heal damage like this, because that 10 second heal might not land in time. The other kind, controlled healing, is what you do when a mob is slowed and his damage is very predictable and easy to handle. A cleric would let a tank get down to 40% or so and CH him. A shaman, though, can just use quiescence. It is extremely cheap, especially when paired with our awesome mana regen. A 3-secondish cast time quiescence with SCRM behind it makes it very easy to keep up on a tank constantly. KM costs twice as much mana (actually 3-4 times as much since SCM, specialize, and mana pres do not work with it), and heals for less than an SCRM quiescence. Thus, quiescence is infinitely better suited to the task of healing than KM is.

In addition to the power of quiescence, tnarg's is also a really nice heal. With blessing of reverence and my agnarr shield (25% beneficial haste), I have a 2.4 cast time on tnarg's. I also have ornate arms (improved healing 4), which are now quite easy to obtain and available to everyone. My arms add between 1% and 20% to each heal. On top of that, you have healing adept AA's, which can add up to 19% (sadly, in 221 aa, I have not found time to do this line yet). Add in all those modifiers, and you've got a 2.4 second casting heal that regularly hits for 1700, sometimes more. On top of that, with maxed healing gift (which I DO have), you will crit quite often (3k+ crits!).

So, who needed khura's mending anyways? I sure don't :)
