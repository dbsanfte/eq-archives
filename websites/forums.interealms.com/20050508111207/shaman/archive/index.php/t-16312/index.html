Serv
1) Shaman soloing

Generaly shaman soloing is root rotting or tanking a mob in teh face while using torpor / Q to keep hps up.

2) DoT

There are two types of shaman DoTs, disease and poison. Disease tend to be longer lasting, and poison quicker but more damaging. Every shaman dot stacks with each other.

3) Torpor line

When using it soloing and tanking you're going to want to box yourself into a corner anyways. I never really cast it on others minus during downtime (Q is much better for that), but it doesn't overwrite SoW, it just overwrites the effect. Once Torpor wears off, they run SoW speed again.

Roxxanne
Before the 50's, the pet is too wimpy to tank.

There is a phase of shaman life in the 50's (after you get Turgurs at 51) where the pet can tank pretty well against the slowed dark blues you are fighting (think COM), which works nice while you sit on a horse medding and blast away with your JBB, with maybe one dot running to speed things up. This style of fighting worked well even in PoG soloing at 61.

In the 60's in PoP, the mobs hit too hard to let the pet (or even the shaman, really) tank without blowing too much mana on heals. Root rot is the way to solo in PoP.

In the 60's outside PoP, in the face shaman tanking with Torpor/Q up is the way to go. This is also what lets shaman, unlike the other big soloing classes, solo summoning mobs.

1) Shaman soloing

Cast slow, cast dots, mob beats on you, pet chews on mob, heal yourself. This is typicaly the most efficient way to solo until PoP (where mobs just hit too hard and we are forced to root/rot.)

2) Dots

They all stack. I could cast every dot I have from level 1 to 65 on a mob... of course its not really mana efficient to do that. Typicaly you wont cast more than your latest dot and maybe the one from the previous rank.

3) Torpor line

Torpor and Stoicism both snare and melee slow the person being healed. Once torpor/stoicism wears off the person returns to their previous speed -no need to re haste/sow. Torpor (and I believe Stoicism) is limited to your group only. Quiessence provides the healing power of torpor without the drawbacks.

These are very efficient heals, but until you get Q melee classes will hate having them cast on them during combat. Excelent for use on yourself or casters tho.

Calabar
1) Shaman soloing

How well you solo and the method you use will greatly depend on your level and your equipment. Until you get your first pet you are a weak warrior with a bag of tricks. This is not a bad thing, as you have some nice tricks. The ability to slow your opponent and haste yourself is very powerful. Even the lower level versions of these spells makes a noticeable difference.

Once you get your spirit puppy you can deal with mobs even better. Your first few pets are not tanks, but they are a powerful addition to your arsenal. When soloing I would pull a mob with slow and root it near my pet. I’d cast my dots and when root broke I’d tank it while my pup bit it on the butt. This worked very well for me.

Currently, at level 61, I’ll pull a mob with slow and as it slowly beats on me I cast my dots. My pet and I are both hasted, but to be honest I do very little damage to it with any of my weapons. My puppy, on the other hand, does massive amounts of damage. So basically I’m playing meat shield to keep the mob from slowing down the damage my pet is doing. My pet and my dots are doing the real damage. I could also pull with a lesser spell and let my pet get aggro and let him tank. But the mobs I’m soloing right now cast slow on him and the fights take a lot longer that way. With non-slowing mob I’m sure my level 61 pet will make a much better tank.

As you gain levels your solo options will broaden. But how much so depends on your “stuff.” I have never owned a JBB. If you have one soloing becomes much easier. I don’t have my epic. If you do then soloing becomes easier. With great effort, and expense, I now have most of my major damage and healing spells. If you don’t have these for your level then soloing will be more difficult. I don’t have very powerful weapons. If you do then soloing becomes easier. But even the most poorly equipped shaman can solo; it’s just more difficult than it might otherwise be.

2) Dots

Yes, shaman dots stack in any combination, except for casting the same exact spell of course. But so do all druid dots. And from what I understand this is true for all classes. It didn’t used to be true but it was changed with a patch some time back. The same exact dot will also stack on a mob if it’s cast by two different casters. I almost feel sorry for the mobs.

When soloing I rarely cast more than a couple of dots in order to conserve manna. I can canni it back of course, but it’s just that much more down time I’d rather not have. But how many spells to cast will differ from mob to mob. And I always get KEI, if possible, before leaving town, whether solo or grouping. It makes life so much easier.

3) Torpor line

Stoicism, and later Torpor, are incredibly useful spells. They allow us to heal back the damage we do to ourselves with cannibalize in an extremely manna efficient manor. I couldn’t believe how much better life was as a shaman when I got stoicism. And when I got Torpor, less than a week ago, I found myself in awe once again. It’s simply an amazing spell.

Yes, it slows down your run to a slow walk and reduces your attack speed. But when casting it on yourself it really doesn’t mater. When acting as a caster in a group you don’t need to move anyway. And when soloing, unless you have an amazing weapon, you aren’t doing a lot of damage with your swings so swinging a bit slower won’t really hurt. You can cast Torpor when tanking for your pet and still cast spells while your pet is chewing through the mob from behind. Granted, its not the best spell to cast on your group’s tank, but there are times when it could mean the difference between his/her life or death.

Shaman can be very effective at soloing. You just have to approach it differently than other classes. After playing a druid for so long I still miss snare. But we do have root and that can be a very effective tool as well. Try a few different methods and see what works best for you.
