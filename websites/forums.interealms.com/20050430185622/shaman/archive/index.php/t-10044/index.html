While I agree taunt needs some work I think it is important to point out how it exactly works (which in reading through all these posts I did not see listed).

When anyone presses the taunt button (warrior, pallie, sk, etc.) they have a chance based on their skill to succesfully taunt. When this happens the taunter gains the top aggro of anyone currently aggro on the mob +1. This is the key point he is only 1 better then next closest person on the aggro list. Lets look at this from made up numbers:

Monk pulls has 4pts of aggro.
Warrior taunts and succeeds has 5pts of aggro.
Shaman casts slow and generates 6pts of aggro.
Mob proceeds to attack shaman.
Warrior taunts and succeeds and now has 7pts of agro.

The main difference over everyone else and warriors is that they can generate aggro through means other then melee attacks. Warriors for the most part cannot (aside from weapon proc's). So if we look at the same scenario with a paladin:

Monk pulls has 4pts of aggro.
Pallie taunts and succeeds has 5pts of aggro.
Pallie stuns and gains 5pts of aggro for 10 total.
Shaman slows and generates pts of aggro.
Mob stays on pallie.

It is important that everyone grouping with the warrior know how to manage their aggro. The warrior can beat the living crap out of the taunt button, but the only way he has to generate aggro is either proc or melee damage. So it takes him longer to establish aggro then hybrids. It is also easier for somebody to steal aggro from him because he does not generate as much.

If you are using a pallie/sk as a tank in a exp group, then the cleric is going to have to heal a bit more, but everyone can nuke/dot like mad men. It is close to impossible for anyone to take aggro from an SK or Pallie if they don't want to loose it.

If you are using a warrior to tank in a exp group, the cleric will need to heal a lot less. This frees him up to toss some nukes or do some melee which makes up the shortfall in DPS that comes from the other people in the group having to back off a bit (and it isn't that much backing off more then it is nuking later in the fight). Generally if you wait till 80% to begin nuking like a mad man aggro is pretty well on the warrior and it will stay there.

In a raid situation versus anything tougher then Emperor Crush, you are going to need a warrior with defensive/evasive. For this situation there are Enchanter/SK buffs to help the warrior establish and maintain aggro (and to reduce others aggro).

So while a warrior may not be as good at generating aggro or gaining it at times, the rest of the party can do things to help him out. The next time a mob jumps on one of you, might want to consider if maybe your own actions brought it on as opposed to somebody elses (i.e. that god damn warrior won't hit the taunt button).

More importantly I am expendable, the slower, cleric, and tank are not.

On some occasions my snare is resisted, or slow is resisted. In this situation kite and resnare. Now this may seem like alot of time wasted that the group could be killing the mob, but I am usually doing it while the group is finishing up the last mob. On a good run I can chain pull for an hour, and drop a snared/slowed/debuffed mob on the warriors head to taunt off right as we get EXP for the last kill.

Using this strategy in PoV our only healer is often a Shaman or Druid, and they stay FM almost all the time while I chain pull. So we have a AM3 ranger, Warrior, Shaman, and 3 other DPS classes in group. EXP flies...

Now that I am flagged for BoT, HoH, and CoD I find this strategy works equally well, but we usually bring a cleric along because the mobs hit hella hard and resist alot more.

The point of all this. Warrior taunt WORKS. You just need to have your hybrid build aggy at the start of the fight for the warrior to TAUNT over. Then your DPS people can go crazy.
