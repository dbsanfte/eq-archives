Lol, well here is one shaman who not only can do it, but I like to do it. I have 192aa's and pretty well decked out so its a lot easier than it used to be. Now, I'm not saying I'm so uber and great, etc. so off my case in advance (lol), but, it is a good challange and keeps me busy instead of watching tv or browsing the internet on my other computer with a regular group with a cleric.

Chaining single pulls? lol, in my sleep. It's only tough with adds.

But to be safe, I must have crack and virtue and the tank must have virtue (or similar) or I just can't keep fm in a chain pulling group for the "bad pulls". And I have clicky root pants also, very handy for parking. This is anywhere in BOT (except tables where there are just too many adds) and in Tactics pit.

For my fellow high aa shammys I would strongly suggest doing this every once in a while because its fun and a good test of your skills. Now, it is tough at first to get used to it - it takes some practice to keep the tank up with 1 or 2 adds, but it can be done.

Ok and for tricks when you know a bad pull is incoming,

1) make sure you cast Q on tank during the incoming pull (even if the old q is not worn off!!!)

2) start casting malos during the pull, and hopefully on the same mob as your tank is locking on to(lol).

3) use puppy to get first hit on the mob, especially with a warrior the puppy takes the first hit before the warrior gets agro. That's a good trick I rarely see any other shaman do. Saves a whole round of unslowed hits on the tank and rarely kills puppy (although it hurts him big time, lol). But, don't do this on multi-pulls because it will get you agro, just use it on singles to save on mana so you are FM when the bad pulls occur.

4) if 2 mobs make sure someone offtanks an add immediately and its usually just easiest to fight both mobs at the same time. (no need to park). I usually find in BOT and Tactics that the mobs are too resistant to root on incoming unless you use the aa root. (which is another way to do adds if you have the space and time to root on incoming).

5) if 3 mobs make sure someone offtanks an add immediately for just a sec, then drops back to main mob. This gives the shaman time to malos and slow mob #2. Then you need to decide whether to blast the tank with a quick heal or continue on to mob #3 to slow/park. Sometimes mob #2 gets in your face, then you need to root asap, back away and continue to #3.

6) I don't use a horse/lizzy so I can sit down to peel off the mobs to park um.

Now this may sound pretty simple, but the timing is pretty crucial and you must be ready to ignore the 3rd mob and heal the tank a few more times if appropriate. That's usually the most critical split second of decision you have...if the tank dies you are big time toast. That's where I have to disagree with Dread (a few posts up) - I take it very personally when my tank dies.

If there is a 3rd melee in the group things are a bit easier on 3 pulls since the 2 can offtank for just a sec to help you get the slows in.
