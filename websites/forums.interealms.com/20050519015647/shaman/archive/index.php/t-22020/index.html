My only real suggestion is to always remember that, being a shaman, you have the power to operate on your own when you so desire.

That means you need to learn how to solo. Soloing isn't that hard, really, but learning how to do it when you're level 20 means you'll be a master at it when you finally ding 65. Your tactics while soloing will change as you gain access to new spells and new abilities and challenge mobs that have new and different aspects and capabilities, but the ancient tenets of "root rot" and "tank IN TEH FACE" never really change.

Gear can make you a more efficient shaman, it can make you a tougher shaman to kill, it can make you a shaman with a deeper mana pool, but it can't make you a BETTER shaman. Only skill and practice can really do that. I've seen shamans wearing little better than newbie armor and swinging Fine Steel hammers play circles around people who twink out their new shaman in 100k worth of gear they bought out of the bazaar, because they knew the class and knew how to exploit its strengths and minimize its weakenesses, where the uber-geared twink shaman has to rely on his gear to survive and really learns NOTHING about how to be a good shaman. Gear can never take the place of good old fashioned skill.

That being said, remember that your spells always--ALWAYS--come first. You can't be a proper shaman unless you have all your spells. Get your spells and practice with them, come to understand how they work and how you can use them. Even spells that seem to have little value like Vision and Flash of Light (the most underestimated spell in the game!!!!) can have great value when used correctly and wisely.

Oh, and never be afraid to ask questions. That's a good way to learn...by sampling the experience of people who have already been there. They can help you avoid the potholes they encountered as they came up. ;)
