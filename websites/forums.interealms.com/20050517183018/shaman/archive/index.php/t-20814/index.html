The problems came as soon as slow mitigation started. That's our key ability, and basically the whole point to the shaman class. I'm not saying we don't have other things, but slow is and has always been our absolute core. Deciding to mitigate it directly meant mitigating our usefulness - right from the very start.

Giving slows out to so many classes -- slows essentially as good as ours when counting mitigation, exactly as good as ours when mobs are unslowable in the first place, and just a bit different than ours in practical terms in groups(ours are still worthwhile in many raids, at least pre-GoD) -- further devalued the very core of our class.

At this point, outside of raids I would recommend a chanter or a beastlord for most groups over a shaman, easily. In GoD, I have raids to look forward to and some trials to look forward to where my contribution will be essentially nothing, and I will be a burden to my teammates in some of those trials. Not encouraging.

GoD has made our weaknesses apparent to even those thinking of including shamans in other content. I used to get constant tells for groups until recently. Now, whenever people are looking for slowers they are looking for chanters most of the time, or else beastlords. If they didn't realize it before -- as I knew they eventually would -- they're realizing now that slows that are mitigated are essentially the same. In other words, the core thing a shaman brings is the same as what other slowers bring, so there can be only two reasons to bring a shaman. First, that he offers enough outside of his slow that he's preferable to a beastlord or enchanter, or second, that there is no other slower available at all.

At this point, it's quite clear to most people in the game that the first reason to include a shaman is not valid. He does NOT add enough outside a heavily mitigated slow to make him a better choice than a beastlord or enchanter. So to get groups I'm forced to rely on people who want my shaman for the second reason -- there are no other slows available. Or to play only with the kind of very old friends who don't mind playing at a lower level of effectiveness. So I can rely on desperation on the one hand or the pity and/or charity of old friends to get a group, or I'm out of luck.
