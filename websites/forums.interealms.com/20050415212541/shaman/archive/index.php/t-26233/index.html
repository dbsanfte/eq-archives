I posted earlier on how I came very close to soloing the Fabled Baron in Sebilis and I speculated that the Fabled Duke would be of similar difficulty. Well, suffice to say it took six hours, but I'm now the proud owner of this:

http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=82715

The good news is that it's soloable by guildless, pre-VT folks although it's not an easy fight by any means! So, here's a quick rundown of what to do if you want one:

Prerequisites:

* VP 3 (lesser levels may work, but not checked)
* Spirit Call AA (any level)
* Spirit of the Leopard / Panther
* Spiritual Channeling
* Ancestral Aid is useful, but not necessary

And now the walkthrough:

* Head over to Old Sebilis and the bridge near the zoneout. Clear the trash beetles and frogs down to the pyramid.
* Retrace your steps and head into the Disco. Your goal is to grab the ilis wizard on the podium, but don't hurt him. Head back to the pyramid door (with the wizard thumping you) and once you're there, DoT him. Move as close to the door as you can, spin round and VP the wizard through the door - he'll summon you through.
* Clear down into the crypt (beware that a Fabled Caretaker can spawn down there - with luck there'll just be a ghoul frog that you can leave alone).
* The first tricky part is the two cleric golem mobs - I VP'd one in a corner then meleed the other, with Leopard on both the pet and myself. They go down quickly and if you've got a stun proccing weapon or the Slam skill all the better.
* There are four rooms off the Crypt, each with a named or placeholder skeleton. The nameds run, the Fableds and the placeholders don't (so if you're daring you can invis past the clerics and fight the mobs in their rooms).
* The room you're after is labelled A on the EQAtlas map:

http://www.eqatlas.com/kunark/sebilis1map.html

* If the fabled mob is up, he'll rush you as soon as you step in the room. I always made sure I had Q, Focus, haste and Leopard on the wolf and Q on myself before going in, otherwise it would be messy. There are three possibilities:

* Skeletal duke (common) - light blue at 70, hits in the 130s or so, summons, easy kill.
* Arch Duke Latol (rare) - the normal Duke, hits in the 170s, easy kill, drops Ykesha club, snare whip or JBB.
* The Fabled Arch Duke Latol (rare) - the Fabled mob. Triples for 827, mitigates slow to "mostly", enrages and summons.

Once you've got the Fabled Duke, the main thing is to get him slowed - Nihil landed first time, as did Turgur's when I tried. Then, while Q is still on you and the wolf, release the wolf pack and cast Leopard on them, pausing to heal the pet if needed (and reslowing with Turgur's would be a good idea too).
My priority was to stay the hell away from Latol, leaving the wolf to tank. There's a strange feature of EQ in that healing pets is zero aggro, so just pop SC, keep Leopard and Q on the wolf as best you can and heal away.

You'll need to keep an eye on Latol as he enrages at 10% - that'll be fatal to your wolf and it's how I wiped the first time to the Fabled Baron. The best strategy on enrage is to move up and tank the mob yourself, chain healing yourself while the wolf procs away from behind. With luck, 4 or 5 minutes after you started you'll have a shiny new Fabled JBB to play with!

If you're feeling adventurous the Fabled Baron drops a Fabled Tash stick and the Fabled Harbinger drops Fabled knuckledusters (MNK/BST only). I've not seen a Fabled Hierophant yet, but I can guess what he'd drop! Strategy for the others is the same as for the Duke. Respawn is 30 minutes, FWIW.

(As an addendum, an alternative strategy would have been to chain heal myself and tank - however, I tend to get stunned a lot and there are some snare/boil blood type spells cast as well. Without leopard it would be much harder as the pet wouldn't hold aggro at all.)
