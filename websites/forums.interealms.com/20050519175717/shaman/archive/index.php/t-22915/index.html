Exes, since you asked ... IMO:

You are still pretty new to the game. Whichever class you decide to play, take it slow. Learn the class. That is the biggest fault I see in newer players now - the presumption that 65 is where they have to be and they have to be there yesterday.

You will see the comment "it is the journey, not the destination" used a lot around here. There is great truth in that. There are so many places you can go and experience first hand at the appropriate level that will simply rock-your-socks with fun and excitement. Each level those places become fewer and fewer.

As for which class, the truth is you have to make that decision. My suggestion, however, would be shaman. Take your time and learn what shaman can do and when is the best time/place for them to do this-or-that. You won't use all your tools all the time. Figuring out when to use based on the situation at hand is part of the fun.

Reason I suggest shaman is their greater room for error. Shaman can make mistakes and still recover to live and succeed through an encounter. You will learn a lot from those mistakes. On the otherhand, the margin for error with enchanters is much smaller. While both classes have the tools to recover, the shaman being able to take more damage while getting things back under control is the key. While the details are hard to explain, enchanters are a finesse class and take tons of patience to play to higher levels.

That is fancy talk - enchanters die a lot. As you level up there will be more deaths from those tanks trying to "help" by attempting to aggro a mob you are mezzing, casters popping off an AE just as you lock down the last add, someones pet getting a proc off just as mez lands ... on and on.

So I suggest shaman. Not to avoid the pitfalls of an enchanter, but rather to get an experience handle on the game first. You can always make an enchanter later.

The last reason for shaman over enchanter - shaman can solo or group with equal ease. Early on enchanters are very tough to solo.
