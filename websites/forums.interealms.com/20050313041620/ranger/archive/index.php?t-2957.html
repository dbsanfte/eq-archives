Choose any race you want, stats are eventually irrelevent in EQ as gear will overcome any starting stats problem. That said, the halfling has the best stats. It's up to you if you want to be at crotch level for the rest of your EQ life.

Wicca, welcome to the ranks of the long-suffering elite.

My biggest advice to you now is to start studying and practicing tactics now, when it isn't of critical importance. Rangers are tactical fighters. We live or die by tactics.

The biggest two things for you to start studying are Aggro (or hate) management and pulling.

Aggro management is learning to gain or lose the attention of the monster when ever you want.

Pulling is where you leave your group in a safe place, then go out and get something to chase you back to where your group waits.

I suggest you invest some time using the search feature on this list to read up on these two topics. We have some very talented people on this list.

With the resources here on the Glade, you can easily become a 20th level ranger who is more skilled than some 40+ rangers.

Lots of new EQ players HATE HATE HATE being night-blind. I recommend staying away from any of the night-blind races for your first character.

I tell people this all the time, and nobody listens but... treat your first character as a throw-away. Just make something and play around with it for 5-10 levels. Then use what you learned to start over with what really appeals to you.

Finally, when it comes down to what's really important in this game, looks > stats. Yep it's true. Stats will be adjusted so much with buffs and gear that those 5 points here or there won't matter. You are stuck with your looks forever. Well at least until you get wolf-form at level 49.

Ok - here is how I break down rangers:


Halflings:
Best overall starting stats, but stuck at butt and crotch level their entire lives. Unknown at this time if they will have line of sight issues with archery at higher levels.

Wood Elves:
If you even think you might get into tradeskills, go with wood elf as they get all the ones that pertain to rangers.

Humans:
Even starting stats overall, but blinder than a bat at night to the point that they will be dependant on vision enhancing items or spells.

Half Elves:
Traditionally have been the best looking rangers, despite what those jealous of our looks will say. In any case, we are the most adept with archery due to having higher base Dex (the stat it works off of) than humans & wood elves and lack the shortness issues of halflings. Our weaknesses are low base wisdom and a lack of culture (which only really hurts if you do tradeskills.)
