Is it a good idea to augment a weapon that already has a proc? Executive Summary: Nobody knows yet, and imbued procs (e.g. Cry of Thunder) are not an issue.

There's a largish thread in the general forum about this issue. The conclusions so far are:

1) An inherent proc (e.g., AHB's lifetap) and an imbued proc (CoT) do not interfere with each other, except that...

2) It's not clear that an inherent proc and an imbued proc can fire on the same swing of the same weapon -- but that would be such a rare occurence anyway, it's negligible if they cannot.

So, imbued procs really aren't part of the debate. Several people have parsed this, and it's clear that imbued procs and inherent procs get along quite well.

However, the big question remaining is:

3) If a weapon has an inherent proc and you add a second inherent proc (via an augment), will the two inherent procs interfere with each other? In other words, if you add Vampire's Kiss (75-point lifetap) to your AHB, will you now get, overall, twice as many lifetap procs as before? Or will you get EITHER the AHB's normal proc OR the vampire's kiss proc, but with the total # of procs remaining the same as it was before you augmented.

Simply stated, do you get anything out of adding an augmented proc to a weapon that already has a proc?

Nobody has posted any serious tests of this issue on the glade. Somebody's going to have to go guinea pig ;)

Imbued (CoT, NDT, CoF) procs do not interfere with innate procs like Wrath of Ice, and it doesn't make much sense for augment procs and innate procs to compete either. A programmer would have to create extra code/calculations to create such a system, which wouldn't be done by accident. I doubt that they'd intentionally make the augment procs detrimental when (a) it's easier to program innate and augment procs to work independently, and (b) it makes sense.
