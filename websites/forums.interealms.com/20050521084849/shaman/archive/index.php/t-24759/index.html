Blazz, I responded to him. Basically, the argument is this.

Pre mitigation--100/64/47.
post mitigation--100/64/47.

Pre mitigation-1000 dps mob. 250 dps slowed
Post mitigation-400 dps mob. 250 dps slowed (assumes a 50% mitigation)

Applicable percentages:
400/1000 = 40% weaker
62.5%/25% = 250% stronger (dps after slow with 50% mitigation vs dps after 75% slow)

Now, if SoE tunes the slowed mobs to our level, then the mob does the same damage from our perspective. However, since the 100/64/47 is unchanged, then the cleric healing an unslowed mob is now healing a mob that's 40% of what the cleric is tuned for.

Turn it around. If, as I believe, SoE tunes the unslowed mob to the cleric, and uses mitigation to prevent it from being too weak for groups with real healers, then it's doing 250% more damage than before from the shaman perspective. Again, our healing hasn't changed with mitigation.

So, either the cleric is healing a mob that's 40% of where it should be, or the shaman is healing a mob that's 250% more powerful than it should be.

Either way, healing is out of whack. It's a substantial change in relative healing power between shaman and either cleric or druid.

Since I don't hear either class complaining that the game has been completely trivialized since they heal so well, and I see lots of complaints about shaman healing, I'm inclined to go with the theory that shaman healing is too low. :p
