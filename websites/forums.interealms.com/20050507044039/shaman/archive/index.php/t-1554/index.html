OK, here's what's really going on here fellas. (FYI had a high level magician and most magicians understand this phenomenon quite well since they spend considerable amount of time learning to "pet tank" - more on this later)

Pets do not hold aggro like a player does. It's very hard for a pet to actually taunt a mob off of another pet. Basically, in 99.9999% of sitiuations, the mob can be considered to have equal amounts of aggro for all pets currently attacking it.

When a mob has this "equal aggro" for the pets, there is a very predictible way to tell which pet is going to take the hits. It's ALWAYS the pet CLOSEST to the mob - JUST LIKE A ROOTED mob behaves toward players. So what you say? Well, when you consider that the five pet types all have FIXED attack distances (not sure about shrink or grow) and that they differ from each other, you can see why one pet always takes all the hits.

The order from shortest attack distance to longest distance from mob is:

Enchanter, Shadowknight, Shaman, Necromancer, Magician.

Pull mob (no tank), sick mage pet and necro pet - necro pet will take all the hits and die first.

Pull mob (no tank), sick shadowknight pet and shaman pet - SK pet dies first.

Etc....

The only time taunting and aggro comes into play is when you send equal pets at a mob. Two mage pets? Whichever one taunts and hits the best is gonna take the hits.

This makes sense to me and i believe it was intended. If you were fighting 4 mobs - all attacking you roughly the same - wouldn't you kill the easiest one first to reduce your damage taken?

The toughest pets attack from way out, while the weakest pets attack at a much closer range - thus mobs always go after the weakest pet first (assuming no large aggro difference has occured)

When using pets as tanks this can be very tricky to manage which pet takes the hits.

- Drum Roll.... Pet tanking

Pet tanking is a fairly difficult tactic to learn, but once mastered you can assure that dog dog will live to see another day while that hitpoint having mage pet can take the beating.

Basically you park your pets - preferably have the mage pet (or your toughest pet) away from the other pets. Go pull a mob. Run back to camp. Now pull the mob RIGHT OVER THE MAGE PET. Stop running at just the right spot so your mob is standing ON the mage pet. Then you have the mage /pet attack FIRST. Then everyone else /pet attacks. What happens is that the mage pet will attack the mob from the spot it was guarding. All the other pets then engage from their normal attacking distance (which is farther away then what the mage pet is currently attacking from) If the mob moves out of melee distance from your mage pet, the mage pet will move, and then rengage at his normal attack distance (which is far away) Then dog dog dies. But if you get that mob ON the mage pet and keep him there (by not aggroing him), the mage pet will NOT move, and all the other pets will be farther away - thus not taking any hits.

Of course when doing this you have to be careful about not aggroing the mob sicne as soon as he moves to come after you, you'll have zero chance of getting that mage pet on top of the mob again.

It sounds difficult, but you'll find that after 4 or 5 pulls you'll figure it out. It's really not that hard to do, and VERY few people actually do this. I've personally duo'ed with a warrior using this tactic, and since the warrior had a clue, and watched his attack distance (careful not to move the mob) he NEVER TOOK A SINGLE HIT. He'd just pull on top of the pet, make sure he was attacking the mob from as far away as possible, and he NEVER EVER hit the taunt button. Then we'd let the mage pet's regen kick in (with some heals from his master) and then the warrior would go pull another - boom - group without a cleric :)

The problem with this tactic however, is that it's nearly imposible to explain to somebody in-game (look how long this post is) so it's just easier to shout for a healer. But tell your friends and most of all TRY IT OUT, and you'll see that i'm not full of BS :)

Hope that expains the shaman pet taking all the damage phenomenon, and I hope this was clear as it's difficult to explain to somebody without diagrams and the such (if you're confused and really want to learn more, go do a search at the mage compendium on "pet tanking")
