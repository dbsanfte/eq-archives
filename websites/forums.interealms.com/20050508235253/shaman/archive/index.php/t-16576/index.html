Xev_Grammar
You should treat it like any other mez. Your dot will invariably mess up a bard's mez. Mind you, the bard's mez is a lot shorter duration than enchanter mez so you will notice that the bard has to mez, twist twist, then mez again, twist, twist. You would help the bard a lot if you malosini'd or malos'd his mezd targets.

Also, I sometimes root those targets too if they're not close to anyone else to try to alleviate some of the burden off of the bard who has better things to twist.
Zorash
Bard mez is not actually a mez, it's basically an automatic chain stun. The stun hits every 18 seconds, and as long as it isn't resisted, it is enough to keep a mob perma-stunned, giving the appearence of being mezd.

So, if you dot a mob, and do damage every tick (6 seconds), you WILL break a bard's mez, and they won't be happy.

Also, a good point is made about malox. I malos all mobs that get mezd to reduce the chance of an ench/bard getting a resist and getting pummeled. The chance of resists is even higher with a bard since the check occurs every 18 seconds.

Artebus
As a level 65 bard used to grouping with at least one shaman, I can reply to a few issues here.

First, bard mez is a mez and not a regular stun. Any damage will break it, just as with enchanter mez. DoTs will make it impossible to keep a mob mezzed by a bard.

Second, until level 65, all bard mez songs move the mob a little bit. At best a bard can use this for mob placement, at worst it's an annoyance that a bard needs to work around to avoid the mobs ending up in bad places. (The level 65 bard mez song, instead of moving the mob around, makes it do its dance animation, if it has one.)

Third, I personally am all for rooting mezzed mobs, but not as a high priority. I'd prefer malos and slow on the mob first, and then root if nothing else needs doing. Rooted mobs also have the advantage of not going straight for the cleric when you start fighting the mob. Note that bard mez movement, not being a nuke, will not prematurely break root. I tend to keep mezzing anyway, because most of the places I tend to fight are close quarters that have mobs with long reach.

Last, a good bard who uses target toggle can easily keep up a 2-3 song twist while mezzing one additional target. In the worst case, the bard can melee a mob while keeping mez on one other mob. Only when the bard needs to do a lot of retargetting does it get difficult.

I just wanted to bring up that rooting a mob that a bard is mezzing, *may* be pointless, since each time the bard mezzes it pushes/pulls the mob and may break the root.

This is a very very common misunderstanding of how root works.

Root doesn't say "keep critter in this loc"

Root says "reduce this critter's natural movement to zero"

You can move a rooted critter around all you want, it stays rooted regardless of PC/pet push, spell knockbacks or even Gate.

For that matter, you can be rooted and gate to another zone and you'll be rooted at your bind spot until it fades. :)
Kriel Darkbane
Most of the important points have already been made, but I can't pass up a thread about bards and shamans together. ;) I have a 65 bard with way too much total /played (although not nearly as much as some!) and a 57 shaman.

It's pretty trivial far a bard to keep one mob mezzed (using the switch between last two targets button) while melee'ing and twisting - assuming the mez resist rate is low. This means that the best thing you can do to help the bard mez is drop a Mal* on the target. If the mob never resists a good bard can mez while losing virtually nothing (1 of 4 songs out of his twist).

A minor point that hasn't been made yet - bard mez's last UP TO 18 seconds, but the actual guaranteed duration is only 12 seconds. It's a 3 tick song, but if it starts during the last second of the first tick, that counts as the first tick. And it'll break in under 13 seconds. Bardsongs take 2.5-3 seconds to cast (I forget exactly, pretty much 3 seconds). So this means that the theoretical best a bard can do "permanently" is mez four mobs. And that's with no resists. So if the bard is mez'ing 3-4 mobs, Tossing Mal* around on them is even more important. The bard occlusion/harmony of sound resist debuffs also only last 12-18 seconds, so a bard who needs to use those before each mez can only mez two mobs.

Judge whether mal* is needed to help the mez'ing by how hard it is for you to stick slows. The resist check is identical, I think. Just a base MR check, with the high level mez's. The L 50 mez's have some MR mods, but the higher ones don't. If you need MR debuff to stick slow consistently, the bard needs it to stick mezz consistently.

Slowing something the bard has mez'd is very help, just like with a chanter mez'ing. But even morso, since the tiny duration means that a string of resists could pop the mob loose at any moment. Root will play perfectly well with bard mez's. Root parking the mez'd ones is nice, but most likely they'll be right on top of the party when they get mez'd. So if you root them there, the bard still has to mez. A bard COULD move the mob away with the pushback on mez, and then stop mezz'ing, But that'll take time, and unless there's several mobs, it's probably not worth it. In extreme situations (pulls of 5 or more, where the bard has 4+ mobs to mez, which is somewhat beyond his capabilities due to the limited mez duration), it's hugely helpful for the shaman to slow/dot/drag away and root one or two, to get the numbers down to what the bard can handle. In these cases, always pick on the melee mobs and leave the casters to the bard, where mez is much more effective than root and slow (but most all shamans probably know that bit anyway).

More than you wanted to read most likely, but bards and shamans together is my favorite topic, as they're my two favorite classes, and I always love to have the one I'm not playing in my group.
