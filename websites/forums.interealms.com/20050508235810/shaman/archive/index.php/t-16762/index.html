Armak
They stack as far as buffsigns/slots go, BUT the EQ engine then ONLY uses the highest haste buff you got and ignores the lesser haste buff, they dont go 80%+60% = 140% haste.

IF you got a lesser haste buff with some AC/STATS/REGEN or whatever adjustments on it, those will still work, but not the lesser haste that are part of the buff.

Tikker
hastes from the same version will not stack, but you can mix and match haste groups


ie

V1 haste is worn/innate haste. (FBSS, RBG, CoF, etc etc)

V2 haste is cast haste (Celerity, SLTW, Vallon, etc etc etc)

V3 haste is the bard overhaste(Rizlona's, Warsong of Vahshir, etc)

you can stack V1 + V2 + V3

you cannot stack multiples of V1, V2 or V3*



*Bards have a goofy line of spells (ervaj) that is kinda v2.5 [allows you to stack with V2, but won't break the haste cap like V3]
no bards ever use them so just ignore them =P

Harwel
Kinda correct, item haste is different from v1, v2, v3 haste, it is worn haste.

v1 haste: most haste spells and bard songs
v2 haste: ervaj line of bard songs, will not exceed haste cap
v3 haste: Vah Shir line of songs, Rizlona's, Call of the Muse, ranger AA. Will exceed haste cap.

You can stack Worn, v1, v2, and v3 haste and effects will be cumulative. Two or more V1 hastes (eg SLTW, Vallon's, Warsong of Zek) will only use the highest haste.

Monk epic haste is also v1. Monk epic haste stacking is a very common misconception.
