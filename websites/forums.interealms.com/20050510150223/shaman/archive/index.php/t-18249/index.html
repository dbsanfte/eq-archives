Junder
To help clarify the new system, I went to the message boards and looked for
questions that people still had and posed them to the EQLive team. If you have
any other questions about the system, please send them to the Developer's Corner
and I'll try to provide answers. </p>
<p><b>Q: I'm still confused about Endurance. Will it completely replace Stamina?
How will that affect swimming? </b></p>
<p>A: Yes, Endurance will completely replace Stamina. Some of the occasions that
used Stamina in the old system will no longer require the expenditure of a
resource. For example, swimming will no longer use a resource. </p>
<p><b>Q: If jumping costs Endurance, and only melee classes and hybrids have
Endurance... won't that be a problem for casters when they want to jump? </b>
</p>
<p>A: All classes get an Endurance bar and an Endurance pool. So everyone can
still jump. </p>
<p><b>Q: I understand that the new combat skills and all disciplines will use
Endurance, and that's why Endurance won't be affected by any spells that
currently affect Stamina. But why remove the Stamina effects from those spells?
Some classes gain some value to their group through the use of those spells. </b>
</p>
<p>A: Since we are removing Stamina, there won't be any value to spells that
affect Stamina. </p>
<p>There will be additional spells added that modify Endurance. Right now these
will be buffs that increase your total pool. We are not planning on adding any
that would regenerate the pool. These spells will be tuned to balance the
ability to aid in the return of Endurance. Just exchanging the Stamina effects
with Endurance effects wouldn't allow us any control over the balance of the new
system and its interaction with spells. It hasn't been decided which classes
will get those spells yet, but obviously we'll be taking into consideration the
effects of the changes to the existing spells might have on caster desirability.
</p>
<p><b>Q: Will NPCs be using these new abilities? </b></p>
<p>A: Not at this time. Frankly, there's a lot of work involved in allowing NPCs
to use these abilities, and it would have a rather wide-spread effect on the
challenge of those NPCs that would be complicated to evaluate. So we're only
giving this to PCs. </p>
<p><b>Q: There seems to be some confusion about how these new skills will be
used. Will we be clicking a button on the screen with our mouse, or can we make
hotbuttons with the new skills and just press a key to activate them? </b></p>
<p>A: You will be able to click on the icons, use hotbuttons in your hotbutton
box or access a melee skills box only for these abilities through CTRL+ #. </p>
<p><b>Q: You mentioned that you are making some other changes to melee,
including giving warriors an automatic mitigation percentage. Will PC warriors
of all levels get this ability? </b></p>
<p>A: PC warriors of all levels will gain the mitigation boost. </p>
<p><b>Q: These changes look very interesting. But some of us are concerned that
these changes might overbalance things. What steps are you taking to keep the
balance pendulum from swinging too far toward the pure melee classes? </b></p>
<p>A: We'll be testing this system on the Test server until it goes Live. The
big advantage of the system is the flexibility. Adjusting it won't require days
of programmer time. If we discover that a facet of the new skills is over or
under powered, we can adjust it. </p>
<p><b>Q: Regarding the new discipline system using Endurance: will it be
possible to stop a disc midway through its use when it doesn't make sense to
keep it up, in order to save some Endurance? </b></p>
<p>A: Yes, you will be able to stop a discipline while it is active. If you
cancel a discipline, however, you must still wait the normal recharge time
before using it again. </p>
<p><b>Q: You mentioned that these new skills will be learned by finding ancient
tomes. Will these tomes be No Drop? </b></p>
<p>A: Right now the plan is to introduce them as droppable. There may be some
tomes that result from special quests that are no-drop in the future. </p>

I think many of you realized this already, but some comments and questions seem to have missed it.

The Stamina "stat" and the Stamina "bar" are two different things. (to illustrate, when you use up the stamina bar by jumping or swimming, your stamina stat does not fall, and your hp's from the stat are unchanged)

The stamina stat is not changing, as far as I can tell. So Boar, Vigor, etc. should be unaffected. Not sure about Acumen, since you will apparently no longer consume stamina, and would have little use for stamina regen.

It is the stamina bar that is changing - being pre-empted to display a new characteristic, Endurance. This is apparently an entirely new, uh, stat. No existing buffs would affect that new endurance stat.

The buffs for that new endurance stat would make sense for a shaman, but are not "obviously" shaman buffs. Remember, most of our stamina buffs (and all of the ones I actually cast because of their stamina effects) would still do what they have always done.
