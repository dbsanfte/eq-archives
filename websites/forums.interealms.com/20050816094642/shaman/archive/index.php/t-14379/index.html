I'm sure others could explain this better, but here's my take on AA's:

You have to spend 6 general points to 'unlock' the archetype abilities. Common spends are Run3, Regen3, Metabolism, possibly even Wisdom. Most of these cost 1 point per 1 ability (or increase in ability) so Run3 -- 3 levels of Run -- costs 3 AA points.

Once you've spent 6 general (they have to be spent, having 6 points banked won't help!) you can start buying archetype skills. Most of the archetype skills cost 2/4/6 -- 2 points for first skill, 4 for second, 6 for third. So by the time you purchase 3 levels of an archetype skill, you'll have spent the 12 points required to unlock the class skills (or if you purchase one level of six skills).

Most class skills require (in addition to spending 12 archetype skill points) some archetype requirement -- for example, CannV requires MC3. Class skills may have just one level -- like CannV -- or may have three levels; if the latter they usually (but not always) cost 3/6/9 (so 3 levels of a class skill would cost 18 points).

In each of these cases, meeting the requirement of points spent (not levels, not skills, just total points in that category) unlocks the next 'type' of skills -- and grants you a nifty new title to boot!

So a good 'general' roadmap to CannV would be:

Two of the following: Run3, Regen3, Metabolism3.
MC3
CannV.

Then you get to all the 'other' fun AA's -- like SCM3 which saves 10% mana on all spells cast, MGB (always handy for raids), planar power (which increases all your stats maximum by 5), and so on. :)

The simple facts are.

Horses noticably increase the rate at which you eat food. Anyone who's had one can confirm this since it's easily noticable. To me this was noticable to the point that it annoyed to have to stock up on food/drink so frequently using iron rations/pings.

Fact 2
Stat foods and drinks can provide descent stat bonuses. The better the stats the rarer/more expensive the food/drink on average. Someone already linked the platters. 75hps/mana food is nothing to sneeze at. If you want resists you can get those too. Food and drink have become yet another stat slot for alot of people.

Combine those 2 facts together and you get the obvious outcome that it can be hard to use stat food/drinks when you use a horse. You have a couple options in dealing with this. You can buy/make lots of food/drink and just accept it. You can not us either the horse or stats food/drinks. Or you can do what you can to reduce the consumption rate, IE buy metabolism 3. Or you can try force feeding to save your stat foods.

Personally I don't find the force feeding much of a viable option. Force feeding halves the duration that the food/drink lasts from it's normal duration. The decreased duration combined with accelerated consumption from a horse = food doesn't last very long. Making it a real pain to keep the food in stock and increasing the odds of accidentally consuming the good stuff.

Personally use halas meat pies and kaladim constitutionals as my standard foods. That adds 30hps 10 sta 5 agility 5 dex and 5 sv all. Those wouldn't be practical imo with out metabolism. There are other options out there that add even more stats out there too. Those are just what i use right now.

Skeeter
Your topmost food item and topmost drink item are both consumed when you are 'hungry enough' and 'thirsty enough' -- if you forcefeed other food/drink first you'll never reach that point.

Whatever food / drink would be consumed if that point were reached, is what is considered for stat purposes. So if your top bag, top left corner, had a stack of hero sandwiches, you'd get the bonuses. If the stack right below it was MTP's, you wouldn't get the int/wis from MTP -- but if you swap them around (even if you just ate a hero sandwich because you reached that point of hunger) you'd immediately get the int/wis. Same for drinks.

And you can get bonuses for your topmost food *and* topmost drink at the same time (but not two foods nor two drinks)

For those that sneer at metabolism -- and for those that are using fish rolls and milk! ;) -- here is something to consider. Now, if you're max wis / sta this isnt such a big deal, but for the majority of us...

Foods to consider:

Misty Thicket Picnic (aka 'MTP') -- 5 str/sta/wis/int. Miraculous meals, so easy to forcefeed fish rools or iron rations to avoid wasting these. I play on Test, so not sure on prod prices, on Test usually 10ish pp.

Halas Meat Pies (aka 'HMP') -- 5 dex/sta/agi and 30 hps. Also miraculous, but giant sized and weigh 7.2 pounds each. On Test these are 5ish pp each if not cheaper -- can often get stacks for free off folks skilling up.

Hero Sandwiches -- 10 str, 5 dex/sta/wis/int/agi, 30 hps, 30 mana. Takes hero meat from HOH, so not sure if they're for sale -- on Test they're 'friends only.' But yummy! Miraculous meal.

Justice Fruit Pies -- 15 dex, 5 sta/wis, 15 hps, 15 mana. Miraculous meal that takes a POJ forage and a couple of celestial essences -- so plan on paying a bit more for these. Again, on Test I make these for friends only.

Drinks -- not as many options here!

Qeynos Afternoon Tea -- 5 dex/agi/wis/int. Miraculous drink.

Kaladim Constitutionals -- 5 sta, 5 to all resists. Miraculous drink.

Surefall Ranger's Trail Juice -- 4 dex/sta/wis. Miraculous drink.

Grobb Liquidised Meat -- 6 str/sta, -1 int/wis. Miraculous drink.

All of these drinks take a little work to make (either foraged items, groundspawns that take a while to collect, or a lot of combines) so they won't be 'cheap' -- but they're worth it IMO.

I'm sure there are other foods/drinks that are favorites -- but these are the ones I tend to make/use the most...

+5 to MR/FR/CR/DR/PR.
