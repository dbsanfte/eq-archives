Here is my take on it.

I always malos on inc (I just /ass the puller and as soon as it is in range I cast unless they feign then I interrupt, though rarely do feingers even pull). It's not as fast as getting slow on right away sure, but it really doesn't take up too much time. Since most if not all of the 5 seconds are taken up during the inc run, all that I lose are approximately 5 seconds at most for refresh + slow. I very, very rarely get resists this way. This of course changes some on multiple pulls depending on size and group make up and so on.

If I get beat on, I don't care since the mob is slowed. If it's a SK or a Pally, I don't get aggro and that's fine. If it's a warrior, and a decent one he gets it off relatively fast (and then keeps it for the rest of the fight which is nice).

I usually go on to cast 1-2 shots of Tears of Saryrn depending on mob health, mana situation and whether or not I have to redo any buffs, usually Ferine Avatar or SLTW. If I have to heal as well then I don't rain as much either, unless the tank is superbly equipped and quiescence keeps up on it's own. The rains rarely if ever give me aggro, regardless of tank.

The problem I see is slightly different. I have noticed almost no difference at all in pally/sk/warrior tanking. There are bad ones and good ones for each class but on the whole they don't seem much different to me. Now pallies can often stun, and shadowknights tap so this reduces the healing needed for them even more. Most clerics seem to leave themselves room for error (which makes sense, since an unlucky round could result in 1000+ damage taken). This usually means that the HP difference between warriors/sk's/pallies is negated. At least these have been my observations on average. When I heal, I never heal for full HP anyway so the max hp makes little difference.

Given AA's and nice new 2handers (or nice old 2handers) the damage output of the warriors is approximately equivalent to that of a knight. In some cases shadowknights have a clear advantage with a pet, dots, taps and so on.

Now given paladins have useful abilities such as stun, group heals, fast heals, and root; sk's have useful abilities such as feign death, group mana/lifetap, snare, and various attack buffs. Warriors have.................

Unless defensive is needed, or the level of both warrior and mobs that you are fighting allow for a considerable advantage in warrior mitigation (one that is not offset by cautious clerics) then warriors are at a loss even without aggro issues. This is why I don't see why they should have so many problems with aggro.

Hey Guys/Gals,

Just thought I would share my views on shaman agro management in groups.

If I have a warrior as the MT in my group I will not slow until after the warrior has engaged the mob. Warriors have superior hitpoints and defensive skills to hybrid tanks so can take the extra unslowed pounding without any trouble. By doing this you give a warrior time to build agro.

If I have an SK or a Pally as the MT in my group I will slow the mob on incoming as SK's and Pally's have far superior taunting ability to a warrior because of their spells. SK's will snare and Pally's will stun, both which give them instant agro control. Personally in EXP groups I much prefer having a hybrid MT over a warrior.
