Ok beastlord soloability and necro soloability are very, very different.

There are 2 different kinds of soloers. Those who take the damage mobs put out but can deal with it, and those who avoid mob damage.

The former includes things like warriors at level 2, when you dont exactly need support heals to kill an orc pawn, yet it still yeilds exp. Another example is us shamans who solo in teh face, we take the damage of mobs, but deal with it by slowing said mob and with our mediocre heals.

The latter are kiters. People who solo by running out of melee range and killing their opponent.

Now, kiters are usually high DPS or high utility classes, IE bards and necromancers. That's all great and fantastic. They are great at soloing for quick exp because they make fast kills.

However, those who take the damage have more versatility in what they can solo, even if it is slightly less efficient in execution. A kiter cannot solo a mob that summons, or a mob in very close quarters.




That being said, I as a shaman have soloed giants in Bastion of Thunder. I have seen Beastlords solo giants in Bastion of Thunder. We can do this because we slow, take the damage, and kill the mob. Kiters cannot do this, because they cannot handle unslowed mobs, and the giants summon.

A beastlord can solo just about anything a necromancer can, but if the mob does not summon (or if the mob is undead), the necromancer will be more efficient in soloing.
