Spiritbinder
Apparently VI seems intent on 'balancing' until all classes are the same, or close enough. The whole thing reminds me of some badly broken DnD game where the GM keep handing out freebie magic items to the whining players.
Every week now encounters are being made just that little bit easier - as if most of them weren't easy enough.

I think a lot of this can be traced back to "The Vision". Brad and friends came up with a big map of what the classes would do, and how they related, and how the game would be played. Then the game got released, and because no-one in the playerbase was made aware of this plan on how they should play, what grew up was a very different mode of play than VI expected (e.g. Feign Death pulling). Brad reacted badly with this, and tried to tune the game so that people would play his way. Generally speaking, it didn't work, because we still didn't know what "his way" was, and so people just moaned that all their l33t skills were being nerfed.

Fast forwards a couple of years, and suddenly all the keepers of "The Vision" have left VI. The new guys, when they take a look at the desireability of the classes, don't start from Brad's notes - they start from their experiences as players, and from what they read on class boards. So now the tweaks to classes, rather than being designed to move towards a single person's vision of a class, and being moved towards a consensus opinion.

This consensus opinion is, necessarily, less focused than a single vision. So the application of this vision produces more blurred class definitions.

The other thing contributing to the blurring of classes is the desire of the playerbase. Everyone now appears to want to play a class who can solo, and who is wanted both in groups and on raids.
Unfortunately, differing degress of competence in this three areas was obviously one of the design tools used when creating the classes, and so giving everyone equivalent competence in all three areas will further blur boundaries.

Take Clerics for example. There are four major healing classes - Clerics, Paladins, Druids, Shamen. The Cleric lacks the melee ability of the Paladin, the offensive magic of the Druid, or the wide range of buffs and debuffs of the Shamen. What Clerics do have is better healing than any of them. This makes Clerics the healer of choice when serious healing is needed - in raids and tough groups. Less challenging groups can use a lesser healer. Solo, Clerics are gimped.

The thing is - how do you improve Clerics without making them more like a Paladin, a Druid or a Shaman? Any broadening of their
abilities both makes them look more like the other classes, and
weakens the need for those other classes (one suggestion for solo Cleric power - self only heals with a 0.5 sec cast time).

Making it more desirable to group would be a very good thing at this point. There's far too many situations where XP is better solo.

Here's some more of my comedy suggestion for an emergency fix: same level mobs and above yield full xp, one level below and it's half xp, another level and it's an eighth, another level down and it's nothing.
They should just make every single non-raid-encounter mob in the game 50% more powerful to reduce the soloing.
And they should nerf the exp in Fungus Damn Grove / Maidens fecking Eye as well as the rest of the gimp-levelling-circuit (LOIO->OT->DL->FG/ME) to reduce the population pressure at level 60.

Every time I see some idiot bragging about how ubah they are because they levelled to 60 in 30 days I want to laugh. And slap them. Laugh while I slap them.
The one thing I thank the stars for is that it took me 2 years to get to level 60 cos I had a pissload of fun doing it and got to see a mass of content most of these losers don't even know exists.
