Cali
Shaman group heals seem a bit doubtful in my eyes. Especially heals over time.

Clerics received Ethereal Elixer at level 60 from SoL. Before that they had heals from level 34 Word of Health (100hp heal) to level 60 Word of Redemption (Group CH). Word of Replenishment was a PoP spell (Group heal 2500 and cure for the same cost of Group CH 1100) that seems rather lame to me. Ethereal Elixer is the ONLY heal over time group spell clerics have. Paladins have two I believe. Druids have the AA spirit of wood ability but no other group heals.

Now the ONLY cleric group heal that can be Mass Group Buffed is Ethereal Elixer. This is limiting as it should be but still a powerfull tactic. Now imagine shamans with the ability to MGB Torpor. That would be highly dangerous and exploitable. So if you pick Quiescence for a group heal to avoid the snare/slow it steps right into the cleric territory. Then how much mana are you willing to have it cost; 975, 1100, 1500?

S: Torpor is 200 mana for a 1200 heal but has restrictions.
S: Quiescence is 200 mana for a 1200 heal but no restrictions.
C: Celestial Elixer is 300 mana for a 1000 heal. And was what EE was based off of.
C: Ethereal Elixer is 975 mana for a 1200 group heal.
C: Supernal Elixer is 400 mana for a 2000 heal.

All of these can be extended with Spell Casting Reinforcement and SCRMastery. Shaman do not have a very linear progression of Heals over Time. Clerics have a very obvious one.
Trevurg
Does the slow from Stoicism count against their total attack speed, or over-ride haste and count towards their base+item haste speed?

Any slow - even a 1% slow - completely overrides all item and spell based hastes. It doesn't remove the buff, so if something is hasted pre slow it is also hasted post slow.

So - taking as an example me. :) Ceris is at 36% item haste and generally 50% spell haste, so his mainhand swings right around every 1.1 seconds. If I hit him with stoicism, that takes his mainhand to every 2.8 seconds or so... it feels like I've completely stopped attacking. When Stoicism wears off, I'm back up to full speed.
