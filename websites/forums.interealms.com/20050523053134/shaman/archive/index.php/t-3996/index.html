I posted this on Lanys' shaman site, and got quite a good response. I'm curious as to how we fit in with other servers, so I'm posting what I posted there, here :)

I would like to know what kind of gear I should be aiming for in the future. I don't want to start a "who's the best shaman" thread, just want to compare and decide on some upgrades I can hope for :) Also, it may help some of our lower level shaman to better understand how to maximise their shammy playing abilities.

I am basing the calculations to figure out the ranking, very heavily on a friend druid's system., but I did tweak it a lot to bettre suit the shaman class (*in my opinion*).


Mana Pool - This is the lifeblood of any caster. Without it, we can't do jack. But mana for a shammy is not hard to obtain with canni, and is very easy for a lvl 60 shammy to get through canni/torpor. Even so, a large mana pool means you don't have to canni as soon, reducing HP, and increasing your chances of dying. Imo, hp > mana though, as your mana pool will only last till it's empty if you can't canni - with canni it's essentially endless.

Flowing Thought/Mana Regen capability - This includes all mana regen capabilities. AA abilities, FT items, other mana recovery items such as burrower necklace and shei ranged (with lunar whispers and boon of recovery, respectively), along with the 8th shawl. This adds to our ability to regain our mana. More mana regen can only be good. FT1 gives 10 mana per minute, FT10 gives 100 mana per minute. One FT lets you regain one point of mana per "tick". A "tick" in EQ, in case you didn't already know, is 6 seconds. 60 seconds per minute, 10 ticks per minute. This is for the lifetime of your shaman, wherever he is, standing or sitting, this is added to their mana all the time. Now, effects, such as boon of recovery, have rune components as well, but I'm talking mana regen capability.

Hit Points - When HP < 0, you die. You need HPs. HPs are good. As a shaman, hp are doubly important to us, due to the fact that we can turn them into mana. If we have hp, we have the chance at getting more mana. Hp on a shaman are our lifeblood, our provider of mana, our ability to prevent downtime, the reason why we can do so much and not have to worry (in theory) about running oom. They are our number one stat.

HP regen - very very important to a shammy. The quicker we regain HP, the faster and more safely we are able to turn them into mana, which in turn allows us to play our part as a shammy.

Resists - Resists reduce the amount of damage you take from a certain "type" of damage, more specifically, spell damage, as well as your ability to resist negative spell effects (i.e. snare, slow, etc.) Resists are good to have. Having more resists means you will either prevent your HP from going below zero (remember, HP < 0 = bad), prevent you from having your ability to aid your party or kill your mob from being hindered, or just save you from the annoyance of smaller mobs that cast on you as you pass by. Having resists are very nice, especially as shammies have to be in range of mobs' AoE's/spells to be able to debuff them.

Certain stats - Most specifically, sta and ac, then wis. These directly relate to our HP, ability to keep our hp, and our mana pool. It is very important to keep these stats as high as possible, wis is possibly less important, as our mana pool can always be topped up with canni, still, wis is very easy to raise and we should take advantage of this to provide us with more mana to start out with. More mana = good. More HPs = good. AC is important because we draw a LOT of agro, with things such as turgurs, and we get summoned and beat on lots. High AC *does* appear to make a difference.

Everything else - Other stats are all nice and stuff, but it toally depends on your playing style, whether you should focus on improving them (atatck for example) or not. For a soloing shammy, attack is nice for when you melee the mobs you tank with a pet. But not everyone is a soloist - I for one need company while I play - that's why I play the game, the interaction with other people. Seeing as there are the two different styles of play (grp shammy or solo shammy), I won't include this as a aprt of the calculation system.

Abilities - with Luclin, Verant really gave an enormous power to a class that was already very versatile and strong. With canni 5 and SCRM,

<u>Calculation System</u>

<b>1 point per point of mana</b> - This should be straightforward.

<b>100 points per point of FT/additional mana regen capability</b> Some may say this is a bit much, but 15 points of FT (the theoretical FT max, sans AA abilities and other non-FT mana regen items) would give 3000 points, that's a lot of freakin points, but this is very, very important to have. Also, if we all use 200 x mana regen, it doesn't really matter, as it's all balanced.

<b>1.5 point per hit point</b> - the reason it is 1.5 and not a straight 1, is to emphasize its importance.

<b>1.5 points per resist</b>

<b>1 point per AC</b>

<b>1 point per stamina</b>

<b>1 point per wisdom</b>

<b>200 points per hp regen</b>

<b>500 points for malo, SCM, canni5, torpor, each</b>

All these figures should be worked out unbuffed. You can either note your stats down in game, or use magelo. Will be nice if we could each post a magelo link with our calculations though, helps us see what items we should try and obtain.

Btw, most of this post is my opinion only, you're welcome to put your points forward, indeed, enlighten me! :)
