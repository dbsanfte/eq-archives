Muktuk Wolfsbreath
I'll assume that you're not just talking about faction, here, because it's more fun for me ;)

On the one hand, we forbid the art of necromancy, implying we are good, through and through. Then again, we don't have paladins. Neither do wood elves, but they have druids, and perhaps a "good" race has one or the other.

We were created by Erollisi Marr and Mithaniel Marr (way over on the side of good), but our warriors often serve Rallos Zek. I don't know if I'd call him "evil" exactly, or even "neutral"--I don't think he has much in the way of moral concerns unless war becomes involved--but he gets lumped in with the evils most of the time.

I see us Tribunal-worshippers as "officially" neutral, with each shaman having hir own personal tendencies toward neutrality, good, or evil. One shaman might value impartiality over all else, another may temper justice with mercy, and a third might take "vengeance is a sacred duty" to heart.

As for my take on race relations: I respect wood elves for keeping close to the wild, and the halflings for their hospitality to lost strangers. I gawk at gnomes, pity humans for their loss of barbarian virtue, and mock the Erudites for their rejection of same. Love and hate have their place, I suppose, but they blind one to duty: hence my mistrust of both high and dark elves.

The shamanic races... well, I'd treat them like I would a potentially dangerous wolfpup: Show them kindness and hope for the same in return, but be prepared to protect your throat.

Overthink things, Muk? Nahh...

That's one of the common misconceptions about barbarians, that we are neutral or on the lighter side of evil.

As Muktuk points out, according to Norrathian lore, barbarians were created by Mithaniel and Erollisi Marr (the personifications of goodie goodie) so are most definitely a noble and good race, albeit primitive. I always correlated barbarians with Klingons...primitive and warlike, but having strong code of honor.

The Tribunal may represent justice, and justice is blind (neutral). But there is also the warning about making a mistake and being judged yourself by The Six Hammers.
