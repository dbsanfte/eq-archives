Their theory is that if you agro one of the mobs near the zone in then leave Grieg's End, that the mob doesn't reset properly. It will return to it's spawn point, but the game will spawn another one underneath it. So, you end up with a whole bunch of mobs stacked on top of each other, innocently looking like one wimpy one.
