Joril
What Kevagron said is accurate. Its not that the lv70 isnt getting exp, its just that he/she is not getting credit for it. in other words the exp from the kill is split between everyone in the group, but the percent going to the lv70 is not applied to the lv70 person. which means the lower level person isnt getting all the exp, just a faction of it. but that fraction is more then they would be able to get solo or with a full group of the same level (if your fighting reds).

its MUCH better to have the lv70 person outside the group and finish the mob off once its past the 50 percent mark. that way the lower level person WILL get all the exp, not just a part of it. yes it takes a little longer to kill things, but i have found it more then makes up for it in the exp gained per kill department.

Another way is fight near a zone line and let the lv70 do most of the damage with the lower level doing a little damage (like a dot or a nuke). Then just before the mob dies, the lv70 zones. This takes them off the hate/exp for kill list for the mob and the lower level character gets the full exp for the kill. When i was PL my warrior, i would leave a dot on the mob that i knew would kill it. by the time i zoned out and back in, the mob would be dead or near dead. There are LOTS of places where you can PL a character near a zone line.

past 50 or so, it becomes easier to just let them mass kill blues with the lv70 standing by to finish the mobs off and provide back up if more then 5 or so are pulled (depending on class of course).

once past 60, it becomes rather moot. most people cant kill blues fast enough or kill red mobs solo at that level, so it becomes easier to group with the lower level person. but exp does slow down.

Unless of course they are a druid/wizard/insert favorite quad kiting class here, then they can PL themselves at that point.
