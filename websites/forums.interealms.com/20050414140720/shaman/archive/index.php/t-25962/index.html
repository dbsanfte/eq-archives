Giac
Proc rate is indeed a "function of time", however, if you used a weapon with a delay of 600 and no haste, you'd only swing once per minute. This would obviously prevent you from taking advantage of the high proc rate of Panther.

Instead of just saying that the proc rate is a function of time, it's probably better to say that while a proc buff/weapon has an "innate" disposition to proc X times per minute on average, which is independant from the delay you are swinging at, you still need to swing at least X times(X being the coded average of procs per minute) to allow yourself to take full benefit of the high proc rate.

In short, if you only swing 5 times, you can't proc 10 times.

niwggib
Proc rate is a function of time, and not related to weapon speed, haste, etc, in any way.

Kinda correct. It is ratio between a set number of procs per minute and the speed of the weapon.

If it is set for 10 procs a minute then a weapon that swings 10 times in a minute will proc on every hit but a weapon that swings 100 times in a minute will proc every 10 swings.

This is how warriors were AE killing a crap load of mobs in PoN. They would use Earthshakers (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=5667) that have an AE spell and would get the best slow on them they could. It would up the rate of procs per swings and would go off every 1 or 2 swings so a few warriors using a repost disc could eat up a ton of mobs.
