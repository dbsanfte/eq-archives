Vigdis
Anyone here who has played Baldurs Gate 2 (or AD&D for that matter) surely remembers the spell sequencer line.

This allowed magic users to cast a few spells, but save their effect for later.

Imagine you cast malos, slow and a dot, and then place them into a sequencer ready to be activated when you need it (ie pull), and poof, you have a mob slowed, malosed and dotted 5 seconds after pull.

This would solve our problems with the shaman class being dependant on long fights to work to full potential (ie debuffing slowing and dotting takes a while, especially if you want the dots to do full damage).

Not to make this overpowering there should be somewhat of a re-use time comparable to say Can-5. You can make a regular slow/debuff on some mobs, but put up a sequencer now and then to really contribute to DPS while retaining you slow/debuff role.

This could also be used in conjunction with healing spells. Storing a Quiesence and a blast heal would give us a sort of micro lay-hands ability.

If this was an AA, maybe Quickbuff 3 should be the prequisite...?

How does it sound?
