There are other issues beyond simple efficiency involved. The point of doing the trial is to win the trial. An unslowed mob does 2 things which are not in your best interests: (1) they drain healer mana - very bad when you are prepping for a named; and (2) they allow the mob the chance of killing your MT (or anyone else it might agro on). Of course if your healer isnt concerned with the state of their mana bar and/or there is no chance for the mob to kill the MT, then mass healing as opposed to slowing is viable, but that is by definition trivial content. We are talking about challenging content here - only a fool would heal through that rather than slow.
I'm very well aware of what slow does and it's benefits, but numbers have been posted on this board when GoD first came out that showed that healing instead of slowing was more efficient through to the tipt trials (granted, that's dated now since progression goes beyond that and content has been tuned, but still it's rather telling).
