I was in a somewhat similar situation at one point. We had a two guild alliance of very casual, family folks who did a bit of raiding on the side. I wasn't the RL, but I was the main puller on the Monk and/or the main slower on the Shaman. And yes, occasionally two boxing my duo on raids was as demanding as you would think, but we managed. Not that it necessarily applies to your situation.

With that in mind:

1. A solid high level tank (65+, hopefully) is a big plus. A good puller is another plus.

2. Establish a CH chain channel and let them practice, practice and practice. Do NOT add people to the CH chain channel unless they have practiced enough, or else you will wipe.

3. A few "test runs" are OK, but try to concentrate on mobs that drop items which would be noticeable upgrades for your crew. If the stuff that drops at the end of the evening is worse than today's Bazaar gear, it tends to depress people and discourage raid participation. Some low level Luclin mobs still drop gear that is good for the level, e.g. that 100 HP necklace in AR.

4. Raid specific gear. Make sure you know what your people have and what they don't, especially considering the fact that Bazaar equipment is light on effects. If a Druid gets rezzed deep inside a dungeon and can't snare runners because he is OOM, tell him how to get a clicky Snare item. Compile and post lists for clicky invis items, potion vendor locs, gate items, insta junk buffs, etc.

Kael - For learning the ropes on Raiding

Depending on how deep you want to go (Vindi?) your only challenges will be getting past the see invis mobs during CRs (use pets or chanter charm since summoning corpses in Kael in 2005 is just plain embarrassing) and handling the triplets at the door. Everything else should be single pullable and one-groupable even by a group of 50s. The gear is obsolete, but it does have a few useful clickies for non-raiders in their 50s.

Griegs - Never got a chance to hit these due to previous period of retirement, but told that they are a good idea

Regular mobs will not be much of a problem. They are up to 55 at the entrance (hit for 160ish and nuke for 600ish), a little higher deeper in (hit for 210), have 10K HP. However, there are a few level 70ish (red to 68) mobs there that were recently added. They will be, presumably, your targets, and I hear they have a few tricks up they sleeves. Read up on them on Alla and decide if you can realistically handle them.

Umbral Plains - When we got armor and know what we are doing

The minis in UP are not particularly challenging these days, but their loot is nothing special. Doom and RC are a different story for a group of Bazaar geared 50s-low 60s, though. You'll want a solid tank for them and an experienced kiter or two for Doom. Keep in mind that the Dark Masters are slower than UP trash mobs and Doom is faster than them and that she is a corpse camper and plan accordingly. FD and CotH are your friends :)

ANY advice would be welcomed!

One test of your raiders' skills will be the mini events in PoN. The Hedge event, the horseman overlooking the forest (Vazid?), Mura -- that kind of stuff. They hit for 1Kish and require a modicum of coordination and result either in gear upgrades or flags or both. They hit pretty hard, but I have done it with a Bazzar geared (high level gear, 1+ million pp) well AA'd warrior. I think he had under 8K HP buffed at the time, i.e. pre-OoW.

Otherwise, how is your Epic situation, especially Cleric Epics? The Royals in Chardok (Chanter/Warrior/Cleric Epic) are a good test of basic raiding skills because of the adds, the trapped bridge, the hole in the library and the big fights there, especially the Queen with her AE.

On a similar note, are you thinking about the Sky for Mage/Warrior/Pally epics? Granted, Pallies have much better stuff now, but the Warrior Epic is still nice to have and it will teach you about positioning and DTs. Epics are generally good because they build up team spirit. Do the Hole for the Shaman Epic if you can, because it's a relatively easy under-utilized zone that not many people have been to.

I wouldn't recommend Fear until you have you sea legs since it's still unforgiving, especially with that Berserker Epic mob. On the Other hand, Hate is highly controllable, but it's not much of a rading zone any more unless you go for Inny/Maestro. It's more like a place for a few friends to sit and chat while waiting for minis to drop their Epic stuff.

Anyway, that's all that comes to mind right now, have fun raiding! :)
