With just under a yellow left til 52, I returned to KC to solo near LCY as I waited on guildies to get on for our raid.

There was a 3 team group there, and they were doing pretty good. We worked out an arrangement where I would take their adds.

A bit later, a shaman joined them. I told them then I would start working the 3rd or 4th add since they now had a shaman. The shaman sent me a tell saying "why?"

I laughed and replied, "whoops, okies, I'll wander to the basement."

As I turned to go, the entire group shouted in unison: "NO!"

I was perplexed, as the shaman adamantly requested I stay under the "same policy as before", (his words, not mine).

Well as fate would have it, a group comes flying through with a fair train. I go into the usual slow, root, dot pattern, working the adds and spreading them out. I herd the 4-man group to the center of LCY as I now have all sorts of decorations on the walls, (some of whom are casting).

I put dogdog on on of the casters, and the other shaman starts pumping heals in my direction.

Two break root, I put one back where I had him, and other shaman roots the first.. only glitch is that he stuck it in the middle of the room. I re-dot both and nuke to break the root, pull the mob to the wall with the others and re-root.

By then, the train is pretty much dead, I am Canni-dancing, and the last two break root almost simultaneously. The shaman manages to get a root on one, gets resisted and almost killed on the other (slow faded), but the rest of the group dispatches it.

After about 15 minutes of conversation, I explain to the shaman to keep HoS up at all times with chloro, open with slow then drag them away and root/dot as needed.

I then telll the shaman to visit www.eqshaman.com, click on message boards and review The Shaman's Crucible. The young shaman. (my level) was not bad, but seemed to get flustered with more than one add. I explained that the trick is to slow 'em, spread 'em out, then work them down, or, if you see no way out, call for the evac.

By the time I left, that 4 person group was able to handle what looked like 3 adds, so another job well done.

What floored me, is that later we hit the guild raid in PoN and I saw a 59 shaman making the same mistakes. I asked the shaman what was their alt, he replied "63 enchanter" . I said, get root out (he did not have it memmed), and use it like a single target mezz. He laughed, "stack them n rack them, heh". I said "yuppers, because only half my stuff are sticking."

He told the raid, "changing line up, Kadira now working slows and heals, execpt when she resisted LOL"

He told me that he made the shaman becuase his guild needed a slower, and pl'd to 58 in a couple of weeks. He spent the rest of the evening asking all sorts of questions, and ultimately, I sent him to Misty Thicket to practice aggro management on diseased rats. He later told me that MT was now "required training" for new members.

POINT: Most of the "new" shamans are pretty good at working their traditional roles, however, the full range and usage of their spells and abilities seem to elude them on occasion. EVERY shaman I have diffidently (key word there) offered advice too has recieved that advice happily.

At 51, I supposed I have darn near mastered my abilities to this point, but I paid for that knowledge with soe much time played getting to 51 I am almost ashamed to admit it, LOL
