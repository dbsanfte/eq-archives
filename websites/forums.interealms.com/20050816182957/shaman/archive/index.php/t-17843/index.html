Grizlor
Vindi.

He's the first true "CH chain" mob any guild will ever encounter. Assuming you have a warrior with 6k+ HP buffed, the perfect rotation on vindi would be 4 seconds.... if you only have 3 clerics, having one start, the next person count to five, and the third person also count to five, followed by them clicking CH every time it pops up will work, but you'd most likely need a spam healer unless your tank is of Ssra/VT level.

There's also the added benefit of also learning about rampage. Make sure you have someone able to withstand regular damage tag him on incoming. Then designate a druid or two to keep that person healthy. As long as they don't die, you have rampage locked on that person. If he does, then whoever was the next person to enter the agro list (sequentially) becomes rampage.

I've Rampage tanked Vindi using self heals and lived till about 10% health...Also RTed Emperor Ssraezha (but had some help once the druids realized I wasn't gonna drop until I ran out of mana :p)

Something not mentioned that I know our clerics use is /pause in their hotkeys. This takes out most of the human factor and allows even those that can't count to 4 to be able to be a part of the chain. Each number represents a tenth of a second, I believe, or thereabouts. I'll echo what others have said about Va'dynn. If your MA has 6.5-8k buffed HP, is a warrior and some AAs then Va'dynn is an awesome practice mob that drops some decent loot for your level, plus key parts to VT that your guild may some day be ready for to save the farming later. Another awesome part of this is it's great practice for the pull team and CC. This is a straight CH chain mob with some decent damage output and no suprises. He's slowable and the CR isn't too tough with a rogue, SK, or necro nearby. My guild (pre-elemental, pre-VT, totally casual, non-uber) one group him whenever we find him up to get bits for the VT key and to try for the neck piece our MA wants. If you master your system, this becomes a trivial encounter. If not, you'll wipe.

I'm not sure how others do this encounter, but if you consult the AR map, we buff at zone in, invis to the second intersection, park in the corner between the hallway we came up and the hallway leading to Va'dynn, get pets and start pulling all the roamers and hall mobs with the SK puller keeping 2-4 in camp at any given time. SK pulls and MAs, I off tank/CC adds slowing everything. We pull Va'dynn and take him in the corner with the MA's back against the wall and the pets and rogue coming in behind Va'dynn. We don't get many adds at all here and positioning is easy. I'd slow Va'dynn the first couple times JUST to get the hang of it on a 5-6 second rotation depending on your MA. He's got a truckload of HPs so take it easy.

This is taken from my alts guild website...



For those of you who have never done a chain heal and don't know how to make the hot key for it.. :


Go to socials and open up a blank macro.. add the following :

Line #1
Complete Heal to %T


Line #2
/cast (add the number for the spell slot your complete heal spell is in, one being at the top eight being at the bottom.

Line #3
/pause 100 ( number for pause will be changed according to the mob and how long or short a pause you want between heals.. 10 = one second so 100 = ten seconds.

Line #4
GO (name of person who is next in line in chain) GO !! < you can ad lib here as long as the next persons name is clear. >



Here is what mine looks like :

Complete heal to %T
/cast 6
/pause 100
Go! --Name-- Go!


Usually a heal channel is made.. to make this easy to read during a raid make a separate text box for it by right clicking the top of your chat screen and making a new window. Make it so that only this channel is going into that window. If you haven't done this play with it in your spare time and see how it works.
To make the text go into this channel and be seen in the window you have made for it.. Add /# of the channel at the beginning of the two lines with text., line 1 and 4.

i.e. /4 Complete Heal to %t
/cast 6
/pause 100
/4 Go --Name-- Go.. !!

The number 4 is just an example.. set it according to what the heal channel is for you. The number 6 after cast is where mine is. .. make sure you use the number for the spell slot that YOU have complete heal in.

Here is also a suggestion to rid ones self of the fizzle monster:

I would also recomend instead of a single line of /cast #, using several, for example:

Complete heal to %T
/cast 6
/cast 6
/cast 6
/pause 100
Go! --Name-- Go!

This will eliminate the chance of having a fizzle interruption or other interruptions of any kind (toon moving, etc) that could mess up the heal chain. If the First one goes off without a problem you'll only receive 2 msgs saying you cant cast at this time etc... but it will not interrupt the chain. Sometime that first fizzle (even when very rare) can make the difference.

Hope this helps.
