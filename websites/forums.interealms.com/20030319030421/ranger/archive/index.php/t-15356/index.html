decairn
Enrage usually kicks in at about 5-15% of the mobs life. Any hit you do from the front is automatically ripsosted and you get eaten alive. Be wary of it, turn off attack or move behind or to the side. It affects everyone and is a fast way to lose the melee in your raid.

Rampage is a bit harder to explain. It is affected by the 'hate' list, which you can manipulate to your raids advantage by having certain people attack first, use max melee range to reduce each persons agro. A simple way to stay off the list is let the main tank and rampage tanks attack for 20s before anyone else engages. You can often move non-melee out of rampage range. If the mob is rooted (ssra uses this a lot) have rampage people plink arrows and stuff before the main fight to control agro. Lastly, make sure people have hotkeys up to say they're on rampage, they'll be needing some healing.

Enrage is exactly at 10% of a mob's life. I watch for this when we're raiding so I can warn the guild at 12% to expect enrage soon. Enrage lasts for 12 seconds and you'll see a message that it's done at the end of 12 seconds.

Ways to do damage while a mob is enraged:

1) Pretend you're a rogue and get behind the mob if you can.

2) Cast DDs.

3) Shoot arrows. (No, the mob will not riposte arrows.)

4) Make sure whoever is getting beat on has a damage shield.

A good MT will make a world of difference w/ enrage. While melees know to back off when a mob enrages, pets do not. I've seen more pets die to enrage then anything else. A good MT can position a mob which enrages so the pets will be attacking the backs/sides.
