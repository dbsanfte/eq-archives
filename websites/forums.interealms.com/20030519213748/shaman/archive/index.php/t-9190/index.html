taraddar
I voted for 65 but that really depends.

If you a shaman in a higher end raiding guild like myself then the decreased resist rate and the mutual overwriting of debuffs is very nice also. It also means your more likely to be able to access higher lvl spells.

If your in a smaller guild then stopping at 62 or even 60 might be a good idea. Your odds of getting a 63+ spell are less and you have more options for where to get exp. Especially since you won't nessicarilly have access to the higher tier planes.

Also it depends on your aa status. It you were already 60 when pop came out and had some aa already especially cann v then it's probably better to do some more lvling. If you just got 60 and have no aa I would strongly recommend putting most or all exp into AA until you get Cann v before continuing on any lvling.

There is no simple right answer.

Personall I aim to get Canni V first, and also to try to get 61 and 62 spells. Once I have Canni V and the True Spirit spell, I'll save up a few AA for spirit call and virulent paralysis, ding 61 to get them, and then go up to 62 before reviewing the situation.

I am in a small guild, nobody else is over level 60 and most are between 46 and 58. If I went much above 60, I wouldn't get any xp grouping with my guild, and chances of me getting level 63+ spells are slim seeing as I've not got flagged for Tier 2 yet.

If I was in a large raiding guild with Tier 2+ access I'd go for 65 and then AA, on the basis that AA at 65 will flow like water. I don't normally find my mana a big issue on raids, and hence Canni V is less important than in group situations (IMHO).
