Ryam
Virulent Paralysis is Disease based root with knockback. The first level is nice for emergencies, it gives you some time to recover after getting aggro, and pushes the mob out of melee range (if its melee range is relatively short). At 1st level the duration is rather short, so it is a temporary relief, but still quite handy. At level 3 the root lasts about as long as the reuse on the AA (2 minutes), so it can be used to keep a mob rooted indefinitely (unless there are premature breaks). After debuffing Disease resist with Malicious Decay, VP3 is pretty reliable in most cases, and makes root-rotting easier and safer. VP3 also has bigger knockback, and it good for gettting mobs off clecics etc if the group is low on CC (or to bounce mobs on clerics if you are as evil as the average shaman :p).

I only have Lvl 1 of Sprit Call, so I'm not an expert on that, but it already has its uses. The doggies it summons are a pretty good speed bump, as they seem to have about as much HP as dogdog, and they also Enrage. In addition the doggies generate decent push, so they can be used to position mobs. No idea on the DPS they do, haven't parsed it, but it's a fun AA. :)

Jaidin
A note about VP.

While it just might be the best AA to get after canni5 if you plan on soloing, in a group situation it is far far less useful without proper communication.

Any time you use it make sure to explain to your group about VP either a) when you first join the group or b) have a hotkey that mentions the mob is knocked back away from the group and parked. The utility of VP goes right out the door when you have a couple mobs inc and as soon as one get's VP'd a melee or two, or pet, go after it. Instruct the MA that on a multiple mob pull to not go after the knocked back mob if he had switched targets during the pull.

Go straight for VP3 and do not stop at VP1 or 2, the knockback is just that much greater with VP3. VP3's duration is just about 3 seconds longer than it's refresh so you can keep a mob perma-VP'd, which is wonderful for soloing. If you plan on using VP at all make sure to keep malicious decay up at all times. Even though it is disease based, many mobs (especially in LDoN) will resist VP frequently without a disease debuff first. Once the debuff is in, they hardly ever resist (strange pattern no? :))

Otherwise, for a handful of AA (it's what, 9 total for VP3?) it's a GREAT aa to have.

As for spirit call, I was seriously disappointed with it at first and still am. The cast time is entirely too long and once the mob is dead you can't command the pets to attack another target so they just stand there. Sometimes I can get them to attack another target if I take some hits, but still that's not even reliable. In the 6 or 7 seconds it takes to use the AA I could have nuked and canni'd once for the same damage they will do since the mob will be at 50 by the time the dogs go to town anyway. Now, as a pulling tool it is useful because it's like a guaranteed root land if you know for sure you are looking at 2+ mobs.

Basically, two thumbs up for VP3 and one thumb down for Spirit Call.
