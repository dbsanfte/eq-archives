Now see, if one of my class's spell lines gave me the ability to summon a weapon in my primary slot, "CLEARLY" I would care that I couldn't use my spell and my hard-earned epic at the same time. If our epic was secondary but equiping it meant you couldn't have a pet up, I'd sure as hell be pissed. Plus, dogdog is key for a lot of the soloing I do. Same deal with clerics: when the clock is running out I've seen clerics jump in and start meleeing with their summoned hammer to save the adventure. So yeah, it actually seems pretty obvious to me how SoE can justify giving only one class in the game a secondary epic, when that is the only class in the game with a spell line that summons a uniquely useful primary weapon.

Of course, SoE could have preserved the clerics' ability to melee while still giving them a primary, if they just gave them a new spell along with their epic. The spell would summon a hammer with the stats of the epic, but with a proc that's the same as their summoned hammer's. Or they could have the proc be almost the same, but also make it give you a buff that provides the focus effect from the epic. Clerics would still have to play the bard game if they wanted to use the right-click, but since they'd be getting a new epic and an upgrade to an existing spell, I think they'd learn to manage.

Of course, coming up with that solution, or finding some other one that gives every class a primary epic 1.5 but still let's clerics melee, would require some creativity on the part of SoE dev team, which we all know isn't exactly their strong point. So since the clerics do have a shield, and that just isn't going to change, I think that our original epic, and TA for those that have it, should be clickable from inventory. In fact, I see no reason why most clickies shouldn't be useable from inventory. If I want to fill my inventory slots with my fur booties, old epic, JB Bracer, earring of cleansing, JB BP, Dain Ring, chardok ring, and 10 Dose Kilva's potion (hypothetically maybe I really dig DSes), I don't see how that's so terribly unbalancing.

Of all of them, the booties perhaps gain the most by being inventory usuable (they're the only item that'd you'd need to use right away sometimes, without having time to switch them in), and yet they are the only item on the list besides the potion that actually is inventory usuable. The rest are just stat-wise outdated items that still have useful clickies, which people can already take advantage of by pretending to be bards and switching in and out.

So what if instead of switching them in and out people have the option of leaving these items in their inventory slots, a pretty big sacrifice (if I actually filled my slots as I did in the example I couldn't even carry food around with me, let alone components, loot, etc.)? How does that ruin anyone's enjoyment of the game? Heck, several of those items (chardok ring, dain ring, old epic) required people to spend a huge amount of time (unless your a cheating bastard who MQed the dain ring or the epic) to acquire, and now they languish unused in many people's inventories or banks. If people could still derive some benefit from their hard work with only a single click instead of six and some mouse movement, I don't think that would have any ill effects.

So in summary, stop bitching about clerics. Yes SoE shows them unreasonable amounts of love, but if they don't and the clerics riot, it would break EQ. Besides, don't you feel sorry for those poor bastards who have nothing to do except sit on their asses and then stand up every once in awhile to cast CH? A lot of them are really fond of their summoned hammer, and since SoE is too lame to come up with a better solution than giving them a secondary slot epic, then why should we bemoan their luck? Instead, we should be lobbying for SoE to make a lot more items clickable from inventory, because that would make everyone happy (except maybe the tailors that sell bags ...).
