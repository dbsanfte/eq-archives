Your list is significantly off. Dont take offense, but you might have a slightly inflated impression of what is capable at what power levels. I say power levels because once you pass 65, gear and AA starting make huge differences. A bazaar geared person is not going to be able to solo or duo everything a time geared player is. Now add in AA's, such as defensive AA's, pets, crit dots, nukes, etc etc and the gap increases even further.

you are not going to be taking Trak, Venril Sathir, Rhag 1, Rumble Crush, Doomshade, or Woushi anytime in the next year to 18 monthes. You wont have the gear or AA's.

Phinny is a possibility. Sebilis is VERY doable and probably where you should concentrate (minus trak of course). Howling Stones is another place to hone your skill with several named. Lodi can be soloed by a shaman past 65, so thats an extremely easy target. Nathyn i assume is the vamp in katta, never fought him personally, but think he will be out of your league as mentioned above, but cant say for sure. Faydedar is probably doable, but i would put that between 6 months and a year down the road. Ragefire I would put in the same category as Faydedar.

Velketor i dont remember that well, always disliked it, but most of the bigger named (which is what you have listed here by comparison) your not going to be able to take on. The normal random named shouldnt be a problem though.

A nice area to play in is Western Wastes. the static dragons in the nests are extremely easy. Some of the roaming dragons are to, but some of the roaming ones will kick your butt, so I would research which dragons are which. The trench dragons by ToV are a good stepping stone in power levels. they are a good step up from the nest dragons and provide a good strategy learning curve.... nothing like learning how to fight while silenced (means you cant cast ANY spells). They also drop some decent gear for that level if you are cash strapped (top end spells can still get expensive).

Good Luck, remember its a game and dont get frustrated if you cant kill everything you think you should be able to. Come back later when you have more AA's and better gear.

Macnair
I can't imagine agro ever being an issue for these two. The partner is a *shadowknight* afterall.Aggro is not automatic.

It requires skills that must be learned. The process must be managed. People don't know how to do this automatically.

If the SK is just blissfully whacking away with melee when the shaman puts on 3 dots and then pops a heal, the shaman is likely to get aggro.

If the SK has a relatively small mana pool and runs out during a long fight, the shaman is likely to get aggro.

So the two of them need to learn together how much of what causes aggro issues, and they need to learn how to manage that.

Skyfire Mountains is a good place to learn these things.

Magneuss
Velketor, I'd have to see someone's logs before I ever believe someone could solo/duo him.

If both of you are 65, with some AA's, say, 50ish each, I would put cash that you could duo trak. He's not really that difficult of a mob. The key, like stated above, is getting him to come down to the banish point. This is tedious work, but not difficult to do. You have to be able to clear trash mobs, fast. Clear all the mobs between the banish point, and him. There's not a whole lot. Have shaman return to that spot, and let SK continue on to trak. Once ready, SK engages trak, get's banished. After a short amount of time, you'll see trak come cruising up. His DPS is horrible, and he really doesn't have a lot of HP's. We single grouped him, and it seemed trivial. At the time, we were heavily bazaar geared, with some luclin gear thrown in there(ssra and VT mostly).

If two boxing for you is an option, I would suggest adding possibly a ranger to your team. Your still low enough in level that you could catch a ranger up in short time. This will allow your shaman to conserve mana for heals mainly, without worrying about DoT's so much, or at least until you get higher level with a larger mana pool and more efficient DoT's. The AA I enjoy the most is the crit DoT's. It gives you the chance of scoring a crit hit off a DoT on each tik. My highest Bane crit has been 1050, and you can count on critting a few times per fight. Most AA's work hand in hand though. Keep in mind, these generate massive amounts of aggro, so spell casting subtely is a must.

Each encounter presents new techniques. They are not so drastically different though. Your basic jobs are still there. SK gets and maintains aggro. Shaman slows and heals. Don't get too caught up in trying to learn the secret squirrel way of killing Random_named_01. Learning the event is fun, applying what you've learned and beating the mob down is the best. Focus effect items make events easier, yes. As a shaman, you can max out your alchemy and work on making your own focus effect augments. Omens of War provides great HP/mana and other stat items that drop at single group content. Myself (68 shaman) and my guildie (69SK) duo WoS mobs (yes, even namers). You can get some really nice gear there with great focus effect and stats.

It's all about what you put into your characters, as to what you can get out of them. You know this. All you need to do is apply what you know.
