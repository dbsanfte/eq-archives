Indalecio Galronte
Hey all, I'm sure by now we've all seen a very intresting AA called:

Spiritual Channeling
While this ability is active, all of your spells will be fueled directly by the energy of your body, consuming hit points rather than mana.

This is a poor, raped version of the AA I suggested awhile back:

Spirit Channeling 5/10/15

Description:
This grants the shaman the ablilty for a short time to fuel his spells with supernatural haste, and negating the cost of the spells at the cost of the shaman's life force.

How it works:
When you activate this AA, after a casting time (2 secs maybe?) for the next 7/16/30 seconds. All spells you cast will have a 30/45/60% spell haste applied to it. The lowest a spells casting time can be reduced to is 1 second. All recast time for spells during the period of time in which this AA is active is reduced to 0. No mana cost is deducted from the shaman's mana pool for any spells casted by the shaman during this period of time. After a successfully casted spell, the shaman would instead take damage equal to twice the amount of the mana cost of the spell. When this AA is active it will take up a buff slot (and operate much like Quiesscense does) and may end the effect by clicking it off or simply waiting for it to expire.


In my suggestion I realized that Shaman spells have frustratingly long casting times and generally are underpowered compared to specialized classed (mainly comparing nukes and heals here). SO, what I did was design this AA around the a built in spell haste, which makes it leaps and bounds better then the suggested farce above it.

As you all have pointed out, we have tons of mana, casting from our less manipulatable hit point pool would actually be more hinderance. The spell haste was the main goal of the AA, while basing it around hit point usage was an to make it balenced (you will die quickly if careless, our spells are costly and is reflected in the hp consumption). Together they make a very worthwhile AA.

It was also meant to have a fairly short reuse time (something around the area of MGB heals) and to have a fairly short term effect (like under a minute tops). They seem to have thrown both of those out the window there - rabid bear style.

In short, I just wanted to vent and apologize to the community for the mangling of the idea. It may still yet be decent, but the original goal of the idea, BURST utility, seems to have been left out in SOE's grand equation.
