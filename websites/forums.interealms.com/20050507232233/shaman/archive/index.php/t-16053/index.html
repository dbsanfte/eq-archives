Weissblau, in tactics there is a pit filled with mobs. Groups xp down in the pit, which can be exciting and stressful as the proximity of the mobs, the amount of wanderers, and the high level of them can make a single pull all of a sudden become interesting.

About once an hour you will see the message appear saying:

"You hear the pounding of hooves"

At this point pretty much everyone should back up against the wall. A huge herd of boars stampede around the pit in a circular fashion before heading back out of the pit. In amongst the herd is a small piglet that is the mob of choice to get hold of.

It is a really fun zone, but there can be some real jerks in there.

Dread
We don't fight in the tunnels for this very reason. We usually setup shop in the SW corner of the pit. When we hear the stampede message, I will go over to the tunnel ( but not right next to it or you will get big pig agro ). After the piglet comes out of the tunnel, he will pause for a second before/as he turns to run in another direction. Root him then, but do it from close to max spell range. If you are too close you will agro the big pigs and that can be bad. If you do get a couple of the big pig adds, just let them kill you and get a rez, it is better than getting your group killed. Or you can run them out into the pit away from your group and sometimes you can lose them if they get too far away ( they go back to their pre-set path through the pit ).

Once you got the piggy rooted, then malos and slow him. Then get very close to him before you break root. His agro range is extremely small and if you aren't right next to him when root breaks, he will still run off. You can tell if he is agrod on you by which way he is facing. If he is facing you, everything is good and break root. If he is facing the other way, melee him for a sec until he faces you after you back out of melee Then just break your root with a velium strike and walk him back to your group.

I have been able to pull the piggy without other agro the last 10 or more times, no problem. It is really all about positioning, then just watch which way he is facing before you break root. Too bad bulwarks only go for 8k or so now on my server.

Some other tips:
If there are mobs close by when you get the stampede message send a cleric or enchanter to pacify anything that may get in your way. The time between the message and the actual stampede seems to vary some, but you should always have at least a good 30 seconds or so to pacify that stuff.
Once you get the piggy rooted, have someone else snare it before you break root.
Figure out who is gonna be responsible for grabbing the piggy before you see the stampede message.
To figure out about where you want to pull the piggy from ( if you are camping in SW corner ) use the diaku that hangs out in the mouth of the tunnel to figure out about where max spellcasting range is.

Jinheim
Here's how to handle piglet:

First of all, killing your pet is probably a good idea. If you get hit once and your pet charges in, you could have the whole herd on your hands, which would be a wipe.

Second of all, the whole group needs to sit in the safespot. On my server we call it "SS" but appearantly it is different on yours. If you were standing in the stables looking in to that room, it would be the back right corner. I think it is the NW corner but I'm not positive. Everyone stands there. When piglet spawns, one person waits until piglet is about to go out of sight through the first hallway, and then roots him. The root will land with piglet in the middle of that short hallway. Usually you'll get him solo, but sometimes you'll get two adds (whenever I get adds it is always two). As soon as the stampede has passed, everyone in the group runs in to the hallway and engages piglet. STAY IN THE HALLWAY. Once the stampede passes back through the safe room and despawns, you can move piglet. Just DON'T let him get to the stables, because he will despawn if he reaches the doorway.

Usually it is best to pull piglet back in to the safespot in order to avoid roamer adds, but if you have a full group it shouldn't be a problem.

Finally, you can solo this easily with a little practice. I made a decent bit of cash soloing daikus in the wee hours of the morning when tactics is empty. I would get the occasional highly polished gem, and whenever I saw the stampedge trigger, I would invis and run down there, abandoning my daiku I was fighting (he won't follow you down). Piglet is an easy kill for you if you don't get adds, it just takes a little practice to not screw up. Good luck to you.
