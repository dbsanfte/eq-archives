1. Target yourself and type /guildremove, or now with LoY, you can press Alt+G, and hit the DISBAND button.

2. Quality >>>>>>> Quantity. Zerging is gay and not even remotely effective in most situations. Raid with the guild you are considering applying to. See if you can get in on a month's worth of raids. It not only gives you a chance to suss them out, but also vice versa.
Most guilds will require this anyhow, depending on your level and their progress.

XP grouping is another great way to see what the members are like, and it also again gives them a chance to see if you will fit into their family.

Don't forget too in regards to #2, with the right mix of levels/classes/experience, you'd be surprised how much high-end content you can do with just 1 or 2 groups. We do it all the time. :)

3. Good time? I don't know if you can really answer that, as it depends primarily on you and where you aim on heading in the game. Don't think that you HAVE to be in a guild, it's nice and can help you see alot of bigger things in the game, but is by no means a necessity :)

Next time you are on, do some /whos of people around your level, note what guilds they are in, do this for about a week seeing how often people of your level range are on, and what they are doing at the time to see if you would be able to hunt/raid with your new guildmates, get a few guilds in mind that you might want to join.

Send a tell to a member of one of the guilds, ask if an officer is online and available to speak with you a few moments, let them know that you might be interested in joining, ask them some questions about thier guild, ie, how often they raid, what types of rules they have in thier guild, what times they raid approx, what targets they go after, ect

once you get a feel for a few guilds that you might want to join, ask if you could hunt with them to get an idea of how well they work together as a team, if they have any amount of skill, ect... use this info to base your opinion of if you would fit in that guild or not.

Another source of possible guilds would be of course to look at the guildnames of people you are hunting with and in downtime situations ask them a few questions about thier guild.

oh, and just one of those personal things I adhere to... never consider a guild that does open membership such as shouting to a zone to /tell them to join thier new uber guild... *shiver*

I really like Cush’s response. Joining my first guild was a really big decision for me. And that’s basically what I did. I ended up grouping with some of the same people several times and I really enjoyed their company. So I asked about their guild and ended up joining it. It was the best thing to happen to me in game. My guild was small, so we didn’t do much raiding, but we were a family and that added a whole new dimension to the game. Unfortunately, due to leadership burnout, our guild disbanded. But I’ve kept in touch with my friends and I still group with many of them. And some day I’ll try to find another family.

Something to keep in mind when joining a guild is to try to find one with several members close to your level. Due to game mechanics, you can only group with other players of a certain level and still get experience. So if you’re the only level 15 character is a guild of 55+ characters you won’t be able to group with them. Also ask if they have a web page. Many groups will list their rules and what the guild stands for on their web page, along with general chat areas. Visiting their web site is one way of getting to know the guild a little better.

So take your time and look around. There’s no need to rush into joining a guild. But should you join a guild and find that its not the right one for you, please don’t just quit out of the blue. Try to be polite about it. Let them know your leaving before you remove yourself from the guild. And if you can, try to let them know the reason your leaving. As a former guild officer I can tell you it’s frustrating to have a guild member just up and leave without a word. The officers and others you’ve gotten to know are left wondering if there might have been something they could have done to make you feel more at home.

Ack, I’m babbling again.

I wish you the best of luck in finding a guild that’s right for you.
