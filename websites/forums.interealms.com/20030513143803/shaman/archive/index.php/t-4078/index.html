For low levels (pre20): Speed is king, since the damage cap. With newbie zones lik Kurns and PC, you'll be 20 in no time

For 20-45ish: Wurmslayer in primairy and good higher damage off hand. While ratio may be king in off hand, your skill will suck for so long, it's best to have a harder hitting off hand. Atleast when it lands, it hits for a wallop!

45+: Speed is your friend. Don't go higher than 20 delay for primairy if you can aviod it. You want to keep applaying your damage bonus as much as you can. Lammy, Defiance are good for this. For off hand, I swear by the Frostbringer. For those with lots of cash, the fleshgrinder is a sweet primairy weapon for both agro and DPS. Something in the 10/18 ratio range will be your friend at these level for your primairy.

Two handers, and when to use. They start to get good in your 30's, when your double attack starts to become more effective. Good cheapies are abundant in the Bazaar. I use a Windblade now, over Fleshgrinder/Frostbringer (partially since I had to return the Fleshgrinder b/c it was on loan). But with a two hander, you want the best ratio hands down. When it comes down to same ratio, differnt dam/delay, I choose the faster one, to apply the damage bonus more often, and for faster attacks if I Miss.

best of luck to ya.

this is something i read long ago on the warrior boards. it explains how weapons work in eq. very usefull info.


Ratio
The ratio is a way to compare weapons with different damage and delays to see which one is the most damaging. I'll use your two weapons as examples: Glowing Iron Pike is 9Dmg, 36Dly (or just 9/36) and the BALS is 10/29. There are two ways to calcute the ratio.

1) Delay / Damage ( Delay divided by Damage)
2) Damage / Delay

Most people use method 1 above when calculating the ratio, so that is what we will use. It is very important to know which method is being used, because, when using method 1, lower numbers are better, and when using method 2, higher numbers are better. Let's calculate the ratio of your weapons using method 1.

GIP: 36/9 = 4.0 ratio
BALS: 29/10 = 2.9 ratio

Remember, lower numbers are better, so the BALS will do more damage than the GIP. You asked about a Dragoon Dirk.

Dragoon Dirk: 23/6 = ~3.83 ratio
(note: the ~ symbol means about)

Thus, the dragoon dirk will do more damage than the GIP, but not as much as the BALS. The DD/BALS combo is better than the GIP/BALS combo.

So hopefully you understand ratio now. There are many other factors to consider when picking between weapons besides the ratio, and I will talk about them in a moment.


The Damage Cap
Everquest implements a damage cap for characters under level 20. From levels 1-9, the highest Damage rating a weapon can have is 10, and from levels 10-19, the highest Damage rating a weapon can have is 14.

What this means is, if you are level 6, and the weapon you are using has a damage rating higher than 10, it will act like a weapon with 10 Damage. Consider the Wurmslayer, which is 25/40. From levels 1-9, it will act like a 10/40 weapon. From levels 10-19, it will act like a 14/40 weapon. At level 20, the damage cap is removed, and the Wurmslayer will act like the 25/40 weapon it is supposed to be. (Note: above level 20, there may be another damage cap for really high damage weapons, if anyone knows what it is, let me know)

What you need to understand about the damage cap is that certain weapons will not be at their full potential if you are under level 20.


Level Damage Bonus
The Level Damage Bonus, or LDB, is a number that gets added to every successful hit made by your main hand once you reach level 27 (when using a two-handed weapon) or level 28 (when using a one-handed weapon). The formula
for the LDB is:

1-handed weapons: (Level - 25) / 3 (rounded down
2-handed weapons: (Level - 25) / 2 (rounded down)

I will focus on using the 1-handed LDB in my examples, since that is where the LDB is most useful. The 1-handed LDB becomes active at level 28:

(Lvl 28 - Lvl 25) /3 = 3/3 = 1

At level 28, one point of extra damage will automatically be added to every successful hit with your main hand. At level 31, 2 points of extra damage will be automatically added to every successful main hand hit. The weapon in your offhand (secondary hand) does not get a LDB.

How does this apply to choosing weapons? Well, at some point, all those LDB points start to add up quickly, especially with low delay weapons. What this means is that low delay weapons, when you are high enough level to get a fairly large LDB, will actually do more damage than high delay weapons that have better ratios. Look at the:

Jade Mace: 2.0 ratio
Wurmslayer: 1.6 ratio

Recall from the ratio section that lower numbers are better, so we would think that the Wurmslayer is a better weapon. Certain levels, the Wurmslayer is the better weapon, and for certain levels, the Jade Mace is.

Levels 1-9
Jade Mace: 18/9 = 2.0 ratio
Wurmslayer: 40/10 = 4.0 ratio

From levels 1-9, the Jade Mace is a better weapon.

Levels 10-19
Jade Mace: 18/9 = 2.0 ratio
Wurmslayer: 40/14 = ~2.86 ratio

From levels 10-19, the Jade Mace is still better, but the Wurmslayer catches up a bit because the damage capped is raised.

Levels 20-60
Jade Mace: 18/9 = 2.0 ratio
Wurmslayer: 40/25 = 1.6 ratio

From levels 20-60, the Wurmslayer has the better ratio, and is the better weapon, if there was no LDB. Unfortunately, there is no formula that I know of that considers the LDB when calculating the ratio, but I can tell you the following based upon the experience of many warriors.

At level 46, the 1-handed LDB is 7 points, and that is about the level when the lower delay weapons start to do more damage than high delay weapons with better ratios. That is because the lower delay weapons get more swings and thus, get the LDB added more often than high delay weapons. You will get ~2.2 swings in with the Jade Mace in the time it takes to swing the Wurmslayer once, meaning that, at level 46, you will get 14.4 points of LDB with the Jade Mace versus 7 points for the Wurmslayer. Thus the Wurmslayer is better than the Jade Mace from level 20 to about level 46 because of its great ratio, and the Jade Mace is better from about level 46-60 because of its low delay and good ratio.

But, you may say, the Wurmslayer has such a higher damage rating, doesn't that make up for getting less LDB? No, it doesn't, because of the way the EQ combat system works. I'm not going to go into too many details, so you will just have to trust me. Let me just say that with the EQ combat system, you will hit for the minimum a ceratin amount of time, with the minimum being 1 point + the LDB. Since the minimum hit with the Wurmslayer and Jade Mace are the same (8 points at level 46), it's better to hit for the minimum quickly and swing again than hit for the minimum slowly.

What you need to take away from this section is that once you approach level 50, the delay of your main hand weapon becomes more important than just the ratio alone. By your mid 40's, you definately want to start thinking about getting a low delay weapon for your main hand.


Offhand Weapons
Choosing an offhand weapon is fairly easy. Remember that the offhand does not get a LDB, so the ratio is all that really matters. A 9/18 Jade Mace and a 12/24 Crystalline Short Sword (CSS) will do the same amount of damage in your offhand, because they both have 2.0 ratios. (Remember, the Jade Mace will do more damage than the CSS in your main hand because of the LDB though).

When choosing a weapon for your offhand, try to get the weapon with the best ratio you can.


Taunt
As a warrior, you will often be required to hold taunt (that is, keep the monster attacking you). A function of calculating taunt is how often you swing, meaning that lower delay weapons will help you hold taunt better because you are swinging more. Sometimes, you might need to choose low delay weapons over slightly higher delay weapons that have better ratios in order to hold taunt. Let's look at the Lamentaion and CSS. The Lamentation is 9/19 has a ~2.1 ratio versus the 2.0 ratio of the CSS. Let us say that you are deciding which one to use for your offhand - the CSS will do more damage, but the lower delay of the Lamentation will help you hold taunt easier. In a case like this, I would suggest using the CSS in the offhand and watch how well you are holding taunt. If you are consistantly having problems keeping the monster attacking you, it's time to switch to the Lamentation.

In grouping situations when you are not the main tank, you might want to consider carrying around a 2-handed weapon to use. The higher delay of most 2-handed weapons means that you are less likely to take aggro away from the main tank.


Stats and Procs
You also need to consider stat bonuses and procs when choosing weapons. Some people prefer the +6 Str, +6 Sta, and +20 HP that the Lamention gives over the Jade Mace that has no stat bonuses, even though the Jade Mace will do slightly more damage and hold taunt a little better. Some people prefer the 80 point lifetap proc of the 10/21 Bloodpoint with its 2.1 ratio over a weapon with a 2.0 ratio like a Jade Mace or CSS. Procs are a great taunting tool when they go off.


Weapon and Offense Skills
There is currently no advantage in using one type of weapon, like One Hand Slash, over another type of weapon, like One Hand Blunt. There is, of course, a distinction between one-handed weapon skills (1HS, 1HB, and Piercing) and two-handed weapon skills (2HS, 2HB). There is also two-handed piercing, but I believe those types of weapons are based off of the same weapon skill as piercing (someone correct me if I am wrong please). As a general rule, you should strive to keep all of your weapon skills maxed, because there are great weapons of all skill types available, and you would hate to have a low skill for a great weapon. Weapon skills are capped at (Level * 5) + 5, so, at level 6, the maximum skill you could have is 35. This formula applies from level 1-39 for weapon skills, which caps them at 200. From level 40-50, once you reach the cap at 200, there are no more skill raises. Levels 51+, the skill caps get increased to 250 for 1HS, 2HS, 1HB, and 2HB, and to 240 for piercing. This increase gets applied as 200 + (Level-50) *5, which just means that the cap is raised 5 points for every level above 50: 205 at 51, 210 at 52, etc.

Warriors will typically carry practice weapons (even low quality ones) with them to use in appropriate situations to try to keep their weapon skills maxed. The chance for a skill raise increases with every swing you take, so low delay weapons (regardless of ratio) may be desirable to get raises as quickly as possible. Good times to use your practice weapons are when the group is killing quickly and easily, when snared monsters go into "flee" mode, or you can even duel a pet class and attack the pet. When in doubt, ask the group if they mind if you work on your weapon skills.

Remember, some of the best weapons aren't one-hand or two-hand slashing weapons. Look at the Jade Mace (1HB), the Bloodpoint (Piercing), the Truncheon of Doom (2HB), etc. Do you best to keep all of your weapon skills capped as you progress through the levels.


Dual Wield and Double Attack
Dual Wield is the ability to equip two one-handed weapons - one in each hand. Double Attack is the ability to swing twice in one combat round with the same hand. If you are using a 2-handed weapon, you can swing once or twice in any one combat round. If you are dual weilding, you can swing once, twice, three times, or even four times in any one combat round if you successfully dual wield and double attack with both hands. When dual wielding, the offhand will attack independantly based on its delay; there is no correlation between off-hand and main hand swings.

Assuming weapons of equal caliber, dual-weilding two one-hand weapons will do more damage than using 2-hand weapons. This has been proven from in-game experience by many players, and can also be deduced by looking at the classes; Offense classes, the Rogue, the Monk, the Warrior, and the Ranger, can all dual wield, while the defensive classes, the Paladin and the Shadowknight, cannot.

You should focus on getting a good pair of one-handed weapons as this will get you the most damage and the best taunting. As mentioned previously, two-handed weapons have their place too, dealing good damage while not attracting too much aggro. The proposed post-50 increase to 2-hand weapon damage should increase the damage output of these weapons, but they still will not outdamage dual wielding.


Summary
Choosing weapons can be complex. Learn to calcalute weapon ratios so that you can compare different weapons to each other. Remember that as you approach 50, low delay weapons in your main hand become more and more damaging. After you do that, you need to take into consideration some other factors, such as which hand to use a weapon in, how well can you hold aggro with this weapon, how important and useful are the stats and procs on it, etc. Much of this comes with experience, so, if all possible, try out different combinations yourself and see what you like best.
