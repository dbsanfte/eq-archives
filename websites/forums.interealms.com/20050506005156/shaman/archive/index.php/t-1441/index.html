Henora
are you getting that much agro from a landed slow? I mostly had trouble there with agro from resisted slows. A landed slow would gain me a summon, but at most 1 round of melee. Usually none. And I never waited 30 seconds if I could land malosi/malosini faster.

Make sure you try and get tash and mal*something (malosi at your level most likely) in there before slowing if possible. You, other shaman and mages can chain cast your malosi/malosini while the MT is working on gaining agro without much fear of gaining agro. It's a very low agro spell. If you have a 55+ bard, they can help you with Occlusion of Sound as well.

PoZ's only rampage - honestly I don't see much change in rampage dmg on them using cripple. Try not crippling, see if you can tell the difference. I honestly could not...if you do decide to use it, it is high agro - don't chain cast it, try to get another shaman or chanter to do it for you - someone who isn't already high on the hate list.

I was the only shaman on all our guild work in Kael from level 53-60. In all the dozens of trips, I think we had an enchanter like once, so no tash...so I feel your pain. As usual, levels are huge. There was a significant noticeable lessening of mob resists every level. :)

It is certainly possible to avoid summons and beatdowns doing slow+cripple in Kael and in many other places as well. It's just a matter of timing and avoiding resists. Avoid resists through proper debuffing. Your tanks will hold agro and regain lost agro better as they level up as well.

Good luck
-Henora

Boinshar
The easiest way to lose agro = Root. However, it will not help your guild learn agro control, which definately would help in the long run.

Slow and Cripple both generate a lot of agro, and honestly Cripple's only truely useful against mobs that has nasty procs, be it rampage, flurry, or AEs. I'd just skip on Cripple, it should not make much of a difference for PoZs.

As for the actual slowing, remember level plays a HUGE difference in how easily you could land spell and how well an NPC can hit you. For example, at 60 I can slow most PoZs without Malo at all, yet it would be suicide for a level 55 to even try. The key to surviving slowing in general, as others have mentioned above, is to minimize the chances for resist. You did not tell us your level, but since you call yourself a mystic so I'll assume you are lower than 55. Your best bet is to land Malosi before Turgur. If the Malosi bounces, keep casting until they land, then slow.

generally, slowing mobs on raids is a matter of numbers. if you have 5 people trying to slow, as opposed to 1, your raid will be much more successful. and youll die a whole lot less.
in other words, if your having a really tough time, recruit the enchanters and epicced rangers to help out with slows. Theres nothing like getting summoned from a resist, only to see another player give a successful slow message immediately after.
on a more personal level, just experiment a little. see how long you can wait before you can cast a slow and not get summoned.
from your situation, sounds like you dying slows the raid down a lot more than waiting an extra few seconds before trying to slow.
