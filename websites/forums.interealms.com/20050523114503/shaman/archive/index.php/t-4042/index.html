Vorpatril
I know this issue has been "resolved", but I would like to humbly submit an interesting experience I had over the weekend.

I'm in West TOV farming armor. I am one of 2 shamans on the raid. We are both lvl 60. The other shaman has Malo and I do not. So our strategy was, he casts/announces malo, we both cast Malosini till it sticks, then we both cast slow till it sticks, then we both cast cripple till it sticks. Throw a little delay in the casting to try to manage aggro and let the other guy use his mana. Perhaps a little inefficient, but hey, that's why we have Canni/Torpor right?

Here is how the first 20 dragons or so went:
Garek: a fiery defender has MALO
ME: Malosini resisted
Me: Malosini resisted
Me: Malosini resisted
Garek: a fiery defender has MALOSINI
Me: Turgurs resisted
Me: Turgurs resisted
Garek: a fiery defender is SLOWED
Me: Cripple resisted
Greak: a fiery defender is CRIPPLED
Me: Sigh.

It was rather remarkable. I would land spells from time to time, but I was getting resisted easily 75% of the time. Garek was not having that problem at all. Mob after mob came and went and the other shaman was basically debuffing them by himself. We compared notes and couldn't find any differences in what we were doing that would account for the difference.

On a whim, I cast Charisma on me. My charisma in resist gear was 60. After buffing, it was over 110 or so. My resist rate dropped DRASTICALLY. My resist rate dropped to maybe 25%. I had little trouble getting spells to stick from there on.

As a mathematician, I know full well that my unlogged impressions of my resists hardly constitutes proof. But it was more than enough to convince me. Against normal exp-grind mobs, I don't have trouble with resists anyway, but against these high-end mobs I will definitely be looking my shining lizardly best.

Just something to think about,
