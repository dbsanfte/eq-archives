Weyalan
Faern,

Yes, crime on Britain is (slowly) rising. However, what does this prove about guns? Crime rates in Britain, per capita, are still far, far lower than in the USA. Crime rates in the USA are still much higher than in other comparable countries, even if crime is not increasing as much.

The argument about guns / crime is a difficult one. Some countries with relatively high gun ownership rates have very little gun related crime: Canada and Swizerland being the obvious examples. However, there are equally plenty of examples of countries with relatively low gun ownership where gun related crime is very low (Australia, New Zealand, Britain). USA has high gun ownership rates and high gun related crime. Given the contradictory nature of the above evidence, what conclusion can one draw?

I conclude that the rate of gun-related crime is not particularly related to the rate of gun ownership. USA has high gun ownership & high gun crime, Canada has high gun ownership but low gun crime. I would argue that rates of gun related crime are more to do with societal attitudes to guns. In my opinion, if the USA has a problem with gun related crime it is not the number of guns thats the problem, it the attitude to guns.
