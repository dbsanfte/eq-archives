What is AA?

At level 51 all classes gain access to the Alternate Advancement menus. You can access them in game using the command ALT V. You can toggle a portion of your Experience points into a seperate pool that is not counted towards achieving your next level. When this pool is filled you gain 1 AA point, that can be saved or spent on a new Alternate Ability.

There are several catagories of Alternate Abilities.

General: These may be purchased starting at level 51. They cost 1 AA point for each ability.

Archetype: These may be purchased starting at level 55 AND after you have spent 6 points in the General catagory. They cost 2 points for the first level, 4 points for the second level and 6 points for the third level of each ability.

Class: These may be purchased starting at level 59 AND after you have spent 12 points in the Archetype catagory. Their point costs differ, but most cost 3 points for the first level, 6 points for the second level and 9 points for the third level.

POP Advance: Available at different point costs, to people level 61 and over, who have purchased the Planes of Power expansion.

POP Ability: Available at different point costs, to people level 61 and over, who have purchased the Planes of Power expansion. Many skills have seperate level requirements and point costs for each level of the ability.




------------------
What is an AA title and how do I get one?
When 6 points are spent in General Abilities, all classes gain the title Baron/Baroness depending on gender.

When 12 points are spent in the Archetype Abilities, each class gains a new title. For shamans, druids, beastlords and clerics that title is Venerable.

When 24 points are spent in the Class Abilities Catagory, each class gains yet another title. For shamans, druids and beastlords that is Elder.




------------------
Do racial exp penalties apply to AA points?

No, racial exp penalities do not apply to the rate at which a player gains AA points. The class-based exp penalty was removed from everquest years ago and that does not apply to AA points either.

This is a difficult question and one that is best left to the individual. However, most shaman generally use one of a few strategies to plan how they will earn AA points.

1. Level to 65 before any AA.
2. Level to 60/61, stop and do AAs, then level to 65.
3. Work on AA steadily while levelling

What's the best order to do them in?
General: Innate Runspeed 1, 2, 3
General: Innate Regeneration 1, 2, 3 OR General: Innate Metabolism 1, 2, 3
Archetype: Mental Clarity 1, 2, 3
Class: Cannibalize

After that its up to you to decide which ones are the most important and relevant to your playstyle.

See the Shaman AA Chart (http://interealms.com/shaman/guides/aa-chart.html) for descriptions of other AA that shamans have available to them.

Personally, after Canni5, I recommend getting Radiant Cure 1, then working on either Spell Casting Mastery, Quickbuff, Mass Group Buff, Spell Casting Reinforcement/Mastery and Natural Durability. In whatever order you feel like. Others have posted their personal recommendations below.

Do AA skills stack with focus effects?

Yes.

Quickbuff will stack with Buffing Haste and/or Spell Haste focuses to a maximum cast time reduction of 50%.
Example: Quickbuff 2, plus Haste of Mithaniel = 45% spell haste on spells such as Torpor/Quiescience.

Spell Casting Reinforcement will stack with Buff Extension focuses, with no cap.
Example: Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery and the effect on the 8th Shawl, Zephyr of Brell will stack for a +70% duration increase on your buffs.

Healing Adept will stack with Enhanced Healing focuses, with no cap.

Canni5 (5aa +12aa prereq) - Canni5 is a no brainer. Every shaman in the guild has it already so no further discussion needed.

Mass Group Buff (9aa) - Duh. You all have it already, nothing to see here.

Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery (8aa + 12aa prereq) - 50% buff extension, and works with quiescence/torpor. With this AA, my Q always heals 2100 points of damage. Sometimes, with the right lag/timing, it will heal 2400. That's a 2100-2400 point heal for just 200 mana. That is excellent, both for raiding, exp, and dueling. On top of a better Q, you also get longer buffs, which is always nice. Combined with 8th shawl, my buffs have a 70% duration bonus. It is nice to have a 2.5 hour focus, and an 11 minute avatar. Both the buffer and the buffee benefit from longer buffs. This is probably my favorite AA outside of canni5.

Quick Buff 3 (18aa) - Reduces the casting time on buffs (including torpor / Q) by 50%. Having a 3 second Q is a must-have. With 3 second cast time combined with SCRM, you can keep Q up on your entire group without it ever fading on anyone. That is awesome. On top of making Q cast faster, it also makes all those other buffs cast faster. What I usually do is MGB fo7, then cast tali of boar on each group individually. QB really cuts down on the time it takes to buff a raid, and its nice to be able to buff the stragglers quickly when you get those "focus sta plz" tells. One important thing to note about QB3 is that the maximum spell haste is 50%. You can hit 50% with QB2 + blessing of reverence + haste of mithaniel. However, I have nice ft3 items in my shoulder and range slots (the only places haste of mithaniel is available) and I was unwilling to give those up, so I just spent the 9aa. If you have cog encrusted epaults or a wicked bone altl, then QB2 should be enough for you.

Advanced Healing Gift 3 (9aa + 12aa prereq) - Gives you a 16% chance to exceptional heal for double healage (2600pt tnargs, doesnt work with kraggs). I feel that our primary role on raids after a mob is debuffed, is to heal. For minor splash damage, I can heal with Q. If someone is getting beat, they need a blast heal. 1300 is a decent heal, but with mobs hitting as hard as they do nowadays, it isn't always enough. A 2600pt heal, though, is almost always enough to save someone's life for long enough for them to lose aggro or gain the attention of a cleric. I perform lots of exceptionals on raids, as most of you have probably seen, and I have saved many people's lives. I feel that healing adept is a good AA, but healing gift is much much better for our purposes. I plan on getting HA soon, though.

Lightning Reflexes 5 (15aa + 12aa prereq) - This AA makes you get missed a lot more often. I feel that this serves us better than CS + ID. If you get quadded for 2500, and you have ID5, you'll only get quadded for 2000 instead. 10000vs8000 damage doesnt matter, you're still gonna die. If you had had LR5, though, half of the quad might have missed you, and you would only take 5000 damage, which would not kill you. LR5 really works. I often see long strings of misses, and I honestly believe that it has greatly increased my survivability. With this AA, I rampage tanked DXXT, and exp mobs have become laughable. I can now function as the main tank anywhere in a tier2/3 exp group.

When To AA:

I am a firm believer that attaining level 65 should be your #1 goal in improving your character. There are many reasons for this.

First of all, just being level 65 makes you more powerful. Your spells will get resisted less, you will not die as quickly when attacked, and pretty much everything you do will be effected in a positive way. People that are not 65 SERIOUSLY underestimate the effect that being 65 has.

Second of all, if you care about raiding, you need to be 65. Not only does being 65 mean you can slow mobs easier, it also means that your buffs and debuffs will always take hold. On top of that, a sub-65 shaman just is not as useful. If you're 64, and you aren the only shaman on the raid, then you will do ok I guess. If there is a 65 shaman there, though, then any sub-65's are basically just extra lag as far as I am concerned. I am in a large raiding guild and we have had a few shaman recruits that weren't 65 yet. The ones that leveled quickly all turned out to be pretty good. The ones that lingered around and never worked on leveling turned out to be pretty crappy.

Thirdly, being 65 opens doorways for you. A lot of people say "I'm not going to level to 65 because I don't have to spells." This is stupid. Level to 65 and you will get the spells MUCH MUCH easier than you will if you are sub-65. Seriously, all you need to do to get your 65 spells is this: Level to 65 and hang out in BoT all day. If you have tactics flag and know some people that are capable of downing gyklus or overseer, go there. That's it. The end. If you aren't 65, you won't get as many groups in these places and you will spend most of your time in ****hole PoV, where a level 64 toon is a godsend for most groups.

Finally, IT IS EASIER TO GET AA AT 65 THAN ANY OTHER LEVEL. I do not give a flying rats ass if someone gets half a percent more AA per kill at 63 than I do at 65. I will have more aa than them when the day is done. Why? Because level 65's are stronger. A group full of 65's doesn't die. A group full of 65's kills faster. A group full of 65's has an easy time finding level 65 replacements, and the group does not break up. On top of that, if I shout "65 shaman (176aa) LFG" and you shout "64 shaman (22aa) LFG", who do you think is going to get a group faster? Let me tell you the answer: me. I very rarely have to spend more than 10 minutes LFG. Even if I don't get a group, I can solo mobs 2 at a time in tactics or pov. 3 if I feel like concentrating and have raid buffs. You can't do that without blood of saryrn, a level 65 spell.

If you do not believe me that it is easier to get AA at level 65, look at this: http://www.eqrankings.com/?rm=list&class=Shaman&server=&order=AA&page=6 That is a list of shamans, sorted decending by AA. The top 660 shamans listed are all level 65. The 661st shaman is level 62, and has 84aa. If it isn't easier to get AA at 65, then how come the top 660 shamans are all 65?

------

There are exceptions however. I feel that everyone should get run3, probably at either level 56, when you get bane of nife for increased soloing power, or at level 60, when you get torpor and the ability to get in to good groups. Run3 is just 3aa, which should take one afternoon of dedicated grinding. Cannibalize5, though, is going to take another 20aa to reach. Canni5 is THE ONLY aa ability that MIGHT be worth stopping your grind to 65 for. I feel that you're better off getting to 65 before even canni5, but, if you absolutely must do some aa, then just get canni5. I recommend that you do it at level 62, if you must do it. At level 62, you will have all of your easy to get PoP spells, and you will be a high enough level to be an asset to a pov group, rather than a liability. You are also high enough level to zone in to BoT, on the off chance that there is a good group there that will take you. Both these things will make the grind to canni5 a lot easier.

That's my advice. A lot of you are going to disagree or maybe even be angry with it, but that is how I see it.

1. Level to 65 before any AA.
2. Level to 60/61, stop and do AAs, then level to 65.
3. Work on AA steadily while levelling

You should have run3 asap after hitting level 51. Run3 lets you run faster than most mobs in the entire game. It works indoors. It's very quick to get. There's no reason to avoid getting this asap.

General concensus is that the next 20 AA to get should be the ones you need to get Cannibalization (3 more general, 12 for MC3, and 5 for Can5). When should they be acquired though?

There is very little reason to work on AAs any time before level 59. The benefits of getting AAs are minimal in comparison to what new spells will do and what levels will help you. Can5 can only be acquired at level 59. Most people don't enjoy banking AAs at lower level then levelling up. They tend to enjoy levelling and getting new spells and then going for AAs instead. Only get first 6 AAs if you really feel like getting your Baron title, otherwise just get run3 and start levelling to 59 at least.

(FYI: One of the main reasons that people suggested AAing at level 60 is because the old world mobs used to "green out" at level 61, but due to a recent patch, more old world mobs are blue and give xp, so arguements concerning this are invalid at this point.)

You may wish to level up to level 59/60/61 and then start working on AAs (and thus working towards Cannibalization). Level 59 is the minimum level to start using Can5. The level 58 2k heal is efficient enough to use on yourself that you can use can5 and do the slow cheal type heal on yourself to get back mana quickly. If you don't have very good HP gear, you may not be able to live through can5 very well, and the casting time on the cheal spell is 10 seconds, so casting it in combat may be very dangerous. Level 60 is a good level to stop levelling and then start on AAs, because you have access to FoS, GFoS, Malo, and Avatar, which all have very noticable effects. Only consider staying at 59 if you have no chance at acquiring any of these level 60 spells. Level 61 is also a good spell to level up to, because you have access to even more new spells from the POP expansion. The pet and new heal spell are all level 61, and add a great deal of power to the shaman. If you have Torpor banked, there's no reason to stay at 59 and starting to AA. Get to 60 asap and scribe that wonderful spell.

Another reason to think about staying lower level is that many of the mobs in pop that you fight for xp are level 60+. Fighting mobs equal or higher level than you are gives you an xp bonus. The xp bonus is very visible. You may wish to keep that in mind and take in that xp bonus while working on AAs. If you wish to see how much the xp bonus is, level up to a level, say level 61, and kill a few even conning mobs to check the xp rate, then delevel to 60 and kill a few level 61 mobs (yellow cons) to see how the xp rate differs.


The main choice of whether to work on AAs at the 60ish mark, or at the 65 mark is what spells you have banked. Can5 requires an efficient heal. The level 58 heal is storebought for cheap, so you have a good heal when you can finally actually buy the can5 skill. But you want a more efficient heal if you can get one. You want Torpor or Quiessence. If you have Torpor banked but no Q, you will want to level to 60 at least before work on AAs. If you have Q banked, regardless if you have Torpor or not, you probably want to level up to 65 before doing AAs. Q is vastly more powerful than Torpor. If you don't have Q or Torpor, nor have the funds to buy a copy or expect to win one any time soon, then you may wish to just go level 65 before going to AA. Levelling to 65 will give you more time to possibly obtain a copy of Q or Torpor before you get can5. It will also let you get access to other powerful spells. And you really do want the most efficient heal spell for use with can5.

Longer answer: It is not a guarantee. The spell IS resisted quite often by mobs with inherently high (& even w/ 'normal") disease resistance. There are many factors that come into play. Social aggro radius, the distance of the push (VP3 (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=3276&source=Live) moves a mob '16' for distance) and the resistance of the mob all come into play. You CAN use it to TRY to separate one mob from tightly packed nearby mobs w/ a pull by reversing the direction you are facing & smacking your VP key. IF...
1) The spell isn't resisted.
2) You angled the knockback correctly to maximize distance from the nearby mobs.
3) The knockback pushes the mobs sufficient distance to be greater than the normal social aggro radius of the nearby mobs (which will need to be very small for this to work right).
...then it can work the way you describe. But don't bet the ranch on it working. :D It is fun to try though, and when you pull it off it can amaze your friends.

The fact that the root lasts LONGER than the refresh time gives you a mana-free root that should last forever if left unattended and you had pre-cast Malicious Decay. Obviously, for attempting mob separation you don't have the option of prepping w/ Malicious though, so trying that is really a gambit unless you know your target is vulnerable to disease based spells. Kilgaroz
My 2 bits on whether to split your xp pool:

All the people in my guild told me not to split the xp pool because you actually lose some xp to rounding when you do. Say you get 23 point of experience for mob x, and you have your split at 70/30: You should get 16.1 points for your regular pool, but ifrom what I'm told, it's going to get rounded to 16. This doesn't sound like much, but when you consider the number of mobs you kill while xp grinding, whether for level or AA, those rounded bits add up and get wasted.

Having dinged 61, with no Torpor/Malo yet, and having only 3 AA's saved up, I started mulling over my AA path.

I usually put together my LDON groups. I have gotten to the point now where I can log on to EQ and START a good, often hard, ADV within 20 min or so. I do this by inviting from guild, friends list, and by hopping to each ADV camp and personally inviting. I do not worry about not getting a cleric or enchanter, which saves tons of time. I can Main heal, and often get the dr00ds to do that. Sometimes, as long as we have great DPS, I would go with just one non-pulling subtank.

But many wondered "who will pull" or "we have no paci" or "there's no cleric?!" or "don't we need an enchanter?"

When they were surprised by the answers "I will pull" or "I will be main healer" or "I got CC"....they obviously didn't group with many aggressive shamen. Sometimes I struggled to hold it together, but with planning, communication, and teamwork, it is more successful than not.

Replacing the cleric was easy after 58 and Kragg's Mending (and a 58 dr00d). Replacing the enchanter was tougher, but pet off-tanking and root-parking can get the job done. Replacing/augmenting paci pulling using green doggie pet pulling was awesome when I learned how to do it after watching SKs do it. And finally getting our AE slow Tigir's Insects and USING it makes CC even better. Having 2 slows memmed adds real CC to the group (helps having 4.3k HP and 1k AC....)

Best yet was getting VIRULENT PARALYSIS. This AA simply rocks. Yes it is occasionally resisted, but for 3 measly AA points (with NO pre-requisites!!!!) at level 61, I am totally satisfied that this is my FIRST AA purchase. No having to wait 23 AA points later to get that first AA rush from Canni5.

Bargain basement AA shopping for 3 points!! A new dimension added to the versatile shammy. For the aggressive or solo shaman, I highly recommend this as a first if you are 61 with but a few points to spend. You can then keep on trucking to 65 and the more 'conventional' AA's, but will have the instant gratification of a really useful, "Advanced Ability"... Overpulls are no longer a scary thing in LDONs for me 8)
