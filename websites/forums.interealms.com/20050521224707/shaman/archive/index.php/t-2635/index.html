A lot depends on your situation. For some, epic is easier to get than a JBB by virtue of having lots of people to call on to help you - plenty of shamans get epiced at 51/52. But by the same token if you have that social structure around you, your personal damage output isn't that important anyway because you are working more as a force multiplier with your groupmates.

If you're forced into being a lone type by virtue of play hours then you might never get the epic, while the JBB is doable by plugging away at raising the cash. I'm in that boat, and consequently I am completely reliant on my own damage output. For me the JBB was all that, particularly when things go really badly instead of just burning mana to do damage and eating yet another unrezzed death on an unlucky string of resists, I can prevail despite being OOM.

Either way, getting hung up on one goal like the JBB really isn't healthy for the EQ experience. Because of the nature of EQ and the endless timesinks, I find I'd rather be working towards several goals at once so when I find myself being frustrated by being unable to progress I can just switch to another.
