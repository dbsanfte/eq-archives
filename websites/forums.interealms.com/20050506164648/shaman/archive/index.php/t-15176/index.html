blakkon
AA tends to produce three basic things:

1) Additonal stats
2) Focus effects
3) Spell effects

In 1) I lump together things like ND 3 which gives hitpoints, MC3 which gives "flowing thought", and things like increased mitigation, which effectively raise AC, or soft cap ac.

in 2) These are often things you can get with items, of course AA often adds to these, thus making them better.

in 3) we get things like Can 5, spirit call, VP, effects that give us added oomph to our spell lines.

In evaluating the payoff for an AA line I examine the pp cost to get the same effect in gear, when possible.

An example is ND3. This is really a nice AA for most shaman. It gives you 10% increase in geared hitpoints and stamina buffs. For me that was about 375 extra hitpoints. In order to improve my gear to the degree at which I can get that many more hitpoints I would have to spend 100's of thousands of pp, or spend months raiding. 12 AA points seems cheap for what I recieved.

Another example is can 5. what this did for my impending carpal tunnel syndrome is priceless. I have not sat to med in months. This skill essentially cost 17 AA, because mc 3 would have been very low on my purchase list, and is worth every minute spent collecting points.

Spirit call.. I spent 4 aa's to get a 3000 point mana free dot every 12 minutes.. And its a blast. Good points spent here as well.

MC 3 for non shaman may be more valuable.. I mean how much does a droppable FT III items sell for? 100k? 12 points doesnt look so bad compared to farming 100 k, and if you get 100 k in the same time you can have the item and MC 3....

Just evaluate what the AA does, what it adds to you, and how much items with similar effects would cost.
