There are three things that can make EQ fun for me:

1) I am testing the limits of both my own skill as a player and Jinheim's gear/aa. It's fun when you look back on an encounter and realize that you completely dominated it because you kick ass.
2) Dedicating a lot of time to a project and finally reaping the benefits, and advancing your character. Getting uber loots is fun, and doing/finishing huge quests is fun. Tradeskills too, although I know I am in the minority in saying that I like them.
3) This is the big one: Friends. Standing around for an hour waiting for people to get to the raid and get groups made is fun, but only because my guild and all my friends are just really fun and great people.

Mitrian
Fundamentally, it's all about watching your character grow and improve. Skills were the most obvious, because you get those messages all the time: You have improved your skill at Fletching, Piercing, Elder Dragon, whatever! (200) Today the "grind" more about AA exp, and after every kill you see the exp bar move just a tiny bit. This is why most everyone says "Ding!" all the time.

The fun of grinding is not the repetitious acts and hotkeys we set up. It's just fun to watch the numbers go up and visually see your character become better. It's a natural human response to want to better ourselves. If you can do these things in a challenging environment (i.e. pushing your skills) it's even more rewarding.

EQ's success is based on this premise, I believe, and in conjunction with the fact you are sharing these accomplishments (both large and small) with people you enjoy.

I hear of a friend of a friend who just made it into Time, or just completed their epic, or just hit 60 (back in the day) and I will always send them a congratulatory tell. EQ is about growing, and about sharing the growing with friends (and even strangers).
