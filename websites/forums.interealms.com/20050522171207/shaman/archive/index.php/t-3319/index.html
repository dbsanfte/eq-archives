Lol. Aye, NToV is fun. There are only about 3 really hard dragons IMO. Aary, the gatekeeper, Vyemm, and Zlexak(sp?). Zlex doesn't have a lot of HP, but he has a nasty disease AE--we eeked though with 4 clerics, but he can be rough if you're short on healing. He's one of the triplets in the back. Many of the other mobs in NToV aren't that hard but just have a butt load of HP.

The shield you mentioned, the Shield of the Wurms is actually a drop off the yard trash, not the named mobs. Well, let me restate that. There are a few of the flurry drakes that have names, but they aren't really 'named' mobs. By named, I mean the big dragons like Aary, Fesh, Dagarn, etc.

You probably already noticed, but when you are clearing the drakes and stuff out of the way, all the flurry mobs have capital letters in their names--ie A Lava Defender is a flurry, but a lava defender is not. Any of the mobs in NToV other than the true named and the little chickens right before Aary can drop a Shield of Wurms (or any of the other class stuff--rogue and warrior necklaces, barrier of sound, druid shield, ring of superiority, etc).

Welcome to NToV! Glad you had fun!

Irksome
If you wanna see if you are ready for NToV, go in after the named dragons are down. Try out your force on flurry drakes and moving thru. Make it a goal to get to Plane of Mischief, which is up near the triplets spawn point. Get maps, and work out routes and timing and such.

For sure, Aaryonar is the price of entry. He is one mean mofo. If you cannot kill him, and go in when he's down but other nameds are up, you are gonna be considered gimps. For sure, work out how to handle flurries and the rest of the yard trash up there when the nameds are not up, it will save you time on your first run, and will also help you evaluate your readiness. Also, some guilds that have been in there forever may not care about some of the lesser dragons, you might be able to work a deal with them to test yourselves on Koi or some other named (assuming he wasn't up for their clearing, and popped the next day or something like that).

Flurries should be crippled, too. My experience is that you get a noticeable drop-off in the flurry rate once you land cripple. Typically, we do Malo, Tash, OOS, and a bard with tash stick, mages work on Malosini, shaman work on Turgur's, then finally try to cripple. Sometimes you nail it on the first cast of everything, sometimes you die trying the 4th slow. If you fail two slows, put on Torpor before trying the 3rd, it MIGHT save your bacon. Even the best outfitted shaman will likely die to a double shot of flurry.

I love that zone. Always a challenge, and the rewards are great. If you walk thru to PoM, you have a good chance of getting at least 2 'minor' rewards.
