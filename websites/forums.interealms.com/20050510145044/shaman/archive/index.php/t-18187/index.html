Last night I (63 shaman) did an LDON adventure with a 63 Ranger and a 63 Beastlord from my guild, along with a 61 paladin, a 65 Cleric and a 64 Druid from another guild. As it turned out, the Cleric and Druid both ran out of KEI about ... oh, 10-15 minutes into the dungeon. Druid was helping on heals, and I was focusing on slows and damage with a few spam heals here and there. Since the paladin's pacify wouldn't work on the mobs, the cleric had to do it. After two mobs were pacified, she was OOM. Not a problem, Druid takes over heals and I'll back off damage a bit and take SH while the cleric meds up. Right? Nope. Druid healed the paladin one time (he was down to 38% when the heal landed), and said she was OOM (she'd been FM about 2 seconds before the mob was pulled). Not a problem ... sit, quick tweak of the spellset, and I'm now MH, slower, and a bit of damage while they med up a bit. Right? Nope. Druid stayed seated the rest of the fight (except when we moved) and the cleric only got up to pacify a mob every once in awhile (probably every other room). We were dealing with double pulls left and right, and I was peeling with Turgur's, rooting adds, slowing the mob the paladin was on, healing the paladin, and turning around to cast a quick spell to help puppy before cannibalizing. Things were fine ... we ended up losing the adventure, but only by one drop (we'd done a collection).

About 10-15 mobs after they'd gone OOM, the Druid said this ... "Why the hell are we here? You don't need us, you have Mr. I Can Do Anything over there." The cleric chipped in with a few barbs, and they went back and forth about how annoying shaman were ... they felt useless ... etc. etc. I could do nothing in response, my hands were a bit full. Finally, the cleric said, "The one thing I like about shaman is they can't rez. It's about the only thing I like, because it's something they can't do that I can." At this point I was so fed up I shot back, "The only reason I can't rez is because I never bought the spell." Silence, and the paladin, ranger and beastlord were rolling with laughter.

Now, what's the big deal? Does anyone understand this? Is it just so-called "class envy" ... that as a shaman I can take over their jobs when the **** hits the fan and do it well ... and with cannibalize have such sick mana regen that I can be 60m before the next mob is brought to me? Has anyone else had an experience like this? Have I had a Barbarian Moment and missed something obvious? I think not, but as I would really rather not be the reason people think badly of shaman, I'd appreciate some feedback. Thanks.
