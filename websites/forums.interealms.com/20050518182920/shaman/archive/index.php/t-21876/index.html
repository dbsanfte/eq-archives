Razzaziah
In a nutshell this is how stats work:

Agility = Avoidance (dodge, block). The gains were about 3% gain in avoidance from 1 to 305. The proof as was mentioned was the beastlord forum.

Stamina = 4 HP at 65 up to 200. Then its diminishing returns. To a point where 300-305 adds 1 HP. (class dependent). The rule of thumb on the other forum of having 1/2 effect every 50 above 200 is reasonable. So for every 50 above 200 multiply the hp gain by 1/2. So if you have 299 and earn 1. The effect of that 1 stamina is 1/2*1/2 * 4, or 1 hp. Please note that in reality these numbers are fractional (not whole numbers).

AC = Lower damage you take. More specifically it "lowers the probability an NPC hits for max damage". The steelwarriors brought out the proof with their whole Damage Interval (DI) discussion. Basically an npc has a Min Hit and a DI. The mobs max and min damage are Min Hit + 1*DI, Min hit + 20*DI. AC directly works against this 1,2,3,...,18,19,20 roll an NPC gets in damage calculations. There are well known cliffs of ~ 1300 AC and ~ 1900 AC where the return on investiment of an individual AC point is not noticed. An NPC counters AC by having high ATTACK. Since HP buffs for shaman are ~36 and the scale of importance is measured on 100s for practical purposes a shaman AC buff will not help in combat.

Attack = ~ 1% DPS per 10 attack gained. The proof was on the rogue forum (so figure its less for other classes). There is a worn item cap of 250 (including proc avatar items). In this world Ferine Avatar adds 14% DPS to a rogue. Since rogues do nearly twice the DPS of other classes, expect 1/2 the effect on other classes. Realize that Luclin Mobs are known for high AC. AC reduces the effect of Attack (read AC).

Dexterity = ~ 10% gain in proc rate, and 20% gain in channeling range. Proof was Steel Warriors (maybe 8 months ago) and Graffee. Note that when a player casts, they can move X distance in /loc units without being interrupted. Dexterity increases this distance by 20%. So if you can move 0.25 loc, you can move move 0.4 loc (the channeling aas have greater effect). Proc rates are typically 2 per minute, so a 10% gain would be 1 extra proc every 5 minutes. Note both are VERY basic tests. Channeling in specific.

You can make your own conclusions. Nobody should accept the developer saying we should prove it, as the results of many many hours of labor are still available on the various boards. Even look at magelo. As they had to implement the forumlas approximating the stats effects on Atk and Ac and Hp and are widly accepted as pretty close to true.

The statement that buffs > 300 are meaningless or offer trivial advancement is justified.

NOTE: NPCs and PETs have a proc rate of about 10 per minute (2 per minute for players).
