The primal bow outdamaging the war bow also included avatar being up for primal over no avatar for war bow. With avatar up, war bow outdamages the primal. When taking bow ratios in account, you need to take in account am3 and TS. These are modifiers that are based off the base damage of the bow. You must take in account what the modifiers do to the base also. Without the modifiers, yes, ratio is all you look at. Moving the damage/delay to 33/30 effectively nerfed the bow if you have am3.

One thing to remember here is that there is no auto-attack on archery. (Sorry if someone pointed this out, and I missed it.)

"What has that got to do with it?" you ask.

Simple. You can't actually press 5 (or whatever your hotkey is) in time to actually shoot the arrow exactly when it's ready. Taking network lag into account, you can't even do it with a gamepad trick. Even if everything is going really well, you might expect 0.5 sec delay on an average bow shot right? So a 10/17(0.588) bow is really more like a 10/23 (0.435) bow when you count the delay. And that 30/38 (0.789) is 30/43 (0.698).

"Yeah you lose damage due to lag... so what's your point?" you ask.

Take a look at how much DPS you lose in each case. The 10/17 lost 26% of it's damage to lag while the 30/38 only lost 12%.

So what you really need to do is add in the arrow damage. (Sure I shoot 3 damage arrows, but you might be shooting 12 damage arrows). Then you need to add a bit to your bow delay. (I estimated 5.) Then you can compare.

So with those fancy 12 damage arrows:

10/17 = 22/22 (1.000)
30/38 = 42/43 (0.977)

Isn't that interesting...

With my lame 3 damage arrows:

10/17 = 13/22 (0.591)
30/38 = 33/43 (0.767)
