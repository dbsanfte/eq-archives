Meglo Maeneac
First off, let me say that this is not a personal attack against you, it's just common sense.

People always ask, "Why do we have to raid ToV when there are better drops out there."

The answer is simple, because you need the experience.

ToV offers many unique features that help in upper level raids. There are three I'm thinking of at the moment:

(1) 'The door (of doom)'

I don't think I've ever met a person who did not shudder when this door is mentioned. It's important because it teaches people to listen. Simply put, you open the door, are kos and are in front of the door, or 100 other things, and you're guild wipes. Simple as that. One person clicking the door, and BOOM, 30+ people die. Grats.

How is this practical? I'm now thinking about the BoT towers and how if you don't listen, and refuse to hug the walls how you can pull a ton of giants onto your guild. I'm thinking about many other isntances where not listening results in massive death. The door is a wise teacher.

(2) AE Fights

ToV has unique fights which are not found in alot of non-PoP content. For example, AE spells. Yes, I know that many zones offer AE spells, but ToV helps MANY people learn how to time AE spells so they can hop out, cast, and get back before getting hit. ToV thus helps you get your timing down, as well as teaching people not to stand in the path of AE if they can avoid it.

(3) Special monsters

ToV offers a wide variety of dragons to kill, each with a different weakness and strength. You use a fire spell on a red drake and it'll be resisted. Ice spell on a blue drake has similiar results. This teaches people to not only listen to the raid leaders, but to break the mold on their thoughts.

ToV Teaches:
-Ability to obey orders
-AE timing, AE hiding, Patience
-Understanding that not all creatures can be killed in the same way.

Here's my recomendation.. you want to get out of ToV? Then push your guild as fast as you can to do those Kael Raids. I've done the Arena with 2 people before, a xp group could certainly do it. Then push your guild to raid ToV as much as you can so you can get out of there.

Rather than quit, why not work at improving your guild?

Let me make a few assumptions, based simply on the fact that your guild sounds a lot like mine. It probably takes around 1-2 hours for you to get a raid force in place and buffed. People most likely do not really know their raid roles and do not really know the mobs they are after. About a third of your raids get called off because of poor attendence, or the mob is not up, or you are missing key classes. On a good day you can take Vindi (assuming you have someone for the pull, offtanking his 2 mates is out of the question)

Mana regen, you have a chanter, probably gives you access to C2/3 and about half the guild casters have solstice earrings. The top end people in your guild are playing with around about FT5. Not everyone has a mount, but that doesn't matter because Kael is an indoor zone anyway.

Now, you have been doing a few LDoNs, then went nosing around on the net, and you realised real early on that the Thurg armour is nowhere near as good as the stuff you are getting in LDoN. Well doing an LDoN is nothing like raiding. Sounds kind of obvious really, but it isn't. In an LDoN, I can normally slow a named mob first or second time without malosinia. In HoT a couple of weeks back I got a chain of 17 resisted spells. Kind of knocks the wind out of you, and you have to reassess how to do the job. I did, and now I can get back on top of my job while raiding.

As for the crappy loots in Kael, you're right, most of the drops are not that good, kill Tormax, and you could get his head, no stats, piece of junk. Not even vendor trash. But if you were to do the quest and hand it in to Yelinak (I think) you can get some pretty nice stuff. Unfortunately, Yelinak, being a dragon is just going to see you as a tasty snack because you don't want to do the faction work.

Now, if you want to see tougher mobs as you put it, try and take your guild there. PoJ trials only need a single group and as a rough rule of thumb, I think you need to be able to put out around 600-700 dps to do it. That should open up PoS/PoV for you if you don't have the levels (although it is unlikely that you will pass a PoJ trial if you are under 55th). Once in PoS, collect Daiku emblems to gain access to PoTac. Your access to PoTac will not count towards planar progression, but it will let you through the door. Once your guild can comfortably deal with Vindi, try PoS giants and the BoT key. Once in BoT, collect the parts for a tower key and away you go.

Now, getting personal, you sound like the sort of person I see far too often, who wants to attack tougher mobs (you can usually read this as PoP mobs) and thinks the earlier stuff is beneath them. This is the kind of person who takes great pride in getting backflagged into the elementals while their old guild is still in Luclin or Velious. Further, you are trying to get us to approve your choice to leave your guild and move onto a new one. Ultimately, the choice of what guild you are in is down to you, and the choice of where you will raid is in the guild or whoever puts in the effort to organise raids.
