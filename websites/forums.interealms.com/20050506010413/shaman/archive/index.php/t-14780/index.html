I 3-box regularly with a friend, so we control a full group together.

We planned this ahead of creating (most of) the characters and we chose:

SHM
SK
BRD
MAG
WIZ
CLR

The shm is an obvious choice.

The sk is an incredible tank for exp group purposes. The shm cannot take aggro unless he does something stupid like slow 5 times just to prove he can get beat on =)

The brd brings mana regen, crowd control and monk-like pulling abilities to the group.

The mag brings dps first and foremost, also supplies everyone (except the brd) with focus items and pet weapons.

The wiz is dps, evac and ports.

The clr is your always-available 96% rez. A clerics healing power should be obvious. The casting time reducer make the nukers' and the sk's life easier (stacks with focus items).

We always get KEI before heading out. With the 62/64 brd mana regen song that gives you a total of 35 mana/tick, not counting FT, sitting on a horse, canni, etc. Bard hastes himself and the sk, shm hastes the pets. If needed the brd can lull or pull with fading memories (=always working feign death).

Now since you asked about a 4-box setup, which two characters would I leave at home?

The wizard has no critical function, so thats easy. Though a wizard on a mount with some FT and getting mana-spoonfed from a bard and KEI is crazy dps. Add in a complete set of summoned focus items...

The second one is harder. I'd probably leave the cleric behind over the mage. The mage dps, the focus items and the pet stuff rocks too much, especially since you have 3 pets out (mag, shm, sk).

I'd go with a sk over a paladin (nm warriors). The sk can churn out enough damage with his combined melee and abundant mana (has a mana-collecting proc too) to almost equal the wizards dps.

A paladin would only be more desirable, if you think the shaman cannot heal enough to keep up and you want the extra healing power for emergencies. In practice however, the shm's replenishment and the bards song give about ~70-80 hp/tick regen, so unless you are up against very hard-hitting mobs it's not an issue.

To really understand what a best 4 box would be, you must look at what is needed for a really good group. You need a few things.

-Tank
-Healing
-Slowing
-Pulling

That is what you _need_ for a successful 4-box after the 40ish levels. Of course, you then want to maximise DPS, and also it is highly suggested that you have a snarer.


With this knowledge you then pick the 4 that cover those bases. Obviously there are many different combinations, and here's what I would suggest.


Shaman, especially one equipped with quiescence can cover the slowing and healing bases. paladin tanking in my opinion is far superior to that of other classes, mostly because they can heal themselves, and hold great aggro with stuns. There is a "weak healing spot" in shaman development, where you do not have the 2000 hitpoint percentage heal, and your best heal is chloroblast *cringe*. A paladins ability to heal himself will help you get through those levels. I would then pick a ranger for pulling, good DPS, and snaring. Then finally I would get a mage to further maximize DPS.

One thing to note, the question is "best 4-box", not "best 4-person group". Multiboxing requires a combination of classes that require relatively little interaction, since only one person has to play all 4. Bards are obviously right out. Rogues would also be difficult, since for optimal usage they need constant repositioning. Similarly, I would argue having a shaman would not be ideal, not because we aren't uber (we are) but because if you aren't constantly casting or canni-ing or torporing yourself, you aren't running at maximum efficiency. ;)

You can set up a nuking/healing class with simple macros that do "stand up, nuke, sit down" - making them much easier to play by oneself.
