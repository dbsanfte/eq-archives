I hope everyone appreciates Jinheim's position. He has been a voice of moderation throughout the discussion regarding shaman balancing. Being a Plane of Time equipped shaman, he's seen virtually all aspects of what a shaman can become in LDoN. And for the most part, pre-GoD he's been satisfied.

There was a precedent set in the design of upgrades for shamans that began after the Kunark expansion. The precedent was that because of a shamans ability to slow, buff, and regenerate mana, they were more powerful than any other class, and therefore, needed the fewest and smallest upgrades. This thinking has dominated every expansion (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=19149&highlight=analysis) , and was clearly applied to the problem of priest balancing.

This precedent has reduced the overall relative power of shamans. Jinheim has argued persuasively that as of the last expansion, rough parity has been achieved. I won't dispute this conclusion, although I don't agree entirely with it. But if parity was achieved in LDoN, GoD now represents a clear disparity between shamans and other classes in virtually every aspect of the game.
