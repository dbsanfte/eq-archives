Karshak_rn
I went to storms at 56 with a guild group and prior to heading up there (I was the only shaman) we talked about slows and the prospect of it being too much for me. The group decided to give it a shot and our backup plan was to head to pon to grind if that failed (Nice of them as they are all high 60's and I was 56).

Anyway, we hit the desert in storms and started picking off trash (storms/cacti/snakes/etc.) and were really surprised that it became a damn good grind session. I would malosi on incoming and slow. Most times it hit first time, max amount of attempts was 3 (hit on the third attempt).

One thing to be ready for is that you will be summoned. The sickening crunch still makes my stomach drop, particularly when you look and see 500hp gone in one melee round, but it just makes life interesting.

Good luck and have fun. It doesn't hurt to try, although I wouldn't recommend starting on the giants, they might not take too kindly to your efforts. :P

I first went to Storms at 55, duoing with a 65 mage. She'd hit them with Malosinia (70pt debuf) and my slow was never resisted.

If, as you hint, Tash is available, hit them with that first. I don't know if you will need Maolsi after that, but it usually has no trouble landing after Tash. I've almost never had slow resisted after landing Malosi (in tier 2).

At 57, the Golelms in the wasteland of Valor seldom resisted my slow after the enchanter hit them with Tash. I only used Malosi if slow was resisted.

The thing to remember is...

THERE IS A GRAVEYARD. THERE IS NO NEED TO TRAIN ANYONE.

You'll do ok and get great experience up until the first wipe, then spend time recovering and do great again. If you have a smart group with some high levels you will probably do much better than in the Tier 1 zones. An all-50's group or a dumb one will lose more XP to CR's than it's worth.

Definately Malosi everything before you attempt slow. Malosi has much lower aggro than slow. Your BF probably has ranger epics, which means he can get a procc'd slow in there to tide you over until you can land yours.
