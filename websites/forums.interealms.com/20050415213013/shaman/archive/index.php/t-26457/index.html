1) Be convenient to use
2) Provide a reasonable damage per mana ratio
3) Be balanced for solo, group and raid encounters
4) Provide an appropriate amount of aggro for it's use
5) Allow for skilled, attentive shaman to get more benefit from the spell

a) Keep the cast and recast times the way they are now
b) Keep the changes to change the pet proc rates
c) Keep the aggro on the recepient and don't put on the caster
d) Tweak the proc damage and mana cost to meet goals #2 and #3 if you feel they are out of line (which I don't)

If SOE can't balance #2 and #3 with recommendation (D) above then consider making the spell a group only buff.

550/proc x 10 procs/ min x 10 proccers = 55,000 dpm = 916 dps.

At some point I'll run out of mana, and that will slow things down. Plus nothing in life runs at 100% efficiency, and for sure this project won't. Can we maintain 70% efficiency? 50% efficiency? Who knows.

Let's say I can genuinely maintain 70% efficiency. That would put me at 641 dps for a theoretical sustainable maximum dps in a group. Is that too much? Maybe it is.

So here's my group question: What is the theoretical maximum sustainable dps, that we don't think a shaman in a group should ever be able to surpass?

Raid. The group scenario above is carefully crafted to have max Panther targets and no need for the shaman to do anything but cast Panther on those targets. That's a pretty unusual group. But it's pretty easy on a raid to have a shaman who has nothing to do but maintain Panther on as many characters as possible during the Burn call on a boss mob.

550/proc x 10procs/min x 18 proccers = 99,000 dpm = 1650 dps

Note that the buff only lands on character number 18 at the point that we are 90 seconds into the fight, and is just fading out on the first one in the same moment. The shaman has been building toward 1650 dps for a minute and a half, and now must maintain it. Let's say that the total fight lasts three minutes. That gives us 90 seconds that builds toward 1650 dps (averaging 825 dps over those 90 seconds) and 90 seconds at 1650 dps (must keep casting Panther on people even when the mob is down to 4% health). That gives a shaman a theoretical burst dps of 1237 over those three minutes, assuming 100% efficiency.

But again, you can't achieve 100% efficiency: people will wander out of range, you will mistarget, and so on. But let's assume 90% efficiency for this brief time period. That would yield an attainable burst dps of 1113 dps over those three minutes. Is that too much burst dps? Maybe it is.

Here, then, is my raid question: What is the theoretical maximum burst dps, that we don't think a shaman in a raid should ever be able to surpass?

Once we have answered these two questions, then we can ask the third question: what is the best way to adjust these spells so that they cannot surpass these levels of damage?

Anything we say before answering these three questions is just shooting in the dark. Is a 30 second refresh timer going to do it? Is a 10 second refresh timer going to do it? Is increased mana cost or aggro modifiers going to do it? If we haven't decided what the cap on shaman sustained damage / burst damage ought to be, then we are just doing blind guesswork and seeing what happens.
