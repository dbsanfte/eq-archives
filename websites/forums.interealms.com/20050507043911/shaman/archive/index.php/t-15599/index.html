I'd like to clear up something about Mage Cannibalization. Most mages call it Pet Canni. It starts being useful at level 49 using the Shovel of Pons quest item from Sol Ro.

Using the spell reclaim energy cast free from the shovel, mages get between 1 mana per 8 to 10 hp (the experts disagree which) of the pet. Since the 49 earth pet has 3150 hp, this nets approx 115 mana after the casting cost. Add in a summoning focus item and you add more mana gained. Add in specialization (assuming the mage is conjuration specialized rather than evocation) and you add another 20 mana per cast roughly. This is all at level 49 with no AAs. Total cost is one malachite, figure 5 silver.

At level 56, when you begin adding in Specialization Mastery, Quick Summoning, etc, it creates a huge increase in mana production. Typically, the more AA's involved, the more mana. However, someone above mentioned the AA Improved Reclaim. What this allows is the ability to gain a set amount from each pet regardless of it's current life. Testing has shown that this decreases the amount gained per pet used for Canni purposes, but adds significantly for pet chaining, where the mage reclaims the pet when it's near dead and summons another.

Being only 62, I can easily gain 225 mana in 2 ticks (10 seconds plus or minus my own lag). I understand that 65 mages with the Son of Rathe (65 earth pet) and the focus item for post 60 pets are gaining around 302 mana per cast with no monetary cost.

In a chain pulling exp group, I have never once had a group that begrudged me the time off in taking a single pull with no damage done to go from OOM to FM.

I hope this explains things a little.
