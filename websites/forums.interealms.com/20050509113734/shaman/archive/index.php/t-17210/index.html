Jinheim
MC3 and Canni5 first. You are insane to do anything else.

For the most part, I've found that concentrating on one AA line at a time is more rewarding than spreading them out. I have 302AA at this time, which is basically everything except resists and stat AA, so I like to think I speak from experience.

My reasoning is this: You will not see much of a difference with a few 2% bonuses to different things. Those minor bonuses will not help you. However, if you do an entire line of AA and get a 10% or even larger bonus, you will notice it and it will help you.

Which AA's to get first depends on your gear, your playstyle, and your personal preference.

Regardless of what you do, ND3, SCM3, and SCR3/SCRM are all good investments. I would say that SCRM, for the 6-7 tick quiescence, is the most powerful archetype AA (its a class AA, but is really just an extension of an arch aa) we have. Out of the three AA I listed, I got it last, but I wish I had gotten it first. ND3 should only be your first pick if you absolutely must have more hitpoints. If you don't have enough HP to hit canni5 after a rezz (1924) then you need to train ND. If not, you can wait until after the other big two. ND3 increases your HP by 10%, and SCM3 effectively increases your mana by 10%, by giving you great mana savings. I rarely get hit unless I intend to, so honestly I would find SCM3 a better buy and would do it first, but I would definately get ND3 after that.

After that, there's plenty of ways to go. If you mainly group, consider healing AA's to help keep your group alive. Also quickbuff 2 or 3 for situations where you find yourself as main or patch healer. If you raid, QB is practically a necessity for keeping your sanity. On raids, once a mob is debuffed, I almost solely heal, so I find healing AA useful once again.

If you like to solo, defensive AA's should be at the top of your list. If your initial root or slow gets resisted, you're going to need them or else you're going to go splat. I have channeling focus 3 and its not a dramatic improvement, but I guess it's something else to consider if you're a soloer.

As for PoP AA's:

A lot of people will rant and rave about spirit call, but I don't find it that amazing. Extra free damage is nice when you're soloing, but the truth is that your pets are going to die before they do their full damage. Higher ranks of spirit call will help this exponentially, though. The reason being is that mobs don't pick one pet and attack it until it is dead. They spread out their damage. Thus, with 5 pets, most of them are likely to live against a pov golem, whereas if you just have SC1, all three of your pets will probably die. I solo almost exclusively in pofire now, and SC is completely worthless. The die in one round of attacks, so I usually don't even bother. For grouping, SC is only ok imho. It is free damage, but the damage they do is pretty insignificant compared to what a full group does. In LDoN mobs very rarely last the full minute duration, so oftentimes it gets wasted (just like our dots). A lot of people also talk about how SC is awesome for crowd control, but I think they are full of **** honestly. They say stuff like "I just cast SC on a mob and it is under control for the next minute". That's a load of bull. In the 6 seconds it takes you to cast SC, you could have cast turgurs or root. Also, if the mob is already slowed, your SC pets aren't going to outaggro you. If it isn't slowed, they're all going to die. So basically, SC isn't good for CC. It's ok for sicking them on a mob thats arleady rooted and slowed just for the free damage, but that's it. SC is useful for pulling however: send your pets in to a room with three mobs, then pull them off and root them one by one. So basically, SC isn't a terrible AA or anything but it isn't something I'd recommend getting early.

Virulent Paralysis, on the other hand, is a great AA. I waited to get this until around 280AA, and I really wish I had gotten it sooner. If you solo a lot, this is a must. I would still wait until after SCRM, ND3, and SCM3, but it would be high on my list after that. Many mobs that have innately low disease resist are completely dominated by VP. Pofire guardians for example: If I pull with malicious decay, VP will never, ever, break early. I've soloed over a hundred of them since I finished VP3, and it has yet to let me down. I'm sure there are mobs in lower tier planes where this applies, as well. It definately doesn't work for them all, though. On top of its uses soloing, it is great for crowd control. Usually, when an add comes to the group and you root it, you have to spend about 20-30 seconds talking to your groupmates to get them to understand that it is rooted and they should back away from it instead of just standing underneath it. With VP, the knockback component can, if aimed right, just knock it out of camp so that you don't have to try to make your groupmates understand that they should move. This is a really really excellent AA for LDoN and has been a great help. So, get this AA pretty soon, before 100. Also, if you are going to train VP, train it all the way to rank3. It isn't nearly as effective before then, and you can't really use the root for soloing at ranks 1 or 2, because it will wear off before you can recast, whereas with VP3 you have about 10 seconds to recast once the button pops.

I used to love radient cure but I don't like it very much now that LDoN came out. It doesn't cure **** except AE tash from chanter mobs, and maybe the occasional trap. I'd say 4 out of 5 times, it doesn't work. I would train rank1 of this, just for the chance at curing yourself of snares and malos and other counterless effects, but save rank 2 and 3 until later.

If you have any questions just be more specific and I'll try to answer.

edit: Changed my mind about defensive AA. No matter who you are, you should get them. When I am in an exp group, I am almost always with a guildie warrior with a blade of war or a beast of an SK, so even if I get a resist on an early slow, I'm still not going to get aggro. Most people don't have that luxury, though, so you're going to need defensive AA for those resisted slows. When I was still doing pickup groups in pov and bot, I'd say 80% of my deaths were from resisted slows where defensive AA might have made a diference. I'd still rate defensive AA as being more important for a soloer, but still important for a grouper (and a higher priority than healing AA).

On raids, defensive AA aren't that important because if you get aggro on a boss mob, you're going to die. If you get aggro on a yard trash mob, either you'll get heals and live, or you won't get heals and you'll die. Defensive AA won't help you there.

First, get MC3 to open up Canni V, then get Canni V. -Nothing else will give you the boost that will. Even if you are the only shaman on the raid, you will not be the reason for the med break ;)

If you raid, get MGB. It will save you tons of time and mana. Even if there are other shaman around to do the MGB buffs get it. Being able to refresh a MGB on the fly when its needed keeps raids moving.

Then go for SCR3/SCRM. Its a lot of AA, but your buffs will last forever. Since this effects torpor/Q as well, it really helps with recovering health when cannibalizing.

SCM3. You wont notice the savings per spell, but it really does stretch your mana pool a lot. This coupled with the above AAs makes you a mana machine. You just wont use mana nearly as fast as you regain it.

By this point you may be burnt out on grinding the big AA numbers, so its a good time to pick up the cheap but usefull PoP AAs... Radiant Cure, Virulent Paralysis, Spirit Call. They go fast and you can see the effects.

Now its time to get the survival AAs. ND3, CA3, LR5. They will allow you to go toe to toe with mobs that would take you down in a round or two before you got them. When soloing, or when you get agro on a raid you will be glad you had them... plus its fun to be able to tell your group that you can tank while the meat shield is afk :)

This question comes round quite a lot, I guess that's why there's a sticky AA guide on it but despite that, there's still some hard decisions to make.

The one thing that is clear to me is the Run3/Regen3 (or maybe Metab3, especially for kitties) -> MC3 -> Canni V. Anything else you hear, forget it. Low health aggro? Don't worry THAT much about it, its still worth getting canni V asap. Most often you'll have buffs to put you above it anyway. Canni V transforms the shaman's life.

Beyond that its a lot harder to choose. Me, I got SC1 and VP1. Perhaps not the most uber AA, but after grinding MC3-Canni V I wanted some FUN! Whats more, I do actually find these very useful, particularly VP.

Then I got serious again - as the main shaman in a (small, HoT/Vindi level) guild, I got MGB. No regrets there, it takes away a lot of hassle and I'm soooo glad I have it now. Now MGB is a pain to get, so once again I went for a little fun and got SCF1 and Healing gift 1 (crit heals). Wouldn't recommend doing that but they are fun to see those crits :D

Next for me came SCRM, now this is one sweet AA skill but again a big grind to get too. But now, my quiessence heals for 1800 hp minimum, every time. My main buffs last just go on and on and on, just like the batteries in that advert with the bunnies. And best of all perhaps, I now only get calls for ferine avatar and haste half as often as I used to!

Then I went for QB2. Another great AA skill, I love it best for the quick heals and quiessence, but its also nice for casting the standard buffs much faster. Now I'm working on MC and ND.

Well, as you can see my path was a little off the norm, but if you want to forget about fun and go for utility here's my suggestion. Everything after Canni V is all fairly situational but I'd say everything up to the line break should come before the stuff after it, unless you're extremely focussed on one thing (e.g 3-4th shaman in raiding guild may focus more on healing AA, soloing shaman may focus more on ND3 and defensive and utility AA etc)
