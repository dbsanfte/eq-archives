Kianor
A hell level was previously where the experience multiplier for a level was increased. Because of the way it was applied in EQ, instead of simply making one level harder than the next, it worked out that the experience needed was equivalent to making all the previous levels the same amount harder, and lumping all of that into one level.

That meant in effect, that the experience required to complete a hell level was more than double that needed for the previous level.

This applied to 30, 35, 40, 45, but also to 54 and 59 - the levels before title changes.

The pre-50 progression was smoothed out first, but the post-50 progression was also smoothed out some time later.

However, each level post 50 does get progressively harder than the previous level. 59 is still slow, as you've observed, but take it from me, it's not as slow as it used to be. I levelled my ranger to 60 pre-change and my shaman post-change.

You can always take comfort from the fact that 60 is a breeze. :)

You are writing:
"Death at 60 seems to cost more because it takes signifigantly less points to get through it. You still lose same amount of exp, but it's coming out of a smaller point pool, so looks like a larger loss."

Before I started my shaman, I played a cleric for 2 years, and my experience is that you are wrong here. When you die, you loose a fixed percentage of the experience you needed for the previous level. So dying in lvl 60 will cost you a percentage of the huge amount of exp you needed for the lvl 59 hell level. Getting a res at lvl 60 will thus also give you back way more exp.

But you are correct when saying that it looks worse because it is compared to the smaller amount of exp needed on the current level.
