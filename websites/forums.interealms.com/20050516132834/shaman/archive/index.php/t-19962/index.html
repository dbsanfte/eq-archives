LaughingWoman
Tendaena - my boyfriend and I were in your EXACT situation a short while ago. We both were able to finance our missing 63-65 spells as well as picking up some ornate armor - all in the bazaar, by farming / exploring underused zones.

My first recommendation is for both of you to get suspend minion 2 before anything else. In droga, for example, the mage can pop on his fixed duration invis and CoH you to the named camp of your choice - which is a lot safer with a fully armored and buffed pet at his side.

Second is to put in the time (if you have it) at the takish dungeon for the mage pet augment. It's really ridiculous the benefits you BOTH will get from that enhanced pet.

DROGA -- We spent a LOT of time in droga. Not for the named drops - though we found that the drops sold quite well - but we were originally after the quest bits for the FT2 shoulders for me and the FT2 neck for him. We ended up getting both, but banked about 100k each by selling the named drops and the quest bits that we didn't need. How much will people on your server pay for the quest bits to get a hybrid only FT1 70hp earring? On xev it's 10k or more each. Yes, these drops are rare, but it's a chance to make the devaluation of platinum work for YOU for once. *grin* The plat they drop, and the skins to sell in the bazaar add up as well.

VEKSAR -- maxed cabilis faction by turning in the weapons, while I was at it. Very nice drops and good cash per mob, like droga. Undead-heavy, so it's a good idea to LDoN to bane if you want to help with damage.

CHARDOK B -- again, heavy on the undead, but very easy for a shaman/mage to mow through these critters. Max your chardok faction, and pick up named drops as well as the random drops chardok always had.

ACRYLIA -- haven't done anything here outside of VT shards, but the acrylia sells VERY VERY well (between 500 and 2k per BRICK on xev, people are insane). We plan on going back when we have some spare time.

There are a few threads on each of these zones for more detail, but they're all high-exp dungeon zones, very easy for a 65 shaman/mage duo, and will net you a good amount of cash over the long term. Overall, I've found that there's very little a shm/mag duo can't do, and we've had a lot of fun exploring new places in our hunt for spell-plat.

Aqualung
I've spent a lot of time in droga, and i can tell you that you'd be better off there without the mage.
To optimize your time there, you'll be wanting to invis between camps and tank-fight mobs on their spawn point, to avoid clearing trash. Trying to use a pet in this style is a death sentence, since they will inevitably path out into the middle of the room and make a large mess.
If you fight king where he spawns, the rest of the room won't add. Flame master can be single pulled over the table. Ice master can be singled from on top of the bunk bed in his room. Earth seer is all alone, easy root/rot kill.
You alone can easily keep these 4 spawns cleared before repops. I could usually work in an extra named spot and still have time to get back.
Stone Crook, Whip Cracker, Bone Knight, are all killable without adds.

Your best bet would be to both go to droga, and then split up. Let the Mage take care of a couple of the easier camps that involve clearing a few trash mobs, while you do as many of the camps i stated as you can. Having both of you there will be good, since all the item drops are lore.
Just be prepared to hear people whine about how many camps you are taking up. If you're inclined to be a nice guy about it, put the priority on king and icemaster spawns. Those 2 are the best sellers, for the most part, and also ALWAYS spawn a cave lord or an icemaster for their placeholders. important because they can randomly drop the quest items for the FT2 neck and shoulder.

When i first started farming droga, the prices were about 3x higher than they are now, but you can still make some good cash there, based simply on how many named spawns you will be able to keep cleared.
If you're clearing 5 nameds, you can expect 2-3 items per hour. Almost everything still sells for 4-5k, the hammer a bit more. That equates to around 10k an hour, or more. not too shabby.

Threads like this emphasize the worst design aspect of EQ at present.

It's retarded that people feel so stymied in obtaining their spells that they have to farm up 45k in cash to buy a spell. And sadly that's reasonable (and even expected by some), because hoping for runes drops in BoThunder or PoTactics (if flagged) can be a really slow process.

I scrabbled through getting my spells, and it involved a lot of bazaar merchanting. That's asinine in an adventure/quest game.

Now my guild can farm earth rings, and we'll get like 6 runes a night that no one needs - except twinks. So, we sell them them to the folks who used to be like us, scrabbling along trying to get spells.

The loot scheme is wrong. It should not be about farming plat to buy things in the bazaar. It should be about hunting your spells and gear.

I don't think runes are sufficiently available at present, but ironically they might be overpresent in the elementals.

There's limited fun and adventure farming crap for gear. It just feeds into maggots like Yantis and IGE providing short cuts, when a better game mechanic can both limit their involvement and corruption of the economy and provide a better gameplay reward system.
