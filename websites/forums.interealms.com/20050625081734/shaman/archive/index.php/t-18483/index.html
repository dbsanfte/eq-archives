Haagen
I just recently maxed alchemy, with GM trophy, and I'm cruising pretty good on money. I'm still in the red in relation to how much I spent to get to this point, but every single combine I do is profit. I have no inventory sitting on mules devaluing, failed combines don't cost me a cent in replenishment, and I don't have the aggravation of trying to sell potions on the blue moon when someone will wander into the bazaar and give random_undescribed_potion_01 a glug glug.

At the risk of sounding like Don LePre ("With just a tiny little ad...!") -- how do I do this?

I keep no inventory on hand. I don't pre-combine very pricey potions like gate, greater concentration, etc. That's basically it. I have a couple hotkeys set up on hotbank 8 that spam a /ooc and a message into the bazaar channel saying I'm a GM Alchemist with GM trophy offering my services. I announce that I'm selling such and such potions, some pricey, some not-so-pricey, with very brief descriptions (FT2/3, SoW, DS, ITU, Invis, Gate, Port).

When I get a nibble, I tell the customer they can either:

1). Buy the components themselves. I give them the list of components and where they can find them. I'll even go with them, or

2). Have them give me the money to buy exclusively the components, and no more.

Once we get the components into my medicine bag, I invite the person into my group so they can watch failures or combines. I charge a sliding-scale fee depending on the potion for successes. Gate and FT potions are a certain amount, Invis is a certain amount, insta-heal is a certain amt., etc. This way, I'm not stuck with a surplus inventory that I just can't offload. I'm not stuck with supplies in my bag if a customer changes their mind mid-transaction, which would ultimately lead to having potions that I can't offload.

edit: Another thing I try to avoid is charging a fee greater than the component cost. For example with decent charisma the FT3 components cost ~85pp for one dose. I charge less than that. Which is more than I charge for a SoW combine, but less than I charge for a gate combine.
end edit

Charging for only successful combines makes them feel (accurately) like they're not paying for sucky RNG rolls.

So to sum up:

1. Have customer pay for components outright, either directly (buying themselves) or indirectly (fronting you the money to buy)

2. Charge per successful combine, and everyone walks away happy.

P.S. For the love of god don't tell them the name of the potion unless they ask. You know someone is sitting in the bazaar with a 10 dose potion that costs atleast 850pp to make for 400pp cuz they're tired of selling it. I know that if the customer is in the bazaar and they ask me the name of the potion, I probably lost the sale. But at least I haven't lost plat.

Hope this helps ease some of the pain.
