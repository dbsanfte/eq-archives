snapdragn
I would go with the advice in the other thread - stay in Tier 1 planes until you hit 60 (if not 62 for Tnargs).

The razor fiends are all at least lvl 62, so you're gonna have tons of resists. If the rest of the grp is also around your lvl, they are going to have the same problems, plus low HPs on your tank. I've seen a lvl 61 cleric go OOM on just 2 pulls when a 58 Paladin was tanking...nothing wrong with the cleric, the tank just couldn't last long enough for HoTs and Complete Heals.

So the best strategy to '4-role' in Tier 2 is go get some levels.

Specific to outer: When I have grp'd at Outer with a necro it's usually easiest to aggro-kite the mobs around in the area. pets + melees just run along behind it while necro keeps the fiend focus'd on it. Other classes can be the rabbit (you could try it some time) but necro's are the best by far.

General advice: If you are doing all four roles (haste, buff, slow, heals) you will be spending the majority of your mana on heals (by far). Find ways to reduce the amount of healing required. Aggro kiting, root/rotting with other casters, pet-tanking, etc.

LOL I was thinking exactly the same thing.

Buffing, no-brainer. Give out the minimum buffs you can, try to avoid downtime due to buffing, but you gotta do a certain amount of it.

Haste is not an issue any more thanks to Tali of Alacrity. Longer duration and group-based, woot.

Slow, well, that's just what we do. No need to go into those techniques here.

Healing is one of the tougher ones until you get Quiescence. Takes some careful mana management and constant cannibalization. If you're the sole healer/slower, YOU call the med breaks if necessary.

The riskiest time in the group is when the mob is not slowed yet, but you're the main healer. Make sure the pallys and even rangers are aware of this and maybe they can cover for you while you get slow to stick. Use Regrowth, it really is worth it. And always heal with Stoicism/Torpor/Quiescence when possible - Sto and Tor can only be used on non-melee during combat, but if it looks like the mob is going to be dead soon, hold off on that last heal and Torpor after it's dead.

jdthomas
Your thinking that its just a case of sour grapes from long time players, its not. At 60, you gain the tools you need to do these things, they simply arent there at 59. Malo and torpor are invaluable in this situation. You can have torpor on the tank to mitigate the first shock of combat. Malo will make slow stick almost every time, no bouncing aggro.
Consider this, malosini on incoming, and it fails. You can malosini again, hoping it sticks, or slow. If malosini sticks, the slow will sticks, if it doesn't it can be a little iffy. It is entirely realistic to bounce 2 or 3 spells off one of these without the unresistable debuff. By the time you finally have the beast slowed, the tank is nearly dead and your 10 sec casting ch is simply useless. Torpor would have covered in this situation. It simply IS best to limit yourself a bit in this case, causing wipes because you're constantly over-extended is not a good way to be invited back.
