I don't miss Verant in the slightest. They were the guys who liked to make you count out coins one by one, make nothing stack, make you have trouble opening bags in the bank -- that kind of thing. Anything and EVERY LAST POSSIBLE THING to add in mind-numbing, boring time sinks...the game was ALL about making your experience as time-consuming as possible. The contempt for players seemed to veer into virtual hatred, as evidenced and quoted numerous times by the execs themselves. Verant forgot their own game as soon as possible, and adopted the most blinkered outlook possible -- make it cost time, because time pads our bank accounts. Our customers simply don't care whether they enjoy the game or whether anybody would; they are hooked and it's time to milk them hard and dry.

SOE added things like graveyards and port stones and coin sliders and more bank space and many more things being stackable. They have done lots of things that prove their vision is at LEAST as good as Verant's when it comes to just letting people have fun and get on with it without wasting time.

But...they follow in line of some of the same mistakes of Verant. Why should the value of loot absolutely skyrocket every expansion or two? Sure, people play for upgrades, but they do so because of human psychology. How do you quantify the psychological need? By adding 200 hitpoints to items in the new expansion? 100? 40? 20? How do you quantify the focuses or augments needed to make people want to play to get them, and feel satisfied when they do? How crazily high does it have to get? SOE's answer has been -- pretty crazily high.

And when they make new expansions have monster loot(soloable loot better than ornate anyone? one groupable elemental level loot or better?), they fall into the same trap the game has always fallen into. Give people drastically better upgrades and let them get it according to the same old formulas for the most part -- big fat raids, or smaller groups that are truly uber, and make it stuff so far above what anyone not uber can get that you basically count most of your customers out of the rewards of the expansion not just for the foreseeable future but pretty much forever, for most of them. Make each expansion more or less worthless to the casual player, and exorbitantly rewarding to the hard core, making the latter woop with delight at the incredible ease they have upgrading even their most pathetic low level twinks.

Each time you make loot(including spell) upgrades trivialize loot in previous expansions, you have to worry far more about messing up class balances in terms of spells and abilities, and outdating previous expansions. Upgrading? Fine. Trivializing? Bad move. It's the GIMME GIMME IT ALL NOW desire being addressed with a short sighted SURE, GIMME YOUR MONEY attitude on the part of SOE. Combined with endless flags, it makes more relaxed players burn out on the game and never really meets the needs of the truly hard core, while all the time constantly introducing balancing issues(do shamans now need a better heal for these mobs? has the nature and value of slows changed entirely? is it cool that wizards are tanking stuff that used to wipe tanks out?).

SOE has made the game much less tedious on the one hand, and fallen into the insane loot inflation race that Verant started to get into also. Do they really want 10k necros running around anytime soon, tanking elementals? Well, that's what they're going for.

Smaller, more modest progression would do the game a world of good. SOE, by trying to ram the quick pay-off down the gullet of the average sucker, doesn't have the trust in the player community or the understanding of psychology needed to make more than a quick buck; they lack the ability to see what makes a game worthwhile over a long period of time. Perhaps they don't envision the game lasting too much longer?

At any rate, looking at the game in a shallow way(probably the majority of shamans and hybrid tanks seem to feel SOE has done so during this expansion, for instance) makes SOE seem like they lack a lot of forethought. They seem to have fallen for the holy trinity they had long since previously learned was very limiting and very trying for players, rewarding some players exorbitantly for choosing certain classes and punishing or ignoring the needs of everyone else simply to get a group reliably and PLAY. Slow mitigation was a time bomb waiting to explode; and it finally has. There is no instance in which I wouldn't recommend either a beastlord or an enchanter instead of a shaman at present. It's just that it has taken until GoD to show people how weak shaman truly are to make the implicit obvious, and wise up the slow learners and encourage them to take classes who can not just slow, but slow AND.

Verant was no walk in the park. Their contempt for the customer was breathtaking. In those terms, SOE seem like bumblers. They don't hate the customer and want to make him waste every possible second of time humanly imaginable without consideration that the customer might not like that idea; but they don't have any real vision either. They're assembling the future of the game like someone painting by numbers, more or less mechanically and without real understanding. In a business of customers, you can't forget to sell the customer; SOE isn't cranking out widgets -- they have to dig in there and get their hands dirty relating to people and their needs if they want people to stay.

Because when it comes to game mechanics -- there will be other games out there very soon. And EQ doesn't necessarily fulfill enough needs successfully enough to not lose at least some money when that happens. They are not even close to the point at which they can consider resting on their laurels and just cranking out more or less interchangeable expansions without considering the implications of their designs. In fact, they will have to play some very serious catch-up as things stand.

Personally, I think the holy trinity is about as deep as SOE's insight into the game goes, since they actually made the foolish mistake of coming BACK to it after it had previously been eliminated as a game-crippling handicap. The extra need for flags and keys in yet another expansion hardly helps. Slows and buffs -- dead and gone, forget it. They're over, as issues, and will likely never be meaningfully adjusted or addressed again. I doubt heals will either, so I would just suggest perhaps more DPS as the only likely way they'll do any further work of note on shamans at all; failing their doing so, I would suggest turning your shaman into an alt or two-box toon while you powerlevel your main, and if you can play more than one toon on raids, using your shaman to malos and buff on raids while you concentrate on a class SOE actually has it within the scope of their talents and desire to develop and address -- healers and DPS. Or perhaps a chanter.
