Innate Defense and Lightning Reflexes are "save your ass" AAs. They are streaky and only work sometimes.

Innate Defense, if you "win the roll" for it to be active, will mitigate incoming hits, like Gangas said. You won't be hit for max damage. It operates in such a way that you never really know that it's working unless someone like Fennin Ro or some GoD boss hits you for 20 for a round of melee.

Lightning Reflexes is much better and much more noticeable.
Sariss
A dev posted on the warrior boards, I'm sorry I don't have a link, but he said that Innate Defense line of AAs only raises your AC Cap. Thus, you get better mitigation from your AC, not technically from the AA.

What this means is, if you have a low AC, you buy these AAs, your AC Cap goes up... but it means frick all to you... nothing... because you're not at your cap anyhow, because you have low AC.

If you have high AC, and you get these AAs, you'll get more mitigation bonus from your AC since your cap has now been raised.

The Dev did not post the AC value, but did say that mid-upper end 65 warriors should have no trouble getting to their AC cap for the AAs to be useful.

What that means for other tanks, and priests, I don't know... so these AAs are virtually bottom on my list until my AC is massively high (if ever).

The Reflexes AAs are worth their weight in gold. Streaky yes, no matter what your AA level there is no guarenteed miss... but totally worth it.

Haass Wolvesbane
Warrior here, i'll answer this question for ya, so you don't get a bunch of people giving their ideas and getting confused.

Lets say we make a graph of what a mob hits you for over time. We'll obviously see a spike at certain damages based upon your AC compared to the mob's ATK. What innate defense does (And this is also what AC does actually) is move that graph slightly to the left (IE : Closer to zero).

The maximum a mob can hit you for will not change. The minimum a mob can hit you for will not change (So you will never be hit for 20 by Fennin ro...it's not a number he can hit you for). What it does do, is make it so that over a large sample of hits, you'll be getting hit for less more often than you were before you trained the AA.

Innate Defense is basically equivilant to AC. It reduces the mob's average hit, though the max hit remains unchanged, which reduces your damage intake over time by the given percentage. EDIT: Haass's line graph description is spot on.

Lightning Reflexes is NOT better. LR has the advantage of helping with channeling, because a miss will not interrupt you, but that's where it's advantage ends. ID reduces the same amount of damage over time, and does so more consistantly - mitigation is less streaky than avoidance.

Griz, LR gives a percentage improvement to your chance to avoid an attack. So, if your chance to avoid was 50%, and LR gave (for example) a 10% improvement (I dont know the exact numbers), you would have a 55% chance to avoid. In the case of PA he has a very high accuracy setting, so your chances to avoid are very low. A 10% improvement of nothing is still nothing, and that is why he hit you every time. For PA having ID is way better than having LR.
