Vallon Zek is the server I play on. It is + or - 8 levels as well. There is only 1 guild that is clearly ahead of the others. There are about 2-4 guilds just below the highest one, and then there's about everybody else. The server has mostly become lightie vs darkie with the top guilds being 3-team guilds (all races except darkies). Right now the darkies are a bit down and we lighties control most of the server.

As far as how to play on a pvp server... well, that's a very large topic and one that is still debated on pvp server msg boards. Basically, play with your head, don't expect to level too fast, don't expect to gear up too fast, learn how to play pvp as opposed to pve, develop a good reputation, and learn as you go.

Gearwise... well... like all gear questions i answer this: it depends on your gear as a whole, and not 1 piece vs. 1 piece. The major difference gear-wise on a pvp server is that resist gear is required at level 6 until level 60, whereas on a blue server you don't have to worry too much about resists until the high 40's. At high level you'll probably have your "main" set of gear and two bags or so of alternate gear depending on the pvp situation (not unlike blue servers at high level really).

Rangers in PvP... it all depends on how you play. If you go out there and think you're a warrior you'll get your ass handed back to ya. If you learn and play smart, you can be a monster in pvp. The only ranger on Vallon that I know who has AM3 and endless quiver is a friggin BEAST in pvp.

you'll need to carry a bag of utility stuff (pumice stones, potions such as envenomed heal which will instantly cure tashani when it gets cast on you).

you'll need at least 2 sets of weapons. 1 set as your main set of weapons (highest damage output weapons) and you'll need a jousting weapon (the highest damge 2hander you can find, the delay on the weapon is irrelevant). some end up having several sets of weapons (for instance they'll have they're epics as main set, a 33/51 as their jousting weapon, and a Journyman's stick so they can try and proc tashani on an opponent, very hard to land a snare at high level without somehow debuffing your opponent)

these are just ramblings off the top of my head, it's really something you have to get a feel for. It's a lot of fun, and after 2 years on a pvp server I don't see myself ever going back to a blue server.

My experiences on Rallos Zek (±4 level limit on PvP, one item + coin loot, no race/class/deity PvP restriction) is that the normal blue server leveling path is much more difficult. Outdoor zones where experience comes easily are the nastiest when it comes to PvP attacks. For instance, solo fear kiting of snow cougars in Iceclad Ocean isn't a viable option on Rallos Zek.

I can't speak for the post-50 game, where 30 vs. 30 epic PvP battles in the Planes are not unusual. In the pre-50 game, I'd be sure I was in a full group with a VERY small level range when hunting in the normal outdoor zones. Having one player 3 or 4 levels above everyone else often means death to that person - the PKs DO look at you from a distance, and WILL attack when you least expect it and when you're ill prepared.

You're less likely to be attacked in dungeons (with few exceptions). I learned about Droga and Nurga while playing on Rallos Zek - they're great places to work on indoor (meaning no SoW or Harmony) fighting skills with minimal risk of encountering PKs.

I haven't played on the other PvP servers. They do have the advantage of lower risk (no items can be lost, except possibly on Sullon Zek, where corpse camping is legal). However, you'd have to deal with the politics there - the idea of 4-team guilds is bothersome to me. (That allows for the "invulnerable healer" tactic, in which a group consisting of one high elf cleric and five evils attacks a group of elves. The elves CANNOT attack the high elf cleric, who is helping the evils.) Also, it's more difficult to get to some places thanks to territorial control. I'd imagine it would be a lot harder to get to Nagafen's Lair as a ranger on Tallon Zek than it would be on Rallos Zek.

Yes, having decent MR (to reduce the chance of being snared, rooted, or blinded) is more important. On Rallos Zek, it's a good idea to have a "backup set" of armor for traveling. Losing a banded item or +7 MR ring is no big deal at level 30; having that ivy etched piece that you just spent eight hours getting or the 5AC 55HP ring you paid 200pp for HURTS.
