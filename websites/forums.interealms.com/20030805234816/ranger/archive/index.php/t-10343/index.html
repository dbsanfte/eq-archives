Brannuer
Here's a tip on fear kiting the griffs that I find works pretty consistently for me. Note that I fear kite mainly in a large open valley South East of the Fort, directly West of the 'good' gnolls tunnel (don't remember their names atm), and right beside a ridge which, if I run East over it, gets me to the poacher camp and then the river. Sorry I don't have the loc. I hunt here because there is a wide open grassy bowl with no trees and few rocks. Easy on lag and the feet too.

I pull with snare/ensnare back to the back of the valley, which is the South wall of the zone. What I've noticed is that there is a centerline of the valley, as well as two other, umm, 'boundary lines we'll call them, that seem to be near the base of each of the ridges that seem to decide where your griff will go.
Essentially I think these are mob pathing lines, but I'm just guessing. When you hit a griff with Panic Animal, he'll turn in the direction the line closest to him and keep going that direction until(if) you attack.

So to steer him, I let him path to me (he's aggro'ed with snare, so he'll follow you) until he's closest to the line I want him to go along, Panic to get him directly towards the line, then attack when I've got a clear path North. It will always, immediately turn North and fly North when I attack.

When fear wears off I path him back to the start if I'm feeling neat and tidy, or just keep fearing him along. I have a contest with myself now to see how short a distance I have to kite him go before he dies, since it is always straight north.

This is what I've found in the spot I'm most familiar with, and seem like it works in other areas, though you'll have to find the magic tracks in whatever area you choose to camp. I've also found this to work the same with anaconda's with the added benefit that I don't have to have magic arrows to hit them )
