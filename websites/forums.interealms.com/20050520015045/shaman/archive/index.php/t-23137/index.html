Sariss
I'd like to know what everyone thinks of the current crop of video card, especially with respect for current EQ, EQ 2/WoW down the road, and even other games in general.

Right now I have a GeForce 4 Ti 4400. It's been a workhorse for me since I bought it awhile ago (when they were new). The rest of my computer has been upgraded over time, and only the video card is "old".

I've kinda resigned myself to the fact that the new cards (and even top end old cards) are still expensive. New mid/mainstream cards are in the pipe and coming down (GF 6600, ATI X600) and those should be cheaper.

I generally buy a card, and skip 1-2 generations of cards. I have mostly been an nvidia fan, but the ATI 9800+ lines of ATI have been getting some impressive feedback.

Currently in stock around town (Toronto) I've been able to find name brand X800 Pro's, 3rd party X800 Pros and XTs, X800 Pros that "possibly" can be overclocked/modded into XTs, and original model GF 6800's.

Currently EQ does not support hardware rendering in GeForce 6800s... this has been confirmed on the tech boards, and they are looking into a fix.

Original GeForce 6800s have good performance... but are noisy, take up 2 slots, run fairly hot... not bad cards... just not as "clean" a design as the ATI cards.

There is the pure ATI X800 Pro, which doesn't seem to be upgradeable to the XT level.

There's Sapphire X800 Pros which "maybe" can be softmoded up to the XT level of pipes and clocks for the same price.

There's Sapphire X800 XT's, which cost more but are for sure to be XT level.

I'm kinda picky about my cards... I really don't know, or trust Sapphire... I think that if I wanted to go ATI ... I'd want a built by ATI, and not Powered By... but I could just be cutting myself out of free performance upgrades for the sake of being "pure ati".

Does anyone have any thoughts on "Built by ATI" vs "Powered by ATI"?

For about $500:
- ATI 9800 XT.... old tech (heh, "old")... old speed king... solid card... slightly cheaper.

For about $600:
- ATI X800 Pro... pure ATI, 2nd fastest model available, almost for sure not easily upgraded to XT level.

- Sapphire X800 Pro ... virtually the same as the ATI, $10 more, at least a modest chance that it can be software (bios) upgraded to the X800 XT level. "free" performance upgrade... however this is not guarenteed, and even if it works, stability may be affected. A lot of success stories with early models... but also some problem stories.

- ASUS nVidia GeForce 6800 ... it's their custom card, V9999 Gamer Edition... original GF 6800 (big, noisy, hot). They did some custom upgrades on it... it's nearly equal to the GT performance.

For about $750: (ouch... getting expensive)

- Sapphire, X800 XT. Top of the line "powered by" ATI... at a dang expensive cost.


There's also the option of ordering a GeForce 6800 GT version online... for about $600. The GT is the refined 6800 basically... smaller, cooler, quieter, and better performance than original 6800s. Ordering online has hassles of waiting, might be out of stock, etc... it's not really the waiting that bothers me so much.... it would just piss me off to be dragged along week after week saying "its on order". Thats why I like using real stores, with in stock.

A 6800 looks like a BAD buy right now, with EQ not fully supporting it... I figure SOE will get this fixed up pretty quick ... they are part of the nvidia "The way it's meant to be played" or whatever campaign... nothing says you suck more than not supporting your partners new best card.

As I said... I'm a bit hesitant on the "Built by ATI" Sapphire cards... I'd be leaning pure ATI... but the chance of a free software upgrade to an XT level on the Sapphire is really appealing.

Does anyone have one of these cards and regret it? Like you bought an X800 Pro, and were just disappointed? (or the opposite, really excited?)

I'm also looking beyond Everquest... EQ 2 will require a nice DX 9 card. My current GeForce 4 is only a DX 8 card in hardware I think.... maybe even EQ 1 would benifit from a fully DX 9 card.

(Keep in mind my prices above were Canadian prices, ballpark, take 30% or so off them for american prices)

Thank you for your thoughts.
