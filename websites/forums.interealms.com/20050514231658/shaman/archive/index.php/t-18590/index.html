You can invis with pet suspended.
You can have a spare pet ready if your current pet dies.

Tragik
To put it simply:

Pet Hold -
1. Can be used at any time whether in battle or during pulls.
2. /pet hold followed with /pet back off will effectively put the pet on standby and totally clear it's hate list (pet hold alone will NOT do this it only commands the pet to STOP attacking).
3. It's useful for avoiding pet aggro and early ill-timed engagement during pulling, mob AE and spell casting, etc.

Suspend Minion (assuming level 2) -
1. Pet can be stored indefinitely with all buffs and equipment intact as long as you don't zone, die or log.
2. If any mob is aggro'd on the pet you cannot suspend it.
3. Amazingly useful for moving between camps where invis is required.

Personally I have both on my necro (shaman only has /pet hold [still level 60]) and for me they are both a must-have skill. Pet suspend is wonderful for moving between camps or simply having a buffed and armed backup pet for those "oh crap" moments.

Pet Hold is the best thing next to sliced bread. Pulling casters, using a pet to pull around corners (a shaman pet can single pull anything with /pet attack and /pet hold plus /pet back off that a level 58 could single with a range weapon/spell).

If I had to recommend which skill to go for first I would say /pet hold then get the suspend skills when you have the spare AAs, but pet hold is defiantly a priority.
