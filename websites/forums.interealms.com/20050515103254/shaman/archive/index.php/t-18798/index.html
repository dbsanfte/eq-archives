I really hope you don't believe that Turgurs and Forlorn deeds, or Tepid Deeds have some kind of seperate resist check. They are all MR and have no resist modifier, it's all just a matter of RNG.
As far as slowing undebuffed mobs, we will land ours faster, but when you add in debuffs and multiple mobs, an enchanter has us beat...that is to say, an enchanter who has similar hps and AA's. Also, enchanters have SCS which gives them a 20% reduction in agro and Innoruuks Sarcasm adds an additional agro reduction to their tash line. Their slow is a 5% difference, and while some may think that really matters outside of PoP and in LDON groups, it doesn't matter at all on mitigating mobs.

Takes a Shaman 12 seconds to cast two slows, takes an Enchanter 14.25.
This is when neither cast a debuff, now slap on malos/tash to both and you get: Shaman 25.5 seconds, Enchanter 21.5 seconds.

The only reason people pick shamans over enchanters is because shamans can heal (and are better dps if Charm is not used), and the misconception that shamans are always better at slowing. Most shamans concentrate on hps and mitigate damage better (on trash mobs), so yes, your average shaman will be able to be beat up more than your average enchanter, but as better gear comes along and tougher mobs encountered that gap becomes nil. And it doesn't matter what gear you have when you don't even get hit to begin with (LR).

The best thing in LDON to do, if you are forced to group with an enchanter: have them pull with tash.
