hotelseven
Hello all...just thought Id take a few moments and express how, in my opinion, the bazaar has allowed me to fully enjoy EQ.

I first started playing EQ about 3 years ago. I made a Barbarian Warrior. It was horrible. I got to level 29 and quit because the downtime was far too long and the fights were far too close for my comfort.

I then made a Wood Elf Ranger whom I enjoyed playing until level 30. Then he began dying when fighting low blues! Also, the downtime was very long.

Since I enjoy soloing I decided to then try a Necro. I got him to level 14 and quit him because of the same reasons. Long downtime and inconsistant fighting (sometimes could easilly kill a high blue, then would get smoked by a low blue).

I quit EQ for those reasons for about a year. After realizing that there were no other good MP games out there I decided to give EQ one more shot. I figured, I like to mostly solo and the things that detered me from the game before were: dowtime and dying. So I made a shaman, figuring I could solve these problems with SOW, Canni, etc....

Well I am at level 32 now and am absolutely loving my Shammy. The reasons for this, though, I believe are due to the development of the bazaar. I have equipment now that should last me through my 50's. This was unheard of before the bazaar. Items like my Ceremonial Iksar Chestplate, or my Soulstripper (and every other piece of equipment I have with HP, Mana, Wis, AC, etc) have made dying much less frequent, and have drastically cut down my downtime. I can consistently whipe the floor with high blues, usually without losing much more than 25% health.

Not to say that getting this equipment has come easy. I have spent litterally hours buying and selling at the bazaar. I started off buying spiderling silks and making them into silk threads, then spider silks into silk swatches, etc until I could buy expensive items. I have put over 72 days into this character (most of which have been overnights left on the bazaar hoping to sell my goods).

The bazaar also makes for a nice change when you get sick of hunting stuff. Its amazing how addictive it is when you buy a bunch of stuff at low prices and try to sell them for a bit of a profit. I find myself always coming back to the computer to see if Ive sold anything. Imagine if I invested as much time in the stock market..haha.

Anyways, just thought Id give my 2 cents. Thanks for listening.

Indeed. The bazaar can be a great tool. I wouldn't say that I overly enjoy the financial aspects of EQ but I have learned to leverage them to accumulate a lot of easy plats. And I too have to check my trader regularly to see if I've made any plats. <sigh> I'm an addict.

I'm in my 2nd EQ life having 1st played from release through just before Kunark and I started over from scratch having given away my first account thinking I'd never be back. The EQ economy has come a long way. I doubt my original 50th DE wizard accumulated a total of 10k in the roughly 1.5 years I played him - and he was in an uber guild of that era. In this, my 2nd EQ life, my shaman can easily produce and spend 10k in a week.

I've made a small noobie fortune mostly selling easily acquired or fabricated tradeskill items and the occasional greed roll LDoN drop that comes my way. Enough so that I feel like I'm in very decent gear, have bought every spell (or had em research combined) I've wanted so far, and have even blown a couple thousand plats on gear for my alts.

I haven't made that much doing speculation (buying low and selling high) but if I see something I know sells easily going for cheap while I'm in the bazaar I'll buy it and sell it...but generally I'm not that patient and sell my goods at a price to keep them moving.

At some point I hope to be making more plats via normal xping and adventureing but for now I still make considerably more on side ventures.

I try to help people that are new to the game and struggling to buy gear and spells out with a little knowledge - I inform them of the value of simple noobie drops like bone chips, spiderling silks, and pelts. Few true noobies and many returning EQers have no any idea of the value of these items and the easy money to be had.

Also, whenever someone tries to beg from me I say 'no' and tell them to go farm some silks. You can easily tell the scammers from the real noobies based on the responses you get when you tell them that.
