As for my AA, I would like to see a form of rez for shaman (hear me out, hear me out). This is based on a battle rez implemented in City of Heroes. Here's how it would work.

Shaman casts "Spirit Resurrection" on a corpse. The player gets a yes/no confirmation box as usual. Player clicks yes and is summoned to the corpse as usual.

No XP is returned by the spirit rez, but the corpse's rezzability remains intact, i.e. the player can still receive a cleric rez at some point and get XP back. This is a key difference. Spirit rez is not a true rez, the player must still get a real rez.

Player is rezzed at some percentage of full health and mana (have to do some thinking on the mana issue). The percentage could be the differentiator between the various spells in the line, if they choose to add lower-level versions of this.

The player has a different rez effect than normal, called "Spirit Rot". Effect is a splurt-type DOT doing n points the first tick, 2n the second tick, etc. (actual numbers to be determined by tuning and designed to allow most characters to live about 5-8 minutes). ALTERNATIVE: Spirit Rot does no damage until a set amount of time, and then it starts taking 10% of your health per tick like drowning does.

This would serve a couple of great purposes:

1. Battle rezzing - shaman could rez fallen comrades into battle when clerics/pallies are too busy. Gives us something to do on raids besides malo/turgur's and watch TV. Could even be useful in groups if the rezzee comes back with enough mana to be useful.

2. Rez the rezzer - if the cleric is the one who dies, let the shaman spirit rez them, then let them rez themselves. Saves an annoying CR on the part of the cleric.
