Fantasy RPGs are based on fantasy novels and movies, and both of those are based on GROUPs. When EQ first came out it was this way. Raids were epic events, where you killed DRAGONs (this used to actually be a big deal). But as more expansions came out, and SoE ran out of creative ways to make content for the end-game, so instead they simply made mobs with more and more hp and damage. Eventually even that wasn't enough, and they had to actually create interesting raid events. However, what's been lost is the idea that everquest is a game about groups.

LDoN and OoW have helped bring that idea back. With LDoN I can spend a lot more time than any raider just get the points to buy a single peice of inferior equipment. Likewise with OoW, I can group in a zone for many hours before I win the roll on a single spell, let alone a peice of equipment with less than half the HP of the most uber equivilant. If anything is wrong with this, it's that the rewards are too low, not too high.

To answer you question kevagron, there are three ways to quote that I know of:

1) When you hit the "reply" button that's in the lower right hand corner of every post, you will get the usual response form, except it will have the contents of the message you clicked on surrounded by the code to get nifty formating and "Originally Posted by Loramin."

2) If you want to quote multiple people, or make up quotes, click the "post reply" that's at the top and bottom of each thread page. This will give you an empty response form. There is a toolbar above the empty text box, and on this toolbar there is an icon like that of word ballon in a comic strip. It's between a little earth icon and a number sign icon. Click on it and a window will pop-up asking you to type in the your quote. Copy paste whatever you are quoting here, click ok, and it will insert the quote with the appropriate code.

3) You can also just do the quote thing manually. Type in your quote, add quote, in brackets, in front of it, then add /quote, in brackets, after it. If you want to attribute the quote to someone, add ='their name' inside the brackets after quote (but not /quote). So, if you did: (quote='Bob')Bob is cool(/quote) with brackets instead of parentheses, you would get:
Bob is cool
