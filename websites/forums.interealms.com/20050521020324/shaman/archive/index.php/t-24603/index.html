I haven't played EQ2 beta at all, nor do I intend to. But I can repeat several things posted from my guild's message board about it: YMMV:

We took a "poll" asking how many were planning to at least try out EQ2 once it came out. Only about 10% of guild claimed they intended to. I suspect the number will actually be higher, but I also suspect that most will trickle back, just as they have other times, when DaoC and SWG and WoW beta and other games came out.

But some of the comments from several on there who have played the Beta extensively...

--The game appears to be clearly designed for casual playing. It is NOT a raiding game, and doesn't purport to be, so if raiding is your thing, you won't be attracted to it after the newness wears off (raid max size is 24, btw).
--The game is very pretty.
--Leveling seems very slow, even at low levels.
--Even though Sony claims you can solo at any point in your progression, people are finding that past lvl 15 or so, you can't. You have to group to feel like you are gaining much.
--Classes seem even more interchangable than in EQ1.
--Gear is apparently all "level-limited", meaning that it has a "recommended" level, and if a character below that level wears it, they don't get full effect. Thus trying to fix the twinking problem EQ1 has.
--Gear also wears out, though I gather it can be repaired to full or nearly full usefulness again by a tradesmith. Nonetheless, there is some fear that this will lead to even more farming than in EQ1.

As I said, these are not my comments, I'm just repeating what I've been told. YMMV--take it all with a grain of salt.

One timesink in a lifetime is enough for me, thanks :)

XeroOmega
Well, I made it into beta, and I've been playing it alot over the past few weeks...

the only thing you have to remember is that this is not EQ1.

while it holds many similarities, it is a different game altogether, its far more beautiful than EQ and the zones are absolutely huge (takes ~30 mins to run around the edge of antonica with SoW)...and on my average system it runs great (2.0 ghz celeron, 1.5GB Ram, 128 MB video card, on a DSL line)...there are a number of bugs that need to be worked out, but nothing completely outrageous that I have seen..

I agree that it is geared for the casual gamer, I've seen some light raid content, mostly against small dragon-type creatures in the commonlands, battles are faster paced than EQ and I haven't had any problem soloing past 15...

the game play is fun, and more involved than EQ1 is currently...

I think some of the playerbase will come back to EQ, some will play between both games (as I intend to), and some will stay with EQ2, it really comes down to what you personally like to play...I feel they have made a strong effort to eliminate some of the things that many of the people hated in EQ, namely class dependancy, overcrowding of zones and KSing (honestly put, I'd like to see some of the things in EQ2 patched into EQ1)

I enjoy my shaman on EQ immensely. I'm enjoying playing my Mystic on EQ2 as well, its alot different, (don't go expecting to be a "slower", it really doesn't make too much of a difference in EQ2) the shaman defining ability is now a "Ward", like the enchanter rune of EQ1, that absorbs 100% melee damage and heals the character what damage isn't taken by the ward when time expires on it..

priest archetypes do all have the same spellset...until they stop being a priest and choose a class (shaman in my case), and then a subclass (mystic)...

there is alot that I like, and some things I haven't quite figured out if I like or don't like yet, I can't say that I've found any hard negative yet, but at this point, everyone is a newb, and everyone has things to figure out...

my suggestion (as it usually is) is buy the cheapest version and "get your own opinion"...I read that it should come with a free 30-day subscription...which is just about enough time to play past the first 20 levels as a casual gamer and at level 20 you are the same level as the lowest raid targets I've seen....if you like it, great, you have a new time-sink, if not, you're out about the price of a dinner at a nice restaurant...

just my 2 cents on the topic.
