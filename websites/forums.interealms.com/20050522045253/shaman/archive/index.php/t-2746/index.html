Oldshaman
Oh one of my favorite subjects.

I've been working third shift (11:00 PM to 7:00 AM) for over 20 years and here are some tips on how to adapt and survive as a vampire.

Be consistant-I go to bed within a few minutes of 11:00 AM and get up within a few minutes of 5:00 PM everyday I work and even on days off I come close to this. During vacations this slips and I end up closer to normal nighttime sleep but force myself back to this schedule a few days before going back to work. Remember that 2:00 PM in the afternoon is the middle of the night for you so treat it like that and tell your friends and neighbors that this is when you sleep, some things like lawnmowers and daytime sounds can't be avoided but at least your buddies won't be calling in the middle of the night to borrow your toilet plunger or to ask you to play a game of tennis. The hours may vary but still be consistant.

Caffeine- Avoid it. I'm a big coffee drinker so I violate this rule but I still don't use coffee to stay awake after work. If you are drinking caffeinated beverages to stay awake after work, that's the time when you should be sleeping.

Sunlight-can't be avoided but should be kept to a minimum after work and before sleeping. One of the things night workers notice is that no matter how sleepy they may be at 4:00 AM once that sun starts coming up they get less sleepy. This is your body making awakening chemicals.

Darkness-Good, keep your house dark and sleep in the dark. You still can't fool your body into thinking it's night, there are too many other factors involved but you can at least help it not get anymore awake by keeping your house just like it was getting dark in the evening. This means that electric lights are ok, it's sunlight that is the problem. When I first started trying to sleep in the day I covered my bedroom windows with black opaque contact paper and then dark heavy curtains. Even one tiny ray of light was like a laser drilling into the room. Now I don't need it that dark but I still use heavy dark velvet curtains in the bedroom and every other room has heavy curtains or blinds pulled closed until I go to bed. Yeah just like in a funeral parlor.

Noise-Noise masks noise, old law of acoustics. Get something in the bedroom that makes a constant noise to lull you to sleep and to mask outside noises. The two best sources of this are the plain old box floor fan and a room air conditioner. Turn the fan to medium or high and you'll block most of the impulse noise that wakes you up and even mellow out lawnmowers if you hear them at all. Window air conditioners are even better, I had a neighbor that used a chainsaw in the day and a window unit was able to block that. You don't have even have to have the cooling on, usually the fans are enough. The key is loud white noise, any pings or pops of the fan blades will stand out and keep you awake. I've found the white noise or waterfall sound generators that they sell don't work as well, they never get loud enough.

Face up to the fact that you'll never get a "good nights sleep" in the daytime. You'll always be a bit tired, a bit edgy and always ready to plop into bed when it's time to go to work. This is just biology at work. There is still nothing like sleeping at night when your body wants you to.
