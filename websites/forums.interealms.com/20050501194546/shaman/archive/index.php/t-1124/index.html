BlargX
PR/DR don't seem to matter, and I don't think MR does either. I can get all of them pretty high, and still get zapped the same.

I have probably killed this guy well over 30 times by now(and I mean WELL over), getting his spell for my shaman, my druid, and some of my guildies. Of the first six successful kills, he didn't drop the spell once. This is the only mob in the game that drops "remove curse." (Don't confuse this spell with "remove greater curse.") He's killed me twice, when I was first starting out, because of the interrupts, and because if he summons you and starts interrupting you with both spells AND melee, shamans cast much too slowly to ever get off a spell unless they're very lucky. Even if he doesn't summon you, his AOE's will interrupt you a lot. Of course, if you've left boglings up to help interrupt you and/or kill too, you're really in trouble. I never did, but don't.

So eventually here's what I've started doing, with minor variations. I'm level 60 with full ToV dragon quest armor, torpor and all important spells, but my hitpoints aren't too high -- I can't canni 5 without killing myself unless I have buffs on.

Summon pet and uber buff it, including focus, our AC buff, regrowth, pet haste. Buff myself fully, with regrowth too, but forget the haste. Summon and park pet in the room before the shroom room. Kill all boglings there. Single pull boglings in the shroom room with JBB blasts and run them outside to pet; JBB and pet them down, use epic if you like.

Don't take a long time dotting them, because this mob gets KS'd all the time and is on a precise timer that many people pay attention to. If he's up, you need to kill him quick before someone comes. And ANYONE can kill this thing faster than a shaman, so no fooling around. If possible, leave the boglings at the exit to the shroom area standing so people won't run by, see the boglings missing, and wonder if they have a good KS opportunity.

Anyway, by the time you've gotten all your mana back from buffing yourself and the dog and killing all the boglings, it will be time to reapply regrowth on yourself and dog, and pet haste on dog, just to be safe.

Important: do not fight in the shroom room or even spend much time close to it until you are ready for saprophyte killing. The shroom occasionally tosses out his AOE spells just for fun. If one hits you or the dog, the battle begins -- maybe with all the boglings still chipping in, maybe with you low mana or you or the dog low health, or both.

Anyway, once outer and inner boglings are dead and buffs/health are full, have pet follow you into shroom room, before boglings start repopping. Have him guard there.

Slowing alone has gotten me summoned. Once summoned, don't rely on gating to work. I died twice that way, too many interrupts. If you are going to try a slow, malo first. I have had the shroom resist multiple slow attempts without malo, even though he's a greenie, just like he summons even though he's a greenie to my 60. Malo will not get you summoned, but of course will start the shroom AOE'ing you and the dog, which will start killing both of you quickly. Slowing is not the most important thing and not always the most practical.

Killing shroom, the basic thing is to let pet do most of the work. The pet must not die. You either, of course.

Interrupts foil most attempts to heal either you or the dog. Luckily, even torpored, the dog can do decent damage. So sometimes I torpor him even before the fight begins, and torpor myself, too, before the fight begins. It is very easy to not have a chance to torpor yourself in the fight, so I will usually "front-load" the battle with a torpor on either myself or the dog or both.

Anyway, after that, I order the pet to attack and hit the shroom with malo. You need that to land your spells reliably; take no chances. At that point, the dog starts dying very quickly, even buffed with regrowth, so if I haven't started him off with torpor before the fight even began, I torpor him immediately. He can kill the shroom all by himself given enough time, so landing a damage spell immediately is not important.

As to spell damage, I have tried bane of nife several times, but it takes too long to work. You want this guy dead FAST. It is also very high aggro, and has at least some chance of getting you summoned. Forget poxx, and envenomed bolt is too weak. A 600 point DD, like some people here recommend, is also not very good. They take a while to cast but do little damage. Epic takes MUCH too long to cast to rely upon it, and MUCH much too long to work. I find I only have time for maybe two to four spells in the battle, though I will be interrupted many times before they land -- a damage spell or possibly two if I'm lucky, and a torpor spell or possibly two if I'm lucky. If I get three spells off, I count myself very lucky.

What I use is torrent of poison: three times up to 540 per hit if it lands. Usually I get at least 1k of damage off the spell. Meanwhile, doggy is doing a lot of the work.

That's usually all I need to do as far as offense goes, and it never gets me summoned. Which is important, since I'm using a poison rain that would hit me, too, if I did get summoned.

After a torrent of poison, if I don't have it on me, I cast torpor on myself whether I need it or not. You don't want to have to hope you don't get interrupts when you NEED it; cast it before you need it if it isn't already on you, and if you get interrupted, just keep it up. Check pet for his health constantly; if he dies, you will very likely lose and/or die yourself. If there's not enough shroom health left to bother with another torrent of poison, see if you're lucky enough to get off a JBB round.

Gloves(plus 5 magic/fire/cold I think, nice resist gloves for druids and maybe monks and BL) and earring are dirt common, spell can not drop for MANY shrooms in a row. Don't ask for any help with the shroom from strangers, or you might just get KS'd. Melees can kill this thing fast, as can casters, especially if there's more than one of them, and they can often do it whether the boglings are dead or not. We kill him slow, and boglings provide too many interrupts and will kill your pet, who is always on the brink of death against this mob anyway -- as are you, sometimes.

Anyway, this works for me every time, never gets me summoned, and never even gets my pet killed.
