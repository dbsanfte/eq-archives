Detton
Shaman = great

Necromancer = squishy

I like to think one of my defining abilities is the ability to not panic if a spell gets resisted 4-5 times in a row (at least where I am now. But then again, higher up, i'll have a pet to help, and better slows)

I'm not saying necros are bad. Well, I did, but heres my second-hand scoop. (I get all my knowledge from reading message boards and magazine ads):

Before a shaman gets the Canabalize AA, a necromancer will make a more efficient soloer, with a vastly superior mana regen rate. They have all the tools we do for soloing, for the most part, and suitable substitues for the things they do not have (like slows, though they can slow undead, I believe)

Once a shaman can nab that AA, it evens up the race quite a lot because of the massive heal-over-time spells we have. Sure, we lose a lot of hp, but gain it back pretty quickly.

A necromancer at the high levels will (probably) have a higher average mana pool over the course of his hunting then a Shaman, giving him a slight safety margin there.

Thats my take. I may not be right, but hopefully if I am wrong, I am wildly wrong. :)

Bigmeeno
Shaman can solo. We've got all the tools to do it with. One of the things that really helps is working to get your gear upgraded so that you have as many HP as you possibly can. Being able to slow almost anything we would ever want to solo helps tons. Our DoT spells are among the best in the game. Our pet helps out with the dmg.

At higher levels, get as much FT as you can. I know its not easy, but you can get it over time. Make sure you have all of the spells for your level. Not all shaman think FT is all that valuable for them, but any extra mana regen is a good thing. My shaman is hasnt been my main for almost 2 years now, but I have kept trying to get better items for him, and he can self buff to over 4k HP and has FT12 now. He also has a 4k mana pool. And thats with not raiding. So it can be done.

After you reach 65, if you solo much, get Virulent Paralysis. Even level 1 of it is invaluable. Since we can make mana, the size of our mana pool isnt a huge factor, but having enough HP to be able to Canabilize during a fight offers us a way to get lots of mana back fast.

Uggok
We are decent at it, and I think it is a lot of fun sometimes.

Bards will thouroughly crush you at soloing for xp. Think Lennox Lewis vs your grandma. As for necros, the size of your blue bar isn't everything that matters, it is what you can do with it. They have too many tools and too much damage. Outdoors that means more dps (aggro kiting is better than any method we have available for xp soloing) and more xp over time. Indoors it means more dps and flexibility. FD, insta-invis, insta-itu, harmshield, REZ, summon corpse, snare, etc allow them to get places and do things that we simply cannot. The new lifetaps narrow the tanking advantage anyway.

The big shift in soloing power came when they stopped making all the good solo targets summon.

We remain the best class at soloing one big, hardhitting-but-not-superhard hitting, unsnareable, unrootable, slowable thing outdoors (or indoors and easy to get to). There are still some things we can solo that others probably cannot (zelnithiwhatever). But the list of things that necros can do that we cannot do is undoubtedly longer.

Charmers are a whole 'nother ballgame.
