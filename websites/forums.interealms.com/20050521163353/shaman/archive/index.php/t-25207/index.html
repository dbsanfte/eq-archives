Ideally you would want:

Rez (Cleric, Paladin, possibly Necromancer)
Healing (Cleric, Druid, Shaman)
Tanking (Paladin, Shadowknight, Warrior, Ranger, Beastlord)
Haste/Slow (Shaman, Enchanter, Bard, Beastlord)
Mana Regen (Enchanter, Bard, Beastlord)
Snare (Ranger, Druid, Shadowknight, Bard)
Stun (Paladin, Cleric, Enchanter)
Crowd Control (Enchanter, Bard)
Single Pulling (Bard, Monk, Shadowknight, Enchanter, Cleric)

And of course as much DPS as you can squeeze in. Some decent combinations are:

Shaman/Warrior/Necromancer (only missing stun. nice DPS)
Druid/Enchanter/Paladin (Next to no abilities overlap. lowish DPS without charm)
Cleric/Shadowknight/Beastlord (low on mana regen, slow and haste)

And so on . . . basically select a healer or tank, select the other to overlap the first in as few areas as possible, select the third to bring as many abilities as possible from those you are missing.

Now if you are content with limited content you can get almost any group with a shaman in it to work (I only ever seem to have trouble fitting in wizards in my odd groups).
