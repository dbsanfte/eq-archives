Sticking to one shaman's perspective (lvl 64 casual bear), it's this:

A warrior with Very Nice Gear can usually keep aggro well enough that I can throw on a dot, possibly a rain.

Any joe-average paladin or SK can keep aggro enough that I honestly can't pull the mobs off him if I try. This about doubles my damage output per mob these days, since with ANY form of outside mana regen in the group (or god forbid a chanter AND bard), I can't spend my mana fast enough.

This means that, in my experience, if I have a warrior tanking, I spend a whole LOT of time with full mana, with nothing better to do with it than haste the cleric, since doing any more damage to the mob will result in my tanking. This can be fun, but ultimately REALLY pisses off the warrior. Which I can understand. Poor guy's class is broken...

With a knight, the mobs go down about twice as fast. I'm double or triple dotting, the casters are chain nuking at 90% or 80% mana until the end. Everyone is throwing out damage like there's no tomorrow. Because they can.

I know some VERY good warriors. And they simply can't compete. Top end gear helps, but they honestly don't have the tools available to their class. I don't care how much DPS a warrior can output vs. a knight. That's not why I look for a tank. I don't really honestly much care how many HPs or AC they have - when was the last time your cleric ran out of mana in PoV? It's simply taunt, and nothing else - which knights do better, but which is THE ONLY THING that warriors are supposed to have going for them. There used to be balancing factors to make warriors suck less. Those aren't in place any more. No more exp penalty for knights, no more superior warrior-only gear

And that is the problem right there. The balance used to be threefold. Knights had utility spells to round them out but in exchange they had to deal with having lesser tanking abilities and they couldn't dual wield and often used a shield so they had lesser damage output, they also had the xp penalty as well. The "vision" may not have been 20/20 but it at least had a certain logic behind it

Today, the knight classes have easy access to 1 handers with ratios comparable to 2 hander ratios that let them both use a shield AND do as much damage as a dual wielding warrior. There's so many pally/sk specific 2 hand weapons with ratios as good as any warrior or even Monk weapon that you can no longer really say they do less damage than any other class... Cause they do. Then you add in pets/stuns/drain lifes/heals.... Warriors can't compete.

The Xp penalty has been removed, the thinking being that at high levels All classes are equivalent... HAH !

As for Tanking ability, you get maybe a 200 hp difference at high levels between warr and knight classes, Knights had their dodge and other similar tanking abilities raised a while back... The difference with warriors gets even slimmer. And Taunt is a joke compared to the knight's aggro spells.

The only thing that makes a warrior more desireable than a knight on raids is the defensive disc and I'd bet good money a lot of warriors would be happy to sacrifice it for some better thought out class defining abilities. There's no doubt in my mind that warriors need help right now.

ps: And on a side note, I'd say Monks aren't far behind in needing a fix. Their role as puller took a serious hit when VI fixed the Lull line of spells. They've pretty much become a basic damage class but they're competing with Mages, rogues and rangers for group slots. Not to mention itemisation (particularly weapons) for the middle class monk has serious gaps in it compared to other classes.

As with everyone, what worked for the warriors made them happy, and if it made someone else unhappy, who cares. Unfortunately, things havent been balanced, they have simply pushed the pendulum the other direction.
Someone pointed to the old notion of a 20% xp penalty to hybrids as being fair, actually it was just nonsensical. There is already a 10% xp bonus attached to warrior to help offset the fact that they do have limited abilities. The idea was a warrior did few things, but did them very well. At that time, a knight suffered a severe dps disadvantage opposed to a warrior, he had a substantial hp disadvantage, and difference in gear. Not always a large difference, depends on exactly what expansion we're using for a reference.
Along comes pop, scourge of all classes (except charmers). They overbalanced the power of slow by making mobs hit hard and fast. But they overdid it. The mobs hit SO hard and fast, that it suddenly became imperative that slow land AS SOON AS POSSIBLE. Suddenly tried and true methods of aggro control learned through their eq careers were tossed out the windows. Slowers had to stick a slow quicker, to avoid killing the tank. Unfortunately, this often meant a dead slower instead. In the past, a shaman could take aggro for a while, in anything less then a raid zone. So the knights, mostly paladins at first, stepped forward to tell us a little secret, namely that their spells are aggro. They had been saying all along, we just didnt listen. Now with charm nerfed, they feel like they should be able to find groups quickly, but people have gone on to other techniques, making them no longer required (but still nice to have).

At this point in time, a knight with the right weapons and aa's can outdamage a dual wielding warrior. This is due to the fact that a preposterously large number of melee hits, hit for minimum. Minimum is always 1+damage bonus. For those of you who havent played classes with a db, a slow 2s weapon gets a substantial bonus at high levels. A non-tanking warrior can again pull ahead by using 2h weapons. But a tanking warrior is almost locked into dual wield to maintain aggro, reducing dps. Bear in mind, though, that the knight who is tanking is also suffering from a severe drop in dps. Anytime your casting, your not swinging. To be honest, there is little reason for a knight to swing at all. They dont add substantially to the dps of a group, and can maintain aggro with spells.

The reason warriors have dropped in xp group value is not really because of gear or knight upgrades or whatever. It is because PoP mobs are build on an entirely different theory than mobs in prior expansions.

The old model of xp mob design: mobs hit like wussies. The main way that they get harder is to add more and more hit points and mitigation.

The PoP model of xp mob design: mobs hit like pianos dropped from skyscrapers. They have crap mitigation and relatively few hit points. The main way they get harder is to make them hit even harder.

A lot of things were balanced for the old model but became messed up in one way or another under the new model. The archetypical example of this is charm. But other balances shifted as well, like the warrior vs knight question. Knights always had much better aggro control. But who gives a rat's ass about insta-aggro control when you are fighting mobs that take 5 rounds to kill a naked enchanter. When you are fighting mobs that will one or two round any caster or slower, then the value of insta-aggro control goes way, way up.

Now, the interesting question is how to fix this, how to help the xp group warrior? They aren't going to go back to the old model of mob design (thank God). They also probably just can't give warriors across-the-board aggro increases either.

The first reason for that is the raiding warrior. A lot of encounters would be utterly trivialized if warriors had significantly more aggro generation than they do now. The real culprit here is defensive/evasive. Adding those skills was probably the worst thing they have ever done to EQ (the only close thing is not nerfing the crap out of complete heal when Kunark or at least Velious came out). Any real rebalancing of warriors and knights would have to begin with the removal of those disciplines, but so many encounters are based around them that I don't see SoE ever putting in the work to do it.

The second reason is the xp group knight. If knights don't have significantly better aggro generation, they will be back to their old status, which was worse than warriors are now.

The common answer is to significantly increase the aggro generation of warriors but let the knights still have a significant aggro advantage over warriors. Leaving aside the point that this does nothing to address the raid problem, it is just an example of typical fuzzy thinking you see on MMOG message boards when people discuss balance, buffs and nerfs. The knight advantage is either significant (i.e. it saves casters) or it isn't. It is an essentially binary outcome. If it is, you have changed nothing. If it isn't, you send knights back to Kunark. The lets buff everybody and everything will be warm and fuzzy response is popular, but it probably won't work.
