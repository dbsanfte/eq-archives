I play both an enchanter (64) and shaman (65). Since both of my characters are BoT flagged, and I spend more or less all of my time there, I have had some opportunity to consider this exact issue over the course of the last few days.

My feeling, which I will of course point out is not based on anything but my subjective experience, is that there is approximately no difference in terms of straight-up resists.

There most certainly IS a difference in WHEN slow, lands, though, and that difference is very likely the source of the misperceptions. As an enchanter, it takes approximately twice as long to cast the spell, and there is also the delay that has to be worked in prior to casting Howl Of Tashan (and then Boggle, Boggle, Boggle if the delay was not long enough). When you are slowing as an enchanter, you are taking your life into your hands, so you are obviously very careful in how you go about it. Since this is the case, enchanters tend to be much slower at landing slows. Why is that significant? Probably because during that time the shaman or magician in your group is also casting malo/mala or even malosini/malosinia. These extra debuffs stack, so with both on the enchanter (who is probably by that point getting around to casting slow) has a much better chance to land the slow than the shaman who is most likely either casting cold (with no debuffs) or casting malo/slow one right after the other. (If you find an enchanter that tashes at the same time you malo, then you have either found the uberest enchanter ever or someone with a deathwish.)
