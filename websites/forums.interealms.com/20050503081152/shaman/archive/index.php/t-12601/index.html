Shaman + monk seems a viable option again, thankfully. Ranger is good, and brings a much-wanted snare to the mix.

If you're a Troll shaman, you don't need snare if you quest for the necklaces (one of which has clinging darkness or the like) so you can go for added offensive ability (SK) or added defensive and healing ability (Paladin). Either takes and keeps agro well, so you can lay on more damage.

Until about lev 20 I'd suggest you think of yourself as a tank with an extra arsenal of spells, and twink like that. It's not until 20s that our melee begins to lag a bit, but it's still a viable damage option for us through the early 40s.

I've heard the racial xp penalties have been lessened quite a bit -- any troll / iksar coming up through the ranks now able to confirm that? Sounds too good to be true.

Finally, gear. As somebody who will need to withstand a few blows (and if slow doesn't stick, a few more) you'll find most of the shaman here say go for AC til you get to 1,000, HP second, Wis third.

I had a wisdom somewhere around 160s til I was lev 58 -- but then PoP came out and +wis items began having decent secondary stats, too.

Since our wisdom determines the beginning size of our mana pool, it's good to have stuff that can bump it up for doing things like raid buffs, running around town randomly focusing folks you know, and the like. It just means we can go longer before having to cannibalize.

Once we're in battle, however, AC arguably means we'll be hit less, but HP means we'll both survive longer and have a larger capacity to refill our mana pool and get off that much-needed heal. Get into the habit of keeping yourself as fom as possible and let regen work for you (as a necro, this should be 2nd nature by now!). Even with a lower mana pool, if you're routinely fom or near it you can do things that surprise the heck out of the folks who are stuck medding for mana.

I ended up balancing the stats, and seem to be doing OK. Not uber, but
