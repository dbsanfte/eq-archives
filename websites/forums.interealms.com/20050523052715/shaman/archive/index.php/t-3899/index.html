stormrazor
Seems to me like you've been a group Shaman up to this point. (nothing wrong with that except that your soloing skills really suffer as well as some vital stats). Your first order of business is to increase your defense skill, that is free AC. You also want to max your weapon skills (very vital for effective soloing). Unless you use a Root/Rot method (effective soloing technique - but makes me feel too much like a druid so I don't do it much).

I soloed at 24 in LOIO, quite easily in fact.
The spells I had memorized were (in order)
Healing
Root
Walking Sleep
Envenomed Breath
Affliction
Regeneration
Cannibalize
(I would then swap out the last slot for SoW or Gate)

I would find a Mob and pull with Walking Sleep if resisted cast again, it is important to get the mob slowed, DoT it with Tainted (sometimes I cast Affliction too depending on the mob) and commence to poke the crap out of it with my spear. The mob hitting you will cause your defense skill to raise...that's free AC.
If your health gets a little low during the battle...root and back up, heal yourself. Usually I would only have to use Envenomed Breath as my only DoT, a slowed mob is much easier to handle, use Walking Sleep (you have it for a reason). Slow will become your most vital spell...use it, learn to love it !
I hunted at 2 spots in LOIO. One was at the pillars, I would find a spot on the shore and pull to it. Very safe way to pull, I have never (I repeat NEVER) died at that spot. I've only died in LOIO when I grouped inside the Sarnak fort. Second spot I found was outside the Sarnak fort, you can pull wandering Tigers, Gobbys, Sarnaks, Skeletons...I've never died there either.
Another way to fight solo is use the Root/Rot technique. This is how I used Root/Rot......I pulled mob with Affliction, then root it. Cast Envenmoed Breath, re-root if necessary then cannibalize. When EB fades cast it again, re-root when needed. Watch the mob rot to death (that's the fun part). This is a very effective technique to use on mobs with alot of HP's. But your battle skills will suffer as you won't be getting hit, so your defense skill will not raise. Neither will your weapon skills. So either choice is viable for a Shaman, the choice is yours. Find a basic technique and apply your own style to it. But get your Defense skill maxed it will help you to no end.

Kriel Darkbane
If you grouped in Paludal from 15-24 I'd assume your defense skill is quite lacking at present. It maxes at 120 at L 24, and if you aren't at LEAST over 100 yet you need to work hard on raising it. The advice given by others already is very good, but it may not work terribly well until your defense skill is caught up (neither will mine). Nevertheless, keep at it, tank the mobs down, and get your defense skill caught up. It only gets harder to raise it later on.

One other suggestion: put Frost Strike in your lineup. I'd use Stormrazor's suggested spel lineup, except swap in Frost Strike for Affliction. Frost Strike is not much less mana-efficient than Affliction, and does all the damage in one shot. It's not good for root-rotting, obviously, but you want to tank to raise defense anyway. If your defense is really low, you'll need to nuke the mobs down while you tank them. You'll have a lot of downtime this way, but you'll be making positive exp and your defense skill will go up if you keep at it.

Try to find low blues to work defense with. Start with slow, then Envenomed breath, then melee. Nuke and heal as needed. Root if you need to back off to heal up. Time your SoW and keep it up at all times, in case things go badly and you need to run. If your defense is already caught up somehow, then this should be fairly easy.

You are at a great spot levelwise to experiment with different playstyles. You can die lots and it really won't set you back much at all.

Your DoTs at 24 are really not abusive enough to take out a mob on their own so you will prolly want to throw in some nice Ice DD damage. Try keeping Flash of Light in your line up as well because it can save your azz when a root breaks, your SoW drops, and you are LOM. Your lower level slows are not impactful enough to waste a slot on while fighting outdoors and Frenzy is something I would trade out for another spell.

Given some of the gear that shammies now have access to at low level, I would guess that melee below 34 is pretty viable, but I think you would be better off learning how to keep a mob out of reach till you level up a bit.

Definitely spend some of your solo level time indoors to build skills and just switch up when you get bored. The best gaming in EQ is in dungeons and zones where you can't just run away from a threat.

Kazim
Don't know what weapon you're using, but if you're soloing and using melee, having a good weapon will help A LOT at your level, since you can still hit the mobs. I've seen centi warhammers on sale in the bazaar for 50-100 plat (more often 200-300). Get a weapon with at least that good a ratio. Possible cheap weapons:

- centi warhammer
- slime coated harpoon
- drakkel icegrinder

Don't underestimate the value of having a better weapon. If you deal out 30 percent extra damage during the fight with your weapon, it'll be just as good an improvement as slowing the mob so it does 30 percent less.
