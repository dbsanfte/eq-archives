I don't want to make this another druid vs shaman thread, but druids and shamans are NOT balanced with respect to ICH aggro.

Supposedly shaman ICH is "balanced" against the reduced damage output due to slow. Druid spells that would generate equivalent aggro (e.g., nukes) presumably were not used to balance the effect of the ICH like slow was.

So, to get the same net "effect" of the ICH in terms of damage mitigation, a shaman MUST cast slow + ICH, and often Malo as well to get the slow to stick. Druids only cast the ICH itself. So the shaman uses much more mana, while generating much more aggro. Resists, which are frequent, unbalance the aggro even more.

ICH aggro may be an issue for druids, but it is much more unbalanced for shamans. Shamans just don't complain about it as much.
