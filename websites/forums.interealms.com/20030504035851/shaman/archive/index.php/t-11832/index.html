Well use to play wizzy so root was my life saver. There are few things that are different about the roots and depending on what you are doing some are better then others.

Root (lvl 14)
Mana: 30, Cast Time: 2.00, Duration: 48 Sec

Instill (lvl 34)
Mana: 60, Cast Time: 2.50, Duration: 1.6 Min

Immobilize (lvl 51)
Mana: 80, Cast Time: 1.75, Duration: 1 Min

Paralyzing Earth (lvl 56)
Mana: 100, Cast Time: 2.50, Duration: 3 Min

Greater Immobilize (lvl 62)
Mana: 150, Cast Time: 1.75, Duration: 1 Min
Has a build in Resistance modifier of 30

Petrifying Earth (lvl 64)
Mana: 180, Cast Time: 2.50, Duration: 3 Min
Has a build in Resistance modifier of 30

The bit I know of root spells are as follows this I have learned from both reading up and personal experience. Roots type spells have a given duration they will last. When root spells are first cast the target in question gets to check its resistance against the spell. If the target fails its resist check it becomes rooted. Now for every tick or so till the duration of the spell has completed the target gets to check its resistance against the spell again at a small penalty. If at any time the target receives any type of Direct Damage from a Spell it immediately gets to check its resist verses the spell again at a bonus. This is why most roots break while you are nuking them. I believe that the resist check also has the casters level verses the targets level taken into account as well. I believe this as most higher-level target to the caster tend to break root sooner then the lower level targets. Spells that do not cause Direct Damage to a rooted target do not give the target any extra chances or bonuses to the target-breaking root. So if your casting a mix of DD type spells and non-DD type spells cast the DD spells last.

One more bit interesting fact I have discovered is that the basic Root spell is the exact same length of time as our Poison Dot spells so if you wait a sec or two after you apply your Poison Dot on a creature and then reapply your root you will get a nice warning before you root is going to brake.
