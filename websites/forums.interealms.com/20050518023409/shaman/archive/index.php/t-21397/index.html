Nope, I wasn't trolling. As the analysis by Murtak indicated, I honestly feel like most of these are simply addressing specific problems people have brought up with specific proposed changes to spells that might fix them.

For all of those who think the sum total of these changes would be overpowering, please post a scenario using some combination of these changes which shows how we would all of a sudden become godlike. That's actually the reason why I posted this. I'm hoping we can catch scenarios like that and I can adjust my suggestions so we're fixed, not turned into god mode.

To answer a few of the questions posted:

Specific changes for "new version of cripple":

-10% ATK
-10% AC
-10% damage

So, a 1500 AC, 1500 ATK, 1000 hitting mob would become 1350 AC, 1350 ATK, 900 hitter. Basically, let's have cripple make the mob 10% easier to hit and reduce its damage output by up to 20%. If that tests out to be too much, easy enough to reduce the numbers.

The spell casting curses were intended to work on any NPC cast spell. But I think they'd be nice for PVP even if they worked only on PC spells (might need a resist adjustment built in for that).

I upped the AC dramatically on Ancestral Guard because I thought there should be a reason to consider casting it. I can't remember the last time I cast it. If +100 isn't enough to get people to choose it sometimes over other buffs with which it does not stack, make it even higher. We get these "ac buffs" every release it seems like. And we have an entire line of them. So it seems like they are intended to be a "shaman thing" but are never good enough due to stacking issues with better spells, so don't get much use.

I intended for the 50% resist adjustment on DoTs to be the net result. I don't really know how agro works on dots, so couldn't really get specific on hot to achieve it. But the idea behind doubling the damage per tick and reducing the number of ticks was to have a slight increase in mana efficiency and a significant increase in usefullness in groups. Bane would be 3 ticks. Blood would be 4 ticks. Keeping both of these up for the bulk of a fight is pretty decent dps.

I think the -50 resist on rain spells would be noticeable. It feels to me like winters storm (druid rain) resists a lot less than Tears. I 2-box a shaman and a druid quite often. I think we actually could use rains without being forced to malo first if we had the resist adjustment.

I only reduced our nuke casting times a bit. Not sure why people feel this specific change would be overpowering.
