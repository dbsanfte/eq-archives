Gnoll-hide lariat is the standard answer to that question. They used to be pretty common from Infected Paw. I don't see them much any more. The stun is handy, but I wouldn't rely on it to interrupt too many casts. I use dogdog bashing and a mage air pet a lot in Sebilis and the casters still are able to get off a large number of spells and those annoying dispells on me fairly frequently.
Tarthug
Dual gnoll hide lariats on a hasted, focused, PEed, bashing pet helps a lot for me. Add in the mix a little VP (the AA) action with the knockback and those stupid shaman and wizard casters are toast.
Asta Khaan
I usually go with a mix of these:

-gnoll hide lariat: for stuns.
-staff of writhing: procs chaotic feedback (stun), which is even better than lariat since proc is very hard to resist. Even use this in pvp, where it's good on targets that have even up to 200mr. Lore, can only use one.
-serrated bone dirk: procs engulfing darkness, which snares the mobs, or at least strips their sow. Helps massively in decreasing the amounts of runners/trains you cause.
-sword of skyfire for farming greencons and factioning. Fire rain ae proc.

There's of course also the whole range of mage summoned items, but im usually too lazy to ask for them. The muzzle of mardu is nice tho as it gives your pet a bit extra attack speed.
