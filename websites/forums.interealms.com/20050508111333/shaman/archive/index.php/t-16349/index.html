Thorzion
The time limit isnt as much of a hinderance as you might think. You have 2 time limits to deal with, 30 minutes after accepting a mission to get to the dungeon and 90 minutes once you get inside to complete your mission.

The 30 minutes to get to your dungeon is WAY more than you need. Most dungeons are either in the same zone or one away from where you accept the mission. the MM and deapest Guk are the only dungeons that require some real hoofing, and then only about 10 minutes if you run really slow and have to make a stop along the way.

The 90 minutes in the dungeon is, from my experience, more than enough time. I did a run through in Quicksand on hard setting with pally, warr, cleric, me, wiz, mage and we downed the boss with 15 minutes to spare, and we werent exactly moving at breakneck speed. Our cleric and mage didnt have any 3rd party mana regen and our warrior died once. Overpulls were common and I usually ended up tanking with the meatheads.

Try it out. THEN complain.
Tyrika
I only did one guk adventure but when I did it it gave my group 60 minutes to get into it.
The timer for the actual adventure doesn't start until the first person zones into the adventure. So you usually have time to run up and buff and med to full before going in.

The time limit isn't that bad at all. Even if you don't complete your mission you still get good experience and loot for your level. There are weapons that drop regularly in the orc mines that sell for 3pp-5pp each (this is at level 45).

I did 6 missions yesterday and got about a level and a half (44 to 45 and halfway into 45) and I only suceeded at one of them.

You also do not need the pefect group to complete a mission. The main requirements to finish a mission is that every member stays and pays attention constantly just keep moving.

All in all I think the experience is at least as good here as anywhere else.( keep in mind I must have died 15 times yesterday I did get some rezes but not all and I still gained that much exp) Plus you also have the opportunity to earn some points and this is much much more exciting than the usual grind for experience.
