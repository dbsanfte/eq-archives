First how close are your two computers together? Mine are on the same desk but Ive known people who have them in seprate rooms, jump on one to throw a few heals, basicly just PL'ing.

Anyway, considering both keyboards are side by side here is what ya do.

Go pull with your monk, singles are nice because with haveing to root adds it gets downright annoying.

As soon as you get back to your pull spot turn on auto attack with your monk, switch over to the shaman. Hit pet attack, cast a slow, switch back to your monk and try and pay attention to see if you see "A mob Yawns" if you dont see this message you it didnt stick and you will probibly have to re-cast, if you got the mana cast a dot also.

Switch back to your monk and start melleing. When the mob gets to about 60% (This is just me you might be able to do this sooner/later) reach over and sit, trying not to have your shaman get agro here.

Keep melleing with your monk (Those kicks add up) ocasinoly reaching over and hitting your cana dance key, (/stand, cast canabalize, /pause whenever, /sit)

I suggest you make hotkeys for refreshing a few important buffs on your monk. Haste fading, reach over and hit whatever hotkey you have so he will stand cast and sit. Also I find its usefull to still have a group message so you know if you hit the wrong key. So if you hit the button to cast haste and see "Attemting to slow, you" you will know you screwed up and hopefully can inturupt him.

Thats about it, buffs I suggest you keep hotkeyed so you can hit it and the shaman will stand cast and sit. Regen, slow, heal, haste, SoW, and Canabalize.

An assist key is a must also. Basicly all the targeting keys your shaman will need is F1 and F2 with Assist.
