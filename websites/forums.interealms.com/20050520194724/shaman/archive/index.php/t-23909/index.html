The incoming xp doesn't necessarily need to be affected, if the amount of xp needed got the applied penalty.
Ie. race with no penalty/bonus needs 10.000xp to level.
Troll/iksar needs 12.000
Barbarian needs 10.500
Ogre needs 11.500
and so forth, this way the only loss in xp will be due to truncating the rounded numbers.
These numbers were shown back in the days by the showeq people, and it was back in the days because back then the absolute´numbers were being sent to the client, which it no longer is.
When I mention these numbers, I don't mean the actual numbers I wrote, I mean that it was shown that trolls (my primary interest) needed a larger number (absolute) than for example barbarians.

So this would result in a troll vs. a barbarian killing a mob worth 100xp each running at 90/10 xp/aa with leadership on would gain the exact same number to each part, except in xp it would result in a lower percentage gained due to the higher max for the troll.
My guess with the disctribution would be:
20xp to leadership each.
72xp to level each.
8xp to aa each.

For the troll this will lead to 0.6% level xp.
For the Barbarian this will lead to 0.69% level xp.
Again using the made of numbers of 10k5 and 12k xp above.
For aa and leadership the percentage gained will be equal since the amount needed per level is identical.
This example shows that sometimes there won't be any loss in xp by splitting your xp over aa and level, but it would be easy to pick a number that's not dividable(sp?) by the fractions chosen and then the resulting numbers will be rounded down. That will never happen if you're not splitting xp in several directions (aa and leadership).
