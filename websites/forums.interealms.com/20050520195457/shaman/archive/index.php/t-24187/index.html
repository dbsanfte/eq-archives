Well in my mind, you have to consider a few things when you look at dot agro . The first couple of things revolve around the dot itself - duration, dmg/tick, types of counters, and # of counters. The other considerations have to be in how we look at agro - when we drop a dot, we see the initial agro generated and we see the overall agro generated, which includes the initial agro component.

Damage alone cannot be the sole reason for why we generate the type of agro we do on our dots. 1) Curses out damage poisons per tick but do not generate the same type of crazy agro; 2) Although any good melee will outdamage any dot I drop before the dots duration ends, I still generate more agro; and 3) Low level poisons generate a similar agro level to higher level more damaging poisons.

Agro speculation: Agro is a combination of agro per tick and the initial agro from nukes (assuming there is one). The counters on a dot are multipliers against dmg/tick and the initial nuke, and poison counters are a larger multiplier than either curses or diseases. So given that,

Agro = initial agro + agro/tick

Agro/tick = (counters)*(dmg/tick)
Initial agro = (counters)*(initial nuke)
Counters = (multiplier)*(# of counters)
And multipliers are p.counters > c.counters > d.counters

Total Agro = [(multiplier)*(# of counters)]*[dmg/tick + initial nuke dmg]

In this way, agro isn't solely a function of damage dealt, rather it's a function of counters multiplied against damage dealt. It also explains why curses can deal more damage per tick and have a similar number of counters, but because the multiplier is different, it can generate less agro. But this is all my speculation on how it works.

The entire dot system needed an overhaul yesterday.

Nukes:
-can drop all their damage at once.
-can be partially resisted.
-absolutely no stacking issues.
-slight agro.

Dots:
-if not cast early, will not deal all of their damage, and over short fights, will not deal all of their damage no matter when cast.
-either resisted completely or lands for full.
-stacking issues and competing debuff slots.
-crazy agro problems.

Because they deal damage over time, and not all at once, dots should not only be more mana efficient, they should generate slightly less agro than nukes dealing similar damage.

-critical affliction aa should mirror fury of magic aa in cost and performance.
-dots dealing a similar amount of damage to nukes should cost less mana and generate agro in a similar way to those nukes.
-dots should be resistable on a per/tick basis.
-dot foci should be as widely available as nuke foci.
-dots should cost slightly less mana than nukes that deal similar damage.
