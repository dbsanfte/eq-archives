Thorzek
Depends on the stage of the game. There are really 2 stages: for general purposes, call them endgame and pre-endgame, they generally line up with pre-elemental vs. hard LDON/elemental+/GoD.

You'll be the BEST at a couple different things, and VERY GOOD at many others, until you approach endgame. If you are not an endgame/raiding kind of person, by all means, play a shaman, they ROCK until this phase.

Shaman will indeed allow you to solo, but be very desirable in groups as well.

Since you asked what I hate about it, here goes.

The big issue shamans have is that as you approach endgame, most of the shaman's differentiators are declining, and the delta between what other classes can offer in those areas versus the delta between what shamans offer versus those other classes is out of balance. Consider the following "core" EQ functions:

tanking
crowd control
buffing
debuffing
utility
healing
DPS

shamans were never intended as tanks, so mark this off.

our CC is ghetto at best, and high maintenance. Grab agro, take a beating, try to channel a root. Shoot a VP every 3 minutes.

buffing is a function with diminishing returns as your group/guild/raid progresses, gets better gear, and approaches/exceeds max stats unbuffed. Most elemental level melees will approach this, at least for critical stats.

debuffing is a thing of the past aside from slow. If your first slow doesn't land, by the time you malos and re-slow (which you may as will, given the recast time on turgur's) the mob is at 50% or less. Perhaps the new GoD slow mitigates this some. Not even gonna bring up the whole mitigation thing, which only aggravates this.

utility is something shamans have never been strong in - lev, eb, sow are all nice eye candy, but VoQ and SD are far more impactful.

healing hasn't kept up with the times. 350/tick will suffice in normal LDON/BOT, but much beyond that, it won't keep anyone alive, its primary use becomes topping off DPS or casters that accidentally get agro or after a canniV. Burst heal - pretty much a joke. Druids get a MUCH better burst heal relative to the tanks' HP.

DPS - now for the core of the problem. The game has evolved to one of burst DPS being key, and shamans just don't have this ability. Nukes are a joke, pets are a bigger joke, and our DOTS are too slow and the mob doesn't last long enough for them to be an efficient source of DPS. Bane was a great start, but we really need an upgrade here to keep up with the Joneses.

Obviously, our core function is to debuff (read: slow). When you compare us to the other slowers, we're anywhere from slightly better to significantly better than each. However, each has a SIGNIFICANT advantage in another area that outweighs our advantage, usually making them a clearly better choice as a group member.

Compare the chanter:
their slow is 70% to our 75%, but they can DC or charm some NASTY mobs. Huge DPS. They also bring a huge advantage in CC functionality. VoQ anyone? Nah, clerics don't like that.

Compare the BL
65% slow - a little weaker, but they have a massive DPS advantage. SD never hurt either. Tanking and off-tanking by pets brings a decent ghetto CC

Even compare the bard
52% slow - pretty big difference, but the ability to just about guaratee single pulls and increase the DPS of the party immensely with songs. The mob dies exponentially faster with DPS songs, so the hit in slow effectiveness is effectively cancelled.

To sum it up, I hate the fact that we haven't kept up with the times. I don't mind that our utility sucks, it alwasy has. Our CC is fading fast with how mobs hit nowadays, but I don't wanna see us a CC class anyway. We either need to have a significant boost in healing or _burst_ DPS, or a moderate one in both, in order to be effective and desirable in groups. Buffs that broke the stat caps would get us invited in a hurry as well.

And if you do play shaman, screw fungi staff, go with a nice 1hb/p (winterstorm comes to mind) + shield and fungus covered tunic, lunar preferred, scale acceptable =D

We are the worst healer of the healing classes.

If you are looking for a class that is the best at what they do (as you said), I would suggest looking at a cleric or a warrior.
