Total booty...6 bags, 11 shards, 4 pieces of metal, and more spells than I can count (including the rare Hammer of Divinity for my cleric..yay!). The problem is that I am starting to run into people who ONLY want to do TMs or Shards, rather than help with everything. I think I had one person ask about doing Idols, but everyone else seemed pretty cut and dry Taskmasters-or-shards. The problem is, my last emp key piece is the ring. This afternoon, when I made our first attempt to take a ring camp, we cleared the floor twice when several members of the group stated that they would be returning to the basement to farm shards because they wanted to finish their VT keys and leave, which in a way made sense as the guild was doing VT that day. This however would have left us quite short and probably unable to continue on our own for other camps. Trying to be a good sport, I rallied the troops and went along with their request, and we got shards for those who needed them to finish their keys, and one spare. (The two who finished their keys did in fact leave within about an hour.) We had about half the total number of shards we needed (and our tank needed a supper break) when we decided to try another camp for a while.

Then we got a bunch of new folks to join up and everyone wanted to hit TM again. Okay, we did TM. Scored 2 more bags. Everyone agrees we can finally try for rings. YAY! We pull up to 2nd floor and start clearing...goes great for 90 minutes or so, then a bad pull almost wipes us. Ended up having to have a monk flop us all back down to the first floor for rezzes, because of see invis mobs blocking the route for those who had survived the evac. No big deal, we rez and I ask who is up for another try. Lots of people respond in a positive way, except...

Everyone wants to go back to TM again because they've repopped. We are ringless, and 3 of us need rings to finish our Emp keys. All 3 of us have sat in the zone for almost nine hours today, all but 2 hours of it or so working on other drops than what we needed. I state that they can go back to TM, it's their choice, but then I am sorry but I will need to say goodnight. All of a sudden, I'm the bad guy. People ended up just plain leaving the group. The server crashed 20 minutes later so it ultimately didn't matter, I suppose, but I really am boggled at why folks didn't want to stay and at least walk away with one complete key. After all, we did it for those who needed shards earlier, didn't we? No one balked then. Why now? THAT is what bothers me.

If I could get every drop everyone needed all at the same time I would. Seriously. But what happens when the person who wants to help everyone, wants to camp the guy that drops their own last piece? People shy away. I will camp that darn temple until it turns to dust getting people their drops. That doesn't bother me. What bugs me is "2nd floor in Hate" syndrome*.

* (I chose this term because on almost every Hate raid I attend, as soon as the call to move to 2nd floor is made, classes whose armor only drops on 1st floor start leaving for whatever reason. Basically, they will no longer benefit so they don't want to stay.)

Yes, that last paragraph probably sounds like hypocrisy. I voiced my intent to leave if they went back to TMs, but yet I hate people who do just that. My words were spoken out of frustration for my own situation. Just as I had done with shards, I'd have likely stayed and helped (and watched more successful looters walk away, with my luck). I'm a real sucker for helping people, even when I have a good idea that I will get left behind for it.

What is one to do? Any of you had similar situations and think you can offer me some advice? I'm all ears. I like happy endings, even if they take some work.

I want to finish my key. And PlayerX's key. And Player #2562, 1569, 209, and 214's keys too. I love helping people. I am just starting to feel like I am being taken advantage of and I don't really know what to do about it. I can't progress alone; I need help. The best way to get help is to give it in exchange. The question becomes, how much more do I have to give? If I knew there was a light at the end of the tunnel, I know I wouldn't be this stressed.

Short disclaimer - I am in no way upset with anyone who left or who didn't want to camp rings. People have priorities. It's just the sheer situation that is bothering me, not the people involved. I'd go back tomorrow with every one of them and do it again. I just want that understood now.
