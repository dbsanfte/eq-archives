BlargX
Shaman can tank for rogues well if they have a fungi or torpor, but it's more difficult without strong regen above and beyond regrowth. I did this a lot and it worked great.

Shaman/monk is great. The FD ability lets you take on much tricker situations. And if you have torpor you can keep anyone healed forever.

Because of that, though, the warrior loses some of his value in a duo with shaman. His strengths are his hitpoints, which I already have covered through regrowth and torpor. His weakness is melee damage output, which is my weakness too. Our strengths cancel each other out a lot, and our weaknesses combine.

Monks tend to have lower hitpoints(but not always), but I have hitpoints covered with torpor/regrowth. Anyway, monks take far fewer blows than other melee classes -- warriors have, what, dodge, whereas monks have block? Whatever, the monk version is FAR superior. So absolute hitpoints are again not necessarily determinative. What I'm lacking is high melee damage output. Monks have that covered.

And if things go wrong, monk FD's and I gate. With warrior, one of us is going to die. Maybe I'll stay around too long trying to prevent the inevitable and contribute my death to the mix.

I've duoed many times with 60 warrior and 60 monk, and I can really see the exp slow up with the warrior. His damage output is just too slow.

Warrior does have taunt, though, which is nice. But the thing that makes him most valuable in the general game, hitpoints, are irrelevant when duoing with a shaman who has torpor. We'll both live forever with torpor anyway; hitpoints are not the problem.
