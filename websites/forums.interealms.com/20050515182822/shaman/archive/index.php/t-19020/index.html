Grafsha McDaughts
So many places to go with this.

We are priest classes by category but not by total definition. To call shaman pure priest class is the same as calling an enchanter a pure caster in the same vein as a wizard. Like the enchanter, who is not a dps machine (excluding late game charming) we are not priest whose focus is healing. Our tools lie elsewhere (buffage).

Shaman were also given the best offensive melee skill caps (200) and very destructive dots (at the time) to compensate for our lack of healing ability. While we fall into the priest class technically, we are on the outer fringe of that class. A fringe that bridges hybrid. Hybrids however can actually hit at a much greater frequency when they get weap skill to 200 as well as for more average damage. That gets into a whole different subject.

As for weapons, there are lower end proc'ing piercers that are great for melee use at lower levels. They are not uber or cool or anything but functional. Their usage in combat does what it should - helps to raise skill and the proc's add damage to target. Use them to max your skills.

Then move on to another type weapon (or switch out throughout your career) to max all skills.

Once piercing was maxxed, I moved to 1HB and picked up a soulstripper. It is still in my bag and still gets used for the 75 dmg/heal proc. Or I pull out my Corpsegrinder for the 100 heal and hits of 100+ (average 60 with the usual scattering of between 20 and max dmg.)

Do I use them for PoP mobs hitting for tons of damage. No, and I am not meleeing those mobs in TEH FACE. Neither are warriors or rangers or paladins or shadowknights soloing those mobs without some kind of healing support. But I can kill some of them solo, unlike the melee classes, because I can root/rot and pet/dot and aggro kite. I can in TEH FACE take on mobs that would either kill or at least require tons of recover time for a tank and I can do it and then move on to the next target in far less recovery time.

What I am saying is, in reponse to the original poster - there are other weapons available with decent stats, decent procs. It doesn't have to be piercing. As far as piercing goes, how many out there are doing a low aggro 1200 dmg dot? Shaman epic is it.

Have shaman been left out? Yeah, in the course of 'class balance vision' we have. With melee maxxed skills of 200 we should be significant melee damage fighters. But, sadly, since we were given those skills and it was 'overpowering' the 'vision' changed our chance to hit (priest tables) and increased the max skill level for hybrids and pure tanks. Were we compensated by enhancing our priest abilities? No, we can't heal better than we could in Kunark, relatively speaking.

Clerics and druids can. Palys can. Pet classes even got pet heals. Our heals have gone through the expansions with minimal increased healing power when bounced against the damage taken by the newer mobs. If I recall correctly from parses, based on damage taken to damage healed our heals have decreased in effectiveness while druids, clerics, etc. have actually kept pace.

There is a lot more wrong with the shaman class than not having a cute weapon with good stats available to you in the definition you want at the upper end of the game. Lots of threads deal with what those problems are.

I started playing EQ just over a year ago, as the result of a free cover CD on a games magazine. No expansions, so not knowing much I rolled up a warrior, who made it to 10 and was then deleted.

I decided I wanted a pet (and a wolf sounded better than an elemental or skeleton), so chose a shaman. I didn't know much about Beastlords then, not least because I didn't have Luclin.

The description of a shaman sounded like a battle priest to me, so (cleverly) I put most of my starting points into STR, then some in AGI and STA to round of the numbers - and yes, having a base WIS of 80 haunts me to this day! ;)

Anyway, up until the 30s I was perfectly fine at meleeing, but as I moved through the 40s and 50s I realised that I just couldn't cut it as a semi-tank anymore. Nonetheless, I capped alll my melee skills on principle, thinking that as we have the highest caps of all casters it might make a difference.

In reality, it didn't. Two things hurt the shaman melee wise by the time we ding 51:

* Lack of a damage bonus. No, I don't think we should get one added (as we're just a caster, albeit more sturdy than druids and the robe wearers).
* Caster damage table. As I understand it, most melees are on a better damage table than us right from the off, meaning their hits (when they connect) will do more damage than us casters. Shamans along with the other priests and INT casters are all on the same damage table, I've found, so fully buffed an INT caster is capable of the same maximum damage hits as us. They have lower combat skills, so will hit less often and for less damage on average, but the top cap is the same.

Therefore, at level 50 we have the same caps as a warrior but are already doing much less damage.

Post 51, the melee classes move up to a different damage table, doing even more damage on average. Whereas before they were only one tier above us, they're now two or more (as IIRC beastlords get improved damage tables at 55 and 60 - not 100% sure though). This is when I pretty much stopped meleeing in groups, as my damage was puny compared to others. I then soloed a lot in Dulak, eventually meleeing between JBB casts (funded by the absurd gem drop rate there). Rabid Bear was a must buy for me, and to this day I still find it great fun :)

Even though it's only for a few short minutes, having double attack and a decent ATK makes soloing great fun, although the thought of catching ripostes for 300+ puts me off using it to solo in the Planes.

The point of this post was to say that even if we did have a double attack AA, or improved caps, we'd still be on that caster damage table. IMO, an AA to move us to the pre-50 melee table would be better, as it would restore that "one behind" position with regards to melee classes and could hardly be considered unbalancing. As it is, we're stuck with level 49 skills and a level 1 damage table, which is why we're so poor at melee (IMO).

As for equipping books, I "won" one yesterday, when everyone else in the group passed. It gives me 50 more HPs, around 60 more mana and Extended Range IV.... but I'm a shaman, a battle priest, and even though I'll not be meleeing at all in hard LDoNs, equipping it to me would mean conceding that I really am lousy at melee. I know I could just swap it out for a weapon when soloing, but it just feels wrong somehow to wield it....

And as a final point - after getting Luclin and the rest, I did indeed make a beastlord, although he's only level 45 even now. Proper melee, watered down shaman spells and a great pet.... but my main is and will always remain my shaman.

Retron (http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=563014), aspiring battle priest on Venril Sathir.
