Trena
Umm, really hate to be the bee in the bonnet but, the first two of your suggestions just scream 'overpowered' Mrens. As it is, you have a dev team who believes that slow is one of the biggest mistakes ever made (right up there with complete heal) because it's 'overpowered' and makes balancing encounters more difficult (even with mitigation). To a certain extent, they're right. EVERY shaman on this board can cite examples of mobs that are hard (or downright guaranteed wipes) unslowed but totally gimp once slowed. As it is now, with most mobs (I say most because mobs prior to elementals and Time are more in sheer numbers than ele/time mobs) after your typical debuffs, they're fairly easy to slow. Add those AAs (and make no mistake. If those AAs are added to the game, EVERY shaman of level to get them will grind to snatch them up ASAP) and it stands to trivialize things even further.

Another possible issue is class envy. How many classes can cast slow spells (Shaman, enchanter, necro, bard, beastlord)? Now, if Shaman get that AA (or even just the slow casting reinforcement one), do you leave the other slow casting classes out?

Rez? *checks her address bar* did I make a wrong turn and go to the druid board? :p (Yes, I'm joking. jeez!) Seriously, farming out other classes' abilities is EVUL. Wanna give Pox and Bane of Nife to Beastlords? How about Malos to Mages? A case can be made for both of those scenarios (i.e. Shaman is a parent class of BLs and Mages got an unresistable resist debuff slightly worse than Malo previously). Nothing good ever comes from blurring class lines and abilities.

I think Hastened Cann5 would be a good idea to be added. It's Shaman specific and there's no balance reason why it's reuse couldn't/shouldn't be reduced at least some. If the AA worked like this:

Hastened Cannibalization
Level 1: 10% reduction in reuse time (takes it down to 62 seconds)
Level 2: 15% reduction in reuse time (takes it down to 58 seconds)
Level 3: 25% reduction in reuse time (takes it down to 51/52 seconds)

would be a bad place to start. Hell, double the reductions (bringing reuse down to 55/48/35 seconds) and it's still an ability with a fairly sizable downside (how fast can you regen 1924hp? Using Q eats into the efficiency of cann though in a group setting with certain classes, it would be KILLER. Imagine a 35 second refreshing cann5 grouped with a BL with Paragon or a druid with spirit of the wood).

Double attack? ehhhh, while a VERY compelling case could be made for adding this as an AA (Bards got it. Why not?), I honestly don't know how this would fare under playtesting. They could balance it easily using the skill cap on it. I would think that adding an AA that would allow you to raise your Dodge cap would be more useful though, wouldn't it? Bringing the dodge cap from 75 to say 125 or 150 would be more useful than double attack, I would think.
