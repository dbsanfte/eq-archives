My typical hunting group is only 4 people, myself (61 shaman) and my 3 friends (61 ranger, 61 SK, 61 druid).

Before the last patch that changed drop rates, we would win or lose collection missions by 5 minutes either way...either 85 minutes or 95 minutes to complete. After the patch, we are finishing each collection with about 20 minutes to spare, and last night had a personal best of collecting 29 vampire fangs with 35 minutes to spare; we stayed in the extra half hour til it kicked us out, and we ended up with 39 frickin teeth! So, the change is pretty dramatic, and if we can finish a mission with 30 minutes to spare with a group of 4 (although decently geared, AM2 ranger, and my shaman with Canni V), then better geared, full groups should finish in half the time.

I will agree, the adventures are fun and give back that sense of crawling through a dungeon. Money drops are pretty darn good too, between gems and opal bracelets and the like, and the augments and rares (we got two last night, Flesh Eater Belt and Bracer of Blood Rage, netted 4500pp overnight) really make the dungeons fun and profitable.

My only slight problem is the exp. Typically, in one adventure (usually 100 minutes), I will get about 45% of an AA; but my same group at the King in Droga would get closer to 80% in that same time. But again, the augments, the money, and the points are nice in LDoN. I finally was able to purcahse Anathema and it is really really nice, not a waste of points at all.

Anyway, just input from a shaman on D Ro :)
