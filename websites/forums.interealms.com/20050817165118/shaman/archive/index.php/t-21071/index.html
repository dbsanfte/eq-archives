BlargX
I think it's worth noting, like a poster does above, that if you get crit affliction AA's and expect SCS to help, don't. It has been said that SCS has no effect on DOTs in the first place, and I haven't noticed it having any.

Some state that SCS does work on DOTs though. If you're of that school of thought, bear in mind that although some people swear that critting on a DOT doesn't pull aggro(they seem to take that as a given on the SK boards), anyone who has gotten crit AA's and seen mobs turn toward them JUST as a crit strikes time and again knows full well that crit AA spikes up the aggro considerably at times. Whether that's because crits themselves cause aggro or whether it's just the additional damage/tick that causes the aggro, it's there, and SCS seems to have no effect.

Myself, I tend to lean toward the crit itself causing aggro, because a crit on any of our DOTs has often caused aggro to spike toward me, no matter how small, whereas sometimes popping DOT after DOT on mobs has done nothing to pull aggro if I'm not getting any crits. So it seems the raw damage increase alone is not enough to explain how much aggro you can suddenly get on a crit. Getting away with a bane tick that doesn't crit while getting aggro on a disease DOT that does crit seems to point to the crit being the factor.
