Check my Magelo - I'm almost at 400 now myself. My Magelo is about 10 aa's short. I can't even recall which GoD aa I invested in the second level of, which is sort of sad, but matches what I think you are feeling - our AA future isn't very bright. :o

SCF3 & FoM3 is a long haul aa'wise, but I have noticed a discernible difference in my nuke dps because of them. This was probably most noticeable on Grummus raids (I went on at least a dozen of them pick-up before I was finally flagged) where DoTs weren't feasible and I could only rain situationally. When chain casting Spear of Torment the crits really added up - even if all it really meant was that my damage went from being lousy to just mediocre. :p

When chain raining Tears/Torrent you can do some very nice burst dps with a string of crits. I've seen 1300, 650, 1300, 540, 1080, 1080 before off of just 2 casts. The triple crit has still evaded me though. :p Of course the number of times you can do this for any period of time when raiding is pretty small, but it is fun nonetheless. ;)

Spirit Call is handy for many little things, though if you mostly raid in PoP/GoD it won't do much for you. If you solo, I'd get this next. It can and will save your life.

Pet discipline is nice for grouping when you are the puller, or when you will be raiding a big mob w/ an AE (where push isn't an issue) to keep your pet held until the assist call is made. Will enable you to use dogdog on raids where you otherwise might not bother for fear of inadvertant dog attacks. This does require a bit of extra attention (as /pet hold isn't a persistent command - you have to hit it after every doggy combat) but I'm glad I got it.

Innate Metabolism was worth the 3 aa just because I'm lazy. Saves me from stopping every 2-3 days for food & drink. Instead I only worry about food once or twice every few weeks.

Other than that, maybe a level of Quickened Curing or Secondary Forte (gimped as it is) might be worthwhile.

Sadly, I don't see anything 'vital' for you (or myself) aa'wise. I've purposely avoided Crit Aff 2&3 since I already have aggro issues when I push hard in LDoN.

I've found crit AA fully maxed to be VERY helpful, Frekkels.

It works on your epic and every DOT you have. It seems to help a lot when I'm soloing or duoing at Plane of Fire tables. When I'm there I stack on epic, bane, blood of saryrn, ulthor, and pox and can get a possible 5 crits per tick. Not that 5 crits per tick is going to happen, but you can see how with five minutes worth of dotting every mob, 5 chances per tick means 50 chances to crit per minute, or 250 per mob. With some fudge factor involved on the time, of course, because everything varies, and I don't always keep every DOT on every mob the full way through. Anyway, you can see how much extra damage that could do. Heck, just a couple of crits with bane alone really adds up. And those two-minute disease DOTs become more attractive when you think about how many chances to crit they're going to give you.

It also helps when you're doing scarier mobs, like nameds. There are some mobs in GoD, for instance, that will pop a pet out on you if you don't kill them quick enough -- a pet who is hard to root and hits really fast and hard enough to matter. Or maybe you just need to kill something before a respawn or roamer gets in your face. Every little bit of damage helps when you're on a timer.

I don't DOT on raids, so it means nothing to me there, but solo or duo, exp or loot camping, I really think crit AA is a great one to max out.
