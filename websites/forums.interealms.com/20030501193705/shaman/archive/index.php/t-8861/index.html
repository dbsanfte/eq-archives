I thought the comic was damn funny, like most of his stuff. It sounds like most of us here felt pretty much the same. I do find it interesting and perhaps disturbing though that we would have been targeted as the class who needs to buy the "50 gallon drum", as historically we have been total sheep when it comes to nerfs, or lack of significant upgrades, and any whining that has happened has been pretty much confined to these boards, where we comiserate with our shaman brothers and sisters about SoE's lack of insight regarding our class and it's issues.

Unlike most of the other classes:

- Bards raise holy hell anytime a new stacking issue comes up...we patiently endure bizzare stacking issues and hope they'll get resolved someday.

- Clerics have bitched and moaned for years about their Epic and the bottlenecks in it. Once that was fixed, they switched topics to how they have slowly become less useful in groups (until the new Mack-Truck-hitting mobs in PoP came out).

- Necros have waged a bloody war with VI over many things, mainly nerf after nerf and the issue of stacking dots on mobs. Since we also use big dots, you would have thought Shamans would have voiced their opinion on that issue, but for the most part we were silent. In fact, some of us (myself included) feel that dots should not stack in the way they currently do, as it is not really realistic.

- Tanks and hybrids of all shapes and sizes have battled mightily and vocally for years to define their place in the game, how they should stack up against the other classes in damage, AC, mitigation, etc. Every new discipline, AA Skill, etc., is put thru the ringer.

- Druids - God don't get me started. BY FAR the biggest whiners in the game, the constant squeaky wheel, etc. By contrast, Shamans are the sheep of the EQ world when it comes to speaking up over about our issues. They would be buying vat-loads of the liquid in the comic strip is it were available.

It was only recently that we on this board have fully realized that the only way to get ANY attention from SoE was to becaome something of a squeaky wheel ourselves. Several threads in this forum, in the Spells and Alchemy forums, etc., have been started to attempt to consolidate the views of the Shaman community into a voice we can use to gain SoE's attention. It is not what we as a community WANT to do. But it is what we have come to realize that we NEED to do to have any hope of a reasonable outcome.

Even the outcry over the broken slow mitigation, which in my personable opinion is the single biggest nerf to our class ever made, was focused almost solely NOT on the fact that slow mitigation should not be in the game at all, but rather that what was in the game was broken. They handed us a plate of crap, and all we did was complain that the plate was chipped, not that the food was crap!

I guess the bottom line of what I'm trying to say is that while we may bitch and moan about our lack of upgrades, the poorly considered nerfs to our abilities, etc., we confine those to our little corner of the web and don't speak out like other classes do. Or at least we did until recently. Hey, maybe we'll need those 50 gallon drums after all!

Lastly, Woody is the moderator of his forums, and has the power and the right to do pretty much whatever he wants on them. Killing or locking a thread that is little more than name calling and such is a commonly accepted practice for board mods. Editing the post as he did, however, is stepping over the line, and he is destined to lose the respect of many of his readers by going that route.
