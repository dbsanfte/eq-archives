Well here's my take on shaman healing in GoD.

On any mob that is slowable, except a boss, I've been able to heal without real issues. I've healed Warriors. I've healed Knights. Tipt, Vxed, Kodtaz, even Ikkinz. Sewers? Those slow immune golems beat the crap out of me and I shunned the zone.

My role in most groups is is slim. Slow. Debuffs. Some heals. The buffs? Thats all gone. The buff bot is too entrenched in the high end game. Everyone has access to them. Even Ferine, one of our best buffs, I've had people say don't hit me with it. I'm out of slots and I need more important things. Thats without going into the use of buffs since day one. People like them, I enjoy making people happy, but I can't even bring that to the table. Buffs are dead.

Debuffs. I wish I could feel better about this. It seems like I'm usually tossing Malos on anything that moves just because its a liability if slow doesn't stick on mobs. I think myself fairly equipped, but I lack a TA. I also lack the new GoD slow. Maybe things will change with either item, but usually this just means I am either on the pull, or having the group buy me time to do debuffs. Buying time can be done in most situations, but it is non optimal, in terms of damage.

Heals. Well here's the point of contention. With our mana pools, canni, and everything else Tnarg's in big fights serves as a decent patch that we can provide in a long duration fight. Is a cleric better? Yes. Is a druid better? Yes. Are you better off in most situations with them using their CHs if time is bought? Yes. Can a shaman buy that time? Well usually. Spamming patch on Ikkinz trials is one of the few endevours where I can empty my mana bar totally and not be done with a fight. Breath of Trushar? See earlier comment about buff slots.

The picture is different on trash mobs. If I can slow a mob, Breath is a great spell to help with healing. Toss it on someone who got ramp. Toss it on someone who's getting Ripostes. Toss it on a maintank and use Tnarg's to help cover the 101 proc types that mobs have now. We aren't the fastest at doing it, but in a non boss fight, when you mostly control the pacing, a shaman's regen of mana and ability to distribute heals really makes them useful. I've done Kodtaz trash duo'd with an SK for a while. I don't know how well any other healing class can do that. This one role, hey I feel good about. But is that the whole picture?

For trials, I feel like a weak link. A lot of classes do. In fact I think the only classes that feel that they aren't threatened in some ways are warriors and clerics. Thats more of the issue here. The design of the expansion has hurt everyone. We're included. Do I enjoy that I've heard almost every member of my guild say in frustration that this expansion makes them want to quit? Has this ever happened to me in any other expansion? No
