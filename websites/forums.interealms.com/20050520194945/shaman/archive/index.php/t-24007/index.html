Level of the group doesn't matter, only the level of the leader that gets the expedition. If the leader is level 68+ you get a harder version than if a level 67 or under does, even with the same group make up. It is only dependant on who asks for the expedition. I know there is a cut off at 67, I'm not sure if there is one lower than the two I've been in. The two I have been in were started by 65-67's, and 68-70's. Not sure of the lower range of the first or if there are any lower than that, I haven't tested that much yet.

There are three of them in Dranik's Hollows. Murkglider Hive, Firepit, and Watering Hole. Murkglider Hive is pretty much a linear dungeon, with almost no branch offs. Only models are rats, bats, and murkgliders, with a dragorn quest mob in the high level one. Firepit or watering hole (not sure which) has a lot of dragorn, kyv, noc's, etc type mobs.

No real goal like LDoN, just an instanced xp and loot zone.

Zaviax McCleo
Lot of my guildmates have been spending a lot of time in Murkglider's Hive farming spells and raking in XP. This has been a great alternative to open zones like Karnor's 3.0 (AKA Wall of Slaughter).

The reason we do Murk Hive is because of how close both named are to each other. First, you need a level 68 person to choose the expedition so you get the right one. Trash mobs are level 65-66 and named are 69. XP is pretty nice, about 1% every few kills at level 66 and the mobs aren't too tough. Hit max around 600-700 or so. All fully slowable. Even the named. Few resists on slow after malos and Virulent Paralysis sticks fairly well allowing us to do considerably well with CC.

To get to the spot for the named is easy. Only the Murks are KoS and they're undead. (all mobs are social though and have huge aggro ranges) So load up on IVU and run there. Keep following your nose. At the first intersection go straight across. At the next intersection a ways in, you'll make a left. The next room is a unique looking one with bunch of pools. Go to the tunnel on yor right. At the end of this tunnel you should either see a Tattered Wing bat which is a PH for Silentpaw. Just outside the tunnel in the pool room to your left will be two mobs. One will be a Murk the other a Hideous Rat which is another PH for Inky Murkglider.

Both can drop runes for 66-68 spells. They seem to have roughly a 50% chance of dropping one.

Silentpaw also commonly drops some decent caster boots that don't sell for too much in bazaar. (4-5k I think). His uncommon drop is a +25 HP aug, slot 7. (lore and no drop)

Inky commonly drops a nice leather bracer. Sells for about 15-20k or so. His rare drop is a nice sword. (Not sure how much it goes for)

One thing to note about this place is that it's bugged currently. In the middle of the pool room a Dragorn Commoner will keep respawning on top of each other along with a bunch of rats at his feet. He's level 70 and tougher than the other mobs in the dungeon, though not too tough. He only drops some ring that I have no idea what it's for. The longer you spend in here, the more the mobs stack up and the more lag you'll get. It's not too big a deal since you can just quit out and get a new expedition but if you're puller gets sloppy he'll train you with 20 mobs and you'll experience instant-death, heh. :)

Money here is great too. The crap that drops sells for a nice bundle. Sometimes you have better runs than other. Yesterday we did a 2 hour run and got 2700pp+ total. Not to mention some good tradeskill stuff (Murk hides, Dust of Discord, etc...) Trash on very rare occasiondrop attunable augments too.

I don't know much on the other expeditions but hope to give them a try soon and see how they compare to the Murk Hive.
