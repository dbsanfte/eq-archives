Ragnorokk
I have been attempting to improve my skills in relation to slow speed for high end encounters. I am refering to perticular slowable mobs with high damage output. Such encounteres could be labeled as "Must slow" mobs. Examples can be experienced at all levels of high end play.

Ive discussed different techniques with various people. Ofcourse all stratigies began with the application of the unresistable debuffs tashani and malo. But after there were two main procedures for slowing. One, chain malosini until land, then cast turgurs insects until slowed. The second was to alternate castings of malosini and turgurs, thus maximizing time by overcomming turgurs recast delay.

I have never liked malosini, or any other resistable debuffs for mobs that need to be slowed now. The basic principal is this:

If malosini lands, slow would have landed.

For this reason I call malosini a waste of time on mobs that need to be slowed immeaditly. I dont believe stratigy 1 is the best plan for must slow encounters. After malosini, there is no guarentee your next slow will land, espically on high mr mobs. And even if it does, that mob could have been slowed sooner, because the malosini that you stuck should have been a slow.

Now for the second stratigy. It would appear to be more viable, because it apperantly doesnt sacrafice slow time to malosini, it just utilizes the time turgurs cannot be recast. But, according to my statement: If malosini lands, slow would have landed, it would theoraticly always be better to slow than malosini. Which beings me to the third stratigy.

Far less shaman prefer a third stratigy to slowing. The idea is to memorize two slows, and cast them in sequence, tugurs followed by any other slow during the recast refreshing of turgurs (instead of malosini). To me this best utilizes our time, and speed by which we slow "must slow" mobs.

There are two challenges to this theory. One is that the alternate slow must not have a higher resistance rate than malosini. The second is aggro. To an extent, aggro is irrelivant. For instance, if your raid will have no chance of sucess after 20 seconds of fighting X mob unslowed, it does not matter if you live 21 seconds or 2 minutes, your raid will fail. So there is clearly a diminishing returns to aggro control. But it is still important to live for the 20 seconds, or however long your window of oppurtunity to slow is.

There are really two options to slows we can sequence with. Togors deffinatly does not have a higher resistance rate than malosini, however aggro is an issue. The second is Tigirs. This spell, in my testing, has significantly less aggro than turgurs and togors (when used against one mob) but has a very high resistance rate. I have not determined if its resistance rate is higher than malosini. Also, certain mobs react differently to debuffs, so the spell selection would depend on the encounter.
To be noted, I havent considered any of our other slows.

A final option is to incorperate malosini into the slow sequencing, to percisely controll aggro yet at the same time maximize slow effiecncy.

So im throwing this out here to see what the general community thinks about the various procedures of slowing "must slow mobs".

I want to stress I am not proposing mindless suicide slowing.

the point ragnorokk is making has nothing to do with ourselves living or not. the situation he has set up is simply, the mob must be slowed QUICKLY or the raid WILL lose. we've spoken about this a little bit and i'm far less experienced than he, but i would like to add that i have found tigirs insects to have less aggro than the normal togors/tugurs line. the difference in this though, is that tigirs has a 5 second cast time, while tugurs has only 3. i looked at the cast times and recast delays on castersrealm, and found the following:

tugurs insects: 3 sec cast time, 6 second recast delay
tigirs insects: 5 sec cast time, 5 second recast delay

if we were to examine a time from of say 30 seconds, we can see that alternating between tugurs and tigirs would yield many more opportunities to land a slow, yet the problem is, chain casting this many slows is probably going to get you a very, very dead shaman. during that 3 second recast from tugurs, you could already be seconds into casting a tigirs, so in the situation of MUST SLOW QUICK mobs, where the life of the shaman is inconsequential, and you want to chain cast any form of slow critically needed by your raiding force, alternating spells may not be a bad idea.

this also doesn't take into account, if you have multiple shamans or other slowers on the raid, your odds drastically increase by the number of ppl there. examples include slow procing weapons like willsappers, earthcallers, etc., as well as any enchanters on the raid willing to put their lives on the line.

i don't think ragnorokk is talking about hardcore chain slowing and meaning pretty much instant death for the shaman in terms of many mobs in the game, but there are examples (i'm not experienced enough to name any real good ones), where landing a slow within the very early parts of the battle are going to mean the difference between victory and defeat, and i for one find alternating slows as a viable means for this. although, i do really see myself getting hammered down quick attempting to pull this off, so better have a clicker ready, lol
