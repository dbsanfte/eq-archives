Scalia
You can also Root a mezzed mob and not break the mez as long as the Root doesn't have a damage component the way Druid roots do.

Generally, I find that handling adds in normal LDON is easy for a Shaman. Stand reasonably far away from the MT, pick a mob that the MT is NOT tanking, slow it (thus drawing agro) and the mob runs over to you. Root it, step out of melee range and do the same to the next add. Rinse and repeat.

Things to consider:

1. Will the tank live with 3+ unslowed mobs pounding on him? If it's a warrior, he probably will, especially if there is a Cleric healing him and/or he has Defensive up. If not, you may have to consider using your AE slow.

2. Resists and Malo(s). I don't get enough resists in normal LDON to justify Malo, but opinions differ on the subject. The best argument for using Malo that I can think of is that it helps Root to last longer. If you are <60 and don't have an irresistable debuff, you are probably better off chain-slowing.

3. Unrootable mobs. There is a fair number of them in LDON, especially in Tak, so I try to pick rootable ones as my first slow targets and let the MT handle the unrootable ones. By the time I get to the unrootable mob that the MT is tanking, my slow won't get me agro.

If there are ONLY unrootable mobs in camp, I just slow one, draw agro, offtank and channel more slows through the mob's slowed melee. If your AC/HP is on the low side, this may not work for you.

4. Casters. Root parking casters is generally less effective than root parking melees - a no brainer. Besides, their melee DPS is lower, so slowing them is less effective. Slow/root park melee mobs first and let the melees kill the casters, especially healers, first. I am not a big fan of wizzie mobs either.

5. Pet issues. If your pet is equipped with Mage toys, the proc can break Root (um, or so I seem to recall, not 100% sure), so you may not want to have the dogdog offtanking the add. have him assist the MT instead.

Of course, all of the above only applies to less well equipped adventurers. In a well equipped group, normal LDON is trivial and I just melee or something :)
