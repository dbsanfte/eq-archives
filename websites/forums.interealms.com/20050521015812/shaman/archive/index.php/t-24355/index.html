Chutoi
Now you're discussing something else entirely, Ung. Your issue really isn't class balance with respect to percentages. As long as percentages aren't changed, the classes remain generally in their prior state of balance.

As you noted, the problem is that game changes and content changes make revisiting the healing percentages every once in a while a good idea.

SoE has been firmly wedded to the 100/64/47 percentages since GoD came out. They've stuck through those percentages through slow mitigation, 30 second fights in GoD, 600% increases or more in dps, etc. They have consistently refused to so much as discuss the healing percentages. Any mention of them is soundly ignored. (This can pretty much be laid at the feet of the same ****tard that won't make our pet dual wield--Rytan the Asslord).

The thing is, SoE sets the bar on the dps versus healing (of a cleric). Druid and shaman healing fall out mathematically.

There are other factors that add some meaning to your argument--but it's not a percentage issue. We are healing the same percentage as we have been.

The pinch you are probably noticing is a factor of several things (imo, of course! Agree/disagree as ya like, it's just my opinion).

1) There is (or at least was) a significant 'overheal' component for cleric--and to a lesser extent, druid--heals. Their heals (fast and slow) were typically large enough, and mobs' relative dps were slow enough that their heals seldom hit for full. Thus, their heals had a 'overheal' component above and beyond full hp.

Now let's vastly increase mob dps. With the volatility of hit point variances during a fight, now most druid heals hit for full, and many cleric heals hit for full. Shaman heals, on the other hand, were almost ALWAYS hitting for full BEFORE then dps change. We have no overheal component to cushion the changes.

Result: our healing is disproportionately whacked compared to cleric or druid healing despite the consistent percentages. Rytan is not smart enough to understand this fact, btw, or at least chooses to ignore it so he can justify screwing us over as usual.

Making the game more difficult for the specialist classes is a guaranteed screwing of the generalist classes--we can't keep up.

2) Length of fights. This was a HUGE problem in GoD, especially pre-tuning. 30 seconds fights, usually 8 of which were getting the mob slow, meant that shaman did jack for damage mitigation and did jack for dps. Jack for healing is, of course, obvious.

Post-tuning, with 45 seconds fights, it was better--not good, but better.

OoW actually helps us some. Mobs live 1 minute or so in solid groups. We can slow and, to some extent, DoT.

The problem is that fights vary per expansion, per group, etc. Who do you tune against? The uberest of uber crowds and work down? (probably best--you can measure the highest end more easily than a nebulous 'average' shaman). What kind of equipment do you put on the rest of the group (affecting dps and hence length of fight).

Shaman healing should be a percentage based on length of fight--we have to slow, then catch up on heals, then average heal over the rest of the fight. Some may argue that we shouldn't be slowing AND healing--I thumb my nose at them. If, as has been argued by clerics AND developers, our heals are smaller because we can slow, then I view that as an argument that we should be able to slow then heal and be an equivalent healer to a cleric.

Anyway, the problem is that length of fight determines how well we have to heal (to catch up for unslowed period, then averaged in with rest of fight). The analysis also has to ignore resists, as resists have too large a noise ratio.

I think someone attempted an estimate, and came up with a shaman needing to heal approximately 60% of a cleric. Unfortunately, Rytan could (if he were actually inclined to justify a decision rather than just say, 'no') pick apart any such study because there are 10,000 variables. Empirically, we can see the need for improved healing, but Old Asshat always demands proof (which he ignores anyway)--and proving something with 10,000 moving parts is unbelievably hard.

Bottom line, as I said in an earlier post, I don't disagree with the sentiments, but the argument isn't actually a problem with fixed percentages, but what the percentages are, especially in relation to the mob dps and length of fights. Raw numbers aren't the issue! :)

Ungkor
"shamans havent been nerfed"

You are correct. Anyone with slow was nerfed, starting in late PoP, culminating in GoD, and carrying through into OoW.

Our buff line became rather worthless towards the end of PoP (max stats due to gearflation), was entirely useless except for 540 hp buff and pr/dr during GoD, and was just now partially restored with some overcapping and less reliance on stats in OoW.

In terms of dps, anyone using dots as a primary means of dmg was nerfed due to content changes, faster battles, and lower hp mobs. Necro's and shamans faced this bitterly in GoD. In OoW, mobs have slightly more hp, and curse dots like Bane usually run their course. However, our poison line typically does not. Poor necro fire lines like funeral pyre haven't for a few expansions now.

So... yeah, shamans weren't nerfed alone, but the general content change, widespread slow mitigation, boss mob immunity to slow, gearflation, shorter battles, less hp on mobs, etc.. affected several classes. It just happens to be unfortunate that shamans were affected by all of the above... so in essence.. yeah, shamans felt the changes to most.
