The best way to get fletching skill is by making arrows. Get a good list of the arrow component trivial levels, then make the most basic arrow you can (with all the 7 copper parts, that is) except for the one piece with the higher difficulty. Every arrow has the trivial level of the *most* difficult piece involved.

Astroshak
Depends on how much cash and time you have to spend, really.

Low on cash, but high on time? Hunt acrylia and the condensed substances, and make acrylia arrows to 243.

Low on time, but got a ton of cash? Make darkwoods until 235 then shadewoods to 243.

Wood elf? Got some cash? Make mithril arrows to 243.

Mithril arrows (for a wood elf) take longer than simply camping a bow part vendor for skill; but takes less time than hunting the stuff for acrylia arrows. Also, mithril arrows (at roughly 2k per 120 combines) are cheaper per combine than bows are; but a lot more expensive than the acrylia arrows.

I say to take the skill to 243 for one main reason : whether you believe the geerlock's effective skill caps out at 252 or 255 at 243 you're there with a geerlock. There is no reason, other than to say that you did it, to go beyond 243 if you've got a geerlock.

Astroshak
The Geerlock is an item made by a gnome tinker. The Tradeskill Trophy (generic term) is the item that you craft with the tradeskill, in this case, the Grandmaster's Whittling Knife (or whatever it is called) is made by fletching.

Both the Geerlock and the Grandmaster's Trophy have a plus 5 percent modification to a tradeskill.

I've heard two different things about the skill modifying items. The first one (which I tend to believe myself) is that they can only raise the effective skill to 255. The other thing I've heard is that they can only raise the effective skill to 252.

The difference in success rates between a 250 skill, a 252 effective skill, and a 255 effective skill are going to be minimal, however, unless the item has a very high trivial. And yes, if you compare success rates, it is apparent that while the skill cannot go over 250 base, items can trivial well over 250.

Oh, if you are wondering, the reason I use the term effective skill when I refer to the use of a skill modifying item, such as a Geerlock or a Trophy, it is because the item only modifies your skill for purposes of success (and, for some PoP recipes, whether you can attempt them at all!); it does not alter your real (base) skill at all.
