Agility = 7% avoidance
Ferine = 10% damage

Let's assume the ratio of DPS remains the same for each class.
Approximately:
800 enchanter pet hasted
160 rogue/wizard
140 monk/ranger/beastlord/mage
120 berserker
100 warrior/druid
80 pal/sk
60 brd/shm
Read as: a shaman does 60% the dps of a warrior and a warrior does 60% the damage of a rogue. A sustainable dps from a shaman is generally regarded as pet 30 + spells of 30 (keep in mind your roll is to slow and you have to do that before you can do damage).

Assume that a shaman adds 25% extra dps to anyone they buff (this is an overestimate for elemental+ equiped people who are near stat caps).

800*1.25 = 1000
160*1.25 = 200
140*1.25 = 175
100*1.25 = 125
80*1.25 = 100

Now create a group where the benefit of having a shaman present is greater then that of a bard or beastlord (other common picks for slowers).

When do you want a shaman?
- If you have a full melee group (beyond chanter/cleric) and the chanter charms.

When do you not want a shaman?
- If you have any caster dps (mage, wizard, etc) who benefit more from mana regen (beastlord) or damage bonus/mana regen (bard).
- You are not using a chanter pet (bard over haste, or beastlord dps + ferocity are greater then a 25% bonus + shaman dps)

Did I ignore avoidance?
Yes. Why? Because you will only avoid 1 attack out of 14 attacks. And it is NOT very likely against a 1k hitting mob that you will notice or even be able to sustain 14 attacks hoping to avoid one.

The usefulness of avoidance is really for the MA only and not for single group events where you will avoid one extra attack every few min (non ma).

Do warriors use their evasive discipline when mobs hit for 3k+? Nope due to the fact that avoidance is a "random roll of the dice" and defensive is a "guarenteed lowering of damage". There are no guarentees with avoidance. It isn't like with evasive (50% chance to avoid melee attack) a mob which can hit 4 times a round will never hit more then twice.

Did I ignore healing?
Yes. Shaman can heal. A shaman's healing is not required except under extreme conditions (boss mobs). And typically where a shaman is required as a healer, a druid or another cleric make a better choice.

Conclusion.
Bards, Enchanters, Beastlords provide means of slowing AND increasing dps of Melee and Casters. Shaman only do Melee.

Thus in order to use a shaman in an experience group, you must have melee in order to reap the bonuses shaman provide. Also understand that there is no guarentee an enchanter will WANT their pets doing 25% more dps as their survivability goes down once it breaks.

I still contend that you can easily blur this by adding utility to shaman which is beyond pure DPS calculations.

Even something as simple as a ward which increases mana regen by 2-3 helps work towards this goal.
