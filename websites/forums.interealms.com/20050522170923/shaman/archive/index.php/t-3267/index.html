<b>Spotted Dick renamed to spare shoppers’ blushes? </b>

A supermarket chain may rename one of the nation’s
favourite puddings to save shoppers from blushing when
they buy it.

Tesco is asking customers if they want spotted Dick
rebranded as spotted Richard after it noticed sales of the
pudding lagged behind those of other traditional desserts.


Bosses were puzzled at the sales figures after taste tests
of the pudding met with positive feedback, so further
research was conducted on 1,200 customers.

The results showed that men felt bashful about buying
the suggestively named sponge and raisin dessert,
especially from women checkout assistants. .

Jacqueline O’Neill, from Tesco, said: "We want to know
what our customers think.

"On the one hand, spotted Dick has been a favourite dish
for decades, and so many will want to keep the name.

"However, we also know that many men have been
embarrassed by the name because they seem to believe
it may be interpreted as a comment upon their own
personal condition."

While 98 per cent of male respondents listed spotted Dick
as among their favourite desserts, 78 per cent admitted
its name made them feel uncomfortable. For 7 per cent of
men, buying the dish under its current name was a real
trial when facing a female checkout assistant.

A further 3 per cent of mainly young men said they would
avoid the ordeal entirely and head for a checkout with a
man behind it.

However, women were more low-key, registering only
slight amusement at its name which has caused much
mirth in classrooms over the years. Meanwhile, Tesco will
quiz customers to see if they want the pudding rebranded
when it launches a new range of desserts next month.

Alastair Jamieson
Saturday, 25th August 2001
The Scotsman
