I am a 2-boxer. I have played most classes together at one time or another. When I select player combinations, I like to look for synergies.

There are a few combinations that I have found work really well in some situations (outdoors for example). Once in a while, I find combinations that can work well in many areas. Sometimes combinations only work well together for a certain range of levels.

One of the best overall combinations I have found is a shaman and a necromancer. Granted, the necromancer gets more out of the pairing than a shaman gains. Necros get better mana regen (especially at lower levels - shaman don't start to catchup till later in life). This allows the necro to cast alot more spells than a shaman can.

In this grouping the necro provides the damage and snares while the shaman buffs, slows, regens, helps crowd control with roots, heals, SOWs and occassionally gets to cast a DOT or nuke. Most of the time my shaman has less than half mana while my necro is approaching full mana.

So why do necros think a shaman is the best possible duo partner? Shaman fill in the few gaps that necros have. They provide all important regeneration, slows, spot heals, buffs, SOWs for outdoor work and traveling. Shaman's excellent armor and stamina allow them to be the puller (when FD splitting isn't a requirement) and tank a bit permitting necros to pile on the DOTs.

My biggest beef about EQ is the built in downtime. Nothing worse than having to med for extended periods of time. Necros get the best mana regen in the game. Higher level shaman have the second best mana regen. The beauty of 2 casters is that when you want to vacate a location, you can just gate out. You can also bind right outside a dungeon should you wish to. This reduces downtime a great deal.

If you do a search on the necro boards, shaman are selected as the best partner for a necro by a wide margin. Having played this combination, I can see why. Both classes are arguably among the most powerful in the game in their own right. Together, they are even more so.
