You answered your own question ... pretty much. Enjoy it. At the high end there is still much to enjoy about playing a shaman. There are also - currently - areas in the game where what you could do as a shaman elsewhere can't be done.

When I was leveling up I avoided undead areas. They are poison/disease immune critters for the most part. My best and most powerful tools for dmg were resisted way too much - totally resisted in some places. So I didn't go there - alone.

With every class I have played (and I have quite a few 65 and 60+ classes - played toons) there are places they are not effective. So, those classes don't go there. Each goes to the places they can be most effective (solo) or at the least significant contributors (groups.)

The biggest problem with shaman and GoD is the 'raid' or 'group raid' enounters are where we feel the bite this time. This has happened to other classes in other expansions and time cured it. The zones were changed, abilities were added to compensate, or the classes adapted. Time will tell how this works out with GoD zones-high end.

As others mentioned ... don't sweat the problems 65's are having now. It isn't your problem. Don't race to 65 just to get there. You are having fun now - continue to do so. Don't powerlevel. The journey will teach you many things you have yet to even guess about and you will see many parts of the game a lot of 65's have never experienced. See as much as you can. Play in all the zones you can find to play in. Challenge yourself against content you are at now and work, slowly, up the xp chain.
