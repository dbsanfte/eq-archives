Graal the Dorf
2 focus items that cause the same effect do not stack, as has been mentioned. Any general focus effect will be superceded by a specialized focus effect. I.E. affliction efficiency focus items will be used while the general mana preservation focus effect won't be.

Spells that have a cast time of 3.0 or less are unaffected by spell haste. Hybrids have an innate spell haste of about 7% at level 60. This doesn't affect any of our spells with casting time of 3.0 or lower.

There is also a maximum spell haste of 50%. An example is the mage quick summon AA stacked with a summoning haste foci. Quick summon AA by itself raises puts casting haste on summon spells at 50%. Focus effects cannot increase this haste percentage.

This about equal to melee haste. Melee haste is capped 105% at level 60. This is measured in speed increase, not delay decrease. If you are going to measure delay decrease, it would be 51% delay decrease haste. That's only 1% faster than spell haste is capped at.
