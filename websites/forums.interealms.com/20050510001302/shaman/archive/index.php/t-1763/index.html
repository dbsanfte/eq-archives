Gloriana
I would utilize the search feature, nearly every question you've asked has been covered fairly well.

1) To my knowledge, this is the best EQ shaman community on the web. I sometimes read the shaman forums at Casters Realm, but I love the Crucible.

2) The races are fairly even, but they do have advantages and disadvantages.

Troll - widely regarded as the most powerful race (45% of Crucible shaman responding to a poll said they would choose a troll if they had to restart). Innate regen, can use the JBB, snare necklace if they worship Inny, slam. Disadvantages - large race (can get stuck without shrink), can't wear jarsath, sebilite scale, IGS, faction in the old world. Exp penalty.

Barb - most common shaman race due to evenly balanced wis/sta/str for starting stats. Can wear IGS, jarsath and JBB. Good faction in the old world, but nightblind until level 9 and infravision. Cute polar bear.

Ogre - big advantages - frontal immunity from stun and incredibly high starting stamina. Can wear JBB, slam, large race so size can be a problem. No innate regen, low starting wisdom, poor starting faction in old world. Exp penalty.

Iksar - hotly debated. Strengths - highest startest wisdom, innate regen, innate 40 AC (at level 60 ?), forage, start with 100 swim, good newbie area. Disadvantages - no snare necklace (must worship CT), no slam, no JBB (big!), exp penalty, low starting stamina.

Vah Shir - moderate starting stats, no slam, medium race, can wear JBB, get sneak (pretty cool for questing) and safe fall. No innate regen, no exp penalty (that I know of) can't wear fungi tunic (but can wear the regrowth tunic from the FG caller quest). Pretty rare and cool looking.

Honestly, I think the races are fairly equally balanced, play what looks like fun.

Standard shaman starting gear (if I had 5 plat for every time I typed this out...)

Ears - forest loops
Helm/arms/boots/gloves - JB if applicable
tunic - regen is goal, iksar ceremonial chestplate is good

legs - leg choices suck...really
back - mammoth cloak
wrists - save up for those nice ones from WW, pricey
shoulders - obulus death shroud? starting out I wore a lizzie mantle
melee - jade prod, slime harpoon, howling harpoon, wraithbone hammer
shield - clay guardian shield, SBS

Vah Shir get a great newbie zone, lot's o' quests, tons of mobs (i.e., leveling fodder).

Your choice of race really dependes on playing style if you ask me. Power gamer and stats/abilities most important? Just want a decent race that looks cool? Want to be evil?

Some other equipment suggestions:

Shoulders: Tattered Mantle
Rings: 6ac/65 hitpoints
Rygorr is great shaman armor
Weapon: Spine Chill Spear/Ebon War Spear
Ears: Orc Fang (if you cannot afford Forest Loops)
Shield: Charred Guardian Shield gives you some AC and nice Wis (very affordable these days), may want SBS or Clay Guardian for higher AC if you can afford.

Most of the totemic armor is not that difficult to quest, though you need to buy Dufrenite(sp?) gem for each piece, which runs about 170 pp if I recall. The Totemic Helm has some WIS and decent AC, totemic legs not to bad either. The Totemic BP is very hard to get because of rare drop from Grizzlenot. I believe any class can quest the totemic if you can talk to the quest givers in Lake Rathe, and it is not no drop, so you can use a higher level character to quest it for your newbie shaman (or just buy it).

Gloriana
Like Stormrazor said, trolls are considered an excellent shaman race because:

* Have innate regen (since shaman can convert hitpoints to mana, this is HUGE for shaman)
* Trolls that worship Inny can quest for a necklace which casts a low level snare. It might be a low level snare, but it still adds an extremely valuable spell to our line up
* Trolls get slam
* Trolls can wear fungi

The benefits of a Vah Shir are more subtle, sneak is cool, safe fall is moderately cool...but regen is godly.

That being said, play what looks fun to you and don't look back.
