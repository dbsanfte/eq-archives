Jinheim
Whenever I am soloing something in the old world, for whatever reason (was soloing reports for my seru earring last week, for example) I use my pet to tank and help split adds (by old world I mean anything prior to pop).

I can throw a quiescence on my pet prior to the pull, then pull with malos and send my pet in to the middle of whatever adds are coming. Since malos is practically 0 aggro, all the mobs will stop rushing me and instead attack him. This leaves me free to slow everything one at a time and root it without having to deal with being quadded and bashed in the face by multiple mobs.

Also, for most old world mobs, your pet can tank them just fine with just a quiescence. Seru mobs quad for 180-199ish, and he can tank them just fine. By rooting a mob and ensuring that your pet is within melee range of it, you WILL NOT be summoned, due to the special aggro rules of root. This is very handy. I can easily tank a seru mob with just quiescence to heal me, but it is a real pain because they tend to bash you when you try to reapply dots, thus extending the time it takes to kill them by a considerate amount.

Even against considerably weaker old world mobs, though, your pet's DPS just isn't impressive at all. He does about the same (maybe even better, due to AC factors) damage to pop mobs, but they just have so many more HP that his damage is really inconsequential. I want to say something like 20dps. I still get him out when I am in an exp group, though. The added damage, although it doesn't really do much, doesn't hurt, and you can still use him to distract an add mob for a few seconds (although he will most certainly die to mobs that triple for 500+).

So, basically, your pet is just a situationally useful tool that adds a little bit of damage.
weissblau
You can take down more than 2 greens at a time. I hunt LQ hopper pelts in Marus Seru for leather padding and would root all the hoppers I saw and sic dogdog. Dogdog stays busy with the one he's on, but adds the new one to his aggro list. This makes him very efficient, snapping from one to the next immediately. All I did was root, sic, and loot.

General tips with dogdog -

*) Buff with HP buffs+haste. Optional, STR buffs.

*) Always give two 1H weapons, even rusty daggers work.

*) SOW is helpful if you are going to want to pull him off the mob.

*) He can't hold aggro worth squat. Even one DOT will usually pull the mob off onto you. Root it or tank IN TEH FACE.

*) He has awesome regen. If he gets low and you don't want to heal him, root the mob and back him off for a rest while your DOTs chew on the mob.

*) Any mobs that hit you get on his aggro list. Hence, if you are grouped and don't want him to leave a mob to attack a parked one immediately, target the mob he's fighting, do BACK OFF and ATTACK in quick succession to clear his aggro list.

*) If he's got a mob on his aggro list and that mob drops below 20% health, he'll switch targets to the low one. This means if you're root-rotting one while he's gnawing another he just ruined your plans. Use the BACK OFF/ATTACK trick above to keep him from aggroing on the low health mob.

*) If you're grouped, turn taunt off. If you sic the dog on inc and he has a ways to run, the act of siccing the dog on a mob can cause the mob to attack YOU. I don't know exactly why this is, but I have seen it plenty of times.

Way back in the bad old days, when SOE made a number of changes to pets, it was discovered by someone that dogdog suddenly had the ability to dual-wield. He never had that ability before, and the immediate assumption was that it was a programming error on SOE's part.

So, people wanted to bring this very useful information to the masses--in other words, to Shaman's Crucible--but they were afraid that if they did so, SOE would read the site, and fix the error. So they instead developed a stealth campaign to pass along the information--something referred to as Project Alpha.

It became clear fairly soon, though, that SOE was aware of the change, and had either intended it, or just didn't want to fix it. Either way, the term Project Alpha stuck, and dogdog's dual-wield ability has been referred to in that way ever since.
