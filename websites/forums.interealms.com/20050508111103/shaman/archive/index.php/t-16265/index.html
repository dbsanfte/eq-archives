Here's some examples of unbalanced classes in the past:

Clerics around the time of Kunark and Velious were way too one dimensional and simple in concept to play. They needed 1. res 2. a fast heal 3. cheal 4. hp buffs. That's all that was needed for a cleric to play their class for the most party. They excelled at HP buffs and heals, but everything else like utility and debuffing and melee and nuking and dotting and everything else sucked horribly for them. What they got in Luclin to make up for this imbalance in their class is better spells to debuff like the MoR/MoK type of spells, they have bigger nukes, they have the ability to melee, as well as staying ahead of the pack in their own specialties of HP buffs and heals.

Druids around this time frame were also really in need of help. Druids and shamans healed the same for the most part, except that shaman got torpor eventually. Damage output of each is about on par if druid nukes vs shaman dotting. The major disparity that comes in is that druids get snare and ports for group desirability, where shaman get pets and malo and slows. The utility of a shaman far surpassed that of a druid. So to remedy this druids started getting better heals and better nukes to make up the disparity in the undesirability for their class compared to shamans.

After making these tweaks you have to actually ponder which class you want for your group for a certain role. You have to consider everything that the class will bring to the group, as well as the focus in which that class as at the primary role they'll be playing in the group. If you have situations where one class is better than another, but is worse in other situations, you have balance between them. The imbalance comes when one class is nearly always better than another class for a group spot. There really wasn't much reason to take a druid over a shaman for a healer spot in a group around the Kunark time. That's imbalance right there. One class needs to be weakened or another strengthened. And if you change either of those 2 classes, you have to still watch how they relate to the other classes. Like, if you try to close the gap between shaman and druids by nerfing slows or our debuffs for example, then the shaman vs enchanter comparison gets thrown off, so you have to nerf the enchanter in turn to make up for it, and so on. It's easier to make some small enhancements to a class to make up for disparities, and see how the changes affect the other classes.

Shaman have been balanced since the basic game came out. Our power wans and waxs a bit compared to other classes in some regards, but we are a good base for comparison against so many other classes because we can do so very much and it's why I like the shaman so very much.

Well, people say that slow mitigation hurts the shaman class, I don't really think it has.

Even when mobs mitigate, people want them slowed, and would rather have them slowed by a shaman than an enchanter or beastlord, part of the min/max thing.

Yes, an enchanter adds mind buffs, haste and crowd control. When they can use charm/dire charm, they can add DPS. But an enchanter gets more agro with tash than we do with malos, and is more fragile. How many enchanters do you know who have HP as their prime stat?

Yes, a beastlord adds stacking buffs, and DPS. But their slow is significantly worse than a shamans, and their buffs aren't as good. Plus beastlords aren't yet common enough to be an option much of the time.

A shaman adds buffs, some DPS with pet, debuffs and backup healing (sometimes main healing) all with the best slow.

Of course, they all stack pretty well - I love having both enchanter and beastlord in my group.

When I'm leaving an exp group, and ask who they want to replace me, most often I hear, "Look for another shaman". Sometimes they'll say "Enchanter or shaman". Very rarely has a group suggested beastlord - once when they'd already established there were no shamans or enchanters lfg, and once when one of the group members had a BL friend who was available.

Lets face it, groups love us. With PoP, people have come to realise how powerful slow is. Even in the face of slow mitigation, a group will want a slower. It's almost mandatory. This is why there are so many twink/bot shamans around, along with twink/bot clerics - when groups often fold because of a lack of a class, some people will create that class to 2-box with.

I *do* think we're balanced too much towards slow for most people's liking - it's the main thing we're invited to groups for, it's the main thing people know about shamans. I realise that most shamans don't like to be so one-dimensional.

With slow mitigation on many mobs, I don't think it would hurt for us to have slightly better damage output, nothing to make us challenge a "dps" class, nothing so powerful that we'd be invited to groups because of it, but something to help us feel we're contributing something more to each fight than just the slow.

So yes, I'd like slightly better damage nukes (I'm thinking something like 1100ish damage cold, 850ish poison, with slightly better efficiency than our current nukes, to put us in line with other support classes). And yes, I'd like slightly better melee, we are the "battle priest" with the best melee skill caps (I know many shamans are irritated that clerics can melee better than shamans).

I would like these things, but I will carry on playing without them, and I still don't think we're broken.

This is a bad application of statistics if I've ever seen one. The analysis is also terrible.

First off, a z-score is a measure of distance (and in which direction) from the mean of a Normal distribution (the bell curve). A z-score is (value - mean) / standard deviation.

This effect can be seen without the application of Normal distributions and statistics.
