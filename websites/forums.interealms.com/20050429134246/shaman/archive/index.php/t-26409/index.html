You've pretty much nailed how +def stuff works as far as I understand it. For each five levels of defense, you essentially gain 1 xp level of defensive capability. I don't know where the trade-off point is, is all. As with all these +skill vs +stats items, you don't want to sacrifice too many stats for +skill items. I don't know how much shaman tank at the upper levels, so it's up to you to strike a balance between better tanking ability and loss of other stats.

After all, just like in EQ, better tanking is only useful if you're being hit by melee; if you're eating AE's/spells then it don't do jack, whereas hps are always working for you.

Uggok
At level 60, each point of defense raises your block, dodge and parry by 0.4%. So each 25 points of defense increases your avoidance by almost 2% (very nice) and improves your shield block by 1% (very lame).

Defense also supposedly reduces crits against you, and a substantial amount of anecdotal evidence supports that. However, nobody knows the formula for that.

It is widely speculated that +defense has no effect in PvP.

+ Defense is nice, but it is nowhere near as good as raising your effective level defensively (which would be insanely overpowered, considering the fact that warriors with +90 (or higher) defense are not that uncommon).
