PennyAntics
Ever since I found out about Rabid bear I had thought "wow thats an awesome idea, a wussy caster temporarily becoming a badass melee that does huge damage." Just like the Hulk! I wanted to be in an exp group and get hit by a brann militis and say "you wont like me when I'm angry!" and then just hit that button and floor all the rogues, wizards, and rangers in the room with my damage!

Then I learned the truth. Raid leaders didn't shout "SHAMANS USE RABID BEAR" along with "NECROS USE LIFEBURN" when they needed that extra DPS for a mob. Shaman everywhere mock the AA ability... Sony themselves mock the ability, and your AA points are better spent in alchemy mastery or innate charisma.

I realise this would never happen, because Sony would rather eat one of their own limbs than make a progressive change in everquest without putting a "Forbidden Dungeons of Sushi" label and a $50 charge on it. But I'm here to propose my ideal changes to Rabid Bear. In essence...

RABID BEAR 2.0!



Rabid Bear

Description:

Reuse time: 8:00:00

Requirements: level 59. Must spend 12 ability points in the Archtype Abilities. This ability turns you into a Rabid Bear, boosting all of your offensive capabilities. Lasts 5 minutes.

Statistics:

AA cost 3/6/9

level 1: 20% haste above haste cap, +150 atk
level 2: 30% haste above haste cap, +250 atk and double-attack.
level 3: 45% haste above haste cap, +400 atk, double-attack, and weapon damage bonus equivalent to that of equal level warrior.



Notes:
This makes it so, at the expense of 18 aa points a shaman can essentially put out the DPS of a rogue without backstab for 5 minutes every 8 hours.

Hastened Rabidity would be removed. For the sake of DPS melee classes who might feel threatened by Rabid Bear *cough*rangers*cough*, this ability would have a very long reuse time (the aformentioned 8 hours) and thus would be usable at most 3 times every real life day, typically no more than once in a raid. Instead it is replaced with this:



Feral Rabidity

Description

This is a passive ability; it does not need to be activated.

Requirements: Rabid Bear at level 3. This further improves your offensive capabilities when using Rabid Bear.

Statistics

AA cost: 3/6/9

level 1: Adds a chance to score crippling blows while in Rabid Bear form.
level 2: Adds a proc, 'maul' to all attacks while in Rabid Bear form. Proc information: 200 dd, magic based reistance check at -500 (lure).
level 3: Increases chance to proc maul substantially, and gives a small chance to score a critical proc.




well that's what I would like to see for Rabid bear. I think it would be awesome to, for 5 minutes, become a very very powerful meleer... a force to be reckoned with. And for 36 AA points and to be able to only use it every 8 hours I really see no imbalance with these changes. But like I said, there is no way in hell that Sony will ever do this. *shrug* I had my fun in writing this thread just imagining heh.

Xev_Grammar
For what it's worth, I grouped in droga last night, and a rogue had a realtime dps parser. When I was playing around with rabid bear, I was achieving maybe 30-50 dps (really streaky though) with a 33/30. A 65 SK with a 1hs 28/25 was achieving 75 dps roughly. A 55ish rogue was achieving 70 dps. A 55ish ranger was achieving 55 dps.

Not that great, but it was ok.. and fun nonetheless-- expect to be a lot less inefficient and less effective when you use it, unless you're an uber with Staff of Eternal Flames or something.

If you want real dps, Blood of Saryrn already gives you 47-48 dps by itself, but you'll get agro. Tears of Saryrn will give you anywhere between zero to 289 dps-- note that if you even just hit something twice with Tears of Saryrn, you're already beating the Blood of Saryrn dps and getting less agro.

But since it's hard to Tears a mob while engaging in melee (it's possible-- target a friend on the other side near to the mob and rain him), you can Blood of Saryrn then rabid bear, achieving maybe 77-98 dps while getting beaten up.

My suggestion for defensive Rabid Bear is maybe give us 75 parry 75 riposte 75 block and make that rabid bear proc into a lifetap dot (so that our dps isn't that great, but we become more defensive in the case of an emergency). Instead of 50 CR maybe have 25 resists across the board.

Or maybe an offensive Rabid Bear-- increase our 1hb, 2hb, piercing, and hand to hand to 225. Also 150 dual wield if you're wielding nothing in both hands. Maybe make it really strong meleewise, but it will double your mana costs (or lengthen spellcasting time) whenever you cast stuff. Then make refresh time a lot smaller. Or maybe increase our offense somehow and lower our defense (like D&D berserker rage)-- put us under the effects of torpor for 30 seconds after rabid bear is gone.
