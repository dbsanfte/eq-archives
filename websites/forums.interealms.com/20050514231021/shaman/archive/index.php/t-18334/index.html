Lets do the math...

I'll use Bane as an example.

I will cast Bane a total of 100 times. That would be a total of 210,000 damage in 500 ticks.

If 7% of those dots are crit'd for double damage, that would mean that 7 out of 100 dots would be doing 4200dmg as opposed to 2100dmg for the normal dot. This would be an increase of 14700dmg, bringing the overall total to 224700.

Now lets say that they check for a crit in every tick. If 7% out of 500 ticks were crit'd, that would be 35 crits in that time. A crit tick would equal 840dmg as opposed to 420dmg. This would be an increase of 14700dmg, bringing the overall total to 224700 once again.

This can basically be summed up by a little rule that we learned in 5th grade called the distributive property, but I thought I'd spell it out for the people that don't remember that.

Whether it crits the whole dot, or crits per tick, the damage is the same... why does it really matter?

Thunderwolfjr
OK Piglet and Dissonant, let me use my example:

You cast a 2000 hp dot that runs 10 ticks, or 200 hp per tick.

You have a 10% chance to crit.

If the crit applies to the entire dot, then you have a 10% chance to do double damage or 4000 hp of damage over 10 ticks, 400/tick. Which is how the Wiz dd crit works, all or nothing.

If, on the other hand, the crit is applied to each tick, then you have a 10% chance of getting a crit on the first tick, a 10% chance to get a crit on the second tick, and so on.

Therefore, instead of a 10% chance to double the total damage of the dot, you will (in this example where you have 10 ticks) on average get one tick per dot to crit. This will result in one tick doing 400 hp damage, for a total of 2200 hp of damage over the 10 ticks.

Granted, if you cast 10 of these dots, you will generate (on average) an extra 2000 hp of damage either way. However, as Giac said, it is burst dps that we lack, not damage over time. Therefore, in order to address this short-coming, I would prefer the chance to have the crit applied the total dot damage based on one roll (which I'm sure they will NOT do) vs. every tick.

But, that's just me. Do you follow my point now?

The problem with a DoT focus is two fold.

One, it will not truely increase our burst DPS. In fact, the only way it can increase a shaman's burst DPS in any meaningful way is if that shaman *only* dots when burst damage is needed. Needless to say, that hypothetical senario hurts a shaman's overall dps far more than it helps.

Two, agro. DoT agro is based on 3 things. Initial agro upon cast, poison/desease counters, and additional agro with each tick. DoT crits will increase agro per tick, which is not a very good thing for shamans given our already ungodly high DoT agro and our inability to shed agro short of doing nothing or call upon another class for help.

To recap, DoT crit AA is a good way to add to our sustained DPS, but not without some sort of agro reducer such as a built in one, or SCS.
