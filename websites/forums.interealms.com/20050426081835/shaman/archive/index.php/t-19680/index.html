Jinheim
Stacking Bane, Anathema, BoU, and BoS, expect a doomfire to take about 4-5 minutes to die, then about 1 minute to replenish your mana. Not bad for 14% a pop.

There are two places to kill doomfires. The first one I used is outside the castle, up on the south (?) zone wall. It's the zone wall past the little lava pit and behind the big rock. Nothing will roam back there.

The infinitely better place to solo doomfires, though, is in what is commonly referred to as the "picnic tables" on my server. It is the area where the fennin trigger spawns. The trigger usually isn't up, but if he is, he is easy to avoid. Everything in castle2 has a very small aggro radius, completely opposite from the identical-looking mobs in SRT. To get to picnic tables, just wait for the roamer at the entrance to the castle to path away, then make a break for it. As you come through the door you will see a raised platform with doomfires on it. Go around it to the left, and then run straight with a small building on your left, and the platform on your right. Try to stay pretty much in the center between them or you might aggro them. Once you get past that part, it is easy. None of the other huts have guards in them. Just run past the platform and go through the second archway in to the large open area. You are now at picnic tables - the best known exp spot in the game for a soloing shaman.

If you are especially ballsy, you can solo the magma guardians that guard the bridge behind charm birds. Every now and then when I am in one of those moods, I will stop in and kill them for an hour or so. They have a lot more HP (~60k) than doomfires and give you less xp (11%). On top of that, they can hit you five times in one round, not counting a bash/kick, and hit for 1150ish. They are also fairly resistant to disease, so VP is unreliable. I use paralyzing earth and stand as far away from my rooted target as I possibly can. Why would you solo these? They drop solidified magma and metallic substance for pofire tradeskills. Solidified magma is pretty much the only hard to get component for the ID4 pottery item, so if you get one of those to drop then you're in the money.

Finally, the best possible exp you are going to milk out of pofire is going to be in a group at tables. Get a necro or druid to snare the mob, then you load it up with dots and kite it in a circle. Have the other members of your group (casters preferred - melees suck at this) unload on the mob. I have yet to have anyone take aggro from me unless they were specifically chaining their aggro spells.

Korsib
I just took my first try at soloing tables after grouping there a few times. It's pretty much perma camped on my server so I was lucky to find an opening. If the Guardian of Doomfire isn't up, I would strongly suggest using the bridges for your killing. When your root breaks, it gives you tons of moving space. And as long as people in zone know you won't have someone coming and taking your camp.
I don't have VP yet, but I was able to keep the mobs almost perma rooted with my ornate legs. These things are a godsend for any root rot soloer. The way I did it, is I would basically chain cast the root inbetween my spells, as I dotted, rooted, then would canni/root/canni/root until the dots wore off. Although it seems excesive, the cast time of the root is almost the same as your recast time, plus there's no recast on the ornate legs. The dots I used were Saryrn, Ultor, Bane, and Anathema. Every kill took less than the time for slow to wear off, so I'd say 4-6 min depending on the mob. This is also with BA4 bracer from a mage. Med time was usually 1 min or so. The pulls are very easy, even mobs that are in the same building (two buildings in picnic area), can be singled easily. With the exp after the solo nerf, I was getting 14-16% per kill.

I've also done the magma golems before, and using the same rooting technique you can keep them in the same place almost the entire fight. The fights do take a bit longer than the tables mobs. Usually 7-9 minutes depending on how many times root breaks. Usually 13-14% a kill on these guys. The pull can be a little hard once you've killed the wanderers, as the ones next to each other on the bridge can't be singled. Also, consider this camp pointless once charm birds are up, there's just not enough room to pull if they are.
Rtugok
Get KEI if you can. I prefer it to VoQ because of the duration. Also if you can snag a 9 that's even better. Have a mage summon you a BA 4 bracer and head to fire. Personally I use a Miserable Bauble from Saryrn in Torment. Sow/Lwv. yourself and invis up. Keep gate memmed too. Run over the first castle wall and go downtairs and out the front. Don't get too close to the two birds if they're up. Head right and go along the wall to the second castle. There are two guards that patroll the gate, and they are easy to spot. There are also two static guards by the door. I usually wait til the two patrolling guards are farther out than the static ones and then i run in. Head to the back door and if you dont have aggro youre good to go. If you do have aggro but only one mob kill it if you can or gate and try again.

Once youre set up get a nice spellset up. I use (If I recall) Malicious Decay, Malosinia, Breath of Ultor, Blood of Saryrn, Bane, Anathema, Quiescence, Canni 4. I also use my epic because hey, free dps is free dps. The aggro is small here. There are 9 mobs i think. There are two houses with two mobs in each. You can target the left mob and pull him through the left side and vice versa. Get as far away as you can and pull with Malicious Decay. Hit VP asap and throw te mob where you think is best. Hop on your mount and sinia till it sticks. It isn't usually hard to do w/o Malos, but occasionally they resist like 10 in a row. Stack on the DoT's and watch the exp flow in. I'm still trying to debate whether or not it is faster with or without Anathema. It eats mana pretty fast and causes me to have to canni more after the fight than when I don't use it. Also Critical Affliction makes this go faster. For those fire flagged you can kill many things with this method, and I think I heard frog in PoS are one of those mobs.

Happy hunting, and may fire bring you hours of soloing fun.
