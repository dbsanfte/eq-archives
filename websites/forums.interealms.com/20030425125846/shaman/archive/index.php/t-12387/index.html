Here's the thing with mounts (I'm talking about the cheap no-frills variety here):

1) They don't give you sitting hp regen ever. (I just read this in one of the recent threads -- I previously thought the opposite was true).

2) They don't give you sitting mana regen when moving, fighting or casting spells. (This is right, right?)

So basically, you have to be sitting still and doing nothing to get the regen benefits of a mount. But if I'm still and doing nothing, ain't I gonna be sitting down in that situation anyway, and getting the same mana regen rate as a horse/lizard?

As far as I can see, the only advantage the mount gives me is that I don't attract aggro from sitting down. But then again, I would get the sit-down hp regen rate by really sitting down, rather than being on a horse. And isn't hp regen more important than mana regen for a shaman because of cannabalize? Am I missing something here?

Mana regen is actually more important than HP regen. The reason shaman are so interested in HP regen is so we can convert it to mana. Having a strong mana regen is just cutting out the middle man.

Mana regen items are very expensive or very difficult to get and not nearly as effective for the cost. We can control our own HP regen with our regen line of spells and there are a few items with good HP regen that are available to a shaman. A good example is the fungi. 15 hp per tick HP regen. Go to the bazaar and try to find a comarable mana regen item (FT 7.5) for any price.

The horse is the best mana regen item money can buy.
