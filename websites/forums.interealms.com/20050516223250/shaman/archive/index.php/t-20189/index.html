taggletoe
I just think the game has progressed to burst DPS and burst healing. We're never going to do fast damage but our healing per second has fallen too far behind the other two priest classes. Somebody please check my math and assumptions below:

Tnarg(Shaman) 1300 hp heal/5 seconds(cast and recast) = 260 HP/sec
Nature's Infusion(Druid) 1750 hp/5 seconds = 350 HP/sec
Supernatural Light(Cleric) 2750hp/5 seconds = 550 HP/sec

If you factor AHA3(16%) and 20% healing focus shaman fall even further behind the pack:
Shaman 1300+208+260=1768 or 353.6 HP/sec
Druid 1750+280+350=2380 or 476 HP/sec
Cleric 2750+440+550=3740 or 748 HP/sec

and just for fun throw in the GoD spells:
Shaman(no direct upgrade) 1300+208+260=1768 or 353.6 HP/sec
Sylvan(Druid) 2100+336+420=2856 or 571.2 HP/sec
Holy Light(Cleric) 3275+524+655=4454 or 890.8 HP/sec

For a game where it is becoming increasingly mandatory to have a secondary healer in a group the shaman class is being left in the dust. We will still be a desirable class when a secondary healer is not required because of slow. That was the utility I always saw with my shaman is that I could fill two roles in a challenging environment. I could slow and in a pinch I could supplement healing. I just feel as a progress further I am slowly losing that edge that I once had.
