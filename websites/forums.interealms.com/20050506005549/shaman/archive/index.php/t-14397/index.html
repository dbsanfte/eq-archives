Chutoi
Another problem might be the router software. If it's dropping every 2 hours, it sounds like 1 of 3 things. 1--your isp automatically drops people so that afk people don't take up bandwidth and your router is re-establishig connection. 2--your router is set somewhere in the config's to re-establish or at least drop every 2 hours. 3--you have a memory leak in your router software and it's filling up at 2 hours, and then it promptly resets.

Of the three, I'd guess (3) is the problem. Try upgrading (or perhaps downgrading) the software in the router. A similar thing happens with the lynksys routers every 15 minutes if you use the updated drivers.

Taladaen
Another problem that may be happening if you're losing connections at 2 hours exactly is your router is probably configured to assign IPs via DHCP with a 2 hour lease period. Every 2 hours it's telling your computers to renew their IPs and get a new one.

Check in Admin options for your router and look up how it is assigning IP addresses, try increasing lease time if that's available, otherwise you'll have to change the computers to a static IP.

Rafeeky
I'm gonna go out on a limb here but your routers may have collision or bad packet counters on them. Many do, and when it reaches a certain number, it cause the machine to power cycle or simply reset. It is very possible that some of the data being recieved from Sony is less than satisfactory for these machines and the counters are filling up. It possibly explains why many have the same estimation of time it takes to LD. If they are similiar hardware with similiar buffers, they would fill up at the same rate after a reset while playing. Many times these counters can only be manually adjusted or turned off through the serial interfaces of the devices.
