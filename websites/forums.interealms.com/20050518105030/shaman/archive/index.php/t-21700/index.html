Macnair
So is VP1 just useless or what?VP3 is almost perfect for soloing diakus in Plane of Tactics.

Pull with disease debuff, then hit VP. In maybe a hundred encounters with diakus, I have had a resist on VP once. One time. Only once.

VP3 lasts for a bit over two minutes. It refreshes in exactly two minutes. Thus you can cast it again before it breaks. Because VP has a timer, you can tap the key before it is refreshed, and it will tell you how long before you can use it again. So you are never surprised: you know that it is going to refresh 12 seconds from now, and you stand up and cast it as it refreshes.

In all those encounters it has broken early one time. Just once.

So. VP is not quite perfect. It has failed to be perfect twice in ... oh I don't know, 80 encounters? Maybe 110 encounters? Somewhere in that range.

As others have said, it is also quite useful in LDoN for auxiliary crowd control Cast disease debuff, cast VP, mob is punted off to the side and parked for 2 minutes. Pay attention to which add you punt: pick a melee only one (healers will be quite capable of healing even though they are punted off to the side). Also make sure your group knows that if they nuke or melee or sic their pet on your parked mob, they will break the root effect early. With VP3 plus slow and quiescence, you'll soon find it easy to crowd control two adds in normal LDoN: you VP one and tank the other, while your group dispatches the third one.

VP1 and VP2 have the same characteristics; they just don't last as long.

Just remember: always disease debuff first, and VP will stick for full duration on many many mobs.
