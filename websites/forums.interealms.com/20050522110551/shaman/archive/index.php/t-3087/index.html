BlargX
Don't go worrying about foraging when you have a shaman -- shamans summon food and water. :) It's less of a hassle than relying on forage, that's for sure. Besides, shamans are strong enough to carry regular food, if that's what you like, and just give it to your monk when he's hungry. Anyway, you should get at least one character high enough in baking to make fish rolls anyway, which weigh 0.1 and are, what, a banquet or a feast, I forget...lasts forever, costs almost nothing to make, good stuff. And getting baking that high is extremely cheap in terms of cost -- just a few hundred plat. (And...you can make stat food...)

On ogres, I'm a 60 ogre shaman, and it's true that at the endgame, ogres have the only stats and abilities that really count -- unstunnability from the front and more hitpoints because of greater STA. (Regen is extremely trivial once you get torpor and canni 5. I have a fungi but don't wear it anymore.)

But, ogre is by far the hardest shaman to play. Shamans thrive on regen (pre-torpor/canni 5), and ogres don't have it. Neither do barbarians, right? But...ogres have a 15% experience penalty whereas barbarians have only 5%. Don't believe it when people tell you that the exp penalties don't matter -- they are huge and very noticeable. Over 60 levels, I always noticed people getting more bubbles than me in camps -- sometimes it seemed like a helluva lot more. 15% adds up to a LOT over your lifetime. So, unless you are absolutely sure you're going to play your shaman all the way to 60 and do whatever it takes to get torpor, ogre would be the last choice I would recommend. Not having regen is a big set-back before then.

Now, if you can get yourself an iksar regen chestplate VERY early(6k on my server), then it won't be so bad. But a real newbie won't have anywhere near that kind of money for a very, very long time. If you're rich enough to buy a fungi, play whatever race you want, because you'll kick ass no matter how much of a chump you are(unless you play like a druid with too much root/dotting).

For the average guy, troll shaman is probably the best because of regen and the ability to use the jaundiced bone bracer(JBB), which is an incredible item, and one even 60 shamans can use every day. Their wisdom is lousy, but the regen makes up for it. Their fire resistance is 20 points lower than anyone else's, but you don't always fight fire mobs.

Iksars have wood-elf stats, plus regen, and they can't use the JBB. At 60 the innate armor bonus that iksars get works out to about 40 AC extra for free. They have high wisdom, but that's not all that important. In the endgame, everybody has high wisdom anyway. So they're a very mixed bag. Handbag, that is.

Iksar monk(hooray for regen!) and troll shaman(ditto!) would be the best combo, unless you wanted at least one of your duo to be someone who could easily buy/sell and avoid faction work. In that case, barbarian becomes more attractive, but I'd definitely still stick with the iksar monk.

And level either in the iksar areas or in the warrens off toxx forest, both of which are fantastic for experience and can lead to some good faction work(too lengthy to get into here).
