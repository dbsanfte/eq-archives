If you are intent on soloing its going to be slow going at the post 60 pre 65 level. My recomendation is Droga. There is an exp mod in the zone that will help to eliminate a good portion of your racial exp penalty. The cash there is also good, as are most of the drops. You might not yet be able to solo some of the nameds that drop the 10-30K items, but you will definatly be able to take some of them.

Soloing there at 63 I didnt get any named but still did 63 in about 3 days soloing about 3 or 4 hours a night (only 5% exp penalty for Barbs) and made about 5K in coin drops alone.

Other than that, portions of Seb are still blue to you, but not very good exp, as it is 5 expansions old in terms of exp per kill. Good cash, and the occasional good drop, but IMHO not worth the effort.

LDoN exp is also good if you bought the expansion. That is also a good way to earn points to spend on gear upgrades.

Soloing in Tier 1 planes really isnt very good exp anymore. Much faster even if you get a group in Tier 1 than trying to solo. Groups in PoI are proably your best bet as long as they are willing to travel around a bit. Camping at the ZO is so BORING...

Spritabe
If you just want to rebuild your skills solo, old world dungeons are quite fun!

Seb zone in is quite forgiving at 62, I'd think. Unless you forget to root them at the end, in which case it'll be an exciting run to the zone out... Not much XP here, though your CC skills will be tested.

Nurga is also fun and you can get some XP while at it.

Droga is the big brother of Droga, quite fun but I find it gets old quickly and you can get in big trouble if something gates on you.

Maybe hit the giants in Permafrost? Haven't been there since level 60 to farm a bit, but I remember they come in a wide variety.

Other than that, I'd say get some HoH / BoT / PoV groups and explain that you are just getting back in the groove. I'd recommend opening with whatever is your currently best verson of the MalXX line of spells until it sticks, then land slow. (This will also give the tank a bit of time to establish aggro).
The first 10 or so kills are usually about getting used to how the other 5 members are playing and establishing a steady rythm. When you feel you have the "basics" of slowing in the group down, the resist rate of the mobs, what you can land and not, and so on, you can start adding in the odd nuke and even do some crowd control on multi-pulls.
EQ is a pretty easy game, and it doesn't take much practice to get used to it again.

Good luck!
