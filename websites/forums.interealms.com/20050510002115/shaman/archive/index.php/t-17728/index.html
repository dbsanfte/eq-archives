Well, I got a chance to give the 3-boxing thing a try this weekend.

Friday night, I trotted out my 56 warrior, 56 druid, and 58 shaman to try a Ruj dungeon (the missions are given in E Commons tunnels). I got a mission, ran to the dungeon entrance, and buffed up before heading in.

I ran my druid and shaman in, but couldn't zone in with the warrior. I've done adventures before with that account and in fact that character. But I couldn't zone in. I could see the entrance, but couldn't get into the dungeon. I tried logging and even rebooting, with no luck. Frustrated, I gave up for the night and did some 2-box pulling just for kicks with the shaman and druid. It worked out ok but was tough with multiple pulls.

Saturday morning I woke up wth a clearer head (had been a long day at work on Friday), and remembered that I had re-installed EQ on my warrior's machine. Apparently the LDoN download isn't automatic; I went into the advanced button on the launch screen and checked LDoN, and the auto-patcher downloaded the dungeons. So, that was my problem.

Anyway, I tried again on Saturday morning. I went to the same mission type, Ruj. Visibility is poor in that theme - there's a bit of fog and mist that make it impossible to see very far (unlike MM, which I've done several of with full pick-up groups and had excellent visibility).

I zoned in, fully buffed, and summoned dog-dog. It was then that I realized I hadn't picked up any dog toys. Not even any rusty daggers. Doh. No alpha bet. Undaunted, I got to work. I pulled with the warrior, who was using a Greatstaff of Thunder, which gives a very nice mix of taunt (stun proc) and dps (31/30 ratio). A very nice warrior-only weapon so commonly dropped it's now available for 200pp or less in the bazaar. Too bad there's nothing comparable for my poor monk for less than 10k :(

Anyway, warrior taunt is pretty much broken, as those of you familiar with the class are aware. I'd throw a slow and druid snare on each pull, and sick dogdog on to help. Generally, when slow landed the shaman would get agro, but I could get it back fairly quickly. I'd either dot with the druid's level 56 dot or the shaman's 58 (BON I think I was using). Stacking two dots with the shaman would have created unmanageable agro, and my warrior is pretty well geared (about 4900 hp with no AAs), so I'd much prefer he take the beating.

Anyway, the advice I got on which dungeon to try seemed right on. Ruj dungeons have narrow hallways with very few large rooms (compared to MM, my only frame of reference). I got mostly single pulls or doubles, which I could handle easily. I had to gate out a couple of times when I got 4, but those could have been avoided with better familiarity with my surroundings, I believe.

My first mission, I wasn't worried about the type of mission, because I had no illusions that I would be able to come close to succeeding. So I think I had an assasinate. Needless to say, while I was getting the hang of things, I never even saw the named mob. I'm sure he never spawned (I've heard they only spawn when 60% of the mobs are dead). The second mission, I picked a collection. 28 thingamabobs. I had pretty good luck with drops, had to gate once due to a 4-pull, and failed. But I did get the booby prize with plenty of time to spare.

As for loot: very nice when you don't have to split the drops 6 ways :) I made about 1700pp in the two missions, and I didn't even bother running the first one to completion. As usual, no augments dropped (I've NEVER been in a group where one dropped - do they only come from chests?). I also found several research items I put up for sale on my vendor, as well as some tradeskill items. I would put the entire loot total at well over 2000pp when all is said and done. I saw no named mobs in either mission, so nothing special dropped.

As for experience: I regret not paying better attention to exactly how I made out. I think the second mission, where I went the full 120 minutes (90 + 30 minute extension for booby prize), I made about 2 blue bubbles on my 58 shaman (who is an ogre and has that miserable 20% penalty). I'm funneling 10% into AA, and made about 1/2 blue bubble of AA in that second adventure. I didn't even pay attention to what I got on the other two characters, but it was probably a bit better due to their relatively lower level (56 vs 58).

I should note that several factors, of my own creation, worked against me. I don't generally get KEI, unless it comes from a guildy.
