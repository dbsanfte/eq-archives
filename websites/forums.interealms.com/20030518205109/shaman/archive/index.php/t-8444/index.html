Jinheim
A moktor is like 1/3 of a yeldema, and yeldema is a puss. It is easily doable. You have all the tools you need, all you need is the technique.

Here's what you do:

Pull with malosini (you didn't mention malo so i assume you're missing it)
Slow it (set a timer or look at a clock. You need to reslow in 5 minutes).
Hit him with epic, poxx, bane, ebolt (if your mana is a bit lacking, skip ebolt)*.
Torpor yourself.
Canni over and over again until it is time to reapply a dot (this is very important. When soloing you ALWAYS have to be cannibalizing unless you are casting something else or you are very low on health).
Reapply dots.
Torpor yourself.
Rince and repeat until it is dead.

Tip: Back yourself in to a corner near the ME zoneline. You won't get pushed around and its harder to be interrupted that way.

* Damage dealing is really the only tricky part of soloing a mob like this. A shaman with torpor can tank a mob that hits for 400 (probably a lot more, my last big solo kill was stomples before PoP came out) indefinately, but sooner or later, you have to kill it or it will kill you. If you have a large mana pool, then you can simply apply every dot you have and burn it down quickly, ending the fight before you go oom. If you have a smaller mana pool, though, you might go oom from dots before the thing is dead. Here's my magelo http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=383384. As you can see from the screenshot background, my gear when I first killed yeldema was pretty crappy. 2320 hp buffed and 161 wis. Back then, it took me about 15 minutes to kill yeldema. I used a few dots in the beginning, but after that all my mana was for healing myself and keeping a little extra in case my reslow attempts were resisting. The only damage being done to yeldema was epic, pet, a little JBBing, and DS potion. Now, with my new gear, I simply apply plague poxx ebolt and bane, canni5, heal myself, dot and nuke some more, and finish the fight in about 6 minutes. I have the mana to do this now, whereas I didn't before.

That being said, your offensive tactics when you first start killing moktors should be to primarily rely on epic dot and pet, while you use most of your mana for keeping your health up. Moktors can be a real pain on interrupts and a few interrupted torpors means that you might have to tnargs yourself, which is a serious mana hog.

Good luck.
BlargX
I always pull with malo. These guys hit too hard and fast to be messing around with resisted slows. Slow virtually always sticks after a malo; at the most I take a hit or three.

The trick is to root after that. Every time root breaks, you will get summoned, unless you're lucky or a great deal of aggro has been built up on somebody else you're with. (or someone with low hitpoints is in the vicinity -- anyone's mob will charge anyone else in that case)

After I root, I malosini -- I want my DOTS to stick, but even more, I want my roots to stick! Then I swap in my DOTs; if duoing, I swap in torrent of poison somewhere. Somewhere in there I put on a torpor. I have ALT-F3 as my very well memorized key combination for casting root, so I don't have to mouse over to my spell gems. If torpored when summoned, after root is on, I click topor off and run away.

While I've been getting hit, doggy has been munching the mob. I keep him hasted and HP buffed, and with STR too.

Doing this, it's only a matter of time till I can do what I really do there almost all the time, which is not solo, but duo with my bot. Either monk or new warrior bot. Once the melee takes the aggro, I can torrent of poison almost at will. Sometimes the mob will be not quite dead when slow wears off, so I'll have to reslow. Just depends on the luck.

I really recommend malosini on these guys, as well as malo. What makes it all work is getting root to stick. Once rooted, they will not summon you if a melee(or pet) is attacking them unless you cause a huge amount of aggro somehow, perhaps by putting in so much more damage than your pet or melee partner that even when root is intact, your aggro is too high -- or if you break root.

The robes are bad news because their snare cancels out your SOW, so even when it drops, if you're not alert, you won't be able to run to zone quickly if you haven't noticed SOW is gone. And the snare can last a long time. They tend to reapply it instantly. Also -- they backstab! Sometimes for darn good amounts. They're fast too, so run backwards. P.S. -- many of them see invis, and they have a very big aggro range. Gantrus are better to fight, as their aggro range is really tiny, and only the nameds can see invis.

The shaman gantrus - shojen and shojilen -- cast bane of nife, slow you and doggy and friends, debuff you, etc. I think of them as "spoiler" mobs and tend to let other people kill them. ;)

By the way, park a plate tank there if at all possible -- yours or your friend's or whatever. If you've been thinking of starting one one day, start it now, and park it in UP entrance. Gantru moktors can drop nice plate, including AC 21 legs, for instance. There is also Radulkan armor from, of course, Gantru Radulkan(the very resistant but otherwise easy mob -- low DPS). There are a number of pieces. The plate BP has like 15 sta and 100 hitpoints, plus lots of other stats; AC on it is not superb, but considering the hitpoints on it are raid-level, it's well worth hanging around to load up a twink with this no-drop plate stuff. There is also other stuff around for one, including various droppable items that are great for either selling or twinking.

The corrupted elysian elders drop the Death Watcher's Shroud, which is a nice druid (and other) shoulder item, and the Death Mask of the Elysians, a nice mask that I wear now, as does my druid. Unfortunately, these used to sell like hotcakes, but barely ever sell on my server now. Be careful here; the corrupted elysians, like all elysians, see invis, but pulling them is more dangerous than other stuff there, as they can chain cast DD's, root and snare you, and debuff you too. Being snared and rooted and debuffed and chain-DD'd is not exactly easy to handle. They can get rid of 2 or 3k hitpoints very quickly with their-chain casting. They tend to hang back and just blast you till they run out of mana; if you want them to follow you, often you have to melee engage them, and coax them back to somewhere safe by running very close to them. There are pathing problems around the druid corner too -- they sometimes run right up to it and then turn and run away; in general, you have to do a lot of coaxing to get mobs over there. Once you do, though, you're safe until some druid kites on top of you and dies, leaving you with his overpulls.

Look for the hivefiend, shak dathor warlord, too. You can get nice stuff off either of these; sometimes it sells, sometimes it doesn't.

My best advice for you if you want to make money, though, is to get a second account and PL a melee to take the hits for you and leave you with a far greater margin of safety and DPS per minute. You don't even have to have two computers, either....

I've made 100's of thousands of plat in UP; it's well worth putting up with the noob flood there at least sometimes, to try to get some loot. Good luck to you!
