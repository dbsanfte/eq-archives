Well I have been working on these new bow recipes that came out with the last patch. I'll admit the idea is cool. I'll even admit that the recipe and the work behind it has merit.

However I am disapointed in the ocst vs reward and the sheer dificulty in making the highest end bow even with Max skill.

Let me break down for you the cost of this weapon based on Prices on E Marr.

Mithril Alloy Rod: 3181 pp estimated Value

Large Brick of Acrylia - 50 pp if bought from a Vender. 20 pp or so from a player and is a substantial time investment to farm a handful of these from the various zones. Took me 4 hours of nonstop killing in Tenebrous to get 3 Bricks. Estimated value of 35 pp
Enchanted LArge brick of Mithril - 27 pp for the rick plus the cost of Enchanting. Those in a guild can probably get this enchanted for free. Others may have to pay a fee. For intents and purposes lets say this is Valued at 35 pp also.
Celetsial Temper - Given the new Tradeskill recipes BD's are now worth in the ball park of 3 K. Finding words and Runes of the appropriate type is not as easy as it sounds once your level passes the areas where these normally drop. Have found a few on Venders costing me 6 pp per word. Solvent is cheap, couple gold. Jar of acid is also pretty cheap, about 6 gold. Estimated value per Temper is 3100 pp.
Smithy Hammer - 14 pp and is consumed with each combine. Note: I think this is a bug as the by product of smithing the rod is the Mithril Knife. I think that someone screwed up and your supposed to getthe Hammer back instead.


Mithril String - 17 pp estimated Value

Silk String - couple pp bought from Bowyer Vender
Oak Bark tanin - made from Oak bark and water. Cheap parts for sure but significant time investment required to gather the bark for multiple attempts. Estimated value of 6 pp per tanin.
Small Brick of Mithril - About 8 pp and change from Vender


Mithril Knife - Small brick, 2 molds, and water - Truncating this one as it is a by product of the alloy rod anyway. If you made it yourself estimated value of 10 pp

Fletching kit - Same as above. Small brick, 3 molds, and water estimated value of 12 pp.

Cams - 30 pp each. Bow requires 2

Branch of Planar oak - Requires many hours of time in Plane of Growth foraging. Took me on the area of 8 hours to foarge 3 branches and on average over the last week has taken me almost 3 hours per branch. Significant time investment and more considering once inside the plane we have no means to leave without help or with a Gate potion. Estimated Vlaue based on time invested and dificulty associated with the aquisition is about 1500 pp.

Total cost for the materials alone for the Blessed Faydark Thunderbolt - 7,780 pp

For those who are curious while possible I would not even think about trying to make this bow with a skill less than 230 fletching. The failure rate is way too high. You can infer then the cost of raising your skill from 200 to 230. This will cost you thousands of pp easily. It is not uncommon to blow 5 K for 3 skill points. Once attained this no longer figures into the value of the weapon but it IS a one time cost potentially totally 20 K pp as a ballpark figure.

The cost of raising Brewing and smithing to the appropriate levels is much less but can cost you around 2 K all told. Again this is a one time cost.

Alright fair enough. However, even with the highest skill of 250 the failure rate is horrid. Out of 20 attempts you MIGHT get lucky and make 2 bows with Max skill and max dex etc etc. Chances are you'll only get one bow. Add that cost up in materials alone.

With that kind of failure rate I doubt I would ever even think about trying to make this bow beyond a few for myself and my guildmates. I cannot ever see this being a market item and this is why - Given that kind of monetary investment the minimum I would charge someone to have me make this per atempt is 25 K with 50% upfront fail or suceed just to cover expenses and time. If I succeed I would collect the other 50 % but the initial payment I would have to keep fail or succeed. With the failure rate that is a lot of money that a player would have to fork over. Is it worth it? In it's current state no I do not think it is. I certainly wouldn't be willing to pay anyone that much money with a very good chance I will not see anything for it.

This recipe will fall by the waysidew I think once the initial novelty wears off.

How do we fix this so that people would be willing to pay for the bow?

I have a few ideas.


Give Fletchers an arrow combination that is mostly or all store bought that we can use to practice skill to 250. Use parts that are semi costly still but not as outragous as what we have now. Acrylia arrows are not cutting it. The drop rate on the Small bricks is too low to make this viable. While it works for short stints of fletching your just not able to collect the volume of arrow shafts you need to gain skill points from.
Increase the stats of the 4th bow for sure and possibly the 3rd as well. 4th bow is 30 38 250 range 5 str 5 dex 5 agil 5 wis 10 FR CR MR 50 HP 50 Mana.

Not a bad bow by any means, but when compared with the equivilent Smithing BP for High Elves it doesn't return as much as that BP does for the investment. That BP is bar noine on par with the best BP's in the game for some of the classes that can wear it and only slightly behind the best BP's in the game for the others. Our bow on the othe rhand falls way down the totem pole on the list of bows that it can compoete with.

If this bow were 33 dmg 36 delay ac 5 250 Range 5 str 5 dex 5 wis Hp 50 Mana 50 10 FR CR MR then I believe people would be willing to pay the outragous prices for this Bow.

This would put the bow on Par with Bow of doom, Slightly Behind Windstriker and Primal and on par or a little better than AoW bow. making this about the 3rd best bow in the game which I think is where it belongs.
