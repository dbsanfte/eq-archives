deria
Since the release of PoP, I have found that in my average experience grouping I am often in very short fights.

The sequence of events is usually something along the lines of:

1. Malo
2. Slow
3. Bane Of Nife

The DoT may or may not finish before the end of the fight. It usually does, but regardless of whether or not it does my overall damage contribution is fairly low. Without my coldain military wristguard, that would be 1669 damage. With it, it *might* (due to a rounding issue with the short duration) by 1886.

There is no time to cast a follow-on DoT after Bane Of Nife (even if I cast it immediately after casting Bane Of Nife). The fight simply ends too quickly.

So, all that being said, I decided that since I had all this extra mana anyway, I might as well try direct damage. We have Spear Of Torment. Now, this is a pretty good spell. It does 675 damage for 275 mana. Not as efficient as our DoTs, but much, much, much faster. This worked fairly well -- I was using more mana, but I was able to recover that quickly with Canni5 anyway so hardly a significant issue. I could cast Spear Of Torment sustainably perhaps 4 times a fight. That would be 2700 damage, and no fear of wasting mana if a wizard takes the target from 50 percent to 2 percent in one cast. It also gave me some flexability in that if a fight was looking ugly I could cast the spell another 8 times or so to really lay on the hurt fast. This almost never seems to generate alot of agro, for some reason, which I find ironic.

So, 2700 per fight on average seemed pretty good. I decided that since my grouping playstyle was changing it would be worth investing some AA in spell casting fury, so I bought the first level. That worked out nicely; it gave me something fun to look for when I cast my spell. In terms of overall damage, it was rather insignificant (since you hardly ever crit). I would hit for 900 instead of 675. I was generally pleased though, so I bought spell casting fury 2. With that, I was able to crit for 1125 fairly often (still rare, of course) with Spear Of Torment. Now that isn't too bad, actually, but again the crits are fairly rare.

So what next? Well, we do have a rain spell with 3 waves. I usually ignored it because its use is fairly situational (ie: do not anger the enchanter by breaking mezz). I rarely group with enchanters anymore, though, so I thought I would give it a try. Well, this is what I observed:

1) The normal damage per wave is 540.

2) With scf2, the critical damage per wave is 900.

3) This spell is resisted alot, even after Malo.

So, if all 3 waves land with no crit, I can do 1620 damage. If one wave crits, which happens more often since I now have 3 chances instead of 1, I can do 1980 damage. I have even had a double-crit, for 2340 (which made me laugh).

The trouble is the resists. Now granted, some mobs are more poison resistant than others -- but why do I get resisted on mobs that NEVER resist Bane Of Nife or Spear Of Torment?

According to Lucy, neither spell has a resist adjustment.

The question is how can I make this spell more effective for me? Malosini instead of Malo? Should I be looking to group with a class that has a stacking poison debuff? Should I say "forget it" and level until I get the next rain at 64(ish).

As things stand right now, I'm pleased enough with the damage it does (with crits) that I'm willing to invest AA points for Spell Casting Fury 3 and Fury Of Magic. It's that, or getting one extra tick out of torpor with Spell Casting Reinforcement Mastery. I'd like that extra tick, but I don't actually need it that badly at the moment.

So anyway, what does crucible think? How can I be more effective at dealing damage? I don't want to replace the group wizard or anything, but I want to feel that I'm holding up my end of the deal when it comes to damage.

Several tips to using shaman rains.
MaloSINI/sinia the mobs. If yer lvl 65, you can prolly get away with dememming Malo for most exp grinds and just using sini. Sure you get a resist now ans then, but on the whole you save mana and debuff the mob more for your rains. I do this quite often when in a zone where I have a 70% chance of landing slow/sini first try.

Second, if in a group, using rains is easy, just make sure the mob is not in motion and make sure you won't get aggro from raining it and thus pull it out of range of the melees and additional rains. If you are close to getting aggro, stand just outside of rain dmg range next to the mob.

OR use the trick that works well all the time. If you're solo and have a pet and are tanking the mob, position your pet at the mob's back. Then target your pet with the rain. The mob will take 3 waves and you won't get nailed.

Additionally, get Tears of Saryn. It waves for 650 x 3, which is a nice improvement. It's a mana hog, but rains are. The best thing is that you can mem both Tears and Torrent and alternate them for TRUE PUNISHMENT! This way you can get away from the recast delay on both spells. When not casting one, you can be casting the other for constant rain damage! It's nice.
If you sini the mob and it's not too PR, you can approach 250 DPS with this method! Of course you will have aggro, but targetting the pet will make this method still viable. Keep Torpor on and you shouldn't get beat up too badly.
