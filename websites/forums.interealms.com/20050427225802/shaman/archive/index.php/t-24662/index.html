Now, in my opinion, the level grind is something that has been plaguing MMO's since they were invented. The greater the grind, the lesser the fun, to a certain extent. Allow me to explain.

Level grinding to level X (maximum level) must be meaningful in the three following ways only:

1) It must define a character not only from similar classes but also from other players of the same class - example: Alt Adv and class specific disciplines for EQ, Talents and Abilities and skills for WoW.

2) it must force the player of the character to have a minimum knowledge base of the character to reach level X. Example: controlling agro, crowd controlling effectively, twisting songs, healing correct targets, etc.

3) It must force the player to value their character.


Once the grind to level X can achieve all three of the above, there is NO REASON to lengthen the grind. Instead of adding more quests and more mobs an more levelling areas so that the experience bar can be slowed down, that time should be spent on high-end content accessible to casual and hardcore players alike, so that there is like-level competition for PvP and natural resources.

Soul,

Your theory might hold true for low levels, but not higher. There are considerably more quests for lower level characters. As you reach the higher levels, you won't find yourself "skipping" quests because you level too fast. Quite the opposite occurs: You end up traveling to other parts of the world to add quests to your log if you want to keep your experience gains strictly to questing.

Besides, many of the quests send you to zones you might not have visited otherwise. This, in turn, leads you to more NPCs with yellow exclamation points over their heads (i.e. more quests). Typical EQ grinding has the opposite effect. Once you find a good grind zone for your level, you stay there for days on end.

Ironically, I find WoW levels you more quickly if you solo. Grouping seems to dampen the experience rate somewhat, but opens up the world of "elite" quests which provide the best loot drops for weapons/equipment. It's an interesting experience balancing effect and lets you help others complete a quest you may already have under your belt without significant experience gain.
