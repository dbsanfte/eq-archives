Luclin's Arriana
He can say there is no market, because for chain classes, THERE IS NO MARKET.

A number of pieces of the plate armor are superb...especially if you are a human smith (The rallos zek imbued armor they make for warriors is monstrously good). Chain is...not very good. AC needs to be increased to half again what it is.

The armor is extraordinarily expensive to make (ignoring the time and effort in getting your smithing up to 240ish), and fails a LOT. Maximum skill smiths (250 natural skill, and a +5% skill mod item) across all servers, with 255 wis/int/str/dex, show less than 50% success rate on the final combine. That ignores failed intermediate combines, which can effortlessly eat the blue diamonds that each of these pieces take. Blue diamond cost has skyrocketed since the May patch which put this new armor into the game. Why? Because these armors eat blue diamonds like a raid cleric eats peridots. Breastplates average 44% success rate, game-wide, and take THREE blue diamonds per attempt.

Combine outright cost (metals, imbuing, enchanting, vials of mana, molds, blue diamonds, etc) with the obscene rarity of most culture's temper components (Iksar blood being the most extreme example, with maybe 1 such vial dropping every 3-4 RL days on any given server, and most of those destroyed because nobody thinks they are valuable), the fail rate, and you are looking at 10-15k for even the low priced, higher demand items, like the bracer...which for almost all sets, the best piece. For legs and breastplates, which are the hardest to make, and required the most (and most expensive) components, 50-60k is not out of line.

You can get a kael/skyshrine multiquest for considerably less, and for many slots, the item will be a lot better (or have a spiffy right click, which none of the smithed items have).

It is nice armor, and if you are lucky in your race (ie: Human, who got such a long end of the stick, that the other races can't even see the stick), you could probably make some money at it (only if you hit 242 smithing before June 5th however)...but to say that there is some kind of large market available is just ignorance.
