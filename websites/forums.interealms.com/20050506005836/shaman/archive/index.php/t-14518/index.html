Overfiend's fury is one of his AE's (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=2073&source=Live). It is a slow spell, and is what makes THO suck. If your tank isn't holding aggro, try getting chanters/sk to cast aggro spells on him, and give him plenty of time to build up aggro before engage.

His other AE, Feeblemind, makes you fizzle a lot (http://lucy.fnord.net/spell.html?id=2899&source=Live). In my experience, though, clerics have never had an trouble casting CH with this effect one. Your nukers will fizzle all their mana away, but you should be fine on healing. RGC takes this one in one shot. What I do on most THO fights is stand behind his pillar and cast RGC on every nuker I can.

If you are getting elder respawn, then you just need to engage faster and make sure you aren't positioned anywhere where they can aggro you. Casters behind the big pillar, everyone else meleeing. We have never gotten respawn adds, not sure how you did it.

Luclin's Arriana
THO is a heck of a lot harder than Rhag1/2, who have a good chance of dropping RGC. RGC is also researchable...where the pieces for it drop I have no idea. Lacking that, regular remove curse is good, and better still is radiant cure, which IIRC can be cast through the fizzle curse. Make groups accordingly.

Last time I fought THO, we engaged almost immediately after the last room guard died. He's not a belly caster, and is rooted, so it is trivial to debuff the hell out of him before you start the fight. Melees in close proximity to THO, all casters behind the pillar. The overfiend's fury is a procced AE, can't do much about it, but you can get the timing down for the fizzle curse. Nukers (and for shamans, dotting, THO has crappy PR/DR) step out from behind the pillar, cast a couple times, then hide again. A good tactic is to have your int casters (and shadowknights) practice their no-fizzle-below-level-X AA...it supercedes the fizzle curse, and allows them to cast freely...lower level spells, but better that than NO spells. Never had any adds.

The elders adding during the pull is just how it goes. There are lots of them, and they respawn fairly fast, so they need to die in bunches.
