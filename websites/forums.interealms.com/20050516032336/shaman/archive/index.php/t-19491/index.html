UgZug
EQ is definitely facing a maturity plateau that will develop into a decline of population soon.

Regardless of the game's health, there will always be people who retire for personal reasons - money, bored, no time, etc. These people take their toons with them for the most part. Hence, decline of established user base.

Add to this the difficulty of leveling new characters without systematic power-leveling... i.e., no groups available for the average new character. This equates to a decline of new users.

With a decline in new blood and a loss of old blood, the population is then depending upon existing players to expand into multiple accounts. This has happened a fair amount in the past but is fast becomming saturated. The existing player base that wishes to multi-box has pretty much already aquired these accounts and have created these characters.

With GoD, you see again a focus on 55+ (SoE's words from yesterday's online chat) and no more focus on the newbs. This goes to illustrate that SoE recognizes that for this game to survive they will need to keep their high level players happy rather than trying to entice NEW players into the game.

What this translates to is that the game is developing leaks... Sony recognizes this and are attempting to stick their fingers in the holes to prevent as much loss as possible. They throw bones to the high end players in the form of uber gear, abilities, and expansions in the hope that this will stem the flow... only time will tell whether it is effective or not. Regardless, once a fighter is pushed back onto their heels and goes into a defensive posture, the fight is lost.

EQ IS dieing... how quickly it does so is unknown... but it's matured and the players that made it great are looking to new pastures or have left the gaming community all together.
