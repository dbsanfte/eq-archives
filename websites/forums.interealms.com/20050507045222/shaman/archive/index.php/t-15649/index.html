These are percentages of full slow, remember. I've found the following to be spot on (within the RNG's usual streakiness) for mobs I've faced, with one exception: Boss mobs. If they slow at all, they'll give some sort of message back, but it won't be the same % that the message means on run-of-the-mill critters.

For cannon fodder:
With our full slow being 75% off of base speed, the slowed mob will do roughly 25% of his expected DPS.
Mostly=75% of full effect, or 56% slow. He'll do about 44% of full DPS.
Partly = 50% of full effect, or 37.5% slow. Figure on taking 63% of full DPS.
Slightly = 25% of full effect, or only 18% slow. Expect the mob to do 82% of their usual DPS.

There still seems to be a bug with message reporting. I've failed to get a resist msg on a clearly targeted mob, but the sucker didn't slow down one iota.

The biggest discrepency is at around the 60sec mark, where depending on the delay, an attack may or may not get counted because it would of came at like the 59sec or 61sec mark.

IMO as a developer and game designer -

If I was tasked to implement mitigated slows, I for sure wouldn't hardcode 3-4 values in there like 25, 50, 75. I'd want more control over the mob, so I'd put a number in there. i.e. the mob has a "slow mitigation factor" that can be anything from 0-100, 0 meaning no mitigation and 100 meaning slow-immune.

Then when I needed to produce the MESSAGE, I'd map the slow mitigation number to a message. 0=normal slow message, 1-33 = slightly, 34-66 = partially, 66-99 = mostly, 100 = immune.

That's all argument based on conjecture, but I guess I'd fall into the "range of numbers" camp rather than the fixed steps camp based on it.
