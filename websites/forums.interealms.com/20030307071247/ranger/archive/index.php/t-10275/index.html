Kedawen
Strafing is stepping sideways, using the CTRL+arrow keys. This is especially useful for fear kiting a mob that turns 180 degrees frequently. It is easier to get to the side of the mob and strafe than chasing behind it, where when he turns around, you have to turn as well to keep it in sight.

I don't think you can maneuver around a mob fast enough to avoid being hit, though it is an effective tactic in PVP.

Magrath
Probably goes without saying, but it's also the best way to stay behind a mob if he's doing the panic boogie mentioned earlier, or if he's switching between different party members. If you stay behind him, he can't do riposte damage to you. By strafing instead of turning and moving, you can avoid the whole "you can't see your target" thing while repositioning.

Kelliani
I'm a strafing nut. I use it constantly. Since the PC detonated on me, I'm having a rough life using the laptop with the CTRL key all the way across the keyboard from the arrow keys. :p

The aboslute most important use I have found for it is to keep position on a critter to stop shoving it all over the place. Perfect example of a good use for it is in Halls of Testing. I see way too many people end up on the back end of the mob due to shoving. This is bad because it shoves the critter right over the MT. Eventually, the MT will run out of room against a wall. At that point, the rogues lose the back because the MT needs to spin it out of that position to continue holding aggro.

Personally, I do not see what is so hard about just strafing left or right to keep position on the critter. Instead, people let it slide past them and turn to face it when they get the message that they can't see the target. This has the result of shoving it over the MT even faster. Yes, some shoving occurs. It's going to happen. However it can be minimized if you maintain postition by strafing.

Mephs
Something that seems to have been missed here is the benefit of using mouselook combined with strafing..... of minimal use against mobs that can instantly turn but great to use in PvP I should think. If you use the mouse to turn as you strafe you can circle-strafe (or skiff as it's known in the FPS communities) and always keep facing a target while they turn to meet you.... if done correctly you can keep an enemy in your sights constantly while always remaining out of line of sight. I know it's a completely different kettle of fish but try playing somehting like Unreal tournament or Quake 3 strafing using the alternate key..... then learn to do it properly with mouselook and you will soon find that the ability to skiff hugely increases your maneuverability. using purely keys can make do... and some people swear by it... but if you learn this method I swear you'll never look back.
