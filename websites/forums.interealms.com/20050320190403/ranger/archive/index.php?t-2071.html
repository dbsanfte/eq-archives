When you pull a quote we need to know the source Kullaas, so we can see the level of authority of the source.

The technique he is using is common in political speeches.

1) Lay out some common knowledge, i.e., the fact that history is more than just the holocost. Of course it is, history is huge, the holocost was one major event in it, but its not the sum total of history, ok.

2) Appeal to people's emotions, Christians are getting hated and murdered in the Sudan. Since you are already in agreement with him about history, even if you know none of the facts (I don't for one) pertinent to the Sudan reference, you are much more likely to go either, What! or Yeah! than ask for references.

3) Tie an accusatory reference into the first passage, like the Jews aren't speaking out about atrocities happening to someone else.

4) Introduce the ray of hope, in this case the incredibly right wing Le Pen. Make him the answer to all the problems.

5) Close it with predictions of doom and despair should your hero not be allowed to lead to the solution to the problem.
