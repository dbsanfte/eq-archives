Relv,

I think you vastly underestimate the value of this item.

1. This target market for this item is NOT the top 2% of the player base who can get primal, WBoRZ, BoD, and so on. Many of the people(the top ones) in the target market are raiding PoF/PoH, the lesser Kunark and WW dragons, maybe Kael, and getting wiped out often on all of it..

2. A 30/38 with the outstanding 250 range is the best tradeable bow in the game. The advantage vs AE targets is huge, especially since the people in this crowd usually have poor resists(no NToV gear). And as high damage + high range it's value for pullers is also huge(more then Rangers can use this).

3. This collection of range slot stats is nothing short of amazing for the target market. For these low resist, low hp, low stat people, this thing is great. Even without the fact that it's a great bow, the range slot stats would bring a hefty price.

4. The look. It has a great look. People pay for things with great looks. That's the way it goes.

5. It's rare. It is not trivial at 250, and still fairly hard to make at that skill. It can only be made by Woodelves. The Woodelf is going to have to be a good smith too for the fier'dal forge. It requires hours worth of foraged parts per attempt. It requires at least 2 blue diamonds per attempt and you have 7 differant combines in which you can loose the blue diamonds. Getting the skill is harsh. Going past 202 is very hard, and expensive. Going past 225 is much harder still. (225 to 231 took me 417 fletching attempts at 255 dex, that is not accounting for the associated smithing attempts or the massive time spent camping drops by me and my very nice/helpfull friends). It's hard, and that makes it rare. People pay more for rare things. That's just the way it is.

It will easily go for 20k, if you can even find somebody selling one on your server.

In general trade-based items start high and quickly drop to just over cost. Long-term, there is no way you can continue to support a 700% mark-up (if I am reading this right that's the amount) for long. 2500pp per attempt someone quoted. Even with a 50% failure rate, 20k would represent a 300% profit. Market forces are going to come into play, and just like the fleeting quiver or the velium fire opal ring, eventually you'll have to settle for a profit margin of 50% or less. Depending on failure rate, the eventual price of one of these bows should be 4-7k roughly (assuming 2500pp per attempt is correct and failure rate is in the ballpark of 50%).

So the short version is, if you are one of the first few players on your server to make these, you'll clean up. If you are one of the hundreds who come along later, you'll probably never recover the money you spent learning the skills needed.

Me? I plan to hold out a while. 20k is a lot to spend on a bow. It's a damn good bow, but I can wait till it's 10k or so. It'll happen, and it won't take that long.
