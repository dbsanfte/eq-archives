Since you don't have Malos or Malo, then I would agree with the paladin.

In PoP, prior to Malo, Malos, I would alternate casting Slow and Masosi(ni), because of longer recast of slow. The spell I would start casting first depends on tank and situation. If I can cast on the MOB well before in reaches the camp, then start with Malosi(ni), because of low aggro. If not, then it depends on the tank.

If it's a warrior, then start with Malosi(ni). If it's an SK/Pallie, start with slow. The reason here is 2-fold. First, the warrior can tank un-slowed MOBs better, so it's somewhat safer to debuff first. But secondly (and probably more important), Pallies and SKs can gain hard aggro very easily and quickly, so a resisted slow is less likely to get you attention.

So, IMO, the pallie was giving you good "advice". Either way is not wrong. One is more efficient than the other. The problem is that the one that is more efficient is not always the same and depends on the situation.

The big question is, whether the pallie was just trying to give you advice or was he telling you what to do? If it was just advice, take it. If he was telling you what to do, well, that would merit a different responce, regardless of whether he is right or not :P
