BlargX
Well shamans lack snare and FD, two vital pulling and crowd control spells and two of the very best spells in the game by far.

Shaman's strength is buffing melees.

So...why not add a melee that can bring you snare and FD? Shadowknight. I love mine. He's a melee who can tank, too, and summon corpses. That's a lotta love.

You could also use some more DPS since both SK and shaman are pretty low DPS. And shaman heals leave a lot to be desired.

So why not add a healer who can do some DPS? Druid dps is very respectable -- they can both root and DOT, and when they get enough levels, they can charm pets for very strong pet damage output. Plus, their heals are very respectable. And, they also have snare and very, very good buffs, and can even evac you out of trouble if things go bad, or just port you from place to place. All in all, a very very good class.

That would make an ideal trio I think. You've got healing and buffing and pulling and corpse summoning all very well covered. And, nice utilities like tracking in the druid, damage shields, ports...SK's eventually get a spell that can give you mana and hitpoints in small amounts when they cast it(zevfeer's bite i think it's spelled)...it's just all around a pretty beautiful trio.

Fourth? Whatever you like really. You've got the basics covered.

A bard or chanter would be really nice for crowd control. You already have two snarers and two rooters in that trio above, but you can never have too much crowd control. Chanters eventually become incredibly powerful toons that are central to the game, too. If you get them high enough(and courageous enough) to charm pets, they become the highest DPS class in the game by miles and miles.

And clerics can buff/heal/rez, necros can mana twitch, snare, FD pull, have a decent pet DPS, can kite, DD ok, DOT incredibly well, even mez and charm some mobs and have a necro rez.

Lots of choices.

I really think the trio I recommended above pretty much covers all your major bases, and very well. Not that plenty other combos won't work great too. (Some people are terrified of deaths and won't dream of going anywhere without a rezzer -- so some will not accept a trio without a pally or a cleric.) And after you have heals/pulls/(probably going to want at least one)tank down, garnish as you like with whatever, with a lot more freedom to choose whatever weird combos you like without feeling like you're lacking anything.

BlargX
I think paladin is over-rated, but then I have always found worrying about rezzes over-rated. I'm the adventurous type, and I know in advance I'm going to die sometimes. So what? I pick things worth dying for.

I'd rather have the FD and corpse summoning and snare of a SK than the stuns/rez/buffs/heals of a pally. Pally buffs are undoubtedly nice, and heals, but so are SK AC and ATK boosts and superior DPS, and the regular clockwork infusion of mana/HP that a SK gives with z-bite. Plus, you just gotta have a FD puller. FD and snare are huge in this game.

Obviously, all kinds of combos can work out. Personally, I tried a warrior and missed the FD pulling of my monk a LOT, so I got a FD puller who can actually tank -- sort of like a warrior/monk combo in a way, with many bonuses. Snare pulling and pet pulling and just regular FD pulling with SK's is very fun and can be pretty creative. And nothing helps you land a malos or malos/slow combo on a mob like having the mob run around snared while you do it at your leisure. Pally stuns are great right? They prevent some damage. But so does running something around snared till you have it completely debuffed -- yet with no chance of running for you because you slowed him, like they would when I duoed with my warrior or monk.

To each their own; but FD and snare are really huge, and I'd rate corpse summoning higher than rez any day. One is convenient, and one is a terrible necessity.

Maybe less so now that your corpse goes to that new graveyard zone in a week. If you want to wait a week that is.
