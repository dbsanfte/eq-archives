The Bear gazed around him. The snow blew across the tundra in a lazy fashion. In the distance, he saw the great mamoths searching for food in the desolate wilderness. The sun broke into a thousand colors, glistening off a tear, creeping unnoticed down his great white snout. Forgotten was the dispute with the dragon goddess which drove him from his homeland. Today the Soul Winds didn't blow, so he knew that Lady Vox slept, he was safe here at the moment.Weary paws lead him to the edge of the ice, a deep breath and a splash. The icy waters envigorated him, bringing back memories of cubhood. His mother gazing at him fondly, giving him a playful swat on the rear, to help him swim faster. The underwater cave loomed in front of him.

He heard the giggling of the skeletons in the distance, their toe bones clicking on the rock floor. They didn't concern him, as they stayed out of his are of the cave and wouldn't bother him. At the mouth of his den, he faced forward one more time, and great muscles bunching smashed the side of the cave with a wide paw. Small rocks bounced off his head, but he ignored them, concentrating on his task. Several hits later, he heard a deep groan running through the cave. He quickly backed up, awaiting the outcome. Nothing happened. However, another smash by the great paw saw him retreat into his lair, pursued by a cloud of dust.

As the dust cleared, he could see that the lair was now blocked by many large rocks. He was safe. Weariness washed over him like a drowning wave. Turning his back on the small openings the rocks left, he brushed off a space to lie, with a tired, sore paw. Turning around several times, he sank onto his side, not to rise again.

He didn't know what the future held, but for now, he was safe and he was tired. Now was the time for sleep, As the blue eyes closed on a dark and silent cave, a last glint of light twinkled off a small piece of ice, stuck to the stainless white fur.
