Retron
Apologies if this is already known by you all, but reading a thread on the SOE boards has made me reconsider AGI buffs.

I know AGI won't make you run faster (although it's amazing how many think it does), and even Wrulan only adds a few AC, making it not seem worthwhile. I still cast it out of courtesy, and usually I click it off straight after to save a buff slot.

However, this thread:

http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/Forum4/HTML/050738.html

has made me think twice. I didn't realise that AC is a composite of mitigation/avoidance until now, and it seems that AGI adds to the Avoidance part of your AC. If you can get +10 to avoidance for a simple AGI buff, that seems worthwhile to me (and that works out at around a 2.5% boost in the avoidance AC).

The other thing I didn't realise (from that thread); adding &debug=true to a Magelo URL gives a chart showing how the AC is derived. So, in my case, the 11 AGI I get from my gear gives +3 avoidance - not much, but every little helps, I say! :)

http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=563014&debug=true

Naagruz
Kianor, there are two basic flaws with your proposed design to test Agi and LR5 relationships. First, you are going to need identical tanks with and without LR5 tested before the Agi buff to create a basline for comparison. Second, you need to separate Agi form the visible AC it introduces, so both tanks will need to be at max Agi before you introduce the buffs. Otherwise you just end up testing Agi and AC together and make it impossible to have any valid conclusions.

You will be left asking "was it the added Agi or was it the added AC or, for that matter, was it just some other unseen variable from all that equipment that created my results"???

I wish there was an easy way to test this sort of thing in-game and get valid results, but there isn't. :p

Poguk
So many people focus on the AC gained from agility. For some reason lately, I've been revisiting agility for avoidance reasons. Most folks expect instant gratification -- in other words, a gain they can see and quantify. They see adding a 60 agility buff only gives like 10 AC, and deem it worthless.

But I got to thinking... what if adding agility adds slightly to your AC, but increases your avoidance defense to a large degree? In other words, you get hit less often. You still get hit really hard when you get hit.. but you get hit less often.

I have no proof that this happens; I haven't done any tests, 'cause frankly I got better things to do, but I make sure to buff my monk's agility when he's tanking. I was kinda surprised when I saw this thread and people revisiting the benefits of agility after recently having thoughts about it.

It wouldn't surprise me at all for someone to find out that agility does in fact increase avoidance defense and that perhaps it benefits light-weight classes who are built for avoidance defense like monks, rogues, rangers, etc...
