I commented about why the chanter kept drawing so much aggro on multi pulls... and that's what he told me.. essentially becaue I couldn't slow, he had to. So he stopped mezzing..., causing me to die thus negating any xp gain for that adventure (pally 90% rez). Anyway, we STILL nearly completed the adv, falling short by 30 secs.

My 2nd attempt was a group put together by a 55 cleric... I wanted Guk since I still haven't got Pack Shrew, and he agreed... cool I thought, can kill 2 birds with one stone. But.... this cleric picked a hard adventure, don't know why... anyway while we are buffing, I ask the standard questions "Who's MA, who's pulling, how are they pulling, how we handle CC, etc...". No answer... the cleric says "all Full mana let's go!" I say "whoa, hold the train, what about...", too late, they all zoned in. Well, once we in I ask again. Finally got a reply... "I don't care, just pull!". Oops, well I knew we were in trouble then. Needless to say, after several deaths and lots of finger pointing, the adventure was over (in about 20 minutes or so). At this point I gave up for awhile, went and played an alt, did some chores around the house etc.

Later that same evening, I tried again, I gave up on LDoN, and was soloing in The Grey, had my LFG up anyway, since 2 blues take forever while soloing... after about an hour (almost a whole blue xp gained) I get a tell from a cleric wanting a LDoN. I think to myself, why not? I've just looted my third snake organ (for Amulet of the Grey), and I'm less than a blue away from lev 60... WOOHOO! So I zone to PoK, get new c and head to BB. Get there, join group, etc waiting on last 2 members... cleric up and says "oops I gotta go to work", and leaves group! Well, I finally have had enough. I disband group. Get to work on putting my OWN group together... 10 minutes and I have a group ready to go. Lay out who's doing what etc... and we have a great adventure, I ding 60, we get phat lewtz, all are happy.

Moral of the story?? There are a lot of uneducated EQ players out there, guys who don't know how to play there OWN class, let alone know what other classes can do. No big surprise I know, but I DIDN'T really know until I started doing LDoNs (mostly soloed or did guild stuff before).

Anyway, just wanted to get this stuff off my chest.. thanks for listening.

deria
I backup heal when the cleric or druid goes down... I rarely die...


This is just a sugestion, but you might want to modify that behaviour to instead backup heal *before* the cleric or druid goes down. Some may not appreciate you stepping on their toes (ie: the oversensetive or bad players) but in the long-run your groups will be more durable if you watch the health of the other healers and key group members. A well timed heal from you might compensate for a poorly timed heal from the cleric/druid, or it might save the cleric from a beat-down so that he can in turn save someone else.

No matter what level you are you always want the mob beating on someone other than the healer. If that means you have to pull agro and let it beat on you (either by healing or by pulling agro with an agro inducing spell) then so be it.

Also, regarding the question of "who's slowing", I would say if you have a beastlord in the group and hes not slowing then hes not pulling his weight. Beastlords make excellent preslowers, even when there slow is only 30 percent or 50 percent. I've kicked a beastlord out of my group once before for refusing to attempt to preslow mobs. Sure I can do a better job percentage-wise, but he can do it faster and if we both do it then we get the best of both worlds. Alas, he didn't see it that way.

His replacement did.

EDIT: I also like it when bards pull with occlusion of sound to lower magic resist, or pre-slow the mob while its incoming. Its not something that I would necessarily *expect* them to do, but it sure is nice when they do it. They're pretty busy during pulls, though, so it cant always be managed.
