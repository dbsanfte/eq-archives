Faction isn't that big of an issue. With the last 2 expansions, there are cities that are open to everyone (Bazaar and Thurgadin). I personally use Rivervale for all my banking, since its close to a druid portal.

To get non-KOS in RV, buy several backpacks full of bandages. Then go to the SE corner of Misty Thicket and find a hafling named Jogglo Honeybugger (or something similar). Get wolf form (brings you to dubious), and start handing him bandages, 4 stacks of 4 at a time (16 each turn in). After about 800-900 bandages, you should be non-kos in town.

To bank in freeport, just kill guards in east/west fp. This will gain you faction with the north freeport pallys.

To bank in Kelethin, you need to kill massive numbers of orcs in Crushbone.

Qeynos factoin is gained by killing Gnolls in BB, and also bandits in the Karanas. Save the bandit sashes and give them to one of the pallys outside the S Qeynos Pally guild for some pretty nice faction hits (again, get wolf form to get non-kos to start).

Last but not least, hunt in Stonebrunt and Warrens to gain faction to bank in Paniel.

Or, just bind at the Nexus and use the Bazaar for all your banking needs and don't sweat it.

Depending on level, faction goes from not very important to totally unimportant.

As a troll or an ogre, you can level to lvl 8 in Innothule Swamp, then 12 in South Ro, then 16 in Oasis. At that point you can run over to any number of hunting spots. From like 16-20 on you could hunt in Luclin, where your race doesn't matter, and from about 30 on you can be in Velious, where your race doesn't matter.

For faction, two factors enter into it: race and religion (and possibly class). As an evil race shammy you can't be agnostic, so you have the (fairly big) double faction whammy of being an evil race and of worshipping either Innoruk or Cazic-Thule.

Honestly, if you've been gone a year, you're going to find that the early levels go insanely fast now. Between not having to open the book to meditate, to increased exp bonuses pre lvl 10... you can probably ding 10 in a few hours, long before you'd kill enough things to fix faction.

Hunting down south: South Ro and Innothule are probably the best. Feerott is now a lvl 20-40 zone on the island leading to Cazic-Thule, but still newbie level stuff the rest of the place. Rathe Mountains are fun but a map is a good idea.

So, the questions:

1) Fixing factions can be a royal pain in the posterior. No question. Some factions are unfixable. First remark . . . Qeynos . . . why? There is NOTHING around qeynos worth doing. The guards in the Karana's don't see invis, and if you're a shaman, you can outrun them anyway. Forget Qeynos. You'll probably never go there!

2) I think iksar have the worst faction. I don't know if you can fix their faction for most places. I believe you can do it for neriak (not positive about that as a shaman--I know monks can). Most of the rest of the cities are a royal pain to even try. However, you can fix most of the stuff in velious, and luclin. Trolls and ogres have similar problems, though not, in my opinion, quite as bad. It's gotten a bit worse since VI has added a couple of KoS mobs to the troll and ogre cities that kill the opposite race.

3) Whether or not you care about faction is a question of convenience. If you don't mind being reduced to a few banks, and plan accordingly, then more power to you. That's a personal call. I've gotten used to my faction issues.

4) Try allakhazam.com for faction questions. I think they have a part of the site that is devoted to faction questions and issues. They also list mobs that increase or decrease factions. Unfortunately, you have to know the name of a faction, and it may not always be clear--ie, know that ring of scale is kunark dragons, or know that claws of veeshan is velious dragon faction, etc. Given that there are usually several factions within a city, this can get confusing.

5) There really aren't that many problems with being large--especially since you are talking about playing a shaman. You do get the shrink spell after all! :)

6) Go to allakhazam.com and look at the "levels" section of the site. They have a great level range chart. It will help. Best newbie zone is probably for the leezards in the Field of Bone. However, that is really only a good choice if you are an iksar. With the changes on bodies and not losing your equipment until 10, you can bind rush red mobs and exp to 10 very quickly.

Hope this helps. Good luck to you.
