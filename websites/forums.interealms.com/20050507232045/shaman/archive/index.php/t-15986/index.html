Solariis
A quickie guide on how to not suck as a shaman: If you just did these minimum things you can be good enough to not suck up the place.

1. Keep to the essential buffs.

HP buffs: STA everyone, the extra hp adds up. FoS everyone except the cleric(use IoS on them otherwise focus overrides their self hp/mana buff combo) and beastlord(they like to do their own self hp buffing). In tier2+ zones you need the HP to survive so every little bit counts. Remember to buff yourself first so you know when the buffs are going to run out. FoS also overrides INT caster's self-hp buff but I've yet to meet one that doesn't prefer FoS so I don't bother asking them anymore if they want it.

Regen: Keep replenish on yourself, the tank, any necros and whoever else might need it(like a modrodding caster). The 63 Blessing of Replenishment(group replenishment) makes life much easier.

Haste: Haste the melee if no enchanter. Haste your dog first so you have it timed. There's no excuse for forgetting haste.

Whatever you do keep those buffs refreshed. Don't be slacking on them. The rest of the buffs are up to you. I also keep group AGI up, group sow when available, talisman of diaku and resist buffs depending on the place you are fighting and whether there's a druid to take care of fire/cold resists. Talisman of Epuration is important against shaman/necro mobs that like to dot you. Buff CHA on bards an enchanters.

2. Slowing.

Your #1 job is slowing. When a pull is incoming, assist on the puller and immediately spam your malo spell. You want to malo then slow. Malo will debuff to make your slows land easier. If you don't have malo then you'll have to resort to your malosiniX line which unlike malo can be resisted.

3. CC

A shaman makes an excellent CCer with the combo of root + slow. Ok it's a lot more challening in tier2+ zones compared to the good ol' days of keeping 4 frogs root parked in Seb but it's still an important skill. The key is to slow first then move where you want the mob parked and then sit. Slow + sit generates huge aggro and 99% of the time the mob will come to you. Once it comes to you then you root it. Of course if those are caster mobs they'll be nuking you but it's better than nothing. Use pet to distract the rooted casters if available.

4. Spot Healing.

Keep an eye on your cleric, make sure to keep her alive if she's being beat on. Heal a bit after battle on the casters and non-MT but let the cleric keep the MT healed.

Now, if all you did was follow those easy 4 points you'd be a decent shaman just about anywhere. The rest you'll have to figure out on your own :)
Jinheim
Step 1: Cast focus and sta on everyone. Regen is good too, and sometimes haste if no chanter is around.
Step 2: Cast malo on mob.
Step 3: Cast slow on mob.
Step 4: See if anyone needs a heal. If they need one, do it. If not:
Step 5: Do damage to the mob. I usually just use nukes because dots can be a pain to use in groups. Nukes, dots, and rains will all work fine.

That's it. Shaman is not a difficult class to play decently, but it takes considerable time and skill to play it very well. Just practice and you'll get there.
