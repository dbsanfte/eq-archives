Most of the best advice ever put on this board is long since gone. It tends to vanish over time. Searching through pages isn't going to get you much.

People are going to post here because that's where stuff gets read. Period. I never check the other "mini-forums" here, and I'm probably joined by the vast majority in that. For the most part, I don't care. I've been 60 for a while and I already know where to hunt, and I know what the spells are, etc. When skipping forums about that, however, I also skip the opportunity to reply to anyone asking about those things in that kind of forum. Which is fine by me.

But actually, I might be tempted to help -- and sometimes am -- when I see a thread here in the main forum. If people need info, their best chance of getting it is in the main forum, so that's where they'll continue to post.

As to there being too little new here to read for the old-timers, that is indeed because the game has been around so long. What else is there to say? Mostly the good stuff is what people wouldn't want to give away anyway -- how to make a certain camp much simpler, where to send your twinks, how to complete a certain uber quest with mobs someone else might want to camp or fight you or your friends for for if you spilled the beans. Most of the stuff there is to say either has already been said or people are unwilling to share, so...the boards are actually not as much a place for the old-timers anymore, at least not to the extent they once were.

They really are for the guys now asking all the questions others think are dumb or must probably be intelligently answered somewhere, who knows where but don't bother me about it.

We were all newbies to the class once, though, if not to the whole game even, and the class and the game were damnably full of puzzles that were very hard or extremely time-consuming to figure out, and we've all probably come across many times where some tidbit of knowledge has been immensely helpful.

So when the old-timers get too blase to help or even are tired merely of seeing the same old thing, they should remember that they don't own the boards, and what's trivial to them might be vital to someone else.

Most importantly, it's new blood that keeps this or any game going. I've played a lot of online games, and sooner or later old-timers tend to get sick of newbie questions and sometimes even start to flame and intimidate newbies. That's cutting off your nose to spite your face, though. If this game relied only on the crusty old farts to keep it going, it would start to sputter and die a very premature death. We actually need the newbies more than they need us, if the game is to continue.

So if their threads seem tedious or duplicative or a waste of time, skip them. They have at least as good a chance as us to keep the shaman community and the whole game active and alive. They may even be here long after we're gone.
