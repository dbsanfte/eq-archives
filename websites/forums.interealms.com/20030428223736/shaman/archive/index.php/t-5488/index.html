Sthaz
How bout a 'give shaman every other class defining ability' potion? Honestly, FD? track? ress? summon corpse? While I'd love to have these abilities, these are mainstays of other classes which we do not, and should not posess. Why bother with 13 or whatever classes when shaman can do everything?

What alchemy needs is a kick in the teeth. Make it interesting, make it fun, make it useful, make it WORTH THE MONEY, do not make it so that shaman have every other class defining ability.

Some ideas:

Short(ish) duration skill mod potions (a one shot alternative to or boost to already existing gearlocks).

AFFORDABLE gate potions, everyone already stocks thurg potions anyway, that kills the market for anything we could make at present. Admittedly, it steps on the toes of druids and wizzies with port/TL, but there's already prescendent for such things.

Illusion potions would be fun. Yes, it borrows other class abilities (chanters, roues, bards), but these are not game breaking abilities like FD or ress. Illusions would be limited to playable races or other illusion spells, due to these being global files (not an expert on the mechanics, but it was explained when chanters wanted illusion:froglok, more global files = longer zoning times etc). If they added faction bonuses, that would be great, but might make them too powerful. Make them lore to prevent stacking or no drop to limit their usage (ie, shm only).

Stop making potions that have double and triple restraints. Most potions have short duration, negative effects AND a prohibitive cost. This is why no one wants them or wants to make them.

Make availability the limiting factor in alchemy, as it is in tailoring, not price like almost every other tradeskill. Require dropped components, not all vendor sold. Alchemy tools and subquests would be useful in limiting production.
