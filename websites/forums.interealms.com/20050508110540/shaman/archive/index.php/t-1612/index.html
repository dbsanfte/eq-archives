Gloriana
Hey, if you do a search there is a lot of debate on "the best shaman race."

Barb

Pros:
Slam
Decent starting faction
Decent starting wis
Good starting str/sta
Can wear JB armor, including the JBB
Can also wear Jarsath armor from KC but not sebilite scale armor from OS
Good looking (well, I think so!)

Cons:
Nightblind (only a real hassle up to level 9 when shaman get infra)
No innate regen
Right now, our deity specific quest is pretty crappy, you summon a giant magical hammer /yawn
Very common (maybe not a con to some)

Iksar

Pros:
Innate natural regen
Highest starting wisdom
Innate AC bonus
Excellent Hate planar armor
Can wear Jarsath and sebilite scale
Can't wear JB, but can wear all the Velious quested armor

Cons:
Can't wear JB (including the JBB)
Bad faction in the old world
Low starting sta/str

Neither barbs or iksar can worship Innothule and have access to the deity specific Inny snare necklace (which is very nice). Both barbs and iksar can wear the fungi tunic (vah shir shaman cannot). Neither barbs or iksars get the ogre frontal stun immunity.

While some shaman seem to think the ability to wear a JBB is game-breaking, I really think you should decide on what looks fun to you and what you would enjoy playing.

Gloriana
Hmmmm, starting stats. Are you playing with end game in mind or are you just playing for some fun?

I think int, str, cha and dex are pretty worthless for starting stats, the only important ones to me are wis, sta and to a lesser extent, agi.

What I've found at level 60 is that it's pretty easy to get pieces of equipment with great wisdom. I'm finding it hard to find equipment that also adds sta.

That being said, as a level 60 barb shaman in a raid guild, I would have put more points into sta and less points into wisdom. Although conventional wisdom states that you should put as many starting points into wis as possible and the remaining 5 into sta or agi.

With regen + cannabalize, I still don't think it's as important for shaman to have maxxed wisdom, mana regen was always more important to me than max mana pool (regen items > wis items). So, I think I could have gotten by with 10 less into wis and 10 more into sta.

Okay, for a barb, if I were to start over knowing what I know now...I would have put 20 points into sta and 10 into wis. That's a pretty radical suggestion, so take it with a grain of salt.

For iksars...wow, they have a pretty low str and sta, but a nice high wis...While I would be tempted to put some points into str (since a new character will struggle with encumberance for awhile) I still think I would concentrate the points into 20 sta, 10 wis.

Don't get too stressed out about starting stats, I completely messed Glory up (let's just say she's um, strong) and I managed just fine.

Oh, I forgot another barb pro...being a cute polar bear!

Scaleyy
Iksar definately.

1. Most of your time in eq will be spent in places you are kos anyway. Wherever you hunt things will most likely hate you no matter what you play. Faction doesn't matter. As an Iksar I've been able to accomplish anything anyone else has. Also, if you really wanted to walk around freely in all cities you could easily kill mobs for faction. Even now I spend 99% of my time on the Moon and in Velious where it also doesn't matter what race you are.

2. Highest starting wisdom adds more benefits than it may seem. Sure you'll have more mana for the early levels, but it's a Huge benefit all the way to 60. This means you can still have 255 wisdom and use gear that boosts other things like Health, Mana, or AC.

3. Regen Regen Regen. At level 60 this is like having an extra clarity buff. It all adds up. Also, when you decide to use a BP other than fungi it won't hurt your mana supply as much.

4. Solo'ing. JBB isn't squat. I have always been able to solo wonderfully. Some shamans become so dependant on a 'free nuke' that they don't see there are better strategies. I've grouped a good amount of my 60 levels, but after level 24 and all the way to 60 I did a tremendous amount of solo. Nearly all of level 55-60 I did solo. When I solo now for AAXP, I can kill mobs back to back without stopping to rest...Indefinately. It takes a good CT group to get more xp than I can solo.

5. AC bonus. This isn't huge, but on average you're gonna take less damage than the other races. It all adds up.

6. Some may say, "But OMG they have XP penalty!". Why do you think the XP penalty is there? Because we have the advantage and they have to balance it out somewhere. Also AAXP is the same for everyone, no penalty. Of course, speaking from experience, the xp penalty isn't that big of a deal. Iksar can solo better than any other race so it makes up for it. I will admit though Halflings will definately out level you by a good margin. Even though a halfling cleric friend of mine beat me to 60, but I was 59 so it wasn't a tragedy. We play about the same amount of time.


from level 1 - 60 Iksar is the best. Nothing the other races have make up for it. Only play another race if you just really like it or want to roleplay.

Max your wisdom then put the rest (5?) into stamina. the more health the better.

Ok, I'm an iksar Shaman. They are not the best, there is no "best" race.
Now that's out of way let's get to the heart of things.

It really depends on the type of player you are, if you are a casual player then the 20% exp penalty that Iksars and Trolls get may not be the most practical choice for you. But, if the exp penalty isn't a factor then Iksar is a good choice. No JBB as an Iksar, remember that ! But if you are a casual player like me, you'd probably never see a JBB anyway.
Trolls are excellent, good str, sta, and regen. Not to mention Innoruuk worshipping Trolls can get snare necklace. And yes a 60 Troll can do everything an 60 Iksar can plus more. With the right gear of course. It's true for both races. Faction really isn't an issue anymore with the Moon and Velious.

Barbarian is an easy choice, there are alot of them, they are blind, blind, blind ! Yeah you get ultravision, but you have to wait 29 levels to get that spell. No way could I use a night blind race after using both Troll and Iksar.

Ogres are kinda the forgotten ones they are actually quite good, not sure how viable no frontal stun is later on. As an Iksar I get stunned but I can honestly say I've never been stunned to where it costed me my life. I got interrputed and just recasted my spell, no biggie ! Ogres have alot of str,stamina, which translates to more HP's to canni. Again faction isn't an issue. Believe me regen is a big deal. Saves alot of downtime and you can canni back more mana faster. Some will argue that regen isn't a factor because of Torpor. Well let's look at that..one, Torpor is a lvl 60 spell and two, its damn expensive if you can ever find someone selling it. And camping for a drop of Torpor all I can is good luck !
Until you get to 60 or get your hands on a Fungi (if you're a Vah forget about Fungi until 51) regen is a factor.
Ogres can use the JBB.
Wisdom means JACK ! Once you hit 24 you'll realize that ! AC/HP's are kings to the Shaman.
For starting points it depends on race
For Troll I would put 20 into sta and 10 into wis or vice versa.
For Ogre I'd put 10 into wis 20 into agility.
For Barbs...(see Troll)
For Iksar, there physical stats suck other than agility...20 into sta, 10 str.

Don't know much about Vah'Shir so I won't comment on them.

JBB is a great situational/soloing tool, but I wouldn't base my choice of Shaman race on gear. I won't say the JBB sucks because it doesn't its awesome and if I could wear one I'd try to get one, but I'm not changing my choice because the JBB really doesn't mean much to me. If you can wear it try to get it otherwise don't worry about it.
Play a race you think is cool, because in the end game, strategy, skill, and gear+spells can make the difference between a good shaman and a bad one.

One consideration I have never seen mentioned, but is worth considering:

The majority of shamen are barbarians. When Verant are designing race-specific content then barbs get a great deal more of it than iksar, ogres, or trolls.

The new smithing stuff is a good example: barbs get a 2nd list of cultural armour, trolls still haven't any armour to make at all.

You can expect that to be a persistent trend: it makes commercial sense to spend the most time on stuff that is available for the biggest group of players - minorities are always going to get less.
