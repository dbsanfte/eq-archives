Valgoth Ulv'Sagart
I knew back in ST1, even before pets were fully functional and before Hunter talents were available, that the class was going to be a superior damage dealer.

Case in point, this past weekend. At 34 I was in Southshore. A 32 Undead Warlock and his 33 Undead Mage buddy killed a PVP enabling NPC and set of the local defense alerts. Normally I wouldn't engage lower levels, but there were two of them plus the Warlocks pet. I have minimal Arcane and Shadow magic resists, but was able to fend off their spells and take both of them out easily.

Sic'd pet on mage to keep him busy with stun/cast interupt (I hate being sheeped :)), then I focused on the Warlock while he sent his pet after me. I have decent armor (870'ish at the time), so the pet didn't do a whole lot of damage. The 'lock died pretty quickly, then I turned to focus on the Mage who successfully feared my pet right as I finished off his buddy. Pet had done enough damage that it only took one Aimed Shot Rank 1 (300'ish dmg), one Concussive Shot (that also stunned him) and one regular arrow. Dead Mage.

After playing my Tauren Shaman a bit lately, I've learned to really appreciate the damage my Hunter can do in ranged combat. But Tormac and Aby are right. Melee we're only half baked due to our lower armor compared to true melee classes. As long as I can have at least 8 yards between me and a mob, I'm ok (cave type dungeons are ok since I have room to send in pet and keep sufficient distance). However, building type environments (towers in particular) things can be a little more complicated and get dicey rather quickly if I'm not paying close enough attention (Tower of Althalaxx in Darkshore is a perfect example).

Overall though, I couldn't be happier with my Hunter.
