Calabar
I feel one of the deciding factors of whether or not to level directly to 65 is whom you group with and where you play. If you’d out level the people you like to group with then go ahead and earn some AAs while you wait for them. If you don’t adventure in the planes where you have access to high XP mobs there’s no real hurry to level. If there’s a specific AA ability you really want then earning it before you cap your level can be a very satisfying experience.

I’m a member of a small guild. We don’t raid. We’ve occasionally gone to the planes, but nothing higher than Plane of Nightmare or Plane of Innovation. And since I loath pickup groups its unlikely that I’ll be fighting any high-end mobs anytime soon. So I never saw any reason to focus on leveling alone. I switched to 100% AA experience at 51 to get run three. Then I switched back to regular experience until I hit level 61 so I could get my new pet spell. Then I returned to AA experience to get Cannibalize 5, which I just bought a few days ago. Now I’ve returned to regular experience to reach level 62 to match the players I’m grouping with. Once I do that I’ll have to decide whether to keep leveling or go back to AA experience and pick up a few more abilities that I like.

I’ve read many many posts that have advocated leveling strait to 65, many of which talk about how quick and easy it is to reach that level. And for many people that has worked out best for them. That just doesn’t happen to be true for me. It always takes me a long time to gain a level. I like to work on trade skills, pursue quests and help out a number of young players I know. And since I don’t fight in the higher end plane zones I gain experience rather slowly when I do focus on leveling. For example, I switched to 100% regular experience about five days ago. I’m about twenty percent into my level. It’ll most likely be another three weeks or so before I manage to ding 62. And once I do hit level 65 the only real difference is that there will be fewer mobs for me to fight in order to earn AA experience, since I still wont’ be fighting in the planes.

The one thing that has made any real difference to my game play is LDoN. If I level to 65 I’ll most likely gain pore points per adventure. And since I’ve been drooling over the armor that costs 1492 points per piece, the more points I get per adventure the sooner I’ll get that armor. That may tip the scales for me and encourage me to spend a few months getting my last four levels.

The best advice I can offer is to look at the way you play the game and decide how much of a difference being level 65 will make for you. If you have the opportunity to hunt in the higher end planes then leveling to 65 could be a great move for you. But if you’d rather have access to Cannibalize 5 while you gain those levels then you may want to pause long enough along the way to buy that ability. The bottom line is we all play this game to have fun. Listen to other people’s advice but in the end do what will be the most fun for you.
