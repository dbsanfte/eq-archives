kilher
The new HP updating has screwed things up pretty badly. Sure it's nice to have more frequent HP updates from mobs, but ALL HP updates are delayed between the server and the client. This is bad.

This results in situations like what I had happen to me last night. I died in PoEarth. Got dragged to zone in (no mobs around, fyi). Got rez'd. Looted all my gear (over 1924 HP in gear HP alone). After looting I had about 2450 or so. As I usually do after rez/looting, I double checked my HP and then hit CannV. Even with 2400+ hp I died from CannV immediately.

Of course the GMs wouldn't do anything and failed to understand, and ignored my attempts/offers to demonstrate the bug to them. I believe this is happening as a result of the delay between HP updates from client to server and/or vice versa. The client knew I had 2400+ HP, but the server hadn't been updated yet so when I cast cannV the server thought I died... and of course when it comes to death the server wins over the client any day.

At least 1 other shaman in my guild had this happen to him. Anybody else seeing this? I saw 1 other post so far, but it was buried in another thread. I felt that something this deadly to Shaman deserves its own thread.

BTW, for reference, pre-patch I always did the same routine and it never killed me (rez, loot, check HP, cannV). This was the 1st time I've died from CannV and it wasn't even my fault.

I died due to an error in reported HPs long before this patch. Your actual HPs are those on the server not on the client: the client attempts predict what your HPs are but there has always been the potential for error.

You could actually see your HPs get a torpor boost (client side) and then see your HPs fall again as the client received a HPs update from the server.
