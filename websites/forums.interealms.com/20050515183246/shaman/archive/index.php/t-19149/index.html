I will take a look at what the shaman class brought to a group back in Kunark and what it brings to a group now.
I will also list what other classes are capable of in these areas and what upgrades these classes received to form a baseline of what is acceptable in term of power increase.

Several factors, like spell recast time are left out. This should not affect the validity of this analysis as I am only interested in percentual increases.

I will NOT suggest any changes, nerfs or upgrades for other classes. If you play something other then a shaman and are unhappy with what your class gets please make your own post.

If you find something I have missed or cannot reproduce my numbers please post and I will correct/explain them.

I also stole a few suggestions from other threads and don't remember their origin. If you know please post the author so I can add them.

Please note that this is a comparison of the upgrades shaman spells have received and the upgrades other classes have received in these areas. I am not analysing spell lineups, class DPS, class power or any of the like.

Conclusion
The comparative increase in shaman mana regeneration is pitiful, especially when you consider that shaman mana regeneration is active and thus only useful during downtime - and downtime has been decreasing steadily with every expansion.
Every caster has some form of mana regeneration now. Also shaman only give mana to themselves, to be spent on inefficient shaman spells, while every other class except rangers and paladins that can grant mana regeneration gives it either to the group or is a specialist with highly efficient spells themselves.
Proposal
Give shaman an improved cannibalize spell like: casting time 3.0, recast time 6.0, 400 HP for 250 mana.
This will put shaman mana regeneration to 111 per tick, a 164% increase over Kunark values.

Conclusions
Shaman healing efficiency was upgraded much less then that of the other priests. The least increase went to Torpor/Quiescence, which only got upgraded indirectly by AAs and Foci. Shaman heal speed is downright inadequate if MOBs are not slowed immediately.
Proposal
I am going to assume the lesser efficiency of shaman heals was part of the priest balancing and thus not propose an upgrade to Kragg's or Tnarg's efficiency. A faster heal speed (at the same efficiency) is needed to outheal an unslowed MOB. Torpor however should receive a true upgrade, especially with MOBs now hitting much much harder then during Kunark.
New Torpor: 300 Mana, heals 550 per tick, slows by 50% and snares by 50%. Cast time 6 seconds, no recast.
New Tnarg's: 25% more mana, 25% more healing.

Conclusions
Resist debuffing capabilities have been slighty increased over the board, with the exception of druid fire debuffing capabilities which got tripled on top of getting a -200 resist adjustment on the main resist debuff. Slowing abilities have not been improved for the top classes but Bards and Beastlords can slow effectively now, for many practical purposes negating the slow advantage of the shaman due to cast time. This is compounded by slow mitigation.
Proposal
Give shaman a 300 mana, 75% slow with a -50 resist adjustment and a 2.0 cast time. Recast time and duration are fine as is.
Alternatively add a way (AA, buff, innate ability) to give shaman the ability to reduce slow mitigation by one step on targets no more then 5 levels higher then the shaman (i.e. most raid targets).

SUMMARY

Over the last expansions the shaman class has gotten less upgrades to their primary spell lines then other classes got in that area. If the shaman was remotely balanced it is now severely underpowered, especially in the mana regeneration and damage dealing areas. The advantage shaman once had in slowing is steadily decreasing due to more classes getting slows and slow mitigation.
In addition shaman buffing and healing power has decreased by outside factor, buffing power by stat caps and healing power by the hard hitting low HP MOBs of PoP and LDoN.

Thus small upgrades to the slow line and medium to large upgrades to the healing, nuke and DoT lines are needed.
The buff system would probably be best of with a change in focus to non-capped stats.
