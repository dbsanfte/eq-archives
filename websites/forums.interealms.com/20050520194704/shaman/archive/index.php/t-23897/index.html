Loramin
To those who think the a nerf is desperately needed, I have to ask what exactly you would have instead: New expansions with no loot? Or with loot that is crappier than existing loot? Or maybe have loot that's better than previous expansions, but have every last bit of it be no drop?

SoE wants people to buy new expansions, so expansions have items which are better than previous items. And some of those upgrades are going to be droppable, because SoE wants a server economy; if it didn't it wouldn't have made an entire zone dedicated to trading. This isn't "wrong," it's part of the game and SoE's business model, and it has been around for the life of the game. Remember, before the bazaar, there was EC.

Mudflation is not inherently bad, just as inflation isn't inherently bad. Alan Greenspan doesn't try to completely stop inflation, and if he did every economist in the country would would be up in arms, because inflation is a necessary part of healthy economic growth. Same deal with the EQ economy: it has to constantly grow to survive, and that means inflation. In EQ classic it took a lot more time to earn a thousand plat or to get a 26 AC Shaman BP than it did in Kunark, and it took even less time in Velious, etc.

Now I'm not saying every expansion should have an NPC you hail to get items that are five times as powerful as the best items PoTime. But if too much mudflation is a problem, then not enough mudflation is just as bad of a problem. What is not a problem is when new gear comes out that is in line with the existing power curve. If you stop by Samanna's Reference Desk and take look at the complete armor sets there, you can see this curve. Compare Rune Etched to Runecrafter's, Scaled Heirophant's to Scaled Avatar's, Rosrak's to Kanleku's. SoE created the curve by introducing new and better items in every single expansion. While items from OoW most certainly will affect server economies, unless those items are way off the power curve there's just no way they will ruin the EQ economy.

As for the idea that SoE expected attunable items to "stay in the guilds," that simply makes no sense: SoE pays people to spend their time finding accounts Yantis uses to sell platinum over the web. You can't understand your economy well enough to know that you need to hire GMs to deal with factors coming from outside the game, and yet somehow believe that people aren't going to trade tradeable items through appropriate channels in-game. I'll grant that the EQ Dev team is pretty bad, but they aren't that stupid.
