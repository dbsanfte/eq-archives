I thought it might be a nice change of pace from all the talk about class balance, what shamans need, and all that sorta jibba jabba to utilize the shaman collective for a little project.

I have no problems with the current state of the shaman class, but one area I would like to improve on is damage, because its always useful and always stackable. By improving damage I mean working to streamline it and be as efficient as possible, not sending emails to Sony asking for 1500 point ice nukes.

You can break down shaman damage into 4 different categories: Spell damage, damage through pets, damage through players, and increased mana/decreased mana use. You could add damage shields as a 5th i suppose, but as a shaman its not likely to ever be all that relevent.

Basically what I'm wanting to do is brainstorm for practical ways of increasing the damage in any and all of those aspects beyond the obvious. This can be done with various items and spells, but can also be done simply by play. For instance, everyone knows that pets do more damage from behind mobs, etc.

I'm sure that between everyone that posts here that there will be some really useful ideas with a little bit of effort. I'll even crown a thread winner who will be rewarded with A MAGICAL TEXT HIGH FIVE.

Like I said though, let's try and avoid the obvious, but also stay within the realm of reality here. Also, ideas aren't neccessarily the only thing of use here. Even just previously ungathered raw data can be turned into something practical.
