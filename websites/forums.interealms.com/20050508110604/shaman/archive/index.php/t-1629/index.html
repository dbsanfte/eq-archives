Zorlac Sabboth
If you have a mage with Mala then you should be ok. Always Mala/o a mob, then Tash and Occlusion of Sound then Malosini. Only then slow. If not you are going to have quite a few resists and possible aggro.

Glimmer drakes are immune to magic, only disease slowable. White wurms, blue drakes and glimmer drakes gate. Stop them from gating or have a nasty CR.

Use walls, corners and/or steps to your advantage to avoid AE of mobs.

Zorlac Sabboth
You don't need Malosini, BUT it helps a ton. One thing you don't want as a shaman in a small raid, is to draw aggro. A 2 group gig in WToV or HoT has to be very efficient and error free. You can't draw aggro or you die, then the rest of the party follows. Having always Malosini before slow will help you land slow on first try.

BlargX
The disease-based slow is very high aggro, as is the disease debuff. Put them both together and you have an excellent chance of being summoned, and even killed -- it can be very hard to taunt off you, firstly, and secondly, the mob will be only 25% slowed when pounding on you.

If you're the only shaman on the raid, I would only disease slow if it makes no difference whether you die every time you do it. In other words, if it doesn't slow your pull rate down too much. I'm not sure whether that applies to your situation or not, because that depends how well equipped the people you're going with are and what their levels are. If you're new to ToV, though, they probably have neither very high levels nor very high level equipment, so I would be very careful about when to disease slow or not. Definitely, if you have a disease slow resisted and then try it again, you can expect to die.

If you're mid-50's and such, I would seriously consider bringing in some outside people to bring your total up to more than two groups. That's a small number for people who aren't used to the place and don't have the levels or equipment yet either.
