Depends on which cave, really. I stuck with the first cave (also called small cave or non-laborer cave) at your level. If you do that on, just invis in past the first two, and get a pet next to Felodious Sworddancer. Pull the right hand mob from entrance as you face them. Kill. Then wait a minute (canna, med, whatever) and pull left. Wait another minute. Pull right hand mob at bottom of ramp. I think you see the pattern. At 39-40, you may only be able to do these 4 spawns, but if you are keeping up okay, the next 2 at the bend work the same way, pull right first. Pulling left may get you two mobs (one around the corner), seems to for me about half the time. I just made 45 and I can take the whole cave this way now. XP is very slow at 45 (almost all LB, with a few DB), so I have moved on, although I may come back for more acrylia as needed.

Things to watch: If no one is in the other cave, the laborer will wander in to your cave as well. Surprise! =) Also, the grimling runner paths past the cave if you keep an eye out. Nice cash in bazaar for the pattern if you are not KOS in Katta.

snapdragn
If you hunt the Laborer Mine, you can set up your camp in a *absolutely* safe location.

Standing in the entrance of the Mine, step out and turn to the left, run up the hill towards a boulder. There is a space of about 10ft or so between the wall and that boulder. NOTHING paths up that area, and it is a good place to go /afk, or med, or whatever.

I hunted this zone in my early 40s, and the laborer cave is much better than the other one. Easy to single pull mobs all day long, and the laborer drops a nice little bonus. Plus, since you are outside the cave, much easier to disengage and head to zoneline if you get in trouble.

at 39 - 40 I could clear the first 4 or 5 mobs, once I got a REIF I could clear about 7 or so. Good loot for the level.

Juxtadude
I am having a lot of fun at the laborer grimling caves as a 40-41 shaman. This is the camp described above behind the rock near the wall. Yes wolves do roam nearby, but I either kill them or stay behind the rock and they don't aggro me.

I have found the grimlings to be very easy blues and I can get by with a simple slow and melee debuff. The reason I am not using dots is that I am working on raising 1hb and Evocation since they were both around 90 when I started this camp. Now they are both around 110 and I'm halfway through 41.

I am using a centi warhammer, fbss, ikky regen bp, and 2 6ac/65hp rings. My AC is around 700 and my hp are 1111. So with Fury, Haste, Chloro, Bear form and standard AC and stat buffs, I simply pull with slow (Tagar's, not the newest one to save mana) follow up with Listless Power and tank it. Once it gets low on health I zap it with Spirit Strike (81 dmg) and once it flees I hit it again so I don't have to chase it. That's it. I usually take so little damage that I can even use Canni 2 a couple times during battle to keep from regening to full hp. I usually only pull the roamers and the 2 outside, then either break the guys just past the entrance (watch for geomancers) or I will single pull a wolf or bat. When fighting wolves or bats I also use a VoS to augment my damage. Also the foreman is tougher and I use VoS on him.

I use a slightly different strategy on geomancers. I don't bother with slow or debuffs. I VoS from max range and run over the hill so it can't cast on me. Once it appears on top of the hill, I sick pet on him and run over to attack fast and interrupt. Use fast weapon and drop bear form so you can slam. I generally drop a couple larger nukes (149 or 216 variety) to kill him fast. Problem is, as with all casters, sometimes I end battle with full hp, sometimes I have 2 bubbs left. Not very consistent but I can kill them.

I like it because I can raise my skills, the loot is decent (Katta nearby for selling), and I have killed a couple runners (see Rallic Pack quest).
