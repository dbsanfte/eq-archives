Cimms
Low level soloing without good equipment is easy. Pre lvl 14 nothing really hits you that hard that your newbie quest armor or even leather can't handle. Once you get root at lvl 14, just root and rot the mobs that give you a hard time. Make sure you get hit though to keep defensive skills up. I was soloing lvl 30 wearing banded and using a rusty spear long ago before the bazaar made gear much more available.

Azzikai
Starting from scratch can be rather daunting, particularly when there is little to no economy to speak of.

Probably the best solution would be to make your own banded armor in the interim. There is a wonderful guide out there - http://www.crimsonsnow.com/pdfs/guides.html - that will help you skill up your smithing without losing a ton of cash (even making some money in some instances). That should help take care of some of your gear issues.

There are also some quests in PoK that net some items that do help a bit because of the focus effects. One is a Mask that will require the aid of invis pots or someone to come with you and the other is a ring.

Sure, they're not the absolute greatest of items and on other servers you could buy better in the bazaar for 20pp but when a server is young and there isn't much in the way of an economy you do what you have to.

In Butcherblock mountains there are goblins that roam about (and have camps) as well as "Greenblood" ogres. Both of which are extremely low level. They both drop items which can be sold for a few pp each (heads, blood and insignias <-- I think).

Calabar
As much as I love my shaman, I have to agree with Judana. If the only comparison your making between shaman and beastlords is how easy it is to solo with little or no equipment I feel the beastlord is far and above the easiest class to play. I’ve leveled my barbarian beastlord to 54 and I honestly feel that if I took him out in nothing but his birthday suit I could still solo in the same zone he’s spent the last few levels. He would still have his spells, he could still fight hand to hand if he had to and his pet would rip the mobs to shreds.

And it’s a good thing too. Because one of the drawbacks to the BL class, at least on my server, is that there’s a HUGE price gap between mid level equipment and higher-level equipment. We’re talking from 200pp to 10K pp to upgrade my character’s chest armor. And weapon upgrades are usually worse.

Still, if equipment is difficult to obtain for you, a beastlord might be a good character choice to get yourself established. Then once you have gained enough levels to earn some decent coins you can create a shaman and start having some real fun. :D
