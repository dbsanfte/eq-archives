Goldark
Specialisation and SCM both give you a guaranteed, direct reduction in the mana cost of the spell being cast. The SCM bonus applies equally to all spells.

Specialize skill 50: ~3% mana cost reduction.
Specialize skill 200: ~11% mana cost reduction.
Spellcasting Mastery III: A further ~10% mana cost reduction

I think the confusion over specialisations is that sometimes people add these up, so overall on non spec spells you get 12.5% savings, and on spec skills you get a total of ~20% savings. The contribution from SCM is the same for both though.

The tricky thing is deciding how important 10% mana reduction is when compared to all the other AA. I find mana regen/pool isn't a huge issue in most fights with Canni V and normally at least one of kei/Pot9/SP/Wind of Marr but its normally possible to find something to do with more mana if I have it.
taraddar
Goldark is right. Specialization is a constant mana savings on all spells based on the level of the skill. Spell casting mastery is an addition 10% mana savings on all spells cast.

Mana pres focus items are a random 1 to x% mana saved on every cast.

Both can be pretty easily confirmed now a days with the % bars for mana. Casting a large mana spell it's possible to see the the difference in cost especially if you decrease your mana pool size. For example in my normal gear a PoP imbue spell is anywhere from 10-13% of my mana pool. Take off my mp item and it's varries by 1% at most. Affliction efficiency is an even easier way to see this since it's a 25% mana pres or 30% for bracer of darkness.
