Well, if you're bow kiting, 2 things are going to affect your skillup rate (that I've seen experienced and read around here).

#1) Delay on the bow: less delay = more arrows in the air in less time. You'll go through the same amount or more in arrows, but it'll take less time. (Delay on a Trueshot I think is 45 so you're looking at getting 3 arrows out in the time you were shooting 2 before)

#2) DEX: Higher dex = more chance of skillups in most if not all weapon catergories including archery. If you have the extra cash, grab a GBS and a JBoM for your weapons when bow plinking. That's +30 dex just in those 2.

Of course this is all my opinion, It wouldn't be the first time I was wrong!

Well just a few notes on a couple questions

1. On raising your archery skill. If your goal and endatate is only archery (and not worrig about your fletching skill) then continue to make the cheapest arrow available (personally I used the 1 damage 150 range ones ....).

a. There are a couple of items listed above to improve your dex (rate of skillgain) Tailor your outfit to maximize your dex when you are working skill ups.

b. Save up the cash dear and buy 2 items. One is a fleetiing quiver The quiver is tailored -- best bet is to collect up the items and find a GM tailor to hit the combine key for you or be prepared to spend 2k on one in the Drinal Bazaar. Do not know the prices on your server .... The quiver offers the best bow haste that you can get -- increasing the number of shots you can get off while pulling before the mob comes into melee range. At lower level it is then best to go melee for damage at that point

c. The other item dear is a faster bow. At lower levels, you are more concerned about the speed of your bow to keep up your archery as you level. Remember is it level x 5 + 5 = max skill level for most of the haul to 240. Select a bow with a very low delay-- there are several no drop bows that you my be able to get, or another ption is to go to Trak Teeth and wait for someone to do the hunter forager cycle so they can get their Seblis key. If I recall that bow is a 19 50 bow with 3 dex. Anything is better "speedwise" as compared to the 61 delay that you are currently using. If you have the funds to purchase a Raincaller you get a 45 delay bow with 6 STR & DEX. Older rangers are selling their old ones for about 2000pp these days on my server.

d. Another technique is while waiting for a group, ask them to plunk away at their mobs for skill gain. This is especially nice if there is a higher level tolan bracer wearing ranger or a mage who can summon some arrows for you. Most older rangers will happily summon arrows, if they are not the designated puller (1 cast between pulls was common for me before I got EQuiver) If not craft your arrows as you go, having placed stacks of supplies or arrows in your bag before you go LFG. Most people realize that archery should not mess up the aggro too much on the lower level hunting groups.

e. Lastly do not forget the people in your group, or friends whose buffies (haste, Boon (255dex) or a host of the shammy stat buffie lines) can help you out.

2. I was advised as a young ranger to buy my Trueshot bow and then, over time, slowly build up my fletching skill, crafting only skill gaining arrows for when I pull -- or crafting cheap arrows for those power rchery sessions. Then when I hit level 50, then speand the time and energy, and most important to a broke ranger, the MONEY to raise the fletching skill to where you want it to be. Just one rangers point of view, but I liked it so I took it. (/em credits Ranger Kodiakjak on Drinal)

3. As for Damage increases based on range-- well I do not see any differance really. However having a good clean LOS is always nice. If you can find that grassy knoll (gnoll) from which to fire from, pun intended, you can more often hit the target, without worring about melees jumping in your LOS.

4. At level 40 you will be fear kiting some of the wildlife in Eastern Wastes for a portion of your exp. On those hunting days, another technique is to ffear kite the animal to low health to the point where he is snared and running slow. Then sheath your high damge weapon back off, and equip your orc shovel, or your bow and work on skill gains. Just a technique, and it is your call.

5. Persoanlly, I did most of my gaining while smacking on other peoples msb, waiting for my spot to open up in a group. Took my 2HB from (3) to (Max) while waiting on spider groups in Velks Lab. Although I never use a 2HB these days since tossing my Goranga 2HB, it is nice to know that if you need to use that skill, then you have it available to you.
