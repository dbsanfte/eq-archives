Uman
QB1: -10% casting time for 3aa
5.4 sec torpor

QB2: -25% casting time (another -15% for 6aa)
4.5 sec torpor

QB3: -50% casting time (another -25% for 3aa)
3.0 sec torpor

As you can see, QB1 is the most bang for your AA. QB2 is a good choice if you plan on getting high level spell haste focus items that aren't limited by spell duration (enhancement haste won't affect torpor). QB3 would make spell haste focus useless since the total cap is 50%, but would be a great choice if you have nothing else to spend the AA on.

BlargX
SCR3 is not 15%, it's 30%. That and SCRM, for another 20%, give you a total of 50% extended buffs and torpor.

I posted detailed opinions about this elsewhere, but they came down to basically, I thought the best thing to do was find what helps shamans most and work on that, with the others as accessories.

To me that was torpor and canni. It makes sense, because the ways we can improve torpor and canni add very dramatically to our power. Not just a little -- a ton.

Canni 5 is no question the first thing you should do, but you have it. So the rest of the truly important stuff is all about torpor.

Which aspect of torpor mattered most?

I asked myself what my most dramatic problems were. Solo, if I died or had real problems, it was almost always because I got too many interrupts before a torpor was able to land. I could almost always land a slow sooner or later; landing torpor was the problem, and doing it seemed to solve most problems. Grouping, the biggest problem was the same thing, plus not being able to land torpor on OTHERS fast enough due to interrupts or what have you.

So I went with quickbuff, so I could land torpor faster. The first level, frankly I didn't notice. The second level seemed HUGE. The third level I put off for a while.

Well, then I mixed in SCR and ND. My hitpoints really aren't so hot, so I found myself casting canni 5 less often than I wanted to. I like to do it during fights when possible, but it was always tricky, because low hitpoint aggro isn't a pleasant thing.

I picked up a couple of hundred points by the time I got through ND3. This definitely helped me max canni 5 use. SCR3 made my torpor last 30% longer, which really made a huge difference -- an extra tic on torpor was pretty mighty, bigger than it sounds. And longer hastes are a godsend. I think someone said you can get an extra tick out of torpor just by getting SCR2. I was going to power through to SCRM too though, so I pushed through SCR3 as the prerequisite. SCR3 was very, very worth it.

If I remember right, I snuck in at one level or two of SCM. It's been a while now so it's harder to remember. I was very unimpressed. Ultimately, SCM3 is supposed to save you some nice mana and add up. In truth, I found it hard to notice. Saving mana is simply not a huge priority with me as a shaman compared to some other things. Certainly even with SCM3, I didn't notice anything as dramatically beneficial as I got from ND3, QB2 and 3, SCR3, or SCRM. That's a LOT of points right there -- all of them better value than SCM in my opinion. ALL very notable and useful. I think I got SCM2, found it nothing to cheer about, then went back to it out of a sense of obligation almost after I got SCRM. SCRM rocked the house. Eventually I went back and got QB3.

I'd recommend all the things that effect torpor and canni. At this point, my survivability and the general ease of doing things is far greater, as I can cast in a torpor that lasts 50% longer in half the time for only 160 mana(specialization plus SCM3). A 3.0 heal for 1800(with a possible extra tick sneaking in for 2100) is a huge deal and a massive improvement. That stuff will make a difference you can feel every session.

What else have I gotten so far? Nothing near as important. I don't have too much more than 110 AA. Radiant cure 1 was very nice, spiritcall is fun and free damage. Combat agility 2 -- I suppose it's probaby good. Can't say it's making a difference I really notice. Critical Fury 1 -- it's very nice to crit! Torrents are 720 max, ice strike(with a focus item) I've gotten from the normal 675 to 1022 I think it was, 1021, whatever. But...it doesn't happen often enough. Can't say it adds up to a lot of damage overall, but it only costs a few points, so seems worth it for at least one level. Healing gift 1 -- seems pretty nice when it works -- actually I think I've only critted a heal 3 times in the weeks I've had it, but one might well have saved my life. When you need a heal, sometimes you need it bad. Probably a fully maxed healing gift and its PoP version will be very nice when using the level 62 Tnarg's Mending -- not worthwhile really till then, as you can crit on chloroblast but not kraggs. Crits seem exceedingly rare so far, because I do so much of my healing with torpor or kraggs. MGB -- almost never have used it. Might have been the first person of any class on my server to get it; doubt I've used it more than a dozen times. If you're the only shaman in your guild, its value rises a lot.

Well, that's the stuff I can remember. None of it is worth a damn compared to QB/SCR/SCRM/ND, and to a lesser extent SCM. SCM is eventually a keeper because you might as well save mana, right? Even if it's undramatic. And ND is not nearly as important for shamans with better gear and plenty of hitpoints. But for day in day out always-there and always-working utility, I'd go QB/SCR/SCRM in that order. QB3 may or may not be a priority depending on your focus gear, but QB2 is very dramatic; I truly believe it is by far the best thing to get after canni 5. Er...assuming you have torpor.
