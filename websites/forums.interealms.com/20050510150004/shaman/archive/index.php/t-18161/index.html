Thorzion
If you are having a problem with mobs hitting to hard, go back an expansion or 3. Mobs in Kunark (OT, Swamp ofno hope, FV) don't hit nearly as hard as mobs in DSP, and the exp is comperable. Mobs in Velious (IC at 30ish) hit harder, but not much.

I would aslo second LoY zones, but be warned that mobs there have alot of HP for their levels. You might do well to use a combination of Root/rot to 50% and them melee them the rest of the way. The undead there dont run, but others will which can cause nasty trains. Stay away from diseased drogmors, as I seem to remember their HP regen being insanely high.

Turmoiltoad
Raise your 2hb skill and buy a "blight, hammer of the scourge". It procs Scourge, a level 34 DoT at level 30. Pull with slow and melee away. I also had a Staff of Living Sand that procs a DoT at level 20 and I would interchange them once the first one proc'd. At level 30, I was soloing the Gnolls in South Karana at the spires and at 34 I did the Tizmaks in GD until 38 when I went to the Gunthak Beach. By level 40, have a soulstripper and continue in Gunthak until 43. Then go to Dulaks and start camping for your 44th spell Stoicism and the xp will still keep on coming in. At 44, do the elementals in Nadox for the best solo xp in the game.

illitian icerage
At those levels I would pull with slow (even though it doesn't do too much), put autoattack on, throw a DoT on or two, root, back up and regain hp/mana. When root breaks, melee for a bit, then root and rot it away until death.

With that method, the idea is not to lose too much HP or mana. At 34 it gets so much easier. I normally ended up between 70 and 90 hp and mana after fights. Not too bad when you can always cani back up.
