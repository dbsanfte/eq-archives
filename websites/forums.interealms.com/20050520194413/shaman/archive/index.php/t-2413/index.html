When you are talking about the new SoL uber mobs, you are talking serious high AC. Melee are hitting for 1's and 2's or not at all. Often the uber mob also rampages and flurries.

It is argued that when you cripple a mob, they rampage and/or flurry less. This seems to hold true from my experiences...

Cripple lowers AC/Str/Dex by an significant amount. Allowing melee to hit for 3's and 4's instead of the 1's and 2's. For the duration of a 20 minute fight, the slight increase in melee effectiveness is rather significant. Add that to the supposedly less flurry/rampage and you have a mob that's doing less DPS and your melee doing more DPS.

So I guess the point is, cripple is useless vs. mobs that die in 10 seconds and old world mobs who don't got much AC anyways. However against the new SoL uber mobs like IV, Shei, THO, Grieg... even SoV Tormax, Statue, Vindi type mobs... cripple is a great help.

Graal the Dorf
The effects of cripple are relative.

If a mob has high AC compared to the atk of the melees attacking it, cripple can have a dramatic effect on their damage output. If the mob has low AC compared to the melees atk, it doesn't do much, they are already hitting on the high end of their damage curve.

If a mob has high atk compared to the tank's AC, cripple can cut damage taken by a fair amount. If the tank's AC is more than enough to handle the mobs atk, it doesn't do much.

Cripple gives the biggest bang for the buck on harder mobs and/or when your melees have low atk or AC.

It generally isn't a usefull xp spell unless the mob will take a while to die, such as small groups or soloing. It really helps the damage output of pets.
