I can easily heal a normal LDoN, and I've played ranger with another shaman healing/slowing in a completely non-uber (bazaar level) group - the type of group who can't cope with high risks.

I've been healer in a high risk LDoN, though that does require a suitable group makeup. (We had a good bard pulling mostly singles).

I have geared up with healing in mind, and have my GoD spells. I am a better healer than a lot of druids, because I pay attention, I have the focus effects, I have the spells and I have the AA. People look for druids as healers, but don't take everything into account. It's annoying to be in a decent group of 5 and hanging around waiting for a druid to come online because people don't want shaman as a secondary healer.

Okay so, I got a little fed up waiting for a druid or another cleric to come online as secondary healer in a trial group, so I figured I'd run some numbers.

Using myself as an example, I have Daluda's Mending, max healing AA, 30% beneficial spell haste and Marr's Gift focus. I am not a bad secondary healer. Compare to the druid we added to the group, who's not a bad healer, but doesn't have beneficial spell haste, and doesn't yet have his new GoD heal. In the group, he was chain casting the %age heal, but that's not affected by spell haste anyway.

Karana's Renewal: 4680 heal (75% max) in 10 seconds. With AHA3 (19%), is 5569 hp max, recastable in 12.25 seconds. It can't crit and it's not affected by any spell haste or heal focus, so that's it.

So the most you'll get out of KR is 5569 / 12.25 = 454.6 HP /sec heal

Daluda's Mending: 1550 heal in 3.75 seconds. With 30% beneficial spell haste, and BoR, this is cut down to 2.3 seconds (recast in 4.55). With AHA3, it heals 1844.5, with Marr's Gift, that goes up to an average of 2075.

i.e. I can heal 456 hp /sec *not* including crits.

Along similar lines, if the druid doesn't have beneficial spell haste, and doesn't have the GoD spell (which was the case last night with a druid we added to fill that "healing" slot), with AHA3 and BoR, he will splash heal for a max of 416.5 hp/sec.

Yes, I know I'm not comparing like with like here, but my point is that people naturally assume that druids > shamans as healers, but it all depends on the druid and the shaman.

It's not as straightforward as it appears.
