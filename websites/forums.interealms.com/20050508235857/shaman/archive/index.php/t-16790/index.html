corlathist
"Under powered" vs "Overpowered" vs "balanced" is all dependant on
"point of view" and really what you look at, and also when your looking.

Here's my own completely personal point of view playing level 65 Druid 280ish AA, and Level 65 Shaman 250ish AA. It's also dependant on being in elemental level guild.

Group Desirability.
Pre-new Heals/POP: Druids way underpowered. Shamans way overpowered.
Reason: druids werent good at any of the traditional roles: main heal, slow, tank, dps.

I'd get random tells in zone on shaman from complete strangers "are you lfg" and never on druid.

POP: Druids little underpowerd. Shamans little overpowered.
No slow = No POP Group especially at elemental level.

Druids issue is mainly issue with KR being 600 Mana for 4750. A druid is way behind efficiency curve with a cleric when it comes to XP grinding.
A Cleric with Mana Regen 7 and 4k Mana Pool, makes a better main healer then a Druid with Mana Regen 24 and 5k Mana Pool.
And XPing in Elemental Planes, Enchanters/Shaman are the only good options. Bard 50% is too light for average xp grind. BST 65 can be issue with landing it fast if you dont have a debuff line.

Still find my group looking for slower (Enc/Shaman) in Guild Chat then looking for healer (Cler/Druid). Further complicated by average raiding guild #s. We have a "ton" of shaman at 8. and 7 Chanters. Some of which don't really XP often. During Heavy Time Zone XP time....
Id peg it at 5 to 8 slowers most of the time in guild xping. On other side, we got probably twice that number of clerics and druids especially when you include the cleric bots. At any given time there's usually 10 to 15 Druid/Clerics xping.

Biggest issue, is that the dynamics of what a raid needs number wise, doesn't usually fit what a guild needs xp group wise. I would suspect in most raiding guilds, shaman have a far easier time getting a guild group than druid.

LDON:
Druids Relatively Balanced.
do well competeing with clerics. they also preform decent DPS
at elemental level especially in burst depeartment. About only complaint is outdoor only spell lines and LDON being indoor.
but they are equally valuable to a cleric in LDON. Better here, worse there.

Shaman: don't usually compete for main healer role. Though they can
with Quiessence do the role well. They compete for slower spot with bards, bsts, and chanters mostly. Depending on group dynamics = which is best slower.

You can do 2 Shaman no cleric/druid group better than you can do 2 Cleric/Druid no slower. Roughly equal in terms of getting a group, maybe a tiny edge to shaman in guild chat but I chalk that up to Bards being way underrepresented in EQ, and BST being underrepresented due to newness.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

Soloing:

In some ways Shaman better, some ways Druids.
Shaman can kill summoners more reliably than druids. Shaman who Cann Dance can out XP per hour druid (especially if they have Jbb/Fungi)
in the 50's. Druids however can quad which while it may not be as much xp per hour then a shaman can dancing, is a heck of a lot easier in terms of player effort over time.

Do a quad, then sit and med and relax, go to bathroom, watch tv, play video game, etc while waiting for mana. While shaman to reach that level of XP has to continually be working for Cann Dance advantage.

Shaman could also solo mobs in luclin era that would crush a druid.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

Raid/Guild Desirbility

Druids were way underdesired pre-pop new heals.
After new heals, they got some recruitment, usually though only as a substitute for a guild being short clerics. Wood was a major improvement here. MGB Wood, can be a very valuable asset to a guild.

PS Edit/Add: the other issue was mainly spell dropping and normal AA levels at the time.
Pre-POP you needed Mazarins + POTG > Aego/Ancient Aego.
Mazarins was very rare. Plus not as many MGBs available. This meant
many guilds just MGBed Aego and didnt care about the druid line.

POST-POP. Kazad is a lot easier to get than Mazarins. Virtue/Nine equally rare. And heck Kazad + POTG > Aego/Ancient. Plus more MGBs
this has made Druid Buff more valuable than it used to be.

Shaman: dont stack as well as druids in some ways. But if a guild is short shaman, they hurt more than fi they are short druids.



-------------------------------------------------------------------

Overall:
I think pre-pop, shaman were one of the highest powered classes.
now a days they are balanced. POP added some power to the shaman class, just not as much relatively as to other classes. This has leveled the playing field quite a bit.

Druids were underpowered, but really got more than most as of late.
The biggest flaw lately is still the Harmony line's outdoor only tag.
And Karana's Renewal being too inefficient. ((Shouldnt be equal to CH of clerics. just its not close enough))

EditPS: AA totals are NOT a good judge. As thats usually reflective of play time. In my guild it looks like this:
1) 500s/Maxed AA: 1 Necro, 1 Rogue
2) 400's = 1 Warrior, 1 Shaman, 1 Bard
3) 300 = 2 Druids, 1 Pally,

So if you try to judge it by power, rogues are tied for the most powerful class. With warriors not far behind... NOT
