Weyalan
In terms of natural regeneration of mana (as opposed to cannibalising for mana), you get more mana regeneration sitting down than you do standing up. I don't know the exact figures, but lets say, for the sake of argument, that you get 1 mana per tick standing and 10 mana per tick sitting (a "tick" being 6 seconds)

Mana regeneration is not continuous: That is to say that that, when sitting, you do not get 1 mana every 0.6 seconds over the 6 seconds. You actually get 0 mana for 5.999 seconds, and then you get all 10 mana in 0.001 seconds of the actual tick-over

This means that you can run, jump ride horses and play the piano for most of the 6 second period as long as you are sitting down when the actual tick-over occurs. So, anyhting that you can do in between tick-overs does not effect your natural mana regen, as long as you are sitting at the right time.

Canni-dancing takes advantage of this non linear natuaral mana regeneration. Bascically, because it is possible to stand up, cast canni, andsit down in less than 6 seconds, it is possible to benefit from both natural (10) and canni (35) mana regen. And the natural mana has the advantage that you do not have to sacrifice hp for it.

The important thing is, though; when to be sitting? When is the actual tick-over? The easiest way to know is to open your inventory and watch your current hp display (we will assume that you have a regen spell running). The tick-over is when your hp make the icrease from the regen (hp regen is also not continuous, but comes in discrete bursts at the tick-over).

You can be thankful you were not playing back in the days when the only way to way to see tick-over was to stare at your hp bar and try and see a 15hp jump in a 1 inch bar that was displaying 2000hp.


So, in summary, it not the 2 seconds of sitting, its what happens when you are sitting, if you are sitting at the time when it happens!
