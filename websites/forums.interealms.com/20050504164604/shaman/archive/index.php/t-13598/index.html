Warrior is fun to play, but also frustrating at times, like when I have to deal with Paladins that say " I have to cast stuns, it's what my class does" As things stand now, Warrior taunt and ability to hold agro compared to Knights is horrible, but there are rumblings that it might be looked at in the future. Agro is not only the Warriors job, but managing it is everyone in the groups.

You stand as close to the mob as possible to get proximity agro, and hope that the other melees stand at max melee range. Nothing more annoying than almost standing on some rooted mob's toes and still having it turn and take whacks at the Back-stabbing Rogue. Damage and defense are the same regardless of your proximity to the mob, so if you're tanking, stand on it's toes...if not, take a step back and get away from it's body odor

Warriors direct the fight, prioritizing which mobs to kill when more than one in camp, so they need to know which mobs are unslowable/unmezzable. They have to keep an eye out on the rest of their party in case they have to use their shield disc.

If your Warrior is dual wielding, you can "Ghetto bash". Keep your inventoryopen, or bag if you have a UI that has your primary/secondary slots added to the bag window. Watch your battlespam, and once your offhand weapon has swung, quickly switch in your shield, hit bash, and switch back to weapon. You can do this in the time your melee attack recycles, Bashing with the shield deals more damage than kicking.(Bash damage is based on the AC of the shield you use. At lvl 54 I had a 90+ crit with bash. Biggest kick crit I've had is 62) Under level 55, bash can also stun and interrupt spell-casting mobs, and even after 55, when kick has a chance to stun, it stuns less often than bashing does.

I always love the feeling of finishing getting hit by multiple mobs at the same time and still be standing at the end, be it the whole camp of Orc 2 in EC at level 8-9, or a courtyard train at the entrance to karnors at 50-51. Course, I partner with my g/f's Cleric, since soloing is almost impossible unless you're massively twinked after about level 40 or so.

Any class can be easy to play I guess. I suck at multi-tasking, so I don't do a whole lot of outside chatting in group or guild when I'm in exp group, I need to keep watch on too much when I play either class

Maybe it's not for you though. Whatever the case, don't worry about min/maxing things and just have fun. Good luck

Ahhh... no. Taunt doesn't give X points of agro. Taunt simply puts the warrior to the top of the hate list. Hence, you taunt to gain agro, you don't taunt to build it. It's pointless taunting if the mob is already on you.

A good warrior will not taunt all the time, he will save it to taunt the instant he loses agro, to regain it that much quicker. If he's taunting every time the button ungreys, it's likely that when someone gets agro from him, he'll have to wait a few seconds to regain it.

A good warrior will choose his weapons wisely - it's not important for him to do lots of damage, but it is important that he can hold agro. So it's worth it for a warrior to drop his DPS a little if it brings him more taunt. Even if they have a fairly low budget, they can work with it, you don't need Enraging Blow weapons to be effective. For example, mid 50s, a Silken Whip of Ensnaring is a better weapon than a Lamentation off-hand. A nice new weapon from LoY is a Barnacle Covered Axe. It's only 9/21, but the proc adds hate.
