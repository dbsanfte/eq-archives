Cool!

My wife and I are both in the beta. My initial impression is that it's visually very pleasing, but the interface is a bit clunky if you do NOT have a keyboard. My wife uses a keyboard, I intentionally did not get one, but I think I may get one because it's a pain to use without it - you just can't talk to anyone except canned, preplanned responses. Definately plan on getting a keyboard if you are going to play this game.

The newbie quests seem very well planned out. They remind me of the newbie quests for the Luclin release - they'll take you to lvl 3-5 no problem before they set you loose. However, the problem with any of that is that when the floodgates open and people start pouring in, they will indeed be fighting each other over rats and bats and the like, no question. Give it a few weeks to stabilize, but it's going to be hell the first few weeks.

I have had persistent connection problems, and have reconfigured my LAN a couple of different ways with no luck. We always get dropped after 15-45 minutes online. If we attempt to take two PS2s on line at the same time, the second machine connects, and then usually within 1 minute (but once it took 5 minutes) one or the other connection will drop.

Our LAN configuration is a LinkSys BEFW11S4 (V2) wireless router, connected to a LinkSys WET11 wireless bridge, connected to a LinkSys 4-port hub. Wireless connectivity is good in the location we're using it, I've checked it with a laptop in that location. I can play PC EQ on that laptop no problem over the same connection.

So with the constant disconnects, it's really tough to get a good reading on how the game is. I've only played through level 8 or so.

I like the way death is handled, targeting (cycle through visible targets), the graphics, the quests. However, if you are a PC EQ player, I'm not sure that there's a whole lot here for you to make you want to switch. Graphics aren't enough, at least not for me. I haven't gotten high enough to see what the midgame is like, though.
