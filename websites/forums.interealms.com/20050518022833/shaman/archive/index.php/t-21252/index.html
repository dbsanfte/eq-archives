temuchintehkhan
a lot of players are skeptical how arenanet can pull off this thing without a monthly fee

well, the short answer is they'll be making less money than soe did with eq... and they wont be able to fund multiple other games out of the pot

people tend to be conservative in their outlooks so a lot of players hold the assumption now that subscriber-based MMPOG is the only way to go. but this is not so. the game that set the revenue model that most MMPOG now operate was Ultima Online in 97. it's safe to say that kelly flock would have pulled the plug on EQ in 98 if it weren't for the success of this revenue model of selling a box THEN a monthly fee THEN hardware upgrade fees (for new servers and server upgrade) through expansions. but as close as the weeks of beta there was a furious debate whether UO should a)go with a monlthy subscriber fee with no upfront charge for the game b)monthly fee and also a box charge c)monthly fee and reduced box charge d)no monthly fee just box fee e)no monthly fee no box fee just a large one time subscription fee ($100 or so at the time) etc etc

the decision was made to go with b to maximize profits. they didn't know whether anyone would actually pay to play this huge amount per year but OSI was willing to do the bet... as even within the Ultima franchise Ultima Online was just a small segment thus small gamble

of course this lead to HUGE massive profits since the service basically costs zero to run BUT it's clearly NOT the only tenable way to run a MMPOG. sounds like arenanet expects higher gross units to be sold with no fee and perhaps also lower costs through more porous retention (people dont play as much or as long)

of course this revenue model is much less profitable but if you look at an actual competitive landscape (as opposed to the captive one EQ enjoyed for years) it's probably a more realistic one for a small company's first game

look where all those massive profits got sony... they used the warchest to bloat EQ2 to a 25 million budget in a lengthy development cycle that turned over the entire team a number of times. they bought out OSI's entire austin studio and funded the development of pie-in-the-sky SWG. paid for the entire development of planetside. countless other side projects and useless extentions of EQ

point is, no company needs such huge profits to the point they dont know what to do with the windfall warchest... hunger and nimbler companies who have to actually work for their revenue might have never delivered LoY or GoD
