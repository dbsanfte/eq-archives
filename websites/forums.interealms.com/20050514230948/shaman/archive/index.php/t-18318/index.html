What is the most efficient way to quickly buff a raid, and more importantly, rebuff freshly rezzed people?

Currently, rezzed people are supposed to send tells to the appropriate buffers, but this is far too chaotic and time consuming. I proposed a buffing channel, but no one else in the guild seemed to think much of the idea.

My guild just started playing around with the RZTW encounter, so that should give you an idea of where we are. Most of our buffing classes have MGB, so that's not really an issue.

So please, if you have a simple and efficient method, I'd love to hear it before I go insane.
Njorls
Sending tells for buffs after dying is the best way of doing it. Training the raid force not to ask for buffs in the raid channel is a big big plus.

Designating someone to rebuff people after deaths is nice if you can find people who do it. Having a shaman channel with people communicating who they're buffing helps out. Communicating who has MGB up and when to use it helps as well. Having recruit shaman who are eager to please handling rebuffing is a big plus as well.

There's a danger to being the best shaman buffer, you're going to be the one who gets bugged the most for buffs on raids.

Ultimately though, it's up to each person to make sure they aren't missed. And there are certain rules I push as well.

Things I have tried to emphasize in my guild:

-Only group leaders should be asking for group buffs. If the group leader is afk or just being a dip****, then you tell me who the GL is so I know who to target and I can ignore the 4 other tells that the rest of your group sends me for Fo7.

-Be specific and tell me exactly what buffs you want. Some people want CHA others don't want to waste the buff slot on it. Some have maxxed STA others don't... etc. YOU should know what buffs you need.

-If you are going to send me a tell for a buff, do a /tar sciek first and make sure you are close enough for me to /rt and buff you. I am not going to go running around to find you. Be in range or don't bother me.

-If you ask me for agility, I will put you on ignore for the rest of the night if not longer.

Blazz
Our guild has assigned buffers, 1 for each. This way folks don't have to stop mem and re-mem spells. I can keep FoS (or whatever buff I am assigned) memmed and not worry about it.

We also have a "Send tells for buffs" policy. If I see someone get rezzed in, I hit them with my assigned buff, but its not always that easy. Sending tells does 2 things. It keeps /raidsay clear of some spam and it allows easy targeting. I have a little macro set up:

/rt
/pause 10 (in case of a minor lag spike, 1 second delay)
/cast 5 (the spell slot my "assigned buff" is in)

Works like a charm. I have also suggested to the "high volume death people" (Bard and monk pullers, wizzies who never learn, Chanters, etc) that they make up a quick macro themselves:

/tell Blazz FoS please
/tell Cleric_01 Symbol Please
/tell Shaman_02 STA please
/tell Druid_01 9 Please
/tell Chanter_01 VoQ please
/tell Chanter_02 Haste please

The names can be easily edited for each raid, as buffing duties rotate. I also keep a macro for:

/rs Blazz for Focus, Shaman_02 for STA, send TELLS.

and spam it a couple times after those ever-popular "1/4 wipes".
