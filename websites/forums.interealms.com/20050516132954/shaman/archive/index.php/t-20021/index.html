Thorzion
My experience with an enchanter goes to level 52. Thats not high end by any means, but it has given me an insight into some of the ins and outs of enchanting. Since you asked, heres a rundown of my experience.

Playing an enchanter can be a very frustrating experience at the same time that it can be very rewarding. There is no other character (other than shaman for me, actually) where I can have such a feeling of accomplishment after locking down, debuffing and controlling a 4+ mob overpull; but all it takes to turn that feeling of success into visions of horror is one boneheaded melee mistargetting or trigger happy caster tossing a rain "by mistake" to ruin the work you have done and probably end your life. Playing an effective enchanter means always knowing what is happening in your group; anyone can run a buffbot enchanter that occasionally will mez an add. But it takes attention, persistance and knowledge to run a good enchanter.

Strengths
- groups will want you
- you are the best class at mana regeneration
- you have the best hastes
- a well played enchanter can make any situation managable
- you are king in dungeons (LDoN has changed that a little; I was refering to older dungeons in earlier expansions)
- Charming can make you the most powerful one on one soloer there is (bards win with swarm kiting though)

Weaknesses
- groups may only want you for your buffs, not your other skills
- Once you hit 60, your mana regen spells last so long that you might not be needed in groups, only to cast before a group leaves
- Many groups only want to pull singles, or at most doubles. This makes for a very very bored enchanter.
- you have a few really great buff lines; you will be pestered for those spells every 20 minutes from the moment you get them untill you quit your enchanter
- untill gear begins to make a noticible difference enchanters are the most fragile of the casting classes; it wont take much to turn you into a stain on the ground if you arent carefull
- you will often throw mana away because your mezzes WILL get broken time and again by the same player, whose response will always be "opps, my bad"

Fun Factor
I enjoyed playing my enchater. Though sometimes I wanted to strangle a ranger that mistargetted with a bow or a shaman/necro/druid that dotted an add as soon as it came into camp, I found it a very rewarding class. Grouping is where the enchanter will shine. While soloing can be very fun, it can also be dangerous and lead to many deaths (16 is my record for one night of soloing) Soloing my way around Kael and passing other full groups in exp gain/mob kill rates was a blast.

Tier 1 and tier 2 can be very very boring for an enchanter as single pulls are so easy to get. Many times you might find that you can go AFK for 15-20 minutes at a time and as long as you keep hastes up no one will notice. Places like Droga and veksar are much more fun for an enchanter in their late 40's early 50's because it gives you something to do. I found early on that crawls were much more entertaining than camping because you will often blunder around a corner into the waiting arms of 5 mobs, and that is just always pure joy for an enchanter.

My advice? Try it out. Take the enchanter up in levels and see what happens. Although its all but unavuidable, Paladual Caverns can kill any characters joy early on. Get LDoN groups as fast as you can (level 20). Yes, the exp wont be as good really as PC, but you will have more fun. I've found that an enchanter takes a certain temprement of player to really enjoy; Somone that is very detail oriented and almost obsessive makes teh best enchanter because those are the people that will time buffs and mezzes so that you never have to ask for anything because they will be casting it as soon as it flashes or remezzing 2 secs before it wakes. Im not quite at that level, but I still ran a good enchaner that was always sought after in groups.

Since you already are a shaman main, I didnt go into that portion of the question. Dig about the boards a bit and you will find anything and everything that there is to know about the pros and cons of a shaman.

Nice post, Thorzion.

In a good group pulling multiple mobs there is nothing more fun than being the enchanter.

Good group: Healers that are on you when you need heals (and you WILL need heals.) A MT that all recognize as the MT and who follow the MT instructions on when what to attack. A MT that pays attention to which mob-on-hold to attack next. (I use the "Hail a %T" to let tank know which is next.)

Keep in mind there are going to be mistakes made. Mistakes. That means every now and then something is going to happen that shouldn't have but did. Be prepared.

Keep root up for those 'accidental' dots that get cast. Keep stun up for those 'accidental' mez breaks to regain control. Keep gate up for those 'accidental' groups who have no clue what you are trying to do, or can do.

Enchanters can be a blast to play. If not played in a fast paced, multipull environment they can also be extremely boring.

I play both enc and shm, my shm should ding 65 this next week, halfway thru 64, and my enc is 61.

I love both classes, and would play enc more if I didn't feel I had to focus on a main.

I would often play in a trio of enc/nec/shm, and the enc would be held back by mana considerations the most, easily. Don't be fooled that you'll have 'the best mana regen', everyone has KEI anyways, and even other class-based mana regen which ench does not get. In any case practicing good mana management is what carries you. I don't think for a second that class balance did not include mana regen concerns. Just compare you spell costs and casting times on the shm and enc. In the end you're getting as much mana regen and leeway as SOE is willing to give you.

I have a sort of pessimistic view on how things were on ench for awhile:

I found it kind of annoying to xp the enc past 52 or so, because I felt mostly hobbled. If you want xp at any reasonable pace then, you have to do planes, and at least the early planes are kinda boring.

In many groups there wasn't anything to do but boon a melee and haste everyone. Nobody ever needs anything less than c3, and often they will have a shaman slower. It wasn't exactly a walk in the park playing slower either, because the casting time for enc slow is long and if you need to tash, then your slow isn't going to land for a long time, since you have to wait a few seconds to tash. This is compared to my shm that would malo on inc and slow immediately, sometimes taking a hit or two. If you play shm then enc you will feel the pain I swear it...

Planes are also a joke for single pulls alot, and wide open spaces. Most of the time you'll get single pulls, and alot of time on the double or triple pulls if you're group is half-decent they'll have rooted the add before it gets to group anyway. Eventually this is the preferred way to do it, root or kite adds, even with enc, because you can't mez anything useful once you get to tier2. If you're fighting in PoV/PoS you probably can't mez any adds because they are too high level, at least until you're 61.

It was hellish trying to xp my enc from 58 to 60 because it felt like nobody wanted her. Sometimes I'd have her in a group, and slow/malo/buff out of group on my shm, and patch heal.

Things are definitely better on the horizon I think for my enc, as far as coming spells and utility as a class, and I expect to be wanted more for group 61+. I guess every class goes through growing pains in 50's tho eh.

I feel like SHM has more general utility in a group, but ENC has some crucial abilities that make them so useful and powerful, especially for one grouping things.. Charm, mez, lull, extra slow, best haste, stun are invaluable, compared to shm.

After its all said and done though, enchanter has charm, and charm wins.
