Gloriana
It's interesting to see how raid zone design can really affect guild morale.

After suffering through VT (long camps, long painful raids), my guild really enjoyed the elementals. Sure, flagging was a pain in the ass, but it's almost easier to flag as a guild raid force than to beg 3-4 people to come to Sraa and camp a ring piece for Emp key.

Time is a BEAUTIFUL raid zone. Easy to get to - so if you're late (because you have to work?) and want to join the raid after it begins there is no danger to get there. No see invis mobs, no long run. Just leave a two box alt in Time A, get an invite and you are immediately at the raid, ready to play with your friends, ready to enjoy your gametime immediately.

Compare that experience to joining a guild raid already in progress in Uqua. Running to KT isn't so bad, but KT is a laggy zone, with see invis mobs making the run to Yxxta dangerous. If you die in KT, you're screwed, your guild is a zone away and no one can drag and rez you, your game night is pretty much over.

If you make it to Yxxta you still have to make it past see invis mobs to the raid zone. If you die (and you probably will) it's a serious hassle, gotta get a rogue to come out and drag you, gotta get a cleric to come out and rez you. Shaman have it MUCH easier than a lot of classes, with lev, sow, invis (and improved invis, if you're lucky enough to have the spell).

After 6 months in Time, my guild still gets a 70+ person turnout for Time, we can barely scrape 40 people together for a planned Uqua raid. If I'm leaving work on time on an Uqua night, I rarely even bother to log in. The thought of having to join an Uqua raid already in progress is unmotivating.

I just find it really interesting that the SOE design team goes to such extremes between expansions.
