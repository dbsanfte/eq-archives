...and don't look back.

i bought my horse yesterday, and the difference is huge.
somebody suggested it's around FT 20 - perhaps not that extreme, but a horse gives me a regen boost almost akin to KEI when i'm soloing.

how the horse changed my solo strategy:

i never used to tank for my pet - w/o a horse, HP is simply too precious to waste tanking. decreased HP regen rate = decreased mana regen rate = decreased kill rate. so i'd pull, tank until pet taunted once, e-bolt and back off, sit, root if need be.

now, however, i tank for my pet (letting it have the mob's back - no dodges/parries) and still regen sitting mana, making the sacrificed HP less crucial. no need to regen or defensively buff pet, and i can work up weapon skills and add a little of my own meager DPS. i find my kill rate increased dramatically, maybe around 25%.

grouping:

well, the big issue is aggro management since i'm usually not OOM in groups unless i have to rebuff. but needless to say, horsey helps a lot with aggro (no sitting).

downsides:

the head gets in the way, yeah. shrink x2 and it's pretty much gone.
the old bug with turning off horses/your luclin graphic doesn't work anymore ('You must enable both your race graphic and horse graphic to summon a mount.'), so you still need to deal with the acceleration and decreased run speed (compared to SoW). no big deal, though - bih'li and horse stack, so if you get in a pinch just click off mount and run for it.
you only receive sitting mana regen while on a horse; HP regens as though you were standing. this might in theory be a problem if a larger portion of a shaman's regen came from sitting vs standing. however, as a barbarian with regen III and regrowth up, i regen 20 HP standing vs. 23 sitting. again, NBD.

so that's about it... i did a lot of research and asked a lot of questions before buying my mount, but in retrospect i should've bought first and worried later. it's hands down the gear upgrade that's yielded the most significant improvement in gameplay i've bought. (no JBB yet though)

anyway, just thought i'd post in case any of you are considering buying a horse. do it :)
