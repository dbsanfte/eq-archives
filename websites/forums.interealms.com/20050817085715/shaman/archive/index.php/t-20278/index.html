Sionnach Taibhsear
What would happen if SoE effectively removed the stat caps by raising them to something like 1,000?

In my mind the stat cap is one of the seldom-named reasons for the Shaman’s plight. We certainly argue that our buffs are effectively useless now and should not be counted as a strength, but we seem to take the current game mechanics as set in stone and immovable. I don’t expect for loot not to have nice stats and I certainly can’t blame the players for raising their statistic with the use of items. Our problem is that our lowest buff can usually top off the typical end game player. What would happen if that ceiling was blown off the stats and our buffs could once again raise the players’ stats into the stratosphere?

Is this theoretically possible? I mean heck they are re-coding the graphics engine! The AA system has basically made the “leveling” open-ended. Why can’t the game code be modified to make the player stats open-ended? We have a limit of 15 buff slots and only so many item slots so it is not like the pumping up of stats is unlimited with our current spell selection.

Everyone would benefit from this, I think. Every class that can cast a buff and every class that can receive a buff will benefit. The devs in charge of item creation would have more area to roam. The devs in charge of building mobs can make mobs with higher AC and Attack ratings. It will delay the need for new levels.

Is this possible? Is this a bad idea? Why won’t this work? Where are my socks?

Cragael
The stat cap is definitely an arbitrary construct over which they have complete control, no question there.

The thing is... what do you think removing the stats cap would do? Diminishing return doesn't begin to describes the benefits from stats above 255, much less above 280.

Assuming that you're buffing a tank. He is already at the cap of 305 or very much near it. 60 more points of STR, STA, DEX or AGI are going to bring him... what? Pass 305, those 60 point of STA are likely going to be equivalent to 60-90 more HP, probably less... you think a tank is going to sacrifice a buff slot for that? They already sacrifice more HP than that getting Rangers' SOT or Beastlords' SV buff over the Paladins' Brell, for the additional ATK they provide. And the 1-2 of damage avoidance AC 60 more AGI would bring isn't exactly going to scream 'Buff me!' either. Add another 140 AGI from avatar and you're adding what... 5 AC? They're willing to lose twice that equipping a ring with Vengeance II on it and don't even blink doing it.

You have a better case with DEX or STR, but even there... the +ATK you see from buffing STR is not equivalent to, say, the +ATK from the ATK buff component of Ferocity or Avatar. And that DEX is going to amount to 1-2% additional proc chance. Plus, they're getting those buffs for free, bundled as they are in Focus, which at this point they already get for the less and less significant HP buff component, or in FA which they want for the +ATK component.

Stats buffs are not what's going to make or break the high level shamans. They haven't been since Luclin and, giving that people are asking for the cap to be moved up again, the PoP cap raises haven't changed that either. Asking for higher caps is like asking for a sixth lane on a five-lane highway... yeah, you're going to get there a trifle faster, but you're still going to end up in the same place.

You want significant buffs? Ask for buffs that give passive mana regen. Or actual combat abilities, like Avoidance, Strikethrough, combat skills (1HS, 2HS, parry, bash, etc.) bonuses and so on. Now, any of THAT is going to have the other classes sit up and beg.
