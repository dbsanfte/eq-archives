They have looked VERY impressive thus far, but how long can they keep it up?

Dallas - another old team that is showing their age. Modano and Nieuwendyk were bound to start losing a step sooner or later: you can fight time but you can't escape it forever. The acquisition of Turgeon wasn't a stellar move either (ask Blues fans about that)... his best days were in Long Island and he has ALWAYS been inconsistent, with a tendancy to disappear come playoff time, when his offnesive skills are in demand.

St Louis - not off to the start that many thought they would be. Not sure what the problem is. Their goaltending isn't the greatest, but Brathwaite isn't that bad either. Still waiting for Wieght and Ktachuk to really develop a synergy, then look out.

Teams that have surprised:

Calgary- Turek looks incredible (sorry St Louis) and Iginla appears to be having a breakthrough year, living up to all the potential and more, that the scouts had said was there when the Flames drafted him. Add to them a supporting cast of enthusiastic, young players who have learned how to win... Wow!

Edmonton - Tommy Salo is also having a banner year and this another team of young players that is having a good time and performing well. Crushing blow to them is the loss of Ryan Smyth with a broken ankle... he is maturing into another great winger (FYI, I think its kinda cool that Smyth and Igninla seem to be on the rise at the same time: both drafted in the first round 5-6 years ago, both Alberta boys on Alberta teams... kinda cool... wonder if their small market homes will be able to afford them when their contracts expire this year...).

Montreal - almost the same (brutal?) team as last year except they LOST their best player to cancer (Saku Koivu). That they are doing as well as they have been is unreal.

New York Isles - not as much of a surprise, as they did make some substantial moves, in acquiring Peca, Yashin and Osgood, but who knew that those moves would make them perform as well as they have so far? Nice to see. (WOOT!!! LETS GO ISLANDERS!!!)

New York Rangers - A team polarized between really old expensive talent and highly touted younger talent. Is the chemistry developing? Be interesting to see if the additions of Lindros and Berard continue to impact them. Be interesting to see if Berard and Lindros last the season, with Lindros' tendancy to go through the middle with his head down, and Berard's vision impairment...

Colorado - Defensively sound, but offensively they are are hurting bad. Sakic looks ordinary and no one else is picking up the challenge left by the absence of Forsberg. I never really though of Colorado as that dependant on Forsberg, but other than that, I am at a loss to explain why they can't put the puck in the net...

Florida - the Panthers. Luongo is looking good between the pipes, but it appears that his supporting cast has disappeared. So much for the re-uniting of the offensively gifted Bure brothers...
