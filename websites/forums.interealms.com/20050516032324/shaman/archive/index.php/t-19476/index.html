GOD IS NOT ABOUT SPELLS OR AA.

There, I think that is the key point that sometimes gets lost.

GoD appears to be all about content. 20 new zones for higher level raiders, 10 of which are instanced and 10 of which are not.

The key for GoD will be whether the content is visually striking, fun, interesting or something else.

The second key for GoD will be itemization. Always a tough balancing act, it's one SOE did a wretched job with in PoP.

At this point, there's not a good idea how it will play out. This is not going to an expansion like Kunark or PoP where levels were raised and spell design was huge. It will be more like Velious, which added significant content and a decent storyline, or Luclin which added huge content but with a lot of ugly and not terribly fun zones.

Bear in mind that while Velious is now widely hailed as the pinnacle of EQ, when it first came out I was more disappointed with Velious than any other expansion. It had a lot of big problems, and it took a lot of patching and tinkering over the span of several months before Velious settled down into a terrific expansion.

This is not a science, although LDoN is an attempt at game design by accountants and code-monkeys, it's an art and skill.

Personally, I'd be psyched if they just fixed the dumbass character animations. Watch a barbarian warrior animation in old graphics, and compare it to the animation in the new graphics. It's amazing how nice and fluid the old one is.
