First off, no we are not a hybrid class. We do not share spells with a parent class (although we do share basic priest spells like heal and gate). If you look at a ranger, sk, beastlord, or whatever, most of their 1-49 spells are the same spells their caster parent gets, except the hybrid gets it later. They get some original stuff but most of it draws from the same idea or theme as their parent class (paladins get holy magic stuff like clerics, sk's get evil lifetappy stuff like necros, beastlords get spiritual stuff like shamans).

Second of all, whoever said that the only thing we do better than anyone else is slow is very very wrong and should just delete their shaman now if you honestly believe that.

I am a level 65 shaman with a more or less full spellbook (missing group shrink and group regen), and I have 93 AA. My relevant grouping AA's are canni5, SCM3, QB2, and SCRM. I'm pretty well off on gear/spells/aa, but I am far from being uber. I am not a special case. When it comes to grouping, I am a monster. There is no other class that brings as much to a group as I do - there is no doubt in my mind. Call me egotistical, but I think shaman is the best grouping class in the game. Give me someone with taunt and 4 miscellaneous gimps and you have a functioning group that will never die. I kept a group of 5 melees alive for 10 hours yesterday. We had one person die to a train. That was it. Let me describe what I do in a group:

I give everyone fo7 for the HP.
I give everyone talisman of the boar for the HP.
I give everyone talisman of alacrity for the increased damage.
I give everyone ferine avatar for the increased damage.
I give everyone regrowth of dah khura, and myself and the tank get replenishment. This heals splash damage on the non-tanks (I don't have to directly heal them, they heal themselves by the time the next mob is there).

Lets look at this section. I have people: HP buffs, haste, atk buffs, and hp regen. What other class gets all that? Beastlords, but theirs are inferior to ours. These buffs alone make a group much more powerful. Lots of people on these boards like to be sarcastic about the effectiveness of our buffs, but they really should look a little closer and they might see how big a difference they really make. Next section:

Puller goes out and brings in a mob.
As the mob is inc, I cast Q on the tank. This only takes me 4.5 seconds (will be 3 seconds in a few days). It takes very little mana because I have SCM3 and a decent mana pool. It lasts damn near forever because I have SCRM.
I malos the mob.
I slow the mob.
I apply Q to the puller if he got hit.
If the tank is a knight, I apply blood of saryn. If it is a warrior I skip this because I don't like tanking.
Canni Canni Canni
Reapply Q to tank.
A decent plate tank will almost never need a heal besides Q. If you use a decent ranger to tank he'll need about 1 kraggs per fight.

If there are adds I root them before they get to the group. If I am too slow on the root I just slow them and deal with it. I might have to apply a tnarg's every now and then, but that is not a problem at all because I can get enough mana for 4 or 5 tnarg's with a single press of canni5. I also keep a pet up that I mainly use as a sacrifice to buy me a few seconds when we get multiple adds. He can tank 1 mob long enough for me to slow the rest.

If I am using blood of saryn I will need to use canni5 every now and then. If I am just casting my normal debuffs and Q, I don't need canni5. My FT regens almost all the mana for me and a canni4 here or there tops me out.

If you have a bard in the group, it becomes retardedly easy. Their HP regen means that everyone except the tank gets completely healed of any splash damage before the fight is done, and their mana regen means you won't have to canni4 hardly ever, especially if you can bum a crack off someone (I never go off to exp without a fresh kei from nexus).

A shaman might not be as powerful as a cleric and chanter COMBINED but we are very close. A cleric and chanter is overkill for a normal exp group. A cleric can't do it on their own, and neither can a chanter (unless you have multiple paladins w/ a bard). A druid isn't even a consideration.

You do not even need a full spellbook and AA to do this. I went a LONG time without any 65 spells and barely and 63-64s. SCM3 and SCRM are also recently acquired. You do not need Q to be the main healer in a group. You can do just fine with kragg's, you'll just have to work harder to keep your mana up.

IN SUMMARY, if you think the shaman class is gimp, then it is you yourself that is gimp, not the class. No offense. We are a goddamn good class and it pisses me off when other shamans think differently.
