Eoin MacManus
Problem: Assuming that our heals are tuned to be effective in conjunction with slow, mitigation clearly presents a problem.

Idea: A line of heals that would be cast on a MOB instead of a player. When the spell lands (unresistable), it checks to see if the MOB currently has a slow spell on it. If it does, and if the MOB mitigates slow, the spell puts a super-short duration (a few seconds max) recourse effect on the MOB's current target that heals (instantly) for an "overtuned" amount based on how much the slow has been affected by mitigation.

For example, Tnarg's Mending heals for 1300 base. This new spell might heal for, say, 1300*(1+(slow%-mit%)/slow%); so, if the MOB mitigates a 75% slow down to 50%, the heal would be for 1733 base.

The numbers above are just an example to show the math behind the idea. Actual tuning would be needed to make it realistic. What do you think?

jzak
The original idea of this thread isn't really viable. The coding required would probably be terrible and it's just not general enough. I think the main problem with our poor healing in conjunction with mitigation of slow is that the developers have slipped down the slope of "balance" and they only look at surface numbers when it comes to that. Obkin's Mending is 1690hp currently because giving us a 1750hp heal would make it Nature's Infusion. God forbid we're able to heal like a druid 4 expansions and 2 years later.

Anyway, I propose heal spells that work only in certain zones, kind of like how Call of the Hero works in some zones and not others.

Kragg's Planar Mending: 3120hp, 10s cast (incomplete CH) that works in PoP and GoD zones where mitigation is present.

Tnarg's Planar Mending: 1700hp, 3.75s cast that would only work in PoP and GoD zones where mitigation is present.

Kragg's Discordant Mending: X * 0.67, where X is the hitpoint value of the new druid incomplete CH they will likely be getting in OoW. This would only work in OoW and possibly some GoD zones where mitigation is present.

Daluda's Discordant Mending: 2050hp, 3.75s cast that works in GoD and OoW zones where mitigation is present.

Obkin's Discordant Mending: 2350hp, 3.75s cast that works in OoW only where mitigation is present.

We can keep the version of Tnarg's, Daluda's and Obkin's that are presently there or planned, and those will work in all zones.

Essentially, a cleric should be able to heal in a group where there is no slower present. A druid should be able to heal in a group where there is an enchanter, bard or beastlord. A shaman should be able to heal a group alone due to being able to slow. Priests should be able to substitute for one another. If druids and clerics think this is unbalancing, then they can dps in a group instead of healing if there's a shaman present. Clerics can use their "awesome" hammers and "awesome" melee powers they begged for a while back and druids can nuke and we'll all be happy.

SoE is so afraid of breaking old content that they pass up giving us the appropriate upgrades. A paladin and a ranger can be the only healers in hard LDoN and win it, trust me, I've done it. If you don't want to break old content, at least make it possible for classes to work in new content. They have no problem with giving classes spells to increase their dps (any class that can cast a spell always gets upgrades to their direct damage spells or DD proc buffs), yet suddenly there's a HUGE problem with giving priests (and shamans specifically) better heals to mitigate away the damage mobs in new zones put out. Sounds like a double standard to me.
