My favorite is actually a law that at one time was a good idea but now is not due to the change in times.

It was in Maryland and someone actually used it and brought it to the courts attention.

The law read something along the lines of...

If a person is to leave their house and travel a distance of 2 miles or further from their home then they must carry a sword to fend off any bandits or animals which may threaten them.

Now also in Maryland a much more recent law it is illegal to have on your person a blade more than 6 inches long (and they have a few exceptions such as policed events or theatrical performances).

Well a guy who went around challenging the Maryland legal system as a hobby saw the two laws and decided to push them.

Clearly the law was in conflict with itself so he wanted to see which would win.

He took a GRAND saber and wore it out to the nearest mall which happened to be 3 miles away from where he lived.

Needless to say the cops showed up and arrested him.

At court he presented his case stating that he was following the law as was stated in the colonial sword carrying statute. So therefore he is innocent (and he also mentioned he was doing this out of civil disobedience not as a malicious act).

The judge seeing that on a technicality he was in fact correct and that law was very FUBARed told the guy he was free to go but from now on if he had a problem with the laws of the land to bring it up with the legislation to be changed first before resorting to civil disobedience.

Anyhow to my knowledge the sword carrying law was STILL never repealed.
