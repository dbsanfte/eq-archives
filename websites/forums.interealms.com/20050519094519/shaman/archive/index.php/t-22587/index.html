What we're asking is: is there a difference in average mob damage after it is crippled? To answer this question, we compare the average damage uncrippled with the average damage crippled, taking into account the normal variation we see in mob damage. If the difference in mean damage exceeds the standard deviation by a certain degree, then we conclude that whatever we changed had a significant effect.

Statistically comparing two sample means is pretty easy. I can't do it from the data presented, because there is no measure of the standard deviation on the mob damage per hit given; however, knowing what I do about how widely mob hits vary, I can say with a good degree of certainty that there is no chance that we would be able to conclude that there is a difference after the mob is crippled. The difference in average hits is just too small.

Tugela
I think you need to look at a wider range of mobs. IMO the one that you have choson is not suitable for this sort of parse.

For a start, GoD mobs have relatively low atk values, as can be seen by the average damage being relatively close to the min in the cited example. You would expect the visible effects of cripple to be small in those circumstances, and probably you would need much larger parses to pick them up. The sort of mob you want is one that has a high relative atk, but not so high that is max hitting most of the time.

On the AC debuff part, if your atk is low relative to the mobs AC, it will likely still be low after you have debuffed the mob, in other words you wouldnt expect to see much of a difference. Ideally you should look for a mob with AC at a level where you are average hitting close to max but not near max. Likewise, if your atk is high relative to the mob AC (ie you are max hitting much of the time), cripple would be expected to have little or no effect.

I'm not sure about how it might affect proc rates. Unlike weapon procs, mob innate procs only go off when a swing actually hits the target (weapon procs go off on the swing, not the strike). So, unless you can affect accuracy (which cripple doesnt do), it shouldnt reduce proc rate (unless the probability of a proc is tied directly to mob DEX of course).
