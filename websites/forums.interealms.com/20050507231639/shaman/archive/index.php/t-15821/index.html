Badz
Several things put the fun back into the game for me.

1) I got a second account and now I can play when I want without sitting around LFG. I invite people to play with me and if they are not fun to be with then I boot them out. I get so tired of non-shaman telling me how to play a shaman. I have a shaman-warrior duo, shaman-enchanter duo and a shaman-mage duo. In all 3, I play the shaman and bot the other one.

2) Convinced some RL friends to start playing on Friday nights. We all got our wives to agree that Friday nights was EQ night. So now, we gather at PoK bank at 7:30pm and head off in a full group to fight until 3am. We are all enjoying EQ a lot more now. Since we know we have Friday nights, we seem to place less during the week but enjoy the game more. We had all started 2 sets of characters so that we alternate every Friday to avoid burnout.

3) Make alts. I have an alt in every class except paladin. Yes some are twinked but others have been started on different servers so I can avoid the tempation.

4) Most importantly - get away from the power game. That was the addiction I had to get over. The most painful part was realizing how strung out I had gotten on being the best, having the most, etc. I now like grouping with my RL friends on Friday night and we set off to get a level or two.

Bottom line is I play less and enjoy it more. Anyway, just my 2 cp worth from someone that was burned out. EQ is a game and meant for entertainment.

Out of game options -

1. Read a book.
2. Go to a movie.
3. Call up some friends you haven't seen in a long time and do dinner.
4. Play a board game with your spouse/SO/roommate/friends.
5. Go bowling. It's surprisingly fun.
6. Pick a project around the house and do it (paint a room, wallpaper, build a shelf, etc).

And in-game options -

1. Start a new character and play to level 20, NO TWINKING
2. Go to a midlevel zone and start buffing people
3. Go to a middle-high end zone and own the place
4. Tradeskills!
5. Do a quest you've always meant to do but it looked to hard. Even if you don't want the reward. Find someone to MQ it for if possible so they can use the reward :-)
6. Go to a Tier 1 zone and group. Bad groups will be entertaining, being able to help out good people who are simply low level will be rewarding.
7. Take your group back to Kunark and kill Gorenaire, Talendor, Trakanon, etc. as a one-grouper.
8. Go play Asheron's Call 2 and marvel at the pretty graphics... and how empty the world is.

Lots of other options, really, the sky's the limit.

I started working on Cabilis faction when I got tired of exping more recently. I started by running around the newbie area in LoIO siccing Dogdog after every baby sarnak or goblin in sight. Once I was acceptable to all Cabilis except the necros, I went back to exping for a while. Next time burnout hit me again, I started the cudgel quest. I've finished all of the parts I can solo (having picked up my Venril bones before I even started) and now am back to exping until I get around to dragging some folks into Howling Stones to finish it off. I've been burnt out many times over my career, and when I do I just take a break and play a different game for however long it takes. Usually just 2 weeks or so. Sometimes I play some of the newer, modern games (both single and multiplayer) that are out, and sometimes I did through my old CD-ROMs and find some nice, old school games that can keep me hooked for days at a time. Master of Orion 2, X-com UFO Defense, etc.... The old classics :) I'm going to see if Simcity2000 runs on my machine, and if it does, I'm going to play that for a bit.

If you just want something different to do inside EQ that can get your mind off of the grind, there are a few things you can try. I actually enjoy tradeskills (hell if I know why). If I go and do tradeskills for a few nights running, I am usually ready to go back to exp and raiding when I'm done. Long, time consuming, almost epic quests also work as well. /hugs 8th shawl and seru earring.

Burn out mainly happens because, although you are enjoying whatever it is you are doing, you do it so many times that it stops being fun and starts to be work. When that happens, it is usually best to go do something else until you realize what it is that makes the game fun, and then you're ready to go back. Good luck.

(btw, if you are burned out already at 48, I might suggest taking this opportunity to just stop EQ altogether before it gets its claws in you :) ).
