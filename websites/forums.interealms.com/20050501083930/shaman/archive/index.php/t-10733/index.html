PoP mobs beat ass. Compared to old world mobs, they are raid level mobs without massive hp. This stealth nerf has reduced our ability to handle multiple mobs by a substantial margin. Let me offer an example. I'll pick a low tier mob--the crystal golem in PoV. I think they max hit about 600ish. They double attack. They bash for like 200. I would estimate they attack about once every 1.5 seconds or so, unslowed. They can also proc a 375 crystal glass DD. Let's ignore the proc for now and just focus on melee. Max melee is about 1400 every 1.5 seconds.

With CA3, CS3, ID5, and LR5, you can about half that damage. That's only 54 AA pts. Let's ignore those for now.

Assuming about 70% damage--though they usually hit for max without defensive skills and an AC of about 1300--that's about 980 damage every 1.5 seconds--though we need to reduce that as bash takes longer to refresh. Let's call it 800 every 1.5 seconds. I dunno what average shaman hp is anymore, but I raid buff to about 5600. That's about 10 seconds before it kills me unslowed.

Malo is now a 5 second cast time. Slow often won't land on PoP mobs without malo (less so in this case, as the crystal golems are easy to slow--however, higher tier mobs are much harder). Splitting can be extremely difficult if not impossible in places. With just 2 mobs (and affliction haste not working), that's an additional 5.6 seconds of casting to slow the second mob, assume no resists, and instant recast as soon as spell gems pop. That's an additional 2400 damage. I'll leave you to do the math on a 3 pull. Want to see some more effects? Try working the numbers on say, a two pull of Kriger giants. Max hit 782 with a 500hp proc.

This change has slammed our ability to handle bad pulls quickly. Apparently, though, we're all just 'whiners' for being pissed about it. When is enough really enough? We can't melee past 40, we can't heal worth a crap compared to any other priest class, our slows were mitigated, and now it takes even longer to cast them. Let's just ignore any effort spent to get these now-nerfed items.
