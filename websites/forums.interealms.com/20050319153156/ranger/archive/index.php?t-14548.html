In the first tier planes (Disease, Nightmare, Innovation and Justice) a ranger can still reasonably tank while relying on procced slow. In the next tier planes (Valor, Storms being the most commonly accessible ones) mobs hit too hard and too fast for a non-plate class to tank without reliable slow. Mobs in Valor hit for about 550 max, in Storms it's even a bit higher.

Usually when I suddenly find myself tanking in Valor (because the tank just went down for whatever reason), I tend to turn off attack, draw aggro with a few spells+taunt and switch to 2hs. I've found this helps a lot in reducing damage taken from mob ripostes (Never really analyzed it, but I'd bet 60-70% of the damage I'm taking when dualwielding is from ripostes)

Never confuse standing in front of a 75% slowed mob with tanking. Tanking entails the necessity to conserve healer mana. 'Tanking' without the need to do that is the same as saying 5dps being 'good damage' if its enough to clear all available targets.
