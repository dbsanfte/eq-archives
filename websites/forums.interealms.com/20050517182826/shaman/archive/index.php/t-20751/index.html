So I logged on a friends inactive account, and made a rogue, and it got me to thinking about powerlevelling. The first thing I did was look up boxing characters. I went to http://www.eqwindows.com and downloaded it. This works best with graphic intensive options turned off. EQW allows for quick switching between windows.

Secondly, and I didn't even realize it when I began, get ornate legs. The mana free root is invaluable which I will get to shortly.

Quick overview of where I leveled:
1-3: West Freeport
4-22: Paludal Caverns
23-28: Sol A Goblins
29-36: Sol A Gnomes
36-40: City of Mist

I basically wanted to go as fast as possible, so on trivial mobs (trivial to the shaman) I pulled with the level 1 fire nuke. The fact you are 65 allows for easy singles usually. Every camp I did I found a nice corner to pull to. As soon as the mob got there I clicked the ornate legs root on it. Using root proximity aggro rules, basically closest person has aggro, I had my rogue attacking and backstabing while my shaman tanked. This method worked at every camp as I leveled. One thing to note, pull with slow once the mobs become tougher.

Its been 16.5 hours according to /play and my rogue is level 40 with all offensive skills close to cap. I headed to HHK to do nobles, but my shaman client crashed and could not log back on. I hope the root proximity thing helps. If you want to use this technique on a caster, pick up a dps melee and offer free PL. This will make things go way faster for the caster.

Anyway, another "technique" to powerleveling that I don't see mentioned often is Greeny killing. As soon as your rogue is of level to group with your shaman, you can use this technique. Group up with the rogue and go hunt things that are green to your shaman (but still blue to your rogue). ALL of the exp from the kill will go to the rogue. This way, you can plow through greenies and rack up the exp fast with the rogue. It doesn't matter which one gets the kill, and you get to start practicing your reall duelboxing skills before the mobs are too much of a threat.

Thorzek
This will not work with DS potions though in my experience, as the PL'ee has not done 51% of the damage. If I am using DS potions, just pull a couple, root them on PL'ee, and keep him healed.
DS is "neutral" damage - it doesn't get counted for either party's portion of the damage done.

Take a mob with 1k HP.

If twink does 501 HP damage, he gets the exp. Pretty straightforward.
If DS does 200 HP of damage, that leaves 800 for the exp factor. The twink must do 401 HP of damage in this case.
You could actually let the DS do 999 HP, as long as the twink does the remaining 1 point of damage and not you.

51% of non-DS damage is the watermark.

Also important to remember that when a spell is cast, it's the POTENTIAL damage of the spell damage used for calculations.

So if a mob has 2k HP, shaman can do a sinle 925 nuke, and twink can do the remaining 1075 and get full HP.

Combining this with DS, though:

Mob = 2k HP
DS = (say) 300 HP
Remaining = 1700 HP
if shaman nuke = 925
then remaining damage = 775
and twink gets no exp.
Shaman could use 625 nuke and let twink do 1075 for exp.

Just use caution when using both DS and damage spells, whether DOTS or nukes. DOTS count the full POTENTIAL damage of the spell, whether or not it goes full term.
