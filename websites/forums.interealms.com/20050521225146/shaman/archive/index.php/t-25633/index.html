ribeye
Depending on the raid (if we are expecting many deaths and battle rezzing) we designate one or possibly 2 shaman as rebuffers. When I get the duty, I turn off all names (/shownames off -- that way you only see a name for a pet or a corpse and you know who needs rebuffs). I usually call rebuffs in the shaman chat channel (rebuffing %t!). Also before the raiding gets underway, the designated shaman rebuffer calls in raid that he will be the shaman rebuffer tonight, and periodically repeats it.

As for pre-raid buffing. None of our shamans have group wunshi either. Usually someone MGB's fortitude, someone MGB's sense, and then shamans take like groups 1-4, 5-6, etc and buff everyone with wunshi. We tried MGBing Fo7 and then hitting tanks etc with Wunshi but the guild rebelled and everyone just clicks off Fo7 now, so we bit the bullet and now buff everyone. If you get spiritual channeling you can pretty much buff a full raid of 60+ with wunshi w/o having to med or get pumped.

During the raid, we in the shaman channel designate 1 shaman to cast malos. No sense in having 5 shaman cast it... Then have the rest spam malosinia and turgurs/nihil till we get the mob totally debuffed. Never count on mages to cast sinia; at least our mages are just too lazy.

We usually have about 4-6 shamans per raid, and this system seems to work pretty well. Yah it gets crazy sometimes when 30 people die at once and all want rebuffs (especially when you were just rezzed as well), but such is life.

Tordail Farstryder
Assigning buffs to various shaman will save you a lot of time and headache. In our guild, since I am the class leader, I assign buffs based on who has what and spam /raid with the macro during buffs and after a wipe/semi-wipe. Saves time and confusion.

Anyway, I have the blessing of being one of five shaman on guild raids. The buff load is no longer a problem. But when I was the only shaman in the guild, boy did I grow to hate buffing, lol. Here's how you do it, minimally.

MGB Focus of the Seventh.

MGB Talisman of the Boar.

One shaman hits clerics/mages with IOS, since our Focus line does not stack with the cleric self-only mana regen buff (and I believe there is a mage shield-line buff they like to use that does not stack with Focus-line). You can sooner milk a chicken than get your average cleric/mage to click that buff off.

The IOS shaman then loads up Fortitude and hits TANKS with it.

One shaman hits TANKS with Wunshi.

By the way, TANKS does not mean every single melee in the raid. TANKS means your actual tanks, the ones who take the beating. I also Wunshi/Fortitude my puller.

If you have Spirit of Might, your Rangers/Rogues will love you for it. Zerkers like Might too. Spirit of Sense is another good one. My guild considers those luxury buffs on raids. When someone dies, they get Focus/STA. When we're at the raid start, we might buff Sense/Might if we have the time for it. Otherwise we hit DPS'ers on the run with Might and call it even.

Spiritual Channeling helps with this.

Rebuffs: Do it quickly and efficiently. Our shaman put a rebuff macro to our shaman raid channel (Focus on %T, etc.). Double buffing is just a waste of time and mana.

One more thing: if anybody bitches, tell them folks have done POP targets with POP buffs for quite some time and been perfectly successful. Then put up an AFK message that says something along the lines of: "Unless you've died and want rebuffs, you are raiding with what you got on your body. Live with it."

I have been the only shaman buffing our 60+ raid force, multiple times now. We generally do Uqua+ content, and its a stretch for our guild sometimes (lots of new under geared apps). Consequently, we have a lot of deaths typically. (But win still hehe).

At any rate, with expedition timers running, I rarely have time to med, and most buffing needs to be done ASAP.

What that amounts to, is prioritizing buffing. The beginning of the raid is easy. The only way to buff a raid of that size, being alone, is this:

1. mgb Fo7.
2. talisman of fort or sta by groups.
3. ToT if needed by groups.

That usually runs me fairly low mana. At that point, the prioritizing comes into effect.

I buff wunshi and sense in this order, as my mana comes back.

1. MT/SA/TA
2. Other offtanks
3. Monks
4. Other melee
5. by request only after that.

To cut down on random ranger A-L asking for wunshi when I'm OOM and have monks to buff still, I usually let folks know where I am in that buff sequence.

"mgb fo7 inc"
"fortitude by groups inc"
"tot by groups inc"
"Wunshi single casts for tanks inc"
"Wunshi single casts for monks inc (better not die damnit)".
"Wunshi for dps melee inc"

finally,

"Wunshi or missed buffs at request now".

If I get any tells from druids, wizards, etc.. asking for single casts of this or that, they are ignored until that buff sequence is done.

Basically, if you have limited Shaman (or often only 1), you have to serve the most people as fast as possible. You can't feel bad about ignoring the individual to serve the masses hehe.
