Valkargen
DPS actually has NOTHING to do with generated aggro...nada!

Aggro is based on the potential of the weapon, if damage bonus is applicable, and rate of attacks over a time interval with proc intervention added in.

Raw attack affects DPS, but it has no bearing on aggro.
Weapon skill affects attack and DPS and has no bearing on aggro.
Mob immunity affects DPS and has no bearing on aggro.

You can have a skill of 1 in a weapon, attack a target that is immune to the weapons attacks, and have no attack buffs at all...and you will still generate the same aggro as if your attack were 500 points higher, the weapon could land for damage and were fully buffed...

DPS delivered from a weapon sans proc intervention does not have a direct correlation to aggro generated...it can be indicative however of the weapons potential.

Using SK terror lines for a raw emperical method of calculating hate a modifier of ~1.2 was determined.

Using a 20/20 1hander with a 14 damage bonus:

Mainhand = (20 + 14) x 1.2 = 40.8 hate generated per swing
Offhand = (20) x 1.2 = 24 hate per swing

Calculating AA and weapon skill triggers is relatively easy to actually calculate paced aggro from weapon sources as a hate generated per second.

Its then also easy to see what aa selections, ie aa that increase proc rate, dual attack triggers, dual weild triggers, tripple attack or dmg bonus can increase and impact aggro generation. It can give the player a means to determine their best path to aggro generated by aa spent.

Its also important to note that the increase in proc rate from increased attack is minimal. Mobs heavily mitigate more than they have exceptionally high AC...hence hits are often reduced to 0 and show as misses. Proc determination however occurs prior to mitigation and its why procs can land on misses. Increased attack has a tendency to raise AVG hit far more than actually increase hits landed...so in terms of proc intervention its affects are minimized.

The parses to determine these factors involved a lot of people, and weren't soley done by just me, but have been done by many and prompted by similar observations, on many servers, in many settings since Kunark and rudimentary parsers were available.

Valkargen
The early parsework for aggro generation was done during Kunark and hundreds of hours of parswork was compiled then and since and frequently shared on IRC back in the early days...so I have no idea what interval your talking about being too small. Not everyone posts on the class boards. In fact prior to class boards most were in 1 IRC channel or another as a matter of course for wipe purposes and almost regardless of guild affiliation, which is the reason for the collaboration.

Maiva Furious is a riposte disc not a defensive type disc...it can be argued that its both offensive and defensive in nature.

Aggro from raw melee sources is determined PRIOR to the damage modifier of defensive or aggressive. The damage bonus is calculated the moment you equip the weapon and is part of the mainhands potential and offhand with spent AA...

The formulas are in fact consistent, maybe whatever parses your referring to havent been, but the hate modifier was determined within a few months of SK's receiving their terror line. Used in melee proximity, with back turned to avoid ripostes it is ~1.2 The parsework was extensive and at that time it wasn't unusual for me to have 50 parses in my email box at any given time.

For proof Giac, since you don't require numerical raw hate numbers, grab a warrior with a weapon that doesn't proc. Pull a mob and cripple it 7 times and slow it 7 times...count the swings necessary to pull the mob off you...repeat with defensive. For an even cleaner parse have the warrior equip a mug in the offhand so you don't have to diffentiate between offhand or mainhand. Thats actually how the original parses were done in regards to figuring out that damage bonus was part of aggro generation and what part of the ratio it formed. The longer your sampling the more you will get a feel for it, and you can then switch the mug into the mainhand and see firsthand the relative reduction of aggro in relation to offhand.

In regards to DPS there are Foci, AA, and buffs that affect DPS that have no bearing on aggro generation. Vengeance and its ilk, Combat Fury, Cleave, SoP, SoT, etc. To list DPS as generating aggro isn't accurate, as raw attack and weapon crits have no bearing on aggro whatsoever yet they can markedly increase DPS delivered.

Maive in regards to how warriors held aggro prior to augs and the incite lines...they didn't as you recall, without exceptional proccing weapons, hence the revamp.

To put things in perspective, at level 70 with a warrior weilding a BoC and GBoS, both aug'd with Anger 3, and 305 dex, with WA5, Ferocity and Ambi with 100% haste and Bazu's as their incite variant a warrior will generate more hate from procs and the incite line than the raw hate from pure melee sources...near 55% actually. With the weapon combo Samanna list above proc's and incite would be ~40% of aggro generated.

The proportional relation to offhand/mainhand hate was determined in Kunark...which was actually pretty easy, as most warrior weapon selections didn't proc. My parsing work began during our first VS runs when after using Mightystrike and Aggresive early on the warriors aggro didn't burst despite marked parsed gains in DPS...hence wizards splatting after the same ammount of lures cast during the same period of time. After many hours of testing over the course of months and including defensive in our parsing on numerous runs we determined that Combat crits and raw melee modifiers, nor raw damage for that matter had a direct relation to aggro. At times they decepticely seemed linked, hence the misnomer, but there wasn't a direct correlation.

The addition of weapon damage + damage bonus was confirmed when SK's recieved the terror series and for the first time raw hate generation could be calculated to finally arrive at a hate modifier...which at the time was accepted by the group that I was parsing with to be between 1.2 and 1.26...we typically round down to 1.2 to be on the safe side.

Potential of generated weapon hate became (Weapon Damage + Damage bonus) * Hate Modifier = Hate per swing

Early on we rounded Warrior skill triggers as .82 for dual attack and dual weild...yeilding 1.82 attacks per combat interval for mainhand and 1.49 offhand attacks per offhand interval.

Haste has a direct proportional impact on aggro generated from raw melee sources...the more you swing the more aggro you create with those very same weapons, but sinces its equally proportional its not necessary to include it in hate per swing calculations.

Using those formulas we could approximate hate generated per second sans proc intervention, which had the purpose of allowing a warrior to determine base aggro levels with different weapon combinations.

The reason why we started looking into aggro generation was initially to determine how we could increase warrior burst aggro during quick fights like VS. It was during that early parsing work we discovered the inconsistencies.

I believe that current opinion about agro is that it is based on the number of swings and the damage potential of the weapon only, with procs giving a bonus. Whether you land the hits or use a disc doesn't affect the hate generated. A Zanavir should generate pretty decent hate relative to the sorts of folks doing the average pre-elemental pickup raid.

Using a 2 hander rather than a 1 hander shouldnt be an issue under normal circumstances. I used a BoW for a long time with my warrior and never lost initial agro on a named to a heal unless the heal landed before I actually started hitting the mob. One thing to bear in mind with 2 handers though is that the weapon delay is longer, so if you land a heal too soon under those circumstances you might have problems. Nowdays with the incite line in place, there is no excuse for a warrior not having a decent agro lock on the go under most circumstances. It's possible that he wasnt using incite/bellow/bazu and that might be part of the problem you experienced.

It's been a very long time since I've done Bert, but its possible that some of the kings mem blurr, slow or stun (I'm pretty sure some do at least some of those things). Any one of those things will cause the MA to lose agro pretty easily.
