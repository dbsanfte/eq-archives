Viscerator
I have played, both, and more to the point of the posters questions: yes both play styles are very very similar.

You get to buff other players so get to handle tells, etc from many people at once. Helping other players to be stronger.

You get to cast alot, so need to manage your mana.

When the fighting starts, you need to pay attention. You need to understand what is happening, you can't be myopic and just fixate on one mob. You need to understand all facets of the combat, the pull, the mobs, aggro control, etc.

You need to understand your opponents...what spells work, which don't. Tash or no tash, Stuns, mez, levels....the spells are more restricted then shamans, so you need to understand your arsenal. Shaman's have to worry about what resists a mob has in order to damage it.

Overall I think enchanters have more creative spells to play with, but shaman canni/healing is tough to live without

deria
I am both a 65 shaman and a 65 enchanter.

In answer to the original question, if you enjoy playing a shaman you may also enjoy playing an enchanter. Both classes fill a necessary role in the group, and both classes need to always be looking at the big picture instead of focusing only on the current target.

There are some similarities between the roles of the two classes; both can slow, both can haste, and both can provide enhancements to players in the form of buffs (shaman get melee enhancements, enchanters get mind enhancements and some weak melee enhancements).

The differences between the classes become apparent in the area of crowd control. A shaman is able to do crowd control through root parking, and is fortunately able to take the hits required to do so. The down side of root parking is that it is time consuming, and the mob is still perfectly capable of hitting anyone it likes if they get close enough (or, worse, summoning people to it if it gets slightly damaged by a pet or a group member). The enchanter on the other hand is "fragile" at best, but can use a much more reliable method of crowd control (a mezzed mob cannot move, cannot hit, cannot summon, and cannot become un-mezzed until a fixed amount of time has passed).

If you do decide to play an enchanter, though, be prepared to die, die, and then die again. You may even spontaneously die if a mob looks at you the wrong way. While it is true that enchanters get runes to help them absorb damage, they are not as powerful as poeple think. You can have:

arcane rune (1500 hp) -- no component cost, mana intensive

arcane rune (1500 hp) + rune of zebuxeruk (850 hp) -- 1 peridot, and VERY mana intensive

eldritch rune 1/2/3 (500 hp or 1000 hp or 1500 hp) -- no component, no mana (but reuse time of 15 minutes or so)

eldritch rune 1/2/3 (500 hp or 1000 hp or 1500 hp) + rune of zebuxeruk (850 hp) -- 1 peridot, mana intensive

The arcane rune and eldritch rune do not stack. A 2350 point rune seems wonderful, though, right? Well, not exactly. As an enchanter, every mob that hits you will pretty much hit you for full damage -- you won't be mitigating anything. Now I imagine that the situation is a little better for "uber" enchanters (elemental planes flagged, for example) but my gear is pretty good in my opinion and I get absolutely pumelled when I have agro.

Another difference between the enchanter and the shaman is the ability to charm. There are two types of charm -- dire charm and straight charm. With dire charm, a relatively weak mob becomes your pet (which still does 4 times the dps of dogdog) forever. The charm will not break, and the pet is controllable. With straight charm, a very strong mob becomes your pet for anywhere between 1 microsecond and 7 minutes. This pet will put out DPS in varying amounts depending on how much risk you want; you can haste the pet, give it weapons, cast proc illusions on it, and more. It is easily possible to make one of these pets output between 700 and 1000 DPS. It also also a guaranteed death sentence if the mob catches you. A powerful, but risky ability.

Someone made a point earlier in the thread that a shaman can group 5 DPS and have a viable group, while an enchanter could not. I guess that depends on the DPS classes you get. You could just as easily say that an enchanter could grab one of any class that can snare, and have a viable group. :)

The other thing about playing an enchanter is that it is much harder than playing a shaman. As a shaman, you have some flexibility. If a mob resists slow and starts hitting you, you laugh and then slow it again (or malo it). As an enchanter, you need to really be on your toes. Up until level 60, you have some built in defense mechanisms (like the ability to stun mobs quickly and easily for virtually no mana). In PoP, that all went away and enchanters that got agro were dead enchanters. The LDoN expansion has a new stun spell for enchanters, supposedly good up to level 65 mobs. That would be nice, assuming it works on all PoP mobs.

Anyway, those are my thoughts.
