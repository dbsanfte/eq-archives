I think you're probably doing it the best way now. For pure speed leveling, nothing really beats thorns and mass pulling, just be sure not to slow and be sure you get at least a few hits on every mob. It is good that you are using the warrior to keep your skills up.

The fungi's value depends on how often you keep your pocket druid around. The fungi is amazing at lower levels, but since your druid can heal/regen you, there are probably more worthwhile investments for the money.

The single best advice i can give you is not to go too fast. Its easy to fall into a trance powerleveling and forget to keep skills up, and just plan to do them later, only to find yourself at level 55 still using level 24 spells and wearing crap gear with crap skills. Every short while I'd stop and do some real soloing, or grouping, to be sure you have a solid grip on the class.

Oh I agree completely.. at least once every spell level, I take a day and group, or solo. This helps me understand the progression of spells, and buffs etc.. I buy every spell, and use it to understand. I keep all skills maxed, and make sure that all abilities are up to par before moving on to the next spell level. I've played a troll shammy from 59 -62 so I know those levels and spells, but I think you get a healthier respect for what you can do at higher levels if you've had to use the lower level versions of spells... Such as watching the dot damage done, or noting the root ratios, or watching the Canni progression etc...
