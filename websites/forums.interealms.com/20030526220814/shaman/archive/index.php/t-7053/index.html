I disagree with your assumption that a resisted slow does not generate more aggro.

A ways back when VI added the disease component to slow, we were getting hammered every time we cast slow. What VI did to fix it was when slow actually lands, they give a -400 to the casters hate to counteract the extra aggro the disease component instills. A resisted slow does not place the -400 to hate, thus does indeed generate a lot more aggro. You are correct that a resisted slow makes more aggro than when it lands.
