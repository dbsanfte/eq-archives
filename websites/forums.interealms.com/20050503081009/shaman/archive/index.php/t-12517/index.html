Juka
It would work out half decently, but you'd run into problems taking down mobs that are immune to some of our best spells or mitigate our abilities. As powerful as we are, non-rootable mobs, our inability to single pull a lot of mobs, etc etc. I'd suggest one of you go shaman and the other go either shadowknight or beastlord. Shadowknight for snares / feign death and all around good tanking ability while putting out decent DPS, or beastlord if you really enjoy the shaman aspect of casting. Beastlords are a hybrid tank class with shaman spells as well as having excellent DPS and a pet that pwns and the benefit of having a clarity type spell as well. If you'd both like to play casters, I'd suggest shaman / necro combo - there's not much that that combo cannot duo.

Shamans are an extremely effective solo and group class, but having two in the same group outside of raids is pretty much useless - the reason grouping works (relatively) well in EQ is that you get a broad spectrum of abilities; as each class brings something different to the table. If you both choose shaman you'll miss out on a lot of that diversity.

I'm not trying to dissuade you from going shaman / shaman, but I thought pointing out a few things might aid your decision.

As far as your list of fun:

- Necro's have very little downtime, probably nearest to shaman of all the classes in terms of mana regen (without outside help from clarity line of spells)
- Fighting deep in a dungeon would be easier with a necro partner than another shaman - necros can mez mobs and FD pull singles if they have enough room
- Managing a pet isn't terribly tough ;)
- Necro pet can tank very well with a slowed mob ... at 56+ the shaman can tank so the necro pet can backstab ... at 60+ in PoP you won't want to tank

What are your long term goals for these characters? That would help us give you some advice. A shaman / shaman duo would be fine if you just want to have fun and play casually, but if you plan on making it to the end game you're going to run into problems along the way - most notably, trying to find a good guild with a spot for 2 shamans at the same time is going to be rough. If you ever want to group with others you'll have to do so on your own, as most groups don't want two shamans in the same group.

Once again, I'm not trying to bag on you, but I can't overstate enough the importance of diversity in EQ (except for 3 rogues in a group which is teh win!~ ). Overlapping skill sets make us all the more powerful.

Not to confuse the issue further, but have you thought about a cleric/shaman duo? My wife and I duo a lot and while it's not as efficient as some of the other duo's listed below, it can be a powerful combo (esp against undead).

d) new cleric pet and hammer - my wife loves the chance to get away from sit/ch/sit/ch and mix it up. Once a mob is slowed, she makes a pretty good tank and with the hammer procs, yaulp and stuns holds agro pretty damn well.

e) undead dots/nukes - situational but fairly powerful.

f) ac/hp buffs that stack with ours as well as a reverse dmg shield and lower casting time buff

I'm sure I've forgotten a few things, but if you both want to try casters out this duo might be an option.
