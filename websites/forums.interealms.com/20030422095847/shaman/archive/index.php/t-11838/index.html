Groz
General consensus is Trial of execution is the easiest and most straightforward.

There's tons of tactics/ideas for execution so i won't go into details. however, the key thing for us was for the MT/MA to stay on the whatever he chose initially and to target efficiently as possible after each kill or slightly before. Execution is DPS intensive and every second counts.

I agree with the previous poster. Without a chanter/cleric you are losing out on some really nice buffs, Aego/Virtue, KEI and better haste for melees.

That being said, there is no perfect group. Level and equipment is just as important as what class. Unless there is a long line for the trail, you can try, fail, med up and try again within minutes. Exp is not bad for low 60s.

Concentrate on getting the best DPS possible. For our "lets-flag-the-guild" push, we stacked the deck by having other guild members buff the core group. Druids throwing on thorns, Shaman avatar'ing MT before entering etc. We event lent better haste and weapons to the flagees in order to help out.

It's definitely worth doing. For exp, it's hard to beat PoV/PoS. And if you want to level past 62, it's almost required.

Vexz
The target DPS for the group should be over 400. In both cases I'd add Rogue, Cleric. Paladin can hold a back, gain aggro on mobs that aggro the cleric and is tougher than my paladin so he should have no problem tanking all 4 at once.

We recently did it with Pal, Rog, Rog, Cleric, 58 shm, lvl something deadweight druid with 40m (we got tired of waiting). The key is having the paladin use spells to peel so the melees can stay on target. Don't bother slowing til the boss mob.

Get shaman, ranger, enchanter buffs prior to fighting if you can. I'd skip the enchanter, but get a tank of a cleric that can stand gettin pounded by 2 mobs til the paladin can peel. Enchanter will not increase dmg output only reduce dmg taken (for the most part).
