For a guild that does not have a large number of 60s ( say mostly 55- ), I would suggest using a 53 necro to help break Fear. We use the necros as a security blanket.

What we did was send in a group of monks and the necro with the objective of getting the necro feigned at the SS on the North wall.

After the necro is FDed. We send the rest of the troops for the mad dash from the zone in to the SS. We order the Clerics to camp immediately. If we are not successful in getting a cleric camped out, we have the monks pull the cleric corpse to the SS and then have the necro with an essence emerald rez the cleric.


Once you get your guild some more levels ( 55+ ), you can do the west wall break much easier. Having two groups with a wizard/druid help when attempting the WW break the first time. Send in the two groups and if the clerics or enchanters go oom while fighting, order the evac. Med to full and do it again.


While either of these tactics may seem gimp, they both have less risk than the traditional run to the SS and hope to get a cleric camped.

This is something that my guild has been working on alot in the last few weeks. We are a smallish guild and usually have only 3 groups or sometimes 4. Clerics are frequently in very short supply. We have no necros. we have no Lvl 60s. only 1 lvl 59, 1 57, 2 56. Our highest enchanter is only 53 so breaking fear for us has been a major challenge. Here is what we have settled on:

1) We try to coordinate with larger guilds who are doing boss mobs, to go in after them. This helped us get folks up to speed on the different mobs, like which had high MR, see invis, etc. This may not be an option for you.

2) Since our enchanter is only 53, she has difficulty in mezzing many mobs. Amy's, Scarelings are always Multi-Tanked.

3) usually we only have 2 clerics, sometimes only 1 so camping a cleric out is not an option for us.

4) we make groups based on how many Druids we have. We almost always have more druids than groups. We evac early and often. Breaking Fear is hard, breaking Fear in Mage summoned gear is harder.

5) We always buff to the Max at the portal before zoning in (duh). Although dispelling mobs are not that much of a problem on the break we always make sure that Sow, Invis, are the last 2 buffs.

6) We send in the highest lvl FDer to FD at portal, usually a monk. We he is ready he stands up and pulls any mobs he can away from the portal. Since we have lower lvls thier aggro range is huge and this helps alot. He usually runs northish and then to the west wall and Feigns again. He sometimes dies.

7) at the same time the rest of us zone in by groups about 10 sec apart. We run to west wall and get in as tight a group as possible. Usually someone has managed to get an aggro by this time and we focus on killing one mob. If not we pull one ASAP and burn it down quick.

8) We evac early and often, usually after kiling only 1 or 2 (clerics OOM) sometimes without killing any. I think our record so far is about 8 or 9. just a short run from D-ring to portal where we med, rebuff as needed and try again.

9) If the above has gone well, the portal is broken within 1-2 hours and regular pulling begins. First with nearby roamers then buildings. The building on the west wall south of the portal still gives us lots of trouble.

Hope this helps
