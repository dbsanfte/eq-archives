I've been duoing with an SK all the way from level newbie up to 60 (and still do).

If you stubbornly will run around with a 1-hander and a shield on the SK then yes, a monk will outdam the SK anyday... but if you get your hands on a good 2-hander, like a reaver or something, and take it from there... you'll be doing quite sufficient damage all the way up in levels.
When we were at your levels we were doing very nice exp compared to others, and no single group we tried gave us faster exp rate than just us duoing away.

I think you shouldn't worry about it - both combos are very nice ones. Now, if you want to play a monk instead then that's a different story...

Compare the monks advantage of higher dam output with the SKs advantages: they can tank better (ie. you can go for bigger mobs), makes it easier in dungeons with snare, fear kite rules at lower levels and also their superior taunt ability makes it easy for the shaman ;)
Sciek Spellbottler
SK and monks both make great partners for a shaman IMO. Monks deal faster damage generally, plus mend is a nice bonus heal every few mins. On the other hand the SK will generally tank better, can snare to keep runners from causing trains, and with a good 2hander can still put out very good damage. Not to mention with an SK tanking you shouldn't ever have to tank more than 1 or 2 rounds after Slowing and Dotting, SK taunt ability is Sooo nice. :)

So, either way you'll be fine. I duo with an SK in my guild regularly and we do very well. He pulls as well as a monk, tanks better, and puts out good damage although not as good as the guild monks we have. On the other hand, the SK snares and keeps aggro like nobody else so we never have problems with runners causing trains nor do I get the snot beat out of me which means more hp for cannis and less downtime.

If both are level 37 now, I'd stick with the SK. by the time you PL the monk up to the same level as you shaman you could have gained several levels with both the SK and Shaman. Neither is clearly better as a partner than the other, they're just different...

The only thing an SK lack is a defined role on raids, since many people don't feel comfortable with being just another melee doing damage.

In group situations SKs do a bit less damage until you can find some way to get your ATK significantly higher than your base. My SK twink's got some of the best gear availible, mainly W/N ToV, but his damage is still pretty low since he's only got 900 ATK at 52.

A smart SK can do tons of damage on raids, especially Luclin mobs, there are raids where SKs do damage second only to wizards. The key is of course mana regen. If you are just worrying about duoing through 60 without uber twinkage, an SK will do quite a bit less damage than a monk. Without Death Peace you can't really pull as well as a monk either. The only thing SKs have over monks is versitility and pvp.

Pure damage wise, monks will be above an SK by a fair margin unless the SK has some serious mana regen. However, SK's invis vs undead + other utility spells makes a SK&Shaman duo far more versital. You can duo in dungeons with both live and undead mobs. Killing will be slower, but the SK is an overall more fun charater to play, imo.

Landragoran Heronwing
A SK can grow into a more effective partner with greater ability and versatility than a monk can ever hope to achieve.

SK's have **** at truly high level that makes them (under the guiding fingers of a professional player) a deadly deadly force of friggen nature. A SK can solo places for exp that a shaman that would make a same level shaman blanche and turn away. Amazing class really.

It all starts with the enormously overpowered Tank/Mage combo. Basically if there is such a thing in EQ, which the designers claim that there is not, a SK is a Tank/Mage. Tanking abilities are same as a paladin armor and defense wise. Melee output is slightly higher than a paladin due to self attack buffs they cast, raising attack up to 100 points. That's an avatar folks. Next, for the SK who knows how to control his pet, there is a kick ass pet at lvl 59 that is about twice as good as our best shaman pet. It's a lvl 44 necro pet.

Next you have the spells. The DoTs they cast are mostly of the lure variety given to necros, but of a lower level. Still, they can really lay on some decent DoT damage to aid the DPS they have already.

Lastly they can BOTH snare and FD. This makes the SK the ideal puller for some situations, and a force unto himself in others. Imagine being able to walk up to a mob deep in a dungeon, surrounded by other mobs (you're invis or ITU, whichever), step off to a corner, pull the mob, slay it, snare it, and if you get adds, just FD and try again.

Amazingly overpowered class, akin to necromancers in that respect. SKs are to other melees as necros are to other casters. Very self sufficient, powerful spells and abilities, but less raid friendly than some classes, and not really thought of as "a required class" thus causing them to not be top choices in many groups.

You'll need to be an outgoing person to play an SK at later levels. You need to make your own groups, make friends who want to group with you, and thus show them all how you are someone whom they can't live without.

We shamans have been in that position for a long time, or at least a similar one. We've shown to the world of Norrath that we are very very useful in groups and highly desired overall.
