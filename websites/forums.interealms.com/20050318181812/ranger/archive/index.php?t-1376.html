Rule 1: Life is not fair - get used to it.

Rule 2: The world won't care about your self-esteem. The world will expect you to accomplish something BEFORE you feel good about yourself.

Rule 3: You will NOT make 40 thousand dollars a year right out of high school. You won't be a vice-president with a car phone, until you earn both.

Rule 4: If you think your teacher is tough, wait till you get a boss. He doesn't have tenure.

Rule 5: Flipping burgers is not beneath your dignity. Your grandparents had a word for burger flipping - they called it opportunity.

Rule 6: If you mess up, it's not your parents' fault, so don't whine about your mistakes, learn from them.

Rule 7: Before you were born, your parents weren't as boring as they are now. They got that way from paying your bills, cleaning your clothes and listening to you talk about how cool you are. So before you save the rain forest from the parasites of your parents' generation, try delousing the closet in your own room.

Rule 8: Your school may have done away with winners and losers but life has not. In some schools they have abolished failing grades and they'll give you as many times as you want to get the right answer. This doesn't bear the slightest resemblance to ANYTHING in real life.

Rule 9: Life is not divided into semesters. You don't get summers and Christmas break off, and very few employers are interested in helping you find yourself. Do that on your own time.

Rule 10: Television is NOT real life. In real life people actually have to leave the coffee shop and go to jobs.

Rule 11: Be nice to nerds. Chances are you'll end up working for one have a good day

Rule 12: Smoking does not make you look cool. It makes you look moronic. Next time you're out cruising, watch an 11-year-old with a butt in his mouth. That's what you look like to anyone over 20. Ditto for "expressing yourself" with purple hair and/or pierced body parts.

Rule 13: You are not immortal. (See Rule No. 12.) If you are under the impression that living fast, dying young and leaving a beautiful corpse is romantic, you obviously haven't seen one of your peers at room temperature lately.

Rule 14: Enjoy this while you can. Sure parents are a pain, school's a bother, and life is depressing. But someday you'll realize how wonderful it was to be a kid. Maybe you should start now. You're welcome.

-- Charles J. Sykes Dumbing Down Our Kids: Why American Children Feel Good About Themselves But Can't Read, Write, Or Add

Rule 1: Life isn’t fair. Get used to it, and try to make it more fair for the people around you. Complaining doesn’t help, but maybe you can.

Rule 2: Self respect is more important than the respect of the world. At the end of the day, you have to live with yourself and your conscience. Don’t simply accomplish things and expect them to make you happy.

Rule 3: Don’t worry about money. If you’re willing to work, if you’re willing to learn, if you’re willing to stand up for what you believe in, you’ll get the money you deserve.

Rule 4: If you think your teacher or boss is tough, think about your conscience. It’s a lot harder to keep your integrity in a corrupt world than it is to please some corporate management drone or turn in your homework.

Rule 5: Flipping burgers may be beneath your dignity. Do it anyway. Do it better than anyone else, and maybe someday you’ll be the one telling other people to man that fry-daddy.

Rule 6: If you mess up, it may be your parent’s fault. They’re as human as you are. It may be the fault of your parents, or their parents, or their parent’s parents, but if you don’t take responsibility and solve the problem, then it’s your fault now and someday it’ll be your children’s fault. Solve the problems.

Rule 7: Before you were born, your parents were strange. They’re strange now. Someday, your kids are going to think you’re strange. Accept the strangeness, and accept that everyone has something to learn from everyone else.

Rule 8: Schools still have winners and losers. The ‘winners’ end up pumping gas for a living, since being a jock doesn’t often translate to a marketable skill. The ‘losers’ often make CNN headline news, in any number of a variety of ways. It’s the people who think for themselves and are willing to live for themselves, that succeed.

Rule 9: Life is divided into semesters. They’re called ‘periods of unemployment between lay-offs’ or, more succinctly, ‘career changes.’ If you haven’t found yourself by this time, then do it while you’re looking for another job. Don’t do it on company time, or you’ll be back to manning that fry-daddy.

Rule 10: To some people, television is real life. Whatever you do, don’t let this happen to you.

Rule 11: Be nice to everyone. The people you treat badly often end up in positions of authority over you.

Rule 12: Don’t live your life simply trying to impress people. Live your life for yourself. Looking stupid can be one of the greatest freedoms you can achieve. If someone doesn’t like how you look, then remember we’ve already fought one civil war over what people looked like; hopefully, their kind will breed out eventually.

Rule 13: You won’t live forever. Live every day as if it’s the greatest gift you can be given.

Rule 14: Sure, it seems like this time of your life is a pain. Life is pain. Get used to it, or take charge of it and make it better.



This is coming from a longhaired smoker, so be warned. There’s a lot more to life than repetitive dogma.

This post was not meant as an attack on anyone, but as a rebuttal list.
