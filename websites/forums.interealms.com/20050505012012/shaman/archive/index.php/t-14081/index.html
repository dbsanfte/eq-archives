Packet sniffers are useful, because when a mob spawns, the server notifies all clients in the zone. It also tells you the loot the mob has, in case some of it requires a graphic produced (a sword or such). Packet sniffers hijack the data destined for your client and decode the info to let you know where the mob spawned and what he has on him. Remember TCP/IP packets travelling through a LAN segment are visible to all network appliances in that segment. The only way for SOE to fix this would be to encrypt the packets (the packets would still be visible, but unintelligible), which would require too much overhead, with the amount of packets they normally send.

Actually, the complete quote from EULA is:

"You may not use any third party software to modify the Software to change Game play."

This statement alone wouldn't exclude you from running a log parser. It also wouldn't exclude you from running a packet sniffer either because you're not modifying the EQ client software by running it. The only thing this statement does exclude are any type of hacks/emulators/macros that people may use.

On another note, I noticed something else while looking this up. The EULA also states that:

"You may not copy (except to make one necessary back-up copy), distribute, sell, auction, rent, lease, loan, modify or create derivative works, adapt, translate, perform, display, sublicense or transfer all or any portion of the Software."

This is the statement that would most likely exclude the use of these programs since the basic purpose of both log parsers and packet sniffers is to translate and display portions of the Software. The interesting part is that this statement would also make spoiler/fan sites a violation of the EULA. Afterall, the whole point of creating these sites is to display/distribute derivative works based on portions of the Software. :p
