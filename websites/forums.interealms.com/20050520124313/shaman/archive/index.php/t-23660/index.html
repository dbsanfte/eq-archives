Since I'm sure everybody doesn't have a brand new video card that can handle all the features and I'm unsure what will and will not be on by default, this may prove to be useful for everyone come tomorrow.

Quick system specs: Athlon 1ghz, 512mb ram, 32mb Geforce 2 GTS AGP, Win XP w/ SP2

Right now, you'll see an "Advanced" button at the bottom of the "Display" tab in the Options, but it's greyed out. Tomorrow, it will not be. In this advanced menu are some more advanced options, some of which, you may or may not be able to use, or have any use for except to make things pretty.

If you have an older video card, some of these may cause a crash until you disable them in the eqclient.ini. Right now, my eqclient.ini contains the following...

VertexShaders=FALSE
20PixelShaders=0
1xPixelShaders=0
WaterSwap=0
Shadows=0

... if I turn shadows on, chain desktop crashes. I had to go into the file and change Shadows=1 to Shadows=0 in order to STOP crashing.

Shadows are self explanatory, but look to require a DX9 card to pull it off and, chances are, will be quite a performance hit. The rest of the options seem to be a bit more "fail safe" and won't cause chain-crashing if you turn them on and can't support them. However, if you are having serious performance problems, here would be another place to go in and turn things off or on, depending on your taste.

There's also 2 sliders. One for Actor Clip Plane, another for Shadow Clip Plane. Both should be fairly self-explanatory. Shadow Clip Plane being how far away you can see shadows, actor clip plane being how far away you can see PCs and NPCs (similar to the "Hide traders" option in Bazaar, but as you get close, they'll start appearing, and it works anywhere).

I'm unsure on the dynamic update interval and dynamic sky reflection size options, as my card can't handle much of the advanced options anyway. Additionally, if you are having performance problems, try turning Level of Detail on. What that does is make people/objects farther away, use less processing power to show up.

I'll say this, the actor clip plane will be real nice for those with sub-par machines, especially in the Omens zones. No more staring at walls maybe!
