As a naked shaman at level 60, I could run through the Howling Stones with little problem. I could probably even work my way down to the Observer in Chardok.

A naked warrior at level 60 couldn't make the run to Chardok, much less make it down to the Observer.

Now, tell me that melee balancing has little to do with loot. Disciplines are easily countered with the fact that I can raise my HP, lower a mob's HP, make a mob attack slower, heal my dumb ass and, if all fails, make the mob stand still while I run away faster than it or its friends.

However, check the difference between a level 60 warrior with a BoC and Primal, a Vex Thall BP and a buttload of NToV gear vs a level 60 warrior in assorted Cobalt and Crafted, with a Wurmslayer in one hand and a Yak in the other. The first is doing significantly more damage faster and taunting better than the second. He also has more HP and more AC.

Now check the difference between a level 60 wizard (or a level 60 cleric, even) in full ToV armor and mad FT drops vs a level 60 wizard in an SMR, wearing some Netted Kelp he bought in Greater Faymart. While the first has more mana and regenerates it somewhat faster, he doesn't nuke harder and he doesn't nuke faster. The gear makes very little difference when compared to the difference the gear makes for a warrior or a ranger or a paladin or a SK, ad infintium.

Leaving AAs out of this, of course. It's hard to say that our AAs or the AAs of a cleric do for us what crits, flurries, rampages, AE taunt and the like do for a warrior. :P

Anyway, the purpose of this is to make it so that the power of casters will scale with equipment in the same way that the power of the melee classes scales with better equipment. This has very little to do with the idea of balancing the casting classes against one another, and everything to do with the idea of balancing casting classes as a whole with melee classes.
