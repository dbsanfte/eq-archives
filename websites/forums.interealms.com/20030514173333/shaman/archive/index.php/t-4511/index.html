Gloriana
He's the same fight, I'm pretty sure.

Why I suggested killing him if he's in a bad place:

1) Lord Rak might be up when you want to do your hand in but be spawned in a very unsafe place (near potential adds, water, no place to safely park mages/cleric etc.)

2) Due to the short time of reaver spawn (2 hours?) Rak AND his reaver might be up at the same time.

3) In the past, you could kill Rak's reaver spawn Rak, wait 2 hours, kill Rak's reaver, spawn Rak etc etc. At one time I saw 3 non aggro Raks up, standing around CoM.

This no longer works. Now, if Lord Rak is up and you kill his reaver, nothing will happen, he just won't spawn.

So:

1) Kill the poorly placed Rak without doing a hand in. Just take him down any way possible. You could consider it a dry run of your actual turn in. Practice makes perfect!

2) Pull Rak's reaver to a safe, controlled location. Spawn Rak. Turn in tear, proceed with Rak fight under ideal circumstances, loot scale.

Killing two Raks doesn't add much time to your raid, considering Rak and his reaver are probably up simultaneously. It's good practice for the actual fight and ensures that you aren't stuck killing Rak in a less than ideal location that can lead to:

* Gating Rak - bad
* Rak falling in the water - bad
* Rak fight + adds - bad
