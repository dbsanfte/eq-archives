Hail,

I would also recommend trying a respectful petition.
Explain clearly that it was your misunderstanding & your mistake, and that if it is at all possible you would very much appreciate either a rollback to previous save or simply recovery of your lost items.

I have had excellent luck with this approach on more than one occasion.
A long time ago I inadvertently destroyed a critical part of the Prayer Shawl Quest, and after a few days I was able to get it returned.

More recently (like last week), I accidentally lost my range slot item, the Brain of Cazic Thule, because I left it in a collapsible toolbox that I
purposefully destroyed. That was retrieved the same day after a lengthy wait for a GM (Guide responded within ~15 min though!) & a brief investigation.

If you have the time/date/last known location of your alt's body, that will help your request, as that helps narrow down the searching the GM has to perform in order to verify that you aren't trying to scam them.

Also, while not relavent to your specific situation, if you make a mistake (such as vendor selling something invaluable in error), use the /report function ON YOURSELF to show the lines of text that will prove your case. A friend of mine inadvertantly sold a weapon worth about 20kpp on the open market to a vendor for 11pp and change. :eek: He included a report showing the vendor purchase text and mentioned the report in his petition. It took some time, but he was able to retrieve the weapon (he had to pay the same amount the vendor paid him, lol) using this method.

The old days of online CSR never ever doing anything to help the players are mostly over. It always helps if you can clearly & succinctly state your case (w/o cursing or whining) in your petition as well as having the supporting data that will help them help you.

Remember, a Guide is powerless to help you in this type of situation, and that's who you are going to run into first. Politely explain the situation in greater detail (since your petition is word length limited) and then request the Guide add any missing pertinent details & ask him/her to bump your petition to the GM queue. Thank the Guide for their time & response. Also, if the Guide doesn't let you know, specifically ask the operating hours for GM service on your server, as this can vary.
Normally the GM specific server coverage will be for a specific 8 hour time period, and then there will be another 8 hour period of cross-server coverage where the 'floater' GM's hop around helping the most dire petition cases (which unfortunately yours is not).

It may take a few days or even a few weeks, but it can't hurt to try (as long as you are polite) and if you recover your gear it will definitely be worth it.

Hope that helps & best of luck to you. :cool:

Take care,
