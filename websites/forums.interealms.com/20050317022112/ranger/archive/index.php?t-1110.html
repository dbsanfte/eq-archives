Inspired by the “Know what you Know” thread. :)

Here is the situation as it is presented to you:

You enter a room and see a man in a White Coat standing next to what can best be described as a more comfortable, wired version of a Barber Chair.

He explains to you that you have a decision to make, sit in the chair or not.

If you DO sit in the chair you have the option to make life whatever you want it too be, You can change your life as little or as much as you would like to. Make yourself someone else entirely. You can stay the same person; all the same friends and family, just give yourself $10 million in the bank so you don't have to work. Maybe just give yourself $10,000 a year raise so you can pay off some extra bills. WHATEVER you want. Here is the key, once you sit down, and the switch is thrown, you will forget completely that you ever did sit down. As far as you know, this will become your reality, everyone and everything will continue on as if nothing changed, except for the changes you choose, and you will NEVER remember it happened.

You don't sit down, leave the room and go on as if you where never given this choice.


Do you sit? Why? Why not? How do you know you haven't already sat down?

I know, I know, there is a similarity of sorts too The Matrix, but this was an Ethics class discussion from back in '93 or '94 and I always enjoyed talking to friends about it. Now I pass it along to my friends here at The Glade. I cannot wait to hear your views on this.
