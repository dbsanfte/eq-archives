Reysa
Pet control during battle can be useful to help position the mob. Getting all the pets off a mob while the MT is trying to move it to his favorite spot makes his job a lot easier. That's more of an issue for battles where you can't pin the target.

If a pet has two equally-tempting targets it will attack the closest one. You can use that to make your pet switch targets. Have it follow (/pet hold, /pet back off, /pet follow) you over to the desired one then tell it to attack. /pet hold makes moving the pet around a lot easier. (Use /pet hold, which tells it to ignore attackers, before /pet back off, which clears the hate list, to avoid picking up a new target between the two commands.)

Dealing with AE and enrage were discussed by previous posters. /pet hold is very useful for both.

If you have a friend that was PL'd and has no melee skills you can use your pet as a target to build offense and piercing, 1HB, etc.

Once you have /pet hold you begin to wonder how you ever lived without it. For a shaman, it is not the first class AA you'd want to get, but it is quite useful. (It *was* the first class AA my mage got, but then pets are more important to mages than to shaman.) Of course, if you don't use your pet much then it wouldn't do you much good, but the original poster was asking specifically about pet AAs.
