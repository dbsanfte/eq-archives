I 2 box a necro and shaman.

I chose this combo mainly because I wanted to be flexible whether I am grouped with others or just soloing with these 2 chars.

When solo, I work mostly on the necro since it is easier to pull singles because of FD, snares, roots, and fear. Then on the shaman, I would have slow, pet attack, and 1 dot hot keyed. I would throw a debuff or/and extra dot in that lineup depending on where I am fighting.

When in a group, I work mainly on the shaman specially if I am the only healer. The necro would be hotkeyed only for pet attack and twitching. A snare and dot would be optional depending on the group make up.

It really isn't that hard playing a necro and shaman in a group since you won't have to switch back and forth between your 2 pc's trying to make sure everything goes well. Heck, you can leave the necro FD if you want and just hotkey pet attack so you won't even have to look at that screen.

The best part of this combo is the soloing capabilities. The only mob that gave me problems are those that are totally immune to spells (Gullerback comes to mind). Good thing about those spell immune mobs is you have 2 pets that can buy you time to gate both characters out.

Good luck on your choice.
Jaruden
Their are only 2 big benefit to 2 boxing IMO:
1) You have a required raid class sitting around, and you need 1 more of said class (cleric most common for this, or enchanter sometimes) who is on your 2nd account.

2) You have "issues" with groups. This could be several things: you don't like pickup groups, you don't have time to get into pickup groups, you never get pickup groups, etc. Pretty much, a way to make it easier to solo. Keep in mind, unless you kill things 2x faster (minus a little bit for the group bonuses), you're getting less exp than you would solo.

Personally, I hate 2 boxing, but I'll do it on "raids" (only 3 real people in my guild, so we do a bit of that, if you can call them raids =P) if I must. I never have troubles getting a group if I want one, and the exp in even a pickup group in bot / pov / hoh is going to be better than duoing in almost all cases.


edit: Forgot a 3rd reason: Utility. Cleric to rez you, druid to port you around (less useful since pop), enc to kei you, etc.

Hamenu
Mage seems low maintenance, but I'm not sure how much benefit they'd be aside from summoning pet weapons and burn dps.

Burn DPS is good on a mage in a pinch, but their pets are where the easy DPS comes into play for 2 boxers (especially with a strong damage shield). Look at it from another angle, Mages are a very strong DPS class, but what is their weakness? No Defense.

With Shammie Slow/Root, a Mage becomes a great partner. IMO its easier to control two pets while lobbing nukes, than it is to try and control a melee PC. Yes they fold like a wet napkin when hit, however, in most cases, the shaman will be gaining aggro, and the pets will be peeling the mobs off of the shaman. The mage won't get hit often.

One of the biggest downsides to two boxing is transporting your characters. Having two classes that can gate is definately a plus, mix in Call of the Hero, and you'll be moving around extremely well.

One other thing to point out, strong pet classes like mages and necros farm greens better than anyone else in the game (the /pet attack command can be stacked, and you build up a mini hate list for your pet). If you're thinking of creating a bazaar mule, do it on your main account, and once your pet class is high enough in level, put them to work in the tradeskill zones.
