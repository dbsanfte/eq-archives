I admit to being a geek. I've read the books quite a bit, I own a copy of the Silmarillion (epic creation saga, even more wordy than the books), and saw Fellowship enough in the theaters to catch the trailer for TTT.

I enjoyed TTT immensely (aside from one git who kept shouting "Lord of the Rings!", as if we didn't know what movie we were going to see). I actually got chills during two parts: the opening sequence - no spoilers, but *WOW*; but I can't mention the second because it's a pivotal moment, but Merry and Pippin are involved, and it changes the third characters' POV. And the audience cheered during another part - Legolas pulls off a move that will have fanboi elf wannabes screaming for something like that in EQ. Too bad there are already horses in EQ.... :p

If you aren't certain about it, go for a matinee.

If you've never seen Fellowship, go out and rent the dang thing, then go see TTT.

Gollum was done perfectly, even if people in the audience were laughing during his struggles with himself.

Despite the "creative license", I can't think of anyone who could have done a better job. Chris Carter? Bleh. Spielberg? It would have turned into schmaltz PDQ. Maybe DeMille could have done it, if he'd been alive today.

The story was well told, and yes, TTT is a dark book; just reflect that this is effectively the trilogys' Empire Strikes Back, and anticipate the amazingness that will be Return of the King.

I want to see the extended version of TTT; only ten months to go, la la la....
