1) the mob will be slowed by a x% from the basic slow % ? That will mean the shaman slow will be slighty highter than Chanter, Bst and bards (even 1 or 2 %).

This is correct.

Taken from another thread (I've answered this question a few times :) ):

"When a mob mitigates slow, that slow is reduced by a certain percentage. I'm not sure how much the exact mitigation is, but it is something like 75%, 50%, or 25% mitigated (for slightly, partial, and mostly landed slows, respectively). Thus, a turgur's (75%) slow on a mob that partially mitigates will come out to 37.5% slowed. Grummus (40%) on that mob will come out to 20% slowed. So, basically, turgur's is always the best slow as long as magic lands. If not, grummus is the best."
Einnod
It's a set % of the slow that was cast. Using your example, if you cast Turgurs on a mob(70%) and you got the message partially slowable, it would be slowed by 35%. However if you used a lower level slow(let's say tagar's, not sure of the slow %, but I'l say 50 for easy math), it would be slowed by only 25%.

If you want to test this, find a mob that is "mostly" slowable. Cast a lower level slow on it, like walking sleep, and see if you get the message. If it's mostly slowed with walking sleep, it'd make sense that only 70% of walking sleep is taking effect.
weissblau
Yeah, they really missed the boat on that one. They should have SUBTRACTED the mitigation percentage from the slow percentage, i.e. a 20% mitigating mob with 75% slow cast on it would be 55%. BST casting a 65% slow would get it slowed 45%.

The way the did it, the difference between a BST or ENC slow and a SHM slow gets smaller with mitigating mobs, to the point that a "mostly" mitigating mob shows only a tiny difference when slowed by a shaman compared to an ENC or BST.
