Sengal
Lately I've been using Sengal to PL some alts of mine. Here is some advice on the pitfalls that I encountered:

My first alt was a barbarian warrior. I started out by buffing him to the gills with strength, stamina, agility, dexterity, tailsman, regen, quickness and sow. He was also twinked out in full-crafted and some decent weapons. I then proceeded to kill yard trash until level 5 and then moved on to mammoth calfs with Sengal healing and re-buffing as needed. I hit level ten in a few short hours but discovered that my skills were all crap. I leveled so fast that I couldn't max any skills. When I took my warrior out solo, I couldn't hit anything and got slaughtered.

My newest alt is a human paladin. While I buffed him the same as the warrior, I gave him minimal twinkage. I also focused on killing dark blue and even-con mobs instead of reds and yellows. My leveling was slower (took maybe twice as long), but all of my important skills are near max. As a result, I have a much more effective character.

The moral of the story - be careful about PLing too fast as your charcater will have serious issues once the mobs start getting tough.
