Krup
With two-boxing you'll never have to look for a group, wait around for guildmates, or put up with any in-group stupidity besides your own! ;) This can be both a boon to your gameplay, or a curse to your personal life.

You will become the king of macros as your "2nd" box becomes more and more automated.

You will get to the point where you can play your second box without a monitor (I use my primary box for all the visuals - including targeting. Makes "runs to the zone" a bit hectic though! :) )

Dungeon crawls while two boxing are a big challenge, but static camps are a piece of cake.

Overall I'd say I've gotten MORE than 2 accounts worth of enjoyment out of 2-boxing. You will too Rodahn.

Calabar
I have never played with two accounts, but the friend I’m grouping with does. He plays a ranger as his main and a cleric on his second account. The two of them make a great team. They’re both level 58 now with numerous AAs. I used to play a ranger and a druid, depending on my mood. I’d group with my friend from time to time but I didn’t add a lot into the mix. After my guild broke up and I couldn’t really bring myself to play my two mains I started playing my kitty shaman again. After soloing into my upper 30s, I started grouping with my friend again. Let me tell you, a shaman makes a good third addition to the group. The ranger pulls and tanks, I buff, haste and slow and the cleric heals and does a rez when needed.

From what I can tell, my friend has very little trouble duel boxing. His main attention is on his ranger and he tosses in heals when needed. His timing is very good. There are some minor difficulties, like people talking to his cleric when he’s pulling or otherwise busy. But overall it works very well. The only time I’ve seen him have difficulty getting around is in dungeons where auto fallow doesn’t work well, which is most of them. We have to move a bit more slowly so he can manually maneuver his cleric. And I would expect that a mad dash to zone would be troublesome as well. But to be honest, he plays both his characters so well there is rarely ever any need to zone.

I’ve thought of playing two accounts, but the expense of buying another computer and keeping up with two accounts has kept me from trying it. However, if you have a strong interest in doing so, I’d suggest you give it a shot.

Now that being said I still single my shm. 2boxing can get very stressful and you are amazed the time you have to think and react when you single box.

I tried using a kvm switch but I found my abilities severely handicapped since I couldn't do as much...example, fighting rats in pod a root breaks and the rat starts to flee, to avoid adds I can back off pet while following and attacking the mob with monk. Then while still following and attacking I can try to root again with shm. If I used eqw or a kvm switch the monk would sit like a dumbass while I was on the shm...also, I can canni my shm while the monk is out pulling or using bind wound on the shm. I just cannot see using eqw or a KVM switch being that versatile.
