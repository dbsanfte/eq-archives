Bigguss
Kobold boots: Extended Affliction II (20% longer duration on DoTs under level 45) - probably no more than 50 pp.

Chipped Bone Bracer: Affliction Efficiency II (25% less mana to cast DoTs under level 45) - few hundred pp, tops.

Suddenly EBreath goes from a damage/mana efficiency of 2.19 (219 dmg... 30 + 27(7)... for 100 mana) to a damage/mana efficiency of 3.28 (246 dmg... 30 + 27(8)... for 75 mana). A 50% improvement for a few hundred pp is a good investment.

Between that and the removal of the DoT while moving penalty- it's a good time to be a DoTing low-level shaman! :)

Well firstly I think its simply cheesy. NOT just my personal taste, but a pretty common opinion. Showing your back to the enemy and running away from it is hardly heroic fantasy. If you can't see that, then you might reflect on fantasy novels: how many descriptions of kiting do you come across compared with hack and slay?

Secondly, it causes problems for other players. You need room to kite, so immediately you cause some inconvenience for other players in the same area: lag etc. raises the potential of greater problems still. Thats why root/rot is frowned on less.

Thirdly, its commonly associated with druids. Any behaviour which makes us look like druids is bad news: even the least perceptive person must have noticed that druids are the most loathed class in the game.

Fourthly, its bad tactics if you do it so much that you don't max. out your defence skill. If you can't figure out why thats a problem I despair of you.
