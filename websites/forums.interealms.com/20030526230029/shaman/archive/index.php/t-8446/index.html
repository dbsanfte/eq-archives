I kinda feel wierd answering this as my shammy is only 40, but, I havea 60 mage and before modrod nerfs I used to have pretty much teh same problem as you on "when" to use it when soloing/pairing with a healer.

You are your own healer so soloing is pairing with a healer. Anyways, to answer your question on when to gain excess mana.

Its all a balance between downtime and effeciency. When I was rodding, that means I wasnt nuking and putting out DPS. However, I would run oom and stop puting out DPS so I'd have to rod.

Shammy case is a little different because Cani is on a timer. If you are oom when the timer pops its a simple choice. You Cani. If you are half mana and stil lfighting and gobling mana, its easy. You cani. If you are 60-70% and sustaining that rate while fighting, then it gets difficult.

Its all about DPS if you have "extra" mana. If you dont have extra mana as stated above you are gonna cani anyways so you can slow, ect. If you have plenty of mana, and you can suddenly make more, you will channel that mana into some DPS. The question is what is the best way to do this?

DPS is damage per SECOND. So you need to figure out how long it takes to use your excess mana nuking (I assume you are gonna nuke and not Dot, as you would have doted with your "Normal" mana, nuke mana is the "extra" fast damage mana). You then need to add in the time to Cani and heal. Take the total damage then divide by that total time and see if it makes sense to do the Cani. Ie, if it adds only 10 dps (not likely) and if you would have just medded and reapplied a dot to do the same or more damage, then its not worth it. However, if it adds a nice 50dps whack then its definately worth it.

Point is, take a pencil and do some quick back of the envelope math. Think it out for a second, then set some guidelines for yourself.

I know its not the exact information you were seeking, but its how us mages did it when we were the mana generators. :P
