Urdegish
First of all, ITU potions dont work versus the aliens. You can invis past all the skellies and aliens outside the cave fine (yes, the skellies dont see normal invis). Con every alien to make sure they cant see you, as sometimes the odd one does.

Once you are in the cave, use assidious vision to check the first room for any see invis mobs. Sometimes a shaman type mob spawns and see-invis's the entire room. If so, come back another day, its really not worth the time or effort. After that room, you continue up and check the next for see invis. There is only 2 static spawns there, and a wanderer from the right hand path. Can kill these if one sees invis, just position them away from the wanderer so you dont have three.

If neither see invis in that room, move into the right-hand tunnel. If the wanderer sees invis, engage him deep in the tunnel where you will get no adds. Make sure it is far enough down from the Swarmcaller though.

After that, the Swarmcaller sees invis, have to kill him anyways. IF you struggle with him, gate out there and then to save a rather mushy CR.

Then the ice tunnels. The mage aliens see invis, whereas the melee do not. If you are lucky, they will be all melee. The mage can check this with his eye, since Assidious Vision cant be used (cant acquire target before aggro)

You will nearly always pull all 4 if they see invis, especially at your level. At 65, in partial-ornate, early PoP gear, I can just solo all 4, whatever combination of class they are. Requires tough dog and a stroke of luck sometimes on heals. If one sees invis, I wouldnt recomend pulling them. That is up to your discretion though, maybe 2 of you can do it I dont really know :)

Now, Tawro. max hit is 350, triples, quads if dual wielding (I think, dont quote me). I usually pull with spirit call to get Malos and slow in. I know you dont have either of these, im just stating what I use to indicate what kind of buffer of time you need.

I cant really give you any tactics. The way you duo him without Torpor will be completely different to the way I killed him at first (Level 62, no Malo, but just bought Torpor). He can do alot of damage annoyingly fast, and the odd interupt on either of your fragile selves will be the end of you.

However, I am damn pleased to see someone wanting to give it a go. If you fail badly, leave it for a few levels and hope to get some spells. If you fail at 14% because of unlucky melee damage, try again. There is a difference between the two defeats :)

Good luck if you try it :)

Well, me and my mage buddy finally got around to trying this duo. Long after when I first wanted to try but oh well. We're both 65 now, me with 19 AA and him with bout 80 AA I think. I wanted to post some thoughts and tactics that we used to help everyone out.

We actually had three in group, the third was my Enchanter Bot parked at the zone line she buffed us KEI and sat on her tush for the rest of it.

Setup...

Fully Buffed Mage Pet including a fresh Quiessence was pocketed.
We both did standard self buffs except I picked up a Virtue in PoK for the pull.

Getting to Tawro...

This is often the hardest part of doing Tawro with a small riad force. There is a mix of mobs that can be passed with Invis versus Undead and Regular Invis in the way. In addition there is potentially the Swarmcaller who see's through both and if killed begins an event that will be a pain in the butt. We came up with what I will call an ingenious solution (though painfully simple). Mage parked himself outside the tunnels on the hill with a clear view of the entrance (IVU). I ran inside all the way to the Swarmcaller and agro'd him and any of the mobs that see through regular invis (IVU). I trained everything out of the tunnel and towards the zone line. When the mage saw the Swarmcaller clear the tunnel entrance he made a mad dash for the Tawro Setup spot in the tunnels (last corner before Tawro's room). When he arrived he simply CotH'd me to him. Done.

Tawro Icequake...

Chump. Dogdog is summoned, fully buffed medded back to full. Spellset as follows:

Kragg
Tnarg
Q
Malo
Turgur
CannIV
Anathema
GreaterImobilize (I soon found out was worthless on these guys)

I pulled with Malo
Mage Pet grabbed agro
Mage Malosinia
I slowed
Mage did PetLayHands for first heal at 50pct.
I Q Pet
I Anathema Tawro
I Q Pet
I Anathema Tawro
etc.
Mage does his mage thing.
Tawro Icequake Dies.

Spirit of Tawro...

Same Spell Set and tactics.

I would note that I tossed in a few Tnarg's while fighting the spirit. The spirit can put down some spike dps even while slowed.

We re-slowed and malosinia'd once at the 5 min mark. Tawro Dropped at just about 8 minutes. Mage held his swarm pets and dragon nuke pet till 20pct and Pet-Hold took care of the Enrage. I finished at 50m and didn't burn any mod rod. Mage finished at 5 manna with no mod rod burns as he was nuking hard at the end (note: we could have gotten 2 burns in durring the 8 minute fight).

He dropped the pally sk axe, Cha bracer and the longsword of freedom. With my chanter still parked at the zone we were able to CotH a Bard from the guild in to loot the sword, sadly nobody able to loot the axe so I picked it up as a little momento.

Things I would do different...

Remove Kragg and Greater Imobilize from spellset and add Cripple and Tears.

Replace the first anathema with Cripple and burn both mod rods durring the fight. Use the extra Manna to sneak some dps in with Tears. Prolly finish a little lower manna but who cares.

I think the whole thing took under 2 hours. I think we can get it done in under an hour now that we know what we're doing. I think that a 8k-10k bracer and a nice sword for a guildie is well worth our time. This may be a farm spot after all.
