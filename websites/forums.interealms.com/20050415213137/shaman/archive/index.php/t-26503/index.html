Rytan (the spell guy):

Here’s a break down of the problem as we see it with the current live version of Sprit of the Panther.

The base DPS for an application of panther is roughly 100 (counting crits from applicable Aas) for a focused duration of about 16 ticks or 1:36 seconds. Theoretically with full focus and extended enhancement, perfect targeting, etc, one shaman can keep it up on 16 players. I don’t really think that’s a reasonable balance point though, so lets call it 12 for the sake of argument.

DPS/Target = ~100
Target/Shaman = 12
DPS/Shaman = 1,200

It actually takes a while for this to get going as it’s being cast on several people, so really it ends up being about half that if you don’t reapply it to the first target. So roughly 600 DPS for 7800 mana, which most shaman should be able to handle for at least those 12 applications, and in most cases longer.

600 DPS is higher then even wizards in most situations depending on gear etc. Given that shaman have never primarily been a DPS class, and have a number of other useful abilities to bring to the table 600 is just plain too high. The spell needs to be reigned in.

That being said I’m not dead set on (or even particularly fond of) the incantation that presently sits on test. Other possible solutions I’ve seen that I think have merit are:

1: Lower the recast to 10-12 seconds, lower the damage/proc so that the spell will top out at about 300 DPS under relatively conducive circumstances.

2: Make the spell group, triple the mana cost, add a 50-52 second recast, mark the spell as no MGB.

I think either of those would be viable alternatives given minor tweaks to the actual values.

I’m open to any more feedback you have on the subject.
