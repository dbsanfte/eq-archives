Zaviax McCleo
It's hurting and I'm not really saying that because I want to see it die.

People who were dinging 50 in my guild are leaving out of boredom. Other guilds are reporting the same. A lot of the bigger well known guilds on my server folded, and lots of others are leaving just out of boredom in general or disgust at Sony's Station Exchange program.

Start up and make a new character and you'll see all but a few servers at Low population. The other few are medium only.

They changed the game way too much that a lot of people didn't know what we're playing anymore. The game starts to feel closed off once you go through most of it. Way way way too many instance zones and the access quests are insane. There's just too much that they force down your throat.

I could go on and on, espcially about how simple it's gotten but the biggest problem is that it's not a world. It's an Online RPG still but it spent too much time trying to NOT be EQ that it fails as an attemped sequel. They should have just called it Everquest:The Instance Era

Or whatever... Some people are still having fun and it is fun for most people for the first several months. But it's definately not a true sequel to EQ.

If you want an EQ sequel, so far it seems like your best bet is to wait for Vanguard
