I've seen rangers with trueshot and ubah bows crit-hit kill people in less than 10 seconds. Paladins at 60th with good gear are well-nigh impossible to kill, particularly with a few insta-clickies so you cant get rid of their DOT heals. SKs have always been evil, because they only realistically have to get you down to about 1/3 to 1/2 health, then HT. Monks are bad if they catch you indoors, as are rogues with frontstab, unless you can get a decent dmg shield running. Bards are just obnoxious but not dangerous, until they get their mana-drain song. Warriors....well, if you play one dont expect to do that well in PvP (as far as actual killing goes) unless it's raiding groups, when competent warriors form the foundation for a group victory.

But apart from insta-kills like manaburn, very few classes in EQ can stop a shaman who wants to get away from a fight. Up until the AA abilities came along, the only things I feared outdoors were velocitied epic mage pets and 56+ monks with T-staves. (Inner Flame + 100 Blows hurts :eek: )

But against anyone who doesnt have an assload of AA ability points and who isnt in the uberest of your particular server's uber guilds, a shaman above 55 doesnt do all that badly. Just try and keep all your resists above 150, have Insidious Decay memmed, and your first 2 buff slots open, and you'll do fine against 90% of a server population.

There's also a common misconception about PvP skills, since PvP is seperated into combat and suvival.

Combat skill, as a whole, is being able to not only use the ability of your class to the fullest, but also to know the weakness of ever other class, to use everything at your disposal to capitalize on those weaknesses, to be able to create weaknesses when possible, and to use surrounding terrain to your advantage.

The vast majority of Red players for the most part know more about survival than the vast majority of Blue players. However, there exist Blue players who *are* skilled in combat as mentioned above. Not only are they skilled in combat, they also have a far more experience in dealing with people who are far better equipped and who come prepared against every tactic you can use against them. They are more experienced in ways to crack open the formitable defenses of someone who comes to a fight with a full compliment of potions, insta clicks, insta-heals, and single charged items.

For Red players, most of the PvP experience they referre to include group pvp (which is extremely rare on blue servers for obvious reasons), common sense (hide somewhere while medding, keep an eye out for that druid while soloing), ways to avoid being killed (zoning, plugging, use lag/hilly areas to throw off persuers), and know what fights to pick and when to run away. For the most part, these are survival skills, not PvP skills, although it can be argued either way.

That, is why Blues have won all of the "PvP" contests Verant has hosted, because those contests only test one's combat skill, and everything a Red knows about survival goes out of the window when restricted on a small, flat, and clear platform in the Arena.
