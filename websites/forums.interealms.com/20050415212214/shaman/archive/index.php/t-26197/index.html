Guess I'll post this here as well as it looks like the thread is dealing with ideas on the possible Shaman stance solution.

I know I have said that already, but I will say it again and add a few things to this.
The stance idea per se isn't terribly bad. Of course, given the mechanics of EQ, a very versatile class like Druid will benefit a lot more from it than, say, a mage.
Furthermore, again due to the inherent game mechanics, the power boost is significantly higher, if you can pick between and are able to fulfill core functions(Healing - Tanking - Damage), as opposed to moving your "power slider" only between a single core function and a sub-function. Much more so, if the sub-function is part of the core-function(Slowing -> Healing).
Let's look at this a little closer by looking at what exactly these core-functions and sub-functions (wanted and necessary functions)iare, in order to explain what I am referring to.

Wanted/desired functions(core + sub-functions):

Healing
Slowing(sub-function)
Crowd Control in camp(sub-function)
Crowd Control out of camp(sub-function)
Tanking
Buffing
Damage
Resurrection

These are the "functions" I split up to reflect what the average player wants to have in his group to feel safe. However, many of the functions perceived by the majority of players are only sub-functions which are not really needed, if you do dispose of enough power in the core-functions "department".

Necessary or "core"-functions:

Healing
Tanking
Damage

Now, as mentioned above, in order to be succesful with only the core functions, these group spots need to be filled with characters who can perform very well in one of the three functions. The nature of this group will make sure that whatever other function could theoretically be somewhat covered by the character, 90+% of their time is spent on only one of them, their primary.

With this premise and using Druids as an example, I wanted to clarify why a slow/healing stance would not only not be advantageous, but actually detrimental to the class.

These are the 3 stances Druids will be able to pick from:

a. Normal Their healing and offensive abilities are in balance. This is the state they exist in today.

b. Offensive Their offensive power is boosted by 25%, but they suffer a 50% reduction in the power of their healing spells.

c. Defensive Their healing power is boosted by 25%, but they suffer a 50% reduction in the power of their offensive spells.

As I have tried to make clear above, a Class performing 1 of the 3 core-functions will generally dedicate 90+% of their time to do just that. When looking at the Druid stances, you can basically see, that a Druid will gain a 25% boost to their ability to perform a crucial function, while taking negligeable penalty.

[At this point some may argue, that a Druid in nuke-mode, will be an awful secondary healer, a term I have always hated, as all it's saying is that your character is to weak to perform as a main healer and apparently SoE agrees with me as, judging by Brenlo's post, after these changes, every real healer is supposed to be a main healer and in fact, in all of my career I can only think of 4 group encounters that at one time required an active secondary healere(which I would define as requiring two main healers).

There is no secondary healer role in this game and it makes perfectly sense as only 3 healing classes exist, which means that 1/3 of the group make up would have to be from the pool of only 3 classes, when there are so many classes in this game.

In other words, filling up a player who isn't under attack by throwing an HoT on him, doesn't make it a role.]

Back to the point.

A Druid in normal mode does already heal 35+% more effectively than a Shaman. Apparently, SoE's intentions are to see Druids and Shamans as equally effective at healing and the 35% healing advantage was introduced to account for the Shaman's ability to reduce a mob's damage output through the sub-function Slowing.

SoE's intentions are summed up here:

"We are changing many of the all-or-nothing immune flags so they mitigate the effect instead. We want you to use your abilities on most NPCs, but where we feel the ability might be problematic the ability will have a lesser effect."

This is basically saying that 99% of the mobs that matter in the game will mitigate Slow to one degree or another in the future.
No matter how their plan to make us heal more or less effectively, based upon the level of mitigation, will be realized, we can assume that, after these changes, much like now, by making use of the sub-function Slowing and the core-function Healing or just, if we choose or are forced to do so(for whatever reason), Healing, the Shaman will be able to heal more damage than a mob can inflict upon the tank.

I estimate a Shaman or a Druid healing an adequate tank in RS currently has a margin of about 20-30% healing vs. the incoming damage. His HPS is basically 20-30% higher than the mob's DPS. A Cleric's margin is probably close to 80%(he heals 80% more per second than a single mob can damage their tank for).

SoE has decided that this gap is too large(who would have guessed that they'd have to re-design healing?!):

As with Druids, we are looking to increase your healing ability so you can function better as main healer in groups. You folks are a little problematic though, since your healing ability is the function of not just your heals, but your ability to reduce an NPCs damage output through slows. In a situation where an NPC is fully slowable, you are a superb healer but against an NPC who cannot be slowed, your healing falls behind. If we were to just improve your heals, you would quickly outshine any other healer when facing NPCs that can be slowed. So, we are investigating ways to improve your healing ability only in circumstances where an NPC cannot be slowed or you choose not to slow the NPC (for your own strange reasons). These changes will be rolled out at the same time as any druid healing changes to ensure that one class does not eclipse the other.

I am going to guestimate that both, Druids and Shamans, will be seeing an increase that puts them at about 50% margin. This is where the issues start, though.

By this logic Shaman and Druid will be able to heal at a rate of about 1500 HPS when mobs are doing 1000 DPS, but the Shaman will have to do so by using Slowing and Healing(same as now), but he can't be healing for more than 1500 HPS whatever mode he's making use of.
In the best case, the Shaman is looking at a HPS of 1500, whether he is in a healing-mode or slowing-mode, whether the mob is immune, slightly or mostly affected by slows, because Slowing + Healing must not exceed 1500 HPS in order to not outheal the Druid.
The Shaman does not have a super-mode as he's bound to the 1500 HPS figure, which is divided in "Slowing HPS" and "Healing HPS". The Shaman's pure "Healing HPS" when facing a slow immune mob(thus "pure healing HPS as slowing isn't doing anything) must be inferior or equal to the Druid's, because the Druid does only dispose of pure "Healing HPS" and at the same time "Slowing HPS" + "Healing HPS" must again be inferior or equal the Druid's pure "Healing HPS" at best, in order to maintain the balance SoE intended.

Whenever a Shaman finds himself in the "second best" case, whenever he can not instantly or at will decided which mode is going to be needed, he will be falling behind the optimum, while the Druid will gain even more power through his healing stance that will further increase his HPS by 25%, widening the gap between Shaman and Druid healing even more.

This is why a Slow/Healing stance will not make us more powerful, it will merely shift the slider within the same function towards one end, the sub-function slowing, when mobs are highly vulnerable to it and the other end, the core function healing, when mobs are almost unaffected by slowing, but the magnitude of the total function [Healing] you are performing, will stay unaltered.

On top of this, a stance that allows you to pick between 2 or more core functions like the Druid one will do, will allow you to actually fill 2 roles to the full extent of a core-function. You basically turn into a specialist for both functions with zero penalties 99% of the times. Druids will basically gain a 25% healing AND damage power for the mechanics I made reference to at the beginning, which do not allow you to do 2 things at once anyway.

After this analysis(obviously subjective at this point, as everything I said is based on my personal view), I'd like to suggest a few ideas, that might give us an equal boost as the one in question for Druids.

1. Add a carbon copy of Nihil(have the decency to increase the resist adjust if you are going to give Beastlords a -30+ MR check) that autocasts a HoT on the target of the target.

2. Make the HoT scale with the level of slow mitigation. Set a default healing value for it reflecting a base mitigation(partially, I'd suggest )and multiply it with X for every level of mitigation, that is not the base, to be more or less powerful according to the effects of the slow.

3. The HoT needs to pulse like Bard songs rather than per tick, go into the bard buff box, stack with epic and other HoTs and last 1 minute. Furthermore, to compensate for the fact, that part of our healing is resistable(slow), the HoT would have to be autocast independently from the success of the slow, so that a resist on a slow does not result in a bigger penalty than it already is(hate generation ect.)

I have a lot of ideas regarding an alternate stance much like the Druid's nuke stance, but I feel this post is long enough already. However, it should, imo, allow us to boost a second core-function(just like the damage boost for Druids), if we wish to in trade of a healing penalty of some sort(I'd gladly post ideas on that to in a few) and I think since many of us here agree that Shamans lack the adequate survivability, I'd vote for a "signifcant(25%?) boost in the tanking department stance".
