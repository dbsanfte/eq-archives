If your P3-600 is a Socket 370, here's a mega-cooling fan for the CPU (make sure you got enough clearance above it, this thing is huge)

http://www.axiontech.com/cgi-local/prdtinfo.asp?prdt=ACVACCK6035D




If your P3-600 is a Slot-1, here's a cooler for that:

http://www.axiontech.com/cgi-local/prdtinfo.asp?prdt=ACAVOLGR

(This cooler has 2 power connections, one to your mainboard, one to a regular power connection like your hard driver or CD-Rom uses (I'm using this one on my P3-850 with no problems))



You might consider adding more case-fans... some cases have locations in them for adding extra fans - usually one in the front of the case below the hard drive/floppy drive and one in the back below the power supply. I have all fans in my pc and it NEVER gets hot in there.
EDIT: If you do this, you want the front fan pulling air in to the case, and the back (under power supply) fan blowing air out of the case. Air flow comes in the front of the case, and out the back.


Here's a generic fan:

http://www.axiontech.com/cgi-local/prdtinfo.asp?prdt=ACGNCASEFANB


I've been using this vendor for a couple years now, they're for real.




Edit 2: Years ago, my first pentium developed a 'thermal intermittant' problem like you describe, and one of the chips on the mainboard would overheat, causing lockups - I ran open-case with a fan blowing on the mainboard for a month or so, lessening lockups but in that case replacement was the only option. Haven't seen one that bad in the 7 years or so since, but it was worth a mention. I imagine in your case the fans will do the trick.
