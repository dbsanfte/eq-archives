Gloobonious
Alchemy has the potential to be an extremely beneficial tradeskill. Unfortunately, Verant obviously doesn't care enough to make it that way. I just want to get some ideas out of how to make it more useful to people, and more viable to make profit for the Shaman.

+Stats Potions: These stack with everything. Could follow a "Lesser/Greater" sort of evolution. IE: Lesser Potion of Vitality is +25 HP, and the Greater Potion of Vitality is +75 HP. A gaurenteed hot spot in alchemy if they ever put it live. In addition, this should work with all other statistics. Not too much per potion to prevent overpowering... but really, what tank could resist carrying around a 10-dose potion that adds 20 to STA, or 100 to HP... which stacks with everything?

Component-Free Utility Potions (Think the current ones require a component...): Levitate, Enduring Breath, Gate. Bind Affinity, and any others I forgot. IMHO, Bind Affinity could be one that requires a lot of extremely rare components and a maximized skill. Gaurenteed seller for tanks tired of running from cities. To sum it up, would revitalize the skill.

Improved Port Potions: Port to a lot of places besides the ones granted. Put in an extremely difficult potion that ports someone to the Nexus from anywhere... and there you go, a hot seller. More locations would not hurt either, besides the 3 or 4 destinations potions are currently limited to. Heck, make potions that drop you at any spire in Norrath!

Offensive Potions: Anyone remember UO? Throwing potions at people was always fun. And if that doesn't seem viable, there could be other offensive aid potions. The DS is good, but there should be more. There should be a potion for a combat proc of some sort, perhaps a potion that absorbs melee damage per-hit, a SpellShield style potion, etc. IE: "Potion of Flickering Flame" could give you a chance to proc a fire-based 50DD each hit.

Raising the skill cap for some of the newer potions would be good too (the clarity ones come to mind.) I would personally not open it up to cap at 250 until level 50, though... I've always enjoyed that only the highest-level Shamans can max Alchemy. Should keep it that way.

I would rather have a series of great potions that are difficult to make (a'la the new tradeskilling revamp), over the current selection of easy potions to make that simply don't do anything useful to anyone.

Mitrian
Well, be glad we're not rogues. Alchemy is a dream compared to a rogue's poison difficulties. (In my opinion.)

There's a couple general complaints I see about Alchemy.
1.) Too expensive for the effects.
2.) No demand for the effects.
3.) Much easier/cheaper to get the effects elsewhere.

Ideally, it would be nice to have Alchemy be a tradeskill that is both profitable AND highly effective. Unfortunately, both paths can be realized with precious few potions only (Blood of the Wolf, or Ant's Potion, for example). Overall, though, most shamans pick one or the other (profit vs. effective potions) to pursue as an Alchemist. One of my shaman friends absolutely LOVES alchemy as a profit machine. His skill is only about 80 I think, but he's made a ton of money with the skill just selling the profitable/in demand potions. (Personally I think it would be boring to make just sows, shrinks and ITU, but it's a viable option!)

If Verant was going to make it both a profitable and effective skill though, a combination of some of these suggestions might work:
a) Make more potion effects be based on the level of the drinker
b) Make components cheaper (at least closer to the value of the effect)
c) Add more of the components to mob loot tables
d) Increase durations to many of the effects
e) Do away with so many of the negative effects of potions
f) Get rid of the potion vendors (i.e. I hate that people can buy heal/invis potions but we can't make them!)
g) Or at least allow us to make those potions too!
h) Add damage potions for range/ammo slot (throw a DoT or DD!)
i) I'd love to see a snare or root potion, too, but I suppose I'm dreaming.

Or... just leave it the way it is. All other tradeskills have a lot of useless (or certainly not cost-effective) items produced that no one will ever buy ~ that is certainly not unique to Alchemy. The biggest difference is that there are high-level items in the other skills that have a market, whereas our high-level items no one will pay that much for (more than once anyway)! i.e. 1k for 10-dose Breeze!? Overall, the skill is a fun and useful skill once you figure it out. It's just costly to skill up with no real quantifiable reward for having a max skill.

Just my thoughts...

Shandy Orcbane
Stinging Wort potions are Balm Leaf (35-40p, gypsy vendors, and a vendor in SH) + Ice Giant Toes (off of--you guessed it--ice giants and Gornit).

The effect is "Calillimony" and is +17 Str, +300hp. It's instant-cast. The 300 hp buff is a max-hp buff and 300hp heal (like Altuna, etc) but you start losing the extra hp capacity shortly.

I've heard that it's a 300hp dot as well, but I've not looked that closely at it. Usually I've got Regrowth on, which far outstrips the couple of hp per tick that you might lose.

You can stack this on top of regen spells, but you cannot stack regen spells on top of this.

I use it in 5-shots (when I can afford it) as a security blanket when I'm off soloing. Nice having a 1500hp instant heal in your pocket.

OK heres how calimony works.... I know because I carry a 10 charge on every twink I play as well as my 60 shaman. They are absolutely the GREATEST get out of a crappy situation free card EVER! They are insta cast ( IE while you run you can cast this)!

One dose gives you 300 HPs on a DoT decline. This means that it raises your HP total 300 points (ex you have 2000 hps, you chug one you now have 2300 total Hps) Immediatly you will begin to loose the added HPS at a rate of 10 HPs per tick for 5 min (at the end of the 5 min you would be back to your original 2000 HPs)

The heal effect from Chugging a 5 dose comes in this way. You take the above 2000 HPs. You finish a fight at 500 HPs. You drink one dose this would raise your total Hps to 2300 and your actual health to 800. Taking another charge immediatly (before the dot decline takes effect) Will NOT raise your total HPs again but it WILL raise your health another 300 points bringing you to 2300 total but now you are at 1200 health (500 + 300 + 300). Now at this point the numbers would start to drop slightly because of the DoT decline but for the sake of the excersise we will freeze time. Chugging another potion would take you to 1500 health again with your total HPs staying at 2300.

The heal comes into effect because the DoT decline ONLY effects the extra 300 hps on the total, regardless of your health.

Chugging a 5 charge would give 1500 HPs (also adding 300 to your total available HPS that would be on a dot decline)

SOOOoooooo your 2000 HP guy. Hes at 200 health chugs a 5 charge hes now at 1700 health with an added 300 HPs (2300 total) that will drop at 10 HPs a tick till he comes back to 2000 HPs and the effect drops. The healed HPs are NOT effected unless hes actually healed to the full 2300 then you will see hte total HPs drop slowly but hes not damaged, hes just loosing the total possible.

Theres How Calimony works :)
