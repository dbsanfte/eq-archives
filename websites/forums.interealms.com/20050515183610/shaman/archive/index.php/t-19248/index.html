After all that theoretical stuff I came up with I actually sat down and did some real number crunching.

The adjusted spell cost concept
To make my calculations easier I will be using something I call 'adjusted spell cost'. It is the regular spell cost plus the amount of mana lost by casting (instead of medding or cannibalizing). This will enable me to use the figures for full-time medding or cannibalizing instead.

The scenario
The scenario is as follows: a LDoN hard mission. It is completed in an hour. Fifty MOBs are killed, each of them in 50 seconds. then 10 seconds are spent pulling the next MOB. MOB resists rate for
normal spells is 10%. Resist rate for -30 resist adjustment spells is 5%. Resist rate for -100 resist adjustment spells is 0%. The MOBs mitigate slow by 25%.
I will be calculating what kind of damage the priest classes can heal and how much they can dish out.

The characters
The characters are lvl 65 with 35 AAs. They each have Mana Preservation IV, Spell Haste IV and FT4.

The Shaman
AA skills are: Any 6 in general, Mental Clarity 3, Spellcasting Mastery 3, Cannibalisation

The Druid
AA skills are: Any 6 in general, Mental Clarity 3, Spellcasting Mastery 3, Spirit of the Wood 1

The Cleric
AA skills are: Any 6 in general, Mental Clarity 3, Spellcasting Mastery 3

Mana Regeneration
VoQ is obtained before entering the dungeon. Together with self buffs and emptying the mana pool over in an hour this gives us the following possible regeneration:
Shaman: 130 per tick(includes cannibalizing)
Cleric: 66 per tick
Druid: 70 per tick

The mana loss per second spent casting is:
Shaman: 15.59 (cannibalize mana)
Cleric: 3.67 (medding mana)
Druid: 3.67 (medding mana)

Calculations
Shaman healing
Slow is casted as soon as the MOB arrives, if slow is resisted it is recast as soon as possible. Multiple resists do not happen. This gives us two possibilities, slow sticks on the first try or it takes two casts for slow to stick.
If it sticks on the first try (90%) it will land after 4.95 seconds, giving us 55.05 seconds to heal or generate mana.
The adjusted slow cost(see above) is 250(base) * 0.75 (SCM3, Mana preservation, Specialisation) + 4.95 (cast time, recast time) * 15.59 (mana not cannibalized) = 265.
After a resist(10%) the second slow is landed after 10.4 seconds, giving us 49.6 seconds for healing or mana generation. The adjusted slow cost is 537.
With the shaman being able to generate 130 mana per tick this gives us an average of 1008 (0.9 * 1035 + 0.1 * 766) mana per MOB for healing. The MOB will be slowed, on average, by 49.4%, mainly due to slow mitigation reducing the slow percentage from 75% to 56%.
The adjusted mana cost for Kragg's Mending is 511. Kragg's heals 1950, or 3854 if you factor in slow. Since we have mana to cast 1.97 Kragg's per fight this will heal a total of 7592 damage per MOB. With a fight time of 50 seconds this means shaman healing will keep up with up to 152 melee DPS.

Shaman damage
A fight time of 50 seconds is just barely enough for Blood of Saryrn to run the full time, thus one will be used per MOB. The rest of the mana will be spent nuking.
Starting at a theoretical 1300 mana per MOB and substracting the adjusted mana cost for Blood of Saryrn (572) leaves us 728 mana for Velium Strikes. Thus far we have done 2240 * 0.9(resists) = 2016 damage.
Velium Strike does an adjusted 833 damage for 400 mana. 728 mana will let us cast Velium Strike 1.82 times per MOB, adding another 1516 damage.
Total damage per MOB is 3532, which means a DPS of 58.9, or 70.6 if you count the fight time only.

Druid Healing
A druid will regenerate 700 mana per MOB. The druid semi-complete heal has an adjusted mana cost of 517 and heals 4680. Since a druid will be able to cast 1.35 heals per MOB he will heal a total of 6318 damage per MOB, which negates up to 127 DPS.

Druid Damage
The druid will be casting his big DoT, Swarming Death once per MOB. The rest of the mana will be spent nuking with Winter's Frost. Note that Swarming Death has a -100 resist adjustment. It will deal 1440 damage for an adjusted cost of 326 mana, leaving 374 mana to nuke with.
Winter's Frost does 1395 damage after resists and costs 361 mana to cast. 1.04 casts per MOB add an additional 1451 damage for a total of 2891 damage per MOB, or 48 DPS (58 DPS actual combat time).

Cleric Healing
A cleric's mana regeneration gives us 660 mana per MOB. The adjusted cost for a Comlete Heal is 357. Assuming Complete Heal can land for 6000 a cleric can thus heal 11092 damage per MOB. With a fight time of 50 seconds that means a cleric can keep up with up to 222 DPS.

Cleric Damage
The cleric will be nuking with Condemnation. I will also make a separate calculation for Destroy Undead. Condemnation has an adjusted mana cost of 339 and an average damage of 990. 660 mana per MOB means a cleric will cast condemnation 1.95 times for a total damage of 1931 and a DPS of 32 (39 DPS fight time only).
Destroy Undead is considerably more efficient, doing 47 DPS instead ( 56 DPS fight time only).

Now for some realism
Now I will factor in some movement - fight time is still 50 seconds but the 10 seconds are not spent pulling but moving. The shaman will have 156 less mana available per MOB, the druid and cleric both loose 37 mana. I will also add in Spirit of the Wood. This brings our numbers to:
Shaman Healing: 129
Shaman Damage: 64
Druid Healing: 127 (123 if Spirit of the Wood can only be used on the tank)
Druid Damage: 55
Cleric Healing: 209
Cleric Damage: 36 (53 on Undead)
(All numbers represent DPS and reflect 50 second fights)

The fun part
Now, what happens if you have resistant MOBs? Obviously shaman healing will suffer, druid and cleric healing will be unaffected and everyones DPS will go down.
For the following numbers I assume a resist rate of 50% on all spells without resist adjustments. Malos will bring normal spells to a 10% resist rate. Hand of Ro will lower resist on fire spells to 5%. -100 resist adjustment spells will land for full without debuffs. Druids will switch to Summer's Flame so they can use Hand of Ro to debuff.
I will ignore the fact that the fight time is too short to malos and still have Blood of Saryrn run fully. I will also ignore that everyone will be best of debuffing one MOB and the chainnuking and skipping nuking on the next one.
Shaman Healing: 51
Shaman Damage: 47
Druid Healing: 127 (123 if Spirit of the Wood can only be used on the tank)
Druid Damage: 44
Cleric Healing: 209
Cleric Damage: 18 (53 on Undead)
(All numbers represent DPS and reflect 50 second fights)

Stuff I ignored
The shaman will often have single seconds left and nothing to do except hoping to catch a med tick.
The value of Cannibalisation increases and the value of Cannibalize IV decreases as total time spent casting
increases.
Pet damage, including hammer damage and charm pet damage.
Cleric melee damage.
Druid DS damage (might add that later)

Comments, Conclusions
I was amazed at how much difference the debuffing and moving made. Looking at my numbers I can now understand how easy it is for other classes to say shaman are fine or even overpowered. It takes the concepts of adjusted spell mana cost
and mana lost by not actively cannibalizing to understand why there are problems at all. It also explains why shaman do not notice anything wrong until reaching this level and equipment.
I think this is alarming because the trend of high mana regeneration, high MOB damage, slow mitigation, MOB resistance and time limits, coupled with the overpriced shaman spells will, if it continues, turn what today is a moderate problem for well-equipped lvl 65 shaman into a major problem for every 60+ shaman.

I would love to hear your take on this. Feel free to ask questions or point out errors I made. This is rather complex and I am not 100% sure I did everything right.

During Ldon’s I never get back to full manna after summoning dog dog, and am consistently below 20% manna by the time we’re near the end. I am constantly canniing, so much so that I sometimes fall behind when the group is OTM, as I wait the extra 2 seconds rather than interrupt the canni (or worse am snared by my own dumb stoic). If I’m not casting malo, slowing, or spot healing, I’m canniing. Still the pace is such that there is just never enough time for me to canni to full after I’ve got the mob slowed.

I’ve found it very frustrating that often the other players in my group seem to assume that I will always be full manna because of canni, and if I declare that I’m lom if I just “canni up” I’ll be good , but I quite literally never get enough time to “canni up”, as there is never a brake to the pulling unless the cleric warns he’s lom. For some reason most other classes don’t realize just how much time it takes me to malo, slow, and spot heal, and I can’t do that and canni at the same time.

If I am in a static camp and don’t have to malo, my shaman is uber. If I have to malo often and move, I’m always lom, and usually below 70% health because there is just not enough time for me to debuff, slow, canni, and heal my self, and not skip the next pull.

It seems obvious to me that since shaman spells are inefficient compared to other priest spells with the notion that canni makes up for it, if you put a shaman in a place where it is hard for him to canni (on the move a lot, little to no down time), and then add any other stress (like the extra manna cost and lost time spend on debuffing), the shaman is going to have a hard time keeping up.

A fast paced group is exponentially harder for a shaman to keep up with than other classes. I suspect this is why apparently some shaman think that the class is still the UBER class it of the past, while other players, perhaps in environments with more time stress, feel that shaman are falling behind. If I sit in a static camp where I rarely need to debuff, I have time to canni, and am usually full FM. If I am OTM a lot, it is harder for me to regen the manna, but the inefficiencies in my spells cost are still present, so I am penalized with less compensation from canni to make up for it.
