Ungkor
I two box my shaman and necro from time to time. Honestly, two boxing complex casting classes is A) very hard and B) even harder to do well.

I really feel like I'm only playing half a shaman and half a necro when I two box them. Whereas, when I main play a melee, and box the shaman, I feel like I'm getting 100% of the melee potential, and about 75% of the shaman.

Honestly, I think you'd be better off with a paladin over a another casting class, and then setup a series of keys to assist the pally with your shaman.

Between the shaman and pally, you should have enough heal power to get through most tough situations. And you'll get rez from the pally when things get really tough:)

If you decide to go with the mage, it will limit the content you can fight for sure. As mobs get tougher and tougher, you'll bascially be forced to adopt 2 play styles. Aggro kiting or root rotting.

Until you get Q, your heals won't keep the pets alive at all. Without a tank, you'll need to let the necro run the mob while the mage and necro pets bite it. That means that slow is pretty much pointless for the majority of your fights. In which case, why even play a shaman?

In additon, you need room to kite. That will limit where you can hunt. Dungeons are pretty much out. As are places like BoT for the most part. And while you can do it, fighting any mobs that summon willl be a pain.

Basically, while boring, if your goal is to form a functional group of your own, you need to make some sort of holy trinity. Tank, healer, CC. Any tank, shaman, necro, can perform those roles ok.

Any combination is possible but like others have mentioned, two casters combination may not be the easiest partnership. Both enchanter and a shaman come into power later in their careers. At lower levels a shaman can act as a melee but is soon is made obsolete (compared with the damage a good melee can make). With an enchanter you have to evolve a strategy that is mostly defensive but if you have enough charisma you can attempt to charm pets (and there are many stategies for using pets including carefully keeping charmed mob slightly more healthy than the non charmed one , and then break charm when the fight is over and kill the other mob for experience) There are definite advantages like free clarity, kei, etc,mezes and stuns but if the enchanter gains to much agro, there is not tank there that can taunt the mob away. You may have to be selective about the mobs you go after. For instance since you don't have a tank, you don't want a mob that is MR and hits hard.

With a necro it is easier to partner with a shaman since a decent necro by itself can solo pretty easily (with fear and "snare") Their pets are pretty good and some of their higher lvl spells are nice for "healing" and giving mana to needy casters. Also they can summon dead and if you are willing to pay for the essense gems, they can do a rez in a pinch. If you end up playing with a good necro, most times you will slow the mob, and heal his pet from time to time. They also have their own version of canibalize.

For tanks, I think the best ones are SK. They hold agro very well even if you manage to DOT the mob alot. They also can FD which makes them pretty decent at pulliing.
