Dexterity increases your chances of a skill gain in your offensive skills, including weapons.

Agility increases your chances of a skill gain in defense.

You may not notice it too much much at lower levels, because low level slows aren't particularly aggrolicious, but poor defensive skills are bad news. First, they rise very slowly -- much more slowly than weapons and offense does. Second, your slow at 51 suddenly creates much more aggro than previous slows did, and monsters also hit for a lot, and double and triple attack and bash or slam all at once more, too. So if you fall behind in your defense, it will be time-consuming to raise, and risky as you level higher, since mobs hit for more, and you really NEED that defense skill.

Defense skill raises at 5 per level to a max of 200, and each point is more or less 2.5 AC free, or 12.5 AC per level. That's like suddenly upgrading a piece of your equipment to uber status. Adds up REAL fast, but so does not having it.

At 37, I was only a couple dozen points behind, but it took me 8 solid levels of strictly solo work, meleeing like mad, to finally max out to 200 at level 45. Some rare people may get lucky and get quick increases, but sometimes you can get NO increases in a whole level. Most people's experience are probably a lot more like mine -- slow and frustrating -- than like those of the lucky among us.

If you're duoing, don't make the common mistake of letting the shaman just be a buff monkey for your monk. He needs to develop fully as his own character, too, or he will be extremely weak when he gets older. Let him fight a LOT, so he maxes defense and at least one weapon skill(preferably all of them). If that means holding back on finishing a kill off with your monk sometimes, so be it.

I'd suggest using your second highest slow sometimes, too, so mobs are slowed enough to be safe for you guys, but not so slow that you handicap your progress in gaining defense skills.
