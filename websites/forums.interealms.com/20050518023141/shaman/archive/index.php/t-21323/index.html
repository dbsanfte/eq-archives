BlargX
I wanted to be a shaman for group desireability, ability to heal, and ability to take a punch and melee a bit. I heard they could solo a bit, and that helped; mostly I wanted group desireability, but I liked the idea that I could solo if I wanted to.

I came to EQ after playing and loving Diablo. Moving back and forth to town was painful in that game, as you had to go through level after level, but you had to do it, to cash in your loot. I played a couple classes there, but wound up really loving the wizard -- not only for his power, but for his mobility. He could hop across rivers, get back and forth to town quick, jump in and out of obstacles to mess up foes -- all great stuff. So when I came to EQ, I wanted a toon who could port and such. Druid could, and everybody was raving how SOW was the biggest spell in the game and he got that too -- a mobility spell. I wanted to be a mobile toon, sniper style, and the fact that you couldn't really get hit only added to the challenge and fun.

But then I found that most people hated druids because they wanted ports everywhere, all night, all the time. You could literally port strangers all night and not get a word of thanks because everyone felt you owed that to strangers, and the first guy you said no to was a new guy who hated you for life. It's hard to believe, but there was actually a whole CLASS that as a whole used to be hated in EQ.

Myself, I concentrated on healing and had a very good rep. But that did little good if you didn't find your set of friends on the next time you logged in. Eventually EQ was overrun with druids due to their being hyped up as an incredibly fantastic class, but everyone hated them too and how many druids did one group realistically need, if they needed one at all? EQ was stacked a mile high in druids LFG and the attitude toward them was terrible.

Playing them, it was assumed they were invulnerable and they didn't need heals or tanks taunting off them. "Just run, you have SOW!," came the refrain if you asked for some help or pointed out that dying was no fun. Now, SOW didn't work indoors, and it's not that much good in a small space anyway, but no matter. In the same way, you weren't often healed because people figured you would escape easily, and anyway were a healer and could just heal yourself. Thing was, druids were pitifully weak in melee back then. You would crumple like a pure caster if hit, but your aggro was harder to manage, because you HAD to heal and snare, unlike say a wizzy, who can pick and choose when to blast.

So I found out about a class almost nobody was playing, shamans, and that they could both heal, which I liked doing, and take a hit. Perfect! After coming from a class where there were hundreds a day pointlessly LFG and then logging off, there were hardly any shamans. Yet they still contributed heals, and buffs, and even slows, which was pretty exotic. A lot of people didn't really have much experience with shamans and didn't hold them in high esteem, but once they found you were a halfway decent one, suddenly they were all over you for groups every day.

That's the other thing. Practically from the moment I logged on my shaman for the first time, I WAS getting tells from knowledgeable strangers asking to join their groups. What a thrill! A shaman nobody ever heard of getting asked over and over all night to come group compared to an excellent druid who had proved his worth over and over again getting almost no chance to group at all? Man, it was too great to believe!

Actually, until slows became so heavily mitigated that even I would have to recommend taking a beastlord or a chanter instead of a shaman most anywhere, that's how it stayed, too. Plenty of offers to guild and group. And luckily, the number of shamans stayed low enough that in many matters I could pick and choose where I wanted to go and when.

The number of shamans completely exploded on my server once Ydelma got soloed and it was broadcast on every class board. From being either the only 60 ogre shaman around or one of at most two or three, and one of the very few 60 shamans around period, suddenly shamans were everywhere. Ogre shamans even, who used to be the rarest by far. (Worse yet, tons of them started using variations of my name -- there were Blargg's and Blargh's and Bleargh's everywhere, and that name was my own creation. I can hardly go anywhere online without seeing some variation on that name now.)

Anyway, shaman turned out to be a perfect choice for me. Heavy group desireability for most of his lifespan and absolute uselessness for a small part of it(PoP trials back when they were new, and now GoD and most everywhere else since people have realized how little slow means when it's mitigated). On the whole, though, despite the tedium of canni making shamans a somewhat unsociable class, I found most of my hopes for shamans realized for the majority of my playtime.
