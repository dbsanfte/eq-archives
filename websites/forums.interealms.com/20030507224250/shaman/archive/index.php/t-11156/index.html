Galamor
A lot of people will gate when aggro'd by this guy, but IMO, I found it best to just take him out and save having to gate everytime he paths thru. This is how I handle this guy. Keep in mind – my gear is not uber whatsoever. I do have my epic, but I lack Torpor, Pox, Avatar, VoB. My buffed AC is just over 900 and hit-points just over 2200. However, I do make sure I get KEI in the bazaar before hunting in the PoG.
1st cast is Annul Magic to drop his damage shields. The next thing I cast is Malo and sick my pet (True Spirit d/w Gnoll Hides or Velium weapons). Soon as pet is on the treant I cast turgurs. Now the trick I found is to not to dot with anything other than epic dot and keep him rooted until close to the end. Let the pet do most of the work keeping it healed. When the Treant is at half health, I reapply Turgurs. When the treant reaches 1.5 bubs of health I reapply epic, then stick BoK, EB, and Plague. Now this is the tricky part - after I have stuck these spells - I do the mad sit and spell swap changing out Plague and EB for Spear of Torment and Torrent of Poison. The Treant is gonna gate when low so I am ready to gate if he gets it off. As soon as I think he is close to gating - I cast Torrent of Poison and when spells refresh I immediately cast SoT. After this, it is a bit of luck, but I start chain casting SoT (fast refresh and fast casting time) until he gives up trying to gate and starts his death walk. The Treant has never dropped anything for me, but it is worth getting him out of the way.

Spell Slots

1. Kragg’s
2. Malo
3. Turgurs
4. Bane of Knife
5. Envemoned Bolt – swap spot for Torrent of Poison
6. Cannabilize - swap for Plague – swap spot for Spear of Torment

Galamor
The reason I load up on the dot's at the end is to provide more damage per tick when I start to chain cast SoT. With Epic, BoK, Plague, and EB all stacked - you adding quite a bit of sustained damage. The key is to get the Treant past the point where he is going to gate and starts his death walk. It is also important in case he gets gate off - the Treant will spend most of its mana trying to cast dots on your pet during the fight and will eventually be oom and stop casting till he is ready to gate. If he gets gate off, there is a chance he will die from the dots, but more importantly, it will buy you time to gate out or if you are brave, mem up summon companion and let the Treant summon you. I have never fought him with the 55 pet, but I don't think it would be any different. Its a slow fight and so far root seems to hold like super glue on it compared to the other mobs so you can med through most the fight keeping the pet healed. BTW - I was wrong about the loot - killed him the other day and he dropped Wizard Legs.
