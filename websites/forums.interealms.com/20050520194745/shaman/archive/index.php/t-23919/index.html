3) Blizzard makes a lot more money than SoE. I don't have any numbers to back this up, but go look at the shareholder info for both companies if you don't believe me. That being said, the two companies are almost polar opposites when it comes to dealing with their customers. In SWG after a credit duping exploit was discovered, SoE banned anyone who had received any duped credits. This ended up being a lot of people, most of whom had no idea they'd gotten duped credits, and so all the friends of those people got together to protest. In response, SoE began teleporting people all over the galaxy, including up in to space (which, being the next expansion, doesn't actually exist yet, though this didn't stop SoE from sending people there). Penny Arcade did a great comic on it a few weeks ago, which I linked to in a post here (and am too lazy to re-link).

In contrast, when Blizzard discovered that some people were *gasp* pirating Starcraft, it began keeping a record of any cdkey which was logged in twice to Battle.net. It hadn't done anything to anyone yet except keep this log, but because it did it secretly and someone found out, there was a big stink. Rather than ban anyone on the list and preventing multiple CD keys from being logged in at the same time, Blizzard apoligzed for not being up front with their customers, and declared that they WOULD NOT CHECK CD-KEYS FOR STARCRAFT ON BATTLE.NET EVER! Now of course they did learn from their mistake, and it is impossible to log on to Battle.net with the same Warcraft 3 CD key now for instance. But the point is that Blizzard cares about it's customer rep, and SoE doesn't.

Yes this is just differing business strategies adopted by two for-profit companies, and it doesn't make one inherently good and the other inherently evil. But you can guess which company I'd rather have running my MMoG. In fact, if Blizzard ever bought out SoE and took over EQ, I'd probably orgasm after hearing it. Twice. But since that's not going to happen, I guess WoW is the next best thing.
