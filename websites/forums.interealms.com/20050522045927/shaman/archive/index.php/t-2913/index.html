Class Comparison:

How do my abilities stack up to other classes:

Buffs

Ability scores: At all points in our career, our str/sta/dex/agi buffs grant the largest ability increases. Druids have a line of str spells which occasionaly peak ours, but we stay consitantly above them overall. Enchanters have a line of charisma buffs which grants slightly less of am ability bonus.

Our "talisman" hp line does not grant the most hp's, but it stacks with all other hp buffs except cleric's self only hp/ac line and int casters self only mr line. Our ac buffs are largely overshadowed both in their effect (by cleric's ac buffs) and their mana cost (by enchanter's ac buffs).

Our resistance line begins smilar to clerics and druids. Eventually though we get group poison and disease resistance buffs that grant a larger ability increase than any others.

Our haste spells are far less powerful than enchanters, and we receieve them much later on. However haste spells are a rarity among classes, and should still be valued.

We share a regeneration line with druids. Generaly speeking, we get a specific regeneration spell before druids, but they can cast group versions of the spells. Eventually we can cast group regrowth.

We have other utility buffs. We share levitation effects with many different classes. We and enhanters get vision spells, necromancers get some limited vision effects. Shaman get spirit of the wolf before druids, both get group sow but ours has +20 to attack related with it.


Debuffs

Slows: Shaman, enchanters, bards, and necromancers can all slow monsters. Also, various melee weapons contain slow "procs". Our slows are head and shoulders above the rest though. We get them sooner, and our highest level slow (Tugurs Insects) has a lower casting time and resistance rate than any other slow. Enchanters slows come in second best, and necromancer slows are limited to undead. We also have a special disease resistance checking slow spell. Slows do not stack.

Malisament line: We share a milaisment line of resistance debuffs with magicians. We consistantly recieve the spells earlier, and thus stay ahead of the game. When they finally catch up, our level 60 malo spell is superior to their mala spell. Druids and enchanters also have specific resistance debuffs, but they stack with ours.

Cripple: We share cripple directly with enchanters, with no advantages or disadvantages.

Dot's: Our disease and poison dots stack with every single other dot except and exact copy (same name). They are considered to be among the best in the game.

Pets: We have one of the worst pets in the game. Necro/magi/beast are far better. Enchanter pet will destroy ours one on one, but because enchanter pets are impossiable to control, we are considered to have the advantage. Our pet is beter than druid, wizard, and cleric pets but that doesnt say much.

Other notes on spells: We have no snare, and no stun (both of wich are common among other classes). Our root line is nothing unique. Our nukes are slow casting, and soon overshadowed not only by other classes, but by our own dots. We recieve invisiability far later than any other caster (29) and do not get invis v undead (though we can make a potion of ivu) or improved invis. We have one kind of illusion, with ability and size adjustments but no faction change (bear from line).



AC CAPS

Though nothing have been proven, general theory states that ac provides the most noticable changes when it breaks a 100's marks (900, 1000, 1100). Example, you would notice a far greater difference going from 890 ac to 900 ac than you would going from 1010 to 1020. Some people believe there is also a noticable change at each 50's mark.

A generaly accepted theory is that after 1250 ac, we see relativly little improvemnt for each point of ac we increase.

Some other questions that I see coming up here often that aren't addressed in the FAQ:

Why is it important to keep my Defense skill maxed? How do I catch up?

Keeping your Defense skill maxed is very important to a shaman. You can earn 5 points of defense skill per level. The skill for shaman is capped at 200, so at level 40 you will be done earning defense points. Defense skill points give us free, innate AC. As we know, good AC keeps us from getting hit, or losing lots of hitpoints when we are hit. To raise Defense, simply get hit by blue conning monsters.

I group all the time and I never get hit, what can I do to raise defense?

Well, there are lots of ways to raise defense. You can try the melee soloing method described in this FAQ and raise it as you get exp. You can root the mob and stand close to take hits while you are getting exp in a group. Others also suggest asking a necromancer or enchanter to let you duel their pet in an arena and using that method to gain skill points. The pet must be blue and this method requires 2 people to be standing in the arena rather than out getting exp, but is the safest.

What weapon skills should I keep up?

All of them! Our epic is a piercing weapon, so I advise keeping at least piercing at a functional level. I would recommend keeping at least piercing and 2 hand blunt skills up as you level. Piercing so you can still melee and use a shield and 2 hand blunt so you can still get in some good hits.

Shaman and Race Selection

Okay, you’ve decided to join the ranks of the great and become a shaman! Good call! The next question is . . . how do I select a race?
There is no short easy answer to this question for most people, as there are a number of factors to look at. Each of the races have benefits and disadvantages in addition to the base starting attributes (listed below). I’ll try to go over each race separately after I take a moment to discuss the attributes. The following table shows the races that are capable of becoming a shaman, and their base attributes prior to the allocation of the 30 bonus points available to every character.

RACE STR STA AGI DEX WIS INT CHA BONUS
Barbarian 103 100 82 70 80 60 60 30
Troll 108 114 83 75 70 52 42 30
Ogre 130 127 70 70 77 60 42 30
Iksar 70 75 90 85 90 75 60 30
Vah Shir 90 80 90 70 80 65 70 30

There is a lot of debate on the most important attributes (commonly called stats which is short for statistics) for the shaman. Shaman, while having the ability to fight, are primarily a casting class. Thus, they are dependent on mana (spell points). Shaman, like clerics, druids, and paladins are deemed to be members of the Priest class of casters. Therefore, the determination of their total mana is based on the Wisdom stat. So, should you put all your bonus points into wisdom? Probably not. More on that shortly. Let’s look at the attributes:

Strength is sort of an obvious attribute. It has the expected effects. Stronger characters can hit a bit harder. They can wear more and heavier armor. They can carry more items without being encumbered (moving more slowly). For those of you interested in tradeskills, strength plays a part in your ability to succeed in blacksmithing (Note: it increases your chance of a successful manufacture of an item—only wisdom or intelligence are used in the speed (how many tries) it takes to learn the skill).
Stamina is probably as important as wisdom, and possibly more so. Stamina determines a number of items. First and foremost, it helps determine the number of hitpoints (how much of a beating you can take before you die) that your character will have. It also helps determine how long you can swing a weapon before you get tired, or how long you can swim or jump before you wear yourself out.
Agility is more of a passive attribute. It helps you dodge attacks, and speeds the learning of your defensive skills. Higher agility will also increase your armor class a bit.
Dexterity allows you to learn to hit monsters with your weapons faster (offensive skills). In your travels, you will also probably come across weapons with special effects. For instance, it might be a club that has a small chance of casting a spell every chance it hits. Dexterity affect the chance of the effect going off (called a ‘proc’ by most players). Higher dexterity will see a lot more procs.
Wisdom is the base statistic by which mana for priest classes is calculated. The higher of wisdom or intelligence is also used to determine the speed at which you learn some skills, mainly languages, tradeskills, spell related skills, etc.
Intelligence is the base statistic by which mana for intelligence casters is calculated. The higher of wisdom or intelligence is also used to determine the speed at which you learn some skills, mainly languages, tradeskills, spell related skills, etc.
Charisma allows you to buy and sell for less money to the various vendors across Everquest. Charisma also gives Bards and Enchanters a better chance of landing and holding certain lines of charm and mesmerize spells.

Okay, so what is important to a shaman? The short answer is everything but charisma and intelligence. Any bonus points in those 2 attributes are largely wasted. The distribution of the rest is a little harder to quantify and needs some more explanation.
Starting at level 24, shaman get a new spell called Cannibalize. The Cannibalize line of spells allows you to turn hit points into mana. So, for a shaman, to some extent, hit points are part of your mana pool. Obviously, this is a mixed blessing. It isn’t an efficient process: you will always lose more hit points than you will gain mana points. However, you have the ability to replenish your mana pool more quickly than any of the other classes. For instance, when you sit down and meditate, you regain mana and heal your hit points a little bit every six seconds (6 seconds = 1 tic). The amount of mana you get is dependent on you skill at meditation. The amount of hit points you get each tic is dependent on your level (and race, to some extent as some races get regeneration bonuses). For all practical purposes, the cannibalize spell lets you use your meditation skill AND your natural healing to regenerate mana. Most classes can only meditate and can’t come close to matching the mana replenishment of a good shaman.
Since you have monsters hitting on you, and may also need mana during a battle, you should probably find a balance between allocating your bonus points between stamina for maximizing hit points and wisdom for having as large a mana pool as possible.
A few other facts regarding point distribution:
Agility under 75 creates a negative armor class penalty. Armor class should NOT be neglected in a shaman. It affects not only how often you get hit, but also how much damage you take each hit. Remember, hit points are like mana for you.
Strength can affect your equipment choices at early stages, and also affect your ability to do melee damage to mobs. You will be doing a lot more melee at earlier levels. Depending on your personal play style this may decrease as you level up.
Based on all the above information, I’d recommend the following distributions of the 30 bonus points:
· Barbarian: Add 10-15 to Wisdom, and the remainder to Stamina.
· Troll: Add 15-20 to Wisdom, and the remainder to Stamina.
· Ogre: Add 10-15 to Wisdom, 5 to Agility, and the remainder to Stamina.
· Iksar: Add 10 to Wisdom, 5 to Strength, and the remainder to Stamina.
· Vah Shir: Add 10-15 to Wisdom, and the remainder to Stamina.
Okay, so now you know the starting attributes of the races. Why doesn’t everyone pick an ogre? After all, the sum of their strength, stamina, and wisdom is the highest, right? Well, yes it is, but there are additional benefits and disadvantages to each race as I mentioned earlier. First, there is faction. Only Barbarians and Vah Shir are ‘good’ races. The others are evil races. As a very general rule (until you learn the ins and outs of the game), young evil shaman can only bank at their home cities. In most of the other city areas for the lower level zones, evil races will be kill on sight (KoS) to the guards and most of the non-player-characters (NPCs) in the city. This becomes much less of an issue as you rise in levels. Pretty much all the races can find a place to bank on the continent of Velious (levels 30+), and on the moon if you have those expansions. Good characters can bank in pretty much any ‘good’ city. Evil characters should expect to only bank in their own city for about the first 25 levels or so.
All of the large races in Everquest have the ability to ‘slam.’ Slamming an opponent has a small chance to interrupt them as they are casting a spell on you. Large races include Barbarians, Ogres, and Trolls.
Now to the racial specific stuff:
· Barbarian: Barbarians have to get 105% of the base experience to level. Thus, they have a 5% experience penalty. They have the slam skill, and they are a good race. Barbarians do no have any innate night vision (ie they are effectively night blind). Barbarians have no real restrictions on equipment.
· Troll: Trolls have to get 120% of the base experience to level. Thus, they have a 20% experience penalty. The reason for this penalty is largely the ability to regenerate. Trolls get a innate skill that allows them to regenerate hit points significantly faster than most other races. Like Barbarians and Ogres, Trolls also get the slam ability. Trolls are an evil race, so have some banking restrictions. Trolls have innate night vision. Trolls have a very low fire resistance.
· Ogre: Ogres have to get 115% of the base experience to level. Thus, they have a 15% experience penalty. Ogres are an evil race and thus have the evil race banking restriction. They have the slam ability. They have innate night vision. Ogres also get frontal stun immunity. All monsters have the ability to bash/kick/slam players, and each successful hit has a chance of stunning the character for a few seconds. The stun will interrupt spell casting, fighting, or movement (while the mobs continue to beat on you). Ogres are immune to stun from the front (they can still be stunned by monsters behind them).
· Iksar: Iksar have to get 120% of the base experience to level. Thus they have a 20% experience penalty. Iksar have the same regeneration ability as trolls. Iksar get an innate armor class bonus that is dependent on the defensive skill. It has a maximum bonus of 40 AC points. Iksar have limited foraging abilities (maximum ability of 50). Iksar begin with 100 swimming skill (out of a possible 200) at level 1. Iksar are evil and have the requisite banking restrictions. Iksar cannot wear plate armor. Iksar cannot slam.
· Vah Shir: Vah Shir (or “Cats”) do not have any experience penalty. Cats get the safe fall skill of 100 at level 1. Cats have night vision. Cats consume food and drink at a much higher level than the other races. Cats are a good race and have fewer banking restrictions than the evil races. Cats cannot slam.

Okay, so NOW are we ready to choose a race? Almost! Let’s look at religion and equipment for a moment.
Religion, traditionally, has not played an important part in Everquest. Choosing a god can help and hurt both, in that people of opposing religions (or factions) may try to kill you on sight, or might give you certain quests.
Recent changes to the game (approximately May of 2002) have added some new twists. There are certain type of manufactured armor that is very nice for members of certain religions, but may not be available to agnostic characters. Additional changes may accrue.
That being said, if you choose a Troll you should ALWAYS choose Innoruk as your god. There is a specific quest for Troll Innoruk shaman that results in a necklace that casts snare—an spell not otherwise available to shaman. You’ll learn more about that as you get into the game!
There is one other piece of equipment I need to mention. The Jaundiced Bone Bracer, or JBB. Viewed by many as one of the two most powerful pieces of shaman equipment, it has a mouse-clickable effect that is a mana free spell that can do up to 263 damage to monsters. While it has a long cast time, it can aid progression through levels 45-59 substantially. It cannot be used by anyone under level 45 (thought they can still wear it as armor). Iksar cannot use the JBB as it is tagged as plate armor. JBB are difficult and very expensive to come by, but you should be aware of the race restriction prior to making your character. It is very time consuming to decide to start over and change characters if you do it at level 45. I urge you to get on the web and research the JBB before making a decision. It is a powerful item, but the iksar have some significant racial advantages, as listed above.
Okay, that’s about it. Those are the pros and cons of the races. Choose as you will! Shaman become a true powerhouse at level 60. Cats and Barbarians get there faster. Ogres, Iksar, and Trolls take longer, but have some powerful racial abilities the others lack. The various races will have an easier/harder time at various levels due to the balance of their statistics, but are fairly well balanced overall.
Good luck!
