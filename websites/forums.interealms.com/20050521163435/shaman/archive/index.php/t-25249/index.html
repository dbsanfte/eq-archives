Oh wow, just some of the responses have brought back good memories of games that you do not see or hear about anymore.

My favorite? The only thing that comes to mind to this question is this response: "I can only have one?" :)

Here are some off the top of my head, though they all may have been mentioned already.

1) Master of Orion 2: ...I still play this. For me, being able to design ships, new maps with every game, more than one way to win space battles, and very cool music. (I have actually sat and just listend to the music of the game... something about it makes me daydream. This for me is very cool.) Any game with lots of options for the players to choose from (and hence increases replayablility) is always a good sign.

2) The Steel Panthers series (I'm a wargamer at heart). Again, so many options to choose from. You can make up your own unit of tanks, infantry, artillery, etc. and go through senerios as the comander. Being able to move individual tanks (In 1 and 2) and trying to flank an enemy, or stop him cold from a head on assult gives my mind something to chew on. Then again, being able to plaster enemy positions with heavy artillery until nothing but shell holes litter the field can place a smugness on my face for days.

I remember playing the American vs. Chinese human wave senerio in the 2nd game of the series. The Americans have good artillery, support from machine guns, and the advantage of prepared postions on the small hills. The Chinese have numbers... In that senerio, for me, it almost always came down to the last few turns with the remains of a few infantry squads and the last few rounds of ammo for the artillery to push the chinese off the hills.

In the third version of the game, you command an entire brigade! Lots of untis, turns take minutes for the computer to work out, but man, I tell ya, you can really get into it! With fondness I remember the hypothetical campaign where Syria and Egypt launched a surprised attack on Isreal in the late 1990's. That first scenrio has the Isreals way outnumbered and the Syrians launch a massive assult with tanks, artillery, and air support. You really have to think on this one to beat it. Loved it!

Ok, hehe, got a little carried away there. Excuse me. This post is just bringing back some good memories. :)

3) Civilization II. Spent hours and hours playing this game. I remember many moons ago where I would get up in the morning, go to my computer, and sit there and play Civ 2 for 15 hours -straight. No joke. (Don't do that anymore, hehe.) ...I actually finished a game last month! What can i say, you find something that really appeals to you and you keep it around.

4) The gold box series of D&D games... man, those had some great story lines in them. Last year I had my D&D group going through Phlan in the Forgotten Realms. Bits of of the first game Pool of Radiance, inspired me when I designed the town for the adventure.

5) Starcraft w/Brood Wars. Mmmmm...watching a wave of Battlecrusiers with full upgrades go into a base and have some fun. Ghosts and nukes! Protos Dragoons and Reavers. Mmmmm... good stuff!

6) Mechwarrior 2. Launching two LRM 20's at your target far away and seeing them explode! Running a light mech really fast up to a heavy and plastering him with medium pulse lasers, and then trying to run away! haha! Fighting in the city and plastering buildings as you play hide and seek with the enemy! FUN!

7) Aliens vs. Predator. Love using the flame thrower on the aliens or watching them go down as they charged you when you had two pistols in your hand. That was a rush for me!

Ok, I've said enough. hehe. Going to have to see if I can get a few of these to run on XP (I KNOW MMo2, Civ 2 do work on XP).
