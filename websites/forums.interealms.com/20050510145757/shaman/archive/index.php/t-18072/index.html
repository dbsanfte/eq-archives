The Hyena Shaman
- I MUST be able to solo exp on my own.

Well, ALL classes can solo, some are just better at it. Shaman aren't the most efficient soloers, but we have great staying power. Mages and Necros do damn good, and a well-trained Beastlord can grind damn near anything into the ground. Druids, Wizards, and Shadow Knights also have solo ability, with SK's limping last in line (VERY gear-dependant, but on hte bright side, it's rather cheap gear)

- I want to be handy when I group with my friends in singl groups

Handy how? Since you're posting on a Shaman board, I assume you want examples of Shaman handiness? Slow, Debuffs, haste, buffs, crowd control (Root - Learn it, love it, live it), and even some off-tanking ability in exp groups.

For other classes, the most group-friendly is probably a cleric, though rogue comes in there, too. Maximum sustainable DPS, and you can easily get great backstabs with a Slime Coated Harpoon and levels.

- I would like to be useful on a raid

Well, that's where the shine of Shaman tarnishes. On a raid, more than three Shaman means that a bear is going to be pretending to be a level 40 wizard or a level 12 monk. Shaman are great on raids, provided you're the highest-leveled one there.

For other classes, I would say Druids and Clerics for big heals, and Druids, Wizards, Mages, and rogues for raw DPS. There's only going to be one tank, and it's not likely to be you, so might as well do damage with a class focused towards that.

- I will likely never be uber so I don't want a heavily gear dependant class (IE, melee classes are pretty much out of the question).

Shaman are without a doubt, the most gear-dependant casters in the game. Just dig through the Equipment forum - Most people couldn't imagine a life of poverty without a Fungi, JBB, and REIF at the VERY least. It's a long, hard struggle to get a Shaman geared up respectfully without funds, particularly because every good Shaman item is severely gouged on all servers. And don't even get me started on spells.

Int casters are very easy to equip. Most decent robes run arouns 100pp, and +int items are all over the damn place. The tricky thing is with Necros and Enchanters, you want HP, too, so they can get fairly expensive. They're also more popular than Mages and Wizards, so good ench / necro gear is a little scarecer.

Druids are easy as pie to equip, and so are Clerics. I know clerics who, at 60, are still wearing Bloodtained Tunics. Why? It's +4 wis and they never get hit anyway. For a cleric, just make a high elf, slap on some cheap wisdom crap, and you're set.

Monks and Beastlords are VERY easy to equip, until you look at weapons. There are no good and cheap h2h weapons aside from Polished Steel Ulaks (Sack Full of Rocks is good for Beastlords, too), and the fast delay weapons are also expensive. But, since their fists are just as good, you can skip a bit.

Rogues are maybe the easiest melee class to gear up. A set of Ry'Gorr, and a Crystaline Spider Fang, with a few little bits of HP and AC here and there, and you'll be the top of your game until planes.

Knights are interesting, almost the exact opposie of Monks and Beastlords. Their knight-only weapons and sheilds are simply amazing, and they're cheap as hell, too. However, their armor is pretty expensive, If you wanted, you could make do with bronze or some cheap knock-off armors, though.

Warriors and Rangers... Don't bother. Just don't.

Frankly, there's no one class that fully covers what you want, though Enchanter comes close.

My suggestions would be bard or shaman.

Bard has ALWAYS shined massively in 6 person exp groups. You can fill so many roles. You are the best soloist in the game at many levels (but not all, unless you get certain items). One decent FT item makes you the best soloist to 60+ if you learn to swarm kite - and you could be virtually naked otherwise. Raid Value is quite good, especially for smaller raids. The downside is the constant effort required to play a bard. Back when I used to solo my bard sometimes, the only breaks were when I stopped because I wanted to rest in RL, not because my bard needed downtime. The main question with a bard - do you WANT to be frenetically busy whenever you're solo'ing, and not that much less when in an exp group and trying to play at high efficiency? If you want a more relaxed experience don't play a bard. You are somewhat equipment dependent, but not hugely. I would never want to play a L 55+ bard without his epic, but the bard epic is not all THAT hard any more, like it was back in the day when Trakanon was an uber raid. Other than that, equipment helps a bard, but doesn't really affect his ability to fill his role all that much.

My other suggestion would be shaman, if you want to be a caster type. You can do everything pretty well, and are good at solo'ing, good in a group, good on raids. You aren't the BEST class in any of those categories, but you're good at all of them. And gear isn't a very high priority. The primary downside of the shaman vs the bard is that you'll have a lot more downtime with the shaman, especially early on.

I'd avoid enchanter. They don't solo well at all, IMHO, until very high level. If you want a CC class that can solo at any level, it's shaman or bard, depending on your preferences.

Solo:

Shaman can do it, but its not super efficient. Necros have better DPS, more mana efficiency, better mana regen and a much better pet. Necro utility abilities own all. Druids rank 2nd for soloing on my list. They dependent on external mana regen, but are excellent root/rotters and quad kiting works well. Beastlords get a great pet and good melee ability. They get limited inate mana regen. All in all, they are great solo classes.

Group:

shaman (slow/buff), beast (slow/dps), druid (heal/buff/CC), necro (dps, CC, manaregen). Most of the time groups want slowers so shaman are good here. Druids, excellent primary healers at high levels for an exp group.

Raid:

Everyone wants slow for raids, but its semi unstackable. Other shaman skills are pre-raid buffing etc. High level shaman heals are not bad (Quiessence in particular). Druids get the short stick for raiding because there are so many of them, but they heal very well and have sought after buffs. Druids on full burn do respectable dps. Necros are pure dps: dot and FD, dot and FD, chain neurotoxin. Same for beast, mostly DPS with some buffage (paragon AA rocks).

My experience makes me think a necro is least item dependent. Druids are next in terms of gear dependency, but they do need as much FT as possible. Beast being melee need some nice stuff. Shaman seem very very cost intensive to me. First shaman gear is expensive, second shaman spells seem to be much more expensive than other classes.
