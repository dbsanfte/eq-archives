I play a shaman and an sk, both ogres. The shaman has been an ogre his whole life, but the sk was a troll for a long time. He got rerolled as an ogre because, having played Flufy for so long, I found periodically losing control of my toon for several seconds (usually at the worst possible time) to be unbelievably frustrating.

As a shaman, there have been numerous situations where I managed to get that wipeout-preventing slow off, or hit the tank/cleric/chanter with a heal just in time while getting beat on, but there's a more personal example that sticks out most in my head. I was checking out CS, probably way before I had any business being there. I wound up getting aggroed by a wyvrn, so I run to that tower thingy where the zone out is. Apparently someone else had just had an aggro problem too, because I was going up the stairs, turned the corner, and ran into 5 wyvrns. As they were beating on me, I backed into the corner and hit Gate, and made it with about 15 hp left. Did I make my channeling roll? Most definitely, but it is my firm belief, as they were red to me at the time, that I would have gotten stunned by at least one of them if I wasn't an ogre. That would have meant a multi-hour CR, as this was back when you still needed keys to get TP'd there, and I didn't have one.

On a more mundane level, with no-stun, I can do certain things with a much higher level of comfort than I would be able to otherwise, like pulling, root-parking, and running my tail to the zone when things go bad. Oh, and as far as it being irrelevant when you're running away goes, all I can say is, running backwards is not nearly as difficult as it might seem at first.

It's been commented elsewhere that, pre-60, no-stun isn't that big of a deal compared to regen, ac bonuses, etc, but I have to disagree. It's a big bonus even at the lowest levels, IF you play in such a way as to take advantage of it.
