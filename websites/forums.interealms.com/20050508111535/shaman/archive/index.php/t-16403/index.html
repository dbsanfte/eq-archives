Kianor
Well, it depends. :)

Since you say your guild is very low on shamans, 65 will be a benefit to your guild, the additional levels help with resists etc.

Malos/Fo7 will also help on guild raids if you can get them.

Canni V is very nice, but it's not essential to a raid, so if supporting your guild on raids is where your focus is, I'd suggest you go to 65 first. Once you're 65, the AA come easily anyway.

When I did it, I did the AA for Canni V at 61/62, then went back to level to 65. I was in a small guild at the time though, who didn't raid very much, and I didn't want to outlevel all of them. I also had Malo and Torpor, but not much prospect of buying my 65 spells (or even all the 63/64 ones).

I found that 62 is a good level to get to stop and AA if you're going to stop. At that point you have your best PoP buffs, albeit only single target versions, you have Cloud of Grummus which is a good raid spell, you have your upgraded pet which is good for solo and groups, and you have Tnarg's and Replenishment. All good tools for a shaman.

Also at 62, you can join HoH and BoT groups without being flagged, and if there aren't any spaces there, PoV/CoD groups will be glad to have you, and you can still get fairly decent (though not as good) experience in tier 1.

If you don't have Malo and Torpor, maybe you would be better off heading to 65 and skipping them altogether for Malos and Quiescence. The path to 65 might be a little harder as a result, though.

Bear in mind, the best experience is in tier 3 and above, and people there will group with a 65 shaman over a 62 one, so you might find that going to 65 first will be easier in that way.

Personally, I don't think there's a wrong decision, just do what you think is best for you.
