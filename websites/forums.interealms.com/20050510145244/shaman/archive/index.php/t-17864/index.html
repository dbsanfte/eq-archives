Ranguard
Going to try to answer your questions the best I can, I'm no guru, but I feel I do alright.
1) A Shaman can solo from lvl 1 to 65.
2) Shaman are good soloers from lvl 34, pet helps a lot.
3) Best way I've found to solo is to root, apply slow, DoTs and dogdog, then rush the mob and tank it yourself. At higher levels, you may just want to root-rot with dogdog on mob while you Stoic/Torpor and Canni some mana back.
4) Only snare item I know of that's clicky is for Innorruuk worshipping Trolls other than that, Swollen Fungusbeast Glands (10 charge Snare)
5) Canni 2 is VERY worth it IMO, I got it at 39 with some help, and you will notice it does more for you when you get it, I dread not having it and being 51, since you'll still be Canniing a lot and without it, a lot more.
6) JBB procs at level 45 only Iksars can't use it, so I have no input on price/usefulness.
7) Soloing is an artform, develop your own tecnique(sp?) Usually, best way I've found is to pull with slow, root, load up DoTs, back away and get some mana while DoTs work, then wade in and melee for a bit.

All this is merely my opinion, I'm only level 55 (and grinding AAs to be ready at 59 for CanniV) so my experiences will differ from the higher ups. Hope this helps ya :)

Jinheim
A comparably equipped necro will be a better soloer than his shaman counterpart. This is especially true at higher levels when their mana regen starts to surpass ours. Ours is active (you have to cannibalize) whereas theirs is passive (lich). On top of that, necros have more dots than us and theirs are slightly more efficient. Also, most necros spec conjuration, but a conj spec hurts a shaman in the longrun (unless all you want to do is solo). And, of course, you cannot forget snare and FD. The only time a shaman is a more viable soloer is when taking hits is unavoidable (i.e. summoning mobs or extreme close quarters).

Don't get the wrong idea about soloing shamans, though, we are great at it. Necros just happen to be better (suprassed only by bards, imho). If soloing is all you want to do, necro will be the best choice for you. However, if you want to be a strong soloer in addition to a strong support class, then shaman might be what you want.

You get another 18 mana/tick from maxed out meditate, and you sit still as opposed to the necro running and thus getting 1 mana/tick in natural regen.

So even without using canni 4 once you beat the necro by 15 points of mana/tick. (Slight loss as you can't always cast cannibalization exactly on repop.)

As you sit you can swap spells around, which the aggro kiting necro can't. And gem slots are at a premium.

Pox then Breath of Ultor, then Bane of Nife, then Blood of Saryrn, then Bane. 2088 + 3354 + 1648 + 2240 + 2100 over 2.6 minutes. If necessary you can recast the last two. (If really necessary you could recast Bane of Nife too. But hopefully you aren't soloing mobs with more than 15k hps for exp, and considering that you have the entire 2.6 minutes to recast stuff you would be better off with a third bane, as it only lasts 5 ticks.)

The slightly worse mana to damage ratio would be offset by you sitting alone. You sitting and canni4'ing instead of twiddling your thumbs should put you far ahead. (And yes, you do lose ticks due to having to back up from re-rooted mobs.)

His pet is basically just another dot. It puts out quite a deal of punishment, and can be used while aggro kiting, while dogdog won't be on any root-cooked PoFire mobs. But it's not enough to let the necro kill at even near twice a shammies pace.
