The DVD edition of the UK magazine has got three video clips of EQ2 that I've not seen anywhere else.

The first one is a general trailer. The second is a clip of battle scenes, and the third one is a clip of the talking NPCs.

The more of EQ2 that I see the more I think that I will give it a try. The graphics look very good and more like the original theme of EQ than the alien-esq thing we have now with EQ in SoL/GoD/OoW.

The battle scenes look pretty good with the blade trails giving a real impression of speed when a sword or a staff is used. But It seem weird that you hack at a person for 5 mins and there's not a scratch on him and then he drops dead. =/

The amour looks very nice there appear to be more joints now so that pieces move independently. It's hard to explain, but I saw an NPC laugh and it looks like his shoulders and chest have joints/muscles to give the impression that the amour is external to the body and not part of it.

The other things that stood out were the shadows and the long grass. the grass looks more realistic than the similar stuff you see in places like hollow shademoor.

I've not had chance to read the article yet but I'll add another post if there is anything worth noting in it.

From Gemdiver's review (http://eq2.graffe.com/library.php?id=36)

Soloing - The EQ II dev team wants to make sure that solo'ing is a viable way to progress your character. One of the main problems for casual players in Everquest Live was lack of solo'able content that someone with only an hour or two could sit down and use and still feel as if their character was progressing. At the same time those who group will find themselves able to take on the harder encounters which are likely to reward them with higher experience and better loot.

It doesn't exactly say what it will be but it does say that they want to address the issue. Reading that though it does indeed suggest that grouping is the prefered method of getting uber equipment.
