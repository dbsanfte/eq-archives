Tarthug
As I move deeper into PoP and look back, I wonder where the requirement for CC went. I haven't had a chanter in my group for months and I haven't needed to cast root in my group for ages. When I found situations for it, it was difficult to land and hold, or the target was immune. Granted, I'm a casual player with a regular semi-DPS group mostly EXPing in HoH, BoT and PoN caves. My group is generally Cleric, Necro, Shaman, Ranger (EQ/AM3), Rogue, SK and does some pretty solid DPS. The group is pretty tight and the SK is skilled at aggro generation. Once mobs are slowed, the SK gets everything on him, or I off tank the add, or the ranger snares and kites depending on camp. We don't root, and the necro RARELY mezzes undead flagged mobs (he can't most of the time). What pins my ass to the wall is how we generally power through most situations rather than use old fashioned CC. It just seems like the game takes less skill.

I find that even now as we hit some of the old haunts like cash farming seblis, or the GY in Chardok (for some SK drop for some spell) that we let everything come in and blow through it all. I used to be a solid CC shaman from my solo days (root and rot tactics used mostly), but I find now my skills are in a state of atrophy as they are almost never needed. I'm talking about a very specific part of the game (mostly the root then debuff rooted mob). I'm pretty sure a lot of it has to do with where I'm hunting, but it seems to me that DPS>CC and that classes like chanters and shaman are less critical. My group for instance with it's tactics would be better served by replacing me with a BL and having a shaman buff bot.

Some of this could be remedied when LDoN brings me the Bane spell (I have no ancient spells) as I can add more DPS then, but I'm almost 63 and I find that although my BoN and EBolt dots do make a noticable difference, that my role is generally as BU healer and debuffer. Lately I feel like I'm restricted (every fight casting Malo, Tugur's, at least one Tnarg's, BoN, EBolt, plus canning mana back (cann5 and cann4) ... near the limits of what I can get back in the frequent pull 1 minute fights). I thought with a little CC I could do more and spend less mana rather than just being the "slow and save the necro/ranger/rogue/cleric from a bind point trip guy". Maybe I'm too sensitive about the old crawling days when if a mob wasn't locked down it would be a painful CR, maybe I'm just playing in the wrong zones. Anyway, just venting and hoping for some feedback on what you might think about CC skills, I just think that SoE has made it so that why use CC when DPS will make it dead. I hope one day the new mobs that come out actually make more sense to CC than just plain kill.

Sithrax
Amen, Gart.

While we're at it, let's touch on a few more things that make the game too easy.


-Outdoor zones

Mobs are spaced widely and generally each one has a small aggro radius. Single pulling is trivial even without a bard. Horse addiction is just as bad as KEI addiction. Why worry about aggro from sitting down to med or change spells? Who even sits down anymore? Managing your mana is a lot easier when everyone has FT25 on all the time, too.


-Risk/reward way out of balance

Now that I think about it, the PoP mob level "challenge bonus" and huge xp multipliers were a massive mistake. Pre-PoP, it was pretty hard to find high-level mobs in outdoor zones, which made for generally increased difficulty and tougher CRs. Now, there are a ton of graveyard equipped outdoor zones full of level 58+ mobs. The challenge bonus should have been tied to zone difficulty, not mob level. 4C in CT is far more difficult than Valor, yet mobs there yield much less xp because of their level. This is ridiculous.


-Tanking... why bother?

Except in zones where mobs routinely summon (pretty much just BoT for most of the EQ crowd), why even bother tanking? Aggro kiting makes so much more sense, even with melees in the group. No damage taken = no need for a healer. Replace a healer with a dps class and kill faster, and thus get better xp. Slow mitigation just makes this option even more appealing. Seriously, would you rather have:

SK
Necromancer
Shaman
Rogue
Cleric
Magician

and tank the mobs... or would you rather replace the cleric with another necromancer (or other dps class) and just kite them? Sure, you lose the SK's melee DPS... but his pet and DoTs still work, not to mention the rogue you just picked up is going to add more DPS than the SK ever could. The shaman can be repaced with a higher DPS class, or can actually pump out a solid amount of damage because he NEVER has to worry about gaining aggro.

The ability of some classes to generate virtually unlimited aggro is beyond stupid. Against xp mobs - who cares? Just run away and keep blasting. The vast majority of them don't summon, so there's no risk as long as you can snare. Even if you can't snare, horses and SoW make outrunning nearly any mob trivial. The aggro problem is not limited to plate hybrids, they are just the most obvious symptoms.

As I understand it, there are plenty of mobs in Fire which don't summon, which is apparently the xp zone of choice for the elemental crowd. It's not as if this imbalance is fixed as you progress.


-PoP xp mobs generally lack special abilities

Except for slow mitigation, that is. There are a LOT more mobs that mitigate slow than mobs that mitigate root and snare. Summoning mobs are rare as well. This further diminshes the "challenge" that PoP mobs provide, yet the challenge bonus continues to increase xp gain. (Note that I am not elemental flagged, so if these mobs have some extremely nasty special abilities, perhaps there is some balance to be found.)
