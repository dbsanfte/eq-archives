Helek
Please pardon the sp errors and inconsistencies.


The muffled hiss of the dagger being drawn from it's scabbard rang true through
the clear night's chill air. True enough to Helec's sensitive ears, as strolled through the
shadows of an alley far removed from the central section of town. This area was by far
the worst part of town, yet Helec felt perfectly comfortable strolling through it's dim
thoroughfares. So comfortable that he barely noted the rocks shifting noisily underfoot,
but who could miss that smell.
The smell of pig sweat had initially assaulted him soon after entering the long
alleyway. As he continued, step by step, the stench became stronger. His elven senses
had paid off again. No human would be able to detect this scent this well at six feet,
much less sixty.
Around the next corner a pig faced creature, much larger than the approaching elf,
shifted his grip ever so slightly on his dagger to a more comfortable position. The dagger
itself more closely resembled a short sword but served well as a dagger for the gigantic
beast.
A gleam of intelligence that was uncharacteristic for such a breed, sparkled in his
eye, as he bobbed up and down rhythmically timing the approaching steps. A patience
gained through past reward allowed the long seconds to pass by without effort. Yes this
would be an easy kill. It was well rehearsed, not to mention the numerous others that had
fallen to his blade using this very same technique.
Still bobbing up and down, the orc listened to the grind as his victim plodded along
on the gravel he had spread earlier. Nearer elf, nearer...ready...set... As the footsteps
reached the corner the orc lashed out at the elven heart with the rhythm of a well
practiced performance. Striking only empty space.
The footsteps continued past the orcish assassin nonchalantly padding into the
distance. He looked around, dumbfounded. Brak tried to gather his wits and begin to
follow the invisible quarry but behind him another set of footsteps approached noisily
echoing through the darkness of the alley. The orc whirled to the ready wanting to see
who was playing tricks on him this night. For the same one would die. Ghost or no
ghost, this was definitely not the way it was supposed to be.
His thoughts raced as he saw a diminutive black cloaked figure step out of the
shadows and stop. The hood pulled low over the face of the stranger. Quickly the orc
realized that this was no stranger. This elf was his bounty. Helec Delion. He had never
met the rogue elf even when he was a guild member. He towered over the elf by two feet
or more. The elf said nothing but ended a simple emptyhanded gesture and gave one of
his rings a twist. Eerily the footsteps that walked into the distance ended their traipse
simultainiously as the elf stopped.
Seconds passed as they both sized up the situation and their reputable opponent.
Grak was definately an expert in the art of deqaling death but he lacked the finesse and
grace that was Helec's strongest point. It struck Helec as being odd that the guild would
appoint Grak to the task of murdering an elf that had seen his style and had even trained
aginst him. It was true enough that Grak's hatred for elves and Helec in particular would
be astrong weapon for the orc.
With a sudden roar Grak charged with a speed that belied his great size and quickly
covered the spans of ground between himself and the quiet and calm target of his
frustration.
With ears laid back in a rage the orc swooped in for a quick kill as his dagger
began it's decent upon the elven opponent. Helec knowing the grave danger of the
moment suspected the typical orcish attack. No creature, regardless of intelligence could
avoid it's true nature too very long, especially Grak.
With smooth finesse Helec dove under the attackers swing and inside the defenses.
Unshaken he landed and rolled putting a good distance between the two again. He was up
again at the ready, this time with two daggers of his own, both dripping with blood. The
wild eyed orc ended his downward thrust and slowly turned to the grinning elf. The
tension mounted as the silence was drowned out in the orc's ears by his furiously pounding
heartbeat. Knowing he had been cut was bad enough but to add insult to injury was the
fact Helec had done it so easily and remained unscathed.
The crimson began to pour and the orc suddenly felt weak as he groped at the neat
line that had been drawn across his throat. Then he felt it. Poison. His blood coursed
through his veins carrying it along. He felt numb. Losing control of his muscles he
dropped his weapon to the ground. why weren't his arms responding. His guts burned
with an intense fire. Grak looked up and saw the elf still smiling as he cleaned his daggers.
Helec was eyeing the orc. There was no honor here amongst the shadows. After all he
was just doing his duty or was he just following his nature. Knowing there was an open
reward on Helec's head maybe the orcish assasssin was trying to collect on his rival's
bounty. Helec could understand. But didn't.
With a deft flick of the wrist Helec launched one of the small daggers and a few
moments later he bent over the dead orc to pull the knife from Grak's eye socket. He at
least saved his would-be assassin a great deal of pain and torment from either the poison
or bleeding to death. As he wiped his remaining blade clean he doubted that the orc
would have done the same.
