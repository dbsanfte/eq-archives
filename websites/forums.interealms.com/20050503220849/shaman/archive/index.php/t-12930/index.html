finaljustice
It's not that easy.

Firstly, levels to 60 > AA points

Secondly, The amount of AA EXP required to make any level is the same as regular exp at level 51.

Thirdly, lighter blue mobs give less aggrigate EXP than dark blue or white ones

and finally, different zones give different percentage of EXP to AA EXP. Therefore, if you are in a high AA EXP percentage zone and have your AA EXP off, then you're not making the EXP you could in a low AA EXP zone. If you have 30% AA EXP on and are in a zone that gives 60% regular EXP, then the EXP you will get in regular equals 70% of 60% of the mob's total for your level and your AA EXP equals 30% of 40% of the mob's total for your level. Yes, it's very complex.

The bottom line is you should find zones that give you high AA EXP and has mobs you can handle, if you want AA levels. Otherwise, find high regular EXP zones.

Huh? That is not true at all. Zones have an Experience Modifier value, which affects experienced gained per mob. Whether you are putting your exp into AA or normal experience makes no difference. Anyway based on my experiences, this is my understanding of how EXP works. Feel free to correct me as something as basic as EXP should be understood by all ...

Based on the Zone Experience Modifier, the same level mobs in different zones can give different amounts of EXP points when they are killed. But based on the level & type of mob and the ZEM, killing that mob will reward a fixed amount of base EXP points.

Depending on the number of members in the group, group bonus EXP points are added. Charmed pet and pet penalties are deducted. The total number of awardable EXP points resulting from the mob kill are then split by group members in proportion to level of the members. Higher level players get more EXP points assigned than lower level group members.

Level bonuses, if any, are then applied to the EXP points assigned to each player resulting in net EXP points that a group member gets for the kill.

At this point it doesn't really matter how you choose to assign that EXP between regular exp and AAexp accept that the portion that is assigned to regular EXP is adjusted by a racial penaty, if applicable. The portion that goes to AA does not get a racial penalty.

It is my belief that a statment like "different zones give different percentage of EXP to AA EXP" and the confusing discussion that follwed it is like saying that the sun doesn't shine as bright when I have my sun glasses on. Indeed the sun is just as bright whether I have my sun glasses on or off, but my perception of how bright it is changes. That percention can lead to misinterpretation.

finaljustice
First issue, the mistake. Months back, people were thinking that they'd get AA EXP as if they were level 51 to the mob. Each level of AA is the same EXP requirements as regular EXP level 51 for a race with no penalties.

Secondly, the only true empirical evidence of this effect would be to have ShowEQ running, snooping your segment while you played. All I know is, that if you fight FG, it's really poor regular EXP and good AA EXP (up to level 60). Other zones are opposite, such as Sebilis, which is poor regular EXP and REALLY bad AA EXP.

If you don't want to worry about this at all, fight in the Planes, which have great regular AND AA exp. But if you're in a zone and have your slider set far to one direction or the other and notice that your EXP isn't moving that great compared to the level of the mob, try moving it the other way (and make a note to yourself). Go to zones with good AA EXP if that's what you're after and ones without it, if you're after regular EXP.
