get c3 from nexus or whatever and head out to ww.

when you zone into WW from SG, go out the tunnel, turn left and follow the cliff-line until you hit yeldema, shes a red dragon that looks exactly like Nagafen and Telkaronar, she is sitting on a nest with big eggs. i go to the cliff near the water which is around 600 feet from her, summon pet, give him celerity, and get fom..

1. gate
2. chloroblast
3. kraggs
4. turgurs

Shabura Naarteban
While some of the dragons that are soloable are painfully long and difficult (such as anything that can CH, or the gaters - because fighting on some bind points can be a problem with adds), there are only a VERY few dragons in WW that are not shaman-soloable.

These are:
Category 1) Fluxproc dragons
---Most notable, the Saparas. There isn't really much to do about a 1k+ proc. Maybe someone with really spiffy gear will prove me wrong with one that doesn't proc as often (like Mav Sapara). But I'm pretty sure that no one will be soloing Jen or Rak.

Category 2) Silence dragons
---Esorpa of the Ring, Ionat. They silence too often, for too long. I know someone said they got Esorpa to 60 once, so perhaps it is doable if you are in VERY good gear and get VERY lucky, but I think that would be more fluke that typical.

Category 3) Massive damage
---Sontalak, Klandicar. Because, well ... that should be obvious. Harla Dar can fit in here, too, although its more a problem with her resists and spellcasting than the damage per se.

Everything else CAN be soloed. Now then ... sub 60 ... I think dropping the weak AE dragons (like Gafala or Hachaeva) would be VERY tough, but I suppose it might be theoretically possible. Good hp and resists will help a lot. I'd probably employ potions for that attempt - at least the DS. Soloing purists will argue that the intaclick heal potion is unfair ... but that ought to make these quite doable, although still difficult.
