BlargX
Saying that you should just wait to buy torpor doesn't take into account that it is not often sold on most servers, and when it is sold, it's often snatched up or paid a premium for. If you see it at 40k or 100k or whatever your server price is one day, there's no guarantee that you'll see it at that price again.

Indeed, there's every chance you'll see it for more, as shamans are flooding the game, since they make such useful bots in general and useful mains now that PoP has come out and made shamans a class of choice to have around. With the ability to PL and twink that high level people have these days, getting a shaman to 60 is not much of a problem at all. Not as easy as leveling a melee to 50 in a day of played hours and to 60 in few days more of played hours or less, but not a problem. There are tons of shamans revived from people's accounts now that PoP is out and more being made and powerleveled to 60 (and even 65) very fast these days.

Lots of those old shamans and all of the new ones are going to need to buy or loot torpor. Guess what -- you'll be competing with THEM for torpor; and a lot of them are rich. Somebody selling torpor for best offer and you've got 50k? Fine, someone who wants it makes it 55k and you're done. You can raise another 5, 10, 15k? 25? Fine; your offer is topped by another 5k. Impossible? Hardly. Torpor is virtually never ever sold on my server, not in all the years I've been playing. When it is, it sells for 100k. That was back when I was one of the ONLY level 60 shamans on a very crowded server. Now, shamans are everywhere you look. Rich ones, too.

Further -- I think we're all aware of how often torpor drops.

Further still -- people are not killing old world mobs like they used to. Monopolizing kunark dragons and Sontalak and Veeshan's Peak used to be a great way to make money and get good spells and epic pieces for uberguilds especially, and competition could be VERY bitter for those mobs.

These days, there are better weapons than epics all over the place that often don't take anywhere near the effort and organization of doing epic runs for member after member of a guild. Less reason to kill those dragons.

These days, established guilds and their members have for the most part long ago gotten all their 60 and below spells and many are even busy getting post-60 spells that outdo the spells that you can get off kunark dragons and Veeshan's Peak. Less reason to kill those dragons.

These days, getting keyed for Veeshan's Peak is not like getting a key to paradise. It's not an impressive or compelling place anymore in comparison to what it used to be. Most of the items aren't wanted too much, and the place is a pain; and camping up more keys when people are busy worrying about PoP and its much better loot, experience, and spells is not gonna happen much anymore. So neither are the spell drops. Less reason to kill those dragons.

So -- expect to not necessarily find torpor offered for sale too much, expect not to always find a low or stable price on it when you do see it for sale, and expect the mobs that drop it to be killed substantially less.

All of which goes to indicate that if you see a chance to buy torpor, you have to respect the possibility that you're being presented with an opportunity today that you may not see tomorrow -- or even for four or five or six months.

The rarity of fungi? Not much. PoP, with its mobs hitting for huge amounts, has made its minimal regen less attractive, as have spells like replenishment. It's now mostly good in the old worlds and for twinks. The nerf of monks, the class most benefited by fungis besides torporless shamans, has combined with PoP to release a flood of pent up fungis onto the market compared to the rate they appeared just a bit previously; the price has dropped 20 to 40% on my server, depending on your luck, but averages about 30% less.

So, if you get rid of your fungi, you can get it back. People aren't exactly rushing to create more monks, so that's not likely to change.

Getting torpor again if you blow your chance won't be so easy for most.

My experience? I wore a fungi from 52 or 54, I forget which. It was HUGE. It's hard to imagine it could ever be trivial when you're used to wearing one before you have torpor.

However, torpor really is THAT good. Any old crummy BP will do once you get torpor. It gives you so much more in healing and mana regeneration potential so quickly that it makes a fungi very trivial. I kept a Wolfcallers BP -- the COV dragon quest one -- in the bank till I got torpor, because without torpor I felt it didn't pull its weight. So I know the value of the fungi. (I also have a 60 monk who used one that I created much later.) But torpor made me at LEAST three times the shaman I was without it; a barbed ringmail BP or most anything would have been absolutely fine until I could get better.

With torpor, your soloing ability will skyrocket, and you'll get experience(to get you to canni 5) much much faster. At that point you have the dream combo of the game -- the ability to regenerate massive amounts of both health and mana...and do it perpetually. You'll be massive.

You'll be able to do many things you couldn't dream of with just a fungi.

Get torpor without hesitation, even if you have to dump the fungi or pay a stupid price for it. DON'T expect it to just be there waiting somewhere whenever you're ready if you pass up an opportunity.

Do not wait for the level 65 quiessence spell! If you aren't easily capable of getting torpor, your chances of getting quiessence are probably non-existent.

The only proviso I would add to my advice is this: If you are willing to shoot to 62 and buy Tnarg's Mending, you will have a more aggrolicious version of a decent heal even without torpor. However, that will green out or make relatively useless for experience the majority of the game world. If you're young enough to be sweating torpor, then there is a great deal you likely still have to do there -- get some dragon or giant quest armor, camp up keys to VP, Emperor, Vex Thal, do lots of Ssra, NTOV, camp up cash to buy various upgrades -- LOTS and lots of things. Do you REALLY want to spend the months and months and months(you will not speed through this phase) doing it for NO experience, or so little that it might as well be none?

If so, maybe 62 and Tnargs are okay for you.

I'd get torpor first.

Be aware, too, that while you're working your way through the game and getting AA points, not only will torpor make a fantastic difference in your ability, but you can eventually radically upgrade it, turning it, with the right AA points and our specialization in alteration(pretty much all shamans specialize in alteration), into a spell that casts in 3.0 seconds for about 160 mana and lasts 50% longer, doing 50% more healing -- 1800 guaranteed instead of 1200. Half the time, 50% more healing, 10% less mana cost(and the mana is EXTREMELY trivial already, for a heal with virtually no aggro.

Think hard before turning aside torpor.

By the way, I sold my own first fungi for torpor. Now I can wear my monk's newer fungi anytime I want, as I retired and stripped my monk.

I don't. Ever.
