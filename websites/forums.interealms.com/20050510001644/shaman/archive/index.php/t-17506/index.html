It is not true that you need to get into a good guild to get your high level spells or to get your epic.

I am in a guild that is a very small family guild and they have not made a ton of difference to me getting my high level spells (this guild is "good" for me but not what most people mean when they say a "good" guild):). One of my friends who is not in my guild bought one of my 65 spells for me. One of my other friends who is not in my guild got me some of my rarer high 50's spells when his uber guild went and dropped a kunark dragon on his head for kicks.

I got the last component for torpor for 4k and the last component for malo for 500 pp. 61-62 spells are very cheap. 63-64 spells are not too bad. 65 spells are still rather expensive but can be bought in bazaar.

If the most important thing to you is what equipment you have and what spells are in your spell book then you may be happier trying to find a raiding guild. But if you enjoy a more casual play style and are willing to just take your upgrades when they come, there are lots of guilds out there for you to choose from. If you find someone you enjoy groping with and they are in a guild, ask them about it. That’s how I found my guild. I met a very nice druid lady when running around Misty Thicket when I was playing my ranger and I started grouping with her. That encounter led me to making some of my very best friends in game.

So go out there, have some fun and get to know some people. The rest will take care of itself.

I'm in a raiding guild that is specifically "real life friendly". No raiding requirements, i.e. you don't have to log in just because a raid is scheduled, and even if you are online you are not required to attend a raid. We use a points-based system to distribute loot (not DKP, this one is auction-based) and casual raiders simply take longer to accumulate enough points to win the nice stuff.

Of course, there are practical requirements. Keys/flags require a good bit of work, but much of it is solo/outside raiding hours (like the VT key pieces, for example).
