In order to examine healing effectiveness, I estimated the healing power of a number of spells for clerics, druids, shamans, and paladins. Healing power is the maximum rate at which a healer can repair damage dealt on their target by chain casting a particular spell. I calculated healing power of direct heal spells as heal per second using the following formula:

HPS = (amount healed)/(casting time + recast time)

Here are healing power estimates for some high level direct heal spells:

Cleric, Holy Light: 508
Cleric, Supernal Light: 458
Cleric, Supernal Remedy: 363
Cleric, Ethereal Light: 228
Cleric, Complete Healing: 612

Druid, Sylvan Infusion: 335
Druid, Karana's Renewal: 382
Druid, Nature's Infusion: 291
Druid, Nature's Touch: 126
Druid, Tunare's Renewal: 239

Shaman, Tnarg's Mending: 217
Shaman, Kragg's Mending: 159

Paladin, Light of Order: 156
Paladin, Light of Nife: 141
Paladin, Touch of Nife: 159

Healing power for heal over time spells must be calculated differently. Essentially, the recast time for is 4 ticks (24 seconds), although healing is distributed on a tick by tick basis rather than instantly. HPS represents the average healing per second over the life of the HoT.

Cleric, Holy Elixir: 79
Cleric, Supernal Elixir: 71
Cleric, Celestial Elixir: 43

Shaman, Breath of Thrushar: 48
Shaman, Quiescence: 40

These calculations do not include the effects of AA's or focus effects, which I assume would have similar effects for all classes.

From these calculations, it is evident that in terms of raw healing power, shamans rank with paladins, or slightly better. Kragg's Mending much less powerful than either druid incomplete heal (ICH), and is essential equivalent in power to the two paladin heals introduced in GoD, neither of which are ICH.

How does shaman slowing effect healing power for shamans? The effect of slowing must be averaged over the time that healing is required. The calculations here are a bit more complicated, so I will just summarize my findings. For a mob doing 650 dps (not unreasonable by GoD standards) that is fully slowable (after casting Turgur's Insects and Malos), the effect of Tnarg's Mending is roughly equivalent to that of Holy Light on an unslowed mob. Tnarg's Mending is over twice as effective as Sylvan Infusion on a slowed mob. For a mob that is 50% mitigated, however, Tnarg's Mending is about 80% as effective as Sylvan Infusion. If a mob is slowed with Balance of the Nihil without casting Malos, however, Tnarg's Mending is better than Holy Light on unmitigated mobs (this is because Balance has a rapid cast time of 1.5 sec). On a 50% mitigated mob, slowing with Balance reduces the effectiveness of Tnarg's Mending to half that of Holy Light, and only slightly better than Sylvan Infusion.

Healing capacity is another measure of healing effectivenss. It is the total amount of health that can be healed using a specified spell given the total amount of mana the healer has. It can be calculated as follows:

Healing capacity = (healing power/mana cost) * mana pool

In this thread (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=19080&highlight=gardeltron) I showed that mana costs are much higher for shamans than other priests due to high costs of debuffing. Since their healing power is also lower than other priests, shaman healing capacity must be much lower.

I conclude from this analysis that shaman healing is far less effective than other priests, and consequently, is unbalanced relative to GoD content.

If we're talking solely about fast heals, I wrote this up for an earlier post. Since the cast and recast times are all almost identical, you can also look at it in a purely hp healed to mana ratio and draw conclusions about where shaman should be in terms of pure fast heals.

Looking at the previous fast heals, we have:
Cleric: Supernal Light (2750 hp, 600 mana, 3.75 cast) 4.58 hp healed/1 mana.
Druid: Nature's Infusion (1750 hp, 500 mana, 3.75 cast) 3.5 hp healed/1 mana.
Shaman: Tnarg's Mending (1300 hp, 450 mana, 3.75 cast) 2.88 hp healed/1 mana.

Looking at the new fast heals in GoD, we have:
Cleric: Holy Light (3050 hp, 660 mana, 3.75 cast) 5.30 hp healed/1 mana.
Druid: Sylvan Infusion (1925 hp, 550 mana, 3.5 cast) 3.5 hp healed/1 mana.
Shaman: Erny's Mending ??????


Mathematically, a Shaman's Tnarg's healed ~47% as much as a Cleric's Supernal Light, and ~75% as much as a Druid's Nature's Infusion. A Druid's Nature's Infusion was ~64% as effective as a Cleric's Supernal Light.

Now in GoD, a Druid's Sylvan Infusion is still ~64% as effective as a Cleric's Holy Light. Along these same lines, assuming that our new heal should still be ~47% as effective as a Cleric's Holy Light, and ~75% as effective as a Druid's Sylvan Infusion, then our new heal should heal for approximately 1435 hp. Given that the mana ratio's for the most part remained the same on the new spells, with the exceptions of the cleric's, our new heal should then cost us ~500 mana.


IN SUMMATION:
IF we are going to contend that our healing power should remain in line with these numbers, our new heal will be: Erny's Mending: (1435 hp, 500 mana, 3.75 cast) 2.88 hp healed/1 mana. With these numbers, we maintain the status quo of healing ~75% as much as a Druid and ~47% as much as a Cleric.

IF we ask for a 1600 hp heal, using these mana ratios, it will look like this: Erny's Mending (1600 hp, 556 mana, 3.75 cast) 2.88 hp healed/1 mana. BUT with a 1600 hp fastheal, Shamans now heal ~83% as much as a Druid's fast heal and ~52% as much as a Cleric's.

IF we ask for a 1700 hp heal, using these mana ratios, it will look like this: Erny's Mending (1700 hp, 590 mana, 3.75 cast) 2.88 hp healed/1 mana. BUT with a 1700 hp heal, Shamans now have a fast heal that heals ~88% as much as a Druid's and ~56% as much as a Cleric's.

IF we ask for an 1800 hp heal, using these mana ratios, it will look like this: Erny's Mending (1800 hp, 625 mana, 3.75 cast) 2.88 hp healed/1 mana. BUT with an 1800 hp fast heal, Shamans now have a fast heal that heals ~94% as much as a Druid's and ~59% as well as a Cleric's.


Given that we would only be receiving a new fast heal, and not a complete heal, going with an 1800 hp heal would not be as overpowering as these numbers suggest. If we tweeked the cast time so that it was much quicker, closer to a cast time of 1-2 seconds, and then extended the recast time, we would now effectively have an emergency heal. After focus/aa, an 1800 hp heal would begin to make a dent in tanks which have >10k hp and since we're not looking to take over the position as main healer, these changes would be extremely effective and beneficial. With an 1800 hp heal, we would now at least be effective as a backup/secondary healer when things become shoddy.
