Bartmeista
best way we've found to down Doz is to fight 'em in pit. a few advantages of doing this include:

1) minimal mobs to kill. 2 pit mobs + 4ish wanderers, and yer ready to fight Doz.

2) great place to position him and casters so AE doesn't eat them alive

3) we've fought him up top before closer to door and had some rather.... unenjoyable experience with n00bs opening door. use yer imagination for the result of this one. :(

once you cleared those 6 or so mobs, rebuff and all that jazz, hop down in pit, and toss a shrink on yer MA/SA. Doz has a large aggro radius so as soon as yer puller goes near/opens the door he'll charge, pull 'em as close to that 'room' between the pit stairs as you can, which is where your MA will be. its only a single-shot dispell and 400ish AE, and you *will* get hit for a bit until atk. is called and Doz is pushed fully in the small room there, so expect the early pain, but its downhill from there. early tash is a must, though he's not too terribly tough to land a slow on.

though the 'short' fights are always the most desired, i'd agree Gloriana, most of our Doz fights are perdy darn long as of late due to him popping mid-day due to patches and our lower daytime 'population.' Doz is highly sought after so i wish you luck in finding him up at a time advantageous to the numbers you have online. i've done him with 30ish or so with my guild a number of times and helped kill him a while ago with 21 folks.

g'luck on that oogly bastage, especially on yer desired tear, i've yet to see my precious Ruby Tear drop (last piece i need for Silver Charm).

Gard is right, this is a great spot. Pull him to the right side of the that center wall with the banner on it; keep casters to the left. Vice versa I guess works fine.

I think he has 150k HP, and yes, he'll need to be reslowed unless you bring 50 people so time turgurs. I think we've done him with 30+, which is overkill.

Few other thoughts.

1) He slows very easy. Malo and a near-suicide tash is usually enuf for slow. Sometimes malosini. After he's slowed any CH cycle can keep the MA alive easy.

2) The AE is the nasty part, but it tends to occur in regular intervals. As such, I assign one person to call AEs. "Doz has AE'd, casters nuke quick." Wizzies come around wall, nuke once or twice quickly, and go hide again. This keeps you from healing casters.

3) K, he's slowed and your healers and casters are safe. Now, you have to keep those melee alive for a long fight enduring a brutal AE. Pallies are great in melee groups for group heals. If a melee is hurt, have him go hide and wait for a heal. If he has aggro you all die~

4) Bring mages. God rod is a big plus on this long fight and clerics can go oom easy without it. Have the mage grouped with a torpor shaman so he can make rod II's all fight if needed. Don't waste a cleric's attention healing the rodding mage who's making rods.

5) Really then it's just whack a mob and reclicking your gobby earrings and coldian rings to mitigate the dispells.

This, I realize, is a pretty conservative and obvious approach, but it works :)
