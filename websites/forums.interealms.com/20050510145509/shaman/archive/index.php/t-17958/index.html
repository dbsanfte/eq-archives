Binter
Basicly, I feel that it starts with Wis and ends with HP.

At level 1, Wis is the important stat for mana, but as you go up in levels and get access to Can2, Can3, Can4, then Can5, HP slowly takes over as the primary stat.

My shaman was my first character and I've only been playing about 9 months, so I had to learn that lesson the hard way. ;)

BUT, I do believe that no matter how important HP is, you should always keep an eye on your wis. If a new item will give you a net increase of 25hp but cost you 8 wis, you might want to rethink it. Wisdom isn't critical once you get Can V, but remember that Wisdom DOES affect your fizzle rate. You can get around this with the SCM AA ability (reduces fizzles as well as cutting mana cost of spells), but until you have SCM3 I'd make Wisdom at least a secondary priority.

But even with SCM3, you should always strive for the highest HP you can get combined with as good a wis as you can manage. I have several pieces of gear that has no wis, but I also do a great deal of LDoN and have a few +wis augments. I augment my no-Wis gear with Wis augs.

Augments make it a bit easier to keep your wis up while concentrating on HP.

Kriel Darkbane
HP is largely irrelevant until L 58. Obviously, more is better for purposes of staying alive, but your HP pool before L 58 has no effect on your mana regen capabilities. It doesn't in any meaningful way affect how fast you can get mana back.

At 58 it starts to matter, because you'd like to have enough HP to use Kragg's mending efficiently for cannibalize purposes. That means you need around 2500 self-buffed to get full efficiency out of it, and at that HP level it's still QUITE dangerous to use near anything aggressive. So more would be better. When you get Torpor at 60 you'll use that instead. When you get Canni V. you need over 2k unbuffed hp to stay alive when using it, and at least 3k selfbuffed (more?) so you don't get low HP aggro from using it.

All IMHO.

That pretty much summarizes my views on this topic.

I think a young shaman should aspire to reach a high wisdom for a large mana pool, as shaman aren't very mana efficient early, damage isn't that high at lower levels, and the returns on regen/canni dancing aren't huge. It's not very hard to get a nice high wisdom early in your shaman career and I feel it pays huge dividends as you have a large supply of ready mana complimented by canni/regen.

As you level up and naturally upgrade your gear I think it's good to begin to shift to higher ac/sta/hp gear around about 50ish. There's a bunch of gear that seems to be common for us in the 50-60 range that's rich in ac/sta/hp with good +wis/mana too. I believe that if you get many of these common items for in for that level range you'll be doing well.

I personally started to switch to +ac/sta/hp gear around 46th after getting my first taste of 300's hitters in PoN (any doubt about +ac/sta/hp was erased by that mighty convincing experience). I've aspired to never get below 200 wisdom and I'm near the 2k hp unbuffed mark (which I've seen recommended here by many vets).

With my style I don't take many hits unless things are generally rough - but it's nice to have the comfort level of extra hps to live to contribute to getting those rough situations under control.

I'm only 55th so I can only speculate on 60+ gear. I expect that less pieces of 60+ gear will come from the bazaar and those that do will be brutally expensive. I expect most 60+ gear to be high level drops via conquest and/or raiding (for those that raid).
