Since my guild died, I literally have zero active players in my friends list. I'll be 3-boxing the adventures, with an enchanter, mage, and shaman.

I know the general consensus is that normal LDoNs are super-easy, even in less than full groups. My gear is only fair, however, and I have precious few AAs, so they won't be a pushover for me. Does anyone have experience 3-boxing these things with minimal gear and AAs at the level-63 dungeons?

I've got the DPoC pet focus for my mage pet, which will help some.

Again, I'm just looking for some general advice, opinion, and experiences on the Miragul's theme. Given how many shaman will venture there eventually in search of the Incorporael armor, it may be a topic that helps others, as well.

Grizlor
Find a necro who's LFG, their DoT (Horror) is from Miragul's. Most of the dungeons will have either Undead (which necros can mez/slow) or casters (which they can mind wrack) or both. They also provide snare, which is something you currently lack, and respectable DPS.

The only charmable mobs are the Holgresh, all of which are casters, and they can dispell themselved which is bad. in general charming there isn't worth it. pulls can get a little messy, but your enchanter can pacify so you should be ok. a lot of the dungeons in miraguls go up and down steep slopes, which can make pulling tricky. Ice nukes also don't work too well, but poison seems to do okay.

perceus_eci
Doesn't sound like it would be too hard, but I'd suggest getting 65 and Q, and also malos if you don't have malo. Keep kei/VoQ on all 3 characters, and learn to pet pull with your shaman if you can't seem to get singles otherwise, since crowdcontrol while 3boxing would be annoying. Tank with the shaman, and use the mage and enchanter for dps. I've tanked slowed normal mobs naked before (not easily, but it was doable) so its definitely something you *can* do...keeping Q on yourself will pretty much eliminate the dps of a slowed, non-named mob. If you're really worried about it, donate for a virtue before you head in (hopping around from one camp to the next will just about guarantee you find a cleric willing to buff you in trade for a small donation and/or some peridots) and be sure to take only slaughters and collects. Invest in LR5 asap on the shaman, too, it will make you a much better tank.
