I've read the post on the board, amalgamated a few things and written some tactics for a raid I'm leading tonight. Any comments appreciated :) Image shamelessly reused from another post to these boards, I hope the author will forgive me.


Firstly, here's the map for the ground areas of CoM:

http://www.scienceofwar.net/images/rak_raid_path.jpg

To get the epic mobs (Neh and Rak) we have to kill reavers. Reavers are very very MR, wizards might land lure and I might be able to land disease slow after the unresistable debuff, but that's probably about it. When you kill a reaver, another mob can spawn. This can be another reaver, or a ghost. For certain specific reavers, that ghost can be a named mob - Ghiosk, Neh, or Rak.

To start with we will kill the reavers on the first floor (point 1 on map). The first reaver spawns Ghiosk, an earlier part of the shaman epic. He's just like the reavers, but a little tougher.

The raid force will then retreat to point E on the map, which is clear of any spawns and far away from nasty water. The puller (a monk) will bring reavers from the 2nd floor to this point, with a rogue opening the doors and operating the lift. One of those reavers (not sure exactly which) will spawn Rak - we leave Rak there for now, and don't attack. Rak is now nicely positioned for the final fight, and when (if) he gates during the fight, he'll just gate to where his reaver dies.

Finally we will all get levitate and go back inside the castle, and move up to the sky rooms. These are reached by going behind the throne room, turning right and following the passageway. There's a hidden door in the wall on the right near the end of the passage, that leads up to the sky ramps. Neh's reaver is right at the end of these sky ramps. Respawns are quite fast in the rooms, so if we need to rest we will do so on the ramps. Note that the last ramp is invisible, just to make things more fun :)

Once Neh spawns, I hand in the ring to trigger the fight. Neh is similar to Ghiosk and should be an easy kill. When she is dead, I will gate out to do a hand-in, and then return. We'll then regroup at point E, I'll hand in the tear, and we kill Rak.

Rak is strongly MR but not as bad as the reavers. He has a 600/tick AE effect, although the range is fairly low, and a 30dmg DS which must be dispelled. When I do the hand-in, our tank (with full FR and CR gear) will engage and start taunting, someone will dispell the DS and the mages will send in fire-pets. Once we have some idea how well the tank resists the AE, high level melees with high FR will be sent in if we have the healers to cope, with additional melees added as the situation demands. At 70% health or so, nukers can start (I'd suggest not using fire), using low aggro nukes. At 20% or so, let rip with all your fury.

Mage fire pets are not immune to the AE, and most definitely not immune to the ice comet he now casts - that is why we need a good high level MT. Ideally we'd have a bard to sing resist songs, if we can find one.

Alternative plan: If we end up with a kick-ass force we can probably just fight everything inside the castle and handle respawns as they occur.

To all you shaman out there - any feedback on tactics gratefully accepted :) Am I right that Rak's reaver is found up the lift? If so, are there issues with pulling it downstairs or does the monk just get on the lift and come down, and the reaver follows? Similarly, I know the skyways are cramped - is there a better place to fight the reavers on the 3rd floor, including Neh? If so, any tips on how you pull them down?

Thanks :)

I've done the fight now, and found it a walk in the park.

Rak spawned from the last reaver in the area up from the lift. A 57 monk pulled, with a rogue to operate the lift. A 55 monk stood by to tag as required, particularly if the main puller was blinded during the pull. The reavers were pulled downstairs to the ground floor, just by the throne. Casters stood with their backs to the locked door, at the opposite end of the room.

Once Rak was spawned we went up for Neh - we fought her in the middle room, again monk and rogue were used to pull her to us. Respawns weren't an issue for the force we had but there's plenty of time to kill Neh between spawns if they are a concern.

With Neh down I looted the diary, gated out for the turn-in and ran back to CoM zone in. Meanwhile the raid moved back to Rak, and once back in CoM I got a CoTH back.

I had symbol, focus and hb up, plus my own fire resist buff and resist gear. Torpored myself, did the hand in, and a recently dinged 60 paladin took aggro very quickly. I didn't even need the torpor, would have been just fine without it.

I don't think any casters were hit by the AE, it really is short range. Certainly I kept checking their health bars and they looked fine :) Rak lasted about 30 seconds or so, and in that time the AE did very little damage, even though we had some level 46-50s attending. Resisted everything I cast, but wizards reported some success with lures.

Most people resisted the AE more often than not, and we didnt' have a bard for resist songs. Level 55 monk with 160FR got hit once in about 5 waves, I was about the same.

For anyone doing this fight I'd suggest, assuming a reasonable force, that you just fight in the castle, and send everyone in quick. Just go for the quick fast kill. We had a 55 and 59 cleric, 53 druid, 61 shaman (me) and 60 paladin for healing - it was more than enough. Total force was just under 4 groups, average level probably around 55. This was massive overkill, could have done it with half that (and it would have been less messy too).

I'm afraid I didn't get any info on mage fire pets, but I really suggest people drop this idea and just use a normal raid force - 2 groups 55-60 is probably more than sufficient, provided you have a couple of clerics and some decent melee DPS.

Rathelon
The spot marked #1 on the map we call (not surprisingly) the throne room. There is one reaver there (Ghiosk's reaver), 3 (or 4) up the lift..I always forget how many exactly since I have never pulled them myself, and 1 reaver up in the very last building among the skywalks (Neh's reaver).

Rak'Ashiir spawns off the last reaver up the lift. I'm sure it's a specific spot, but like I said I've never actually pulled them myself so I dont know which spot it is. It's just the last one our monks usually bring.

For killing Neh'Ashiir, we would always go up the skywalks, clear the first building, clear the next section of skywalk, move to the second building and clear it. Then we make camp in the second building, and the pullers go get Neh's reaver from the third and bring it to us. Hardest part really is getting people to move up the first section of skywalks orderly so nobody pushes anybody off, and then controlling the fight as you enter first building. There will be several mobs to control and a very small space to fight in.

For killing Rak'Ashiir, we've fought him in a couple different places. Sometimes we go through the throne room, down the hall to the right, and pull him to the little corner room at the base of the ramp to the skywalks. If you fight there, have the casters on the ramp, and try to keep reaver pinned in one of the corners.

Other times, we have pulled him down to the throne room and pin the reaver against the wall by the throne. Once Rak is spawned (he's not aggro until given the child's tear or attacked), all the casters except mages and clerics go out onto the bridge area and fire pets kill Rak while clerics heal pets. Last time we only had one or two mages, so we just had tanks buff up and fight him also. He's not real tough now, compared to the higher level players. If you fight him in the throne room, make sure you have it timed right. Reavers are a 90 min spawn, so you want to make sure you dont take forever pulling and killing the reavers from up the lift, or Ghiosk's reaver could respawn on you at a bad time.
