I put this in the bottom of Blargx's merged thread, but it's a lot of posts to wade through. So here's my "haven't had my morning glass of OJ" idea:

EQ should look at "pay to play"

Your monthly fee is $6.95. This entitles your toons to move about freely in Antonica, Faydwer, and Odus. You want Kunark - add a buck. You want Velious - add a buck. You want Luclin? Ok we'll refund a buck.

You want PoP Tier-1? Add a buck. You want Tier-2? Ok once you pass the trials and start sending us another two bucks you get access. Tier-3? Same thing - more money.

That way, folks that USE the content can PAY for the content.

I can foresee two problems with this, one accounting, and one to use Zorlac's phrase - c0ckblocking.

1) Accounting - Now this may be tough to set up, but once the system is in place, when your monthly fee rolls around, you decide which zones you think you'll be spending most of your time in. Check the boxes, your credit card is docked and you move on.

2) C0ckblocking: This gets to the crux of the zero-sum mentality surrounding EQ. If I am PAYING for content, I damn sure want to EXPERIENCE that content. I don't expect loot to magically appear in my inventory, but if I'm paying for Tier-X and some guild is continually killing key MOBs then I as a paying customer have a right to complain.

What I * think * would happen is VI would find out VERY fast (as their revenues drop) that end game content is experienced by a small minority and paid for by a large majority.

Like I said - thought of this before my morning OJ.
