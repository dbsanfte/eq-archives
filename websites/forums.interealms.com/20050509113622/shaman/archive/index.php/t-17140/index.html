Well, after 2 years, 3 months, and a /played of 62 days, 4hrs, I am happy to announce Ding! 60!

/nostalgia on
I remember when I started EQ. All my friends wanted to make an all Faydwer guild, so i began playing as a wood elf ranger. Life was tough scraping up enough silvers and gold to buy my first backpacks, and sharpen my first tarnished swords. Straying from the trails meant a sure death at the hands of some wandering orc or goblin!

I remember the first time I saw a barbarian. He was a warrior, and he was UBER because he had 2! :eek: swords and bronze armor! He was tall and cool looking. Then I heard that they could be shaman, and that shaman got SoW at lvl 9. That did it! Korman was promptly born that day.

I remember the truly nerve-wracking run from Everfrost to meet my friends in Faydwer. Talk about heart pounding! The 20 minute run through Western Karana, dodging everything! The crazy suicide run through High Pass, hoping that someone was camping the orcs and goblins, keeping them engaged while I ran through. The suspenseful runs through Kithicor; hugging the mountainside at night praying that I'd make it through. 20 minute boat rides to Faydwer. My triumphant announcement to my wife that I had run all the way from Everfrost to Greater Faydark, and her responding with the eye-brow raise and patronizing "thats nice, honey."

Thanks for the memories! I still play about once a week. Have 2 children now that are lots of fun. Sometimes when they're in bed, and my wife is reading, I miss the "good ole days" when EQ was still new and fresh for me. I still remember being scared to death zoning into Befallen for the first time! :D

/nostalgia off

Call me crazy, or an old-timer, but what made EQ so immersive (sp?) and wonderful to me was that it DID take an hour to go to another continent; death WAS an extremely painful ordeal (corpse runs from the next continent); faction mattered; there were some things that you NEEDED help from other players. That is what separated EQ from just another first person shooter game.
