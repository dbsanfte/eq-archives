Obviously I am exaggerating a bit, but I hope you get my point. You can't expect to look back on a raid and say "I was the MVP of that raid - I'm a hero" just because you are a shaman. If you want people to juice your nuts about how important you are and what a great job you did, consider playing a warrior or cleric. One person should never make or break a raid. You are a small part of a large raid, but even still, you'd be amazed at how much of a difference you can make.

I'll just run through every mob in pofire, since that's probably the zone you'll be spending most of your raiding time in:

Charm Birds - They are a joke and no one is important on this fight except for the secondary and tertiary tanks to take over when the MT gets charmed, and the clerics because they have to pay enough attention to switch. After the slow, I usually try to dot and nuke until it is about time for the charm, then I /assist the bird and try to keep its target alive until the next tank has aggro and the clerics have their rotation going. I have saved quite a few casters who had a poorly timed nuke with my blast heals, and I help contribute to keeping the tank up.

Quavonis - This is also a pretty boring fight once the novelty of shadowstep wears off. Lots and lots of /assist blast heals.

Babnoxis - A shaman is absolutely vital for this mob. Babs has an AE mana drain with poison counters. Your primary role will be to keep all the clerics cured with nadox, and in between AE's, use disinfecting aura to cure any melees/casters who are close to death.

Magmaton - He's basically just an AoW on crack, and there isn't much for us to do here. I usually make myself useful by blast healing the MT or RT (I think he rampages? been a few months, but it might be flurry instead), but some shamans prefer to dot and nuke. I've been over the healing vs. damage thing for raids a billion times, and I assume you're smart enough to make your own decision. I prefer to heal, so that's what I do. More than a few times, I've landed a crit tnarg's for 3.4k on a tank under 10% life. That could quite possibly be a raid-saver, although it is unlikely that anyone will notice it was you that did it.

Blazzax - I love this fight, because I love AE's. They make things much more interesting. After the slow (I think he's slowable - again, it has been a while), I park myself right behind all the melees and just start healing. If MGB heals are off and just don't get cast, you can save quite a few people. A quiescence or AA'd tnargs more or less cancels out one blazzax AE, so every person you heal is someone that might not die. Even if MGB heals are landing when they are supposed to, there are always the low-hp gimps to heal :)

Arch Mage - It isn't so much shamans that are important here, it is RGCers. We have a bonus of not being as susceptable to the mana drain as most people are, since if we are completely oom we can just canni5 and we've got a workable amount of mana. In my guild, every druid or shaman with RGC buddied up with a cleric. The RGCer's job is, of course, to cure the cleric first and then himself. Every time, though, there would be some RGCers who are just plain idiots and are incapable of curing their cleric in a timely manner. I took it upon myself to cure any cleric that called out for an RGC. Whenever a cleric said "need RGC!" I was 3 out of 4 times the first person to hit them. After a few kills, I was no longer buddying up with just 1 cleric. I had three (plus myself). Through a combination of fast reaction time, fast typing, and a 23% beneficial haste item, I was the person that people trusted the most to RGC them promptly. When I left the guild I did EPs with and came to my current guild, my current guild took a night off to just fool around in EPs. Arch Mage was one of the things we killed. I had three clerics in my group, a rogue, a druid, and myself. The druid somehow died, so I was left with the three clerics to cure. I kept them all cured and near full mana. When the fight was over, they all thanked me and one of them even said something in GC about how well I did (I was still a fresh recruit then). That was a good feeling. Obviously, this isn't something specific that only a shaman can do, it is something that I personally took upon myself to excel at. If you can find something like that for yourself, you'll find being a shaman (and everquest in general) much more rewarding.

General Reparm - Mithaniel Marr on crack. He has the highest damage output of any mob until you reach VZ in time. The best thing to do is slow him as soon as possible (even a mitigated slow is a HUGE difference on a mob that does this much damage) and then blast heal your ass off. It isn't a glamorous job or one that you're likely to get praised for, but you'll contribute a lot if you can save a tank from getting two-rounded.

Fennin - Slow and cripple the bad boy, keep the clerics cured, and help blast heal (unless doting and nuking is your thing). Keeping the clerics cured if they stray too close to the AE is VITAL. If you're attentive, you can help out a lot.

I think I'm gonna stop there before I go off and describe every mob in EP's :)

I really hope you get my point, though. Shamans are a very versatile class that can do a lot of good in just about any situation. We usually don't make or break a raid, but then again most classes don't either. You just need to find a niche and fill it.

but what do you do that really important? i mean REALLY important?There is one ability which we get that no other class in the game does: Virulent Paralysis. While some may scoff at this skill, saying "A ranger can just as easily pull agro with snare, run away, and root a mob.", that costs your group and or raid DPS.

This is most noticable in a zone like Vex Thal, when you can root an incoming Eom long enough for you to slow it while enchanters mez the qua and zov's. In places like tactics, I've had Virulent Paralysis has save the raid during the Fake RZ fight. It takes time to gain agro and move a mob somewhere, or to get it mezzed, but with virulent paralysis you can just blast it away from the clerics and out of harm's way, long enough to get it under control.

I've done hard LDoNs with only root for crowd control, and Virulent Paralysis does such wonderful things as interrupt CHing/Gating level 66 mobs, splitting pulls, and all sorts of other good stuff. I honestly can't talk enough good about this skill, but if you get it and make good use of it, people will greatly appreciate it and see the value of having you along :)
