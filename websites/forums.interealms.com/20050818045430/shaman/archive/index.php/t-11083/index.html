Kazim
It's not that there's no point in having both an enchanter and a shaman, it's just that they happen to bring some of the same things to a group. With either a chanter or a shaman, you have both haste and slows covered. So with both present, neither one will need to use his full array of abilities. The shaman will handle the slows and the chanter will handle haste.

With both a shaman and a chanter, you can probably handle disasters better, but when things are under control, the mobs would go down faster with a higher damage class for one of those two slots. All depends on your play style. You can go for redundancy/safety or you can focus on efficiency/higher-risk. If you're trying to build a "perfect" group, you probably want to avoid having excessive redundancy and instead make sure you have all your bases covered.

If you go with cleric+shaman+chanter+sk+rogue, you're missing a couple of potentially useful abilities: tracking and evac. If you replaced the shaman with a druid, you'd get both, along with better fast damage and better backup healing. Since the druid can snare, you now have the option of going with warrior+monk rather than SK+rogue.

If you replaced the chanter with a bard, you get tracking and a wide array of other useful abilities, especially once the bard reaches higher levels and gets the haste and mana regen songs that stack with regular spell haste and kei. Or you could replace him with a damage class like a second rogue or a magician and get a faster kill rate or some focus items.

With a chanter and a shaman you'll probably have less deaths, but your kill rate may be a bit lower than if you replaced the shaman or chanter with a damage class.

(although, a good charm-chanter probably is as good a damage class as anyone else....)

It's all a bunch of tradeoffs. Since you're asking the question, I assume you want advice regarding what the trade-offs are, rather than platitudes like "play what you'll have fun with", so hopefully there's enough food for thought to help you choose your group.
