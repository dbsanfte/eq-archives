Tugela
Well, the cleric "light" spells are their fast heals, and the druid "renewal" spells are their CHs. There isn't an analog for shaman "salve" spells, but its pretty safe to assume that its a heal of some sort.

Clerics allready get CH at 39, so they dont need a new one. Druids are supposed to be getting upgraded heals, so, the fact that the new spells are junior forms of the "renewal" line would seem to suggest that they are going to get a progressive series of spells in the line, starting at 39 (which is where the clerics get their CH). Following that line of thought it may well be that Karana's and Tunare's will be upgraded sometime in the near future.

What might the "salve" line be? thats hard to say, but I doubt it will be filling in the gaps between greater and superior healing, because there isnt a significant gap between 49 and 51. So they are most likely a different form of heal. Shaman do get a CH type spell in Kragg's mending at level 58, so it *might* fit in that series. If the Druid renewal line goes 39 49 58 64, shaman would allready have a spell at 58. 39 and 49 would be consistent. We used to have a partial CH at around 64 too, but it got removed before the release of PoP. Just as the druid healing is supposed to be beefed up, so is ours. I think its not too unreasonable to speculate that the salve line may be a partial CH, and if thats the case Kraggs mending would probably be revised and renamed, and the old 64 spell resurrected in a new form.

Of course its also possible that they may be planning to introduce lower level HoTs for shaman. But, if they did that it would raise the issue of how the new spells would relate to stoicism and torpor, so I have doubts that they would take that form. CH still looks like the best bet to me.

What about the clerics? Since they are "giving something up" if druids and shaman get effective CH type spells they may be thrown a bone in the form of their fast heals (they dont need a new CH). Since their 2 new spells are in the "light" series, what may happen is that they could recieve a larger range of "light" spells starting at 39, with the current "light" spells being beefed up to CH levels with the same casting time and the same hp/mana efficiency they currently have. Superior healing is a 34 spell to them, so it wont be in conflict with the light series, in fact it will be a natural progression. That would increase their healing power and leave them as the undisputed "masters of healing", but at the same time allow shaman and druids a greater role as healers in a more diverse array of content.

Of course, this is all speculation ;). It will be interesting to see how it works out.
