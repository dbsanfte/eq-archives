Lornick
What if I told you I could design a zone with absolutely NO camps, NO trains, NO corpse recovery. What if I also told you I could make that same zone a.) easy to find groups b.) easy and quick to find mobs c.) challenging but fun d.) fast paced ? Am I high on crack? Maybe, but I still have a great idea for a dungeon design. *take notes Verant*

The magic of this zone is a basic concept I like to refer to as "segments". Picture this if you will... a series of "one way walls". As you approach the wall from one side it will appear semi-transparent, but if you walk through it and look back it will be solid. If you can't grasp this concept re-read that first part until you do or the rest of this might as well be written in a long since forgotten language. A segment is an area between 2 of these "one way walls". Now in every segment there are a set number of spawns. I'm thinking the magic number should be around 30, one which will be named. Now pay attention because this is why it will be impossible to camp named mobs. Enemy mobs will not respawn until all the player characters have left that segment. Once all the players have left that segment all the mobs in it will respawn instantly. In this fashion a group will have to continually move forward leaving the segment behind them free for another group to come clear. Between every 3 segments or so will contain a teleport gem back to the zone in.

The zone will be basically designed like a tree. Think of the segments as branches. The zone in is the trunk. As you climb the tree you'll reach forks where your group can chose different paths. The higher you get the harder the mobs are.

You could design a zone like this for any given level range, but I have the high end crowd in mind. To zone in you will need to have completed a key quest. But this isn't just a quest for another lame key wasting good space in your backpack. The quest giving NPC will only talk to characters 55+. The key quest will be fun and linear. In other words you won't be able to collect items for this quest out of order like the vast majority of non-sense quests in EQ. There will be absolutely NO bottlenecks for the quest. Any mobs that need to be killed will be trigger spawns and can be killed with no more than one group. In order to enforce a linear quests, the NPC's will only give you the next part of the quest if you've handed them an item required for completing the last part of the quest. I would like to make this class specific to spread the people out more, but I know this is completely beyond VI's capabilty. They would no doubt make some class quests much harder than others or so on. I'm thinking this should take the average 55+ player around 6hr play time to complete the quest. The "key" will be a necklace, ring, or other item that will have a nice balance of stats so it's worth wearing with the effect of self gate to the dungeon entrance. This will make grouping extremely easy because anyone who has the key has the ability to get there very quickly. Cast time will be long so it can't be abused by non-casting classes. (around 45 second cast time). This key will be soulbound.

I went over why there could be no trains or camping earlier. Groups will have to constantly move forward through the segments. Now I'll go over the no corpse recovery statement. At the zone in there will be a beneficial NPC ghost, cleric, or some such thing that will summon your corpse to them the second you die. This is important for a few reasons. Death will be a bigger deal to your group because they can't just rez you on the spot. They'll either have to fight their way forward with one less person. Succor, or use their new keys to gate out. If there are no clerics in zone willing to rez you, the cleric NPC will rez you for a standard 250pp.

I said earlier that named mobs will basically always be up in every segment. In order to keep items from being brought onto the server too quickly named mobs will only rarely drop nice items. The closer/easier to get to named mobs will drop items around 20percent of the time. The deeper named mobs will drop higher quality items maybe up to 75 percent of the time. The majority of the items will be no drop and either all all, or broken into larger categories like melee/priests/int casters so there is less of a chance for items to rot. (in other words, very few class specific items unless they are sellable)

Between every 3 segments there will be a small segment I'll refer to as a waypoint. This way point will divide off into 2 or 3 more segments for groups to choose from. It will also contain a teleport gem back to the zone in. In this way point will also be a No Drop item that spawns on a platform, altar or some such thing. This No Drop item will respawn almost instantly after being picked up by a player. It will essentially be a "save point". You will need to bring it along with your key to a NPC at the zone in. You turn in both items and the NPC will give you a slightly upgraded key (an extra +5hp, 2ac, +1 stat, etc.). At the zone in will be a number of color coordinated teleport stones to the waypoints throughout the zone. After you've upgraded your key you'll be able to teleport back to that way point in the dungeon. This is important so that players can go adventure in the part of the dungeon that is challenging and fun for them. It also keeps players from entering segments that there is already another group in. CoH would obviously not be possible in this zone. Otherwise it would be too easy for players to pick up the way point no drop items, and get around the death summon corpse thing.

I'd like to see these series of segments have different environments as well. One series would be underwater, another in a cave, another in a temple like setting and so on. At the end of the dungeon will be a zone into a high end dungeon with a similar theme.

This would be a raid dungeon that could support multiple raiding guilds AT THE SAME TIME. Like the dungeon that leads into it, the loot rules and such would be the same. The thing that keeps a guild from just farming this zone is that they'd have to spend a significant amount of time fighting their way to the boss mobs. Death would be a huge deal for these raids. Bodies would be summoned to zone in for this zone as well, so if key members of your raid die you'd have to start all over. Only the greatest of guilds could ever make it to the Zone Boss mob intact enough to kill him. And even this would probably be a rare thing as it would take many hours to even reach him. Of course the risk would be worth the reward. Such a daunting task would require insane loot. The endless challenge and loot would keep guilds coming back for a long time to come. The most important part is that guilds wouldn't have a reason to cause grief to each other in this zone. There would be different routes to take equally rewarding bosses. And the mobs would ALWAYS be up, so there is no need to rush in before another guild to beat them to the kill. I know theres someone who's thinking.. hey... why don't these guilds just leave one player in each segment so if their players die they can just run all the way back to their guild. Here's why, unlike the previous zone designed for groups this one will respawn the mobs in it 20minutes after the segment boss dies... so obviously this would be a bad idea.

Anyway, I spent alot of time thinking of this idea. If anyone sees some glaring flaws in my thinking or has constructive thoughts of their own please share them. I'll post this in a few other forums through out the net so maybe VI catches wind of this and uses it in some future downloadable zone Hey, I can dream right? Feel free to share my ideas with anyone, just give me my credit

Graal the Dorf
You want a camp free dungeon? Easy.

Make a large dungeon that has several linear branches, similar to sebilis but bigger. Have one central collecting point like the seb zone in that has several different branches leading from it.

The big thing is to make the spawn time for mobs about an hour. People will be forced to move to find new spawns. Because of that, the designers can design encounters instead of camps. If each branch takes about an hour to move through, the balance of spawntime to content used is perfect. If there are a total of 5 branches, the zone can support up to 30 people at once without fighting over spawns if it is designed for each branch to hold one xp group.

One of the things I hear VI is doing in PoP is using virtual zones. A single group gets ported into a zone that is created on demand. If another group wants to fight in that same zone, they get ported into a copy of the same zone. This will allow VI to create truely challenging one group encounters with rewards to match. Because of the way the zone is set up, no one can overpower an encounter by bringing more than one group. I think that is a very good idea. It would be nice if they had something similiar for a single person encounter.
