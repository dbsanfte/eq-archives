Soriathus Serpentine
You need an AGP slot (the brownish looking slots) for any of the newer good video cards. There are PCI versions, but you are not going to get nearly the performace if you have AGP. They can run at AGP 1x or 2x depending on the brand, but prefered AGP 4x. This is basically the speed/generation of the AGP slot on your motherboard. What is your motherboard/processor? Pentium II, Pentium 3, Pentium 4, AMD K6-2, AMD K6-3, Athalon, Athalon XP? What's the Mhz of the processor? How much ram do you have? Rrguardless of the video card you want 512MB of ram to reliably run the new models. I personally have a gig of PC133 SDRAM and an AMD Athalon 850 with the GeForce 3 TI 200.

P.S. I completely AGREE, the MX series is CRAP. Don't fall into the marketing trap, the GeForce 3 TI 200's are better by FAR than the GeForce 4 MX series. GeForce 4 TI series rocks, but the MX is CRAP!
