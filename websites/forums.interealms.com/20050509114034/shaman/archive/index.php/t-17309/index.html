General:

-A change to the aggro generated by certain spells used frequently by high level plate hybrids has been put in place. Spells that do not affect a mob will generate no hate whatsoever. In addition, the aggro of certain low-level DoTs has been reduced. We think that Paladins and Shadow Knights should retain their aggro-locking abilities, just not in perpetuity. If you want to lock a mob on you, it will now cost you a decent amount of mana.

-The Lull line has been deleted from the game for all classes that can use it. Monks around the world rejoice.

-The Bard AA ability Fading Memories has been changed to a self-only deathtouch. Monks around the world rejoice more. (Seriously, just delete the ability and refund the points.)

-We have retuned melees to bring some balance to these classes. Warriors now have their own superior mitigation table. Equipment has been tuned such that hybrids can max out at approximately 80% of an equivalently equipped warrior's HP. Monks, now that they can pull, will receive additional tuning once we evaluate the effects of other changes.

-Slow mitigation has now been changed to be somewhat more reasonable. It is now subratctive instead of multiplicative - i.e., it subtracts a certain % from the slow that lands on the mob. This allows shaman to actually be the preferred slowers for people who can do simple math. We have retuned certain encounters to account for this change.

-The level 65 beastlord slow has been reduced to 55%.

-Charmed pets have had their damage capped at a reasonable level. We finally realized that it is ludicrous to have a support class (enchanters) outdamaging entire groups.

-We have increased the range of the shaman epic to bring it in line with the other epics.

-The cost of alchemy components has been radically reduced. All components now cost 1/10 of what they used to.

-The number of detrimental effect slots for mobs has been increased to 60.


DoT and debuff changes:

-The aggro for DoT spells has been reduced to be in line with direct damage spells of the same magnitude.

-Bane has been given a -50 resist mod. We realized that a spell designed to do fast damage isn't that useful if you have to cast a 5 second, 400 mana spell first in order to make it land reliably.

-All DoT spells with a direct damage component have had the DD effect removed.


Shaman-specific changes:

-The duration of all shaman buffs has been increased to be in line with the buffs of other classes. (In other words, Focus of the Seventh now lasts as long as Hand of Virtue, and so on.)

-Talisman of Alacrity has been changed to Talisman of the Wind, and now gives 60% haste.

-You will now be able to shrink anyone in your raid without being in the same group. A confirmation box has been added to the shrink spell, much like the wizard Translocate spell line.

-A number of AA changes for shaman have been added. Alchemy Mastery has been eliminated, and the designer who put it in the game has been fed to a pack of starving wolves. New abilities added/revised:

Innate Malisement (3/6/9), -5/-15/-30 resist mod on all spells cast by the shaman. (Not my idea, but a DAMN good one worth repeating over and over again.)

Quick Debuff/DoT: (3/6/9): Reduce the casting time on detrimental spells by 10/25/50%. Finally, you won't have time to go make a sandwich after casting Malos.

Spell Casting Fury: This ability now affects DoTs as well. A DoT has a chance to land as a critical, doubling the damage every tick for the duration of the spell.

Rabid Bear: Retuned so that it's not a waste of points. It has been made to be like the mage elemental form ability. Hastened Rabidity has been removed.
