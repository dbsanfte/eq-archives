Goldark
I keep hearing how good the Shaman/Bard combo is but could someone be a little more specific?

Bards are masters at soloing with AE kiting, swarmkiting and chant kiting. Frankly, there's not much a shaman can add to a skilled bard when it comes to xp.

A bard's biggest problem in a duo is holding aggro, so you will most often either tank or kite with the shaman. From levels 30-45 or so you can also do well with fear kiting (bard snares and fears whilst meleeing, shaman can use DoTs). From about lvl 30+ the bard can charm mobs, which can be used in numerous inventive ways.

Bard works well with shaman as their health/mana regen song is doubly effective with shaman canni. Shaman buffs help the bard (dex, hp, cha, haste) a lot too. Shaman provides slowing and healing ability, bard can pull very well, snare, fear, mezz, charm (at later levels) - between the pair of you you can do just about all things except rezz and teleport - but with selos you will have few problems moving about anyway

What equipment choices are more important, which stats?

Shaman: Focus items (mana preservation, burning affliction, spell haste). HP, Regen, FT (mana regen)

Bard: HP, Instrument modifiers, Weapons (up to level 30 at least), Dex, Cha (moreso at higher levels). At very high levels, int/mana and FT are also important. See www.eqdiva.com, they are very helpul there if you post in Nillipus's Newbies section.

Klemydia
Learn to AE kite when while you're still low level and don't have to worry about losing xp or dealing with a CR.

At level 5 when you get Selo's Accelerando, go to Crushbone. Twist Selo's and chords of dissonance as you run around the top of trainer hill, aggroing everything. Start with a big circle and slowly close it in until all the mobs are bunched together. Keep the circle tight, but not so tight as they're pounding you. Work on the timing of your CoD twist and try to have it fire off when the majority are in the range of the AE. After an hour, maybe less, you should have the hang of it.

As a bard, you'll want good instruments, preferably ones that go in your primary hand. Look for weapons and armor with instrument mods. Save up for a Trueborn Drum of Dark Rites (or whatever it's called) or if you really have enough scratch, a Thundrous Drum of Karana, as later in life you'll be kiting with your chants (which use the percussion skill).

Once you and your lady hit the 20s, I'd recommend doing the needlites in Echo Caverns since they hit fast and not too hard, and are great for defense skill ups.

Oh, and get a Combine Lute from Katta (or the bazaar) as it's cheap and has a decent mod.
Kelorm
^ Agreed about learning how to AE kite early. I'd suggest the Field of Bone Excavators. They are social, and have the ideal AE kiting AI (Don't run when low hp, will add if an aggroed one passes by one, etc). You can do them as low as level 6, even if they are deep red to you. I did a run of them at level 6 or so, it took about twenty minutes, but I went up to about level 11 from that single pull (I got all of the excavator mobs in the area).

Anyway, a suggestion about AE kiting, you need to play conservatively ever since they put in the new collision system. What you do is collect all the mobs, then I roll them into a nice ball by running in wide circles. After that I run in a straight line, start my DoT song, once the wind-up time has gone down about two bubbles I make a turn backwards at about a fourty-five degree angle. That is a base of where to start, and from there you adjust when you turn during the song's wind-up, and what degree you turn at. Chords of Dissonance has a low radius so you have to cut it pretty close, but start out far and slowly make your way closer.
