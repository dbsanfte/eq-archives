Juujuu
Unfortunately, I think that other player might have been teasing you a bit. I don't know of anyone that buys any armor from vendors, except for leather and cloth armor at very low levels.

The reason that other player has really nice looking gear at low levels is because that play most likely has a higher level character that acquired the gear they are wearing. This is termed "twinking". For example, I have a level 60 shaman that has acquired much of the gear that my level 50+ paladin and monk wear. Much of the gear my monk is wearing is the same stuff I put on him when he was only level 15 or so.

Since you don't have a high level character to acquire the gear you want, the best you can do is hunt what you can for money, save up, and buy the stuff that you can. East Commons or North Freeport or Greater Faydark are the most common "auction zones" in EQ where lots of players gather knowing that other people are also gathering there for the sole purpose of tryinig to buy or sell stuff. You might also want to try joining the auction chat channel to look for gear you can afford.

All of us had to level up our first characters without any additional twinking. You actually have it much easier, as many higher level pieces of gear are much more available and thus cheaper to acquire.

Loot everything you see and sell it to vendors...those crappy copper pieces will add up. Another good investment as a Barb Shaman is a sewing kit. Kill spiders and get silks and start to make swatches, you can sell those to the vendor or begin to make raw silk armor and sell those in the Halas tunnel. I made quite a bit a plat doing that and bought a shield a minor poker, and began to save for leather armor pieces. A tailor hired me to get some hides and he'd make some armor for me since he was practicing his tailoring skill anyway. I had a full suit and I got it for free just by hunting things I was getting exp for anyway.
So a sewing kit is a great low level investment.
