izmalis
With so much discussion (much of it deservedly negative) over all the problems L65 non-raiders are encountering, I thought it would be nice to list the changes SOE has made post-Luclin that have improved the game noticeably for the L65 non-raider, whether by design or chance.

First some explanations on the guidelines. These are only changes applicable to the L65 non-raider, not lower level (e.g. LoY expansion is disqualified based on level) and they must not be changes that also benefit the raid player. For example, PoK books, while nice, did not benefit the non-raid player primarily vis a vis the raid player. By this same token AA abilities, LDoN augs and spells wouldn't count either as they benefit both. Only list things that made the game a more positive experience for the L65 non-raid player from which the raid player did not benefit significantly. These are things for which SOE deserves praise and provides a blueprint of what can and should be done. Notable non-mention: ornate armor due to the fact that its primary drop location is behind a flagged zone and raiders can (and do) make lots of plat selling it in the bazaar (i.e., they benefit from it even if they won't wear it).

With that out of the way, here's what I've come up with so far:
spell research: Although only L60 and under spells, this was a huge boon to the L65 non-raid player as there are several spells of value that are difficult to get in other ways (malo, PE, AoDK come to mind). I was skeptical at first but came to love this.
The unlocking of several tier 2 and tier 3 zones in PoP. An excellent start.
LDoN hard gear: much nicer gear than is commonly available in the bazaar that can be earned by the L65 non-raid player but of little benefit to the raider who has much better.
Single group quest access to Plane of Tactics: while I don't like flagged zones, at least providing an alternate means of access makes this zone closer to Howling Stones vice Sol Ro Tower for non-raider access.
8th shawl: I can't remember if this was post-Luclin or not, but despite being majorly tedious this was a very nice item. Raiders now have better so it's doubtful they would do this quest to actually wear the item.
LDoN armor: again, very nice if you have the patience to slog through the points necessary to buy it. Technically a raid player could benefit from this also but since the disparity is so great it is unlikely.

What else am I missing? Follow the guidelines and list more positive things post-Luclin specifically for the L65 non-raid player.

I actually don't like soloing in Qinimi. I find it harder than in Riwwi, to be honest, the rare-drop loot is poor, and the rares have bad loot table and are out-of-scale challenges. That said, its easy to get to and easy to zone out of, so its worth considering to practice against GoD mobs. Some things tend to be more resistant than others. The Qinimi nocs are sometimes stubborn, in particular. I pull them with Malos with a dog out. Then, I'll send the dog to attack - Malos is low enough aggro that the noc will "stick" to the dog, letting me land a slow, and then hopefully a root. If all goes well, I can even /pet hold the dog off before he dies (obviously, he's well buffed before attempting this). It helps a lot, and its a useful skill to learn whenever you're dealing with hard-hitting rootable mobs that don't summon. As long as you keep them slowed, they aren't too bad - even a 6k shaman should be able to channel a re-root after break without feeling in too much danger unless you have a particularly bad series of resists/interrupts/procs.

Barindu *is* more challenging, and pulling there is something of an art rather than a science, although there are a few safe (or nearly so) spots to set up a group in. As far as soloing Barindu goes, I set up at the Barindu/Riwwi zoneline (its less used, so you won't train raid guild members on the way to Vxed/Tipt if you screw up), and pull the first half-dozen or so mobs to root/rot. Here the pet is nearly essential - these mobs do hit hard enough for things to get nasty fast. Just remember there's a zoneline right there, and don't be afraid to use it. Unless you're well-geared, used to shaman-kiting, and a little stupid, don't pull Barindu nocs - they are immune to root.

Riwwi, on the other hand, is actually a much easier zone than Barindu, despite its admittedly mob-swarm appearance. There are a few very nice places to set up in Riwwi to farm, and the rewards are worth the time. It helps to remember that every rare I've encountered is root/rottable (except the raid mob Taskmistress Krisz, and I've never seen the named Ra'Tuk Yadda Yadda to know for certain about him). In fact, many of the rares hit hard enough that root/rotting is really the only way to solo them. But, hey, if dogdog can die for the cause getting them slowed and rooted, that's 80% of the battle. While some mob types resist the useful dots more than others, nothing seems totally immune to any of the spell lines, and a Malos will often take care of any lingering resistance issues.

I won't talk about strategies for soloing Ferubi, Sewers or Vxed mobs here. Since this is directed to a 6k-6500 shaman, the goal should probably be more gear before trying to solo for a Chanting Stone Mask, for example. Still, Riwwi provides a really solid BP and a lot of nice drops off the rares (Gargantuan Armplates are the one that comes first to mind even though I'm very tired atm - there are lots more), and Barindu provides what is frankly at least a VT-level ring as a random rare drop (probably 1:300 or so, your mileage with the random number generator may vary, spend a couple hours here per day of playtime if you're serious about it, and you'll likely see it in a week or 2 - sounds long, but its 100/100 FT2 and worth the time).

With a ranger, druid, or necro partner, all this becomes largely trivial. Snare is simply a shaman's best friend in GoD, and I duo and group with snare classes at every opportunity. If you've got a solid mana pool and some healing improvements (focus and/or aa) you might try tank/shaman duo also, especially in Riwwi. The old shaman/monk pairing doesn't hold up as well, sadly. The ATK of these mobs tends to be set up to make the mitigation of plate classes more important.

Variations on this don't work well. For example, lets say you add to the danger of a zone... it's riskier... it's more of a challenge... sounds good... but then if the loot isn't better than BoT... or the XP is lower... people won't go... oh sure, a couple times to check it out... but won't stay.

Any zone with less XP than BoT pretty much has to have better loot... and Ornate is near the top of the food chain for a lot of non raiders... it's valuble to sell... and has effects that almost everyone can use.

Droga was a great upgrade. The quests provided valuble upgrades for many... the risk was very low... but the xp was just "good". So people get their quest stuff and leave except for the farmers and a rare group.

Also keep in mind... people like zones where things aren't "required" as such.

Again looking at Droga... you don't need a chanter/bard at all. You really don't even need a slower. My shaman could have tanked if I wanted... point being that you don't NEED a 10k warrior to go there.

It's an excellent... "I have a random group of friends and we wanna go xp somewhere without worrying about our classes".

BoT is "almost" like that. You don't really .... "need" any class... but a more "ideal looking" group will do much better... but since BoT is the center of pickup group activity... you can almost always find someone to fill your needs.

It's actually very easy for SoE to spread people out if they wanted to. They just have to keep those things in mind.
