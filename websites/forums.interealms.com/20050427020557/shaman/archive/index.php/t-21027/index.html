Marsinger Walkthrough (http://www.guildseofon.com/raidinghate.html)

The above link is a great guide to the Marsinger Quests. Unfortunately, if you plan to do this solo it is going to take a very, very, VERY long time at a solo kill rate. Actually, the first level drop will probably take you the longest to get, the pieces for wis casters are just that rare :(

On the other hand, take a group with ya and slaughter everything you see, working your way through the tiers as the quest pieces drop. Solo, with elemental+ gear, a Shaman could probably do it all the mobs associated with the quest if he really had pulling the area down pat. In tier3-4 you will get a lot of adds without fd, paci, or the like. However the mobs aren't extremely resistant, the named don't summon, and half the entire zone spends the whole fight casting anyway :)

Disease slow the golems and the dracoliches require you to be in melee range to land spells.

Also, if anyone else is in the zone, be kind to shout out any quest pieces that aren't wis you run across as others might be looking for them. Doing so can really pay off when the higher tier groups happen to get a nice wis drop that is free to loot :)

Kiisekai
Doing these quests at the moment myself. I have the first one done and all the stuff to turn in for three and four... just need a crest to finish it all up. Tier 1 will be cake (by the way, I'm pretty certain the ranger quest piece does not drop outside tier 1 nameds) but it may take you a while to get the signet. Tier 2 and 3 are pretty easily soloable, tier 2 golems might give ya a problem or two but nothing major. No trash mob or mini-boss (note: mini-boss does not include mobs like maestro, some quest mobs, hand, etc.) tier 1-4 summons. Therefore, tier 4 is soloable... but it gets interesting. There is no real good spot to pull singles to that doesn't have a wanderer or a spot where you'll get a double pull nearby. The thing about tier 4 mobs is they hit for 600+ and fairly quickly and can nuke or HT pretty hard or Cheal depending on class. So, if you get resists on root/slow or adds or an ill-timed root break, etc. you will probably be in for a quick trip to bind and an annoying CR. Bring a tank partner and it will go much easier, a SK friend and I duo tier 4 all the time and the exp is nothing to sneeze at. At least one of the tier 4 named you can clear to and pull with singles so it is possible to do the final quest solo but I wouldn't reccomend it. In the end, the prohibitive factor is not the zone difficulty, it's the painfully low drop rate. I've been killing tier 2-4 clerics on and off for a month or two now and have seen no crests, the signet for the first one took quite a while as well and the only reason i have the drops for the third and fourth quests is because i got lucky while I was camping tier 4 for a crest.

Dragging up an older post, but it caught my eye since we've made it a guild objective to get everybody their Marsinger's Gem in my smallish guild, and we're all just about done.

Really if you have a full group that can dedicate time together, it can go by fairly quickly. It took me about a week with about 3 hours a night to finish up mine. While you're in hate and killing the tier 3/4 nameds you'll be getting BoT level exp on normal mobs, and when you hit a tier 4 named you'll notice a huge spike (talking like 6%-9% aaxp). The loot is decent off of named, I walked away with about 25k's worth a night. You're gonna want to get to the upper tier named's as quickly as you can, cause they can drop lower tier stuff as well. Just set a cycle, and go from one named spawn to the next (we did about 4-5). You'll probably end up getting all the books/drops for last gem, and end up getting stuck on the rare for like the 2nd or 3rd gem. I did the quest a few months ago, and still don't mind hoping up to go with guildies to get there's. BTW remember old world armor drops can be turned into pop guildmasters for gems (usually a diamond).
