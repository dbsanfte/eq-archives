Gloriana
Nothing is to stop you from Grandmastering a trade skill. For example, a grandmaster potter in my guild traded idols and steins for all the other gifts for her earring.

I liked the teamwork, my guild did an "assembly line" for a week or so. We were able to make everything in house reliably except for the Robe (our highest tailor was 175).

Those without trade skills did the foraging, the collecting, the imbuing, the buying, the traveling to pick up items. Those with minor trade skills helped out with the lesser combines. With my 140 brewing I was able to make mistletoe tempers and I made around 300, with my 175 smithing I helped make silver chains for the robe. With my low jewelry skill, I was able to help out and make champagne magnums. Being bound in the firepot room, I went to RV and bought grape yeast, went to Freeport and bought elven wine and mistletoe, and made many trips to EC to buy scent of marr. I made celestial essences until my fingers nearly fell off.

It was a great whirlwind of fun and I was very impressed by the capable people in my guild, those that led the effort and those with the hard to get skills that made it happen.

Average guild contribution for earring: 5K

I'm looking forward to the next one!
