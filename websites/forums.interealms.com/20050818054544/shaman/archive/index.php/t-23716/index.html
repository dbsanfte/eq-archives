Considering that EQLive was developed over 5 years ago with the intent of lasting not longer than a year, it's incredibly impressive that it's held up this long. Not to mention the fact that it has changed developers.

The fact of the matter is this. EQLive was not created with the intent to last as long as it has. Combine this with the fact that there are hundreds of thousands of customers, all with different goals and priorities, and a small but important population of people who chew through content in a matter of months, keeping everybody happy while at the same time providing something challenging AND interesting is extremely difficult.

We may hate Sony, but many of the problems today aren't so much problems they have created so much as they are problems brought about by an outdated overstretched game model - one that is unable to meet the many demands of its customers with the expediency and efficiency they desire, while still turning a profit for Sony.
