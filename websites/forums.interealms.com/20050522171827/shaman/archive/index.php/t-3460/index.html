Irksome
www.eqtraders.com - best tradeskills info and discussions.

For skill INCREASES, you want to max either your widsom or intelligence (the highest of those two is what is used). Some theorize that Dex is a secondary check, so you might as well max buff your dex too.

For SUCCESSES, the only thing checked is your skill.

Alchemy is a money pit. Don't expect to ever make money with it, or be realistic about the investment required before it starts paying off. I was very active selling SoW potions, back before AA skills and TBoots, and never came close to making up the investment.

The re-sell of potions to vendors is horrible. Returns less than 10%. There is a trivial chart at EQTraders that should become your best friend: find the cheapest potion that is above your current skill, and make that to skill up. Eventually, your only real option becomes Titan potions, at about 11 plat per attempt (trivial above the max skill of 180).

Good luck!

Goth, I don't want to be rude, and that is not my intent here. You are simply wrong.

Read the EQTraders site a bit, there have been tons of tests done on this. The ONLY thing that affects success is your skill. All variance is just the random number generator and a human's ability to see patterns in randomness. This is probably the most hotly debated subject in tradeskills, but it has been proven both in objective tests and by discussions with VI employees.

Same is true for the skill-ups. ALL tradeskills, including alchemy, will check the HIGHER of int and wis for skill-up chances. Some tradeskills have secondary stats that are known, others are just theorized. We know for sure that blacksmithing has a secondary check on strength, for instance, as it was uttered by VI at a Fan Faire. I believe there is one other skill where the skill-up chance has a secondary check on dex. Nobody has proven (or pried from VI) that alchemy has a secondary check, but I personally (when I still was able to skill up) max buffed myself (I can get to or near 255 on str/sta/dex/wis).

This is not intended as a flame, and if you believe I am wrong I respect your right to believe as you will. I'm not likely to reply to any flames directed my way on this subject, and I encourage everyone to make up their own minds on things like this. Until we can see the actual code that EQ uses, no one can be certain, so I of course could be completely wrong.

As always, your mileage may vary.

Irksome
Alchemy is very useful, and most raiding guilds need a source for alchemy items.

Shrink potions - all zones. Pack 50 people into a tight space (say, around a single mob) and shrink is invaluable. There is no way to shrink someone in any other way in many zones.

Damage shield potions - 31 damage added to every hit is VERY useful for mobs that cannot be slowed.

For mid-level guilds, where the gear may not be uber, and levels not at 60, the resist potions are very handy. Trak kills go easier when you have an extra 25 poison resistance, for instance.

For sub-50 players, particularly the non-twinked crowd, SoW potions are still economical and effective. They sell for about 10 plat per dose, and can easily save your life running thru a tough zone without invis.

For all non-gating classes, gate potions are extraodinarily useful. I have many people looking for Swamp of Innothule and Field of Bone potions, as they both help the person get out of a bad situation (LD at a raid, stuck deep in a dungeon), and can help them get to either Sebilis or Cazic Thule.


I'm a 180 skill alchemist, and I rarely try to sell potions for a profit. I make probably 100+ potions per week, primarily for guild use, with various special requests from friends (and the occasional random stranger).
