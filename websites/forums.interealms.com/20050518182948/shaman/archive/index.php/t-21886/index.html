Uggok
I think that Alyn has a valid point.

A mob's max hit is not based on a percentage of your healer's base heal. It is a fixed amount. The number of hit points you heal is what is important, not the ratio of your focused heal to your base one. Also consider that when SoE designs new content, they keep max focused heals in mind (if they didn't, healing would be trivial with max focused heals).

Sony has gotten in trouble before with perfectly scaling abilities being overpowered and magnifying class balance problems (see: slows, complete heal, defensive). Focus effects that scale raise the same problems. Then again, focus items that add a fixed amount also raise problems. The core issue is that EQ was never designed with focus effects in mind. We get the shaft coming and going because the 2 weak abilities we have (damage and healing) get increased by a percentage from focus effects, therefore we get the lowest absolute increase in these skills. On the other hand, the one thing that we are best at (slow, by a little) gets reduced by a percentage via mitigation, thereby giving us the largest absolute decrease in that skill.

Now, I think that Jaradis is right that the main problem is that our base heals are so pitiful. But even if that was fixed, powerful focus items could put us back behind the 8-ball. Focus effects magnify the difference now. They will still magnify a smaller difference. But SoE could design content where that difference matters, which would be bad for us.

It is a minor point, but a legitimate one.
