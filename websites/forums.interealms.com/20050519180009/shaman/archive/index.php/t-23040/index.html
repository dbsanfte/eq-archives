howler
Huge changes in this patch. So many aspects of this patch have been needed for years, but of all of them, this whole respawning in Shadowrest is quite possibly the worst idea ever. We could sit here all day and yell back and forth about whether camping is right or not, but I really see this change impacting the game in a negative way.

Scenario: Bard runs through a zone to train a competing guild/team who is about to kill a mob needed for the next level of progression. Though he might not die, the mission is essentially a suicidal one, wherein he's risking the time it takes to get back his gear to stop that guild. On servers where you can't train, it becomes a guild war over that spawn where the ganking guild has the same type of risks. But whereas before you would find people in your guild not willing to risk their necks, because they knew they probably wouldn't get their gear until the next morning, now it won't matter. The gankers have absolutely nothing to lose now but 15 minutes of their time, spent in Shadowhaven with rez effects. Before, the system penalized the ganking guild for pvp'ing, now it penalizes the guild set up at the spawn point.

If you thought this patch was intended to solve griefing at the lower levels, I think you're in for a rude awakening when you find out it just became worse at the high end. In pvp, the consequences that come with death (recovering your gear, losing your spawn, loss of time, etc) create the fear of death. The fear of death is represented in so many implicit and explicit actions taken by zekers. It encompasses the things you do without thinking (hitting / to see who is in the zone) to the zone routes you take to get to a spawn/raid to the way you are so anal about buff order. You begin chipping away at those consequences, making them less meaningful, and you're going to turn griefing, especially at the higher levels, into the norm.
