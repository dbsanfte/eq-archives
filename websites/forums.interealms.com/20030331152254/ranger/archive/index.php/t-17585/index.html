This has got to be the Great Spelling Booboo of 2003, and we're not even through January yet. Everywhere I look people are doing this. It makes me think half the world slept through spelling/grammar class and makes me wonder about our education standards.

It's A LOT, not ALOT, dammit!

If you really can't remember to leave the space in there, use the old-lady-english-teacher phrase: "There is a lot of space between "a" and "lot".

FYI - the word "alot", which is really "allot" misspelled, means to give a certain amount of something. (i.e. I alloted ten minutes for my speech.)

This wouldn't bother me so much if it were isolated cases, but lately it seems nearly everyone using the Net or any message board I visit has had this word engraved into their brain as the proper way. ARGH!

Katalaeia
Yuma, as far as I can tell, when referring to a piece of land, it is in fact a shortening of allotment, but you would still say, "My parents bought the empty lot down the street.", not, "My parents bought the empty allot (or allotment) down the street." The latter just sounds goofy to me.

Of course when referring to land or anything else context is important. If you wanted to say that your parents bought a huge piece of land, you would say they bought a lot of land. By the same token, if you wanted to say your parents just acquired some new land, you would also say they bought a lot (meaning allotment) of land. The context of the surrounding sentences would determine the meaning of "a lot".
