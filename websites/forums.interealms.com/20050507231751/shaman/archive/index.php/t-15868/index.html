Pumyr
Being summoned is simple, by whatever method if you are not in immediate close proximity to a mob, but are on top of it's hate list (sitting is nice hate) then you will be summoned. For a mob to summon it must obey a few rules.

1. Be summon enabled (not necessarily of any particular level)
2. Be damaged to the threshold at which it summons (varies from mob to mob, but 5% damage is a fair guideline)
3. Be far enough away from the centre of the mob for it to summon you this again varies from mob to mob, some will summon you if you are almost stood on their head, others won't summon till you are a fair way off.

Basically get to the top of the aggro list of a summoning mob and you can't evade it (unless like me your main is a rogue of course).

PennyAntics
if a mob is damaged by x amount, it will summon the person at the top of the hate list if they are out of melee range.

For example, if your group has melee engaged with the mob and you attempt slow at 95% from a distance, you are likely to be at the very top of the hate list and thus be immediately summoned despite doing no damage to the mob yourself. There is a lot of dispute over what the value of the "x" variable is, weather it be a certain number or percentage.

This is why the "nuke anti-kite" works. Lots of guilds have a bard or other class kite a large named mob so that they have technically engaged it first so therefore they can kill it. If however that guild has competition and that competition decides to nuke the mob being kited, it will be damaged enough to summon and the bard will go squishy.

EDIT: one other thing a lot of people don't know is that all mobs get only ONE summon per tick (6 seconds). If your main tank decides to reposition his mob and gets summoned while you simultaneously cast slow thinking "I wont cause ping-pong, I'll just get summoned if I get too much aggro" you will not get summoned, the mob will run after you and ping pong. and if you run away, 6 seconds later you can expect to get summoned =)
