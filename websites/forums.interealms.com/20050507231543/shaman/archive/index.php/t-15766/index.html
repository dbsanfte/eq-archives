Steelbear
Rains only do a maximum of 3 waves of damage to a single target, or 4 hits if there are multiple targets. For example, if you cast it on a cluster of 4 mobs, you will get only 1 wave of damage against each one. If you cast on a single mob, you only get 3 waves of damage. If you cast on a pair of mobs, you'll get 2 waves against each one (this assuming the mobs always stay under the rain area).

The waves do take time so it is possible that a moving mob could get hit, move away, and come back and be hit again - but usually if they move out from under the rain area you'll lose the rest of the damage. If you get caught under the rain area, you will take damage as well - and I believe that damage is not counted against the number of waves/hits. Pets and other pc's (on blue servers) do not take any damage or subtract from the number of waves/hits.

Rains also do not generate as much aggro as our dots or slow, so they are very good spells to use in a group once the melees have a mob occupied. Though, resistances vary and often some or all the waves get resisted entirely.

The druids were pulling and kiting while everyone else sent in pets/charmed pets or dotted or blasted. I started casting rain on the pull if they had an add, and that got the wizard and druids started doing the same.
