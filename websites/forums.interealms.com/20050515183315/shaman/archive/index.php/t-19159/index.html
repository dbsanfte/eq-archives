To the Sony Online Entertainment Developers . . .

The shaman community has been trying for the last couple of years to come up with some form of unified commentary to express unhappiness with the state of our class. This effort has met with failure a number of times, probably due to the sheer utility of the class. We can solo, group and raid—so people with different play styles have different views of the basic problems. The net result is that for the most part, we can’t agree on much of anything, other than the fact that the shaman, as a class, has issues.

Shamans are plagued with the perception that we are being punished for the powerful combination of slow and torpor back in the Kunark expansion. Each and every expansion we fall farther behind other classes in healing and damage (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=19149)

With the impetus of the current spells and abilities from Gates of Discord (“GoD”), discontent seems to have increased substantially. The general consensus is that we have reached our pinnacle of usefulness in Kunark and have wound up at the bottom of the heap in just about any measure of class balance. Mediocrity compared to the other specialized classes we can understand. Being dead last in basically all categories is a bit much of a penalty for utility.

Another difficulty the shaman are facing is that we are suffering deficiencies in so many areas, that it looks like we’re asking for the world if we suggest fixes for them! However, in terms of balance, fixing one or two of the issues would effectively negate four or five others. For instance, addressing damage per second (“dps”) covers nukes, damage over time spells (“DoTs”) and pets concurrently. A fix in one of those areas might make changes in the others overpowering. Accordingly, we decided to post a list of problems we feel need to be addressed desperately. After all, you folks at SoE have to decide what constitutes balance in the end.

We are definitely not asking for every suggestion to be fulfilled!

Top 5 Issues as a result of the What do we need the most? (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=19072&pagenumber=1) Poll


1. Healing

Shaman healing is extremely weak when we have to debuff mobs before each encounter. This weakness is compounded greatly with mitigation. It is most severe on mobs that have high dps but die relatively quickly. That is why PoP-type mobs, with high dps and low hp relative to older expansion mobs, have marginalized shaman healing and slowing ability. We often fall into the dreaded "tnarg chain" cycle: a slow is resisted and the main tank takes so much damage we can't stop casting tnarg long enough to attempt another slow.

Shaman healing is way behind other priests even when slow is factored in. Healing needs to be re-evaluated in comparison to other healing classes, including paladins. This review should include cast time, mana cost and amount healed. Our heal over time spells need improvements and we need access to a group heal. Exp group mobs hit harder in one round than we can heal.

* No group healing ability
* Smallest direct healing ability
* No upgrades to our amount healed in our heal over time line, which we received in Kunark.
* Proposed Quiescence upgrade with the new expansion is insufficient, an extra 60 hitpoints per tick can't match the increased mob damage
* No MGBable/AE heal, like other priest classes and our hybrid class, beastlords.


Healing Suggestions:

* Some sort of group heal or group heal over time (with MGB option).
* Some sort of upgrade to or improvement of Quiescence.
* Some sort of faster, larger, and more efficient quick single-target heal, more on par with PoP mob damage
* AA HOT ability


2. Mana Regen

In the past, cannibalize and our ability to regen mana was blamed for shortcomings in other areas, such as mana cost and duration of our buffs and manacost of heals. With the new and widespread abilities in passive manaregen for other classes, an upgrade to cannibalize 4 needs to be implemented. We are the only class forced into a position where we have to sacrifice so much to regain mana. Most other classes simply use a buff slot.

* Other classes get mana regeneration methods that are nearly equivalent to shamans
* FT and other enhancements increase mana regen rate across all mana using classes
* Mana regeneration spells are widely available to other classes
* Cannibalize is active mana-regen and has a large number of penalties when compared to passive mana-regen (can't cast any other spells while regaining mana, can't move while regaining mana, cannot engage in any social interaction while regenning mana due to constant clicking)
* Permanent Spell Slot required
* The current Canni upgrade is unsatisfactory and does not address any of our concerns.


Mana Regen Suggestions:

* New AA ability for Cannabalize.
* An upgrade or addition to Cannibalization, allowing (at the same ratio), greater or more frequent conversions of HP to Mana
Alternately, some across the board adjustment in mana cost for our spells, considering the cost of similar spells to other classes
* Upgrade Can 4 - bigger bites, less clicking
* New Canni AA - hastened cannibalize
* Can 4 as an AA, to eliminate "greyed-out" spell gems
* CAnnabilize over time spell/AA



3. Direct Damage Spells


Our nukes need help. Even with major improvements to our DOT spells, we need a better nuke. There is no reason that hybrids should be nuking on the same level us a pure caster class.

* Under powered
* Slow casting (especially velium strike)
* Resist rates on rains are high
* No nuke over 1000 damage, rangers can nuke almost as well as shamans. All other casting classes have a higher DD spell (cleric, enchanter, mage, wizard, druid)
* Highest level poison nuke is 675dmg (compare to hybrid nukes from POP expansion, RNG -700dmg, BST -675dmg, SK -660dmg)
* No specialized damage increasing focus for poison nukes

Direct Damage Suggestions:
* Velium Strike changed to casting time 6.5 seconds
* Alternative, a new poison nuke. Suggestion:
Poison based nuke, level 65, casting time 6.0, 1200dmg. 400mana
* An upgrade to our FIRE based nuke line, introduced at level 1 but then abandoned, would be nice
* Addition of a disease nuke
* Keep the poison/cold nukes, up damage
* True AE damage, ditch the 4 target limitation!
* Focus effects for our nuke lines (poison, proposed new disease nuke)
* Detrimental spell haste AA (similar to Quickbuff AA) to reduce casting time
* In an overwhelming majority, Crucible shaman are distressed by the proposed GoD 2.1 minute disease dot, please reconsider adding a new DD in its place!!!!



4. Slowing

In the past, it has been justified that many of the things we are lacking at (such as healing) are a result of the powerful nature of slow. This argument is no longer valid. Encounters are designed using slow mitigation and with full knowledge of the effects of slow. The difference in slows between shamans and enchanters is small, beastlords are close behind with their very fast casting slow. Other classes like bards, necromancers also have the ability to slow pretty well too. With the proliferation of slow spells across other these other classes, we cannot see why shaman should continue to be penalized for having slow. Additionally:

* Current new slow in GoD is substantially inferior to our level 51 slow due to recast time and short duration, it will not replace turgurs.
* Lack of non-essential spell gems preclude wasting a slot for new slow and having 2 slows memorized
* Slow mitigation has decreased the substantive advantage we have over other classes that can slow
* GoD Slow upgrade is insufficient, recast times and short duration, when paired with a nerf in effectiveness of the slow makes this spell undesirable

Slowing Suggestions:

* Reduced agro, mana cost, resist rate, and/or recast time on (an upgrade to) Turgur's Insects, which has been in its current state since Kunark was released six expansions ago
* An AA ability that mitigates mitigation
* An AA ability for a random chance to bypass mitigation


5. Buffing

Once the masters of melee buffing, shaman have seen their buffs become at best marginally useful. Weapons with avatar procs were introduced all the way back in velious (with no component!), many players are reaching their stat caps unbuffed, and the proliferation of ATK buffs from other classes make our attack buffs of less relative worth even with stacking. Our hybrid class, the beastlord, can buff HP, ATK, resists and mana regen to higher numbers than shaman, and can buff nearly as well in resists and statistic buffs. Further, there is no discernable reason that our buffs should cost more mana and last a shorter duration than other priest classes

* Stat buffs at level 65 are largely useless because of stat caps
* Our hybrid class can buff more pure HP than we can, plus more of the other relevant stats at 65, ATK and RESISTS
* Our buffs are shorter duration and higher mana cost than other priest classes

Buffing Suggestions:

* Group FA
* Stat buffs that are attractive to the stat capped
* A new shaman buff of adding +10% to weapon skills, +10% dmg to procs, +10% to avoidance lasting 1 hour. As str/dex/sta is pretty much maxed by so many people at the high end making the only beneficial means of buffing being hp and resists
* Endurance buffs! Logical choice for shaman
* Buffs which go past the sta cap (For example: So is someone with 305 Stamina was buffed with Boar they'd hit 355. Same would hold true for people at level 60 with 255. They could reach 305 with our buffs. And at 65 with 280 you'd hit 340.)
* Integrating attack buffs with focus
* Focus or FA augmented with the following:
+30 accuracy (lowers hit/miss ratio)
+30 combat effects (increases proc rate)
+2 shielding (lowers damage taken)
+20 Strikethrough (You swing, they dodge, you strikethrough and hit them anyway)

Another topic the shaman community would like to bring under your attention is that sometimes we don't understand the developer's vision behind some of the abilities/spells that we are given.

We (as a community) feel that there are major differences between how the current Dev teams views the shaman class and its role in grouping/raiding scenarios and how previous dev teams saw this role. So we would like to invite one or more of the Devs to come to our board and start a dialogue with us on the views they have for our class and how we fit into the big picture, after all it is much easier to talk when you know how both parties see things instead of making assumptions.

Such an explanation will also be helpful to understand if certain perceptions we have about game mechanics are correct (slow mitigation and resist rate on slow spells for example) and we from our side can show how those game mechanics affect our enjoyment in the game (take it from me, chain casting Cannibalize 4 is not very good for your ability to have a nice chat in your group).

Nice layout and post.

Reminder of suggestions mentioned in other posts to add as possible options for SOE to address specific changes:

Healing: Our semi-CH is on the same cast time as cleric/druid CH. 10 seconds for minimal gain. After our upgrade fast casting heal, this spell stays in the book 90% of the time.

If increasing the healing power is not an option, suggest reducing the cast time to half its current value (5 second cast time.)

If increasing the healing power is an option, increase the healing power by 10% from base level each level (195 hps) up to 65. This adds 1365 healing to the spell at 65 for a total of 3315 hitpoints healed. Still way below the cleric 39 level complete heal while a significant upgrade to shaman healing ability. At 65, this is not overpowering as a heal.

Dots: That disease dots didn't make the top 5 is more an indication of the the lack of their viable use. In short, shaman are not using them in day-to-day utility use. The reason is beat to death: excessively long duration. These spells are not worth casting in xp groups which essentially eliminates shaman damage power from being a contributory factor.

As a shaman increases in levels the duration of the disease dots should decrease with the damage dealt per tick increased to make up the reduced time. There are 2 ways to handle this. Place disease and poison dots on the same duration times and damage values. Alternately, reduce disease duration by 1/3 (~1.5 minutes vice 2 minutes) and increase the individual tick damage accordingly.

If this cannot be incorporated into the spells themselves, provide AA's that can be earned that allows shaman to acheive this. Being able to cast dot spell faster and having them then take 2 minutes to cycle thru negates the point of casting it faster. Disease dots need to do their damage faster and more efficiently.

Slow: The number of slow spells obtainable after 51 Turgur's suggest SOE is attempting to give shaman abilities to enhance their primary role. The same can be said of the maloxxx line. With maloxxx this has been acheived with unresistable Malo(s) and the option to take the chance on higher debuff values using Malosinia. The slow line, however, seems to be stumbling upon itself while at the same time other classes have been relatively advanced to near equality with slow ability.

The core problem with slow is resist rate. This is compounded by a long recast time. Keep in mind THE slow spell is STILL a Kunark based, level 51 spell. The solution to these problems are simple to fix: reduce the resist rate and/or reduce the recast time.

This can be done in one of 3 ways:

Upgrades to Turgur's with the same affect (75% max slow) that reduce the resist rate and/or recast time. Preferably resist rate. At level 65 an unresistable, long duration, Turgur's based slow.

Shaman specific AA's that do the same thing.

Change the maloxxx line to increase the debuff line for slow allowing it a significantly increased chance of landing.

Slow Mitigation: The changes that gave shaman the slight edge in amount slowed is too minimal to provide an advantage. If shaman can slow a mitigating mob the amount that we can potentially slow needs to be significantly higher than other class abilities.

To earn that ability, however, should be a class defining AA along the lines of 5, 10, 15% slowability.
