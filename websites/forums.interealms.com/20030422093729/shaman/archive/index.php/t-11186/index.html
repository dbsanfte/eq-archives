This review makes a number of very stupid points. First of all, when they let this guy make a movie, then he can make it 6 hours long, so they fully explain everything. Or he can make it a movie about a blind guy who goes around trying to buy equipment to make a Swiss Army cane and hiring fashion designers to color coordinate his costume. People go to an action movie to see action, not to see people wax mournfully about their past.

Everything you needed to know was explained, albeit quickly.
The movie was obviously made with people already familiar with the comic book in mind. However, my mom, who had never even heard of Daredevil before she saw the previews, had no trouble following along. If you didn't like the other characters, then you need to take it up with Stan Lee, who either developed or was involved with the development of all of them.

Daredevil is not Spider-man or Superman. He is more like Batman, more of an athlete with gadgets than a superhero with magical powers. If he goes up against a supervillain with magical powers, then he would get creamed, end of story.

This movie isn't great, and it isn't as good as Spiderman or the first Batman, but it is certainly entertaining for what it is.
