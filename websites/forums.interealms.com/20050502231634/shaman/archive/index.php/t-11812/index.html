--The Problems with Everquest and the High End Game.

By-Sneekii-Feenin Ro

For many of you out their EQ plays a large part in your everyday life. If fact odds are if your reading this you probably encountered this essay while surfing EQ related sites. Everquest for many is an escape. For some it is their life. For myself EQ is my leisure time. EQ keeps me home from the bars and spending a lot of money. Before I played EQ I would normally go out to local bars and nightclubs 3 to 5 times a week. EQ has given me a hobby that is relatively inexpensive compared to most others. I started playing EQ in 1999 and continue to play to this day. I am a 28 year old Customer Relations Manager for The Germain Motor Corp. with a 3 year old son to an ex-girlfriend. My EQ playtime is roughly 3 to 5 hours a night 3 to 4 times a week. We all know that many have succumbed to the lure of EQ regardless of Age,Title,Race,Gender, or Location. The reason I have provided so much information on myself is so one can better see my position on this subject.

EQ in general is a fantastic game which takes you into a fantasy world shared by many others from around the world. When EQ first came out it was filled with danger and fear of the unknown. You had to tread carefully and keep track of where you went and how you got their. Monsters and creatures loomed at every turn. While many of these aspects remain today their impact has been greatly lessened. Now we jump ahead 3 years to the current state of EQ. Today EQ for me and many others consists of one of two things. You either spend your time slowly grinding away at levels or you adventure with friends or guilds after dragons and other boss type encounters. Many people reading this might be asking themselves 'What is the problem?' .

--THE PROBLEM
Today in EQ over 80% of the gaming population has achieved the level of 50 to 65. Now by the time they reach these levels they generally have had their fill of experience grinding and desire to encounter high end content (Dragons,Bosses,Gods etc.). The problem is that High end bosses and dragons are not available.

Generally most Dragons,Bosses,Gods, and other high end raid type encounters spawn one to 2 times a week. When they do the guild with the most players that can get to them the fastest get to kill them. We will call this the Dragon Race. To have a shot at encountering these high level events one must participate in this dragon race. To be successful at this a guild or group must be online for numerous hours tracking and running from encounter to encounter trying to outrun others and even if they succeed they usually have others breathing down their back and hoping that they will fail and sometimes even helping them to fail. I fell this problem came about when travel became easy in EQ. No longer did people have to travel but simply ask to be teleported.

As I stated earlier my life is rather hectic and I do have obligations and such that I must attend to outside of the game as do many others. Even with those obligations I feel my playtime is above average compared to many. With EQ in its current state friends or guilds cannot plan out any raid attempts and are left with experience grinding while the groups and players who play quite a bit more go around killing boss after boss 30 min after they spawn. I personally don't recall any fantasy book or movie having the fearless adventurers turned back by a ravenous force of campers or charging into the dungeon to kill the vile dragon only to find that he was not home. What this effectively does is cater to the powergamer and clockwatchers and takes away from the casual players. As bad as this may seem their is a solution.

--THE SOLUTION
The solution to this problem is rather simple and has already been experimented with by Sony. The solution is to make all Boss,Dragons, and God type encounters triggered either via quest or some form of turn in. This works very well with Ragefire and enables someone who does not have the time to sit in a zone for 18 hours watching for a spawn and hoping he has enough friends on to kill the beast. This takes nothing from the heavy players since they still play more which equals more shots at mobs. What is does allow is people to take on more challenging content when they are the most powerful and on their own timetable. Now I know many of you out their are wondering how to balance this?

Balance is simple. Lets use the well known Vindicator or Kael. Now he actually spawns quite often however he is highly contested. To spawn Vindicator perhaps one much acquire an item that drops off of ANY mob in the zone. So for a guild to be able to kill vindicator they would be required to spend time in the zone killing other giants and taking faction hits to get said item and spawn vindicator. This would also balance the faction system and prohibit cross factioning to kill the rare bosses. Lets also use another example in Lord Nagafen of Nagafens Lair. Lets say we place a scared peasant NPC outside of the zone who is looking for his daughter and fears she may have been taken by nagafen. All that he has found so far is half of her pendant. Now lets say that any mob in Nagafens Lair can drop the other half of the pendant. Once acquired you hand the pendant to the peasant and he asks you to kill nagafen to help get his daughter back. The shortly after Lord nagafen will spawn with a full Fire Giant spawn as well. Obviously these are just examples but they do show how such a system could be put in place and how a great deal of storyline can be added to the game.

While reading this many people might say that by adding such a system you would have too many boss mobs and dragons being killed and loot entering the world too fast. Well to ensure that this doesn't not happen you simply control the d\frequency of the item you need. If for example the necklace only drops 1 or 2 times a week then you will only have naggy dieing 1 or 2 times a week. The advantage however is that people can form and schedule with friends for such an event.

Now I do understand that these guilds and groups that continually farm these events and encounters would not welcome such a change. What needs to be understood is that EQ is a game for everyone and should not be geared towards powergaming. That being said powergaming still rewards someone by giving them more time to get items to spawn bosses and attempt them. The key to this system is placing the necessary quests on random things and in areas where people of the appropriate level can gain experience while doing the quest or searching for the items.

-- THE RESULT
The result of such a system would be grand. You would see more roleplaying enter EQ. Guild fighting and shouting across zones would lessen. The days of 90% of EQ high end content being gobbles up within 72 hours of a patch would be gone. Powerful guilds of freinds and people who work well together yet might have busy lives could attempt and defeat these encounters when they want as opposed to spending hours waiting around hoping for a chance. I also feel we would see less and less frustrated people leave EQ.

THE DOWNFALL
The downfall of this system is that the players who continually farm these encounters would not be able to kill as many and would need to devote resources to something other than killing dragon after dragon. However they would still have the advantage of acquiring and doing these quests more often and still get a chance to kill more creatures.

CONCLUSION
In conclusion one might say that this system would create a sort of harmony among EQ players. Guilds would send out their troops in group format to different zones in hope of gaining an item or items that would allow them to try an encounter on a Dragon or God. Since the drops would come off random mobs one could not camp a certain mob but would gain experience in certain zones while hoping for these items. It would also allow people to fail or succeed at their own pace. Hopefully at least games in the future will handle this scenario correctly and succeed where EQ has failed.

Actually there is a much easier solution to racing and unavailable game content.

Each boss should spawn every 3 hours (before you panic, continue reading).
Every boss will have 3 aggro lists:
(1) The aggro list we know that it exists, no change will be made to this, none is needed.
(2) Temporary perma aggro list
(3) Perma aggro list

How it would work? Simple, same concept as the way Vox/Nagafen handle 52+ players.

If a player aggroes this mob he will be on regular aggro list AND temporary aggro list. Temporary aggro list is only wiped if mob is back at it's spawn point, full health and no detrimental effects, or the mob despawns (AoW, Bertoxxulous, etc ...). Even if players is dead, logged out, zoned, whatever ... temporary list is maintained while mob is aggro and/or has negative effects.

The moment mob dies, temporary list is moved to permanent list. Permanent list is erased once a week to "simulate" mob spawn.

What will this accomplish?

When a player gets on aggro list AND this player is on permanent list, he/she/it is teleported to her bindpoint. That player has to wait until perma aggro list is cleared to fight this boss again.

How feasible is this?
Pretty much very easy. Code base already inplace for "banning" players (Vox, Nagafen & Coirnav). Rampage list for mobs in Ssra is already beyond player death, logout or zoneout. Basically if you on AL rampage and you die, when you get rezzed back, welp ... too bad, welcome to rampage again. This mechanism could be used for temporary list. And of course character flags can be used for permanent aggro list.

Result:
(1) Every guild has access to EVERY SINGLE boss mob. No more racing, no more competition for entertainment content in a PvE game.
(2) Mobs will still be available to guilds like they would spawn regulary.
(3) People could actually enjoy the entertainment value of EQ instead of molding their lives around the endgame. Get home from work earlier because important mob is up!

SOE is already doing this in much of their newer content - particularly for encounters designed for small forces (1-3 groups).

Consider the final encounter in Hate's Fury. To fight the captain of the ship, you must collect 4 items from the zone and hand them over to the gnome trigger. Again, this prevents high end guilds from bottom feeding off of the boss of Hate's Fury. I really like content of this type, and hope to see more of it from SOE.
