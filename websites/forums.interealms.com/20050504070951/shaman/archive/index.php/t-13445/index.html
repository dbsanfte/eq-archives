Shabura Naarteban
I like the idea of opening more content to players not in a high-end raiding guild in principle, but there are some issues that this change would cause that bother me greatly. I suppose I should note that I am a member of a high-end raiding guild. I have killed all of the flagging targets up to Rallos Zek, and will be entering Tier IV likely within a week - so not much of this directly affects me. That doesn't mean that I don't care about what happens to other part of this game. And it doesn't mean that I think other players are "d00dz" or unworthy of getting their money out of this expansion - 4 zones of content was a raw deal.

That said, here are the problems I see:

General problems
1. Initial Chaos
Life is going to be very messy in all these zones for at least the first 2 weeks. Yeah, eventually people will fall back to the hardest content at their level of competance, skill, and power - but that's gonna take some time, and nobody is going to like the chaos that will reign until that gets sorted out.

2. Zone Size
Flatly, most of these zones were designed with the idea that they would have restricted access. There just isn't much room in them. Valor, for all that people laud it, isn't very big at all. Outer cave, inner cave, left castle, right castle, and lever - and the zone is full. You can squeeze groups at frogs and maybe steps (no one hunts undead or guards) - but that's still looking at 36-42 people for comfortable capacity, where everyone gets spawns at a steady rate. Yet we often see 60-80 in Valor. And many of these zones have less available space than that. As people try to push to the bigger, harder, flashier locales, there will be serious overcrowding issues.

3. Flagging Mobs
A lot of people here have assumed that because, for example, Aerin'Dar will no longer be needed to enter Halls of Honor, that the flag from Aerin'Dar will no longer be needed to enter Temple of Marr (HoH-B). I posit that will not be the case; completing the three Trials of Honor without having killed Aerin'Dar will result in the message "While you have fought with Honor, you lack the Valor to enter the Temple of Marr" when you attempt to zone in (you receive this message if you meet those conditions now, in fact). Many of these events are blowable, others interfere with non-raid groups. I'll try to detail these below, also, but suffice to say, without changes to the zone structure, several places may get very messy.

Bastion of Thunder
1. Training
Bastion of Thunder probably has the greatest potential to be a training nightmare with this change. Its not really very hard to get a group that can take a giant, even a group that is lower level than current BoT residents. But this zone is already crowded, and the influx will push people into less optimal pulling spots. Combined with the random AEs in zone, and the difficulty in splitting mob in many (most?) wings, *especially* water, and a lower level group can get into trouble fast. This is compounded by the easily-trainable nature of BoT - linear wings set around the zoneout is a nice layout, but makes training tempting, and easy. Most newcomers won't train, of course - but we all learned in Karnor's that it only takes one.

Crypt of Decay
1. Carpin Script
The Carpin Script (or whatever your server calls the quest for the key to lower ruins) is the poster child for blowable content. Carpin Deatharn isn't really THAT hard - a 2 group endevour can probably pull this off. And that blows the event. Without a revamp to the way this zone works, getting keys to visit Bertox downstairs is going to be very, very challenging.

2. Zone Size
This is also the poster child zone for zone size problems. Where exactly are more than about 2 groups going to FIT in here?

Halls of Honor
1. Honor Trials
The Honor Trials are blowable. One group can easily kill the Trial mob and spawn the Custodian. They're in for a surprise, and will likely die, but that won't stop people from doing it. Competition for Honor Trials is already steep on a lot of servers; an influx of people messing about with the quest givers won't help. The fact that they look just like the normal mobs and have similar names to the occasional rares in here won't help prevent accidental attacks on the Trial givers, either.

2. Zoneline Griefing
Its really, really easy to mess up the entrance to HoH-A, a fact I am reluctant to admit here, even. Some mobs see invis, and trains so equipped are nasty, but beyond that, some of the "bruiser crew" of mobs in here (Undead Inhabitant, etc.) don't reset to their spawn when moved. Some of them can see invis, too ... and a see invis Undead Inhabitant parked at the zoneline effectively makes zoning in instant death until GM intervention. Unless this is altered, I can't wait to see people who think this is funny - or who want to deny content to others. Its happened before with just the people in Honor who keyed or flagged in; it'll be really exciting when anyone 46+ can do it.

Plane of Storms/Plane of Valor
While Valor is going to be more crowded than a 100 man raid on Beholder's Maze, I actually support this idea. The Justice Trials (while *not* hard, I promise) are artificially slow, and tended to be a chokepoint. And, really, its not like these 2 planes have anything that is either blowable or horridly destructive. They are just nice zones. Okay, okay ... people COULD haul the spawned bosses (Drornok or Ston'Ruok) to the ZI to *really* grief people (they don't reset), but that's more work that your typical player is going to go to - even the kind that do this sort of thing.

Plane of Tactics
1. Rallos Script
This is a problem even NOW. The Rallos script kills people ALL OVER the place. The spawned Tallon in particular shoot people through walls at times, sometimes as far away as the ring around the pit (or the pit itself). I have a neat screenshot somewhere of being blasted for 2764 by Tallon while in the pit, soloing. The problem will be a lot worst with a lot more people here. And I cannot imagine the carnage when someone spawns Rallos Zek the Warlord and all the players who didn't heed the warnings (if they were given) or who "wanted to watch" stayed in the pit.

2. Zoneline Griefing
The side paths that lead to Vallon and Tallon don't have signs warning people that the mobs there kill. But drag a Henshin Shadow Master to the zoneline and watch folks die as they zone in. Bring that named guy (whose name escapes me - he pops near ZI *anyway*) for even more fun. And even if a group is pulling these who can kill them, it ought to be fun to watch the "Vallon wing" group accidently kill off the "pit ledge" group when a Henshin Shadow Master does that funny AE super-knockback and sends them spiraling to their death in the pit below. Oh - and did I forget to mention that split mobs parked at the ZI are really, really bad?

Torment, the Plane of Pain
1. Uselessness
This plane will still never see the light of day. The mobs are too hard for not enough. Oh, and there isn't much room either. Spare people who aren't racing for elementals access to this plane - they may not know it, but they'll be thankful for the locked entrance. =)
