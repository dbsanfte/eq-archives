Donar
In the early levels, as the others have mentioned, you sort of have to do it all. You will melee the mobs, while throwing in the nuke or dot needed to take the mob down. If you get in a bind, root the mob and heal yourself a bit. Later on, you'll still find you can tank, but will be better served by relying almost exclusively on dots and nukes for your damage, not to mention the pet when you get it. At some point though, particularly in PoP, you will find some mobs do too much damage to tank and kill efficiently so you will need to root/dot them or kite them. I soloed in the face until 60 but it took quite a bit of upgrading to solo in the face in PoP.*

I agree also with the others that you should group a fair amount as well. Both grouping and soloing teach you alot about what you can do and how the game works, and how other classes work also. Oh yeah! Keep your casting skills up, and your defense. Did I mention, keep up your defense? ;) It's important, especially if you want to tank mobs, and you will tank some mobs whether you want to or not.

*Note: At higher levels, much of the ability to solo in the face will be due not only to gear, but also will depend on having torpor or quiescence. I never had stoicism as I was 60+ when it was added so it may help. It can't hurt, but I don't know how powerful it really is.

If you want to solo as a shaman I recommend tanking until you get to 195ish defense or better. It makes a huge difference, and you practically have to solo to do it since you won't take enough hits in groups to really max defense.

Another good option is to skill up alchemy until you can make DS potions and go to needite room in echo caverns. Buff you hp as high as possible, dmg shield up and pull with a dot. Needites hit FAST but for 12ish dmg. Your defense will shoot up and the dmg shield will kill them.

But if you really wanna solo play a necro. Much more mana efficient and necros have every important skill needed short of sow.
