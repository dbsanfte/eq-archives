I used to have that problem.

The way I do it now is this:

At the start of the adventure / encounter / group / session I tell everyone that I am going to load my buff spell set and hit everyone with a full set of buffs. I further tell them that if they want *me* to keep them buffed, they need to click off any Shaman buffs they currently have.

I then proceed to use the group version, and only the group version, of every buff I cast. Depending on the group, I might use Soul instead of Khuras. Really not that much difference in the two.

This allows me to actually participate in the killing of bad guys instead of waiting for the Ranger's Fo7 he got 90 minutes ago to wear off so I can stick my Focus on him.

Yes, it might mean that I am not always using the absolute best buffs I have. You know what? I don't care. I buff because I feel it's an important part of what my character brings to a group. But watching buff timers is not what I consider "fun" . . . so I don't do it. Yes, I'll use Avatar (no FA yet) on a tank if I know we are about to hit a difficult mob, but I won't maintain it for general progression.

Either way, you are the one who is playing Shaman, you need to set the rules in your groups as to how you are going to handle buffs. Set the rules, stick to them, and most of all, don't let any aspect of your duties get in the way of having fun.

Sudedor
Combat % mods are direct changes to different combat abilities. Perhaps the easiest to explain is avoidance.

If I cast a buff that adds a 7% Avoidance mod (as our new AGI buff is rumored to do) what I have done is given that character a flat 7% chance to avoid an incoming blow.

That is a combat % mod.
