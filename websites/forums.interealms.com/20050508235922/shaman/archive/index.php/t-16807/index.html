Xev_Grammar
Was wondering if you could feedback this-- perhaps they'll listen (haha).

Currently, if you have Quickbuff 2 and Haste of Mithaniel, your Quiescence is 3.3 seconds. If you use an ability, like say, Cannibalization, and immediately followup with Quiescence, you will get a "Spell Recovery Time not yet met" message after you finish casting it, and the spell will be completely aborted.

This is VERY VERY bad. You don't want your Quiescence to be aborted after a Canni 5-- or at least I don't. hehe.. (and you don't want to Q then Canni 5 if you're full health, or even canni 4 a few times then Q and Canni 5-- you're lowering your efficiency in both cases by delaying your canni 5)

I've had QB1 and haste of mithaniel for a very long time so I'm very very used to canni 5-Q, and I keep getting that stupid message now that I have QB2. Now I have to force myself to pause after canni 5, making me delay even longer (poor human reflexes) than if I had just QB1 and haste, which is just plain silly.

This is true for many quickcasting spells, and I think that it needs to be addressed.

Possible solutions:
If a spell is cast way too quickly, instead of giving the message and aborting it, just continue "casting" until the server is satisfied that you've waited a long enough time-- then complete the spell successfully. I think this would solve all of our problems in regards to that annoying message (band-aid approach, need to mess with server/client communications).

Another option might be to revisit all AAs and add the minimum recovery time to them like they did to spells, but that might actually make it worse for us (brute-force script-and-change-all-and-likely-break-something approach. requires mass-changing lots of spells/abilities).

Or how about getting rid of the spell recovery time not yet met message altogether, and just have item effects/AA abilities cast without affecting your refresh timer (so you can't use an instaclick to ungrey your spell icons to cast right away)? (correct fix, may require a restructuring of how item effects work).
