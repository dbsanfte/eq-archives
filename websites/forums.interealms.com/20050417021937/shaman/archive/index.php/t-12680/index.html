Healer/Slower - Yours truly

DPS
DPS
DPS

Rolls pretty nicely. Had an all-pet group with SK (he had his baby
skeleton out), necro, mage, shaman, beastlord, necro. That was pretty
fun.

General tips...

Find a couple people who like to grind experience in the same hours you
do. Schedule groups, much easier and more efficient than going LFG.

When you start a group (or join someone else's) ask beforehand how
long people are going to stay, and ask them to be courteous and find
a replacement before they leave. Downtime hunting LFG's kills your
experience rate.

If the experience is sucking, for whatever reason, it's time to excuse
yourself and find a replacement. Don't waste your time in bad groups.

Contrary to popular belief, not only charmers can solo in PoP. Solo the
zoneout in PoJ on off hours. Root/rot trash in PoN if you're hard pressed.
Heck, soloing in Katta, though outdated is still ok experience.
Your experience per hour rate is significantly lower than PoP groups, but
you can roll at your own pace, don't have to sit LFG and don't have
much downtime. Or find a partner, BL or necromancer works great, and
explore tier 1 planes for a decent place to exp.

I try to keep the PoN/PoD groups as small as possible but still have good DPS. Usually, it is me (61 shammy), 61 SK, 61 rogue, and sometimes a 4th member. We do the gargoyles in the fort, or just yard trash usually. The gargs are fairly dangerous, though, and if you’re not careful you can lose somebody pretty fast. Gargs also resist slow fairly often, so they need to be debuffed.

Sometimes we add a chanter and a cleric to that mix, and we chain pull whatever mobs we can find on hedge side. We try to avoid getting over 5 members, though. The fun part is that I get to be the puller for a change. The tank pulls the first mob and engages, then after that I pull with slow and bring mobs back to camp, where the chanter mezzes. Once mezzed, I will usually malosini just for the heck of it, and then I’m off to pull again. Once we have 4 or so mobs in camp, I’ll stop and cannibalize a bit, maybe adding a DoT and refreshing slow on some of the mezzed mobs if the melee types are a little slow killing. I recommend having a pally or SK tank for this since a warrior has to taunt several times before breaking mezz, and that either slows things down or makes life hard on your chanter. I’m not knocking warriors here – I leveled one to 60 myself. =) As long as the tank has decent AC and doesn’t drain the cleric, the only limitation is the chanter’s mana. We usually do it in 30-45 minute runs before the chanter needs a med break, and sometimes we don’t have to stop at all.
