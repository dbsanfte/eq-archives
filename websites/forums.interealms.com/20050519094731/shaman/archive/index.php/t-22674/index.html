AtommWarbird
Hello,

I've been reading posts here for over 2 years and trying to contribute as I can. It occurred to me the other day that one of the major advantages that Druids have over us in a zone with pure animals is that almost always their DC pet is stronger than our summoned pet. So....

What if we asked for the ability to charm aminals and get Dire Charm just like druids? That would cure the pet problem in a lot of zones (I realize not all) and would honestly I think allow the higher end Shamans something to do (keeping a tougher animal constantly charmed).

Perhaps, since we all complain about our lack of dps, SoE could slightly expand the definition of "animal" so that we can dire charm more mobs out there than Druids can.

What do you think about something like this? I did get "Charm Animals" way back when and I do think it kind of fits with the Shaman spirit. Dog Dog is nice but any mob over say....level 20 can out hit him. A DC pet (or a charmed pet) would significantly increase our DPS and possibly allow us to fill the "DPS needed" slots in groups.

I know druids will complain bitterly about this, but when you think about it I think that being able to DC more pets (like the frogs in LDoNs) that they cannot with *no snare* means to me there is a lot more risk, especially after we buff our new pet up. I think you could control the "overpowering" nature of having a charmed pet by either capping his DPS while charmed (just let it rise to at least a BL pet!) or by having the Charm spell slot block HoT's (which is the only mana-efficient way we're going to keep it alive).

I'd be interested in everyone's comments on this. DC Pets is something I haven't seen proposed here for a long time. Maybe I'm way off base, but I could see how this could fix our low dps woes.

haruuq
i think even requesting DC for a shammy is ridiculous. i'm not intending to be rude or offensive to the originator of this thread, but in general, i see (between this forum and ones for other classes) a derth of requests for this or that class to have access to abilities that are considered major edges to other classes.

DC and charm abilities are already more than appropriately distributed among the classes they are in line with conceptually (i love that mages can charm elementals, w/o the ability to DC--very nice touch), as well as being factored in as far as the balance goes.

charm is a big advantage, whether people want to admit it or not. while a dire charmed pet as it stood w/o buffs isn't anything spectacular, if you were to give shammies the ability to DC or charm, we would be able to use swift like the wind, FA, FoS, an AC buff, and regen, as well as maintaining the pet with our heals. in other words--we could do more to improve a charmed pet than any other class. even enchanters can't heal their own pet, and while mages can do crazy things with their charmed pets, they still can not DC (at present), and can not slow mobs that their pets fight. the combination of major charm ability plus slow, heals, buffs, and our dots and debuffs would lead to situations where we could effectively solo mobs that we really should not be able to.



aside from that, if we keep taking cornerstone abilities and giving them to other classes, we're eventually going to wind up with four classes. tank, healer, melee dps, caster dps.

i don't want that. i'm happy with dogdog. if you want to be more like a druid, PLAY ONE.

i want to remain shaman.
