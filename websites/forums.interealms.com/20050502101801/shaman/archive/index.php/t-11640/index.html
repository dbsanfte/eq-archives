Cesta
The coolness factor goes to Druids by a *small* margin. The Teleports and the non-root breaking DoT's give Druids a wider hunting range solo than any other class. Necros are a bit stronger but they have to deal with pet pathing issues, which can be a major PITA in some zones. Shaman are still cool but I'd give this one to Druids.

Druids and Shaman contribute different things to groups. In a group without Enchanter I think a Shaman is a must for the slows. If you have Enchanter and Cleric then I think a Druid is a slightly better choice because of the Evacs and fast damage capabilities. In terms of grouping I consider Druids and Shaman to be equally valuable.

Soloing is very even also. Druids have an easier time getting around Norrath and setting up and Shaman are probably slightly stronger against the typical mob. People tend to view Druids as weaker soloists against magic-resistant mobs but realistically Shaman have the same issues against these because of the inability to keep the pet alive without landing a slow.

Druids do more damage with spells. Shaman pets make up the difference, at a real cost in mana. Typical scenario when hunting with pet is to have to slow-cripple-dot-dot along with the pet damage. This takes just as much mana as the Druid's dot-root-dot-dot. If a Shaman chooses not to dot and relies solely on pet damage then the sequence is slow-cripple-heal-heal-heal (pet that is) and then stepping in to do a bit of melee damage at the end to avoid the XP reduction code.

I hunt regularly with two Druids who are a couple of levels north of me and it's not worth bringing out the pet in that scenario (I can avoid slowing with no pet to protect.) Of course having the Shaman dots break root makes for interesting tactics. I tend to double dot pull and then canni after they've rooted the target and dotted it. When the target is dead I go pull another.

Ogre Shaman can definitely tank some. The frontal stun immunity allows for reliable spell casting and once a dark blue mob is slowed and crippled I can usually stand in front of it until it is dead. As with anything else there are some exceptions to the rule that I definitely cannot tank. We get chain, not plate, and at 53 I have about 950 AC self-buffed. I don't know if other Shaman races are comfortable tanking, I would be a lot less comfortable without the frontal stun immunity.

Pets are okay damage and can take damage fairly well from a properly debuffed mob. That said, the Shaman pets are nowhere near as strong as the Necro, Mage and Beastlord pets. There are a lot of cirumstances, even solo, in which I choose not to summon dogdog because he's not worth the effort. My pet has conned light blue at every level I've had him at.

Shaman and Druids are not as similar as you would think. The main difference is not in the spells as much as the attributes and method of play. Shaman are high hp low mana and tend to constantly hover at 50-60% of both as they play. They very rarely actually med, using cannibalism and hp regen as their main source of recovery. Druids, on the other hand, are more traditional casters with lower hp's and a bigger mana pool. They tend to blow their mana on a kill or three and then regen more traditionally by medding. In terms of actual play I find the Shaman to be more like an Enchanter than a Druid, both solo and grouped.
