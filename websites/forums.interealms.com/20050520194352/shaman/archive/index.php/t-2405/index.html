Shubert TZ
Worked on this guy for my friends epic. Had quite a bit of trouble but finally killed him. You need to have a melee heavy group of people...prefereably 55+ but definately 50+ (Under 55 get hit hard with proc and fear). Main assist should be 55+ and use defensive. Buff up with a few dummy buffs (he dispells on his ae) and then usefull one. Fire, AC and HP buffs are most important. It also really helps to have a bard with the AE fire resist. All casters move on to the hill, they can avoid the AE here. Main tank sits on beach with other melee back near the hill. Druid hands in piece spawning Fay, Main tank engages and hits defensive. Clerics come down to beach to keep MA alive but avoid agro. After 30 seconds or so, all other melees attack. Healers heal main tank. Around 30% health, casters dump mana, I believe that you can only hit with cold based attacks. Main thing is for the MA to keep agro. If he doesnt, then Fay will either pingpong or summon healers and caster which was why we failed so many times. NOTE: if you are wiped by Fay, then have all log onto an alt or chatroom for a while. Fay has been know to corpse camp that beach. Fay will despawn after a while but each time it agros, that timer is reset. Another option is to summon bodies. Neither being very fun. Good Luck i hope this helped.

Dagor
This is the one Raid style Mob I have had some experience with. I have been on enough raids with different people running them that I have seen different tactics put to use. The main thing is keep the casters and healers out of the range of the AoE. I am only level 49, and have done Fay several times since turning 45 or so. I have NEVER been feared though, so I am not sure why some say the fear is a problem. I don't have much MR gear, and only wore FR gear for the raid. My general tips.

Let the main tank build agro for a good 30 seconds before calling assist.

Fay agroes on low HP, so keep everyone above half life if at all possible.

Have everyone MAX their FR. summond mage blankets and shields are a big help.

I tell the melles in my group to stay on the near side of Fay, so I can dart in to heal them, and back out. Alternatively I have seen a guild call for their melees to leave combat and run toward their appointed healer for a heal. This keeps the healers completely out of the AoE damage.

The last raid I attended featured only one 50 plus cleric. In this raid I was appointed the task to keep him alive from the AoE damage he was taking. An interesting feature of this raid was that the lack of MT healing was compensated for by having the 4 pallys on the raid set up a LoH order. Once the MT engaged they walked up one at a time and stood next to him. When the MT reached 2 bubs or so of health the Pally would LoH and call it out. This saved the clerics all of the healing in the beginning of the fight.

I have never seen Fay slowed.

The one thing I have always wondered is if Lull would stick on Fay. Since this is a MOB where ping pong kills, if you could reduce the range Fay would agro from low HP, you would reduce this encounter to near trivial. The only wipes I have seen were caused by The dragon ping ponging between low HP healers, and straggled melees.

Also No sitting, and if you are low HP and see Fay turn to face you...give it up and charge him. You dying at his feet is much better than having him walk over to you to kill you..Ping pong kills.

Just make sure all the tanks have 100+ magic resist for the fear, and good fire resist for the AE (if they are in front: Fay's fire only goes to the front).

When druid does turn in, everybody except the MT should be standing so Faydedar will aggro and run straight to MT. Have the other tanks engage from the sides and rear while MT holds aggro and faces dragon away from the healers.

As far as debuffs, dont worry about it. Just get tank heavy and healers and beat him down. We've killed him about 10 times, and I didnt bother debuffing him any of those times.
