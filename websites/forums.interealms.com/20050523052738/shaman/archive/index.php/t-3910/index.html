Gresgoth
To explain how pets tank Rampage, we first must know how Rampage works.

Rampage is a single target based attack in ADDITION to the normal single target attack of a mob. How does one get Rampaged you ask? Well, it's very simple. The second person to aggro Rampaging_mob01 gets added to the "Rampage List". Rampaging mobs have 2 aggro type lists. One is normal "aggro list", and the second is the "rampage list". If you are the second person to aggro the rampaging mob, your name gets added to the "Rampage list" forever (unless the mob dies).

Let's give an example: High Priest of Ssraeshza is a rampaging mob. The first person to attack (or cast on) the high priest is immediately added to the aggro list. Now, the next person that aggros him will be put on the "rampage list". When in melee range, the high priest will attack the first person on his "aggro list". In addition to the single target attack, when you see the message "High Priest of Ssraeshza goes on a RAMPAGE!", the first person on the "rampage list" is being attacked. *Note: if the person on the "Rampage list" is out of melee range, the high priest will attack the next person who is in range on the "rampage list".


Phew. Now that Rampage is explained, I'll explain how pets relate to this.


Once people found out how Rampage worked, they begin to use pets to "absorb" this extra damage output from rampaging mobs. Specifically, Beastlord pets work best to absorb rampage damage because the pet always has the same name and will stay atop the rampage list if it was second to aggro the rampaging mob. Remember, Beastlord warders are named "(Player name)`s warder", so once the Beastlord's pet's name is on the rampage list, he's there for good and will be rampaged by the rampaging mob (if in melee range of the rampaging mob. See above).


Hopefully this 100% clears up the "Rampage List" mystery.

About the pets.

There is a maximum number of mobs that can attack you at any 1 time.
