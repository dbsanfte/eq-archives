Kryscha
Before I say anything, I'm going to admit that horses weren't in game until I was 60, and I never bothered to get one until 65, so what I think may not be as valid at the poster's levels.

1. Given your situation, a fast horse (100k+, the only useful one imo) is going to be out of your reach, unless you've got some rich friends.
2. The moment you melee on a horse, you lose any mana regen advantage.
3. Horses are great for the 'easy' groups where all you do is slow on inc, throw some nukes/dots, and maybe a few heals around here and there.
4. Be aware, if you are on a horse, you CANNOT duck to interrupt a spell, you must walk around on the horse, or click the horse off and duck.
5. Due to (4.) I found a horse extremely restictive when soloing or grouping, and after playing with it for a few weeks, never used it again.
6. The slow 'start-up' period on a horse if you have to run away can get you killed. If you're running, it's because you're in trouble, and if you're in trouble, you probably need to get out of there ASAP, not wait for your horse to pick up speed.
7. Did I mention how frustrating it is not to be able to duck to interrupt a spell? Nothing like having an "Oh Crap, I need to cast xxx" moment, and then having to stuff around clicking off horses/moving around until spell stops casting/etc
8. You either need an AoN, or new models on to use a horse. I personally loathe the new models (my Iksar looks fugly) so for pure self-enjoyment, I prefer to look at myself and think "Damn, I'm one sexy Iksar!"
9. A horse is a real pain to use in melee, especially if you have dog dog chewing on a rooted mob with you. As you should know, a rooted mob hits whoever is closest, and on a horse that position can be hard to work out. If you misjudge this distance a lot, you'll end up wasting any extra mana advantage healing your pet back up.

My personal opinion on horses is that for an active caster (I primarily think shaman here) a horse is more of a disadvantage than an advantage for someone playing to the full capabilities of the class, even at lower levels.
