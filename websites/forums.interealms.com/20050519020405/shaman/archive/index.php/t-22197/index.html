Pretty sure anyone who was caught bid fixing in my guild would get the immidiate boot.

There is a big difference between the systems though. The "Loot has a price, whoever wants that has the most current points wins" method works on the trickle down economics theory, where eventually you will get enough of an item that the less active members will be able to get one too. Bidding basically allows for anyone to get any item at any time. The drawback here is that the guy who plays twice a month can come in, score something large, then not play much anymore, thereby de-valueing the drop for the guild overall. Personally, I do not think one is better than the other. In both cases, a person is being rewarded for participating.

Asthore McTavish
My guild uses a total loss system. For every raid you get a point. If clearing takes an hour or more you get a point for that. When an item drops you need to have 6 points or more to bid major upgrade on it. Bids are taken in private and the one with the highest points win. They then loose all their points. Some items are limited to who can bid, bard mod items, backstab items, EB items, things like that. Once all the proper classes have those items then they are open to all the other classes. With the exception in time where everything is open to anyone who could use it. We also have our system set up where folks can take a minor upgrade on an item and only spend 5 points. So if no one claims major the item drops down to minor. Here is a chance for folks with no points to put it for it if it is a major upgrade. Its also a choice for those who only go up maybe 10 hitpoints or so to claim minor. When telling the loot master your name for minor you link the item your replacing. If its a major upgrade and you have points your informed to either take it major or drop out. Its then randomed to those who put in minor and they loose 5 points. If they dont have any points they loose nothing.

Its a fair system and we haven't had any problems with it.

Our guild uses a Full loss DKP system, which is like most other DKP systems in that each boss mob/raid event is worth a set # of points. Loot is then given to the person with the highest # of accumulated points. Once loot is won, your points go back to zero. There is no bidding. There is no points decay. This ensures even the casuals eventually receive good items while still maintaining that the people that raid the most get the best/most gear...

Another benefit to this method is less items rot than with traditional DKP systems, tho I cannot remember why at this particular moment (not enough coffee yet...lol...).

We also try to limit certain specific items to classes that can benefit from them the most, IE EB items go to warriors first, Fire focus items go to the people that have fire based spells, etc...

This seems to work pretty well for us and many people coming from other DKP systems think this one is the best ever...
