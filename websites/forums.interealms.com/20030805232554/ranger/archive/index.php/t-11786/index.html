I have farmed all over the world... here is some of the things I've found.

Mid 20s
Get a group in the dungeons in Highhold. Great loot. 200 to 300 a day in full group

Mid 30s
Find a cleric and fear kite seafuries for thier FS weapons, toes, jewelry, and mugs of sea foam which are used in smithing tempers.
Fear kite giants in RM
Solo gnolls at the gnoll spires in SK (good solo exp at the level and most drop FS. Sell at the gypsys or at the aviak vendor at the top of the treehouse. A bank is only a spire ride away

Mid 40s
Ry'gorr orcs down in CC. Drop velium weapons and frozen weapons words and various other things. Both vendors, bank, and guards to run to within easy reach. Also get rare drops off the nameds such as the Foreman's Skull Cap, Cloak of Ry'gorr Oracles, Crystalline Robe, Blackened Crystalline Robe, and Herbalist Pack (nice WR back to store food or arrows in)

50+
Ice Giants in Everfrost. 3 distinct camps which ellimantes alot of the fighting over mobs and KSing. Spawn time seems to be around 6 minutes. If they are already camped then they also provide a fine way to work on your archery skill as the druids keep them rooted when farming them and the necros will keep pet on them. Most people seem to have no problem with rangers plinking away at thier mobs especially if you are willing to snare or root the mob in case the casters get in a little trouble, heal the caster if they take some hits, or buff the pet and caster (str of earth helps with the bieng encumbered from all the phat loot) in the case of Necros. Usually you will find either a druid, necro, or rarely a ranger camping these spawns. Halas is close for vendors and banking as well as an archery supply store in case you decide to work on your archery while your there. WIth a bow delay of 40 and a Lvl 55 druid dotting the giant I was pumping in about 2 stacks per mob before the druid killed it.

Also don't forget the old tried and true methods.
Farming quality cat pelts in EK
Farming Spider Silk in EK
Farming Spider Silk in Lguk
Farming Blackened Alloy from the gobbys down in Runnyeye
Foraging tradeskill pieces
-Sylvan Oak from various places in Velius
-Oak from BB and Kithicore
-Planer Oak from PoG
Iron Oxide off Elementals near the Mini caves in SteamFont
Griffienne Blood in NK or Laggedpine Forest
Highquality lion skins (used in making the fleeting quiver) off lions in HK

Now yes.... none of these ways will make you rich any time soon. You get about 100pp per stack of pelts and silks, 1plat per foraged item is about average, 100 plat per Planer Oak branch seems average, 5 plat per grif blood seems average... Iron Oxide.... no idea on that one.

The other thing to keep in mind is that you won't find anyone posting a farm in here that isn't pretty well known. The more people that know about it the more people will be there doing exactly the same as you. For stuff that you sell to other players remember that there are other players also farming the stuff to sell and you have to stay competitive. As for the question "Whats a good camp" There are no good camps. The best way I've ever found to make plat is still a bug group in Seb when you can avoid getting a loot whore cleric. On that one... don't hold your breath. Some clerics will be happy just to get back what they spent on dots for the group.... and then everyone gets to share in the gems and jewelry that drops.... other clerics are just plain old fashioned greedy and will insist on all PP loot that drops. Giants in RM are a good choice but also a widely known choice. Mobs are overcamped, trained, and frequently KSd. Iron Oxide is another thing. Sells for good plat (up to 500 plat per) but it is also a well known farm. I've seen Level 60 Necros farming those elementals and they are noob mobs. Everyone wants to make a buck. One good way to earn some money.... Baking. Make fish roles untill 130 or so...... fillets untill they trivial... then patty melts. After patty melts take the griffon meat you looted while farming blood and you can get your skill to 240 or so. These by themselves don't make plat..... but... once you get your skill to 200 you can start making the things like Halas 10lb meat Pies and the like which do sell well in the Bizaar and are an expendable resource so people are always wanting more of them. Here is the info on that

Fish Roles. Bat wings + fresh fish. Both available from Vendors in Freeport.
Fillets. Filleting Knife + certain type of meat. Lion meat works and can be saved while farming pelts or can be purchased from a vendor.
Patty Melts. Bread + Cheese + certain meat + nonstick frying pan
All the ingredients can be bought from vendors except the frying pan which is a smithed and pottery created item. The cheese is sold in Laggedpine Forest.
Griffon Melts. Griff meat +bread+cheese+non stick frying pan

This is one of the most under exploted money making attempts I have seen but that is changing quickly. Want the best way to make money? Make an enchanter.... twink it (int gear is cheap) and then get it to as high a level as you can. Cha and Int are a enchanters primary statistics needed for battle... but those same statistics help them learn tradeskills faster and sell the products to vendors for more. Plus they get enchant materail spells which you can use to make enchanted materials for other players. Also there is of course good old fashioned crack whoring, but with the wide availability of KEI that is becoming less profitable. Could make a port whore but with the already easy travel via Nexus and soon the travel plane from PoP that is also not a very profitable thing. Lastly... Make a cleric.... get the best gear you can for it... and play it often. Clerics are a somewhat expensive class but they also get a lions share of the loot. From 40+ it is considered normal that the cleric get all gems that drop no matter what they have cast on the group. Also they can be rez whores. No matter how easy it is to travel or whatever else people are still dying and loosing exp and not liking it. Just go to a zone with lots of deaths (ME, DSP, DL, and the like) and just wait for a shout asking for a rezzer. Think about it... how often have you seen a truely poor cleric?

In summation basically if you are looking to farm something don't expect much per item, try to farm things that sell to a vendor rather then a player because that takes alot less time and the price is always the same. Expect to spend alot of your life doing it if you want to get rich. Occasionally you will get lucky. When Verant where about to Nerf the smithing trivials suddenly everyone wanted to get thier stats maxed before the nerf which created a sudden dire need for quality pelts. Personally I made 5K that day selling pelts to a single smith. I personally think that pelts and silks in EK is the way to go. Not at all challenging but as a ranger you can track the mobs and find them... and if you have sow you can get there quicker then the others.
