I understand how frustrating it can be to log on and not get a group. You have my sympathy. I also know that things can and do get better. Here are some things that work for me:

1. I make my own group. It is the ONLY way I ever get a group.

Sometimes my own group is a pally and a druid. Other times it is a full group with the cleric/chanter combo. But by far the majority of XP I do is with what is called a "gimp" group. We hit Nexus for KEI and head out. Sure it limits WHAT we can hunt but what we do hunt =XP. Sure it can be slower but we are hunting and having a good time, cause well these are my friends.

2. I can and do solo. HoH has those glorious suits that do not summon, so snare, dot, bowkite, rinse and repeat. Pure aa's is where I am now so the XP is nice for time invested. As a ranger I think it is a given that I am going to solo... Rangers are that good! :D

3. I do not go LFG in /ooc or in /gu. Tells to friends and guildies get more done.

4. Call me crazy but I like to fish. Then I take the fish and turn it into a baked product for food for my druid friend who is my bestest healer/hunting partner. So when friends or guildies are not available I fish or tradeskill. Feeding halfling druids works wonders for you!

5. Quests take up a lot of productive time. I usually have one or two on the backburner for those dead periods.

6. Being in a guild also means we have raids which take up the other part of login time.

So basically when I log in I am never bored. I have Plan A and Plan B and so forth.

Everquest has much one can do.......you just have to decide what you want and then how to get there.
