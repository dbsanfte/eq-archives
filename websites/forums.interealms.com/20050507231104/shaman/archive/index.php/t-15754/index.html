Vorpatril
My little party (62 shaman, 62 monk, 47 mage) has lived in Sebilis for quite a while. Generally, we've been farming for cash and exp for the mage. Most of the mobs are light blue or low blue to the lvl 62s. The killing is easy as most of the mobs hit for under 140 and have low ac and hp. The lvl 62's averaged maybe 1 blue of real exp per hour.

We got bored of Sebilis and decided to move to PoP. Since we weren't sure of the monk's tanking ability vs the much harder hitting mobs of PoP, we start out with the trash mobs of PoN - ravens, treants and spiders. We fought for two hours chain pulling without too much undue difficulty.

At the end of the two hours, it appeared that we had made roughly two blue of real exp - or almost exactly what we would have gotten in Sebilis. A little research reveals that the mobs we were fighting were roughly lvl 52 - or about the lvl of a Krup frog.

So, in summary, we fought PoP mobs that were MUCH harder than Sebilis mobs, but received almost the exact same amount of exp per hour. I can't help but wonder if this applies across the board - will lvl 58 UP monkies that hit for 200 give roughly the same exp as lvl 58 PoP mobs that hit for 500?

Yes, I know that now the 6-person group fighting a steady stream of lvl 60+ mobs is going to have maximum exp. But if you are fighting small-groups like mine, I have to wonder if the old world is a hidden mine of exp.

This is an update to the patch message posted in another link. The most significant change is that the reward for PoP is not going to be implemented at this time. Read on:

June 11, 2003 3:00 am
------------------------------

** Experience System Change - Grouping **

We've made some improvements to the way experience is rewarded in EverQuest. These improvements are aimed at positively reinforcing the act of grouping. After all, this is a Massively Multiplayer game.

Prior to this update groups gained a 2% to 20% experience bonus for having two to six members.

As of today this bonus has increased to range from 20% to 80% for having two to five members. When a group adds a sixth member, the 80% bonus remains, but the experience gained is only divided by 5 before being distributed. The sixth group member no longer causes the experience gain to be divided by 6.

Planes of Power zones had an increased experience award above and beyond those of most other zones. Because of these new dramatic improvements to group experience gains, we will be reducing the zone-specific experience bonus for Planes of Power zones a bit. As long as character is grouped with at least one other in these zones, they will see an improvement in experience gain over the old system. The one down side to the new system is that there will be a small decrease in experience for those who do not group, and only for those who do so in Planes of Power zones.


** Experience System Change - Level 60+ **

In addition to the above changes, we have increased the range of NPC levels that give a character experience after they reach level 60. This will open up many of the "old world" zones to players previously restricted to Planes of Power zones for experience gain. The range of NPCs that will be "blue" to a level 65 character has been increased as far as level 50, with the "light blue" range extending to level 45. The best experience gains, however, are still for fighting things around and above your level. This should open up areas for high level characters in groups and solo.

Guys, I think a lot of you missed his point. He's wondering if hunting in PoP with his already established trio is a good idea xp wise.

My answer would be no.

Your mage friend's pet would be shredded the first time an unslowed add even sneezed on him (that's assuming you're fighting mobs even halfway challenging to the 62s). Fighting trash in PoN/PoI/PoD isn't worth the time, effort, or aggravation. You have a nice spot (and believe me, I've been looking into Seb to level my shaman) and it works for you. PoP isn't a good idea IMO since, as you mentioned, the mobs are tougher for approximately the same reward (except the loot part).

I'm assuming you're not in this for the 'great xp' if you have a 47 mage with you so that being the case, stick to the old world stuff and forget about PoP til your Mage gets fairly deep into the 50s. At 55, s/he can accompany you to PoV or PoS where the xp would be appreciably better (because the mage would be able to contribute on more equal footing).

Killing Krups gives you a shot at Sebilisian Berserker Cloaks and Runebranded Girdles. There aint NOTHING of similar value ever gonna drop off a blood raven or treant.

I know people rave about LoY but I just don't see the allure. From what I've been able to tell (playing a then-45 ranger in Gunthak and Dulak's and later the 65 rogue in Torgiran Mines and Nadox), the mobs have more hp/ac and hit harder than mobs of comparable level elsewhere. If I'm going there with a group of friends, that's one thing. But to solo or 2box, there are other places where my time is better spent IMO. I don't have LoY on Amorae's account atm and there are only 2 reasons for me to get it: 1) 8 extra bank slots, 2) Stoicism. Coin splits are decent but as mentioned, there's better available elsewhere.

My advice is stick to what's working for you now. Anyplace you go that's challenging for the 62s, the 47 either won't be able to come with you (i.e. BoT/HoH/PoV/PoS) or if they can come with you, they probably wouldn't be able to contribute much (deep Nurga, Chardok B, etc).
