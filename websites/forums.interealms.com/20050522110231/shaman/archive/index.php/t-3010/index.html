Some additional info...

Ogres can use bearform to reduce size, and it works outdoors. Though visibly very large bearform ogres can fit into places that ogres cannot, mostly negating the size penalty of ogres. There is an early version of this spell in SoL I believe.

After having gotten 100+ aa points, I _strongly_ recommend that a shaman get quickbuff 3, spellcasting mastery 3 and spellcasting reinforcement (in addition to the skills listed in the faq) before working on other archtype or class skills.

Quickbuff 3 reduces cast time of buffs by 50percent, most notably Group Focus, from 26 to 13, CELERITY from 8 to 4 and TORPOR from 6 to 3. I don't think I need to explain why this is good.

Spellcasting Mastery 3 in average reportedly reduces mana cost of spells by 15%. That adds up. It also should reduce fizzle rate.

Spellcasting reinforcement adds 50 percent to the endurance of any buff spell. Most notably this includes avatar, primal avatar, and torpor. Unfortunately it does not seem the effect group buff spells, at least not on people other than the caster. This may have been fixed. With SCRM, torpor lasts 6 ticks instead of 4. The usefulness of this should be obvious.

These AA skills affect many other spells than listed above.

One thing missed was the effect wisdom has on fizzles. Each point of wisdom reduces your fizzle rate by 1%, you can never have a fizzle rate higher than 95% or lower than 5%. I used to know the formula for determining fizzle rates but that is long forgotten now.

Another subject: FOCUS ITEMS.

Improved Healing III: This affects healing spells by adding an addition random 1 to 20 percent effect (drop fractions) to any heal spell. AA skills do not increase this effect, but otherwise do stack.

Extended affliction III: Seems to add one tick to poison dots and four ticks to our disease dots. It does NOT affect non-dot spells. It is not know what it does to our epic dot, if anything.

Affliction haste III: Reduces dot cast time by 1/3.

Affliction efficiency III: Reduces mana cost of dots by 25%.

Spell haste III: Reduces cast time of ALL spells shaman cast by 10%.

Mana efficiency III: Reduces mana cost of ALL spells shaman cast by 10%.

STA buffing - also might note under the stat buffs that buffing Stamina can help alleviate problems with fatigue(yellow bar, stamina bar, whatever ya wanna call it). If STA > 100, max fatigue means nothing more than you are unable to jump. If STA < 100, max fatigue has a negative effect on combat ability (I believe less chance to hit and/or hitting for less when you do connect?) and IIRC mana regen rates (?) If a melee character doesn't have 100+ stamina unbuffed then it's worthwhile to buff STA at any level, and a tank with under 100 stamina is most likely a low level tank anyway. STA also increase time you can hold your breath, so when EB fades you don't start drowning so quickly if fighting underwater. ;)

Buff Order stuff...

Or you can just be a buff nazi and tell the tanks and whoever else to suck it up and you'll buff them as you get mana to spare and they'll like it or go without! /ROAR

I typically do a stat buff first(STA), then hp(Focus), then another stat buff a minute or 3 later (AGI). At lower levels or when without a cleric I'd do AC after that followed by the other stat buffs. If I'm hasting I'll do that first and immediately follow with STA and hp buffs. Obviously SoW gets in first round too if outdoors.

Why? you may be asking... So they don't ALL wear off within a 5 minute interval!!!

I buff everyone that needs that particular buff at once. So, I chain cast the short term ones as needed (SoW, Haste, Regen) then chain cast through STA on everyone (group now that I have that available) and then the HP buffs as fast as they refresh. Then I wait a couple minutes to cast the next round so that they don't ALL wear off at the same time. It takes a few minutes extra to get everyone buffed, but during that time we can go ahead and start killing anyway. I'm cannibalizing to regen mana during the fights for the next round, and when the buffs DO start fading, I've cast them on ME first so I have a warning and can immediately start rebuffing before the rest of the group even has a chance to bug me for them. Since I've spread the buffs out, I refresh 1-2 buffs at any given time and am still left with mana to slow, thus the group can keep on killing and stay fully buffed.

I know several shaman that do the same, but I also have seen plenty that just bufff everything all at once, then when the first buff starts fading everything else fades right after and the shaman is screaming OOM while s/he frantically tries to get everyone rebuffed as needed... A wee bit of thought put into the buffing up front makes it a lot easier to keep them up all night without slowing the killing pace at any time! ;)

What Are My Skills, When do I Get Them, and When are the Maxed?

There are basically 2 different sets of skills a Shaman will Posess.

The First is his Melee Skills, they consist of:

Piercing (one handed piercing only)
One-Hand Blunt
Two-Hand Blunt
Hand to Hand
Dodge (You do not recieve this skill until lvl 15 and need to put one point into it at the trainer to start using this skill)

A Shaman can increase his skill in these (2) ways. First by actively using them in the field(meaning attacking creatures). You can only gain skill increases by attacking experience giving creatures(ie, light blue and above). The second way to gain skills in these fields is by using the Shaman trainer, just beware it can get quite expensive when your skills are near their cap.

The Formula for determining what is max skill for a given level is -
(lvl x 4)+ 4 and the Max skill you can attain in each is as follows:
Piercing - 200
One-Hand Blunt - 200
Two-Hand Blunt - 200
Hand to Hand - 75
Dodge - 75

A Subset of Melee is your offense and defense skill. You gain both of these at lvl one. The only difference is that you cannot train these at a trainer, you need to actively attack and be attacked in order for these skills to go up. Do not let these skills lag especially Defense. Since every point you gain in Defense will raise your AC lvl. Offense and Defense skill raise by a different equation. Your skills will raise by (lvl X 5) with a max skill of 200.

Also at lvl 1 we recieve the skill Bind Wounds. With this skill you can bandage yourself or others to help them heal up HP faster.
This skill has two different skill caps: The first being a skill of 200. You can only raise your skill in Bind Wounds over a skill lvl of 200 once you have leveled your character up to lvl 51. Once you have reached a lvl of 51 you can increase your Bind Wound Skill to a lvl of 201 and eventually to a skill of 210 by lvl 60. The importance of this is that with a Bind Wound Skill of 200 and under, you can only Bind a person up to 50% of their Health. But once you reach a skill of 201 you can Bind them up to 70% of their health.
The Formula for skill increases in Bind Wounds is (lvl X 5) +5 .

The Second Set of Skills a Shaman must be concerned with is His/Her Casting Abilities. There are 5 different casting abilities, and we recieve them all at lvl 1; they are :

Abjuration(for casting our AC buffs, as well as our resistance buffs and our quickness line)
Alteration(for casting our Stat Buffs and heals, as well as our SLOWS and our other debuffs)
Conjuration(for casting our summoning spells, as well as our DOTs{damage over time} spells)
Evocation(for casting our direct damage spells)
Divination(for casting our Sight spells)

The Formula for skill increase is as follows : (lvl X 5)+5 and they all max out at 235.

In order to cast any spells you need to have Mana, which is our magical energy. Each spell takes a certain amount of mana to cast. Once you have depleted all of your mana you must wait until it regenerates. A skill that we recieve at lvl 8 helps this process to go faster. At lvl 8 we recieve the skill Meditate. What this skill does is while you are sitting you will regain your mana at a considerable amount faster then standing. All you need to do to activate your Meditate skill is to sit. This is another skill that you need to put one point into it at the trainer in order to start using. Prior to lvl 8 and you obtaining your Meditate skill, it doesnt matter if you are standing or sitting to regain mana, you will regain it at the same speed. The Formula for skill increases is as follows : (lvl X 5)+5. This skill Maxes at 235 for people lvl 50 and under, and will max out totally at 252 between lvls 51 and 60.

There are two other skills we also get to help us with our spell casting. The first is Channelling. This skill gives us a better chance of casting a spell with out interuption, or being physically attacked. We first obtain this skill at lvl 4 and need to put one point into it at our trainer to start using the skill.

The Formula for our Channelling Skill is - (lvl X 5)+5 and it maxes at a skill of 200 for people lvl 50 and under and will totally cap at 220 between lvls 51-60.

Also at lvl 30 we obtain a choice of Specializing in one of our Casting abilities. Specializing helps reduce the mana cost on the spells in that casting ability. You can choose any of the 5 casting abilities to Specialize in. The 2 Most common are Alteration, for our heals, buffs and Slows; or Conjuration for our DOTs. You play style should dictate which you choose. Once you reach lvl 30 you must put a point into Specialization for each of the 5 Casting Abilites. The first one to reach a skill lvl of 50 will be the one you have chosen to Specialize in. That Casting ability will be able to reach a Specialization lvl of 235 while the other 4 will only reach a lvl of 50 Specialization. If you know what Casting Ability that you want to Specialize in, your best bet to ensure that is to cast the lowest lvl spell in the casting ability over and over again on yourself until it reaches a skill lvl of 51, then just go about your business and it as well as the others will raise on their own. If you are unsure which Casting Ability to choose, you should put one point into each and let your play style decide for you. Whichever Spells you cast the most will cause that Casting Ability to raise faster and that will be the one that you Specialize in.

The Formula for Specialization is - (lvl X 5)+5
