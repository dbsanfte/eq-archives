Shalara De'Tan
Shaman will not be the *easiest* class on the list. Quite probably the most versatile and fun of the three, but not the easiest if you are set on soloing all the time.

While a shaman *can* solo from 1 - 60, there are lulls in the life of a shaman where soloing can be quite painful. You hit one that lasts several levels just before you get Dog Dog at level 34, and there is another rough spot before 49. (And I won't even mention 52-59, which was pure unmitigated hell for me, grouped or solo -- although that was before the balancing was done and we got new healing spells. Oops, i mentioned it! ;)) In fact, if you look at the shaman spell list, the last three levels before a new poison dot upgrade are usually pretty rough on a soloing shaman.

If you are set on ONLY soloing, no matter what, and will get frustrated when it gets rougher, then druid is probably your best bet. (Dang, its VERY hard to encourage another drood out there ;)) If you decide to go with druid, take a look at the halflings and see if thier appearance appeals. They have good stats, and they actually have a racial bonus -- they need 5% less XP than the base human to level.

Also, worrying about cash seems to be the biggest hobby of the shaman ;) Nearly everyone wants to get a JBB by level 45 and a Fungi Tunic as soon as they can swing it. That's 40-70k worth of items, depending on your server economy. And shaman have no easy way to make money. Alchemy is a joke for making cash, unless you want to spend HOURS raising skill, crafting SoW potions, and then /auctioning them constantly. You could make 100pp in 2 minutes of porting around a desparate person. It'll take you a lot more time than that to make 100pp with alchemy.

I don't mean to make it sound like Shaman suck. On the contrary, Shaman are awesome! I've played Shalara since release, and I enjoy logging in and being a shaman as much today as ever. I just don't think that shaman really meet the requirements that you set in your post.

blakkon
It depends on other factors as well.

All three classes have their strong points. However, if you want to stand toe to toe with mobs then shaman is your only real choice. But dont be mislead into thinking your melee damage will ever be anything more than incidental after the late teens.

Of those three there is another factor to consider. Ease of play. I think Shaman are the most powerful and versatile of the three (else why would i play one ;)). In the long run they can go places and do things that Druids and Necros can't do. However... To do this you need to learn to play a class that can change dramatically every few levels.

Necros are probably the easiest (faction issues aside). They can go 60 levels with darkness, fear, dots and pet. They can do alot more of course but this will always be their dominant strategy and it primarily works outdoors. Any situation where you get several mobs at once results in a feigned or actual dead necro if they are solo. This rules out alot of indoor camps, though some undead things are easily doable. In a group, necros can actually be very useful, though they are often underappreciated and sometimes spurned. On raids you will pump mana.

Druids travel the best of the three. They also take abuse like a wet paper towel. If Necros spend their careers with things running away from them, then the opposite is true of druids. You will always be running from something (hopefully snared and maybe rooted). Starting in your mid levels you can quadkite which is a very easy way to gain exp. but you wont be doing this inside. You will get groups alot easier than a necro. You can evac, backup heal and do some buffing. If you get agro you will also die quickly. So qucikly in some cases peopel may think you were the victim of a bug. On raids you have soem buffing. Then will do chloroblasts until oom or nuke if you don't get resisted too much.

Shaman start their life as a tank. You can melee alot further than any other pure casters. With slows you can (and need to) tank exp mobs all the way to 60. As a soloer you have a bit more variety. You can kite, root-rot, melee and dot, tank for pet. Depending on your spell level your strategy may vary. You have excellent mana regen so are rarely oom (unless also out of hp). Groups love you. Soem raids cant be done easily without you, though you may not stack well with too many other shaman.
