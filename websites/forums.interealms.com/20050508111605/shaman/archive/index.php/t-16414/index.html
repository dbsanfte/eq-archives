Well, I haven't actually played a beastlord at all, and my shaman is still sub-L60, but I can partly answer your questions, I think, having been around a long time...

I think there's very little a beastlord can do with multiple mobs. They can slow, but slowing the add will probably pull aggro off their pet. I think it would be hard for a beastlord to keep 2, much less 3 or 4, mobs all on their pet, while healing it enough to keep it alive. And a beastlord lacks CC beyond slow, so those adds would end up beating on him, and often killing him, I'd think.

My L 56 shaman, on the other hand, can take 3 or sometimes even 4 dark blue cons at once, if he's either outside (can use horse) or has KEI. Root is a powerful CC tool, and can let you split mobs up that a beastlord would have to tank all at once (or have his pet tank). I can also kill many yellow cons solo, even without malo/torpor/all the uberstuff a higher level shaman has. Haven't tried any reds yet though. ;) Shaman is one of the strongest classes if you want to kill a powerful mob solo, especially if you want to be manly about it and not kite. ;)

I dont' know what gear your shaman has, but, in my mind, the holy foursome (what's "four" for trinity? hehe) for a leveling shaman is a horse, a regen BP, a JBB, and the epic. You're already starting to surpass the levels where the JBB makes a BIG difference, although it's still very useful. So if you're iksar don't worry about not being able to use one. If you're troll or iksar, the regen BP is less important, also. But all of those items really help, because they give you faster mana regeneration or mana-free damage. Try to get any of those that you don't have, and it'll really help. I have accumulated all 4 on my shaman, and I just don't have mana problems. Sure, I can run out, but unless it's a really big pull, I always have more mana than I need. I recover it much faster than almost any other class, so the group is rarely or never waiting on me.

If you're having mana problems due to buffing, not sure what to tell you. It's easy to run out of mana buffing a group, but it should come back fast. Best general advice I can give is to space out the buffs. Do Kragg on whoever needs it. Five minutes later, do Infusion of Spirit. Only give them to people who might actually need them. And so on. If you try to do all your buffs on everyone at once, you will be very oom. Without knowing more about how you play it's hard to suggest anything specific.

I started duo boxing about 2 years ago,

I started a shm / sk duo, then dropped the sk at 30. Then I pl a monk to 50. Didn't like it either, so I made a ranger. Got the rng to 57 and shm to 58 when I got bored to zoning on 3 mob pulls, and lack of mana regen on ranger. So I pl my bst, and have never looked back.

They are both 65 now with 102/103 aa each.

Since, I've pl the bst, I opted to play split tanking. The key I found is to slow 1 mob, seek warder on it. Pull others away from pet, via sit aggro. Slow another add [should aggro shm at this point]. Root the mob that aggro the shm, this should bring 3rd mob with bst. Root that one. So bst is tanking 2, while dog and warder is chewing away from 1st mob. This might sound tough or easy, but it's rather trivial for me now when I duo.

Equipment wise for this duo, I went after anything that has 5+ ac, 30+ hp, some resists, and focus item. And the way I do this is to converse mana. Don't over do dots, nukes. Bst needs mana to heal pet, and nuke to gain aggro, and shm need mana to root and debuff.

Up to level 60, it was rough. Just pick your targets wisely. 1-5 newbie, 6-24 PC, 24-35 DSP, 36-52 EC [Task Master], 53-55 DSP [Rockhoppers], 56-58 ME [nice exp and good upgrades], 58-62 PoD flies, 62-65 PoJ [MH].

Now that my shm has Q, FA. Bst with Sha's Revenge and Ferocity. Things are much easier. Paragon + Ext. Buff = Uber, Pet Hold is crucial for pulling casters and manage pet aggro.

Hope this helps.
