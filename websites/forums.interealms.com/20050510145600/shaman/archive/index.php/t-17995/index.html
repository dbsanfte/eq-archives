Jinheim
The pull is the only tricky part. Two monks should be able to do it just fine, although they might die a few times learning it. Once you have him single in the arena, the monk jumps over the side walls to where your raidforce waits. Vindi will jump over the wall as well, at which point you have a ranger use weaponshield disc and go wild on the aggro. Once his disc is about to wear off (12 or 15 seconds I think) your MT uses AE taunt and pulls vindi in to the corner. Hopefully the ranger lives, but it isn't necessary.

When you corner him, your MT stands with his back in the corner, so that he faces ******d. Since your tank's hitbox is smaller than vindi's, if you also push vindi in the corner, he will have to look in to the corner in order to attack your MT. This means that only your MT will be in front, and everyone else will be able to attack from behind, meaning higher DPS, no ripostes, and melee can attack through enrage (though if your tank is a sadistic F'er like ours, he will turn the mob around for enrage and see how many rogues he can kill).

It is important that your start your CH loop BEFORE your tank has aggro. Have your clerics start the loop as soon as vindi is over the wall, and just duck out if the MT doesn't have aggro yet. This loop preparation time is a second bonus to using the weaponshield tactic.

If you have a tank successfully holding aggro on a mob, and clerics successfully looping CH on him, then you've more or less beaten everquest. Good luck.

Goldark
Not seen much talk about rampage, the other key points have been covered.

You need one dedicated rampage tank, and one person healing them. On the pull they need to get a very small amount of aggro right at the start. This can be before or after the MT aggros, but must be before a single heal lands on the MT and must be low enough aggro that the MT will aggro Vindi without fail.

A shaman with quiessence and tnarg's can heal almost any 60+ knight/warrior on rampage with no major problems. Rampage can be spiky though so if in doubt, assign another healer to help out. Palladins make good rampage tanks as they can heal themselves if they think its looking bad. If you use an SK on rampage, make sure they don't FD.... I've had it happen and it cleared them from rampage :(

If you mess up on the rampage tank, Vindi will soon rampage on the healers, and at that point you're looking at a probable wipe because your CH chain will break.

Other tips:
DS the MT as much as possible, bards are good to have in MT group for the DS and other songs.
If you think mana might be tight, bard in the cleric group
If you can spare it, have a patch healer on the MT. They should start casting a heal aimed to land at about 30-40% health on the MT. If at that time another heal has landed, duck out. If the MT is below 40% (you can reduce this when you're more comfortable with it) then let the heal run.
Test the range and timing on CH chain before the pull. Timing will depend on tank, somewhere from 3-5 seconds usually (for a first time kill, I'm sure some guilds can use far less).
