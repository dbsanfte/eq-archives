Azhdarkhos
Here's a report from personal experience up through the 7th shawl:

I'm one of those lunatics that actually like doing quests and tradeskills. If you're not seriously into both of these, you definitely want to skip this series of quests.

One piece of good news: failing on a combine does not destroy the shawl, so you never have to start the series completely from scratch.

The tailoring skill required for the 5th shawl is roughly 135. You can get to that skill level doing the Crystal Silk armor using the silk you get from spiders in Crystal Caverns. This shawl requires you to combine Crystal Silk Threads (no-drop, stackable - drop off of the transparent spiders in Velk's Lab) into silk fibres and then into glowing silk. To make all the glowing silk you need for the final combine, you need 75 crystal silk threads (assuming no failures.) That's a lot of spiders. You also need 5 Glowing Wurm Biles from the wurms wandering around in Great Divide - not a common drop, but if you hunt at night any wurm carrying one will glow in the dark.

The 6th shawl is the make or break point for most people doing the quest series - the trivial on the final combine is up around 200, which means your success rate won't be great. I was at 158 tailoring when I completed this quest (same for the 7th - 158 requires making Wu's Fighting Armor to achieve, and it requires a metric truckload of regular spider silk, 2 metric truckloads of Heady Kiolas and a really patient enchanter willing to make a huge pile of mana vials). I failed on the first attempt, succeeded on the second attempt. The items needed to make the thread used aren't that hard to get, just a bit tedious - patience pays off.

The 7th shawl requires a fairly high level in almost all of the trade skills (all but smithing, which evidently is used in the 8th.) The other tradeskills basically just require lots of cash and lots of patience - you can buy everything you need to raise the skills from vendors. All of my tradeskills were in the 180-190 range. I had no failures on any of the combines, but even at that skill level you always have at least a 5% chance of failure on any combine. The trivial on the final combine (tailoring) to make the 7th shawl is a lot lower than the trivial for the 6th shawl, so odds are a lot better of success (reports say the trivial is 162, only 4 points higher than my skill level at the time.)

From the looks of things, the 8th shawl will require smithing Velium Armor. Reports put the trivial on the armor close to 200, and it requires numerous Velium Tempers (at 26pp a pop) and Diamonds and other expensive gems which are processed using jewelcraft again.

NOPE.

Here's why: Your skillups are dependent on wis/int/str/dex, depending on what trade you're talking about. You likely won't break 200 wis until after you hit level 50. You can buff yourself to the nines, and you'll still spend far too much time and money doing the skillups, much more than the high levels who already have the wis gear to make the difference.

If you want to skill up on cheap easy stuff before 50, take a look at pottery, baking and fletching. These are relatively cheap and easy.

Fletched goods sell back to vendors for not much less than the cost of the materials.

If you want to raise pottery skill cheaply, DON'T buy firing sheets. You get your skillups on the wheel, not in the kiln. Destroy the unfired stuff. The firing trivial is very low.

Bake fish rolls. They trivial at 135, take one fish (use your fishing skill.. you need it for the shawls anyway) and one batwing (from freeport mage's guild or shadowhaven) and combine in an oven. Not only dead cheap, but lucrative. A stack can sell for up to 10pp.

Keep those old world wolf, cat and bear pelts until you're a bit higher level. Same with spider silks. You'll thank yourself later, when your higher wis leads to faster skillups without all that tedious hunting of greenies.

Also, spend some time at http://www.eqtraders.com. You'll get a good idea of what's required for the trades. Make no mistake, tradeskills are going to be a focus in the next expansion. It's a good idea to be a tradesperson, but get your levels and make it easy on yourself :)
