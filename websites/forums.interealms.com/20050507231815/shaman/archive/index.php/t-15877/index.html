dugann
The NPC receiving the item keeps track of what was gotten, not who gave it, so you dont have to both have trade windows open at the same time. Last person turning in that completes the quest gets the item.

As an example, back when I was doing totemic armor, I turned in all the components but one of them was wrong. 2 days later I ended up with the piece I wanted, went back and turned it in and still received the reward.

Jinheim
Here's an example of an MQ:

Jboots requires these things:

the ring off ancient cyclops (NO DROP)
shadowed rapier off shadowed men (droppable)
3250? gold coins (droppable, obviously)

One person typically farms the hard to get piece (the ring) and then goes and rounds up a shadowed rapier. He then shouts that he's selling MQ's for a certain price (usually 5kish on my server). Someone wants to buy it, so they go to the turn in guy in rathe mountains. The farmer gives the rapier and the coins to the other player that wants the ring. Then, the farmer gives the ring to the turn in guy. After that, the other guy gives him the rapier and the gold to the turn in NPC, the quest is completed, and the right player recieves the jboots.

Works the same way with thurg armor. One person turns in the armor, the other person turns in the 3 gems.

There is a problem with this, though. Lets say that I was feeling evil. I could go to the turn in guy for jboots and give him 3250 gold and a shadowed rapier. I wouldn't get anything, but the next time an MQ was attempted, the initial turn in of the ring would complete the quest and the WRONG person would get the boots.
