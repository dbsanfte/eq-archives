here's how i do it, i've only failed once due to my own stupidity.

group make up:
a nice tank. this is pretty important from what i see. i've only tried it with quality tanks, and even then he came close to dying (with only me healing). recommended 8khp+(buffed) w/ full tanking AA's.
dps
dps
dps
dps
cleric w/ supernal elixir OR if u have a nice tank, a shaman with quiescence.

basically what i do, when we zone into the room, everyone runs to the front right corner out of aggro range of the mobs except for the tank. he stays within aggro range when the mobs pop.

when the mobs all pop, they all aggro the tank. everyone assists the tank and kills the mobs one by one, concentrating ur DPS only on one mob at a time so u can kill them quickly and reset the walking guy. healers heal ONLY with heal over times. any kind of normal heal, CH, fast heal, whatever, will drag ALL the other mobs that the tank isn't beating onto u, and healer dead = game over.

for this reason, you need a nice tank. you gotta make sure the heal over time will be enough healing for him to stay up while 5-6 mobs are hitting him. of course it's easier with a cleric since supernal elixir is 500hp/tick i think, but i was able to do it with Q (300hp/tick). the reason why i failed (like i stated above), is because my tank was reaching 25% hp, and i was scared because Q wasn't healing fast enough, so i used tnargs, and that promptly got ALL the mobs on me and i died and game over. i had lost faith in Q. but from then on, i used ONLY Q, and while it came to the tank with 25% hp, it has ALWAYS held out till all the mobs were dead. have faith in quiescence/supernal elixir.

if u can do this, it should be no problem. i never bother slowing either. the key part is just using the heal over time so the other mobs won't aggro onto the healer. you guys will ONLY be touching 1 mob at a time (for faster kills, and reset the walking guy), so that one non-HoT heal will be enough to drag the other mobs onto u since nothing else has touched them.

since Quiescence uses so little mana, i was also able to nuke between the Q's fading, which i'm sure helped some.

good luck

Kriel Darkbane
Well, I have a different perspective on how to do this. I usually play a bard, and haven't gotten my L 58 shaman through the trial yet. So with that word of caution, here's my advice. First of all, that group should be able to do it.

1) If you're struggling, get DS potions for everyone. The extra damage WILL help. When you get to the later mobs in each wave, they've taken a little damage, and will die quicker. This is helpful if your nukers are getting low mana. DS up everyone with your mage or druid.

2) The mage can banish the rock guys with a single cast. This counts as killing one. If you can, leave a rock guy up each wave, and if you get down close to the wire, the mage can banish a virtually unhurt mob. This virtually guarantees beating that wave, as long as you don't have a death. Druids can do the same thing. Take both classes as your nukers and any wave with a rock guy in it should be nearly trivial.

3) Fundamental change in tactics. I disagree with some of the other posters about how to do this. Mez? Totally unnecessary. ONLY the main tank getting aggro? Totally unnecessary, and counterproductive, if you're using the DS strategy I mentioned above. You have a cleric, a shaman, AND a paladin. You have backup healing up the wazoo. This means that it should NOT matter if you or the cleric get aggro. If you have a big DS, this is good! And if there's an emergency, you have a paladin to lay hands. Back at L 61/62 when PoP had just come out, my bard and my wife's cleric got a LOT of people through this trial. We did it over and over back when very few people were winning. We were generally averaging L 61.5, with an average of 40-50 AA's, some people less (like the cleric). The key was that my wife's cleric wasn't afraid of aggro. She'd heal, she'd end up with a couple mobs beating on her, she'd live until we got to them. She could keep herself alive even if she was the only healer, usually. I'd mez at most one mob, as that could be done quickly. But even with 4 unmezzables she'd make it. So it isn't THAT hard to keep a healer alive. If it is, get some AC on that cleric. As the cleric, I'd suggest a self-HOT as soon as the mobs spawn, then a heal on the shaman after the Tigir's. Then spam heal of self if needed. I'm not sure it will be needed much, if the Tigir's did well.

Anyway... this weakens the DS strategy, but I'd use Tigir's Insects as your only CC for each wave. 4 Spawn. Arrange positioning so they all rush the tank. You Tigir's. Typically you'll affect 3, making keeping the healers alive pretty damn easy at that point. The tank will have aggro from one, and you and the cleric can bounce aggro around from the others with heals as needed. The Tank will need basically no healing, since he'll likely have one slowed mob on him. The 3 melees can all beat on one target, plus the mage pet. The mage can banish if needed. Finally... since you've AE slowed, and you and/or the cleric have signiificant aggro from heals... the mage can AE. They have some NASTY PB AE and targetted AE spells. The use of AE nukes in this trial is INVALUABLE. If the mage can drop a couple AE's while you're killing the first mob, the others are all down SIGNIFICANT HPs when you get to them. And the melees and two pets can down them fast enough even without nukes, if mana is low. As a shaman, you can try AE rains if you have time/mana, depending on how much you or the cleric is getting pounded.

The last time I did this trial, with my bard, I just AE slowed constantly, along with the group DS/resist song. I didn't mez at all. We did it easily, but our group was higher powered than yours will be.

It's hard to know WHY you're losing, since you didn't say anything about what's going wrong. But, some more random bits of advice: keep MR as high as possible on everyone. Melees getting slowed will hurt you badly. Someone (probably you, maybe the cleric) has to be ready to cure slows as fast as possible. If someone gets slowed, they have to announce it immediately, and you have to cure it as quickly as possible. This is the area my bard makes a group shine, as his resist song means no one is ever slowed, unless I let the song drop from my twist.

Ok, that's a good tip too: Get a bard if you can. If he twists Psalm of Veeshan, Melody of Mischief, and wind of Marr he's gonna make things a LOT easier. His low-resist AE slow means he'll have a fair amount of aggro, and even if he doesn't the healers will be only getting hit by 45% slowed mobs. Makes it easy for the druid/magician/wizard to AE nuke. DON'T get a bard who's going to waste his time mez'ing.

One final caveat: Mezzing can work perfectly well if you have a good mez'r, and the rest of the group is high DPS. But I really don't like that style here. If you have a chanter, AE stunlock and bring a couple AE nukers. If you have a bard, have him AE slow/dot, group resist buff, and suck up the hits. If you don't have either, you AE slow and backup heal. So at least 4 of the other characters can focus purely on damage dealing.

Well, I guess that's enough without some more specific questions. Guess I got going on this one. ;)
