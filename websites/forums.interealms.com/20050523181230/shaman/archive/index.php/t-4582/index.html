If you are ever-present in the bazaar, you can get some really good deals on some things.

Some low level items are often sold at BELOW the sell-back to vendor price, offering the savvy buyer a chance to pick up an item, sell it to an npc, and make a little cash in under a minute.

Some old popular items that are now obsolete with the advent of Luclin can be purchased for very little money. Much of this is still good gear for mid-level to mid-50's characters that don't have the benefit of a guild backing their equipment upgraes.

There is a booming discount resist gear market on old JC made jewelry, with BD bracers and such often sold at LESS than the cost of the BD at current market values. /boggle

You can get some fantastic deals on some common drop spells that you don't have yet, and many of the turn-in quest spells are sold at 50pp or less, giving you a chance to fill out a few of those low 50's spells without a lot of the heartache that I went through.

Unfortunately though, the rare spell market has some rampant inflation going on, with spell prices sometimes changing HOURLY, and not to the customer's benefit in many cases on upper level (shaman in particular) spells.

Unfortunately, the very small minority of professional traders generally snap the good deals up and then immediately resell them at a higher price, often grossly inflated. :( I can relate to the "uber" guild's desire to unload their spell vaults for good cash, and even if they offer at a reasonable price, the sharks snap them up and put them up with an extra 20kpp tacked on for their profit. /sigh

My advice. Cultivate contacts in the most powerful guilds on your server, and try to negotiate a fair pricing scheme face-face or in /tells instead of with a level 1 unguilded rogue named Benduoverforspells. :p
YMMV

Bigguss
A few observations:

First and foremost, the bazaar is not the place to be looking for high-end gear. Generally most high-end gear that's up there is priced waaaay too high, due to silly speculators or people like me... who, when setting up their trader before going to bed, fill the remaining slots in their satchels with their main droppable gear- priced high enough that if someone actually buys it, I'll be able to quickly buy a replacement and make a nice profit. I sold a bracer of sense for 18k the other day (paid 10k three days before), and no one was more stunned then me !

Really it's nothing more than the economy at work. If the fungi is not worth X, no one will buy it from the speculators. If it is, the speculators will make a profit. Over time, those that speculate and overinflate will find themselves with excess inventory they can't get rid of. In the meantime, as perceived normal prices raise, people with that gear in their inventory will be more and more tempted to sell it ("I can get 70k for the fungi I've got that sits in a pack most of the time since I got torpor? Wow, I paid 30k for it only six months ago! I'll sell it for 60k to get a quick sale and try to find Malo!")

I've done some speculating (although almost exclusively on mid-level items, and driven by buying super low- my rule of thumb is no more than 33% of the regular market price- then selling low- generally 66% of the market price) and think it's a great way to make some cash. For that matter, buying from the /trade channel, and reselling in the bazaar can be a nice way to generate cash while you sleep. :)

That people take the high-end gear to ridiculous prices and that high-end people take those prices seriously is indicative of the huge disparity in wealth between the mid and high level game, and the similar disparity between the high and uber level game. There was a guy a few hours ago in /trade saying "Paying 200k for X, 150k for Y, 100k for X, 150k for ZZ", etc. - people see that and think "Why not try to make a few bucks off a guy like that?" (Of course, he's probably a guild buyer, and those speculators will get stuck with their excessively priced inventory... c'est la vie!)

If there are X of some item available for sale, and more than X people want it... and no other prioritizing factor (friendship, guild membership, etc.) comes into play, why not money? It's a self-correcting system.

Also, don't be afraid to send a tell to a bazaar guy "Would you take 60k and an iksar BP for that fungi?" - you might just be surprised. :)
