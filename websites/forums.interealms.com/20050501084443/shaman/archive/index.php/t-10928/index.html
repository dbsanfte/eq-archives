Sebazy
Work on getting your self buffed AC to around 1100 if your duoing with a rogue, then you can happily tank for him for some additional dps. Past that point, AC isnt worth focusing specifically on imho.

Beyond that, litgruv has it spot on, 200wis is a nice figure to aim for, and of course, Hps > All.

If you can get hit those goals and have a decent HP tally, you'll find that a rogue is actually a pretty fine partner, don't listen to him saying that rogues are no use, he spent far too long in leather /nod =( (Weak attempt at humor before I get flamed!)

If you can get torpor you'll find that with enough avoidance AA's you'll tank trash suprisingly well. If not, get a means of snaring between you 2, load up disempower and just agro kite, its hardly optimal but it works and in the right spot its actually reasonable xp.

Nruter
Rogue/Shaman combo at 60 with Torpor is great, with the addition of the mini complete heal I could picture it at 58.

The problems you will have for a lot of levels is the Shaman will eithere not be able to heal enough to be an efficient tank or the rogue will have to tank and basically be a warrior with less hitpoints/ac and not be able to deal the damage that is the reason your husband is playing the class. If you are starting from level 1 I would strongly recommend against this combo unless you are willing to go through some tough times.

If your husband is set on being a melee I would recommend any other possible choice over a rogue.

Rogues also have it very tough from level 40-50 (no new abilities and damage output does not increase enough to accomidate higher level mobs you will need to fight), so the weakest levels for the rogue will be exactly when the shaman's healing is getting to be insufficient.

I do play both a rogue and a shaman and loved the combination at 60 (Velious--Luclin era), still like the combination but not as much in PoP tier 1.

Shaman/Rogue Duo works really well both pre and post PoP.

To get maximal advantage at levels 60+ the shaman needs to tank.
That is so that the shaman can use his ultra-efficent Torpor heals AND to take advantage of the rogues Backstab damage.

In this situation (shaman tanking - rogue backstabbing) you will supposedly outdamage any other shaman/melee combo.
