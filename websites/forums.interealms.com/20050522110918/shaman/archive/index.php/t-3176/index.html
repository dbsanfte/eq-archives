Don't forget you can also tag-team the malXXX duty with a magician. Mages like to feel useful beyond dumping mod rods on the ground :)

Order is generally:

*) Annul magic, as situationally needed. Can be done as soon as the MA engages as it is generally very low taunt. Helps to have 2-3 casters simultaneously cast annul in hopes of fully debuffing the mob. This is a great job to have necromancers, magicians, wizards, et al. do as they are generally not too occupied with the front-end job. Excellent for removing SOW, HP buffs, AC buffs, and damage shields.

*) Malo/a (malo preferred) and Tash. Can be cast simultaneously or one right after the other, but Malo/a must be cast *after* the dispelling is done. It is very helpful if the slowing shaman casts Malo (see below). Tash may have to wait until the MA builds sufficient aggro. Malo/a has low enough taunt value that it can be done as soon as dispelling is finished.

*) Bard occlusion. Should be in place when resistable debuffs are applied.

*) Malosini. If you are splitting the debuff load, have the mage be in charge of Malosini. It's the exact same as the shaman version, but it lets you share the load a bit. You'll know when it lands because you'll get a message that your Malo spell has worn off.

*) Slow and other debuffs. Hopefully your MA has had sufficient time to build up taunt so that you don't take too much of a beating, but lots of resists will still get you hammered.

If you are in a zone with mezzable mobs, you can also apply the full set of debuffs MINUS the dispelling on mezzed mobs before the MA gets around to them. It is very useful to have an enchanter who religiously memblurs the mobs if you are going to do this, though, as the mobs will generally run straight for you when mez breaks otherwise. Granted, that's often better than the mob running straight for the enchanter, so taking sacrafice hits might be a good tradeoff in some situations.
