Here are my thoughts on how /auction etc should work....that is, if everyone had a shred of conscious, integrity, and/or courtesy for their fellow player :

First > Pay attention to what is being auctioned, do your research, and likewise to the auctioneer...don't list something unless you can answer questions about it. This works both ways.

Second > Even though this IS /auction, everyon knows it isn't REALLY an auction. Try actually "auctioning" something sometime...most people get flamed and/or ridiculed. All this really is is a sales board..you post your item and perhaps it's price. Haggling of course is very welcome (I rarely post a price on anything unless I'm tired of it sitting in my bank and I'm offering it really cheap) and encouraged.

Third > Some commen courtesy ethics here...in my manner of doing things, the first person who sends me a tell on an item has priority. I usually have a set price on an item, I tell them what it is. As long as they can get to me within a reasonable amount of time, it's theirs. Also, if you're interested in buying something, you should darn well make sure you're going to be able to get to the person selling it within a few minutes...I don't care what I'm selling...making someone wait is rude and just plain self assuming that everyone else has time on their hands.
> One other thing....you see someone auctioning something for a ridiculous price and it really bothers you, send them tells. Don't try to make them feel stupid in a public channel. If you feel you need to publically humiliate people, then you obviously have some personal problem...like and inadequacy issue. Just in case you didn't know...you really just get laughed at except by others with inadequacy problems.

Lastly > Imo, it all boils down to respect. Treat others how you would want to be treated, and eventually things might actually work out for you.
