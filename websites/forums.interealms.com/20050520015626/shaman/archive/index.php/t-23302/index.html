taraddar
I know it's still in beta and I know things are subject to change but so far they haven't seen any real changes in a while. These are my own personal point of view and hopefully Sony will see these and at least choose to do something.

First we have 6 Omen-Shaman-PH spells still. Obviously something needs to be done with them. Looking at the info that is left in them they seem to be single target and group diaku which should probably be changed back to dex type spells and should be combat effects like str and agility are being changed. A root upgrade which isn't a big deal either. The malos upgrade which wouldn't be a bad thing. Personally I'd like to see more improvements in the cast time/recast time than in the amount debuffed. Finally single target and group hastes. For single target biggest request would be LONGER DURATION and for the group haste bring it up to the same level as the single target one.

Starting from the bottom and working up.

Putrid Decay
New combo pr/dr debuff spell as a hard to resist disease spell. Don't see anything wrong with the spell but not really sure when this is going to be useful.

Obkin's Spear of Venom
New poison nuke. Once again poor efficiency on the spell and due to the small size of the nuke doesn't end up being any better dps. Make the spell more efficient or make it better dps compaired to Ice age.

Ancestral Regrowth and Talisman of Ancestral Regrowth
New regen spells. Seem to be fine as far as mana cost and amount regened goes. Regen spells in general seem to be rather useless. Best use seems to be balancing out weak ae dots. No issues specific to the spell tho.

Ancestral Grace and Talisman of Ancestral Grace
First really new spell so to speak. Agility being replaced by an avoidance mod seems like a great change IMO. Would be nice if spell still raised agility too for that occational person who still wants it but really rather minor issue.

Wanvu's Focusing and Talisman of Wanvu
Hps wise fairly straight forward and nice upgrade to previous focus spells. Seems to be currently setup to still buff str/dex and allow them to go over the cap. Instead it really should be the same added dmg and combat effects that str is and dex buffs should be. No reason to go half way and make us cast both Focus and what would have been the stat buff. Once again it would be a nice bonus if it added the new effect and the old stat buffs (not going over cap is fine that would be a bit much I'd imagine) to cover all bases but not required.

Snarling Spirit
Can't really tell much about the pet yet but I will say in general shaman pets need some major help. They do very poor dps and have hps, ac and defensive abilities so low that they die very easily. General requests based on previous pets.

1) Make pets innately duel wield. We're still the only class that has to give thier pet 2 weapons to get them to duel wield. Even sk pets will innately duel wield.

2) Much higher hps and defensive abilities. I don't expect a earth pet that can tank for me but it would be nice if the pet could survive more than one round of melee or an ae. My guess puts our pet hps at 2-3k hps unbuffed. Really needs to be 6K or so to have a viable chance of surviving in current content.

3) Innate sow. Hey the damm thing is a spirit wolf after all.

4) better dps. In part 1 will help with this some but even weapon equiped to duel wield our pet dps is extremely low.

On a somewhat related issue please make up your mind if we are a pet class or not. We get all the pet aa's to use with our marginal pet but we don't get the pet focus's to help keep our pet at least remotely useful. It's not like allowing us to focus our pets is suddenly going to turn them in to mage pets. It's just going to help them keep up with changing content.

Obkin's Mending
Given the current precident that shaman heals are 47% of a clerics heal I expected to be disappointed by this spell and it didn't even live up to that. Our OoW heal drops to only 44% of that of a clerics heal and drops in efficiency. At the very least this spell needs to be upped by 80 hps to 1770 hps healed with out changing the mana cost currently on it to keep the current shaman vs cleric ratios in tact and keep our hps to mana ratio the same.

Beyond that tho I feel the healing gap between clerics and both shaman and druids needs to be closed. On the shaman side that is going to take giving up some on our slow. That doesn't mean changing our spells in any way. It means more common and higher amounts of mitigation. Make our new heals much better and in those zone make every mob at least mitigate to the partially slowed level if not slightly slowed. Totally immune should still be reserved for boss type mobs tho. Game design issues would be much easier making slow less effective accross the board and balancing shaman would be much more viable. Sure we'd be pretty over powered in old content where slow is effective but hey honestly we already are.

Crippling Spasm
Upgrade to cripple and nothing I see wrong with the spell it's self. If the aggro follows that of the cripple line I do expect it to be pretty high aggro tho. My problem is I get the strong feeling that this is going to be Sony's answer for hey shaman have another useful debuff. My problem with that is half the spell has no real effect on mobs as far as we can tell. AC debuff will help out the melee dps delt to the mob and the dex debuff will help decrease procs by the mobs including things like rampage and flurry. Agility and str tho haven't had any noticable effect on npcs that I'm aware of. One useful debuff (ac) and one situationally useful depending on the mob (dex) debuff isn't going to make this a major spell. Sure I'll probably cast it on named mobs but in normal group situations the benifits are few to small to justify it's use. Add something more substantial to the spell if you want us to see this as being a important new spell. Possible ideas include attack debuff, decrease duel wield/double attacks by mobs.

Ancestral Vigor and Talisman of Ancestral Vigor
Upgrade to our sta spells. If lucy is reading the info right it increases sta by 75 but allows 40? of it go go above the cap. Stats buffs going above the cap isn't a bad idea to make them always contribute something but it does have issues still. Stats in general and sta can easily be confirmed, have diminising returns as they get higher. Going from 170 to 180 sta on any class adds more hps then going from 290 to 300 does even tho they are both 10 sta improvements. Past 255 sta seems to give half the hps that sta before it does. More hps is something that people almost always like but given the returns on sta at that point it really seems pretty sub par even if it all goes above the cap. Assuming a warrior with all aa's get something around 3 hps a sta past 255 (i think thats close, shaman are half that) sta buff would add 225 hps. For casters it would probably be close to 100hps added. Doesn't seem to bad until you compare it to the 1830 hps clerics new virtue buff adds or even the mere 700 hps that shaman buff adds or the amount of hps you can get on gear at this point. I'd much rather see the spell converted to some sort of time effect like Str and agility have been. Sheilding would be the obvious choice it seems altho stun resistant is another possibility.

Ancestral Bulwark
Upgrade to our ac spell. Since it doesn't stack with cleric ac buff or virtue so it's rarely used. Seems like a pretty normal upgrade tho. Nothing really to say. Straight forward upgrade to an unused spell.

Pained Memory
Exact same spell as our GoD ancient spell. All in all not a bad thing really. Nice upgrade to the cann line making it less of a hastle to use with out making it over powering. Ancient upgrade to the spell looks nice too.

Obkin's Rain of Venom
Pretty straight forward upgrade to our poison rain spell again. Unfortunately these spells seem to have some innate issues. Inefficient, difficult to use and seem to hard coded to have a high resist rate. Pretty much a pointless spell till issues with rains in general are addressed.

Ancestral Strength and Talisman of Ancestral Strength
Nice spells. 5% more melee dmg rather than str of the cap is a nice improvement. Not gonna be a 5% dps improvement but it's something different and useful. Problem is druids get Lion's Strength 1 level before Ancestral Strength. It has the exact same effect and duration but shorter cast time and costs less mana. Typically speaking druids have had str buffs but they have always been behind the level of shaman's why is this one the same effect and slightly better spell in other ways?

Ice Age
Typical of shaman nukes. Slow casting, smaller and relatively ineffective in general. Not surprised by this spell but still fail to see why shaman should be so totally innept at nuking that even clerics and enchanters nuke significantly better. At least bring us up to parr with clerics and enchanters.

Breath of Wanvu
It's a disease dot. Nice upgrade from the previous version I guess but still a rather useless line of spells. 2.6 minute spells in the era of 30-45 seconds fights are worthless. ~4.5k dmg base dmg for 687 mana is descent if the spell ever had a chance to run threw.

Probably the best thing that can be done with disease dots would be to add some useful debuff on to them like enchanter dots have. Ac debuff, attack debuff or maybe even something new like a spell casting slow or increased chance to fizzle/be interupted.

Ancestral Vengeance
Not a bad upgrade on the most useful of the dots. 30% increase in dmg and slightly more efficient at dealing it. Still a bit behind nuke dmg especially considering even this the fastest dot we get doesn't always last it's full duration. Wizards can do 40% more dmg with a single spell than our dot does in it's 30 seconds and they do it for the same efficiency. As pointed out by Chutoi necro's curse dot seems to have been updated to be more comparable to nukes in dps and efficiency department but the rest of thier dots and our dots don't seem to have gotten the same treatement.

Now dots in general have problems. They seem relatively week in comparison to nukes since they often do less damage and are no more efficient than nukes but have to work over a duration. Additionally they seem to generate more aggro and are riskier to use. The exact cause of the aggro seems to be dificult to tell. My best guess is it's a variety of factors. Counters, especially poison counters, seem to generate a fair amount of aggro from just the counters. Some spells also seem to generate aggro because of additional debuffs included on them, ac debuffs for example. Part of it may be that it's just constantly applying aggro every tick and that is just that many more chances to end up on the top of the aggro list. What ever the cause Dots are riskier to use and less effective than nukes and still have the disadvantage of working over time.

Spiritual Serenity
Big problem here. You know when you went and upped all the Heal over time spells so they where actually useful? Well you forgot this one. Cleric HoT seems to have the same issue. Following the scaling of previous HoTs this should be some were around 950hps a tick and 630 mana cost.

Champion
Nice upgrade but once again seemed to stop half way. Adding the improve dmg 10% is nice, especially with the normal capped stats included too, but why not throw combat effects and avoidance on there too?

As a general thing on the spell line why does it have to be soo irritating to use. It's ok for enchanters to increase dps with haste by 68% and add 45 attack for 42 minutes with a group spell or a ranger to increase attack by 90 for 1 hour and 20 minutes with a group spell but 160 attack can only last 6 minutes with a 1 minute recast time and only be single target? There is nothing about this spell or spell line that justifies it being such a hastle to use. Increase the duration up to 30 minutes and give it a normal recast time at a minimum. A group version too would be nice.

Blood of Obkin
Pretty much the same story as Ancestral Vengeance. Descent upgrade to the spell but still has all the general dot issues and will most likely be particularly bad on aggro because of the poison counters, especially since those are doubled.

I'm sure that this has ended up being a long post and I'm hopeful some of these are just obvious things sony hasn't gotten around to yet. Unfortunately release is coming ever nearer and I don't want these to be overlooked just because it wasn't brought to thier attention.
