1. Understand you are 95% of the time a grouping class. You can solo a little bit but you will get vastly faster experience grouping. If that bothers you, if you want a solo-machine, make something else.

2. Team with a high dps class now and then to level fast. Mage, Monk, Ranger, that kind of thing. Don't have to always duo, grouping in real groups is vital, but this helps now and then.

3. Abjuration will be your toughest skill to raise since it's rarely used.

4. Raise both 1h blunt and piercing.

5. Do the canni 2 quest or buy it, the spell is very useful.

advice from a higher level....
1) Ignore what most say, Canni 2 is worth it.
2) Try to avoid mana regen buffs, get used to running wihout and canni'ing. You'll understand later in life why.
3) When asking for ANYTHING... NEVER EVER EVER shorten words to the point of them not being words, or purposeful mispellings. IE sow plz=ignore, can u plz donate some lewt?=ignore, Excuse me, Mr. Kitty sir, would you mind casting spirit of the wolf?=I'll travel a few zones to go buff the guy, and then it'll most likely be a full buff.
4) 2 items to look into getting are a parrying dagger (found on decaying skellies, or vendors near them, Halas usually has some on vendors), and a fishing cleaver (vendor sold, in shar vahl, one of the vendors in the little hallway between the walls going to the palace). look like a dagger in off hand and sword in primary. insta-rogue. couple with /role or /anon, and very very few buff beggars, the ones that do ask are people that know you or cheaters with hack programs.
5) If you wanna say you solo'd X mob, kei and virtue DO NOT COUNT. they aren't your buffs, so you had help killing.
6) Get clickies. Which ones? The ones that make you more effective in a pinch. Keep them on you 'just in case' as the case usualy arrives when you don't have them. IE snare, tash, stun, etc... If you don't know which, ask. Someone will.
7) If you are in a rush, still quest. Quests don't always give the best items, but it does help make more of a story out of the game. Quests also break the monotony of grinding.
8) Even if you solo 99.9% of the time, and don't care to raid, find a good "family" guild early in your career. Stick with them, they'll be new people when you meet, but you'll have a family to fall back on when needed. If you outgrow them, still keep in touch.
9) Don't get mad if you want to solo all the time and see X or Y shaman with really nice gear that you can't get. IF you want it, get into a riad guild, but be prepared for the game aspect to be gone, you will then have a job with set hours, and occasional loot your only payment.
10) Most important of all, if you are in doubt, seek the answer. Either in game by doing a /who all shaman 65 and sending a tell, or coming and posting here, there's usually an answer, and alot of times, good advice to be had as well.

If you are on the Xev server, feel free to drop me a line. Drylar, Prophet of the Dar Khura. Kitty shaman of the 65th hairball. Following #3 in my post, if your tell includes mispelled words such as "phat" or "lewt" or you shorten please, you, or the like, i'll ignore it. if it's really bad or gets spammy, you go on /ignore. if you're not on xev, i'm at xev.Drylar if you wanna catch me.

Kromnik
Buffs:

Cast them on yourself first, so that you know when to refresh.

Cast them on the MT last, so that you have time to refresh before they wear off. If no enchanter, this becomes important with haste.

Enjoy the game in the lower levels, where a Shaman is still a valued class. You can usually get by being the healer for the whole group, if you limit your buffing. Our versatility and power pre-lvl 60 is/was one of the things that made this class great.

Be generous. As you travel, buff the newbs. Make a spellset for the newb buffs (sub-40), to save time. When you see a newer player having problems with a mob, stop and help/heal them.

Post 60, we become less valuable. Learn to enjoy dungeon crawling. Droga, Veksar, and AC become your new zones. Pay for your 61-65 spells by living in these zones.

Get a VP key, if you want to want to add to your places to go.

If soloing get's boring, and you really like the game, start a second account a 2-box a SK/Shaman combo. Hopefully, you made a Troll or an Ogre Shaman, so you can make your SK the same race and both enjoy the same city.

Evil Shammies kick butt.

You don't have to be a member of "uber guild 01" to enjoy this game. Join a guild that is strong enough for you to attain your epic, but isn't so overwhelmed with raiding that you lose the perspective of having fun. Yeah, they're still out there. Fewer of them now, but still there.
eximius
Try grouping or duoing with different players--do not be locked into a single model for play.

When you group with an unusual class grouping or new players, start with something you can handle pretty easily to get a feel for how good you are.

SHaman stack OK in a single group at certain levels. With cleric and druid demand high for their better healing, shaman stack pretty well on healing in areas where slow is not mitigated too much. Between two of you, several mobs can get slowed and rooted or off-tanked prety quick, and combined, your heals can keep most folks alive if they can step out of range of a slowed mob or tank. Shaman can even off-tank slowed mobs in many zones.

Learn how to pull as soon as possible using stalking probe, root, debuffs, and slow.
Tordail Farstryder
Take your time. By this, I mean the following:

1. Enjoy yourself. Take the time to explore some old world zones, and don't rush to get to 65. Enjoy each level, and enjoy the zones you play in and the people you meet.

2. Be polite! Buff folks who ask, and make some friends. When you ask a question of someone, be polite and courteous, and flaunt your command of the English language (ie. no "plz" or "thx" crap). When you leave a group, find a replacement. Just show simple, common courtesy to folks. A good reputation is invaluable.

3. Work your skills up. This is long, boring and tedious, but it will serve you well in your older days. Yes, this means standing in a place where you won't bother folks with cast spam and casting that spell over and over and over and over ...

4. Cannibalize. Some folks here have suggested you purchase a mount ASAP, but I'd really rather see you spend the 9k on good, serviceable gear that will last. Learn the art (yes, art!!) of the canni-dance.

5. Don't purchase/quest gear just to have it. Money will be tight for many seasons, so when you purchase a piece of gear, make sure it is something that will last. HP/AC, STA, WIS. Learn it, tattoo it on your arm.

6. Read the Crucible every single day. Read the Medicine Cabinet. Read the Equipment gear check threads. Read, read, read.

Those are my pieces of advice thinking that you are looking to get to 65 with many AAs and stuff.

- Keep your defense maxed.
- Group mainly to make friends / get fast experience / learn how to do your job.
- Solo to keep your melee skills up or to get drops that you need.
- If you want to do tradeskills hunt in areas for your level that have drops you may need later. Even if you have to hold em in a mule bank for many levels. It's a waste of time kill greenie just to get tradeskills up.
- Keep your defense maxed.
- Keep every melee skill up.
- Once you get close to cann5 try to get equipment that add HP and stamina. Your goal is to be able to use it self buffed without get low hp aggro.
- Dont waste AA points in stats. Just get run speed 3 and hp regen3.
- Get everything you need to get Cann5 first.
- Your 1 point bash can interrupt spells and freeze the mob for a second.
- Keep your defense maxed.



And looking at common mistakes:

- Learn how to manage aggro when in a group. Don't ping pong mobs wasting clerics mana on you.
- If the cleric in group don't get it... Explain to use fast heals until the mob is slowed. Then it's ok to complete heal.
- If you want to melee when grouping hit the mob from the back to avoid getting riposte on you.
- If you use your pet while grouping try to sick it on the mob from the back.
- Don't melee the mob and then say that you need to meditate.
- In group don't waste mana using dots that will just do damage for just few ticks.
