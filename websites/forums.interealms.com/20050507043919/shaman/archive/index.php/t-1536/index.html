When you use this, keep in mind that your pet remembers if he was set to 'guard here' somewhere. So, after you summon him, he'll try to run back to his guard point. You have to hit 'follow me' or 'guard here' again after you summon. Also, you can't summon the pet if he's aggroed. It's a nice spell, but only because pathing is such crap in most dungeons.

Donar
Yeah, I like this spell alot too. It's expensive, but worth it. It sux to run through a zone and your pet draw aggro from some mob that's green to you, but not to the pet. It's usually an easy kill, but if you're in a hurry, it's annoying. I usually summon my pet near a zoneline when I get to the zone I want to hunt in, park the pet (/pet guard here or /pet sit) and then summon my pet to my side when I find a good place to pull mobs to. As mentioned in other posts, make sure you /pet guard here or /pet guard me after you summon the pet to you, or it'll head back to where you parked it. This spell makes having a pet much more useful, especially for dungeons or at places where you don't want "unplanned for" aggro. This is one spell every shaman who uses a pet regularly should have.
