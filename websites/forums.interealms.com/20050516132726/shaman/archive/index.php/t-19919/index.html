Detton
I have looked into beastlords. How do they compare to shaman?

Beastlords get a limited arsenol of older Shaman spells (much like rangers get weaker druid spells, etc)

They also wear leather armor. To make up for this, they can duel wield and fight passably well (about the same as a bard) and they have an INCREDIBLY powerful pet. Combined with even our weaker level slow spells, they make VERY powerful soloers, and with their great pets (and the buffs for their pets - that adds proc, a lot of haste, etc) they make excellent additions to groups/raids in the form of DPS.

They never get as "good" of a slow as us, but theirs does cast faster. And at level 65, it is a 65% slow, and the different between them isn't so much that they can't take over slowing duties.

They dont get as good of buffs as Shaman do, but in the end game, so many people have maxxed - or near maxxed - stats that it doesn't make a huge different there, either.

They are fun to play, i'lll admit. They are powerful. Some people here are jealous of the fact that they compare in so many areas that a Shaman had, basically, to themselves before they were released.

I personally duo with a beastlord consistantly, and despite the overlap, we do very well.

They can add to groups and raids very effectively, as well as solo very good. (not as good as a necromancer, or charm enchanter), and they have trouble with multiple mobs (even a Shaman can off-root one- BSTs get NO root or snare spells of any kind. The AA proc doesn't count any more then a magician's earth pet root counts as a reliable root)

I like 'em. I like my Shaman more, but a Beastlord is easier to play, and still enjoyable.

~Detton
