Gavving
Easist, but least Archery friendly way to fight him is to corner him. Do this by shrinking your warriors so that hey can back him into the corner with everyone else pushing vindi into the corner. Normally I see the corners in the area below the ramps on the left or right side used for this.

The pull will bring his 2 guards. I'm not exactly sure how monks split this pull, mostly by warping I think. Vindi will not stand over an FD monk waiting to be split He and his guards will immediately run back to their spawn point.

I'm not sure what the cheal timing is on him, but just 3 clerics isnt much room for error. It'd be nice to have another or a 58 druid at least.

Spam heal (everyone including hybrids) tank on engage, if the clerics get the cheal rotation on and stand the tank up, your gtg. Then its just a matter of positioning and a race against cleric mana and Vindi's life. Maximize the clerics mana of course w/ bards, mod rods, KEI, Beastlord mana buff, necros, etc.

Also, I'd probably recommend that pets not be summoned until the fight is engaged. I've seen some very odd aggro by pets attacking vindi even before he's hit anyone. Oh and Vindi has proximity aggro. Everyone except the MT stands at the edge of melee range. GL

Kohpry Mossarrow
Don't worry Teaen, Vindi respawns every 8 hours or so-- plenty of him to go around.

This is what we do for him, to the best of my memory:

Put the raid in the corner closest to the Arena right ramp-- but where you can't see the ramp itself. It's just past the see-invis giants entrance, to the right. This is so your pullers have room to work for splitting Vindi's pets from Vindi himself. Pull Arena Right clear, pull any mobs between you and Vindi. Once that's done, move the raid to the icewall. Park everyone except the main tank and lead puller on the hill to the right as you enter the icewall area. Shrink the main tank-- unless he's a runt-- and slap your best damage shield on him, then park him in the corner made by the left entrance and the support beam.

Start the CH rotation as soon as Vindi paths near, and have the main tank tag Vindi off the lead puller. As soon as Vindi is in melee range of the main tank, have the monks and shadow knights step in, take one swing and Vindi, then back out and wait for the main tank to call the attack; you do this to try to keep your clerics off of the rampage list. After rampage team does their thing, debuff him. As soon as the main tank calls attack (generally around 10 seconds of "soloing" Vindi), use whatever offensive discs are appropriate for the encounter for the melees and have the casters go as full burn as they can without getting Vindi love taps. And pray you can kill him before he kills too many of YOU!
