Landragoran Heronwing
I can answer this question.
First, it's not apparent at first blush just how much aggro Bane generates. Some newbs have said it's comparable to Blood of Saryn. However, they are quite wrong here.

There are no poison counters, which as we know generate insane aggro when applied. Thus spells like Tashania (8 counters I believe) are rather high aggro spells, and our poison dots all are much higher aggro than comparable dps from any other known source.

DPS/aggro ratio on Bane is MUCH nicer, Bane generates bout half the aggro per damage as our poison DoTs do. This is very nice, but one must also realize that this damage is being done FASTER, and thus will create DPS aggro much faster than any of our other DoTs.

Thus, if you want to choose a good DoT for raid mobs, use Bane. It's the lowest aggro DoT we have when you compare DPS/aggro ratios. It's comparable to Velium Strike in that regard. You newk at a bad time and yer gonna get hammered, same thing goes for Bane. However, if you're using Bane you won't be just aggro summoned like crazy in out-of-proportion ways.

Using Bane in a group, well, just don't cast it before bout 10 sec has gone by with MA getting aggro. Otherwise the sheer DPS of it will get u aggro and likely keep u high enuf on hate list to keep you MA for a while.

The best things about Bane are:
1) no initial damage
2) magic based
3) high DPS

Using only spells without initial dmg makes for a short list (BoS, Bane, Anaethema, Grummus, Epic). However, these dots can be used in conjuction with good root spells to reliably root/rot (without undue breakage) mobs to death in a very efficient manner. Case in point (and talked about) PoFire tables solo camp using VP3. Bane takes about 3 minutes off each kill time. And the mana really isn't an issue.
