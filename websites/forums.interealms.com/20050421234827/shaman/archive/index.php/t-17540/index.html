Goldark
Just did Xanamech yesterday, had a dearth of shaman on the raid (4 level 65s, one 61), but no mages at all! Had the 61 shaman on disease slow just in case, one to malos first, and the rest of us stood just behind the MT and alternated slwo with malosinia.

I cast malosinia(resist), slow(resist), malosinia(got stunned), slow (landed).

One thing this did highlight is that, although its best to stand by the MT for aggro purposes, it does also put you in the way of the directed AE stun. If you stand behind, you don't get this problem. So a bit of a dilemma there. Basically, you want to know your tank. If you are sure they can hold aggro, then get in melee range behind the dragon. If you have doubts over their aggro holding ability, stay by the MT and just take the stuns as they come.

Oh, and quiessence/torpor/stoicism yourself as the gnome walks in (make sure it lands before Xana is activated else that's a bit more aggro for you), then you don't have to worry about any AE damage whilst slowing.

As for letting mages malosinia - you can't chain cast turgur's due to spell refresh time, and malosinia actually fits quite nicely inbetween. Its also low aggro which is good. So you might as well help out with the debuffing, particularly if you're like us and didn't have any mages along anyway :)

All in all, I'd agree with the others here, you should have him slowed pretty fast.

Spritabe
I've slowed Xanamech loads of times for my guild (yes, we farmed the 41% neck item a lot). I've rarely been resisted, even from pretty far away. My standard debuff order is Malos, wait for tash call, wait for OOS call, Malosinia, Turgur's and voila.

To whoever said that doing sinia was a waste of time: An additional -15(?) debuffage to make sure that the next Turgur's has that much higher chance of landing is not a waste of my time. I consider malosinia my primary resistance debuff on raid mobs, not Malos.
Malos helps me land sinia, which in turn lets me debuff with that much more confidence. It's 5.50 extra seconds (counting recast time) - your clerics *should* be able to cope with that, and you have the bonus of DD spells from yourself and other classes being more likely to land.

Leaving the siniaing to the mages is all well and good, but when I want something slowed, I want to be sure the debuffs are landing not one second later than they're supposed to = I cast them.
