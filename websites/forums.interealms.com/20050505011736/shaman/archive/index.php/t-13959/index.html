Froglok Guide 101



1 kg / 2.2 Ilb of fresh frogs legs
400g / 14 oz garlic
100 g / 2 oz flat leaf parsley
60 g / 1.5 oz unsalted butter
500 ml of goose fat
A bowl of plain flour mixed with salt and ground black pepper


Method to prepare the garlic puree

Place the garlic cloves in a saucepan of boiling water. Bring to the boil, drain and repeat 3 times. Once fairly soft you will find they peel very easily. Place cloves in the blender and blend until thick, smooth and glossy. Set aside.

Method to prepare the parsley sauce


Wash the parsley. Pick the leaves off and place in boiling water and simmer gently for 3-4 minutes, run under cold water to stop further cooking. Place leaves in a blender and blend until a paste. Squeeze the puree through a sieve, place in a saucepan with a little water, enough to obtain the consistency of a coulis, so it covers the back of a spoon smoothly and evenly. Set aside.

Preparation of the frogs legs


Remove two muscles from the shin part of the leg. Roll the legs in the bowl of plain flour mixed with salt and ground black pepper. Fry gently in the goose fat in a shallow sauté pan. Cook the legs until crispy and slightly browned on the outside. About 3-5 minutes. Place on some kitchen towel to absorb the excess grease.



This dish is best served on a white plate as it is so colourful. Paste a layer of the parsley puree all over the plate. Create a small mound of garlic puree in the middle. Uniformly place the frogs legs around the garlic in a circle. About 12 frogs legs per person is recommended


Hope this helps !

My froglok shaman is now level 53 and a trip to Fear and a fight in The City of Mist away from getting his epic. So far, I have found there to be nothing froglok-specific that I can suggest to you. It's all been more class related rather than race related. Imagine that. ;)

What I can suggest is that you stick with it until you get to level 34. That's when you get your first pet spell. The whole game changed for me at that point as I began to see the possibilities of life with dogdog. People will tell you that shaman pets stink and cannot tank. I suppose in a pure sense that may be true. But remember, with you slowing, your dog suddenly becomes much more powerful relative to the slowed and debuffed mob.

At 53, I am soloing pirates in Dulak and making some decent cash. I slow em, send in the hound, maybe throw in a dot for good measure, and canni back to full mana so I'm ready for the next pull when the mob drops. Just heal your pet if necessary and you're good to go.

Also, when you get close to 39, do the Canni 2 quest. You'll need help from higher level folk but IMHO it is worth it. I've had it since level 40 and I'm only 1 level away from Canni 3 (finally!). Canni 2 plus Stoicism (level 44 quested spell) equals minimal down time for you. Even without KEI!

Hope this helps you out some.

I used the same levelling tactics for Froggy shammy as I've used for all my other classes.

Level in your n00bie area til about 8 or so, then head to the Hollowshade Moor side of Paludal Caverns. Those underbulks there are blue at 8 and you get the nifty exp bonus that a lot of folks have come to love about being in Paludal in the first place....you just have to turn off /shout and /ooc because of all the LoIO idiots that seemed to have migrated there.

When you've had your fill of 'Spreading Crud' wander over to Marus Seru and go nuts. Or if you're feeling a bit froggy (pun intended), 'hop' over to dawnshroud and pick on the shrooms and small zelinaks.

Then of course you could go with the ol' OT/DL grind to take you into the 50's, even though I think of it as a fate worse than death to have to level in those two places, the exp is nice and steady while soloing. Turn your /taunt off your pet, root mob, slow mob, dot, dot and let it chase you around the NE corner of the map there while your dog picks at it from behind (the slow is so just in case root breaks before you can double dot em, they won't interrupt your spells as often).

That's about it. I sorta skipped over the n00bie Gukta quests with my froggy as the running back and forth between npc's kinda got me bored after the 3rd or 4th one. Although I would recommend them to anyone as the n00bie armor for frogs is actually pretty decent if you have the patience. Same with the Shield quests from Gukta.

Th only quests that seem to matter for shammies anymore (besides the ikky cudgel one) are the Thurgadin chain armor quests as they are about the only ones that seem to give 'somewhat' decent risk vs. reward (and quest gems for priest classes seem to be a whole helluva lot cheaper than any of my melee classes were...).
