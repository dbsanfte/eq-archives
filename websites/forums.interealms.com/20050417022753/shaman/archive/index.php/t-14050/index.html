OK, faced with a challenge like 'its out of the question', I just had to give it a try. Here's the rundown.

The troopers (warriors) and crusaders (SKs) both hit for a max of 187; the SKs HT for a max of 701. About 5-6k HPs it seemed.

Neither of these are all that bad, except that they spawn equipped with 2 swords (troopers) or a hammer and shield (SKs), which means that they DW like crazy; it was almost as though they were hasted. I tried creating a newb and handing them a 2hander, but they just gave it back.

The biggest challenge I found was their aggro transfer range. Although the aggro range itself was reasonable for level 50 mobs, they would transfer aggro to their friends across what seemed like 1/2 the zone.

However, at one of the entrances (WW if I remember), there are only 2 warrior spawns and a wandering SK. By using the cazic-thule trick (root 1, wait for other, root it at the zone, then zone out and back in), I could break the spawn and hold it. Of course, thats only 3 mobs, and the next location in is another 2-spawn that has to be broken the same way, which is hasslesome.

I could probably also have just slowed one, and sic'ed muttmutt on the other to keep it busy while I slowed it too, but those five spawns are all there are at that entrance. I didn't stick around that long, but I am guessing they are on a 20-minute timer, which means a fair bit of waiting for spawns if you have a horse to med on.

So, overall: doable, but probably not worth it.

I looked around farther in, and there are a couple of lone guards scattered around East Cab near the necro guild, but again, there are only a couple of campable ones.

One other thing: with all of the canals and bridges, east cab has more pathing bugs and potential exploits than any other zone I have ever seen (except maybe CoM).

Using lev, I was even able to find a cool little abandoned firepit and lean-to in a scout crevice high up in the rocks. And yes, I was able to cast from there and not get hit (although that requires another trick, since you can't see them below you), but thats all the detail that is fit to print, cause I think thats lame. (Its also really, really obvious to anyone in the area what you are doing).

I've been scouting around too...here is what I have been looking for.

1) should be a zone that includes newbie mobs. This is key, since its the newbie yard bonus (+25% exp) that makes it worthwhile, and I'm not sure that this is present in mob-less cities like Erudin or non-newbie zones like shadowhaven.

2) indoor/outdoor not really a factor, since you should get the dungeon exp bonus (10%) indoors, although no horse.

3) ideal location would be someplace buried in the back of the zone, since fewer people to bother you, and usually fewer wanderers and guild leaders.

4) preferably old-world, since mobs are easier.

5) preferably on a worthless faction, since that will undoubtably get hosed.

So far, the cities that make this cut include: paineel, akanon, cabilis, neriak, grobb, and maybe oggok (it was redone, so mobs may be too tough, but I haven't been there).

Paineel is good, although the mobs green out by about 35-ish.

Cabilis is doable (at 56/3k HP/1k AC, anyway), but not likely to be efficient unless you are willing to exploit the many pathing issues there, and I am not.

Neriak has been reported on, and looks to be similar to cabilis; doable, but likely not worth the hassle.

Akanon looks promising (I hunted there with my necro long ago), particularly since mobs are usually spread out, and there is a nice opposing faction to work with.

Guess I am headed to ogguk next. ;-)
