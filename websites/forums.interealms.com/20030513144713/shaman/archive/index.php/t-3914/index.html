If you are looking for a skill that is cheap to raise... pottery is one.

You could probably get to 199 skill now for under 500pp. With the new recipe that is out from Jagged Pine.

Spiritstone can be attempted at that skill, but you will fail a whole lot. I have 213 skill (each attempt/combine to raise skill after 199 is about 23pp each) and I am only 3 out of 50 attempts at spiritstones. Each attempt at a spiritstone requires 2 sapphires (106pp each) and a level 49+ enchanter to make mana for you.

Took me a week of all of my (substantial) playing time to gather everything needed to make a spiritstone. In the process, I trained my pottery up to the mid 100s. BUT after all of that time, I only had enough components for 5 attempts. Not worth pissing them away due to low skill, imo.

So, do all of the work you can. Hire a druid to do your foraging. The biggest hang up of all is the iron oxide, but, if you do some research at eqtraders, you can find some ground spawns (2 of my 5 were ground spawns). It seemed to me that the best drop rate was in Steamfont and I spent plent of time in Najena and The Hole for comparison.

Anyway, gather everything up. Have every piece combined that you can so that all the potter needs to do is the very last combine.

Try to get a potter that is 240+ skill and has a geerlock. That matches trhe potter I found and she went 2 for 5. I sold the extra one for 5k and have since made somewhere in the neighborhood of 30k off of Stinging Wort potions now that I can gate to EF anytime to buy toes.

This, of course, allowed me to buy Malo for 32k. =) What I am telling you is that they are really handy.

Another example...get your hands on a manastone. Go to a dungeon, Sebillis, for example. Bind there (can you bind in sebillis?). Fight until oom. Gate home. Manastone up, sow, gate back. Very handy as a cleric.
