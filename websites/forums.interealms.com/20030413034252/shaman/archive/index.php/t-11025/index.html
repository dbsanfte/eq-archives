Dragonsfyre
Alchemy will never make you rich, and if you do not have high skill yet, it will make you poorer to raise it up. That being said, I do make a decent amount of plat selling potions in the bazaar, primarily I think because my server is not very cutthroat on potion prices beyond the SoW potions.

Every week or so I restock my sales mule and scrape off the profits. It never amounts to more than a few Kpp in profit, but that is plenty to keep me and my alts in food and water, and allows me to buy the occasional trinket or KEI when I need it.

These days most of my sales are in Shrink and Grow potions, and sometimes both to the same people. I wonder sometimes if they go around shrinking and growing themselves...heheh. Gate potions still sell decently, but not at the profit point that some might like. 2000pp for a 10 charge to the destination of choice is what I sell them for.

Occasionally I'll have someone clean me out of DS potions also. And the Lev and ITU potions sell ok. Beyond that not much else moves.

It's kind of a pain to refill the mule, but I love coming home from work and seeing how she did that day. :)

Overall..even with 180 skill now I have never made enough money to recoup my losses in gaining the skill.

I have actually been in groups where melee's were talking about "Damn..how am I going to get another portal potion now!?"..and they didn't even think to ask me. I had to explain to them that I'm an alchemist to finally persuade them to let me make them a few potions at cost...which are trivial.

Don't get me wrong, I love being able to make high-end potions. I use my own ITU potions, sell a few damageshields and stuff, but it's mostly pretty marginal benefits.

(I just realized I can make levitation potions now that dont need batwings..I'll have to keep a few of those around now.)

Anyway..you can make marginal profits on SoW's and shrinks, but don't expect to make back anything close to what a smith or jewlerycrafter can make.
