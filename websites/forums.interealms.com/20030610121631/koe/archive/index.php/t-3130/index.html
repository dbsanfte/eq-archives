DETROIT -- Detroit Red Wings all-star center Sergei Fedorov was arrested early Sunday morning in Royal Oak, Mich., and charged with driving while impaired, the Royal Oak police department said in a release.

The 31-year-old Fedorov was was arrested and charged at 2:50 a.m. on Sunday. After posting bail, he was released on bond once his blood alcohol content dropped below the legal limit.

A vehicle driven by Fedorov was observed driving around a car that was stopped for a red light. Fedorov's vehicle then proceeded through the intersection and the red light at a high rate of speed. Fedorov's vehicle was stopped by police. Fedorov was the lone occupant of the vehicle.

The news released said an investigation was conducted resulting in Fedorov failing field sobriety tests. He was arrested and agreed to take a breath test, resulting in .09 percent blood alcohol conduct. In Michigan, a blood alcohol conduct of .08 or .09 results in a charge of driving while impaired. A blood alcohol level of .1 or above is needed for a charge of driving under the influence or drunken driving.

He must appear in 44th District Court no later than Oct. 3.

After practicing Monday morning with the Red Wings, Fedorov refused comment.

Red Wings spokesman John Hahn also refused comment.
