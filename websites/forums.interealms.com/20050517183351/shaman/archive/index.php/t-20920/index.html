I enjoy CoH Litgruv, but my disclaimer is that it's not EQ. That may be a good thing for you, not sure?

Combat is exhilirating, gfx are amazing, when they aren't stress testing there is virtually zero lag. Character customization is the largest seen in any game so far I'm willing to bet. There are many many many different directions to take a single character, alot of your play time is in instanced zones so "camping" is either eliminated completely or reduced to nearly zero (outside missions).

There is a great storyline that unfolds the longer you do missions, if your just taking the missions to level up or get new missions and don't pay attention you'll miss one of the best parts of this game.

There is not "loot". No bodies to look into. This scares alot of EQ players I think because it's the desire for that next shiny that seems to keep us going. The loot you get is money (called influence) and "enhancements" that fit into the slots of your spells (powers) that enhance a particular aspect of that spell, be it, reduced endurance (mana), faster casting, longer range etc etc.

Enhancements automatically pop into your enhancement bar as long as you have an open slot, for unused enhancements you can either sell them to NPC for influence or trade them amongst your friends.

It's a new genre brought to MMORG status and as such is in it's infancy. I expect much better things to come from Cryptic Studios in the way of expansions. Much like the EQ of old was fun but evolution of that game was paramount in keeping us playing such is the same with CoH. But for now they've got the basics in and it's quite fun.

There are no trade skills.

As a level 20, for instance, you can "sidekick" a person 5 levels or more lower and they can "con" mobs at the 20th level so their spells hit more often. They won't do more damage then normal but your late comer friends are not excluded from playing with you. The exp at sidekick status is reduced but it's close to what you would of got soloing stuff.

About sums it up for the high and lowest points really, it's the first game in a long time that gave me even close to that awe I felt when loading up EQ for the first time in 1999.
