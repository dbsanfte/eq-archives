Ungkor
Obviously, its pretty basic. Like movie theatres make the majority of their money from concessions, EQ makes the majority of its money from expansions.

However, I don't see why they can't charge 0.50 more per month, and let that movie be invested into new features.

Its a choice thats most likely driven by marketing/executive leadership. Its very hard to justify what it takes to retain existing customers with freebies. You can't say directly that "Hey, that 10 extra bank slots that we had three 60,000 dollar a year programmers create over 4 months = retaining 33% of our existing customer base that we would have lost otherwise".

Expansions are easy to see the net result. You instantly see that at the expansion release, X dollars was returned due to sales. Freebies are harder to meansure, and therefore, harder to sell to the executives.

In addition, expansions probably equal the same retention value that freebies do. No one that hasn't played EQ before is going to somehow hear that "Hey, I heard that EQ now has this new free feature added, so I think I'll try it out". Expansions are physical things that sit on game store shelves, and expand the EQ market.

Actually, I don't mind dungeons that get progressively more difficult the deeper you go so that you have to find a "sweet spot" where the risk vs. reward ratio is optimal for your level. Crushbone and Befallen are like that and so are a number of mid-high level dungeons. However, there are distinctly different areas to these dungeons and, at worst, you may have to clear some greenies to get to your camp spot.

The problem with The Warrens is that very low level kobolds (green even at level 6) are sprinkled throughout the rest of the mobs that are blue-yellow at that level. So if your group is fighting 2-3 XP mobs and a greenie or two join in, you have to be very careful not to hit the greenies until there are no more XP mobs in camp. Warrens green mobs can start running at 40-50% health even though XP mobs are still fighting, and at that level you won't be able to stop them reliably and they will come back with friends and relatives.

That's different than what you will face in most higher level dungeons (Crystal Caverns, Seb, Droga, etc) where none of the mobs around your XP camps will be green to you. There are a couple of exceptions, like the green spiderlings in the spider tunnel in Crystal Caverns, but nothing like the nightmare that is The Warrens.
