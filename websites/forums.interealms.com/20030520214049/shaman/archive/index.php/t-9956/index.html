Most people suggest a plate tank these days, since POP pretty much demands a real tank. Most suggest SK, as FD and snare are great, and SK's get aggro very easily, which will leave you free to canni and dot, etc.

Pallys are nice and can stun, and give some small buffs; starting at 59, they can also start making very notable contributions to healing.

Both SK's and pallys don't damage too quickly, really, but there are plenty of great weapons coming out(windblade) that let any class that can use them do very nice damage. Plus, pallys and SK's of course have their spells to help get aggro.

People always used to say monk, but monks do not have the ability to get the high hitpoints necessary for survival in POP as a true "tank," although a monk who winds up with tons of great no-drop armor can eventually have nice hitpoints. Still, it will come very slow and hard to a monk, and still not really make him an ideal tank. Retired my 60 monk for just this reason. He and my 60 shaman shared many fond memories. :(

Now I have a 51 warrior. You can buy extremely good crafted armor for some warrior race/diety combos(I chose rallos zek ogre, so it works for me) -- fierce heraldic being the one with highest hitpoints that sells frequently. Most pieces have 30 HP and up per piece, and a good number have 60 and up. They also have lots of dex, and agi and STA.

I chose warrior because he is easy to equip to high functionality and high hitpoints without any droppable loot, unlike the monk; because having a SK/pally would mean I would have to try to canni and do all my shaman duties while also trying to spam spells with my melee, whereas with a warrior I could just taunt here and there and concentrate micromanagement on the shaman side of things; and because warriors have the "defensive" skill that lets them take far less damage than other tanks do, which will be very handy indeed if my slows and/or debuffs ever wind up taking too long to land. A side benefit is that warriors are essential to raids, so having one around could be handy in that way, too.

Got him to 51 in 1 day and 5 hours. He's nice to have around, and will only get better.

Is it the ideal choice? I have to agree that SK is probably better, and I've always found the whole conception of the SK to be very cool -- and FD is definitely godly. However, more work is involved; and I find that when I am doing two characters, I like it being relaxed and lazy, without a lot of switching between screens, not frenetic. And, having the 1000 or so extra hitpoints a warrior gives over a hybrid tank is a very valuable thing.
