I have more wins than losses and you want to know the secret about LDoN? It isn't high dps and it isn't being top notch. It is all about pulling.

Pulling is the single most important thing any player could do to help their group to win an adventure. Anytime there is not a mob in the group getting beat on, that is lost time. Any time the puller has to run over 30 seconds after a mob dies to get another into the camp; lost time. If your melees are pulling, you are losing dps.

The best way you could get the wins for yourself and any pick up group is learn how to pull. Our dps isn't something that needs to be in the camp for it to happen. You can sick dog, toss a rain, use a dot, but after that, you are just sitting in the camp canning or staring at the mob while it dies. Use that time to fetch another so your dps is constant. Make sure your group moves with you so you don't have overly long pulls.

A shaman pulling in LDoN is great because you can pull while the group is still finishing up the last mob. You can bring an already gimped mob into camp ready for its demise. I usually start pulling about 20% on the mob, depending on how far I have to pull and the group's dps. You can root split mobs. You can use aggro pulling. I actually had to teach an SK last night on how to aggro pull because he was so hampered by FD pulling (which is too slow for LDoN). If you don't know what aggro pulling is, you get semi close to mobs and start sitting down. They will eventually aggro onto you, but the best part is if there are a ton of mobs in a room, the closest aggros only come onto you. You can split a room of 6 by just doing this. If you root or use slow you will usually aggro more than just the close ones. I was taught aggro pulling by a beastlord (Silvermaene) and best thing I ever learned for LDoN.

If you don't feel comfortable with pulling, then always get a bard for your groups. Bards are THE best pullers for LDoN, hands down. Don't let anyone tell you different. They can single pull until the sun goes down and their dps isn't all that super that by them leaving the camp it will take the mob forever to die. My 60 bard gets tells constantly for LDoN because I have shown groups just how great they are for pulling and making groups better. But that is off the subject.

If you get a chanter instead of a bard, that is just as good. Have the puller bring 2 -3 at a time so you always have mobs in the camp.

So what is the moral of the story? Learn how to pull. If you rock at it, people will remember and always want you for groups. It just takes practice and the willingness to die to learn how to do it. But isn't that what EQ is all about?

A couple of things to think about.

If your main problem seems to be running out of time, this can either be a DPS problem or a pulling/CC problem. What is your typical group composition? At this level, having a solid melee DPS or two can make a big difference. You're not likely to be grouping with uber-equipped and AA'd toons at 58, so focus on the basic damage dealers. Rogues, mages, monks, rangers, necromancers, and beastlords can all fill this role quite effectively. A chanter or necromancer with the skills to keep a charmed pet will often outdamage your entire group, so this is also an option.

A typical group you might look for is:

Cleric/Druid
Tank (warrior, SK, paladin, ranger/monk if well geared)
Shaman (that's you)
DPS
DPS
DPS

As long as you keep the melees hasted, you should be golden in this sort of group. Ideally, you will be the one doing the pulling. Keep a mob in camp and a mob or 2 on ice at all times. If there are extended periods where your DPS is not engaged in killing mobs, that is the root of your problem. Pulls must run together like in a chain-pulling xp group. There should nearly always be a mob for the tank/DPS to be working on.

This is the time to make those root-parking skills shine - give your DPS a steady stream of slowed mobs to kill and the points will flow. You don't have backspawn to deal with, so just park the mobs behind where your group is fighting. Use the suggestions in this thread to keep the pulls manageable. Slow aggro is a valuable tool on when adds come on a pull, be sure to use it to pull mobs off the group for root parking.

Don't bother using Cripple. The most important things you can do are a) slow all mobs, b) keep the melees hasted, c) crowd control. Your personal DPS contribution is less important than making sure your real DPS is kept busy.

Thus far I have about 80 wins and 3 losses. And those three losses were during the first week of LDoN’s release. I think the two biggest reasons for my group’s success are that we each know how to play our characters and that our core group always groups together so we each know how the others play. Obviously, not everyone can do this. But I would recommend making a couple of lists of people you group with. One for those you would like to group with again and the other for those you wish to avoid. After adventuring for a while you should be able to build a network of friends and your odds of winning should greatly increase.

I’ll describe a typical adventure for our group and perhaps that will provide a few insights. Just keep in mind that there are dozens of different ways to successfully complete an adventure. Just because a certain method works for one group doesn’t mean it’s the best method for yours.

Our core group is made up of a 62 ranger (puller), 62 cleric, 62 bard and 62 shaman (me). We try to find some DPS to fill in the last two slots. This is usually another melee, such as a ranger or beastlord, and a caster, wizard, mage or necromancer. But we have had two melees, two casters and sometimes another bard or another shaman and we always win.

We always pick either a slaughter mission or a collection mission of 26 or higher. We’ve done smaller collection missions, but they always seem to take more time.

We buff up just before entering the adventure and start pulling as soon as the pets are up and ready. The ranger pulls fast. If there are only one to three mobs he pulls them. If there are more the cleric pacifies them. However, since we have an amazing bard that can keep an amazing number of mobs mezed at a time, it’s not unusual for the ranger to pull an entire room. We’ve had eight mobs or so on us and we’ve been able to handle them with little trouble. And let me tell you, that can really get the blood pumping. But the normal pull is two or three at a time.

As soon as the nearest mobs are cleared we all move up to the next “room.” I have to agree with a previous poster that moving up to the mobs saves time. I also feel that any manna regen is a good thing. We always have KEI and the bard plays the manna/health song even during combat, alternating it with her other songs. I always use my pet and equip him with a couple of rusty daggers so he can duel wield. He may not do a lot of damage but every little bit helps. And we completely ignore chests. We could try them after we complete the quest, but we’ve found them to more of a pain than a reward. We would rather get the next adventure started that much sooner.

As for my role, it’s mostly to keep everyone buffed and to slow the mobs. As soon as a mob is advancing on the group I start casting my slow. If the ranger hasn’t snared it yet he soon will and the mob will return to him. I can slow most mobs on the first try. Some take a second cast. And a very few seem to be very resistant. And for just those very few I don’t find it’s worth debuffng them. I do keep cancel magic memed now, as many mobs in our current theme tend to have damage shields and I like to drop those as soon as possible. If I have extra manna, which I usually do now that I have canni five and torpor, I cast either a direct damage spell or a DoT if it’s a tough mob with a lot of hit points. You’ll get a feel for which ones those are after a few adventures in a given theme. When we pull several mobs I slow every one as soon as I can. If they start beating on me that’s fine, I can take it for a while. I’ll cast torpor on myself if it gets bad. This give the bard time to mez and if they break mez they are already slowed.

Something else to keep in mind is that some themes are harder than others. I just finished about 50 adventures from the Everfrost camp to unlock my armor. We could often finish a mission in 30 to 40 minutes. We’ve just moved to the North Ro camp to unlock some armor for out bard. The North Ro camp is significantly harder than the Everfrost camp. Its nothing we can’t handle, but the mobs are tough, with more casters and it takes us longer to complete the missions. However, the drops from the named mobs are also much better. Most of the drops are actually upgrades for many in our group. I’m still trying to win the roll for a mask that’s dropped twice with decent stats and see invisibility.

Like I said, I think the biggest secret to success is having people you can depend on in your group. If you are all competent players that don’t panic during big pulls you have a very good chance to complete your mission. So go out there and find some good players to group with. :D

With my bard, lull'ing is a huge part of my technique. But the key, as has also been stated, is to keep a mob in camp getting killed at all times. When pulling with my bard, I'll leave camp to go pull when the last mob is anywhere from 50% to 100% health, depending on how many mobs I need to go pull. If I'm going to be doing a new room with a lot to lull, then I leave as soon as we start the last mob (or as soon as I've slowed it, if I"m the only group slower). A bard can lull an entire room of 6+ mobs, then keep coming back and single pulling without relullling. Their lull lasts that long - not sure if other classes have that kind of duration on lull. If not, they should just pull clumps of 3 or so, depending on the group capabilities.

My goal when pulling with the bard, is to ALWAYS have a mob in camp (two pulls are great, of course), or to have the group moving up to the next pull spot. Ideally, I will tell the group to move up as soon as the present mob is dead, and when they get to the next pull spot I have 1-2 mobs arriving just when they do to be killed. The key is to ALWAYS be fighting. Short pulls, no downtime. Keep those melees and pets fighting as much as possible.

My biggest keys to LDoN are:
- Communication. It seems simple but just getting everyone on the same page as far as how CC will be handled, who's MA/MT, who's looting, and those kind of little details are a good thing to have firmly established before you zone in.
- Pace/pulling. I've seen it done many ways but it's just important to pull fast and constantly. At lower levels I found we could often take our time a bit - at 50+ I'm finding there's an ever decreasing margin for error and we need to move fast. There's no time for lots of breaks, discussions of loot, or whatever. I prefer to pac pulling and most often prefer to have the cleric or (the rare on Xev in my level range) enchanter taking care of preCC while everyone else is fighting. Bards in my level range are rare it seems - but if they're good they are amazing pullers. I know a few clerics that are awesome pullers. I haven't tried pulling myself yet....something to consider.
- Crowd Control. I've been in some strong groups of people that were familiar with each other - and we intentionally multi-pulled and just hacked it out for fun - but generally you want some kind of CC. I prefer preCC (lull/sooth/pac/lament etc) to reactive CC (mez) but having mez available is nice. There seems to be a lot less carnage and things go smoother with preCCing. Generally the slug it out thing doesn't work that well unless you have a very high DPS group with plenty of pets and/or you are in a group with people you know and have a high degree of confidence in - it's entertaining if you can pull it off.
- Know your class. Another simple thing. I do almost all pickup groups for LDoN and if everyone knows their class and role we usually crush regardless of group makeup. You see an amazing amount of 50-65 folks that are clueless for one reason or another - just one of these folks, particularly if they're disruptive and/or uncooperative, can make for a bad run. I was actually in one group where one person was intentionally being disruptive enough to put the group at risk and the group leader booted him - and then things became much smoother and we finished with ease.
- DPS. I don't think you need overwhelming DPS but it doesn't hurt. I've won with all kinds of groups. If you're lacking in any other area massive DPS can make up for it.

My style for LDoN involves (general practices, always adaptable, and pretty basic stuff):
- Slowing. Of course. I rarely have mana issues so I use my biggest slow (Turgurs). I also usually keep the next lower slow memmed as a backup for those occasional resists to avoid waiting for Turgurs to reset. I *think* I see a difference using Turgors vs Torgors as far as reduced damage and less cleric healing and it casts fast so it's helping faster.
- Cleric support. I always have root memmed for self defense and to help out aggroed clerics or casters. Generally if I root and slow and add they're a nonthreat. I'll put a regrowth on folks that get hit infrequently to keep them topped off and heal the nonMTs and cleric to save cleric mana, espeically if they're doing pac stuff. I'll also touch up the MT if the cleric is burning a lot of mana (rare).
- Buffing. Particularly haste since there seems to be few enchanters in my level range. I try to keep hastes updated so avoid the calls for it from tanks and I try to rebuff core melee buffs (and usually buffs on nonmelees) on-the-fly as needed (usually about an hour in when the biggies start to fall).
- Looting. I'm often MLer for my groups since I have high charisma for selling, have multiple ways to stay high in mana so looting doesn't drag me down, make it a point to keep my packs uncluttered, and take care of business quickly adhering to NBG practices.

I've been playing a few alts and have been amazed by some of the basic mistakes that lower level LDoN groups make, which can lead to failure. I think the three most common that I've seen involve pulling:

1) Don't let your tank pull. A constant flow of mobs is a good thing. You don't want to have to wait for the last mob in camp to die before you have more incoming.

2) Get a low-DPS class to pull when possible (or a class that can do their damage while they pull). The faster stuff dies, the better you are. A necro, beastlord, SHAMAN, etc, can still contribute to the group via their pet, DOTs, et cetera while they are pulling. A monk, rogue, or warrior needs to be in camp to kill things.

3) Generally don't waste time single pulling. Lulling mobs and feigning takes time. A decent puller can pull doubles and triples in most LDoN situations (Guk can be a definite exception here), without feigning or pacifying. Two or three mobs are easily controllable via root if you don't have a mezzer, and your DPS classes can be doing their thing the whole time.

I actually found the lower level adventures to be much tougher than the high level runs, simply because all the dungeons seem to have been balanced with regards to level, but without considering AAs.
