Jinheim
I have max rank of all of the AA's you are considering buying, as well as all the AA you currently possess, so I am speaking from experience here :). I did spirit call a little earlier in my career, in the mid 100's. Besides that, though, these are pretty much the last AA's that I trained. Out of all of them, Virulent Paralysis is without doubt the best of them all, and I really wish I had gotten it sooner. Here is a thread that gives a much more in depth analysis on the power of VP and my opinions on it: http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=16806 I didn't train it until around my 270-280th AA, and I seriously regret waiting so long. I should have gotten it in my first 100. So, I'd advise you to train that first.

After that, I'd rank them in this order (from first you should train to last):

1 - Spirit Call. It is a handy AA, and only costs a total of 9 AA points. It is pretty handy for pulling purposes, and it has a very dramatic effect when dueling casters. A little extra DPS never hurts, as well. I don't really consider this to be all that great of an AA, but you've already got practically everything so might as well go for it, it is only 9 points

2 - SCF3 and FOM3. This is a lot more useful than spirit call, but the downside is that you're going to have to spent 30 freaking points on it. I have this trained as well and it is pretty cool to get a string of crits when I am chain nuking. Not a serious upgrade in DPS, but like I said, at this point you just don't have much to pick from.

3 - Rabid Bear. The +50CR is nice against offossa and some other places. The actual melee damage can be pretty effective if you're dueling a caster. Against real mobs, your damage is laughable compared to what you can do with dots and nukes. Besides that, rabid bear is really only a novelty. I didn't train it until around 290AA and I don't regret waiting that long at all.

4 - Innate Metabolism. This is really pretty worthless. You're an uber shaman with lots of AA, you should have no trouble scrounging up 1k a month to buy yourself some food. The total time I spent exping in order to train IM 3 is probably about 10x greater than the total time I have spent buying food, so the "convenience" argument doesn't really hold water.

As for resist AA's, you don't really need them. Judging from your magelo your guild is probably right where mine is: Farming EPs, trying to gear up to kill the gods. I've killed everything in the EP's except xegony, rathe, and coirnav. I've never found myself in need of more resists, and your resists are quite a bit better than mine. Especially your CR, sheesh.

Another option (which I would place above rabid bear on my list) is Suspended Minion. It is nice to have in LDoN, because if your pet dies you just pull another out of your pocket. Also cool for whenever you need to do something that requires invis, and definately very good for dueling. Nothing like dueling someone and them working very hard to kill your pet, only to bring out another one. This AA makes using a pet a lot more convenient, and I've actually started summoning them again since I got it.

Enjoy, and good luck.

Chutoi
Consider the first three, definitely. I'd order them as follows:

1) Fury of Magic 3
2) Spirit Call 3
3) VP3

My reasoning is thus--SC and VP are very situational on raids. SC can be very useful on boss mobs to aid in positioning--they have huge push, but seldom last long in mob AEs, reposts, or enrages. However, they do fairly little damage, all things considered. You can search for parses, but even if they did 10,000hp damage over 2 minutes (they don't), that will barely scratch a boss mob. As I mentioned, I find their main use is as a push tool--3 shaman with this skill can push the hell out of a mob, or pin it on a wall for a tank.

VP is also conditionally useful. Few boss mobs are ever rootable, but many of them come with adds, guards, or spawn help at some point in the encounter. Not a lot of mobs have high DR, so VP can really help CC on some encounters by pushing the mob out of the clerics and tying it up for a few moments. Unfortunately, there aren't very many of those encounters--offhand, I can think of about three for which I would consider it very useful. You can adapt it to be marginally useful in a few more.

Fury of Magic, though, is nice. When you need dps, you NEED dps. Let's face it, shaman aren't designed for quick high dps--it's just not our forte. If we DoT on raids and nuke, we're gonna get summoned and rapidly dead. However, when we need high dps and your raid leader calls for a full burn, alternating velium strike with tears of saryrn when both have a decent chance of critting--well that's more than conditionally useful. Extra damage is always nice.

Just my 2 cents! :)

Oh, one more thing on spirit call--depending on how you want to use it for positioning, the dogs that you summon come out one at a time. Once the ability is used, you can move. The dogs go straight for the target, absent poor pathing in the area. Thus, if you want massive push from behind, just stand there and let all 5 dogs push from one direction. If you want to help pin a mob against a wall, strafe around a bit right after you finish the cast time on your ability--you can have dogs pushing from all directions to help stabilize the mob.
