I recently made this post in my guild forums. It is geared towards training them how to ask for buffs but it gives you an idea of what buffs do what. This was written for one buffing shaman on a raid, which I usually am.

----------
Kaesha's Guide to Shaman Buff Shop

Buffs I can cast that I get asked the most for:

Focus of Soul (single) / Focus of the Seventh (group)
Hps: 544 Str:75 Dex: 70
Does not stack with cleric self buff line or caster self buff line

Endurance of the Boar (single) / Talisman of the Boar (group)
Sta: 60

Agility of the Wrulan (single) / Talisman of the Wrulan (group)
Agi: 60

Strength of the Diaku (single) / Talisman of the Diaku (group)
Str: 35 Dex: 28
Stacks with Focus

Primal Essence (single)
Sta: 20 Str: 20 Dex: 20 Agi: 20
Stacks with Focus

Spirit of Bih'Li (group)
SoW plus Atk: 15

Ferine Avatar (single)
Atk: 140 Str: 140 Dex: 140 Agi: 140
Stacks with Focus, Does not Stack with Primal Essence
lasts about 9 minutes

Talisman of the Tribunal (group)
DR: 65 PR: 65

----------------------------------------

How to Get My Buffs On Raid

I will cast MGB Focus if available when all raid force is present.
If MGB is not available I will cast Group Focus on Full Raid groups one at a time.
I will cast group stamina and group agility on full raid groups.

You do not need to ask for focus/agi/sta buffs personally unless you arrived late after they were cast, died, got dispelled and need rebuffed. These buffs will be done last, when raid and groups are formed and the majority of the people are there.

When I have the mana I will /rsay something like /tell me for Primal Essence. When you see this send me a /tell asking for a Primal Essence. I will be getting a lot of tells sometimes up to 15 at a time or even more so please be patient. I may miss you since I will be getting mass tells. Please wait for me to /rsay /tell me for Primal Essence again and then ask for it again if I missed you the first time. Please Do Not ask for sta/agi/focus at this time. I will be doing MGB Focus and Group Sta/Agi in its own time (see above).

If you would like any of the other buffs that I can cast send me a /tell asking for it by name. I will get you when I can.

After asking for a buff in tells and I buff you please do not respond with a thank you or something in another tell. Since I use /rt to target the last person who sent me a tell so I can buff them easier sending me a tell after you get the buff messes me up.

If you know I am in buff request mode please don't start chatting with me in tells since it makes it hard to handle the buff tells and chit chat tells all at once. if you do you will likely just get ignored until i am done buffing. :wink:

I do not respond to buff requests that are not asked for in /tell but instead asked for in /gu or /rsay. I will respond for buff requests in group chat. I do not respond to any buff requests from Nludien.

Since Ferine Avatar is a short duration buff I will not constantly be recasting it on everyone for general mobs. Just stick with Primal Essence in general. If we are going to engage a tough mob I will avatar as many as I can before we engage, but I cannot get everyone since it is a 30 second recast.

Pay Attention to call for buff requests in raid channel. If you slack off and miss your chance for buffs too bad. I will not be buffing you during the middle of a fight or 1 minute before we engage.

If you show up late on a raid and missed my buffs just send me a tell saying that you just got there and let me know what buffs you need. I will get you when time/mana permits it.



Thank you and have a nice day....

This is roughly what I end up casting in raids...

Pure casters and clerics: Sta, PE, IoS

War: Sta, Acu, Cha, Feral/Ferine Avatar, Daiku (if REALLY hurting for str/dex), Focus

Knights: Sta, Acu, PE (if no Primal/Prismatic), Focus

DPS: Sta, PE (if no Primal/Prismatic), Acu (monks are ALL blind), Focus, Regen

The rest: Sta, Focus, PE

There are usually quite a few other shammies around to help out (WOOT!) and buff needs change with certain encounters, but those are the basics. :)
