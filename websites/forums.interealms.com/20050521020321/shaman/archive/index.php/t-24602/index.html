We would look different and perhaps we would all bring different things to the table, but in the end no matter what skills/spells/tools our character has we would contribute the same amound of healing, the same amount of Damage, the same amount of EVERYTHING to the group that every other member does.

Does that seem absolutely retarded to anyone here besides me?

What does matter is that all classes are balanced against the content they will be facing. This means that no one class is totally overpowered (As Shaman used to be circa Kunark, and various other classes have been over the last 5 years at different times) and that no one class is totaly underpowerd, and that all classes should be able to contribute meaningfully to a group that is facing the content.

In GoD most classes were NOT balanced against the content they were facing. Thus GoD was seen as an increadibly hard expansion, and one that most players avoided. Personally I liked GoD for the challenge it presented, especially before is was "rebalanced" and made easier. However GoD was basically an exspansion for the High End Player only because of it's difficulty and that was by far the biggest thing wrong with it.

To date I personally believe that OoW is very well balanced. There is a nice mix of content for High end and Low end players. The Trials in MPG are particularly enjoyable. It's nice facing encounters (Single Group or Raid) that make you think instead of just pull, kill, rinse and repeat. All classes can contribute meaningfully to a group in all the zones. Hell most groups I'm in we can switch out just about any one class for any other class.

As a class we recived some nice new AA's and some nice shiny new spells. (Some suck, but hey they can't all be good!) Spells are also fairly easy to aquire, with the exception of the 69 and 70 spells. Nice gear is dropping (EP Equivalent) from single group killable mobs. I'm going to go out on a limb and rank OoW as the best expnasion since SoV.

I've been in xp groups with 3 Clerics, hell I was in an xp group with 4 Warriors and a Ranger, and another with 5 different casters. All did very well and killed quickly and consistantly. I've been in groups with all kinds of different make-ups since OoW was released, and ya know what? I love it.

The probelm as I see it is people are stuck in a certain mindset. They think they MUST have a cleric, a tank, a CC class etc in order to have a good group and gain xp. This is simply NOT the case. Sure there are places/encounters where certain classes are required to be successful but these are rare. A group with almost any composition can do well in most zones in OoW.

Most players are just too afraid to think outside the box and try something different. They need their security blanket, they need to have a rezzer incase they die, they need to have a CC'er incase of a bad pull, they need to have that rogue to kill fast and on and on.

By far the most overlooked aspect of any toon, is the SKILL of the player at the keyboard. With good/skilled players group composition becomes almost irrelevant. People need to learn to understand this.
