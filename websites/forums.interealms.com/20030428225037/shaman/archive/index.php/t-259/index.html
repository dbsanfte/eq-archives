weissblau
Here's a 'stupid inventory trick' that some people might like to have explained to them.

1. Rearrange your inventory so the backpack you want to put the herbs in is in the top slot. Buy the herbs.

2. Re-rearrange your inventory so your empty bearskin potion bag is in the top slot.

3. Open your medicine bag and your herb bag and place them right next to each other and right next to the open inventory window. I put the herbs I need in the right-hand column of the herb bag and the medicine bag to the right of that.

4. Hold down CTRL (never let it up) and click on an herb. Move to the Medicine bag and click to drop it. Repeat for each herb. Click COMBINE. You do not have to let up on the CTRL key for any of this, clicking COMBINE works fine with CTRL pressed.

5. Drop the completed potion (if you are so lucky) into the AUTOEQUIP area of the inventory. It will go into your bearskin potion bag since it's topmost in your inventory. Keep a mental count so you know when your bearskin potion bag is full.

Yeah, it's not rocket science, but there are still people who don't know about CTRL-click to pick up a single item or who don't think about using autoequip to fill their potion bag instead of opening and closing multiple bags.

Original Alchemy
For some reason unknown to the downtrodden alchemists of Norrath, the advent of Luclin saw a reappearance of the original herbs used when alchemy was first implemented. These herbs, which had disappeared when alchemy was revamped after the release of Kunark, were snuck back onto a couple of vendors in Shar Vahl in a small building next to the bank.

To begin using the herbs, a good starting point is the books of Jonas, which can be found on vendors in Oggok and Halas. These give some of the basic recipes. More advanced recipes can be found at EQTraders.com in their alchemy section.

In contrast to the revamped alchemy, the recipes using the original herbs require a component dropped off creatures only found in the old worlds: Antonica, Faydwer, Odus and the Planes. Unfortunately, some of these components can be relatively rare and may take some effort to obtain. As the original alchemy was something of a mess, there are a few problems with the recipes. Several do not combine, while many of the potions that can be created only give very low level buffs. A few of the potions also have duplicate effects, despite having different names. However, there are some real gems among the trash and the beauty of the original potions is they are all INSTA-CAST. A bonus for the levitate and enduring breath potions is that they do not require batwings or fish scales to use.

There may yet be other potions waiting to be discovered using the original alchemy herbs. For example, next time you buy some balm leaves or dragonwart from your favourite gypsy vendor, note the other herbs they sell: larkspur and elf leaf. So far, no recipe has been found using these mysterious components…
