4) what seperate parts did you buy for it? quality? ie: pentium _, geoforce_, ram, dialup..cable...dsl..etc?
See question 1. Be careful. AMD names their XP chips 1500+, 1600+, 1700+, 1800+, etc but this is not a direct indication of processor speed. Indeed my 1800+ is clocked at 1.533Ghz, but they use something called Quantispeed Architecture with their chips which helps the 1800+ compete with Intel's 1.8Ghz the 1900+ compete with Intel's 1.9Ghz etc..you get the point right?(they benchmark pretty close together that is)
5) about how much did it run total money wise afterwords?what price range am i looking at?
For my system which I think is pretty mid ranged i would shoot for $1,000..you can get a decent one for maybe $600-800. I already had a monitor though so i only had to get the case, motherboard, cpu, RAM, graphics card, wireless card, cd drive, etc. add about 200-500 bucks more if you need another monitor.
6) what is your opinion on the best computer set up, to run EQ? (mind you, ill be using the new comp to play EQ 90% of the time)
Get a decent graphics card and solid motherboard. People oftentimes place most of the burden of problems on the speed of their computer on their cpu chip. This is not true. A good graphics card will get you less choppy gameplay (i.e. on raids when it is crowded), a good motehrboard will keep you from crashing liek crazy if it is instable, and a good amount of ram (i recommend 512MB or 1gig if you can, ram is pretty cheap these days. Keep in mind that Everquest is not very efficient use of CPU resources :) and i think this may be on purpose. VI didnt want anyone to do anything while playing eq. So just keep that in mind.

I bought an Alienware computer a few years ago, like the poster above has. Good company, good customer service.

I would advise that if you build your own, you be VERY careful about matching the motherboard and cpu up. Right now for instance, you could buy I think three different kinds of Pentium 4 CPU's of the same speed -- I think 1.7's for instance, come in socket 473 and in two different socket 478 configurations. 478a's are the most modern versions. The two types of 478's will fit in the same type of motherboard, but the 473 is an outdated chip that is still selling like hotcakes, as are the motherboards that go with them.

Keep it very much in mind that MANY MANY vendors are trying to clear out their inventory of socket 473 motherboards and chips, and when they build stuff for the unknowledgeable, they will often stick them with the outdated socket 473 stuff. What that means to you is that when you want to upgrade, you won't be able to. Socket 473's are not being built to a level you'd want to upgrade to by the time a decent computer you make now starts to seem old. You'll be looking at socket 478 chips, which won't fit in your old motherboard.

So be VERY sure you get a socket 478 motherboard and a 478(make it a 478a) chip, if you're going the P4 route.

I wouldn't bother getting less than a GeForce 3 video card. They're great cards. Research well before buying -- there is a GeForce 4 card out now that is basically only a GeForce 2 in disguise; no point in buying that at this point.

And Luclin is a huge memory hog -- 512 RAM minimum.

Oh, and on that ram -- don't get one of those rigs that uses a nice modern chip but uses SDRAM. SDRAM will NOT let your computer live up to its potential for gaming; you really need the newer types of RAM -- PC 2100 or PC 800. There is no question that it is worth the extra money.

Finally, I'd stay away from built-in anything except for built-in RAID, which can actually give you extra EIDE channels -- places to plug hard drives, CDRW's, etc., into. That's actually kinda nice, but will probably run you 25 bucks more or something like that. But built-in sound and video cause problems for an awful lot of people, not all of them technical know-nothings. The N-force or whatever motherboards may be different, I don't know, but I think it's probably a whole lot better just to follow the Army's creed when it comes to building computers -- KISS. Keep It Simple, Stupid.
