Ungkor
we may find it necessary to make fundamental changes to the game

One change that I've always wanted to see, is a complete eradication of any debuffs that lower mob dps. In return, debuffing classes (slow, cripple, etc..) would need new, creative contributions to group and raid play.

For instance, if the entire game weren't entirely based around pure dps...(by that, I mean, any encounter seems to revolve around a mob doing X dps, and healers needing to heal for X-Y, where Y is the amount debuffed)

What if a zone were filled with 1/3 mobs that blind, 1/3 mobs that mez, and 1/3 mobs that suffocate you? Current debuffing classes would then have cure blindness, cure mez, and enduring breath that would be hightly coveted in groups.

Not the most perfect example, but you get my drift.

The issue with slow and any debuff that reduces mob dps, is that it is just too hard to tune once the numbers get too high. The higher the dps, the greater the chance the unslowed dps will kill a tank outright, whereas the slowed dps is trivial.

We know SOE made the majority of all mobs mitigate, and ZERO was given back to the slowing classes.

The only fundemental gameplay change that would really matter in the long run, would be a complete redesign of EQ content, to not entirely focus on dps/healing, and instead mix in new factors to battle, and give the "cures" or "solutions" to those new factors to the classes affected by SOE"s continual nerfs of class defining abilities.
