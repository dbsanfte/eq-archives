As for Mill, I went with arcane because it provides more utility than fire.
Fire pretty much just adds more damage straight up, with chances to stun mobs a bit. It's pretty boring IMO. The only really exciting thing is the ae stun, but I can live without it.

The arcane tree adds a lot of stuff.
-mages do huge damage. damage = aggro. less aggro means less wasted heal mana from healers
-I hated my fireballs being interupted by mobs meleeing me. arcane missles always does full damage for me, unless the mob uses a stun attack.
-clearcasting 10% of the time means roughly 10% less mana usage on my DD spells.
-better dampen magic means I take noticably less damage from any caster
-better energy shield means I can use my immense mana pool as a health pool with less refreshing of the buff
-instant casting arcane explosion is huge. there's none of this annoying casting time to have to deal with. In gnomeregon, I can run into a pack of 6+ gnomes, chain arcane explosion about 6 or 7 times in about 10 seconds, and they all die. I can also AE 3 mobs of my own level at the same time for xp gains without problems. I could probably do 4 or 5 if I have a good healing potion on me.
-silencing counterspell. hits casters hard. when combat is measured in the seconds, 4 seconds of inability for a mob (or player) to cast a spell is huge. casters have lower hp than other mobs, so they live even shorter, so this 4 seconds hits them really hard. fighting casters like shaman, this is very useful. before I got this, I'd counterspell a lightningbolt, and immediately i see the mob casting a heal spell on himself. Silencing counterspell just turns of casting period. Also, it works even if the npc isn't casting a spell. You can preemptively stop casting for 4 seconds to stop instant spells too.
-presense of mind is very very useful. Need to push out as much dps as possible in a short period of time? Instant fireblast followup with instant Fireball. Need to polymorph that mob quick? do it instantly, without worry about interupts. Want to AE those mobs like a champ? Pull with the big fire explosion, then follow it up with an instant fire explosion. Mob chasing you that you can't sheep (golem, dragonkind, etc) that resisted your ice root? Instantly ice nuke it and run away. Also, there's enough talent points to be able to buy the super super fireball from the fire tree, and still get Arcane power, and a few more arcane talents if you so desire.
-arcane power is huge too. Mages can push out a large amount of damage. Why not just push that damage out even faster? If you can kill a mob with X mana in Y seconds, why not do all that damage in Y/(1-0.35Y) seconds. Let's say something dies in 15 seconds of fighting you. If you channel this ability, it dies in about 10 seconds instead. That's 5 seconds less damage you take. When you combine this with the number of instant spells a mage can dump out, you can really spike your dps over a short time.

The other stuff from arcane is ok, but nothing really worth mentioning.

OH! I forgot about the other ability, Evocation.
This is the best mage talent ever IMO.
Once every 10 minutes, you go from OOM to FM in about 8 seconds.
It's a channelled spell, so being damaged will make you lose some of your total mana regen.
BUT, you see that spell shield ability we have to make us lose mana instead of health?
Guess what? If you activate it, then pop off evocation, your channelling of spells isn't being interupted while it's still active.
I'm not sure of exact numbers, but I'll estimate here to show how it ends up working.
Suppose my spell shield absorbs 300 damage. With my talents, it absorbs 450 instead.
Suppose a mob hits for 90 a hit.
It'll take him about 5 hits to get rid of my spell shield.
Instead of taking the 90 damage, I lose 180 mana instead, and 90 damage is absorbed from the spell shield before it gets removed.
In total I'll lose 900 mana instead.
But, evocation makes my mana regen so huge, that I *still* go from OOM to FM, even dispite the fact that the mob is beating on me.
So when evocation ends, I'm back up to full mana, and spell shield is about to drop.
I can pop spellshield back up, and nuke down the mob with my new full mana pool, and take zero damage.
It's sick.

Other important times I've had this come up is when you get adds you weren't expecting, like when repops happen, or whatever.
Those fire mages at level 40 have around the same stats as I do. They have their 1k health pool and 3k mana pool. Same with me.
Except that every 10 minutes, I have a 6k mana pool.
That rocks.
