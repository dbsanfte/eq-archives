Hail,
Stick to the nested (sitting on eggs) dragons at first. Avoid the scar (AE'ing) dragons until you are more experienced.

You can solo the nested easily as long as you can keep from getting adds. Even with a single non-dragon add you can usually pull it off if you are careful. I recommend binding at the ToV entrance, and keeping Gate up if you are worried. If you have to evac, immediately zone into ToV to clear aggro, then gate back.

If dragon slaying solo still has you worried, bring a single Mage friend, or heck, a single friend of ANY class. Magician/Shaman combo is pretty deadly for dragon slaying though. Mage gives you all the pet toys & summoned stuff you could want, and with pet & nukes makes the kills much faster. Your tanking in the face with poison DoTs pretty much assures the Mage won't ever get aggro, so he can nuke pretty hard w/ his bolt spells.

A single solid group for the scar dragons should be plenty in my estimation. Look up the quest pieces beforehand. You can have a wizard with the right faction shuttling back and forth to do the weapon quests, which can still net your group a decent amount of money in bazaar sales.

Best of luck,
Jaruden
Take a group of friends, you can wipe out all the nest dragons in an hour, probably get at least 1 copy of can4.

Yeldema is easiest, with no AE. Then any other nest dragon is equal, some have fire ae, some have cold, but all hit exactly the same otherwise. Valley dragons are another step up from that. Ionat is the one that silences, the rest are just more resistant and have stronger ae's (some are wizards too).

Pulling is interesting. There are very few safe spots in the zone, and all the dragons will run at low health. It's mostly going to be trial and error until you can find a spot, best bet is to sit down and watch the area around a nest for a while and get the hang of it, then kill as fast as you can.

You don't mention what level you are or what spells you have access to. As was already mentioned yeld is the easiest. He's the lowest level, 55 i think, and has no ae and basically no special abilities. If your 60+ with torpor and preferably malo you should have no problem doing him now a days. If your sub 60 or don't have torpor it is still doable but it's alot harder just because you don't have an efficient heal. It also helps alot to be non kos to dragons initially so you can scout and watch pathing and such with out having to worry about aggro.

If your sub 60 or with out torpor I'd recommend bring a friend or a couple and then you should easily be able to handle yeld or any of the nest dragons.

As was said avoid the obvious of klandicar, sontolak, any sapara (proc 1k gflux) and cargalia (same ae). There's also a named wurm that can spawn and is pretty mean since he's pretty resistant.
