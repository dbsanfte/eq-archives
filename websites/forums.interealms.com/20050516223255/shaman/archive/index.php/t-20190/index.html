Hello. I would like to echo the thanks that many have posted for your willingness to listen and address shaman concerns.

Would it be fair to sum up your current concerns in the following 3 points?

1. Healing spells upgrades are rendered irrelevant due to the massive amount of damage Gates mobs dish out.
2. Slow is most often resisted and when it does stick it I most likely not a factor at all in combat as the fights are over quickly.
3. The Gates DoT’s similarly are ineffective in Gates combat due to the quickness of the battles and low damage output.

May I offer a perspective on the three points you list.

GoD mobs put out massive damage. In order to succeed there, your team must be able to kill them quickly. A fight that keep on going for as long as one full minute probably means that the party is wiped out.

The question I would ask is, what is the shaman's role intended to be in fights like this in GoD? Traditionally, a shaman is (1) a priest class with strong heals, (2) with a very significant role to play in slowing the mob's attack, and (3) the ability to inflict serious harm on the mob with damage over time spells.

1. The amount of damage these mobs put out currently rules out the possibility that a shaman can serve as a healer. The new shaman Heal over Time is a very minor upgrade over Quiescence, and is completely insufficient to keep anyone alive. A shaman's quick heal spell provides a base of 1300 hp of healing :: with mobs doubling and tripling for over 1000 per swing, a 1300 hp heal is completely insufficient to keep anyone alive. This means that the role for a shaman will not be healing in GoD, with the present setup.

2. Most of these mobs mitigate and resist slow. A group that fights in GoD cannot wait for the shaman to succeed in slowing the mob: so they must endure the damage the mob inflicts and go on about the business of killing the mob. By the time a shaman casts Malos, casts Turgurs, gets resisted, waits for it to refresh, and then casts it again, the mob will routinely be down under 50 pct health, often as low as 30 pct health. That means the group has had to kill the mob without benefit of a slow. To be successful in GoD, a group cannot count on a shaman to slow the mob early and reliably: instead, they must be able to kill an unslowed mob before it can kill them. This means that the role for a shaman will not be slowing in GoD, with the present setup.

3. A successful group in GoD needs to be killing a typical mob there in 30 to 40 seconds. Shaman DoTs will have at most 30 seconds to run: less than that, if they do any healing or slowing work at all. A DoT that would do its damage over a period of 2 minutes is only going to do one fourth of its damage if it only gets to run for 30 seconds. So a spell that looks powerful -- a 4000 point DoT -- would have no more killing power than a 1000 point nuke. This means that the role for a shaman will not be using DoTs in GoD, with the present setup.

The bottom line on all this is: with the present spell lineup, a shaman in GoD cannot succeed as a healer, as a slower, or as a DoTter. No group that hopes to succeed in GoD can rely on shaman heals, shaman slows, or shaman DoTs. Thus, none of a shaman's traditional roles can be fulfilled in GoD.

This lines up quite closely with the three points in your summary. I offer this perspective as one player's sense of why these three are so significant. Thanks for your time.

The simple fact is that with Shamans these waters have ALWAYS been muddy. Let me explain the situation as I see it.

Shamans are not Clerics, and Druids are not Clerics. All of our healing concerns and adaptations from the point of SOE are based on a comparison to Druids. So this is where it begins.

Shamans heal. Druids heal.

Shamans slow mobs which in turn mitigates damage output, requiring us to heal less overall HP than a druid would need to heal to a melee under similar circumstances. Druids heal.

Shamans can Cannibalize by many means to convert our considerable HP pools to mana allowing us to Heal and Slow more often and turn over a higher rate of Mana per encounter. Druids Meditate so that they can heal.

In PoP, Shaman heals were upgraded, slightly. Tnarg's Mending is a lovely spam heal, nothing more. In concert Druid heals were improved as well, and to a wider margin. This is where the gap began to widen.

PoP also saw the rise of Slow Mitigation. This began to create a problem whereas we could no longer compensate for our reduced healing potential with impressive damage mitigation. GoD has further widened the gap, not by mitigating more, but by increasing the damage the mobs are doing on a per-hit basis. It's not uncommon to encounter Blue-conning mobs, that can be 75% slowed but can hit for 1200+ her hit. WE SUCCEED BASED UPON HOW LONG THE BATTLE LASTS, ESPECIALLY WHEN SOLOING.

I think a short paragraph or two about our past, present, and future as shamans and what we are to the game from the SOE design core would go a long way to clearing up any misunderstandings that may have arrisen since the days of 'the vision'.


As for addressing "The History" as I recall the last 4 (or is it 5) years:

In the past, we were always pretty much a 'support role' for a group with our primary role being as incoming damage mitigation and outgoing melee damage enhancement with good secondary healing and magical direct damage.

With the advent of Kunark, we shifted a bit to having better healing (hello Torpor!) and more outgoing magical damage. But at the same time we backslid on the outgoing melee damage enchancement (hello stat and haste caps!).

With Velious things pretty much stayed the same for us, we got some enhancements (new cold nukes!) but there wasn't much that could be done about the diminishing returns on stat buffs and maximums.

For Luclin things started to get strange. We got some improvements to our outgoing damage again, and we saw the advent of our first 70% CH spell. But as many of the maximums were relaxed for capped damage and stats we saw less effect from our new Stat bonus spells. Additionally, the creation of the BST class showed that the real benifit for outgoing melee damage enhancement lay in the +ATK bonus spells and since most melee in Luclin had obtained gear that would max them out even with the smallest of buffing.

Planes of Power blew the top off the old stat maximums and just as rapidly the melee caught up again. Once and for all it was shown that the spells that melee wanted were +ATK, +Speed, and +HP since those stats had the best effects for them.

The shaman lines of spells received very little in the way of aids to these. Stacking issues that had been plaguing us had finally been resolved but only STA and Focus line proved to be of any need. Our Second Level 75% CH (called Khura's Mending) would have a nice addition but this is where it went 'strange'. That spell never left Beta and while the other priests all received upgrades to their healing lines the directed healing never increased for shamans. And while they did receive a version of Torpor that didn't snare it's target it really is only a stopgap and the existing Cleric versions were already better and over-wrote it.

Expansions since PoP have shown the same trends. Little or no growth in the healing lines while other Priests and now Priest-Hybrids have grown in healing and cure powers while there has been little to no way for the shaman to keep pace. We've received some growth in the outgoing damage department with DoTs that dont break root and swarm pets (ignoring the broken second generation swarm pets that replaces instead of supliments the better first generation) but our original functionality of Outgoing Melee Damage enhancement has been completely handed off to +ATK based spells that we never receive. And let us not even mention +END. That spell as a ward is giggled about at every raid where we cast it. Druids and Clerics both deride it at their leasure.
