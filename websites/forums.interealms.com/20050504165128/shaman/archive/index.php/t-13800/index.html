What is it?
Specialisation is the means by which a caster increases their mana efficiency in a given area of magic. Once an Intelligence based caster reaches Level 20, or a wis based caster reaches level 30, they obtain the ability to specialise in the fields of magic. Whilst a caster can specialise in all five fields of magic, only one field can exceed a skill of 50. Whichever skill first exceeds 50 becomes the primary specialisation of the caster from that time on. If you choose the wrong specialty, you can alter it by completing a quest in the Temple of Solusek Ro.

How does it work?

When a spell is cast a roll is thrown to see if it receives the specialisation bonus. The higher your specialisation skill the more likely it is the roll will succeed, and the greater the benefit of the roll. A successful roll will do two things:

It will reduce the mana used on the spell.
It will reduce the chance of fizzling.
An unsuccessful roll will merely cause you to cast the spell with normal mana cost and fizzle chance.

Note: It has been confirmed by Geoffrey Zatkin, the once Arch-Magi at Verant that:

Specialisation does not increase chance of recovering after interruption. This is based purely on Channelling
Specialisation does not make spells cast faster. This is a fixed amount.
To raise your skill you will need to spend one practise point in the skill initially. This will raise your skill to around 25 or so. Spend 1 point in each of the five skills. After this the best idea is to pick the specialisation you want, memorise a Level 1 spell of that type and cast it on yourself for an hour or so until you reach 51 skill. After that you can simply go and play normally safe in the knowledge your primary specialisation is set. It rises fairy quickly so don't be surprised to see it climb up fast. The max skill limit on specialisation is 5 + (5 x Level), with a maximum of 200.
