Adds are certainly a hassle...

The most obvious solution is to find a good camp spot- one that doesn't have a lot of wanderers going by.

Park your pet at that camp spot (don't bring him with you to pull, as he might aggro additional baddies.) If you get more than one, root it far enough away from the campsite so that when root breaks, it won't come back. Sometimes this might even mean running away from the camp before casting root.

If you have more than one at the campsite (ack!), peeling one off to root-park it can be a chore. If your pet can handle taking a beating for a little while, just grab the one he's not beating on- get its attention, then run to where you want to root it, root it, and come back.

A handy trick I've been forced to use a few times... if something is beating on your pet and you need to get it off, cast Flash of Light on the monster, then hit my "/pet back off" hotkey (just keep mashing the button until dogdog listens.) The monster should go running once he's out of melee range. Follow and root it once it's far enough away. Of course, you need to be careful with this one... don't train other people with your running adds. :)

Also, just face the fact that you will lose a pet every now and then to adds. In spite of everything, it happens. It sucks, but usually you can sacrifice dogdog and at least make it out of there yourself. An expensive get out of jail free card... but better than the alternative.
