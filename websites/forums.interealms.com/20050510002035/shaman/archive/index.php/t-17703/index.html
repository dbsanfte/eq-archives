Caelum
Needless to say, I hit 65, and then...burned out with constant slowing/canning action.

I want to take a break, so, I'm making an alt. Being a caster, I figured I would hit the other end of the spectrum and make myself a melee.

However I can't decide which one to make.

Warriors are good, get the disciplines later on. Can use just about every weapon and the best armor. But, no utility spells to back them up. Not even simple invis to get to tough places where your group is waiting.

Rangers are great for DPS, plus some nice utility spells. Not to mention what they can do with a bow later on *drool*. Problem is they don't get the AC/HP that a warrior gets. However I can swap out some chain gear through shared banking for ghetto twinkage.

Paladins are nice support melees, can help keep a group healed and can even res to a limited degree. Lay on Hands is a lifesaver at that too. However same problem as Clerics when it comes to utility spells.

Shadowknights are nice for messing up a mob. Nice with the DPS, snare (Darkness line), and Feign Death is a saver at times. Of course if you need to patch heal a group member, should have been a Paladin.

So I figured I would ask my fellow shaman and see what they have to say. Have any of you tried an alt and found one you really liked, and think a fellow shaman would like in general? Should I just roll a 4 sided die?

Poguk
I have a ranger and monk, both above 55. At one point in time they both took turns as my "mains". Shaman was my most recent alt, and now he's my main and I wish I had found the shaman class earlier.

Anyway, rangers are very strong right now. Great damage and great utility. Monks aren't very strong right now. Little to no utility and their slight DPS advantage isn't enough to make up for their lack of utility. That said, I still enjoy my monk, mainly for conceptual reasons. Martial arts and minimalism is a neat thing to play around with. :P

But if I had the time and ambition to play a melee alt right now, I'd have to go rogue. There is nothing more absolute than just pure DPS.

Most groups these days consist of a big healer, a big tank, a slower, buffer/backup-healer/crowd-control toon, and 2 DPS, or some variation of that. It's good safe xp. Able to handle a lot.

One time though, I got a group in PoV that consisted of me (shaman), a SK, rogue, BST, mage and AM3 ranger. All of them fairly "well-endowed". It looked like raid-spam, and spell-spam. I was the only healer and main slower, but it didn't matter --- the mobs were dropping jaw-droppingly fast, and I didn't need to be a uber-healer. The mobs weren't alive long enough to do any serious damage. We cleared the *entire* cave in PoV with one group and were waiting for mobs to spawn. If we pulled an add, a caster would kite one 'til the first was dead.

My guild is starting learn the ropes of raiding (we're a family guild who hasn't raided much), and we have a great group of supporting classes.... clerics, chanters, shamans, druids, etc... we have a few good tanks. We're lacking in DPS classes, and it shows on the big fights. Sometimes our plan is good and well-executed --- we just don't kill it fast enough, and what started off as a fight well-in-hand soon becomes an OOM and ping-pong fest.

I'm so impressed with rogue damage at 65... I'd love to have one of my own guided missles to aim at the back of a mob. Despite not having as much utility as other classes or being able to whip out something special when the fit hits the shan, I still feel comfortable knowing a rogue is present and making those mobs die faster.

If I wasn't restricted to a melee alt, I'd consider either a rogue or a mage.
