This idea grew out of a discussion on the Ranger's Glade with our disappointment with all of the overwriting issues involved with our level 60 Luclin spell, Warder's Protection. Why do buffs other people cast on me overwrite buffs I already have up in the first place? As far as I can tell, this "feature" is a holdover back from the time when you couldn't always click beneficial buffs off. But now you can. Other people being able to overwrite my buffs is a borderline exploit. Example: Why should a paladin, in a non-PVP setting, be able to LOWER the ATK of a ranger (thus reducing the ranger's damage output) by casting Brell's Mountainous Barrier on the ranger and overwriting Warder's Protection? What compelling reason is there for beneficial buffs to overwrite something I already have up?

I propose that it should be MY choice to click off the interfering buff if I want someone else's buff that may not stack with what I have on for whatever reason. Control of what buffs I have on is MINE and MINE alone. If someone casts a buff on me that is better in some aspect than what I already have on, it should not take hold. It should be my responsibility to click off the interfering buff if I want what that other person is trying to buff me with.

If I have Warder's Protection up for the ATK boost and for some silly reason, as a ranger, I want to give up the ATK for extra HP of Brell's... then I should have to voluntarily click WP off to accept Brell's, not involuntarily get a buff overwritten without any control over it, and then have to expend even more mana to recast it.

Advantages: This would solve ALL annoying spell overwriting issues in the game. People with group buffs that have stacking issues won't feel like their spells are useless because only a certain subset of people actually want them. People needing certain buffs won't have to complain anymore when they get their buffs overwritten.

Disadvantages: People might actually need to learn more about spells so they know which one they need to click off in order to accept a certain buff that otherwise won't take hold on them.
