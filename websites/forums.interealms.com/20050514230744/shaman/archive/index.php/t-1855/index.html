This is how to MQ for other races and classes By: Anonymous
Posted @ Tue, Apr 16th 5:43 AM 2002 Score: Excellent[5.00]

Okay, I lost my slate several times doing this so I parsed out exactly the steps needed to MQ citizenship for other races, which thus far hasn't been posted very clearly.

First, take your main character to Mignah, who is upstairs in the building marked number 8 on the EQatlas map. Say exactly this: "I need an acrylia slate" and you will get a slate.

Now, take your main to the Registar room. The room to go into is just to the left of the "16" marked on building number 16 on the EQatlas map, and you will find the door on the inside of the little courtyard of the building (you enter the courtyard from the south). Camp your main character here.

Now, create a mule Var Shal. It doesn't matter which class. Have the mule give the newbie note to its guild leader, and you'll get a note in return and be told to take it to the Registrar.

Do that, and you'll be given in return a note that you are supposed to take to the Tax Collector, and told to also get a slate. Don't get the slate (your main already got it). Take the note to the Tax Collector, who is in building number 14 on the EQatlas map (he is behind the bankers). He will stamp your note and give it back to you. Give that stamped note to the Registrar; do NOT give him a slate with your mule Vah Shir.

Now, one of two things will happen. He should say "This means nothing by itself." At this point, you camp your mule and log in your main, and have the main turn in the slate, at which point your main gets a note to take to the King.

If the Registrar instead gives your mule the King note, then that means someone gave the Registrar a slate without giving them a stamped tax note, and you're gonna have to delete your Vah Shir mule, make a new one and go through the steps again up to this point.

Now your main character has the King note. Give it to the King, who is in the building marked number 4 on the EQatlas map (he's easy to find). He'll give you a note to return to the Registrar. Do that, and the Registrar will give you a note and your slate back, and be told to take both of them to your guild leader.

If you are NOT one of the five Vah Shir classes (WAR SHM BST ROG BRD), you can take the note and your slate to any of the Vah Shir guild leaders you like. They will give you your slate back and a cloak, and tell you to give your slate to a nearby NPC for further instruction. Do NOT give your slate to that nearby NPC, because they will eat your slate and say you are not the right class. I think you can just get rid of the cloak because it's meaningless to you; you're just doing this quest to be able to do some of the Progressive tradeskill quests (which requires the slate). I doubt you really need to do this step at all, it's just something I found I could do. If you're not sure who the guildleaders are, create a newbie Vah Shir of the class and the note will tell you.

If you ARE one of the five Vah Shir classes (BRD SHM ROG WAR BST) but of another race, I THINK you should be able to hand the slate in to that nearby NPC, but I do not know this for a fact. The items that result from the progressive class quests seem to be valid for all the races for that class, so they should give you the futher instruction rather than eat your slate. Again, to find out which of the NPCs is the guild leader, create a newbie Var Shal of that class and the newbie note will tell you.

In destroying my slate accidentally several times, I had to do the MQ citizenship quest about 4 times. This was enough to get my Erudite Wizard up to Ally with the Registrar, who is on the Citizens of Var Shal faction. At warmly or below, when I asked him "I need a new slate" he would just say "You need to further prove yourself etc". However, once I hit Ally, I can get a new citizenship slate just by saying "I need a new slate" and not go through all the MQ stuff.

You may wish to intentionally destroy your citizenship slate and do it several times until you reach Ally with the registrar - I imagine this opens up some other Var Shal quests. It's not a particularly long or hard quest to do, and it gets your Citizens of Shar Vahl faction up VERY quickly. It's also pretty good exp for characters level 7 and below.
