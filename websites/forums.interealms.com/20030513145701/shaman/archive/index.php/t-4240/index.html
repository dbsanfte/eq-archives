- Pet Resistance Changes: Pets will now use their master's level and resistances when saving against spells cast by NPCs (against PCs pets will use their own resistances and level as they always have) - unless the pet has special resistances, in which case it will use that resistance.

So in this case, the owner's resistances and level do not matter since the fire pet has its own special resistance to fire. Or in other words, Rak should be the same old Rak.

I completed my epic last night thanks to the suggestions on this board. Here were my observations.

We had 3 magicians, 4 clerics, and a raid force behind them. I instructed everyone to fully raid buff including fire resistance. I made sure that nobody buffed the Magicians or their pets with fire resist buffs. I think the highest fire resist was our epic mage who had 87 FR. Two of the magicians summoned the 49 fire pet, and the third mage summoned her 51 fire pet as she hadn't researched the 49.

We intended to pull Lord out to the point that was specified in the map above. I was lucky to have the guild's best puller with us so I was pretty sure we could get him there. Unfortunately, Lord was already spawned in the building. Since I wasn't informed of this, I allowed the puller to pull the black reaver to our point and we killed him. Of course, no Lord Rak spawned. Then he told me that Lord Rak was still in the room.

We altered our plans and decided to fight him in the room. He was in the NW corner. We gathered everyone into the room in the SE corner and instructed them not to get close to Lord Rak unless the fire pets failed. The magicians then parked their pets on the opposite side of Lord Rak. We also equipped the pets with gnoll hide lariats. We kept taunt on for the two 49 fire pets and turned the 51 fire pet's taunt off.

I turned in the tear and aggroed Lord Rak. He began beating on me and I called for the pets to attack. Within 30 seconds I was able to back away from the fight and join the rest of the raid in the corner. The fight lasted 10 minutes and eventually pushed Lord Rak into the SE corner. Our raid moved around him. The deaths were minimal, and really could have been prevented. We were very surprised at how small his AE range was.

Anyway, I looted the scale and was very happy. After the fight I talked to the clerics. They never had to complete heal. They used remedy or celestial heal two or three times on the pet that Lord Rak was beating on. I know that I superior healed the main pet a few times. The other two pets never took any damage.

I wanted to thank the members of this message board for their assistance and guidance in the various steps of my epic. The information was invaluable, and the encouragement was appreciated.
