gummox
How many people complain regularly about the power gap between casual players and hardcore raiders?

These "casual" players, especially the ones that are complaining about this power gap, will be level 70 quickly. I guarantee it. It's one of the major selling points of OoW.

One of the features of the next patch is the addition of a quest for some (my best guess is the two that can drop pre Kod Taz) GoD spells. This change implies to me that some (and hopefully all) of the level 65-70 spells will be attainable by the "casual" level 70 player that's willing to put forth enough effort.

Here's something that I'm sure some people still haven't realized yet: Spells are designed and balanced with the focus effects factored in. Do you think that maybe the focus effect on these arms (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68886) had something to do with shaman only getting a 1550 hp heal instead of what was requested?

The stats on ornate gear and other Focus IV items are already pitiful compared to the items dropping in higher end PoP, let alone GoD or OoW. Nobody wears ornate any longer than they absolutely have to, and thanks to LDoN there are actually a few options to replace ornate with. But not all the focii on ornate armor exist in LDoN, which means that there are people that are stuck wearing ornate armor that has horrible stats because they need the focus effect on it.

Yes, I said they need the focus effect, just like they need spells, and just like melees need weapons. They can get by without, but they're being penalized in effectiveness for not having the proper gear to use thier spells in the capacity they were balanced around.

What's going to happen to the gap between the casual player and the hardcore raider when the named focus effects that raiders have available to them work on the lvl 70 spells but the generic IV level effects don't?

On a somewhat related subject: I personally think the way focus effects are implemented in EQ is very close to ideal, but far enough away to be frustrating when choosing upgrades. I would be much happier if focus effects were smaller, say 2-5% per item, but the smaller effects on multiple items stacked.

You wouldn't be tied to any one item because it's focus was irreplacable, and you wouldn't have any one item that instantly makes a caster 20-45% more effective.
