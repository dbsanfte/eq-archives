let's take the same mob for both of your examples.

at level 51, you get x amount of exp for killing mob Y, it takes you say 10 minutes to solo it dead.

at level 65, you will get the same X amount of exp for killing mob Y, it takes you 3 minutes to kill it dead solo.

AA exp is always the same it takes the amount "M" to get AA #1, and it takes the same M amount to develop AA #100, unlike level exp which rises as you level. level 51 takes amount P, level 52 takes P+R to develop.

taraddar
There are a couple different things to consider. It used to be that dinging 61 greatly increases the level that a dark blue was. (something like 45 at 60 and 53 at 61) That was so it isn't as bad but it does get slowly higher and ends up being mid 50's or so for dark blue at 65. So there are things at 65 that you wouldn't be able to hunt for exp that you could have at 60.

There is also the difficult mob bonus for fighting mobs closer to you level. So a level 58 mob killed by a level 60 is gonna give you more exp than the same level 58 mob killed by a 65.

Obviously the best situation is being high level and killing mobs at or near your level. In a good group in PoFire i've done entire aa's in under an hour. If however you aren't able to hunt higher level mobs like this you'll get the best exp by killing mobs near your level possibly at a lower level.

Obviously the rate at which you can gain exp can vary greatly based on a number of different factors. Leveling up typically requires you to kill higher level mobs but potentially yeilds greater exp.

The actual numerical amount of exp required for an aa is constant reguardless of level, race and class so the faster you can get exp the faster you can get aa's.
