Sudedor
Short of having a full install available I don't think you can do this.

The patches are downloaded by a launcher written by Sony. In fact, you don't even get the actual file. For instance, if there are changes to Everquest2.exe you get a file called Everquest2_exe.delta which is MUCH smaller. Basically it tells your machine how to reconstruct your Everquest2.exe into the new and improved version.

Now, if you have a full install, you could just run the installer from wherever you are and copy your entire EQ2 folder back to your home machine.

To expand on what Sudedor said, I think in EQ2 they're using partial file patching, which really helps to cut down on the download time. But unlike EQ, where they pushed down GZIP compressed versions of the whole file, you don't have a way to apply the new patches. Unless you're really skilled with a hex editor or something. :)
