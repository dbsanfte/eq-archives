IBM Announces Butterfly Grid
IBM working on grid-based service for online video games.


IBM in collaboration with Butterfly.net are planning to launch a grid-based service for running online games. According to the deal, IBM will provide the servers and physical space for the Butterfly Grid project, while software developer Butterfly.net will supply the software for game developers. Butterfly Grid will operate on the open-source Globus Project software. Software development kit is available for download from Butterfly. CEO David Levine, said that Butterfly Grid would offers significant benefits for massively multiplayer games such as "EverQuest" or "Ultima Online" reducing the price of games. "Our whole focus is to get the price point below a dollar per player per month," Levine said. "Right now, for a lot of games it's between $4 and $6."
