Well besides the obvious choice of going with the one that you prefer, I'd try to figure out which one your new guild is going to need the most.

With 3-4 active shamans, I'd say you have enough for most raid situations already. Will an additional shaman be needed over an additional mage? Over all though ask your self in a given night with your guild, do we need an additional slower, or an additional DPS class. If guild does not have groups in need of slowers I'd make the mage the main. But if your guild has a shortage of slowers, then the shaman would be the first choice.

BlargX
Forget what your guild needs. You're playing for yourself; it's a game after all. If you don't do what you want to do, you'll tire of the game and the guild grind much more quickly than if you're doing what you want to do. Make yourself happy and you'll have a much better chance of making the people around you happy too.

I think you might want to ask yourself how much time you'll be raiding. Shamans have a lot of responsibility, and even when they don't, they spend a lot of time clicking canni instead of talking to their friends, popping out of their seat to grab a soda, etc. Every time someone dies, it's time to rebuff. If you can't find someone who needs healing, you're probably not trying hard enough. There's always something to do. And chances are, there will always be someone who says you should have dropped whatever it was you were doing and instantly fixated on them, their buffs, their health, whatever.

Being a shaman can be aggravating and tiring. There is some small chance for glory sometimes, and appreciation too. If you want to be busy all the time, whether you like it or not, be a shaman. Sometimes you'll feel you're doing something pretty vital, as one of your pay-offs.

If you want to kick back more, have a chance to leap up and take a pee without people much noticing, and generally feel like the game is a bit less of a grind, be a mage. There's a lot to be said for having a little time to relax and enjoy yourself -- this being a game and all -- without feeling that you'll be letting people down if you pet the dog or exchange a few tells with friends.

If you want to gear up quickly, silk rots like crazy from what I see. You will be able to get your mage geared up very quickly compared to your shaman, so if the idea of having a very powerful toon very quickly appeals to you, you won't have to wait for it near as long with a mage as you do with a shaman. And when you do wait, it won't be as frustrating. People won't bug you and moan and groan if your DS doesn't last extra long, if they even want it at all, but they will pester your shamans if your many buffs wear out quick, and the shaman will notice if someone gets off debuffs far faster than he does. A mage critting for a little less isn't going to draw much attention or pressure for it, and it will fix itself soon enough when you upgrade quickly.

Anyway, I've found silk stuff to drop very quick compared to chain, from HOT in Velious days to the elemental planes where I'm at now.

When I was maximum crazy for this game, shaman would have been the only choice I'd even consider. After a number of years though, kicking back sounds better and better. Unless you have a serious preference for the shaman, I think the mage actually has quite a bit to recommend it.
