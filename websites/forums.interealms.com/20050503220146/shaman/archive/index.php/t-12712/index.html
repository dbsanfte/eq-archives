Kianor
Assuming you mean chloroblast, not chloroplast...

I still use chloroblast. In fact, I never have it unmemmed. It's a nice, quick heal and is suitable for backing up the cleric when a caster's being beat on or something. You can think of it in these terms: In an emergency situation, Chloroblast will heal 425 HP in 3 seconds time, with Stoicism you can heal 160 HP in 6 seconds time. You can see how it can be a lifesaver.

Stoicism is, however, a workable main group heal, and efficient with it. It's great for cannibalizing also, or for healing casters when the situation isn't immediately lifethreatening. Indeed, because it's a heal over time, you can also do other things (such as cannibalize) while it's in effect.

I haven't long had Stoicism, and still haven't got a good feel for when to use it - I still use Chloroblast on tanks a lot, to avoid the downsides. I even have been main healer in a PoP group using mostly Chloroblast. :) I've just hit 58, so now I have to get a feel for Kragg's Mending too!

I'll say this: it's nice having these different types of heals, it gives us options, but it's a pain trying to work out where to fit them all in my spell line up. :)
