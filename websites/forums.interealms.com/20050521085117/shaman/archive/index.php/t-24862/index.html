veeshanMurtak
Can someone explain why Humans always have the next to shortest lifespan?
Because of fantasy stereotypes and because some people think they need to have realism in a fantasy game.

Tolkien's Elves essentially lived forever. Thus 99% of all elves in all games live much longer then humans. And after all, all fantasy games need elves, right?

Humans live for 60 to 100 years in most games, because we can't have them living longer then real humans do, because that would be unrealistic (in a game where I can shoot fire out of my hands, mind). Why next to no games have humans living 30 to 50 years, which is much more realistic given the living conditions in most games, I cannot say.

Then you get orcs and ogres and trolls and the likes. If someone bothered to include them at all they will have shorter lifespans. Because, you know, they are like, dumb. And Neanderthals are dumb. And Neanderthals lived for less then humans live now.

It is sad but next to every game has long-lived, wise, pointy-eared bow-toting elves, grumpy, axe-wielding, smithy-loving dwarfs, middle-of-the-line humans, dumb, strong orcs that die early and sneaky, agile prankster halflings.

If you want to get rid of this you will have to look for some of the more creative pen & paper RPGs or else design your own campaign setting. (I love the goblin empire I set up in mine. Nothing like a highly organised goblin army to make the players go "huh?".)

As for seeing something different then the above in a computer game, you will probably be out of luck for years to come. Companies design for the crowd and the crowd's initial reaction to new approaches always seems to be "hey, why can other races shoot a bow better then my elf?".
