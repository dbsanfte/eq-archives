Well... I did the whole "Almost naked Monk soloing with their fists and a Survival Staff until level 22" thing back in late 2000 when I created Scalia, my first melee character. No twinking since I wanted to get a feel for the class. It was certainly doable and it will be vastly easier with a Shaman for backup, although Human Monks have no scales and are thefore weak and worthless, even if I say so myself :) :)

But jokes aside, these days Monks are quite dependent on their equipment. Perhaps not as much as Warriors, but at higher levels it is very very noticeable. Last time we wiped on Vindi and had a messy CR, I was helping with the "see invisible" Giants before the Arena area and was quite shoked by my pitiful DPS. With Shamanic slows and haste, you will probably be OK, but only if you adjust your expectations accordingly.

If you want to run a melee/shaman duo to 60 quickly and without having to rely on equipment too much, you may want to consider a Beastlord. They tank at least as well as Monks and their DPS comes mostly from the pet which, like all of their pre-61 spells, is store-bought - very important on a new server - which makes you relatively equipment independent. Having to control two pets in tight quarters may be a bit too much, though.

Also, consider what kind of levelling path you have in mind. A Shaman/Monk duo can do dungeons in relative safety as long as the Monk knows how to FD split casters using corners. Almost any other combination needs to be either overpowered for the dungeon or hunt near the zone line or have some other means of CC/escape for the melee. I suppose the Thurgadin gate potion may work as long as you can Root all mobs with your Shamans and get out of the casters' line of sight, but the casting time is 10 seconds...
