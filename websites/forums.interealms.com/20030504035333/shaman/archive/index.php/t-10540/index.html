Well first off Turgurs improves with level, its 65% at lvl 51 and 75% from 60+. The other slows we get along the way are situational including a disease based slow (can land on mr creatures) and an AE slow (can hit 4 targets). Both are lesser slows than turgurs but can be nie in certain situations.



Malo and malos are unresistable. You land these then try and land one of the bigger (malosini) resistable slows.
Goldark
The best slow is at 51, but its effectiveness continues to increase up to level 60. At level 60 its a 75% slow - at level 51 its only 66%. Note that a level 60 enchanter slows better (70%) than a level 51 shaman - although after tashing they may regret it! At 56, no other class is better at slowing, period.

You may be getting confused by Tigir's, Plague of Insects, and Cloud of Grummus. Tigir's is an AE slow (50%, max 4 mobs) and PoI and CoG are disease based slows for 25% and 40% respectively. Tigir's is very situational and rarely used in xp groups. I like it in chardok raids.

The disease based slows are generally of use for a limited number of boss mobs with very high mr but much lower disease resistance. With malo and insidious decay (unresistable pr/dr debuff) you'll find the disease slow lands where the normal one won't.

http://www.dith.demon.co.uk/slows/ shows you which mobs to use the disease slows on - they are very few and far between.

As for the malo line, Malo and Malos are unresistable. Malosini and Malosinia are resistable. On highly mr mobs you might start with malo/malos and then try to add malosini/malosinia to get that little bit more of a debuff. Other cases you may just use malo, or just use malosini - depends how mr the mobs are.
