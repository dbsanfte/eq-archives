OK, I recently upgraded my computer from Windows 2000 to Win XP Pro. And everything works fine, EXCEPT - my chat channels do not work.

After 5-60 seconds I get a message "your connection with the chat channel server has been broken. Try zoning to reestablish the connection." Zoning doesn't work, though.

Other factoids:
1. Win XP Pro, 1 gig mem, 3.2 Mhz P4, plenty of HD space, recently defragged. ATI 9600 Pro, Turtle Beach Santa Cruz, latest drivers on both. All Windows Update patches applied, including DX9.0b. XP Pro was installed to a clean, formatted C: drive; my EQ data was on the D: drive.

2. My Win2k configuration worked fine. My wife's XP Pro machine works fine. My Win2k laptop works fine. It's just MY XP Pro machine that doesn't work.

I'm about to wipe EQ and reinstall from scratch, but if anyone has any other suggestions for something quick and easy to try, I'm all ears.

WOOT! PROBLEM FIXED!

Thanks to Pressto-Rathe on the EQ tech support forums. He said that the Linksys firmware version 1.44.2 is the ONLY version known to work well with EQ, firmware versions higher than that drop chat channels. I downgraded my firmware from 1.45 to 1.44.2z (available on http://www.linksys.com) and the problem went away.
