Nothing is going to beat a tank if you're going to get summoned. I wouldn't worry that much about DPS of your shaman partner if I were you; the main thing is whether you can slow mobs so THEIR DPS is manageable compared to your healing, mostly with quiessence/torpor. Shamans can last almost forever, so it's about outlasting the mobs, is how I see it.

I gave up the DPS of my monk for first a warrior then a SK. I found the monk DPS was nice, but far more important was survivability. A paladin is really not bad at all on that count, and their stuns can save you mana by not making you need to heal as much, since a stunned mob does no DPS. The aggro on a hybrid tank will let the shaman DOT and DD at will, too.

The only problem is -- no defensive discipline.

Zorash
I hate to knock melees, but honestly, in many of these situations I'd think that an Ench would be your best bet. Charm a pet, snare (from a clicky item), haste/buff/equip him just before engaging. Pull with malos, engage pet, back the ench way off after tash to allow re-charming if it breaks (remember, mob's snared), let the shaman try to debuff/dot/nuke/heal if needed. Then just sit and watch the pet destroy the boss.

This is, of course, situational, as there won't always be mobs tough enough around to charm and send in. But, in the case of many of the mobs, this would probably work just fine, assuming things went well.

Assuming things DON'T go well, well, then this is a nasty combo, since a pet can be a lot to handle for some enchies, especially in a small combo.

For situations where this isn't viable, I would say that a plate tank is probably your best bet. Pallies have stun, heals, and LoH for survivability, Sks have dots, taps, nukes and HT for extra dps and a little added health regen. Warriors have nice discs, more hp/mitigation, and decent taunt/dps with the right weapons. Really it just depends on who you have with you :)

Anywho...have fun :)
