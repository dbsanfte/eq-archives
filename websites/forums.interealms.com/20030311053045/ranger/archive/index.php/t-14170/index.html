Spritle
This may or may not have been addressed here previously but I'll post anyway.

In my opinion, Bind Sight is one of the most under utilized and under appreciated spells in a Ranger's arsenal. By using this simple spell, it is often possible to scout an entire zone by remote viewing. Last night I toured almost the entire PoN zone without ever leaving the zone in area. Simply target a mob, bind to it, then use the F9 key to switch to panoramic view. Target the next mob and recast. Using this method, one can hop around from mob view to mob view, garnering valuable recon data in areas not normally accessible.

Try this out and practice using it. At the very least, it gives you something to do while waiting for the cleric to med, etc.. Also comes in handy when you're sitting at the PoJ trails waiting for your turn. You can watch the current group's progress and know how they are doing before it gets announced in OOC.

Spritle
No, you do not need line of sight any more. It was nerfed at one time so that you had to have line of sight to a target to use it but that was changed a few patches back. The only requirements now to bind sight is to have the mob/NPC/Player targeted and be in the same zone. Then with the use of the F9 key to pan out in third person view, you can target other mobs and recast onto them, hence jumping around from mob to mob and scouting at your leisure.

By the way, this is an easy way to single pull the castle steps in PoD. Bind to a wizard's Eye of Zomm and use it to harmony the firebone casters on the second flight of stairs. Then do the same for the two deathbones up top.

Just try it out and you will soon get the hang of it.
