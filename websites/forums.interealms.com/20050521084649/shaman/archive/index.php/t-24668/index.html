Loramin
I second what Weyalan said. VP3 is good for everything: if you solo, it's good whenever something gets resisted or wears off and you end up with an unslowed mob in your face. If you group, it's great when you accidentally get agro and can't afford to take a few hits, or when you have no enchanter and need to ghetto mez. On raids ... well on raids it's less useful, but there are times when it is handy to move stuff around or just shove some trash mob aside so you can focus on the main task. And if you are ever like me, and occaisionally fill your spell slots with buffs in zones other than PoK, then it is great when something random gets trained on you and you don't have root up.

As for Mnemonic Retention, this may sound odd, but even though OoW has only been out for a few months, I already have a hard time remembering what it was like to only have 8 slots. When I play caster alts, I keep expecting the 9th slot to be there. Again, this AA is useful for every type of play; for me personally, I've found it lets me have AE slow, gate, FA, a nuke, or anathema (I've been one EC LDoN away from Bane for soooo long) up at various times when they are handy, but not as important as eight other spells. Plus if you have the perfect 8-slot set for a given activity, that 9th slot is perfect for letting you mem random buff or utility spell X without having to disrupt your nomal lineup. IMHO, especially considering it is only 3 AAs, Mnemonic Retention is right up there with VP, RC, and SCR(M) in being a must-have after CannV.
