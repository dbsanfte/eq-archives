Smithing is UNGODLY hard to raise now...worse than tailoring for several races (as an important note, all shaman races but ogre are one of these).

At maximum skill, the success rate across all servers (for the armor for all races) is just under 50%

Each combine eats at least one blue diamond. If you can supply the smith with all of the ingrediants ready for a final combine (which will mean a not-inconsiderable amount of tradeskill work yourself in tailoring, brewing, and smithing), you could probably get them to press the button on the combine relatively cheaply, since they aren't risking any cash of their own. Be sure to group with the smith when they do it so you can see the success/fail message, and anticipate failure, cause that is likely what will happen.

Dandi
When figuring price, please remember that this stuff isn't yet trivial at 250. Even with 250 skill and a geerlok, it fails...sometimes a lot. Just the other day I blew 15 blue diamonds making a set of the plate equivalent (helanic tundra) legs. They might be harder than the chain (this stuff) but there isn't enough data to say for certain, all we know for sure is neither is trivial at 250 and both fail on a regular basis after that. I've had people inquire about buying artificers from me, but most are shocked by the price for something that is made with tradeskills rather than dropped. I flatly refused to make someone the gloves because coldain skin go for 3k easy and are actually better, while the artificers take 2 blue diamonds to make. I'd have to be lucky to come out of it at cost on the bds alone.

On the other hand, a few of the pieces are really nice and worth much more:

Arms: AC 16, WIS 5, HP 30, mana 30
Greaves: AC 22, STR 10, WIS 5, HP 40, mana 40
BP: AC 30,STA 5, WIS 10, HP 40, mana 40

Compared to what else is out there for barb shamans, this is pretty nice. For those of us for whom Velious quest armor isn't an option (or is a very slim chance), it's downright wonderful.

When setting my prices I simply look at what else is out there and what it sells for. For example, on the BP, I'd say it's not quite as good as, but close to, JB and the various dragonhides. Those sell very roughly for 30-40k based on my quick research. I'd say this is the same or better than a Phase Spider Carapace or Emerald Dragonscale Tunic, which go for 13-20k each, so I'd probably aim for 20k. At 3 blue diamonds a pop, and 1.5-2k each per bd on my server, and the demand for them making them hard to find at all, just a few failures (entirely possible) would wipe out any profit so I'd probably aim for 20-25k. I'd be willing to negotiate, though, if the customer wanted to provide bds or wanted to buy at least a couple other pieces, so that I'm more likely to profit even if I get unlucky on a piece or two. The one or two blue diamond pieces would naturally be proportionally lower because of the reduced risk of losing as many blue diamonds and a hope that the trivials are lower.

That's my thought process when I'm trying to set a price, at least. When I first started selling I encountered a LOT of resistance to the idea of selling a tradeskill item for prices equivalent to drops. I've had people call me every name in the book (and some creative new ones) for selling an item for twice the cost of a single attempt, in spite of the fact that they often fail multiple times. But if an item that it took a few hours and some luck to acquire is worth a given price, I fail to see how an item of comparable stats is worth any less just because someone made it. The combine took just a few seconds, but acquiring the components may have taken much longer and required a phenomenal investment of time (months at least) and money in acquiring the skill.
