Well I have played my shammy for almost 2.5yrs, and the great social experiment of EQ is almost over for me. I've been mostly a lurker, occassional poster on these boards over the past 2yrs, but have found it very entertaining (flame on johnny storm) and informative. As I reflect on all the hours I happily wasted in front of the computer (even happier when i was drunk :D ) I pause to ponder just what made me continue to play the shaman class. I had played other classes: wizzie, war, monk, no class really held my attention for long. I came to realize my love for the shaman class was not just because of the incredible versatility, independence, groupability, solo-ability, or bad-assness. No, it was just plain FUN. Even as I was runnin around in full cloth armor with my mighty splintering club at lvl 25, I was having a blast.
At 61 I still appreciate the hell outta the shaman class. We are more adept than any class (IMHO) at doing amazing things, and still be half mana to boot. However, I never appreciate my class more than when breezing through the nexus laughing at all the KEI krak whore addicts (hehe) dependent on some chanter to make them more effective. Bah! I can go 0-FM in around 5minutes, and that's without torpor.
The shaman class, of course, is not perfect. Alchemy, worthless stat buffs, alchemy, spell drop rates, and alchemy are just a few of the obvious and legitimate gripes that concern us. Despite the gripes, I still feel compelled to salute the original devs that laid the foundation for the shaman class, as I have taken much pleasure in their creation. I also salute the crucible, as it has given all us who are endeared to our alter ego shammies a /rant outlet ;)
