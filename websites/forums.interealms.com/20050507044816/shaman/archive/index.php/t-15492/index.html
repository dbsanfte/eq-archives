The hilarious thing is that I actually do exceed the DPS of most other classes in many groups I am in. I don't mean this in a wacky hole-in-space-time Bracy way, although I can see his point in solo situations.

What I mean is that, yes, our DDs are less efficient than other classes, but I can spend 60% of my mana during a fight and still be FM when the next pull comes in.

Now, there are places where our rains are resisted, but there are many places where they are not. And if I am casting Tears of Saryn X 2, with Torrent of Poison in the gap on each mob with few resists, thats a good chunk of damage, especially when you figure in the dual wielding hasted pet, who spits out over 30DPS all on his own, even using cheap daggers instead of proccing mage weapons.

And those rain spells are nice LOW aggro. I can cast ToS, then ToP, and ToS again and very VERY rarely ever get aggro.

Now again, this is very situational, and there are situations where it just doesn't work. In BoT for instance, the resists are just too much to use the rain spells for me, or for that matter any of our damage spells.

But seriously, those rain spells are some fun stuff in many cases, check em out.

I couldn't help but notice all the people in that thread talked about DoTs when talking about shaman DPS in exp groups. In most cases DoTs just arent any good in exp groups. Soloing is, of course, a different matter.

-Dredak

EDIT: I don't mean to say that I can exceed the DPS of a rogue, for instance, or an uber wizzie with all his AAs, or a proper ranger. My point is that, in many cases, a shaman really can contribute a good chunk of DPS, to the point where having 2 shamans in a group wouldn't necessarily be inefficient.

If I can do 5K in spells over the course of a typical 1 minute PoV exp group fight, then add in my pet DPS, I am contributing around 100 DPS. With rain spells, torpor, cann 4, cann 5, a horse, and KEI, you can do this and keep it up over a long period of time.
