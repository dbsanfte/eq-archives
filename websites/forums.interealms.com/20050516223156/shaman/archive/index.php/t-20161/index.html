Chutoi
The Vexd trial really is cake. It almost doesn't matter what make-up you use if you have elemental+ geared tankage and a competant cleric (with divine arbitration--don't overlook that skill in these trials). Pal, SK, warrior, are all fine. The rest of the group is really dependant on whether you have a chanter for charm pet. If you do, I'd go a defensive mix and go heavy back-up healers for charm breaks--you'll be slow on kills without the charm pet, but much safer overall.

Frankly, in vexd, once you have the tank/cleric filled out, the rest can be just about any mix.

Tipt is a different story. The named there is brutal. It might as well not be slowable (since the 18% or so slowable it actually is isn't all that useful and is darn hard to land). You don't HAVE to have a defensive warrior, but if you have a SK or Pally tank, they almost have to be time geared.

Note, on tipt. IMPORTANT--DO NOT PULL THE NAMED IN THE LAST ROOM OUT OF HIS ROOM. KILL HIM IN THE ROOM! If he's pulled out of the room, he gets substantially stronger. I think he hits for 3400s outside of the room, but max of 1800s in the room. One of our first guild groups to kill him took him out with a pally tank OUTSIDE the room! I don't recommend going for a repeat!

Tipt groups have to be a lot more circumspect in their choices of people. 1 heavy tank, 1 cleric, one solid back-up healer, someone who can take rampages on named, and the rest ought to be dps classes. Crowd control isn't really needed in either trial if you don't try to skip content--just have a good puller, and/or one who isn't afraid to just die to avoid training a group.

Once you get them down, neither trial is particularly difficult (except the tipt named), but they do get really tedious. I've probably done both trials a good 10 times now to help flag guildies.

veeshanMurtak
Vxed is not too bad, just long and tedious. All you really need is a tank and sufficient healing. It may take ages with a low DPS group (personal worst: 5.5 hours) but you will get there.

For Tipt you will have to build your group with the final named in mind. That means:
- a cleric
- a second healer
- either a solid tank or a charm pet or (risky) 2 people with riposte/parry disciplines
- damage. lots of it. the faster you end this fight the better.

And make absolutely sure to dispel the boss. If some roaming Shaman hasted him he may well rip through your tank even with an optimal group.

That being said, try to conserve your main tanks' and clerics' energy for Tipt. You should be able to do Vxed without or with fewer of them.

I know it sounds funny, and it's not something that I've ever done, but when you got the mobs hitting as hard as they do, you do what's necessary to survive.
