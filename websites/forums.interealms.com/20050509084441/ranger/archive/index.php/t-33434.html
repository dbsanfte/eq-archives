[QUOTE='Farsky']Either a Human Wizard or a Human Dark Avenger. Don't know what that is, but it sounds cool...[/QUOTE]
Human Wizard is a first tier(job change) class, you'll still have to select a second tier class later at 40. Your choices includes Sorceror, Necromancer and Warlock.
Warlock is basically a human summoner. There are three scenario as to their summonings, his/her pets will provide support to the caster's offensive capability, or he/she will support the pet's offensive capability, or both caster and pet will be on the offensive. Striking a balance will affect how well the caster perform. As such, they have several different types of pets available.
Necromancers are basically offensive support human mages who reduce enemies's effectiveness in combat through debuffing and dots.
Sorceror are basically the "Big Guns" of the human casters. They provide high damage offensive spells in combats and includes in their arsenal AoE spells. Thus in large skill combats, a group of them can effectively wipe a large group of enemies or concentrate their firepower to destroy a very powerful enemy. However, they do have their draw back of being unable to protect themselves and thus have to rely on groups and tanks to protect them.

Dark Avenger is a second tier class and well...closest thing in EQ would be a Shadow Knight. They are basically like the Paladins (Lineage 2's not EQ's) except that their magic are evil and beneficial to self only(like poison and life taps) whereas Paladins' spells are more beneficial to others.

That is the basic description available on the Taiwan Official site. You'll have to check forums for more in-depth description from players.

I think most, if not all, the rangers here who wants to play Lineage 2, are going either blade dancer or gladiator just because of dual wield. :)

Anyway, for those who do not know, anyone can use any weapons, but if you do not have the skill for them, you will not have bonuses applied to using the weapons. That said, there are no dual wield daggers currently in game. Swords are the only weapons which can be "crafted" into dual swords (take two swords, which meet the minimum grade and requirment, and give to a NPC smith to combine them I think, can't remember).

Only two classes have Dual Wield Mastery, they are Gladiator (Human) and Blade Dancer (Dark Elf). However, many healers (especially Prophets (Human)) use dual wield weapons also as the m.attack rating on weapons rarely affect their spells and also that their weapon skills is Weapon Mastery, which applies to all weapons they use as it does not specify which weapons. Fighters also get Weapons Mastery (stops at level 3), but they will begin to use specific weapon mastery upon choosing specific class path at level 20. Weapon Mastery for mages and healers progress up to 24 levels throughout their job path.

Note: There is no penalty for using items not designed for your class. However, you will not be able to use them as efficiently as the classes who have the skills for them. E.g. Shillien Knight will get a lower increase to their attack rating when using a dual katana compared to a Blade Dancer who is using the same weapon. However, without Expertise (Grade level) skill, using a weapon or armor of a higher grade than what you can use, will result in a penalty. This is to prevent twinking. Expertise (Grade) is automatically gained when you reach the level, its level dependant.
