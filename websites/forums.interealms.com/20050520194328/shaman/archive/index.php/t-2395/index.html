BlargX
Ogre 15% exp penalty is far worse than the troll or iksar 20% penalty, and they will level notably slower than either of those races -- they will level by far the slowest of all races.

This is because immunity to stun is great for vital situations like ToV or really big mobs where if you're the only shaman and you get wiped out, it's big trouble, but not nearly so incredible for normal experience mobs or soloing.

Regen at lower levels will rocket your health back up, and at higher levels, your regen rate increases, but it "feels" less dramatic.

Still, after a kill, an ogre will sit around for a very long time compared to an iksar or troll.

My ogre shaman wore an iksar regen breastplate, which is only 5/6 per tick, and the difference between wearing that and any other chest piece besides the fungi was like night and day. He could actually GAIN hitpoints in many fights once a mob was slowed. And 5/6 is a pittance compared to innate regen like trolls and ikkies get. When my ogre shaman put on a fungi, it was astounding how little time it took him to get health(and therefore mana) back, and wonderful that he would often be full health and mana or close to it after a fight. Take the regen off -- ouch.

Same with my human and iksar monks. The exp penalty was a joke considering how much regen negated it. The human had maxed bind wound skill and poured points into BW at every opportunity; the iksar barely bothered with bandaging until in his teens, and put almost no points in it. Yet still he was ready to go ASAP and much safer in fights because that regen was always ticking away.

Regen is godly to both shamans and monks, and having an innate regen shaman or monk makes a huge difference.

You REALLY feel the 15% on the ogre; on a troll or iksar, you not only don't feel it, you feel great about how quick you're ready to go again. Any non-regen race who has ever swapped in and out of a regen BP will know what I mean.

Of course, the more you solo, the more you notice how much it sucks not to have regen, and the more you group in _balanced_ groups, the less regen matters, because your personal regen or lack of it will become less crucial. You will notice the exp penalty more in groups than solo, because you won't have incredible regen making up so much of the negatives of a bad exp penalty. You'll just feel the exp penalty.

Once you get 60 and torpor, stun immunity is by far the best ability to have; with torpor and canni 4 (and eventually cann 5), neither mana nor hitpoints are really a problem -- at least not one than regen can solve. It's all very easy.

It's just that the first 59 levels are so much harder. ;)

I would recommend ogres to people who know they'll be in the game long enough to get torpor and really love playing shamans, or who can afford a regen BP. But if you already have a main active EQ character, I wouldn't start one without having bought at least an iksar BP first. Life will be dramatically easier with regen -- it's like night and day the difference it makes.

If you're just a casual player or don't know how long you will stick with the game -- inny troll is the easiest choice, and probably is in general the best shaman. It's the one most people would have the most fun with, since regen keeps you moving, not medding, and the troll is strong and can use JBB.

If you really like ogres, they're excellent shamans, but it takes torpor to overcome the incredible advantage that trolls are born with. As are iksar -- but that's another matter. And -- you will FEEL it when you have to sit on your arse all day after a kill as a non-regen race compared to how easy you have it when you yourself are a walking fungi tunic.

Regen is king, until torpor, when it becomes trivial.
