I haven't played a bard, so take this with a grain of salt . . .

The mage pets, especially the air and earth pets, are pretty darn good tanks. "Good", as in taking a beating fairly well and holding agro at the same time. They won't crank out huge DPS, but it's not bad, either. I play a mage quite a bit, and I'm fairly impressed with their pets as tanks. With a slowed mob, almost anything can tank, but the mage pets do a good job while throwing in some decent dps as well. With a good agro pet, like the 61 air pet, you can use your DOTs more often, and the mage can nuke often enough to throw in some serious DPS.

A mage/shaman combo, with a necro thrown in, would be a REALLY nice dps team. You're a little lacking in some areas, but virtually any trio is. Don't underestimate the tanking power of a mage pet - they really can hold up pretty well.

A mage's biggest weakness is mob control. Mage pets help suppliment that quite a bit, but shaman and necro can easily make up in that arena, and the Earth Pet later on is IMO a more preferable tank than a bard. You wanna go somewhere tough?

Mage dies.
Necro FD drags to the spot, rezzes mage.
CoH Shaman and you're there.

Stun is about the only biggie I can think of, I dunno if necro or mage can stun.

Tragik
Just the shaman and necro are an amazing duo already. Necromancers are also excellent kiters if you find a situation that requires it (usually for efficiency if that matters to you).

In that case any class for the 3rd slot will work. I'd actually stay away from a tank class if you want to avoid a trinity type setup and stay with the bard or, as many suggest, throw in a second pet class. A mage, beastlord or even another necro will work fine.

It was mentioned that bards can slow and while this is true, remember that it's only for 3 ticks (18 seconds) and forces the bard to sacrifice other areas of utility. Also in this trio he won't be doing much tanking later on without very good gear which means he'll be twisting dots mostly or leading the kite. I like the idea of a bard though. Incomparable utility and decent DPS ability = a very powerful and versatile class.

I also have no idea where the rumor begin that necro pets are horrid tanks. I wouldn't call them awesome tanks and they're certaintly not mage level, but mine tanks anything that hits for up to mid 500's (slowed 70% or more) just fine, even if it's just me (as the necro) doing the healing. With Turgur's, Q and Tnarg's most mobs up to Tier3 are a joke for the 65 nec pet to tank with nothing other than the LDoN focus. The only exception to this is the rogue class pets (53 and 63rd level).

Another bonus to having a shm and nec is the ability to combine Malos/Malosinia and Words of Terris (high level undead charm) to rake in some very nice exp and farm nearly anything you want in places like Veksar.

Shadow Knight brings:
Tanking

Snare
Corpse Recovery

Beast Lord brings:
Slow
Damage

Haste

Cleric brings:
Heal
HP Buffs
Res
Caster interrupt (stun line)
Invis to Undead

The only thing you are double up on is Invis to Undead. What you are
lacking is Resist Debuff (for easier landing of slow), backup healer (chloroblast really doesn't cut it when mobs hit for more than it heals at the levels the BL would be using it), and ports.

This is the best three man group I can think of. Personally I find it to be superior to the "holy trinity" in versatility. The main downside to this three man group is that a lot of the utility of the beast lord does not come until later levels (mana regen and haste most notably).

Now if by holy trinity you mean "any tank" "any slower" "any healer" then obviosuly this group doesn't meet your requirements. But caster pets, as good a tanks as they may be, will not take you very far. Since PoP, the game has been more and more tuned such that groups require a true tank, a true slower, and a true healer.
