our roots do damage, hence the reason they break mez, so i primarily use root for crowd control when there is no enchanter, or the enchanter needs the mob off him/her NOW. Root does not make a mob unmezzable since it does not do damage over time. I don't believe melee damage can break root, but i tend to avoid rooting the main mob if at all possible, since it makes agro ping around (if the warrior isn't right on the ball) and can be somewhat annoying. My technique for crowd control without a chanter is to ensnare an add as it comes in, this generates enough agro usually, for me to kite it away into a corner or something and root it there. If there's a chanter, i'll simply ensnare each mezzed mob, this will give the chanter a little more time to remez if mez is broken for some reason, and sometimes (depending on what else the chanter has cast on it) the mob will agro on me instead of the chanter when mez breaks, which is a good thing.
Maian62
It sounded to me like the original question was something like - if the main tank can't keep the main mob off the chanter, should I root it even though the proximity aggro will override the hate list ordering?

In my opinion, quickly grabbing aggro from the chanter and then restoring it to the main tank is one of the things we do best. Personally I almost never use roots on the main mob, usually only when peeling adds. If the main tank can't do their job with aggro and the chanter is taking hits, I will start mashing taunt/disarm while hitting and throw in a snare and/or DoT if I haven't landed one already, until it turns on me, then back off and revert to normal melee while the main tank gets aggro back. I figure I can take a few more hits than the chanter. In fact I figure its my job to die if necessary to save the chanter anyway. If there is room I will kite it around and let them all backstab it, easy way to kill stuff.
With adds, the difference is that once I get aggro I lead them out of melee range if poss then root, whether the chanter is mezzing or not. My chanters usually don't mind remezzing a rooted mob if necessary. And yes always good to snare the mezzed ones if poss. If you do end up rooting the main mob just a message letting the others know should be enough to enable them to adjust . Or else nuke it until it breaks...
