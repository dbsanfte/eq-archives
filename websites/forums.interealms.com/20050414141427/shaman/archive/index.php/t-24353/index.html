I assume the CPU is 64-bit (not sure what ADA is)? Either way though, IMHO, it isn't worth it. Go down to your local bookstore or wherever, and pick up one of those hardcore PC magazines. Maximum PC is a good one, for instance. It will cost you a little under $10, but hey you are buying a $2600 computer so ... (also if you want you can just read it in the store). Anyhow, all those mags do reviews of new high-end gaming computers, but more importantly they have a metric assload of advertisements (usually toward the back) from computer companies. The Maximum PC in front of me for instance had 8 pages of just the kind of ad where they list multiple systems, with various configurations at various prices, plus a few more pages thta only had a single system on them.

However, if you really want to get the best system for your money, I think you should build it yourself. Now, I personally am comfortable with doing anything except soddering (sp?) random weird cooling sstems on to my MB. I'm guessing you aren't. Still, if you shop around (especially if you are lucky enough to have a major computer store like Fry's nearby) you can probably find a package that gives you the case with a power source, fans, motherboard, and CPU already installed. All you have to add then is your drives, your RAM, and your AGP/PCI cards, all of which just involve just pushing components in to slots, or connecting ribbon cables from a drive to your MB (well, that and a bunch of screwdriver work).

If you want to go that route, I suggest using sites like tomshardware.com and cnet.com to review various parts, and sites like techbargains.com and CNet's price comparison thing to find the best deals. But however you end up doing it, good luck building/buying your new system :)

P.S. If you want to just buy your system techbargains.com, CNet, and other price comparison sites can be useful too.
