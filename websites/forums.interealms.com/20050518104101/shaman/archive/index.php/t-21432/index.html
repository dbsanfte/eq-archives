Tvarr
I have trolled these boards for many months but never posted. A couple of Shaman friends of mine suggested I post how I successfully main heal Hard LDoNs with a 65 group. This thread is not meant to be pro or con to the threads of “shaman upgrades” so please don’t hijack it for that purpose.

Perspective: I group with a core group of 4 people that make this work. We can add just about any other 2 classes and be successful 91% of the time to date. I have as of this writing 300+ LDoN victories. I and the folks I group with are not uber equiped. Most of us have a couple pieces of VT gear and some LDoN gear. I will post my magelo. The core group is a 65 Pally, 65 ENC, 65 RNG, and me a 65 Shammy. I only have one level of healing adept and the Pally has maxed his mitigation AAs. We normally do RUJ or MIRs for reasons I will explain later.

Spell line up is thus: Tnarg’s, Q, Bane, Canni, Velium Strike, Turgur’s, Tigir’s, BoSaryn.

Normal pull:
I watch the Group ToT Health of the puller to ensure I am not canni IV/V when he acquires a target.
RNG pulls with snare and has pull msg.
I immediately cast Q on the Pally and watch for adds coming down the long halls of RUJ or MIRs
The ENC Tash and Slows the incoming mob while the Pally is chain stunning for agro.
If it’s a single pull and I have time I will cast Bane at this point while counting to myself how long Q will last. Everyone else is in damage mode. Don’t forget your puppy.
If it is a multiple pull the Pally always takes the first mob to reach the pull spot, I immediately slow the Pally’s mob while the ENC locks down the adds and slows them.
Once mobs are locked down and slowed it is Tnarg’s Mending to patch heal the Q on the Pally, Q on anyone that needs heal, Canni IV, extra dot, Vel strike, or Canni V depending on at what health the mob is, your mana, other party members health, etc (that’s the art of being a shaman)

In a normal Hard LDoN the pally will group heal about one time and have to use his LoH about every 3rd dungeon we do. Occasionally we do loose folks but have a pally for rez and overall it is fun and rewarding to do Hard LDoNs at 65 as the main healer.

If you have questions I would be happy to try and answer them.

Tordail,

I find trouble in main healing and slowing in addition because of the aggro that doing both generates. I've gained the attention of a few mobs by doing one to many patch heals on a mob that was just slowed.

A few general tips on Main healing as a Shaman in Earth:

1.) Q the tank when you hear the inc message from the puller. This gives you enough time for the following.

2.) Click Malos madly so that it starts casting the millisecond the mob gets in range. Extended Range/Hasted spells obviously hugely beneficial here.

3.) Usually the mob has just reached the tank by the time Malos lands. Naturally. Click turgur's maddly until it starts to cast.

4.) This is where your knowledge of the tank comes into play. By now slow has landed, if it is resisted, you will want to use your best judgement as whether to toss a heal then try to reslow, or let the tank soak the damage and repair it when slow has landed. Regardless, slowing the mob is high high proiroity, as healing on an unslowed mob is very difficult at best for us.

5.) Assuming slow lands on the first try, a decent tank will probably have taken a good 2 or 3 rounds from the mob and be between 65-80% life. Now, here's the trick...

6.) Do not use Tnargs unless you must! Dust of Kragg's (yeah, I said it), mem that sucker and use it now! Yes, you will be alternating casts of Q and Kragg's. Try to keep Q up and on the tank through the duration of the fight. Kragg's casting time is forever, but has the benefit of being the second most efficient heal we have. You should be able to push the tank's life back up to 80-100% by the time the mob is 1/2 dead.

7.) If the tank is near full health, you should consider casting Kragg's unless you need to canni (and you probably will want to squeeze some staggering in these moments), or now is the time to cann5 and Q yourself while the tank can take a few good rounds without your attention. Q the rest of the team as needed.

- Remember, your mission is to keep everyone alive, capping people off to full health is only reserved for the tank, conserving mana is key. If the puller is taking damage getting the mobs to you, consider Qing him/her before they set out for the next mob.

- The group will always think you are "gtg" since you're a shaman, inform them of your status after every 2 mobs or so, sometimes an extra 5 seconds of canning can mean the difference if you ask your puller to not run off so fast for that next mob.

- Chain pulling is hard to heal for as a Shaman, you're going to be needing that incoming time for prep and debuffing. Make sure your puller doesn't think he has to impress the group or anything.

- If you think your group will get regular adds occasionally (can't be helped sometimes), or if the puller is inexperienced with the mobs/zone/etc. Don't go for maximum cycle on cann5, use it as your emergency reserves, go ahead and cycle it if you're above 60% mana to get it to you faster, you should be ok for a few mobs.

-Instruct your group to make a big effort to ghetto mez adds if no chanter is present. Root, VP are equalivalent to 100% slow, make sure you and your group make use of that.

That's what I have to offer of the top of my head.

I'm not sure I agree on using KM.

Initially it looks pretty good. 1950hps healed for 400 mana or about 5hps per mana vs 1300hps healed for 450 mana or ~3 hps per mana but that changes as you get better gear/aa's.

Lets look at things with maxed aa's now and good focuses.

Kraggs is now healing for 2320 and can't crit and isn't focused by Improved healing focuses. Mana cost is ~300 mana after specialization and SCM. We'll call it 7.7 hps per mana.

For Tnarg's is healing for an average of 1677 and criting for over 3k. For mana cost you get the 25% from spec and SCM plus an additional ~5% from a normal mana pres item so about 315mana. Ends up being about 5.3 hps per mana. A bit closer than the unfocused situations.

Taking it up to elemental focus's improves things more. Kraggs doesn't change at all but tnargs does. Tnargs now healing for an average of 1710hps for about 300 mana. That pushes it up to 5.7 hps per mana. Obviously still behind kraggs but closing the gap.

Keep pushing it for better imp healing focuses and mana pres focuses and the gap keeps closing. With best current focuses you'd heal for an average of 1840hps and mana cost of 292 pushing it upto 6.3.

Also if your tank has enough hps for it to be safe you can let him drop enough for you to crit for almost double the hps on tnargs. That should average out to about a 20% improvement in amount healed. That puts the elemental shaman at about 2010hps healed averaging out that crit and about 6.7 hps per mana. Fully focused it's 2150hps for 292 or 7.3 hps per mana.

When you start factoring cast time and spell casting hastes it's enough to make tnargs the prefered option IMO. With your cast time below 3 seconds on tnargs vs 10 seconds on krags the difference in mana cost can easily be covered by more time to cann for mana. For efficiency I'll use Q or breath of trushar which can get into the teens for hps healed per mana.

If your lucky enough to have Daluda's Mending it's even better since it starts off a little bit more efficient anyway ~3.2 hps per mana.
