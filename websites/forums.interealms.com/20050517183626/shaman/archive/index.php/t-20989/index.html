Chutoi
I posted this over at the EQ boards just for curiousity sake. Just wondered if anyone here had any comments. Probably completely infeasible from a programming standpoint, but kind of a fun intellectual analysis.

"Well, the developers are actually going to look at shaman now that a third of the end-game shaman have retired and/or changed classes. That got me to thinking . . . what can they do? Heal over time and damage over time spells are not well designed for a short-fight situations. Nor does that only affect shaman. Necros, druids, enchanters, and BLs come to mind as well.

I've posted some discussions on balancing before, but I was curious about a thought I had the other day. DoT classes are kind of screwed in short fight situations (GoD and, from developer comments at fan fair, likely all future expansions). So from everyone's perspective (NOT just endgame), what do you think the effects would be if they could code something like the following:

DoTs (from all DoT classes/procs/items) do the same damage, but

(a) do damage every 3 seconds instead of 6 (ie maybe 210 every 3 seconds instead of 420 every 6)
(b) take mana each damage tic or half-tic (spell costs 400 mana and lasts 5 tics, it would take 80/tic before AAs or focus item effects)
(c) the DoT will end when target dies
(d) make casting times substantially faster

From what I see at a first pass, it preserves the damage already set, so won't unbalance classes substantially in terms of dps adjustments. It makes DoTs mana efficient in short term fights if they cut off when the target is dead and the mana is only used as damage is done. The cast times would allow a DoT class to stack a couple before the mob died. The faster damage (every 3 seconds) would allow damage in short-fight situations with no real increase to average dps.

Thoughts? Completely outside programming difficulties (which would probably be substantial), what would the above unbalance? I'm sure I've missed something!

Chutoi"

What do you guys think?

Anjar
When you're casting a DoT in a fast paced environment, I still don't see how cast times have much effect on their use. If the DoT gives me 400 damage / tick and lasts 10 ticks, that's 60 seconds for full use. The cast time added onto that adds only about 5 seconds to the time required for full use of the spell.

If the DoT goes full duration, then I've done 4000 damage in 65 seconds. Mathematically, going from 65 to even 60 seconds (instant cast) doesn't change the DPS significantly, doesn't change the mana / HP damage.

If the DoT doesn't go full duration, the only thing that lowering the cast time can do is to give me a chance of getting one additional tick's worth of damage (difference in cast time / 6 seconds per tick * 100%). Shave 3 seconds off the cast time, for example, and there's a 50% chance that I'll get one additional tick. Sure, that would be useful, but I don't think it a dramatic change in the behavior of DoTs and certainly not enough to persuade me that DoTs are more useful than DDs.
