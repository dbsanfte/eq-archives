Blargx I have a 61 Enchanter who is a pretty substantial TS'er.

Here's my estimates on certain skills with my level in said skill in parentheses:

1) Fishing (200) - generally cheap as all get out to 200.(The max) 500 pp at MOST. CAN be maxxed using training points. Other than that - is boring, and fishing poles can break at random. Faern still has his very first pole from the days of sitting on Halas docks dredging up sandals etc. My Monk OTOH breaks a pole 1/10 casts. Favorite spot? CS - love the music and the otters running around. Can go from 1-200 in one sitting if you have enough bait and poles. (and patience)

2) Smithing (167) - Repetitious to the extreme. Must make a lot of sub components for combines. Depending on your race / religion you can make serious BANK. Realize that once you sell a top of the line item to a person - you're not going to see them for awhile. Smithing is like gambling - you may be up for a time as you sell items to a vendor - but in the end - you're going home with empty pockets. Think it has cost me 8K to this point. I estimate 10K per 10 points until 250. You will run around more than a newbie drood with first time aggro. Supplies are all OVER the place and often not conveniently next to a forge.

3) Baking (191) - Cheap and easy to get to this point. Figure 1k. To get to 227 you can keep doing Halas 10 pound pies. A LOT of clicking. Fish, Bat Wings to 135; and then Halas Pies. Mammoth Meats (The limiting factor in Halas 10 pound pies) are your friend. I estimate 2K to get to 227. Not sure after that. Not that much running around.

4) Tailoring (162) - is the DEVIL. You are going to be SICK of silks, swatches, heady Kiola's etc. Once you get past Wu's - it gets REALLY expensive. Figure 6K through WU's then 100K + to 200. Hopper pelts and Acrylia can be farmed - BORING - or bought - EXPENSIVE. Not a lot of going from zone to zone to buy supplies - but to farm supplies.

5) Brewing (189) - is click central. To attempt ONE combine at this level I need to click the mouse about 8 times. Cheap yes. Do the Cyclops skull to 151 or so. A Side bennie is that Halas is a great place to work this skill. Think it has taken me 3-4K to get here. Pain in the butt moving from zone to zone.

6) Pottery (188) - cheap, cheap to this level. Maybe 2-3K. PAST this point is apparently plat city. Vendor farm zombie skins and pelts for the poison vials. Or go to unrest and buy them. Bazaar is rip-off central. Thurgadin is not too bad if you have supplies - otherwise, you will be running about.

7) Fletching (165) - Figure 3K to 200. Can make boatloads of arrows (Bows are a suckers bet) and the limiting factor to 200 is your wrist. PAST 200 it gets prohibitively expensive and unless you have a Ranger - I'm not sure why a Shaman would go this route. There are no Tribunal Bows etc. Can go from 1-200 in one sitting in front of one vendor.

I have an advantage with an Enchanter as illusion and Collaboration allow me to enter ANY city pre-luclin. Heck, I've done enough faction work I can walk around a lot of em in Tier'dal form. A Shaman will NOT have this option unless they have some sort of mask (ala Juujuu and his halfling mask) or pre-nerfed mask of deception. I can also buff my Charisma to the magical 105 level.

I hope this helps.

EDIT: Forgot to add. Some of these skills require items from other TS's. (ie Tailoring requiring heady Kiola's)

Also - you need a way to generate plat in droves. TS's will drain your account unless you are moneybags. (My Enchanter sells KEI - call me a pimp - but it pays the bills).

Sell Focus, sell your ability to Malo, offer to solo that Dragon in WW and sell tickets - I dunno - but you need to make money cuz TS's COST money.

Jewelcraft (250) - Ok, can be done in one (LONG A55) sitting. For you as a non-bar-enchanting Shaman expect what Henora paid 8K. If you can Enchant the bars I did it for 1.4k with my Enchanter. EDIT # 3 - BUY A GEERLOCK, BUY A GEERLOK, BUY A GEERLOK. They pay for themselves VERY rapidly.

I did make alot of money doing smithing but I was lucky to be into it at the right time with the right resources.

As far as cost of tradeskills to master it's really hard to price mosts. Price depends on too many factors and often times depends on the situation. What recipe you choose, your luck on combines, your stats (effect skill up rate) are all going to end up effecting your cost. Also your economy is going to effect most trade skill. Either should sell for at least enough to cover costs.

Jc is easy. Everything vendor bought. If your an enchanter and actually spend the time to enchanter all your metals you can do it very cheaply. Fletching is pretty cheap and easy upto 202. Making arrows is pretty easy and sell back is good. Past 202 it gets time consuming or expensive or both. Either making bows or acrylia arrows.

Pottery is like fletching. Pretty dirt cheap and easy upto 199? or something like that on vials and casarole dishes. Past that your stuck making idols, steins or something. Requiring money, or time or both. The star ruby steins used to be good for skill up and you could make a profit selling them for the Solstice earring quest. Now a days most servers seem to have too many people have tried that and you can't even beak even anymore on them.

Smithing is a pain in the rear and time consuming and expensive. Personally I wouldn't touch high end smithing in most situations. Too much work to just compeate with all the established smiths.

Tayloring is about as bad or worse than smithing depending on who you talk to. Expensive and timeconsuming to GM.

Smithing and tayloring do have a shortcut if your lucky enough to be able to pick up a 15% skill mod item. Other wise your looking at a long and painful road.

For those dedicated enough or crazy enough depending on who you ask to spend 18 aa points and all the time to GM all the skills there is an earring quest in PoK. I am a GM Brewer and can say that as far as the various trade skills that its is by far the less painful option; you can sit in Halas in one store and literally go from 120 skill to 248 skill in one sitting if you so choose.....granted you will have carpal tunnel and be drooling from the mind numbing tedium and boredom, but hey, its better than UO :)
Smithing and Tailoring are god aweful hard to raise now...thankfully there are plentiful supplies in Bazaar on most servers, just you have to have deep pockets to abuse it well...tailoring for the right race is a instant money maker....halflings and their weight reducing bags have almost made me play one of the hairy footed freaks just to work on that skill....so far though I cant bring myself to do it though....but the 100% wt reduction bags are a insta 5k on Karana with people often auctioning WTB in the trade channel, so it might be worth the effort.
I personally love brewing, I cook up Kaladim Constitutionals for myself and my alts, Grobb Liquid Meat for some RL friends of mine that prefer it (/shrug one less stamina and +5 to all saves seems to be better to me, but what do I know :D) and occasionally make Demi Sec Champagne for people on request....I am also working on changing my factions around so I can whore out coldain ring war combines (I have seen this work very well as a money maker and is the only reason that I can see cuddling up to the darn shorties).
It basically boils down to what your interested in and what you can tolerate as far as cash layouts and time sinks...you can go solo acriyla drops and hide drops so tailoring or smithing is a viable option, but it takes from exp grinding and grouping. Brewing, pottery and Jewelcraft are pretty common, but work well and are almost all vendor bought (I know pottery is a pain for hides to make poison vials though).
