Chutoi
I posted a response to a similar question in the alchemy section. Someone asked which tradeskill in which to specialize. My response was smithing or pottery.

Alchemy can't be specialized, tailoring is an utter nightmare since materials for every single combine must be gathered (I'm at 155 and I have little interest in going high), brewing and cooking don't have much to offer as yet, jewelcraft will always be dominated by enchanters since only they can enchant the metal, and fletching is much the same with rangers (not to mention a small market).

Tailoring is a good skill, and it's rare to find someone with a really high tailoring skill, but it does take a mashochist to get tailoring past 158.

So, I would say smithing or pottery since both of those skills just got major upgrades and have some VERY nice items now. The bad news is that you are very limited on time. Right now (6/3/02) you can get up to 168 with banded fairly cheaply. Fine plate will take you up to 205 or 225 (don't remember). Unfortunately, I believe the patch on (6/5/02) is rumored to have some smithing nerfs. I believe the trivials are about to be lowered substantially on banded and fine plate. That will make it a lot harder to get the skills up. I hope the rumor is wrong, but I don't think it is.

So, yes, there is a market for high end pottery and high end smithing-but we are talking in the 250 skill range, and items very difficult to make. Some of the spiritstones are going for 15kpp a pop. I'm sure that the new crafted armors with 50+ac may do the same.
