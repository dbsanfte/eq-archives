Kianor
I wouldn't suggest having a shaman in a complete heal chain at all, to be honest. On any mob you're likely to need a CH chain with, and on any tank likely to be tanking such mobs, our 1950 heal is probably not worth the effort unless you're very very short of healers.

If you're main slower too, you may well need a healer of your own anyway, slowing and being critical to healing in the same fight isn't a good way to go. Once the mob is debuffed, you can help out with patch healing.

Having said all that, though I've never been in a chain myself, I should think it is possible if you're that short of clerics. You'll probably want the person after you to cast sooner than the rest of the chain (for example, if you have a 4 second chain, with a shaman the next link might need to be only 2 seconds later).

If you do get involved in a chain, I suggest you talk to the clerics and druids involved at the time and ask their advice, since the actual method can vary a bit.

I won't suggest how to set up your hotkeys, etc, since I've only seen chains in action. For more information, I'd suggest you go read the cleric boards, there are probably a number of posts there about how to.

Hi, I'm a Ogre Prophet on Kane.

I have som experience in being in a CH chain (Even set up chain order 2 times)

Sure shamans aren't the best in CH chains, but sometimes you lack healers and end up with 2 options. Either use a shaman/shamans in CH chain or not kill the target. There are some typical targets which this might happen, Derakor the Vindicator being a good example since it's imune to slow. I have set a chainorder for Vindi once. We had 3 shamans and 1 cleric. Many doubted we could kill Vindi with only this for a chain. But we tried anyway and succeded. We used 4sec pause for cleric and 2sec for shamans. When you have someone not used to being in CH chain you have to have the pause included in the hotkey so it makes it easier. Proffessional chainers don't need the pause in the hotkey, they just use a timer in their mind to time the CH perfectly.
All the info they need is the chain-order, tank(tank-order) and the chain timer and they will know what to do.

Let me give an example of a hotkey I use:
/cast 5
/pause 2,/auction MircoCH ===== %t =====
/cast 5
/pause 18
/auction Next CH = NOW!!!

I will now try to explain the lines of the hotkey for those that don't understand it clearly allready :P

/cast 5
- Got Kraggs mending in spellslot 5 so casting that first.

/pause 2,/auction MircoCH ===== %t =====
- This pause is used in case of a fizzle(notice the second line of /cast 5 later). This pause will happen AFTER the /auction message, NOT before. The pause is very short but neccessary for a fizzle-proof hotkey :)
- The channel the CH message is in can be anything, but note that the chat channels you make yourself is more unreliable due to lag than the real channel like /auction used in this example.

/cast 5
- second is in case of fizzle and will normally not do anything, unless you fizzle first one that is :P

/pause 18
- The pause between CHs. Generally /pause 10 = 1 second, so if shamans are to use 2 sec you use /pause 20. (I use /pause 18 due to the fizzle proof hotkey using /pause 2)

/auction Next CH = NOW!!!
- This can include who the next one in chain is, but everyone should know the chain order anyway so that's redundant. And in case someone must be skipped it can be confusing when the wrong names show up next.

Hope this answer your questions.

When a mob rampages, it means it attacks one person other than the main tank. It is usually the second person to have gained aggro (unlucky if it is a healer, or debuffer)

A rampage tank, is a tank who purposely attacks second, to prevent a cleric, for example, from getting hit when rampage goes off. It is a random occurance, some mobs like Rumblecrush in Umbral Plains do it alot, and some mobs like enforcers in PoJ only do it a little.

Geal Icehammer
Some high level mobs get a special attack called rampage. Basically it an extra set of attacks. They have a special text message when this happens. These mobs have a second agro list that does not change ( no ammount of taunting or spell casting will make you go up the list or down ). So the list is in the order that you get agro. Who ever is first on the ramp list but not on the person tanking the mob will be subject to rampage attacks if they are in melee range.

When the mob in question hits for over 1000 a swing, whoever is taking the rampages will also need a healer. Ideally some warrior or knight is the rampage target, the ramp tank. Shaman aren't bad ramp tanks because we can heal ourselves and tend to have more hps, defensive aa's, etc. than other casters.
