To upgrade pet abilities you have to tame a new pet. Here is a pretty good list of pets and their abilities:

http://www.goodintentionsguild.info/hunters.html

You basically have to put your regular pet in the stable and go out and tame a new one and keep it until you "learn" its ability. Then you can let it go and teach the new ability to your regular pet. Not all pets can learn all abilities. I know bears can learn them all.

After further review, I am liking my resisty wolf. He can tank caster adds almost indefinitely in instances. If they are several levels higher than him, the spells start to hurt him, but he still eats fireballs way better than I do (big fat donut in all resists). I miss the near instant taunt of the high attack speed cats, but over the course of the fight, pets without claw seem to hold aggro better because they have enough focus to growl when it pops.

Claw = extra dps, better initial taunt, sucks focus dry so maintaining taunt may be more difficult

Bite only = less dps, slower initial taunt, holds aggro better after taunt is achieved.

Before I went with a no-Claw pet, I was considering making a macro to tie Arcane Shot and Claw together, to sort of create an artificial recast delay on claw, which would conserve focus.
