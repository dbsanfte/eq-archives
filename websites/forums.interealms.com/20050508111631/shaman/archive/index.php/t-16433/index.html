Bits of LDoN Dungeon Wisdom...

1) Main Tank should be with in 2 levels of the highest character if not the highest.

2) GET a map. It cuts down the time you spend trying to decide to go right or left. (Sometimes it don’t matter, other times it VERY much matters)

3) Only pull the mobs far enough that they will not cause adds when they try to run. (You should be moving every pull or every other pull, unless clearing a Room)

4) Don’t be afraid to call for evac or to RUN. Turn around time to get back on track is FAR less then with deaths.

5) Mob Difficulty is based off of Group Average Level. So the larger the spread between levels the more likely your going have troubles.

6) KILL those blasted Healers. Nothing is worse then having the Melee mob that hits like a freight Train get CHed in stead of the mob CHing himself.

7) If you get two healers separate them by pulling one back the way you came in and rooting him. If they are far enough apart they can’t assist each other with heals. This works even better when there is no mezz casting player with you.

8) Slow and steady progress is far better then burst progress. You mana pools tend to find a nice equilibrium. Just because you have time to cast more DD don’t mean you should. If you’re killing a mob a min on most collection or slaughter missions you will complete it in plenty of time. Rushing will cause you to wipe and fail an adventure just as fast as going too slow.

9) Have the Puller set up a hot key "%T incoming Move to me no Farther." This gets everyone in the habit of moving. Lets the Puller set the spot to kill at.

10) Be ready to turn around and head back to the last intersection when you reach a dead end. Waiting on stragglers wastes time. By saying CLEAR or ROOM CLEAR. You let people know what is going on and that they need to go back the other way instead of continuing on the way they are headed.

11) On collection & Slaughter Missions clear all routes so you do not have to back track. Unplanned back tracking wastes the most time of all. (Map help 100% here) Clear the tunnels in a systematic manner if you don’t have a map it makes keeping track far easier.
