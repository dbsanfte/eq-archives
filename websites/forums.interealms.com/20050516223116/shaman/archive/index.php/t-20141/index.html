Disclaimer: The following is a separate issue from class balancing, though the two may overlap at times. Please pretend all classes are balanced when reading this.

A lot of people have complained about the need for warriors and clerics in the GoD single group trials. I believe this is largely because of the zone and encounter design in these trials, namely MOBs with huge melee DPS and medium to low HPs.
Huge melee DPS favors warriors and clerics, and the short fight duration means some other classes' contribution, for example shaman slowing is diminished.

So far the pattern in GoD is the same - next tier, up melee DPS on the MOBs. There is no changes apart from that at all on the trash and few changes on the nameds, usually in the form of slow mitigation.

What does that mean?
It means there might as well be a single type of MOB in GoD, with minor changes to HPs and varying melee damage, depending on the tier. This single MOB type favors clerics and warriors.

What can be done?
Not a whole lot at this point I would think, unless SOE actually wants to spend a lot of time going back through the GoD zones and redesign them (I can hope though).

What could have been done? What can be done for future zones/expansions?
What would have been great is a more variable MOB design. For example, alongside the current low HPs, high melee DPS MOBs there could be numerous smaller, lesser hitting swarm type MOBs. There could be fewer but very high HPs MOBs with somewhat lesser DPS capabilities. There could be MOBs that do a lot of their damage via procs or AEs. There could be casters casting NPC only damage spells that hit for, say 6K.

What would that accomplish?
The current MOB type favors clerics and warriors. High HP MOBs favor the DoT classes. Swarm MOBs favor the mez classes and classes with group heals. MOBs with big procs favor the knights (superior warrior mitigation does not kick in and both classes can self heal) and Wizards(Spellshield). MOBs with AEs favor casters and classes with group heals.

A scenario
Imagine the Tipt trial. Now, imagine as you zone in you had 2 possible paths. One with the current MOB type, one with high HP MOBs (but fewer of them). A group with a cleric and warrior will take left, a group with a Shadowknight, Necromanc,er Druid and Shaman will take the right.
A while later the paths merge and split again. The left side has the Cragbeast as is, the other has a different boss, say a single MOB with an AE slow. A group with solid tanking and healing takes the left, a group that does not rely on melee DPS takes the right.
Each group kills a boss, the paths meet again. The left side now has swarm type MOBs, the right side has MOBs that proc huge DDs. A group with a lot of tanks or pets or lots of group healing takes the left, a group with a Knight and a rune class takes the right.

Random or fixed (I think I would prefer fixed so you do not run into an all swarm type trial or the like), this would give SOE the opportunity to design challenging trials while not relying on the same 'new tier, up melee DPS by 30%' formua they currently seem to be using. It would allow the DoT classes to actually use their DoTs. It would allow the 'light tank' classes to actually get to offtank. It would allow mages to offtank again. It would allow the clerics to use stuns, group heals and CH, depending on the current MOB type. It would allow Knights to act as tanks while still challenging warriors. It would even allow classes to live with minor to medium class balance issues without immediatly feeling underpowered.

You could even reuse the same MOB types in raid encounters and people would recognize them from their single group trials. (For example, Pookahs could be the swarms, golems the high HP MOBs, etc.)

All in all I think I would have enjoyed the expansion a lot more if the trials had been designed that way and people would not wait for hours to find a cleric or three druids so they can go and flag someone.

Furious is commonly used as a prequel to defensive since it allows the warrior to build a huge hate platform without being damaged. In the old days when discs were linked you couldn't do this, but after the melee balancing, discs were separated into 4 timer groups which can be used independently. Basically the 4 groups are (1) defensive discs (2) offensive discs; (3) invul discs; and (4) utility discs. (4) is rarely used but (1) and (3) are basic tools for modern warriors. Generally in the course of an encounter you lead off with furious (our riposte disc) to build agro and to allow time for debuffs and the chain to set on you. The moment that fades you hit your defensive disc. Usually that would be defensive, but sometimes evasive or stonewall might be used. During all of this a tank would likely pop off a few incites, so at that point their endurance pool would be mostly drained. But, if your end pool is large enough, you can hit your offensive disc as soon as the defensive one falls. If your warriors aren't doing this, they need to take some basic tanking lessons ;) [I suspect they are, they just aren't telling you about it :D ]
