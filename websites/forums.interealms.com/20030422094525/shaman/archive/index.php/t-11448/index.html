Shokar
I attended a seminar once that had this message:

"Figure out what your time is worth, and don't do ANYTHING that does not pay you your hourly worth"

What this means is this: If you are a IT Profession, and you make, say $100.00 an hour, and your company sends you off to fix someone's computer. A Techie could have fixed that computer for say, $50.00 an hour. So, the company just blew $50.00 an hour on you.

If you are a physical therapist making $100.00 an hour, would you do a job for $50.00, thus losing $50.00 an hour?

Let's take this into the EQ world. I read another thread where someone stated they can make 5K a week selling bargain basement sow and shrink potions. So I got to thinking.....

Okay, maybe after selling 5K worth of potions, they start to recoup their developmental costs in building potions up to trivial levels. Then after that, they are making a very small profit.

But is it WORTH their time to do so? So I priced out my time. At level 57 with my Paladin, I can make 250 plat an hour, easily. If I want experience too, and can get a camp in Ice Giants, I can make, say 175 plat an hour? Or I can do OOT Seafluffies and make maybe 150 an hour. Or I can group in UP and get one Blue Exp and 100 plat in say, three hours. (with a chance at a sellable drop, or upgrade to my equipment.)

So, that means to me that my time is roughly worth 200 plat an hour, and that one blue of exp is equal to 750 -1000 plat for me.

So, with our friend that is MAKING 5K a week. How much time does it take to make potions that net him a profit of 5K? It is my guess that if he were to use my above examples, then he is LOSING money and experience by selling bargain basement potions.

If the amount of time it takes to make 5K profits in potions were used on experience hunting or farming hunting, his profits would 10-15K. SO, by him opening a bargain basement shop, and doing one of the most TEDIOUS and BORING aspects of EVERQUEST, he is LOSING 5-10K a week.

That is a colossal loss of time and experience, and a colossal waste, because he could cut his potion building time in HALF by pricing his wares at a realistic market value. SO, if he would spend half the time making his potions, and sell them at correct and reasonable prices, he could double his earnings and experience and reduce boredom by 50%.

It just makes good business and gaming sense.

Mafdet de Leone
You are totally screwing the young shaman who have barely trivialed them, and cannot make even enough money to continue alchemy because of failures!

I wonder how you think other shamans got to where they are now?
I started my alchemy because it was part of my character as a shaman. I hunted in the different zones, and saved up my plat, and made potions as I had the money. I never expected to make any HUGE amounts of profit, but enough to keep me happy.

Some sold. Some didn't. This includes SoWs. I sold SoWs to my friend at cost just to recuperate the cost of making them.

But since the bazaar has opened, people EXPECT to make pp just by tradeskilling. Before the Bazaar opened, you had several choices:

1) Carry 10-dose SoW potions (or whatever potions) in your backback on the off-chance someone wants to buy them

2) Spend several hours in the market channel hawking your wares.

3) (In the case of Innoruuk server), Spend severa hours in the EC tunnel haggling with people to buy your stuff.

Prior to bazaar, we BARELY had a market for our stuff. Sure, we could charge that 100 pp we wanted, but it wasn't making us hand-over-fist plat.

Now, the bazaar opens, and the people begin to see the pp possibilities as "endless" (yeah. right. LOL) But however, the Bazaar introduces a new concept: the buyer's market. NOT a seller's market like the EC tunnel of Innoruuk or selling them out in the zones.

In a buyer's market -- Someone will ALWAYS undersell you to make a profit. Some will be happy with a lower cost-to-profit ratio than others. This is the BASIS of the Free Market Economy.

How many times in RL do you wait for that "Sale Sign" to go up, telling you "50 % off!! Lowest Price. We'll beat our competitors prices!!!"
Guess what? People in EQ will also wait for that little bit lower price, or go buy from someone else who is selling for cheaper.

You'll never get the whole of EQ to FIX prices. It has never worked. It will never work, because you'll never get the entirety of the community to agree to it.

Do what other shamans in the past have done before. Get your skill up, save your pp, and when you're in a place where you are happy with your cost-to-profit ratio, start selling the bazaar.

In real life, you never hear the jr. engineer bitch because s/he has to learn all this stuff while on the job and not making enough $$, while the more established engineer is making a ton of $$ because they are more experienced. You still have to do the grunt work in order to reap the rewards.

Now, I'm happy that I can just sell stuff now each night, and not have to worry about carrying several 10-dose SoWs in my backpack just on the "off-chance" someone will buy them from me while running around in the zones. Nor will I have to spend hours in the EC market tunnel trying to sell goods.

Now, I can freely XP, make potions, and sell them off. It only takes me a couple hours a week to make potions, then I swap out backpacks for trader satchels, and I'm good to go. Even with the lowered SoW potion prices, I still make a decent living through the bazaar. I sell off not only potions, but drops I've gotten, and the other tradeskilling items I make.
