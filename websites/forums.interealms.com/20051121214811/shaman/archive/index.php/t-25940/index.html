What's your playstyle? Are you PvP or PvE? (my sincere applogies if you mentioned this before, just trying to get my caffiene in right now).

PvE players will push stats like int and spi, because honestly, a smart caster can really outshine a blunt instrument caster with these. In PvP, it's all about lasting and surviving ridiculous attacks out of no where, be it from a group, or a stray rogue. Survival means Stam, AC and DPS. AC and Stam help you survive the initial attack and solid DPS helps you shock and awe your opponent so they turn and run. Once an opponent tries to run, you win. Survivability is good, hard to argue the whole "you can't cast if you're dead" thing and I totally agree with it. Being a horde player as well, a shaman can be a viable off tank or even a priest's bodyguard for most of his career. Making yourself more durable adds functionality to often warrior starved horde parties.

Let me give you my personal exp on sets ... the bonuses are nice, but they suck. I have two pieces of scarlet armour and I get a whopping +10 AC ... yeah ... underwhelming. I currently have 3089AC at lvl 40 and I have to say that 10 AC makes no difference whatsoever. One more piece and I get a +2 to defense and while it's nice to have, I mean every little bit helps, I wouldn't re-mortgage the house or sell off my RRSPs to get the bonuses. Most equipment in WoW seems to get replaced every 4-6 levels. Anytime you see a bonus to resisting shadow magic ... don't laugh, just ignore it. In short, most set bonuses really are not worth it.
