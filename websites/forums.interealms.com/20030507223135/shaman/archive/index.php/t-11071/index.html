Weyalan
All shaman DoTs stack with all other shaman DoTs, so you can stack as many different poison and disease spell combinations as you like.

You can stack any posion DoT with any other posion DoT and any disease DoT with any other Disease DoT. You cannot stack any DoT with itself - i.e. casting "sicken" twice on the same mob will not double the damage, because the second casting will overwrite the first casting.

I have heard that Verant have changed casting rules so that if 2 shaman cast the same DoT on a mob, bith DoTs will stick and do thusly do double damage, but I have not verified this for myself.

Typically though, you would stack your best poison DoT and your best disease DoT only. Obviously the disease DoT is slower acting so you would only need to re-apply the poison DoT.

In actual fact, if I recall correctly, the shaman DoTs at level 14 are rather weak, and are, in fact, of only marginal benefit (they are however, much more efficient than our puny DD spells!). Nevertheless, you should teach yourself how to "root n rot" efficiently, because as your DoTs become more powerful, it does become a more viable tactic at higher levels. It is better to learn the basics of your tactics against mobs that are relatively weak - learning new tactics at higher levels can be a painful learning curve!

N.B. As a shaman, it is important that you do not become too dependent on "root n rot" (you are not a druid!). It is a good efficient tactic, but if used exclusively, it will be to the detriment of your other abilities. You need to keep your weapon skills maximised, and also your affense and defense. Defense is the most vital ability you have, so keep it maxed at every level - The reason for this will become increasingly apparent as you gain levels
Xanden
Don't bother rooting and rotting at your level. The root will drain your mana, and for very little effect.

At your level you should just melee and dot. Save that root mana for heals, because even meditating, you won't get your root mana back before it breaks most of the time. When you get slow and haste, throw those on as well.

Eventually you will have a large enough mana pool that root will cost about 1% of your mana and you will be able to cast it a lot more often. However, as a shaman, I've never root-rotted unless I was in a very odd one-time situation. Our dots just break root too often to make it a good tactic, and eventually you will get a pet and a good slow, and then you'll only root to park mobs.
