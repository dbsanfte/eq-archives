First and foremost, I think you'll find that equipment upgrades won't change the game much at level 10 (at least not those upgrades you'll be able to afford at level 10.) :) Banded and a nice weapon is more than good enough- you're in very good shape. If anything, I'd try to grab a piercer so you can keep both your blunt and piercing weapon skills up. It gets harder to raise them the higher you go- so you'll kick yourself later if you don't max them out now. Same with your Defense skill- make absolutely sure it's maxed at every level, because Defense = Free AC.

This thread (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?s=&threadid=500) asks and answers some "new shaman" questions- might be worth a look.

And don't forget to put a training point into Channeling- I'm embarassed to say I just forgot until the mid 20's- ack.

Good hunting!

You aren't going to find a lot of super great upgrades for about 200P, but let's look at a shopping list that you might want to try and find:

Blackened iron girdle. 5ac 5sta if I remember. Good belt. Gives AC and HP. I think blackened iron is only a few AC more than banded per piece, so expect to pay just a little more than that for it. If banded armor still sells for roughly 1P per AC of the piece, expect about 2-3P per ac on banded?

Shiny Brass Shield is 10ac 10mr? Can probably get one for about 30P

The coldain shields that are all over thurgadin you can probably get for almost nothing. They are lore and super super common. 15ac and 10 cold. Don't act like a beggar asking if someone can kill one for you to loot it, but offer some PP for someone to get you one. They'll probably just give it to you if you don't act like a beggar. :)

Legs will be hard to upgrade for a while. You might be able to pick up a set of gatorscale legs for 200P or so if you are lucky.

Bloodstained tunic is about 50P I think. It's got decent AC and wis and HP? on it. Comes from unrest.

Bloodstained mantle is from unrest too. 6ac 10mr is decent for about 30P or so.

For your bracer, if you want to go the +wis route, try to get some polished bone bracers. they are 2ac 3wis 3int i think, and now have a focus effect. They used to sell for about 100P back in the day, but not sure now. I'd guess about 70P nowadays.

Earrings won't be easy to upgrade for a long time, unless you are a barb. Get those imbued ivory ones mentioned above. Either that, or save up for some 2ac 35hp 25mana gold black sapphire earings. That'll probably cost you about 200P each though.

Rings will be a bit expensive, but very useful if you get an enchanter to make you some gold fire opal rings. I can't remember the name, but they are the 4ac 45hp rings. If you can find an enchanter that will just combine the stuff for you, the fire opal will cost you 50P to get from temple of sol ro, or maybe cheaper if you buy it from a player that just came back from a dungeon where they drop. A gold bar costs about 1p? Definately less than 10p though.

As for arms, try to find hollowed bone bracers. they are 6ac 7mr and some +mana. I think they have a focus effect on the now too. I'd expect to pay about 100-200P for them though, maybe more with the effect. They were rather rare from najena, and I barely ever have seen them for sale, and I've only gotten 2 of them in many many hours of romping around najena.

Splitpaw gloves have like 4ac and 2 or 3 wis on them. I'd personally just stick with banded for the AC, but it's an option if you need some more mana. I'd guess about 25P for them.

For your mask, you can try to get a splitpaw hide mask. they are 3ac 5mr 5pr i think. They'd be cheap too at about 25P i'd guess.

For your necklace, try to get a chrysoberyl talisman from lower guk. It's 5ac 25mana 3wis i think. I used to sell them for 100P all the time, but would even give them away often considering they were lore items, when I was camping a named spawn next to the mob that dropped them.

There's lots of piercing weapons out there that you should try to get. The one that comes from lake of ill omen is the most common one, as his spawn time is only 2 hours for the mob that drops it. It's roughly a 11/35 piercer with wis and sta on it.

OH. Try to get to cazic thule and see if you can buy one of the weapons that drops off the mobs there. They are the common weapons that drop off the mobs there. Their ratios are about 1:3.5 (like a 7/22 or 8/26) with +1 damage vs lizardmen and 2pr or dr or something. You can probably get one for free if you offer someone 15 or 20P for one of them (roughly how much a vendor buys them for).

Your range slot won't have anything to put in it for a long time I'm afraid, unless you can find a potter who can make something for you. I don't know the costs nor difficulties involved in making something like that though.

There's also a bunch of items that drop in the Ferrott now. They used to drop in cazic thule before the upgrade. There's some shoulder that has like 5ac 3wis i think. I think there's also a mask from there.

For your helmet, try to get a blackened iron crown. they will be a bit more costly due to their stats, but it's a good choice. It's 5ac, 5sta and/or 5str and 5 to all resists. I think they used to sell for about 100P, but try for something like 50P or less if you can manage that.

There's probably a lot of items from Luclin that I haven't mentioned here. Basicly, at your level, here's the importance of stats for the items you want: HP > AC > mana > wis > resists > sta > agl > str > dex. When you get higher in level (like 30s-40s) wis will become more important, but as it is you barely get any mana from increases wisdom. Stamina isn't very important at lower level for the same reason, that you bare get any HPs from it. Just make sure your stamina is over 100. If it isn't, then when you run out of Yellow_Bar (people also call this "stamina") then some of your physical stats drop by 10 until you get stamina back. Also make sure your agility is over 75. If it isn't, you get a LARGE AC penalty. If your stamina is under 100, then make sure your agility is over 85, as you be working at that small penalty.

Here's some good rules of thumb when deciding on equipment upgrades (they are kind of obvious but sometimes need to be said):
1. If you are dying too often to mobs you are fighting, you don't have enough HP and AC, thus pick your equip accordingly.
2. If you don't have enough mana to kill the mobs you are fighting, or run out of mana in combat healing group members, you don't have enough mana and wis, thus pick your equipment accordingly.
3. If you are taking too much damage from casters, you want equipment with more resists, thus pick your equipment accordingly.
You can keep this in mind from level 10 - 60.
