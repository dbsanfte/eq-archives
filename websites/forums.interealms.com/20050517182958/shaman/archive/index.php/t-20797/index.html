LaughingWoman
Can't recommend a beastlord highly enough. Especially a troll.... You missed luclin, so here's the sum up. Beastlord is what a lot of us thought we were getting when we rolled up a shaman. Great buffs, good soloing, worthwhile melee. Get an embalmer's skinning nife for free bandages forever, and go to town. Beastlord is everything you loved about the shaman, but with more soloing power. Slow is a little worse, but that's okay, you have the pre-nerf monk avoidance tables and a really amazing (90%) damage avoidance discipline at 51. Quick heal isn't that great, but you get the best pet heal in the game. Atk buffs and mana/hp regen buffs mean you'll be a benefit to any group, even before you add your significant damage potential.

It's a very very self-sufficient class, and can solo better than any non-kiting class.
Sudedor
I second that.

Frankly, many of the expansions are so inexpensive I'd probably just get them all.

But, to me, PoP is a *must* have. It greatly improves travel.

LDoN is also quite important, you can buy a lot of neat stuff with LDoN points, to a Shaman it means a new line of fast acting DoT's . . . a Curse line.

LoY has some very useful things . . . primary among them is the Shaman Stoicism quest. It's a mini-Torpor which at the level you get it is EXTREMELY useful.

Luclin gives you the ability to enter the Bazaar to buy and sell items and utilize Paludal Caverns for leveling your character very quickly. There are a number of spells that the vendors in Shadowhaven are the only source for.

Kunark and Velious each have some unique spells, though these spells are often available in the Bazaar on Luclin.

I do NOT recommend purchase of the GoD expansion UNLESS you plan to be a group leader. If you plan to be a group leader, the leadership abilities in GoD are wonderful.
