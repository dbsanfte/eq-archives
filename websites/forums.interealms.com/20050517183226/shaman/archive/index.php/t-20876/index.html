I used my lvl 60 ranger to PL a gnome rogue to lvl 20 last week (got tired of finding tinkers to make stuff for me, so I'm just gonna make my own tinker, and gnome has to be 16 before you can raise tinker level).

Started in Qeynos newb yard: DS the rogue, and get a cleric to throw Temperance on him. Then have him run around the yard tapping everything he sees that is white or yellow to him. Once you have about 10, drag them all back near the castle. Proceed to let the mobs beat themselves to death on the DS. At lvl 5, move out into Qeynos Hills. Same tactic, pulling red bats, wolves, etc. Lvl 8 in about 15 minutes.

At lvl 8, moved into Blackburrow. Pulled half the zone at a time, basically. Only trouble there was that the gnolls tend to run when they get hurt too bad, so ranger had to snare some of them. Another 20 minutes work.

At lvl 12, moved to Paludal Caverns. Up past the loop a bit, pulled 4-6 mushrooms or bugs at a time, all red. Spent probably 75-90 minutes there, rogue level 20. Could have done 4-5 more levels there, if I had wanted to move down and do hoppers, but I didn't need him any higher anyway.

PLing is easy. Just use the zones with the highest ZEM, throw the biggest DS on you can, and have a healer stand nearby.

(This guide assumes you have HUGE amounts of Plat and thr resources to handle 3 toons at once, and can afford the best twink gear plat can buy.)

Step 1. Get your hands on a 65 Shaman and a 65 Druid.

Step 2. Make a melee toon.

Step 3. Buff Level 1 toon (Dru/Shm HP Buffs, Chroloplast, Alacrity, SoW) and put the highest possible Damage Shield on that toon. Then run around the Newbie zone killing everything you see at lightning speed. Do not slow any of these mobs as Regen and DS will kill them faster and keep you alive.

Step 4. (20 minutes later) You should be level 5-6. Port to West Commonlands and go to Befallen. Appropriately Buff said toon like you did before... Have the Druid train EVERYTHING to the zoneline with AE Snare. Zone Druid out and let the Mayhem ensue as the level 6 Supertwink aggros all the mobs and tags them for 1-2 hits each as they punch themselves to death on your Damage shields... Have Shaman heal as Necessary as Druid keeps DS refreshed.

Step 4. Paludal Caverns. Do what you did in Befallen until about level 20

Step 5. Dawnshroud Peaks - Same thing at the Mushroom infested Ruins until 26ish. Then move to Higher level wolves and Zelniaks.... Proceed to level 30.

Step 6. Kaesora... Repeat the Befallen Process. Until about level 45.
(you will be buffing your twink with all the beneficial HP, Haste, Regen and ATK you can, as well as Druid DS, around level 40 it also becomes beneficial to begin stacking Shaman DS potions with Druid DS. - Expensive, but effective)

Step 7. Dawnshroud again... Druid Trains Rockhoppers onto Twink. Shaman slows them ( NOTE, Until this time, you have not used slowing AT ALL! This is when mob DPS/HP outweighs the benefits of Regen/DS tanking.) This is good till about level 50

Step 8. Old Sebilis, Disco Camp... Lotsa LOW HP Mobs. Train the twink like mad... AE Slow with Shaman, and keep DS's up.

Step 9. Around 58, you want to move to Planar zones (Justice is good)

Aternative - If you are in a Planar/Elemental Guild.... Get the Twink Flagged for Elemental Access (Plane of Fire)... Begin PLing Twink there with Shaman/Druid after 60.

The levelling after 47 or so will slow right down... but 47 should take about 30-40 hrs played. Also, if you wanna really amp up the levelling.. Add a Cleric and a pulling class to the mix. Add Cleric HP and AC buffs... don't slow a thing.

Druids + twink = fast levelling to 50.
I have a bard I've levelled up relatively fast.
Druids can give you their level 44 or 49 hp buff whatever level it is, and their 49 dshield.
If the druid has the AA heal over time with dshield component, that's an even bigger benefit.
If the druid has the AA that gives you a 600 point 1 shot dshield, that helps too.

Level up to level 10 or so in whatever newbie dungeon like crushbone or blackburrow or whatever.
Then, go to Paludal Caverns, and have the druid pull AE trains of mobs for your twink to kill.
At level 25, a single pull of about 15-20 mobs would give 1/2 a level roughly, which takes about 15 minutes to pull, kill, and med back up. (can't remember specifics. it's been a while)
At level 20-25 range go to sola and pull ae trains there too. You level once every 20-30 minutes.
At level 30, head to lower guk, and do ae trains to the bedroom area.
My bard could handle trains of about 20 mobs at a time.
If the AA heal over time dshield was up on druid, I could just barely handle 30 mobs.
You can pull an AE train of about 20-30 mobs every 8-10 minutes with kill time and medding.
At level 40, head down to the safe hall, and do AE trains of everything from the BR loop down to the Frenzy area and up to where the dars start spawning.
You get a level roughly every 45 mins doing this.
At about level 45, you can start ae pulling bloodthirsties and dars since the wans will be going light blue.

Hit level 10 in about 1 hour.
Hit level 20 in about 1.5 more hours.
Hit level 30 in about 3 more hours.
Hit level 50 in about 5-8 more hours (depending on stupid deaths).
Ding, level 50 in about 8-12 hours time.

From there, I'm not sure what's the best xp.
You can probably go to nightmare, and dshield kill the mobs for good xp.
Another possibility is to duo with a 65 melee with huge weaponry and tri-comp a healer out of group.
You might just want to go somewhere like the hole where there's a big xp modifier and level up there.
Old zones like SolB and Chardok are good places too due to no population of other people to train you and kill the mobs you want.
You might also take an hour out of your xp time to do a poj trial, and go straight to pov to get even better xp than pon.

Juujuu
For powering a Wizard, or any other class that has a point blank AE spell, load up the fastest casting AE spell you have. You only need to do 1 point of damage to a mob from the AE to get the xp if zero other non-dsheild damage is done by the pulling druid.

If you don't have an AE on your twink, you can have your twink do the pulling (though it will be smaller pulls). The twink runs around and melees each mob for 1+ damage before running along to tag the next mob. As long as you have sow you should be safe to tag a fair number of mobs. Bring the train back to the druid, and when the druid heals the twink, heal aggro should suck most of the mobs onto the druid who stands and tanks down all the mobs.

Gear on the twink and the druid matter a lot as well. My bard I was levelling up has about 2k hp unbuffed at level 50. His gear is mostly tradeskill made cultural armor, epic, 40-50hp earrings, 55-65hp rings, cof, fungi, etc etc. The druid I was using is full elemental plane armor with a bit of time gear here and there. If you don't have the crazy gear like that to tank masses and masses of mobs, pull smaller trains.

Also, if you are AEing stuff, pull mobs that are blue cons to the twink. Red cons will hit your twink almost every hit, interupt most spells, and do loads of damage. Blue mobs are pretty pansy compared to twinks when you are in the old world.
