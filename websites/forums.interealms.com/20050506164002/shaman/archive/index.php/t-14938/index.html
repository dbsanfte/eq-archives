Ok I never RvR'ed or PK'ed in EQ, but I do in DAOC - A LOT.

DAOC's RvR is designed around 3 realms (Midgard, Hibernia, and Albion) fighting each other in designated areas. These areas include: Darkness Falls (A dungeon) and the four 'frontier zones' of each realm. (One gets there by either: a) Walking through a gate to YOUR frontier or b) Buying a teleport ticket).

There is no way (other than a hack or exploit) for somebody to get into the n00bie area inside your realm. Now, if you are a 'young un' and out in the frontier - yeah, you are fair game. (There is a supposed exp bonus to frontier hunting).

Darkness Falls is a different animal. It is a dungeon whose 'ownership' changes hands depening on the number of keeps (Located in the frontier) owned by a particular realm.

However, it is possible for people to fight in DF. Stealthers (People who can go 'invisible' (Sorta) make their living fighting and being fought in DF. Anti-stealth groups form and hunt out stealthers in DF.

DAOC PvE blows donkey nuts. It is horrid, slow, tedious and not exciting at all.

DAOC RvR is well done and a blast. (Unless you don't like that in which case you're probably playing the wrong game).

IF You are into hand-holding, touchy-feely crapola then the DAOC "cooperative" server is for you. All three realms work together to take keeps in all three frontiers and groups can include members of Midgard, Albion, and Hibernia and they can communicate with each other.

I played on the co-op server for 3 days. Paint the walls with my brains. Same stuff as regular PvE.
