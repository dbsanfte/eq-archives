Flufy
Thank you for asking your question in a clear and unambivilent manner.

Well-played beastlords and shamans both require a considerable amount of hands-on management, so if you're twoboxing, its gonna be real hard to get the most out of both toons. The same is not true re: the SK and shaman duo.

With an SK/Shaman setup it goes something like this:

SK pulls mob with snare, then switches to shaman.

Mob walks in slowly.

Shaman hits the autoslow macro (which halfway through tells the pet
to attack) and then jumps back to the SK.

SK starts getting serious about building aggro, getting all those debuffs/taps in, fixing pet positioning, etc., and then moves back to the shaman.

And thats it. Back to the shaman for the rest of the fight to dot, nuke, canni, heal, etc, unless some wierd **** happens requireing you to have the SK do something different, or if you get bored by not really playing the SK much in reality. I spend my time aabout 50/50 for that reason. The point is, the option to slack some (or, to not go crazy) without losing signifigant DPS is there for you in a way it can't be for the Beastlord/Shaman arrangement.

There are many, many other issues that you didn't ask about re: why the SK is the superior partner. Some are valid, most not. I think, sometimes, that most of what's sayable on that particular topic has already been said, and can be found through the search function, as the harpies mentioned earlier. Don't let that stop you from asking questions here though. Zones, abilities, and game mechanics change constantly, so telling people to use search can be iffy, especially if the quesrion is somewhat subtle or otherwise unusual. Also you tend to get more info from live (current)discussions than from reading reams of possibly outdated and/or irrelevant posts.

Good luck with the twoboxing, it really rocks.
