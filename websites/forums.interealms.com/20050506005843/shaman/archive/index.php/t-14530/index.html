Zhorn
I'm a GM barbarian smith and it took me like 2 MONTHS of pharming to do it "the cheap way" (i.e. Shadowscream). That meant no exp time.

Here's a rough guide for your percentage chance to make Artificers....
At max modified skill (252) you have a 40% chance of success. That would be 240 raw skill + geerlok.
For each point below 252, subtract 1% but don't go below 5% as there is always a chance to succeed (or fail!).

If you are in a big raiding guild you can score a +15% smithing item maybe and "shortcut" from 192 to 212 (on Ethereal Brick combines) and then be on max skill at 219.

Or you can try to get the +10% belt I got (massive girdle of the forge from Praesortium Bikun in Santus Seru) and shortcut from 200 to 212 on Ethereal Brick Combines.
If you find a guild camping Seru keys maybe you can ask nicely for a belt if no one else needs it....

Eventually you can make Mistletoe Cutting sickles for skill ups and break even on the sell back but they need like 320 subcombines for 20 attempts! And at 1 skill up per 20 attempts-ish you are looking at an assload of clicking!!!!!

Not trying to discourage you, but I am just saying smithing is a Massive time-sink and maybe that time could be better spent leveling up (and therefore getting better gear/plat anyway etc etc).

Btw I sell bugger-all Artificiers or Tundra Helanic plate. Most of what I sell is Tae Ew and/or Hurricane armor. On some servers the Tae Ew Market is wrecked so bad you almost have to sell it at COST. :(

Luclin's Arriana
Finding a specific GM smith to make cultural armor (artificer, darksplint, etc) can be nightmarish, depending on server...but finding just any old GM smith to do things like Tae Ew combines really shouldn't be that difficult.

Check the marketplace forums for your server on eqtraders, or possibly your servers own boards for a trading forum.

On Luclin, our marketplace forum has a stickied post where 200+ skill tradeskillers can submit their name and race for inclusion so if you are looking for someone to do work for you, it isn't too hard to find them. If you have all the ingrediants for Tae Ew stuff ready to go, I wouldn't pay 9k for a pre-made shield. Get 2 sets (for fails), let the smith keep the second set if the first one succeeds. It will be cheaper than buying the item made already, and will get you a friend in that smith.

Just make sure that you really do have EVERYTHING that is needed, and that the things you bring are the right things. Nothing bugs a tradeskiller more than someone who purports to have done all the work for you only to find that they are missing half the stuff, or a couple of crucial items are the wrong thing.
