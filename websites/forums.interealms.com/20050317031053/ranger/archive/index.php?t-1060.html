The Flat Tax program has been fought against by proponents of the current tax system with several cliche'd and old arguments.

First misconception, Flat Tax system would still have the same overhead as the current tax system so no advantage there.

Well simply not true. Currently I read that the IRS is only able to Audit 1 out of every 256 filings. Research suggests that a flat tax system in which you get rid of ALL deductions so that you only have the current income and the flat tax to figure would increase the Auditing levels to 1 out of every 50.

Also depending on which Flat Tax system is proposed it is likely that the Tax information required to be turned would fit on a postcard instead of multiple forms.

Second minsconception: A Flat Tax system would hurt the poor.

Again not true. The current tax system is already very harsh on the poor who still have to lose a certain amount of income each year so that it cannot be applied to living expenses then at a later time in the year get back after they had already accrued debt for previous living expenses.

Secondly as of RIGHT NOW the IRS is MORE likely to Audit poor people and MORE like to actually go after them. For a nice commentary on this please read this. (http://slate.msn.com/?id=1005162)

And thirdly former leader of the United Way had this (http://www.heritage.org/news/99/nr042299.html) to say about Flat Taxes and the likelyhood of charitable giving

Third minsconception: a Flat Tax system wouldn't be able to address the financial needs of the US government.

Again false because:
A) the Current system already doesn't cover the financial needs of the government due to our government not being able to control its own budget...
B)a Flat Tax can still be adjusted each year as needed. One year it may be 14% for EVERYONE and the next year it may be 20% for EVERYONE.

Forth misconception: While certain small countries have used the Flat Tax program successfully they are of such limited size that they are not good examples for what the US needs.

Well Russia is putting that in its place. Russia has revamped its tax code and has I believe a 13% Flat Tax rate. It is now enjoying its highest percentage of claimed taxes and generating more money than any time previously in Russian history.

Fifth misconception: A Flat Tax would hurt the economy.

Again this is false. First let me present a study (http://news.uns.purdue.edu/UNS/html4ever/010723.Wilson.flattax.html) from an Agricultural Economist on the likely outcomes of a Flat Tax.

Also Russia has been enjoying a BOOMING economy (which is a complete turn around from just a year ago) and some of the lowest unemployment levels in its history. Basically this last misconception has been one of the leading arguments against a Flat Tax but Russia is now showing us that in fact it can work wonders for:
A) Citizen earnings
B) Government Earnings
C) National Economic Earnings.

So now I have to ask.. Isn't about time we too started to enjoy the simplicity of a Flat Tax and truly achieve equal representation through equal taxation.

A bracketed tax system, by definition, is more complex than a flat tax. A flat tax has one number. A progressive system has more than one. That means it's more complex. Of course, you can burden a flat tax system with exceptions and credits and additional rules, until it becomes more complex than a simple bracketed system, but you can do that with any system.

Bracketed systems, in particular, have the additional disadvantage of charging relatively more for people at the low end of each bracket, and relatively less for people at the high end. I'd prefer a formula-based system, where tax rates are a smooth curve.

As for use taxes, the problem is that usage is not always voluntary. For example many low income people are forced to reside far from their place of work, and make long drives to commute. Is it reasonable for these people to pay additional gas and road tax for their commute? They're already burdened by the simple expense in the first place.

The other question with a pure usage tax system would be who pays for essential services with no immediate consumer? Who pays for the police? Who pays for the medical examiner? Do you charge some poor shmoe whose house just burned down for the cost of the fire department?
