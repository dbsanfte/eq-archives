Like others have said, experience is the best teacher, as you balance dps, cc, mana management, and the variations with slightly different groups.

I would note one specific principle, though: choosing your tactics, instead of falling into them.

If you've agreed that the paladin will grab as many as three at a time and you'll all just engage the three mobs and plow your way along, then there's no problem, the paladin was doing his job.

But if the paladin's actions were making the adventure harder, and if he wouldn't change when you asked him, then part of your strategy should be to slow the paladin down. You do that by letting the mobs beat him down to 20 pct or so, and then cast Torpor. The goal is to keep him around 20 pct: not actually dying, but worrying about it. That may enable him to be more thoughtful about his pulls.
