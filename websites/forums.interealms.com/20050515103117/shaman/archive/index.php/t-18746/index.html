Flufy
Mmm, how to be a good shaman? Don't laugh at the other casters when they run OOM, it's bad form. :)

Seriously, most important thing to being a good shaman is paying attention. Know what's going on. Know what needs to be done. Figure out what isn't being done. Then do it. Shaman excell at adapting to the needs of the group. A good shaman is one who doesn't get caught in the trap of thinking "I'm a slower" or "I'm a nuker" or "I'm a healer". A good shaman lets the situation determine what role he plays.

Other tips, arranged in no discernable fashion:

You can keep your best slow and your second best slow both memmed at the same time. This is nice when there's the possibility of overpulls (or just large normal pulls) to avoid that annoying recast delay.

When you're out of hp, you're out of options. Get as much as you can, however you can, it's the most important stat.

Wis is crap. At all levels of the game. Most decent shaman gear nowadays has both wis and hp, so you probably wont have to choose much, but if you do, leave the +wis stuff for the classes for whom the size of their mana pool matters. Keeping your ass from getting dead is a lot more important than having an extra 20 mana.

Use your pet.

Don't die. A dead shaman casts no slow.

Don't be afraid of dying. Yeah, I said don't die, but it happens. You can't piss off mobs for a living and not have it go bad sometimes. :) Maybe Malox the mob first next time so you dont have to spam-slow it.

If you target a mob, you can switch back and forth between the mob and you by hitting the Tab key. If you go into Options/Keyboard and select Target from the drop down menu you can assign a key to toggle between your last two targets, whatever they are.

If you have several mobs clumped together, watch their feet. It's easier to tell them apart, and with the new targeting ring thing figuring out which mob you have targetted is a breeze, nice when you're trying to make sure everything is slowed.

Make a hotkey that says
/pause 'however many 10ths of a second it takes for the canni you're using to cycle',/cast 'whatever slot your canni spell is in'
every line for the first 3 or 4, and
/cast 'whatever slot your canni spell is in'
for the last line. Mine goes
/pause 50,/cast 7
/pause 50,/cast 7
/pause 50,/cast 7
/cast 7
/burp (so I know I'm done)
so you can stand there and chew yourself down while you have a drink, scratch your butt, stare vacantly out the window, whatever.

There's nothing wrong with making a canni-dance hotkey, but maybe keep a hotkey that just casts canni once, so you don't wind up sitting when you shouldn't. Macros rock, but they tend to be very situational and can screw you up bad if they go off at the wrong time. I have 5 hotkeys that are always up, /assist, and 4 canni-related ones. All the other stuff, the /assist-/cast malo-/cast slow-/cast nuke-/pet attack-type things, get swapped in when the situation demands.

You can make macros that mem certain spellsets. The macro is /mem 'name of your spellset'. This can be handy if you tend to find yourself doing lots of different jobs in a short period of time, or are just lazy like me.


Hope some of this is useful,

Important keys are under my left hand on the keyboard. Target nearest mob is G, target nearest person is S. (anything to avoid F keys)

90% of the time, I cast spells by hitting ALT-1 thru ALT-8. The mouse takes too long, and all my hotbuttons are used for other things. (save for canni, my fingers would fall off)

Assist the mob to heal someone. Once this becomes a habit, it's SO much easier to save someone in an emergency.

Assist your dog to target corpses that fell into the wall, or those ANNOYING FREAKING whirlwinds in miragul's. As long as kujo hasn't engaged anything else, he'll point you to the last thing he was fighting.

BE FLEXIBLE. This is the #2 rule after don't panic. Sometimes I spend most of my time healing, sometimes I do whatever I can to add to dps. Hell, last weekend I did a couple of dungeons where I never slowed unless we had 3 or more mobs at once, and then, of course, it was tigir's. Important point is, I try to remember to always be looking for a way to maximize my usefulness to my group by adjusting what I'm doing to complement our strengths and make up for our weaknesses. I find that this makes up a lot for the crappiness of my gear compared to that of my raiding guild friends. *grin*

I skimmed the other messages so I may have missed these, but...

1. stand next to the MA when slowing if you tend to pull agro. That way it doesn't screw up the melees and pets that are trying to get at the mobs back (if you are somewhere else when you get agro the mob runs to you and screws up the melee dps.)

2. in LDON, use a lower level slow to save mana. Mobs die fast anyway, so I use my lvl 39 slow most of the time in ldon. (I'm 57 now btw)

3. when there is more than 1 mob incoming, don't send the pet in until the situation is under control. Dogdog is great for taking a couple hits while you get a mob parked. If you send it in after the main mob and you get interrupted when the adds start beating on you when you slow em, the whole group suffers because now you can't do your job of slowing/debuffing the main mob.

4. if the MA can't take multiple mobs at once (easily), immediately pulladds away on incoming via slow. (the root park em)

5. last, but by far the most important...COMMUNICATE. Talk to your group constantly and tell them what you are doing (at least until you get a feel for how you all play) and ask if they have any concerns. Also, don't be bashful about giving advice to others. (although understand that they may reject your advice...sometimes for good reason, and sometimes... :rolleyes: )
