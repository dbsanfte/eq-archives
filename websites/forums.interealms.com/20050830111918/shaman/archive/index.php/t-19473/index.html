Hail,

It IS THAT EASY. But if your gear and spellbook aren't very good, then don't try going after King Dronan or the other NW/SW named on your first foray into the zone.

Head to the NE & SE sectors of Droga and it is much easier. Mobs hit for less (many are light blue cons at 65) and they STILL drop very nice loot.

Thoroughly read the 2 Droga threads in this forum. Read carefully ALL of the OTHER threads (especially the Beastlord's Guide) linked in those 2 threads. Check the bazaar for prices. See who else is farming Droga on your server. Make a plan for what you are going to camp that is within your capabilities and stick to it. If casters are giving you a problem, stick to the melee mobs like Whipcracker Krazzik (sp?) and the other melee mobs. They are trivial to root/rot, but you do have to be careful.

Bone Knight whatever his name and the Slinker named (very close to each other) in the upper right corner of the zone are EASY named to take out even if you don't have any 65 spells or the best gear.

Plot out which you will take out, and after a day or two stretch yourself a little and go after a different one. Find your comfort zone and stick to it. The Copper Hammers of Striking may sell well, but if farming them involves repeated deaths (or gating) for you (w/ possibly long CR's), then go after the lesser mobs and farm them instead.

Droga is a gold mine. IA can also be, though personally it is too much of a pain to run all the way over there - which is to your advantage because many other players feel the same way.

As another option, the mobs in Grieg's End are pretty easy, and they drop some okay coin. Getting past the entrance is probably the hardest part.

Take care,

GO NE/SE. Kill those named there.

King Dronan, Earth Seer Gamolk, Ice Master Cialin, Flame Master Japal (& a few others) are not for the poorly geared or soloing novice. Stay away from those mobs until you have more experience in the zone.
READ the GUIDE!!! The Beastlord Guide even COLOR COORDINATES the map for you. Stay in the green zone your first few times, then try some yellow for awhile. Slowly work your way to the red section over time.

You can bootstrap your gear (& spellbook) up in Droga using this method. Selling the loot from the green area will fund your spells & some upgrades so that the yellow area is doable. Rinse & Repeat for the yellow to the red area.

The plethora of buffers for pp in PoK & Nexus makes going into Droga with KEI & Virtue (or even Pot9+Kazad if you want) very easy. This can be your safety buffer if you aren't 3k+ hp unbuffed. KEI of course just helps reduce your downtime. Ensure that you put these 2 or 3 buffs (donate extra for a Druzzil if nukes are a problem because of low MR) go in the last spots on your buff bar, as in my experience those hardly ever get dispelled.

Don't get discouraged. :) Give it another try! ;)

I'm going to agree with those that recommend learning the bazaar.

I've been soloing Droga for a while, and many of the camps do seem easy (at least the "yellow" and below camps) as long as you have the CC skills to get one or two at a time (root/dog park and learn which ones you can single pull with malo). Still the cash in Droga is not flowing fast as I would like (I took a 100k loan from a guild friend, and I'd like to get it paid off quick).

Watching my friend reselling things in the bazaar makes me think I've been missing the real way to make cash in this game. With some advice I've started selling simple combine items, and am easily making more money than I ever did in Droga. Try to find a tedious combine that people need to lvl a trade skill. One can make five figure sums selling these skill up combines, as long as you can leave your trader up for extended periods of time. Look for combines that one trade skill requires that involves a different trade skill (for example a common lvling combine for tailoring requires a brewing combine). A lot of times people do not have the patience to spend time lvling a secondary skill so they can raise a different trade skill.

This is easy, safe plat. Of course you're not getting any exp while you do this, but if you're under geared and have a hard time in Droga, Veks, AC, or other farm zones, you’re not gong to die and waste time with corps runs and rezes either.

I’m not recommending reselling items, that seems to be as risky as dungeons (listening to my friend sweat over 400K worth of items that he purchased, that then started to go down in price, I know its not for me).
