It depends on what you are fighting.

In melee combat its a comparison of AC + random vs ATK + random (**SIMPLIFIED VIEW**)

This is where the concept of softcaps came from. It is possible to reach a point where your AC is high enough that the mob hits at minimum frequency or for minimum damage.

In each expansion the average attack rating of the mobs has gone up, thus a higher AC value is required to hit that sweet spot where you take minimum hits for minimum damage.

It can be very difficult to give stat to stat comparisons because many things in EQ are based on "tiers" not just raw totals.

Example might be that the difference between 975 and 1000 AC would be more dramatic than the difference between 1025 AC and 1000 AC, even though the point differential is the same.

This can be pretty easy to measure with something like a resist statistic, its much more difficult with a stat like AC.

Now, onto your question. The calculation of damage avoidance and mitigation is not a linear function, so there cannot be a hard rule for AC ~ HP conversion. That is, going from 800 to 900 ac will give you much more mitigation/avoidance than going from 1400 to 1500 ac.

Given the ac available on most shaman gear, you will likely be best served looking for HP gear first and worrying about AC as a secondary stat, along with wisdom. As posted above, most gear that has good HP, also has acceptable AC.

As a general rule of thumb, an unbuffed baseline of 3k HPs, 200 wisdom, and 1k AC is good for a 65 shaman with no AAs, and can be achieved using only droppable or single-group farmable gear (see my magelo).

Purdypog
Well, I can tell you there has been a bunch of study of this subject on the melee boards.

Quick summary of findings:

Pre Pop - Basically 1200ac soft cap for AC - meaning that anything above that was pretty much worthless. Many studies, pretty much accepted...Did shaman need to be at the full cap and perhaps give up some hit points? Probably not, thus the 1000 ac and max your hit points mantra you see on this board.

POP - With the mobs Attack rating rising so was the impact of player AC regarding damage absorption. Warrior board studies show ac of 2000 still provides increased absorption compared to 1900 ac. Many studies, pretty much accepted.....Do shaman need to be at or near the cap? Again probably not if you have to give up hit points. What is POP "sweetspot" for Shaman? Well it certainly it varies according to how much you get smacked around, but the "mantra" on this board from folks who have read the melee boards and adjusted from pre-pop suggest 1200+ with anything over 1500ish probably overkill. Some peeps still hung on to 1000ac as the mantra even in POP because they did not believe that the damage absorption (even being that far below the soft cap) was worth giving up hit points - which is certainly true if you are not getting beat on too often. But, I for one saw a significant difference from 1000 - 1200 AC and it was not necessary to give up hardly any hps because of the nature of the better armour which had both excllent ac and good hit points.

LDON - I have not read any studies yet, but just my observations of the mobs hitting for 250-300 lead me to believe that the AC "softcap" in normal LDON is between the pre-pop 1200 and POP 2000+, with my best somewhere aroun 1400-1500ish.

What does this mean? Well I for one sincerely believe that shaman who try to max hitpoints while keeping ac at 1000 are not utilizing the most efficient combination of hps and ac for POP, raiding and LDON. Unless they are super passive shaman and are not getting smacked around at all, I think increased ac is adviseable and actually easy to obtain these days.

I think that this forum is most valuable to the newly 60-65 shaman who just got Q (or Torpor) and canny5 and is trying to figure out what the heck to do with hps vs ac. Well my advice would be to get about 3000-3200 hitpoints minimum unbuffed even if that means falling to the 1000 ac mark buffed with virtue (now thats pretty weak AC in my book for pop mobs, but others may disagree). There is almost no disagreement that there is a significant breakpoint at 1000 according to many studies so don't fall below that!

Once said shaman has the necessary hit points to safely and easially use canny5 I suggest that he continue to access his upgrades trying to hit the next significant ac mark of 1100, then 1200 all the time trying to continue to grow hit points but not at the expense of AC. At 1200 AC I think the shaman is probably at the point where most pieces of armour he is looking at as upgrades give both excellent AC and hitpoints so just upgrade slot by slot pretty much trying to keep the same or better ac and adding hit points (and mana). Thus at this point AC will grow much slower than hitpoints, but both will grow because of the nature of the "good" armour.

I tend to agree with this view of things also. The ac vs attacks with random components involved does mean that at some point your ac gets high enough relative to the mobs attack that they pretty much hit the hard coded mins that verant has put in. We all know that no matter what your ac is a mob always has a chance to hit you. After all those pesky skellies that attack you still manage to hit and not miss every single swing possible. The other interesting thing is that the other end applies too. If your ac is too low the attk side will almost always win and you'll be at the maxes for a mob. The result if you graphed it would be an s curve. Starts off fairly flat when your ac's too low. At somepoint it starts to curve up when your ac gets to the point that it's able to help you start avoiding/mitigating blows and then levels back off when you hit the max. If your ac happens to fall into that inbetween point then it can have a very definite effect on how often and how much your getting hit for. If on the other hand your above the max or below the min it isn't gonna do much.

The other thing that seems to be involved is that relative level seems to have an effect too. A level 55 player trying to tank a level 60 mob is going to be less effect than a level 65 even with everything else the same. This is easiest to notice right when an expansion pack comes out that raised the level cap. Watching my roommate go from 60 to 65 on his warrior and the difference it made in tanking the golems in valor etc was noticable even with no gear/aa changes.

Ac definitely doesn't hurt expecially when it comes to stuff an expansion or 2 old. Usually gear has improved enough that as a shaman you can actually have ac better or equal to what tanks where sporting with a bit of effort.

Personally I've never worried too much about ac it's just come with the better quality gear I've gotten just like wisdom and mana has.
