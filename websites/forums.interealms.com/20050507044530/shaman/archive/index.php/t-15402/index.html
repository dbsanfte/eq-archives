Ungerargh
Pure melee hit with more consistency for several reasons:

1) They have a damage bonus (minimum hit). While this doesn't tighten the range of values they can hit for, it does bring them closer to each other percentage-wise.

2) Think of it in terms of 20 sided-dice. The higher your atk value, the worse roll you can get and still do ok damage. For example, my monk has 52 more offense and 52 more 1HB skill than my shaman. Think of this as effectively giving him a +5 to every damage roll he makes. So while a shaman rolls and just gets whatever they roll (supposing a 1 gets me a min hit of one damage, and a 20 gets me a max hit of 84 damage, with my spear of fate), my monk rolls a 1, he actually gets a 6, he rolls a 15 or higher, and he hits for max.

3) Try this: get a beastlord to cast ferocity on you, and a shaman to cast ferine avatar on you, a ranger to cast spirit of the predator and strength of tunare, an enchanter to cast speed of vallon, a bard to sing warsong of zek, and cap your strength (should put you at somewhere around 1600ish atk? /shrug). Now start attacking. You should notice that you hit more often, and for a higher average damage. By adding all those +atk buffs, you are getting yourself into the range of a melee's atk value (and a well-buffed or well-equipped one, at that), and you'll notice a huge difference.

EverQuest doesn't check for your distance from a mob to determine how much damage you do, of that I guarantee you. It's just your atk value versus the mob's AC. The only difference between melee and caster/priests in this regard is that melees are going to have higher atk values due to their higher skill caps.

ciccero
As far as range goes - all objects in the game have a bounding box for the object to be rendered . If you're standing within the mobs bounding box, you can hit it. If you're outside the bounding box, you can't.

That's the reason they had to take the Megalodon out of Lake Rathe. It's graphic was such that the mobs bounding box extended onto the shore in some places, so players in certain places could attack the Meg from the shore and the mob wasn't able to fight back.
