LaughingWoman
I'm in a much different situation. Most of my favorite folks from my old guild burned out just before or after I did.

Getting over EQ reminds me of nothing so much as the AA 10 step recovery program. One of those steps is denial.... where people who complain about EQ, SoE and those wretched devs - refuse to consider switching.

But everybody hits the stages at different points. I remember being fed up with EQ but continuing to play because (stop me if you've heard these before!)

*I've put too much *WORK* into EQ to quit now
*I'm unwilling to be a noob again
*There's no way I'll find a group of people in another game as great as this group

And more. But I realized that a game doesn't have to feel like work, and being a noob again actually brings back that magical feeling, and that there are great people out there, lots of em.

But you can't force anybody to realize it, I don't think. But if you just gently put the word out there, I think - over time - a lot of that group will join you in WoW. (especially if EQ2 is built to have the same kinds of frustrations that made me flee EQ - which seems to be what I'm hearing from beta)

I'm not sure how well that addressed what you were asking, but it's something I think about quite a bit. Best of luck to you, however you go.
Krup
My guild plays all sorts of games. We originally formed in UO, many (most) of us moved to EQ where we expanded some (seldom over 30 members), moved to DAoC, tried SWG as a guild and dumped it, tried CoH as a guild and dumped it, and are now scattered across the galaxy of MMORPGs awaiting the NBT (next-big-thing: a running joke phrase in our guild). We've also poked around with games like Unreal Tournament, Planetside, Space, Above and beyond, WWII Online, Tribes, Sims Online, and Horizons, but none of those held our interest.

I was the only member of our guild to get into the WoW closed beta.

After the WoW stress test (where 16 guildmates joined me) and due, in part, to the ongoing web page I keep of my WoW adventures, WoW will probably be our NBT.

We still have guildies strewn across UO, EQ, DAoC, COH, and SWG. We all still meet on the guild message boards and talk about what's going on. Occasionally we all congregate on a particular game and have great fun together. We also meet new people in these games and add they become permamnent members of our roster. Our core membership was originally from the same company. Our current membership is from across the USA in at least 8 cities, but many of us have managed to meet each other in person - usually tied in on business trips.

Back to your post. In my opinion, your guild is not as tight-knit as you might think. To us, the camaraderie is the key element of the guild - the games we play are incidental to that. In your guild it seems like a big split between what game is being played and who you are playing a game with. My advice is if you want to play a different game, then do so. Some of your guildies will come along because they like playing with the other guildmates. Other players will stay behind because they're not willing to give up their investment in their EQ character. If you leave EQ along with some others, you will be with the right folks.
