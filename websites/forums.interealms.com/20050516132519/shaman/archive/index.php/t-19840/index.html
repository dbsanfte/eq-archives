greyfish
65 necro, 65 shaman 2 boxer here. I've not played a SK so I have nothing to compare against, but I would like to offer my experiences with a necro/shaman combo. Obviously you have to fight mobs that don't summon. The comment about getting summoned and beat down is moot because your not going to go up against mobs that summon. I'll use PoV here as an example. The back walls by DG are great places to kite. Sow the necro, pull with snare, lay on the dots. Send in both pets, reapply dots, reapply snare for good measure. Very easy really. Also, it's very easy to kite the mob past the shaman for added damage. Very easy, don't let people tell you it's difficult to time it, it's not. Also, Wake the dead aa adds to dps. It's next to impossible to pull the mob off the necro, even if you send in the pets right away. This works in HoH as well, the courtyard area is a great place to kite. Works in PoN too if you know where to set up.

I'd think both scenarios are actually viable, a lot depends on your play style. I don't think your going to level any faster(that dramatically anyways), but I do think the necro will outdamage the SK. I'd think the SK might offer you more places to go duo, so the choice is really up to you.

As your shaman gains AA points, you'll be able to train defensive abilities and it's fun to tank once in a while. What i do is pull as I've done above, lay on the dots with the necro, slow with shaman, and then FD the necro and tank the mob. send in pets, reapply dots as needed.

btw, I'm about 4700 hps fully buffed, no elemental/time gear with about 120 aa's, necro has 100 aa's.

BlargX
I played a 60 shaman/monk combo for a long time, and then a 65 shaman/60 monk, and now a 65 shaman/65 SK.

The SK is really a lot of fun. He doesn't have the mitigation or hitpoints of a warrior, or discs, but he is so much more fun to play. He does have three things I really love -- FD(not as good as my monk's) and summon corpse(which necros also have) and the ability to survive being summoned by many mobs, and to just tank generally. It gives you a lot of flexibility to be able to survive being summoned. Sk snare also causes a lot of aggro, so I can make it so my shaman never even gets hit at all by mobs -- pretty important when you're in charge of both healing and slowing, and a nice luxury indeed.

Necros do put out tremendous DPS though.

But another way to look at the situation is, if you are tired or just feeling like a change of pace, what do you do? With a shaman/SK, you can play one toon(shaman) at a time that has enormous flexibility and responsibility and must constantly canni. It can be very rewarding and very irritating sometimes too. But with a SK, you can experience a very different type of play, and one that I find far less involved in micro-management. Like when I used to just single play my monk sometimes, playing the SK can feel like a refreshing vacation that helps keep me from getting bored with the game.

I think a necro is one heck of a great toon, and thank goodness it doesn't have to canni, but it's not as radically different from a shaman as a SK is. You won't get as much of the feeling of variety going from one dotting caster to another as you would going from one dotting caster to a tank -- one who can even FD pull!

In raids, too, SK's can do a fair amount of kicking back compared to shamans, who always have to be slowing, healing inside and outside their groups, curing, and recasting buffs.

I wouldn't mind having a necro at all myself, but I don't think it's as much of a change of pace if you decide to play it as a single every so often. My SK has saved my sanity many times.
