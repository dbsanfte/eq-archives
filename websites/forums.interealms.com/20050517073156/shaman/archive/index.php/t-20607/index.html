I was thinking about this the other day. The original point of slow mitigation was to allow content to be more finely tunable so they could make mobs slowable without making them gimped. There should be more choices in designing mob DPS than Full Slow, No Slow, or Grummus only. This is fair enough, we've caved and accepted it.

So why is there still content where mobs are outright immune to slows altogether? A "Slightly" slowable mob only sees a 16% reduction in DPS when slowed by a shaman. If you need to tune a mob's DPS to a certain point, why not simply lower the delay of the mob slightly and then add at least some capability for slow to do SOMETHING. That way a class who slows will have something to bring to the table. They use slow mitigation as an excuse to gimp the ability, then ignore the ability to implement it in the name of "making content challenging".

What do you call a rogue without backstab? a ranger who can't cast. Imagine if content mitigated backstab so it was a glorified "kick" in some areas. Some mobs were outright immune to it. This only affects rogues, because they are the only class reliant on this ability to be desirable or useful. You look at the "active" skills melee classes receive that are similar, manually triggered boosts to DPS (I guess you could consider Flying Kicks, Ranger nukes, Sk spears and Bst nukes with thier 30 second recasts along this line?), and all those classes bring other utility to the table besides it for which they are desirable.

Case and point, Look at the 1 groupable ikkinz trials. You have the named which hit for 2200 and are slow immune. I fail to see the point of this, other than to further punish people who strive for any form of group diversity. I'm all for making content fun and challenging, but I still fail to see why the dynamic of removing slows entirely from the picture does anything other than completely ruin the benefit we bring to any sort of group. If it was at least slightly slowable, I could toss a nihil on it at the start of the fight in hope that maybe, just maybe the 15% slow it would provide would somewhat lessen the humungous gap in the ability to crank out hp/sec we're stuck with.

Even for raid content, the elimination of slow is simply a lame idea. in time, all the gods (save quarm) are immune. Granted, the CCable mobs are slowable, but I fail to see why removing a dynamic of the fight makes anything more fun? I'd like to know I'm there and able to make a difference in the fight other than being relegated to /rt cures.

Getting back to the vxed/tipt/ikkinz example, Two clerics, or a cleric and a druid with NO SLOWS should NOT be more suited for content than a shaman and a cleric. End of ****ing story.

Murtak, there are two aspects to healing.

Firstly, there is the healing required to keep up with the mobs total dps. If the dps was very even, but exceeded the rate of healing available, the tank's hp would slowly diminish until they were dead. In most situations where you stand a chance of winning, this one included, healing is more than sufficient to keep up with average dps. Unless your healer goes oom, its the spikes that get you, not the average dps.

Secondly, mob dps is very seldom even. You will have periods of low dps, average dps, and high dps (damage spikes). Setting up a healing strategy you need to account for these spikes or you will lose. On raids its simple, the next tank AEtaunts and picks up agro, but that isnt realistic in 1 group scenarios. If the dps over a short time frame (between heals) exceeds the tank hp, the tank dies (obviously). The frequency with which this happens varies depending on the frequency of healing, the size of the heals and the tank's total hp. Changing the mobs dps will increase the frequency of these potential lethal damage spikes. Now, the frequency of healing can only be changed by increasing the number of healers, and the size of the heals can only be changed by moving to a higher rank of healer. Since most healers in KD are equivalent in that respect, the only other variable that can be changed is the tank hp. As mob dps increases you will need more hp on your tank to accomodate the larger damage spikes, and more hp to cover the inevitable hp attrition that follows chain healing. This incidental attrition will increase as the ratio of available healing/dps gets smaller. So the difficulty of the encounter increases exponentially. Likewise, with lower dps, your tank needs fewer hp to survive the spikes and is much less likely to suffer hp attrition. So the encounter becomes exponentially easier as dps decreases.

Btw, I dont have exact numbers, but I expect a mature VT level pre-elemental warrior will have about 1000 - 1200 hp less than a mature elemental warrior, who will be around 1000 -1500 hp short of a mature time warrior. And that is more or less the order of magnitude we are talking about here.
