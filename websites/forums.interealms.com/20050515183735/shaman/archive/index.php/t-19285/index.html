Iynna
Getting higher level mobs debuffed is often a coordination of multiple classes.

For a raid, I prefer to have a debuf chat channel with enchanters, mages and shaman.

Shaman leads with malos can calls it out in channel. Malos is very low aggro. Next the enchanter tashes. After Tash is called, shaman tries to Turgurs while mage tries to Malosinia. Doesn't always get you there on resistant mobs, but this is usually the best approach.

Call slow resists in group so that the group healer knows to watch your health if they can.

And those of you that believe in Cha and standing under the belly of a mob, just do it.

I try to adapt to the situation. With some mobs I can stand at a distance and slow. If I pull aggro, I get summoned. If however the mobs run to me when I aggro, this is a bad thing so I adjust and stand next to the Main Tank when I slow. I believe for certain mobs, you are less likely to have slow resisted if you are within melee range. This was the case resently when I farmed ice giant toes in permafrost. Greenie mobs to a 65 shaman and I still needed to back up against a wall and channel my slowes, dots and roots at close range. Rooted mobs at a distance were resisted often and burned too much mana. And this isn't just old world mobs, at 4-way in Baston of Thunder, it also seemed that I was less likely to get resisted when I was closer to the mob.

And relative to Cha, I am a believer. I spent roughly half of my EQ time from level 53 to level 59 two boxing a monk in the basement of Karnors before Luclin came out. If I couldn't slow the mob right away, I got behind and had to spam heal resulting in a long med break or a train to the zone. Over and over again I saw strings of resisted slows, cast Cha, then only occasional resists. After that experience, I almost always have Cha on myself, be it an LDoN group or a Elemental/Time raid. If I have too many bufs and I am worried about mob effects overwritting my good bufs, I don't hesitate to remove Cha after I've removed haste, ranger attack bufs and other stuff I consider useless.

It cost so little time and mana to cast, I just do it. I don't care to argue with people about the value of Cha, they have their opinions and knowledge base, I have mine. And I don't think anyone cares or notices that I cast Cha on myself after I hit the tanks.

I'm sure am glad that my Shroud of the Stoncrafter (DRU CLR SHM) has a 5% channeling mod and CHA+15. Hey why they put Cha on this and other high end gear? Oh yeah, so I can get better prices from merchants.
