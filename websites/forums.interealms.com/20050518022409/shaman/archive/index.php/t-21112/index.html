Shadrak
I 3-box a LOT. Almost exclusively. My current trio, that I really like, is enchanter/mage/cleric. They're not 65, though, so these comments may not apply to you.

The enchanter is level 61. I get arcane rune - a must-have spell for enchanters. The mage is level 57, and the cleric is level 55.

This trio has an average level of 57.667. So when I 3-box an LDoN, I get a level 53 dungeon. The points suck, but they're quite easy.

In a 53 dungeon, every mob type is charmable. In a 58 or 63 dungeon, only a single mob type is charmable per theme. If you get unlucky with mob placement, there may not be a charmable mob anywhere near the entrance. If that happens, my group would fail, because my damage output from a non-charming enchanter and a cleric is basically zero. A mage alone cannot do enough dps to win consistently.

So, I really like the enchanter/mage/cleric trio. I'd like to swap out the cleric for my shaman, but the shaman is too high in level. I've tried, with a 61 enchanter/57 mage/62 shaman, and got destroyed. Can't pacify the mobs, can't charm, got tons of resists - it was brutal.

Once I get enough points in Takish to get my mage her pet focus, I'll probably go ahead and level up to the 62-ish area, where I'll once again try some 3-boxing. Maybe a level 61 mage pet will be a good enough tank that I won't need to charm.

The way that trio works, I spend all my attention on the enchanter. The cleric just heals - pretty easy there. The mage just sends in the pet and throws in some nukes. And malo-s the enchanter pet. I spend all my time concentrating on the enchanter. I pull with the enchanter, and have to be ready for charm breaks. At this level, it takes a long time for a charmed dual-wielding NON-HASTED pet to eat through my 1500hp rune spell, so I almost always have time to mez and re-charm. Note - I don't haste my pets. I don't feel comfortable doing it - they already do enough damage to be fine without it.

So, for my money, an enchanter/mage/cleric is a super trio. I can definitely see a shaman working in place of the cleric, for more dps, but it just doesn't work for me at this point, since I'm trying to do LDoNs exclusively. In a normal experience setting, the shaman would be a better choice, I think.

So what char would you want to add to a shaman/mage combo and why?
I 3-box my shadowknight main with a shaman/mage duo very often. Assuming competant skill levels, you have:

* great puller
* strong tank: two, if really needed - mage pet usually does ok as an offtank, once the mob is slowed
* strong dps: both from shaman/sk dots and mage/sk dd's
* 3 pets(one of which is very nice) for additional dps, as well pet haste/proc items to increase their dps even further
* damage shields
* snare and/or root, depending on what the circumstances require
* sufficient healing: slow + quiescence/trushar + lifetaps are almost always enough, if not a tnarg's will be
* Call of the Hero: can be invaluable if you accidentally make a bad pull and can't get a feign off
* decent mana regen: between Pact of Hate, Mental Corruption/Horror, Canni, mod rods, Zevfeer's Bite, and Xegony's Phantasmal Guard, each character will regen a minimum of 18 mana per tick + FT + natural regen.

I can't think of anything else I'd even want to do(within reason), short of res or ports, and PoK eliminates a lot of the need for ports.
