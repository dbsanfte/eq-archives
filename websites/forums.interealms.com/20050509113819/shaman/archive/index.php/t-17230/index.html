Kriel Darkbane
MGB stands for Mass Group Buff.

It is an AA ability that allows you to cast a group-targetable buff spell on everyone within a certain range of you. You can hit a great many people with the spell all at once this way.

CC stands for Crowd Control, in the context you're referring to. It means the ability to prevent several monsters from beating the crap out of your group. Normally it refers to using Mesmerize spells, or sometimes charm. Shamans do a weaker, but still quite effective version of crowd control by using attack speed slow and root spells. In my mind, one of the things that distinguishes a good shaman from a bad one is the degree to which they can perform effective crowd control.

My personal favorite is when the group enchanter is pulling in LDoN, gets a critical lull resist, and 3-4 mobs come piling in and kill him almost instantly. Then I slow and root park everything and we happily kill them all and resurrect the enchanter, about the time he's asking if anyone lived. ;)

MGB= Mass Group Buff. Means the caster can hit everyone in their area with the spell, regardless of whether they are in the caster's group. AA ability

HOS\IOS= Harness of Spirit\Infusion of Spirit. The top level buffs that a Shaman can cast on someone below 45. Do yourself a favor, buy HoS, but ALSO get IOS. Takes more buff slots and time, but IOS wil let you stack your STR, DEX, AGI, and STA buffs on top. HOS won't, and casters will hate you for casting it on them anyway, since it mucks with their personal line of shielding. Also, you can stack Altuna on top of IOS. Heh.

CC= Crowd Control. 5 baddies coming in to camp? You need CC. The Enchanter and Bard are best for it...they can mez the adds. Other methods are the Necro's ability to ghetto mez, rooting the adds away from the group, fearing them (okay, that is usually a Very Bad Idea). Anything that keeps multiple mobs from becoming an issue is CC.
