I wish I could find the post that someone made about mitigating slows. They did a parse of the damage of slowed mobs, and then ran a nice regression analysis. The formula that most fit the facts was something like the following for MITIGATING mobs (ie, just use normal slow percentage for non-mitigating):

Mob is slowed by (100-(X + ((Y-X)*Y)
where X a mob class (ie, all Dufus Wallbangers) specific amount for which the mob is FULLY slowed, and Y is the percentage slow (75% for shaman, of course).

The net result being, each mob has an amount for which it is fully slowable. Let's say the X amount for a Dufus Wallbanger is 50% slowable.

100 - (50 +((75-50)*.75) = 100 - (50 + (25*.75)) = 100 - (50 + 18.75) = 100 - 68.75.

Thus, the mob is 68.75% slowed, or moving at 31.25% of it's previous speed.

I'm probably not explaining this well (it's early for me and I haven't had coffee yet), but the general gist is that each mitigating mob is fully slowable for X percentage. Then mitigation is applied to the remaining amount of slow over that cap of X.
