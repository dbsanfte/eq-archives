Get one level each of

Radient Cure
each of these AA's adds to the things you can do at a moments notice and each one is an instant 'new' thing that increases your power significantly. SC and VP are fun effects to see happen :)

If I was doing it all again, I think I would go for Combat Agility/Lightning Reflexes before others. A single miss per fight from a mob hitting for 500+ is more HP that any ammount of A will get you, and with CA/LR you get a lot of misses. Live Shaman >>>> Dead Shaman in all circumstances.

With CanniV, you've done some mana production AA's, so I'd wait on getting SCM for a while and get something different.

Focus on our core competency spell that can be affected by AA; torpor/quiesence. Up it's duration and reduce it's casting time (SCRM and QB).
The upped duration of buffs = reduced mana expenditure over time, especially as you will be casting Quiesence a lot - I know I do. 9AA's getting +25% extension on Quiesence = a spell that is 25% more mana effective - the increased mana effectiveness from the same AA's SCM is about 10%-12%. The extended duration of buffs that are annoying to cast is a massive boost to our power and a great reducer of tedious buffing (Haste, FA, Quiesence, Replenishment)
I know SCM affects all spells you cast, but it felt to me that getting SCR gave me the same boost in mana capabilities as SCM gave. Going to SCRM from SCR was equally totally worthwhile.

Tugela
I notice that most of you take CA3/LR5 but not CS3/ID5, or at very least CS3. As someone who plays both a warrior and a shaman I think you are nuts. You get a much bigger bang for the buck from CS3 than you do for LR5, yet it takes 3aaxp less. Now, I know that you are thinking it is better to be missed than hit for less, and that might well be true for things that hit for 1000 or more, but I expect that the vast majority of shaman usually fight things that hit for much less. In LDoN for example most people are fighting stuff that hits for 100-300 in general, and with ~5khp buffed (which most self respecting shaman should be able to achieve) and for hits like that CS3 is much more usefull than ID5.

If you are getting hit a lot (which shaman are), survivability >>> everything else. Other than certain basic essentials such as canni5, mgb and rc, screw everything else and do your defensive skills first. I would suggest CA3/CS3/ND3, followed by ID5/LR5. After that you can work on the fluffy stuff.
