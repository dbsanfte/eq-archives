post tier 2 tier 3 pop would i be gimped?Whatever class you try, you will not have raid-drop equipment unless you raid. So compared to people who raid, you would be gimped: there would be things that high-level-raiders can do that you won't be able to do, because they'll have a few thousand more hp than you will, as well as additional focus and mitigation and so on.

If your goal is to have the same equipment as raiders, then you will not succeed. You will only frustrate yourself.

On the other hand, there is some pretty good equipment at this point that is obtainable with single-group activities. Check out Samanna's website to see what kind of stuff is available.

On the other other hand, if your goal is to have fun, there is nothing that needs to prevent that. There will be plenty of places your shaman and ranger can go, plenty of projects that you can accomplish.

The key question will be, can you be satisfied having fun in Everquest without having ever-more-impressive equipment?

Slein Jinn
As a shadowknight who regularly boxes a shaman and makes an effort to group with them, I would say they're not particularly gimped at all. They are versitile, yet not watered down by that versitility. Content and class balance are closely tied, and the nature of the content in GoD was very hard on shamans, just as it was SK's, but OoW and DoN have moved toward content that is much more friendly to both classes.

They're not perfect, but they have a lot to offer. In trivial content like much of DoN, they can fill both slower and main healer roles simultaneously, leaving an extra group slot open for DPS. In harder content, they function well as slowers and backup healers. Shaman buffs have been somewhat revitalized with OoW spells; they can now buff past stat caps and have buffs that add directly to things like damage mods and avoidance.

They could use a few tweaks, such as slows being mitigated a percentage rather than a flat amount, and potentially a small increase to their fast heals in view of the imminent boost to hybrid healing, but overall, as primarily a member of another class, I would say shamans are in fairly good shape right now. Sure, people bitch - they do that on every class board, but they're a solid class that's still part of almost the top several 2-box combinations.
