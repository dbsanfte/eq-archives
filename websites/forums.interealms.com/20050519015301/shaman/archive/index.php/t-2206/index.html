Graal the Dorf
DEX has absolutely nothing to do with double attack or dual wield rates. Those are based completely on skill and the RNG.

Hit/miss rate is dependent on weapon skill, offense skill, and avoidance AC of the mob you are fighting. Avatar doesn't affect any of those variables.

Max hit is dependant on weapon skill, STR, and + atk buffs.

Damage distribution is dependant on STR and + atk buffs.

Avatar will raise the max hit (up to a ceiling of 5.7 times weapon damage) and will increase the average damage.

In the test linked above, the parse is a pet against a soulbinder. Average damage did go up by a reasonable amount. If the parse had been against a mob that didn't have such insanely high mitigation AC, the increase in damage would likely have been more dramatic. Can ranger attack buffs be cast on pets? Will they stack with pet buffs? That might be worth trying, there is likely an attack threshold that would yeild greater returns.

Try parsing against average xp mobs and see what the difference is.
