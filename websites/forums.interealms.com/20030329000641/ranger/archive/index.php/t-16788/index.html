In all cases, we followed the following design rules (this is what worked for us):
1 tank (warrior, paladin, shadow knight)
1 crowd control (bard or chanter)
1 healer (cleric, druid, shaman)
3 dmg (ranger, necro, monk, rogue, mage, wiz, bst)

No one class is better than others but there are +'s and -'s to each obviously. Most important thing seems to be having people that will work together. The big thing for us was getting the casters to stay close enough to the melee so there is no ping pong. Nothing will kill your trial faster than trying to kill a mob chasing one of the robes.

Some suggestions:
-A chanter with DC helps loads with DPS if you can find one.
-Having a necro in group to feed the healer before the executioner also helps (especially if using a druid or shaman for a healer)
-keep the group close together
-don't let people convince you that you one class is more needed than another. all classes can play a worthwhile role in the trials.
-pickup groups for trials don't work nearly as well as guild/friend groups. Teamwork is essential.


hope my ramblings are somewhat helpful. :)
