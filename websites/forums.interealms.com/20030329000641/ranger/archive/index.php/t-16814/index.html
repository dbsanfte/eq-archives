Kerak states: "Just curious, why hasn't anyone suggested Eagle Eye as a focus effect on some of the armors"

For the relative difficulty of obtaining the patterns for the armors, Eagle Eye would be a very weak effect to place on a piece of armor when compared to the effects that the other classes get, even if was a continuous effect. I'd rather something that improves my ATK in all areas not just melee or archery, makes spellcasting more efficient, and/or gives me a useful ability/spell effect. (read: An appropriate spell I don't have in my repertoire or one that I currently use, not a situational one with an insanely high casting time or an obsolete one that's already been replaced by better spells in my own repertoire.)

I personally don't agree with whoring out spells on items just to 'simplify the lives' of pure melees or even really giving items with spell effects or abilities way out of sync of their class roles.
Even if the spell effect is 'weak' compared to what a real casting class is capable of by the time it's useable/obtainable, the ability to see invisible creatures/players, right click heal or buff resists, stats etc. has absolutely nothing to do with a monk, warrior, or rogue's primary role of damage dealing or damage absorption.
Casters and hybrids pay the price for their magic use by reduced melee skills, hit points, dependency on mana etc. yet it seems to be forgotten that the price those pure melee were to pay for their specialization was the inability to use magic. And it kills me to hear complaints from pure melee classes that summed up says they think they're entitled to access to any spells they want without expenditure of mana or drawbacks. It must be nice to be able to summon infinite bandages or throwing weapons, cast Invisibility, Spirit of the Wolf, Avatar etc. without ever having to go through the expense of acquiring the money to purchase components when you're in a random pick up group where gems are auto-defaulted to the cleric and the rogue is pickpocketing mobs dry or the same person is looting every single corpse before it hits the ground. Much less endure the frustration of multiple spell casting fizzles to work up spell casting abilities like Channelling, Alteration, Divination or Conjuration etc.

As far as focus effects/ATK enhancements go, I know this sentiment may not be exactly popular, but I don't think warriors, paladins, and SKs should be getting the same amount of ATK value increases that a monk, rogue, or ranger does on the exact same armor piece. Damage dealers should be getting the superior ATK values as tanks already get the superior AC and hit point values, which is in line with their established roles. The reverse should be true.
