Warren Maguson Emarr
I've thought about DoT aggro and I'll make a disclaimer now, I'm no mathematician or programmer but I've speculated that everytime a tic goes off, the monster calculates hate by the total amount of damage done by the DoT so far.

If hate calculation was done this way, then it means that a DoT would generate an enormous amount of hate towards the end of the DoT's cycle. Multiple DoT's would exacerbate this whole thing, rapidly generating more hate than a melee could possibly generate.

So for instance, say you stuck a 400 point poison dot that would run for 7 tics. On the first tic, the DoT has done 400 damage and you get bumped up on the hate list. On the second tic, it adds 400 damage to your total but combines it with damage previously done so it calculates the hate generated at that point as 800 for the tic, 1200 total, on the 3rd tic, it adds 400 to 800 (the total done by the DoT so far) for a combined total of 2400 hate points, on the 4th tic, 400 + 1200 + 2400 would equal 4000, and so on.....

If DoT's generated hate like that, it would explain why one DoT would keep you so high on the list even after a melee did thousands of points of melee damage.

But again, this is pure speculation on my part. SOE has time and again stated that they would not release exact numbers or formulas to preserve the mystery of the game and has built in rules against reverse engineering their code in the EULA.

This makes it all the more frustrating for us when SOE says, "Give us proof something is broken." since we basically have a devil of a time trying to do so when we don't really know where to start. Even if we show them circumstantial evidence, they can still say, "I'm sorry, we checked the code and nothing's wrong."

Now look at any DD spell and attrib1 is 0 and base1 is the value of the nuke, but negative, since it alters hitpoints negatively. That's why heals are sometimes based upon DD templates in the history, since DDs and heals are the same kind of spell; one gives hitpoints and one takes them away.

For SK aggro spells attrib1 is 92 (alter hate attribute) and base1 is the value of the hate to be added. Any spell or ability that directly affects hate will have an attrib value of 92 in its raw data page.

There are still about 22 values that are labeled as "unknown" and one of those could be how much "internal" hate a spell generates. The last 6 unknown listings are for spell resist values and such during PVP and for categorization in the database.

For heals and direct damage spells, the hate they generate may be in the legacy code. Something like if a wizard lands a nuke for 1800, then X% of that value is added to that wizard's hate value on the mob's hate list. I figure the same for heals, but at a lesser percentage. Some spells don't do damage or heal damage, so hate has to be calculated a different way. I would assume an internal value is set somewhere for the line of spells and kept at that value for the entire line. From a programming perspective, that's the easiest alternative to putting in a specific calculation for each class of spells.
