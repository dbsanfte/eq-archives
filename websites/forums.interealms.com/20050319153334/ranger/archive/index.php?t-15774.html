I've seen three types of endgames:

EQ1: ever-larger groups of characters are needed to cope with the endgame mobs. This quickly causes a tradition of waiting during the organization of a raid as long as the actual raid takes.

AC2: ever-increasing character levels and mobs (via patches), and some PvP (depending on server). There basically is no endgame.

PvP MMORPG's: ever-larger groups of characters are needed to have a chance of winning against the opponent's ever-larger groups of characters. The games I've seen in this category all have caps on character level.

First, should there be further choices?

Second, is AC2's "no max level" a desirable end-result? Or perhaps EQ1's "max level but that max will be raised via expansions" is the best way to keep lower-level characters from falling too terribly far behind?

Personally, I like the "no max level" concept, but if it's a PvP game, then (being semi-blue), there should be some way to keep extremely-high-level chars from eating a steady diet of newbies.

Thoughts?
