There are 2 factors to tanking, avoidance and mitigation. Avoidance is affected by your AA's (CA, LR, PE (again, not sure if shaman get PE)), your avoidance table, your dodge skill, and some say your AGI (Originally, when EQ was in beta, there were 2 numbers that affected your AC.. Avoidance and Mitigation. Avoidance was directly affected by AGI. Verant later combined the 2 numbers into 1 to make it easier to understand) Mitigation is affected by your AA's (CS, ID, PE?), your defense skill, and your AC.

It's been proven that there are seperate avoidance tables for different classes.. (Hi monk nerf!) so if shaman and rangers are on the same avoidance table (no clue about this, never tested it) then yes, a shaman with LR5 and CA3 would be missed just as much as a ranger with the same AAs would against the same mob.

Now, you have to factor in AC though, because tanking isn't just about being missed. While AC won't affect the number of times you're missed, it will greatly affect the amount of damage you take from each hit. If you have, say 1000ac and your ranger has 1500ac, he's going to see a significant reduction in the number of times he's hit for max damage.

Shamam
hit % is pretty random, a few lucky miss can make a big difference result since sample is small,

eg do some simple statistic analysis:
test A : Stormrider vs Henora with no combat agility.
Total Dmg: 49,610 - Avg Dmg: 324
Hits: 153
Misses: 71 (Dodged: 7)
total hits = 153+71+7 = 231

Accuracy: 153/231 = 66%

Test B : Stormrider vs Henora with Combat Agility 3.
Total Dmg: 51,601 - Avg Dmg: 323
Hits: 160
Misses: 64 (Dodged: 6)
total = 160+64+6 = 230

Accuracy: 160/230 = 70%

assume it is binomial distribution
for test A :
with n1 = 231, p1 = 0.66
so mean u1 = 153 with s.d1 = Sqrt(231x0.66x0.34) = 7.2
95% confidence interval is : between 170.4 and 135.6; hence between 58.7% and 73.8 %

for test B :
n2 = 230 , p2 = 0.7
Mean u2= 160 with s.d2 = Sqrt(230x0.7x0.3) = 7.0
95% confidence interval on hits is between 160+14 and 160-14; hence between 174-146; that is 63%-75.6%

you can see the interval is pretty big which hard to compare and ; a higher sample size will reduce this error and make it easier to compare
