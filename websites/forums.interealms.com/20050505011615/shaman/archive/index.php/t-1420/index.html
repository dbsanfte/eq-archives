Azzikai
Take a look at Allakhazam's info on Scarlet Desert. The plateau is a bit high for you at 50 but there are plenty of other areas that offer nice XP and some pretty decent drops. The XP there isn't bad and you and the dog should be able to get to 51 in short order just from the mobs around the zone in :)

The only one who can decide if it is worth it is you. I play my Shaman knowing full well that I might never ding 60, getting my spells will be luck, getting an epic may never happen and being in better gear is a long shot. I've known that since I hit 48 and left my first guild. For me it has been worth it. I've had some fun, met some nice people and been entertained.

If you have a good guild and enjoy playing with them then, by all means, continue to do so. You don't have to spend 10 hours in a zone with them if that isn't the type of thing you enjoy, there is a lot of content that you can sample for a few hours with a group or two (like Howling Stones) instead. There are also quite a few zones, Scarlet Desert especially, where you can get some XP alone in a few hours and make progress towards 60.

I would wait on making any decision about the future of your Shaman until you hit 51. Getting Turgur's changes things in a big way. Sure the mobs are harder but the encounters are much easier because of that one little spell.

That said, twinking is also great fun. Every few months I go and level a twink for a bit instead of grinding towards 60 (probably why I've been 50+ for almost a year) and I never regret it.

Good luck and have fun :)

> Can anyone tell me if my relative power is going to decrease much as I move toward 60?

Yes, it will decrease hugely. When I got 49 I felt like a god, mobs were dying to me left and right even though I had no JBB. At 55 with the JBB I had to pick my fights carefully. At 59 taking on blue mobs solo without a fungi or epic is a real effort, because around level 45 mobs suddenly get a MASSIVE increase in HP and my damage output has been static.

That said, getting to 60 is still quite doable with the gear you have, since I'm pretty much there with less. My playing hours fall within 2am-7am PST so as you can imagine, I have little choice except to solo outside the weekends when I can hook up with U.S. players and actually do stuff. Despite that, I'm still gaining XP at a reasonable rate - log on, summon pet, buff pet, pull with slow, sic puppy, JBB until dead, rinse, repeat until log off.

The power upgrades aren't much, but they are something. Getting Turgur's made me feel like I was really making a MAJOR contribution to a group for the first time. Same for Celerity and Regrowth. Group buffs for raiding (and especially group regrowth) are simply great now that their effects have been fixed to be as strong as the single-target ones. I didn't think they would be either, but when you have 2 or 3 full groups to buff they turn raiding from hard work trying to keep everything timed and staggered (in addition to being the slower) into something vastly more enjoyable.

The upgrades are there, they are just mostly group-oriented ones which benefit other people. Your friends WILL appreciate the differences as you level, even if they don't give YOU more power.
