taraddar
Wisdom and mana items do not let you cast more spells over the long run. I know your gonna have to stop and think about that abit but it is true. If bob has 3k mana and john has 4k mana john can cast 1k worth of spells more and then they are both oom and have to med up.

The thing that becomes important is not how big your mana pool is but how fast you regen it.

Think of it this way. You mana is water and the amount of mana you have max is a bucket. Sure a 10 gallon drum is better than a 8 gallon container but I'd much rather have the one filled up with a fire hose than the one filled up with a garden hose when i'm putting out a fire.

For a shaman we can convert hps into mana. We can regen hps back naturally and we can augment that with spells and items too. This essentially alows use to increase our mana regen by using hps regen.

Hps and ac protect that ability to convert hps into mana. The less dmg you take when you get aggro and more hps you have the more you can convert into mana.

Now there are some times when mana pool can be important but if you regularly go from fm to oom with out a chance to med then your probably fighting something too tough.

Hps become especially important when you get to 59+ and can get the cann aa skill that converts 1924hps into mana in one big hunk. Thats alot easier and safer if you have more hps.

Everyone has to find thier own balance point for where they are comfortable. One thing you might consider is a shadel bandit ring instead of a velium fire opal. Adds almost as much hps and a descent amount of mana along with some resists too.

There are other common peices that provide good hps and wis/mana.
