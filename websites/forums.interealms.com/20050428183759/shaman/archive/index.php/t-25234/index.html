Zaviax McCleo
EQ 2 is actually nothing like EQ 1. It seems almost everything WoW players like about WoW is available in EQ 2 with the exception of PvP.

Soloing, great tradeskilling system, tons of quests, both have it. Though EQ 2 is more demanding for system requirements.

A lot of people will bash EQ 2 for the sake of it being Sony's new game. Which can easily be understood given how much they let EQ 1 go to the crapper, but it's a new team working the game and with some serious competition out there now they've really been on their feet. When problems rise up, they fix it quickly. I'm still getting used to it as I often find myself searching for news if Hell has frozen over.

In short, neither are better. Both are great choices. You just need to choose yourself. If you're into PvP or want to enjoy an entirely new world, the choice is easy, go WoW. If you like the lore from EQ1 and have a great system, EQ 2 is a great option.

As for dieing by tradesklling in EQ 2, that's only in theory. I've never seen it happen myself. ;)

I saw what eq2 looks like the other day at a friend's place.
The graphics quality of the characters and textures and stuff I think is far superior in eq2.
The way they do stuff like shadows properly mapping onto other players armor when you pass in front of them is big.
Stuff like rocks actually look like rocks as opposed to rock shaped 3d objects with rock textures painted on to resemble a rock.

I talk to a coworker about the similarities and differences between the 2 games, and when it comes to tradeskill interfaces, quest interfaces, and stuff like that, the two games are the same. The UI is very very similar and you can tell there was a lot of borrowing of ideas from one another.

I haven't seen much of the storyline in eq2 or heard about it. I don't even know if there is much storyline. The quests that I've had for the Tauren shaman though are amazingly well written. The quests link into each other in meaningful ways. The areas you hunt in are logicly laid out for a good progression when levelling and questing. I had more storyline happening in WoW in about 1 hour of gameplay than I had in total of all of eq. The reasons why you are hunting X creature for this npc make sense. For example, this goblin in Booty Bay is a leatherworker making shoes. He wants you to go out and do multiple quests for him hunting different creatures for their skins and other item drops. Once you finally get the different type of drops that make sense as to why he'd want them, he thanks you, and says he's got some leftover materials to make you a new set of boots too. This makes a hell of lot more sense than random X npc saying "I hate badgers! Go kill some for me and bring me their tails!" or whatever. The progression of jungle animal hunting in Stranglethorn vale is another good example, where there's a hunting expedition stationed along the river asking you to join up with their hunt and find certain creatures in the area, going up in level each quest, and having to hunt down big nasty named mobs.

For me though, I'll never be trying eq2. I vowed long ago to never ever buy another SOE game because of how they refused to learn how to properly patch and put out expansions for eq. Wow has been very fun and I'll be sticking with it for my main entertainment.

Here's my 2 cents for what it's worth. No need to flame if you disagree, that's cool if you have a different opinion, no big deal.

WoW is surprisingly more like early EQ1 than EQ2 is. Early EQ1 had this sense of many different moods and flavors to zones, of a lot of in jokes and humor, stylized graphics that were simple but effective. WoW has all that, it does a good job of bringing back that early EQ1 magic in an updated form. If you miss those days and cherish them, then this is the game for you.

EQ2 is more of a next generation mmorpg, it has more advanced graphics, tradeskilling and a few other things including wide use by NPCs of talking to the point where when you play EQ1 and WoW the game seems oddly silent by comparison. If you want the latest best type of mmorpg, in the sense of feeling cutting edge, then this game is probably more for you.

They are both good games, you can't really go wrong with either.
