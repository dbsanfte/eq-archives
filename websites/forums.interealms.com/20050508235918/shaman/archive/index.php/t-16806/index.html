Jinheim
A few days ago, I posted this thread: http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=16768 . At the time, I still wasn't sure if VP was a worthwhile AA or not (at 270AA). No one really answered my question, so I decided to just bite the bullet and train VP to rank 3. Dinged VP earlier today, but didn't really have a chance to test it until tonight (was grouped the whole time and its considered bad form to randomly root mobs in a kite group :D).

Here's what I found: VP owns. A lot. I seriously regret not getting it a long, long, time ago. I have only gotten to play with it for one night, but so far it is looking to be one of the best AA's I have ever bought. The thing I really wanted to know was how big the knock is. Well, here it is:

http://home.comcast.net/~misterconservative/before.JPG[/img]

http://home.comcast.net/~misterconservative/after.JPG

As you can see, that is a pretty big fooking knockback. Even VP1 was knocking mobs out of melee range, and VP3 knocks them [i]way back. You might also notice that the mob isn't being knocked straight back away from me. You can turn your body to face a certain direction, and the mob will be booted in that direction. Typcially you are looking at the mob when you cast, so it knocks it backwards, but you can manipulate it so that it goes in whatever direction you want. This is awesome. Another note on the knockback: It seems to have some rather... dramatic... effects in pvp. Note the following screenshot, where a 53 monk jumped me in arena. This was early today, when all I had was VP1.

http://home.comcast.net/~misterconservative/knockback.JPG[/img]

That's [i]insane. It happened more than once, too. Typically VP was just doing a small knockback, only slightly out of melee range, but sometimes it would just knock them way back until they hit a wall. I knocked a 63 wizard through the gate and clear out of the arena.

In addition to the awesome knockback effect, I found something else. When soloing in pofire at least, if you pull with malicious decay, VP does not break. I did an entire AA point there, which means 8 kills and 5 VP's per kill. In those 40 VP's, not a single one broke before VP refreshed. I didn't get hit a single time. After soloing these things using greater immobilize and having it sometimes break a few seconds after applying it (often killing me), this is just simply incredible. It makes them a total joke for me to solo. Used to, before I got VP, I wouldn't even try them unless I had virtue and kei. I needed virtue because I just didn't have the hitpoints to survive a broken root and an interrupt when trying to stick it. I needed the kei because I would constantly be having to /dismount and run my ass away to get enough distance to safely cast root, and then heal myself from the damage. I had trouble maintaining mana. With VP, though, both these concerns are completely taken care of. I repeat: I didn't get hit a single time.

So, to sum it up, VP is freaking awesome and if you solo you should get it.
