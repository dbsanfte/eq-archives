Absolutely depends on your budget restraints. If money is no object and you don't want to build it yourself, I recommend www.alienware.com. They build some AMAZING high end systems that'll be solid for a long time.

If money is an object and you are going to build it yourself, www.computershopper.com is an excellent link as well as www.tomshardware.com -- Tom's is an evaluation and information site. You can find out basically anything you want from it.

To give you an example, due to my recent PC demise (which at this point is still an elusive issue), I'm in the midst of building a new machine. This is the basic configuration I'm looking at:

an ATX case from www.colorcases.com (not sure which one)
EPOX 8KTA3 VIA motherboard ($125 ish)
Palomino XP 1.7PR AMD Socket A ($175 ish) -- about 1.4 GHz
Thermal Dragon Cooling Fan ($25 ish)
2x SD PC100/133 512 MB RAM sticks ($65 each)
PC GeForce 3 (not the Titanium version) ($275 ish)
floppy drive, mouse and keyboard ($50 total)
2x 20GB Western Digital 7200 RPM HDs ($90 ish each)
3COM 3C905C-TX NIC card ($50)
Soundblaster 5.1 ($50)
CDRW 24/10/40 ($100)
300Watt Power supply ($50)

Basically, I went with the lesser known EPOX motherboard because a friend uses it and it runs amazingly well. The weakness of the motherboard is that it only has two RAM slots. Hence the reason, I went out and bought 2 512MB sticks.

I avoided the Titanium version of the Geforce 3 card because of the benchmarks Tom's Hardware has done with it and the original version. They were unimpressed and, based on the results of those tests, so was I. It's a personal preference on the chip; but, the case I'm buying from www.colorcases.com will be an overclockers case in the event I decide to "dial-up" the Palomino chip. Supposedly the Palomino can get to 2GHz. Always thought a machine running that fast would be fun to play with.

Anyway, I just wanted to give you an example of what I'm building. It may or may not fit your needs or desires; but, hopefully it'll help a bit.

-Quinn

Athlon XP outperforms the P4 on almost every test and costs about 1/3 as much. Athlon also uses DDR SDRAM which is about ½ the price of RDRAM that most P4 systems use. I currently have an Asus A7M266 motherboard with 256MB (upgrading RAM this weekend thank to VI) with a 1.4GHz Athlon and it rocks.

If Price is not an issue I would go with this config.

Case from colorcase.com with 300w Powersupply
Asus A7M266 Motherboard
Athlon XP 1900+ Retail processor
1 gig of PC-2100 DDR SDRAM (2 512 mb Micron lifetime warranty modules) (to be ready for Verant's next upgrade that will start at 512mb Ram LOL)
IBM deskstar 40gb or larger DMA 100 ATA Hard drive
SoundBlaster audigy 5.1 sound card
Klipsch promedia 5.1 speakers from www.klipsch.com (I have the 4.1's and they ROCK!) lots o'cash but worth it, and TXH certified too.
Nv 25 geforce 4 video card. Coming out in a month or so. Gona have a lot of VooDoo tech in it from what I hear.

NEC MultiSync LCD1830 (Black Cabinet) Flat panel monitor. If you don't like flat panels or don't want to spend the $1200 for one, go with a nice NEC, ViewSonic, Sony or other high quality CRT monitor.
Plextor 12/10/32 CD-RW drive
Toshiba DVD Drive
LS-120 drive (reads LS 120 (120 mb disks) and floppy disks.

Total cost, around $3,000

www.Alienware.com or www.xdream-machines.com both have sweet systems you can configure. They use retail parts for most of their stuff and optimize them for gamers so I would go with one of them.
