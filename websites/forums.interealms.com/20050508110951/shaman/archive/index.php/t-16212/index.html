Last time I completed an adventure I got 51 points for normal (might still be tweaked before launch) on a 65 adventure. Ammount is based on levels and difficulty. Never got into a raid so can't help you there.

I didn't have too much time to spend and most points I ever got on a single character was 200 which was enough to buy some low level gear and agmentations. Heard 500 starts to get some pretty sweet stuff.

One thing I have seen so far is all existing items that can be upgraded have a single type 1 expansion slot. So far I've only seen slot 1 drop and non that can be bought making per-LDoN gear harder to upgrade.

The levels themselves are very nice looking but all have a cookie cuter feel and after a few times through each type you'll feel like you've seen it all. The new models are absolutely terrific and definately top notch work from the art team. Wish they could do some of that magic on the poor looking character models. Skelly's are getting an upgrade including PC pets. Bearform next please m'k.

As far as the time limits I really was unhappy with them. They do make you optimize your group considerably. You always feel rushed and as time starts running out some people tend to hurry and make stupid mistakes. Pullers are almost pointless since you don't have much time to split, you're better off bringing everything and using CC (feel so sorry for monks, they basically obsolete now). One death can sometimes kill your chances if you're not moving fast enough and unless the cleric bound right outside one wipe and you might as well start over. Good new is CR's are easy due to lack of repops.

Sometimes you'd get lucky and find the rescue target early and be done in twenty minutes or you could take a wrong turn at the start of the dungeon and end up wasting 3/4 of your time going the wrong way. Same problem with the collect item missions, sometimes you could get your items fast and sometimes even with a high kill rate you'd never find enough. My normal group of 5 (sham, cle, mag, enc, monk all 65 except monk which is 62) had trouble completing a lot of the missions due to lack of DPS unless we got lucky with no wrong turns or item drops.

The assassination missions work a bit differently. The target isn't up when you enter the dungeon. You have to kill so many mobs before he spawns and then you have to find him. Faster you kill the mobs the faster the name spawns and the more time you have to get to him. Even if you don't have a tracker they recently added occasional messages "you hear a commotion off in such and such direction"

I am really happy with the bane spell line. It will be very nice in exp groups. I'd place the agro a bit below our poison line as I wasn't always pulling agro off the monk (about 50% maybe) with it where I will 99% of the time with blood. With the mage bracer it can hit for over 500, only drawback is a 25 second wait if it is resisted. It will be going into my normal spell line up.

Exp for the adventures isn't as good as you get grinding out AA in BoT or Tactics, but was enough and the ability to earn points and other rewards is very nice. I would have liked to have seen it about equal to BoT exp though, no reason not to be able to have fun and get exp at the same time.

The new alchemy recipies I found are a complete joke. Group SoW, Group EB, and Group Lev potions that according to the book have lessened effects. Didn't actually find the components to make these.

Overall its not all I expected when I first heard about it, but I still had a lot of fun and did preorder it on all of my accounts. It will be great for something to do other then exp grind, can always grab a few friends and know theres something for you to do other then jump around 3 or 4 zones looking for a spot to exp. I would recommend anyone that plays with a regular group and likes to do things other then grind out another AA in BoT to buy this expansion.

I have mixed feelings about that expansion.

Time-limit

The absolutely worst thing they came up with is the time limit. The time is running as soon as the first player of a group entered the dungeon.

IMHO there is no frecken way that a L 65 group with less than 6 people can be successful in this time. I was in a group with 5 time-equipped players on an assassination mission (find named and kill him) and we had only 5 minutes left (of 90 minutes) when we found and killed him. And we killed fast and nonstop. And this was on normal difficulty level. This was no dungeon crawl, this was a dungeon run.
With that time limit there is not much room for afk-for-bathroom/doorbell/spouse, not much room also for someone dieing/rezzing, and no room for a DPS-suboptimal group.

In hindsight i would not have preordered Ldon because of that time-limit-feature. It prevents good friends to crawl together through a dungeon and it forces you again into those DPS-optimalized cookie-cutter group-compositions.

Layout
Also the dungeons i crawled so far had a pretty dull layout. All rooms and alleys had like 90 degree-angles; rarely there were rooms with more than 2 exits and everyone of them were at the north west east or south wall. The people responsible for that should take a look at grand layouts of dungeons like CT, DN, Sebilis, Guk, SolB.


Models, Architecture
The monster-models i saw were top notch; great look and great animations! Also the interiors of the dungeons looked very well.

Loot
L 65 sight: PP was in the range of 1 gp-3 pp per mob. They also drop random cash items, tradeskill things, very rarely augmentations (one per 40 minutes), and also rarely armour pieces (i found some pants which were slightly better than my Tae Ew Chain legs).
The quality of the augmentations was low; so far i only saw things like plus 6 to Str or Resist disease or resist cold.


XP
XP was around 3 percent AXP per 4 mobs killed which was okay imho. If you want better XPs then you should head to BoT.


Tradeskills
Alchemy, Smithing, Fletching, Baking and Brewing recipes were introduced. I took a look at Smithing and Alchemy.

There is a book with 3 new potions: Group sow (Pack Spirit), Group Levitation, Group EB.
Every potion requires one drop of an Ldon dungeon (e.g. Pondweed) and several other components.
I made a group sow potion; the cost for the ingreds was at around 60-70 pp. Whoever is responsible at Verant for this part of shamanistic skill-content handling should be fired asap.

There are also some new smithy recipes. I made several uninteresting Chain pieces; Chest had AC 27, Resist Dis 6; Legs had AC 9.
No idea who would wear that.



Conclusion
As soon as that unnecessary and artificial time limit is removed i would recommend everyone to buy the expansion. But in its current state the casual players should stay away from it.

I would reccomend for a true guide on them you go to sony's main pages, but what I recall of it is this:

All gear now has at least 1 Augment slot on it.

The Augment slots will have specific Types allowed. Basically the higher the allowed type, the better the augment you can make.

Type 1 augments will augment a SINGLE stat or resist, by 5 or 6 points.

Higher type augments may have effects, or multiple stats.

You can add augmentation to armor by combining armor + augment gem in a special container.

You can remove augment by placing armor into the container alone and combining. Augmentation that is removed is LOST, not returned.


Once augmented, the armor description will state what item is augmenting it, and you can click on the name to get a seperate window detailing the stats from augmentation.


The special container for augmentation is 2 slot, which leads me to think that you will only ever see single augment slots. However, the discussion of augments made it sound like some armor could handle multiple augmentation, so maybe you just do them one at a time.
