Delete, start over...level 2 is just finishing the tutorial, Glori :P

With the possible exception of scrappers, it is REALLY DIFFICULT to gimp your character beyond all redemption. About the only way to do it is to take large number of pool powers, as they tend to be nowhere near as good as primary/secondary abilities. This is one of the nice things about the game, that almost any combination of powers can work.

For the shaman duplication, definately controller or defender would be the choices. With so many different skillsets, there are a lot of ways to go. A forcefield defender plays WAY different than an empathy defender. The descriptions for the abilities are typically pretty useful, and give you an idea of what that power set will give you. The closest I can come to a shaman would be an Ice/Empathy Controller, or a Defender with either Kinetics or Radiation primary and Dark secondary.

As far as Magic/Tech/Science/Mutation/Natural, there's really no difference. These are primarily roleplaying tools. They do have *some* influence over the initial missions you receive and what enemies your contacts will have you fight, but after level 7 or so, you won't notice. They also affect which Enhancements you can use.

Enhancements are a little more complicated. Think of them as items. They *can* be sold, to the red and white field training heroes that are near most of the trainers in the city zones (Miss. Liberty and the Back Street Brawler, for example). They are a good supplementary source of Influence, which you'll need once you get to level 12 and can start buying dual-origin enhancements (which cost a fortune).

Enhancements come in 3 varieties, Training, Dual-Origin, and Single Origin. Training can be used by anyone. Dual-Origin can be used only by people in one of the two origins they cover. There are 5 types of Dual Origin...Magic/Natural, Magic/Mutant, Natural/Technology, Mutant/Science, and Science/Technology. Each origin type is represented twice, and shares with another similar types. Then the single origins (5 again) are only useable by people in that specific origin.

The basic mantra with enhancement types is:
Training is always better than nothing.
Dual Origin is always better than Training.
Single Origin is always better than Dual.

That of course comes with a disclaimer...red enhancements provide NO BONUS. So a green Training is going to be better than a red single origin, because the red one is not doing anything.

Ideally you want to keep your enhancements above your level if possible. You can use enhancements up to 3 levels higher than your security level, and enhancements 4 or lower become red and ineffective.

A 5++ enhancement functions the same as a 7 enhancement. A 7+ enhancement functions the same as an 8 enhancement. A level 40 training enhancement provides the EXACT SAME BENEFIT to a level 40 character as a level 1 enhancement does to a level 1 character.

As a rule, Training enhancements provide +8% bonus, Duals are +16%, and Singles are +32%. Specific enhancements can vary (example, +range enhancements for distance powers are 5/10/20%), but most follow that rule.

When you get an enhancement higher than your level, it gets a bonus. When it is lower it recieves a small penalty. It is about 1% per level. Not something you are generally going to notice a great deal. A training enhancement 3 levels above you is giving about an 11% bonus. A dual-origin 3 levels below you is giving 13% (thus, why duals are better than training).

If you have a level 5 damage enhancement, and pick up another one from a kill, you can go to the enhancement screen and combine them into a 5+ enhancement (effectively making it level 6, and more effective). This can be done up to twice to make something ++. When you do the combine, there's a chance it will fail. Worst case, the lower level enhancements gets booted. If enhancements are the same level, it never fails. Every level step they are different, is a 5% chance of failure on a combine. Once you get an enhancement up to ++, the game will only allow you to combine it with something higher. A 5++ enhancement can't be combined with anything less than an 8, which will result in an 8+.

I think that covers about all of it for enhancements...

For the slots, this is where some of the greater flexibility comes into play. In the current level 40 cap, you get a grand total of 72 enhancement slots (23 powers which each come with 1, and 49 by leveling up), so you have quite a few to play with. Many skills (like brawl, rest, sprint) should never get anything but their initial 1 slot...more is wasteful. Any one power can have up to 6 slots.

Care needs to be exercised with putting in slots, because even though you do get a lot, you do not get an infinite amount, and currently there's no way to change them once you've placed them. Don't be afraid to put lots of slots into your beginning powers. Those are the "bread and butter" abilities, and unlike low level EQ spells, do NOT become obsolete with age.

How you put enhancements into a power can greatly effect how that power works. If you put 6 damage enhancements into a power, it will do a big old sh*tload of damage when it hits...but that specialization is coming at a cost. Someone with a +accuracy, -endurance cost, -recycle time, 3x damage setup is going to hit more, use less endurance to activate, and activate the power more frequently. But they won't hit as hard. It is a playstyle issue more than anything else. You can't really place enhancements wrong, because you can replace them any time you want by dropping a different one into the slot.

You start with 2 powers, and get to choose a new power at every even level up to 32, when it switches to every 3 levels (so, 35, 38...and when the level cap goes up, 41/44/47/50). Picking one bad power is not going to destroy you :P

You are best at your primary powers. Some of the archetypes overlap (especially controllers/defenders and defenders/blasters), an empathy defender is going to heal better than a controllers with empathy secondary. An electricity blaster is going to nuke harder than an electricity secondary defender. When in doubt, pick a primary ability, it is what your archetype is best at. But don't ignore secondary powers, they can be quite good.

Power Pools are available to everyone. They tend to be catch-all abilities that don't strictly fit into other power sets (like flight...lots of "real" heroes can fly by various means that aren't strictly related to their other abilities). You can start taking them at level 6. The first 2 listed powers in each pool become available at 6. The third power requires A) Level 14, and B) Having picked one of the first 2. The 4th power requires A) Level 20, and B) Having picked any 2 of the first three.

So, if you take Hover at level 6, you can take Fly at 14, and then Group Fly at 20. Or you could take Air Superiority at 6, Hover at 8, and Group Fly at 20.

You can have a total of 4 different pools. So if you went Hover, Hasten, Combat Jumping, and Heal Other, that is 4 pools, and you could not take (for example) Stealth. That gives you a maximum of 16 power pools possible to purchase (4 pools, 4 powers each). Course, with only 20 abilities to pick, that leaves you with only 4 out of your prim/sec abilities, and I can't really see a character built like that being too great. There might be a way though, and the game is flexible enough that it might be possible.

Each power you choose intially has one slot that you can put enhancements into. You can only put an enhancement into a slot that remains lighted (if full) or green (if empty) when you left click on the enhancment. There are vendors near the trainers and your initial contacts that will purchase enhancementst you can't use. They are dressed in white and red outfits. In Galaxy City, there is one immediately inside the door as you come into the building where you meet your first contact. She will buy back any enhancements you have. If I'm not mistaken, there is also a vendor to the right of Back Street Brawler, the trainer in front of the building in Galaxy City. Similar trainers are available in Atlas Park. From a distance, you might mistake them for player characters. So if you get more than 10 enhancements, simply sell them to one of these vendors. If you're still having trouble finding them, PM me and I'll make a character on your server to show you where to go.

What about the extra slots you can add to powers? How do you decide where to put it?

Most powers will accept more than one kind of enhancement. For example, a damage dealing power will accept accuracy enhancements (light yellow), damage enhancements (red), etc. So if you're missing a lot with a damage power, add an accuracy enhancement. If you want more damage, add the damage one. But maybe you find you want both, better accuracy and more damage. Then when you get new slots, put a second one on the power that you want both accuracy and damage enhancements for.
