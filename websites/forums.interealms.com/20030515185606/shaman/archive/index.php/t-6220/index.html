There is a little issue that has always somewhat bothered me regarding shamen velious quest armor effects versus other wisdom caster velious armor effects. To illustrate my point I won't even go into the lesser armor pieces, I only want to discuss the respective breastplate effects.

Universal velious quest/pog breastplate effects:

Cleric: Mark of Karn, level 56, 125 mana

Shaman: Rightous Health, level 54, 150 mana

Druid: Regrowth of the Grove, level 58, 600 mana


From mana cost of these spells, it would seem that clerics get the shaft, followed closely by shamen. But, if one thinks about it, not only is the cleric BP effect a higher level spell, but a spell that must be cast once per mob, minimum, therefor creating a mana-conservation-over-casts kind of effect. Now Druids, however, get a breastplate with an effect that replaces a GROUP effect spell costing 600 mana, an effect that must be recast every fifteen minutes. Finally, shamen recieve on their breastplates an effect that replaces a 150 mana, single target spell that lasts over an hour. Doing a little math by reasoning, who's left behind here? Logically, all the breastplates have very equivalent stats (disregarding druid's cause, hey, its leather), so why not have logically equally useful effects?

Obviously this subject is a little behind the times, considering that the armor sets in question came out two expansions ago, but I think the point has validity as long as the armor is still in wide use, which it is. Forgive me if i'm bringing up an old/dead subject already discussed here, I just wanted to hear what anyone else might think about this . . . inequality? Heh.
