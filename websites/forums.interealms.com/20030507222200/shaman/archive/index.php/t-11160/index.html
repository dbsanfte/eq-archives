Jaruden
From what I've seen they're very similar to the regular wings, but the mobs are a little tougher. And there seem to be more spellcasters (if that's possible). I've never seen tower mobs drop any more SP's than wings (though I've spent a lot less time up there).

I believe there are named mobs that are pretty buff in each tower, on several day respawns. 2-3 group thing I think.

For the most part look at it as 4 additional wings, that are a little harder to split and pull. I thought the exp was a little slower, due to the extra care you have to take when pulling. If you're looking for exp, stick with the wings. If they're all crowded up, the towers aren't too bad though.

Dissonant
The towers are basically holding tanks for the Named mobs. These aren't a good place to grind if you want to be efficient. Mobs give the same xp as wing mobs and they quite a bit tougher. To do a comparisson, each wing has a couple Kriger's that spawn. These guys are considerably harder than the other giants. Now imagine every mob is a kriger and some are cleric's and druids.

Each tower contains 1 boss mob and 1 mini-boss. The mini-bosses tend to drop nice earrings, decent weapons as well as the occasional SP. The boss mobs have some really nice loot table but tend to be pretty stingy. Each boss mob drops a symbol or torden(10 slot container). I can't tell you how many times we've dropped the named only to get a symbol, or symbol and SP... hardly worth our time. But, they do drop Runes and when they drop loot it's very nice.

If I can give you 1 piece of advice.... avoid the sandstorm tower. Skygge's suck!!!!!!!! They can cut through an 8k tank like butter, and they're just typical mobs.

For most of the bosses I would say prolly 3 groups for your first attempt, and after that you could prolly cut it down to 2. We single group most, but that's l33t str4ts :p

Also, the bosses are on a 6 hour timer. Usually very punctial, but if they're more then 5 minutes late, they won't be up till the 7 hour mark(atleast that's the pattern we've noticed)

If you've spent a lot of time in the rest of BoT, you've probably found the few places where the pathing is ... inspired. Well, the pathing in the Towers is even more entertaining. Those giants clearly spent years training with the goblins of Droga to perfect their "why the hell did we get aggro" and "where IS that giant going" techniques.

Worst offender is the wind tower. Imagine a series of small (and I do mean small) platforms connected by ladders that curve around and make it really easy for you to fall. Imagine that falling is death. Imagine that the mobs can fall, too, leading to amusing summoning death. Its got all the worst features of Laef's wing plus a lot of the annoyances of Plane of Sky. At least there are no annoying keys (well, except the one to get in ... and the Agnarr key. Hmm ... maybe it *is* the child of PoSky.)
