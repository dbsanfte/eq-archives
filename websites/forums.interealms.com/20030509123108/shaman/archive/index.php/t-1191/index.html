taraddar
Rak's ae is pretty small range. Last time we did him for another shaman in the guild we did him in the room just past the first locked door and even on the same wall you didn't have to be on opposite ends of it to avoid the ae.

As far as the actual fight goes the best thing to do is pull him all the way out to the arches where you have lots of room and no spawns to worry about. When he spawns he should be non kos to everyone and make sure they do not attack him. Have the mages come up and park thier pets right next to him. When you hand the tear in wait until he ae's which should aggro the pets and then back off. You can then get healed up safely. It's usually useful to have the pets buffed with at least hero and talisman first to give them some extra hps. Then just sit back and watch the show. Once he gets down to 30% or so we usually have an enchanter start seiving to make sure he has no mana to gate with and the tank charge if they are bored and don't mind dying =)
