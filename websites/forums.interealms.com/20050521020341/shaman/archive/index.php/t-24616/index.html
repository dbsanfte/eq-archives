What's really cool is that scientists take measurements while the eclipse is only partial, so they can see the effects of particulate in Earth's atmosphere. Volcanos going off etc, would make the edge of the shadow blurry and give it a faint yellow color.

The color you're seeing when the moon is fully in the Earth's shadow is completely due to the effect of the atmosphere bending the light. If we had no atmosphere, then the light that reached the moon due to gravity etc, wouldn't be enough so you could see the moon at all (if you could see, standing on a planet with no air :) )
