There are as many answers as there are Shaman. Here's just one. After level 24, when you get the cannibalize spell, you never get to rest. If your mana's not full and you have some reasonably safe amount of hit points, you're canni-dancing (cast canni, sit briefly, stand, cast canni, sit briefly...seemingly forever).

When you have mana, you're keeping dexterity (e.g. spirit of monkey), stamina (e.g. spirit of ox) and agility (e.g. spirit of cat) on anyone meleing. You might also be keeping fleeting fury on anyone using a weapon that procs. You're also buffing yourself at the same time so you can tell when to rebuff others. You're keeping regeneration on yourself and anyone who even might get hit, unless someone else is doing that already. You're healing and rooting when necessary, especially if nobody's snaring. You're slowing and/or debuffing if the fights are long enough and nuking if you have spare mana. If everybody's twinked and/or has temperance, then you're probably mostly nuking and canni-dancing.

If you're not the healer, or you're with more than one, you can work out with them when they should heal you after you cannibalize your health down far enough to make full use of whatever heal spell they're using. If you're in a group that's amenable, you're jumping into the mele now and then to get your defense, offense, and weapons skills up. You want really fast weapons, regardless of their damage, for that purpose.

The only times you ever get to rest are if the group's pulling slower than necessary, you have excess healers, or your health and mana are both dangerously low.

As for soloing, there are lots of methods. Probably the simplest is to either root the MoB in place, or pull with slow (drowsy) or debuff (malaise) then root and move out of mele range. Then stack on your damage over time (DoT) spells and canni-dance until root breaks or a DoT wears off and continue until you're sure of success, then practice meleing. Once you get a pet it gets more fun. If you want to remove the challenge, level faster, or plow through yellows and lower reds, get temperance.

Weyalan
Group environment:
Haste buff dps classes
Hp buff only the MT
Sit on your kilted butt all the time and heal as necessary
Once you hit level 24, regen on self all the time. Make a Cannidance hotkey and use it all the time

Solo Environment:
Until you hit 24, don't bother. (back in the day, I got smacked around by light blues until I dinged 24, then I went straight out and soloed a yellow)

Level 24 - 33:- Root 'n Rot (max your hp / mana pools)
Pull with Maolsi (from max range) (if you run away while spell gems refresh, you should be able to get next spell off before mob gets to you)
Root mob
Cast disease DoT
Cast poison DoT
Reapply root as necessary (whenever you re-root the mob, move to maximum spell casting range)
Reapply DoTs as necessary
Cannidance wherever possible
Use "flash of light" if you need to buy some time
Run around a lot if you need to regen some hp / canni some mana - SoW is faster than mobs

Level 34 onwards - Tank 'n Pet (wear your best AC gear and buff your dex). Buff self with hp, str, dex, haste, sta, SoW, regen
Make pet, give pet 2 rusty weapons, buff pet with hp and haste
Pull with malosi
Send pet (pet will take aggro)
Slow mob (you will get aggro)
Move into melee range and tank mob while pet bites its ankles
Back away and let pet get aggro if you need to heal yourself
Move back in to melee if pet hp get low
Tank n pet is great for getting your defence skills and weapon skill up
You can also cast a DoT if you want to kill mobs faster, but Pet n Tank, IMO works best without - you save the mana for heals and reduce downtime, and you are doing to max weapon / offense / defence skills anyway
