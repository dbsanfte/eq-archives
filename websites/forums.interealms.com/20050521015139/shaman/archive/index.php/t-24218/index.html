Ssti
Loved playing one during the stress test. Check the sig to see what bear form looks like. At the time I managed to get to 20th and had access to bear form (warrior) Evil seal form (water travel) Lion form (rogue).

You can't use spells or items while changed so it's a choice you make during the fight if you want to cast spells or not. However shape shifts were instant cast and uninterruptable so you can shift from one shape to another as needed (mana cost was pretty steep at low levels though). However you have to be carefull since you're more vulnerable in some forms and a poorly timed shapechange will get you killed or leave you oom.

The way solo fights usually went was front load a few damage spells (nuke, dot), Toss up a Heal over time and damage shield on yourself (also instant cast), change to bear form, start kicking ass and taking names. You regain mana while shape changed so you're gtg when you change back.

In groups, As usual everyone loves a good healer, but if there's already a shaman, priest or other druid present you can simply shift to whatever other form is needed. I've been in groups with a shaman healer, a warrior tanking and 2 druid kitties pouncing on everything. It was great. Want to group with another druid ? One tanks, the other heals and voila, you're in buisness.

The way warrior (bear) worked was with rage. You start the fight with an empty rage bar. Fighting, getting hit, and some special abilities generate rage. You then use that rage to fuel things like taunts, extra damagy attacks and such.

The rogue (Lion looking cat) worked with stealth. You start with a full bar of stealth and use that to fuel special attacks. Some special attacks give combo points which unlock other more powerfull attacks for you to follow up with and also determine damage on some even more powerfull attacks.

The cat form also had a stealth mode (invisibility) where you creep along belly to the ground. Near as I can tell it drastically reduced your aggro radius so you could walk through a crowd without being detected. however if you get too close to a mob, you can still get whupped.

The water form was basically a waterbreathing / underwater sow, not for fighting, it's a travel form. It looks like some type of grinning walrus or seal. I tried swimming across the sea when I got it but drowned after becoming exhausted :D. That's how they enforce the off coast limits. Get too far from land, you start getting fatigued, and eventually die if you don't turn back. It will definitely come in handy though. A large part of the continents are accessible from the sea so you can swim to speed up travel and bypass dangerous areas. And the underwater decor was easily as beautifull as anything on land.

On the last days, I was invited on a kind of raid into the human lands, to get there we swam along the coast to bypass some of the much higher suicide level zones (we were levels 20 - 30 ish). Like 50 people swimming along with a few seals thrown in for comic relief.

Unfortunately I didn't get to try the cheetah travel form as it was much higher level.

Shaman had their spirit wolf travel form which allowed them to run fast and go invisible. Came in very handy when wiping in a dungeon. He'd run back as a spirit wolf and rez us back in where we got killed.

Much fun.
