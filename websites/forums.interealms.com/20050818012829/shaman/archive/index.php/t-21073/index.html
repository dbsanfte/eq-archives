Which means, if you want to get into high level GoD, you would want an alternative to going through Plane of Time or, by extrapolation, any zone or series of zones that required flags that are hard to get.

I'm not a fan of flags, myself. You didn't need any flags to get into Guk, or HoT or NTOV, or Ssra, or Kael. The keys to get into Sebilis and HS were pretty trivial. And those expansions are still probably regarded by a lot of people who actually were around back then as the high points of EQ's game design.

The game has put in place starting with PoP huge roadblocks in the form of flags which cannot be done without large numbers of people. The keys you needed to get to sleepers or veeshan's peak or HS or Sebilis, contrarily, were individual efforts or could be done by smallish numbers. The game became Raid or Die, and not just for goodies -- for the permission to even get near the goodies. And there are alternatives; the same ones that worked in the past.

It would be nice if you could slowly work your way up to high level gear and spells and abilities the old fashioned way again, just with you and some friends, or you and a fairly casual guild. Doing everything 72 people at a time and more can be a stultifying, uncreative routine.

It would be nice if some creativity came back into the game such that there was really more than one way to play without getting left so distantly behind.

And gear inflation ratcheted up so insanely in PoP as a result of those flags, that the disparity between the hard core and the people who just want to play a game at their own pace with other people of like mind has become way too glaring, making people more unhappy than they have to be. People play the game to get improvement, but they don't need to get gear that diverges insanely in utility from one expansion to the next. Ornate gear having 100 hitpoints less than elemental gear is swell enough for the elemental folks, but really, they would have been absolutely thrilled with far, far smaller leaps too.

The game has become near incoherent because it's hard to design for, say, warriors that are both 6k and 12k at the same time. The bright glittery candy of upgrading 100 hp at a time may enrapture players like shiny car keys amaze your cat, but really, games like this just need to give people the feeling that they are progressing while having challenges, not that they are hitting the lottery. And when nobody is hitting the lottery, the vast majority of players who come nowhere near the lottery don't feel like they are so impossibly far behind they will never conceivably catch up. Slow and steady wins the race would be a far better conception of how you keep players going and interested in gear and playing than making some strike it rich and the others go suck an egg.

Even if a lack of understanding makes Sony unbalance their game by creating a gaping disparity between the equipment levels of even the dumbest hard core raider and the cleverest player who doesn't want to be part of a 72-player zergfest every night, they could still not compound the problems for the casual gamer by forcing them to either accept a single style of gameplay or accept no real advancement at all, even including the ability to see just for a moment the majority of zones in expansions they pay for. Sony could make alternative ways to do things, like they did with the quests that let you get into tactics for experience, and COD, etc. Or they could just add a lot more content into the game so the casual player could finally play just as big a game as the hard core raider. Or so the hard core player who didn't want to get wrapped up in a hard core guild could just do things with small groups of friends, people met at random.

My way or the highway is kind of the way things stand now. Yes, you won't get into high end GoD if you haven't been through plane of time first, or if you do, you won't have the gear to survive there anyway. It would be nice if people would buy the game and find that it was really like stepping into a big wide world with many ways of doing things and plenty of rewards for pretty much all of them, depending on how hard you worked and how clever you were, instead of being funneled down a narrow chute if you wanted to get anywhere at all.

There definitely should be more than one way to do things in a game. It being a game, and all.
