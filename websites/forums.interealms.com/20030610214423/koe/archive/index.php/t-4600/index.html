Hoarmurath
I was there at midnight as well. I consider myself quite the Tolkien purist, and had very high expectations for the movie. I am glad to say that I was only slightly disappointed on two occasions, and those were almost completely forgotten in comparison to what the film "got right."

I was worried about what all would be cut from the original books, and also worried about some of the things that Peter Jackson was planning on "adding" to the story. I was pleasantly suprised to find that I didn't really miss anything that was cut from the books, and the added parts didn't overshadow the original story (more like exaggerations than additions, really).

I think that all but the most hardcore purists will be completely satisfied with the films ("satisfied" is kind of an understatement). The film does an excellent job of catering to both hardcore fans and the "newbies," although I think it may be slanted a bit more toward those with some kind of "Tolkien experience."

Finally, I want to mention how overjoyed I am that Alan Lee and John Howe were the conceptual designers for the films. These two are probably the most popular Tolkien conceptual artists, and they really bring Middle Earth to life just like I imagined it.
