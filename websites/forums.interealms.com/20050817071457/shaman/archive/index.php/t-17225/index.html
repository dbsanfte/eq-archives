LaughingWoman
Pocket pet 2 is really a must have for this kind of thing. The mage will want (or already has) this for pet canni anyway. What's really truly handy is for the mage to use his nice fixed-duration invis to romp down to the camp, pop his (already buffed and equipped) pet for protection, then CotH you (and your pet, who would normally die when you were summoned, but he's safe in your pocket) down.

When pulling a new area (like when we were first flagged for PoS - when it needed a flag, sigh) you'll be exploring and pulling while the mage chain-casts CoH and interrupts before it goes off, so that hopefully, when you hit your "OMG AAAAAAGH!" panic key, the spell is almost done casting...

This duo is the most fearsome thing I've ever encountered while pairing up with people in EQ. The mage spends 95% of his playtime at 90m or higher. Then suddenly, you're crapping your knickers because you ended up with 2 or 3 times as many mobs as you're used to. Before you know it, the mage has fried them to cinders, and his goliath of a pet has kept taunt very nicely.

A big thing to remember that the mage probably won't confess -- mages have exactly two methods of defense: pet and gate. Your eye must be firmly fixed on the mage at all times (especially when he's not using earth pet) because he can't root, snare, or do anything else by way of escaping aggro. Very handy to have a high-aggro, low-mana spell up to peel mobs off the mage when necessary, in some situations (especially when his pet is nuking and proc'ing your well-meaning roots off instantly).

I ended up pulling with malo (or sinia) just about every time with the mage, since every resist is a tragedy to them *grin* and things just went a lot smoother when I sacrificed the mana rather than him.

Lodi and UP critters should be cake for you two. The regen ring (luggald flesh) quest was a good bit of fun (and a bit of a challenge on the last critter) as well.

My main is a magician and I have a shaman bot. Its a fairly popular duo amoung mages. I typically have both pets loaded with mage goodies, both pets buffed with max str and max dex (for weapon procs), DS on mage pet. Tends to work pretty well, although I haven't moved to Tier 2 mobs duoing, I tend to look for groups.

Mage is 65/70 w/FT15 and a several pieces of elemental gear
Shaman is 59/0 w/FT3 and all droppabe gear, nothing great.

With mage's malosinia my shaman lands slow pretty easily, even in places like PoV and Tactics.

I duo PoN mobs pretty easily. Although for better exp duoing I plan to move to PoS and use CoH pulling to try and single tougher mobs. I like the duo because slow/buff/heal/dot/canni works really well with nuke/DS/proccing pet weapons/focus items/pet heals/pet canni. The only problem I have with a 2 boxing duo is managing both chars when things get tough is difficult. But playing with a friend should make this a good duo.

Mage pets certainly make good tanks. And with the 61 Air pet's stun, it holds agro really well, while healing generates practically no agro. And a mage's nukes can help burn down a mob when it heals itself or runs.
