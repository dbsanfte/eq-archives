Nowadays when people talk about *zerging* in EQ, it usually does NOT mean that they keep dieing and rezzing dead people in till they win, instead, the term is used to point out, that while a 100 people raid has double the healing/dps/CC power and whatnot of a 50 man raid, these encounters were never designed for that many people(zergs).
Having higher numbers simply never has any drawbacks, making the encounter a lot easier.

My definition of an expansion that encourages *zerging* is this:

If adding a twink(usually equipped with droppable gear or with gear of 2+ expansions ago) to your raid yields a higher benefit than uber equipping a regular guildmember, then the balance is screwed(ergo encourages zerging) and instead of wasting so much time of a whole guild on equipping their members with high quality gear, they can just bring along their alts/box and save themselves the hassle.

Everquest's implementation of a level based game has been their biggest challenge. All too often friends were made enemies by the divisive nature of leveling at different paces. Now it's easier for people to keep up with each other, and PLing is not required to gain 5 levels in a week (in the 30s and 40s).

Are 1 - 60 worthless--yes and no. They've always been worthless, as is 60 - 65 IMHO. Level based games are not realistic and only cause divisions among friends. Skill based games have always been superior to level based games.

Look at it from a D&D vs reality standpoint. 25 peasants with pitchforks in D&D would get slaughtered by a lvl 20 knight in plate mail. Historically, 25 peasants with pitchforks would rip the knight off his horse, rip off his helmet, and brutally murder that lvl 20 knight.

Secondly, hit dice focused games, like everquest, imply that your body becomes expenentially tougher as you increase in levels, while in reality, no ones body can become 650% tougher without turning into pure titanium.

My point? That this is just one more bandaid to a gaming system that is fundamentally flawed. If they had chosen a less D&D style model to create their gaming system, EQ would have had far less problems than it currently has.

Dont' even argue fantasy vs reality--you won't convince me.
