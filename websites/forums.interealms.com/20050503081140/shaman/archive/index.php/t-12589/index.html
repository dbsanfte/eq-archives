deria
As a player of both classes, I have some insight on this topic.

Enchanters have it rough when it comes to slowing -- they lack ac/hp, so resists usually end with a dead or seriously wounded enchanter (which in the case of wounding is a serious drain on cleric mana and overall group performance).

While you can use Tash to minimize resists, the unfortunate fact of Tash is that it is very high agro. In order to use it without getting agro, you need to typically wait until the MT has solid agro before casting. This delay, combined with the much slower casting time of enchanter slows, usually results in the mob being slowed much later than the same mob being slowed by a shaman.

When viewed in perspective, the actual DPS output of a mob is going to be much lower with a shaman slowing than with an enchanter (despite the negligible 5 percent difference).

It is also important to realize that a shaman has the luxury of not actually *requiring* malo/malos to land a slow safely. If the slow bounces, you can malos and slow again without any real fear of dying. An enchanter doesn't really have that luxury.

Beastlords, on the other hand, are much better situated than enchanters for slowing. If they had a reliable way to proc tash (or some variant of malo) than Shaman would indeed be in trouble group wise. :)

The bottom line, though, is that there is no reason to worry much about this.

A shaman is a superb slower, a decent healer, a decent nuker, a decent buffer, and a barely acceptable crowd controller.

An enchanter is a superb crowd controller, a decent slower, a decent buffer, a barely acceptable nuker, and a frighteningly bad healer (go go gadget-rune). An enchanter can also (at tremendous risk) send overall group DPS through the roof.

A beastlord is... well, lets just say that he could cast Spiritual Vigor and Spiritual Purity and call it a day. (Ok, they do more, I know.) I actally really enjoy grouping with beastlords, *especially* beastlords that slow. A 50 precent (or 65 percent) slowed mob means that I can be more aggressive in landing my own slow, especially in situations where it is normally hard to do so (like ToV for example). With the beastlord slowing, I not only know that Turgurs *can* land, but I can chain cast it until it DOES land.

(That being said, I swear Beastlords have an easier time landing their slow than shaman do. They shouldn't, according to lucy, but it just seems to often work out that way.)

The 20% difference isn't fuzzy math, it's very real, and is the reason Turgur's didn't gain power from level 60->65. It did, during PoP beta, and was rightly seen to be insanely overpowered. Read and understand:

If you take for granted that a mob WILL be slowed if players are really fighting it (a very valid assumption in PoP, no one fights mobs unslowed. just not done), then you see the real difference. So you don't compare a mob slowed with Turgur's and a mob slowed with Forlorn Deeds (FD) against unslowed mobs; unslowed mobs don't exist. You compare them to each other (and to mobs slowed with Sha's Revenge or Requiem of Time).

So a mob that normally attacks at "100" attacks at 30 with FD, 25 with Turgur's. The difference between 30 and 25 isn't 5%. 30 is 20% more than 25. A mob will do 20% more damage if you use FD instead of Turgur's for your slows.

Did you follow that? Middle school math. 30 is 120% of 25.

*20%*. That's like a difference of FOUR HUNDRED AC on the MT. Casting Turgur's instead of Forlorn Deeds on a mob has the same effect as equipping a Velious tank in full Raex's Armor of Destruction! (blah blah, same level/aa's, just change gear) This on top of the huge advantages Turgur's has as a spell in general (cast/recast time), and in specific as a spell that shamans have, to go with their other spells and abilities (Malos, Quiescence, melee mitigation an order of magnitude above chanters, and a hitpoint advantage).

At level 28, you're **** outta luck. The chanter really can do as well as you, slowing mobs. The things you're fighting don't hit for crap, your strength in that arena just isn't needed. You have to get a group spot as healer at that level, if you want to group. Even better, take the spot of both healer, and slower, and just get 5 damage dealers (petcasters, btw, >>>>>>> melee, especially at that level) to hang out with. You'll get way better exp than that cle/enc/whatever. And sleep soundly at night, your role later on is unthreatened. Any slow less effective than shaman slow is both measurably and visibly worse. ;)
