I completely understand your post, for a game, it really doesn't seem like much fun sometimes.

If you like raiding, the best thing to do would be to level (as a member of a raiding guild, I would welcome the freedom to just do what I wanted, when I wanted including leveling like crazy) to 60 and attempt to join a raid guild. It doesn't have to be the uberest guild on your server, but any nice, high level, stable raid guild on your server.

If you're bored and you see a guild where you know some folks doing a raid, ask to come along! Help out all you can and learn as much as possible.

Network! In a guild, I find myself grouping with the same people 95% percent of the time. I have a passing acquaintance with lots of high level people on my server (mainly from my server board) but few out of guild close friendships.

As far as good exp groups, pay attention to who are the same people on every night the same time as you. Build a regular group of likeminded, daring yet skilled adventurers, do fun things! Crawl Chardok, crawl HS, crawl SG.

Because a grandmaster in one of the tradeskills. The Xanthe earring quest has really demonstrated the value of being a 250 tradeskill person (250 tailors are insane, imo). Hopefully, more quests like this will come along, that reward individuals for their hours of clicking.

Stop into Halas, buff the hell out of some true newbie shaman, give them a little trinket and let them see what a shaman can do.

As far as gear goes, you would flat out laugh if you had seen what I was wearing at level 56. RMB? Jarsath pants? Epic quest shield? The availability of very nice gear attainable by a single group is staggering. Divine inspiration, bone braced buckler, bracelet of sense....

If all else fails, take a break from the game. Norrath can be a very frustrating place to be sometimes.

Rodahn
When you start taking EQ out of what you feel its intended context is.

I will explain with a personal example:

About a month ago (as some here may remember), I made a post saying I was taking a step back from EQ because I felt like a hamster on a wheel, chasing after rewards I could never catch.

EQ, obviously, means different things to different people, but to me, it's meant to be a game that I can enjoy, fine-tuning and customizing my characters until I feel they are "done" (which will probably never happen).
"Fun" in EQ to me is mainly experiencing new things and advancing my characters (both in levels and in equipment). Also, perhaps more obscurely, EQ satisfies my creative hunger. I have an extremely active imagination, and if I do not find an outlet for it on a daily basis, I get very restless and frustrated. For me, EQ is that outlet. By immersing myself in EQ's fantasy world, I am able to create my own fantasy worlds and satify the aforementioned "hunger."
That being said, the above requirements of my having fun in EQ should never overshadow the fact that, when it comes right down to it -- EQ is just a game!

But I digress . . .

At the time I made that post, I no longer felt that I was in the context of what I thought EQ should be.
I was seeing and doing the same old things; I was stuck in the same old gear; and it really was not the creative outlet that it once was. But, more importantly, I was taking these things too seriously.

So I took a breather for a few days and re-evaluated what I wanted out of the game.
Sure, I want to strive to see and do new things, fine tune my character's skills, and get new gear. But I also needed to put it all back into context (that context being the fact that EQ is a computer game and should not run one's life).

When I decided to come back, I resolved to actively seek out these things I needed instead of whining about them and waiting for them to come to me. Since that time, I got my epic and a pair of SS chain boots waiting to be turned in. Not huge upgrades, I know, but fairly big steps nonetheless.
Also, I really worked hard to build up my twink ranger. He is currently lvl 40 with decent gear, and is really helping me to enjoy the game more.
And the most important thing is -- I am advancing again, instead of being stuck in the same old mold.

Anyway, the point to that long-winded response is that it's time for a break whenever EQ stops being what you want/need it to be.
And I say "time for a break" instead of "time to quit" because chances are, you will return to EQ eventually once you leave it.
I know this for personal experience, hehe. I've probably "retired" about ten times.
Also, hang on to your gear! Don't sell it, give it away, or delete it. Because, again, chances are you will come back eventually and want to jump right back in again.
There are, of course, special cases that require one to flat out quit EQ (like serious RL problems, computer issues, relationship problems, etc.). And RL should always > EQ.

But anyway, that's my 2cp. Take them for what they are worth, and never forget that the decision and reason to leave EQ is ultimately up to you.
