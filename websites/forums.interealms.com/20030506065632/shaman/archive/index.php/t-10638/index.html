Valsuvious
quickly put, once you hit 51, you can officially change the way that you get exp. By this, I mean that you can make it so that 50% of your exp goes into your regular experience pool, while the other 50% goes into your alternate experience. Now, just remember that your alternate exp. goes up at a higher rate than your normal experience does, although I do not know the exact reason for this.

Oh, and if you are wonderingg what to do with them once you get them, well...it's not that hard to figure out. Every time that you ding (it is just like normal experience where it takes 5 whole yellows to complete a level). Once this happens, you get a chance to put that point into one of the options under the general abilities. You have to spend 6 points under that category before you can put a point in one of the archetype abilities (and you have to spend 2 points for any of the abilities). Then to be able to use the Class specific abilities, you have to have spent 12 points in Archetype, for a total of 18 AA points first.

Grippa
The reason people say to wait for level 60 to get AA points is that at level 60, AA xp can be earned a lot faster. This is because an AA point is always a fixed amount of xp, and at level 60 the amount of xp you are getting for a blue mob is much more than you get for blue mobs at level 51.

Having said that, do what you enjoy. I got a few AAs before level 55, and then waited for 59 to start getting more. When I obtained my level 60 spells, I made it a priority to get level 60 rather than accrue more AA points. I found getting "Baron" (6 points) at level 54 to be a pleasant diversion from levelling, but other people have different opinions.

But it is most certainly true that you can earn AA much much faster at level 60 than you can at level 51.

Oh by the way, the "4 pets" skill is called spirit call, and it's actually a Planes of Power skill - which means you have to be level 61 to get it.
