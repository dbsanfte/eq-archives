If you're level 54 already, the heyday for the JBB is over. It really only helps significantly from lvl 45 - 55, so I wouldn't bother saving up for one at this point unless you're happy with your current equipment setup, which you aren't.

As for dropping items with WIS on them, remember that HP / AC > WIS. I've been level 60 for months now and just this month hit 200 WIS unbuffed.

Suggestions from me that are pretty cheap and affordable, just off the top of my head:

Velium Fire Wedding Rings (ac 6 +65 HP rings) 1k per pair
Sarnak Battle Shield (ac 25 + some stats) 2-3k
Ivory Imbued Collar (ac 8 + stats) 500-1k
Forest Loops (can't remember but decent) 100-300pp
Tattered Mantle (ac 12 +20 hp and some resists) 200pp
Enshrouded Veil (ac 7 + 30 hp +30 mana +stats) 1k
or Mushroom Veil (ac 8 12 dex 55 mana +10 poison) 1k

I wouldn't bother with Plane of Growth raids as someone above mentioned unless you want to go for the learning experience. It's nice to get some raids under your belt, but I found that in my low - mid 50's the Plane of Growth was a great way to waste 10 hours by spending 5 of those hours doing corpse recovery. Even at level 60 it still gets a little rough sometimes clearing it to kill Tunare =) Not to mention that shaman armor seems extremely rare in PoG, I've been on about 15 raids there over the last year or so and have *never* seen any shaman armor drop.

What I would suggest instead of doing planar raids is to pick a Velious faction (I would start off with Thurgadin) and go raid. Thurgadin armor is the easiest to acquire, with only 2 groups of mid 50's required to kill in the arena of Kael. I would wait until your high 50's or even 60 before switching over to Skyshrine / Kael faction since Temple of Veeshan is much more difficult. Mobs in Kael drop quest armor pieces pretty regularly, and the armor from the quest is very good. You can also find people willing to multiquest armor pieces for 1-3k usually as well, but expect to pay around 10-15k for BP or legs MQ. Also by choosing dwarf faction, you can do the Kromrif Military Leggings quest, which results in a nice pair of pants that did me well until level 56 or 57.

Hunting in Velk's Lab is also good at your level, and some of the dropped items there would be a good upgrade for you - Crystal Chitin Gauntlets and Armplates, Spider Fur Cloak and belt; and you can also pick up some gems needed for Velious armor quests and some other items which sell for a couple k plat while you're there.

Bigguss
Horses only work in outdoor zones, so keep that in mind if you do a lot of dungeon hunting. :)

I'd hit Maiden's Eye and grab some of the nice no drop stuff that drops pretty frequently. Last time I was there I got a Shrouded Medallion (http://www.magelo.com/eq_item_info.html?num=32318) and a Stone Idol (http://www.magelo.com/eq_item_info.html?num=32323), both very nice pieces (in the 4-arms camp.) One of these days I need to go back there and pray for some Divine Inspiration. ;)

The real game changing items aren't cheap. I'll make some gear change recommendations, but slant them toward upgrading as cheaply as possible, with the idea of saving toward a JBB, Horse, Fungi Staff, or Iksar BP- remember, Mana free healing/damage > All.

Ears: Imbued Golden Ivory Earring (http://www.magelo.com/eq_item_info.html?num=20595) (this is an easy one- increases your WIS and AC- and super-cheap, 100 pp each max.)

Face: Arctic Wyvern (http://www.magelo.com/eq_item_info.html?num=24813) is the cheapest way to go. Lose 3 wis, get 6 AC/CHA, and a minor save. Good trade, and you'll make some money on it.

Neck: Ry'gorr (100 pp, a great deal for ac 9, +10 mana, +4 sta, +4 magic) / Ivory imbued if cheap enough / or the Shrouded Medallion from Maidens

Fingers: 6/65- yeah, you'll lose WIS, but it's worth it. If you can find a cheap one, grab a Shadel Bandit Ring (http://www.magelo.com/eq_item_info.html?num=28708) (the +55 mana is equivalent to 5 wisdom.)

Wrists: Leave 'em alone- shaman wrist upgrades aren't cheap. Save for your JBB.

Shoulders: If you're serious about raising money, you could sell these and replace them with a tattered mantle (100 pp, or go whack Gullerback in Burning Woods) - you'd lose WIS but gain AC- and you might be able to use the cash to fund something more important.

Chest: Not worth upgrading cheaply. An Iksar BP would be a nice upgrade- maybe something to save for or camp. If you're looking to upgrade, I think the progression (cheapest first) is TreeWeave / Froglok BP / Barbed Ringmail / Iksar BP / Phase Spider Carapace / Fungi Tunic.

Back: Fine- no cheap upgrades here. If you're looking to upgrade, those Arcylia reinforced (+10 wis) cloaks are coming down in price (2k?) and the Seahorse cloaks (ac 20, +25 hp) make a great ac/hp cloak for the money.

Waist: Fine- no cheap upgrades here (spider fur is a nice alternative, substituting WIS for AC/HP.)

Legs: Tough spot to upgrade- I wouldn't worry about it yet.

Boots: Sell the GEBs for cash and get some JBs. More cash!

Shield: Definitely ditch the CGS- replace it with a Clay Guardian shield (ac 25, +50 HP, few hundred pp.) Your next upgrades would be the Crystal Chitin (if WIS/Mana is your primary concern) or the Sarnak Battle Shield (resists, HP). Thing is, these are each going to set you back a couple k. I'd stick with the Clay guardian for now- you should be able to break even or make a little on that swap.

Sell the Howling Harpoon and replace it with a goranga idol, stone idol, etc. You might make a few plat on this deal as well.

Hopefully at the end of this all you'll have a couple thousand more plat than you started with, to be applied toward one or two really good gear upgrades.

Hope this helps. :)
