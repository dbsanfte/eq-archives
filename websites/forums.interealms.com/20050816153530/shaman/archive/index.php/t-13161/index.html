How much does FT really help?
With FT3 boots, and Solstace earring, anyone can get FT4 relatively easy.
4 mana per tick ---> 40 extra mana every minute ---> 2400 extra mana every hour
Blood of Saryn costs 535 mana.
With an Afflicition Efficiency IV item, Blood of Saryn costs 401.25 mana (basicly 400 mana)
Over a period of 1 hour, with FT 4 vs no FT, you can cast a grand total of 6 more dots.
The average xp group I hunt in will usually kill between 30 and 60 mobs in an hour; mobs which have approximately 20-25k hp.
Casting 6 more dots in a single hour than I was casting before isn't really going to be that noticable.

If you can somehow get FT 15, that's 9000 mana per hour you get.
That's 22 more Bloods you can cast per hour.
It's still good for adding DPS to the group, but a single 2k dot on a 25k hp mob isn't all that amazing to tell the truth.

Most likely the way you'll get your FT up to 15 is through gear with good HP and mana already on it.
My advice is to not concentrate solely on getting FT at the cost of hp gear.
Go for HP/Mana gear that happens to have FT.

Boinshar
Focus before all else.

After all, we *are* casters. Our ability to cast spells is our greatest source of power. HP/AC makes sure we can live long enough to cast, same with resists, FT gives us extra mana with which we can cast more spells.

However, only focus items directly improve our ability to cast. Casting haste, casting efficiency, and to a lesser extent, extended range and even extended duration changes the game for a shaman more than anything else.

Spell haste is irreplacable once you get it. How many times have your target died 1 sec before you can land a heal? How many times have you died 1 sec before you can finish casting root/turgur/heal? A high hp/ac shaman who died because he couldn't finish a spell is equally useless compard to a cardboard shaman who died because he doesn't have enough hp/ac to take punishment.

Casting efficiency is more effective than FT during the short term. If your mana preservation item has a high percentage, it can equal or surpass FT in the long run as well.

Extended range and extended duration have relatively smaller impact, but they still grant abilities that you otherwise will not have.

HP is still important. In fact, I'd rank it below casting haste and casting efficiency focus effects. However, there comes a point where anyone who doesn't have defensive gets creamed in 1 or 2 rounds (about 1.5 secs) if they get agro--hp only goes so far.

For a class like shaman, who has unsurpassed class based mana regen, FT merely translates to more damage over time. Focus items, on the other hand, increases the fundamental effectiveness of every single spell that falls under the influence of those effects.
