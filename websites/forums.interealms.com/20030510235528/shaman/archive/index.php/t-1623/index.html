Weyalan
In most PvP encounters people try to max resists, but in particular magic resist. If he managed to get good posion resist it was beacause he would have known that he was gonna fight a shaman and did his homework...poison is where most smart shaman pin their PvP hopes, although the very smart patient ones go with disease.

Basically, magic resist is usually highest, so if you cast Flash of light and it doesnt stick in the first 2 or 3 casts forget magic based spells - no slow, no root, no malo, etc. Go with poison or disease. Disease is actually better because your disease resist debuffer is disease based rather than poison debuffer that is magic based.

Use cancel/annul magic as much as possible - he will have plenty of buffs, with his crappy buffs stacked first. If he has any sense he will put SoW near the end of his buffs, so keep clearing his buffs until you clear SoW (better hope he isnt super smart and has 2 insta-cast R clickable items). If you can get rid of his SoW your chances got a heap better! Remember you will have chloro happening and he wont (unless he is super smart and paid a shaman to make him a regen potion in advance). So, if you have SoW and he doesnt you should be able to run around and regen damage while he will be unable to get to within melee range. Note, it is a fine line between being in his melee range and being in his bowkiting range - ouch

Bottom line is that rangers are one of the tougher PvP classes to beat. And even 2 levels difference is a real difference. Essentially, and perhaps sadly, A lot of PvP boils down to gear. Ok, a poorly equipped smart player is able to be able to beat a well equiped no-brainer, but really, if 2 competent smart players of similar level go PvP, the one with the better gear will win...

Also, in my opinion, classes go through phases where they are relatively weak and relatively strong compared to other classes. For shaman, levels 20-23 and 30-33 are relatively weak, levels 24, 25, 34, 35 are quite strong. Level 41 is a relatively weak level. You do not get a strong level now until 49 - that is a huge power surge - level up some and then kick his ass

Ok... here's what I would do. Of course if nothing sticks then you're screwed and dead, but gotta try, right?

Spells to have up:
Mal___
Slow
Dispell
Disease dot
Nuke (sometimes you have to think unconventional, he probably won't have good MR, DR, AND CR)
Heal
Root
Blind

Buff up first of all, every buff you have. Also buff your dog with everything you have. I know it takes forever, but it's worth it. Also, in 3 levels you get summon companion, so you can park your pet away from you and summon him as you start your duel, which is unnerving for someone who didn't know you had a pet up, as it kinda causes some confusion in their plan. May or may not be possible there, I have never been to the arena so I haven't seen the layout.

Open with dispells. Dispell the heck outta him while he goes after your pet. As a general rule, anyone who's dumb enough to kill the pet first will lose to a well played shaman.

Don't panic and try and run all the time, just attempt to channel through his hits if he turns on you while you're casting.

Dispell at least twice, I'm thinking you should do it more like 4 times IF you have time. Then hit him with blind, if it doesn't stick then try malisement, blind again, strafe out of his melee range and behind him (or elsewhere) and then slow him, then dot him.

If you can get a DS potion before this he will have done some of your work in advance for you, because at lvl 41, you'll be doing something like 21 or 22 in damage for every one of his hits, even if he just hits for 1.

Back off a little, and heal yourself as needed (you don't wanna let him get close and not be able to channel a heal, you don't need to be FULL health, but you don't want to be at 40% either) and land your disease dot on him. Depending on mana situation, chain nuke or canni some... if your nukes are resisted at all don't bother and just be patient with your disease dot. Keep him blinded, and sit down and swap out your nuke with poison dot at this point really fast if the nuke is resisted. It's very unlikely that he has resists high in CR, DR, MR, AND PR. Apply this dot, let your pet beat on him, if he is kickin your pets butt, recall it...

Don't get in melee range unless he's not hitting hard vs your damage shield potion. if you're doing more damage to him with it than he's doing to you, he'll kill himself. Otherwise, stay out of melee range.

Try this, it works well. If he breaks out the bow, you are going to have to learn (really fast) how to stay in between melee range and bow range.

You may wanna get a couple stinging wort potions, it's a 300hp instant heal, gives you a 2nd chance almost...

They key to winning duels for shamans is to be patient. We aren't wizards, we can't make the kill instant. The key is to simply shoot for outlasting the enemy. If you keep yourself alive till he has no more arrows and no more mana, then all he has is melee to kill you. Root him, rot him, and sit back while he tries to med and you hit him with your 5 point nuke every time he sits down to make it so he can't med.

Keep him blind and he can't even find you ;)
