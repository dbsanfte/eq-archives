Roxxanne
The discussion of the Q upgrade (is 20% more enough?) highlights a real design challenge as EQ gets older. For those of us not yet in LDON hard or the elemental planes, the existing Q is fine for healing and a 20% upgrade is about right for the new mobs we will likely face in GoD. But those already in EP and above are finding they need more like a 500hp/tic heal from Q to be an effective healer. We live in two different worlds, and our needs and balancing are different.

You could deal with this by having the 500hp/tic Q upgrade be available as a no drop spell in the highest raiding zones, so only those who need it can get it. The problem is that those shaman can also go to all the other zones, and it leaves the rest of us a second rate shaman in those other zones and situations. Over time, if you pursued this design approach, the ubers and the casuals would really be playing two different games in the same online world.

Maybe Sony needs to acknowledge this and just have some "uber" servers with different balancing that you graduate to when you kill Quarm or something.

Sony needs to keep providing upgrades for folks who play 40+ hours a week that are not available to folks who play 20 hours per week, or the game will not hold the interest of the hard core. But how do you do this without trivializing the role and sense of accomplishment of the casuals, who fall further and further behind in their relative competency in the "open" zones and raids?

I dont know the answer to this, but it seems like a core issue for the game going into year 5.

Jinheim
I think that the original implementation of ancient spells was good. Think about it like this:

When Luclin came out, the only items with focus effects were those in VT. I could be wrong, but I don't think there are any Improved Damage type focuses in VT. There certainly weren't any Burning Affliction type items then, (there is one there now) as BA4 wasn't implemented until well in to PoP. So, without these focus items, all shamans were doing the same amount of damage via bane of nife, poxx, and whatever nuke we used then. Obviously, some shamans had bigger mana pools and more FT than others, but everyone had the same potential damage output - some could just sustain it longer. Then, with ancient scourge, those shamans who had reached the final stages of the expansion got an upgrade that did 254 damage a tick (versus 214 for bane of nife). That is an 18.6% upgrade (more or less the same improvement you get with the best dot focus in Time http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=3509). This gave the uber shaman the capacity to deal damage slightly faster than a non-uber shaman. Considering the time it took then (and it still isn't easy now) to get a guild VT keyed, is this upgrade unreasonable? I don't think it is.

Also consider ancient feral avatar (the only spell in my spellbook that I have never cast), a 20% upgrade from the previous two avatars. This about how big of a difference avatar makes for a melee. What is 20% of that improvement? Pretty insignificant.

The original purpose of ancient spells was to make the uber players' spells just a little bit better than the non-uber players' spells, but they did not add any new functionality (with the exception of the ancient bard mezz, which is unresistable). I feel this was fair and justified. VT also brought us mana preservation effects, which once again made uber players slightly better than non-ubers. It is nice to have a way to improve your character other than just throwing on more mana and hp.

Then, in PoP, there were no ancient spells. Everyone gets the same spells, and anyone that puts forth a decent effort can obtain them. We're now back to where we were in velious - everyone has the same spell damage output. However, in place of Ancient spells, we now have a series of escalating focus effects. Anyone can get an ID4 item for 5k in the bazaar or after spending a little time in tak (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=43409). There are spell haste, detrimental extension, and beneficial extension augments available as well. There are also cheap pottery items available for further DoT focus effects, which anyone can obtain easily. Regretfully, there are no extremely easy MP4 items to get, although they are still obtainable by anyone that can save money (PoWater pottery item and Ornate boots). I stand by what I said that anyone can get these focus effects and items that I just mentioned. It will take a pretty significant amount of time and money-saving, but a guildless person can still do it, albeit slower than one that has a crew to group/farm with every day.

Combine 10% more effectiveness with 10% less mana used, 10% less time spent casting a spell, and spells that last 10% longer, and you'll find that all these focii add up to make you a lot more powerful than you were without them.

Players that raid get improved focus effects. Instead of 10% mana pres, I get 15%. Instead of 20% improved healing, I get 30%. Instead of 10% extension, I get 20%, so on and so forth. This makes up for a lack of ancient spells. My spells are more effective and more mana efficient than someone without these upgraded focii. Considering all the time I have spent raiding, I don't think this is an unfair reward. If it wasn't for focus effects, itemization would be extremely boring.

I would even go so far as to say that it is these effects, rather than hp/mana, that creates most of the gap between raiders and non. The extra HP and mana really only gives you a safety buffer, it doesn't make you kill faster or heal for more.

So, basically, focus effects have replaced the need for ancient spells, and this makes me wonder why we are getting them in GoD and what it could mean for the division between raiders and non. If focus effects in GoD are as much an improvement over Time effects as Time effects were over VT, then I am not sure why ancients would be necessary. Right now, I suspect that there will be significantly improved focus items in GoD, and less focus items will trickle down (i.e., casual people will have focus effects as good as what I have now, while I have something 10% better). This is fine. However, if you throw in ancient spells, that's a big jump in power (all ancient spells except ours and sk's are an exact 20% improvement on its predecessor). I can see how this would be unfair and frustrating to those that couldn't obtain them (remember, though, we still have no idea how hard they will really be to get).
