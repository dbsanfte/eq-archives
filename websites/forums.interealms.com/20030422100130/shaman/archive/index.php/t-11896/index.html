cattywumpus
The real problem with farming cash is everyone else is too.

HGs are good if you can get there when there are less than 10 people in the zone. Any more and the profitability drops off considerably (and the frustration goes up even quicker).

IGs are good if you can get the camp but that's a dream on high population servers.

Never been to PoG but it sounds good since it's harder to get to.

Coterie castle is over farmed to the point that all the good droppable stuff can be bought in the bazaar for c.100pp each.

Great Sapprophyte is dependent on luck. He's a 2 hour 45 minute spawn so you don't get that many chances in a day. He is also camped quite a bit since he's the only one who drops a spell used by several classes. If he drops the spell you can do well, if he doesn't nothing else he has is really that good. Spell drops about 1 time in 4 so figure 2kpp/12 hours. Not the way to go.

The place I go for cash camps is Stonebrunt Mountains. The Highland Kobolds drop fine steel weapons, etched iron, blue wicker, and wicker armor. If you can keep the camp cleared (and at 61 that should be trivial) you can net 150pp-200pp an hour. There are two camps and both are open 90% of the time. You can sell to the kitties in the zone, in Gunthak or in Paineel. You also get Heretic faction so after a few hours you will be amiable in Paineel for better prices. If you can clear both camps (might be doable but I've never tried) you could likely exceed what you can make from HGs. Bring lots of weight reduction bags though, the stuff is heavy.

Gludorp
Beyond hunting in a particular zone, there is a lot of money to be made in the bazaar. Here's what you can do:

1) Keep a running list of items which you think you have a very good feel for as far as how much they're worth. Periodically check their prices in the bazaar and snatch them up when they're priced low, then sell them high for a nice profit (you can use a mule permanently stationed in the bazaar for this). As you gain more experience in the bazaar, you'll be able to do this with more and more items.

2) Take a gander at Eqtraders.com and look at what kind of items--of the type not normally sold by NPC vendors--tradeskill folks need to make their high-end items. Go out and farm those items, either through hunting or finding things players have sold to NPC vendors, and sell it on the bazaar. I don't want to give away my particular trade secrets, but I've been hauling in 5k per week doing this by just devoting a few hours per day to farming stuff I know tradeskillers have a great need for. And I'm not just talking about spiderling silks. ;)

3) Dabble in tradeskills yourself. You don't have to be a grandmaster tradesman to make money through crafted items. Again, do a little research and you'll find you can make some items at low skill levels that are in demand. You won't make huge profits all at once, but you will get a steady stream of income that adds up over the long run.

Leather padding is good if you're on in prime time and hate standing in line for spawns (like me).

Hunt rockhoppers in Marus Seru for LQ padding - my 61 shaman got three stacks in an hour.

Hunt spiderlings in Innothule (since it was reworked there are lots of spiderlings there now) or anywhere else for spiderling silk.

Then in a tailoring kit:
2 silks -> silk thread
silk thread plus LQ rockhopper pelt -> leather padding

Trivial at like 22, sells for up to 30p EACH PIECE in the bazaar on Rodcet. That means one hour hunting pelts + two hours hunting silks yields 1800 plat, 600p per hour.

You can do better on some of the more profitable item camps, like the Crypt in Seb, but you have to fight for the camp and probably bring friends.
