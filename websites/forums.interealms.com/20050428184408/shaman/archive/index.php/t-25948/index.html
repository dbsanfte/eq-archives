Serepi
I play a 54 priest and am going with Int/Spirit rather than Stam. I say this from my viewpoint/perspective and observations.

I think the pros of +spirit outweigh the cons for the following reasons:

1. Spirit Tap. I labored for a long time under the misconception that there was a soft cap on spirit at 300. This was true in beta, where the spirit/mana regen ratio was calculated at spirit/3. It was soft capped to stem off hyper mana regen. However, it is now 13+spirit/4, and there is no cap. With Spirit Tap at 5/5, there is a 100% chance that I'll gain 100% bonus to spirit after a kill (including pvp kills within level limits). My spirit is at about 280 right now. If I'm spirit-tapping, I push 560. The mana regen while soloing is significant and noticeable.

2. Grouping. I'm not taking as many hits as I used to. Plus, with Inner Fire going, even if I'm soloing, they aren't hitting as hard. Fire off a Renew (Imp. Renew 5/5, Spiritual Healing 5/5) along with Inner Fire and my hps just aren't going down at a rate that I'm concerned. If they do, I just heal. (Imp. Flash Heal 2/2, Inner Focus for those oom times).

If you're a smart priest, you'll fire off your instant spells in groups, so that you can overcome the 5 second mana regen rule. Don't space out the renews. This lets your high spirit kick in.

If the mobs are all on me, an extra 200 hp (one hit, maybe?) isn't going to save me, but that extra mana may keep a melee up that could save me. For me, my mana is more important than my hps. If I can refill my water gun quicker, having a huge watergun isn't as helpful. (Int at 240, Spirit 280 atm).

3. You already have stamina! You get Imp. PWF at 2/2 at you're getting a big stam buff already.

Bottom line for me: the amount of mana regen is too hard to ignore for what I perceive to be marginal benefits of stamina. If I'm getting beat on, I'll be saved through Fade, Shield, or a Flash Heal. Not the sheer numbers of my hps.

1 vote for spirit.
