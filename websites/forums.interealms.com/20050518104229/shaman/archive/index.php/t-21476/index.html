Juujuu
I don't really have any weighting system like that.
When you raid for gear, stuff like that doesn't factor in as much.
Focus effects and flowing thought and HPs are all I pay attention to anymore really.
For example, I would wear a ceramic shield of valor for extended buff 4 more often than I'd wear Cazic Thule's brain (100hp mana and FT1).
Once you get into raiding for gear, good stats on gear goes along with all items with good focus.

To try and put a weighting system on stuff:
Str, Sta, Wis, Int, Dex all have zero weight for me because I have max str, sta, wis, and dex, and my int doesn't matter.
Cha has zero weight for me because I have over 115 cha unbuffed, so I don't have to buff myself to sell crap to vendors.
Agl has next to zero weight only because I can buff myself to max it, though I'd like to have 305 agl without the need for any buff slot being wasted.

What does matter to me:
Focus effects for me have utmost priority over everything else. There's mana pres, mana pres for dots, spell haste, spell haste for detrimental, spell haste for beneficial, longer buffs, longer debuffs, bigger nukes, bigger cold nukes, special Time Gear mods and GoD armor mods like Stun Resist, Spell Shield, Shielding, Avoidance, Dot Shielding, etc, haste, weapon procs, and right clickies of all sorts. There's so many of them, that the absolute priority over all other things for my gear selections is finding a slot for each focus effect. With raiding time and God, getting a piece of gear with a focus in a wrong slot can have "repercussions" in your later gear choices.

For example, I have better healing on my arms. If I get the time arms, I need a new healing focus, which would be my shield slot from Quarm when he drops it. If I was to hold out for getting the GoD armor from Qvic/Uqua with better healing on it, then I wouldn't have the time arms with it's spell haste of whatever type it is, so instead of getting a hammer of hours I wanted to get easily, I'll have to hold out for getting the illuminated hammer from Ikkinz or whatever zone it is. Other options are to get the healing shoulder from time, and still go for hammer of hours if you want timeburn, but then you need the bloodlink chest for mana pres instead of going for time chest for chronoburn.
When you raid high end gear, picking up a focus item in a slot means you might have to give up another focus you already have in that slot, and have to find a replacement elsewhere, and focus effects aren't usually spread on a lot of items. There's only 1 shaman item with chronoburn on it, while there's multiple with good mana preservation. There's only 2 items shaman can get with chrononostrum, so if you want those focus effects, you have to plan your gear choices around them.

Aside from focus items, this is where your personal preferences come into play for what gear you pick.
My main choices are to go for in order:
1. Items with awesome right clickies. Quarm ring is a fine choice. Items with "form of XXX" are great. Ornate BP is great for free SLTW. etc.
2. Items with massive HP are second in priority.
3. Items with 15-20 resists to all, or massive 30+ resists to 2+ slots are good for consideration.
4. Items with lots of mana are worth of consideration.
5. Don't forget to keep your AC up there. No ac = beats.

If you are shopping for gear from the bazaar you might want to let focus effects slide, and concentrate more on the HPs and mana.
If you don't have max sta, then sta equates to HPs and is much more valuable.
If you have enough HPs to comfortably hunt in your places of choice, then consider focusing more on getting focus items even if it means sacrificing some HPs for it.
Resists are also important to keep up in general. The locations you hunt and the mobs you fight determine how much you need resists. Fighting in ToV for example you want high resists to deal with Wurms and Drakes and Dragons. If you fight in POP, resists aren't that big a deal.

Stamina will go to 0 once my worn stamina is 280. It will go to 1.2 once my worn stamina is 255. Mana is .4 because that is about the CanniV ratio of hp to mana. When everything hits the fan, a nice large mana pool is helpful.

Focus effects are much more important than stats. I recommend the following as the four most important: Haste of Mithaniel, Haste of Solusek, Extended Enhancement IV, Mana Preservation IV. The shawl effect is longer than EBIV, but does not affect Q. I have two of the four in the scaled avatar boots and the Wicked Bone Atlatl. After I get these, I am also hoping for Improved Healing IV, which is on the scaled avatar arms.

Finally, certain clickies are very nice. I would recommend especially a root clicky, which is on the scaled avatar pants and a ring. With VP3 and a root clicky, you may feel OK leaving root unloaded most of the time except when you are primary cc. You will need a couple of INSTANT spell icon clickies for debuffing mobs, mostly raid targets. The telescope works for a few seconds at a time. The Velious and Jaundiced Bone armor buffs are slow casting, so pretty worthless for this role. Of course, stinging wort and other old world instant buff potion work, but can get expensive.
