Hail,

It looks like this was as intended. A shame they couldn't have used the spot the Timorous Deep Halas Firepot uses (right by the dock next to town) instead. :p

As a side note for tradeskillers: The igloo location the Spiritstone sends you too is an AWESOME spot to farm spiderling silks and a fair spot for padding pelts, though there are more ruined than anything else.

I leave the (heavy) stone in the bank now, and after I die on a raid and a rez doesn't appear to be an immediate thing, I gate to TD, go to a bank town (Halas, Felwithe, wherever), then click my stone. Even naked (well, in bearform for my dignity), farming padding components is effortless there with dogdog. Use those worthless spirit pouches to hold the loot. After the rez, dump them on the ground except for one or two with the good stuff in it, and transfer loot to real bags. :) Presto, efficient use of downtime! (If you don't tradeskill, leather padding stacks sell to PC's for around ~100-200pp a stack now. Something to think about...)

Take care,

Hail,

www.eqtraders.com is THE place to go for tradeskill information.

That said, to answer your question, Spiritstones are made with the pottery tradeskill. It takes an extremely talented Master or Grandmaster potter to be successful, and some of the components aren't easy to come by, as well as being a little pricey to attempt. The failure rate is HIGH.

On my server, spiritstones were priced at 10kpp for awhile, but have now settled to the 4-6kpp range. YMMV.
