BlargX
Well, my feeling is that shaman eventually brings more to the table, but you don't have one now. You might as well just hit 60 ASAP with at least ONE class before you mess around creating alts, even if they're great alts.

Torpor and turgurs(75% slow) are godly, and once a shaman has torpor, he can get his mana back extremely quickly -- unlike a druid. There's also focus, a huge buff to your melee stats and hit points, and primal essence, and haste, blah blah.

Druid has nice buffs too, but they don't compare with a shaman's buffs, and nothing he has compares with torpor or slow. Snare and tracking are very nice, though, and evac is okay, but nothing to write home about.

Furthermore, velious quest armor for druids and BL's are both leather, so you'll have to get everything twice. Shaman velious quest armor is chain, so you can profit from more drops.

Additionally, a shaman is almost always wanted in groups, raids, and guilds more than a druid is. Druids often sit so idle they become little more than port machines.

If I were you, I'd get the druid on CoV faction after he has all the quest armor he needs, so he can port your BL around.

And...I'd start a second druid so you can port your shaman around too. Just use him as a port mule. Sounds crazy, eh, two druids? Well, porting is a huge benefit.

Btw, I'm a 54 druid and 60 shaman(now 52 monk also), and have a bit of personal experience on some of this. I've fought with a level 58 BL many times, and I can contribute tremendously to him. My slow makes his irrelevant, he loves my buffs and heals and haste. A shaman/BL pair is extremely durable and useful. You'd be very happy with it, I'm sure.

But ... I'd get at least one of your characters to 60 first. You might find you like your druid or BL so much at 60, or are so darn busy doing great raids at 60, that you only play one character anyway, and whether or not to replace your druid becomes a non-issue.

P.S.: Being 60 when new expansions come out is godly too. ;) You get first crack at all the new undiscovered loot, much of which gets nerfed within the first month or even first week of a release. Better to be running around with a 60 or two at that time than have three 50's. (And a druid is a DARN good new-release exploiter, too!)
