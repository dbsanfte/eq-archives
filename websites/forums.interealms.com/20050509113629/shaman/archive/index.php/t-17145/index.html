Doing some very simplified approach here:
FT15 equals 2 casts of Can4 per minute, or 300 HP that you won't have when you need them. That makes one FT equal to about 20 HP.
On the other hand it also means a savings of 2 less casts of can4, about 10 sec per minute, that you save. And that can be important when you are chain casting heals and can squeeze just one more cast out before you have to hit that canni button.
Since most FT items have decent HP to boot I usually pick FT over HP. Once you get VT/elemental/time gear, where there is actually a massive trade-off, you will cap FT fast with a few peices anyhow.

Do the math assuming that you have max spell haste and a healing4 item, (ignoring healing AA, heaing focus' as they affect both equally and thus the results scale to each other) and stuff like that and compare alternating Tnargs/Canni4 with chain casting Kraggs.

Tnargs = 2.85 + 2.25 (recast) = 5.1 seconds = 1300 + 10% from IH4
Canni4 = 2.25 + 2.25 (recast) = 4.5 seconds
=148dps of healing

Kraggs = 10 + 2.25 (recast) = 12.25 = 1950
=159dps of healing

Kraggs is a better heal. and kraggs only costs 400 as opposed to 450 for tnargs.

Now drop out the canni4 and just chain tnargs = 280dps of healing. That's a shag load more than Kraggs, almost double.

Mana Regen and Mana Pool = king for this kind of thing.

Jinheim
I feel FT is very important for a shaman, but it is not as important for us as it is for other classes with inferior mana regen abilities. Between my FT and mana pres focus, I can pretty much chain cast tnargs for a 5 minute raid boss fight (with raid buffs and bard song, of course). Without my FT, I'd have to stop sooner.

Here's how I rate things when I'm thinking about a new piece of gear:

1) Focus Effect & Clicky Effects - I'd wear my elemental boots for their preservation of xegony effect even if they weren't awesome stats. Ditto for my det and bene haste items. Marr's Gift on my arms is also an awesome effect, but I could live without it so I rank it slightly lower than other focus effects. I'd like an improved damage item but it isn't high on my list of priorities. I'm a GM pottery and I have made a few of the ID4 pofire items, but I don't swap them in for my mace of confusion, even though it would mean only a small loss of stats. There are also some other effects I would like to have, mainly just Faerune or just some form of truesight. I have had a 85hp/85mana helm in my bank for a while, but I didn't start wearing it over ornate until I finished my seru quest earring last week. I hate casting acumen, but seru earring is instant clicky acumen so that makes it pretty painless, and I finally upgraded the ornate. Other clicky effects are also good. Best example would be ornate legs. I don't know if I will ever be able to live without right-click root. I'd also like a right-click dispell item for dueling, but I wouldn't spend a lot of DKP on it unless it had really great stats as well.

2) Flowing Thought - I absolutely love flowing thought, but of course there are limits to how much hp and mana I will sacrifice for FT. I wore FT3 boots over vindi boots, and I probably would have considered wearing them over ornate boots if I wasn't already at the FT cap when I got ornate. Another example is when I was wearing a 65hp 65mana ft3 range, and the opportunity arose to claim my present ranged it (130hp, 145mana, 18% detrimental haste). That was a no brainer and I destroyed the ft3 range, dropping me down to FT12. I miss the FT and I do notice the loss, but the improved stats and detrimental haste made it an easy choice. Also, one of my weakest items is my neck (ft2, 60hp, 20wis). I am ready to upgrade it for an EP quality neck (125/125 or better), but I wouldn't upgrade it for something like the talisman gate quest neck. I'd get significantly better stats with the quest neck, but it wouldn't be worth dropping the ft2. I would, however, upgrade it for a VZ or TZ neck (whichever one has the 18 to all resists).

3) Hit Points - No further explanation needed.

4) Mana - Very important for when you need to chaincast tnargs on a raid, but on long sustained fights (exp groups) our mana regen makes our mana pool kind of irrelevant.

5) Resists - For most people resists would be at the bottom of their list. Resists seem to do practically nothing in PvE. Bard song + seasons + guard of druzzil + tribunal should handle all of your resist needs on raids, unless you have absolutely horrible base resists. The only resist that should really be important for most shamans is MR. Dots you can cure, nukes you can heal, but if you get rooted or snared it can mean serious trouble. I, however, like resists a lot. I duel a lot, and I also have a bit of a fetish for seeing really great resists.

6) AC - Good to have but I'm pretty convinced it doesn't do much for us after 1200. If an item has AC, great, but my decision on whether or not to claim an item is not swayed at all by its AC. My AC is significantly lower than most shamans with gear on the same level of mine, mainly because my secondary (agnarr orb) has no AC at all, whereas most other shamans have a VT shield or something like that with massive AC.

7) Everything else - My wisdom is capped, and my stamina is almost capped (easily caps with self buffs), so I don't need these any more. Everything else is pretty worthless for a shaman.
