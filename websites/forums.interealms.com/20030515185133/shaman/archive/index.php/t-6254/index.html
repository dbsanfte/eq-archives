Hail Taladean,

Sorry to hijack this thread momentarily, but I had to respond.

You realize what you are doing to all the shaman on your server, right?

Other, NON-shaman selling their JBB and other shaman gear will see YOUR prices, and price their own wares accordingly. Some shaman themselves may alter their pricing to somewhere between your price and the standard price, hoping to undercut you but make more profit than standard pricing schemes. This whole process repeats itself and can artificially inflate the Shaman gear economy for awhile without actually changing the supply of items at all. Demand is still high, so pricing high means others will too, and some poor 45+ something shaman is going to take it in the shorts to get his/her JBB. :(

The Uber-twink shaman knows what the going rate for a JBB is, and will likely not pay but a few extra kpp tops for the convenience of "buying it NOW". Or want to trade uber gear with an uber friend for one. Pricing items at double cost isn't doing anyone any favors, and I doubt you'll sell anything at that cost, unless you are selling Malo/Torpor. :p (Maybe not even Torpor now that Kragg's Mending can be had for 5pp & change.)

I highly doubt you'll sell items at 2x (or more) standard pricing.
On many servers, there are people who develop reputations for gouging prices on items like this, and it isn't one I'd ever want to have. Pricing fairly (or even low) may increase your "faction" with other players such that some time down the road you aren't going into hock paying some outrageous price for something YOU want. I know I keep a list of people I won't do business with because of shady/mercenary trading practices.

The Golden Rule comes into play here in my opinion. Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.

That's why I price my extra spells at the lowest (or close to them) advertised in the bazaar with my Trader Mule. Extra spells that I have that a friend (or heck, even an acquaintance) could use, I generally GIVE away freely, or at minimum cost. This has served me well so far. YMMV.

Take care,
