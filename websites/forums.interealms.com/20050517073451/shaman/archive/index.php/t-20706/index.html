I've been answering this question alot lately. AA path is pretty individual, tailored to your preferences. But what I say to shaman working on AA is this:

The hallmark of our class is our utter, inexorable survivability. We don't put out a pounding under normal circumstances, and we aren't great at keeping others alive in day-to-day situations. What we ARE good at is surviving long past when the cleric and druid have bit the dust.

Bearing that in mind, I recommend the following AA path (this is assuming SCRM/MGB/Can5)

RC1
LR5
VP3
PP5
HA3
HG3
AHA3
AHG3

AFter that it's a free for all.

But those first three are must-haves if you want to walk away from insanity with your life. LR5 is huge.
Caanus Grey
Ignoring all other AA and just focusing on your CA/CS question this is my opinion.

CA and LR mean avoid hits completely. As such, it is prone to EQs RNG. That means streaks of not getting touched, and others of being slapped like nobody's business.

If you love to slow early and normally wind up getting a round or 2 of aggro, this can help a bit with getting in a second slow when needed.

If you farm a lot, this means you can start tanking 10 or 20 greenies at once and still casting spells. Pretty nice IMO, but useless to a non tradeskiller.


CS and ID mean that you take less damage per hit. But ONLY on the hits that did not hit for full. The usefullness of this is closely tied to your AC. I would say if you are over 1100 and in Luclin or Tier 2 or lower PoP that this is a decent option.

The way I have read that this particular AA works is that it adds a percentage to the amount of damage you DID NOT take. So if your check against AC says you should take 100 less than the max hit of the mob, you will take 105 less with a 5% mod from this AA.

So for your particular situation of heading to VT, if your AC caps 1100 with buffs, it is a toss up. If you farm for tradeskills or quests a lot, go with CA and LR. Otherwise stick with CS and ID or you probably won't ever get em
veeshanMurtak
Combat agility makes you better at channeling through getting beat on. Combat stability makes the damage you take more predictable.

Other then preventing those procs that can only go off on a hit I vaguely remember some parsing showing that the two skills prevented similar amounts of damage.

Given the above combat agility is superior if you still want to cast after you aggro (i.e. groups) and stability when someone else will heal you (i.e. raids).

The margin of that superiority is very very small however.
Jaruden
LR5 is better than ID5 for us easily IMO. If you get hit (even for less), you still might have to channel your spell cast, or you might get stunned (if non-ogre). If you don't get hit ... spell goes off with no problems. Plus, it's a hell of a lot more fun to get agro on a mob that triples for 1200 and see miss, miss, miss, miss, miss, miss =)

That being said, SCRM is better than both of those combined IMO. Ups Q healing by a huge amount, ups duration on haste if you're doing it for a group (ldon), and let's you cast less FA's than you would have otherwise (big bonus). Whether you like buffing or not, you're probably casting a lot of them, and 50% more duration is huge.

Popping at least 1 point in VP, spirit call, and radiant cure are also well worth it, so you can at least try out the skills. Odds are, you'll put 1 point in VP and immediately want 2 and 3.
