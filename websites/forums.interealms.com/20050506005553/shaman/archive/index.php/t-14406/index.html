Yes, level 60-, your cap is 255. However, as you approach your cap, the benefits that you get out of your stat have diminishing returns.

Level 61 - 65, you gain 5 points per stats to the maximum you can have, for a maximum of 280 at 65.

Level 61 - 65, you can train one point in Planar power, each gaining 5 points to the max in each stat, for a maximum of 305 at 65 with PP5.

Aikoiko

Please remember that many of the posters on this board are a bit jaded coming from having played the game for 3-4 years, 100's of AA's, full spellbooks, and top gear.

The key in your case is your current level of your Barb Shaman. If by using your sig (Shaman of the 51th Foozle) we can say that you are an lvl 51 Shaman ... then going back to your Troll Shaman is not a good choice. By level 51, you will have options with spells and gear to overcome any stat issues. But if your Shaman was same or lower level to your Troll ... then you could have a decision to make on what investment of time/effort you want to follow in either of them.

Nevertheless, the real issue in EQ is at this stage of the game ... Stat's really are meaningless. STA really does not impact fatigue. AGI impact on AC is nominal. CHA (at least for Shamans) only influences vendor prices. Etc - Etc. The game mechanics have allowed stats to blur more into marketing hype than meaningful determinates of Avatar performance. In other words, Stat's mean nothing in Everquest today.

IMHO, one of the best things SoE could do for the game is to add value of stats back into the game. Then the answer would clearly be yes ... your Barb or Troll's starting Stat's WOULD have an influence on your game play ... even at the end game.
