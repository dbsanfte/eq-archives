Enkebesh
Dragonlace reader here as well! :)

For Shaman personalities you have to distinguish between "Old school Shaman" and newer Shaman I guess.

Old School Shaman are mostly not into it because of power, we knew nothing of the power we could attain. Damn, I remember, the first time I saw Cannibalize I thought "What a useless spell" :)

For me it is utility first. We can turn other classes into gods (well theoretically, if the stats would work like we thought they did), and we can debuff the enemy to hell. And we can heal, till Level 30 we make damn good mainhealers.

Then there is diversity. We are the swiss knives of the classes, and we can turn around an xp-group from a wipe to a success in more than one ways (Im talking lower levels here, not the level 60-with Torpor variety). I had groups that saw me in three different roles as people left the groups and others came. That meant it wasnt boring for me and that was a good thing.

And I wouldnt say Ego-Problems, I would say pride. Back in those days clawing yourself through the lower levels into the range where the power slowly came meant something to us shaman. It felt like we accomplished something. Of course we were proud of it! :)
Something easily forgotten when you twink and powerlevel your Shaman through the hard stages. Along with the pride comes humiliy, and that does wonders handling the powers we finally get at the high levels ;)

And no, we arent the DoT-King, Necros are!
