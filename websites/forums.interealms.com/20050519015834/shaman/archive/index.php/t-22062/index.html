The idea that the customer is there for YOU and YOUR profit and YOUR convenience, and whose needs matter little, and not the other way around, on a practical basis does lead some businesses to fail. Providing bad service or poor goods because you don't think it matters leads many restaurants that try to cut costs by serving smaller portions or inferior food or service to close down; same with many other kinds of businesses. Looks like there are going to be alternatives to EQ in fair abundance too, finally.

An unpleasant customer, by contrast, still at least pays money. If you can't handle that, you need to get out of the business or maybe out of business period -- take up another form of work that doesn't require face to face time with customers, because you obviously can't handle it.

The last way to address that problem is to just lay a permanent attitude of not giving a damn out over everyone you meet. Lots of people don't realize how crummy this is on a personal basis and how lousy it is on a professional one; sometimes, luckily, those guys get their just deserts and go out of business, get fired, whatever.
