Ungkor
I'm looking for an effective strategy on raids to split up the duties of our debuff classes. Primarily on raids where we pull multiple targets continually.

Method 1: Each shaman is assigned one spell. One on slow, one on malos, etc.. I have found that I really despise that method. The cast times on Malos and Turgurs are long, and if 6 mobs come into camp, the one shaman on slow is just sitting their waiting for it to refresh.

Method 2: Each shaman picks a target at random and full debuffs it himself. This seems to be what most raids end up like. Works ok sometimes, but often, we'll pick the same target, and precious time is wasted.

Method 3: (never tried it, but am thinking about it). Debuffers are assigned to tanks. The tanks need to figure out who is responsible for offtanking. Two shaman are for MA/SA, then each addition bst, shaman, or enc is assigned to an offtank. These people have three assist keys. If their third assist has a mob, they debuff that mob first, then backup the shaman supporting the ma/sa.

I don't know. I think 3 would work well, but its a lot of organizing. We now have all debuffers (mostly) using a separate channel, which seems to work well.

Any advice would be welcome.

BlargX
We have two teams. If we have only one shaman per team, then one shaman IS the team. We call out our debuffs in a debuffer channel, along with all the other debuffers -- bards, chanters, mages, druids, shamans.

The first team assists the main tank, or whoever takes over if the main tank dies. The second team is on adds, hopefully picking the unmezzables first.

A team consists of one guy doing malos and the other doing slow.

That way, the main tank's mob is almost always slowed very quickly, and if nobody sees a slow message being hotkeyed out, they know they had better stop slowing adds or just fooling around somewhere and stop the main tank's mob immediately.

So teams 1 and 2 have a possible four people in them, all combined.

Extra guys slow extra adds, do malosinia if needed. Some like to do cripple -- fine. Everyone uses their best judgment to do their job the best they know how. Guys who are laggy are never put into the first team, or guys who say they feel off or tired that day.

As an officer, I am often busy either taking names for who wants in on loot, dealing with dropping hope stones and fragments, looking up DKP, shuffling groups around so they stay balanced, etc. So I slow when I can, but combining my other duties with my frequent lagginess, I try to make it so the raid doesn't rely much on my slow/malos.

We set up druids to do ro(not the DOT one), eci, er I think one more, spacing just now; mages do malosinia, and at least one bard to HOS the main mob and one the adds. Everyone hits their hotkeys when their job is done so we all know where we're at except for chanters, since they're sometimes too busy with mezzing to tash, so they come into and out of the loop at their own discretion.

It's a bit confusing to get used to, and most people don't like getting used to it, much less setting it up for their class or other people's classes. It's so much different than what people are used to when they just do regular groups. But it promotes order and discipline and reminds people that we are all supposed to not be AFK and working together; that in itself makes the system worthwhile even if there were no conceivable benefits as far as the actual debuffing, to my mind. You're much less likely to AFK or play like a dope when everyone's watching you and waiting for your hotkey to be on the right mobs at the right time.

We do it the same way on every mob we can get to, trash or not.

I find varying your approach on this kind of thing mob by mob can only lead to confusion and arguments and people finding some way to justify slacking off or screwing up. Just do it, do it right, and do it all the time, and everything will be fine, nobody will say or think you're not doing your job or get resentful or put on airs.

It's helpful to prevent the psychological "star system" that so many people want to become the focal point of -- you know, slowing and debuffing and generally doing things almost at random or very capriciously, leading people to dread their next tantrum, lack of initiative, or, bout of AFK fever.

Wildarea Deathwitch
I find it easiest to have every shaman make 3 hotkeys.

One = ;(whatever your channel name is Malos on < %t (target) > in 5 seconds (or less for BoR and spell haste
Have it cast malos 2 times with a pause 3 in between each cast, in case of fizzle/lag

So mine looks like:
; Malos will land on ~> %t <~ in 3.8 seconds
/cast 1
/pause 3
/cast 1

This hotkey serves to tell the other shaman what mob you are casting on, whoever hits this hotkey first, casts, and if someone also hit thiers, they duck out and hit the slow spell. Key thing is all mobs are to be malosed before they are slowed, to cut down on resists and shaman agro.

The next hotkey tells if a mob is slowed, and in my guild we put it in both the raid, and channel.

Mine looks like this:

; ~> %t <~ is PARTIALLY slowed.
/rs ~> %t <~ is PARTIALLY slowed. (alternate /rs for ;2 when my guild does PoTime, so instead of spamming raid, i spam our sections raid channel for p1 and p2.)

I never understood why one of the shaman wanted to say if a mob is partially slowed or not, since 90 % of the mobs in Elemental+ are partial, with most of the exeptions being during Fennin script and PoTime. Only way for this to really work is to make 4 slow hotkeys, one for each type of slow, partial, mostly, slightly, and full. I don't know about most shaman, but my hotkey page already has too much stuff on it.

Anyways, as soon as malos lands that shaman hits thier slow spell gem, casts slow and if it hits, check to see if another shaman has already said it was slowed, if they did, then you dont say anything, if they did not then they hit the next hotkey and YOU hit this one, stating that Target is now slowed.

The last hotkey is for a resisted slow. My guilds healers rule, so this has not really been needed, but the theory is that if slow is bounced, the shaman would take agro, and be tanking a very hard hitting mob. So in turn, the other shaman would be healing this shaman at the same time they are trying to slow the mob.

Looks like this:

; Slow was resisted by ~> %t <~
/em tried to slow ~> %t <~ but was resisted

The /em is to let other people know that slow was resisted without adding more to raid spam.

Since we statred to use this system, shaman don't die as much, mobs are slowed faster, and it provides more healing power to the raid, since after all present mobs are slowed, we switch to assist healing. For me I use two assist keys, one is to assist the main agro, and another is just a plain assist key, to assist whoever my target is. So I hit they MA assist key then hit my heal assist key to find a target to heal, works well when agro is bounceing all over the place.

System works well for us, but its not for everyone, key is to reduce spam in raid say and still communicate with other debuffers on what is going on. Chanters keep tash in channel and only have messages for what is mezzed in raid say, shaman keep everything but slow messages in channel.

Shaman in my guild dont cripple or cast malosinia. The few of us that have clicky neck from VT use that when we can, but for the most part, we leave those jobs to the chanters and magicians. We do this in part because of the high mana cost of slow and malos, and the long cast/recast times associated with our spells. The other part is neither mages nor chanters can cast heal, while we can.

Goldark
I guess it depends what we're raiding and how many shaman we have.

Quite often I don't find slowing trash mobs is particularly effective. In those cases we generally say each shaman to themselves, and we'll announce in channel what mobs we've slowed.

If mobs tend to be single but steady pulls, and hit hard, then I quite like to split malos/turgurs between shaman. This allows very fast slows but with multiple pulls it doesn't work so well.

For multiple really tough mobs, e.g. Sol Ro tower doing Dresolik, we'll assign a shaman to each tank. That shaman then assists their tank and fully debuffs. If there aren't enough slowers you can get beastlords to help, and shaman can double back to support the beastlord once their first mob is done. Doing this on trash clearing though means you are now doubly reliant on the offtanks picking up mobs first. I don't think this is a good idea unless you really know they are on the ball.

Boss mobs, we'll generally pre-assign someone for the unresistable debuff (malos or MD) and everyone else will try to land the resistable ones (slow/cripple etc)

Overall my general opinion is that it really doesn't make any significant difference. So long as trash dies fast, offtanks and chanters are on the ball and nobodies off afk, people don't tend to die clearing trash on raids very much.
