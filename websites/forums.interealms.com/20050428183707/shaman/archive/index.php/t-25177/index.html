I'll agree with Zaug, Nadox at 55 is tough work - lotta hp on the mobs and iffen you run into the caster types it can get quite nasty... with 2 people or 3 it would be worth it but not solo. However - there is the benefit of getting some spell quest items in there. Blood of Nadox which is very much worth the effort - and if I recall very good exp on the final turn in.

Torgirin mines would be fine - tons of blue mobs right from the entrance and they just get progressively tougher the deeper you go. The wandering Trolls are a notch up in difficulty as compared to the static mobs that they patroll - no casters really except for the occasional named.

Nurga is a nice little place - but plenty of casters in there and pulls aren't always solo like they usually are in LOY zones. Can be tricky but very rewarding...NOTE: bring weapons for pets as nothing in here drops even a rusty dagger.

The Enraged Soulstealer camp is a very good spot at lvl 55. Many things are light blue up until his PH but his PH and anything past there is blue. Casters aren't that nasty either except for the SS himself.

You could solo in PoJustice no problems. Pulling and agro seemed pretty good when I was in there - had no trouble getting singles (trap rooms excluded). Gotta be able to tank em though so would recommend a virtue beforehand.

PoInnovation is soloable but a step up from Justice and I usually pulled with Malo in there instead of just slow...Very difficult to take 2 mobs on respawn pulls though.

You could do bugs in SolB...even pull the odd imp but be forewarned - they are old world agro ranges and LoY type zones have spoiled one into a false sense of security at times. Very good exp there though.

Skyfire sucks in every aspect...I wouldn't recommend this zone to anyone and yet I enjoy it there...maybe because it almost always empty. ALthough I think that's not much of a factor anymore in any zone in EQ nowadays.


Good luck. If I think of more I'll add to the list...

Zone into the Plane Of Tranquility and then look on the map. You will see a windmill...run down there and then enter. Once inside, go find the little Gnome standing there. Then turn and look at the center column of the windmill and look up. You will see one working windmill thingy (left side) and a broken one on the right. Click on the broken one and voila! You are now in PoI...and no you don't need a flag. No clue about PoP armour - someone else will have to answer that for you.
