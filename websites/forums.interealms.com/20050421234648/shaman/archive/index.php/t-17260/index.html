junkilo
I finally figured out how to split the 2 entrance mobs without much difficulty.

1. maloXXX one of them and zone
2. maloXXX the other one and zone.
3. slow the one you intend to pull and zone
4. root the one you didnt slow and pull him back to the zone and root him and zone.
5. zone in refresh root, summon pet and go to town.
6. refresh slow.
6. make sure you give pet a full set of goodies if you are using the lv 55 pet. Its important to make sure he stays on the top of the aggro list if you dont want to be summoned. Its easier to let him do the dps with your dots trailing behind so you can focus on keeping mana up.

I did this at 57, but I'd imagine it would be a whole lot easier with canni4 and kraggs.

Poguk
This zone is a good challenge. I'm not one who gets to solo much, being the main shaman in my guild, so I get a lot of requests for groups. But that doesn't mean I don't get the hankering to solo every once in awhile!

I usually do CT, 'cause it's a challenge, and there's a small chance for decent loot. So far I've only done the entrance/CY. But I've got it down pretty good.

The 2 door mobs at the entrance are always melee mobs. Either a warder or warrior. Lotsa hps and interrupt casting annoyingly often, but they do not cast any spells. Get setup in the corner, summon dogdog, buff him, get buffed up, have slow and root memmed (the 64 root is excellent), then root one of them.

The other will run towards you, so target him and sic dogdog on him before he gets to ya. Dogdog grabs agro pretty quick.

Slow the one dogdog is on, or dogdog will meet a quick death. This will bring agro onto you, but not a biggie. He's slowed, so you should be able to take his hits. Then re-target the rooted lizard down the hall, Malosinia him, slow him, then re-apply root. Now you've got both of them in a very controlled situation.

Go back to the one beating on you, DoT the shiot out of him, keep you and dogdog healed and he'll go down in less time than it takes for the other to break root.

Get healed up and get some mana on ya and about that time the other root will break... re-slow him, and do the same thing. DoT, DoT, DoT. Soon ya got the 2 door lizards dead and split up pretty good.

Now the fun part! Open the door to the courtyard, and there is a campfire right there. 2 mobs spawn here and can be any type of lizard. Sometimes you'll get 2 melee mobs and if so, then use the same tactic to split them as you did the door mobs. Not that hard.

But half the time a caster or 2 will spawn here, and these guys can make life a living hell! Make sure you have dogdog parked back at the entrance, and be ready to spam the "pet back off" key. Root one of the casters and RUN back to the entrance to avoid the incoming nukes. Chances are you will get nuked anyway. This will 'cause dogdog to go running after the caster, and this will mess you up bad. The most important thing in this fight is to keep dogdog away from the casters while you are splitting and pulling them!

These casters dispell, nuke and most of all, poison AoE. This sucks! If you're like me and poison resists are your worst resist, these rains will do a good number of damage to ya. Make sure you have yer best poison/disease resists up!

Anyway, at this point, you and dogdog should be back at the entrance with *one* caster engaged. Malosinia and slow him.

Then! run back to the mob outside at the campfire, malosinia him, then root him! Now root should disable this mob for the rest of the fight. Run back to the entrance, where dogdog is most likely in a world of hurt from the caster rains. Torpor/Stoic/Q can keep him alive while you do that.

Casters have less hps than the melee mobs, but do a heckuva lot more damage, and never seem to run out of mana, so the real battle is staying alive and killing the caster as quick as you can. I usually mem Velium Strike for this, 'cause the casters usually have high poison resists and will resist your poison nukes, even after Malosinia.

Keep yours and dogdog's health high and then Nuke like a mofo! Burn him down, and it should only take a min or 2 to kill him.

Now you still have one mad caster rooted out in the courtyard. You have a couple of choices here; zone out and clear agro and then come back in and lick yer wounds...

Or be a man, and confront him! Make sure you got enough juice to do it, then wait for the root to break.

Same thing, just keep yourself Torpor'd, and nuke him into the ground. Like I said, these casters will chain AoE poison rain all over you, and it's not uncommon for me to walk away from those battles minus dogdog, as he doesn't resist the rains very well.

But once you have those mobs split up, you now have a fairly easy job of doing the entrance and CY now with single pulls, which makes things much easier.

The raptors will sometimes add -- these guys are pussies, and once slowed are a cinch. I normally just make sure me or dogdog doesn't do any damage to them ('cause they'll summon you and annoy the crap outta you) and keep them rooted off in the distance until the first mob is dead. Then quickly dispatch of the raptor. They have less hps, so they're much easier to take down. They're mainly just for annoyance, as they don't drop much of anything and give very little xp.
