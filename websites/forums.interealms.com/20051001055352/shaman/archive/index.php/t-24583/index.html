weissblau
I could have sworn I posted this already in another thread, but here it is again. I levelled from 61-63 almost exclusively on these puppies.

I am assuming you do NOT have VP3. No pet. SOW on. Go to the NE corner of the zone, where you have plenty of wall space to kite on.

Pull with Malo/Malos at maximum range (unresistable).

Run directly to wall while spell gems are refreshing.

Root the mob. I use the level 14 plain ole root for 30 mana. I usually turn to face the mob for a very practical reason - if I wait until I have turned to face, then I have been in that spot long enough that I won't get a spell interruption. You should be able to root the mob before it gets to you, but I have VERY rarely had a root resisted after Malo/s was already in. If you get a resist or interruption, just start running again along the wall and start over.

Apply Malosi/nia. It should land in 1-2 tries since Malo/s is already on. This little bit of extra insurance lowers the MR a bit more and makes root stick a little better.

Apply Turgur's and DR debuffer.

Apply the DOTs that do not break root - Breath of Ultor, Blood of Saryryn, Bane/Anathema/Odium, Epic, whatever you have. I always apply epic first because hey, it's free, and because you have to get REAL close to the mob to apply it. After epic, retreat to maximum cast range to give yourself more time to react on a root break. Refresh Root every once in a while, it only lasts 60 seconds.

Now either just refresh your non-breaking DOTs if you have enough of them or start applying the root-breakers. In either case, when root breaks you run away until your spells are refreshed and you have a good lead on the mob, then turn and root. I heartily recommend the slower, more mana-efficient disease-based DOTs since the longer run time means less breaking of root when you have to recast compared to poison DOTs.

I do not use a horse when root-rotting with regular Root since it is so unreliable and I may need to run at any time. At all times while casting, have your fingers on the D key to interrupt your cast if root breaks, and reroot. If you are at max cast range you can reroot before the mob gets to you. If you have JUST cast the spell when root breaks, you may have to run away until the spell gems refresh.

If you have VP, pull with DR debuffer instead and root with VP, but have regular root up as well since golems WILL break VP.

Main point to remember is getting a group that can chain pull will get you more AAXP per hour than soloing can. But at 5% AA per 6 min kill at lvl 65, now that sounds like a winner...

I'm 65, but not Ele/VT/VP/GoD flagged at all. With tribute, I can get 5.6k now self-buffed and with Conv + BSS/SV. Only Ornate armor I have are the Scaled Avatar's Gloves. I don't have one item on me that is 100+ HP, but I'm not afraid to tank. So 5k + buffed is very possible, Sudancia.

Chain-pulling in BoT is my preferred method of AA grinding... it's relatively safe, easy, and quick to find decent grps. I like to pull for the group, as well as MH, slow, and DOT to add DPS. South or Deep south are easy to single pull. My philosophy on shaman pulling is 1) you're in control of the situation, 2) by pulling with Malo/s, then running to camp, you can guarantee mob is slowed as soon as it's in camp, 3) you keep all DPS members at camp, and keep their hands full and busy = maximum DPS for group. Just make sure to go and get next mob before the current one is dead (around 10-20%, depending on group DPS). When first mob dies, group will have another in camp ready to kill. Main exception to this is of course when you have mobs that gate....wait till it's dead to be safe.

I park puppy in camp, pull with Malo (I only have MP3), sic puppy on mob in camp (faster than tank getting his aggro), then slow right away. Group engages, I throw on a Bane, cap off the MT with Q, and either join in and melee for fun (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=43418 with http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=42118 and http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=24753 and SCF1 and 280 max stats with Voice of the Berserker). Or I pull up mount quick, cani4/5, unclick mount, then go and pull next. Every few rounds you will not be able to afford a DOT, so throw in your puppy pack to make up for the DPS, or don't. Not throwing on a DoT slows group DPS, which buys you more time that round to get mana back anyway. You don't have to be near FM to keep going... push it until around 20% mana...more than likely your canni5 will refresh soon enough.

Using your available tools (including advice from this forum), will help make your shammy as powerful as it can get. Not many classes at 65 can solo 5 summoning giants in a row in BoT DS while waiting to get another grind group going...
