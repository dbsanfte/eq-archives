PennyAntics
Please realize that I realize that none of the following will ever happen. Sony is too stupid. They will just continue to nerf the usefulness of tradeskills to oblivion.

Sony, it really isn't that hard to make a tradeskill model which works. You are totally afraid of allowing us to create items which will sell for profit, with the only limiting factors being the speed at which you can do a series of clicks. So what's the solution? Add more limiting factors, genious.

1) All tradeskill products will yield 50% to 150% of the cost required to make it. This large range varies not based on Charisma but based on which merchant you are selling it to - IE, the Freeport Bartender wants Batwing Crunchies so will give you 150% of cost for it. However, Soulbinder Mira couldn't care less about your crunchies.

2) Stacking is gone. No tradeskill components are stackable. Period.

3) repetitive clicking is gone. Skillups are guarenteed on successful combines - however, the failure rate is very very high, until you approach the trivial level. From there it goes up exponentially, until you have 100% chance of success.

4) NPC Stocks are limited. Each tradeskill item-selling NPC will have, at most, 40-50 of each tradeskill item, 10 are added every 8 realtime hours.

5) NPC Money is limited. All merchants have a limited amount of platinum on them to return. Once they are out of money, they can no longer buy from you, or anyone else. They will start with about 500pp and add 100pp per 8 realtime hours.



If you'll notice, this makes the bottlenecks in tradeskills no longer how much clicking you can do and how much money you have - but instead, the bottlenecks are NPC item availability and money availability, as well as knowing how much each tradeskill product sells for to each vendor. A tradeskiller who skills for profit will no doubt have a daily routine: Bake 12 pies, sell them to 'X' Merchant in Halas. Forge 15 silver spoons, sell them to 'Y' merchant in Plane of tranquility. He could not simply amass silver spoons and sell them for limitless amounts of platinum - his resources would run out, and the NPC buying them would run out of money. This forces the tradeskiller to actually invest time running to merchants delivering batches of each product to where it yeilds the best profit. Popular and most profitable places would run out of resources quickly as other players used them, so tradeskillers would have to be diverse and know of other lucrative places to craft and sell, they could not have one set-in-stone routine for mega ppzorz.


And that, Sony, is how you do it.
