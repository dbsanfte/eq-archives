Thats a nice computer. I personally wouldn't want a flat panel monitor. But if you going to get one, dell makes some of the best. The price for a pre-built pc is good too. I wouldn't plan on it taking you for 4 years though, I think within 2 years 64 bit will start to move into games with longhorn and windows XP both comming in 64bit versions for consumers.

If you were building a computer I would definatly recomend AMD, but its hard to find a good amd pre-built, so for the money, I'd say thats a good deal.

As for the raid 0, you wont notice it all that much. I run raid on my machines

p4 3.0ghz 1 gig ram, ati 9800 pro, audigy 2 zs., 3 sata 200 gig drives(raid) (windows xp) and a p4 3.0ghz 1 gig ram nvidia 5900, audigy 2 zs , 4 sata 160 gig drives (raid) (debian unstable)

You will only notice the raid 0 on disk intensive applications.

In any case, i'd say if you have the money, and dont want to do it yoruself, thats a good buy.

I went and looked up your basic system, getting good at this now after three weeks, and it looks good save for the monitor. I realize it's a free upgrade but sometimes free isnt the best. It has a response time of 25ms. If you play first person shooters or graphic intesnive games your gonna get ghosting with this monitor. I would recommend dumping the monitor, theres a button saying no monitor and it will save you 90 bucks on your system. Then go to newegg.com and type NU in the search screen. Nu makes a flat panel monitor with the exact same specs as the sony's but cost only $250. It has a refresh rate of 16ms which is ideal for gaming unless you can afford a 12ms refresh rate monitor. If you really didnt want a flat pannel at all bank the $90 and buy some coke's.
