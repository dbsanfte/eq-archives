It all boils down to faith - and faith comes in many flavors.

There are those that have faith in the existence of a supreme being and choose to acknowledge that faith through the practice of organized religion. Call them religious. They say, "I have seen the light - it is truth and good"

There are those that have faith in the existence of "something out there greater than we can understand" but disdain many of the bad things surrounding organized religion. Call them agnostic. They say, "I see light, but wonder about its source."

There are those that have faith that science is the unfettered search for the truth, uninfluenced by archaic religious dogma. Call them scientists. They say, "Light is but a series of photons that behave both as particles and waves - sometimes simultaneously."

There are those that have faith the billions of religious practitioners and spiritual people in the world are wrong and are certain their belief in "what you see is what you get" is the truth. Call them atheists. They say, "Light? Dark? What's that got to do with reality?"

Then there's me. I try to observe things that go on around me in a spiritual way, believe some things are inherently good, some things are inherently bad, and situational ethics are a cop-out for the intellectually challenged. Much of what we see is currently unexplainable, and therefore should be investigated thoroughly by the means available to us. Call me undecided. I say, "The light is shiny and good, but why does it exist in the first place?"
