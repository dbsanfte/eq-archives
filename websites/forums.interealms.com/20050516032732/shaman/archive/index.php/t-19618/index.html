It's a fun ability for just 4 points. You can use it to help with pulling - just send in your dogs on the mob and all the adds will attack them, then pull off adds one at a time with slow. You can also use it to help push mobs in to corners on raids.

Trevurg
I picked up Spirit Call 3 somewhere in my 40s or 50s in terms of AAs, don't regret it at all.

Very very few things can channel a CH or a gate through the five additional dogs and your pet beating on them. It also lets you position things just where you want them, or distract a second mob while you deal with a first.

It depends a lot on your playstyle. I do almost all my playtime in small, non-perfect group situations where everyone has to be flexible and any little trick you have helps.

Someone who spends all their time in PoP or LDoN pickups or a full time raider wouldn't place the same importance on it. All depends on your priorities. :)

I think all three of the PoP ability style AAs (RC, VP, SC) are really handy in terms of extending our class, not just making you more efficient at stuff you can already do.

Calling it DD is a bit of a misnomer, since it does no damage. It's more like an instant aggro component, a fraction of a second before your first pet is off and running.

It can actually be quite useful, in my opinion, since it seems to be very minor aggro, and it can be used to pull your first target slightly away from any other mobs that might add if you cast a higher aggro spell.

I recently tried a triple pull with SC1. To my surprise, the mob came solo, he got about 5 yards before my dogs intercepted him. But far enough not to cause adds. By the time he was hit with malos, slow and root, he'd killed a dog, but it worked very well.

Previously, on the same pull, pulling with Malos resulted in a triple pull.

There may have been other factors involved, but for the life of me I can't think what they were.
