Gardeltron
Our guild just got RZ, and we've just begun to filter into the elemental planes. My impressions of the shaman's role in experience groups in these planes is not encouraging.

I've grouped in Water and Earth so far with guild mates and here's what I've seen. First, everything is mitigated, period. Unless its immune. Second, resists are very high. I've found that I sometimes cast slow 3 or 4 times after Malos before it lands, only to be mitigated. For many mobs, by the time its slowed, its 3/4 dead. I end up expending so much mana on slowing, that I have little left for other things. Third, there's really only one "other thing" that I do with what little mana is left, and that's spot heal if there is no enchanter for CC. Finally, I get the impression that my ONLY contribution to the group is the buffs (I've started trying to keep FA on the melees because that seems to make some difference).

My impression is that the optimal elemental exp group is 1 cleric, 1 enchanter (for mana regen, CC, and if there's time, a slow or two), and 4 dps. Slowing just isn't worth the mana. Since shamans can't generate much DPS with DoTs, I end up feeling I'm not contributing much to the group. I should just sit down, keep my mana high, and chain cast FA.

Basically, even though the exp is very nice in the elemental planes I've visited, I feel my role in exp groups has greatly diminished. Most groups would be better served by someone two-boxing their shaman twink for buffs outside the group.

I haven't raided in the elemental planes yet, and I'm assuming that the shaman's role in elemental raids isn't too different from before. Maybe I'll feel better when we get to raiding and I begin to roll in the phat elemental planes lewtz. But I'll feel a bit guilty about taking that loot since I'm taking stuff from other classes whose roles are much more meaningful.

Someone please tell me it gets better than being a one-dimensional buffer in the elemental planes.
Jinheim
In my experience in EP, slow isn't necessary, but it is damn nice to have. 1 warrior + 1 cleric with no slow can manage, but if the cleric screws up or there is an add or something, chances are pretty good that there are going to be deaths. On top of that, the cleric will probably start having mana problems, since they have to CH and blast a LOT vs. unslowed mobs.

Slow isn't required, but it makes things much safer. On top of that, it is very good to have a backup healer in EP groups. I am usually kept on my toes healing people. A chanter can slow, but they cannot heal. This makes shamans good to have. In addition, almost every mob in PoE has some kind of nasty proc that you can only get rid of with radient cure. Our ability to RC also makes us valuable here.

The mobs in PoE are very resistant. PoWater and Air they are pretty resistant, but no as bad. PoFire mobs, though, have much lower resists. I almost always land slow on my first try, there, and PoFire will be your exp spot as soon as you have access, which shouldn't be too long. Solro is a gimp compared to RZTW.

Basically, a shaman in pre-EP planes groups can make or break the group. A good shaman can pretty much insure that a group will succeed anywhere prior to EPs. In EPs, though, you are going to need a full group of competent people in order to do well. A shaman is still a valuable addition, but we aren't the deciding factor of whether or not a group will succeed. This, imho, is not really a bad thing. I think we're balanced just fine, now. That is just my opinion though, lots will disagree.

Talking exp groups, not raiding: Admittedly I've only grouped in the elemental planes for experience a couple of times, but I wouldn't say we're reduced to buff bunnies by any means!

Take for example a group I was in, in earth yesterday. There, most things mitigate slow, though few are totally immune. Even so, though, I think I was a valuable member of that group. Slows, resist debuffs, buffs, backup heals, damage.... I was even off-tanking occasionally.

Even if a mob is only slightly slowed, the tank is still taking less damage, and even if a mob mitigates, shamans are still the best slowers.

I malos (at least) everything, it helps the nukers get more value for their mana.

My buffs help the whole group - HP, haste, atk. I don't see anyone complain there.

Backup heals save lives, save clerics mana. Depending on the cleric, I often find that helping out with a Quiescence here and there makes the difference between clerics going low mana or not. It's silly if I'm at 80% mana when the cleric is 20%.

I see damage as the least of all the things I do, but it's another way I can contribute. It all adds up.

Off-tanking when we're low on melee or someone's gone LD. It's not ideal, and soaks up healing mana, but it's doable in a pinch. Even I could do this, with only around 5000 hp buffed and decidedly non-uber gear. Once a mob was slowed, I could keep Quiescence on myself, Tnargs as necessary, and hold my own for long enough for the group to kill the first mob and help me out. Did I like it? No, not really, but I could do it!

Slow is our big thing, the one thing we're better than any other class at. But even if mobs routinely mitigate most of our slows, there's still so many other ways we can contribute.

I've only recently joined an elemental guild and got flagged, but none of my time there, raiding or grouping, has been boring or one dimensional!
