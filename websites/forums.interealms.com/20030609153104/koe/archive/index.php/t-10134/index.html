Buttermilk Pumpkin PIE

Ingredients:
Pie crust
3 eggs
1 egg yolk
3/4 cup firmly packed light brown sugar
2 tbsp. all-purpose flour
1 tsp. pumpkin pie spice
1 tsp vanilla extract
3/4 cup canned pumpkin puree
1 1/4 cups buttermilk

Method:
Roll out dough on lightly floured surface to 12 inch round. Carefully transfer to deep 9-inch pie pan. Press into pan. Trim and decoratively crimp edges. Let stand in refrigerator for 2 hours or overnight. Freeze until firm, about 15 minutes. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Bake crust until just golden, about 15 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to 350 degrees F.

Meanwhile, whisk eggs, egg yolk, and sugar in large bowl until blended. Mix in flour, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla. Stir in pumpkin and butter, then buttermilk. Pour filling into crust. Bake until set (center will puff slightly) about 45 minutes. Cool completely.


Beer Bread
Ingredients:
3 cups flour
3 1/2 tsp baking powder
2 tsp salt
1 tbsp sugar
1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
12 oz beer (gotta use cheap stuff...better beers don't have nearly enough yeast to make the bread rise. Budwiser works well.)

Method:
Grease 9x5x3" loaf pan. Combine flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar in a large bowl. Add beer and cheese, mix well. Spread batter in prepared pan. Bake at 350 F for 45 minutes. Turn out on rack to cool. Let cool completely before slicing.

Not a huge recipie type of person, but this dip goes well with chips, crackers, etc.

1 jar of salsa
1 2 pound block of velveeta
1 can of chili
1 pack taco seasoning mix

Mix chili, salsa and taco mix in a microwave bowl. Add peper, chili powder, tabasco, etc to taste. Put in microwave for about 12 minutes, stirring once (should be warm enough that a bit of steam is coming off).

Melt cheese in another bowl. Add other mix to cheese after it is melted. Stir and mix well.

I think it is better if you put it in the fridge and let it sit over night, but you can use it then. To serve, warm slightly and stir.

You can adjust the "heat" with what type of salsa you use along with what type of velveeta and how much you throw in for spices.

So what is a good Thanksgiving wine? Well don't go with the old addage "white wine with white meat, red wine with red meat." Do both! I think a Riesling (off dry) will please even the "I don't like wine" people as well as match up with that cranberry sauce, yams or whatever sweet thing on the table. If you like reds, go for a Pinot Noir, or if price is not an issue, go for the French Pinot Noir, Burgundy. Others worth mentioning: Chianti or Sangiovese and Zinfandel (not white, but red).

Main thing is just enjoy the day, the company, the food, and the wine! Just make sure you don't drink too much, so you can sneak on line later to say "Hi" to all your Turkeys in EQ!!

Poppin Cranberry Sauce
1lb fresh cranberries
3 Oranges
Half a Lemon
Red Wine
Brown Sugar
Regular Sugar
Karo Syrup
1 cup water

In a med sauce pan add the cranberries the sugar water and wine. Mix it together gets kinda thick until the sugar breaks down. Chop the lemon and Oranges into pretty small pieces. I cut them in 4ths and then slice them down from there. Add the lemon and orange. Over med heat let it cook for about 40-60mins. The cranberries will pop as they cook. Cheesecake Pumpkin Pie

1 can evaporated Milk
1 big can of pumkin solid NOT the MIX
Cinnamon
Vanilla
3 oz stick of Philly cream cheese softened
Frozen or homade pie crust
2 eggs
Karo syrup 1/2 cup
Brown sugar or reg sugar your preferrence

Preheat oven to 325 which from cookin the bird it should be :D

In a med size mixing bowl beat the cream cheese and no Baracus not with a mallet :P. With the mixer on med speed beat it until it gets kind of fluffy. Add 1/4 cup of karo syrup(half of the half cup) and vanilla. After it's mixed up pretty good set it to the side.

In a second bowl add the pumkin lump(can of solid pumpkin), eggs, evaporated milk, remaining karo syrup, sugar, cinnamon and small pinch of salt. Beat until smooth. Pour pumpkin mix into pie crust.

Add tablespoons of the cream cheese and I use a fork or thin spatula to mix it in. It also adds that swirled marbled look.
bake for 50-60 mins and boomba you got a kickin pumpkin pie.
For a twist you can make it with a graham cracker crust.

The kicker to all this is it serves 4-6ppl easy and I get it usually at under 50 bucks.
