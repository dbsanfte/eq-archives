Boinshar
CS does not work the way you assume. It is possible to decrease a mob's overall damage without touching its max hit.

I remember with the onset of Luclin, warriors were eagerly parsing CS to figure out how it worked, since the assumption was that it was like a mini-defensive. The conclusion they reached was CS3 gives a 10% chance to lower a mob's DI (damage interval). Hence while they parsed at 10% less damage taken, the max hit remained the same.
Giac
CS works like this(at least that's how it seems to work to me):

Let's say the max hit of X mob is 500 and his min hit is 100.

Everytime you mitigate a portion of the damage(i.e. you do not get hit for full(500 in this example)), you will additionally mitigate a percentage(let's say 10% for CS 3) based on the damage you mitigated.

So a CA 0 Shaman will get hit for 450, while a CA 3 Shaman will take 445 damage(10% of 50 = 5) from the same hit. You won't drop below the min hit however and obviously if you mitigate 0 you will still get hit for 500, sicne 10% of 0 is...0!

In a Nutshell....

Combat Agility Reduces actual HITS you take. CA3 has a 10% chance increace that you will Avoid a hit... it doesn't mean you'll get hit 10% less, just that you have a 10% greater CHANCE of not getting hit. Lightning Reflexes increases this chance to roughly 15%.

Combat Stability is similar in that it increase your chance to Mitigate a hit's Damage by 10% more percent, Not that you will mitigate 10% more damage overall.

Here's where Rumour Steps in... I know Several HIGH AA Clerics who have CA3/LR5/CS3 and they say the following.... with CA3/LR5, you do INDEED notice less hits. With CS3 you notice a small decrease in Damage taken, but apparently.. you also notice your ability to Channel a spell increases.

Here's what I have seen....

I play A Shaman with 88 AA, I have CA3, and LR2 so far, I buff out to 1400 AC and 6500 HP, I have Slightly more AC and HP than my friend's level 65 Ogre Warrior who DOES NOT have any points spent into Combat Agility, Stability or any other Defensive AA's besides Dura3.

Against Golems in PoV, on a Slowed Golem, I can tank about 80% as well as he can. Considering I'm a Caster with no Parry/Riposte skills and a pathetic Dodge skill.. that's a GREAT improvement.
