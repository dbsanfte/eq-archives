For my epic, we fought Rak in the hallway behind the thrown. This was because someone had killed his reaver and he had not depopped before the fight. Also, another guild was working on reavers, so we couldn't go to the "safe spot".

To prevent an already spawned Rak from moving and wiping out your party, you should:

1) Have mages park their fire pets directly in front of Rak.
2) Be sure that mages know exactly when you turn in your tear. Give them plenty of advanced notice.
3) Be as fully buffed as possible with hp and fr.
4) Slowly back away from Rak after the turn in to make sure pets have aggro.

Numrum
Losing your tear completely sucks. I feel for you. However, a little research would have prevented it. It's been known for a while that people have experienced sporatic problems with Lord Rak versus fire pets. The general consensus is that if you buff the mages fire resist above a certain level, the pets lose their immunity. In the fight that I had, I made sure nobody buffed the mages, and that the mages didn't put on fire resistance gear. We had 2 49 fire pets and 1 55 fire pet. After the fight, the clerics reported having to heal the pets only a few times, and never had to use complete healing.

The posts on this board have been thorough and concise and have worked in every instance in the epic process. You're right though, Verant should have spelled it out, but posts on this board did anyway.

Good luck in your second try.
