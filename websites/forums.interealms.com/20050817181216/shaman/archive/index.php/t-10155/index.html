Dread
When I was in my low 50s, I thought it would be awful cool to have a druid alt to duo with. I figured he could nuke for free ( lumi staff ) and heal me for free while I cannibalize ( ES greaves ). I bought him an ODS, lumi staff, ES greaves, and the cheap ES stuff for free snares, dots, lev, wolf form, etc. I leveled him up to 47 and he now has all the ports. I usually duo with a good RL friend ( 57 SK ) and thought the druid would be a good addition.

In practice, it wasn't nearly as good as it sounded on paper. He could heal some and do some small nuking, but the effect wasn't as good as I had anticipated, and he required constant attention to have noticable effect. I believe that he would be very useful if I got him high enough to get charm, but that would take a signifigant amount of work. I am glad to have him though, cause the ports are nice, but not as neccessary now with PoP.

Since then, I decided to make a melee instead. Since I almost always group with my RL SK buddy, I thought a fun DPS class would be good. I almost went rogue, but decided to make a ranger instead. I have leveled my ranger up to 51 and he has 3 AAs ( run 3 ). It took under 50 hours to PL him to 51. I really think that a ranger complements a shaman very well. First off, they are self sufficient ( unlike a warrior ), so he can be fun to solo with when I need a break from the constant buff requests I get as a shaman. Secondly, he has snare which is a critical ability that I lack as a shaman.

Here are the reasons that I picked ranger for an alt:
1. snare
2. self sufficient - sow, invis, SoE later, root, healing, etc
3. good DPS now - level 51 with windblade
4. incredible DPS later - once he gets AM3 / EQ
5. easy of duoing. It is easy to run him to the mob and turn on autoattack. If the mob starts running around, I just backup and hit my auto archery attack on the gravis gamepad.
6. Harmony pulling
7. Can pass down my Rog/Rng/Shm gear to him as I get better. Can also MQ any chain pieces I get to him if I wished.
8. Variety of grouping techniques. I can tank for him while he melees from behind. I can let him tank/melee. I can tank while he shoots, or we can root/rot with me Dotting and him using archery. We could also kite mobs, but that would seem to intensive to duo.
9. Tracking

My goal is to get him EQ/AM3 and let him pummel mobs for over 100 DPS with auto archery attack. I wouldn't have to run him around, or try to keep him behind mobs ( like a rogue ), or have to keep reaching over to cast spells, etc. No fear of ripostes or agro. Just 1 button for massive DPS boost, and 1 more button for snare.
