There's an easier way to fix it, which I've wished for before (but here I go again).

Keep all the slow percentages the same, but change the formula used to calculate their effect.

Right now we believe it is

E = B*s*m (today's formula)

Where
E = effective attack speed
B = base attack speed
s = slow amount, as a fraction
m = mitigation amount, as a fraction

what it should be is

E = B*(s-n) (proposed formula)

Where E, B, and s are the same and
n = new mitigation amount, as a fraction

I am assuming here that mitigation is implemented as a sliding number, with some mobs mitigating 100% ("immune to slow"), some at 0% (old world), and some in the middle ("mostly", "partially", etc). From what I have observed, this seems pretty likely to be the case; if it's not the case, then it's done by "binning" the mobs into categories for mostly/partially/immune, which is a subset of this technique using specific preselected values for m. I am also assuming that the calculation is multiplicative, like I show, which seems to be the consensus although I don't have any solid numbers to back that up.

OK, so there are some esoteric formulae up there... what impact would it have?

Let's say you have a shaman throwing a 75% slow, an enchanter doing a 70% slow, and a Beastlord with a 65% slow. Furthermore, let's assume that the mob mitigates slow such that when slowed by a shaman he does 50% of his normal attack rate. That makes 'm', his mitigation amount using today's techniques, 50%. 'n', his mitigation amount using the technique I am proposing, is 37.5%. The formulae become

E = B*(.75)*(.50) = B*(0.375) using today's technique, or
E = B*(.75 - .375) = B*(0.375) using my proposed technique. So far so good - there's no change in the way the shaman slows.

But the enchanter's scenario is

E = B*(.70)*(.50) = B*(0.35) (today)
E = B*(.70-.375) = B*(0.325) (proposed)

and the BL is

E = B*(.65)*(.5) = B*(0.325) (today)
E = B*(.65-.375) = B*(0.275) (proposed)

This puts the relative effectiveness of shaman/chanter/BL slows at 100/87/73, which is a lot closer to the ratios used in describing our healing abilities than the multiplicative version, although still a bit high.

To put it another way, if we slow 5% better than a chanter, then we reduce 5% more of the mob's damage output, regardless of the mitigation factor of that mob. Today, the difference between chanters and shaman SHRINKS as the mitigation goes up.

Again, I don't know for SURE that slow mitigation is multiplicative like I suggest above, but if it is, that could be part of the problem.
