Haleice
I two box on one PC using EQWindows (http://www.eqwindows.com) I find it much easier to use than the botched job Sony did.

I run Haleice at 1024x768, and run my second account at 800x600. I use two different UI's and overlap the screens with Hals stats on the left hand side of the screen, and my trade mule/druid/pet SK/ etc on the right hand side. This way I can see that stats of both chars, and more importantly who is getting beat on.

A few things you need to bear in mind are that you will need to swirch the sound off on at least one of the accounts, otherwise you end up with loads of lag. I also turn down the clip view and turn off spell effects on the second account.

It takes a while to get used to using two accounts but it's not that hard

I used to two box a lot more before pop came out as I was leveling up my druid for ease of getting about, but now I tend to use it to log on my friends cleric for ressing people.

As for the two fastest chars to level up? I don't think it really matters with places like PC to level up in.

Due to how fast technology works, just do a google search and find the program. Even found directions which have been stickied to other class boards. Doing a quick search myself found me 3 different versions of the dang thing. You will have to decide which to install yourself.

Read this first (http://www.microsoft.com/WindowsXP/expertzone/columns/mcfedries/03june02.asp)

Following Quote is taken from Druids Grove Post (http://pub149.ezboard.com/feqdruids24038frm9.showMessage?topicID=668.topic)
You'll need to download and install the toolkit. Then:

Open the Compatibility Administration Tool.

At the top go to Search - Query.
Search for EverQuest.
Double click the item the search finds.
Right-Click the EQGame.exe entry and choose Disable.

Not exactly related to this but could be of interest...

If you want to use other resolutions than the standard ones offered by the EQ interface (800x600, 1024x768 ....) you can actually edit the eqclient.ini file and put in other values for horizontal and vertical resolution. I am running 2 EQW sessions side by side on a 1920x1440 resolution monitor so I changed my eqclient.ini for both accounts to 960x720. Works like a charm.
