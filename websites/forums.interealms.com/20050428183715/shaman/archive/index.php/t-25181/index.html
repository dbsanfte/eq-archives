drib
Dungeons follow a different set of rules than the outside game. Almost nothing is leashed in an instanced dungeon. You can't train anyone other than your own group, so there's no need for a leash. Aggro ranges are a little bigger, especially if you're fighting elite mobs and if the mobs are higher level. My suggestion would be to stand way back if someone is pulling. I generally prefer pulling to charging in normal LDoN style and killing everything. Even a full group can hardly handle 4 elite dungeon mobs.

Like I was saying to Iz, I think staying backed off away from the fight is the worst thing to do as it extends the inital aggro range to the new position you start at in the beginning of the fight and you tend to not start running until folks are dead or near-dead. Starting out in the fight shortens the initial aggro range, gives you much clearer picture when things start to "go south," and let's you drop your first countermeasure (I drop a claw for the ~2 extra seconds lead it gets me) as you head out.

Twice, since release, I have survived a WC group wipe, as a shaman, by running back toward the instant zone-in. In both sucessful cases I was able to stop prior to the instance entrance. Once, when running from the Anachondra druid side (before we got to the boss) I jumped off the cliff leading to the river and stopped at the top of the ramp that leads from the river up to the exit level - nothing followed me there. The other time was when fighting the raptors (the upper portion). We we're wiping near the point opposite the zone-in and I made it all the way to the narrow passage before the zone-in and survived.

In closed beta, I survived about 5 times in RFK doing the same thing - they didn't follow you nearly as far in that instant, but I haven't been there since release.

Also, at level 30, Shamans get a self rez capability with a 1 hour cool down period for those times when you don't start the run-away soon enough.
