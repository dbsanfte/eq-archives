I think it has to do with stacking. I think. The numbers of the slots, 1-6, are important. If another npc tried to cast a different spell on you that had, say, a change to Attack Speed in slot 5, it wouldn't stack. That's not a common example though. Here's a better one, from Lucy:

Avatar:
3: Increase ATK by 100
5: Increase AGI by 100
6: Increase STR by 100
7: Increase DEX by 100

Voice of the Berserker:
3: Increase AC by 24
5: Increase AGI by 74 (L59) to 80 (L65)
6: Increase STR by 84 (L59) to 90 (L65)
7: Increase DEX by 74 (L59) to 80 (L65)
These two spells do not stack with each other, because they affect some of the same stats in the same slots. They both affect Agi in slot 5, Str in slot 6, and Dex in slot 7. It is, however, possible for spells to stack even if they affect the same stats, so long as the effects are in different slots. For example:

Talisman of the Daiku:
5: Increase STR by 45
6: Increase DEX by 35
This spell stacks with both Avatar and VotB because while they all affect Str and Dex, TotD does it in different slots. That is why if you click on the Stacking detail for a spell in Lucy, it has separate lists for each slot.
