Juujuu
Being an evil race nowadays really isn't that big of a deal.

Evil races can shop and bank in Shadowhaven, the Bazaar, Shar Vahl, Plane of Knowledge, Neriak, Oggok, Thurgadin, and the Overthere Outpost. Rallos Zek followers (like ogres and human warriors) should be non-kos in Kael off the start as well. With a bit of faction work, you can use Skyshrine as a town. With a bit of faction work, Paineel opens up to you as well as a new town. With some faction With a lot of faction work, but very worthwhile work it is, you can enter Cabalis to bank and shop too. With a lot of faction work, Qeynos too can be used for banking and shopping. Also remember that you can buy a port stone to return to your home town quickly to shop and bank, then gate back to the area you are hunting in, so it doesn't matter how far away from home you are bound.

When I levelled up, it was before there was Kunark. There wasn't even a Paineel, or any Planes like Fear or Hate yet. If I wanted to bank or shop, I had to go a bit out of the way to find certain vendors or a town I could safely go in to. But even then, it wasn't so bad. If you know the landscape and positioning of hostile NPCs in the area, it's quite easy to get to wherever you want to go without dying or being chased by big nasty mobs.

Being evil I always found made it much more of an adventure getting to certain places. For example, when Kunark came out, I usually got xlocs into GFey to bank because I didn't have to zone once arriving. I hunted in MM enough to have the guards love me. I would just slow the banker, and let him hit me while I banked, then gated out after. It's stuff like that that was kind of fun as an evil race, having the noobs around me asking if I was gonna kill the banker.

The only thing that was kind of annoying was the inability to do most of the quests that were originally in the game. Most of the cool quests in the game were for good races, so if you like questing, you might end up missing out on a bunch of neat stuff.

If you are more into just hunting with friends in xp groups, then you don't need to worry about the evil race aspect. You can hunt in just about every dungeon in Antonica, Faydwer, Odus without worrying about faction. You can hunt in most of Kunark without worry about faction. If you don't care for the Overthere Outpost faction, you can hunt pretty much everywhere in Kunark. You can hunt anywhere in Velious without worrying about faction except for whichever of the dwarves/giants/dragons you care to side with. You can hunt almost anywhere in Luclin without worry about faction. You can hunt almost anywhere in PoP without worry about faction.

My advice is play the evil race for a while, and see that it really isn't as bad as your are initially thinking it might be.
Xev_Grammar
It's really no big deal, especially since you can get a spiritstone to transport yourself near Oggok or Neriak.

At one point it was a real release to be able to kill anything you wanted with no guilt.

After getting the Wood Elf Crown though, I've calmed down a bit with the senseless killing, so that all that stupid guk factionwork wouldn't be for nothing.

Basically being evil hardly effects you, especially if you don't hunt in the oldworld-- if you know what you're doing in the oldworld, it may even be to your advantage to kill the goodies (i.e. dwarves at butcherblock mountain docks, highpass keep nobles/bards/guards).

If you avoid killing the goodies, you can get a crown that solves most of your oldworld faction problems, although you hardly need any of it.
