Romidar
I'm one of the admins on PoN.

Put simply: every class has people who want outrageous things. I read about 6 class boards and chuckle at all of them when I see it. It may even be more amusing when one class thinks they have a "more reasonable and helpful set of people playing the class" - if you haven't noticed, every class thinks this. ;)

I could point to threads I've seen here with shaman complaining that they can't tank anything more than "PoV trash mobs" or want double attack, etc. etc. I can point to rangers who would have archery nerfed to gain tanking ability, etc. etc.

"Whiners" are the easiest people to notice. People don't post very often to say "Thank goodness our class is in such good shape!" That's just not the nature of message boards (or real life - newspapers don't have many stories pointing out that "hey, no one in town was murdered today!").

There are exceedingly few paladins who think our healing is somehow in bad shape - at least exceedingly few post if they do think that. Most think our heals fill a niche - emergency heals with fast cast times that are made up for with lower effeciency and/or longer recast time. Our lack of specialization and generally low mana regeneration & healing focus effects (unless you are one of the minority who are uber) guarantee that we aren't going to be very good healers. (Sure, a paladin in plane of time will probably heal better than a shaman who isn't in a raiding guild - just like a shaman in plane of time will probably tank better than I do :)).

Most of the members of PoN (who post, of course) are happy with where we are now - but like every other class I've seen are VERY unhappy with the AA's and spells being introduced in GoD. Incredibly expensive abilities for very little effect. When you read a lot of class boards, you start to recognize the "sameness" - every board has some really reasonable, helpful people; some people who are very scientifically minded; some people who would be happy no matter what SOE feeds them; some people who are chicken littles and will claim the sky is falling at the slightest provocation; etc.

It's actually funny to read a bit and start to see, "hey, he's basically our bob. She's basically our mary. Huh, he's going to get banned from here just like we banned George."

We have close to 12,000 people registered for PoN. To claim that there is some common "type" of person who posts on a large message board is just stereotyping based on a few people.
