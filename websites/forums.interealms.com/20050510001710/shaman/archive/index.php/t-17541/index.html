bakkal
I will offer the analogy of the Car industry. The Car industry makes cars for us. We drive them. Some of drive them slow, some drive them fast, some take them off road and jump over ditches like they were never meant to do, some do stupid things and run them into things. Now the parallel you will bring up is to say "Yes, and the roadways are regulated, you will eventually get caught speeding, playing crash derby, etc."

The difference is that I use a car because I have a NEED for it, in a real time world. The game Everquest is merely an outlet that people use to escape from the real environment. They made an environment, the players interact with it. If EQ were turned into an expressway with policemen, stop signs, strict rules and no dynamic content, I would not play it. Right now when I play EQ the only rules I want are "Don't run over people with your car, Don't run into Huge buildings" In other words.. Don't go TOO crazy, interact and enjoy.

I think people have a tendency to blame everything else in the world for the problems, in this case the developers of the game. The truth is, as much as you want to complain, EQ is the most successful game EVER made to date. More people play it right now than ever have. New sign ups happen all the time. How can we sit and say "They are screwing up the game" Yes, it is all about money to them. Guess what, if they don't make their payroll and have money for development it won't work. That's how the economy works. You put out a good product, you market it to all walks of life, you reap the rewards and ride the wave as long as you can. When you see the peak of the wave beginning to come, you entice more players, retain your current players, cash in your chips and shut it down, or make a new product for people to enjoy. Right now Sony is doing these things. They continue to run Everquest, present new content and at the same time are making a new cutting edge game so that their player base can continue on into the next gaming era with their company.

For all of the complaints about developers, GM's, Sony, and everything else there is no denying they are a merely a company trying to succeed in an industry where 75%+ of companies put out complete junk. EQ is a pretty darn good game. It was day 1, it was today. Whether you enjoy it still or not is irrelevant. The fact is that even if you don't enjoy it, the numbers of subscribers are increasing. While you may not be one of the ones they retain, they are appealing to 2 more people in your place. Right now I enjoy EQ and continue to play it.

Ford puts out the Ford Explorer every year in an effort to make money, knowing full well that 1000 people will die inside of them each year. It's the price of success that companies pay. They aren't running a AD&D pen and paper game, they are running an online game. If you like it, play it, if you don't someone else will.. When they start dropping in total number of subscribers they will address it.

It amazes me how people get behind the computer with all the answers to the gaming world when the answer is right there in front of you. Sony is making money whether you are the one paying or not. Just because their vision isn't yours, doesn't mean they are wrong.
