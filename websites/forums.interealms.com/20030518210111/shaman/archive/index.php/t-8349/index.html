Put 2 junk buffs in your top 2 spots. Then buff yourself saving your best buffs for last.

Buy some pumice. Click off your 2 junk buffs so when he dots you, they are in your top 2 spots. Use the pumice to remove his dots. Use the pumice to strip the druids buffs. Debuff the druid and start stacking the dots. Heal yourself when you need to and melee him in between. Try to keep him rooted and slam to interrupt his spells.

Shinerbokk
Where does one acquire Punice? Is it sold, farmed, or a ground spawn?

It's sold by vendors in most cities. The same vendors that sell cloudy potions and food crystals and such. It's full name is crystallized pumice.

It is a 5 charge stone with Pillage Enchantment. (removes buffs) In the old days it used to be called egg-shaped pumice and had an instant casting time. About a year ago Verant nerfed it and gave it a casting time and re-named it crystallized pumice. It's still effective though.

A Druid V Shaman fight should be easy for the Shaman.

I play on a PvP server, even druids quite a bit higher then me make me laugh in PvP, there hard to kill for sure (as the run off) but they haven't really got the power to take a Shaman down.

What they got to attack you with?

DoT's and DD's

Your resists should be around 120 at your level self buffed IMHO, if there not... well maybe I'm just affected because I'm on a PvP server, but my resists have ALWAYS helped in PvE to.

Some of his DoT's he will not land because there resistable, he then has virtually unresistable DoT's, which can be stipped off easily with either pumice or cancel magic etc.

Dispell his DoT's, heal yourself when he nukes you.

The above advise about having your two top buff slots free is sound, if you have your SoW boots then SoW should be your first buff after that to make dispelling snare (if you want to) easy, and also to help with keeping your buffs up through dispells.

So hes DoTing you and nuking you, you are dispelling his DoT's and heling his nukes, which costs you far less mana.

While this is happening your pet is chewing on him making it difficult to cast, may have been snared or rooted but those will wear off or can be dispelled.

Dispell his buffs when you can, pretty soon he'll be oom, you'll have a pet and probably half your mana.

Get Mal to stick, may as well keep casting until it sticks, when it has stuck your quick acting DoT's will stick and he hopefully will have no mana to cure.

All the Druid can do at that point is die.

This is assuming your going for an Arena off lake rathe fight.

If the Druid is at all clever, or in fact not completely useless, your DoT's will not be of any use at first, they have both counteract poison and disease from level 29 so will not have a great deal of trouble removing your DoT's.

Let the Druid take the offensive.

Let him DoT you and then remove it.

Let him nuke you, and then heal it.

Dispell his buffs, dispell him constantly.

I would like to add that your pet will be a HUGE factor in this fight, do not let your pet get isolated from yourself and taken out, if he snares and or roots your pet, so what? You can dispell them if you have your pet set up right, or just wait for them to wear off.

Time and mana he spends casting on your pet is time and mana in which he is not casting on you, he has TWO targets to worry about.

On a PvP server in PvE the resists on pet are based on the casters resists, in PvP it is based on the level of the pet I "think".

Let me repeat, dispell his buffs, ALL of them.

If you knock his SoW off he will be in trouble trying to reapply it, you have got your epic SoW boots now haven't you?

Play the mana game with him, cause when hes oom and you have a pet, mana and a **** load better melee damage then him its the end for your tree hugging friend.

Weyalan
1. Better to have Dogdog and let him wear his resist gear - dogdog is good in PvP.

2. Do not buff Dogdog at all (just project alpha) - he will try to root / snare dogdog - if he does, just either cast cancel magic (or use pumice) to remove the root / snare

3. Aim to get him oom - remember, once a druid is oom he is useless. Pumice his DoTs where possible, heal self where necessary. Cast low mana spells (blind is really good in PvP) to preserve your own mana. Melee as much as possible - dont use bearform, wear a shield so you can bash to interrupt his casting


4. Use your disease based spells - cast insidious decay first because it cannot be resisted, then use scrouge and plague. If he tries to cure disease, you can reapply insidious decay as necessary to "protect" your other DoTs.

5. Cast annul magic on him as often as possible - it will strip all his buffs. Druids get some quite nice buffs, and taking away his SoE, Natureskin, strength, Damage shield, etc

6. Make or buy as many stinging wort potions as you can afford. If you have 2 or 3 x 10 shot stinging wort, you WILL win
