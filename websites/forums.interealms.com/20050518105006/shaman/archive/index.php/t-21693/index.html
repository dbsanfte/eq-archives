I'll admit it - I am a LDON junkie. Love em. Some themes more than other's - but I love em.

However, I had a couple of ideas and wanted to run a sanity check by the folks on this board:

1) Add a mission difficulty between 'Normal' and 'Hard.' Normal MOBs hit for 388, Hards hit for 988 and mitigate slow. Add an 'Intermediate' where MOBs hit for 688 and only certain ones mitigate.

2) Allow augments to be recycled. Don't change them from ND, but lord, we're jsut REMOVING them from an old piece of equipment - not DESTROYING them. 3) Scale points for successful missions under the 90 minutes. For example, normal mission, you complete it in 78 minutes so you get 51 for the win and 12 ( 1 point per minute) for a total of 63.

Intermediates could be 2 points per minute and hards 3.

Thoughts ?

Sariss
I was just talking to a friend of mine about LDON changes that could give a really quick / short term boost to the casual player crowd, and even a few of the raider crowds. Some of this is pretty much what you said.

1 - Adding of a medium option. Without quoting numbers for the hits... I was thinking along the lines of BoT. A random smattering of slightly slow mitigating mobs, but mostly just harder hitting, standard mobs.

The points, xp, average plat, dropped augs, and loot would be somewhere between the normal and hard. Really I'd settle for just the points/xp if SoE wanted to get lazy and not make new items... but ideally... items that are better than normals, but not the very nice hard items.

2 - Remove and Re-use Augs. Even if they made the augments SLIGHTLY more expensive as a trade off... it's so friggin stupid to have to buy an expensive augment, and if you happen to get lucky and upgrade that slot ... all wasted.

3 - All but the best focus items should be on the merchants. Along with more FT / Regen options, and even some unique effects.

The "best" should not be... I think most people agree that the raiders should get access to the best available... it's part of the reason they're out there raiding and commiting so much time.

But there should be more options to allow the gear gap to decrease... not disappear... just decrease.

So if you want a 30% range... or 30% healing focus... it's there for you... the cost can be high for the specialized higher focus items... but there should be an option.

4 - LDON raiding... it had so much potential... and flopped... for just about everyone... but mainly the non-hardcore raiders.

You can keep the objectives and style the same... but go back and re-vamp the difficulty settings.

Allow for a very wide range of guild "skills/strengths"... all with appropriate rewards.

As an example...

Kunark Level ("Easy") - Main boss approx equal to Trakanon
Velious Level 1 ("Medium 1") - Main boss approx equal to Vindi
Velious Level 2 ("Medium 2") - Main boss approx equal to AoW
Luclin ("Hard 1") - Main boss approx equal to Emp / VT Boss
PoP ("Hard 2") - Main boss approx equal to one of the harder Ele bosses
LDON Current ("Hard 3") - Current LDON raids... very hard

That might look like a lot... but it's actually very simple... and provides a clear upgrade path of AVAILABLE raids for any guild to try.

Mom and Pop guild can say "We're trying a medium LDON raid on Monday night... " and plan for it, knowing it's up... and can try a couple of different camps to learn it.

Loot would scale of course... there'd be no reason for a time guild to kill an easy LDON raid unless they were really bored. But if you're a small guild, or just learning to raid... there's something for you to do.

And most importantly... LDON raids always being available (except for the built in delays... but you're only competing against yourself).

Also the mobs and sub-challenges of the raid would scale. The Trakanon level raid might have just normal LDON mobs in it... while the hardest would be just like it is now.

I think these are 4 easy options to implement really... I think the SoE dev team could do these changes in a couple weeks or less... and it would be AWESOME for everyone, but especially the more causal, or less hardcore guilds.

I think there's more wrong with EQ than just LDON... but I think some LDON changes could give a great boost.
