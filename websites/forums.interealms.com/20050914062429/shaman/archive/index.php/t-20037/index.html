Zorash
Greetings,

I'm in the same situation that you are, Frekkels. I'm sol Ro flagged, and no one else in my guild is even close.

Sol Ro is a great zone in my opinion. The mobs are tough, but not ridiculously hard, just hard enough to make you think about what you're doing (not like flopping through a normal LDoN while yawning), at least at my gear level(might be boring for time/GoD geared people, but no uber gear here).

The mobs have resists like you'd expect in a fire zone, high fire, low cold. Also, their poison resist is a little high. Not too bad after malos, but still expect an occasional resist. They also have large aggro ranges, so watch yourself.

The best news, though, is that they are relatively low in the MR department, and are fully slowable. Since most of the pulls in this zone are pretty long, I tend to malos on inc anyway since I can still get slow to land before the mob hits camp...but it's not needed on about 70% of the mobs, from my experience.

Unfortunately, until you're pretty seriously equipped (4500-5K unbuffed) you can't really think about soloing, as the mobs in Sol Ro summon. They are, however, fully slowable, and easy enough to single pull with someone that can lull or FD.

If you get a group (more about this later), it's pretty straight forward. You group up at the zone in, get buffed, decide roles, and head in like any other xp zone. The mobs hit harder than tier 3, but not enough to change your tactics like GoD mobs. I've offtanked here when I had to, though it's a bit tense when I have to. ;)

As you zone in, be sure to stay close to the wall nearest the GY (behind you when you Zone in) until you are ready to move. There are 2 wanderers that roam through the area, and with the huge aggro range of mobs in this zone, they can aggro pretty easily if you're not tucked back close to that wall.

From the zone in, you can head either left or right, it doesn't matter which way, as they both lead to the same main hall in the middle at the top. I recommend clearing at least one, if not both of these wanderers before moving to the stairs, to avoid adds later.

Once you choose a direction, there will be 2 mobs at the archway into the tunnel (left or right), where you can see the base of the stairs. Split these, and the mobs inside, pulling them back to the zone in. Slaughter them and move to camp at the base of the stairs.

Have your puller split the mobs at the top of the stairs and drag them down to you. Singles or pairs are fine, the mobs are mezzable, and can be kited as long as you don't injure them to induce their summoning. After clearing the top landing (and inside the archway, carefully), proceed up to the top. Once there, you should be able to CAREFULLY make your way into the center room between the two staircases. Make sure to stick to the wall! This is very important, as I've said, the mobs have large aggro ranges, and you can easily drag a mob or three onto your party while getting into position.

Once in the middle of the wall, facing the two sets of stairs...you're in probably the most common position to grind. This will allow you to pull the spawns from the top of both staircases, from the main room between them where you're camping, and from each of the lower towers (up each set of stairs in front of you).

The drops for ornate are very streaky, as has been said, but they do drop, and with enough frequency to make it worth the time camping, especially since the xp here is pretty good.

As was mentioned, the fire elementals seem to drop ornate SLIGHTLY more often than the giants, but the xp isn't as good, and they have a nasty DS (it can be dispelled, or removed with MoK). Usually in a good group, we'll end up pulling most of the giants in reasonable reach, then moving to some elementals while waiting for repop. The zone repop is about 45 minutes, if I'm not mistaken.

This upper area supports 2 groups fairly easily, more than that and it gets a little sparse...

Now, on to the problems you'll face...

The biggest one? Finding a group.

It's extremely hard to find a group for the higher end zones just by putting up LFG. I know Frekkels personally, and I know you have lots of friends in the high end, so it may not be as much trouble for you, but don't expect to find a group just LFG in Sol Ro. It's a BYoG kinda zone. :-/

Other than that, be careful, have fun, enjoy exploring and getting that sweet xp. :)

Yes, the mobs see invis...so no invis scouting. They are NOT rootable, either. Even if they were, you couldn't rot since they summon. But no, can't park adds either.
