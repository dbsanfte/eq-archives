deria
Are you sure she really wants to play a shaman? I would suggest something that complements your character instead of duplicating it.

I went with a bard for my second box, myself. The bard can pull (with lull -- works indoors and outdoors) and do crowd control (with mez or charm, as you please). The bard can also increase your mana regen, your health regen, and provide songs to increase your direct damage and damage over time spells. The bard is also useful for a variety of utility skills (fast movement, levitation, enduring breath, invisibility, and more). As if this were not enough, the bard is *also* a middle-of-the-road melee. Well, middle of the road might be too generous. The bard is a slightly-better-than-dogdog melee, anyway. I've only got my bard to level 55 so far, so I'm expecting her to get alot more useful in the near future (feign death, invulnerability).

One of the nice things about bards is that you are not forced to rely on their melee; put a instrument on, and you can output acceptable damage through songs alone. At 55, its around 200 damage per tick, and gets slightly better as the bard levels. This is not alot, but when you factor in the melee damage and the fact that unlike the shaman the bard can keep outputting that damage forever, its significant.

The only downside (and its big) is that playing a bard is annoying -- without buying some basic gear (a weapon-drum, a weapon-brass instrument, and a non-weapon stringed instrument ring) you will be forever switching gear in and out. Its gotten to the point where I'd rather fight my way through a zone than put my stupid flute in my hand just so I can invis myself.

The point that I'm slowly making is that there are many classes that can compliment the shaman, whereas two shamans gives you nothing all that special. After you malo a mob, then slow it, you'll just be slowly dotting it to death. Thats fine, I guess, but choosing the right class could make your little duo that much more effective.
Kianor
I agree with Deria actually.

The fact that she wants to play a shaman is probably because she's seen you play, and that's what she knows. It's somewhat flattering, but it doesn't mean that it's the right class for her to play.

Firstly, when she catches up with you enough to group together, shamans don't stack too well in groups. Your grouping needs will be much better served with a character that you can group with with at all times.

If she picks something that you can duo with, and yet forms the foundation of a larger group, that means you can always play together. Pretty much any class can duo with a shaman, so it depends if she would rather play melee or caster, but that's a discussion for another place.

There are other reasons why it might be better for her to pick a different character, less related to game mechanics.

Naturally, I don't know what you're like as a couple, but there could be some problems with rivalry playing the same class. I speak from experience - I know it may have nothing to do with your situation, but it may be worth thinking about.

If she picks shaman, she'll be following in your footsteps. That might be good, and might be a great introduction to EQ for her. However, it might also be restrictive after a point. If you can convince her to try something different, then you can still help her start out, and can teach her a lot about the game itself but she can learn her class and have *different* knowledge to you. I think it might help if she can learn the game from a different perspective.

Personal experience stuff: When my brother started playing, my main character was a ranger, and he created a ranger because that was what he knew from me. He got quite competitive about it (more so than me, but I still responded to it) and although at first he learned from me, before long he resented any advice I gave (and as you might notice, I'm one of those people who tries to help and give advice, whether asked for or not!). There was definitely some rivalry going on there, more so with him, but then he's the younger of us. On top of that, we were also in the same guild and to some extent were competing for some of the same drops. When he joined a different guild and also later when I changed main to shaman, it made things a lot easier. I also saw similar happen with a friend who played an enchanter, when *his* brother picked an enchanter and joined the same guild.

Also, another experience, I was dating another ranger (who I met in real life before I ever met in game), for about a month - and there was definitely a certain amount of resentment when I made ranger-based suggestions there too, even if they were perfectly reasonable things... Of course in your situation, she's starting out from scratch, which wasn't the case here, T was already around 55 (my ranger was 60, shaman was 52ish) when we started seeing each other.

Like I say, it's going to be different with you but it might be worth thinking about these things.

Kriel Darkbane
I would highly advise AGAINST PL'ing up a shaman for her. When you get her to 55+, will she know how to play? No. She'll be asking you how to do everything, and not feel at all confident. She won't be having fun with you telling her what to do constantly. You also won't stack at all decently in a group. You will have to either duo together, or make all your own groups, because no one else (except friends) will ever want two shamans at once in a group.

And, on top of all that... will she even like playing a shaman? Is it the style of character she really wants?

I think it would be MUCH better to describe all the character classes to her (maybe you've already done this), and have her pick whatever sounds the most fun to her. It would probably be VERY good to have her play 3 or so different characters up to L 15-20. This is quite fast if you do most of the leveling in Paludal or the Warrens. Each time she starts over, YOU start over as well, with a complimentary class.

Once she knows which character she really wants to play, and has a basic inherent understanding of how to play it, THEN you might want to PL her up to near the level of your shaman. Or just keep playing your duo together, and play your shaman when you're not playing with her.

This'll take longer, but there's a MUCH greater chance she'll actually enjoy the game.
