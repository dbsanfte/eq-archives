JaeredMT
From what I've seen there are a few different ways to explain what you're seeing. For warriors their only real way of gaining "snap" aggro is with the Taunt ability, which puts then 1 higher than the highest person on the hate list. The Incite line helps to some extent as well, but alone the Incite line isn't enough to grab immediate aggro over most casters.

The problem with Taunt is that it fails alot, especially on mobs higher level than the warrior. Taunt fail rates on red con mobs is something on the order of over 50%, so in high end zones with lots of high level mobs Taunt isn't very reliable. On the other hand Pallies and SKs have spells that virtually guarantee them immediate aggro from mobs regardless of their level.

The aggro difference between Balance and Malos/Turgurs is noticeable only because of the time it takes for each one to be done. Since Balance is so fast we get alot of aggro very early, which the warrior either has to successfully Taunt off or we take a few hits until they can generate enough aggro to out-aggro Balance. With Malos/Turgurs taking several seconds longer to land on a mob (even Malos'ing on inc and TA Turgur's right after takes a couple seconds more than just Balancing it), that time is used for the warrior to establish more aggro via melee/Incite ticks which makes it a bit harder to pull aggro off.

With knight tanks we don't have to worry about it because they generate most of their aggro before the mob is even in melee range.
Chutoi
So much of this is timing related that it's really hard to respond to a lot of your questions.

As noted, if you malos/turgurs, the mob is usually in camp and the warrior has hit it a couple of times. Base cast time (before haste) is 10.2 seconds, or 8 seconds with a TA. Few mobs are that slow. Thus, even if you malos on inc, the warrior has hit him a couple of times and built at least some agro. Balance, on the other hand, is 1.5 seconds--and often cast with no malos.

That's one difference.

Spell casting subtlety may also explain the differences others are having. It helps a LOT on slows (huge difference)--just not DoTs.

Warrior equipment, augs, and AAs help as well. Not to mention GoD abilities like incite. Our guild MT, for example, needs about 1-2 seconds on a mob, and I'd have to chain cripple to get a mob off her. I can triple DoT and not pull agro. She generates absolutely insane amounts of agro (couple of anger augs, massive agro proc weapons, AAs, incite, etc.). I've never had a problem getting agro with her tanking.

There are also some other questions as to timing. If the shaman is pulling, and slows before the mob gets to camp, the longer it takes the mob to get to camp, the less the agro seems to be. If I slow to pull, then run back to camp in say, 10 seconds, I've had a poorly geared warrior hit once (no proc) and take agro (no taunt). I'm not sure what happens there as far as agro. Perhaps no-damage hate is reduced over time to near 0 (which makes sense in regards to some other gameplay issues).

Another suggestion, if your tanks can get a GoD jekli rune (incite) is to slow around incite. It's a slightly longer cast time disc than taunt (takes about 3-4 secs I think), but will almost always take a mob off a shaman. Try to land slow on inc before the warrior gets his incite off.

Still, I don't think that balance is much more (if any) agro that turgurs. I honestly think it's more a question of timing of when the spell is cast.
