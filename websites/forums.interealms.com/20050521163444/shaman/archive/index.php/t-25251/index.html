I've seen a few of these threads on different boards, including this one, but I just love hearing stories from different people that remind me of the game that EQ once was. I'm not going to get into the arguement of it being better now or before, but I can tell you that I had much more fun in the game back then. So many things were broken, many classes unbalanced, the world was very crowded, etc.. but nonetheless it had a certain charm to it that has been all but lost since then. I started playing EQ during the final beta phase so I've seen a lot of changes in that time. I quit and came back many times through the years but I really stopped playing for the most part after Velious because that's when I felt it started to change too much for my taste.

Anyways a quick recap of my first days in EQ. I started playing in the last phase of beta and after playing different classes I settled on starting a Ranger during release. Got him to level 20 and then rerolled a wood elf Druid and took him to 50 before PoF opened up. Back then hitting 50 quick wasn't trivial due to the crowding of the zones and lack of content to support the level 40+ crowd. Everything, and I mean everything that could give exp was camped. I only was able to do it because I worked swingshift and ended up playing all night long when the population was low.

At any rate here are some things that I remember about the game back then, both good and bad, but they really stir up the memories. I'd love to hear from other about what they remember, especially what you were fond of.

Remember when...

Harmony didn't work. I was level 50 before they ever got harmony to do anything.

Snare was better than ensnare. I think ensnare used to last less than snare even though it was higher level. No one could figure out what was better about it so no one even used it until it was fixed.

You had to sit with spellbook closed to meditate until you hit level 35. That really sucked when I was kiting hill giants at level 29. I used to run far ahead then stop and med and wait for the footsteps to get close with earphones. I had it down to a science after a bit.

Monks could wear rubicite armor for a short time.

Shamans could charm. Imagine that heh.

There used to be 80+ people in lower guk and you had to wait in line for a spot.

Doing the paw of opolla quest and finding those damn rings. Especially from kerra isle.

Permafrost was still perma-empty most of the time.

Everyone had their own theory on how to spawn Pyzjin(sp?) and get the glowing black stone. I still don't know if it was ever officially figured out or if he still spawns to this day.

Blackburrow was so crowded you could run to the deepest part and not see one spawn because everything was always camped. That and the constant "TRAIN!" spamming every 5 minutes. I think I saw my first surname on a player in this dungeon and wondered how in the hell they got 2 names.

Befallen was a dungeon to be feared. I fell down the well before I was level 20 and convinced my 2 friends to follow me down and we actually worked our way to the locked door without a map. We died before it opened but damn I had white knuckles the whole time we fought our way there.

The BBC(bone bladed claymore) was one of the uber weapons of the time. I camped mine for 12 hours with other people until i finally got mine and was so damn proud of it, even with the -agi modifier. I ran to unrest with that bad boy and unleashed fury(or so I thought).

You knew Karg Icebear had the GZ when he was putting out a lightsource. They later added a lightstone to his drop table to throw off the farmers (me :P).

When 50% of melee characters had no clue that their taunt wasn't working their entire character lives because they didn't train 1 point in it.

When newbies griped at high level characters for killing their guards. Back then they were some of the only mobs to get exp from, unfortunately it made newbies have a harder time surviving.

The mino lord was the bane of everyone in the steamfont zone. God I can't remember how many times I saw the mino lord being trained to the guards, not to mention how many times he killed me in that time. Thankfully I got my revenge.

Jboots dropped from Drelsna and there was a constant line to get them.

Everyone mid level player was in south karana killing aviaks, centaurs and of course the hermit.

The Oaken Scimitar. God I remember how much hype evolved around that item that never even existed.

The ancient crok in upper guk was camped 24/7.

Dorn B'Dynn. Nuff said. I finally got revenge at level 30 with my druid.

Ambassador Dvinn. Again, nuff said. I can't count the number of times I zoned in only to be handed my ass by him at the zone line.


I'll add more as I think of them, but I'd love to hear from you old schoolers that are still around.
