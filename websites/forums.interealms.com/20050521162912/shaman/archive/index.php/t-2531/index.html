Gardeltron
I find it curious that in all the discussions of caster balancing, no one has mentioned caster melee abilities. I find this curious for 2 reasons:

1. Melee classes have increased tremendously in both melee and magical abilities. A large number of spells are available to melees and hybrids from equipment, etc. Melee abilities have been greatly enhanced by the "free" disciplines they get post 50.

2. Caster NPCs have had their melee abilities greatly enhanced in each expansion. Many caster mobs do a majority of their damage through melee damage. Often their spells are minor annoyances (dispells, minor dots, slows).

The only increase in abilities PC casters have had have been in the form of spells. Spells which are severly limited by mana regen. PC casters still have melee ability caps that reflect the strength of mobs from the original game.

Some may say that casters shouldn't melee, but should rely totally on their spells. However, there if there is nothing wrong with increasing the abilities of melee classes to use spells, what is wrong with increasing casting classes melee abilities?

I'm not arguing that casters should be able to melee like monks, rogues, or warriors. I just think that the gulf that separates the least powerful melee classes ( e.g., bards) and the most powerful meleeing caster class (probably shamans) shouldn't be as great.

The fix would be easy. Simply raise caps on caster class melee skills (including bash), maybe add double attack to shamans.

As it stands now, there are some very awesome melee stats on some caster useable items, but the items are a joke because casters rate of hitting higher level mobs is almost nil (I've got a 40/38 1hb that I never use unless I want to whack that annoying green skeleton that will still aggro on me even tho I'm over 50 levels greater than it).

So in the caster balancing equation, don't forget to balance out melee abilities. I'd dearly like to melee better than those froglok shamans in OS who are ten levels beneath me.

Actually, drifting death(think that's the name of it -- my druid has used it many times but i mix up the names sometimes) from druids is better than our dots, because it is resisted far less frequently.

Plus, don't forget the obscene length of time it takes disease dots to work. It's pretty unusual that at least ONE disease dot in a fight doesn't lose half it's damage because the mob dies before the full thing can be dragged out.

Bane of nife is great, but drifting death is a bit better. Pox of bertoxx is nice but the length of time it takes renders it irrelevant a great amount of the time.

When you're talking about our DPS and mentioning the wonders we can do with canni and torpor, you really miss the boat when you forget that canni and torpor both take time. And canni doesn't really give you that much mana that quickly; you have to do it a number of times to get enough mana for another dot. Torpor takes a good long while to cast and to work. It makes no sense to calculate numbers from JBB use when you're busy doing canni alone, much less canni plus torpor. And just try running after the guy who is leading your mob away when you're torpored. ;)

And remember spells can fizzle or be resisted. Then you've lost a great deal of time and possibly a good amount of mana, too. Very hard to catch up from that. A JBB takes about 8 seconds to cast -- for only 263 damage IF it is not resisted, fully or partially. You can quite easily spend 16 seconds for 200 damage and change, and that's nothing to crow about. Too many "best-case scenarios" working together do little to clarify our REAL damage/second.

And remember, DD damage or melee damage gets aggro and starts to potentially steal a mob right away; by the time a shaman gets done casting his first dot, a melee or DD caster can already have landed his damage and aggro and be running away with the mob, leaving the shaman to try to duck out and pathetically run along behind trying to hold still long enough to try another DOT. We root the mob? DD caster breaks the root with a blast and we've wasted more time. Melee just keeps on pounding in further damage.

The fellow who was talking about our pets forgets that we often don't have pets up. Some shamans don't have JBB's. Some of us don't have torpor(epics are more common). Some of us don't have bane of nife(epics are more common). Some of us don't even have epics.

I have all of them, lucky me, and all my spells and some nice gear. But still it's pretty easy to KS me.

If you want to look at how fast we damage things, check out how fast monks solo at 60 compared to us, even without buffs and heals. With their DOWNTIME not even fighting at all they still kill notably faster than we do over time.

Which is not to say shamans aren't a great class and a powerful one. But DPS is not our forte. I have to fight two or three shrooms in Fungus Grove at once, non-stop, to come close to getting the AA exp/hour a monk does fighting only one shroom at a time.
