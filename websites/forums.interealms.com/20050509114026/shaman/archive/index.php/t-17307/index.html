Ruatonim
canni5 is game altering; its the one thing that is definitely as good as everyone says it is.

Rains cost a considerable amount of mana if you chain them, and that would be my guess on your mana issues. Also, just look at the mana cost on your spells now..

The other issue is our most efficient way of doing damage (DoTs) dont last full duration in most situations, and this hurts us the most in terms of mana usage.

When i do LDON i usually use the 61 psn nuke (spear of torment). With ornate gloves the mana cost is comparable to velium strike and the cast time is MUCH less. If i'm using too much mana i'll switch to velium strike to limit what i can do in a certain period of time, and that usually helps me save mana. The other thing i tend to do is alternate damage dealing rounds (i.e. i nuke one mob down and next mob i'm in recovery mode--canni'ing down). It ups my efficiency rate because i'm never full hp or full mana, and it saves my sanity from trying to nuke/dot/rain/etc down every single mob. The downside to this method is sometimes the puller doesnt realize what i'm doing and they run out and grab a new mob and current mob will be at ~20% >< so then i'm forced to nuke it down.

Its also dependant on your buffs. If you have a druid/chanter/bst/bard in your group you're probably having trouble spending your mana, but if you just have a druid or a bst or chanter or bard, then you're going to have to compromise on how much mana you can use per mob.

Heya -

I am not at all suprised that you are having mana difficulties. I think you will need to work on the following to get your mana to the point where you have a ton extra:

1) Canni5
2) Spell Casting Mastery 3
3) A Mana Preservation item
4) Level 65 (fewer resists will save you a lot of mana)
5) SCRM (to prevent having to redo buffs mid-adventure)
6) Flowing Thought Gear

For the immediate short term, I would suggest that you grab an obulus death shroud or similar (if you can't afford a mana preservation 4 item). Mana Preservation 3 will work on Turgur's and Malo, which are probably where most of your mana goes. Since you are already 64, you may want to just stick it out and get 65 before you work on aaxp.

I know a lot of Shamans have a macho attitude about our mana regen once they have Canni5 - but I am a big believer in getting the aaxp, focus effects, and flowing thought needed to really live up to the Shaman's reputation of having the best mana regen in EQ. Just try casting Malosinia, Turgur's, Bane, and Velium Strike on every single mob in a hard adventure while keeping Ferine Avatar on every melee and pet - you will rapidly discover that we have great use for every bit of mana regen we can possibly get.
