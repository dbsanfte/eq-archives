Okay, I'm going to assume by University of Phoenix you do not mean a University local to you in Phoenix with the full run of academic disciplines, but instead mean the national diploma mill that actually makes you do some the work (good thing), but in an accelerated manner.

Stay there, the UoP Diploma is accepted as a valid college degree by nearly all employers. It doesn't matter what the degree is in, what matters is that you have the degree. Sound lame, yes it is, but what the employers look at is whether or not you got the sheepskin. Then they look at what certificates you have on top of it. As you are going through UoP's program you can always be picking up internet courses or catching a arts/graphics class or three at the local JC.

Get the free schooling. When the economy is in a slump the best place to be is in school. Its starting to turn around but no guarantees that we are going into another big boom time. My guess is UoP is going to be around for awhile yet. Your job should be secure.

Good luck with whatever you choose.

Actually,

For those of you who don't know, University of Phoenix is a nationwide private university. They have a rather limited range of academic specialties. They are an adult education university - you have to be 25 to enroll, I believe.

That's where I'm getting my MBA. I graduate in seven weeks. It's been an interesting ride, but the accellerated courses are kind of like drinking from a firehose sometimes.

My advice would be:

Get an education. It's amazing the doors that will open up for you just by having a bachelor's degree, no matter what it is in. A lot of times the traditional school is too hard for working adults, which is what makes UoP so nice. You have class one night a week, and a meeting with your learning team one other time during the week. It's a lot more convenient than a lot of schools.
I know in Denver they offer a BS in Information Technology. While that might not be EXACTLY what you're looking for, it would sure be a step to getting out of tech support, and making a real carreer.

You might also look into other adult education courses in your area. Once I had enrolled at UoP, I found out there are literally a dozen different programs in the Denver area. They ususally are good about helping with financial aid, so it can't hurt to try. A few thousand dollars in student loans can be well worthwhile.

It sounds like you know what you want to do already. You wouldn't be happy studying business or IT, and you know what you are interested in. Given that, and assuming you're really committed to working in the graphic art field, I have to disagree with the idea of staying at UoP and getting a degree in a field you're not interested in.

I say pursue your dreams - if you're going to spend the time to GET a degree, then make it a degree that means something to you. It will require sacrifices and no, it's not the easiest route, but if what you really want in life is to pursue graphic art, then do it and don't try to force yourself into a mold you're not comfortable with. Really, what happens when you get a degree in business; you move into another type of office job, still not doing what you want to do, still having to find time to juggle what's now a more demanding job while you go to school, and now facing another 2-4 years of your life not doing what you really want to be doing.

If I were you, I'd find out about graphic art programs in your area. If you can go to school during your off-hours, then stay in your current job and do it. If your job conflicts with your schooling schedule, then find one that fits your needs. If money's an issue, just remember there are a lot of financial aide and scholarship programs.

Don't "settle" . . . go out and get what you really want. :)
