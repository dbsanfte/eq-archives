.......get your Tolkien Elf lore straight people:(

Legolas is the son of Thranduil who is king of the Wood-elves of Mirkwood forest, he's NOT one of Elrond's people in Rivendell, and though acting as a messenger to the council, he's certainly NOT a "messenger". Pure "wood elf". Keep reading and I'll explain where wood elves fit in.

In the beginning, the elves split into the Eldar, who decided to make the journey to Valinor, and the Avari ("unwilling") who never saw the light of the two trees, and never left middle earth.

The Eldar who journeyed into the uttermost west from Cuivienen (the place of their birth) were divided into three Kindreds.

The Vanyar or Fair elves, led by Ingwe, the "most high lord" of all the elves, is the first branch. Once the Fair elves made it to Valinor, they never left it, save once, and thus you don't hear much about them at all. If anyone would be considered "high elves" this is them.

The next branch is the Noldor, or Deep elves, led by Finwe. These are the elves most featured in Tolkien's stories. Their tragedies and oaths and wars and tales make up the bulk of the Silmarillion. Galadriel is the highest born, and probably oldest remaining Noldorin elf in Middle earth at the time of LotR. Tolkien doesn't say, but Elrond gathered into Rivendell most of the remaining Noldor in Middle Earth, some of great age. Glorfindel, who meets Aragorn and the hobbits on the road (NOT Arwen, for those of you who have only seen the movie), has been to Valinor, and its possible, though never stated, that there may be Elves as old or older in Rivendell. It is possible, though not likely, that any of them could be as old or older than Galadriel. HOWEVER, she certainly has the highest blood. She is a "princess" so to speak, of the royal house of the Noldor.

The third Kindred is the Teleri, or Sea-elves. Their two lords (there were a lot of them) were Elwe (later Elu Thingol, King of Doriath), and Olwe, who led the majority into the west. Most of the Teleri made it to the Blessed realm eventually, but quite a few didn't and stayed in middle earth. Those that stayed, of this kin, Elwe collected under his rule in the Kingdom of Doriath, and they became the Sindar, or Grey-elves. Celeborn, husband of Galadriel and lord of Lorien, was of this folk, as are Thranduil, and his son Legolas. They are elves that never made it to the blessed realm, but were willing to make most of the trip. They are called Umanyar, a division of the Moriquendi (Elves of the Darkness).

Those elves who made it to Valinor, are known as Calaquendi, or Elves of the Light. This has nothing whatsoever to do with the color of their skin, a la Drizzt, or DEs in EQ, but simply reflects that the elves that made it to Valinor saw the light of the two trees, before they were destroyed. The elves left in middle earth are elves of the darkness, because they never saw light save the stars, till the Sun and the moon arose, after the fall of the trees.

Confused yet:)?

Thranduil's folk were referred to as wood elves because they lived in Mirkwood forest, not because of race. They are related to Celeborn's people in Lorien, because both, originally, are Teleri.

Tolkien, technically, has no high elves. If any reference is made to high elves, it merely refers to an Elf that has seen the light of Aman. VERY few of these left in middle earth at the time of LotR. If you were to ask an elf :) they would say that the high elves are the Vanyar.

Eol IS referred to as a dark-elf. I think this confuses people, but shouldn't, because all it means is that he is an elf that never left middle earth, and never wanted to. He's Moriquendi.

Sigh, the Aragorn thing comes up so many times:( Hes NOT a half elf. Elrond, Arwen, and Elrond's two sons, are peredhil, or half elves. They, and they alone, have the choice to live as an elf, or as a man.

Aragorn, though, is NOT completely human. As pointed out, his grandsire about 60 times removed is Elros - Elrond's brother, also a son of Elwing (elf) and Earendil (man). Elros' choice was to live as a man, and he was the first ruler of Numenor, and the line of kings descended from him. Thus he has a BIT of elven blood, but is human - just not completely human.

Alatar and Pallando is correct. They are mentioned once (Unfinished Tales?). They went eastward, and no one knows what happened to them.

The oldest ELF left in middle earth would be Cirdan the Shipright. The oldest Maiar? I doubt anyone knows, but Iarwain ben-adar is prolly the oldest constantly inhabiting middle earth, and I doubt its possible to argue Sauron is older or younger.
