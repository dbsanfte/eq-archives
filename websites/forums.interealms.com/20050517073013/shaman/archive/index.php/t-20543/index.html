So far that is as it has always been and should be. However in previous content there have always been several ideal group configurations, each class being included in at least one of them. This means you have some flexibility - lost a cleric, get a druid and maybe switch the shadowknight for a paladin. Lost a shaman, get a beastlord.

In GoD the ideal group always includes a cleric and warrior and usually an enchanter. And if you could you would actually prefer adding more of these instead of other classes.

In PoP druid / shaman / shadowknight / rogue / magician / monk was a fine and balanced group, possible ideal for certain content. You could add a cleric instead of the druid and gains some healing and loose some mana regeneration - that might be a good deal or not, depending on the situation. You could add him for the shaman and loose slows and haste for healing - that would probably not be a good deal.

In GOD it's a nobrainer. You add the cleric - he can replace both the druid's and the shaman's healing by a large enough amount to make up for the loss of slow, freeing up a group slot for an enchanter who will at least double your DPS. And of course you switch out the SK. Pulling singles is easy, he taunts marginally better then a warrior and he tanks so much worse on nameds he is a liability.

Can you still do certain content with the inferior group? Yes. Is it balanced against a group of cleric / cleric / warrior / druid / enchanter / enchanter? No. It is a lot worse. Should it be worse? Certainly not. If anything it should perform a lot better. It does not though.

This does not necessarily mean that any of the above classes are over- or underpowered overall. That is something for a thorough analysation. It does however mean that certain classes are over- or underpowered in GoD. And the margin is way too high to be acceptable.

Until recently, I'd not felt useless. Ever since dinging 65, I've avoided BoT and PoV like the plague as they really didn't appeal to me.

Normal LDoNs are nice and relaxing, although I'm not really keen on being the slower and main healer. I can do it, but I'm constantly worrying about what would happen if we had 3 or 4 adds, as I'd die in pretty short order. Level 60 dungeons (of which I've been in a few with guildies) are fine, though -I don't mind being healer and slower in those.

Hard LDoNs are more of a challenge - I realise I can't realistically act as main healer in those, which does at least mean a more relaxed feel when I accept the group invite.

GoD is the latest area I've explored, in particular Vxed farming with some elemental flagged raiding guild members. I'm not elemental flagged, and until recently the highest hitting mob I'd seen was a 1015 hitting named in LDoN. I've been fortunate to group with an enchanter (or two :p ) in there, and the insane damage put out by a charmed pooka - well, it's mindblowing, mobs crumple within 20 seconds or so.

Then we found a named - I'd expected to Malos, slow, then nuke or patch heal, but no, the suggestion was for me to chain Tnarg's on the (warrior) MT, and not bother slowing. I was sceptical, but it actually worked even if it was draining mana wise. 1400 or so for a heal really isn't much when the mobs are hitting for 1800s, but in conjunction with Supernal Light from the cleric it kept the tank alive. Never before have I been asked *not* to slow, though - it still seems odd.

Yesterday, I scribed Balance of the Nihil, thinking it would help with the Malos, Slow, mob at 20% sort of thing that had been going on before. It doesn't bounce as much as Turgur's, but I've had several mobs where it bounced twice (and then the mob was dead, so I couldn't test it any more). I've also been summoned and died for slowing with Balance when the mob was at 60%, which makes it pretty awkward to know when to slow..... Anyway, after a few deaths it was put to me that slow wasn't even needed, how about I just patch heal instead? Again, less deaths and it did seem to work, but boy did I feel useless.

I appreciate that without an enchanter things would have been different, but sitting there chain casting Q and spamming Tnarg's when needed isn't the sort of playing style I'm used to.
