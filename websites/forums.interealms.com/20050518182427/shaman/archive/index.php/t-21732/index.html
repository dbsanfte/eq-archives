Certainly wasn't perfect. Back then I made a bunch of feedbacks and posts to the effect that bossmobs should have fast respawns, but whenever you raided one of them you got a flag that would banish you if you tried to fight it again for a week (or whatever). De facto instancing. Only a small fraction of worthwhile raid content is truly instanced today, so it isn't like we live in some instanced Utopia now. To be honest. I don't remember all that many serious problems until Luclin, when Europeans and guilds with largely school-aged North American populations got a chokehold on the content because of the spawn times and frequent patches. But different people have had different experiences, I suppose.

Today, I guess there is a "mass" of content, but there are still times there isn't much worth doing, because there is also a "mass" of people doing raids. The rigid progression structure narrows your reasonable targets to a small portion anyway. You used to be able to take a shot at, say, Yelinak whenever he was up. Now you'd need to make sure everyone has their Dain flag, their Velks flag, their Lord Dojo flag, their Tormax flag, their Vindi flag, and 37 other flags before you could even think about having a go at Yeli. Counting flags in the alt-r window is some scintillating gameplay.

In any event, I'd rather race to content that doesn't suck than have the latest "ring of annoying crap" event handed to me on a silver platter. It is like running to the library to check out the last copy of Hamlet vs having Reader's Digest delivered to your door.
