Lasalle Stormreaver
Here's what happens with the LFG process.

Dozens of people whose guilds don't have any exp groups going turn on LFG. The, something called "diffusion of responsibility" occurs-- all the LFG folks assume that they will be invited to an exp group that is already running.

The trick is that as exp groups in PoP lose people, those folks tend to find in-guild replacements or find replacements from people who are LFG in /ooc in that particular zone. The quickest method to get their replacement so they can log.

The short message here is, always take the initiative to start your own group with the other people who are LFG.

Step 1) ask guild/friends if anyone has a group, or if they know of anyone looking for someone that a ranger would fit.
Step 2) put LFG on
Step 3) start actively looking for groups. If it's not above your annoyance tolerance, have friends in the popular exp zones to look for you in /ooc or whatever.
Step 4) /who all LFG 55 60 <-- fit in level range of choice. you can also add in a class to that search. It's powerful. :p
Step 5) have people you're forming the group with ask their friends or whatever. This should happen automatically, but not always. :p

There's nothing worse than people who sit somewhere LFG doing tradeskills or whatever and whining how they can't get a group. Getting a group is a proactive deal.

Reandn
I've probably had more good pickup groups than bad ones, so I don't avoid them. But I will try to make sure it looks like a decent group before I commit to it. I like to stick with a group once I've joined it, not ditch them the first time some little thing goes wrong. There is a feeling of satisfaction that comes from getting a group of random people to become an efficient XP machine, especially after getting off to a bad start. But if things are really hopeless, I'm going to ditch them and avoid those people in future.

Fortunately CR's in POP are trivial, so at least a bad group that results in a death isn't going to result in hours of CR and a ruined evening.

I'll throw on my LFG flag, try for a guild group, and see what happens. Sometimes I get something right away, sometimes two hours later I'm still looking (in which case I have been soloing or doing something during that time, not sitting there staring at the screen...).

It depends a bit on my mood also. I'll try to put together pick-up groups sometimes, but it helps a lot in that case to already have some of the core people for it. It is rather daunting to sit there and try to find cleric, shaman, (CC), (plate tank) all at once. Much easier to fill out a group with one or two more than to build one totally from scratch. If it takes too long to put together the group, people will get bored and wander off or join a different group (especially if they are the key classes like shaman or cleric).

It helps a lot to be able to solo while having LFG on. I used to solo in POD while LFG, now I solo in POV (root/shoot in both cases). But in all cases (unless I am in a really anti-social mood or planning to camp in a half hour) I prefer an XP group to soloing.
