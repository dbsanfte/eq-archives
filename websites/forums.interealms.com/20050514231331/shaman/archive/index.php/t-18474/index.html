enarcheos
High Level soloing: A Ranger's dps is very powerful at higher levels in archery, especially with True Shot. This is their weakness soloing indoors since they have to kite to maintain archery in a small limited area. Shamans can work indoors well since they slow a mob to 25% attack speed, heal themselves efficiently, and tank while their DOTs are beating a mob down.

High level groups: Rangers are VERY good pullers in groups and can use all the archery they want as long as there is a Main Tank keeping agro. Shamans are VERY good at making the group stronger and the mobs weaker.

High level RAIDS: You WILL be bugged 100% of the time by raid members asking for buffs. Alot of the requests will be outrageous and will come from people mere seconds before everyone starts to engage a raid mob. Rangers will not get nearly as many requests. Rangers will have the raid role of dps. Shamans will have the role of spending half their mana disappointed that none of their slows landed and will eventually switch out to nukes and dots if they aren't already dead from agro.

Level 20: At this level, Shamans are almost as good at healing as Clerics, Rangers are almost as good at tanking as Warriors. Melee will remain a part of the life of a Ranger, but Shamans will branch off into being better casters at this point. Pretty much all classes at level 20 though will be "HORRID" indoors. Level 20 is NOT an accurate view of what these two classes will eventually become when invested.

Ruatonim
SHooting arrows at a rooted mob isn't very likely to break root (i've played around on my friends 65 ranger enough to know this). The downside to a rooted/moving mob is you lose the double damage of your shots.

One possibility for a ranger is to use swarmcaller or earthcaller to slow a mob and then position the target mob so there is a mob directly behind it. You then are technically shooting the mob behind it, but you are hitting the mob in front of you (and therefore get your double damage but are tanking the mob).

The downside here is rangers aren't very efficient at healing themselves so if you're tanking you are probably going to have downtime (depending on your gear and where you are soloing). Shaman, on the other hand, are very good with healing and can slow a mob more efficiently without relying on a proc.

Shaman will be more efficient with soloing, but either class can do it. Rangers can also fear kite up to the mid 50s. My opinion is soloing past 55 with a ranger isn't very practical (although its doable).
