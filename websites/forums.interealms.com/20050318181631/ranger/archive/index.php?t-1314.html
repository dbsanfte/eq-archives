This is a little documentary of a peice that i got done on my back a while back. I actually have had more work done since then and my back will someday be complete (Will be 90% covered at complete).

I'm actually a tat fanatic. The sensation varies from person to person. I describe it as an annoying feeling, where I'd just like to smack the fk out of the area being done while it's being done. I wouldn't describe it as a pain as much as a bother. I have work over very sensitive areas, spine, shoulder blade, inner arm, etc... Even on the inner side of my arm it tickled more than it was a pain.

I will forwarn you however to eat a good meal before going in. If you're blood sugar is too low you can pass out quite easily. It's nothing to be embarrassed about, I know many people that it's happened to, including myself ;). Also don't tense up - just relax and let the artist do their work. Tensing up can cause delays and if you tense up in the area where the peice is to be set you can do some irrepairible damage.

04-15-2002, 03:14 PM
I have one on each ankle; the left hurt FAR more than the right, even though the right is a "bracelet" style that goes across my Achilles Tendon. The artist that did the right side and some touchup on the left said that most people will have more sensitivity on one side of the body than the other & it's frequently related to which is the primary hand (I'm right handed).

For an idea of what it feels like (or at least what it felt like for me), go get an Epilady and try it on your lower leg. Yes, I'm talking about that torture device that was used for removing leg-hair by many masochistic women in the late 80's (myself included). ;) Seriously, that's probably about the best comparison I could come up with. It's not horrifically unbearable, but it's not exactly pleasant either (I think Aegien is just blessed with thick skin).

Have fun with it and *don't* drink alcohol or take pain killers before going to get it done (thins the blood and makes you bleed more). Oh, and if you walk in to the tattoo parlour and it doesn't "feel" right, find someplace else to go. :)

I have 2, one on each shoulderblade. The one on my left is 2 roses, 3 hearts, and it's not done yet.. couldn't find an artist who could do freehand calligraphy small enough to put my daughters name in the ribbons around the hearts. The second, on my right shoulder, I had done more recently, and it's a tribal style panther. If you have a decent background in art, some parlors will trade you tattoo work for your art, provided that it's original, and you don't want huge works of art that will take hours of work. Both of my tattoos would normally have cost in the 60 to 70 dollar range. Neither one was painful, and I noticed that after about 5 minutes, my shoulders seemed to just go numb, so I couldn't feel the needle. This was a good thing to me, heh. Anyhow, make sure you know what you want, and make sure that you pick someone who is not only liscenced, but keeps a clean work area. Its better to be safe than sorry.
