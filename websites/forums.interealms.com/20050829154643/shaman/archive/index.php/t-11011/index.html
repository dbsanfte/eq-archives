I think the idea behind limited spell slots is that your character can only hold so much information in its mind at one time. Since memorizing spells requires a lot of concentration you can only hold 8 at any time in memory. This leaves open some room for improvement however, for instance the following changes would still allow for the original intent of the limitation but could also make room for more spells:

1) Base the number of spells memorizable on your class primary stat. This would encourage people to use thier AA stat boosting abilities.

2) Create a second row of spell gems but make them cost double the time to cast, simulating the notion that you can't completely memorize them but you can partially memorize them.

3) make any spell castable from your spellbook but at significant time or mana cost increase.

If I am going to have to debuff a mob with disease slow, I usually know more than 5 seconds in advance and can mem the necessary spells in place of turgur's and malo, so it is a non-issue.

That leaves me 3 slots to play with. My guild is seriously short of shamans, and it is not uncommon for me to be the only one there. That leaves me as the sole buffer for 60 people. Thus, I usually keep focus and stamina up. I've found that any time i unmem them, someone usually asks me for them within 10 seconds so I do not bother. This leaves me with one slot. If there is a cleric or druid in my group, then I mem tears of saryn. If there isn't, then I mem kraggs.

In an exp group, what I mem depends on my role. If there is a cleric and chanter in the group, then I use my remaining 3 slots for damage spells and primal avatar if anyone wants it. If there is no chanter, I mem root and damage spells. If there is no cleric, I mem kraggs, and it doesn't matter what I do with the other 2 because I will never have time to do anything but cast kraggs and canni. If there is no cleric or chanter (my favorite kind of group) then I mem kraggs, root, and haste.

So basically, knowing which spells to mem is part of the skill of being a shaman. We can fill many roles, but not all at once. I would say 8 gems is fine, but 10 would be nice to reduce the tedium of being a buffer on raids.
