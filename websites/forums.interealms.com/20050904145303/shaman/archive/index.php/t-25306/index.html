If you have WinXP do this

Right click the "My Computer" icon go down the pop up list to "properties" and click it.
A window should appear with 7 tabs along the top of the window. You want to go to the "advance" tab. Under "Advanced" you will see it broken down to 3 sub areas. The one you want to look at is "Performance" and click the settings button. That will open a new window with 2 tabs Visual Effects and another one named Advanced. Click the advanced tab and once again the window is broken into 3 groups. Virtual memory is the bottom one and is the one you want so click "change" The window will open for Virtual Memory anf in the two white boxes in the center of the screen you can increase the numbers. I leave the maximum size alone and just increased my initial to match the maximum.

XP users

1. R-clcik my computer and click on properties.
2. go to the advanced tab.
3. Under Performance click "settings"
4. Clcik the Advanced tab on the newly opened window.
5. Clcik the "change" button
6. Increase the "initial size (MB)" limit to meet the Maximum.

After doing this it should provide a noticable improvement in your resource capabilites of the PC depending on your maximum available MB you can allocate.

I hope that helps some. If you have something other than XP i would do a google search for "page filing" and include your OS type.
