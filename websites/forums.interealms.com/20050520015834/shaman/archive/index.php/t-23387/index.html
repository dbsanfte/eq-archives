Grafsha McDaughts
The argument that clerics are overpowered in their healing abilities doesn't address the real problem(s). Frankly, to stick to topic question asked, when healing is required in such volume - who should be the class to provide it?

Now, to walk away from the topic question, and get to the real point. SOE decided the direction of the game is for the fast/high dps/low mob hitpoint encounters. Their stated rational is that "players" did not want long fights. I have no clue what 2 players they asked to get this opinion from.

So in developing these new 2-second fights, SOE opted to remove shaman from the equation. If fights were going to be fast then the challenge was determined to be mass-healing against mobs that cannot be slowed (or who mitigate.) In short, mob dps cannot be minimized. Right there is SOEs definition of new encounters. The counter to this was to provide clerics with faster casting, higher cost yet encounter significant heals that can be spammed in reverse-damage burst. Think of cleric healing for these encounters as the healing equivalent to wizard nuking - fast, effective, dominating.

To clarify: shaman were intentionally marginalized to change encounter dynamics - clerics were intentionally ramped up due to the new encounter dynamics. Had clerics not been beefed up in quick healing abilities the effect of unslowable/mitigating mobs would have made these encounters impossible to win ... without even more and more clerics required.

Shaman are looking for that one unique spot to fill. It used to be slowing. It used to be backup healing. It used to off-tanking adds. It used to be a number of things based on the needs of the moment. A good shaman saw what was needed and jumped in to make "the impossible trivial."

The quality of those unique spots in newer content are not there. That is not to say a shaman cannot contribute. It is to say shaman are not capable of contributing in the same manner or with nearly the same impact as the class was designed to do (or can do in older content.) This is the crux.

Keep your heals, clerics. SOE - change encounters to make them worthwhile to all classes, as well as to require all classes to provide their UNIQUE abilities in order to succeed. I know this falls upon deaf ears - but there really is the crux. Give shaman the ability to do their damn jobs as shaman.

It is a pretty lame argument to justify slow nerfage because "players want faster encounters" ... when it takes hour upon hour to work your way up to that encounter.
