Caanus Grey
Aye, my guild went up after ranger stones (well, we HOPED for some) and I must say that it was quite the thrill :) I was looking closely at all aspects thinking about soloing, came up with a few wonderments.


First off is the largest threat... Adds. At 65, I was able to deal with most all the first floor 2 or 3 at a time without too many problems. Slows would wear out before I could finish them all solo, but I could handle it easily. Most everything is rootable, and very little casters.


Next is named. Most every named was pulled, so i still have no clue where they all spawn at. But I am pretty sure that you could dodge around most of them if you did know where they were. But still, nothing in here was harder than anything I handled before, so I would think that adds are again your big problem.


Second Dilema... Spell slots. Need to have up Tnargs, and Q and canni, so that leave 5 slots. Assume you have up slow and root if you want to live. Down to 3 slots. I would personally want gate up at all times as well, so 2 slots left for DPS. I don't think that you can easily predict every time that you will pull a golem, so that means leaving up Grummus.... 1 slot left, and I don't have malo/sinia in there at all. So that means if I want to play things safe I have NO DPS spells. Now, I can bring up a horse in hate, so that means swapping spells midfight is quite feasible, and I could be re-scribing malosinia for Haste, DOTs and rebuffs as needed midfight, but again that leaves me vulnerable to adds.


My decision so far is to use my Tash stick as the only form of debuff if I get adds, and to keep up malosinia at all times, scribing and refreshing haste between each set of fights and using melee and pet as the only form of DPS unless I am CERTAIN I won't get any adds (not often that happens).


Another problem is runners, so that means bringing a bag or 2 of twine, or some time in FG getting 10 charge snares. I think I'll just try to have 2 in camp at all times, or to root the last one alive till it dies.



Second floor, this is where is becomes easy :)

Pathing is still screwey as Hecuba. If you get too much aggro (very easy if you try pulling things) just step through a doorway and the mobs path away and forget you long enough to camp the aggro out.

If you don't want to fight, you dont' have to. Almost nothing sees invis. But when you do fight, you can be in a world of pain, cause everything is VERY resistant.


Large problem that I recall hearing of is that if you leave up too many cleric mobs on first floor, then you encounter problems where they are healing your fights on second floor. So you pretty much have to clear all the clerics on first, or at least all of them in the area you are going to fight Inny at. Then of course you need to clear enough room on second floor to do your Inny fight in.


Inny himself isn't too hard if you get him slowed, then he is barely more than a WW dragon with an annoying AE. Would be a VERY long fight however.



I would think that having Spiritcall, and suspend minion, possibly even Paralyzing earth would be definate necessities up here, just so you can have an easy sense of confidence. Canni5/Torpor or Q is also needed if you want to finish this in any reasonable amount of time.
