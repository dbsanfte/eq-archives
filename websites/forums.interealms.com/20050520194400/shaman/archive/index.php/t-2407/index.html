Maboun
Without totally giving away my strat I'll give you a few pointers.


1) Make sure people know if they get blinded to NOT MOVE. It seems every other time we kill him someone doesn't listen and trains the centis/shadows from sides and back on us after being blinded/knocked back.

2) You can debuff him before fight since he's rooted but this will leave debuffers on rampage list, his range is pretty big and extends to slightly behind the broken wall behind him.

3) Which brings us to managing knockback/blind. Hmm what's that behind him? Use the zone geometry / landscape to your advantage. You can figure out the rest. You cna do whole fight with no adds whatsoever.

4) Debuffs are hella important. He has high ac and malo/malosini/occlusion/tash should be popped on and druid and necro ac debuffs (disjucntion and whatever the necro spell is) as well as CRIPPLE. Cripple seems to stop his rampaging somewhat and helps ower the ac enogh that tanks/melee are getting more hits. If you debuff before fight have debuffers camp out and keep all bard songs off while debuffing. When you come back you can /assist IV to see who hes aggro on. Mem blur used to work , has seemed wierd last few times though.

5) If you fail on him you can have a cleric camp out on far edge of carpet or at back of pillar and not be aggro. You DO have to let him regen to FULL before pulling any corpses or he will instagib you. This takes about 40 mins usually after a failed attempt. Obviously having someone FD in room to watch him would be helpful.

6) His blind spell is annoying, have tanks make a hotkey to mash when they are blinded then cast RC (need 2 casts usually) or GRC on em to clear blind.

7) last thing make sure people do not wander too far left or right from back wall. If you pass the small pillar/collapsed walls to left and right of big wall behind IV you will get adds and likley wipe. Side respawns are pretty fast maybe less then 10 mins?

Given that you should be able to whack him, he has been buffed once since release but once you get the strat down its just another CH / MT fight with a few twists.

Hope that helps.

Jing
You actually don't need to camp out after debuffing. Just mem blur. IV has extremely low MR, so low that Cripple is very rarelyh resisted, even when standing far outside melee range. Place warriors nearest to IV when you blur (and have them sit down), and he will immediately aggro on the warriors, placing them on rampage hopefully. We have 4 or 5 warriors all engage at start, with 1 taunting. This gets only warriors on rampage list. If you get adds, someone screwed up. You should never get adds on IV.

He's a lot like Tormax but has more hp and an annoying proc.

As for curing the blind, I think he has 2 blinds. One is normal blind, and one is a curse blind. Cure blindness cures one, and remove curse cures the other one. That would explain why sometimes you could cure, but sometimes you couldn't.

*Edit: We don't bother curing blindness anyway. Just have people stand still when they are blinded. When they move around while blind, they get adds usually.

Gorslam
To explain the negative feelings toward people who want spoiled strategies:

Just about every high-end mob revolves around a sound strategy. Ever notice that you can kill some mobs with half the numbers you had at your first successful run, just a few spawns later? It's because you've perfected (or greatly fine-tuned) your strategy. If you can kill IV, I could spend 5 minutes explaining strategies to you and you could probably kill Cursed next time he spawns.

What it boils down to is you didn't "earn" the kill when you have someone telling you which button to press and when. You didn't solve the puzzle. The person who gave you the strat put the entire thing together and then you walked by and put in the last piece, collected your loot, and put a corpseshot on your website. If you kill something with a handmedown strat off the safehouse, you don't feel the same sense of accomplishment you would have if you'd spent the time to figure it out yourself. Wouldn't you feel pride for yourself and your guildmates if after those other three guilds had failed him, you guys had wiped to him a few times learning how he works, only to end up killing him, with stuff you figured out yourself?

Alot of it also deals with the fact that when strats are spoiled, high-end guilds immediately lose a figurative share of the mobs they had to work for to figure out. That said, the flaming is mostly lighthearted joking, no matter how many swears are used. Take it as such.
