Well, from browsing around the web boards, and reading most all of the posts that come by on this one, it's become pretty apparent that a lot of the higher end shamans are unhappy, both with their spell ability and generally with their grouping desirability.

I've read through all the suggestions about healing changes and stat buff changes and pet changes..and so on and on. As I was thinking about ways to improve the class and the things that we are really known for. An idea kind of tickled the back of my mind, one that was kind of introduced a few expansions ago with LoY. Curses.

Yes, I said Curses. With our new line of ldon curse spells that were introduced, it added a little bit of freshness to our spell lineup. We are very/used to be very good at debuffing monsters to make raids and group xp'ing situations better. Well, if the devs are so stuck on limiting the usefulness of slow, why not add some different style of debuffs into the game besides just slow/cripple. What I was thinking about was a new type of debuffs called the Curses...just like the ldon curses you get when playing around with traps. These wouldn't necessarily need be limited to just the shaman class...I was thinking about doing them for shamans clerics and necromancers because all 3 of these kind of need a little boost to what they bring to a group.

Examples

Curse of Feebleness - Shaman - when cast causes your target to lose the abiltiy to either dual wield or either double attack for the length of 30 seconds. The benefits of this should be quite apparent. This spell should also be a very high aggro spell. (Stacks with other curses)

Curse of Regeneration - Shaman when cast on target causes a 25% chance that any spell the mob casts will be a super regenerations spell (something along the lines of 100 hp tick) on its targets group. Curse lasts for 1 minute. (Does not stack with clric or nec)

Curse of Mistargeting - Cleric - when cast on target causes a 25% chance that any spell the mob casts will be Word of Replenishment (2500 pt group heal) on its targets group or Complete Heal on a single targeton. Curse lasts for 1 minute. (Does not stack with shm or nec)

Curse of Insanity - Necro - when cast on target causes a 25% chance that any spell the mob casts will be Mind Wrack (300 pt group mana) on its targets group. Curse lasts for 1 minute. (Does not stack with shm or clr)

Now, why 30 seconds you say on the first shaman one? Well, cutting out the dual wield or double attacking ability on a high end mob could be quite effective, but in a lot of the older content, could completely trivialize long encounters. The short duration and high aggro would mean the shaman would have to be very careful about when and how often he casts on mobs.

These spells would all have great uses for the classes listed...and when you have more than one of the classes, you will have to decide whether you want the mob healing your group, mana feeding your group, or insane regenrations on your group. Perhaps this should be adjusted to affect raids also?

The Shaman melee debuff should be somewhat difficult to land, but defintiely nothing requiring more than a malo or tash. The other 3 curses should all be easily landable because there is only a 25% chance that it will happen. As for fighting against MELEE only mobs, instead of a 25% chance in the cast, it should be changed to a 10% chance of proc'ing.

So, am I completely insane for coming up with this idea? Would it even be possible to code? Or do you like it and have suggestions or other changes that might even make it better? Thanks for reading and I hope we can all find a way to make the class we love more useful and enjoyable to everyone that loves to play them.

Otoole Frostypants
I think that if people are actually concerned about the new adjustments Shaman need having the ability to trivialize old content, there is a way to avoid this BEYOND No Drop in high zones for certain spells.

If we want some kind of upgrade that is "limited" to being used ONLY in the content where we feel we have become useless, make it similar to how some spells are coded to only be cast in certain circumstance (appropriate for the specific situation). Some already existing spells can only be cast at night. Some spells can only be cast on undead. Some spells can only be cast outdoors or indoors. Obviously the coding to limit a spell to a certain circumstance is already in the game. Do this same thing for the upgrades we seek.

While any realistic shaman will easily concede that our class is fine in all but later tiers of PoP, high risk LDoNs, and aspects of GoD content, it's apparent there needs to be an addition/upgrade/enhancement made to the class to aid us where we fall short but not allow that addition/upgrade/enhancment to alter that already acceptable level of power than a shaman has in other content.

Now, while it's possible that the shaman who claim to not have access to some content will most likely be annoyed by their not "using" these new spells or abilities because they don't enter the content in the first place, it should not actually impede them since they are not IN the zones where the severe decrease in power is felt anyway. They would remain fine in the content they currently access. Should the access they have to such content change, they will THEN make use of the spells/abilities where they would be needed...at the appropriate time.

I think it is reasonable to consider a "bane/boon weapon coded" line of spells that are truly SITUATIONAL (read: appropriate for the situations of higher tier PoP and GoD). Bane spells would denote detrimental spells. Boon spells would denote beneficial spells.

Bane/Boon of the Elements, Bane/Boon of Time, Bane/Boon of Discord spells could be a way to give us what we DO need while not having it affect the class in places were we don't need it. These could be situation specific buffs like +avoidance, +strikethrough, +shielding ideas that have been tossed around for these specific areas. Such buff enhancements weren't really "needed" to make us group attractive in previous zones. Perhaps +pet ehnancements for the enchanter charmed. ;) Maybe they could be % spell casting enhancements (I'm thinking along the lines of cast time reduction) that would improve our own as well as other casters detrimental spells (damage and debuffs) based on the fact that fights in these specific zones are shorter than in others.

Since spells like Spirit of the Wolf can be coded to only be cast in "outdoor" zones these "zone specific banes" could be coded in a similar way. Bane/Boon of Discord spells could not be cast in any other zones than Gates of Discord zones. Even if they were limited to the instanced zones (trials)...SOMETHING would be better than nothing.

I would also think that coding such "Boon" type spells for zone specific requirements, even "expansion" specific requirement would allow for all classes to receive healing/buffing upgrades that would fit the circumstances of that particular design of the game while not having the overwhelming effect of trivializing older content VIA SPELL LINES. Obviously, increased power by equipment upgrades is always going to have an effect, but increased healing power for classes like the shaman in particular could potentially be better balanced.

If we, as a class, were TUNED to "increase" the viability of other classes in a group...just because we had a zone specific Boon Spell that shortened the cast time of detrimental spells, we would already be desired for a group...even without a healing upgrade. Conversely, our own debuff line would be greatly improved in effectiveness in short duration encounters because of the reduced cast time. In effect, that mostly and partially mitigated slow has a beneficial effect because it would land prior to the mob being 1/2 to 3/4 dead. Maybe our backup heals would have a greater effect in such a circumstance...

If such a "boon spell" would not be a good idea, then healing spells COULD be introduced that were zone (or expansion) specific. Healing for a shaman is specifically a problem in Eplanes, Time, GoD...so then give us an increased healing spell that can ONLY be cast in those areas.

I agree that our healing is fine when balanced around slow in other areas outside of the ones in question. That is why I do not see a "need" for us to have healing spells that could be cast in those other areas.

Just some thoughts.
