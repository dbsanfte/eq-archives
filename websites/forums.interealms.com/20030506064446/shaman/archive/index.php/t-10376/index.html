Meecham
The reason that you don't see advice on statue is that you are blowing an AoW spawn if you have to slow the statue to take it down (since you can't do AoW if you have to slow statue).

Umm, not strictly true. Idol stays up, so unless you kill him too (not likely if you're having trouble with statue) the cycle is not "blown".

About Vindi guards, not sure what the camp comment was about but my guild must have killed at least 40-50 vindi's (really, Im not kidding hehe) and we never had to camp after killing his guards.

He is a great mob to start learning on, as he needs a fairly solid CH chain, and also teaches you rampage control. As suggested before, having extra tanks shoot arrows when he is incoming is usually enough to get rampage on them. Remember to have them stay in range tho, or rampage will jump to someone else, and of course make sure they get enough heals. :) Usually not a good idea to have your SA or TA on rampage, cos if MA goes down SA will often already be half dead from rampages, which doesnt give you much chance of getting the heal chain switched over.

Agro-wise, Vindi's agro is very dependant on proximity; MT can tank him for a good 30 seconds but if he backs off too fast it will turn on the next closest person. This can make it tricky to get him into a corner. I would suggest trying this : MA tanks for about 10 seconds (while CH chain gets underway, if your MA dies now you might as well camp out and start again), then calls assist and everyone attack from behind the mob, at max melee range. As long as your MT has his back to the corner, and backs up slowly, you can use "melee push" to move Vindi to the corner. MA should be shrunk so he can stand right in the corner with Vindi on top of him and still be able to hit.

DPS wise, for a guild of your levels and equipment this is probably going to be a longer fight than the 3 mins Defensive disc, so be prepared to speed up the CH chain near the end of the fight.

Hope this helps abit. Good luck on the fight, and have fun. :D

A little late, but here's my take. Before I quit, we were killing both fairly regularly, so..well..yeah




Pulling is the easiest part. Even a warrior with a well timed CoH could do it.


Basically agro Vindi and run out towards the arena (the big door) and feign once he and his friends warp to you. They all walk back, but vindi 'warps' back to his spawn point, making it so you can stand up and the guards get to you before vindi does -- just have someone shoot them.


We never fought Vindi at the bottom of the ramp or anything. We did it along either of the long walls leading towards vindi, immediately after the ramp (I don't know how to explain it really, basically the long wall you first see when you come up the ramp.)

Personally, I'd say fighting him in a corner is just making things harder on yourself for two reasons:


Learning how to surround a mob and not push it every which way is a great way to get ready for the higher end mobs. That and when he's in the corner or against a wall, it's pretty hard to have your rogues backstab him, which is a lot of lost damage.


As for healing, it's hard to say, I don't know your guild. Have your clerics cycle CH every 5 - 10 seconds (It's hard to say how long your main tank will survive if I've never saw him, just use your best judgement.) (Cleric 1 heals, cleric 2 waits 5 seconds and heals, etc,)


Have your main tank attack him solo (With heals, obviously) for a few seconds (5ish) to build a little agro.


All other melee stand as far away from him as possible (Max melee range). If they get attacked, turn attack off for a bit and maybe back up. His agro works a lot like low level undead mobs - they attack whoever is closest, so your MT should have his head burried in Vindi's crotch.


Oh, and get Giant's Bane if you can -- it's costly to cast, but it works (For your Wizards, I mean)


Statue is pretty much the same except he can be slowed. We fought him at the ice-like wall that you can see through. Adding a few charmed giants helps as they do pretty decent damage.


It's a fairly long fight, so be ready for adds.
