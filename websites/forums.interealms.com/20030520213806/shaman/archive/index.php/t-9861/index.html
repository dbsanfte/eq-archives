Slowed rage with turgurs the last time we did him. We had 1 group with me a warrior, an sk, a monk, a cleric and a rogue. Most lvl 65. I buffed mr and fr. No one got feared for any substantial amount of time that i saw and i resisted most ae's with 225ish fire resist. After the fight i asked the cleric if she ever healed and she wasn't sure. I'm sure I could probably solo ragefire especially if I was fully raid buffed.

The 2 biggest issues I would see for you would be no malo / no torpor. With out malo it's going to be alot harder to land a slow on him. Even with malo at lvl 65 he resisted turgurs once or twice last time I did him. With out torpor you have to use the less efficient and much riskier KM to heal yourself.

As far as specific strats I would definitely use an appropriately equiped and buffed pet since they now use the owners resists and can actually survive the ae and deal some descent dmg. Also obviously get your resist buffs up and idealy I'd say 200+ on mr and fr if you can reasonably do it.

I'd setup a bit away from ragefire. When he spawns I'd lead with insideous decay and then try to time a disease slow so that it landed on him right as he got into range. Then I would try a malosini and turgurs on him altho i'm not sure how possible thats going to be with out malo.

Once you've got the debuff side of things done there are 2 routes you can go. You can either dump dots on him and nuke him etc and hope you can kill him before he kills you or you can go for the slow death mode. Don't do any damage but mana free dmg (jbb/epic/pet/melee/ds etc) and save all your mana for healing yourself. As long as you can survive long enough he will die eventually that way.

If you want to practice one of the easy ae dragons in ww would be fairly compairable I think.

Malo and torpor would definitely make it alot easier. Lvl 61 for the new pet, new disease slow and Spirit call if you've got some aa would make things even easier.
