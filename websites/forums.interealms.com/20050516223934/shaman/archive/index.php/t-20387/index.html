Greetings:

I would like to see some new content and abilities for shaman. The new stuff does not have to be "unbalancing", but the changes should help shaman cope with the new content of Gates.

It seems like many of the current ideas being tossed around are staying too close to the parameters of the spells we already have - just adding a few hps to a heal, a bit of mana there, etc. There are new ideas being proposed in the subsequent discussions, but they may be being lost in the "noise." Let's get some ideas out on the front page for consideration.

For example:

1. Slow AA - If SOE is set on the proposed heal change, what can be done in another area to alleviate our concern?

What about an AA slow ability with a 3 minute recast (like our virulent paralysis) that is highly unlikely to be resisted and slows a target 50%?

This would give us some way to gain instant control over the fight - albeit at a reduced level. This slow AA, combined with the new heal ratio, would allow our heals to work to a meaningful degree at the time of the initial arrival of the mob. The AA should have a significant chance of landing without a malos. Maybe it should even be unresistable.


2. HP Swap AA - This was suggested by a cleric on another thread - appologies for not recalling your name. =)

This AA ability, with a 3 minute recast time, would bring the target's hps to 50%, draining the shaman of 50% of thier current hps. (So if you hit this AA while at 80% health, you go down to 40%.) If it's too much to bring the target to 50%, bring them to 40%. Attach Cheal size aggro to the HP Swap AA to balance the ability with reasonable risk.

3. AA Line to Reduce Slow Mitigation -- Each level would reduce the mitigation factor by a given percentage.

4. Beat the Shammy Instead AA Buff -- (This one could also be a spell). Idea is that you could cast it on another player, and a percentage of the hits they get would be transferred to you.

5. Shaman Rez AA -- But like all good shaman spells, it involves the shaman bleeding all over the place. The shaman rezes at a cost of their own health and mana. One possible twist is that the shaman risks death with this rez. Perhaps even xp loss that cannot be replaced with a subsequent rez. Another possible twist is that if the shaman lacks sufficient hps to bring the patient to 50%, the shaman dies.

6. Slow Enhancements AA line -- AA's that make slows less likely to be resisted or mitigated less. Idea is to maintain our position as the optimal slowers.


7. GoD aa Tregars Insects (3 levels : cost 5 / 10 / 15 aa points) required level 65. Casting time: 3 seconds. Reuse time: 1 hour.

Level 1. Slow mob 25 %, cannot be mitigated, resist mod -150, reduce shaman hp to 100.

Level 2. Slow mob 50 %, cannot be mitigated, resist mod -150, reduce shaman hp to 100.

Level 3. Slow mob 75 %, cannot be mitigated, resist mod -150, reduce shaman hp to 100.

If the shaman casts this on a mob that is immune to slow, the mob remains immune, the refresh timer counts down again from one hour, and the shaman's health is reduced to 100 hp.

If the shaman dies as a result of using this aa, it is like a sacrifice. The shaman can be rezzed, but the experience loss from dying is not recovered.

Discussion. This aa is quite likely to overcome the resistance of highly-resistant mobs, but it still is not a sure thing. It will create high aggro from the slow and from the shaman's very low health, so probably the shaman is toast: but possibly the tank can hold aggro at just the right moment, possibly the healer can time landing a heal to keep the shaman alive. Most often, using this aa will make the shaman a sacrifice, like deciding who is going to take the death touch.

Using this aa will have no effect on the mob's single target or aoe spell damage. It will have no effect on the mob's resistance to other forms of magical attack. It will affect only the rate of the mob's melee damage.

It will not make it possible for a shaman to solo otherwise unsoloable content: one hit from the fully-slowed mob will still kill the solo shaman reduced to 100 hp, long before he can possibly heal himself.

The one hour reuse time means that it cannot be used to trivialize raid content with multiple severe mobs. Nor will it make much change in ordinary experience groupings.

Its chief benefit will be in providing a strategy option in a raid setting for a guild that is trying to take on a melee-heavy boss mob that they cannot quite overcome. Their current option is, get stronger healing and tanking. This aa would give that guild a second option: one of their shamans can channel almost all of his/her health into a slow that is very likely (still not guaranteed) to land, usually at the cost of his / her life, with no exp recovery from resurrection.

8. Canni 4 as an AA - The idea being to give shaman back a spell gem

9. OR FA as an AA - Again, give the shaman back a spell gem.

10. Dodge Bear AA -- Some short duration AA that allows the shaman to dodge all damage. Something akin to Bard's Nimble, or Riposte, or Weaponshield, or DA. 24 seconds to not die basically.

If you don't like these ideas, suggest others and I'll add them to a list. This list is not meant to be a final list of demands, it is just a list of possibilities to get us thinking - so almost anything suggested will go on the list. Even if you do like 'em, suggest more! Feel free to re-suggest ideas found on other posts. Try to be better then me though, and actually atribute the idea to the writer by name.

I'd like to keep the tone of this discussion positive please. The point is to generate a list of ideas -- a mass brainstorming opportunity. Please Do feel free to discuss the merits or problems with proposed solutions, but keep the tone of your discussion constructive.

Thanks!
