Zyphyr
Given that every DoTing class can stack every single DoT (assuming no effect other than the DoT) they have now (up to a limit of a total of 30 effects on the mob), the only real limitations in a solo situation are a)Mana, b)How long the mob survives (if it dies before you even get to cast DoT #4 on it, you obvously hit a limit in how much you will be able to land), c)how long the DoTs lasts, and d)How willing are you to swap spells in/out.

Given that, a Necro is going to be able to stack more damage onto a mob with dots simply by virtue of having more lines of dots. That means they have more dots in a given level/damage range.

Shaman :
Blood of Saryrn : 320/tick, 7 ticks
Breath of Ultor : 165/tick, 26 ticks
Pox of Bert : 90/tick, 18 ticks
Bane of Nife : 214/tick 7 ticks
Total / tick : 789
Factoring in casting times, canny work, and either keeping the mob rooted away from yourself or healing yourself if tanking that is about all you might reasonably expect to do.

Necro :
Blood of Thule : 335/tick, 7 ticks
Embracing Darkness : 70/tick, snare, 20 ticks
Saryrn's Kiss : 200/tick, lifetap, 10 ticks
Dark Plague : 132/tick, 20 ticks
Pyrocruor : 111/tick, 18 ticks.
Total / tick : 848/tick

Necro doesn't need to spend time healing, since one of the DoTs is a lifetap, and doesn't have to canny, so it is reasonable to assume they will have time to stick at least one more DoT.

Both of those presume that the caster is willing to take some time to swap spells in/out.

The number are pretty close, but the necro has a slight lead.

Thyramon
Well, I can toss an opinion in here for ya as my two highest level toons are a shaman(56) and a necro (53).

I've been alternating these two for quite a while now and can't really settle on one or the other as my absolute favorite. Both can solo almost anything they can stick a spell on without excessive difficulty. Tactics are the big difference here, not dps, and as such, if you want to root dot, the necro is the toon of choice.

Necros can snare, root, and pile on the dots. Most of their dots DO NOT have a DD component so root breaks less. If root breaks, they can feign death to avoid dying. With Lich, ArchLich and the other mana spells they can regen mana at a steadier rate than a shaman (Archlich is better mana regen than even KEI), even while feigned. They have life tap dots which offset the damage they do to themselves liching and can lifetap DD when needed for extra hp or a final kick to finish a tough mob. A (and here's the catch) WELL-PLAYED Necro can out root-dot any other class, but they get into melee range of the mob and they die fast. They have low hp and horrid damage mitigation. Also if you get stunned as a necro or fail a feign death, start looking for a rez cause it's all over (feign has a bad recast time). Over all, Necros are tougher to play cause you have a much smaller margin of error to work in. Against PoP or equivalent mobs, you're walking a razors edge to live through the fight, and you'll find it easy to take a bad step and die.

Shamans on the other hand have fewer lines of dots (only 2 plus epic) and most shaman dots have DD component causing them to break root. On the upside, slow + higher ac + higher hp + better damage mitigation means that a shaman can take it when root breaks without being dropped like a dirty shirt and can heal themselves outside of combat. They do take longer to root-dot a mob to death since they have fewer spell lines and therefore less dps, and so are less efficient as root dotters overall, but definitely stand a better chance of surviving if things go south (provided of course they can run for it, if that's not an option you stand a good chance of dying).

At any rate, that's my analysis of the situation. Personally I love my shaman and my necro, but I don't, strictly speaking, root-dot with either of them very often.
