Irksome
Best MR debuffing is:
Malo/Mala + Tash (both unresistable, and stack, order not important)
OOS (unresistable bard song)
Bard, weilding tash stick/orb and procing (bard procs will stack)
Bard, weilding the other tash stick/orb and procing (order matters here, must be done after first one, and I don't recall which is which)
Malosini (overwrites Malo and Mala, is better, but often need Malo or Mala to be able to land Malosini)

At this point, MR is reduced to super low levels, and slow will likely land fairly easily.

If you don't have a 60 shaman or mage, you need your bard to do OOS, chanter Tash, and try to have your bard weild a tash stick and land a proc. Then chain Malosini til it lands (remember that mages have Malosini also), then try to slow. If you subscribe to one of the superstition theories (charisma or wisdom affects success), get buffed.

I was in ToV West before level 60, and even with Malo in (from someone else, of course), I couldn't land slow more than 20% of the time. Level matters a ton for resists, and the mobs in HoT are going to be significantly higher than you, on average.

For the shaman, level 60 is the best damned upgrade in the game. Even without Malo, your resist rate will drop a ton just by getting the level.

Ziorina1
The tash proc of a Journeyman's Walking Stick will only stack with enchanter cast tash if it is a bard that procs it. It has to do with the screwed up coding for bards -- their songs are coded to stack with those from any class, and procs are treated the same way.

Along the same lines, maximizing damage shields involves bards stacking some extra DSes on top of mage/druid and shaman (potion) ones (in addition to the song-based damage shields).
