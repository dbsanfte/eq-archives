The beastlord class has some strengths and some weaknesses. It is meaningless to say that one class is overall better than another, or more desirable, since everything is so situational, but people should realize that on message boards everyone tends to focus on the good of their class and ignore the bad.

So, Shaman strengths/weaknesses:

1) slow = excellent
2) healing = above average (quiessence, tnargs, kraggs, various healing AA abilities)
3) crowd control = above average (various roots, including a disease based root with knockback)
4) burst damage = average (poison and cold based nukes only; not very efficient, but offset by our mana regen)
5) damage over times = excellent (poison, disease, and curse based damage over time spells)
6) debufffing = excellent (unresistable debuffs for disease and for everything but disease)
7) buffing = excellent (shaman buffs use very few buff slots, and generally do more for weaker characters than a beastlord can)

So, Beastlord strengths/weaknesses:

1) slow = excellent (at 65, with rune dropped spell) and average until then
2) healing = average (chloroblast)
3) crowd control = bad (pet can off-tank one mob, if it doesn't decide to just attack whatever is hitting the beastlord anyway)
4) burst damage = above average (fast casting nukes, not very efficient, but if you have a mana bar you might as well use it)
5) damage over times = average (beastlords get some of the poison based damage over time spells from shaman, and some of their own)
6) debufffing = bad (no debuffs AT ALL except through randomly proccing weapons like the tash stick)
7) sustained melee dps = excellent (the beastlord and the pet together both contribute excellent dps)
8) bufing = above average (beastlords can contribute enough in the way of buffs to satisfy pretty much anyone, despite having to use alot of buff slots to do it)

Now, looking at all that, can you say one is "better" than the other? Only if you know the situation that you will find yourself in.

For the moment, though, you can rest easy in the fact that beastlords have a HUGE public relations problem. People a) have little idea what they can do and b) get really annoyed when beastlords are constantly talking about how good they are :)

1. Malo the mob (If needed)
2. Quiescence the MT
3. Slow the mob

Drop the Quiescence on the MT that you would be casting on yourself due to the slow aggro. It is more efficient on the MT anyway since it will likely all get used and the MT will mitigate damage better than you will.

Drop a malo on the mob to enable others to cast easier (if needed).

Then slow.

By the time you do this your slow will land without pulling aggro and it is really not that different from the:

1. Slow the mob
2. Quiescence the Shaman
3. Malo the mob (If needed)

Except of course only the MT gets hit which is more efficient for the group.

If you are fighting a mob that absolutely has to be slowed or it will waste the MT then it will also absolutely waste the shaman even with slow up without assistance from the cleric. Which places you right back at the situation where it is better to wait and not draw aggro if at all possible.

There are times and places where you have to slow early but they are much rarer than the "every pull" scenario that many shamans follow.

As for the question of what you should be doing for those first 10 seconds ... how about you do what everyone else is doing? Waiting until the MT secures aggro before casting ... or of course you could Drop a HoT on the MT and Malo the mob or Canni IV.

Most people have their own playstyle, which variest from situation to situation, but in LDoN I generally:

1. Stand beside the MT and slow on inc.
2. Don't Q anyone.
3. Don't malos anything.

If the MT is a PAL/SK I may not get hit at all. If the MT is a warrior, I will get hit a few times but almost never will I get hit enough to make me actually feel the need to cast a heal on myself.

After a few repeats of this, my health may be at 80 percent or 70 percent -- and thats fine. I'll cast a Q then, or Canni5 and then cast a Q.

The mobs just dont hit hard enough to make me feel the need to go out of my way to avoid the 2 or 3 seconds of agro that I might get.

If I am grouped with a cleric that does not use his own HoTs, I'll cast Q on the bank every fight (after the mob is slowed) to help the cleric keep up on mana (or rather free them up for doing DPS, lol) but if they use their own HoTs then I'll just focus on slowing/DPS.

All that being said, lately I've been using Tigir's Insects alot (the AE slow) and liking my results with that. It casts slowly, but hitting 4 targets at once is pretty nice. It has a built in hate reduction, as well, so I usually don't get agro (or if I do its not any worse than I'd normally get).

I guess what I'm saying is that you need to roll with the punches. There will never be a One True Way to play your shaman; you'll do what needs to be done in any given situation.
