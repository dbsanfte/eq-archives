shamuofxev
Ikky BP are usually to be had at a rate of one to two per evening play. You definitely need a tracker, bard or ranger prefereably. The pulls can be kind of long, and druids die too often on the way due to adds.
After each forager dies you have about a one in five chance that a named mob will pop, and about a one in 20 chance that it will be the crusader. Get a list of named mobs, e. g. from Allak, and have your tracker check frequently.
Other decent loot on the forager cycle include the hangman's noose and the shm-only necklace from the large sabertooth tiger.
Full group of high 40's or three 55's can do the cycle without downtime.

Jenoe
The normal ph's are a froglok forager and a froglok hunter (you want to kill the forager btw) and they walk one of three paths

1. Along the northern zonewall from the ej ent towards the sonh ent

2. West of the lake and along a north/south line

3. South of the lake in a west/east line all the way towards seb, follow the wall if nothing else.

Once you kill a ph or a namned then another (ph/named) will pop directly along one of these routes, so the faster you kill the greater your chance of a decent drop.

I prefer to pull towards the northern edge of the lake just below the little house with birds where the little stream lets out into the lake (nothing wanders by).

Conas
I did it at 52 with a 55 ranger. Foragers arent hard but we on occasion would get two named in a row and if that happens you will get down time unless you have decent dps.

The key to farming bps is to kill the foragers fast. They are on an (essentialy) instant repop so the faster you kill the better chance you have. We got two bps in two nights. The iskar mask and iky bp are the best items in the cycle. The haste belt is decent for sub-dragon haste but is no-drop.
