Kivorn Teleri
Hello there. I'm a frequent lurker of these boards thanks to my shaman alt.
As for this post, it's about rehashing the common pooled of shaman knowledge regarding stats. It's my hopeful intention that this thread can serve as database to direct people mistaken of the roles of the different stats.
Why? Well. Simple. I'm a numbercruncher. I like optimizing my character. I like knowing what stuff do, what they don't, and by how much or how little. I also really hate urban legends.

So I ask the inhabitants of the crucible to correct me in my misconceptions and if possible, turn this into a healthy debate regarding stats.

Here's what I believe. Feel free to prove me wrong:

ATK - Broken down into two numbers. Chance to hit and damage affection. Call of the predator would increase your chance to hit, but not your max damage or increase your modal damage.

AC
- Broken down into two numbers. Damage avoidance and damage mitigation.
- Avoidance allows the blows to miss (a drolvarg rager tries to hit YOU but misses!).
- Mitigation part lowers the modal damage done by said mob.

Strength
- Increases your modal damage.
- Increases your maximum hit limit.
- Can be used as an alternative stat for skillraises within smithing.

Stamina
- Increases your max HP
- Increases your lung capacity
- Stamina beyond 100 allows you to expect effects of fatigue except for the inability to jump.
*Does NOT increase your damage mitigation*

Agility
- Increases your damage avoidance (see AC)
- Used for skillgains on defensive combat skills
*Does NOT* increase dodge, parry, disarm, dual wield, double attack.

Dexterity
- Increases your chance to proc with the mainhand weapon (not offhand)
- Increases your archery to hit percentage
- Increases the chance to score a critical hit
- Used for skillgains on offensive combat skills
- Can be used as an alternative stat for skillraises within fletching.
*Does NOT* increase dodge, parry, disarm, dual wield, double attack.

Wisdom
- Determines mana for priest casters
- Determines skillraises on tradeskills (if INT is not higher or an alternative stat is used)
*Does NOT* affect skillgains for combat skills.

Intelligence
- Determines mana for priest casters
- Determines skillraises on tradeskills (if WIS is not higher or an alternative stat is used)
*Does NOT* affect skillgains for combat skills.

Charisma
- Determines the prices you recieve from merchants

Have at me,

I believe that attack is not a composite of 2 numbers. I think it's just one. Yes, an increase in attack skill does mean you hit more. I know there is a lot of conjecture about it, but I believe it is the same for mobs and players in this case. If you recall from AC softcap threads, the soft cap on AC is dependent on the attack level of a mob. The higher the attack skill, the more AC you require. Higher attack means that the AC of the mob (or player) does less damage mitigation--hence more damage. You can play around with avatar and get those numbers. I have a monk friend that doesn't like avatar at all on raids. She's at 1431 attack, but with avatar (and 1531) she can't attack because she gets too much agro). However you cut the cookie, avatar will increase the average damage noticeably--even on tanks with maxxed strength. I believe it is more correct to say that attack numbers determine the AC mitigation of damage of the target--and AC determines misses and amount of damage both.

AC--see prior paragraph

Strength--I may be mistaken, but I believe that strength does not affect the speed at which you learn, but gives you a higher chance at making a successful combine. Think of it as a +smithing mod.

BTW there are 2 parts to attack, just as there are 2 parts to AC.

ATK is made up of a component that determines chance to hit, and a component that determines damage distribution. Pure atk buffs modify the damage distribution part.

AC is made up of a component that determines chance to hit, and a component that determines damage distribution. Pure AC buffs modify the damage distribution part.

I've heard those different components called hit/hurt and miss/soak for other systems.

Those components can themselves be made up of other components. Avoidance AC is made up of defense skill and AGI, for example.
