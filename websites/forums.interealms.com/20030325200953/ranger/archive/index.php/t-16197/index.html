Walruses is what i just did, the loot is the worst I've ever seen in the game, lots of pelts that vendors won't even buy & are otherwise useless, and some meat. The rare loot's a walrus tooth that's not worth much & is lore, with some pages and words & other stuff if you're real lucky.

Wolves are better loot I think, & mammoths too. Also there's kodiaks & wolves in EW and GD, so there's some variety with them.

At 38, any of the blue ones you can kill, any of the white ones with some work, and I did yellow cons some, but they wern't worth the time it took. Because of the time stuff, I stuck to the blue cons mostly.

I'm probably going to go back and do some more of them mostly becasue at this point I've optimized the harvest.

2 things to watch out for that're life & death:

1. Avoid letting your mobs path around left side of the Kael arch, allthough they'll travel in a nice straight line along the edge by the entrance to Kael, there is a trap there. Basicly you'll be fighting & if you're lucky a Dwarf will appear from `under the snow', & eventualy will despawn if you leave him be. If you're not lucky, and chances are more that you won't be, you will be "ambushed" by a Krommif Death Squad, which is like 4-6 Elites, right on top of you. With SOW & immidiate & non-stop flight, you -might- live, but odds are not good. I died the first time from it, but the second 2, I managed to outrun them (onetime to the iceclad bridge, & I needed to re-sow on the way, quite dicey...)

2. The entrance to Kael has a huge courtyard, with big brown stone walls around it on the outside, along with a huge, deep `Trench of Doom' leading into it, that's not well marked. It is -very- easy to walk into it. So pay a lot of attention on the approach. You will most likely die if you don't. I got lucky the one time I fell into it, and only took 600+ in damage, that on approach after I'd spent a lot of time there.
