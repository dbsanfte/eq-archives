eximius
Maintaining a quick healing ratio designed on prior game design parameters will not work. Efficient Complete Heal and group healing is the cleric's domain. For secondary back up healing , i.e., the quick heal line, there is little or no reason for the shaman emergency healing to heal less or to be slower to cast or refresh than the cleric's emergency healing.

Shaman's healing overall is so ineffective that the only spell that can be used is Tnarg's mending. In GoD encounters, a shaman can be back up healer at best. Thus, the shaman's quick heal is the often if not usually being used on the cleric, or to support a tank until the CH lands.

While it would be unbalancing for the quick heal to be so efficient that a shaman can chain cast it to take the place of a cleric's better CH efficiency, this balance issue should be dealt with solely through mana efficiency. Thus, give cleric's, druid's, and shaman's quick heal the same cast and refresh timing and amount healed, but make the mana cost for druids and then shaman be that much higher (the ratios above might work). Everyone is happy--shaman can spot heal to save the cleric, druis or tank, but cannot chain cast the quick heal because of mana efficiency.

We need enough quick healing power to manage one round of unslowed attack on a cleric. 1550 is not going to do it for GOD. If we cannot keep a cleric alive for one round, we are useless as secondary healers in these Gates of Discord encounters.
