Foramorm
Before you do anything..SPAM CANCEL MAGIC! You have no idea how much this can mess someone up. If you are fighting a pet class, cancel magic their pet first, it will usually be the bigger threat. Remember to utilize Spirit Call and ole' speed bump.

1. If the person is a higher level than you, they are going to resist your spells more often. Once you hit 65 resists will happen less.

2. I usually hit them with XXX decay and spam Grummus until it sticks. Once this has happened i'll MaloXXX them and try to root them
(while all this is happening, i've sicked dogdog on them, sometimes they will get off your and focus on the pet. If you have spirit call, use it now.)

After they are slowed, your job becomes much easier. If you are fighting a melee (especially rogues) put your back against the wall. This way, they will have a harder time interrupting you and backstabbing.

You should usually never have problems when fighting a melee. The only people that have given me much trouble are bards (of course) and necromacers. Generally, just make sure to keep them (or their pet) slowed, and keep Qui/Torpor on yourself. Remember to use spirit call! It's more usefull than most think. It can throw them off guard, and if they are a caster, they'll have a heck of a time getting a spell off with 3+ dogs beating on them.

Anyways, that's what I usually do. It's worked pretty well for me, but i'm by no means a hardcore PvP'er. So, take it with a grain of salt.

howler
if you're casting root or blind in a duel, then either

1) you're a lowbie, which usually means
2) you're fighting somebody with crap resists or
3) you don't know how to duel/pvp

but it's usually in that order...

with magic being the prime resist in pvp, casting any non lure detrimental magic based spell (except for cancel magic and snare) is a waste of time, mana, and effort.

to answer your question though, it kinda depends. you're seeing a variety of opinions in this one post mainly because of variances in gear/aa, skill, and personal experience. pre 50, we are the strongest pvp class in the game, even with the influx of new gear. after that point however, our relative power begins to significantly decline. since our nukes and dots spells have no negative resist component to them, and malos is not significant enough when dueling vex thal + geared folks, you will find it extremely difficult to actually kill your opponent. if they're not geared in such a way, and you have decent gear and aa, it will still be a war of attrition so be prepared for the long haul.

your tactics will vary depending upon your opponent's experience and skill, class, and gear/aa. but if you expect to last in the arena, you first need to get your magic resist above a base of 150. 150 is the magic number. it used to be 100, but after they changed resists around on blue servers, the base seemed to change. after buffing, you should have close to 200 svm.
