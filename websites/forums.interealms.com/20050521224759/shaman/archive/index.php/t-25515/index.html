Attuned Discord Earring (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68636) is a very nice earring which drops off trash in Yxxta. Over the last few weeks whenever i've been lfg or had little time to group and concentrate on EQ, I've been gonig to Yxxta to solo in an attempt to get it

If you zone into Yxxta using the west enterance from KT you'll be faced with 2 mobs at the zone in, either 'a wary hynid' or a 'A Ravenous Cragbeast'. Both of the mobs are rootable, slowable and don't summon. They both are single pullable. Usually after using mali decay or putrid decay VP holds for full duration on the hynids, but I always keep normal root up for the occasional early break. For the crags you need to use normal root and refresh every 30% or so. Basic strat is to pull with a debuff (malos for crag, DR debuff for the hynids) and root them using VP or a root spell depending on the mob type. From there on its just a usual root rot technique. If things go wrong the zone out out is less than 10 seconds away.

Also, every few minutes 'a noc swiftblade' will roam up between where the two mobs are that can drop the earring. The noc is the same as them, slowable, rootable, doesn't summon (root works better than VP). It has a chance of dropping this (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=68635) ring, personally its not a big upgrade and I'm hoping to get a different ring from elsewhere so I never bothered with him so I have no idea on the drop rate.

For me it took about 2, 2 hour sessions and 1, 3 hour session to eventually get the earring while afk inbetween spawns (20 mins) doing stuff irl. The exp is fairly nice, 7-9% AA each kill at level 70 but as you only kill 2 mobs/20 mins its not a good place if you want to solo out some fast AAs. Hope this helps people towards getting what I think is a great earring.

well, writing this for a second time..lol...but ok.

the actual strategy I use for killing the mobs in Yxtta are as follows.

head to the Uqua side of Yxtta, there are 2 spawns you'll be killing regularly, they are "A Wary Hynid" and "A Ravenous Cragbeast" (most commonly Hynids, but they are the only types of mobs that spawn in these two locations), behind them, are two more mobs that are randomly Nocs, Matruq, and Ukun. there is also a rotation of mobs that wander up the middle and stands in between the two mobs. they WILL add if you pull while they are standing there, and will add if you root the mobs a little too close to their path. Cragbeats, Hynids and Nocs are all soloable by a shaman, Mastruqs and Ukun are not, don't even think about the Kyvs..

I like to have all the spells I'll be using memmed already, I don't like to switch spellsets at this camp when I have engaged a mob, as it isn't hard for root to break, and you don't want to be sitting if and when it does.

load up your debuffs, malos, MD (PD if you have it), DoTs, canni and root. load slow if you want a little extra security.

VP3 is HIGHLY recommended for this camp. it is possible to do it with only spell root, but expect root to break frequently, and to do a few more CRs.

any extended range item really makes this work. stand at MAX casting range and pull with debuff, PD if you have VP, and Malos if you intend to use spell root. start spamming root/VP right before the debuff goes off, I have attempted before to pull farther from spawn location before and ended up dying more often than not, as it uses up valuable casting time to pull closer to Zone In (Hynids and Nocs run fast, but not quite at SoW speed, and Crags appear much slower)

if the mob does not resist, he will be rooted out of melee range. if he does, you should have just enough time to run to zone without taking more than one hit.

immediately apply the other other debuff you memmed and back to max casting range. if using magic root, reapply it and do so frequently. start loading DoTs onto the mob. the mobs themselves do not have much HP, but do hit very hard, they can one-round lower end shaman, and 2-3 round higher end ones.

if you are using VP, occasionly press the hotkey to get a readout of how much time you have left. VP reuse timer will usuallly pop before VP wears, so be prepared to reuse VP when the timer gets close.

the mobs will start running at 18ish percent health. so it is safer to approach closer to mobs once they reach this life range, but don't be stupid, stay well outside of Melee range. keep it rooted, because they don't have to walk far to get help.

hopefully you'll have more luck with this camp than I have, I'm still at it after a good number of hours. just remember, Crags, Hynids, and Nocs only. the EXP in this camp is VERY good on a per kill basis, but only average over time because you have to wait for wanderers and spawns.

I'll provide info on primal caves if I ever get brave enough to venture that deeply in without a group.

See invis mobs otw to Yxtta:

1) wandering Ukuns. There are two, one in the first canyon heading south from zone in, and one pathing the East-West between the Yxtta entrances. For the first, the east wall has plenty of room to pass by. For the second, the south wall.

2) undead. specifically, the trusik ghosts in the area called Martyr's Passage. That's the canyon with little buildings. High on the west wall is safe from these fellows.

3) basically everything in the small temple at north end of Martyr's Passage. You can cross the middle of the passage safely to get to the west wall.

Always the same mobs, no variation. Pay just a lil' attention to that last wandering ukun, you should be perfectly safe crossing Kod'Taz whenever you like.

Here's what I do when I'm using VP to root-rot: Bring up the AA window (default is the "V" key), select the tab for the page that VP is on, then select the Virulent Paralysis line. You will either see "READY" in the lower-righthand corner of the window or a countdown timer until VP is ready again. Then hit "V" again to close the window. The window stays like you left it so you can hit "V" any time to see the counter, or just move the window to the top of the screen so only the timer is showing. The "V" key always seems to work, even while you're casting.
