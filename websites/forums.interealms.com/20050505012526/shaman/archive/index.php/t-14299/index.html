Jinheim
Stamina does not effect how many new hitpoints you get per level - that is constant. All shamans of the same level will have the same base hitpoints, modified by stamina. For example, at level 65 with ND3 I have 1435 hp naked (barb with base 105 stamina). 1000 of that might be base HP, and the other 435 I get from my 105 stamina. An ogre shaman would have a little more hp than me, because he has higher naked stamina. He might get 1000 from his base hp, and then 550 from his 140ish stamina. However, if we both had 305 stamina (the cap) and the same raw +hp gear, we'd have the same hitpoints since all races get the same benefit per sta.

As for how much HP each point of stamina yields, it varies by level. At level 60, shamans get exactly 3hp per 1 point of stamina. All int and wis casters also get 3. All melees get 5, except for warriors, who get 6.

The formulas are as follow:

Priests and Casters: Level/20 (3.25 at 65)
Melees: Level/12 (5.416 at 65)
Warriors: Level/10 (6.5 at 65)

I have natural durability 3, which adds 10% to the yield of any sta bonus I get. This leaves me with a 3.575hp per sta yield.

Buffing myself with primal essence (20 sta) takes me from 201sta to 221sta, and takes me from 3223hp to 3295, a 72 hp bonus, or 3.6 per sta. I then took off some of my gear and left my stamina at 175. Giving myself 20sta gave me 71hp, or 3.55 per sta.

Past 255, stamina returns start to dwindle. Buffing myself from 261 to 281 stamina gives me only 36hp, or 1.8 per stamina. Basically, half of what it should be giving me.

Hope that helped.

edit: I would recommend against putting AA points in to stamina. It has very limited returns after 255, and it is not difficult to hit that number with self-buffs. Once you have gear that caps your stamina, those points you put in to stamina AA are wasted. Spend them on run3 and regen3, which are never obsolete.

The above link to The Steel Warrior (http://steelwarrior.xwarzone.com/index.php?s=) message board contains the formula that Magelo uses to compute hp's for warriors. The only change for shaman(I would assume from appearences) would be in the levelmultiplier variable. The formula is as follows:

HP = TRUNC[ 5 x ND ] + TRUNC [ Lvl x LM x ND ] + TRUNC [ TRUNC [ ( STA - TRUNC [(STA-255)/2] ) x Lvl x LM / 300 ] x ND ]

the TRUNC[(STA-255)/2] value being =0 if sta <255 (that part just mean in fact any STA point after 255 give only half benefits).

with ND multiplier due to AAs (=1 without hp-giving AAs) and LM based on class and level :

Warrior:
Level 1-19: Levelmultiplier = 22
Level 20-29: Levelmultiplier = 23
Level 30-39: Levelmultiplier = 25
Level 40-52: Levelmultiplier = 27
Level 53-56: Levelmultiplier = 28
Level 57-59: Levelmultiplier = 29
Level 60-65: Levelmultiplier = 30


I would assume you can get the complete info, including the levelmultiplier for shaman from Magelo. I would check it for you, but Magelo does not like my comp/browser for some reason. Hope this helps.
