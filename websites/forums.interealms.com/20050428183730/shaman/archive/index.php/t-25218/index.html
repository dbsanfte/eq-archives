My playstyle thus far in WoW is pretty much solo unless and until my rl friends login, in which case we group up. Every now and then I accept a group invite out of a morbid sense of curiosity. So far I'm batting 0/7 on pickup groups. I realize that they don't have the coordination that my crew does after playing together so long, but still. Is basic competence asking too much? Some classics in the last week:

Classic #1: Invited to a group in Lakeshire to do some elite quest. I tag along and the warrior pulls an elite mob, then proceeds to train the group with 6 more. I desperately try to heal 4 other people besides myself but go oom in about 15 seconds. Then all I hear in group is "heal!!!" "**** heal!!!" etc... After wipe I get a chance to look at group composition: warlock, warrior, warrior, warrior and me (paladin). Erm, yeah, I'm going to main heal 4 clowns on an elite quest. /disband

Classic #2: So this morning I'm doing another quest that requires me to break into a densely packed orc camp and work my way into the mine behind the camp. Using my "l33t" pally skills I work my way through taking out groups of 1-3 at a time depending what spells I had up. A group comes up behind me and invites me- like an idiot I never learn and accept. Now, instead of simply following the path of cleared mobs to me or asking me to come back, they walk through the center of the camp and train 8 mobs onto me. Complete wipe. I disband, rez and continue on. The following conversation then occurs:

Clueless pickup groupee: Sorry, not sure what happened.
Me: No offense, but I have yet to meet a competent pickup group.
Clueless pickup groupee: We're not incompetent, we just didn't know how to avoid all the mobs in the center of the camp.

They then proceeded to walk through the camp again and got owned (again). Fortunately while they were running for the hills I was able to grab the ore node and chest the mobs were guarding, so not a total loss.

After that long introduction, has anyone been in a competent pickup group? I don't remember LOIO being this bad which is saying something.

I did my first instance the other night, Ragefire Chasm. We had 3 EQ players: me playing my 19 Shaman, another 65 Monk/Shaman/Necro and a Velious-era retiree, both playing Druids. We were 17-21, still learning our classes, to say nothing of other classes' abilities, and we were new to instances. We were learning, upgrading equipment, finishing quests and generally having a good time.

On the other hand, we also had a 26 Mage along for the ride during the first half of the trip. He was not communicating, pulling random mobs even though he was not the puller, never sheeped anything and was pretty much soloing for all practical purposes. So the lesson, as I see it, is to add the good guys to your friends list, the bad guys to the "black list" (not necesarily the ignore list) and try to educate people within reason but not fret over it. It's not like a wipe deep in Fear in 1999 after all :)
