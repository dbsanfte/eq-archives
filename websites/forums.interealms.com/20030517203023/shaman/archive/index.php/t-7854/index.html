Hail,

WITHOUT a DOUBT, get a horse. :)
The cheapest glue-factory horse will give you a tremendous boost in mana regen. ESPECIALLY if you have a JBB.

The Solstice earring is a nice earpiece. But you won't notice the FT1 effect if that's the first FT item you've ever put on. I have both. The Solstice earring was a nice upgrade for my earrings from the Tribunal earring I was wearing, but didn't change anything. The pony immediately improved my soloing power from the very first mob I killed astride my glue-factory rescue pony.

You may like dungeons. Fine.
Realize most of PoP is outdoors. Both the Plane of Innovation & Plane of Decay have some amazing exp grind spots for the high 50's & low 60's player. If you are mid-50's there are some great areas outdoors to grind on the moon. High 40's there's CoM...outdoor dungeon! Low 50's there's lots of places you can solo astride your pony.

Grinding on horseback will make your mana stretch that much farther. NO aggro from switching out spells. That's significant in PoP when sit aggro can end up wasting cleric mana on you and possibly wiping your group.

Take care,

baazak
I'd never really considered buying a horse before, but after reading this thread I thought I'd give it a shot. I cleaned out my bank account - buffed up my charisma and headed to the stables. I grabbed Kei and took my new nag to my current hunting grounds (Spirocs in TD) and mounted it. The first thing I noticed - Great big horse head blocking my view. This was very annoying but on a hunch I cast shrink on myself and the horse head disappeared, though still about half the time I tried to click on or loot a mob I'd end up selecting myself or conning my horse. There were also two other annoyances that became apparent. First the acceleration and deceleration periods take some getting used to - I wasn't always sure when I stopped to cast a spell. Second on horseback you can't crouch to interrupt a spell - didn't realize how useful that was until I couldn't do it anymore. Despite the annoyances I noticed a marked improvement in mana recovery. From the time I got to the Island to the time my Kei gave out (slightly less than two and a half hours) I pulled in just over 3 yellow in exp. I'm normally happy getting 2 yellow per Kei. All told I think it was definitely worth it. I'll be keeping my horse and looking to upgrade to a faster one in the future - I just wish Iksars could purchase a mount that better suited Iksars.

Actually, a horse makes a lot LESS of a difference with KEI.

Let's say you get 25 mana per tick medding. If you are standing, with no horse, you get 1 mana per tick. With a horse, you get 25 mana per tick... 25x the mana regen.

With kei, you get an extra 14 mana per tick, whether medding or not. So without a horse, standing, you get 15 mana per tick, and with a horse you get 40 mana per tick... About 2.5x the mana regen.

So a horse makes 10x more difference with no kei...

So get one :)
