Jinheim
My guild does this:

1) Pets are not allowed, especially AA swarm pets (this is for all classes).
2) Rogues attack from behind.
3) All other melee attack from the sides.
4) Paladin stuns and monks with dragon punch are used for initial positioning and to compensate for overpush in one direction.

Once your melees stop being tards and learn which side is "the back" and which side is "the side" you're pretty much set.

All this is assuming that the MT can't back in to a corner and keep the mob pinned there ("aow it" as well call it). If you can do that, feel free to go buck wild with the pets.

Eatbugs
All nukes push a little bit even though they don't have it listed in spdat.

True, but this isn't the reason for push problems in most cases. (Chain casting a no-push spell can't push as much as one Warrior, Monk or Ranger in melee.) There's a great list of push on spells at this site (http://personal.ghguild.com/croso/pushback.htm). (Personally, I was surprised to find that a number of DoTs have push. Mostly the ones with an initial DD component, but a few without the DD have push as well.)

As for pets - they don't actually have more push than a melee class with the same number of attacks, it's just that a pet will move in instantly to close the gap when something moves away from it. Position a fast attacking melee class (same % haste as the pet) on the other side of a mob and the mob will go nowhere. The problem with pets and raid push is that most pet owners don't reposition pets to account for push the way melee players reposition themselves...
