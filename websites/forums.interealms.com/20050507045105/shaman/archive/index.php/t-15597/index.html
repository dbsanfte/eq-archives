Rorgg
1. i want to be a van shir can they be shamans?
Yes. While not the "power choice" in the end it really makes little difference, plus Vah Shir don't have the faction issues that the "power" races have. While this is a non-issue for long-time players, if you're just starting out, it might not be a bad idea to play a race nobody hates.

2. i decided to be a shaman because they seemed like good solo and group players, but i would like to hear if there are any pros and cons about them.
Pretty much got it on the nose. Shamans solo very well after about level 34, and are a highly desireable grouping class pretty much at every point.
There is a difficult stage in the game between about level 18 and 33 where your primary ability is healing and buffing. This can get pretty dull, and if someone burns out on the class, this is generally where it happens. Also, historically the very best shaman spells as you approach level 60 have been disproportionately expensive compared to those of other classes, though this may be mitigating somewhat recently.

3. i like the idea of group quests but do people like to bring shamans with them?
The new high-level content puts almost an onus on many groups to include a shaman or enchanter. Many, many groups consider shamans an optimal choice for grouping.

Also keep in mind that shamans are sort of a jack-of-all-trades class. There's not much we do better than anyone else, but we do a lot of really necessary things pretty well.

You shouldn't niche yourself. There are other classes just as versatile. be sure to try them all out. That's the great thing about lower levels, you can get a good feel for the class and its possibilities in the first twenty levels, and those go fast anymore. The game also now has a lot of help files to move you along, as well as the various classboards. While I adore my Shaman, my Paladin was my first love and still has a place in my heart. (Shut up, it's sappy but true!) Just remember to play around and play what makes you happy, not what will get you groups. Your personalityt and reputation will get you groups for you, regardless of class :)

The Pros and Cons of any class, though, come from the player behind the computer. Any class in EQ is capable of almost anything: the only limiting factors are gear and you -- and the gear isn't always that important.
