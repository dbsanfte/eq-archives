I don't know about others, but I've mastered the art of "hot-swapping," changing spells on the fly during combat, using the right-click spellbook feature.

I have spells I commonly use saved as a 1 spell spell set (you do this by unmemming all your spells and memming only that one in the spell slot where you want it to go, then saving the spell set. Done this way, the auto-mem doesn't delete all your memmed spells, it only mems the spell in the spell set), so when you activate the spellset, only one spell gets memmed. If you unmem the spell in that spellslot before hitting the spellset, it happens so quickly that you can sit, mem, and stand up if you're tanking a slowed mob, before it has a chance to hit you.

We have 8 spell slots, but when I solo, I use about 12 spells all the time...so I have 6 "one-shots," 6 one spell spell sets. I use a spell, unmem it, then swap in the next one.

This makes cycling DoT's fast and easy, preventing you from having to keep them all up and active. I only have one DoT spell slot, which I cycle, through BoS, BoU, Bane of Nife, Bane, and even occassionally Envenomed Bolt (I don't have my epic yet, but I can put about 5 DoT's on a mob at once by using this trick).

You can pull this trick even without using spell sets just by unmemming a spell and using the right-click feature on the empty spell slot. If you know exactly where the spell you want is on the menu, you can get the spell memmed before Turgur's refreshes after a slow.

Even if you don't use this trick, I suggest everyone create a "gate" one-shot...Gate in the slot of your choice. That way you can quick-mem gate and root like a wildman til it activates, so you can gate to safety if things go wrong. :)

I'm not sure about creating a hotkey for it, since I always use the right-click.

As to roots, it depends on the mob as to how I'm soloing it. I tend to be a "Shank", Shaman tank, so when I solo, I'm usually tanking the mob after I've slowed and crippled it, while dogdog bites his butt. I hit it with DoT's while it's trying to beat on me, hot-swapping my DoT's during melee.

On rougher mobs, I use a dual approach of pet tanking and root rotting until I have my DoTs on him, then I wade in for melee after it's dying by degrees. I pull the mob with a weak spell (BURST OF FLAME baby!!!!) and sic dogdog on it to gain aggro. This is EASY when you've equipped your dogdog with mage proc weapons and maxed his dex with Mortal Deftness and IoS, with Talisman of Kragg on him for some extra HP. Dogdog attacks, gains aggro. You slow the mob after dogdog has taken him down to about 97-96%hp to build up some aggro, Q your dog (he'll need some healing), then DoT the mob until you get aggro. Then you root, back up, DoT, and keep at it until root breaks or you've loaded the mob with all your DoTs. If root breaks, just root again, back out, hit it with another DoT, and repeat until you've stacked all your DoT's on the mob. Then you wade in for some in-your-face melee action to keep your now seriously wounded dog from dying. ;)

It doesn't give you much time to canni, but when I solo, I kill the mob first and worry about mana regen after it's dead. :D

I don't solo in PoP (I lack uber gear and only have 14 AA points), so I've never tried this tactic against anything that hits harder than 200-250 a shot. But against the mobs I do solo, either of these techniques works, and works well.
