Thyramon
There has clearly been much debate on this topic of late. So let's approach the subject logically and analyze our mana regeneration mathmatically. This comparison is based on shaman vs necro SELF mana regen abilities only and assuming 0 aa's.

A 60+ shaman who is doing nothing else but canni-ing and torpor/Q-ing who has no aa's is capable of regenerating approximately 6.0 mps (mana per second) factoring in cast/recast times of the spells, factoring out the mana cost of Q/torpor, presuming 0 lag and only casting other spells and contributing to group activity during the 24s that torpor/Q is running. (Note on the mps calculation: this assumes using canni 4, casting 8 times, then torpor/Q, pause canni-ing until healing complete, resume canni. Over all a 76.2 second canni cycle this provides us a 24 second window of opportunity to do something useful). This results in a shaman spending a full 68.5% of his time Canni-ing and doing nothing else.

By comparison, a 65 necro (only other pure caster class that trades hp for mana) passively regenerates 8.6 mps with his highest level lich and mana regenerating skin spell up. Now then, if you factor out the mana cost for a lifetap over time to maintain their hp, necro mana regen works out to about 5.4 mps. During which time they are dealing 10.5 dps MINIMUM to a mob (best lifetap over time, cast at the minimum frequency to heal maximum possible hp). Additionally, they are not limited in their use of their other abilities. They can FD, cast DoTs/nukes, and not impact the efficiency of their mana regen at all.

So, by using active mana regeneration we gain a 0.6 mps edge and simultaneously sacrifice the ability to do anything else 68.5% of the time without serious drops in our regen efficiency.

SO it works out that we're giving up the majority of our time to casting canni for a whopping 11% edge in mana regen in a best case scenario. Then we are further penalized by having higher mana costs for all our other spells.

Admittedly, the fact that KEI/Tranq/VoQ all stack with our canni while it does not stack with necro lich muddies this up.

Therefore, my suggestion is to change the bearform line of spells into a passive hp to mana conversion spell for shamans that allow us somewhat lower amounts of mana regen amounts than we can accomplish with canni-ing at those levels (i.e. Form of the Bear would be like casting canni 1 once per tick instead of twice, Form of the Great Bear would be like casting canni 3 once per tick instead of twice.) and add a 3rd Bearform with the equivalent of casting canni 4 (or even lower the efficiency from 1.8 hp per mana to 2 hp or even 2.2 hp per mana on it) once per tick. Flag each of the Bearform spells to block your ability to be effected by regular canni while in bearform to block abuse.

This way, shaman's no longer HAVE to actively cann to be efficient. We have the option to use a passive mana regen that is less efficient. This gets sticky when we factor in our AA canni, BUT, this makes the AA canni only marginally more powerful then it already is (by a factor of one cast of Canni 4).

It's just a thought, submitted to the community for your opinions.
