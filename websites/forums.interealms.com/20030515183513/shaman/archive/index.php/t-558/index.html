House
Experience for me has always been much, much better in a decent group. Duoing with any melee/hybrid class except Rogue is a pretty safe bet. Try to find a decently-populated zone in which you could both solo and group, so that you can be looking for a group while soloing, rather than running from zone to zone and possibly ending the night having gotten no experience. Avoid dangerous zones that require a full, well-balanced group, as you'll waste an hour sitting around, waiting for so-and-so's guildmates the Cleric and Enchanter to show up, because everyone refuses to fight without rez and Clarity at hand.

At Level 44, I eked out the last bit of solo experience in The Overthere, and then headed to City of Mist and Timorous Deep for pickup groups from 45-50. I wish I'd started grouping earlier, at Level 40, with Togor's and maxed Defense.

Donar
In my experience, a good group will rake in the experience much faster than just soloing. You don't necessarily have to have a full group, but a duo or trio would be nice. Most of my levels have been the result fo grouping with a warrior and ranger. We made a great trio. The experience was incredible. I have soloed a lot lately to catch up some lagging skills and the experience still comes in nicely, but it doesn't compare to the experience I gained while grouping with them. It was nothing to gain a level in a single gaming session. Levels come more slowly now, of course, but fortunately, the spells are increasing in power these days, so soloing is much more viable. Also, it's nice to have a break from looking at other people's hp-meters. Both grouping and soloing have their advantages and disadvantages. I'd recommend that if you don't have much time to play, solo. Or just put out the work that you're looking for a group. Solo until you get a response. That way, you don't waste any time waiting. Some nights, you'll find a group immediately, others, it could take a while. At level 44, though, you should have no problem getting into groups. By those levels, most players have come to realize how nice it is to fight SLOWED mobs as opposed to non-slowed mobs. Buffers/healers are almost always welcome also. Just do whatever you're in the mood for on a given evening. That's the great thing about being a shaman. You can group wonderfully, but if you wanna keep to yourself, soloing is fine too.

At your level, I did a lot of soloing.... I was in the same fix. Finding a group can be a pain at times. What you can do is pick a nice zone that requires a slower (usually in a dungeon) and with mobs near by that you can solo. I usually spend no more than 10 mins LFG. If I don't find one I solo...
The main reason why I would pick one place to group is that you want to develope a good rep. If you are a decent player, you will soon become a desired commodity. Finding groups becomes very easy. People tend to pick people they know...
For weekends get variety so that you don't get burned out on a place.
