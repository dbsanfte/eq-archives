Viscerator
I'd like to see what everyone considers a "true solo"?
This is largely opinion based, and there is many variables to consider but for me it involves the following:

#1 Only shaman castable buffs. This means only spells you are capable of putting on yourself. Aka spellbook and clicky items. If you own equipment or items that have effects, they would be considered legal. No aego, no KeI.

#2 Potions are legal. See #1. Ideally the shaman was actually the one that made them with his skills. State buffs, mini-clarity, damage shields all are good.
Exception to this rule is stinging wort potions because of their cumulative effect. I'd even go so far as to say 1 Stinging wort cast per fight, but no more, not chain casting it.

#3 Pets are legal and encouraged.

#4 Any tactic is legal; this means kiting, root/dotting, root zoning, gating, whatever it takes to get the job done. (obviously exploits don't count)

#5 You must pull the target solo. You must engage the target solo. You can split the targets however you think is best, but it must be done by the shaman.
This means you can't have a group clear an area for you, so you can kill "boss".
I think fighting to a target, and then waiting for respawns is fine, and obviously locked doors require assistance.

That's the things I can think of off the top of my head. What else is there?
