Durdend
For all of you wanting to get through these lvls, seriously consider SolA.

I went there while just a shade in lvl36 and made it to lvl39 in about 6-8 hours. I was getting 2-4% of a lvl per kill solo on Clockworks.

I was down in the area where the EXG spawns, which drops the Charred Gaurdian Sheild and the Charred Gaurdian Breastplate (got two sheilds - they aren't lore - which sold in 30 minutes in the Bazaar price around 150pp). Here are a few tips.

1) I had PoP so I bind in the Bazaar so I can always gate back and donate for a KEI (I never solo with out it now - man that crack is ADDICTIVE!). Take the Neriak portal in PoP to get to Lava, or donate for a port.

2) Use EQAtlas to find where the EXG camp is. All casting goblins in SolA see invis, so I fought my way there or just ran for the zone down by the Foreman, then fought my way in from there if I had too (only 3 goblins between the Foreman zoneline and the EXG camp).

3) After getting the the EXG camp, I'd get out dogdog if not already out. If you are KoS (troll or ogre - not sure if iksar start out kos to gnomes or not), buff onthe stairs leading down to the EXG area). NOTE!!!! ALL CLOCKWORKS SEE INVIS!!!!!!!!!!

4) Kill the roamer first. Wait on the steps, eventually on clockwork will wander up the steps - this is the EXG or it's PH (note EXG is COMPLETELY 100% immune to magic - although beneficial spells will stick to it - DS's cast by pesky gnome conjurer's). Kill the roamer (for the EXG I just tanked and let dogdog and myself melee, starting my heal when down around 70% - that way incase I was interrupted I'd have another shot instead of running for the zone).

5) After killing the roamer, the next step is the hardest part of the camp. Splitting the first two clockworks. Aggro one, you aggro both. Use pet gaurd me, root one, back up debuff (slow, listless power, Scrouge and Envenom Breath) the one that dogdog is tanking. Then slow the rooted clockwork, I'd also dot it while dogdog and I were finishing off the first. I used Winters Roar to speed up killing the first to help spread the spawn out a little.

6) The next two spawns are single spawns. Cake. One is a clockwork, the other is a gnome (miner - warrior, curator - cleric or conjurer - mage). Nuke the heck out of the curator and conjurers, I hate them.

5) At lvl36 I was able to do these 5 spawns no problem. I figure anyone with a pet could do them (before repops), and make good exp and some decent money (1gp - 3pp each kill, plus FS weapons and some modules that sell for 1-3pp).

7) At lvl39 I can do 9 spawns np before repops (which is 18 minutes in SolA). Still making at least 2% per kill. I figured I could hit lvl40, where exp may take a dip and have to move on. But the exp modifier in SolA is awesome which makes up for alot.

8) Being an old world dungeon, the clockworks hit like sissies (around lvl38 I stopped using Listless Power to save mana for nukes/dots/roots). Also they don't have many hitpoint AT ALL. About 70% of the hp of the same lvl Velious or Kunark mobs.

9) Just be prepared to be hated in Ak'Anon (which is an easy sacrifice in my book). Been there 3 lvls and still not KoS as a Barbie Shaman - but I am down to Dubious.

10) Only vendor close is the Druid out in Lavastorm. Sell all unwanted FS to her along with the words/runes/modules.

I hope that helps some of the other soloing shaman out there in the lvl34-39 range.

I made a small map of where Durdend was fighting, I think anyway. I did all this at work using paint so it's sorta crappy. The black marks the run in. I did this last night, it was pretty exciting. I sow'ed myself before I zoned in, then just booked it. Some of the passageways are fake walls, around the foreman and into room 6, so keep cool and run through them. I paniked a bit and went in a small circle but I made it to solb.

After the run I zoned back into sola, got my pet up, medded up to full, took out the 2 or 3 golbins in the room marked 6, then i moved to the green spot. You might run into one or two more in the room next to the green spot but they're easy. Then I sat and medded at the green spot, on the stairs. this is also where I buffed my pet, gave him tnarg, and regen if he needed it, I also hasted him but I didn't bother to keep it up after it wore off. You're hps and mana shouldn't be to low, so if the wanderer (red line) does come you should be able to beat him easy, or move so you don't get argo.

The hardest part is breaking the 2 clockworks next. They both come if you pull them, so fight them the best you can. I would start by rooting one, slowing the one you're fighting then dotting both, but i'm sorta new to solo'ing so if you know a better way let me know.

The next 2 spawns are easy to kill. Just be careful of the bridge. If you can break the spawns up the camp is super easy and really good exp and loot.

I never went further then the last red dot marked on the map. Past that I saw 2 more gnomes, one was a caster so I didn't even bother. But those mobs should keep me busy for a while.

The hard part for me is only fighting one at a time, it's hard for me to take on more then one. If I get one go down really easy. I'm going to make another stab at it later today. Another thing to remember is always root when you're fighting one.
