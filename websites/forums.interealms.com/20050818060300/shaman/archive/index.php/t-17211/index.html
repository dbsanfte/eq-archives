Fudapuda
Honestly, though, offensive combat skills aren't hard to keep maxxed, you just have to go about it the right way.

There are a number of NPCs spread out through the game who you can stand and tank against for long periods of time without either killing them or dying to them. The exterminators in the various towns are a good example: I used the exterminator in Neriak to level up the combat skills of an alt of mine--he never hits for more than about 3 points of damage, but he has probably 1 million HP, and regens fast besides. And he's in a spot where you can solo him without adds. So just take a selection of weapons, and tank away at him for a while. Buff up your agility and dex (one of them supposedly raises your combat leveling, but I can't recall which one).

There was also a mob in GD you could use for this at higher levels, though I've heard he may be gone now. Mist Panther in Wakening Lands works well for this too, though he hits a bit harder. And I think there are others out there as well.

Its just a matter of devoting the half-hour or so to getting the skill up instead of making exp. Same as with swimming--put on an EB item, park yourself swimming in a corner of Halas lake or somewhere like that, and come back in a half hour or so, to see how much you've leveled.

Kriel Darkbane
I'd just pick one of either 1h blunt of Piercing and stick to that one. It's very good to have one up-to-date weapon skill to use for slaughtering greens if you need to. Past a certain point, that's all a weapon skill is for a shaman. It's not something you use much when you're seriously engaged in combat.

I picked piercing because it was easy to twink with an uber piercer at the time (rune etched icewurm fang), and because I wanted to get my epic someday, and have the skill to melee with it if I felt like it. 1H blunt would be fine too, if you have a reason to prefer that.

I think 1h choices are preferable, because that way you can use a shield, and get more AC and stat bonuses than you'll normally get with a 2h weapon. There are some advantages to 2h weapons in some cases, but overall I didn't feel it was worth working on a second weapon skill. I've never regretted doing only one, so far, at L 58.

And if for some odd reason you find yourself desperately desiring some other weapon skill post-60, you can always sink a few trainings into it (or not) and work it up then.
Calabar
I have four characters past level 51 and for a very long time one of them was a ranger. So it was drilled into my head to max all my weapon skills. As you’ve discovered, it’s not as easy as it sounds. I agree with what others have said, pick either piercing or 1H blunt to focus upon, depend on what your best weapon is. If you manage to max that skill then switch to your other weapon if circumstances permit until you level again. But don’t worry too much about your other weapon skills too much until you stop leveling as quickly. By then you should have a good number of training points saved up.

Unless you want to use some of your points starting trade skills, and its not really necessary for most of them, then you don’t really have much to spend your points on. What I did to give myself a boots when I wanted to max all my weapon skills was spend training points on them. You can spend up to about 60 points on a weapon skill before the cost starts to become prohibitive. You can do this with your remaining two skills and have a much better chance of hitting then if you started at zero.

If you can find one for sale, the Bamboo Bo Stick (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=486) is a nice 2H blunt weapon to raise your skill. Its faster than the abandoned orc shovel (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=467) someone else mentioned, though more expensive, and it actually does some damage: Damage: 8 Delay: 20.

But as others have also said, chances are you won’t be using your weapon skills every much. You need to max your defense skill as soon as possible, but if you group you usually be casting. Still, its nice to have the skills for those times you find someplace you can solo. It makes for a nice change of pace.
