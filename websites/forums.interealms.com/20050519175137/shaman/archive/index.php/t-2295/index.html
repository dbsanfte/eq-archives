Sciek Spellbottler
Well, there's two ways to look at it...

1. The items that you're wearing now were good the day before yesterday, thus they're still good items today. Some items, such as the Shadel Ring IMO, were already very nice items for the difficulty of obtaining them. So, adding in a level III focus effect may have made them a bit TOO nice for the difficulty to obtain.

Similarly, some of the items with crappy stats that nobody used previously are now useful. Obulus Death Shroud for example. Drops in a fairly difficult area, but IMO at least, was pretty crappy item. I've seen them rot several times before because everyone in group had better. I don't think they'll be rotting anymore though heh.

2. Then there's the comparison mode of thought. You look at Item A that drops off a relatively easy mob and see that it has an effect you'd love to have, whereas the item you're wearing in that same slot dropped off a mob much harder and has no effect or a lesser effect. So, you end up feeling slighted somewhat...

Now the potential problem with this view is that you have to also look at the harder to acquire item as a whole before comparing to the easier to get item. If you have a bracelet with ac20, 50hp, 5svall that drops in ToV then see another bracelet outta Seb that has mana pres III, first glance may seem like you're being slighted. However that seb bracelet only has ac5, 2int/wis, and 10hp so is it REALLY better than the ToV bracelet?


In the end, as said before, I think we'll have to wait for the dust to settle and see what other effects get added in the next round and how many more items get focus effects added. THEN we can make some more accurate judgements.

Grippa
The whole level restriction thing seems to have been done backwards. Items with level restrictions should get better as you level up, not useless. Or at least that's the established pattern for level restricted items in the game so far.

Here's how I would have done it (isn't hindsight a wonderful thing?)

- Instead of level restrictions, give the focus effects different percentages. For example, level I = 5%, level II = 10%, level III = 20%.

- Restrict the items' levels using the recommended level system, so that a level III item scales to a level I item for a level 20 character, and so on.

- Make the percentage effects cumulative, like flowing thought, but with a sensible cap, maybe 50%.


This way, a high level person could collect several (for example) level I casting haste items, to get the equivalent of a level III item, but at the cost of using more inv slots. Higher level items would still be better, but low level items wouldn't be useless.
