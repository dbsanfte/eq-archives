Rorgg
Remember, the point of using any Mal* spell besides Malo(s) isn't really to get slow on faster -- the point is for you NOT to bounce a slow, which will lead to you having a very angry unslowed mob in your face. As many others have pointed out on various threads, if Malosinia (or replace with other spell here) sticks, Turgur would have stuck too; they have the same resist check.

It's still a good usage -- I often go Malos -> Malosinia -> Turgur on boss mobs, not to get Turgur on in fewer casts, but so that I don't bounce a Turgur, and if I do, the main tank's had more time to build aggro, since Mal* is such low aggro. Heck, occasionally I'll go Malos -> Malosinia -> Cripple -> Turgur for the same reason.

Kianor
Ok, here is my question....

What check do MoBs use to see if MoB resists the Mal* line of spells? It seems like my debuffs (Mal*) gets resisted as much as the slow itself does. If it uses a magic resist check (the same as the slow) why not spam cast the slow untill it sticks? Aren't the odds of either landing the same?

You're right, they both have the same chance to resist, until you get the unresistable ones of course (Malo, Malos).

However, and this is the big difference, a resisted slow is high agro, and an unslowed mob pounding on your head hurts. A resisted mal* is low agro, and is unlikely to take agro from the tank.

So if you mal* first, then when you do slow, you're less likely to resist and to have an unslowed mob hitting you.
