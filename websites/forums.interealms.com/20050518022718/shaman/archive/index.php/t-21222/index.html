In order of preference, what I think the shaman should be able to do is as follows.

1. Buffing (meaningful non stat related buffs)
2. Debuffing (effective short or long term debuffs)
3. Damage (not too much, but surely we should be able to do more than 1/20 of a mob's life during a short battle)
4. Healing (if the druid or cleric dies, I'd like to think I can heal fast enough to keep someone up for 30 second till the battle is over and the real healers can be rezzed. Perhaps a bigger, horribly mana inefficient fast heal is in order).

I should always be able to buff and have it mean something. With stat gear now, buffs are worthless. FA is nice, but its so damn tedious to maintain, that I dread hearing melee in my group ask "So, you got FA?".

Debuffs. Well, thats a hard one. I can understand it being hard to tweak encounters where a mob is either hitting 100%, or hitting 25% of the time. To make an encounter tough when its 75% slowed, would mean that at full speed, the mob would chew thru tanks like mad. But, to be honest, thats SOE's fault for no seeing that. You can't devise an entire class around the power to slow, then take it away in GoD and on many boss mobs, and expect everyone to feel ok about that.

If I were developing EQ way back at the beginning, and knew what I know now, I would have never allowed the concept of slowing a mob into the game. Debuffs that maybe take 10-20% of a mobs power away, sure. But 75%... The extreme to which SOE made slow such a vital part of EQ has come back to bite us all when they make mobs immune to it. You can also say the same about battle duration. DoTs for damage, and long cast times on Malos, in order to land slow, are not working well in GoD, as many of you know.

The problem with debuffs is, there isn't an easy answer. If you give us more damage, more healing, and more buffs to makeup for lack of slow, we take that increased power back to content not designed for it. We'd have shaman balanced in GoD, but overpowered in any older content that is slowable and long duration.

Really, the shaman class needs a complete overhaul. Or GoD does. We either need a completely different role that works in the old and new content, or the new content needs to keep in line with the old.
