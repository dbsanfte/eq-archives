Kamidari
As a side note, you can bring up the tribute window when you're out and about, too. It only lets you downgrade (like ft5->ft4) or turn off tribute effects while in the field though. Have to enable them at the tribute master.

The 10 minute timer starts when you close the tribute screen at the tribute master, and pauses if you re-open it. When the counter reaches 0, it deducts the cost for your currently active effects and starts over. If you turn off all effects, the timer stops, but stays at it's current value, so it continues down from there when you re-enable any effects. you can use this to work the system a little (if your timer is down low, buy something cheap and wait for it to roll over, then enable the expensive stuff), but in the long run, that's probably not really worth the effort.

Saldaora
About inflation...

Casino will remove vast amounts of platinum out of the market. In isolation this would cause deflation (negative inflation). People have less money in their pockets to buy that Obulus Death Shroud therefore will not buy. Mr Vendor who's trying to sell the ODS will think "***, why isn't this selling?" And reduce his price. Prices will go down until people start buying again. It's called the "invisible hand" in economics (iirc!).

Tribute system will remove vast amounts of low to mid-end items. In isolation this will reduce the number of low to mid-end items making them rarer on the open market. Vendors will be able to sell their Black Alloy Medallions at higher prices since they have fewer competitors when attempting to sell it. Buyers will have to pay more for them since there are so few available. This is also called the "invisible hand".

In combination these two changes will... uhm... I figure reduce the prices of high-end items and increase prices of mid to low end items. Don't quote me on that tho. This is on the assumption that higher end items will NOT be turned into tribute NPCs.

My economics classes are far in the past, perhaps people still working or studying it can enlighten us further or correct me if I'm wrong :)
