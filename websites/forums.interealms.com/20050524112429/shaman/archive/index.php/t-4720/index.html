Nektar
There are a lot of skills and spells out there that provide for good eye candy, but don't really amount to much in the long run. Look at rabid bear, reload time is insane, and only lasts 3 mins or so. Some people are quick to dismiss eye candy in such cases as nothing more than just that. But lemme tell you this, Everquest is a game, a game where you're meant to have fun. Sounds kinda cliched, but it's true. That last sentence also sounded cliched, but damnit it's true too! :D

A lot of these eye candy skills/effects/spells make the person all warm and tingly inside. People are cheering you on, and you're liking it. Look at me for example, I have 1 point in healing gift. Not the most useful skill, especially for a shaman, but getting to the top of the AA ladder is not a race, I can take my time.

I became venerable only once I got 22 points, but it didn't bother me in the slightest, I was sprinkling my points left and right. Some skills were vital (hi, 80 base wis here!) and others not so vital ie spell casting fury (***). On the other hand, I see a lot of shamans spending their first 6 points in general (run3 + something random), then the next 12 on MC, and then straight to canni. Not that there's anything wrong with that, canni 5 makes us that much more efficient, especially on raids, but is this efficiency always fun?

That's why I've been dabbling around really. As much as I want Canni 5, I don't wanna feel like a mindless drone grinding 24/7 constantly reminding myself "must... have... Canni!" I wanna kick it along the way, and the crits I pull off every once in a while do actually make me happy and make the game more fun.

Example: Few days ago, there was this pickup Tormax raid at around 5am. Since the fugger is resistant to ice, I decided to mem burst of flame for ****s and giggles. The healing was amazingly very well handled for a pickup raid of this calibre, so I had time every once in a while to BoF. Well, eventually, I got a crit, for a whopping 6 damage! People were thinking of this as a fluke, but soon enough, I score another crit for a WHOLE 6 damage! Anyways, it did bring a smile to my face, and that early in the morning, any form of amusement uplifted my spirits significantly.

But it's the very little things like that which make my gaming experience more pleasurable. I like it when I perform an exceptional heal because of the word exceptional, makes me feel all special and stuff, especially in a raid with 40 people. There you have 10 people chaining heals on the MT, and there I am, standing out alone in the crowd as the only shaman with a crit heal, so chances are, people are gunna remember me more than anyone else because of that one single crit emote.
