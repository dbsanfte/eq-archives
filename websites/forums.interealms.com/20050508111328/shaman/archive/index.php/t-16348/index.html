PennyAntics
After modifications and lots of adventuring, items are purchasable that surpass Vex Thal loot, and for all we know may be better than Plane of Time loot. The thing that is most notable about this however is that now players who are willing to invest time in everquest but never had the chance to join a large and serious raiding guild will have access to very powerful items.

The question at hand however is if the balance of this reward system is well tuned. One has to step back and think, "does x number of LDoN adventures equal the same amount of work it takes for a person to progress to Vex Thal or Early Planes of Power?"

Hardly a rhetorical question - it has yet to be answered. Will folks be able to casually farm up points yet quickly be able to contend with people who do the old fashioned style of obtaining uber loot? Problems like mobilizing your raid and more importantly finding the mob you wish to raid spawned are problems not applicable to the system LDoN uses.



In no way am I saying there is anything wrong with LDoN. I simply dont know right now; nobody does. The only thing that bothers me is that Sony just took a giant leap in a completely new direction, and I am worried about how that will effect the game.

Jinheim
I am liking the ideas behind LDoN very much. It gives unguilded people a chance to work for something specific, rather than just "go sit at that bot camp and hope i win some loot I can sell for pp to buy what I want." You can now set a goal and go straight for it, which I really like. Raiders, like myself, now have something to do when they aren't raiding. In the last month or so, I have gotten very burnt out on EQ. I have over 250AA, and really don't feel like grinding out more just for the sake of doing it. There are also very few 1 or 2 groupable items or quests that I can do to improve my gear, so I am stuck there as well. LDoN might very well be an answer to this. I haven't seen the high end loot of LDoN yet, but I am sure that there is plenty of it that I can use. Augmentation is also very very cool, and an excellent idea.

Another thing I like about the expansion is how it was implemented. It is VERY convenient to get started doing things. You can just port inbetween camps instantly to wherever you want to go, then you just pick a mission and get to it. There is very little bull**** involved with this expansion. It is all very convenient and straightforward.

The only qualms I can forsee myself having is that the missions might get very old, very fast. Even though there are 4 different types of missions, it is really just the same thing: Clear mobs until you reach your objective. However, when you consider what a normal exp group is (clear mobs until you get so bored you want to cry and log out), it just might work out well :)

The final thing I like about LDoN is the new skeleton models. I own an AoN, and I am very pleased with how I look now: Glowing green orb in my offhand, glowing green snake-stick in my primary, and they match my glowing green eyes.
