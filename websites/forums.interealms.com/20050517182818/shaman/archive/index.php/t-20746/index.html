Two negatives and one kinda misperception usually come through on any race comparison thread.

The misperception -- ogres have low wisdom. They have 3 less wisdom than barbs, which are never noted for their low wisdom(since they don't have it). So calling ogres low wisdom is kind of really stretching the point of how valuable 3 wisdom really is. At high or low level -- it's not much.

The unmentioned negatives -- trolls have negative 30 to fire resist, and iksars have negative 30 to cold resist. That's actually quite a lot. It's not as important as it used to be when the game was in velious, and in raids you were constantly fighting dragons that cast fire/cold spells -- or even in kunark groups, where many more monsters you battled were wizards casting that kind of spell(very few are now).

On a side note, I would have to agree that if you are big into raiding and seeing the high end game, shaman isn't the best choice. Damn. it's sad seeing people on the Shaman board saying "Don't make a Shaman" :( it's even more sad that i agree with them.

Here's the thing with Shaman. They're a pain in the rear in the early game. They level slow, not just because of possible racial penalties, but because shaman kill things very slowly, and in the early levels (1 - 50) don't have a terrible lot to offer a group. Once you hit 49, though, you really take off. You get some nice DoTs, a shiny new pet, and in two levels your Spell, Turgur's. From 51 - 62, it's a hell of a ride, and then, at 62, it slows down quite a bit, and just sort of grinds along as you watch other classes do the same things you do, only bringing more to the table, and you can't really solo for exp anymore.

But, if you do anyway, here's some recommendations :

The best Shaman race overall is probably Ogre. Stun immunity from the front, high hit points, still nice wisdom. Slap 20 points into Wisdom and 5 into Agility (if you have under 75 Agility, you take a rather hefty AC penalty).

Oddly enough, I would say Froglok is #2, because it has a small, low-lag model, innate ultravision, same stats as a barbarian, better than Iksar swimming and breath, some very nice newbie quests, and the absolute best animations in the game... Their armor graphics look very dippy, though, more like original EQ skins than luclin polygons. For them, I would slap all into Wisdom, and the remaining 5 into Stamina (which is already pretty high for an amphibian)

For a full newbie, I would advise against Iksar or Vah Shir, both for the same reason - They don't get free armor. Sure, the Vah Shir get Ghulam, but, personally I found this to be disgustingly tedious.

Finally, for your skills... Max your Defense, your Channeling, and your Meditate every level if you can. Save your skill points for stuff you have to buy (Channeling, meditate, Dodge) and tradeskills. For a weapon, stick with one handed peircing or blunt with a shield - There's really no 2hb worth the loss of AC and stats a sheild can give until some really high-end stuff.

While I certainly respect your opinion, your statement, "you can get better results from a host of other classes, both for soloing fun, raiding fun, or all-around balance." doesn't wash with me.

Most classes in game can not solo, so this statement is not true for almost all melee (except BST), also, chanters can certainly solo using charmed pets, but it's much higher risk and they will die on a regular basis, I can't even remember the last time I died solo. So which classes are left? Necros and Druids can certainly solo very well, but neither is able to get groups as well as a shaman (justified or not, just reality). Yeah, Beastlord can solo and group, but again, this assumes you want to play a melee (which some folks don't) or u want to play pet cleric (boring). Oh, and which classes are better balanced than a shaman, could you please point them out to me? Maybe druid, maybe bst if you don't care about heal, maybe necro and bard. That's perhaps 4 out of 13 classes. Doesn't really seem like a host of other choices. And let's not forget, they all have their weaknesses too.

Some of you are quick to discount shaman strengths and overstate our weaknesses. Using this logic, you can make any class look like do do. Keep your comparisons objective and I think you will see that things are not quite as bad as you may think.

Yes, there is a huge difference between a shammy's 75% slow at level 51 and a BST's 50% slow until level 65 (and most BSTs I know don't even have the 65% slow spell). There is also a huge difference between being able to heal for 428 (at level 62) and being able to heal for 1950 or 420/tick for 4 ticks. So, yeah, BST dps is nice, not at the level of a true dps class like a wiz, magician, rogue, monk or ranger, but nice. Coupled with their other talents make them a great class. But they in no way take away from what a good shaman can do. They have their strengths and weaknesses just like every other class. So when you compare, be fair.

And, most melee's I know would rather have a mob slowed 75% than not at all, and have to rely solely on healing and dps to keep them alive. One thing I personally hate about playing a tank (especially warrior) is that I am totally dependent on the healer to keep me alive. At least with a mob whose dps has been cut by 75%, I have a much greater chance of survival even if the heals are a bit slow in coming, or the healer is distracted for a tick or two.

Also, the comment about "why didn't you roll up a druid?" is again, very simplistic and flawed logic. I guess then, if you want to heal, you should be a cleric, if you want to dps, you should roll a wiz or rogue, and if you want to tank, roll a warrior. I guess there is no need for hybrids or any of the other dps classes. Only problem is that they are some of the classes that are most fun to play. Sure, the one-dimensional "specialist" classes will always be better at a specific task than a hybrid, but that is why they can also be very boring to play.

Bottom line is that I do not share the overly dramatic "end of the world" view of some of the folks on this board. Sure, shaman and other classes could use some tweeking, in this type of complex game, it is to be expected, class balance will NEVER be perfect. However, I would never, EVER, discourage someone who wants to play a fun and versatile classs from picking a shaman.

On the contrary, I believe shaman are still one of the best choices, if not THE best choice, available for someone who plays to have fun. Yes, to me, being able to heal well and save someone from death is huge. Being able to buff their stats, atk, haste, regen, speed, thus making them better at what they do, is huge. And being able to solo with doggy when I feel like it is nice too.

Give me a shammy anyday.
