Stor
Hydrotha is no joke. He'd be simple were it a fight on dry land, but the knockback of water makes it an absolute bitch.

We killed him for the first time after about 3 days of playing with him, with about 45 people and 8 clerics. Our main tank is about 9.5k.

As far as I can tell, curing the blind is impossible. It's much better to have your tanks 2 box so they can see if they get blinded.

Just outside Hydrotha's room, there is a T intersection and a large rock. We set up just outside this room and pull him to here. The tank should try to position him with his own back to the rock. This should help a little with knockback, but a he will inevitably be pushed around, up, and over the rock, especially once your retarded melee start pushing Hydrotha all over the place.

Knockback makes proximity agro touchy, so tell your melee to SURROUND Hydrotha and keep it him roughly centered in the middle of the room. Don't push him into the wall or ceiling, because he can and will warp. Have your melee go easy on the procs and agro-inducing attacks. Turn off attack occasionally, or back up. Most of your damage should come from nukes. Pets are also a bad idea as their push is constant, unless the master is not a complete tard. This is a feel-it-out kind of fight, and I don't think it's really possible to break it down to a science.

Also, we set up the pulls and initial heals in an unorthodox way. To keep clerics from being eaten, we had our 3 paladins use LoH for the first 3 heals, giving the tank time to build agro so the clerics don't get eaten. Surprisingly, the paladins didn't die. I suppose you could also pull with a weaponshield ranger, but that didn't work as well for us.

Make no mistake, Hydrotha is a bitch.
