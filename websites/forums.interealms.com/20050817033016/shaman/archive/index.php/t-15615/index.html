Bigmeeno
I agree. CA and LR are much more effective than CS and ID. CA/LR make the mob MISS you more, so when it works, you are effectively eliminating 100% of the dmg the mob would have hit you for. CS/ID on the other hand only mitigates the dmg the mob does do to you, reducing a hit by some number depending on which level of the ability you have at that point. But you can bet that mitigation will be lots less than 100%.

I also would suggest SCM3 and QB2 or 3 (depends on your preferrences, I went with QB3). They save you mana/casting time on buffs. QB3 will also cut your casting time on Torpor/Quie in half. That makes it much easier to get it on yourself when you are being beat on.

Also consider getting RC1. It will remove many curses and other detrimental effects not only from you, but from your group members as well. Its also the only way I know of to remove malo and tash short of dispelling yourself. And you can use it mana free every 3 mins.

Those are the main ones... We get alot of good/fun AA's The trick is getting the points to buy them all.

Ungerargh
Radiant Cure 1 removes 9 poison/disease/curse counters, and has a chance to dispel one negative effect that doesn't have a counter (malxxx, snare, root, necromancer snare/fire dots, etc.).

Radiant Cure 2 removes 12 poison/disease/curse counters, and has a chance to dispel three negative effects.

Radiant Cure 3 removes 16 poison/disease/curse counters, and has a chance to dispel six negative effects.


In my experience, radiant cure 1 is good...but not good enough. There are times I've had a malo wear off via duration on me, while popping RC1 every time it was available. Like a dispel, there's no guarantee it's going to remove counter-less effects. With RC2, you're virtually guaranteed to remove one negative effect, and, more often than not, two. Unless you're really unlucky (or have low resistances), you're not likely to have more than 1-2 counter-less effects on you at a time (one possible "worst case" scenario could be malxxx, snare, root, necro fire dot, necro snare dot, etc.), so I agree that RC2 would be sufficient for most instances.
