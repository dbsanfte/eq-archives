BlargX
I've found focus items extremely useful. They can turn relatively crappy items like the Obulus Death Shroud into very good items that can really help you notice a difference in your gameplay.

I have six of them, I think. Some junk like shadel ring, some not droppable like shissar ring. Just got a ceramic gavel of justice, range slot, and spent 18k on it. Might be way out of line for it, I don't know. Terrible except for the focus effect. It's affliction efficiency IV, which supposedly is a 20% savings on DOTS/slow/debuff. That's fine for me when soloing, as I now tend to solo mobs I will put at least four bane of nife's on, as well as slow and malo, so 20% per each of those spells will add up to some solid mana savings.

Sometimes, as you see with your own amulet of insight, though, you have to wear an inferior item to get that reward. To use the AE IV item, I have to take out an item that's actually fairly decent, the Divine Inspiration, 15 AC 15 wis, I think maybe 35 mana besides. I'm sure I won't always be swapping in the ceramic whatsis instead; just sometimes. But since you have money to burn, you might want to look for one. I was thinking it might be pottery made, and you can make it yourself? No idea.

Some droppable ones that I don't have are the wicked bone atlatl range slot and the sharp claw ahlspeiss range slot. The former is something like haste of mithaniel(speeds up spell casting) I think, and sharp claw ahlspeiss is I think affliction extention or efficiency IV. I saw the atlatl for 25k once and have never seen the ahlspeiss sold; probably won't, since it comes off a gator in the PON caves that hardly anyone ever hunts.

Orb of Infinite Void used to be 35k only a handful of months back; now I've seen it going as low as 15k. That's a very nice upgrade to snark shield you have.

If possible, buy MQ's of some quest armor(dragon or giant) after you get ally status with either of them. They will be huge upgrades to you and can be a real pain to get yourself sometimes; you can raid for months without getting or being awarded a chain tunic for instance. Expect to pay a lot of money. Don't get the gloves; they're garbage. The wrists are nice enough but frankly I have them and wear my coldain military wristguard and my JBB bracer all the time, which I see you have. The legs, helm, feet, chest are particularly good though, and the arms don't completely totally stink. This stuff is far from top end these days, but far above what you're going to be able to buy, generally.

Oh, Veterans Chain Leggings drop off the Hedge Maze flagging sometimes. They're pretty nice, too.

Shield of Bane Warding wouldn't be bad, either; I camped up two of them myself. Try to bring someone who can track, especially track undead, like a pally. It will make hunting the skeletons in Tenebrous MUCH easier; you can get through it in a day instead of a week if you're lucky. The skellies spawn consistently in one place, but not often; however, they also run more widely around the zone as well, and if you miss one, you may have added hours to your camp. It's much faster to do the tenebrous part with a tracker.
