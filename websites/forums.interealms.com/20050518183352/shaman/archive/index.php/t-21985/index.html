Sariss
It's not fair to call us unbalanced because there's not 9 of us in a raid.

Clerics are the most, because in most raids, they have to be the most. When you can't get enough clerics, you tack on enough druids to help make up... and lastly shamans. If some night all your clerics show up, fine, your druids can backup heal and nuke.

I'm fairly confident that when looking at a class, you have to look at

- Solo Play
- Group Play
- Raid Play

These are 3 very different aspects, which people all enjoy differently. For some, Soloing is what they want most... not having to bother with groups, or only have a short time, or just want to stay away from people in general... WHATEVER.

Some people like to raid... just look at the "casual vs uber" debates... not every "non-raider" is "casual". I certainly logged enough hours on EQ, but didn't look for a raiding guild.

Finally there's the people who just enjoy a good group, hanging with friends, whatever.

The fact is, classes typically fit into some of these different, and have almost forever.

* Shamans are excellent soloers.

* Shamans are very good in groups - could we be better... sure... the new "faster fight" style doesn't go well with the slow process, but we still do offer a lot, and depending on the content can be healers.

* Shamans are "required" for raids, however you truly require only 1. That might be a busy job, and a bit riskier, but you really truly NEED 1 shaman... after that, more is gravy, backup, share duties, etc... Except for the healing, shamans don't "stack" well on raids.

Of course these are "generalizations" but I believe them to be mostly accurate.

Now lets compare to the clerics.

* Clerics are pitiful soloers. They CAN solo some things... but really, it's not productive. It's not that clerics want to be massive soloers, it's the "I just have 30 minutes and wanna do something"... or can't find a group.

* Clerics are very good in groups. I don't say "Excellent" here because it really depends on the content... the older the content is, the more shamans and druids have been able to replace them... whereas in the GoD content, it's more "cleric required" (I'm not saying REQUIRED FOR EVERYTHING... just leaning more to them in most cases, if not required). Clerics still shine in groups, but in cases where hardcore healing is not required, you can substitute more DPS.

* Clerics are Excellent in raids. They stack almost indefinately. This is because of the way a lot of content is designed sadly... but it's still a reality of the game.

So right there... when you look at a raid... almost always you'll find a ton of clerics... and maybe not so many shamans. All it does is show the balancing in the "raid" portion of the game.

While not every shaman wants to solo... we have exceptional soloing skills, and that IS part of class balance.
