Being able to stick ~400 hp/mana per slot is pretty far into the realm of the ridiculous. Sure, you can keep escalating the general damage output of new encounters, but everything that came before offers diminished rewards and fails to maintain player interest. Since older content gets trivialized by new expansions, you create a constant need for even more expansions to keep new content flowing to those players who are pushing the EQ envelope.

I think Blazz has it right in regards to SOE creating too large a gap between gear sets in new areas. If they would have maintained a more moderate pace with gear stats and just made the encounters more intricate, it would have kept more of EQ viable and alive for a larger number of players. Currently, there really is no need to ever visit the older parts of the world unless some quest npc gets stuck there or you want to take a trip down nostalgia lane.

I would love to see them release HUGE expansions again rather than giving us multiple bursts of little expansions, which are great a generating revenue for SOE, but fail to really draw you into the EQ world like the old expansions did. You can seriously get lost in some of the Kunark/Velious/Antonica zones if you ditch the compass and maps, but would only be able to achieve that same feat in the maze-like zones of GoD or OoW. Most of the regular new zones are so simple and small, that you can practically see the ZO from the ZI.

Although DoN is not out yet, it's rapid release feels more like a shot from the SOE marketing department to gauge current interest in the game than a serious expansion. Anytime they bundle utility features into an expansion, it usually means they skimped on zone content ala LoY.
