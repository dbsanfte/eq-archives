House
As for your second question, faction is not as big of a deal as it once was if you have Velious and Luclin (even Kunark isn't that bad). The best thing that Trolls get (along with Iksars) is improved natural regeneration. Troll regeneration probably isn't going to save you during a fierce battle, but over the lifetime of the character it really adds up. The biggest downside to being a Troll is the experience penalty: it is definitely there and it is noticeable. Starting Wisdom is also the lowest of the Shaman races, though personally I don't think this is that big of a deal. Starting Stamina, on the other hand, is pretty good.

I've been very happy with my Troll Shaman, and the only other race I'd consider playing, were I forced to start another character, would be an Ogre.

BlargX
Sneak isn't really a traveling thing. You have invis for that, and SOW.

It's really great for buying from merchants who wouldn't normally buy from you, though. That makes buying and selling everywhere possible, which is very handy.

Being agnostic is extremely handy, too. Lack of deity-specific things doesn't matter much, and not nearly as much as the freedom to walk, bank, buy and sell most everywhere freely. That's really, really handy.

That also means you can get the Overthere hammer, too, which is a great way to teleport yourself instantly there to save travel time, bank, buy/sell, buy spells, etc. It's a really great item to have. And if you're agnostic, you're able to do the sarnak beserker tails quest, too, to raise your OT faction. This is an incredible quest; killing the mobs that drop the tails is great experience, and turning in the tails four at a time is outrageously good experience.

Good-aligned race, agnostic for deity, and sneak means you have an incredible ability to move freely throughout the world. That's very nice in EQ, where faction can make things very difficult and time-consuming, and trying to fix it can be incredibly tedious.

Safe fall is pretty darn nice too. Bad falls slowing digging away at your health is annoying, and without batwings when you're on a corpse recovery, you either have to go much slower to avoid it or just lose a lotta health when you have no gear on at all.

Anyway, cat shamans have some real negatives, too, but they have some very nice positives as well.
