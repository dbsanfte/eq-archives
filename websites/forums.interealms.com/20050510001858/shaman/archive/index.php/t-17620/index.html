deria
The hard adventures are, in a word, hard. They are certainly doable, but you really do need to have the right group -- which typically means a very high DPS group with some redundancy. A good group would consist of cleric, shaman, paladin, beastlord/mage, beastlord/mage, ranger. You do not need to be PoTime (or even elemental) equiped but the better equiped you are the better off you will be.

I include the shaman in the group because... I'm a shaman. Realistically, a shaman is NOT a requirement if a) you have a resistance debuffer such as an enchanter or magician and b) have one or more beastlords with their good slow.

On the whole, I prefer normal adventures. I fight in adventures for points, and I am rather indifferent to the experience/loot to be found during adventures. I would personally rather spend 90 minutes and have a guaranteed 102 points than spend 90 minutes on a 50/50 chance of getting 107 points or 0 points.

If you're looking for a challenge, or you're looking for loot, hard may be the level for you.

Jinheim
In all honesty, if your group isn't all strong level 65's, you are going to fail in hard mode. You might be able to support one weak toon or maybe even two if everyone else is uber, but hard really is hard. You don't all need to be in full EP gear with 300+ AA, but it definately helps. The most important thing is to have a strong tank. If your tank sucks, he's going to die or lose aggro, then it is all over. You will also need a competent ench who is capable of taking a few hits. Other than that, the other toons need to be attentive and capable of doing their jobs well. They don't necessarily need to be uber, but I would say that being 65 and having above average gear is a necessity. Also very important: Don't use idiots. I can join just about any ldon normal group and unless it is just absolutely horrible, I can pull them through to a win. A hard mission, though, everyone needs to be a good player.

If the ench uses CoD, it will help your DPS a lot. The better your ench, the better your charming will go. A very uber ench has a chance of being able to survive a charm break when his pet is duel-wielding and hasted (i.e. quadding for 980), but a normal ench won't. Usually when I do hard, we leave the pet unhasted and without weapons. More than that and the risk isn't worth it. The most important thing you can do is keep the pet sinia'd and watch for the mezz break. When it breaks, slow the pet ASAP. Just use abolish disease to remove turgur's once it is charmed.
Chutoi
The guys telling you it can be done with less uber equipment, and groups without XX, etc. are doing you a disservice. Mobs double hit for 990 on the level 65 hards, and they mitigate slow. If you get 3 in camp (not uncommon), can you take that kind of damage while you get them sorted out? No? Don't do hard. That simple.

Nameds on hard hit for 1150 and quad I think, if I'm remembering correctly.

I really don't recommend doing hard without some form of crowd control--bard or chanter. You can get by with a GOOD necro in an undead-type dungeon.

Some of the dungeon types are harder than others. Guks hards are HARD--lots of multiple pulls because you can't target around those sharp corners. ECs are pretty easy on hard level--lots of single pulls and lots of room. Nro isn't too bad, but there are a lot of big rooms where you can get LARGE pulls. Pacify on hard level is not particularly safe--it gets resisted a lot. Mistmoore isn't too bad on hard. Everfrost is hit or miss, depending on which theme you get. Some are hard, some aren't too bad.

I've done some hards with some non-traditional groups. I did a hard with a paladin, warrior, cleric, mage, wizard, and myself (shaman) in mistmoore. We finished with literally 1 minute left. I wouldn't recommend doing that unless you have a STRONG guild group--people used to working together. They're doable if you want a challange, but I really really recommend getting some crowd control.

Have fun with whatever you choose! Forget the win/loss thingie and just enjoy.
