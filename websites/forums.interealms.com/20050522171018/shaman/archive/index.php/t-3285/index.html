Boinshar
Walking around all over the world isn't a good way to find a group, considering 80% of your time is spent between camps. Your best bet is to remain in one dungeon, preferably one you can solo while waiting for a group. Karnors, Seb, Chardok, Skyfire, Velks etc would all work.

Secondly, form your own groups. While soloing, or waiting in a zone, it's inevitable that some people would zone in and LFG. Talk to them, join up, and kill things while you wait to form a full group (full groups are waste of time imo unless you are doing CT or 1 group pog, hate, and fear).

The goal is to never sit around while waiting for groups to magically apear and pick you up, take the initiative by forming one, or just duo with someone. In most zones, a shaman + any other class is more than enough kill things for decent exp.

Irksome
I also suffered badly in finding pickup groups thru most of my 50's.

My answer was:
- form a duo, and add LFGs as they show up. Start killing ANYTHING, and next thing you know you have the best camp in the zone with a full group.

- join a guild, and make it a habit to LFG there. Failing that, offer up where YOU are, and say, 'taking any and all to join me killing X in Y zone'. I do this all the time, and it often results in a flood of tells from everyone else in the guild who ALSO couldn't find a group.

- if all else fails, solo something. I spent quite a bit of 58/59 soloing because of odd play times (late night/early morning infant feedings). Soloing a light blue sarnak outside Chardok is more experience and fun than spending all night running all over Norrath.

Initiative is good. Just don't be selective in your partnering choices, a shaman can effectively duo with ANY class.

From a post I made to another forum:


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

1) Learn to use the "LFG" tag. If you're looking for a group, type "/lfg ON" and then do a "/who all LFG XX YY" (where XX is the lowest level person you're looking for and YY is the highest level person). I can't tell you the number of times that I've run into some REALLY great people by checking the LFG tags that are up and inviting them to group.

2) Be outgoing and learn to search. If you're anti-social or simply shy, you're not gonna be able to find a group very easily. When nobody shows up when I do a "/who all LFG 55 60" I start doing "/who all 50", "/who all 55", "/who all 56" and so on. I then start sending tells to people who aren't in raid zones. If I see a 55 monk in KC, I send him a tell to ask if his group needs a shaman. If I see a 59 Warrior in Cazic Thule, I check to see if the rest of his guild is there, and if not, ask him if a spot in his group will open up anytime soon. At lower levels, learn to frequently check the "who" list to find people of your level range and SEND THEM A BLOODY TELL. This is not an old, crowded server where groups are going to fall into your lap the moment you log on -- you've got to work a little bit for them, but I've NEVER seen a creative and outgoing person at a lack for groups on this server.

3) Camp XP spots. I know a person who was spending hours every time he logged in running from Velks to KC to HS to the Hole to FG to ME and so on... just to find groups. Not a very smart way to search for a group. First of all, if he knew how to search by level and class, he could have located groups in each of these zones. Second of all, many of these zones are pretty accessible -- he could have parked a low-level mule in them to check to see what sort of action was happening at the moment.

Even so.... there will be times when you are having trouble finding a group...

If you're a solo-capable class (bard, nec, monk, druid, shm, wiz, mag, enc, .... the list goes on and on, really) then you should take heart in knowing that all of the golden XP spots are usually uncamped here. I leveled a monk to 37 in a matter of less than 3 days played because it was so EASY to find xp teats to suckle from uninterrupted.
