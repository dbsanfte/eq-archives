I'll bite too . . .

There *is* no comparison between Shaman in EQ1 and EQ2. None. At. All.

Shaman in EQ2 have a well defined role which their given skill set enables them to fulfill properly. We are Priests. Healers. We keep our group alive. Any content.

Are there some things that could stand to be improved? Yes, certainly. But all the classes in EQ2 are still feeling their way around. Everyone is discovering what their particular class brings to the table - and what they don't.

In EQLive, Shaman were a jack of all trades, good at many things, but excellent at none. As the game progressed, that flexibility left Shaman more and more wondering what it was they were supposed to be doing. Even Shaman couldn't always agree what their role was.

Rest assured there is no such ambiguity in EQ2. Shaman are healers, and they are VERY good at it. They still have that utility, you won't start playing your Shaman and be lost, but they are definitely not second class citizens in the healing department any more.

Sudedor
Yes, you are awful early on to notice much difference with Slow, but then again, in EQLive, there really wasn't much noticeable when Drowsy was cast either. We all seem to remember Turgur's effect, it was pronounced. What we don't remember is the 50 levels that preceeded it.

Anyway, the other thing with Slow is that it ONLY affects the actual melee swings. It does NOT affect Combat Arts! This is very important to remember. Slow is a little easier to see in terms of damage prevented AFTER the mob runs out of power.

Lastly, Slow is best used against a single, very powerful target. It is of almost zero worth in a fight against 4 or more targets.

Slow isn't the game breaker it was in EQLive, don't expect it to be. However, because of that, we can expect to be treated more equally as healers.

Bendarin
Just a note on what I have found... Each of the classes of Priests in the game heal in a different way.

Shaman heal by casting a ward on a tank, this ward absorbs melee damage and prevents huge spikes of damage to tank. This allows our so so heals to easily keep up in most situations.

Druids heal by casting regen spells coupled with their healing spells.

Clerics heal by casting a reactive heal on tanks (it activates and heals in response to attacks on the tank) and using the regular heals.

Each priest class does it differently but all are viable. If I had my druthers each group I was in would have 2 healers of different types for tougher encounters, works out very well and no one gets their toes stepped on!
