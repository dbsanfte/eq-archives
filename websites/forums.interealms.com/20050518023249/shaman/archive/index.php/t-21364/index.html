mcroc
As a person and at different stages of the game, I think everyone has different urges to what kind of class they want to play. So taking on this mindset;
If you fancy playing a class that can hide, sneak and is effectively hasslefree then pick a rogue.
If you fancy a class that can feign death and can tank then pick a sk.
If you fancy a class that can port anywhere then choose a druid or Wizard.
If you fancy a class that has a lot of similar spells to shaman but with added melee and offensiveness then choose Beastlord.
If you fancy a class that could be challenging and versatile to play then choose bard.
If you fancy the role of a warrior then choose warrior.
If you fancy being able to rez then consider cleric or paladin.
If you fancy having the versatility of tools for using on the undead choose Necro.
If you fancy having a powerful pet then choose mage, necro or beastlord.
If you fancy playing the role of crowdcontrol, or want to try charming then try bard or enchanter.
Are you looking for a class strong in groupability, soloability, or partner/trioability.
Or do you fancy twinking a class up?- in that case, melees are probably the most twink-friendly.

Personally, I think I played all classes and they had all their advantages/disadvantages. If I am allowed to break up by level, then funwise I enjoyed druid/rogue to mid30s, and shaman/ranger/enchanter to 50s.(although I played shaman to 65-but I found 1-60 the funnest) (Additional Note I levelled up 2 rangers and shaman(s). so they were probably my personal favourites)

Also, personally, I can say what I did not enjoy playing too, I did not enjoy playing tank classes or pure melee. I tried a couple of those classes at level 65 with betabuff and never found their fun, however, some people enjoy them immensely.

ACK! You should definitely use macros. Make a macro for each song that has 2 lines like this:

/stopsong
/cast 1

That will cut your keystrokes in half immediately. Then, if you value laziness > effectiveness, you can cut keystrokes in half again by making a 4-song twist that only takes 2 keystrokes. Here is the setup:

Macro 1-
/stopsong
/pause 30, /cast 1
/stopsong
/cast 2

Macro2-
/stopsong
/pause 30, /cast 3
/stopsong
/cast 4

Now you have a 4-song twist with only 2 keystrokes! Of course, fizzles will make this inefficient, so I use 4 macros normally, and switch to the 2 macros only when fingers tired.
