Chutoi
Actually, there have been parses (sorry, but I lost the link) that show that yes, there is a difference within each slow message. It's most visible in some mobs like Fennin Ro in the plane of fire. He is slightly slowable, but honestly, the difference is like 1 or 2%.

I believe the most complete parse test I remember seeing finally tracked down the determination to a formula that was something like:

(100-(X)) * slow percentage = amount slowed
where X is a mob specific amount, and may be coded differently per mob.

The net effect is that mobs are X% unslowable, and then (for shaman) we multiply 75% times 100-X.

Thus, if a mob is fully slowable, then X = 0, and we have (100-0) * .75 = 75% slowed.

If a mob is coded to be 50% unslowable, then we have (100-50) * .75 = 37.5% slowable.

I haven't seen specific parses per message, but I'd strongly suspect that it's a category.

Fully slowable = 75%
mostly slowable is probably around 50-74%
partially slowable is probably around 26-49%
slightly slowable is probably anything under 25%.

Those are all ballpark figures of course. It's probably easiest to assume a single target for each level--75%, 56%, 37% and 17%. With a few exceptions, those assumptions will allow you to tailor heals as needed (if you have a real healing class, of course)
