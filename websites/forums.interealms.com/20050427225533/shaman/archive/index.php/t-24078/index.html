Korelen
You do have Stoicism, so you can use that for Cannibalization. This is what I did until I was able to purchase Quiescence. Canni V is a life changing event; sometimes it could turn the tide if you are oom, canni v pops, canni, and crit heal to save your dying tank. This happened to me quite a few times :P

I would get level 60 first. Turgurs will slow 75% for you at 60, and after you have a little xp buffer, work on AAs to purchase Canni V.

For places to solo xp: I think you have a few options.

1) Sebilis - I see that you are keyed there. Earn some plat and make some xp.
2) Get your Hollowing Stones Key - you can get xp as well as work on getting goo for epic 1.5 if you are working on it.
3) Chardok - you can work your way towards zone out, slaughtering mobs in your wake.

Gwynfor
I'm not sure it's the most efficient way to do it, but if you're not into min/max'ing I've found it a good comrpomise. Since about 55th (just hit 58th now) I've set AA exp at 50%. By the time I hit 60th I'll have Mental Clarity 3 and be ready for Cann 5. For now, I find that Regen 3 is a real bonus with Cann 4, plus a couple of hp regen items and my Spiritseer neck. When I hit 60 I'll AA grind for a piece to ensure that I have a well rounded character (also working on group leadership and tradeskills). As the ranking shaman in my guild (the guild is strong on clerics and DPSers, ok on casters, but not to good on the tank/slower/all rounder front), I feel it is more important for me to have depth so I can adapt to any situation, whether it be a Grummus raid or helping others with epics/quests.

For soloing, I recommend Natimbi. If you're the cautious type, go north of the camp and pull turtles back to the camp with slow, root them, rot them, and let the pet go to work. I use slow, PE, anathema then cripple, and finish off meleeing to ensure I keep raising my pierce and 1hb skills. When the turtles/stonemites run out, go for a quick dip and grab a couple of crayfish. Squid under the bridge are fun too. Just be sure to keep lots of fish scales handy. I find I don't solo long before I get a souls group, or a group in my new favourite spot, at the top of the stairs behind the souls. Masruqs, hynids, lots of the named versions, and pull nocs from the other side. Just watch for the Cragbeast Queen.. she be a mean one hitting for 4k+ a chomp.

On Xegony I have no problems getting groups. Slowers are always being called for in PoI, PoN, PoV, PoD, and the tanks/DPSers always crave those shammy buffs. I'm oft-times on with my cleric firiend, who is also 58th (we ensure we stay within about one yellow of exp by fiddling the AA exp %). With a cleric/shaman base to a group we swiftly fill the remaining slots with, usually, one tank and 3 DPSers, or a tank, 2 DPSers and a caster. Our best group for LDoNs seems to be something like Shm, Clr, 2 of Bst/Rog/Mnk, 1 of Nec/Enc/Mag/Wiz and 1 of War/Ber/Pal.
