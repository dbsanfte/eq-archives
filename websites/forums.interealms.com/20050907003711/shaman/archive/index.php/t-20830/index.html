To be realistic ebay is a crap shoot. I personally sell a bunch of stuff for the computer store I work at on ebay, usually refurbs or customer returns. We are reputable and back our products but can't say the same for everyone.

NEVER buy anything that is sold AS-IS! Quite a few people put known bad parts on ebay as-is for free money. It sucks

DO buy parts that have a manufacterer's warranty that you can return strait to them. If the processor is retail box it has a 3 year warranty. MSI, Asus motherboards have 3 year warranties and have end-user RMA service, for example.

Reguarding the acution you listed:
1. Does not say CPU is retail. If it is OEM it probably has no warranty at all.
2. ECS does not have an end-user rma process.

So if something goes wrong your screwed!

You really are better off buying a system from a local retailer or a reputable online store like newegg. Yes it will be more expensive but there is always someone to talk to face-to-face if something goes wrong. Most will also give you tips on installing yourself.

For your second question, the low end processors you can get these days are pretty much 2ghz and up. Anything you buy will be plenty fast enough. RAM is really expensive right now so you will probably only want to get 256 or 512, but plan on upgrading to 1gb after the prices drop. Video card you want as fast as you can afford! Plan on $150-250 and get a Radeon 9600xt, FX5900XT or a Radeon 9800(pro).
