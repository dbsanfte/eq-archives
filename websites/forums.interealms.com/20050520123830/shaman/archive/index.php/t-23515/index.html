Breath of Trushar really does make a huge difference, and Daluda's Mending is noticibly better than its predecessors. I can't speak for post-Uqua zones, since that freaking Bartx hasn't died for us yet.

But the point is, YES, shaman took the shaft here, but NOT because they are shaman. They took it because SOE was stupid enough to throw us into a situation we absolutely weren't ready for yet--we hadn't had the opportunity to lvl up to 70, to get the OoW spells, to get the OoW gear. And they added insult to injury by making even the 3 GoD spells very hard to get, and the zones, frankly, too easy to get to, which encouraged us to try stuff that was designed for lvl 70 OoW-geared characters.

Do we have problems doing that? Of course we do. And so do most other classes.

Do I want to see those problems solved? Of course I do. And I should NOT have to buy yet ANOTHER expansion to see them solved. It is insulting that SOE would provide us content that is beyond the horizon of most characters' abilities, and then tell us we have to pay more money to gain the abilities and skills and gear to be able to pass over that horizon.

All that being said: the list of OoW spells and AAs DOES look promising. Its not perfect, by any stretch of the imagination. It won't fix our issue with being unable to be solo healer in high-stress situations (and it especially won't if they keep our best OoW heal as an Ancient spell). But I really really think that SOE has made the decision that shaman are never again going to be able to be solo healers in high-stress situations, and they are going to expect us to either live with that, or walk away. In the meantime, they think they are throwing enough other bones to us to make us an acceptable group member WITHOUT that solo healing ability.

Fixed rate invis is a bone, but I like it nonetheless. Buffs that add specific mitigations is something we've asked for for a long time, so now we get them. Champion will still suck, but tanks WILL want it--and after all, we HAVE asked for a reason for shaman to actually be included in groups, not just botted, and a spell you have to cast every 10 minutes definitely requires you to be present all the time. Many of the other items, while not perfect, are probably as far as Rytan and SOE are willing to go to make us more attractive.

Only time will tell if they are correct in that assessment. For now, I know that I have a harder time getting groups every day, and I look forward to logging on less and less. But I also know that I'm not dead yet, and if nothing else, OoW looks brighter than GoD did a week before IT was released.
