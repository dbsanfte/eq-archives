Pulling:
________________________________________________
I find that, as a shaman, I can pull fairly well. Better than a warrior, but worse than an SK (and much worse than a monk/bard). I often find myself performing this role in pickup groups, or groups without a tank. The main advantages to me pulling are 1) that the mob comes pre-slowed and 2) I have a pet to distract mobs (a la SK pulling)

This will all change once I get my VP AA =)

If there is only one mob, I do the following.

1) Malo the mob and send the pet in (and now that I知 level 60, my malo cannot be resisted! Woohoo!) The malo is not enough to pull the mob away from the pet, so my pet is now getting beaten on.
2) I cast slow on the mob. This is enough to shift the mobs attention from my pet to my frog.
3) I back up to the tank and let him pull away the now slowed mob.
4) Heal myself if I am hurt, then I do something else

If there are two mobs, I do something like the following

1) Get as far away as I can.
2) Send the pet in on mob 1, while I malo mob 2. I try to get both to hit at the same time.
3) Slow mob 2. By this time, pet has taken moderate damage. MT at this point should start wacking at mob 2.
4) Malo and slow mob 1. By the time I知 done with that, Pet is dead/almost dead and mob 2 is off of me.
5) Root-rot mob 1 while group is working on mob 2.
6) Heal myself, and recast pet.


If there are three mobs, I warn group that we may wipe and do the following:

1) Get as far away as I can.
2) Send in pet on mob 1 while I root mob 2. If it resists root, I die. Group wipes. Wheeee!
3) Mob 3 is now beating on me. I slow without malo. If it resists, I知 in trouble, but I usually have time for another slow or a root. If it sticks, I知 sitting pretty.
4) Malo and slow mobs 1 and 2, order depends on how pet is doing. Group may still wipe at this point!
5) Root mob 2, and root-rot mobs 1 and 2 as root breaks. Heal self.
6) If group survives, recast pet.

Okay, I'm not going to rip *anyone* apart and certainly not in general discussion. ;-p However, I do have a couple tips for you.

1) We've already discussed staying too far away from the group in dungeons. Standing away from the mob is a good idea, but standing too far away does 1 of 2 things in many cases. First, it could draw adds, especially in expeditions where re-pops are sometimes unpredictable. Second, it could draw agro anyway and then you have a ping pong situation with you out of range for tank to draw agro or cleric to heal you. Careful standing too far from group in any zone with bad spell pathing.

2) I'm glad you've picked up a tactic for pulling. Expect to use it in pickup groups. Expect not to use it in guild groups.

3) Backup healing. Backup healing is an odd role to play because requires intense concentration, particularly when you are doing other things like slowing/maloing. Remember that your big heal still snares people, so use it sparingly and preferably not at all on the melee classes. Chloroplast might be better to use on them. If the cleric isn't keeping on heals enough that you have to repeatedly chloroplast, you've got a bad cleric or something else is wrong in the group. (You probably have a bad cleric.) I always call my heals and you did a great job watching who I was healing and how fast others were taking damage. The general rule on healing: Heal the person taking the most damage first. If a caster is taking damage, heal them right away and use the fastest heal you've got. You may have noticed that Den and Div both go down pretty fast. When you've got a real tank (warr, sk, pali), remember that the cleric deliberately will not heal them until they are around 40%. If you don't see a heal call when the tank hits 35%, start casting. If you see a CH call and the tank seems to be taking damage faster than that 10 seconds allows, toss 'em a little heal to get them through. I am notorious for taking chances with CH and I love having a good backup healer around just in case I'm wrong. One other thing...if you hear me call DB, that means I've cast Divine Barrier on myself and I *cannot* heal for 25 seconds. I only do this if I've got two mobs pounding on me or if I can't get a root off on the other one.

The more we explore OoW and GoD zones, the more I find myself wishing for a backup healer. These zones are stress-intensive on the healer. Mobs seem to be almost immune to taunt and easily agroed by casters. On top of that, they hit damn fast and damn hard. If I'm tossing quick heals right and left, I'm losing massive amounts of mana. It's great if someone else can pick up some of that for me, especially someone that can regen their own mana.

Last thing...try not to canni with mobs in the camp. It isn't that important that you have a lot of mana all the time. If you are trying to just get enough to toss a heal here or there, that's fine, but if you're cannibalizing for dots or nukes, all you are doing is giving the cleric heart failure!

You're doing a great job! *hugs*

Couple of tips:

Pulling: If you have a 2-pull situation, root one and pull the other (or, better yet, have adruid root 1 as you mal/slow the other). If you have a potential massive multi-pull, summon a lower level pet, and use it to pull. If the pet is a green /con to the mobs, they will not aggro when it starts biting your target mob, so /pet attack followed by /pet back off (repeatedly) will single pull. However, if pet dies, you get the whole lot, so buff pet with SoW (or shrew) and any hp buffs (at 65, use Quiesence for super safe pulls)
