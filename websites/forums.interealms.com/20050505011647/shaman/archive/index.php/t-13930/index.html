I've been 2-boxing for a few years now and I've tried Shaman/Monk, Shaman/Ranger, Shaman/SK to some extent or another. By far, the most effective combo I've found is Shaman/SK. The other melee choices I mentioned are fine choices - and would work just fine in most cases. The difference between those choices and SK are that the SK has superior tanking ability (crucial if you ever plan to duo in PoP Tier 2), a decent FD, the darkness line for snaring (crucial for runners - and very handy for keeping agro), and some self-healing ability with their procs and lifetaps. Poguk
Not speaking in a PoP-tanking sense, but I've found the ranger/shaman combo to be pretty good. I also have a monk the same level as my shaman, but it's nowhere near as fun, and the ranger does practically the same damage, while providing the same tanking, but way more utility. I've all but retired the monk.

Ranger has good DPS, with his epic and decent main-hander. He has nukes/DoTs/Damage shield to compliment his damage. Snare is just awesome... keeps running mobs from running, and keeps the fight in a close territory. Snare makes dungeon crawls much easier. Tracking is great for locating named mobs or just scouting the area out to see what you're up against. Ranger gets some decent buffs that stack with shaman buffs. In outdoor zones and dungeons, rangers can single-pull easily (in fact, easier than FD splitting) with Harmony. He can spot heal (300-pt heal) and root adds. If my shaman is getting beat on, a ranger can grab agro real quick with snare/taunt/damage/root.

On the other hand, the shaman compliments the ranger's damage with a decent pet and good DoTs. Shaman buffs are right up a ranger's alley, and they do need buffs. The biggest thing to the ranger that a shaman can provide is slow and debuff, as even a ranger can tank most slowed mobs if he has decent gear. Together with slow and the shaman's decent heals, it keeps the ranger happy.

With a ranger/shaman combo, you got:

- Slow (Turgur's)
- melee/resist debuffs (Cripple/Malosi)
- Good enough healing (between the both of them)
- Tracking
- Snare
- DPS
- Root
- Haste
- Damage shields
- Pet (throw some weapons on a shaman pet and help his DPS)
- Regen
- DoTs
- Decent buffs

It's just a great system: get buffed up, ranger single-pulls something and snares it, shaman slows/malosi's/cripples, send pet in, DoT, and let ranger and pet and DoT eat up the mob. Keep health topped off, and do it again. If you got KEI on both toons, there's almost no down-time. If you don't have KEI it's not a big deal --- shaman canni's and down-time is still not bad at all.

I have considered a SK combo, and I think this might be really good for high-end content once you're able to get your SK a Windblade or equivalent, but until then... the SK is gonna lack in DPS... a ranger rips through stuff pretty damn quick, but I'd need a serious weapon for a SK to keep up. No question that a SK can tank better, but with slow, things are very doable with a ranger. If you start getting into high-end content that is untankable by a ranger, grab a cleric and plate-tank and you're set.

Keep in mind that a duo is meant to be efficient --- but they just aren't built to handle extremely tough fights... there will always be situations where a bigger group with a cleric, enchanter, plate-tank, DPS person will do a much better job than a duo: but there are many situations where a good duo can do some big stuff if you know what you're doin'. There isn't a duo out there that can cover every angle.

I do like the necro/mage, necro/rogue, necro/shaman --- necros make good partners simply 'cause of fear-kiting... but there are many situations where fear-kiting isn't feasible. The ability to stand in one tight spot and take stuff down via a tank/healer combo is pretty valuable. If you're looking outside the tank/healer duo... then just make a necro and be all-in-one hehe.
