Willow, I believe the recommended requirements for DAoC are the same as the minimum requirements for EQ-SOL. However, given that Camelot has a PvP aspect that can be very sensitive to lag, I'd consider the hardware requirements about equal. (Getting killed by a mob b/c you lagged is not fun; getting killed by an enemy PC b/c you lagged will make you pound your head on your keyboard.)

In terms of content, DAoC is far ahead of where EQ was 2 months after release, but behind where EQ is today, especially in high-end content. Considering that the "average" casual Camelotian is only now approaching 30th level, that's not a big issue.

In terms of stability, DAoC was the smoothest release I have ever seen. It was more stable 1 week after release than EQ is today. It typically does take VI a few weeks to shake the bugs out after releasing new content, whereas Mythic does that on their Test server before rolling it out.

I'll be getting SOL for Christmas, and am certainly looking forward to seeing everyone again, but I don't think SOL will bring me back to EQ going forward.
