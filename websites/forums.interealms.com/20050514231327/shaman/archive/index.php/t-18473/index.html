Grafsha McDaughts
All pet classes take an xp hit - and that is part of the game that makes if fun for most.

Don't look at it as you fighting a mob with your pet helping you. Rather, think of it as you fighting your pet for a share of the xp from the mob the pet is fighting. It's like a race - you and your pet are racing.

To win the race you need to do more damage than your pet and you have to absolutely make sure the mob is always trying to get to you (refered to as aggro control). This means you have the xp edge and as long as you keep this xp edge you will get the xp.

If your pet ever ... EVER ... takes aggro away from you then the pet will take most of the xp away. Mages are the best at beating their pets with clerics and wizards (in their 50's when they finally get their pets) next. For shaman it isn't that big a deal because after 55-58 if your pet ever steals aggro from you the mob usually kills it in 2 or 3 hits.

Best advice I can give you, if you want to play a pet class, is to make a cleric. Their warrior pets can be pretty cool but you have to give them random drops and cash to keep them summoned.

So shammys can snare really well? I need to get clear on this... Can they snare? If so, how good? Can they root? Is so how good? Compare it to something.
Shaman can not snare. Snare reduces how quickly a mob moves (Anti-SoW).

We have slow, which increases the decreases the number of swings a mob makes over a given period of time.

We can root, but I wouldn't say root varies enough between classes for it to be a major reason to not pick one. Our best root is an AA skill - Virulent Paralysis. It's disease resistable (as opposed to magic) and has a knockback effect on it. This makes it handy for "parking" a mob that's hitting you. As far as I know, no other class has a knockback effect on their root and they are all tied to magic resists. Druid root has a DD component tied to it. Other than that, root is pretty much root - the only difference between each variant is duration and possibly resists.

Hope that helps.

Toktuk Wartalon
whitch class is more mana effeciant? Like, more good spells for how much mana it costs... shammy or necro, also are they good for regaining mana? Like canni and other spells for necro?
Having never played a Necro, I couldn't comment on their mana efficiency. However, we both have ways of converting HP to mana and then regaining the HP back.

Necromancers have Skeleton/Lich Form, which passively converts their HP to Mana. You only have to cast the spell once for it to work. They can regain HP by Lifetapping mobs. The downside is that the mana regen from their form does not stack with KEI.

Shaman have Cannibalize, which converts a set amount of HP to Mana instantly (more or less). Until you get the Cannibalization AA, it's done in very small doeses, so you'll find yourself tapping your Cann key a lot to regain mana. There's even a little dance you do to make it work more efficiently which I absolutely do not feel like going over. Just do a forum search for Canni Dance. We can obviously regain our lost HP through Regen spells and heals. Our Heal over Time spells are extremely mana efficient. Also, because our HP/Mana conversion is not passive, it does stack with KEI.

-Tok
