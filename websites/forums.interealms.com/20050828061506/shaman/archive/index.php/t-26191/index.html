In a neverending effort to squeeze some usefulness out of the three gimpadins in my crew, we recently made the decision to remove me (L52 paladin) from MT role and make me main healer. Our shadow priest, already irritated and burned out at having to heal to begin with, now remains in shadowform 100% of the time. I admit that I was a little worried at first given my paladin's low mana pool and inefficient heals.

What I've begun doing is picking up as much druid gear (leather seems to work best for stats, not sure why) as I can and replacing my plate. I still have a bunch of slots left to convert and I'm in the mid 3000's for mana (priest runs 5.5k mana in comparison). Fortunately it's possible to pick up all the worthwhile healing talents as a paladin while remaining dps spec'd, so that's what I'm doing. We started testing this in Sunken Temple the last few runs and it works flawlessly. Here's a typical fight:

L52 paladin MT runs into pack of mobs, fires off AE. He's immediately getting hammered. I proc my talent (divine favor) and hit him with a mana free crit heal which I can do every 2 min (basically every fight)- that acts as a free cheal early in the fight when he's in the most danger. It is also zero aggro due to paladin hardcoded low heal aggro- I tried and tried but could not get heal aggro, ever.

Anyways, I then run into melee, pick a random mob and fire off seal of wisdom and start swinging. If it's an elite I judge wisdom and put seal of wisdom back up. So now if I'm not healing I'm proc'ing mana regen through the following, all of which stack:
- judgment of wisdom (46 mana/proc)
- seal of wisdom (71 mana/proc)
- fist of stone weapon proc (50 mana/proc)

If you've never seen this it's insanely good mana regen that I can use while benefiting from the 5 sec casting rule as my MR shield and blessing of wisdom continue to pump up my mana as well. So long as I don't have to remain in spam heal mode I can destroy the healing capacity of our priest, strictly because I regen mana at an insane rate. How fast? Faster than drinking the best water- on some fights I was down to 1/2 mana and finished just shy of full, while healing. I drink about 1/3 as much as the priest did when he healed.

So anyways, paladins still pretty much suck but at least I've found my role: healer. With the rest of the int gear I'm buying I should be able to hit the magical 4k mana pool mark. I'm considering respeccing to pick up the +10% mana talent, may not be a bad idea given that my dps is insignicant.

Given the upcoming paladin dps nerf (again) and our shadow priest's insane dps, I'm just happy we've found a temporary solution until our dps alts catch up.
