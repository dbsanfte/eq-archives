Well, my main dual box setup is Shaman/Monk, but I think I know why most shamans prefer SKs to pallies as partners.

Admittedly, paladins have a few advantages compared to SKs. For starters, you always have a rez if you die in an out of the way place. Rez-on-demand is not to be underestimated, but personally I prefer a standalone level 49 rezbot Cleric. Another pally advantage is decent (and stackable) HP buffing and decent healing. So far, so good.

On to the "wash" category. Agro-wise, Paladins may be a bit better, but both can keep agro with low level mana efficient spells. Stuns are nice against casters and especially gaters, but higher level mobs are often unstunnable, so it's not quite as big a deal at 65. Finally, Paladins can root park, but Shamans can do it already, so there is little extra functionality there.

Now, what does a SK bring to the table that a pally doesn't?

First, FD and all that comes with it - pulling, ability to lose agro on demand, easier travel (especially with their invis and IVU spells), etc. After playing my Monk for 3.5 years, the many uses of FD are hard to live without, although, of course, SKs' FD is not as good as Monks'. When that mob gates on you in the middle of Droga and the Shaman hits Gate in a hurry, would you prefer to have a SK who could quickly FD or a Paladin who could rez himself after the fact?

Second, Snare. Sure, my Inny-worshipping Troll Shaman has his Snare necklace, but the effect lasts just over half a minute and has to be carefully timed and monitored. Certainly not something that I want to do as a Shaman when there are so many other things that I could be doing. On the other hand, a SK can "snare and forget" - plus it helps him with agro. Oh yes, and not all Shamans have even ghetto Snare :)

Third, Summon corpse. If I were to die at the Royals in Chardok, it would do me no good to be able to rez myself if I could't get to the corpse. SC on demand makes life much more worry-free when it comes to dungeon crawling.

Fourth, DPS. A Bazaar equipped SK can do respectable damage with melee, spells and pet. A Bazaar equipped Paladin will lag far behind.

Finally, pet pulling may get nerfed at some point, but for now it is a great thing, especialy when you can cast Shrew on the pet.

And as an added bonus, most SKs can easily get that nice Overthere Gate hammer. Admittedly, Paladins can get one too (Malo + Tash + Puppet Strings), but it's a pain. Besides, getting that proc to work at the last moment and appearing at the OT docks with 3% HP and OOM only to be killed by a greenie skelly there is simply embarrassing :)

I think the answer to your question is whether or not you're 2-boxing (2 accounts, 1 person) or If you're going to duoing with a friend.

When doing with a friend I have found it's a toss up between a druid and a beastlord. Druids are great because you get Mana Regen, travel (ports, etc..), and good kiting skills. Beastlords are good because you get lesser mana regen, a tank (of sorts) and fantastic DPS.

If you're 2boxing, I think it depends on whether or not you want to "bot" the Shaman or the other class. When botting the Shaman I think an Enchanter is one of the best classes. Pacify, Mana Regen, Charm, Haste, etc.... just can't be beat. If you're planning on botting the Shaman I think that a Shadow Knight is an excellent choice. Snare, FD pulling, Life/mana taps, etc...

Personally I 4box a Shaman, Pally, Enchanter and Magician. Mages are great to bot and with the recent changes their pets are awesome once you get a Diamond Prism of Companionship.

I two-box my shaman/sk combo quite often, and agree with everything that Scalia said on Paladins versus SKs with a few caveats. SK DPS is greater than a Paladin's, but it is also really no greater than Warrior DPS (except at bazaar level where SK > War DPS), so I'm not sure if I'd call it respectable. And as for Paladin buffs, while they will certainly max your HP higher (and much better healing for when it hits the fan), those extra HP are of limited use wth Shaman healing. What will help though, is the fact that the SK will have about 100 more AC on average over the Paladin. The SK can add about FT9 on average to the shaman mana regen rate through ZB, and can lich for KEI-like mana regen for himself, which means that both classes have some nice methods for increasing mana regen and going KEI-less is less painfull. And while a rez is nice, EXP loss is no big deal, just go 10%/90%AA until you max your EXP again. And, of course, GY zones means it much easier to buy a rez or two if you really want (while negating summon corpse too, so this is arguably a wash). The above mostly pertains to level 65 in retrospect.

Snare and FD were the primary reasons for pairing a SK with my shaman.
