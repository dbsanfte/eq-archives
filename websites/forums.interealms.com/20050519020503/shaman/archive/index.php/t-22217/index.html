Yeah, it was March 15/16, '99, if you actually count that, since you couldn't really play until a few weeks later. I also played in beta 4, and I remember that on the first day of launch of that phase, the servers crashed somethin' awful. I bought the game and loaded up my first character on launch day. I didn't make my beloved shaman until the conception of the Firiona Vie server.

I was 15 when it launched so like, EQ dominated a good portion of my adolescence, which probably explains why I didn't really date at all until a couple years later... curse you Norrath...

And personally, I am glad they never implemented housing. Anyone who played pre-tent destruction UO knows what I'm talking about.

"Let's go adventure!!!" ... group then spends its time weaving its way through alleys of houses all the way until the dungeon, where 10 tents block its entrance. Yay..... how immersive... almost as fun as someone using the hide skill outside your house till you came out, then running in behind you and taking your stuff.

The only type of housing I support is instanced houses or very very large and expensive housings with daily UPKEEP, allowable in certain zones for high guilds, or, of course, buildings for pvp siege games, since they come and go.

Badly implemented housing can ruin a game. When they changed house placement rules to five tiles in front and behind of an existing house, I spent 10 hours combing the entire FACE of the UO world (Otaria, was it?) and found ONE spot where I WOULD have been able to set up a house if a tiny little mushroom wasn't in the way on the fifth tile out. I was ticked. But EVERY last INCH of the planet was covered in housing.
