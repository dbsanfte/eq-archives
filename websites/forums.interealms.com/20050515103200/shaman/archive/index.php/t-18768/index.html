Jinheim
Gear doesn't save you from stupidity. An "average" Beastlord cures his pet and dispells your DS. He will dispell your Q as well, if he's remotely smart(If you are prepared, you can get around this sometimes though).

First off, when I am about to duel someone, I put up about 7 crap buffs before I put up real buffs, and DS is always the last buff I give myself, because it is often the most important. In addition to that, I carry 3 instaclicks in to duels. Good luck dispelling my quiescence, let alone my DS.

As for the curing, there is a way around that as well. In my experience, pet classes' pet heals take the first disease buff away, so if you have, for instance, three debuffs with disease counters and turgur's is the third, it will take three casts to cure it.

So, when I duel a beastlord, his pet's buff list looks something like this:

1) Spirit Of Relic
2) Whatever their haste buff is
3) Malos
4) Malicious Decay
5) Pox of Bertoxx
6) Turgur's Insects

When he cures his pet, it will remove malicious decay, which I just reapply. It goes back in to the 4th buff slot and will be the first debuff to be removed next time he cures, as well. If I am unable to reapply right away and he gets a second cure in before I can refresh, it takes pox, which I also just reapply (rosrak's legs).

If this isn't working for you, though, just blind the thing - he can't cure that.
