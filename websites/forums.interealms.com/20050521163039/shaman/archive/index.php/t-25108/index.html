Ok, I’ll share what I usually do in a group, and see if anyone can learn from it, or has some techniques that work better for them.

My group makeup is usually Shaman/Mage/Warrior/Predator/Shaman/Predator.

On regular pulls (Blue or better mobs) I’ll slow if it’s a named, otherwise I start with a Spectral Ward on the MT before the pull. I get agro if I do it after the encounter has engaged.

If anyone else (Including me) pulls agro after we engage I’ll fire off the group ward and assist the main tank and whack at the main mob with my hammer.

If it’s a group of blue mobs I’ll toss some contagion on each mob and re-ward as needed for the main tank. I don’t know the ward has faded until I see the main tank take a hit or two, so after about 3 wards I toss in an Adept 1 Minor Heal and keep whacking.

If the group takes damage beyond what my group ward can heal then I’ll toss another group ward followed by a group heal. (Which usually pulls the mobs off everyone and onto me) so I’ll toss a Spectral Ward on myself and allow the MT to get the agro back.

By this time the first mob should be dead and the rest are at 1-2 bubbles from the AE’s.

I’ve noticed that Minor Healing Adept 1 works better and faster than Minor Arch Healing Apprentice 3, so I rarely use that.

When triggering HO’s I’ll use Contagion if the mob is above 60%, or Smite Adept 1 (Over the Winter nuke Apprentice 3) to trigger my part, unless it’s a heal that’s needed, in which case I toss a Minor Healing.

For maintained buffs I keep up Courage, Spirit of the Bull, Auspice on the main tank and Spiritual Seal. (If the other Shaman is with us I’ll have her cover Courage and Spiritual Seal and Auspice myself since I tank inadvertently sometimes.)

My group experience has been quite varied, so I'll start with the duo setup I can do in my sleep now. :) I regularly duo with a Monk we fight all sorts of mob arrangements, so I'll go over each.

Single mob, normal difficulty (whites, yellows, and oranges) - She engages mob, I jump behind the mob and start melee, Contagion, then Spectral Ward. Once Spectral Ward hits she does a solo HO, then I do my solo HO. By then Contagion is about to wear off so I refresh it and reapply ward if need be. She does another solo HO and the mob is usually dead by then as the second Contagion is just wearing off.

Single mob, ^^difficulty (greens and blues, whites can be a gamble) - Ward before inc, let that one tick go for power regen, then she engages and I jump behind to melee. I immediately Wailing Haze, Contagion, Weakness, then check Ward. If it's good, she solo HO's, then depending on how the fight is going I might do an HO, otherwise I just keep Contagion and Ward up as she gets as many HO's in as possible. Some blue^^'s can take a lot of mana and the white^^'s certainly do, so I don't like to spend much of it on anything but Wards, cures, debuffs, and heals.

2+ mobs, normal or ^ difficulty (have yet to take on 2+ of ^^) - Basically the same as one mob except I'll ward before the pull and instead of my HO I work in a Wailing Haze on the additional mob(s).

Grouping though, is a whole different story.

Single healer, Grouping, normal difficulty mobs - Basically the same as how I duo. I tend to ward after the tank takes a little damage to use some of his hitpoints and mitigation to establish aggro. I actually have yet to use either group spell as my group members just never take damage more than one person at a time. I don't know if that's lucky or what. :)

Single healer, Grouping, ^^ mobs (whites and yellows, I have only healed an orange^^ in an emergency situation and the downtime was terrible) - I will ward before pull then Wailing Haze and Weakness almost right after engagement. By then the ward is probably gone so I'll reward, curing and healing as necessary but saving my power for debuffs, heals, cures, and wards.

Duo healer, Grouping, ^^ mobs (whites, yellows, oranges) - I make sure to work together with the other healer. If it's a Shaman one will ward and debuff while the other covers direct heals, or one will debuff and both just direct heal. Orange^^ mobs 20+ are pretty nasty. :) I haven't grouped with a druid yet, but when grouped with a cleric we decided that I would debuff, both direct heal, and he would reactive heal.

My sustained buffs currently are bearform, seal, bull, courage, and badger. I ditched Auspice when I got bearform since auspice really isn't very good for 2 concentration points, especially single target only! I suggest getting app3 and adept versions of your buffs as they get really good. :)

Personally, I use Contagion almost every fight. It's used in triggering our HO, and it's more effective (for me) in doing so because it's got a quicker casting time AND a lower mana cost. It usually will outdamage Smite too, even if it doesn't get to completion.

I always ward *before* the fight if I can train (heh heh) my pullers to wait for it =p The ward aggro is just too crazy, and I'm pretty careful about managing it. I find that it's also much more effective to pepper my wards with heals; using ward as the primary heal is just way too mana-intensive for me. I also rarely use the group ward for that reason as well, unless the situation calls for it. Same goes for group heal; only when clearly needed. That's just me though!

I usually ward prior to the fight, slap a slow on when the mob is around 85% or so to keep aggro on the MT. If it's a named or a tough orange/red^^, ward might be down pretty quick, even right after the slow. I might slap another ward on the MT so I can re-slow if resisted, or follow up with delerium/weakness.

Depending on the difficulty of the fight I'll keep the mob dot'd whenever possible, while using Cont to trigger HOs. Stack Cold Fire line if the damage is needed.

Fire off a ward + heal when needed and it's all good!

I found the class to be an unbelievable powerhouse of versatility in beta, especially in the later levels when the class continues to flesh out even more. Since some spell lines start as late as 29, there's a lot more to come. Group slow, the Prophetic Guard/Shield line (another +1 conc group buff to fill that empty bubble, woo!), the pet at 45, Avatar at 47... by the end of the line I had 3 hotbars of assorted offensive/utility spells, and 4 or 5 different mastery skills I could use to keep me busy during battle depending on what was needed. Combat is *so* fun and engaging for me.
