Zhahz
I believe it's 'high' diff, not 'hard', not that it matters. It affects the level of critters you face based on your groups average level, and it all breaks down on ranges by average group level. Selecting high diff bumps up your adventure by one grouping/bump which is approximately 5 levels.

So, if you're lower level you want to be careful selecting high diff because if your average level happens to be very close to the critter levels for normal diff you can easily get overwhelmed. Conversely, if your average group level is at the high end of the range and you're almost to the next cutoff point then you really need to do high diff for decent xp and loot.

For example, at higher levels the cutoffs are ave group level of 58 then 63. If you have a group with an average level of say, 62.5, and did a normal diff you'd face critters level 54-56, which are extremely easy for your group. If you select high diff you get critters level 59-61, which is more of a challenge and better xp and loot.

Of course, if you uber equipped, all 65's, and have 100's of AA then you'd want to do high diff because normal would be completely trivial. I have mediocre gear and don't even have canniV yet and I consider normal diff at 65 to be easy unless you have a really horrible group (rarely has happened to me in the 60's).

The original post was by a 59, I believe. If you had an average group level of 59 and did high diff you'd face level 59-61 critters - and would be facing a good number of white and yellow cons (for the 59's and lower, at least) - which could be rough unless you're in a strong group.
