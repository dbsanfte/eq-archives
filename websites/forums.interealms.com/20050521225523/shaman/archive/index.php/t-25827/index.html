Tugela
I dont think there is any reason to delay the release of DoN, unless it wasn't finished, and apparently thats not the case. I know that I personally basically had done most of the single group content in OoW about 2-3 months ago. Most of my online time these days is spent raiding, and there is still a lot of stuff to do there. I expect a fair number of other people are in the same boat, and DoN should provide them with something to do outside of raids, particularly for those folk who dont raid (who also get the incentive of a new round of potential gear upgrades). Delaying the release of the expansion by 6 months would be extremely unhealthy for the game IMO.

In some ways DoN has been likened to LDoN, which is not necessarily a bad thing, if it has been implemented right. The problem with LDoN was that the content itself was very sparse. There were a lot of dungeons, but they all looked the same, and the missions were all the same, so that after a few weeks or less you had essentially done pretty much all of the content, even without trying very hard. The only reason people kept on doing them was to earn points to get gear. Once people could readily get better gear else where with the release of OoW, the number of people actually doing LDoN instantly dropped to allmost zero. There was little other reason to do them, besides the charm aug, and the rate at which you can upgrade the aug is prohibitive in terms of reward v effort. They could have put content in LDoN, by having complex storyline driven shawl type quests running through a series of dungeons, or by having real bosses or minis of differing degrees of difficulty to match the state of development of different toons, and have real rewards matching the degree of difficulty. But, they didnt do that. Of all the expansion releases, I would rate LDoN at the bottom because of the lack of content. Hopefully they have learned from LDoNs mistakes, and that the content is present in the DoN system, or it is going to suffer the same fate.
