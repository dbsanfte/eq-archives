-Class 2 Items: These are the best and worst items. Best in the sense of reward to effort, worst in the sense of being poorly thought out. These items drop off of "named" mobs in dungeons. Most over 50 hitpoints/mana, some with focus or special effects and riddled with stats and resistances. The problem with these items is not their statistics but drop rates. And it's a big problem. The number of named mobs on a map varies widely, and the average I have seen is about 2 on a typical assigned adventure, and 4 if the entire dungeon is torn through. That means one can expect 3 pieces of very decent loot per 90 minute run. 30 minutes per piece of loot.

Second problem with these items is they are not NODROP. The vast majority are droppable, meaning sellable. Meaning inflation. Meaning disruption of economy.

-Class 3 Items: I have little experience with these items. They drop in LDoN raids. I do not know how difficult they are to obtain but I do know they have incredible stats on them.

I don't think this is how inflation works. Inflation is an increase in prices or a decrease in purchasing power of currency. Inflation occurs when there is a lot of puchasing power (i.e. cash) and no increase in the supply of goods available. Vendors will set their prices higher because demand will be up due to more disposible cash. Now, a lot of platinum drops in these dungeons (200pp per person split for an hour is pretty nice for a level 60-65 group), but it's nothing to change the economy of the game. I can make more money per hour by soloing for tradeskill drops in EPs. If you say this fantastic loot drop commonly off named dungeon mobs, then there should be an abundance of them. An increase in the amount of goods does not lead to inflation unless the amount of purchasing power increases faster. (Note: I've only done two adventures so far and the dungeon boss dropped nothing but platinum or research words.)

In general, when expansions with new items come out, people try to "cash in" on other players' stupidity by charging outrageous prices for gear that is similar to items already out there that sell for a fraction of the price. The EQ economy will be fairly unaffected by LDoN after the "oh wow, shiny, new things" phase wears off.

To get stuff that is VT quality or better you have to do a lot of adventures.
Math time:

1492 for VT quality item with say 3 slots in it.
1150? for each of 3 +40 HP or +40 mana augments or a FT augment or something
= 4942 points

51 points for a victory on normal.

You are looking at doing 97 adventures in order to get a super high end item.

In comparison:
You can get a VT key completed in about 2 days of hard work.
Side effect of VT key is a good ring that is worth the effort in itself really.

Now, once your guild is keyed, it takes about 7 hours to clear 10 named in VT.
Include travel time to VT and you are looking at about 9 hours start to finish.
In that 9 hours, you will get about 30 items to drop.
If you have a raid of say 60 people, that means you have to raid VT twice on average for every person on the raid to get an item.
So you are looking at about 18 hours of work for a new item if you raid VT.

The average adventure will take about 90 minutes with travel time if you are really cruising through all your adventures and finding reps quickly.
1.5 hours * 97 adventures = 145.5 hours of work to get a VT quality item.

If your guild raids VT, you'll have to work about 8 times less for a single piece of gear to upgrade.

Why LDoN is really nice is you can upgrade a specific slot that you haven't seen an upgrade for. You can pick and choose all your gear upgrades.
It's also nice in that you can eventually get an upgrade for yourself through xping. It may take a hell of a lot longer to obtain than by raiding, but you'll eventually get it.
