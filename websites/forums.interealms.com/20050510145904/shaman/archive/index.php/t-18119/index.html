Good stuff above.
You'll have plenty of cause to swing by some of the old (not PoP) planes while working on those epics, which can also provide some halfway decent armor - about on scale with thurg, for the most part, and you don't need faction or gems. Don't overlook tradeskills, either. One or two high-end tradeskillers can re-outfit half a guild in tae ew alone (might not be quite there, yet) -- which (at least for shamans) is better or on par with the armor you'd be getting in HoT.

I think more important than WHAT you kill at this point is going to be HOW you organize it. The most important thing is to keep people moving, killing, and progressing. For that you need scouts, transportation, and discipline. Finding a target isn't as important as being able to get to it before someone else. And neither of those mean anything if your people don't show up on time, or get lost, can't find their way, get killed en route...

Happily for you, these are things you can work on without being in game. Make sure that your officers, at least, have updated in-game maps. Delegate - and make sure that no one person is responsible for scouting, and transportation, etc.

And for the love of god, have a well-outlined and strict loot policy going in, or fights over it will tear your guild apart.

Hrm, also - if you can convince your guildmates to avoid exp grinding in the planes, get them to places like Sebilis crypt where they can be adding spells to the guild bank. If you're using DKP you can offer them in return for quality guildbank submissions.

One important suggestion for the beginning of a raid guild: Try to find a dedicated main tank or two, and start equipping them. Most Velios and early Luclin fights are straight forward CH the MT fights, and the more HP and better aggro weapon the easier these fights get. An ogre in the full human RZ cultural set with a copper hammer of striking is probably a good (and cheap to get) start.
After you got the basic working of pulling and complete heal chains down, you start farming Vindi for the BPs, working your way up to the statue.
You then start looking at the first brood dragons, Zlandi and Klandi being the easiest. This introduces you to AoE fights where you have to split your healing between MT and the rest of your raid. Those techniques will last you a long time, until you get to the "tricky" Luclin mobs, IV, THO etc that have special abilities you have to learn to deal with.
My tip on beginner raiding - make sure you announce raids well in advance - but don't ask. If you ask "when", you won't raid, ever, there is always someone who can't make it. Schedule regular raids, maybe on a 4 or 5 day rotation so that you don't exclude someone who always works the "raid day".
Also, make clear from the beginning what the loot rules are. If you anticipate a limited participation of your guildmembers (30 hardcore vs. 150 members) I suggest against full random, nothing upsets your hardcore raiders more then a "visitor" walking off with that prime loot on a lucky role to never be seen again. And it will be your hardcore raiders that provide the progress for the guild as a whole. DKP, random for the top x in your merit list, straight officer award, there are tons of models our there.
Good luck on your journey, it can be a lot of fun.

The other classes armor in there isn't half bad either. When you get to high 50s in UP you can start to farm Fire/Ice, both of which drop the bracelets that everyone loves so much, and some ok weapons and staffs for casters. This is doable with 2 groups of high 50s, one even if you have a good slower and cleric. When you get to high 50s its possible to move into HoT if you think you have a large enough raid force. We've now moved into HoT with about 2 groups of us, average lvl 60, and thats where we are now. We're now gearing up to make our first attempts at Vindi. We still go back to Kael whenever we get new members, its the perfect zone to learn raid tactics that you'll use forever. HoT is also good as you'll learn to deal with AE mobs.

So many people nowadays want to skip these Velious raid zones, they see the so called uberness of the EP and want to get there asap, I don't think they realize the immense value in learning raid tactics that your classic "velious progression" gives you. I'd recommend getting started doing that type of thing, sprinkle some of the PoP mobs into it where you can and you're able, and eventually (I hope my guild does :) ) you'll get there. I think the important thing is to set yourself up to be successful when you finally do get there, not just to rush into it as fast as you can.
