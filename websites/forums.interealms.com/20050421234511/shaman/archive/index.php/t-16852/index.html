I just came back to EQ recently after a two year break. I noticed the new /memspellset command to mem a set of spells, but I cant find anything that tells how to actually name a set of spells so that they can be memed. If anyone could point me in the right direction I would appreciate it.
deria
Step 1: Memorize all the spells you want to be in your new spellset.

Step 2: Right-click the spellbook icon (but button you would normally left-click to opent he spell book).

Step 3: Click "Save spell set".

Step 4: Enter the name of the new spell set.

To use /memspellset, use "/memspellset name".

You could also use a number for the /memspellset command, but then everything gets crazy if the order of your sets changes.
Ssti
You can also use: "/mem name" for similar results

or

/mem 1 - 10

You have 10 spellsets max. Here's an example of how I have mine set up. I use letters a - j to determine the order.

(Names are exactly as is.)

"a Standard" (slow,malos,FA,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Dot)
"b Healer" (slow,malos,Nadox,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Krag Heal)
"c Disease Slow" (slow,malos,Disease Slow,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,malicious Decay)
"d Patch Buffage" (slow,malos,singe Boar,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Single Focus)
"e Raid Buffage" (slow,malos,Group Boar,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Group Focus)
"f Invis" (slow,malos,Invis,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc, Group Acumen)
"g Bear" (slow,malos,Bear Form,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Group Shrink)
"h Dogdog" (slow,malos,DogDog,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Single Shrink)
"i Dottage Disease" (slow,malos,Disease dot,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Disease dot)
"j Dottage Poison" (slow,Curse Dot,Ancient bane of Nife,canni 4,root,Tnarg Heal,Quiesc,Blood of Saryne)

This lets me switch modes on the fly quite fast using /mem 1 - 10 since it usually means 2 spells will switch at a time and similar spells from every mode will allways be in the same slots therefore don't unmem. Doing it this way also means you're never vulnerable because some important spell is unmemmed.

Typically when I get somewhere and set up shop. This happens very fast.

/mem 7 (Bear mode) cast bear/shrink group.
/mem 5 (Raid buffage) Buff group/mgb if applicable.
/mem 8 (Dogdog) Cast dog, shrink dog.
/mem 4 (Patch buffage) Focus dog.
/mem 1 (Standard) Gtg, occasionally switch to patch buffage to rebuff the dead and late comers.

If I'm Root/dotting

/mem 3 (disease slow) Pull with malos, Root mob,Slow Mob,debuff disease resists.
/mem 9 (Dottage disease) Implement Disease dots, reroot as necessary)
/mem 10 (Dottage Poison) Dot the hell out of the mob, it'll die before disease dots wear off and you can keep 4-5 dots going on the mob at a time. (I've been doing this in plane of Fire. Works very well)

If running with group somewhere.

/mem 6 (invis) Group acumen for see invis. Invis melees+you, RUN FOREST RUN.

Depending on actual situation I may modify a spellset to accomodate a particular need (reSaving a spellset with the same name overwrites it). Other spells are memmed as needed using the popup spell lists. Your mileage may vary. I'd be curious to know how others have adapted to spellsets if anyone else has any suggestions or examples.

weissblau
UnderToad's tip is REALLY USEFUL, please note!

I'll restate it with punctuation and capitals so hopefully some more people will understand.

If you keep a spell slot empty when you save a spellset, that slot will be left alone when you load that spellset.

That means you can have your Slow - Root - Cannibalize - Heal combo that never changes, and swap out the other four slots. Create a spellset called "Base" with the four biggies (or whatever you consider your biggies) and then a series of spellsets for other purposes.

Only way you can save spell sets with a custom UI is to mem the spells, switch to default UI, save using the book icon, then reload your custom UI. Thankfully you only have to do that once per spellset (or you can switch and do as many as you want before switching back so you only have to do it once).
