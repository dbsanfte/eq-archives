With LDoNs, some people feel so confident in their ability that the content is trivial to them. It sounds like whoewver was pulling had that attitude. While in a group that works well together (and in a RARE pickup group), pulling that way can speed things up provided: 1. everyone knows their class well, and 2. everyone trusts the others to play instinctively for the benefit of the group. Secondary tanks who steadfastly assist the MT while the healer is getting beat up by the other three mobs should PROBABLY be off-tanking. Classes who can root but don't root-park, are missing a vital step, etc. Basically, in order for such a game plan to work in an LDoN, everyone needs to be on the same page. In the group described above, it sounds like people weren't. In addition, it seemed some egos started showing through as well.

As the above post stated, find people you trust and add them to friends list for LDoN. Making your own group will be easier that way. Also, if you want one but not lead, use the LFG tool and be discriminating aboiut the group you choose.

At the levels you were at, you were high person level-wise (with another). I'm guessing the rest of the group were low enough to put you at the next lower tier of instancing (not sure where the cutoffs are, but I'm guessing you were near it). Adventuring with people the same level with you would net you mobs closer in level to you, and consequently more experience. Also, being at the low end of the spectrum would allow the group to fight tougher mobs as well, giving you better experience as well. Basically, your situation seemed prime for a low experience gain by you.

I'll let someone else explain the average group level and exactly where the cutoffs are for various instancings. I know it exists, just not knowledgeable enough to explain it accurately myself.

If you want my advice, Sudancia, forget LDoNs from 50-64. In my opinion the XP is not worth it, considering the time wasted before and afterwards, and the 20 odd adventure points you get at your level do not make up for it.

At 54, I'd head to PoI, PoD or PoN. You'll get a more steady stream of XP that will last as long as you want, not 90 mins. Unless you are in a kite group or have great CC, most of the time the group will want to pull singles so, as long as you give the tank a tick or two, you won't have to worry about getting agro too much. Lastly, most of the camps in these zones are near the zone out so escape is a few steps away. If PoP isn't your cup of tea, other good alternatives include boats in Dulak.

After doing several hours straight in one of these places, you'll wonder why you ever bothered with LDoNs. I certainly did.

LaughingWoman
The /note function was one of my favorites for EQ. Particularly LDoN.

I formed all my own LDoN groups, and checked my /note while inviting. Check to see if anyone from the GREAT list is LFG. Then grab people from LFG who aren't on the EVIL list. I wouldn't start inviting others until I had the chanter or bard or healer - after which point I could start inviting everything else (I had the benefit of a near constant SK partner buddy). People learned that when *I* invited them to a group, we'd be entering the dungeon within 10 minutes, and they'd race to join.

Like most anything in EQ, it sucks and is slow to start out (it took a while to build up my EVIL list). But after a little while of building your Great LDoN Group Leader reputation, you'll have people holding out about the time you're usually forming your group... looking for you because they want a good dungeon - or 3 or 4. The longer you do it, the easier and faster it gets.

I'd say, stick it out if you really want the LDoN gear - work on learning what makes a successful group, what classes you can sub in for others in a pinch (e.g. with some groups, you can take a necro to mezz in miragul's dungeons) - to avoid keeping the group waiting too long, etc. It'll pay off before too long.
