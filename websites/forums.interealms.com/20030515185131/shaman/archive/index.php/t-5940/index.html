At 49, you're fully able to take on the Taskmaster. How?

Your pet. You will NOT be able to land any poison / disease on him (he even resists our supposedly unresistable disease debuff... totally immune). What you do is bring up your pet, slap HoS, guardian, chloro, and alacrity on him. If you have them, toss on some spiny weapons or such. Pull with Malaise, slap on Togor's, and send the pet in. You'll have to play cleric to your pet, and probably tank a bit for him, as well. If you have a JBB, zap the hell out of him, he has nothing for Cold resist. Average magic resist rate. It might be a bit close depending on your mana pool, but the 49 pet can take him, given heals.

He's on a half-hour spawn table, drops normal shissar loot, plus the phosphorescent fiber (~50pp with cha to a merchant), and a shissar revenant skull, which I haven't figured out yet. The drop you really want are the Shissar Organs for the enduring breath necklace.

The skeletons around him are non-aggro, and also easily killed by your pet. However, they have crap resists and surprisingly low hit points. They drop a mining pick that sells for about ~6pp, but is lore.

Around you are underbulks, Stonegrabbers, and fungus things. The stonegrabbers are very nice loot for luclin, dropping up to 10pp. The underbulks are worthless, as are the fungusbeasts... but it's still experience. Fireclaw (named underbulk) is immune to root and has some nice HP, but drops Fireclaw (decent h2h weapon) and fireclaw's carapace (so-so melee back item). There's a named fungus, Trilcor, who is a badass. He's like a harder-hitting taskmaster with less hitpoints and a disease-based stun that lasts way too long.

Echo Caverns has a massive exp bonus. At 52, killing a light blue skeleton was the same as killing a dark blue on Kunark or something. The taskmaster was a very nice bump to my exp, and Trilcor... well, He's a total waste.

Durdend
Been soloing TM since lvl44. It's not hard. Just gotta know how to do it.

1) Summon pet, and buff up. I normally just haste pet and give him two FS weapons. Or if I was just in the bazaar - Muzzle of Mardu (http://lucy.fnord.net/item.html?id=1348), a Girdle of Magi'Kot (http://lucy.fnord.net/item.html?id=28598) and two Fists of Ixiblat (http://lucy.fnord.net/item.html?id=28596). Total cost about 45-55pp, Girdle was the most expensive at 20pp.

2) When you arrive at the TM room, clear the two mobs on either side of the tunnel up to the TM room. I've had only one bad experience camping the TM, and it was when these two mobs added as I was doign my thing.

3) After clearing enough room to work with, I mem Instill, Togor's, Malisement, Blizzard Blast and Incapacitate or Winter's Roar (I always have Alacrity, Chroloplast and Canni II mem'ed).

4) Walk up to top of tunnel, target TM and cast Togor's (he's only resisted once). And back down the tunnel, while telling dogdog to attack. The TM will come as well as the first Undead Slave to the right (to the right facing the TM).

5) Both mobs will stop to try and put a hurt on dogdog. Target the Undead Slave and root and slow. Switch back to TM (as he could have dogdog down near 50-60% by this time), cast Maslisement, then nuke until TM comes after me.

6) After that it's "tank in teh face" while dogdog does his thing. I always nnuke too. One Blizzard Blast is about 10% of the TM's health, and I can usually get them in between blows.

7) After TM is dead, then kill Undead Slave.

Doing this I made all of Lvl44, and so far all of lvl45 (I'll ding lvl46 there too, need one more organ for my Amulet of the Grey Wastes, then it's to LoY for Stoicism). Not too hard with a KEI. And besides, I love walking up and soloing the TM room in front of the duo clearing the lower TM room but are too afraid to attempt the TM and his room.
