Macnair
I'm not saying I feel these changes are enough. Im just saying I feel its wrong to dismiss them completely. There have been changes in the last six months ... Grim I have a request to make.

There are two points that I have never seen developers talk about in any meaningful way. If your conversation is going well with them, as it seems to be, it would be quite helpful for me, and I expect for others, if you could get us some feedback on this.

1. Maintaining shaman healing ability as a constant percentage of cleric healing ability, because of the shaman's ability to slow. Shaman can heal for about half the total amount of hp healed as a cleric, in most situations (various math demonstrations put it at 47%). If game developers have written any posts defending this proportion, I have never seen it; but there is a fair amount of data out there that suggests this was fairly workable, in the kunark and velious eras, on fully-slowable mobs. In Planes then in Gates and now especially in Omens, more and more mobs mitigate slow. So slow is no longer as much of a factor as it used to be.

Question 1: In the developers' minds, what is the reason for continuing to use this ratio based on unmitigated slows, as the game adds in more and more mitigation?

2. Maintaining shaman healing ability as a constant percentage of cleric healing ability, instead of as a constant difference from cleric healing ability. When raw numbers increase, maintaining a constant percentage does not maintain the status quo: it means that the difference between the two gets larger and larger.

[a] Suppose a cleric can heal for 2000 and a shaman can heal for 1000. Because mobs hit you for actual hp rather than for percentages, the important point is not that the shaman can heal for half as much as the cleric; instead, it is that the shaman heals for 1000 less than the cleric.

[b] If a cleric can heal for 4000 and a shaman can heal for 2000, the ratio may be the same, but the difference in healing is now 2000 hp. There are many more situations where the damage caused by the mob will fall into that 2000 hp gap, and the tank will die.

[c] If a cleric can heal for 6000 and a shaman can heal for 3000, the difference in healing has become huge: 3000 hp. There are vastly more situations where that 3000 hp gap will mean a dead tank.

A 1000 hp disparity in healing power is a real disparity: it means that in a given situation it won't be as easy for shamans to heal -- but they will usually still be able to get the job done. In contrast, a 3000 hp disparity is immense: there will be lots of healing situations where they won't be able to get the job done, the tank will die and the group will be wiped.

Question 2: In the developers' minds, what is the reason for maintaining a constant percentage instead of a constant difference between shaman healing and cleric healing?
