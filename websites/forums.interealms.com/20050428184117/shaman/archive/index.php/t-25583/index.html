Jaruden
This is how we do it (are a little higher, trying to get some rings):

Priest (me) sits in the back of the room (away from the door you come in). Spawn Arch., warrior tanks him and pulls back next to me. Our Druid / Mage combo can kill the white guys no problem. They spawn every few seconds, starting in the hback and going around. There's a pattern, generally spawns a couple on each side then starts alternating. Whoever our 5th is (constant 4 man group, typically grab a 5th from guild) kills a few white guys till the warrior has some agro built up, then he gets on Arch. as well. I stand around poking Arch. too, but don't do too much aside from heal the MT and the druid.

At 50%, all 6 black statues come alive. At this point all but the MT disengage and kill them. MT still wailing away at Arch. When those are dead, all back to what they were doing. At 30% the big golems will come alive. We've decided killing them is a pain in the ass, so we don't. The warrior nabs agro on one, druid on the other, and everyone rushes full on to kill Arch. I keep those 2 alive. We've never had a problem killing Arch before the golems do too much damage, and once he's down, everything else poofs.

Collect loot, rinse, repeat. Our top run so far was 43 minutes to get to him, about 7 on fight(avoiding other named). Were average level about 46.

Agro control is pretty key, especially if you're lower levelled. For the most part I never have to cast a heal on anyone except the MT and the druid. Use potions early so you can hopefully get a 2nd use later in the fight if things go long enough.

You should have plenty of healing, put those 2 extra paladins on the adds and they can heal each other... the lower levelled priest should be able to hold up the MT against Arch, he doesn't do that much damage. Getting to 45 is a big difference (for the MT and rogue/dps especially), most classes generally have trouble hitting mobs more than 3 levels above them.
Barliman
The last three times I've killed the boss in Uldaman our group consisted of a warlock (me), paladin, druid, warrior and rogue. Paladin jumps in and begins to tank, druid heals, warrior and rogue concentrate on the boss.

My primary job is to deal with the adds but I also don't have a problem debuffing and dotting the boss. I usually use my imp in groups but summon the succubus for the boss fight as her dps isn't based on mana and I can dark pact for mana all fight long. The white dwarves that spawn frequently have very few hitpoints so my pet can kill those in 2-3 hits.

We all stay pretty packed in so when the 5 or 6 dwarves come in about mid fight I AE. I let them beat on the paladin for a few seconds so he gets a little agro from his damage shield and AE. These dwarves don't have a lot of hitpoints either so about 5-10 seconds of AEing and they are dead.

The 2 stone warders that spawn when the boss is around 25% health are kind of a pain. The good news is they despawn when you kill the boss. With a healthstone, health potion, and drain life I can usually tank one of them while the warrior tanks the other. While we are distracting the warders the other three members unload on the boss and have him dead in about 20-30 seconds.

As far as time based or health based when the dark dwarves and stone warders spawn I'm not completely sure but I think its based on the bosses hitpoints.

I'm not sure how strong paladins AEs are but I think your plan sounds pretty good. I would maybe consider designating one paladin to killing the white dwarves. When the 6 dark dwarves spawn shield the priest like you said and then AE. When the warders spawn stick a paladin on each one while the other three group members concentrate on killing the boss as fast as possible.
