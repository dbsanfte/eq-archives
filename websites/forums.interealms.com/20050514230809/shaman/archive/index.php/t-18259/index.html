Trying to get a grip on who can do what regen-wise, so I started collecting a bit of info. I'm realizing how little I know about some of these classes, first off. Per tick, this is what I have:

Beastlords: 33 hp, 12.5 mana (SD, regrowth, and paragon stretched over recast)
Druids: 25 hp, 8 mana (replenishment and Bot9) or
29 hp, 0 mana (natureskin and replen)
Enchanter: 0 hp, 18 mana (just VoQ, could figure tap regen for NDT, but screw it)
Ranger: 24hp, 0 mana (natureskin, force of nature and regrowth)
Bard: not included, for reasons below
Necromancer: also N/A
Shaman: 25 hp, 0 mana (replenishment)

Mostly, I wondered if anybody had anything to add to this - I'm sure I'm missing a good few things? Like all such complations, this takes slice of the class ability pie and tries to examine it in a vacuum, separate from other abilities, which is very limited and Thott-like, and not terribly illuminating in the grand scheme of class balance.

That being said, I think looking at what the classes can do on a simple buff basis (no songs, no taps, simple long-duration buff advantages, with most AA excluded) gives an idea of the PASSIVE benefits that these classes can bring to their groupmates with a very minimal time/mana investment (when viewed over time).

Now, for what I think of what this shows me. I view shaman as a support class. We heal, buff/debuff, and sit in the sidelines in groups, making others more efficient (and the creatures less efficient) at what they do. For this power, we sacrifice the fun of doing wicked amounts of damage. We give up tanking in teh face (when not soloing) - or we can go ahead and tank, but we'll be doing little else.

Of the classes who can provide health regen, we have the worst. We have no mana regen, which the other health regen classes DO get. To wrap it all up, beastlords end up being a better support class than their parent class, providing amazing health AND mana regen, in addition to being one of the most formidable DPS classes in the game. A ranger can provide nearly identical hp regen to a shaman. We're on par with (or surpassed by) hybrids in terms of passive regen.

Maybe a nice, moderate Endurance regen buff (with a long duration) is exactly what we need to nose us up as far as benefit-over-time buffing goes. Our stat buffs have been marginalized to near uselessness by the gear and augments that've entered the game ("anyone want diaku?" "what's that?" "str and dex" "nope, I'm maxed"). As a support class, it would be nice to be able to lend a bit more... support.

Or, I could be completely off. Nobody here will hold back to save my feelings. *chuckle* Looking forward to illumination and some good debate. (and I'll keep dreaming the impossible dream that this won't devolve into a screaming class rant session)

** notes: Bard regen starts at 20hp/20mana per tick, not counting amplification, but they give up just about everything for that, so I dont' really class it as regen. Necromancers - 720 hp over 9 ticks to all group members...300 mana over 4 ticks (group) -- I dont' want to include this mana-intensive and/or extreme stuff, in order to draw a line between regen and twitching/CH. Yes, beastlords have an AA included in their tally, but paragon is arguably a class-defining ability, is manaless, and relatively few AA. **
