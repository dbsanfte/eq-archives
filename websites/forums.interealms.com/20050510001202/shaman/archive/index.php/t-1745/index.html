You can get away with not having some of the "perk" spells for awhile like Levitate, endure breath, spirit of the snake, etc...depending on where you hunt. Here are some of the ones that I use the most (besides the ones you already have):

Its probably easier to tell you which you dont need from 14-24, don't forget everyone plays differently, what I would say may not be what someone else would say

Spells you can live without:

14
Burst of Strength < tricky this, some love this line, others dont bother with it. If you end up meleeing alot then get it or wait for Frenzy at 19.

Enduring Breath < get this eventually but you're unlikely to be spending much time underwater for a while, come back for it.

Invisibility versus Animals < useful in some very specific situations only.

Turtle Skin < Uses snake skins which can be a pain, just dont bother

19
Frenzy < as with Burst of Strength

Grow < useful in certain indoor situations, can wait if youre outside alot.

Infectious Cloud < AE, dont bother

Vision < might be useful for banking/using merchants through elven size doors if you ever get non kos with any.

24
Creeping Vision < didnt they nerf this? Not sure I ever bought it

Invigor < you wont have room to mem it anyway

Poison Storm < AE, dont bother

Spirit of Cheetah < useful for travelling but can get it later

My opinion on essential spells to 24:

Spirit of Wolf (always have this memmed - saved my bacon more times than I would care to remember)

Root (always have this memmed)

Poison DOTs (I rarely use the area effect DOTs or the disease ones)

Slows (always memmed - this really defines our class)

Regen - essential

Canni - essential

Buff spells - strength, stamina, and dex are arguably the most useful although the charisma buffs let you buy spells cheaper :)

Healing spells - essential

Debuffs - not too critical early on, but essential as you level


Ok, so my list includes most of our spells :) . Luckily (or unluckily depending on how you look at it) most of our spells are pretty darn useful. There are some duds and many situational spells, but overall we have a pretty nice line-up.

If you are having money problems, just farm some low-level mobs for cash. High quality bear pelts sell for 20-30pp each to another player and drop off of newbie mobs. Bone chips and bat wings are easy to farm and sell for 2 - 5pp a stack.

If you have a magic weapon you can start hunting wisps - graeter lightstones have a quest associated with them that can get you nearly 10pp (check alakazam's website for quest info).

Hope that helps,
