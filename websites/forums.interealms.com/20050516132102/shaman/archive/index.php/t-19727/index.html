Ranguard
As a 57 Shaman, I thought the new expansion was pretty decent. In Natimbi, I ran around with my wife (52 Monk) and we were duoing just about everything. Mobs hit kinda hard and fast, so slow is a big priority. Resists at 57 seem to be about 50/50 firing off Turgur's without Malosini. Basic strategy for us was slow, epic DoT, sick dogdog and Stoic near the end. No deaths, not even close. The trains are a bit sick with people running around like chickens missing their heads. Couldn't zone over to The Hanging Gardens zone because of level lock (which was patched) Abysmal Sea zone is a bit confusing as far as layout, but the mini-quests seem helpful for beginner tradeskillers. The new potions make me very happy, especially snare. Only downfall to them is they don't stack and could be bothersome to carry, as 6 slots plus an empty medicine bag is hard to handle (I'm a packrat). The tribute system, if it stands as it is, will be very nice. Can keep the effects going indefinately just by farming for one or two nights a week. I have amassed 100k FP and only spent 3kpp.

Overall, I believe that GoD is a decent expansion. Back to what I had come to love about pre-PoP EQ. I have a few lvl 65 friends who got wiped on the same mobs that my wife and I were taking out, so that tells me that all the lazy people who raced to 65 in PoP and developed no tactics can't run through content. Other than the level locked zone, the content I saw was impressive. I like the new models, the low HP mobs and the mini-quests available. I have no opinion on the spells as I have quite a ways to go before seeing any. Nice, challenging expansion.

Anyways, I love this. This is fun for me. All of this is challenging. Fun and challenging. No longer can you move around the whole zone and not worry about losing invis or being seen by something. This whole concept about graveyards is obsolete now. You can't just step into a zone and go from point A to point B without making sure that nothing will see you. It benefits to pay attention to the layout of the zones to figure out the best way (it's like, you can take Qinimi to Barindu, but we've found a good % of the time it's best to run through Riwwi to Barindu instead). All of the Kyvs seem to see invis so avoid them.

The exp in places is goin to be better than others. The plant has great exp, but it's alot more challenging than the other sewer zones. The HP in Barindu is train central area, and it pays to pay attention to where you are standing in there (there is a safe place to stand, however I guarantee that not everyone is that smart to do it, and will train you). I get in there, remove invis, hail the HP and immediately invis again just to make sure.

The area by the apprentice isn't totally safe, it all depends on what mob is roaming. Sometimes it's a kyv, and when that happens, you have to run back towards the mtn pass and wait for him to pass before you hail the Apprentice.

What's great is that these areas won't be frequented by the normal run of the average person who doesn't know how to play. There will be occurances of course, but unless you are smart about how you move about here, you aren't going to get very far. Sometimes its unavoidable and someof the mobs are unforgiving, but for the most part, it's not too bad.

On that note, some areas of course aren't going to be able to be handled by a normal tank. If you do make it to Vxed and Tipd, a tank with 7k hps isn't goin to cut it there. Mobs hit too hard. There is alot of room to get only single mobs, but they have an extremely high risk right now. Exp isn't too bad there, prob cold be just a bit better though.
