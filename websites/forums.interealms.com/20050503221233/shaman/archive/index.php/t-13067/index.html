Mamutoi
It is also useful for moving a mob, either for positioning reasons or to stop one about to gate. A little trick, say you have a mob that is stuck in a corner and no one seems to be able to pull him out. Position yourself in front of the mob facing AWAY from him (looking in the direction you want him to be moved to) cast VP on him and he will be "knocked back" in the direction you are facing.. out of the corner. The knockback effect uses Your position when it moves the mob.. it pushes the mob away from you in the direction you are facing not necesarrily knocking the mob backwards.

VP uses a disease check for resists.

Hail,

I have found VP very useful in certain situations. I was extremely happy with it initially. Now I still enjoy it, but it isn't as wonderful as I first thought.

In Valor/Storms, it is extremely effective to root park an add without having to waste a gemslot on Greater Immobilize or Petrifying Earth. If the puller has problems bringing back multiple (more than two) mobs, you still have to keep a root spell memorized for safety in my opinion.

I have found VP to be of limited use in HoH, BoT, and Tactics. Many of the times VP will get resisted, or the mobs are immune to snare/root. I may then actually be worse off if I don't have a root spell memorized & I was counting on VP for cc.

I think VP is an excellent investment in AA's early if you are grouping or soloing in Tier2 or below. I don't begrudge the points spent in it (rather late in my AA progression), but I'm using it less now in Tier3 than I thought I would be. The knockback effect is fun to use though, especially with a Warrior MA grouped to push the mob off of yourself and right back where the warrior is standing.

It is a good ability to have, and doesn't cost much to get. Nice tool to add to the belt. If you have scaled Avatar legs though, it is probably not worth the points unless you are finding GI bouncing alot.

Take care,
