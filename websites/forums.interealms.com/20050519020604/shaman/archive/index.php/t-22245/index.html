Throughout the history of EverQuest there have been quests that were epic on proportion. In the olden days they were our particle weapons, specifically the shaman Spear of Fate. In Planes of Power it was less creative, but the charm that recieved statistical upgrades as you progressed through the planes. This wasn't implemented until the Legends of Ykesha expansion though, if I remember correctly. In LDoN it was an epic augment for a charm. The creativity is somewhat lacking here. And now in today's EverQuest, the GoD expansion, there is an epic quest for an augment for any slot, that is quite nice.

There is an excellent guide available for this quest <A HREF="http://www.frmug.org/~archer/Breakdown.html">here</A> but I wanted to take it a step further and analyze the quest from the perspective of a shaman.

-----

<H3>Qinimi</H3>

First you need to collect three items to make the Stone of Entry. The Stonedust Particles in Riwwi colloseum area is self explanitory. Just head to Riwwi, invis to the arena, and you can pull the mobs there single. There are 2 doors in guarded by 2 golems each, and if you are keeping the golems dead there are two wanderers per spawn cycle. Any of these mobs can drop them. Also if you are lucky you might also get Reyna's Bloody Earing for the Riwwi portion of this quest. Mobs in the arena don't hit hard so you can tank them or root rot them, but note magic based root tends to last the duration while Virulent Paralysis does not.

A Glob of Mud from the sewers. Later on in the quest you will need a mechanical type drop from each sewers expedition. The drops are only off of the named, so you will be spending some time in them for those parts. Along the way you will get many globs. I have even seen one mob drop 2 of them before. They are far from uncommon. Sewers can be sloed either by root rotting or tanking and using heal over times and patches. It all dpeends on your style.

Lastly you need a piece of chalk from any mob in Riwwi. If you look farther down there is a part in Riwwi where you need to collect 10 keys. One of the keys drops randomly off of nocs and kyvs in Qinimi. What I did was killed two birds with one stone. Invis over to the Riwwi zone line. It is nothing but nocs over there who have a chance to drop the ley and the piece of chalk. These can be rooted if I remember correctly, but any mob in Qinimi is basically trivial.

Once you complete your stone of entry you will need to gather a group of friends. You will need a tank, a cleric, yourself, and I find that 3 high dps classes will work well. You can bring an enchanter or bard if the crowd control makes you feel secure. The event is described in the walkthrough. My tip for this is that as soon as the named snake lady dies, slow the executioner and run up to it. If you don't agro him soon enough he will slay the gnome, and you will have to do this again. When you win the event you hand in the Stone of Entry to the gnome and he hands you the Writ of the Magi. There is no limit to how many people can turn in the stone, so you can do it for all 6 people in one go if they are all up to that point in the quest.

The final portion of Qinimi is an 18 man raid. you will want that many as there is a timer and dps is key. You may or may not have trouble with this depending on how helpful your guild is. Throughout this event there was me and another shaman, and both of us had Turgur's and Nihil up because the mobs came in waves of 6 and overlapped each other. As soon as the named archer dies, some AE stun goes off, so you will want EVERYONE against the wall, or they will be within melee range of the remaining mobs, and will more than likely die. This trial loots 3 quest pieces, so try to have 3 people that need it there, as people aren't too fond of these events.

----

<H3>Barindu</H3>

Part one is collecting two journal pages. Any mob in Barindu can drop these. Barindu mobs are pretty tough. Some of them flurry and rampage so if you are going to tank them you should be geared somewhat like me. You can also root rot the vast majority of the mobs, although i recall the nocs are not rootable, and they rampage. A failsafe is that you can get a sewer expedition before any questionable pulls, and just zone in to it if you are not faring so well. Once you get these hail the appropriate npcs.

More collecting! You are going to need 2 drops from natimbi. The zone is so unbelievably trivial to a 65 its crazy. the tide feasters are in the river in its north most part that flows east and west. It may take a few to get the drop.

Any hynid in natimbi can drop this item. There are some on the main island, and some between the KT zone and Qinimi zone. Kill these until you get the drop. When I was doing this I got a flowing thought necklace off of a hynid and was able to sell it for 80k.

The last two drops are from the sewers, and evaded me for a bit. i forget what themes have the mobs, but the sludge lurkers drop the slime, and the larva mobs drop the larva item. Sewers mobs are sometimes rootable, but be prepared to tank them and be warned that they can hit you for 1000.

Once you have these four items you can make the fruit. After you have the fruit you talk to a few more npcs and recieve a serving tray. Now for the fun part!

Ikaav Ixvet Pox. Make sure she's up before you get people together. We only needed a little more than two groups for this, but we clear time in 5 hours and have beaten ikkinz 4. If your guild is only elemental, or you havent quite made it to elementals yet I would suggest stopping by Barindu to slay this as part of the nightly raid schedule. The loot is pretty good and you can get 3 people the quest item at once.

We met up at the Tipt/Vxed zoneline. We cleared our way to the building Chiaka is in and proceeded to pull the golems out of Ikaav's building. I handed in the tray and fruit and that npc went in and "died". I was told that if you kill the 2 Aneuk Controllers at the same time that the golem will not go live. We however did not use this method. The aneuks and ikaav are rooted, so stay away, but if the golem goes live, he is not rooted. Everything is slowable except the ikaav, who doesn't hit too hard. We killed one controller, the golem went live, we offtanked the golme and killed th eother controller, then we slayed the golem. Then everyone burned the ikaac down. The quest pieces are on the remains behind the ikaav. You will have to melee attack it to get it open.
