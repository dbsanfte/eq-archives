The "you can solo, so you don't need anything" card against shamans, haven't been played too much recently, but still is played around by other classes that argue against any proposal by shamans to improve or fix our class.

So, I went over the official homepage of Everquest and looked at the descriptions of each class. I found them interesting and amusing, and so I posted excerpts:

Bards
"Bards do well both alone and in groups."

Beastlords
"Beastlords can adventure solo well in many places."

Druids
"Druids are a well-rounded class, prospering in both solo and grouped situations."

Mages
"Magicians are able to adventure solo, but also provide value in a group situation."

Monks
"Monks provide a powerful ally in a group, though it's not unusual to see a monk adventuring alone."

Necros
"Necromancers are a very self-sufficient class and often choose to adventure alone, though they can provide great value to a group as well."

Paladins
"Paladins can sometimes work alone, healing themselves with magic, but in a group a paladin really shines, going head to head with his enemies and occasionally falling back to heal." *I really like that last part :rolleyes: *

Rangers
"Rangers can hunt alone or in a group, though allies allow them to best use their abilities."

Shadowknights
"Shadowknights can use their dark powers to adventure solo for a time."

Shamans
"Shamans are a well-rounded class with strong abilities in both group and solo situations."

Wizards
"Wizards are capable adventurers both solo and in groups, and are an excellent choice for players who live to blow things up."

So, apparently and officially, many classes can solo and many of the above have had numerous complaints like shamans are having today about issues, and yet they got what they wanted. It seems we aren't alone in the tank "IN TEH FACE" category.
