This is how I duo, typically (a friend of mine is a ranger).

He pulls with harmony and snare (a controlled pull is a good pull). He runs the mob past me so I can malo it, slow it, and cripple it (if I am so inclined). This is not normally enough for me to get agro (which is good). When I am ready (after putting quiessance or torpor on myself) I cast bane of nife once -- and once is all it will take -- and have agro for the remainder of the fight. If, by some miracle, I lose agro I can cast Bane Of Nife again and then nothing short of my death will make the mob look at anyone but me.

I suppose I could pull, but honestly a ranger is much better at pulling than a shaman.

Viscerator
Poisons and slow are the best way to get aggro and keep it. If the melee isn't actively trying to steal aggro, you will hold it the whole fight.
Higher level spells will generate more hate because of their larger damage and poison counters.

Giving the melee the back is the best for all the reasons you have mentioned.

Alternately if it is too hard to tank straight up, you can do the traditional snare kite and ping-pong aggro as needed. You could ask the ranger's glade for confirmation, but from everything I have seen and been told, they can shoot any mob just the same rooted, snared, tanked...as long as it stays out of line of other objects/walls. The back positioning being the only factor that matters.
