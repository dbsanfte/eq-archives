I am not level 65, not even level 60. The problem though I see is this. In PoP Tier 1 zones, you are basicly required to have 3 things. A tank, a healer, a slower. In optimal situations, a ranger cannot fill one of those roles. On my server there are tons of folks filling these three roles that are either already in groups or LFG in PoP.

Basicly that leaves you with three spots open in a group that you can fill....along with a ton of other classes. Pretend that you have a warrior tanking (most likely ideal), then a cleric as the main healer (again most likely ideal), then for sake of best slows a shaman (again ideal). The next thing a lot of exp groups will look for is a mana maker/crowd control (enchanter ideal, bard will work too).

That leaves 2 slots open. With the classes left: Shadowknight, Rouge, Ranger, Paladin, Beastlord, Druid, Wizard, Mage, Bard (if not taken from above), Monk (did I forget a class here?).

Obviously I am on the ranger's side here being one. As "general_group_001" though, if all of those classes were left, and 2 slots in the group were left, who would they choose? Not saying what would be the best choice, but who would YOU THINK they would choose. This is just a random "I don't know any folks in zone" type of group, not guildys or friends.

Pretend we consider Enchanter and bard as taken as one slot, just to eliminate a class. Well, playing devils advocate, you would still need a melee or two, and I think that they would take monk and rouge over us, or a back-up healer if they had no shaman.. Rouge for backstabbing and monk for pulling singles with FD seems to be what I see often.

In non optimal situations, there are still other classes that can more or less fill the role of one or more of these three.

Tanks: Besides warriors, Pallys and SK are looked at as the next viable tanks. Heck even monks still. For that matter I don't even want to go into beastlords, I personally know a 63ish BL that has a level 60 pet with around 4800 hp. All I have to do is stand back and plink arrows.

Healer: Well Shaman, Druids, even Pallys to a certain extent can take either a secondary role in healing or be main healer in a pinch, but the group will still leave a slot open for a cleric for big AC/HP buffs and rezzes when needed.

Slower: Mobs hit for a ton, and you need a slower. Shaman, Enchanters, and Bards (even beastlords?) can all do this to an extent, along with haste. (Yes, we Rangers can slow with a proc, but not reliably).

So we rangers are sort of tossed in with the remaining classes, all LFG. I honestly think I add more to a group than DPS, and have a lot to offer, but it takes a lot to convince other people. We all know what rangers can do, but with exp grinding in PoP mainly being based on Tank, Healer, Slower/Haster, it makes it hard for us to get a group. This is nothing THAT new, but with PoP fairly new to all of us still, there is a huge wash of people LFG at any given time, and I see rangers passed up all the time (us non AM3/EQ folks). Do I like it? No.
