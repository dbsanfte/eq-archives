We in America used to question authority, challenge broad statements, asked people to prove things. When did we just start believing anything the government told us at face value? Did the "Greatest Generation" shield us from so much hardship that we now have lost the desire to challenge authority and have become callous to others suffering because we haven't suffered? Or for the younger of you, your parents haven't suffered?

My parent's Generation (the WWII aka "the Greatest" generation) lived through the Great depression and WWII and with that background gave us Medicare, Medicaid, expanded Social Security, gave us Affirmative Action to combat centuries of racial discrimination, Voting Rights Act, the Civil Rights movement, Women's rights (ERA excepted) Environmental Protection, while driving this countries economy through the roof. (This thread is not to debate these programs just to show what they did to make the country a better place).

Is it just me that all we as a country learned from them is the desire to build and grow (aka the greed) and lost touch with their compassionate side? Did they give it all to us (or our parents for the young ‘uns) so we are now becoming a shallow myopic country? They lived through harsh times and tried to build a society that would not make their children live through it. I have seen people who would step over homeless people to go to an “Animal Rights” rally. We have all heard about those who shoot and bomb people to prevent abortions. Every side has their wackos.

Look at the old school Republicans and Democrats - the late Barry Goldwater, Gerald Ford, even Richard Nixon on the Republican side; and Lyndon Johnson, John and Robert Kennedy on the Democratic side. Could they even exist in their respective parties today as a party leader? Bob Dole wasn't conservative enough. Bush the elder's view points are quite in contrast with his son - although he will never ever admit it in public - you have to look at what he said 10 years ago.

This is a condemnation of the polarization of the left and the right. We used to compromise in this country. Not any more. Every thing seems to be a zero sum game. If you can embarrass your opponent, all the better.

Have we become such a shallow country that we as a Nation, could not take the body count of an Omaha beach or an Iwo Jima. And Mill, it is not just the left that would cry out - it is the right would as well, especially if there was a Democrat in the White House (just look at there complaints about the war in Kosovo as exhibit 1).

When did we just start believing that what our government told us was right and true? Did our parents (grandparents) do too good of a job protecting us from the harsh realities of the world and made us believe we could have it all and never have to pay? Did they protect us from the thoughts that the Government could lie to us? Did they make us strong in needs and weak in deeds?

My father is 82 years old, was a door gunner on a B24 in North Africa and Italy. He came back from Europe, became an electrical engineer (his first engineering job in 1949 was working for the company later to become UNIVAC programming computers for the Navy, but thought there was no future in that) and raised 6 kids, put them through college and did not consider saving for retirement until age 55. He made his fortune AFTER retirement. His father wandered the country in the 30s and worked whatever he could to make money to send home (my mother’s father was a bootlegger in the Black Hills to make money during the depression and spent some time as a guest of the Federal Government in Kansas). I hope I can live up to the huge shadow they cast.

Can we match the drive, courage, and compassion of that Generation? Or are we too stuck on TV, No fat decaf Grande Lattes, and wanting the world to think our child is THE honor student at Horatio H. Blowhard elementary school – even though they give that award to all students who don’t excessively drool.

Just my thoughts on a day that I have had to read through complete horse hockey rationalizations on why people have suddenly become conscientious objectors after they have been mobilized after serving for years in the reserves.

I am sorry if I have bored you all with my meandering musings.
