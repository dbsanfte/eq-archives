I just read an interesting thread over on the bard forum:

http://pub142.ezboard.com/feqdivabardsofeverquestfrm2.showMessage?topicID=797.topic

I'm not finished with the thread yet, but basically this bard crunched some numbers on bard damage shields, and discovered that they really create a lot of DPS. Most importantly to the shaman community, they found this tactic worked best on our hated nemesis, the unslowable mob. I know that I for one am going to start making damage shield potions to give to my MT when we see an archaic in PoI, or some other unslowable mob, and my bard friend (who first showed me the thread) has been similarly affected.

P.S. Don't stop after the first page (like I almost did). The thread has more good stuff in the following pages, such as this break down of stacking DSes:

Buff slot 1 damage shields:
Chardok ring, Mage, Ranger and Druid damage shields. Mark of Karn. Bard bracer (shield of flame). Psalm line of bard songs. Lava diamond. Feedback.

Buff slot 2 damage shields:
Shaman potions. All Coldain rings. Riftwind, Call of Earth/Rathe, Karana pally/warrior helmet, Illusion:fire elemental.

Buff slot 3 damage shields:
Thistlecoat, Barbcoat, Bramblecoat, Spikecoat, Thorncoat, Bladecoat, Blessing of the Vah Shir. Basically, druid self only, or item-right clicks.

Buff slot 4 damage shields:
McVaxius' Rousing Rondo, Thorny Shield, GM buffs.

Buff slot 5:
Blessing of Vah Kerrath.

Buff slot 6:
Cloak of the Akheva (SK, self only).

Not a Buff slot:
Mark of Retribution, the cleric spell you target the mob, does indeed stack with all the damage shields.
