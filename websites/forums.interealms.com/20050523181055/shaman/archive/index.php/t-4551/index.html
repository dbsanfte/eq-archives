I'm not sure if this topic was already discussed (I'm thinking it has, A lot), but to the point.

I was recently involved in some (flamewars?) in my previous topic, which involved druids. I myself currently have a level 9 druid, but a 34 Shaman, and would much prefer the shaman anyday. It is believed to be the most popular idea that druids are overpowered compared to a shaman. I recently read a thread which involved bashing druids because wizards are said to be the masters of travel, but druids get port spells and SOW spells. I then started to believe this general idea, and soon wanted to start playing my druid. I then compared the skills and spells both the Shaman and the druid get, and realised that both classes are un-believablly balanced. Why you ask?

First, druids get snare, where as shamans don't. This is equally evened out with shamans getting slows. They get ensnare, we get Turgur's. Secondly, they have dot's to add with their ability to snare. What they don't get are the powerfull dot's we achieve at lower levels. At level 24, we get Envenomed Breath, where as they get Creeping crude. Shaman's E-Breath = 219 Damage over 42 seconds - Druid's Creeping Crude = 210 Dmg over 60 seconds. I know what your thinking, not really a big difference eh? Well, at level 34 both classes get their newest DoT's. Shaman's Scourge = 542 Dmg over 126 seconds - Druid's Drones of Droom = 340 dmg over 60 seconds. To equal the amount of damage scoure does in 2 minutes, and druid would have to recast Drones two times, making the 170 mana the shaman cast into 282 mana the druid casts. Now, even though our DoT's have a DD component that breaks root, ours stack. Their's don't. For instance, scoure stacks with E-breath, creating a total of 761 damage over 126 seconds.

Druids get ports? Cry me a river. What they don't get is alchemey. Most shamans that are expierenced in this field know that alcehemy has better uses then making money, as do druids know porting isn't the best and only source of income. If either skill is chosen for money making purposes, it can still make a decent amount of income.

Quad kiting. Now there's a skill most shamans envy. Sometimes, I wish we had the ability to snare, but then again that would take away our ability to pet kite, and to slow. I was recently grouped with a druid the other night. Even with KEI, he had to wait until FM to kite one mob. I also noticed he was afraid to kite anything above a blue con for fear of resists. I ALSO noticed they did not practice the skills of kiting without snare. I was showing him a good spot to solo when he told me he wouldn't be able to do. Why not? The mob would resist his snares and he'd be dead. Why not run ahead of the mob and root it or dot it? "it would catch up to me". I was absoultly dumb-founded. I'm not sure about you guys, but when I kite and a DoT wears off, I run about 5 seconds ahead of it and re-cast the dot. Druids rely on their snares so much they never learned to do that. Now, why did he only attempt blue mobs? The resists right? Right. Druids can quad kite, but only mobs that con lower then them. Anything else is suicide to quad kite, much less single kite. The exp a druid gets from 4 blue kills equals the exp a shaman gets from 1 or 2 yellows or one red. A pet kiting shaman would end with maybe missing one bubble to maybe half, and continue with another yellow pull, while a quad or single kiting druid would end with 0m, and having to med back to full before attempting anything again. Shaman's have many different types of kiting they can preform, while druids really only have one choice (snare and nuke/dot). I've read and seen everything from shaman's quad kiting, pet kiting, pet tanking, actual tanking (Dangerous to perform without Turgur's), to the original kiting. I missed a few or a lot, but I know there are more styles. I'm very biased towards shamans, but now some, if not most of you will know that both classes even out perfectly. In the endgame, Shaman's will be dominant, imho.

Just my two cents.

-Exodus

Oh, by the way, I'm talking pre-epic and pre-jbb. I'm not twinked, and neither is the druid I grouped with.

I'll go out on a limb here and disagree with you about the druids endgame, folks. I don't think they are underpowered at all. I LIKE grouping with druids. Snare is great for runners, the back-up heals are very nice, especially since nature's touch doesn't slow the tanks. Not to mention, in a good group, with some AA points, they kill far too fast to use DoTs--druid DD spells are GREAT for that kind of group.

On raid level mobs, when I lead raids, I REALLY appreciate druids. Natures Touch and Chloroblast are extremely important as back-up heals when switching main tanks. On mobs with obscene damage, where you almost HAVE to have defensive (yes, there are a few--just not many), a strong core of druids can make or break a raid. When, due to lag, or just a mistake, you lose a MT and people aren't ready to switch cleanly, it helps even more. Sometimes knowing you have to switch is easier than a switch on the fly, even with an alert crew of clerics who know that the people at the end of the chain need to DL and remedy while the front of the chain gets established.

I've seen some smaller guilds make a related mistake on vindi. They set a heal chain too slow, and try to make the druids (and shaman) make up for the slow chain with assist heals. With current heal spells, none of them can really make up for a cleric (unless you have a LOT of shaman and druids--most small guilds don't). The other problem is DPS for small guilds. With focus items and AA points, druid nuking is VERY nice. It adds up to a substantial amount of damage to mobs--damage they can't do if they are forced to heal full-time. I've seen 3 small guilds trying it this way on our server. They need to up the DPS with druids and shaman blasting, because over the length of a fight without the druids blasting, there is too much chance of a mistake because the fight is much longer.

Completely outside of the ports and evac, I think druids are much maligned. Of course, as has been said, part of that is because it is such an easy class that it has a lot of real idiots in it.
