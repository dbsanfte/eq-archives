Shamans are not overpowered, and they are not gimped. I think they are just about right.

For example, yes, you can't nuke/dot uber mobs as the "best Dot" thread is arguing about, but you're not alone - many other EQ classes can't either. Yes, I think some slightly better heals would be nice - I'm not talking complete heal, but maybe something that heals a bit more for a better mana ratio so that groups can *get by* without a cleric - I think we all agree we didn't start shamans to be clerics - we just want to backup heal in a pinch. Verant is working on it too...

Yes, there's no lvl 59 or 60 pet. It would be nice to have - but let's be realistic here folks, a lvl 60 shaman can take stuff no other class can even think of taking - our slows make us powerful, and a bad arse pet may actually be unbalancing...

Drop rates on high spells stink - that's a downside - but not the end of the world either - sort of makes up for our easy as pie epic, wouldn't you say?

And of course the list goes on and on. It just irritates me how people always want the best for themselves, so many greedy people. After playing numerous other classes, I can say that the shaman isnt' without flaws, but they are pretty darn fun. We aren't gods as jealous rangers on these boards would have you believe, nor do we suck because he can't heal like a cleric and torpor doesn't appear in your bank vault upon dinging lvl 60.

Sorry - maybe this belongs in the rant forum, but I just get tired of people bad mouthing the Shaman class (both ways) when it's not true. Enjoy your shaman for what they are - a reasonably well balanced class in EQ that's fun to play.

Each class has a moment where they really shine whether group or soloing.

Druids/wizards rock for the quad kiting/burn them all down in a group moments (dungeons a tad harder though for that).

Necros for being able to solo practically anywhere.

Mages for having good offense and some kick ass pets to boot.

Enchanters for charm, slow and some not too bad spells (I know several that are soloing to 60..give them dire charm and they are really gross).

But shamans...the love of my life. We do everything. No zone is too hard to solo in (indoor/outdoor). Summoning mobs are nothing special, slow owns a mob (good bye damage), dots that are decent, kickass mana regeneration (Can 5 + torpor = any caster's wet dream of mana regeneration), solid buffing and healing line up, debuffs that are sickening and decent AC and HPs to boot. We are not the perfect class but if you want one that can get thru most things relatively unscathed well this class can do it.

For instance, today I was with a 53 cleric, 2 x 54 warriors, 54 bard, 57 wizard and 60 shaman (me). We were doing bar in sebilis with puller messed up and brought in 4. No biggie..quick malo to aid CC with bard, slows around the clock, speed refreshes on melees and dots on the main target per usual. Can 5 twice to FM and kept on rocking. Room spawned on us so at one point we hit 8 mobs in room. Cleric was running lom (no clarity or song due to CC) so I took over healing as well as refreshing slows, doing malo on new mobs and dotting others. End of fight, I have FM, group cheering each other for great job (they did well and I salute them) and cleric realizing how dangerous a well played shaman is. ;) Twice evac'd was called and I forced them to hold. Thank you shaman mana regeneration, healing and dots.

What is great about shamans is the same as chanters...we make the impossible possible and add that extra edge to a group that allows it to go beyond its expectations.
