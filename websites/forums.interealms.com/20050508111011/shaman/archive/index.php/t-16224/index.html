Kaalisti_Painstorm
First, welcome to the crucible :D
This is one of the best spots to learn about everything shaman.
We do have trolls though so don't get discouraged if someone's a smartalek.

Now, go read the Sticky (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=13787) at the top of the forum. Pretty much everything you need to know is in there.

"What I mean is, do these stats go up as my character levels up or are they fixed?"
Your stats will fluxuate dependant upon your gear. They dont automatically increase with level (str, sta, wis, etc)

Should you re-roll your shaman?
Personally I'd say no probably not, but others might argue with that. It tends to all work out in the end, but your path to 65 may be a bit harder than someone who distributed their points differently.

"Is there a way to improve my wisdom in the short term?"
Yes, get buffed by a enchanter. It's not a permenant fix though. You can also try to get +wis gear.
At Your level you should be looking for +wis / +mana gear as well as AC. HP won't do you a lot of good till you reach canni level.

Keep this in mind:
A shaman is a completely different experience at various levels. Pre-24th, 24th-34th (or whatever level we get the pet), 34th to mid-50's, then 60+
Cannibilize changes everything. So does the Pet. Mid-50's we start getting some really good group buffs, and then Torpor/Quiescence changes everything again.
Shamans are VERY versitile, we can do just about anything in a group, depending on where you're at in your spell progression.
Most shamans love their toons fiercly, and keep coming back to them when they try other classes.

Dandi
Powerlevelling is when someone else, usually much higher level, helps you level up much faster than you normally would be able to. It can involve buffing you, healing you, debuffing mobs, pulling things for you, etc. The key ingredient is that they are doing things for you that that are far, far beyond what you would normally be able to do for yourself.

As for stats, I can't remember what all I put mine into (looong time ago hehe) but I know I didn't put anything into wis. With the chance to do it all over, I would probably focus on agility and stamina, in that order. Shamans are relatively easy to play up until we get canni, and once we have canni mana is a lot less of a problem for us than for most other casters. And wis is much, much easier for us to max than agi or sta.
