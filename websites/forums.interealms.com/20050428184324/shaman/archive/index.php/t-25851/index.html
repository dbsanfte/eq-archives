I've went pure maces / combat from day one (55 now) and I still laugh at dagger rogues. Here's another thing: You can easily macro weaponswapping and tie it to abilites so that you can still backstab if you want to. Or ambush if that's your thing. But the dps of SS and the stuns maces bring is exceptional. The *only* downside of going with maces is you really need slow, high dmg weapons to reap the rewards, and that really renders poisons a lot less effective than they are for faster weapons, where landing is a percentage of your swings.

Note, I still do swap in the dagger and BS in situations. Such as when SS / maces pulls agro from the tank. Then BS is like a step down in dps and sometimes easier to deal with.

Basically, the way it's done (and I'll post the real text when I get home if there is interest) is you have 2 steps to, say, your backstab macro:

1) Backstab
2) Swap weapons

So when you are finding yourself wanting to bs. you tap that twice, first tap 'fails' BS cause you don't have a dagger, but step 2 gives you the dagger. Tap 2 performs the BS and puts the mace back in main hand. (Until they put more / better pause functionality in macros, that's the best I could do.)

Extend this to gouge, ambush, etc and you can really go without losing the fun of daggers.

Maybe in PvP mana spring sucks.
In PvE it's very useful, especially if you are a restoration shaman.
When you are in a group though, it's huge.
Let's look at a restoration shaman at level 60: For 185 mana (actually 205 minus 10% cuz of talents), everyone gets 12.5 mana per 2 seconds (6.25 mana per second due to talents) for 69 seconds (15% longer). 6.25 mana per second * 69 seconds = 431.25 mana regained....per group member.

So, you are in an instance. You get a pull of 2-3 mobs. You slap down mana tide. Up to all 5 group members get back 431 mana, at the cost of 185 mana to just the shaman. If you have 5 casters in the group (maximum efficiency), you get 2156 total mana regenerated every 1.1 minutes, for the cost of 185 mana. Oh, and unlike mana regen that comes from spirit, this mana regen works regardless if you are casting spells or not.
Juujuu
Mana Tide on the other hand is huge:
49 mana cost (54 - 10%)
12 sec duration
restores 240 mana every 3 seconds
240 x 4 = 960 mana regained by all group members for 49 mana cost.

Now, suppose you go into a long fight.
You pop off mana spring at the start when people are first dumping their mana.
After it goes away, and people's mana levels are dropping, you lay down mana tide for another ~1k mana regen.
When it goes away, you put up the mana tide totem again for the ongoing mana regen.
Throw in a paladin doing their mana regen blessing, and you have a *huge* amount of perpetual mana regen happening.
Over a 1.5 minute fight, with mana spring and mana tide, you can give all group members about 1400 mana, for a net cost to the shaman of about 240 mana.
I like those numbers.
