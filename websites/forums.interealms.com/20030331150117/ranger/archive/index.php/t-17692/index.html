Magic resists stuff like Snare, Slow, Lightning nukes, Fear and all that other stuff. So I guess you could say that it covers everything that "doesn't fit" into the other saves. I never really looked at it like that myself.

As for level-appropriate saves blah-blah, you really only see saves becoming useful beyond 200+ or that was the point they were at last time I was in ToV. There aren't that many AE mobs in PoP.
SwordMage
Are you saying that resists only matter in the end game and are not useful in the amounts available to characters in their 20s to 40s when fighting in XP groups?
Elured
Sort of. For instance, the majority of exp mobs have very weak nukes, when rangers and monks run around with 5k HP in PoV, the occasional blast for 600 is not going to bother you much.

This is scaled up from the absolutely pitiful Burst of Fire that drachnids seem to like casting. 32 HP is *nothing* in Dreadlands, even at level 39. Of course, this was almost two years ago, so the caster NPC overhaul may have changed this, but judging from the inanity of deathbone magi in PoD I doubt it.

In EverQuest, resists are often an all-or-nothing. For instance, the resists simply don't matter when you're exp grinding, because the spells are measly and pathetic. Then you go on a raid and have to put your resist gear on because Maestro AE lifetaps, say, and you want all the MR you can get. There's very little middle ground.
