Henora
Have people noticed additional dot agro when we have burning affliction focus?

Last night my guild went to go kill Seru. He has many many HPs, I prefer to use dots on him instead of DDs. Gwain (my guildmate) was handling the slowing and GRC on my group and so that left me free to dot at will.

I was in full DPS mode, I had no other responsibilities to heal or cure or debuff, a rare thing for this shaman. My first spell set was Pox, Ultor, Blood, Bane of Nife. With this set I was doing about ~120dps or so, before BA. If I had a round where all 4 dots got the full 20% boost it would be about ~150dps. I figured I would run this set and then as the MT gained more agro I would start chain casting velium strike until I went oom! :p I sustained these spells for about 3 minutes until I got summoned. Then I had to tank for about a minute before the dots wore and the MT got agro back.

I waited a couple minutes before resuming dots with just Pox, Ultor and Blood... and throwing on a velium strike from time to time. Gwain then started throwing on his dots. At one point he had our 3 top dots from each of the poison and disease lines. He was slowing, healing and curing. AND I WAS SUMMONED AGAIN, with just 3 dots on, to his 6. I wasn't sitting or low health.

I ended up being summoned twice more during the fight. Gwain was logically generating more agro than me, as he was keeping up basically the same dots as me, but usually a couple more! Gwain agro'd Seru before me! He was healing me when I got summoned (thx!) and he was healing and curing our group. The only difference I could figure was that I have burning affliction and he doesn't. Even our necro who always get summoned didn't get summoned. :(

Has anyone else noticed this kind of big agro difference?

For reference, our MT uses a Bloodied Berserkers Blade (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=30530), with an Anger 3 proc (not using a bane weapon). He used incite regularly and did his agro disc. He has 500MR and resists the stuns consistantly. He is a good tank, I group with him regularly and he knows how to keep agro.

Jinheim
I'm pretty sure that this is not due to BA4. Remember that if your tank is not using a bane weapon versus seru, he isn't doing any melee damage. That makes his aggro dependent entirely on procs and thus streaky and unreliable. More likely than not, you got summoned during a bad streak.

Also, if you started dotting right away and the other shaman didn't start dotting until even a minute later, that's a HUGE difference in aggro, and you said it was several minutes. Aggro doesn't go away if you stop dotting for a while. The warrior's taunt can put him at the top of the list, but without actual melee damage, he just isn't generating enough aggro to stay on top of a dotting shaman.

(Numbers made up). After the warrior has been meleeing for a while, he's at say... 5000 aggro. Then you start throwing on dots. The warrior is generating 2000 aggro per minute, but your dots are generating 4000 per minute. In two and a half minutes, you're going to get summoned when you pass him at 10000. You stop dotting and as your dots wear off, you stop generating aggro and he can taunt to get back on top at lets say 15000 aggro. Then, the other shaman starts dotting. He's been doing a little bit of healing and slowing, but those activities are pretty inconsequential compared to poison dots. He starts dotting and is putting out a whopping 6000 aggro per minute, but he was at ~2000 aggro to start with. Thus, he isn't going to be getting summoned unless the fight goes on for a lot longer. However, you are still near the top of the aggro list, and you started dotting again. It doesn't take long before you once again pass the warrior and get summoned.

The moral? Use a knight to tank Seru and camp out every 5 minutes to clear aggro.
