Trevurg
The "kunark problem" that nobody seems to be bringing up was the experience system at the time.

It was generally known that if you were a ranger / paladin / SK / bard, you took more experience to level. Troll SKs had the worst of it, taking something like 40% more experience to get a level.

Nobody really cared. If you made yourself a TSK, people called you a masochist but that was about it.

Then came ShowEQ, and people discovering that, when you GROUPED with that Troll Shadowknight, he got 140% of his "split" of the XP from any one kill - and he took that extra 40% from the GROUP's experience. Everyone else got 92% of their "split" of the XP - and you got less and less with every hybrid you added.

That's when hybrids - not just knights - had the worst time, because they a) took experience from their groupmates and b) didn't bring anything to the table worth losing that XP for.

Verant did two things:

First: They changed the way experience was divided in a group. It went from being divided based on the ratio of character's XP to a ratio based on levels. This eliminated the penalty of grouping with a TSK.

Second: They admitted that hybrids weren't actually powerful enough to deserve the XP penalty, so they engineered it out of the game by having hybrids get more XP per kill.

They did a bunch of things after that - got rid of the double whammy mana penalty, improved 2 hander damage, introduced a bunch of hybrid-only gear, and made hybrid-unique spells that they didn't share with the parent class.

But the biggest "fix" to hybrids was to make it so that, if you grouped with one, you didn't automatically level slower.

Now the point of all that:

Currently, if you use a warrior as your group's tank, you will kill slower. People have to watch their damage output or they will get aggro off the main tank, which wastes healer mana and causes death downtime.

If you have a warrior in your group, You Get Less Experience.

If you have a paladin / SK tank, You Get More Experience.

It's the hybrid XP penalty all over again.

I don't want to see knight classes nerfed. I have way too many friends who play hybrids, and I have SK and bard alts in their low 50s.

I DO want the game to be in a state where an XP group gets the same amount of experience regardless of the class of the tank.

Because, as it stands now, even after playing a warrior for 65 levels, I won't take one as a tank in any pickup XP group I'm in with my shaman, unless I know the warrior personally.

That's broken.

There are three dimensions to a high-level EQ character, in no particular order:

1. Utility on raids
2. Utility in a group
3. Soloability

if you want to add a fourth add

4. Farmability - ability to farm greens for tradeskills/quests/profit

Warriors score high on #1, low to medium on #2 (Pally/SK more desireable), and off the bottom of the chart on #3 and #4. Warriors were never designed to solo, but at LEAST they should be able to kill greens better. And they need a definate boost in #2. Warriors shouldn't have to continue to get the short end of the stick everywhere except raids.

My suggestions for adjusting the class?

1. Give Warriors an ability like Assassinate that has a chance to kill a green mob instantly. This is not likely to affect any high-level raid encounters, but it sure would boost their farmability, and everyone has to do a certain amount of that. Yes, they already get Finishing Blow, but that requires the mob to be at low health. Another alternative would be to give them a brand-new and very generous damage table for fighting greens.

2. Give Warriors a second taunt-like ability. Well-equipped warriors got BoCs or similar weapons that proc aggro, three expansions ago. Those abilities, perhaps on a lesser scale, should be standard equipment by now, just like the spells from two expansions ago are standard equipment for casters.

3. Warriors need better damage mitigation when NOT using defensive. POP mobs just eat them up too fast. This should be phased so that lower-AC warriors see more benefit - i.e. Time-equipped warriors probably don't need the help. This might take the form of a rune-like ability to limit the effectiveness on longer raid encounters, or a straight Bulwark of Vie-type AA in addition to defensive disc.

4. Warriors need a downtime self-healing ability that works faster than bandaids. This ability should take 100% of the warrior's concentration, like bandaging does, so he can't do it during combat or while pulling, but it would reduce the downtime after a fight if no healer is available. Perhaps simply an AA to increase the sitting mana regen by, say, 50 or 100 per tick for a specific duration would do it.

5. Increased DPS but ONLY if the warrior is the focus of the mob's attention. Basically a clickable AA ENRAGE functionality that causes all of the mob's hits to generate a high-damage melee attack back. The fact that the warrior must be the focus of the mob's attention means that this ability won't unbalance raid encounters, but the extra DPS should, for the duration of an AA, bring the warrior's DPS up to, say, that of a monk or rogue. This would increase the warrior's desirability in groups AND boost the solo/farm ability without breaking raid-level encounters. Tune it so that the monks and rogues don't get shafted by messing with the duration and reuse time.
