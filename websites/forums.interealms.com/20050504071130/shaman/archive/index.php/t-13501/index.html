It takes a lot of work by many people of differing talents to put out a game title. Keeping a complex system up and running while making major changes is one of the hardest projects in ANY field of endeavor. I have done both of these - game titles and complex systems - albeit not under as much public pressure as SOE does, and I think they are doing an OK job.

I was project lead for the SAS/C Compiler for AmigaDOS way back in the day. We caught a LOT of flak from the developer community for lack of certain features, or problems in subsystems or libraries, etc. The people most affected by these shortcomings just couldn't understand why we didn't just FIX them - it would only take a few days. Well, in fact, some of the fixes DID just take a few days - but they had to stand in line behind other fixes or improvements. We were constantly doing triage on the product to find where we'd get the most bang for the buck on changes. Some recognizably desireable projects got put on the back burner because a major rewrite was in the pipe that would remove any need for a minor fix; because it would affect only a small number of users; because we thought it would take too long (it's easy for YOU to say it'll only take 2 days, if I know the code and think it'll take 2 weeks, my estimate is the one we use.)

Litgruv has it right - if you're that filled with bile over SOE's handling, take a break. Or quit. It's not healthy. Me, I'm still having fun.
