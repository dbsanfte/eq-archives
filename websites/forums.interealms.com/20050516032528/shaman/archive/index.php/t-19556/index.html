Ruatonim
These are the only uses I can figure out (all of them are highly situational for most of us):

1. You have Time's Antithesis and want a back up slow spell.

Advantages: Resist modifier and fast cast time make it superior to turgurs when something is resisting.

Disadvantages: The slow is lesser and is only 1.5 seconds faster than clicking the TA. It only lasts 1.5 minutes, if you are slowing multiple mobs you will end up clicking the TA to overwrite the spell or casting the new slow again.

Overall: Situational at best, and probably not worth the spell gem.

2. You are on a raid and want to slow things that are highly resistant.

Advantages: -40 resist mod above and beyond any other debuffing already on the MOB (tash/malxxx/OOS/etc).

Disadvantages: It's still only a "pre-slow" and you'll still be chaining Turgurs (most likely) for the extra slow. It only lasts 1.5 minutes.

Overall: Probably the ONLY real use of the spell, and it might make things a little more smooth for slowing.

3. You are in a group and slowing after applying malos is usually not worth the mana.

Advantages: Resist mod makes it unlikely (but still possible) mobs will resist the slow. Mana saved on malos (not to mention time). Quick cast time allows us to canni4 once extra (unless you raid and got chaotic pain, but I'll leave that ripoff of a spell for other threads).

Disadvantages: (hello, multiple resists on certain things even after the -55 on malos!). Multiple mobs in camp means you either have another slow up anyway (or you have a TA, and that is discussed above).

Overall: This is the weakest application of the spell. Group with a 65 beastlord and you WILL see "Your (worthless slow spell) will not take hold" eventually.
