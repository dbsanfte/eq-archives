Eredril
The reason you are hitting so hard with the 2HS is because 2HS weapons carry a damage bonus based on the Delay of the weapon. The larger the Delay, the larger the bonus. The damage bonus you see on your Bow and Arrows has absolutely no effect when calculating Archery damage. Your Archery damage is based on your Bow Damage, Arrow Damage, and Archery Skill.

You are getting low hits because of your level and Archery Skill. The hits you stated are perfectly normal for your equipment, level, and skill. You won't see a big increase in damage until you start getting Double Damage hits on stationary, unrooted mobs at level 51+. The 500+ point hits you hear about are from Rangers using the Trueshot Discipline (lvl 55+) and/or have trained in the Archery Mastery and Endless Quiver AA abilities (lvl 59+).

Eredril
As far as I know from all the data I have seen, this is how it's broken down.

ATK increases te amount of average to maximum damage hits you score. ATK does not increase the maximum you can hit for, nor does it make you miss less often. ATK is calculated by Offense Skill, Weapon Skill, Strength, and ATK buffs.

The maximum damage you can hit for is calculated by the Dmg on the weapon, Offense skill, and Weapon skill. Nothing else can make you hit any harder.

Dexterity increases your chances of scoring a critical hit and making a weapon proc. I have also heard that dexterity also makes you miss less often, but I've never seen anything to prove that, nor have I noticed missing less myself with 255 Dex.

How often you hit or miss is determined by your Offense skill, Weapon skill, and the AC of the mob you are fighting. As I mentioned above, I have heard rumors that Dex also effects this, but have never seen any evidence of that.

Coincidentally, if you'd like to know what your ATK is with your Bow, then put a Piercer in your Primary hand that has the same amount of Strength and/or ATK stats on it as the 1HS that you normally use. Since our Piercing and Archery skill caps are identical, this should give you the actual ATK rating that your Bow has. That's assuming that you've maxed Piercing and Archery or that your skills in both are identical.
