You know what would be really funny...if you could give caster pets a mod rod.

As far as resistances go, our pet's resistances are supposedly so incredibly low that buffing their resistances makes no difference. I've buffed mine with resistances plenty of times, and personally have never noticed a difference.

As to buffs on the pet, unless I am going after a particularly tough one-shot spawn, I generally give them only buffs that I can cast off my armor(riotous health, and JBB armor earlier in my career), SOW if outside(can make a BIG difference in how fast a mob dies or if it gets far enough away from me to poof back to spawn point), and the pet haste spell.

I figured out on many mobs and camps that I would actually spend less mana conjuring a new pet than I would refreshing buffs on the old one, and since I didn't notice much improvement really after casting buffs, I didn't bother.

Now that I have torpor, though, I will generally put focus on the dog, too. The strength seems to make a bit of an improvement in the damage he does, but more importantly, the hitpoints are a nice safety net in case I get distracted or he takes a few bad rounds of damage. Torpor makes the difference to me, because now mana is very trivial; all the more so with canni 5.

When fighting the great saprophyte, though, I buff him up good -- focus, shifting shield or whatever that AC buff is, regrowth, pet haste, riotous health.

Sometimes I'm even smart enough not to try to cast them on him when I'm out of the water and he's not. :P
