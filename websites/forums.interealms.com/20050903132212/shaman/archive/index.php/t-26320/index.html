Ungkor
Hmm. Been meaning to do this for a while, but I think I might make a branching AA tree flowchart type thing.

The order of the AA's you choose really depends on what type of content/roles you expect yourself to be in.

There are obvious basics: run/regen, canniV, mgb (only if raiding).

But then you might ask yourself, ok, self:), should I get lighting reflexes or advanced healing...

Its not so straight forward. If you solo mainly, you might want lighting reflexes over healing, as you'll be using a heal over time on yourself for the most part, and advance healing won't help you that much.

If you expect to do lots of ldon's as the main healer, or are part of a raiding guild, then yeah, healing AA's > defensive AA's.

Other things: If you are mainly grouping and solo'ing, you probably won't want to bother with max radiant cure (or the next level). I rarely feel the need to cast that just in groups (you have rgc, poison and disease cures, as well as pure spirit no counter cures already).

But if you are in a raiding guild, then you'll want max radiant cure, as well as max ancestral aid asap.

I also disagree with a poster above who said not to get buff duration AA's.

You have to remember, that Torpor/Quiessence/Spirtual Serenity are considered buffs. Maxing buff duration AA's extends the healing power of these hots.
