personally, i found ldon was a nice new realm of hunting for my alts, because i had badly tired (at the time) of the oasis-loio-fm circuit, hph-hk-solb circuit or the paludal-neth-echo circuit. here was a varied and changing circuit with flavors (snowy death, undead crypts, scorching desert, etc). granted the dungeons got old relatively fast because you ended up seeing the same cookie-cutter sections OVER and OVER again. what would have been a better approach, i feel is if the instanced zones had actually constructed the dungeon at spawntime, thus maps would have been rendered useless. you could tell they were constructed by hand out of about 8 different pieces (for each venue), rearranged slightly and given different decor (boxes, fires, etc.). why not randomize it, thus there would be upwards of 100's of possibilities (ruling out the pathalogical constructions, since combinatorically, there would be well over billions of possible constructions) to challenge people at each venue (camp).

but i have to aggree, as designed, ldon was fun, but it grew tiresome quickly. personally, though, i have to vote for pop as the worst expansion ever. the books completely nerfed the old world, and before they added lvl flagging for tier1/2, having people pay $30 for an expansion that was, in effect, unreachable for about 80% of the eq population (including the huge discrimination in the PoJ trials [it is virtually impossible to get a pickup group for the execution trial as a shaman because, for the most part, we're pointless on it. i had to be carried through, in effect by my guild]) as least with kunark, velious, luclin and ykesha, you could reach about 80% of the content without some sort of flagging or special keys. putting out new content that is geared primarily for raid guilds is just poor marketing. why not split the expansions, putting out the general (fix bugs and introduce new features) expansions separately from the (new uber content for the power gamers) expansions. but thats just my 2cp worth.
