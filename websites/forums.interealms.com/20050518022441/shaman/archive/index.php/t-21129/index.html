Goldark
Life is tough for a shaman at your age, there's no denying it.

Ideally you would be tanking mobs in teh face (i.e. getting up close and meleeing them) whilst you use walking sleep and disempower to reduce their power, and DoTs to wear them down. However, you need reasonable gear for this to work effectively, and you may not have the funds for this yet. It is the best method if you can take the hits, as it keeps your defensive and offensive skills up whilst also adding more damage - and faster kills = more xp.

If you can't stand one-on-one against mobs, use root to keep them away from you and use your DoTs to do the damage. Envenomed breath, which you get at 24, gives some reasonable dps. For longer fights combine this with affliction, which whilst low dps offers good mana efficiency if it lasts the full duration. You can often hold off a couple of mobs at a time whilst the DoTs slowly work away. Alternatively if there's a wide open space you can just kite (run away from) the mob whilst the DoTs wear it down.

Keep regen up on yourself all the time, and cannibalise for more mana when your health is good. Don't worry about maintaining all your other buffs all the time, the key ones are sow, regen, AC and haste.

Hope that helps a little.

Pre fight buffs at your level I would go with the following:
Protect
Spirit of the Ox
Spirit of Cat
Regeneration

Protect and Cat will improve your AC, and OX your HP so they are worth keeping up.

For the fight itself I would use:
Evenomed Breath
Affliction
Frenzy
Root
Walking Sleep
SoW
Healing
Regeneration

If you have room, ie won't get adds, root the mob then hit it with Affliction and E-Breath. Use your disease base first as it lasts longer and takes longer to do the damge. Both dots will do dmg when cast so be ready to reroot if need be. Hit the mob with walking sleep, and yourself with Frenzy. By now root should have broken, tank the mob preferably with a weapon that procs. When you hit 50% hp, root the mob back off and heal yourself. Reapply your dots as needed.

Canni back your mana after the fight, rinse and repeat.

BlargX
Honestly, whether you can solo or not doesn't matter much. Shamans who solo too much, especially at early ages, tend to have glaring faults. Note that I'm not saying they'll ADMIT them. Far from it. I expect to get flamed. Just that they have them.

First, you won't have good grouping skills. Shamans have a vast array of spells, and usually people will want you to cast every one of them, most every second, and then do it again immediately. Part of being a shaman is making judgment calls and telling people to shut up so you can do your job. Especially at low levels, when your spells are mostly single target(they were all single target when I was young, but I don't know if those days have completely passed), cost a lot of mana, and wear out very quickly, you have to make mana choices while still not enraging the idiots and know-it-alls around you. It's a tough job. You need to learn to get that rhino hide and figure out things under pressure.

Many shaman do NOT, so don't assume you will. Many shaman are notorious for worrying about their pitiful DPS and not healing people instead of letting DPS do their job and keeping them alive enough to do it, for instance. Show-off shamans are a dime a dozen, and you don't want to get to the end game and still be that way because of false notions in your head and an ego that's never had to grapple with group psychology and dynamics and general ignorance.

Second problem with non-grouping shamans is most people who make shaman twinks are melees who think of shamans as someone to supplement their melee toon or just as if they were pure casters. There's little distinction in many people's minds between any toons that have mana. So what happens is, they listen to the wrong people or pridefully and confidently make up their own minds that shamans are all about wisdom, and make druid-type shamans. They root/rot their way up through their levels, have no hitpoints or plus-mana items to speak of, no weapons skills, and especially no defensive skills, since the entire focus of their character becomes simply to avoid being hit.

Then, when they hit level 51 and suddenly get a huge surge in aggro, and find their defense 50 points too low, they get killed almost every time they slow.

At this point they either abandon their shamans completely, or they become horrible shamans. They refuse to slow because they are afraid they might get aggro. They refuse to heal because they're afraid they might get aggro. They run out of range of anything the party is doing because they're convinced every mob that's incoming means a rez for them unless they can fool the group into accepting that they're really not there to do anything at all.

Group up, learn your spells, and GET HIT. Sometimes you absolutely will have to solo to get hit enough to keep your defense high. But don't get too addicted to soloing. The experience is not that great these days, and progressing through the different spells and group make ups you bump into as a shaman is an invaluable primer on how to spend your mana and when, how to crowd-control mobs to meet the dangerous needs of the moment, etc.

Soloing is fine, but it's very easy to make a truly terrible, ignorant shaman by soloing too much. The shaman is a complex character, full of potential, and has a job rife with difficult, contradictory decisions on what to do with his mana and time -- and how to deal with people who think that just because he has so many spells, he can and should cast every single one of them, on demand, every time. Like a necro or a druid who solos too much, you can very easily become a terrible shaman doing that. It's even more likely, because unlike those guys, your duties are much more complicated and often more vital, and expectations of you and your skills will be far, far higher.

Solo to keep your defensive up and when you have to. But don't think you'll solo your way to high levels without losing a LOT of invaluable knowledge and experience, no matter what class you've played and ubered all up before.

If anything, your past experience with another class is more likely to make you a bad shaman than help you. Get with people ASAP; because shaman is an easy class to see the distinctions in who is good and who is bad, to a knowing eye. People will be quite happy to leave you behind if you suck, unless they're desperate. You don't want to have to rely on desperation to get a group.
