Zyphyr
There are several basic methods that I have seen people use for organizing their books:

1)They go in in whatever order they got scribed and never get moved. Not real an organizational plan - just mentioning it for completeness.

2)Alphabetical. It works for some people, but I personally can't see it.

3)Broken up by spell type : Example : Buffs on page 1/2, Heals/Cures on 3/4, DoTs on 5/6, etc...

4)As #3, but with page 1/2 holding the most commonly swapped in spells (those spells are not with the others of their type).

5)As #4, but with the 'basic' post-rez spell lineup on 1/2

6)A combo of 4 and 5

Those are all I can think of off the top of my head, but there may b some others I have missed.

personally I went with putting the stuff I use the most at the front of my book. though I didn't really do this until lvl 50 or so. just keep the BEST version of that spellline in the front and throw the rest to the back somewhere

mostly buffs/auxilary type spells on the first 4 pages (heals buffs, group buffs, gate etc)

debuffs/dots/sight spells/levi/eb/ root type things on the next few pages

resist buffs,cure disease/blind/etc on the next few pages...

I think my first 10 pages or so are spells I use fairly often, and everything else is just thrown in the back somewhere. and then there's the quirks where I only had room for one DD on the page with my dots, so I've got my best ice nuke there, and my best poison nuke is on page 13. and I just have to remember it's there

6) Page 10 = Cures

Valrik
Page 1/2 = Healing/regen spells, Canni spells. Latest and greatest versions of each at the front. Older versions get swapped to page 3/4.

Page 5/6 = Shield-type spells (HoS, Spirit Shield, Resist buffs). Old get moved to 7/8.

Page 9/10 = Buffs (Stat buffs) Old get move to 11/12

Page 13/14 = DD spells. Old to 15/16.

Page 17/18 = DoT/Rain spells. Old 19/20

Page 21/22 = Speed (SoW, slow). Old 23/24

Page 25/26/27/28 = Misc (Summon food/water, Invis, Summon dogdog, etc.)

Page 29/30 = Vision spells (Ultravision, See Invis) [I'm a big dumb barbarian..'fraid of the dark].

Last pages filled with very ver low level stuff that I'm too lazy to delete.

Have on my 3rd hotkey bank 8 hotkeys set up:

1. Heal = /book 1
2. Shield = /book 5
3. Buff = /book 9
4. DD = /book 13
5. DoT = /book 17
6. Speed = /book 21
7. Misc = /book 25
8. Sight = /book 29.

When I need to swap out Alacrity for Gate, for instance, I forget Alac, go to my 3rd Hotkey bank (SHIFT-3), hit 7 (/book 25), and mem Gate. Took about a half hours worth of work to set it up, and if you're switching between the new UI and the old UI (as I had to do once or twice right when the new UI went live) it'll take you forever and a day to reset your spells.

I'm essentially type #4. Page 1/2 is commonly used but not always memmed stuff like Charisma, Lev, EB, Regrowth & Group Regrowth, Acumen, Cann, Bind, Shrink, Grow, etc.

Page 3 is Buffs, page 4 is Group Buffs.

Page 5/6 are offensive spells.

Page 7/8 are a lot of miscellaneous stuff, like imbues, annul magic, cures, creeping vision. This is also where my always-memmed spells go, like Gate, Root, and Chloroblast.

Page 9/10 are the level 49 and under buffs for casting on folks too low to take the 50+ versions.

Then way in the back are all my old spells sorted by school of magic (abjuration, alteration, etc.) and then grouped by spell line (each stat buff together, all the rage spells together, all the DDs together, etc). I'd never delete any of them as I still find every now and then that I can use something I never thought I'd need again.

This has worked very well for a good 10 levels now.
