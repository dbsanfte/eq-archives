My theory is that each level requires the exact same amount of experience, however, as you level up, if you continue to kill the the same mobs, they give less and less experience per kill due to the level difference between you and the mob. It seems to be about 5% percent per level.

For example: Lets say you have a level 62 and a level 61 in group killing a mob. The level 61 will get 5% more experience then the level 62 due to the relative level between themselves and the mob they just killed.

Thus, experience would seem to slow down 5% per level from 61 to 65.

Sorronn
Death mechanics - In EQ when you die you lose 11% of the prior level's total experience cost. This is what made death in the "post-hell" levels so bad. Since the hell level was so large, often 2-3 times the size of a normal level, death in the next level would be very large.

The fact that you are losing 4% in 61 means that 11% of 60 = 4% of 61. Do the math but 61 sounds almost 3 times the size os 60. And losing 7% in 62, which equals 11% of 61, means that 62 is about 75% larger than 61.
