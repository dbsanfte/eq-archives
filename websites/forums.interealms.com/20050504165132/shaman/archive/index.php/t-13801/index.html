Quinner
Having recently placed my shaman on Quellious into semi-retirement, I've taken up residence on Stromm. It is a fascinating place with no real uber-twinks running around. Banded is being sold in the bazaar and forging weapons is a viable trade.

On the server, there is a certain guild that is racing to the top with all possible speed. It is quiet possible that by the end of the summer, all of their members will be 65. They will most likely be the first guild on the server to make it into the Plane of Time.

It made me start thinking about the way people play the game. What is their motivation? Is it to reach the top and have all of the right numbers pop up on their screen? How much of the game will they miss in their race to the top? Their webpage states that the guild will not be helping members with their epics since the majority of them are outdated. Is that the only reason to pursue the epic? Because it adds certain numbers?

SOE has stated that they will be adding new content to EQ. New dungeons are the focus of the next expansion. I have to wonder, however, how many players will experience the wonders of them? What will become of those zones that don't offer the most efficient experience or "phat l3wt?"

I'm having fun experimenting with some new classes on Stromm (One of which certain Shamans would disapprove) and have decided to make it a personal goal to visit every unkeyed/unflagged zone in the game before hitting level 60. Not just to pop in and pop out but to explore and hunt.

All the discussion makes me reflect on my Paper & Pencil RPG experiences. Sure we went through a phase when we had to have the super high-level characters but the most fun came when we got over that and just enjoyed playing our characters at all levels. (Levels 5 - 9 seemed to be the most fun for all classes in AD&D.) Can this same thing be true in EQ? Should I be worried about how fast I level or shoudl I just enjoy playing the game?

So I have to ask the rest of my Shaman Brethren - What type of player are you?

Dandi
I'm just as goal based as anyone else, but my goals are all over the place. I do get that thrill from a Ding! (new spells!!) or AA point or sweet new piece of gear as much as anyone else. But I also love raising my tradeskills, maxxing factions, completing quests (however little I need the rewards), exploring a new zone, making something I've never before made, etc.

So all of that, plus RL commitments, means I'm not uber by high level standards. After almost four years of play on the same character I'm 61 with 7 AAs. But I sometimes went weeks or even months between levels while I was doing things that that gave little or no exp. I am seeing some upper level raid content, though. Not Vex Thal, but ToV and tier 2 and some of the easier dragons. And my guild is going strong, slow but steady, close to taking on harder dragons and tier 3 and VP, etc. From talking to my friends in uber guilds, I know I enjoy my guild's easygoing raids more than they usually enjoy their zillionth attempt at Rallos, so I don't regret my situation.

So my advice always is to just do what's fun. For some people, exp grinding and power raiding are truly fun, and that's great. But if you're not in the mood for that, try other things until you find something fun. You're playing the game for your own enjoyment, so it's pointless to force yourself to the exp grind if that's not keeping your interest. :)
