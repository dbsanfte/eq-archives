SisterLi
I thought Shoa was saying that while some posts are "good and constructive," some are just whining. I read that as arguing that tone makes a difference. It's not that the content of your post is verboten -- i.e. that we don't want to see a post arguing that shaman are under powered. The point is that the tone of many of the posts is not helpful. Some of this comes from months and months of frustration, and is understandable. But it is counterproductive nonetheless.

I'm not certain that I'd use the term whiney to describe them, as it does not capture the essence of why they are objectionable. Whiney works as a description for some, but is off for many.

Hence it is not responsive to say "But our whining got us lots of good results, and we want more." I'd presume that your *arguments* got you good results, in spite of the climate created by the "whining". Presentation matters. Attitude matters.

I support (and have suggested before) the idea of creating a separate section for Class Balance, Fixes, and Future Plans for the Shaman. Might help lighten the overall tone in the general section, and place our many problems into perspective.
