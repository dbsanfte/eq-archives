Baz3
The charms I have seen have, uh, interesting aspects. I am intrigued by the idea of items that vary in effectiveness/power based on the PC's skills or whatever. But I have not seen much in the way of useful or desirable effects.

For charms to boost dex, or agi, or str, or cha seems kind of disappointing. This board has hosted many a thread on how useless or marginal stat buffing is.

In fantasy worlds charms can be items of great power (okay, everything in the fantasy epics has great power), or at least useful.

So - here we have a new slot, and a chance for a new concept. I do not think we can reasonably talk about huge boosts in power, like a priest item that gives the wearer the warrior defensive discipline, or a wizards mana gathering trick. So - what is reasonable (stretchy word that) and desirable?

A big boost in WIS or INT sounds nice, but it is apparently not that hard to approach the caps on those stats now. Flowing Thought is always desirable, but maybe too powerful. But it does seem to be a natural place for focus effects, doesn't it? or right click effects?

Is this the way for shaman to finally get Improved Invisbility? Or a non-potion Invis to Undead?

Would a charm of cornocopia allow you to remove the food and drink from your bags?

Or raise the wearer's resistance to curses - not MR or Dr - just curses.

or increase the wearer's resist to nukes or AE's? pvp would love that - say roll twice and take the higher roll 8)'

I just would like to see creativity on the effect similar to the creativity used to tie the effect to weapon skills or whatever.
