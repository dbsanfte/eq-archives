Ruatonim
If they hadnt removed the ZEM this is what would have happened:

mob gives 100 exp (7.3% aaexp).... assuming pop ZEMs at 37% (more or less not really important). And lets say for the sake of argument this mob gives 10% aa per solo kill ( i.e. 137exp= 10% aa exp gain)

Assuming you were in a 6 person group you got (137*1.2)/6 or 27.4 exp per person. this would be 2% aaexp.

after the patch (assuming ZEM left in) mob gives each person in a 5/6 person group (137*1.8)/5 or 49.32 exp. this would be 3.6% aaexp.

You'll notice this is roughly half the exp the mob was originally worth, and more than 1/3rd the exp the mob is worth after ZEM is applied. This doesnt even begin to account for whatever the bonus is for mobs close/above your level.

currently, the sample mobs give (100*1.8)/5 or 36 exp per person in the 5/6 person groups. this would be 2.6% aaexp.

This is what happens when they try to encourage grouping, if they kept the ZEM in place and gave the grouping exp bonus; everyone would be 65/full aa's pretty quickly and we'd all cancel our accounts. If they truly want to encourage smaller groups they could just increase the gain for lesser people so its at least the same as it was before the patch..this shouldnt be hard to implement...whether they will is debateable.

Anyway to sum this up...you get ~30% MORE exp now in a group of 5 or 6..i didnt do the math for lesser groups as i'm not completely sure what the bonus is for them, i'm sure its nearly status quo on 3/4 person and lesser exp for solo/duo. The nerf was a by product of them wanting to raise group exp but not raise it THAT dramatically that it becomes problematic. Everyone will still probably be able to get more exp solo than in a group, but now it wont be quite as much, and it wont be the same exp you got yesterday.
