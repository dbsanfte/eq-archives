Jorgan
Our guild has been comfortably clearing NToV for the past few weeks, working on ST keys, talking about starting the camps for the emp key in SRAA, and eventually making its way into VT. The end goal is to make it into the elemental plains and I'm sure most have visions of eventually entering PoTime.

Even before LDoN there was a lot of discussion about whether it was a better or worse idea to follow that line of progression or skip VT (and possibly Sraa) altogether and just try to gear up through PoP to get to and through the elemental planes. Some of the considerations leading guilds in this direction was the massive competition for Sraa mobs and even VT.

Assuming that the goal is to get to the elemental planes and time, and with the kinds of equipment that you could gear your guild with from LDoN merchants, would you still want your guild to go the Sraa and VT route to get to elementals and time? Or would it you rather gear up through LDoN missions.

I know that LDoN equipment, even fully augmented, is NOT at the level of VT loot, but it's pretty damn close. (See http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=16660 for a sample of what's possible for shamans.) The advantages to bypassing Sraa and VT that I see are: (1) People will actually get a lot of xp going through LDoN, and get a lot of really useful AAs. (2) The adventures are actually fun, and even after the 100th adventure, they will probably still be more fun than camping shards. (3) Avoids the timesinks of camping shards, pieces for bane weapons, emp key. (4) Avoids the sometimes nasty inter-guild competition for mobs in Sraa and VT. (5) For individual members, the certainty that you can pick the equipment most needed and most useful for you. (6) Avoids the uncertainty of spawn times, contested mobs--LDoN adventures are always available, Sraa and VT mobs aren't--particularly for an EST guild that has a four-six hour raid window each night.

The disadvantages of gearing up through LDoN: (1) No raid experience and refinement of encounter strategies that you get by actually raiding. (2) Possible loss of cohesion as a guild if everyone is either doing their own thing in LDoN or even just grouping. (3) Never getting to experience what Sraa and VT are like. (Or is this an advantage?) (4) Not being able to get some really exceptional equipment. (5) Will not get any status or reputation points from prospective applicants who value going through Sraa and VT.

I'm sure there are other advantages and disadvantages that I'm not seeing at the moment. Those are the ones that I see right now. I also realize that it would take a significant amount of time to gear up through LDoN. I'm not sure if it would take a longer or shorter amount of time.

So, again, assuming the "end" goal is getting to Time, and if you had it all over to do, or even if your guild is in a similar situation as ours, which would you do, and why?

Thanks

Juujuu
I would still absolutely go the VT route.
Here are my reasons why:
1. Focus effects. There are soooo many focus effects that you can get in VT. If you try going straight for Elemental Planes, or if you gear up in LDON, you flat out will not get nearly as many focus effects on your armor as if you stop in VT first. You can get all sorts of improved damage, burning affliction, spell haste, extended range, and mana preservation types of effects in VT (but have different names for whatever reason).
2. Flowing Thought. VT is packed with flowing thought items. There's the ft4 belt, 2 different ft4 cloaks, ft2 floating rune, all sorts of ft items from AHR including the awesome neck, ft1 mark of judgement, and whatever else I haven't remembered.
3. Ancient spells. For some classes, if you have your level 65 spells, the ancient spells are unnecessary. For other classes, you will use your ancient spells anyway. I know that when I'm trying to go for fast damage, I use both the 65 poison dot and the ancient 60 poison dot. I know some other classes still get a good amount of use out of their 60 spells too.
4. Relative ease of acquiring loot. Once you know your way around VT fairly well, getting loot there is sooooo easy. The named mobs hit from 600-about 1400? You have simple cheal rotations for many of the named mobs. You can slow the mobs that don't allow for cheal rotations. When you compare this to the difficulty of say killing Bertox or Mith Marr for comparable loot, it's a breeze. AE rampage is a nightmare to learn to deal with. Mobs that hit for 2000 a pop and have AEs that really HURT are rough. If I was wanting to settle into "loot farm mode", I'd want the easy fights and not the hard ones.
5. Time investment vs reward.
-When you raid stuff in POP before ele planes like Bertox or Mith Marr or Saryrn or Carpin, you are looking at doing about 2-4 hours worth of fighting (plus travel time) for about 3 pieces of loot on average. Those are hard fights. You need at least 60 people on each of those raids if you don't have VT gear in the first place and are gearing up off them. 60 people * 3 hours on average = 180 man hours for 3 pieces of loot which equates to about 60 man hours of work for a piece of loot.
-When you go to VT with those 60 people, you come back with about 30 new pieces of gear off the named, not to mention ancient spells + random drops. In total it will take youa bout 9 hours to clear VT including travel time to VT and getting coh's. 60 people * 9 hours = 540 man hours for 30+ pieces of loot which equates to about 18 man hours of work for a piece of loot.
-Hunting in LDON can be done by just XP groups to gear up. It takes an average of about 90 minutes with travel time to do an adventure for 51 points. To get a piece of gear that's VT quality or better, you end up spending about 5000 points for the gear + augments, which works out to about 100 adventures. 100 adventures * 1.5 hours per adventure = 150 hours of xping (work) for a piece of gear. You have 6 people in the group all adding manhours, but everyone is getting a piece of gear out of the work as well, so it doesn't change the numbers much.
-raiding Luclin targets for loot is going to be a similar time investment as doing POP targets.
-VT is the fastest way to gear up a guild with gear. Raiding other targets is slower, but still faster than gearing up in LDON. Gearing up in LDON is the slowest by far, but on the plus side you also get XP for doing so.
6. The key quest for VT really isn't hard when you look at the difficulties in keeping a guild flagged for the planes. Backflagging is a perpetual nightmare. Every few weeks or months, when you get new recruits, you have to go back and flag every one of them again. You need to drag the entire guild out of the elemental planes or wherever you happen to be raiding, in order to kill targets that will find it difficult to kill more than about 5 people during the course of the entire fight. In comparison, a shaman can obtain solo 9 out of the 10 shards (just needing a bit of help obtaining ssra shard). Shards can be obtained on your own time in xp groups (albeit crappy xp, but it's still xp). Camps for key parts for emp's chamber is another xp camp. The only real raid you need is for the insignias, but luckily the drop rate for insignias is very very high. You can expect to get an insignia approximately every 20 or 30 minutes.
7. Right clickies! There's a bunch of really good right click items in VT that you really don't want to pass up if possible. Right click human illusion, skeleton illusion, fire elemental illusion is there. Shrink items for everyone else, and the one for shaman is there. Right click invis is there for the melees and cleric classes. Right click invis undead is there on an all all earring. There's also necklace of malosi which is one of my most prized possessions. And there's also the call of sky proc orb thingy. I know there's some druid arms that right click (a debuff I think). There's right click c3 sticks for enchanters that are wonderful for getting mana to mass c5 after a wipe. Also, there's summon mass food and water items with good stats on them (though you'll hate them after a while).
8. Vengence items. VT has lots of vengence items for your tanks. Nuff said.
9. You don't have to do the whole clearing in 1 day. 7 hours of raiding straight is rough. Our guild used to do the first 3 or 4 hours of it, then camp out at a safe spot or just leave a coh mage there, and continue the raid again the next day. It's not really so bad.

When you factor the whole risk vs reward vs time investment, I don't think you can go wrong heading to VT.

Zorlac Sabboth
VT is the anti SOE zone, meaning there is a LOT of reward for little time invested.

There are many things lacking in elementals, or some are not lacking but much harder to obtain compared to VT:
- Rings: they are either very rare drop or you have to do insanely long and tough ring events: Air Avatars, Xegony, Coirnav, etc ... VT has many very high quality rings: Ring of Judgement, Ring of Crimson Bull, Ring of Flamewarding. Also since Emperor kill is a prerequisite to VT, you get Azazili's Ring of Analogies too. All this is far easier to obtain than killing Xegony for instance.
- Earrings: same boat as rings. Yes there are a few very nice ones in elementals but they are either insanely rare (Fang of Flame) or again, hard to get like Coirnav event.
- BPs/Robes: No elemental plane item comes remotely close to Aten Robe or something even better: Emperor's robe. BP of speed or Great Shadow Platemail is just superior to anything, no I won't count Rathe BP because frankly it's an event a guild does 3-4 times to flag and never goes back. Sanguine robe is also very nice.
- Legs: nothing in elementals prior to rare dropping elemental legs (again hard to get due to rarity and tough encounters) is as easy to get as Aten legs, Nightcaller Leggings or Leggings of Fiery Might.
- The entire Aten loot table is still extremely powerful when compared to elemental plane drops.
- There also some other different very powerful trinkets: Shawl of Awakenings (Emperor), Staff of Lost Rituals (DoT damage booster focii), Twilight Shroud (Emperor Shawl Light(c)(tm)), Orb of Sky (ranger proc buff), Gloves of Dark Rituals (pet summon focii) and plenty of other items.

VT also supplies guilds with much needed FT, Resists and Vengeance.

IMHO, VT and Elemental planes complement eachother well:
- Get most of caster shoulders, jewelry, BPs and Legs from VT/Emperor.
- Get rest of visible armor from Elementals along with very nice weapon upgrades.

If your guild can have a reasonably good shot at VT, don't hesitate a minute to do it. It's more than worth the effort.

weissblau
Emp requires you to pharm ore in the mines of ssra. He can't be hit by normal weapons.

Well, sort of. My guild discovered that this isn't really all that necessary.

The thing is that although non-bane weapons can't hit the emp, they still generate aggro as if they are hitting him. So if your MT is worried about maintaining aggro, use your BOC/Bloodfrenzy/whatever and screw the DPS.

Back in August when the patch broke bane weapons, my guild tackled the emperor for only our second kill, and were successful with NO bane weapons. Necros and mages propped up the clerics with manafeed and mod rods, nukers and DOTters had a field day, those melee with damage-proccing weapons whacked on him, etc. Took a good bit of time but we won. And the MT kept aggro just fine despite having a DPS of zero.

So while it does benefit you to have more bane weapons, they are not strictly necessary, and it's certainly not necessary for EVERY melee to have 1-2 of them.
