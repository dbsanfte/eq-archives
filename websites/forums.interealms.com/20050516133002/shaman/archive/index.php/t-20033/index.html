deria
So there I was, practicing my alchemy (three points to go until 200) when out of the blue, a very nice guildee sends me a tell asking me if I want my level 65 slow (it dropped for his guild group, which had no shaman).

Of course, I said sure and proceeded to get to the sewers in Barindu where I looted the spell and scribed it.

I played with it for the entire evening during a bertox run (for those who have not done it, its 90 minutes of constant slowing). I kept Balance and Turgurs up, and used whatever was handy at any given moment during the event.

Initially I thought this slow would be a mild improvement over Turgurs, but wasn't too excited. As the night went on, though, I started to get more and more excited by this spell.

The -40 resist adjustment is definitely noticeable. The spell still gets resisted, of course, but on the whole I could definitely see a trend of less resists when I was using it vs. when I was using Turgurs.

The cast time is snappy, but not exceptionally faster than Turgurs if you have good detrimental spell haste (this spell is not affected at all by detrimental spell haste).

The duration was noticeably shorter than Turgur's, but in most situations you'll never have a mob outlive it (unless your soloing).

The difference between 60 percent and 75 percent was... meaningless, actually. When I'm doing an LDON, I normally use Tigirs instead of Turgurs just because I like the AE effect and I'm not too worried about the difference between Tigirs and Turgurs. This seems like a more happy middle ground.

All in all, over the course of the evening, I was very impressed by this spell and I would certainly encourage people to seek it out. It doesnt drop in some obscenely hard to get area, it doesnt cost you 100k, and it really feels like an actual upgrade to our slowing capability (despite the reduced percentage).

I feel like I can slow almost as good as a beastlord now. :)
deria
As a quick follow up to my own post, I checked my log and here is some (rather unscientific but still interesting) information:

Turgurs: 99 casts, 40 resists (40.4 % resist rate)

Balance: 83 casts, 17 resists (20.4 % resist rate)

I did not debuff anything prior to casting either slow (who has time, its the Bertox event!).

The resist numbers may be slightly wacky, because I just brute forced the slows through when I got resisted. (ie, 1 mob might resist 5 times and then no mobs will resist for 10 minutes).

I wouldn't place too much weight on these numbers, but I found them interesting and if they hold true then I'll be very pleased indeed with this spell (since the only time I malos is on highly resistant mobs or on raid targets).

EDIT: It might be significant to note that the distribution of mob levels was on the high side: maybe 60 percent blues, 30 percent evens, 5 percent yellows, and 5 percent reds). Thats a guestimate, but sounds about right.

60% vs 75% is a huge difference on non-mitigating mobs by the way.

75% slow means the mob is doing 25% damage.
60% slow means the mob is doing 40% damage.

40/25 = 1.6

That means a 75% slow will mitigate 60% better then a 60% slow.

Now, on mitigating mobs the math looks far better of course. So far GoD has been pretty mixed - sewers have been mostly fully slowable for me, mountains mitigate, ikkinz its a lot of fully slowables again. But if you use Nihil on fully slowable mobs odds are Turgur's would be better.
