Hey Jaidin, I hear what you're saying about difficulties with NGB in HoT. I think my guild came up with a really great approach to HoT farming. We used a "leapfrog method."

Our guildmaster created a place where every guildmember could list their gear level for each slot (head, boots, arms, bracers, bp, helm, gloves, legs, etc). It was each guildmember's responsibility to keep the table updated if they had an upgrade.

The system we used was:

1) Non planar, lowest level (we had casters in loam, for example)
2) Planar
3) Kunark
4) Thurg
5) Velious quest (SS/Kael)
6) High level raid drop, superior to SS/Kael

So, let's say a pair of chain gloves dropped. The officers would ask the chain wearers to send a tell with their armor level for that slot. Let's say: Ranger01 has level 4 Thurg, Rogue01 has level 2 planar and shaman01 has level 2 planar. Rogue01 and shaman01 would random for the gloves.

A tiny bit of personal accountability has to go into this method. For example, if someone were eligible for 3 leapfrog upgrades in one day (bracer, boots, gloves, for example) over people who have had no upgrades that day, it was considered the honorable and gracious thing to do to bow out for the rest of the raid after receiving an upgrade.

This method is GREAT for gearing up a guild, but it does require some sensitivity. A person that has been in the guild awhile, with really high playtime might have a full set of Thurg (for example). With leapfrog, they might get passed over for upgrade in HoT several times while people newer to the guild would get upgrades to replace sub-Thurg gear.

It did work out very well for us. I was a high playtime person in nearly full Thurg (minus bp) when we first started hitting HoT. Although it did take a really long time, I was full SS by the time we left, and so was nearly everyone else.
