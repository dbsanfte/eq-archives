Unless for some reason they want every class to be identical, it seems that some classes would shine solo, and others would do better in a group. Scrappers and blasters (up to 10 at least) seem to be phenomenal soloers, however build em. My defender and controller really only shine when there's at least one other person with me, though.

EQ seemed to ebb and flow like that - chanter/cleric/dps/tank seemed to cycle from feast to famine and back again over time.

According to the Devs, the differences between classes as far as HP, ability to take damage, and differentiation is very small in the first levels, and players are somewhat considerably overpowered for their opposition to give people time to work out the kinks in their characters and figure out how things work. Starting in the late teens, when Dual-Origin Enhancements start becoming common, you'll start seeing the archetypes fall into their categories a bit better. Tankers, for example, just start to be able to stand around in the middle of huge groups of yellow/whites without being in any real danger (it just takes them forever to kill a group like that).
