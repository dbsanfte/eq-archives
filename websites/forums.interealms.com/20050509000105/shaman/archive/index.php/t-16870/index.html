Giac
The problem with Shamans becoming less and less "competitive" is right in this thread and the misconception of the word balance and how it is supposed to work in EQ.
When you talk about balance, you can't leave out what other classes can do, hell, a class is balanced against another class and in refernce of what that other particular class is capable of.
Immagine a libra for a second, to keep a "balance", you will have to even out both sides constantly, so if the "Shaman-side" retains the same weight, the balance will still shift the second you change the weights on the other side and the balance is gone.

When you take something away from a class or weaken it, you will have to add something else to make up for it. This hasn't been happening. In theory the Shaman has a higher mana regen than the necromancer, in practice, though, both the necromancer and the mage will find themselves with a more mana over time than the Shaman due to the game dynamics.

Solution: Add an AA skill with an adequate timer that allows us to keep one of the spells pertaining to the Cannibalize line up full time, as if it was a buff(just like the necro lich form). We would have the advantage to be able to keep Canni and Clarity buffs up at the same time, yet the timer restriction would keep it from putting us too far ahead of the necro.
or: Introduce an item that casts Can IV without a recast time and without the need to equip it(just like the black fur boots). This will give us an edge in terms of "active" mana regen in comparison to other classes, while keeping us last, when it comes to "passive" mana regen, as they probably have been intending.
