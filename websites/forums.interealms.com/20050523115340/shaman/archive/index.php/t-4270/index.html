Grippa
Here's a few tips from a super slow leveller, so I've had to cope without group buffs for a long time :)

Before the raid starts, tell all the melee classes to send you a tell to "get on the buff list". They never do, so make your own list using /who.

Prioritise buffs. Usually "real" tanks (ie warriors, pallies, SKs) are a higher priority than rangers, rogues. If a ranger's getting hit and having HoS is going to make a difference, then your raid is already wiped.

Dedicate a hot key bank or two to your buff list. I use the first 8 slots to target people, slot for canni and slot 10 for the buff I'm casting.

Make a hot key for each person on the buff list - this hot key will read "/target soandso". Put the high priority buffes on one bank, and the low priority on another.

Stagger your buffs. Most important is probably HoS, so cast that first. Cast it on yourself as a timer, and then a minute later start working though the buff list. Because you have the targeting keys, canni and the buff spell on one bank, it's dead easy to target, cast buff, canni once or twice, and move on to the next target.

10 minutes later ask for a short delay in the pulls so you can do Stamina. 10 minutes later do Agi if you want to.

One of the most important ways of coping, I think, is to prioritise who you buff. A reasonable ranger will say "Fine, I don't NEED HoS, I'll buff my own Strength". Of course if you can, buff everyone, but my guideline is as many as I can buff with a full bar of mana, which is usually about 8-10 casts of HoS (with some cannibalising mixed in). But make a hot key for every melee class in the raid, because the raid leader might decide to switch who's MA or something, or perhaps a cleric shouts "quick heal soandso I am OOM" at a crucial time. You can just flick to your target hotkey bank and heal the person straight away without messing around trying to target them with the mouse.
