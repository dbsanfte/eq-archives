I will discuss the Spell Book organisation mostly as Spell Bar organisation is very much down to preferences.

SPELL BOOK ORGANISATIONS

Idea #1 :
In the left page (new UI) you could put the 8 most useful Spells just as you have them most often in your Spell Bar, in the SAME ORDER. This will help you re-mem your 'regular' spell easily and quickly without checking the order of spells or searching for the spells.

In the right page, you could place the other most useful spells, as well as other Combat Buffs, in DESCENDING ORDER of Refresh Time. Memmed first will be the spells that need refreshoing more often. Such as ThistleCoat. Memmed Last will be the longest duration ones such as Spirit of Wolf or Riftwind's Protection.

When you need to do a re-buff session, remove from 3 to 8 spells off your Spell Bar and mem all the buff spells from the left and right page, skipping the utility spells. Buff yourself. Then re-mem your regular Spell Bar spells from that you have on the left page in the exact order as you have them in your Spellbook, quick and easy, no need to struggle with what spell goes where.

I hope that makes sense :p

PS: All other remaining spells go to the next Spell Book pages, grouped by utility / spell types, as you wish.

SPELL BAR ORGANISATION

Generally I place spells where they feel 'natural' or intuitive.. but I suppose that's down to your type of memory, mine is 'associative'. For example my typical Spell Bar looks like this:

1. Spirit of Wolf (if not me I often drop one on passers by for fun)
2. Healing
3. Tangling Weeds
4. Careless Lighting / Flamelick
5. Camouflage
6. Call of Earth
7. Stinging Swarm / misc. buff for passers-by or a single buff refresh
8. Grasping Roots

1, 2, 3, and 8 are ALWAYS in my Spell Bar. Grapsing Roots saved my life oh so many times :p Tangling Weeds is great just because I hate running after fleeing creatures :P

For a quick single -buff refresh or cast of a spell on other person, I use slot 7 or slot 5.

Pre fight slot 7 will be a Cancel magic or DoT depending on situation.

I liked to use Flamelick at least till level 30, even if it is weak the 'Placebo' factor works for me :) And it's fun. Technically. I suspect sometimes a 4 ac decrease MAY bring the mob's AC down one 'step' in the ac 'slices'.
