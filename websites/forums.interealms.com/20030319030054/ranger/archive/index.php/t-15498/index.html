Akelan
Trials are all about DPS. Slows just are not needed, the mobs go down at the same rate slowed or not, and a good cleric will keep the MT healed well enough.

Forget the DC pet. If you take more than 2 - 3 seconds to break the DC and start killing it, you have lost. Have the chanter do AE Stuns rather than mez, the shaman buff the tanks to the 9's and haste everyone that swings a blade. Use a heal-over-time on the MA immediately before engaging, and between every round ; Pre-heal the damage that will be inflicted on the MA and you get less aggro.

If you have to chase a mob, you've failed. If you have to peel aggro off a caster, you've failed. If you focus on the mobs, lock them down with the stuns and assist the tank, you are there.

Save the Disc for the High Executioner, you'll need WS for him if the MT goes down or the cleric goes OOM. Shaman spam healing anyone besides the MA will make the biggest difference here; and slowing the executioner might help but I have not see it done; he is the only mob that you fight like "normal"; everything else is "yard trash" level but needs to be killed FAST.

Sabine
You can use archery but a few things to keep in mind.

If the cleric/chanter/druid are going to be on a horse, make sure they are shrunk and you are not shrunk. If you lose line of sight, nuke then move to melee. I find that in each wave of exe, it is easier to melee the first of the wave then use arrows on the 2/3/4 of the wave.

Exile Summoned is grand for extra damage on the rock critters.

Getting a druid through the trials will be hard. Chances are the druid will be oom by the third wave just from chain healing the enchanter and cleric. The warrior will probably never see aggro til the prime executioner.

I would make sure the druid has First of Karana, and gets in at least 1 blast per wave. It is a targettable ae that hits 4 but if all 4 mobs are close, it is very efficient compared to upheaval/catastrophe (mana pigs). If the cleric is not going oom from healing the chanter, try having the druid blast twice each wave.

Make sure no one sits, if the casters want to med, have em get the cheapest horse from bazaar and shrink em.

Timing is everything. With the dps of that group, it will be down to the wire on each mob unless you are nailing good bow shots every time.

If you get to the pe, be ready to kite it since the casters will almost certainly be oom, just run the silly pe around the platform in front til folks have mana and make sure folks have their health topped off before engaging. No sense going in with folks at 60% and then the cleric drawing low health aggro after 20 seconds.

Leiker
I've done the Exe alot of times successfully with Archery. As usual in PooP archery is so much better DPS than melee unless you're in the top level of equipment really and the circumstances allows for it - which is does here.

Levi up, stand on the platform (Where the prisoner is) from there, you can move around (And leave platform too and still keep altitude for better LoS)

Keep TS for boss (Or to have WS ready in case the brown stuff hits the big windy thing) - just remember, don't panic! (In big friendly letters) When you get to the Executioner himself, you have all the time in the world - kite him abit if needed, tell druid / shammy to go off damage and just make sure everyone stays alive with patch heals as needed.

Have MT attack asap, no delay in targetting. Assist asap. Make him go something along the line of Rock Named / Alien Named / Rock / Alien. But it's vitally important he NEVER switch target - whatever he targets first, be it intentional or by accident, tell him to stay on it until it is either dead or banished.

CrowdControl is nice - but can be substituted with an extra backup healer - they don't hit all that hard. Just make sure it's a good equipped cleric and/shammies who can take the beating. You shouldn't bother peeling of them, you'll loose too much DPS - if they can't take the beating they need to be more careful. If they can't figure out either solution, they have no business in tier 2+ planes.

Never PingPong. Tell everyone to stay within 5 feets of eachother - running to a mob is a waste of good dps (And you can still get a good shot with bow - it's not like it's a 40+ member raid)

Banish is good. Not needed though.

DPS and Levels are key - everyone 62+ makes a world of difference.

Be a good puppy and heal in the breaks - also mem that cure poison to remove any tashes that landed during the wave.

You will know after wave 1 if your group can make it.

You can nuke if you have to, I personally never do it. Sure our summoned nuke is nice, but I don't use it. Calefaction - forget it really, in the time it casts you have the potential of doing 4 x the potential calefaction damage (Granted, archery can make you do 0 damage too in that time :) but more often than not, am3 outdamages it)

Nothing is more stressing than seeing one of those unlucky 10 misses in a row in a fight on these mobs :D


Pay attention - it's just 15 mins, you can focus for that long :)


Edit: About Charm - well no Dire Charm. Normal charm can be a big boost and a big help.... Just be careful, we failed once cause our enchanter didn't realize he was fully buffed. So when he tried to invis himself to break it, guess what ? :D the 4 secs for him to pick a buff and recast invis made us loose :p
The clear advantage of charm is of course, one less mob to CC or patch heal from, and it adds damage on the first three mobs.
