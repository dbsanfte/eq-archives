Kazim
In easy-medium zones, shaman role is main healer (via slows + heals).

In difficult zones where a cleric or druid is main healer, I typically regard my role as "reducing risk and increasing efficiency". It's comprised of:

- malo on all mobs if there are casters in group
- slow on all slowable mobs
- pet attack on all mobs
- cripple on mobs where it matters (rare)
- haste all melees if no chanter in the group
- other buffs as needed / asked for (ferine avatar for example)
- patch healing non-MA people
- tossing Q on the MA to make cleric CH less risky
- fast healing MA if it looks like CH won't hit in time
- DoT/Nuke mobs as mana / time permits
- crowd control of adds (malo + slow + root park)

You and I are Shammy's. We are the great Jack-of-all-trades. Because of that, our roles aren't as cut and dried as everyone else's. Sure, we'll always slow and buff. However, healing, hasting, pulling, crippling, Nuking, OT'ing and kiting are all other roles that we can take up, as the situation comes up. Always start with your primary roles and then see what the group and situation require. Those other classes are stuck in niches and can only support their group in a set way. We get to be more intelligent about how we help out.
