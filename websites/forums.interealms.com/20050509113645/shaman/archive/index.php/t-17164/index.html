Popu
As 2nd shaman of equal level one I like the idea of having a primary debuffer and primary slower. Primary debuffer loads malo and malos and keeps a slow up, slower loads turgurs, togors, and grummus. This way you can get around the crappy recasts these spells have yet get mobs slowed as quickly as possible. Main debuffer can back you up when he's done. I've never been upset about someone doubling a slow. On Boss mobs (depending on the boss) you may be relegated to patch healer for your Main Tank. And you can try to stick some dots on in between.

This strategy has worked for me when I have consulted with the other shaman and chanters on the raid.

shamuofxev
My guild is usually having 4 - 6 shaman on, and everybody spams slow on incoming. In elemental planes and time, you want a mob slowed "right now", and with multiples coming in you can't always be mana concious and sort out who slows what. And a wasted slow is much less resource waste then having to rez and rebuff.
If you're just getting started with serious raiding, have a shaman channel, and agree on who does malos and who does the first slow. Have the malos guy make a hotkey to your channel when he casts, and you know if you start turgurs a second or so later your slow will hit a debuffed mob. Fast slow, but less chance of resist and shaman mush on the floor.

weissblau
Assign one to MR debuff (malo/s and Malosinia) and one to slows, for singles.

For multiples, if you have add tanks assign a shaman to the add tank/s and one to the main mob. Or go FFA. Depends on how tough the mobs are and how many are inc.

Cripple is also good, usually have the one who did not slow land Cripple to spread the aggro around. Malicious Decay is useful to land disease-based DOTs if you're doing that.

Straight cold nuking or, if conditions are right, AE rains are the best damage methods on a raid. Generally you have lots of mana due to raid buffs so the efficiency difference is not so big.

Also, we tend to assign shaman as group healers on our raids so the druids can nuke more. Especially once you have Quiescence it's quite doable as long as you're not healing a MT.

Goldark
On anything that has a fair chance of resists, I like to have all shaman hitting slow and malosini(a), you want it to land asap on any significant mob.

Having one shaman assigned to malo(s) on inc and then switch to slow/cripple works well for singles, and for multiples assigning shaman to a specific tank makes good sense to avoid mass confusion. By starting with malo(s) they're normally one slow behind the rest so have a bit less aggro, making em good for crippling duties. On very resistant mobs care is obviously still needed.

Split buffs up equally, sta/focus and maybe tali of tribunal in some cases can be split between 3 mgb 65 shaman with ease. If you don't all have the same spells and AA then share it out as appropriate.

Shorter buffs like FA are more of a pain, either do it by groups or by classes. Regen I normally leave to druids :D

Once the mob is debuffed and any buffs needing refreshes are sorted, you'll either be helping out healing (e.g. stick quiessence on rampage tank and chain casts tnargs, let the druids have fun nuking) or you'll be doing whatever dps you can muster. I'd guess the new bane is good in many situations (no poison counters for aggro, yey!) though I don't have it myself. I tend to stick to rains and nukes, its situational as to which is better. Stuff with lots of hp gets the standard DoTs; just take care with aggro from BoS and BoN.

I have a simple but effective FA tracking system.

It requires a pencil (or pen) lined piece of paper and a post-it tape flag.

I write folks name down on the lined piece of paper I'm faing. I have a small red post-it Tape Flag (small red marker basically). When FA pops:
/tar ubber_meele_name
/cast 4 (FA)
after its started to cast and not fizzled move tab.
Rinse repeat.

I taped a stick note with the numbers 1-6 going down the left side to my desk. I use if I'm just doing my group.

Not fancy, but it works. Its too easy to forget who you are responsible for, and who you cast it on last.... or maybe I'm just too old and forgetful.
