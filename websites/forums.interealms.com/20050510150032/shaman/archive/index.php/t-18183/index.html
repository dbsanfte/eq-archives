Jinheim
I'm so honored to be quoted :D

I wrote that guide long before LDoN came out, so let me give my thoughts on what these AA mean nowadays.

In LDoN, we can play a variety of roles. In a pickup group, I am usually functioning as the puller. I pull several mobs at once, and use VP + ornate legs to CC them while the rest of my group picks one and kills it. If there is no ench (and thus no mezz) I dot and nuke the adds while my group works on the rest. Stacking Bane + BoS + a few nukes is actually surprisingly good damage. However, we all know what happens when you start stacking dots. You get hit, a lot. What I do is just dot a lot of mobs, try to keep them rooted, and if I can't keep them rooted I just back in to a corner and quiescence myself, then continue on with the dotting. Between full defensive AA's and respectable AC, I can pretty much ignore the melee damage of a 2-3 slowed LDoN mobs. In my mind, this greatly outweighs the benefits of healing AA's. Don't get me wrong, healing AA's are still great for ldon, but they aren't nearly as good as defensive AA's.

In hard adventures, defensive AA are even better. In a normal adventure, defensive AA allow me to play like a jackass and not die. In hard adventures, they are a major boost to my survivability. The mobs hit very hard, and you really want to have defensive AA. Healing AA's, again, will help you a lot, but not nearly as much as defensive AA.

On raids, it really depends on what you are raiding. There are some places, like vex thal, where you don't really need to heal that much. There are, however, a ton of adds and you are bound to get aggro repeatedly if you are trying to slow them. The mobs don't hit overly hard to begin with, so with defensive AA, you're have a much better chance of debuffing and surviving. I rarely have to heal in VT (assuming I am doing it with a whole guild and not a small 2-group crew), so healing AA doesn't help that much there. On most pop raids, there are fewer adds, and they are easier to control. Thus, you are less likely to get aggro while debuffing. Also, the mobs hit much much harder, so backup heals are more and more necessary as you get further in to pop. There are exceptions to this, though, such as the bertoxx ring, where (if you are doing your job, at least) you can expect to be getting hit nonstop for 500ish. You definately need defensive AA there.

For most PoP bosses, you are pretty safe from ever getting aggro unless you are trying to. You already mentioned it yourself, and I have said it a million times: I feel our role on raids is to debuff first, and then heal. I do not dot on raids, I chain-cast tnargs on whoever needs it. It is very satisfying to watch yourself crit for 3200+ repeatedly, and even more satisfying when one of your crits saves someone's life.

So, to sum it up, when I am fighting a pop boss, I get a lot more use out of my healing AA's than I do my defensive AA's. When I am clearing for pop raids, it is pretty evenly split, they are both valuable. In LDoN, I use my healing AA's a lot, but my defensive AA's are getting constant use. You should decide which of these is most important to you, and spend accordingly. However, I would suggest doing defensive AA's first. As someone else said, you can't heal if you are dead.

If I wasn't lazy and had a bit of foresight, I'd have parsed LR5 myself.

However, I am lazy, so instead of using parses, I will argue for LR5 from another perspective.

I have all healing AAs as well as LR5 and ID5, and from my personal perspective, I use LR5 far more often than I need healing AAs. To clarify that statement, the life of a shaman who stretches his abilities to their limits is a life full of "so and so hit you for such and such damage", and getting hit for less, or not getting hit at all, is always better than having to spend mana to heal.

From a raid perspective, there are too many mobs that can beat a shaman into the ground in 5 secs or less, therefore even a tiny chance of avoiding a hit is more valuable than any potential healing I can do in these situations.

With that being said, healing AAs make a *huge* difference in our healing ability. In fact, with full healing AAs, I can match a lesser equipped cleric in raw healing power from doing LDoN to camping regruas in PoWater.

LR5 and healing AAs are both important, but remember, the dead heals no one.
