Jinheim
People exaggerate when they post how much exp they are getting in a particular spot. They think they are getting better exp than they really are because they just look at how much exp they get over a few kills, not a long period of time. Here is what you do to determine what the best exp is for you:

First, find a potential spot. At your level I was soloing katta guards, which were excellent exp at the time. The exp there hasn't gotten any worse, but it MIGHT be worse in comparison to PoP. You'll have to test this for yourself.

Once you have your spot, WRITE DOWN your exp (use a UI that gives you percentages) when you start.

Set a timer for 1 hour, and just go at it. Take note of how many mobs you kill per spawn cycle (at katta guards I think I could kill 4 or 5 w/o my friend's fungi, 6 or 7 with it), and any other information that might be useful (did you die? If so, why? Is it unavoidable?)

At the end of the 1 hour, write down your ending exp. See how much exp you get per hour.

Find another potential spot, and repeat these steps. In one afternoon you can try out 5 or 6 spots and see which one is the best, then spend the rest of your time there.

Here are a few things I looked for while I was still leveling up:

1) Plenty of spawns - Waiting for respawn KILLS your exp. You don't want a camp where you can clear the entire thing and then have to wait for more to spawn. In katta, at either the bank or twightlight zone in, I never succeeded in soloing all the spawns before repop.

2) A safespot - You do not want to be forced to sit somewhere where you get roamers or trains. 1 unexpected add can usually be handled, but it lowers your effeciency and thus your exp. If you sit somewhere where you get trained frequently, your exp is going to suck from constantly having to run to the zone/die. This is why the right castle camp in PoV sucks. It would be an awesome camp except for the stupid bridge overhead where trained mobs path back over.

3) Spawns near your safespot - Long pull times mean slower kills and less exp. In the best camps, you can just target your next victim and cast slow without having to move. Fungus Grove was an excellent exp zone before pop, for this reason. You have a patch of mushrooms that I couldn't quite clear before repop, no roamers to aggro you and few trains, and the mobs were all in a nice little cluster, waiting to be killed.

4) Easy, low-risk mobs - Dying is bad. Dying will hurt your exp gain more than anything except falling asleep. When PoP first came out and I was hunting in PoD at 60, a string of unlucky resists usually meant death or running to the zoneline. Dying or even having to zone really sets back my exp gain, for me. If it takes you a minimum of 10 minutes to find a rezz, summon pet, rebuff, and get to FM every time you die/zone, and you die/zone once an hour, your exp was just cut by 1/6. That sucks. Fight easy mobs that you know you can kill consistantly. I leveled from 54+ mainly in katta and FG, and my combined deaths for those two zones is probably no more than 10.

5) Exp per kill vs. Time to kill - Pretend you have two mobs. One mob will give you consistently 1% per kill, and takes you on average 85 seconds to kill from start to finish (you should be timing these things!). Some mob in pop gives you 4% per kill, but takes you 7 minutes to kill. Which is better? The first one. Do not be fooled by big exp gains. Quite often, more easy mobs will give you better exp than a few big mobs. Time yourself and take notes.

6) Any special weaknesses/strengths on the mobs - This doesn't come in to play much since most mobs are just warrior classes, but there are a few special cases. The one I will pick are FG mushrooms. They have 2 spells and a proc. The spells are a slow and a stun, and the proc is a 200dd (I think?) and has a big mushroom illusion with it that roots you. The slow makes FG a real pain for a melee to solo, but has absolutely no effect on a shaman. In fact, you WANT them to slow you, because when they are casting their slow they aren't hitting you. The stun and proc are annoying, but they are easily avoided. How? All 3 of these spells are based on disease resist. We get a juicy DR buff and there is a lot of cheap DR gear available. Once you have 180 DR, you are immune to mushrooms. They just sit there and try to slow/stun you and never melee. This enabled me to do 4 at a time at level 60. FG is obsolete now, though, so this is just an example. I honestly don't know of anything like this in PoP that can be taken advantage of.

A few more things to consider:

Are you soloing or grouping? I have found, that in general, I get better exp over time when I solo than when I group. This is because when it is just me, things are very predictable. I know I can pull a PoV golem every 6 minutes (not sure on my actual time, I haven't had a chance to try them since I got the new dots and Q), and I will never die. I can do this for as long as I want, only having to take a break every few hours for a kei refresh and a smoke.

When I am grouping, though, things are far from predictable. The number 1 thing to ask about your group is: Does it suck? All it takes is one incompetent person and your exp gain can be killed. If the healer, tank, puller, slower, or crowd controller sucks, the group will probably suck. I am very powerful in an exp group, but even I can't save a group consistantly when there are 4 unslowed and unmezzed mobs brought by a crappy monk.

At your level, it will be very hard to find a group that can match your soloing exp rate, simply because the characters are not as powerful as they will be at 65, and the person behind the toon probably isn't as experienced as they will be at 65. You'll need more support classes (2 or 3 people - slower healer CC) at your level than you will at 65 (My normal group is myself and 5 melee - I am all they need to function). That means less slots open for damage dealers, which means slower kills and less exp. Also, having 3 people doing support roles means it is 3x as likely that one of them will be afk during a pull, LD, or just plain suck. With just me as the support person, I KNOW I can do my job and never screw up. At 58 in a group, you don't have that luxury. You have to depend on other people. Soloing, though, you can solo forever and never die or mess up if you are competent and follow the rules I posted :)

To sum it up:

Try out lots of different spots, and get numbers. Don't just say "hmmm I think my exp here is better," get a timer and a calculator and figure it out.

Soloing is almost always going to be better exp for your level. However, I liked to group plenty because I made friends and I don't get bored as easily with people to talk to. 2 hours of soloing and I usually get bored and log, but I can go for 10 hours w/ my guildies.

Calabar, what Jinheim is talking about is sometimes referred to as "min/maxing" - playing the game like a numbers game to get the most benefit. If I'm logging in with an eye toward gaining XP as quickly as possible, yeah, I'll min/max and crunch the numbers, and I think of most of the factors he mentioned (short pulls, fast pops, no adds/trains, relatively low risk, high reward/unit time). I often do this when soloing, because I see no point in soloing if I'm not making significant progress toward some goal.

But Monday nights I have a PoN grind set up with some friends in their 50's. XP sucks rocks at 62 (like 4% per hour or less ), but I keep in touch with my friends and relax a bit. Other nights I participate in guild raids, which do almost nothing for my XP. Or I'll work on tradeskills, help a friend with an epic fight, etc. XP is not my goal on these nights, and min/maxing wouldn't apply.

Yet other nights I'll be after loot - cash or items - and will treat that differently as well.

So no, I don't think you're alone in the way you feel, but I think most of us in analytical fields (which, frankly, is most EQ players :-) have thought about the best way to maximize XP from time to time.
