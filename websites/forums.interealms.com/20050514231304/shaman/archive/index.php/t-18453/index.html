Most of it looks pretty good, but:

Level 67 Clinging Drowsiness -- Slows an already slowed mob 2% extra to a max of 75%... Hence if a monster resists slow 50% such that tugurs is 37.5% -- each shaman casting this would add 2% to the slow -- IE 5 extra shamen = 47.5% slowed.

Won't happen. Mobs are designed with their mitigation as it is for a reason. To allow a mob to be slowed from the 37.5% it was designed at all the way back to 75% by zerging it with tons of shaman would defeat and trivialize many encounters.

Everyone still gets all pissy about mitigation, but no one seems to grasp that partial mitigation is a GOOD thing. Mobs are designed to be fought in their slowed state. So if they want a mob to do 1000 DPS when slowed, if its not a mob that can mitigate then it must do 4000 DPS when unslowed, which means some top end mobs would kill tanks and half the raid near instantly when pulled. Having a mob that mitigates to only 20% slowable, means that the mob is doing only 1250 DPS when unslowed. Which makes the intial period after pulling while debuffing harder but manageable.
