lestumpette
To me this is an interesting question since I think the constraints forced on a person while leading a raid as guild leader might have a significant impact on their ability to play a character to the limit. While it is difficult to argue which is the easiest class to manage under raid conditions, I am certain it isn’t a shaman :). I’m aware that leading a guild and leading a raid are often not accomplished by the same person, but I am also aware that many guild leaders DO lead their own guild raids and this is the situation I am curious about.

The only character I ever play is a shaman and from my experience with this class, leading a raid would be a serious challenge to my ability to perform at my best. Although I haven’t seen many shaman fill the dual role of guild/raid leader, I’m sure there are more than a few players out there that do it. I would love to be corrected on this, but it seems to me that we shaman are in a minority in this area of the game.

I’ve known warriors and clerics that filled this role very successfully, while still maintaining their character functions perfectly, and it would be naïve and disrespectful of me to suggest some classes couldn’t rise to the challenge. However, when a guild is at the forefront of the game doing the really really difficult content, are all classes equal in this capacity ?

Heya Caanus. Yep, I'm still around! Unfortunately, I got banished to Princeton, New Jersey for work for 4 months, so I play on a laptop and I'm putting in 12+ hour days--which doesn't give a lot of time for posting!

Anyway, it's entirely true that (absent a first run on a mob) about 85% of a raid leader's job is done PRIOR to engaging. Assign roles, positioning, heal strats, rampage, go over mechanics of fight, etc. Ideally, in a well run guild, you have class leaders to whom you can delegate. Usually, I just have to know who's in the raid, relative hp/ac for splits, know which players are best at paying attention, and assign roles accordingly. Once you know your resources and the fight mechanics, everything is cake. I'll give a quick recap of the fight, tell everyone what's needed, and assign resources in a general way. I'll ask the warrior CL for a MT/RT list, say I need 2 druids on rampage heal, 3 paladins on cure duty, etc. Tell your clerics whether you need a CH chain, or a specific spam heal tactics, etc. Work around your AEs--counteract mana drains with bard/cleric groups, or SKs for mana taps, and concentrate your healers into mana generating groups if you can. High damage AEs, spread your healers, etc. Most everything is situational. Got a killer AE? Then there's probably some trick to the thing you're missing. Plan. Plan. Plan. Stuns and knockbacks will screw chains. Spam healing eats mana like nobody's business. Estimate mob dps and raid dps to determine length of fight to estimate proper healing strats, etc. That's not really the point of this thread. Thing is, most of that is done well before you engage.

Actually during the fight, I'm doing relatively little in active guidance absent some major screw up. Warn of special attacks or emotes in case people miss them, emergency healing the RT or MT. Re-arranging healing assignments on the fly for dying/oom healers, timing AEs for dodging them, or just watching for screw-ups, etc. If I can't do that during a raid and handle my own duties, I don't have any business running raids. It's just multi-tasking.

Yeah, it ain't always fun (frequently frustrating in fact), but the better you run your raids, the better attendance you get, the more fun people have, the more they stick out the tough ones, etc. It's a self-perpetuating cycle. If, by a bit of effort on my part, I can make it fun for everyone else AND make sure we kick ass, I'll do it willingly.
