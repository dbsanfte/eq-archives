Uloff Bonecracker
Basically, I don't think I'm going to do it anymore.

A few months ago I was in Sebilis doing the crypt (Emp, blood, crypt) where I lucked out and won another JBB. Thinking I could use this to fund my missing spells I began to auction these wrist wonders at the bargain price of 8K. I’m an Australian, so I don’t play at peak times so there wasn’t too many shaman around. Anyhow, after a minutes I received a tell from a fellow shaman – lvl 49 – saying that he would be interested in purchasing the item, but he didn’t have the cash on him. He joked and asked if he could “pay it off”. I thought to myself.. Sounds fair enough, at level 49 I would have killed for a JBB. So, I bought a port and handed him the JBB… After a few days I thought to myself if he contacts me to tell me he has a payment I’ll just give him the item without having to pay a cent. Fast forward to about a month ago, I see him online so I send him a tell to say hello and not to worry about paying me back. He thanks me and I continue on with whatever I was doing. Fast forward again to last night, I see him again in the bazaar. I find out he’s only a level higher and he sold the JBB for some cash……. If I had have known he’d do that, there’s no way I would have given him the item in the first place especially after I’d made it clear in the first place I’d only give it to him IF he used it. Anyhow, this isn’t the first time I’ve seen this happen but it’s made me very jaded. Anyone had any similar experiences?

I remember the first thing I ever had given to me, still -- a fine steel mace. I was shocked -- stunned! This was the kind of thing you had to be in your 30's to get, maybe when fighting in Cazic, not something you get at like level 10, like I was, without a plat to my name. It wasn't the kind of thing you just give away, and I could never afford to buy one. I was thrilled!

It takes very little to thrill a real newbie, because ANYTHING to a real newbie is huge.

I never give anything to anyone who asks for stuff, because to me that's 99% confirmation that they are not really a newbie, but instead someone experience at the game. Why the heck should those guys get freebies that rightly should go to REAL newbies? Should be ashamed of themselves, the punks. I don't wander into shopping malls and ask for free merchandise, or sit down in the office lounge and ask people to fix me a snack. I wouldn't even think of it -- same in the game. You have to be taught that kind of bad manners, or teach them to yourself; they don't come right away. Real newbies are often very polite and even intimidated, wondering what the heck is going on -- not on the greed patrol. A lot of them even take a long time to learn how the channels work; they're not spamming /shout and /ooc with begs or sending tells begging, because they usually wouldn't know how to do it even if they wanted to.

However, I have given lots of stuff to real newbies. I like handing out greater lightstones to blind people, giving people quest components, etc. Back in the day when that fine steel mace was really great, I used to port my first character, a druid, in to west commons from cazic, or run all the way to kelethin, and pass out lots and lots of fine steel I would loot from there. Back then, my losing the five plat from not selling the FS actually felt like something, too. But it was payback for the guy who gifted me when I was young, and it really thrilled the newbies, which was fun for me too.

That's what it's like with REAL newbies.

Nowadays, I don't think there are all that many real newbies around. You hear level 7 people /ooc'ing what they think about COCW vs. COF, etc. And you get begged constantly. I dread going into Cabilis with my monk because there's always some "newbie" camped at the bank waiting for people to show up so he can beg for money or items. Our old ancestral newbies, the real ones, almost NEVER did that. Now most of them do, it seems to me. A lot of us old-timers who were cooler newbies have turned into jerkier elders. Then again, so many people are twinked and powerleveled these days that a lot of people hit high levels who normally would have been winnowed out by the time demands of the game and the effort level it USED to take, at least for the non-twink/PL's.

I've mostly been broke throughout my EQ career except for very rare spurts of being flush with cash, but I've enjoyed helping friends complete quests or kill epic mobs or mobs that drop something special for them. That's the main way I help out these days. I just twinked a friend's level 20 shaman with some nice stuff because he's an old friend, and he's broke too, and doesn't really know what he should buy or equip, or why or when. He has a 60 monk and is working up a shaman; I have a 60 shaman and am working up a monk. That kind of helping is cool and fun; helping out some random beggar just turns me off. I'm on my third post-50 character now, and if I could get this far without begging, so can they.
