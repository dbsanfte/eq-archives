I think the best idea is simply to overhaul the effects again.

When VI did the overhaul the first time, the new recipe potions were pretty good, compared to current equipment and spells. An example is the Potion of Greater Purity ( +25 Strength, + 25 Poison Resist, - 15 Charisma ) to be used for Trak raids, with a Strength boost as a bonus. Unfortunately, that is probablye the most usable of the stat potions.

The utility potions had quite a few that would be of greater use - the vast majority of them, actually - if not for a few glaring problems.
#1, price (remember kiddies, herb prices are half what they were when it was introduced). Who'd pay even base cost for a Infravision potion at 6.3 plat per charge?
#2, components - the Levitate and Enduring Breath potions STILL stupidly require the user to have bat wings / fish scales to use. Why? Especially with the new componentless spells?
#3, limited use/minor effect - AC buffs that are extremely minor for the level needed to craft them (6 & 18), a cure disease, cure poison, cancel magic & a couple hastes. Where's the variety? The only standard sales are from SoW, Shrink, IVU, and Gate potions, with some minor sales of the damage shields.

When it was revamped, the potions kinda fit in with the quality of loot in the world. After two expansions (think the revamp was mid-Kunark) later, they've dropped in desireability.

I think Asta had a good point - revamp the effects (or add new global herbs & recipe's), by usage. Fun factor stuff (shrink & grow included, along with whacky stuff), raid usage, and everyday adventuring usage.

And give us some @$#% recipe books!

There are 4 ways to fix it.

One is to make potions that are actually useful to everyone. Shrink/SoW is a good example of this. New ideas include gate potions to places people actually care about (bazar, west wastes, grey), potions that add a % increase to various trade skills, or potions that increase the level of all pets you cast.

Another is to make potions that enchance the shaman class by adding abilities they dont normaly have. Invis v undead is a good example. These could all be no drop potions so they only help shaman: potions that cast improved invis, fd (5.5 casting time), increase faction, regen mana (4/tic), extended dots/debuff duration.

A third step is to remove existing potions from the game that arnt shaman made. Eb potions (that dont require a component, unlike ours), levitate, and gate potions are all sold by vendors.

The final is to add powerful raid oriented potions. Aggro increase/decrease potions, haste cap increasing potions, +15hp/level of drinker (max 90), increase disc refresh time (15%).
