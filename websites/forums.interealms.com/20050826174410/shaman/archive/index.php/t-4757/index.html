For you people who say that Canni 1 is no faster than medding, here's your sign. There is NO comparison between normal medding and canni-dancing. I can remember many times when the groups main healing duties was split between myself and a druid.... we'd both get to 10m and say "med break", and 1 min later after Cannibalizing I'm at 70m or 80m while the druid is at 30 or 40m. Then the druid would say something like "damn shamans and their mana-regen". I canni-dance like a damn fool, and I got plenty of mana to show for it. It's not even a contest. It's free-mana, people! How can you not see that? With regen on, if you're full health then that regen is going to waste. Canni down a few hundred hitpoints (you're not using hitpoints in a group anyway, the tanks are) and gain extra mana. Then regen those hitpoints back again and Cannibalize again.... It's not even comparable.

About buffing, I kinda just go by what I'm capable of doing. If there is no other healer but me then I gotta do what I can. If the tanks are dropping like flies then I just give them all regen, haste and then just heal --- not enough mana to heal and fully buff every tank. If there is another healer, I'll buff fully every tank. The benefits of buffing make a very noticeable difference, and I buff whenever my mana allows it. But next time you're in a tank-heavy group, fully buff all the tanks and notice a fairly large difference in the decrease of damage taken and the speed that the mobs drop.

About Healing, yes it sucks when you gotta focus only on healing, but we can heal pretty darn good in the 30's, so if that's what you gotta do, that's what you gotta do.

About Slowing, there is two factors that determine if I slow or not: 1) how fast the tanks are ripping through the mobs 2) if everyone is buffed and healed, then I slow. In tank heavy groups where mobs are dropping fast it's not mana-efficient to slow. But in groups where the mobs take forever to die, then slowing is very beneficial to reduce the amount of damage taken over the long haul. Same thing for quest mobs that are difficult.

And also I'd like to say that while I *can* tank a bit... it's not the best way to go about things. Yes, shamans can take a hit better than most other casters, but that doesn't mean that you should be the main tank and only focus on hps and AC. Then you're just a ranger, basically, defensively. Get your hps and AC to respectable levels but don't forget about Wis and mana gear. I try to find a healthy balance of both. I focus on Hitpoint/sta and mana/wis gear, with AC bringing up the rear. Tanking is not something shamans specialize in, just something that we can play around with. It's not good enough to depend on. At 35, with all my AC/hp gear and fully self-buffed, i can still get my clock cleaned.
