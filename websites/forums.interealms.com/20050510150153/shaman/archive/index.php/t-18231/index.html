I'm generally an EQ newbie, only been playing a year. Level 65, most of my spells, familiar with most low-end zones/XP areas of PoP zones.

I belong to a very small "family" guild of about 16 people. It was formed from a group of people who group together virtually every day, they picked me up, I became part of the group, and we just made it official with a guild tag.

Now all of the original group is 65 and all have at least 50 AA (I'm the 50, school disrupts EQ grinding), so we're slowly moving up into that area where group-based xp is dropping off in terms of challenge. Last night we did an LDoN with me as the healer, got 5 pulls in Mistmoore, and took them all down after losing only the group's chanter. When our C-healing druid and chanter is in the group, we can easily handle 4-5 BoT mobs at once. We've single-grouped Zlandicar, the Protector of Fire ring event, and the Sleepwalker (though P.o.Fire and Sleepwalker really aren't all that hard).

Recently, we've picked up a number of new members, all in the low to mid 50's in level, so our guild has kinda created two tiers...the 50's tier, and the 65 tier. We've been powerlevelling the youngsters as best we can by grouping them all together in sweet spots like PoV IC and stacking a couple of 65's in there with them ungrouped, doing debuffing, CC, or adding DPS to help them out. When we get them up to 65 and reasonably well equipped, we plan to start low-end raids and such, as well as get keys for better zones. In short, we're starting down the very long path to the Elemental planes.

Last week, one of our founding guild members quit the guild. He said he'd grown tired of grinding and was looking to apply to a raiding guild. He wanted the gear you can only get from raiding, so he applied to a guild that's about 2 steps from elemental planes. When we explained that with our new recruits we could start down the path of raiding, he told us that he wants to do it NOW.

Needless to say, most of us were stunned. He'd never said a word to any of us, and what he said got me to thinking about what I want to do. I realized that since we have to do it all (flagging, getting keys, etc.) anyway, why not do it with others you love being around? This way, we're all going to get our keys, raid these mobs for the first time, die a lot, but learn from our mistakes and come back and beat them. We'll get our keys together, get our flags together, then when all is said and done, we'll reach where we want to go together and feel pride in having travelled the road from the beginning to the end.

Given your choice, would you do what we intend to do, start from scratch and go all the way to the end, or would you jump into a guild that's already well down that road?

I don't know everyone's little personality quirks, or intimate details about their real life, or any of their inside jokes, but that will come with time.

As for starting a raid guild from scratch, you really have quite a task set out before you. When a guild decides to start raiding, they have to recruit more people. At this stage of the game, there is really slim pickings as far as recruits go. If you're at the bottom of the guild totem pole, the only people that are going to join you are people with little to no raid experience, and people who have been rejected from more established raiding guilds. Most of your members will not have the drive to push forward and accomplish guild goals. If they had this drive, they would most likely be in a raiding guild already. I don't mean to be discouraging, but the simple truth is that most guilds who form with the intent to start raiding will fail. Obviously, not all of them do, but the odds are against. The single most important factor is leadership. If your leaders can organize and lead raids, inspire people to care about the guild's progress, and teach the members to treat each other and other guilds with respect, then you might have a chance. If you do decide to try to build your guild up, then I wish you the best of luck. It is a very rewarding experience, almost enough to outweigh the risk of failure.

All that being said, in your situation, I would leave. However, I am not you. What I want out of the game is probably not the same thing you want. The only person who can really answer your "leave or stay" question is you. You have to ask yourself what is important to you and your enjoyment of the game. However, if you are serious about wanting to see highend EQ, your best bet would be to join a guild that is already well on their way there. Ask yourself, are you really that attached to your guild now? When I left my guild, a lot of people told me they were sad to see me go, but only a few kept in touch. Those people were my real friends, and they are still my friends. Switching guilds doesn't mean you have to sever all ties with them. If your guild is really like a family to you, and they are the main reason that you play EQ at all, then you should stay. Chances are that if you left them, you'd quit the game within a month. You're the only one that can decide what is best for you, so good luck.

I joined a guild called Eternal Shadows in early 2002. They were a guild made up of just a bunch of friends that like to level. We had about 12 active characters who just levelled together each day. I was lvl 60 at the time, with very few AAs(might have been venerable, but no canni5), and no epic. I was guilded for about 2 weeks before they got my epic. As we got more AAs and got some more epics, we decided we'd start moving up in the world, and we started killing in HoT, with me as the only shaman. Within a month, we had almost doubled in size, and were still in HoT getting gear. By this time, I was full SS quest armor, and most of the original members were getting there. We moved up in the world, killed vindi(a huge step), and started killing him on a regular basis.

We kept going up the tier lines, killing zlandicar, getting ST keys for a single group. That group would head into ST not knowing a thing about the zone, and attempt to kill the MotG and that other named near the entrance, and getting no primals ;)

Onward and upward we went. Killed NToV, Sontalak, Keeper, etc. etc. etc. Got more people keyed for ST, along with a CoH mage bum, and started killing warders on a regular basis. We started heading to ssra and taking out the creator and arch lich. We felt like we were invincible. We had become probably the #4 guild on the server in terms of what we could do with the numbers we had. A little while later, we lost our guildleader and a few respected members, but some officers took over, and we kept rolling.

Then we killed the cursed. A great accomplishment, we could now start getting taunt proccers for our warriors. Then our main cleric/rogue(always 2 boxed no raids) sold his characters because of RL issues(cleric went to a guildmate so we still had him). That was a huge loss for us, he was a great guy. A few months after this, about a year after i joined the guild, the guildleader comes back and says he wants to take back over(at least that's what I gather from it). A lot of our old members didn't like the idea, and left to form a guild of their own. These were about 70% of the hardcore players in the guild, and all of my better friends. I left to be with those friends, and my RL friends in the guild followed me. I stayed in that guild for about a month with my buddies, when they decided that this guild wasn't working out. We didn't have enough clerics, and were wiping out to(what was then) simple mobs, like AL.

So, a joint decision was made with the officers of the new guild to disband after letting all the members know. We discussed it for a week or so, and in the end, a few of them decided to go to Wudan(#2 guild on server, and the others were waiting on me to decide. I was either going to Wudan or Magus Imperialis Magicus, but i wanted to sit down with my RL buddies and see what they wanted. After a weekend of discussion, we decided on MiM because of my friends' gear. 1 of them could have gotten into AD but the other 2 had no chance. So into MiM we went, and about 6 other people came with us, all of which were the friends that I wanted to stay with. The others went to random guilds, and a couple went back to Eternal Shadows.

So that's my story, and you can see that, in the end, all my good friends stayed together but went to a raiding guild. I would suggest, if he's as good a friend to you as my friends were to me, to follow him, and bring as many of your buddies as possible.

Nobody knowing the zone, only having a map from EQAtlas printed out, no clue on mobs or strategies and spending hours on CRs. Then 2 months later you zone in with the same 3 groups and wipe the zone clean. You earned that, you worked for it and YOU being there made a difference. That's the accomplishment factor.

But what stops me is knowing that I cannot follow. 95% of raiding guilds just follow along, like sheep. 5% do the actual work. Now you know why people all say, 'bah' all the time :p

My dignity couldn't handle going to a large guild and them backflagging for me. It would be like a pity flagging :p I just am not the type of person who can sit in the corner while the other 5 ppl in the group get me my flags in PoJ trials and I think Binter might be talking more about that type of thing. Another apology for another long post ...

I don't think a guild really needs to PL it's lower level members ... it seems like, in SOTA at least, they do it on their own. They just need a little incentive.

It strikes me, sometimes, how much my guild's 50's Crowd looks up to those of us who are in our 60's. When those of us in our 60s ding, the chaos and excitement over it fill my chat window every single time, and (it never fails) then begin comments like, "I need some more levels so I can do adventures with so-and-so" ... or "What new zones do you get to go to now?" ... and that's one of the great things. I completed the Trial of Execution not too long ago, and the moment I did, I was flooded with tells from guildies wanting information on it. I ended up having to post details on our forum for them, and ever since they've been begging me to take them and let them try a trial. When I told them I wanted them to be 60+ to start a trial, it got quiet in Guild Hall for a few moments ... and then the "OMG I need # levels!!" started flying through Guild Hall. They've been working on it diligently ever since, and each time one of them dings, it's "Hey Tordail, I'm one level closer to doing a trial with you!!!!"

SOTA is preparing for it's second attempt on Vindi on Thursday. The first time, we wiped, and wiped terribly and horribly. But not one person complained, I kid you not. It wasn't long after the raid (oh yeah, every last one of us) popped back at our bind points that the "OMG OMG that was so freakin' cool, when do we get to try again?!" started. They wanted to rez the corpses and try again right then and there, matter of fact. But the Council told them we'd come back when the guild had a few more levels under its belt ... and we do. We're going Thursday evening, and since it was announced Sunday that's been the Talk of the guild nearly non-stop. One of them actually said, "If we wipe again, then that means I need more levels. I have a new DD this time around ... maybe I can get it to stick on him!"

Quite honestly, as an officer, I find myself having to hold them back oftener than I am pushing them forward. Hold them back? Yep ... making sure they're not leveling too quickly for their skills, making sure they don't zone into places they're not quite of a level for ... Generally speaking, they're about the most ambitious bunch I've yet seen.

Maybe some of you are reading about my guild's ambition and snickering a bit. Maybe POJ Trials and attempts at Vindi aren't the way to encourage lower levels to work hard and level up. Maybe we need to be leveling our lower crowd a hell of a lot quicker than we are, and maybe we'll never, ever get to where we wanna go. Well, if that's the case ... I say this.

You should never be ashamed of failing ... only of never trying. And there's nobody in Norrath I'd rather fail next to than my family.

In a family guild here, only guild I've been in since I started playing 2.5 years ago. A RL friend is the guild leader and I've been on council since a few months after playing.

We went through some rough times. About 10 months after playing we decided to concentrate on our lower levels and help build them up. A lot of higher levels got annoyed with it and left. What really hurt us though is that we had one week where we had about 7 officers leave to move onto "bigger" guilds.

That hit hard, and we almost folded the guild all together, but we held on and kept concentrating on lower levels and building ourselves up. From then on the guild just grew and grew.

When we started working on epics we realized we didn't have enough people and the force to take down some of the mobs and/or do the raids. So we became freinds with a few other guilds and formed an alliance.

We now have about 60-70 active members. (Guild roster has 385, but a hell of a lot are alts, lol) We also may be adding in another 25-50 with a possible guild merger in 2 weeks. We're doing a lot of things on our own we never thought we'd be able to do. Our levels are mainly 50-65 and we stick to raids where anyone in those levels can do. We don't bother PLing the lower levels, we found they usually can make it to 60+ on their own quickly enough without our help.

Do we still lose people who rather skip ahead and move to an already established high end guild? Of course, you'll always get people like that, sometimes the ones you never thought would leave. And as hard as it is, you have to respect their decision. Interesting enough for us though we find about 80% of those who leave come back to us. In fact of the 7 officers who left in that one week over a year and a half ago, 6 had come back. We even re-invited another old officer last night who left us eariler for a raiding guild.

So from my won experiences I'd suggest sticking it out with your guild. You may be down by your friend leaving and there is a bit of feeling of betrayal but trust me, it will feel good if you can get that guild of yours going and continue on and make it grow..

And for now you may want to get the elemental planes out of your head. Not saying not to strive for it, just not now. Work on raids that your guild can do now, get people used to how to raid and get them better equiped and work your way up the ladder.

If ya decide to keep the guild going, I wish ya the best of luck! :)
