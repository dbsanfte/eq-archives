Aurigabone
I haven't played a bard past level 10, but I believe that they continue to use low level songs all the way to 65, and that the effect scales up as they level. To a degree, some of our spells do the same i.e. Turgur's scales from 66% at level 51 to 75% at level 60, but that's not a big range. Why couldn't or didn't Verant just give us Turgur's at level 5 and say it scales from 10% at 5 to 75% at 60? Does it have something to do with adjusting mana costs perhaps?

The effect (whether good or bad) is that the spell is arguably overpowered at the time it is first memmed and then severely underpowered when it is time to purchase the upgraded version several levels later. Take greater healing for example. Healing 300hp at level 29 is awesome - healing 300hp 21 levels later at level 50 is weak, to say the least. I am currently 54 and using sup healing which is adequate in conjuction with Turgur's. When I reach the magic level of 58, I will go from healing 583 to healing almost 2k, and the mana efficiency is nearly doubled as well? Does it really make sense to have those magic levels where a caster receives a spell that dramatically increases their playability and groupability, or would it be better to try to have a single spell that scales to the level of the player?
