Game related... not so much I suppose. Chalk this one up to boredom at work and too many whines about whining.


My personal view is this: If you aren't complaining, you aren't thinking.

It takes absolutely no effort to agree with something. To say that it works for what it works for and to take it at face value is trivial. That is why being a "yes-man" or a suck-up is quite common and rather looked down upon.


Why do you see so much complaining and bellyaching here? Simple, because shaman have become as powerful as we are/were by thinking. We like to look at things from all angles and every perspective.

Tell me, what is the use of lauding the success of an action already taken or an item already existing? Not much. If that is all we ever did, then we would be very content cave dwellers or some such extreme example.

When people are complaining about the faults of something, that means they are attempting to advance and improve. You cannot improve upon anything unless you first find the faults in that which exists. When you realize the flaws of an object, you find it's limitations. The more well defined those limits are, the closer to them you can push safely and consistently.

Whining is nothing new. Back with Torpor we pointed out that the slow and snare was a nuisance. When clerics also started to get HoT's we began talking about needing one that didn't have the downsides as well. Did we at the same time refuse the use of Torpor? No, we just looked at it and realized it was meant for someone tanking a mob, but not really doing melee damage... ie, us. So while we were bickering about needing a HoT that we could use on tanks, we went out and solo'd some WW dragons.

Tell me, what are the chances that we would have used a normal HoT for the same effect? Personally, I believe they would have been relatively marginal. It would have taken quite a while longer to finally see someone solo the dragons since the mentality would have been that the spell was for use on tanks and people would have been doing duo jobs instead.

It is through complaint and analysis that we can see what to improve and what to use. We don't completely shun that which we are given, we embrace it and push to the limits, then we talk about what can come next.


So, to all those who are tired of hearing people complain, look past it to what we aren't saying. Nobody wants to take the time to post the obvious (wow, this spell like... restores health or something... huh). Such posts are pointless and spark no discussion. Lively debate and interaction come through seeing the faults and possibilities of what is there.
