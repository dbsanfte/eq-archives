Basically, AA works like this.

You can put a percentage - or all - of your xp into AA xp. This is a seperate pool from regular xp, and has a yellow bar just like regular xp which must be filled. I've been told AA is like level 45, but it always seems a bit higher than that to me. So to get a single AA point you have to "redo" level 45 in essence.

Each time you fill it, you get a "ding", only it's an AA ding and you're informed that you have a new AA point to spend. Like leveling, the yellow bar resets to 0, and it's time to grind again.

You store AA points, until you choose to spend them. There are 4 tiers to AA points - General, which you can start spending in at 51, Priest, which starts at 56 I believe, and Shaman which you do at 59, and then Planar AA which I think is 60? I don't really know much about that. In addition to the level requirement, you must have 6 points in General to buy any Priest, 12 points in Priest to buy any Shaman specific aaxp, and then 24 shaman points to buy Planar. Or so I believe.

There are different abilities at each level. For instance, natural regen is a general ability, so you can buy all 5 points in it as soon as you reach 51, provided you have the aaxp points. But in order to buy mental clarity, which is a priest archetype, you need 56 and 6 points anywhere in general.

Does this make sense? :)

Just think of it as buying ala-carte, with certain tiers, and you'll see how it works. I hope :)
