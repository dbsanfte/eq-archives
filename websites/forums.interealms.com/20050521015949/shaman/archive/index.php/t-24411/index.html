There are 4 sides to survivability: hp/s by healers, mob dp/s -- affected by cc/slow/ac/defense, hp cushion, and prevalence of silly mistakes by group members.

To see what is possible we need to know healing, damage, and hit points. Healing is best characterized by sustained hps. Damage is defined by mob characteristic vs. ac/defense of our tank. There is a sweet spot on a/c for mobs though--if your tank is below the level/ac needed for the sweet spot it makes a huge difference. The sweet spot tends to be by expansion, if I recall correctly. There is a lot of stuff in Steel Warrior about this. Moral is your tank needs to be equipped for the expansion. The tank's aa's and disciplines can reduce damage too, just like our aa's can increase healing. This should be an average percentage and affects what a group can hit.

Reducing mob dps through level, ac and defensive skills of your tank substantially reduces required hp/s, and a larger hp buffer on tank also increases safety.

Why focus on our healing hp/s? Because keeping the group alive by healing or slowing mob dp/s is our role in any group. That is where we come in, our contribution is either as main or secondary healer. Our slow, when it works at 75%, is the single biggest factor in any encounter. That is why it has been mitigated to near uselessness in bleeding edge content. The big mobs arent slowable. If the biggest mob is not slowable, you need healing for an unslowed situation, and slow is not necessary--in short fights it is a waste of mana.
