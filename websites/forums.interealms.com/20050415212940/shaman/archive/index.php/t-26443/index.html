Okay, normally I'm not a spelling nazi, but 2 things have been driving me nuts over the last few months. Accordingly, the following is a public service announcement--

The 2 most common spelling errors I see around here:

MITIGATION--To moderate (a quality or condition) in force or intensity; alleviate. To become milder.

Midigation is not now, nor, to the best of my knowledge, have it ever been a word. Midigation sounds like Norse term for leaving Asgard and heading to Midgar.

Ridiculous--Deserving or inspiring ridicule; absurd, preposterous, or silly.

Example: Equating Rytan with intelligence is ridiculous. Expecting something beneficial for shaman from Rytan is ridiculous.

It's bloody hard to write anything that's remotely thought-provoking or persuasive when you immediately run into a spelling error. It's hard to attach significance a post when your first thought is, "How much weight should I attach to the opinion of someone who's too stupid to spell something that they claim has totally screwed them. If it was that important, they'd have learned to spell it, right?"
