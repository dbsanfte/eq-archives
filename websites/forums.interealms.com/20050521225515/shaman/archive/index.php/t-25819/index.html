Xelnag Ashenpaw
If you get one make sure it has a 16ms response time or lower.

Response time is subjective. Most people are perfectly happy with 25ms panels, especially with a graphically slow game like EQ. I've got a pair of Philips 190B5CS panels(S-IPS 25ms) and I have zero ghosting problems even when playing something like UT. It probably doesn't help that the response time quoted is fairly misleading as TN+Film matrices(what most 17" gaming panels are based on) just happen to be *really* fast as switching from black to white. Switching from black to grey(which is largely considered a better measurement of panel performance) isn't that much faster than S-IPS.

Also, depending on your needs outside of EQ, the sub-16ms panels might not be suitable as they are invariably 18 bit colour panels, not true 24 bit colour, that use dithering to achieve the rest of the colour range. The 18 bit panels are usually fine for games, but suck for any kind of graphical work. And depending on the quality of the panel electronics DVD playback can also suffer(the dithering shows up as weird "sparkly" effects).

Basically the panel market breaks down into something like this:

TN+Matrix - Fast response time, very cheap. ****ty colours, not suitable for professional graphics work.

S-IPS - Clean, bright colours, true 24 bit. Expensive(partly because you can generally only find them in 19"+ panels) and slower than TN.

MVA/PVA - Older technologies that most consider on the way out. Both vary a fair bit with quality although I think MVA is still considered the best for high end graphical work as the colour reproduction and contrast ratios are still the best. Both are much slower than S-IPS and TN, with response times in the 30+ms range.

Xelnag Ashenpaw
24 bit and 32 bit are effectively the same, the latter just has 8 bits of colour assigned to the alpha channel(which isn't used when displaying an image on the screen, only for internal rendering operations).

The ****ty colours is in comparison to S-IPS and most good CRT's, but like the response time, is somewhat subjective.
butuglia
The only problem I see with a flatscreen monitor is the inability to use odd resolutions. For most people this is a non issue but for some gamers like myself it makes some games unplayable. I am specifically talking about Mame or other arcade emulations. They use bizarre resolutions that a flat screen can not reproduce. Remember a flatscreen has a set number of pixels. You get no more or no less. Resolutions are decided by how many of those pixels you use whereas a crt has virtually (I know this is exactly true) unlimited pixels up to the maximum.

I always tell people about this one caveat because it can sneak up on you and ruin your gaming experience. Other than that flat screens are very nice for most people.
