Hail,

This isn't a flame directed at you (post author).

Your description of your HG fight (congratulations) DOES seem more like that of a druid battle than a shaman, from my perspective. This is not bashing druids, just a description of tactics employed by two different classes.

We can "burn" a mob down chain casting cold/poison DD nukes after rooting as if we were a wizard, but you don't see it often when soloing. It isn't efficient. That doesn't mean wizards fighting in this manner are bad, just different.

In group settings, all classes are useful in my opinion, some more than others. But I usually LIKE having a druid in my group for taking care of regen/sow/other minor stuff that I don't want to waste my mana on in a fast hunting group.

You say that you are a pet kiter. The word kiting is inherently linked to druids and wizards. You will most likely learn to dislike certain members of these two classes. They often compete with our targets in a dangerous manner, frequently causing death from quad training, or even just dismay at taking mobs that we've just slowed and were on their way to us. Other solo classes (mage/necro) don't threaten our safety when they hunt nearby us. The fact that druids can level so quickly quadding generally means that there is a minority that are rather clueless when it comes to endangering others, and gives the class a bad rep. I've DEFINITELY been trained by more druids than any other class.

This is one reason your choice of the word "kiting" may have set off some shaman who frequent here and cause you to be a target of jokes/ridicule. Throwing in a question about tracking in the same post, kind of made you an open target for a smart-ass comment about being a druid.

I rarely, if ever, pet kite in the manner you described, unless I misunderstood your description. Here's what I generally do solo (pre-epic/JBB):

Self buff HP/AC/AGI/STA & hp regen. Strength not needed, Dex only if you have a proccing weapon. If you are on horseback, (which I'd doubt at your level due to cost) you won't be poking, so skip dex regardless.

Haste and hp regen dogdog, and sic'em on the mob after pulling with slow. Dogdog taunts (I leave it ON unless raiding) twice or thrice, and I back up a few steps, but there is no frantic running involved (that's a druid tactic). Dogdog takes aggro, I land second (& third, depending) dot. This will give me the aggro usually. Tank the mob until dead. If YOU are taking too many hits, root the mob and let dogdog take the heat again. If dogdog is taking too many hits, wade in to take the heat off him. Dogdog is your partner. He is weak, but he's our best friend.

When you hit 45 and can employ a JBB (racial restrictions not withstanding), you will be chain-casting it after the mob is slowed and dotted once, whether you or your pet is tanking.

Take care,
