weissblau
It's been a huge year for EQ, lots and lots of great changes since POP came out this time last year.

LDON is super - Think of it as your own personal camp spot with no annoying /ooc, no trains, and no camp stealers. Decent XP and cash loot, possibility of reasonable drops, and Adventure Points to buy additional treasure with as a bonus.

Some nice new spells available to us now. There is a real difference between 60 and 65. The two new DOTs - Blood of Saryrn and Breath of Ultor - have no DD component so they don't break root, and they do a lot of damage.

DOT damage is now reported to you. This is a great thing, really nice to see what you have running.

POP abilities Radiant Cure and Virulent Paralysis are nice.

They un-nerfed XP in old world zones to the point that it's possible to get a reasonable amount of XP while grinding for gear, helping lower level friends, etc. I wouldn't go to Seb to grind XP, but if I'm there, I want SOMETHING to show for it, and now you can get it.

New zones - all the LoY stuff, Veksar, Chardok B, revamps of Siren's Grotto, Veeshan's Peak, Plane of Mischief, and others... a significant amount of new content over and above the large chunk LDoN offers.

For me, it's a bit hard to distinguish between what's new by getting to level 65 from what's new in the game itself, but you may well be in the same boat. 65 is definately worth it. I have never felt like I had so many options as I do now.
