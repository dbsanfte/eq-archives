Giac
I can't improve my healing abilities currently, as I have SS, YM and the highend GoD foci, which are currently on par with the best ones I have seen in Omens, so my opinion should probably reflect what the state of Shaman healing is like at one side of the spectrum.

The high-high end Shaman can currently heal adequately in any non-raiding environment. I can main heal in any exp group, no matter in which zone for several hours.

That said, when you pass this along to a Dev, it should be mentioned that there are 3 fundamental issues with Shaman healing:

1)
In order to be up to the tasks mentioned above, Slow needs to land too early. I say too early, because Shamans lost any durability they once had and are just as vulnerable as a caster anymore. Any other Slowing class will survive longer in a pinch.

2)
In order to be up to the tasks described above, the Shaman relies on a resistable spell that generates relatively high aggro. The Shaman has no choice here.
He must cast the spell over and over till it lands, disregarding anything else.

3) The Shaman is highly dependant on his tank. Not only does the Shaman need a high-high end tank with supreme durability in order to perform above mentioned tasks, but he's reliant on the aggro generation of his tank, too. Which, in part, is random and varies with the number of procs early on in the fight.

Of course I am just looking at the Shaman class here. Everything is relative and even more so in a game like EQ. The gap between Shaman - Druid - Cleric healing widens each expansion and things will never be designed properly, if this issue isn't solved first. In short, the healing ratios need to be reviewed.
The healing utility of a Cleric is incredibly powerful, so that reducing the ratios to 100/95/90 (for HPS) would not hurt the Cleric class.
The Druid would be lacking in the efficiency department in that picture, which should be countered with an upgrade to their CH(95% of the Cleric's). Shamans would never get any form of large CH-type heal forcing them to bridge their fast heals constantly with HoTs.

That's my picture regarding the unavoidable healing revamp.

First off, I'm 67, with 4 FT, have no healing AA's or focus effects (unless you count SCR1 and 8th Shawl after patch affecting Q), and no GoD or OoW heals. So maybe some healing AAs/Focus Effects/New Spells would help. But ...

I cannot main heal. Well, that's not true: I can main heal in HoH or PoV when screwing around with a couple friends, and I can main heal in LDoN normals. But in any serious content, there is just no chance. In many places I can fill in for a cleric if they go LD, so long as we have single pulls or a chanter. But trying to slow, maintain buffs, and heal using our insanely innefficient heals drains my mana way faster than even CannV, CannIV, C3, my 4 FT, and my Drogmor can replenish it, so I can't do it for very long. And in places where the mobs hit really hard, or if our tank isn't so uber, I can't even main heal for more than a single mob (if the mob hits hard and the tank sucks, then not even for that), because I don't have enough time to slow and tnarg's before the tank is dead, so I just have to chain Tnarg's (with Q thrown in whenever I can fit it).

IMHO,it just comes down to the spells: clerics get CH (7.5k HP for 400 mana), and druids get CH Lite (4.68k for 600 mana), but we get "this doesn't deserve to be called a CH" (1.95k for 400 mana). And since 1.95k every 12.25 seconds just doesn't cut it for healing, we have to use Tnarg's/Daluda's/Yoppa's instead, which are even more inefficient. Even with our great mana regen, we just can't compete because of our massive inefficiency at healing. And then if slow doesn't land, or we get an add, or there is any other sort of damage spike, we are basically screwed because unlike Clerics we don't have a quick heal.

Now, backup healing I can do more consistently. In fact, I generally assume that after landing slow my biggest responsibility is keeping everyone but the tank healed (and even throwing a tnarg's at the tank if the CH gets started too late), and I haven't really had any serious problem meeting this responsibility. The thing is though, with all the clerics I've grouped with (and many of the druids), backup healing is really not needed, since they know when to start CHs and are capable of keeping everyone at full health without my help. If a mob starts beating on them my heal is nice, but they (well the clerics at least) have DA/DB to handle that if it's just one mob, and if we have several then my heals aren't going to make a difference anyway. So it sort of seems like maybe backup healing is something which eases stress on the main healer, but certainly isn't necessary to a group.

I largely agree with Giac that we need a serious revamp of healing in the game, because with clerics being everyone's first choice for main healer in challenging content, and druids (second-class citizens of healing) being the only real alternative (except for high-high end game shaman like Giac), there really just aren't enough healers to go around IMHO; this explains why you can always find hordes of DPS classes LFG, and usually find both CC and tank classes LFG, but the sticking point most of the time is finding a healer. So I agree that Druids should be upgraded to near-cleric level healing: what else do they have going for them? They can't rez (before 70, and even then not for exp) and most people prefer V over 9, so their only advantage over clerics is their nukes, which they don't get to use much when they are busy healing, and their DS, which is nice but doesn't add that much more damage (and clerics even get a DS too).

However, I don't think the solution for shaman is to just to give us cleric healing abilities:
A) We would actually be better healers than clerics if we had their spells, because with Cann we could cast them more often
B) We have slow, and if we could heal as well or even close to as well as clerics and slow at the same time, nobody would want a cleric.

Now, I won't say that I would be devastated if shaman were actually more desirable than clerics or anything, but realistically SoE will never allow it. But since there are 2-4 (depending on if you count bards or necros) other classes that can slow, and only 1-2 others that can heal, it really would be nice if we could fill both roles depending on who is and isn't LFG.

So, here's one thought: what if SoE gave us new heals that were 80%-90% as good as cleric heals, but tacked on a restriction that all of them responded to slows the way every spell responds to Voice of the Berserker. In other words, if you cast Turgur's, Tigir's, etc. you are then unable to cast any of these new healing spells for 1-2 minutes. This way, if the group already has another slower but no cleric or druid, you can use your new healing spells to play a main healer role. But if the group has a cleric/druid and no slower, you can slow and use crappy old heals just as you did before. No matter what though, you couldn't replace both a chanter and a cleric, because the moment you act like a chanter, you get put in the healing penalty box for a bit and are prevented from acting like a cleric.

This is just one thought that I found appealing, but I'm sure there are many other ways SoE could increase the healing abilities of other classes (mainly shaman, since we are the "clerics" of the savage races, and druids, since the game has enough DPS classes already, but I'd even be cool with seeing Necros or Mages given significant healing powers) to the point where making every group does not revolve around finding a cleric, while at the same time not making the existing healer (or healers if you count druids) undesirable. The only question is, will SoE ever see past their blind love of their golden child (clerics) and recognize that the game would be better off if more classes could heal well, and if they did would they be willing to put some effort in to figuring out how to make it happen?

P.S. A lot of pretty high end shaman say they can heal in most challenging content, including planar and OoW exp zones. I don't doubt them. But I also don't think this is really relevant for healing balancing, as the power gap between end-game equipment and bazaar gear is so extreme that it in my opinion it equates to at least five exp levels of power, and I don't think anyone would say that because a 65 shaman can heal in Sebilis everything is ok ;) If you consider the really tough places, where a cleric of that level can heal just fine but a shaman wouldn't stand a chance, I think you'd see the same disparity in healing abilities, or at least close since the more AAs/effects you throw in to the equation the less unbalanced CH (as CH is not affected by AAs/effects, while all other heals are).
