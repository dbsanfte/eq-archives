I absolutely love it on FV. Thera is 34 now and although I could never totally give up all the friends I have on Bristlebane to move full time I play on FV 1-3x per week.

taggletoe
FV has changed alot since it first started. It has much of a blue server ruleset no with a couple distinctions:

TLC- Despite what people may think this rule is terriffic. No more finally getting a group of 20's together to go to Runnyeye just to find a level 55 tearing apart the third level. It basically serves as a "no farming" rule. It does pose some difficulties with quests like Stormfeather but you can use the /raid feature to bypass TLC.

Virtually No "No Drop" items- I ventured back to Drinal the other day and was completely frustrated having to destroy all those damn "no drop" quest pieces. Ever get a group together to go to the Howling Stones just to find out the cleric doesn't have a key? No problem on FV. You can pass down your Hate BP to others, sell it on the market, or keep it in the bank for a rainy day. Primal is even tradeable.

No "Common" Language- It is a real pain at first. The three standard languages that everyone learns is human, elvish, and tier dal. My guildmates keep trying to teach me that filthy "halfling" dialect.

"Bind Rule" Restrictions - This is a major annoyance for me and I have no idea how this lends to roleplay. Casters are limited to meele bind restrictions. It sucks not being able to bind a cleric in an opportune location.

"One Character Rule"- Thanks Sony for making me buy another account.
Every shaman needs a twinked monk.

Overall I think FV displays some of the most mature people of any server I've played on. Yes there are always going to be idiots but I must say the "dood" population on FV is among the lowest.

Roleplaying is hit or miss. I didn't chose this server for roleplaying reasons. I ended up choosing this server because a group of us from a popular board wanted to start characters from scratch on a new server and raid once a week. Well a core of us started playing on FV more than our regular servers. I'm fulltime now though once in a while I miss my friends back on Drinal. /em waves to Wolfish, Shminka, Nivis, and all the others.

thevolcanogod
FV has its positives and negatives like any server but I think the positives far outweigh the negatives. You will have to deal with a lower server population but that means that if you decide to join a raiding guild, the mid-high end is wide open. There are about 5-6 Tier 2-3 guilds out there right now and 2 VT capable guilds who usually are not interested in farming lower tier mobs. The server has a relatively more mature player-base than other servers I have played on and the community is generally welcoming as long as newcomers who wish to raid don’t spit on those who wish to roleplay and vice versa. I feel that there is actually a good proportion of Shamans on FV – meaning that groups can usually find a shaman if they are looking for one. You may have a hard time finding some high-end gear and spells – the lower population plays into this. I made the switch to FV from Torv after I took a long break and came back to find my old guild disbanded and haven’t looked back.
