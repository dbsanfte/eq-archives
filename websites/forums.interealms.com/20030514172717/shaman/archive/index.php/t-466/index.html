Ok..the archetype for shamans is the rough-hewn figure with the spear and shield..it looks cool and is very effective for most of the encounters that a shaman will have.

However for soloing, and I'm guessing for PVP, a bit of flexibility is in order. 2hb damage (my hammer) for a 52 shaman(me) with 200+ str can be up to 95 per round. Add in a decent proc and it's no wonder you get owned in PVP. Add up all the rounds that your spear procs (even if it IS an 80hp dd or more) and you are still being outdamaged.

My suggestion? For PvP make up inventory and melee slots for quick equipment changes like Bards. When you are out of mana swap to the high damage output weapon and lose the shield. Fully buffed with my Hammer of Scourge I can do 60+points per hit and with dex buffs procs a 500hp dot repeatedly with the 40point DD component of Scourge.

I'm a 52 iksar shaman, and fully buffed I can get up to around 2800hps or so (maybe more...I didn't look recently). After a few rounds of spellcasting, and maybe a few JBB shots from a barbarian foe I'm sure to be hurting and well under half my hit points. However, I also have an Iks BP, natural regen and regrowth on all the time, so I'm regenning quite a bit per turn. With my Chill spine I can hit probably 12-26 per round (plus procs of course). Discounting the procs, that means I can probably regen hps' faster than I could stab myself! The key here is high damage output. If it's me with spear vs an opponent with the same regen with a hammer, I am going to be a dead lizard much faster than I can poke my opponent to death.

To sum up: For last ditch toe-to-toe fighting, I dont' hit too often, so i better make it count. For raids and groups you'll find me with a spear and shield 'cause nothing beats it for building a mana pool.
