But requiring a type of technology that impedes access in a time of need is a bad thing in my book. If it was optional, sure. Actually I'm heavily in favor of this idea.

It would punch a huge hole in the Anti-gun proponents arguments as it would nearly eliminate the accidental deaths caused by children and it would make the theft of guns from homes useless as well.

I'm not up to speed on the technology issues regarding this but some problems I see with a few of the proposed systems are...

Finger print identification... Injuries to the hands such as cuts and scrapes will interfere with the recognition technology. For cops or citizens in need of self defense this could cause problems with reliability. Couple that with the fact that fingerprint scanners are at best primitive and have high energy requirements this makes this idea unfeasable for the time being.

Grip recognition indentification... The problem with this is as a person becomes more comfortable in firing a fire arm their grip will change. Change in the grip means gun won't fire. Also grips in target practice tend to be much more relaxed while in live fire situations will be tensed. Grip recognition technology I think is a bad idea as well due to the uncertainty of identification.

Furthermore both the above technologies might have limitations in multiple users using the same gun. Guns in police departments and militaries are sometimes "community" weapons used by several officers on different shifts. And in families spouses often share the same weapons for target practicing and possibly self defense. This could be alleviated I suppose but having chips issued with the guns that remember how a particular user holds the gun or remembers their fingerprint.

The system I think which will see the most favor and has the least complexity is ring identification. Have rings which contain a chip that identify whether this is a valid user of the gun. If the ring is within a 6 inch radial field of the grip of the gun the gun is activatable. Yes this opens the door to possible theft of rings but if the rings are unobtrusive and fashionable (say the size of and look of a wedding band) I would wager many people might be inclined to wear them all the time and if only a limited number of rings are provided with the firearm then only the valid users would have the rings on them at all time. Or if they simply didn't want to wear the rings all the time store the rings in a second secret and secured location (which introduces a second level of security against theft and nosy children so long as a different code is used to access the rings)

The final problem however with ALL these systems is it now introduces electronic components to an otherwise mechanical device.

Electronic components are susceptible to water and other environmental changes and thus by default make guns less rugged. This will be a factor for police military and civilians alike that might make a hurdle to the successful use of this technology.

The other problem is the destruction of electronics by EMPs. EMPs fields and areas are becoming more and more common due to our technological society. More over in the event of a nuclear war the nukes would make all the guns useless. This is primarily an issue for the military which may just forgo using this system all together, but should we be sieged by a foreign nuclear power the civilian ability to resist would be tough if their weapons are no longer functional.

Overall I'm supportive of the idea for application to all legal users of guns and certainly this idea has more merit to me than simply trying to ban guns. It can allow gun ownership to continue while removing a substantial amount of the risk involved.
