Sitting can generate LOTS of aggro. Except then if the mob lands a hit, it hards you HARD!

Have the rogue try 1) evading every single time hide ungrays or 2) evade just prior to each backstab. Depends how much aggro your spells do.

I am sure you know how Rogue "evade" works? But just incase...
Basically the rogue turns OFF attacking for 1/2 a second, then he presses his Hide hotkey. If successful this lowers the rogue on the mobs hate list. Then the mob turns his back to go after you, the rogue backstabs and then presses attack again.
Repeat until mob dead :)

Pretty much the whole life of a rogue consists of doing that over and over and over...... Kinda why I changed from a rogue "main" char to shaman eventually.

< turns off attack >
< waits a split sec >
< hits the Hide key >
< see's if it worked or not >
< presses Backstab >
< turn attack back on >
< waits 6 to 8 secs >
< repeat from start til mob dead >

Backstab itself (successful or not) generates A LOT of aggro so quite often it'll be a continual ping-pong everytime there is a successful evade.

Note However there is a limit to how far down the hate list you can evade so you may still need to throw a spell or two (or melee).

To make a evade hotkey, goto your blank social button things and type this:

Line 1: Attack off(forgot if this is the exact line, its attack, autoattack, something like that)
Line 2: Use ability 1(or whatever slot your hide skill is in)
Line 3: Attack on
This is a very nice way of using evade, and you can use it with pickpocket or anything else that needs attack to be off. One click of the hotkey and you'll have performed the skill without ever manually turning off attack, hope this helps!
