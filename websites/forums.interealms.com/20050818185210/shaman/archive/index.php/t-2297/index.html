Developer's Corner: NPC casters (http://boards.station.sony.com/ubb/everquest/Forum1/HTML/000601.html)

Obviously the recent change to the way NPCs choose their spells has been receiving some attention from players. I think it’s one of the best changes we’ve seen in a while (sure, I like the pet thing too ).
I just wanted to pop in and let you all know that the intent of these changes is to make things a little more… (I would normally use the word “realistic” here, but that usually causes me trouble…) internally consistent. Basically, the tuning stopped when the NPCs were picking spells that were similar to those that PCs pick.

But the spell selections aren’t the only things we’re changing.

For example, and I didn’t even know this happened, Enchanter NPCs used to get an automatic chance to hit with their spells, regardless of the resistances of their target. We took that ability away from them. Now if they want to have a better chance to land spells they are going to have to cast a Tash spell.

The goal here is to make things fairer when it comes to NPC spell casters, and it applies to both sides.

We hope to be examining other aspects of this whole fairness thing as time goes on.

Alan

There are a few MAJOR flaws to this retuning of NPC casters.

The first major flaw is that the NPC's are not constrained by the same false limits that we are as players. A caster that starts casting on you will continue to cast that spell and can hit you no matter where you go. They don't get the "you cant see your target from here" bullstuff that we get, sometimes even when mobs are standing right in front of you even. They have AE spells that are not limited in number of players they can hit (look at tigirs insects being cast by a player versus tigirs being cast by a mob. The mobs is PB. Look at caster NPC's that use rain spells. If you have 30 people in the area of effect, all 30 people get all waves of the rain. If you are a player, then you get like a maximum 4 hits if more than one mob there, or 3 waves if only one mob there. If there are 4 mobs there, each takes only a single wave. More than 4 mobs? That means some of them will be unaffected. Either our restrictions need to be removed, or they need to be enforced upon the mobs as well.

The second major flaw is that it is significantly more difficult to interrupt the casting of an NPC than it is a PC. We can be interrupted just as easily from normal melee as anything else. An npc typically will not be interrupted by melee, only by special attacks, stuns, bashes, kicks from right people, etc. Some NPC mobs cannot be interrupted at all in even these manners.

Third major flaw is that NPC casters typically have nearly as many HP as melee mobs of their same type, and can dish out as much melee damage as the melee mobs of their same type. The mobs should have their melee reduced for the caster versions, and their HP reduced as well.

Fourth major flaw is that the game was balanced based on the way that caster npc's USED to behave. The HP that players have, the gear that exists, the skills that are around, the spells that are in existence...these are all balanced and based on the way that mobs used to behave, including the caster mobs.

Fifth major flaw that I can see is that from what others are posting, mobs are spending a lot more time dispelling people as well. I can tell you that from my point of view that means me having to rebuff people more and more over and over again during the course of a night, even more than before. You have to keep in mind that these NPCs, though smarter, still don’t have the advantages of being played by human beings.
