Tank, in the context used here, is a verb. The ability to take damage with out dying. Not counting slows or heals (self or otherwise), to tank a MOB is to take the damage that MOB is dishing out with out dying. The only factor of tanking a MOB lies in the tanks ability to mitigate the damage that tank recieves. The vast majority of a real tanks ability to mitigate damage lies in their defensive abilities which no other classes have. This is not the case for clerics. An average 65 pally can out agro any class. If they are not out agroing a monk then they are way below average.

As for mitigation, max hit on mobs is always the same regardless of class say that sand giant lord hits for 1500 will hit every class for 1500 when it hits for max.

(just pulling these numbers out of my head as an example, not real figures)

The idea of mitigation is that when you don't take max damage different classes take it differently.

Say plate classes mitigate that 1500 to an average of 750, while chain classes mitigate it 900, leather 1100, cloth 1300 ect. However it doesn't actually work like that as each class has its own mitigation table that doesn't depend on the armor type. Some leather classes mitigate beter then others ect.

When they nerfed monks they didn't change how often they got hit, they changed only thier mitigation level so instead of mitigating damage like a warrior (lot of mid range hits) they were mitigating it like a druid (lot of max hits). They didn't completely change them back, now they are somewhere around a ranger in mitigation.

What people keep getting confused is avoidance and mitigation.

Avoidance determines if you get hit at all. Every class has its own avoidance skills and skill caps. The more skills and the higher the caps the more hits they avoid all together.
