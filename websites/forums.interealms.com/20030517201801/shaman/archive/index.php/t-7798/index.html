Now I've just done a search on rampage and found this interesting info from Gresgoth explaining how rampage works

Rampage is a single target based attack in ADDITION to the normal single target attack of a mob. How does one get Rampaged you ask? Well, it's very simple. The second person to aggro Rampaging_mob01 gets added to the "Rampage List". Rampaging mobs have 2 aggro type lists. One is normal "aggro list", and the second is the "rampage list". If you are the second person to aggro the rampaging mob, your name gets added to the "Rampage list" forever (unless the mob dies).

Let's give an example: High Priest of Ssraeshza is a rampaging mob. The first person to attack (or cast on) the high priest is immediately added to the aggro list. Now, the next person that aggros him will be put on the "rampage list". When in melee range, the high priest will attack the first person on his "aggro list". In addition to the single target attack, when you see the message "High Priest of Ssraeshza goes on a RAMPAGE!", the first person on the "rampage list" is being attacked. *Note: if the person on the "Rampage list" is out of melee range, the high priest will attack the next person who is in range on the "rampage list".

Harwel
Back out of melee range. If this isn't possible due to mob having a huge melee range (ie you can't get away at all, or can't get away without aggroing adds), then you either live through it, or you don't... lol. If a really heavy-hitting rampage mob is what you're fighting and can't afford the damage, then you should be having "rampage tanks" engage after the MT engages in order to fill up the rampage list with people who can stand the hits, BEFORE you start debuffing.

Edit: in my memory, Maestro has always Rampaged... due to his size and range, and the relatively small size of the safe spot you probably pulled him to, this is one of the cases where you should either wait for some other melee to engage, or just take your lumps. :p
gandalfh

Jaidin
The rampage list goes according to the linear order in which people engaged the mob. It is independent of aggro which is why when your rampage tank goes down, if its a big mob, he will usually rampage clerics (as they are the ones who have healed the MT before other melees have engaged).

If you are taking rampage hits theres no way to "derampage" yourself basically. Just try and live, or die :)

As for melee range affecting rampage, I am unsure as some people say that some mobs rampage differently. Perhaps some will not summon players who they are rampaging on, but I do know that others will summon when they rampage.

Basically, if you are taking rampage hits then you cast on the mob a little too early or the rampage tank(s) have fallen.
