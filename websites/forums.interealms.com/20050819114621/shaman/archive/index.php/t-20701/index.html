Thunderwolfjr
From reading these boards everyday for the last year or so I get the impression that many high end shaman are dissatisfied with how EQ is evolving and chosing to either play alts or leave the game.

However, it has also become apparent to me that (at least on my server, the Nameless) very few new players are making shaman or leveling them up to 65. My proof, well, over the last couple of weeks, the number of level 60-65 shaman spells for sale in the bazaar has risen considerably on the Nameless server. This of itself is not terribly unusual, the availability of spells is always dynamic to a certain extent. What is odd, and telling, is that these spells are not selling. This morning, I checked my mule, and saw 9 copies of Malos, 5 copies of Torpor, 3 copies of Malo, 4 copies of Q and 13 copies of FA. One mule has had 5 copies of Malos selling for 5k each for the last 3 days and has not sold a single copy. FA has dropped from 30-50k to 10-30k.

Obviously, supply and demand dictate pricing, however, these trends make me believe that while the supply has been fairly consistent, (except for a period right after any new expansion when everyone abandons the old world zones to explore the new zones and content) demand has dropped sharply.

I may be arriving at my conclusion a bit prematurely, but seeing this trend in the bazaar, reading these boards, and doing a "who all" in game and finding a quadzillion beastlords, makes me believe that there really are less shaman in game now.

Just my observation, I would be interested in hearing what others are seeing on other servers.

Serepi
It's not just shaman spells though; I have seen the prices of most spells drop - although, necro spell prices seem to be holding firm.

I've actually been thinking about this topic quite a bit, as I am my guilds treasurer/vendor/banker/spell guy/tradeskill item bank etc. I have a trader that I keep up in bazaar for extra runes etc. that would have rotted. Q is down from 60k to anywhere between 25-35k depending on where the competition is pricing. But it's not just shaman spells. SoS is down to 30k or less, HoV is down to 20-30k (down from a solid 50k even a few weeks ago.) I think it's just that theres a glut in the market as more and more supply comes into existance, and there isn't a corresponding increase in demand. To have the best price, guild mules are undercutting, trying to get those last few fish in the sea, so to speak.

Amazingly, necro spells seem to be holding value. I don't know why, but Child of Bertoxx is a cash cow. I can never seem to keep enough in stock, and they swiftly move even at 50k+. Must be attributable to necros ability to farm and a willingness to pay a premium.

Bottom line: There's a finite market for spells. They're not consumed (like food/drink) and you don't need to replenish your spellbook once you have Q, etc. That fact, combined with increased supply (at least on Rodcet Nife server) and static demand = falling spell prices all around.

It seems to be the same way on my server, Brell Serilis, although it could just be my imagination. There are three factors to take into account and all three effect the shaman community.

1. PoTime cap / GoD expansion: you tend to see a lot of people retire after the "end game" has been reached. For many, the aggravations involved in getting to the previous end-game (PoTime) makes looking at the new end-game (GoD) seem like more hassle than its worth. People just get tired and fed-up.

2. DX9: to have this thrown in shortly after the GoD really was a kicker. For many that stayed and wanted to continue to progress this was a slap in the face. Some upgraded, some are going to upgrade, and some just said to hell with it.

3. Shaman nerfage: What is percieved is always stronger than what is reality. Shamans have had a lot of nerfage recently due to content- along with many other classes. A few notice, and post their concerns on the boards. They have problems with X content. The Shaman public snowballs it into we have problems in X, Y, and Z content. To be honest, we do have problems in X, Y, and Z content, but there are always groups who don't have the specialists that need the "jack-of-all trades." Shamans feel in general that they are inadequate and many retire/reroll.

I have noticed recently a lack of shamans lfg on a regular basis, and from a guild standpoint I have seen a lot of classes hang up the towel due to the headache of PoP flagging stacked on top of GoD trial flagging. It is pretty much the only way to progress in the game, so gear upgrades and time well spent are showing up less and less. The game is 5 years old, and I don't think it was to progress as far as it did. People are just getting tired and are moving on to something else.
