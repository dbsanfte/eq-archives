Jinheim
You can duo well with any melee except a rogue, really. A rogue duo is doable but not really practical, and it requires a lot more effort, especially if two-boxing.

At level 65, I would rather duo with an SK than any other melee. They can hold aggro over your dots without trouble, their damage is significantly better than a paladin, and they bring snare and FD to a group. A well geared and AA'd ranger can tank fine in pop, but it will take a while to get him to that stage. Same thing for monks, except they will have trouble holding aggro. It is rare that I see a monk tanking any more. Beastlords are like monks except they don't mitigate as well (I think), but have an easier time holding aggro. Warriors and Paladins do just fine, but I prefer SK.

On the way to 65, though, you can do just fine with any melee partner. Monks aren't as great as they used to be, but mobs damage output is still pretty gimpy so they can handle it. They bring great damage and FD, so they're still perfectly viable. SKs and Paladins will do fine, but their damage is pretty crappy. Warrior will do ok. Ranger will work pretty well if you have decent gear on him. Beastlord would be great, but in my experience two boxing a bst (my only alt is a bst) there are just too many spells that you need to cast to play both characters to their full potential, which makes twoboxing a pain.

So, basically, just pick whichever melee class you think is the coolest and play that. It is pretty easy to make any duo work when one of the characters is a shaman.

BlargX
65 shaman, retired my 60 monk with 18 AA's when the monk nerf hit. Unless a monk gets really really good gear, he is not the tank he used to be. In PoP, groups often dissolve unless you have a plate tank.

Tried warrior, but I missed the FD power of a monk, and liked the idea of having better aggro than a warrior gives, and having snare, the one spell I most wished my shaman had. So I made an SK, and really love him.

I put him in cultural armor, and at level 58 he has 218 stamina unbuffed without any AA(except runspeed 2). He's an ogre, and almost any buffs put him at maxed STA and STR.

I love the flexibility! He can snare pull and then someone can pull the mob separated away from the slow mob who also charges in while he FD's, or he can wait for the other mobs to re-set and lose aggro, then pop up and still have plenty of aggro on the snared mob(SK snares are also very aggrolicious DOT's) and get it as a single. There's a good deal of flexibility. SK's also have very very fast DD spells, so you can take quick advantage if you see a sudden opening to aggro something. The combination of FD pulling and snaring is a powerful one that you can really play with. Sometimes I pull with the shaman, sometimes with the SK; sometimes I root the pull, sometimes I don't; a lot of flexibility.

He doesn't put out the damage my monk did, but he could be buffed to 255 sta/str in his low 20's and at 52 he had more HP than my monk did with natural durability AA's ! Holey moley. His damage is still fine, and he has the wonderful ability to use lifetaps to keep his health high even without my help, or, since healing him is no probably at all with quintessence, he can use his mana taps to keep his mana very high, letting him cast DD's and snares and dots.

My monk used to sometimes have a devil of a time getting aggro even though he was well equipped; my SK almost never loses aggro. This lets me canni away peacefully and happily while he does his business, and do my DOT's or DD's as much as I like. Could not do that with monk; my shaman could DD with him, but DOT's were much more of a problem.

Also, although I lost a bit of damage output with the SK, perhaps, I gained snare, a great deal of flexibility, a character with BOTH invis and ITU, I kept the FD power, and got a character who can do a lot of interesting things and even has a second weakie pet to add to the equation, plus a nice harmtouch to deal with those jerks who like to try to KS mobs. And as an ogre, unlike my monk, he's not stunnable from the front, so he's not about to lose aggro due to being stunned.

Further, SK's can get one of the best items in the game, and I really do love it -- the circlet of shadow, which casts invis instantly; you can even have it drop or click it right in front of mobs and have it re-invis you without them detecting it. They can also lev, and anyone can get run3 or jboots, so this is a melee toon who can get around very very easily.

Rangers are very powerful, but they're like bards sometimes in that they spend so much effort trying to show how powerful they are that they become mana sinks and screw everything up. Really, if they would just stop getting the cleric summoned, everyone would be a lot better off.
Rangers are extremely far from good tanks these days; their uberness comes from their bows and utility spells like snare.

If you are with a shaman duoing, you want someone who can first of all get high aggro but also can tank. Leaving aside pride-based arguments and elemental-based armor and weapons, ranger fills only one of those two necessary abilities; monk only one of those, warrior only one of those(and is entirely too dependent on a very few weapons in the game to hold taunt well), SK both, pally both, rogue only one, BL sometimes both but too complicated and you might need to root a lot. Necro is very strong but he won't be pulling any aggro off the shaman without taking a good chance of dying. Bard playing songs and shaman playing at his max is just much too much for the average or even really sharp player. The bards I know who duo mostly play their bard and the other toon contributes minimally.

The shaman is flexible and powerful enough to cover up for toons that don't quite fit in with him, though, so no choice will really be a terrible one. Of my 60 monk, 52 warrior, 54 druid, and 58 SK, I'd have to say that both my personal experience and reasoning point pretty clearly to the SK.
