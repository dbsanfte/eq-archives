I love Everquest, but it's a different game now than it was back then. I remeber Verant always being reluctant to make changes, because they had a very specific Vision of what Everquest should be. Today that Vision has been dumped upon more times than I can count, and basically thrown out the window by SOE. Some people are happy about this, and would use The Vision(tm) as a punch line, but to me this is what kept me playing this game longer than all the other games I have ever played combined.

I like some of the changes:

-New guild management feature
-10 hotkeys instead of 6
-movable buff window
-no more book (i did my time with the book, and in a way I want others to have to walk in snow, but in a game of graphics, having to stare at a book for 35 levels and miss the game is ridicilous)
-items stacking changes (holding ctrl etc)
-being able to type exact number of coins
-soulbinders
-latest ragefire change.

Things I don't like:
-Port stones -- travel was fun, and it forced you to learn to navigate
-Map feature -- see above
-Graveyards
-cleric epics
-no corpse loss 1-10
-increased exp 1-10

Everquest was fun for me because it was immersive. I was walking blindly through a world that I knew nothing about, and the world was abosultly unforgiving. If I got to close to the wrong skeleton, I was dead. If I walked into a hollowed out tree, I lost a corpse. If I didn't pay attention to where I was, I was lost for hours, and often lost a corpse. If I wanted to hunt in commons or oasis, I faced a days worth of traveling and sneaking around, praying I didn't get lost or killed by a ranodm hill giant. I felt genuine fear of zones like east karana, gorge of king xorb, kithcor etc.

Todays version of EQ is suited more for the masses, it still provides its entertainment, and I play more heavily now than I did 4 years ago. However I play for different reasons. Now I enjoy the challegne of doing new things with my guild and experience new raid content. Back then I simply enjoyed being in the world.
