Is there an upper limit on how much you can be hasted by spells and item effects? If you have 2 haste items do they stack or do you just get the larger of the two haste increases?
Goldark
You can stack one item haste with one spell haste, for a combined maximum of (I think) 100% haste. For this purpose, bard songs count as spell haste. Bard songs may appear to stack if sung after the haste, but this is just to allow the non-haste components to stack. So whilst both buffs are there, you only recieve the best individual haste. The haste from two items does NOT* stack.

This is all then thrown out the window by a few bard songs and the odd item or self-buff, which WILL stack with another haste spell/song, and can even break the haste cap. The most common example is War Song of the Vah Shir which adds 25% more haste. They are the exceptions to prove the rule :)

I'm no expert on it though, so someone please correct my mistakes :)

* I think the eye patch of plunder has a haste effect that does stack. I might be wrong...check Lucy.

Eyepatch of plunder doesnt stack, its just the same as regular old spell haste.

Its Best item haste (EG an fbss) + Best regular spell haste (EG Swift like the wind) + Best Overhaste (EG Warsong of Vahshir or Rizlona's call of flame) + Special case haste (The only example I can think of being Bow haste from a quiver).

Bow haste from a quiver count as item haste. It's only taken in account in those cases where it's better than the best, 'general usage' haste available from another item. And only for bow usage, of course.
