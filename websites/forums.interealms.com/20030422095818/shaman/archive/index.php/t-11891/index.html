Hail,

Here's how I do it. It isn't perfect, it probably isn't even smart sometimes, but it works for me when grinding exp. Raid mini-bosses or named are handled differently. This method requires Torpor & Malo to be effective.

Preparation:
Two simple hotkeys setup to /assist Puller & /assist Maintank.
I force the puller to announce his/her incoming. "Inc %t" is all I really need.

Action:
1. On the incoming mesage, I hit my puller assist key. I spam click it until a target shows up in window.
1a. If the puller announces adds or is intentionally pulling multiples, I automatically cast Torpor on myself at this point. (Torpor is hotkeyed with a "Torporing %t" message btw.)

2. Spam click Malo until the mob is in casting range. The aggro on this is extremely mild, and if it is a warrior tanking, his arrow shots on incoming will overcome the Malo aggro. (If the warrior didn't bring arrows & the group has no access to free ones, expect to have initial aggro.) With Paladin/SK tanks, this isn't even an issue.

3. Cast Slow with Turgur's as soon as your gems refresh. Note, on most pulls, this will have Turgur's landing or resisting right as the mob enters the camp. With a good paladin/SK tank, the aggro still goes right to them if slow lands the first try because they are stunning, casting Darkness, etcetera on the mob as it is incoming as well.

4. Towards the middle or end of casting Turgur's (step 3), /pet attack. Sending in the dog at this point will generally position him on the opposite side of the tank, which is what I want so he avoids enrage damage, and saves the tank from having to flip (which they generally won't do just for a shaman pet anyway).

(If slow is resisted, I go one of two routes, usually dependent on the tank's class & my trust in the primary healer.)

5a. With a Warrior tanking, I Torpor myself before casting Turgur's again. (While casting Torpor, I announce slow was resisted x1.)
5b. With a Pal/SK tanking, I cast Turgur's ASAP again. Recast up to a 3rd time & then Torpor self. I announce all slows resisted. "resist x2, resistx3, I'm dead now," etc. :D

I'm sitting at around 4400-4600hp buffed most of time while exp grinding w/ AC buffed anywhere around 1070-1150, so nothing uber, but good enough to get the job done. This is enough to be able to tank most Tier1 or 2 mobs (while Torpored) for the period of time before aggro shifts. Unless slow resists more than twice, I generally never require ANY outside healing.

It takes some time to train the pick-up primary healers NOT to heal me unless they see "resists" in /gsay. Some never learn. But most clerics & druids eventually see the light. Torpor w/ SCR3, SCRM, & 8th shawl is an extremely efficient HoT.

Take care,

It depends.

In my prefered group with a bard pulling and a SK tanking, the mobs never look at me... The bard will occlusion and slow the mob before it gets to camp, the SK will snare as soon as its in range (even ifits not snareable, its good agro), I slow as soon as its in range, and never get agro.

In a typical group, if the tank pulls they will have enough agro for me to slow with only occasionaly getting agro. I malo as soon as the mob is in range (on its way to camp) and slow as soon as the gem refreshes (usualy land slow before the assist call).

If we have a dedicated puller (fd splitting or lull/pacify pulling) I know I will have agro at the beginning of the fight. I stand where the MT is waiting for the mob (so he wont have to chase it). I torpor myself at the end of the fight (while cannibalizing back whatever mana I used) and if things are moving along this means I have torpor on when the puller brings the next mob in. I malo as soon as the mob is in range, and slow as soon as the gem refreshes. Slow will usualy land just as the mob reaches camp -and I will take the first few hits while the mt attacks/taunts/whatever it is they do. Once the mt has agro I move back a few steps, we kill it, rinse and repeat.

In any case, the mob is slowed as soon as it reaches camp. I use malo even at 65 to ensure that my slow lands on the first try. I can tank a slowed PoP mob for a bit if I have to. I cant keep a tank alive versus an unslowed PoP mob, much less a caster.

If there are multiple mobs, I trust the crowd controll person to handle them. If I am the cc person, I root the first one in range, and then try to land slow on the one we are fighting before the tank dies... if not, well.. I told them to pull singles :p
