weissblau
These complaints fall into one of four major categories -

1. complaints about bugs in the game

2. complaints about unimplemented or poorly implemented
features

3. complaints about poor customer service

4. complaints about SUBJECTIVE phenomena in the game ("They aren't making the game work like *I* want them to!")

I got news for you - #1 and #2 are opposites. You make changes to add or reimplement features, you WILL add bugs. You can't have your cake and eat it too unless you are willing to spend hella money on software testing. The only applications that make this economically worthwhile are medical applications, some military applications, and flight applications - basically applications where human life is at risk. Ain't gonna happen for EQ. Want to spent $100/month on the game? I didn't think so.

From what I've seen Verant has changed since November in that they are trying to address #2 harder than they did before. This has a natural affect of increasing the number of bugs in the short term. Before the VI employee purge, they placed a higher emphasis on stability and gained a reputation for being unresponsive developers.

As for customer service, I haven't seen any evidence that this has gotten better or worse in the last six months. I know I have seen more GM events. Again, economics rears its ugly head here. Paid employees are EXPENSIVE.

And #4 is just whining. Pay your money or get out.

$13 isn't the ransom of kings, folks. Software is difficult and expensive to develop and support, and sometimes murderously difficult to extend. VI is working on a four-year-old code base and keeping it running under full use pressure. Yeah, there are some things that could have been done better, but in general they are on the right track doing the right things.
