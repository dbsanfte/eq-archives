theknightmax
Basic shaman fighting style mostly consist of slowing the mob so it does a lot less damage adding a dot to it and fighting it face to face (IN TEH FACE). Once you have a pet you still fight the exact same way. You posistion the pet so it is biting at the heals of the mob.

It really is neccessary to fight like this through your 40s till your deffense is maxxed. And this method is very valid on almost any pre pop mob.
Donar
As Oogrunk stated, you'll need to debuff the mob, i.e., slow the mob and then melee the mob while dotting it and/or nuking it to death. Some mobs may require you to root/rot them. By the way, always have root memmed. It's very necessary at times if you need to step back and heal yourself. As recommended, keep your defense maxxed for each level, if possible. In the lower levels, you have to use all your tools to solo mobs. You have to melee some, dot or nuke some.. if you have the pet, you need to use him too. Sometimes, you and the pet will have to take turns tanking, especially if the mob is really dealing out some damage. The best way to learn, though, is get out and try for yourself. Find out which style of combat suits your personality best.. while maintaining your defense skill.. ;) In groups, you'll be slower, healer, buffer and crowd controller at times. Life as a shaman can be hectic, but rarely boring. Good Luck!! :)
Solariis
At 19 the root-dot method is not ideal because your dots aren't very good yet, root will break a lot and your slow isn't great so that when root does break the mob is going to pound on you pretty fast still. If you are a non-twink I suggest grouping as much as possible till 24. If you are heavily twinked you can probably tank the mob quite well.

At 24 life changes a lot with regen, canni1 and envenomed breath. By stacking tainted and envenomed breath mobs will die fairly fast and the best part is that with canni and regen you can regain a lot of your mana while the mob is rooted.

As you progress in levels and get your pet and your slow gets even more effective and your dots start doing some nasty damage the whole process just gets easier and easier. At that point you pull a mob with slow, sick pet on it, throw on a dot and root mob and do your cannidancing to regain mana. You'll want to join with pet and melee to keep offensive and defensive skills up. When you do this make sure to be on the opposite side of the mob as pet since mob's can't dodge, block or riposte from behind. That makes ol' Vebarn even more effective. If you can't get aggro just root the mob and step in its face so it aggroes you and puppy can hit it from the back.

At 19 I couldn't solo worth a crap with my newb equipment so I mostly grouped. At 24 I could solo a lot better but it was still pretty slow going. At 49 we really start to shine and at 51 we get the best slow in the game and soloing becomes a breeze.
