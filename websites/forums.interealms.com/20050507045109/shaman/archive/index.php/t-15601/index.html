Athenae
Hmmm yeah that quest is a loooong one. I personally have all the items to make the captain spawn except the Special Brew and let me tell you it is damn rare.

Here is what you need...Attendent Mia Ta (something like that) at the very back room of Hate's Fury asks for 4 items to make the captain spawn: a Mystical Spyglass, an Ancient Compass, Map of Norrath's Oceans, and the captains favorite spirits = Okeil's Special Brew (spelled something like that).


Mystical Spyglass: This can drop randomly and rarely off of any of the above-deck named mobs ie Uumuvan the Soulless, Scout Shin Ci, Lieutenant Commander Vzain, Harbinger.

Ancient Compass: This can drop randomly and rarely off of any of the below-deck named mobs ie The Broken Skull Armsmaster, Soqui the Forgotten, Quartermaster Fisan, High Priest Anaanci etc

Map of Norraths Oceans: This drops off of a Broken Skull Navigator who is located in the globe room (the room with High Priest Anaanci in it). The Navigator has a ph if he is not up already. The place holder is the one who is just to the left of the High Priest spawn on the sunken down floor there. You will see three mobs on the sunken floor, your ph is the one closest to the High Priest spawn.

Okeil's Special Brew: This is going to be the hardest part to camp by far. The Drunken Buccanear spawns in Quartermaster Fisan's area. If you follow the ladder down from zone in, then follow the hall back from the torture chamber you will see a room with a few Broken Skull Weaponsmiths in it. Go in that room and you will see a little nook on your left with 3 rats in it.

The drunk spawns right there next to the three rats. But unlike the rest of the mobs in the zone this is a triggered spawn as opposed to having a placeholder you kill over and over. I'm not exactly sure what triggers him to spawn but you can usually do it by killing every mob in that room. So you finally spawn the drunk and kill him and he has the brew right you say? Wrong, the drunk only drops the special brew very rarely, his common drop is a bottle of vodka which other than making for a tasty refreshment is totally useless. So good luck with this part its the only piece I need. I know he drops the special brew because a friend of mine got it.

Once you get the 4 pieces get a few strong groups and go turn them to Attendant Mi'Ta be ready to fight Captain Jrank. I know he spawns several 'Fists of Jrank' that assist him, not sure if they mezzable but probably. Also note that the pieces needed in this quest can be multiquested so that might make it easier. I'll bet he drops some truly unique stuff if you can kill him.


Good luck :)

I've done overlord, overseer, and *attempted* the captain (who, at least for us, was named Krasnok, not Jrank).

The only hard part about overlord (for his level, and whom his gear is targetted at) is his AE curse. Feeblemind sucks. Of course, with radiant cure, this shouldn't be too much of an issue, as he really doesn't have that much HP.

Overseer Wrank we were able to spawn within about 45 minutes or so of entering from Dulak's. He's cake. The only thing special about him is his AE that makes casting twice as long, although the melee slow portion of it is really annoying, as well; it makes it take a while to kill him.

Some notes from our fight with the captain:

We took four groups, average level 62 or so. This would have been plenty, had we known what to expect. Here's what happens:

Captain and FOUR "Fist of <Captain's Name>" spawn simultaneously. The Fists spawn in the corners of the room, and immediately bumrush the center. I believe our shaman said they were disease slowable. Here's the catch: they won't leave melee range of the captain. Any attempts to pull them out of melee range of the captain will result in them beating on whoever is highest on the captain's aggro list. If they are kept beneath the captain, they should be offtankable - but it may be a feature of his rampage that he wipes aggro on them when he rampages. In that case, knights are best to tank them, as they can get aggro back quickly.

The captain has four AEs:

Hate's Fury
Drowning Panic
Bleeding Ether
Languish of the Sea

Info on them is as follows:

2: Cancel Magic(9)
3: Decrease Hitpoints by 100

Recast Time: 4.8
Resist: Magic
AE Range 200
Interruptable: Yes
Target Type: PB AE

From what we could tell, you have to be within melee range of the captain to land spells. A level 65 wizard couldn't land any lures from outside melee range (tried all three resist types).

The fists have a 122 DB, and 9.6 DI (max hit 314). The captain has a 192 DB, and 22 DI (max hit 632). I can't really give details as to the total HP of the captain; things went downhill too quickly after the MT went down (her primary healer went down because of too much aggro from quick heals, we didn't really know what to expect).
