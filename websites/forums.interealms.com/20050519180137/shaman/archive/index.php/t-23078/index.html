Asta Khaan
Back then resists were hard to come by, and you had to prioritize your gear and/or switch gear depending on opposition. Most everyone put primary focus on mr to resist root/snare/blind and cr to resist ice comet, alternatively fr versus mage and druid nukes.

This benefited shaman because it was rare to find an unbuffed pr above 70. Disease often lower than that, with only int casters going a bit higher with stein of moggok equipped. The bane of shaman were the ogre butcher gloves with their 25pr/dr, specially on a warrior with indo boots 15pr/dr.

However, apart from that ebolt almost always landed. Melees got knocked down to half life by it, and any caster who couldnt heal would die from it unless they had some hp buff up. Remember hp gear was very scarce too. Ebolt was pretty much fire and forget. Curing ebolt was only an option for the priests, and they seldom had the spell memmed anyway.

Root, blind and slow actually landed for crowd control. Add shaman utility, sow speed and resist buffs. Yes we were total powerhouses, only really rivalled by a wizard who got the jump on you. Even then ebolt had a 0.1sec lead on IC cast time, which was always fun to exploit.

Didn't really need any strat back then. With the basic spell lineup:

dispell
root
canna
togors or sow, depending on opposition
ebolt
VoS or plague
blind
gheal

What you have to remember is that back then instacast storebought pumice still existed, 13pp a pop. Anyone who realized the power of this and used it extensively (im talking 10 full pumices a fight) would be very much difficult to beat. Imagine being trailed by a troll warrior who hits you with a dispell every 5 secs. You wont be running away from him. It all comes down to his magic resist and if you can land a blind. Root would be dispelled instantly and togors had too long cast time.
