Steelbear
Good post in that other thread, Grafsha. At least I'm not the only one anymore. This will be a long post - fair warning.

Keep begging for scraps at the table, folks. Just dont be surprised when you get your nose slapped. We all should know by now how these things work - you ask for one thing, and you get half, at best. Keep asking for only an 1800-point direct heal, because that will solve all our healing problems, without infringing upon clerics or druids. You'll end up with a 1425 direct heal that costs twice as much mana as Tnarg's.

I know it's too early to be predicting gloom and doom because, based on Brenlo's comments, these changes had nothing to do with our discussions here. But you need to realize now what will happen if you continue to accept your position beneath druids as priests, and dont change your tune.

They will happily leave us as third-rate healers below druids, meanwhile paladins just got a step closer to us with a 1-second cast 1000 point heal. I cant bring up Lucy right now, but I believe that paladin heal is one of the 30-second recast ones, right? Even so, how often do you cast Tnarg's? Unless it's an emergency like an overpull or resisted slow, do you honestly cast Tnarg's more often than that? I know I dont. Yeah, we're still better because we can cast ours more often, if the situation demands it, but havent we already discovered that in today's level 65 arena Tnarg's just doesnt cut it?

You cannot honestly use slow as an excuse any longer. Too many classes have it. It's been mitigated in every expansion since they came up with the idea of mitigation. And hardly anybody ventures into any new or difficult content without some kind of slower. So even though druids and clerics cant cast it - somebody with them most likely can. And, guess what - somebody else's mana pool is saving them healing mana.

If anything, I think cannibalize (CannV, more specifically) is what keeps us from getting better heals, in the view of the developers. They probably think we'd be able to sustain our mana indefinitely if we had druid-sized heals coupled with cannibalize and CannV. In practical use, this theory is flawed however.

Cannibalizing only helps us recover mana faster, with the penalty of not being able to do anything else while we are cannibalizing. During a melee, which typically doesnt last longer than a minute (or two if you have multiples), if we're slowing we cant recover mana. If we're healing, we cant be recovering mana. If we're rooting, or VP'ing, or Spirit Calling, we arent recovering mana. If you're getting beat on after a slow, you cant recover mana (for a couple reasons). And if CannV happens to be fresh, or refreshes during the fight, then we can get a shot in the mana pool - but we have to spend some of that mana right away to heal ourself, and then maybe another heal for the person getting hit while we spent the time to heal ourself. And hopefully, the mob doesnt aggro when we hit the tank with that heal, and turn on us. One swipe from a 1200 hitting mob eliminates the Q you just dropped on yourself, and since you just CannV'ed, now you probably have low health aggro and you die. Or you play it safe in a fight with heavy hitters like that, and you dont CannV at all - in which case, it's benefit is nil.

All of these things we may need to do in order to help keep the party alive in one way or another, prevent us from recovering mana and on top of that are usually costing us mana at the same time. Couple this with the fact that our spells typically cost as much or more than our counterparts, for less effect, means we are just as easily run out of mana as they are - something the general population doesnt realize. They think shamans = endless mana. They understand if a cleric or druid has to med after a big fight, but they dont understand if a shaman has to spend time to recover mana. They think you're a bad shaman, when in fact your other functions prevented you from recovering mana during the action.

Now, in a good group - one that moves along nice and orderly, I probably wont ever run out of mana. In fact, I usually dont drop below 60 mana. But, then neither does the druid or cleric, in such a group. But ideal situations are not the place to measure anything. Proving grounds are usually the harshest situations - because those actually provide a test of the abilities. And even good groups can go bad sometimes, and that's when you find out who can cut the mustard. And with the current state of our heals, even if I was the smartest and best shaman in the world, I wouldnt make the grade if the **** hit the fan.

The solution to this particular problem, in my opinion, is to restore shamans to equal healing power as druids are now, but make our heals slightly less efficient. Key word is slightly - not 200% or 150% mana cost, more like 120% mana cost to offset CannV, but the same amount healed, and cast times. This will bring us back to the table as a viable backup healer, or primary healer when you cant get a cleric (or the cleric dies in the middle of a fight). We're still not going to eclipse the other priests due to cannibalize, for the reasons I outlined already above...bigger heals, that cost more mana, are going to run us out of mana even quicker than before. But it will give us a fighting chance in those bad situations, if we are wise with our mana.

We're not going to replace clerics, because they still have the real complete heal, and far superior quick heals and group heals (something we dont have at all). We're not going to replace druids altogether, either. Yes, if the group needs a healer, and doesnt have a slower yet - they'd pick us. If the group already had an enchanter or beastlord - they'd be better off taking a druid for snare, damage shield, and better nukes.

For those of you who will say this will stick us into the healing role and nothing else, I ask you this; how does improving our healing take away from anything else that we already offer? Our other spell abilities dont up and evaporate if our healing is improved. We will still be able to do everything else we did before, but we'll also be able to heal once again on an equal footing with our counterpart priest (the druid).

And dont come along and quote the part where I said I never run out of mana or usually dont go below 60 mana, and say that proves my entire argument wrong. Because quoting things out of context doesnt prove anything, except that you can ignore the actual relevant points which preceeded or followed it.

Are there other issues that need to be addressed also? Some minor things, perhaps. Overall, I'm satisfied with most of my other abilities, but my healing is very lacking. As a priest, that concerns me more than whether the resist mod or percentage on the new slow is good enough, or whether focus gives enough hps compared to blessing of the ninth, or whether I get a new nuke spell that doesnt suck balls, or whether our bastard stepchild class can outbuff us. Yeah, those things may rub me the wrong way, but I can live with them. As a priest, I cant live with my heals - and neither can my group - and that threatens my very usefulness.

It's all about hit points healed per second and throwing in all these other factors(slow, canni, etc...) just confuses the issue. This is where we have significantly fallen behind the other two priest classes in healing power.

Tnarg(Shaman) 1300 HP/6 sec = 216.66 HP/Sec
Sylvan(Druid) 2100 HP/6 sec = 350 HP/Sec
Holy Light(Cleric) 3275 HP/6 sec = 545.83 HP/Sec

The one thing I do disagree with Galdeltron on is:
These calculations do not include the effects of AA's or focus effects, which I assume would have similar effects for all classes.
If you factor in a healing focus item(20%), Healing Adept(10%), and Advanced Healing Adept(9%) this disparity becomes even greater.

Tnarg 1300+260+247= 1807 or 301.16 HP/sec
Sylvan 2100+420+399 = 2919 or 486.5 HP/sec
Holy Light 3275+655+622 = 4552 or 758.7 HP/sec

My math and assumptions may not be completely correct but if a mathematician wants to point out my flaws then please post the correct results and you'll still get a similar comparison.
