I'll answer this, because I see you play a 56 shaman, so you're in a similar situation to me. This is my approach in Tier 1 planes and other places - it may be different for Tier 2+ where mobs hit harder, but I'll find that out when I get there.

At 59, my fast heal is Chloroblast. 428 hp heal, 3 second cast. Turgur's Insects is also 3 second cast.

Chain casting Chloroblast or using Stoicism will possibly keep the cleric alive against an unslowed tier 1 PoP mob, but maybe not. So, if the cleric is nearly dead, I'll heal. If there's a little leeway, I will slow, and leave the cleric to heal himself or DA. My heal isn't going to help a lot against an unslowed mob, but the slow will make a difference. Even if the slow is resisted, the mob will usually turn on me, unless the cleric is very low health. If the slow hits, then healing for me or the cleric won't be a problem.

However, I would like to say that this doesn't happen very often. It's usually when we get adds that one pounds on the cleric when he heals the tank, and I mostly have time to slow, or to get resisted slow agro before the cleric gets to the point of low health agro.

Of course, at 62 we get a vastly improved quick heal, 1300 hp in 3.75 seconds cast, which will be much more effective than Chloroblast.
