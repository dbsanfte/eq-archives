Shabura Naarteban
My top 10. These aren't necessarily the most impactful, or the most destructive. Flair and uniqueness helped a lot at getting into the top 10. Anyone can be a crash bug or lag. It takes more to be one of the best!

10. The Field of Bone earth elemental. The quasi-rare Burynaibane Spider had, briefly, a very different proc than it was supposed to (summoned a level 50+ mage earth elemental pet under your control). And you thought Paludal Caverns was easy levelling.

9. Involuntary Nexus transportation. For awhile, the Nexus Scions were ready souls, and would eat people even without the transit item. IT was fun to watch players flee from the GFay spires, hoping to avoid being sucked into Luclin unwittingly.

8. Nakes graphics. For their sheer novelty, and the humour left in its wake.

7. Free Coldain Velium Tempers. Probably the best documented of the economy-damaging bugs, this not only shook up bazaar behavior for months afterward, but revealed other exploits, and helped lead to a frenzy of "fake" bug stories as exploiters sought distractions to buy time for their own effects (like the infinite plat banker, and some if not all of the shark exploits).

6. The Lord Inquistor Seru exploit. Preserved forever as the Cuirass of Sad Exploisation. An an asside, I knew about this, and didn't did it - I now wish I had, the CoSE would be such a unique trophy piece!

5. The LoY pre-order level 50 bug. For the sheer amount of panic it instilled in the populace.

4. "Working as Intended Day". As rangers fondly referred to it. The archery machine gun was unprecendented in its uniqeness. And it got a truly humorous emergency patch, at that.

3. Faction wars! Best known for PoTactics fun, but doable in Storms (and Temple of Veeshan!) as well. Disruptive beyond belief, but truly a sight to have seen all the same.

2. Sentinel. The first of the crash-inducing bugs. Everything else is just playing second fiddle. The Paladin Act of Valor AA gets an honorable mention thought, for being such a sucky AA that it took an amazing amount of time before anyone even KNEW that it crashed the zone!

1. Clicky spell refresh. When they finally fixed the system so that clicking an instant-cast spell no longer removed the recycle time between casting spells, people across Everquest where forced to rework casting strategies and timing, a change that I would argue was instrumental in the push to make EQ put focus effects in game (especially spell haste). Few bugs have been so widely used, or taken so much for granted, and fewer still have had such a lasting impact when they went away. Clicky refresh, I salute you. =)
