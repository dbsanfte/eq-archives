Grafsha McDaughts
Necro - hands down.

BIGGEST PERK OF ALL: regen. Shaman and necro are individually self-sufficient on mana regen - the necro passively. A mage WILL have to worry about having KEI or such at higher levels to keep a steady, fast killing pace. With shaman providing necro with regen (health) it is just heaven.

When a necro talks about aggro kiting don't fall for the illusion that a large kite path is needed. As long as there is enough room to keep out of hitting range a necro can aggro kite.

As for who is the main the and who is the "bot," Galelor, chill a bit. I duo a shaman and necro and neither is the main, neither is the bot. There are situational instances where the shaman is primary (most attended to) and the necro is support. The reverse is also true. Neither is "special" as they are working as a team - each contributing what is needed in a particular situation.

Both are capable of saving the "group" in more ways than one. Getting aggro can sometimes be more important than preventing it - especially when duoing. For both shaman and necro getting aggro is just a spell cast away.

DO NOT FORGET necros also get slow - for undead mobs. Charm also. These are very handy spells during mid levels up to 65. Veksar is a nice leveling as well as gear improving zone if you are working camps to gear your toons as they level. Besides slow and charm, the necro undead nukes are extremely mana efficient.

As you can tell, I am necro biased as a shaman duo partner. That shouldn't take anything from mages. In my experience, a mage is a great shaman partner also. I do however think the options and opportunities for a mage/shaman are more limited and much less imaginative.

Shaman and necro are ying and yang.
