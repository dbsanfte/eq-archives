Jaruden
If it's unslowable... it's unslowable. Mobs generally have 4 "stages" of slowable-ness:

1) Unslowable. You get a client message that says "this mob cannot be slowed" or something very similar. Doesn't matter what class or what spell.

2) Very high resistance. Damn near impossible to slow usually, but on rare occasions you'll get a big hit on the RNG and land it. This is kinda split. Some mobs have very high MR and grummus will still land. Other high DR but turgurs will. Some mobs have high resists for both.
A lot of raid mobs are in this category, causing people to think they're unslowable, when they aren't. Kunark dragons back during kunark were like this. MR so high only about a 1 in 10,000 chance one would land, but occasionally someone would stick a slow on them.

3) Partial slowable. You'll land your slow, but it will be mitigated by some amount. I *think* (not 100% sure) that the levels are about 25%, 50% and 75% effectiveness.

4) Fully slowable. Land your spells and get the full effect.


So in conclusion, if you see the "xxx yawns" message, the mob got slowed at least somewhat, and is not "unslowable".
