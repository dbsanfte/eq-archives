Gloriana
A shaman's ability to convert hitpoints to mana is key. Overall manapool size can be considered less important than the speed of regenning mana. Unlike many casters who have no other option to regain mana besides sitting on their butts, shaman actively convert hitpoints to mana.

1) AC - less damage taken per hit = more hitpoints to convert to mana.

2) Hitpoints - more hitpoints = more potential mana

3) Regen - create more hitpoints = more potential mana

Wisdom is not UNimportant for shaman, it's just one of many factors when considering gear. For example, I would consider a pair of velium fire opal rings better shaman gear than +7 wis rings.

Shandy Orcbane
Used to be difficult for shaman below 50 to get good Wis gear. I think you'll find yourself carrying situational gear for downtime and initial buffing -- if you have that +7 Wis ring, put it on when you're done hunting. It's OK if you need the mana for initial buffs, etc. Like Glori said, it's one of many factors to consider. Looking at some of the lovely (and cheap!) LoY, Luclin, and Planar items that are plentiful now, you should be able to do very well no matter what your playstyle is.

What we have is mana regeneration -- we can get it back very fast. Can1 and regen balance out nicely, and we come out right around Clarity-level mana regen if we Canni dance. Worth it to me.

Can2 and Chloroplast (or better, Stoicism!) tip the scales a bit further. Can3 and Regrowth is lovely -- Can4 and Torpor is godlike. Can5 and Torpor is obscene.

Here's the rub, though: We're filling our mana pool back up. You can NOT cast as much as quickly as some of the other casters can, but you can keep doing it forever. We are the masters of sustainability. If we can root it and slow it, we can kill it.

Weigh things carefully:
- You draw a lot of agro when you slow and dot. HP/AC.
- You wincing in the corner trying to pull mana outta your arse for that needed heal cuz the cleric's a nuking fool: Hm, maybe 14 more Wis might have helped a tad, but OH, I need HP or I'll cannibalize to where -- URK!

I strongly suggest a target wisdom around 150-160. Good solid mana pool there, and gives you lots of room for HP and AC.

Higher Wis in a shaman of your level and at least till Can III is for how much mana do I have for this ONE particular fight and how much do I have in a pinch. If a fight's going badly, the last thing you want your shaman to say is "root and back off, so I can get more mana." Higher Wis avoids this by allowing you to use more pure mana before you have to convert HP into mana on any single fight.

I've said it before, concentrations change as your levels and functions change. Earlier on, AC is king, because you're in teh face a lot. Then Wis is good, because HP>mana conversion isn't efficient at the low mid levels. Finally, HP's are key because CAN is your clarity.

Don't try to confuse your gear requirements with the gear requirements of someone 60+, they ain't the same! You won't have a single piece of equipment that's the same once you make 60, that you're wearing now (unless you got twinked with something like a Solstice earing). Gear yourself for the next 15 levels, not the next 30.

gummox
Shandy was the only person to mention canni-dancing, which is very important at lower levels. Chain casting canni 1 really isn't much faster than meditating.

You need to set up a hotkey that looks something like this:

/stand
/cast 1 (or whatever gem your canni spell is in)
/pause 20 (i'm just guessing on the pause time, 20= 2 seconds and should be enough time to finish casting canni)
/sit

To use it properly wait till you see your hp or mana tick then hit the button. When you sit back down, you'll still get the meditation mana regen as if you were sitting the whole time. This takes a little effort, but with canni and meditation being about equal mana regen speed, you're pretty much doubling your mana regen.

Of course you don't need to do this with a horse, since you can chain cast canni and meditate at the same time.

Pre kunark it was wisdom all the way, and you ended up seeing shaman in gatorscale leggings and banana boots (shudder). When Kunark came out and mobs all of a sudden started hitting for a lot more damage, the AC revolution came about, and suddenly you had shamans with 220 wis, have instead 1300 ac and 130 wis. When Luclin and PoP came out and with it Cann V everyone started realizing that they needed loads of hps do deal with the insane dps the mobs were putting out as well as making sure you had enough to cann V.
