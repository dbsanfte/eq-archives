Anyone besides me hate how they just retro-actively added the focus effect to existing items?

It seems so cheesey to just log in and find the widget_01 that has been in your bank vault for the last 10 levels now has casting l33tness III on it.

It certianly makes no sense from a roleplaying aspect (I'm sure not many care about that) Still, I have to wonder where did these changes to my equipment comes from? A gift of the gods or what?

I always liked the idea of upgradable equipment. Back when totemic armor first came out I always felt it was a great quest from that stand point. We were taking that old banded armor of ours and tuning it up a bit at a small cost to us (minus camping parts which mostly sucked) this served 2 purposes - it aleviated the trickle down of banded this revitalizing low-level smithing to a degree, while at the same time sucking plat from the enconomy. Heh, I know I was broke at level 19 after doing 2 totemic bracers.

Along those lines I think a cool idea would have been to implement these as quests. Not typical VI farm fests, but more like the totmeic quest, i.e. go to the a quest npc in some random zone give him (for sake of example) 2 chipped bone bracers, a buyable gem and some random research words and you get back a new "improved" chipped bone bracer with an effect on it. The effect would be pre-determinded the same as it is now.

For Level 3 items its would be a little too costly to implement it in this way, but you could still do it slightly differently by just requiring 1 of the item instead of 2, but raising the plat cost much higher.

At least this way they'd be adding new abilites but not giving them away completely free, and at the same time draining some of the lower end items and a bit of platinum from the economy.
