Aselus
The only problems you run into by doing this are when you sign up for a new expansion. EQ will check the registry to see if it's installed or not and give you an error. I'd also advise against it if you're moving from Windows 98 or ME to XP. If you're going from XP to XP I would suggest installing EQ and all expansions normally to get them embedded in the registry, then overwriting it with the files from your old HD to avoid patching.

I did the download over dial-up (26.4 no less). One overnight session and one day at work took care of almost all of it. At worst it's two days of time not at your computer. It isn't as bad as you think :)
Chazic
What you do is load all the exspansions on your computers new hard drive like your installing it for the first time. Once you have done this all registry entries will be correct. Most of your files will just be out of date in the EQ directory. At this point the easies thing to do is then copy all the files in the EQ directory and sub-directories to the new hard drive into the EQ Directory.

This will make sure 99% of your files are upto date from the last time the old hard drive connect to the EQ servers. After doing this I would also make sure you goto the advanced settings section of the patcher and uncheck any exspansions you dont have (Legends) and select all the ones you do have (LDoN, LoY, Ect....). That should cut several hours off your down load. Also if you do know your going have multi hour down load just before going to bed is nice time to do that or while your off at work depending on if you have people at home while your off at work.

The only registry entry that is made for EQ is in the ADD/REMOVE Programs. You don't even need that entry. You can copy the entire EQ folder to another machine and log in without installing a thing.

Done it many times on WinXP, Win2k, Win9x... As long as you have Direct-X 8 or higher with the corresponding Video Drivers for your Card you will be fine. I run Direct-X 9 on my machine and EQ runs fine. Just need to have the correct video driver.

Your mileage may vary.
