IMHO, after messing with both a bard and a warrior for some time, go fast, faster, fast as you possibly can while keeping a 10 or so damage class.

Chmee
For some general information about how damage works check out this link.

Damage prediction guide (http://pub13.ezboard.com/fpaladinsofnorrathtomesofknowledge.showMessage?topicID=13.topic)

Note that this is from a while back, and some changes have been made to the game since then, so this may not be as accurate as it once was, but it gives a good general feeling for how things work.

Generally speaking, you want a fast weapon in your main hand. This is because the main hand gets a flat bonus no matter what its ratio is (at level 60 it is 11). The person who started the thread Bem linked to is misleading in calling this a strength bonus. From everything I have read strength has nothing to do with the prime hand bonus, it is based purely off your level. You can't completely ignore ratio in your prime hand, but speed does make a big difference. This is why the moss covered twig, with a terrible ratio of 3/10 was at the time the most damaging main hand weapon in the game, because it was so fast. Note that since the bonus is level based it is less important at lower levels (in fact I don't think it comes into effect at all till 20-25 or so).

In your off-hand, you don't get a bonus so you just want the best ratio you can get. A possible exception to this is if you are tanking in which case you may want a faster weapon since fast weapons tend to taunt more.

Usually the most damaging weapon selection at the high end of the game are duel wield combos. There are a few 2 hand weapons (particularly monk weapons) that do pretty well but generally duel wield yields more damage. This is at the high level though when the duel wield skill is pretty high. At lower levels a two hander might not be as bad of a choice if your duel wield skill means you rarely swing with the off hand. Two handers are also useful when you want to stay lower agro.

I have to disagree with Fallanthas about the faster weapon being better because of misses. If I have a weapon that hits 100 times a minute for an average of 1 point with a miss ratio of 40 percent I will do 60 points of damage on average. If I have a weapon with the same damage to delay ratio but swinging slower, say 10 times a minute doing an average of 10 points a hit then with a 40 percent miss ratio it will also do 60 points of damage. The reason you want speed in main hand is just because of the flat bonus added to the damage (or if you are trying to taunt more). Faster weapons will mean you have a smoother curve of damage where the slower ones will be a bit more streaky, but in the end average damage would be the same (without the bonus).
