Grixx
My main is a 65 Ogre SK (elemental planes guilded) and I'd disagree with some of what has been said here. I often pull right along-side the monks on the pull team and I'd be happy to hand you a few pointers.

1. Choose the race that you'd enjoy looking at most for 65 levels + Although some argue that certain races are better suited - in the end, it all equals out really. I personally prefer Ogre because being stunned drives me up the wall. We have 3 other SKs in the guild (all 65) - Troll, Iksar, and DE - similarly equipped, and all of us are roughly equal. The only thing that is going to distinguish you in the end is your skill. Race really won't matter. Choose what you think looks the best.

2. As far as gear is concerned, whatever race you choose, imho, HP is the most important stat you have or will ever have. STR, STA, AGI - all good and worthy stats, but in the end, the factor that will determine whether it's you or a mob standing will be if you outlasted them. SKs are tanks (hybrid tanks admittedly), simply put, and tanks are about HPs. At lvl 65 with over 100 AAs, I have ~8400hps. Going back to the race comment above, the other SKs of differing race in my guild have similar HPs. Most folks at lvl 65 (who are guilded, etc) are going to have max STR, STA anyhow - obviously it will vary a bit depending on your gear and Planar Power AAs. However, when grouped, chances are that you'll be tanking (I find that I'm the tank 90% of the time in exp groups) and in raids, you may find yourself split-tanking adds from time to time when warriors and paladins aren't available/are tied up with other mobs. In a raid situation, you may even find yourself pulling once in a while - depending on the guild you're in and the mob. Bottom line, HPs are king. Get your STA as high as possible during character creation, and then get you some Cultural armor as you near level 45 (that's the recommended level). One caveat: If you choose DE, I'd probably put the points in STA; If you choose Ogre, you might consider putting some points in INT or AGI (just to get it above 75). Again, however, everyone is going to have differing opinions. Cultural armor (the Ogre version anyway) can last you til Ornate if necessary - as the AC/HPs on it are quite nice. Hell, I still wear my cultural greaves and will until I can get enough AP's for the Takish greaves (I've spent most of my guild points on non-visible slot upgrades and weapons).

3. Suddail had a good idea when it comes to 2-boxing. I recently restarted on Firiona Vie and have a 49 SK and 59 Shaman on separate accounts. It's a fantastic duo. I recommend it if you'd like a change and the ability to take on some mobs that perhaps you weren't able to in the past.

Again, choose a race that best suits your idea of your character and stick with it. Whatever you choose, have fun with it. I am continually amazed at what SKs and Shamans can do at high levels. Teamed together, they are an amazing combo. Whatever you do, I hope you enjoy it.

Shoot me a tell ingame sometime if you'd like to chat about it.

I'd agree with much of what Suddail said, but because of my playstyle, I'd suggest Troll over Ogre. I tend to solo a lot, due in part to not being a very social person and in part to liking and often being in ghost-town old world zones where I couldn't find a group/partner if I wanted one. A Troll's regen is huge in this situation, especially at 51+ when it goes up by quite a bit. Iksar have innate regen as well, but you don't have the easy cultural armor option, afaik. Not only do trolls have relatively inexpensive cultural armor available, but their cultural has a 3:1 hp:mana ratio on most pieces, unlike the 2:2 on most hybrid-useable cultural. Mana's great and all, but imo not at the expense of hp, especially for a tank. Other races, especially small/medium races, are viable.. but without a decent-sized budget they'll suffer from lower stamina (less hp) and other less important, but still worthwhile stats. Additionally, small/medium races can't bash while wielding a 2handed weapon without spending a few AA points.

Coccyx Oracle of Thule
HP > all for tanks. (although since this is a twink we are talking about, regen is actually more important, but that is the only exception to the rule) After that, you have to generate aggro, so a good weapon, good haste and some way to prevent yourself from going OOM.

Everything else is unimportant till you start raiding.

And don't let anyone tell you what race to play. Grixx is 100% right on that point. Yeah, ogre's no stun from the front is nice, or the troll's extra regen, or the iksar's AC bonus, but none of them are big enough to justify you choosing that race because of them. (Although the Iksar's inability to wear plate armor IS a valid consideration, but there's a fairly large pool of all/all twink gear that is perfectly suitable instead.)

I'd reccomend (depending on your cash reserves) picking up the best haste item you can afford (They have high versitility, so they are a good buy even if you don't end up keeping the SK), a decent 2-hander (like blackened acrylia halbred or something similar) and a set of cheap armor (Sentient comes to mind, or maybe Crustacian).

This is nowhere near what would be considered a "good" twink package, but it's enough to get you through your first 35 levels or so fairly quickly, and give you a taste of the class without breaking the bank.

If you end up liking the class, or if you don't feel like taking the reccomendation above, go for broke. Get a Cowl of Mortality, a Windblade, a good 1 hander / shield combo (I don't know any off-hand), a full set of Dark Prince armor and whatever uber non-visibles you can get your hands on.
