As much as I like to complain about class balance I do think that class balance is much improved compared to Velious (or heavens forbid, original EQ), both in overall class desirability and power and in regards to content balance.

What changed though is the leveling curve. People spend a much lesser amount of their time leveling then back in Velious and a much greater amount of time grouping and raiding with people of their exact same level, number of AAs and gear.

Class imbalance is much much noticeable in such a setting then it is when you routinely group with people as much as 5 levels higher or lower then you and with staggering gear differences. That is not even going into how general game knowledge (don't confuse this with playing skill) is much more widespread today then it used to be back then.

I remember warriors giving up half of their AC for 30 more dex. I remember routinely having more HPs and AC then the tanks I grouped with even if they went for all AC and HPs simply because I used to spend a decent amount of time looking for gear upgrades. I remember grouping with sub-50 people while I was nearing 60.

Today I group with lvl 65 people, mainly from my own guild, with a similar number of AAs and similar gear. Since we are a raiding guild the mindset and playing skills/knowledge also tend to be similar.

Thus any imbalances are much much more visible today then they were back then. In Velious I would not have noticed that I tank worse then warriors just by playing the game. In Velious I would not have noticed that clerics had mana regeneration issues just by playing the game. The gap in gear, levels, knowledge and skill within the group of people I grouped with was simply too large to notice anything like that.

That does not mean class balance was better back then.

assuming you're not some moron-come-lately for whom MMPOG have ALWAYS existed...like the cellphone and processed cheese... the promise of "live team support" for new content has always been a red herring: primarily a marketing promise to make a monthly subscription palatable. there's no question about that. after all, it was just as likely as not that NO EXPANSION would EVER be released for EQ (I'm sure very few of you know that all the designs for the continent of Kunark were done PRIOR to EQ's original launch... but were shelved indefinitely as too ambitious when antonica, odus and fayder were polished and released. originally smed really didn't expect eq to go more than a year or two MAX and gradually promising to release the new zones already envisioned seemed a safer bet than planning on an expansion for a game that no one expected to even outgrow the level 50 cap

now, 5 years down the pike it's become clear that "live support" for additional content is a nonstarter... not only for EQ but for ANY MMPOG. a new zone every year or every year and a half doesn't consititute policy but a treat. just as seeing a GM on Halloween and Christmas doesn't constitute customer support. zones released are like the Fabled spawns or 5th year anniversary events... someone's pet project or released because other mmpog are going live...

this isn't the case only for EQ. NO MMPOG supports regular publishing of content by a live team. just like it was at one time the thing to PROMISE support, it's now become accepted by convention, that just like EQ, game developers just dont do this. in fact a game like guildwars.com is basing its entire revenue model on the idea that MMPOG consumers BUY EXPANSIONS (and unlike other devs who screw people coming AND going with subscriptions AND expansions, arenanet's only gonna get.. less desperate... people once)

why is this? how can an entire GENRE, not to mention the largest mmpog studio in north america get away with wholesale deceit of its consumers on the main selling point for its enormous revenue model. has the system broken down? where is my congressman???
