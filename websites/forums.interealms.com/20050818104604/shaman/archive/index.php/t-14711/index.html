deria
In pickup groups the expectation is generally that everyone will roll on everything, unless they have already won something.

If you do not like that system (and many do not) then you can usually specify at the beginning of the group (ie: before any loot drops) that you are looking for an NBG group (need before greed). If the rest of the group is fine with that then you are set; if not then you haven't wasted any time grouping with them.

I've always found the "it's a new day" argument to be kind of annoying, especially when people use it simply because one person left the group and was replaced. If I were in a group like the one you describe, I would tell them to find a new shaman. Actually, come to think of it, I would more specifically tell them to find a new camp since I was going to solo this one and they were dead weight anyway... :)

If it is a constant group (you mentioned you had spent two weeks with them) then why should you receive nothing for your effort? Are you not contributing equally? If it was a different group makeup every day, then you'd probably have to live with the roll of the dice.

Loot is always a sticky issue, but basically I would suggest that since the item you're looking for is a fairly common drop you should just say "since this is a regular group and this item drops frequently, lets take turns on the belts".

I almost always master loot for groups that I'm in. EVERYTHING is NBG first, nodrops /randomed immediately, everything else /randomed at end of night. There is no such things as "for alts". NBG means you will equip it to replace something you have and use it > 50% of the time you adventure. Rarely have had to worry about "resist gear" type rolls so far, but when somethign like that has dropped it would go to a >50% use person before it would fall to a "resist gear" person. Once you get a NBG item, you get no more NBG items unless you are the only NBG person on that item. Once you get an NBG item, you can't have a "gravy" item until everyone has gotten one item (nbg or gravy). If you got 2 NBG items, you can't get gravy until everyone else has a combination of 2 items of nbg and gravy. I sell all vendor loot at end of the night and then /split the proceeds. I'm always encumbered not long into the night ;-)

In my RL Friends group, when the second string started getting higher levels and could replace the primary in the group, we started letting a person decide which character they were bringing based on where we were going as long as "base group needs" were met.

Exception on rolling for alts (rl friends group only) - If no one needs a SPELL in group but there is an alt within 10 levels that could use, then alt gets the spell. Trying to avoid having anyone in RL friends group pay for spells.
