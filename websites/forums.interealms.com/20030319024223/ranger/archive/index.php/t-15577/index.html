Aethien
A "more interesting" server, eh? Yes, it certainly is that... I would argue that random PKing (AKA "ganking") is the rule, more than the fun kind of fights against even /cons that you'd expect.

Let's see what we can do to make you a bit more survivable until you get EQ and AM3. (Hah, like I'll ever make it that far...)

1) Know your guilds and the most frequent gankers. This comes with time, of course, but you can get a head start by looking at the Sullon Zek Scoreboard. (http://everquest.station.sony.com/stats/sz_summary.jsp) It has the daily points leaders, and guilds - and their alignments. Know your friends, too - now that you're hitting your 20s, you should think about getting in a good Good guild. I'd recommend mine (Wolves of Retribution) but we're still kind of small, sort of starting over after some folks decided to stay behind while the rest of the guild merged with another. Black Crown and Black Prophecy are two of the larger, raiding guilds; Sanctus Lumen accepts good-quality lower-level folk, I hear. Flowers of Happiness is just a strange outfit. Don't know much about Fatalus Invictus, but see a lot of them. Oh, yeah, and Legendary Warlords is another Good guild that seems to have attracted a lot of folks lately.

2) Know when those folk are in the zone. Do a /who every couple of minutes, if not more. I just do a '/' without even thinking about it. Remember the 'hiccup' - that weird little hitch when you're running or fighting, that means that someone just entered the zone. Newest folk will be at the top of the list.

Remember that there's a bug that will let you know someone's guildstatus, even if they're anon and too far away to target. Just do /guildst Aethien, and it will tell you if they're in a guild, etc.

3) Know which zones tend not to be frequented by PKers. This may have changed with PoP - for example, I've been in Everfrost since just after it was released. It used to be deserted, with only rare PKers. With time, as more and more people get PoP, more and more PKers have started coming to the zone. Steamfont is nice and empty, a lot of times, despite the Druid ring there; Lesser Fay, too. I'll let you know about Stonebrunt in a couple of days... Playing at odd times helps, too. Parts of Luclin are still probably pretty abandoned.

4) Try to play as much as possible in 3rd person view. I rarely move too far through a zone in 1st person, except for city zones. I go to one of the views where you can pan around - hit autorun through the Karanas, for example, go to 3rd person view, elevate the camera, and pan around with ALT + cursor arrows. (Or is is CTRL? Dang, I can never remember; my fingers just go to those keys automatically.) This will at least give you warning, along with Track, of PKers around you.

Use that view while fighting, too. Lots of folks like to sneak up on you while you're occupied and down on health.

5) Depending on how much time you think you'll be spending before hitting 39, get a See Invis item. Of course, depends how much plat you have, too; cheapest one I've seen in the Bazaar is Majdd's Choker for something like 1500, I think. Or, just beg it off of passing casters, I suppose (along with SoW, of course).

6) Keep checking /who.

7) Don't turn off /shout, /ooc, etc. - most warnings of PKers will be in OOC, I have found. The annoying chatter in GFay makes me want to pull my ears off, but it's a tradeoff for learning when and where the PKers are hanging out.

8) Let's see... Remember to Hide often and Camo yourself. Most folks will have a See Invis item or spell, but not all do, and it can save you every once in a while. I camo'd myself to run through Freeport while doing the Trueshot quest - I zoned into NFP, and there's a Troll sitting there... who evidently didn't have See Invis, as I ran right by her. Remember the /target <self> trick to see if Hide was successful.

9) Check /who again.

10) Hate to say it, but don't be shy about starting an alt if you find that you hunt at popular spots in popular times for PKers. Of course, this will slow you down in your quest to get EQ and AM3...

Don't know if this is what you were looking for, but there ya go. Have to run. Drop me a tell if you see me online.

Edit: Just thought of another one; it's probably obvious, but, hey. If you're soloing, med and heal up after fighting in out of the way places. In Steamfont, I would always go up this one hill and hide behind a tree. Keep in mind that on the Evil team, only Bards will have track - and that only after 30th level, right? Neut Druids and Rangers will, of course, but at least you can minimize the number of people who can find you.

I wanted to clarify a few terms:

gank ... To suddenly attack an opponent in a decisive manner, where the opponent is ALREADY engaged and weakened by another mob or player.

cc ... Corpse Camped ... Your corpse is being monitored by one or more opponents who refuse to offer LnS.

LnS ... Loot and Scoot ... The act of recovering your corpse and leaving the contested zone.

griefing ... The act of continuously pk'n an opponent until they log or leave the contested zone.

And I have three pieces of advice for you:

Play two EQ characters as described in the above post. SZ can be extremely frustrating at times.

Don't ignore the skill aspect of pk'n, if you want to be a better pk'r then practice pk'n.
