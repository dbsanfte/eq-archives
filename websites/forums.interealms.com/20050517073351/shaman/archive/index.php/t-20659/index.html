Jinheim
Unforunately, Gorgos is pretty correct.

You are going to want a full raid of 36 people, all of them level 65. In order to participate, each of these 36 people will need two wins in the theme you want to do.

No loot drops until the very end, and you get 6 loots (might vary from theme to theme but 6 is average) - a mixture of real loots and augs. Augs are stuff like +25ac, +speed of mithaniel, +50hp, etc... Click my magelo and look at my earring (not grimel) for an example of normal level loot. Hard level loot is much better, but honestly you aren't going to be beating one of those any time soon.

When you win you get the loot (it is in chests) and 30 adventure points (YAY). Raids vary in length. Ruj hard raid has a 4 hour timer, and you need all 4 hours. Mistemoore normal is I think 3 hours? They vary from 2-4 hours, mainly. I think one of them might have been 90 minutes. Oftentimes, you'll have a list of subgoals to accomplish in the raid, and you will only have a set amount of time to do it. For example, after you kill miniboss1, you might only have 30 minutes to kill miniboss2 or else you fail, even though the timer says 155 minutes left. The only way to figure out these timers is guess and check (oh by the way you can only do each theme once per week).

Also, some themes (mistemoore hard for certain and I think mistemoore normal - other themes as well but not sure which) have a death limit. I don't know what the limit is but basically, if your raid wipes twice, you lose. This can be helpful because quite often it is impossible to CR and without hitting the death limit you'd have to wait 3 hours for your corpses to pop (mistemoore hard is a good example of this - good luck getting out of the entrance room).

As for strategy:

What you can expect: Failure, Frustration, Anger, and raw loathing Hatred - in more or less that order. Mobs hit extremely hard, although they usually don't have that many hitpoints. You will have only vague clues (or sometimes none at all) as to what to do and you will have to try repeatedly to figure out what you are supposed to do, and then you will have to try several more times to successfully execute it. Get used to seeing "YOU HAVE FAILED" pop up on your screen for no appearant reason. Also expect all kinds of cool gimmicks: mobs that spawn on top of your raid if you aren't standing in a hallway, a gauntlet filled with 99 mobs that hit for 5k each and you have to figure out the trick to despawn them, mobs that ignore you when you attack them and instead run away like feared, coming back with a dozen friends. And of course, prepare to experience the nostalgia of AE FEAR. It's just as fun as you remember.

The easiest dungeon imho is mistmoore normal. It isn't too bad. Basically you just clear through a dungeon, and then clear three wings at the end. Then, three bosses spawn simultaneous, one in each wing. Go in and engage all three bosses at the same time. They're easy. One flurries, one ramps, and one spawns adds. I can't remember which is which.

That's all the info I can remember about LDoN. I tried to block most of it from my memory. My closing advice is this: skip ldon raids.

Ramhorn
You are going to want a full raid of 36 people, all of them level 65. In order to participate, each of these 36 people will need two wins in the theme you want to do.

This is specially important, because there's no difference in difficulty if you have 18 people or 36 people.

Also, there are death limits, and once it's reached, you get the failure message (you also get ported out). It appears to be a huge limit for normal settings, and happened to us once, but seemed it was buggy. In hard, a wipe-and-a-half, and we failed.

Now, my guild has tried each theme, and we never had mobs greet us at the zone in. Mobs can/are close though, so becareful wandering around. On our very first LDON raid, however, our raid leader accidently chose Hard setting (MM theme) and nobody thought anything about it. Mobs did greet us in the zone in with 1k+ quads. That wasn't fun.

For normal setting raids:

MM- We have this down pat. The rewards, in my opinion are the better well-rounded ones compared to the others. The mummies are immune to slow, with exception to the named mummy final boss. Most of the other mobs don't mitigate slow, but nearly needs the "works" to land a slow.

Guk- Only tried once. A gnome greeted us and judging by his words and setup, we had to say some phrases to get stuff going. Maybe we were dumb, but we just couldn't figure it out, so we left ><.

Mirigul's- Only tried once. Kind of an interesting setup. A boss-type mob sends his minions to attack you in waves. We didn't get too far though. This is where we got a failure message due to deaths, but seemed pretty buggy. We had some people unable to zone back in, despite us still inside. Eventually all of us did after some more deaths.

Ruj- Did it only once. We had issues with mobs appearing and running off. Some mobs you couldn't do anything to. I'm sure it's scripted to be so, but we gave up, not to mention it was only 3 groups of us messing around for fun.

North Ro- We "won" twice. It's real buggy. You pretty much kill everthing, including special traps that spawn loads of critters. Reason for the quotes is, near the end, the last few mobs were under the world. GM's wouldn't/couldn't (mostly because no real GM's around) do anything and only told us to bug report it. Both times we had about over an hour to spare.

Personally I like LDON raids. It's something for us to do once in a while to change the scenery. But most don't seem to work for us, and only create headaches. There are some really nice rewards.
