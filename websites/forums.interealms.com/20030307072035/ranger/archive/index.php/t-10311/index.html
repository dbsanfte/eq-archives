Taewedar
I led our guilds first Inny killing back when I was dragging them up to hate every few days in search of my emerald so...I suppose I qualify :)

First there are two keys to killing Inny. One Slow him and keep him that way. Debuff the crap out of him and then get him slowed. 2nd keep him positioned away from the walls and towards the side of his room where his room is. Otherwise things heal through wall and he just won't die.

He doesn't DT. he has a Gflux aoe that strips buffs. It's annoying as heck. But you get used to it. He has I'm guessing 150kish of hitpoints. It sometimes seems like more however because he'll get a random heal or the gfluxes really reduces melee damage.

I used to camp a cleric out when I first tried him. Respawns take awhile on the 2nd floor so it's pretty safe. Although Inny takes forever to reset if you fail. [ok it seems like forever]

I've seen him done with 18 folks. We typically have 30-40. Everyone likes to kill him :)

As far as getting to him goes. Get to the 2nd floor and clear out as you move along west to the wall, then follow the wall up and around as you clear stuff until you reach his "house" then clear up the ramp and setup in the hallway outside his room/hall area. That is probably the safest bet. It typically takes a 4-6 hours to clear to him unless you have done it alot.

Hope this helps. Most of his loot isn't that great these days, but it's still killing a god so i always enjoy it :)

Bring a 60 shaman, preferably a 57+ enc, and ideally a 55+ bard. All together, these three can get inny's mr to drop through the floor and make him very easy to slow, cripple, etc

From there, yeah, just work out positioning to compensate for the grav flux and stuff. He takes awhile to die, but once debuffed a ton he has very little damage output.

note, a lot of heals that you might see inny getting are from necros or SKs.. these are pretty meaningless. They do add up over time, but it's nothing to fret about. If you see him being chealed though, time to move him lots ;)

Aim for stability. Once you get that down, a debuffed Inny should fall without much effort.

Been a while since I've done an Inny fight so I don't recall the fight too clearly, but I did find an old e-mail I sent someone in another guild explaining what we did to kill him. Maybe this will give you some ideas. Innoruuk is a lot easier than you'd think he'd be, if you got a sound strategy.

Hello there! Gonna outline a brief plan for how we figured out how to fight Inny. We did this with 29 people, but probably would have gotten
away with 24 since we've been raiding together as Unleashed since February now, so we've had some time to get used to everyone else's quirks.

Okay, first of all, Inny is a wizzy. He'll g-flux the raid like mad. That's part of what makes this fight so much fun! Second is that he also debuffs like mad. EVERYONE-- and I mean EVERYONE-- will be stripped by the time he's dead. If you want to be buffed for as long as possible, hit point-wise, bury Aego in slot number 15. Not that this will do you much good after the first minute or two of the fight, especially if you're the main tank. Inny also has a one-target knockback spell that I believe also debuffs. So not only can you be knocked up and down, you can also be knocked back and forth and side to side. Bring the motion
sickness pills if you're susceptible...

Second of all, be prepared for a long fight. It took us 11 minutes to kill him. One of the chanters and shamans' main tasks was to respeed
the melees periodically to keep up the damage production. We also had a magician on hand that we kept busy non-stop summoning mod rods [Note: As of Wednesday this part of the tactic no longer works]; he only paid attention to the fight long enough to sic his pet on Inny. I'm told FU uses a CH rotation on Inny, but we got away without one. You can also perform battle rezzes during the fight quite easily because of this-- we did this for the lead-chanter so she could re-debuff Inny
again before taking another dirt nap. Inny hits for a maximum of 400 or so on a level 60 warrior, and I'm not sure if he quads or not. I don't
think so, because our main tank was mocking Inny for being a wuss in shouts, so it was obvious he wasn't really worried after we got him
slowed. But don't quote me on that. [Additional note: a decently-equipped level 60 SK works great for a tank, too!]

Third: Yep, Inny can be slowed. I'm not exactly sure what debuffs were needed to make those slows stick (if any at all), because we played it better safe than sorry and had a level 60 shaman and level 60 enchanter do their worst, but slows stick to him like newbie beggars at the bank. Heck, even my Earthcall proc stuck to him for a while before it got overwritten! Also, guild leader was periodically reminding shamans, druids, and necromancers to re-dot him, so I assume those spells stuck on him. Sorry, I don't know what resists he's weak on, as I was too busy paying attention to Inny to notice what the wizards were casting.

Fourth: Setting up the fight. Once you're on the second floor, you can pretty much invis up and sneak past a lot of the mobs on that floor until you're on the zone wall closest to the entrance of Inny's house. Inny's on the second floor of his house, and because of g-flux and that knockback spell, it's best to fight him in an enclosed space. Park the raid party on that zone wall and send in a FD puller to clear the first and second floor mobs in Inny's house, leaving ONLY Innoruuk alive in the house. Once everything else is dead (and if Inny is still set in his spawn point-- if not, give him some time to reset), move the entire
raid party into the house and up the stairs to the second floor. We parked the raid in the corner room before the hall that leads to Inny's room.
