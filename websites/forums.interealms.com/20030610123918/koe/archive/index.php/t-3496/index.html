In case you don't frequent the main Everquest.com message boards, Verant/Sony has asked for all of our input on their customer service policies as they are revamping them and want our opinions on what we would like changed or what we think is good now. I've posted their message below so you can have the email address to send your concerns to. I've sent off my 2 copper worth of ideas and possible problems I see already.

----------------------

Hello All,
In the past few weeks we have been doing a full review of our Customer Service policies and procedures from top to bottom. On Wednesday October 24th, we will be announcing these changes on EverQuest.com. Windigo
Here's what I sent in case anyone is looking for ideas. I know some may not agree with my thoughts or I might be off a bit on some of my perceptions, but it's rare that they ask for our input and say they are truly going to listen to us, so I wanted to respond to them:

-------------------
It was recently posted on the EQ main site that you wanted customer service
opinions and possible changes from your player base. For the most part
customer service has been excellent for me. Policies have changed a lot in
the over two years I've played, so I'm not 100% sure where they stand now,
but I would like to voice my opinion on some of the main issues that have
been in affect lately.

At one time there was a fair policy in place where if something ended up
missing on a character due to a bug that the petitioner would be reimbursed
whatever item(s) was missing then a review would be done to see if in fact
the problem was true. If it wasn't the customer's account would be
suspended or banned and the reimbursed item removed from the game. This was
changed later to where no one would be reimbursed for lost items. I had
since been with guild members who I had helped get certain quest items and
seen them use them, only to have them log in the next day and the item was
gone. All requests to have the characters reset to the day they had the
item to prove that it in fact existed were denied. I think it was a fair
policy to protect people who honestly had problems and punish people who
wanted to abuse the system and I would like to see it reinstated so that
people who spend time getting items actually can have faith in knowing that
they can continue to use the items. No computer programming is perfect and
there are bound to be glitches here and there that might leave people
without things they've accumulated over time.

I feel that sharing accounts is bad, as it should be due to it being stated
as wrong in the license agreement. However the policy of banning the
account of someone who finds that their character account has been hacked
and banning the person who hacked it is wrong. There are malicious people
in the world who will always try to break into someone's computer and steal
their passwords and they in fact should be punished. Those who are
innocently affected by the many viruses and trojans however should not be
punished. Many of today's anti-virus programs don't find EQ-specific virus
infections so someone may have their account infected without knowing it. I
think at most someone who has their account affected should have it disabled
for a week while it is investigated and fix options are given to that person
so they could make sure that they are no longer affected. Someone who
spends weeks, months or years building a character and friendships should
not be punished because someone steals their information without them
knowing. If someone shares an account and that information gets passed
around and something bad happens they deserve it, otherwise it's like taking
someone's house away from them because it was broken into.

I would also like to see the player counts back on the servers. Often when
there is a linkdeath problem or other server problem a player can see that
there are hundreds less people logged on than there were minutes before so
they know that the problem is widespread and not with their computer or ISP.
This is extremely helpful in diagnosing what a problem is when often guides
in the chat rooms don't readily know about issues. It is also very helpful
when planning raids and camps to see server population fluctuations to know
if there is a problem and to possible reschedule a gathering due to
technical difficulty, be it with the routers, servers, or connection between
Verant and the general population.

It would be nice to see more GM events in the game. I feel since GMs and
guides are part of the customer service program that this is a customer
service issue. The storyline in the game is amazing and quite detailed if
you choose to participate in it. However if one has played for a long
period of time killing orcs or doing small quests eventually gets stale. I
would like to see weekly or semi-weekly events on all servers to allow the
stories to grow and to get more people involved in the history of the game.
It would not be hard to do as there are many creative fantasy fans involved
in the guide program or who could be included in the program to develop in
game stories and quests for items, faction, and fun. Pre-programmed
interactions can do only so much.

Finally, I would like to see more rezzes for LDs caused by connection
problems between the general public and Verant. True there are localized
problems often between certain ISPs and Verant, and those should not be
assisted because playing an online game involves link death as one of the
possibilities. However when people in Kansas, California, England, and
Florida are being knocked off at the same exact time it is often a problem
with the ISP that Verant has subscribed to to provide bandwidth and while
not Verant's immediate problem, it is a problem with the service Verant
purchased to allow customers to connect to their servers, and therefore not
a localized problem. So I feel when a certain number of people are being
kicked from servers that rezzes should be given if found to be a problem
with a Verant-subscribed ISP.

That's all for now. Thank you for your request for these opinions and the
time taken in reading and responding to them. If I think of others I will
be happy to forward them on to you.
