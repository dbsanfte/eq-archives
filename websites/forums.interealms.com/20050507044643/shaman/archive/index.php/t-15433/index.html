It's nice to see them actually giving DoT casters some love when it comes to focus items. Now there's one pretty big flaw that needs to be looked at.

Imagine being a rogue happily meleeing away with your dart of Immobility/Double Edged Stormblade combo when all of a sudden you begin to see:

You pierce Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 55 points of damage.
You pierce Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 63 points of damage.
You backstab Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 130 points of damage
You backstab Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 475 points of damage
You slash Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 34 points of damage.
You slash Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 62 points of damage
Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra was hit by non-melee for 150 points of damage.
Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra is slashed by flying debris
A Massive force knocks you into the air. You have taken 1 point of damage.
You were hit by non-melee for 22 points of damage.
You were injured by falling
Your target is too far away, get closer!
You pierce Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 35 points of damage.
You pierce Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 42 points of damage.
Your backstab did not take hold
Your backstab did not take hold
You slash Kaas Thox Xi Aten Ha Ra for 58 points of damage.

Well of course that wouldn't happen, because all melee stacks. The only limit on the number of melees attacking a mob is technical space around it to stick people. So what is keeping DoTs from working in a similar way? Well, it's because mobs are limited to 30 debuffs.

I'm sure any of you who've raided along with a couple other shamans/druids/necros can feel my pain, and it's only aggravated by adding weapons like VZ's scimitar (Obsidian Shatter), that fist off Bertoxx (Scourge), or the wicked shank (Heat Blood) Which essentially have cute but otherwise worthless effects that eat up precious debuff slots. Melees can't seem to understand that for every time they proc some useless effect on a mob, they're taking DPS away from the raid, and it's one less Blood of Thule, Funeral Pyre, or Blood of Saryrn that's carving that 950khp monster up nicely.

After typical Debuffing, a mob will look like this:

1) Malos -> Malosinia
2) Howl of Tashan
3) Harmony of Sound
4) Aura of Darkness (That SK resists tap)
5) Turgur's Insects (or Grummus)
6) Insidious Malady
7) Cripple
8) Hand of Ro (some dr00d ATk/FR debuff)
9) Ro's Illumination (Another funky dr00d debuff that takes off Atk and FR or something)
10) Aura of Hate
11) E'ci's frosty Breath (AC/Cold Resist Debuff)
12) Mark of Kings
13) Mark of Retribution (whatever the upgrade is called)

So that's almost half the slots used up without even getting into DoTs and procced Effects yet.

Assuming you have 2 necros, 2 shamans, and 2 druids on your raid:

Shaman:
Breath of Ultor is like 21ish DPS
Blood of Saryrn is 53
Pox is 18
+ Epic (sometimes) or nukes. Let's call it 3 DoTs per shaman

Necro: Blood of Thule is 55dps
Funeral Pyre is 43
Saryrn's Kiss is 33
Splurt averages to 19dps
Dark Plague is 21.5

Let's say each necro uses 5 DoTs (though I know some who do more and throw in pyro too, or hell, an epic click)

Then you have druids, who don't really have very good DoTs...
Swarming Death is 40dps
then there's some fire thing which I really can't see them using

I'm just gonna say druids use one DoT...

So right there is around 16-18 slots in damage, which already pushes you at the cap on slots, only including a meager 2 of each class that used damage over time spells...

I know shadowknights get a couple (bond of Death, Blood of Hate) and bards who aren't doing overhaste or AE resists/Mana are gonna be twisting some chants...

Essentially, if a spell is not going to take hold because there aren't enough slots to hold it, it should not expend mana, so we can instead spend it on some pathetic nuke and not be completely out of the mana... Or you can just increase the number of slots to something reasonable ceiling that all but the most zergtastic 200 man raids are going to bump thier heads against. 75 would be more than sufficient.

Again, I'd like to say thank you for at least giving damage over time spells a bit of a nod with the addition of Focii, but don't fumble the ball at the 5 yard line.... go the full mile and actually make these spells a decent DPS factor in raids, rather than a liability
