Uhmn, well....from time to time, some bewildered soul comes up with an idea he believes to be earthshattering......until he gets some feedback from the more down to earth people, letting him know just how far out he's been wandering.

“Alright, Enormious. Thinking outside of the box is a good thing. But not that far. Now go straight to your room and don't come out until you've learned how to be a good boy!”

So, in the good old fashioned “What if...” tradition, here's a concept I thought would be great for a game like EverQuest. Read it if you want, and feel free to print it out and use it for toilet paper afterwards :)

Craft Item
An ability any character gets at lvl 20. This ability allows you to craft an item which fits into the slot of your liking. You also get to name the item yourself. The name starts with your character's name, and then you could choose from several options, customizing it into a name you'd love to wear, ie. Enormious' Tiny Ring of Grand Wisdom.

Now, for sanity's sake, I will be using this ring as an example of how I envision that this ability would work. At lvl 20, I go to my GM and activate the ability. This allows me to to spend some of my experience collecting Craft Points (much like how the AAs work). Now, at lvl 20, I would only be able to craft an item which would give me a maximum boost of +1 to all stats and saves, +5 hp, mana and endurance, +1 attack bonus and 1 augmentation slot. Such an item would cost me a total of 27 points, which would also be the maximum number of points I would be able to accumulate at lvl 20. (stats costs etc. will be further explained later). Also, the rate of which I would accumulate these points would be equal to my level. If 100% xp went to Craft Points, by the time I should have reached lvl 21, I would have accumulated 20 Craft Points insted. With me so far?

Now, at every EVEN numbered level, the stats on the crafted items would raise. This does not mean that an item crafted at level 20, would increase in power as I level, but that I would be able to accumulate more points as I level, in order to craft more powerful items later on. But the more powerful the item, the more points I would have to spend. Here's the layout:

Crating an item
+1 WIS = 1 point
+2 WIS = 3 point
+3 WIS = 6 points
etc....this goes for all stats, saves and ATK

+5HP = 1 point
+10HP = 3 points
+15HP = 6 points
etc..this goes for mana , AC and endurance as well

1 Augmentation slot = 10 points
2 Augmentation slots = 30 points
3 Augmentation slots = 60 points


Max points accumulated pr. lvl & Rate of points accumulated pr lvl:
(the numbers in brackets show how many point you'd need to accumulate at current level if you had already saved up your max. number of points)

[LVL] [MAX] [SPEED] [stats/etc] [Mana/etc] [Aug]

20 | max: 27 | rate: 20 | +1 | +5 | 1
21 | max: 27 | rate: 21 | +1 | +5 | 1
22 | max: 61[34] | rate: 22 | +2 | +10 | 1
23 | max: 61 | rate: 23 | +2 | +10 | 1
24 | max: 112[51] | rate: 24 | +3 | +15 | 1
26 | max: 180[68] | rate: 26 | +4 | +20 | 1
28 | max: 265[85] | rate: 28 | +5 | +25 | 1

And the list goes on and on.....
Now, what we have here is a major time sink, but one which I believe is balanced and would enhance gameplay. Low level characters would be able to craft low stat items in little time (as leveling is faster in low levels), and high level characters would be able to craft powerful items if they put in a LOT of time (as levelling is slower in high levels). And if one were to invest all the points accumulated from every level, a lvl 60 character would be able to create a tremedously powerful item at the cost of 3987 points and month and months of play (even years):

Enormious' Gigantic Helmet of Potent Health:

+21 STR | +21 Save vs Poison | 105 AC
+21 STA | +21 Save vs Magic | +21 ATK
+21 AGI | +21 Save vs Disease | +105 HP
+21 DEX | +21 Save vs Fire | +105 ENDR
+21 WIS | +21 Save vs Cold | +105 Mana
+21 INT | 3 Augmentation slots
+21 CHA

This might look like a gamebreaking item, but a casual player would need years of gameplay to accumulate points for this item, and even someone being PLd would need several months. And once this item has been created, a lvl 60 character is stuck at gaining points at the speed of 60 points pr. level if 100% of his xp went into Item Crafting. That means he'd have to go through lvl 60 almost 67 times in order to craft a second item just as powerful. Add AAs to that timesink, and I believe you have something I for one would love to see in a game like EverQuest.

As a finishing note, I'd like to add that these items would have to be No Drop, and that it is of course fully possible to create as weak items as one wants, or just concentrate on items boosting one or two categories, like crafting a ring which only gives saves or some boots with just Stamina and HP.

Yup, that's all, I think. If you hate the idea and think I need to be committed into a mental institution, that's ok. And if you like it, that's ok, too. It's just an idea, and I needed to get it down on paper since it kept me from getting any sleep when I tried to go to bed yesterday :o)
