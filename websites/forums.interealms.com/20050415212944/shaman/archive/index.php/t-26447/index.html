Gotta agree with Sam on this - I bought WoW today (haven't played yet) and couldn't help but feel that it'll never replace EQ simply because EQ was the first of its kind. It was a completely new concept for most of its players and will always be the staple to which newer MMOs are compared. Many would agree that today's EQ is twisted and quite different (in a negative sense) than it was years ago, but make no mistake, those same people have stories and stories of the good ol' days.

Personally, I feel Velious > anything about any other game, ever. FF7 was awesome, MGS2 was awesome, participating in a 10th Ring War with 80 other people back when it was challenging, making new strategies on the fly when something went wrong, recouping in between pops, watching the event develop, seeing Narandi finally fall - **** like that is irreplacable simply because you can't boot up your PS2 or Xbox whenever you want and do it all over again. The people you fought with and the fight itself were always unique, and finally watching the hours of work pay off and celebrating with 80-odd other people was the real deal. This doesn't apply just to the 10th War though, you'll always remember other events like spending months keying up for ST/VT and finally zoning in for the first time, first seeing/fighting Naggy/Yelinak/AoW/Emp back when they were King Badasses, getting your epic (back when it was somewhat challenging), actually working to develop plans to fight raid mobs because it was more rewarding than just using Random_UberGuild_014's published strategy and succeeding after repeated failed attempts.

I'm rambling, my point is: As bad as EQ gets, for a long long time it was the **** - there's no denying that. It'll always be a guideline, and the feeling it created at first will never be duplicated.
