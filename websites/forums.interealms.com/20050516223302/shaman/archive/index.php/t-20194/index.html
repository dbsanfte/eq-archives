CamaenMT
Adjust to the situation.

Well my guild (Quarm-GoD) usually has 4-5 shamans a raid - and I've seldom if ever heard any complaints from anyone of shamans not stacking. We all try and debuff to reduce the mobs initial dps (akin to trying to put the brakes on a jetliner) - then after that its all about adjusting to the particulars of the encounter.

Some raids are no-brainers where you can fire off debuffs then nuke to your hearts content. Other raids -Some PoP and GoD- can be trips into EQ hell where group hp's are in constant flux. A mark of a good raiding shaman is being able to tailor how he is playing the game to the situation at hand and add up his abilitys to contribute the sum of them all.

Multiple adds? Assist heal and assist debuff. If adds throw in heals on TM's and debuff the adds - cure any nasty dot's when able. If you see an add run from one side of your screen to another...its a good bet that wasnt intentional and its a good target to assist heal.
Hard hitting bosses? Assist heal MA after mob is debuffed - while watching group/raid hps for rampage/dot's.
Low Dps on raid? Nuke as much as possible - but always keep an eye on the goal of keeping everyone alive.

It's been beaten into the ground here again and again that shamans are not the best healers/nukers. But we do have the ability to do both and increase Dps via buffs, when you have alot of shamans the challenge is just finding the most effective way to utilize these abilitys.
