Finally, Sony has recognized that EQ has roleplaying aspects tied into it. At level 65, once you receive access to each elemental plane, shamans receive a unique AA that is based upon the character's religion. Each AA costs 35 points, but depending on your religion, you receive an innate skill that will affect your entire group!

Rallos Zek followers, being war mongers, can expect to see their melee friends' minimum damage raised by a small percentage, causing more damage than ever before.

Innorruk followers, which feed on fear, scare the bejezzees out of their enemies, allowing their group's melee to more easily strike through the mob's defenses.

Cazic Thule followers inspire their group's melee to hatred, making them swing faster, if endowed with powerful haste to begin with.

Followers of Mithaniel Marr boost their group's moral, increasing their ability to resist harmful spells and melee damage.

Sony has seemed to focus on the Shaman's power over melee based characters, which is good, because this seems to fit in with many roleplaying themes in addition to the historical background of the class itself when it was first implemented. All in all, it is an interesting set of AA's.
