As before, I took off my Innovator's Ring to get significantly below 255 STA, then juggled potions, buffs and tribute benfits until I was blue in the face.

This explains the strange results found in my less thorough parsing over on Gloriana's thread:

http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=21804

Basically, before 255 STA each individual point adds between 3 and 4 HPs. Post 255, all the way to 305, you need two points of STA for the same effect, just raising STA by one doesn't necessarily raise your HP total.

And that's pretty much it - it's a straight 50% reduction, achieved by the simple method of making you need 2 STA for a boost rather than a single point. The only fly in the ointment is what happens around 280 STA, which is something I can't explain....

Nytmare
For priest classes:
At 255 STA or less, each point of STA adds (level/20) HP.
Over 255 STA, the amount is cut in half.
ND3 AA adds 10%.

65 Shaman with under 255 STA and no ND, 1 STA = 3.25 HP
65 Shaman with over 255 STA and no ND, 1 STA = 1.625 HP
65 Shaman with under 255 STA and ND3, 1 STA = 3.575 HP
65 Shaman with over 255 STA and ND3, 1 STA = 1.7875 HP

Other classes are different. I know with warriors their formula changes with levels, at 65 their STA gains are about twice what Shamans get.
