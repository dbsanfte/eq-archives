There's a difference between insufficient and inadequate.
Our heals are enough that we can get by.
Our heals are not enough that we can get by in enough harder situations (ie slow bounces or unslowed adds).
Slow + heals is enough for a shaman to be a main healer in 99% of the game.

Herein lies the problem facing shamans. Shamans are balanced with "Slow + Heals" in mind against other healing classes who don't have slow to be balanced against themselves, but still benefit from its effect more often than not via one of the other slowing classes. Other slowing classes have also become more numerous - Bard slows have become meaningful and beastlords have entered the picture.

Additionally, mobs are tuned with slows in mind. As such, shamans' slows are checked twice in the balance picture: once in shaman vs. cleric/druid balance, and once in player vs. content balance.

This leaves shamans with subpar heals + slows vs. clerics' and druids' full strength heals + slows coming from an outside source (which, incidentally, is preferable anyway, as you can't cast heals and slows at the same time as a shaman).

This makes shamans great against more or less trivial content, where weakened shaman healing is sufficient, because they can effectively fill both roles simultaneously, saving the group a slot for another DPS class. Sure, another healing class + another slowing class might be more powerful in the strict sense, but against content where you don't need that extra power, shamans are great in that they fill both at once.

The problem is, that advantage doesn't carry over to more challenging content. It doesn't matter if you can contribute both healing and slowing when two other classes could fill the role more effectively. Against the content that matters, the increased power is needed and thus well worth the extra group slot, leaving shamans nothing to show for their versitility except an overused LFG tag.

That the other slowing classes are desirable for other roles in addition to slowing doesn't help the matter. Enchanters' charmed pets are a force to be reckoned with (where applicable) and they are the best choice for CC. Beastlords contribute unique buffs and respectable DPS. Bards are EQ's Swiss Army Knife. When you've already got sufficient healing, Shamans aren't wanted as healers, thus one of the other slowers is preferable because their other contributions are rendered more important.

The way the game has developed has further damaged shamans' postition. Their highly effecient HoT spells have been rendered virtually useless against current non-trivial content - the modern game is all about spam heals, and it is in this area that shamans are most inept.

The obvious solution is to quit balancing shaman's heals with slow in mind - to bring shaman healing to the point of relative equivilance with druid healing. This also means focusing more heavily on shamans' fast heals and putting less emphasis on the HoT line; shamans' HoT spells are useful only against content where they are well off, thus improvements in this area are not warrented.
1) Shamans' fast-heal capabilities should be increased to be roughly on par with that of druids.
2) Druids should recieve a line of HoT's for use against trivial content.
