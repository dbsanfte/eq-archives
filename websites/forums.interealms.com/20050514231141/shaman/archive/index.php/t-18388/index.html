Cragael
Ah, the wishlist... another time honored, seasonal tradition. Always willing to give it a whirl.

First, the spells...

Need a new slow. Yeah, yeah... we got a slow in every expansion but Velious. And, don't get me wrong, I much prefer having the disease-based slow (however wimpy) than not. But the way mitigation was implement basically trivialized the difference between our slows and the slows of the other classes. So, here is 2 ideas for a slow:

- a level 62, magic-based slow. Keep it at 75%, keep it mitigable. Make it cheaper with a faster recast and a decent resist adjustment, say -50 or -100. This is the workhorse spell for the class, after all.

- a level 64, cold-based, unmitigable slow. Keep it reasonnably weak, say between 40%. Make it moderatly expansive, say 400 or 500 mana, with a 5/5 seconds cast/recast. The idea is to keep the shamans significantly more able than the other slowers in at least a PORTION of the slow-mitigated fights.

For the buffs... At this point in the game, no amount of stats buffing is going to do anything but impressed the newbs. Beside the travel mechanic effects (movement speed, sight, lev, etc.), the only things worth buffing are: HP, Mana, HP regen, Mana regen, AC, damage shield, ATK, attack speed and spellcasting speed. All those are well covered and distributed among the all the casting classes already, so they can't be just handed out to shamans. But the new melee system offers opportunities... how about giving shamans:

- Endurance buffs. From level 5 to 61+, a gradation of raw endurance buffs to give the melees associated with a shaman that little bit of edge that all the STR buffs in the world SHOULD but NEVER DID give them.

- Endurance regen buffs. Make those warriors fight harder and longer was always supposed to be the theme of the shaman class.

On the self-buff front, we have 2 'line' already, which is fairly par for the course as far as a caster class is concerned. Except we're the only class that hasn't gotten a better self-buff with PoP (or since Velious for that matter). We got the 'frenzy' line (topping with Voice of the Berserker, level 59, from the Kunark era), which is fairly useless beyond the AC component, not to mention that, even though it's no longer the last-ditch spell it use to be, it still blanks out the caster all spellgems for 30 seconds on casting. And we have the 2 'bearform' spell, which would be fairly cool if the benefits weren't so meager and the graphic use so antiquated (with a graphical glitch that makes us all look like drooling idiots). Luckily, it's all fairly easy to fix…

- For the frenzy line, remove that 'no casting for 30 seconds after using it' crap, lengthen the duration to 2 hours, add a small HP regen component (2 for frenzy, 4 for Fury, 6 for rage, 8 for Voice) and add a level 63 version (with a nice AC upgrade and a 10HP/tick regen component).

- For the bear line, update the graphic (either the Velious kodiaks or the PoP bear, the one that can sit), replace the HP regen with a mana regen component (say 2 for the level 29 one and 4 for the level 55 one) and add a level 65 version (with 6 or 8 passive mana regen and UV/See invis).

Where is our fixed-duration invis? Every other robe or healer that can invis has one… why not us?

A 'real' (i.e. cast, as opposed to a triggered AA) canni upgrade. Something for level 62-ish, maybe 160 mana for 275 HP

A good review of the DOTs would be nice. For the amount of aggro they generate, they should either be a lot easier to land, hit a lot harder or be a lot cheaper. Yeah, I can pile Bane, BoN, Scourge, BoS, Pox and Breath and do a lot of damage. The problem is that if I do that, I'll likely be dead before I finish casting the second round of poison DOTs, not to mention, between the resists and the fizzles, seriously struggling for mana. During the same time, the wizard will still be happily blasting away and the necros will be landing just as many DOTs, doing more damage for less mana and half the aggro.

I have given up on our DD and Heals… 925 Cold is something we should have gotten BEFORE level 60, with Velium Strike being in the 1200 range. Tnarg should have healed for 1500+ and there should have been a Knarg upgrade at level 63 or 64. And Tears should have been in the 750/wave range. But they have obviously decided that for some reason we're both gimp healers and gimp nukers. I expect that boat has sailed and we won't see much change to those. But one can hope...

Now, for the AA...

- Spell Casting Subtlety. Oh come on… it's not rocket science. We're the class that could make the most use of that. That they gave it to the robes and not the healers was already a major brain fart. It's like if they had given Spell Casting Fury only to the hybrids.

- DOTs offer so much possibility for AA it's not funny. You could have an AA to make them longer. Or to make them hit harder. Or cost less. Or cast faster. Or go in easier. Or generate less aggro. Pick 3. And give them Archetype, Class and PoP levels.

- Group Quiescence anyone? Once every 12 minutes maybe? It's not Spirit of Wood or Paragon of Spirit but still.

- Resist Adjust AAs, once for each resist type (except Fire obviously)... Kind of an innate malo debuff added to each spell cast. Just that would go a long way to compensate for the gimpy spell upgrades of the last 3 expansions.

- Cannibalization upgrades… quicker recast, more HP for more Mana or both

- Revamp rabid bear… either make it the high-level bearform upgrade we never got or replace it completely but that short-use, long re-use, overblown melee self-buff is just embarrassing. Or make it something we can cast on others...

- A pet buff AA. Something nice, like making it tougher and nastier. Nothing to compete with the Beastlord, Mages or Necros, but something decent nevertheless.

Finally, the miscellaneous stuff…

- Herbalism skill. Level limited same as Alchemy (5 at level 25, +5 per level afterward, max'ng at 200). Put 3 to 5 herbs in every zone, with maybe a couple common among similar themed zone, plus one uncommon and one rare. It's not going to make us rich (alchemy components sell for silvers) but it's going to encourage people to work up their unique skill a bit.

- Slam… well, duh. The shaman are the most melee-oriented of all the casters, they get beter weapon skills, they actually use shields and three of the 6 startin classes even have bash as a racial ability.

Finally, what I really wish for is something NEW. The last 'new' thing the design team gave us was the ability to buff ATK with Avatar for KUNARK, which they promptly turned around and pimped out to most of the hybrids and everyone with a Sleeper's Tomb key the very next expansion. It would have to be something not too powerful, probably something that doesn't affect combat too much, if at all. Maybe the ability to summon mounts? Or a weak form of rez? Or a new, more powerful SOW. Something that make me say "Cool, there is actually SOMEONE looking over the Shaman at SOE this time". Haven't had that impression in a really long time…
