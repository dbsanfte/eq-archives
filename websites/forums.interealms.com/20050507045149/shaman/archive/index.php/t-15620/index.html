Tragik
Just some quick key things you may not have known about or would be too confusing to find in a search.

With Planes of Power you now have a central zone that connects all the newbie zones together, making travel much easier. It's also full of mid to high level quests, trainer NPCs and a central library to purchase vendored spells.

Due to a bug in the past, shaman pets can now dual weild, but you need to give the wolf two 1h weapons to "fool" it into dual weilding.

The Legacy of Ykesha expansion introduced a new spell for shaman called Stoicism (sp). It's basically a mini torpor and from what I've seen, extremely useful on the trip to level 60/65 and torpor/quiescence (sp). There is also pure blood which is a group version of Disinfecting Aura. LoY is full of nice appropriate level hunting grounds from 35 - 65 also. Nice loot to be had there as well, might find some upgrades.

Nurga and Droga have been revamped into level 45 - 55 and 56 - 65 zones, respectively. A new zone, Veksar, has been completed and can be found at the bottom of the lake in Lake of Ill Omen (look for the temple where the bloodgills have always been). Chardok and Solusek B have new temporary zones (Halls of Betrayel and err, Sol C - can't remember the right name suddenly).

Other then that, hunting in the 1st tier planes made leveling/AAs insanely easy. So easy in fact, that I see high level players running around in level 40 gear all the time now. Luclin and PoP introduced zones with bountiful experience but pitiful loot. Ran into a necro the other day weilding a stein of moggok and a havester wearing a crytalline robe from Crystal Caverns. He was level 64 with 50 something AA and thought he was l33t heh.

Anyway, kinda went off on a rant there. Dunno how much plat you have in the bank but check out the bazaar. Tons of new decent equipment for low prices now due to LoY/Veksar. Fast leveling available in PoP zones though you may want to wait until level 55 and quest the Stoicism spell first.

Everquest has seen a lot of good changes recently with a few minor negative side affects. Overall, it's much more enjoyable to play now.

Good luck and have fun.

I find that I can DoT in a (small) exp group now without necessarily tanking the whole fight.

PoP gives us access to zones that would've been entirely too far of a trip for a simple group. Things like Droga, which used to be a wicked little run from FV (with a possible boat ride) is as simple as OT book, to FM and bam you're in. Veksar? Even easier - FV book to LOIO.

Go get a spiritstone. Now you can bind wherever you want, and be 2 zones (spritstone click, then book) from that centralized plane of Knowledge and from there, anywhere. Codicil to that - don't die while you're... say ... bound in Veksar, but not hunting there. Annoying little tromp. ;)

Go partner with a mage of your level and laugh at the things you can kill together with little effort.

Decide NOW if you want to join a guild full of nasty little children in order to see the "endgame" and get a look at all the big zones. If not, enjoy yourself, and don't fret the flags, the loot, or the exp. LDoN is coming out, and it looks like what they've said is true - you'll have things to do with a single group that are worth doing.

The gear these days is so far above what was available to shamans in the past (some tae ew in particular) for the effort and price, that you can run out and solo/duo things that would've been a challenge for a full group in the past.

You didn't miss much with luclin. Skip it. Lord what a waste. Bazaar and a couple of quests.

Oh, yeah. At 61, we finally get a pretty good pet. You'll want to read up on getting a horse (unless you're a die hard dungeon crawler) and a solstice earring.

Tragik's post is right on the money.

I'll add to that by saying it's worth getting the LoY expansion. While some may disagree, I think it's a fantastic addition to the game. There is some wonderful equipment to be had, some beautiful zones (Dulak and Hate's Fury are awesome) and some great new quests/spells.

Making travel easier was the best thing to happen to EQ since it was released, IMO. With the Plane Of Knowledge and an Everfrost Spiritstone, you can bind at your hunting grounds, gate out to Everfrost, make a 1 minute run to the PoK book to sell your loot, pick up a KEI, look for a group etc etc and be back at your camp within a few minutes. There is no PoK book in LoY, which is a little annoying but there are teleports available to Stonebrunt Mountains, one zone away from the first LoY zone.
