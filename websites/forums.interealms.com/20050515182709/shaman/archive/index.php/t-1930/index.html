Hrothgar
Unfortunately, Everquest 2 featured technology rather than gameplay. When asked questions about corpse runs and other gameplayer-related items, the team ducked and focused more on how great the game looks.
Using all sorts of graphical tricks, the game looks lightyears ahead of its predecessor. Using a totally new engine built from the ground up (not the same engine used for Star Wars Galaxies, even), the EQ2 team set out to make a beautiful upgrade worthy of the EQ name.

All the materials use pixel shaders and bump-mapping, so things such as metallic armor have a glossy sheen to them. Due to multilayered texturing (a "clean wall" has four layers of grime, moisture, and dirt textures applied on it) walls look appropriately grimy without taxing resources.

The characters are well-animated and are filled with all sorts of little details. Dragons have appropriately leathery skin, and watching it move around, I noticed how a part of its chest was heaving with every breath; a small detail that wasn't needed, but Sony Online Entertainment created it anyhow.

The only gameplay detail we could dig out of the team was the character generation, in that you only create the character's look at first. Class is decided by the actions you take while doing newbie quests, so you literally create your class while you game as a newbie.

Hopefully, we'll soon see actual gameplay, but for now, Everquest 2 is one of the prettiest MMORPGs our crusty jaded eyes have seen.
