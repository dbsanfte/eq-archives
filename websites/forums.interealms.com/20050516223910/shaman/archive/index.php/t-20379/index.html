I didn't have much time, due to the patch today, but I was able to start on a new project of mine. I've always been a firm believer in AC, thinking it makes a great deal of different to tanking. Shamam's parses in BoT showed there was actually very little difference, so I thought I'd try mobs in Nadox to see if there was any effect on mobs with a much lower ATK (and level).

My target was a level 50 (so barely DB) troll guardian, which normally doubles for 135 at most. My combat AAs consist of LR5 and CS1; this had been the first time fighting these mobs since I had CS1, and eventually once I have CS3 I intend to repeat the experiment. My AGI in both cases was 255.

I fought the mob, unslowed, for a shade over 10 minutes. When parsing, I used the data from the mob's first swing to exactly 10 minutes afterwards. I know it's a very small sample, but hopefully around 500 swings is enough to draw some sort of conclusion. I intend to complete tests for 1025 AC and 825 AC as well, with a 625 AC parse if I can stay alive long enough! ;)

(Edit: 625 was a pain to get to, literally. For the rest, Virtue, Bramblecoat and VotB were up, with the only changes in AC being due to raw AC changes.)

taraddar
Warrior parses have show that what is effective ac is definitely dependant on the mob. The mobs attack, for lack of anything better to call it, and probably level definitely effect how your ac effects things. Xegony for example has been parsed quite a bit since it's a 45+ minute fight anyway. The parses have show that for xegony the point where it really starts making a difference is around 1900ac and it shows a steady decrease in dps of about 10% for every 100 ac up to at least 2200ac.

I've always felt that ac's effectiveness is a s curve. Relatively flat below a point where your just getting hammered ever which way and it doesn't really matter. You then reach a point where it really starts making a difference and starts making noticable shifts on average hits and dps. Then on the top end it flattens out. Things have skewed towards mostly lower hits and further ac doesn't decrease it signficantly more. For shaman we're typically an expansion or 2 behind as far as obtainable ac compaired to the cut off points. I figure by the end of GoD shaman will be able to get the 2k+ac to be able to tank PoP gods well =)

Where that curve is depends on the mob. Thats one thing that pop did seem to change. Unlike SoL where all raid mobs seem to be so high attack that it was hard to get to the bottom of that curve, pop definitely allows tanks to get to the point where thier ac is effective and it is definitely a noticable difference.
