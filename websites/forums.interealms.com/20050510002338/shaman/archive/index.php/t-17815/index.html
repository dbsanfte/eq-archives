Jinheim
Spell casting haste is the most important focus effect we can get. This is because it takes soooo long to debuff mobs. Any little bit of detrimental haste helps. Detrimental haste also helps a lot for soloing, if that is your thing. Beneficial haste is also crucial. I think a shaman's primary role after debuffing is to heal, so beneficial haste is a must for me. Most shamans disagree with me on this, so chances are you will get a lot more mileage out of detrimental haste than you will beneficial.

After spell haste, I would say improved healing is the next most important focus effect. Again, though, I heal a lot more than most shamans do so you probably won't like it as much as me.

Thirdly, mana pres. This is just an awesome effect, whether you prefer to heal, dot, or nuke. Despite popular belief, it is possible for a shaman to go oom. If you don't have time to cannibalize, then you have the same mana regen and mana pool as everyone else (in fact, yours are probably worse). If you're ever in a situation like this where you cannot stop to cannibalize (i.e. chain casting tnargs on a raid) then mana pres focuses are golden. I'd recommend mana pres focuses to everyone, but I would say it isn't as big of a priority for someone that rarely finds themselves in a situation where they can't stop to cannibalize.
