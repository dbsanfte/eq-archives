Radag
A young shaman should be thinking about the kills he will nead when he gets up in level and work hard to train up those skills.

I would tank solo each level untill offense, defense, 1hb, 2hb, and pierce are all maxed. The low levels go by so fast that you will not be able to keep all your skills up but you should be able to get them all up to level by the time you reach your early to mid 20s.

I would consentrate on ac gear for damage mitigation and things like an iksar chestplate with regen can make a nice differance. I would also pick up a cheep hast item like fbss or schw to improve damage output untill you get and can use a JBB.

By the time you reach 40 your defense ( wich dirctly improves your ac ) and other mele skills should all be around 200.

At this time in your life Regen and Hast are very nice then AC > HP > Wis.

Before you get the JBB, then the Horse is nice but not great except when you are grouping. I would get JBB first then horse.

JBB is very hard to interupt and you can easily tank for your dog while clicking the JBB. If you don't have a horse or are indoors then I would equip a nice big 2hb to swing between JBB blasts. If you are on a horse then you tank but don't mele becuase you will loose the med benifit from the horse if you turn autoattack on.

High AC + Horse + JBB + a regen item is very very good untill you start going to PoP zones. Pre-pop, having a lot of wisdom or HP is rather useless. It provides a safty margin but does nothing to improve efficency.

At 58 you get a fraction CH and HP becomes more important. HP becomes critical with Can V and AC is marginalized by the high attack rated mobs in PoP. I would recomend starting to give more importance to HP at about level 50 or so with the goal of having a lot of HP buy the time you reach 60.

I would never recomend paying much attention or giving much value to wisdom at any time. Even fairly common gear at the higher levels will have wisdom on it and shaman have a fantastic mana generation rate at high levels wich can dramaticaly compensate for a smaller mana pool. Instead, I would consentrate on AC and HP gear and wisdome will creep up all by itself as your gear get better.

A properly equiped and trained shaman can solo very effectivly all the way into the 60s and still function well as a raid slower. The cost of this is some minor reduction in our rather poor healing ability within a group because of the smaller mana pool. A hard ac shaman is also much more effective at maintaining his place high on the agro list for root parking and protecting the enchanter and other casters in the group. When mez breaks the mob should come pound on you instead of the enchanter or the cleric.

I would solo some each and every level to make sure all the mele skills are maintained in good order, but I would also spend some times in groups in order to gain crowd controll, targeting, and healing practice.

This is a great class that is complex to play and a lot of fun. I hope you have a great time.

Goldark
I levelled reasonably fast, and grouped much more than I soloed. I find much more variety in grouping. Anyways, all I can tell you is how I did it, you must find your own way. Hopefully, my experiance will be of some use though.

Due to grouping a lot, I did find my defence got a little left behind. Defence is key at 51+ as you start becoming the main slower against mobs that hit hard and fast. Once you start visiting PoP zones you really must have defence maxed.

When I solo in PoP I pull with malo, send the pet in, and then slow before the mob arrives in camp. At 61 the pet can hold off a mob for the few seconds you need to slow. Gnoll hide lariats with stun proc can help a lot. Next I cripple - I find it helps with PoP mobs, but I never used it much before PoP. Then poison dot, epic dot, disease dot. Chain cast JBB, renew poison dot if required. All the while I am sitting on my horse, not meleeing (so I get med benefit), and tanking the mob. I find one cast of torpor is enough for 90% of fights.

Before I got my epic, malo and torpor I used the same system, but due to the risk of interrupts on Kragg's Mending I would mem root - the I could root and back off to heal if required.

So what does it mean for young shaman?

1) Max your defence each level. That means, don't root rot, don't kite. Tank IN TEH FACE. Solo a bit every now and then, even if you prefer to group.
2) Don't worry too much about melee skills, but try to keep at least one of them maxed. Tanking IN TEH FACE will help with this.
3) At lower levels, regen > ac > hp > wis; For PoP, hp > ac > regen > wis/ SELL those velium jasper rings. Get the fire wedding rings (6ac 65hp) instead.
4) JBB >> all at 46. Do what you can, sell your soul, get a JBB at 46. If you do, you will be leveling from 46 to 50 faster than 40 to 45.
5) Horse >> most once you start hunting mainly outdoors (PoP). If you haven't already sold your soul for a JBB, sell it now for a horse (or lizard thingy)
