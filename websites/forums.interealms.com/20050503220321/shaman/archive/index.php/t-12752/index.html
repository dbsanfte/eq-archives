Goldark
Depends a bit on your role in the guild. If you are one of the main shaman and slow a lot, and hence get aggro, then the defensive abilities would probably suit you well. ND3 first, then the others like CA3, LR5 etc.

RC is very useful, I only have RC1 though, I think RC2 is a notable improvement to the number of things it will cure.

If you are often buffing people on raids, then SCR/SCRM is useful. Similarly, if you use torpor/quiesence a lot, its great and really increases its effectiveness.

Spirit call is more a fun AA, not much use on a raid. VP, again not much use on raids but great for soloing and sometimes in XP groups.

Quickbuff is not just a gimic, faster torpor and patch heals can be really useful. Don't knock it. Again, depends on your role, I don't find myself healing much beyond trying to keep myself alive if I get slow aggro.

So in summary:
Debuffing shammy - ND3, CA3...other defensive AA
Buffing/Healing shammy - SCR/SCRM, Quickbuff

Go for RC when you get bored and want some quick gratification, I think you will like it.

Just my opinion, as I'm not an uber shammy in a top-end guild :)

taraddar
All handy aa's.

Depending on your current hps ND3 can be very nice. If you gear already has you over 3k unbuffed tho it's probably not a big deal other than bragging rights. If you want survivability CA/LR would be better.

Like some people have said if you do a large amount of the slowing and get the aggro from it. CA/LR would be a good idea and eventual CS/ID.

SCRM is wonderful. With a crown of abivolence if your lucky enough to pick one up it makes torpor/q last for what seems like forever. Longer lasting buffs goes well with MGB. Sure focus lasts long enough base for mgb to refresh but since mine lasts 70% longer (scrm and 8th shawl) I can wait for mgb to refresh and not have to reuse it shortly after and can save it for wipe outs, dispellers, what ever. Very handy if your the only shaman around or only with mgb/7th.

Quick buff is nice. It definitely cuts down on the hastle of buffing if you can cast them faster. This was especially true with 60 group focus. 7th is already alot faster casting base. It's also really nice on torpor/q. Personally I stoped at qb2 and use a spell haste item to get me close to 50% cast time and hit it with cleric buff if i bother to get it.

Radiant cure is a steal at 2 points for the first level. It's a must get IMO. The later levels cost more and don't add as much imo but still nice to have but can wait.

SCM is good too. Less mana spent has the same effect as increasing mana pool and mana regen at the same time. Yeah shaman can make mana back at high rates but we have to actively make it meaning we aren't doing anything else.

The healing aa's or nuke aa's aren't bad depending on what you typically do.

Unfortunately there isn't as clear an answer once you get past cann v. I'm sure you would even see some arguements about the mgb that you already got.
