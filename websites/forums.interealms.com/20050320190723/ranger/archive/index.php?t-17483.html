(This is my apology for the long-long post below, I’m sorry, reading the other posts sparked me)

Now what I'm hearing is a lot of talk about being a traditional Ranger. And if you're like me, and devour Tolkien's literature , then you want a traditional ranger. The sad thing is, EQ, and for that matter technology isn't advanced enough to make a world where we can actually become a person and role-play our characters, and be massively multiplayer at the same time. If I thought we could, then I would ask for EQ2 to stay in development for many more years, until they could create landmasses with actual distances to travel and lands that are beneficial and hard to access, and separate cultures that rarely mingle. That way getting lost in a forest would be perilous, and a Ranger would be necessary as a guide figure, or a hunter, someone that knows the way. (Instead people look for a wizard to teleport them to the nearest city.) Also in that way, you could actually get away into places that aren’t infested with the games society and that are actually beneficial.

Most of us here like the idea of a ranger being the character that gets out and goes the distances and maps the lands, and EQ has to make it possible for rangers to do that and not allow the traveling stamina we must have to leak into every other class. Now that leaves a problem, what do the other classes do if we are the ones out in the wild. Well here EQ is going to have to develop structured societies and create methods and ways to make other players want to role-play, and role-play different classes. And that is the problem with role-playing not only Ranger, but just about any class in EQ. EQ is more Massively Multiplayer than it is Role-Playing, and every thing that is to be revealed is done with several people, and I think I speak for a lot of us when I say that maybe being your own Ranger just might prove more interesting than a nice guild and raids all the time. Maybe we actually do want a different world in which to role-play the characters that you read in books, or at least do the things they do without relying on 50 other people. But in that same aspect, I would want the game to be massivly multiplayer, and have players dependant on other players (depending on class) just not so much as the current EQ is.

So for the ranger in EQ2, I want all of the things that have been spoken in this thread, and more. But I also think we must realize that EQ2 will have to take a larger leap in gaming than the original EQ had done. A leap large enough to keep people addicted to a game without making them become addicted together and allow more room for the RPG aspect of EverQuest than the Massively Multiplayer one.

Since several people have brought up Tolkien, I'll point out something that most people seem to have missed.

I think most people have been sidetracked by D&D into thinking Ranger is a class (i.e. type) of person (maybe a profession or lifestyle). The Rangers were, however, a group of people (the exiled royalty of Gondor and their retainers).

Aragorn wasn't just some random wood wise hunter that could fight. Aragorn was the King of Gondor in exile, heir to long life, elven magics, secret lore, mighty heirlooms (although broken)... He had access to councils with other movers and shakers in the world. He was a guardian of his people, forced to live in the wilds until he could regain his throne.

If we want to break the D&D mold, we must call the "class" hunter or woodsman, since we aren't playing "The Grey Company" members when we choose ranger.
