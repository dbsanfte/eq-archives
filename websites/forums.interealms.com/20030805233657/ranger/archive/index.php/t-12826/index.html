WARNING: There will be NO profanity in this post. If this offends you, I appologize in advance.

I Like Everquest. I think the Customer Service does NOT Suck. I think the Game Developers have done a tremendous job. I do not fault Sony for including features in expansions in order to sell more of said expansions.

I will expand:

Customer Service: Over my 3.5 years of playing Everquest, I have had maybe 6 occassions to petition or call Customer Service. On each occassion, they have been helpful and friendly. I have not always been given the answer I hoped for, but what help could be given was. I understand when they can't help me because "if they do it for me, they have to do it for everyone" and frankly, everyone is not as reasonable as I am :)

Game Developers: I rarely hear praise of these folks, and yet look at the world they have created! It is tremendously complex. When they develop features, they do so in a small environment, then test in a slightly larger test server. Things can change dramatically (for good or ill) when it is released to 3000 uniquely clever people at the same time.

When things DO work out, or work better than intended, great. They get flamed because it "unbalances" the game for those that were not affected. When the change works out badly, they get flamed for the error, and they get flamed for correcting it.

'Greedy' Sony: They are in business to make money. They can choose which features to include as part of an expansion or as an update. We have gotten many, MANY features, zones, and other enhancements without cost that were not part of the game you purchased. They chose to include some of the new features in PoP, that is fine by me. I am still entertained, I am still buying.

This game has evolved tremendously since it's inception. On the whole, I think the changes have been for the better, even though new players have it easier than I did.

To think I could take the $12 per month that entertains me for 100 hours of fun, and spend it on a 2 hour movie, play 4 holes of golf, play in a casino for 15 minutes (on average), go to dinner (at Mcdonalds, or buffet here). No, I like the $12 right where it goes now, and will continue to go for some time.

Thanks for listening

Tekk, I don't think your view is unpopular at all. The fact of the matter is, EQ is hands down the most cost-effective form of entertainment there is. Even regular TV isn't this cheap (when you consider the electric bill that comes with using a TV).

Here's the deal though. EQ players are passionate about the game and their characters. They've spent days, weeks, months, YEARS developing them so they feel they have a sizable stake in how the game turns out. This isn't a bad thing and you know this from your programming background. 989 Studios/Verant/SoE knew that in order for their game to be truly successful, people would have to become attached to their characters which breeds passion. They also knew the downside of creating such a passionate environment.

You're right, their testbed is really small in comparison to the playerbase at large. In addition, there are many people who play on Test who have never played a character to the level their Test characters are at. That's very limiting. There are some issues that would never occur on Test. Like, how many times does Temple of Veeshan have 120+ people in it on Test? Happens (or happened) a lot on the older proddy servers. Test is also a mainly cooperative environment (by this I mean the players are a lot more friendly to each other, since the community is so small). You can't find bugs that can be exploited in a purely malicious manner unless you have people willing and able to use them. Anyone remember the zone crash bug?

My main problem with SoE as it relates to EQ is how they prioritize things. Bugs that harm the players are low priority but if a bug actually helps the player, omg emergency patch asap! Also, I love it how they allow "bugs" concerning loot tables and the like to stay in for the first week an expansion comes out then drastically kill the drop rate on anything with any inherent desirability to the players (parchments, windblade/girdle of mithaniel, etc) after that first watershed period.

That said, I enjoy playing the game, warts and all. Fact of the matter is, you get what you pay for usually. Given the amount of hours I play in a typical month, I'm paying like 6 to 8 CENTS per hour to be entertained playing EQ (yes, I did the math last month :p). Just like I wouldn't buy a Kia Sephia and expect to get all the features and bells and whistles that come with a Bentley Azure, the same goes for EQ. Besides that, I realize the 'multiplayer' aspect adds a TON of issues all its own. If EQ was a single player game, it probably could be much better in terms of QA.


The game rocks but as with most things, it could always be made better.
