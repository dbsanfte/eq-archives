On my server, The Rathe, we have a rotation system for most raid-level targets. There are some that are FFA, but the majority of raid-level targets require you to perform some action to get on the rotation.

In the case of NTOV, you need to kill Aaryonar. Once you do, you are on the rotation and you have a certain amount of time to clear NTOV every time your turn comes up (my guild is not yet on that rotation; soon though).

The rotation system is quite nice, actually, in that it gives everyne a chance at the highly contested targets. There are still enough FFA targets to fight, though, that you never have trouble finding something to raid. At first I was not entirely sold on the idea, but I've since seen that the guilds on the rotation already are generally very nice about letting you attempt a rotation target (it is considered common courtesy on our server to ask permission of the guild that is currently up in the rotation -- you technically do not have to, but its a nice thing to do).

NToV rotations are common because it's a pain in the ass to clear all the yard trash, or at least it was.
