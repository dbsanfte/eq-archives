STA isn't important for melee or archery except for it to be >100. If it is <100, fatigue will affect your melee stats. STA is important for increasing your total HP total, although not as important as pure HP items. HP are important for raids against monsters with AE attacks, e.g., Temple of Veeshan.

DEX affects Archery damage output. However, it doesn't affect melee damage output except to increase marginally the percent chance for procs on your weapons and Call of... proc buffs.

WIS is the stat that affects a ranger's mana pool and fizzle rates.

Well I disagree with boosting Str to the neglect of all else, but Dex does not improve your chance to hit with a melee weapon. Chance to hit is based on your Level, your skill in the weapon you are using, raw ATK buffs, and your target's level and avoidance AC.

Dex helps raise skills in melee skills and archery, but that's not incredibly useful. Dex does help proc rate, crit rate, and archery damage, but if you're not using archery, a procing weapon, and don't crit, it's dormant, i.e. does absolutely nothing. Again, Dex does not improve chance to hit with a melee weapon. Dex is not much use to a Ranger until you get Call of Sky or get a procing weapon (in fact, before then, it's nearly useless), then it moves up a notch, then it gains a little more respect when you get Jolting Blades. When you get crits, it's again a little more useful. By the time you get Archery Mastery 3 and Endless Quiver, Dex is probably the most important attribute, but that's a long ways off. On the whole though, until/unless you get Archery Mastery 3 and Endless Quiver, you should not be overly concerned about Dex.

Str does boost your DPS... but not much. Its effect is very small. Boosting Str to the neglect of all other stats is not very wise considering it doesn't really increase DPS much. From what I hear, and I tend to agree, Str is so easy to get most Rangers don't even worry about it and just let it come naturally. I'm at 199 Str self-buffed and I didn't even try.

Getting to 100 Sta is a milestone. Never worry about the yellow bar again, unless you're jumping. More Sta is nice, more HPs are always needed. There's not much to say about the Stat.. the effects of it are obvious and not open to much debate.

Int is pointless for a Ranger. Doesn't help fizzle rate, doesn't help our mana pool. It's less useful than Cha for us.

Wisdom is proven to decrease our fizzle rate and gives us more mana. Mana is very important for us, and if anyone tells you differently, they do not play their Ranger to max potential.

I hope this cleared things up for you.
