I played EQ2 from late beta to about X-Mas. If they have already introduced the things i complain about, i take them back. Unreservedly.

Here's the things that drove me out of EQ2 -- I liked it alot when i first started playing:

The arrows are restrictions dressed up as recommendations. When i was playing, if you did not bring exactly the right combo of players with the right combo of lvls, you got no loot from mobs. Forget sticking a monster in a zone and saying "here she is, whack her and you will find unbelievable treasure!", the message is: "bring 2 clerics and a guardian with no more than 2 lvl differences, and 3 other players, but make sure none are from different cities, or more than 4 lvls different, but don't bring 2 groups, and make sure you are all wearing red socks! On the left foot. Except dorfs. Who need green..."

Ok it's an exaggeration, but the design intention seemed pretty clear: you do this exactly how we want you to do it, or not at all.

Same with Tradeskills. I found the TS system more enjoyable than the adventure system, despite it being bugged as all hell on release. Again, up to lvl 20 or so, actually 30. Then they made you make only blue widgets for another player, who can turn them into brown left-angled wodgets for another player to make a part of a the leg of a red table, for another player to assemble an antique victorian table that requires 7 different parts from 7 other players. A daisy chain of interdependence that is simply just frustration.

Same with quests. I could spend 30 mins trying to find a group to complete various quests and the whole thing would take a couple of hours after player B killed the four gnolls he needed, then player C did the 5 ghost gnolls she needed, the we finally got rouind to the 2 pink gnolls that i needed.

OR i could lvl up solo and go back and do the quests when they were trvial in 1 hour. And not have to spend so much time putzing around or asking friends who were busy trying to get their own quests done. Which is what i usually did.

If they had used the storyline of Qeynos/Freeport to have some epic battles of good v evil -- which seemed to me to be the best thing about EQ2 and the drop dead most obvious bleeding hook ever lucked on to keep players involved in the world and motivated to take part in -- i would probably still be playing. Instead, they send you out to collect more gnoll tails, because these are vitally important to the stability of Qeynos whole political system... but don't hang out with trolls and ogres because they are evil n stuff!

I have zero interest in a grind for months to be denied access to more content.

**Cliff notes: static world, tedious progress, the dull graphics started to bug me, the restrictions on mobs and grouping became an annoyance, and the whole experience started to remind me of the bad bits of EQ1.**

Tradeskills are a trainwreck IMO. The Con system is annoying at times but honestly I have to say that the trivial loot code is probably for the best. How many EQ players were driven nuts by the farmers camping what were trivial mobs for them, thus blocking you from any chance at fighting mobs that were challenging and rewarding for your level?

I think the Arrows are a bit much when combined with the group/solo distinction. The death penalty is so minor in EQ2 that I think they should just give you con colors and let you figure out from trial and error which mobs are extra tough or not. Leave the group/solo distinction in though so groups don't waste their time killing solo mobs.

Previously, it pissed me off that apparently you got X experience for Y encounter regardless of whether you had a 3 man group or a 6 man group. However I see in the latest letter that they are finally fixing that... so I'll hold my tongue on that until I see how the fix works out.

The one thing that does still annoy me is that group mobs are either trivial to solo or damn near impossible, thus making a LOT of quests off limits to soloists. By the time you can solo the group mobs needed for the quest they're often grey and thus you can't get a chest to collect a needed item. In other cases as you get higher level, the lowest level of green con mobs are utterly trivial in that they can't hit you at all. I can often fight the lowest level green mobs as a lvl 36 illusionist and they simply can't hit me at all. Nothing but misses. That's out of whack as well, just in the opposite direction. ;)

Overall, I'd rate EQ2 a very solid game although it's certainly got flaws. It's come along pretty quickly since release and heading mostly in the right direction as far as adventuring. Tradeskills need a MAJOR revamp to put it mildly though. The interdependancies are so overdone that they're just ridiculous. Personally I've always found tradeskills tedious so I can't really comment on the overall 'fun factor' of creating items objectively. I do like that it's a much more interactive process than simply hitting the combine button and waiting on fail/success at least.
