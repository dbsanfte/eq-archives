1) Yes, SoW potions really sell. They sell better than any other potion...that doesn't mean they sell *great*....but yes you can sell them.

2) You won't make money at it. SoW potions Trivial at 37. At that point you will fail about 35% of the time. It takes you 15 combines to get one 10dose potion...at 6pp each thats 90pp in materials for a 100pp potion...WHOO! I made 10plat! I am teh rich shAmAN!!1

3) To get where you can make Sow Potions reliably get your skill up to 70 - this will take several thousand plat. Probably about 5k or so. Then you will have a 95% success rate and you can start making 40 - 50 plat on each potion.

4) Sow Potions are 100pp for a 10 dose. Thats the price. Don't try to 'undercut' the other shamans by selling for 90 - all you're doing is ruining the economy. However, it is customary to offer a discount for people buying several at once. Plus the average rate for guildies or good friends is about 75plat.

If you want to get repeat customers, throw a 'bonus potion' their way...I sold 4 shrink and 4 SoW potions to a repeat customer...I gave him a slight discount and threw in a 5 dose Damage Shield Potion. Hopefully he will get 'addicted' to the DS potions and start buying them.. (15pt dmg shield for about 15mins). I also give out 5dose cure poison or cure disease to people that can't cast those spells if they buy a few potions from me.

uglytobone
The fastest way by far to make a lot of pp is getting good at the buy/sell/trade game.

As for sows... if you wait to start alchemy at level 29 (30?) and then train 21 pts you can start out skilling up in alchemy at near zero cost, especially if you can sell sows for 120pp. People will pay this much on Tunare. Once trivial you make sows to finance making shrinks to skill up. This puts you back down to zero cost again (except for time spent) for skilling up.

As your skill increases sows and shrinks become more profitable and pretty soon you are decent money per sale. Set the price as your server dictates. I think sales depend more on who is in the zone than whether you sell them for 100pp or 120pp.

With no failures and decent charisma material costs for a stack of sows is about 53pp. Once shrink is trivial your actual cost will be around 55-60pp. Always carry potions with you, refill when convenient, and it will be a consistent source of income.

SisterLi
Sow potions sell at 120 plat. Folks might tell you that 100 is the price, but it is not. When I had less of a skill, I found that is was usually sufficient to say "At 100, I make maybe 10plat in profit. That's just not worth my time. My sow potions sell for 120 plat." Then the person either ponyied up the cash, or went on about their business. No reasonable person is going to begrudge you a reasonable profit. Making potions takes time, effort, and a lot of bag space. 100 just isn't a reasonable amount of compensation for all of that. (Until you get to the point where you are succeeding 9 out of 10 -- but that is several levels and skill levels away from 39. At that point I would (and did) still hold the line at 120. No need to undercut your fellow shaman.)

It's a 10-charge shot of independence. There are a lot of zones where attempting to run across sans sow is suicide. My standard line, when I see someone auctioning 100plat sow potions is that they need to change their ad to the following:

"Sow potions, 120plat each. Or auction for a rez with the money you saved by not buying my potion."

My Alchemy skill is 180, and I ceased selling sow potions a long time ago. I don't need the cash, and prefer to leave the sow market to shaman who are working on their skills. But do not undercut the product. 120 is a reasonable price, hold out for that.
