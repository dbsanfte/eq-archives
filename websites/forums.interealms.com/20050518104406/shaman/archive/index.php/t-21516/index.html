Shenen Alquimista
I know the pain your guild is going through. Mine was also in the same position at one point. There's a few things to keep in mind:

1. It's a cycle - every guild goes through it.

2. Perhaps your aa are set too high. Instead of requiring an app to have aa that you'd like them to have, think of where you are in pop currently and only require what is absolutely necessary for that class. You may find, that even if the aa requirement is dropped by 10aa, you'll have a few more ppl interested.

3. Flags - are there any open raiding group around on your server? Fennin Ro is lucky enough to have Khasel open raids, and a few other people that normally lead open raids. These raids are a great source of backflags. If that isn't available to you, then you may have to lower your flag requirements and set up a once a week or every two week type of backflagging event within your guild. This is for current members, applicants, and potential future applicants.

4. Potential future apps - Is there a channel you have for the guild/apps/potential apps to get together and talk? We have one and it works GREAT for spreading the word about your guild for recruitment purposes, as well as giving your guild a chance to chat outside of tells with apps and potential apps.

If you have any other questions, please feel free to contact me either here on the boards, or send me a cross-server tell.

Fudapuda
On Bertox, we have two things happening that might be good ideas for you.

You mention "loose friendship" with another guild. We have two or three guilds that routinely raid together as a group--they retain their individual memberships, but they just seem to do most of their raids together. I have no idea how the separate out the loot, but if your main objective is to get more people flagged, this is a good way to do it. Takes some work by the officers to work out who is Raid Leader, etc, but it would get you the bodies you need for a RZWL hit or Sol Ro hit.

Another idea is to organize "pick-up" raids, and advertise them. The "base" of the raid becomes your guild, but you allow essentially any warm body who isn't a known asshat and who meets minimum qualifications (generally just a min level, such as 60) to join the raid. You get a LOT of dying this way, but you can also get 72+ for the raid. Your guild gets the flags, and you also meet new people you might want to recruit later on. On Bertox, we have a couple of guilds who tend to raid this way, as well as one or two fellows who work hard to organize complete pickup raids (insanity, imo).

Beyond that, the only suggestion I would have would be to approach one of the current elemental guilds/Time guilds, and see if you can work out a "tag-along" system whereby you essentially apprentice your guild under them--you provide warm bodies for their raids (since they may also be suffering from low numbers atm) and you get flags in return.

Stuff you can consider doing:

1. Do a massive backflag push for an entire month. Announce that anyone that's been interested in joining your guild should app now since you'll be doing a complete backflagging of elemental planes. For every 10 apps, you'll probably get about 2 - 3 good recruits out of it is my guess. But, those are most likely recruits you'd never have had if you didn't do the backflagging.
2. Open up recruitment to every class, regardless of AAs. There are actually some good newer players out there, believe it or not. Just because some don't have the required AAs doesn't mean they aren't a great asset to a guild. They can get the AAs they need later on.
3. Our guild used to use loot council, but we've since gone to DKP. I love it, and many many of those that were hard against changing the 3+ year loot system have since come to like DKP moreso than loot council. DKP auctions for loot are fast. People spend what they want for items based on how valuable it is to them, not by how much other people think the item should be valued by someone. Also keeping track of DKP adds incentive for people to actually log on. Doing backflags for us gives additional bonus DKP multiplier (depends what the target is. First time succeeding on Ele Plane gods was 1.5 multiplier. doing backflags is usually 1.1 or 1.2). We've had problems with ninjacampers when ****ty backflag raids were announced. DKP helps stop that, especially when those people that stick around for the raids get bonus dkp for the back flag.

Whether you like it or not, to stay up on your numbers, you'll need to backflag.

After having been allocated gear with loot council for multiple years, and having used DKP for about 7 months, DKP is a far far far superior system IMO.
here's a link to our dkp system
http://www.eqwatchers.com/points.htm
in short:
-for us we earn 1 point for every hour you raid.
-items are "sold" to the highest bidder via a silent auction.
-we have an online database we can log into to see our current point total and how we've spent our points etc.
I wish we'd gone to DKP much much longer ago.

Reasons our silent auction works so well:
-it's fast. usually loot is done in about 5 mins tops
-items are worth different amounts to different people. An all/all haste shoulder item from earth probably isn't worth much to a melee who has a haste item already. But to a caster where haste items with good stats are far and between, it's fantastic, so they'll bid more on it. Plate items with FT aren't worth much to a warrior, but a lot to the other classes.
-items' values go down as you equip yourselves better and can do harder raids. ele plane armor for us used to go for 20-60 points per item on average. now pieces go for 1-20 when we even bother to do ele plane raids. no one would dream of paying 50 points anymore for ele plane armor when we raid time every week, and do GoD raids on the off nights. having a library of items constantly needs to be reevaluated as to what an item is worth as you get access to new and better items.
-guildmembers have ZERO right to bitch to officers saying "why the hell didn't I win the item over soandso?! I'm clearly a better choice blah blah blah". With points, if you deserve to win the item, then spend the points. If you don't have the points, you don't deserve the item. Simple as that.
