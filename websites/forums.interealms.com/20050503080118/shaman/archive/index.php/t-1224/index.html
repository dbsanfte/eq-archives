What we know:
-Cripple lowers proc rate of mobs due to the dex debuff

What we don't entirely know:

Whether or not the AGI/STR debuffs does anything is tied to whether or not MOBs can be affected by stats.

It's fairly easy to check that pets and MOBs are NOT affected by change in Stamina (just cast the buff on your pet or on a charmed MOB... no more HP after than before). From this you can reasonnably infer that STR and AGI won't be really impacting the MOBs either.

AC, on the other hand, is alays good to debuff, especially with those extremely high-AC MOBs in Luclin.

Chutoi
Jonaman, those are very hard questions to quantify, because the effects are interrelated in some aspects.

I can confirm that the AC/AGI debuff component DOES make a difference. Want a good check? Go to the Deep, and cripple an underbulk. You'll notice immediately that your tanks average damage per hit rises. However, since agi also affects AC . . . well, you can see why it's going to be all but impossible to check the results of stats versus pure AC.

The strenth debuff is harder to see. Frankly, I have crippled a lot of mobs looking for a change, and I have never noticed that the mob damage was any less after crippling. My guess is that the strength component has no effect on mob damage, or so little effect that it isn't noticeable.

For all practical purposes, cripple can help to mitigate damage in 2 ways. One, your group can kill the mob faster because they can hit it harder and more often, and two, the mob won't proc as much. The melee damage the mob produces doesn't seem to be affected by strength.

As for crippling high level mobs that don't proc, flurry, or rampage . . . I'd say probably not, unless it has a very high AC.

Just my 2 cents ;)
