Getan
As a player of both a high level shaman and high level cleric I would have to disagree with the statement that Shaman's are at the bottom of the heap. I beleive that all three Priest class are reasonably well balance. Each has its strengths and weakness.

If I were to rate the 3 priest classes I'd do it in the following way. Clerics with their Plate mail and Specialization in raw HP both interms of buffs and heals are on one end of the spectrum. Druids with their Leather armor, snares and ability to quad kite are on the other end. Shamans with their Chain mail are in the middle.

Clerics are almost totaly dependent on a group to get xp. Unless they are killing undead, or 56+, they have very few soloing options. Druids are hermits by nature. Most druid spend most of their time on their own quadding. Then, they complain they can never find a group. Shamans are once again in the middle, they are not totaly dependent on groups for xp but they can effectively solo most mobs.

When it comes to damage mitigation, again each class is very different. Clerics rely mostly on raw AC and HP to mitigate damage. They can also use stuns but in the long run that is a very inefficient way of of mitigating damage. Druid mitigate damage by not letting mobs get within range to hit them. Shamans reduce a mobs offensive capabilities to the point where almost anyone can tank it.

When it comes to buffs, things are sligtly different. Here, Clerics are on one end of the spectrum and Shamans are on the other with the Druid in the middle. At 60 Clerics become a one trick ponie as far as buffs go, they have Aego and thats it. To be honest they were always a one trick ponie in the buff department. Aego is essentialy, Heroic Bond, Symbols of marzin and Bulwark of Faith as one buff. Druids are stuck in the middle with inferior ac/hp buffs that don't stack with Cleric buffs and a Strength buff that sometimes stack with Shaman buffs. One reason Druids are passed over in groups is that people think Druids should buff and heal in group situations when in fact they are better off in a group dealing damage. Here, Shamans are without a doubt King. Not only can Shamans buff all the physical attributes but they have haste and regen spells too. Just ask melee classes in the know whether shaman buffs make a difference and whether they prefer to group with or without a shaman.

If you asked me, I'd say that the Shaman has the coolest spells. When I play my Shaman, I actualy look forward to trying out new spells when I level. With my Cleric I see very little reason to reach the next level because all I get is yet another AC/HP buff of dubious use and or yet another heal that is not as efficient as CHeal. Another big plus of playing my shaman is that I don't have to go on roleplay all the time to avoid all those nagging people asking for a rez.

As for saying that Shamans are not priest, each priest class has a real world equivalent. Clerics are similar to Clerics in the Christian, Islamic and Judaic faith. Druids are based on the Celtic Druids of England and other earth based religions. Shamans are based on Native American spiritual paths and those of many other Aboriginal cultures. To say shamans are not priest is like saying Native American and Aboriginal cultures have no legitimate faith.
