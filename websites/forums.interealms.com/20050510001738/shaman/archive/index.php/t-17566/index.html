Aselus
I don't think the exact formulas have ever been decoded, but in the end it boils down to ATK vs AC with respective levels thrown in. Raising your weapon skill raises your ATK in turn. A check of some sort is made against the target's AC based on your ATK, and your damage is calculated from several factors including weapon base damage, level modifier, 2h bonus, how much you beat the AC of the target by, etc. Other mitigation factors include things like dodge, block, parry, riposte, etc.

So in conclusion:
Max weapon skill = good
Higher ATK = good
low AC mobs = good, high AC mobs = bad

I think the Steel Warrior forums are your best bet for actual statistics.

Retron
Formula for max hit goes something like this: max damage=(strength+weapon skill)/100 * weapon listed damage + damage bonus.

As an aside, for priests (or shamans at least), assuming you have 200 Offense and 200 skill in wielding your weapon, the formula's something like:

4.18 * DMG

Based on a 74 DMG Dathor Great Hammer maxing at 310 and a 39 DMG Hammer of Spiritcalling maxing at 163.

The only fly in the ointment is that I've seen a couple of 165s from the Hammer of Spiritcalling, smashing hundreds of Kobolds in the Warrens with FA up. Using Rabid Bear didn't produce any 165s, nor anything above 310 with the Dathor hammer.
