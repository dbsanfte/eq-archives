Thanks very much for doing this parse. :)
I know this is a very small sampling, and that the dataset isn't perfect in the first place, but I noticed one or two things that were definitive enough for me to make a judgement for my own personal gear choices.

At 1288 AC you took just over 67k in damage in 21 minutes.
At 1002 AC you took just over 70k in damage in 21 minutes.

Okay, now I figure that's a fairly good damage average for A Stormrider. So now let's break it down to an average exp fight for a single group by using 10% of the above numbers...

At 1288 AC you would likely take 6.7k in damage over ~2.1 minutes.
At 1002 AC you would likely take 7k in damage over ~2.1 minutes.

So if an average fight against A Stormrider (melee version) had the shaman tanking the entire time, and the mob dies w/in 2.1 minutes, the difference between the 1002 AC & 1288 AC is going to average around 300points (plus or minus a few hits of course, which w/ their damage could be ~1k easily, so the "average" will suffer from greater deviation.) or so. (Obviously, I wouldn't be able to kill a Stormrider in that period of time on my own, but in a single group setting I think that's a fair time.)

Considering that it isn't likely that I personally will be tanking A Stormrider for an entire fight in most cases, this is significant to me. It shows me (and opinions will vary widely I'm sure) that if I invest in a huge amount of AC gear (to get to 1288+), I'm PROBABLY only going to see a 5% decrease in my total damage taken, an average of a few extra misses on me by the mob, and a 5-10% decrease in my damage taken per hit.

Since I'm NOT going to be a MA in most cases, and my tanking will be short duration (resulting in my death or the MA retaking the aggro), I don't see AC anywhere near as critical as I once did beyond 1k. When I am soloing and tanking "in teh face", I don't see bonuses given by a ~150 improvement in AC (since I'm around 1130-1150 buffed I think) important enough to sacrifice any HP over, since the difference is merely overcome by one tick of Torpor on an average fight.

I've been a proponent for HP>AC>Wis for some time now, but this definitely seals the deal for me. My AC is decent enough now (somewhere over 1k unbuffed, definitely not above 1100) as is that I don't need to worry about improving it unless the upgrade comes with a concomitant improvement in my total HP.

Thanks again for sharing!

Jharassik
is that the attack rating of the mob has a large impact on the ability of AC to mitigate.


Let me simplify that. For tank classes, the "softcap" is the mob's attack for anything after kunark. If mobX has an attack of 1300, the softcap for plate classes is just above that(1350 for example, it does seem to go up in hops of 50). If mobY has an attack of 1400, 1450ish would be the point of diminishing returns.

Do *NOT* try to figure priest or caster soft caps the same way. That'd just be silly, given the caps for defensive skills, etc.

When you take in the big picture of shaman ac softcaps, you have to understand that we won't be tanking THAT big of mobs(IE we're not gonna MT the AoW, even though I'm sure it could be done with the right shammy) for any length of time. In other words, there's no use trying to get 1400ac for say Statue, because the only time we're gonna be getting hit is if we have aggro from slow. Given that, and the fact that normal experience mobs have lower attack(thus lower softcaps), it makes more sense to choose better insurance against sporadic damage(hitpoints) than constant damage(ac).

That is, basically, why I believe a good target to work towards is 1k unbuffed ac and then go all out for hp.

--edit--
I forgot to add that another good reason for choosing HP>AC is AE damage, which occurs much more often these days than say a year or two ago.
