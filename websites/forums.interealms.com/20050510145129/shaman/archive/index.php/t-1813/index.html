Bigguss
Looks like you might actually come out ahead on this deal. :)

The differences in gear between a druid and shaman usually have to do with the fact that a shaman wants to balance WIS with AC and HP. As we progress through out career, shaman end up taking more and more hits, so AC/HP gear becomes increasingly important. Also, because HP can be converted into mana (through the cannibalize line of spells) HP also serve as a mana reserve.

For example, at 34 you'll find the pet is a godsend... but that it can't take a hit. You will need to tank for it if it's going to stay alive- and that means you'll need to be able to take a punch. Later on, when grouped you'll find that slow is a big taunt- and you'll often get pounded on in appreciation for your efforts.

How to balance WIS, AC, and HP has been the subject of much debate around here so I won't rehash (use the Search function if you want to read the debates) but everyone seems to agree that for a shaman, AC, HP, and WIS are all very very important. With that, here are my gear recommendations:

Ears: Sell both earrings (vendor will pay 250+ for forest loop, not sure what players are paying for hp/mana ring) and get a couple Imbued Golden Ivory Earrings (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=4930) (ac 3, +4 wis, +4 vs cold) - they're jewelcrafted and sell for 100 pp each or less.

Neck: The cheap alternative is a Ry'Gorr Chain Collar (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=3826) (ac 9, +10 mana, +4 sta, +4 vs magic) which sells for 100-150 pp. Next step up is a Ivory Imbued Collar (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1257) (ac 8, +5 wis, +10 mana, +10 vs poison and disease, +2 vs all else) - these tend to sell for 500-1000 pp. Of only the HP/MANA necklace had some AC to it.... :)

Face: The acrylia mask is a good one- probably no change needed here. Another popular choice is the Arctic Wyvern Mask (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=5289) which sells for surprisingly little (200-500 pp) since tailors make them to get their skills up.

Head: The JB helm is a good one- no need to change anything. I still wear one at 51.

Fingers: No need to change anything here- 6/65 rings are great.

Wrists: You might want to consider changing the runed mithril to something with a little more AC- for example Totemic (ac 9, +5 dex) for around 100 pp, Ry'gorr (ac 9, +5 hp, +2 str, +2 cha) for a little more, Sentry (ac 9, +2 wis/int, +15 hp/mana, +5 disease/poison, +2 magic, +3 dex) for a little more still. This isn't a critical change, though.

Note: Soon you might want to think about saving for The Bracer. The Jaundiced Bone Bracer (JBB) has a right-click 263 damage ice nuke, takes 8 seconds to cast, and has limitless charges. This is one of the most powerful items a shaman can have (along with the fungi tunic and the epic.) The nuke starts working at level 45, and the bracer generally costs around 10-15k (depending on server, etc.)

Arms: Get JB arms (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=2424). They're cheap (few hundred PP), provide great AC, and a a right-click STR buff once you hit 45.

Gloves: Get JB gloves (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1619) - again, cheap, great AC, a right-click buff (DEX this time). A more expensive choice- probably 2k or so- are Coldain skin gloves (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=4686) (ac 12, +6 wis/int, +20 hp.mana, +9 sta), or for a little more than that, CC gloves (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=3799) (ac 20, +35 mana, +10 str). Definitely upgrade this slot- as the gnome skin gloves stink for a shaman.

Shoulders: The
tattered mantle (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1063) (ac 12, +20 hp, +10 vs disease) is a nice upgrade for only 100-200 pp. More expensive alternatives include Chokidai hide spaulders (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=6812) (ac 6, +8 wis, +5 poison/disease/magic) for around 1k or the Elder wolf hide (ac 10, +4 wis/int/str, +30 hp) for around 1k.

Chest: Ry'gorr is a nice BP- no dire need to upgrade here. One alternative is the Treeweave (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=2045) (ac 25, +10 str) for around 1k.

Note: Just as mana-free damage (the Jaundiced Bone Bracer) is one of the most powerful things a shaman can have, mana free healing/regen is another. You might want to start saving for a regen BP- you'll find it really makes a huge difference in your overall power. An iksar regen BP (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=2025) will give you an additional 3000 HP per hour to convert into mana, or just use as free self-healing- price depends on server... around 5-6k on Karana. The fungi (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=11737) gives 9000 hp/hour, but costs a lot more.

Back: Oracle cloak is nice, probably no need to upgrade right away. The acrylia cloaks (player made) are coming down in price- might be worth it to just wait for the best one (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=8158) to get low enough and snag it. The next upgrade ac/hp wise is the seahorse cloak (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=4714) (probably around 3-4k).

Belt: Crystalline belt is good, no need to upgrade for now. Alternatives include a spider fur (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=3891) belt (couple hundred pp), flayed flayed turmoilskin (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=987) belt (around 1k), or smiters girdle (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=7489) (just under 1k)

Legs: This is a particularly tough spot to upgrade. Common choices are totemic (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1332) or ry'gorr (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=3893). The next upgrade is generally jarsath (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1350) (a hassle because they're NO DROP and drop pretty far into Karnor's Castle) or planar. Dragonleg (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/adv_search.html?stn=dragonleg) legs are starting to come down in price, but they're still a little pricey for you (around 5k for the cheaper ones.)

Boots: Okay, you need to get rid of the GEBs! :) Get some JB boots (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1809) for a few hundred pp... great AC, right-click AGI buff at 45, and no one will make fun of you for looking like a druid. :) You should also pocket some nice plat on the deal. If you can't bear to be without some wisdom in that slot, get some tribal war boots (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=6820) instead (nice combination of stats and ac, for around 1-1.5k.)

Your weapons and shield are great- no need to change anything. Your next weapon upgrade would be a REIF (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=3969) but that is a little pricey for now (4-5k probably) and wouldn't provide the power increase an iksar regen BP or the JBB would.

Hope this helps!

AvalanchW/Moogalak,

The one problem with the necklace is that it provides zero AC. Until the soft cap is reached, AC is pretty important. At 51 with 980 AC self-buffed, I still get the tar slapped out of me on dark blues if slow resists.

Well, the AC and the fact that +mana items are said to scale with level (I believe it was Abashi that first threw this out there when asked why dark mail gauntlets with +50 mana didn't double the mana pool of a ranger at level nine)... so while a +65 hp designation will always give 65 extra hp, a +65 mana item will give varying amount of actual mana dependant on level. I have never seen experiments on this, so I can only speak of it second-hand. If mana scales anything like the mana/wisdom ratio, the ivory collar (+5 wis, +10 mana) gives very similar total mana to the necklace. In any case, it will give very similar mana in a few levels even if the scaling did not exist.

So then it just becomes a tradeoff of 65 HP vs 8 AC, +10 disease/poison, +2 fire/magic/cold. Generally, I've seen the basis for comparison when thinking about items to be 1 AC = 10 HP. Throw the saves in and I think a good case can be made that the collar is a better investment in the short term and long term.

One more consideration- jewelry depreciates like crazy (even faster than other items generally do.) I remember when +65 HP/mana necklaces went for 10k, now they're 1k. Their value recently took another hit when the cap for int/wis was raised to
255.

I read the original question to be: "I have these druid items and this much money- how can I rearrange it to better equip my wife's shaman?" I'm definitely not saying GEBs or the necklace are crap (I'd've killed for them at 30- I vaguely remember kobold boots and a black iron medallion- ack !!) but in comparison to the 4k in cash those spots represent; the ivory collar, JB boots, and 2500 extra pp are (imho) a better value.

Not trying to offend anyone. :)
