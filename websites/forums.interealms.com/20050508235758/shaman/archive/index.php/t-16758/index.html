LDoN actually has quite a good storyline, if you read the story window stuff that was coming out occasionally. Although stymied in his efforts to escape from the Plane of Time, Zebuxoruk has been able to contact Calliav Giniuar because of Calliav's devotion to the demi-god. He has given Calliav what basically amounts to a vision of the apocalypse (crash of Luclin into Norrath to set the stage for EQ2), and has told Calliav to seek the Grozmok stone (part of the storyline of LoY).

To this end, Calliav sought out the Wayfarer's Brotherhood. Zebuxoruk told them of his visions of dungeons that had been lost for thousands of years. The Wayfarer's Brotherhood is well-versed in the history of Norrath, and they tell you of it, piece by piece, as you complete their adventures.

Basially, LDoN is tying together the PoP and LoY storylines. I imagine the next expansion will be a furthering of the arcing storyline that SoE has been building for the last year. If you think about it, PoP was really the first expansion to really have much of a storyline, as opposed to a backstory. They kinda dropped the ball with LoY, but LDoN is bringing the storyline back into play.

At any rate, I give EQ a lifetime of about 4 years +/- 1 year after the final expansion comes out. If they keep on expanding, it will never die, unless SoE decides to end it. Hell, it took them over 3 years just to reach a churn rate equal to the new account growth rate. EQ would still be profitable even with 100,000 players as opposed to the current >420,000.
