Mysti, find another game until you see shamans ecstatic that the fixes are really "done." There is no situation now where I wouldn't rather have a chanter or a beastlord in a group. I also play an elemental level SK, along with my elemental shaman, and have also extensively played a druid and monk, as well as dabbled with a warrior, so I'm not saying this just out of being a bummed-out shaman. It's really true.

There's not any point in being a class that can so easily be replaced by not just one but TWO others who can do essentially the same thing (slow and give reasonable buffs) plus add an awful lot more -- a better buff or two plus offtanking ability and much more damage for beastlords, or, for a chanter, much superior buffs, mez, better haste, KEI/VoQ, and even the possible use of a pet that so vastly outdamages your group that it renders both the rest of the group and the rest of the game as close to trivial as imaginable.

Either one of those classes kick our butts so thoroughly that at this point only the ignorant or the most loyal friends choose to include shamans in groups. And in raids--well, we don't really stack all that well, and there is a flood of shamans into the game going back to the days when a weak dragon was first soloed in Velious, so we're not in very great demand for raids either.

Very sorry to say all this, and it sounds so negative that it's natural to think that it couldn't possibly be true or must be somehow exaggerated or mistold in the telling, but...that's my honest summation of my experiences in the game and insights for now.

It may change later, but later is not now.

Give it I would guess at least six months, so some more shamans quit and burn out, and maybe Sony will actually feel inspired by competition to make all classes worthwhile again instead of just being incredibly lazy and stupid by making the game revolve around the holy trinity of cleric/chanter/warrior yet again.

When that happens, and there's a true place for shaman that you can really look forward to, think about coming back. But there's no hurry. There may be something for shaman to look forward to, but not now. Maybe that day will come, but there's no point in spending your money and wasting(perhaps vast amounts of) your time fooling around in the bungled mess they have right now. Wait for them to get it right, then come back.

If you want to listen to those who are at lower levels saying everything is hunky-dory, be sure you're at that level yourself and definitely have every intention of staying there for a long, long, long time. If you have moving up as a motivation, though, you won't be well-served by coming to a situation in which you can spend many hundreds of hours more just to come to a dead end.
