Megumi -- price caps are DIFFICULT, but not impossible to enforce. All it takes is the majority of ACTIVE shaman to agree to it, and suddenly every lucky schmuck that wins a Torpor drop and does a "<b>PC on torpor plz thx u</b>" gets back "5k," "5kish", "seen it go for 5k" and so on. Price agreements by Shaman do have a REAL effect on easily manipulable EQ server prices.

I have seen ONE person with three accounts push the price of certain farmed items up by 200% in a single day of auctioning. I have seen two dedicated (and greedy) guild bankers coordinate a system of alts to gouge the f'uck out of Shaman, Chanters and Clerics for their rare spells.

These markets are nowhere near transparent. Your economic theory stufff, while sound in a perfect market, doesn't hold up too well in these illiquid and opaque markets.

Unions exist in the real world and they DO have an impact on wages. Groups that band together are able to move numbers (whether they be wages paid to them or prices charged for raw materials). Shaman banding together in game can achieve a similar effect. Sure, there will always be "scabs" busting picket lines, but effective unions with energetic and talented organizers can have huge effects on numbers that would otherwise be controlled by raw supply and demand.
