Gardeltron
There are a couple of threads that deal with the question of just how effective shaman slows are versus those of other classes. There are a lot of numbers being thrown around, and some misconceptions. I thought a new thread was needed to clear up some of the confusion.

The most appropriate way to compare slow effectiveness is to compare the total amount of damage done by mobs slowed by different percentages. Comparisons of DPS are misleading because DPS is broken down as follows:

DPS = DPH (damage per hit) * HPS (hits per second)

Slow only effects HPS, not DPH. Total damage (X) is then

X = DPS * time

A mob slowed by 75% means that HPS with be 25% of its unslowed value. Thus, total damage with be 25% of X.

Now we are ready to compare slows. Relative effect of a slow on mob damage output is independent of X, and hence independent of DPS. To see this, let's compare enchanter slowed mob damage output with shaman mob damage output:

X = unslowed damage output
0.25 X = shaman slowed damage output
0.30 X = enchanter slowed damage output

Relative decrease in damage output of shaman slowed mob to enchanter slowed mob is then:

(0.30 X - 0.25 X) / 0.30 X = (0.30 - 0.25) / 0.30 = .167

So a mob slowed by a shaman will do 16.7% less damage than one slowed by an enchanter. This is close to the 20% figure attributed to Rytan at the FF.

By the same logic, a mob slowed by a shaman will do 28.5% less damage than one slowed by a beastlord.

These comparisons among classes remain unaffected by slow mitigation. Although total mob damage will increase with mitigation, the relative effectiveness of the class slows remain the same. *Edit: This statement is incorrect. See Murtak's post below*

The problem with slowing is not in the percentages, but in the nature of short, high intensity fights. These fights marginalize the need for slowing since the outcome of the fight is decided so quickly. As many have pointed out, any slow will do for a short fight.

Your equations are slightly different than mine. X is the total damage output in my equations, your X is really DPS. But using your idea, I came up with the following:

The general equation for damage is:

X = t1*DPS + slow proportion * DPS * (T - t1)

where t1 is casting delay and T is fight duration.

For shamans: t = 5 sec (Malos) + 3 sec (Turgur's) = 8
For enchanters: t1 = 1 sec (Tash) + 6 sec (Forlorn) = 7
For beastlords: t1 = 3.5 sec (Sha's Revenge)

Xs: total damage for shaman
Xe: total damage for enchanter
Xb: total damage for beastlord

Relative efficiencies are:

Xe/Xs for enchanter vs shaman
Xb/Xs for beastlord vs shaman

When relative efficiencies are equal to 1, the two classes are equivalent. If less than one, the class being compared is better than the shaman, when it's greater than 1 the shaman is better.

DPS factors out of the relative efficiency, but it changes with duration of the fight. Resists are not factored in here.

The bottom line is this:

Enchanter slow is better than shaman slow if fights last less than 22 seconds.
Beastlord slow is better than shaman slow if fights last less than 37 seconds!!

veeshanMurtak
These comparisons among classes remain unaffected by slow mitigation. Although total mob damage will increase with mitigation, the relative effectiveness of the class slows remain the same.
This is not true at all - apart from the weird formula being used to determine slow mitigation there is another problem.

Even when the percentage differences between slow percentages stay the same the DPS of the MOB does not necessarily do the same. Consider this:

Unmitigated an enchanter slows a MOB down to 30% - a shaman down to 25%.
The enchanter slowed MOB thus does 20% more damage then the shaman slowed one.

Mitigated by 50% an enchanter slows the MOB down to 65% - a shaman down to 62.5%.
The enchanter slowed MOB thus does 4% more damage then the shaman slowed one.

Why is this?
We compare MOB DPS but the figure that actually gets affected by slow mitigation is the slow percentage.
MOB DPS is original DPS * (1 - slow percentage)
Mitigated MOB DPS is original DPS * (1 - (slow percentage * slow mitigation))

The effect of slow mitigation on MOB DPS is thus
(original DPS * (1 - slow percentage)) / ( original DPS * (1 - (slow percentage * slow mitigation)))
or
(1 - slow percentage) / (1 - (slow percentage * slow mitigation))
This formula is not at all unchanging with slow mitigation.
