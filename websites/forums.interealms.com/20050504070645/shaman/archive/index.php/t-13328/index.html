Viscerator
A mob has to have lost a percentage of his life to start summoning. So this varies by monster as to the amount of damage needed to be dealt.

Once he is not at full health, he will start summoning ANYONE that is on his hate list in order. The person at the top of list gets eaten, then the 2nd, 3rd, 4th so on and so forth. Until his hate list is clear.

If the monster is not engaged and is still not at full health he will continue to summon anyone that gets on his hate list until he regens his health.

Any spell that does no damage or very small amounts of damage are safe to cast and the mob will never summon. This includes: debuffs, roots, dispells, slows, Shaman epic dot for the first few ticks.

(this assumes a summoning mob, not all have the ability and the level rules changed with Luclin)

Gwain Buffmonkey
Mobs that can summon do not do so unless they are not at full health. They start summoning at 97 or 98 or so percent of their full health.

Once they start summoning, they do so to the highest person on their agro list. The tank gets most of the summons, but it's fairly easy for casters to get summoned...overnuking wiz/druid/mages, overdotting necro/shaman, slow too early or too resisted, a tashing ench, etc. Clerics often get summoned if they heal too early or if the MT goes down (leaving them relatively high on the agro list). Don't over agro and you won't get summoned.

Oh, and mobs can't summon every time the person they're going after is out of reach. It has a refresh time of like 6 (I think?) seconds.
Jaruden
96% health is the start for summoning. Easy to tell back when luclin perma-rooted mobs (itraer vius is a good example) had a gazillion hp. We'd have enchanters chain tash to build up agro, then let a warrior go to town behind him to build up agro of his own. At 96%, if the warrior hadn't overtaken the enchanters agro, the enc got summoned (and owned).

Mobs cannot summon above that point, no matter how many stories you've heard from your grandmother's sisters boyfriend's cat's owners next door neighbor.

Once past the point, they attack the person highest on their agro list. If that person is not in melee range, they summon them. There is a small refresh timer on summons, so if you're near a zone and get summoned, sometimes you can run to the zone before it resets and you get summoned again. Mobs will chase the person with highest agro during this downtime.
