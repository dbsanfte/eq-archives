Shabura Naarteban
Unlike our nuke, and to an extent, our rain, the problem with our dots is not the magnitude or efficiency per se (except to the extent that the entire dot system is flawed and need improvements across the board).

The significant number is the resist modifier. Druid: neg 30 or neg 100. Necro: neg 50. RANGER: neg 100. Shaman: zero modifier - always.

And therein lies the problem. We dot bigger, and better, on the whole, than other classes - when those dots land. But in every possible circumstance, our dots land less, and that effect is a large hit on our dots in terms of both time and mana efficiency. I presume that was set up "because shaman get maloxxx", but denying us the front-loaded resist modifier on the basis of a 5 second cast time/400 mana spell alters the efficiency of our dots to such a degree as to make them substantially poorer choices than other classes.
