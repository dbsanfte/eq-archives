...to save cash :

The level 65 spells are a carbon copy of the level 60 spells. If you're leveling fast enough, just skip paying the cash for malo/torpor in favor of malos/quiessence. If it takes you a while to level, it's probably best to purchase these spells for your trip from 60-65.

Likewise, don't buy Avatar. Put the money toward FA. Don't bother buying the disease dots if you're 100% group xp'ing. But learn to love the poison dots. Check out the medicine cabinet for essential information about skills, spells, and AA routes.

The level 1 (or whatever) root spell is great all the way up to the endgame. It's supercheap on mana (practically free) and it's your only source of crowd control. Become a root artist and you will save the group more often than not on heavy pulls.

At level 40 start thinking about doing the Stoicism quest. It's pretty easy and Dulak is good xp for you up to 50 solo. Stocism is the first of our Heal-over-Time spells with excellent ratio.

I have a 40 shaman and want to know how that differs from playing a 65 shaman.

At 40, you have some of the tools that you will have at 65, but by no means all. Let's go over the differences quickly:

1. Slowing. As I recall, you have a 51% magic based slow at level 40 and no disease based slow. Your magic based slow will max out at 75% at level 60. Also, you don't have unresistable debuffs until level 60. That's the bad news.

The good news is that mobs don't hit all that hard at level 40 and have lowish resists, so your tools should be adequate for now and will scale up nicely with monsters' abilities as you level up. Your slow will be your most important tool at 65 and your signature spell -- when it is not mitigated, that is, but that's a different story.

2. Healing. Healing is one of your weaker abilities at 40. A level 40 Cleric has his 7.5K HP Complete Heal and you have your, er, 300HP Greater Healing. A bit unbalancing. On the other hand, you have Chloroplast and it's a powerful spell at that level if you let it do its job right (hint: don't heal to 100% during the downtime).

The good news is that you will get much better at healing at 58+. Our 10 second mini-CH is nothing to write home about, but it wil be situationally useful for a few levels and then you will get Tnarg's and eventually Quiescence, which will make you a passable healer up to and including PoP. If you go to GoD (where you wil get spell upgrades as well), things may be different, but that's yet another story.

3. Mana regeneration. Canni 2 is a decent mana regeneration tool (get used to hitting it over and over and over), but Canni4 will be much better and Canni5 is a godsend. The difference in mana regeneration between a level 40 Shaman and a level 65 one is huge.

BTW, by the time you get Canni5, you will want to make sure that you don't go under 1.5-2 orange bubbles of health when you take 1924HP damage from it, preferably self-buffed. Might as well start working on HP (and to some extent AC) items now. With today's Bazaar gear, there is no reason to drop below around 200 WIS (which is where the WIS/mana ratio gets poor anyway), but otherwise it's HP, HP and HP. Well, and STA if it's not maxed :)

4. Root parking. Functionally, a level 40 Shaman and a level 65 one do the same thing when they are on root parking duty - hit Root and back off. The difference is that XP mobs tend to have higher resists at level 65, but then your Root will have a built-in resist adjustment by then, so it's a wash. On the plus side, you will have Virulent Paralysis, a nifty Disease based AA Root, which is hard to resist. You can use it for crowd control or for root dotting - quite useful either way.

For now, just learn to root park and you will be fine.

5. Buffing. Level 65 buffs are obviously better and last longer than level 40 ones, although some stat buffs become irrelevant as people approach 305 [note: things are a bit different in OoW]. With appropriate AAs, Shamanic buffs last a long time except Ferine Avatar, but that's life.

6. DOTs. Bane and your post-60 DOTs do not have a damage component, hence they don't break Root. Makes root-dotting much safer, especially when you get VP3.

Finally, to address the issue of which school of magic you will want to have maxed first. Alteration is the most important one (slows, heals), but it will be maxed in no time because of Canni. Conjuration (DOTs) is nice to have maxed for DOTs, but it's not a game breaker. Evocation (nukes, AE rains) can be situationally useful in groups, but it's frosting on the cake, really. Abjuration is limited to the rarely used AC buffs (do not stack with Virtue), self-only AC/stats buffs (shortish duration) and resists, which are all situational. Divination is mostly Invisibility, so it tends to lag behind.

GL!

Functional differences between a level 40 and a level 65 shaman are mainly the following:
- Slow will become more powerful.
- Haste will become more powerful.
- Root will become so cheap that the only cost in rootparking is time and health lost, not the mana for root.
- Your heals will loose power compared to cleric heals. HoTs partially make up for this.
- Regen will decline in usefulness.
- Your ability to tank will decline but the increase in slow will make up for this for quite a while.
- Your damage, especially your melee damage will make less and less of a difference to a group.
- AAs can make additional differences but this differs wildly depending on how many you get at what levels.

Thus your role shifts from doing everything to a support / healer / debuffer role. It is not all that much of a change really and rather subtle - the main difference you will notice is the damage part and the power of an unmitigated 75% slow.

In between you will also have a Stoicism/Torpor area where you will alternatively yell "YAY I HAVE ENDLESS MANA" and "I WANT TO RUN AGAIN". That does not change your role in groups though, except for being able to berate tanks that accepting the slow and snare is all for the best of the group.

Basically unless you have been tanking and DoTing in groups a lot your role will not change much. And unless you have been one of the wisdom/mana shaman you will not have a need to develop new skills at all, merely nuances in old skills, such as learning to rootpark while snared. When you start getting some AAs is when the real learning begins, such as when to use canna 5, how to use Virulent Paralysis to kill annoying group members and how to use Spirit Call to supress summoning.

Changes in the spell lineup will also be rather small. For me its usually Cannibalize, Heal over time, Heal, Malos, Malosinia, Slow, Random slots (used for damage spells or buffs usually, Root would be in here if I did not have a root ring).

I suspect for you it is Cannibalize, Heal, Malosi, Slow, Root, Damage Spells / Buffs? If so that lineup will not change much other then adding over time heals and of course switching them out for newer and better spells.

I have a 40 shaman and want to know how that differs from playing a 65 shaman. I want to make sure that I have the right skills built up for when I get there.

If you want the right skills to play a good shaman, I suggest you solo all the way to your mid 50's. I'm not a big believer in telling a young player what spells he or she needs to be using or how to use them. I don't think people learn as well when they're spoonfed things that they don't yet understand. Experience is always the best way, and there is no better experience than being tossed headfirst into the pool, so to speak.

So, I'll just suggest a progression for you.

In your early to mid 40's, head to the Luggald caves in Dulak's harbor and try solo'ing all the way to the back of the cave. At the higher levels, mobs will resist slow a lot and you'll be dealing with high agro and lots of mobs. These mobs don't yield a lot of experience, but you'll certainly learn something about control, agro ranges, and efficiency.

At 44, find the elementals in Nadox. They're terrific experience (the best experience from 44 to 49 actually), but more importantly, if you're running the cycle of mobs quickly to maximize your experience, you'll find yourself 60 life 60 mana after having finished killing the last mob in the cycle and the named instantly spawns from the corpse of the elemental you just killed. Are you a fight or flight shaman?

From 49 to 57, you'll never find faster experience or money than when you solo the nobles in Highkeep hold. Learning how to be efficient yet effective will be key. Can you deal with 3 green guards interrupting your spells while you try to lock down 2 angry nobles? When puppy bites the dust after finishing off the first noble, and you're 50 life 50 mana, will you dare to stay and test your mettle? By the time you've left this camp, if root hasn't become your best friend, I'll eat a shoe.

But not only will you'll be dealing with lots of mobs and a fast cycle of mobs in HHK, you'll also be dealing with moronic people trying to bum buffs from you or steal your camp. So, will you give up your camp, or will you flex those burly muscles and show that pesky druid he can't killsteal your mobs? (hint: chain nuke till you've done over 50% damage - they can't keep up with the mana reqs.) And when you log on and somebody else is sitting in that middle room, looking smug, can you negotiate with the guy to get part of the camp or will you just create more problems later on? People problems are a large part of the highend too.


Once you get the basics down, there's not a whole lot that's different. Just stick with it, don't rush through it, and enjoy the game for what it is - a game.
