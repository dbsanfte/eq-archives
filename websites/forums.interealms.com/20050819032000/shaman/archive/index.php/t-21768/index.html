Just curious, but why can't SOE just make a character in game already and post it on Yantis's site? They get the money from the sale, but Yantis gets his commision too. Taking Yantis out of the picture just means that SOE gets all of the profit.

Exactly, and I think that is what people fail to see. Yantis can only control how much he "farms" for the said items, and he can only "control" the sale of droppable items. SOE already controls the supply and demand of the very items that Yantis profits from. Putting the power of selling those items to someone who controls the flow is creating a nasty monopoly. People assume that bestowing this power on the game's creator will be beneficial because it would be controlled by the "good guys."

"Power corrupts and Absolute power corrupts absolutely"

It would be in SOE's best interest to not only create "bottlenecks" in game content, but also create "bottlenecks" in item/plat/level acquisition because it would profit off of both. This idea hurts the casual player more than anyone because large amounts of plat and high level items will be out of reach of them by a larger majority than people with larger playtimes and resources (guilds) to get the items in question. SOE would be profiting out a player's "inability to play"- something that has already been argued in multiple threads already.

Players assume that an item in question like "Time's Antithesis" would be "only 20 bucks", and here is what they are not thinking. An item such as this has a high demand (low drop rate), and a small supply (need to be in a Time guild). Therefore, due to this many players would purchase the item due to "well...I will never see the content, so why not?" You set a dangerous precident by doing this, what was a rare item is now common and, thereby, accepted as a standard upgrade in progression. You still have a high demand (it becomes standard), but a restricted supply (still need a Time guild to get one) so people are forced to shell out $20 bucks to "keep up with the Jones's". Multiply this a few times, and EQ quickly becomes a game of "how much is in my wallet".

My answer, obviously, is a resounding "no". I would not ever buy this item. If it comes to a point where it is almost a requirement to buy an item such as a "TA" then I take my money elsewhere.
