A warrior's main attribute is tanking with higher AC and high HP. Since you are duoing, you will be mostly fighting blues and the higher AC is not as helpful (monks tank blues very well). The high HP is not helpful either since shaman can't Complete Heal. Throw in FD for pulling/splitting, 25% heal every 6 minutes (mend), 20% more damage output and now you start to understand why a monk is a better duo than a warrior for a shaman.

As others have mentioned, monks may not be the best duo overall, but monks are excellent partners and definately better duo for shaman over a warrior.

Uggok
Monk is a vastly superior damage dealer. The fact that you say the duo will be untwinked seals the deal. The shaman's biggest weakness solo is damage dealing, and the monk compliments that perfectly. With slows, tanking is not nearly as important and monks are roughly equivalent to warriors in tanking in non-/defensive situtions anyway.

The monk also brings utility to the duo (mend saves a significant amount of mana over the long haul and fd iis very useful both to break spawns and as an escape when things go wrong) The only thing a warrior brings to this duo is a ton of hit points and a taunt button, and max hit points are irrelevant to xping if you do not have someone casting complete heal (which is why clerics make good duo partners with warriors) and taunt is not a problem with monk damage output and root.

In short, the shaman is an almost brokenly overpowered defensive soloist with some offense, and the monk is an almost brokenly overpowered offensive soloist with good defense. Together, wowza.

Necros, as mentioned, are a nice choice as are rogues (albiet higher maintenance). Rangers are also quite good. Basically, look for damage. SKs and Pallies (particularly at high levels) bring some nice tools but, like warriors, do significantly less damage than rogues, monks and rangers. This is not to say that a duo with a warrior (or even a non-necro caster) won't work, of course.
