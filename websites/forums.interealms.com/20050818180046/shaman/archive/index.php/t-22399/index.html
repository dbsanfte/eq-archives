Just a quick note. A lot of peoples problems with ati cards are due to the fact they are upgrading from nvida to ati. Nvidia drivers (and ati drivers) leave a bunch of crap behind on the system after they are uninstalled. This extra crap causes some huge driver issues when installing a new ati card after using a nvidia card. If you do decide to switch from nivida to ati make sure you download a cleaner to completly remove the nivida drivers.

I'm an ati fan simply because I think AA looks better on ati cards then nvidia cards. I've personally only ever had one problem installing an ati card. And that was on a nvidia nforce2 chipset. jdthomas
In addition to the whole 'shader day' issue, there was a lot of posting at the time of the rollout about problems with the graphics. In large part the complaints were by nvidia owners, with ati owners being a touch happier.
I haven't got any bone to pick with either graphic card, my house comps have a gf2, gf3, and radeon 9800pro. Last generation Nvidia card simply did not support dx9 in hardware. Functionality was implemented in software, working around the hardware limitations.
The whole thing about half life2 was a little unfortunate. Until that point, developers had used Nvidias' optimised pathways in their engines. Nvidia controlled the market, so it always made sense to make the software as fast as possible with that hardware. Hl2 was being developed along strict dx9 guidlines, and Nvidia suffered horribly. No one really knew that it would be that big of a problem before, since there were no big name developers working along strict dx9 functionality.
That lack of hardware functionality affected eq as well, some of which has since been improved.
