either way, proactive and positive seems to be the way to go.

what i'm noticing a lot among those with complaints about their classes are that their suggestions often encroach upon the turf that other classes hold even more dear. an example of this being the current frustration of SK and pally players with not being able to tank certain mobs in end content. seems to me that being the only class able to tank some of those very high end mobs is just about all warriors have going for them. sure, their dps is better than sk or pally, but sk seem to be overlooking the fact that on those huge raid mobs, they are still desirable for their ability to HT it to help bring it down, and they can contribute some minor debuffs that stack with the ones shammy and enc will be throwing at those mobs. pally, of course, can heal, and offer Brell's to the buff pool, etc. all warriors have is dps and sheer meatwall ability. additionally, outside of the MT and a couple more warriors to round out the tank line, it's not like every tank you take has to be a warrior--having pally and sk along where the meleers are being bombared with aoe dmg helps a lot--especially when you take into consideration those other offerings.

similarly, while we would ALL love more healing ability, healing and rezzing are all clerics have going for them, and being second to clerics in healing is all that really gets druids spots on a raid. their nukes and such are ok, but they're not so dazzling that they're going to get looked at over a mage if they aren't also the 2nd best healers. as far as i'm concerned, let them remain the 2nd best healers, and even kick them a little extra nuke ability. what i'd really love to see for us is more impact in short term fights through consequential melee buffs and maybe an extension on the Cripple line that makes them very desirable for those fights, as well.

ultimately, the shaman should remain the best melee buffer/debuffer, and if slow is going to be taken out of the equation in end content, so be it--there are other ways to debuff that will help us make a big difference. how about a very big combination Malos/Cripple with a 2-3 sec cast time? make Tash and our Malos versions stack regardless of which one sticks first so that enchy and shammy both benefit, with Tash at end game level debuffing by 150 or something, and malos debuffing MR for about 100 with a built in 100 point ac debuff and 10% decrease to the mob's melee output.

just some ideas, trying to think of things to do that won't cause a loss of role or identity for any other class, yet still making the shammy desirable for end game content.
