deria
Well!

I have to say that I am very pleased with the recent changes. It really looks like SOE took a look at what we wanted, thought about a good way to implement it, and then followed through.

Returning Disinefecting Aura to its original function: Good!

Adding Blood Of Nadox: Good!

Adding priest researh: Good! (sort of -- would have been nice if it wasnt in the hands of INT casters, but good enough)

Changing STR/DEX: Good! I got my first ever spectral parchment yesterday and would have felt terrible turning it in to get Aim Of The Gindan or Talisman Of The Gindan. It had to help that people were saying "take it out and dont replace it with anything, just get rid of it". :)

Fixing Doors In Freeport: REALLY GOOD! Er, wait, no, not really. NOT BAD though.

New Alchemy: Good!

New folding alchemy potion bag type things: Good! I really, really, really hated carrying around two bearskin potion bags and a medice bag for 45 levels.

In general, there are really only two things that I think the shaman community is hurting over now:

1) DoT agro / usefullness

This is somewhat subjective. DoTs have a place in their current form, it is just unfortunate that that place isnt in groups. A problem, I think, but I can live with it. Its certainly not the end of the world, and SOE has done a little to alleviate it with the much less fizzle-tastic rain and spear of torment (a much faster DD than we ever had before) and Velium Strike (a crit for 2K is a crit for 2K).

So I can live with DoTs.

2) Focus Items

We're sort of out in the cold on some focus items. You can get a few good ones as a shaman (like a wicked bone atlatl or a ceramic rod of storms) but the ones that affect our DoTs are so weak that its not really even worth the effort of getting them.

Again, a problem, but there ARE some good ones so I can live with the focus item situation too.

In general, I think we gained alot in this patch and I also think that we should be truly happy with the changes and the focus on our needs.
