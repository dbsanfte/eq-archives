I'm working on some Graphs for Direct Damage, I might get around to doing some for DoT spells. Here's the first one: Nuke Efficiency.

I simply took the most efficient nuke (not counting bolts, rains, or AE spells) from each Era, and plotted 'em using my elite copy of Excel '97. I plan on making a chart for rains as well.

I'll also follow this up with one for Nuke DPS, since being efficient isn't always as good as "Making **** Dead"

For clarification, here's the spells I used:

Shaman (Cold): Blizzard Blast, Ice Strike, Velium Strike
Shaman (Poison): Blast of Poison, Spear of Torment
Wizard: Ice Comet, Sunstrike, Ice Spear of Solist, Ancient, Destruction of Ice, Strike of Solusek. I probably should have changed Luclin to Garrison's, which has a DPM of 3.88.
Magician: Shock of Swords, Shock of Steel, Ancient: Shock of Sun, Sun Vortex
Cleric: Retribution, Reckoning, Judgment, Condemnation
Druid: Starfire, Wildfire, Moonfire, Ancient: Starfire of Ro, Winter's Frost
Enchanter: Discordant Mind, Dementia, Dementing Visions, Ancient: Chaotic Visions, Insanity
Necro Nukes: Ignite Bones, Chill Bones, Ancient: Lifebane, Neurotoxin
Necro taps: Drain Soul, Touch of Night, Touch of Mujaki
SK Taps: Life Leech, Drain Soul, Touch of Innoruuk
SK Spear: Spear of Plague

Ancients seem unreasonably high, but a lot of them are more efficient than POP equivalents, though less DPS. Ancient: Destruction of Ice remains the most efficient nuke in the game. The necro ancient nuke (Lifebane) is actually more efficient than velium strike, although it's 250 less damage than neurotoxin. In most cases though, the focus obtained on ornate/elemental gloves will counter the efficiency margin that ancient nukes have.

Conclusions:

Shamans are below Druids and wizards/magicians efficiency-wise by quite a longshot, and below enchanters much more than expected. The Necro/Cleric nukes are actually less efficient than velium strike, but have lower cast times and do more damage. Efficiency isn't the problem for us really. I'd even advocate a bit of tuning to cleric/necromancer nuke ratios if they tweak the damage/cast time on Velium Strike. Our poison nukes are a joke, but handy due to the low cast time, and thus we're stuck using them (or rain spells)on raids.

Rains and Bolts:

Both these spell lines are innately given boosts to efficiency due to limitations. Rains are penalized by having an innate 20% resist rate, and bolts gain efficiency due to Line of Sight issues.

Spells used:
Shaman: Gale of Poison, Torrent of Poison, Tears of Saryrn
Wizard: Energy Storm (Wizards didn't get good rains pre kunark), Tears of Prexus, Tears of Marr
Druid: Avalanche, Blizzard, Winter's Storm
Magician: Rain of Swords, Manastorm, Maelstrom of Electricity, Maelstrom of Thunder. Maelstrom of Electricity sucks, I have no clue why it's a higher level spell. What were the luclin people thinking?
Magician Bolts: Lava Bolt, Seeking Flame of Seukor, Firebolt of Tallon

Conclusions:

Since rains have an innate 20% resist rate, it removes the advantage in efficiency that they had over straight DDs. In order to make these spells useful for all classes, the resist penalty should be removed and the damage scaled down apropriately. This would make rain spells superior in grouping situations, but still limited for raiding purposes, since crowd control is an issue.

Also, druids gained a huge boost in this area in efficiency as well as DPS (See Below, Later). In addition, Storm's Fury has a -50 resist mod, which doesn't matter as much when you consider the above, but every bit helps. Tears of Saryrn could use a bit of a boost.
