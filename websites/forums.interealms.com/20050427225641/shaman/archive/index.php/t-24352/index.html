I don't know about you guys, but I am pumped up. Raiding was the best design element of Everquest. Period. Without a shadow of a doubt. Raiding brings together the powergamer aspect of all MMORPG's, and then pits it against brutal strategy, reflexes, intelligence and awareness.

However I use the word "design" element intentionally. Everquest had the right concept when introducing raiding content, but the execution was terrible.

Now, the WoW team realizes the difference between hard-linked (hard stop gap) raid content, soft-linked (natural stop gap) raid content, and non-linked raid content (read the article to understand). Furthermore the WoW engine and server comes prepared to handle instanced zones and dungeons, and the team understands fully well how it's important to have a good balance of instanced zones as well as zones that demand competition. I LOVE to see the team instantly understanding that lower level and hard-linked content needs to be instanced, while natural-linked is better for non-instanced.

I can't wait.


You will now be able to set a threshold for loot. Let's say I set it to blue items. Everything else is going to go round robin or free for all like normal, depending on how you have it set. When a blue item appears, everybody gets a roll or pass option. You have to click it right away, and if you roll and win, you get the item. There is also a master looter version, where the raid leader gets to assign blue items or higher. We think most groups will set a threshold of green or blue and use normal loot options for the rest of the loot. We think this will make looting more fair in raids, since the loot there will be so much better.
