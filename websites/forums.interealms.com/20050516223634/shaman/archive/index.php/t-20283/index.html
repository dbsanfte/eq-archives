There's a lot of incorrect information about both sides here, mostly about the mana regen capabilities of a Shaman. When discussing Shaman mana regen, keep it in the realms of a fairly challenging or fast paced xp group and with a competant Shaman. For example, some things to consider:

-all the relevant cast times and total time spent for the active mana regen
-low health danger (from canni5)
-the number of spells that a Shaman casts on each mob
-the risk of a resisted spell
-the upkeep of buffs and utility spells

In a good xp group as a 65 Shaman with regular mod rods, ft1, mc3, canni5, Q, c3, and either bard or beastlord mana regen I can get maybe a nuke in every other mob. This scenario is fighting somewhere that Q can keep the players alive mostly. I spend 100% of my time casting a spell.

I am just trying to clarify that Shaman mana regen is very high, but well balanced by our very high mana use, ineffecient and slow casting spells, as well as forcing us to choose whether to regen mana or to cast other spells.

What I would also like to present to Clerics here is regarding your usefulness outside of GoD, LDoN hard, and Elemental+ zones. The feelings here in this thread seem to be that with a larger and / or faster Shaman direct heal that will make clerics fully antiquated for trivial content such as LDoN normal and PoP tier3 or lower zones. Increasing the burst healing of a Shaman so that, if needed to, a fully focused and AA’ed chain cast Shaman heal would keep a plate class tank alive against unslowed trash mobs does not make Clerics obsolete.

Considering that this is a balance issue it would be expected that if SoE were to improve the desirability of Shamans for GoD, LDoN hard, and Elemental+ content that they should also improve the desirability of Clerics everywhere else (hammer procs that scale in damage when fighting lower level mobs for example).
