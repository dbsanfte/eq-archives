It's worth mentioning that there was a change in the healing vision during the caster balancing that occurred late in the Luclin era. The concerns specifically was the dependence on clerics and CH, and the lack of a significant role for druids in raids. The level 58 75% CHs (Kragg and the druid equivalent) were introduced in the Luclin era to increase the healing power accordingly. At the time most toons had much lower hp than what they do now, and the druid version was for practical purposes equivalent clerics CH (allthough not at the very high end high hp characters). The shaman version was not nearly as good though, and was really only a "CH" for casters. The spell was widely regarded as useless by shaman at the time who had torpor and was primarily used as a poor mans torpor. The PoP spells continued this paradigm shift with the fast heals and an upgraded version of the 75% CH. The shaman version of the level 64 75% CH was initially on test, but was dropped shortly after before going live.

I'm not sure why the shaman versions of all these spells healed for less than druid equivalents, but I doubt slow had much to do with it. Most groups at that time would have a slower of some sort. It more likely had something to do with the mana efficiency of using cannibilize to recover mana. Since canni converts hp to mana, the faster you can replace that hp, the faster you can replace lost mana. At the time of the caster balancing PoP was in late stages of development, and devs would certainly have been aware of the impact of slow mitigation. If there really is a case for the argument that shaman heals have fallen behind, it needs to be made in that context of mana generation, not slows.

One other thing, comparing BL buffs with shaman buffs isnt really valid since many of the BL ones are overwritten by shaman or paladin buffs. Those that arn't overwritten stack happily with shaman buffs, so there isnt a conflict between the two classes in that regard (where there is, BLs clearly have inferior alternatives). Also, IoS is also a shaman spell, not just a BL one. The only people who use that one are clerics anyway. Both classes can haste, but shaman get better hastes, both in magnitude (if worn haste doesn't bring it to the cap) and duration (tali).

On the curing front, disinfecting aura is as effective as crusaders touch for poison/diseases counters, and both classes get access to RC/RGC. Only shaman classes (shaman and BLs) get abolish poison, so the statement that pallies are better single target curers than us is incorrect. We also get blood of nadox, which is a very usefull spell when dealing with poison/disease based traps and AEs. In at least one mob it is the difference between a tough encounter and a trivial one.

Let's stop and think about the things (slow, canni, dots, healing) that made such a great impact back in "the day" and what they've become for today's high end shaman. Maybe this will help people understand why we "whine".

Slow - Mitigation. While a very good thing for the game, it greatly diminishes the most valuable aspect of a shaman's existence in both raid and group settings. Also, when you give this same ability (offset by 5-10%) to 3-4 other classes, the "shaman impact" only further decreases.

Cannibalize - Mana regen from Canni is pretty sweet. But hello, "Active" regen! We are the only mana class that has to cast a spell every tick to activate this ability. Also, the end game is all about quickness and responsiveness. Cannibalize doesn't fit into that scenario at all because you can't do anything (can't even move) when you're cannibalizing. Effectively you take yourself out of the game for six seconds every time you cannibalize. It's even worse than meditating -- you can at least socialize or AFK while you med. And of course, you can't meditate and cannibalize at the same time (without canni-dancing, which will make you want to poke your eye out).

DoTs - A great source of damage, the potential for DPS is pretty good with DoTs. Unfortunately, mobs die too quick at this level for DoTs to be effective, due to the amazing DPS of the many other classes. Not to mention, if we're maximizing DPS with DoTs, we're not doing much else, AND we're tanking (ask Dregen about my aggro). On raids, more than 2 DoTs and we risk compromising the MT, especially if we have debuff aggro too. And finally, on raids with 4 necros and 5 shaman, the mob bounces our dots pretty quick.

Healing - With the introduction of mitigation, our healing now has only slightly more impact than that of ranger heals prior to PoP (I'll let you do your own math). Yes, we can heal, and yes our healing does help. But in my opinion, it doesn't qualify us as a priest class anymore. Quies is fantastic for it's efficiency, but useless on mobs that triple for 700. And it's single target, making it ineffective on raids.

Buffs - Maccuul made better comments about this already. To point out the gross misperception of shaman buffing, a cleric in my guild seemed to think people would prefer shaman buffs to cleric and druid buffs.

In summation, every class-defining ability we have has been rendered mediocre at best due to increases in game content. I am not upset about other classes improving; I am merely frustrated that we have been ditched as an impact class.

The people who really want shaman in their group are either 1) unaware of our actual impact (we still live off our Kunark reputation), or 2) doing content like BoT, CoD, and normal LDoN. Otherwise beastlord, chanter and bard are all preferable. Look at it this way: these classes are preferable because overall, their value is greater to a group than our "class-defining" roles would provide. It isn't that we suck.

This stuff has been repeated over and over and over. While part of me wants to say "get over it" to both sides, like others in this thread, I would rather keep doing whatever it takes to get Sony's attention.
