Well, I do not play on a pvp server, but I have done a lot of dueling. About 95% of winning a duel is knowing what you need to do to kill the other guy, and to counter his attempts at killing you. The other 5% is carrying out your actions. It really isn't difficult, you just have to know what to do. Heal here, run there, dot him now, dispell yourself soon, etc... All you really have to do is make sure you're targetting the right person and casting the right spell. It is a bit more difficult when you throw in some extra players or even a 3rd team (I have played a bit of daoc, so I am not completely unfamiliar with pvp), but still all it really takes is practice.

I also used to play counterstrike competatively (Former CAL-I, competed at 4 CPLs, if that means anything to you.), and I have to say that the hand-eye coordination required in EQ is laughable compared to what you need for CS. There are really only 2 things I can think of that I have done that have required any real ability to navigate in a 3d environment. One of them is jousting, or just circlestrafing your opponent so that he is always in front of you and you're moving in circles around him. I've actually beat true melees before in straight melee duels because they cannot move around as well as I can. The second thing is fighting rangers, where you run alongside him in that sweetspot where he can't quite reach you with his swords, but you're just slightly too close for him to use his bow. It took me about 20 seconds to learn the sweetspot against rangers, and I have known how to circle-strafe for almost a decade.
