Trena
Hate to be the contrary one in this thread but unfortunately Merrygrin, all things considered, your guild (like many others btw) is probably the odd one out. Though Papabear is right, there are a lot of encounters you can do now that would be very challenging. The problem is, those same encounters can be done by people who a) play more and/or b) are better equipped (and thus, the encounters pose little to no challenge). Thus, they're rarely alive for more than a few hrs after spawning.

The game is fairly long in the tooth so there aren't as many guilds progressing through expansions as there once was. A lot of folks are levelling up much faster and choosing to join big guilds that are leaps and bounds beyond their experience level (i.e. a person can level to 60 or even 65 and join a Luclin+ guild without ever doing a single Kunark or Velious level raid). Thus, the steps of progression that many of the oldtimers remember are no longer required. The smaller/more casual guilds who have always progressed slower than their larger counterparts often find there isn't much of a pool to recruit from unless they drastically lower their level requirements. But even that causes its share of problems (like, how would you like to be a low/mid 50s member of a guild that's mainly 60+?). Of course, this is just the opinion I inferred from what I've seen.

NToV can be done by as few as 2 groups (maybe not in its entirety but I really doubt many small guilds have the consecutive hours available to worry about that) and doesn't require elemental level gear to do. Never underestimate how much 5 levels PLUS 2 expansions worth of AAs can trivialize previous "uber" encounters. As Kaison mentioned, the Crusaders outside Yelinak's chamber would be very challenging as well. A GOOD (by skill, not necessarily gear) 2-3 groups can kill Yelinak. With elemental gear, you can single group him. Jharassik, you'd be surprised at what folks can do when they TRY. There was an Aussie guild on Tholuxe (Wreckers) that used to pride itself on what it could kill with minimal numbers. Telkorenar with 18, pre-PoP with no NToV/ST gear? I wonder if Silkenshammie (the leader of Wreckers during that period) still comes by here :)

In my view, many guilds like Merrygrin's and Genine's don't want loot pinatas. They just want encounters that will challenge them without requiring zerg tactics or more members than they have. What I would suggest to them is perhaps try allying with similar guilds so in the event you do find a challenging mob available, you can try it out. Honestly, with EQ being as old as it is with as many people entering the high end (in level if not content progression), the people feeling the pinch more now seem to be the smaller guilds. Don't know if there's anything that can be done to remedy that.

Geal Icehammer
Ok as I see it you have two problems. One, you don't really have any formalized raid time for your guild. Two, your having trouble finding mobs when you do have a raid force on.

Sit down with your guild and pick some time when most can attend for a raid time. As a small guild you have to try and focus your strength by getting everyone on. Big guilds can get away with calling impromtu raids with who ever is on hand. Having a raid force in hand makes things alot easier. Also, having a set raid time is alot more important with LDoN, because you shouldn't have your people spread out doing adventures forcing 90 minute delays.

Ok, your raid force is on now to find something to kill. This isn't as hard as you make it out to be. You may not find the mob you really want up, but there is always something. Sit down and make list of raids with-in and just beyond your guilds ability. On raid night ( or day ), send out the trackers and go down that list. Learn the spawn time of the mobs on those lists. Vindi spawns every 8 hours, so if your raid day is Saturday (or Sunday) you may find that he isn't up in the morning but he will be up in the evening. Some things are always up as well. Someone mentioned the Caller quest in Fungus Grove, that would be a good fall back target. The Halls of Testing in ToV rarely runs out of mobs. The PoI Dragon (prolly a bit beyond your guild but ya never know....) is spawned by handing in quest pieces to a gnome who will spawn again a couple hours after someone else has done the raid. I will however say that highly contested mobs like Torrmax you aren't likely to find when you want. Even when you do don't expect 2 tries at him.

Finally keep adding mobs to that list, keep pushing the boundries. As your raid more you will get better at it. Your guild will also get better equiped.

Vindi - this is a test mob of your guilds raiding ability. Tactics learn from doing him will come up again and again. Easily tracked by a Rallos Zek worshiping ogre.

Yes. It is easier to make content to entertain people 75 characters at a time than it is to make content that entertains small agile groups.

Think about it. You are in charge of allocating resources to design zones and encounters. You have a choice in front of you. Which one has more bang for the buck? 75 >> 12.
