I posted this on my guild forums - but as it's from a shaman point of view, it might be useful here as well. :)

Way back in the mists of time, any mob over level 35 had the chance to drop a 100% WR item, the Box of Abu-Kar (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/item.html?id=11703). It didn't last long as it was thought to be unbalancing!

Kunark saw a similar thing where frogs in Sebilis could randomly drop a haste cloak, which provided an added incentive if the exp and gems weren't enough.

After that there was a lull, before Gates of Discord reintroduced the idea - most zones from then on have a smattering of items which can drop from any mob, albeit very rarely. The only quote from the developers is that it's "much less" than a 1% chance of seeing them! However, for soloing classes it can be a good way of aquiring focuses as the items tend to be better than named drops in the same zone. And if you're exping, it can come as a nice surprise to see something drop....

FWIW, since Gates came out I've seen the following drop:
* Mask in Vxed, twice (lost roll both times)
* Shield in NC, Cloak in NC (twice each soloing)
* Necklace in Natimbi (on the first day of GoD, solo)
* Gloves in Qinimi (also on the first day of GoD, grouped)

...and that's it, despite playing for hundreds of hours in Gates/OOW zones. They really are pretty rare! On the other hand, if you're a caster with half an hour to spare, why not try for some of the rare items?
