Wihle AC/HP vs WIS is definitely a scale that needs attention- you're leaving out a couple really important factors. To me, HP/mana regeneration and mana-free damage are the most important stats. Your biggest real upgrades will come from an Iksar BP, a horse, a fungi staff, a fungi tunic and (at 45) a JBB. Of course, these are all expensive... but better to spend a lot on these then on a bunch of little stat increases.

Personally, I think your equipment looks good. At 51 I routinely run around with 120-130 WIS, and have no problems with mana (of course, I'm a regen/canni fiend.) In case you do want to shift your focus a bit, let me make some suggestions that won't break your bank, offer nice wisdom upgrades, and don't totally trash your AC. Also, I'm going to throw one more consideration in there... focus effects.

Neck: Get a BA Medallion (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=6020). Stats aren't bad (ac 6, +5 wis, +5 int- so you'll lose a couple AC points, and gain 5 WIS.) You'll also get "Mana preservation II", which will cause all spells you cast under level 45 to cost 10% less. These should also be pretty reasonably priced (few hundred PP.)

Wrist: Swap out the totemic bracer for a Chipped Bone Bracelet (CBB) (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=809). I know, I know... but hear me out. You'll lose 5 AC, you'll gain 5 Wisdom, and you'll get "Affliction Efficiency II" - which will cause all DoTs under level 45 to cost 25% less mana to cast. Again, this should just cost a few hundred PP.

Waist: Swap out the spider fur belt for a Reed Belt (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=959) - you'll lose 4 AC, you'll gain 5 WIS, and you'll get the effect "Extended Affliction II" which will cause all DoTs below level 45 to last 20% longer. Probably be a bit tougher to find one of these- and it might run you 1k, so this might be a longer-term change.

I'm not saying that I think you need to make these swaps- I'm a bit of a AC/HP junkie myself, but if I were going to make changes I'd try to make them where I could also get focus effects.

Other nice upgrades for you:

Imbued Golden Ivory Earring (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=4930)

Arctic Wyvern Mask (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=5289) - few hundred PP and great combination of stats. The Enshrouded Veil (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=1899) also makes a nice choice (nice stats, and has "Summoning Efficiency III", which will cause your pet to take 25% less mana to cast) but will be a bit pricier. I still wear the AW mask. :)

JB boots (nice AC boost, only a few hundred PP.)

Good hunting!

1) 809 ac is good for 35. If you drop about 6 raw ac, you're going to fall below 800 ac. There's a school of thought that says (correctly, as far as I can tell) that the game looks at ac in discrete chunks, 700, 750, 800, etc, and makes no distinction for melee purposes between, say, 802 ac and 809. This also means that there isn't any difference between 799 ac and 751. The upshot is, dropping "a few" points of ac in favor of wis may be costing you more than you think.

2) Skill raises. The only way (unless you're being PL'd) you're NOT going to max your inportant skills is if you don't use them. If you don't use them, then having 180 wis isn't going to make any difference.

3) At 35, each point of wis is worth about 6 mana. That means, if you raise your wis by 20 points, you get 120 mana. Thats 2/3rds of a Greater Healing or 2/3rds of a Scourge. If that extra 20 wis were free, that'd be great. It won't be, though, it's likely gonna cost you 40 or so ac, possibly more.

4) Wis has NO effect on how fast you can pump out mana. None. Zilch. Zero. You don't med faster, cann faster, or regen faster. ALL it does is give you a bigger bucket of mana to pour from, and the ONLY time that's useful is if you're mana-dumping because you got a bad pull. If you got a bad pull, it's more than likely that you're taking some hits yourself, because you're either healing the other people in your party or debuffing the mobs. If you're taking hits, it's fairly important that you stay alive, at least until you finish debuffing/go oom from healing. AC/HP gear helps this (duh). From a mana standpoint, AC helps preserve your hp so you can turn it into mana if you need to.

5) Focus Items. Most of the focus effects that are useful to shamans are on druid crap. This kind of sucks. To be fair, there are some sexy-as-hell effects out there (mana efficiency 3, please-and-thank-you), but staying alive remains job one, so how these will effect the game is still less clearly defined for shamans than it is for those classes that would be wearing this stuff even if it didn't have effects. If using one doesn't drop you below an ac threshold, sure, go for it. Otherwise, I dunno...tough choice.


A quick-and-dirty rule of thumb is, do you (or others) tend to die while you still have mana in your pool? If so, more wis won't help. On the other hand, if you run OOM every pull, just from normal heals/debuffs, then maybe more mana is the key. I know for me, people rarely die because I didn't have enough mana. When they do, which isn't often, it's usually because spells take so damn long to cast, or the person I was casting on ran out of range (it amazes me, at 55 people still do this). I am admittedly biased. I like to be right in there, and I like to cast slow, etc when it needs to be cast, rather than when it's safe (for me) to do so, so ac, etc, may figure more for my play style than for some others.

Although the Ac/Wis/Hp/mana debate rages on, it is nowhere near as polarized as it used to be. People are starting to realise that the relative merits of AC, wis and hp are entirely situational. There will be different times when each is the most important.

As a young shaman it is important to try to keep your defense maxed. To do this you need to melee, and the easiest way to melee is to solo - in this situation AC & Hp are more important than mana. However, in a good group you should rarely get hit, and in this situation wis/mana (and to an extent hp) are more important than AC

Balance, I think, is the key. You should solo enough to keep your melee skill up (especially defense), and you should group enough to learn how to function efficiently in groups of different compositions of classes.

Without being too anally retentive about it all, and being limited by my small budget (damn you alchemy), I solve this problem by carrying around various pieves of gear that I will swap depending on the situation: For example, I carry a Thunder Runed War Spear for meleeing when I am soloing (13/30 +5AC +hp +various other stats), but for large group raids I use a runed shell pestle (not a weapon, 5AC 5wis 50mana). For small grouping where I may have to tank adds a bit, I use a spine chill spear because it is an OK weapon and it has wis.

Obviously there is a limit on how many different pieces of equipment that you can realistically carry around, especially if, like me, you play one a PvP server where you are obliged to carry around resist gear anyway). So, realistically, you try to achieve a balance in your gear that may not give you the absolute best solution for soloing or small grouping or large grouping, but that does allow you to function in any of those situations without compromising your performance significantly.
