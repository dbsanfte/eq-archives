Agassa
MGB? Well, if you plan to raid a lot then this AA is important. If not, then i suggest getting VP III as soon as you can. It is one of the most useful shaman AAs, allowing you to root-rot any non-summoning mob with ease and also providing great CC power in groups. It's also cheap compared to other major AAs like:
ND III - grants additional hitpoints(always good!).
SCRM - adds additional ticks to Q and also extends the duration of other buffs(FA still is the devil).
There have been lots of discussions how good SCM really is, but ~10% mana pres is always useful.
CA III/LR V - Defensive AAs. I suggest getting the spellcasting AAs first, then head for these.

But before you even consider getting any of this AAs(well, except VP if you solo alot)...
GET CANNI V!
It's really that good. Get it. Use it. Be amazed. Love it.

Sebekiz
I would advise (almost) anyone not to put any aa into any stats until they are 500+ aa - if they intend to progress through PoP. I have well over maxed stamina and I haven't broken into Time yet - although I have a lot of EP pieces.

I'd have to agree here. There's a lot of very useful AAs, and your stats are going to be hitting the caps (at least with buffs) before too long. Even without Time gear, the stuff that you can get with 1 to 2 groups in Omens is probably enough to have you hitting the caps eventually.

The only general AAs that I would recommend beyond the "normal" ones (Run, Regen, Innate Metabolism) are the Resists. The cap on these is pretty high, and with the dispells that some encounters have you can't always count on buffs to max you out.

Beyond the basics to get Cannibalization, you kinda need to look at what role(s) you usually find yourself in. I did up to LR5 to survive aggro a bit better, but have been focusing more on the Healing AAs since I'm spending more time (and mana) on spam heals or desperately trying to fill in when the Cleric or Druid die/go OOM. For someone that solos or tends to do more DPS spells (nukes, etc.) you may want to work on SCF or DOT Crits.
