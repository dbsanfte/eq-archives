basically boils down to:
1FT = 2.5hp/tic regen = 10ac = 40hp = 12wis = 50mana
naturally, the values above are just a general guide to an items value. I wouldnt always adhere to this myself when choosing an item. there are also things to watch out for/take into account when attempting something like this. wisdom values need to be decreased when over 200, to maintain the 10:6 mana ratio. what i did above is assuming wis is already over 200. if wisdom is already 255, the value of adding more is 0, obviously. Also, there is the AC cap to think about. If you are operating above the cap, then the value should be decreased. haste, and other item effects also make a calculation such as this very difficult to do. so i left them out. while everyone's opinions are different, i feel the values above are reasonable, at any rate. Just a few observations ...

For a shaman, 1 Sta = 3Hp. So the Sta:HP ratio has to be 1:3 (I think this is right - the logic applies even if my numbers are wrong).

And similarly the mana:HP ration is 1:2 (the ratio of conversion with cannibalise)

If you put a value on flowing thought, regeneration is numerically worth half this (per point) due to the cannibalise ratio. But a lot of people would value regen more highly as hit points are more versatile than mana.

As for the wisdom of higher level shamans, I'm lvl 55 and have a wis of about 150 - I think I'm definitely at the less-uber end of this board's population.
Sciek Spellbottler
I'd put em along these lines, starting with ac at 1

1ac = 2wis = 10hp = 15 mana = 5sta = 5agi (roughly same as 1ac)
1ac = 0.2 svm/f/v = 0.1 svd/p
ac/hp are always a premium stat for a shaman IMO. Well, always until you break 1100-1200 unbuffed, then perhaps the returns for ac diminish, although that is still debated in many circles. Hitpoints are always a prime stat to look for an equipment, level 1-60.

I put STA at 5 STA compared to 10 hp because you asked about lower levels as well as the level 60 game. Also, by the time you get to level 60, if you've been following this general idea all along your STA will be adding up fast and straight hp items ALWAYS add more hitpoints, whereas stamina only adds hp up to 255 where it is hardcapped.

Once you are geared up to where you have 1100ish unbuffed ac, then ac starts to become less important IMO, but by that time you should have a good idea what suits your playstyle best anyway.

STR/DEX/CHA/INT are bonus stats. I don't even take them into consideration since they have basically no effect on a shaman aside from being encumbered which is easily solved via STR buffs.
