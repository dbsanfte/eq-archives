Siriath
In Jaggedpine, I don't know about the Basilisks, as I never screwed with them. What I did was pull Griffawns to the village and kite them. Guards won't attack them and just fear off any adds. That works until around level 40 or so when you can take the occasional Griffon with some trouble. I could also take one anaconda, but I ended the fight oom and half dead, so I don't recommend that one.

After 45, very few Griffawns will con dark blue, and if you can get a Lupine Dagger, do so. Wolf Form is your best friend here as it reduces the aggro range of the flying birds for adds, and makes everyting but Potameids, Poachers, and Gnolls indifferent. Also, while in Wolf Form, Anacondas will not add, so that's a bonus too if you decide to go after them.

Whatever you do, DO NOT kill Wolves, bears, or Panthers here. I don't know about Dryads, as I am indifferent to them, so I didn't ever attack one. Wolves, Bears, and Panthers will ruin your zone faction here badly.

Right now I find myself killing 4 or 5 Griffons to every dark blue Griffawn that pops on track. I don't touch the Gnolls because this is my solo zone, and I don't touch the Potameids either.

Good Luck.

As far as Basilisks go, you can do them pretty reliably as early as 31 or 32. Maybe even 30. They can be feared with Panic Animal, and don't give any faction hits. The only ones that will aggro or that are social are the Genitors - and they also have eggs that can be sold for a few gold, or used in Baking.

To find them, go to the caves on the West wall of the zone. There's even a bank and some merchants there, if you haven't killed any of the Brell Gnolls there.

I found it kind of boring after one level, unfortunately. Kind of like Highpass Keep, or whatever it's called. Sure, nice experience, convenient, but kind of mind-numbing.
