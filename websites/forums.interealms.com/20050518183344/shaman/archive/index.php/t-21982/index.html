It's all about risk v efficiency. You can be very efficient by maximizing dps, but that comes at the cost of increased risk. When you are doing trivial content that risk is very small to begin with, but if you are doing challenging content that risk becomes greater. As a shaman it is your responsibility to minimize and manage that risk. On a more serious note, the original poster is just starting to realise what has been said here all along :) Shaman need something to add to this new fast paced GoD style playing (both Raiding and Grouping) because we were originally designed around long drawn out battles.

Evidence for the statement that we were designed for long fights is obtained by looking at our orginial spell line ups:

debuffs, including slow - poor effect on short fights, but a compounding return on longer fights
buffs - a +200 Str or Attack buff does nothing for a single fight, but has a compounding effect over multiple fights
DoTs - self explanatory, designed to be effective for long fights, pointless casting in short fights due to cast time + effect time,
HoTs - regen in its original form, Torpor and Qu in newer forms... with old content + slow + a good defensive tank, HoTs were a viable method to heal ... but with slow mitigation, huge DPS mobs and fast fights, there is no way we can heal a tank.

I hate healing, but I did a bit of an experiment just to prove to myself that the developers are FoS when they make claims that the 100 / 75 / 47% heal ratio is justified for priests... this was my experiment:

In a Vxed trial, I tried HoT the WAR on incoming + slowing a vxed mob with Turgurs (luckily it landed first time without Malos, so this is an EXTREMELY rare case, but it did mitigate) + spam healing the WAR in /defensive with Tnarg's (maxxed healing AAs) ... he died before Qu wore off (~30 secs into fight) ... there is nothing else I could have done to heal him any better .....

Interestingly enough, a Cleric had no trouble keeping him alive without /defensive and without HoTs and WITHOUT slow ....

Even more interesting, a Druid managed to keep him alive without /defensive and without slow, but he opted to use SoTW at start of fight ....

Same was repeated in KT as well ....

So if the developers think we can heal sufficently in GoD with HoT + Malos + slow + spam heal with Tnarg's + max healing AA + a WAR with /defensive, they are sorely mistaken ....

If they think a fast cast slow with a slight resistance modification will change things, then they are sorely mistaken also (as shown by my experiement, my Turgurs casts in 2.2sec, and that spare 0.7 secs that Nihil would have saved me would not have made a difference to the dead WAR) ...

I challenge ANY developer to make a 65 Shammy, 65 Cleric, 65 Druid and give them all same healing AAs (hell, even give the Shammy all the healing AAs and forget the AAs for the Cleric and Druid) and try to keeping a WAR alive in vxed, Tipt or KT ...

My next experiment is to see if an enchanter with a pet for massive DPS + chain runes can keep a /disc WAR alive better then I can ... I am betting with Tash / Slow / Pet / Runes, the fight will be ~20 secs and the chanter can keep a Warrior alive with Runes ....

Now I don't want to be a main healer, but I would like to bring SOMETHING to a GoD group ...maybe massive DPS boosts thru buffs, something like a group over haste cap + 150 attack (is this really that unreasonable, think of the DPS a chanter brings, and they have slow) .... maybe better defensive abilities thru buffs / debuffs (+avoidance buffs, +shielding buffs, +fizzle debuffs, etc etc) .... a DI type spell that rarely fails (hell, we can't heal fast enough, maybe some way of compensating for it :P ) ... some minor Rez functionality for when the Cleric dies cause we can't heal them fast enough .... etc etc (there are plenty of creative ideas already on these boards) ...
