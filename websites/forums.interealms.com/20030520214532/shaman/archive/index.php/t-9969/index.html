I used the pull spot on the ridge side of KC, and would agro-kite with dogdog, root/rot. I found that rather than pulling with a slow, pulling with Malisement kept mobs parked. at 43, move to Tenebrous Mountains and take the Grimling Runner camp at the GF zone. Pull the Sonic Dire Wolves with slow and sick pet on him. Once pet has agro (just a couple of ticks into him) Dot with Poison and med. It'll be tougher at 43, but once you ding 44 and come back you can make it from 44 to 46 in about 4 hours if you're consistent. I didn't really need KEI here, but it wouldn't hurt to allow you to throw in some DD and make it go faster. Use the Grimling runner pouch for the Rallic Pack quest (800pp auction on Ayonae Ro) and you should be on your way to financing JBB's (20k pp on server). Grimling runner is a 1.5 hour spawn cycle that spawns IN the cave you're pulling too.

at 46 Move to CoM and solo entrance. I am there at 47 and averaging a yellow every hour at entrance. I haven't had a problem soloing with a slow pull, but there is another good thread outlining those strategies!

My opinion is Echo Caverns, the side to the Deep, past the dwarf side of SH. The underbulks in there are great for shaman, and the first area has 4 spawns that can be single pulled with a little practice. If you get in trouble the guards and zone are close. They start turning light blue around 47 to 49. Great solo XP, challenging, and will give you time to med if needed. It's also close to Nexus/Bazaar for KEI recharge!
If you want a faster pace than first camp then head a little further in and take the immediate right past the first spot. If not camped, there are 7 spawns in there (Fiends, Stonegrabbers, and Underbulks). Problem here is they might agro when running away, but all rarely resist root. Stonegrabbers are really offensive spell resistent, but can be debuffed easily.
This also leads to the Taskmaster room, I wouldn't suggest playing with him alone until 49 spells, and slowing is a must. If you see a big alien looking guy whose name starts with a "X", don't even bother. He's a 55+ Chanter and spawns if you keep clearing the Taskmaster's room. Highly suggested to keep atleast one skelly alive to prevent this. I wouldn't start in this room until the latter 40's either way. I think the incline between Taskmaster's room and first room is a nice pull spot once initial spawn is broken, or just pull to the entrance to first room, so people will know it's camped.
If that room is camped keep going straight from first camp, pass the turn to second camp, then hug right wall until it opens up into a very large room. Follow wall all the way back until next tunnel starts. Now you'll be close to zoneline to the Deep. That huge room has 9 spawns of Underbulks, Fiends, and Stonegrabbers. Only one roams around and could be any of the three critters listed. It spawns close to the legde by tunnel to the Deep. This ledge is your friend and pull-to location. Park dog there for solo, or have group stay there while puller pulls. Casters can see and root adds easily from the ledge.
Now the critters can be single pulled with practice and high enough level. I think around 44, maybe earlier. Form the ledge you'll see roamer, and a static spawn right next to it, usually a Stonegrabber lying on the ground. Behind them you'll see 4 Underbulks. To the left of the Underbulks below a ledge is 3 static Fiends.
Solo'ing it will be constant pulling if mana holds up. I think these things are on a 10 to 15 minutes spawn cycle. With a duo or group, you'll be pulling the first camp too and it almost works out perfectly. Breaking up the first 4 Underbulks and 3 Fiend groups will be fun, but after spawn is broken, you'll have time to med, relax, and BS if in a group.
Oh the problems with these camps. The named ones. Trillcor and Fireclaw. Solo'ing, these guys will kill the fun of the spot because they will kill you if you are under 45 (severly twinked will most likely have difficulties under 42), and are a pain/mana drain solo'ing with 49 spells. Duo will be a fun challenge (maybe deadly), and grouped will add excitement and a change of pace. Trillcor (looks same as Fiends so be careful when you pull them) hits hard and fast, not real magic resistent, and I have never seen him drop anything, but fun nonetheless. Now, Fireclaw, you will know he pops when you see the Underbulk that's back touches the ceiling. He too hits hard and fast, and for added excitement, cannot be rooted or snared. He'll get the blood pumping just at his size. His back is higher than an Ogre is tall, makes for a nice 3rd person screen shot. He'll drop Fire Carpace almost always, and an uncommon drop of the fireclaw. Not great loot, but lower levels like them. Remember your egress routes when fighting these two and run early if things look bad, because they will catch you without SOW.
Worst thing about this camp is it's inside so no SOW!!
I told you the spots and a little of what to expect, but left the pulling and fighting up to you to experiment with. Hope this helps and is a challenging and enjoyable spot. I was amazed how fast I flew through the 40's to 50 here. I got 51 here too, but mainly had to pull from Taskmaster's room, and it was slower than I was use too. There might be better places, but I don't know any. Overall, great Xp, crappy loot, and on many servers empty.
