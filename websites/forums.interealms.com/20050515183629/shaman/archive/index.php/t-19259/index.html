That's pretty creepy, especially with how much I play, as well as how much I am on the internet when i'm not playing EQ..

I am on a diet, though (the adkins diet - it's horrible. You never know how much of the good stuff you ate has carbs until you cut them out..) and I'm in a Judo club.

I dont have too many problems with typing. I learned how to REALLY type. My instructor was VERY strict about people using improper wrist placement.

Heres another thing I would add to suggestions.

If you have the money, put $250 into a good chair. If you work at a desk for a living you know what i'm talking about. Your biggest expense for a computer area isn't the desk, or even the computer (on a part-by-part basis. The whole thing will cost more, but no one piece costs as much as my chair does)

If you're in a chair for LONG periods of time, and very often, make sure it's a good one, preferably that helps your posture. Dont get the smooth leather covering (rips and discolors easily), cloth lasts longer. And make sure it's metal, or a REAL word (oak). Or you'll end up buying a new one in a couple years.

This may seem like it's just advice to keep playing EQ - when if you are in the conditions that the people mentioned before are in, you should take more breaks from it. But a good posture , and a comfortable sitting place (that doesn't put pressure on any specific place for extended periods) is a good thing.

It's hard for some people to believe there are actual physical dangers involved in playing computer games, and whatnot, all the time, but they are there. This has been pretty eye-opening.

If you're stiff and sore after playing EQ, you're either playing too long, or you need a better chair. What Detton wrote is right on the money. Take an AFK every couple hours and stretch. Also don't play in a dark room with the computer monitor as the only light, that's a great strain on your eyes. Keep the room well-lit. Junkilo is right too -- ergonomic equipment will help immensely, *especially* wrist guards or a wrist pad. I've even got a wrist pad on the mouse pad, it's built right in. Go easy on the soda and snacks, and exercise regularly, and you're good to go IMO.
