Crypt of Decay entrance is solo-able. I've done it 4-5 times when I only had one computer (normally I 2-box).

Foul Puslings are the easy zone mob, Corrupted Puslings are harder but still doable. Greater Blah Puslings are not soloable (I think there are two different Greater puslings).

You can do the Cazic Thule zone splitting approach there if you don't get a solo pull. Try to pull solo, if you get multiple, root one and zone (go fast, these guys are MEAN). Heal up, invis, zone in and you should have a single to kill.

Spear of Fate and poison DoTs land well. Be sure to have him slowed (I also cripple, did I mention these guys are mean?). Malo* a good idea as well. Root/rot them. Manage your mana, time your debuffs, and root/rot. I get 6 percent AA (at 100% allocated to AA) at level 62. Even considering the time required to kill one mob, it is the fastest solo experience I've found. And generally, I'd rather poke myself in the eye than do this very often.

Soloing at 62
If you are PoV/PoS flagged, I've found you can solo the planarian larvae if you are careful in Valor. Make sure you have KEI and Aego (Virtue preferred of course) to help out. Fully self-buffed on horse w/ dogdog buffed w/ Focus, PE, and haste. Canni5 really helps ensure victory.

Spells memmed: Malosini(or Malo), Turgur's, Pox, Bane, Ebolt, Tnarg's Mending, Torpor, Canni4.

Self-torpor before the pull. Pull with epic dot. Two or three seconds before it lands, sic dogdog on the larvae. Malosini (seems to land okay, but you can use Malo to ensure some form of debuff sticks if you want) and then slow. Next unload Pox/Bane/Ebolt. Reapply dots & Torpor as required, casting JBB or nukes (mana is a problem on these fights, so pulling the JBB out of the bag helps) in between.

The larvae do run at low health, and they enrage as well, so position dogdog accordingly. Time your Canni5 for when you are near full health, because a bad round of larvae hits could end up killing you otherwise. That's why I use a gem for Tnarg's, even though I may not end up casting it at all. :D

Just to the right of the GY exit to PoT there is usually a single larvae squirming around. He makes a great test case to see if you can handle it. If things start to go bad, dismount from horse and zone. Since you are near the larvae's spawn point in the first place, and they are non-aggro, you aren't training anyone. :)

Watch for adds when they run, because the larvae are social. ;)

The rare spawn is "a festering worm". I saw him at the zone-out spot for the first time today and killed him. He seemed a bit harder than a normal larvae, but I was able to solo him w/o problems. I was 63 at the time, but did nothing different from when I was 62 except use SLTW for dogdog. He dropped Trinket of the Enlightened (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=19395). Nice little PR/DR resist earring for me, and not a bad general day-day wear for some people.

The exp isn't outstanding (as there is downtime between each pull, as this method is very mana intensive) but it was better than doing nothing while /lfg. I think I got 8-10% aaxp per kill at 62. I didn't check today, since I'm on a full regular exp burn to get to 65 right now. :p

Hope this helps,
