Also some of thier game mechanics are a bit wierd, for example the whole server choice thing when starting, but once you get into the game players generally report that it is superior to everquest, the USA launch of the game went very easy on them, I heard of no problems at all. All classes also get 'disciplines' that they can fire off once every 2 hours even as newbies these special attacks tend to do alot of damage, there are also much more common techniques that you can do, and when one person fires off a technique, a window is opened, if another party member does another technique in that window it is even more effective, and if someone else follows that one... that is what is called a 'skill chain' which helps make combat alot more interesting.

The class/level system definately sounds like a winner, in a nutshell you can change your class any time you want, of course the new class starts out at level 1 and you can again group with newbies, at level 18 you can do a quest that will unlock the ability to dual class, the 2nd class is capped at half the level of your primary class, but you must level that class up as well normally. Say for example you were playing a level 15 warrior and decided to try out a thief, you would go to your house, change class to the thief and play. Now, later on, if you decided you didnt like the thief and went back to your house and switched back to warrior, you don't lose any levels at all in that class.

So with that big of a paying population, you definately cant dismiss it as small time.
