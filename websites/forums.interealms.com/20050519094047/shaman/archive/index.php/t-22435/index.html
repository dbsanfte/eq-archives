Anyway . . . 2 waves of Tears + Velium Strike = 123.6 DPS. Add in dogdog's 25 DPS and we're just below and unhasted Ranger, generally thought to be around 157 DPS.

I think there are a few factors that make this comparison misleading.

1) This hypothetical ranger can easily grow his or her DPS a lot by being hasted, as haste is generally available in most groups and all raids.

2) This ranger is using weapon damage, which is going to be useful whether or not the mob has poison resistance, or cold resistance, or magic resistance. On many mobs some shaman DPS spells are rendered useless.

3) The shaman is forced to use mana to do this DPS. The ranger can autoattack and go forever, but we are forced to use up limited resources. I thought the idea was that using mana made for more powerful 'burst' attacks to make up for the limiting cost.

4) I am not sure adding dogdog is completely fair. Particularly in a raid instance his lifespan is very limited.

5) Shaman are going to have to deal a lot more with aggro than a meleeing ranger.

6) Melee DPS is more even. With shaman the long cast times mean that often in group instances by the time you get the mob malosed and slowed, there is barely time to get good use of Velium Strike, much less a rain or a dot. Melee can keep going right up to the time the mob dies without being wasteful.

7) Melee is not going to break mez or trip traps or pull adds onto the ranger the way rains will for the shaman.

8) To sustain our DPS over the long haul, shaman will have to use our 'active' mana regeneration by spending time using cannibalize. It's very popular for shaman to 'pay' for their good mana regeneration with penalties in other areas, but as long as that is true, it has to be taken into account that time spent regenerating our mana is time we can't spend doing DPS.

I don't necessarily think 155 DPS is bad for a shaman in theory (although it is definitely not great either), but there are so many extra costs to our current methods of doing DPS, that I don't think we are really as well off as your post seems to suggest. Just my perspective.
