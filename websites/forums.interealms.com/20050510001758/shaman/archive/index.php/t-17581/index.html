I've often wondered what kinda effect it would have if Warrior had an offensive disc that could be turned on fulltime that would give them say a 20-30% increase in DPS ability at a loss of say 30% of their tanking ability, unlinked from their other disciplines. Perhaps a big boost to dual wield, double attack, triple attack and a big penalty to all defensive skills like riposte, parry, dodge. Thus in a roleplay sense the warrior is focusing his attention on hitting the mob by ignoring his abilities to avoid damage. In essence turning a warrior into a near monk for damage dealing purposes.

The idea being to make warriors stack at least somewhat in an xp group and on raids. If you're not the MT, then you go into damage focus mode where you ignore your parry, dodge, etc skills and focus purely on dishing out damage. If the mob turns on you then you can turn it off and concentrate on those skills again allowing you to quickly turn from monk-like damage potential to normal warrior damage and tanking abilities.

The biggest problem I can see right off the bat with it is that monks are pretty hosed already right now and this would make them even less desireable without some significant upgrades. What those upgrades should be... I dunno heh. Just an idea I had tossed into my head for warriors to make them a bit more versatile.

By itself it seems to me it'd help the stacking issues and perhaps desireability of warriors for xp groups, BUT it still wouldn't "Fix" them since their ability to tank in an xp group is trivialized by knights with comparable hp/ac and vastly superior aggro abilities. It also fails to address the problems the other pure melee classes have. Mostly, just something to give warriors a wee bit of versatility. *shrug*

Truly Fixing warriors and pure melees in general is going to be a tricky road I think. IMO, Knights are overpowered at the moment and need some drawbacks to their aggro abilities. With the massive amounts of mana regen a knight can burn fast casting, low mana cost spells damn near nonstop for an xp grind creating insane aggro. Up the cast times to increase the chance of an interrupt and thus at least introduce a chance at failure on those aggro abilities as well as increasing the mana costs so that they need to be used judiciously instead of non-stop... then we'd see a more balanced set of knights.
