It's really amusing to see some of the commentary here. My advice is to go to some of the EQ2 sites that have what limited info is available and read up before making comments that have no foundation.

All MMORPGs will have level grinds, just like almost every PnP RPG did - it's a core concept of the genre. It's amusing to see people write that they don't want to level again. So, you'd rather be at max level, with all the best gear available, and do what? Farm lesser loot? Kill gods with ease? What's the point? The only game that's somewhat like a MMROPG that has no real leveling that I know of is PlanetSide - which is already consolodating servers due to subscription loss. Leveling is a part of character development is a core part of RPGs.

Item decay is not a bad thing. It's good for player economy. Having tons of items constantly entering the economy while zero items leave it wrecks a player based economy. Just look at some of the most popular shaman items, like Leaf Scale Gauntlets (20p maybe) or Metal Linked Crwon (50p maybe) for examples of how great items can be nearly worthless once they're readily available. The best items in EQ are usually NO DROP, so they don't enter the economy. It's unlikely that they would make NO DROPs or any other supreme high end loot degrade to the point of destruction - that isn't fun. The point with item decay is to be a money sink (you pay for repairs to keep your gear fine tuned) and/or to eventually remove some items from the economy to stimulate economic growth vs decline. Check out DAoC if you want to see a game where item decay is very well implemented.

Gee, I just slaughtered about 2000 critters and my sword never lost it's edge - it's a miracle! Gee, I just got brutalized repeatedly by hordes of critters and my armor isn't scratched in the least - isn't that amazing?!

Graphics don't make a great game, but since the majority of us do own a decent computer with a decent graphics card then we might as well be playing games that use that power, eh? EQ graphics aren't completely horrible but they are dated and they aren't very efficient. DAoC has arguably (sp?) better graphics but you often get horrific graphical lag/lockups. I don't play that many games that really use my graphics card to its potential, but I am currenly finishing up Knights of the Old Republic for PC and let me tell you, it has some damn spiffy graphics that seem to be using the full power of my video card - and I haven't had ANY graphical lag issues at all playing the game despite some wicked effects being used. I would love to see a new MMORPG that came close to having the scenery, player animations, and magic (force) effects that KOTOR has. And KOTOR probably isn't even the most graphically amazing game out there.
