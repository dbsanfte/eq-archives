Chutoi
Okay, I don't know if I can add a lot to this discussion, but what the heck, I'll throw in my 2 cents. Maybe someone will get some use out of it. I'm level 65 and got 212AA atm, so I have a smattering of skills.

Here's the profile. Equipment is a bit out of date, but the AA's are correct. http://www.magelo.com/eq_view_profile.html?num=261042

I agree with most people on the initial stages--run3, regen3, MC3, canna5.

If you are in a raiding guild, MGB. Also nice are SCR3 and SCRM. One note on SCRM--if you have something like the blessed coldain prayer shawl, or another buff duration extension item, I'm not 100% sure it stacks with SCRM. I have heard (though never bothered to check) that buff duration can't go past 2x the original spell. Perhaps someone here has tested it and can correct me on that.

My recommendations on AAs from that point depend on your equipment. It's a no-brainer that hitpoints are vitally important. I'd recommend going with hitpoints whereever possible until you can canna 5 with self buffs on, and still remain above 1.75 bubbles of hp (ie, not get low-hp agro). After that, my personal recommendation is to go for combat agility 3 and lightening reflexes 5.

ND3 might give you 250 hp. So what? That's less than 2 canna's, and not even 1 hit from a mob. CA3 and LR5 will get you hit 1/3 less. With mobs hitting 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8 hundred a hit (frequently doubles or even quads), which is better? 250 more hp or not getting hit for a couple of times?!? No brainer there. CA and LR are GREAT. Sooner or later, you will get agro unless you are so cautious that you're limiting your group(s).

Personally, I'd avoid the healing stuff until you have pretty much everything else covered. The healing AAs only work on tnargs, endgame. That's quite a limit. 12AA to get an aditional 130 hp on your heal isn't worth it when there is other stuff to get.

I would also recommend combat stability and innate defense over additional HP skills if you can self-buff canna with no agro. On most yard trash, my average hit with CS3 and ID5 is probably about 200-300 depending on how hard their max hit is (still toast on boss mobs, mind you). It goes REALLY well with LR5.

Quick buff isn't bad, but it isn't worth the AA IMO compared to LR and ID. Especially LR. Let's face it. Aside from torpor/quiesence (which can't really heal that well in PoP anyway), why do you need to cast buffs fast? Answer: you don't. If you have spell haste items, it's a real waste. Spell haste is hard capped at 50% regardless of what items/AA you have. You can't shave more than 1/2 the cast time off.

Radiant Cure 1 is a good investment early. You can take a lot of stuff off with it.

Virulent paralysis 1 is a good investment to get a mob off of you when root fails. It's situational, but it works. Also, if you have 2 stacked mobs and can't target . . . well, it's surprisingly useful at times.

If you want a toy, spirit call is fun and cheap.

Bottom line, I can't recommend the CA/LR line highly enough. The CS/ID line is also very good. Maxxing both of those takes 54 AA, and it's WORTH it. You can start to see improvements in just about every level of them you get.

After you max LR and ID, then I'd probably go for SCM, depending on your mana regen/wis. It helps, but not as much as people seem to think. You may have some kind of mana preservation device anyway. If you have something with preservation of mithanial, or mana preservation IV, it just isn't gonna help all that much in comparison.

I haven't noticed a great improvement from channelling focus, so . . . up to you. I wouldn't be in any hurry to get it.
