Mamutoi
The Spirit Call aa skill calls forth wolf pets that seem to be the same as your level 61 pet. They attack the mob that you have targeted when you summon them, or any mob that attacks you after they are summoned. They last one minute. The refresh rate is 12 minutes. It does not cost any mana. The first aa level costs 4 aa points and calls forth 3 pets, the second level costs 3 aa points and calls 4 pets, the third level costs 2 aa points and calls 5 pets.

I have done two levels of this skill and I like it. I use it for big mob kills, when I am soloing or when I am in an xp group and we get an add.
Juujuu
Just to add to the above post:
The ability does about 750-1000 damage per pet, depending on the level of the mob.
The pets will enrage on low health.
The pets have HUGE push. If you send the pets at a mob, expect the mob to be pushed by the melee quite far from the original spot the pets engaged. To counter this, strafe around the mob as your pets are "ejected" from your toon, and they'll surround the mob, and push from all sides, thus countering the push factor.

deria
A lot of people overlook the fact that there are some mobs that are more or less immune to shaman. If a mob resists cold and poison, you will not be contributing anything significant in the way of damage (epic and disease based DoTs are useful, but not in short fights). The spirit call gives you the chance to do 4k-5k of melee-based damage, which can't be resisted.

I also find spirit call useful for positioning mobs. Some have said they use it to root-park adds, but I've never really found it too useful for that. It has a rather longish cast-time.

When all is said and done, it is a very low-cost AA to get. 4 points, then 3 points, then 2 points. I have all 3 levels and like the skill quite a bit.

I like SC, but I wouldn't rate it as a must-have like canni V. It is mainly just good fun, but it has also saved me once or twice whilst I've been jumped upon whilst doing a tricky CR - mana free damage and a tank in just a few seconds is very useful.

The interrupting capability is also very useful when soloing, particularly against casters that gate (Emperor in Seb is a prime example). I love soloing in Seb for some quick cash, and if you get a few wizard adds spirit call can be very handy.

Lastly, use it when you get unrootable adds, either to help hold off the add from the party, or to kill the first mob that bit faster.

All in all: AA points are probably better spent in SCR, Quickbuff etc, but SC is far more fun, very versatile, and doesn't take long to get so it gets my vote.

I've got SC2, about to buy SC3 tonight.
