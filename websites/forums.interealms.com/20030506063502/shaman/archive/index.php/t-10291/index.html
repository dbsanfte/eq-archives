Zyphyr
I get it from several souces:

1)When they were brand new, people using ShowEQ tested them and proved the mana savings is variable using the mana numbers sent from the EQ servers.

2)I have performed some rather trival but time consuming tests that prove it as well using only the information provided in-game. If you would like to test it for yourself do the following:

A)Mem a high mana spell (300+ base cost) and have something you can target to cast it on. Pets make good targets for it.
B)Place yourself in a nice safe out of the way location.
C)Remove any flowing thought items and mana regen buffs you have on.
D)Cast the spell from A until you get the insufficient mana message.
E)Turn on logging if you don't normally have it on.
F)STAND there repeatedly casting the spell until you are succesfull. This is the point where the test really starts.
G)Continue standing around. Do not sit. Do not Canny. Do not let anyone cast a mana regen buff on you. Keep trying to cast the spell every second or two untill successful.
H)Repeat G several times - as many as you can stand to do, but at least 3 additional repetitions.
I)Exit EQ and open your log file.
J)Calculate the time-lapse between successful casts.

If the effect is a constant, the variation in time between casts will be no more than 6 seconds.

If it is variable, you will find a much more substantial variation. Something on the order of 1 to 2 minutes if you used a 300+ mana spell.

I have performed this test on Mana Preservation, the Mage Summoned Focus Earrings, and on Summoning Efficiency. I haven't gotten around to doing it with Affliction Efficiency yet, but there is absolutely no reason to believe that it will be any different.


BTW, if you remove all mana saving foci (or at least any that would effect the spells you are testing with) you can use the same exact method to prove that Specialization is a fixed amount.


If it said "UP TO" in line 1, I'd have to agree with you, but it doesn't.

Then you clearly do not understand how Lucy works.

The spell data files do not contain the text "1: Decrease Spell Mana Cost by 25%"

They instead have two numbers : 132,25

From testing it was determined that effect code 132 means "Decrease Spell Mana Cost", and that the 25 associated with it indicates how much.

There is nothing at all in the file that indicates whether or not the saving is fixed or variable.

The people who maintain Lucy chose that particular wording and use the word 'by' when they probably should use the the phrase 'up to'. That doesn't change the absolute undeniable fact that the effect is variable.
