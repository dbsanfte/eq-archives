Summary: A shaman that maxes out his healing AA (healing adept3, advanced healing adept3, healing gift3, advanced healing gift3) will NEVER achieve a Tnarg's Mending mana-to-hp heal ratio that matches the mana-to-hp heal ratio of Kragg's Mending. This is a bit of a bummer.

By default, Tnarg's heals 1300 hp for 450 mana. This is a ratio of 2.889 hp per mana spent. In comparison, Kragg's ALWAYS heals at a ratio of 4.875 hp per mana spent (assuming each of the spells hits for full).

With maximum AA points in healing abilities, a shaman can improve the ratio of Tnarg's heals to 3.988 hp per mana, not shabby... just don't compare it to a cleric or druid with healing AA maxed.

Gikk the Unholy
Not totally accurate. Mana preservation items DO work on Tnarg's, but do NOT work on Kragg's. Also, heal enchancing focus items DO work on Tnarg's but again NOT on Kragg's.

Now do the math. Maxed with Advanced Healing Adept 3 you're cranking out 1547 point Tnarg's. Add a Healing 4 item (as i understand it, random between 1% and 20%, we'll say 10 for the sake of arguement) and you're up to an average of 1677 a heal. Now factor in critting 19% of the time and you average 1995 a shot. Factor in a mana pres 4 item and your mana/heal ratio shot up to 4.93. The same shaman is kicking out Kragg's for 400 mana at 2320 a pop, or 5.8 mana/heal ratio.

Really looking at max potential? Add a preservation item like the Aten mask (18%), shaman elemental sleeves (25% healing bonus) and your ratio jumps to 5.51 (2034 healed for 369 mana). Throw in the cleric haste buff and nightcaller's leggings and you're casting time is down to 2.4 seconds.

What it comes down to is this. Without any AA or focus items, Kragg's mending is a good choice for efficiency, and Tnarg's is a good choice for speed. But as you get healing AA and items, Tnarg's gets a lot better while Kragg's changes little. As you become more powerful you'll find yourself using Tnarg's more and more until you no longer have Kragg's memmed, ever.
