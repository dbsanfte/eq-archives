-If your dex is higher than your int and wis, then it's the stat used in determining if you get a skillup in fletching.
-Higher dex increases the frequency of procs on your weapons.
-Higher dex increases the rate at which you learn weapon skill, and I think it secondary attacks like flying kick, kick, bash, etc.
-Higher dex increases the rate at which you critical attack.

As far as I remember, that's the entire list of things that dex does as found out from tests done in the past, as well as this is everything that dex has been told to affect as per Sony. (I think it's in POP expansion rulebook that it now correctly says what the various stats actually do).

The only thing that dex might also do is affect the crit rate of DD spells, but I've never seen anyone do any parses to see if say, 100 dex made you crit a lot more than 280 dex, for example.

Steelbear
Posted a couple years ago by Abashi:

We're posting this for clarification, and because previous explanations have not been accurate (manual, etc). We will not, however, engage in debate and our stance on how much some stats affect game play is that it's by design to inhibit some aspects of twinking. Are there better attribute systems out there? Yeah, but we're not going to change something as fundamental as a stat system in EQ after the game's been out for more than a year.

Anyway, here you go:

DEX: how quickly you learn weapon skills; how often
weapon will proc; how quickly you learn rogue skills;
how hard you hit with bows

STR: determines how much you can carry; influences
maximum and average damage; influences how quickly
you learn many offensive skills

STA: affects how many hit points you have, how long
you can hold your breath

INT: directly affects how quickly you can learn most
skills; affects the amount of mana for bards and all
mages, as well as shadow knights

WIS: affects the amount of mana the priest classes
have, also rangers; also affects how quickly you can
learn many skills if your wisdom is higher than INT.

CHA: affects amount you will be paid for goods by NPC
merchants, and how much they will pay you; affects
the saving throw on certain bard and enchanter spells
(charms in particular)

AGI: affects how quickly you can learn some defensive
skills, how difficult it is to hit you as well as how much
damage you take when you are hit.
