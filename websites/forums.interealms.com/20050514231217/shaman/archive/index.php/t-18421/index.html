Boinshar
It would help if you use the right spells vs the right mobs.

Rains have several uses:
- Fast and low agro dps on raid level mob with low PR.
- Fast and efficient dps on 2-4 low PR mobs in an exp group.
- Fast way to clear trains of greens.

Rains should not be used for:
- Killing mobs with decent or high PR, which include most of luclin.
- Soloing. Since rains reach peak efficiency when used against at a minimum of 2 mobs that are closed together, you'd be forced to fight at least 2 mobs at once when you could be just dealing with one.
- PvP. Unless your target has very low PR, rains won't do too much. It is best used as a means to force low hp and low PR casters to constantly move, thus giving them less chance to cast.

Like all spells, Rains' uses are situational. When used against the right mob, a shaman can sustain very respectable dps with rains.

EDIT: Herm, since you are on Rallos, rains probably aren't the best spells to use on raids or in groups.

LaughingWoman
Dots - mana-efficient damage over time (i.e. slow)
Rains - mana-inefficient fast(er) damage.
Nukes - drastically mana-intensive instant damage.

That's how it boils down for shamans. All rains suck in their randomness (mages have the same gripe, 3rd wave almost never lands, and all 3 waves CAN be resisted outright). What they're for is a chance at good, sustained DPS when you have an overabundance of mana - which I frequently have.

I use rains on (some) raids (have to be very careful on that one), and in LDoN if we have C3, beastcrack, or a bard around. Or if there's actually someone in the group healing, other than myself.

They're extremely situational, but can be very rewarding.
