weissblau
SG run is really easy, I was doing it at 55 no problem.

Zone in from CS, invis if you like (it won't do much good), and move to the edge of the water.

Jump in, swim off to the left into the tunnel there.

Keep swimming to the end and face UP and swim... you will swim up the waterfall. Sometimes you end up way at the ceiling and fall back into the water :-)

Get out of the water and re-invis if you have a chance (NOW it will do you some good).

Find the little gold fish statue closest to the waterfall. There's a tunnel just to the left of it; go in there. Go as far as you can until you come to a 90 degree right turn. You will now be heading due East. Run to the end of that tunnel and take a left, you're now in WW.

Once in WW - the biggest factor is whether you are KOS to CoV or not. Check your CoV faction by porting in to Cobalt Scar and conning a Wyvern or Drake. If you are NOT KOS, then killing the hounds is easy, I could do it pre-60 without difficulty. Kill mastodons for profit while you're at it (just stay away from Tantor!)

If you are KOS to CoV, you are in for a rough ride. There are tons of wandering drakes. Most do not see invis, but if you are fighting you'll be in trouble. My suggestion: go kill at the arena or plate cycle in Kael until you are not KOS to CoV.

Trevurg
I got the drops from Siren's Grotto solo at 61, so you may be able to skip the friends part. It's a pretty frustrating zone to fight in, so it's a good thing that the quest item drops are fairly common.

Once in Western Wastes:

Find a cliff edge and pull velium hounds to it. They have an insane aggro range, so "pulling" consists largely of running around until one jumps you. You can also use Sense Animals, tho you're likely to get a nonhound animal. Kill it anyway, it will free up a spot for another hound to spawn.

They run FAST and are quite resistant. Treat them with respect.

Good luck.
Winney
Finding and killing the hounds are not that bad really assuming you can take the hits which at something like 170s a pop is next to nothing by today's standard. I got all the furs I needed and more a few weeks after the 8th shawl went live (before the level cap of 65 and new spells) solo in about 5 hours total. I used the spell Sense Animals as a poor man's tracking. It is not perfect, but it works great for the job. I was not KoS so I just ran around the zone casting it. If it targeted a hound, great, if it targeted a rhino or mammoth I would run over and kill to open the spawn slot and continue along. Now, where it gets tricky is with the Burrowers (the worms) and the Wyverns. Sense Animals will target the Burrowers, but that would be a risky kill for you right now. Later on, they are not hard at all, but for now you would do better to avoid them. So what happens when you run around and the ONLY things you are getting targeted are the Burrowers? Easy, you kill the roaming (not the ones in the cave) Wyverns. Sense Animals will not target them but they are plentiful and killing them gives no faction hit. They share the spawn slot with animals, this is why you read the stories of there never being any animals around. This means you must kill them to open a spawn slot. Even though they do not give a CoV faction hit, CoV mobs will assist them so you need to pull them to a spot far enough away from a potential add (and don't pull it through a mob either). The beach to the left of the zone in is good. Now plan on tanking them dead, they do summon so do NOT pull with a DoT or some damaging spell. Tank them dead, loot (a few plat) and continue along with your poor man's tracking. The Wyverns do run on low health, so be sure you can either blast them dead at the end, or use poor man's snare (chain root) and tank them until zero health. It will take some getting used to, I was very cautious at first, but after an hour you should have the hang of it and be collecting your furs

Hope this helps you out, the shawl is great, I still wear it /nod
