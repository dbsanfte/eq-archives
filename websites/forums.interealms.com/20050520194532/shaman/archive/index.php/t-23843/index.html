If you are mostly a gamer How, the AMD is a better bang for your buck game-wise. Intel is the best for having more than one program going at one time. Of course you have to be comfortable with the label so if Intel is your brand go for it.

I just put together an AMD64 3000+ which is overclocked to a 2.2Ghz with the same memory and HD but with a FX5700LE (waiting till EQII comes out to upgrade). It runs the EQII CC at default at about 32 for the tower and 20 for a Male Barbarian. I can play EQLive with all the bells and whistles on even in a group but the effects are blinding.

According to my AquaMark benchmark my video card is the big bottle neck. CPU is rated 9100 or so but the video is only 2000.

I can only guess that since the AMD64 3000 is supposed to be equal a P4 3Ghz that your set-up would be going about a AquaMark 50,000 to 60,000 with your video rated at around the 7000 mark. I assume your CPU rating would be around 9000. I am not familiar with the other benchmark programs so I can't say what your score may be there.

Oh and if you have the sound and netcard, I think it would be safe to save the money as long as it supports possible CPU and Memory upgrades to faster CPU and RAM.

Good luck
