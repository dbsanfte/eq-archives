What is reputation?

See those little green squares at the bottom left of a post? That is the reputation of that poster. The more green squares, the higher that persons reputation. If they have one grey square, or one or more red squares, they have had a lot of negative feedback. Reputation is a feature of these boards, and is raised or lowered by the other users of these boards.

How do I check my reputation?

On the top right of the screen is a button labeled user cp http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/images/top_profile.gif Click this and at the bottom of the page, your current reputation will be listed, and if you have any, the previous 5 rep-bumps you have recieved.
You have to be logged in to check your reputation.

You can also check the reputation you recieve on posts by clicking on your own post in a thread, just as if you were adding reputation. A pop-up windows lists the last 5 rep-bumps and comments you got for that post.

How do I bump other's reputations?

When you are logged in, click the little talking head icon next to "Edit" on the lower right of someone's post. A pop up box will appear and you can either select "I Approve" or "I Disapprove." There is a space for comments as well.

How do i check other's reputations?

By clicking on this <a href="http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/memberlist.php?&sortorder=DESC&sortfield=reputation&perpage=30&ltr=">link</a> or by listing members, and then sorting by reputation. By consulting the table of titles below, and seeing what title a user has, you can get the range that their reputation is within.

How long can my comments be?

Your comments can be as long as you want, but have to be on a continuous line. It is considered good etiquette to leave your name with feedback.

Why are some reputations worth more than others?

Your reputation is based on a number of different criteria. Sunmoon (the owner of this board) has set these at their current levels.


Administrators can add or subtract 5 rep points
For every 180 days you have been registered on the board, your rep bumps are worth 1 extra point
For every 100 posts you have made, your rep-bumps are worth 1 extra point.
For every 100 points of reputation, your rep-bumps are worth 1 extra point.

So, as a formula, the amount one of your rep bumps is worth is (round all numbers down):
(days registered/180) + (posts made/100) + (current reputation/100) = current rep-bump power

Why do I have grey dots when I check my reputation?

You have to have made 30 posts before your reputation effects others. The grey dots are from other users that have not reached the 30 post threshold yet.
Also, if your reputation is below 50, your rep-bumps do not effect others. If you pass the two checks, you can alter others people's reputation.

How many Rep-bumps can i give in a day?

You can give 4 rep bumps over a 24 hour period. Administrators have no limit. You can also only give reputation to a person you have already given reputation to, after giving rep-bumps to another 15 people.

What are the different reputation titles?

The number at the end of the title is the reputation level required to reach that title. You can see these titles by leaving the mouse pointer over the green squares in the bottom left of a posters box.
