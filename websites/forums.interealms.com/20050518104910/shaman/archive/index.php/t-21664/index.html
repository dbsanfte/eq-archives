As a software developer myself, I'm going to disagree with this. It isn't that hard, and I can virtually guarantee you that the developers already know about 75% of the bugs we see day to day, but can't spend time fixing them, because management has already filled their plate with unreasonable deadlines for new content.

Every software job I've ever had, management seems to be fundamentally ignorant of the "holy trinity" of software development--features, quality, and cost. You can't increase any one part of the equation without sacrificing something else. You want more features without sacrificing quality? Well, it's going to cost you more. You want more features without increasing cost (the most typical demand)--well, that's going to mean a lot of bugs. Seems pretty simple, doesn't it?

However, the overwhelming trend in software these days, whether you're in gaming development, or operating systems, is to push more features out the door as quickly as possible because in the minds of management, "features" == "sales".

We're finally getting to the point in EQ where the customers aren't buying it anymore. Literally.
