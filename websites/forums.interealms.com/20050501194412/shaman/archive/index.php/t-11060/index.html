As a shaman the following are rough to pvp against:

Bard: too high resist, cant catch and control them, too fast, unresistable manadrain and annoying dots to wear you down. Unresistable disease debuff followed by Virulent paralysis (VP) is perhaps our strongest card here. NonEbayed bard who isnt afk should be unkillable.

Ranger: outranges and outguns us... all of it while running faster than us. Archery means pretty much a definite loss versus a ranger in terrain which is flat and free of mobs. For me the best chances have always been to stay close to the ranger, as most rangers melee dps is only about half of their archery dps. Dmg shield potion helps then as well. Smart rangers switch to 2handers. Rangers have insane atk as well so most of our sturdiness from high ac is more or less useless.

Paladin: forget it. They can cure anything we toss at them. Their manapools are almost as big as ours and they have massive hps.
Holy steed, invuln, efficient and fast heals, insane 2handers perfect for pvp and lay on hands. Hate em. Landing a tash proc followed by a quick malo and blind/root can work sometimes, but is usually not worth the time.

Shadowknights: just like paladins, but on the plus side the fight wont last longer than 10 seconds so you aren't wasting your entire evening killing them. 3k+ pvp dmg harmtouches at the least with full PoP abilities. Stat drains to mess up your bufflist and ac/resists. Hp drains to keep them alive and you running. Snare counters any usage of pets and kill you too if it lands. Unholy steed, invuln, insane 2handers and shadowstep.

Wizard: almost unresistable nukes (yes friend resisted 3 lure of thunder in a row yesterday) and strong root + MB. Runes means our pet(s) cant get in there and interupt. Shadowstep and nexus insta gate means you cant really die in pvp as a wizard. People often whine about MB needing to be nerfed on pvp servers. I'd say it's the insta nexus gate that needs nerfing. No risk no reward please.

Necro: only applies if the necro has lots of AA's in crits. In that case he can simply chaindrain you to death. He has invulns and shadowstep to keep him alive. All his dots are dispellable or curable. Add LB for the surprise gank. His pet you can nullify with flash of light etc.

Druids, clerics and shaman are not so much 'tough' as tedious. It usually boils down to who has the more instaclickies, potions, nifty AA's like radiant cure. Theft of thought mace is a good tideturner as well.


Rogues, monks and warriors are easy when you have space to move around. Their lack of channeling make it almost too easy sometimes. Then again, pure melees with 180+ in base resists and PoM flowers we cant do jack against.

Mages are pretty easy as well. Just control their malas and keep their pet pacified with blind or root. Beastlords are more or less like mages except they lack mala but have higher maneuverability and their pets last longer.

Chanters can go both ways. A chanter who knows what he is doing is capable of killing ANY other class (bar SK using gimptouch). Most chanters dont know what they are doing pvp wise. Before rapture and the upgrades in tash and their new partial resist nukes they used to suck at pvp, so not many chanters ever bothered with it. The ones who did are very dangerous. Recent bug with chanter pets have made them totally immune to spells.


My list of what I can handle loosely resembles the above guideline. Variations occur and are usually caused by gear differences, terrain, who gets the jump and the opposition not having their brain properly plugged in.

All in all the shaman class has lost in pvp over the last two expansions. Mostly due to the high resistability of all our spells, the insane increase in overall player resists, lack of reliable dmg output but most of all due to other classes gaining skills/spells that counters ours. Right now I would rank shaman somewhere just below average. We used to be in the top three together with bard and shadowknight.

Call me insane, but I still think it's fun.

Asta Khaan
In any situation involving two groups (or more) pitted against each other a shamans main strength is dropping malo on every single opponent. Starting with whoever is at the top of the hitlist.

After that your mission is rooting/blinding and generally staying alive. If time permits drop some cures/heals. If you find yourself unhindered by melees breathing down your neck, you may even drop some slows on rogues and rangers. I usually opt for disease slow since people have lower dr than mr and it is harder to remove.

In most mass pvp, well at least the way it works on Vallon, the absolute majority of dmg inflicited comes from melee damage and wizards nuking. To some extent also necros draining. Tash/malo/snare/root a target, sic almost everyone on that target, beat it into a pulp, choose a new target. Pets mostly serve as interuption factors against non-targeted casters to keep them busy. Paladins and SK's strafe out and keep non-targeted casters from doing anything constructive.

Mass pvp hitlist, order of killing importance (my subjective opinion). + means a class pvp strength which is harmful to you, this is the reason the class needs to die fast. - is a STRONG reason why the class is hard to kill and thus goes lower on the list. You dont want to spend 15 minutes chasing a selod bard around while their wizards are tearing you up.

It loosely takes into account the classes strengths/weaknesses and how they work together. Many of the class rankings depend on them having special or situational abilities or pets up. Or simply being a notorious pvper. Example: Bards indoors arent ranked very high on the kill list at all. A bard grouped with four rangers and a cleric needs tash/malo/snare asap.

As far as pvp goes...

Hybrids
-Leave them alone, any hybrid worth their money will cream you.

Rangers will take you down with bows unless you use terrain, and even then, there's not much you can do to them unless you get lucky with chain blind and resists (HIGHLY unlikely, since they are faster, and can STILL fire arrows at you blind).

SKs will burn you down in under 10 sec if they have UT3. If not, it's just going to be extremely painful for you--trying to stay out of melee range while his dots are doing their work. Again, blind and VP are your only options, if they get resisted (and you don't die in 5-10 sec) you are screwed.

Paladins are highly annoying. It's like playing quake against somebody who has God mode. Nothing you do works on them.

Bards are even more annoying than paladins. Not only do they have god mode, you can't get away from them.

Pure casters
- You've at least got a chance.

Enchanters are either gods, or the "suckiest sucks that ever sucked the suck" to quote Homer J. Simpson. Pray you found the latter.

Wizards as long as they don't have MB, you've got a chance. Their highest nukes are difficult to resist, but they take a decent amount of time. If you can use rains to force them to move (thus not casting) and stick BoS on them, they are toast.

Mages are easy. Blind = pet go bye bye. Mage spells are resistable, and like wizards, if you force them to move and kill with DoTs, there's nothing they can do.

Necros aren't difficult either--as long as his gear doesn't out match you too much. The only way a similarly eqipped 65 necro can beat a 65 shaman (full spells) is by luck. All of his dots are curable/dispellable, and his nukes aren't all that. Your first focus is to keep yourself healed, use SC/pet to interrupt his casting, rains to force him to move, and keep your health up against LB. Hopefully you'll do enough damage to him so his LB doesn't hurt that much.

Priests
- You are in luck.

Clerics are annoying. They heal a lot, summon their hammer a lot, but they are harmless. Their nuke damage can be out healed easily, their hammer can be meleed to death. It'll take a while, but if he doesn't flee you can kill him--eventually.

Druids are sucky. Dispellable dots, and resistable nukes. If he sticks around he's toast.

Shaman vs shaman is nothing short of retarded. You can sit there for a year and half throwing everything you've got at eachother--and still maintain 90% mana, forever. The only way to kill another shaman is to have better gear and more toys--dark mace of thought is very effective against other shamans, and clerics too.

EDIT: Oops, forgot the pure melees.

Pure melees--you can kill them, every single one, without any issues what so ever.
