They should do what they should have done in the first place, but with a twist.

Instance the Elemental planes.

BUT.

Do NOT instance the loot tables.

What do I mean?

First off, the planes become instanced. That means they become split between "yard trash" zones which are more like grouping-miniboss areas and "raid" zones. This might take a little rearranging, but you have to work to fix things.

Ok, the spawn timer remains the same, being whatever it is for that mob. Whoever comes along and kills the mob when it's "up" will receive loot from the corpse when it's dead.

However, these zones will be instanced, so the next raid to zone into the "raid" part of the zone receives a fresh instance. But, if the mob's not "spawned," i.e., the spawn timer has not reset, the mob drops no loot. You get the flag for killing it, but you get no loot for your effort.

No raid that instances a zone receives a loot-bearing mob until the respawn timer counts down and enables the loot table.

There, now everyone can have their own uncluttered shot at the Elementals, yet not have the game become flooded with overpowered gear.

Problem solved.

On a side note, the new "XP" areas of the zones (elemental "A") would be opened to non-raiders who complete a very long, very difficult quest, THAT THEY COULD NOT COMPLETE IF THEY DID NOT HAVE THE GEAR OR SKILLS TO SURVIVE IN THE ELEMENTAL PLANES IN THE FIRST PLACE. This allows the casuals who have evolved past BoT, Tactics, and LDoN to get access to greater challenges, yet removes the uberplayer's rants and whines about why they should not be there.

They cannot get uber loot aside from what might drop off named mobs out in the XP zone.

They cannot mess up raid triggers.

They cannot raid (they lack the "raid" flag).

And the quest they performed for access was just as long and difficult as getting the elemental flag the normal way.

There, everyone wins.

The massive gear imbalance between ubers and normal people is getting out of control, and must be addressed. This will help address it. It also solves a problem of overcrowding in non-uber areas.
