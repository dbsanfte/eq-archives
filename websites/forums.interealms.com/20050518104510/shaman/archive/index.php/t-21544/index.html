weissblau
Thought I'd share a neat trick I developed to help my guild out on raids.

We generally assign specific people to rebuff specific buffs. This lets each buffer keep a single buff memmed at all times. The system works best when the buffers stay relatively alert and buff people upon rez, but often the just-rezzed person has to send tells to get a particular buff.

So who do you send a tell to? Some people were setting up hotkeys, but then you have to spam them frequently, or you clutter up raid chat with 'someone hit the hotkey please' and the result of the key.

So I created a bot (level 1 halfling warrior). Left him in Rivervale with an AFK message:

/afk FOCUS=shaman1, STA=shaman2, IOS=shaman3, VIRTUE=cleric1, SYMBOL=cleric2, VOQ=chanter1, VQ=chanter2, GoD=chanter3, Pot9=druid1, BSS=pally1

Then I get a guild officer to put the name of the buff menu bot into the guild MOTD, and I'm done. When someone needs a buff, they shot the bot a tell and the bot lets them know who to ask. Doesn't completely eliminate the raid chatter, but it helps a lot.

Yeah, our apps are in a feeder guild. I had three additional reasons for not putting it in the guild motd, too:

1. Motd is often a couple of sentences anyway, this is a lot of material to add.

3. It requires an officer to update the motd every time the buffer lineup changes, and frankly, they are all too busy to do this. This way I can get them to add ONE pointer to the motd and then take care of updates for the rest of the night.
