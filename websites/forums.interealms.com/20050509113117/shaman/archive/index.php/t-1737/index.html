Landragoran Heronwing
Balance is the key to a powerful character.

If you go all AC/hps, you will lack in the caster aspect, having more fizzles, less skill ups, and a small mana pool that might limit your effectiveness for some situations. You might get a rep as a bad shaman who doesn't even have the mana for a larger than normal fight.

If you go all wis you will turn yourself into an int caster with no snare and spells that are slower casting than any other caster in the game. Basically you might as well reroll as an enchanter with fast spells that do more if you're going to play that way. People won't comment so much when you die in 3 seconds if you're an enchanter. They will if you're a shaman tho, and you'll get a reputation for being a pansy.
Not only this, but at lvl 30 mobs get another level of difficulty. They hit harder, you need about 700 ac to negate a lvl 30 mob's attacks.

I better give my guide to levels and AC again. Reminder: these numbers are based on the amount of AC you need to avoid or reduce the majority of a mob's attacks against you, in effect buying yourself more time to either avoid the mob or kill it.

lvl 30 mobs = 700 ac.
lvl 35 mobs = 750-800 ac.
lvl 40 mobs = 850 ac
lvl 45 mobs = 950 ac.
lvl 50 mobs = 1000-1100 ac.
lvl 55 mobs = 1200 ac

If you can come reasonably close to those ac numbers for the level of mob you are fighting, you will notice a large difference in how often and how hard they hit you. Keep in mind that the levels given are MOB LEVELS and not player levels. I was hunting mostly lvl 35 critters when I was lvl 35-40. I was hunting lvl 40 critters when I was 45-50. I've been hunting lvl 45 critters from lvl 51 to level 60. Of course there were a few lvl 50 critters in there, and still are more than ever really. So that's why people say if you can get to 1000-1100 ac, you are set for general hunting. It's cause normal exp mobs are nicely foiled by that level of AC.

Sengal
If you go all AC/hps, you will lack in the caster aspect, having more fizzles, less skill ups, and a small mana pool that might limit your effectiveness for some situations.

I just wanted to comment that I went the AC/HP route from level one. My Wisdom is currently at 110. I have never had any difficulty keeping all of my casting and melee skills maxed at each level and I rarely fizzle. Casting spells go up very fast regardless of your wisdom (they may go up faster with a higher wis - but what's the point? Sit and chain cast spells if you are worried about your skill level).

As for the mana pool, I rarely go OOM - and each time I did it was in an over-pull situation. If you are smart with your mana, and fight mobs appropriate to your capabilities, you will rarely go OOM.

More AC = less chance of getting hit and you will get hit. Our slows generate a lot of aggro and it is nice to see a mob missing more often than hitting. The hitpoints are a nice cushion that allow you to absorb some damage and (once you get canni) quickly replenish your mana bar.

I just don't see the value in wisdom, unless you are going to do a tradeskill :) , but that is ME.

Everyone has their own opinions on this - my advice is play around with your equipment and see which route serves YOU better. You may enjoy being a caster with 250 wisdom OR you may like standing toe-to-toe with a mob taking hits like a tank OR you might end up somewhere in between. Good equipment is cheap enough nowadays that you can afford the luxury of tinkering with your gear.
