Macnair
What does "must have" mean?

I think it means that this AA is judged to be the difference between success and failure, in an "out on the edge" situation. It is an AA that gives you the ability to do something you can't do any other way. So if you have it you can win; if you don't have it you will probably fail.

That contrasts with "incredibly useful" AA, which make a strong difference, but not usually a win or lose difference.

On that basis, I would propose:

Must Have:
Call of the Wild. If you can get one cleric back into the fight, who can then divine rezz one more back into the fight, then you will be able to change a marginal event from failure to victory. There is no other way a shaman can do this: this is a must have. It's also a convenient time saver, but that's not what makes it a must have.

Spiritual Channeling. If you can pump spells nonstop for four minutes, regardless of mana, then you will be able to change a marginal event from failure to victory. There is no other way a shaman can do this: this is a must have. It's also useful for buffing, but that's not what makes it a must have.

Ancestral Aid. Honorable mention. If you can restore the health of your whole group with an instant click and then go on to other things, then you will be able to change a marginal event from failure to victory. This is "honorable mention" in this category because you can also do this with the clicky on epic 1.5. But it is still in the must-have category because we have no group healing spell.

Incredibly Useful:
All healing AA
All defensive AA
Extra spell slot

These all add very significantly to what shaman can already do.

With all the healing and defensive AA, a shaman is pretty tough and can get a lot of healing done. I like them all. But they are a percentage increase on what we can already do; they are not adding an ability which we could not do any other way.

I love the 9th spell gem and I'd like to have 12, but what it has taught me is that I just can't keep memmed all the spells I want. I have to figure out -- and often reconfigure -- two spell sets, and switch between them with the /mem command, on the fly. Nine slots, swapping 3 gems back and forth, yields 12 spells available. I could have managed 12 spells with 8 slots, swapping 4 gems, but it would have been slower and more dangerous.

Origin I see as a convenience for melees. No more need to buy gate potions to get out of the dungeon. I think "needing" it for a shaman is pretty excessive. I have forgotten to rebind myself when I had set a bind point in Western Wastes, and sure enough I got myself killed with my gear in Ssrae and a naked barb standing in the tunnel at the entrance to Sirens Grotto. Origin would have been handy then; I wouldn't have been in the embarrassing situation of needing a guild mate to come rescue me. But the lesson there is for me to recognize that if I forget to reset my bind point after leaving a zone like that, I am liable to need to ask a guild mate to come rescue me again.
