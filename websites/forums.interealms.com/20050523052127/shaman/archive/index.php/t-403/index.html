A quick comment on the cure disease for those of you not familiar with it. Disease counters determine the 'severity' of the disease if you will. I think shaman slow puts on 16 disease counters (very high) as Eoin noted in the prior post. If you did a low level cure disease, it might 'cure' one or two disease counters. That's why you have to cast cure disease like 3 or 4 times at low levels. It removes counters, but won't 'cure' the disease until all the counters are removed. I believe abolish disease automatically removes all disease counters--though I'm not sure if it only removes the first disease spell, or all disease spells currently on the target. Okay, I tried it out last night. I was almost right. :p

First thing I was wrong about: When curing spells with counters, the topmost spell is removed first. Time order has nothing to do with it; it's totally based on the icon position (which is kinda dumb, but fits the EQ spell system).

The other thing I guessed wrong is how the counters are removed. Here's what I did:

1) Cast Insidious Decay on myself (adding 9 disease counters and a spell icon).
2) Cast Plague on myself (adding 9 more counters and another spell icon).
3) Cast Counteract Disease on myself (removes 8 counters). 8 of the 9 counters from ID were removed. Both icons remained.
4) Cast Counteract Disease on myself again. The last counter from ID was removed, and the icon was removed.
5) Cast Counteract Disease on myself for the third time. Plague was not removed. This means that the second casting of CD only removed the last counter of ID. It did not apply its remaining 7 counters to Plague as I had guessed it would.
6) Cast Counteract Disease on myself for the fourth time. Plague was removed.

So, CD affected each spell in the exact same way, despite having counters left over from the second casting. Once enough counters were removed to kill the topmost spell, any excess curative power was wasted.

However, the above phenomenon only applies to cures that don't have enough power to remove all of the topmost spell's counters. For example, I recast ID and Plague on myself, then cast Abolish Disease. Both spells were removed by it. So, if the cure has enough power to remove the topmost spell, its leftover counters will be applied to the next spell down.

I hope that made sense. :)
