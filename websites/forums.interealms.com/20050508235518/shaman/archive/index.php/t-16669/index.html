Weyalan
The relative efficiency of the various canni spells is a big fat red herring. To an extent, the improved efficiency of the canni-dance is also a red herring - a worthwhile exercise, to be sure, but a red herring nonetheless.

The bottom line is that for a low to mid level shaman, there is a simple lesson to be learned, and that lesson is the interchangeablilty of hp and mana.

Mana = hp and hp = mana, they are the same, interchangeable. You convert mana into hp at a ratio of approximately 1:2 and you convert hp to mana at 2:1. It is so simple, but so many young shaman just dont get it. Your total POOL is the sum of your hp pool and your mana pool. If you are low on one, convert some of the other.

Being a good shaman is, in my opinion, all about keeping your hp and mana bar at as close to the same level as possible. if you are at 50% hp you should be at 50% mana, etc. Frankly it isn't that difficult.

The returns for the canni 1-2 spells were changed quite recently, when they changed, someone posted the following, and I coppied to my tips file :) So thanks to the nameless shaman who compiled it.

Never go to Caster Realms for spell information. They have the lousiest database maintenance. Just go to Lucy to get the latest data translation from the spell.dat

The real ratios are
- Canni 1: 50 HP for 20 (@24) to 29 (@65) mana, so 0.40 to 0.58
- Canni 2: 67 HP for 30 (@39) to 37 (@65) mana, so 0.45 to 0.55
- Canni 3: 74 HP for 39 (@54) to 42 (@65) mana, so 0.53 to 0.57
- Canni 4: 148 HP for 81 (@58) to 82 (@60) mana, so 0.55
- Canni 5: 1924 HP for 1066 mana, so 0.55

So, basically, yes the ratio stay relatively equivalent between the different spells as the level rise. But it's easier/cheaper to heal 148 HP at level 58 than it is to heal 50 at level 24... Volume is the kicker with upgrades, not the ratio.
