*GEEK ALERT*

First approximation:

It's cost-effective to nuke between swings if the dmg/mana ratio of that spell is greater than one and a half times the ratio of your DPS to the mob's DPS.

So take CoF, which has nearly a 2.0 d/m ratio.

2 > 1.5(your DPS/mob DPS)

so if the ratio of your DPS to mob DPS is less than 1.33, it makes sense to cast CoF in-fight rather than to wait til after wards to heal up the extra damage taken by not casting.

This assumes no loss of your melee damage by casting which is not technically correct but works as an estimate. It does not take into account fizzles or resists.

This makes sense intuitively, because if you're way outdamaging the mob, it doesn't feel right to nuke but rather to let your swords do the work. But if you're in a tight fight and the mob is hitting for 100 in the time you hit for 100, then you're going to benefit from nuking instead of saving the mana for healing up later.

Anyone see anything wrong with this analysis besides the obvious dork factor?

Okay first off let's look at the numbers:

Normal Melee DPS (target dependent of course) is on the order of 75dps.

CoF -- base damage 206 + mage bracer = 247 max damage. Max DPS = 123.

Calefaction -- base damage 450 + mage bracer = 540. Max DPS = 216


By way of comparison to a pure caster -- i.e. a Wizard

Ice comet -- base damage 1120 + mage bracer = 1344. Max DPS = 213


The real difference between wizards and us is that our DD spells have long recast times. That said, we fill the time with melee damage output. The next effect is to give us a very high sustained damage profile.

We are quite simply unlike any other class in this respect. Toss us some KEI + PoTG and a Bard song and we can average out to over 100 DPS on non resistant mobs over extremely long periods of time.

As for mana regen/HP buffs == Opti buff combo for us (whether solo, exping in a group, or raiding with most encounters) is as stated KEI + PoTG + Symbol. The combo gives the same 1150HP that Aego does but trades the AC for an extra 6mana per tick.

Also of note for those who say we don't have enough mana to constantly cast while in melee -- Our mana regen (standing) can be as high as 38mana per tick (PoTG + KEI + Bard + AAExp + Normal) At that rate of regen we can quite simply cast either CoF or Calefaction constantly (Calefaction takes just over 7 ticks to refresh, while the mana used is replenished in six, similarly CoF takes three ticks to refresh comparied to just over three ticks to replenish the mana) You will eventually depleat your mana bar if you constantly alternate between the two, but you will find that with even minimal down time you are in good shape.

When to nuke?

When you want to be a damage dealer. We can do many things, and do them well. Buff, CC, aggro control, downtime heal, etc., and when we choose to, we can lay the damage on thick.

An example, using rough numbers, but close enough:

39 Ranger gets his Careless lightning spell (99 dd). Casts it a few times, says "kewl" and never uses it again.

His hasted DPS (50%) with, say, a Lammy and a Centi SS = (roughly) 20

His rogue buddy's hasted DPS with, say, a Trak Tooth and a Centi SS = (roughly) 31

Careless lightning, 99 damage with a 2.75 casting time DPS = 36

How long does a fight last at that level, 20 seconds? 3 Casts of Careless lightning will have that Ranger doing Rogue-like damage for half of that fight, and coming close to, equalling, or exceeding the Monk's damage.

(This assumes that the nukes are landing for full damage, so it's situational, of course)

It only gets better as the bigger nukes come up. If you've got the mana to nuke, and a mob they'll land on, not nuking is limiting your damage potential, and that, in a word, is bad.

Since I'm frequently the only Ranger in attendance at the guild raids I've been to lately (HoT, DN, Wuoshi, Gore etc), nuking is usually not an option. buff_slave_01 reporting for duty =/

Being a hybrid is all about using every advantage you have to generate impressive results for any situation.

In the damage arena, we've got the superior attk ratings, the ability to keep a mob from ever turning our way in the first place (unlike rogues), nukes, great straight-up melee damage, and self-procs to make any narrow-focus melee class respect our dps.

We can't always use every advantage, but when we can, ouch.

This is all beside our end-game archery untouchableness.

Like most other people, I will say it is situational. On a low AC mob, there is no need to nuke, better to save your mana for healing (if you are soloing) or other spells. On a high AC mob, especially one that is not fire resistant nuking is definately the way to go. At level 60, firestrike casting time drops down to 3.2 seconds leaving it just under 100 DPS for a full hit. Start swapping out your normal items for mage summoned focus items (if a mage is available) and your nukes become an even more important part of the damage you can deal. I can personally nuke during a raid with KEI and focus items and get higher damage than melee due to the fact that alot of the raid content in SoL is high AC, the drawback is that alot of the mobs have higher fire resistance too so knowing your targets is required. When I am soloing, I am generally dual boxing a chanter so I always have KEI, haste and a slowed mob, so I can nuke as I see fit without having to worry about mana much at all.
