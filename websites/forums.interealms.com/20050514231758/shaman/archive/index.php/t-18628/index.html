I don't know jack about playing a shaman from a firsthand perspective. I know the game well, been playing since two months after release. Started as a druid, powerleveled a bot warrior and got a taste of melee, then a shadowknight and a cleric bot. I have since stopped the cleric bot after the highest res. Now I'm grooming a shaman bot (L49). That is my perspective.

As a shaman, you are not expected to melee. In fact, you're hardly expected to contribute damage. Maybe send your pet, and if you have gobs of extra mana then cast a DoT spell. Basically, the only time you need to damage the mob other than your pet is emergencies or when you are able to keep up with your other duties without causing the action to stop because you need to meditate for mana.

Now I don't know about cripple and malo all that much. I'm not really an expert on shaman tactics. But as a tank, I want you to do whatever you can to get the target slowed in the shortest amount of time without getting yourself killed. As a knight, I am not too worried about how soon you slow it, I will keep it pinned to me. Some tanks can't/don't do this, but most try to.

If that means you need to use debuffs to get it slowed, more power to ya. All I care about is that it gets slowed, and gets slowed right away. Once slowed, the game moves at a more manageable pace. Clerics can keep me alive better, and if they aren't paying attention I can take other steps to keep myself alive. But it's important that the game is slowed down so that I have time to react and do my job, which is staying alive and making sure the mob hits only me.

The entire game is a domino effect. Each person does their job, so that the worst case scenario (wipeout) does not happen. Puller's job is to bring in as few as possible, crowd controller's job is to get the pull sorted out, slower's job is to slow down the game to a manageable pace, tank's job is to keep mobs hitting them, healer's job is to keep the tank alive and correct any other unexpected damage, rest of the group's job is to kill as fast as possible so you can repeat.

Buffs are maybe in between all that. You need to factor them in, but you need to know what is important. You don't need to cast STA buffs on casters.. they don't get a lot of HP per STA so it's not usually worth in day-to-day situations (ok, if you're some uber d00d in PoTime then whatever, that's not day-to-day to most of us so nobody wants to hear you toot your own horn, it's irrelevent). You don't generally need to cast a CHA buff on a tank (again, I know about those Death Pact type spells.. blah blah blah.. it's not day-to-day so shut it). The first mana you need to spend on buff's is for things like HP on the main tank, haste on melee classes if they don't already have a haste buff. STA on the main tank.

After that, tell people you are done with standard buffs and will take requests. Don't just assume the main tank wants STR. Most do, but not all. At level 65, I don't bother getting STR buffs. They don't add enough noticeable damage and I don't really care about small increases because the tank role is more defined at higher levels.. tanks aren't expected to put out huge DPS, the group just wants somebody who can stand out front and survive while keeping the mob on them long enough for the rest of the group to kill it. Rangers are generally the same way.. I see them requesting DEX from shamen, but most rangers have enough STR with their self buffs already.

Basically, as a buffer you will be well-served to understand how other classes work and what they truly need. A plate-wearing tank will be asking you for HP, STA, Haste. A ranger will ask you for DEX and Haste.. sometimes HP/STA if they have some reason to fear for their life. Rogues are the as rangers except STR instead of DEX. Enchanters, I dunno, probably want HP to stay alive through mez and maybe CHA to make mez stick better. Other casters will most want HP when they are afraid of getting hit. Make that a last priority because most of the time they will not get hit. Try to optimize your mana by giving buffs based on biggest impact to the group. Buffing a wizard's STR will not help much, though he might want it to carry back fine steel weapons or something.

As for tanking, umm no. Maybe up to your early 50s it's technically possible. But shamen who do it generally don't understand how much better plate classes mitigate damage until they hit PoP. You have those HP and chain armor not so you can't tank, but so you have a slightly better chance of not having your brains plastered against a wall when you draw aggro from your slows and debuffs. Those HP are also useful for the cannibalize spell. Don't get accustomed to tanking, you simply cannot do it in PoP and most things that you can tank will be below you to the point that they aren't worth fighting.

Your offensive melee skills are also bad. Yes, you can swing a spear and single attack with very low skills. You might hit for like 50 every now and then. You might outdamage your pet (at L49 my dogdog seems to outdamage me already). But that doesn't compare to the amount of damage true melee classes are putting out. Yes, it's better than nothing, and if you constantly have a full mana bar and are bored then go ahead.. but don't think you're doing your group some kind of favor.

Send in your dog, it will probably help just as much as you meleeing, and then you can meditate and make sure the group is taken care of. You will not get groups because of how much damage you can do. Heck, as you level up even your buffs are about third place on the reasons why groups love you (your buffs are generally just "nice to have" but aren't imperative in day-to-day groups). The number one thing in your arsenal is slow. Every group in PoP wants a slower, it's practically a requirement.

Honestly, when I'm setting up groups, the fact that a shaman can heal is more important to me than their buffs. I try to avoid forcing them to be the primary healer because I know they can get themselves into enough trouble trying to slow, but I like the fact that once a mob is slowed, a shaman can patch heal behind a cleric, that's a nice bonus. Especially if there's a bard in the group who is providing the clarity, or if the group recently bought C3 in PoK. Because I can buy C3 in PoK, but I can't buy slow casts.. I would say shaman are above enchanters for desirability in many groups.

Hopefully this gives you another perspective.

Necessary buffs for MT only and ROTATE THE BUFFS - i.e. don't fully buff and then med, cast one buff, have the tank pull, cast another one after the fight, etc. This gives you a chance to canni back the mana. If you are not casting, you should be cannibalizing. Use the Cannidance (see the Medicine Cabinet). If your best slow is running you out of mana, try your second-best slow. SLOW IS YOUR BEST WAY TO KEEP THE TANKS HEALTHY: DAMAGE NOT TAKEN IS DAMAGE YOU DON'T HAVE TO HEAL.

If you are not main healer, debuff as much as is practical. Learn when to cast slow so you don't die. Stand RIGHT NEXT to the MT if you cast slow on inc, or right next to the mob if you cast after it is in camp and engaged. Trust your MT to take aggro from you. After engaging, cannidance again if you can, or just cannibalize if you can't sit.

A horse is REALLY nice because you get mana regen while cannibalizing without using cannidance. However, cannidance is still better than a horse if you hit every tick, because you get the health regen as well.

Keep regen on yourself constantly and cannibalize down to 80% or so. Some people cann farther down than that, but it's nice to have the HP in reserve in case you have to tank, and I figure that as long as HP is not 100% I am not wasting health regen, so it really doesn't matter where I am with that. Sometimes I'd dip to 60% or 70% if I had a lot of rebuffs to do.
