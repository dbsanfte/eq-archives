Some have said our community lacks cohesion and I got to thinking why that might be. I suggest it is caused by the varied nature of our class.

In your opinion, what is a shaman?

I think I am going to take some heat for my opinion but here goes.

Simply put I am a support class.
I like being a jack-of-all-trades.
I like buffing people.
I like taking care of my group however they need.
I like keeping a cool head and "fixing" problems when they occur using the many tools I am given.
I slow.
I malos.
I don't add much damage to a grind. I dot or nuke on occasion.
I aid with backup heals but rarely main heal.
I don't raid much.

I feel I represent the average player. Not in skill but in style. I bring it up because I hear some vocal people here talking about how they don't want a new buff because they fear being a "buff bitch". In your eyes I AM that buff bitch but I don't think you represent what most of us are. I checked his Magelo (sorry I forgot your name) and he is an uber among ubers. You represent such a small minority IMHO yet I feel it is the Time capable player that SoE is always trying to please. I think I should be their target. Trouble is that you often never hear from us. Admitting you like to buff or act strictly in a support role is not cool here. I have often kept my opinion to myself for that exact reason. It's too bad too because I may have advice of value to the "average shaman".

Anyway I think this is me saying I am out of the closet. I am not uber and that is OK. I am a really good player and I enjoy this game. Most people are happy to have me around. I am starting to sound like Stuart Smalley now so I better stop :)

Please tell me how you play. If you like to buff and like to support others quietly, say it. And if you hate this aspect of our class tell me why you stayed. What was it that kept you leveling this class while enduring the hell of buff requests?

I am not trying to start a fight. I really want to talk about it.

Here is my view on the Shaman.

We are not the best at anything (Slowing semantics aside), but we are very good at a lot of things. However, though we can perform many different things, we can't do them ALL at the same time, so it seems we get out-shined by the specialists.

This is a common error, and one thankfully many people see through.

The Shaman is not the hero. We do not swing the sword that fells the dragon. We do not throw the spell that takes down a legion. We do not cast the heal that saves the lives.

No, Shaman are not heroes. We are more then that. Shaman make everyone ELSE into heroes.

While everyone else shines in the spotlight, we are in the back, keeping everything together. It is not just the warriors strength that wins the battle, it is our strength with the warrior. It is not the wisdom of the cleric that saves the day. It is our wisdom with the clerics. And it is not the magic of the wizard that turns the tides of battles - It is our magic, with the wizards.

I, for one, am proud to be a Shaman. We may not be the best at any one thing, but we are the keystones which hold everyone together and strengthen, be it a partner, a group, or an army.

Once again. We are not heroes, and we never will be. We are better then heroes. We are Shaman.

~Detton

The need for slow is integral to the design of EQ encounters, and Shaman are the rulers of slow. Slow lowers a melee mob's offensive capabilites by a factor of god knows how many levels. Just about anyone who's played this game for 50 levels with any class can /ass target and mash the slow button.

A great shammie on the other hand allows the group to ramp up the overall difficulty of a given encounter to what it would have been without slow in the first place. In other words the party will be able to pull enough incoming mob DPS to greatly surpass that of the original "shamanless" group which translated means WAY more xp/ time.

In LDoN:

- played as mindless slow bots they allow a group to play casually with little to no fear. Or- to play "normally" without a dedicated healer.

- played really well (in combination with other classes played well) shaman facilitate massive ownage of adventures.

- And then there's hard missions which aren't even practically feasible without a shammie and a bard. (sure it's doable but certainly not in a standard pickup group) Even a great shammie isn't gonna allow a group to defeat 6 "900's hitting" mobs at once without a _damn good_ group.

Speaking as a non-shaman (65 clr is my main) I can honestly say I've met very few bad players who are shaman. I don't know if the class demands skill (weeds out the bad players) or molds good players by virtue of it's role in the group.
