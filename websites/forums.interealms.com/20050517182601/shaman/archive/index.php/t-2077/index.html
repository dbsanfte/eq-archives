Lorace
Paludal Caverns is far and away the best place for up to level 23 or so. Almost nothing runs when low on health, and there are endless pulls. The zone is broken into several level ranges, and the xp modifier seems to be massive.

I have no idea how you are doing it, but I was using a 51 druid with the 24-point damage shield/SLN/chloro to PL a shaman after switching servers, and found you could get from 1 to 20+ in a single night there. It was easy to always have 3 critters on me at a time taking damage from the shield.

After that, I moved to Marus Seru and found it great up to level 30 or so. The south end near the zone to Sanctus is best. You will have to throw a root on the critters here when they run so that they stay and keep beating on the damage shields (really hope you are using Damage shields, or it will be very slow going with a caster) I definitely agree with Nazgruul that it is WAY harder to PL a caster than a melee type. Takes a lot more time, and much more effort. There are other ways to do it besides damage shields, such as beating stuff down, using memory blur/atone, and finishing off with the caster.

As has been mentioned above, your best bet with a high level shaman is to recruit a melee class to join your little group, and keep them buffed/hasted/healed while your lower character sits there.

Not that you asked, but I don't recommend powerleveling a class that you haven't played to a high level before or otherwise know well. You learn a lot more than you think about a class at the lower levels. (I'm only doing it now because I already played a shaman to level 57 on my original server)

Kappy
With a high level druid and a little help, powerleveling casters becomes easy. Things that help: Fungi Tunic (for druid), Velious BP (no mana regrowth), lvl 60 helps even more, as does good AC.

Lets say the caster is 20, and the druid is 55+ with fungi tunic\regrowth on them. They are in SolA. The druid pulls three mobs, around the casters level. The druid has a 32+6pt damage shield for 38 damage per hit. The druid doesn't attack the mobs at all, but since he pulled with snare they stay on him. The caster attacks each of the mobs until he does 1pt of damage to each of them, more if you want. The mobs will kill themselves on the druids DS. The last mob of the 3 will want to run away so either the caster has to do 10 damage to it so the druid can root it , or just have the caster root the mob so it attacks the druid again. 3 exp kills for the caster and the druid should be nearly full mana and just 1 heal or so. Works good up to level 50, try lower guk once solA goes green.

Landragoran Heronwing
There are two ways I know of to give a noob caster unlimited mana for nuking.

1) Modrod2 mage + cleric or Torpor-casting-shaman. Mage uses modrods to make mana to make more modrods for the noob caster to use. Each mod rod has a potential 600 mana in it for the noob. All you need to do is heal the damage it causes them.
This process is kinda resource intensive, as it requires two specific classes to PL one.

2) Necrolicious twitching. This works for quite a while as the necro can actually aid in dmging the mob and then FD. Also snares make it easy for the noob caster to deal with and avoid the mob. Necros have mad mana regen anyway, so that should be all you need for good PLing. Necros can heal over time as well, provided there are mobs about to lifetap.

The last method is to invite an enchanter of the same level into the group. The chanter mem blurs the mob after it has started to run, then your noob caster nukes it dead easily. Breeze/clarity keeps your noob in plenty of mana for this small task.

However you do it, PLing is not much fun after the first hour or so. It gets old fast, and IMHO it's stupid to play a game if yer not having fun.
