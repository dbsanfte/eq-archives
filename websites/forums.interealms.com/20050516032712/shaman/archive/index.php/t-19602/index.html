I kinda missed this one and I feel it is worth saying it again: PoP and to a lesser degree SoL and LDoN increased the amount of mana regeneration available to all classes. However this was a flat increase, meaning everyone got a potential extra 50 or so mana per tick - regardless of how well that class regenrated mana before. Now, no matter how casual or how uber, how hardcore or laid back you are there will eventually come a point where noone really goes out of mana in a LDoN adventure or a short raid. That means our mana regenration ,which we pay a high price for in terms of raw spell power and efficiency is completely or at least largely negated.

In addition to that individual fights got a lot shorter and nastier in terms of MOB HPs and MOB damage. LDoN also introduced a need for constant movement and due to zone design a need to be able to handle multiple pulls or an efficient way to split them.
Shorter fights mean our slow is devalued because the amount of time the MOB is slowed grows smaller compared to the amount of time the MOB is unslowed (the time spent debuffing it).
Harder hitting MOBs mean higher damage spikes and thus a need for fast, big heals.
Constant movement means less cannibalizing - other classes mana regeneration is not similarly affected.
Multiple pulls again devalue slow and add a need for group and/or fast heals.

Now, on top of that you add slow mitigation, which easily halves the effectiveness of slow, even when looking at the lowest amount of mitigation and you have several factors that, while not individually all that bad, combine to vastly reduce the amount of healing, damage prevention and mana regeneration a shaman is capable of.

Back to your statement - Yes, given sufficient gear a druid will outheal a shaman even without a slower in his group and he will add more HPs to the group and he will add mana regeneration to the group and he will deal more damage then a shaman. To counter that a shaman will have his feeble group haste - single target haste will cost too much mana and time to even halfway keep up with the druid's healing.
