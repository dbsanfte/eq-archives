Dandi
To clarify, it's not a thing you can see in your inventory. They're like character flags, something attached to your character but intangible. When you use a key, it's added to your keyring. From that point on, you will automatically be able to get past the door or zone or whatever it was requiring the key, without actually having the original physical key on your cursor. Once you zone into Sebilis with the key on your cursor, you can stick it in the bank or even delete it and will still be able to get into Sebilis just by clicking on the zone entrance. It is recommended to check that the key was added to your keyring (via the /key command that Wudoman mentioned) before you actually delete it.
Mozeley
It's actually a 'virtual' key ie something that you never physically see.

As soon as you use your key to get into somewhere like Seb, then it is added to your key ring. You can then destroy it if you want. You don't have to leave it in the bank.

ToFS has a few extra quirks. It *seems* that once you use a key on any level, it adds keys to your ring for every floor up to the highest level key you have. It also seems that using any key on any mirror, even a key that doesn't belong to that mirror, will give you all the keys you're entitled to. So if you have the key off the third level and the key off the fifth level, and click on either first floor mirror, you will now have the first, second, third, fourth, and fifth level keys on your keyring. Remember you also have to have used the key since they implemented the /key function (and possibly a while after it was first announced, since it was buggy at first.)
