Intellectual Property being one of my main concerns, here are some notes:

EULAs have been found in several states to be non-binding. They're not an agreement, they're a non-binding statement of intent, disclaimer, content warning, and iteration of copyright concerns. In particular, Microsoft EULAs have been gunned down. US copyright rights and "violent or inappropriate content warnings" are the cornerstone of the EULAs presence.

Yes, I know there's a little "agree" button. Just hear me out; I don't care about this either way, just want to make sure folks bitch and whine intelligently for a change.

Say Joe lives in NYC, and John lives in Sheepdip, MT.

If Joe buys a copy of a game, copies it electronically or on fixed medium, then re-destributes several copies, that's illegal.

If Joe buys ONE copy of a game, and transmits THAT copy of the game to John, it's legal if and only if he does not keep a copy for himself.

As EQ doesn't seem to have 64-bit or higher encryption, it doesn't seem governed by most of the software export acts. Exporting intellectual property is something of a quagmire, I'm generally concerned with books and print.

So, as long as there's one copy purchased for every copy transmitted, there's no copyright violation. Like I said, I'm unsure of the export laws.
