Last summer we see hints of Lure of Insects; a 25% lure based slow. Shaman get excited. Sure it's only 25%, but at least we can do *something* to previously unslowable mobs.

In the fall, many mobs have their MR tweaked to permit other classes to cast on them easier and the Immune to Slow flag was introduced. Where we had a Hail-Mary's chance of slowing some mobs, now we have none. Ever fight a guard in Icewell Keep? Unslowable coupled with insane cold, poison, and disease resist = shaman need not apply. This change in game mechanics makes Lure of Insects undoable.

Luclin ships and we get Plague of Insects instead -- disease based, not a lure, yet still a mere 25%. It becomes a frustrating niche spell. Often you need to cast Insidious Killme to get it to land and, honestly, the 25% you slowed that glimmer drake isn't worth the mana the cleric just spent trying to keep you alive, not to mention the ping pong match you started. Luclin is the first expansion ever that provided no damage spells at all to the shaman class when two DD's are pulled from the spell lineup.

Recently, Verant added disease counters to slow and we got excited about being able to stick one of mobs like Sontalak. But Verant makes him flat out unslowable. Apparently, adding quite a bit of aggro to the spell as well.

Unslowable is a nerf. Slowing is the shaman's thing and Verant is taking it away. When we face an unslowable mob I don't feel like a member of the team, more like a vendor hocking AGI in the grandstands. What if they go bonkers with unslowable in Planes of Power? If a level 48 coldain guard can be unslowable, what about gods?

Sorry, ranting buff monkey here.
