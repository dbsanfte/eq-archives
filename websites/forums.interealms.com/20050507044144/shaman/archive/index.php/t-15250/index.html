"Shaman" can be used as the plural, or "shamans" can be used as the plural. Both are reasonable grammaticaly.

If Shaman is used as the plural then Shaman's is the plural possessive.

If "Shamans" is used as the plural then Shamans' would be the plural possessive (even though it looks stupid) and would be pronounced "shamans-es".

This is one of the reasons I favor "Shaman" as the plural, because it makes the plural possessive easier on the eyes and ears.

All that said, one doesn't really use the plural possessive that often, so it ain't that big a deal.

Grimdreg
Not to be too much of stickler, but shaman actually is a Russian word. The Russians borrowed and slightly modified it from Evenki, the language of the nomadic Evenks of the Siberian taiga. The Evenks got it from Tocharian, which in turned borrowed it from Parkrit, which lifted it from the Sanskrit word sramah, meaning religious exersice.

Ugric languages are rooted father West than Evenki. Most member languages of the Finno-Ugric group are spoken in the Ural region, though it can be found as far North as Finland (hence Finn-Ugric.) The most prominent example of an Ugric language today is probably Hungarian, though there are several less well know people groups both East and West of the Urals that still speak an Ugric language.

You're right about shamans, however. Its an inflected word in English, so it catches most folks by surprise, cause it just don't sound right!

afreeman
Casters generally have their power vs. level curves slanted toward late game, and shammies are no exception, although I think we have one of the worst curves around.

Levels 24-34 are particularly bad, since your best heal is maybe 1 bubble of health on a tank, your dots/debuffs are expensive and seemingly useless, and your buffs have the double disadvantage of both sucking and not lasting very long. I quit my shammie at 34 for about 6 months in frustration, and I suspect that many of my contemporaries started alts during this period as well.

Seek the light at the end of the tunnel.

By 34 you will get your first dog and begin to taste the power, and by 51-52 you will have become one of the more powerful classes in the game, and will both be good at solo'ing and highly desired in groups. By 60, you'll be able to brag to guildies about solo-farming mobs for cash or loot that would take any 3 of them to kill. Even at 62, I can farm Droga and Sebilis with such ease that even necros in my guild are envious. So trust me, it may suck now, but it gets a lot better later.

BTW, look at beastlords for a class that was well-designed in that it retains consistent power throughout the level heirarchy. Compare this to druids or any melee class, and you will see how power curve balancing is one the persistent design issues in EQ, since all of these classes are too powerful in early/mid-game and too weak in late-game.
