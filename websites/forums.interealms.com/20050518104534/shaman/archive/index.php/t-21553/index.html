It is very true that there are a lot of shammy bots out there, but with playing on the Brell server there seems to be a shortage of shaman mains. I tend to spend more and more time in /role mode when farming/traveling through PoK, but that doesn't seem to stop me from getting tells for groups. I just recently broke 200 aa's and finished up the healing section, and Wow! its a substantial difference. I went from healing for 1300 to healing 1600-1700 (crit for 3.3-3.4k [often]) on heals with aa's/focus effect. I see no problem covering the main healing roll for any group role outside of GoD. (GoD is broke, lousy content) I have also noticed that the people I group with have increased "gear-wise". Elemental/Time guilds seem to be hurting on shaman attendance outside of raid times, and are picking up indies. Those indy shamans that cut the mustard on competence get repeat tells in the future and so on. I played around with different dps options in LDoN, (I had the same problem- felt useless in the dps department for quite a while) and here were some things I noticed:

1. It takes longer to build up a shaman than other classes, many of our abilities start out as sub-par, but with aa's go to avg/above avg.

2. If you want to up your dps- I think LR5/ID5 is mandatory. (you are going to get aggro at some point)

3. Get Bane / Tears of Saryrn asap [getting VP3 is also highly recommended.]

reasons:

- Tears of Saryrn seemed to solve a few issues for me. It helped regulate my mana usage better than any other nuke. Before I would use Spear of Torment (2-3 casts) where now I usually cast 1-2 ToS.
ToS has a higher resist rate for being a rain, but seems to get resisted as often as every other spell in our book so its not like we are losing out on that one.

- Bane is a good backup spell when you have too many mezzed/rooted mobs jumbled up together where your aoe would be detriemntal. I also use Bane when I need an opportunity to Canni.

- VP3 is great for mob positioning in LDoN when you need to make some room for ToS or split adds off the MT. [Side benefit: VP irritates 9 out of 10 enchanters- thumbs up for me!] [Another side benefit: when a mob somehow happens to be punted to the top of a wall- no one suspects the shaman- another thumbs up for me!]

PoP zones seem to work the same way for me as LDoN, just mobs with more hps, so I sometimes switch out nukes for dots. GoD on the other hand, is a completely different story.

Ferubi seems to be the flavor of the month on Brell- it has a decent xp rate and doesn't require flagging to access. Group make-up is pretty standard: tank, monk/bard, dps, slower, cleric, dps/cc. Same setup applies to the Sewer trials, and think the druid is hurting more on invites to these areas because a. they can't slow b. they can't rez.

Tipt/Vxed trials are another matter and mostly because you need: A. a competant chanter that is used to playing with charmed mobs and B. a decent to above avg tank. Tanks really have a hard time actually tanking the mobs if they are not sitting at around 8k unbuffed, so if they can't then the enchanter pet is tanking which tends to get ugly real quick. Class dps is neglible in the trials where you have a chanter pet, so I see mages hurting more than us. I also see rangers and druids interchangable depending on the tank's hitpoints. If the tank is at 8k or above then groups tend to grab a druid for extra healing, if the tank is below then they tend to grab a ranger for the weapon shield disc.

As for OoW, its going to have to answer a lot of complaints from a varied customer base in order to be successful:

- A brand-new fun timesink: titles or the "quest system" might do it.

- New, upper tier, finished, un-craptastic, raid content that kicks the GoD guilds out of time with viable upgrades for equivalent risk.

- New content for the casual player: "quest system" might do it.

Its a lot to live up to for one expansion that seems to be rushed, but I will stick around to see what happens.
