Long post from an ex-monk.

The class actually is pretty messed up. The mitigation nerf is only a tiny part. Several fundamental changes have, like some norrathian spotted owl, displaced this class. Please bear with me while I try to describe what I've watched transpire over the last two years.

Pre-Velious, monks had crappy armor, healthy innate AC and avoidance skills, the best weapons, and excellent DPS. They made fantastic partners for shaman – killed things fast and, against a 75% slowed mob, took mild damage. We had crappy heals and they had crappy HP’s. Synergy.

Velious came out and monk quest armor was still junk. NToV was a different story. Verant went Monty Hall on All/All gear with huge AC and HP’s. Monks began to close the gap with tanks. If you didn’t need /disc defensive or taunt, monks made decent light tanks for experience grinding. This was not a terrible thing, IMO.

Luclin ships and Verant treats us to boss mobs with insane attack ratings and just silly amounts of HP’s. With attack that high AC becomes nearly moot. It’s going to hit hard. Some guilds began to use monks as rampage tanks since they had the best chance of sidestepping the attack. Other classes begin to get worked up. Tanks feel threatened. Melees are disgusted at well equipped monks with fungis they see soloing non-stop in Velk’s. Let’s admit it – it’s really easy to dislike monks. The class attracts a lot of arrogant jerks and feign death is the ultimate griefing tool. We’ve all had mobs dropped in our laps from some iksar named Slamfoo Kickinutz.

The next big change – NPC Casters. These guys used to be pretty stupid, never flipping beyond page one of their spell books. Overnight they become deadly. For a few days players are slaughtered with high level nukes from low level mobs. Thankfully they turned it down a notch.

Then came the resist change. It’s hard to describe but it used to be your innate resists gave you pretty good partial resists but levels didn’t help much -- greens could root you. Afterwards, greens landed fewer spells but your innate resists became less effective against mobs in your level range. Nukes hit hard.

These two changes made splitting way more fatal for monks. Got of bunch of corpses somewhere nasty? Monks used to be the answer but now they just add another body. Doesn’t matter at all in PoP -- graveyards.

October 2002 brought the manaburn nerf, the modrod nerf, and the monk mitigation nerf. SoE spelled out the first two in great detail, but only made vague comments about the last. They didn’t want monks to have the high AC gotten from All/All gear but they didn’t want to go and tweak a ton of items already owned by players of every class. Their solution? Give monks an inherent, hidden, AC penalty. Monks become the only class with a mitigation handicap.

Setting aside the actual impact on gameplay for a moment – monks psychologically feel both crippled and punished. They worked just as hard as any other class for their items. Why should their equipment give less return on investment as the SAME ITEM worn by another? It was demoralizing.

Now, back to the actual effect. It appears (from testing, because SoE wouldn’t say squat) that monks were given an AC penalty of around –200. What did this do? The high end monks with 1450 AC now had an effective AC of 1250 and tanked pretty much like they always did. So the nerf missed it’s mark. Who did it hit? Low end monks. The player with 800 AC who now has 600. You folks know what losing 200AC at lower levels will do. With better gear and a ton of AA's, it can be surmounted.

PoP comes out. SoE wants to try a completely different approach than Luclin and gives us mobs with less hitpoint but hit like Mack trucks. This killed monk soloing anyway. It killed shaman tanking IN TEH FACE too (ok, some still do but rare). It gave warriors back their jobs. Then the warriors lost their job to knights because of aggro on demand. Sorry guys. Monks, who have no taunt and (usually) lack the mitigation and hitpoints, are delegated to the job of rogue-light. Get behind it and press attack.

PoP gameplay turns out to be pretty shallow. Pull kill. Pull kill. Ala Dreadlands.

Instead of using monks to split, it’s way faster and convenient to let the enchanter, or druid, or ranger, or bard, or cleric, or paladin pacify it. Splitting used to be an art form where your senior guild monks would teach the younger ones the trade. People used to be impressed by tough split. What was skill is now just mana. Bards, not monks, were given a 100% memory wipe skill making them the ultimate puller.

PoP also gave all melees access to some pretty incredible weapons. You can get a 50/44 Windblade on my server for 18K. It would cost me 90K to get something comparable. Remember how I said that Velious blurred the line between tank and monk AC? PoP has blurred the line on DPS as well.

The last aspect is just dissention. Many players perceive monks as gimped or whiners -- either way they aren't as welcome in groups. The monk boards have been filled with complaining for ten months. Even within the class people can’t agree. There’s a ton of bad feelings surrounding monks. You know those “what partner?” threads we see every other week? Two years ago it was monk, monk, monk! Today, shaman have written them off.

I don’t ask anyone to agree absolutely with my assessment. The only thing I can tell you is I miss the days when being a monk was fun. It’s not fun anymore so I put him away.

Regards,
Barm
