Of all the improvements I'd like to see in the pre-65 Shaman, my short list includes:

Make the class-specific Alchemy worth doing.
Simply decreasing the cost of ingredients would go along way. Why should potions that trivial over 200 (a skill reachable only by a level 63 Shaman) and only decrease mana consumption by 5% cost 127p per dose to make? I pay 50p every three-four hours to an Enchanter who would have to be mathematically challenged to turn around and buy most any potion I could offer. I especially want affordable potions that don't duplicate our primary buff lines. Shaman provided affordable mana preservation would be my favorite.

Faster mana regeneration or decreased mana usage.
Whether with Canni 2 and Stoicism or Canni 4 and Torpor it's hard to hold a conversation as a Shaman. Canni dancing constantly until you can afford a drogmar beats the heck out of not having mana. But in a game that's fundamentally social, making the choice between talking and being ready for the next fight is awkward at best. Anything that speeds regeneration or decreases mana cost is welcome. Even being able to afford to use our own potions would help, but it's not like we'd have the buff slots for them anyway.

The ability to ever become reliably invisible.
Wasting an hour on a completely un-necessary rez, cussing SOE the whole time, has never been my kind of entertainment. For those with foraging friends who can stomach LDoN, at least the new potion's available. Otherwise, getting the swamp vine component in the Bazaar makes each dose cost 100-150p. That's more than the rez. So much for mystical Shaman alchemists supplying Norrath with a fixed duration invisibility potion folks might actually want. An AA that removed the randomness of spells like invisibility or a new spell somewhere in the 50s would be welcome.

One soft DoT or a Root that works.
We suffer through not ever getting any form of snare (unless we can use the Iksar clicky, if I'm not mistaken), so is it too much to ask for a root that works? If I'd wanted to kite I would have played a Necro, Druid or Bard. Once I can finally afford my Scaled Avatar's Leggings and can at least get the spell gem back it won't matter. Until then one of eight slots is wasted to the emergency-only "root so I can gate" spell. OK, so that's a slight exaggeration. I'd be overjoyed with an AA that removed the DD component of my DoTs, or better yet replaced it with even a short duration snare.

Not having to look forward to worthlessness.
Having (euphemistically) "donated" for my share of Temperance, KEI, Blessing of the Nine and Virtue, I've always resented Focus of the Seventh, let alone all its baby predecessors. I'll get my MGB AA, but it would sure be nice for Fo7 to last long enough so that I could MGB a group version of Ferine Avatar or a group heal in between.

Cragael
Spirit of Cheetah was supposed to be an escape spell, like Divine Aura, Harmshield or Feign Death, not a travel spell. Of course, it never worked. 10 minutes recast time, overwrite SOW, short duration, interruptible... whoever try to use it as an escape spell has to sacrifice a spell slot for a spell that, if they can cast it, will leave them crawling in short order. So nobody ever did once they fixed the bug that made spells auto-refresh on zoning.

You want to fix cheetah? Make it insta-cast (so not interruptible), give it a normal recast and make it so it doesn't overwrite SOW. With that, you can even *shorten* the duration and it will still be a useful spell that shaman can use to 'sprint' away at the cost of draining their mana.

Another thing that's pre-60, annoying and should be fix? Our self-buffs. The problem? They didn't start as self-buff but as another attempt at providing a 'safety net' spell for the shamans. the original spells of the 'Rage' line use to drain your mana while enormously boosting your HP regen, some stats and AC. But when they introduced the paladin's line of mana-for-hp spell (with Velious?) they screwed up ours to uselessness (the spell would stop when we ran out of mana, which didn't make it very useful as a last ditch kinda thing). So they removed the mana-for-hp component, added some stats and proudly announced that shamans now had a line of uber stats self-buffs...

Except those cretins, in their typical slap-dash approach, forgot to remove one little side effect: casting those spells also blanks out your spell gems for 30 seconds. In the original line, it was so you could just cast the spell, then use up the remaining of the mana before the spell drained it up, effectively sacrificing your spellcasting abilities for better melees. As a self-buffs, it's just freaking irritating, especially since they kept the ridiculously short duration of the spell untouched (compare the 20 minutes duration of the level 58 VoB to the *2:24 HOURS* of the clerics' BAotR, by example). So another fix begging to happen would be to remove the spell gem blank out effect and raise the duration to something decent... considering how useless the stats are anyway, it's at best a glorified self-AC spell so it's not like it's going to be a immense powerboost.

On the subject of mind boggling annoyance built into our spell... why the hell do our AC spell have that annoying 12 seconds recast? Is there somehow a balance issue with shaman chain-casting mediocre AC spells on people? You would think those spells had a CH component to them or something.
