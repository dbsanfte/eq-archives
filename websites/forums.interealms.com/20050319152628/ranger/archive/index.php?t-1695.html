I was in my early teens when the Seattle music explosion happened. Truth be told I had heard of bands like Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Alice in Chains, and Soundgarden before they became popular, and I loved them. Then the "Seattle scene" started to grow because of MTV beginning to play the "Smells Like Teen Spirit" video. It just snowballed from there and all the other bands from the area got popular as a result of it. I loved it all, and to this day I still frequently listen to AiC and Soundgarden, and several other bands from that era/area. Alice in Chains' music really personifies my teenage years, and I still consider 'Dirt' to be one of my all time favorite albums.

This kind of hits me hard, because he was one of my favorite singers. Another of my favorite singers died of a heroin overdose several years ago as well (Bradley Nowell of Sublime), and it's always sad to see the same cycle of drug use repeated in anyone.

I wonder if Jerry Cantrell will keep the band going, or reform the band with the other guys like he did on his solo album...

/me puts Dirt in the CD player
