There is a tome tucked away in the library of the Plane of Knowledge, it is hidden among the least interesting and most mundane of subjects analyzing the correct and proper etiquette in addressing the Nobility of the Combine Empire. The tomes have been left alone for many years, all save one, that keeps getting "misplaced" here to be forgotten as all the others.

The book is small, about the size of an grown man's palm, and plain with no adornment, though it is made of fine leather and parchment. On the spine of the book etched in the leather, is the title "The Oathtakers"

In a tight orderly script, the etiquette of taking a formal oath is described, some where however, about two thirds of the way down the page, a prophecy begins with the words

"Years will pass between the turning of the first star and the last, and the dark one will think he has won, but when the final star turns to a blood red hue, the War of the Oathtakers has begun.

One will be strong of the northern clime, the other a child of the rain, the third born of privilege will forsake it in time, when property is brought to life..."

The passage is immediately followed with the correct phrasing of allegiance to the King depending on rank and relationship. In the margin of the book is a date some 17 years in the past, and the words "The first Oathtaker has been born".

"I see that you have received my present dear." Her mother nodded to the unsealed scroll in her daughters hand.

Estoria,

I am sorry but I am not strong enough to go with him. I can not let him take me, though I know that I belong to him now. They only way that I can stop him is in death, and I choose that over life as his whore.

Nina did not wake up. She did not die either, but stayed in a perpetual slumber, where she would toss and turn whimpering over and over again how she was sorry, so very sorry.

Estoria had her clothes sent from the manor house to the Temple, where she did not move from her friends side for the 19 days that passed between her own birthday, and that of Nina's.

Once, her mother had tried to come down and talk to her, but she never got more then her daughter's name before Estoria's glare drove her from the room and the temple. Kissing Nina on the cheek, Estoria closed a parchment into Nina's sleeping hand, and whispered to her a simple phrase, "Nina, you are free."

A small smile crossed Nina's features in her sleep, and her breathing became peaceful and quiet. Estoria was certain that Nina's soul had heard her and felt the bonds release.

She knocked on a bleak yet ostentatious door, and old and sourly manservant answered eyeing her suspiciously.

"What do you want?" The servant answered unpleasantly.

"I wish to see Mr Collins, it is about repayment for a slave that he bought, but that was 'damaged' before she could be delivered." Estoria gritted her teeth through the last, but the manservant did not notice the distaste for the words she chose, and he showed her to a receiving room that was equally as dark, and stern.

"And who shall I say is calling?" the servant asked.

"Estoria, will be all the name that he needs." she replied.

Estoria fingered the sharp dagger like comb that held her hair in its place, taking special care not to prick herself with it while she waited. She had dressed in her most provocative, yet appropriate dress, hoping that her anger would enable her to complete her ruse.

She did not have to wait long, as the manservant returned quickly and requested that she follow him into the Mr. Collin's private study. The servant knocked once, and opened the heavy door to a room with many odd assortments of knives and daggers, animal pelts and bones. It was the study of a person who liked the more morbid and painful side of life, and it was just as Estoria imagined Mr. Collins would have.

The servant left quickly closing the door behind him, and Estoria crossed the room towards the desk where Mr. Collins was seated, steeling herself for the coming charade.

"Estoria, it is so very good to see you." Mr Collins said in his most pleasant and oilily voice. "I only wish that it were among better circumstances."

Estoria nodded curtly, and boldly moved in next to Mr. Collins, leaning into him and offering her hand to be kissed.

"Yes, it is a shame, but I come to rectify that I hope. I come to see if there is possibly a replacement that can be sent?" Estoria remained close as she said this hoping that she was subtle enough, and yet forward enough that Mr Collins would think her but a foolish young girl whom he could manipulate into promising something she would not want to keep.

"You see, Nina was really but a replacement for one already, one of such soft beauty and nobility, that I dare never ask for the privilege of spending a night with her, let alone it be granted."

"Yes," Mr Collins smiled to himself, as he sat back down at his desk, the fast acting poison that Estoria had introduced into his system began to take effect. "Until that night."
