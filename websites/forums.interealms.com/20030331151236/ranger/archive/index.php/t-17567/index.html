loKase
Well, I was one of those crazy people and it truly was an excellent experience. If you are new to EQ, have a Ranger and want to improve your ability to play the game do all of the Ivy Etched quests.

I started at level 27 and was able to do the bracers on my own.

I definatly needed help with Meldrath in Steamfont - one nasty little caster.

Maldyn in Rathe Mountains is an elusive (rare) spawn but fairly easy to take down.

Windwalker in OOT was also a tough spawn, but once you figure out the technique for making him spawn it is not that bad.

Slathe the Slayer in Lower Guk was not hard to kill but the 3 Minataurs that guard him are nasty and its hard to find someone on the light side to help you as most higher lvl peeps are in the dark side (luke I am your father...)

The hardest part of the full set of armour were the boots - alot of faction work and you need 2 Granite drops from Mistmoore. I finally figured out the camp in mistmoore after dying many times.

All in all these quests produced some of the most exciting fights I have had in the game. Apart from getting some nice armour these quests really teach you how to play EQ effectivly from researching the Mobs, to forming a game plan to gearing yourself appropriatly for the camps. I am now 47 and am working on the Tolan's armour now (anyone have an extra BP - cheap - lol) but I still am wearing a couple of the Ivy Etched pieces. If for nothing else the Ivy Etched makes for good resist gear for raiding.

Hope you have fun and smack Meldrath upside the head for me when you see him. :)
