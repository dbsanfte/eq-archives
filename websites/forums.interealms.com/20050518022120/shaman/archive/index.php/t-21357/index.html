Now I know most of you have retired, in the process of retiring, or are busy doing other things. I haven't seen too many posts dealing with thoughts of the new expansion, so I figured I would post some thoughts.

-Increasing the level cap to 70

This might actually be a good thing because it breaks down the raid requirement for a lot of old content. Yes, it trivializes old content which may annoy some of the casual player wanting uber items. Bottom line, if items dropping from trivial content is better than what you are wearing- its a good thing.

SOE will take care of viable zones to level to 70 because everyone has a right to level to 70, so we have nothing to worry about with viable xp. If its too hard, SOE will nerf it.

-Spells

Unlike GoD, we should see a significant amount of spells for the new expansion for all classes. I expect we will see a system similar to the PoP style of parchments. They will probably be tradeable in some form because there will be entirely too many spells to get out to the populous if they are no-drop.

-Spells (shaman specific)

Spells we will probably see will probably be:
-new Focus/Stat buffs (you know they are going to raise the stat
cap)
-new dog-dog (they haven't stopped that spell line, so expect a
new one [FYI: check your spirit call aa's after patch- make sure
they raise the pet level]
-new dots/nukes
-new haste (they gave us a group haste before- expect them to try
to sneak a "group SLTW" as the "new" upgrade)
-new utility spells (imbue x, regen, etc.)

I am curious how they will handle our GoD spells with the new
expansion. It is a little early to trivialize GoD spells, so I am
wondering if we will see a new heal/canni/torpor clone/slow.

To be honest, I doubt we will see anything revolutionary in
terms of spells right away. Reasons are:

-E3, not sure on the date of this and how much resources will be
pulled for it.
-Developers dealing with making GoD something other than "Faces
of Death XVIII"
-releasing of the new expansion and fixing it after it comes out.
(Releases are never perfect for any game- let alone EQ, so expect
resources focusing on fixing DTing Quest NPC's that only respond
to gnome rogues on every fifth Sunday when the moon is full.)

So, I wouldn't expect too many changes until after OoW release, it
looks like SOE is juggling too many balls right now. Don't blame
Brenlo for it- he is the PR guy. Blame SOE for trying to do too
much in a small amount of time.

-Casual Content

- Expect a line of semi-easy to complete (read: Ornate 2.0) line of
gear. Gear will probably be on level with the current Elemental.
:Note: semi-easy means some sort of flag/checkpoint list to
complete.

-Raid Content

-This should be interesting because they have already specified
new high-end raiding content. The question is how they will treat
GoD in this regard- will it be reworked and required as a form of
progression or will it be thrown to the wayside like LoY, LDoN raid
content? My guess is that GoD will become the new Vex Thal-
nice place to upgrade, but not necessarily required for OoW
progression.

-Difficulty

-This should be interesting to see how the Devs work this. Many
players today signed on to EQ after PoP, so they didn't see how
difficult Tier 1 planes were for the Thurg geared tanks. Casters
should have an easier time due to hitpoints not being as important
as your tank classes. Casual players may just shrug off the
content in lue of PoP being a more accessible form of xp. (Anyone
remember people leveling past 60 in Velks?)

-AA's

-I expect we will received at least 2 "pages" of aa's similar to what
we recieved in PoP. The first page will be stat enhancements to
combat the new stat increases, while the scond one will tailor to
abilities. AA's I think we will see:

-Lightning Reflexes/Innate Defense Mastery (tanks are going to
need it.)
-Healing Adept/Healing Gift Mastery
-Burning Affliction Mastery (devs will probably rework the aggro
issues of dots or there will be a lot of necros/shamans main-
tanking mobs over 1k dot crits.)
-Natural Durabilty Mastery

After this I dunno, we will probably get 2-3 toy/pointless aa lines
because the devs ran out of ideas, but expect a few new tricks
as well.

-Itemization

-Hopefully the devs will go back to raid named having 8-12 items on
their loot table. There is a lot of hybrid gear/one class usuable gear
rotting in GoD, and calling the current loot tables "tiny" is an
understatement.

Overall, this was a long post and I tried to come off as objective as possible. OoW looks to be an expansion for everyone, not just a second attempt at getting the "Time guilds" out of Time. I will be buying the expansion because it looks to have enough toys to keep me occupied at least a little longer, and I think I will get more enjoyment out of it than I have with GoD.

P.S. For those farmers/alt-twinkers, (I only say this because there are quite a few of you) get it before it gets cold. Trivialized content will be seen by SOE as trivial, so that nerf bat will start swinging about a month or two after OoW comes out. The nerf bat is usually a stealth one, and they tend to target drop rates as opposed to difficulty. Examples of this are/were PoHate,PoGrowth gem drop rate; Kael Arena armor drop rate; LDoN xp/cash drop rate....you get the point.

Predicus
What about a 5% increase in slow if not more to encourage the necessaties of shamans in a group again?

Because slowing ability is flawed with the evolving world of EQ. Basically EQ revolves around a few constants; one being that your dps/cc/healing ability has to out-weigh the dps of a mob. You also have to set a mobs dps in increments, so that 1. the main tank has be able to survive the initial dps of a mob, and 2. A mob has to still be challenging after it is slowed. Here is where the developers hit a problem- a full slow on a mob drastically changes the output of dps. They choose to add mitigation to allow them to further a mob's dps, without hitting the point where a mob could 1-round a tank. This worked for a while- we see a lot of mobs in PoP, GoD, and LDoN hard with mitigated slow. Unfortunately, it is only a matter of time before they will just have to make mobs immune to slow, so they can continue to up the difficulty. Percents in an evolving game are bad, that's why we are having trouble healing- because it's based off a percent of the cleric's ability to heal- the same applies to our slow.
There are a few abilities in the game currently that are "over-powering" as far as the devs are concerned, and I think they are slowly trying to phase them out. The three abilities that come to mind are: slow, snare, and charm. Slow and Charm have already been discussed, but many people overlook snare. Snare changes a caster's ability from only having time to cast a really fast nuke to being able to cast: long cast time dots, long cast time aoe's, long cast time charm spells. Druids/Wizards would have never been able to quad-kite without snare- there just isn't enough time to land anything. Verant/SOE tried to nerf this by adding the "you have been summoned" to mobs, but that wasn't enough. That is why we are seeing more and more mobs "uneffected by run-speeds." SOE should be running into a problem soon as more and more "utilities" become over-powered. Adding more classes defeats the purpose if in the end are classes are nerfed to the points to where we become clones of one another.
