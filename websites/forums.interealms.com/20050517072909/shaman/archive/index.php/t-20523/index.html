Quiggle
Farming Vxed seems to be the best way to go for spells. This is how we did it:

Shaman (you)
Druid
Enchanter (A good one who knows how to charm and isn't afraid to haste his pet)
Cleric
Warrior (highly geared hybrids might work, but I don't recommend trying it)
Bard

Get the adventure, or if you're not flagged, have someone who is to get it for you. Have the Bard or Druid zone in to check on names. The Trickster and Umbiki are close to each other, both drop spells and some pretty nice loots. If these names aren't up, wait 5 minutes and get a new adventure. Other names, like the Ukun Bloodbeast and 1 other one, can't be pulled to the zoneline and are considerably harder for the bard to pull. I recommend getting adventures until either the Trickster or Umbiki are on track.

Once you get an adventure with a named or 2, set up your group at the zone in and have your bard start clearing up the right side. The second pull will usually be a pooka for the chanter pet. Once you get your pet, you'll be killing faster than the bard can pull.

About 10 mobs or so later, you'll get to Umbiki or his placeholder. The Trickster is in the cave to the right after Umbiki. He's surrounded by pookas, all of them are lullable, and he will come single if you lull the two on either side of him.

When you're ready to pull either named, have the bard pull with HoS/slow. If his slow sticks, your job is to spam heals. If it doesn't, have him re-HoS, you malos and your slow should stick. The tank should use defensive disc on names. With the hasted pet, the named should go down in about 15 seconds.

Umbiki rampages, so you should concentrate on healing whoever's on ramp. The Trickster doesn't ramp, so just spam heals on the MT, although you shouldn't need it with a cleric and druid healing.

So far, we've gotten about 4 plate legs, 2 earrings, 2 enchanter spells, one BL spell and one Daluda's Mending. Once you kill the names, you can either wait for repops and hope for another named, or zone out and get a new adventure.

-Quiggle

Just as a side note - you don't need a tracker necessarily to check spawns. A rogue or bind sight works great too :D

Also - a lot of clerics don't know this, but it's a good idea to have a stun loaded for when the chanterpet drops. It buys them time in remezzing and often saves the chanter's life. Torpor/Stoicism is a great tool for keeping the pet slightly subdued during downtime. It snares it (if you're snarerererless) and slows it down a bit, buying the chanter even more time to recover the pet.
