Frankly, it's slow that has been our problem for a long time. I'd be willing to give it up forever. Or trade it for anything at all.

Better healing, more melee DPS, more spellcasting DPS in terms of DOT'ing or nuking, whatever--anything but more stat buffs, which even imbeciles have known since Kunark are at best borderline useful when they matter at all.

Slow is the thing that is always used to balance us against the entire rest of the game and the other classes, and frankly, right now it's our biggest downfall, not our biggest asset. Slows are mitigated or sometimes mobs can't be slowed, and if we can get slows on, mobs die so fast that they only get on the mob toward the end of its lifespan anyway. Slows can be resisted too.

And mitigating slows makes the slows of enchanters and beastlords effectively the same as ours. Nobody includes a shaman in groups anymore for slows specifically; they include us if they want extras like our lousy healing because they already have enough DPS, if they include us at all. Which, if they're smart, they don't. The only real reason to bring us along for slows is that they have no other choice of halfway decently played or geared beastlord or enchanter online at the moment. Our best asset has become NO REASON TO INVITE US!

That means our best asset is no real asset at all.

And yet it is the defining thing about a shaman, and the excuse given to give us no upgrades or trivial ones, not just for the least couple of years, but in perpetuity from this point onward. Don't think it's going to change. Slow will NEVER have the importance in the game again that it used to because the function of slows in EQ has fundamentally changed. Slows are now a "helper" spell, not a vital need. Replace a shaman with another cleric or druid and you'll do fine without the slows.

So shamans will NEVER be balanced properly in this game again, because slows do not function as just another utility for shamans, the way they do for beastlords or chanters. They are their core asset and singular defining ability. Turning a core asset into a mere utility trivializes slows, and by extension, shamans themselves. You can't trivialize the spell without trivializing the class that is constructed solely around it, and will continue to be balanced around it forever as it if were still vital rather than a mere utility, doable by another number of other classes who can also do a great deal more of significance besides.

Chanters and beastlords, if they had to depend only on slows for groups, would be screaming bloody murder. So it's no surprise if lots of us do, too. It would be foolish not to.

They can have slows. Anybody can. Clerics want it? You got it bud. Even though you don't need it, you love to bring it up when we mention how crappy our healing is. Well, take it.

IT'S NOT DOING US ANY GOOD.

I want a playable class, not an endless argument over whether slow is so damn powerful and by exactly how incredibly, world-shakingly much. Predicus
A successful group relies on one thing:

-DPS of group > DPS of mob

That being said, all EQ classes bring a total of 4 things to a group:

1. DPS
2. Increasing group dps
3. Mitigating mob dps
4. Healing

Some classes cover all four areas, many don't. Slow, Cripple, Mez while seperate skills all fall under number 3. EQ developers design content where you can only do any of the 4 things to a set amount. It wouldn't matter if shaman's were given a spell called "improved cripple" or "summon crap"- if it mitigates mob dps, it does the same thing (to the percent that game developers choose)

Now, if SOE decided to remove slow from our spell line-up- they have four choices on what to give us. Swapping out a banana for something that looks,feels, and tastes like a banana is.... still a banana.

If they choose to give us something else instead then we become just like another class- just with different spell names/effects. We do not become a druid- just heal/dps like one with the ability to cast haste/FA.

The point I guess I am trying to make is: Slow is an aspect and a detriment to further changes simply because it falls into one of the four categories. Taking Slow away would require SOE to replace it for a similar effect or improve other effects. Any other utility that does not fall into those 4 categories is "useless" for the group equation.

Example: Druid's Evac ability

if Group DPS > Mob DPS then evac is not needed

if Group DPS < Mob DPS then evac is needed, but the group is a "failure"
