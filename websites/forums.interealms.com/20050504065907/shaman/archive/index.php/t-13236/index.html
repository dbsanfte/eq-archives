Shaman (lvls 56 - 60) with:

Ranger (lvls 52 - 57) Works well. Good Dps, buffs, snare, and tracking. Starting to get limited due to lack of Ranger tanking ability. Ranger starting to need more heals.

Actually, if you're 2-boxing with a shaman, it's a very good test. Rangers can buff HP, AC, ATK and damage shield, all of which stack with shaman buffs, and they can buff the shaman too. It's an advantage the monk won't have in a duo, and hence, in my opinion, a valid comparison to make.

However, either of those classes would be a good partner for a shaman. They can both can dish out the damage and can both tank well enough for the duo. The monk has the edge on damage dealing, and the ranger has the edge on getting agro and utility.

I 2boxed a shaman and ranger till both got to 62. It was a good combination. I spent a lot of time in UP, where the ranger could tank any of the common mobs (after it was slowed) and a few of the named: gantru dralkenr, gantru raldukan, fallen sergeant. In fact, it was a very good combination to hunt down these named for loot. Track, obviously, was invaluable for this, but the instant camo aa (which I had gotten just for this purpose) helped a great deal as well. It's far easier to find and pull a mob in UP with an instant camo ranger than a FD'ing monk. I spent a LOT of time in UP farming named and was able to get some nice gear for both my shaman and my ranger.

But then I started spending more time in PoP. I CAN 2box this combo with the trash mob frogs and larvae in Valor, but the xp is NOT as fast as in a full group in any of the camps. So now, my shaman is 65 and ranger is stuck at 62--it's much easier to find groups with my shaman and I haven't really tried to foist the ranger on a group that invites me.

More recently, because I hit 65 and don't really 2box the ranger much any more, I decided to sell a lot of his gear that I had gotten through the months that I'd spent in UP to get Quiescence. I know I wouldn't have been able to do without his ability to both track and the instant camo aa, but at the same time I regret all the time and effort that I've put into him.

Now, I've even been wishing that I would have instead rolled a pally to 2box, but not just to 2box by themselves. A shaman/pally combo (of high enough level) is a solid base for an xp group in Valor or Storms. Shaman can handle almost all healing responsibilities. Pally can buff well enough to get a group by, can tank, keep aggro, has a great stun at 62, and has nice group heals for emergencies. Both can ghetto CC. Now just add your friends or guildies who aren't that sought after shaman/chanter/cleric by other groups.

Don't forget a 60 lvl ranger can /disc weaponshield. Of course, it's only once every 66 minutes or so, but for 20 seconds, he will parry every attack from the front, which always gave my shaman time to root one, slow the other, split em with ranger and then slow the second. Keep root on the first with shaman and everything stays under control.

Weaponshield is awesome. It's sometimes surprising how many people don't understand how it works. A ranger doesn't even need aggro to use it, so long as the mob is in front of him AND is pointed in the ranger's direction. It's great to give a shaman time to get a slow in on a hard hitting boss mob without getting flattened from a resist.
