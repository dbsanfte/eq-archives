I wouldn't look at this question in terms of raw power, if I were you. Is your friend going to want to get up and smack the mob around? Beastlord is the only choice in that case. I have to agree with what a few others have said, THE deciding factor on what character your friend should try is your friend’s play style. It took me trying about six different classes before I decided to play a druid as my main. Then I played a ranger because I found myself trying to tank with my druid. After both characters hit level 51 I ended up putting them on the shelf, for reasons not worth going into here, and I started playing my shaman. I also have a level 54 beastlord for the same reason I created my ranger. I like playing both a caster and a tank, depending on my mood. With two main characters I can switch off depending on how I feel at the time. So your friend should try to figure out if he prefers to tank or to stand back and cast. Or perhaps like me he’ll end up liking to do both.

The beastlord is a good class. One advantage of the class is that the pet is so powerful that what gear you have isn’t very important. And this is a good thing because there is a huge gap in the price of gear. You can outfit a beastlord in decent low to lower mid level gear for a reasonable amount of money. But then there’s a gap the size of the Grand Canyon if you want to buy any upgrades. Prices, at leas on my server, jump from about 500pp for a weapon to about 15000 for the next upgrade. But as I said, the BL pet is powerful enough to make up the difference until you can manage to buy or loot better equipment.

I’ve never played mage but I’ve grouped with them and have a lot of respect for them. They have a variety of pets, which is nice and I know at high levels their pets become very powerful. And one of their earth pet can snare, which is extremely helpful. That’s one of the drawbacks to soloing with a beastlord, no snare or root. And of course all the summoned items mages have at their disposal are extremely useful. But don’t expect to live for long if you start getting hit.

I’ve only played my necromancer to level 20, but one of the people I regularly grouped with played a necromancer to level 63. And I must say I’m incredibly impressed with the class for both soloing and in groups. If you have a necromancer in your group who knows how to play his character, especially at higher levels, it makes killing mobs so much easier. Not only can they lifetap for huge amounts of damage, but they can cast group healing spells when there’s a mob to suck the life from and they can cast group manna regen if you are facing a caster mob. They can also keep a couple of mobs stunned or fear them if adds aren’t a worry. They can slow and charm undead and dire charm them with AAs. They can even pull for a group and feign death and let the tank grab them. And if mobs are too tough to tank fear kiting can work great in many areas.

There are a few down sides to necromancers. As a caster they can’t take many hits. But they have feign death that can easily keep you alive if you learn to use it properly. The class is evil, so you have to deal with being KOS in many cities. But if you should want to try to reduce that somewhat you can choose a gnome necromancer and be a bit less hated in many places. Or you can say to heck with it and just kill everything you come across. *grins* And for some reason necromancers often have trouble getting groups. I chalk this up to ignorance, as I’ve groped with many necromancers and know from personal experience that they can be awesome in a group. But it can be a problem if you have to put up the LFG flag very often.

Best of luck to both you and your friend. I’m sure you’ll have fun with whatever class your friend picks.

Uggok
Pre-PoP, you can't go wrong with any of them.

You are not going to really want to duo by tanking in PoP unless you are with a real tank class. You can, but it won't be that efficient. If she cannot beat on the mob, most of the BL's damage is gone. You will be looking at root dotting or kiting or charming.

Root-dotting, the necro hands-down obliterates any other choice.

Kiting, the necro is also the best choice, because they bring snare to the table. It is possible to full-speed kite (shammy gets lots of aggro and the BL and pet attack or the mage pet attacks and the mage nukes) with SoW or a fast horse. But it is more dangerous, more annoying, and requires a lot more space (and can therefore be done in fewer places).

That leaves charming. BL's cannot charm, so they are out. As far as PoP xp zones go, Mages can charm in BoT, Necro's in CoD. Zone advantage probably goes to the mage here, since there is better loot to be had in the former zone. If you are into slumming/farming, the necro probably has a wider variety of places to charm overall.

Seems like Necro is the best choice unless all you want to do is farm BoT for xp and loot.

Also, consider utility. Mage focii and coh and cannipetting are nice. Beastlord crack is OK, but the beastlord's biggest utility skill -- slow -- is redundant in a shaman duo. Necro has a _ton_ of neat tricks -- FD, snare, summon corpse, res, instaclick invis, innate undead invis, harmshield, etc etc -- that makes them not only great pullers in tricky spots, but a nice Swiss army knife to have around all the time.
