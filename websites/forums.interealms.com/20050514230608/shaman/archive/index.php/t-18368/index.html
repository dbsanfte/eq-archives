Baz4
I play a troll shaman, and I sometimes felt the impact of the exp penalty. I was curious, so I checked it out, and the total impact is roughly 4 or 5 AA's, as near as I can tell.

Using an old exp calculator at http://www.geocities.com/sstair/EverQuest/ I figured it took a troll shaman 100, 440,000 more exp points to finish up to the end of level 60 than it took a Barb shaman.

One AA is supposedly (roughly) equal to doing level 52 (rumor oft repeated, not trying to say that is a fact). I used a human warrior on the calculator to see that level 52 took 23,396,670 to complete level 52, without any class or race xp penalties (the calculator still uses class penalties, so I chose a warrior to minimize this effect).

Therefore the exp penalty up to completion of level 60 is a little more than 4 AA points (100 million divided by 24 milion). To reach level 65 would had a little more, so maybe (at most) one more AA point.

so - not "100 AA points" but not insignificant, either. Figure the Barb buying Canni 5 when the Shaman buys MC3, if all else was exactly equal.

At 62 as a frog its about 2-3 kills per 1% reg. exp in a group setting at tier2 or higher. Using a 15% penalty on the troll say i kill 300 mobs in a group setting for each level at 62+, therefore the the troll has to kill 345 for each of his levels. That means that the troll has to kill 1380 mobs between level 62 to 65, and I have to kill 1200. That's a difference of 180 tier2 type mobs. Average net AA from 180 tier2 mobs at 2% each and you get roughly 3.5AAs.

Flattening the curve to timespent, say I spend 1 day played per level on average. A troll will have to play almost 10 more days group/xping the same way I do. Not a bad tradeoff if you like trolls.
