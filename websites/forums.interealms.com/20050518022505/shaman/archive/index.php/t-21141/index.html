Thunderwolfjr
Simple way to look at things:

1. Play the class that supports the playstyle you enjoy.
2. Do you want to sit or stand.

In other words, do you want to be a caster that supports the group and tries not to get hit, or do you want to be a melee that will be in the mix most of the time.

Personally, it depends on my mood. For the most part I want to be a caster, hanging back, keeping tabs on the battlefield, and doing what is needed to help the group. But, sometimes I just want to kill stuff with my hands, so I switch to one of my melee alts (Pally or BST). Usually, a couple of hours of melee and I get it out of my system.

To be honest, if I had to do over and had not put a year into the shaman (and a bunch of no-drop equipment), I'd go with the BST. Most of the casting power (except poor self healing), good dps plus a great pet.

Solo wise:

At younger levels a BST is not all that hard to play. The ability to play cleric to your pet works out just fine. I would say it is easier then to play a BST.

Towards the End game the shaman's ability to heal himself and manage his mana in an on-going battle allows to win some pretty hard fights.

The problem with BSTs is when they solo they need to kill the mob fast because healing isn't an option all the time unless you can fire your one paragon in the 12 minute time it take to refresh. Also trying to healing your pet over and over at the upper levels is to mana costly and to risky with the casting time on the heal.


Group wise:

Both shaman and BST take different roles in a group. Thou BST can still slow. They don't provide healing. And often haste draining on there mana, to constantly refresh. And a BST can be limited in CC thou there pet can take one add.

Primarily BSTs in a group provides DPS.
While Shamans provide utility and heals.
