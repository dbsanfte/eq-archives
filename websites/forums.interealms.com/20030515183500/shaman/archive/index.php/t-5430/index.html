Hail,

My guild wiped again last night.
No big deal. We dust ourselves off & we try again, or regroup to ponder what went wrong if we aren't ready to sally forth right away. Last night could have been different if we'd had a few key people alive for a little longer. We decided to call it a night. I went to bed a bit disappointed but w/ caffeine still racing in my veins, and thought this up in bed.

I wanted to toss this idea out to my fellow shaman for peer critique/review.

My initial impression is this would be a situational tactic for small/medium size guilds used for key people only. What about using a double "Call of the Hero" system for saving key people's lives when manpower is short?

What I'm thinking is if there is a situation where there is only one shaman, and the key to downing the mob is slowing it and quarters are tight. The shaman finally lands Turgur's (or PoI/Tigir's, whichever) and of course gets fatal aggro that isn't worth a CH rotation's mana to save the one life (especially since the shaman's main job is now likely done).

What about parking a mage (hot-boxed even better for this one) IN the zone but far away w/ COTH loaded & charged, & shaman targetted. This mage is in a safe area free from aggro, probably across the zone, but definitely well out of aggro range of the rest of the raid party and any mobs they might be fighting. (Like ToV zone-in.)

Before the shaman casts slow, the first mage begins casting CoTH. Shaman casts slow. IF it lands, the shaman immediately hotkeys for CoTH (remember the casting is already in progress) to be completed. IF it fails, hotkeys for "Interrupt" (to the mage) and recasts slow. Upon interrupting, the mage recasts CoTH as soon as gem refreshes again. If too many slow attempt failures occur (drawing fatal aggro again from the attempts), the CoTH cast can go through to completion anyway. Shaman aggro is pretty obvious. :D

Immediately after the first CoTH, the second mage (not boxed & involved in the battle as a caster) casts CoTH to bring the SAME shaman BACK into the battle, only now with aggro gone and all hate lists for the shaman completely wiped. Shaman can re-commence trying to slow, or if it landed originally, can now aid with poking, dots,epic,JBB,back-up heals, etc. Not to mention being available for adds.

My example uses a shaman, but it could be used for clerics as well. Obviously it requires some good communication skills, low lag for the mages, and excellent timing.

So the COTH group could be:
Mage1
Cleric1
Cleric2
Cleric3
Shaman
Mage2

This brings the key person(s) back into battle with little to no downtime. The first CoTHing Mage could even have a mod rod in hand ready to hand-off to the CoTHee! Druids can keep the key person alive with Chloroblast/Sup Heals until the COTH goes through if the few seconds of aggro is likely to crush the COTHee.

That's my little brainstorm.
All feedback appreciated. Is there a giant hole in the plan that's obviously not visible to me? Does this sound like a hare-brained scheme doomed to failure or a viable tactic for limited use?

All feedback appreciated. :)

Take care,

Malo as soon as the mt engages, even start casting it on the pull (if your sure theres only 1) and land it the split second after the mt engages. Then start a malosini/slow chain.
