Necro hybrid... Do you even play a Shaman?

Oh I’ve had a shaman since the last stage of EQ beta. Now I will admit I quit the game along with many of the PvP oriented guildmates I was in when we finally realized how poorly balance PvP was in EQ. Played DAOC and ShadowBane for a long time, but a couple rl friends kept pestering me into playing EQ, and finally I dusted Tormac off and started playing again last November. I think the fact that I quit the game for a couple years, then came back to my shaman makes it easier for me to see what our class has become. I was not around for most of the gradual widening of the healing gap. In fact when I started playing EQ again, I was puzzled why folks would ask me if I seriously thought I could heal, and then were surprised when I doggedly said of course I’m the main healer for the group. But after finally leveling Tormac back up and hitting some of the new challenging content, I understand. We are certainly not priests.

Ok, for those of you that doubt me, if shamans are priests, then why does Sony not act like it? You can think of your shaman as a priest all you want. You can think of your shaman as a warrior with a doggy pal, or a primitive enchanter that performs crowd control through slows and roots. It makes no difference how one thinks of the shaman.

The fact is Sony has not treated shamans like priests for quite some time. You do realize that that is what is important don’t you? Look at the heals clerics and druids have gotten and are getting. Look at the heals that shaman have received. Our defining spells are detrimental afflictions that we consign onto others, not heals.

If we’re priests, why are our heals being neglected to the point that even patch healing is problematic? You can call shaman what ever you want, but that does not change what shaman actually are.

Verant may have one thought of us as primitive priests, tribal witchdoctors. That was when in comparison to other “priests” we still healed ok.

Sony does not see shaman as a priest class. Face it they do not. They see us as some sort of necro hybrid whose power comes from curses of the spirit world, inflicting others with debilitating disease and parasites that slow their offense, cripple them, and then slowly kill them over time.

Maybe necromancer is not the perfect word to describe us. As a warhammer player I would say we were chaos sorcerers of Nurgle, but if you’re not familiar with the warhammer world that would not make sense either. But its is clear to me, after playing again for a few months, that shamans are no longer priests, but something else.
