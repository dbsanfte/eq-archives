Chutoi
To be perfectly honest, I didn't bother with resist gear until I was in my 50's. Frankly, under 50, there just aren't all that many mobs that require resist gear. Sure, there are a few casters, but for the most part, it's not even worth losing the hp/ac. When you start raiding, ESPECIALLY when you get AE mobs, then you need resist gear.

Check with your guild and see if you have an enchanter with maxxed jewelry. Then collect 8 blue diamonds and 5 diamonds. You only need 6 and 3 respectively, but you can resell them as needed if the enchanter doesn't fail (I think 4 of the combines require greater than 255 skill for trivial).

That will get you a tiarra, 2 velium diamond rings, 2 BD bracers, 2 BD earrings, a veil and a necklace. Pare it down as necessary, of course. Personally, I didn't bother getting a tiarra, necklace, or veil myself, as I don't want to give up the ac/hp and wisdom I have on those pieces for the relatively low resists they offer. Even without the veil, necklace, and tiarra, I can get most of my resists up to the 290's with raid buffs.

If you get into a high level raiding guild, you'll find that you replace a lot of the above stuff with items of equal or greater resists. Right now, I'm looking to get a Crown of the Kromzek Kings sometime in the next few times we drop Tormax. Compare that to a tiarra! ;)

Luclin's Arriana
Stone of Morid being Iksar only helps the original barbarian poster not at all...

Froglok crown is THE essential resist item (until you get into *really* high end stuff like the aforementioned kromzek crown). I wouldn't bother to pay for one, just head down to Lguk when you get to around 45, and camp the king.

Giving up AC for resists is expected, and really, unimportant. Raid mobs will eat a shaman fast no matter what your AC is, and if the mob is attacking the shaman, the raid is wiped anyway. Be more careful about giving up hitpoints though. The cloak of the icebear is a VERY worthwhile trade against the hooded black cloak against vox or another cold user.

For ToV, definately shoot for unbuffed resistances to fire/cold at 125+.

Order of resists to care about, in the order they should be cared about (IMO) would be: Magic, Fire, Cold, Disease, Poison.

Magic: If you are feared, you are worthless....worse than worthless, since people might waste mana trying to heal you.

Fire: Lots (and lots) of things use it, and there aren't any easy-to-get mega fire items like the icebear cloak. Resist stacking isn't too friendly, which makes your items a larger factor.

Cold: Lots (and lots, and lots) of things use it, but is is fairly trivial to raise through items, and current stacking rules allow for resist cold AND circle of seasons at the same time (which can put basically anyone above 200). Plus this is your strong suit resistance as a barbarian.

Disease: Hardest resist to raise, but also the one most rarely used by mobs. If raiding VP, raise this to #1 or 2, cause Hoshkar is an ass ;)

Poison: Trivially easy to raise, since DMF and shaman poison buffs stack. Epic + mistmoore shield + talisman of epuration + DMF = basic immunity to poison breathers, of which there aren't too many to begin with (Trakanon and Severilous are the only ones most players are likely to face).
