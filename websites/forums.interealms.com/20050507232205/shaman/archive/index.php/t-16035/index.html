afreeman
First off, I concur with the majority opinion here that you were a bit light on levels/HPs/etc. to really be pulling this off, but once you hit 60...

You can mitigate this crummy situation by using Torpor on yourself and then the tank just as the pull comes in (have someone besides the MA do the pulling) and then malo/slow'ing the mob.

Time it so that Torpor lands on the MA a second or two before the mob is into malo range, and then hammer on the malo key until it starts casting and then follow with slow ASAP. Stand right beside the MA when you do this, so that when you get aggro with malo, the MA can pull it right off you without moving around. When slow lands, you will get aggro again, but then the MA can safely click off torpor to get aggro.

You can also use root to force the mob onto the MA after the slow. Since aggro is based only on proximity when a mob is rooted, you can accomplish a target switch by rooting the mob and then taking a step back while the MA steps forward. With practice, you can even spread damage out amongst group members this way.

Also consider using a pally or SK for MA duty in these situations, since their aggro generation is not as dependant on attack speed as a warrior's. (Paladins are especially handy here because they can both cast root and hold aggro really well.)

Other:

(1) don't ever use the fast heal (or even the big heal) in these groups unless someone absolutely will die otherwise. Its not as mana-efficient as torpor, and forcing your group to stand around just a few seconds between pulls will give you the time necessary to cann back that mana. The only person that should ever be getting a % or fast heal is the MA.

(2) absolutely keep regen up on everyone; there is no more efficient heal than regen. Also, leaving the 2nd or 3rd damage dealer at 1/2 or 2/3 health between battles tends to really encourage them to control aggro the way they should be and won't be a big deal if everyone is on the ball. Rogues, monks, and rangers all have ways of avoiding unwanted attention (at the cost of some DPS), and wizards/mages at this level should know better than to get aggro.

This may seem like a sub-optimal setup, but I have been in full groups with just me and 5 melees and have had it work out really well, even though nobody in the group was at full health for hours at a time. Its all about maxing out mana efficiency and being with other players that understand aggro management. Oh, and if you aren't currently a HP shammie, a group like this will rapidly make you a believer.

Shaman, unfortunately, are a class that really dramatically rises in power later in their careers; the difference between a 55 and 60 shaman is night and day, compared to many classes. Our healing abilities never get that good, but they are particularly weak throughout the 50s.
