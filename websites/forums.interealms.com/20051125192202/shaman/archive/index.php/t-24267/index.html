Mary the Prophetess
It is late.

An old woman, leaning heavily on her staff, enters the nearly deserted tavern. She makes her way slowly to a dark corner, and sits down quietly. No one seems to be paying her any mind.

"It is well," she says to herself.

She looks around slowly, observing both the patrons and the furnishings. She has been here before she thinks; but perhaps it is just her mind playing tricks on her again.

She shrugs.

She reaches down into her satchel, and takes out a rolled piece of parchment and lays it on the table.

"They will find him if they search for him," but she doubts any will seek him out.

She rises slowly, pauses briefly before the hearth, and then makes her way to the doors, and out into the night.

THE MIDNIGHT OF THE SOUL

Questions we ask, and the answers we seek
Under dark clouds that are dismal and bleak.
Ever we yearn always longing for more
Standing alone on a dark distant shore.
Torn by despair, a silent prayer speak;
But all that's heard is the surf's crashing roar.

Rewalk the paths that your young heart once knew
In days gone by when the world was still new.
Now do the proud and the famous and strong
Grieve for the cause that's been lost for so long.
Enter a glade, that's been hidden from view;
Refind thy way in the words of a song.


TABULA SMARAGDINA

Truth must be truth as the wise men well know,
Revealed above and revealed below.
In one is all, on the Stone it was read,
Sired by the Sun and the Moon it is said.
Made by the Wind, the Earth fed it to grow,
Every work of wonder in the world has it bred.

Great from the small, and fire from the earth,
Into the heavens to seek it's rebirth.
See by a new light as obscurity flees,
Thus strength of strength will bring all to their knees.
Unknown in the lands is the Stone's real worth,
So secrets and signs you now see with ease.


PRIMA MATERIA

Make ye your choice, for the quest soon begins
A friend ye must seek where a windmill now spins.
So all ye who wish a new journey to make,
Search through the mists for the path ye must take.
Approach now a cave on feet more like fins,
Cross under the ruins 'neath the floor of the lake.

One's purpose must be to prove trust alone
Now by thy deeds will thy true faith be shown.
For ye to find life, prepare first to die,
Under cold waves ye will make thy last sigh.
Seek shadowed rest near a cold shaded stone,
And read in the cards where the true path must lie.
