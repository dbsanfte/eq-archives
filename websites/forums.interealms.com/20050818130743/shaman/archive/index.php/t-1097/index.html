Lumppy
Main tanking is a lot more than just having the HP and AC to stand in and take a lot of punishment. In early levels when forming single groups a lot of different characters can tank, I used to do a lot of tanking with my ranger up until level 45 i think it was. However as the mobs start to hit for more then the tanking roll in a group changes. The warrior starts to come into his own as a main tank and an SK also does a great job here. This is fine for groups but lets change to the subject of raids. It a raid situation one of the main tanks jobs is to target mobs and to get and maintain agro. The tank in a grind group learns how to do this but still does not have the experience of standing in front of a high level killer mob. In a raid situation using a Troll or Ogger for a main tank has definite advantages besides being able to take a hit. The biggest one is the visual advantage. Players can SEE them better. So if you have 5 mobs attacking at the same time and the big ugly troll or ogger is standing off to the side hitting one, the rest of the raid just has an easier time of locating the main tank and getting in the area of the proper mob.

Having stated that here is the next big thing to be a successful main tank. Being able to relax and have the confidence of the people you are raiding with. When I first started tanking ubber mobs I would sit at my computer and just shake with extra adrenaline pumping through my body:) I almost always died. After a number of encounters with these mobs I was able to relax more and I started to live through a few of them. As I relaxed more and lived through more my guild mates started to relax and get confidence in me as a tank and it snowballed from there. The fact was I got more opportunities to tank large mobs just because I was a Troll and could be spotted easily in a raid situation, because of these opportunities I learned how to relax and my guild built up confidence in me. Now put a character with the same HP and AC as me in the same situation with the same people and I guarantee they will die a lot more often. Why?? confidence and experience. It has very little to do with the extra little bit of regen, or the slam or my cute face :) How many times have you been on a raid only to wipe a couple of times, then the main tank of the guild logs on and everyone goes ah great "so and so is here we will get this mob next time" and sure enough the mob goes down. Confidence in the main tank:)

taraddar
Some general comments.

Main tank and Main assist are 2 very different things. Main tank is the tank that stands there infront of the really big mob and takes the beating with the CH chain keeping him alive. It's his reponsibilty to keep the thing aggroed, in the right location and pointing the right way.

The Main assist is most of what you desribed. Picking the one mob out of five that the raid should be killing. It can be done by anyone just hitting f8 and everyone else assisting them. It doesn't have to be a tank even. Ideally it's someone who pays attention well and reacts well and is fairly knowledgable about game dynamics to make inteligent choise about targets such as taking the unmesable mob first or the one that ae dispells last etc.

For MT an ogre might have a slight racial advantage due to thier immunity to stun from the front. Otherwise any tank given the same stats will do just as well.

For the hps quest race isn't a factor in your hps other than that it effects your starting stats, namely sta. If 2 warriors have the same sta they will have the same hps no matter if they are an iksar or ogre.
