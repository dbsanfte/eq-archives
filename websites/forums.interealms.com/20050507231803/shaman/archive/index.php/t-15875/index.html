For some time now I have been wondering why it is that in EQ every caster always ends up with the same spell line up.
In my opinion it would be much more fun if caster had a basic set that they all shared (per class that is) and then had the option to customize some spells.

Now I know that you can not make the difference in spells that are available to big or you will end up with everybody still picking the same spells. (min-max effect)
However there are a few options that from my point of few would work.

Examples could be:

1 – A DD spell where you can pick the resist type it checks against
2 – A heal spell where you can pick from various amounts of HP healed per second cast time (as example: 3 sec cast time = 600 HP healed, 4 sec cast time = 800 hp healed, etc)
3 – A proc buff with various effects
Etc

You can create some diversion this way without making some spells extreme rare (ancients when first created) or the result of long quests.

And as SoE has started with displaying what spells do they can simply put this type of spells on merchants who shows you what each spell does and checks that you can only have 1 spell per set.
ciccero
Dark Age of Camelot had something like this with their caster class implementations.

Basically, in DAoC you received nnn character points each time you leveled, and you could choose to spend them along a skill-progression tree that branched out into the various types of magic specializations.

Everyone of a base class got the same base spells, but depending on their specialization path - characters receive different higher power spells.
Rorgg
It was a little funkier than that... in DAoC, each caster class got 3 "schools" (I forget the exact terminology" of magic, and there were 2 spell lists in the school, the base, and the specialization. Each would have a spell every 2 or 3 levels.

As you leveled up, you automatically got the base line spells, you also got specialization points equal to your level, and that's what it cost to raise the specialization level, which gave you both that line of spells, plus it helped the base line spells, usually with smaller variance. For example, if you're a shaman, raising your mending (I think that was the name) specialization gives you better heals in the specialized mending line, but instead of your base mending heal doing from, say, 50 to 200, it might do 150 to 200.

The way the cost system was set up, it encouraged splitting your points between two spec lines, or one heavily and a smattering with two
lesser ones. For the classes where it was balanced, it led to 2 or 3 seperate common paths. For some others though, the power path was pretty clear.

I sent in an idea to SOE to just add in some AA related "fields" of magic. Where you could progress along spells in a field by spending AA towards it. That wouldn't remove anything already there, but it could enable different people to get new spells - or maybe new spell lines as they wanted, and have spell scrolls (maybe researchable), specifically for those fields of study rather than any specific class...

Something like "metamagic" was an idea I offered, where metamagic spells would handle magic relating to other magic - say enabling you to cast a spell-reflection buff on yourself or your partymembers that could absorb up to a set amount of damage/mana from incoming spells ... or have a mana-absorption metabuff where incoming magic would be partially converted back into your manapool as it hit you, depending on the strength of the metabuff spell.
