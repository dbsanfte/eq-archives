Reason for the post is somewhat because, I feel that the end game/progressional path things are done wrong in EQ...If that really makes any sense.

As it is now, on most servers as per this thread, there are some ruling guilds on every server it seems, and virtually no guilds down to NToV or there abouts.

That leaves a huge gap, both performance and experience wise in the two divisions on play. Now I know there are several "classes" of players in EQ, but for the basis of this, I am just interested in the High End game and the state of that.

The Tier system is an ok way of showing how I reckon the natural state of EQ should look, ideally, with a decent amount of guilds at each. But it doesnt, and thats where the conflict is, or somewhat of a conflict.

As it is, there is a huge gap between the High End players, and those just below, again speaking generally.

Most top end guilds can field tanks of about 7.2-7.5k hp, casters with avg. ft 10 at least, and gear to go with it.

Below them are those inspiring to the same, but performance wise, gear wise, experience wise, they are way below. Simply because the top end guilds kill the same things they did a year ago.

In terms of recruitment, it means that the people high end guilds are able to recruit, are subpar to those in the rest of the guild, and unless some "twinking" is done, it will take forever to bring them up to standards. Unless they come from another high end guild.

What I am seeing, is that gap getting bigger and bigger, with more high end content being added as well. because there arent any takers to the content being added, besides the high end guilds, who can kill quite a bit. Likewise I see conflicts in having 300 people hitting eachother up, over a spawn which allows them to progress a wee bit further from their current stage.

I see server politics going top level, because they influence more than 600 people directly, basically turning guildleaders into diplomats, and company leaders, who all struggle to gain more for the guilds they represent. And I see the high end game becomming more and more exclusive.

And I was curious to see whether or not, CT was the only server like that. Because it is mindboggling to see and experience whats currently on the CT server. And it isnt for lack of trying, we have a larger number of very ambitious people, even after being split a while back.

I represent a euro guild, and as such, dont have that many problems racing, nor running into the 4 other top end guilds/alliances.

And 300 people in ssra basically represent half of what could be there basically, if all the high end guilds on CT wanted to race.

And I am pretty sure thats what not Verant planned it to be like, at thats my hope.

Am just trying to get bearings on how bad it is on other servers, which in their own right dont mean anything to me, directly, but if they experience the same sense of wrongness I do, then I reckon there is something about it :p
