Case in point is normal LDON. As most shaman know Q is a very efficient healing spell, and I can usually be the primary healer if the group composition warrents it. Some of my friends and I try to go thru an entire adventure where i never cast Tnarg's and I am only healer.

The other spectrum is Hard LDON or Vxed/Tipt where the mobs hit very hard and there is NO way a shaman can be the main healer and Q only provides a bit of buffer before a cleric heal or nasty death when someone gets agro. It is almost impossible for a druid a main healer in the most difficult zones because their quick heals are a touch light and less mana efficient. Generally the cleric handles all healing duties on the main tank and I spot heal and Q peeps up. Thus the cleric does the lion's share of the healing as is appropriate given their efficiency but they run dry on mana faster than most shaman.

Just as an aside, the reason I am less than happy about the upcoming upgrade to Q is that it often overheals in its current state in hard content. I use it before incoming on the main assist tank and to top off the other group members. Now the proposed upgrade is better than nothing and it will affect healing, as will the improved regen, but in harder content its effect will not as much as you might expect (because of the overheals).

But what I got out of my parses are that it is fairly reasonable to consider that my average fight only lasts 36 seconds. The time spent casting malos and slow and maybe throwing out a nuke and taking a random canny4 when you are at full health and a canny5 whenever if refreshes pretty much takes the entire fight. It is also important to be ready to spot heal and refresh buffs. But we knew that already.

The basic difference between a well equiped high aa shaman and a light aa shaman is pretty much about a nuke a fight if you are really fast and the ability to help out the cleric more if they need it. So, an extra 800 damage a fight for 350 aa's and every focus item.....lovely.

Every harder content group has a slower IMHO. Just talking pure class balance now, with partial slows the shaman advantage narrows to almost nil and the slow section of the fight is about 25 seconds on average (assuming you bite into the 36 second fight).

Extrapolating data is not to be done lightly if you want to do it correctly. But, the #'s of 7-15% of a groups damage and 9% on healing in hard content is probably about right. Beastlords probably beat that and stack much better in raids. Enchanters are situational on dps but bring a element of safety to a group that can be very valuable.

Let's break it down a bit, and JUST look at what Purdydog said about FA.

Let's very conservatively guesstimate melees (w/o FA) doing 175dps (will vary by # of melees & class of course) on a 36 second fight. Melees being anyone swinging in battle, whether that's a pet or player.

36 seconds x175dps = 6300 in damage per fight.
FA adds 140attack, which generally equates to a 10-20% increase in DPS, depending on who you ask. We'll use 10, 15, and 20% for examples to display the low, medium, and high ends.

175dps from melee (w/o FA) + 10% (w/ FA) would add 17.5dps.
175dpsx15%=26.25dps boost because of FA.
Using 20% as a percentage damage increase from FA would add 35dps.

(10%) 17.5dps (due to FA)x36second fight=630 (FA added) damage.
(15%) 26.25dpsx36second fight=945 extra points of damage because of FA.
(20%) 35dpsx36second fight=1260 extra damage.

So his 730 damage per fight from FA I can believe. In fact for melee heavy adventure groups I would imagine this # is on the LOW side if the shaman is religious about keeping FA up on those who are swinging weapons. 175 melee dps can be cranked out by a single good rogue without FA w/ the right weapons & AA's. So melee damage increases from FA may actually be much higher than Purdy stated. Would be interesting to review the existing FA parses to compare.

Then again, consider the benefits of keeping FA on a super-whamodyne charm pet... ;)
