phileepae
Natimbi is an excellent zone to hunt in. I personally started there at around 56 or so and have always soloed there. Here's what I did:

Set up pet and buffs and everything you do to get ready to solo, and set up camp behind the boxes stacked up behind the camp. Here, there are no wanderers and you can saftely pull singles to your spot. Pull with your best malo and run straight back to your camp. Start spam hitting your slow spell and you will get it off before the mob gets to you. Root him, step back and cast cripple. Then, because i had my epic, i'd hit him with one of those, then do an ebolt of BoN and either sit and med or melee the rest of the fight, rooting when they began to run. Now, I'm a very physical shaman, I almost always tank my fights and my melee stats are all 200 atm, so it may have been easier for me and you may have more trouble if your stats aren't that high.

There are also some nice named in the area. Underwater, where the reef crustaceans spawn, there can spawn a named crustacean. I have seen him twice, and the first time he dropped a calloused shell helm or something, and the second time he dropped a +20 mana aug. Named turtle has Fierce in it's name, and I would not recommened fighting him until you are over 60. He would be easier if he didn't hit for 300+, have a sh*t load of hp, and be very magic resistant.

There are more named out there, like for the dog things and such, but I don't want to type anymore. Good luck and I hope this helped someone.

Viridovix Frosthammer
Gear wise, I am far from uber, but not badly equipped. Seeing as my entire outfit would sell for maybe 20k in bazaar, I am sure many of you are better off than me :p

I started soloing there at 55, mostly just fighting the tureptas and stonemites. At that level the hynids were a bit tough, the souls were doable, but with tons of downtime and the fierce cragwalker was death.

Now, at 58, I can clear the turtle pond (4 spawns, 1 can be fierce), then clear the hynids on the hill near Qinimi (6 spawns) and be back to the turtle pond as they start spawning again.

As someone mentioned, the named spawns (which includes the fierce cragwalker and the swift scavenger) summon, but once they are slowed they aren't too bad. Each named spawn drops something and the two necklaces that have been mentioned are on their loot tables.

Another really nice item that you can get there is Kiki's Recipe Book (http://everquest.allakhazam.com/db/item.html?item=26397) (nice for those under 60 anyway). Talk to Kiki Mischiefmaker at the camp, she's the halfling near the fire. She wants 4 suculent turepta meats to make a soup. The meats are no drop, not stackable and somewhat rare (took me about 5 hours to get 4 of them). They drop from the turepta cragwalkers which are just about the easiest mobs in the zone to kill.

Exp isn't too bad actually. It's a little more exp per mob than Chardok (the current "hotspot") but the mobs take a bit longer to kill. The only things I have seen add are the stonemites and the nameds. Even pulling a turepta with another standing on top of him, the second turepta didn't add.

Overall, I really like this zone. It takes a bit of learning, but once you know how it works, a shaman can do really well there.
