OMG Holth.... You are so kind and open minded. Thanks.

I am not sure about recent threads over the ac / wis / hp because I have been away for some time and have only recently started playing again.

The basic arguments are resonably simple, and high hp is more of a gole to be acheived at the same time as you get Cani V.

The two basic models for a shaman are

Wisdom.. caster shaman.
AC .... tanking shaman.

The advantages of a high wisdom shaman are mostly found in grouping and having a large mana pool for healing when things get bad. The large reserve of mana does provide a bigger margin of safty.

The AC heavy shaman: because we get the canibalize line of spells, hp == mana. We can improve how efficient we are buy converting our hp regeneration into mana as well as meditating. With high ac the hp side of the mana pool is protected when you are getting pounded on in solo / group / or raid situations. That preserves your health for conversion into mana wich means that in a given amount of time you will have more mana to use for casting.

If you are a wisdom shaman then you are limited in the teckinques that you can use to solo and must be more carefull about gaining agro in a group. A wisdom shaman can solo via pet kiting, root doting or any other variation that keeps him / her from getting pounded. If you are a high AC shaman you just stand in the battle and let the slowed mob beat on you while you tank for your pet and add some free damage with a big 2hb or other weapon of choise. This also gets your defensive skill maxed in short order which equates to free ac.

In group / raid, the high ac shaman has little fear of slow agro and can slow as soon as the mob reaches the kill spot. This cuts down on the work the cleric has to do and it is much better to have agro on the shaman then on the cleric or enchanter.

The high ac shaman can also do a very nice job in crowd controll buy using slow / criplle to gain agro on adds then moving out of mele range to root park. With out a good base of AC and a nice hp pool, this is a lot more dangerous.

Another consideration is the enchanter in the group. I do my very best to make sure that every mezed mob hates me a lot more then the enchanter. As soon as the chanter mezzes I slow or even before. After slow I like to crip to gain even more agro. If mez breaks the mob should run to the shaman and not the enchanter. If a shaman is good the chanter never dies.

AC has some limits. If you get your ac up into the 1200 range it starts to have diminishing returns on damage mitigation.

HP is more of a canni V thing and it gives you a better chance of survinving a slow resist on a rade target.

Canni V remove a huge chunk of health 1900 and somthing all at once and converts it into mana. A shaman with nice hp and torpor can manufacture mana at a tremendous rate.

At this time, my gear puts me at about 1800 hp, 920 ac and 155 wisdom in 1hb / group mode. When soloing with a 2hb my self buffed ac still breaks 900 and my wisdom drops to about 142 or so. I have no fear of slowing for our gimp rades. I can solo like a mad man with just pet tanking and slow spending no mana at all for damage. All my solo damage comes from the pet and the hammer and my pull rate is all but constant. I find a little down time to get more mana for the next slow or when I have a big chunk of buffs drop.

My personal goals at this time is to...

1) break 1k self buffed ac in solo mode
2) add a big chunk of hp for when I can Can V
3) get a horse and a JBB.

I truly hope this helps and a lot of others can add much rich detail to this very basic outline.

I personaly think equipment selection is fairly simple up too the Can V / torpor level. After that, I do not have a good feal at how to balance hp / resists and ac.
