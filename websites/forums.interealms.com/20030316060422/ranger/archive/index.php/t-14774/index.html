Beohram
Aye, you guys got it. Stacking, when referring to spells, means the spells cast will have a cumulative effect.

Buffs:

Skin like wood and Skin like rock do not “stack.” If you had skin like wood up and then cast skin like rock on yourself, SLW would be overwritten. Trying to cast SLW while SLR is up and you will get the message that SLW will not take hold. They do not “stack.”

Casting Barb coat while you have SLR up and they will both take hold on you. They “stack.”

Offensive spells:

While the majority of the offensive spells cast in game are instantaneous, many have an effect that lasts over time. (snare for example) Cast snare 10 times on the same mob and it will not become any slower. Snare does not “stack.” In most cases, the spell with the higher level effect will take hold over other spells of the same line. Ensnare overwrites snare (they do not “stack”). A slow from a level 60 shaman will overwrite the slow cast from a lvl 50 Shaman.

Damage over time spells are one of the few exceptions. Once upon a time, many of our DoT spells would not “stack” with our own DoT spells, much less the DoT of a higher level druid. (Immolate would not stack with flame lick). In a recent patch, this limitation was removed. You can now cast each and every DoT you own on a mob and they will all take hold AND they will all do damage. This is true even if a high level druid were casting their DoTs on a mob.

Casting the same DoT multiple times will only have the effect of casting it once.



Hope this helps a bit.
