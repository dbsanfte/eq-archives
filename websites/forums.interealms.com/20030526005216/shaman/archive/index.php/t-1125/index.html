Chutoi
I still use root exclusively at level 60.

The whole root line works the same way--skip the next part if you're familiar with it! :) Root has a magic resistance check on the mob, to see if it lands. If it hits, the mob has an immediate check to see if it breaks root (also against MR), and then an additional check every six seconds (a 'tic') until it either breaks root, or until the spells wears off. The mob has an additional check to see if it breaks root each time it is hit by melee damage or a direct damage component of a spell. Damage shields and DoTs will NOT offer an additional chance to break root (outside of the initial damage component of some DoTs which DO offer a chance to break root).

Comparisons of cast times and mana costs are important. Root has one of the lowest cast times and lowest mana costs in spite of its short duration. At 60, I have a 9 second cast on epic, and about a 5-6 second cast on both Bane of Nife and Pox of Bertoxulous. Bane has to be reapplied every 48 seconds. In my experience, direct damage of spells and melee seem to have a higher chance of breaking root then the normal 'tic' check--especially the spells. I don't know if people have looked into that or not. If you are using spells of short duration that have to be reapplied, then root is MUCH more mana efficient. If you are casting on the mob every 30 seconds, it's less mana to have to reapply a root than a paralyzing earth.

In terms of cast time, assume you have mobs beating on you or your party and you need to park it NOW. Root is the second fastest casting of the line at 2 seconds. Immobilize is the fastest at 1.75 seconds. Root is 30 mana, immobilize is 80 mana. Both are effective in parking mobs that a puller is bringing in. You can target from a distance and root it fast enough once it gets in range that it stops before it gets to the party. You may or may not be able to get the 2.5 second or 3.0 second spells off fast enough.

Level seems to help substantially in terms of roots landing AND lasting through the duration of the spell (absent someone hitting the mob, of course).

The only time I use longer roots is when I am playing ghetto chanter for my group, and we have high HP mobs. Most good groups tear through mobs fast enought that we are ready for the next within 30 seconds. It's annoying to have to wait on rooted mobs. I'd rather re-root than have to wait or try to break root and drag it back to camp (if you are in a spot where the group can't afford to move up or back much). It's VERY rare and situation that I ever use longer roots--mostly in places like Cazic Thule or maybe Siren's Grotto where the mobs take a little longer to kill.
