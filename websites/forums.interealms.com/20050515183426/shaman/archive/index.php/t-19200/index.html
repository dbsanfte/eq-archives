Redhenna
What sort of percentage does it give?

From this (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?s=&threadid=2259) thread:

going from ID0 to ID5 (on Valorian Guards in HoH) will net you an average 15hp/hit mitigation - which is roughly 15/2.87 = 5.2% decrease in damage taken. Given the linearity of ID cost, I'd wager a round of beer that it's intended to give you ~1% of damage per skill level (or ~4% for an increase from DI1 to DI5, for comparison).

Going from LR1 to LR5 will increase your avoidance by 3%, from 32% misses to 35% misses. Miss+Hit at LR1 is 85.6% of the swings, while Miss+Hit at LR5 is 85.8% of swings. 32% of 85.6% is 37.4% of damage avoided while 35% of 85.8% is 40.8% of damage avoided - or about a 4%-point increase in damage avoided.

From this little test, I would hypothesise that ID and LR are about the same efficiency in reducing total damage taken - however, LR will make you get hit less which can affect any innate proc related additional damage/stuns.
Hannduvdeth
Understand that Combat Stability does NOT reduce the amount of damage taken directly by X%. If a mob hits for 900 max, you can still by hit for 900 whether you have no CS or max CS/ID. What CS/ID does is it allows for more minimum hits and less maximum hits (according to my understanding of this (http://www.thesteelwarrior.org/forum/showthread.php?threadid=5224) Steel Warrior post).
Redhenna
Yup, that is right. Mitigation shifts hit distribution towards lower hits, while higher attack shifts hit distribution towards higher hits. Formula for hits is DB + (n * DI), DB being damage bonus(in this case being a base damage), DI is Damage interval, and n is a random number between 1 and 20. All mobs have a set DB and DI, an example being Vindi, who has a DB of 200, and a DI of 25, resulting in a min hit if 200 + (1 * 25) = 225 and a max hit of 200 + (20 * 25) = 700. These numbers do not change(outside of shielding mods and warrior innate mitigation bonus), only the distribution of the hits.
