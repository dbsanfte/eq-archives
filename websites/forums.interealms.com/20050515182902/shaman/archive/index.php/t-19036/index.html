Tyck
I think we are all pretty much in agreement on the issue of Shaman advancement in recent expansions. We're just seeing the same old tired set of spells bumped up a few points or tweaked a bit.

I've been reading some books about RL and historical Shamans and thinking about what could be applied to a game like this.

There are 3 common themes in the literature I've read:

1. Shamans affecting physical well being thru cures, curses, poisons (both mixed and magically 'thrown' at people like direct damage spells), and evil spirits (take the last one as 'diseases'),

2. Shamans as sources of knowledge and wisdom,

3. Shamans as controllers of the spirits.

EQ Shamans are definitely hit number 1 on the nose, the second they completely miss, and the third is relegated to our 'puppy' spells.

Now, to think 'outside the box' that the EQ Developers have placed us by consentrating on item 1 above.


Currently, I suspect that any type of expansion of our 'information gathering' abilities about mob placement beyond the 'bind sight' like would be viewed as infringing on tracking abilities. We once did get some 'Sence' spells, but they are pretty useless in the current context of the game.

Twoflower talked with me a bit ago about what if we could cast a spell and LEARN something about a monster. Consider it a 'Sence Weakness' line of spells.

- One could 'Sence Immunities' that would tell us that when given mob could not be slowed, mezzed, or rooted.

- Another might be a 'Sence Resistances' that would list out a message such as "%T is %1 to %2" where %1 would be one of (immune, highly resistant, very resistant, resistant, mildly resistant, vulnerable, and not resistant) and %2 would be given for each of (magic, cold, fire, disease, poison).

- Some others could be 'Sence Detrimental'/'Sence Benificial' that would list out a message for each of the detrimental/benificial spells running on the target.

- And to show the full potential of this line of spells, a 'Sence Allegances' that would list out what factions the current mob is allied and opposed to.

All of these would fit with the Shaman as the delver into the mystic realms of knowledge. They also fit with Shamans not being the ones that do the damage but rather that they are the ones that help the ones doing the damage.


As for the controller of the spirit realms, that is problematic from the standpoint that it would infringe upon the other charm classes. I say 'other' because the original Shaman class was a charm using class to begin with but other than the ability to charm low level animals we lost that ability.

I would suggest that along the line of Necro's getting a charm undead that Shamans be augmented with a higher level charm animal spell and a set of spell lines 'Break Spirit Bond' and 'Forge Spirit Bond'.

'Break Spirit Bond' When cast upon groupped player's summoned pet (swarms and charmed pets don't count and maybe not warders or familiars) it would sever the link to between that player and the pet and bind the pet to the Shaman.

Spells in the 'Break Spirit Bond' would work as a benificial spell similar to TL giving the dialog a popup to confirm its use when used on players and only be able to used on players in the same group.

'Forge Spirit Bond' would work in reverse that it would transfer the current Shaman's pet to the targetted player in group.

For balance reasons both these spell lines would probably have to act like 'store pet' and remove all buffs and gear on the pet unless the Shaman invested in AA's to preserve those buffs and gear. Additionally, low level versions would have limits to the max levels of the summoned pets that Spirit Bonds could be worked upon.


Now it might be argued that this type of thing should be in the Enchanter field of expertise since they already have extensive spell lines for charming a pet for themselves. But, this isn't about doing it for oneself, this is about having an ability to master something and then bind to help another. This is a central feature in many Shamanistic practices. To bind a spirit being to a person to help them in some way.

- Tyck of RN
