Flufy
Why AC/HP? Two major reasons and one minor one:

1) Post level 24, we can cannibalize, to turn hit points into mana. Since we also get regen spells, this lets us regen mana faster than any other class, period. If any other class needs more mana than they have to cast x spell, they have no choice but to wait. Shaman can trade their health for mana and be ready to cast in an EXTREMELY short period of time, relatively speaking. This means that the relative size of a shamans mana pool is markedly less important than anyone elses, assuming they have a decent pool of hp to cann from. Hp=mana, and since hp has two functions (generates mana AND keeps us from being dead) and mana only has one, many people consider hp to be far more valuable. Hp can also be regained fairly fast with relatively cheap equipment/free spells. High ac simply helps to protect this valuable hp pool by reducing the amount of it that is lost when we draw aggro, which brings me to...

2) Aggro. Shaman catch aggro, it's a fact of life. Between debuffing, healing the melees, and dotting, we tend to piss off the mobs. Ac/hp gear helps us survive when this inevitably happens so we can keep doing our job. This is somewhat less of an issue currently since they nerfed disease aggro (/rude Verant, way to screw two classes at once) but is still a very important consideration, especially since they might actually correct this at some point. Here's hoping, anyway.

3) It lets us do neat stuff like ghetto mezzing, where you grab aggro on a mob by slowing it, then drag it away from the party and root it.


You can root/rot to 60, I suppose, if you really want to, but I'd recommend tanking at least some every level. Later on in the game, having low ac simply won't be a viable option, so you're going to want to keep defense skill maxed. It's easier to do this a little at a time while you level than it is to try to raise it 50 points at once when your like 46. And yeah, basically shaman soloing is dot, slow/debuff the mob, melee. You can switch it up, root/rot first, then tank when root breaks, tank while debuffing/dotting, then root, etc. Try different stuff. The neatest thing about shamans is the wide variety of tools we have.

Crashem
Bah don't listen to these people. Listen to the troll, the troll knows (just kidding).

If you are planning on soloing to 60, it is entirely possible as a shaman. When it comes to solo xp, the three limiting things are HP regen, mana regen, and damage output. The amount of total hp and mana matter a bit but if you are optimizing your xp, you shouldn't ever be full on either. HP regen is accomplished by sitting and regening or, in the case of the shaman, heals. Mana regen is accomplished by medding or at higher levels canni.

Considering this lets discuss the various things:

AC: A lot of people misunderstand AC. AC is a damage reducing effect by making the miss or by reducing the damage. Now, there is NOT a max amount that AC will help. But after a certain point, AC has a quickly reducing effect on your damage reduction (soft cap). What is often misunderstood is that this AC is not based on what zone you are in. People often quote 1200AC being the soft cap. This is not really true. AC soft cap is verus THE MOB ATTK value. So, if you are attacking a low level mob, the soft cap may be at 600AC. The result is that someone with 700AC and someone with 1500AC would take similar damage.

So how does it matter to you? As a general rule, if you are in melee range of the mob, you should be trying to max AC to the soft cap based on the mobs you are killing. There are lots of ways to work this out by just analyzing the log files or just working it out.

HP: A lot of people say HP > AC > WIS. I don't necessarily agree with this. HP does equal mana, but high HP is exactly the same as high wis and therefore high mana pool. High HP doesn't help you gain HP back faster. It does matter at lower levels cause you are more likely to live and therefore not lose xp from deaths. Don't get me wrong, at the end game, high HP is really necessary cause of canni5 and the aggro you get from mobs. Most end game equipment allows you to go for max HP while maintaining high enough AC to reach soft caps for most/all mobs as well as high enough mana pool cast what you need to cast. The main reason why most people are convinced on HP is your don't have the reducing affect that you have with soft caps in both ac and wis. 100 hp is a 100 hp at all levels while +10 wis and 10 AC means different things depending where you are at.

WIS: Although no one mentioned this, +Mana goes into this category. In fact at lower levels, +mana is more important as it will likely add more to your mana pool than +wis items but do the calculations yourself. Wis/mana items add to the total amount of mana you have. Similar to hp, a high mana pool can save you from death, but if you are soloing correctly, you shouldn't ever get to the max or min of hp or mana.

DPS (Damage per seconds): This is the thing that is not oftened mentioned. In my opinion, DPS is the MOST important factor in leveling quickly. If you are meleeing, that means a good weapon. If casting damage spells, that is the new focus items that improve damage. Why is DPS the most important factor?
High DPS does so many things. You kill mobs faster and therefore get xp faster. You take less damage from the mob cause it is dead and doesn't have a chance to hit you which makes your downtime less cause you lost less HP. I also put into this category any mana free DPS which becomes important to shamans at higher level (note 1st level shaman can get epic and jbb useable at 45th). DPS doesn't just mean during the battle but also any related downtime. With manafree right click damage items, you don't use mana so you your effective DPS is raised.

SO, if you understood my above statements, DPS items then +mana/hp regen items should be your priority (fungi, iskar breastplate, FT items, mana saving focus items, etc.). After that, it totally depends on your method of xping. If doing a lot of melee range action (not necessarily meleeing), try to reach the AC soft cap for the mob you are on then go for HP then +mana (via +mana/or +wis items). Of course you need to judge for yourself whether xyz item is worth abc item.

Lets go over briefly the different methods a shaman solos. Which method you use depends on what equipment you have and what level you are. Just try each one and see which allows you highest xp per hour/minute etc.

1. Melee Man: Melee the mob to kill it and just use your mana to heal and add extra damage if you have the extra mana. This is the typical method for killing mobs at low levels. DPS (weapon) > HP/MANA REGEN > AC > WIS

2. Root Park: Root the mob and DOT it up. You might be using the method at various points in your career. This method really becomes efficient when you get canni and don't have a good slow. Slow is a relatively mana expensive spell. If the slow doesn't save you enough mana to make up the mana you would use otherwise for heals/damage, why bother. With the root park technique, slow is probably not needed. The only important thing is MANA REGEN and DPS related items. DPS items (mana saving focus, damage improvment focus, mana free dps) > MANA REGEN > ALL

3. Pet Kite: In this method you gain aggro by Dotting the mob up and summon your pet. The mob dies as it runs after you from both the dots and pet damage. pet taunt off and if it aggros on pet, have a high aggro spell ready. The negative thing about this is our DoTs get reduced effectiveness because of xp code about how much damage they are reduced for not being in melee range. Obviously you need the pet and you don't need to slow the mob. Just damage it up and run (sow please). DPS items (see Root park) > MANA REGEN > ALL

4. Slow Melee: Probably the final way you will xp. You slow the mob. Stay in melee range and take the damage so you get full benefit of DoT damage. Then DoT, melee, pet, right click items. Whatever will get you a sustainable and high DPS. This method becomes really effective at 60th level cause torpor is SO mana efficient heal. When should you use this method? Just do the calculations on how much mana you save from not needing heals and killing mobs faster verus slowing the mob (may be slow spell and any debuffs). DPS items > mana/hp regen > soft cap AC > hp > any extra AC > WIS/MANA

There are lots of other methods but not solo. I would recommend duoing with melee as a really good xp method.

BlargX
No matter what you're using your shaman for, you need good HP and AC, because shamans take a lot of damage if they cast slows, and sometimes notable damage if they cast heals.

At 51, especially, the 75% slow spell they get is very high aggro. It can be very difficult for people to get aggro off a shaman who has slowed a mob.

And don't forget the tricky part -- slow doesn't always stick. Sometimes you have to cast it two or three times(let's try not to think about the times you have to cast it more than that). Often, anything more than two or three tries is suicide for the shaman, since we don't have near the hitpoints melees do unless we have extremely good equipment. For instance, at 60 my shaman has usually at least 1000 less hitpoints than melees do, and sometimes more than 2000 points less. Sometimes ... more than that. For reference, my shaman has full ToV, crystal chitin gloves he likes better than the ToV ones, epic, and a shield of wurms from North ToV. Not the greatest, but comparably equipped melee have many more hitpoints than I do.

Anyway, shamans can and do get aggro. You will get it when you heal stuff, when you root it, when you slow it, when you DOT it, when you blast it -- you name it. Unless you play your shaman as nothing more than a mule to haul around coin loot and bandages for your monk, you'll get hit a lot. Especially after level 51.

So you might as well prepare for it. My shaman meleed mobs very well at 52, so you can melee a lot. He didn't cause much damage, but with regrowth and slow, he could take it. But do it solo -- groups don't need you being low mana when they need a heal because you've been busy working up your melee skills at their expense. You'll be remembered if you're a clown who likes to melee when you've been added in to HELP the melees. They REALLY need your heals and slows; your melee is extremely trivial, to say the least, compared to a real melee's.
