Sebekiz
I'd definitely start out with the "standard" AA route for Shaman, working your way up to Canni 5:

1. Run 3
2. Either Innate Regeneration 3 or Innate Metabolism 3
3. Mental Clarity 3
4. Canni 5

I've seen it argued that Regen is better or worse than the Metabolism, but at only 1 point each I don't think it's all that critical (not as fast as AAs can flow these days!) Pick whichever works best for you (Regen is nice if you Canni a lot, but not critical with Quiesence, etc., Metabolism's really only important if you use stat foods)

Then I'd work on Spell Casting Mastery and Spell Casting Reinforcement (and the Mastery upgrade to it). One will reduce the mana cost of your spells by up to 10%, the second increases buff durations (especially your heal over time spells) by up to 50%. With SCRM, Quiesence costs a little over 300 mana and heals for 2500 HPs on average (sometimes more if you are lucky with the timing).

Beyond that it depends on your play style. MGB's pretty much a necessity if you raid. Healing Adept and Healing Gift are pretty important if you help with healing. Radiant Cure is great for cleansing many detrimental effects. Virulent Paralysis can be a lifesaver, rooting and knocking the mob back. The defensive AAs are great, especially if you solo or tend to grab aggro quite often.

As for Omens AAs, Call of the Wild (AA Rez) can be handy if your party's only rezzer dies. Spiritual Channeliung sounds rather interesting, though I'm going to work on some other AAs before I worry about that one.
