Krol
Simply put, the shaman class is too weak in high-end raiding.

After reading that first line i'm sure there are people screaming at their computer screen, "You're an idiot!". That remains to be seen, but the statement is in fact true. Before i explain myself, let me state something. I do not want Shaman healing or damage dealing abilities buffed. I also do not want any of our current skills or spells buffed. After i explain my reasoning, i'll list a few ways to improve Shamans.


Turgur's Insects is not an excuse to keep us weak:

1. For a long time players have considered this spell to be overpowered. In some cases they may have been right, but only if you overlooked the fact that mobs would have been different if the spell never existed. Fights are tuned with this spell in mind.

2. We aren't the only class that can slow. If you can win a fight with a 75% slow then i'm willing to bet you could also win it with a 70% (enchanter) and a 65% (beastlord). Turgur is obviously better and easier to obtain, but are lack of spells really an issue for endgame guilds and exceptional players? Absolutely not. The addition of Slow Mitigation only amplifies this issue.

3. Beastlord's are better 'raid plowing' slowers. Most trash mobs a guild kills to reach bosses don't have long lifespans. A 65% slow in these situations is just as beneficial as a 75% slow. Considering the damage capabilities of PoP mobs, a Beastlord is better suited to handle the slows. Their higher hps, defensive skills, and the discipline 'Protective Spirit' (reduces melee damage taken to 1/10th for 12 seconds) give them the clear advantage. This narrows the Almighty Turgur's superiority to named and boss mobs.


Our debuffs aren't so needed:

1. Malo/Malos are only a factor on mobs with extremely high MR. In these cases they would lend a Marginal advantage over the spell 'Mala' (magician) concerning casters ability to land spells (primarily nukes).

2. On many mobs malos/malo/mala isn't even needed. On occasion i've noticed mages landing malosinia straight up or with the help of Tash only.

Consider it. If a shaman decided to not debuff a boss, could you still win? Unless you are winning all your fights by the skin of your teeth and have zero skill, then yes. Mala + Tash is enough to get malosinia and 70%/65% slow on a boss (if they aren't immune obviously). Cripple isn't a factor since enchanters have the exact same spell.


Buffs don't make up for our shortcomings:

1. Focus is nice. It's extra health that helps keep ppl alive. Useful? yes. A huge contribution? no.

2. Stamina is the only stat worth buffing. As we get more and more gear with high stamina values, this spell becomes even less important. Especially when considering the reduced benefits of stamina over 255.

3. Ferine Avatar. Ah yes, melee love it. However, it's still limited to a handful of players and requires constant rebuffing over short periods of time. Not acceptable. If a buff is going to be limited in use and duration, it needs to be more powerful.


Healing and Damage:

1. Our heals are pathetic. With lucky crits and healing focus our spotheals might save our friends, but we know who is more important in this field. Druids and Clerics put us to shame.

2. We can deal reasonable damage. Assuming a mob isn't immune to poison and disease, we can lend respectable dps but are extremely limited by the high aggro. I don't think i have to explain our nuking.

The bottom line is that when shamans are reduced to healing or trying to do damage, we are just taking up space. The raid would be better off with another cleric, wizard, druid, rogue...the list goes on and on.



Shamans are useful, but are we as important and vital as some make us out to be?

I would like to propose some changes to improve our class concerning high-end gameplay (and maybe even lower levels). 2 new spell lines and 2 new AA skills.


Self Buff:

The addition of bearform was interesting, but it's just too wussy. Given the situations shamans are constantly put in, through raiding and grouping, something better is needed. If this was to be available to lower levels as well, then apparently the benefits could be scaled back or adjusted for balance.

For the most part I agree with the first part of Krol's post. Shamans are one-dimensional buffers/debuffers. None of the things we do are essential to a group or raid. Other classes that can do those things nearly as well as we can bring much more to a group/raid in terms of healing, dps, or both. Shamans persist because there are lots of us who have built up reputations over the years for doing some neat stuff.

Part of the problem is the one dimensional thinking that SOE generated during Kunark/Velious/Luclin. Turgur's was highly powered in these encounters, and the only way to deal with it was slow immunity.

With PoP, developers began thinking outside the slugfest box, and this has had some pretty negative results for shamans. Slow mitigation is obvious. But it is far more than this. PoP encounters require massive healing over short periods of time to counteract high DPS mobs. Furthermore, these mobs have low hp and must be killed quickly. Damage mitigation has moved from debuffing (e.g. slowing) to individual PC AA based attributes (e.g., natural durability, combat stability, innate sta, etc.)

What this means is that slowing is much less important in PoP, while healing and rapid damage dealing have increased in importance. Str and dex are still important to max out melee damage, but the effect is probably small over short time periods. Shamans cannot heal at the PoP level beyond Tier 2. Shamans cannot damage rapidly. For these reasons, PoP marginalizes shamans because we have been balanced based on the old pre-PoPslugfest model.

I'm not so enthusiastic about Krol's fixes. There are some nuggets of good ideas there, but as they stand, they are probably to radical for SOE. Furthermore, they are poorly balanced. And there's no way I'm going to pay 25AA for a level 3 skill.

But I agree that something must be done to rebalance shamans for PoP.

- slow stacks (multipile slows from different casters as long as the same slow.)

- each mobs has a slow cap
Mob_00 has a slow cap of 75%. Shaman_01 slows mob_00 with turgur by 75%. 75% isn't over slow cap so slow hits full. Now shaman_02 casts turgur too. Mob_00 would have been slowed by 2x 75% = 150% but slow cap is at 75% so the resulting slow is still 75%.
Mob_01 has a slow cap of 50%. Shaman_01 slows mob_00 with torgor by 65%. 65% is over the slow cap so remaining slow is 50%. Shaman_02 casts turgur and overwrites torgor. 75% is over slow cap. Mob_01 would still only be slowed by 50%.

c) slow cap xx%, 100% mitigation = immune to slow.

I have this theory:

Shaman are an enjoyable class purely by accident.

I suspect we were meant to cast SoW, STR, DEX, AGI, and STA on every member of the party, then CHA as we hauled our riches out Runnyeye. I believe our class was designed around buffing physical stats in a game that waters them into nothingness. You can sense the developers expected stats to be huge when you look at early loot -- items with +2 AGI and potions that added 10 STA and STR, but stole 10 DEX less you become too much of a god. It sounded great on paper, but they shipped a game where stats do almost nothing.

Why?

Gnomes is why.

Gnome warriors more specifically. If strength and stamina really made the warrior, why would anyone ever roll a gnome fighter? They had to make every race viable and weren't up to the challenge of balancing racial stats. Small races could have been more agile thus mitigate better. Instead, they watered it down.

At level 62 we get an AC buff that adds 36. Sixty-two levels of shaman grinding for a 36AC buff. What's more embarrassing: Sixty-two levels of shaman grinding to get a cheesy 36AC buff or having that cheesy buff be way more effective than adding 60 AGI to someone?

So we took our cannibalization that was meant for giving STA, DEX, and CHA to bozos and we used it for DoT's instead. We learned to dance with our fungis for a bit more. We took our anemic puppies and taught them to be rogues. We slowed dragons and killed them in steady-paced marathons. That was our happy accident.

You want to fix shaman? Fix stats. But look, the sun is setting and it's much too late in the year to start tearing up the roofing. I'm an old shaman and I'm finding it harder to care anymore.

When I join a group, I don't bring stats. I bring that dance I learned -- my steady paced marathon and things just flow. We're not the only class that can slow, but we're the best. Lose a chanter, lose a healer, get an add, someone goes LD, a shaman smooths out all those bumps so quietly no one sees it.

When I play my monk, I am a victim in a disjointed group.
When I play my shaman, I am the remedy.
