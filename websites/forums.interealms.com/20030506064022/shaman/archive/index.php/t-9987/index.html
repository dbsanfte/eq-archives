Jinheim
Stop using eqshaman.com, it is very outdated. eq.castersrealm.com will have most of your answers about spells.

51-60 spells can no longer be bought from your old guild vendors. You have to go to either Firona Vie or the OT Outpost to get them (depending on your race). You can get the majority of your spells here. The rest of them are either drop spells or come from a turn in quest.

In your turn in quest, you turn in either:

Talisman of Jasinth (level 51)
Spirit of Scale (level 52)
Cripple (level 53)
Cannabalize III (level 54)

and you recieve:

Insidious Decay (level 52)
Talisman of Shadoo (level 53)
Shroud of the Spirits (level 54)
Torrent of Poison (level 55)

There is no correlation between what spell you turn in and what spell you get back. It is random.

There is a thread stickied at the top of this forum that tells you where all the rest of your spells can drop. You can either hunt here and hope for drops, or just buy them in bazaar. Most kunark spells are very very common and sell for next to nothing, now.

The parchments you asked about are the answer to your third question. There ARE 61+ shaman spells and they come from parchments. There is a vendor in plane of knowledge that accepts both varieties of parchments, and glyphed word runes (just called runes, normally). When you hand him an ethereal parchment, you get back a random level 61 or 62 spell. Spectrals yield a 63 or 64, and runes yield a 65 spell. The ethereal variety spell are very common now and easy to get. The spectral variety are still rare, but it is starting to become more common to see them on the market. 65 spells are still rare but they're starting to hit the market, usually for ridiculously high prices. Expect to see prices for everything come way way down by the time you're ready to start buying 61+ spells.

To answer your final question, you do not need a raid force to get any of your spells. Torpor and Malo are rare kunark spells, and drop most commonly from kunark dragons (gorenaire, severlous, etc...). These dragons will take a medium sized raid force for mid 50's players to kill, although they can be done fairly easily by a single group of 65's. The other sources of torpor and malo are a one-group effort.
