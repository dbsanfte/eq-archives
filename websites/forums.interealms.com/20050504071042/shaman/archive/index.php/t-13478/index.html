Hail,

It really depends on the situation for me.

Today, helping some lower level friends in PoI (while hunting Sillicorrosive grease for the Cloak of Greater Pernicity) I typically led with Slow (I was pulling) and then cast Bane & Ebolt. This would have me tanking (Torpored) for half to sometimes the entire fight. Occasionally I'd recast Bane if the group was really slow. This is a very ABNORMAL situation for me, since I was basically slumming in Tier1 searching for the grease and helping friends out at the same time.

USUALLY, in a normal Tier3 exp group, I get one to two Tears of Saryn in on the mob, and occasionally a Velium Strike as a finisher, depending on the group's DPS & my mana level. On a named, I'll cast some DoT's after aggro is well established. If the group's DPS is poor, but we have a SK/Pally as a tank, I'll use Bane early (Torpored) and then cast a single Tears of Saryn, possibly adding a Velium Strike as a finishing nuke.

If the group has a CC person (Ench/Bard) AND a good cleric I trust, I will cast Tears & Torrent back to back when the mob is about 60% health (or more if the tank is uber), and finish with a Velium Strike. I have to give up an extra gem to do this, so I don't do it often unless I know my groupmates.

In Tier2, I'll normally act as main healer and slower, and I'll cast an epic dot (that will never finish) and either a single Tears or Bane, group DPS depending. I'll sometimes put that ancient JBB on and use it just for something to do when I have a bit of free time, but that isn't often and the DPS it puts out is paltry.

Dogdog is out there putting out his usual damage in most cases.

I tend to use the epic dot as a "time-spacer" for myself in many situations, as I often get impatient and want to nuke (w/ a rain) or dot early. The long epic cast time is usually enough to guarantee that I'm safe to cast something else as soon as it lands. I don't count this as damage really (it almost never finishes), and I won't use it at all if the possibility of adds means danger for the group because I have put a horridly long duration (& mostly worthless) DoT on a mob.

If I have a mage (summoned) damage bracer on, I find myself chain casting Velium Strike more often than I've listed above towards the end of the fight. With the bracer, I get hits of 1100 to 1110 every so often, which is always nice.

Take care,
