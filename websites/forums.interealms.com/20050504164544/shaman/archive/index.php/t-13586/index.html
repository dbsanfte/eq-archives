Kianor
For the effectiveness of wis - after 200 wis, you get less mana per point of wisdom, but you still get some, it's a soft cap.

If you're *just* going for mana pool, you'd look at +mana and +wis items, and try to get a balance to give the biggest mana pool. Magelo helps for this.

However, a shaman is not a druid. For a shaman, of course, HP is good for cannibalization, and you can convert HP to mana, but then, if you have a big mana pool, that might not bother you so much.

But, the biggest consideration to my mind, the one thing you need as a shaman, is to be able to withstand a beating from the mob you just slowed or tried to slow, and got a resist from. If you get beaten to a bloody pulp at the first resist, and die before you have a chance to slow again, you need to work on HP and AC some more. I know I did. :)

Up to your mid 40s, it's not so important, work on wis and mana if that's easier. But if you intend to play into your 50s, bear in mind that a shaman doing his job as slower *will* be tanking from time to time, and you'll need to work on your HP to improve your survivability.
cattywumpus
I would recommend holding off on new equipment until you are 24th level. Canni/regen is one of the three biggest changes in a shaman's life (pet and stoicism/torpor are the others). Once you see how those spells affect your play then you can make far more informed decisions about equipment.

24 is not that far away and it will give you time to save a little more money and be able to buy even better equipment.

I made long term decisions with my equipment since this is my second shaman. The game is pretty easy up into the 40s and equipment will have less of an importance. A decision to buy equipment that will be useful at 50th and beyond will not hurt you much now and will save you a ton of money replacing outdated equipment.
