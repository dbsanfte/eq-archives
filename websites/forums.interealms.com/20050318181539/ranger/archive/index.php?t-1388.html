There are several reasons I object to this and it has more to do with enforcement than it does with any sort of moral concept.

Firstly its too easy to pass a 17 or even 16 or 15 year old off as 18 which is the legal age.

The provider could lie about the age of the "model" and yet the downloader would be nailed. The downloader was attempting to be lawful (if not morally corrupt) but was duped by the supplier which is technically in any other market misrepresentation and therefore the supplier is completely liable and the buyer isn't.

Secondly its WIDE open door for abuse and blackmail. Say the government or some other party wanted to frame or blackmail a person they could upload pictures of very small size say a JPG into a crowded and rarely looked at directory say one of the system directories and then call the authorities on it.

The real owner of the system never downloaded the stuff and never wanted the stuff but simply because its on his machine he can be arrested.

More over in applying this a hardcopy found in a home. Someone breaks into the house while you are not there plants a magazine in your dusty bookshelf then calls the cops.

Mainly because of the Social backlash against this kind of stuff even if the pictures were found to be a plant you still show up with a warning on your permanant record and you would run a high risk of being a pariah if it got out in the public. The way the laws are set up and the way its handled essentially makes you guilty before proven innocent.

Like I said morally I think a lot of the child pornography law is dead on... but the technical side of things makes this hard to enforce and easy to abuse.
