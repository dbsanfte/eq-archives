kitsinu
Worlds of Warcraft is also talking about one character per server. This isn't an isolated incident, it is an industry trend (with 300 k people paying around 10$ per month for three years for just one example, it IS an industry).

Benefits for the industry : More money from families and people determined to twink no matter the cost. Less storage (not sure how important that is considering the cost of recordable DVDs).

Benefits for the player : Less twinking, more value per character that plays seriously. The characters have more value because you either need to recruit from another server, get someone to buy another account, or delete an existing character. While there will be cross server twinking, any character you twink from another server is one that you are either recruiting away from the other server's guild, or that will compete with your own guild. There will be far less farming to twinks. Farming to get trade goods yes, but not many will be twinks, so prices will be much more reasonable for the level of the item (which will reduce the amount of farming due to the lack of value). More focus will be devoted to low levels, which will make starting a new character more fun.

Personally, I am looking forward to WoW more than SWG or EQ2. While EQ2 has the better graphics, WoW will be a breath of fresh air after three years of Verant's philosophy. WoW's philosophy will probably end up like Verant's, but there will be a time that it isn't a grind.

We will see if it is a better or worse system. I think it has a lot of potential. What all the problems associated with it are, we will have to wait to see the full extent.
