Donar
Malo. I know, most people think it's just a raiding spell, but I use it every day, whether exp'ing or raiding. If you're hunting in PoP zones and get a bounced slow, it can hurt.. BIGtime. ;) By casting malo on the incoming and slow as the mob gets to the camp, I almost never get a resist. Since you have torpor, you shouldn't be hurting too much for mana by casting malo, especially if you have a horse on top of that. I would like to have Pox, but get by without it. I would like to have Avatar (well, primal avatar -- I'm too poor to use regular Avatar) but get by without it also. However, I would be much less confident in my ability to slow mobs consistently, without malo (esp as I am not level 65 yet). Besides, malo will not only help you land slows more consistently, but it'll help the other casters in your group and make roots/snares more reliable. Malo is pricey, but if you have the cash to get it, you won't regret it. Just my opinion. Your mileage may vary. ;)

Roxxanne
Malo is great, and I use it on most everything I fight. The one key thing a Shaman must do in a group or solo is get the mob quickly and reliably slowed, and Malo gives a lot of safety and predictability to that process. Malo on incoming, no agro from Malo, then first slow almost always lands. Easy for the cleric to get into a rhythm knowing slow is going to land. And lets face it, the most common cause of death for a shaman in an exp group is the mob that is overagroed by multiple resisted slows.

Solo, it is really helpful to have already landed a PR debuff because resisting Bane is all too common.
