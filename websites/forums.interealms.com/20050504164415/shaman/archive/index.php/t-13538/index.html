Loramin
Oh dear *shakes his head and sighs*. I'll give you my advice, because I have had a similiar situation, but only if you'll excuse my occaisional inside jokes. My real life housemate and friend is currently a 65 cleric, with wayyy too many AAXP compared to my slacker 59 self. Anyhow, I started her on the game when I was in my 30's (coincidentally we just discussed this during tonight's Buffy episode, so the levels are all fresh in my mind). She then passed me when I was 53, and she's been ahead of me ever since.

At all the times we've been able to group together (from the 40's to the 60's) we've had serious difficulty duoing. What it comes down to is that cleric and shaman are two very good support classes, but together they lack an essential element: Tank/DPS. So you have the following options:

1) Root rot undead together. Not so fun IMHO.
2) Have one of the three of you (shaman, cleric, dogdog) tank while the other two try to add DPS and heal the tank. This works, just not as well as say ... well, most other possible duos.

However, I'm not a negative person by nature, so I want to leave you with a ray of hope ;) I just wanted to make sure that I sufficiently crushed the idea of duoing first *grins evily*. Anyhow, the ray of hope is option #3:

3) Grab a tank and trio. This works incredibly well, and you can use a pretty loose definition of tank. For Lanalini and I, it can be our friend Enderii (Bard, currently 59), our friend Doc (65 warrior), our friend Ffehc (Ranger, currently retired :( ), etc. Pretty much anyone but an int caster or a druid (or another shaman or cleric of course).

In this trio, the tank typically pulls large amounts at a time (in Doc's case) or a steady succession of single pulls (in anyone else's). The shaman slows, dots, JBBs, sicks dogdog, etc., contributing damage while also saving the cleric mana. Since the shaman is keeping the cleric from having to heal as often, she has plenty of mana for fun stuff like her wacky summoned hammer pet, wacky handheld proccing hammer, direct damage spells like Condemnation (no wacky hammers, but still fun), and anything else she can think of. Meanwhile, the tank is just glad to be fully stat-buffed, healed, and most importantly (at least for all the tanks I know) hasted.

I'll admit, this is all a bit high level compared to your situation. But if these are characters you plan to play for awhile, you'll reach these levels eventually. Anyhow, hope you find this helpful.

P.S. About that whole "crush your hope of duoing thing," I was only joking. If you do go ahead with duoing, I wish you all the best of luck, as we need more players who are experimentative enough to keep trying unpopular class combinations until they find something new and original.

I think you'll be happily surprised by how effective you can be with that combo. My wife, cleric, and myself, Shaman, duo quite a bit.

I guess it's interesting for us because there are quite a few different ways to play the combo (vs healer/tank combo). Either of us can tank after slowed, we can both tank, we can both sit back and root/rot/dd etc. We both have pets that can help and root for crowd control. If we find a good undead spot (nadox lately, HS, burning woods, dreadlands, etc in the past) her dots/nukes come into play making the combo very efficient. We also sit 3 feet away from each other, so either duo or in a group, we're very effective in keeping folks (each other) alive, crowd control, etc because of the instant communication.

Shaman/cleric is also a pretty good start to any group, especially 2 that play together often (and/or have the advantage above). We group fairly often with the same people but I'm sure if we decided to look for a group it wouldn't take long.

One last remark, while MoR is reduced by our slows, MoK is not. Haste both of you, get some low delay weapons and you'll see a small but noticeable difference in your fights. For what it's worth, she MoR/K's 90 percent of the mobs we fight and at higher level's you'll find items that cast one of them (can't remember atm and she's not at home) so the mana usage is negligible.

Again, it comes down to whether you're having fun or not (to us at least). If you're not happy playing a char (tank) and want to play a shaman, don't let the fact that there are better combo's out there stop you. It's a bit like waiting all day for the perfect group instead of taking the folks you have and making it work in a different spot or zone. Heck, you could probably find undead at virtually every level if you really wanted to max efficiency.
