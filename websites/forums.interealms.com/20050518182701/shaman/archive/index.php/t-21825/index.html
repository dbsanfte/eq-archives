Thorzion
Much of my problem answering this question stem from the dualityof my thought concerning shaman.

I have in my head one idea of what a shaman is, and I have in practice another idea of what a shaman is. First, to my thoughts on what a shaman is:

A shaman is a priest. With the notable exception of frogloks (who should have had enchanters and NOT shaman to fit in with then current game lore and practice) shaman are the only healers of the "primitive" races: barbarian, troll, ogre, iksar and Vah Shir. A shaman of these races is charged with healing their warriors in battle and using magic to equip them with the ability to wage war. A shaman, as a product of a more basic civilization, is trained in the art of war and while probably not able to deal as much pain and damage as his brother warrior is still competent enough to earn his enemies respect.

Through his ability to heal sickness and cure disease the shaman has gained a mastery over the forces of posion and disease, allowing him to use those dreaded plagues as a weapon against any that should stand against him. Able to weaken his enemies and rot them from the inside out, a shaman is truly a fearsome enemy to have.

That is what I thought of when I read the original description of a shaman that came with the Kunark Box. I was a healer...so like a cleric but not. Cool. I can DoT...cool. I'm gonna be a battle priest....very cool.

And then, after 4 years of playing I have a more accurate description of shaman in practice:

I can slow a little better than others. I can spend large amounts of mana on stat buffs that may or may not make any difference to the outcome of any given fight. I can dot, but unless I know that the fight will drag on for a few minutes its not mana efficient to do so. I can patch heal while I wait for a real healer to do some efficient healing to my targets. I can do damage via melee, but only if I am going against targets that are long green or in content 4 years old. I can, however, take a good beating because of my heal over time spells.

I wont get into raiding with a shaman because I stopped doing that over a year ago. We can only stack 2 or 3 deep and still be effective. Any more shaman than that show up get to try and DoT (with our no resist mod huge agro dots) or play FA whore to all the people that can make a difference in the raid (damage dealers).

Am I bitter? A little. Am I discouraged? Alot. Am I angry? Well, when I hear and see bards soloing 30 exp mobs at a time and then hear people say that my class is overpowered because we can solo dragons that used to take a group and dont even give noticible experience or appreciable loot anymore, then yes, I get angry. When I hear devs that have no idea what happens in the game beyond level 50 (when they all apparently stopped playing) tell me that I'm fine, then yes, I get angry.

Somewhere along the line, someone got confused about shaman. And something needs to change.

Seems like a fitting comparison to me. Since my notion that shaman are not longer priests, but a debuffing necro hybrid, I've tried to come up with the perfect analogy to describe our class.

Farm tractors seem like it. While on some levels farm tractors seem like a very versitle "utility" class the reality is, because of its slow speed, a farm tractor is a very specialized vehicle, master of a particular setting but of little use outside of the muddy farm dirt. I hate to say it, but this is the direction that the shaman is heading (well ok we're already there, but after reading the summit reports from Glor, I fear we will continue down this road of specialization). Shamans will continue to be the best debuffer it seems by a small margin and unable to fill any other role.

On the face of it a farm tractor could do anything, haul tv's, pick up groceries, take the family to the movies, a night out on the town. With its ability to towe anything all one would need to do is hitch up the right wagon for your tractor, and your good to go. But in practice this notion is absurd.

Shamans are aporaching the status of a specialist. Our specialization seems to be debuffing, and rapid personal manna regeneration. For all the talk of shamans "untility" the truth is often hybrids can best us in all our secondary area's of ability. We are a specialized class, our general "utility" has diminished with every expansion. I hope that we get better debuffs that justify our specialization status.

I started my shaman thinking I would be a "battle priest" a primative healer, but SoE has disuaded me of that notion.
