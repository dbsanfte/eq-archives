Groovdog
Hehe a topic after my own heart. Stick with the SK if you are set chosing between the two.

Technical issues: if you have the machine, SOE boxing works just fine (download the MS program as mentioned). Personally I 3 box/2 box and single on 3 machines to do my 6 box. All are SOE only, EQW needing to be updated on patch days is concern if you are using it. I have adopted the brute force approach in that I carry 2gb+ ram on the 2 boxed machines and weakest is 2.8Ghz while the 3boxer is a HT 3+ Ghz machine. Fast HDDs (serial) and video cards (Radeon 9800Pro and better) helps as well. Bband is very helpful but I have 3boxed in a stationary setup (pull to grp) on dialup. But if you die on one all will die as the load lag will kill you literally :)

As far as boxing goes, you have to figure what works for you. Two main rules apply IMHO.

#1 get a good base setup. This means machines/classes/hotkeys, etc. that work for you. Personally I run shammy/chanter/warrior on top layers (principal character on each machine) and wizard/pally/cleric on lower layers. Notice a couple things, the complicated guys (chanter/shammy) tend to be on top. The easy guys (wizard/cleric/pally) tend to be on bottom. Pally is for dps/grp heal/BSS/LoH only. He is a no aggro setup. He is likely soon to be replaced with a ranger for better dps and track so I will likely rework the layers so I can autofire the ranger (which means he has to be on solo machine). Warrior is currently solo on a laptop as he is the sacrificial lamb and main tank (8K+ unbuffed) so he can save the grp with AOE taunt and enable me to get a evac off if needed. Overall notice the tendency to stick to simple classes when boxing. Boxing a bard is not recommended unless your others are single function classes (wiz/cleric). Pet classes are not good for boxing either as the tend to require more maintenance relative to mob placement (the tendency to push creates issues). Hotkeys should be setup to do main functions (nuke/heal) etc. and you will likely want an /assist and potentially a /follow function hotkeyed depending on how many you are boxing and how you play. I rarely cast a spell (or take any action for that matter) in combat that is not hotkeyed.

#2 thing and most likely the more important of the 2. Boxing is about control. Places where you can not at least partially control the environment (i.e. adds, roamers, places with large aggro range) are not good for you. The more you box the more true this becomes and is especially relavent 4+. Having other live players helps :) Simple mobs (not lots of AE effects that have to be dispelled) is helpful as well. Funny thing is this describes a whole lot of the 1grp content anyways. Know your limitations but also your strengths and play to them. With strong characters you would be shocked at what you can duo.

The best part of largescale boxing is the control (and the loot) the bad part is 1) no social aspect and 2) when things go really south you are likely talking CR. I am better able to rescue situations up to a point (I run all guys so I can control what happens) but after that point you just cant mash enough keys to fix it.

Oh and shammy is likely the #1 boxing partner because they literally do it all in boxable content. Cant beat heal/cc/slow/dps even if shammy arent the best at it all. This is especially true if you have uber shammy. My shammy tanks (well offtanks actually) all content under 1000hitters so I can take 4 pulls in LDoN hards fairly ok if I survive the first 5 secs (i.e. get #1 mob slowed and 3 and 4 adds mezzed while warrior tanks #2).
