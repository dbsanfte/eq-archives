Here's what I've learned:

This game is about two (2) statistics, and only two.

My HP. And Mob_001's hp.

The relative negative delta (change) of those two numbers is ALL THERE IS TO THIS GAME.

It's easy to argue (given the number of posts here) about the differences/balance of the classes, and the playstyles of the people involved, but the truth of the matter is that the power of any class can be summed up by the formula:

((My hp - (mobX's damage to me x MobX's type) x (my damage to mob + my ability to increase my hp)/time + survivability)) - (group desirability + raid stackability) = TotalPower.

Where: X=(mob total hp + damage to me + mob total regen + mob total resists)

Where: MobX's type = MobX x

1 if MobX = normal
-1 if MobX = undead
-5 if MobX = unslowable
-15.25 if MobX = unrootable
-475,396,127.993 if MobX = unslowable + unrootable

Where: damage to me = mob damage - (slow + heals to me) + summoning + (+/- undead + (+/- resists to class specialities) + friends brought during engagement.

Where: survivability = FD + gate + pet + DA + memblur

Where raid stackability = (dps - aggro) + (healing ability - specialization penalty reductions in healing power)

Where groups desirability = specialization - raid stackability

Conclusion:

Against all mobs other than undead/unslowable, on average, shaman is the most powerful class in the game.

Against undead, non-summoning etc: other classes have more TotalPower than shaman.

Percentage of unsummoning/undead mobs, versus "normal"/level = inconclusive.

Final conclusion: shaman + time = TotalPower.

So my bitch is: shaman - time = EQ.
