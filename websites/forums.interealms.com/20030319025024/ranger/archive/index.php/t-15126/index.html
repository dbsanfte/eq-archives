Gavving
Well my experience with AA's is that they don't really significantly impact your play style or relative power until you get into the Class aa's.

The 3 more hp regen might be noticable <55 and chloro, but after that it's not really a noticable increase. Runspeed 3 helps in dungeons and getting around in raids. All the other options in general are pretty worthless.

As for Archtype... Crits are neat, but maybe give you an extra 100-200 damage a fight total. Nothing to write home about. (with CF1) Mental Clarity 1 is marginally noticable, but if your paying to KEI to solo anyway, it's kinda pointless. 10 extra mana a minute isnt gonna save the world. Basically all the archtype skills are like this. The first level is marginally useless. The 3rd level is marginally useful. Natural Durability is the major exception to this. First level is good, and 3rd level is great.

As for class skills of course they're really good. But as for getting them while your still 59, I don't really see this as a good idea. Theres a significant power increase for rangers at 60 with WP, weaponshield, call of predator, etc. If you can't xp in PoV, then just stay 60 and get class AAs.

The most efficient method of advancement is Level to 60 or even 65, then AA until the cows come home. But do it however you want to make the game interesting and fun in my opinion.
Linara
As for your first 2 AA points, I would sink them into innate runspeed.

For any indoor situation runspeed is a a lifesaver. Infact, I might almost recommend getting run3 before moving on with your leveling.
Once you hit 60 you can do 2 different things. You can finish off your leveling or move to aa.

The aa grind to am3 eq is pretty intense. If you plan on using your bow exclusively for groupping/raiding, I would recommend this aa path.
run3, regen3, combat fury3, EQ, AM3, Innate Camo

If you plan on mixing up melee with archery for damage, put combat fury off to the side and invest in Natural Durability instead.

I can't say for certain which path will be faster, levels or AA. That's something that you will have to decide. I went on long exp runs right before PoP came out so I wouldn't have to make this decision. If I had to, I'd prolly level 60 then go straight for am3/eq, then get 65 then work on the rest.

Happy Grinding :)
