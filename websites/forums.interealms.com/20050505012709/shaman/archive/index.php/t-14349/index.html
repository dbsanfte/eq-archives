Don't worry too much about gear at your level. I think in that period
I wore a lot of banded or bronze brigandine (from paludal caverns)
armor. Pretty much whatever I could pick up while exp'ing. I think
SplitPaw has a couple decent armor drops for shaman. But frankly, I'd
say push for experience until ~40, then either harvest some platinum
in Rathe Mountains or Crystal Caverns or newbie zones for silks, pelts
and other tradeskill items. By and large, you'll probably find better
gear for time invested by farming platinum and buying in the bazaar than
you would farming equipment.

I'd suggest buying cheap items for every slot, shoot for 1) Armor Class,
2) Hit Points, then 3) Wisdom/Flat Mana. AC will keep ya alive, HP you
can easily convert to mana via cannibalizing, and wisdom/mana comes
last as mana regen rate > size of mana pool.

Once you have decent stuff in each slot, save up for larger items that
will last you for many levels.

Let me think of some suggestions.

Rings: It's pretty standard for shaman to wear velium fire wedding rings.
I've been wearing mine for ages, still using it at 61. They're 6 AC
and 65 hitpoints. If you an find one for 300p or below, buy it.
Chest: Ry'Gorr battle mail is pretty nice and cheap. Should run ~100p.
Misc...: Jaundiced Bone stuff is pretty nice. Good AC, cool plate graphic,
and the occasional useful mana-free click spell.
Hands: SplitPaw hide gloves are quite nice and may in fact be attainable
by you at your present level. Or, Leaf Scaled Gloves are nice and
very very cheap (50-100p).
Head: Metal Linked Crown is cheap and will last a while. ~100p.

Just a few ideas. Do a search in the bazaar for each slot. Make sure
to select your class/race/deity combination and also a price range, then
search each slot for Hit Points items.

If you prefer to camp or quest for your armor, try SplitPaw, perhaps the
East Wastes Ry'Gorr camp (not until high 30's though, I believe), the
Twilight Sea quests for totemic armor, and also Hollowshade Moor is a
very interesting zone with a lot of great items for the level they're
attainable at.

Anyway, hunt the bazaar, farm some cash, work on levels, *cough*
befriend people working on tradeskills and nudge them for extras *cough*
and as always, search and read these boards and sites like Allakhazam.

Best of luck to you!

Teiresias of Lanys

While good advise, dont discount some of those big wis items. As an ogre, you wis is lower than alot of other classes. While being able to turn hp into mana is an invaluable skill that has ruined every other caster class for me (thier mana regen is way to slow, exp with the KEI nerf) doing it ALL the time gets old fast. Nothing made me want to stop playing my shaman more than having to canni all the time in order to keep up with mana.

When my shaman was young (25 or so) I stumbled across this board and folowed the same advise...AC/HP then wis. My base wis was 105 or so. It didnt move much as I tried to aquire the highest AC/hp items I could. Was forced to canni my way to the early 30's and HATED my shaman because of it. Buffs and spells took SOOO much mana because they were balanced against my ability to regen mana by cannibalizing.

Then I decided to increase my mana pool to a size I was comfortable with and THEN worry about the other stuff. At the base of it all shaman ARE casters after all, and live and die by thier mana pool.

I got my wis up to the mid 160's and got some good +mana items and that helped immensely.

The point? Try both ways if you want, but find what works best for you.
