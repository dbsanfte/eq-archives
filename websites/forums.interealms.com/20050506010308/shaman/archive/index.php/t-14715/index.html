Loreddar Of Karana
If you don't have LoY and cannot get Stoicism, then I would say that Canni II isn't worth the trouble. You don't have much to gain with it, especially if you solo a lot. The nice thing about using a Canni spell is to regain mana as quickly as possible, but you correctly point out that maintaining HPs is also crucial because of your soloing style. I also solo almost exclusively and can identify with that. At lvl 39, your best ways of regaining the lost HPs used by Canni II are Greater Healing and Regeneration. Greater Healing gives back 300 HPs for 150 mana. However, it takes 5 Canni IIs to return to you the 150 mana you used to cast Greater Healing, but uses 335 HPs to do so, for a net loss of 35 HPs. Not a good result. Regeneration gives you an increase of 5 HPs per tick. At lvl 39, Regen will last for about 13 min (give or take - I haven't figured it out exactly). This will be 130 ticks of Regen, or about 650 HPs for 100 mana. The Canni II cost to regain that 100 mana will be 268 HPs (which actually gets you 120 mana). However, you still have to sit and wait for the Regen to work. You may be able to gain mana more quickly, but you still have to sit and wait for Regen to recover your HPs. This is a much better result, but you're still going to be sitting and waiting (which will also bring mana back up on its own).

The situation changes dramatically with Stoicism, because you can recover a LOT more HPs for much less mana, making it more efficient. This is what I do now that I have both spells. It takes 6 Canni II spells to recover the mana used by Stoicism for an HP cost of 402. However, I get back 640 HPs from Stoicism, so I can cast 3 more Canni IIs before I start having a net loss of HPs. This means I can gain 90 extra mana by casting 9 Canni II spells for every 1 Stoicism with no net loss of HPs. And I have a couple of focus items that makes this ratio even better. It still takes some time for all the casting, but it's a lot less downtime than just sitting.

Now, things change quite a bit if you're not soloing and have a cleric that can heal you. You no longer have to worry about the HP cost to regain mana, because someone else is healing you. This is like getting free mana. When you're not casting spells to help the group kill mobs, then you cast Canni and let the group healer give you back your HPs. If you plan to do groups instead of soloing, then Canni II would be worth the effort.

Weyalan
People tand to focus too much on the hp:mana ratio, when really, its not that important. At the end of the day, its all about rate of mana return over time - Canni2 allows you to reclaim mana faster, and yes, that mana might come at a slightly higher price in terms of hp cost, but you are missing the point if you worry about it.

Let me put it another way: Hypothetically, lets assume that you have a huge hp pool of say 5000hp. Assume you have a mana pool of 2000mana. Lets assume you regen 20hp per tick. Ok, calculate how long it it will take to cannibalise from OOM to full using Cannidance1. Do the same thing using Cannidance2. Obviously, you will get to full mana using Canni2 much faster. But if you sit on your butt for the difference you will regen more hp than the loss from the ratio.

Using 67:30 for Canni2 and 50:24 for Canni1

Canni2: it will take 67 casts (& ticks) @ 30mana, and will cost 4489hp (at 67hp per cast) and take 6 minutes 42 seconds.

Canni1: it will take 84 casts (& ticks) @ 24mana, and will cost 4200 (at 50hp per cast) and take 8 minutes 24 seconds.

so, using Canni 2 will cost you 289 more hp to generate 2000mana, but will take get you the mana in 1 minute 42 seconds less. If you then sat on your but you would regenerate 340 hp in that 1 minute 42 seconds, which is more than the difference. So, actually, Canni2 is more efficent than Canni1 if you take hp, mana and time into account

To answer your question, use Canni2 as soon as you can get your hands on it!

Landragoran, while I agree Canni II is worth using because it's faster, it is still worse ratio.

From Lucy:

Cannibalize:
1: Decrease Hitpoints by 50
2: Increase Mana by 20 (L24) to 29 (L65)

Cannibalize II:
1: Decrease Hitpoints by 67
2: Increase Mana by 30 (L39) to 37 (L65)

The mana isn't 1 extra per level, it's a linear scale between the level you get it and level 65.

At level 39, when you get Cannibalize II, the mana return from Cannibalize is 24.

Given by: 20 + ((65-39) * (37-30) / (65-24))
(Think of this as a line on a graph - 65 is the level you get 37 mana, 24 is the level you get 20 mana, 39 is the level we're looking at)

At 39, the ratio of HP/mana for Canni I is 2.08. At 39, the ratio of HP/mana for Canni II is 2.23.
I.e. At 39, Canni II takes more HP per point of mana returned - it has a worse ratio.


If you don't believe me, you can simply look at level 65: At 65, Canni I is 1.72 HP/mana, Canni II is 1.81 HP/mana. Furthermore, Canni III is 1.76 and Canni IV is 1.80. The Cannibalization AA skill is the same as Canni IV.

In summary, at level 65 (and at most levels), Canni I is the most efficient in terms of simple HP/mana, but no-one would dream of using it. The difference is insignificant in the scheme of things and fewer casts is better. :)
