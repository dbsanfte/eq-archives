Calabar
I agree with Justice. Sony isn’t about to cut their financial throats by allowing what you suggest. I think the best we can hope for is that they will open up a second bazaar zone so more people can set up vendors. And though I’d love to be able to sell items while I’m also playing, the bazaar and now the shared bank space makes buying and selling so much easier than they used to be that I’m perfectly happy just selling while I’m asleep or at work.

Just as a basis of comparison, UO handles vendors in a very different way. Or at least they did a couple of years ago before I quit. If you owned a house, or were friended to a house, you could buy a vendor deed. This cost you X amount of money. You then stood in the spot in your house you wanted your vendor to stand and activate the deed. This included the porch and steps if you wanted your vendor to be visible from the outside. Once activated, an NPC vendor would appear with a backpack you could fill with items to sell. Since this wasn’t one of your characters it would remain up at all times. However, you also had to pay your vendor or he would go poof and drop any remaining items on the ground. As long as he was holding money for you he would take it out of that fund. But if you didn’t lave any money on him it could be a problem. I can honestly say I never had this happen to me or knew of anyone it happened to, as the payment was rather small.

From a buyer’s perspective you had to run all over the planet looking for what you wanted to buy. You’d run up to a player house and click on a vendor to see what he was carrying. If he didn’t have what you wanted you continued your search. I kept a list of where I bought my items and hoped I’d find more when I went back the next time. From a seller’s perspective you just crossed your fingers and hoped that people interested in buying your product could find your small cottage hidden in the middle of nowhere behind thirty other houses filled with vendors.

Shopping in UO was a lot more exciting than EQ. It was very much like a treasure hunt. And you could keep you items up for sale at all times, even when playing your character. But EQ’s system is a hundred times more efficient for both buyers and sellers. Just think of what it would be like if you had to search through every vendor’s inventory, one by one, looking for that specific weapon you wanted. And then think of what it would be like if these same vendors were scattered haphazardly across Norrath instead of all being located in one room. Of the two systems, I prefer EQ for its efficiency, even if it does cost us a bit of the excitement of UO’s system.
