You main group functions are to pull and tank. On raids it will be to mostly assist and split tank as necessary.

At 64 I have up
1) Death Peace (our best feign)
2) Rotating Slot- Mental Corruption *50 health 100 mana*
Shroud of Death *50 health, good for disease immune mobs*
Deathly Temptation *life to heath*
Replace Shroud of Death with Shoud of Chaos if your son has it
3) Torrent of Hate -Steals Attack and gives it to the group
4) Festering Darkness - Another important spell. Great for holding aggro as it's a weak dot + snare
5) Aura of Pain - Does 200 dd which can crit and steals a good chunk of ac
6) Touch of Volatis - 350 Lifetap which can crit from nukes or Theft of Life for bigger heals
7) Zevfeer's Bite - 200 point group heal + mana can also crit and can exceptional heal for every member of your part at the same time.
8) Spear of Decay, or Deny Undead.

The only hotkeys I'd worry about are Assist, Incoming, and a /attack off /cast # /cast # /cast # for feign. This is because it tends to fizzle a bit and having it try and cast 3 times will save you some hassles.

The major gear piece for most SK's is the prenerf Circlet of Shadows. It has unlimited charges of insta cast gather shadows. It's a fantastic tool if you can find one for sale.

Soloing is possible but most find it more hassle than it's worth. The general strat is to aggro with dots and run while your pet kills the mob from behind. Since SK pets are really really weak you have to constantly watch that it doesn't get aggro.
Jinheim
Playing an SK is pretty easy. Basically any of your atk taps, dots, or snares will hold aggro very well. Festering darkness is amazing for aggro, since it casts so fast. Ayestole casts slower, but is just an absurd amount of aggro.

Apart from aggro, you are also going to want to use your atk taps to help increase the DPS of the melees in your group, and clinging darkness to help with heals and mana regen. Just remember to pace yourself so that you don't go oom, which can cause problems in maintaining aggro.
