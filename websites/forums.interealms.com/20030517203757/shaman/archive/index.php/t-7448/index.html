Harvyst
Wow. I never had more than 4 different items at a time.

My own personal favourites:

Extended Affliction - that extra 20% time on dots means life or death. EA III and pox.... oh, I feel faint. :) It also makes slow last longer, woooooo....

Extended Enhancement - again, extra 20% time on buffs. EE III is great - focus lasts 2 hours, sta lasts an hour and twenty, replenishment and celerity last longer, it's a keeper. More time between buffs is more mana, basically. I know some people prefer to put things on and off, but I'm a buff-as-we-go shaman, and if I had to stop, put thing in bag, cast replenishment, put thing back in bag every 20 minutes, I'd go crazy. "want acumen? wait, I'm putting my necklace on".

I think mana preservation is the next most useful one, and if I had to only pick 3, those would be the ones I'd pick. It's kind of an all-around nice focus effect.

The others, well:

Spell haste - nice, but really, unless you're casting focus, and I'm not sure it works on focus, the spell times aren't that long. I'm more worried about running out of mana than I am about not getting spells in first.

ER - nice, but we don't kite. We tend to root/rot or tank, so while ER makes for nicer pulls in some tight situations (katta, splitting the guards), that's kind of rare. Usually I'm right up against the mob I'm fighting.

Affliction Efficency - well, it's sort of covered under mana preservation, IIRC. Nice, but I wouldn't sacrifice hp/ac for it.

Improved Healing - Err, I'm usually healing during a downtime, and I'm never the primary healer if I can wangle it. I'd rather be slowing buffing dotting attacking and patch healing, not primary healing.

Impressive collection of baubles by the way.
