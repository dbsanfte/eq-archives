Ruatonim
I want to start by saying that I really hadn't planned on buying this game. There really is no specific reason that I chose to try it out. In fact, the very fact that I couldn't really find anything besides the one post on this board from a couple weeks ago is what prompted me to write out my feelings- to help people make an "informed" decision with their money and time.

I will also preface this by saying that I cancelled my EQ account around march of this year, although I did continue to log in every few weeks for a couple hours to chat with a friend that had taken over my account. So I wasn't "driven" to WoW or anything silly like that. My intent isn't necessarily to bash EQ, although I WILL bash EQ. If my EQ-bashing is going to offend you, just click back now and save us all the time. I will be doing constructive bashing, but it's going to be bashing nonetheless.

Finally, before I go into discussion about WoW, I want to say that when I first started EQ, I was impressed by the game. It was about the beginning of 2001 that I started, and the newness really drew me in. However, as time went on, there wasn't anything new about EQ to me. Encounters were roughly the same, although there were different MOBs and different group compositions, but the overall theme was pretty much the same. This repetition is what ultimately drove me from EQ as it just wasn't fun anymore. I'll go into a better discussion about this later, but for now, yes, I DO realize that WoW will eventually be the same way (at least, I assume it will).

-----------------------------------------------------------
I bought the game last night. I debated between the collector's edition and the regular box. The price difference isn't a huge drawback, but I couldn't justify the extra $30.00 for the pet. I'm sure the other extras (map/artbook/etc.) are nice, but the only long term benefit I saw was the pet, and I don't know if that will be as big of a thing by end-game anyhow. I've seen one person with a mini diablo pet so far, and they were running away from me towards another town, so I can't comment on what benefit (if any) there is to pet ownership. Also, for my night elf, I am able to purchase a pet owl for 50 silver (which is a steep price currently - level 11). So, while it might not be as good as a zergling, at least there are options for pets in game without the extra $30 expenditure.

When I got home, I installed the game, which actually took quite a bit longer than I expected. I guess 4 GB of files being installed on your computer via (4) CDROMs takes a while. It did give me time to peruse the manual a bit and decide upon what class/race to choose (more on that later). I then created my account and signed onto the server. At this point, there was a patch, but it was only 19.2 MB. Since I have dial up and because it was late, I decided to let it patch while I slept for the night. I think it took an hour to download the patch. When I woke up, I logged back on and chose my server (normal server - PVP servers were VERY crowded by the way).

This brings me to my first EQ vs WoW moment. Load times. There isn't any load time. Well, okay, there is, but I haven't noticed more than one minute of load time upon first logging into the game. After that point, I don't think you ever load; I haven't in the 9 hours of play I've had. Even after you die you don't load, and you will die, but I'll discuss that later. Conversely, EQ has the most horrendous load times of ANY game I've ever played. I guess the higher polygon count models and other stuff makes for this, but really it gets to be excessive when you run from point A to point B and spend more time loading than actually running. Some zones are almost instant load, but others (POK anyone?) can take more than a few minutes depending on the number of people in the zone or what trees the zone has.

When I first arrived in the game world for the first time, I was actually pleasantly surprised. I've been expecting cartoon world, but other than the PCs and NPCs, everything is pretty realistic looking. If nothing else, the graphics for the landscapes are ON PAR with EQ, although I think they are better. As far as the PC and NPC animations, who cares? The humans in EQ don't look human just as much as the humans in WoW don't look human, and there aren't elves, trolls, ogres,vah shir, tauren, erudites, high elves, wood elves, etc. in real life anyway. The whole "I can't play a game with 'cartoon' graphics" argument is pretty BS anyway. I found the graphics to be relatively high quality in any case, and the main thing is they do the job and they don't detract from gameplay (for example in EQ, the 3rd person rotations where you see inside your head if you are too close to a wall).

Speaking of gameplay, this is the one area that really shines. First, the game is FUN to play. You don't just hit auto attack and then eat lunch/watch tv. Well, okay, you COULD do that in WOW, but battles are over pretty quickly (solo), and every class has unique abilities that they can employ (casters cast, melees have special abilities). I'm still pretty low level so things could change, but I really think that battle length will remain about the same throughout the non raiding portion of the game.

Second, quests are integrated into leveling. Yes, you can level up to 60 and probably never do a single quest, although I think you have to do a few to advance to new areas. In any case, there are always tons of quests that you can take on at any given moment. I have yet to not be involved in at least one quest, and as I advance in level, NPCs start getting new quests for me to work on. Most of the quests so far have been collect items or slaughter X-number of monsters or find the NPC. However, quests have been very straight foward so far. For example, in EQ when you get a collect X-number of items quests, how many hours do you spend killing until you finally get ONE item to drop? Anyone who's ever done the coldain prayer shawl quests, how many wurms did you slaughter before you got your glowing wurm biles? Yeah, we all know those lucky SOBs that found them on the first kill(s) (maybe you ARE that lucky SOB), but the rule of EQ is quest pieces are rare. So rare, in fact, that you are likely to spend more than 2 hours to complete a simple quest where there is NO reward whatsoever.

The opposite to the EQ quest is the WoW quest. First, you KNOW what reward you are going to get in WoW, it tells you when you first sign up. If you don't want the downgrade piece of cloth armor or the shoddy beaten up hammer, you simply don't accept the quest and save your time. EQ? Yeah..you don't know what the quest reward will be. Usually its an amount of experience that you can't even tell you got and probably an item that is worse in quality than what you got when you first created your character in 1999. However, EQ did get better after Luclin as far as quest rewards go Second, you know what you need to do for a WoW quest. The downside is it does take some of the mystery out of the quest (perhaps more advanced quests aren't quite the gimmies?); however, at least you know the quest is either working or broken. In EQ more often than not, the quest is probably broken and that is why you can't figure it out. Third, there is a story line tying all the quests together. Does EQ even have a storyline? In fact, the storyline is good enough that you don't really think about how you are leveling.

This then is my third gameplay issue. You aren't playing the game to get level-X, you are playing to advance the story. Leveling is a by-product of your time in the game. EQ is the opposite of this, you log on specifically to gain a new level or AA point (or raid). EQ becomes a job that you pay to have, and this is where a lot of people become disenchanted with EQ I think. Every race in WoW has a different storyline to follow (possibly different classes have different storylines within those races, though I'm guessing this is only at the upper levels 30+). This means there are at least 8 times to play the game before you will be done with the storyline. Then there will be raid events to participate in. Frankly, I'm a little wary about this part of the game as I think it ruined EQ when you started getting the disparity between the haves and have-nots. In the end, its a necessary part of the game; I just hope blizzard does it better than sony.

Fourth, equipment. In EQ you have no chance to upgrade your equipment outside of loot camps or raid kills (quests in EQ, by nature, ARE one of these two categories). Before the tradeskill revamps, you could either buy from a vendor or buy the exact same item from someone who tradeskilled it. If you wanted a good item, you had to camp somewhere and loot it, although this has slightly changed in EQ today. In any case, in WoW, you start with a weapon that is good enough to fight off the low level monsters. You then upgrade that weapon within the first 5 levels with a quest to prepare you for the next 5-10 levels. During this time, you may find a weapon that will be even better as loot, but you can count on quests to provide your weaponry. Also, Tradeskills represent logical upgrades in WoW already!! Granted I haven't seen end game gear, but EVERYTHING is level restricted or soul-bound (no-drop) anyway, so it hardly matters if better gear exists at the upper end. The game is meant to be played with what you can get by playing it (not by farming it and twinking). I think this is a great balance concept, and it should lead to less mudflation in WoW.

Fifth, tradeskills offer advancement for your character and gear upgrades or beneficial effects. Combines are skill restricted. For instance, you need level 45 tailoring before you can make a bag (this is the level at which you are trained to make the item). Recipes are either bought in this fashion or found as quest rewards (all upper end recipes are quest rewards). The interface is almost exactly like the new EQ interface, but slightly better. I don't think you can fail a combine. Therefore, the only limitation on WoW tradeskills is you need to have a certain level of skill, be a certain level and have the necessary components. The only other factor is time as all combines have a casting time during which you cannot move. The glaring downside is you can only master two tradeskills in addition to cooking and fishing(you ARE able to unlearn a skill though). As a certified tradeskill masochist, I'm disappointed in this decision and I hope they reverse this stance, but I will survive if they keep it as is. Tradeskill gear is also soul-bound on equip (attuneable).

Sixth, death is only temporary. You don't lose levels. I don't think you even lose experience. Resurrection spells are simply a way to gate back to your corpse rather than run back. Trust me, you will die. You will also be glad that there is no EQ CR. I don't care if people think it dumbs down the game, CORPSE RUNS ACROSS MULTIPLE ZONES ARE NOT FUN!!!!! Why should there be an unfun aspect of a game?? I would describe death in WoW as a learning experience or a minor inconvenience. Death in EQ goes from a minor inconvenience to a major catastrophe depending on what zone and where and who you know and what time it is.

Finally, everyone can solo!!! My night elf priest has no issues soloing monsters his level or lower, and sometimes can take monsters up to a couple levels higher one on one. If the healer class of the game can solo, everyone should be able to. Downtime, what's downtime? Yeah, I know you were thinking that there has to be a catch, but there isn't! Downtime is a max of 18 seconds, and that is going from 1hp/1mana to full.

Now for the bad. There are moments of lag lock up for me. This is probably because the servers are being crowded by hordes of people wanting to try the game (and also due to my dial up :) ). However, I lag worse in EQ. Its only bad because its an annoyance at times. I've only died once due to lag related causes, and there were two on me so I was probably going to die anyhow.

Monsters are mostly variations of the same model just slightly bigger with a different name. However, I've been in only one area of one race's starting position on the map. This doesn't bother me; I could handle it if all the NPCs were human models (ala EQ glitches) though, so this might bother other people. It's too early to judge this though; I'm sure things change past level 20.

Hard to find groups. Its still early. Every class can solo. People like to explore without the limitations of a group and get the maximum loot. Frankly, to me this isn't a problem yet as its easy to solo (hoping this is true to 60). This doesn't imply there is no risk, but one of the advantages is that risks are more of an annoyance than say...breaking into plane of fear when fear was an endgame zone of EQ.

$14.99 a month charge. These seem to keep getting higher as time goes on

My final thoughts are the game is definitely worth a try. I have yet to run into anything that isn't fun about the game. Everything runs smoothly. Quests are integrated seamlessly into leveling, and often you are more concerned with finishing a quest than finishing your level. There aren't spell levels like in EQ, you usually get a new ability every or every other level. There are also quite a few dungeons that I've come across.

I did choose a priest and love it. My reasoning was that I wanted to be good aligned, and I wanted to have a place on raids assuming they resemble EQ (not taking any chances!). I think I've covered my experiences up to level 11 as a night elf priest pretty well. If anyone wants more info/sys req's/advice/etc. I will answer any questions posted or PMs.

PS I will be ignoring flames.
PPS I won't be returning to EQ. In fact, it was deleted off my HD last night!

collect quests are the most painful. typically, there are 4 types. first is the rare drop like the tiger/shadow panther quest in stranglethorn. rare drops are 2-5% i'd estimate. for that quest people kill anywhere from 20-100 (literally) tigers before they get the drop

most commonly, there's the uncommon drop. it's hard to estimate but i'd say 1/2 or 1/4 of mobs drop this. typically these are the collect 10-15. these are the fastest.

then there's the high collect/uncommon drop. these things demand lots of drops 20-30 but typically this quest is meant to be done over time and overlap with other quests you're concurrently doing. for example, in your 30s you'll have 5-7 troll-killing quests you work on, all the trolls have a chance to drop teeth. you need to collect a buttload of teeth, but the quest itself is a sort of bonus for completing the arc of quests

finally there's the imobile mob collect. this collect requires you to explore/infiltrate an area and collect items off inanimate objects guarded by creeps. for example, when you need to collect eggs from the glass spiders in lower blackrock spires in your high 50s. these collects are anywhere from 5-15 or so. but typically each inanimate "nest" will yield from 1-3 individual drops

couple other things. blizzard improved the looting system so that if grouped, you have a better chance of getting drops, so that each mob will be eligible to load a drop if anyone in the party is doing a particular quest. for me however, it's more efficient to do collects solo. in general it's best to group up for higher con kill/delivery/escort quests and go solo on even/lower collects.

of course if you play horde just about every quest pre-20s is a collect... even after 3 revisions of barrens. you'll definitely level slower than alliance side at those levels... but in the long run I think it's okay. you'll end up better at your class and probably ahead on tradeskills by the time you hit contested lands
