KEI, for a shaman with Canni V, is certainly not necessary for you to do your job.

But do you want to just "do your job" or do you want to excel, and add all you can to the group?

If its the latter then you should get a kei, and instead of hitting canni IV you can cast an extra rain, dot or nuke and help out the group dps a bit, or help out with heals so the druid has more mana for their rather more effective nukes, or the cleric can add in some of their rather decent dps vs undead.

I don't always go in with KEI either - I like the fact I can get by just fine without it and some days I just want to take it easy. In general I will get a kei where I can, if that means porting to pok and paying for it even - but I won't wait around just because I can't find anyone casting it, nor will I pay to refresh a kei before an adventure if I know its got 30 mins+ left on it still.

Do as you like, but I wouldnt' be surprised if some people get a little upset because you may be perceived to not be adding everything you can to the group. At the end of the day though, any group who won't accept you on that basis is silly - the skill of the player is far more significant.
