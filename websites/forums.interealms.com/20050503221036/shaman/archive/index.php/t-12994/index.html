Islowem
Wisdom has nothing to do with your spells being resisted by mobs.

Resists are figured by (and Im sure someone can come up with real numbers, but here's some made up ones for the concept of it...) ...

Resist check of the spell. All spells have a resist modifier (plus 20, negative 100, negative 300, etc). The more positive this number is, the less likely it is to be resisted.

Mobs Resistance. Mobs, like players, have resist stats (fire, cold, magic, poison, disease). Now, Im fairly certain most mobs are far above most players, but they also have resist stats.

So, when you cast a spell, it checks the modifier on the spell, against the mobs resist to that type of spell (ice based, fire based, etc), and if over a certain amount, the spell lands. If below this amount, its a resist.

So, I hope I didnt confuse you too much, but the main point is, your stats have nothing to do with spells being resisted by monsters. It has everything to do with the modifier on the spell, and the mobs resist stats.

Hope this helps,

Excellent information. I thank each of you for your input.

My understanding of this issue is:

1) Wisdom only affects the amount of mana in your mana pool and how quickly you learn certain skills. A Shaman should seek out equipment primarily for Hit Points and secondarily for Wisdom. This is to facilitate a larger virtual mana pool thorough the use of the Cannibalization line of spells.

2) A mob resists spells cast upon it by factors that involve the difference in level between the mob and the caster, by the natural resistance the mob possesses, and by the spell resistance modifier of the spell being cast upon the mob. Furthermore, Wisdom has no part in the equation involving a mob’s resistance of a spell cast upon it.

3) The amount of Wisdom needed by a level 65 Shaman for hunting is somewhere greater than 150 with 200 to 240 as a good target range. Though, it is probably not necessary to worry about Wisdom, the majority of one’s equipment should have Wisdom modifiers as a secondary benefit. However, Hit Points should definitely be maintained as the top priority when choosing equipment.

That is a good summary, Aztkius.

Regarding how much wisdom you should have at level 65, I would say that the answer is "as much as you can without sacrificing hp/ac". I personally have the view that balance is more important than extremes. Ideally, you would like all of your statistics to be very high. Since you obviously DO need to choose, though, I would lean toward, HP, AC, MANA, WIS, followed by all other statistics. It is important to keep in mind that there are diminishing returns as your wisdom goes up. The difference between 265 wisdom and 285 wisdom is not all that significant. This is why at very high levels a piece of gear with +MANA is more desirable by far than a piece of gear with +WIS.

My unbuffed wisdom is 247 at level 65. This is a comfortable amount for me -- not extremely high, but not extremely low. As other posters have said, though, you will accumulate WIS as your gear changes. Without seeking wisdom increases, my wisdom has gone up dramatically in the last 6 months (it used to hover around 190 when I was level 60ish).

Ok, it has become an article of faith among shamans to sacrifice hp for wisdom so that you can solo mobs "IN TEH FACE" (ITF). This is certainly true from 34 when you get your first pet on up through level 60.

This has changed with PoP. First, melee damage by a shaman drops to effectively zero by the time one moves past 60. Second, PoP mobs past Tier 1 hit too hard to fight ITF, even if slowed. Third, PoP mobs require high DPS to fight. Finally, the ONLY way a shaman can achieve a high level of DPS is to root a mob and stack DoT's.

What this means is that to solo in PoP, you have to have lots of mana, the more the better, and as rapid mana regen as possible. You want to avoid being hit by a mob as much as possible. So to be an effective soloer at 65 you should dump all your hp gear and stoke up on FT, Mana, and wis gear.

Hp can be substituted for mana (Cannibalization, Cann-spells)

With Quiescence (/Torpor) we have a great ratio on mana->hp transfer, the problem lies in getting hp->mana, which is insanely much slower.
The more I play the more important the manapool seems to get cause the whole problem is with manaregen.
The bigger manapool you have, the more you can stack away in it for later... As long as it's never completely full it's all good, the larger pool, the larger margins you have to play around with not to "waste" resources.

Then there's always the view from a raiding standpoint.
Endurancebattles, like Xegony/Fennin/etc where you need to sustain much mana for a long time.
You will never be able to keep up how much you spend with your regen in those battles, so, the bigger manapool you have, the longer you "last" until you have to throw in the towel. This is definately worse for clerics than for us, but noticeable on those fights for us as well, the blue bar shrinks constantly, and one cann every 3 min isn't really enough, and you don' thave time to cast the spells. This might not be the kind of situation you are looking for though.

Get it balanced, if you die, you have too little hp, if you run oom too much and barely ever die, then you can probably increase your manapool a bit (or buy SCM, which if one of the most underrated skills we have, imo)

You are going to want to make sure that you have enough Hps so you can Cann V in the middle of a fight and your hps don't drop so low that you get low health agro.

Its really all about balance, enough wisdom so you don't go oom all the time, enough ac so you don't get smacked around too hard and good hit points so you can cann efficiently and not die too quickly when you get agro.

I am wearing a few "balancing" items. They don't have the best hps but they make up in large amount what I may be lacking overall. For example I wear a high AC/WIS helm with no hps because I am lacking in AC overall and it gives me a nice wis/mana boost as well. I will probably continue to wear it until I can raise my ac on my other gear without sacrificing hps, or find a helm with better hp stats that also has enough AC to keep me balanced.
