First, anyone discussing "utility" as a shaman class feature either needs to define it better, or admit that they are still playing 3 expansions ago. Back in the Luclin-and-earlier days, there were utility classes and utility effects - things that could be done other than raw tanking, raw healing, and raw dps. The number of these that have remained useful has shrunk as the difficulty curve of mobs has pushed against the upper end of several class's models. Regeneration was important in the days of the Fungi Tunic; now it is a wasted buff slot (anyone arguing that 25 hp/tick matters, at all, anymore, needs to come back to this discussion after sitting down to understand the game's math). Stat buffing has become irrelevant as gear has pushed most - if not all - stats first near, then to, and now far far beyond their hard caps. Movement speed enhancement has been rendered obsolete by IRS/SJ. And, most importantly for shaman, slow is being phased out of "utility" because of a two-pronged changed to the game; mitigation makes it less effective in general, and low hp, high resist mobs simply die too quickly for slow to have an effect (if the mob only would have 3 rounds of combat after slow lands, then its effect is essentially trivial). There *are* still "utility" effects that are useful in the new model of EQ. Damage shields used to be considered utility - now, an argument can be made that they are a factor in raw DPS (and another fact that works counter to slow, I might add). Evacs (NOT ports) are a death-management effect, and in some cases (Trial 2) are an integral part of the content. Charms have gone from being a risk to the party to being the only way to kill the new content in sane amouns of time. Snare is useful anywhere that mobs can run, and anywhere where pets might be charmed (between the 2 of those, that's everywhere). But none of these useful "utility" features are the domain of shaman.
