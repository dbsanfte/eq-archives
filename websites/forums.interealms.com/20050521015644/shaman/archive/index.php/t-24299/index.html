taraddar
What happens is there's a base exp per mob. Thats dependant on the level of the mob and exp mod for the zone and I think thats it. Things then get interesting from there as that base exp gets modified for a number of reasons.

Group exp bonus adds additional exp before it splits them amongst the group. Sometime mid PoP this was greatly increased to encorage grouping over soloing. At this point going from 5 people to 6 people adds an additional 20% more exp so you always get at least as much exp with the 6th person there.

Hybrids get an exp bonus to cancle out the exp penalty that they had previously. This was added way back when in kurnark. Rather than changing the exp tables and causing hybrids to change level they instead just added more exp to cancle out the penalty. IE if a regular class needed 100 xp get to level 2 and a hibrid needed 120. Lets say they also got 10 exp per kill and took 10 kills to level up. Old way the hybrid would take 2 extra kills. New way that hybrid gets 20% more exp per kill so gets 12 exp per kill and now need 10 kills also to level up. Obviously this shouldn't effect exp going to aa's just regular leveling.

Then there's the difficult mob bonus that seems to be applied individually that gives a bonus for difficult mobs. IE same mob gives someone lower level more exp since it's red to them and "difficult" as opposed to blue and "easy". This was added a while back to encourage killing higher level mobs instead of LOTS of easy mobs. If memory serves it went a bit before PoP came out.

The amount of exp an aa requires is the same if your lvl 51 or lvl 70 so the differences is how much exp your getting. In this case the mobs are getting "easier" so your getting less, no bonus or maybe even a penalty for them being too easy at 66 compaired to 65. Once again if memory serves when they first added the exp bonus for difficult mobs they nurfed exp for easy mobs. I think they undid that after people complained but it's been a while but exp is still much better killing "difficult mobs".

The exp you actually get end up being pretty complicated when you factor in these different bonuses, splitting it between 3 different potential spots (regular, aa and leader) and when the particular bonuses are applied and should be applied.

Chutoi
Not necessarily, Wulf. As stated (by Tarradar, I think--too lazy to scroll up and check!), there is a set amount of experience per mob. It's a base amount. If you kill a mob within 5 levels of you, you get a bonus amount of experience. If you are grouped in a group of 6, you get an additional 20% experience added to the mob before the experience is divided among the group.

If you solo light blues, you are getting exp for a mob that's probably 10-15 levels below you (whatever light blue is now). The base exp for a level 60 mob isn't very high. You are also not getting that 20% exp bonus for mobs near your level. That all adds up quickly.

You aren't getting a smaller base, you're just not getting a bonus in some cases.

What isn't necessarily clear is that races with an experience penalty are somewhat hosed if you split AA/regular experience. I've asked for clarification from SoE, and I've been told that a developer has been looking into it (so who knows if I'll ever get an answer), but let me offer an example.

Hypothetical:
Assumptions:
Troll bard (you know you always wanted one!)
20% exp penalty.
Solo's a blue-con mob 6 levels lower for a base exp of 100 points.
AA exp set to 90%.

Scenario one: (Apply split before penalty)
100 x 90% = 90 exp to AA
100 x 10% = 10 x 20% penalty = 8 exp to regular exp.

Scenario 2: (apply penalty first)
100 x 20% penalty = 80 exp
80 x 90% = 72 exp to AA
80 x 10% = 8 exp to regular exp.

In scenario 2, you have an 18% exp bleed off of AA compared to scenario 1. Unfortunately, I'm about 80% sure that scenario 2 (the bad one) is the current operational calculation. As I said, I'm still waiting for confirmation from SoE. The situation is further complicated by leadership and raid AAs. I don't know where the 20% from leadership is taken--you may get additional exp bleed there.

There is an additional problem with the calculation from what I've heard. In order to save calculation time, SoE uses a rounding feature that is said to reduce exp on split AA/reg exp. For instance if a mob does 88,888 exp exp, and the split is 60/40, you should get: 53,333 and 35,555. SoE might round down after the 1000s and get 53,000 and 35,000 and lose you 888 exp. (if they rounded normally, they'd get 55,000 and 36,000--which would be an experience gain of 112). While I can see why they round down, it still nets to an exp loss.

Bottom line, I'd recommend having 100% to AA or 0%. Don't split.

snapdragn
If you are grouped in a group of 6, you get an additional 20% experience added to the mob before the experience is divided among the group.

Just to clarify. Prior to May 2003, an exp bonus was awarded for grouping on the order of:

2 people in group: 2% bonus
3 people in group: 4% bonus
4 people in group: 8% bonus
5 people in group: 12% bonus
6 people in group: 20% bonus

So a person duo'ing got 51% of the normal xp for killing the mob. Each person in a full grp got 20% of the normal xp for killing the mob (120% / 6).

The May2003 patch changed it to the following:

2 people in group: 20% bonus
3 people in group: 40% bonus
4 people in group: 60% bonus
5 people in group: 80% bonus
6 people in group: 80% bonus but only split 5 ways.

Now a person duo'ing gets 60% of the normal xp, and each person in a 5 or 6 person group gets 36% of the normal xp for killing the mob (180% / 5).

This is only one part of the big equation.
