rookz
Well, as a shaman Extended Enhancement just owns. When you're expected to keep buffs up and running for long periods, the % extra extension turns into mana that you would otherwise be wasting on a recast. Not to mention it will make haste last longer, which at the lower levels is a pita to keep up.

Improved Damage really doesn't help too much in the lower levels unless your soloing. I really didn't get to start whipping out my damage spells in groups till 44 (when I got stoicism and could canni more), and you really don't scale that up dramatically untill 58 when you get canni IV and can start turning back mana pretty well. Before then it's kinda hard to have the mana to waste casting damage spells, so improved damage is a lower priority.

Not very familiar with burning affliction... apparently it increases the damage your dots do... seems like it would be good for solo, but not very useful for a grouping shaman, as we rarely get to stack several dots on a target in groups.

Mana Preservation is on par with extended enhancement at lower levels, if not better. It basically reduces the mana cost for your spells, so you really can't go wrong there.

Improved Healing obviously is considerably more useful for a grouping shaman than a solo shaman, But at the lower levels I never saw enough of a difference to give it a high priority.

Spell Haste... Spells casting faster is obviously very helpful, but if your planning on outdoor hunting like ot and DL, then it's not that important for your lower levels. Sure, landing slow a half second sooner helps, but after that there is plenty of time between actions for shaman, (outdoors at the lower levels) so the haste isn't gamechanging. However, if your doing LDoN's it's pretty damn good (just behind mana pres and extended enhancement). When you get adds you need to cast incredibly fast as there is TONS of things to be doing (especially when your main healer), so the spell haste is a noticeable help.

Affliction Haste is not that useful imho. Since your not casting all that many affliction spells, having a whole other focus effect just to have a select few spells casting faster seems silly to me. If you can gnab a spell haste focus item, then don't worry about the few % extra haste on damage spells... at least not pre 52 where your not chain casting rain spells or whatnot.

Gloriana
It's cool, there's a lot of confusion around Extended Enhancement/Extended Blessing and the AA SCRM.

Extended Enhancement - most of us get our first taste of this with a ceramic shield of valor, player made via pottery. Extended enhancement extends buffs (spell must have buff icon) by 15%. It is limited to spells which have a duration of 48 seconds or longer (therefore, doesn't work with our heals over time: stocisim, Torpor, Q).

Extended Blessing (sparking ruby of fortune) - Type 7 LDON focus (meaning goes on most old gear). This focus item increases buff duration by 10% (not as good as extended enhancement) but affects spells with a duration of 24 seconds or longer...meaning works with our heals over time. Henora posted a nice log somewhere that shows that it consistently adds ticks to Q.

SCR/SCRM - AA ability (arch SCR - 2/4/6 class SCRM - 8), adds to buff duration and affects the duration of our heals over time.

Now, where it gets fun...Extended Enhancement stacks with SCRM. I would guess that Extended Blessing would also stack with SCRM but I don't have an item to double check.
Jaidin
Now, where it gets fun...Extended Enhancement stacks with SCRM. I would guess that Extended Blessing would also stack with SCRM but I don't have an item to double check.

To clarify a little, EE and EB stack with all levels of SCR, not just SCRM. However, EE and EB do *not* stack with each other just as all other focus effects, ie: two effects that do the same thing, for example mana pres and affliction efficiency.

EB stacking with SCR and SCRM has been shown a few times on this board.

I still refer to this site fairly frequently for focus effect info, http://www.bjornstar.com/focusitems.html. Now, as for my preference of focus effects:

1) Spell Haste - When you get to 51, having a faster slow is really great. When you get to 60+ with malo(s), having both a faster slow and malo(s) is even better. In addition, our direct heals are pretty slow casting and are helped with spell haste.

2) Mana Pres - We cast SO many spells that this helps very very much, especially since we use pretty much all of the spell types.

Now, the rest are fairly subjective depending mostly on how you play.

Solo:

3) Affliction Effeciency - 25% mana savings on dots is HUGE
4) Burning Affliction - If you stack 3-4 dots, this adds up very fast
5) Affliction Haste - most useful versus summoning mobs really. The time savings isn't much until you start getting interrupted during dot refreshes
6) Extended Enhancement/Extended Blessing - Having self only buffs last longer is nice, especially for dogdog's haste and self regen, but not really a great help.
7) Improved Damage, Improved Healing - Lowest on the list because you won't be doing much direct damage or healing while soloing.

Group:

3) Improved Damage - I nuke a lot during groups and having permanent ID adds a lot of damage, however it does not work on rains
4) Extended Enhancement/Extended Blessing - It's really nice to have mana intensive buffs last longer, particularly haste and especially ferine avatar.
5) Improved Healing - Meh...*NOTE* this might be a priority for you if you will be doing a lot of patch healing in groups. I group 99.9% of the time with a cleric and she is annoyed enough that Q heals so well, so she gets to heal whenever possible :)
6) Affliction Haste, Efficiency, Burning Affliction - I haven't dotted in groups for quite some time, however these might rank higher if you duo/trio.

Does Mana Preservation and Affliction Efficeincy stack on our Dot's?

No focus effects stack with each other, ever. The best focus effect will take precedence. In this case, if you had both a mana preservation and an affliction efficiency item, the affliction efficiency item would go off while casting dots.

Focus items can stack with AA abilities, however :)
