Hail,

I've posted in the hunting forums about PoI grouped in mid50's.

The zone is also a gold mine at 60, though there are a few dangers you have to be aware of.

Avoid Archaics. They are immune to slow. Do not pull them. If you get one by mistake, sic dogdog on it, do NO damage, and hopefully you can stay up long enough for pet to aggro so you can escape without training. If you can't escape, die with pride instead of bringing it to the graveyard zoneout.

Other than archaics and rapid respawns, there is little danger to an Oracle here that is game created.

The REAL danger of course are the other players. Trains to zone are common. If you can get an idea beforehand which direction the train is coming from, you can usually move to the other side (outside GY) and avoid it entirely as long as the entrance mobs are clear, which they usually are.

If you see 2 or more groups (of whatever makeup) pulling to the graveyard, you should leave in my opinion. Most full groups don't stay long before moving along the paths, but if you get a group hugging the graveyard, it is generally because they know they have few skills & will usually end up training you.

My solo strategy in PoI is broken down into 3 methods:

1. Pull to East corner of Graveyard area. Nothing should add on you there (except for mobs from trains), dogdog can push the robot into the corner while you are tanking.

Remember to root the tiny obsolete models at 20%, because they will run away towards the right path & other mobs. I keep dogdog with me at all times instead of parking him at my pull spot. This method works extremely well during low zone population times. As soon as it gets crowded, it is time to move to another method or another zone entirely.

This is generally very low risk if you don't persist in staying during busy periods. I can pull and clear the left path past the factory ramp easily before respawns occur. If there is another group pulling to graveyard (or about to move out), I ask to split the Tin Soldiers at the factory entrance. Once these two spawns are broken (can't single pull at 60), you can include them for better exp and possible parchment drops.

2. Pull to the factory ramp at the top. This requires a moment's assistance to get these spawns broken a few minutes apart for respawns, then you can pull to the factory door. This works extremely well, as you avoid MOST of the trains (people leaving factory may train you, but they usually have more of a clue, and at least give warning, unlike the graveyard trains). This method has greater risk as you must keep the Tin Soldiers timed well for repops, but the reduced aggravation from GY groups (rangers/druids trying to kite or root/rot) and the /tell focus for my group please is worth it to me.

3. This requires the PoI factory flag, which I only recently acquired. (Thank you again Gloriana!) Pull to East corner of GY until path to ramp is clear. Clear the Tin Soldiers at ramp door entrance. I can take both at once if at full mana/health with raid buffs, but a single slow resist usually means death. Once inside, you can single pull the 2 tin soldiers on your right into the entrance room (with the gnome). Proceed down that path and you can single pull 3 more Tin Soldiers. If you broke the door spawns (outside factory), you can pull them if you have time.

Generally speaking, the 5 Tin Soldier spawns are plenty for me. By the time I'm done getting mana back from the last Tin Soldier, the first one pops within a minute. Rinse and repeat for safe, steady exp. If there is another group at factory entrance, once you clear the first two Tin Soldiers on the right, you can pull to a spot in the hallway to leave the entrance room for the group pulling Clockwork Golems. The Golems are much better exp, but I can't single them at 60, and haven't tried soloing one yet. This is the easiest of the 3 methods in my opinion, but it does require the factory flag.

If you add a single person (ANY class, I duoed w/ an enchanter) here, you can pull clockwork devices or whatever while you wait for Tin Soldier respawns.

One final note. Bring lots of room to carry loot. Archaic circuitry (can drop off any robots) isn't stackable but sells for ~32pp. Disassembled Servos sell for 80pp. Multiple other weird broken robot pieces sell for 1-12pp. Innovative Clockwork Bolts and Gears are valuable to tradeskillers, as are much of the other items used by Tinkers. You also have the opportunity to get exp items as loot. Hail the gnome outside zone in the windmill to get a listing of what he wants.
