Andal
Oh vey. I've been playing since beta, and it's been understood that tradeskills were not implemented as ways to generate cash.

You can, of course, but complaining that Alchemy is not one of the profitable skills misses the point. Take up Smithing.

If someone wants to eek out a meager profit by selling lower than everyone else, that's their business and their right. If the margins are too low, then you can do something else.

But calling the person selling for 1pp profit "greedy", when your intention is being able to make 50pp profit, is a classic example of what psychiatrists call "projection".

The person who sells as high as possible is obviously more "greedy" than the one who sells as low as possible, from the viewpoint of the consumer. And that is the only relevant viewpoint. The guy selling low is doing a public service to those who purchase potions, and is therefore charitable, not greedy.

If you take a look at the stat potions, you will find that they've been designed to be used in sets of three. The proper combination of three potions will result in no stat reductions to the imbiber. Most potion boosts will stack with similar spell buffs, therefore potions have utility for the imaginative.

What I think would be nice is the ability to forage or find as drops the potion components. Other than that, I'm very happy being the Alchemist!

It's a unique ability, you get fun stuff to give to your friends. It's not about cash, it's about being a Shaman.
