kayen85
Warning: Long... This is targeted at 65 Shaman who do not have access to groups who regularly do Hard LDON aventures.

I like a good challenge and high risk LDON adventures are fun, exciting and the risk vs reward is just about perfect. Exp is noticeable and loot drops are outstanding for anyone outside of elementally equipped guild. However, if your server is anything like mine going LFG for a hard ldon adventure is mostly likely only going to result in one thing. Killing green mobs outside the wayfarer camp for a few hours while you wait in vain and log. Obviously this isn't the best approach to doing adventures so after much failure and frustration I developed a pretty sound strategy to winning these high risk adventures using a regular old pickup group that you can start yourself from the LFG list. Would like to add I’m not in a guild and my gear is mostly bazaar bought (3.8k hp self buffs) so what I am saying can applied to just about anyone. I will also admit you won't win every adventure and but the more you do the easier it gets.

Step 1: What zone to start in.
If you are just starting out doing high risk adventures the best zone to begin in is East Commonlands. Wide open spaces, low mob density, few unsnareable mobs and mobs do not enrage (excellent shaman loot too). Next in order of increasing difficulty would be Everfrost, Mistmore, Tak and Guk. For the purposes of this guide I will focus on doing east commons adventures since the are the most winnable with a wide variety of groups. *Note all mobs hit ~990 max, mitigate slow slightly. Thus you’re playing in a situation where you do want your group members being hit if they don't have to.

Step 2: How to make your group.
Now making a high risk group is not as simple as your regular missions where you can toss together any 6 people and win without thinking but is also not so rigid that you will never be able to get the right combo. Now this may sound crazy but you do not need classic crowd control for these adventures. You are a shaman you have the spell root and it works quite well in conjunction with a snarer/malos. Biggest thing to realize is these mobs don’t summon so you can kite or root adds without a problem. Of course you can work with an enchanter but you have to realize your giving up a valuable dps slot (yes some enc can charm but I wouldn't bet on it). I prefer non int casters because they don't die so fast.
Group make up all 65:
1. Shaman
2. Cleric (lots of these around pick one you know is ok)
3. Tank. (Believe it or not your tank doesn't have to be uber any moderately defensively aa'd knight or warrior with 7k + hp will survive on a hard mission however you will need to adjust how much you as a shaman heal per tank)
4. Snarer Ranger/Necro/SK/Druid (Ranger best for this of course)
5. Feign Death Monk/SK (If you have a good SK tank you can add any dps here as well as survive without another snarer)
6. Tracker/DPS Bard (Some bards can pull well others don't stand a chance) Ranger / Bst, Rogue ect...
As you can see you have a lot of options as to how to make your group and its often more dependent on the player’s skill then class.

Step 3: Not all missions are created equal.
Do collects but note what missions give which zones and which drop rates. It is not random. Look for missions that give you the best chance of winning. Some zones are much more challenging. Missions are identical in drop rate on map on normal and hard. Stay away from slaughters no matter how tempting.
Example. Collect 21 Ocular Lenses in EC is a very EASY mission. Collect 20 Orc Studs can be challenging.

Step 4: How to pull the adventure and handle adds.
Biggest problem with high risk adventures is running out of time cause you have to FD split every single mob and/or your group can not handle adds. To win you have to kill consistently and keep your deaths to a minimum. FD pulling singles an entire mission will often result in the monk/SK needing a lot of res's from failed FD's(talking about average equipped players) and you running out of time. This where you Mr. Shaman come in. Since you will be starting the group you will have to take a lot of responsibility for the win. Take maps like EC/EF where you often will have long stretches of double pulls. Initially instruct your snarer to make sure he gets everything snared as a first priority. Then instruct the rest of the group if they have agro and shouldn't just run away a bit from the snared mob , don't try and tank it.
Single Pulls: For single pulls just pull with malos run back let tank take agro and slow should rarely resist, easy enough. You can scout any pull with the "vision" line of spells.
Double Pulls: Give yourself a lot of room. For doubles, call which mobs will be inc and which one you will root. Root one the other agros towards you let tank engage and slow then quickly slow the rooted one, while this is happening the snarer should get the one you parked snarered so when it breaks it will be easily re rooted. Make sure to keep aggro on the unengaged mob so it doesn’t run and gank any group mates when root breaks. You have now successfully handled two at a time quickly and efficiently. Another note should be if slow is resisted at any point the mobs should be snared so just back off and let the tank retake aggro often not wise to stand there and take the hits since they can kill you fast.
*What do I do if root is resisted initially on a double pull? Just send the dog in and it takes instant aggro and of course enrages in 0.1 second absorbing probably 20k+ in damage from the two unslowed mobs while you toss a vp on one and slow the other. What if Dog doesn't get to enrage? Let tank engage and quickly VP one. What if VP is resisted, hope the snarer can draw aggro quickly and kite and park it. Key is just making sure everyone knows what to do in each situation.
Triple + pulls: FD puller time. Just wait till two mobs are left on the puller and root one as previously described. No need to try and split to a single cause often mobs are very linked. Once you get the strategies down you can easily handle 3 at a time but it is risky.

Step 5: What should you as a shaman do to ensure victory.
Patch heal and keep Q on your tank. Mobs hit hard can take even the best tank down quickly if numbers are unlucky. Tnargs is less mana then rebuffing a res'd tank. With the group make up I have described you should have enough dps which will allow you to focus on keeping the tank alive. Course the better the tank and cleric you have the less you have to do in this area.

Step 6: What if I loose!
It might happen... But remember if you have all inexperienced high risk players they will in my experience have had a great time and be ready to try again this time 100% ready for what is in front of them. And if you got any named loot the mission before you can be sure they will want to come back again. You will quickly meet many good players and build a good base for doing these missions without trouble.

Step 7: What if I win!
Congrats! Please insert 25 cents and play again.
Note, for the harder zones(Tak, Guk) as I stated earlier you need to rely more on FD pulling and real crowd control thus it helps to get the basics down before trying them.

Hope this might inspire some of you to give these adventures a chance because they are not as scary as they seem and can be extremely rewarding and fun.

Glad you all enjoyed the post. In regards Odinson's question on how can you tell which adventures will be easier or harder. The answer is experience. The adventure recruiter gives two kinds of collects, specific ones and more general ones. For example in east commonlands there is a collection mission asking for 29 Prayer Idols. It is unique and it will spawn the same exact zone with the same exact drop rate every single time (by the way DON'T do this mission). On the other hand the mission asking you to collect 20 Orc Bone studs can spawn one of three different zones each having if its own drop rate identical every time but you just don't know which of the three its going to be. As you do more and more missions you will begin to remember which give easy zones with good drop rates and which give hard zones ect. For east commonlands you will want to aviod collect 24 missions because they can often spawn the "Arena of Chance" zone which if you have ever tried it on hard is very difficult. However collect 21-23 will always give you a zone that is manageable with any group. As I mentioned in my initial post if you want a guarenteed easy zone start practicing hards in look for the collect 21 Ocular Lenses in EC if your group is patient.

In regards to charming on Hard missions, here is what I have experienced from having enchanters charm on the missions. With exception of a few very skilled players what I see is once the enchanter gets the pet their entire focus is on keeping the pet and are no longer able to perform crowd control duties effectivly. Which is understandable because having a 990 hitting pet break on you doesnt leave you many options as to what you can do. I found as a shaman and the cleric would as well end up spending all their mana spam healing the enchanter on breaks and often we would loose a tank because we could not allocate any heals to both of them in a lot of situations. If you can work out a sound strategy(and there are a ton) for dealing with charm breaks then I fully agree its amazing tool for winning, really depends on having an experienced enchanter.

I have set up several pick-up Hard missions. My key is class priority.
First off, everyone has to be 65, no question.
I pick a plate class to tank, very much preferred if they have over 7k hps (or else the cleric heals overtime if slow gets resisted or you have multiple pulls).
Bard or Enc (Bard very much preferred for pulling and CC, plus they can do some fair dps).
Cleric, for group and complete heals, druid just can't keep up with the healing efficiency of a Cleric.
And any DPS after that.
This doesn't take into acount Time-equipped characters. I've had a time equipped ranger tank for me in hard easily.
It may take time, and your group mates may get impatient while you set up the group but it very much pays off.
In sticking with these rules, we very rarely have deaths, the pulls are kept to a minimum and things move swiftly.
Some things to note. Mages can mez most mobs in Tak, and necs can do similar things in MM/EF.
And an enc can work wonders with a charmed mob, your DPS goes through the roof with the cost of the charm breaking at bad times.
