Here's one from the perspective of someone who may be creating a shaman soon. :)

I play a lot of characters; I've tried every class except necro during my EQ lifetime, as it's too close to my main for me to enjoy currently. Shaman was one of the classes that I always wanted to try because I thought they had great potential. I made one, briefly, as a troll.

However, I couldn't stand it--it seemed to take forever for skills to go up, and growing up in Innothule when I was a much poorer player was much rougher than it should have been.

Thus, when looking at a new one this time around, I thought, what do I want to create? Barbarian is commonplace--and aside from my high elf cleric, I stay away from common race/class combos as best I can (it's just personal preference). Ogres and trolls are 1) ugly, which in this game of fashion DOES affect our decisions, 2) bad faction, 3) large races, 4) lower int/wis....

A lot of people stop on 1) and don't go further. Vah Shir are "cute", although I never got into them. Iksar are to me the most tolerable "ugly race" but you're in for a huge uphill faction battle. So..

Personally, I chose Iksar. But a lot of people don't want to deal with faction. They want to take the ones with the highest starting wisdom. They want ones that can wear all of the class armor (Totemic, JB, are the ones I think of as a non-shaman). They want ones that have a high stamina, for better hp down the road.

To me, there's one race that combines all of those--Barbarian. It's as safe a choice as high elf cleric, or wood elf druid, or barbarian warrior, or dark elf necromancer. *shrug* There's nothing wrong with ANY of those choices, of course. But I suspect if you take a cross-section of the classes and divide them by race, you'll find that there's a huge congregation of "optimum race per class". This isn't a "there's a best type of shaman" comment at all--but there's definitely a "what many people think is the best type" for classes, especially when people like me haven't played one and maybe haven't researched it fully.

For shamans, barbarian is it.

As has been stated already, many people started their shamans when the only choices were Barb, Troll, and Ogre.
And since people just wanted to jump into the game and play it without worrying about faction penalties, Barb was the most popular choice.

And, as snapdragn pointed out, it's a trend that has continued to this day, regardless of how much more we understand about the game.
Faction is nowhere near as important as it has been . . . yet most people starting shamans choose Barbs.
Racial abilities (like regen, infravision, etc.) have long since been discovered and add more flavor/interest in some's eyes (but truthfully have minimal overall impact) . . . yet still people choose Barbs.
People know that Barbs are a dime a dozen (an irrelevant fact IMO, but some people think it seems to matter) . . . yet they are still the most popular choice.

I cannot really explain why this is, but I can tell you, for me, I chose a Barb because they are what I think of when I picture a shaman. Well, compared to the choices EQ offers that is. Technically speaking, when I think of a shaman, I think of a bronzed-skin Native American human in animal hides with trinkets and charms dangling from his clothing. But since humans could not be shamans in EQ, and since the Native American skin texture does not exist, I chose the closest thing -- Barbarian.

If I had to do it over again, I'd still choose Barb. If push came to shove, I might consider trying one of the regenning races to see for myself if it truly makes a difference (even though common sense tells me that, at most, the difference would be minimal).
