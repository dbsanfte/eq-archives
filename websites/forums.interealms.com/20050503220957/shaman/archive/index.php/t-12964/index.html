Irontree
It's intuitve for casters to try and raise wis/int to increase their mana pool, but even ignoring the hp->mana (cannibalize in shaman case) angle, its still frequently inefficient.

Wisdom above 200, returns mana at an inferior rate to below. They upped it in SOL but its still not as efficient.

Adding +MANA frequently will add more mana to your pool than more Wis, if you are living at 200, or 230+.

You can use magelo to play around with the numbers to fine the right mix if you want to min/max it.

With that said, its STILL much more important for a canni/lich/modding class to work on HP than mana pool, once the pool is functionally large enough.

Mana recovery rate dictates pull rate, kill rate, xp rate and surviability in a hostile non-stop crawl.

Let me see if I can put into words how my account became wealthy over the last ten months or so.

First of all I started life in EQ as a Dark Elf Wizard whom I loved playing dearly. I do remember how awesome it was to finish my full suit of quest newbe armor, and I remember when I got my first drop that had a stat on it: Sensate Reishi Cap with its awesome AC of 3 and Int +1... heh.

Since then I played a Vah Shir Shaman to 12th with a very good friend of mine who left EQ and has recently returned to the game. My first shaman was his character's companion, so I refused to play him anymore unless it was with him.

So, after my wizard dinged 25th I started to wonder what the other character classes had to offer. I then played a Shadow Knight to 25th, a Druid to 25th, and a Cleric to 11th, but my thoughts still lingered with the fun I had playing a Shaman.

When LoY was released I saw this as my chance to start a new Shaman. I played him to 6th in the crater surrounding Shar Vahl, then I continued to 25th duoing and grouping in Paludal. This time I knew I'd finally found my true calling and have been terribly addicted to the Shaman class ever since.

Now I’m not a new player to EverQuest, but I am still very new to the advanced concepts of the Shaman class. To that end I really thank everyone who has been openly pitching in advice.

Now that covers my playing, but none of what I've been through produces jack for plat, except for the 5 levels I obtained duoing Hill Giants in Rathe. The revelation of how to make my fortune in EQ came after stopping by the bazaar one day with my wizard about 10 months ago. I noticed that spider silks and spiderling silks were selling for 5 pp each and bone chips were going for 1 pp each. Man you should have seen how fast I ran to the local software store after a second copy of EverQuest with visions of plat dancing in my head. I had finally found a way to avoid spending another two years of game time to achieve the equipment I wanted to have. But that ended up just being a stepping-stone. I am a businessman in real life, so after applying a bit of real life experience to my bazaar account, I've managed to amass the small fortune I’d dreamed of having many months ago.

Bazaar Advice:

The Bazaar is a fluid beast, so never put all of your money into one type of selling. Instead try to achieve a balance of trade skill crafted goods, spell research crafted goods, alchemy potions (Blood of the Wolf and Kilva’s Blistering Flesh being two of my personal favorites), items that you’re reselling from others who just wanted to unload them cheep, and items you win via /random 1 1000 while out hunting and raiding.

Seek out and become acquainted with the crafting community. Many of them, for a portion of the final sale price, are willing to pitch in and help you to combine crafted items your current trade skills or spell research skills could never craft alone.

Lastly, remember this is just a game. Get out and enjoy a taste of real life now and again. It makes the time you spend playing EverQuest that much sweeter knowing those around you have not been forgotten or ignored. I mention this after two months of hard-core EQ’ing and personally loosing sight of real life for a short time in the process.
