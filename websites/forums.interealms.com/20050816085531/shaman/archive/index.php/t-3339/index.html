Old World: Kedge. 6 months ago, I didn't know squat about this zone, and I'd say it's an acquired taste. But as an officer, I was forced to fish out some kedge guides on the internet, and subsequently spent around 3-4 hours getting a good feel for it. I don't regret it though, it's a very unique zone and it's fun cruising around, I swim around so fast that I often end up taking falling damage :D (think about it).

I don't think many people would agree though. Apart from phinni raids, I tour there maybe twice a week to smack cauldronbubble and estrella up. Hardly anyone in zone except for the occasional tracker mule, and I know why:
1) Your spells get interrupted much easier when the mob is hitting you
2) CR's are a pain if you're stuck without an extra eb item or fish scales in bank
3) AE's are not really viable
4) Most mobs are immune to cold-based attacks (= no jbb sorry =/)

But it still doesn't change the fact that it's very well thought out. An entire zone submerged under water, permitting you to freely move about the z-axis.
