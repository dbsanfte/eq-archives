Shrewed, let's not forget the "awesome" changes to Seal of Command! Instead of a Holy damage proc that was able to be parried, missed, and dodged. We get to exchange all that for it's ability to be resisted. Doesn't seem like much to whine about before you factor in 2 things:

1.) Seal of Command deals Holy damage. Holy damage is supposedly unresistable and unmitigated by armor. Nice for higher con/elite mobs. Even with it acting more "proc-ish" by not being able to be parried, dodged, or missed, being resisted flat out means it will be even less reliable then before.

2.) Seeing as it's going to be resistable now, alot of Paladins are confused as to what base stat the seal will be using to crit now. Currently it acts as a melee ability, influenced by agility. Many Retribution (read 'Looking For damage') Paladins thusly ended up with agility heavy builds - raising normal melee critical chance as well as Commands critical chance. Logically speaking, since Command will be resistable it seems like it is going to be treated as spell ability, which is influenced by intelligence to critical.

SO, what does all this mean? It's really to early to tell. I'm waiting on the Blizzard response to this, as there are several posts by Paladins waiting with baited breath for this answer. If the changes are implemented as above, then it's arguably a triple-screw for the majority of Paladins (myself included).

Praying for no "awesome" changes,
