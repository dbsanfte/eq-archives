weissblau
The USB adapters are not officially supported for PS2 games. A few games shipped using the SN Systems TCP/IP stack - like Tony Hawk - and they work with specific models. I would not expect games that ship with Sony Computer Entertainment International's TCP/IP stack to support USB ethernet.

SOE will be using SCEI's stack.

The reasons given for this lack of support are that those USB adapters are subject to change without notice by the manufacturer and that change might impact the software driver. Apparently that's already happened once and there are two batches of one particular USB Ethernet adapter that behave differently despite having the same model number - i.e. there's absolutely no way to tell which is which.

weissblau
PS2 games are very high polygon count/relatively low texture, PC games are the reverse. It doesn't make one better or worse than the other. PS2 animations generally look smoother and special effects look nicer because the programmers know exactly what the hardware can do.

More to the point, EQOA is a complete new game written to 2002/2003 standards, whereas EQ is limping along with a rewritten graphics engine that has to support a compatibility mode, running on a variety of old graphics cards and hardware and who knows what. EQOA ought to blow the regular EQ's graphics away. Whether it looks better than more modern PC games running on hot hardware is another question.
