I've been pondering ideas for abilities/spells that I think would be a good addition to the shaman class, while not being overpowered. They would give us back some uniqueness and would be both interesting and useful. The upcoming melee changes open up a whole new arena of effects to choose from.


We've had a few threads in the past couple of weeks on these subjects, here (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=18388) and here (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=18305). However there threads got quickly derailed by people asking for spells of other classes and for unrealistic things. We are a very diverse class, in a very diverse game with a multitude of different playstyles. We will never have consensus on what is most important. However, I hope that people can look beyond their own little niche and think about what abilities and spells would be both good and balanced for the game and for shaman of many different stations.

Let me be clear what this thread is NOT about. This thread is not about asking for melee upgrades, unresistable slows, rezzes, charm, etc. This thread is also NOT about asking for things we have asked for one million times already. While having these abilities (AE shrink, improved invis, etc) would be nice, we've talked about them before a lot. I would like this thread to be about proposing well thought NEW ideas for future expansions (or other improvement periods) for both AA/spells, primarily focused on level 65 (or higher, in the future).


I have several ideas, in the catagories of Debuffs, Buffs, Mana Regen. I'm not talking about our healing or damage abilities in this post, because I don't have anything new to say (NOT because changes aren't needed in those areas).



Debuffs
Our slows are powerful enough to have warranted nerfing (through the new mitigation system of Planes of Power). I do not think we will get a slow spell upgrade again. So I am not going to discuss slow. Instead I want to talk about the Cripple upgrade that POP failed to bring. With the new melee system, there is a valuable opportunity to make an addition to the cripple line both useful, interesting and unique. A crippled mob could present more "openings" for meleers to use to execute their new melee abilities.

A new cripple could also directly modify a mobs ability to hit, decrease their ATK directly and/or decrease their mitigation (see the aggressive discipline on direct mitigation reduction (http://lucy.allakhazam.com/spell.html?id=4498&source=Test)) avoidance. There are a plethora of opportunities to upgrade the cripple line. The chance to dodge, parry, dual wield, etc can all be modified independently now with the new melee system.

I propose there could really be 3 new cripple spells (Cheesy names included free of charge).


Assailant's Ally - Opening Enhancer - Casting this spell on your target will cause the target to display 10-20% more openings (could be a multiple stage AA).

Debilitating Fog - Offensive Ability Decreaser - This spell clouds the mind of your target, decreasing their offensive concentration. This will decrease their chance to hit and causes their ability to double attack (and/or double attack) to degrade by 10%.

Corrosive Aura - Defensive Ability Decreaser - Casting this spell will reduce the integrity of your target's armor, reducing their AC and mitigation and encumbering their movements(not snare), hindering their ability to parry and riposte.







Buffs
Shaman have been described in the past as the master of stat buffs. Unfortunately as every day goes by stat buffs are less and less useful. This is a combination of gear progression, AA points towards stats and ldon augs for stats. Even in the beginning, statistic buffs were much less meaningful than HP/AC/Mana/Resist buffs. Now with the proliferation of other specialty buffs including things like bulwark of vie, massive damage shields, etc... stat buffs are less desireable than ever in the face of limited buff slots.


The only worthwhile non-utility buffs we have left (at moderate levels of advancement at 65), are buffs that provide either direct hitpoints or ATK.

As I discussed above, with the new melee system changes, there is a tremendous opportunity to allow shaman to do what they once did better than anyone else. Buff meleers. There is now the opportunity to give us buffs that increase chance to hit, increase chance to dodge, parry, riposte, increase overall avoidance or mitigation, increase dual wield rate or double attack rate. There is opportunity to add diversity to buffs here.


My proposal is for the avatar line to be attended to first. Avatar was once our premier buff. Just one expansion after we got the buff, it was turned into a weapon proc that was BETTER than the original spell. The upgrades to avatar have been welcome but minor. The statistics component (Str/dex/agi) of the buff we use now, Ferine Avatar, are largely useless. Most meleers at moderate levels of advancement (Pre-Elemental) will max STR and DEX with just Focus. The returns of AGI are very minor. So the only important part of FA is the ATK portion.

I'd like to see future versions of avatar drop the statistics portion and add in direct bonuses to melee abilities, perhaps adding something else interesting, like the effects on time loot (+ strikethrough ) or whatever.

Mana Regen
I discussed this in depth in this post (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showpost.php?postid=203938&postcount=44). The quick version is that chain cannibalizing isn't fun or balanced anymore. We are punished in 5 ways for cannibalize. We most devote a spell slot, we are prevented from doing anything else while cannibalizing, we must sacrifice HP. Then there are the related areas where we are penalized for this "superior mana regen" with increased mana cost, increased resists on our spells. All other classes have received updated mana regen abilities (that are passive, allowing them to do other things, not devote spell slots, etc) in Luclin or POP and we haven't since Velious.

I propose we are given an improved cannibalize that doesn't have to be chain cast, but can be used more often and in conjunction with cannibalize 5. Decrease the cast time to .5 and up the reuse time. Make it take more HP and give back more mana. You can tune it so the rate at which we can cannibalize for mana is similar to what it is now. Just make it useable less often.
