The thing to remember with trio is that you dont have the other 3 folks to contribute to damage... yes yes yes I know this is painfully obvious but think about this for a second. Throw a Wiz into a normal group and they get to boom once per mob for 4k-6k damage. This is their only source of damage so they are running even on mana with one boom per mob. Now turn that to needing them to pull the weight of the other classes you should have there and they are going to be running OOM a lot.

I'd suggest: Sham, Mage, SK.

For a lot of the same reasons folks already said. The mage pet is constant sick damage. They can give your puppy and the SK pet weapons and gear to increase their potential (you are looking to maximize the damage each person does).

Mages can also throw out a DS on the SK. Go with an Ogre SK and you can get the Cultural in the Baz for a reasonable price and dont worry about the Stuns to lose agro. We have clocked the druid DS at roughly 30-45dps on normals w/o slowing the mobs. This is on a SK with 2k AC when we were about to swap to doing Hards though. So with 2kAC the SK was damn near never getting hit... get hit more= more DS damage but also more healing on your part. Work the numbers a few times and figure out what works for you.

I think it's a pretty simple plan to go with the Mage and SK but that is just me. I could certainly see the Necro working very well due to their ability to lay down some serious damage on long living mobs (long living due to missing half the group ;) )

3 person minimum.

On the "low collects" comment above - keep in mind that those can burn you. The lower number of pieces you have to collect, the fewer mobs in the dungeon actually have it. So you could potentially need to kill more mobs to complete a low collect than a high collect.

Most of the times I've lost on collect has been on 23 or less to get. 25 - 26 seems optimal in my experience of 200+ adventures.

Sudedor is correct on the collect missions. Being a statistics person myself, it is explained by a couple of factors. If you want safety (ie: more even distribution of collect items) the higher # is better. If you want a chance that you can get lucky on the distribution the lower # is better. And I agree 100% that somewhere in the middle is the best of both worlds. But, I wouldn't sweat it too much since the collects were fixed a while ago. Before that you were taking a huge risk with a low collect.
