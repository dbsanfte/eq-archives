weissblau
Awww Redspeed, be nice. It can be a little more complex than that. What if you have multiple shaman, or if at least one shaman has MGB? And things changed a bit with the raid window, too. Plus on a large raid I'd never wait for tells to do buffs, I want to get the buffage out of the way so I can clear my spell gem slots for combat spells.

There are basically three raid buffs that shaman always provide:

1. Focus (or HoS, or Focus of Soul, etc)
2. STA
3. AGI

Depending on the situation you may also be providing PR/DR in the form of Talisman of Epuration or a combination of PR/DR buffs.

If there is at least one 60+ MGB shaman, he/she provides MGB Focus. Duh. Otherwise, Focus is one of the other buffs the shaman is responsible for.

If there is exactly one buffing shaman on the raid, my suggestion is to go to the raid window, get a buff up, get a healer on you to heal your cannibalizes, and buff from the top down. Then switch to a new buff and do it again. Cann when you have to.

If you have more, you have to decide whether to split the groups up or to split the buffs up. Personally, I like splitting the buffs up; I can keep my buff in a gem and recast on resurrected peeps easily, and everyone knows who to go to for a given buff. However, if you have more than 3 shaman, that leaves some without anything to do, so you might choose to split up the groups, or some combination (i.e. ShamanA does Focus for groups 1-5, ShamanB does Focus for 6-10, ShamanC does AGI for all, ShamanD does STA for all, ShamanE does Epuration for all).

After you think you are done, always inform the raid that you are done. Usually a few people will come out of the woodwork who were LD, clearing lag, latecomers, changing groups, out of range, etc. and they'll have to be individual buffed.

One other thing: if you are fighting a dispeller, you're going to want to lay on 4-5 junk buffs first, so you must announce to the raid that that's what you are doing and get them to click off the first 4-5 buffs in their list. Usually I DO go with tells here - ask them to get 4-5 junks from within their group, and send tell if they can't get enough. I like to use the group PR and DR spells as junks if PR and DR are not required, group Charisma, and individual Acumen (since it lasts a long time). If you are in a junk-buff situation, ALWAYS buff AGI first, then STA, then Focus to put Focus higher up on the debuff list.

*** Send tells with your request ***

Ever wonder why none of the three Shamans react to you /shout /say /ooc "Shammie bufs plz" ?
Maybe because every one of them thinks the other will buff you?
Maybe because they are sick of wasting mana by casting that Focus at the same time at you as the other two Shamans?
Shouts, oocs, says tend to get buried in all that other zone-spam. A tell-text not.

*** Be selective ***

It wont help you much sending tells for the same buff requests to each and every Shaman in the raid. They will detect this waste of mana soon and hunt you down. And sick their puppies at you.

*** Be precise ***

/tell Shaman "Gimme al ur Shammie buffz"
Some Shamans have 30 pages in their spellbooks full of interesting buffs. Some Shamans will react to that request by starting at the bottom of their spell books and buff you things like Invis to Animals, Ultravision, Inner Fire, Enduring Breath. Some will serve the more precise requests of the other tank first.

*** Keep the request short ***

/tell Shaman "Well it seems that the dust of the battle has settled down so please can you focus your attention bringing me up with riotous health buff but only if mana permits, if not then please take your time but remember me in 15 minutes if yadda yadda yadda yadda yadda yadda......"
/tell Shaman "Focus"
Two requests. Guess which one will be served first?
People like to be polite. Shamans like politeness too. Shamans have also a lot to work to do at raids, and most of them like short precise requests over politeness. Hugs and kisses can be exchanged when that Dragon is dead.
Note: I also like the short requests being written in capslock. Helps with recoginzing and reading them in all that other spam in the chat window.
Note: I like to chat, but not at raids. Too busy. So i will reply rarely to a buff request other than by casting it on you. /bighug

*** Learn the buffs ***

/tell Shaman "Focus, Str, Dex"
Shaman sighs.

/tell Shaman "Dex"
Shaman gets message: "That spell wont have hold on the target"
Shaman sighs
Tank tells you "What is that Focus buff i have?"

Great news to you: Some buffs stack, some not. Some buffs have several effects blocking other buffs being casted on you. Example: Focus of Spirit not only adds HP but also Str and Dex.

/tell Shaman "Talisman"
Shaman opens his spellbook: Talisman of Tnarg, Talisman of Altuna, Talisman of Kragg, Talisman of the Serpent, Talisman of the Raptor, Talisman of the Brute, Talisman of the Rhino, Talisman of the Cat, Talisman of Shadoo, Talisman of Jacinth
Shaman has also Focus of Spirit in his spellbook, maybe the client wants this?

*** Learn more of the buffs ***

Did you know that Focus of Spirit is a L 60 spell? Ever wondered why that Luminary (L55-59 Shammie) wont talk with you anymore after you requested four times in a row a Focus from him? There are nice websites out there with spell lists. Or you even may *cough* ask a Shaman what level a certain spell is (please do this after the raid though).

*** The Right-klick-check-mantra ***

/tell Shaman "STA"
Shaman gets message: "That spell wont have hold on the target"
Tank tells you "Oh i already have that buff from another Shaman"

Check your Buffs BEFORE sending a request to the Shaman. Its so easy. Right-klick your buffs and check them.
Please all relax now, take a deeeeep breath and slowly repeat my mantra:
"Right-click-check!"."Right-click-check!"."Right-click-check!"
This can also be helpful in other situations and may prevent you one day from giving your Tranquil Staff to Ssolet Dnaas instead of the Burnished Wooden Staff.

*** Request Buff-refreshs asap ***

Raidleader shouts "CHARGE"
SleepyTank_01 tells you "can i have quick Focus and DR?"
SleepyTank_02 tells you "Focus quick!"
SleepyTank_03 tells you "I was rezzed 15 minutes ago and have no buffz"
Buff icons start blinking for 30 seconds before fading. Thats not to hypnotize you. Its a signal for you to wake up, check which buff fades and send tell to Shaman. Its your responsibility to be combat ready all the time; you never know what mob is around the corner or when the raid-leader calls charge.

*** Sit down ***

No joke.
Spells have a certain range. If you are running around like a headless chicken you will probably out of range when the Shammie tries to buff you. Or you will miss that group buff. Or slip on a banana peel.
Also asking for a buff and running away 10 seconds later to pull a mob wont help you much. Really.
Note: I am aware that there are situation which forbid sitting down due to complicated aggro-thingies or such.Then try to reduce your movements to a minimum while the raid buffs up. You will help lots by NOT behaving like being intoxicated with 19 cups of coffee.

Good-bye Mr WW-Dragon!

They noticed that monsters dispell one of their first 4 buffs. So its wise decision to bury the mana-expensive buffs deep deep deep. And then there are some instant-self-buff-casting items. They fill in nicely that just dispelled dummy buff and prevent nasty Mr. WW-Dragon from breathing deeper.
So if you fight dispelling monsters, please take care of buff sequence. Grab some cheap buffs (see invis, Ultravision or such) first; THEN ask shammie for the big guns. And try to get hold of a shrunken goblin earring or another instant-casting item.

*** Be patient ***

You learned all the basics. An Icon is blinking. You checked your buffs. You sit down. You sent the Shaman a short precise tell : "FOCUS, STA, ACUMEN". But nothing happens. A whole 60 seconds no reaction. He must be asleep! You have to take measures and inform the raidleader (who is just sitting around he whole time doing nothing) about this.

Please dont get angry! Instead let me tell you a few things about my job so you understand the problems i face.

- Mana pool
The Shamans manapool is roughly around 2500-3000 mana.
The best buffs eats lots of mana.
Example: one casting of Focus of Spirit eats 500 mana. So after buffing 6 tanks with Focus the mana pool is empty.
Even with cannibalizing and high regrowth-rate it will take a bit time to regain some mana.

- Spell-slots
8 Spell-slots. Thats not much. My standard raid setup claims 6 slots: Cannibalize4, Torpor, Turgurs, Malo, Malosini, Cripple. Thats 2 slots (maybe 3 if I kick cripple out) for memming buffs. There are far more Standard-raid-buffs: Focus, Riotous Health, Acumen, several group-stat/resist-buffs. Even more when the Shaman is not Focus-enabled.
So the probability is high that I dont have that buff memmed you have requested and have to dememorize one buff and memorize your Focus. This takes time and klicking and rummaging my spellbook. And some buffs also have a certain time after being memmed during which they cant be casted. So sometimes i am not dreamily staring at you while scratching my butt. Instead I wait for that darn spell-Icon to be klickable.

- Debuffing is also our job
Shamans not only buff, they also debuff. Turgurs slows monsters down (250 mana). Malo debuffs resists (350 Mana). So during pulling many Shamans like to keep at least one blue bubble of mana for debuffing instead of going oom. A slowed Sebilite Protector is worth more than that one additional Focus on a tank.

- Berserking makes silent
There is that nice Berseker-line of self-only Shaman-buff spells.
Example: Voice of the Berzerker (L 59) adds 84 Strength, 24 AC, 74 Dexterity, 74 Agility.
I need theses additional stats because some monsters dont like being debuffed and beating the crap out of me and it helps me survive and channel through that Turgurs or Malo.
The drawback: After going berserk the Shaman is silent for 30 seconds and cannot cast anything. Doh! Rumors are that the Shaman is not even able to send a tell or reply during this time.

- The PE-Problem (Rant)
Let me tell you about that nice toy from Veeshan Peak: Hammer of Dragonborne, SHM only. 2 HB, some stats. And it can cast a very nice Buff with high stackablity: Primal Essence. This adds 20 Str, Dex, Agi, Sta. Tanks love it. And request it often once the word has spread.
Most Shammies love the hammer when they get it.
And then they will get aware of the two major drawbacks: Casting time is insane 15 seconds. Hammer must be equipped for casting.
So that what happens in that typical ToV-raid situation: Tanks tells you "PE".
Things i have to do now:
- open inventory
- open bag
- de-equip Sarnak Battle Shield
- de-equip Spear of Fate
- equip Hammer
- target tank
- klick hammer
- wait 15 seconds
- deequip Hammer
- equip Spear
- equip Shield
Yes i would keep the hammer equipped all the time. But I will lose tons of stats, AC, HP and resists because of that. I need AC and resists when that incoming Elder Drake has to be slowed. Sometimes i am thinking of deleting that hammer.

- We are not perfect and tend to get tired after 6 hours
There are rarely more than two Shamans at one raid. Often only one.
There are often more than two tanks at a raid. Often 15-20.
There are often more than two mobs to slow/malo.
There is rarely more than two minutes pause for a Shaman.
So after the sixth hour our shiny perfection starts to crumble and shatter and a buff request might slip our attention.
So if you dont get your buffs even after waiting a certain time (i suggest 2-3 minutes) just refresh yor request. Its simple as that.

I hope these ramblings help you all to understand a bit the role and work of a Shaman. And make you and your next raid a bit more efficient and rewarding.

Ancient Council - Shaman's Guide to Raiding
Jaas (Oracle - Saryrn - Ancient Council)
May 2002

This guide provides information which should be useful for shaman's involved in a number of high level encounters as a raid participant. There are many variations depending on equipment, spell availability, group makeup, and other members of the raiding party. This guide is aimed at level 50 plus shaman. At the end there is a FAQ for when the optimal raiding group is not present.

Group Makeup

A Shaman can contribute as a secondary in many roles on a raid. Primary role is in buffing players and debuffing mobs and slowing, however, shaman's can play a valuable role in healing, damage (DOTS), and curing disease, poison or curse during and after the fight..

If you are in the position as the primary healer in the group, see if another shaman on the raid can perform buffs which are mana intensive.

I would also recommend if you are on a raid to volunteer managing the groups several times. This provide excellent insight into what works and does not work with groups as groups request different makeups.

Pre-fight, otherwise known as "Suck Mana Dry"

The busiest time for a shaman is prior to the battle in order to help prepare the raiding party for engagements.

When there are more players on the raid than mana, you need to prioritize. It does no good to have everyone buffed but no mana for debuffing when the mobs arrive. If not doing group buffs, start with the highest players and work down, these should be the ones gaining agro and needing them most. You can save yourself reading a lot of tells by announcing your intentions in the raid channel.

This is when the shaman is doing all possible to acquire mana for the buffing which needs to be done. There are some things which can help with mana:

• Get assigned to a group with a bard
• Use the secondary healers to heal you while you cann
• Have the necros dump mana into you
• Have other classes do junk buffs if you are strapped for time
• If there are that many to buff and it's a single mob engagement, have people camp out after buffs and return on demand

Make sure that the buffs are protected with junk buffs when fighting mobs that can removed buffs and that the correct buffs are protected. For instance Resist Fire can be a junk one time but primary buffs for another engagement.

If buffs are wearing off, let the raid leader know so that adjustments can be made.

See Duty priority list for sequence of buffs.

Mob Engagement, otherwise known as "see how hard the mob hits"

The primary focus for the shaman during the initial part of the fight is sticking a slow on the mob as quickly as possible once engaged. This also makes you the focus of the mobs affections.

This has many benefits such as reducing the amount of healing required during the engagement, saving mana for all the healers, and reduces the number of deaths.

Of course, you want to wait until annul is complete and the Main Assist has acquired agro. It may be required to do malo, tash, malosin and bard debuff before attempting slow. If the mob resists frequently, having multiple shaman on slow duties can help here.

For mobs which rampage, cripple is essential as well. This is a spell which shaman and enchanters can cast.

Battle Assignments

One shaman on the raid should provide raid assignments to ensure that all critical debuffs are completed and prevent overlapping buffs. Considerations should be made for what other duties have been assigned to the shaman for the raid or if the shaman is hydra'ing other characters who have primary raid duties.

Post Battle Activities

After the battle or between pulls there may be players which have been rezzed, hopefully not you. Rebuff these players and help heal them up if you have spare mana.

FAQ's

Q1) I am the only healer in the group with five melee types, how can I keep the members alive?

A1) Have the melee's back off when they reach 60 percent hit points, this give you an opportunity to heal them. If you are relying on only chloroblast try and get a cleric to help, but since you have been assigned primary healer, chances are there are no spare clerics.


Q2) I can't keep up with the buffs for the whole raiding party, I am the only one. How do I make this work?

A2) Don't buff everyone, prioritize.


Q3) Should I buff everyone with everything?

A3) Only if you have the time and the mana. Primarily you want to add HPs to all, focus/kragg/harness, and STA. AGI can help melee classes.


Q4) Why bother buffing a caster with stamina?

A5) It provides hit points which gives the healers a little more time to keep the casters alive. The Kael BP is particularly useful here, cast on all members of the raiding party between fights.


Q5) How can Alchemy help in large encounters?

A5) Damage Shield Potions are useful, so are heal potions. By the time you get to raiding the large mobs most people should have the resists up with gear. Gate is also useful in cases where melees are stranded at the end of the battle and the last porters have left.


Q6) Why assign more than one shaman to the same duty, that would seem to waste mana?

A6) Some mobs need many casts if resists are high. Make sure that the secondary and tertiary shamans watch for the debuff tells before burning a cast (i.e., malo, tash, malosini)


Q7) When the Main Assist has just died, how can I help?

A7) Use /target mob to select the next player on the agro list and heal the next agro person immediately, this gives a small buffer while the cleric(s) re-acquire the new MA.


Q8) When should we not perform the debuffing duties?

A8) If the mobs are dying within 30 seconds due to a larger than normal raiding party, don’t debuff mobs engaged. Do however, debuffed mezzed mobs.


Q9) How can enchanters help keep me alive, I keep dying?

A9) Enchanters can mem wipe, removing you (and them) from agro list. The enchanters should already know this.


Q10) What effect does chain casting debuffs have on a mob?

A10) Opportunity for Rez.


Q11) People broadcast in raid channel for buffs, what do I do.

A11) Ignore them, raid members needing buffs should do direct tells and for group buffs only the group leader, when present should do the tell.


Q12) High level mobs resist slow but the web indicates the mob should be slowable, what can I do?

A12) Try lower level slows, progressing to higher level slows over time. Careful not to chain cast, see A10.


Q13) The same character dies every time and asks for full buffs after every fight, you are out of mana, what do you do?

A13) Tell him you have no mana for buffs, change his fighting tactics or leave the raid till he acquires the required resist gear.


Q14) What about pets?

Q13) Only four pets can engage at any given time, make sure necros and mages get their pets in first. Otherwise, even if it is only 1000pts of damage, every little bit helps.


Q15) Some people carry coffins, what's the point of that if shamans cannot summon corpse?

A15) CR typically happens at the end of a long night, if it is unlikely a rez is done at the camp and SC is required, get your butt back to the zone in with a coffin, there may be a SK or Necro there which can help you. This can save an hour at the end of a long night. If you want to help other members, carry a stack of small coffins to get them back faster if coffins are not enroute.


Q16) I can tell that this is a lost cause and at least one cleric has camped, what should I do?

A16) Remember, restarting the raid after CR takes a long time, you can help by camping with the cleric to get the raiding party going faster with heals after rez and buffs as the members are rezzed.


Q17) When mages are CoH for raid, how can I help?

A17) Heal the mage, he/she is most likely using mana rods to gain mana for CoH.


Q18) On normal plane raids there are multiple incoming, do I wait for MA to call assist?

A18) No, get as many slows in as possible as mobs are incoming to help keep the enchanters alive.


Q19) When should I cast Avatar?

A19) Due to it’s short lived nature, only immediately prior to the MA engaging the mob, and then on the secondary tank as the spell becomes available again.
