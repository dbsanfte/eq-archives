Which race should a beginning shaman be?

In the end game, there is only one class who gets a raw, inherent combat advantage over each other and that is Ogre. With the highest base Stamina and the inherent front side invulnerablilty to stun, that alone gives them an advantage no other has.

Barbarians, though, recieving no inherent bonuses, nor infravision, are the light race choice with an excellent newbie area adjacent to their city, the ability to take advantage of a majority of quests and a solid physical base to go with their strong wisdom base.

Here's an attempt while waiting for more emails to flood in at work:

All races have their strong points but can quickly be summed up to some positive and negative issues:

Barbarian:
Pros: Good starting Wis. Good faction with all "light" cities. Smallest exp penalty.
Cons: Poor night vision but is quickly overcome by your own spells. "Cookie cutter" shaman

Iksar:
Pros: Natural Regen helps reduce downtime for all of your levels. Small bonus to Natural AC. Planes armor usually concidered superior to the other races' armor.
Cons: Highest exp penalty. Faction is poor with all cities other than your starting continent. Unable to wear Plate armor including the fabled JBB.

Ogre:
Pros: Highest natural stamina and unstunnable from the front. Generally accepted to be the strongest race at lvl 60.
Cons: Faction work will be needed if you wish to go to most cities. High exp penalty.

Trolls:
Pros: As with Iksar, Natural Regen will help with downtime. Worshipers of Innoruk can get a quest necklace that casts Snare.
Cons: Higest exp penalty shared with Iksar. Same faction issues as Ogre

Vah Shir:
Pros: Sneak and Safefall give some small advantages in some zones. Faction and Exp Penalty is comparable to Barbarian. Damn sexsay looking
Cons: Food consumption double normal races. Starting cities on Luclin - not recommended unless you have a high-end computer.

Starting Stats on all races:
It is generally concidered that your two most important starting stats are Wisdom and Stamina. Stamina is one of the hardest stats to raise and will give you more HPs at high levels, HPs can be converted to Mana with our Cannabalize spells. The general consensus is to split your points between the two with more stamina being a more "aggressive" tactic that will make lower levels harder to play but have a larger payoff at high levels.

Nektar
Which race should a beginning shaman be?

DO I hear neighing? Cuz this is a dead horse. Every class possesses its advantages, it's up to you which you like best:

1) Barbarian: Lowest exp penalty of the possible shaman races, not ugly :D, can equip JBB, *good* faction standing. Anyways, not like faction matters as much anymore with Luclin if you don't kos yourself to Shadowhaven/Bazaar.
2) Troll: Natural regen, can quest snare necklace (right click level 8 necro snare, lasts around 30 seconds, farily easily resistable), can equip JBB.
3) Ogre: Can't be stunned from the front, can equip JBB.
4) Iksar: Higher innate wis and ac (not 100% sure about ac), starts out with 100 swimming, natural regen.
5) Vah'Shiir: No clue.

I believe ogres and trolls start out with the highest amount of stamina too = more HP's.

What is the best way to spend AA points?

A lot of people start out by putting 3 points into run. Might sound illogical since we have sow, but comes in handy in dungeons. I like to pull sometimes, and the faster I get back to camp, the less chance I die. Or if some typical noob trains me, I'm a catch back up to him, re-tag him with mobs, and head straight to zone :D

As for stat points, I'd first work on the ones which are important to shamans but that you neglected when you created your character. I had put no points into wisdom for example, so I trained that after run 3. You can splash your points around, but after having spent 6 points in general, I'd move to priest abilities and spend 12 points there to be able to progress to shaman archetype.

There are a couple nice abilities for priest archetype, and if your intention is to be able to train for shaman archetype asap, I'd put 12 aa levels in for 3 mental clarity points. Mental Clarity 3 will enable you to start training Cannibalization which can be extremly useful for downtime.

Raid-wise, Mass Group Buff is also vital if you have Khura's Focusing. Some other nifty skills, but if you want more info, check this <a href="http://eq.castersrealm.com/archetype/default.asp?Action=Cat&ClassId=4">page</a> out.

What good is wisdom over 200?

Pre-Luclin, having wisdom over 200 didnt help much. You'd get on average 1 mana per extra point of wisdom, and in that respect, it was much better to get +mana gear once over 200. It might have changed with Luclin, I don't know, but I still wouldn't opt for 255 wis at the loss of mucho AC.

Are any stat buffs worth casting? Which ones?
At lower levels, you want to have your hp's and ac buffed. Shamans get 2 different lines of AC buffs, agility and those little yellow shield icons which use up abjuration.

As for hp's, the only buff you'll have at first is stamina, but eventually you'll be stacking targ/tuna onto it.

At 24, you get your first regen spell. I don't think I need to go lengths to explain why regen is so important.

At 29, you get bearform. It increases wisdom by 5 and regen by 1 point/tick.

If you have a proc'ing weapon, buff your dex up too; if you don't, don't bother.

Then at 49 you get Harness of the Spirits. Although I personally never need the str and dex in combat, I always cast Focus (level 60 upgrade to HoS) on myself because:
1) It gives more hp than the standard hp buff, which at 49 would be Altuna and at 60 Kragg.
2) I can always use the extra strength because my inventory is always fullacrap.

Haste... I know some people like to play battle-shamans, but it's best to just give your pet haste imo if you're soloing or melees if you're grouped. If you have no problem whatsoever keeping up with mana, then knock yourself out and add to the damage output :cool:


How do I get started with Alchemy?

<a href="http://www.eqshaman.com/">Link</a>

Personally, I havent trained alchemy in a while, and although the idea does sound appealing, I think alchemy is a lost cause. I've trained it up to 70 for trivializing on shrink potions, and a couple potions out there as useful such as sow and ivu, but unless you got a lot of cash and patience to burn, I wouldn't recommend putting too much effort into the skill.

I make sow potions for my monk, shrink potions for fun, and ivu potions for undead-infested areas ie Lguk, Kaesora etc.

I was looking at the list however, and there are a *couple* useful potions I could make use of like Skin Flame and Greater Rejuvenation, but even for those I'm only thinking about high end solo encounters.

If you're thinking gate potions though, forget about it. Way too expensive to make, long cast time, takes high alchemy skill, and now people can make thurg potions anyways with pottery (a much less expensive tradeskill).

Why is alchemy so expensive?

To make a potion, you need 2 or more reagents (ingredients). Most of them are sold by NPC vendors and therefore they can charge whatever damn price they want :p Some reagents are relatively cheap like the ones needed for sow, but some are ungodly expensive like the ones for gate to InnoSwamp/Everfrost/FoB.

What is "cannidancing" and how do I do it?

Basically, when you med, your mana increases every 6 seconds (assuming you're not starved). So what a shaman does is stand up right after he notices an increase in mana, casts canni, and sits back down in time for the next mana increase.

Cannibalize I has a casting time of 1.25, so this gives the shaman ample opportunity to canni and sit back down in time - same thing for Can II and Can III. Can IV is 2.5 seconds, but it's still doable.

I want to solo with my shaman instead of grouping. How can I solo effectively?

This is totally dependent on level. There are several different solo stages in a shaman's life and accordingly different soloing styles. For any given stage, you even have several possible strategies. The following I have to offer is based on my own experience, and by no means encompasses all forms of soloing:

1) Pre-pet, I guess it's root, dot, kite. For the AC freaks, I suppose it's doable by tanking the mob head on, casting slow, dots/dd's, and bashing it with some high dmg weapon like a velium brawl stick.

2) 34-45: If you're soloing outdoors, shaman pet is an excellent addition to your solo capabilities. I didn't use it as a tank, but as a manafree dot nipping at the mob while it was chasing me. Dots are best used in this scenario imo. Remember that if you use this method to put the pet on /notaunt.

If you're soloing indoors, I'd be very careful with pets because of pathing and aggro. Pets have a hard time following you when there's a lot of twists and turns, and since they're quite a few levels lower than the shaman himself, his aggro radius is much larger.

3) 45-59: All JBB for me, which kinda sucks if you're not iksar. I'd pull with slow, then tank it all the way while chaining my bracer. This doesn't go to say soloing for iksars is out of the question, but it's a little harder. They can still tank the mob head on, but instead of chaining jbb, they'd cast like dots or something, and epic when they get it. Epic is nice too.

4) 60 (with malo and torpor): Hardly ever use jbb, just don't have the time when the mob is doubling for 150-250. I sometimes tank, but not all the time. If my pet can get aggro, the fight will be slower, but it makes it that much easier for me because I won't be interrupted. But basically for high end encounters, I think it's safe to say oracles chain torpor and canni all the time.

I also mentionned malo because some mobs would be almost impossible to solo without (or exceedingly risky if you dig euphemisms). I can think of seahorses in siren's ghetto for example.


What is "slow" and why should I do it?

A slow is a debuff which reduces the mob's attack speed by X%. It starts as low as 11% at level 5 and goes up to 75% at level 60. Trust me, once mobs start hitting for over 100, you'll realize how vital it is. I'd say by the time you get tagars you will notice a significant change in efficiency, not only because of the percentage increase in slow, but also because mobs start getting less and less wussier ie giants in FM and whatever in OT.

Okay, I know why I should slow, but everything seems to resist, these spells use a lot of mana!

Some mobs have higher natural resists than others. Slow is kinda messed up in the sense that it's now considered a disease but it has a magic resist check.

If you feel the mobs you're fighting resist your slows too often, ask the chanter to tash on a regular basis. If you got a bard, ask him to sing mr debuff song until mob is slowed. If you got a mage, ask him to malise. If all else fails, try to malise him yourself. Use a low-mana cost one and chain it until it sticks. Malise has the advantage of generating very little aggro, and if it sticks, at least you know the mob is definatly slowable (unless the mob is naturally immune to slows despite his resists - not something really worth retaining, but I'm just pointing this out in case some smart (_|_) decides to point it out :p).

Stats:
The short answer: They don't do much. The longer answer: They do a little bit, but not too much.

Str: Will increase the average damage a hit will do. Since tanks swing very often, you want it to be as high as possible and it'll add up over time. Blacksmithing (and possibly more tradeskills) use Str as an alternative stat to base skill increases on.

Dex: Increases chance of learning offensive skills and making a weapon "Process" aka proc.

Agil: Increases chance of learning defensive skills and adds a small amount of AC. Note: Agil below 75 will take a larger hit against AC

Wis: Amount of mana for Priest type spellcasters. At low levels +mana items will usually benefit you more but they cost more. At 200 the amount of mana you get per point of Wis is not as significant but has been improved from the past. Tradeskills use the higher of Int or Wis (and occasionally Str)

Int: Same as Wis but for Int casters such as Wizards, Mages, Necros and SKs.

Charisma. Break points for buying & selling from vendors are 75, 103 and 132 (IIRC. I'm not as sure about 103 it might be higher). You want this as high as possible when buying or selling from Vendors. There is a frequently debated point on whether this stat helps slows, fears or charms to land but there is very little statistical evidence to back these claims up.

Playstyle techniques

I want to solo with my shaman instead of grouping. How can I solo effectively?

Two and a half methods. The first is the root and dot. This is pretty self explanatory. Root the MOB, sit a safe distance away and DOT (Damage over time spells) until MOB dies. The benefit to this is you're gaining mana back as the MOB loses health. The downside is your melee skills suffer.

The other method is the pull with slow method. (Your Txrgxrs spell line). Make sure you have all the appropriate AC, Hit point, haste and melee buffs. Then you can fight the MOB down. The downside is your downtime may be greater.

The hybrid of these is root, slow, dot, then melee the MOB down. The upside is you are combing of a Shaman strengths. The downside is your downtime may be greater.

What is "slow" and why should I do it?

"Slows" (again your Txrgxr, walking sleep, drowsy), affect a MOBs attack speed. You do it because you get the best slows in the game, you can take a pounding better than the Enchanter (who can also slow), and in the current EQ paradigm, Shaman slow.
