Give it a new perspective....

I have been asking myself lately if the flavor is lost cause we have been asked to swollow to fast. If you know you get a drop now that will be outdated in a month, why work so hard to get it? Stuff is being trivialized and it almost means nothing when you obtain it. The whole point is to release, upgrade, progress. Just to up your stats and really there is no motive or drive. What is the point?

I looked forward to getting a TA and time gear and enjoying it for awhile, than the new releases come and bam my gear is worthless with the old focus items. Gear is upgraded and before you can really feel like you accomplished something you are getting upgrades for gear you have been in a month. I remember wearing my vindi BP for at least a year or more. That felt good when you knew you did a hard encounter and you could enjoy a piece of armor and be set for awhile.

Maybe its just me, but I think the money bus needs to slow down. Releasing expansion upon expansion only lessens the experience of the game. If you can't enjoy a zone or an expansion for at least a month before the new one comes out, you kind of question why even bother.

If you take away the hard work people put into a game and minimize it for money, I guarantee people will loose interest. People want the hard work and effort to mean something. They want to enjoy the game and the gear they work hard to get.

I think SOE really needs to focus on shifting the pattern of content and making EQ an experience of its own. I am talking about rounding out the whole game. Make it an experience that you can only get in EQ1. Bring everything on par with each other, but revamp the delivery.

Stop releasing new stuff and maybe combine some encounters with quests. Progress a guild through quest encounters and raid experience.

I would try to tie the encounters to a path in the game for guilds. Have them follow a story line and some form of faction. For instance make guilds choose a faction and stick with it to gain better abilities. Then allow them drops aligned with those factions, that was the fun of velious. You could eliminate people from using the gear for twinks if you make it faction specific.

The other fun for me in this game was competition for content. I am sorry, but that in itself attracted many folks to EQ. It was competitive.

If the guilds could prosper as a whole it would make it much more exciting. If one guild could get an edge over another through the use of skill, it would ignite the game back to life. If EQ could somehow move toward a group benefit as opposed to an indivdual benefit it might bring a new perspective to the game. Make guilds work to quest special traits that only they can master based maybe on faction. Only one guild could align with a faction this making them masters in that field. No other guild could gain that faction once its taken. You could have the different factions allow only those guild members special abilities in the encounter or game.

I would take the emphasis off gear and make higher end progression a more complex beast than linear progression of gear and harder hitting mobs. The same equation with new character models gets old fast.

Making a new game and starting over in a different world will only hold the attention of people for so long.

I am writing this cause I don't plan on leaving my shaman and it pains me to see other people's hard work thrown down the drain. It bothers me everytime someone says they are leaving this game. This game just needs to be adjusted to keep people playing. I hope that a few ideas might be considered or is it to little to late?

As much as I hate to agree with this, I do - for the most part. My disagreement though is with the idea that this is EQ's mentality. It isn't. It is the majority player view. The majority believe the game doesn't start until the "end game."

SOE has fed that viewpoint with the release of OoW. Think about it. GoD release resulting in a significant loss of high end players. Guilds that finally said, "sorry, tired of outrageous content that is not finished, drops that are too rare, events that are too bugged, etc .. " So they left. Follow that with the SOE belief that the majority player base is in the 35-55 range. (Yeah, I know, no one, except SOE - possibly - believes this.)

The success of the formula for building expansions to cater to the high end guilds died with GoD. Pretty much this is natural. How long are folks willing to rinse and repeat the same template to stay high end? Apparently not forever.

OoW is a radical response for SOE to the problem of lossing the cash flow from a tried, true and now outdated formula. Very nice, EP quality, items dropping from single groupable (and rare drops from soloable) mobs initially energized the remaining base of players. Smaller guilds, unguilded, unflagged folks have a way to get gear that is similar to drops from high end PoP mobs.

Ironically folks are not maintaining the energized level about these possibilities of gear. Initially yes. But as the formula is the same they have had to deal with to get sub-par gear in the past - camp a spot, pull to spot, kill over and over and over - the enjoyment value is just not there. It is just BoT without giants. While the gear has clear value, the journey leaves no real sense of new, challenging content and so there is no acheivement. Just like folks use mains to get gear to twink alts ... OoW mobs are twinking mains. Minimum effort for maximum benefit.

Just like BoT, folks camp and camp and camp and progressively get items and spell drops. Without challenge. No skill required.

There is where EQ is failing. And it really isn't their fault so much as the fault of what they see from player "wants." So we get back to the 'journey vs. destination.' EQ could easily be renewed and reinvented if SOE developed the game to focus on journey. Instead their design is responding to what they see the player base wanting. The paradox is the player base doesn't really want what they claim to want - they just don't know what the solution is that will give them what they actually do want.

Challenges, real quests, real progression and a personally unique game flow path to the game would generate great new interest in the game. At least for the old, been playing since forever, base.

For all the complaining and yelling and hours upon hours of frustration in the evolution of this game, as I look back, the majority of the good times and fond memories were the result of overcoming the frustration. Camping Raster for 2 weeks straight, 4-5 hours a night with a friend (monk) to progress was mind-numbingly repetitive at the time. In retrospec, we had fun. We found things to do to keep entertained between spawns. We chatted. We chatted with guildmates. Guildmates came and went, joining us for a few rounds of spawns. This was just one camp.

The number of camps and raids and efforts to progress mine and other's epics or class quests or dungeon crawls for gear upgrades are way too many to count here. The memories of great times that often involved lengthy CRs - while a pain at the time - are many. Hell, as a noob I lost more levels trying to get my body back in Befallen than I will admit here. Damn that well!!

Today that wouldn't happen, but if it did the player would likely quit. More likely they would log on a friend's high level necro to summon them, then a cleric to rez their level 12 toon so they didn't loose XP ... and also for a Temp buff. For me, at that time, there was no way I was going to lose the int ring I had camped Cuthbert for a week to get. Or the water crystal staff a friend had loaned me ... LOANED!!

Jumping ahead to the first epics - finally getting it was a major sense of accomplishment. The progression was slow, irritating, frustrating at times ... and absolutely "too cool" to finally get to the end and get it. Now it is taking a couple of weeks of focused effort to get the 1.5 epic. And folks complain about the 'work' necessary to get there. Amazing.

SOE has given in to the gotta-have-it-now players. These players yet complain about the slow pace ... because, for them (not SOE) the destinitation is the point, not the journey. No sense of true effort leading to true accomplishment.
