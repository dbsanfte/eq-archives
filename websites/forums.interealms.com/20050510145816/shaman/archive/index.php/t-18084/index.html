Sariss
As of 1 or 2 patches ago, I began to notice that slow now only affects casters melee attacks, and no longer affects their spell casting.

It seems to behave just like slowing PCs now. If you and your wizard friend dueled, and you cast slow on him/her... big deal... they can still sit there and chain nuke you. If they pulled out their staff and tried to smack you... they would be slowed... but spells are unaffected.

Maybe I'm wrong, and just never noticed before... but in the past, whenever you slowed a NPC caster... it would slow down their spell casting rate as well.

For arguments sake...

Lets say NPC_Wizard attacks you every 1 second. Every 1 second the wizard can either cast, or melee. It might look something like this:

1s = nuke
2s = nuke
3s = root
4s = melee
5s = melee
6s = nuke
7s = melee
8s = snare

etc...

Random... melee or spell every 1 second.

Now lets say... after you slow the wizard, they attack every 5 seconds.

1s = nuke
5s = melee
10s = nuke
15s = root
20s = melee

etc...

Now it appears... that slow only affects the melee speed... and not the spell casting speed.

1s = nuke
2s = nuke
3s = nuke
4s = nuke
5s = melee
6s = nuke
7s = nuke
8s = nuke
9s = nuke
10s = melee

(note: I realize that they can't cast a spell every 1s because of cast times and such, times used are only for example purposes)

This of course makes NPC casters much much more of a problem than they were before this.

Can anyone else confirm this? At first I thought I was imagining it... and forgot to slow or something... but now others seem to be seeing the same thing.

Did this just go in with the last patch? Or the one before? I could have sworn that slow always affected casters. Maybe it didn't, and I'm just not realizing it (which is kinda sad if true, but oh well).

I guess in theory this is the way it should be... this is how it works on PCs. Still not a nice thing to slip in un-announced.
