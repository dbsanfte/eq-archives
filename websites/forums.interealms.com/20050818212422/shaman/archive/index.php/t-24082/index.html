Fudapuda
The only thing I see missing is siccing dogdog on the mob. My general routine in a group like that is Malos on incoming IF the mob is hard to Slow without it OR if I have casters in group who will be more effective in nuking with Malos. Otherwise, I skip that step. Then Slow with either Nihil if I know it will be less than 90 seconds of fight, or Turgur's if its gonna be longer.

Next step is to sic dogdog on. Try to always be behind the mob when you do that, for two reasons: first, it helps to have dogdog pushing TOWARD the tank, rather than away. Second, if you happen to be on a mob that Enrages, being behind will keep dogdog from dying on every pull.

After that, its really situational: if you dots land well on this type of mob, by all means, drop them on. I almost always hit stuff with Bane, and then Saryrn if the fight will last long enough to make it worth it. Then its either heal or Canni. I hardly ever bother to nuke--shaman nukes are just not worth the effort either in terms of damage done or in efficiency. Plus, I usually find I can fill the time checking buffs: I usually overwrite my own Haste fairly often, just to keep the tanks from bitching about it dropping. I rotate FA on anyone who is doing melee, even the idiot cleric if they want it (although I WILL remember the clerics that tank, and try to avoid grouping with them again--I've died TOO many times because the asshat cleric was so busy meleeing they didn't notice the add come in and start beating on people, or notice that aggro had shifted and I was getting a beatdown, or whatever).

There's certainly plenty to keep yourself busy most of the time, and the nicest part of being a shaman (if you don't mind being busy) is that you have so many options, all of which count as "productive" use of your time.

Ungkor
Depends on your pull rate, type of mobs, and your role in a group. Ideally, you want your mana to remain constant. Starts at 100m, mob inc, malos slow dot nuke, 80m. Canni continually until next mob. Mana is hopefully near 100m again.

However, using JBB is not smart. Better off canni'ing for 8 seconds and then casting another dot or nuke.

Just remember this:

"A good shaman never stops casting, ever".

If you stop casting for any reason, chances are you aren't doing enough. If you are sitting there at FM casting a jbb... you need to be casting a big nuke+rain each mob and then canni back to FM between fights. JBB is a waste of time.

If your cleric/druid in group is saying "OOM" ever, chances are, you need to cut out one nuke and cast Q or a patch heal from time to time. Or if the ranger is kiting and gets hit to 60%, heal him.

How you choose to spend your mana is ultimately up to you. But your choices should be based on your groups goals. If the group goal is fast steady pulls, balance healing/dmg/debuffing to maximize your groups kill rate. But again, if you aren't continually casting something, you aren't a good shaman.
