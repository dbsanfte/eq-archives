In replying to another thread it just came to me.

I now know where the 100/75/47 came from and why . . . and why it should go away.

Many of us have said all along that Shaman were healers at their inception but somewhere along the way this was lost. I agree.

In order to understand the healing ratio, we have to go back to the days of original EQ. Back *before* the level cap was raised from 50. Back *before* Turgurs.

In those days, our maximum Slow was a 50% Slow. We didn't have Stoicism or Torpor. We didn't have our 75% heal.

At that time, Clerics big "blast" heal was Superior Healing, ours was Greater Healing. Oddly enough, that puts us at right around 50% (51% to be exact) of the Cleric heal. But remember, we could Slow, and *nothing* mitigated Slow.

Now, we also had "Incapacitate" . . . and I'd be willing to bet that in the minds and calculations of the Devs *at that time* the effect of Incapacitate plus Slow was such that Shaman needed to heal only 47% as well as a Cleric to be a Main Healer. And surprisingly, at that time, this formula WORKED.

Fast forward to now . . . you have many changes in Devs, many expansions and new spells and abilities. I'd be willing to bet that none of the new Devs even know *why* that ratio exists. They simply know that this is the ratio that was passed to them as being "right" and that any changes to it would "break" EQ balance. And while that message might have been perfectly correct when it was written, I think we can all see how *wrong* it is in the context of todays EQ.
