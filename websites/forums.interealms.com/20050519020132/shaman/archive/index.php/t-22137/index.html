Shaman
Shamans serve as priests to the tribal races of Norrath, able to draw upon the power of the spirits and focus it for their own purposes. Shamans are primarily a magic-using class, able to wear chain armor and wield blunt weapons and spears.

Shamanic magic has two main focuses - enhancing the mind and body of the shaman's allies, and weakening or damaging their enemies. A shaman's enhancement spells are always in demand, massively improving the strength, stamina, health, and other physical aspects of the shaman's group. Shamans also are able to heal their allies and have excellent health regeneration spells.

The weakening and damaging spells in the shaman's arsenal are tremendously effective in battle. The shaman attacks his enemies with diseases and poisons that cause massive damage over time, while using cold and ice magic to inflict damage directly. A brutal line of crippling spells can slow a monster's attacks, hamper their resistance to spells, and lower their physical prowess.

Shamans are a well-rounded class with strong abilities in both group and solo situations. An experienced shaman can turn the tide of any battle, bolstering their allies while weakening their enemies significantly. The shaman is an excellent class for players who enjoy working in groups.

Shaman. Similar to a Cleric, but closer to a tribal witch doctor, the Shaman can be found amongst the more primative races. Her primary focus is healing, but she can employ many augmentation and offensive spells as well.

Back in 99 when I created my shaman, this is the definition I remember - not that I paid much attention to the written definitions. Mainly I chose shaman for what I saw of it's abilities in the game - and primarly that was because I wanted to be a healer, but not a cleric (or druid). I didnt chose shaman to be a slower, because people didnt know what slow was then. I wanted buffs and healing. I knew I wasnt going to be as good as a cleric, but shamans and druids then were good enough to be the healer in a group in practically all situations. I spent many nights raiding Plane of Fear in the time before Kunark was released, and I was always assigned a group healer duty. Never once did a raid leader assign me or anybody else (shaman or enchanter) as a slower, even though slowing was something I started doing as soon as I got Drowsy.

Back then, some people would actually complain about me slowing and tell me I should be saving my mana for heals. They would change their tune though when I stopped casting Drowsy or Walking Sleep or Targar's and they saw the difference firsthand. Then Kunark came along and totally blew the lid off of the class - Turgur's practically mezzed mobs. From then on, we started quietly sliding out of the priesthood and into some other psedo-enchanter-necro niche. It didnt happen immediately because I still got groups for my healing and buffs, but I was also expected to slow as well now. I remember Kunark era as being the most taxing time for my character - trying like hell to keep mana but always being low mana.

Velious and Luclin rolled around and druids started getting better heals than shamans, and in time slow and buffs became my only duties. And for a long while I was actually relieved because of the difficulty I had during Kunark trying to do all three roles in a group. Part of the reason however, was because I was a slow leveler after Kunark. I had raced to 50 prior to Kunark and pretty much burned myself out on grinding. As I finally started catching up again and hit max level I had the mana and utility spells to allow me to do all three roles again without so much strain - but at this point my heals were now not up to the job. That's when I started to voice my concerns here and through feedback. That's when most people told me I was crazy - 'shamans arent healers'.

I guess what I'm saying, Purdydog, is that some of us who talk about shamans being primary healers in a group do so because that's how we were made. We just got the rug yanked out from under us, as somebody else put it. Not that I really care if you or anyone else gets it at this point, I ended billing 3 months ago.
