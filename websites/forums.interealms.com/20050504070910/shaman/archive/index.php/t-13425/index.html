55 is new pet, but puts you in xp range of level 60's and since corpse camping is legal, I'd reccomend staying out of there, the only AA's you need in 55 immidiatly are MC3 for Canni 5.

Malo is HUGE for PvP, but make sure you have the spell before going all the way to 60, Get a Journeyman's Walking Stick for tash, and a Di'Zok Oracle Shileah for Togors, and Epic for DoT, and you'll be a mana free machine. Procs have a very low resist rate on PCs, So you can Tash with the stick, then slap a malaisment and it will be similar to Malo.

Shaman own Melee in pvp.. Slow, Tank, Pet and DoTs... Disease of course works best, and remember that shaman are all about longevity, not the quick kill. invest some time into getting snare items, and those melee items to further aid that. Magic Resist is key against pure melee, as its stops snare, which is the only thing to be really afraid off. Stoic will be your best freind, *most slowed melee can't deal over 26dps except a ranger, or a really good strafeing rogue, use mouse look to follow a rogue and you'll be fine.

Rallos PvP isn't xp loss, but if it were I'd be at a lower level I can promise ya (well maybe not if I get xp for killing people) but as far as AA
s go... Canni5 is amazing, it lets you keep going and going and going, energizer bunny style, but then, Spirit Call, VP, and RC. All three of those are amazing in PvP, RC will cure DoTs ect that are on you, VP will push a melee back and root him... and its Disease based /drool, and SC3 is like 5K melee (unresistable) damage from multiple pets (meaning harder to kill them off) personally, I would stay 54, get the AA
s for MC3, Mass Canni, SC1 VP1 and RC1, and then power to 61. You will then be a pvp machine. 58 pet, 3 dogdogdogs, push back (interrupts) root, and disease cure, infinite mana with Canni 5 and torpor, and Malo. Rabid bear is only good if you can get them to stay still, and silly people don't wanna stay still.
