WHEN TWO TITANS MEET


Stacks Belch, purging,
screaming smoke
and howling steam behind.
A behemoth of steel
defies a young child's eyes.
Tugs push her out to sea,
guiding left to right,
finally letting go.

Solid flood of awkward construction,
crystallized perfection approaches
costumed in passive power,
hiding destruction.
Gliding within arms reach,
brushes the side,
people laugh and cry in merriment,
picking up chunks of ice,
light fluffy shards
and toss it back and forth
in child like play.

Lifeboats full only for precaution,
left adrift in the freeze,
with teddy bears and fathers
left to face chance,
waving goodbye from the deck.

A mighty crack,
like blackened lightning spent
by the hand of god.

Inside fine ballrooms,
turn upside down and back,
spinning.
Walls explode
with the force of water,
halls flood full and fast.

The coffin augers away,
to rest and be disguised
in explosive silts,
trimmed in swarming life.

Ramshackled in the deep
with the same contagious dark
as midnight after
candles are blown out.
From her decks,
songs and holy oaths
no longer sworn by sailors, echo.
Murmurs still humm.
