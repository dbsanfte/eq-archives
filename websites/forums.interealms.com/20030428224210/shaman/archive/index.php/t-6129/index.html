Dragonsfyre
I think pretty much everyone from the Shaman community up to Verant realises that Alchemy in it's current form is in dire need of a complete overhaul from the ground up. I am posting this as my suggestions for a revamp of Alchemy. I'm sure there are lots of ideas that can be added to it as well, just from reading the otrher posts in this forum.

Skillcap
--------
First, Alchemy is the only Tradeskill that maxes out below 250, at 180. There is no reason in my mind why this could not be remedied, and any overhaul of alchemy should start with this as it's first step. Currently, Alchemy is limited by the level of the Shaman, starting with level 25. While I do not believe that this restriction should be removed, I do think that the formula needs to be adjusted to accomodate the new Level 65 level limit, as well as allowing younger Shamans to start Alchemy at an earlier age.

The current formula is as follows: (Level - 24) * 5 = Max Skill Allowed

This provides for a max skill of 5 at level 25 up to a max skill of 180 at level 60. If this formula was kept going forward into PoP, we could expect that the new max skill level would be 205, still nowhere near the 250 other tradeskills have.

The formula I would propose is as follows: (Level - 14) * 5 = Max Skill Allowed

This new formula would provide for a max skill of 5 starting at level 15, and go up to a max skill of 250 at level 65. This would provide us the ability to fully explore a wide variety of potential new potion possibilities.


Prices
------
One of the longest running complaints from Shamans has been the prices for herbs and the buyback prices of potions. Base herb prices has a tremendous impact on how saleable our potions are on the player market. Buyback prices are mainly a matter of returning some portion of the amount spent on regents for potions that are not readily sellable on the open market. Making a potion for 20pp that is not sellable to any player for that much money, yet returns only a little over 9gp from an NPC merchant, has been and is unacceptable.

I don't think that anyone believes that Alchemy should make them rich. Other than Jewelry making in the old days, or being at 250 skill with the recent PoTC quest, few tradeskillers in any skill make their fortune from their trade. Also, just as most of the possible combinations in Jewelry and other skills have no viable PC market, not all potions should be viable to be sold to players. The majority of them in fact would be in place primarily to build skill, not to make plat. However, those potions for which there is absolutely no market must be sellable to a merchant for a minimum of 90% of the cost of the materials, just as manufactured Jewelry is for a jeweler. Lost money would come from failed combines as it should be, not from successful ones.

Obviously, setting prices for herbs is difficult due to the different economic conditions on the various servers. On some servers, several potions may be more saleable than on others, based on their cost compared to the relative economic prosperity of the population. Titan potions for example, which have little practical value, seem to sell much better for some Shamans than for others on other servers. Whether this is purely an economic or a cultural difference is unknown, but it certainly must be accounted for.


Recipe Complexity
-----------------
Virtually all of the tradeskills in the game have in the last year received an overhaul, making them far more viable to pursue beyond the 200 skill point. In most cases, this has involved multi-step recipes, ingredients that are foraged or found as ground spawns or looted from mobs or purchased from other tradeskillers, the use of special tools or utensils, many of those having to be constructed in some fashion as well. Some smithing recipes seem to be 10 steps deep. In Alchemy, 90% of our potions are "combine 2 or 3 store bought herbs in the medicine bag" and there you go. Old Alchemy had more of the "find the ingredients" type things, but most of those recipes are no more viable than the current Alchemy.

Where are the multi-step potion recipes? Where is our complexity? To be honest, I'm not asking for Alchemy to become like Smithing. I know few who could go thru that sadistic process to get to 250, and it would never be worth it. However, there are tons of things that would make good Alchemy ingredients:

- Items created by other tradeskills (brewing, baking, etc.)
- Enchanted items from Enchanters (vials of mana, etc.)
- Various summoned items (from Mages, Necros, etc.)
- Foraged items (Herbs, Zone Essences, etc.)
- Herbs that need to be Imbued or Enchanted in order to form the potion - Add a spell for doing this to our book
- Ground spawns
- Looted items


Foraging
--------
It always amazes me that Shamans do not have the ability to forage for things in general, but specifically for Alchemy ingredients. It totally fits with the mythos of the Shaman that they go out to the woods to find their herbs. I can't imagine that giving Shamans the Foraging skill starting at Level 30 or 35 would unbalance anything in the game. Considering how underappreciated the Shaman community feels today, this is a bone that VI could throw us that would cost them nothing. If need be, it could be capped at 100 skill up to level 50, then 150 beyond there, although I don't see why it would need to be. Foraged herbs could be a great thing for alchemy and doesn't negatively impact anyone.


Stat Potions
------------
Basically, stat potions are never going to be something that the Shaman can readily sell, no matter what the price, unless they were made so good or so cheap as to be unbalancing to the game. In my opinion, these potions exist only to raise skill. As stated above, buyback prices must be increased to make these more than just a moneysink for the fledgling alchemist. The same is true of resist stat potions.


Utility Potions
---------------
Here is the bread and butter of Alchemy. Shrink, Invis to Undead, Levitate, Enduring Breath, SoW, Regen, Damage Shield, etc. This is what the players are interested in, what they will buy if the price is right, and the area that expansion is needed in the most. We have Invis to Undead potions but not Invis potions. We should be capable of making Invis potions that are 10 charge, as carrying around a bunch of invis potions is a pain.


Mind Buff Potions
-----------------
The existing collection of mind-buff potions are not only a joke but a bad joke at that. I can't imagine anyone being willing to pay so much for so little return. They may as well not even be in the game. The thing that bugs me is that there are SO MANY viable mana-related potions that could be created that would be useful, saleable additions to our repetoire. The key is in the economics. Today, and for some time to come, almost anyone over level 20 is willing to pay their local enchanter 100pp for a shot of KEI. The niche potions need to fill is for when there is no 60 Enchanter around, and you don't want to run back to Nexus for a KEI recharge. These potions would be far more affordable and powerful than those already in the game, but by keeping them in their proper niche, basically meainging not quite as good as a real mind buff, but less expensive, we keep things in balance. Gate Potions
------------
Everyone pretty much agrees that Gate potions take too long to cast, have very limited locations available and cost too much. The OT gate Hammer and Thurgadin pottery potion have also lowered the demand for our potions significantly. It is my feeling that in addition to adding a Potion of the Moor for the Vah Shir Shamans, going to Hollowshade Moor, that there should be 2 potions to each of the locations, one of which costs the current 168pp each but has a 5 or even 3 second casting time, and the second potion being the same 30 second casting time as the current potions, with a much lower price.

This is a *great* post and pretty much sums up a lot of other ideas floating around in this forum as well as effectively tackling one of the most common complaints of the shaman community, 'Fix Alchemy.' While I agree with the majority, I have a few points I take issue with or would expect others to.

Ress potions. While I think this would be an extremely useful addition, I just don't think it's ability shaman should have. Some feel that clerics already lost alot of their day to day utility in the Incomplete Heals that just went in (not my opinion, see other threads). I think this would only further that opinion. No, clerics will NEVER be useless, but farming out their abilities to other classes is a little unfair I think. Maybe.... just MAYBE, a revive potions of some sort that didn't prohibit an exp ress later would be in order.

Shaman forage? Erm, Iksar already have this. Not to be petty, and I'm NOT trying to get into a debate on which race is the better shaman, but if this racial ability is handed out to the whole class, shouldn't they be compensated in some way?

Gate potions, while I think the revisions you've posted are necessary, I honestly think they should be cheaper to make. Why? I know a guy who 2boxes a 60 shaman and 60 warrior. He uses Thurg potions despite a high skill in alchemy because stocking up on ingedients is cheaper/easier then supplying alchemy made ones to his warrior (and the location is more appealing) and this guy has absolutely no problems generating a lot of money when he wants it.

Mana regen potions, again, I fully support your proposed revisions, but I doubt the enchanter community would go for it. While I like them depending on chanter made ingredients, I can't help but predict those vials of mana would skyrocket in price in the bazaar. Shaman being able to effectively 'cast' KEI just isn't right (tho I sure wish we could!).

Illusion potions should be illusions only, imho. Just seems a little too much otherwise. No faction, no bonus, just a toy to keep it interesting. Make em lore and/or do drop to limit usage (tho I'd love to have gnome rangers out there for sac to the loot gods). I'd still make em with no bonus, and I know folks who would buy them at 100pp or so apiece if they were tradable.

MOST importantly, which you mentioned and I fully and totally agree with, make availability the limiting factor. Stop making alchemy depend on your bank account, there's MUCH better things to spend hard earned pp on (see high level kunark spell drop rates). Make mob drops and ground spawn, or other tradeskill items, part of the process.

I'm a shaman who would very very much like to master alchemy, simply because it's part of our class identity, but at present, there's just no motivation to spend the money.

Here's hoping at least some part of our input goes in...
