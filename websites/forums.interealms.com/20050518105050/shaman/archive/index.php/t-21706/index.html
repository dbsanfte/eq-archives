Thunderwolfjr
Depends on how you want to play.

- Shaman/Ranger means Ranger tanks, not ideal.

- Shaman/Druid (which I do with my RL buddy all the time) works well enough. He can charm animals to tank and snares all, I buff pet, debuff and slow mob, add my pet, and root mob if charm breaks. I also heal him if he has taken damage and is busy re-charming. Will be easier once he gets dire charm. However, this tactic is limited to animal charming, so only good in certain zones/areas. Or you can root and rot. Have done this also, both can root, and he can snare just in case. Pile on dots, shammy LDoN magic dots and 60+ dots don't break root (as you probably know), so this works great. Lots of DPS and snared mob means time to re-root or escape. Not as fast as charm killing, but you get full exp and is less risk.

- Chanter/Ranger again either means Ranger tanks, not real good, or charm killing. Ranger handy for root/snare and dps, but lousey heals, so you have to be careful. Dire charm obviously lowers risk considerably, but does take a while to get. In the meantime charm breaks (always at the worst times) will mean high drama.

- Chanter/Druid is a great combo, two charmed pets is insane, plus heals, buffs, snare and root. Can't be beat.

If you want to charm for a living, go Chanter, but Shammy/Druid is a viable choice also.

Don't mean to rain on your Disneyland parade, but allow me to offer some perspective on this.

First off you need to get to 65. PL'ing is great and shortens the trip, but it is still a road to travel. BTW if you suck at Charming or are not comfortable with it then you just added to the journey. Then we're talking AA's.

You're at least 27 or so AA's from Project Illusion. (I have over 150 AA's and still haven't gotten this one)

Now that spell. It's level 65. It's a Rune. And it costs (on Xev) 10K+. Which admittedly is down from the 40k or so it used to be. Oh BTW, C5 and VQ also cost 40k or so. Admittedly you play on FV so the prices may be different.

You don't need to strip your Shaman clean to twink your Enchanter. AAMOF there are stories of people (Eugee) who did Enchanting work in Sebilis naked to prove it could be done. (People kept trying to give them robes).

Borrow 5k and go the following route:

1) Put all points into Stamina then Charisma. You can buff your own INT; and STA gear for an Enchanter is tough. My Enchanter has a 253 STA and it is a pain to get there.

2) Velium Fire Opal Rings.

3) HP Gear. Don't focus on 300 INT at level 1. Focus on 300 HP. I have never died due to lack of INT. (Shaddup! :)) I have died to lack of HP a lot.

4) Race. Gnome (For tinkering) or DE (Ultravision and Hide). You want hide because it allows you to break Charm when MOB gets low without using a spell slot.

Since you have a Bard friend, then your Shaman can heal the Enchanter, the Bard can pull and DS and you can NUKE. (yes Nuke). AT level 12 your nukes are as good as a Wizard. Enjoy it - because the paradigm changes quickly.

Better to have an unstripped Shaman and not need him, than need him and not have him.

Binter
Keep your shaman.

As someone who plays both a shaman and an enchanter, I can tell you that a shaman/druid combo is nearly as effective as a chanter/druid combo. You'll lose some DPS from the chanter's charmed pet, and you'll belimited to zones where you can root-rot, where there are animals, or where the shaman can effectively tank the mobs, but that's still a heck of a lot of zones. My shaman can solo just about anywhere outside tier3 PoP. A well played shaman with VP can be just as effective as a chanter at crowd control, and your druid can also crowd control pretty darn effectively, so you won't lose too much there.

If you do go chanter though, I suggest you take your time at the lower levels to learn the class, every aspect of it. Crowd control is your primary need to learn, this DOES take a bit of practice, but you also need to learn how to charm effectively and efficiently (i.e., not get your group killed). Charming is a skill that requires practice, nerves of steel, and fast reflexes to do right...but you already have everything but the practice part of it down, because shamans have the same requirements. Any enchanter who does not know how to charm is wasting over half of the class' effectiveness.

The Enchanter is one of those classes where you MUST be good at what you do before you reach the higher levels, or you'll get your entire group killed.

And before I get stepped on, yes, shamans are another of those classes. Basicly any support/healing class MUST be good, because the entire group depends on them, and one mistake by your chanter, shaman, or cleric can wipe the group.
