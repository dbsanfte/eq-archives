1) How solo friendly is the game? Do I need groups to advance my character? In EQ2 there are many locked zones that single player can never unlock without a well balanced group.
WoW is very solo friendly. All classes can solo up to level 60. They can all do so well.
To advance your character you do not need groups. To advance your character faster and gain the best possible gear, you either need to save up your money for the expensive Rare items that hit the AH, or get groups to do elite instances. Rare items are usually about 20% better than anything else comparable that you can acquire solo. Rare items are either bind on pick up from harder quests, elite bosses, or very very rare random drops. Cash drops are better off elites, and they drop higher level gear more often than what you can usually kill solo.
I have not seen any locked zones so far, except for Onyxia's lair. Then again I'm only 44 and it might just need a quest to be able to go there.

2) I heard the game is built for PvP. If I don't want PvP and pick a PvE server, am I losing a lot from the game?
If you play on a PvE server, you can play the entire game without a single bit of PvP, almost. There are a very very very small number of quests that require you to kill an NPC of the opposing factions, which will flag you for PvP for 5 minutes. You can look around for opposing people before you hit the npc for your quest, and then run away or gate out to your home city where you will be completely safe.
If you go on a PvP server, you will have more to deal with than just NPCs trying to kill you. On the plus side though, you will have much more to do once you hit level 60 than just farm mobs for cash/drops or raid. Fighting other players is entirely different than fighting against robotic npcs that have predefined combat styles.
You can at any time type /pvp to flag yourself for pvp though. And doing so will most likely get other people to attack you. So there's always that option if you want to do some PvPing.
If you play PvE only, you won't be disappointed though, for what you might miss out on playing PvP.

3) I mentioned that I don't want a game that feels like a 2nd job, ie mindless exp grinding, killing the same mobs over and over and over for hours and days etc...will WoW be any different?
Most quests in WoW are designed to be able to be completed in about 5-30 minutes, including travel time to and from the quest destination. Some are as simple as "bring this item to soandso". The longer quests are ones that involve you travelling to far off places, killing npcs in the area to get X number of item drops, then returning back to the quest npc. Those take a lot longer if you've never been there. Though those quests are a lot fewer, as they are usually the "linking quests" that are designed to get you to explore newer higher level areas that you might want to hunt in at your current level, or possibly in a few levels from your current level.
The only quests that take a lot longer are the ones that need for you to go into instance dungeons. Instances have elite mobs in them, which have about 3 times as many hps as your average creature. Some classes can kill elites of equal level, some can't, but in any case they are usually difficult fights for any class. If you are familiar with LDON from EQ, then going into an instance is about as close as you'll get to an expedition from LDON. Instances will have about 4-6 quests associated with it that you can do while inside it. You'll have 1 or 2 that involve collection and/or slaughterfests of certain npcs. You'll have to locate an item in the instance and bring it back to so and so. You'll have to locate a particular place in the instance and report back. You'll have to kill the boss npcs in the dungeon and report back. Doing an elite dungeon can take as short as 30 minutes, but will usually take closer to 1-2 hours to go through the entire thing.

4) Regard to my RL situation mentioned above, will WoW be more suited for me?
You'd probably be happy with WoW. You can solo very easily, regardless of class. You can attain equipment upgrades solo, with zero need for visiting the auction house. Most quests (like 85%) can be completed in 30 minutes or less. You can often do 2 or 3 quests simotaneously off the same npcs or in the same area before returning to town to be even more efficient (for example, one quest needs X items that drop off panthers, while another quest giver wants you to kill Y number of panthers). You can log in, go do a quest, or pick up on the progress you made previously, then camp.

One more thing that is of huge benefit to a part time player. Your offline time gives you more xp for when you play online. When you are camped out, your rest meter goes up. The longer you are offline with that particular character, the higher your rest meter goes up. While you are online killing things and are still rested, you get double xp for killing stuff. Xp from questing is unaffected, and doesn't affect your rest state. Once you've killed enough stuff to catch up to your rest state, you will once again be at normal state instead of resting, and go back to normal xp. So, in your case, if you play very seldom, you will probably be fully rested every moment of your playtime. Another thing you can do to (ab)use the rest state is to have multiple characters. When you are no longer rested on your current character, camp out at the inn (to make rest state go up fastest) and log on another rested character. Your normal character will become rested while you are playing the other characters. This way every character you play should be getting double xp bonuses from hunting. It's something that is designed specificly for part time players so they get can try to see the higher end gameplay as well, without the need for grinding xp.
