deria
Im not sure if the "run it back through the translator approach" really works. After all, the translator translates your so/so english into not-so-good (even somewhat broken) French. If you then give it the not-to-good (even broken) French as input you will get Really Bad (tm) English.

I'm not saying that the translators are great, just saying that the quality of the translation should be judged by someone who speaks the language and should not be used to source a second translation for judgement.

The phrase:

I want to go to the store soon.

becomes:

Je veux aller à la mémoire bientôt.

but when you retranslate it, becomes:

I want to go to the memory soon.

As the text becomes more corrupted, the translation degrades. And thats with a remarkably simple statement.

Wish I spoke a second language so I could really tell. :)
