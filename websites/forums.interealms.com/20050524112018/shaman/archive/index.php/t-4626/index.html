Cush
To me, the Shaman is a unique class. We share certain attributes with others, but we are not the same as those others. We are priests, of a sort, but more primitive. As such, we don't deal with mortals, but with spirits, who do our bidding / lend us aid.

If you look in most fantasy stories, that's where the shamanic responsibilities lie - not so much in healing their people, but keeping the spirits at bay, or sending the spirits after enemies. Gameplay, what role do we fill? Debuffers, buffers, damage dealers, and healers.

Notice I stuck healers last.

My alternatives:

1) Take a serious look at Torpor. At the very least, up the drop rate - take Khura's Focusing as an example. When Luclin was new, this was going for upwards of 15k. I waited, and bought it for the low, low price of 12k. Now, it goes for under 3k. Torpor, on the other hand, still goes for an insane price - easily over 50k on almost every server, and Kunark has been out how long now?

Instead of a new, bigger heal, possibly remove the group/snare restrictions from Torpor - I don't see the need for a mini-Torpor, if the drop rates are upped to a reasonable level.

2) In line with our existing role (and instead of making us into a poor cleric clone), I've raised the idea of a new buff line before.

Totem of (insert animal type) - weapon buffs, essentially. Single target buff, adds raw attack (as Avatar does, amount based on level), and a weapon proc based on the weapon type for that line.

Examples - serpents for piercing weapons, adding a poison dd proc. Bear or other large animal, for blunt weapons, adding a stun + dd. And on from there.

This would be an addition that would fit the spirit (heh) of the Shaman class, would up our raid/grouping power a bit, and help to make the class a bit more unique - instead of a cleric clone with a poor complete heal. Not that I really see it being added, as it would take a bit of work, and it really would have to span multiple levels to fit in - but it's something I'd rather see than a 2k CH.

I love the idea of mini-torpor... or even mini-Avatar.

I'd LOVE a self-only buff to jack up melee skills (double attack... /drool.) Make it like the old Rage line, where your mana keeps draining as long as you use it.

Here's another similar idea- a spell that provides a "mana rune" - as long as the spell is up we take damage from mana (at a ratio significantly worse than our healing ratio) instead of HP, or some combination of the two. Boy would this one open up the HP vs WIS debate. :)

While I'm thinking of the relationship between HP and Mana- how about a class AA skill- "HP burn"... reduces us to 1 HP and does that amount of damage to the target? :)

Improved animal charm. I'd love to to see a dire charm like ability that worked for progressively higher level mobs. The level would be a few levels under our summoned pet level and cost a ton of mana (like a pet summon) - but you'd have variety. When fighting a mob that uses fire, you could first grab a fire resistant mob as a pet.

Along the same lines, animal only tracking.

Back to the pet thing, how about a "reclaim energy" like spell... causes your pet to poof (only a summoned one, not a charmed one), and returns a percentage of the pet's remaining HP to you?

How about undispellable buffs? There are definitely circumstances where I'd pay 200 mana for an undispellable SoW at level 52. At 54 do you cast your best STA buff, or the level 39 one for double the mana that won't be dispelled? Hmmmm...

Stat combo buffs? Of course AGI+STA would be nice (as would invis+see invis), but what about one that buffs STA 20 points higher than your best STA buff, but eliminates (through stacking) the ability to have an AGI buff? +60 MR, -10 PR, -10 DR?

Here's a stupid buff idea! I don't need to eat or drink while this buff is on me. It stays on until I attack or zone. Call it "Spirit of the Bazaar". ;)

If they put DoT stacking in, might be neat to have lower level DoTs available with Lure resistability (Level 52 spell: Venom of the Badass Snake, just like the level 39 VoS but almost unresistable, and similar in mana cost to EBolt.)

Heh- here's a strange one... a spell that causes the target to go into a blind frenzy for a short period of time (3-5 ticks, maybe?), attacking anything near it. "Spirit Possession" or something. HUGE aggro on this one. Getting the resists right would be key on this one- a "Spirit Possession rotation" should be a risky proposition.

How about Mini-Malos? Make three of 'em, one for Fire/Cold, one for Magic, one for Poison/Disease (each -35 instead of Malo's -45). Each is unresistable but only lasts 3-5 ticks, and they don't stack (you only get one on a mob at a time.) This would help mid-50's raid shaman effectiveness.

Well, that's it off the top of my head. I realize some of 'em are just silly (more stat buffs- ick!) but at least they're new ideas. :)
