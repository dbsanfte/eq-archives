The same guy that gives out the harvester also offer robe, gloves and boots that about the same quality as the harverster.

Boots and gloves are no drop but the turn in items are not if I remember correctly. The clothing is all solid black in color.

Honestly, 3 - 4k dropped in the bazaar will net you far better items for less of a price. Follow the same rule of thumb as you do when equipping your shaman and look for mana and HP items (AC isn't a priority in this case). They'll go the farthest in today's EQ.

be careful here. I started out the same as you, created a necro alt for some fun.......

My necro is now level 63, 20% from the next level. I can't tell you when the last time I played my shaman was, other then to fill in a group spot if I decide to group with my necro that day, and then I only switch to him for a slow, or to Q the tank, cast a buff here and there. The rest of the time I am on the necro.

Can't say it will affect you like it did me, but this could be the retirement of your shaman if you are not careful, heh.

Played my shaman since 1999, and now before 2003 comes to an end, he is finally being put to rest. BTW, this necro is the only other character I have played. I know most of you have several characters above 60 or even at 65, but I myself could not get a single alt past level 5 in all those years. Shaman was just to fun, that is until I played a necromancer.

They should put a warning label on them or something.

For gear, I think the norm is to get your INT to 200. Or as close as you can get it to 200. After that, work on HPS/MANA gear. It is extremely easy and cheap to hit the 200 INT range from Bazaar. Really cheap. Now, trying to switch out gear to keep that 200 INT pool, yet add HPS/MANA gear, well that gets a bit more expensive, but until you reach level 50+ you won't need to worry about it. I myself kept that harvester equiped until I was like level 61 heh. And I still did great with my necro.

Also, be on the look out for an item, Pre-nurf CoS. Circlet of Shadow. These don't drop any longer, but they give the necromancer insta-click invis. Trust me in this, it is the greatest thing since sliced bread for a necro. Now, normally these can run anywhere from 50k to 250k depending on your server. Now before you go no way, I can't spend that kind of cash remember this. I said the same thing. But, I kept on the search, and after a few months, I found a necromancer that had switched servers. He had however kept his old Pre-nurfed CoS on the server I live on. I told him I was broke as could be, which was true, I had about 10k saved up at that time with my shaman. He sold me the CoS for 4k......You read right, 4k, for probably one of the single best items a necromancer can have. So always keep an eye open for this item.
