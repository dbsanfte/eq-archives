Kazim
you probably need to go into setup on both EQ installations and turn pretty much all models off and run them on low rez settings. Sounds like your computer doesn't have enough horsepower to run 2 full copies of EQ on whatever settings you're using. If that doesn't work, you probably need to upgrade one or more of: memory, cpu, video card.

I'd also make sure you've got the most recent drivers for your video card.

taraddar
I haven't 2 boxed on 1 computer personally but given your video card and memory your probably gonna have to optimzing things a bit.

Turn off all new models that you can. Go for the smallest texture sizes and anything else thats gonna eat up memory. Also go in and turn off sound. Since your trying to run 2 at once you basically need to make sure it's doing as little work as possible.
Kazim
My machine is an amd 1.7ghz, 1gig memory, radeon 9800 128mb vid. I turned most of the models off on the second account, dialed it down to a very small size (640x480 or something), and brought in the clip plane on both accounts. It runs, but not all that well (a bit laggy). I didn't do anything special in terms of third party software, just copied my EQ installation into a second folder. I'd guess that memory/video memory is what's blocking you.

Hopefully some of the 3rd party programs people have recommended, combined with reducing your video/memory intensive configuration settings as much as possible will get you going.

Oh yeah, I also have a radeon 8500 on my 2nd computer. The latest ATI drivers make that run a lot better. If you're not on the latest radeon drivers, I'd install them for sure. I didn't notice much improvement on the radeon 9800, but it sure helped the 8500, so I'd guess it'd help the earlier cards also.
