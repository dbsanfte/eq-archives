Heres the most common I see from Raiders:
"Raiders dont get as much pure-XP time as non-raiders"
Tough. Raiders have access to MUCH better loot. Raiders can make up HUGE variances in this type of ability with a couple K extra HPs and Mana, not to mention incredible foci that just arent available to the common man. If the Raid level player has the time to dedicate to this type of AA ability list IN ADDITION to his raid responsibilities, then why SHOULDNT he be "that much" better than other members of his class? Lets keep ONE THING in persepective here. Time spent in EQ is what translates into advancement, no matter what level you wish to play. If you do not want to raid, you have to spend time camping pp loot, wearable items, LDoN, etc etc. If you want Raid loot, well most guilds dont just hand that stuff out. You gotta put your hours in raiding.

Next arguement:
"The rich get richer"
They should. Thats the game. Time spent is one thing, time spent productively is another. I can sit in the Bazaar on my character and make about 1K an hour on average, just selling stuff I tend to loot/win like LDoN drops, research materials, tradeskill materials, etc. However, I am hardly advancing my character that way. Pumping my time into AA is another story though. Heck, combining the 2 is even better. Skipp the greenies for TS and cash loot drops, and focus on areas where you can get reasonabl XP towards UPGRADES and reasopnable loot/pp towards UPGRADES. What is LDoN again? Oh yeah, a DKP system for EVERYONE.

Remember this as well. The average Non-raider probably has a larger percentage of his online time available for "straight XP" compared to the average "Raider". Wouldnt this balance things out somewhat?

What else we got. Oh yeah, heres an oldie but a goodie:
"Advancement should not be tied to AA abilities"
Wake up. It already is. Period. Lets just take it one step further at this point. Lets STOP using GEAR as the ONLY ladder to a "comptitivly advanced" character, and use something that SOE actually WANTS to see, "Time In Game" as a form of advancement. No keys, no flags. Linear progression based on dedicated time spent.

Let me ask you this. If they said RIGHT NOW you could get Balance of Nihil for 30 aa, would you save the points for it? I bet the answer varies. The variance is between those who will "probably have a shot" based on their current station in the game, and those who "probably would have to wait a WHILE" based on the same thing. Herin lies the problem. If something like this were available to EVERYONE (albeit taking time to achieve) then isnt that better than the common "I cant get that focus, that 200 hp/mana, that spell" thing we see every day? And isnt it better for the Raid level guys who alraeady have access to "upper level" stuff to have to establish NEW goals they would BE FORCED to committ time too in order to advance, instead of "show up, bid, loot your Chronoburn Focus, thx"?
