>> "... They can't solo as well as almost any other class, so progression is slow.."

A bit off the subject, but I have to argue this point. From the levels 27 to around 53 or so, bards are probably the BEST ( or close ) soloing class in the game. Of course, that's a bold assertion and could be refuted based on style of play, types of gear etc. But in general, I think it holds. Lvl 27 is when bards get mana free charm. Outdoor zone + mana free charm + selos == unlimited exp. It's tricky and can be tiring, but until the lvl 27 charm stops working it is insane. The charm is not often resisted, even by low red cons. I've seen a lvl 41 bard charm kiting all 4 spectres in Oasis at once using this technique.

After they get their third Chant song, they can also do "chant kiting" where they run in a big circle casting their Chant dots. Since this does not cost mana, this can be done non-stop. The distance and the damage output is pretty good in the 30s to low 50s.

Combining these tactics can result in dramatic victories. As a non-twinked lvl 40 bard, I soloed a lvl 47 Mountain Giant Patriarch in Dreadlands. It took a loooong time... but I won. I'm not sure how many non-twinked classes could do that.

On the downside, after around lvl 51 or so it starts getting tough to solo. Charm kiting gets much riskier since the mobs hit so hard, and the higher level charm song takes mana, so one can do it for only so long. Chant kiting also doesn't work so well because the damage output does not go up as fast as mob HP.

It DOES get tiring to play a bard. After I made a bard I made a cleric. Sitting down all the time and healing once in a while was just what my hands needed. ;)

Uh, well, given the amount of time it will take to get a group together to go to the Hole, set up, and start pulling, you could have stayed in DL and made up that 25%. And what happens to our Hole group when someone has to leave? Where are you going to get reinforcements? More hassle, more downtime, less fighting. All of that makes 25% (or whatever) seem really insignificant.

Now if the ZEM was like 300% relative to DL, then that's would make it worthwhile. Even if you had to jump through all sorts of hoops to get to The Hole, just a short time fighting there would get you a lot more exp than DL. Also, with a bonus that big, people would be more likely to hang around there hoping to get a group, thereby decreasing the hassles of getting a group. I realize that bonus seems enormous - I'm not saying make it easier to level. Putting a negative ZEM on outdoor zones would be just as effective. The point is to just make the difference between indoors and outdoors very large.


To summarize: as it stands now, I think that the system encourages people to take the dull outdoor path. Unless one has a ready group, the dull path is fastest exp-wise. I would like to see that changed, but it won't change until there is an exp bonus large enough to draw people into dungeons.
