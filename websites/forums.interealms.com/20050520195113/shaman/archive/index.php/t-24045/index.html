Greetings.

I think the time has come for me to remove myself from the position of Correspondent. I find that my time spent is not producing worthwhile, or even noticeable dividends with regards to the Shaman class in EQ.

Since I will be "highly unlikely" to continue in EQ when EQ 2 is released, I think it's best if I do this now rather than later.

I had hoped to give this "experiment" six months to work. I had hoped to see progressively more response, communication and positive change as a result of this. I have given it three, and I have been very disappointed.

I am not leaving EQ, nor my Shaman, at present. I am simply taking a less visible role, in expectation of leaving completely when EQ 2 goes retail.

I'd like to thank all the folks who have encouraged me over the last three months, your thoughts are appreciated. I'd like to thank those who have disagreed with me, you forced me to constantly evaluate my perceptions to be sure they were accurate.

I hope that things change. I know, and have tried my best to communicate to Sony, what the problems facing the Shaman class are. While things may be temporarily alleviated by the current expansion, the overall problems still exist and will exist until truly addressed.

It's been a fun ride, but as the end of my ride here nears, I choose to spend it enjoying what I can and ignoring what I can't. I wish you all the best.

"developer it's painfully clear that they don't understand the game well enough"

This is where you need to change your thinking. The developers know this game better than you can imagine... that doesn't mean they can do anything about it. I mean technically *they can*, but that's not how it works in a corporation. Hiring a developer just to fix things isn't standard. They hire developers to develop new functionality, ideally. My applications aren't perfect, and I could spend almost 100% of my time going back and perfecting and adding new functionality to them, but my corporation doesn't want that. In their eyes, the application is out there, it works (for the most part), and they want their money spent towards me developing new stuff that they can put in their metrics. Corporate politics. And there is nothing you can do about it really... everything is about the bottom-line, about results... "Fixing things" isn't highly visible in quarterly metrics and statistics. New development is. Cost savings is. Etc...

It's not like you have people just sitting there waiting to fix things. Our team does not have a designated "maintenance guy" who goes around cooing with the customers and fixing problems... as much as we all would love to have a guy like that, there is no room in the budget for that. If something comes up in an existing application, the developer must then put his current new application on hold while he fixes the old one, and that doesn't make boss happy.

My point is, blaming the developers is like blaming the drywall hanger in a construction project for a building that leans to the east 3 degrees. Everyone is pointing to the wrong people. Which actually says a lot about the customer base... they recognize something wrong, but aren't informed enough to know who is actually responsible, so then you got customers who look like idiots. You need to look higher up the chain if you're unsatisfied with a product.

In order to put the "terminology" to bed . . .

I had this exact discussion not long after I became a Correspondent. Here is SOE's terminology:


Dev - Any of the people who get paid to work on EQ. Could be ANYONE . . . they even consider their CSR's (Brenlo & his crew) to be Devs.

Designer - These are people like Rytan who actually make the decisions about things related to gameplay & content design.

Artist - responsible for . . . duh. Art. Textures, models, etc.

Programmer - This is people like Grumbuk who do the coding. They don't make gameplay decisions, they are the ones who implement the gameplay decisions in code. These are the folks who fixed all the DX9 collision bugs & stuff.

Love it or lump it, those are the terms that SOE uses.
