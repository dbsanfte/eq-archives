Kianor
The usefulness of Run3 depends on whether you spend a lot of time indoors. Also, if you don't have jboots/black fur boots, it could save your life outdoors.

Other than that, it all depends on what you find fun, what your goals are in game etc.

If your guild is mostly mid 50s, doing smaller raids, levelling might help them accomplish the raids, but it might also put some distance between you and the guild. I found that level 61+ puts a big gap between you and the people at 50-55. Not that you can't group with them, but it's better to group with people about the same level.

If you want to join a raiding guild, or to group in tier 2/3 planes, then levelling is the most important thing. On the whole, levels make you more effective than AA. That's not to say that a level 60 with 200 AA isn't as effective as a 65 with none, but it's not too hard to get from 60-65, and 65 with 180AA is more effective than either. :)

Personally, I put 10-20% into AA until I got run1, then levelled to 61. I got Canni V at 61/62, then levelled to 65. Getting 65 first, then working towards Canni V would also work. I did find the extra mana useful on the way, but it wouldn't have made a lot of difference either way.

But, if you enjoy getting AA skills more than getting new levels and spells, and if you're not in guild which raids regularly, then by all means stick around and get AAs on the way. Levels make you more effective, but if you're happy waiting and doing lower level stuff rather than being level 65, that's fine. Not everyone wants to be 65 and exping in tier 3, nor should they.

On splitting AA/normal exp - it's true that if you split experience to anything other than 100% you're waiting longer to get something useful. Each AA or level will make you a little more effective.

However, I found that bleeding off a little exp either way 80-20 or 90-10 means you're mostly working towards one thing, but putting a little aside can help - it's psycological more than anything else. I still do this at level 65 when I want to rebuild my cushion in the level - I put 10% into normal experience until it's where I want it, but I'm still working mostly on AA. It doesn't make a lot of difference in the long run.

In the end, it's all down to what you want from the game. Even that is likely to change as you level, also.
