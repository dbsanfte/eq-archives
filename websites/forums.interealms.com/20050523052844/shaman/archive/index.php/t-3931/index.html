Shaman00 begins to cast a spell, and every mob in range is slowed/Maloed/dotted/rooted. IMHO, the only AE-type spells we should get are Rains and group buffs. Everything else is the domain of other casters.

What I'd like to see: diversification of our buffs, dots, and debuffs. Throw in a few of those types of spells that do things in a different way than what we've already seen and gotten used to. The fellow who suggested the Infectious Disease had a great idea, use it. Maybe some spells/skills based off our Alchemy skill level. You'd think tinkering with herbs and spices and such would grant us an insight into the way the body works, why not take greater advantage of that in combat? Maybe use Alchemy as part of a new Shaman spell quest? Combine X Alchemy items, receive spell; require a relatively high skill for success.

One idea/change I'd *really* like to see would be a pet spell that summons a pre-sowed pet. For the love of God, what IS our pet if not THE SPIRIT OF A WOLF? Always bothered me having to cast "Spirit of Wolf" on a Spirit Wolf.

Maybe start relating spell drops to areas where you'd logically think they'd drop. IE: An upgraded Disease-based DOT spell dropping in the Plane of Disease. If anyone would know how to cast a Disease spell, it'd be the inhabitants of that Plane, right?

What I'd really like to see (nothing to do with spells) would be some new blood in the mob AI. Adapt it a little to what's generally accepted as our playstyle. Look at it this way; if the player were in the mob's position, what would the player do vs. other players? He's not going to go willingly by himself against a crowd of 50 unless he's pretty damn sure he'll be able to kill them all. He's sure as hell not going to go right for the melees, he'll see the Clerics CH and kill them to prevent it from happening. I think a lot of the EQ experience is getting a little watered-down in terms of challenge, I'd like to see it spiced up in a way that doesn't involve my screen going black or mobs spawning in waves.
