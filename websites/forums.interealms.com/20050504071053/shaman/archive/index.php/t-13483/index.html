In your situation, I'd recommend SCM next. The investment is grueling to get to SCRM, but I think it is worth it.

I'd also recommend you level to 65, and not let lack of spell availability hold you back. I went from 62-65 with NO change in my spellbook.

When I'm looking to grind, I use the LFG tool, even if I'm not going to be ready for 5-10 minutes. I put what my preferences are in the block, and I'm rarely looking for long. ("Tactics, HoH, BoT preferred" is what I use.) Level snobbishness is a real factor for some pickup groups.

Oh, and I find that at odd hours many people are looking for a shaman for a Tactics group, because their existing one (like me!) is going to bed. This could be to your advantage if you network enough. :)

Shabura Naarteban
65 first. Resists are noticeable improved at 65 versus 62. You'll feel the change in BoT, especially. Feel free to live in BoT - its the best place for random Spectral drops, imo.

Then, SCM3 for your situation. I am not a wholesale fan of SCM for everyone - a shaman with Canni V, a decent mana pool, access to some form of mana regen in groups (you can find PotC/PotG/Bot9, Spiritual BST-buff-of-the-day, and/or some chanter crack in most any group situation, or pay for it easily before grouping), and at least MC3 + FT1 (I assume everyone 60+ has, or will soon get, a Solstice Earring) should be able to do most day-to-day shaman casting without running oom. Because you keep busier than I usually do, what with healing, buffing, slowing, AND ghetto-CC, you'll appreciate the mana savings.

After that, SCRM is really, really good. Its 20 AA total, and that sucks, but longer-lasting buffs mean less annoying recasts, and the effect it has on Torpor/Quiescence is simply amazing.

Although planning *past* that (31 AA more than you have now) is a long way down the road, consider Combat Agility (and its follow-up, Lightning Reflexes). Shaman get hit a lot, especially agressive ones like you have to be with your mixed roles. PoP mobs hurt. A lot. Being missed when you draw aggro not only keeps you alive, but makes you smile when you see the spam of "attacker misses you" text. ND is good, too, if you need a bigger HP buffer for Canni, etc.
