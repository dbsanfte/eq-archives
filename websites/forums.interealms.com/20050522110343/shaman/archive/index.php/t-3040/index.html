When soloing, I tend to use one of two strategies for pulling: pull with slow (when mobs are not highly resistant) or pull with maloX (when they are). When I pull with maloX, I haul ass as soon as it casts, run until I can cast again, stop, pause, cast slow. After years of doing this, I've got the timing down pretty good, usually can get the slow off before the mob gets to me. If you have troubles getting it off in time, just pull further. :)

ok, now I've got a slowed mob that's kinda pissed at me. But because it's slow, I can nearly always get my spells cast. Root is your friend, use it often. Root, back off, dot/debuff/heal/etc.

If you play toe-to-toe, drop a cripple in to reduce the damage output (and increase your damage output). If you just root/rot, don't bother with anything other than slow (for when root breaks), maloX (if resistant), root, and dots.

I solo'd a bit in the low 50's, and had fun stacking 6 dots on a mob at a time. I used the top 3 poison dots, top two disease dots, and my epic dot. I felt sorry for the mobs, but that didn't stop me.

If you can save up 8.5k plat, invest in a horse. See other threads about this, but it is the best investment you will ever make for hunting in outdoor zones.

Vurm: Yeah, that's normal. There is an especially large power difference between blue casters- some of them I can take down (at 52) without any danger, some have me Gating when they're still at half-health (/rude Sarnak Zealot)

I try to stick to melee-type mobs when soloing, and the sequence is usually VoS/Root/Ebolt/Root if needed/...JBB/Root(repeat as needed)... sometimes I'll throw on a second VoS or Ebolt on there if I have to. With a couple level 2 (cheap) focus items, VoS becomes very cheap to cast, and between the horse and cann'ing between JBB blasts, I get most of my mana back by the time the fight is over. One thing you'll notice... I don't usually slow or malxxx melee mobs. I have a decent AC, and I can take a few hits while I get them re-rooted (nice mana savings, too). Againt mobs that hit hard and fast (Some wurms in BW, for example), I will slow... but against most I don't bother. I'm sure this will change as I level up.

With casters I buff all of my resists first, get to full mana, and tend to just unload on them (pull with cancel magic to wipe their buffs/SoW, malxxx, plague, ebolt, VoS, blizzard blast), maybe slow, then run up and try to interrupt 'em with slam while they try to do anything. If I have a pet up, it makes it easier... but I don't usually find it worth it to summon and buff a pet just to take out a caster mob.

Hope this helps.
