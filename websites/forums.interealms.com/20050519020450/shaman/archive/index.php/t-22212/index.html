Found this Interview with Brad McQuaid (http://www.homelanfed.com/index.php?id=24076) rather humorous. He seemed to take a few jabs at the current market especially EQ:

HomeLAN - What gameplay features do you think are unique and important to the game?


Brad McQuaid - There are quite a few, some of which we’ve revealed and many of which we’re keeping under wraps for competitive reasons, and so that we can test them, and so that we can maintain anticipation for the game by revealing more and more information over time.



I’ll go ahead and list several, in no particular order:


A truly seamless yet still detailed world; a world that’s fun to explore and where if you can see a place, you can most likely get there.


A ‘dual sphere’ system in which you choose both an adventuring and a crafting class for your character, and where adventuring and crafting are intertwined into an interesting and compelling interdependent relationship.


A game built on a traditional MUD and MMORPG foundation, where problems seen in the past are addressed primarily by tweaking and adjusting as opposed to throwing out (e.g. no ‘instancing’ but players still doing dungeon crawls, and making travel more fun without having to resort to easy teleportation (just to name a few)).


A detailed and in-depth traditional High Fantasy world led by Art Director Keith Parkinson, who not only painted several of the first EverQuest box covers, but also many fantasy and science fiction novels as well as several of the earlier D&D module covers.


A development team (programmers, artists, and designers) with an unparalleled degree of experience in making and maintaining massively multiplayer games and expansions.


An emphasis on traveling and vehicles, in which the player will own horses and ships early on in their play experience and use those vehicles for travel, item storage, and combat.


A challenging, tough, but very rewarding game designed to entertain players for years, not days or months. We don’t think challenging and tough mean that a game has to be tedious, and we think there’s been a trend lately to appeal to more gamers by ‘dumbing down’ massively multiplayer game mechanics. This won’t be the case with Vanguard and we will provide content for casual gamers, average gamers, and the ‘hard core’ or ‘power gamer’ too.

Some claim that you must be wearing blinders to see only the nostalgia of the past and overlook the annoying time-consuming endeavors of the game before Brad left. Well, I for one feel that while some simple convienences have been implemented the vast majority of the changes have been instituted either for lack of creative talent or to cater to the insta-gratification of our "ADHD" society.
I could't agree more man, big rep bump from me. I am hopefull that the portions of "The Vision" that were just plain SUCKY have been identified and adjusted accordingly, but honeslty, time-consuming content is a MUST HAVE for any game to be succesful on a month-by-month subscriber basis. Here is a quote from a friend of mine about that subject that sums it up for me:
As much as I bitch, to myself anyway, about how much I hate XPing and the mindless grinding required to gain xp, aa, and other time-sinks, when I played other games that required less work like daoc, and AO I grew bored very quickly.
That says it all, it really does. I dislike the grind and the tedium as well, but if the reward is adequate, the grind loses its impact "after" you have achieved your objective. During, it BLOWS FROGS for sure. For a specific example, I will once again point at the Aid Grimel "Signet of the Arcane" quest. 18 aa required out of the gate for maxxing Taanin Mastery to 6 (3 per). 220 in seven tradeskills. Numerous semi-rare drops for failable combines (failed the Tailoring combine portion 3 times, thats 9 Obsidian Saps. A full group at Castle 2 pulling about 1.5 aa an hour worth of mobs is LUCKY to get 1 sap/hour). I spent about 20 hours JUST farming cash loot to fund the tradeskills (PoM mostly, Yaeths 73R for RGC is a good 30K+, and I get moderate XP while farming it). Overall, not counting the tradeskilling I did to get 8th Shawl, it probably took me 100 hours and 500K pp. But you know something? Once I put that baby on, all that bullcrap went away, and the ENORMOUS feeling of accomplishment that flowed in me made it seem worthwhile. How can you get that kind of "High" without the "Low" of certain tedious, PITA time investments? You can't.

Granted there are portions of the old EQ that just totally sucked for no reason whatsoever. But looking back, a LOT of those things were bugs. Falling off the boat. Wierd "through the wall" aggro. But there is a balance to be achieved between convienence and tedium, and I think that Vanguard will be much closer to that "place" than EQ was, or is now. For example, how about we start by cutting the amount of PoK books in HALF. Now travel isnt instantaneous, but its not MASSIVLY inconveinent either. Right now, you can get almost anywhere with a book and 1 or 2 zone-crosses. Anywhere. In Kunark, I made racial decisions based on certain things, like a DE Cleric twink so I could use the OT Hammer. Now choices like this are meaningless. EQ is basically one big race with different models, thats all.

I think its safe to say "The Vision" has changed since that fateful day I first saw the words "Welcome to Everquest" on my screen. Nothing will ever be able to replace the magic of those days, and perhaps a big part of our optimism IS the whole "rose colored glasses" concept. But if anything, that just further proves my point, that once you get PAST the tedium, if the rewards are good, there is nothing more satifying than that sense of accomplishment, and the bad parts drift away in our minds.

I dont want Diablo 2, or MTV, or 10 seconds of "fun" in exchange for short-termed enjoyment. I want a game that provides depth. And like it or not, tedious grinding content is a MUST for this to happen, it really is. WoW is probably going to be a great game, but in the end, I have the feeling it will be a shooting star like many other EQ killers that are FANTASTIC out of the box, then just lose their appeal because overall, there isnt enough "real" depth to make it worth the effort. People are already saying this about CoH.

Now don't get me wrong. I am in a place right now in EQ that is more exciting than I can remember since I first hit VT, so perhaps my personal optimism is biased. My guild just got into Time. There is a TON of freshness and fun in this place, and already I am having a ball with Tier 1. Sure, after a few times, Tier 1 will be old, thats true of ANYTHING, but let me tell ya, I have done it 3 times now and NONE of it seems old yet. Thats the way it should be, IMHO. Brad doesnt want people sitting on a boat for half an hour at a stretch for no reason other than to slow down travel. He wants each and every part of his game to be "an adventure", the type of adventure that doesnt get old too fast, either. Boredom is inevitable, but if the "Road" isnt entertaining, and the "Destination" is too easy or short-lived to achieve, where is the "Game"? Ultimately, Brads goal is to keep people playing and playing because its ENTERTAINING overall. Good and bad. Up and down. These are things that have to exist for a game to truly succeed IMHO.

Its possible I am an anomally. Perhaps there arent a ton of people who think like me, who see the "point" of a lot of the content that others feel is the worst part of the game. People who would rather play the Road. Thats fine. Games like Diablo 2 have tons and tons of folks playing it hour after hour. I put a buttload of hours into that game myself. But in the end, it was just far too easy to "get" there. I dont want to be able to hit the top levels in a month. I dont want to be able to get the very best gear without working HARD for it.

I dunno, I have rambled enough. Speaking for myself, since thats really all that matters, I think Vanguard is going to be a far more entertaining, long term game than CoH, WoW, or whatever other EQ-Killer v5.075 you want to name.
