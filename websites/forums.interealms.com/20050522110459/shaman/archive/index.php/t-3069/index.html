I think that is correct Roxanne. The bards have several haste songs, some of which are better than our haste spell, and some of which stack with our haste spell.

Melody of Ervaj (5%) and Composition of Ervaj (15%) stack with Any other haste, BUT they do not raise the haste cap.

Luclin songs -- Battle Cry of the Vah Shir is a 15%, and Warsong of the Vah Shir is 25%. These songs stack with any kind of haste, including the Ervag songs, and they will exceed the haste cap by the percentages given.

Battle Cry is level 52
Melody is 50
Composition and Warsong are both level 60.

In the non stacking haste department, Epic proc (Dance of the Blades) is 55% haste.

Non Stacking Haste Songs:

Villa's Chorus of Celerity is 45% haste -- level 54 song. This does not stack.

McVaxius's Rousing Rondo is 21% haste, but is also 41 str, 27 atk, and a 15 point damage shield (without an instrument). This is a level 57 -- Will often be sung because of the other effects.

Verses of Victory is 30% haste, 59 str, 59 agi, and 41 AC (no instrument). This is a level 50 song.

Please not that all of the haste numbers are consistant, regardless of the level of the bard. All of the other stat benefits are those enjoyed by a level 60 Warbler, with Instrument Mastery 2 cause thats the table me roommate left out on her desk. =P



You should make the call based on:

1. Your mana supply -- if you are primary healer in the group, let the bard take over all hasting duties.

2. The ferocity of the encounters -- bear in mind that bards and shaman often work together on crowd control -- mezzing, slowing, and root parking. But if your bard is mezzing, they are not singing haste.

3. What other songs the bard could be singing instead.

4. The haste items worn by melee.

We usually rely on the Epic proc, the bard sings Warsong to bump it up a bit, and works in Rondo if the Epic hasn't procced yet. If she gets tied up in crowd control, after I have everything slowed, I'll sit down, mem celerity, and deliver.

In a pick-up group situation, discussing it with the bard as you go is the best plan. Be flexible -- the answer may change based on what you expect the puller to bring back.

There is no standard rule because bards have so many songs that they have significantly more lattitude in how they play the game.
