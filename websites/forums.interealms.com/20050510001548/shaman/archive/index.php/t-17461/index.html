Poguk
Well for the first time in the history of my shaman I finally attained something that made a marked difference in the performance of my shaman. Getting levels isn't hard anymore, so it's really about spells and AAs and upper-end gear and focus items when it comes to maxin' yer shaman out. Anyone can get a shaman to 65 in a short order of time.

I'm Norrath's brokest Prophet --- I suck at getting cash. Been 65 for a few months now and I'm still missing 3 level 64 spells, let alone all of my 65 spells. I never got Torpor (been missing one research component for a month... hasn't shown up in the bazaar). I get some weird looks using Stoicism at level 65.

When I look at Magelo's on this forum, compared to my gear.... I suck! I've typically have 800 hps and 800 mana less than most other 65 shamans, so my gear flat-out sucks.

But I got heart! ;) It's gotten me this far. I dream of the day I can scribe Quiescence and Fot7 and Ferine Avatar, Blood of Saryrn, etc... and the day I get my first Ornate piece.

I've got FT4 (MC3), and a mana pool of 3600ish, both of which are rather puny. I do what I can though. I join a group, throw my group buffs on, haste on the melees, etc... and then once buffs are done, I do the typical Malosinia and Turgur's and /pet attack on incoming mobs, then nuke/rain with whatever mana I got left and refresh buffs when they drop. To keep a nice rhythm, I can usually afford to drop maybe 1 rain and 1 nuke during a fight, then spend the rest of the fight canni'ing. So... my damage contributions are minimal.

But last night I got my 25th AA and trained in Cannibalization! :) After getting comfortable with it, I soon found that I was nuking like a madman just to *clear* out some mana to do a Canni 5. And my KEI had worn off at this point! Rain after rain inter-sprinkled with a nuke here and there --- it was great! I get 30% of a mana bar when hit can5. I've got so much more mana to play around with, it's just a great feeling :) And although I've always saw the importance of having a nice amount of hitpoints... I now see why you guys go for as many hps as you can... even with Virtue I was losing 40% of my health hitting can5. Self-buffed I'm only around 2800 hps, maybe 2900. So I need to have another hp buff to feel comfortable doing it.

What a game-breaking ability :) This was the single biggest surge of power I've experienced playing my shaman. :)
