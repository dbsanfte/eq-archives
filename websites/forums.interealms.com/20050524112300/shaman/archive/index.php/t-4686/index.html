Christabel
DAoC had some interesting things clerics might like.

1) Point blank, insta cast mez for short duration. Refresh time 15 minutes. Mana cost - 0

So for instance... every 10 minutes, a cleric could hit a button, and mez everything within 5 paces of him for 10-15 seconds. It would have the same resistance check as any normal magic based spell. But it would have no cast time.

Because of the long refresh time, it would never step on chanter or bard toes for primary CC. But he could use it in emergencies to freeze pursuing mobs if he was making a dash to the zone (and DA was down). Or he could use it to freeze a couple of mobs who ganged up on a robe caster so he could cast a quick remedy.

YOu could say that it "blurred class lines." But it's just an idea. And since clerics get so much aggro, it's not a bad idea to let them have an extremely limited emergency mez.

2) Insta cast single target and group heals for X% of target hit points (ex. 50%). Refresh time 15 or 20 minutes. Mana cost - 0

This is just like the paladin's lay on hands. The draw of it is that it would have no cast time, and be uninterruptable (like the mez idea above). So if your enchanter gets 2 or 3 mobs on him and his health bar starts plummiting faster than you could possible get a remedy to him, you can just target him with the f key, and hit your insta heal macro to squeeze in a fast +600. The 15 minute refresh time would make this an emergency tool to keep in your back pocket (rather than a new 'bread and butter' heal spell).

Those are two "unique" things I'd consider giving to clerics. They might not fit with EQ as it is currently (I don't really know). But in DAoC, those exact spells were what made the pure healing classes a clear cut above the hybrid healers. The small insta heal with a semi-fast repop time would make clerics specialists in keeping robe casters alive (instead of being on the same level as shamans and druids).
