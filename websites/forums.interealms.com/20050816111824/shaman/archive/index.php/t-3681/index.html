Taladaen
Look at it this way, it's easy to put equipment on and get your wis to 200+. It's hard to get your shaman's def maxxed out, learn how to handle slowing while getting beat on, all the other things that make our class stand out, etc.

Invest the time to make your shaman into the battle-monster he can be. What happens when you die down in some dungeon and your 200 wis, paper armor shaman has to go marching down to drag your butt out? Each level, spend some time with AC gear on & let him tank to get defense increases, then put on the wis gear & just sit around.

Iynna
I am new to shaman class ... picked up a somewhat under equipped shaman when a guildie retired. At level 52, Iyanna (barb shaman) had never trained channeling or dodge and none of the specialities were above 100. Yes, that's right. At level 52 channeling was zero!!!! Now at 54 her channeling and alteration speciality (did the specialization reset quest) are now near or above 200.

I duo the shaman with a level 58 monk. As with most situations, it's all about aggro management, even more so since my monk's taunt button is broken. If the only one that gets beat on is main melee, then go with pure wis/mana to maximize the mana pool for slows and heals. This was my initial approach. But slow gets resisted, adds don't like the monk getting healed, roots break and Iyanna gets beat on. If a shaman has thin skin, canni's down some and then gets beat on, they can easily get into low life aggro and only death or root will return agro to the melee. Thus, some AC is appropriate.

I carry a lot of bagged gear and switch it out based on the situation. If i get C2 before heading into battle, I can put on all my wis(232)/mana gear as part of the buf cycle. When monky starts pulling, I switch out to my AC breastplate and other AC gear(wis about 200) so that I can take a few hits while the monk tries to regain aggro.

I don't have a JBB, but have obulus death shroud and coldain military wristguard and an FT1 earring all which helps out on mana. Monk's role is clean single pulls. Shaman slows (it's mob specific, but in KC basement I never debuf). Based on monk damage given/taken, shaman DoTs with plague or bolt or both. If a wandering mob adds, or I have to pull two to get a named, then monks pulls aggro on mob to be rooted. Shaman Dots target mob, then roots, then monk switches to primary target and moves outta range of rooted mob. All pretty typical stuff. Shaman melees some, but primarily is a caster. For me, combo AC wis/mana seems to be best.

I have only used the shaman pet a few times. (have a beastlord so somewhat familiar with pets). Being my primary concern is always controlling aggro and pets often get in the way, I'm more comfortable without a pet. If I'm at a fixed location and there are no spawns at that location, then I'm more likely to have a pet active.

BTW, Monk/shaman duo is killer. Monk gets in trouble on pull and FD's. Shaman stands and heals. Outta range, put on supple scale armband (extended rangeIII) and try again. Shaman low on mana, monk bind wounds shaman while shaman cannies. Monk has first aid3 and can bandage up to 100% health at nearly 90hp a bandaid.
