Jalelin
Why would there be scheduled stuff the 1st week of release? That's a rhetorical question btw. :P
There's "scheduled months in advanced" scheduled. This is major feature updates and large, non-critical, bug fixes.

There's "coming down in two hours" scheduled. This is usually for game breaking bugs (e.g. Bards after most of the above patches in the first three years of EQ). These patches aren't really planned or wanted, but they still involve the operations team bringing the servers down at a planned time. You try and avoid these down-times as much as you can, but accept the fact that they are going to happen (which is not the same as saying they are acceptable when they do).

Lastly there's server crashes. These aren't scheduled. You try and avoid these at all costs.

So, to answer you (rhetorical) question; there are scheduled patches during the first week because there are bugs that weren't caught in beta that can't wait for the first previously scheduled patch. If they are scheduled an hour in advance, they are still scheduled.

The fact that they are happening could be a sign that the product wasn't tested fully. If WoW doesn't have those kind of problems when it goes live, then it might say something for the relative length of it's beta program over EQ2's. If WoW has these same kind of patches week one (or worse, server crashes), I guess it may proove that perhaps SOE had the right idea (push it live and deal with it).
