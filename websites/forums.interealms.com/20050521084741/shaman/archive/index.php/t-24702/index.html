Chutoi asked ...
"How exactly are the experience penalties applied? I know they don't apply to AA, but they do apply to regular exp. However, I don't know WHEN the penalties are applied. It's easy to understand if the player goes 100% regular exp or 100% AA, but it's when the exp is split I have a question. Let me use an example: a hypothetical troll bard.

Assumptions: 20% penalty, 10,000 exp for a mob kill, 90% AA experience, 10% regular experience

1) If penalty is applied first
2000 penalty
800 reg exp
7200 AA exp

2) if the penalty is applied last
9000 reg exp
200 penalty
800 reg exp

There is an potential experience 'bleed' of 18%. Similarly, how does it work with leadership exp on? Will a racial exp penalty ever hit the leadership experience pool?

This has been bugging me for a while! Thanks very much if you can help!"

***

I asked and after a time "was delayed due to fanfaire" I recived the following answer

***

This is from Dev - Please feel free to distribute as you feel necessary.

Penalties are not applied to XP gained; they are applied to XP required. A troll warrior gets exactly the same XP that a human warrior gets for a kill. They simply require more XP to level up. The example would look like this:

10,000 XP for the NPC, 90% to AAs, 10% to normal.

Both warriors get 8,000 towards AA and 2,000 toward normal bar.

Human warrior needs 500,000 to level up. Troll warrior needs 600,000 to level up. So human sees a 2% increase in his normal level bar, but the troll only sees a 1.67% increase because he needs more XP to fill it. The AA bar grows the same for each.

These numbers are just to show how the system works; they are not the exact numbers from the game.



Ashlanne Barefoot
Sony Online Entertainment

Yes, it answers that. If you have 2 level 65 players, one of them suffering a penalty, and the other not suffering a penalty. The player with the penalty will require more exp to fill his regular exp bar, but the same amount of exp to fill his AA bar. So if both players are on full AA they will both gain the same percentage of AA exp per kill, and if they are both splitting their AA/regular exp by the same amount (90/10 like in example), then they will both gain the same percentage of AA exp per kill, but the person without the penalty will gain a higher percentage of regular exp than the person with the penalty, because it takes more exp for the person with the penalty to fill his bar.
