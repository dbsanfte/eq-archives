Ungkor
I've seen posts on this issue before... but given all I've seen debated, and the data being brought forth (http://forums.interealms.com/shaman/showthread.php?threadid=22161), does it make sense to remove dps reducing debuffs from EQ entirely?

I mean, as far as I can tell, the vast majority of class balancing issues, as well as game tuning issues, revolve around debuffs that affect mob dps. I'm at the point where I think....

-----------------

They should have just left debuffs that reduced mob dps out of the game. There isn't any way to balance classes and at the same time balance encounters by having ANY dps debuffs in the game.

Buffs that increase player dps, either through physical or mental enhancement, or debuffs that increase the damage that a mob receives, via reduced resists or reduced AC, etc.. are fine. These type of spells do not alter the mob dps and therefore allow the encounter to be tuned to an acceptable difficulty level.

At this point in the game, I'd be in complete favor of completely removing any debuffs the reduce mob dps. Namely slow, cripple, etc..

My vision of the shaman would therefore become:

Shaman prime roles:

1. Increase the damage a mob receives:
----a) Buffing the player
..........i. melee buffs: attack, str, dex, strikethrough, etc..
..........ii. caster buffs: focus type effects (mental strength, chance to crit, etc..)
----b) Weakening defense
..........i. help melee hit: reducing AC, agility, etc..
..........ii. help casters hit: reducing resists

2. Damaging the mob:
----a) Dots: Need a complete overhaul.
..........i. Aggro: Dots should be less aggro than nukes. period.
..........ii. Duration: Suggestion 1) Dots burn mana as they tick, and can run forever if you can keep mana up. Suggestion 2) Dots "splurt" backwards. 800, 500, 200, 100 etc..
----b) Rains/AE: Let this be our "specialty".
..........i. Reduce the resist rate
..........ii. Increase Variety
__________1) Reintroduce our fire line as a single target 3 wave "nuke" type rain. Highest resist rate, similar to our poison now.
__________2) Buff our poison line. Give it -50pr, increase dmg. 4 target like now.
__________3) Reintroduce disease cloud. True AE, but lower damage, however easiest to land.
----c) Nukes: Bah, let the druids have them

3. Complimentary healers:
----a) Heal over time
..........i. Largest single target heal over time of any priest.
..........ii. Group Quiessence
----b) Wave healing
..........i. AE Heal in a rain form
..........ii. Single target rain heal.
----c) Weakest direct heals of any priest.

They can take our slow, cripple, disease dots, and ice nukes and toss them away imo.

So then we'd have this for priests:

Cleric: Best fast direct healers and CH, best hp buffs

Druid: Second best direct healers and CH, best nukes, second best HP buffs

Shaman: Weakest direct heals but best HoTs to compliment either a cleric or druid, not the best direct damage but more versatile with AE, ability to stack dots on longer battle means we outdamage druids over time, third best hp buffs, but dps increasing player buffs.

Ok.. hehe. I typed a bit more than I intended. But jeez, I'm so tired of trying to "balance" the game with an obvious flaw in it. The only way that I see classes becoming more balanced, and retaining the fun diversity in the shaman line, is to remove all dps reducing debuffs from the game. It'd be a complete fix to soe's mess, but its probably way too complicated for them to pull off. So I"m not holding my breath lol.
