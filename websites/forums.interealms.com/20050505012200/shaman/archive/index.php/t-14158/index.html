Bah, I don't think it was a bug. I think the thought process was this:

"Buff spells should be level appropriate. We'll restrict them to 75% of the spell's level over 50, to leave the old functionality. Oh but wait, there's a really big grouping range once you get up high ... bigger than that. Even a level 40 can group with a 60. And single-buffing those people would be a pain, so we'll let you buff your own group with it regardless."

Now, that makes sense, right? And it does make sense unless you're popping in and out of groups to cast group buffs. But hey, it's no big deal right, because you'd have to follow the group around anyway, so whatever, there's a price being paid.

Then they came up with a 2.5+ hour buff that everyone wants.

So I don't think it's a bug -- I think that the duration of KEI broke the balance they had in mind for the restriction. And since KEI was designed much later than the 50+ buff system, it's possible nobody was around from the first decision-making process going "err, whoa. Bad idea."

BTW, I read the CR Enchanter boards regularly and they're in general highly in FAVOR of this nerf. Why? Partly due to the reasons above, that people don't learn to play without it. DAoC was a lot closer to what i'd call correct, and a lot of the reason people are so horrified that it's going -- WITH KEI, downtime is what they feel is appropriate. And I can't really argue that.
