I think you're going about it the right way Foramorm. There are many ways to do this stuff, this is the one I use.

Buff like hell (suggest Temperence from PoK, well worth it, it makes you're healing so much more efficient). I'm assuming no DS as potions aren't worth the price till you get a few more levels.

1-24 in Paludal
24-26 in Merus Seru
26-40 in Overthere (STAY till 40! Exp is nutz in the late 30's)
40- 46 in City of Mist

Technique below 44:

Shaman pulling. Hotkeys for assisting PL'ee and for targeting PL'ee.

Pull mob with Burst of Flame. (caution don't let mob hit you)
Root mob near PL'ee.
PL'ee engages Mob.
Pull next mob with Burst of Flame
Root mob near (but not on top) of PL'ee
Target PL'ee to check health heal if necessary
Assist PL'ee to check Mob's health, if 49pct or below sick dogdog
As soon as dogdog is on the mob PL'ee switches to next target.
rinse and repeat.

Trick I've found is that the PL'ee should always be fighting a mob... never idle. PL'ee should never actually kill the mob... that's dogdog's job. Shaman's job is simply to heal, keep mobs in the camp and send the dog at the right time.

44-46 is when you start grouping up with the PL'ee. Technique changes a bit then. I use CoM cause there are mobs a plenty and they just plain go down quick. DS yourself and PL'ee with potions have him pull a train and just slaughter them. Be sure to focus DPS to put em down. If you really wanna roll your dude through the low 40's find a 65 warrior that is willing to hang out in CoM for awhile. group him with PL'ee and you stand outside group and heal. It's nutz.

Keep alt away from the mob til the dogs poof, then attack. Alt will get 100% of the xp regardless of how much damage the spirit call pets did to it, provided the shaman doesn't do any damage to it.

Hail,

I PL'd a cleric recently to 46 (now almost 48 and not powering through anymore) and I honestly see no way in heck a shaman could do it anywhere near as fast or efficiently as a druid can at low-mid levels. I used a druid instead of my 400+ AA shaman. :eek: You can use a shaman to do it - and it will be very safe - but it won't be nearly as fast.

The PL'ee only needs to hit the mob ONCE using the DS method.
Priest & caster classes all have ceramic bands available at level 20. Click the band from inventory & PL'ee is done (if not resisted) so they don't even have to land a swing. Sadly, I don't know if SK's have anything similar to that which has unlimited charges. Not much of an issue really because even a 1pt barehanded punch will work.

Legacy of Bracken + Brackencoat is a 53pt DS that costs nothing but mana. The druid sits on a mount so even while getting whacked by 10 mobs she is meditating at the sitting rate. Your PL'ee pulls so initial damage is almost guaranteed. Shield of Thorns can be cast on the PL'ee for a 24pt DS to help while bringing mobs to the PL'er. Druid casts Bonds of Tunare (AE snare - no damage) to draw aggro and adds to it with heal spell aggro (chloroblast early on, Nature's Infusion later.

I'm by no means a pro at this (was my first real try at 'from scratch' PL'ing, it was more of a brute force attempt than anything else but it was effective.) but I just can't see my shaman PLing anywhere near as fast. Maybe in the 40's it would be easier, but exp went so quick (pulled Mistmoore castle upper 30's-44) it was crazy.

Shaman DS using Kilva's Skin of flame potions is expensive to maintain & even with Coldain ring click & Bramblecoat Kunark BP clicky it just can't compare to a druid in my opinion.

Finding the right spots is also a major component of it. Once you get your SK to 46 it won't matter, then he can tagalong almost anywhere. I've been duoing Innovation with my baby cleric + a 65 enchanter.

Take care,
