Tastian
Ok let's see. Aggro numbers are arrived at by using an SK + a shaman. I have another post on the board in regards to dot aggro where I was showing crit ticks and focus raising dot aggro.

Melee aggro isn't tied to damage, but is tied to the swing. The swing whether it hits or misses determines aggro. This is based upon the weapons damage and the damage bonus. A few good threads on this and this is what helps me narrow down the aggro numbers so close by using a weapon like a FS dagger that is only 12 aggro per swing.

Resists are just as much aggro as the spell. Landing a 675 base damage nuke for 0 (resist), 23, 400, and crit'n/focused for 1400 gives the same aggro. That is one of the big problems between dots/nukes atm. Even if a dot starts off being on par in terms of aggro to damage like plague once you add on ~10% aggro free damage from ID, add in aggro free damage from crits, etc well the "tick" portion of dot aggro continues to do more aggro from the extra damage then even dots like plague that were close in terms of aggro to damage ratio start to fall behind and a dot like Ebolt or VoTS just get's down right silly.

Basically I pull with shaman, get numbers for initital aggro, then re-test several times to make sure it's right (things like double attack on last round to turn mob, etc). Then I pull and use a spell, record how much aggro it takes to turn, re-run tests. All tests were done atleast 3 times, most far more. SK always turns the second they get aggro, never ripostes, both characters are the same level, fight in 3rd person to keep both about as close to the mob as possible, etc. Hope that answers everything; might of missed something hehe.

qhor
alright, your methods make sense to me.
the main points it seems you've proven through your experiments are:

1) nukes generate hate at a fixed rate of aproximately .785 points of hate per point of the nuke's base damage. this figure is completely independant of the actual damage done by the spell (resists, mitigation, foci, crits).

2) dots generate hate in two ways: a) an initial amount of hate based on the counters they add and the non-melee damage they inflict when cast (not all dots have this initial non-melee hit) and b) an amount of hate added each tick of aproximately .785 points of hate per point of actual damage the spell does. this second amount of hate varies depending on foci and crits.

it looks like you need to do some more experimenting to derive a more accurate ratio of agro per counters for disease, poison, curse, fire and cold counters. on a side note, it bears mentioning that other spells use these same counters, notably slow, the malo lines and the tashan line. i also didn't see a formula for the agro of resisted dots.

so a quick comparison of nukes vs. dots looks something like this:

-mana efficiency:

the top wizard nuke, ancient strike of chaos, does a base of 3288 points of damage for 768 mana. given the max focus (discordant fire - 45% and echo of the trusik - 25%) of 70%, this spell reaches an ideal damage per mana ratio of 5589 damage for 768 mana or 7.278 dpm.

the top necromancer dot, horror, does a base of 430 damage over 6 ticks, or 2580 damage, for 470 mana. given the max focus (discordant energy - 45% and echo of the trusik - 25%) of 70%, this spell reaches an ideal damage per mana ratio of 4386 damage for 470 mana, or 9.332 dpm.

-agro efficiency:

the top wizard nuke, ancient strike of chaos, generates .785 x 3288 (base damage) or 2581.08 points of hate. given the max 70% focus, this nuke can has an ideal damage per agro ratio of 5589.6 / 2581.08 or 2.166 dpa.

the top necro dot, horror generates an uncertain but negligible amount of agro based on the curse counters it initially adds. its agro is then calculated at an aproximate rate of .785 x the actual damage dealt by horror over the 6 ticks it runs. given the max 70% focus, this dot generates a total agro of 4386 x .785 or 3443.01 agro, plus whatever agro is generated from the curse counters. its ideal damage per agro ratio is therefore 4386 / 3443.01 or 1.274 dpa.

these numbers ignore specialization checks and mana preservation foci, as well as chances to score critical hits (it bears mentioning that this chance is substantially higher with max nuke crit AA's than with max dot crit AA's.) they also ignore dot extension foci. they also assume that the dot is given time to run its full duration and achieve maximum mana efficiency. the damage per agro number for a poison dot like blood of saryn would be substantially lower due to the huge additional agro of poison counters.

...so...

dots are generally more mana efficient than nukes, assuming they have the time to run their full durations. this assumption is highly fallible, though, especially considering the brief nature of most gates of discord group and raid situations.

nukes are hugely more agro efficient than dots, due largely to the fact that any damage added to nukes by songs, familiars, foci, etc is completely agro free.

nukes crit substantially more than dots (i don't have the numbers for this, but the max gates crit nuke aa yields a much higher crit rate than critical affliction III).

all in all, nukes are a much better means of dealing damage than dots in almost any group or raid situation.
