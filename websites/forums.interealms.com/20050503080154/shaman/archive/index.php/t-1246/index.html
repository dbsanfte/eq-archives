Warning: long anti-Sony rant ahead!

Sony's credit card billing system is seriously fubar'd.

I was having problems getting them to accept my credit card last summer. The funny thing of all of this was that I had two accounts using it, and when I went to renew one of the accounts, it wouldn't take the same credit card this time, even though the other account was and still is being billed fine on it.

I called them more than a few times trying to sort this out. Their method for testing the credit card verification system is very advanced: they log in on your account the same way that you do when you want to edit your billing information, and then they try it themselves.

Surprisingly enough, it didn't work for them either this way (although I must admit something this advanced could have been easily screwed up on my end, entering all those numbers and doing the clicking is very difficult for me). Basically, this was the end of the line. They had no other means of inputing/verifying my credit card number: either it is accepted by the system or it isn't. This clearly makes no sense because, as I pointed out, they've been billing that same card on another account for a year now.

Then I called my bank a few days later to check on another, unrelated issue, and learned an interesting tidbit. Every time Sony verifies a credit card number, they claim that they send through an authorization of $1.00 just to make sure the account exists. You aren't actually charged this money, but the bank will place a "hold" on your account for this amount. Well, much to my surprise, Sony all along had been issuing an authorization for the full amount to my card. So, since I was trying to sign up for 6 months of access, the "hold" on my account wasn't $1.00 but the full cost of the 6 months, $50-something.

My bank verified all of this information for me, and thanks to Sony's very skilled methods of trying for themselves to input my credit card information (see above), they had accumulated something like $300+ in holds on my account in the span of 2 days. Luckily I didn't bounce anything, as these holds count as a negative against my balance for 24 hours, but I was a little ticked.

They tried to blame it on my bank, and after that, I just said the hell with it, gave up, and used another credit card.
