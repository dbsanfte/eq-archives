Uman said "Druids don't deserve to heal more efficiently than Shaman. Torpor was always king." I disagree. Torpor and kraggs are much different than Tunare's Renewal. Torpor is a heal over time spell. Druids don't have one. That means that, while torpor is on a character, a shaman can also cast another heal. They supplement each other very well. The more common arguement is slow. Slow, however, is resistable, and causes the caster to get aggro. In addition to resists, there are slow immune mobs. However, one class can't be fit for all situations.

Imagine taking a group of 1 warrior, 1 wizard, 1 rogue, and 1 shaman. All four characters have very good equipment and over 100 aa points each. That's my standard group. We can clear all of juggs and trak's lair, including the reets, because a shaman plays many more roles than just a druid. Now imagine a group of the same classes, except replace the shaman with a druid. Not only would your melee be lacking haste (assuming that both of them don't have an eyepatch of plunder, which is only 20% haste), but you'd also be lacking a key element to all groups: healing power. Druids don't have enough mana regeneration to keep up against unslowed mobs all the time, even with the new heal. A shaman does. Infact, most of the time, I was above 75% mana and tabbed out reading the boards instead of playing my character. Why? Being a shaman is cake.

About shaman dots and their resists. I agree, the spells are too susceptible(sp?) to being resisted, however that's the way it always has been and, unfortunately, I don't expect it to change. However, shamans have resistance debuffs to cause other spells to be resisted less... things like root and other spells, besides dots. While a druid may be able to dot a mob to his heart's content, the wizard beside him is getting resisted crazily because a debuff isn't in. The shaman can cast sini and both spells have an advantage.
