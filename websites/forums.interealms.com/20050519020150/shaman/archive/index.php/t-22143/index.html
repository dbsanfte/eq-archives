Brohg
What's most cool to me about wizards is how calculable their damage is. Horses make meditation constant, instead of just between spells, and with Malo-x in, the only unknowns (real 'sit' time & resists) almost completely goes away!

This as opposed to melee damage, which is incredibly variable. The math behind it is very concealed in EQ. It fluxes based on player atk (which is actually an approximation of accuracy+power, summed out-of-sight of the player), mob ac (also an invisible sum of avoidance+mitigation), mob defensive skills, what buffs you're wearing, the player's ability to stay in melee contact, phases of the moon, whim of the gods, etc. Besides all that crap, melee dps over time takes a HUGE dive if there's any downtime at all, whereas casters can be seen as extending their "active time" by medding before/after a fight, and just concentrating their damage while burning mana. Not to knock melee damage in general , btw. All this crap just makes it hard to calculate.

Anyhow, back on wizards? all you have to do is add up their mana regen, and multiply out by how much damage/mana they get for their spells. In practice you'll probably beat that number, even, since from the start time to finish time, you get to go from fm to 00m, if you want to, and not just use the mana regened during the exercise.
