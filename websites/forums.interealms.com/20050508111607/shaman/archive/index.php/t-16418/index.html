As you can see, I have earned a total of 153pts in Miragul`s, and 51 in each Takish & Rujarkian. I have earned none in Mistmoore or Deepest Guk. I have spent some of those points, giving me 135 points avaliable for spending.

I can only buy items from a certain theme, costing the same as, or below the total amount I have earned there.

Taking the picture, I am currently at Ruj. I can buy the Orc's Earthen Belt (40pts) but not the Dusty Old Coin (52pts). If I wanted to I could buy the belt and the Orc-Tooth Necklace (40pts) since the total earned stat doesnt change when you spend points.

You can usually see a few items just out of reach of your current total points at a camp, to wet your appetite for what you can get next.

There are several excellent explanations floating around. This is the one that stuck in my head (from Zanithar I think):

A - When you 'win' a dungeon, you get *2* types of points - 'spendable' points and faction points.

B - Your spendable points can be spent to buy stuff at any wayfarer camp you want. They are the "Points Available" in the picture.

BUT

C - "Total Points Earned" (in the picture) are faction points. A merchant will *only* sell you something that costs less than your Total Points Earned for his camp.
Slurm
There are 2 kinds of points... think of one as faction points and another as spendable points.

They acrue at the exact same rate. (ie an adventure that gives 51 points gives 51 faction, and 51 spendable)

Spendable points can be spent anywhere.

Faction points are for the specific camp you adventured at.

so... I earn 1000 points at rujarkan hills, and 500 at deepest guk: I can spend all 1500 at either place, but the vendors in rujarkin like me more -so they will sell me 1000 point items wheras the vendors at guk will only sell me up to 500 point items. I could buy 3 500 point items at either camp.
