Your examples are:

1. Guild group of folks who have a history of playing together.

2. Same group with pick up cleric.

Apples and oranges really. Your 'core group' has found their balance on what works for you all. The 59 cleric instinctively knows when to heal the paly, what to expect normally on a pull, who to watch for quick heals, etc.

The 62 pick up cleric doesn't have this working relationship with your group. He may be overhealing thinking the paladin is going to take more damage than he may actually be taking (conservative over healing.) He may also play a shaman and "knows" shaman torpor/Q themselves after Canni-5 because that is what he does. Or his shaman friend has taught him that is what shaman do.

The real lesson here is your question about getting heals from a cleric to cover your Canni-5. Don't expect it. Don't plan on it. It is not the norm. As others have mentioned, having the cleric heal your damage from canni is much more inefficient than healing yourself. On the flip-side, having a core group where the balance has settled on the cleric being able to heal your canni damage and everything still works well is a blessing. Don't lose those folks!!

Granted, I avoid pick-up groups like the plague, so I don’t have to deal with people who don’t know what they are doing. Again, this is assuming I’m in a decent group with similar gear. In KT, mobs hit for a good amount, but there aren’t enough of them, unless you want 3 minute pulls. In PG, the mobs are so watered down and so far apart that I was in a guild group with a ranger for tank, and still had so much mana I had to nuke each mob so I wasn’t “wasting” any. They wouldn’t have been able to sub me out for a DPS class, either, because the healing power wouldn’t have been sufficient.

Anyway, I agree that ideally you want to keep the group busy enough so that the cleric isn’t going out of their way to find someone to heal, but with an experienced, decently-geared group, you’re generally not going to find something to fit that criteria.
