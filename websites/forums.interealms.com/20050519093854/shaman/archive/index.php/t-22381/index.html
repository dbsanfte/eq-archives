Torp
First and foremost, these mobs can drop Yiktu and Eril runes, Jelki comes only from KT. This doesn't need to be a debate on whether or not shamans are good in GoD, just providing a model for how a normal group without an enchanter can farm Yiktu and Eril runes!

http://www.criesofinsurrection.com/files/crucible.jpg

An evacer can be very helpful if you are low DPS. Basically you fight to the right, as can be seen on the map below, spawns are marked. The Chav PH starts down, as well as 2 of the roamers.

The spawn time is 15 minutes, give or take. This means you get about 4 chances for a named every 15 minutes. And in my experience, named are pretty common, maybe 1 Chav for ever 2 Chav PHes, and 1 Umbiki for every 6 Umbiki PHes.

The roamers are marked at the lowest point they roam to (as I have seen, not 100% sure on this) and all roam atleast up to the corner before the Chavoor the Trickster PH. To start out, pull the 2 static spawns at the front, and then the roamer might be really close. Try to pull the first 4 static (red marked, they are not red cons) mobs before doing roamers because statics spawn nice and far back and are no danger once you move up, whereas roamers have a habit of popping right on top of your group.

After pulling the 4 statics, if you have just zoned in and are clearing up for the first time, this is about when the chavoor PH will pop along with the Umbiki PHes. This leads me to believe that the roamer that is up when you zone in cannot pop Umbiki as well as the "never seen him pop here" mark on map cannot pop Umbiki.

Alright, so assuming no named, kill your way up, make sure not to move up until after the roamer that comes down to there is dead. Once you kill the high roamer, you have two spots you can pull Chavoor PH to, marked with X's. Chavoor PH is kinda a funny situation, I have been grouped with warrior who just run up and shoot at arrow at him and he comes single, I freak out when I see this, but then after a couple PHes I find out they can do this every time with no adds. Meanwhile, I group with bards/monks/SK's who try to get too fancy and it takes them a couple minutes to split the PH... Once ya get used to it a shaman can pull it with Malos. Chavoor doesn't ramp so this is especially useful on him.

Now, if you have fought up to here and killed the PHs, run down to the start again or evac, evac is nice if you are slow and repops have started.

So you have the basic farming strat down, now lets imagine a named pops.

Umbiki: http://eqbeastiary.allakhazam.com/search.shtml?id=14551

Umbiki triples 1550, rampages(single target) and has a really wussy proc that doesn't matter. Shamans can ramp tank with good gear, or any of the group melees etc. Doesn't rampage much after slow, barely rampages after cripple. Before slow hits patch heals are best, after that cleric can chain CH with shaman patching incase of spike damage with a reasonable tank. This guy drops Yiktu Rune for Balance of the Nihil. Some Robe and Hybrid Cloak that has rotted about 6 times in 10 kills for me :0.

Chavoor the Trickster: http://eqbeastiary.allakhazam.com/search.shtml?id=14588

I can't remember if this guy reall does hit 2200, but hes a truck compared to Umbiki. No rampage, shaman can pull with malos, nice to start with whirlwind/furious/weaponshield(might be overdoing it)/Nimble but it really isn't that big of a deal without it. Patch heal before and after slow, sometimes with just malos slow gets a resist, if a melee has a Jman stick have them use it, but usually slow isn't a big deal, but if you get an unlucky string, it burns time you can be patching and ideally you want slow on a few seconds after riposte disc is over at the latest. He drops Eril Rune for Daluda's Mending, some reports say he can drop Yiktu rune also, I don't believe this. Drops nice plate pants and melee earring.

Both of those guys drop BiC pieces also, I don't really keep track of those.

Good Luck getting your spells, you can do this with Clr/Shm/Tank/3DPS! Don't forget! Convince your friends to try it without an enchanter!

I've killed 25-30 nameds in Vxed and seen one (1) spell rune drop. Enraged Maternal Cragbeast in Tipt, 10+ kills, no runes, other Tipt stuff I've killed less often, never seen a rune though. If you want your first two spells, get them from the sewers. Trushar level Runes from KT are a common drop from nameds there. We'd farm Sewers1 with Cleric, Rogue (tanks usually), Monk, Shaman, sometimes adding a Ranger and another class. A farm run (clear enough to spawn named, go scout, kill/invis to nameds or quit out) takes less then an hour per run, half that if PH's pop. Far easier then Vx/Tipt, and far more common of drops. We'd get a spell rune in the Sewers about every 3-4 hours of farming, which is from my experience at least 25 times more common then runes from Vxed or Tipt.

As far as if your tank is ready for Vxed. Take him to Sewers1. If a Shaman can keep him alive on unslowables in one non-defensive, he can tank in Vxed. Even an 8500 HP tank can be iffy on stuff like Crags, They hit for 1550 max and have a pretty decent proc (want to say 700'ish off the top of my head), 5-6K "burst" damage rounds aren't too uncommon (10K 2KAC Rogue, so for a Warrior would be a bit lower). Chavoor has rounds of 6-7K on a non-defensive tank. Occasionally you get one that's more deadly then others, I've parsed two Crags who did in the neighborhood of 600DPS after slow and cripple. You don't have to be extremely uber, only the Rogue in my normal Vx group is Ele Flagged, and he's tanking so he's down to around 200 DPS. But he does have 10K HP, and our normal group is averaging 600+ DPS. If your tank has 8.5K HP, your Cleric has a 4.5K Mana pool with FT10, and your group is putting out 450DPS, it's still doable but it's gonna be awfully slow and it's doubtful you're going to be able to get to, let alone kill the nameds.
