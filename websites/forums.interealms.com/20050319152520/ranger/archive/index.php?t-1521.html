Best to know your local mechanic, not one that works at a dealership, but self employed. I would go to some of your local fixit shops and ask around.

Most of the guys who work everyday on cars and trucks can give you an idea of problems they see with different companies, models, and engine sizes.

I have a very good friend who owns his own shop, and they have access to all the updates and recalls also.

Take for instance the Ford Explorer/Bronco. They have a problem with the engine stuttering, and can't seem to figure out why.

The Chevy S10 v6s seem to need more work than the 4 bangers.

When the quad 4s came out, they had alot of problems with blown head gaskets. This was due to the block and head being two different materials, iron and aluminum. Both would heat and cool at different rates, causing the gasket to leak.

For big trucks, I personally like GM trucks. Ford has always been to generic for me on the interior, and I have always liked the chevy engine/drivetrain better than ford's.

For small trucks, the old Mazda was a great truck, I even own one still. But since the buy out from Ford, you are basically getting a Ford product with a few minor changes in appearances.

The Toyota has always had the rep of being a vehicle you could depend on for lots of years and miles. To me, their trucks just plain lack power compared to the others on the market. They are also priced a little too high imo.

Nissan is a decent truck. Not too expensive, and if taken care of, will give you pretty good wear and tear. I would again probly go with the 4cyl here. A bit better gas mileage, and the Jap makers just seem to do better with the smaller engine imo. With the advancement of technology, you won't see a huge amount of difference in performance either. Unless you want to tow something heavy, in which case, you shouldn't be buying a small truck.;)

Dodge products have always been low on the list for me. All the way back to the day they had those dang starters that would whine after you try to crank them. They might be comparable to other makers now days due to technology, but I still put them low on the list. They seem to give you less than Ford or Chevy in performance and such. I pretty much lost all faith in Dodge/Chrysler/Plymouth after the early 70s.

Jeep also made/makes a truck, not sure if they still do. One word on Jeep------parts-----expensive. To me, Jeep makes only one type of vehicle worth buying, the CJ7 models.

I long for the day when Honda makes a truck. I would like to see how that would turn out.

Best bet though, buy what you really want/like. After all, you are the one paying the bills, and if you buy something you really didn't want just because of consumer reports, you might find yourself unhappy in the long run.

happy shopping!
