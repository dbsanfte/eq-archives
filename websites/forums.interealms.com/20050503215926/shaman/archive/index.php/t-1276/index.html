Donar
It really depends, in my experience. I just got a JBB not too long ago myself, so I may not be the best person to answer you, but I'll try and maybe someone will come along and correct me and we'll both learn something. :) I am currently in RM fighting HGs to get enough plat to pay back a loan from a friend to get my JBB, so I'll use the HGs as an example. If I am fighting one HG, I usually pull with slow, then when I get close to where I want to fight, I root the mob, blast it with the JBB a few times until the root breaks. Then I send in the pet (if he hasn't joined in already), continuing to blast away at the mob. [Note: Tank for the doggie as much as possible. He doesn't like to get pounded on and if he gets beat on too much, he goes back to the land of puppy spirits.] If I am taking too much damage, I root the mob again and let the doggie take some hits while I get a few cheap shots in. Once root breaks, the mob is back on me, so the pet doesn't take too much damage. I still dot when I want a fight to end quickly, but it's not always necessary. Dotting is especially useful for adds, but if given the choice between slowing an add and dotting (depending on how much mana you have), slow the mob as it makes it easier to get the JBB blasts off and you or your pet don't take as much damage if still in melee range. The JBB is great for handling adds (at least so far). At 44, if I'd gotten an add in RM, I'd have had to run or ask for help if I'd been fighting a while already. At 45, with a JBB, I have been able to take two adds and still end the fight at roughly 60% health, 60% mana (admittedly, I was near full mana when the adds showed up). I do practice root/dotting sometimes, to work on the technique and to add some variety to my hunting strategy. You don't necessarily have to concentrate so much on that though, if your AC is high enough. Get down and dirty with the mob. I usually slow the mob, then tank/jbb/dot if needed and have my pet nip away at the mobs hindparts, using root to control adds or keep the mob from wandering off and bringing back friends. You'll find that at times, just blasting away with the JBB while tanking for the pet is enough. It's not uncommon for me to end a fight having only used the mana for slow and maybe one dot or a few roots. Percentage-wise, that takes about 10% of my mana. Still, if you're getting exp pretty quickly using your method, stick with it. This is what works for me, but there are lots of variables that will determine your "fighting style". Experiment. See how much damage you can do while taking the least amount of damage and expending the least amount of mana. There may be more efficient methods. I would really like to hear how others incorporated the JBB into their styles also.

If you want to know which is better, dotting + bracer or only bracer, there's an easy way to check.

1. Get a stopwatch.
2. Get to full mana and fully buffed
3. Pull a mob and kill it, timing how long it takes for you to kill it AND get back to full mana.
4. Average your times over a few kills to get a good average.
5. Pull a mob and kill it in a different method, timing how long it takes for you to kill it AND get back to full mana.
6. Average your times over a few kills to get a good average.
7. Compare average kill/med times. Use the faster method.
8. Go to step 5 if you feel you might have a better method.
