Update on Disappearing Items in New UI
Athelyn [8/7/2002-1:26:59 AM]  Comments[4]

Yesterday in the Developer's Corner, Absor posted some information that EQ Technical Director, Scott Hartsman, gave about the disappearing items bug in the new UI. Here's what Scott had to say:
Wanted to take a moment and follow up on at least one of the open concerns that we heard quite a bit about both during Fan Faire and beforehand. Items occasionally poof in the new UI if you loot them from a corpse too quickly.

The problem is that the new UI doesn't follow some of the same inventory handling rules as the old UI. For now, the best way to get around it is to loot containers slower, as if you were using the old UI.

Remember how the old UI would pause you between looting bags from your corpse? Unfortunately, for now, you'll have to do something similar on your own. Please pause for a few seconds (longer if you are badly lagged) between looting items, especially containers, and you shouldn't be affected by this problem any longer.

In the meanwhile, if you do not wish to take this precaution, you may always switch back to the old ui via the /NEWUI command, then logging out and restarting EverQuest.

We have a fix that will be working soon on Test (if it isn't already) and will be rolled out to the live servers in the next patch, courtesy of Messrs Wamtin and Rowyl.

Thanks both for the patience, and all of the information. We'll get this fix live just as soon as it's stable.

- Scott Hartsman (aka Gallenite)
Technical Director, EverQuest

New Bazaar and UI Information
Athelyn [8/8/2002-1:51:48 AM]  Comments[2]

From the Developer's Corner, Absor writes:
There are some new(ish) things to report about the interface, bazaar and other odd things. Some of this you may already know, but some of it you may not. I’m just going to give you a list of stuff.

---

We recently changed the way the UI looks for its files. If you load a skin that is missing pieces it will find and use those missing pieces in your default folder. What this means is that if you forget to copy a file into the folder for your new skin, the game will use the default file. It also means that if we update the interface and add new files, your existing skins should continue to work, since the UI will look for those files in the default directory.

---

/loadskin used without any parameters will give you a usage statement. But now it also gives you a list of all of the folder names it finds in your UI directory. That’s basically a list of your available skins.

---

We’re adding three new labels:

-Current Alternate Advancement points available to spend = 71
-Current experience percentage assigned to Alternate Advancement = 72
-Character Last Name = 73.

---

The issue that was causing folks to target and inspect people, open doors and all sorts of other things when using the mouse to move has been fixed.

---

I’ve had several emails asking for the ability to search for hand-to-hand weapons in the Bazaar. That ability already exists. Use “martial” on the dropdown list to find them.

---

Yes, we’re aware of the issue with tracking. Items leaving the list (like NPCs dying) causes the list to resort and the focus to jump to the top, making it very hard to use. This is fixed on Test.

---

/camp is ignoring optional parameters (like Desktop). We know about this, but it’s not a real priority item. We’ll get it when we can.

Alan

Mana Cap & Mana Enhancing Items
Athelyn [8/12/2002-2:35:17 AM]  Comments[3]

Everquest Lead Designer, Rich Waters, on the mana cap and mana enhancing items:
The mana cap has been a big topic lately, so I'd like to take a minute to address it. Mana caps have been in EverQuest since the game was released. They haven't been changed, made more restrictive, or added at a later date in order to keep casters down. The mana cap as it is now has been there since you created your character, and only recently become a topic of concern for players.

Most things in EverQuest have a point of diminishing returns - a point where adding more of a resource yields little or no improvement. This is most obvious in your basic stats, where you can easily see that after your strength score hits 255 it does you no good to put on more strength enhancing gear. These kinds of caps aren't intended to cause you distress, and in many ways they can help support a well balanced game system.

While we have an appreciation for the benefits that stat caps can bring to a system, we're also willing to look at things like this with an objective eye. The mana cap on worn items has been in since release, and hasn't come up before as an issue that players felt overly limited by. With all the attention focused on the cap in recent days, the dev team looked it over and we agree - there's no reason to place an artificial cap on mana enhancing items.

On test server now, we've changed the rule so that players get full value from all mana increasing worn items. We expect this will go live with the next patch.

Exploit Reminder
Athelyn [8/13/2002-1:47:38 AM]  Comments[6]

From the Developer's Corner, Absor posts a reminder that game exploits are still against the rules:
Ok, that sounds like a pretty obvious thing to say, and normally I wouldn’t bother. Most of you already know not to do things that seem like cheating.

But I wanted to make sure that everyone understands that we will suspend and ban accounts that we find using bugs to their advantage. We can and do catch folks doing such things, and it’s no fun for us or for them when we take away their accounts.

If you find some odd bug that allows you to do something that you normally can’t do, the best thing to do is to use /bug to report the bug and avoid doing whatever causes that bug to happen. Nobody gets in trouble for finding a bug, only for exploiting it.

Thanks for your cooperation.

Alan

Exploit Clarification
Athelyn [8/14/2002-12:10:51 AM]  Comments[5]

More from the Developer's Corner, regarding game exploits - Scott Hartsman writes:

I'm not going to repeat the things that Alan's already stated so well. 99.9% of the people who play EverQuest do so in a fair manner, and agree that there is no room for cheating as part of EverQuest.

Unfortunately, this message is addressed to the other 0.1%.

If you've "accidentally" ended up with items equipped that your race or class cannot normally equip, and/or have any items that haven't been earned in the lines of acceptable gameplay under the EULA, we strongly recommend that you log in your character before tonight's patch and unequip any mismatched items and delete any duplicates.

As of tomorrow's patch, please do not be surprised when items gained in an illicit manner and/or wrongly equipped are creatively, forcibly un-equipped.

We already have logs of who's done what, and we'd like to extend an opportunity to those who may have tried something out "just for fun" to take their actions back.

As a team, we've been congratulated quite a bit lately for many of the positive changes that we've been making to the game, and we're glad to continue to improve upon what we still believe is the most enjoyable online game out there.

However, please do not mistake our intent. The rule does stand: If you cheat to get around the system, you must be willing to accept the consequences.

Cheat in EverQuest; Bad Things Happen.

Once again, to the vast majority of people who play EverQuest in the true spirit of the game, we're sorry that we've had to take up your time with this.

We thank everyone who has submitted /bug reports and feedback here in the Dev Corner on this issue. No punitive action has been or will be taken against those who discover and report bugs, and stop taking advantage of them.

Thank you.

Scott Hartsman
Technical Director, EverQuest
Plus, Absor gives us some additional information on the upcoming patch:
As a clarification to the earlier warning regarding item exploits. By "creatively, forcibly" unequipped, we mean the following:

After the patch occurring at 3 AM PDT Wednesday August 14th, any character level 11 and over who is wearing items that cannot be legally equipped by the character, or are equipped in illegal slots, will suffer an immediate duel-style death to forcibly reset their inventory.

Characters will not lose experience, but will return to their bind.

Please make sure your characters are in legal shape before the patch.

Thank you.
