1. Someone at Verant said in one post that the Rogue's ability to sense and disarm traps is useless. If it is useless and will never be updated, why not replace then with more useful skills? Maybe Climbing, Negotiating (used on a merchant to reduce prices, based on your Charisma skill {maybe for Enchanters also}).

We've compensated for the problems with sense/disarm traps in different ways that balance the class. As much as we'd like to remove all traces of the sense/disarm trap skills, they are in the character files and therefore can't just be yanked out. "Climbing" wouldn't work out well since the world is already designed taking into account the locations that a player can or cannot reach. As for "Negotiation", the prices of items are already modified in regards to charisma.

2. I have thought of a new skill. Could there possibly be a sort of skill similar to Crit. Hit in which you target a portion of the body for upped damage and possible disability with a greater chance of missing? For example, a Warrior may have a chance to cut off a finger, making the monster's attacks a bit slower, or a Rogue poking a creature's eye(s) out, causing blindness.

We have added subsequent effects to skills. For instance, Crippling Blow has a chance to stun the opponenet. As for additional effects, we'll consider them as we have a need for additional abilities and skills.

3. With the new Expansion, there are new requirements. Because of these new requirements, I cannot play Ruins of Kunark. Does this mean I cannot even see the new weapons, spells, textures, etc.?

Most of the new graphics/textures are based upon world geometry and objects in the Kunark lands, not on mobile objects that travel between the two. As such you won't have a need to see the majority of the new art if you don't purchase Kunark. As for the mobile stuff, you should be able to see all of that without a problem.

4. Why don't monks get some sort of critical hit? Warriors get a Crit. Hit and Crippling Blow with melee and kicking (I think) , we Rogues get a Crit. Hit and Deadly Strike by throwing things, and Rangers get Crit. Hit (dunno about a Deadly Strike-ish attack for Rangers) using bows. Any plans in the future for monks in this field (Judo Chop, Groin Punch, Eye Gouge, etc.) ?

Because they are well-balanced without critical hit. They get things like the built-in haste to their unarmed combat speed instead. As for the additional skills you mentioned, there are currently no plans for any pre-50 skills for any of the classes. As for what happens post-50, people will need the expansion to find out :)

5. New classes ... any new ones planned (Witches {Warlocks for the males} or priests {possibly a pure caster form of cleric} )?

Nothing planned at present.

6. Speaking of priests and religions, is there any possibility to convert religions? Maybe a place to which you make a pilgrimage and give an offering to the deity you wish to worship. Just a thought.

Factions are quite complicated and don't readily support a deity-change. As such we don't really have any plans to make this an option.

7. I have been wondering something for a while now. When I have my dark elf form on, why do Dark Elves still hate me even though I have never even touched their faction? I think it is because I worship Bristlebane.

Illusions can only do so much to combat any negative faction that you have. It's the only way we could make them both useful and balanced.

If it is, how can they tell? I don't know about other players, but I don't run about shouting, "Praise Bristlebane! Innoruuk sucks!" and other things of the sort.

In a way, you do. When you choose your deity, you are determining the way your character ran his or her life prior to you gaining control over it (from an RP standpoint). From a gameplay standpoint, this is another case where we had to make a choice, and that choice was that religion would be both useful and balanced. In order to balance usefulness, there must be a downside associated with it. As such, NPCs can see through your religion.

So far, I have seen pictures of Cazic-Thule, Innoruuk, Tunare, Solusek Ro, Rallos Zek, and Mithaniel Marr. Any plans to have other gods make a guest appearance?

Take a look at our server list. Anyone on there is libel to show up at any time.

8. Hair ... any guys with long hair on the way (doesn't really matter, just curious)

No. Hair is part of the character model, and long hair was only built on to the female models. It is for all intents and purposes an extra appendage.

I'm an Officer in a growing guild, and it is my duty to maintain an oft-changing roster. There are many frustrations in this, and I've got some ideas for making it easier.

1) Add a /who guild command, which will allow guildmembers to generate a list of guildmembers who are currently online. Currently, I have to do a '/who all justice' (My guild is the Justice Keepers) and this only lists those who are online and not anonymous, and it doesn't always seem to get everyone. If this is a privacy issue, then maybe only allow Officers to use the command.

2) We need some way for Officers to list all characters registered in a guild, whether online or off. As Keeper of the People for my guild, I hate loose ends. No matter how tight the guild, you always have players that cancel an account, delete a character, or leave a guild as the drift away. Not everyone notifies the rest of the guild, which leaves me with a huge roster and no idea if some of these characters even exist anymore.

3) A way to /guildremove a player if that plaer is offline. Sometimes a character has a problem in a guild, and tempers flare. The player may need to be removed from the guild, or may have an alt-character that needs removing. Unfortunately, not all players are considerate enough to remove themselves, making it necessary for an officer to add the names to their friends list and constantly look for them to pop online before they can be removed. If there was a way we could issue the command while they're offline, and have them removed as soon as they log on, it would make my job that much easier.

4) We need /guildstatus to work across zones. Frankly, there's got to be a better way so see if an anonymous player is in your guild than to /guildremove.

Anyway, those are just some Advanced Guild Management tools I'd be very interested in seeing soon. I certainly enjoy helping organize and run a guild, but the easier it is on me, the more time I have to enjoy the game itself!

Now that we have that out of the way...

John Smedley on Anti-Cheating measures
joe [4/5/2000-3:56:20 PM]  Comments[23]

A reader sent this email from John Smedley our way, thanks Joe!

Ok. We put the poll in, and with roughly 15,000 people articipating the poll came up with 83% of the people being fine with us running the check for cheating. DESPITE THIS POLL we have decided that it's the wrong thing to do. Enough people have convinced us that it's chipping away a little too much at people's privacy EVEN if they do consent for us to implement this policy.

Therefore, the change to the EULA will read as follows:

Solely for the purpose of patching and updating the Game, you hereby grant us permission to (i) upload Game file information from the Everquest directory and (ii) download Game files to you.

Now, before anyone wonders exactly what this is, let me explain. Technically speaking we probably should have had this language in there from day one for you to consent us to even download new game files to you in the first place. We apologize for not realizing that we should have gotten this consent, but live and learn.

We can admit when we make mistakes, and I believe this is a case where we owe an apology to our Playerbase. In our haste to try and thwart people from damaging the game we went overboard.

There will be absolutely no scanning of anyones computer for any reason other than the normal patching process (which won't do any sort of checking on what you have running).

Regards,

John Smedley
President and CEO
Verant Interactive, Inc.

House Ya'Ler is pleased to announce that we will be hosting a Tournament of Champions on Saturday, April 8th, 2000, at 3pm EST in the Arena zone on the Povar server.

This will be a TEAMS event, with an entry fee of 250pp per person, with a split for first, second and third place teams to be 60%, 30%, and 10%. Many people will show up late and/or individually, and thus teams are allowed to be formed after the event has begun, but NOT after the first round is over. We are still waiting to hear a response from the Server GM as to if he will be able to come and witness this event, and even offer to be an impartial referee.

Rules are as follows:

-Teams will pit themselves against each other, one at a time.
-Teams will have a MAXIMUM of six (6) people each.
-The last team left standing will win that round, and move on to the next.
-No potions, pumice stones, or clickable items (other than manastones, journeyman boots, and masks of the deceiver) will be allowed.
-No pets.
-Once you are chosen to enter the Arena, you will have two minutes to finish buffing/medding before the games begin.
-You will be warned that you are on-deck for the next battle, so use this time to buff.
-PRIOR to battle and OUTSIDE the fighting area, you ARE allowed to receive buffs from anyone.
-DURING the battle, ANY tactic may be used except those noted above.
-Outside interference by registered combatants will result in disqualification of your entire team. Interference can include casting any spell on a combatant, or using ooc, shout, or auction in excess.
-Anyone entering the Arena while a contest is being fought, or anyone assisting any of the combatants, will be attacked and killed. Contestants will be allowed to enter when ASKED in order to destroy the interloper(s).
-You may enter more than once, on more than one team, but each entry requires payment of the entry fee.
-Teams will NOT be allowed to change members once they have registered. Members can be added if the team is less than six (6), but a team that loses a member cannot be replaced.
-All combatants will bind themselves OUTSIDE the actual fighting area before the fight.
-Once you die, you may NOT reenter the Arena, and will ONLY do so to drag your corpse out of the fighting area, AFTER the fight is done and a winner declared. NO LOOTING IN THE FIGHTING AREA.
-No special consideration will be shown to anyone who is Linkdead. It is a risk we all must take, and the show must go on. -The entry fee is nonrefundable. All proceeds are going to the winners.

In the spirit of fair play, we ask that you not let someone die through a DOT or allow them to bleed to death, as they WILL lose experience if this happens. This event is to vent anger, make (or most likely lose) money, and above all, have fun. We know that there are some of you out there that just want to enter this to kill your rivals, and we'll try to accommodate being able to fight against them. No promises however! Hopefully, this event will run without a hitch, and will indeed become a regular event on Povar. For more information, email houseyaler@aol.com, visit our website at http://members.xoom.com/eqveritas, or speak to Velglarn Ya'Ler in-game.

Scanning my tasklist for hack programs is not that big of a deal and if it gets rid of the hackers anyway, I say go for it. IMO it is not an invasion of privacy to do this. I give out more information, personal information, everytime I use my credit card at the store

Unfortunately it is a case where paranoia ended up winning out. I think that we could definitely have done a better job explaining what it was we were doing, and that would have lead to a bit more buy-in. Instead, some people decided to make up reports that we were scanning directory trees (false), internet files (false), internet history (false), cookies (false), and email (false), and unfortunately many people believed them.

The general paranoia resulted from the assumption that we (meaning: our servers) were actively collecting information from your system. This simply wasn't the case. The client simply would examine a small subset of information on your system, none of it containing information personally identifiable to a third party, and only send it to our server in the event that you were "running" an illegal program at the same time you ran EQ. We had absolutely no interest in what was installed on your system, only what you were running when you connected to ours.

I think privacy is important as well, but I don't really care about what a piece of client software is doing on my system. I only care when that piece of client software is transmitting information from my system to an outside source. In this case, the only time any data transmission was to take place was when something bad was found by the client. There was to be no server-side analysis of raw data. I'm sure that most people would agree that we do have a right to insure that our software license is being complied with.

In any case, I guess it's water under the bridge now. I'll blame Hollywood for all of the misunderstandings.

-Gordon
