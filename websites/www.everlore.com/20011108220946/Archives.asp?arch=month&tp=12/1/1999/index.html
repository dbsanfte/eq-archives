Test Server News
[12/1/1999-4:07:00 PM]  Comments[0]

Latest news from the Test Server regarding Druids:

December 1st, 7:00am
--------------------

To dissolve any illusion that Druids were "nerfed" of their Tracking skill, we updated the Test server on Monday with a patch, which resulted in the loss of the Druid's Tracking skill. Though this only remained up for approximately 1 hour, it did result in the loss of the Tracking skill icon - This is NOT permanent. Early next week, a revision will be put up which will replace the Tracking icon. Understand that this ONLY occurred on the Test server and will not be moved to the live servers.

- The EverQuest Team

 

New Patch Message
[12/1/1999-4:09:01 PM]  Comments[0]

Here is the new Patch Message:

December 1st, 7:00am
--------------------

The following changes and enhancements have been introduced with the latest patch:

*Monk Enhancements*

The Feign Death changes which were mentioned on November 16th are now live.

*Spell Enhancements*

Tishan (Enchanter) and Malise (Magician/Shaman) spells now stack.

The Pet bug (where if you have a pet, certain monsters can attack its owner anywhere in the zone) has been fixed.

Alenia`s Disenchanting Melody (Bard) should now work

Screaming Terror (Necromancer) has changed. It is no longer a "Run Away" Fear spell - instead the targeted creature will cower in terror until it takes damage (similar to the Enchanter's Mesmerize spell). The Environment limit (Dungeons only) has been removed.

Pact of Shadow (Necromancer) now has a 12 second recast time

Summon Waterstone (Magician) has been added to the game.

- The EverQuest Team

Brad's Post on Usenet
[12/2/1999-2:51:31 PM]  Comments[0]

Everquest Producer, Brad McQuaid has responded to a rather large post on alt.games.everquest concerning various things about Everquest:

Brad,

After playing EQ almost since its public debut, I have seen your responses to several posts and have read your annoucements. However you seem to 'skirt' around some very key issues. Please take the time to answer these questions.

I don't intend to 'skirt' issues, but I do often purposely fail to reply to issues that I've addressed before and/or are addressed by other players as well if not better than I could have addressed them :)

#1 - Why is there patch after patch dealing with supposed 'game/class balance' issues any very few dealing with game engine bugs? There are game bugs out there that have been there since I purchased the game last April (i.e. Zoning bugs, group bugs where people can't invite or disband w/o logging off and back on, MOB pathing bugs).

I assure you we continue to fix probably 100 times as many bugs than we implement negative 'nerfs'.

#2 - In reference to question #1, why are the vast majority of class balance patches negative in their effect and why is there no discussion of these patches before they come out?

The vast majority of class balance patches as well as patches in general are not negative (if you really want me to post a list of enhancements we've made to the game since release, I can or someone else can). I'm not sure what you mean by no discussion... just about everything related to EQ is discussed both internally and all over the internet.

You have stated that you have a vison for EQ but the fact is that you have released your vision to the world and you're having people pay you for it. As such, you have created a community of players and I think as a community we should have some say in changes to the game.

Sure, and you do. We read newsgroups and message boards all of the time and have received great feedback and ideas, and have also been made aware of problems in the game and areas where we've made mistakes. I think we have a great track record of both listening to our customers and making a big effort to communicate with our customers. Is the process perfect? Heck no, but it's good, and it's getting better.

I think the huge success of EverQuest is indicative of both the fact that we made the game a lot of people wanted, and that we continue to maintain and update a game that a lot of people want.

I.E. The Feign Death changes: I do not have a monk but the numerous posts by monk players that I have read lead me to believe (can that many people be wrong?) that the unannouced changes (meaning no one saw them coming) adversly affected the monk class and that there was nothing wrong with Feign Death to begin with. Such sweeping changes should be brought to the EQ community at large. This should not be done via poll but rather an 'email discussion'.

I don't think an 'email discussion' with 150,000 people is practical. I do feel polls can be very valuable, but not always -- it is our job to safeguard the game as a whole and often players will look at an issue only from their perspective (and they should -- this isn't necessarily a criticism).

You make these changes based on what you would like to see done but seem to forget that you and your staff are not the only players of EQ out there.

Of course we aren't, but we are also responsible for the short and long term overall health of the game.

I believe that you would have a *much* happier player base if you involved us in some way in the decision making process (and i'm not talking about polls, I mean in some way where we know we *are* effecting the outcome). It starts to ruin the enjoyment of the game if large effect changes are made simply because of a disgruntled vocal minority or because "verant just thinks it should be this way"

I hate to break it to you, but the message boards and newsgroups are the epitome of a vocal minority :)

The "Verant just thinks it should be this way" almost implies we are making arbitrary decisions for the heck of it, and I assure you that this couldn't be farther from the truth. We have tuning meetings in which controversial issues are discussed and debated in detail, and hard decisions are often made, and never lightly.

#3 - Several times I have seen patches added without comments in the patch text file. Can we have it where *all* patches small or large are commented? I understand that if new items or quests are added you may not want to advertise what/where they are for roleplaying reasons but simply saying 'added new items/quests'would be nice so we're not wondering is something now gone or added or did they fix a bug, etc.

I agree -- we need to do a better job at communicating changes via the patch messages, even if we do have to be vague as per your example. You have my commitment that we will improve in this area.

#4 - When are more quests going to be added and when are the broken ones going to be fixed?

All the time. There are always new quests being added and we fix broken quests whenever we are made aware of them. I'd venture to say the number of quests in the game now relative to when we released has probably increased by 25%.

I feel that it detracts from the enjoyment of the game when you take real time to run your character from one placeto another and back again only to find that there is no 'end result'.

I agree.

Also, there are some quests out there that seem to yield squat in relation to the time/level of difficulty it takes to complete the quest. Can this be fixed as well?

They are -- many if not most of the quests that people felt yielded too little of a reward, whether it was in terms of items and/or experience, have been 'fixed'.

#5 - Events. Several questions here. I have been on when some events have occured and read about others and they all seem to start around 11pm or later eastern time. For those people that have to work or go to school the next morning or are just not nite owls, this time frame is not acceptable. Can more events be schedule for early evening eastern time?

We can try, but the larger events require many GMs and Guides and therefore often have to be scheduled around when a large body of these people can be online at the same time.

When there are events, again the ones I have seen and read about, they all appear to be geared to high level characters. Are there plans to have events where low or mid level characters have a chance to survive and 'win'?

I don't think that's entirely accurate -- there have been and continue to be quests that are geared toward all sorts of level ranges, and given that the majority of our player base is approaching what I'd call mid-level, most quests and events should be focussed in that area.

--

---------------------------------------------
Brad McQuaid
Producer, EverQuest www.everquest.com
Vice President, Verant Interactive Inc.
---------------------------------------------
