How to you record text in EQ in a LOGGED.TXT File?

How to you record text in EQ in a LOGGED.TXT File?

The /log command will toggle text logging to the EQLOG.TXT file in your EQ directory. It defaults to *off* each time you log on to EQ, so if you wish to log everything, you'll need to turn it on each time you log on.

Skill increases during Dueling?

Maybe this has been answered before but why can't you get skill increase while Dueling another player?

Skills are meant to be increased by fighting creatures that actually intend to kill you, thus imparting *risk* in exchange for the *reward* of a skill increase. Allowing people to skill up while dueling would allow someone to neglect a particular weapons skill all the way up to the high levels, then build it up from nothing without risk.

-Gordon

Rallos Zek: The Locetalon War
Emerld Val'Sharess [6/4/2000-11:56:39 AM]  Comments[1]

Lord Sahvo Weiss, Duke of Saronthal and leader of the Council of Aeton, gripped the reins of his white steed and, placing a foot into a stirrup, hefted his heavily armored bulk onto the horse. He adjusted the pack behind the saddle and, waving his page over, accepted the long pole atop which blazed his emblem in white and gold. Speaking in a booming voice, he shouted, "Open the gates!"

As the massive wooden gates began to part, he turned to his wife and son who approached from the side.

Emma Weiss, his wife of seventeen years, grabbed his hand. "Be careful, my love," she admonished and then smiled. "I know you will be victorious once more." Sahvo leaned down to steal a kiss from her and then placed his hand on the head of his son, who looked concerned.

"Be strong, my son. Have faith in Mithaniel Marr that good shall overcome." Straightening in his saddle, he added. "The day I fight for a cause that is not just is the day I shall fall, and not before."

Without another word, he urged his mount forward into the morning sunlight outside his keep. Arrayed on the hill before his home was a legion of his guard, sunlight flashing off the polished steel of helmets and swords, each man's shield bearing the emblem of Saronthal in white and gold. At the sight of their duke on his steed, they raised their swords as one and cheered.

His steed reared at the sudden onslaught of noise, and he patted its neck gently to calm the aggressive animal. He raised an arm and quiet fell.

His voice carried on the wind as he shouted, "Today we march for Freeport to join the armies there for the battle to come. In one week, we shall shed blood over the Desert of Northern Ro, the blood of any whom would stand in defiance of justice and honor! We fight to recover a vital artifact which will bring us one step closer to the Straylight Gem, once step closer to harnessing that power for truth, and not evil!"

He pitched the end of his banner into the dirt so that it stood without his help, the banner snapping in the breeze. With his hand, he pulled his sword from its scabbard and raised it high above his head.

"In one week's time, we shall all march into battle, but we do not march alone! Mithaniel Marr will be there with you, at your side, the power of his might on your blade and his truth in your heart! Believe in what is right and we cannot fail! I am proud to stand beside you in battle and I salute you as the best warriors in the realm!"

The cheers from his men rose as he resheathed his sword. Grabbing the banner from the dirt, his horse broke into a gallop and took him to the head of the regiment. "To Freeport we march!" he said and they began the journey.

Verant, please change how cleric ress works.. or remove it.

Ressing is an unbelievably valuable tool to have in Everquest. Probably more than anything else it is an ability which singlehandedly can save people more time and reduce their frustration than any other spell or skill I can think of. I love it when I get it, and I'm sure everyone else feels the same way. But I still believe that, as it stands, cleric ressurection spells have to be changed drastically removed.

The first reason: Everquest is a game that is played 24/7. The lowest amount of people I ever see on most servers is about 500, and it usually happens all at the same time (Early in the EST AM). This means that people who want to have the insurance of a ress are almost forced to play at only times around the 'peak' (the majority of the day, actually), which in many cases is simply impossible for people due to time constraints. Let me make that clear: It is MANY TIMES due to their not being able to play at any other time, and not 'their choice' and 'a calculated risk' that people take, so please don't feed me that line. In the early AM PST, the amount of ress-capable clerics is minimal (at least, on the servers I play on, particularly Povar), and the odds of getting one to help you is extremely low compared to at the peak. They're spread too thin.

[Snip]

Bloodlord,

Thank you for taking the time to write up your opinion. Though I understand the issues that you are bringing to light, we wouldn't be interested in removing resurrection or diluting it by adding it to a few other classes. Two classes have the ability to do high-xp componentless resurrections now, giving an advantage to those people who group with or make friends with those classes.

The game is meant to give an advantage to those who form relationships and/or fight in groups. The ability to get a resurrection when you'd like one is one of those advantages.

If there is a shortage of clerics and paladins in the early hours of the morning, it should only be a matter of time before some people move in with those classes to fill the gap. Clerics are one class that nearly everyone would agree is necessary for high-level groups, so the ability to go places that you couldn't normally go without a cleric will draw people to play them.

I do wonder why you would suggest that it be removed. There is no disadvantage to it being in game, only an advantage for when you are able to obtain a resurrection. My suggestion would be to start looking at resurrection as a benefit rather than an entitlement. Perhaps that way you could not expect resurrections, and be pleased when you are able to obtain one. There's nothing to gain by removing it.

-Gordon

Oo boy Verants workin Mages up yet again !!

I've only one comment for this thread so I'll keep it brief.

It is not our goal to make every class the same. One class getting an ability or spell doesn't entitle everyone else to that ability as well. Even when we give that ability to a bunch of other classes and not do one or two, it does not entitle everyone else to the ability.

Wizards and enchanters remain the only casting classes unable to heal themselves. Clerics are the only pure casters that can't cast invisibility or levitation. Developer discussion -- Myschyf's Roundtable
Catalyst [6/4/2000-7:53:04 PM]  Comments[0]

A recent chat was put up called Myschyf's Roundtable where a discussion was conducted with some of the biggest players in the MMORPG market right now including Brad McQuaid, Geoff Zatkin and Raph Koster, plus many others. This is a very interested (but long) read, especially if you're interested in more of the inner workings as far as gaming goes. Here's a snip from the page.

Myschyf Why is fiction in gaming important? Why do we want to do this? Its content heavy and not cost-effective (so far) and a pain in the tukus, so why? Brad, as fate would have it we are going in semi-alphabetical order and you happen to be next. Don't you just love popping in and being thrown right into the fire?

Aradune Storylines are important to set up an immersive world -- they give it depth. Storyline in an MMORPG should be history you can interact with. The problem, of course, is MMORPGs are supposed to keep people's attention for years, not months, like single player games and since it takes perhaps 6 months to write a book and a week to read a book you always have the problem with players consuming content. They can be voracious, can't they? So, so far, MMORPGs have relied on quests/storylines/etc. as a secondary mechanic, and have used hack-n-slash, be it PvP or PvE, as a primary mechanic.
