My enchanter is a son of Innoruuk and although it has been an inconvenienc from time to time it can be gotten around. My advice is to watch your faction carefully. Try to kill the occassional orc pawn/gnoll when it is reasonable to do so. Also, if you are going to do the Stein quest, DO NOT KILL THE FISH YOURSELF. Either make a mule, or have a friend do it or trick a newbie. Whichever suits your ethical standards. Alternatively, you *could* try to alliance the woman in the temple of life in Qeynos and then do the blessed oil quest a few times then kill the fish. But then you will need to rerun the oil quest quite a few times to rgain faction.
Bottom line: your character would have some things easier had you chosen agnostic, but the difficulties are not insurmountable. Good Luck!

No, you are not totally screwed.

I follow Innoruuk, and I leveled from 6 to 12 in Blackburrow. The Guards in Qeynos quickly went from scowling to glaring to glowering, and eventually to indifferent. I got my first Stein at level 8 and I performed the quest numerous times since then. I was forced to kill the fish as often as 4 times to get a fish body for the quest, and that hurt my faction some. Now, in DE form, the guards scowl at me. In human form and with benevolence, the guards glower, the temple of Life people glare, and the merchants are all amiable. The jewel merchants anyway. Those are the only ones I care about. When people tell you you need to get a mule or a friend to kill the fish, they are dragging you down to their own cowardly level. Do your OWN dirty work! Get that blood on your hands! You can't call yourself evil if you don't. I spent a lot of time in blackburrow to be able to do that, but I was getting xp the entire time, so it was worth it. Now I go on little expeditions there, spend a few hours casting clarity and augmentation on newbies and casting my lowest level nuke on everything that moves. Maybe after doing this for a while, the guards will be more kindly disposed to me again.

Of course, I am looking at things from my own point of view. Maybe you are scared to go into Oggok because you are a worshipper of Tunare. I think that you should be able to get by in most areas with your allience spell and the appropriate illusion. The only time I have had trouble is in Firiona Vie. No illusion or faction changing spell would help me. All guards were scowling, and all the pilgrams saw thru invis. If you are evil, Firiona Vie is a very dangerous place.
