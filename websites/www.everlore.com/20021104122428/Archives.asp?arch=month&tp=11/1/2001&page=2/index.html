Guilds on Antonius Bayle
Korenth [11/13/2001-2:37:30 PM]  Comments[1]

Yesterday, Absor added this message to the Developers Corner on the offical Everquest boards about the necessary guild wipe to the Antonius Bayle server. Here's the details:

In our initial enthusiasm to help get things rolling on this new server, we made a mistake with guilds.

Basically, we started creating guilds before we removed all the guild associations from the characters. It tends to cause all sorts of trouble when we add someone to a guild when they are already associated with a guild (even if that guild doesn't really exist).

So we had to wipe all the guilds from Anotnius Bayle and start over.

We will be creating guilds again on Monday the 19th.

IMPORTANT: If you were in a guild before this wipe, we need you to make certain that you zone at least once before we recreate your guild. This should clear the guild name from above your head if you still have one. And it is a good idea to do it even if you don't have a guild name over your head, just in case.

We're very sorry about the confusion and the delay.

Alan

 

DirectX 8 Moves to Test Server
Korenth [11/13/2001-2:45:08 PM]  Comments[3]

The test server will soon be updated with DirectX 8 files. Check out the developers Corner for the details:

Some time about the middle of next week we plan to put the files for DX8 onto the Test server for you all to download if you'd like to help us test it out.

What we really need here is for people with all sorts of configurations and hardware and such to try this out and let us know if there are any problems.

Please try to make sure that you have updated drivers for both your sound and video cards, and that you are indeed running DirectX 8 before you test out the new files. See this post for some suggestions about how to upgrade to DX8.

If you encounter a problem (assuming you've done all the stuff above), here's what you can do to help us fix the problem:

1) go into your EverQuest directory and delete your existing dbg.txt (this file can get really big, and having a fresh one helps us a lot).
2) go back into the game and see if you can recreate the problem. If you can't, then we need to consider it a fluke.
3) if the problem repeats, then get the following information:
3a) your dbug.txt file (in your EverQuest directory)
3b) your dxdiag information (just go to Start, Run and type dxdiag.exe and tell it to Save all information – send us the resulting file)
3c) a detailed explanation of what happened that caused the problem
4) send all that information to us at Luclinbugs@station.sony.com. Please use the subject: DX8 Problem.

We appreciate any help you can give us with this upgrade. We really don't expect there to be too many issues, but we'd like to catch any of them that we can sooner rather than later. We just can't test on enough systems internally to prevent them all.

Thanks in advance for your help.

Alan

Small GM Event in North Ro - an Unseen Imp
Dedarius [11/19/2001-12:45:20 PM]  Comments[5]

November 17, 2001

As my story begins, I was hunting in Oasis of Marr and got killed by some annoying aggro, bringing me back to West Freeport. I was making my way across the lands back to Oasis and right as I zoned I saw a shout of someone complaining about bathing in the water or something. As I zoned my eyes widened, realizing that the one who shouted had both their first and last named displayed in their shout (meaning a GM event, silly).

I quickly zoned back with great distaste for my lack of armor, hoping that there wasn't going to be some kind of battle - which there wasn't. There was an ogre with no armor sitting down about 1/3 in the water, just along the shoreline and no more than a few meters south of Frankel the Pirate. His name was Harshuin Bargs if I remember it correctly. I immediately turned on the roleplaying in hopes that I'd be one of the first and most interesting people there, so that maybe I would become more eligible for any loot afterwards.

As I inquired him, he informed the others and I that there was an Unseen Imp which had stolen his armor and the Imp taunted the ogre with a few actual emotes. Someone there was kind enough to hand out see invis, but it made no difference as no one ever saw the imp. Harshuin shouted once or twice that the Imp was too weak to carry his heavy armor, and the imp shouted an emote ('*does whatever*' format) of himself dropping a piece of armor. I was running around as most of us were expecting to find the imp and kill it, and to my delight saw and picked up a dropped piece of large bronze (vambracers), which had screwed up weight to prevent fakes. They were just as any other item dropped by a PC, a little sack on the ground. The ogre obviously rejected anyone that made an attempt to give him any different form of armor. I came to him and gave him his vambracer, and in exchange he gave me a pearl and 30pp. The Imp continued to drop weird large bronze, say... maybe 1 piece every minute or two. Unfortunately, my lack of stamina then forced me to quickly poke my head into Oasis and get my corpse. I ran back to nro, and apparently nothing out of the ordinary had happened during the brief amount of time I left.

The Imp continued to drop pieces, until all that was left was the ogre's "shirt" as he called it. At some point around now he stood up and moved out of the water wearing all the pieces he had at the moment. A lot of people were roleplaying in shouts and such, calling the Imp a coward, saying they were going to make him pay, etc. They, not unlike myself, were probably expecting the Unseen Imp to appear at one point so we could all smash him to pieces. I ran around hoping to find the breastplate, and lucky me I did =) which I think is pretty sweet since there were only maybe 8 pieces total in the event.

I came over to him and joyfully presented to him the final piece of armor, his breastplate, and recieved as a reward 30pp and a Black Pearl. He then got up and looked around a little. He invited me to join a group which got me really excited, and I thought that I was about to become solely involved in something very cool. He disbanded the group after about half a second, and the invitation was probably a mistake =( doh! He then ducked and moved up to me, saying "not bad for a little gnomie" and I /chuckled with a swell of gnomish pride, /grin. After a few seconds he un-ducked and told everyone he had to go now. He then waved goodbye to everyone. It was pretty funny when the ogre started to run away, because everyone obviously crowded up and followed directly behind him - including myself - as everyone was naturally hoping for the event to continue. He just vanished shortly and that was the end of it, though.

Lots of fun overall, I'm not sure what it was like to others but I ended up with the highest end of the loot there was, meager though it may be. Just a very small event but I was really happy that I had the opportunity to participate, even get the ogre to give a good word out about gnomes =). I inquired others who gave him a piece of his armor, and apparently they all got 30pp and a pearl, although I got 30pp and a black pearl at the end for the breastplate. In short: pretty neat event, good fun.

Dedarius
Gnome Necromancer < Echoes of Eternity >
Follower of Bertoxxulous
Mithanael Marr Server

GM Event - Lanys T'Vyll
Disregarded [11/20/2001-11:22:44 AM]  Comments[7]

Yesterday, while leveling up my Wood Elf Bard in the Greater Faydark, I hear a shout from Ol'Pappy saying something about the Crushbone Orcs cluttering his yard. 

Being mostly inexperienced people in the zone, nobody knew that it meant a GM event, except me. So I turn on Selos and run on down to orc hill. I immediately see a giant gnome wearing banded armor and wielding a cane (fishing pole). He starts rambling about his yard being trashed and that it will never be the same. Being the only on on the hill at the time, I offer to help clean it up. After a few dead orcs, some more people come looking for experience on the hill. They immediately notice Ol'Pappy and hail him repeatedly, all the while him saying, "I dont know what you people are talking about, there isnt any rain!" or something to that effect. He repeats his problem to the new comers and they all set to work killing the orcs. After about half of an hour, he starts handing out some loot to everyone whos been here longest. I am joined by a High Elven Enchanter at this time (sorry I can't remember your name). When the newbies start /ooc ing about the GM event, the high levels come on down and start buffing PC's and killing orcs.

Heres where it starts getting bad... A gnomish rogue by the name of Neeps, in his foolishness, shouts that Ol'Pappy isn't really a GM, but an NPC (which he figured by doing a /who and seeing that there isn't an Ol'Pappy in zone). So he comes down and starts poking the poor old gnome. He gets a few hits and backstabs in (Neeps is only level 22), and, much to everyone's dismay, he despawns saying that the young people always have to pick on the old guy.

All in all, it was a pretty fun event. The loot that Ol'Pappy handed out was:
Blackened Iron Medallion (to my partner)
Polished Bone Bracer (to me)
Dragoon Dirk (a level 9 wood elven ranger)
and some PP (didn't catch the names of who got this)
Ol'Pappy was in the process of handing out items to the orc slayers when Neeps starting attacking him, so alot of people missed out on their loot. There might have been more, but the people who did get stuff /ooc'd what they got.
