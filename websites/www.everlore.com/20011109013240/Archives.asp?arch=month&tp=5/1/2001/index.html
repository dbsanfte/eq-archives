Eqq Timer is a timing program written with Everquest in mind. Eqq Timer does not intercept nor interfere in any way with the Everquest datastream. See the documentation for more information. This is the first public release and includes the following exciting features:

-Time any number of events (spells, spawns, etc.)
-Plays user-defined sound when event has 1 minute left and when event expires
-Color coded so you can tell which events are near expired
-Configurable auto-restart and require-confirmation
-Written in JAVA so it works on nearly every platform out there (linux,mac,windows,etc.)

I wrote this for fun, and because I needed a tool like this. I am releasing it to the Everquest Community so that I can get suggestions for improvement and so everyone can have the power to time whatever they want =) Go to the Maniacal Legions Website and click on the downloads link to download the app. Please mail all suggestions, comments, etc. to: eqqtimer@maniacallegions.com

GM Event on the Nameless: Mezmo Lil'thumbs in North Ro
Talaen [5/3/2001-8:47:19 AM]  Comments[5]

Last night, the gnome Mezmo Lil'thumbs appeared at the Wizard spires in the Northern Desert of Ro, asking the adventurers in the area to bring him desert tarantula chitins for his research.

He was offering a platinum piece for large ones, and five gold pieces for small ones.

Because of the recent events involving the Plague Bringer and Firiona Vie, suspicious adventurers gathered around the wizard spires and questioned him about his intentions. He said that he was trying to "help the world" with his research, and that his machine, when finished, would be a great asset to be used for good. Despite the mistrust, he was quite friendly and kept up a sense of humor.

Eventually, he had enough chitins, and teleported back to his lab to complete his research. Shortly after this, a Mechanoid appeared near the Wizard Spires and began attacking people gathered there. When it attacked Nikc, we jumped in to destroy it. Bizahnt, Nikc, and I destroyed the thing, and recovered some broken parts from it, but nothing that seemed out of the ordinary.

A few minutes later, Mezmo reappeared at the Dervish camp, upset that his machine had been destroyed. We told him that it had attacked us, and he claimed that it had malfunctioned. He asked for more chitins, which he recieved, and he said that he was sure that he'd get the next one right. He then teleported out again.

No more mechanoids appeared, so I can only assume that he is being more careful. Whether this relates to any of the other happenings lately, I can't say.

/ooc Nikc, Bizahnt, Tsukune and myself from Serenity Within went to North Ro to check out rumors of something going on. The zone was rather empty, and the Mechanoid happened to appear right on top of Nikc, so we had to kill it pretty quickly. I'm not sure what would have happened if it hadn't attacked him. It conned blue to a level 34, as did Mezmo, and dropped some gears and sprockets. It also feigned death once during the fight.

Murder of Tallena Kieras
replay promisekeeper [5/3/2001-4:37:05 PM]  Comments[7]

As I traveled through the Lesser-Greater Faydark pass yesterday afternoon, I came across an emotional Koada’Dal by the name of Tallena Kieras. She pleaded to passerby’s to hear her out, but none paid attention and scurried away. I asked if there was something I could help with and she then proceeded to tell me the story of her estranged husband, Falthin Vess. Recently, Falthin started becoming irrational and violent, culminating in the abandonment of his family. She last heard that he was spending time in Dagnors Cauldron and wanted us to talk to him to find out why he left his wife and children. She would be waiting for our return. Obliging her request, I gathered up some willing folks and began our journey – this wife beater was going to pay.

Eventually, we made our way to Dagnor’s Cauldron and found Falthin sitting under a tree at the bottom of the pass. We began questioning him, such as why he was here, where did he come from, that people who cared were looking for him, and so fourth. Falthin avoided our questions while trying to find out who sent our band to him, but we promised Tallena we would not reveal her identity.

There were a few strange things about Falthin that we noticed but ignored until it was too late, such as the Dark Reaver he wielded, something no respectable Koada’Dal would ever use. He was very short in stature, looking like a Koada’Dal but having the height of a Teir’Dal. He accused us of being spies and referred to himself in the plural, ‘We’.

Frustrated that we would not reveal the person who sent us, and our increasingly prying questions, Falthin ran for the lake. We ran after and stopped him, and started maiming him when Tallena appeared. Unbeknownst to us, Tallena had been hiding further up the Cauldron. She ran down and pleaded us not to kill him, so we released him. While the group stood between the two, they exchanged some quick words and in the next second Falthin cast a spell that we were unprepared for and killed Tallena. As we began cutting him up and shortly before he died, he squirmed past us, bent over, and took a deep breath over Tallena’s corpse. The necklace that he wore glowed a sickly green during this time.

While we were still in shock at what had just happened, another Koada’Dal by the name of Vaelian Vess approached. He said he was the brother of Falthin, and asked for an explanation so we gave it to him. Vaelian was calm and looked normal except he also referred to himself in the plural and also made some strange comments that we did not pay enough attention to.

He asked for the necklace of his brother, which was an important family heirloom and would ‘clean our brother’s soul’. We asked what he would do with Tallena, if he worshipped Tunare, and why he kept talking about wanting to enter the Plane of the Growth but he avoided replying. First hesitating to give Vaelian the necklace, we later succumbed to his pressuring and gave it to him – it is likely he would’ve taken the necklace off the corpse if we had not.

Vaelian yanked the necklace towards him then resurrected his brother. To our surprise, Falthin was a Dark Elf! Vaelian also revealed his true form at this time. Appalled and angered, the ten or so adventurers immediately attacked the brothers. Falthin and Vaelian killed one adventurer and injured many more before shouting their thanks to Innoruuk and disappearing. Defeated, we wept for the dead and tended to the injured. The clouds above us opened up for a brief momen, with rays shining down to Tallena and then sweeping her away. At a loss for what to do, we waited a few more hours before going on our way as night was coming early this day.

- Replay Promisekeeper, Crusader

Major PvP Server Changes Announced for Next Patch
Ksian [5/4/2001-5:23:58 PM]  Comments[21]

Absor making some pre-patch announcements dealing with some big changes for the PvP servers:

When we patch next, the following changes will be implemented on the PvP servers:

Consider: /Consider will only have three results when used to consider a Player Character. The character will show as 'green' if he is below the range that allows you to attack him. The character will show as 'white/black' if he is within your ability to attack. And the character will show as 'red' if he is above your range.

Sanctuary Levels: Characters below level six will not be able to engage in PvP combat, unless they take part in a Guild War, duel or use the Arena.

De-Leveling: Characters that lose levels will only retain the skills of a character two levels higher. This means that if a character of level 10 somehow loses 5 levels, that character will only have skills that would be available to a 7th level character. This change will apply to all servers (it has to, code-wise). So the two level buffer is there to make certain that people that lose levels during normal gameplay will be minimally affected (hopefully not affected at all), while those that purposefully lose levels with the intent of causing other players grief will find themselves at no great advantage.

You can view the entire thread here. Please note that Absor does specify that these changes regarding "consider" and "sanctuary levels" are due to only take place on the PvP servers, whereas the changes due to de-leveling will apply Gamewide.
