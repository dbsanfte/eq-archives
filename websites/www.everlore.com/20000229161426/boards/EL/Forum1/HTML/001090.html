Currently the person or group that gets credit for a kill is the one who deals the most damage. This in turn allows high damage dealing classes to KS from others easily. I think the method of counting only the damage dealt to a creature is ignoring half of the fight, the damage absorbed is ignored.

A simple solution to this would be to add up damage dealt to a mob and damage absorbed from a mob when determining ownership of a kill. Actual damage dealt to you would not be a perfect method though, as some characters rely on dodging hits altogether or high AC or magic resistances to limit damage, and those people should not be penalized for that.

Each mob should have an average damage number. That would be equal to the average damage per hit it is supposed to do vs an average character of appropriate level. Each time a mob swings at you or casts on you (hit or miss, big hit or little one), that number would be added to your running total of damage for that fight. At the end who ever has the most total amount of damage dealt plus damage that should have been absorbed gets the kill.

There could be limits on how damage absorbed could be factored in. One method would be damage absorbed acts as a multiplier to damage dealt, ie if you take all the hits your damage total is doubled, if you take 75 percent of the hits your damage is multiplied by 1.75, etc.

If something like this was used, it would require someone to deal 67 percent of the damage to get credit for a kill without taking a hit. It would also mean people could not abuse the system by letting stuff almost kill them and call for help, as in Anyka’s example.

I also like Drakthirteen’s idea about having higher level characters damage count for less and less the greener the mob is to them.

KSing exists because it is very easy and works almost all the time. Combine these two ideas and you have balanced the game enough so that it makes KSing something less than a sure bet.
