Exactly what is... balance?

Whenever a class debate (flame) comes up Verant offers the same response. "All classes are currently balanced." What exactly does that mean?

Balance is about there being a compelling reason to choose one class over another aside from the desire to roleplay a particular type of character. It really isn't about relative power compared to another class, nor would we claim that that relative power is meant to be fixed from levels 1 to 60.

Offensive power is only one equation in determining "balance". Other factors are usefulness to a group, and special skills or abilities. For instance, though a wizard's teleporting capability does not at all affect their offensive efficiency, it does contribute to other factors of "balance". A bard gives up much offensive prowess in exchange for being a very desirable member of a group.

By "compelling reason", we mean that one class does not invalidate another. For instance, the monk can do more offensive damage at a give level than any warrior. Now, if they had the hitpoints of a warrior, could wear their armor, and carry as much weight, then there would be no compelling reason to play a warrior other than roleplay. The monks weakness opens the door for another class to be viable.

Now any time I speak about "compelling reasons", posts on the board follow along the lines of "Just what is the 'compelling reason' to play a [insert class here]." All I can say in all of these cases is that "compelling" is in the eyes of the beholder, and I would encourage everyone to find that out on their own. Our opinions might differ from yours, but we believe that there are compelling reasons to play every class in the game, which is why we consider them balanced.

-Gordon

Abashi's Legit Canada @home comments. Will Verant do the same???

When we can prove the troubles are on Verant's side...can we do the same and expect a response?

Of course, but the nice (and bad thing) about running a destination service where 25-60K people are connecting at once is that when we have a problem, we know it because we lose a good number of people at one time. We have alarms set up internally that polls the number of people connected and lets us know if a number of people drop all at one time. Aside from the zone server, where we could lose only a small number of people, any other error that we have control over would affect thousands of people.

Source services (such as ISPs) don't have that advantage (and disadvantage). ISP XYZ might only have a few people connecting to the EverQuest servers, so when they have a problem with the route to us, they only have that small number of people who call in.

-Gordon

Mana regen Nerf? - Abashi Question

Not to sound like I am complaining, but did the mana-regeneration formula somehow get lowered ? I only say this .. I spent 12 hours online on Saturday .. my mana used to regen to three bubbles in around 4 minutes and to full in 7-7.5 ( with a wisdom of 181 ) .. on saturday however it seemed that I regained my mana at a lowered pace .. getting 3 bubbles in 8 minutes and back to full in about 11 or 12 .. was the formula adjusted or am I just hallucinating from spending to much time in Kunark?

There has been no intentional change, however due to the number of people reporting it I'm not ready to dismiss it just yet. However, I highly suspect that the observations are due to a number of things, not related to the actual rate. For instance:

1. Perception: Mana above level 50 continues to increase at a rate much like it was before, however the mana regen rate does not increase on the same curve, giving the perception that it takes longer to med, even though you are still getting the same amount of mana back per tick. This is something that people also notice and claim as they level up or gain +INT/WIS/MANA items. They increase the amount of mana that they have without increasing the regen rate in a linear manner.

2. Watched Pot: The addition of Kunark has caused people to spread out more. The net result of this is that the ratio of people to targets has improved. Where a group used to be at full mana the next time it was their "turn" to kill a particular spawn, they are now staring at their mana bars while a surplus of critters walk by that they could be killing.

3. Placebo Effect: The "Placebo effect" also causes the "Watched Pot". Someone, perhaps due to one of the reasons mentioned above, perceives that the mana regen rate has changed, and comments on it. This causes other people to become sensitive to the issue, causing them to pay close attention to their mana bars, giving the impression that it is taking longer to regen.

4. Of course there is always the good old fashioned "bug", which shouldn't take too long for us to figure out as soon as I can corner the programmers I'm looking into it this afternoon.

-Gordon

Class Envy Posts.

That's over half of the posts on the first page that basically consist of "My class isn't balanced, isn't powerful enough, isn't desired in groups, can't do something some other class can." or something along those lines. The other 18 posts are perhaps issues that we'd like to answer, but people constantly bumping the class-envy posts keeps pushing them down. That isn't fair to the people who have issues that we can and are willing to address or do something about.

I'm very sorry if we haven't been able to give everyone the answer that they are looking for in regards to their class, but being as clear as possible, notwithstanding current planned and announced enhancements, we consider all classes to be essentially well-balanced at this time. This means that we feel that there is a compelling reason to play each class beyond the desire to roleplay. It does not mean that every class is equivalent in offensive power, desirability to a group, or has some unique special skill or ability, just that there are tangible reasons someone might choose to play your class.

I don't wish to get involved in the unending recursive arguments that surround class-envy posts any longer (e.g. Explain how we are balanced, what is the compelling reason to play us, etc). This is all meant to be part of the discovery process in the game. Part of the process where everyone finds out what character class suits them. We cant tell you what you should like about your class, and if there is no other way to convince to you like your class other than to start handing out enhancements, then we may be at an impasse.

If you wish to keep discussing these issues on our boards, its perfectly fine. Well read them and we may respond to some issues raised from time to time providing that the content is respectful and the answer would be of benefit to players as a whole, but we wont feel compelled to answer class-envy posts regardless how many people comment in them. Well close the threads when they hit between 300-400 posts, and if the number of threads reach spam proportions (like the 13 rogue posts on the first page), well start closing them before that.

It is not at all our intention to suppress anyones ability to discuss these issues, but we want to be sure that people with issues that we can address, and that wed like to address, have priority on the boards.

Thank you for understanding, and I do intend to follow up on this thread today if content allows.

Gameplay posts from Gordon Wrinn
Catalyst [5/2/2000-11:03:54 AM]  Comments[5]

Monks: Human vs Iksar---Please respond Abashi

Up to this point, the only differences between characters of different races but same classes have been racial differences. Never has a class been different for one race than it is for another--to me this seems logical and straightforward, and I see no reason to change it.

Previously, with only one class capable of becoming a monk, balancing the monk class on the basis of race was not difficult. However, the inclusion of the Iksar, with many different racial attributes, has made this a bit harder.

Infravision and regen make life much easier for the young Iksar, which leaves us with a need to validate humans as a monk class. In addition, monks are the only race that get an AC bonus based upon their race. This leaves them already getting one just by virtue of being a monk, and now another because they are also an Iksar.

In this case, we decided that in addition to including the expected XP and faction issues, we've also made it so that Iksar monks will not be as "good" at *being* monks as humans. It's not by a large extreme, but human monks will always be better at doing what monks do: dealing damage.

-Gordon
