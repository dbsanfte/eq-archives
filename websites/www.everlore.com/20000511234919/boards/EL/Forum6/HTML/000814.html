Well it also depends a lot on what you want to do with your druid.

If you want to solo (kite) a lot from 10 to your mid 30s I would suggest recreating at once. Mana is extremely important for a soloing druid and you will often end up missing the mana for that one last blast that would end the fight easily.

And dont expect to have above 200 wisdom (witch is the maximum number you need) in your 40s with a base wisdom of 90 like the poster above suggests. I strongly suspect that his level 51 vicar had something to do with that

I havent spend a lot of time finding wisdom items lately and I will just now start to go for plane gear but at level 49 my wood elf druid is "only" in her 180s wisdom and I think my base was 105 (I didnt know either).

If you at some time (probably no earlier than in your 40s) end up with 175 wisdom and want to get to 200 wisdom (witch you would already have had if you had created the "perfect" druid from the start) the difference will be +wisdom items worth an insane amount of pp (My guess is more than 50,000 pp on my server if people will even sell those items you need). From my current wisdom it costs me about 2K to just get 2 extra wisdom and it gets worse item for item. Alternatively you have to do a lot of plane, dragon and rare drop camping which you can best do if you are in a powerfull guild.

If on the other hand you plan to always group your maximum mana and thus your wisdom will be of little importance. In higher level groups the mana regen rate and not the total mana tends to be the limiting factor. And a 200 wis druid and a 150 wisdom druid both regain the same amount of mana in lets say 3 min between each pull. This is the reason why I now feel pretty relaxed about being level 49 without 200 wisdom. (Of course I am not saying that it dosnt matter at all. Only that you rarely wait for full mana before each pull anyway.)

The choise is yours.

But remember. The time it takes you to start over and get to level 10 again is no more than the time it will take you to get 2 bubbles of xp in level 45

I don't know if many others share my opinion, so I am sorry if I offend anyone with this one. I am just throwing in my 2cp to help any potential druids, and don't want to start a heated argument.
So, in my own opinion, if you are going to start a druid, or are young enough to start over, do yourself a huge favor and DO NOT be a half elf. They have the worst starting wisdom, 70. I have a woodelf, and my starting wisdom is 115 after my points, 90 before. Humans are a little bit better for druids, with a wisdom of 85, but I believe that wood elves and halflings make the best druids. Halflings start out with 90 wisdom base and even a little more stamina. Also, woodelves and halflings get infravision (and yes, I am aware that half-elves get nightvision), so that is another reason to choose them over humans. So, do yourself a favor and go with either a woodelf or a halfling. I hope I helped, good luck.
