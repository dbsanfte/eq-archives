Post-hell death penalties: bug or design?

It is well known that there is double-exp loss associated with post-hell levels (31, 36, 41, 46). I suspect there is a bug somewhere.

Experience loss is calculated as a percentage of total experience, not the experience required for a current level. The number of bubbles that are lost really doesn't matter. This is just the way that the math works out. You don't lose a higher percentage of total experience, it just happens to be a higher percentage of experience required for the current level.

-Gordon

Multiple Issues

Hello all,

There are a few issues that are on the boards in multiple threads, so I thought I'd address them in one post:

-TZ/VZ Moves-

We'll be opening the /movelog command on TZ and VZ this Friday and leaving it open through Monday for move mid next week between those two servers. Specific's will be posted in the patch message tomorrow morning. If you can point out any issues that you think should be covered in the patch, please follow up in this thread.

In regards to possible moves or looting changes for RZ, none are planed or likely. RZ was created at a different time than the two other servers, and has maintained its ruleset since release. Though we tried out the loot idea on TZ, our desire is to keep Rallos as it is at present.

-Shrink Potion-

We'll be giving the shrink potion (created via alchemy) the ability to shrink the user anywhere in the near future. It will be announced in the patch message when it goes in. I can't say for certain whether this will be tomorrow or whether it will be held off until the next item patch at the moment.

-Spell Drop Rates-

We'll be increasing them, but do want to maintain a rate at which they are still desired drops, and not loot that you just destroy because it's taking up space in your backpack.

-Alchemy Prices-

We'll be adjusting these, making alchemy less expensive. It won't be in tomorrow's patch, but probably in the one after that.

-Poison-

Still looking good for an end-of-june implementation.

-Clipping Plane-

Several months ago some people complained that they were no longer able to adjust the clipping plane out as far as they had been. We've gone through all of the zone headers and put the "Maximum Clip" out as far as the data export would allow. This has been live for a while and we've been unable to find any other problems.

-Gordon

New Patch Message
Xanthus [6/6/2000-12:45:18 PM]  Comments[14]

Here is the latest patch message for today:

June 6th, 8:00am
-----------------

*Patch Day*

Greetings! The live servers have been updated to reflect the newest data on the Test Server:

*General*

- Some new spells are available for the hybrid classes. Check your Kunark spell vendors.
- Found and corrected a problem causing people to fall to their deaths upon zoning. The majority of these issues should no longer occur.
- Corrected outstanding issues with several quests, including the Brain Bite, Shackle of Rock, and the Barnal Flamehands quest.
- Increased treasure in many Kunark zones including The Emerald Jungle, Sebilis, and the Frontier Mountains.
- Upon review we've increased the drop-rate of level 51+ spells in Kunark. They should be more plentiful now.
- Updated pathing information in many zones, making it easier for pets and other NPCs to get around the zones.

*Items*

- A problem with the spell effect for the Luminary Two-Handed Sword. Current owners of this weapon will need to contact a GM to trade their sword out for a working one.
- The Gunthak Harpoon is now rentable. It will remain in your possession when you leave the game.
- A couple pieces of Blood Ember armor have been reduced in weight.
- Reduced the weight on several Monk weapons found in Kunark.

*PvP Teams Servers (VZ/TZ) and Illusions*

Due to an outstanding bug that allowed a player to permanently mesmerize or stun opposing team members when coupled with an illusion, illusions will now automatically cancel when the person wearing the illusion casts an offensive spell upon any player-character. Players should keep this in mind before attacking with spells in an area where their faction standing is less than desirable.

Illusions are defined as any spell that makes a character look different than they actually are. This includes all racial illusions, as well as other illusions such as Treeform, Wolfform, and the Call of Bones series.

*Risk and Reward*

The concept of risk and reward is very important to the game of EverQuest. Ideally, situations that provide a greater reward should also provide substantial risk. Over the past several weeks, we've been identifying areas in game where risk and reward are at a disparity, and making necessary adjustments.

In most cases, those adjustments are in favor of increasing the reward for a specific action. For example, we recently increased the experience awarded in many Kunark zones to offset the sometimes-considerable risk associated with adventuring there. We also do this quite frequently by increasing the reward associated with a quest by enhancing the item, as we did last week with the Burning Rapier quest.

However, in some cases there are instances where reward is far in excess of the risk involved, and we will be correcting these issues like those above as we come across them. For example, there are many cases where a solitary and stationary outdoor creature has a "fast spawn", yielding the reward of fast experience-gain without the commensurate risk.

Though it is not our policy to announce changes to individual NPCs, we did want to let you will see risk/reward disparities corrected over the next several weeks.

*PvP Teams (VZ/TZ) Character Transfers*

As many people playing upon the PvP-Teams servers are aware, Vallon Zek and Tallon Zek have different rules regarding looting rights from PvP combat. Vallon Zek allows the victor to loot all coin plus one item from the fallen, whereas Tallon Zek allows only coin-loot with no right to loot items.

Due to the fact that the looting rules were changed on Tallon Zek post-production, we've decided to allow characters on both servers a one-time character-move. Registration for the move will open at 6:00pm PDT on Friday, June 9th, 2000, on both servers, and will be open through 12:00pm PDT on Monday, June 12th, 2000. You can register for the move by executing the "/movelog yes" command during the registration window. There is no limit to the number of people that we will allow to move between Tallon and Vallon Zek, so there is no reason to rush to enter the command.

Please remember that once you register for the move, your decision can not be rescinded. The actual character-move will take place sometime next week, at a time to be announced later. Please keep in mind that corpses are not moved in a character-move, so we'd recommend against leaving unclaimed corpses around after Monday of next week if you've asked to be moved.

Questions about the move process will be addressed in the Gameplay Message Forum available via www.everquest.com.

- The EverQuest Team

Player classes are not balanced in terms of PvP offensive power. What I mean by this is that we didn't throw each class in a pit and hold one on one battles in some effort to create equality. Classes are balanced primarily for Player versus Environment (PvE).

All remaining clicks towards the right (suppose to be 100%) do NOTHING.

This might be the misunderstanding that we are experiencing. The clip plane slider is not an indication of a percentage, with the far right being 100% or infinity. The clipping plane slider represents a number of "feet", and that number of feet is constant from zone to zone.

In other words, if the slider goes from zero to 2000 feet, and you are in a zone with a min-clip of 500, and a max-clip of 1000, the left 25% of the slider will do nothing, nor will the right 50%. It's very possible that you can get into some zones where the slider does nothing, for instance, if min and max clip were set to the same value. Other zones may show a difference in the clipping for the entire range of the slider.

The reason that the slider does not represnt a percentage of max is that the user would have to keep adjusting it for every zone that they entered (assuming that they weren't able to do 100% in every zone). For instance, if they can typically see 750ft, and the current zone allows a max clip of 1000ft, and the bar represented a percent, they'd set it at 75%. However, when they went into the next zone that allowed a max clip of 4000ft, the bar is still at 75% causing them the need to adjust it down to about 20% for the game to remain playable.

Min and max-clip values change from zone to zone. The range of the clip slider doesn't.

-Gordon
