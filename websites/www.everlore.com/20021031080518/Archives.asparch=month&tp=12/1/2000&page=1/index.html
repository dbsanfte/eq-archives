"Sword of Runes"

Thanks for seeing that as a fix. I do. :)

"Call of the Hero

However, Verant has said that the aggro will not be cleared all the time. This has interesting effects:

1) The groupmate will be pulled out of the situation, but likely still in danger. Since the groupmate may still be on the hate list, the mob may give chase or summon the groupmate back."

Yup

"2) The mage and groupmate can use CoH to pull a mob they otherwise could not pull.

It's this last one that's interesting to me. Does Verant realize that this change will give groups with CoH-equipped mages the ability to pull big mobs more easily? I think clearing the hate list is the right thing to do. The mob believes the player to have gated, unless the summoned player is within the normal aggro range of the mob."

Well, it could be used that way, I suppose. But the chance of the hate list not being cleared is supposed to be pretty low… it might be faster to pull it the normal way.

"And thanks for fixing the pet suicide!"

For you, anything. :)

"Spell Skill Class
-----------------
I see Verant continuing the evocation path with magicians. This is disheartening. We are conjurers. Let the wizards have their evocation. Long ago I asked GZ why spells like "Rain of Spikes" were evocation. His answer? Paraphrase: "It was likely a mistake, but we can't change it now since people may have based their specialization on it being evocation." Sigh, I went to magician school. Mages should conjure heavy and sharp flying objects rather than rely on fire so heavily IMHO. Why are so many of our spells (with Kunark and current Velious spells) fire-based?"

Umm… I dunno? I'm, again, not really going to comment about Velious spells. But from reading the – oh so excellent – Prima Strat Guide, it does suggest that magicians should either specialize in conjuration or in evocation, and that many mages choose evocation.

Is that really how it should be? I dunno. But GZ is right about it being a little late to change horses mid-stream. I'll mention this comment to the folks here and let them think about it, though for whatever is in Velious it's likely too late to fiddle with that.

"Rain Spells
------------
Why do things that can't be affected by these spells reduce their effectiveness? I'm a magician...I'm going to have a pet up nearly all the time. Why is it that the rain loses a wave to my pet when my pet can't be harmed by the rain? Our rain spells are efficient only when all waves have a chance to hit. We pay for that efficiency with a resistance check on each wave and the risks of aggroing more mobs with its AE nature. Totally losing a wave on our pet makes these spells mana inefficient and is one of the main reasons they are not more heavily used. If you can't "fix" the pet problem, would you consider making the rain spells target only rather than AE? Again, the resistance checks on each wave should balance the spells."

It's just as easy to argue the other way, that pets should be damaged because they are obviously being hit by the spell…

The real way to look at this is from the game mechanic point of view. Is having the pet take a wave making the spell too weak? Would making him not hit make the spell too strong? Honestly, I don't know. I do plan to bring this up, but probably not until after the holidays. I know that's not fast enough, but it's as fast as it's gonna happen. Verant deserves a holiday too…

Necros can fear a mob, giving their DOT time to work. Magicians have no means of lengthening a fight other than to cast more slowly. In fact, the longer a fight continues, the worse shape magicians are in, typically.

You have to be kidding me. Corpse dragging is instant argo in some SoV zones?

How about a weeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeee bit more info. Will Rogues hide / sneak be broken while pulling bodies? Will regular invis disappear? Are you prepared to tell a player that looses everything that he spent months if not years getting that "Sorry, there is nothing I can do" if he cant get a Corpse summoned or pulled out? Just what are the limits?

Ok, here's a bit more information.

The idea is that there are some places where, with a good faction, characters can travel unharmed. Some of those places are very, very dangerous to those that are not aligned with the folks that live there. These will be major adventuring/hunting zones for those that don’t mind not being able to wander around free there and talk to the locals.

We really like it that way, but setting things up like that leads to a simple problem. Folks that are hated by the natives can make assaults on them and have their 'friends' who are well liked by the locals simply drag their corpses to safety, right in front of the people they were trying to kill. There are other potential abuses of this situation that I won't describe…

So, to maintain the risk of fighting in these difficult places, we had to have these mobs get upset when you drag their kills off. And, yes, rogues and invisible types will have to be a lot more careful doing their recoveries once they actually lift that corpse up on their shoulders and start to scamper away.

You will not have a problem dragging your own corpse (at least not any more of a problem than you would normally have). So if you accidentally fall to your death and you are friendly with the natives, you will be able to drag your own corpse all over the zone with no additional problems. But then, you won't need a safe spot either…

This will make corpse recovery a little bit more difficult in the few places where this rule is used. But we see this as a necessary and useful inclusion. We wanted to tell you about it before hand to help reduce the number of people surprised by this addition.

Alan

The active global EverQuest subscriber base is comprised of players from various countries throughout the ''real'' world, including the U.S., England, Canada, Singapore, Zimbabwe, Saudi Arabia, France and New Zealand.

Luclin: The Stryngs of Ayre Tournament
Flarestar Bladesinger [12/5/2000-1:57:05 PM]  Comments[0]

The Stryngs of Ayre will be hosting a non-profit, player run tournament for these four classes. These are meant to be contests of skill, and nothing but. As such, the rules are going to be a bit different. Any characters who are between level 25 and 39 are invited. The fee is 30pp per character, with the winner of each bracket taking the prize. Combatants will be chosen within a specific level range of each other, with no combatant being more than four levels above another to ensure a fair fight as well as is possible and still be able to hold the contest. ;)

Here's the catch. As these are contests of skill, no special gear is allowed. That means for all three classes, come equipped in leather armor. It's cheap, requires no special effort to get, and gives no stat bonuses to give either combatant an advantage. Weapons are to be basic, meaning fine steel weapons only. No jewelry, again to keep from giving an advantage. Anyone caught breaking these rules will be disqualified.

Rogues are allowed to use any poisons they choose, except for those causing effects similar to those listed below. Rangers are allowed the use of all magic except for the types prohibited below. Bards are to be allowed their choice between using weapons and instruments. However, should they choose instruments, they will be store-bought, not magical. All classes are to be allowed ranged weapons of their choice, provided they are store-bought.

Hide/sneak, invisibility, etc. IS allowed. The only tactics disallowed are fear and charm. Stunning and mesmerization are allowable. All combatants are to remain on the raised platform at all times. If you move off of the platform, you will be disqualified.

Spectators who wish to attend are welcome, of course. All we ask is that you keep off of the platform, refrain from excessive talking and casting during combat due to lag reasons. No outside buffs will be permitted, only those the combatants themselves are capable of. Combatants themselves will have to decide whether it's worth taking the risk their opponent won't attack while they are buffing themselves.

Matches will be alternated between classes for each tier, meaning that the rogue 25-29 tier will fight, then rangers, then bards. After each tier is finished, the winners of each class may face each other in combat, provided all three agree to it. If they choose to do so, they will each do a /random 100, and the two highest fight first. The winner of that fight faces the third. Winner of that fight takes the entire pot for all three classes of that tier.

Fights will continue as long as there are people who want to fight. Combatants will pay their entry fee as their tier and class begins combat. Basically, when the 25-29 tier of rogues is called, anyone who wishes to enter into that tier pays then. Once the tier has started, of course, no more entrants will be allowed. That should help to keep people from having to sit around for ages waiting. ;)

Hopefully I'll have a Guide on-hand to assist. If not, then we'll just do the best we can. :) If anyone wants any clarification on the rules, please feel free to email me at ziyadah420@hotmail.com. I hope to see all of you there.

EQ Patch message - Dynamic Lighting
Catalyst [12/5/2000-2:00:10 PM]  Comments[0]

December 5th, 4:00am
------------------------------

*Emergency Patch*

This morning's patch was to correct a problem with the EverQuest client that caused those trying to exit The Hole or The Plane of Hate to crash and not be able to reenter the game.

As part of this patch we've added a new /-command. /dynamiclights [on/off](on by default) will turn on or off your ability to view dynamic lighting in game, such as that created by a torch or lightstone. Dynamic lighting uses CPU power. We've found that those participating in large raids with many people can increase their framerate by setting this to off for the scope of the raid.

New settings to this option will take place after you zone, and the preferred setting is stored in the EQCLIENT.INI

- The EverQuest Team

With the last patch (or maybe the one before that), we decided to turn off mip mapping for everything but the world (in other words, it's not on for characters and objects).

We did this because mip mapping all those textures was causing performance problems and pauses for many players. That, and we really think subjectively that the advantages of mip mapping are for the most part apparent and beneficial only on world geometry.

That said, you seem to disagree. We'd love to hear some other feedback on this. Perhaps we could provide several options (for example, no mip mapping, mip mapping world only, mip mapping all textures) were there a demand.

Let us know, and maybe Rowyl can look into it and let us know if such options are possible.
